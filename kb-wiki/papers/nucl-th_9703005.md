# Nuclear and Neutron Matter Calculations with Different Model Spaces

> In this work we investigate the so-called model-space Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (MBHF) approach for nuclear matter as well as for neutron matter and the extension of this which includes the particle-particle and hole-hole (PPHH) diagrams. A central ingredient in the model-space approach for nuclear matter is the boundary momentum $k_M$ beyond which the single-particle potential energy is set equal to zero. This is also the boundary of the model space within which the PPHH diagrams are calculated. It has been rather uncertain which value should be used for $k_M$. We have carried out model-space nuclear matter and neutron matter calculations with and without PPHH diagrams for various choices of $k_M$ and using several modern nucleon-nucleon potentials. Our results exhibit a saturation region where the nuclear and neutron matter matter energies are quite stable as $k_M$ varies. The location of this region may serve to determine an "optimum" choice for $k_M$. However, we find that the strength of the tensor force has a significant influence on binding energy variation with $k_M$. The implications for nuclear and neutron matter calculations are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper investigates the model-space Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (MBHF) approach and its extension including particle-particle and hole-hole (PPHH) diagrams for nuclear and neutron matter, finding that there exists a saturation region where the calculated energies are stable with respect to the choice of the boundary momentum \(k_M\), but this stability and the optimal \(k_M\) value depend significantly on the strength of the tensor force in the nucleon-nucleon potential.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent MBHF calculations for nuclear matter and neutron matter using several modern nucleon-nucleon potentials (Bonn A, B, C, CD-Bonn, Nijmegen OBE, and Nijm-I), varying the model-space boundary momentum \(k_M\) from the Fermi momentum up to 3.8 fm\(^{-1}\). They then extended these calculations by solving the random-phase approximation (RPA) equation to sum the PPHH diagram series within the model space, including both two-body and one-body vertex corrections, with exact treatment of the self-consistency condition for the RPA eigenvalues.

## Key numbers

- The empirical nuclear matter binding energy is \(BE/A = 16 \pm 1\) MeV and saturation density is \(\rho_0 = 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\) (stated as target values).
- For MBHF calculations with the Bonn A potential, the energy variation is less than 0.5 MeV for \(k_M\) between 2.0 and 3.5 fm\(^{-1}\) at \(k_F = 1.2\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- The MBHF approach yields about 3 MeV additional binding energy compared to standard BHF calculations.
- The upper bound for \(k_M\) is set at 3.8 fm\(^{-1}\), beyond which the single-particle potential becomes repulsive and the G-matrix would become complex.
- For MBHF calculations, \(k_M = 3.0\) fm\(^{-1}\) is identified as an optimum choice, with \(E/A\) varying no more than 0.5 MeV for \(k_M\) between 2.5 fm\(^{-1}\) and 3.2 fm\(^{-1}\) for both Bonn A and C potentials.
- For PPHH-diagram calculations, \(k_M = 3.8\) fm\(^{-1}\) is found to be an optimal choice for Bonn A and B potentials, while Bonn C shows saturation at higher \(k_M\) values.

## Context

This work extends the model-space Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach of Ma and Kuo (1983) and the PPHH-diagram calculations of Song et al. (1987) and Jiang et al. (1988) by systematically investigating the dependence on the boundary momentum \(k_M\) for multiple modern nucleon-nucleon potentials. The results address the long-standing Coester band problem in non-relativistic nuclear matter calculations, where simultaneous reproduction of empirical binding energy and saturation density has proven difficult. The findings enable more reliable application of the MBHF and PPHH methods by providing criteria for choosing \(k_M\), and they clarify the role of tensor force strength in determining saturation properties, complementing the self-consistent Green's function approach of Dickhoff, Polls, and Ramos (1989, 1991, 1992).
