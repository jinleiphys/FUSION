# An effective gauge field theory of the nucleon interactions

> We discuss the possibility of constructing an effective gauge field theory of the nucleon interations based on the ideas of isotopic invariance as well as hypercharge invariance as a local gauge symmetry and spontaneous breaking of this symmetry. The constructed model predicts the structure of interactions of protons and neutrons with $ρ$- and $σ$-mesons, with pi-mesons and photons, as well as interactions of these particles with each other. The Lagrangian of the model consists of several parts parts involving dimension 4 and 5 gauge invariant operators. Feynman rules for physical degrees of freedom as follow from the Lagrangian define the structure of diagrams for one-boson exchanges between nucleons predicting the internucleon one-boson exchange potential as well as nucleon scattering amplitudes. The range of applicability of the model is discussed and estimates are made of the resulting coupling constants. The model predicts the mass of the neutral $ρ^0$-meson to be about $1\,MeV$ larger than the mass of the charged mesons $ρ^{\pm}$. The vector $ω$-meson, which is a sterile particle with respect to the considered gauge group $SU_I(2)\times U_Y(1)$, can be added to the scheme by means of a gauge-invariant operator of dimension 5, as shown in Appendix ~A.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an effective gauge field theory of nucleon interactions based on local isotopic and hypercharge symmetry with spontaneous symmetry breaking, but the theory operates in a strong coupling regime (α_N ≥ 1) and is only applicable at energies below the characteristic QCD scale, with the expansion parameter v_I/M_N ≈ 0.26.

## Method

The author builds a gauge-invariant Lagrangian for nucleon (proton and neutron) fields under the symmetry group SU_I(2) × U_Y(1), introducing vector fields identified as ρ-mesons and a complex scalar doublet whose spontaneous symmetry breaking generates masses for the gauge bosons and a σ-meson (identified with f_0(500)). The Lagrangian includes dimension-4 and dimension-5 gauge-invariant operators, with pions introduced as isotopic spin triplets with zero hypercharge, and the ω-meson added via a dimension-5 operator. Feynman rules are derived from the Lagrangian to define one-boson exchange potentials and scattering amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The predicted mass difference between neutral and charged ρ-mesons: M_ρ⁰ − M_ρ± ≈ 0.989 MeV
- The vacuum expectation value: v_I ≈ 260 MeV
- The gauge coupling constant: g_N ≈ 5.97, giving α_N = g_N²/(4π) ≈ 2.84
- The σ-meson mass range: M_σ = 400–500 MeV
- The quartic scalar coupling: λ_I = 1.2–1.8 (for M_σ = 400–500 MeV)
- The pion-sigma coupling constant: λ_πσ ≈ 5.5 (for M_σ = 500 MeV and Γ_σ ≈ 400 MeV)
- The expansion parameter: v_I/M_N ≈ 0.26
- The ρ-meson decay width formula: Γ_ρ = (g_N²/(48π)) M_ρ β³, with β = √(1 − 4m_π²/M_ρ²)
- The σ-meson branching ratios: Br(σ → π⁺π⁻) = 2/3, Br(σ → π⁰π⁰) = 1/3

## Context

This work extends the original Yang-Mills (1954) idea of treating isotopic symmetry as a local gauge symmetry, combined with the Brout-Englert-Higgs mechanism of spontaneous symmetry breaking as used in the Standard Model, to construct an effective theory of nucleon interactions. It builds upon the long history of meson exchange potentials including the Bonn potential (Machleidt, 2000), the Nijmegen potential (Schulze, 2011, 2013), and the Moscow potential (Neudatchin, 1975), as well as Weinberg's (1968) chiral approach to pion-nucleon forces. The theory enables calculation of one-boson exchange potentials and nucleon scattering amplitudes from a gauge-invariant Lagrangian, with the specific prediction of a small mass difference between neutral and charged ρ-mesons that motivates more precise experimental measurements.
