# Relativistic approach to the nuclear breathing mode

> Microscopic theory of the nuclear response based on the relativistic meson-nucleon Lagrangian is applied to the description of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) in a variety of nuclear systems. It is shown that the parameter-free inclusion of beyond-mean-field correlations of the quasiparticle-vibration coupling (qPVC) type in the leading approximation allows for a simultaneous realistic description of the ISGMR in nuclei of lead, tin, zirconium, and nickel mass regions, which is difficult on the mean-field level. The calculations employ the finite-range effective meson-nucleon interaction, which, in combination with the qPVC, has consistently demonstrated the ability to reliably describe many other nuclear structure phenomena. Systematic calculations of the isoscalar monopole response for nickel isotopes help reveal the central role of the coupling between the ISGMR and the low-energy quadrupole states in the placement of the ISGMR centroids.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including quasiparticle-vibration coupling (qPVC) beyond the relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (RQRPA) in a parameter-free way allows a simultaneous realistic description of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) across lead, tin, zirconium, and nickel mass regions, which is difficult at the mean-field level; the key hedge is that this is demonstrated only in the leading approximation of the qPVC, and the theory is stated to be systematically improvable.

## Method

The relativistic quasiparticle time blocking approximation (RQTBA) was applied, which extends the RQRPA by including the dynamical kernel of the equation of motion for the response function, specifically the quasiparticle-vibration coupling amplitude in the leading approximation. Calculations used the finite-range NL3* meson-nucleon interaction (compression modulus K_infinity = 258 MeV) with the phonon model space including RQRPA modes of natural parity up to spin J = 6 and energy 20-30 MeV, with a subtraction procedure to ensure convergence. The superfluid pairing correlations were treated within Bogoliubov theory using monopole pairing forces with pairing gaps adjusted to empirical values.

## Key numbers

- ISGMR centroid in 208Pb: RQRPA provides a good description, while the RQTBA centroid remains practically intact after including qPVC
- ISGMR centroid in 120Sn: RQRPA positions it too high; RQTBA moves it to lower energy, achieving visible improvement
- E(2+_1; 208Pb) = 4.09 MeV, much larger than E(2+_1; 120Sn) = 1.17 MeV, supporting the conjecture about stronger qPVC in open-shell nuclei
- ISGMR centroid shifts in nickel isotopes 56-70Ni due to qPVC: the downward shifts Delta<E>_ISGMR show a clear countertrend with E(2+_1) values, with the decrease of E(2+_1) corresponding to increase of the ISGMR centroid shifts
- For 90Zr: the experimental energy of the first 2+ state is 2.19 MeV; the RQTBA improves both the width and centroid of the ISGMR compared to RQRPA, with a more modest shift than in 120Sn
- The qPVC-induced shift of the ISGMR centroid can amount up to 1-2 MeV in softer mid-shell nuclear species

## Context

This work extends the relativistic quasiparticle time blocking approximation (RQTBA) pioneered in Refs. LRT2007 and LRT2008, which previously demonstrated good performance for a variety of nuclear excited states including neutral and charge-exchange channels. The paper directly addresses the long-standing problem that DFT parametrizations with commonly accepted K_infinity values describing the ISGMR well in 208Pb notably overestimate the ISGMR centroid in tin isotopes (Garg 2018). The results highlight the insufficiency of DFT description of complex nuclear phenomena and the necessity of consistently including complex correlations for determining the nuclear equation of state.
