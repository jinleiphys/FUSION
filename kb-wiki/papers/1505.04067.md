# A short review of some double-parton scattering processes

> A few examples of quickly developing field of double parton scattering are discussed. We present arguments that the production of two pairs of charm quark-antiquark is the golden reaction to study the double parton scattering effects. We discuss briefly also mechanism of single parton scattering and show that it gives much smaller contribution to the $c \bar c c \bar c$ final state. In this context we discuss also the contribution of perturbative parton-splitting mechanism which should also be added to the conventional DPS mechanism. The presence of the letter leads to collision energy and other kinematical variables dependence of so-called $σ_{eff}$. We briefly discuss production of four jets. We concentrate on estimation of the contribution of DPS for jets widely separated in rapidity. Finally we briefly mention about DPS effects in production of $W^+ W^-$.

## Key claim

The production of two pairs of charm quark-antiquark is presented as the "golden reaction" to study double parton scattering effects, with the qualification that the single parton scattering contribution to the $c \bar c c \bar c$ final state is much smaller, and a perturbative parton-splitting mechanism must also be added to the conventional DPS mechanism.

## Method

The paper reviews double parton scattering (DPS) formalism using a factorized Ansatz where each hard scattering is calculated in the $k_t$-factorization approach, with the total DPS cross section expressed as a product of two single parton scattering (SPS) cross sections divided by an effective cross section $\sigma_{eff}$. For $c \bar c c \bar c$ production, the author compares DPS contributions to SPS contributions and to the perturbative parton-splitting (2v1) mechanism, and compares calculated distributions for $D^0$ meson pairs to LHCb data. For jet production, the author compares DPS contributions to collinear pQCD results and to BFKL Mueller-Navelet calculations at leading-logarithm and next-to-leading-logarithm order.

## Key numbers

- At LHC energies, the cross section for DPS production of $c \bar c c \bar c$ and SPS production of $c \bar c$ become comparable, a "completely new situation"
- The SPS cross section for $c \bar c c \bar c$ at LHC energies is more than two orders of magnitude smaller than that for $c \bar c$ production and much smaller than the DPS contribution
- For the empirical $\sigma_{eff}$ calculation in $c \bar c c \bar c$ production, $\sigma_{eff,2v2} = 30$ mb and $\sigma_{eff,2v1} = 15$ mb
- For jet production with cuts $y_1, y_2 \in$ (-4.7,4.7) and $p_{1t}, p_{2t} \in$ (35 GeV, 60 GeV), DPS gives sizeable relative contribution only at large rapidity distance
- For $W^+ W^-$ production at $\sqrt{s} = 14$ TeV, the DPS cross section is 0.6367(-03) compared to the $q \bar q$ contribution of 0.06402; at $\sqrt{s} = 100$ TeV, DPS is 0.03832 versus $q \bar q$ at 0.53820

## Context

This review extends the long-standing study of double parton scattering, recognized since the 1970s and 1980s (LP78, T1979, GSH80, H1983, PT1984, PT1985, M1985, HO1985, SZ1987), with recent theoretical developments (S2003, KS2004, SS2004, GS2010, GS2011, DS2011, RS2011, DOS2011, MW2012a, MW2012b, DK2013, DKK2014). The work builds on the recognition by LMS2012 that $c \bar c c \bar c$ production is a good place to study DPS, and on LHCb data for simultaneous production of two charmed mesons (Aaij 2012). The paper enables future experimental studies at the LHC and FCC to distinguish DPS contributions from SPS and BFKL mechanisms, particularly for $c \bar c c \bar c$ production, jets at large rapidity separation, and $W^+ W^-$ production as a background to Higgs production.
