# Sequential Heavy Ion Double Charge Exchange Reactions and the Link to Double $β$-decay

> Heavy ion double charge exchange reactions are described by sequential meson-exchange, corresponding to a double single charge exchange (DSCE) reaction mechanism. The theoretical formulation is discussed. The fully quantum mechanical distorted wave 2-step calculations are shown to be reproduced very well by approximating the intermediate propagator by its pole part. The role of ion-ion elastic interactions is discussed. As a first application, calculations are performed for the reaction $^{40}$Ca $(^{18}$O $,^{18}$Ne $)^{40}$Ar at 15 AMeV. Results are compared to the data measured at LNS by the NUMEN Collaboration. The common aspects of DSCE reactions and double $β$- decay are discussed by a detailed comparison of the respective nuclear matrix elements (NME).

## Key claim

Heavy ion double charge exchange (DCE) reactions at energies well above the Coulomb barrier can be described as a double single charge exchange (DSCE) process driven by collisional nucleon-nucleon interactions, and the resulting reaction amplitudes show a striking similarity to the nuclear matrix elements of $2\nu 2\beta$ decay, though the Single State Dominance assumption that works for $2\nu 2\beta$ decay may not hold for heavy ion DSCE reactions.

## Method

The authors developed a fully quantum mechanical distorted wave two-step formalism for sequential DCE reactions, where the reaction proceeds as two consecutive single charge exchange (SCE) events mediated by isovector nucleon-nucleon interactions. The intermediate propagator was approximated by its pole part (Pole Approximation) and compared to full partial wave two-step calculations using the FRESCO code. Nuclear structure aspects were described using Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) ground-state densities and Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) transition densities, folded with the anti-symmetrized nucleon-nucleon T-matrix. Calculations were performed for the reaction $^{40}$Ca $(^{18}$O $,^{18}$Ne $)^{40}$Ar at 15 AMeV and compared to data measured at LNS by the NUMEN Collaboration.

## Key numbers

- The DCE reaction $^{40}$Ca $(^{18}$O $,^{18}$Ne $)^{40}$Ar was studied at 15 AMeV (energy per nucleon).
- The suppression factor from initial state and final state interactions (ISI/FSI) was found to be $N_{\alpha\beta}\sim 10^{-5}$, indicating strong absorption effects.
- The measured angular range covered momentum transfers up to $q_{\alpha\beta}\sim 400$ MeV/c.
- Intermediate states up to $E_x$ = 15 MeV and $0^\pm \leq J^\pi \leq 5^\pm$ were included for both $^{18}$F and $^{40}$K to achieve convergent results.
- The experimental angular resolution used for folding theoretical cross sections was $\Delta\theta_{exp}=0.6^\circ$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigations of single charge exchange reactions (Lenske 2018, Lenske 2019) to higher-order double charge exchange processes, and builds on earlier heavy ion DCE studies that focused on pair transfer mechanisms (Dasso 1985, Dasso 1986). The theoretical framework enables the extraction of nuclear matrix elements from DCE cross sections, providing a laboratory-accessible probe of nuclear structure aspects relevant to double beta decay, complementing rare weak interaction processes studied in the NUMEN Collaboration's experimental program.
