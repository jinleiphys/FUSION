# Nonequilibrium quark production in the expanding QCD plasma

> We perform real-time lattice simulations of nonequilibrium quark production in the longitudinally expanding QCD plasma. Starting from a highly occupied gluonic state with vacuum quark sector, we extract the time evolution of quark and gluon number densities per unit transverse area and rapidity. The total quark number shows after an initial rapid increase an almost linear growth with time. Remarkably, this growth rate appears to be consistent with a simple kinetic theory estimate involving only two-to-two scattering processes in small-angle approximation. This extends previous findings about the role of two-to-two scatterings for purely gluonic dynamics in accordance with the early stages of the bottom-up thermalization scenario.

## Key claim
The total quark number in the expanding QCD plasma shows an almost linear growth with time after an initial rapid increase, and this growth rate appears consistent with a simple kinetic theory estimate involving only two-to-two scattering processes in the small-angle approximation, though the overoccupied regime with gluon occupancies ~1/αs cannot be approximated by the perturbative power counting underlying such kinetic descriptions.

## Method
The authors performed 3+1 dimensional real-time lattice simulations of SU(2) gauge field theory with Nf flavors of degenerate quarks at weak coupling in a longitudinally expanding geometry using co-moving coordinates. They initialized the system with a highly occupied gluonic state characterized by a saturation momentum Qs and vacuum quarks, then evolved the classical-statistical gluonic fields coupled to stochastic fermion fields representing quark dynamics, extracting quark and gluon distribution functions and number densities per unit transverse area and rapidity.

## Key numbers
- Coupling constant g = 10^(-2) used in simulations
- Initial time Qs τ0 = 100 adopted for the start of the incoherent gluon plasma evolution
- Initial gluon distribution: fg(τ0, p⊥, ν) = (n0/g²) Θ(Qs - √(p⊥² + (ξ0 ν/τ0)²))
- Lattice momentum maximum occurs at kmax ≈ floor(0.286 N⊥) for the improved derivative scheme with coefficients c1 = 4/3 and c2 = -1/6
- Debye mass scale: mD² = 4g² (1/(τ L⊥² Lη)) Σ_{p⊥,ν} (1/p)[Nc fg + Nf fq]

## Context
This work extends previous real-time lattice simulations of purely gluonic dynamics in the longitudinally expanding non-Abelian plasma (Berges 2013, Berges 2013) that identified a number-conserving regime described by two-to-two scattering processes in agreement with the early stages of the bottom-up thermalization scenario (Baier 2000). It also builds on fixed-box lattice simulations with dynamical quarks (Gelfand 2016) and kinetic descriptions with longitudinal expansion (Tanji 2017). The results enable future comparisons to full effective kinetic theory solutions with quarks and provide evidence that such an enterprise is worth pursuing.
