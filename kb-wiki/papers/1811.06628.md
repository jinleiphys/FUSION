# Parameter Optimisation for the Latest Quark-Meson Coupling Energy Density Functional

> The Quark--Meson--Coupling (QMC) model self-consistently relates the dynamics of the internal quark structure of a hadron to the relativistic mean fields arising in nuclear matter. It offers a natural explanation to some open questions in nuclear theory, including the origin of many-body nuclear forces and their saturation, the spin-orbit interaction and properties of hadronic matter at a wide range of densities. The QMC energy density functionals QMC-I and QMC$π$-I have been successfully applied to calculate ground state observables of finite nuclei in the Hartree-Fock + BCS approximation, as well as to predict properties of dense nuclear matter and cold non-rotating neutron stars. Here we report the latest development of the model, QMC$π$-II, extended to include higher order self-interaction of the $σ$ meson. A derivative-free optimization algorithm has been employed to determine a new set of the model parameters and their statistics, including errors and correlations. QMC$π$-II predictions for a wide range of properties of even-even nuclei across the nuclear chart, with fewer adjustable parameters, are comparable with other models. Predictions of ground state binding energies of even-even isotopes of superheavy elements with Z$>$96 are particularly encouraging.

## Key claim

The QMCπ-II model, which adds a cubic self-interaction of the σ meson to the previous QMCπ-I framework, achieves predictions for nuclear ground-state observables comparable to other energy density functionals while using fewer adjustable parameters, but the fit quality for charge radii and proton pairing gaps is slightly worse than QMCπ-I.

## Method

The QMCπ-II energy density functional was derived from the quark-meson coupling model, incorporating a σ meson self-interaction term with parameter λ₃, and was implemented in a Hartree-Fock + BCS calculation using the SKYAX code for axially symmetric and reflection-asymmetric shapes. A derivative-free optimization algorithm (POUNDeRS) was employed to fit seven free parameters (three effective meson-nucleon couplings G_σ, G_ω, G_ρ, the σ meson mass m_σ, the σ self-interaction λ₃, and two pairing strengths) to experimental binding energies, charge radii, and pairing gaps of 70 magic and semi-magic even-even nuclei, while also constraining nuclear matter properties at saturation.

## Key numbers

* Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.15 ± 0.01 fm⁻³, binding energy per nucleon E₀ = -15.78 ± 0.02 MeV, asymmetry coefficient a_sym = 30.6 ± 0.3 MeV, and incompressibility K₀ = 270 ± 2 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter.
* Slope of the symmetry energy L₀ = 70 MeV, not included in the fit but calculated from the final parameter set.
* Percentage deviations for the 70 fitted nuclei: binding energy 0.34%, rms charge radius 0.59%, proton pairing gap 25.7%, neutron pairing gap 16.1%.
* RMSD for binding energies of 669 even-even nuclei (Z < 96) not in the fit: 2.23 MeV; RMSD for charge radii: 0.030 fm.
* Best-fit parameters with 95% confidence intervals: G_σ = 9.05 [9.02, 9.08] fm², G_ω = 5.29 [5.27, 5.30] fm², G_ρ = 4.71 [4.64, 4.78] fm², m_σ = 495 [491, 498] MeV, λ₃ = 0.049 [0.048, 0.050], V_p^pair = 288 [280, 295] MeV, V_n^pair = 275 [261, 290] MeV.

## Context

This work extends the QMC model lineage from QMC-I (Stone 2016) and QMCπ-I (Stone 2017, Guichon 2018) by adding a σ meson self-interaction, which reduces the previously high incompressibility (340 MeV for QMC-I, 319 MeV for QMCπ-I) to 270 MeV, consistent with giant monopole resonance data. The improved L₀ value of 70 MeV brings the model closer to constraints from terrestrial measurements and astrophysical observations (Li 2013) and to the finite-range droplet model (Moller 2016). The results enable systematic predictions for superheavy elements and provide a framework for further refinements, such as including the Wigner effect for N=Z nuclei and improving charge radii descriptions in deformed regions.
