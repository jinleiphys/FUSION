# Second order chiral restoration phase transition at low temperatures in quarkyonic matter

> In this Addendum to our recent paper, Phys. Rev. D 77, 054027 (2008), we point out that a chiral restoration phase transition in a quarkyonic matter at low temperatures is of second order within a manifestly confining and chirally symmetric large $N_c$ model. This result is qualitatively different as compared to NJL and NJL-like models that are not confining and might have some implications for the existence or nonexistence of the critical end point in the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim
The chiral restoration phase transition in quarkyonic matter at low temperatures is of second order within a manifestly confining and chirally symmetric large \(N_c\) model, in contrast to the first-order transition found in non-confining NJL and NJL-like models. This result is obtained under the caveat that the ground state is approximated as a quark Fermi gas, which may not be realistic within a confining model.

## Method
The authors solve the Schwinger-Dyson (gap) equation for a large \(N_c\) model where the only gluonic interaction is a linear confining potential of the Coulomb type. They compute the chiral angle \(\varphi_p\) and the dynamical quark mass \(M(p)\) as functions of the quark Fermi momentum \(p_f\) at zero temperature, and examine their behavior at the critical Fermi momentum where chiral symmetry is restored.

## Key numbers
- The chiral restoration phase transition occurs at a critical Fermi momentum \(p_f^{cr} = 0.109 \sqrt{\sigma}\), where \(\sigma\) is the string tension providing the dimensional scale.
- At \(p_f = p_f^{cr}\), the chiral angle takes its trivial value \(\varphi_p = 0\) and the dynamical mass \(M(p) = 0\), with both quantities approaching these values continuously.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Glozman and Wagenbrunn, Phys. Rev. D 77, 054027 (2008)) of chiral restoration at finite density and zero temperature. It contradicts the first-order chiral restoration transition found in NJL models (Nambu and Jona-Lasinio) and Polyakov-improved NJL models (PNJL), which lack explicit confinement. The result suggests that if the low-temperature, high-density chiral transition is second order or a crossover, there may be no critical end point in the QCD phase diagram, contrary to predictions from non-confining models.
