# Bag model and quark star

> In this paper, incorporating the property of the vacuum negative pressure, namely, the bag constant, we presented a new model of the equation of state (EOS) of quark matter at finite chemical potential and zero temperature. By comparing our EOS with Fraga {\it et~al.}'s EOS and SQM1 model, one find that our EOS is softer than Fraga {\it et~al.}'s EOS and SQM1 model. The reason for this difference is analyzed. With these results we investigate the structure of quark star. A comparison between our model of quark star and other models is made. The obtained mass of quark star is $ 1.3 \sim 1.66 M_\odot$ and the radius is $9.5 \sim 14 Km$. One can see that our star's compactness is smaller than that of other two models.

## Key claim

The authors present a new, renormalizable equation of state (EOS) for quark matter at finite chemical potential and zero temperature by incorporating the vacuum bag constant into a previously developed QCD-based EOS, and they find that their resulting EOS is softer than both Fraga et al.'s perturbative QCD EOS and the SQM1 model, leading to quark star masses and radii that depend strongly on the chosen bag constant value.

## Method

The authors start from a quark propagator with complex conjugate poles (the 2CC parameter set from Maris) and use path integral methods to compute the quark number density and pressure at finite chemical potential. They then identify the zero-chemical-potential pressure term with the negative bag constant -B, and construct the energy density as epsilon = -P + mu * dP/dmu + B. Using three phenomenological values of B, they integrate the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain quark star mass-radius relations, assuming homogeneous pure three-flavor quark matter with no crust, no chemical equilibrium, and no electric neutrality conditions.

## Key numbers

- Quark star mass range: 1.3 to 1.66 solar masses (from abstract); maximum masses for specific B values: 1.75 solar masses for B = (65.5 MeV)^4, 1.42 solar masses for B = (92 MeV)^4, 1.3 solar masses for B = (108 MeV)^4
- Quark star radius range: 9.5 to 14 km (from abstract); specific radii: 14 km for B = (65.5 MeV)^4, 10.6 km for B = (92 MeV)^4, 9.2 km for B = (108 MeV)^4
- Central number densities: 3.2 times nuclear saturation density (rho_0) for B = (65.5 MeV)^4, 5.2 rho_0 for B = (92 MeV)^4, 6.6 rho_0 for B = (108 MeV)^4
- Bag constant values used: B = (65.5 MeV)^4, (92 MeV)^4, (108 MeV)^4; for comparison, SQM1 uses B = (164.34 MeV)^4 and Weber (2000) gives range (145 MeV)^4 < B < (162 MeV)^4

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QCD-based EOS (Zong et al., 2005) by adding the vacuum bag constant, analogous to the MIT bag model approach. The resulting softer EOS is compared to Fraga et al.'s perturbative QCD model and the SQM1 model (Baron et al., 2007), and the authors note that their model is more appropriate for low-density quark matter while the other models work better at high density. The paper enables future studies that could incorporate chemical equilibrium, electric neutrality, and crust structure, which the authors acknowledge are needed for a more realistic quark star model.
