# Covariant electroweak nucleon form factors in a chiral constituent quark model

> Results for the proton and neutron electric and magnetic form factors as well asthe nucleon axial and induced pseudoscalar form factors are presented for the chiral constituent quark model based on Goldstone-boson-exchange dynamics. The calculations are performed in a covariant framework using the point-form approach to relativistic quantum mechanics. The direct predictions of the model yield a remarkably consistent picture of the electroweak nucleon structure.

## Key claim

The chiral constituent quark model based on Goldstone-boson-exchange dynamics, when treated covariantly via the point-form spectator approximation, predicts all electroweak nucleon form factors in remarkable agreement with experiment without introducing any additional parameters beyond those already fixed by baryon spectroscopy. However, the model slightly underestimates the axial charge at zero momentum transfer and the axial radius.

## Method

The authors used the point-form approach to relativistic quantum mechanics, constructing a Poincare-invariant mass operator for the three-quark system with a linear confinement potential and a hyperfine interaction from pseudoscalar Goldstone-boson exchange. Electromagnetic and axial current matrix elements were computed in the Breit frame using the point-form spectator approximation, where the current operator acts on a single constituent quark treated as a point-like Dirac particle, and the nucleon wave functions from the chiral constituent quark model of Glozman et al. (1998) were boosted exactly to the initial and final states.

## Key numbers

- Proton charge radius: 0.81 fm^2 (PFSA prediction) vs. experimental 0.780(25) fm^2
- Neutron charge radius: -0.13 fm^2 (PFSA prediction) vs. experimental -0.113(7) fm^2
- Proton magnetic moment: 2.7 nuclear magnetons (PFSA prediction) vs. experimental 2.792847337(29) n.m.
- Neutron magnetic moment: -1.7 n.m. (PFSA prediction) vs. experimental -1.91304270(5) n.m.
- Axial charge g_A: 1.15 (PFSA prediction) vs. experimental 1.255 +/- 0.006
- Axial radius sqrt(<r_A^2>): 0.53 fm (PFSA prediction) vs. experimental 0.635(23) fm
- Quark-pion coupling constant used: g_pi q^2 / 4 pi = 0.67

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model of Glozman et al. (1998), which had successfully described baryon spectroscopy, to the electroweak form factors of the nucleon. It demonstrates that the same effective degrees of freedom (constituent quarks and Goldstone bosons) that explain the mass spectrum also produce a consistent picture of nucleon structure when treated relativistically. The results contradict the nonrelativistic impulse approximation, which fails dramatically even at low momentum transfer, and show that the neutron electric form factor and charge radius require both the hyperfine interaction (to produce mixed-symmetry wave function components) and Lorentz boosts. The authors note that quantitative improvements could come from including two-body currents or effects from possible constituent quark substructure.
