# Astrophysical aspects of $^{12}$C$(p,γ)^{13}$N reaction

> The Carbon-Nitrogen-Oxygen (CNO) cycle is fundamental to the process of hydrogen burning in stars, serving as a pivotal mechanism. At its core, the primary reaction involves the radiative capture of a proton by $^{12}$C, crucially influencing the isotopic ratio of $^{12}$C to $^{13}$C observed in celestial bodies, including our Solar System. We have addressed this reaction mechanism by extrapolating to low-energy cross sections and S-factors with the aid of astrophysical R-matrix. Our investigation aims to shed light on its implications for nuclear reaction rates, thus influencing the abundance ratio of $^{12}$C to $^{13}$C in the cosmic environment.

## Key claim
The paper reanalyzes the low-energy cross section and astrophysical S-factor for the 12C(p,γ)13N reaction using R-matrix theory, but the results depend on fitting parameters to experimental data from a single recent dataset (Gyurky et al. 2023) and the extrapolation is limited to energies below 2.0 MeV.

## Method
The author performed an R-matrix analysis using the AZURE2 code to fit experimental cross-section data for the 12C(p,γ)13N reaction from 300 keV to 1900 keV proton energy. The calculation included two resonances (at 421 keV and 1556 keV), direct capture contributions, and their interference effects, with the direct capture amplitude normalized using an Asymptotic Normalization Coefficient (ANC). The best-fit parameters for resonance energies, proton widths, and gamma widths were obtained by minimizing chi-squared, yielding a reduced chi-squared of approximately 1.01.

## Key numbers
- ANC for the ground state of 13N: 1.525 fm^{-1/2}
- First resonance energy: 421 keV (J^π = 1/2+), fitted proton width Γ^p = 35.87 keV, fitted gamma width Γ^γ = 519.37 meV
- Second resonance energy: 1556 keV (J^π = 3/2-), fitted proton width Γ^p = 44.95 keV, fitted gamma width Γ^γ = 542.03 meV
- Reaction rate at T_9 = 0.10 GK: 1.75E-05 cm^3 mol^{-1} s^{-1} (this work) compared to 2.07E-05 cm^3 mol^{-1} s^{-1} (CF88)
- Reaction rate at T_9 = 1.00 GK: 559.358 cm^3 mol^{-1} s^{-1} (this work) compared to 8.68E+02 cm^3 mol^{-1} s^{-1} (CF88)
- Q-value for the (p + 12C) channel: 1.9435 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous measurements by Rolfs et al. and Vogl by reanalyzing the recent experimental dataset from Gyurky et al. (2023) using R-matrix methods. The results are compared with the analytical approach of CF88 (Caughlan and Fowler 1988) and with NACRE reaction rates, showing agreement within 2% at lower temperatures (T_9 = 0.30). The improved reaction rates at low energies (0.1 to 1.0 GK) are directly relevant for modeling the 12C/13C isotopic ratio in Asymptotic Giant Branch stars and for understanding CNO cycle nucleosynthesis.
