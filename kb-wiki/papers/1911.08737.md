# The $ϕ$ meson with finite momentum in a dense medium

> The dispersion relation of the $ϕ$ meson in nuclear matter is studied in a QCD sum rule approach. In a dense medium, longitudinal and transverse modes of vector particles can have independently modified dispersion relations due to broken Lorentz invariance. Employing the full set of independent operators and corresponding Wilson coefficients up to operator dimension 6, the $ϕ$ meson QCD sum rules are analyzed with changing densities and momenta. The non-trivial momentum dependence of the $ϕ$ meson mass is found to have opposite signs for the longitudinal and transverse modes. Specifically, the mass is reduced by 5 MeV for the longitudinal mode, while its increase amounts to 7 Mev for the transverse mode, both at a momentum scale of 1 GeV. In an experiment which does not distinguish between longitudinal and transverse polarizations, this could in principle be seen as two separated peaks at large momenta. Taking however broadening effects into account, the momentum dependence will most likely be seen as a small but positive effective mass shift and an increased effective width for non-zero momenta.

## Key claim

The dispersion relation of the φ meson in nuclear matter acquires a non-trivial momentum dependence that is opposite for longitudinal and transverse polarization modes, with the longitudinal mass decreasing and the transverse mass increasing as momentum grows. However, the authors note that in an experiment that does not distinguish polarizations, the effect will most likely be seen as a small positive effective mass shift and an increased effective width rather than as two separated peaks, once broadening effects are taken into account.

## Method

The authors employed QCD sum rules for the vector current of strange quarks, using the operator product expansion (OPE) up to dimension-6 operators including the full set of non-scalar (twist) operators that encode the broken Lorentz invariance in the medium. They applied the linear density approximation to evaluate nuclear matter expectation values of the condensates, used a "pole + continuum" ansatz for the spectral function, and performed a Borel-transform analysis to extract the momentum-dependent spectral parameters (mass, coupling, and continuum threshold) for both longitudinal and transverse modes separately at normal nuclear matter density.

## Key numbers

- At a momentum scale of 1 GeV and normal nuclear matter density, the longitudinal mode mass is reduced by 5 MeV, while the transverse mode mass increases by 7 MeV.
- The vacuum φ meson mass obtained from the sum rule analysis is 1.020 GeV.
- The quadratic parametrization of the mass shift gives coefficients: b^T = 0.0067 ± 0.0034 GeV^{-2} for the transverse mode and b^L = -0.0048 ± 0.0008 GeV^{-2} for the longitudinal mode, with a zero-momentum coefficient a = -0.0067.
- The quadratic behavior of the mass shift is valid up to |q| ≃ 3.0 GeV; above |q| ≃ 4.5 GeV a valid Borel window can no longer be defined.
- The in-medium widths tested for the Breit-Wigner analysis were 15, 40, and 65 MeV, compared to the vacuum width of 4.26 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule studies of the φ meson in nuclear matter (Hatsuda 1991, Asakawa 1994, Gubler 2014) by including the full set of non-scalar operators up to dimension-6, enabling the first calculation of the momentum-dependent dispersion relation separately for longitudinal and transverse modes. It provides predictions for the upcoming J-PARC E16 experiment (Aoki 2015), which will measure φ meson dilepton spectra at finite momentum. The results contrast with earlier hadronic effective theory calculations (Ko 1992, Klingl 1997) that predicted strong broadening rather than mass shifts, and with the E325 experiment (Muto 2005) which reported a negative mass shift of 35 MeV and a width increase by a factor of 3.6 at zero momentum.
