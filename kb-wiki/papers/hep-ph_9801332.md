# Quark Orbital Motion in the Nucleon

> An unified scheme for describing both spin and orbital motion in symmetry-breaking chiral quark model is suggested. The analytic results of the spin and orbital angular momenta carried by different quark flavors in the nucleon are given. The quark spin reduction due to spin-flip in the chiral splitting processes is compensated by the increase of the orbital angular momentum carried by the quarks and antiquarks. The sum of both spin and orbital angular momenta in the nucleon is 1/2, if the gluons and other degrees of freedom are neglected. The same conclusion holds for other octet and decuplet baryons. Possible modification and application are briefly discussed.

# Quark Orbital Motion in the Nucleon

## Key claim

Within the chiral quark model, the reduction of quark spin due to spin-flip in chiral splitting processes is exactly compensated by an increase in orbital angular momentum carried by quarks and antiquarks, such that the total angular momentum of the nucleon remains 1/2, but this result holds only if gluons and other degrees of freedom are neglected, and relies on the assumption that the orbital angular momentum produced in each splitting is equally shared among the three final-state particles (quark, quark, and antiquark) with a partition factor k = 1/3.

## Method

The author calculates the spin and orbital angular momentum contents of the nucleon using a chiral quark model with SU(3) and U(1) breakings, where constituent quarks couple to Goldstone bosons (pions, kaons, eta, and eta-prime). The quark spin reduction from spin-flip fluctuations q↑ → q′↓ + GB is computed from the transition probabilities a, εa, εηa, and ζ′²a, and the induced orbital angular momentum is determined by assuming that each spin-flip process transfers one unit of angular momentum to the orbital motion, which is then equally partitioned among the two quarks and one antiquark in the final state.

## Key numbers

- The total orbital angular momentum carried by quarks in the proton is ⟨Lz⟩q^p = 2ka(1 + ε + f), and by antiquarks is ⟨Lz⟩q̄^p = ka(1 + ε + f), with k = 1/3.
- Using a ≃ 0.15 and 1 + ε + f ≃ 2.0, the numerical estimates are ⟨Lz⟩q^p ≃ 0.20 and ⟨Lz⟩q̄^p ≃ 0.10.
- The ratio of orbital to total angular momentum is ⟨Lz⟩q+q̄^p / ⟨Jz⟩q+q̄^p ≃ 3/5, meaning nearly 60% of the proton spin comes from orbital motion of quarks and antiquarks, and 40% from quark spin.
- The ratio ⟨Lz⟩q^p / ⟨Lz⟩q̄^p = 2 is independent of the chiral fluctuation probabilities.
- Including a gluon contribution ⟨Jz⟩G(1 GeV²) ≃ 0.25 ± 0.10 gives modified values ⟨Lz⟩q+q̄(1 GeV²) ≃ 0.15 ± 0.07 and ⟨sz⟩q+q̄(1 GeV²) ≃ 0.10 ± 0.07.
- The proton-neutron magnetic moment ratio improves from −1.33 (without orbital motion, k = 0) to −1.38 (with k = 1/3), compared to the experimental value −1.48.

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark model approach of Manohar and Georgi (1984) and the earlier application by Eichten, Hinchliffe, and Quigg (1992) to explain both sea flavor asymmetry and the smallness of ΔΣ. It builds on the SU(3)- and U(1)-breaking formulation developed by Song (1997) and provides a unified scheme for spin and orbital angular momentum that was independently suggested by Cheng and Li (1997). The results enable future calculations of baryon magnetic moments that include orbital contributions, and can be compared with lattice QCD results such as those from Dong, Lagae, and Liu (1995) and with deep-inelastic scattering data from the SMC, SLAC, and HERMES collaborations.
