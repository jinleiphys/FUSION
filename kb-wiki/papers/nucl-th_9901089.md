# A linked cluster expansion for the calculation of the semi-inclusive A(e,e'p)X processes using correlated Glauber wave functions

> The distorted one-body mixed density matrix, which is the basic nuclear quantity appearing in the definition of the cross section for the semi-inclusive A(e,e'p)X processes, is calculated within a linked-cluster expansion based upon correlated wave functions and the Glauber multiple scattering theory to take into account the final state interaction of the ejected nucleon. The nuclear transparency for 16O and 40Ca is calculated using realistic central and non-central correlations and the important role played by the latter is illustrated.

## Key claim

The paper presents a linked-cluster expansion that includes both initial-state correlations (ISC) and final-state interactions (FSI) to all orders in Glauber multiple scattering, but the abstract's claim that the role of non-central correlations is "illustrated" hedges the finding that realistic central and non-central correlations increase the nuclear transparency by only about 2%, whereas central-only Jastrow correlations increase it by about 12%.

## Method

The authors calculate the distorted one-body mixed density matrix for semi-inclusive A(e,e'p)X processes using a linked-cluster expansion based on correlated wave functions and Glauber multiple scattering theory. The nuclear wave function is constructed from harmonic oscillator orbitals with correlation operators corresponding to the V6 Reid soft core (RSC) interaction, including central, spin, isospin, and tensor correlations. The nuclear transparency is then computed for 16O and 40Ca by integrating the distorted momentum distribution, with the Glauber profile parametrized using standard parameters (sigma_tot = 43 mb, alpha = -0.33, b_o = 0.6 fm).

## Key numbers

- For 16O with central correlations: T = 0.57 (T_SM = 0.51, Delta T^SM_FSI = 0.020, Delta T^H_FSI = 0.032, Delta T^S,1_FSI = -0.013, Delta T^S,2_FSI = 0.022)
- For 16O with realistic correlations: T = 0.52 (T_SM = 0.51, Delta T^SM_FSI = 0.003, Delta T^H_FSI = 0.009, Delta T^S,1_FSI = 0.001, Delta T^S,2_FSI = -0.001)
- For 40Ca with central correlations: T = 0.47 (T_SM = 0.41, Delta T^SM_FSI = 0.020, Delta T^H_FSI = 0.028, Delta T^S,1_FSI = -0.011, Delta T^S,2_FSI = 0.023)
- For 40Ca with realistic correlations: T = 0.42 (T_SM = 0.41, Delta T^SM_FSI = 0.002, Delta T^H_FSI = 0.008, Delta T^S,1_FSI = -0.001, Delta T^S,2_FSI = 0.001)
- Glauber profile parameters: sigma_tot = 43 mb, alpha = -0.33, b_o = 0.6 fm

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of nuclear transparency that used simplified Jastrow-type wave functions with only central correlations, such as those by Bianconi et al. and Nikolaev et al. The results agree with exact calculations for 4He by Ciofi degli Atti et al. using the same realistic interaction, showing that triple- and higher-order Glauber multiple scattering contributions from correlations are negligible. The approach enables more reliable calculations of nuclear transparency for both light and heavy nuclei, which is a prerequisite for interpreting experimental searches for Color Transparency effects at large Q^2.
