# Expanded empirical formula for Coulomb final state interaction in the presence of Lévy sources

> Measurements of momentum space correlations in heavy ion reactions are a unique tools to investigate the properties of the created medium. However, these analyses require the careful handling of the final state interactions such as the Coulomb repulsion of the involved particles. In small systems such as $e^+ + e^-$ or $p+p$ the well-known Gamow factor gives an acceptable description but in the case of extended sources like that are created in heavy ion collisions, a more sophisticated approach has to be developed. In this paper we expand our previous work on the investigation of the Coulomb final state interaction in the presence of a Lévy source. Such sources were shown to be a statistically acceptable assumption to describe the quantumstatistical correlation functions in high energy heavy ion reactions.

# Expanded empirical formula for Coulomb final state interaction in the presence of Lévy sources

## Key claim

The paper provides an expanded empirical parametrization of the Coulomb final state interaction correction for Bose-Einstein correlation functions, valid for Lévy sources with Lévy index α between 0.8 and 2.0 and source radius R between 2 fm and 12 fm, with a maximum relative deviation of about 0.07% from the numerical lookup table. The parametrization is intended to replace slower numerical lookup tables in experimental fitting procedures, but the authors caution that the functional form cannot be used to extrapolate beyond the fitted q range of 0 to 0.2 GeV/c.

## Method

The authors constructed a numerical lookup table by evaluating the integral of the Coulomb-modified two-particle wave function over a spherically symmetric Lévy source distribution, then fitted an empirical parametrization to the table values. The parametrization generalizes a previous CMS collaboration formula for the Cauchy (α=1) case by introducing α-dependent coefficients A, B, C, D in a rational function, combined with an exponential damping term and a power-law smoothing function. The resulting Coulomb correction K(q,α,R) is then inserted into the Bowler-Sinyukov formula to produce the full correlation function.

## Key numbers

- The parametrization is valid for Lévy index α in the range 0.8 < α < 2.0 and source radius R in the range 2 fm < R < 12 fm
- The q range of validity is 0 GeV/c < q < 0.2 GeV/c
- The maximum relative deviation of the parametrization from the numerical lookup table is approximately 0.07%
- The smoothing function uses a cutoff parameter q₀ = 0.07 GeV/c with exponent n = 20
- The exponential damping fit for the high-q range (0.1–0.2 GeV/c) uses coefficients such as A_a = 0.126253 and B_a = 19.31620
- The parametrization reproduces previous PHENIX results from PhysRevC.97.064911

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation (Csanád 2019) of Coulomb final state interaction for Lévy sources, and generalizes a parametrization used by the CMS collaboration (Sirunyan 2017) for the special case of Cauchy sources (α=1). The parametrization enables faster fitting of Bose-Einstein correlation functions in heavy ion experiments compared to using numerical lookup tables, which can cause fluctuations in χ² landscapes and are slow to use. The Lévy source assumption itself builds on prior work showing that Lévy distributions provide a statistically acceptable description of correlation functions in high energy heavy ion reactions (PhysRevC.97.064911, Kurgyis 2018), and may carry information about phase transitions, partial coherence, or in-medium mass modifications (Csörgő 2003, Csörgő 2005).
