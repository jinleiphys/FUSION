# Stability and structure of quark matter in a molecular dynamics framework

> We study stability and structure of quark matters as a function of density in a framework of molecular dynamics (MD). Using appropriate effective interactions and the frictional cooling method, we search for the minimum energy of the system. Transition from confined to deconfined phase is observed at densities of 2 -- 3$ρ_0$, where $ρ_0$ is the nuclear matter saturation density. The $uds$ matter becomes more stable than the charge-neutral $ud$ matter at 3$ρ_0$, but the $udd$ matter is the most stable even at high density.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a transition from confined to deconfined quark matter occurs at densities of 2–3 times nuclear saturation density (ρ₀), and that udd matter (with twice as many d quarks as u quarks) is the most stable configuration across all densities studied, even though uds matter becomes more stable than charge-neutral ud matter at 3ρ₀.

## Method

The authors used a molecular dynamics (MD) framework with Gaussian wave packets for quarks, neglecting antisymmetrization but introducing a phenomenological Pauli potential to mimic Fermi statistics. They employed a Hamiltonian including kinetic energy, a color interaction (with string tension K=900 MeV/fm and α_s=1.25), and a meson exchange potential (σ and ω mesons) between quarks. The ground state was found by solving cooling equations of motion with friction for systems of 90 quarks in a periodic cell at various baryon densities.

## Key numbers

- Transition from confined to deconfined phase occurs at densities of 2–3ρ₀, where ρ₀ is nuclear matter saturation density.
- Constituent quark masses used: m_u,d = 300 MeV, m_s = 500 MeV.
- String tension K = 900 MeV/fm; QCD fine structure constant α_s = 1.25.
- Meson-quark coupling constants: g_σq = 3.42, g_ωq = 9.0; meson masses μ_σ = 300 MeV, μ_ω = 782 MeV.
- Pauli potential parameters for u and d quarks: C_p = 131 MeV, q_0 = 1.8 fm, p_0 = 120 MeV; for s quarks: C_p = 79 MeV.
- Quark wave packet size L = 0.43 fm; effective size L^eff = 0.7 fm used for meson exchange potential.
- At 1ρ₀, 90 quarks are placed in a cell of size 5.6 fm.

## Context

This work extends the hypothesis of Bodmer (1971) and Witten (1984) that strange quark matter might be the absolute ground state of strongly interacting matter, and builds on previous molecular dynamics studies by Maruyama (2000). The results are relevant to understanding the interior of neutron stars and quark stars, where high baryon densities may allow coexistence of confined and deconfined phases. The authors note that their model lacks a flavor-dependent ρ meson exchange potential, which they expect would improve the description of udd versus uds matter stability.
