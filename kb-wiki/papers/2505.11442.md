# Neural-Network Correlation Functions for Light Nuclei with Chiral Two- and Three-Body Interactions

> Finding high-quality trial wave functions for quantum Monte Carlo calculations of light nuclei requires a strong intuition for modeling the interparticle correlations as well as large computational resources for exploring the space of variational parameters. Moreover, for systems with three-body interactions, the wave function should account for many-body effects beyond simple pairwise correlations. In this work, we design neural networks that efficiently incorporate these factors to generate expressive wave function Ansatzes for light nuclei using variational Monte Carlo. Our neural-network approach for A=3 nuclei can capture, already at the level of variational Monte Carlo, the overwhelming majority of the ground-state energy estimated by Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC). We can find a 91% improvement over standard variational Monte Carlo and achieve a ground state energy within 0.45% of the GFMC result for 3H using the softest chiral interaction with neural networks. The result indicates the potential of neural networks to construct effective trial wave functions for quantum Monte Carlo calculations.

## Key claim

The authors claim that neural-network-generated correlation functions can produce variational Monte Carlo (VMC) trial wave functions for A=3 nuclei that capture the overwhelming majority of the ground-state energy estimated by Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC), but this result is demonstrated only for the softest chiral interaction with resolution-scale parameters (R0 = 1.2 fm, Lambda = 1 GeV) and the performance degrades for harder interactions.

## Method

The authors construct trial wave functions for 3H and 3He using neural networks to generate the spatial correlation functions in the standard VMC Ansatz, which includes two-body spin-isospin operators and three-body operators at N2LO in chiral effective field theory. Two neural networks are employed: NNa encodes features of individual particle pairs from their separation distance, while NNb takes both the pair distance and the sum of NNa outputs from surrounding particles to generate all correlation functions. The neural networks are trained via VMC by minimizing the estimated ground-state energy, with importance sampling performed using normalizing flow models to generate Monte Carlo samples.

## Key numbers

- For 3H with the softest chiral interaction (R0 = 1.2 fm, Lambda = 1 GeV), the neural network VMC achieves a ground-state energy of E = -8.30 +/- 0.09 MeV, which is within 0.45% of the GFMC result of E = -8.34 MeV
- The neural network VMC shows a 91.2% improvement over standard parameterized VMC, estimated as (E_param - E_neural)/(E_param - E_GFMC)
- For 3H with two-body-only chiral potentials at R0 = 1.2 fm, the neural network result is -7.678 +/- 0.005 MeV compared to GFMC at -7.740 +/- 0.005 MeV, a 0.8% deviation
- For 3H with two-body-only chiral potentials at R0 = 1.0 fm, the neural network result is -7.338 +/- 0.008 MeV compared to GFMC at -7.554 +/- 0.007 MeV, a 2.9% deviation
- For 2H with two-body-only chiral potentials at R0 = 1.0 fm, the neural network result is -2.217 +/- 0.005 MeV compared to GFMC at -2.21 +/- 0.02 MeV, a 0.3% deviation

## Context

This work extends the application of neural-network wave function Ansatze from simplified nuclear models to full chiral effective field theory interactions including tensor, spin-orbit, charge-independence-breaking, charge-symmetry-breaking, and three-body forces, which previous neural network studies such as Yang (2023) had not addressed for pion-exchange interactions. The results enable the use of neural-network-generated trial wave functions as inputs to imaginary-time quantum Monte Carlo methods like GFMC or auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo, potentially improving both efficiency and accuracy for light nuclei up to A=4, with future extension to larger systems via neural-network-constructed Slater determinants.
