# Role of the Pauli principle in collective-model coupled-channels calculations

> A multi-channel algebraic scattering theory, to find solutions of coupled-channel scattering problems with interactions determined by collective models, has been structured to ensure that the Pauli principle is not violated. By tracking the results in the zero coupling limit, a correct interpretation of the sub-threshold and resonant spectra of the compound system can be made. As an example, the neutron-12C system is studied defining properties of 13C to 10 MeV excitation. Accounting for the Pauli principle in collective coupled-channels models is crucial to the outcome.

## Key claim

The Pauli principle must be explicitly enforced in collective-model coupled-channels calculations for low-energy nucleon-nucleus scattering; without such enforcement, many spurious bound and resonant states appear in the compound spectrum, and even when states can be matched in energy and spin-parity, their underlying nucleon-plus-core compositions are incorrect.

## Method

The authors used the multi-channel algebraic scattering (MCAS) theory, which solves coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equations in momentum space via a finite sum of energy-independent separable terms (sturmian expansions). The orthogonalizing pseudo-potential (OPP) method was applied to select sturmians that exclude components where the external nucleon would occupy already-filled orbits, thereby enforcing the Pauli principle. Results with and without the OPP treatment were compared in the zero-deformation limit (\(\beta_2 \to 0\)) for the neutron-\(^{12}\)C system, using a rotational collective-model description with Woods-Saxon form factors and deformation taken to second order.

## Key numbers

- The \(0s_{1/2}\) shell in \(^{12}\)C has occupancy 1.963 (protons or neutrons) in the ground state, and the \(0p_{3/2}\) shell has occupancy 3.054, indicating dense filling.
- In the zero-coupling limit without Pauli blocking, a spurious \(\frac{1}{2}^+\) state appears at \(-26.57\) MeV (attributed to \(0s_{1/2} + 0^+_1\)), whereas with Pauli blocking that state is absent.
- The \(\frac{1}{2}^-\) ground state of \(^{13}\)C is bound at \(-4.685\) MeV in the zero-coupling limit with Pauli blocking; without Pauli blocking, the ground state has wrong spin-parity and excessive binding.
- The \(\frac{5}{2}^+\) bound state tends to \(-0.171\) MeV in the zero-coupling limit, and the \(\frac{1}{2}^+\) bound state tends to \(-0.837\) MeV; the difference between these and the resonance at 4.267 MeV equals 4.438 MeV, the excitation energy of the \(2^+\) state in \(^{12}\)C.
- The OPP method used \(\lambda = 100\) MeV as a finite value approximating the infinite-limit requirement.

## Context

This work extends the MCAS approach of Amos et al. (2003) by explicitly analyzing the role of the Pauli principle in collective-model coupled-channel calculations, which had previously been known to be violated (Mackintosh 1969; Gridnev 1996) but without quantification. The OPP method, originally developed by Kukulin (1978) and Saito (1969), was generalized in the 2003 MCAS paper but its implications for collective models were not discussed. The results enable correct identification of sub-threshold and resonant states in compound nuclei like \(^{13}\)C, and the method is applicable to any coupled-channel problem involving fermions.
