# Chemical and thermal freeze-out of identified hadrons at the LHC

> This proceeding briefly summarizes our recent VISHNU hybrid model investigations on the chemical and thermal freeze-out of various hadrons species in 2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions. Detailed analysis on the evolution of particle yields and the last elastic collisions distributions during the hadronic evolution reveals that the two multi-strange hadrons, $Ξ$ and $Ω$, experience early chemical and thermal freeze-out when compared with other hadron species.

## Key claim

Using the VISHNU hybrid model (viscous hydrodynamics + UrQMD hadronic cascade) for 2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb collisions, the two multi-strange hadrons Ξ and Ω experience earlier chemical and thermal freeze-out than other hadron species, due to their much smaller hadronic cross sections. The abstract's claim of "early" freeze-out is qualified by the finding that baryon-antibaryon annihilations delay the chemical freeze-out of Ξ and Ω compared to simulations without such annihilations, though they still freeze out earlier than protons, kaons, pions, and Λ.

## Method

The authors used the VISHNU hybrid model, which couples (2+1)-dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (VISH2+1) for the QGP expansion with the UrQMD hadronic transport model for the hadron resonance gas. Smooth initial conditions were generated from the MC-KLN model with specific shear viscosity η/s = 0.16, and the hydrodynamic-to-UrQMD switching temperature was set to 165 MeV. Chemical freeze-out was studied by tracking the time evolution of particle yield densities and their changing rates during the UrQMD stage, while thermal freeze-out was analyzed by extracting the space-time distributions of the last elastic collisions for each hadron species.

## Key numbers

- Proton and anti-proton yields are reduced by ~30% due to baryon-antibaryon annihilations during hadronic evolution, improving agreement with ALICE data.
- For Ξ and Ω, the peaks of their thermal freeze-out time distributions are located around 10 fm/c.
- For p and Λ, the peaks of their thermal freeze-out time distributions are located around 20-30 fm/c.
- The hydrodynamic simulations start at τ₀ = 0.9 fm/c with specific shear viscosity (η/s)_QGP = 0.16.
- The switching temperature from hydrodynamics to UrQMD is T_sw = 165 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier statistical model approaches (Braun-Munzinger 2001, Andronic 2005, Becattini 2005) that assumed uniform chemical freeze-out temperatures for all hadron species, and pure hydrodynamic simulations (Peter) that used a uniform thermal freeze-out temperature. The VISHNU hybrid model had previously been shown to fit multiplicities, pT spectra, and differential elliptic flow of π, K, p, Λ, Ξ, and Ω at various centrality bins (Song 2013, SongMultistrange). The results enable future extraction of effective chemical freeze-out temperatures for individual hadron species by analyzing the space-time distributions of their last inelastic collisions.
