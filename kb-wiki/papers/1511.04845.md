# Strong decay patterns of the hidden-charm pentaquark states $P_c(4380)$ and $P_c(4450)$

> With the heavy quark symmetry and spin rearrangement scheme, we study the strong decay behavior of the hidden-charm pentaquark states with $J^P={\frac{3}{2}}^{\pm}, {\frac{5}{2}}^{\pm}$ assuming they are molecular candidates composed of ${\bar D}^{(\ast)}$ and $Σ_c^{(\ast)}$. We obtain several typical ratios of the partial decay widths of the hidden-charm pentaquarks. For the three S-wave $(\bar{D}Σ_c^*)$, $(\bar{D}^*Σ_c)$ and $(\bar{D}^*Σ_c^*)$ molecular pentaquarks with $J^P={{3}/{2}}^{-}$, we have obtained the ratio of their $J/ψN$ decay widths: $Γ\left[(\bar D{Σ^{\ast}_c})\right]:Γ\left[({\bar D}^{\ast}Σ_c)\right]:Γ\left[({\bar D}^{\ast}{Σ^{\ast}_c})\right]=2.7:1.0:5.4$, which may be useful to further test the possible molecular assignment of the $P_c$ states.

## Key claim

Assuming the hidden-charm pentaquark states \(P_c(4380)\) and \(P_c(4450)\) are S-wave or P-wave molecular candidates composed of \(\bar{D}^{(*)}\) and \(\Sigma_c^{(*)}\), the authors derive ratios of their strong decay widths into charmonium plus a nucleon using heavy quark symmetry and a spin rearrangement scheme, noting that the absolute widths depend on unknown dynamical matrix elements and only the ratios are predicted.

## Method

The authors decompose the initial molecular pentaquark states and the final charmonium-plus-nucleon states into heavy spin and light spin components in the heavy quark limit. They then calculate the \(T\)-matrix elements for the strong decays using the spin rearrangement scheme, focusing on S-wave and P-wave decays because D-wave and higher partial waves are suppressed by phase space. The ratios of partial decay widths are obtained by combining the decomposition coefficients with the heavy quark symmetry, leaving two unknown reduced matrix elements that cancel in the ratios.

## Key numbers

- Mass of \(P_c(4380)\): \(4380 \pm 8 \pm 29\) MeV; width: \(205 \pm 18 \pm 86\) MeV (LHCb).
- Mass of \(P_c(4450)\): \(4449.8 \pm 1.7 \pm 2.5\) MeV; width: \(39 \pm 5 \pm 19\) MeV (LHCb).
- For the three S-wave \((\bar{D}\Sigma_c^*)\), \((\bar{D}^*\Sigma_c)\), and \((\bar{D}^*\Sigma_c^*)\) molecular pentaquarks with \(J^P = 3/2^-\), the ratio of their \(J/\psi N\) decay widths is \(\Gamma[(\bar{D}\Sigma_c^*)] : \Gamma[(\bar{D}^*\Sigma_c)] : \Gamma[(\bar{D}^*\Sigma_c^*)] = 2.7 : 1.0 : 5.4\).

## Context

This work extends dynamical calculations of \(\Sigma_c \bar{D}^*\) and \(\Sigma_c^* \bar{D}^*\) systems using the one-pion exchange model by Chen, Liu, Li, and Zhu (2015), which predicted molecular states near the masses of \(P_c(4380)\) and \(P_c(4450)\). It also complements studies using QCD sum rules (Chen et al., 2015) and the Bethe-Salpeter equation (He, 2015) that assigned specific spin-parity configurations to these states. The predicted decay-width ratios provide a test for the molecular assignment of the \(P_c\) states and can be compared with future experimental measurements.
