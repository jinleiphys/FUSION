# Photon-jet correlations in p-p and Pb-Pb collisions using JETSCAPE framework

> It is now well established that jet modification is a multistage effect; hence a single model alone cannot describe all facets of jet modification. The JETSCAPE framework is a multistage framework that uses several modules to simulate different stages of jet propagation through the QGP medium. These simulations require a set of parameters to ensure a smooth transition between stages. We fine tune these parameters to successfully describe a variety of observables, such as the nuclear modification factors of leading hadrons and jets, jet shape, and jet fragmentation function. Photons can be produced in the hard scattering or as radiation from quarks inside jets. In this work, we study photon-jet transverse momentum imbalance and azimuthal correlation for both $p-p$ and $Pb-Pb$ collision systems. All the photons produced in each event, including the photons from hard scattering, radiation from the parton shower, and radiation from hadronization are considered with an isolation cut to directly compare with experimental data. The simulations are conducted using the same set of tuned parameters as used for the jet analysis. No new parameters are introduced or tuned. We demonstrate a significantly improved agreement with photons from $Pb-Pb$ collisions compared to prior efforts. This work provides an independent, parameter free verification of the multistage evolution framework.

## Key claim

The JETSCAPE multistage framework, using the same set of parameters previously tuned for leading-hadron and jet observables without any additional tuning, achieves significantly improved agreement with photon-jet correlation data from Pb-Pb collisions compared to prior single-module efforts, providing an independent parameter-free verification of the multistage evolution approach.

## Method

The authors used the JETSCAPE framework with PYTHIA for initial hard scattering, MATTER for the intermediate shower in p-p collisions, and MATTER combined with LBT for Pb-Pb collisions, followed by PYTHIA string hadronization via a colorless hadronization scheme. Photon-jet transverse momentum imbalance (x_J) and azimuthal correlation (Delta phi) distributions were computed for p-p collisions at 2.76 TeV and Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, including all photon sources (prompt, shower, and hadronization) with isolation cuts matching CMS and ATLAS experimental criteria. Pre-generated hydro profiles with initial conditions were used for Pb-Pb collisions, and 27 million p-p events and 66 million Pb-Pb events were generated.

## Key numbers

- p-p collisions at 2.76 TeV: jet radius R_jet = 0.3, isolation cone Delta R = 0.4, isolation momentum p_T < 5 GeV, jet p_T > 30 GeV, photon pseudorapidity |eta_gamma| > 1.44, jet pseudorapidity |eta_jet| > 1.6
- Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV: jet radius R_jet = 0.4, isolation cone Delta R = 0.3, isolation momentum p_T < 8 GeV, jet p_T > 31.6 GeV, photon pseudorapidity |eta_gamma| > 2.37 with exclusion of 1.37 < |eta_gamma| < 1.52, jet pseudorapidity |eta_jet| > 2.8
- 27 million events (in 54 pT hat bins) for p-p analysis at 2.76 TeV
- 66 million events (in 66 pT hat bins) for Pb-Pb analysis at 5.02 TeV
- Azimuthal cut for back-to-back condition: |Delta phi_{gamma-jet}| > 7pi/8

## Context

This work extends the JETSCAPE collaboration's previous studies of leading hadron and jet nuclear modification factors, jet shapes, and jet fragmentation functions (Kumar 2019, Park 2019, Kumar 2020) to photon-jet correlations, providing an independent test of the multistage framework. The results demonstrate that the same set of parameters can describe multiple observables across different center-of-mass energies, contrasting with single-module approaches that could not simultaneously describe all facets of jet modification. The study enables future improvements by noting that medium-induced photon emission terms are not yet included in the energy loss modules, leaving room for further theoretical development.
