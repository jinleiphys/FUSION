# Gluon Pair Production From Space-Time Dependent Chromofield

> We compute the probabilty for the processes A -> q \bar{q}, gg via vacuum polarization in the presence of a classical space-time dependent non-abelian field A by applying the background field method of QCD. The processes we consider are leading order in gA and are simillar to A -> e^+e^- in QED. Gluons are treated like a matter field and gauge transformations of the quantum gluonic field are different from those of the classical chromofield. To obtain the correct physical polarization of gluons we deduct the corresponding ghost contributions. We find that the expression for the probability of the leading order process A -> gg is transverse with respect to the momentum of the field. We observe that the contributions from higher order processes to gluon pair production need to be added to the this leading order process. Our result presented here is a part of the expression for the total probability for gluon pair production from a space-time dependent chromfield. The result for q \bar{q} pair production is similar to that of the e^+e^- pair production in QED. Quark and gluon production from a space-time dependent chromofield will play an important role in the study of the production and equilibration of the quark-gluon plasma in ultra relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper computes the leading-order (in \(gA\)) probability for gluon pair production from a space-time dependent classical chromofield using the background field method of QCD, but notes that this result is only a part of the total probability and that contributions from higher-order processes in \(gA\) must be added to obtain the complete expression.

## Method

The authors apply the background field method of QCD, which allows Feynman rules involving a classical chromofield and quantum gluons, to compute the amplitude for the process \(A \rightarrow gg\) via vacuum polarization. They use the vertex for a single classical field and two gluons in Feynman gauge, sum over gluon polarizations using the standard sum \(-g^{\mu\nu}\), and then subtract the corresponding ghost contributions to obtain the correct physical polarization. The resulting probability is integrated over the phase space of the two produced gluons, with a factor of 1/2 for identical particles.

## Key numbers

* For SU(3) gauge group, the probability for gluon pair production from the leading-order process \(A \rightarrow gg\) is \(W_{Agg}^{(1)} = \frac{11 \alpha_s}{8} \int_{K^2>0} d^4K ~\theta(K^0)~[ K^2A^a(K)\cdot A^a(-K)-K\cdot A^a(K)K\cdot A^a(-K)]\).
* For quark-antiquark pair production from a space-time dependent chromofield in SU(3), the probability is \(W_{q\bar{q}}^{(1)}=\frac{\alpha_s}{6} \int_{K^2>4m_q^2} d^4K ~{(1-\frac{4m_q^2}{K^2})}^{1/2}(1+\frac{2m_q^2}{K^2}) ~[K\cdot A^a(K)K\cdot A^a(-K) -K^2A^a(K)\cdot A^a(-K)]\).
* The gluon pair production probability is transverse with respect to the momentum \(K\) of the field, i.e., it vanishes when \(A^\mu\) is replaced by \(K^\mu\).

## Context

This work extends the study of particle production from classical fields, originally developed by Schwinger (1951) for constant electromagnetic fields and later for space-time dependent fields in QED (e.g., Rajaraman and Ravi), to the non-abelian case of gluon production. It provides the first calculation of the leading-order gluon pair production probability from a space-time dependent chromofield, which is needed as a source term in the relativistic non-abelian transport equation for partons (Nayak, Heinz) to model the production and equilibration of the quark-gluon plasma in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. The result is a step toward a full calculation that must include higher-order terms in \(gA\) to be complete.
