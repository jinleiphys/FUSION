# A realistic spectral function model for charged-current quasielastic-like neutrino and antineutrino cross sections on $^{12}$C

> A detailed study of charged current quasielastic neutrino and antineutrino scattering cross sections on a $^{12}$C target with no pions in the final state is presented. The initial nucleus is described by means of a realistic spectral function $S(p,{\cal E})$ in which nucleon-nucleon correlations are implemented by using natural orbitals through the Jastrow method. The roles played by these correlations and by final-state interactions are analyzed and discussed. The model also includes the contribution of weak two-body currents in the two-particle two-hole sector, evaluated within a fully relativistic Fermi gas. The theoretical predictions are compared with a large set of experimental data for double-differential, single-differential and total integrated cross sections measured by the MiniBooNE, MINER$ν$A and T2K experiments. Good agreement with experimental data is found over the whole range of neutrino energies. The results are also in global good agreement with the predictions of the superscaling approach, which is based on the analysis of electron-nucleus scattering data, with only a few differences seen at specific kinematics.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a realistic spectral function model incorporating nucleon-nucleon correlations via natural orbitals and the Jastrow method, combined with final-state interactions and two-body meson-exchange currents, can reproduce charged-current quasielastic-like neutrino and antineutrino cross sections on carbon-12 across a wide energy range, though the model's predictions are nearly identical for harmonic-oscillator and natural-orbital spectral functions, indicating insensitivity to the detailed momentum distribution.

## Method
The authors construct a spectral function S(p,E) for carbon-12 using either harmonic-oscillator or natural-orbital single-particle momentum distributions, with nucleon-nucleon correlations implemented through the Jastrow method. Final-state interactions are included via a time-independent optical potential, and two-particle two-hole meson-exchange current contributions are evaluated within a fully relativistic Fermi gas model. The resulting nuclear responses are folded with neutrino and antineutrino fluxes from MiniBooNE, MINERvA, and T2K experiments and compared to double-differential, single-differential, and total integrated cross section data.

## Key numbers
- 2p-2h MEC contributions are approximately 20-25% of the total response at the maximum for MiniBooNE neutrino data, and approximately 15% for the most forward angular bin (0.9 < cosθ < 1.0)
- For MiniBooNE antineutrino data, 2p-2h MEC relative strength increases for backward scattering angles (cosθμ < -0.2)
- The HO+FSI and NO+FSI models differ from SuSAv2 predictions by less than 5-7% at the maximum for MiniBooNE single-differential cross sections
- For MINERvA antineutrino data, the spread among the three models is approximately 1-2%, with 2p-2h MEC contributions of approximately 20-30% of the total
- For T2K data, 2p-2h MEC contributions are approximately 10% of the total, smaller than for MiniBooNE due to the narrower T2K flux peaked at about 0.7 GeV
- The total flux-unfolded cross sections show 2p-2h MEC contributions of approximately 30-35% compared with pure QE contribution at all neutrino energies
- FSI effects on the double differential cross section at most forward angles are approximately 10% for T2K and approximately 2-4% at the peak for MiniBooNE

## Context
This work extends the SuSAv2 superscaling approach presented by the same group (Megias et al., 2016) by replacing the superscaling prescription with realistic spectral functions that account for nucleon-nucleon correlations through the Jastrow method. The model is compared with the SuSAv2-MEC model and experimental data from MiniBooNE, MINERvA, and T2K collaborations. The results enable improved modeling of neutrino-nucleus interactions for oscillation experiments like DUNE and HyperK, though the authors note that the 2p-2h MEC calculation uses a relativistic Fermi gas ground state rather than the spectral function ground state, and that the NOMAD data are overpredicted by all models when 2p-2h MEC are included, consistent with NOMAD selecting true quasielastic rather than quasielastic-like events.
