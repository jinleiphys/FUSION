# 4$α$ linear-chain state produced by $^{9}$Be+$^{9}$Be collision

> Extreme nuclear deformations provide great insight into the geometric formation of quantum many-body systems. In this work, the $4α+2n$ linear chain is assessed in $^{18}$O. We predict excitation energies, moment-of-inertia, $α$-, and $^{9}$Be-decay widths by using the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. We show that the $K^π=3^-$ linear-chain states may be verified by the head-on $^{9}{\rm Be}+{}^{9}{\rm Be}$ collision experiments.

# 4α linear-chain state produced by ⁹Be+⁹Be collision

## Key claim
The 4α+2n linear-chain configuration in ¹⁸O may be verified by head-on ⁹Be+⁹Be collision experiments, but only the K^π = 3⁻ negative-parity band (not the K^π = 0⁺ positive-parity band) has sufficiently strong ⁹Be+⁹Be correlation to be observed via resonant scattering.

## Method
The authors used antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with the Gogny D1S nucleon-nucleon interaction to describe the excited states of ¹⁸O. They imposed a constraint on the quadrupole deformation parameter β to describe extremely deformed 4α linear-chain configurations, performed angular momentum projection, and applied the generator coordinate method using β as the generator coordinate. Decay widths were calculated using reduced width amplitudes in the α+¹⁴C and ⁹Be+⁹Be channels.

## Key numbers
- Quadrupole deformation parameter β = 1.34 for both the positive- and negative-parity intrinsic states
- Moment-of-inertia parameter ħ/2ℑ = 55 keV for the positive-parity band and 58 keV for the negative-parity band, compared to a rigid-rotor estimate of 85 keV
- Excitation energy of the 0⁺ band head: 27.85 MeV (4.21 MeV above the ⁹Be+⁹Be threshold)
- Excitation energy of the 3⁻ band head: 31.09 MeV (7.45 MeV above the ⁹Be+⁹Be threshold)
- α-decay width of the 0⁺ state to ¹⁴C(0⁺; LC): 382 keV; to ¹⁴C(2⁺; LC): 1136 keV
- ⁹Be-decay width of the 3⁻ state to ⁹Be(3/2⁻)+⁹Be(3/2⁻): 264 keV; to ⁹Be(3/2⁻)+⁹Be(5/2⁻): 253 keV
- Overlap of the K^π = 3⁻ linear chain with the ⁹Be+⁹Be Brink wave function: 0.84 at r = 6.5 fm
- Overlap of the K^π = 0⁺ linear chain with the ⁹Be+⁹Be Brink wave function: 0.52 at r = 5.0 fm

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical studies of linear-chain states in carbon isotopes (3α+xn) by Suhara (2010), Ebrahimi (2014), Baba (2016, 2017), and others, which identified 3α linear chains in ¹⁴C. It also builds on earlier predictions of 4α linear chains in ¹⁶O by Suzuki (1972), Ichikawa (2011), and Suhara (2014). The key advance is using ⁹Be+⁹Be scattering (the only stable beryllium isotope combination) rather than the experimentally difficult ⁸Be+⁸Be reaction. The results enable experimental searches for the 4α linear chain via resonant scattering of ⁹Be on ⁹Be, targeting the predicted K^π = 3⁻ band with its large ⁹Be-decay widths.
