# Symmetric cumulants as a probe of the proton substructure at LHC energies

> We present a systematic study of the normalized symmetric cumulants, NSC(n,m), at the eccentricity level in proton-proton interactions at $\sqrt s\!=\! 13$ TeV within a wounded hot spot approach. We focus our attention on the influence of spatial correlations between the proton constituents, in our case gluonic hot spots, on this observable. We notice that the presence of short-range repulsive correlations between the hot spots systematically decreases the values of NSC(2,3) and NSC(2,4) in mid-to-ultra central collisions while increases them in peripheral interactions. In the case of NSC(2,3) we find that, as suggested by data, an anti-correlation of $\varepsilon_2$ and $\varepsilon_3$ in ultra-central collisions, i.e. NSC(2,3)$<0$, is possible within the correlated scenario while it never occurs without correlations. We attribute this fact to the decisive role of correlations on enlarging the probability of interaction topologies that reduce the value of NSC(2,3) and, eventually, make it negative. Further, we explore the dependence of our conclusions on the values of the hot spot radius and the repulsive core distance. Our results add evidence to the idea that considering spatial correlations between the subnucleonic degrees of freedom of the proton may have a strong impact on the initial state properties of proton-proton interactions [1].

## Key claim

The paper shows that including short-range repulsive correlations between gluonic hot spots inside the proton can produce a negative normalized symmetric cumulant NSC(2,3) in ultra-central proton-proton collisions at 13 TeV, whereas the uncorrelated scenario never yields a negative value when the number of hot spots is set to three. The authors attribute this to the role of correlations in modifying the probability of different interaction topologies, but note that the result depends on the interplay between the hot spot radius, the repulsive core distance, and the number of hot spots.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo Glauber simulations of proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV, modeling each proton as a set of gluonic hot spots (default three) whose transverse positions are sampled from a distribution that includes a short-range repulsive correlation term. The wounded hot spot criterion uses an inelasticity density depending on the hot spot radius and the transverse distance between pairs, and each wounded hot spot deposits entropy according to a fluctuating double Gamma distribution. The normalized symmetric cumulants NSC(2,3) and NSC(2,4) were computed from the spatial eccentricity moments epsilon_2 and epsilon_3 on an event-by-event basis, using 4.5 million generated events, with centrality classes defined by the total entropy deposition.

## Key numbers

* NSC(2,3) becomes negative in the [0-0.1%] and [0.1-1%] centrality bins only when repulsive correlations with r_c = 0.4 fm are included; in the uncorrelated scenario (r_c = 0 or fixed r.m.s.) NSC(2,3) remains positive in those bins.
* For the correlated scenario with r_c = 0.4 fm, the default hot spot radius R_hs = 0.32 fm and average proton radius R_p = 1.34 fm; for the uncorrelated case (r_c = 0), R_hs = 0.41 fm and R_p = 1.28 fm.
* NSC(2,3) becomes positive for R_hs/r_c greater than approximately 1.3 and negative for values between about 0.6 and 1.3 in the [0-1%] centrality bin; for R_hs/r_c less than about 0.6, NSC(2,3) is again positive.
* The minimum number of wounded hot spots in the [0-1%] centrality bin is three; for N_w = 3, the average number of collisions is 2 in all correlation scenarios.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of eccentricities in proton-proton collisions (Albacete 2016) by analyzing not just mean values but fluctuations via symmetric cumulants, and it responds to experimental measurements by the CMS Collaboration (2017) showing that NSC(2,3) becomes negative at high multiplicities in p+p, p+Pb, and Pb+Pb collisions. The results support the idea that spatial correlations between subnucleonic degrees of freedom are important for initial state properties, complementing studies of elliptic flow in p+Pb within the IP-Glasma framework (Mantysaari 2017) and wounded quark models (Broniowski 2016). The work enables future constraints on the number of gluonic hot spots, their radius, and the repulsive core distance by comparing model predictions to experimental data on symmetric cumulants.
