# Relaxation of fast collective motion in heated nuclei

> The damping of the collective vibrations in hot nuclei is studied within the semiclassical Vlasov-Landau kinetic theory. The extention of the method of independent sources of dissipation is used to allow for irreversible energy transfer by chaos weighted wall formula. The expressions for the intrinsic width of the giant multipole resonances are obtained. The interplay between the one-body and the two-body channels which contribute to the formation of the intrinsic width in nuclei is discussed.

## Key claim

The intrinsic width of giant multipole resonances in hot nuclei is calculated using a semiclassical Vlasov-Landau kinetic theory extended with independent sources of dissipation, but the resulting expression cannot be represented as a simple sum of widths from independent damping channels due to the coupling between Fermi-surface distortion and memory effects in the collision integral.

## Method

The linearized Vlasov-Landau equation was solved with a source term combining a collision integral for two-body collisional damping (including memory effects) and a relaxation-time approximation for one-body dissipation modeled via the chaos-weighted wall formula. The dynamic Fermi-surface distortions were truncated at multipolarity ℓ = 2, and the dispersion relation was solved for the complex frequency of collective vibrations under the assumption of slightly damped motion (|Im S / Re S| ≪ a). The intrinsic width was then obtained from the imaginary part of the frequency for giant multipole resonances.

## Key numbers

- The contribution of collisional damping to the giant dipole resonance (GDR) widths at zero temperature does not exceed ~30% of the experimental values (Fig. 1, mass-number dependence).
- For the nucleus ¹¹²Sn, the GDR energy was taken as 15.6 MeV, independent of temperature, and the intrinsic width expression is valid for temperatures no more than ≈5 MeV (condition Γ/ℰ ≪ 1).
- The evaluated width shows systematic large deviation from experimental data in the temperature range from 2 to 5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical Vlasov-Landau kinetic theory approach of KMP and collaborators (1990s) by incorporating both one-body and two-body damping channels for giant multipole resonances in heated nuclei. It builds on the method of independent sources of dissipation from KPS (1990s) and the chaos-weighted wall formula from TS (1990s). The results enable future studies of the interplay between collisional and one-body damping mechanisms, though the author notes that deviations from experimental GDR widths in ¹¹²Sn at temperatures 2-5 MeV may require inclusion of angular momentum effects and thermodynamic fluctuations of nuclear shape as considered by MBC (1990s).
