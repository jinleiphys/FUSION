# Spectroscopic factor and proton formation probability for the d3/2 proton emitter 151mLu

> The quenching of the experimental spectroscopic factor for proton emission from the short-lived $d_{3/2}$ isomeric state in $^{151m}$Lu was a long-standing problem. In the present work, proton emission from this isomer has been reinvestigated in an experiment at the Accelerator Laboratory of the University of Jyväskylä. The proton-decay energy and half-life of this isomer were measured to be 1295(5) keV and 15.4(8) $μ$s, respectively, in agreement with another recent study. These new experimental data can resolve the discrepancy in the spectroscopic factor calculated using the spherical WKB approximation. Using the R-matrix approach it is found that the proton formation probability indicates no significant hindrance for the proton decay of $^{151m}$Lu.

## Key claim

The long-standing discrepancy between the experimental and theoretical spectroscopic factor for proton emission from the d3/2 isomeric state in 151mLu is resolved by new, more precise measurements of the proton-decay energy and half-life, provided that the spectroscopic factor is calculated using the spherical WKB approximation; however, the authors argue that the proton formation probability extracted via the R-matrix approach is a more proper and model-independent quantity, and it indicates no significant hindrance for the decay.

## Method

The experiment was performed at the Accelerator Laboratory of the University of Jyväskylä using the recoil-decay tagging (RDT) technique. A 58Ni beam at 266 MeV and 274 MeV bombarded a 96Ru target, and the evaporation residues were separated by the gas-filled recoil separator RITU and implanted into double-sided silicon strip detectors (DSSDs) of the GREAT spectrometer, where proton decays were correlated with preceding implants. Prompt gamma rays were detected by the JUROGAM Ge-detector array, and the data were analyzed with a triggerless data acquisition system using position-energy-time correlations.

## Key numbers

* Proton-decay energy of the d3/2 isomer in 151mLu: 1295(5) keV.
* Half-life of the d3/2 isomer in 151mLu: 15.4(8) microseconds.
* Proton-decay energy Qp (including recoil and screening correction): 1317(5) keV.
* Experimental spectroscopic factor S_p^exp for 151mLu from the present data, using the WKB approximation: 1.01(+0.18/-0.15).
* Theoretical spectroscopic factor S_p^th for 151mLu from RMF+BCS theory: 0.86.
* Proton formation probability |RF_l(R)|^2 for 151mLu: 0.049(3) fm^-1.
* Energy difference between the 151mLu and 151gLu proton-decay lines: 62 keV.

## Context

This work resolves a long-standing discrepancy for the d3/2 proton emitter 151mLu, where earlier data (prc1999) gave a spectroscopic factor of 0.26(+0.14/-0.08) that was much lower than theoretical predictions of 0.73 (Cwiok 1987) or 0.67 (PhysRevC.55.2255). The new results are consistent with a recent study by Taylor et al. (PhysRevC.91.044322) that reported a lower proton-decay energy. The paper extends the R-matrix approach of Qi (2012) to extract proton formation probabilities, showing that the new value for 151mLu fits the trend of spherical and oblate-deformed proton emitters, in contrast to the earlier apparent hindrance. The results enable a more consistent description of d3/2 proton emission across the Z=64-82 region and suggest that the 151mLu isomer may be spherical or have a small oblate deformation (beta2 around -0.07), rather than the beta2 = -0.12 inferred by Taylor et al. (2015).
