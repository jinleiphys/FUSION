# Possible Shape Coexistence and Magnetic Dipole Transitions in $^{17}$C and $^{21}$Ne

> Magnetic dipole(M1) transitions of N=11 nuclei, $^{17}$C and $^{21}$Ne are investigated by using shell model and deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock+blocked BCS wave functions. Shell model calculations predict well observed energy spectra and magnetic dipole transitions in $^{21}$Ne, while the results are rather poor to predict these observables in $^{17}$C. In the deformed HF calculations, the ground states of two nuclei are shown to have large prolate deformations close to $β_2$=0.4. It is also pointed out that the first $K^π=1/2^+$ state in $^{21}$Ne is prolately deformed, while the first $K^π=1/2^+$ state in $^{17}$C is predicted to have a large oblate deformation being close to the ground state in energy, We point out that experimentally observed large hindrance of M1 transition between $I^π=1/2^+$ and $3/2^+$ in $^{17}$C can be attributed to a shape coexistence near the ground state of $^{17}$C.

# Possible Shape Coexistence and Magnetic Dipole Transitions in $^{17}$C and $^{21}$Ne

## Key claim

The experimentally observed large hindrance of the M1 transition between the $I^\pi=1/2^+$ and $3/2^+$ states in $^{17}$C can be attributed to shape coexistence near the ground state, where the first $K^\pi=1/2^+$ state has a large oblate deformation while the $K^\pi=3/2^+$ ground state is prolately deformed, a situation not present in $^{21}$Ne.

## Method

Shell model calculations were performed in (p-sd) model space for $^{17}$C and (sd) model space for $^{21}$Ne using three effective interactions (PSDMK2, SFO, and WBP). Deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock+blocked BCS calculations were carried out with the SkO' interaction assuming axial symmetry, using a density-dependent pairing interaction in the BCS approximation, to compute energy surfaces, deformations, magnetic moments, and M1 transition probabilities.

## Key numbers

- Ground state deformation $\beta_2 = 0.366$ for $^{17}$C and $\beta_2 = 0.391$ for $^{21}$Ne (prolate), from deformed HF calculations with SkO'.
- First excited $K^\pi=1/2^+$ state in $^{17}$C has oblate deformation $\beta_2 = -0.270$ at excitation energy $E_x = 0.56$ MeV; in $^{21}$Ne it has prolate deformation $\beta_2 = 0.287$ at $E_x = 2.33$ MeV.
- Experimental B(M1) for $^{17}$C: $0.010 \pm 0.001\ \mu_N^2$ for $1/2^+ \rightarrow 3/2^+$ and $0.082^{+0.032}_{-0.018}\ \mu_N^2$ for $5/2^+ \rightarrow 3/2^+$.
- Experimental B(M1) for $^{21}$Ne: $0.33 \pm 0.05\ \mu_N^2$ for $1/2^+ \rightarrow 3/2^+$ and $0.128 \pm 0.03\ \mu_N^2$ for $5/2^+ \rightarrow 3/2^+$.
- Shell model predictions for $^{17}$C $1/2^+ \rightarrow 3/2^+$ B(M1) range from 0.077 to 0.084 $\mu_N^2$ with bare g-factors, nearly an order of magnitude larger than experiment.
- Core overlap of BCS vacuums between $I=3/2^+_1$ and $I=1/2^+_1$ states in $^{17}$C: $\langle q'(\beta_2=0.366)|q(\beta_2=-0.270)\rangle = 0.0378$; in $^{21}$Ne: $\langle q'(\beta_2=0.391)|q(\beta_2=0.287)\rangle = 0.982$.
- Experimental magnetic moment of $^{17}$C ground state: $|\mu| = 0.758(38)\ \mu_N$; of $^{21}$Ne ground state: $\mu = -0.661797(5)\ \mu_N$.

## Context

This work extends earlier deformed Skyrme HF+BCS calculations by Sagawa (2004) on C and Ne isotopes, focusing specifically on M1 transitions and shape coexistence in N=11 isotones. The shell model results for $^{21}$Ne agree with prior work by Rolfs (1971, 1972) identifying rotational band structure, while the poor shell model performance for $^{17}$C highlights limitations of standard effective interactions for neutron-rich nuclei. The shape coexistence mechanism proposed here provides an alternative to the halo effect suggested by the experimental collaboration (RIKEN, 2005) as an explanation for the hindered M1 transition. The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted second $K^\pi=1/2^+$ state in $^{17}$C near $E_x \sim 1.2$ MeV, which would distinguish between shell model and deformed HF predictions.
