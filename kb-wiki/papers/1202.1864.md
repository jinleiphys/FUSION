# Antisymmetrized molecular dynamics and its applications to cluster phenomena

> Structure and reaction studies with a method of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) were reviewed. Applications of time-independent and time-dependent versions of the AMD were described. In applications of time-independent AMD to nuclear structure studies, structures of neutron-rich nuclei such as Be, C, Ne, and Mg isotopes were described focusing on cluster aspects. Important roles of valence neutrons were discussed. The results suggested a variety of cluster structures appear also in unstable nuclei as well as in stable nuclei. Deformation and cluster phenomena in $Z\sim N$ nuclei in $p$- and $sd$- shell regions were also discussed. Applications of time-dependent AMD contain various topics such as fragmentation in heavy-ion collisions as well as nuclear responses. The AMD calculations successfully describe multifragmentation which is one of the remarkable phenomena in heavy-ion collisions. The approach is able to link reactions and nuclear matter properties. The studies suggested the important balance between single-particle motions and correlations to form clusters and fragments.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD), in both time-independent and time-dependent versions, can describe cluster phenomena across stable and unstable nuclei, but the key hedge is that the method's success depends on combining AMD with the generator coordinate method (GCM) or resonating group method (RGM) to properly handle non-yrast states, halo tails, and the balance between single-particle motions and correlations.

## Method

Time-independent AMD was applied to nuclear structure studies of neutron-rich Be, C, Ne, and Mg isotopes, using AMD+GCM to calculate energy surfaces and spectra for shape coexistence and molecular states. Time-dependent AMD was used to simulate fragmentation in heavy-ion collisions and nuclear responses, linking reactions to nuclear matter properties. For halo nuclei such as 31Ne, AMD was combined with RGM to properly describe the exponential tail of the density distribution by treating the valence neutron coupled to a deformed core.

## Key numbers

- The ground state of 31Mg is an almost pure 2p3h configuration (two neutrons promoted across the N=20 shell gap), with a calculated magnetic moment of -0.91 μN compared to the observed -0.88 μN (Ney05).
- In 31Mg, the 3p4h configuration constitutes a Kπ=3/2- rotational band starting at 720 keV, and the normal 0p1h configuration appears as the 5/2+2 state at 1.6 MeV.
- For 31Ne, the calculated one-neutron separation energy is Sn=0.45 MeV, while the observed value is 0.29±1.64 MeV (Jur07).
- In 31Ne, the p3/2 ⊗ 30Ne(2+) configuration amounts to 41%, larger than the p3/2 ⊗ 30Ne(0+) configuration at 37%.
- In 32S, the superdeformed band has a 16O+16O cluster component of 57%, and the highest excited rotational band with 4ħω excitation of relative motion agrees with observed 16O+16O molecular resonances (Gai81, Mor85).

## Context

This review extends prior AMD work by Kanada-En'yo, Kimura, and Horiuchi (2003, 2010) and the mean-field and cluster model studies of the island of inversion by Warburton (1990) and others. The results enable systematic investigation of shape coexistence, neutron halos with deformed cores, and molecule-like states in neutron-rich nuclei near the drip line, with ongoing analysis of candidates such as 35Mg and 37Mg. The approach also unifies the mean-field and cluster-model descriptions of superdeformed states in the A~40 region, as demonstrated for 32S.
