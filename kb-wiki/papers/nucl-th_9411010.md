# A stopped Delta-Matter Source in Heavy Ion Collisions at 10 GeV/n

> We predict the formation of highly dense baryon-rich resonance matter in Au+Au collisions at AGS energies. The final pion yields show observable signs for resonance matter. The Delta(1232) resonance is predicted to be the dominant source for pions of small transverse momenta. Rescattering effects -- consecutive excitation and deexcitation of Deltas -- lead to a long apparent lifetime (> 10 fm/c) and rather large volumina (several 100 fm^3) of the Delta-matter state. Heavier baryon resonances prove to be crucial for reaction dynamics and particle production at AGS.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the formation of a dense, baryon-rich resonance matter state in central Au+Au collisions at AGS energies (10.7 GeV/nucleon), with the Delta(1232) resonance being the dominant source for pions of low transverse momenta. The hedge is that this prediction is based on the RQMD transport model, and the existence of such a state depends on the specific dynamics of consecutive excitation and deexcitation of Deltas, which extends their apparent lifetime beyond the natural decay width.

## Method

The authors used the Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (RQMD) approach, a microscopic phase-space transport model that treats two-body particle scattering, resonance excitation and decay, and string fragmentation for high-mass excitations. They simulated central Au+Au collisions at 10.7 GeV/nucleon with impact parameter b ≤ 3 fm, tracking the time evolution of baryon resonances, nucleons, and pions, and analyzed the transverse momentum spectra of freeze-out pions to identify contributions from different resonance decays.

## Key numbers

- At midrapidity (|y| ≤ 1) in central Au+Au at 10.7 GeV/n, 189 Deltas are found, constituting 48% of all baryons.
- The maximum baryon density reaches 7 times nuclear matter density (7 ρ₀), while the Delta density reaches 2.5 ρ₀.
- At maximum Delta density, the Delta volume is V_Δ = 500 fm³, approximately one-third of the baryon volume (V_B).
- The average baryon and Delta densities in this volume are 3 ρ₀ and ρ₀, respectively.
- The apparent lifetime of the Delta resonance in the system is τ = 15 fm/c, compared to ≈ 5 fm/c when the πN → Δ formation channel is switched off.
- Pions from heavy resonances dominate the spectrum for m_t - m_0 > 600 MeV, while ρ mesons contribute almost 10% of the total yield and higher resonances contribute only 5%.

## Context

This work extends earlier predictions of resonance matter formation at lower energies (e.g., GSI-SIS energies around 1 GeV/n, as studied by Metag (1993) and Bass (1994)) to the higher AGS energy regime, where the number of resonances exceeds the number of nucleons. The paper builds on the concept of "Delta matter" introduced by Sorge (1991) and uses the RQMD model previously validated for nuclear stopping and particle production from 1 to 200 GeV/n. The results enable experimental verification via low-p_t pion spectra, as proposed by Sorge (1993), and motivate further study of Delta propagation in dense medium as discussed by Ter Haar (1987).
