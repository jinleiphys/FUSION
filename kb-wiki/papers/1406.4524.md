# The two-photon exchange contribution to muonic hydrogen from chiral perturbation theory

> We compute the spin-dependent and spin-independent structure functions of the forward virtual-photon Compton tensor of the proton at one loop using heavy baryon effective theory including the Delta particle. We compare with previous results when existing. Using these results we obtain the leading hadronic contributions, associated to the pion and Delta particles, to the Wilson coefficients of the lepton-proton four fermion operators in NRQED. The spin-independent coefficient yields a pure prediction for the two-photon exchange contribution to the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift, $ΔE_{\rm TPE}(π\&Δ)=34(13)$ $μ$eV. We also compute the charge, $\langle r^n \rangle$, and Zemach, $\langle r^n \rangle_{(2)}$, moments for $n \geq 3$. Finally, we discuss the spin-dependent case, for which we compute the difference between the four-fermion Wilson coefficients relevant for hydrogen and muonic hydrogen.

## Key claim

The authors compute the two-photon exchange contribution to the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory including the Delta particle, obtaining a pure prediction of 34(13) μeV at one-loop order (O(p³)) without the need for new counterterms, though this result depends on the chiral expansion parameters and the inclusion of the Delta as an explicit degree of freedom.

## Method

The authors compute the spin-dependent and spin-independent structure functions of the forward virtual-photon Compton tensor of the proton at O(p³) using heavy baryon effective theory (HBET) with explicit Delta particle degrees of freedom, performing a double expansion in mπ/mρ and Δ/mρ where Δ = MΔ − MN. They then match HBET to non-relativistic QED (NRQED) to determine the Wilson coefficients c₃ and c₄ of the lepton-proton four-fermion operators, from which the two-photon exchange contribution to the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift is extracted. The computation includes one-loop pion diagrams, tree-level Delta contributions, and one-loop diagrams with both pions and Delta particles.

## Key numbers

- The two-photon exchange contribution to the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift: ΔETPE(π&Δ) = 34(13) μeV
- The Delta-nucleon mass splitting Δ = MΔ − MN is used as an expansion parameter with mπ/Δ ∼ 1/2
- The chiral expansion is performed at O(p³) in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory
- The charge moments ⟨rⁿ⟩ and Zemach moments ⟨rⁿ⟩(₂) are computed for n ≥ 3

## Context

This work extends previous chiral perturbation theory calculations of the forward virtual-photon Compton tensor by Nevado and Pineda (2007) for the spin-independent case and by Ji et al. (1999) for the spin-dependent case, incorporating the Delta particle as an explicit degree of freedom following the large Nc ideology of Dashen et al. (1993). The results were used in the recent determination of the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift and proton radius by Peset and Pineda (2014), and the computation aims to reduce the theoretical uncertainty that currently limits the precision of the proton radius extraction from muonic hydrogen measurements at PSI by Pohl et al. (2010) and Antognini et al. (2013).
