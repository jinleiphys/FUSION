# Curvature of the chiral pseudo-critical line in QCD: continuum extrapolated results

> We determine the curvature of the pseudo-critical line of strong interactions by means of numerical simulations at imaginary chemical potentials. We consider $N_f=2+1$ stout improved staggered fermions with physical quark masses and the tree level Symanzik gauge action, and explore four different sets of lattice spacings, corresponding to $N_t = 6,8,10,12$, in order to extrapolate results to the continuum limit. Our final estimate is $κ= 0.0135(20)$.

## Key claim

The curvature of the chiral pseudo-critical line in QCD is determined to be κ = 0.0135(20) after continuum extrapolation, which is significantly larger than previous estimates from Taylor expansion techniques that reported κ ∼ 0.006, but the result depends on the choice of strange quark chemical potential and on whether quartic corrections are included in the fit.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with Nf = 2+1 stout improved staggered fermions at physical quark masses and tree level Symanzik gauge action, using imaginary chemical potentials to circumvent the sign problem. They explored four lattice spacings corresponding to Nt = 6, 8, 10, 12 with aspect ratio Ls/Nt = 4, and located the pseudo-critical temperature by monitoring the inflection point of the renormalized chiral condensate and the peak of the renormalized chiral susceptibility. The curvature was extracted by fitting the dependence of Tc on the imaginary chemical potential to a quadratic function and then extrapolating to the continuum limit assuming corrections proportional to 1/Nt^2.

## Key numbers

- Final continuum extrapolated curvature: κ = 0.0135(20)
- Continuum extrapolated curvatures from individual observables: κcont from renormalized condensate (prescription 1) = 0.0134(13), from renormalized condensate (prescription 2) = 0.0127(14), from renormalized chiral susceptibility = 0.0132(10)
- For the case μs = μl on 32^3 × 8 lattices: κ(μs = μl) = 0.013(3)
- Pseudo-critical temperature at zero chemical potential: Tc(0) ≈ 155 MeV (from multiple lattice sizes and observables, with values ranging from 148.2(3) to 155.6(7) MeV depending on lattice spacing and observable)
- Bare quark mass ratio fixed to ms/ml = 28.15, with pion mass mπ ≈ 135 MeV

## Context

This work extends the preliminary results of Bonati et al. (2015) on Nt = 6,8 lattices by adding Nt = 10,12 simulations to enable continuum extrapolation, and addresses the discrepancy between earlier analytic continuation results (κ ∼ 0.013) and Taylor expansion determinations by Kaczmarek et al. (2011), Endrodi et al. (2011), and Borsanyi et al. (2012) which reported κ ∼ 0.006. The results enable more precise comparison between the chiral crossover line and the chemical freeze-out line from heavy ion collisions, and provide a baseline for searches for a possible critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram.
