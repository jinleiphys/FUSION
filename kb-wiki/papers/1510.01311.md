# Initial conditions from the shadowed Glauber model

> The two component Monte-Carlo Glauber model predicts a knee-like structure in the centrality dependence of elliptic flow $v_2$ in Uranium+Uranium collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=193$ GeV. It also produces a strong anti-correlation between $v_2$ and $dN_{ch}/dy$ in the case of top ZDC events. However, none of these features have been observed in data. We address these discrepancies by including the effect of nucleon shadowing to the two component Monte-Carlo Glauber model. Apart from addressing successfully the above issues, we find that the nucleon shadow suppresses the event by event fluctuation of various quantities, e.g. $\varepsilon_2$ which is in accordance with expectation from the dynamical models of initial condition based on gluon saturation physics.

## Key claim

The standard two-component Monte-Carlo Glauber model (MCGM) fails to describe Uranium+Uranium collision data because it predicts a knee-like structure in the centrality dependence of elliptic flow and a strong anti-correlation between elliptic flow and multiplicity that are not observed; by introducing a phenomenological nucleon shadowing factor that suppresses energy deposition from nucleons seated deep inside the nucleus, the shadowed Monte-Carlo Glauber model (shMCGM) can reproduce the experimental data and also narrows event-by-event fluctuations of eccentricity, bringing it closer to dynamical models based on gluon saturation.

## Method

The authors modify the standard two-component MCGM by assigning each participant nucleon and binary collision a suppression factor S(n,lambda) = exp(-n*lambda), where n is the number of nucleons from the same nucleus that lie ahead of the source along the beam direction and lambda is a free parameter. The energy deposition and charged-particle multiplicity are then computed using the same two-component formula as MCGM but with these shadowed weight factors, with source weights sampled from a negative binomial distribution. The model parameters (n0, k, f, lambda) are fixed by fitting the multiplicity distribution to IP-Glasma results for Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and U+U at sqrt(s_NN)=193 GeV, and the resulting initial eccentricities epsilon_2 are converted to elliptic flow v_2 via the scaling v_2 = kappa*epsilon_2 with kappa=0.2.

## Key numbers

- For shMCGM, the fitted parameters are: n0 = 2.83, k = 1.1, f = 0.32, lambda = 0.12 for both Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and U+U at sqrt(s_NN)=193 GeV (Table I).
- For MCGM, the fitted parameters are: n0 = 2.37 (Au+Au) or 2.30 (U+U), k = 1.1, f = 0.14 (Table I).
- The Gaussian smearing width for energy deposition from each source is sigma = 0.6 fm.
- The scaling factor from eccentricity to elliptic flow is kappa = 0.2.

## Context

This work extends the standard Monte-Carlo Glauber model (MCGM) by incorporating nucleon shadowing, an idea first discussed by Glauber (1955) but rarely applied in heavy-ion collisions. It directly addresses the failure of the two-component MCGM to describe STAR data on U+U collisions (Adamczyk 2015), which had ruled out the simple geometrical model in favor of QCD-based approaches such as the IP-Glasma model (Gale 2012) and the EKRT model (Niemi 2015). The shMCGM successfully reproduces the centrality dependence of v_2 and the v_2-multiplicity correlation in top ZDC events, and its narrower event-by-event eccentricity distributions agree with IP-Glasma predictions, suggesting that geometrical models can be brought closer to dynamical models by including shadowing effects.
