# Effect of Siegert's Theorem on Low-Energy Neutrino-Nucleus Interactions

> We examine the importance of conserving the vector current in calculating low-energy neutrino-nucleus interactions by implicitly invoking Siegert's Theorem in describing the vector transverse electric current. We find that at low neutrino energies (E? <50 MeV), Siegert's Theorem can change neutrino cross sections for normal-parity non-spin-flip excitations by about a factor of two.The same is true of muon capture rates. At higher neutrino energies the effect of Siegert's Theorem diminishes, and by about 100 MeV the effect is very small.

# Effect of Siegert's Theorem on Low-Energy Neutrino-Nucleus Interactions

## Key claim
At neutrino energies below 50 MeV, invoking Siegert's Theorem to enforce conserved vector current (CVC) in the transverse electric operator changes neutrino cross sections for normal-parity non-spin-flip excitations by about a factor of two, but only for multipoles with ΔS=0 and ΔT=1; Gamow-Teller 1+ transitions are unaffected. The effect diminishes by about 100 MeV.

## Method
The authors modified the standard transverse electric transition operator from O'Connell, Donnelly, and Walecka (ODW) by replacing the nucleon convection current term with an operator that manifests Siegert's Theorem, using the current continuity equation to replace ∇·J with -i[H,ρ]. They applied this modified operator to calculate neutrino-nucleus cross sections for a pure giant dipole resonance (GDR) state in 16O using a harmonic oscillator p→sd transition, and performed full 2ħω shell-model calculations for 12C including multipoles 1+–5+ and 0−–4−.

## Key numbers
- For a pure GDR state at 22.3 MeV excitation in 16O, implementing Siegert's Theorem increases the neutral-current neutrino cross section at low energies by a factor of about 2.5.
- For electron neutrinos from pion decay-at-rest (Michel spectrum) on 16O, the GDR cross section is 1.58×10−42 cm² with Siegert's Theorem and 0.97×10−42 cm² without, a 63% increase.
- For the 12C(νe,e−)12N* cross section to excited states (excluding ground-state Gamow-Teller), Siegert's Theorem increases the total cross section by 11% at 30 MeV and by less than 2% at 75 MeV.
- Muon capture rate to the 2+1 state (0.953 MeV) in 12B: 0.167×10−3 s−1 with Siegert's Theorem, 0.441×10−3 s−1 without; experimental value is 0.321×10−3 s−1.
- Muon capture rate to the 1−1 state (2.621 MeV) in 12B: 0.98×10−3 s−1 with Siegert's Theorem, 1.75×10−3 s−1 without; experimental value is 0.47×10−3 s−1.

## Context
This work extends the application of Siegert's Theorem, long used in electromagnetic interactions (Riska and Brown 1970; Friar 1976), to weak interactions by enforcing CVC in neutrino-nucleus scattering. It builds on the formalism of O'Connell, Donnelly, and Walecka (ODW) and the single-nucleon operator tables of Donnelly and Haxton. The results enable more accurate calculations for low-energy neutrino processes relevant to supernova neutrinos, pion decay-at-rest spectra, and muon capture, and show that explicit meson-exchange current models can be partially avoided at low energies through Siegert's Theorem.
