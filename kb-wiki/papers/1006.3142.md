# Comments to the paper "Effect of Coulomb forces on the Position of the Pole in the Scattering Amplitude and on Its Resudue" published in Phys.At.Nuclei, {\bf 73} (2010) 757

> Certain comments on the paper of Yu.V. Orlov, B. F. Irgaziev and L. I. Nikitina published in Phys.At.Nuclei, {\bf 73} (2010) 757 are made.

# Comments to "Effect of Coulomb forces on the Position of the Pole in the Scattering Amplitude and on Its Residue" (Yarmukhamedov, 2010)

## Key claim

The expressions (30) and (31) from Igam1997 connecting the nuclear vertex constant (NVC) with the effective range parameter are correct despite the criticism in Orlov2010, because the approximate equation (25) criticized by Orlov2010 only modifies a term in the denominator that does not depend on the relative momentum, and therefore does not affect the differentiation that yields those expressions. However, the author partially agrees that equation (25) itself was indeed written without proper allowance for Coulomb interaction.

## Method

The author re-examines the derivation of the nuclear vertex constant (NVC) for the virtual decay \(a \to b + c\) with two charged particles, comparing the formalism of Igam1997 with that of Orlov2010, Iw1984, and Mark2009. The analysis shows that the normalization of the Coulomb-nuclear partial amplitude in Igam1997 differs from that in Orlov2010, Iw1984, and Mark2009 by a factor of the Coulomb phase \(e^{2i\sigma_l(k)}\), and that accounting for this factor renormalizes the right-hand sides of expressions (31) and (34) of Igam1997. The author also reports that the connection equation (25) has been generalized for charged particles in Igam2010, and that the effective-range expansion has been extended to terms up to \(k^6\) for analysis of experimental phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- The generalized connection equation from Igam2010 transforms to the original equation (25) of Igam1997 when the charge of particle \(b\) (or \(c\)) tends to zero.
- The extended effective-range expansion up to \(k^6\) was applied to \(\alpha t\)-scattering, reproducing experimental \(p\)-wave phase shifts at energies up to about 5 MeV.
- For resonant states, the binding energy \(\varepsilon_{bc}\) (or \(\varepsilon_{bc}^{NC}\)) should be replaced by \(-E^{(r)} + i\Gamma/2\), where \(E^{(r)}\) is the resonance energy and \(\Gamma\) is the width.

## Context

This comment addresses the critique by Orlov (2010) of the earlier work Igam1997, and defends the correctness of the NVC expressions derived there while acknowledging the need to correct the connection equation (25) for Coulomb effects. The work extends the formalism of Igam1997 and Igam2010 by generalizing the effective-range expansion to higher orders (up to \(k^6\)), and applies it to \(\alpha t\)-scattering data from KB2007. The results enable self-consistent determination of NVCs (or ANCs) and effective-range parameters from scattering data, and are applicable to both bound and resonant states of light nuclei such as \(^7\)Li in the \((\alpha + t)\)-channel.
