# Towards a novel energy density functional for beyond-mean-field calculations with pairing and deformation

> We take an additional step towards the optimization of the novel finite-range pseudopotential at constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov level and implement an optimization procedure within an axial code using harmonic oscillator basis. We perform the optimization using three different numbers of the harmonic oscillator shells. We apply the new parameterizations in the O-Kr part of the nuclear chart and isotopic chain of Sn, and we compare the results with experimental values and those given by a parameterization obtained using a spherical code.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a finite-range pseudopotential energy density functional optimized in an axial harmonic-oscillator basis with 10, 12, or 14 shells yields binding energies that differ only slightly from each other across the O–Kr region, but the parameterization obtained from a spherical coordinate-space code (REG2c.161026) gives systematically less bound light nuclei when evaluated in the axial basis.

## Method

The authors optimized a local next-to-leading-order finite-range pseudopotential (9 free parameters plus a zero-range Skyrme term with fixed parameters) by minimizing a chi-squared penalty function against binding energies and radii of 8 doubly magic and semi-magic nuclei, six pseudo-observables in infinite and polarized nuclear matter, and an isovector density constraint in 208Pb. They used the axial Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov code HFBTEMP with a harmonic oscillator basis, performing separate optimizations with 10, 12, and 14 oscillator shells, and then applied the resulting parameterizations to compute binding energies of even-even nuclei in the O–Kr region and Sn isotopic chain at the axially deformed HFB level.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy residuals (E_Th – E_Exp) for nuclei in the O–Kr region are shown for parameterizations optimized with 10, 12, and 14 harmonic oscillator shells, compared to the REG2c.161026 parameterization computed with 14 shells; differences between the three new parameterizations are described as "small" in this region.
- The zero-range Skyrme term used a fixed strength t_0 = 1000 MeV fm^3 with x_0 = 1.
- The Gaussian regulator for the finite-range pseudopotential used a range parameter a = 1.15 fm.
- The optimization data set included 8 doubly magic and semi-magic nuclei: 40Ca, 48Ca, 56Ni, 78Ni, 100Sn, 120Sn, 132Sn, and 208Pb.

## Context

This work extends the finite-range pseudopotential approach introduced by Dobaczewski et al. (2012) and Bennaceur et al. (2017), where the first parameter adjustment gave promising results but showed large propagated errors in deformed nuclei. The present study quantifies how the harmonic oscillator basis size affects the optimized parameters and binding energies, finding that the basis dependence is largely absorbed into the parameters during optimization. The next step, stated as work in progress, is to include data on deformed nuclei directly in the penalty function to improve predictive power for beyond-mean-field calculations.
