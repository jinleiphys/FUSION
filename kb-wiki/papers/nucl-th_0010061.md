# Interactions, Currents, and the Structure of Few-Nucleon Systems

> Our current understanding of the structure of nuclei with up to A=8, including energy spectra, electromagnetic form factors, and weak transitions, is reviewed within the context of a realistic approach to nuclear dynamics based on two- and three- nucleon interactions and associated electro-weak currents. Low-energy radiative and weak capture reactions of astrophysical relevance involving these light systems are also discussed.

# Interactions, Currents, and the Structure of Few-Nucleon Systems

## Key claim
The "nuclear standard model" based on the AV18 two-nucleon potential plus the Urbana-IX three-nucleon potential (AV18/UIX) predicts low-lying energy spectra of systems with A ≤ 8 reasonably well, but systematically underbinds A=6–8 systems by a few percent and underpredicts spin-orbit splittings, with underbinding becoming more severe as neutron-proton asymmetry increases.

## Method
The Hamiltonian includes kinetic energy with charge-symmetry-breaking components, the Argonne v18 (AV18) two-nucleon potential fit to pp and np scattering data up to ~350 MeV laboratory energy, and the Urbana-IX three-nucleon potential with Fujita-Miyazawa and dispersive terms fit to the triton binding energy and nuclear matter saturation density. Bound-state wave functions are obtained from Green's function Monte Carlo calculations, and continuum states are treated with the transition-correlation-operator (TCO) scheme that includes Δ-isobar degrees of freedom. Electro-weak currents are constructed consistently with the interactions, with model-independent terms derived from current conservation and model-dependent terms from Δ excitation.

## Key numbers
- AV18 and Bonn two-nucleon potentials alone give binding energies of 24.28 MeV and 26.26 MeV respectively for the α particle, compared to the experimental value of 28.3 MeV
- The magnetic dipole-dipole interaction in the deuteron gives 18 keV extra repulsion
- For pd radiative capture at 40 keV, the fitted magnitudes of reduced matrix elements are: |m₂| = 0.340 ± 0.010, |m₄| = 0.157 ± 0.007, |p₂| = 0.363 ± 0.014, |p₄| = 0.312 ± 0.009
- The ratio r_E1 ≡ |p₄/p₂|² from theory is ≈ 1, while from experimental fits it is 0.74 ± 0.04
- The nd capture cross section at thermal neutron energy is calculated to be 578 μb with AV18/UIX, compared to the experimental value of 508 ± 15 μb
- The hep S-factor at zero energy is predicted to be 9.64 × 10⁻²⁰ keV b (full calculation including S- and P-wave channels), approximately 4.5 times larger than the standard solar model value
- The one-body only contribution to the hep S-factor at zero energy is 29.0 × 10⁻²⁰ keV b, while the full calculation gives 9.64 × 10⁻²⁰ keV b
- Calculations using AV18 alone and AV14/UVIII give zero-energy hep S-factors of 12.1 × 10⁻²⁰ keV b and 10.2 × 10⁻²⁰ keV b respectively

## Context
This review extends the program of realistic nuclear dynamics based on two- and three-nucleon potentials and associated currents, building on the AV18 potential of Wiringa, Stoks, and Schiavilla (1995) and the Urbana three-nucleon models of Pudliner et al. (1995). The failures of the AV18/UIX model in binding energies and spin-orbit splittings have motivated the development of new Illinois three-nucleon interaction models by Carlson et al. (2000). The work enables first-principle calculations of solar neutrino reactions, particularly the hep process, and provides constraints on solar neutrino oscillation solutions through precise predictions of the hep neutrino flux.
