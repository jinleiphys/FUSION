# Low-lying isovector monopole resonances

> The mass difference between the even-even isobaric nuclei having the valence nucleons on the same degenerate level is attributed to a Josephson-type interaction between pairs of protons and pairs of neutrons. This interaction can be understood as an isospin symmetry-breaking mean field for a four-particle interaction separable in the two particles-two holes channel. The strength of this mean field is estimated within an o(5) algebraic model, by using the experimental value of the inertial parameter for the collective isorotation induced by the breaking of the isospin symmetry. In superfluid nuclei, the presumed interaction between the proton and neutron condensates leads to coupled oscillations of the BCS gauge angles, which should appear in the excitation spectrum as low-lying isovector monopole resonances.

## Key claim

Low-lying isovector monopole resonances, distinct from the higher-energy isovector monopole resonances observed at approximately 170 A⁻¹/³ MeV, are predicted to appear exclusively in superfluid nuclei due to a phenomenological gauge-restoring interaction between proton and neutron condensates, with the caveat that this interaction is treated as a mean-field approximation for a microscopic number-conserving four-particle Hamiltonian.

## Method

The author constructs an isoscalar four-particle interaction separable in the two particles–two holes channel, defined as \(H_4 = -G_4 \sum_{\mu=-2}^2 Q_{2\mu}^\dagger Q_{2\mu}\), where \(Q_{2\mu}\) is an isospin quadrupole operator built from proton–proton, neutron–neutron, and proton–neutron pair operators within an o(5) algebraic model. For non-superfluid nuclei, a cranking term \(-\omega T_0\) is introduced to restore isospin symmetry, yielding a kinetic energy term interpreted as isorotational symmetry energy; for superfluid nuclei, the gauge-restoring interaction \(-\delta_0 (N_+P_- + N_-P_+)\) is treated semiclassically using time-dependent variational BCS trial functions, leading to coupled equations for the proton and neutron gauge angles and the prediction of small oscillations of the Fermi levels.

## Key numbers

- The excitation energy of the \((0^+;2)\) state in \(^{36}\)Ar is 10.858 MeV, used to estimate the isorotational moment of inertia \(J_g^0\) via \(1/J_g^0 = E_{0^+;2}/2\).
- For the 1d\(_{3/2}\) level in \(^{36}\)Ar, the pairing strength is taken as \(\tilde{G} = 0.6\) MeV, yielding two solutions for the four-particle interaction constant: \(G_4^{(1)} = 2.56\) MeV and \(G_4^{(2)} = 0.08\) MeV; the latter is adopted as it is close to the quadrupling constant \(G_Q = 0.09\) MeV.
- The gauge-restoring mean-field parameter for \(^{36}\)Ar is \(\delta_0 = 0.07\) MeV, and for \(A \ge 28\) it scales as \(\delta_0 = 2.7/A\) MeV.
- The energy of the low-lying isovector monopole vibration for superfluid nuclei with half-filled valence shells is \(\omega_v = 22.8\,A^{-1/3}\) MeV, using \(\tilde{G} = 21/A\) MeV and \(\delta_0 = 2.7/A\) MeV.
- The symmetry energy from the Weizsäcker mass formula is \(k_w = 28.1/A\) MeV; for \(A \ge 28\), the relative difference between \(W_{\text{sym}}(A,4)\) and the experimental \(E_{0^+;2}^A\) decreases to 1.5 % for \(^{52}\)Fe.

## Context

This work extends the o(5) algebraic classification of nuclear states previously applied by Camiz and Catani (1970s) and the quadrupling four-particle interaction studied in earlier work (e.g., the author cites references [9,10] for the quadrupling interaction \(H_Q\)). It contrasts with the standard hydrodynamic model predictions of isovector monopole resonances at \(\sim 170\,A^{-1/3}\) MeV observed in charge-exchange reactions on nuclei from \(^{40}\)Ca to \(^{208}\)Pb (references [3,4,5]). The predicted low-lying IVMR at \(\omega_v = 22.8\,A^{-1/3}\) MeV could be tested in high-resolution charge-exchange or electron scattering experiments on superfluid nuclei near the \(\beta\)-stability line, providing a test of gauge-angle dynamics in superfluid nuclear systems.
