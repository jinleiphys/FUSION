# Bose-Einstein Correlations in a Fluid Dynamical Scenario for Proton-Proton Scattering at 7 TeV

> Using a fluid dynamical scenario for $pp$ scattering at 7 TeV, we compute correlation functions for $π^+π^+$ pairs. Femtoscopic radii are extracted based on three-dimensional parametrizations of the correlation functions. We study the radii as a function of the transverse momenta of the pairs, for different multiplicity classes, corresponding to recent experimental results from ALICE. We find the same decrease of the radii with $k_T$, more and more pronounced with increasing multiplicity, but absent for the lowest multiplicities. In the model we understand this as transition from string expansion (low multiplicity) towards a three-dimensional hydrodynamical expansion (high multiplicity).

## Key claim

Using a fluid dynamical scenario for proton-proton scattering at 7 TeV, the authors reproduce the experimentally observed decrease of femtoscopic radii with increasing transverse momentum for high-multiplicity events, but find that this decrease is absent for the lowest multiplicity class where only string expansion occurs.

## Method

The authors employ a 3+1 dimensional ideal hydrodynamic model with flux-tube (EPOS) initial conditions, a core-corona separation, and a hadronic cascade after hadronization. They compute Bose-Einstein correlation functions for π⁺π⁺ pairs from roughly ten million simulated events, then extract femtoscopic radii Rout, Rside, and Rlong using a three-dimensional Gaussian parametrization of the correlation function.

## Key numbers

- Energy densities exceeding 100 GeV/fm³ are achieved for the highest multiplicity class (mult 8, five times minimum bias).
- Four multiplicity classes are studied: mult 1 (less than minimum bias), mult 4, mult 7, and mult 8 (five times minimum bias).
- For mult 1 events, Rout and Rside are essentially flat as a function of kT, while Rlong shows some kT dependence.
- For mult 4 through mult 8, all three radii show a pronounced decrease with increasing kT, matching the trend seen in ALICE data.

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic approach previously applied to AuAu collisions at 200 GeV (Werner et al., EPOS2) to proton-proton scattering at LHC energies, motivated by the observation of similar kT-dependent femtoscopic radii in ALICE data. The results suggest a transition from longitudinal string expansion at low multiplicities to three-dimensional hydrodynamic expansion at high multiplicities, supporting the possibility of a mini quark-gluon plasma in pp collisions as proposed earlier by several authors (oldhydro1, oldhydro2, oldhydro3, oldhydro4; Werner et al., 2009; Werner et al., ridge). The study enables direct comparison between hydrodynamic predictions and upcoming LHC pp data to test whether collective flow occurs in small systems.
