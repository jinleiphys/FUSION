# Probing New Physics and CP Violation in $ν_τn \to Λ_c τ^- (π^- ν_τ)$ and $\barν_τp \to Λτ^+ (π^+ \barν_τ)$

> We study the processes $ν_τn \to Λ_c τ^- (π^- ν_τ)$ and $\barν_τp \to Λτ^+ (π^+ \barν_τ)$, with particular emphasis on the pion energy and angular distributions, as a possible signal for lepton flavor universality violation, and in general of physics beyond the Standard Model (SM), as well as a sensitive probe of the $τ$ polarization vector. We work within an effective low-energy extension of the SM with all dimension-six four-fermion operators. In this framework, complex Wilson coefficients which encode new physics can generate CP-violating contributions. We propose an observable that provides a genuine CP-odd signal due to its sensitivity to particular transverse components of the $τ$ polarization vector. Namely, we show that the asymmetry in the azimuthal-angle distribution of the pion in the decay $τ^\pm\to π^\pm ν_τ$ constitutes a smoking-gun prediction of such a beyond the SM scenario. We estimate the strength of this effect extrapolating nucleon-hyperon form factors recently obtained from lattice QCD calculations.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the azimuthal-angle asymmetry of the charged pion from tau decay in the processes $\nu_\tau n \to \Lambda_c \tau^- (\pi^- \nu_\tau)$ and $\bar\nu_\tau p \to \Lambda \tau^+ (\pi^+ \bar\nu_\tau)$ constitutes a genuine CP-odd signal that would indicate physics beyond the Standard Model, but only if the Wilson coefficients in the effective field theory framework are complex; the asymmetry is defined as a ratio to reduce dependence on hadronic form factors, which carry large theoretical uncertainties.

## Method

The authors work within an effective low-energy extension of the Standard Model including all dimension-six four-fermion operators for the quark-level transitions $d \to c$ and $u \to s$. They derive expressions for the full cross section and the azimuthal-angle asymmetry of the pion from $\tau^\pm \to \pi^\pm \nu_\tau$ decay, incorporating the $\tau$ polarization vector. The hadronic inputs use nucleon-hyperon form factors extrapolated from lattice QCD calculations by Meinel (2017) for the $\Lambda_c$ case and from Bacchio (2025) and Mintz (2004) for the $\Lambda$ case.

## Key numbers

* The double-differential cross section $d^2\sigma/(dE_\pi\, d\cos\theta_\pi)$ for $\nu_\tau n \to \Lambda_c \tau^- (\pi^- \nu_\tau)$ is evaluated at $E_{\nu_\tau}=10\,\mathrm{GeV}$ within the Standard Model, showing pions predominantly emitted at small angles relative to the incoming neutrino direction ($\cos\theta_\pi \approx 1$).
* The Wilson coefficient $C^V_{dc\tau RL}$ is taken with magnitude $|C^V_{dc\tau RL}| = \mathcal{O}(10^{-1})$, specifically $|C^V_{dc\tau RL}-0.04| \simeq 0.12$, consistent with constraints from Drell-Yan data and leptonic $D$-meson decays from Fuentes-Martin (2020).
* The asymmetry $\Delta\sigma$ is defined as $\sigma(0<\varphi_\pi<\pi) - \sigma(\pi<\varphi_\pi<2\pi)$, where $\varphi_\pi$ is the azimuthal angle of the pion relative to the plane defined by the incoming neutrino and outgoing hyperon.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Hernandez (2025) on inclusive quasi-elastic reactions on nuclear targets by incorporating the tau decay, which provides access to the full tau polarization vector including transverse components. It builds on the effective field theory framework applied to $b \to c \tau \nu$ anomalies by Fajfer (2012) and to $c \to d$ transitions by Fuentes-Martin (2020), and follows the proposal by Kong (2023) to use $\nu_\tau n \to \Lambda_c \tau^-$ as a probe of Wilson coefficients. The results enable future experimental searches for CP violation in neutrino-nucleon scattering at facilities such as DUNE, though the present work omits nuclear effects, which will be addressed in a subsequent study.
