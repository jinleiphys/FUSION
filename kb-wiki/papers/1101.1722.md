# Plausible explanation of the $Δ_{5/2^{+}}(2000)$ puzzle

> From a Faddeev calculation for the $π-(Δρ)_{N_{5/2^{-}}(1675)}$ system we show the plausible existence of three dynamically generated $I(J^{P})=3/2 (5/2^{+})$ baryon states below 2.3 GeV whereas only two resonances, $Δ_{5/2^{+}}(1905)(\ast\ast\ast\ast)$ and $Δ_{5/2^{+}}(2000)(\ast\ast),$ are cataloged in the Particle Data Book Review. Our results give theoretical support to data analyses extracting two distinctive resonances, $Δ_{5/2^{+}}(\sim1740)$ and $Δ_{5/2^{+}}(\sim2200),$ from which the mass of $Δ_{5/2^{+}}(2000)(\ast\ast)$ is estimated. We propose that these two resonances should be cataloged instead of $Δ_{5/2^{+}}(2000).$ This proposal gets further support from the possible assignment of the other baryon states found in the approach in the $I=1/2,3/2$ with $J^{P}=1/2^{+},3/2^{+},5/2^+$ sectors to known baryonic resonances. In particular, $Δ_{1/2^{+}}(1750)(\ast)$ is naturally interpreted as a $πN_{1/2^{-}}(1650)$ bound state.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the PDG-listed $\Delta_{5/2^{+}}(2000)$ ($\ast\ast$) resonance is not a genuine single state but rather an artifact of averaging two distinct resonances, $\Delta_{5/2^{+}}(\sim1740)$ and $\Delta_{5/2^{+}}(\sim2200)$, which the authors claim are dynamically generated from the $\pi-(\Delta\rho)_{N_{5/2^{-}}(1675)}$ system; the existence of these two states plus a third predicted state below 2.3 GeV resolves the "puzzle" of why the nominal 2000 MeV mass does not correspond to any direct experimental analysis.

## Method

The authors performed a Fixed Center Approximation (FCA) to the Faddeev equations for the three-body $\pi$-$\rho$-$\Delta$ system, treating the $N_{5/2^{-}}(1675)$ as a dynamically generated $\rho\Delta$ bound state in the $I=1/2$ sector. They used unitarized chiral amplitudes for the $\Delta\rho$, $\Delta\pi$, and $\rho\pi$ two-body interactions, with the $\Delta\rho$ subtraction constant tuned to reproduce the $N_{5/2^{-}}(1675)$ mass at 1675 MeV, and included finite-width effects for the $\Delta$ (120 MeV) and $\rho$ (150 MeV) mesons. The total $\pi-(\Delta\rho)$ scattering amplitude was then solved to identify poles corresponding to dynamically generated resonances in the $I=3/2$, $J^P=5/2^+$ sector and other sectors.

## Key numbers

- The $\Delta\rho$ bound state for $I_{\Delta\rho}=1/2$ is fixed at $\sqrt{s_{\Delta\rho}}=1675$ MeV, corresponding to the $N_{5/2^{-}}(1675)$ mass.
- The $\Delta\rho$ bound state for $I_{\Delta\rho}=3/2$ is located at $\sqrt{s_{\Delta\rho}}=1887$ MeV, assigned to $\Delta(1900)S_{31}$, $\Delta(1940)D_{33}$, and $\Delta(1930)D_{35}$.
- The $\pi N_{5/2^{-}}(1675)$ threshold is at 1814 MeV, lying between the $3q$ model mass ($\sim1910$ MeV) and the candidate $\Delta_{5/2^{+}}(1740)$.
- Three dynamically generated $I(J^P)=3/2(5/2^+)$ baryon states are found below 2.3 GeV, compared to only two cataloged in the PDG ($\Delta_{5/2^{+}}(1905)$ and $\Delta_{5/2^{+}}(2000)$).
- The data analyses cited extract masses of $\Delta_{5/2^{+}}(\sim1740)$ and $\Delta_{5/2^{+}}(\sim2200)$, with the latter having an uncertainty of $\pm125$ MeV from one analysis (Cut80).
- The subtraction constant for the $\Delta\rho$ loop is $a_{\Delta\rho}=-2.28$ at scale $\mu=800$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Pej09, which dynamically generated $\Delta_{5/2^{-}}(1930)$ and its partners as $\rho\Delta$ bound states, and of Gon07, which suggested the $\pi N_{5/2^{-}}(1675)$ system could generate a $\Delta_{5/2^{+}}$ state. The paper contradicts the PDG assignment of a single $\Delta_{5/2^{+}}(2000)$ resonance and instead supports the existence of two separate states at $\sim1740$ MeV and $\sim2200$ MeV, as previously indicated in analyses by Man92, Vra00, Cut80, and Suz10. The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted third $\Delta_{5/2^{+}}$ state below 2.3 GeV and suggest that the $\Delta_{1/2^{+}}(1750)$ ($\ast$) can be reinterpreted as a $\pi N_{1/2^{-}}(1650)$ bound state.
