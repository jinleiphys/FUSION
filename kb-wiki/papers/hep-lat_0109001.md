# Numerical Portrait of a Relativistic Thin Film BCS Superfluid

> We present results of numerical simulations of the 2+1d Nambu - Jona-Lasinio model with a non-zero baryon chemical potential mu including the effects of a diquark source term. Diquark condensates, susceptibilities and masses are measured as functions of source strength j. The results suggest that diquark condensation does not take place in the high density phase mu>mu_c, but rather that the condensate scales non-analytically with j implying a line of critical points and long range phase coherence. Analogies are drawn with the low temperature phase of the 2d XY model. The spectrum of the spin-1/2 sector is also studied yielding the quasiparticle dispersion relation. There is no evidence for a non-zero gap; rather the results are characteristic of a normal Fermi liquid with Fermi velocity less than that of light. We conclude that the high density phase of the model describes a relativistic gapless thin film BCS superfluid.

## Key claim

The high-density phase (μ > μ_c) of the 2+1d Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model does not exhibit conventional diquark condensation with a non-zero gap, but instead displays critical scaling of the diquark condensate with source strength j, implying a line of critical points and long-range phase coherence analogous to the low-temperature phase of the 2d XY model, characteristic of a relativistic gapless thin film BCS superfluid.

## Method

The authors performed hybrid molecular dynamics simulations of the 2+1d Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model on lattices of sizes 16³, 16²×24, 24³, 32³, and 48³, with coupling g²=2.0 and bare fermion mass m=0.01. They introduced diquark source terms j and anti-diquark source terms j̅ using a Gor'kov basis to enable measurement of diquark condensates, susceptibilities, and masses as functions of source strength j, with j=j̅ chosen real and positive to keep the pfaffian real and positive. Simulations were performed at chemical potentials μ=0.0, 0.2, 0.8, and 0.9, with additional runs at four intermediate values in (0.8, 0.9) for quasiparticle studies.

## Key numbers

- Critical chemical potential μ_c ≈ 0.65 (in lattice units), coinciding with the dynamically generated fermion mass Σ ≈ 0.65 at g²=2.0
- For μ=0.8, the diquark condensate scales as ⟨qq₊⟩ ∝ j^α with α=0.314(2) (χ²/dof=2.3), giving critical exponent δ ≡ 1/α ≈ 3.18
- For μ=0.9, the diquark condensate scales as ⟨qq₊⟩ ∝ j^α with α=0.213(3) (χ²/dof=0.4), giving critical exponent δ ≈ 4.69
- At μ=0.8, baryon density n_B ≈ 0.25 quarks of each isospin per site; at μ=0.9, n_B ≈ 0.4 quarks of each isospin per site
- The 48³ simulation at μ=0.8, j=0.025 required approximately 40 SGI Origin2000 processor days

## Context

This work extends previous numerical studies by Hands, Morrison, and Lucini (2000-2001) that found evidence for enhanced diquark pairing but no unambiguous signal for a condensate ⟨qq⟩≠0 in the 2+1d NJL model. The results contradict mean-field predictions by Berges and Rajagopal (1999) that the diquark gap Δ should be comparable to the constituent quark mass Σ. The observed critical behavior is interpreted in analogy with the 2d XY model as described by Kosterlitz and Thouless (1973), where long-range phase coherence rather than long-range order characterizes superfluidity in thin films. The quasiparticle dispersion relation shows no gap, consistent with a normal Fermi liquid as described by Landau (1956-1957), with Fermi velocity less than the speed of light. This work enables future studies of relativistic superfluidity in two spatial dimensions and suggests that neither long-range order nor a gap are necessary conditions for superfluidity in thin-film systems.
