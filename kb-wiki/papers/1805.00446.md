# Coulomb Potential Is Not a Part of The QCD Potential

> The Coulomb plus linear potential is widely used in QCD. However, in this paper we show that the Coulomb potential of the form $\frac{1}{r}$ is not a part of the QCD potential. This is because the form $\frac{g^2}{r}$ is for abelian theory (not QCD) and the form $\frac{g^2(μ)}{r}$ in QCD at short distance is not of the Coulomb form $\frac{1}{r}$ because $g(μ)$ depends on the mass/length scale $μ$. Similarly at long distance the QCD potential corresponds to the potential in the classical Yang-Mills theory which does not have the Coulomb form $\frac{1}{r}$ because the fundamental color charge of the quark is time dependent in the classical Yang-Mills theory. This is unlike the QED potential which reduces to Coulomb potential at long distance.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the widely used Coulomb-plus-linear potential for heavy quarkonium is fundamentally incorrect because the QCD potential never takes the pure Coulomb form \(1/r\): at short distances the running coupling \(g(\mu)\) makes the potential scale-dependent, and at long distances the classical Yang-Mills potential involves a time-dependent color charge that prevents a static \(1/r\) form.

## Method

The author compares the path integral derivation of the photon propagator in QED (which yields the Coulomb potential for static charges) with the analogous path integral for gluons in QCD, showing that the non-abelian gluon field tensor contains a self-interaction term \(gf^{bhd}Q_\lambda^h Q_\nu^d\) that prevents an exact analytical solution. The paper then examines the tree-level single-gluon exchange potential in perturbative QCD, noting that the coupling \(\alpha_s(Q^2)\) is momentum-dependent, and analyzes the classical Yang-Mills potential for a quark at rest, where the color charge \(q^b(t-r)\) depends on the retarded time and therefore on \(r\).

## Key numbers

* The QED potential between static electron-positron pair is \(V(r) = -e^2/r\) (exact Coulomb form).
* The tree-level single-gluon exchange potential in pQCD is \(V(r) = -C_F \int \frac{d^3Q}{(2\pi)^3} e^{i \vec{Q}\cdot\vec{r}} \frac{\alpha_s(Q^2)}{\vec{Q}^2}\), which does not reduce to \(1/r\) because \(\alpha_s(Q^2)\) depends on momentum transfer \(Q^2\).
* In the limit \(g \rightarrow 0\), the classical Yang-Mills potential for a quark at rest is \(A_0^b(t,r) = q^b(t-r)/r\), where the color charge \(q^b(t-r)\) depends on \(r\), so the potential is not of the Coulomb form \(1/r\).

## Context

This work contradicts the standard assumption that the QCD potential contains a Coulomb term \(1/r\) at short distances, as used in lattice QCD predictions (e.g., the Coulomb-plus-linear potential for static heavy quark-antiquark pairs) and in phenomenological heavy quarkonium models. The paper extends the author's earlier derivations of the classical Yang-Mills potential (Nayak 2004-2007) and argues that the QCD potential at long distance is identical to the classical Yang-Mills potential, which is time-dependent even for quarks at rest. This result challenges the conventional definition of the static QCD potential via the Wilson loop in Euclidean time formalism.
