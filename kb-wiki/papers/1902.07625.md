# Neutron Skin in CsI and Low-Energy Effective Weak Mixing Angle from COHERENT Data

> Both the neutron skin thickness $őĒR_{np}$ of atomic nuclei and the low-energy neutrino-nucleon ($őĹN$) interactions are of fundamental importance in nuclear and particle physics, astrophysics as well as new physics beyond the standard model (SM) but largely uncertain currently, and the coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CE$őĹ$NS) provides a clean way to extract their information. New physics beyond the SM may cause effectively a shift of the SM weak mixing angle $őł_W$ in low-energy $őĹN$ interactions, leading to an effective weak mixing angle $őł^*_W$. By analyzing the CE$őĹ$NS data of the COHERENT experiment, we find that while a one-parameter fit to the COHERENT data by varying $őĒR_{np}$ produces $őĒR^{\rm{CsI}}_{np} \simeq 0.68^{+0.91}_{-1.13}$ fm for CsI with an unrealistically large central value by fixing $\sin^2 őł^*_W$ at the low-energy SM value of $\sin^2őł_W^{\rm{SM}} = 0.23857$, a two-dimensional fit by varying $őĒR_{np}$ and $\sin^2 őł^*_W$ leads to a strong positive correlation between $őĒR_{np}$ and $\sin^2 őł^*_W$ with significantly smaller central values of $őĒR^{\rm {CsI}}_{np} \simeq 0.24_{-2.03}^{+2.30}$ fm and $\sin^2 őł^*_W = 0.21_{-0.10}^{+0.13}$. Although the uncertainty is too large to claim a determination of $őĒR^{\rm{CsI}}_{np}$ and $\sin^2 őł^*_W$, the present study suggests that the multi-dimensional fit is important in future analyses of high-precision CE$őĹ$NS data. The implication of the possible deviation of $\sin^2 őł^*_W$ from $\sin^2őł_W^{\rm{SM}}$ on new physics beyond the SM is also discussed.

## Key claim

While a one-parameter fit to COHERENT data fixing the weak mixing angle at the Standard Model value yields an unrealistically large neutron skin thickness for CsI (0.68 fm), a two-dimensional fit that simultaneously varies the neutron skin and the effective weak mixing angle produces a strong positive correlation between the two parameters and a significantly smaller central value for the neutron skin (0.24 fm), though uncertainties remain too large to claim a determination.

## Method

The authors performed one-parameter and two-dimensional chi-squared fits to the COHERENT CEőĹNS data from a CsI detector, using both symmetrized Fermi and Helm form factors for the nuclear density distributions. In the one-parameter fit, the neutron rms radius was varied while fixing sin¬≤őł*W at the Standard Model value of 0.23857; in the two-dimensional fit, both the neutron rms radius and sin¬≤őł*W were varied simultaneously. The analysis used 12 energy bins (i=4 to 15) from the COHERENT data release, with systematic uncertainties on signal rate (28%) and beam-on background rate (25%) included as nuisance parameters.
