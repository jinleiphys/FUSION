# Neutron Skin in CsI and Low-Energy Effective Weak Mixing Angle from COHERENT Data

> Both the neutron skin thickness $ΔR_{np}$ of atomic nuclei and the low-energy neutrino-nucleon ($νN$) interactions are of fundamental importance in nuclear and particle physics, astrophysics as well as new physics beyond the standard model (SM) but largely uncertain currently, and the coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CE$ν$NS) provides a clean way to extract their information. New physics beyond the SM may cause effectively a shift of the SM weak mixing angle $θ_W$ in low-energy $νN$ interactions, leading to an effective weak mixing angle $θ^*_W$. By analyzing the CE$ν$NS data of the COHERENT experiment, we find that while a one-parameter fit to the COHERENT data by varying $ΔR_{np}$ produces $ΔR^{\rm{CsI}}_{np} \simeq 0.68^{+0.91}_{-1.13}$ fm for CsI with an unrealistically large central value by fixing $\sin^2 θ^*_W$ at the low-energy SM value of $\sin^2θ_W^{\rm{SM}} = 0.23857$, a two-dimensional fit by varying $ΔR_{np}$ and $\sin^2 θ^*_W$ leads to a strong positive correlation between $ΔR_{np}$ and $\sin^2 θ^*_W$ with significantly smaller central values of $ΔR^{\rm {CsI}}_{np} \simeq 0.24_{-2.03}^{+2.30}$ fm and $\sin^2 θ^*_W = 0.21_{-0.10}^{+0.13}$. Although the uncertainty is too large to claim a determination of $ΔR^{\rm{CsI}}_{np}$ and $\sin^2 θ^*_W$, the present study suggests that the multi-dimensional fit is important in future analyses of high-precision CE$ν$NS data. The implication of the possible deviation of $\sin^2 θ^*_W$ from $\sin^2θ_W^{\rm{SM}}$ on new physics beyond the SM is also discussed.

## Key claim

While a one-parameter fit to COHERENT data fixing the weak mixing angle at the Standard Model value yields an unrealistically large neutron skin thickness for CsI (0.68 fm), a two-dimensional fit that simultaneously varies the neutron skin and the effective weak mixing angle produces a strong positive correlation between the two parameters and a significantly smaller central value for the neutron skin (0.24 fm), though uncertainties remain too large to claim a determination.

## Method

The authors performed one-parameter and two-dimensional chi-squared fits to the COHERENT CEνNS data from a CsI detector, using both symmetrized Fermi and Helm form factors for the nuclear density distributions. In the one-parameter fit, the neutron rms radius was varied while fixing sin²θ*W at the Standard Model value of 0.23857; in the two-dimensional fit, both the neutron rms radius and sin²θ*W were varied simultaneously. The analysis used 12 energy bins (i=4 to 15) from the COHERENT data release, with systematic uncertainties on signal rate (28%) and beam-on background rate (25%) included as nuisance parameters.

## Key numbers

- One-parameter fit (sin²θ*W fixed at SM value 0.23857): ΔRⁿᵖCsI ≈ 0.68⁺⁰·⁹¹₋₁.₁₃ fm for CsI, with neutron rms radius Rₙ = 5.46⁺⁰·⁹¹₋₁.₁₃ fm (Helm form factor) or Rₙ = 5.47⁺⁰·⁹¹₋₁.₁₃ fm (SF form factor)
- Two-dimensional fit: ΔRⁿᵖCsI ≈ 0.24₋₂.₀₃⁺²·³⁰ fm and sin²θ*W = 0.21₋₀.₁₀⁺⁰·¹³ at Q ≈ 0.05 GeV, with Rₙ = 5.02₋₂.₀₃⁺²·³⁰ fm (Helm form factor) and χ²ₘᵢₙ = 2.498
- The two-dimensional fit predicts a decrease of approximately 3.2% in total event counts compared to the one-parameter fit
- Variation of neutron diffuseness by ±30% (sₙ from 0.63 to 1.17 fm) changes ΔRⁿᵖCsI central value by ±0.03 fm in the 2D fit
- The best-fit deviation Δ sin²θ*W = -0.02857 from the SM value corresponds to an estimated NSI parameter δ_NSI ≈ 0.008

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Cadeddu et al. (2018), who extracted ΔRⁿᵖCsI ≈ 0.7⁺⁰·⁹₋₁.₁ fm from a one-parameter fit to COHERENT data, by demonstrating that allowing the effective weak mixing angle to vary resolves the unrealistically large neutron skin value. The results are consistent with the PREX measurement of ΔRⁿᵖ²⁰⁸Pb = 0.33⁺⁰·¹⁶₋₀.₁₈ fm (Abrahamyan et al., 2012) and with calibrated nuclear models, while the deviation of sin²θ*W from the SM value could indicate new physics such as nonstandard neutrino interactions, neutrino charge radius effects, or dark parity violation. The work enables future multi-dimensional fits to high-precision CEνNS data that could simultaneously determine neutron skin size, shape (diffuseness), and the low-energy effective weak mixing angle.
