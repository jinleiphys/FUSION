# Global study of separable pairing interaction in covariant density functional theory

> A systematic global investigation of pairing properties based on all available experimental data on pairing indicators has been performed for the first time in the framework of covariant density functional theory. It is based on separable pairing interaction of Ref. [1]. The optimization of the scaling factors of this interaction to experimental data clearly reveals its isospin dependence in neutron subsystem. However, the situation is less certain in proton subsystem since similar accuracy of the description of pairing indicators can be achieved both with isospin-dependent and mass-dependent scaling factors. The differences in the functional dependencies of scaling factors lead to the uncertainties in the prediction of proton and neutron pairing properties which are especially pronounced at high isospin and could have a significant impact on some physical observables. For a given part of nuclear chart the scaling factors for spherical nuclei are smaller than those for deformed ones; this feature exists also in non-relativistic density functional theories. Its origin is traced back to particle-vibration coupling in odd-$A$ nuclei which is missing in all existing global studies of pairing. Although the present investigation is based on the NL5(E) covariant energy density functional (CEDF), its general conclusions are expected to be valid also for other CEDFs built at the Hartree level.

## Key claim

The optimization of scaling factors for the separable pairing interaction within covariant density functional theory reveals a clear isospin dependence in the neutron subsystem, but the proton subsystem allows similar accuracy with either isospin-dependent or mass-dependent scaling factors, leading to uncertainties in pairing property predictions at high isospin.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations using the NL5(E) covariant energy density functional (CEDF) with a separable pairing interaction of finite range. For each even-even nucleus with available experimental pairing indicators, they determined neutron and proton scaling factors by requiring that the theoretical pairing gap Δ_uv equals the experimental Δ^(5) pairing indicator corrected for time-odd mean field effects in odd-A nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The average deviation between calculated Δ^(5) indicators and Δ_uv pairing gaps is approximately 5% for nuclei away from shell closures, requiring only about 2% modification of scaling factors to compensate
- For the best neutron fit f_ν = C_ν |N-Z|^α_ν, the parameters are C_ν = 1.249 and α_ν = -0.0471 with RMSE = 0.0529 (measured+estimated data)
- For the best proton fit f_π = C_π A^α_π, the parameters are C_π = 1.529 and α_π = -0.0672 with RMSE = 0.0549 (measured+estimated data)
- The relative difference between Δ^(4) and Δ^(5) experimental indicators is D_π = 5.50% and D_ν = 2.68% when excluding shell closure nuclei
- The time-odd mean field corrections follow ΔE_ν = 0.73 N^(-0.58) MeV and ΔE_π = 0.37 Z^(-0.54) MeV

## Context

This work extends prior localized studies of pairing in CDFT, such as those by Afanasjev and collaborators (2013, 2014) for spherical nuclei and rotating actinides, by performing the first global optimization of separable pairing interaction across the nuclear chart. The finding that scaling factors for spherical nuclei are smaller than for deformed ones, also observed in non-relativistic Skyrme DFT, is traced to particle-vibration coupling in odd-A nuclei that is missing in all existing global pairing studies. The results enable improved predictions of pairing properties for neutron-rich nuclei and provide a foundation for incorporating beyond-mean-field effects in future global studies.
