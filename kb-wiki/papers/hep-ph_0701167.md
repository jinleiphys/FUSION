# Interpretation of Recent SPS Dilepton Data

> We summarize our current theoretical understanding of in-medium properties of the electromagnetic current correlator in view of recent dimuon data from the NA60 experiment in In(158 AGeV)-In collisions at the CERN-SPS. We discuss the sensitivity of the results to space-time evolution models for the hot and dense partonic and hadronic medium created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and the contributions from different sources to the dilepton-excess spectra.

## Key claim

The NA60 dimuon data in In(158 AGeV)-In collisions favor models predicting a large in-medium broadening of the ρ-meson spectrum with little mass shift over those implementing a dropping ρ mass, but this conclusion depends on a careful description of both the in-medium electromagnetic current correlator and the space-time evolution of the fireball, including non-thermal sources such as primordial Drell-Yan annihilation, non-equilibrated ρ mesons, and ρ decays after thermal freeze-out.

## Method

The authors evaluate dilepton emission from a thermal source using the retarded in-medium electromagnetic current-correlation function, which in the vacuum and for masses below 1 GeV is saturated by vector-meson spectral functions (ρ, ω, φ). The vector-meson propagators are evaluated at finite temperature and density using many-body theory based on an effective hadronic Lagrangian, with parameters adjusted to empirical hadronic decay rates and scattering data. The dilepton spectrum is then integrated over a cylindrical thermal fireball expansion with linear transverse flow, starting in the quark-gluon plasma phase at T₀ = 197 MeV and evolving through a mixed phase to hadronic freeze-out at T ≈ 120 MeV, with non-thermal sources added separately.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature of the fireball for central In(158 AGeV)-In collisions: T₀ = 197 MeV
- Hadro-chemical freeze-out temperature and baryon chemical potential: (T_c, μ_B^c) = (175, 232) MeV
- Thermal freeze-out temperature: T ≈ 120 MeV
- Fireball lifetime: ~7 fm/c
- Transverse acceleration parameter: a_⊥ = 0.085 c²/fm
- In-medium ω meson width at nuclear matter density (ρ₀ = 0.16/fm³): Γ_ω^med ≈ 50 MeV
- In-medium φ meson width at nuclear matter density: about 50 MeV
- Pre-hadron absorption cross section for primordial ρ mesons: σ_ph = 0.4 mb
- Hadronic absorption cross section for primordial ρ mesons: σ_had = 5 mb
- ρ formation time for primordial mesons: 1 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous description of dilepton emission from thermalized strongly interacting matter (van Hees and Rapp, 2006) by refining the treatment of non-thermal sources, particularly replacing thermal emission for ρ decays at freeze-out with a Cooper-Frye prescription. The results are compared to NA60 dimuon data in In(158 AGeV)-In collisions, which have sufficient mass resolution and statistics to discriminate between models of in-medium ρ modification, favoring the broadening scenario over the dropping-mass scenario. The next step is to implement the electromagnetic current correlator within chiral models constrained by Weinberg sum rules to establish a direct connection to chiral-symmetry restoration and the QCD phase structure.
