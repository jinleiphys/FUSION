# Dineutron clusters in $^7$He and $^8$He structure

> The hyper-radial barrier strongly hinders formation of more than three clusters. We investigate how well the dominating cluster components in $^7$He and $^8$He, respectively can be described as $α$+$n$+$^2n$ and $α$+$^2n$+$^2n$, where $^2n$ is the dineutron. Effective interactions compatible with $^5$He and $^6$He are used. We vary the lesser known $n$-$^2n$ and $^2n$-$^2n$ interactions, where very small strengths are required. We provide energies, radii, and partial wave decomposition of all computed, predicted or measured, ground and resonance states. We predict substructures within each of the three-body quantum states. We also calculate the neutron-structure sensitive invariant mass spectrum of the four-nucleon system, after fast removal of the $α$-particle from $^8$He. We show that all available experimental information are fairly well reproduced. Very little room is left for variation of the effective interaction parameters. Thus, the dominating features of the subsequently derived reaction and structure properties are well supported.

## Key claim
The authors investigate whether the dominant cluster components in \(^7\)He and \(^8\)He can be described as \(\alpha+n+^2n\) and \(\alpha+^2n+^2n\) respectively, but the success of this description depends critically on the choice of the poorly known \(n\)-\(^2n\) and \(^2n\)-\(^2n\) interactions, which must be very weak to reproduce experimental data.

## Method
The authors use a three-body hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method with complex scaling for resonance calculations. They construct effective two-body Gaussian interactions for the \(\alpha\)-\(n\), \(\alpha\)-\(^2n\), \(n\)-\(^2n\), and \(^2n\)-\(^2n\) subsystems, fitted to reproduce known properties of \(^5\)He and \(^6\)He. They then compute bound and resonant states of \(^7\)He and \(^8\)He, varying the \(^2n\)-\(^2n\) and \(n\)-\(^2n\) interactions, and compare with experimental energies, radii, and the missing-mass spectrum after \(\alpha\)-removal from \(^8\)He.

## Key numbers
- The experimental four-neutron separation energy of \(^8\)He is 3.11 MeV (Tilley 2004); with zero \(^2n\)-\(^2n\) interaction (Potential I) the calculation gives 2.20 MeV, requiring a three-body force of strength \(S_\mathrm{3b} = -2.48\) MeV (range 4.5 fm) to match experiment.
- The experimental rms radius of \(^8\)He is \(2.53 \pm 0.02\) fm (Wakasa 2022); Potential I gives 2.80 fm without and 2.58 fm with the three-body force.
- Potential II (\(S = -8.3\) MeV, \(b = 1.5\) fm for \(^2n\)-\(^2n\)) reproduces both the 3.11 MeV binding energy and the rms radius (2.54 fm) without any three-body force.
- The computed \(0^+_2\) resonance in \(^8\)He has energy 1.33 MeV (Potential I) and 1.50 MeV (Potential II) above the four-neutron threshold, with widths 0.13 MeV and 0.24 MeV respectively, compared to the experimental value of \(1.43 \pm 0.06\) MeV (Yang 2023).
- The \(2^+_1\) resonance in \(^8\)He is computed at 0.43 MeV with width 0.31 MeV for both Potentials I and II, compared to the experimental \(0.43 \pm 0.06\) MeV and width \(0.89 \pm 0.11\) MeV (Tilley 2004).
- The \(1^-_1\) resonance is computed at 0.33 MeV (width 1.35 MeV) for Potential I and 0.19 MeV (width 1.39 MeV) for Potential II, versus experimental \(1.25 \pm 0.20\) MeV (width \(1.3 \pm 0.5\) MeV).
- The \(3^-_1\) resonance is computed at 0.70 MeV (width 0.23 MeV) for Potential I and 0.01 MeV (width 0.23 MeV) for Potential II, versus experimental \(4.05 \pm 0.04\) MeV (width \(0.1 \pm 0.1\) MeV).

## Context
This work extends the cluster-model description of neutron-rich helium isotopes, building on prior three-body calculations for \(^6\)He (\(\alpha+n+n\)) and \(^8\)Li (\(\alpha+t+^2n\)) by Garrido et al. (2025). It directly addresses the dineutron correlation in \(^8\)He previously studied by Hagino and Sagawa (2008) using a core+\(4n\) model, and by Nakayama et al. (2025) who found 88.3% \(\alpha+^2n+^2n\) configuration in the \(0^+\) ground state. The results enable future tests of the predicted substructures and the \(n\)-\(^2n\) interaction through measurements of \(^7\)He properties and refined missing-mass experiments.
