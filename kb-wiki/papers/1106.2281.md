# Extraction of Hadron Interactions above Inelastic Threshold in Lattice QCD

> We propose a new method to extract hadron interactions above inelastic threshold from the Nambu-Bethe-Salpter amplitude in lattice QCD. We consider the scattering such as $A+B\rightarrow C+D$, where $A,B,C,D$ are names of different 1-particle states. An extension to cases where particle productions occur during scatterings is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new method to extract hadron interactions above inelastic threshold from the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter amplitude in lattice QCD, extending the authors' previous approach that was limited to elastic scattering below threshold. The method works for scatterings such as A+B→C+D and can be generalized to cases with particle production, but the extraction requires the NBS wave functions at two different energy eigenvalues in finite volume to determine the coupled-channel potentials.

## Method

The authors define coupled-channel non-local potentials from the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave functions for two (or more) channels, such as AB and CD, using the coupled-channel Schrödinger equation in finite volume. At the leading order of the velocity (derivative) expansion, the local coupled-channel potentials V_{AB,AB}(r), V_{AB,CD}(r), V_{CD,AB}(r), and V_{CD,CD}(r) are obtained by solving a 2×2 matrix equation using NBS wave functions at two different energies W₁ and W₂. Once these potentials are determined, the coupled-channel Schrödinger equation is solved in infinite volume to extract the scattering phase shifts δ₁(W), δ₂(W), and the mixing angle θ(W) for each partial wave.

## Key numbers

- The method is designed for total energy W satisfying m_A + m_B < m_C + m_D < W, where the inelastic threshold is at m_C + m_D
- For the nucleon potential in quenched QCD, the momentum dependence of the local potential V_k(r) was found to be very small between k ≈ 0 MeV (E_k^{NN} ≈ 0 MeV) and k ≈ 240 MeV (E_k^{NN} ≈ 45 MeV)
- The non-locality of the effective single-channel potential below threshold is exponentially suppressed as exp(-M|r - r'|) where M² = -q² for the closed channel

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD Collaboration's previous method for extracting hadron interactions below inelastic threshold (Ishii 2007, Aoki 2008, Aoki 2009) to the regime where inelastic scatterings become important. The approach provides an alternative to Lüscher's finite volume formula (Lüscher 1990), which gives only two conditions from two energy eigenvalues but is insufficient to determine three observables (two phase shifts and one mixing angle) in coupled-channel scattering. The method has recently been applied to extract potentials in the flavor SU(3) limit (Inoue 2010), where the flavor singlet potential was found to be strongly attractive, suggesting a bound state in that channel.
