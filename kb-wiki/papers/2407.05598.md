# Probing the nature of the anticharmed-strange pentaquark states: mass spectra, decays, and magnetic moments

> Within the framework of the quark delocalization color screening model, a systematic investigation of the anticharmed-strange pentaquark system is performed using the resonance group method. The currently estimations predict three bound states with estimated masses to be 2886 MeV, 3039 MeV, and 3153 MeV, respectively. Additionally, three resonance states are identified in various scattering phase shifts processes. Among them, two resonance states $ΣD$ and $Σ^{\ast}D^{\ast}$ with quantum number $\frac{1}{2}(\frac{1}{2}^{-})$ are detected in channels $ND_{s}^{\ast}$ and $ND$, and $ΣD^{\ast}$ and $ΛD$, with masses and decay widths of ($M_{R}=3053\sim3055$ MeV, $T_{total}=13.0\sim13.4$ MeV) and ($M_{R}=3389\sim3390$ MeV, $T_{total}=10.4$ MeV), respectively. In the $ΛD^{\ast}$ and $ΣD^{\ast}$ channels, a resonance state with quantum number $\frac{1}{2}(\frac{3}{2}^{-})$ is discovered, with its mass and decay width being $3250\sim3252$ MeV and 4.4 MeV, respectively. These predicted pentaquark states have $\bar{c}snnn$ quark compositions, allowing them to be recognized as genuine pentaquark states. To validate these predictions, it is expected that upcoming experiments will further explore the predicted resonance and bound states in these possible decay channels.

## Key claim
Within the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM) using the resonance group method, the authors predict three bound anticharmed-strange pentaquark states with masses 2886 MeV, 3039 MeV, and 3153 MeV, and three additional resonance states, but these predictions depend on the specific model parameters and the assumption that only color singlet-singlet channels need be considered because the color screening confinement effectively describes hidden color octet-octet channels.

## Method
The authors employed the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM) with a Hamiltonian including color-confining, one-gluon exchange, and Goldstone-boson exchange potentials, using the resonance group method (RGM) and generator coordinates method to solve the five-body problem as a two-body problem between a baryon cluster and a meson cluster. They calculated effective potentials for S-wave channels with isospin I=1/2 and I=3/2 and total spin S=1/2, 3/2, and 5/2, then performed single-channel and multi-channel coupling dynamical calculations to identify bound states below thresholds and resonance states from scattering phase shifts.

## Key numbers
- Three bound states predicted with masses 2886 MeV (I=1/2, J^P=1/2^-), 3039 MeV (I=1/2, J^P=3/2^-), and 3153 MeV (I=3/2, J^P=3/2^-)
- Resonance state in ND_s* and ND channels with quantum number 1/2(1/2^-): mass M_R=3053-3055 MeV, total decay width T_total=13.0-13.4 MeV
- Resonance state in Sigma D* and Lambda D channels with quantum number 1/2(1/2^-): mass M_R=3389-3390 MeV, total decay width T_total=10.4 MeV
- Resonance state in Lambda D* and Sigma D* channels with quantum number 1/2(3/2^-): mass M_R=3250-3252 MeV, total decay width 4.4 MeV
- Single-channel binding energies for I=1/2 states: N D_s channel E_B=2 MeV, N D_s* channel E_B=2 MeV, Sigma D channel E_B=-1 MeV (unbound), Sigma* D* channel E_B=-8 MeV (unbound)
- Single-channel binding energy for I=3/2 state: Delta D_s* channel with I=3/2, J^P=1/2^- has E_B=-34 MeV (unbound in single channel)

## Context
This work extends previous studies of open-charm pentaquarks with strangeness (quark content c-bar s n n n) that began with Lipkin (1987), Gignoux (1987), and Karl (1987), who predicted stable pentaquark doublets, and continues the QDCSM approach previously applied to hidden-charm pentaquarks by Huang (2015) and Liu (2022). The predictions contradict earlier results from Fleck (1989), Zouzou (1993), Genovese (1997), and Stancu (1998) who found S=-1 open-charm pentaquarks were probably unbound, and the Fermilab E791 Collaboration (1997, 1999) found no experimental evidence for such states. The results enable experimental searches in decay channels such as ND_s*, ND, Sigma D*, Lambda D, and Lambda D* using upcoming LHCb data from Lambda_b^0 to D+ D- Lambda decays.
