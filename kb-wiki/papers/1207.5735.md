# Functional renormalisation group for few-nucleon systems: SU(4) symmetry and its breaking

> We apply the functional renormalisation group to few-nucleon systems. Our starting point is a local effective action that includes three- and four-nucleon interactions, expressed in terms of nucleon and two-nucleon boson fields. The evolution of the coupling constants in this action is described by a renormalisation group flow. We derive these flow equations both in the limit of exact Wigner SU(4) symmetry and in the realistic case of broken symmetry. In the symmetric limit we find that the renormalisation flow equations decouple, and can be combined into two sets, one of which matches the known results for bosons, and the other result matches the one for fermions with spin degrees only. The equations show universal features in the unitary limit, which is obtained when the two-body scattering length tends to infinity. We calculate the spin-quartet neutron-deuteron scattering length and the deuteron-deuteron scattering lengths in the spin-singlet and quintet channels.

## Key claim

The functional renormalisation group (FRG) flow equations for few-nucleon systems decouple into two sets in the limit of exact Wigner SU(4) symmetry: one set matches the known results for bosons (spatially symmetric channels) and the other matches results for fermions with spin degrees only (mixed spatial symmetry channels), with the symmetric channels exhibiting the Efimov effect and limit-cycle behaviour while the mixed-symmetry channels do not.

## Method

The authors constructed a running effective action (REA) for few-nucleon systems that includes local three- and four-nucleon interactions, expressed in terms of nucleon fields and two-nucleon boson (dimer) fields representing the deuteron (spin-triplet, isoscalar) and its isovector analogue. They derived the FRG flow equations for the coupling constants using a truncation to local terms, with Litim's optimised regulator functions, and solved these ordinary differential equations nonperturbatively both in the SU(4)-symmetric limit and for realistic broken symmetry with unequal neutron-proton and proton-proton scattering lengths.

## Context

This work extends the FRG approach previously applied to bosonic and fermionic few-body systems (Diehl et al., Moroz et al., Schmidt, and Birse et al.) to few-nucleon systems up to four nucleons, laying groundwork for extensions to larger numbers of nucleons and nuclear matter. The use of Wigner's SU(4) supermultiplet symmetry, known to be a good approximate symmetry for light nuclei (Wigner, 1937), allows the authors to connect the FRG treatment to effective field theory approaches (Beane and Bedaque, van Kolck) and to study how symmetry breaking affects three- and four-body observables. The results enable future work linking nucleon scattering in vacuum to pairing in dense nuclear matter.
