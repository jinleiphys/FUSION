# Thermodynamics of Quasi-Particles at Finite Chemical Potential

> We present in this work a generalization of the solution of Gorenstein and Yang to the inconsistency problem of thermodynamics for systems of quasi-particles whose masses depend on both the temperature and the chemical potential. We work out several solutions for an interacting system of quarks and gluons and show that there is only one type of solution that reproduce both perturbative and lattice QCD.

# Thermodynamics of Quasi-Particles at Finite Chemical Potential

## Key claim

The authors generalize the Gorenstein-Yang solution to the thermodynamic inconsistency problem for quasi-particle systems whose masses depend on both temperature and chemical potential, showing that among several possible solutions only one type simultaneously reproduces both perturbative QCD and lattice QCD results. However, this consistency holds only for the full thermodynamic functions and not in a perturbative expansion, with one exception.

## Method

The authors develop a general formalism in the grand canonical ensemble where an extra term B (bag energy) is added to the ideal gas Hamiltonian, with arbitrary constants α, η, γ, and λ governing how B modifies the grand potential, internal energy, entropy, and particle number respectively. They derive differential equations for B from the thermodynamic relations and solve them for different choices of the constants, including the Gorenstein-Yang type (γ=0) with two branches (GY1 and GY2) and the Bannur type (η=λ=0). The asymptotic behavior of each solution is computed using HTL (Hard-Thermal-Loop) quasi-particle masses for gluons and quarks at next-to-leading order, with an effective running coupling g_s inspired by perturbative QCD.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for the QCD phase transition at vanishing chemical potential is around T_c ≃ 190 MeV (citing Cheng 2006).
- The Debye mass is given by m_D^2 = g^2((2N_c+N_f)/6 T^2 + (N_f/2)(μ^2/π^2)), where N_c is the number of colors and N_f the number of flavors.
- The bare quark mass used in numerical calculations is m_0/T = 0.4, following Allton (2005).
- The asymptotic Stefan-Boltzmann pressure is P_SB = (π^2/90)((7N_cN_f+4N_g)/2 T^4 + 15N_cN_f μ^2T^2/π^2 + (15N_cN_f/2) μ^4/π^4), where N_g = N_c^2-1.
- The integrals for B converge in the asymptotic region only if the conditions Λ < 2 and Γ < 2 are satisfied, where Λ = λ/α and Γ = γ/α.

## Context

This work extends the general formulation for quasi-particle thermodynamics at zero chemical potential from Gardim and Steffens (2007) to the case of finite chemical potential, generalizing the earlier solution of Gorenstein and Yang (1995) which only treated temperature-dependent masses. The approach is an alternative to HTL perturbation theory (Braaten, Andersen) and the 2-loop Φ-derivable approximation (Blaizot), and is related to the Polyakov loop enhanced Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model (Ghosh 2007, Roessner 2006). The framework enables consistent quasi-particle descriptions of the quark-gluon plasma equation of state for use in hydrodynamics calculations of heavy ion collisions, where the matter has high temperature and small chemical potential.
