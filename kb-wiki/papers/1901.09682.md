# Single pole dominance in short- and intermediate-range $NN$ interaction

> It is demonstrated that both elastic and inelastic $NN$ scattering at laboratory energies up to 600--800 MeV, at least in some partial waves characterized by a large inelasticity, can be described by a superposition of the conventional long-range one-pion exchange and a specific short-range interaction induced by the $s$-channel dibaryon exchange. For the $^3P_2$, $^1D_2$ and $^3F_3$ partial waves, the pole parameters giving the best fit of the real and imaginary parts of the $NN$ phase shifts are consistent with the parameters of the respective isovector dibaryon resonances found experimentally. In the $^1S_0$ channel, the suggested interaction gives two poles of the $S$-matrix -- the well-known singlet deuteron and an excited dibaryon. On the basis of the results presented, a conclusion is made about the nature of $NN$ interaction and its strong channel dependence.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that elastic and inelastic nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering at laboratory energies up to 600–800 MeV, at least in partial waves with large inelasticity, can be described by a superposition of long-range one-pion exchange and a short-range interaction induced by s-channel dibaryon exchange, with the caveat that the model does not include explicit t-channel NΔ coupling, which is treated as background.

## Method
The authors employed a two-channel model coupling an external NN channel (driven by one-pion exchange potential, OPEP) to an internal channel containing a single "bare" dibaryon state with a complex energy eigenvalue. The internal channel was eliminated to produce an effective energy-dependent NN Hamiltonian with a separable term, and the resulting S-matrix pole condition was solved to obtain the renormalized (dressed) dibaryon mass and width. The energy dependence of the bare dibaryon width was parameterized using a phase-space integral for the dominant πNN decay mode, with orbital angular momenta and a cutoff adjusted per partial wave.

## Key numbers
- For the ³P₂ partial wave: dressed dibaryon mass M_th = 2.23 GeV and width Γ_th = 0.15 GeV, compared to experimental values M_exp = 2.197(8) GeV and Γ_exp = 0.130(21) GeV from the ANKE-COSY Collaboration.
- For the ¹D₂ partial wave: dressed dibaryon mass M_th = 2.18 GeV and width Γ_th = 0.11 GeV, compared to experimental values M_exp ≈ 2.16 GeV and Γ_exp ≈ 0.12 GeV from Meshcher (1955).
- For the ³F₃ partial wave: dressed dibaryon mass M_th = 2.22 GeV and width Γ_th = 0.17 GeV, compared to experimental values M_exp ≈ 2.26 GeV and Γ_exp ≈ 0.2 GeV from Auer (1977).
- For the ¹S₀ channel: the S-matrix has two poles, the singlet deuteron at E = -0.067 MeV on the unphysical sheet, and a broad high-lying resonance with M_th = 2.59 GeV and Γ_th = 0.63 GeV.
- The bare dibaryon parameters used for ³P₂: M_0 = 2.21 GeV, Γ_0 = 0.096 GeV, coupling λ₁ = 0.065 GeV, and scale r₀ = 0.71 fm.
- The bare dibaryon parameters used for ¹D₂: M_0 = 2.168 GeV, Γ_0 = 0.08 GeV, coupling λ₁ = 0.048 GeV, and scale r₀ = 0.82 fm.
- The bare dibaryon parameters used for ³F₃: M_0 = 2.23 GeV, Γ_0 = 0.15 GeV, coupling λ₁ = 0.06 GeV, and scale r₀ = 0.5 fm.
- The bare dibaryon parameters used for ¹S₀: M_0 = 2.364 GeV, Γ_0 = 0.044 GeV, coupling λ₁ = 1.184 GeV, and scale r₀ = 0.51 fm.

## Context
This work extends the dibaryon model for NN and 3N interactions developed by Kukulin et al. (2001, 2010), which previously reproduced real phase shifts up to 500–600 MeV but did not compare pole parameters to experimental dibaryon resonances. The present results contradict the conventional view that dibaryon poles contribute significantly only near resonance energy, showing instead that a single pole combined with OPEP can describe phase shifts from zero energy up to the resonance. The findings support the dibaryon interpretation of NN interaction in channels with large inelasticity and enable future inclusion of explicit NΔ coupling and extension to isoscalar channels.
