# Regularization of singular terms in $N\bar{N}$ potential model

> We suggest a method of singular terms regularization in potential model of $N\bar{N}$ interaction. This method is free from any uncertainties, related to the usual cut-off procedure and based on the fact, that in the presence of sufficiently strong short-range annihilation $N$ and $\bar{N}$ never approach close enough to each other. The effect of mentioned singular terms of OBE potential, modified by annihilation is shown to be repulsive. The obtained results for S- and P-wave scattering lengths are in agreement with existing theoretical models.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the singular attractive terms (e.g., \(1/r^3\)) in the one-boson-exchange (OBE) potential for \(N\bar{N}\) interaction can be regularized by adding an infinitesimal imaginary component, which is physically justified because strong short-range annihilation prevents \(N\) and \(\bar{N}\) from approaching close enough to "fall to the center." The hedge is that this regularization works only if the inelastic (imaginary) part of the potential grows faster than \(1/r^{(s/2+1)}\) near the origin, and the resulting scattering observables are independent of any specific short-range form.

## Method

The authors treat the \(N\bar{N}\) interaction as an optical model combining the real Kohno-Weise OBE potential (without any cut-off) with an imaginary term \(-iA/r^3 \exp(-r/\tau)\) that becomes effectively \(-i\omega/r^3\) in the limit \(\omega \to 0\). They solve the Schrödinger equation analytically for homogeneous inverse-power potentials with complex strength, showing that an infinitesimal imaginary part selects a unique solution (full absorption boundary condition) and eliminates the collapse. They then apply this regularization to the \(^{13}P_0\) state and compute S- and P-wave scattering lengths for all relevant spin, isospin, and total angular momentum channels.

## Key numbers

* Scattering volume for the \(^{13}P_0\) state (\(T=0\)) in the regularized model: \(a_r = -7.67 - i4.87\) fm\(^3\); the Kohno-Weise model with cut-off \(r_c = 1\) fm gives \(a_{KW} = -8.83 - i4.45\) fm\(^3\).
* Parameters of the imaginary potential: \(A = 4.7\) GeV fm\(^2\), \(\tau = 0.4\) fm.
* S-wave scattering lengths (fm) for the regularized model (Reg): \(^{11}S_0\): \(-0.08 - i1.16\); \(^{31}S_0\): \(1.05 - i0.55\); \(^{13}S_1\): \(1.19 - i0.64\); \(^{33}S_1\): \(0.7 - i0.65\).
* P-wave scattering volumes (fm\(^3\)) for the regularized model (Reg): \(^{11}P_1\): \(-3.19 - i0.59\); \(^{31}P_1\): \(0.81 - i0.46\); \(^{13}P_0\): \(-7.67 - i4.74\); \(^{33}P_0\): \(2.46 - i0.15\); \(^{13}P_1\): \(4.75 - i0.15\); \(^{33}P_1\): \(-2.09 - i0.79\); \(^{13}P_2\): \(-0.12 - i0.82\); \(^{33}P_2\): \(-0.14 - i0.39\).
* Nearest S-matrix poles for the \(^{13}P_0\) state: \(k_+ = 44.8 - i54.3\) MeV/c and \(k_- = -58.4 - i73.8\) MeV/c.
* WKB failure distance for the \(^{13}P_0\) state: \(r_0 = 1.4\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the earlier full-absorption boundary-condition approach of Dalkarov and Myhrer (1980s) by providing a mathematically rigorous regularization of the singular OBE terms without any cut-off radius. It directly addresses the collapse problem of attractive singular potentials noted in Landau and Lifshitz (Quantum Mechanics) and the cut-off sensitivity criticized by Carbonell et al. (1990s). The results are compared with the Kohno-Weise (1990) and Dover-Richard (1980s) models, showing good agreement. The paper enables a parameter-free optical model for low-energy \(N\bar{N}\) scattering and predicts that near-threshold resonances (e.g., the P-wave enhancement seen in LEAR experiments) arise from quantum reflection off the potential tail, not from deep quasi-bound states.
