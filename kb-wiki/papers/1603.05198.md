# Universal relations between nongaussian fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions

> We show that universality near a critical end point implies a characteristic relation between third- and fourth-order baryon susceptibilities $χ_3$ and $χ_4$, resulting in a banana-shaped loop when $χ_4$ is plotted as a function of $χ_3$ along a freeze-out line. Including the individual enhancements of $χ_3$ and $χ_4$ near a critical point, these features may be a consistent set of observations supporting the interpretation of baryon fluctuations data as arising from criticality.

## Key claim

Universality near a critical end point (CEP) implies a characteristic banana-shaped loop when the fourth-order baryon susceptibility χ₄ is plotted as a function of the third-order susceptibility χ₃ along a freeze-out line, provided the freeze-out line near the CEP is not too parallel to the μ_B axis.

## Method

The authors use the 3D Ising model, which shares the same universality class as QCD, to compute the non-analytic parts of the magnetization and its derivatives κ₂, κ₃, κ₄ (analogous to baryon susceptibilities χ₂, χ₃, χ₄) as functions of the external magnetic field H at fixed reduced temperature t > 0. They then apply scaling symmetry to obtain the susceptibilities at all t > 0 and map the resulting contours onto a plausible QCD phase diagram. To explore scenarios where the CEP is at T ≤ 0 or at μ_B < 0, they perform calculations in the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with varying coupling strengths and quark masses.

## Key numbers

- In the 3D Ising model at fixed t = 0.2, κ₂ has a smooth peak centered at H = 0 and asymptotes to zero as H → ∞; κ₃ and κ₄ are obtained by derivatives and show peaks at different |H| values.
- For the NJL model with K = 0.65 K₀, the CEP temperature T_CEP ≃ 0; for K = 0.4 K₀, T_CEP becomes negative by extrapolation.
- In the NJL model with K = K₀ (reproducing QCD observables), freeze-out lines yield m₂ values that can exceed 1; for freeze-out lines far from the CEP, both m₁ and m₂ are limited to values ≲ 1.
- In the chiral limit (all quark masses zero), the m₂ versus m₁ plot shows a clockwise behavior, in contrast to the anti-clockwise loop seen with a CEP in the first quadrant.
- Preliminary STAR data for net-proton distributions in 0-5% centrality Au+Au collisions show m₂/m₁ ∼ 4 ≫ 1 at the lowest collision energies.

## Context

This work extends the universal scaling approach to critical fluctuations in QCD, building on prior studies of higher-order baryon susceptibilities near a CEP by Stephanov (2008, 2011) and model calculations by Asakawa (2009), Fu (2010), Skokov (2011), and Chen (2014, 2015). The predicted banana-shaped loop in the m₁-m₂ plane provides a consistency check for interpreting experimental data from the RHIC beam energy scan (Aggarwal 2010, Adamczyk 2013, Luo 2015) as signatures of criticality. The results also connect to the concept of quantum critical points in high-T_c superconductors, where scaling symmetry at finite T is controlled by a critical point at T = 0 masked by a superconducting regime.
