# Far from equilibrium attractors in phase space

> We clarify the connection between attractor solutions known from studies of Bjorken flow in conformal models of relativistic fluid dynamics and the more general description of attractors as submanifolds in phase space. We show how to determine sections of the attractor on slices of constant proper time, and point out that in conformal models one may choose a parametrisation of the extended phase space such that the intersection of the attractor with a slice of constant proper time is the same on all slices. In consequence, it is possibleto project these intersections and one finds that the result reproduces the attractor solution. This projection allows for an expressive picture of hydrodynamisation, but the attractor can be equally well described without taking advantage of this convenience. An immediate implication of these observations is that conformal symmetry does not play a significant role in the emergence of early-time attraction. We illustrate this by describing the hydrodynamic attractor in a simple model where conformal symmetry is broken by the equation of state. Our findings can be applied to studies of hydrodynamic attractors in more general situations, also beyond the context of quark-gluon plasma dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper clarifies that the far-from-equilibrium hydrodynamic attractor, previously studied in conformal models using a simplified decoupled equation for the pressure anisotropy as a function of \(w=\tau T\), is more generally a submanifold in an extended phase space; conformal symmetry is not essential for the emergence of early-time attraction, as demonstrated by a nonconformal model where the attractor sections on constant proper time slices evolve from slice to slice but still govern the dynamics.

## Method

The author analyzes Mueller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) theory under Bjorken flow, first in the conformal case and then in a nonconformal model where the equation of state breaks conformal symmetry via a mass scale \(m\). The attractor is defined by imposing regularity of the pressure anisotropy \(\pa\) at asymptotically small proper time, and its sections on constant proper time slices are computed numerically by evolving a family of solutions with the regular initial condition \(\pa_+\) and a range of initial temperatures. In the hydrodynamic regime, the attractor sections are obtained analytically in parametric form using the asymptotic gradient expansion, with the integration constant \(\Lambda\) serving as the parameter along the curve.

## Key numbers

* In conformal MIS theory, the two regular early-time limits of the pressure anisotropy are \(\pa_\pm = \pm 6 \sqrt{C_\eta / C_\tau}\) (dimensionless), where \(C_\eta\) and \(C_\tau\) are dimensionless transport coefficients.
* In the nonconformal model with equation of state \(p = (a/3) T^4 / (1 + m^2/T^2)\), the mass scale is set to \(m = 200\) MeV and \(a = 8\pi^2/15\) (the high-temperature limit for pure SU(3) Yang-Mills theory).
* The speed of sound in the nonconformal model drops from the conformal value \(c_s^2 = 1/3\) at high temperature to \(c_s^2 = 1/5\) at low temperature.
* At late times in the nonconformal model, the temperature scales as \(T(\tau) \sim \Lambda / (\Lambda\tau)^{1/5}\) and the pressure anisotropy as \(\pa(\tau) \sim 12 C_\eta / (\Lambda\tau)^{4/5}\), where \(\Lambda\) is an integration constant.

## Context

This work extends the concept of hydrodynamic attractors beyond the conformal MIS theory studied by Heller and Spalinski (2015), where the attractor was defined as a solution to a decoupled first-order differential equation for \(\pa(w)\). It builds on the phase-space approach of Heller et al. (2020) and the regularity-condition method of An et al. (2023), showing that the attractor can be described by its sections on constant proper time slices even when conformal symmetry is broken. The findings enable the study of hydrodynamisation in nonconformal systems relevant to quark-gluon plasma dynamics, cold atom gases, and cosmology, without relying on the technical simplifications of conformal models.
