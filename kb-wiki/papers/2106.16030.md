# Inclusive $α$ Production for $^{6}$Li+$^{51}$V System

> Measurement of angular distributions and energy spectra of $α$ and deuterons through breakup, transfer and incomplete fusion processes to dis-entangle their relative contributions and to investigate relative importance of breakup-fusion compared to transfer. Inclusive $α$ production cross-sections have been measured for $^6$Li + $^{51}$V system near Coulomb barrier energies. Theoretical calculations for estimation of various reaction channels contributing to $α$ production have been performed with finite range coupled reaction method using \textsc{FRESCO} code. The cross-sections from non-capture breakup (NCBU) ($α$ + \textit{d}) and 1\textit{n}, 1\textit{p}, and 1\textit{d} transfer channels, compound nuclear decay channel and incomplete fusion (ICF) leading to $α$ production were estimated to get the cumulative production cross-sections. Contributions from breakup, transfer and incomplete fusion channels could reproduce the integral direct $α$ production cross-sections and their angular distributions quite well. The direct $α$ production cross-sections are in agreement with other targets. The $α$ production cross-sections are higher compared to the deuteron production. Kinematic analysis of the energy spectra of $α$ particles and deuterons suggest that $α$ particle spectra is dominated by breakp-fusion and deuteron spectra have contribution of breakup and transfer reactions. A systematic study of direct $α$ production with various targets follow a universal behavior on average but noticeable differences are observed for different targets. A ratio of $α$ and deuteron yields for a wide mass range of targets shows a saturation above barrier and an increasing production of $α$ particles relative to deuteron around Coulomb barrier.

# Inclusive α Production for ⁶Li+⁵¹V System

## Key claim

The direct α production cross-sections for the ⁶Li+⁵¹V system follow a universal behavior on average when compared with other targets, but noticeable differences are observed for different targets, indicating that nuclear structure effects influence the various transfer channels contributing to α production. The kinematic analysis suggests that breakup-fusion is the dominant process for ⁶Li projectile compared to transfer, contrary to findings for ⁷Li where transfer was reported to be dominant.

## Method

Inclusive α and deuteron angular distributions and energy spectra were measured for the ⁶Li+⁵¹V system at beam energies of 13.6, 19.7, 22.7, and 25.7 MeV using silicon surface barrier telescope detectors in ΔE+E arrangement. Compound nuclear contributions were calculated with the statistical model code PACE and subtracted from total α yields to obtain direct α production cross-sections. Theoretical calculations for non-capture breakup (NCBU) were performed using the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) method, and 1n, 1p, and 1d transfer channels were calculated with the finite range coupled reaction method using the FRESCO code; incomplete fusion (ICF) α contributions were estimated from fusion cross-sections.

## Key numbers

- Direct α production cross-section at 25.7 MeV: 510 ± 46 mb
- Direct α production cross-section at 22.7 MeV: 490 ± 68 mb
- Direct α production cross-section at 19.7 MeV: 403 ± 11 mb
- Direct α production cross-section at 13.6 MeV: 239 ± 8 mb
- Total deuteron production cross-section at 25.7 MeV: 136 ± 27 mb
- Total deuteron production cross-section at 13.6 MeV: 36 ± 7 mb
- Non-capture breakup (NCBU) α cross-section at 25.7 MeV: 62 mb (≈10% of direct α)
- Calculated ICF α cross-section at 25.7 MeV: 277 mb
- Calculated 1n transfer cross-section at 25.7 MeV: 43 mb
- Calculated 1d transfer cross-section at 25.7 MeV: 61 mb
- Calculated 1p transfer cross-section at 25.7 MeV: 16 mb
- Total calculated direct α cross-section at 25.7 MeV: 459 mb (compared to experimental 510 ± 46 mb)
- Complete fusion cross-section from PACE at 25.7 MeV: 1056 mb
- Complete fusion cross-section from PACE at 13.6 MeV: 279 mb

## Context

This work extends systematic studies of inclusive α production for ⁶Li projectiles on targets including ⁵⁸Ni, ¹¹⁸Sn, ¹²⁰Sn (Pfeiffer 1973), ⁵⁹Co (Souza 2009), ⁶⁵Cu (Shrivastava 2006), ⁹⁰Zr (Kumawat 2010), ¹⁵⁹Tb (PhysRevC.88.064603), ²⁰⁸Pb (Signorini; PhysRevC.67.044607), and ²⁰⁹Bi (PhysRevC.85.014612). The finding that breakup-fusion dominates over transfer for ⁶Li contrasts with results for ⁷Li+⁹³Nb (Pandit 2021) where transfer was dominant, possibly due to the smaller binding energy of ⁶Li. The ratio of α to deuteron yields for a wide mass range of targets shows saturation above the Coulomb barrier and an increasing production of α particles relative to deuterons around the barrier. The work enables future particle-γ coincidence experiments to further disentangle the reaction mechanisms.
