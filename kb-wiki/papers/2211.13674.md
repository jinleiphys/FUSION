# Jet radius and momentum splitting fraction with dynamical grooming in heavy-ion collisions

> We investigate the medium modifications of momentum splitting fraction and groomed jet radius with both dynamical grooming and soft drop algorithms in heavy-ion collisions. In the calculation, the partonic spectrum of initial hard scattering in p+p collisions is provided by the event generator PYTHIA 8, and the energy loss of fast parton traversing in a hot/dense QCD medium is simulated with the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model. We predict the normalized distributions of the groomed jet radius $θ_g$ and momentum splitting fraction $z_g$ with the dynamical grooming algorithm in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}$ = 5.02 TeV, then compare these quantities in dynamical grooming at $a=0.1$, with that in soft drop at $z_{\mathrm{cut}} = 0.1$ and $β= 0$. It is found that the normalized distribution ratios Pb+Pb/p+p with respect to $z_g$ in $z_{\mathrm{cut}} = 0.1$, $β= 0$ soft drop case are close to unity and those in $a=0.1$ dynamical grooming case show enhancement at small $z_g$, and Pb+Pb/p+p with respect to $θ_g$ in the dynamical grooming case demonstrate weaker modification than those in the soft drop counterparts. We further calculate the groomed jet number averaged momentum splitting fraction $\rm \langle z_g \rangle_{jets}$ and averaged groomed jet radius $\rm \langle θ_g \rangle_{jets}$ in p+p and A+A for both grooming cases in three $p^{\rm ch, jet}_{\rm T}$ intervals, and find that the originally generated well balanced groomed jets will become more momentum imbalanced and less jet size narrowing due to jet quenching, and weaker medium modification of $z_g$ and $θ_g$ in $a =0.1$ dynamical grooming case than in the soft drop counterparts.

# Jet radius and momentum splitting fraction with dynamical grooming in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The authors predict that in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, the normalized distribution ratios Pb+Pb/p+p for the momentum splitting fraction $z_g$ show enhancement at small $z_g$ for the dynamical grooming algorithm with $a=0.1$, while the corresponding ratios for the soft drop algorithm with $z_{\mathrm{cut}}=0.1$, $\beta=0$ remain close to unity; however, the medium modification of the groomed jet radius $\theta_g$ is weaker in the dynamical grooming case than in the soft drop case.

## Method

The partonic spectrum for p+p collisions is generated with PYTHIA 8, and the energy loss of fast partons traversing the quark-gluon plasma is simulated using the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model, which includes both elastic scattering via the linear Boltzmann equation and inelastic scattering via the higher twist formalism for induced gluon radiation. The bulk medium evolution is described by the 3+1D CLVisc hydrodynamical model with initial conditions from the AMPT model. Jets are reconstructed with the anti-$k_T$ algorithm using jet radius $R=0.4$ for p+p baseline comparisons and $R=0.2$ for the medium modification studies, and the groomed jet radius $\theta_g$ and momentum splitting fraction $z_g$ are computed for both the dynamical grooming algorithm (maximizing $z_i(1-z_i)p_{T,i}\theta_i^a$) and the soft drop algorithm (condition $z_g > z_{\mathrm{cut}} \theta_g^\beta$).

## Key numbers

- Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 5.02$ TeV, centrality 0-30%, for inclusive charged jets.
- For $a=0.1$ dynamical grooming: $\langle z_g \rangle_{\mathrm{jets}}$ in p+p ranges from 0.322 (40-60 GeV) to 0.317 (80-100 GeV); in Pb+Pb from 0.312 to 0.304.
- For $z_{\mathrm{cut}}=0.1$, $\beta=0$ soft drop: $\langle z_g \rangle_{\mathrm{jets}}$ in p+p ranges from 0.232 (40-60 GeV) to 0.217 (80-100 GeV); in Pb+Pb from 0.210 to 0.196.
- For $a=0.1$ dynamical grooming: $\langle \theta_g \rangle_{\mathrm{jets}}$ in p+p ranges from 0.478 (40-60 GeV) to 0.333 (80-100 GeV); in Pb+Pb from 0.443 to 0.310.
- For $z_{\mathrm{cut}}=0.1$, $\beta=0$ soft drop: $\langle \theta_g \rangle_{\mathrm{jets}}$ in p+p ranges from 0.582 (40-60 GeV) to 0.424 (80-100 GeV); in Pb+Pb from 0.539 to 0.383.
- The difference $\langle z_g \rangle^{\mathrm{pp}}_{\mathrm{jets}} - \langle z_g \rangle^{\mathrm{AA}}_{\mathrm{jets}} \approx 0.01$ for $a=0.1$ dynamical grooming and $\approx 0.02$ for soft drop across all three $p_{\mathrm{T}}^{\mathrm{ch,jet}}$ intervals.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of dynamical grooming and soft drop grooming algorithms from p+p collisions, where ALICE (2022) measured $z_g$ and $\theta_g$ at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV, to Pb+Pb collisions. It provides predictions for the medium modification of these jet substructure observables using the LBT model, which has previously described jet quenching measurements such as boson-tagged jet/hadron production (Luo 2018, Zhang 2021) and single inclusive jet suppression (He 2015, Cao 2016). The results enable direct comparison with future experimental measurements from ALICE and ATLAS, and test whether the dynamical grooming algorithm, which is less sensitive to fine-tuning of parameters than soft drop, reveals different patterns of jet modification in the quark-gluon plasma.
