# Exploring bound states and interactions of the nucleon-antinucleon system in a constituent quark model

> In this work, we study the nucleon-antinucleon system in a constituent quark model. We first construct the nucleon-antinucleon wave function such that the multiquark and multiantiquark components each satisfy the Pauli exclusion principle, and then investigate the possibility of a bound state using a Hamiltonian that includes color-color, spin-dependent interactions and three-quark potentials. Our results indicate that, in specific channels, the nucleon-antinucleon interaction exhibits significant attraction, suggesting a strong possibility of a bound state.

## Key claim

The nucleon-antinucleon system, treated as a compact six-quark state (three quarks and three antiquarks) in a constituent quark model, shows significant attraction in specific channels, suggesting a strong possibility of a bound state when the total spin is zero, though the authors hedge that the three-quark potential is repulsive and the bound state conclusion depends on the dominance of the octet-octet color component.

## Method

The authors constructed a fully antisymmetrized six-quark wave function for the nucleon-antinucleon system using Young-Yamanouchi basis states, ensuring the Pauli exclusion principle is satisfied separately for the three-quark and three-antiquark components. They employed a Hamiltonian containing two-body color-color and color-spin interactions plus three-quark potentials, with parameters fitted to the N, Delta, Lambda, Sigma, and Sigma* baryon spectra, and used a variational method with Gaussian spatial wave functions to compute the total energy as a function of the nucleon-antinucleon separation.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon mass is fitted to 939.2 MeV (experimental value 938.27 MeV); the Delta mass is fitted to 1231.4 MeV (experimental 1232 MeV).
- For the (I=0, S=0) channel, the ground state mass is 1828.88 MeV, with expectation values: color-color interaction -1356.65 MeV, color-spin interaction -1177.46 MeV, and three-quark potentials (L^{C-C}) 138.27 MeV, (L^{S-S}) -16.81 MeV, (L^{C-S}) -2.29 MeV.
- For the (I=0, S=1) channel, the ground state mass is 2099.9 MeV, with expectation values: color-color interaction -1236.33 MeV, color-spin interaction -319.24 MeV, and three-quark potentials (L^{C-C}) -46.39 MeV, (L^{S-S}) -4.58 MeV, (L^{C-S}) 73.32 MeV.
- The electric potential between a proton and antiproton at a separation of 0.4 fm is approximately -3.6 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the nucleon-antinucleon system that used potential models and effective field theories (Datta 2003, Chang 2004, Yan 2004, Wang 2006, Kang 2013) by explicitly constructing the full multiquark wave function with Pauli principle constraints, going beyond molecular-state treatments. The results are motivated by experimental observations from BESIII of threshold enhancement in p-pbar from J/psi decay (BES 2003) and the X(1835) and X(1840)/X(1880) resonant structures (BES 2005, BESIII 2023). The finding that the octet-octet color component dominates at short range for S=0 suggests a compact bound state mechanism distinct from the G-parity-driven attraction discussed by Klempt (2002), and the authors note that future work should explore the equivalent three-meson basis to connect with asymptotic pion configurations.
