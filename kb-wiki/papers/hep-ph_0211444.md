# Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering on Spin-1 Nuclei

> We consider the Generalized Parton Distributions for spin-1 nuclei in general and on the deuteron in particular. We use the impulse approximation to obtain a convolution model for them. Sum rules are used to check the validity of the approach and to estimate the importance of higher Fock-space states in the deuteron. Numerical predictions for the Beam Spin Asymmetry in deeply virtual Compton scattering are presented.

## Key claim

The impulse approximation, which models deuteron generalized parton distributions (GPDs) as a convolution of the deuteron wave function with the isoscalar combination of nucleon GPDs while neglecting higher Fock-space states, yields a beam spin asymmetry that is sizable and should be measurable, but the sum-rule check shows a residual variation with ξ that quantifies the missing physical ingredients.

## Method

The authors model deuteron GPDs using the impulse approximation, where the virtual photon interacts with a single nucleon inside the deuteron while the other nucleon acts as a spectator. They compute the helicity-independent vector GPDs for the deuteron by convolving the deuteron wave function with the isoscalar combination of nucleon GPDs (H^IS and E^IS). The validity of the model is checked by verifying the ξ-independence of the first moments of the GPDs (sum rules relating integrated GPDs to form factors), and numerical predictions for the beam spin asymmetry in deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) are presented for kinematics relevant to JLab and HERMES.

## Key numbers

- The sum-rule check is performed at fixed momentum transfer t = -0.5 GeV², showing the ξ-dependence of the integrated quantities I_i(ξ) ≡ ∫_{-1}^{1} H_i(x, ξ, t = -0.5 GeV²).
- Beam spin asymmetry predictions are given for two kinematic settings: (left) x_Bj = 0.2, Q² = 2 GeV², electron beam energy E_e = 6 GeV; (right) x_Bj = 0.1, Q² = 4 GeV², positron beam energy E_{e+} = 27 GeV; in both cases t is fixed to -0.3 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the formalism of generalized parton distributions, previously applied to nucleons and studied experimentally by the HERMES and Jefferson Lab collaborations (HermesCEBAF), to the deuteron as a spin-1 target. It builds on the theoretical framework for deuteron GPDs established by Berger et al. (2001) and on earlier work by Kirchner et al. (2002) on hard exclusive processes off nuclei. The results enable future experimental tests of the impulse approximation for the deuteron at JLab and HERMES, and the sum-rule deviations provide a quantitative estimate of the importance of higher Fock-space components (e.g., ΔΔ or hidden-color states) that are neglected in the model.
