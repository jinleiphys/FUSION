# Which Neutron Stars Reach the Stiffening Regime? Multimessenger Constraints on Core Sound Speed and Stellar-Mass Thresholds

> We present a concise multimessenger inference of the neutron-star core sound-speed profile using GW170817 and three \textit{NICER} mass--radius posteriors (PSR J0030$+$0451, PSR J0740$+$6620, and PSR J0437$-$4715). The main result is not only a preference for intermediate-density stiffening within smooth equation-of-state families, but a translation of that inference into the stellar masses that access the relevant density regime. In the baseline smooth peaked family, the posterior probability that $c_s^2 > 1/3$ at $3.5\,n_{\rm sat}$ is $85.4\,\%$, while equal-prior averaging over peaked, monotonic, piecewise, and transition-capable families gives a more conservative $79.0\,\%$. Posterior-resampled exact Tolman--Oppenheimer--Volkoff solutions show that the onset density of the inferred stiffening is typically reached near $1.6\,M_\odot$, whereas the peak region is accessed only near $2.1\,M_\odot$. A J0740-like $2.07\,M_\odot$ pulsar reaches the onset in $91\,\%$ of posterior draws but the peak in only $46\,\%$, showing that current data mainly constrain whether massive stars have entered the stiffening regime rather than traversed its full peak.

# Which Neutron Stars Reach the Stiffening Regime? Multimessenger Constraints on Core Sound Speed and Stellar-Mass Thresholds

## Key claim

Current multimessenger data favor intermediate-density stiffening (supra-conformal sound speed) in neutron-star cores, but the strength of this conclusion is model-dependent: the posterior probability that the squared sound speed exceeds 1/3 at 3.5 times nuclear saturation density is 85.4% in the baseline smooth peaked equation-of-state family, but drops to 79.0% when averaged over peaked, monotonic, piecewise, and transition-capable families. The key astrophysical translation is that the onset of stiffening is typically reached near 1.6 solar masses, while the peak region is accessed only near 2.1 solar masses, meaning current data mainly constrain whether massive stars have entered the stiffening regime rather than traversed its full peak.

## Method

The authors performed a multimessenger Bayesian inference combining the tidal information from GW170817 with NICER mass-radius posteriors for PSR J0030+0451, PSR J0740+6620, and PSR J0437-4715, together with the requirement that the equation of state support radio pulsars above 2 solar masses. They used a smooth five-parameter sound-speed profile as the baseline inference scaffold, constructed thermodynamically consistent equations of state from each sampled profile, solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff and tidal equations, and evaluated the multimessenger likelihood using kernel-density estimates from public posterior samples. The production run used 100,000 prior proposals with an effective sample size of 58,000, and results were validated by recomputing exact TOV solutions for the highest-weight posterior samples and by comparing across four different equation-of-state families (smooth peaked, monotonic, piecewise, and transition-capable).

## Key numbers

- Posterior probability that squared sound speed exceeds 1/3 at 3.5 times nuclear saturation density: 85.4% in the baseline smooth peaked family; 79.0% when averaged over four equation-of-state families
- Onset density of stiffening: 2.43 (+1.52, -1.00) times nuclear saturation density, corresponding to a threshold mass of 1.59 solar masses (90% credible interval: 0.72 to 2.14 solar masses)
- Peak-density proxy mass: 2.08 solar masses (90% credible interval: 1.47 to 2.46 solar masses)
- A 2.07-solar-mass pulsar (J0740-like) reaches the onset density in 91% of posterior draws but the peak in only 46% of draws
- A canonical 1.4-solar-mass star reaches the onset in only 34% of draws and the peak in just 2% of draws
- Posterior median chirp-mass-weighted tidal deformability: 205 (+38, -25)
- Inferred radius at 1.4 solar masses: 12.1 (+1.1, -1.3) kilometers

## Context

This work extends the multimessenger equation-of-state inference framework developed by Annala (2018, 2023) and Brandes (2023), who argued that neutron-star cores may exceed the conformal sound-speed value at intermediate densities, by translating density-space constraints into stellar-mass thresholds that are directly testable with observations. The key advance over prior analyses is the identification of threshold masses (1.6 solar masses for onset, 2.1 solar masses for peak) that specify which observed neutron stars actually probe the stiffening regime, enabling a targeted observational program: future NICER-like observations and next-generation tidal measurements of stars between 1.8 and 2.2 solar masses can directly test whether the current posterior picture is correct, with the transition-capable family (which allows strong intermediate-density softening) identified as the main loophole that weakens the supra-conformal inference.
