# QCD Phase Transitions in the 1/Nc Expansion

> We study the QCD phase diagram at nonzero baryon and isospin chemical potentials using the 1/Nc expansion. We find that there are two phase transitions between the hadronic phase and the quark gluon plasma phase. We discuss the consequences of this result for the universality class of the critical endpoint at nonzero baryon and zero isospin chemical potential.

## Key claim

Using the 1/Nc expansion, the paper finds that for QCD with two flavors at nonzero baryon and isospin chemical potentials, there are generally two distinct chiral phase transitions between the hadronic phase and the quark-gluon plasma phase, rather than one; this result is conditional on the assumption that the critical temperatures for deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration coincide at small chemical potentials, as suggested by lattice simulations.

## Method

The author performs a 1/Nc expansion of the QCD pressure and chiral susceptibilities, deriving expressions for the critical temperatures of deconfinement (from the specific heat) and chiral symmetry restoration (from the chiral susceptibilities) as functions of the quark chemical potentials. By imposing that these critical temperatures coincide for the special case μu = ±μd (as observed in lattice simulations), constraints are derived on the expansion coefficients, leading to the prediction that for μu² ≠ μd² the chiral critical temperatures for up and down quarks differ at order 1/Nc. The generic phase diagrams are then constructed from these results and compared with a modified Random Matrix model.

## Key numbers

- The reduced critical temperature for deconfinement is given by t_c = (1/N_c) Σ_f f(μ_f²) + O(1/N_c²), where t_c = (T_c - T_0)/T_0 and T_0 is the critical temperature at zero chemical potentials.
- The reduced critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration for flavor f is t_f = τ_0(μ_f²) + (1/N_c)[ N_f τ_1(μ_f²) + Σ_g (τ_2(μ_g²) + τ_3(μ_f μ_g)) + τ_4(μ_f²) ] + O(1/N_c²).
- The difference between chiral critical temperatures for two flavors is t_f - t_g = (1/N_c)[ N_f(τ_1(μ_f²)-τ_1(μ_g²)) + Σ_h (τ_3(μ_f μ_h)-τ_3(μ_g μ_h)) ] + O(1/N_c²).
- The separation between the two chiral critical temperatures is proportional to μ_B μ_I / N_c.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous 1/Nc analysis of the critical temperature at zero chemical potential (Toublan 2005) to the case of nonzero baryon and isospin chemical potentials. It confirms predictions from Random Matrix models (Halasz et al. 1998), NJL models (Toublan et al. 2001), and ladder QCD studies (Klein et al. 2003) that the phase diagram is qualitatively altered when μu ≠ μd. The results enable future lattice tests using large-Nc lattice methods (Neuberger 2004) and suggest that the critical endpoint at nonzero baryon and zero isospin chemical potential belongs to the Z₂ × Z₂ Ising universality class, which has observable consequences for heavy ion collision experiments.
