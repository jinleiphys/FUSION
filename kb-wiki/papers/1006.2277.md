# The r-Process in Black Hole Winds

> All the current r-process scenarios relevant to core-collapse supernovae are facing severe difficulties. In particular, recent core-collapse simulations with neutrino transport show no sign of a neutron-rich wind from the proto-neutron star. In this paper, we discuss nucleosynthesis of the r-process in an alternative astrophysical site, "black hole winds", which are the neutrino-driven outflow from the accretion torus around a black hole. This condition is assumed to be realized in double neutron star mergers, neutron star - black hole mergers, or hypernovae.

## Key claim

Black hole winds from accretion tori around black holes (in double neutron star mergers, neutron star-black hole mergers, or hypernovae) can produce the full range of r-process nuclei, but only if the wind material is sufficiently neutron-rich (initial electron fraction Y_e0 < 0.20), a condition that favors mergers over collapsars.

## Method

The authors apply a semi-analytic, spherically symmetric, general relativistic wind model (previously developed for proto-neutron star winds) to the black hole accretion torus geometry, replacing the torus with an equivalent spherical neutrino surface between 2 and 5 Schwarzschild radii. They compute nucleosynthetic yields using a nuclear reaction network of 6300 species from protons to Z=110 isotopes, for five initial electron fractions (Y_e0 = 0.10, 0.15, 0.20, 0.25, 0.30), and mass-average the yields over the entire wind region.

## Key numbers

- Central black hole mass: M_BH = 4 M_sun, with Schwarzschild radius R_S = 11.8 km
- Accretion torus (neutrino surface) lies between 2 R_S (= 23.6 km) and 5 R_S (= 35.4 km) from the center
- Neutrino luminosity L_nu increases linearly from 10^51 erg/s to 10^53 erg/s between 2 R_S and 3 R_S, then constant outward
- Inner winds achieve asymptotic entropies up to ~800 k_B per nucleon (at 0.5 MeV) and expansion timescales down to ~1 ms
- Neutron-to-seed ratios exceed 100 (required for 3rd r-process peak formation) in innermost winds for all Y_e0 cases
- Y_e0 = 0.25 and 0.30 cases contribute only up to the 2nd r-process peak (A = 130) due to very small mass ejection rate in inner winds
- Y_e0 < 0.20 required to account for 3rd r-process peak formation (A = 195)

## Context

This work addresses the severe difficulties facing core-collapse supernova r-process scenarios, where recent simulations (Fischer 2009, Hudepohl 2009) show neutrino-driven outflows are proton-rich, and nucleosynthesis calculations (Hoffman 2008, Wanajo 2009) produce neutron-capture elements only up to A = 90. The paper extends earlier phenomenological studies of merger nucleosynthesis (Pruet 2004, Surman 2008) by applying a semi-analytic wind model (Wanajo 2001, 2006) to black hole winds, and suggests that Galactic chemical evolution studies (Prantzos 2006, Ishimaru 2010) do not exclude mergers as the dominant r-process site. The results enable future hydrodynamic simulations of the neutrino field in mergers and collapsars, and motivate studies of detectable electromagnetic transients from neutron-rich ejecta (Metzger 2010).
