# The Longitudinal and Transverse responses in inclusive electron scattering within the RPA framework

> The longitudinal and transverse nuclear responses to inclusive electron scattering reactions are analyzed within the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) framework. Several residual interactions are considered and it is shown that the exchange terms in the RPA make very difficult to find an effective residual interaction capable of reproducing simultaneously the quasielastic peak of both the longitudinal and transverse responses. By means of a simplified model it is illustrated that the residual interaction used in a ring approximation must fulfill some restrictions in order to qualitatively reproduce the full RPA results.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that within the full Random Phase Approximation (RPA), including exchange terms, it is very difficult to find any effective residual interaction that can simultaneously reproduce the quasielastic peak of both the longitudinal and transverse nuclear responses, a problem that is masked when using the simpler Ring Approximation (RA) where the interaction channels can be adjusted independently.

## Method

The authors calculated the longitudinal and transverse nuclear response functions for inclusive electron scattering in non-relativistic nuclear matter using the full RPA framework with a Hartree-Fock (HF) basis, employing three different finite-range effective interactions: a meson-exchange interaction with pi, rho, and omega mesons plus a g' term (V^I); a contact-term plus pi and rho meson exchange interaction (V^II); and a Bonn potential parameterization with pi, rho, sigma, and omega mesons (V^III). They compared the full RPA results with RA calculations and with experimental data on Ca-40 at momentum transfers of 300 MeV/c and 410 MeV/c, using a Fermi momentum of k_F = 235 MeV/c. They also developed a simplified contact interaction model to derive analytic relations between the antisymmetrized coupling constants that must hold for qualitative agreement between RA and full RPA.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfers studied: q = 300 MeV/c and q = 410 MeV/c
- Fermi momentum used: k_F = 235 MeV/c
- For the V^II interaction, the parameter set that gave acceptable reproduction of both channels at q = 410 MeV/c was: f_0 = -0.1, f'_0 = 0.2, g_0 = 0, g'_0 = 0.5
- Pion cutoff mass: Lambda_pi = 1.3 GeV; rho cutoff mass: Lambda_rho = 1.75 GeV
- The ratio mu_s^2 / mu_v^2 is approximately 0.035, indicating the small contribution of g_ant relative to g'_ant in the transverse response

## Context

This work extends the RPA framework for nuclear response functions developed by Bauer (1996) and De Pace (1998), and addresses the long-standing difficulty of simultaneously reproducing longitudinal and transverse quasielastic responses that was noted in earlier RA-based calculations. The authors show that the common practice of adjusting effective interactions within the RA to fit experimental data is problematic because the resulting interactions do not satisfy the antisymmetry relations required by the full RPA, and therefore cannot be reliably used in calculations of other many-body correlations such as ground state correlations, final state interactions, and meson exchange currents as reviewed by Boffi (1996). The paper enables future work by establishing that any effective interaction used in a simplified RA approach must preserve the relations between antisymmetrized coupling constants to maintain qualitative agreement with full RPA results.
