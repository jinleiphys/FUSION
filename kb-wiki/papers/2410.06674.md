# Subtleties in the calculation of correlation functions for hot and dense systems

> We discuss subtleties in the calculation of loop integrals in studies of hot and dense systems as they appear in both perturbative and non-perturbative approaches. To be specific, we address subtleties which appear in situations where the order of integration, differentiation, and limit processes plays a crucial role. For example, this applies to computations of the effective action and the computation of momentum-dependent correlation functions. In particular, the zero-temperature limit is delicate in systems with fermions because of the presence of discontinuities at the Fermi surface. We provide a general discussion of scenarios where the computation and evaluation of loop integrals in the context of relativistic theories requires particular attention as a change of the order of the involved mathematical operations may lead to a different result. Our general considerations are then illustrated with the aid of concrete examples, namely by the computation of masses from fully momentum-dependent correlation functions in the context of the Gross-Neveu-Yukawa model and quantum electrodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in loop calculations for hot and dense systems, the order of integration, differentiation, and limit processes does not commute when the chemical potential exceeds the Silver-Blaze threshold, and that the zero-temperature limit of finite-temperature calculations yields additional Dirac-delta contributions that are absent in direct zero-temperature calculations for poles of order n ≥ 2.

## Method

The authors analyze generic one-loop integrals with internal fermion lines using residue techniques and the Matsubara formalism, examining the conditions under which differentiation under the integral sign commutes with momentum integration. They then compare direct zero-temperature calculations with the zero-temperature limit of finite-temperature calculations for a prototype fermion propagator function f_τ(p) = 1/[(p_0 + iμ)^2 + x_τ²(p)]^n, and investigate the static and plasmon limits of momentum-dependent correlation functions at finite chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The Silver-Blaze threshold is defined as μ_SB(τ) = min_p |x_τ(p)|, below which the chemical potential does not affect the system
- For n ≥ 2 and μ ≥ μ_SB(τ), the zero-temperature limit of the finite-temperature calculation yields a term proportional to δ(|x_τ(p)| - μ) that is absent in direct zero-temperature calculations
- The discontinuity at the Fermi surface produces a jump of magnitude 2π c_{y*}/(n-1)! when the pole crosses the integration contour, with c_{p*} ∝ (1/(2μ))^(2n-1) > 0
- For n=2, the zero-temperature limit of the finite-temperature computation gives (1/(4x_τ²(p))) (θ(|x_τ(p)|-μ)/|x_τ(p)| - δ(|x_τ(p)|-μ))

## Context

This work extends discussions of subtle mathematical issues in finite-density quantum field theory, particularly building on the analysis of integration order effects by Gorda (2022). The authors use the functional renormalization group framework of Wetterich (1992) as their primary tool but note their considerations apply to perturbative approaches as well. The paper enables more careful treatment of correlation function calculations in hot and dense systems, especially for extracting masses from momentum-dependent correlation functions in models like the Gross-Neveu-Yukawa model and quantum electrodynamics, where the zero-temperature limit and static/plasmon limits require careful handling.
