# Nucleus giant resonances from an improved isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport approach

> We have studied the isoscalar giant quadruple resonance (ISGQR) and the isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) in $^{208}$Pb based on an improved isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport approach using an improved isospin- and momentum-dependent interaction. With the isoscalar nucleon effective mass and the nucleon-nucleon cross section which reproduces respectively the excitation energy and the width of the ISGQR strength function, the slope parameter of the symmetry energy and the neutron-proton effective mass splitting are constrained respectively within $36<L<62$ MeV and $0.08δ<(m_{n0}^*-m_{p0}^*)/m<0.42δ$, by comparing the resulting centroid energy of the IVGDR and the electric dipole polarizability with the experimental data. It is found that nucleon-nucleon collisions have considerable effects on the resulting electric dipole polarizability, which needs to be measured more accurately in order to pin down isovector nuclear interactions.

## Key claim

The slope parameter of the symmetry energy and the neutron-proton effective mass splitting are constrained by comparing the centroid energy of the IVGDR and the electric dipole polarizability with experimental data, but only after first fixing the isoscalar nucleon effective mass and the nucleon-nucleon cross section to reproduce the ISGQR excitation energy and width, respectively.

## Method

The authors used an improved isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport approach with an improved isospin- and momentum-dependent interaction (ImMDI) on a lattice Hamiltonian framework. They simulated the ISGQR and IVGDR in 208Pb by applying small initial perturbations to the ground-state nucleus, then extracted the strength function from the time evolution of the corresponding moment, and compared the resulting centroid energy and electric dipole polarizability to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- ISGQR experimental excitation energy: 10.9 ± 0.1 MeV, reproduced with isoscalar nucleon effective mass m*_{s0} = 0.9 m
- ISGQR experimental decay width: 3.0 ± 0.1 MeV, reproduced with nucleon-nucleon cross section σ = 40 mb
- IVGDR experimental centroid energy: E_{-1} = 13.46 MeV from photoabsorption reactions
- IVGDR experimental electric dipole polarizability: α_D = 19.6 ± 0.6 fm^3, corrected for quasideuteron contributions
- Constrained slope parameter of symmetry energy: 36 < L < 62 MeV
- Constrained neutron-proton effective mass splitting: 0.08δ < (m*_{n0} - m*_{p0})/m < 0.42δ at saturation density and Fermi momentum
- Corresponding isovector nucleon effective mass range: 0.73 < m*_{v0}/m < 0.86

## Context

This work extends the use of Boltzmann transport approaches, previously applied to heavy-ion collisions (Ver14, Xu19, Col20), to the study of giant resonances in finite nuclei. It builds on prior studies that used the ISGQR excitation energy to extract the isoscalar nucleon effective mass (Boh75, Boh79, Bla80, Klu09, Roc13a, zhangzhen16, Kon17, Bon18) and that used the IVGDR centroid energy and electric dipole polarizability to constrain the symmetry energy and neutron-proton effective mass splitting (zhangzhen16, Kon17). The constraint on L narrows the recent constraint of L = 58.7 ± 28.1 MeV (BAL13, Oer17), and the constraint on the neutron-proton effective mass splitting is consistent with various approaches in the literature (BAL13, Li15, Hol16, zhangzhen16, Kon17, Bal17, Mon17). The results enable more precise determination of isovector nuclear interactions, though the authors note that more accurate measurements of the electric dipole polarizability are needed.
