# Microcanonical studies on isoscaling

> The exponential scaling of isotopic yields is investigated for sources of different sizes over a broad range of excitation energies and freeze-out volumes, in both primary and asymptotic stages of the decay in the framework of a microcanonical multifragmentation model. It was found that the scaling parameters have a strong dependence on the considered pair of equilibrated sources and excitation energy and are affected by the secondary particle emission of the break-up fragments. No significant influence of the freeze-out volume on the considered isotopic ratios has been observed. Deviations of microcanonical results from grandcanonical expectations are discussed.

## Key claim

Isoscaling (exponential scaling of isotopic yields) holds within a microcanonical multifragmentation model across a broad range of source sizes, excitation energies, and freeze-out volumes, but the scaling parameters α and β depend strongly on the specific pair of sources and excitation energy, contrary to the simpler grandcanonical expectation that these parameters depend primarily on temperature and chemical potential differences.

## Method

The study used a microcanonical multifragmentation model with two stages: a break-up stage that respects strict microcanonical constraints (fixed mass, charge, total energy, momentum, and angular momentum) followed by a sequential particle evaporation stage for secondary decays. Isotopic yield ratios were calculated for pairs of equilibrated sources with masses from A=40 to A=200, excitation energies from 2 to 15 MeV/nucleon, and freeze-out volumes from 3V₀ to 10V₀, in both primary and asymptotic stages.

## Key numbers

- Increasing excitation energy from 2 MeV/nucleon to 15 MeV/nucleon for sources (200,80) and (185,80) at V=6V₀ caused α to diminish by a factor of 2.5 and |β| to diminish by a factor of 3.5
- For small sources ((112,50) and (124,50)) at E_ex=5 MeV/nucleon and V=3V₀: α_bk=0.37, α_as=0.50, β_bk=-0.49, β_as=-0.55
- For small sources ((112,50) and (119,50)) at E_ex=5 MeV/nucleon and V=3V₀: α_bk=0.23, α_as=0.32, β_bk=-0.30, β_as=-0.34
- Freeze-out volumes from 3V₀ to 10V₀ showed no significant influence on α and β for sources (200,80) and (185,80) at excitation energies from 2 to 15 MeV/nucleon

## Context

This work extends prior isoscaling studies that were primarily based on grandcanonical or canonical statistical models, such as those by Tsang (2001) and Botvina, by using a microcanonical ensemble that is more appropriate for finite nuclear systems. The results contradict the canonical SMM predictions of Tsang and collaborators (which found α independent of source size) and instead show strong source-size dependence, while also contradicting the same group's finding of freeze-out volume dependence. The study enables more reliable extraction of the nuclear symmetry energy from multifragmentation data by clarifying which source parameters actually affect isoscaling parameters.
