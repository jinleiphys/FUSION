# Isotopic equilibrium constants for very low-density and low-temperature nuclear matter

> Yields of equatorially emitted light isotopes, $1\le Z\le 14$, observed in ternary fission in the reaction $^{241}$Pu($n_{\rm th}$,f) are employed to determine apparent chemical equilibrium constants for low-temperature and low-density nuclear matter. The degree of liberation and role of medium modifications are probed through a comparison of experimentally derived reaction quotients with equilibrium constants calculated using a relativistic mean-field model employing a universal medium modification correction for the attractive $σ$ meson coupling. The results of these comparisons indicate that equilibrium is achieved for the lighter ternary fission isotopes. For the heavier isotopes experimental reaction quotients are well below calculated equilibrium constants. This is attributed to a dynamical limitation reflecting insufficient time for full equilibrium to develop. The role of medium effects leading to yield reductions is discussed as is the apparent enhancement of yields for $^8$He and other very neutron rich exotic nuclei.

## Key claim

The experimentally derived reaction quotients for lighter isotopes (Z ≤ 5) produced in ternary fission of ²⁴²Pu agree with equilibrium constants calculated using a relativistic mean-field model, indicating chemical equilibrium is achieved for those isotopes; however, for heavier isotopes the experimental values fall well below calculated equilibrium constants, attributed to a dynamical limitation from insufficient time for full equilibrium to develop rather than to medium effects.

## Method

Yields of equatorially emitted light isotopes (1 ≤ Z ≤ 14) from the reaction ²⁴¹Pu(n_th,f) were taken from the Koester data set and used to calculate effective reaction quotients Q_c^eff for isotope formation from nucleons. These were compared to equilibrium constants K_c^eff calculated using the relativistic mean-field model of Pais et al. (2018) with the FSU Gold EOS, employing a universal scalar cluster-meson coupling fraction x_i,σ = 0.92, at T = 1.4 MeV, total density ρ = 2.56 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁻³, and proton fraction Y_p = 0.34. The density was determined by matching the theoretical free neutron-to-proton ratio to the experimentally observed ratio.

## Key numbers

- Temperature of the emitting system: T = 1.4 MeV
- Total density: ρ = 2.56 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁻³ (with uncertainty ±0.20 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁻³)
- Proton fraction: Y_p = 0.34
- Experimental equilibrium constant for ⁴He direct formation: K_c(⁴He) = 3.02 (±1.07) × 10¹⁸ fm⁹
- Effective experimental equilibrium constant for total observed ⁴He yield: K_c^eff(⁴He) = 3.66 (±1.30) × 10¹⁸ fm⁹
- Theoretical K_c^eff(⁴He) with x_i,σ = 0.92: 4.62 × 10¹⁸ fm⁹
- Experimental Q_c^eff for ⁸He: 2.60 (±1.97) × 10³³ fm⁹, compared to theoretical K_c^eff(⁸He) = 3.76 × 10³² fm⁹ (experiment an order of magnitude higher)
- Ratio R_exp/theo drops to approximately 10⁻⁵ for the heaviest isotopes
- Coalescence volume extracted from thermal coalescence model: 2937 fm³
- Density derived from nucleation model fits: 4 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the approach of Pais et al. (2018, 2019), who studied in-medium modifications of light cluster properties using relativistic mean-field models and compared equilibrium constants to data from intermediate-energy Xe+Sn collisions. The present study uses ternary fission data from Koester et al. (2000, 1999) to probe even lower temperatures and densities, providing an experimental counterpart to theoretical virial equations of state (Horowitz et al., 2006; Roepke et al., 2013). The results enable testing of nuclear equation of state models in the low-density limit and motivate further investigation of neutron-rich exotic nuclei such as ⁸He, which may exhibit alpha-tetra-neutron structure (Kisamori et al., 2016) or tetra-neutron condensate formation in low-density neutron-rich stellar matter (Ivanytskyi et al., 2019).
