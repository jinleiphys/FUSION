# Time-dependent approach to many-particle tunneling in one-dimension

> Employing the time-dependent approach, we investigate a quantum tunneling decay of many-particle systems. We apply it to a one-dimensional three-body problem with a heavy core nucleus and two valence protons. We calculate the decay width for two-proton emission from the survival probability, which well obeys the exponential decay-law after a sufficient time. The effect of the correlation between the two emitted protons is also studied by observing the time evolution of the two-particle density distribution. It is shown that the pairing correlation significantly enhances the probability for the simultaneous diproton decay.

## Key claim

The time-dependent approach to many-particle tunneling is extended to two-proton emissions in a one-dimensional three-body model, showing that the pairing correlation between valence protons significantly enhances the probability for simultaneous diproton decay, though the authors note that in reality two protons are never bound in vacuum, unlike in their one-dimensional model where an infinitesimal attraction always produces a bound state.

## Method

The authors constructed a one-dimensional three-body model with a heavy core nucleus (A_c=60, Z_c=30) and two valence protons, using a Woods-Saxon nuclear potential and a soft-core Coulomb potential for the core-proton interaction, plus a density-dependent contact pairing interaction between the two protons. They prepared the initial state as the lowest eigenstate of a modified confining potential, then solved the time-dependent Schrodinger equation by expanding the wave function on a basis of eigenfunctions of the modified Hamiltonian, computing the survival probability and decay width from the time evolution.

## Key numbers

- The core-proton potential parameters: V_0 = 46.5 MeV, R = 1.27*A_c^(1/3) fm, a = 0.67 fm, with A_c=60 and Z_c=30
- The bound state of the modified potential at epsilon = 2.71 MeV, with the modified potential value at x_c=10 fm being V_mod(x_c)=4.21 MeV and the barrier height V_b=4.75 MeV at x=8.1 fm
- The decay width estimated at t = 1200 fm/c, with values in the same order as experimental widths for ^6Be and ^16Ne
- For the uncorrelated case (g=0), the initial state eigenenergy E_0 = 5.42 MeV; for g=20 MeV*fm, E_0 = 4.56 MeV
- The time mesh used was Delta t = 0.01 fm/c, with a box size of X_box = +/- 120 fm

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent approach previously applied only to two-body decay phenomena such as alpha decays and one-proton decays (Carrier 1994, 1998, 1999, 2000) to the many-particle case of two-proton radioactivity. The one-dimensional three-body model follows the approach of Hagino, Vigezzi, and Polls (2011) and addresses the same physics as experimental observations of two-proton emitters like ^45Fe (Pfutzner 2002, Giovinazzo 2002, Miernik 2007) and ^6Be (Ogloblin 1989, Grigorenko 2001, 2009). The results enable future extension to realistic three-dimensional calculations for nuclei such as ^6Be and ^16Ne.
