# Coherent Pion Pairs from Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The degree to which a nucleus can act as a source for coherent pion pairs is investigated for intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions. Creation through both isovector and isoscalar channels is considered. Two experimental signals are proposed for evidence of two-pion coherent production, two-pion enhancement and the focusing of outgoing pions along the beam axis.

# Coherent Pion Pairs from Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim
For intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions, a nucleus can act as a source for coherent pion pairs through both isoscalar (coupling to baryon density) and isovector (coupling to neutron-proton density difference) channels, but the calculated yield is highly sensitive to the time step chosen in the simulation and the magnitude may change by as much as a factor of two due to details of the calculation.

## Method
The authors used an effective four-point Lagrangian coupling pion pairs to nucleon densities, with separate treatments for isoscalar (via p-wave pion-nucleon coupling) and isovector (via rho meson exchange) channels. They extracted baryon density evolution from a BUU (Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) simulation of a zero-impact-parameter 150 MeV per nucleon collision of A=100 nuclei, then constructed trajectories representing the baryon density by tracking fractional containment boundaries. The Fourier transform of these trajectories was used to compute coherent two-pion emission spectra, with absorption estimated from a three-fermi absorption distance.

## Key numbers
- For the isoscalar channel at 150 MeV per nucleon, the number of charged pions per event was 1 per 10^5.
- At 100 MeV per nucleon, the isoscalar yield was 30 percent of the yield at 150 MeV per nucleon.
- For the isovector channel at 150 MeV per nucleon (assuming 42 protons and 58 neutrons per nucleus), the charged pion yield was one third of the isoscalar charged pion yield, with no neutral pions produced.
- The single-pion energy spectra peaked near 100 MeV kinetic energy.
- Calculations excluded pions with kinetic energies above 200 MeV.
- A three-fermi absorption distance was assumed.
- Trajectories were calculated from BUU output at 2 fm/c intervals and splined to 0.5 fm/c time steps.

## Context
This work extends theoretical investigations of coherent pion sources (Andreev, Plumer and Weiner, 1991; Pratt, 1996) and connects to studies of disoriented chiral condensates and CENTAURO cosmic ray phenomenology (Rajagopal and Wilczek, 1993; Gavin and Muller, 1994). The authors propose two experimental signatures: focusing of outgoing pions along the beam axis (which had been observed in Ar+Au reactions by Schubert et al., 1994, but could be confused with shadowing effects) and two-pion enhancement relative to single-pion yields. The calculations could be improved by considering other emission sources, better absorption treatment, nuclear correlations, and non-zero impact parameter collisions.
