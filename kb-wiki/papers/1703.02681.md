# High-mass twin stars with a multi-polytrope EoS

> We show that in the 3-polytropes model of Hebeler et al. \cite{Hebeler:2013nza} for the neutron star equation of state at supersaturation densities a third family of compact stars can be obtained which confirms the possibility of high-mass twin stars that have coincident masses $M_1=M_2\approx 2~M_\odot$ and significantly different radii $|R_1-R_2|>2-3 $ km. We show that the causality constraint puts severe limitations on the maximum mass of the third family sequence which can be relaxed when this scheme is extended to four polytropes thus mimicking a realistic high-density matter EoS.

# High-mass twin stars with a multi-polytrope EoS

## Key claim

Within the three-polytrope model of Hebeler et al. (2013) for the neutron star equation of state at supersaturation densities, a third family of compact stars can be obtained that confirms the possibility of high-mass twin stars with coincident masses near 2 solar masses and significantly different radii, but the causality constraint severely limits the maximum mass of the third family sequence unless the scheme is extended to four polytropes.

## Method

The authors adopt the piecewise polytrope equation of state scheme of Hebeler et al. (2013), where the supersaturation density region is split into three density intervals, each described by a polytropic relation P = κ n^Γ. They assign the second polytrope region to a first-order phase transition with constant pressure (Γ₂ = 0) and perform a Maxwell construction to determine the critical pressure and density jump. The Tolman-Volkoff-Oppenheimer equations are then solved for static, spherically symmetric stars to obtain mass-radius sequences, with the polytrope index Γ₃ of the high-density phase varied as a free parameter while enforcing causality.

## Key numbers

- Twin star masses: M₁ = M₂ ≈ 2 M⊙, with radius difference |R₁ − R₂| > 2–3 km
- For set 3 parameters: hybrid star maximum mass M_max^HS = 1.991 M⊙, minimum hybrid star mass M_min^HS = 1.934 M⊙, with hadronic maximum mass M_max^NS = 2.01 M⊙
- For set 4 (four-polytrope extension): hybrid star maximum mass reaches 2.106 M⊙
- Critical pressure P_crit = 63.177 MeV fm⁻³, critical energy density ε_crit = 318.26 MeV fm⁻³, energy density jump Δε = 253.89 MeV fm⁻³
- Transition onset density n₁₂ = 0.32 fm⁻³, transition end density n₂₃ = 0.53 fm⁻³
- For set 4, additional polytrope boundary at n₃₄ = 0.75 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the piecewise polytrope approach of Hebeler et al. (2013) for neutron star equations of state by demonstrating that high-mass twin stars can arise even within this relatively conservative scheme, not only from exotic constructions. It builds on the classification scheme of Alford et al. (2013) for hybrid star mass-radius topologies and the Seidov constraint (1971) for instability at phase transitions. The results enable observational verification through missions like NICER or SKA by measuring radii of high-mass pulsars such as PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis et al., 2013) and PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest et al., 2010; Fonseca et al., 2016), and connect the high-mass twin phenomenon to the existence of a critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram.
