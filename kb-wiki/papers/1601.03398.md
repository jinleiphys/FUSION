# Alpha-constrained QSE Nucleosynthesis in High-entropy and Fast-expanding Material

> We investigate the nucleosynthesis process in high-entropy ($s/k_{\rm B}\gtrsim100$) and very fast-expanding ($τ_{\rm exp}\sim10^{-3}\ {\rm s}$) materials. In such a material with the electron fraction near 0.5, an interesting nucleosynthesis process occurs. In this process, the abundance distribution of heavy-nuclei of $A>100$ achieve quasi-statistical equilibrium (QSE) at high temperature and the abundances are frozen at the end of the nucleosynthesis. We explain this abundance distribution using the "alpha-constrained QSE" abundances formulated in this paper. We demonstrate that this nucleosynthesis would occur in neutrino-driven winds from massive proto-neutron stars in hypernovae, where $A\sim140$ $p$-nuclei are synthesized.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in high-entropy (s/kB ≳ 100) and very fast-expanding (τexp ∼ 10−3 s) material with electron fraction near 0.5, heavy nuclei of A > 100 achieve a quasi-statistical equilibrium (QSE) that can be described by a new "alpha-constrained QSE" formulation, but this nucleosynthesis process only occurs when the production of heavy nuclei is insufficient to supply alpha particles back to equilibrium values.

## Method

The authors formulate an "alpha-constrained QSE" (αQSE) abundance distribution by adding a fourth constraint (fixed alpha particle abundance) to the standard QSE framework of Meyer (1998), then solve for abundances using Lagrange multipliers under conservation of mass, charge neutrality, and fixed heavy-nuclei abundance. They compare the αQSE abundances to full nuclear reaction network calculations on simple adiabatic expansion models with constant velocity, varying entropy s, expansion timescale τexp, and electron fraction Ye. They then apply the framework to neutrino-driven wind solutions from massive proto-neutron stars in hypernovae, using the wind model and nuclear reaction network from Fujibayashi (2015).

## Key numbers

* Entropy threshold for the process: s/kB ≳ 100
* Expansion timescale for the process: τexp ∼ 10−3 s (1 ms)
* Temperature where triple-alpha reaction becomes insufficient: ∼ 6 × 10^9 K
* In the αQSE example at T9 = 5, ρ5 ≈ 2, Ye = 0.50: a 2% lack of Yα from its normal QSE value (Yα = 0.98 Yα,QSE) raises proton abundance to Yp ≈ 6.9 × 10−3 and neutron abundance to Yn ≈ 1.4 × 10−3, which are 2.4 and 2.6 times larger than normal QSE values
* In the hypernova wind model: A ∼ 110−140 p-nuclei are the main products
* For the case B expansion (s = 254, τexp = 4.07 ms): average mass number ⟨A⟩ = 159
* For the case A expansion (s = 153, τexp = 4.07 ms): average mass number ⟨A⟩ < 60

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Meyer (2002) that fast-expanding, high-entropy material could produce light p-nuclei, and the hypernova wind model of Fujibayashi (2015) which showed such conditions could be realized in neutrino-driven winds from massive proto-neutron stars. The paper provides a theoretical framework (αQSE) to understand the nucleosynthesis process, showing that the abundance distribution can be understood as a transient phase toward the αQSE abundance rather than proceeding through the αQSE abundance itself. This enables quantitative predictions for p-nuclei production in hypernova winds, addressing the underproduction of heavy p-nuclei (110 < A < 125) that has been a problem for the gamma-process in core-collapse supernovae as studied by Rayet (1995) and Rauscher (2002).
