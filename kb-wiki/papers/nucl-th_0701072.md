# Density reorganization in hot nuclei

> The density profile of a hot nuclear system produced in intermediate energy heavy ion collisions is studied in a microcanonical formulation with a momentum and density dependent finite range interaction. The caloric curve and the density evolution with excitation are calculated for a number of systems for the equilibrium mononuclear configuration; they compare favorably with the recent experimental data. The studied density fluctuations are seen to build up rapidly beyond an excitation energy of 8 MeV/u indicating the instability of the system towards nuclear disassembly. Explicit introduction of deformation in the expansion path of the heated nucleus, however, shows that the system might fragment even earlier. We also explore the effects of the nuclear equation of state and of the mass and isospin asymmetry on the nuclear equilibrium configuration and the relevant experimental observables.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the equilibrium mononuclear configuration of a hot nucleus produced in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions undergoes density reorganization that depends sensitively on the nuclear equation of state, and that the system may fragment even earlier than the rapid buildup of density fluctuations at ~8 MeV/u would suggest if deformation along the expansion path is explicitly considered.

## Method

The authors employ a finite-temperature Thomas-Fermi (FTTF) framework with a momentum- and density-dependent finite-range Seyler-Blanchard (SBM) interaction to generate self-consistent density profiles for hot nuclei. The equilibrium mononuclear configuration at a given excitation energy is found by maximizing the entropy under the constraint of fixed excitation energy, allowing the system to expand via a self-similar scaling approximation. The effects of correlations (through an energy-dependent effective mass) and of volume-conserving quadrupole deformation are also incorporated.

## Key numbers

- The softer SBM interaction (nuclear incompressibility K∞ = 238 MeV) yields a limiting excitation energy above which the mononuclear configuration ceases to exist at ~11 MeV/u; for the harder EoS (K∞ = 380 MeV) this limiting excitation energy is somewhat higher.
- The surface tension coefficient at saturation density for symmetric matter is taken as σ(ρ₀, X=0, T=0) = 1.06 MeV fm⁻², with a surface asymmetry coefficient a_s = 1.7826 MeV fm⁻².
- The critical temperature for nuclear matter used in the temperature dependence of surface tension is T_c = 15 MeV.
- The break-up density fluctuations are seen to build up rapidly beyond an excitation energy of ~8 MeV/u.

## Context

This work extends earlier schematic calculations by Sobotka et al. (2004) and the more realistic Thomas-Fermi calculations by De et al. (2005) that used a Skyrme interaction (SkM*) for only one system (¹⁵⁰Sm). The present study uses a finite-range SBM interaction to avoid numerical instabilities associated with zero-range forces in expanded systems, and systematically explores the effects of the nuclear equation of state, mass, and isospin asymmetry on the equilibrium density and caloric curve. The results enable more reliable extraction of break-up densities from experimental data and provide constraints on the nuclear symmetry energy relevant for supernova nucleosynthesis and neutron star properties.
