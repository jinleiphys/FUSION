# Thermal QCD with external imaginary electric fields on the lattice

> We study QCD at finite temperature in the presence of imaginary electric fields. In particular, we determine the electric susceptibility, the leading coefficient in the expansion of the QCD pressure in the imaginary field. Unlike for magnetic fields, at nonzero temperature this coefficient requires a non-trivial separation of genuine electric field-related effects and spurious effects related to the chemical potential, which becomes an unphysical gauge parameter in this setting. Our results are based on lattice simulations with stout improved dynamical staggered quarks at physical quark masses.

# Thermal QCD with external imaginary electric fields on the lattice

## Key claim

The electric susceptibility of thermal QCD can be extracted from lattice simulations using imaginary electric fields, but only after separating a spurious infrared-divergent term proportional to the chemical potential mixing, which is absent for magnetic fields; the authors present a Taylor-expansion method in imaginary electric fields but do not perform an analytic continuation to real electric fields.

## Method

The authors simulate QCD with three flavors of stout smeared staggered quarks at physical quark masses using the tree-level improved Symanzik gauge action on \(24^3 \times 6\) lattices. They compute the current-current and density-density correlators \(G_{\mu\nu}(x_1)\) and convolute them with a quadratic kernel to obtain the bare electric and magnetic susceptibilities, then subtract the \(T \approx 0\) values for additive renormalization. The electric susceptibility is defined via oscillatory field profiles to maintain equilibrium, with the physical part isolated by removing the \((LT)^2\) term proportional to the quark number susceptibility \(c_2\).

## Key numbers

- Lattice size: \(24^3 \times 6\) with \(m/T = 0.08\) for the free case demonstration
- Temperature for correlator plot: \(T \approx 176\) MeV
- Electric field quantization condition: \(a^2 q \hat E = 2\pi N_E / (N_1 N_4)\) with \(N_E \in \mathbb{Z}\)
- Minimal electric field in thermodynamic limit: \(q \hat E_{\text{min}} = 2\pi T / L\)
- The infrared divergent mismatch term in the naive electric susceptibility is proportional to \((LT)^2\)

## Context

This work extends the lattice methodology for magnetic fields developed by Bali et al. (2020) to the electric field case, where additional conceptual challenges arise: electric fields accelerate charged particles out of equilibrium and mix with the chemical potential at nonzero temperature. The magnetic susceptibility was previously determined using the same approach and showed diamagnetic behavior (negative \(\chi^r\)) at low temperatures due to pions and paramagnetic behavior (positive \(\chi^r\)) at high temperatures due to quarks. The electric susceptibility results presented here are preliminary, with analytic continuation to real electric fields left for future work.
