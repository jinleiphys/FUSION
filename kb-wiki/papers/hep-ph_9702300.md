# Analyticity, Shapes of Semileptonic Form Factors, and B to pi l nu

> We give a pedagogical discussion of the physics underlying dispersion relation-derived parameterizations of form factors describing B -> pi l nu and B -> D l nu. Moments of the dispersion relations are shown to provide substantially tighter constraints on the f_+ (t) form factor describing B -> pi l nu than the unweighted dispersion relation alone. Heavy quark spin symmetry relations between the B -> pi l nu and B^* -> pi l nu form factors enables such constraints to be tightened even further.

## Key claim

Moments of dispersion relations (higher derivatives of the two-point function at q²=0) provide substantially tighter constraints on the B→πℓν form factor f₊(t) than the unweighted dispersion relation alone, and heavy quark spin symmetry relations between B→πℓν and B*→πℓν form factors can tighten these constraints even further, though the reliability of higher moments is limited by the convergence of the operator product expansion when corrections approach 100% of the leading result.

## Method

The authors derive model-independent parameterizations of semileptonic form factors using QCD dispersion relations and analyticity, expressing form factors as F(t) = [1/(P(t)φ(t))] Σ aₖ z(t;t₀)ᵏ where the coefficients aₖ satisfy Σ aₖ² ≤ 1. They compute the nth derivatives of the vector current two-point function at q²=0 (χⱼ⁽ⁿ⁾) in perturbative QCD at one and two loops, including condensate contributions, and use these to constrain the expansion coefficients for the B→πℓν form factor f₊(t) by requiring that the weighted integral over the pair-production region be bounded by the perturbative calculation.

## Key numbers

- For B→πℓν, the maximum value of the expansion variable z is zₘₐₓ = 0.52
- For B→Dℓν with t₀ = t₋, the maximum value of z is zₘₐₓ = 0.06
- The one-loop constraint on a₂⁽⁰⁾ for n=0 gives -0.77 < a₂ < 0.79; for n=3 gives -0.26 < a₂ < 0.28 (a factor of three tighter)
- The two-loop constraint on a₂⁽⁰⁾ including condensates for n=0 gives -0.78 < a₂ < 0.79; for n=2 gives -0.44 < a₂ < 0.45
- Including heavy quark spin symmetry (HQS) at two loops for n=0 gives -0.54 < a₂ < 0.58; for n=2 gives -0.36 < a₂ < 0.36
- The two-loop perturbative correction factor ρ⁽ⁿ⁾_pert for n=0 is 0.23; for n=3 is 0.42
- The condensate correction factor ρ⁽ⁿ⁾_cond for n=0 is 0.01; for n=3 is 0.28
- The B* resonance mass used for the Blaschke factor in B→πℓν is 5.325 GeV

## Context

This work extends the model-independent parameterization approach of Boyd, Grinstein, and Lebed (1995) and Lellouch (1996) by incorporating higher moments of dispersion relations and heavy quark spin symmetry relations between B→πℓν and B*→πℓν form factors, following the spin symmetry approach used by Caprini (1996) for B→B elastic form factors. The improved constraints enable more precise extraction of |V_ub| from experimental measurements of B→πℓν by reducing the theoretical uncertainty from form factor shape parameterization, which is critical since uncertainty in |V_ub| feeds into unitarity-triangle constraints from CP violation in the kaon system as the fourth power.
