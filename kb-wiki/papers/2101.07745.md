# Feasibility of an experimental search for a resonance of a pion and a light nucleus

> A hypothesis is proposed herein, suggesting that a pion-nuclear resonance may be observed in the $α+d\to{}^6\mathrm{Li}(3.563)+π^0$ reaction. The resonance has a $πNNα$ structure, containing $αNN$ and $πNN$ subsystems. The former corresponds to the $A=6$ isotriplet ($^6\mathrm{He}_\text{g.s.}$, $^6\mathrm{Li}(3.563)$, $^6\mathrm{Be}_\text{g.s.}$), whereas the latter is a hypothetical $NN$-decoupled dibaryon. We propose an experiment to search for this resonance using the $^7\mathrm{Li}(p,d)$ reaction.

## Key claim

The paper hypothesizes that a sharp structure observed in the excitation function of the α+d→⁶Li(3.563)+π⁰ pionic fusion reaction at CELSIUS may be explained by the formation of a previously unproposed πNNα resonance, rather than by the cluster-structure model that failed to reproduce the data.

## Method

The author reviews existing data from the CELSIUS pionic fusion experiment (Andersson 2006) and proposes that the πNNα system, with quantum numbers I(J^P)=0(0⁻) and containing πNN and αNN subsystems, could account for the observed cross-section anomaly. The feasibility of a new experiment is evaluated by estimating the differential cross section for the ⁷Li(p,d) reaction at 392 MeV using the elementary pn→dπ⁰ cross section, an effective neutron number from Glauber model calculations, and a spectroscopic factor from Fagerstrom (1976), then comparing the signal rate to background levels inferred from ²⁰⁸Pb(n,d) and ¹²C(p,d) data.

## Key numbers

- The πNN subsystem resonance was calculated by Garcilazo (1997) at 2018 MeV (42 keV above the πNN threshold) with a width of 1.75 MeV.
- Ueda (1998) found a strong structure in the πNN system at 4–5 MeV above the πNN threshold with a width of 4–5 MeV.
- The point-proton charge radius of ⁶He_g.s. was determined to be 1.85(8) fm from fitting the CELSIUS data.
- The estimated differential cross section for ⁷Li(p,d)⁶Li(3.563)+π⁰ at 392 MeV is approximately 80 μb/sr.
- If the sticking probability to form the πNNα resonance is 1%, its formation cross section is approximately 0.8 μb/sr.
- The background level near the π emission threshold is estimated to be of order 30 μb/sr/MeV.
- With a 10 nb proton beam on a 100 mg/cm² ⁷Li target, thousands of πNNα resonances may be produced per hour, compared to a background of approximately 2×10⁵/MeV per hour.

## Context

This work extends the study of meson-nucleus bound states, such as the K̅NN system observed at J-PARC (Ichikawa 2015; Yamaga 2020) and the η-³He system (Pheron 2012; Xie 2017), into the pionic sector with a six-nucleon system. The proposed πNNα resonance is distinct from the established d*(2380) ΔΔ dibaryon (Adlarson 2014) and NΔ dibaryon candidates (Oh 1997), as it involves s-wave πN interactions and does not couple to NN channels. The planned ⁷Li(p,d) experiment at RCNP, using the GRAF beamline and Grand Raiden spectrometer, could provide the first direct test of this resonance hypothesis and, even if no peak is observed, would yield unique data on the π⁰-⁶Li interaction.
