# New Forms of Deuteron Equations and Wave Function Representations

> A recently developed helicity basis for nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering is applied to th e deuteron bound state. Here the total spin of the deuteron is treated in such a helicity representation. For the bound state, two sets of two coupled eigenvalue equations are developed, where the amplitudes depend on two and one variable, respectively. Numerical illustrations based on the realistic Bonn-B NN potential are given. In addition, an `operator form' of the deuteron wave function is presented, and several momentum dependent spin densities are derived and shown, in which the angular dependence is given analytically.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a recently developed helicity basis for nucleon-nucleon scattering can be applied to the deuteron bound state, yielding two sets of two coupled eigenvalue equations (in two variables and one variable, respectively) that avoid the need for partial wave expansions of the nuclear force; the numerical illustrations are based specifically on the Bonn-B potential, and the authors note that the scheme automatically provides full insight into the deuteron quantum numbers without requiring a-priori knowledge.

## Method

The authors project the deuteron eigenvalue equation onto a helicity basis where the total spin of the two-nucleon system is treated in helicity representation, obtaining two coupled integral equations in two variables (momentum magnitude and angle relative to the z-axis). They then derive an operator form of the deuteron wave function in momentum space, from which they obtain a second set of two coupled one-dimensional eigenvalue equations in momentum magnitude alone. Numerical solutions are obtained using an iterative Lanczos-type technique with Gaussian grids, employing the Bonn-B potential.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: 2.224 MeV, obtained with the Bonn-B potential using 72 grid points for the q' integration, 32 grid points for the cos theta' integration, and 10 quadrature points for the phi' integration
- The q' integration interval was cut off at 30 fm^-1
- The wave function components phi^0_0(q,theta) and phi^0_1(q,theta) show cosine-like and sine-like angular behavior respectively, with phi^0_0 having a larger maximum than phi^0_1
- For M_d=1 and M_d=-1, the maximum of phi^{M_d}_1(q,theta) is larger than that of phi^{M_d}_0(q,theta)

## Context

This work extends the three-dimensional helicity approach developed by the same authors for NN scattering (Fachruddin, Elster, Gloeckle, 2000) to the deuteron bound state, avoiding the partial wave expansions required by standard treatments. The operator form of the deuteron wave function in momentum space parallels a configuration-space representation given by Ericson (1988), while the graphical study of wave function properties extends similar work in configuration space based on the AV18 nuclear force (Wiringa, Stoks, Schiavilla, 1995). The derived analytical expressions for spin densities of polarized deuterons may have applications for electron scattering on the deuteron.
