# Phenomenological Consequences of Enhanced Bulk Viscosity Near the QCD Critical Point

> In the proximity of the QCD critical point the bulk viscosity of quark-gluon matter is expected to be proportional to nearly the third power of the critical correlation length, and become significantly enhanced. This work is the first attempt to study the phenomenological consequences of enhanced bulk viscosity near the QCD critical point. For this purpose, we implement the expected critical behavior of the bulk viscosity within a non-boost-invariant, longitudinally expanding $1+1$ dimensional causal relativistic hydrodynamical evolution at non-zero baryon density. We demonstrate that the critically-enhanced bulk viscosity induces a substantial non-equilibrium pressure, effectively softening the equation of state, and leads to sizable effects in the flow velocity and single particle distributions at the freeze-out. The observable effects that may arise due to the enhanced bulk viscosity in the vicinity of the QCD critical point can be used as complimentary information to facilitate searches for the QCD critical point.

## Key claim

This work is the first attempt to study the phenomenological consequences of enhanced bulk viscosity near the QCD critical point, but the authors emphasize that this is an exploratory study using simplified 1+1 dimensional hydrodynamics with a non-critical equation of state and specific choices for the location and width of the critical region, meaning the results are illustrative rather than definitive predictions.

## Method

The authors implemented the expected critical behavior of bulk viscosity (zeta ~ xi^3) and bulk relaxation time (tau_Pi ~ xi^3) within a non-boost-invariant, longitudinally expanding 1+1 dimensional causal relativistic hydrodynamic evolution (Israel-Stewart theory) at non-zero baryon density. They solved the hydrodynamic equations with initial conditions tuned to roughly imitate SPS Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=17 GeV, comparing evolution trajectories with and without a critical point while keeping the same initial conditions and equation of state. The hydrodynamic evolution was ended using the Cooper-Frye freeze-out formalism at constant energy density epsilon_f = 0.25 GeV/fm^3, with bulk viscous corrections to the particle distribution included via Grad's moment expansion.

## Key numbers

- Bulk viscosity near the QCD critical point scales as zeta ~ xi^3, where xi is the correlation length
- Bulk relaxation time near the critical point scales as tau_Pi ~ xi^3
- The critical region was chosen with width (Delta mu_B, Delta T) = (0.1, 0.02) GeV, centered at (mu_B^c, T_c) = (0.22, 0.16) GeV
- The maximum correlation length was set to xi_max = 10 xi_0
- Hydrodynamic evolution started at tau_th = 1.5 fm/c
- Freeze-out occurred at constant energy density epsilon_f = 0.25 GeV/fm^3, corresponding to T_f ~ 0.15 GeV at vanishing chemical potential
- The presence of a critical point can enhance dN_ch/dY in forward-rapidity by as much as 30%
- For the no-CP scenario, |Pi| is an order of magnitude smaller than equilibrium pressure; for CP, |Pi| can become comparable to equilibrium pressure within the critical regime

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hydrodynamic evolution near a critical point by Paech (2003), Nonaka (2004), and Herold (2013), which did not incorporate critical behavior of bulk viscosity, and complements studies of critical fluctuations by Stephanov (1998, 2008) and Athanasiou (2010) that focused on static properties. The results suggest that enhanced bulk viscosity near the QCD critical point could produce observable signatures in rapidity distributions of charged particles and net baryons, potentially providing complementary information to fluctuation measurements in the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC. The authors note that future work using state-of-the-art 3+1 dimensional hydrodynamics could explore connections to the observed non-monotonicity in directed flow of net-protons reported by Adamczyk (2014) and triangular flow reported by Adamczyk (2016).
