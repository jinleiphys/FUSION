# Saturation properties of nuclear matter in the presence of strong magnetic field

> Different saturation properties of cold symmetric nuclear matter in the strong magnetic field have been considered. We have seen that for magnetic fields about $B> 3 \times 10 ^ {17}\ G$, {for both cases with and without nucleon anomalous magnetic moments}, the saturation density and saturation energy grow by increasing the magnetic field. It is indicated that the magnetic susceptibility of symmetric nuclear matter becomes negative showing the diamagnetic response especially at $B< 3 \times 10 ^ {17}\ G$. We have found that for the nuclear matter, the magnitude of orbital magnetization reaches the higher values comparing to the spin magnetization. Our results for the incompressibility show that at high enough magnetic fields, i.e. $B> 3 \times 10 ^ {17}\ G$, {the softening of equation of state caused by Landau quantization is overwhelmed by stiffening due to the magnetization of nuclear matter.} We have shown that the effects of strong magnetic field on nuclear matter may affect the constraints on the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter obtained applying the experimental observable.

## Key claim

For magnetic fields above \(B > 3 \times 10^{17}\ \mathrm{G}\), the saturation density and saturation energy of cold symmetric nuclear matter increase with field strength, and the softening of the equation of state caused by Landau quantization is overwhelmed by stiffening due to magnetization; these effects hold both with and without inclusion of nucleon anomalous magnetic moments (AMM).

## Method

The authors used the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method with the \(AV_{18}\) two-body inter-nucleon potential to compute the energy of cold symmetric nuclear matter in a uniform magnetic field along the \(z\)-axis. The energy functional was expanded to the two-body cluster term, with one-body energies including Landau quantization for protons and spin-field interactions for both protons and neutrons. The two-body correlation functions were obtained by minimizing the two-body energy under a normalization constraint, yielding the saturation density, saturation energy, magnetization, magnetic susceptibility, and incompressibility as functions of magnetic field strength.

## Key numbers

- At zero magnetic field, the saturation density is \(\rho_0 = 0.310\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) and the saturation energy is \(E_b = -18.46\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- For \(B \lesssim 3 \times 10^{17}\ \mathrm{G}\), the saturation density decreases slightly to \(\rho_0 = 0.309\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) (both with and without AMM), and the saturation energy reaches \(E_b = -18.48\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (with AMM) and \(E_b = -18.47\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (without AMM).
- At \(B = 10^{19}\ \mathrm{G}\), the saturation energy is \(E_b = -17.63\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (with AMM) and \(E_b = -16.63\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (without AMM), showing the system remains bound.
- The magnetic susceptibility at \(B = 10^{17}\ \mathrm{G}\) is \(\chi/A = -0.343 \times 10^{-32}\ \mathrm{G}^{-1}\), indicating diamagnetic behavior.
- The incompressibility at zero magnetic field is \(\mathcal{K} = 302\ \mathrm{MeV}\); it decreases at low fields (softening) and increases for \(B > 3 \times 10^{17}\ \mathrm{G}\) (stiffening).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of nuclear matter in strong magnetic fields using relativistic Hartree theory (Chakra07) and effective baryon-meson exchange models (Diener and Scholtz, 2011), which found increasing saturation density with field but disagreed on binding energy trends. It also contrasts with non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations (Aguirre, 2011) that examined compressibility and magnetic susceptibility. The results enable refined constraints on the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter for applications to magnetars and heavy-ion collisions, where magnetic fields can reach \(10^{15}\)–\(10^{19}\ \mathrm{G}\).
