# Evolution of elliptic and triangular flow as a function of beam energy in a hybrid model

> Elliptic flow has been one of the key observables for establishing the finding of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) at the highest energies of Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). As a sign of collectively behaving matter, one would expect the elliptic flow to decrease at lower beam energies, where the QGP is not produced. However, in the recent RHIC beam energy scan, it has been found that the inclusive charged hadron elliptic flow changes relatively little in magnitude in the energies between 7.7 and 39 GeV per nucleon-nucleon collision. We study the collision energy dependence of the elliptic and triangular flow utilizing a Boltzmann + hydrodynamics hybrid model. Such a hybrid model provides a natural framework for the transition from high collision energies, where the hydrodynamical description is essential, to smaller energies, where the hadron transport dominates. This approach is thus suitable to investigate the relative importance of these two mechanisms for the production of the collective flow at different values of beam energy. Extending the examined range down to 5 GeV per nucleon-nucleon collision allows also making predictions for the CBM experiment at FAIR.

## Key claim
The experimentally observed weak energy dependence of elliptic flow \(v_2\) between 7.7 and 39 GeV per nucleon-nucleon collision can be qualitatively reproduced by a hybrid model, but only because the diminished hydrodynamic contribution at lower energies is compensated by increased flow production from the pre-equilibrium transport phase; this compensation does not apply to triangular flow \(v_3\), which decreases considerably faster and reaches zero in midcentral collisions at 5 GeV.

## Method
The authors used a (3+1)-D ideal hydrodynamics hybrid model (Petersen et al., 2008) where the initial state is generated by the UrQMD string/hadronic cascade, followed by SHASTA-algorithm ideal hydrodynamics with a chiral parity doublet equation of state including quark degrees of freedom, and finally a hadronic transport phase in UrQMD. Flow coefficients \(v_2\) and \(v_3\) were computed from final particle momentum distributions using the event plane method for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5\) to 200 GeV, with the transition from hydro to transport set at energy density \(2\epsilon_0\) where \(\epsilon_0 = 146\) MeV/fm\(^3\).

## Key numbers
- \(v_2\) in midcentral collisions (\(b = 8.2-9.4\) fm) shows hadronic rescatterings contribute about 10% to the final result.
- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \leq 7.7\) GeV, hydrodynamics produce very little elliptic flow; for midcentral collisions, \(v_2\) is in practice completely produced by transport dynamics.
- Above \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 19.6\) GeV, the hydrodynamic phase is clearly the dominant source of \(v_2\).
- \(p_T\)-integrated \(v_3\) in central collisions increases from \(\approx 0.01\) to above 0.015 with increasing collision energy.
- In midcentral collisions (\(b = 6.7-8.2\) fm), \(v_3\) rises from \(\approx 0\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5\) GeV to \(\approx 0.02\) for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \geq 27\) GeV.
- The start time of hydrodynamics \(t_{\text{start}}\) changes from 5.19 fm at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5\) GeV to 1.23 fm at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 19.6\) GeV.
- The critical energy density for particlization is \(2\epsilon_0\) with \(\epsilon_0 = 146\) MeV/fm\(^3\).

## Context
This work extends the hybrid model approach of Petersen et al. (2008) to lower beam energies, down to 5 GeV per nucleon-nucleon collision, making predictions for the CBM experiment at FAIR. It addresses the RHIC beam energy scan finding (Adamczyk, 2012) that inclusive charged hadron elliptic flow changes relatively little between 7.7 and 39 GeV, which contradicts the naive expectation that \(v_2\) should decrease without a QGP. The results show that \(v_3\) is a better signal for QGP formation than \(v_2\), but preliminary STAR data (Pandit, 2012) suggest a low-viscous state persists at lower energies more than expected, motivating further study at FAIR energies.
