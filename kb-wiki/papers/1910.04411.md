# Deformed $K^-$ nuclei in the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach

> The properties of kaonic nuclei are studied using a two-dimensional Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model with a $KN$ Skyrme force. We focus in particular on the instability of the solutions for a too strong $KN$ interaction, which determines a maximum value of the kaon binding in this approach. We then analyze the change of the deformation properties of several core-deformed nuclei caused by the added kaon, and find a shrinking of the core and in some cases a complete loss of deformation.

# Deformed \(K^-\) nuclei in the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that within the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach using a simple phenomenological \(KN\) Skyrme force, there exists a maximum value of the kaon-nucleon interaction strength beyond which the nuclear core becomes unstable against collapse; this instability limits the maximum kaon binding energy to not much more than 150 MeV for heavy nuclei with symmetric \(K^-N\) interaction, and much less for light nuclei or asymmetric interactions.

## Method

The authors employ a two-dimensional (axially deformed) Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model with the SLy4 nucleonic Skyrme force and a simple phenomenological \(KN\) Skyrme energy-density functional \(\varepsilon_{KN} = -a_0 \rho_K [(1+x_0)\rho_p + (1-x_0)\rho_n]\), where \(a_0\) controls the interaction strength and \(x_0\) controls the isospin asymmetry between \(K^-p\) and \(K^-n\) interactions. The self-consistent mean-field equations are solved in cylindrical coordinates using an axially-deformed harmonic-oscillator basis, with pairing forces included for nucleons within BCS approximation. The kaon removal energy \(B_K\) and deformation properties are studied as functions of \(a_0\) for both spherical core nuclei (\(^{16}_{\ K}\)O, \(^{40}_{\ K}\)Ca, \(^{208}_{\hspace{.7em}K}\)Pb) and deformed-core nuclei (\(^{\,8}_{K}\)Be, \(^{20}_{\ K}\)Ne, \(^{36}_{\ K}\)Ar).

## Key numbers

* Maximum stable interaction parameter \(a_0^\text{max} = 902\) MeV fm\(^3\) for \(^{208}_{\hspace{.7em}K}\)Pb with symmetric interaction (\(x_0=0\)), yielding maximum kaon removal energy \(B_K^\text{max} = 165.4\) MeV and central kaon potential \(V_K^\text{max} = 362.2\) MeV.
* For asymmetric interaction (\(x_0=1\)), instability occurs much earlier: \(a_0^\text{max} = 559\) MeV fm\(^3\) for \(^{208}_{\hspace{.7em}K}\)Pb, with \(B_K^\text{max} = 87.7\) MeV and \(V_K^\text{max} = 173.7\) MeV.
* For the light deformed nucleus \(^{\,8}_{K}\)Be with \(x_0=0\), the maximum stable interaction is \(a_0^\text{max} = 740\) MeV fm\(^3\), giving \(B_K^\text{max} = 77.9\) MeV and \(V_K^\text{max} = 291.9\) MeV; with \(x_0=1\), \(a_0^\text{max} = 330\) MeV fm\(^3\), \(B_K^\text{max} = 14.4\) MeV.
* For \(a_0 = 300\) MeV fm\(^3\) and \(x_0=0\), the kaon removal energies are: \(B_K = 21.1\) MeV for \(^{16}_{\ K}\)O, 37.4 MeV for \(^{40}_{\ K}\)Ca, 62.2 MeV for \(^{208}_{\hspace{.7em}K}\)Pb.
* For \(a_0 = 700\) MeV fm\(^3\) and \(x_0=0\), the kaon removal energies are: \(B_K = 87.3\) MeV for \(^{16}_{\ K}\)O, 107.5 MeV for \(^{40}_{\ K}\)Ca, 126.8 MeV for \(^{208}_{\hspace{.7em}K}\)Pb.
* The deformation of \(^{\,8}_K\)Be vanishes completely for \(a_0 > 253\) MeV fm\(^3\) (with \(x_0=0\)); for \(^{20}_{\ K}\)Ne, deformation vanishes for \(a_0 > 768\) MeV fm\(^3\) (with \(x_0=0\)).
* The core nucleus \(^8\)Be has deformation parameter \(\beta_2 \approx 0.63\) with most Skyrme forces.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Skyrme-Hartree-Fock study of kaonic nuclei (Zhou, 2015) by replacing a chiral \(KN\) interaction with a simpler phenomenological Skyrme force and by treating deformed nuclei in two dimensions. The results confirm the collapse instability previously observed for spherical nuclei and demonstrate that the kaon induces a shrinking of the nuclear core and a reduction of quadrupole deformation, analogous to the well-known "shrinking effect" in \(\Lambda\) hypernuclei studied by Tanida (2001) and others, but with much stronger effects due to the larger \(KN\) interaction strength. The maximum binding energies found here (up to about 165 MeV) are substantially smaller than the deepest predictions of 150–200 MeV from the phenomenological density-dependent optical potential model of Friedman (2005), and the instability limits suggest that reliable predictions for very strongly bound kaonic nuclei require better knowledge of \(NN\) and \(KN\) interactions at high density. Future work should include the imaginary part of the kaon optical potential and fit parameters to experimental data from J-PARC, FAIR, and HIAF.
