# Fluctuations in Hadronizing QGP

> The dynamical development of the cooling and hadronizing quark-gluon Plasma (QGP) is studied in a simple model assuming critical fluctuations in the QGP to Hadronic Matter (HM) and a first order transition in a small finite system. We consider an earlier determined free-energy density curve in the neighbourhood of the critical point, with two local minima corresponding to the equilibrium hadronic and QGP configurations. In this approach the divergence at e = 0 eliminates fluctuations with negative or zero energy. The barrier between the equilibrium states is obtained from an estimated value of the surface tension between the two phases. We obtain a characteristic behavior for the skewness and the kurtosis of energy density fluctuations, which can be studied via a beam energy scan program.

## Key claim

The paper models critical fluctuations during the QGP-to-hadronic-matter phase transition using a free-energy density curve based on a Laurent series (rather than the usual fourth-order polynomial) to avoid unphysical negative-energy-density states, and it predicts characteristic sign changes in skewness and kurtosis of energy density fluctuations as a function of hadronic volume abundance during hadronization.

## Method

The authors construct a free-energy density as a function of energy density using a Laurent-series expansion, with parameters fixed by the bag-model equations of state for the QGP and hadronic phases, the surface tension between phases (sigma = 0.05 GeV/fm^2), and a characteristic droplet size (xi_0 = 3 fm). From this free energy they compute the probability distribution P(e) proportional to exp(-beta Omega f(e)) for a finite QGP volume Omega, and then evaluate the skewness and kurtosis of energy density fluctuations as a function of the hadronic volume abundance r_h (the ratio of hadronic-phase probability to total probability) during a rapid, thermally equilibrated phase transition.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature used: T_c = 0.169 MeV (note: this appears to be a typo in the paper; likely 0.169 GeV is intended, but the text states "T_c=0.169MeV")
- Surface tension: sigma = 0.05 GeV/fm^2
- Characteristic droplet size: xi_0 = 3 fm
- QGP volumes considered: Omega = 10 fm^3, 50 fm^3, and 500 fm^3
- Skewness changes sign from negative to positive at approximately 80% hadronic volume abundance (r_h = 0.8) for Omega = 500 fm^3
- Kurtosis reaches a minimum at approximately 80% hadronic volume abundance (r_h = 0.8) for Omega = 500 fm^3

## Context

This work extends the earlier critical-fluctuation model of Csernai and Neda (1994) by computing the skewness and kurtosis of energy density fluctuations during the QGP-to-hadronic-matter phase transition, quantities that can be compared with experimental data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program. The model uses a Laurent-series free energy to avoid the unphysical negative-energy-density states that would arise from the standard fourth-order polynomial Landau-Ginzburg approach, and it predicts asymmetric behavior in the higher moments due to the large energy-density difference between the two phases (e_q >> e_h). The results are intended to be implemented in hybrid or multi-module models (e.g., PIC hydro coupled to the PACIAE cascade, as in Yun (2010)) and to be tested against mixed-event methods for isolating critical fluctuations from collective flow effects.
