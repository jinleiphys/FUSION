# Charmonium Suppression with cc~ Dissociation by Strings

> We study the production of cc~ pairs in nuclear reactions at SPS energies within the covariant transport approach HSD. The production of cc~ is treated perturbatively employing experimental cross sections while the interactions of cc~ pairs with baryons are included by conventional cascade-type two-body collisions. Adopting 6 mb for the cc~-baryon cross sections the data on J/Ψsuppression in p+A reactions are reproduced in line with calculations based on the Glauber model. Additionally the dissociation of the cc~ pairs by strings is included in a purely geometrical way. We find good agreement with experimental data from the NA38 and NA50 collaboration with an estimate for the string radius of ~0.2-0.3 fm.

# Charmonium Suppression with $c\bar{c}$ Dissociation by Strings

## Key claim

The observed suppression of $J/\Psi$ in heavy-ion collisions at SPS energies can be explained by dissociation of $c\bar{c}$ pairs in the color electric fields of strings during the prehadronic phase, without invoking quark-gluon plasma formation, provided the string radius is approximately 0.2–0.3 fm. The authors note that this radius should be seen as a lower bound, and that adding the $c\bar{c}$ radius would give an effective string radius of approximately 0.4–0.5 fm.

## Method

The authors use the covariant transport approach HSD (Hadron String Dynamics) in cascade mode, propagating baryons, mesons, and explicitly including the dynamical evolution of strings formed via the FRITIOF model. $c\bar{c}$ pair production is treated perturbatively using experimental cross sections with $A_P \times A_T$ scaling, and the pairs are propagated as free particles through the hadronic background; dissociation occurs either by conventional two-body collisions with baryons (using a 6 mb cross section for the $c\bar{c}$-baryon interaction) or by a purely geometrical condition: a $c\bar{c}$ state dissociates immediately whenever its center of mass lies inside the cylindrical volume of a string (radius $R_S$, length given by the string evolution formula).

## Key numbers

- $c\bar{c}$-baryon absorption cross section: 6 mb, adopted for the color-octet $c\bar{c}$-g Fock state with lifetime $\tau = 0.3$ fm/c
- String radius giving best fit to NA38 and NA50 data: $R_S \approx 0.2$ fm
- With $R_S = 0.2$ fm in central Pb+Pb collisions: total $J/\Psi$ absorption probability $A_{tot} = 0.74$, of which $A_B = 0.44$ from baryons and $A_S = 0.30$ from strings (i.e., 40% of absorbed $J/\Psi$'s are dissociated by strings)
- String formation time: $\tau_F = 0.8$ fm/c
- In central Pb+Pb at 160 AGeV: number of strings $N_S \sim 420$, average string length $\langle l \rangle \sim 0.5$ fm
- String volume for $R_S = 0.3$ fm: $V_s \approx 65$ fm$^3$; for $R_S = 0.4$ fm: $V_s \approx 115$ fm$^3$; total central cylinder volume (1 fm thick, radius of Pb nucleus $R_{Pb} = 6.6$ fm): $V_t \approx 130$ fm$^3$
- For p+U with $R_S = 0.4$ fm, only 2% of $J/\Psi$ are absorbed by strings

## Context

This work extends the hadronic absorption scenario for $J/\Psi$ suppression studied by Cassing et al. (1997) within the same HSD framework, which had attributed suppression to absorption by baryons and comoving mesons. The authors instead propose an alternative mechanism: dissociation of $c\bar{c}$ pairs by the intense color electric fields inside strings during the prehadronic phase, motivated by the semiclassical flux-tube calculations of Loh et al. (1997) using the Friedberg-Lee color dielectric Lagrangian. The approach reproduces p+A data (consistent with Glauber model calculations) and the NA38/NA50 data for S+U and Pb+Pb, but cannot reproduce the abrupt jump in suppression near $E_T \approx 50$ GeV suggested by NA50 data, which the authors note might indicate a deconfined QGP phase as discussed by Satz (1998). The work enables further study of prehadronic dissociation mechanisms and calls for better theoretical foundations of charmonium dissociation in color electric fields.
