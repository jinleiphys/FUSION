# Distribution of spectral widths and preponderance of spin-0 ground states in nuclei

> We use a single j-shell model with random two-body interactions to derive closed expressions for the distribution of and the correlations between spectral widths of different spins. This task is facilitated by introducing two-body operators whose squared spectral widths sum up to the squared spectral width of the random Hamiltonian. The spin-0 width is characterized by a relatively large average value and small fluctuations while the width of maximum spin has the largest average and the largest fluctuations. The approximate proportionality between widths and spectral radii explains the preponderance of spin-0 ground states.

## Key claim

The preponderance of spin-0 ground states in the nuclear shell model with random two-body interactions can be explained by the distribution of and correlations between spectral widths of different spins, specifically because spin-0 has a relatively large average width with small fluctuations while maximum spin has the largest average and largest fluctuations, and spectral widths are approximately proportional to spectral radii.

## Method

The authors studied a single j-shell model with n = 6 fermions in a shell with spin j = 19/2, using a two-body random ensemble (TBRE) where the two-body matrix elements are uncorrelated Gaussian-distributed random variables with zero mean and unit variance. They derived closed expressions for the probability distribution functions P_J(σ) of spectral widths and for the correlation probabilities p(J,J') that spin J has a larger width than spin J', by diagonalizing the overlap matrix S_αβ(J) = d_J^{-1} Tr(C_α(J) C_β(J)) and introducing orthogonal two-body operators B_α(J). The spectral radius R_J was related to the spectral width σ_J through a scaling factor r_J, and the probability that r_J σ_J is maximal was compared to the probability that spin J is the ground state.

## Key numbers

- For 6 fermions in a j = 19/2 shell, there are a = 10 two-body matrix elements (TBME) since two fermions can have spins j_α = 0, 2, 4, ..., 2j-1.

## Context

This work extends the finding of Johnson, Bertsch and Dean (1998) that for even-even nuclei, an ensemble of nuclear shell-model Hamiltonians with random two-body interactions is likely to yield a spin-zero ground state, a result that was surprising because the probability for a spin-zero ground state was much larger than the fraction of spin-zero states in the model space. Previous approaches based on ensemble-averaged spectral widths failed to provide an explanation for a single j-shell (as noted in reviews by Zhao, Arima, and Yoshinaga (2004) and by Zelevinsky and Volya (2004)), while Mulhall et al. (2000) found that spectral centroids form a band or inverted band depending on the sign of the effective moment of inertia. The present work enables a semi-quantitative understanding of spin-0 ground state preponderance through the distribution and correlations of spectral widths, and poses the analytical problem of deriving the eigenvalues s_α(J) using group-theoretical techniques.
