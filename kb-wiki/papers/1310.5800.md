# Fermionic Functional Renormalization Group Approach to Superfluid Phase Transition

> Fermionic functional renormalization group (FRG) is applied to describe the superfluid phase transition of the two-component fermionic system with attractive contact interaction. Connection between the fermionic FRG approach and the conventional Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) theory with Gorkov and Melik-Barkhudarov (GMB) correction are clarified in details in the weak coupling region by using the renormalization group flow of the fermionic four-point vertex with particle-particle and particle-hole scattering contributions. To go beyond the BCS+GMB theory, coupled FRG flow equations of the fermion self-energy and the four-point vertex are studied under an Ansatz concerning their frequency/momentum dependence. We found that the fermion self-energy turns out to be substantial even in the weak couping region, and the frequency dependence of the four-point vertex is essential to obtain the correct asymptotic-ultraviolet behavior of the flow for the self-energy. The superfluid transition temperature and the associated chemical potential are calculated in the region of negative scattering lengths.

## Key claim

The fermionic functional renormalization group (f-FRG) approach, without introducing auxiliary bosonic fields, can reproduce the BCS+GMB result for the superfluid transition temperature in the weak coupling limit and, by including the fermion self-energy and frequency dependence of the four-point vertex, can go beyond BCS+GMB theory; however, the analysis is restricted to negative scattering lengths and the case of positive scattering lengths is not accessible at the level of approximations used.

## Method

The authors apply a fermionic FRG method to a two-component non-relativistic fermion system with an attractive contact interaction, truncating the vertex expansion at the four-point level. They derive coupled flow equations for the fermion self-energy and the four-point vertex, using Litim's optimized regulator with a self-energy term, and solve these equations numerically to obtain the superfluid transition temperature and chemical potential. The critical temperature is determined via the Thouless criterion, i.e., the divergence of the fermion-fermion scattering matrix at zero total momentum.

## Key numbers

- The BCS critical temperature in the weak coupling limit is given by \( T_c^{\mathrm{BCS}}/\varepsilon_F = (8/\pi) e^{\gamma_E - 2} e^{-\pi/(2k_F|a_s|)} \).
- The GMB correction reduces the BCS critical temperature by a factor of \( (4e)^{-1/3} \), i.e., \( T_c^{\mathrm{GMB}}/\varepsilon_F = (4e)^{-1/3} \cdot T_c^{\mathrm{BCS}}/\varepsilon_F \).
- The particle-hole contribution shifts the inverse scattering length by \( 1/a_s^{\mathrm{eff}} = 1/a_s - (2k_F/\pi)(1+\ln 4)/3 \).
- For asymptotically large \( k \), the four-point vertex behaves as \( \Gamma_k^{(4)}(p) \simeq -(3\pi^2)/(m(k - 3\pi/(4a_s))) \).
- The self-energy at large \( k \) decreases as \( \sigma_k \simeq \sqrt{2m}(\mu+\sigma_0)^{3/2} / (2k(1 - 3\pi/(8k a_s))) \).

## Context

This work extends the BCS theory and its Gorkov-Melik-Barkhudarov (GMB) correction (Gorkov 1961, PhysRevLett.85.2418) by using a fermionic FRG approach that includes self-energy effects and frequency-dependent vertex corrections. It builds on prior FRG studies of the BCS-BEC crossover (Birse 2005, PhysRevB.78.174528) and Shankar's RG analysis of interacting fermions (Shankar 1994). The method enables systematic and unbiased study of interacting fermions without the ambiguities of introducing auxiliary bosonic fields, and it provides a foundation for future calculations in the unitary regime and for positive scattering lengths.
