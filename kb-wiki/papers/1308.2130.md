# Multi-pomeron repulsion and the Neutron-star mass

> A multi-pomeron exchange potential (MPP) is proposed as a model for the three-body repulsion indicated in neutron-star matter, which works universally among three- and four-baryons. Its strength is determined by analyzing the nucleus-nucleus scattering with the G-matrix folding model. The EoS in neutron matter is obtained including the MPP contribution. The neutron-star mass is calculated by solving the TOV equation. The maximum mass is obtained to be larger than the observed one $1.97 M_{solar}$ on the basis of the experimental data.

# Multi-pomeron repulsion and the Neutron-star mass

## Key claim

The multi-pomeron exchange potential (MPP) model for universal three-baryon repulsion, with strengths determined from nucleus-nucleus scattering data, yields a neutron-star equation of state stiff enough to produce maximum masses exceeding the observed 1.97 solar masses, but only if the quartic pomeron term is included; the triple-pomeron term alone is insufficient to reach that mass.

## Method

The authors introduce a multi-pomeron exchange potential (MPP) as a model for universal three- and four-baryon repulsion, with coupling constants for triple and quartic pomeron vertices. They embed MPP into the Extended Soft Core (ESC08c) baryon-baryon interaction model and construct G-matrix folding potentials for nucleus-nucleus scattering. The MPP strengths and a phenomenological three-nucleon attraction (TNA) term are adjusted to simultaneously reproduce the differential cross section of 16O+16O elastic scattering at E/A=70 MeV and the nuclear saturation energy of symmetric matter. The resulting equation of state for pure neutron matter is then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation for neutron-star masses.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron-star mass for MP1a (including both triple and quartic pomeron) exceeds the observed 1.97 solar masses; for MP2a (triple pomeron only) the maximum mass is substantially smaller.
- Incompressibility K = 280 MeV for MP1a and 270 MeV for MP2a at the saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter.
- Symmetry energy at normal density: 32.5 MeV (MP0), 33.4 MeV (MP1a), 33.5 MeV (MP2a); slope parameter L = 71, 72, 73 MeV respectively.
- MPP coupling constants: g_P^(3) = 2.34, g_P^(4) = 30.0 for MP1a; g_P^(3) = 2.94, g_P^(4) = 0.0 for MP2a; TNA strength V^0_TNA = -36 MeV.
- Saturation energy per nucleon approximately -16 MeV at normal density.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Nishizaki, Takatsuka, and Yamamoto (NYT) who proposed that three-nucleon repulsion (TNR) works universally for YN and YY as well as NN interactions to prevent softening of the equation of state by hyperon mixing. The MPP model is developed within the ESC08c framework of Rijken and collaborators. The folding-model analysis follows the method of Furumoto, Sakuragi, and Yamamoto (FSY) who used G-matrix folding potentials to study TNR effects in nucleus-nucleus scattering. The results are compared to the Urbana IX (UIX) three-neutron interaction used by Gandolfi et al. (2012), which also yields a stiff enough equation of state for the 1.97 solar mass neutron star J1614-2230 observed by Demorest et al. (2010). The present work enables future studies of hypernuclear properties and neutron-star matter with hyperon mixing, where the universal MPP repulsion is expected to prevent the softening that plagues models without three-body forces.
