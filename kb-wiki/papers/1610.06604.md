# Production of exotic charmonium in $γγ$ interactions at hadronic colliders

> In this paper we investigate the Exotic Charmonium (EC) production in $γγ$ interactions present in proton-proton, proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energies as well as for the proposed energies of the Future Circular Collider (FCC). Our results demonstrate that the experimental study of these processes is feasible and can be used to constrain the theoretical decay widths and shed some light on the configuration of the considered multiquark states.

## Key claim

The experimental study of exotic charmonium production via two-photon fusion in ultra-peripheral collisions at the LHC and FCC is feasible and can be used to constrain theoretical decay widths, but the cross sections depend strongly on whether the exotic state is a compact tetraquark or a loosely bound meson molecule; the paper presents cross sections assuming a molecular configuration, noting that tetraquark cross sections would be much larger.

## Method

The authors use the equivalent photon approximation to compute the cross section for the process \(h_1 h_2 \rightarrow h_1 \otimes R \otimes h_2\) in ultra-peripheral collisions, where \(R\) is an exotic charmonium state produced by \(\gamma\gamma\) fusion. The cross section is obtained by integrating the product of the equivalent photon spectra of the two hadrons, the \(\gamma\gamma \rightarrow R\) cross section (given by the Low formula in terms of the two-photon decay width and spin of the resonance), and an absorption factor that excludes hadronic overlap. The photon spectra are calculated using either a monopole form factor for nuclei, a dipole form factor for protons, or a point-like (\(F=1\)) approximation to estimate theoretical uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- For Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 5.5\) TeV, the predicted cross sections for exotic charmonium states range from approximately 5 \(\mu\)b (X(3915), 0\(^{++}\)) to 142 \(\mu\)b (Y\(_p\)(3912), 2\(^{++}\)) using the monopole form factor.
- For p-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 8.8\) TeV, cross sections range from about 2.5 nb (X(3915), 0\(^{++}\)) to 48 nb (Y\(_p\)(3912), 2\(^{++}\)) using the monopole form factor.
- For pp collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 14\) TeV, cross sections range from about 0.84 pb (X(3915), 0\(^{++}\)) to 16.3 pb (Y\(_p\)(3912), 2\(^{++}\)) using the monopole form factor.
- The cross sections for Pb-Pb collisions at the FCC energy (\(\sqrt{s} = 39\) TeV) are approximately one order of magnitude larger than at the LHC (\(\sqrt{s} = 5.5\) TeV); for example, the X(3940) 2\(^{++}\) cross section increases from 48.4 \(\mu\)b to 167.4 \(\mu\)b (monopole form factor).
- The predictions for the X(3940) production cross section differ by a factor of 4 depending on whether the spin is assumed to be 0\(^{++}\) or 2\(^{++}\) (e.g., 11.8 \(\mu\)b vs. 48.4 \(\mu\)b in Pb-Pb at 5.5 TeV with monopole form factor).
- The predictions for X(3915) (0\(^{++}\)) and Y\(_p\)(3912) (2\(^{++}\)) production in Pb-Pb at 5.5 TeV differ by a factor of about 20 (7.3 \(\mu\)b vs. 142.2 \(\mu\)b with monopole form factor).

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Bertulani et al. (2003) for nucleus-nucleus collisions and by Vic Wer et al. (2009) for proton-proton collisions, updating them to include pp, pA, and AA collisions at LHC and FCC energies, and focusing specifically on photon-photon production of the exotic charmonium states X(3915), Z(3930), and X(4160) in addition to X(3940) and X(4140). The results enable experimental searches at the LHC and FCC that could discriminate between molecular and tetraquark configurations of these states, complementing earlier work by Vic Mar et al. (2015) on \(\gamma h\) interactions which produce states in the forward region with larger background, whereas \(\gamma\gamma\) production yields central rapidities accessible to current LHC detectors.
