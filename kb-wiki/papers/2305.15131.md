# Systematic shell-model study for structure and isomeric states in $^{200-210}$Po isotopes

> We report systematic large-scale shell-model calculation for Po isotopes with $A=$ 200 to 210. We have performed calculations using KHH7B interaction in the model space $Z$ = 58-114 and $N$ = 100-164 around doubly-magic $^{208}$Pb. We allow valence neutrons to occupy in the $1f_{5/2}$, $2p_{3/2}$, $2p_{1/2}$, and $0i_{13/2}$ orbitals, while two valence protons beyond $Z=82$ are occupied in $0h_{9/2}$, $1f_{7/2}$ and $0i_{13/2}$ orbitals. The calculated energies and electromagnetic properties are compared with the available experimental data and predicted where experimental data are not available. We have also reported shell-model results for different isomeric states of these nuclei.

## Key claim

The KHH7B shell-model interaction reproduces the low-lying energy levels and electromagnetic properties of $^{200-210}$Po isotopes reasonably well, but high-lying states (above approximately 1.5-2.0 MeV) are systematically compressed, suggesting that core-excitation across the $Z=82$ and $N=126$ shell closures is needed for a complete description.

## Method

Large-scale shell-model calculations were performed using the KHH7B effective interaction in the model space $Z=58-114$ and $N=100-164$ around doubly-magic $^{208}$Pb, with valence neutrons occupying the $1f_{5/2}$, $2p_{3/2}$, $2p_{1/2}$, and $0i_{13/2}$ orbitals and two valence protons beyond $Z=82$ occupying the $0h_{9/2}$, $1f_{7/2}$, and $0i_{13/2}$ orbitals. The matrices were diagonalized using the NUSHELLX and KSHELL codes, with the largest dimension (for $^{200}$Po ground state) reaching $1.3 \times 10^9$. For $^{210}$Po, calculations were performed both without and with core-excitation (allowing two neutrons across the $N=126$ shell closure).

## Key numbers

- $^{200}$Po: $B(E2; 2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1) = 5.5$ W.u. (SM) vs. $6.10(34)$ W.u. (experiment)
- $^{202}$Po: $B(E2; 2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1) = 5.7$ W.u. (SM) vs. $6.36(172)$ W.u. (experiment)
- $^{204}$Po: $B(E2; 2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1) = 5.5$ W.u. (SM) vs. $\geq 8.7$ W.u. (experiment)
- $^{206}$Po: $B(E2; 8^+_1 \rightarrow 6^+_1) = 1.9$ W.u. (SM) vs. $2.45(16)$ W.u. (experiment)
- $^{208}$Po: $B(E2; 2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1) = 3.7$ W.u. (SM) vs. $3.4(13)$ W.u. (experiment)
- $^{210}$Po: $B(E2; 2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1) = 3.7$ W.u. (SM) vs. $3.4(13)$ W.u. (experiment)
- $^{210}$Po: $B(E2; 11^-_1 \rightarrow 9^-_1) = 2.59 \times 10^{-4}$ W.u. (SM) vs. $0.0052(7)$ W.u. (experiment)
- $^{200}$Po: yrast $0^+$, $2^+$, $4^+$, $6^+$, $8^+$ states formed from $\pi(h_{9/2}^2)$ configuration with probabilities 18.19%, 17.34%, 24.92%, 24.73%, 24.81% respectively
- $^{210}$Po: with core-excitation, the $23^-_1$ state calculated at 8.725 MeV matches the tentative experimental (23) state at 8.893 MeV

## Context

This work extends systematic shell-model studies of polonium isotopes using the KHH7B interaction, following earlier calculations by McGrory and Kuo (1975) for $^{210}$Po and neighboring nuclei, and more recent work by Wilson, Berry, Wahid, and Anil using the same interaction. The paper provides predictions for energy levels and electromagnetic properties where experimental data are unavailable, particularly for $^{200-210}$Po. The results enable future experimental studies at facilities such as RIKEN, GSI/FAIR, and CERN to test the predicted level schemes and B(E2) values, and highlight the need for core-excitation treatments to describe high-spin states above approximately 2 MeV excitation energy.
