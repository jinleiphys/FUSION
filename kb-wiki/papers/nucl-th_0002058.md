# Radiative Corrections to Electron-Proton Scattering

> The radiative corrections to elastic electron-proton scattering are analyzed in a hadronic model including the finite size of the nucleon. For initial electron energies above 8 GeV and large scattering angles, the proton vertex correction in this model increases by at least two percent the overall factor by which the one-photon exchange (Rosenbluth) cross section must be multiplied. The contribution of soft photon emission is calculated exactly. Comparison is made with the generally used expressions previously obtained by Mo and Tsai. Results are presented for some kinematics at high momentum transfer.

## Key claim

For initial electron energies above 8 GeV and large scattering angles, including the finite size of the nucleon via a hadronic model increases the proton vertex correction by at least two percent in the overall factor multiplying the one-photon exchange (Rosenbluth) cross section, relative to the standard Mo and Tsai treatment that neglects proton structure.

## Method

The authors calculated radiative corrections to elastic electron-proton scattering to order α relative to the Rosenbluth cross section using a hadronic model where the proton current includes form factors of monopole or dipole form with a cutoff Λ ≈ 1 GeV/c. They evaluated the elastic cross section contributions from the proton vertex correction (including form factors and no soft-photon approximation for the virtual photon), the electron vertex correction, box and crossed-box diagrams (with a less drastic soft-photon approximation than Mo and Tsai, keeping exact denominators), and vacuum polarization. The inelastic (soft bremsstrahlung) cross section was calculated exactly using closed-form integrals from t'Hooft and Veltman, without the approximations used in prior work.

## Key numbers

- For initial electron energies above 8 GeV and large scattering angles, the proton vertex correction in the hadronic model increases the overall factor multiplying the Rosenbluth cross section by at least two percent.
- Radiative corrections can be as large as 30% of the uncorrected cross section depending on experimental conditions (beam energy, momentum transfer, detector resolution).
- To obtain cross sections accurate to 1%, the radiative correction must be known to 3%.
- The fine structure constant used is α = e²/4π = 1/137.036.
- The form factor cutoff Λ is of the order of 1 GeV/c.
- The high-energy approximation neglects terms of relative orders m²/ε², m²/(-q²), and m²/M².

## Context

This work extends the standard radiative correction formalism of Mo and Tsai (1969) by including the internal structure of the nucleon through form factors, which those earlier calculations neglected by setting the photon momentum square k²=0 in the proton form factor. The exact evaluation of soft photon emission integrals (using closed forms from t'Hooft and Veltman) and the less drastic approximation in box diagrams (keeping exact denominators) represent improvements over the Mo and Tsai treatment. The results enable more accurate analysis of electron scattering experiments at facilities like MAMI and TJNAF, particularly at high momentum transfer where nucleon structure effects become significant.
