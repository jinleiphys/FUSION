# Asymptotic normalization coefficients (nuclear vertex constants) for $p+^7Be\to ^8B$ and the direct $^7Be(p,γ)^8B$ astrophysical S-factors at solar energies

> A new analysis of the precise experimental astrophysical S-factors for the direct capture $^7Be(p,γ)$ $^8B$ reaction [A.J.Junghans et al.Phys.Rev. C 68 (2003) 065803 and L.T. Baby et al. Phys.Rev. C 67 (2003) 065805] is carried out based on the modified two - body potential approach in which the direct astrophysical S-factor, $ {\rm S_{17}(E)}$, is expressed in terms of the asymptotic normalization constants for $p+^7Be\to ^8B$ and two additional conditions are involved to verify the peripheral character of the reaction under consideration. The Woods-Saxon potential form is used for the bound ($p+^7Be$)- state wave function and for the $p^7Be$- scattering wave function. New estimates are obtained for the $^{\glqq}$indirectly measured\grqq values of the asymptotic normalization constants (the nuclear vertex constants) for the $p+^7Be\to ^8B$ and $S_{17}(E)$ at E$\le$ 115 keV, including $E$=0. These values of $S_{17}(E)$ and asymptotic normalization constants have been used for getting information about the $^{\glqq}$indirectly measured\grqq values of the $s$ wave average scattering length and the $p$ wave effective range parameters for $p^7Be$- scattering.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) for \(p+^7Be\to ^8B\) can be extracted directly from precise experimental astrophysical S-factors for the \(^7Be(p,\gamma)^8B\) reaction, with ambiguities from the choice of Woods-Saxon geometric parameters reduced to within experimental errors, provided the reaction is verified to be peripheral through two specific conditions.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed the experimental astrophysical S-factors from Junghans et al. (2003) and Baby et al. (2003) for the \(^7Be(p,\gamma)^8B\) reaction at energies 116–400 keV and 1000–1200 keV using a modified two-body potential approach. They expressed the astrophysical S-factor \(S_{17}(E)\) in terms of the ANC for \(p+^7Be\to ^8B\) and tested two conditions for peripheral character: that the function \(\mathcal{R}_{l_f}(E,C^{(sp)}_{l_fj_f})\) is independent of the single-particle ANC \(C^{(sp)}\) for fixed energy, and that the extracted \(C^2\) is constant across energies. Woods-Saxon potentials with varying radius \(r_o\) (0.90–1.50 fm) and diffuseness \(a\) (0.52–0.76 fm) were used for both bound and scattering wave functions, with depths adjusted to fit the \(^8B\) binding energy.

## Key numbers

* Weighted mean ANC for \(p+^7Be\to ^8B\): \((C^{exp})^2 = 0.628 \pm 0.017\) fm\(^{-1}\) (uncertainty 2.7%), derived from all experimental data points.
* Corresponding nuclear vertex constant: \(|G|_{exp}^2 = 0.114 \pm 0.003\) fm.
* Recommended zero-energy astrophysical S-factor: \(S_{17}(0) = 23.40 \pm 0.63\) eVb.
* For the \(p_{3/2}\) component: \((C^{exp}_{p_{3/2}})^2 = 0.558 \pm 0.015\) fm\(^{-1}\) and \(|G_{p_{3/2}}|_{exp}^2 = 0.0995 \pm 0.0027\) fm, assuming \(\lambda_C = 0.125\).
* For the \(p_{1/2}\) component: \((C^{exp}_{p_{1/2}})^2 = 0.070 \pm 0.002\) fm\(^{-1}\) and \(|G_{p_{1/2}}|_{exp}^2 = 0.0145 \pm 0.0004\) fm, assuming \(\lambda_C = 0.125\).
* The conditions for peripheral character were satisfied within \(\pm 1.1\%\) for the energy ranges studied, with residual \((r_o,a)\)-dependence of \(\tilde{S}(E)\) up to \(\pm 1.0\%\) for each fixed \(C^{(sp)}\).

## Context

This work extends the modified two-body potential approach previously applied by Igamov and Yarmukhamedov (2007) to the \(t(\alpha,\gamma)^7Li\) reaction, now applied to the \(^7Be(p,\gamma)^8B\) reaction of solar neutrino relevance. The extracted ANC value \((C^{exp})^2 = 0.628 \pm 0.017\) fm\(^{-1}\) differs noticeably from earlier values obtained from peripheral proton transfer reactions using the modified DWBA approach by Mukhamedzhanov et al. (2001) and Trache et al. (2006), which gave \(0.462 \pm 0.072\) fm\(^{-1}\) and \(0.466 \pm 0.049\) fm\(^{-1}\), respectively, as well as from microscopic cluster-model calculations by Descouvemont (2004) which gave \(C^2 = 0.668\) fm\(^{-1}\) for the Minnesota potential. The result enables improved extrapolation of the astrophysical S-factor to solar energies (below 115 keV) and provides an experimentally determined ANC that can serve as a nuclear observable for \(^8B\).
