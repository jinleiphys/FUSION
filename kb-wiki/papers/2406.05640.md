# Spectroscopic factors of resonance states with the Gamow shell model

> We provide an investigation of the spectroscopic factor of resonance states in $A =5-8$ nuclei, utilizing the Gamow shell model (GSM). Within the GSM, the configuration mixing is taken into account exactly with the shell model framework, and the continuum coupling is addressed via the complex-energy Berggren ensemble, which treats bound, resonance, and non-resonant continuum single-particle states on an equal footing. As a result, both the configuration mixing and continuum coupling are meticulously considered in the GSM. We first calculate the low-lying states of helium isotopes and isotones with the GSM, and the results are compared with that of \textit{ab initio} no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations. The results indicate that GSM can reproduce the low-lying resonance states more accurately than the no-core shell model. Following this, we delve into the spectroscopic factors of the resonance states as computed through both GSM and NCSM, concurrently conducting systematic calculations of overlap functions pertinent to these resonance states. Finally, the calculated overlap function and spectroscopic factor of $^6$He$(0_1^+)$ $\otimes νp_{3/2} \to $ $^7$He$(3/2_1^-)$ with GSM are compared with the results from \textit{ab initio} NCSM, variational Monte Carlo, and Green's function Monte Carlo calculations, as well as avaliable experimental data. The results assert that wave function asymptotes can only be reproduced in GSM, where resonance and continuum coupling are precisely addressed.

## Key claim

The Gamow shell model (GSM) reproduces low-lying resonance states and their spectroscopic factors more accurately than the no-core shell model (NCSM) for A=5-8 nuclei, but only when the resonance or continuum coupling is significant; for bound or narrow resonance states, both models yield nearly identical spectroscopic factors.

## Method

The authors performed Gamow shell model calculations using a Woods-Saxon one-body potential with an inert 4He core and the Furutani-Horiuchi-Tamagaki (FHT) residual two-body interaction, employing the complex-energy Berggren basis that treats bound, resonance, and non-resonant continuum states on equal footing. For comparison, they performed no-core shell model calculations using the Daejeon16 two-body interaction with harmonic oscillator basis and maximal truncations of Nmax=12 for A=5-6 and Nmax=10 for A=7-8. Spectroscopic factors and overlap functions were computed for p3/2 and p1/2 partial waves in neutron-rich helium isotopes and their proton-rich isotones across A=5-8 systems.

## Key numbers

- 5He(1/2-) resonance width: GSM gives 5959 keV, experimental value is 5570 keV
- 7He(5/2-) excited state width: GSM gives 1831 keV, experimental value is 1990 keV
- Spectroscopic factor for 6He(0_1+) ⊗ νp3/2 → 7He(3/2_1-): GSM gives 0.487+i0.101, NCSM gives 0.455
- Spectroscopic factor for 5Li(3/2_1-) ⊗ πp3/2 → 6Be(0_1+): GSM gives 2.421-i0.353, NCSM gives 1.540
- Spectroscopic factor for 7He(3/2_1-) ⊗ νp3/2 → 8He(0_1+): GSM gives 3.222-i0.664, NCSM gives 3.384
- Spectroscopic factor for 6He(2_1+) ⊗ νp3/2 → 7He(5/2_1-): GSM gives 0.140-i0.070, NCSM gives 0.208
- Spectroscopic factor for 7B(3/2_1-) ⊗ πp3/2 → 8C(0_1+): GSM gives 3.788+i0.112, NCSM gives 3.340
- Difference between calculated and experimental widths for helium isotopes and isotones is smaller than 200 keV

## Context

This work extends the application of the Gamow shell model to spectroscopic factors and overlap functions of resonance states, building on prior GSM studies of unbound states in drip-line nuclei such as 4n resonance systems (PhysRevC.100.054313), 28O ground-state energy (PhysRevC.103.034305), and 18Mg two-proton decay (PhysRevC.103.044319, PhysRevLett.127.262502). The results are compared with ab initio no-core shell model calculations using the Daejeon16 interaction (Shirokov 2016), as well as variational Monte Carlo and Green's function Monte Carlo calculations (PhysRevC.84.024319). The work demonstrates that continuum coupling, which is absent in NCSM due to its harmonic oscillator basis, is essential for reproducing the asymptotic behavior of wave functions in unbound nuclei and for describing isospin symmetry breaking in mirror nuclei. This enables more reliable predictions of spectroscopic factors for reactions involving exotic nuclei near the drip line.
