# $A=4-7$ $Ξ$ hypernuclei based on interactions from chiral effective field theory

> We investigate the existence of bound $Ξ$ \break states in systems with $A=4-7$ baryons using the Jacobi NCSM approach in combination with chiral NN and $Ξ$N interactions. We find three shallow bound states for the NNN$Ξ$ system (with $(J^π,T)=(1^+,0)$, $(0^+,1)$ and $(1^+,1)$) with quite similar binding energies. The $^5_Ξ\mathrm{H}(\frac{1}{2}^+,\frac{1}{2})$ and $^7_Ξ\mathrm{H}(\frac{1}{2}^+,\frac{3}{2})$ hypernuclei are also clearly bound with respect to the thresholds $^4\mathrm{He} + Ξ$ and $^6\mathrm{He} +Ξ$, respectively. The binding of all these $Ξ$ systems is predominantly due to the attraction of the chiral $Ξ$N potential in the $^{33}S_1$ channel. A perturbative estimation suggests that the decay widths of all the observed states could be rather small.

## Key claim

The paper claims that several light Ξ hypernuclei (A=4-7) are bound when using chiral effective field theory interactions, but the binding is shallow and depends on the specific isospin and angular momentum channels, with the NNNΞ system showing three bound states while the (0⁺,0) state is unbound.

## Method

The authors employed the Jacobi no-core shell model (J-NCSM) with chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions at N⁴LO+ and ΞN interactions at NLO from chiral effective field theory, both evolved via similarity renormalization group (SRG) transformations. The explicit coupling of ΞN to ΛΛ was omitted and instead incorporated by re-adjusting the strength of the V_{ΞN-ΞN} potential in the ¹¹S₀ channel, while couplings to ΛΣ and ΣΣ channels were retained. Binding energies were extrapolated to infinite model spaces using a two-step procedure involving minimization over harmonic oscillator frequency and exponential fits over increasing model space sizes.

## Key numbers

* NNNΞ (1⁺,0) state: Ξ separation energy B_Ξ = 0.48 ± 0.01 MeV, estimated decay width Γ = 0.74 MeV
* NNNΞ (0⁺,1) state: B_Ξ = 0.71 ± 0.08 MeV, Γ = 0.2 MeV
* NNNΞ (1⁺,1) state: B_Ξ = 0.64 ± 0.11 MeV, Γ = 0.01 MeV
* ⁵_ΞH (1/2⁺,1/2): B_Ξ = 2.16 ± 0.10 MeV, Γ = 0.19 MeV (measured with respect to ⁴He + Ξ threshold)
* ⁷_ΞH (1/2⁺,3/2): B_Ξ = 3.50 ± 0.39 MeV, Γ = 0.2 MeV (measured with respect to ⁶He + Ξ threshold)
* The ΞN scattering length in the ¹¹S₀ channel after re-adjustment: a_{ΞN}(¹¹S₀) = -7.00 fm
* The Ξ single-particle potential at nuclear matter saturation density: U_Ξ(k=0) ≈ -9 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of light Ξ hypernuclei using chiral EFT interactions, following calculations by Miyagawa (2021) which found ³_ΞH unbound with the same chiral potential, and by Hiyama (2019) who studied A=3-4 systems using the Nijmegen ESC08c and HAL QCD potentials. The results differ substantially from the ESC08c predictions (which yield deeply bound states due to strong attraction in the ³³S₁ channel) but show some similarity to HAL QCD results, though with additional bound states in the (0⁺,1) and (1⁺,1) channels. The findings provide predictions for upcoming experiments at J-PARC targeting ⁷_ΞH via the ⁷Li(K⁻,K⁺) reaction, and enable future studies incorporating isospin breaking effects and Coulomb interactions.
