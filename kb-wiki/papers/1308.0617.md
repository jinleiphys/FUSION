# Heavy quark dynamics and hadronization in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions: collisional versus radiative energy loss

> We study the dynamics of energy loss and flow of heavy quarks produced in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions within the framework of a Langevin equation coupled to a (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model that simulates the space-time evolution of the produced hot and dense QCD matter. The classical Langevin approach is improved such that, apart from quasi-elastic scatterings, radiative energy loss is incorporated by treating gluon radiation as an additional force term. The hadronization of emitted heavy quarks is simulated via a hybrid fragmentation plus recombination model. Our calculation shows significant contribution from gluon radiation to heavy quark energy loss at high energies, and we find the recombination mechanism is important for heavy flavor meson production at intermediate energies. We present numerical results for the nuclear modification and elliptic flow of D mesons, which are consistent with measurements at both LHC and RHIC; predictions for B mesons are also provided.

## Key claim

The authors claim that a modified Langevin equation incorporating both collisional and radiative energy loss, combined with a hybrid fragmentation-plus-recombination hadronization model, can describe D meson nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow data at both LHC and RHIC energies, but the agreement with D meson elliptic flow at LHC is less satisfactory and may require event-by-event hydrodynamic initial conditions.

## Method

The authors simulate heavy quark evolution using a Langevin equation that includes a drag force, a thermal random force, and a recoil force from medium-induced gluon radiation, with the gluon radiation spectrum taken from a higher-twist calculation. The QGP fireball evolution is provided by a (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model with MC-Glauber initial conditions. Heavy quarks hadronize via a hybrid model: fragmentation using Pythia 6.4 and a "sudden recombination" coalescence model using Wigner functions for mesons and baryons.

## Key numbers

- Spatial diffusion coefficient of heavy quarks set to D = 6/(2πT), corresponding to a gluon transport coefficient q̂ around 3 GeV²/fm at T = 400 MeV
- Crossing point where radiative energy loss equals collisional energy loss: approximately 6 GeV for charm quarks and 16 GeV for bottom quarks in central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC
- Lower cut-off for gluon energy set to ω₀ = πT
- Hydrodynamic evolution starts at τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c; critical temperature T_c = 165 MeV
- Charm quark mass taken as 1.27 GeV; bottom quark mass taken as 4.19 GeV
- Thermal masses: 300 MeV for u and d quarks, 475 MeV for s quarks
- Effective temperature for recombination normalization: T_eff = 175 MeV
- Number density at hadronization: 0.24/fm³ for u and d quarks, 0.13/fm³ for s quarks
- Wigner function width parameters: 0.106 GeV for charm hadrons, 0.059 GeV for bottom hadrons
- Nuclear shadowing reduces D meson R_AA by a factor of four at low p_T at LHC
- Using KLN initial conditions increases D meson v₂ by 25% compared to Glauber initial conditions at LHC

## Context

This work extends the Langevin approach for heavy quark energy loss, previously limited to collisional energy loss (Svetitsky 1987, Moore 2004), by incorporating radiative energy loss via a recoil force term. It builds on the authors' prior studies (Cao 2012) and uses the higher-twist formalism for gluon radiation (Guo 2000, Majumder 2009). The hybrid hadronization model follows the "sudden recombination" approach developed for light hadrons (Fries 2003, Greco 2003) and applied to heavy flavors (Oh 2009). The results enable predictions for B meson observables at both LHC and RHIC, and suggest that event-by-event hydrodynamic fluctuations may be needed to fully describe D meson elliptic flow.
