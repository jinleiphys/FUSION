# Emergent Gribov horizon from replica symmetry breaking in Yang--Mills theories

> We show that the Serreau--Tissier (ST) replica sector can dynamically generate a Gribov--Zwanziger (GZ)--type horizon functional in Yang--Mills (YM) theories. After integrating out the replica superfields, the expansion of the determinant of the Faddeev--Popov (FP) operator in the regulator $ζ$ produces, at linear order in $ζ$, a nonlocal kernel with the same color and Lorentz structure as the Gribov horizon functional, thereby defining an induced Gribov scale. Depending on the replica phase selected by the dynamics, the ST sector yields either (i) a local Curci--Ferrari (CF) screening mass (replica-symmetric phase) or (ii) an induced horizon-like interaction (replica-broken phase). In the latter case, the resulting BRST-invariant local formulation leads to a tree-level gluon propagator of the refined Gribov-Zwanziger (RGZ) decoupling type, whereas in the former it reduces to the massive FP/CF form, avoiding double counting of infrared scales by construction. A superspace derivation confirms that the induced horizon term originates from the ST superdeterminant, providing a microscopic mechanism for the emergence of the Gribov scale within the replica framework.

## Key claim

The Serreau–Tissier (ST) replica sector can dynamically generate a Gribov–Zwanziger (GZ)-type horizon functional in Yang–Mills theories, but only in the replica-broken phase (where the replica curvature vanishes, \(\hat\chi=0\)); in the replica-symmetric phase (\(\hat\chi>0\)) the same construction yields only a local Curci–Ferrari screening mass and no nonlocal horizon kernel.

## Method

The authors integrate out the nonlinear sigma replica superfields from the localized ST gauge-fixing action, obtaining an effective action containing \(\ln\det[\mathcal{M}(A^h)+\zeta] - \ln|\det\mathcal{M}(A^h)|\). They expand this determinant in the regulator \(\zeta\) to linear order, project the resulting \(\mathrm{Tr}\,\mathcal{M}^{-1}\) onto the bilinear sector in the transverse gauge-invariant composite field \(A^h_\mu\), and identify the nonlocal kernel with the same color and Lorentz structure as the Gribov horizon functional \(H(A^h)\). A Hubbard–Stratonovich transformation with Zwanziger auxiliary fields localizes this kernel, defining an induced Gribov scale \(\gamma_{\mathrm{ind}}^4\).

## Key numbers

- The induced Gribov scale is given by \(\gamma_{\mathrm{ind}}^4 = \kappa(d,N,\mu)\,\zeta + \mathcal{O}(\zeta^2)\), where \(\kappa(d,N,\mu)\) carries mass dimension through the renormalization scale \(\mu\).
- The tree-level transverse gluon propagator is \(D_T(p^2) = (p^2+M^2)/[(p^2+M^2)(p^2+\mu_{\mathrm{IR}}^2)+\lambda^4]\), with \(\mu_{\mathrm{IR}}^2 = m^2 + \Xi_{\mathrm{rep}}\,\beta\) where \(\Xi_{\mathrm{rep}}=1\) in the replica-symmetric phase and \(\Xi_{\mathrm{rep}}=0\) in the replica-broken phase.
- In the replica-symmetric phase, the propagator reduces to the massive Faddeev–Popov/Curci–Ferrari form \(D_T^{\mathrm{ST}}(p^2) = 1/(p^2+\beta)\).
- In the replica-broken phase with \(\beta=0\), the propagator reduces to the standard refined Gribov–Zwanziger decoupling form \(D_T^{\mathrm{RGZ}}(p^2) = (p^2+M^2)/[(p^2+M^2)(p^2+m^2)+\lambda^4]\).
- The replica mass gap equation relates parameters: \(8\pi^2 \beta_R / (\bar g_R^2 \bar\mu^2) = (\hat\chi_R + \zeta_R)\ln[(\hat\chi_R + \zeta_R)/\bar\mu^2] - \hat\chi_R \ln(\hat\chi_R/\bar\mu^2)\), with \(\hat\chi_R \ge 0\) in the replica-symmetric phase.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous unified ST–RGZ construction (Terin, 2025) by showing that the horizon interaction need not be imposed as an external ingredient but can emerge dynamically from the ST replica sector in the replica-broken phase. It connects the Serreau–Tissier copy-averaging approach (Serreau, 2012; Serreau, Tissier, and Tresmontant, 2015) with the Gribov–Zwanziger framework (Zwanziger, 1989; Dudal et al., 2008, 2011) and the Curci–Ferrari massive model (Tissier, 2010, 2011), providing a microscopic mechanism for the Gribov scale within the replica framework. The results enable a unified BRST-invariant description where the replica phase selects between CF-type screening and RGZ-type horizon suppression without double counting of infrared scales.
