# Natural Resolution of the Proton Size Puzzle

> We show that off-mass-shell effects arising from the internal structure of the proton provide a new proton polarization mechanism in the Lamb shift, proportional to the lepton mass to the fourth power. This effect is capable of resolving the current puzzle regarding the difference in the proton radius extracted from muonic compared with electronic hydrogen. These off-mass-shell effects could be probed in several other experiments.

## Key claim
Off-mass-shell effects at the photon-nucleon vertex, arising from the internal structure of the proton, provide a new proton polarization mechanism in the Lamb shift that is proportional to the lepton mass to the fourth power and can resolve the discrepancy between proton radii extracted from muonic versus electronic hydrogen, provided the corresponding effect in electronic hydrogen is no more than a few parts per million.

## Method
The authors introduce a phenomenological off-shell correction to the electromagnetic vertex function of the proton, parameterized by an off-shell form factor F(-q²) that vanishes at q²=0 to satisfy current conservation. They evaluate the lowest-order two-photon exchange diagram where one vertex is off-shell, compute the resulting change in the Lamb shift for muonic hydrogen using the scattering approximation, and determine the parameter values needed to produce the required energy shift of 0.31 meV.

## Key numbers
- The energy difference between the 2P₃/₂^{F=2} and 2S₁/₂^{F=1} states of muonic hydrogen is given by ΔE = 209.9779(49) - 5.2262 r_p² + 0.0347 r_p³ meV, where r_p is in fm.
- The difference between the Pohl and CODATA values of the proton radius would be entirely removed by an increase of the first term by 0.31 meV = 3.1×10⁻¹⁰ MeV.
- The required value of λ/b² = 2/(79 MeV)² produces a 0.31 meV shift in the muonic Lamb shift.
- The corresponding change in the electronic hydrogen Lamb shift for the 2S-state is about 9 Hz, below current uncertainty.
- If ξ is changed from 0 to 1, the required value of λ increases by about 10%.

## Context
This work addresses the proton radius puzzle arising from the muonic hydrogen measurement by Pohl et al. (2010), which gave a proton radius 4% smaller than the CODATA value from electronic hydrogen. The proposed off-shell mechanism extends earlier concerns about off-shell effects raised by Zemach (1956) and Grotch and Yennie (1969), and differs from dispersion relation approaches by Pachucki (1999) and Afanasev and Merenkov (2005) which do not include this effect. The result enables experimental tests in lepton-nucleus scattering and lepton-proton scattering where two-photon exchange effects are relevant.
