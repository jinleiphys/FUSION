# Magnetic Phases in Three-Flavor Color Superconductivity

> The best natural candidates for the realization of color superconductivity are quark stars -not yet confirmed by observation- and the extremely dense cores of compact stars, many of which have very large magnetic fields. To reliably predict astrophysical signatures of color superconductivity, a better understanding of the role of the star's magnetic field in the color superconducting phase that realizes in the core is required. This paper is an initial step in that direction. The field scales at which the different magnetic phases of a color superconductor with three quark flavors can be realized are investigated. Coming from weak to strong fields, the system undergoes first a symmetry transmutation from a Color-Flavor-Locked (CFL) phase to a Magnetic-CFL (MCFL) phase, and then a phase transition from the MCFL phase to the Paramagnetic-CFL (PCFL) phase. The low-energy effective theory for the excitations of the diquark condensate in the presence of a magnetic field is derived using a covariant representation that takes into account all the Lorentz structures contributing at low energy. The field-induced masses of the charged mesons and the threshold field at which the CFL $\to$ MCFL symmetry transmutation occurs are obtained in the framework of this low-energy effective theory. The relevance of the different magnetic phases for the physics of compact stars is discussed.

# Magnetic Phases in Three-Flavor Color Superconductivity

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a three-flavor color superconductor in an external magnetic field undergoes a symmetry transmutation from the Color-Flavor-Locked (CFL) phase to the Magnetic-CFL (MCFL) phase at a threshold field determined by the gap parameter, and then a genuine phase transition to the Paramagnetic-CFL (PCFL) phase at a much higher field set by the gluon mass scale; the CFL-to-MCFL transition is not a true phase transition but rather a field-induced decoupling of charged Goldstone bosons that makes the reduced MCFL symmetry effectively observable.

## Method

The authors derive a low-energy effective theory for the Goldstone bosons of the CFL phase in the presence of a rotated magnetic field, using a covariant formalism that preserves gauge invariance under the rotated electromagnetic group. They expand the unitary matrix Sigma in terms of Goldstone fields, introduce covariant derivatives with the rotated electromagnetic potential, and transform to momentum space using Ritus wave functions for charged scalar fields in a uniform magnetic field to obtain the Klein-Gordon equation and dispersion relation for charged mesons. The threshold field for the CFL-to-MCFL symmetry transmutation is then found by equating the field-induced meson mass to twice the gap parameter (or four times the gap in a quartic-quark picture), while the PCFL phase transition is analyzed via a Ginzburg-Landau approach for charged gluon condensation.

## Key numbers

- Threshold field for CFL-to-MCFL symmetry transmutation: $\widetilde{e}\widetilde{B}_{MCFL} = 12 \Delta_{CFL}^2$ (using weak-field approximation $v_\bot \simeq 1/\sqrt{3}$)
- For $\Delta_{CFL} \sim 15$ MeV, the threshold field is $\widetilde{e}\widetilde{B}_{MCFL} \sim 10^{16}$ G
- The PCFL phase transition occurs at $\widetilde{B}_{PCFL} = m_M^2$, where $m_M \sim g\mu$ is the magnetic mass of charged gluons
- At asymptotically high densities, the hierarchy is $\widetilde{B}_{MCFL} \ll \widetilde{B}_{PCFL}$ because $\Delta_{CFL} \ll m_M$
- In the weak-field limit ($eB \ll \mu^2$), the pion decay constant is $f_\pi^2 \approx (21 - 8\ln 2)/18 \cdot \mu^2/(2\pi^2)$ and meson velocities are $v_\bot \approx v_\| \approx 1/\sqrt{3}$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of the MCFL phase (Ferrer and de la Incera, 2006) and the PCFL phase with gluon vortex condensation (Ferrer and de la Incera, 2007) by systematically mapping the field-strength scales at which each magnetic phase becomes physically relevant. It contrasts with an earlier approach by Manuel (2001) that found a threshold field depending on the decay constant $f_\pi$; the present paper argues that result came from considering a subleading contribution, whereas the correct threshold depends only on the gap $\Delta_{CFL}$. The results enable predictions for the cooling and magnetic field evolution of compact stars with color superconducting cores, as the different phases have distinct low-energy excitation spectra (five neutral Goldstone bosons in MCFL versus nine in CFL) and the PCFL phase provides a paramagnetic antiscreening mechanism that could amplify stellar magnetic fields beyond what is possible in conventional neutron stars.
