# Quantum Clan Model description of Bose Einstein Correlations

> We propose a novel numerical method of modelling Bose-Einstein correlations (BEC) observed among identical (bosonic) particles produced in multiparticle production reactions. We argue that the most natural approach is to work directly in the momentum space in which the Bose statistics of secondaries reveals itself in their tendency to bunch in a specific way in the available phase space. Because such procedure is essentially identical to the clan model of multiparticle distributions proposed some time ago, therefore we call it the Quantum Clan Model.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that Bose-Einstein correlations (BEC) can be modeled by working directly in momentum space, where bosonic particles bunch in a specific way, and that this procedure is essentially identical to the clan model of multiparticle distributions, provided the clans contain particles of the same charge and nearly the same energy.

## Method
The authors construct a numerical algorithm that generates events by first selecting a seed particle with energy drawn from an exponential distribution \(f(E) = \exp(-E/T)\), then adding additional particles of the same charge according to a geometrical distribution controlled by a parameter \(P_0\) (playing the role of chemical potential). Each additional particle's energy is smeared around the seed energy using a distribution of width \(\sigma\), and the process repeats until the available energy is exhausted, with corrections for energy-momentum conservation and charge neutrality enforced event-by-event.

## Key numbers
- The mean number of particles in an elementary emitting cell (EEC) is given by \(\langle k_1\rangle = P(E)/[1+P(E)]\), where \(P(E)=P_0\cdot\exp(-E/T)\).
- The model uses three free parameters: temperature \(T\), chemical-potential-like parameter \(P_0\), and energy smearing width \(\sigma\).
- The overall multiplicity distribution in the Quantum Clan Model follows the Polya-Aeppli distribution, which differs from the Negative Binomial distribution for small multiplicities.

## Context
This work extends the clan model of multiparticle distributions proposed by the authors of the Negative Binomial Distribution (NBD) approach (Giovannini and Van Hove, 1980s) and the earlier information-theory treatment of BEC by O. V. Utyuzh, G. Wilk, and Z. Wlodarczyk (2004). It also builds on the concept of elementary emitting cells introduced by Bialas, Srednicki, Wosiek, and Wroblewski (1990s). The Quantum Clan Model offers a first-principles alternative to afterburner approaches that modify existing Monte Carlo event generators, and it enables future studies of how two-body BEC functions depend on the EEC parameters \(T\), \(P_0\), and \(\sigma\).
