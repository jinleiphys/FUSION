# Gauge Symmetry in the Large-amplitude Collective Motion of Superfluid Nuclei

> The adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method is a practical method for the description of large-amplitude collective motion in atomic nuclei with superfluidity and an advanced version of the adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory. We investigate the gauge symmetry in the ASCC method on the basis of the theory of constrained systems. The gauge symmetry in the ASCC method is originated from the constraint on the particle number in the collective Hamiltonian, and it is partially broken by the adiabatic expansion. The validity of the adiabatic expansion under the general gauge transformation is also discussed.

## Key claim

The gauge symmetry in the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method originates from the constraint on the particle number in the collective Hamiltonian, but this symmetry is only partially preserved after the adiabatic expansion; the moving-frame HFB and QRPA equations break the gauge symmetry that is still present in the equation of the collective submanifold.

## Method

The paper analyzes the ASCC method using the Dirac-Bergmann theory of constrained systems, treating the collective Hamiltonian expanded up to linear order in the particle number as a Hamiltonian with a primary constraint n=0. The author examines the generators of gauge transformations, derives the transformation properties of the collective coordinates and momenta, and tests the invariance of the ASCC basic equations (canonical-variable conditions, moving-frame HFB equation, and moving-frame QRPA equations) under various gauge transformations. The analysis is performed for the one-dimensional ASCC method with a single component for simplicity.

## Key numbers

- The collective Hamiltonian is expanded as H(q,p,n) = V(q) + (1/2)B(q)p^2 + λ n, where λ(q) = ⟨φ(q)|[H, iΘ]|φ(q)⟩
- The Hessian matrix (∂²L/∂q̇ⁱ∂q̇ʲ) has rank 1, leading to one constraint p₂ = n = 0
- The gauge generator takes the form G = ε(q,p,φ,n,t)n, where ε is an infinitesimal function
- Under the transformation in Example 1 (G = αpn), the Lagrange multiplier transforms as λ(q) → λ(q) + α∂qV(q) + (1/2)α∂qB(q)p²

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Hinohara et al. (2007), who first identified a "gauge" symmetry in the ASCC method and proposed a gauge-fixing prescription applied to the multi-O(4) model and shape coexistence in proton-rich Se and Kr isotopes. The paper places the ASCC method within the Dirac-Bergmann theory of constrained systems (Dirac 1950, 1964; Anderson 1951), showing that the gauge symmetry arises from the particle-number constraint and that the adiabatic expansion partially breaks this symmetry. This analysis enables a more rigorous understanding of gauge fixing in the ASCC method and clarifies the conditions under which the adiabatic expansion remains valid.
