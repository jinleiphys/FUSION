# Woods-Saxon-Gaussian Potential and Alpha-Cluster Structures of Alpha+Closed Shell Nuclei

> The Woods-Saxon-Gaussian (WSG) potential is proposed as a new phenomenological potential to describe systematically the level scheme, electromagnetic transitions, and alpha-decay half-lives of the alpha-cluster structures in various alpha+closed shell nuclei. It modifies the original Woods-Saxon (WS) potential with a shifted Gaussian factor centered at the nuclear surface. We determine the free parameters in the WSG potential by reproducing the correct level scheme of $^{212}\text{Po}={}^{208}\text{Pb}+α$. It is found that the resulting WSG potential matches with the M3Y double-folding potential at the surface region and makes corrections to the inner part of the cluster-core potential. We also find that the WSG potential with the almost same parameters determined for $^{212}$Po (except for a rescaled radius) could also be used to describe alpha-cluster structures in $^{20}\text{Ne}={}^{16}\text{O}+α$ and $^{44}\text{Ti}={}^{40}\text{Ca}+α$. In all three cases, the calculated values of the level schemes, electromagnetic transitions, and alpha-decay half-lives agree with the experimental data, which shows that the WSG potential could indeed grasp many important features of the alpha-cluster structures in alpha+closed shell nuclei. The study here is a useful complement to the existing cluster-core potentials in literature. The Gaussian form factor centered at the nuclear surface might also help deepen our understanding on the alpha-cluster formation taking place around the same place.

# Woods-Saxon-Gaussian Potential and Alpha-Cluster Structures of Alpha+Closed Shell Nuclei

## Key claim

The Woods-Saxon-Gaussian (WSG) potential, which modifies the standard Woods-Saxon potential by adding a Gaussian form factor centered at the nuclear surface, can describe level schemes, electromagnetic transitions, and alpha-decay half-lives for alpha+closed shell nuclei across a wide mass range, provided the radius parameter is rescaled for each system while the other four parameters remain fixed to values determined from 212Po.

## Method

The authors introduce a five-parameter phenomenological nuclear potential of the form V_N(r) = -V_0 / (1 + exp[(r-R)/a]) * {1 + alpha * exp[-beta * (r-R)^2]}, where the Gaussian correction is centered at the same radius R as the Woods-Saxon term. They determine the free parameters by minimizing chi-squared against the experimental level scheme of 212Po (208Pb + alpha), using the Bohr-Sommerfeld-Wildermuth quantization condition with G=18 to mimic Pauli blocking. Alpha-decay widths are computed via the two-potential approach with a preformation factor P_alpha = 0.6, and reduced quadrupole transition strengths B(E2) are calculated from the radial wave functions. The same parameter set (with only R rescaled) is then applied to 20Ne (16O + alpha, G=8) and 44Ti (40Ca + alpha, G=12).

## Key numbers

- WSG potential parameters for 212Po: V_0 = 203.3 MeV, a = 0.73 fm, R = 6.73 fm, alpha = -0.478, beta = 0.054 fm^{-2}
- For 20Ne: R = 3.25 fm; for 44Ti: R = 4.61 fm (all other parameters unchanged)
- Ground-state alpha-decay half-life of 212Po: experimental 299(2) ns, calculated 439 ns
- 18+ state alpha-decay half-life of 212Po: experimental 4.5 x 10^10 ns, calculated 4.83 x 10^10 ns
- B(E2; 6+ -> 4+) in 212Po: experimental 3.9 +/- 1.1 W.u., calculated 6.3 W.u.
- B(E2; 2+ -> 0+) in 20Ne: experimental 20.3 +/- 1.0 W.u., calculated 18.341 W.u.
- B(E2; 2+ -> 0+) in 44Ti: experimental 13.0 +/- 4.0 W.u., calculated 13.152 W.u.
- M3Y double-folding potential renormalization factor: lambda = 0.51

## Context

This work extends the binary cluster model approach by proposing a new phenomenological potential that addresses a known failure of the standard Woods-Saxon, M3Y double-folding, and Cosh potentials, which produce inverted level schemes for 212Po (Buck 1995, Buck 1996). The WSG potential reproduces the correct level ordering, matching the performance of the WS+WS^3 potential used by Ibrahim (2010) and others. The Gaussian form factor centered at the nuclear surface is motivated by earlier cluster-configuration shell model calculations by Varga (1992) and Delion (2012), and by the physical picture that alpha-cluster formation occurs around the nuclear surface (Delion 2010). The approach enables systematic phenomenological studies of alpha-cluster structures in both heavy and light alpha+closed shell nuclei using a single potential form, and raises the open question of the microscopic origin of the Gaussian correction.
