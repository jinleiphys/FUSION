# A transverse momentum differential global analysis of Heavy Ion Collisions

> The understanding of heavy ion collisions and its quark-gluon plasma formation requires a complicated interplay of rich physics in a wealth of experimental data. In this work we compare for identified particles the transverse momentum dependence of both the yields and the anisotropic flow coefficients for both PbPb and $p$Pb collisions. We do this in a global model fit including a free streaming prehydrodynamic phase with variable velocity $v_\text{fs}$, thereby widening the scope of initial conditions. During the hydrodynamic phase we vary three second order transport coefficients. The free streaming velocity has a preference slightly below the speed of light. In this extended model the bulk viscosity is small and even consistent with zero.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, in a global model fit with 21 varying parameters including a free-streaming prehydrodynamic phase with variable velocity, the QGP bulk viscosity is small and even consistent with zero, contrasting with previous work that required a finite bulk viscosity to fit mean transverse momenta and anisotropic flow.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian global analysis using the Trajectum framework, which combines a TRENTo initial condition model with a free-streaming prehydrodynamic phase (with variable velocity v_fs), a viscous hydrodynamic phase with three varied second-order transport coefficients, and the SMASH hadronic cascade. They trained Gaussian emulators on 1000 (2000) design points for PbPb (pPb) collisions using 6k (40k) events per point, then ran Markov Chain Monte Carlo with 600 walkers for approximately 15k steps to obtain posterior distributions for all 21 parameters.

## Key numbers

- 21 varying parameters (bold in the text) were included in the global fit
- 418 datapoints for PbPb collisions and 96 datapoints for pPb collisions were used
- 1000 design points for PbPb and 2000 design points for pPb were simulated
- 6k events per design point for PbPb and 40k events per design point for pPb were used
- The free streaming velocity v_fs has a preferred value close to unity, with an alternative scenario at v_fs ≈ 0.85
- The shear relaxation time ratio τ_π sT/η is constrained to ≲ 7, comparing with the holographic value of 2.61 and weak coupling value of 5
- The second-order coefficient ratio τ_{ππ}/τ_π is constrained to ≳ 1.5, consistent with weak coupling (10/7 ≈ 1.43) and holographic (≈ 1.92) results
- The bulk viscosity maximum (ζ/s)_max peaks at zero, consistent with an approximately conformal theory
- The minimum shear viscosity (η/s)_min is consistent with the canonical string theory value of 1/4π at lower temperatures

## Context

This work extends the global Bayesian analysis approach of Bernhard (2016, 2019) by including transverse momentum differential observables for identified particles, a free-streaming prehydrodynamic phase with variable velocity, and three varied second-order transport coefficients. It contradicts the prevailing view from Ryu (2015) and Bernhard (2016, 2019) that a finite bulk viscosity is needed to simultaneously fit mean transverse momenta and anisotropic flow. The analysis enables future studies to incorporate more non-trivial experimental data and to investigate whether pPb collisions can be described by hydrodynamics, while also highlighting the need for more physically motivated initial stages and smoother transitions to hydrodynamics.
