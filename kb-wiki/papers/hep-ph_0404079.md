# Pion Velocity near the Chiral Phase Transition

> We focus on the pion velocity at the critical temperature and study the quantum and hadronic thermal effects based on the vector manifestation (VM). We show the non-renormalization property on the pion velocity $v_π$, which is protected by the VM, and estimate the value of $v_π$ to be close to the speed of light.

## Key claim

The pion velocity near the critical temperature of the chiral phase transition is protected by the vector manifestation (VM) from receiving hadronic or quantum corrections, and is estimated to be close to the speed of light (0.83–0.99), in contrast to chiral theory predictions that it goes to zero.

## Method

The author used the hidden local symmetry (HLS) effective field theory, which includes both pions and vector mesons as dynamical degrees of freedom, and applied the Wilsonian matching procedure to determine the bare theory from underlying QCD. The intrinsic temperature dependences of the HLS Lagrangian parameters were obtained by integrating out high-energy quark and gluon modes in medium, and the thermal matrix elements of non-scalar operators in the operator product expansion (OPE) were evaluated by extending the dilute gas approximation to include both pions and longitudinal vector mesons near the critical temperature.

## Key numbers

- The bare pion velocity at the critical temperature is estimated as \( V_{\pi,\text{bare}}(T_c) = 0.83 - 0.99 \).
- The physical pion velocity near \( T_c \) is the same as the bare value, \( v_\pi(T) = 0.83 - 0.99 \), due to the non-renormalization property.

## Context

This work extends the vector manifestation (VM) framework developed by Harada and Yamawaki (2003) and the analysis of pion velocity in hot matter by Sasaki (2004). It contradicts the result from chiral theory by Son and Stephanov (2002), which predicted that the pion velocity goes to zero as \( T \to T_c \). The finding enables experimental discrimination between the VM picture (pion velocity near the speed of light) and the chiral theory picture (pion velocity approaching zero) using dielectron mass spectra data from CERN SPS.
