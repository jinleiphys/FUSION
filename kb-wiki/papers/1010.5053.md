# Isospin mixing in the vicinity of the N=Z line

> We present the isospin- and angular-momentum-projected nuclear density functional theory (DFT) and its applications to the isospin-breaking corrections to the superallowed beta-decay rates in the vicinity of the N=Z line. A preliminary value obtained for the Cabbibo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix element, |V_{ud}|=0.97463(24), agrees well with the recent estimate by Towner and Hardy [Phys. Rev. C{\bf 77}, 025501 (2008)]. We also discuss new opportunities to study the symmetry energy by using the isospin-projected DFT.

## Key claim
The paper presents a preliminary value for the CKM matrix element |V_ud| = 0.97463(24) obtained from isospin- and angular-momentum-projected density functional theory, but this result has poor confidence level due to the low spectroscopic quality of the SV Skyrme parameterization used.

## Method
The authors performed isospin- and angular-momentum-projected nuclear density functional theory (DFT) using Skyrme-Hartree-Fock solutions from the HFODD solver as the starting Slater determinants. They removed spurious isospin mixing by rediagonalizing the Hamiltonian in a basis of states with good isospin and angular momentum, and computed the isospin-breaking correction δ_C to superallowed Fermi beta-decay matrix elements by evaluating the matrix element of the isospin raising/lowering operator between the ground state of even-even nuclei and the isospin-analogue state in odd-odd N=Z nuclei.

## Key numbers
- |V_ud| = 0.97463(24), obtained from the average statistical-rate function for 12 out of 13 known superallowed transitions
- Average statistical-rate function: bar{cal F}t = 3069.4(10)
- Isospin impurity alpha_C in ^40Ca: approximately 0.9%
- Isospin impurity alpha_C in ^100Sn: approximately 6.0%
- Isospin impurity alpha_C in ^80Zr: 4.4%, agreeing with empirical value from giant dipole resonance gamma-decay studies
- Systematic error in delta_C due to basis cut-off: approximately 10%
- For A<40 nuclei, harmonic oscillator basis of at least N=10 full shells needed; for A>40, at least N=12 full shells needed
- Symmetry energy a_sym for SLy4: 32 MeV; for SV: 32.8 MeV
- Interaction part of symmetry energy a_sym^(int) for SLy4: 14.4 MeV; for SV: 1.4 MeV

## Context
This work extends the self-consistent mean-field approach to isospin mixing by applying symmetry restoration techniques that remove the spurious isospin components present in mean-field calculations, as previously discussed by Engel (1970), Caurier (1980), and Caurier (1982). The results are consistent with the recent compilation by Towner and Hardy (2008) but use a quantum-mechanically consistent method with no adjustable free parameters, unlike the shell-model approaches that require specific approximations. The isospin-projected DFT opens new opportunities to study the interaction part of the nuclear symmetry energy in odd-odd N=Z nuclei, particularly its dependence on time-odd terms that are poorly constrained for Skyrme energy density functionals.
