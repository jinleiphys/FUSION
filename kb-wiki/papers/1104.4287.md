# Gluon distributions in nuclei probed at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> Using updated gluon distributions from global fits to data, we investigate the sensitivity of direct photoproduction of heavy quarks and exclusive production of vector mesons to varying strength of gluon modifications. Implications of using these processes for constraining nuclear gluon distributions are discussed.

## Key claim

Using updated gluon distributions from global fits, the sensitivity of direct photoproduction of heavy quarks and exclusive production of vector mesons to varying strength of gluon modifications is investigated, with the caveat that the nuclear gluon distribution remains the least constrained aspect of global fits and large uncertainties persist at both small and large x.

## Method

The authors calculate cross sections for inclusive photoproduction of charm and bottom quark pairs (via photon-gluon fusion) and exclusive photoproduction of J/Psi and Upsilon(1s) vector mesons (via leading-order two-gluon exchange) in ultraperipheral PbPb collisions at the LHC. They use the equivalent photon (Weizsacker-Williams) method for the photon flux and four different gluon distribution sets: MSTW08 (free nucleon, no nuclear effects), EPS08, EPS09, and HKN07 (nuclear-modified). The photonuclear cross sections are convolved with the photon flux to obtain total cross sections and rapidity distributions.

## Key numbers

* Lorentz factor gamma_L = 7455 for p-p and 2930 for Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC
* Charm quark mass m_c = 1.4 GeV; bottom quark mass m_b = 4.75 GeV
* Total cross section for c-cbar photoproduction in PbPb at LHC (dynamic scale Q^2 = s-hat): MSTW08 = 1170 mb, EPS08 = 890 mb, EPS09 = 1000 mb, HKN07 = 1080 mb
* Total cross section for c-cbar photoproduction in PbPb at LHC (static scale Q^2 = 4m_c^2): MSTW08 = 1090 mb, EPS08 = 780 mb, EPS09 = 910 mb, HKN07 = 1000 mb
* Total cross section for b-bbar photoproduction in PbPb at LHC (dynamic scale Q^2 = s-hat): MSTW08 = 6.2 mb, EPS08 = 5.8 mb, EPS09 = 6.0 mb, HKN07 = 6.1 mb
* Total cross section for b-bbar photoproduction in PbPb at LHC (static scale Q^2 = m_b^2): MSTW08 = 7.0 mb, EPS08 = 6.2 mb, EPS09 = 6.6 mb, HKN07 = 6.7 mb
* Correction factor zeta_J/Psi = 1/3.5 and zeta_Upsilon(1s) = 1.0 for the vector meson photoproduction amplitude
* Slope parameter b = 4.5 GeV^{-2} used for vector meson cross sections on protons
* Woods-Saxon parameters for Pb-208: central density rho_0 = 0.16/fm^3, radius R_A = 1.2 A^{1/3} fm, skin depth d = 0.549 fm

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions (Bertulani 1988, Cahn 1990, Baur 1990, Klein and Nystrand 1999, Goncalves 2001) by using the most recent nuclear gluon distributions from global fits (EPS08, EPS09 from Eskola, Paukunnen, and Salgado 2008-2009; HKN07 from Hirai, Kumano, and Nagai 2007) that were not previously considered together. The results show that heavy quark photoproduction and exclusive vector meson production can discriminate between different gluon shadowing scenarios, with the rapidity window -2 < y < 2 providing the best sensitivity. The calculations are limited to leading order and do not include resolved photon contributions, which were treated in detail by Klein, Nystrand, and Vogt (2002).
