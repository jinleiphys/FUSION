# Mesons, PANDA and the scalar glueball

> The non-perturbative nature of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) at low energies has prompted the expectation that the gauge-bosons of QCD -- gluons -- might give rise to compound objects denoted as glueballs. Experimental signals for glueballs have represented a matter of research for various collaborations in the last decades; future research in this direction is a main endeavour planned by the PANDA Collaboration at FAIR. Hence in this article I review some of the outstanding issues in the glueball search, particularly with regard to the ground state -- the scalar glueball, and discuss the relevance for PANDA at FAIR.

## Key claim
The scalar glueball cannot be identified as a pure, unmixed state; instead, the best that can be achieved is to identify a resonance that is *predominantly* a glueball, and this identification requires a complete knowledge of all scalar meson states in the relevant energy region, a condition that may not yet be satisfied due to the possible existence of the $f_0(1790)$ resonance.

## Method
The paper reviews the experimental and theoretical status of the scalar glueball search by examining the five $IJ^{PC} = 00^{++}$ states listed by the Particle Data Group (PDG) up to 1.8 GeV: $f_0(500)$, $f_0(980)$, $f_0(1370)$, $f_0(1500)$, and $f_0(1710)$. It compares their production mechanisms (radiative decays and $\gamma\gamma$ collisions) and decay-width ratios against the predictions for a pure glueball state under unbroken flavour symmetry, and then discusses the BES II Collaboration's claim of a distinct $f_0(1790)$ resonance and its implications for the PANDA experiment at FAIR.

## Key numbers
- Five $IJ^{PC} = 00^{++}$ states are listed by the PDG up to 1.8 GeV: $f_0(500)$, $f_0(980)$, $f_0(1370)$, $f_0(1500)$, and $f_0(1710)$.
- For a pure glueball under unbroken flavour symmetry, the ratio of pion decay width to kaon decay width is 3/4.
- The ratio $\Gamma_{f_0(1500)\rightarrow \pi\pi} / \Gamma_{f_0(1500)\rightarrow KK} \simeq 4$ (PDG data).
- The ratio $\Gamma_{f_0(1710)\rightarrow \pi\pi} / \Gamma_{f_0(1710)\rightarrow KK} \simeq (0.2 - 0.4)$, depending on collaboration.
- The BES II Collaboration reported $m_{f_{0}(1790)} = 1790_{-30}^{+40}$ MeV and $\Gamma_{f_{0}(1790)} = 270_{-30}^{+60}$ MeV.
- Assuming $f_0(1710)$ and $f_0(1790)$ are the same resonance leads to a contradictory pion-to-kaon-decay ratio of $1.82 \pm 0.33$ from reactions (i) and (ii) versus $< 0.11$ from reactions (iii) and (iv) (BES II data).
- The PANDA Collaboration plans to study antiproton-proton interactions in the momentum range of 1.5-15 GeV.

## Context
This review extends the long-standing search for glueballs, which began with theoretical predictions by Jaffe and Johnson (1976) and others, and addresses the persistent ambiguity in identifying the scalar glueball among the $f_0$ resonances. The paper highlights the conflicting experimental results from the ALEPH Collaboration (2000) and the L3 Collaboration regarding $\gamma\gamma$ signals for $f_0(1500)$ and $f_0(1710)$, and emphasizes the BES II Collaboration's (2004) claim of a distinct $f_0(1790)$ resonance. The work sets the stage for the PANDA experiment at FAIR, which is uniquely positioned to confirm or disprove the existence of $f_0(1790)$ and thereby clarify the scalar glueball identification.
