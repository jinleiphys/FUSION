# Effects of Nuclear Medium on the Sum Rules in Electron and Neutrino Scattering

> In this work, we study the influence of nuclear medium effects on various parton model sum rules in nuclei and compare the results with the free nucleon case. We have used relativistic nucleon spectral function to take into account Fermi motion, binding and nucleon correlations. The pion and rho meson cloud contributions have been incorporated in a microscopic model. The effect of shadowing has also been considered.

## Key claim

Nuclear medium effects (Fermi motion, binding, nucleon correlations, meson clouds, and shadowing) significantly modify the values of the Gross-Llewellyn Smith (GLS), Adler (ASR), and Gottfried (GSR) sum rules in nuclei compared to the free nucleon case, and these modifications introduce a \(Q^2\) dependence that is absent in the free-nucleon parton model predictions.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic nucleon spectral function within an interacting Fermi sea, combined with a local density approximation, to compute nuclear structure functions for electron and neutrino deep inelastic scattering on finite nuclei. Pion and rho meson cloud contributions were incorporated via a microscopic model, and shadowing effects were added following the approach of Petti (2002). The nucleon parton distribution functions were taken from the CTEQ6.6 parameterization, and calculations were performed at both leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO).

## Key numbers

- GLS sum rule in \(^{56}\text{Fe}\): using the spectral function alone, the integral decreases by 7–8% from the free nucleon value at all \(Q^2\); adding shadowing further reduces it by \(\sim 10\%\) at low \(Q^2\) and by 3–4% at \(Q^2 = 12\text{–}15\ \text{GeV}^2\) (LO); NLO further decreases it by \(\sim 10\%\) at low \(Q^2\) and 6% at \(Q^2 = 10\text{–}15\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- Adler sum rule in \(^{56}\text{Fe}\): using the spectral function, the integral decreases by 14–18% from the free nucleon case in the range \(2\ \text{GeV}^2 < Q^2 < 15\ \text{GeV}^2\); adding shadowing and mesonic effects gives a further reduction of \(\sim 10\%\) at low \(Q^2\) and 3–4% at high \(Q^2\); NLO produces a very small change.
- Gottfried sum rule in \(^{56}\text{Fe}\): using the spectral function, the integral increases by 14–15% at \(Q^2 = 2\text{–}3\ \text{GeV}^2\) and by 30–35% at \(Q^2 = 10\text{–}15\ \text{GeV}^2\) from the free nucleon case; adding shadowing and mesonic effects gives a net increase of 7–8% at low \(Q^2\) and 30–32% at high \(Q^2\); NLO reduces it by 2–3% at low \(Q^2\) with negligible change at high \(Q^2\).

## Context

This work extends the study of parton-model sum rules (GLS, ASR, GSR) from free nucleons to nuclear targets, using a relativistic spectral function approach previously developed by Fernandez de Cordoba and Oset (1991) and Marco et al. (1995). It directly addresses the interpretation of high-precision neutrino and electron scattering experiments (e.g., CCFR) that use nuclear targets, showing that nuclear medium effects must be accounted for before testing the fundamental sum rules. The results enable future experimental analyses to separate genuine QCD effects from nuclear modifications, and they highlight the need for \(Q^2\)-dependent corrections in sum-rule tests.
