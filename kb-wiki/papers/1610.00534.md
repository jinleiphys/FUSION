# Spin dipole nuclear matrix elements for double beta decay nuclei by charge-exchange reactions

> Spin dipole (SD) strengths for double beta-decay (DBD) nuclei were studied experimentally for the first time by using measured cross sections of (3He,t) charge exchange reactions (CERs). Then SD nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for low-lying 2- states were derived from the experimental SD strengths by referring to the experimental GT (Gamow-Teller) and F (Fermi) strengths. They are consistent with the empirical SD NMEs based on the quasi-particle model with the empirical effective SD coupling constant. The CERs are used to evaluate the SD NME, which is associated with one of the major components of the neutrino-less DBD NME.

## Key claim

Spin dipole (SD) nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for low-lying 2- states in double beta-decay (DBD) nuclei were derived for the first time from measured (3He,t) charge-exchange reaction (CER) cross sections, and these CER-derived NMEs are consistent with empirical SD NMEs based on the quasiparticle model with an effective SD coupling constant, but only for the simple quasiparticle SD states that are strongly excited by the central tau-sigma interaction.

## Method

The authors used differential cross sections from (3He,t) CERs at finite angles (around 2 degrees, corresponding to momentum transfer q ≈ 50-60 MeV/c) to extract SD strengths B(SD) for low-lying Jπ=2- states in seven DBD nuclei (76Ge, 82Se, 96Zr, 100Mo, 128Te, 130Te, 136Xe). The SD strengths were obtained relative to the experimentally known Gamow-Teller (GT) and Fermi (F) strengths by taking the ratio of the SD differential cross section at its angular-distribution maximum to the GT or F cross section at forward angles (q ≈ 0). The relative SD NMEs were then converted to absolute SD NMEs using proportionality coefficients derived by comparing the relative CER NMEs with empirical SD NMEs from beta-decay ft values in neighboring nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For 76Ge: relative SD strength B_RGT(SD) = 0.052, CER-derived SD NME M_GT(SD) = 0.20 × 10^-2 n.u. (natural units, where 1 n.u. = 386 fm)
- For 82Se: B_RGT(SD) = 0.041, M_GT(SD) = 0.17 × 10^-2 n.u.
- For 96Zr: B_RGT(SD) = 0.018, M_GT(SD) = 0.12 × 10^-2 n.u.
- For 100Mo: B_RGT(SD) = 0.021, M_GT(SD) = 0.13 × 10^-2 n.u.
- For 128Te: B_RGT(SD) = 0.178, M_GT(SD) = 0.37 × 10^-2 n.u.
- For 130Te: B_RGT(SD) = 0.250, M_GT(SD) = 0.43 × 10^-2 n.u.
- For 136Xe: B_RGT(SD) = 0.302, M_GT(SD) = 0.47 × 10^-2 n.u.
- Empirical effective reduction factor k^eff (ratio of experimental to quasiparticle SD NME) is approximately 0.23 for mass region A=72-88, 0.30 for A=94-106, and 0.22 for A=122-140
- Proportionality coefficients: R_GT^(1/2) = 8.6 × 10^-3 n.u. and R_F^(1/2) = 2.8 × 10^-3 n.u.
- Estimated uncertainty on the derived SD NMEs is 15%

## Context

This work extends the established methodology of using (3He,t) CERs to extract GT strengths for 2νββ NMEs (as done previously by Akimune (1997), Guillot (2011), Puppe (2011-2012), Thies (2012), Frekers (2013, 2016)) to the SD channel relevant for 0νββ NMEs. The SD NMEs derived from CERs agree with empirical NMEs from beta-decay ft values in neighboring nuclei (Ejiri 1978, 2014, 2015) and with quasiparticle model calculations using the empirical effective reduction factor. This enables experimental evaluation of the SD component of 0νββ NMEs, which is one of the major components of the neutrino-less double beta decay matrix element, and also provides SD response data relevant for supernova neutrino studies.
