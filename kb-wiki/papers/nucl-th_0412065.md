# On radiative np -> 1s + gamma transitions, induced by strong low-energy interactions,in kaonic atoms

> We calculate the rates of the radiative transitions np -> 1s + gamma in kaonic hydrogen and kaonic deuterium, induced by strong low-energy interactions and enhanced by Coulomb interactions. The obtained results should be taken into account for the theoretical analysis of the experimental data on the X-ray spectra and yields in kaonic atoms.

## Key claim

The rates of radiative transitions \(np \to 1s + \gamma\) in kaonic hydrogen and kaonic deuterium, induced by strong low-energy interactions and enhanced by Coulomb interactions, are calculated to be about 0.3–1.5% of the pure electric dipole transition rates; these contributions should be taken into account in theoretical analyses of X-ray spectra and yields, but only if experimental precision reaches the sub-percent level.

## Method

The authors compute the amplitude for kaon–proton Bremsstrahlung \(K^- + p \to K^- + p + \gamma\) using an effective Lagrangian with a contact strong interaction proportional to the S-wave scattering length \(a_0^{K^-p}\), and incorporate Coulomb effects via explicit non-relativistic Coulomb wave functions and Coulomb Green functions. The atomic transition amplitude is obtained by weighting the Bremsstrahlung amplitude with momentum-space Coulomb wave functions of the \(np\) and \(1s\) states of kaonic hydrogen, then integrating over momenta. The results are extended to kaonic deuterium by replacing the reduced mass and using the estimated energy-level displacement of the kaonic deuterium ground state.

## Key numbers

- Rate of \((K^-p)_{2p} \to (K^-p)_{1s} + \gamma\): \((2.3 \pm 0.3) \times 10^9\ \text{sec}^{-1}\) (kaonic hydrogen, using theoretical shift and width \(\epsilon_{1s} = -203 \pm 15\ \text{eV}\), \(\Gamma_{1s}/2 = 113 \pm 14\ \text{eV}\)).
- Rate of \((K^-p)_{3p} \to (K^-p)_{1s} + \gamma\): \((2.7 \pm 0.4) \times 10^8\ \text{sec}^{-1}\) (kaonic hydrogen).
- Rate of \((K^-d)_{2p} \to (K^-d)_{1s} + \gamma\): \((7.4 \pm 1.8) \times 10^9\ \text{sec}^{-1}\) (kaonic deuterium, using estimated \(\epsilon_{1s} = -325 \pm 60\ \text{eV}\), \(\Gamma_{1s}/2 = 315 \pm 50\ \text{eV}\)).
- Rate of \((K^-d)_{3p} \to (K^-d)_{1s} + \gamma\): \((8.7 \pm 2.1) \times 10^8\ \text{sec}^{-1}\) (kaonic deuterium).
- Pure electric dipole rate for \(2p \to 1s\) in kaonic hydrogen: \(4.0 \times 10^{11}\ \text{sec}^{-1}\); in kaonic deuterium: \(4.8 \times 10^{11}\ \text{sec}^{-1}\).
- Strong-induced rates are \(0.6\%\) (kaonic hydrogen \(2p\)) and \(1.5\%\) (kaonic deuterium \(2p\)) of the pure dipole rates.

## Context

This work extends the authors’ previous calculation of hadronic decay rates \(\Gamma_{np}\) for kaonic hydrogen (Ivanov et al., 2004) to radiative transitions induced by strong interactions, complementing the standard treatment of pure electric dipole transitions from Bethe and Salpeter (1957). The results are needed for interpreting X-ray spectra from kaonic atoms measured by the DEAR Collaboration and others, and for cascade calculations that currently use \(\Gamma_{2p}\) as an input parameter (Koike, Harada, and Akaishi, 1998). The approach parallels the effective field theory based on chiral perturbation theory by Gasser and Leutwyler (1983) and the derivation of the Ericson–Weise formula for \(K^-d\) scattering.
