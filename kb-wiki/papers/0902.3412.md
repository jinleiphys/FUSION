# Low-Energy D-Wave Effects in Neutral Pion Photoproduction

> The contribution of D waves to physical observables for neutral pion photoproduction in the near threshold region is studied. Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory to one loop, and up to ${\cal O}(q^4)$, is used to account for the S and P waves, while D waves are added in an almost model-independent way using standard Born terms and vector mesons. It is found that the inclusion of D waves is necessary to extract the $E_{0+}$ multipole reliably from present and forthcoming data and to assess the low-energy constants of Chiral Perturbation Theory. Arguments are presented demonstrating that F-wave contributions are negligible in the near-threshold region.

## Key claim
D-wave contributions to neutral pion photoproduction in the near-threshold region (up to photon energy ~165 MeV) are significant enough that omitting them compromises the extraction of the S-wave multipole \(E_{0+}\) and its associated low-energy constants, even though the S+P-wave approximation alone can fit the existing data equally well.

## Method
The authors used Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBCHPT) to one loop and up to \(\mathcal{O}(q^4)\) to compute S- and P-wave multipoles, then added D waves using standard Born terms and vector meson (\(\omega\) and \(\rho\)) exchange in an almost model-independent way. They performed two fits to 171 differential cross sections and 7 photon asymmetries from Mainz (threshold to 166 MeV): one with only S and P waves (SP model) and one including D waves (SPD model), fitting the same five low-energy constants in both cases.

## Key numbers
- \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 1.23\) for the SP model and \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 1.25\) for the SPD model, with 70% (90%) confidence levels at \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 1.27\) (1.29) for SP and 1.28 (1.30) for SPD.
- The combined low-energy constant \(a_+ = a_1 + a_2\) at 70% confidence level: \(a_+ = 6.48^{+0.82}_{-0.93}\ \text{GeV}^{-4}\) for the SP model and \(a_+ = 6.57^{+0.83}_{-0.92}\ \text{GeV}^{-4}\) for the SPD model.
- P-wave multipoles changed by less than 1% between SP and SPD fits.
- Data spanned photon energies from threshold up to 166 MeV.

## Context
This work extends prior HBCHPT analyses of neutral pion photoproduction (Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner, 2001) that used only S and P waves, showing that D waves must be included to reliably extract the \(E_{0+}\) multipole and its low-energy constants. The results enable more accurate interpretation of new data from the Crystal Ball Collaboration at Mainz (Hornidge, 2009) and future polarization experiments, and the authors note that their extracted \(a_+\) and \(a_-\) values are closer to those from a dispersion-analysis approach (Vladimirov, Krewald, and Meissner, 2005) that partially included D waves.
