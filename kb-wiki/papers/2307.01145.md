# The Standard Model theory of neutron beta decay

> We review the status of the Standard Model theory of neutron beta decay. Particular emphasis is put on the recent developments in the electroweak radiative corrections. Given that some existing approaches give slightly different results, we thoroughly review the origin of discrepancies, and provide our recommended value for the radiative correction to the neutron and nuclear decay rates. The use of dispersion relation, lattice Quantum Chromodynamics and effective field theory framework allows for high-precision theory calculations at the level of $10^{-4}$, turning neutron beta decay into a powerful tool to search for new physics, complementary to high-energy collider experiments. We offer an outlook to the future improvements.

## Key claim

The authors review the Standard Model theory of neutron beta decay and provide a recommended value for the electroweak radiative correction to neutron and nuclear decay rates, noting that some existing approaches give slightly different results and that the discrepancies are thoroughly examined.

## Method

The authors analyze the structure of inner radiative corrections to neutron beta decay using Sirlin's formalism, separating the corrections into two-point and three-point function contributions and a gamma-W box diagram. They evaluate the gamma-W box diagram using dispersion relations, effective field theory, and lattice Quantum Chromodynamics frameworks to achieve high-precision theory calculations.

## Context

This review extends the work of Sirlin (1978) on radiative corrections in semileptonic beta decays and addresses discrepancies among existing approaches for electroweak radiative corrections. The improved precision at the 10^(-4) level enables neutron beta decay to serve as a powerful tool for searching for new physics complementary to high-energy collider experiments, and allows for precision tests of CKM matrix unitarity when combined with results from superallowed nuclear decays and kaon decays.
