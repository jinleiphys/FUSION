# Symmetry restoration in the axially deformed proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation for nuclear beta decay: The effect of angular-momentum projection

> We examine the effects of symmetry restoration on nuclear beta decay within the axially deformed proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA). We employ the proton-neutron finite-amplitude method (pnFAM) to compute transition amplitudes, and perform angular-momentum projection both after variation and after the QRPA to restore rotational symmetry. Exact projection reduces the calculated beta decay half-lives from those that use the needle approximation by up to 60%, and even more when taking the effects of projection on the ground-state energy into account.

## Key claim

Exact angular-momentum projection (AMP) within the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pnQRPA) reduces calculated beta-decay half-lives by up to 60% compared to the needle approximation, and even more when projection effects on the ground-state energy are included, but the method is applied only to a few neutron-rich iron isotopes and relies on the quasi-boson approximation for the projected ground state.

## Method

The authors extend the axially deformed proton-neutron finite-amplitude method (pnFAM) to include exact angular-momentum projection after variation (PAV) for charge-changing transitions. They solve the pnFAM linear response equations iteratively for each complex frequency to obtain transition amplitudes, then construct QRPA phonon operators and project the initial and final states onto good angular momentum using projection operators. The projected transition matrix elements are used to compute Gamow-Teller strength functions and beta-decay half-lives via contour integration of the response function weighted by phase-space factors.

## Key numbers

- Exact projection reduces calculated beta-decay half-lives from those using the needle approximation by up to 60%
- The Skyrme energy density functional SKO' is used throughout, with pairing strength V1 = -264.216 MeV fm^3
- Quasiparticle states are limited to those with energy E < E_cut = 60 MeV in both HFB and response function sums
- The constant κ = 6147.0 ± 2.4 seconds is used in the decay rate formula
- The nuclear radius is taken as R_A = 1.2 A^(1/3) fm
- The oscillator frequency is ℏω_HO = 1.2 × 41 A^(-1/3) MeV
- The saturation density ρ_c = 0.16 fm^(-3) and α = 0.5 for mixed pairing
- The mass difference ΔM_(n-H) = 0.782 MeV is used for the neutron-hydrogen atom mass difference
- 12 Gauss-Legendre quadrature mesh points are used for the Euler angle in the interval [0, π]
- 60 mesh points are employed for numerical evaluation of contour integrals
- In ⁶⁴Fe, the J=0 minimum after projection is at β₂ ≈ 0.12, smaller than the value β₂ = 0.29 from measured B(E2: 2⁺ → 0⁺) and the rigid-rotor approximation

## Context

This work extends the axially deformed pnFAM developed by Mustonen et al. (2014) for beta decay by incorporating exact angular-momentum projection within a projection-after-variation scheme, generalizing the approach of Porro et al. (2024) from charge-conserving multipole strength functions to charge-changing processes with pairing correlations. The needle approximation of Krumlinde and Møller (1984) and Ring and Schuck (1980) is shown to be inadequate for nuclei with small deformation, while exact projection enables more reliable predictions of beta-decay rates needed for r-process nucleosynthesis simulations. The method can be applied to systematic studies of beta-decay half-lives across wide ranges of neutron-rich nuclei, though the quasi-boson approximation for the projected ground state may lead to sum-rule violations that require further investigation.
