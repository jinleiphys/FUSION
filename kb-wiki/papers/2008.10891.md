# Nuclear suppression from coherent $J/ψ$ photoproduction at the Large Hadron Collider

> Using the data on coherent $J/ψ$ photoproduction in Pb-Pb ultraperipheral collisions (UPCs) obtained in Runs 1 and 2 at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC), we determined with a good accuracy the nuclear suppression factor of $S_{Pb}(x)$ in a wide range of the momentum fraction $x$, $10^{-5} \leq x \leq 0.04$. In the small-$x$ region $x < 10^{-3}$, our $χ^2$ fit favors a flat form of $S_{Pb}(x) \approx 0.6$ with approximately a 5% accuracy for $x=6 \times 10^{-4} - 10^{-3} $ and a 25% error at $x=10^{-4}$. At the same time, uncertainties of the fit do not exclude a slow decrease of $S_{Pb}(x)$ in the small-$x$ limit. At large $x$, $S_{Pb}(x)$ is constrained to better than 10% precision up to $x=0.04$ and is also consistent with the value of $S_{Pb}(x)$ at $\langle x \rangle =0.042$, which we extract from the Fermilab data on the $A$ dependence of the cross section of coherent $J/ψ$ photoproduction on fixed nuclear targets. The resulting uncertainties on $S_{Pb}(x)$ are small, which indicates the potential of the LHC data on coherent charmonium photoproduction in Pb-Pb UPCs to provide additional constraints on small-$x$ nPDFs. We explicitly demonstrate this using as an example the EPPS16 and nCTEQ16 nuclear parton distribution functions, whose uncertainties decrease severalfold after the Bayesian reweighting of the discussed UPC data.

## Key claim

The authors extract the nuclear suppression factor \(S_{Pb}(x)\) for coherent \(J/\psi\) photoproduction in lead-lead ultraperipheral collisions at the LHC, finding that a flat form \(S_{Pb}(x) \approx 0.6\) is favored for \(x < 10^{-3}\) but that a slow decrease at very small \(x\) is not excluded by the fit uncertainties.

## Method

The authors fit a piece-wise parametrization of \(S_{Pb}(x)\) to the rapidity-dependent cross sections of coherent \(J/\psi\) photoproduction in Pb-Pb UPCs measured by ALICE, CMS, and LHCb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV and 5.02 TeV. The nuclear suppression factor is defined as the square root of the ratio of the measured photoproduction cross section on lead to the impulse-approximation cross section, which is constructed from a data-driven parametrization of the \(\gamma p \to J/\psi p\) differential cross section and the Woods-Saxon nuclear form factor. The fit uses 17 data points and four free parameters, with the ALICE mid-rapidity points at \(y=0\) fixing the normalization.

## Key numbers

*   \(S_{Pb}(x = 6.17 \times 10^{-4}) = 0.63 \pm 0.025\) from the Run 2 ALICE data at mid-rapidity.
*   \(S_{Pb}(x = 0.00112) = 0.62 \pm 0.057\) from the Run 1 ALICE data at mid-rapidity.
*   In the small-\(x\) region \(x < 10^{-3}\), \(S_{Pb}(x)\) is determined with approximately 5% accuracy for \(x = 6 \times 10^{-4} - 10^{-3}\) and 25% accuracy at \(x = 10^{-4}\).
*   At large \(x\), \(S_{Pb}(x)\) is constrained to better than 10% precision up to \(x = 0.04\).
*   From Fermilab fixed-target data, \(S_{Pb}(\langle x \rangle = 0.042) = 0.90 \pm 0.10\).
*   The fit yields a total \(\chi^2 = 8.4\) for 17 data points (\(\chi^2/\text{d.o.f.} = 0.76\)).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous phenomenological studies of nuclear suppression in coherent \(J/\psi\) photoproduction at the LHC (Guzey et al., 2013) by including all available Run 1 and Run 2 data from ALICE, CMS, and LHCb, as well as fixed-target Fermilab data (Sokoloff et al., 1986). The extracted \(S_{Pb}(x)\) is compared to the ratio \(R_g(x,\mu^2) = g_A(x,\mu^2)/[A g_N(x,\mu^2)]\) from the EPPS16 (Eskola et al., 2016) and nCTEQ15 (Kovarik et al., 2015) nuclear parton distribution functions, and the authors demonstrate via Bayesian reweighting that the UPC data can reduce the uncertainties of these nPDFs severalfold. The results also enable future constraints on small-\(x\) nuclear gluon distributions and tests of gluon saturation models.
