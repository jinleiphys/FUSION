# Mean Charge of the Light Sea Quarks in the Proton

> The modified Gottfried sum rule multiplied by 3/2 can be interpreted as the one to measure the mean $I_3$ of the [(quark) - (antiquark)] in the proton . Based on this interpretation we find the sum rule which can be understood as the one to measure the mean charge of the light sea quarks (u,d,s) in the proton, and show that it takes the value 0.23 for the proton and 0.34 for the neutron .

# Mean Charge of the Light Sea Quarks in the Proton

## Key claim
The mean charge of the light sea quarks (u, d, s) in the proton is 0.23, and 0.34 in the neutron, derived from a model-independent sum rule based on the modified Gottfried sum rule and a hypercharge sum rule, with the caveat that the result depends heavily on the small-x behavior of parton distributions and assumes the pomeron is flavor singlet.

## Method
The author derives a sum rule for the mean charge of light sea quarks by combining the modified Gottfried sum rule (which measures the mean I3 of quark minus antiquark in the proton) with a hypercharge sum rule in SU(3) flavor group, expressed in terms of integrals over pion-nucleon and kaon-nucleon total cross sections. The bilocal current formalism on the null-plane is used to relate these sum rules to measurable quantities, with the pomeron intercept taken as αP(0)=1+b with b=0.0808. The numerical evaluation uses previously estimated values of Iπ, I_K^p, and I_K^n from experimental cross sections.

## Key numbers
- Mean charge of light sea quarks in the proton: 0.23
- Mean charge of light sea quarks in the neutron: 0.34
- Mean I3 of light sea quarks from modified Gottfried sum rule: -0.055
- Mean hypercharge of light sea quarks: 0.56
- Right-hand side of hypercharge sum rule (1/3)[2Iπ - I_K^p - I_K^n]: 2.12, with Iπ ~ 5.17, I_K^p ~ 2.39, I_K^n ~ 1.61
- Right-hand side of modified Gottfried sum rule: 0.26 ± 0.03
- Pomeron intercept parameter b: 0.0808

## Context
This work extends the author's previous formalism relating the modified Gottfried sum rule to kaon-nucleon scatterings (Koretune, 1993, 1995) and the bilocal quantity approach (Koretune, 1984). The sum rule provides a model-independent constraint on the hadronic vacuum, complementing the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule and the Gottfried sum rule which have been studied experimentally. The result enables a test of existing parton distribution sets such as those from the CTEQ group (1997) and the MRS group, and the author shows that the CTEQ4M set does not satisfy the required flavor-singlet symmetry at small x, indicating that the relations can help constrain sea quark distributions in the poorly determined small-x region.
