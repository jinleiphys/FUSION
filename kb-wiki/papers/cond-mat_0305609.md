# Fermion pairing in Bose-Fermi mixtures

> An effective interaction between fermions in a Bose-Fermi mixture is derived. It is induced by density fluctuations of the bosonic background. The contributions from states containing both one and two virtual phonons are taken into account self-consistently. The time dependence of the effective interaction has been removed by assuming that the velocity of the fermions at the Fermi surface is much larger than the sound velocity in the Bose gas. The fermions are considered in only one magnetic state, so that pairing can be effective only in odd-l channels. It has been found that the onset of the superconducting phase can occur at temperatures (> 100nK) of the same order of magnitude as the Fermi temperature (300nK), and the energy gap in the excitation spectrum is a small fraction (1%) of the Fermi energy.

## Key claim

The paper derives an effective attractive interaction between fermions in a Bose-Fermi mixture, induced by density fluctuations of the bosonic background, and claims that the onset of a superconducting phase in the Fermi component can occur at temperatures above 100 nK, with an energy gap about 1% of the Fermi energy. The key condition is that the Fermi velocity is assumed to be much larger than the sound velocity in the Bose gas, which is the opposite of the usual approximation neglecting retardation effects.

## Method

The author uses a functional-integral approach to derive the effective fermion-fermion interaction from the grand-canonical partition function of a homogeneous Bose-Fermi mixture, treating the Bose gas within the Gaussian (Bogoliubov) approximation. The effective interaction includes contributions from both one-virtual-phonon and two-virtual-phonon intermediate states, evaluated in the static limit (equal times) because the Fermi velocity is much larger than the sound velocity. The gap equation for the pairing field is then solved for odd-\(l\) channels (since fermions are in only one magnetic state) using an angle-average approximation for the gap.

## Key numbers

- The Fermi temperature is approximately 300 nK, and the onset of the superconducting phase can occur at temperatures above 100 nK.
- The energy gap in the excitation spectrum is about 1% of the Fermi energy.
- For the mixture of \(^{87}\)Rb (bosons) and \(^{40}\)K (fermions), the boson density is \(n_B = 1.45 \times 10^{13} \, \text{cm}^{-3}\), with a fermion-to-boson density ratio \(n_F/n_B \sim 0.5\).
- The boson-boson scattering length is \(a_{BB} = 113 a_0\) and the boson-fermion scattering length is \(a_{BF} = -330 a_0\), where \(a_0\) is the Bohr radius.
- The critical temperature for Bose condensation is \(T_c = 110 \, \text{nK}\), and the temperature range considered is \(46 \, \text{nK} \le T \le 87 \, \text{nK}\), corresponding to condensate fractions \(0.4 \le n_B^0/n_B \le 0.8\).
- The ratio of Fermi velocity to sound velocity is about 20, justifying the static approximation.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of phonon-induced fermion pairing in Bose-Fermi mixtures, such as those by Bar67, Bij00, Hei00, Efr02, and Viv02, which considered only single-phonon intermediate states and neglected retardation effects. By including two-phonon states and using the opposite approximation (static limit appropriate for experimental conditions), the author shows that the two-phonon contribution is of the same order as the one-phonon contribution when the condensate depletion is significant. The results enable estimates of the superconducting gap and critical temperature for mixtures like \(^{40}\)K-\(^{87}\)Rb, as studied experimentally by Roa02.
