# Beyond relativistic mean-field studies of low-lying states in neutron-deficient krypton isotopes

> Neutron-deficient krypton isotopes are of particular interest due to the coexistence of oblate and prolate shapes in low-lying states and the transition of ground-state from one dominate shape to another as a function of neutron number. A detailed interpretation of these phenomena in neutron-deficient Kr isotopes requires the use of a method going beyond a mean-field approach that permits to determine spectra and transition probabilities. The aim of this work is to provide a systematic calculation of low-lying state in the even-even 68-86Kr isotopes and to understand the shape coexistence phenomenon and the onset of large collectivity around N=40 from beyond relativistic mean-field studies. The starting point of our method is a set of relativistic mean-field+BCS wave functions generated with a constraint on triaxial deformations (beta, gamma). The excitation energies and electric multipole transition strengths of low-lying states are calculated by solving a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) with parameters determined by the mean-field wave functions. # Beyond relativistic mean-field studies of low-lying states in neutron-deficient krypton isotopes

## Key claim

The five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) approach based on relativistic mean-field calculations with the PC-PK1 force reproduces the main features of low-spin spectra and the systematics of excitation energies and transition strengths in neutron-deficient Kr isotopes, but the reproduction of large E2 transition strengths between the 2+1 and 0+2 states requires strong configuration mixing that is better captured by calculations using the Gogny D1S force. Triaxiality is confirmed as important for reproducing the energy order of oblate and prolate states, though an exact treatment of configuration mixing of particle-number and angular-momentum projected triaxial states is still needed to pin down these effects quantitatively.

## Method

The starting point is a set of relativistic mean-field plus BCS wave functions generated with constraints on triaxial deformations (beta, gamma) using the PC-PK1 energy density functional. Excitation energies and electric multipole transition strengths of low-lying states are calculated by solving a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) with parameters determined from these mean-field wave functions. To examine the role of triaxiality, configuration mixing of both particle-number and angular-momentum projected axially deformed states is also carried out within the generator coordinate method (GCM) based on the same energy density functional, and results are compared with similar 5DCH calculations using the non-relativistic Gogny D1S force.

## Key numbers

- The global minimum in the energy surface for 76Kr is shifted to spherical shape at N=40 with about 1.1 MeV lower in energy than the prolate minimum at the mean-field level
- Zero-point energies deepen the prolate deformed minimum in 76Kr and reduce its energy difference with the spherical shape by about 0.9 MeV
- Angular momentum projection for J=0 gives an energy gain that increases rapidly to reach about 2.7 MeV around beta approximately -0.15 and 3.7 MeV around beta approximately 0.20 in 76Kr
- The 5DCH calculations using the SLy6 force (or PC-PK1 force) with zero-range pairing force give rho2(E0:0+2 to 0+1) = 0.148 (or 0.153) for 76Kr
- The calculated B(E2: 0+2 to 2+1) value in 74,76Kr by the PC-PK1 force plus separable pairing force is only half of the value of the neighboring nuclei

## Context

This work extends previous beyond-mean-field studies of shape coexistence in neutron-deficient Kr isotopes, which were investigated using the mixing of particle-number and angular-momentum projected axially deformed HFB states with Skyrme force SLy6 by Bender (2006) and using 5DCH calculations with the Gogny D1S force by Girod (2009). The study also builds on earlier 5DCH calculations for neutron-rich Kr isotopes around N=60 by Xiang (2012) using the same PC-PK1 force. The results confirm the conclusion of Girod (2009) that triaxiality is important for reproducing the energy order of oblate and prolate states, and they highlight the need for exact GCM calculations combined with three-dimensional angular momentum projection to fully quantify triaxiality effects.
