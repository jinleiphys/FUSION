# Particle number projection with effective forces

> The particle number projection method is formulated for density dependent forces and in particular for the finite range Gogny force. Detailed formula for the projected energy and its gradient are provided. The problems arising from the neglection of any exchange term, which may lead to divergences, are throughly discussed and the possible inaccuracies estimated. Numericala results for the projection after variation method are shown for the nucleus 164Er and for the projection before variation approach for the nuclei 48-50Cr. We also confirm the Coulomb antipairing effect found in mean field theories.

## Key claim

The paper develops an exact particle number projection (PNP) method for density-dependent effective forces, specifically the finite-range Gogny force, showing that the method is feasible without divergences only when all exchange terms are included, a condition that zero-range Skyrme forces and relativistic mean-field approaches generally fail to satisfy.

## Method

The authors formulate the particle number projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory for non-separable, density-dependent interactions by applying the generalized Wick theorem to compute projected matrix elements as sums over discretized gauge angles using the Fomenko expansion. They derive explicit expressions for the projected energy and its gradient (needed for variation-after-projection calculations) for both the density-independent and density-dependent parts of the Gogny force, considering two prescriptions for the density dependence: the projected density prescription and the mixed density prescription. The formalism is applied to projection after variation (PAV) for 164Er and variation after projection (VAP) for 48-50Cr.

## Key numbers

* For the Fomenko expansion discretization, L=8 points are typically used in the calculations to approximate the exact projection integral.
* The projected density prescription for the density-dependent term requires calculating one field (the projected density), while the mixed density prescription for L=8 requires calculating 34 different fields.
* Numerical results are shown for the nucleus 164Er (PAV method) and for the nuclei 48Cr and 50Cr (VAP method).

## Context

This work extends the exact particle number projection formalism, previously developed only for separable forces like the Pairing Plus Quadrupole model (Egido 1982), to non-separable density-dependent effective forces such as the Gogny force. It addresses the limitations of the Lipkin-Nogami method (Lipkin and Nogami, original references), which is an approximate correction for particle number symmetry breaking that cannot be derived from a variational principle and has been applied with Skyrme forces (Gall 1994, Bonche 1996), Gogny forces (Val 1996), and relativistic mean-field approaches (Ring, Lipkin-Nogami). The paper enables exact variation-after-projection calculations with realistic effective forces, which was previously impractical due to divergence problems arising from neglected exchange terms in zero-range forces.
