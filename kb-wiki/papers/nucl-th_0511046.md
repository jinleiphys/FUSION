# Hadronic dissipative effects on elliptic flow in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We study the elliptic flow coefficient v_2(eta,b) in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 A GeV as a function of pseudorapidity eta and impact parameter b. Using a hybrid approach which combines early ideal fluid dynamical evolution with late hadronic rescattering, we demonstrate strong dissipative effects from the hadronic rescattering stage on the elliptic flow. With Glauber model initial conditions, hadronic dissipation is shown to be sufficient to fully explain the differences between measured v_2 values and ideal hydrodynamic predictions. Initial conditions based on the Color Glass Condensate model generate larger elliptic flow and seem to require additional dissipation during the early quark-gluon plasma stage in order to achieve agreement with experiment.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the observed deviation of elliptic flow from ideal hydrodynamic predictions can be fully explained by dissipative effects in the hadronic rescattering stage when using Glauber model initial conditions, but that Color Glass Condensate initial conditions produce larger elliptic flow that requires additional dissipation during the early quark-gluon plasma stage to match experimental data.

## Method

The authors employ a (3+1)-dimensional hybrid model that combines ideal fluid dynamics for the early quark-gluon plasma stage with the JAM hadronic transport cascade for the late hadronic stage, switching at a temperature of 169 MeV. They compare two types of initial conditions: a modified BGK (Glauber) model and a Color Glass Condensate (Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi) approach, and compute the pseudorapidity and centrality dependence of the pT-integrated elliptic flow v2 for charged hadrons in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 A GeV.

## Key numbers

- Switching temperature from hydrodynamics to cascade: T_sw = 169 MeV
- Initial time for hydrodynamic evolution: tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section: sigma_NN^in = 42 mb
- Beam rapidity parameters for BGK model: Delta_eta = 1.3, sigma_eta = 2.1
- Soft/hard fraction in BGK model: alpha = 0.85
- Normalization constant in BGK model: C = 24.0
- Impact parameters studied: b = 4.0 fm (3-15% centrality), 6.3 fm (15-25% centrality), and 8.5 fm (25-50% centrality)
- Average saturation momentum in CGC model: <Q_s^2(x=0.01)> ~ 2.0 GeV^2/c^2 for central 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions
- Regulator in CGC model: Lambda = 0.2 GeV/c
- CGC parameters: K = 0.7, lambda = 0.2
- Upper limit for pT integration in CGC model: 3 GeV/c
- Chemical freeze-out temperature: T_ch = 170 MeV
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature in pure hydrodynamic calculations: T_dec = 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier (2+1)-dimensional hydro+cascade models by Teaney (2001) and the (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic approach of Nonaka and Bass to study the rapidity dependence of elliptic flow. It directly addresses the debate between "early viscosity" in the quark-gluon plasma phase, as argued by Heinz and Kolb (2004), and "late viscosity" in the hadronic phase, emphasized by Teaney (2003) and by Gyulassy and collaborators. The results show that constraining the viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma requires better understanding of initial conditions, and the authors propose three-dimensional jet tomography as a probe to distinguish between BGK and CGC initial conditions.
