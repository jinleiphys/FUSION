# Entropic destruction of heavy quarkonium in a rotating hot and dense medium from holography

> Previous studies have indicated that the peak of the quarkonium entropy at the deconfinement transition can be related to the entropic force which would induce the dissociation of heavy quarkonium. In this paper, we study the entropic force in a rotating hot and dense medium using AdS/CFT correspondence. It turns out that the inclusion of angular velocity increases the entropic force thus enhancing quarkonium dissociation, while chemical potential has the same effect. Furthermore, the results imply that the quarkonium dissociates easier in rotating medium compared to static case.

# Entropic destruction of heavy quarkonium in a rotating hot and dense medium from holography

## Key claim
The inclusion of angular velocity increases the entropic force acting on a heavy quark-antiquark pair in a rotating hot and dense medium, thereby enhancing quarkonium dissociation; chemical potential has the same effect. The results imply that quarkonium dissociates more easily in a rotating medium compared to a static case, but the authors note the model is not a consistent one and the entropic force mechanism applies primarily to charmonium, not bottomonium.

## Method
The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence, employing an AdS-Reissner Nordstrom (AdS-RN) black hole metric extended to a rotating case via a local Lorentz boost in the t-phi plane. They compute the inter-distance L of a heavy quark-antiquark pair and the entropy S^(2) associated with the connected U-shaped string configuration using the Nambu-Goto action, then analyze how the entropic force (proportional to the derivative of entropy with respect to inter-distance) depends on angular velocity omega and chemical potential mu.

## Key numbers
- Angular velocity values studied: omega = 0, 0.1, 0.3, and 0.5 GeV (with l = 1 GeV^{-1} for the rotating axis radius).
- Chemical potential values studied: mu = 0.025, 0.1, and 0.3 GeV.
- The temperature of the rotating black hole is given by T = (r_h/pi) * sqrt(1 - omega^2) * (1 - Q^2/2), with Q in the range 0 <= Q <= sqrt(2).
- The chemical potential is given by mu = sqrt(3) * Q * r_h * sqrt(1 - omega^2).
- The local angular velocity in noncentral heavy ion collisions is cited as 0.01-0.1 GeV, with angular momentum on the order of 10^4-10^5 hbar.
- The Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC covers chemical potentials in the range 0.025 <= mu_B <= 0.72 GeV.
- The charm quark mass is about 1.27 GeV, while the bottom quark mass is about 4.2 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the entropic force approach of Hashimoto and Kharzeev (2010) for N=4 SYM plasma to the case of a rotating medium with finite chemical potential, using the AdS-RN black hole background. It builds on prior studies of entropic force in static plasmas (Zhang and collaborators) and moving quarkonium (KBF). The results suggest that rotation and translation have similar effects on quarkonium dissociation, and the work enables future studies using more consistent holographic models such as the Kerr-AdS black hole, as well as comparisons with experimental data from RHIC and LHC on charmonium suppression.
