# Relativistic effects and quasipotential equations

> We compare the scattering amplitude resulting from the several quasipotential equations for scalar particles. We consider the Blankenbecler-Sugar, Spectator, Thompson, Erkelenz-Holinde and Equal-Time equations, which were solved numerically without decomposition into partial waves. We analyze both negative-energy state components of the propagators and retardation effects. We found that the scattering solutions of the Spectator and the Equal-Time equations are very close to the nonrelativistic solution even at high energies. The overall relativistic effect increases with the energy. The width of the band for the relative uncertainty in the real part of the scattering $T$ matrix, due to different dynamical equations, is largest for backward-scattering angles where it can be as large as 40%.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the scattering solutions of the Spectator and Equal-Time quasipotential equations are very close to the nonrelativistic solution even at high energies, but this conclusion is qualified by the fact that the study uses only scalar particles interacting through the Malfliet-Tjon potential, and the exact relativistic answer is not known, so the nonrelativistic Lippmann-Schwinger equation is taken as the reference.

## Method

The authors solved five quasipotential equations (Blankenbecler-Sugar, Spectator, Thompson, Erkelenz-Holinde, and Equal-Time) numerically without decomposition into partial waves, using the Malfliet-Tjon potential as the interaction kernel for scalar particles in the center-of-mass frame. They compared the scattering T-matrix amplitudes from each equation with the nonrelativistic Lippmann-Schwinger equation result, analyzing separately the effects of negative-energy state components of the propagators and retardation effects at two laboratory energies: 300 MeV and 1 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The relative uncertainty in the real part of the scattering T matrix, due to different dynamical equations, can be as large as 40% for backward-scattering angles.
- Calculations were performed at T_lab = 300 MeV (where small relativistic effects were expected) and T_lab = 1 GeV (intermediate energy range).
- The Malfliet-Tjon potential parameters used were: V_R = 3.1769 with mu_R = 305.86 MeV, and V_A = 7.9210 with mu_A = 613.69 MeV.
- Typical numerical grid sizes were 17-25 points for momentum magnitude and 20-50 points for angular discretization, achieving accuracy of order 1% in verification of the optical theorem.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of quasipotential equations for scalar particles, following earlier studies by Tjon and collaborators (1980) on the Blankenbecler-Sugar equation, Gross (1969, 1992) on the Spectator equation, and Mandelzweig and Wallace (1987, 1989) on the Equal-Time equation. The authors note that previous work by Wallace and Mandelzweig (1996, 1998) and by Nieuwenhuis and Tjon (1996) had shown that the Bethe-Salpeter equation in ladder approximation does not reproduce the one-body limit and excludes non-negligible crossed-box diagram contributions. The present study enables a systematic comparison of how different quasipotential formalisms incorporate relativistic effects, providing guidance for choosing appropriate equations in nuclear physics applications at intermediate energies.
