# Resonance structure of $^{8}$Be with the two-cluster resonating group method

> A two-cluster microscopic model is applied to study elastic alpha-alpha scattering and resonance structure of $^{8}$Be. The model is an algebraic version of the Resonating Group Method, which makes use complete set of oscillator functions to expand wave function of two-cluster system. Interaction between clusters is determined by well-known semi-realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials of Hasegawa-Nagata, Minnesota and Volkov. Detail analysis of resonance wave functions is carried out in oscillator, coordinate and momentum spaces. Effects of the Pauli principle on wave functions of the $^{8}$Be continuous spectrum states are thoroughly studied.

## Key claim

The algebraic version of the Resonating Group Method, using a complete set of oscillator functions, reproduces the resonance structure of ⁸Be with semi-realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials, but the agreement with experimental data depends on the choice of potential and worsens for higher angular momentum states (2⁺ and 4⁺) compared to the narrow 0⁺ ground-state resonance.

## Method

The authors employ an algebraic version of the Resonating Group Method (RGM) where the inter-cluster wave function of two alpha particles is expanded over a complete set of three-dimensional harmonic oscillator functions, transforming the dynamic equations into a system of linear algebraic equations. The Hamiltonian includes kinetic energy, semi-realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (Volkov N2, Minnesota, and modified Hasegawa-Nagata), and Coulomb interaction; the Pauli principle is treated exactly via antisymmetrization. Scattering states are obtained by splitting the oscillator space into internal and asymptotic regions, matching the internal solution to regular and irregular Coulomb functions in the asymptotic region to extract phase shifts and resonance parameters via the Breit-Wigner formula.

## Key numbers

- The 0⁺ resonance energy calculated with the MHNP is 0.091 MeV above the α+α threshold, compared to the experimental value of 0.092 MeV.
- The width of the 0⁺ resonance calculated with the MHNP is 5.183×10⁻⁶ MeV, compared to the experimental width of (5.57±0.25)×10⁻⁶ MeV.
- The 2⁺ resonance energy calculated with the MHNP is 2.818 MeV (experiment: 3.122±0.010 MeV), with a width of 1.122 MeV (experiment: 1.513±0.015 MeV).
- The 4⁺ resonance energy calculated with the MHNP is 10.633 MeV (experiment: 11.442±0.150 MeV), with a width of 1.816 MeV (experiment: 3.500 MeV).
- The optimal oscillator length b is 1.376 fm for the Volkov potential, 1.285 fm for the Minnesota potential, and 1.317 fm for the modified Hasegawa-Nagata potential.

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of ⁸Be using the algebraic RGM (Vasilevsky and collaborators, 2017) by providing a detailed analysis of resonance wave functions in oscillator, coordinate, and momentum spaces, and by systematically studying the effects of the Pauli principle on continuous spectrum states. The results enable future applications of the algebraic RGM to three-cluster systems and to reactions involving ⁸Be, and they provide a benchmark for comparing semi-realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (Volkov, Minnesota, modified Hasegawa-Nagata) in describing alpha-alpha scattering.
