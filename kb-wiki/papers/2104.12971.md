# Isospin effect on quark matter instabilities

> We have studied the mechanical and chemical instabilities as well as the liquid-gas-like phase transition in isospin asymmetric quark matter based on the NJL and the pNJL model. Areas of the mechanical instability region and the liquid-gas coexistence region are seen to be enlarged with a larger quark matter symmetry energy or in the presence of strange quarks. Our study shows that the light cluster yield ratio observed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions may not be affected much by the uncertainties of the isospin effect, and favors a smooth hadron-quark phase transition in compact stars as well as their mergers.

## Key claim

The study shows that while the isospin effect on the instability region reached in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is small due to the small isospin asymmetry, the liquid-gas-like phase transition becomes a smooth one in isospin asymmetric quark matter compared to the first-order transition in isospin symmetric quark matter; in compact stars or their mergers with large isospin asymmetries, the hadron-quark phase transition is likely to be a smooth crossover.

## Method

The authors employed the 3-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model and its Polyakov-loop extension (pNJL) to study mechanical and chemical instabilities as well as the liquid-gas-like phase transition in isospin asymmetric quark matter. They solved the mean-field thermodynamic potential self-consistently to obtain quark condensates, Dirac masses, and the equation of state, then constructed the isothermal spinodal (mechanical instability) and diffusive spinodal (chemical instability) boundaries in the density-isospin asymmetry plane. The liquid-gas coexistence region was determined via Maxwell construction on chemical potential isobars.

## Key numbers

- The critical isospin asymmetry above which there is no mechanical instability region at T=20 MeV is δ_c=1.02 for the NJL model and δ_c=1.17 for the pNJL model.
- The pNJL model has the critical end point (CEP) at a higher temperature and a larger instability region compared with the NJL model.
- The NJL model parameters used: m_u = m_d = 3.6 MeV, m_s = 87 MeV, G_SΛ² = 3.6, KΛ⁵ = 8.9, cutoff Λ = 750 MeV/c.
- The pNJL model parameters: a=664 MeV and b=0.028Λ³, giving a simultaneous crossover of chiral restoration and deconfinement around T ≈ 212 MeV.
- The pure gluodynamics first-order phase transition in the pNJL model takes place at T = 270 MeV.

## Context

This work extends studies of isospin effects on phase transitions from nuclear matter (see e.g. Li (2008) and Xu (2008)) to quark matter, addressing a gap noted by Cos20. The results are relevant for interpreting light cluster yield ratios (N_t N_p / N_d²) measured at SPS and RHIC-BES energies (Ant16, Ada19, Zha19, Zha21) as probes of the QCD phase diagram, and for understanding the hadron-quark phase transition in hybrid stars (Hei93, Mar07, Bla20) and compact star mergers (Mos19, Bau19). The findings enable future studies of how density fluctuations grow in the instability regions and how the isovector coupling affects observables in both heavy-ion collisions and astrophysics.
