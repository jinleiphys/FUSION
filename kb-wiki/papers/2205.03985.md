# The Big Bang Nucleosynthesis abundances of the light elements using improved thermonuclear reaction rates

> Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) is an important stage of a homogeneous and isotropic expanding universe. The results of calculation of the synthesis of light elements during this epoch can then be compared with the abundances of the light elements. The theoretical calculation of the BBN model depends on the initial conditions of the early universe and reaction cross sections measured by the nuclear physics experiment. Recently, an update of the NACRE (Nuclear Astrophysics Compilation of REactions) database is presented. This improved compilation comprises thermonuclear reaction rates for 34 two-body reactions on light nuclides (fifteen are particle transfer reactions and nineteen are radiative capture reactions). In this work, we calculate the BBN abundances by using these updated thermonuclear reaction rates in the framework of the code \texttt{AlterBBN}. Our results suggest that the new numerical result of the primordial Lithium abundance is 7.1 percent larger than the previous calculation.

## Key claim
Using the updated NACRE II thermonuclear reaction rates in the AlterBBN code increases the calculated primordial lithium abundance by 7.1 percent relative to previous calculations, which worsens the discrepancy between BBN predictions and observed lithium abundances (the "primordial lithium problem").

## Method
The authors implemented 29 updated two-body thermonuclear reaction rates from the NACRE II compilation (15 particle transfer and 19 radiative capture reactions) into the AlterBBN code, which solves the Friedmann equation, energy conservation, and a network of 88 nuclear reactions using 4th-order Runge-Kutta integration. The code assumes 3.0 neutrino species, a baryon-to-photon ratio of 6.09e-10, an initial temperature of 2.7e10 K (2.3 MeV), and a neutron lifetime of 879.4 seconds. The resulting primordial abundances of light elements were compared to both observational data and previous calculations by Arbey (2012).

## Key numbers
- Primordial lithium-7 abundance: 5.028e-10 (Li/H ratio), a 7.1 percent increase from the previous calculation of 4.694e-10
- Helium-4 mass fraction (Yp): 0.2462, compared to the previous calculation of 0.2476 and the observational value of 0.2453 ± 0.0034
- Deuterium abundance (2H/H): 2.589e-5, compared to the previous calculation of 2.515e-5 and the observational value of (2.527 ± 0.030)e-5
- Helium-3 abundance (3He/H): 1.045e-5, compared to the previous calculation of 1.015e-5 and the observational upper limit of (1.1 ± 0.2)e-5
- Baryon-to-photon ratio used: 6.09e-10, from Planck observations

## Context
This work extends the BBN abundance calculations of Arbey (2012) using the AlterBBN code by incorporating the updated NACRE II thermonuclear reaction rates from the compilation published in NPA2013. The results confirm that the updated rates do not resolve the primordial lithium problem, where BBN predictions (5.028e-10) remain nearly three times larger than the observed lithium abundance of 1.58e-10. The authors note that recent measurements of the 7Be(n,alpha)4He reaction from 10 meV to 10 keV neutron energy hint at a minor role for this reaction in BBN, leaving the lithium problem unsolved and motivating future inclusion of new experimental data.
