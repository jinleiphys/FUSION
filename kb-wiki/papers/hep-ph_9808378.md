# Bremsstrahlung of a Quark Propagating through a Nucleus

> The density of gluons produced in the central rapidity region of a heavy ion collision is poorly known. We investigate the influence of the effects of quantum coherence on the transverse momentum distribution of photons and gluons radiated by a quark propagating through nuclear matter. We describe the case that the radiation time substantially exceeds the nuclear radius (the relevant case for RHIC and LHC energies), which is different from what is known as Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect corresponding to an infinite medium. We find suppression of the radiation spectrum at small transverse photon/gluon momentum k_T, but enhancement for k_T>1GeV. Any nuclear effects vanish for k_T > 10GeV. Our results allow also to calculate the k_T dependent nuclear effects in prompt photon, light and heavy (Drell-Yan) dilepton and hadron production.

## Key claim

The authors investigate how quantum coherence effects modify the transverse momentum distribution of photons and gluons radiated by a quark passing through a nucleus, specifically for the case where the radiation (formation) time substantially exceeds the nuclear radius, a regime relevant for RHIC and LHC energies but distinct from the standard Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect which assumes an infinite medium. They find that coherence suppresses radiation at small transverse momentum \(k_T\) but enhances it for \(k_T > 1\) GeV, with all nuclear effects vanishing for \(k_T \geq 10\) GeV.

## Method

The authors use a light-cone approach in impact-parameter representation, where the quark's Fock state containing a photon or gluon fluctuation is treated as a color dipole with a "frozen" transverse size during propagation through the nucleus. They extend the factorized formalism of Gribov (1970) and later authors to compute differential cross sections as functions of the radiated quantum's transverse momentum \(k_T\), eikonalizing the nuclear interaction by replacing the nucleon dipole cross section \(\sigma_{\bar qq}\) with a nuclear survival factor involving the nuclear thickness function \(T(b)\). For gluon radiation, they use the cross section of a colorless \(g\bar qq\) system and apply the dipole approximation \(\sigma_{\bar qq}(\rho) \approx C \rho^2\) to obtain analytic results.

## Key numbers

- Suppression of radiation occurs for \(k_T < 1\) GeV; enhancement (antishadowing) appears for \(k_T > 1\) GeV.
- Nuclear effects vanish for \(k_T \geq 10\) GeV.
- For Drell-Yan lepton pair production at virtual photon mass \(m_{\gamma^*} = 4\) GeV, the exponent \(n(k_T,\alpha)\) of the atomic number dependence (parameterized as \(A^n\)) is calculated for \(A=200\) using a constant nuclear density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- For gluon radiation at \(\alpha \ll 1\), the differential cross section for a nucleon target is approximately \(\frac{6 C \alpha_s}{\pi^2} \frac{k_T^4 + m_g^4}{(k_T^2 + m_g^2)^4}\) for transverse polarization, with effective gluon masses \(m_g \approx 0.15\) GeV or \(m_g = 0.75\) GeV considered.
- The formation time is given by \(t_f = 2\omega / k_T^2\) (in the nuclear rest frame), and for RHIC/LHC energies \(t_f \gg R_A\) holds for gluons with a few GeV transverse momentum.

## Context

This work extends the light-cone dipole approach of Hufner, Kopeliovich, and Nemchik (1996) and Zakharov (1996) to differential cross sections in \(k_T\), going beyond the integrated radiation spectra treated previously. It contrasts with the standard LPM effect (Landau and Pomeranchuk, 1955; Migdal, 1956) which applies to infinite media and short formation times, and it provides a framework to calculate nuclear effects in prompt photon production, Drell-Yan dilepton production, and inclusive hadron production (the Cronin effect) at collider energies. The results enable predictions for proton-nucleus and heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where the condition \(t_f \gg R_A\) is well satisfied.
