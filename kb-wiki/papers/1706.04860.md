# Transverse momentum spectra of hadrons in high energy pp and heavy ion collisions

> We present a study of transverse momentum ($p_{T}$) spectra of unidentified charged particles in pp collisions at RHIC and LHC energies from $\sqrt{s}$ = 62.4 GeV to 13 TeV using Tsallis/Hagedorn function. The power law of Tsallis/Hagedorn form gives excellent description of the hadron spectra in $p_{T}$ range from 0.2 to 300 GeV/$c$. The power index $n$ of the $p_T$ distributions is found to follow a function of the type $a+b/\sqrt {s}$ with asymptotic value $a = 5.72$. The parameter $T$ governing the soft bulk contribution to the spectra remains almost same over wide range of collision energies. We also provide a Tsallis/Hagedorn fit to the $p_{T}$ spectra of hadrons in pPb and different centralities of PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV. The data/fit shows deviations from the Tsallis distribution which become more pronounced as the system size increases. We suggest simple modifications in the Tsallis/Hagedorn power law function and show that the above deviations can be attributed to the transverse flow in low $p_T$ region and to the in-medium energy loss in high $p_T$ region.

## Key claim

The Tsallis/Hagedorn function describes charged hadron transverse momentum spectra in pp collisions from 62.4 GeV to 13 TeV, but in heavy-ion collisions the data show systematic deviations that increase with system size; these deviations can be attributed to transverse flow at low pT and in-medium energy loss at high pT.

## Method

The authors fitted the Tsallis/Hagedorn distribution function to published charged-particle pT spectra from PHENIX, ALICE, and CMS experiments for pp collisions at eight center-of-mass energies from 62.4 GeV to 13 TeV, and for pPb and six centrality classes of PbPb collisions at 5.02 TeV. They then proposed a modified two-component function: one term incorporating an exponential factor for transverse flow at low pT (below a threshold pTth) and another term incorporating a power-law energy-loss shift proportional to (pT/q0)^alpha at high pT (above 7 GeV/c). The parameters of both the standard and modified functions were extracted from fits to the data.

## Key numbers

* Power index n for unidentified charged particles in pp collisions follows n(sqrt(s)) = a + b/sqrt(s) with asymptotic value a = 6.81 +/- 0.06 and b = 59.24 +/- 3.53 GeV (chi^2/NDF = 0.78).
* Tsallis temperature parameter T for unidentified charged particles in pp collisions follows T(sqrt(s)) = c + d/sqrt(s) with c = 0.082 +/- 0.002 GeV and d = 0.151 +/- 0.048 GeV^2 (chi^2/NDF = 0.71).
* Asymptotic n values for identified particles: charged pions 6.41 +/- 0.10, neutral pions 7.23 +/- 0.48, charged kaons 6.72 +/- 0.18, protons 8.76 +/- 0.36.
* For the most central PbPb collisions (0-5%) at 5.02 TeV, the modified Tsallis function yields n1 = 8.28 +/- 0.92, beta = 0.62 +/- 0.06, alpha = 0.47 +/- 0.03, B = 5.72 +/- 0.23 GeV/c, with chi^2/NDF = 0.29.
* The standard Tsallis fit to pp collisions at 5.02 TeV gives chi^2/NDF = 0.79; the modified Tsallis fit improves this to chi^2/NDF = 0.31.

## Context

This work extends studies by Cleymans (2015) and Wong (2015) on Tsallis fits to pp hadron spectra at LHC energies, and by Tang (2008) and Khandai (2013) on including transverse flow in heavy-ion Tsallis fits. The authors show that the log-periodic oscillation pattern in data/fit ratios observed by Wilk and Rybczynski (2014-2015) for PbPb collisions can be better described by explicitly modeling transverse flow and energy loss. The modified function enables quantification of medium effects (flow velocity beta and energy-loss parameter B) across system sizes from pp to central PbPb, and the asymptotic n values provide a baseline for comparing particle production mechanisms across collision energies.
