# Quartet correlations in N=Z nuclei induced by realistic two-body interactions

> Two variational quartet models previously employed in a treatment of pairing forces are extended to the case of a general two-body interaction. One model approximates the nuclear states as a condensate of identical quartets with angular momentum $J=0$ and isospin $T=0$ while the other let these quartets to be all different from each other. With these models we investigate the role of alpha-like quartet correlations both in the ground state and in the lowest $J=0$, $T=0$ excited states of even-even $N=Z$ nuclei in the $sd$-shell. We show that the ground state correlations of these nuclei can be described to a good extent in terms of a condensate of alpha-like quartets. This turns out to be especially the case for the nucleus $^{32}$S for which the overlap between this condensate and the shell model wave function is found close to one. In the same nucleus, a similar overlap is found also in the case of the first excited $0^+$ state. No clear correspondence is observed instead between the second excited states of the quartet models and the shell model eigenstates in all the cases examined.

## Key claim

The ground states of even-even N=Z nuclei in the sd-shell can be described to a good extent as condensates of identical alpha-like quartets (J=0, T=0), but this description works especially well for 32S where the overlap between the condensate and the shell model wave function is close to one; the same holds for the first excited 0+ state in 32S, but no clear correspondence is found for second excited states.

## Method

The authors extended two variational quartet models (QCM and QM) previously used for pairing forces to a general two-body shell-model Hamiltonian (USDB interaction). In the Quartet Condensation Model (QCM), nuclear states are approximated as a condensate of identical J=0, T=0 alpha-like quartets built from all possible isovector and isoscalar pairs; in the Quartet Model (QM), the quartets are all different from each other and determined variationally for each nucleus. The quartet amplitudes are optimized by minimizing the energy expectation value, and the resulting states are compared to exact shell model results for 20Ne, 24Mg, 28Si, and 32S.

## Key numbers

- Ground state correlation energy for 32S: QCM gives 122.02 MeV (0.40% deviation from SM value 122.51 MeV); QM gives 122.29 MeV (0.18% deviation)
- Overlap between QCM and SM ground state for 32S: 0.98; for QM and SM: 0.99
- Overlap between QCM and SM ground state for 24Mg: 0.85; for 28Si: 0.86
- Overlap between QCM and SM first excited 0+ state for 32S: 0.95; for QM and SM: 0.99
- Interaction energy between two quartets V(2) for full USDB interaction: -3.51 MeV (attractive)
- Interaction energy between two quartets V(2) for pairing-only USDB components: +3.38 MeV (repulsive)
- Single-particle occupancies in the QCM quartet for 32S: n_d5/2 = 3.46, n_s1/2 = 0.34, n_d3/2 = 0.21

## Context

This work extends the quartet condensation approach previously applied to pairing Hamiltonians (Sambataro and Sandulescu, 2013; 2015) to realistic two-body interactions, and improves upon the static quartet model of Sambataro (2015) where quartets were fixed from 20Ne. The results show that alpha-like quartet correlations are significant in sd-shell N=Z nuclei, with the full USDB interaction producing attractive quartet-quartet interactions, in contrast to the repulsive interactions found for pure isovector-isoscalar pairing forces (Sambataro and Sandulescu, 2015) and for the exactly solvable SU(4) pairing model (Dobes and Pittel, 1998). The work enables future investigations of whether QCM states near the alpha emission threshold correspond to physical alpha cluster states, as studied by Tohsaki et al. (2001).
