# A systematic study of initial state quark energy loss in fixed target proton nucleus collision

> In this article, we investigate parton energy loss in cold nuclear matter by studying the ratio of Drell-Yan production cross sections in fixed-target proton-nucleus (p + A) collisions. We analyze Drell-Yan production cross-section data from the Fermilab E866 and E906 experiments using two different quark energy loss parametrization models and various parton distribution functions for 800 GeV and 120 GeV proton beams incident on light and heavy nuclear targets. The sensitivity of the energy loss parameter on the employed parton distribution function has been thoroughly investigated. Our results have been used to predict the target mass dependence of Drell-Yan production in upcoming proton-induced collisions at SPS and FAIR.

## Key claim
The paper systematically reanalyzes Drell-Yan data from Fermilab E866 (800 GeV) and E906 (120 GeV) to extract the initial-state quark energy loss in cold nuclear matter, finding that the extracted energy loss parameter is highly sensitive to the chosen parton distribution function (PDF) and that the precise path-length dependence (linear vs. quadratic) of the energy loss remains unsettled.

## Method
The authors performed a leading-order (LO) analysis of nuclear Drell-Yan cross-section ratios (Fe/Be, W/Be for E866; Fe/C, W/C for E906) as a function of Feynman-x ($x_F$). They employed two phenomenological energy-loss models, the BH model (linear path-length dependence) and the BDMPS model (quadratic path-length dependence), combined with two different PDF sets: CT18ANLO (free proton) and EPPS21 NLO (nuclear PDF). A simultaneous fit to the heavy-target ratios was performed separately for each experiment to extract the energy-loss parameters $\alpha$ (BH) and $\beta$ (BDMPS).

## Key numbers
*   For E866 (800 GeV) with EPPS21 NLO PDF: BH model yields $\alpha = 0.465 \pm 0.203$ GeV/fm, corresponding to a specific energy loss $dE/dx = 0.46 \pm 0.20$ GeV/fm in tungsten.
*   For E866 (800 GeV) with EPPS21 NLO PDF: BDMPS model yields $\beta = 0.068 \pm 0.030$ GeV/fm$^2$, corresponding to $dE/dx = 0.35 \pm 0.15$ GeV/fm in tungsten.
*   For E906 (120 GeV) with EPPS21 NLO PDF: BH model yields $\alpha = 0.467 \pm 0.053$ GeV/fm, corresponding to $dE/dx = 0.47 \pm 0.05$ GeV/fm in tungsten.
*   For E906 (120 GeV) with EPPS21 NLO PDF: BDMPS model yields $\beta = 0.068 \pm 0.008$ GeV/fm$^2$, corresponding to $dE/dx = 0.35 \pm 0.04$ GeV/fm in tungsten.
*   The $\chi^2$/ndf for the E906 BH fit with EPPS21 NLO is 0.31, while for the BDMPS fit it is 0.56.

## Context
This work extends previous analyses of Drell-Yan data from E772, E866, and E906 (Arleo 2018, Song 2020, Xu 2022) by performing a separate, simultaneous fit for each experiment to probe beam-energy dependence, rather than a global fit. It explicitly avoids using data from $\pi^- + A$ collisions (NA3, NA10) due to larger statistical errors and less precise pion PDFs. The results are used to predict the target-mass dependence of Drell-Yan production for upcoming experiments at SPS (NA60+) and FAIR (CBM), aiming to resolve the ambiguity between linear (BH) and quadratic (BDMPS) path-length dependence of quark energy loss.
