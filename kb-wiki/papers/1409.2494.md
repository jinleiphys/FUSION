# Multiple scattering effects on heavy meson production in p+A collisions at backward rapidity

> We study the incoherent multiple scattering effects on heavy meson production in the backward rapidity region of p+A collisions within the generalized high-twist factorization formalism. We calculate explicitly the double scattering contributions to the heavy meson differential cross sections by taking into account both initial-state and final-state interactions, and find that these corrections are positive. We further evaluate the nuclear modification factor for muons that come form the semi-leptonic decays of heavy flavor mesons. Phenomenological applications in d+Au collisions at a center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV at RHIC and in p+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV at the LHC are presented. We find that incoherent multiple scattering can describe rather well the observed nuclear enhancement in the intermediate $p_T$ region for such reactions.

## Key claim

Incoherent multiple scattering (double scattering) gives positive contributions to heavy meson differential cross sections in the backward rapidity region of p+A collisions, and these contributions can describe the observed nuclear enhancement in the intermediate $p_T$ region, but the heavy quark mass prevents the double scattering corrections from being written in the same simple compact form as for light hadron production.

## Method

The authors generalized the high-twist factorization formalism, previously applied to light hadron production, to open heavy flavor (charm and beauty) meson production in p+A collisions. They calculated explicitly the double scattering contributions to the differential cross sections by taking into account both initial-state and final-state interactions for the partonic processes $q\bar q \to Q\bar Q$ and $gg \to Q\bar Q$, as well as light flavor fragmentation contributions. The nuclear modification factor $R_{pA}$ for muons from semi-leptonic heavy flavor decays was then evaluated using PYTHIA for the decay kinematics, with the twist-4 correlation functions parametrized as $\frac{4\pi^2\alpha_s}{N_c}\,T_{q,g/A}^{(I)}(x)=\xi^2 \left(A^{1/3}-1\right) f_{q,g/A}(x)$ using $\xi^2=0.09-0.12$ GeV$^2$ extracted from deep inelastic scattering.

## Key numbers

* At RHIC: d+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, backward rapidity $-2<y<-1.4$, the nuclear modification factor $R_{pA}$ for muons from heavy flavor decays shows Cronin-like enhancement described by the model with $\xi^2=0.09-0.12$ GeV$^2$.
* At LHC: p+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV, backward rapidity $-4<y<-2.96$, the nuclear modification factor $R_{pA}$ for muons from heavy flavor decays is described by the model, though slightly below the ALICE data.
* The heavy quark mass $m_c = 1.5$ GeV was used for $D$ meson production, with factorization/renormalization scale $\mu \sim m_T=\sqrt{m_c^2+p_{h\perp}^2}$.
* A phenomenological $K$-factor of $K=2$ was used to account for higher order QCD contributions in the p+p baseline cross sections at both RHIC and LHC energies.
* The twist-4 correlation function parameter $\xi^2=0.09-0.12$ GeV$^2$ was used, originally extracted from deep inelastic scattering data in e+A collisions by Qiu and Vitev (2003).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of incoherent multiple scattering for light hadron production in p+A collisions at backward rapidity (Kang et al., 2013) to open heavy flavor production. It contrasts with the approach using universal nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs), such as the EPS09 parametrization by Eskola et al. (2009), which gives a less satisfactory description of the RHIC heavy flavor decay muon data. The results indicate that cold nuclear matter effects may be non-universal (process-dependent), and the backward rapidity measurements provide a unique opportunity to investigate perturbative QCD dynamics of multiple parton interactions and constrain the properties of cold nuclear matter.
