# RPA equations and the instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter equation

> We give a derivation of the particle-hole RPA equations for an interacting multi-fermion system by applying the instantaneous approximation to the amputated two-fermion propagator of the system. In relativistic field theory the same approximation leads from the fermion-antifermion Bethe-Salpeter equation to the Salpeter equation. We show that RPA equations and Salpeter equation are indeed equivalent.

# RPA equations and the instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter equation

## Key claim
The particle-hole RPA equations for an interacting multi-fermion system are equivalent to the Salpeter equation from relativistic field theory, provided one applies the instantaneous approximation to the amputated two-fermion propagator and substitutes full one-fermion propagators by free ones. The derivation holds for both nonrelativistic and relativistic fermionic systems, but the instantaneous approximation cannot easily be interpreted in terms of Feynman diagrams in more general relativistic field theories like QCD.

## Method
The authors derive the RPA equations by starting from the two-fermion propagator and applying the instantaneous approximation to the amputated two-fermion propagator M, assuming particles and holes interact instantaneously. They then substitute the full one-fermion propagators by free propagators, which leads to a matrix equation h F^ρ = ν_ρ N F^ρ. Separately, they take the Salpeter equation (the instantaneous limit of the fermion-antifermion Bethe-Salpeter equation) and rewrite it in a discrete basis using free Dirac spinors, showing it takes the identical matrix form.

## Key numbers
- The polarization propagator Π(ν) has poles at ν = ±(E_λ − E_0 − iε), where E_λ are excited state energies and E_0 is the ground state energy.
- The free one-fermion propagator in the chosen basis is given by [iS^F_0(t)]_{αβ} = e^{-iε_α t} δ_{αβ} · [Θ(t) Θ(α−n) − Θ(−t) Θ(n−α)].
- The matrix Ω in the RPA equation has diagonal elements (ε_A − ε_b) for particle-hole indices (Ab) and (ε_B − ε_a) for hole-particle indices (aB).
- The Salpeter equation in matrix form uses the free Dirac Hamiltonian H(p) = γ^0 (γ·p + m) with ω = √(p² + m²).

## Context
This work extends the known correspondence between nonrelativistic many-body methods and relativistic field theory, previously established for the Hartree-Fock approximation and the Gap equation in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Nambu and Jona-Lasinio, 1961). The authors close a gap in the literature by providing a systematic derivation connecting the RPA equations (as treated in Ring and Schuck, 1980) to the Bethe-Salpeter equation (Bethe and Salpeter, 1951; Gell-Mann and Low, 1951) and its instantaneous limit, the Salpeter equation (Salpeter, 1952). The equivalence enables transferring results on RPA stability and spurious solutions from nuclear physics to the Salpeter equation, as noted by the authors with reference to Piekarewicz (1993) for a related direct derivation.
