# Antikaon condensation in magnetized neutron star matter within the framework of the $σ$-cut scheme

> This study investigates the effects of strong magnetic fields on antikaon condensation in neutron star matter using the extended FSUGold model model. It is found that the presence of strong magnetic fields alters the threshold density of antikaon condensation significantly, which means the threshold density of antikaon condensation is shifted to higher density compared with the magnetic field-free case. In the presence of strong magnetic fields, the equation of state (EoS) becomes stiffer than that of the zero field case. The effects of the $σ$-cut scheme on the EoS are also researched when the appearance of antikaon condensation is occurred. Through careful choice of the parameter of the $σ$-cut scheme, we are able to produce a maximum mass neutron star heavier than 2$M_{sun}$.

# Antikaon condensation in magnetized neutron star matter within the framework of the σ-cut scheme

## Key claim

Strong magnetic fields shift the threshold density of antikaon condensation to higher density compared to the field-free case, and the σ-cut scheme can stiffen the equation of state enough to produce a neutron star heavier than 2 solar masses, but only with careful choice of the σ-cut parameter cσ; the effect is conditional on the specific optical potential depth UK and magnetic field strength B.

## Method

The authors use the extended FSUGold relativistic mean field model to describe neutron star matter with antikaon (K−) condensation in the presence of strong magnetic fields, incorporating Landau quantization of charged particles and anomalous magnetic moments of nucleons. The σ-cut scheme is applied by adding a logarithmic function ΔU(σ) to the Lagrangian to quench the decrease of the nucleon effective mass at high density, with the parameter cσ controlling the strength of the modification. The equations of state are computed self-consistently under β-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation is solved for the mass-radius relation, assuming spherical symmetry despite the magnetic field.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of antikaon condensation in neutron star matter under strong magnetic fields (e.g., PhysRevC.77.045804, PhysRevC.107.035807, Dey 2002) by applying the σ-cut scheme originally proposed by Maslov (2015) to stiffen the equation of state. The FSUGold model alone produces maximum masses below the observed 2 M⊙ threshold, a problem noted by Antoniadis (2013) and others. The σ-cut scheme had previously been applied to hyperon-rich matter by Ma (2022, 2023) and Wu (2020), but this work investigates its effectiveness when antikaon condensation is also present under magnetic fields. The results enable future studies of crust properties within this framework and help constrain the possible internal magnetic field strengths of neutron stars.
