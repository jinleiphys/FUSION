# Thermal hadron production in relativistic nuclear collisions

> We present the status of the description of hadron production in central nucleus-nucleus collisions within the statistical model . Extending previous studies by inclusion of very high-mass resonances (m>2 GeV), and the up-to-now neglected scalar $σ$ meson leads to an improved description of the data. In particular, the hitherto poorly reproduced energy dependence of the K+/pi+ ratio at SPS energies ("the horn") is now well described through the connection to the hadronic mass spectrum and, implicitly, Hagedorn's limiting temperature.

## Key claim

By including very high-mass resonances (m > 2 GeV) and the previously neglected scalar σ meson in the hadronic mass spectrum, the statistical model now reproduces the energy dependence of the K+/π+ ratio at SPS energies ("the horn"), which earlier statistical model calculations could not describe.

## Method

The authors performed global fits of hadron yields measured in central nucleus-nucleus collisions from AGS to RHIC energies using a hadro-chemical equilibrium model with chemical freeze-out temperature T and baryo-chemical potential μb as free parameters. They extended the hadronic mass spectrum to include resonances above 2 GeV and the σ meson, then compared the calculated yields and ratios to experimental data across the full energy range.

## Key numbers

- Inclusion of the σ meson and higher-lying resonances increases calculated pion yields by about 16%, with the increase leveling off near the limiting temperature
- For Λ hyperons, the new high-mass resonances increase calculated production by about 22%
- For protons, the increase is up to 6%; for kaons, about 7%
- The limiting temperature Tlim is 164 MeV, slightly higher than the earlier value of 161 ± 4 MeV (Andronic 2005)
- At top RHIC energy (√sNN = 200 GeV), a fit of STAR yields alone gives T = 162 MeV, μb = 32 MeV, V = 2400 fm³, with χ²/Ndf = 9.0/11
- The parametrization for μb is: μb [MeV] = 1303 / (1 + 0.286 √sNN(GeV))
- The K+/π+ maximum occurs at a beam energy of 30 AGeV (√sNN ≈ 8 GeV)
- The Λ/π ratio shows a pronounced maximum at √sNN ≈ 5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the statistical model studies of Andronic (2005) by including an improved hadronic mass spectrum, and addresses a long-standing discrepancy where the K+/π+ "horn" structure could not be reproduced by earlier thermal models (Andronic 2005, Becattini 2005, Letessier 2005) or hadronic cascade models (NA49 2006). The results support the interpretation that the horn structure reflects the QCD phase boundary and Hagedorn's limiting temperature (Hagedorn 1985), and open the question of whether chemical freeze-out at lower energies traces the QCD phase boundary or is influenced by exotic phases (McLerran and Pisarski 2007). The model enables predictions for future measurements at RHIC low-energy runs, the FAIR facility, and the LHC.
