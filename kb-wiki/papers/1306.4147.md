# Feasibility guidelines for kaonic-atom experiments with ultra-high-resolution X-ray spectrometry

> Recent studies of strong interaction effects in kaonic atoms suggest that analysing so-called `lower' and `upper' levels in the same atom could separate one-nucleon absorption from multinucleon processes. The present work examines the feasibility of direct measurements of upper level widths in addition to lower level widths in future experiments, using superconducting microcalorimeter detectors. About ten elements are identified as possible candidates for such experiments, all of medium-weight and heavy nuclei. New experiments focused on achieving good accuracy for widths of such pairs of levels could contribute significantly to our knowledge of the $K^-$-nucleon interaction in the nuclear medium.

## Key claim

Direct measurements of upper-level strong-interaction widths in kaonic atoms using superconducting microcalorimeter detectors are feasible for about ten medium-weight and heavy nuclei, provided the ratio of the upper-level width to the transition energy feeding that level is comparable to or larger than the detector's relative energy resolution (approximately 53 eV at 100 keV, scaling as the square root of energy).

## Method

The authors used a phenomenological K⁻-nucleus optical potential (Eq. 3) with parameters fitted to existing kaonic-atom data (χ²=103 for 65 data points) to calculate binding energies, strong-interaction widths, and transition energies for circular states in kaonic atoms across the periodic table. They then performed atomic cascade calculations using a program due to Batty (1999) to obtain absolute and relative X-ray yields, assuming a statistical (2l+1) initial population distribution with possible deviations parameterized by an exponential factor. Feasibility was assessed by comparing the calculated ratio Γ_U/E_X (upper-level width divided by the X-ray transition energy feeding that level) against the detector's energy resolution, taken as either a fixed 53 eV or scaled as 53 × √(E_X/100) eV.

## Key numbers

- Detector energy resolution: 22 eV FWHM at ~100 keV for a single pixel; average 53 eV at ~100 keV for a 256-pixel spectrometer with 5 cm² collecting area (Bennett 2012)
- Phenomenological potential parameters: b = -0.15 + i0.62 fm, B = 1.65 - i0.045 fm, ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³, producing χ² = 103 for 65 data points
- For Ni: rms radius of the 1N real term is 3.82 fm, of the mN real term is 2.86 fm (difference 0.95 fm); for the imaginary part, 1N is 4.46 fm, mN is 3.12 fm (difference 1.34 fm)
- For Pb: rms radius of the 1N real term is 5.71 fm, of the mN real term is 4.78 fm (difference 0.93 fm); for the imaginary part, 1N is 6.23 fm, mN is 5.00 fm (difference 1.23 fm)
- Relative yields for the upper-to-lower level transition range from 0.019 (Cr, 3d-4f) to 0.331 (Yb, 6h-7i)
- Absolute yields for the transition feeding the upper level range from 0.570 (Cr) to 0.706 (Sn)
- Candidate elements identified: Ca, Ti, Cr (3d-4f); Se, Kr, Sr, Zr (4f-5g); Sn, Te, Ba (5g-6h); Yb, Ta, Pb (6h-7i)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Friedman, Gal, and collaborators (2013) who showed that comparing "lower" and "upper" levels in the same kaonic atom could separate one-nucleon (1N) absorption from multinucleon (mN) processes, building on the antikaon-nucleon scattering amplitudes of Ikeda, Hyodo, and Weise (2011, 2012) constrained by the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen measurements. The paper enables future experiments using TES microcalorimeter detectors to directly measure upper-level widths, which previously could only be inferred from relative yields with large uncertainties; the authors demonstrate that a carefully selected set of about six targets could provide information equivalent to the full existing database of 24 kaonic-atom species, with parameter uncertainties reduced by nearly 50% when upper-level widths replace relative yields.
