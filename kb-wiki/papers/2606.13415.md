# Transport simulations with a constrained momentum-dependent Chiral Mean Field EoS at different iso-spin fractions

> We present a comparison of the UrQMD model using a chiral mean field EoS (CMF) with flow and pion production data in heavy ion collisions at low beam energies $E_{\mathrm{lab}}=0.2-2.0 A$ GeV and varying iso-spin fraction. The CMF model parameters are constrained by known properties of the high density equation of state of QCD at varying iso-spin fractions. This allows us to calculate the equation of state as well as nuclear interactions for different physical systems like neutron stars and heavy ion collisions in a consistent way. It is found that heavy ion reactions at the upcoming FAIR facility will only have marginal sensitivity on the iso-spin dependence of the high-density equation of state. At lower beam energies, comparing to FOPI, HADES and S$π$rit data, the sensitivity is higher but the observed deviations and systematic uncertainties of the existing data are larger than the sensitivity to the symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The chiral mean field (CMF) equation of state, constrained by nuclear saturation properties and chiral effective field theory, predicts that the nuclear potential becomes almost insensitive to iso-spin content at densities above twice nuclear saturation density, making it difficult to constrain the high-density symmetry energy from heavy ion collisions. The authors find that the sensitivity of heavy ion observables to the iso-spin dependence of the high-density equation of state is marginal at FAIR energies and, at lower beam energies, the observed deviations and systematic uncertainties of existing data are larger than the sensitivity to the symmetry energy.

## Method

The authors implemented a chiral mean field (CMF) equation of state into the UrQMD transport model, with model parameters adjusted to match nuclear saturation properties (density 0.16 fm^-3, binding energy -16 MeV, incompressibility 242 MeV) and the momentum dependence of the nuclear optical potential from elastic proton-nucleus scattering data of Hama (1990). They then performed UrQMD-CMF simulations for heavy ion collisions at beam energies from 0.2 to 2.0 A GeV, using two fixed values of iso-spin per baryon (I_3/A=0 for symmetric matter and I_3/A=-0.1 corresponding to Au nuclei), and compared the resulting elliptic flow and charged pion production to experimental data from FOPI, HADES, and SπRIT.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: n_0 = 0.16 fm^-3; binding energy E/A = -16 MeV; incompressibility K = 242 MeV
- Nuclear symmetry energy: E_sym = 31.9 MeV; slope of symmetry energy: L = 54 MeV
- Beam energy range for heavy ion simulations: E_lab = 0.2 - 2.0 A GeV
- Elliptic flow comparisons performed at E_lab = 1.2 A GeV (FOPI) and E_lab = 1.23 A GeV (HADES)
- SπRIT comparisons performed at E_lab = 0.27 A GeV for three Sn isotope systems with varying neutron-to-proton ratios
- Simulation runtime limited to t = 40 fm/c to avoid late stage artifacts
- CMF coupling parameters: g_σN^(1) = -10.78, m_0 = 300 MeV, g_ωN = 8.14, g_ρN = 5.0, λ_6 = 1.35 × 10^-4 MeV^2, k_0 = 571^2 MeV^2

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous approach (Steinheimer 2024) of using a chiral mean field model to consistently describe both heavy ion reactions and neutron star observables, now specifically studying the sensitivity of observables to iso-spin fraction. The results contradict the expectation that charged pion ratios (as suggested by Li 2002 and the SπRIT collaboration 2021) or differences in proton and neutron flow (as suggested by Li 2008 and Tsang 2008) can effectively constrain the high-density symmetry energy, because the CMF model predicts that the nuclear potential becomes nearly iso-spin independent above twice nuclear saturation density due to the appearance of parity partners and other degrees of freedom. The work enables future studies to focus on the systematic uncertainties in existing data rather than the iso-spin dependence of the equation of state, and provides a consistent framework for connecting neutron star constraints from NICER (Riley 2019, Riley 2021, Choudhury 2024) with heavy ion observables.
