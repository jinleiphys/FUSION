# Estimation of potential radius based on momentum distribution of a constituent particle

> We propose using the potential radius as a probe of the structure of hadrons, particularly to classify exotic hadrons as hadronic or quark composite states.In this study, we focus on the radius of the effective potential felt by each constituent particle. Using a simple model with a square-well potential, we demonstrate that the potential radius can be estimated from the momentum distribution of a constituent particle not only for deeply bound states but also for shallowly bound states.We find that the momentum-based quantity provides a more robust estimate of the potential radius in the shallow-binding regime.This is because the momentum-based length scale decreases to zero as the potential radius vanishes, whereas the RMS radius approaches a finite value set by the binding energy.As a result, the momentum distribution avoids the finite-intercept problem that can make the inverse estimate of the potential radius ill-defined.With future experimental data on the momentum distribution of the constituent nucleon in $\overline{K}NNN$ production at J-PARC, the potential radius may be determined within the present framework.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the potential radius of a hadronic system can be estimated from the momentum distribution of a constituent particle, and that the momentum-based length scale \(r_p\) provides a more robust estimate than the RMS radius \(\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle}\) specifically in the shallow-binding regime (\(\varrho R < 1\)), because \(r_p\) avoids the finite-intercept problem that makes inverse estimation of the potential radius ill-defined.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrödinger equation for a ground state in a three-dimensional square-well potential, treating the mass \(\mu\), potential radius \(R\), and binding energy \(b\) as input parameters while the potential depth \(V_0\) is determined by \(R\) and \(b\). They calculate the RMS radius \(\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle}\) and the RMS momentum \(\sqrt{\langle p^2 \rangle}\) and introduce a momentum-based length scale \(r_p \equiv \frac{3}{2}\hbar/\sqrt{\langle p^2 \rangle}\) to compare both quantities on the same length scale. The dimensionless parameter \(\varrho R\) (where \(\varrho = \sqrt{2\mu b}/\hbar\)) characterizes the binding regime, with \(\varrho R > 1\) for deeply bound states and \(\varrho R < 1\) for shallowly bound states.

## Key numbers

* For the deuteron using the CD-Bonn potential: \(\sqrt{\langle p^2 \rangle} = 121\ \mathrm{MeV}/c\), giving \(r_p = 2.4\ \mathrm{fm}\); with reduced mass \(469\ \mathrm{MeV}/c^2\) and binding energy \(2.2\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (\(\varrho = 0.23\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\)), the extracted potential radius is \(R = 1.44\ \mathrm{fm}\) (\(\varrho R = 0.33\))
* For the \(\overline{K}NNN\) system with reduced mass \(670\ \mathrm{MeV}/c^2\) and binding energy \(b = 20\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (\(\varrho = 0.83\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\)): if \(\sqrt{\langle p^2 \rangle} = 300\ \mathrm{MeV}/c\) (\(r_p = 1\ \mathrm{fm}\)), then \(R = 0.78\ \mathrm{fm}\) (\(\varrho R = 0.65\)); if \(\sqrt{\langle p^2 \rangle} = 150\ \mathrm{MeV}/c\) (\(r_p = 2\ \mathrm{fm}\)), then \(R = 2.5\ \mathrm{fm}\) (\(\varrho R = 2.0\))
* For a shallow-bound state with \(\varrho\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle} = 0.78 \pm 0.10\), the corresponding \(\varrho R\) range is approximately \(0 < \varrho R < 0.4\); for \(\varrho r_p = 0.43 \pm 0.10\) with the same error, the range is approximately \(0.1 < \varrho R < 0.3\)

## Context

This work extends the study of hadron structure classification, particularly for distinguishing exotic hadrons as hadronic composite states versus quark composite states. The authors demonstrate that the commonly assumed approximate equality between \(\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle}\) and \(r_p\) (which holds when the Heisenberg uncertainty product approaches \(3/2\)) breaks down in the shallow-binding regime, where the uncertainty product grows as \(\sim \mathcal{O}(1/\varrho R)\). The method is applied to the deuteron (using the CD-Bonn potential from Machleidt (2001)) and to the \(\overline{K}NNN\) system, with the latter potentially measurable at J-PARC through the decay process \(\overline{K}NNN \rightarrow \Lambda + N + N\) as discussed by Kienle (2005). This framework enables experimental determination of the potential radius from momentum distribution measurements, providing a diagnostic tool for hadron structure classification.
