# Quantum Chromodynamics of the Nucleon in the Framework of Complex Probabilistic Processes

> Soon after the postulation of quarks by Gell-Mann, Zweig and Fritzsch the experimental confirmation of these sub-nucleon formations, Feynman, Ravndal and Kislinger proposed a relativistic three-quark model of the nucleon to study its internal structure and state. Despite the obvious progress in describing the internal motion of a system with confinement of quarks in a nucleon, it should be stated that the model is not realistic enough. The fact is that the model ignores the processes of gluon exchange between quarks, as well as the influence of continuously formed pairs of quarks and antiquarks (quark sea) on valence quarks. To overcome this difficulty, the problem of self-organization of a three-quark dynamical system immersed in a colored quark-antiquark sea is considered within the framework of the representation of complex probabilistic processes satisfying the stochastic differential equation of Langevin-Kline-Gordon-Fock type. Taking into account the hidden symmetry of the internal motion of a dynamical system, a mathematically closed non-perturbative approach has been developed, which makes it possible to construct the mathematical expectation of the wave function and other parameters of the nucleon in the form of multiple integral representations. The developed approach can be especially useful for studying the state of nucleons in critical states, which occurs, for example, in massive and dense stellar formations such as neutron stars, etc.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a mathematically closed, non-perturbative approach to nucleon structure can be constructed by treating the three-quark system as immersed in a colored quark-antiquark sea, described by complex probabilistic processes satisfying a stochastic differential equation of Langevin-Klein-Gordon-Fock type, thereby overcoming the limitations of the Feynman-Ravndal-Kislinger relativistic three-quark model which ignores gluon exchange and quark sea effects.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic Klein-Gordon-Fock equation for a three-quark system with a four-dimensional harmonic oscillator potential ensuring confinement, then introduce a complex random function representing the influence of colored gluon fields and the quark-antiquark sea. They reformulate the problem using stochastic differential equations of Langevin type for the gluon field components, assuming Markov-Gaussian (white noise) processes for the random generators, and derive a Fokker-Planck equation for the distribution of gluon fields in the limit of statistical equilibrium. The mathematical expectation of the nucleon wave function is then constructed via functional integral representations, using the generalized Feynman-Kac theorem to obtain factorized double integral representations.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon mass is approximately 1 GeV, while the masses of up and down quarks are several MeV, highlighting the mass generation problem addressed by the model.
- The ground state wave function normalization constant is given as \((\Omega_0/(3\pi))^2\).
- The Betti number characterizing the topological features of the two-dimensional sub-manifolds is \(n \leq 4\).
- The metric tensor components for the additional sub-spaces are given as \(g^{11} = \bar{\varepsilon}^{(r)}\), \(g^{22} = \bar{\varepsilon}^{(i)}\), and \(g^{12} = -g^{21}\), with the determinant \(g = \bar{\varepsilon}^{(r)}\bar{\varepsilon}^{(i)} + (g^{12})^2\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic three-quark oscillator model of Feynman, Ravndal, and Kislinger (1971) and the covariant harmonic oscillator formalism developed by Lipes (1972) and Kim (1973), which did not account for gluon exchange or quark sea effects. The approach enables non-perturbative study of nucleon states in extreme environments such as neutron stars, where gluon field fluctuation spectra and powers change, and provides a framework for calculating nucleon radius and mass from constants characterizing colored gluon field fluctuations.
