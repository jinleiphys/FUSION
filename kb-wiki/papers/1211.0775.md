# Production of the H dibaryon via the (K^-,K^+) reaction on a $^{12}$C target

> We study the production of the stable six-quark $H$ dibaryon via the $(K^-,K^+)$ reaction on a $^{12}$C target within a covariant effective Lagrangian model. The calculations are performed within a factorization approximation, in which the full production amplitude is written as a product of the amplitudes for the $K^- + p \to K^+ + Ξ^-$ and $Ξ^- + p \to H$ processes. The $K^+Ξ^-$ production vertex is described by excitation, propagation and decay of $Λ$ and $Σ$ resonance states in the initial collision of a $K^-$ meson with a target proton in the incident channel. The parameters of the resonance vertices are taken to be the same as those determined previously by describing the available data on total and differential cross sections for the $p(K^-, K^+)Ξ^-$ reaction within a similar model. The $Ξ^- + p \to H$ fusion process is treated within a quark model where the $H$ dibaryon is considered as a stable particle. For the $K^+$ meson angle fixed at 0$^o$, the $H$ production cross-section is found to be about 2.9 $μb/sr$ for $H$ mass just below the $ΛΛ$ threshold at a $K^-$ beam momentum of 1.67 GeV/c. This is an order of magnitude larger than the value for this quantity reported earlier in calculations performed on a $^3$He target using a different model for the cascade hyperon production. We have also calculated the beam momentum dependence of the $H$ production cross section and the energy spectrum of the emitted $K^+$ meson.

## Key claim

The production cross-section for the H dibaryon via the (K⁻,K⁺) reaction on a ¹²C target is about 2.9 μb/sr at a K⁻ beam momentum of 1.67 GeV/c for an H mass just below the ΛΛ threshold, which is an order of magnitude larger than earlier calculations on a ³He target using a different model for cascade hyperon production, but the calculation assumes the H dibaryon is a stable particle and uses a factorization approximation that may not capture all higher-order processes.

## Method

The authors used a covariant effective Lagrangian model within a factorization approximation, where the full production amplitude is written as a product of the amplitudes for K⁻ + p → K⁺ + Ξ⁻ and Ξ⁻ + p → H processes. The K⁺Ξ⁻ production vertex was described by excitation, propagation and decay of Λ and Σ resonance states (including eight resonances with masses up to 2 GeV), with parameters determined previously from fits to data on the elementary p(K⁻,K⁺)Ξ⁻ reaction. The Ξ⁻ + p → H fusion process was treated within a quark model using Gaussian wave functions for the internal quark structure, with the H dibaryon considered as a stable particle, and the initial bound proton was taken from the 1p₃/₂ orbit in ¹²C with a binding energy of 15.96 MeV.

## Key numbers

- H production cross-section dσ/dΩ at θ_K⁺ = 0°: about 2.9 μb/sr for H mass just below the ΛΛ threshold at K⁻ beam momentum of 1.67 GeV/c
- Upper limit from previous experiment (Yoo et al., 2007) for H production in ¹²C(K⁻,K⁺)ΛΛX: 2.1 ± 0.6 (stat.) ± 0.1 (syst.) μb/sr at 90% confidence level at beam momentum 1.67 GeV/c
- Peak cross-section for ¹²C target occurs near p_K⁻_lab ≈ 1.05 GeV/c, approximately 0.30 GeV/c above the production threshold of 0.735 GeV/c
- For ³He target, peak cross-section occurs near p_K⁻_lab ≈ 1.20 GeV/c, with threshold ≈ 0.80 GeV/c
- Near p_K⁻_lab ≈ 1.75 GeV/c, cross-section for ³He target is about 0.95 μb/sr
- Ratio of cross-sections (¹²C to ³He) near peak positions is about 10, varying between 4 and 2 in the tail region (p_K⁻_lab > 1.6 GeV/c)
- Width of K⁺ momentum spectra in double differential cross-section: about 90 MeV
- Oscillator parameters used: R_p = 0.83 fm, R_Ξ⁻ = 0.73 fm, R_H = 0.95 fm
- Color-flavor-spin recoupling factor Γ₀ = √(1/20)

## Context

This work extends the effective Lagrangian approach previously applied to Ξ⁻ hypernuclear production via (K⁻,K⁺) reactions (Shyam et al., 2012, 2013) to the production of the H dibaryon, and differs from earlier calculations by Aerts and Dover (1982, 1983) and Aizawa et al. (1992) by using a more detailed treatment of the K⁺Ξ⁻ production vertex with bound state spinors for the initial proton rather than parametrizations of experimental data. The results are directly comparable to experimental upper limits from KEK (Yoo et al., 2007) and provide predictions for future measurements at JPARC (Ahn et al., 2012), potentially helping to resolve the long-standing question of whether the H dibaryon exists as a bound state near the ΛΛ threshold, as suggested by recent lattice QCD calculations (NPLQCD, 2011; HAL QCD, 2011) and chiral constituent quark model calculations (Carvajal et al., 2012).
