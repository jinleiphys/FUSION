# Shear transport far from equilibrium via holography

> In heavy-ion collisions, the quark-gluon plasma is produced far from equilibrium. This regime is currently inaccessible by quantum chromodynamics (QCD) computations. We calculate shear transport and entropy far from equilibrium in a holographic model, defining a time-dependent ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density, $η/s$. Large deviations of up to 60% from its near-equilibrium value, $1/4π$, are found for realistic situations at the Large Hadron Collider. We predict the far-from-equilibrium time-dependence of $η/s$ to substantially affect the evolution of the QCD plasma and to impact the extraction of QCD properties from flow coefficients in heavy-ion collision data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, η/s, receives significant corrections of up to 60% from its near-equilibrium value of 1/4π when evaluated far from equilibrium in a holographic model, with these corrections being of order one for all peak temperatures and heat-up times relevant to heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC.

## Method

The authors employ a holographic model based on a time-dependent charged Vaidya black brane spacetime in Einstein-Maxwell theory, dual to superconformal Yang-Mills theory at strong coupling. They introduce a shear perturbation to the metric and compute the time-dependent two-point function of the stress-energy tensor via linear response theory, then apply a Wigner transform to define a far-from-equilibrium shear viscosity η(t_avg) at any average time. The time-dependent entropy density s(t) is derived from the on-shell action via holographic renormalization, using the relation s = (∂P/∂T)_μ where P is the pressure.

## Key numbers

- Deviations of η/s from its near-equilibrium value of 1/4π reach up to 60% during the heat-up phase for realistic LHC conditions
- The minimum of η/s occurs around T = 1.3 T_C, where T_C = 155 MeV is the critical temperature of the QGP phase transition
- Heat-up times considered are 0.24 fm and 0.59 fm, corresponding to the duration for temperature to rise from 5% to 95% of peak temperature
- Peak temperatures studied range from 2 T_C to 10 T_C, covering 300-600 MeV for RHIC and 600-900 MeV for LHC
- For peak temperatures T ≳ 4 T_C, the heat-up time becomes the dominant out-of-equilibrium parameter dictating the smallest possible η/s

## Context

This work extends holographic calculations of η/s from equilibrium approaches (Policastro 2001, Kovtun 2004) into the far-from-equilibrium regime relevant to the early stage of heavy-ion collisions, which is inaccessible to perturbative QCD methods (Arnold 2000, 2003) and where lattice QCD and functional renormalization group results (Christiansen 2014, Astrakhantsev 2017) only provide near-equilibrium values. The results enable improved extraction of QCD properties from measured flow coefficients at RHIC and LHC by providing time-dependent η/s as input for hydrodynamic models, and open the possibility for theoretical insight into time-dependence far from equilibrium at the microscopic level.
