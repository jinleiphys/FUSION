# Short-Range Correlations and Urca Process in Neutron Stars

> Recent measurements of high-momentum correlated neutron-proton pairs at JLab suggest that the dense nucleonic component of the compact stars contains a fraction of high-momentum neutron-proton pairs that is not accounted for in the familiar Fermi-liquid theory of the neutron-proton fluid mixture. We compute the rate of the Urca process in compact stars taking into account the non-Fermi liquid contributions to the proton's spectral widths induced by short-range correlations. The Urca rate differs strongly from the Fermi-liquid prediction at low temperatures, in particular, the high threshold on the proton fraction precluding the Urca process in neutron stars is replaced by a smooth increase with the proton fraction. This observation may have a profound impact on the theories of cooling of compact stars.

# Short-Range Correlations and Urca Process in Neutron Stars

## Key claim

The sharp threshold on proton fraction (x_p >= 0.11) that precludes the direct Urca process in neutron stars under Fermi-liquid theory is replaced by a smooth increase of the Urca rate with proton fraction when non-Fermi-liquid contributions from short-range correlations are included, but this conclusion depends on modeling the proton spectral width via a T^2 scaling derived from scattering cross sections.

## Method

The author computes the antineutrino emissivity of the Urca process in neutron-proton-electron matter using the spectral representation of retarded Green's functions, where the proton spectral function includes a finite width gamma_p modeled from angle-averaged neutron-proton and proton-proton scattering cross sections (7 fm^2 and 4 fm^2 respectively). The neutron spectral function is kept in the quasiparticle approximation, and the emissivity integral is evaluated numerically for fixed density rho = 1.5 rho_0 with effective masses m_tau*/m_tau = 0.7, comparing results with and without the proton width.

## Key numbers

- The threshold proton fraction for direct Urca in Fermi-liquid theory is x_p >= 0.11, corresponding to the step function theta(|p_Fe + p_Fp - p_Fn|).
- At temperature T = 0.2 MeV (~2 x 10^9 K), the direct Urca process becomes comparable to the modified Urca process already at proton fraction x_p = 0.85 (not 0.11) in the finite-temperature quasiparticle limit.
- The proton width is modeled as gamma_p = a_p T^2 [1 + ((epsilon - mu_p)/(pi T))^2], with a_p derived from scattering frequencies using cross sections sigma_np = 7 fm^2 and sigma_pp = 4 fm^2.
- Calculations are performed at density rho = 1.5 rho_0, where rho_0 = 2.7 x 10^14 g cm^-3.
- The modified Urca emissivity ratio epsilon_mUrca/epsilon_0 is shown as a horizontal line at T = 1 MeV; at T = 0.2 MeV the ratio is epsilon_mUrca/epsilon_0 = -5 (in log scale).

## Context

This work extends the pioneering direct Urca calculation of Lattimer (1991) by incorporating non-Fermi-liquid effects from short-range correlations, motivated by JLab measurements of high-momentum neutron-proton pairs (2014). It contrasts with the Fermi-liquid-based modified Urca calculations of Friman (1979) and the more recent Brueckner G-matrix calculations of Shternin (2018). The finite-width approach builds on earlier treatments of neutrino bremsstrahlung by Raffelt (1996, 1999) and Sedrakian (1999, 2000). The results suggest that cooling simulations of neutron stars should replace the sharp transition between slow and fast cooling at a threshold mass with a smooth dependence on proton fraction, potentially affecting all equations of state for neutron star matter.
