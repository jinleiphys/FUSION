# Electric-dipole transitions in $^6$Li with a fully microscopic six-body calculation

> Exploring new excitation modes and the role of the nuclear clustering has been of great interest. An interesting speculation was made in the recent photoabsorption measurement of $^6$Li that implied the importance of the nuclear clustering. To understand the excitation mechanism of $^6$Li, we perform a fully microscopic six-body calculation on the electric-dipole ($E1$) transitions and discuss how $^6$Li is excited by the $E1$ field as a function of the excitation energy. We show the various cluster components in the six-body wave functions and discuss the role of the nuclear clustering in the $E1$ excitations of $^6$Li.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a unique "soft" Goldhaber-Teller dipole mode, characterized by out-of-phase oscillation of the valence proton and neutron around the alpha cluster, dominates the electric-dipole (E1) excitation of $^6$Li below the alpha breaking threshold, whereas the typical Goldhaber-Teller mode appears at higher excitation energies. This interpretation depends on the choice of the $u$ parameter in the Minnesota nucleon-nucleon potential, with three values (0.87, 0.93, and 1.00) examined to reproduce different ground-state properties.

## Method

The authors performed a fully microscopic six-body calculation using the correlated Gaussian method with the stochastic variational method to construct the ground-state wave function of $^6$Li. For the final excited states, they expanded the wave function in 18490 correlated Gaussian basis functions incorporating three configuration types: single-particle excitation, 4+1+1 cluster (alpha + proton + neutron), and 3+3 cluster (triton + helion) configurations. They diagonalized the Hamiltonian including all configurations and found approximately 2000 states below 100 MeV excitation energy, using the Minnesota nucleon-nucleon potential with three different values of the $u$ parameter.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy convergence achieved with 600 basis functions, refined until energy converged within tens of keV
- For $u=0.87$: largest B(E1) values in low-energy region: 0.122 $e^2$fm$^2$ at $E_x=13.6$ MeV; intermediate region: 0.202 $e^2$fm$^2$ at $E_x=19.1$ MeV; high-energy region: 0.285 $e^2$fm$^2$ at $E_x=30.6$ MeV
- For $u=0.93$: largest B(E1) values in low-energy region: 0.140 $e^2$fm$^2$ at $E_x=12.1$ MeV; intermediate region: 0.249 $e^2$fm$^2$ at $E_x=22.8$ MeV; high-energy region: 0.236 $e^2$fm$^2$ at $E_x=30.6$ MeV
- For $u=1.00$: largest B(E1) values in low-energy region: 0.124 $e^2$fm$^2$ at $E_x=12.9$ MeV; intermediate region: 0.179 $e^2$fm$^2$ at $E_x=23.8$ MeV; high-energy region: 0.240 $e^2$fm$^2$ at $E_x=31.2$ MeV
- The $h+t$ threshold opens at 15.8 MeV; the $\alpha+d$ and $\alpha+p+n$ thresholds are also indicated
- The ground state of $^6$Li has a large $\alpha+d$ spectroscopic factor of approximately 0.9 for all $u$ parameters
- States with $S_{ht}^2>0.50$ show three large E1 strengths after the $h+t$ threshold, corresponding to observed levels at $E_x=17.98$ and 26.59 MeV with decay widths 3.0 and 8.7 MeV respectively

## Context

This work extends the correlated Gaussian approach previously applied to $^6$He (Mikami, 2014) and addresses a speculation from photoabsorption measurements of $^6$Li (Yamagata, 2017) that implied the importance of nuclear clustering in E1 excitations. The calculation reveals a "soft" Goldhaber-Teller mode unique to $^6$Li, different from the soft dipole mode seen in neutron-rich nuclei such as $^6$He (Mikami, 2014). The authors suggest that measuring alpha inelastic scattering cross sections near the $\alpha+p+n$ threshold could provide complementary evidence for this mode, and propose exploring similar soft GT modes in heavier nuclei such as $^{18}$F (as $^{16}$O + p + n).
