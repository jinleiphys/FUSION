# Study of the Q.Q Interaction- Single Particle Behavior to Elliott's Rotations

> We perform shell model calculations using a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction (Q.Q).We show results in single j shell spaces and the full S-D shell . We show that one gets useful results with Q.Q in both spaces.. We emphasize the importance of the choice of single particle energies in order to obtain the results of Elliott using a Q.Q interaction without the momentum terms. We show a J(J+1) spectrum for a ground state band but with B(E2)'s different from the rotational model. We also show excited J(J-1), J((J-1) and J(J+3) spectra.We find spectra starting with J=0 which have both even J and odd J members.

# Study of the Q.Q Interaction: Single Particle Behavior to Elliott's Rotations

## Key claim
Shell model calculations using a pure quadrupole-quadrupole (Q.Q) interaction, without the momentum terms that Elliott originally included, can reproduce Elliott's SU(3) rotational J(J+1) spectra *provided* that specific single-particle energy splittings are artificially introduced. However, the resulting B(E2) values differ substantially from the rotational model, and the model also produces unexpected excited bands with J(J-1) and J(J+3) spectra that include both even and odd J members.

## Method
Shell model calculations were performed using a Q.Q interaction of the form -χ Q.Q = -χ √5 [(r² Y²)ᵢ (r² Y²)ⱼ]⁰, with χb⁴ set to 1 MeV (energies in units of χb⁴). Calculations were done both in single-j shell spaces (e.g., f₇/₂ for ⁵²Fe) and in the full S-D shell (sd-shell) for ²⁰Ne. To obtain Elliott's SU(3) results, single-particle energy splittings of 18χ' in the S-D shell and 30χ' in the P-F shell were introduced, with the higher-L orbital placed at higher energy. The NuShellX code was used, and a small 0.1 MeV splitting between d₃/₂ and d₅/₂ was added to break degeneracies and assign isospin correctly.

## Key numbers
- In ⁵²Fe single-j shell (f₇/₂): Q.Q calculation gives J=2 at 0.849 MeV (set to experiment), J=12 at 7.168 MeV vs. experimental 6.958 MeV, with J=12 lower than J=10 (7.389 MeV), reproducing the isomeric state.
- In ⁵³Co: predicted J=15/2, 17/2, 19/2 states at 4.448, 5.145, and 3.588 MeV respectively, with J=19/2 isomeric.
- In ⁹⁶Cd: predicted J=14, 15, 16 states at 4.138, 4.201, and 3.483 MeV, with J=16 isomeric.
- In ⁹⁶Ag: predicted J=10, 11, 12 states at 2.535, 3.089, and 2.482 MeV, with J=12 isomeric.
- Ground state band in ²⁰Ne (full S-D space): energies follow E(J) = 0.149 J(J+1); B(E2) from 0→2 is 427 e²fm⁴; quadrupole moment Q(2) = -18.96 e fm².
- Excited band 1: E(J) = 4.772 + 0.149 J(J+1); bandhead at 5.073 MeV.
- Excited band 2: E(J) = 4.772 + 0.149 J(J-1); bandhead at 5.073 MeV.
- Excited band 3: E(J) = 5.073 + 0.149 J(J+3); bandhead at 5.073 MeV, includes both even and odd J starting from J=0.
- B(E2) from 0₁→2₁ in ²⁰Ne: 427 e²fm⁴; B(E2) from 0₁→2ₙ to all other 2⁺ states is zero except for a weak transition to a T=1 state at 6.565 MeV (12.56 e²fm⁴).
- Quadrupole moment ratios Q(J)/Q(2) in ground band are identical between Elliott model and rotational model: 1.273 for J=4, 1.4 for J=6, 1.474 for J=8.
- B(E2) ratios B(E2)J→J+2 / B(E2)0→2 in Elliott model: 0.456 for J=4, 0.310 for J=6, 0.167 for J=8; rotational model gives 0.515, 0.455, 0.430 respectively.

## Context
This work systematically reexamines the Elliott SU(3) model (Elliott 1958) using a pure Q.Q interaction without momentum terms, extending earlier work by Zamick et al. and Moya de Guerra et al. on the required single-particle splittings. The single-j shell results connect to earlier studies of seniority non-conservation by Talmi and of spin-gap isomers by Auerbach and Talmi. The paper shows that while the Q.Q interaction produces rotational J(J+1) spectra, the B(E2) values drop off much faster with J than in the Bohr-Mottelson rotational model, confirming a statement by Elliott. The discovery of J(J-1) and J(J+3) excited bands with mixed even-odd J members provides new predictions for experimental searches and suggests that the simplest aspects of the Elliott model deserve further attention.
