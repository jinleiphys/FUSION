# Baryon-baryon interactions in free space and baryonic medium

> We study the interactions of the octet baryons in a relativistic meson exchange approach connecting free-space and in-medium interactions with those in finite (hyper)nuclei. A short sketch of the model including the relativistic T- and G-matrix and the mapping of the G-matrix onto density dependent vertex functionals in the density dependent relativistic hadron field theory (DDRH) is given. From new spectroscopic data on medium mass hypernuclei we extract the single particle spectrum including spin-orbit splitting. An extraction of the $Λω$ vector- and tensor vertex as well as the $Λσ$-vertex shows a strong breaking of SU(3) symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper extracts the Λω vector- and tensor vertex as well as the Λσ-vertex from hypernuclear spectroscopic data, finding a strong breaking of SU(3) symmetry; the favored region with χ² < 1 is centered around Rσ = Rω = 0.25 and f/g = 0, whereas SU(3) would predict values of 2/3, 2/3, and -1 respectively.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic meson exchange framework with density dependent relativistic hadron field theory (DDRH), mapping G-matrix interactions onto density dependent vertex functionals. They perform a least squares fit of DDRH relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculated Λ single particle energies to experimental data from KEK on ⁸⁹_ΛY and ⁵¹_ΛV, including a refined fit that accounts for jj interactions and core spin, to extract the Λ-meson coupling constants Rσ, Rω, and the tensor-to-vector ratio f/g.

## Key numbers

- Extracted single particle spectrum of ⁸⁹_ΛY: 1s₁/₂ at -20.94 ± 0.64 MeV, 1p₃/₂ at -17.02 ± 0.07 MeV, 1p₁/₂ at -16.68 ± 0.07 MeV, 1d₅/₂ at -10.26 ± 0.07 MeV, 1d₃/₂ at -9.71 ± 0.07 MeV, 1f₇/₂ at -3.04 ± 0.11 MeV, 1f₅/₂ at -2.26 ± 0.11 MeV.
- Spin-orbit matrix element E_ls = 223 ± 153 keV and jj matrix element E_jj = 61 ± 3 keV from the refined fit.
- Favored region with χ² < 1 centered at Rσ = Rω = 0.25 and f/g = 0, indicating strong SU(3) violation.

## Context

This work extends the DDRH theory previously applied to pure isospin nuclei (Fuchs 1995, Hofmann 2000) to hypernuclei involving octet baryons (Keil 1999), using new spectroscopic data from KEK (Hotchi 2001) on medium mass hypernuclei. The results contradict SU(3) symmetry expectations and earlier simpler fits that did not account for core spin and jj interactions; the extracted weak Λ couplings suggest the strange sector nearly decouples from the isospin sector, enabling future high-accuracy γ-spectroscopy experiments at KEK, JLab, GSI, and MAMI-C to measure nuclear bulk properties like mass radii and deformations through Λ hypernuclear spectroscopy.
