# The lowest-lying baryon masses in covariant SU(3)-flavor chiral perturbation theory

> We present an analysis of the baryon-octet and -decuplet masses using covariant SU(3)-flavor chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-leading order. Besides the description of the physical masses we address the problem of the lattice QCD extrapolation. Using the PACS-CS collaboration data we show that a good description of the lattice points can be achieved at next-to-leading order with the covariant loop amplitudes and phenomenologically determined values for the meson-baryon couplings. Moreover, the extrapolation to the physical point up to this order is found to be better than the linear one given at leading-order by the Gell-Mann-Okubo approach. The importance that a reliable combination of lattice QCD and chiral perturbation theory may have for hadron phenomenology is emphasized with the prediction of the pion-baryon and strange-baryon sigma terms.

## Key claim

The covariant formulation of SU(3)-flavor baryon chiral perturbation theory (BχPT) up to next-to-leading order can simultaneously describe both the physical baryon masses and the quark-mass dependence of 2+1-flavor lattice QCD results from the PACS-CS collaboration, provided the meson-baryon couplings are fixed to their phenomenologically determined values and the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme is used to restore power counting.

## Method

The authors calculated the baryon-octet and -decuplet self-energies up to 𝒪(p³) in covariant SU(3)-flavor BχPT, including leading loop diagrams with pseudoscalar mesons, octet baryons, and decuplet baryons. They used the EOMS renormalization scheme to restore power counting and fixed the meson-baryon couplings (D=0.80, F=0.46, 𝒞=1.0, ℋ=1.13) to phenomenologically determined values. The free low-energy constants (LECs) b₀, b_D, b_F, M_B0 for the octet and t₀, t_D, M_D0 for the decuplet were fitted to 24 lattice points from the PACS-CS collaboration (three lightest points per baryon with pion and kaon masses below 600 MeV) and to the physical baryon masses, with finite-volume corrections included.

## Key numbers

- Physical octet masses from covariant NLO fit: M_N = 941(2) MeV, M_Λ = 1116(1) MeV, M_Σ = 1190(4) MeV, M_Ξ = 1322(3) MeV (experimental values: 940(2), 1116(1), 1193(5), 1318(4) MeV respectively)
- Physical decuplet masses from covariant NLO fit: M_Δ = 1234(2) MeV, M_Σ* = 1376(1) MeV, M_Ξ* = 1523(1) MeV, M_Ω- = 1673(1) MeV (experimental: 1232(2), 1385(4), 1533(4), 1672(1) MeV)
- Extrapolated masses from covariant NLO fit to PACS-CS data: M_N = 893(19)(39) MeV, M_Λ = 1088(20)(14) MeV, M_Σ = 1178(24)(7) MeV, M_Ξ = 1322(24)(20) MeV; M_Δ = 1222(24)(49) MeV, M_Σ* = 1376(24)(29) MeV, M_Ξ* = 1531(25)(8) MeV, M_Ω- = 1686(28)(13) MeV
- χ²/d.o.f. for covariant fit to PACS-CS data: 2.1; combined fit including experimental data: 2.8 (compared to HB values of 9.2 and 36.6 respectively)
- Predicted sigma terms from covariant fit: σ_π(N) = 59(2)(17) MeV, σ_s(N) = -4(23)(25) MeV; σ_π(Λ) = 39(1)(10) MeV, σ_s(Λ) = 126(26)(35) MeV
- Largest finite-volume corrections at lightest lattice point: M_N (-7 MeV), M_Δ (-25 MeV), where m_πL ≈ 2

## Context

This work extends the covariant SU(3)-BχPT approach previously applied to hyperon magnetic moments (Geng 2008, 2009) and semileptonic decays (Geng 2009, Camalich 2009) to the analysis of lattice QCD baryon masses. It directly addresses the difficulties encountered by heavy-baryon (HB) χPT in describing the quark-mass dependence of 2+1-flavor lattice results from PACS-CS (Aoki 2008) and other collaborations (Walker-Loud 2008, Ishikawa 2009), where reasonable fits required unreasonably small meson-baryon couplings. The covariant approach achieves a good description using phenomenological couplings and provides predictions for pion-baryon and strange-baryon sigma terms, which are relevant for dark matter searches. The results enable future analyses of lattice data from other collaborations and studies of discretization uncertainties.
