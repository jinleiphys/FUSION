# Tidal deformability in neutron stars from a microscopic point of view

> We present results for the tidal deformability in neutron stars, the tidal Love number $k_2$, and the effective deformability of a binary system. The microscopic equation of state for cold $β$-stable neutron matter is based upon high-precision two-neutron forces and includes the chiral three-neutron forces required at the chosen order. We review and motivate our choices for the high-density continuation of the microscopic equation of state. We discuss our predictions and observe that they are well within multimessenger constraints. In contrast, stiff equations of state that yield radii larger than about 13 km are ruled out by GW170817 constraints.

# Tidal deformability in neutron stars from a microscopic point of view

## Key claim
The authors predict tidal deformabilities and Love numbers for neutron stars using a microscopic equation of state based on chiral effective field theory, and find their results are consistent with multimessenger constraints from GW170817, but only when the high-density continuation of the equation of state is carefully constructed to respect causality and support maximum masses above 2.01 solar masses. The abstract's claim that "stiff equations of state that yield radii larger than about 13 km are ruled out" applies specifically to the GW170817 constraints, not to all possible equations of state.

## Method
The authors construct a microscopic equation of state for cold beta-stable neutron matter using high-precision two-neutron forces and chiral three-neutron forces at N2LO and N3LO orders. They extend the microscopic equation of state to high densities using piecewise polytropes and parametrizations of the speed of sound that respect causality, with matching densities at 0.277 fm^-3 and 0.506 fm^-3. The tidal deformability and Love number k2 are computed by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations together with the linearized Einstein equations for the quadrupolar tidal perturbation.

## Key numbers
- Maximum masses achieved: 2.07 solar masses for the causal speed-of-sound parametrization, ranging up to 2.50 solar masses for N3LO with polytropic index gamma=3.8
- Canonical neutron star (1.4 solar masses) radius predictions: 11.84 km (N2LO, gamma=3.3) to 12.30 km (N3LO, gamma=3.8)
- Dimensionless tidal deformability at 1.4 solar masses: Lambda_1.4 = 355.25 +/- 85.64 at N2LO, combining chiral truncation error and polytropic index variation
- Tidal Love number k2 for 1.4 solar masses ranges from 0.0782 (N2LO, gamma=3.3) to 0.091063 (N3LO, gamma=3.8 with causal speed of sound)
- Effective tidal deformability for GW170817-like binaries ranges from approximately 380 to 401 across mass ratio q from 0.73 to 1.0
- Chirp mass constraint from GW170817: M_c/M_sun = 1.188^{+0.004}_{-0.002} at 90% confidence

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous microscopic equation of state studies (Sammarruca and Thapa, 2025) by computing tidal deformability properties for the first time with their chiral EFT-based approach. The results are compared against Bayesian analyses from Landry and Hinderer (2019) and multimessenger constraints from Huang et al. (2025). The paper demonstrates that the tidal deformability is primarily sensitive to the equation of state at medium to moderately high densities, where the microscopic theory has influence, while being essentially insensitive to the super-high density continuation. This enables future gravitational wave observations to constrain the nuclear equation of state in the density regime accessible to chiral effective field theory.
