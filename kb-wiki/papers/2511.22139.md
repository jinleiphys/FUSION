# Does the suppression shown at LHC in O-O collisions follow the systematics obtained for A-A collisions ?

> In this paper we present to what extent recent experimental results obtained for $π^{0}$ suppression in O-O collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=5.36 TeV fit into the systematics for much heavier systems. The systematics with which the comparison is made was published a few years ago \cite{Pet_1} in terms of charged particles suppression $R_{AA}$ as a function of $\langle N_{part} \rangle$ and $\langle dN_{ch}/dη\rangle$. The values of $R_{AA}$ at $\langle dN_{ch}/dη\rangle$ and $\langle N_{part} \rangle$ experimentally measured and estimated by Glauber MC, respectively, for O-O collisions are in good agreement with the systematics obtained for A-A collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the measured suppression of π⁰ in O-O collisions at √sNN=5.36 TeV is in good agreement with the systematics established for much heavier A-A collisions (Cu-Cu, Au-Au, Xe-Xe, Pb-Pb), but this conclusion is conditional on the specific comparison using the average RAA as a function of either ⟨Npart⟩ or ⟨dNch/dη⟩, and the authors note that the alpha-like cluster structure of oxygen nuclei could in principle produce peculiar behavior that is not seen in the average.

## Method

The authors compare preliminary ALICE results for π⁰ RAA in O-O collisions at √sNN=5.36 TeV against previously published systematics from Petrovici et al. (Pet_1, Pet_2) for A-A collisions. They use Glauber Monte Carlo estimates for ⟨Npart⟩ and ⟨Nbin⟩ in O-O collisions, and the experimentally measured ⟨dNch/dη⟩ from ALICE, then overlay the O-O data points on existing scaling plots of RAA versus ⟨Npart⟩ and versus ⟨dNch/dη⟩ for Cu-Cu, Au-Au, Xe-Xe, and Pb-Pb systems at various RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- O-O collisions at √sNN=5.36 TeV: ⟨Npart⟩ = 10.8 for 0-100% centrality, estimated via Glauber Monte Carlo
- The RAA value for O-O (preliminary ALICE result) is plotted at ⟨Npart⟩ = 10.8 and shows very good agreement with the Npart dependence of RAA obtained in Xe-Xe and Pb-Pb at similar collision energies
- The RAA for O-O is in good agreement with results from much heavier systems at RHIC and LHC at the same ⟨dNch/dη⟩ value

## Context

This work extends the systematics of nuclear modification factor RAA scaling established by Petrovici et al. (Pet_1, Pet_2) for heavy-ion collisions (Cu-Cu, Au-Au at RHIC; Xe-Xe, Pb-Pb at LHC) to the much lighter O-O system. The results support the idea that the average suppression in deconfined matter follows a universal scaling with ⟨Npart⟩ and ⟨dNch/dη⟩ across system sizes, and enable future tests of whether alpha-clustering effects in oxygen nuclei produce deviations from these systematics in more differential measurements.
