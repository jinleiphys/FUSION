# Quantum Late-Time Decay and Channel Dependence

> Quantum mechanics predicts deviations from exponential decay at short and long times, yet experimental evidence is limited. We report a power-law tail after $\sim$10 lifetimes in erythrosine~B fluorescence, confirmed by two detectors probing distinct bands but yielding different power coefficients. The data match a divergent but normalizable spectral density, and theory predicts oscillations as a future test. A novel and general result is that in multichannel QM (and QFT) decay the lifetime is universal, but the late-time deviations exhibit a sizeable time window in which they are channel (or band) dependent, a feature consistent with our data.

# Quantum Late-Time Decay and Channel Dependence

## Key claim
The paper reports a power-law tail after approximately 10 lifetimes in erythrosine B fluorescence, confirmed by two detectors probing distinct spectral bands but yielding different power coefficients (β ≈ 1.5 and β ≈ 2.0). The authors claim this is consistent with a general quantum-mechanical result: in multichannel decay, the exponential lifetime is universal across channels, but the late-time power-law exponent is channel-dependent over a sizeable time window.

## Method
Fluorescence decay of erythrosine B dissolved in methanol (10⁻⁵ mol/dm³) was measured using time-correlated single-photon counting on a confocal microscope with picosecond laser excitation at 438 nm. Two photomultiplier detectors recorded decay simultaneously in distinct spectral windows separated by a dichroic mirror and bandpass filters (520/35 nm and 600/50 nm). Seven decay curves were collected across three measurement campaigns (October 2024, November 2024, February 2025) and combined. The data were fitted with both a two-exponential model and a non-exponential model consisting of an exponential plus a power-law term I(t) = C exp(-(t-t₀)/τ) + C_p (t-t₀)^(-β) + b.

## Key numbers
- Power-law exponent β = 1.5469 for channel 1 (520/35 nm band), with reduced chi-squared χ²_ν = 1.049
- Power-law exponent β = 1.9985 for channel 2 (600/50 nm band), with reduced chi-squared χ²_ν = 1.080
- Exponential lifetime τ = 0.44802 ns for channel 1 and τ = 0.4475 ns for channel 2 (essentially identical)
- Fitting range: 0.960 ns to 94.752 ns (channel 1) and 0.960 ns to 94.656 ns (channel 2)
- Power-law onset occurs after approximately 10 lifetimes (approximately 4.5 ns)
- The spectral function model uses exponent γ related to β by β = 2γ + 3, giving γ ≈ -0.73 for channel 1 and γ ≈ -0.50 for channel 2 (both in the range -1 < γ < 0, where the spectral density diverges at threshold but remains normalizable)

## Context
This work extends the limited experimental evidence for late-time non-exponential decay, previously reported by Rothe et al. (2006) for rhodamine and polyfluorene fluorescence with power exponents β between 2 and 4 and turnover times between 8 and 20 lifetimes. The authors also cite theoretical work by Khalfin on power-law tails, by Ghirardi on energy-dependent widths, and by Giacosa (2011, 2021) on multichannel decay in quantum mechanics and quantum field theory. The novel theoretical result, that late-time power-law exponents are channel-dependent, is derived from these prior formalisms and is consistent with the measured difference between the two detector bands. The paper predicts oscillations in the survival probability as a future test, though these would be washed out by ensemble averaging and finite time-binning in the current setup.
