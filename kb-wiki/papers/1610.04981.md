# Dynamics of the tri-nuclear system at spontaneous fission of $^{252}$Cf

> To describe of dynamics of ternary fission of $^{252}$Cf an equation of motion of the tri-nuclear system is calculated. The fission of the $^{70}$Ni+$^{50}$Ca+$^{132}$Sn channel was chosen as one of the more probable channels of true ternary fission of $^{252}$Cf. The collinearity of ternary fission has been checked by analyzing results of the equation of motion. The results show that if initially all nuclei are placed collinearly (potential energy of this position is the smallest) and the component of the middle fragment's initial velocity which is perpendicular to this line, is zero then ternary fission is collinear, otherwise the non collinear ternary fission takes place.

## Key claim

Ternary fission of ²⁵²Cf is collinear only if all three fragments are initially placed on a straight line (the configuration with the smallest potential energy) and the component of the middle fragment's initial velocity perpendicular to that line is exactly zero; otherwise, non-collinear ternary fission occurs.

## Method

The authors solved classical Lagrange equations of motion for a tri-nuclear system (TNS) in two-dimensional Cartesian coordinates, using the double-folding procedure with a density-dependent effective nucleon-nucleon interaction to compute the total interaction potential (Coulomb plus nuclear). They studied the specific channel ⁷⁰Ni + ⁵⁰Ca + ¹³²Sn, varying the initial positions and velocities of the three fragments to determine which conditions produce collinear versus non-collinear trajectories.

## Key numbers

- The local minimum of the total interaction potential for the middle fragment (⁵⁰Ca) occurs at coordinates \(R_{3x} = -2.9\) fm and \(R_{3y} = 0\) fm when the relative distance between Ni and Sn is \(R_{12} = 20\) fm.
- For \(R_{12} \geq 22\) fm, this minimum transforms into a saddle point, indicating the TNS becomes unstable.
- In the collinear case, the decay time of the tri-nuclear system is approximately \(t \approx 13.5 \times 10^{-22}\) s.
- In the non-collinear case with an initial y-displacement of 0.5 fm for the Ca nucleus, the decay time is \(t \approx 13.2 \times 10^{-22}\) s.
- In the non-collinear case with an initial y-velocity of 0.1 cm/ns for the Ca nucleus, the decay time is \(t \approx 13.4 \times 10^{-22}\) s.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Tashkhodjaev 2015, Nasirov 2014) on possible channels of true ternary fission of ²⁵²Cf, where the channel ⁷⁰Ni+⁵⁰Ca+¹³²Sn was identified as one of the most probable. It provides a dynamical justification for the collinear cluster tripartition (CCT) mode observed experimentally by the FOBOS group (Pyatkov 2010, 2012), confirming that collinear fission is possible only under specific initial conditions. The open question for future investigation is the probability (weight) of populating each initial condition in nature.
