# Possible studies on generalized parton distributions and gravitational form factors in neutrino reactions

> Spacelike and timelike generalized parton distributions (GPDs) have been investigated in charged-lepton scattering and electron-positron collisions via deeply virtual Compton scattering and two-photon processes, respectively. Furthermore, we expect that hadron-accelerator-facility measurements will be performed in future. The GPDs will play a crucial role in clarifying the origins of hadron spins and masses in terms of quarks and gluons. It is also possible to probe internal pressure within hadrons for understanding their stability. Gravitational form factors of hadrons used to be considered as a purely academic subject because gravitational interactions are too weak to be measured in microscopic systems. However, due to the development of hadron-tomography field, it became possible to extract the gravitational form factors from the actual GPD measurements without relying on direct gravitational interactions. Neutrino reactions can also be used for GPD studies in future, for example, by using the Long-Baseline Neutrino Facility at Fermilab. The neutrino GPD measurements are valuable especially for finding the flavor dependence of the GPDs in a complementary way to the charged-lepton experiments. We give an overview of the GPDs and discuss possible neutrino GPD measurements using the single-pion production processes $ν+ N \to \ell^- + N' + π$ and $\barν+ N \to \ell^+ + N' + π$.

## Key claim

Neutrino reactions can be used to measure spacelike generalized parton distributions (GPDs) via single-pion production processes, providing complementary flavor-dependent information to charged-lepton experiments, but only if future facilities such as the Long-Baseline Neutrino Facility (LBNF) at Fermilab deliver sufficiently intense beams in the 2–15 GeV energy range.

## Method

The authors review the formalism for extracting GPDs from the exclusive pion-production processes ν + N → ℓ⁻ + N′ + π and ν̄ + N → ℓ⁺ + N′ + π, where the amplitude factorizes into hard and soft parts described by quark and gluon GPDs. They present the cross-section formula for these reactions in terms of the GPDs H, E, H̃, Ẽ, including both quark and gluon contributions, and show numerical calculations from Pire, Szymanowski, and Wagner (2017) at s = 20 GeV². The paper then discusses how the DUNE near detector complex at LBNF could measure these channels on both free protons and nuclear targets.

## Key numbers

- The LBNF neutrino beam energy range for GPD measurements is 2–15 GeV (high energy option).
- The cross-section calculations are shown at s = 20 GeV² for the processes νp → ℓ⁻ π⁺ p and νn → ℓ⁻ π⁰ p.
- More than 54% of events with the default low-energy LBNF beam have W > 1.4 GeV, providing sensitivity to GPD measurements.
- The longitudinal cross-section σ_L is given by an expression involving coupling constants C_q = 2π C_F α_s V_dc/3 with C_F = 4/3, and C_g = π T_f α_s V_du/3 with T_f = 1/2.

## Context

This paper extends the established program of GPD measurements from charged-lepton facilities (HERMES, COMPASS, JLab) and future electron-ion colliders (EIC, EicC) to the neutrino sector, analogous to how neutrino deep inelastic scattering historically determined flavor-dependent parton distribution functions and the valence-quark distributions via F₃. The work builds on the GPD formalism developed by Diehl (2003) and the timelike GPD (GDA) studies by Kumano (2017), and specifically references the numerical calculations of Pire, Szymanowski, and Wagner (2017) for neutrino-induced pion production. The proposed measurements would enable extraction of gravitational form factors (A_q, B_q, C_q, C̄_q) from the second moments of GPDs, providing access to hadron mass decomposition and internal pressure distributions without direct gravitational measurements.
