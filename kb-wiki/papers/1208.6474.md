# Decay of the pseudoscalar glueball into scalar and pseudoscalar mesons

> We study a chiral Lagrangian which describes the two- and three-body decays of a pseudoscalar glueball into scalar and pseudoscalar mesons. The various branching ratios are a parameter-free prediction of our approach. We compute the decay channels for a pseudoscalar glueball with a mass of 2.6 GeV, as predicted by Lattice QCD in the quenched approximation, which is in the reach of the PANDA experiment at the upcoming FAIR facility. For completeness, we also repeat the calculation for a glueball mass of 2.37 GeV which corresponds to the mass of the resonance X(2370) measured in the BESIII experiment.

## Key claim

The branching ratios for two- and three-body decays of a pseudoscalar glueball into scalar and pseudoscalar mesons are parameter-free predictions of the chiral Lagrangian approach, but the absolute decay widths depend on an unknown coupling constant \(c_{\tilde{G}\Phi}\) that must be fixed by experimental data or large-\(N_c\) considerations.

## Method

The authors constructed a chirally invariant effective Lagrangian coupling the pseudoscalar glueball field \(\tilde{G}\) to the multiplet \(\Phi\) of scalar and pseudoscalar quark-antiquark states, using the interaction term \(\mathcal{L}_{\tilde{G}}^{int}=ic_{\tilde{G}\Phi}\tilde{G}(\det\Phi-\det\Phi^\dagger)\). After shifting scalar fields by their vacuum expectation values and applying wave-function renormalization constants for pseudoscalar fields, they computed tree-level decay widths for \(\tilde{G}\rightarrow PPP\) (three pseudoscalar mesons) and \(\tilde{G}\rightarrow PS\) (one scalar and one pseudoscalar meson) for two assumed glueball masses.

## Key numbers

- For \(M_{\tilde{G}}=2.6\) GeV: branching ratio \(\Gamma_{\tilde{G}\rightarrow KK\pi}/\Gamma_{\tilde{G}}^{tot}=0.47\); for \(M_{\tilde{G}}=2.37\) GeV: same channel gives 0.47.
- For \(M_{\tilde{G}}=2.6\) GeV: \(\Gamma_{\tilde{G}\rightarrow\eta\pi\pi}/\Gamma_{\tilde{G}}^{tot}=0.16\); for \(M_{\tilde{G}}=2.37\) GeV: 0.17.
- For \(M_{\tilde{G}}=2.6\) GeV: \(\Gamma_{\tilde{G}\rightarrow\eta^{\prime}\pi\pi}/\Gamma_{\tilde{G}}^{tot}=0.095\); for \(M_{\tilde{G}}=2.37\) GeV: 0.090.
- For \(M_{\tilde{G}}=2.6\) GeV: \(\Gamma_{\tilde{G}\rightarrow a_{0}\pi}/\Gamma_{\tilde{G}}^{tot}=0.083\); for \(M_{\tilde{G}}=2.37\) GeV: 0.10.
- The decay \(\tilde{G}\rightarrow\pi\pi\pi\) vanishes identically: \(\Gamma_{\tilde{G}\rightarrow\pi\pi\pi}=0\).
- For the resonance \(X(2370)\) with mass 2.37 GeV and total width \(\Gamma_{X(2370)}=83\pm17\) MeV, the coupling constant is determined to be \(c_{\tilde{G}\Phi}=4.48\pm0.46\).
- The vacuum expectation values used: \(\phi_N=0.158\) GeV, \(\phi_S=0.138\) GeV, derived from \(f_\pi=0.0922\) GeV and \(f_K=0.110\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the effective chiral Lagrangian approach of Schechter (1980) to describe pseudoscalar glueball decays, building on lattice QCD predictions for the glueball spectrum from Morningstar and Peardon (1999). The results provide testable predictions for the PANDA experiment at FAIR (searching for a 2.6 GeV pseudoscalar glueball) and for the BESIII candidate \(X(2370)\), enabling experimental discrimination between glueball and conventional quark-antiquark interpretations. The study also connects to mixing analyses of scalar resonances \(f_0(1370)\), \(f_0(1500)\), and \(f_0(1710)\) from Giacosa et al. (2005) and Cheng et al. (2009).
