# Non-Abelian color fields from relativistic color charge configurations in the classical limit

> We study the dynamics of color fields as generated by simple configurations of relativistic particles with Abelian and non-Abelian (SU(2)) charges in the classical limit. We find that chromodynamic (non-Abelian) systems generally show Coulomb-like features by analogy with electrodynamics. A peculiar feature in the non-Abelian case is the additional strength of the chromoelectric and chromomagnetic fields caused by the contribution of changing the color charge. This change of color SU(2) charges results in a rotation of the color vector which is getting very fast at close partonic distances. The presence of this non-Abelian additional term in the chromoelectric and chromomagnetic fields creates a 'color charge glow', which is manifested as a distinct color wave disturbance arising due to the finite distance at which the color interaction becomes active. This situation may be relevant to the hadronization phase in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, where the partonic state is governed by strong local color fluctuations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that classical non-Abelian (SU(2)) color fields generated by relativistic pointlike color charges exhibit Coulomb-like features analogous to electrodynamics, but with an additional contribution to the chromoelectric and chromomagnetic fields arising from the time-dependent rotation of the color charge vector; this "color charge glow" manifests as a distinct color wave disturbance that becomes significant at close partonic distances and may be relevant to the hadronization phase in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors solve the classical Yang-Mills equations for SU(2) color fields using an approximate superposition of Lienard-Wiechert potentials for pointlike color charges, where the color charge vector is allowed to change in time according to the compatibility condition (continuity equation). They analyze specific configurations of two color charges, a color charge with a color dipole, and two color dipoles moving along straight lines toward each other, comparing the non-Abelian results to the corresponding Abelian (electrodynamic) solutions for the same coupling strength.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant is taken as $\alpha_g = g^2/(4\pi) = 0.3$
- The total energy concentrated in the color field for two charges is estimated as $E \simeq \alpha_g (\widetilde P \widetilde Q)/|\mathbf{x}_1 - \mathbf{x}_2| + \alpha_g^3 I (\widetilde P \times \widetilde Q)^2/|\mathbf{x}_1 - \mathbf{x}_2|$, where $I = (6 - \pi^2/2)/4$
- The vector field $\mathbf{a}$ develops a constant longitudinal component $|\mathbf{a}_\| = 1/(4\pi |\mathbf{x}_1 - \mathbf{x}_2|)$ along the line connecting two color sources
- The chromomagnetic field component $H_\varphi$ for two color charges at points 0 and 1 on the $z$-axis is given by $H_\varphi = g^3/(4\pi)^2 \cdot (1/r) \cdot ((1-\mu)/(1+\mu))^{1/2} \cdot (1 - (1+r)/(1-2r\mu + r^2)^{1/2})$
- The coupling constant threshold for vector field restructuring is $\Pi = g^2/(4\pi)^2 \widetilde P^2 \leq l(l+1)$, where $l = 0,1,2,\dots$ are integers
- The exponent $\sigma$ for solutions near the origin is $\sigma = -1/2 \pm ((2l+1)^2 - 4\pi)^{1/2}/2$

## Context

This work extends classical field treatments of color charges in the McLerran-Venugopalan model (1994) and the color glass condensate framework reviewed by Gelis, Iancu, Jalilian-Marian, and Venugopalan (2010), providing practical estimates for space-time dependent non-Abelian fields that can be incorporated into transport approaches like the parton-hadron-string dynamics (PHSD) theory. The results enable the development of extended transport codes for ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions that include color dynamics of gluon fields beyond the processes currently implemented in PHSD, potentially improving the description of the early non-equilibrium stage and hadronization phase where color forces play an important role.
