# Quantum relativistic fluid at global thermodynamic equilibrium in curved spacetime

> We present a new approach to the problem of the thermodynamical equilibrium of a quantum relativistic fluid in a curved spacetime in the limit of small curvature. We calculate the mean value of local operators by expanding the four-temperature Killing vector field in Riemann normal coordinates about the same spacetime point and we derive corrections with respect to the flat spacetime expressions. Thereby, we clarify the origin of the terms proportional to Riemann and Ricci tensors introduced in general hydrodynamic expansion of the stress-energy tensor.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method to calculate the mean value of local operators in a quantum relativistic fluid at global thermodynamic equilibrium in curved spacetime, showing that the leading corrections to the flat-spacetime stress-energy tensor are linear in the Riemann and Ricci tensors, with coefficients that can be expressed as Kubo formulas; the authors clarify that these curvature-induced corrections are of quantum origin for free fields (vanishing in the limit ℏ → 0) and that the frame is defined by the four-temperature Killing vector rather than the traditional Landau or Eckart frames.

## Method

The authors expand the four-temperature Killing vector field β in Riemann normal coordinates about a spacetime point x, then expand the density operator using the Kubo identity to obtain corrections to the mean value of local operators. The integration over the hypersurface Σ is approximated by an integration over its tangent timelike hyperplane, allowing the use of flat-spacetime quantum field theory operators (four-momentum P and angular momentum J) plus a new operator Θ that captures curvature effects. The mean value of the stress-energy tensor is then decomposed into irreducible components with respect to the rotation group normal to β, and the correlators are expressed in terms of scalar coefficients χ that depend only on proper temperature and chemical potential.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Becattini (2015) on quantum relativistic fluids in flat spacetime to curved backgrounds, providing a systematic derivation of curvature corrections to the stress-energy tensor that were previously introduced phenomenologically in hydrodynamic expansions by Baier (2008) and others (Roma, Batta, Tifr, Moore, Starinets, Kaminski). The method clarifies the origin of terms proportional to Riemann and Ricci tensors and provides Kubo formulas for their coefficients; it enables future calculations of these coefficients using standard thermal field theory techniques and suggests applications in relativistic astrophysics and extended theories of gravity.
