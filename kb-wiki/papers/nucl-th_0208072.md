# Strong Decays of Strange Quarkonia

> In this paper we evaluate strong decay amplitudes and partial widths of strange mesons (strangeonia and kaonia) in the 3P0 decay model. We give numerical results for all energetically allowed open-flavor two-body decay modes of all nsbar and ssbar strange mesons in the 1S, 2S, 3S, 1P, 2P, 1D and 1F multiplets, comprising strong decays of a total of 43 resonances into 525 two-body modes, with 891 numerically evaluated amplitudes. This set of resonances includes all strange qqbar states with allowed strong decays expected in the quark model up to ca. 2.2 GeV. We use standard nonrelativistic quark model SHO wavefunctions to evaluate these amplitudes, and quote numerical results for all amplitudes present in each decay mode. We also discuss the status of the associated experimental candidates, and note which states and decay modes would be especially interesting for future experimental study at hadronic, e+e- and photoproduction facilities. These results should also be useful in distinguishing conventional quark model mesons from exotica such as glueballs and hybrids through their strong decays.

## Key claim

The paper provides the most comprehensive survey to date of strong decay amplitudes and partial widths for strange quarkonia (both $s\bar s$ and $n\bar s$ states) using the $^3$P$_0$ decay model, but the authors caution that the model is not derived from QCD and its accuracy in novel decay channels is unknown, so predictions should serve primarily as a guide for future experimental searches rather than definitive calculations.

## Method

The authors employed the $^3$P$_0$ decay model with simple harmonic oscillator (SHO) quark model wavefunctions and physical relativistic phase space to numerically evaluate all energetically allowed open-flavor two-body strong decay amplitudes and partial widths for strange mesons in the 1S, 2S, 3S, 1P, 2P, 1D and 1F multiplets. They assumed a fixed $^3$P$_0$ source strength with pair production amplitude $\gamma=0.4$ and SHO wavefunction scale parameter $\beta=0.4$ GeV, using physical charge-averaged PDG masses for well-established candidates and estimated masses otherwise. The calculations covered 43 resonances decaying into 525 two-body modes with 891 numerically evaluated amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Total of 43 resonances, 525 two-body decay modes, and 891 numerically evaluated amplitudes for all strange $q\bar q$ states with allowed strong decays expected up to approximately 2.2 GeV
- For $\phi(1019) \to KK$: predicted width $\Gamma_{thy} = 2.47$ MeV compared to experimental $\Gamma_{expt} = 4.26 \pm 0.05$ MeV, giving $\Gamma_{thy} / \Gamma_{expt} = 0.58$
- For $K^* \to \pi K$: $\Gamma_{thy} / \Gamma_{expt} = 0.42$; for $\rho \to \pi\pi$: $\Gamma_{thy} / \Gamma_{expt} = 0.32$
- For $\phi(1680)$: predicted $KK/KK^*$ branching fraction ratio approximately 0.35, compared to experimental ratio $0.07 \pm 0.01$ from DM1; predicted $B_{\phi(1680) \to \eta \phi /KK^* } \approx 0.18$
- For $f'_2(1525)$: predicted total width 80 MeV (experimental $76 \pm 10$ MeV), with predicted partial widths $KK = 61$ MeV, $KK^* = 8.6$ MeV, $\eta\eta = 10.4$ MeV, $\pi\pi = 0$ MeV
- For $2^1$S$_0$ $s\bar s$ state: predicted total width varies from 11 MeV at mass 1415 MeV to 100 MeV at mass 1500 MeV
- For $3^3$S$_1$ $s\bar s$ state at estimated mass 2.05 GeV: predicted total width approximately 380 MeV
- For $3^1$S$_0$ $s\bar s$ state: predicted total width approximately 175 MeV
- Experimental upper limit on $\pi_2(1670) \to b_1\pi$: $B < 1.9 \times 10^{-3}$ at 97.7% confidence level
- FOCUS measurement of $X(1750)$: mass $1753.5 \pm 1.5 \pm 2.3$ MeV, width $122.2 \pm 6.2 \pm 8.0$ MeV, with $KK^*$ limit $\Gamma_{X(1750)\to K^o K^{*o}} / \Gamma_{X(1750)\to K^+K^-} < 0.065$ at 90% confidence level
- L3 measurement of $\eta(1440)$ two-photon width: $\Gamma_{\gamma\gamma}(\eta(1440)) \cdot B_{KK\pi} = 212 \pm 50 \pm 23$ eV

## Context

This work extends previous $^3$P$_0$ model calculations of strange meson decays by Kokoski and Isgur (1987), Godfrey and Isgur (1984, 1985), and others, providing the most complete survey of strange meson decays to date. The results are intended to help distinguish conventional quark model mesons from exotica such as glueballs and hybrids through their strong decay patterns, particularly in light of upcoming experimental facilities including GlueX at Jefferson Lab and upgraded $e^+e^-$ facilities at VEPP and DAPHNE. The paper notes that the strangeonium sector remains poorly known experimentally, with only three well-established dominantly $s\bar s$ resonances (the $\phi$, $f'_2(1525)$, and $\phi_3(1854)$) out of 22 expected states up to 2.2 GeV, and identifies specific decay modes such as $\eta\phi$ and $\eta'\phi$ as "smoking gun" signatures for identifying $s\bar s$ states.
