# Selectivity of the Nucleon Induced Deuteron Breakup and Relativistic Effects

> Theoretical predictions for the nucleon induced deuteron breakup process based on solutions of the three-nucleon Faddeev equation including such relativistic features as the relativistic kinematics and boost effects are presented. Large changes of the breakup cross section in some complete configurations are found at higher energies. The predicted relativistic effects, which are mostly of dynamical origin, seem to be supported by existing data.

## Key claim

Relativistic effects in nucleon-induced deuteron breakup, included via the instant form of relativistic dynamics, produce large changes (up to ~60%) in the cross section for certain exclusive breakup configurations at higher energies, but these effects are mostly of dynamical origin and are not seen in integrated observables like the total breakup cross section.

## Method

The authors solved the three-nucleon Faddeev equation in both relativistic and nonrelativistic forms, using the CDBonn NN potential as input. The relativistic formulation employed boosted NN potentials and relativistic kinematics within the instant form of relativistic dynamics, with Wigner spin rotations neglected after checking they had negligible effects. Calculations were performed for nucleon laboratory energies up to 250 MeV, using partial wave states with total two-nucleon angular momenta up to j ≤ 5 and total three-nucleon angular momenta up to J = 25/2.

## Key numbers

- At 65 MeV lab energy, the symmetrical space star (SSS) configuration shows a relativistic reduction of ≈ 8% in the five-fold differential cross section
- At 65 MeV lab energy, the quasi-free scattering (QFS) configuration shows a relativistic reduction of ≈ 10% in the five-fold differential cross section
- At 200 MeV lab energy, certain breakup configurations show predicted relativistic effects of up to ≈ 60%
- The ratio of relativistic to nonrelativistic phase-space factor at 250 MeV is 1.175
- Total relativistic breakup cross section: 90.25 mb at 65 MeV and 43.37 mb at 250 MeV
- Total nonrelativistic breakup cross section: 91.12 mb at 65 MeV and 45.41 mb at 250 MeV
- Integrated elastic scattering cross section: 71.25 mb (relativistic) and 71.40 mb (nonrelativistic) at 65 MeV; 9.33 mb (relativistic) and 9.57 mb (nonrelativistic) at 250 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic Faddeev calculations for elastic Nd scattering (Witala 2005) to the breakup reaction, and addresses discrepancies between nonrelativistic theory and data that remained even after including three-nucleon forces (3NFs) of the 2π-exchange type. The selectivity of complete breakup configurations allows relativistic effects to be observed that are averaged out in elastic scattering and total cross sections, and the results suggest that remaining discrepancies at higher energies in integrated observables must come from 3NF contributions of a different character than 2π-exchange.
