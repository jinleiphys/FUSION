# Simple solutions of fireball hydrodynamics for self-similar elliptic flows

> Simple, self-similar, elliptic solutions of non-relativistic fireball hydrodynamics are presented, generalizing earlier results for spherically symmetric fireballs with Hubble flows and homogeneous temperature profiles. The transition from one dimensional to three dimensional expansions is investigated in an efficient manner.

## Key claim

The paper presents exact, self-similar, elliptic solutions of non-relativistic fireball hydrodynamics that generalize earlier spherically symmetric solutions, but these solutions are restricted to an ideal gas equation of state and a homogeneous temperature profile, and cannot be trivially generalized to arbitrary equations of state.

## Method

The authors assume an ideal, non-relativistic fluid described by the continuity, energy, and Euler equations with an ideal Boltzmann gas equation of state. They introduce three time-dependent scaling variables for the principal axes of an ellipsoidal expansion and assume all hydrodynamic functions factorize as products of functions of each scaling variable. This ansatz reduces the system of partial differential equations to a set of second-order ordinary differential equations for the scale functions X(t), Y(t), and Z(t), which describe the time evolution of the ellipsoidal fireball.

## Key numbers

- Initial conditions for a Landau-like expansion: X0 = 4 fm, Y0 = 3 fm, Z0 = 1/4 fm, with all initial velocities zero and T0/m = 0.1; this yields almost one-dimensional flow with only small transverse flow generation.
- For a more spherical initial profile: X0 = 4 fm, Y0 = 3 fm, Z0 = 3 fm, with initial velocities X0_dot = 0, Y0_dot = 0, Z0_dot = -0.5, and T0/m = 0.1; this generates a large amount of transverse flow and an almost spherically symmetric final flow.
- For the same spherical initial profile but with T0/m = 0.01, the flow becomes approximately one-dimensional again, demonstrating that increasing initial kinetic energy relative to thermal energy suppresses transverse flow.
- A general initial condition with X0 = 4 fm, Y0 = 3 fm, Z0 = 3 fm, initial velocities X0_dot = -0.2, Y0_dot = 0.1, Z0_dot = -0.5, and T0/m = 0.05 results in a dominantly two-dimensional expansion.

## Context

This work extends earlier exact non-relativistic hydrodynamic solutions for spherically symmetric fireballs with Hubble flows and homogeneous temperature profiles, such as those by Csorgo et al. (1996) and by Csorgo and collaborators (1997), to the case of elliptic, non-spherical expansions relevant for non-central heavy ion collisions. The results enable efficient numerical study of the transition from one-dimensional to three-dimensional expansions and can be applied to interpret elliptic flow patterns observed in experiments such as those by Poskanzer and Voloshin (1998) and Ackermann et al. (1999).
