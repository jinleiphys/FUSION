# Excitation spectra of heavy baryons in diquark models

> The excitation energy spectra of heavy baryons consisting of a heavy quark and two light quarks are investigated by using diquark models in order to examine the nature of the diquark as a constituent of single heavy baryons. We consider two diquark models; in model A the diquark is treated as a point-like particle, while it has a spatial size in model B. We determine the masses of scalar and axial vector diquarks by the mass difference of the ground state charmed baryons to the $Λ_{c}$ baryon, while the mass of the $ud$ scalar diquark in the $Λ_{c}$ baryon is assumed to be 500 MeV as a reference. The parameters of these models are fixed by the $1p$ excitation energy of $Λ_{c}$. We find that model A reproduces well the excitation energy spectra of the charmed and bottomed baryons, although the string tension of the confinement potential in model A should be a half of that of the charmonium, while Model B suggests degeneracy of the $2s$ and $1d$ states, which is not seen in the $Λ_{c}$ spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper finds that a diquark model treating the diquark as a point-like particle (Model A) reproduces the excitation energy spectra of charmed and bottomed baryons well, but only if the string tension of the confinement potential is taken as half of that used for charmonium; a model that gives the diquark a spatial size (Model B) predicts degeneracy of the 2s and 1d states that is not observed in the Λc spectrum.

## Method

The authors treat heavy baryons as two-body systems of a light diquark and a heavy quark, solving the radial Schrodinger equation with a central potential and ignoring fine structure from spin-dependent forces. In Model A, the diquark is point-like and the potential is a Coulomb-plus-linear form with string tension k = 0.5 GeV/fm, half the charmonium value. In Model B, the diquark has a Gaussian size distribution with parameter β = 1.0 fm, and the potential is obtained by folding the quark-quark interaction (taken as half the quark-antiquark interaction) over the diquark size distribution, including rotational excitations of the diquark with even angular momentum.

## Key numbers

- The spin-weighted average 1p excitation energy of Λc used to fix parameters is 0.33 GeV, calculated as (m(Λc(2595)) + 2*m(Λc(2625)))/3 - m(Λc).
- In Model A, the string tension is set to k = 0.5 GeV/fm, and the absolute potential constant is V0 = -0.054 GeV.
- In Model B, the Gaussian size parameter is β = 1.0 fm, giving a root mean squared diquark size of sqrt(<r^2>) = 1.2 fm for Lρ = 0, and the absolute potential constant is V0 = -0.565 GeV.
- The scalar ud diquark mass is assumed as 0.5 GeV; the charm quark mass is 1.5 GeV and the bottom quark mass is 4.0 GeV.
- Diquark masses determined from charmed baryon ground states in Model A: m^0_[qs] = 0.76 GeV, m^1_{qq} = 0.80 GeV, m^1_{qs} = 0.96 GeV, m^1_ss = 1.10 GeV; in Model B: m^0_[qs] = 0.75 GeV, m^1_{qq} = 0.79 GeV, m^1_{qs} = 0.94 GeV, m^1_ss = 1.07 GeV.
- The level crossing between 1dλ and 1dρ states in Model B for Λc occurs at β = 0.95 fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Jido (2016) and Kumakawa (2017) that first suggested the confinement potential for heavy quark-diquark systems should be weaker than for quarkonium, and that a finite diquark size could explain this. The paper systematically applies two diquark models to multiple heavy baryon families (Λc, Ξc, Σc, Ωc and their bottom counterparts), determining diquark masses from ground state masses. The results enable future work to distinguish between point-like and finite-size diquark pictures by measuring the 2s excitation energy of Λc and the ordering of 1d states, and to test whether the Λc(2765) state is a 2s excitation as predicted by Model A.
