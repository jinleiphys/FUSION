# Improved chiral nucleon-nucleon potential up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order

> We present improved nucleon-nucleon potentials derived in chiral effective field theory up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order. We argue that the nonlocal momentum-space regulator employed in the two-nucleon potentials of Refs. [E. Epelbaum, W. Gloeckle, U.-G. Meißner, Nucl. Phys. A747 (2005) 362], [D.R. Entem, R. Machleidt, Phys. Rev. C68 (2003) 041001] is not the most efficient choice, in particular since it affects the long-range part of the interaction. We are able to significantly reduce finite-cutoff artefacts by using an appropriate regularization in coordinate space which maintains the analytic structure of the amplitude. The new potentials do not require the additional spectral function regularization employed in Ref. [E. Epelbaum, W. Gloeckle, U.-G. Meißner, Nucl. Phys. A747 (2005) 362] to cut off the short-range components of the two-pion exchange and make use of the low-energy constants c_i and d_i determined from pion-nucleon scattering without any fine tuning. We discuss in detail the construction of the new potentials and convergence of the chiral expansion for two-nucleon observables. We also introduce a new procedure for estimating the theoretical uncertainty from the truncation of the chiral expansion that replaces previous reliance on cutoff variation.

## Key claim

The authors present improved chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials at N3LO that use a coordinate-space regularization scheme, which they argue reduces finite-cutoff artifacts compared to the nonlocal momentum-space regulator used in prior work (Epelbaum, Gloeckle, Meissner 2005 and Entem, Machleidt 2003), while maintaining the analytic structure of the amplitude and avoiding the need for spectral function regularization.

## Method

The authors constructed chiral NN potentials up to N3LO using a local regularization of pion-exchange contributions in coordinate space, rather than the nonlocal momentum-space regulator employed in earlier potentials. They used low-energy constants c_i and d_i determined from pion-nucleon scattering without fine tuning, and introduced a new procedure for estimating theoretical uncertainty from truncation of the chiral expansion that replaces previous reliance on cutoff variation. The potentials were fitted to the Nijmegen partial wave analysis phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff range employed: Lambda = 450 to 600 MeV (consistent with Epelbaum, Gloeckle, Meissner 2005)
- The Idaho N3LO potential (Entem, Machleidt 2003) is available for two cutoff values: Lambda = 500 MeV and Lambda = 600 MeV
- The breakdown scale estimate: M_rho approximately 770 MeV or Lambda_chi approximately 1.2 GeV, but the authors note a more conservative estimate is appropriate
- Pion decay constant: F_pi approximately 92 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT approach to NN potentials pioneered by Weinberg (1990) and developed quantitatively by van Kolck (1994) and Ordonez et al. (1995). It directly improves upon the N3LO potentials of Epelbaum, Gloeckle, Meissner (2005) and Entem, Machleidt (2003) by introducing a coordinate-space regularization that avoids the spectral function regularization needed in the former and the variable regulator functions used in the latter. The new potentials enable consistent treatment of three-nucleon forces at N3LO within the Low Energy Nuclear Physics International Collaboration (LENPIC) framework, and provide a more reliable estimation of theoretical uncertainties for ab-initio calculations of nuclear structure and reactions.
