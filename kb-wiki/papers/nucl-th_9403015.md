# A quark-meson model for nuclear and neutron matter

> An explicit quark model, based on a mean field description of non-overlapping nucleon bags bound by the self-consistent exchange of $σ$, $ω$ and $ρ$ mesons, is used to investigate the properties of both nuclear and neutron matter. We establish a clear understanding of the relationship between this model, which incorporates the internal structure of the nucleon, and QHD. Finally, we use the model to study the density dependence of the quark condensate in-medium.

## Key claim
The paper shows that a quark-meson model, which treats nucleons as non-overlapping bags bound by self-consistent σ, ω, and ρ meson exchange in a mean-field approximation, reproduces the bulk properties of nuclear and neutron matter while also providing a clear connection to Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) and allowing the density dependence of the in-medium quark condensate to be studied.

## Method
The authors construct a mean-field model where nucleons are described as quark bags (the "quark-meson coupling" or QMC model) and the σ, ω, and ρ mesons are treated as classical fields that couple directly to the quarks inside the bags. They solve the self-consistent equations for nuclear matter and pure neutron matter, and then compute the in-medium quark condensate as a function of baryon density.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear matter saturation density is reproduced at a Fermi momentum of approximately 1.3 fm⁻¹.
- The in-medium quark condensate decreases with density, reaching about 0.8 of its vacuum value at nuclear saturation density.
- For neutron matter, the energy per particle is higher than for symmetric nuclear matter at the same density, consistent with the symmetry energy.

## Context
This work extends the earlier QHD approach of Serot and Walecka (1986) by explicitly incorporating the internal quark structure of the nucleon, rather than treating it as a point particle. It also provides a framework for studying the behavior of the quark condensate in dense matter, which is relevant for understanding chiral symmetry restoration in neutron stars and heavy-ion collisions. The model enables future calculations of in-medium hadron properties and the equation of state for astrophysical applications.
