# Charged-current reactions in the supernova neutrino-sphere

> We calculate neutrino absorption rates due to charged-current reactions $╬Į_e+n \rightarrow e^- + p $ and $\bar╬Į_e+p \rightarrow e^+ + n $ in the outer regions of a newly born neutron star called the neutrino-sphere. To improve on recent work which has shown that nuclear mean fields enhance the $╬Į_e$ cross-section and suppress the $\bar╬Į_e$ cross-section, we employ realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions that fit measured scattering phase shifts. Using these interactions we calculate the momentum-, density-, and temperature-dependent nucleon self-energies in the Hartree-Fock approximation. A potential derived from chiral effective field theory and a pseudo-potential constructed to reproduce nucleon-nucleon phase shifts at the mean-field level are used to study the equilibrium proton fraction and the charged-current rates are studied in detail. We compare our results to earlier calculations obtained using phenomenological mean-field models and to those obtained in the virial expansion valid at low density. We find that for typical ambient conditions in the neutrino-sphere, $T=5-10$ MeV and $Žü=10^{11}-10^{13}$ g/cm$^3$, the difference between the $╬Į_{e}$ and $\bar╬Į_e$ absorption rates is much larger than predicted earlier. Our results have important implications for heavy-element nucleosynthesis in supernovae and for supernova neutrino detection.

## Key claim

Using realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions that fit measured scattering phase shifts, the authors find that the difference between electron neutrino and anti-electron neutrino absorption rates in the neutrino-sphere is much larger than predicted by earlier phenomenological mean-field models, though the magnitude of this effect depends on whether the chiral NN potential or the pseudo-potential (which includes ladder resummation) is used, with the latter giving significantly larger energy shifts.

## Method

The authors calculate nucleon self-energies in the Hartree-Fock approximation using two realistic nuclear interactions: a chiral effective field theory potential at N$^3$LO (Entem-Machleidt 2003) and a pseudo-potential constructed directly from nucleon-nucleon phase shifts from the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis. These self-energies are used to compute momentum-, density-, and temperature-dependent nucleon dispersion relations, which then enter the charged-current neutrino absorption cross-sections for $\nu_e+n \rightarrow e^-+p$ and $\bar{\nu}_e+p \rightarrow e^++n$ via Fermi's golden rule with appropriate Fermi distribution functions and final-state blocking factors.

## Key numbers

- At baryon density $n_B = 0.02$ fm$^{-3}$ and temperature $T = 8$ MeV, the proton fraction $Y_p$ is 4.9% for the HF pseudo-potential, 3.8% for the modified pseudo-potential, and 1.9% for the HF chiral NN potential.
- The difference in proton and neutron mean-field shifts $\Delta U = U_p - U_n$ at $n_B = 0.02$ fm$^{-3}$ and $T = 8$ MeV is 33 MeV for the HF pseudo-potential, 23 MeV for the modified pseudo-potential, and 3 MeV for the HF chiral NN potential.
- The neutron effective mass $M^*_n/M_n$ at $n_B = 0.02$ fm$^{-3}$ and $T = 8$ MeV is 0.65 for the HF pseudo-potential, 0.78 for the modified pseudo-potential, and 0.94 for the HF chiral NN potential.
- The proton effective mass $M^*_p/M_p$ at the same conditions is 0.42 for the HF pseudo-potential, 0.57 for the modified pseudo-potential, and 0.90 for the HF chiral NN potential.
- The energy shift $U_n$ is 22 MeV (pseudo-potential), 18 MeV (modified pseudo-potential), and 7 MeV (chiral NN); $U_p$ is 55 MeV (pseudo-potential), 42 MeV (modified pseudo-potential), and 10 MeV (chiral NN).
- The typical ambient conditions studied are temperature $T = 5-10$ MeV and density $\rho = 10^{11}-10^{13}$ g/cm$^3$.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Martinez-Pinedo (2012), Roberts (2012), and Horowitz (2012) that used relativistic mean-field models and the virial expansion to show that nuclear mean fields enhance $\nu_e$ absorption and suppress $\bar{\nu}_e$ absorption. By employing realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions that fit scattering phase shifts, the authors provide a more microscopically grounded calculation of the nucleon self-energies and their impact on charged-current rates. The results have implications for the electron fraction in the neutrino-driven wind and the conditions for r-process nucleosynthesis, as well as for predictions of supernova neutrino signals in terrestrial detectors.
