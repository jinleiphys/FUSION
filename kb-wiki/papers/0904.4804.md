# Dynamically generated resonances from the interaction of vector mesons with baryons

> We present the results of the first calculations involving the interaction of vector mesons with baryons, by means of which one generates a large amount of dynamically generated resonances, many of which can be associated to known resonances, while others represent predictions for new states.

## Key claim

The interaction of vector mesons with baryons, treated within a hidden gauge formalism and unitary coupled-channel approach, dynamically generates a large set of baryon resonances, many of which can be associated with known states while others are predictions for new resonances; the results are obtained under the approximation of neglecting the three-momentum of the particles compared to their mass, which leads to spin degeneracy (1/2^- and 3/2^- for the octet case; 1/2^-, 3/2^-, and 5/2^- for the decuplet case).

## Method

The authors use the hidden gauge formalism for vector mesons, which provides Lagrangians for vector-vector interactions and for the coupling of vector mesons to baryons of the octet and decuplet. The transition potentials for vector-baryon scattering are derived from vector-meson exchange diagrams, and the scattering amplitudes are obtained by solving the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation in the on-shell factorization approach, with the loop function for a vector meson and a baryon calculated in dimensional regularization and convoluted with the spectral function of the vector mesons to account for their width.

## Key numbers

- For the vector-octet interaction: a pole at 1977 + i 53 MeV (mass 1972 MeV, width 64 MeV) is associated with N(2080) (3/2^-) and N(2090) (1/2^-); a pole at 1784 + i 4 MeV (mass 1783 MeV, width 9 MeV) is associated with Λ(1690) (3/2^-) and Λ(1800) (1/2^-); a pole at 2039 + i 67 MeV (mass 2039 MeV, width 64 MeV) is associated with Ξ(1950).
- For the vector-decuplet interaction: a pole at 1972 + i 49 MeV (mass 1971 MeV, width 52 MeV) is associated with Δ(1900) (1/2^-), Δ(1940) (3/2^-), and Δ(1930) (5/2^-); a pole at 2052 + i 10 MeV (mass 2050 MeV, width 19 MeV) is associated with Λ(2000); a pole at 2449 + i 7 MeV (mass 2445 MeV, width 13 MeV) is associated with Ω(2470).
- The theory predicts a degenerate N* state around 2270 MeV (bump) and a degenerate Δ* state around 2200 MeV (bump) from the vector-decuplet interaction.

## Context

This work extends the successful application of chiral unitary approaches from pseudoscalar-meson–baryon interactions (which generated J^P = 1/2^- resonances, as in Kaiser (1995) and Oller and Meissner) and pseudoscalar–decuplet interactions (which generated J^P = 3/2^- resonances, as in Kolomeitsev and Lutz and Sarkar) to the vector-meson sector. It builds on the hidden gauge formalism (Bando et al.) and on prior work on ρΔ interactions by Vijande (2008) and on vector–decuplet interactions by Sarkar (2009). The results enable further experimental searches for predicted resonances, particularly in the Ξ and Ω sectors, and provide a framework for understanding the nature of baryonic resonances as dynamically generated from vector-meson–baryon interactions.
