# Surface tension in the cold and dense chiral transition and astrophysical applications

> The surface tension of cold and dense QCD phase transitions has appeared recently as a key ingredient in different astrophysical scenarios, ranging from core-colapse supernovae explosions to compact star structure. If the surface tension is low enough, observable consequences are possible. Its value is however not known from first-principle methods in QCD, calling for effective approaches. Working within the framework of homogeneous nucleation by Langer, we discuss the steps that are needed to obtain the nucleation parameters from a given effective potential. As a model for deriving the effective potential for the chiral transition, we adopt the linear sigma model with constituent quarks at very low temperatures, which provides an effective description for the thermodynamics of the strong interaction in cold and dense matter, and predict a surface tension of Sigma ~ 5--15 MeV/fm^2, well below previous estimates. Including temperature effects and vacuum logarithmic corrections, we find a clear competition between these features in characterizing the dynamics of the chiral phase conversion.

## Key claim

The surface tension for the cold and dense chiral phase transition in QCD is predicted to be \(\Sigma \sim 5\)–\(15\) MeV/fm\(^2\), well below previous estimates, but this result is obtained within the linear sigma model with constituent quarks and should be considered with caution when applied to compact stars or the early universe because the model does not reproduce nuclear matter features such as saturation density and binding energy.

## Method

The authors adopt the homogeneous nucleation framework of Langer, constructing a coarse-grained free energy functional from an effective potential for the chiral order parameter \(\sigma\). They use the linear sigma model with constituent quarks (LSMq) at very low temperatures and finite quark chemical potential, computing the effective potential up to one-loop order including medium and vacuum fluctuations in the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme. The surface tension is extracted from the critical bubble profile obtained as an extremum of the coarse-grained free energy.

## Key numbers

- Surface tension for the zero-temperature chiral transition: \(\Sigma = 4\)–\(13\) MeV/fm\(^2\), well below previous estimates of \(50\)–\(150\) MeV/fm\(^2\) (Voskresensky 2002) and \(\sim 300\) MeV/fm\(^2\) (Alford 2001).
- Including thermal corrections at \(T = 30\) MeV, the surface tension reaches a minimum of \(\sim 3\) MeV/fm\(^2\).
- The metastable region in the presence of quantum vacuum terms is \(\sim 40\%\) larger than in its absence; the critical chemical potential shifts only \(\sim 2\%\).
- Nucleation parameters up to \(T = 10\) MeV show variations within \(\sim 10\%\) of the zero-temperature values.

## Context

This work extends previous estimates of the QCD surface tension by Alford (2001) and Voskresensky (2002), which gave values too high for observable astrophysical consequences. The lower surface tension found here makes nucleation of quark matter possible during the early post-bounce stage of core collapse supernovae, as discussed by Sagert (2008) and Mintz (2009). The results enable more realistic modeling of phase conversion dynamics in compact stars and primordial quark-hadron transitions, though first-principle QCD calculations in this regime remain out of reach.
