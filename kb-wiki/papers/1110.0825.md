# Jet Quenching via Gravitational Radiation in Thermal AdS

> We argue that classical bulk gravitational radiation effects in AdS/CFT, previously ignored because of their subleading nature in the 1/Nc-expansion, are magnified by powers of large Lorentz factors gamma for ultrarelativistic jets, thereby dominating other forms of jet energy loss in holography at finite temperature. We make use of the induced gravitational self-force in thermal AdS5 to estimate its effects. In a thermal medium, relativistic jets may loose most of their energy through longitudinal drag caused by the energy accumulated in their nearby field as they zip through the strongly coupled plasma.

# Jet Quenching via Gravitational Radiation in Thermal AdS

## Key claim

Classical bulk gravitational radiation effects in AdS/CFT, previously ignored because they are subleading in the 1/Nc-expansion, are magnified by powers of the large Lorentz factor γ for ultrarelativistic jets, potentially dominating other forms of holographic jet energy loss at finite temperature, but only if the jet is sufficiently relativistic that the γ² enhancement overcomes the 1/Nc² suppression.

## Method

The authors compute the gravitational self-force on an ultrarelativistic particle falling along a geodesic in thermal AdS₅, using the Mino-Sasaki-Tanaka/Quinn-Wald formalism for the gravitational radiation reaction force. They expand the retarded graviton propagator in small proper time ε ≈ 1/γ², retaining the leading singularity, and evaluate the covariant expansion of the Van Vleck determinant in terms of the Riemann tensor squared. The result is integrated over proper time with a resummation that yields a finite longitudinal drag force, which is then translated via holographic dictionary to a 4-dimensional energy loss rate.

## Key numbers

- The proper time cutoff scales as ε ≈ 1/γ², where γ is the Lorentz factor of the jet.
- The gravitational self-force in AdS₅ yields a longitudinal drag with energy loss rate dE/dt ≈ E² / Nc² × F(z/zh), where F is a function of the holographic coordinate z relative to the horizon at zh = 1/(πT).
- The ratio of gravitational-radiation drag to color-string drag is approximately γ² / (Nc² √λ), which for typical parameters gives a range of 10²..⁴ / (10 × 5).
- For cyclotron radiation in flat 3+1 spacetime, the intensity scales as I ≈ e² γ⁴ / R²; for gravitational radiation around a Schwarzschild black hole, I ≈ G₄ m² γ² / R².

## Context

This work extends the analysis of gravitational radiation reaction from flat and Schwarzschild backgrounds (Khriplovich 1973) to thermal AdS₅, motivated by the need to understand jet quenching in strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma as observed at RHIC and LHC. It contrasts with prior holographic jet quenching studies using falling strings (Gubser 2008, Chesler 2008, Arnold 2011) and with the color-drag mechanism from trailing strings, which scales as γ √λ T². The result suggests that gravitational radiation could produce energy loss with power p > 1 in dE/dx ≈ E^p, leading to a finite final jet energy independent of initial energy, a behavior previously noted by Kharzeev (2008). The work enables future quantitative comparison with RHIC and LHC jet quenching data and motivates more precise calculation of the function F(z/zh).
