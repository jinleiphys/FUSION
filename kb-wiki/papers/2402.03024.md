# Improved analysis of isovector nucleon matrix elements with $N_f=2+1$ flavors of $\mathcal{O}(a)$ improved Wilson fermions

> We present an update of our determination of the isovector charges $g_A^{u-d}$, $g_S^{u-d}$ and $g_T^{u-d}$, and the isovector twist-2 forward matrix elements $\langle x\rangle_{u-d}$, $\langle x\rangle_{Δu-Δd}$ and $\langle x\rangle_{δu-δd}$ on the $N_\mathrm{f}=2+1$ gauge ensembles generated by the Coordinated Lattice Simulations (CLS) effort. We have significantly extended our coverage of the parameter space by adding ensembles at the physical pion mass and fine lattice spacing, at nearly-physical pion masses and very fine lattice spacings, and at very large physical lattice volumes, enabling a well-controlled extrapolation to the physical point. Another major improvement is achieved owing to the extended range of source-sink separations, which allows us to perform two-state fits to summed correlator ratios, leading to a much higher level of control over excited-state effects. Systematic uncertainties from the chiral, continuum and infinite-volume extrapolations are incorporated via model averages based on the Akaike Information Criterion. Our final results at the physical point are $g_A^{u-d} = 1.254(19)_\mathrm{stat}(15)_\mathrm{sys}[24]_\mathrm{total}$, $g_S^{u-d} = 1.203(77)_\mathrm{stat}(81)_\mathrm{sys}[112]_\mathrm{total}$, $g_T^{u-d} = 0.993(15)_\mathrm{stat}(05)_\mathrm{sys}[16]_\mathrm{total}$, $\langle x\rangle_{u-d} = 0.153(15)_\mathrm{stat}(10)_\mathrm{sys}[17]_\mathrm{total}$, $\langle x\rangle_{Δu - Δd} = 0.207(15)_\mathrm{stat}(06)_\mathrm{sys}[16]_\mathrm{total}$, and $\langle x\rangle_{δu - δd} = 0.195(17)_\mathrm{stat}(15)_\mathrm{sys}[23]_\mathrm{total}$. While our results for the isovector charges are in excellent agreement with the FLAG\,21 averages, we note that our error for the tensor charge $g_T^{u-d}$ is considerably smaller.

## Key claim

The authors present updated lattice QCD determinations of six isovector nucleon matrix elements, claiming significantly improved control over excited-state contamination through two-state fits to summed correlator ratios, but note that the chiral extrapolation is not strongly reliant on the validity of chiral perturbation theory because the data exhibit a flat chiral behavior that does not clearly show the curvature imposed by chiral logarithms.

## Method

The analysis uses \(N_f=2+1\) flavors of \(\mathcal{O}(a)\) improved Wilson fermions on CLS gauge ensembles, with an extended parameter space including physical pion mass, fine lattice spacings, and large volumes. Ground-state matrix elements are extracted via two-state fits to summed correlator ratios (summation method) with source-sink separations down to \(\tsepmin \approx 0.2\)–\(0.4\) fm, and the physical extrapolation in pion mass, lattice spacing, and volume is performed using NNLO \(SU(2)\) baryon chiral perturbation theory models, with systematic uncertainties incorporated through Akaike Information Criterion model averaging.

## Key numbers

- \(g_A^{u-d} = 1.254(19)_\text{stat}(15)_\text{sys}[24]_\text{total}\) at the physical point

- \(g_T^{u-d} = 0.993(15)_\text{stat}(05)_\text{sys}[16]_\text{total}\) at the physical point, with an error considerably smaller than the FLAG 21 average

- \(\langle x\rangle_{\delta u-\delta d} = 0.195(17)_\text{stat}(15)_\text{sys}[23]_\text{total}\) at the physical point

## Context

This work extends the previous analysis by Harris (2019) with additional ensembles at the physical pion mass, finer lattice spacings, and larger volumes, as well as improved excited-state control via two-state summation fits rather than the ratio fit model used previously. The results for the isovector charges agree with the FLAG 21 averages, while the tensor charge \(g_T^{u-d}\) achieves a notably smaller total error. The improved precision and controlled systematics enable more stringent tests of nucleon structure predictions and provide input for phenomenological analyses of neutrino-nucleus scattering and searches for physics beyond the Standard Model.
