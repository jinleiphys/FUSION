# Discovery of Jet Quenching and Beyond

> Recent observation of high-$p_T$ hadron spectra suppression and mono-jet production in central $Au+Au$ collisions and their absence in $d+Au$ collisions at RHIC have confirmed the long predicted phenomenon of jet quenching in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. Detailed analyses of the experimental data also show parton energy loss as the mechanism for the discovered jet quenching. Using a pQCD parton model that incorporates medium modified parton fragmentation functions and comparing to experimental data from deeply inelastic scattering off nuclei, one can conclude that the initial gluon (energy) density of the hot matter produced in central $Au+Au$ collisions that causes jet quenching at RHIC is about 30 (100) times higher than in a cold $Au$ nucleus. Combined with data on bulk and collective properties of the hot matter, the observed jet quenching provides strong evidence for the formation of a strongly interacting quark gluon plasma in central $Au+Au$ collisions at RHIC.

## Key claim

The observed suppression of high-$p_T$ hadron spectra and mono-jet production in central $Au+Au$ collisions at RHIC, combined with their absence in $d+Au$ collisions, confirms the long-predicted phenomenon of jet quenching caused by parton energy loss in a hot dense medium; however, the quantitative extraction of the initial gluon density depends on a pQCD parton model incorporating medium-modified fragmentation functions and a single free parameter fitted to DIS data, yielding an initial gluon density about 30 (100) times higher than in a cold $Au$ nucleus.

## Method

The analysis uses a leading-order pQCD parton model that incorporates medium-modified parton fragmentation functions, where the modification is calculated via a twist-expansion approach to multiple parton scattering and induced gluon radiation, including the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal interference effect. The model is applied to single-inclusive hadron spectra in $Au+Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, assuming a one-dimensional expanding medium with a gluon density profile proportional to the transverse participant nucleon distribution, and the energy loss is parameterized with an energy-dependent form that includes detailed balance (gluon absorption). The single free parameter $\widetilde{C}(Q^2)=0.0060$ GeV$^2$ is fixed by fitting the nuclear modification of fragmentation functions in HERMES deeply inelastic scattering data on nuclear targets.

## Key numbers

* Initial gluon (energy) density in central $Au+Au$ collisions at RHIC is about 30 (100) times higher than in a cold $Au$ nucleus.
* Quark energy loss $dE/dL \approx 0.5$ GeV/fm inside a $Au$ nucleus, extracted from HERMES DIS data with $\langle x_B \rangle \approx 0.124$.
* The twist-4 matrix element parameter $\widetilde{C}(Q^2)=0.0060$ GeV$^2$ at $Q^2 \approx 3$ GeV$^2$ with $\alpha_s(Q^2)=0.33$.
* To fit the observed $\pi^0$ suppression in central $Au+Au$ collisions, the parameters used are $\mu=1.5$ GeV, $\epsilon_0=1.07$ GeV/fm, and $\lambda_0=1/(\sigma\rho_0)=0.3$ fm, with a variation of $\epsilon_0=\pm 0.3$ GeV/fm.
* The predicted nuclear modification of fragmentation functions shows a quadratic $A^{2/3}$ nuclear size dependence, verified by HERMES data on $^{14}N$ and $^{84}Kr$ targets.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing theoretical prediction of jet quenching by Gyulassy and Wang (1992) and the subsequent pQCD studies of parton energy loss by Baier et al. (BDMPS) and by Gyulassy, Levai, and Vitev (GLV) in the opacity expansion framework. The analysis builds on the twist-expansion approach to medium-modified fragmentation functions developed by Wang and Guo (2001) for DIS off nuclei, and applies it to heavy-ion collisions at RHIC. The results, combined with data on bulk collective properties and the absence of suppression in $d+Au$ collisions (which rules out initial-state effects), provide strong evidence for the formation of a strongly interacting quark gluon plasma in central $Au+Au$ collisions, and enable quantitative jet tomography of the dense medium.
