# Case for quarkyoniclike matter from a constituent quark model

> Based on the fact that the constituent quark model reproduces the recent lattice result on baryon-baryon repulsion at short distance and that it includes the quark dynamics with confinement, we analyze to what extent the quarkyonic modes appear in the phase space of baryons as one increases the density before only quark dynamics and hence deconfinement occurs. We find that as one increases the baryon density, the initial quark mode that appears will involve the $d(u)$-quark from a neutron (proton), which will leave the most attractive ($ud$) diquark intact.

# Case for quarkyoniclike matter from a constituent quark model

## Key claim
Using a constituent quark model that reproduces lattice results on baryon-baryon repulsion at short distance, the authors find that at high nuclear density (above about 4 times nuclear saturation density) the energy of a d-quark excitation mode from a neutron becomes smaller than the baryon excitation mode at low momentum, but the initial quark mode involves only a single d-quark (or u-quark from a proton) leaving the attractive (ud) diquark intact, rather than a full shell-like quarkyonic phase.

## Method
The authors calculate the energies of baryon excitation and quark excitation modes for two nucleons separated by an average inter-baryon distance corresponding to a given density, using a constituent quark model Hamiltonian with color Coulomb, confining, and color-spin hyperfine interactions. The baryon-baryon repulsion and quark-nucleon repulsion are parametrized from the constituent quark model calculations, and the minimal uncertainty relation r = 1/k is used to express position-space energies in momentum space. The onset condition for the d-quark plus ud-diquark configuration is found by extremizing the energy density at fixed total baryon density, requiring the chemical potentials of the neutron mode and the d-quark plus diquark mode to be equal.

## Key numbers
- The baryon-baryon repulsion fit parameters: a_B = 0.218 GeV·fm, b_B = 1.474 fm (from the I=1, S=0 channel)
- The quark-nucleon repulsion fit parameters: a_Q = 0.2 GeV·fm, b_Q = 0.754 fm (from the I=1, S=0 four-quark configuration)
- Constituent quark mass for u,d quarks: m_Q = 0.343 GeV
- String tension in vacuum: sigma = 0.962 GeV/fm, with medium modification sigma_n = sigma * (1 - 0.05 * n/n_0) where n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- The d-quark excitation mode becomes energetically favored over the baryon mode at nuclear densities n > 4 n_0 (above about 4 times nuclear saturation density)

## Context
This work extends the quarkyonic matter concept introduced by McLerran (2007) from large-N_c QCD to a constituent quark model framework that can be tested against lattice QCD results. It builds on the finding by Park (2019) that the constituent quark model reproduces lattice baryon-baryon interactions at short distance. The analysis supports phenomenological applications of quarkyonic matter to neutron star studies by McLerran (2018), Jeong (2019), and others, which found that a hard-soft evolution of the equation of state appears naturally inside neutron stars. The results suggest that the transition to quarkyonic-like matter begins with single-quark excitations rather than a full shell-like momentum distribution, which would require higher densities and multi-neutron interactions to develop.
