# Many-body effects in 16O(e,e'p)

> Effects of nucleon-nucleon correlations on exclusive $(e,e'p)$ reactions on closed-shell nuclei leading to single-hole states are studied using $^{16}O(e,e'p)^{15}N$ ($6.32$ MeV, $3/2^-$) as an example. The quasi-hole wave function, calculated from the overlap of translationally invariant many-body variational wave functions containing realistic spatial, spin and isospin correlations, seems to describe the initial state of the struck proton accurately inside the nucleus, however it is too large at the surface. The effect of short-range correlations on the final state is found to be largely cancelled by the increase in the transparency for the struck proton. It is estimated that the values of the spectroscopic factors obtained with the DWIA may increase by a few percent due to correlation effects in the final state.

## Key claim

The quasi-hole wave function calculated from realistic many-body variational wave functions describes the initial state of the struck proton accurately inside the nucleus, but is too large at the surface; the effect of short-range correlations on the final state is largely cancelled by increased transparency, so that spectroscopic factors obtained with the distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) may increase by only a few percent due to correlation effects in the final state.

## Method

The authors calculated the quasi-hole wave function for the $^{16}\mathrm{O}(e,e'p)^{15}\mathrm{N}$ reaction leading to the $3/2^-$ state at 6.32 MeV from the overlap of translationally invariant variational wave functions for $^{16}\mathrm{O}$ and $^{15}\mathrm{N}$, using the Argonne v$_{14}$ two-nucleon interaction and the Urbana model VII three-nucleon interaction. They then studied three modifications to the ejected proton wave function: replacing the Perey factor with an effective-mass correction from the local-density approximation, multiplying the distorted wave by $\sqrt{Z(\vec{r})}$ to account for short-range correlations, and multiplying by a factor $\lambda(\vec{r})$ from the pair distribution function to account for the increased transparency (correlation-hole effect). The resulting cross sections were compared to NIKHEF data for the 6.32 MeV state.

## Key numbers

- The quasi-hole normalization $Z_h$ from the full variational calculation (including two- and three-body cluster terms) is 0.82; the authors expect a complete calculation would give $Z_h = 0.81 \pm 0.02$.
- The empirical spectroscopic factor for the 6.32 MeV state from fitting NIKHEF data is $Z = 0.53$, while the many-body calculation gives $Z_{6.32} = 0.86 \times Z_h = 0.70$.
- The center-of-mass effects alone reduce $Z_h$ from unity to 0.88; including only central correlations $f_c(r_{ij})$ gives $Z_h = 0.87$.
- The cross sections obtained with the fully corrected ejected proton wave function are approximately 3% smaller than those obtained with the standard Perey-factor treatment.
- The cross sections obtained with the $\sqrt{Z(\rho)}$ correction alone (without the transparency correction) are smaller by approximately 10% than those from the standard treatment.
- The equilibrium density of nuclear matter used in the local-density approximation is $\rho_o = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$.
- The 6.32 MeV state in $^{15}\mathrm{N}$ carries 86% of the total $p_{3/2}$ strength in the three lowest $3/2^-$ states.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of quasi-hole orbitals in helium liquid drops by Lewart and collaborators, and builds on variational calculations of $^{16}\mathrm{O}$ by Pieper and of $^{15}\mathrm{N}$ hole states by the same group. It contrasts with the Brueckner-theory calculations of Muther and Dickhoff, who obtained $Z_h = 0.91$ for the $p_{3/2}$ state by neglecting center-of-mass motion. The results suggest that the variational wave functions used here do not adequately describe the nuclear surface, and that improved treatments of surface clustering are needed. The approximate corrections to the final-state wave function indicate that spectroscopic factors extracted from NIKHEF data may need to be increased by a few percent, but a full calculation with the present wave functions still substantially overpredicts the observed cross sections.
