# Recent Advances in the Microscopic Calculations of Level Densities by the Shell Model Monte Carlo Method

> The shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method enables calculations in model spaces that are many orders of magnitude larger than those that can be treated by conventional methods, and is particularly suitable for the calculation of level densities in the presence of correlations. We review recent advances and applications of SMMC for the microscopic calculation of level densities. Recent developments include (i) a method to calculate accurately the ground-state energy of an odd-mass nucleus, circumventing a sign problem that originates in the projection on an odd number of particles, and (ii) a method to calculate directly level densities, which, unlike state densities, do not include the spin degeneracy of the levels. We calculated the level densities of a family of nickel isotopes $^{59-64}$Ni and of a heavy deformed rare-earth nucleus $^{162}$Dy and found them to be in close agreement with various experimental data sets.

## Key claim

The Shell Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method can now calculate level densities (which count each level once, ignoring spin degeneracy) for both even-mass and odd-mass nuclei, with the key technical advance being a Green's function method that circumvents the sign problem that previously prevented accurate ground-state energy determination in odd-particle systems.

## Method

The authors used the SMMC method with a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to represent the Gibbs operator as a functional integral over auxiliary fields, enabling calculations in model spaces much larger than conventional diagonalization methods. For odd-mass nuclei, they extracted ground-state energies from the asymptotic slope of imaginary-time single-particle Green's functions of neighboring even-even nuclei, avoiding the sign problem that arises from projecting on an odd number of particles. For level densities, they projected onto the minimal absolute value of the magnetic quantum number (M=0 for even-mass, M=1/2 for odd-mass nuclei) using a discrete Fourier transform, then applied the saddle-point approximation to the M-projected partition function.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of 62Ni: E0 = -271.87(3) MeV (determined from saturation of thermal energy at beta > 3.5 MeV^-1)
- Ground-state energy of 59Ni: E0 = -251.72(1) MeV (determined from Green's function method using both 58Ni and 60Ni)
- SMMC level densities for nickel isotopes 59-64Ni show close agreement with experimental data from proton evaporation spectra (Voinov 2012), level counting (Ripl3), and neutron resonance data (Ripl3)
- For 162Dy, SMMC level density agrees with level counting data below Ex ~ 2 MeV, renormalized Oslo data (Oslo2000, Guttormsen 2003), and neutron resonance data (Ripl3)
- In even-even 60Ni, the moment of inertia is strongly suppressed at low excitation energies relative to the rigid-body value; in odd-mass 59Ni this suppression is weaker, with the odd-even effect observed below Ex ~ 8 MeV

## Context

This work extends the SMMC method for state densities previously applied to mid-mass nuclei (Nakada 1997, Langanke 1998, Alhassid 1999) and heavy nuclei (Alhassid 2008, Ozen 2013) by adding two key capabilities: accurate treatment of odd-mass nuclei via the Green's function method (Mukherjee 2012) and direct calculation of level densities via M-projection (Alhassid 2013). The results enable direct comparison with experimental level densities from proton evaporation spectra (Voinov 2012) and Oslo-type measurements, and reveal signatures of pairing correlations through odd-even effects in the moment of inertia.
