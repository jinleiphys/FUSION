# Thermodynamically consistent formulation of quasiparticle viscous hydrodynamics

> A novel formulation of second-order relativistic viscous fluid dynamics based on the effective Boltzmann equation for quasi-particles with medium-dependent masses is briefly reviewed.~The evolution equations for the shear and bulk dissipative corrections, and the corresponding transport coefficients, are presented. Resulting approach allows for thermodynamically-consistent incorporation of lattice QCD equation of state in the fluid dynamical framework.

## Key claim
The paper presents a second-order relativistic viscous hydrodynamics framework for quasiparticles with temperature-dependent masses that, unlike previous approaches, allows thermodynamically-consistent incorporation of any realistic lattice QCD equation of state, provided the non-equilibrium mean-field ansatz is adopted to restore energy-momentum conservation.

## Method
The author derives evolution equations for shear and bulk dissipative corrections from an effective Boltzmann equation for quasiparticles with medium-dependent masses, using the Chapman-Enskog-like iterative solution. The collision term is treated in the relaxation-time approximation. Thermodynamic consistency is restored by introducing a non-equilibrium mean-field term \(B^{\mu\nu}\) in the energy-momentum tensor, split into equilibrium and non-equilibrium parts, with the non-equilibrium part constrained by an ansatz that limits its independent components.

## Key numbers
- Bulk viscosity coefficient: \(\zeta = \beta_\Pi \tau_R\), where \(\beta_\Pi = \frac{5}{3} \beta I_{3,2} - c_s^2 (\mathcal{E} + \mathcal{P}) + \kappa c_s^2 m^2 \beta I_{1,1}\), with \(\kappa \equiv (T/m)(dm/dT)\) and \(\beta \equiv 1/T\).
- Shear viscosity coefficient: \(\eta = \beta_\pi \tau_R\), where \(\beta_\pi = \beta I_{3,2}\).
- Second-order transport coefficients for bulk pressure evolution: \(\delta_{\Pi\Pi}\) (Eq. 18), \(\lambda_{\Pi\pi}\) (Eq. 19), and for shear tensor evolution: \(\tau_{\pi\pi} = 2 - 4\beta I_{3,3}/\beta_\pi\), \(\lambda_{\pi\Pi} = -2\chi/3\), \(\delta_{\pi\pi}\) (Eq. 21), with \(\chi\) defined in terms of \(I_{n,q}\) integrals, \(c_s^2\), and \(\kappa\).

## Context
This work extends the quasiparticle formulation of relativistic hydrodynamics that previously suffered from violation of basic thermodynamic relations when using temperature-dependent masses, as noted by Gorenstein (1995). It builds on the non-equilibrium mean-field idea first introduced for anisotropic hydrodynamics by Alqahtani (2016, 2017). The resulting framework enables thermodynamically-consistent use of lattice QCD equations of state in heavy-ion collision simulations, addressing a long-standing problem in the field.
