# Effects of the Tensor Force on the Multipole Response in Finite Nuclei

> We present a thorough analysis of the effects of the tensor interaction on the multipole response of magic nuclei, using the fully self-consistent Random Phase Approximation (RPA) model with Skyrme interactions. We disentangle the modifications to the static mean field induced by the tensor terms, and the specific features of the residual particle-hole (p-h) tensor interaction, for quadrupole (2+), octupole (3-), and also magnetic dipole (1+) responses. It is pointed out that the tensor force has a larger effect on the magnetic dipole states than on the natural parity states 2+ and 3-, especially at the mean field level. Perspectives for a better assessment of the tensor force parameters are eventually discussed.

## Key claim

The tensor force has a larger effect on magnetic dipole (1+) states than on natural parity states (2+ and 3-), especially at the mean field level, but the overall impact on the multipole response depends on a delicate interplay between modifications to the static mean field and the specific features of the residual particle-hole tensor interaction.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) plus Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations using Skyrme interactions supplemented with zero-range tensor terms, for the magic nuclei 40Ca, 48Ca, and 208Pb. They disentangled the effects of the tensor force on the static mean field from those of the residual particle-hole tensor interaction by analyzing the shifts in unperturbed HF energies and RPA eigenvalues for quadrupole (2+), octupole (3-), and magnetic dipole (1+) responses, using the SLy5 Skyrme set with added tensor parameters and the T44 set where tensor terms were fitted together with all other parameters.

## Key numbers

- For the low-lying 2+ state in 48Ca with SLy5 plus tensor, the mean-field shift ΔE_HF = 1.41 MeV, the RPA shift ΔE_RPA = 0.83 MeV, and the extracted average residual tensor matrix element ⟨V_tensor⟩ = -0.58 MeV.
- For the low-lying 2+ state in 208Pb with SLy5 plus tensor, ΔE_HF = 0.60 MeV, ΔE_RPA = 0.17 MeV, and ⟨V_tensor⟩ = -0.43 MeV.
- For the low-lying 2+ state in 48Ca with T44, ΔE_HF = -0.13 MeV, ΔE_RPA = -0.18 MeV, and ⟨V_tensor⟩ = -0.05 MeV.
- For the low-lying 2+ state in 208Pb with T44, ΔE_HF = -0.54 MeV, ΔE_RPA = -0.55 MeV, and ⟨V_tensor⟩ = -0.01 MeV.
- For the lowest 3- state in 40Ca with SLy5 plus tensor, the extracted ⟨V_tensor⟩ = -0.76 MeV.
- For the magnetic dipole state in 48Ca with SLy5 plus tensor, the unperturbed neutron f7/2→f5/2 configuration shifts from 7.06 MeV to 10.68 MeV; the RPA peak moves from 9.28 MeV to 12.31 MeV, giving a tensor correlation shift of 3.03 MeV and ⟨V_tensor⟩ = -0.59 MeV.
- For the magnetic dipole state in 48Ca, experimental M1 peak is at 10.23 MeV; theoretical values are 9.28 MeV (SLy5 without tensor), 12.31 MeV (SLy5 with tensor), and 10.47 MeV (T44).
- For the magnetic dipole response in 208Pb with SLy5 plus tensor, the two RPA peaks move from 7.39 MeV and 9.14 MeV to 7.79 MeV and 10.57 MeV; extracted ⟨V_tensor⟩ values are -0.20 MeV (proton) and -0.25 MeV (neutron).
- For the magnetic dipole response in 208Pb with T44, the two RPA peaks are at 6.12 MeV and 8.27 MeV, compared to experimental values of 5.85 MeV and 7.30 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of tensor force effects on single-particle energies (Otsuka et al., 2005) and on bulk properties (Lesinski et al., 2007) by providing the first self-consistent analysis of tensor force effects on the multipole response of finite nuclei within the Skyrme-RPA framework. It complements related work by De Donno et al. on low-lying magnetic excitations using Gogny forces, and by Davesne et al. (2009) on the response of uniform matter with Skyrme forces and tensor terms. The results enable future efforts to better constrain tensor force parameters by comparing RPA predictions with experimental data on excited states, particularly magnetic dipole states, and to improve Skyrme parameter sets that currently have unsatisfactory spin and spin-isospin Landau parameters.
