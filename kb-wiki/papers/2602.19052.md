# Probing the structure of pygmy dipole resonance with its gamma decay

> The isospin properties and the collectivity of the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) are long-standing open questions in nuclear structure studies. To answer these questions, the $γ$-decay of PDR states in $^{208}$Pb to the low-lying $2_{1}^{+}$ state is investigated using the Skyrme particle-vibration coupling (PVC) model. It is found that the $E1$ $γ$ decay from the PDR states to the low-lying $2_{1}^+$ state is strongly suppressed compared to that from the isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR), which reveals the predominantly isoscalar character of PDR. A detailed decomposition of the decay diagrams of the amplitudes of the processes contributing to the decay demonstrates the non-negligible presence of the 1 particle-1 hole configurations coupled to the $2_1^{+}$ phonon in the PDR wave function. Furthermore, we give a quantitative way to identify the components of complex-configurations in the wave function, and it is found that such component in PDR is smaller than that in IVGDR and much smaller than that in isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (ISGQR).

# Probing the structure of pygmy dipole resonance with its gamma decay

## Key claim
The E1 gamma decay from PDR states to the low-lying 2_1^+ state in 208Pb is strongly suppressed compared to that from the IVGDR, revealing the predominantly isoscalar character of PDR. This conclusion depends on the cancellation between proton and neutron contributions to the E1 transition amplitude, which is analyzed using the Skyrme particle-vibration coupling (PVC) model with three different energy density functionals (SGII, SLy5, LNS).

## Method
The authors performed fully self-consistent random phase approximation (RPA) calculations for 208Pb using three Skyrme energy density functionals (SGII, SLy5, LNS) with a box size of 20 fm and energy cut-off of 150 MeV for single-particle states. They then computed the gamma-decay transition strengths from selected PDR states (6-8 MeV) and GDR states (10-18 MeV) to the 2_1^+ state using the Skyrme PVC model, which includes 12 lowest-order diagrams at the RPA and PVC levels. The ratio R = B^pn_gamma(E-H)/B^pn_gamma was introduced to quantify the proportion of 1p-1h⊗2_1^+ configurations in the wave function, isolating this component from isospin effects and collectivity.

## Key numbers
- The PDR states in 208Pb are located around the neutron emission threshold E_th = 7.37 MeV, consistent with the (gamma,gamma') experiment of Ryezayeva (2002).
- The fractions of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) exhausted by the selected pygmy dipole states are 0.65% (SGII), 0.78% (SLy5), and 0.81% (LNS), compared to the experimental value 0.92 ± 0.09% from Poelhekken (1992).
- The slopes of the symmetry energy at saturation density L for the three functionals are 37.6 MeV (SGII), 48.2 MeV (SLy5), and 61.5 MeV (LNS).
- For PDR decay, B_gamma is less than half of the average proton-neutron contribution B^pn_gamma; for GDR decay, B_gamma exceeds twice B^pn_gamma.
- The ratio R = B^pn_gamma(E-H)/B^pn_gamma for PDR is 61% (SGII), 48% (SLy5), and 33% (LNS); for GDR it is 64% (SGII), 94% (SLy5), and 79% (LNS); for GQR it is 99% (SGII), 95% (SLy5), and 101% (LNS).

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of Brenna (2012) and Lv (2021) for gamma-decay of giant resonances to the pygmy dipole resonance region, using the Skyrme PVC model. It addresses long-standing open questions about the isospin character and collectivity of PDR, which have been debated in experimental studies by Savran (2006), Wieland (2009), Rossi (2013), and Martorana (2018), and in theoretical work by Tsoneva (2008), Roca Maza (2012), and Spieker (2020). The results enable future experimental tests at facilities such as the Laboratori Nazionali di Legnaro (LNL) and the Shanghai Synchrotron Radiation Facility (SSRF), and provide a quantitative method to identify complex-configuration components in resonance wave functions.
