# Monte Carlo simulations of the S-shaped neutron guides with asymmetric concave and convex surface coatings

> During the last decades, neutron beam transportation has been a well-known and established subject for designing proper neutron guides. However, sometimes unusual adaptation or adjustments are required out of original projects and after operation beginning of facilities. Inter-center transferring of instrument locations also requires a new approach that is not necessarily described in the literature. Inside these situations, the use of S-shaped guides has not been fully discussed in the literature. From a geometrical analysis, we develop a formalism of construction of a minimal S-shaped guide by only considering the exclusion of the Line-of-Sight. We study this guide model through the wavelength cutoff and the neutron transport efficiency analysis. Here, Monte Carlo simulations using MCSTAS software are applied. By intending to optimize these guide systems, simulations of this study also consider scenarios that have different supermirrors. A formalism to determine wavelength cutoff for unique and variable index guide systems is also developed. Simulation results show a good agreement between theoretical and simulated wavelength cutoff values. In addition, we have found specific configurations that combine efficient neutron transport and lower index values on the convex surfaces of curved guides that form the S-shaped guide.

## Key claim

The paper develops a formalism for constructing minimal S-shaped neutron guides that exclude line-of-sight (LoS) using only geometric constraints, and shows through Monte Carlo simulations that specific configurations can combine efficient neutron transport with lower supermirror index (m-value) coatings on the convex surfaces of the curved sections, but this optimization is conditional on equalizing the concave-surface m-values via a virtual curvature adjustment.

## Method

The authors derived geometric equations for the minimal arcs and straight-guide lengths needed to exclude LoS in S-shaped guides composed of two oppositely curved sections connected by straight entrance and exit segments, considering three configurations (a, b, c) based on whether the curved arcs are larger or smaller than the characteristic angle. They then performed Monte Carlo simulations using MCSTAS 3.0 with three ILL source types (cold, thermal, hot) and varied the supermirror indices on concave and convex surfaces, validating the theoretical wavelength cutoff formula and testing the effect of asymmetric coatings (m_out > m_in) on transport efficiency.

## Key numbers

* Guide width and height were fixed at 5 cm for all simulations.
* The characteristic wavelength is given by λ_c = (1/(1.73×10⁻³ m)) √(2W/ρ), where m is the supermirror index, W is guide width, and ρ is curvature radius.
* The theoretical wavelength cutoff for a classical S-shaped guide (configuration c) with equal curvatures is λ_cut = (1/(1.73×10⁻³ m)) φ, where φ = √(W/ρ) = Ψ_c/√2.
* The maximum vertical displacement Z achievable with minimal LoS-excluding arcs is 8W, obtained when L₂ = √(8Wρ₂) and L₃ = √(8Wρ₃) under the assumption L << ρ.
* For equal curvatures (ρ₂ = ρ₃), the singularity in the straight-guide length equation occurs at a ratio R₂/₃ = 35.35%, where R₂/₃ = γ_i / (2Ψ_c) is the ratio of the curved arc to twice the characteristic angle.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Mildner (1990) on acceptance diagrams for curved guides and the asymmetric coating approach of Cook (2009), which showed that different concave and convex mirror indices (m_out < m_in) can maintain the same neutron flux for a specific wavelength range. The study addresses a gap in the literature regarding S-shaped guides, which have been used in practice (e.g., at FRM-II for the SANS instrument described by Gilles (2006)) but lacked a systematic geometric treatment for minimal LoS exclusion. The results enable cost-effective design of S-shaped guides for facilities like the European Spallation Source (ESS), where long guide systems may suffer from misalignment due to foundation settling (Husgard (2020)), and for cold-neutron instruments such as LoKI (Andersen (2020)) where wavelength cutoff of epithermal neutrons is beneficial.
