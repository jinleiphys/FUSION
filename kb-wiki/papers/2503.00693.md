# Possible Explanation of $F_2^n/F_2^p$ at Large $x$ Using Quantum Statistical Mechanics

> The recent accurate measurements of the scattering of electrons off of the mirror nuclei $^3$H and $^3$He show with small errors that the neutron to proton ratio $r(x)=F^n_2(x)/F^p_2(x)$, approaches a value larger than 1/4 for $x \to 1$. We suggest a possible explanation for this experimental result by studying the consequences of the Pauli exclusion principle for the parton distribution when the ratio is described by quantum statistical mechanics description in terms of three parameters inspired by the quantum statistical approach.

# Possible Explanation of $F_2^n/F_2^p$ at Large $x$ Using Quantum Statistical Mechanics

## Key claim

The MARATHON experiment's measurement that $F_2^n(x)/F_2^p(x)$ approaches a constant value of approximately 0.45 as $x \to 1$, rather than the expected 1/4, can be explained by the quantum statistical approach where quark distributions follow Fermi-Dirac statistics, provided the temperature parameter $\bar{x}$ and chemical potentials are appropriately chosen.

## Method

The authors fit the MARATHON data for $F_2^n(x)/F_2^p(x)$ using a simplified form where the sum of two Fermi-Dirac functions for valence quarks is approximated by a single Fermi-Dirac function with an intermediate chemical potential, yielding an expression for $d(x)/u(x)$. They then perform a new global fit including the MARATHON data and the latest SeaQuest Drell-Yan data (2208 data points total) at $Q_0 = 1$ GeV, evolving the parton distribution functions to higher $Q$ using NLO QCD.

## Key numbers

- The MARATHON data show $F_2^n(x)/F_2^p(x)$ approaches $\sim 0.45$ at the highest $x$ values, significantly above the Nachtmann inequality lower bound of 1/4.
- For $\bar{x}=0.099$, the fit gives $X'_u = 0.415 \pm 0.013$, $X'_d = 0.301 \pm 0.010$, and $r(x\to 1) = 0.455 \pm 0.006$ with $\chi^2 = 0.68$.
- For $\bar{x}=0.101$, the fit gives $X'_u = 0.419 \pm 0.013$, $X'_d = 0.303 \pm 0.010$, and $r(x\to 1) = 0.453 \pm 0.005$ with $\chi^2 = 0.63$.
- For $\bar{x}=0.128$, the fit gives $X'_u = 0.486 \pm 0.018$, $X'_d = 0.332 \pm 0.013$, and $r(x\to 1) = 0.436 \pm 0.007$ with $\chi^2 = 0.32$.
- The $F_2^n(x)/F_2^p(x)$ ratio decreases by $0.212 \pm 0.026$ in the $x$ range [0.195, 0.51] and by $0.057 \pm 0.019$ in the range [0.51, 0.825].
- The new global fit yields $\bar{x}=0.101$, $\Xuup = 0.438$, $\Xddown=0.302$, $\Xudown=0.337$, $\Xdup=0.240$.

## Context

This work extends the quantum statistical approach to parton distributions originally proposed by Bourrely, Buccella, and Soffer (2002), which has successfully predicted $\bar{d}(x) > \bar{u}(x)$ (confirmed by Fermilab E866 and SeaQuest) and $\Delta \bar{u}(x) > 0 > \Delta \bar{d}(x)$ (confirmed by STAR at RHIC). The MARATHON data contradict the conventional CJ15LO parametrization from the CTEQ-JLab Collaboration, which predicts $d(x)/u(x) \to 0$ and $F_2^n/F_2^p \to 1/4$ as $x \to 1$. The quantum statistical approach provides a natural explanation for the slower fall-off of $d(x)/u(x)$ at large $x$ due to the Fermi-Dirac form of quark distributions, and the new global fit demonstrates that the MARATHON data can be simultaneously accommodated with existing DIS and Drell-Yan data.
