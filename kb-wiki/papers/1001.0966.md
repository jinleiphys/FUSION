# Nuclear forces from chiral EFT: The unfinished business

> In spite of the great progress we have seen in recent years in the derivation of nuclear forces from chiral effective field theory (EFT), some important issues are still unresolved. In this contribution, we discuss the open problems which have particular relevance for microscopic nuclear structure, namely, the proper renormalization of chiral nuclear potentials and sub-leading many-body forces.

## Key claim

Despite the great progress in deriving nuclear forces from chiral effective field theory (EFT), the proper renormalization of chiral nuclear potentials and the treatment of sub-leading many-body forces remain unresolved issues that are particularly relevant for microscopic nuclear structure.

## Method

The authors review the status of chiral EFT for nuclear forces, focusing on two open problems: the nonperturbative renormalization of nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials when inserted into the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, and the derivation of three-nucleon forces (3NF) beyond next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). They analyze different renormalization schemes, including infinite-cutoff approaches and schemes where the cutoff is kept below the chiral symmetry breaking scale, and examine the convergence of the chiral expansion for 3NF both with and without explicit Delta-isobar degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

* The chiral expansion parameter is \((Q/\Lambda_\chi)^\nu\) where \(\Lambda_\chi \approx 1\) GeV is the chiral symmetry breaking scale.
* Leading-order (LO) NN potentials start at \(\nu=0\), three-nucleon forces start at \(\nu=2\) (but cancel at that order), and four-nucleon forces start at \(\nu=4\).
* The wound integral in nuclear matter for the renormalized LO interaction with infinite cutoff is about 40%, compared to 5–10% for conventional nuclear forces.
* The leading four-nucleon force at N\(^3\)LO contributes about 0.1 MeV to the \(^4\)He binding energy (total binding energy: 28.3 MeV).
* Typical cutoff values used in chiral NN potentials are \(\Lambda \approx 0.5\) GeV, well below \(\Lambda_\chi \approx 1\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT program for nuclear forces pioneered by Weinberg (1990, 1992) and developed by many groups including Epelbaum, Glöckle, and Meißner (2005) and Entem and Machleidt (2003). It addresses criticisms raised by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1996) regarding the renormalization of Weinberg counting, and subsequent investigations by Nogga, Timmermans, and van Kolck (2005) and Valderrama and Arriola (2005–2007). The paper enables future systematic studies of cutoff independence using Lepage plots and motivates the explicit inclusion of Delta-isobar degrees of freedom to improve convergence of the three-nucleon force expansion.
