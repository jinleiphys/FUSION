# Exploring the QCD phase diagram via reweighting from isospin chemical potential

> We investigate the QCD phase diagram for small values of baryon and strange quark chemical potentials from simulations at non-zero isospin chemical potential. Simulations at pure isospin chemical potential are not hindered by the sign problem and pion condensation can be observed for sufficiently large isospin chemical potentials. We study how the related phase boundary evolves with baryonic and strange chemical potentials via reweighting in quark chemical potentials and discuss our results. Furthermore, we propose and implement an alternative method to approach nonzero baryon (and strange quark) chemical potentials. This method involves simulations where physical quarks are paired with auxiliary quarks in unphysical "isospin" doublets and a decoupling of the auxiliary quarks by mass reweighting.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that reweighting from simulations at pure isospin chemical potential (which avoid the sign problem) can map the QCD phase boundary for small baryon and strange chemical potentials, but the results are preliminary and obtained on small (8^4) lattices, with an unexpected behavior of the chiral condensate at non-zero baryon chemical potential that may be unphysical.

## Method
The authors perform lattice QCD simulations at non-zero isospin chemical potential with physical quark masses, using a staggered fermion matrix with explicit symmetry-breaking terms to regulate pion condensation. They then apply two reweighting techniques: (1) reweighting in chemical potentials from finite isospin to finite baryon and strange chemical potentials using determinant reduction, and (2) a novel reweighting in quark masses where auxiliary quarks paired with physical quarks in "isospin" doublets are decoupled by increasing their mass. Both methods are tested on 8^4 lattices with overlap and sign-problem diagnostics.

## Key numbers
- The strange quark condensate drops significantly for strange chemical potential \(\mu_s > 0.6 \cdot m_K\) (kaon mass), with the onset of kaon condensation proposed at \(\mu_s < 0.865 \cdot m_K\) by Mammarella (2015).
- The reweighting overlap criterion \(\gamma > 0.5\) is used to select reliable results, with the partition function required to be real and positive.
- The chiral condensate shows a depletion surface bending toward larger \(\mu_I\) values at non-zero \(\mu_B\), with an unexpected falloff across the line from \((\mu_B=0, \mu_I=m_\pi/2)\) to \((\mu_B=3m_\pi/2, \mu_I=0)\).
- For mass reweighting, the auxiliary quark masses are tuned to be about 11 times heavier than the physical light quark mass to improve overlap, with optimal overlap when varying the auxiliary mass by a factor between one and three.

## Context
This work extends the continuum phase diagram of QCD at pure isospin chemical potential previously obtained by Brandt (2017) with physical quark masses, which showed Bose-Einstein condensation of charged pions. The reweighting approach from isospin chemical potential provides an alternative to Taylor expansion methods for accessing small baryon and strange chemical potentials, which are otherwise hindered by the sign problem. The proposed novel mass-reweighting method aims to reach regions beyond the nucleon production threshold that are inaccessible to standard reweighting techniques, though the results remain preliminary and require larger lattices and further investigation of the unexpected chiral condensate behavior.
