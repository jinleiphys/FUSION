# In-medium pseudoscalar $D/B$ mesons and charmonium decay width

> Using QCD sum rules and chiral SU(3) model, we investigate the effect of temperature, density, strangeness fraction and isospin asymmetric parameter on the shift in masses and decay constants of the pseudoscalar $D$ and $B$ meson in hadronic medium, which consist of nucleons and hyperons. The in-medium properties of $D$ and $B$ mesons within QCD sum rule approach depend upon the quark and gluon condensates. In chiral SU(3) model, quark and gluon condensates are introduced through the explicit symmetry breaking term and the trace anomaly property of the QCD, respectively and are written in terms of scalar fields $σ$, $ζ$, $δ$ and $χ$. Hence, through medium modification of $σ$, $ζ$, $δ$ and $χ$ fields, we obtain the medium modified masses and decay constants of $D$ and $B$ mesons. As an application, using $^3 P_0$ model, we calculate the in-medium decay width of the higher charmonium states $ ψ(3686)$, $ψ(3770)$ and $χ(3556)$ to the $D \bar{D}$ pairs, considering the in-medium mass of $D$ mesons. These results may be important to understand the possible outcomes of high energy physics experiments, e.g., CBM and PANDA at GSI, Germany.

## Key claim

Using QCD sum rules combined with the chiral SU(3) model, the authors calculate the shift in masses and decay constants of pseudoscalar D and B mesons in a hadronic medium that includes nucleons and hyperons, with the medium modification entering through the scalar fields sigma, zeta, delta, and chi. The abstract's claim of investigating temperature, density, strangeness fraction, and isospin asymmetry is qualified by the fact that the temperature dependence enters solely through the temperature dependence of the scalar fields calculated within the chiral model, rather than through a separate pion bath contribution.

## Method

The authors first solve the coupled equations of motion for the scalar fields sigma, zeta, delta, and the scalar dilaton field chi within the chiral SU(3) model using mean-field approximation, for different values of baryon density, temperature, strangeness fraction, and isospin asymmetry. They then express the light quark condensates (langle bar{u} u rangle and langle bar{d} d rangle) and the scalar gluon condensate (langle frac{alpha_s}{pi} G^a_{munu} G^{a mu nu} rangle) in terms of these scalar fields. These medium-modified condensates are used as input in QCD sum rules for the two-point correlation function of the isospin-averaged pseudoscalar current, from which the mass shift and decay constant shift of D and B mesons are extracted via the forward scattering amplitude and Borel transformation. Finally, using the in-medium D meson masses, the partial decay widths of the higher charmonium states psi(3686), psi(3770), and chi(3556) to D bar{D} pairs are calculated using the 3P0 model.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear matter saturation density: 0.15 fm^{-3}
* Vacuum masses used: M_{D^+} = 1.869 GeV, M_{D^0} = 1.864 GeV, M_{B^+} = 5.279 GeV, M_{B^0} = 5.280 GeV
* Vacuum decay constants used: f_D = 210 MeV, f_B = 190 MeV
* Average coupling constant g_{DN Lambda_c} approx g_{DN Sigma_c} approx g_{BN Lambda_c} approx g_{BN Sigma_c} approx 6.74
* 3P0 model parameters: oscillator parameter beta = 0.3 GeV, ratio r = 1.04, vertex strength gamma = 0.281 (fitted to the experimental decay width of psi(3770) to D bar{D})
* Light quark masses used: m_u = 5 MeV, m_d = 7 MeV
* Dilaton field parameter d = 2/11 (determined from QCD beta function at one loop for N_c=3, N_f=3)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on scalar, vector, and axial-vector D and B mesons (Chhabra and Kumar, 2016) using the same combined QCD sum rule and chiral SU(3) model approach, now applied to pseudoscalar mesons. It builds upon the chiral SU(3) model developed by Papazoglou et al. and the QCD sum rule formalism for D mesons from Wang (2012). The calculation of in-medium charmonium decay widths using the 3P0 model follows the approach of Friman et al. The results are relevant for interpreting J/psi suppression in heavy-ion collisions at CBM and PANDA at GSI, as the in-medium mass shift of D mesons affects whether higher charmonium states decay to D bar{D} pairs rather than cascading to J/psi.
