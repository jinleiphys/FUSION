# Resonances and Decay Widths within a Relativistic Coupled Channel Approach

> We present a microscopic model for hadron resonances which contains, in addition to constituent (anti)quarks, mesonic degrees of freedom. It is assumed that the (anti)quarks are confined by an instantaneous potential and that the mesons can couple directly to the (anti)quarks. This system is treated within a relativistic coupled-channel formalism in order to take the dynamics of the mesonic degrees of freedom fully into account. It is demonstrated that the mass eigenvalue problem for such a system can be reformulated as a purely hadronic eigenvalue problem in which bare hadrons, i.e. eigenstates of the pure confinement problem, are coupled via meson loops. The substructure of the bare hadrons is then hidden in (bare) hadron-meson vertex form factors. It is shown for a simple toy model that such a kind of approach may lead to reasonable (non-perturbative) decay widths for hadron resonances.

# Resonances and Decay Widths within a Relativistic Coupled Channel Approach

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a relativistic coupled-channel formalism, in which bare hadron states (eigenstates of a pure confinement problem) are mixed and dressed via Goldstone-boson loops, can produce non-perturbative decay widths for hadron resonances. The authors show for a simple toy model that this approach yields reasonable decay widths, but the results are limited to a simplified scalar model with only radial excitations and the full chiral constituent quark model for baryons remains to be tested.

## Method
The authors formulate a mass eigenvalue problem for a two-channel system consisting of a valence (anti)quark component and a valence (anti)quark plus Goldstone-boson component, using point-form relativistic quantum mechanics with the Bakamjian-Thomas construction. They eliminate the Goldstone-boson channel via Feshbach reduction, obtaining an optical potential that couples bare meson states through Goldstone-boson loops. The resulting nonlinear eigenvalue equation is solved iteratively for a toy model with harmonic oscillator confinement, scalar quarks, and a scalar Goldstone boson, considering only radial excitations with a maximum of two bare states.

## Key numbers
- The (anti)quark mass is set to 0.34 GeV and the Goldstone-boson mass to the pion mass.
- The Goldstone-boson-quark coupling is varied within the range allowed by the Goldberger-Treiman relation: 0.67 ≤ g²/4π ≤ 1.19.
- With g²/4π = 1.19, the two lowest-lying states have masses of about 0.8 GeV and 1.44 GeV, respectively.
- The first excited state has a decay width of 0.026 GeV at this coupling.
- The iterative solution converges after 5 iterations, and increasing the number of bare states changes results by only a few percent.

## Context
This work extends the chiral constituent quark model (Glozman, 1995) by incorporating relativistic dynamics and a non-perturbative treatment of mesonic degrees of freedom, addressing the known problem that perturbative calculations of strong decay widths in constituent-quark models are notoriously too small (Melde, 2006; Metsch, 2008). The approach reformulates the quark-level problem into a hadronic eigenvalue equation with vertex form factors, enabling future application to baryon resonances within the full chiral constituent quark model, where typical decay widths of 0.1 GeV or more are expected.
