# The nuclear and Coulomb breakup of the weakly bound $^{6}$Li with targets in the range from A = 59 to 208

> We have performed CDCC calculations for the $^{6}$Li + $^{59}$Co, $^{144}$Sm and $^{208}$Pb systems, to investigate the dependence of the relative importance of nuclear and Coulomb breakup on the target charge (mass) at near barrier energies. The calculations were in good agreement with the experimental elastic scattering angular distributions for these systems and then, their predictions to the nuclear, Coulomb and total breakup were investigated. Although the relative importance of the nuclear breakup is, as expected, larger for lighter targets, this effect is not very pronounced. We also investigate a scaling of the nuclear breakup with the target mass and we compare the predictions for the integrated total breakup cross sections with experimental fusion cross sections at similar energies.

## Key claim

The relative importance of nuclear breakup over Coulomb breakup is larger for lighter targets, but this effect is not very pronounced; the nuclear breakup cross section scales nearly linearly with the cube root of the target mass number, while the Coulomb breakup cross section scales linearly with the target charge, at near-barrier energies.

## Method

The authors performed Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) calculations for the breakup of \(^{6}\)Li projectiles on \(^{59}\)Co, \(^{144}\)Sm, and \(^{208}\)Pb targets at near-barrier energies. The projectile was modeled as a bound \(d+\alpha\) cluster, and the real parts of the cluster-target interactions were given by the double-folding Sao Paulo potential, while the imaginary parts used Woods-Saxon functions with depth \(W_0 = -50\) MeV, radial parameter \(r_{0i} = 1.06\) fm, and diffusivity \(a_i = 0.2\) fm to represent fusion absorption. Inelastic channels for collective target excitations were included, and the numerical calculations were performed using the computer code FRESCO.

## Key numbers

- Coulomb barrier heights: \(V_{\rm B}(^{59}{\rm Co}) = 12.8\) MeV, \(V_{\rm B}(^{144}{\rm Sm}) = 23.8\) MeV, \(V_{\rm B}(^{208}{\rm Pb}) = 29.4\) MeV.
- For \(^{6}\)Li + \(^{59}\)Co at \(E_{\rm lab} = 14.0\) MeV: nuclear breakup cross section = 8.72 mb, Coulomb breakup cross section = 9.27 mb, total breakup cross section = 11.56 mb.
- For \(^{6}\)Li + \(^{144}\)Sm at \(E_{\rm lab} = 27.0\) MeV: nuclear breakup cross section = 43.6 mb, Coulomb breakup cross section = 57.3 mb, total breakup cross section = 69.6 mb.
- For \(^{6}\)Li + \(^{208}\)Pb at \(E_{\rm lab} = 33.0\) MeV: nuclear breakup cross section = 38.7 mb, Coulomb breakup cross section = 66.8 mb, total breakup cross section = 82.5 mb.
- For the nuclear breakup scaling at \(E_{\rm c.m.}/V_{\rm B} = 1.07\), the fitted parameters are \(P_1 = -49.89\) mb and \(P_2 = 15.41\) mb.
- The breakup cross section for the light \(^{6}\)Li + \(^{59}\)Co system is nearly two orders of magnitude smaller than the fusion cross section.

## Context

This work extends the CDCC-based investigation of breakup processes for weakly bound \(^{6}\)Li projectiles, previously applied to the \(^{6}\)Li + \(^{144}\)Sm system by Otomar (2010), to a broader range of target masses from A = 59 to 208. It tests the nuclear breakup scaling law proposed by Hussein, Lotti, and Nunes (2006) for halo and non-halo systems at high energies, now applied at near-barrier energies, and finds a similar linear dependence on \(A_{\rm T}^{1/3}\). The comparison of predicted breakup cross sections with experimental fusion data from Beck, Rath, and Wu enables assessment of the relative importance of breakup and fusion channels across different target masses.
