# Light and heavy flavor dijet production and dijet mass modification in heavy ion collisions

> Back-to-back light and heavy flavor dijet measurements are promising experimental channels to accurately study the physics of jet production and propagation in a dense QCD medium. They can provide new insights into the path length, color charge, and mass dependence of quark and gluon energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma produced in reactions of ultra-relativistic nuclei. To this end, we perform a comprehensive study of both light and heavy flavor dijet production in heavy ion collisions. We propose the modification of dijet invariant mass distributions in such reactions as a novel observable that shows enhanced sensitivity to the QGP transport properties and heavy quark mass effects on in-medium parton showers. This is achieved through the addition of the jet quenching effects on the individual jets as opposed to their subtraction. The latter drives the subtle effects on more conventional observables, such as the dijet momentum imbalance shifts, which we also calculate here. Results are presented in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV for comparison to data at the Large Hadron Collider and in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV to guide the future sPHENIX program at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the modification of dijet invariant mass distributions in heavy ion collisions is a novel observable with enhanced sensitivity to quark-gluon plasma transport properties and heavy quark mass effects, because it combines jet quenching effects on individual jets rather than subtracting them as in conventional dijet momentum imbalance measurements.

## Method

The authors used Pythia 8 to simulate dijet production in p+p collisions at both LHC and RHIC energies, validating their simulations against experimental data from CMS and ATLAS. They implemented medium modification effects using the SCET_M,G framework with radiative and collisional energy loss, calculating medium-induced splitting functions via Monte Carlo integration with the VEGAS algorithm. The medium-modified double differential cross sections in transverse momentum bins were then used to compute dijet invariant mass and imbalance distributions through the approximate formula relating these observables.

## Key numbers

- Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV for comparison to LHC data; Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV for the sPHENIX program at RHIC
- 8 million events simulated for each of inclusive and b-tagged dijet processes in Pythia 8
- Jet radius R = 0.6 for inclusive dijet validation against CMS at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV; R = 0.4 for b-tagged dijet validation against ATLAS at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV
- Coupling between jet and medium set to g = 1.8, consistent with previous studies
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross sections: sigma_in = 70 mb at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV; sigma_in = 42 mb at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- The approximate mass formula deviates by less than or equal to 10% from direct Pythia 8 simulation
- For b-tagged dijets at LHC, the b+bbar subprocess provides greater than or equal to 50% contribution across a wide kinematic range; at sPHENIX, the b+bbar channel dominates above p_T = 10 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dijet asymmetry and momentum imbalance in heavy ion collisions (Qin 2010, Young 2011, He 2011) by proposing dijet invariant mass as a more sensitive observable. It builds on the theoretical framework for jet quenching in the SCET_M,G formalism (Kang 2016, Ovanesyan 2011) and earlier work on b-jet production in heavy ion collisions (Huang 2013, Li 2018). The study provides predictions for the upcoming sPHENIX experiment at RHIC and comparison with CMS measurements at the LHC, enabling future experimental tests of the mass dependence of parton energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma.
