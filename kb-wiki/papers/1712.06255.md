# Phenomenological QCD equations of state for neutron star mergers, a talk presented at CPOD17

> We delineate the properties of dense QCD matter through equations of state constrained by the neutron star observations. The two solar mass constraint, the radius constraint of 11-13 km, and the causality constraint on the speed of sound, are used to develop the picture of hadron-quark continuity in which hadronic matter continuously transforms into quark matter. A unified equation of state at zero temperature and beta-equilibrium is constructed by a phenomenological interpolation between nuclear and quark matter equations of state. For applications to supernovae and neutron star mergers, the unified equation of state is perturbed by temperature corrections.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a smooth hadron-quark continuity picture, without strong first-order phase transitions between 2 and 5 times nuclear saturation density, can simultaneously satisfy the two-solar-mass constraint, the radius constraint of 11-13 km, and the causality constraint, but only for a narrow range of quark-model coupling parameters (vector coupling \(g_V \gtrsim 0.7 G_s\) and diquark coupling \(H \gtrsim 1.4 G_s\)).

## Method

The author constructs a unified zero-temperature equation of state for neutron star matter in beta-equilibrium using a three-window approach: the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall (APR) nuclear equation of state for densities below \(2n_0\), a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type quark model with vector and color-magnetic interactions for densities above \(5n_0\), and a polynomial interpolation in baryon chemical potential between these two regimes. The quark model is solved in the mean-field approximation with electric and color charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium conditions, and the resulting mass-radius relations are compared against the two-solar-mass and radius constraints. For finite-temperature applications to neutron star mergers, the author outlines a program to compute thermal corrections using the phase shift representation of the thermodynamic potential within the random phase approximation.

## Key numbers

* The baryon density domain relevant for neutron star physics is \(n_B \sim 1-10 n_0\) where \(n_0 \simeq 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) is the nuclear saturation density; the corresponding baryon chemical potential is \(\mu_B \sim 1-2\ \mathrm{GeV}\) and temperature \(T \sim 0-100\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The radius constraint from X-ray analyses converges toward \(R = 11-13\ \mathrm{km}\) for a \(1.4 M_\odot\) neutron star.
* For the parameter set \((g_V, H)/G_s = (0.8, 1.5)\), the radius of a \(1.4 M_\odot\) neutron star is \(11.3-11.5\ \mathrm{km}\) and the maximum mass satisfies the two-solar-mass constraint; the set \((0.5, 1.4)\) is slightly below \(2 M_\odot\), while \((1.0, 1.6)\) slightly violates the causality bound.
* In the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase at \(n_B \gtrsim 5 n_0\), the diquark gaps are \(\sim 200\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and the typical excitation energies of Nambu-Goldstone modes are \(\sim 50-200\ \mathrm{MeV}\), except for \(K_+\) and \(K_0\) modes which are anomalously light.

## Context

This work extends the hadron-quark continuity picture developed by Masuda et al. (2013) and Kojo et al. (2014) by incorporating recent neutron star constraints from two-solar-mass discoveries and radius estimates from X-ray analyses (Ozel 2016, Steiner 2016). It contrasts with approaches that assume strong first-order phase transitions, which are disfavored by the causality constraint when connecting soft low-density to stiff high-density equations of state (Alford 2013). The finite-temperature extension aims to provide equations of state for neutron star merger simulations, where current standards are the hadronic Lattimer-Swetsy (1991) and Shen (1998) equations of state, and where few finite-temperature quark matter descriptions exist (Kurkela 2016, Masuda 2015). The phase shift representation for thermal contributions follows the method of Beth and Uhlenbeck (1937) and Dashen, Ma, and Bernstein (1969).
