# Modeling of heavy-flavor pair correlations in Au-Au collisions at 200 A GeV at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider

> We study the nuclear modification of angular and momentum correlations between heavy quark pairs in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. The evolution of heavy quarks inside the thermalized medium is described via a modified Langevin approach that incorporates both elastic and inelastic interactions with the medium constituents. The spacetime evolution of the fireball is obtained from a (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics simulation. The hadronization of heavy quarks is performed utilizing a hybrid model of fragmentation and coalescence. Our results show that the nuclear modification of the transverse momentum imbalance of D\bar{D} pairs reflects the total energy loss experienced by the heavy quarks and may help us probe specific regions of the medium. The angular correlation of heavy flavor pairs, especially in the low to intermediate transverse momentum regime, is sensitive to the detailed energy loss mechanism of heavy quarks inside the QGP.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the angular correlations of heavy-flavor pairs, especially at low to intermediate transverse momentum, are sensitive to the detailed energy loss mechanism (collisional versus radiative) of heavy quarks inside the quark-gluon plasma, while the transverse momentum imbalance of D-Dbar pairs reflects the total energy loss and can probe specific regions of the medium. This sensitivity is conditional on the transverse momentum cuts applied, with differences between energy loss mechanisms diminishing at higher transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors simulate the evolution of heavy quark pairs in Au-Au collisions at 200 A GeV using a modified Langevin approach that includes both elastic (collisional) and inelastic (radiative) interactions with the medium. The spacetime evolution of the fireball is obtained from a (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulation, and heavy quark hadronization is performed using a hybrid model of fragmentation and coalescence. The initial heavy quark pair production is simulated with Pythia 8, and the transport coefficient is calibrated by comparing D meson R_AA to experimental data from RHIC and LHC.

## Key numbers

- The spatial diffusion coefficient D = 5/(2 pi T) provides the best description of D meson R_AA when both collisional and radiative energy loss are included; when only collisional or only radiative energy loss is used, D = 1.5/(2 pi T) or D = 3/(2 pi T) respectively are needed to describe the data.
- The critical temperature T_c for the QGP to hadron gas transition is set to 165 MeV.
- The initial time for hydrodynamic evolution is tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c.
- In central Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV, there are on average around 10 pairs of c-cbar produced per event.
- The average x_T (ratio of subleading to leading meson p_T) changes by about 6% when moving from proton-proton collisions to central Au-Au collisions.
- The number of D-Dbar pairs per triggered event drops below 1 when centrality exceeds about 50% for a 2 GeV p_T cut.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous heavy flavor transport model (Cao 2011, Cao 2012, Cao 2013, Cao 2015) to study pair correlations rather than single inclusive observables. It builds on earlier studies of heavy flavor angular correlations by Zhu (2006, 2007), Gossiaux (2006), Akamatsu (2009), Younus (2013), and Nahrgang (2013), and on momentum imbalance studies by Gossiaux (2009) and Uphoff (2013). The results enable future experimental measurements at RHIC and LHC to distinguish between collisional and radiative energy loss mechanisms, and to use momentum imbalance as a probe of the path-length dependence of parton energy loss in different regions of the QGP fireball.
