# Nuclear time-reversal violation and the Schiff moment of 225Ra

> We present a comprehensive mean-field calculation of the Schiff moment of the nucleus 225Ra, the quantity which determines the static electric dipole moment of the corresponding atom if time-reversal (T) invariance is violated in the nucleus. The calculation breaks all possible intrinsic symmetries of the nuclear mean field and includes, in particular, both exchange and direct terms from the full finite-range T-violating nucleon-nucleon interaction, and the effects of short-range correlations. The resulting Schiff moment, which depends on three unknown T-violating pion-nucleon coupling constants, is much larger than in 199Hg, the isotope with the best current experimental limit on its atomic electric-dipole moment.

## Key claim

The Schiff moment of 225Ra, calculated in a mean-field framework that breaks all intrinsic symmetries and includes the full finite-range T-violating nucleon-nucleon interaction, is much larger than that of 199Hg, but the result depends on three unknown T-violating pion-nucleon coupling constants and carries an estimated uncertainty of a factor of two or three.

## Method

The authors used the code HFODD (v2.14e) to solve self-consistent mean-field equations with zero-range Skyrme interactions, then evaluated matrix elements of the finite-range T-violating pion-mediated nucleon-nucleon interaction by fitting the interaction (including short-range correlations) to a sum of four Gaussians separable in Cartesian coordinates. The calculation included both direct and exchange terms, allowed breaking of Kramers degeneracy in the intrinsic frame, and used the SkO' Skyrme interaction as the primary functional, with SIII, SkM*, and SLy4 for comparison.

## Key numbers

- The coefficients for 225Ra with SkO' (finite-range, direct + exchange, with short-range correlations) are: a0 = -1.5 e fm^3, a1 = 6.0 e fm^3, a2 = -4.0 e fm^3.
- The zero-range approximation (direct only) gave coefficients a0 = -5.1 e fm^3, a1 = 10.4 e fm^3, a2 = -10.1 e fm^3, showing finite-range effects reduce the direct matrix elements significantly.
- For 199Hg, a recent RPA calculation gave a0 = 0.0004 e fm^3, a1 = 0.055 e fm^3, a2 = 0.009 e fm^3, making the 225Ra coefficients more than two orders of magnitude larger.
- The local dipole moment in 225Ra is 82% of the Schiff moment for the s1/2-p1/2 atomic transition and 87% for the s1/2-p3/2 transition.
- The negative-parity partner state in 225Ra lies 55 keV above the ground state.

## Context

This work extends earlier estimates of Schiff moments in octupole-deformed nuclei by Spevak et al. (1997) and the authors' own preliminary calculation (Engel 2003), which used a zero-range approximation for the T-violating interaction. The calculation improves on RPA-based work in 199Hg by Dmitriev et al. (2003, 2004) by including octupole deformation effects and a more complete treatment of the finite-range interaction. The results enable quantitative comparison with the existing 199Hg experimental limit and support the case for a planned 225Ra EDM experiment.
