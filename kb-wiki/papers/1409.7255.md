# A general analytical solution for the variance-to-mean Feynman-alpha formulas for a two-group two-point, a two-group one-point and a one-group two-point cases (submitted to The European Physical Journal Plus)

> This paper presents a full derivation of the variance-to-mean or Feynman-alpha formula in a two energy group and two spatial region-treatment. The derivation is based on the Chapman - Kolmogorov equation with the inclusion of all possible neutron reactions and passage intensities between the two regions. In addition, the two-group one-region and the two-region one-group Feynman-alpha formulas, treated earlier in the literature for special cases, are extended for further types and positions of detectors.We focus on the possibility of using these theories for accelerator-driven systems and applications in the safeguards domain, such as the differential self-interrogation method and the differential die-away method. This is due to the fact that the predictions from the models which are currently used do not fully describe all the effects in the heavily reflected fast or thermal systems. Therefore, in conclusion a comparative study of the two-group two-region, the two-group one-region, the one-group two-region and the one-group one-region Feynman-alpha models is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives fully general analytical Feynman-alpha (variance-to-mean) formulas for two-group two-region, two-group one-region, and one-group two-region neutron transport models using the Chapman-Kolmogorov forward approach, but explicitly states that no analysis of the diagnostic value of the obtained formulas is carried out and that extraction of system parameters from the decay constants is deferred to later work.

## Method

The authors used the Kolmogorov forward (master equation) approach to derive generating functions for the joint probability distributions of neutron populations and detector counts in two energy groups (fast and thermal) and/or two spatial regions (A and B). For the two-group two-point case, they solved a system of ten coupled differential equations for modified second factorial moments in the stationary state, obtaining a fourth-order characteristic equation whose four roots (omega_1 through omega_4) determine the time dependence of the Feynman Y-function. The two-group one-point and one-group two-point cases were treated as special cases of the general formalism, with delayed neutron precursors included in both reduced-dimensionality models.

## Key numbers

- The Feynman Y-function for the two-point two-group case takes the form: Y(t) = sum_{i=1}^{4} Y_i (1 - (1 - e^{-omega_i t})/(omega_i t)), with four decay constants omega_1, omega_2, omega_3, omega_4 obtained from the characteristic equation omega^4 + a*omega^3 + b*omega^2 + c*omega + d = 0
- The coefficient d in the characteristic equation equals the product omega_1 * omega_2 * omega_3 * omega_4, as shown in the expression for d
- The asymptotic Y-value for fast detection in region A is given by Y_0 = Y_1 + Y_2 + Y_3 + Y_4 = (2 K_0 lambda_{A1d})/(omega_1 omega_2 omega_3 omega_4 bar{N}_{A1})
- Stationary neutron populations bar{N}_{A1}, bar{N}_{A2}, bar{N}_{B1}, bar{N}_{B2} are expressed as rational functions of the source intensities S_A and S_B, the first factorial moments of source emission r_A' and r_B', and all reaction and transition intensities

## Context

This work extends the earlier two-group Feynman-alpha theory of Anderson (2012) and Pal (2012), which neglected fast fission and thermal detections, and the one-group two-region formulas of Anderson (2012) which neglected delayed neutrons and allowed detections in only one region. The paper also addresses the need identified by Yamamoto (2013) that variance-to-mean ratios in fast systems may be contaminated by energy-higher order mode effects. The derived formulas are intended for application to accelerator-driven systems and nuclear safeguards techniques such as the differential die-away self-interrogation (DDSI) method of Menlove, and for assaying plutonium isotopes as suggested by Chapline (2011).
