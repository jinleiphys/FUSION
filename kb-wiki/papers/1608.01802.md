# Three-body calculation of the $1s$ level shift in kaonic deuterium with realistic $\bar{K}N$ potentials

> The $1s$ level shift in kaonic deuterium was calculated using Coulomb Sturmian expansion of Faddeev equations. The convergence of the method yields an $\sim\ 1\ eV$ accuracy for the level shifts. We used three different, realistic, multichannel $\bar{K}N$ interactions reproducing all known experimental two-body $\bar{K}N$ data. The different results suggest, that the level shift should be in the range $ΔE\sim(800\pm30)-(480\pm20){\rm i}\ \ eV$. The (almost) exact level shifts were compared with values, given by the commonly used approximations.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first exact three-body calculation of the 1s level shift in kaonic deuterium using realistic multichannel antikaon-nucleon interactions, and suggests that the level shift should be in the range (800±30) – (480±20)i eV, though the abstract's hedge is that different realistic potentials, all equally good at describing two-body data, give somewhat different results, with the chiral value differing significantly from the two phenomenological ones.

## Method

The author solved homogeneous Faddeev equations in the Noble form, expanded on a Coulomb Sturmian basis, for the kaonic deuterium three-body system (K⁻, p, n). The strong interactions were treated using three different realistic separable multichannel antikaon-nucleon potentials (SIDD1, SIDD2, and Chiral) that reproduce all known experimental two-body K⁻N data, with the coupling to πΣ and πΛ channels incorporated via exact optical potentials. Two different np potentials (a simple separable and a more realistic attractive-plus-repulsive separable) were used to model the deuteron structure, and the complex energy eigenvalues were found by solving Det(I - A(E)) = 0 near the unperturbed Coulomb-plus-deuteron energy.

## Key numbers

- The converged level shift for the SIDD1 potential with the realistic np potential is ΔE = 767 – 464i eV (basis size n_max = 44, total basis 8100).
- The converged level shift for the SIDD2 potential with the realistic np potential is ΔE = 782 – 469i eV (basis size n_max = 44).
- The converged level shift for the Chiral potential with the realistic np potential is ΔE = 835 – 502i eV (basis size n_max = 44).
- The overall suggested range from all three potentials is ΔE ∼ (800 ± 30) – (480 ± 20)i eV.
- The corrected Deser formula gave values of 831 – 367i eV (SIDD1), 840 – 364i eV (SIDD2), and 881 – 363i eV (Chiral), showing poor agreement with the exact three-body results, especially for the imaginary part.
- The "best" two-body optical potential approximation gave 785 – 509i eV (SIDD1), 797 – 512i eV (SIDD2), and 828 – 527i eV (Chiral), in reasonable agreement with the exact three-body results.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous test calculation (Révai, 2016) that used simple one-channel absorptive potentials, and the earlier Coulombless Faddeev calculations for the antikaon-deuteron system (Révai, 2012) by including the Coulomb interaction exactly. The method, originally proposed by Papp (1997) for simultaneous treatment of short-range and Coulomb forces in three-body systems, is here applied for the first time to a hadronic atom with realistic multichannel interactions. The results enable a direct comparison with the upcoming SIDDHARTA 2 experiment and provide a tool to constrain the uncertain properties of the basic antikaon-nucleon interaction without relying on the commonly used corrected Deser formula, which the paper shows to be unreliable for the imaginary part of the level shift.
