# Exploring the impact of $Δ$-isobars on Neutron Star

> We include the $Δ$-isobars in the equation of state (EOS) of neutron star (NS) and study its effects with various parameter sets of the RMF model. We compare our results with the NS's constraints from the mass-radius measurement of PSR J0348+0432, PSR J1614-2230, PSR J0030+0451, PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0952-0607, and tidal deformability of GW170817. We calculate the mass-radius profile and tidal deformabilities of the NS using 21 parameter sets of the RMF model.Analyzing the result with various parameters, it is clear that only few parameter sets can satisfy simultaneously the constraints from NICER and GW170817. NLD parameter set satisfy all the constraints of NICER and GW170817. For its strong predictive power for the bulk properties of the neutron star, we take NLD parameter set as a representative for the detailed calculation of effect of $Δ$-isobar on neutron star properties. We demonstrate that it is possible that $Δ$-isobar can produce at 2-3 times the saturation density by adjusting the coupling constants $X_{σΔ}$, $X_{ρΔ}$ and $X_{ωΔ}$ in an appropriate range. Bulk properties of the NS like mass-radius profile and tidal deformability is strongly affected by the interaction strength of $Δ$-isobar. Our calculation shows that it is also possible that by choosing $X_{σΔ}$, $X_{ρΔ}$ and $X_{ωΔ}$ to a suitable range the threshold density of $Δ^-$-isobar become lower than $Λ^0$ hyperon. For a particular value of $Δ$-coupling constants, the $R_{1.4}$ decrease by 1.7 km. This manuscipt give an argumentative justification for allowing $Δ$-isobar degrees of freedom in the calculation of the NS properties.

# Exploring the impact of Δ-isobars on Neutron Star

## Key claim

The inclusion of Δ-isobars in neutron star equation of state calculations can produce these resonances at 2-3 times saturation density, and with appropriate tuning of the coupling constants X_{σΔ}, X_{ρΔ}, and X_{ωΔ}, the threshold density for Δ^- isobars can become lower than that of Λ^0 hyperons, but only a few of the 21 RMF parameter sets tested simultaneously satisfy all constraints from NICER and GW170817 observations.

## Method

The authors employed 21 parameter sets of the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model to compute the equation of state of neutron star matter including Δ-isobar degrees of freedom. They calculated mass-radius profiles and tidal deformabilities for each parameter set and compared these against observational constraints from pulsar mass-radius measurements (PSR J0348+0432, PSR J1614-2230, PSR J0030+0451, PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0952-0607) and the tidal deformability from GW170817. The NLD parameter set was selected as representative for detailed analysis of Δ-isobar effects due to its strong predictive power for bulk neutron star properties.

## Key numbers

- For a particular value of Δ-coupling constants, the radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star (R_{1.4}) decreases by 1.7 km.
- Δ-isobars can be produced at 2-3 times the nuclear saturation density when coupling constants are adjusted in an appropriate range.
- The study used 21 parameter sets of the RMF model to evaluate neutron star properties against observational constraints.

## Context

This work extends previous RMF model studies of neutron star structure by incorporating Δ-isobar degrees of freedom alongside the more commonly considered hyperons, addressing a gap in the treatment of baryonic resonances in dense matter. The finding that Δ^- isobars can have a lower threshold density than Λ^0 hyperons for suitable coupling choices challenges the conventional priority given to hyperonic degrees of freedom in neutron star cores. The results enable more complete comparisons between theoretical neutron star models and the growing observational constraints from NICER (PSR J0030+0451, PSR J0740+6620) and gravitational wave (GW170817) measurements, while identifying which RMF parameter sets remain viable under multi-messenger constraints.
