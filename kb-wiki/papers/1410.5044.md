# Jet-dilepton conversion in expanding quark-gluon plasma

> We calculate the production of large mass dileptons from the jet-dilepton conversion in the expanding quark-gluon plasma at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energies. The jet-dilepton conversion exceeds the thermal dilepton production and Drell-Yan process in the large mass region of 3.9 GeV$<M<$5.8 GeV and 6.3 GeV$<M<$8.7 GeV in central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.76 TeV and 5.5 TeV, respectively. We present the numerical solution of ideal fluid hydrodynamics. We find that the transverse flow leads to a rapid cooling of the fire ball. The suppression due to transverse flow is appeared from small to large mass, the transverse flow effect becomes important at LHC energies. The energy loss of jets in the hot and dense medium is also included.

## Key claim

The jet-dilepton conversion mechanism becomes the dominant source of large-mass dileptons in specific invariant-mass windows at LHC energies (3.9 GeV < M < 5.8 GeV at √sNN = 2.76 TeV and 6.3 GeV < M < 8.7 GeV at √sNN = 5.5 TeV), but only after the background from heavy-quark decays is subtracted; the effect is not prominent at RHIC energy.

## Method

The authors solved the ideal relativistic hydrodynamic equations with cylindrical symmetry and boost-invariant longitudinal expansion using a first-order Lax finite-difference scheme, obtaining the temperature T(τ,r) and radial velocity vr(τ,r) as functions of proper time and radial distance. They then computed the dilepton yield from jet-dilepton conversion by integrating the relativistic kinetic-theory rate over the space-time history of the expanding QGP, using the WHS and BDMPS formalisms to model jet energy loss via gluon bremsstrahlung. The initial jet production was calculated from leading-order perturbative QCD with nuclear parton distribution functions and K-factors for next-to-leading-order corrections.

## Key numbers

- Initial conditions for hydrodynamics: Ti = 370 MeV, τi = 0.26 fm/c for RHIC (Au+Au, √sNN = 200 GeV); Ti = 636 MeV, τi = 0.088 fm/c for LHC (Pb+Pb, √sNN = 2.76 TeV); Ti = 845 MeV, τi = 0.087 fm/c for LHC (Pb+Pb, √sNN = 5.5 TeV)
- QGP lifetime with transverse flow: Δτ = 2.56 fm/c at RHIC; Δτ = 4.352 fm/c at LHC (Ti = 636 MeV); Δτ = 8.233 fm/c at LHC (Ti = 845 MeV)
- Average jet propagation distance before conversion: ⟨l⟩ = 1.28 fm at RHIC; ⟨l⟩ = 2.18 fm at LHC (Ti = 636 MeV); ⟨l⟩ = 4.12 fm at LHC (Ti = 845 MeV) (with transverse flow)
- Jet-dilepton conversion dominates over thermal and Drell-Yan in the windows 3.9 GeV < M < 5.8 GeV at √sNN = 2.76 TeV and 6.3 GeV < M < 8.7 GeV at √sNN = 5.5 TeV
- Energy-loss suppression of jet-dilepton yield: about 20% at M = 1 GeV and 16% at M = 4 GeV at RHIC; about 21% at M = 4 GeV and 20% at M = 6 GeV at LHC (2.76 TeV); about 26% at M = 6 GeV and 23% at M = 9 GeV at LHC (5.5 TeV)
- Transverse flow suppresses thermal dileptons by a factor ~2 at M ~ 0.5 GeV for Ti = 845 MeV; suppresses jet-dilepton yield by a factor ~1.5 at M ~ 6 GeV for Ti = 845 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of jet-photon conversion by Turbide et al. and jet-dilepton conversion in 1+1 dimensional Bjorken expansion by Refs. [jet-dilepton5, AMY1, jet-dilepton2] to include both transverse flow from ideal hydrodynamics and jet energy loss via the WHS and BDMPS formalisms. The results show that transverse flow reduces the QGP lifetime and suppresses dilepton yields across all masses, while the jet-dilepton conversion provides a potentially observable signal at LHC in mass windows where it exceeds both thermal dilepton production and the Drell-Yan process. The authors note that the main background from open charm and bottom meson decays must be subtracted, citing studies by the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics approach (CEL3) that find such backgrounds are lower than thermal dileptons at intermediate and high invariant mass at LHC.
