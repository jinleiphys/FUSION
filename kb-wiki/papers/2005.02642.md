# Description of the newly observed $Ξ_c^{0}$ states as molecular states

> Very recently, three new structures $Ξ_c(2923)^0$, $Ξ(2938)^0$, and $Ξ(2964)^0$ at the invariant mass spectrum of $Λ_c^{+}K^{-}$ observed by the LHCb Collaboration triggers a hot discussion about their inner structure. In this work, we study the strong decay mode of the newly observed $Ξ_c$ assuming that the $Ξ_c$ is a $\bar{D}Λ-\bar{D}Σ$ molecular state. With the possible quantum numbers $J^p=1/2^{\pm}$ and $3/2^{\pm}$, the partial decay widths of the $\bar{D}Λ-\bar{D}Σ$ molecular state into the $Λ_c^{+}K^{-}$, $Σ_c^{+}K^{-}$,and $Ξ_c^{+}π^{-}$ final states through hadronic loop are calculated with the help of the effective Lagrangians. By comparison with the LHCb observation, the current results of total decay width support the $Ξ(2923)^0$ as $\bar{D}Λ-\bar{D}Σ$ molecule while the decay width of the $Ξ_c(2938)^0$ and $Ξ(2964)^0$ can not be well reproduced in the molecular state picture. In addition, the calculated partial decay widths with $S$ wave $\bar{D}Λ-\bar{D}Σ$ molecular state picture indicate that allowed decay modes, $Ξ_c^{+}π^{-}$, may have the biggest branching ratios for the $Ξ_c(2923)$. The experimental measurements for this strong decay process could be a crucial test for the molecule interpretation of the $Ξ_c(2923)$.

## Key claim
The authors propose that only the Ξ_c(2923)^0 resonance can be described as a D̅Λ–D̅Σ molecular state with quantum numbers J^P = 1/2^−, while the Ξ_c(2938)^0 and Ξ_c(2964)^0 cannot be reproduced in the molecular state picture. The claim is conditional on the assumption that the states are S-wave D̅Λ–D̅Σ molecules and uses the compositeness condition Z=0 to fix coupling constants.

## Method
The authors compute partial decay widths of the Ξ_c* states into Λ_c^+K^−, Σ_c^+K^−, and Ξ_c^+π^− final states via hadronic loop diagrams, using effective Lagrangians with SU(4) symmetry and hidden gauge formalism. The coupling constants of the molecular state to its constituents (D meson and Λ or Σ baryon) are determined by the compositeness condition Z=0, which sets the renormalization constant of the hadron wave function to zero. The calculation is performed for four possible spin-parity assignments (J^P = 1/2^± and 3/2^±) with a Gaussian correlation function (size parameter α = 1.0 GeV) to regulate ultraviolet divergences.

## Key numbers
- Ξ_c(2923)^0: mass M = 2923.04 ± 0.25(stat) ± 0.20(syst) ± 0.14(Λ_c^+) MeV, total width Γ = 7.1 ± 0.8(stat) ± 1.8(syst) MeV (LHCb measurement)
- Ξ_c(2938)^0: mass M = 2938.55 ± 0.21(stat) ± 0.17(syst) ± 0.14(Λ_c^+) MeV, total width Γ = 10.2 ± 0.8(stat) ± 1.1(syst) MeV (LHCb measurement)
- Ξ_c(2964)^0: mass M = 2964.88 ± 0.26(stat) ± 0.14(syst) ± 0.14(Λ_c^+) MeV, total width Γ = 14.1 ± 0.9(stat) ± 1.3(syst) MeV (LHCb measurement)
- Coupling constant g_h = 4.64, determined from the K*+ → Kπ decay width (Γ_K*+ = 50.3 ± 0.8 MeV)
- Coupling constants g_Λ_cpD = 10.7 (central value) and g_Σ_cpD = −2.69, borrowed from prior work
- For the Ξ_c(2923) with J^P = 1/2^−, the Ξ_c^+π^− decay channel almost saturates the total width, while Λ_c^+K^− gives minor contributions

## Context
This work extends the molecular interpretation of heavy baryons, following studies by JimenezTejero (2009), Yu (2018), and Nieves (2019) who showed that D meson–Λ/Σ interactions can form bound states near 2930 MeV. It contrasts with conventional three-quark interpretations by Yang (2020), Wang (2020), and Lu (2020), who assigned these states as P-wave or 2S Ξ_c' baryons in the quark model. The results enable experimental tests: measuring the Ξ_c(2923)^0 → Ξ_c^+π^− branching ratio could distinguish between the molecular and quark-model pictures. The lattice QCD calculation by Bahtiyar (2020) is also noted as attempting to determine quantum numbers.
