# Neutron halos in heavy nuclei -- relativistic mean field approach

> Assuming a~simple spherical relativistic mean field model of the nucleus, we estimate the width of the antiproton--neutron annihilation ($Γ_n$) and the width of antiproton--proton ($Γ_p$) annihilation, in an antiprotonic atom system. This allows us to determine the halo factor $f$, which is then discussed in the context of experimental data obtained in measurements recently done on LEAR utility at CERN. Another quantity which characterizes the deviation of the average nuclear densities ratio from the corresponding ratio of the homogeneous densities is introduced too. It was shown that it is also a good indicator of the neutron halo. The results are compared to experimental data as well as to the data of the simple liquid droplet model of the nuclear densities. The single particle structure of the nuclear density tail is discusssed also.

# Neutron halos in heavy nuclei -- relativistic mean field approach

## Key claim
Using a simple spherical relativistic mean field (RMF) model, the authors estimate that the halo factor \(f\) and the newly introduced density deviation \(h\) can indicate neutron halos in heavy nuclei, but the agreement with experimental data is good only for most nuclei except extreme cases like \(^{176}\)Yb, where the theoretical factor (3.1) is much lower than the measured value (8.1), suggesting that deformation or pairing may be needed.

## Method
The authors solve the coupled Dirac and Klein-Gordon equations iteratively within a spherical relativistic mean field model to obtain neutron and proton density distributions for nine nuclei. From these densities, they compute the antiproton absorption widths \(\Gamma_n\) and \(\Gamma_p\) for antiprotonic atomic states (summing three to five states) and derive the halo factor \(f = (Z/N) \sum \Gamma_n^s / \sum \Gamma_p^s\), assuming the pion escape probability \(P(r) \approx 1\) and a constant \(\bar pN\) interaction length. They also define an average density deviation \(h = 1 - (Z/N)(\bar\rho_n / \bar\rho_p)\) and compare both quantities to experimental data from LEAR at CERN and to liquid droplet model calculations.

## Key numbers
- For \(^{176}\)Yb, the calculated halo factor \(f_{\rm RMF} = 3.1\) versus the experimental value \(f_{\rm exp} = 8.1\).
- For \(^{144}\)Sm, the calculated halo factor \(f = 1.5\), indicating a possible proton halo rather than a neutron halo.
- For \(^{96}\)Ru, the halo factor \(f \approx 2\) while the density deviation \(h\) is negative, suggesting no neutron halo.
- The average antiproton-nucleus distance \(B \approx 30\text{--}40\) fm for the nuclei considered, based on the Bohr radius scaling with \(n=8\) and \(m_{\bar p}/m_e \sim 2000\).
- The approximate expression for the density deviation \(h \approx 3 (a_n - a_p)/R\), where \(a_n\) and \(a_p\) are the neutron and proton diffuseness parameters.

## Context
This work extends the experimental measurements of neutron halo factors from LEAR at CERN (Jastrzebski 1993, Lubinski 1994) by providing theoretical calculations using the relativistic mean field model, which is compared to the simpler liquid droplet model (Myers and Swiatecki 1974, 1983). The results show that the halo factor \(f\) and the new quantity \(h\) are sensitive indicators of neutron halos, but the discrepancies for \(^{176}\)Yb and other nuclei suggest that future work should include nuclear deformation and pairing interactions, which the authors state are in progress. The single-particle nature of the density tail is emphasized, with one neutron orbital contributing up to 90% of the density at large radii for some halo nuclei.
