# Berry phase and backbending

> Backbending is a typical phenomenon in the rotational spectra of superfluid nuclei. It is caused by the rotational alignment of a pair of nucleons and depends on topological properties of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov spectrum in the rotating frame characterized by diabolic points and Berry phases.

## Key claim

Backbending in superfluid nuclei is caused by the rotational alignment of a pair of nucleons in high-\(j\) intruder orbits, not by a collective pairing collapse; the phenomenon depends on topological properties of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov spectrum in the rotating frame, characterized by diabolic points and Berry phases, and the sharpness of the backbend is determined by the residual interaction between the ground-state band and the aligned two-quasiparticle band.

## Method

The paper reviews cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory, where the intrinsic wave function is obtained from a variation of the unprojected Hamiltonian in a system rotating with constant frequency \(\omega\), with particle number and angular momentum conserved only on average via the Inglis condition. The self-consistent solution of the rotating HFB equations yields quasiparticle energies (Routhians) as functions of \(\omega\) and the chemical potential \(\lambda\); level crossings between the ground-state band and a two-quasiparticle band are analyzed in the \((\lambda,\omega)\) plane. To treat the level crossing properly at constant angular momentum, the projected shell model (PSM) of Hara and Sun is introduced, where wave functions are projected linear combinations of a deformed ground state and two-quasiparticle states.

## Key numbers

* The pairing correlations in the aligned band are reduced by roughly 20% because of the blocking effect.
* In the Rare Earth region, the intruder orbit responsible for backbending is the neutron \(1i_{13/2}\) shell, which has magnetic quantum numbers \(K = \pm\frac{1}{2}, \pm\frac{3}{2}, \dots, \pm\frac{13}{2}\).
* The pattern of diabolic points in the \((\lambda,\omega)\) plane for the Rare Earths shows a row of 5 diabolic points at small \(\omega\) values, with further diabolic points at higher spin.
* Pair transfer matrix elements for \(^{168}\)Hf are shown as a function of \(\omega\) for several constant values of the gap parameter \(\Delta\).
* Projected shell model calculations for the Hf chain show diabolic points in \(^{166}\)Hf and \(^{172}\)Hf.

## Context

This work extends the cranked shell model (CSM) introduced by Bengtsson and Frauendorf (1979) and the self-consistent cranked HFB calculations of Banerjee, Mang and Ring (1973), which established that backbending is caused by alignment rather than a pairing collapse as predicted by Mottelson and Valatin (1960). The topological analysis builds on the discovery of diabolic points in rotating HFB spectra by Bengtsson, Hamamoto and Mottelson (1978) and on Berry's (1984) general theory of geometric phases. The paper enables a microscopic understanding of the oscillating behavior of backbending and introduces the concept of diabolic pair transfer, which predicts a sign change and strong reduction of pair transfer matrix elements near diabolic points; experimental observation of this effect remains an open challenge, as attempts by Helmer et al. (1993) have so far not succeeded in populating states beyond the diabolic region.
