# Exploring parameter dependence of heavy-flavor dynamics in small collision systems

> Observations from high-multiplicity proton-lead ($p$-Pb) collisions indicate that small systems may exhibit collective behavior in both heavy and light hadrons. This work investigates the roles of initial- and final-state interactions in shaping the nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow of $D$ mesons measured in $p$-Pb collisions. Initial-state effects, including the Cronin and shadowing effects, are considered in the heavy-quark initial conditions, while final-state interactions are simulated through Langevin evolution combined with the coalescence model of hadronization. Different initial geometries attributed to fluctuations in the medium's energy density are parametrized and translated into the momentum anisotropies of both light and heavy quarks. The corresponding $R_{pPb}$ and $v_2$ of D mesons in 8.16 TeV $p$-Pb collisions are calculated under different assumptions for the final-state interactions. Assuming that the initial-state effects only modify the transverse momentum spectra without altering the azimuthal distribution of heavy quarks, the measured $R_{pPb}$ of D mesons can be qualitatively reproduced by the combined influence of initial- and final-state effects. However, the observed $v_2$ cannot be accounted for by final-state interactions alone. These results suggest that additional contributions to azimuthal anisotropies of heavy quarks originating from initial-state effects are required to explain the experimentally observed $v_2$.

# Exploring parameter dependence of heavy-flavor dynamics in small collision systems

## Key claim

The measured elliptic flow ($v_2$) of D mesons in 8.16 TeV p-Pb collisions cannot be accounted for by final-state interactions alone, even when varying the anisotropic geometry of the medium; additional contributions to azimuthal anisotropies originating from initial-state effects are required to explain the experimentally observed $v_2$, while the nuclear modification factor $R_{pPb}$ can be qualitatively reproduced by the combined influence of initial- and final-state effects under the assumption that initial-state effects only modify transverse momentum spectra without altering azimuthal distributions.

## Method

Heavy-quark propagation in the deconfined medium formed in p-Pb collisions is simulated using the classical Langevin equation with a spatial diffusion coefficient $\mathcal{D}_s(2\pi T) = 3$, including drag, random thermal kicks, and medium-induced gluon radiation. Hadronization is implemented through the coalescence model, where charm quarks recombine with thermal light quarks (mass 0.3 GeV) to form D mesons, using a Gaussian Wigner function with mean-square radius $\langle r^2 \rangle_D = 0.43^2$ fm$^2$. Initial-state effects (Cronin effect with parameter $a_{gN} = 0.15$ GeV$^2$/fm and shadowing from EPS09 NLO) modify the initial charm-quark transverse momentum distribution from FONLL, while the medium evolution is simulated using the MUSIC hydrodynamic package with initial energy density profiles modeled as either elliptical Gaussians or multiple Gaussian hot spots with varying eccentricities.

## Key numbers

- Spatial diffusion coefficient: $\mathcal{D}_s(2\pi T) = 3$, representing a strongly coupled medium
- Cronin parameter: $a_{gN} = 0.15$ GeV$^2$/fm, adopted based on previous studies
- Mean-square radius of D mesons: $\langle r^2 \rangle_D = 0.43^2$ fm$^2$ for both $D^0$ and $D^{*0}$
- Hadronization ratios: $H_{c \rightarrow D^0} = 9.5\%$ and $H_{c \rightarrow D^{*0}} = 20\%$
- Critical temperature for hadronization: $T_c = 0.16$ GeV; light quark mass: 0.3 GeV
- Initial energy density eccentricities explored: $\epsilon_2 = 0.60$ and $\epsilon_2 = 0.88$ for elliptical Gaussian profiles; $\epsilon_2 = 0.23$ and $\epsilon_2 = 0.51$ for two-hot-spot configurations with separation distances $a_y = 1$ fm and $2$ fm, respectively
- Gaussian width of each hot spot in multi-hot-spot configuration: $\sigma = 1$ fm
- Final-state interactions alone contribute approximately 3% to charm quark $v_2$ prior to hadronization, accounting for roughly one-third of the experimentally measured D meson $v_2$

## Context

This work extends studies of heavy-flavor collective flow in small collision systems, building on the AMPT model approach of Zhang (2024) which attributes both $R_{pPb}$ and $v_2$ to a combination of the Cronin effect and final-state interactions, and the Color Glass Condensate model of Zhang (2019, 2020) which attributes significant $v_2$ to initial-state effects generating azimuthal correlations. The study also draws on the proposal by Soudi (2023) that initial transverse momentum distributions of partons inside unpolarized nucleons can lead to observed azimuthal anisotropy without modifying angle-integrated spectra. The results indicate that future work must incorporate event-by-event fluctuating hydrodynamics and a more complete treatment of initial-state effects to explain the experimental D meson data, particularly the large $v_2$ values that cannot be reproduced by final-state interactions alone.
