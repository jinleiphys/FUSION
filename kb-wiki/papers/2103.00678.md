# Role of unitary correlation operator on high-momentum antisymmetrized molecular dynamics using bare NN interaction for 3H and 4He

> We extend the high-momentum antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (HMAMD) by incorporating the short-range part of the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) as the variational method of finite nuclei. In this HMAMD+UCOM calculation of light nuclei, the HMAMD is mainly in charge of the tensor correlation with up to the four-body correlation, while the short-range correlation is further improved by using the UCOM. The binding energies of the 3H and 4He nuclei are calculated with this HMAMD+UCOM using the AV8' bare nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction. The different roles of the short-range and tensor correlations from the HMAMD and UCOM are analyzed in the numerical results. Compared with the previous calculations based on the different variational methods, this newly extended HMAMD+UCOM method can almost provide the consistent results with the ab initio results.

# Role of unitary correlation operator on high-momentum antisymmetrized molecular dynamics using bare NN interaction for 3H and 4He

## Key claim

The HMAMD+UCOM method, which combines high-momentum antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (HMAMD) with the short-range part of the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM), can produce binding energies for 3H and 4He that are consistent with ab initio results, but only when the double HMAMD basis (two high-momentum pairs) is included alongside the UCOM transformation. The UCOM is more effective for the compact 4He nucleus than for the less compact 3H nucleus because short-range correlation effects are stronger in denser systems.

## Method

The authors start from the AV8' bare nucleon-nucleon interaction and construct HMAMD wave functions by introducing imaginary momentum components to Gaussian centroids of nucleon pairs, creating single and double high-momentum pair excitations. They then apply only the short-range part of the UCOM transformation (C_r) to the HMAMD wave functions, transforming the Hamiltonian into an effective one with up to two-body operators, and perform variational optimization of the UCOM parameters (alpha, beta, gamma) in the R_+(r) correlation functions for each spin-isospin channel. The total wave function is a superposition of all basis states with grid-sampled momentum shifts D, with coefficients determined by diagonalization of the Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- For 3H with double HMAMD+UCOM: total energy -7.08 MeV (kinetic 48.74 MeV, central -23.50 MeV, tensor -30.42 MeV, spin-orbit -1.91 MeV), radius 1.60 fm
- For 4He with double HMAMD+UCOM: total energy -23.17 MeV (kinetic 98.55 MeV, central -57.34 MeV, tensor -61.02 MeV, spin-orbit -3.36 MeV), radius 1.46 fm
- For 3H with double HMAMD without UCOM: total energy -6.66 MeV (kinetic 47.20 MeV, central -22.25 MeV, tensor -29.58 MeV, spin-orbit -2.03 MeV), radius 1.61 fm
- For 4He with double HMAMD without UCOM: total energy -21.01 MeV (kinetic 89.86 MeV, central -51.08 MeV, tensor -57.07 MeV, spin-orbit -2.73 MeV), radius 1.49 fm
- GFMC reference values: -7.76 MeV for 3H and -25.93 MeV for 4He (Kamada 2001, Wiringa 2002)
- Momentum grid for 3H: D up to 19.5 fm with 1.3 fm spacing; for 4He: D up to 13 fm with 1 fm spacing
- Size parameters nu: 0.18 fm^{-2} for 3H and 0.25 fm^{-2} for 4He

## Context

This work extends the HMAMD framework (Myo 2017, Zhao 2019) by incorporating the short-range UCOM transformation previously applied to the tensor-optimized shell model (TOSM+UCOM, Myo 2009) and to nuclear matter (Myo 2019). The results are compared with TOSM+UCOM (which gave -6.02 MeV for 3H and -19.46 MeV for 4He), TOSM+S-UCOM (-22.30 MeV for 4He), and tensor-optimized antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (TOAMD, Myo 2017, which gave -7.68 MeV for 3H and -24.74 MeV for 4He). The HMAMD+UCOM results are competitive with these methods but still fall short of the GFMC benchmark, suggesting that further improvement could come from using S-UCOM (partial-wave dependent UCOM) or adding triple high-momentum pairs.
