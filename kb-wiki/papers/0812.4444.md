# Three-Nucleon Low-Energy Constants from the Consistency of Interactions and Currents in Chiral Effective Field Theory

> The chiral low-energy constants c_D and c_E are constrained by means of accurate ab initio calculations of the A=3 binding energies and, for the first time, of the triton beta decay. We demonstrate that these low-energy observables allow a robust determination of the two undetermined constants. The consistency of the interactions and currents in chiral effective field theory is crucial to this remarkable result. The two- plus three-nucleon interactions from chiral effective field theory defined by properties of the A=2 system and the present determination of c_D and c_E are successful in predicting properties of the A=3, and 4 systems.

## Key claim

The chiral low-energy constants \(c_D\) and \(c_E\) can be robustly determined by combining the A=3 binding energies with the triton beta-decay half-life, because the determination of \(c_D\) depends only weakly on short-range correlations in the wave functions and on the three-nucleon force itself.

## Method

The authors performed no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations of the \(^3\)H and \(^3\)He ground states using the N\(^3\)LO nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential of Entem and Machleidt (2003) and the local N\(^2\)LO three-nucleon (NNN) interaction. They computed the reduced matrix element of the axial-vector current \(E_1^A\) at N\(^3\)LO, including two-body meson-exchange currents (MEC), and varied \(c_D\) and \(c_E\) along the trajectory that reproduces the A=3 binding energies until the calculated \(\langle E_1^A \rangle\) matched the empirical value extracted from the triton comparative half-life.

## Key numbers

- Triton comparative half-life: \((fT_{1/2})_t = (1129.6 \pm 3)\) s.
- Empirical reduced matrix element of the axial current: \(\langle E_1^A \rangle|_{\text{emp}} = 0.6848 \pm 0.0011\).
- Constrained range for \(c_D\): \(-0.3 \leq c_D \leq -0.1\).
- Corresponding range for \(c_E\): \(-0.220 \leq c_E \leq -0.189\).
- With \(c_D = -0.2\) and \(c_E = -0.205\), the N\(^3\)LO NN + N\(^2\)LO NNN calculation yields: \(^3\)H ground-state energy = \(-8.473(4)\) MeV (experiment: \(-8.482\) MeV); \(^3\)He ground-state energy = \(-7.727(4)\) MeV (experiment: \(-7.718\) MeV); \(^4\)He ground-state energy = \(-28.50(2)\) MeV (experiment: \(-28.296\) MeV).
- With the same constants, the calculated point-proton radius of \(^4\)He is 1.461(2) fm (experiment: 1.467(13) fm).

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to determine \(c_D\) and \(c_E\) from the triton binding energy combined with the nd doublet scattering length (the Phillips line) or the \(^4\)He binding energy (the Tjon line), which suffered from large uncertainties due to correlations. It also builds on the suggestion by Gardestig and Phillips (2006) to use triton beta-decay as an electroweak constraint. The resulting chiral Lagrangian predicts, without further free parameters, the properties of A=3 and A=4 systems, and the method provides a path toward parameter-free predictions of quantum chromodynamics in chiral effective field theory once the NN interaction is fully determined.
