# Thomas-Fermi approximation to pairing in finite Fermi systems. The weak coupling regime

> We present a new semiclassical theory for describing pairing in finite Fermi systems. It is based on taking the $\hbar \to 0$, i.e. Thomas-Fermi, limit of the gap equation written in the basis of the mean field (weak coupling). In addition to the position dependence of the Fermi momentum, the size dependence of the pairing force is also taken into account in this theory. Along isotopic chains the Thomas-Fermi gaps average the well known arch structure shown by the quantal gaps. This structure can be almost recovered in our formalism if some shell fluctuations are included in the level density. We point out that at the drip line nuclear pairing is strongly reduced. This fact is illustrated with the behavior of the gap in the inner crust of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The authors present a new semiclassical Thomas-Fermi (TF) theory for pairing in finite Fermi systems that improves upon the Local Density Approximation (LDA) by keeping the size dependence of the pairing force matrix elements, and they claim this TF theory works in the weak coupling regime where \(\Delta/\mu << 1\) and even where \(\Delta < \hbar\omega\), a region where LDA generally fails.

## Method

The authors take the \(\hbar \to 0\) (Thomas-Fermi) limit of the BCS gap equation written in the basis of the normal mean field, replacing the quantal density matrix with its semiclassical Wigner transform. The resulting TF gap equation uses the classical Hamiltonian (with position-dependent effective mass and mean field) to compute the level density and pairing matrix elements, which are then solved self-consistently with the particle number condition. For realistic applications, the mean field and effective mass are obtained from Extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) theory using the Gogny D1S force.

## Key numbers

- For the tin isotopic chain from \(^{100}\)Sn to \(^{132}\)Sn, the TF gaps decrease smoothly with increasing neutron number, washing out the quantal arch structure.
- In the inner crust of neutron stars, the ground state energy per baryon was computed for average densities in Wigner-Seitz cells ranging from \(2.79 \times 10^{-4}\) to \(7.89 \times 10^{-2}\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The homogeneous phase in the inner crust is reached at an average density of about 0.08 fm\(^{-3}\) (corresponding to \(^{982}\)Ge\(_{32}\)).
- For the Woods-Saxon potential in a box, the TF gap vanishes just at the drip line when the chemical potential equals the depth of the single-particle potential, for box radii of \(R = 25\), 50, and 100 fm.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical approach to pairing beyond the standard Local Density Approximation (LDA) developed by Schuck and collaborators (Kucharek, 1989), which requires the coherence length to be smaller than the oscillator length. The new TF theory avoids this LDA condition and can describe the average behavior of pairing gaps where LDA fails, particularly in the weak coupling regime. The authors demonstrate that the quenching of the pairing gap near the drip line, previously studied by Hamamoto (2005) for weakly bound neutron levels, is a general feature of superfluid fermions in finite confining potentials approaching overflow, with applications to the inner crust of neutron stars as described by Negele and Vautherin (1973).
