# $πΞ$ Correlations: an Interweaving of Resonance Interaction, Channel Coupling and Coulomb Effects

> A method is presented for analysis of correlation function of two non-identical particles with strong and Coulomb interactions, resonance formation, channel coupling and spin structure. For resonance reactions we derive a formula giving the small distance contribution to the correlation function. The formalism is used to analyze the preliminary RHIC data on $π^{\pm} Ξ^{\mp}$ correlation measurements. The $Ξ^{*}(1530)$ resonance is successfully described. The $πΞ$ source size is obtained.

## Key claim

The paper presents a theoretical framework for analyzing πΞ correlation functions that successfully describes the Ξ*(1530) resonance peak and extracts the source size, but the authors acknowledge that the simple Gaussian source model used is insufficient to simultaneously describe source sizes and emission asymmetries, and that more elaborated source models are needed.

## Method

The authors construct the two-particle wave function for πΞ pairs including Coulomb and strong interactions, resonance formation, spin-flip amplitudes, isospin structure with channel coupling between π⁺Ξ⁻ and π⁰Ξ⁰, and the contribution from the inner potential region (r < ε ≈ 1 fm) using the Lüders-Wigner formula. The correlation function is computed by integrating the squared wave function with a Gaussian source function, and the formalism is applied to fit preliminary STAR collaboration data from 10% most central Au+Au collisions at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- Bohr radius for π⁺Ξ⁻: a_B = 214 fm (reduced mass μ = 126 MeV)
- Ξ*(1530) resonance mass: M* = 1530 MeV, width Γ = 9.1 MeV
- Resonance c.m. momentum: k = 150 MeV, with η = 1/(k a_B) ≈ 1/160
- Preliminary STAR results: source radius R = (6.7 ± 1.0) fm, shift parameter Δ_out = (-5.6 ± 1.0) fm
- Threshold difference between π⁺Ξ⁻ and π⁰Ξ⁰ channels: ≈ 11 MeV
- Gaussian source radius used in successful fit: R = 7.0 fm

## Context

This work extends the femtoscopy formalism for non-identical particles developed by Pratt and Petriconi, and by Lednicky and Lyuboshitz (1998), to the specific case of πΞ pairs which present challenges due to the mass difference, strangeness quantum numbers, and the Ξ*(1530) resonance. The authors note that previous analyses by the STAR collaboration (Sumbera 2007, Chaloupka 2006) using the Blast Wave model combined with the FSI model from Pratt and Petriconi could not simultaneously describe the Coulomb and resonance regions, whereas the present work achieves successful description of the source size using a simple Gaussian source. The formalism enables extraction of πΞ scattering lengths and further study of the space-time separation between different stages of fireball evolution in heavy ion collisions.
