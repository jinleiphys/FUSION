# sp(4,R)-systematics of atomic nuclei. F-multiplets and nuclear structure

> A systematics of the atomic nuclei in the frame of the nucleon number $A = Z + N$ and the proton-neutron difference $F = Z - N$ is considered. The classification scheme is provided by means of the non-compact algebra $sp(4,R)$. In this scheme the nuclei are ordered into isobaric multiplets, for which $A={fix}$, as well as in F-multiplets, for which $F={fix}$. The dependence of the mass excess $Δ$, the first exited states $E_{2^+}$ and the ratio $R_2=E_{4^+}/E_{2^+}$ on the nucleon number $A$ is empirically investigated within the $F$-multiplets. Appropriate filters are used to study the properties of the mass excess. Many structural effects are observed. The mirror symmetry is clearly indicated for the energy levels of the nuclei with the same value of $A$ and opposite $F$-values.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that classifying nuclei by nucleon number A and proton-neutron difference F (rather than Z and N alone) reveals systematic structural patterns, with the sp(4,R) algebra providing a formal framework. The authors empirically observe that mass excess, first excited 2+ state energies, and the ratio R2 = E4+/E2+ show clear signatures of magic numbers, sub-magic numbers, and mirror symmetry within F-multiplets, though many observed features remain unexplained.

## Method
The authors map even-A and odd-A nuclei onto the two irreducible infinite oscillator representations of the non-compact algebra sp(4,R), ordering them into isobaric multiplets (fixed A) and F-multiplets (fixed F). They empirically plot the mass excess Δ, the first excited 2+ state energy E2+, and the ratio R2 = E4+/E2+ as functions of A for fixed F, using experimental data from the ENSDF database. They introduce discrete filters (first and second discrete derivatives, a D-function) to analyze the staggering and coupling behavior of the mass excess curves.

## Key numbers
- Mass excess curves for F = 0, -4, -23, -44 are shown, with minima at nuclei such as ⁵⁶₂₈Ni₂₈ (F=0), ⁹⁶₄₆Pd₅₀ (F=-4), ¹²³₅₀Sn₇₃ and ¹⁴¹₅₉Pr₈₂ (F=-23), and ²⁰⁸₈₂Pb₁₂₆ (F=-44).
- Deviations in R2 curves are observed for the N=78 series: ¹⁴⁴₆₆Dy₇₈, ¹⁴²₆₄Gd₇₈, ¹⁴⁰₆₂Sm₇₈, ¹³⁸₆₀Nd₇₈, ¹³⁶₅₈Ce₇₈.
- Deviations in R2 curves are observed for the N=88 series: ¹⁵⁴₆₆Dy₈₈, ¹⁵²₆₄Gd₈₈, ¹⁵⁰₆₂Sm₈₈, ¹⁴⁸₆₀Nd₈₈, ¹⁴⁶₅₈Ce₈₈.
- Deviations in R2 curves are observed for the Pt series: ¹⁸⁴₇₈Pt₁₀₆ through ¹⁹⁸₇₈Pt₁₂₀ (eight isotopes).
- Mirror symmetry E_{Jπ}(A,F) = E_{Jπ}(A,-F) holds for all examined mirror pairs except three ground-state exceptions: ¹⁶₉F₇ (0⁻) vs ¹⁶₇N₉ (2⁻) at F=±2; ²²₁₃Al₉ (3⁺) vs ²²₉F₁₃ (4⁺) at F=±4; ²⁵₁₅P₁₀ (1/2⁺) vs ²⁵₁₀Ne₁₅ (3/2⁺) at F=±5.

## Context
This work extends the earlier classification of nuclei by Selinov (1948) using A and (Z-N)/2, and formalizes it within the sp(4,R) algebraic framework. The observed deviations in R2 curves for the N=88 series (e.g., ¹⁵⁴₆₆Dy₈₈) are noted as left neighbors of candidate X(5) nuclei (Casten, 2001), suggesting a connection to shape-phase transitions. The systematic F-multiplet approach enables future investigation of proton-rich nuclei via mirror symmetry and may help identify new sub-shell closures beyond the standard magic numbers.
