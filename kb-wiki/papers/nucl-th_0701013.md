# Are low-energy nuclear observables sensitive to high-energy phase shifts?

> Conventional nucleon-nucleon potentials with strong short-range repulsion require contributions from high-momentum wave function components even for low-energy observables such as the deuteron binding energy. This can lead to the misconception that reproducing high-energy phase shifts is important for such observables. Interactions derived via the similarity renormalization group decouple high-energy and low-energy physics while preserving the phase shifts from the starting potential. They are used to show that high-momentum components (and high-energy phase shifts) can be set to zero when using low-momentum interactions, without losing information relevant for low-energy observables.

## Key claim

High-momentum components (and high-energy phase shifts) can be set to zero when using low-momentum interactions derived via the similarity renormalization group, without losing information relevant for low-energy observables, provided the interactions have been evolved to decouple high-energy and low-energy physics.

## Method

The authors apply the similarity renormalization group (SRG) to evolve nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials (Argonne v18, CD-Bonn, and chiral N3LO) via a unitary transformation that drives the Hamiltonian toward band-diagonal form in momentum space, using the flow equation dH_s/ds = [[T_rel, H_s], H_s] with T_rel the relative kinetic energy. They test decoupling by setting the evolved potential V_s(k,k') to zero for all momenta above a specified cutoff kmax and then computing phase shifts and deuteron binding energy, comparing results to those from the unevolved potentials. They also evolve the number operator a^dagger_q a_q alongside the Hamiltonian to examine the deuteron momentum distribution.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron binding energy calculated with the Argonne v18 potential is 9.9 MeV unbound when momenta greater than 2 fm^-1 are excluded from the wave function (kinetic energy 11.5 MeV, potential energy -1.6 MeV); contributions up to 4 fm^-1 are needed to obtain a bound state.
- For the SRG-evolved potential with lambda = 2 fm^-1, setting high-momentum matrix elements to zero with kmax = 2.2 fm^-1 changes the deuteron binding energy to only 2.2 MeV (compared to the full SRG result).
- Phase shifts from different initial potentials (Argonne v18, CD-Bonn, chiral N3LO) start to disagree for laboratory energies above 300-400 MeV.
- The SRG evolution preserves phase shifts to within less than 0.1% numerical accuracy in the worst case.

## Context

This work extends the renormalization group approach to NN potentials (Vlowk) developed by Bogner, Furnstahl, and collaborators (2003-2006) and directly addresses concerns raised by Benhar (1993) about high-momentum components in the deuteron wave function being necessary to explain electron scattering data. The SRG method, based on earlier work by Glazek and Wilson (1993) and Wegner (1994), provides a unitary transformation that explicitly preserves all observables while decoupling high-energy from low-energy physics, enabling clean tests that were not possible with earlier Vlowk approaches. The results enable future work on evolving three-nucleon interactions consistently and extending decoupling tests to systems with more than two nucleons.
