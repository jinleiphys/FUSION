# Stationary States for Fermions in an External Electric Field

> We present a relativistic analysis of fermions in an external electric field by non-perturbatively solving the Dirac equation with a static gauge. Different from the magnetic field effect, the fermion wave function in an electric field oscillates asymptotically, which results in the absence of bound states in an infinite system. For a confined fermion, the confinement is gradually canceled by the electric field, and the fermion becomes deconfined when the electric coupling is stronger than the confinement coupling. However, a fermion in an electric field can be confined to a finite system by applying the MIT bag boundary condition, namely, the disappearing normal component of the probability current at the boundary. The solutions obtained can serve as a basis for calculating dynamical processes in the presence of a strong electric field, such as those occurring in the early stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where an extremely strong electric field is expected to be generated.

# Stationary States for Fermions in an External Electric Field

## Key claim
Bound states for a charged fermion in a uniform electric field are impossible in an infinite system, but can exist in a finite system when the MIT bag boundary condition is applied; when a scalar confinement potential is added, the electric field partially cancels the confinement, and the fermion becomes deconfined only when the electric coupling exceeds the confinement coupling.

## Method
The authors non-perturbatively solved the stationary Dirac equation in a static gauge for a uniform electric field along the z-axis, using parabolic cylinder functions as the basis. They analyzed three scenarios: an infinite system with only the electric field, an infinite system with both electric and scalar linear confinement potentials, and a finite system with the MIT bag boundary condition (vanishing normal component of the probability current at the boundary). The energy spectrum was obtained by imposing continuity and boundary conditions on the wave functions.

## Key numbers
- Charm quark mass taken as $m_c = 1.29$ GeV and bottom quark mass as $m_b = 4.7$ GeV for the confinement study.
- Scalar confinement coupling constant $g_s = 0.2$ GeV$^2$ used for both charm and bottom quarks.
- Electric field strengths considered: $|qE| = 0$, $0.05$, and $0.1$ GeV$^2$ for the confined quark energy level calculations.
- For highly excited states in a finite system of length $2L$, the asymptotic energy quantization is $p_0 = E_n = (1+2n)\pi/(4L)$, independent of electric field strength and particle mass.

## Context
This work extends the study of fermions in external electromagnetic fields beyond the well-studied magnetic field case (e.g., Landau levels, chiral magnetic effect of Kharzeev (2007), magnetic catalysis of Fukushima (2012)). It provides a rigorous proof of the absence of bound states in a uniform electric field, complementing the classical acceleration argument. The results enable calculations of dynamical processes in strong electric fields, such as heavy quark pair production and Drell-Yan processes in the early stage of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where field strengths can reach the QCD scale as discussed by Tuchin (2014) and Deng (2012). The MIT bag boundary condition approach follows Chodos (1974).
