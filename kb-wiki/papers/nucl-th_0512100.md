# Hadrons in compact stars

> We discuss $β$-equilibrated and charge neutral matter involving hyperons and $\bar K$ condensates within relativistic models. It is observed that populations of baryons are strongly affected by the presence of antikaon condensates. Also, the equation of state including $\bar K$ condensates becomes softer resulting in a smaller maximum mass neutron star.

## Key claim

The paper argues that including antikaon condensates in beta-equilibrated hyperon matter softens the equation of state and reduces the maximum neutron star mass, but this result depends on using a strongly attractive antikaon optical potential depth of -160 MeV at normal nuclear matter density.

## Method

The authors adopt a relativistic mean-field model for baryonic matter composed of the full baryon octet (n, p, Λ, Σ⁺, Σ⁻, Σ⁰, Ξ⁻, Ξ⁰) plus leptons, with baryon-baryon interactions mediated by scalar and vector mesons including hidden-strangeness mesons σ* and φ. They introduce antikaon degrees of freedom via a minimal coupling scheme, treat K⁻ condensation as a first-order phase transition and \(\bar K^0\) condensation as second-order, and enforce global charge neutrality and baryon number conservation using Gibbs phase equilibrium rules for the mixed phase.

## Key numbers

- Mixed phase (K⁻ condensation) begins at baryon density 2.23 n₀, where n₀ = 0.153 fm⁻³ is the saturation density.
- Λ hyperon is the first strange baryon to appear, at 2.51 n₀ in the mixed phase.
- The mixed phase terminates at 4.0 n₀; \(\bar K^0\) condensation sets in at approximately 4.1 n₀.
- Maximum mass of a static neutron star with \(\bar K\) condensates is 1.57 M☉; without condensates (hyperons only) it is 1.79 M☉.
- Antikaon optical potential depth at normal nuclear matter density is U\(_{\bar K}\) = -160 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hyperonic matter in neutron stars (Glendenning 1985) and the prediction of kaon condensation in dense matter by Kaplan and Nelson (1986). It uses the GM1 parameter set for meson-nucleon couplings and determines hyperon couplings from hypernuclei data and SU(6) symmetry, following the approach of Schaffner and Mishustin (1996). The results enable future comparisons with neutron star mass and radius measurements from observatories such as Chandra and Hubble, and with flow data on antikaon-nucleon interactions from heavy ion collisions (Pal 2004).
