# The Positive-Parity Baryon Spectrum and the Role of Hybrid Baryons

> We calculate the low-lying spectra for the positive-parity $Δ$ and $N$ at two pion masses of 358 and 278 MeV using an isotropic clover action with two degenerate light-quaark and one strange-quark flavors through the application of the generalized variational method within the distillation framework. The spectrum exhibits the general features observed in previous calculations using an anisotropic clover lattice, with a counting of states at least as rich as the quark model. Furthermore, we identify states that are hybrid in nature, where gluonic degrees of freedom play a structural role, indicatinng that such states appear a feature of the excited baryon spectrum, irrespective of the lattice action, or the precise details of the smearing of the lattice interpolating operators used to identify such states.

## Key claim

The identification of hybrid baryons, states where gluonic degrees of freedom play a structural role, is robust across different lattice actions, lattice spacings, and operator smearing methods, as demonstrated by comparing calculations using an isotropic clover action at finer lattice spacing with earlier anisotropic clover results, though the identification becomes less clear for the nucleon at the lighter pion mass of 278 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations using an isotropic clover action with 2+1 flavors of dynamical quarks on ensembles with pion masses of 358 and 278 MeV and a lattice spacing of approximately 0.094 fm. They employed the distillation framework with 64 eigenvectors to construct baryon interpolating operators containing up to two covariant derivatives, including chromomagnetic operators that vanish for trivial gauge configurations, and extracted the low-lying positive-parity spectra for the Delta and nucleon using the generalized variational method.

## Key numbers

- Pion masses used: 358(3) MeV and 278(4) MeV, on ensembles with lattice spacing a = 0.094(1) fm
- Lattice volume: 32^3 x 64 for both ensembles, with 349 configurations at m_pi = 358 MeV and 259 configurations at m_pi = 278 MeV
- Distillation space dimension N_D = 64 eigenvectors used for the primary analysis
- For the Delta spectrum on the a094m358 ensemble, one hybrid state identified in the J=1/2 channel and one in the J=3/2 channel
- For the nucleon spectrum on the a094m358 ensemble, two hybrid states identified in J=1/2, two in J=3/2, and one in J=5/2
- Energies for spin 5/2 and 7/2 states obtained in the H_g irrep were within 1% of values obtained in the G_1g and G_2g irreps

## Context

This work extends the earlier anisotropic clover lattice calculations of Edwards (2011) and Dudek (2012) to a finer isotropic lattice with smaller lattice spacing, testing the robustness of hybrid baryon identification across different lattice actions. The results confirm the counting of states at least as rich as the quark model and the presence of hybrid states as a feature of the excited baryon spectrum, while also showing that the Roper resonance remains absent in calculations using Wilson-type actions. This enables future studies to investigate the quark-mass dependence of hybrid baryons and to incorporate multi-hadron operators for a more complete understanding of the excited spectrum.
