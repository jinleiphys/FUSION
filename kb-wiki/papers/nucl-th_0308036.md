# Counter Terms for Low Momentum Nucleon-Nucleon Interactions

> There is much current interest in treating low energy nuclear physics using the renormalization group (RG) and effective field theory (EFT). Inspired by this RG-EFT approach, we study a low-momentum nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction, V_{low-k}, obtained by integrating out the fast modes down the scale ~2fm^{-1}. Since NN experiments can only determine the effective interaction in the low momentum region, our chief purpose is to find such an interaction for complex nuclei whose typical momenta lie below this scale. In this paper we find that V_{low-k} can be satisfactorily accounted for by the counter terms corresponding to a short range effective interaction. The coefficients C_n of the power series expansion C_n*k^n for the counter terms have been accurately determined and results derived from several meson-exchange NN interaction models are compared. The counter terms are found to be important for the S, P, and D partial waves. Scaling behavior of the counter terms is also studied, and we discuss the use of these methods for computing shell model matrix elements.

# Counter Terms for Low Momentum Nucleon-Nucleon Interactions

## Key claim

The low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction V_{low-k} (obtained by integrating out momentum modes above ~2 fm^{-1}) can be accurately represented by the low-momentum part of a bare NN potential plus a short-range counter term series, but only when the bare potential itself (not just one-pion exchange) is used as the starting point; attempts to fit V_{low-k} with one-pion exchange plus counter terms gave unsatisfactory results.

## Method

The authors constructed V_{low-k} from realistic NN potentials (CD-Bonn, Argonne V18, Nijmegen, Paris) by integrating out high-momentum components above a cutoff Λ ~ 2 fm^{-1} using T-matrix equivalence, preserving deuteron binding energy and low-energy phase shifts. They then performed χ-square fits of a momentum-expansion counter term series (C_0 + C_2(q^2+q'^2) + higher terms) to the difference between V_{low-k} and the projected bare potential PV_{bare}P, determining coefficients separately for each partial wave channel up to D-waves.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff momentum Λ = 2 fm^{-1} (also tested at 2.1 fm^{-1})
- For CD-Bonn potential, ^1S_0 channel: C_0 = -0.1580 fm, C_2 = -1.309×10^{-2} fm^3, C_4 = 3.561×10^{-4} fm^5, C_4' = -8.469×10^{-4} fm^5; rms deviation Δ_rms = 0.0002
- For CD-Bonn potential, ^3S_1 channel: C_0 = -0.4651 fm, C_2 = 5.884×10^{-2} fm^3; Δ_rms = 0.0003
- Deuteron binding energy reproduced: 2.225 MeV (full CD-Bonn) vs 2.224 MeV (counter term approach)
- Phase shifts reproduced up to lab energy 325 MeV with only slight deviations at the highest energies
- Spin-dependent delta interaction parameters from Table I coefficients: f_0 = -0.382 fm, f'_0 = -0.077 fm
- Shell model matrix elements for ^1S_0, ^3S_1, and ^3D_1 channels agreed to four significant figures between V_{low-k} and (V_{bare}+V_{CT})

## Context

This work extends the V_{low-k} program developed by Bogner, Kuo, and collaborators (2001-2003) which constructed low-momentum NN interactions by integrating out high-momentum components from realistic potentials. It contrasts with traditional RG-EFT approaches (Weinberg, 1990; Lepage, 1997; Kaplan et al., 1998) that use a chiral symmetry breaking scale ~1 GeV and one-pion exchange as the starting potential. The authors show that their "MEEFT" (more effective effective field theory) approach, using a cutoff at the pion production threshold where experimental data exist, enables accurate representation of V_{low-k} by a simple counter term series, making shell model calculations more tractable across a wide range of nuclei (sd-shell, tin region, lead region).
