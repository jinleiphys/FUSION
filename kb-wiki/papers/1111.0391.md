# Parity-violating photon circular polarization in $nd\rightarrow$ $^3Hγ$ with effective field theory at thermal energy

> We study the parity-violating effects in $nd\rightarrow$ $^3Hγ$ process using pionless effective field theory (EFT($//!/!/!/pi$)) at thermal energy. For the weak $NN$ interaction the parity-violating Lagrangian contains five independent low-energy coupling constants (LECs). One can fix the coupling constants by comparison of the calculated observables with a sufficient number of experimental data. So, we need five observables to obtain LECs. One of the five parity-violating observables is the photon circular polarization ($P_γ$) in $nd\rightarrow$ $^3Hγ$ process. We calculate $P_γ$ at the leading order in terms of LECs. We compare our results with the previous $P_γ$ calculation based on the model of Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein.

## Key claim

The photon circular polarization \(P_\gamma\) in the \(nd\rightarrow ^3\mathrm{H}\gamma\) process is calculated at leading order in pionless effective field theory (EFT(\(/\!\!\!\pi\))) in terms of five unknown low-energy coupling constants (LECs), and the result is compared with previous potential-model calculations based on the Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (DDH) framework; the calculation assumes that no parity-violating three-nucleon interaction enters at leading or next-to-leading order.

## Method

The authors use pionless effective field theory with dibaryon fields to compute the parity-conserving (PC) and parity-violating (PV) amplitudes for \(nd\) scattering and the \(nd\rightarrow ^3\mathrm{H}\gamma\) radiative capture process at thermal energy. The PC sector is treated at leading order using the Faddeev equations for the \(Nd\) system, and the PV sector employs the leading-order PV Lagrangian containing five independent weak \(dNN\) coupling constants corresponding to \(^1S_0\leftrightarrow ^3P_0\), \(^3S_1\leftrightarrow ^1P_1\), and \(^3S_1\leftrightarrow ^3P_1\) transitions. The PV \(Nd\) scattering amplitude is obtained by inserting the PC amplitudes into integral equations with PV kernels, and the PV capture amplitude is evaluated from diagrams involving E1 transitions at threshold.

## Key numbers

* The thermal energy for the calculation is \(2.5\times10^{-8}\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The isoscalar and isovector nucleon magnetic moments used are \(k_0 = 0.4399\) and \(k_1 = 2.35294\) in nuclear magnetons.
* Previous DDH potential-model results for \(P_\gamma\) in \(nd\rightarrow ^3\mathrm{H}\gamma\) are \(P_\gamma = -1.39\times10^{-6}\) (super-soft-core potential) and \(P_\gamma = -1.14\times10^{-6}\) (Reid-soft-core potential).
* Previous DDH results for the photon asymmetry \(A_\gamma^t\) are \(0.81\times10^{-6}\) (SSC) and \(0.61\times10^{-6}\) (RSC).
* The experimental value for the PV photon asymmetry in \(nd\rightarrow ^3\mathrm{H}\gamma\) is \(A_\gamma^t = (4.2 \pm 3.8)\times10^{-6}\) (ILL measurement); no experimental data for \(P_\gamma\) in this process have been reported.

## Context

This work extends the application of pionless effective field theory to parity-violating observables in the three-nucleon sector, following earlier EFT(\(/\!\!\!\pi\)) calculations of the parity-conserving \(nd\rightarrow ^3\mathrm{H}\gamma\) process by Sadeghi, Bayegan, and Griesshammer (2006) and of PV observables in the two-nucleon system by Phillips, Schindler, and Springer (2009). The results are compared with the DDH meson-exchange potential calculations of Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (1980) and with later PV calculations by Song and collaborators (2010). The calculation enables future extraction of the five weak LECs once sufficient experimental data on PV observables in few-body systems become available, and it can be extended to other observables such as the PV photon asymmetry.
