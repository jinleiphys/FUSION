# Nuclear excitations within microscopic EDF approaches : pairing and temperature effects on the dipole response

> In the present work, the isovector dipole responses, both in the resonance region and in the low-energy sector, are investigated using the microscopic nuclear Energy Density Functionals (EDFs). The self-consistent QRPA model based on Skyrme Hartree Fock BCS approach is applied to study the evolution of the isovector dipole strength by increasing neutron number and temperature. First, the isovector dipole strength and excitation energies are investigated for the Ni isotopic chain at zero temperature. The evolution of the low-energy dipole strength is studied as a function of the neutron number. In the second part, the temperature dependence of the isovector dipole excitations is studied using the self-consistent finite temperature QRPA, below and above the critical temperatures. It is shown that new excited states become possible due to the thermally occupied states above the Fermi level, and opening of the new excitations channels. In addition, temperature leads to fragmentation of the low-energy strength around the neutron separation energies, and between 9 and 12 MeV. We find that the cumulative sum of the strength below E$\leq12$ MeV decreases in open-shell nuclei due to the vanishing of the pairing correlations as temperature increases up to T=1 MeV. The analysis of the transition densities in the low-energy region shows that the proton and neutron transition densities display a mixed pattern: both isoscalar and isovector motion of protons and neutrons are obtained inside nuclei, while the neutron transition density is dominant at the surface region.

## Key claim

The isovector dipole response in Nickel isotopes is studied using self-consistent QRPA based on Skyrme EDFs, showing that the cumulative sum of low-energy dipole strength below 12 MeV decreases in open-shell nuclei as temperature increases up to 1 MeV due to vanishing pairing correlations, but then increases again at higher temperatures due to new excitation channels from thermally occupied states.

## Method

The self-consistent finite-temperature QRPA (FT-QRPA) based on Skyrme Hartree-Fock BCS approach was applied to study isovector dipole excitations in the Nickel isotopic chain. Calculations were performed using the Skyrme-type SLy5 and SkI3 energy density functionals with a zero-range density-dependent surface-type pairing interaction, assuming spherical symmetry with box boundary conditions in a 20 fm box and a quasiparticle energy cutoff of 100 MeV. The discrete dipole strength was smoothed using Lorentzian functions with widths of 1.0 MeV at zero temperature and 0.5 MeV at finite temperature.

## Key numbers

- Neutron pairing gap values for SLy5 (SkI3): Delta_n = 1.36 (1.49) MeV for 58Ni, 1.60 (1.87) MeV for 62Ni, and 1.83 (1.62) MeV for 66Ni at zero temperature
- Critical temperature values for pairing phase transition: 0.84 MeV (SkI3) and 0.69 MeV (SLy5) for 58Ni; 1.01 MeV (SkI3) and 0.93 MeV (SLy5) for 62Ni; 0.82 MeV (SkI3) and 1.0 MeV (SLy5) for 66Ni
- Total low-energy strength between 8 and 12 MeV in 70Ni: 0.75 e2fm2 (2.77% of EWSR) with SLy5; 1.67 e2fm2 (6.33% of EWSR) with SkI3
- Slope of symmetry energy L: 48.27 MeV for SLy5; 100.52 MeV for SkI3
- Cumulative dipole strength below 12 MeV in 56Ni at T=2.0 MeV: 0.987 fm2 (SkI3) and 0.834 fm2 (SLy5)
- Cumulative dipole strength below 12 MeV in 66Ni: 1.670 fm2 at T=0 MeV, decreasing to 1.390 fm2 at T=1.0 MeV, then increasing to 1.720 fm2 at T=2.0 MeV (SkI3)

## Context

This work extends previous finite-temperature RPA studies by Yifei (2009) using relativistic DD-ME2 functionals and by Khan (2004) using Skyrme functionals with continuum-QRPA, as well as the authors' own development of self-consistent FT-QRPA (Yuksel 2017) applied to 68Ni and 120Sn. The results are relevant for understanding the pygmy dipole resonance and its connection to the neutron skin (Piekarewicz 2011, Carbone 2010) and symmetry energy (Roca-Maza 2012), and for predicting radiative neutron capture cross sections important for astrophysical r-process calculations at finite temperatures.
