# Coulomb breakup effects on the elastic cross section of $^6$He+$^{209}$Bi scattering near Coulomb barrier energies

> We accurately analyze the $^6$He+$^{209}$Bi scattering at 19 and 22.5 MeV near the Coulomb barrier energy, using the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) based on the $n$+$n$+$^4$He+$^{209}$Bi four-body model. The three-body breakup continuum of $^6$He is discretized by diagonalizing the internal Hamiltonian of $^6$He in a space spanned by the Gaussian basis functions. The calculated elastic and total reaction cross sections are in good agreement with the experimental data, while the CDCC calculation based on the di-neutron model of $^6$He, i.e., the $^2n$+$^{4}$He+$^{209}$Bi three-body model, does not reproduce the data.

## Key claim

The four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC), which treats the projectile as a three-body system (n+n+4He), reproduces the measured elastic and total reaction cross sections for 6He+209Bi scattering at 19 and 22.5 MeV with no free adjustable parameters, while the three-body CDCC based on the di-neutron model (2n+4He) overestimates the total reaction cross section by a factor of about three.

## Method

The authors applied four-body CDCC to the 6He+209Bi system, treating it as an n+n+4He+209Bi four-body system. The three-body breakup continuum of 6He was discretized by diagonalizing the internal Hamiltonian of 6He in a space spanned by Gaussian basis functions (the Gaussian expansion method, GEM), with internal states up to 7 MeV excitation energy and including 0+, 1-, and 2+ states. The coupled-channels equations were solved with maximum orbital angular momentum L=200 and asymptotic matching at R=200 fm, using optical potentials from Koning and Delaroche for n-Bi and from Barnett and Lilley for alpha-Bi.

## Key numbers

- Incident energies: 19 MeV and 22.5 MeV in the laboratory frame for 6He+209Bi scattering near the Coulomb barrier.
- Number of discretized continuum states for 6He in four-body CDCC: 37 for 0+ (including ground state), 44 for 1-, and 53 for 2+ states.
- Number of discretized continuum states for 6He in three-body CDCC: 9 for each of the 0+, 1-, and 2+ states.
- Maximum Gaussian range parameters for four-body CDCC: 15.0 fm for both y and r coordinates (larger than the 10 fm used in previous nuclear breakup studies).
- B(E1) strength from the di-neutron model: 1.5 e2fm2; from the n+n+4He model: 0.9 e2fm2 (the latter agrees with the experimental value from Aumann et al.).
- Three-body CDCC overestimates the total reaction cross section by a factor of about three compared to experimental data.

## Context

This work extends the CDCC method to four-body systems (n+n+4He+209Bi) for low-energy scattering where both nuclear and Coulomb breakup are significant, following the earlier development of four-body CDCC applied to 6He+12C scattering at 18 and 229.8 MeV (Matsumoto et al., 2004). It directly addresses the failure of the di-neutron model used by Keeley et al. (2003), which overestimated the total reaction cross section and underestimated the elastic cross section; Rusek et al. (2005) had shown that scaling the dipole coupling strength by 0.5 could reproduce elastic data, but the present work demonstrates that the full four-body treatment is necessary without such ad-hoc scaling. The results enable accurate analysis of 6He scattering on heavy targets near the Coulomb barrier, with implications for understanding halo nuclei and the interplay of Coulomb and nuclear breakup.
