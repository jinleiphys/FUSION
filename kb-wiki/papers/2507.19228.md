# Polarization of the $ϕ$ meson in the hadronic phase with nucleon scatterings and a viscous hydrodynamic background

> We extend our previous work on the spin alignment of the $ϕ$ vector meson in the hadronic phase of the Quark Gluon Plasma, by including effects of nucleon scatterings. The emission rates are calculated in a realistic hydrodynamic background simulated with the code Fluid$u$m, for different beam energies. We find that all the effects taken into account cannot explain the out-of-plane spin alignment of the $ϕ$ meson observed experimentally.

## Key claim

The authors extend their previous work on phi meson spin alignment by including nucleon scatterings and using a realistic viscous hydrodynamic background, but they find that all considered effects together still cannot explain the experimentally observed out-of-plane spin alignment of the phi meson, remaining consistent with zero (rho_00 approximately 1/3) at variance with the data.

## Method

The polarized emission rate of phi mesons was calculated including interactions with a bath of kaons (from previous work), viscous corrections, and newly added nucleon scatterings via a minimal phi-NN Lagrangian with Dirac and Pauli couplings. The hadronic medium was simulated using (2+1)D boost-invariant viscous hydrodynamics with the code Fluidum, with constant shear viscosity over entropy density eta/s = 0.14 and bulk viscosity over entropy density zeta/s = 0.12, and the free coupling constant G_V was fixed by fitting to experimental phi meson transverse momentum spectra at each collision energy independently of spin alignment data.

## Key numbers

* At sqrt(s_NN) = 11.5 GeV, the baryon chemical potential to temperature ratio was mu/T = 2.1; at 200 GeV, mu/T = 0.2.
* The hadronic phase was identified as fluid cells with temperature between 110 MeV and 170 MeV.
* Best-fit coupling constant G_V ranged from 1.3 (at 11.5 GeV) to 2.0 (at 62.4 and 200 GeV).
* For nucleon scatterings, the phi-proton and phi-neutron couplings were set to g_phi_p = g_phi_n = 1, with anomalous magnetic moments kappa_phi_p = 2.79 and kappa_phi_n = -1.91; for the N(1880) resonance, g_phi_N = -1.47 and kappa_phi_N = -1.65.
* The resonance N(1880) was included with mass m_N = 1.88 GeV and width Gamma = 0.3 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation (Grossi, 2024) that considered only kaon interactions and found very small spin alignment in a static fluid and Bjorken flow, which was consistent with STAR data at 200 GeV but not at lower energies. The results contradict the experimental observations from STAR (2022) showing positive rho_00 - 1/3 for the phi meson, especially at low collision energies, and the authors conclude that the origin of phi meson spin alignment must be sought in earlier stages of the collision rather than in the hadronic phase.
