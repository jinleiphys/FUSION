# Nonextensive statistics based on Landsberg-Vedral entropy

> The general formalism for the nonextensive statistics based on the Landsberg-Vedral entropy was derived. The formula for the first law of thermodynamics and the exact relations of the thermodynamic quantities to their ensemble averages were obtained. It was found that under the transformation $q\to 2-q$ the probabilities of microstates of the nonextensive statistics based on the Landsberg-Vedral entropy formally resemble the corresponding probabilities of the Tsallis statistics with escort probabilities. However, the nonextensive statistics with the Landsberg-Vedral entropy does not require introduction of the escort probabilities and generalized expectation values which are used in this version of the Tsallis statistics.

## Key claim

The paper derives a complete formalism for nonextensive statistical mechanics using the Landsberg-Vedral entropy, showing that under the transformation \(q \to 2-q\) its microstate probabilities formally resemble those of Tsallis statistics with escort probabilities, but without requiring the introduction of escort probabilities or generalized expectation values.

## Method

The author applies the principle of maximum entropy with Lagrange multipliers to the Landsberg-Vedral entropy \(S = \frac{k_B}{1-q} \left(1 - \frac{1}{\sum_i p_i^q}\right)\) for \(q>0\), deriving the probability distributions and thermodynamic relations for the microcanonical, canonical, and grand canonical ensembles. The first law of thermodynamics is derived in each ensemble, and the ensemble averages of thermodynamic quantities (energy, pressure, chemical potential) are shown to take the same linear form as in Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics, but with the new probability distributions.

## Key numbers

- The Landsberg-Vedral entropy is defined as \(S = \frac{k_B}{1-q} \left(1 - \frac{1}{\sum_i p_i^q}\right)\) for \(q>0\), with the Gibbs limit \(\lim_{q\to 1} S = -k_B \sum_i p_i \ln p_i\).
- The nonextensive additivity rule for two independent events A and B is \(S(A+B) = S(A) + S(B) + \frac{q-1}{k_B} S(A) S(B)\).
- In the microcanonical ensemble, the entropy becomes \(S = \frac{W^{q-1} - 1}{q-1}\) where \(W\) is the number of microstates.
- In the canonical ensemble, the probability of microstate \(i\) is \(p_i = \frac{1}{Z_c} \left[1 + (q-1) \frac{\Lambda - E_i}{T}\right]^{\frac{1}{q-1}}\), with \(Z_c\) defined by eq. (17) and \(\Lambda\) determined by eq. (19).
- Under the transformation \(q \to 2-q\), the canonical probability \(p_i = \frac{1}{Z_q} \left[1 + (q-1) \frac{E - E_i}{cT}\right]^{\frac{1}{q-1}}\) formally recovers the Tsallis escort probability form, with \(c = q Z_q^{q-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the line of nonextensive statistical mechanics initiated by Tsallis (1988) and builds on the Landsberg-Vedral entropy introduced by Landsberg (1999). It addresses an indeterminacy in Tsallis statistics related to generalized expectation values and normalization conditions, as discussed by Tsallis (1998), Plastino, Abe, and Parvan in prior work. The formalism enables a nonextensive thermodynamics that preserves standard linear expectation values and the Legendre transform structure, potentially applicable to complex phenomena in high-energy nuclear physics where Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics fails, such as nontrivial macromotions, particle transformation reactions, jets, and internal currents.
