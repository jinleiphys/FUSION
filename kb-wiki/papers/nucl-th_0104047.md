# New results for two-neutrino double beta decay with large particle-particle two body proton-neutron interaction

> A model many-body Hamiltonian describing an heterogenous system of paired protons and paired neutrons and interacting among themselves through monopole particle-hole and monopole particle-particle interactions is used to study the double beta decay of Fermi type. The states are described by time dependent approaches choosing as trial functions coherent states of the symmetry groups underlying the model Hamiltonian. One formalism, VP1, is fully equivalent with the standard pnQRPA and therefore fails at a critical value of the particle-particle interaction strength while another one, VP2, corresponds to a two step BCS treatment, i.e. the proton quasiparticles are paired with the neutron quasiparticles. In this way a harmonic description for the double beta transition amplitude is provided for any strength of the particle-particle interaction. The approximation quality is judged by comparing the actual results with the exact result as well as with those corresponding to various truncations of the boson expanded Hamiltonian and transition operator. Finally it is shown that the dynamic ground states provided by VP1 and VP2 are reasonable well approximated by solutions of a variational principle. This remark constitutes a step forward finding an approach where the RPA ground state is a solution of a variational principle equation.

# New results for two-neutrino double beta decay with large particle-particle two body proton-neutron interaction

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a time-dependent variational approach using SU(2) coherent states (VP2) provides a harmonic description of the double beta decay transition amplitude for any strength of the particle-particle interaction, including the region beyond the critical value where the standard proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pnQRPA) breaks down. The authors claim that the VP1 formalism, which is fully equivalent to standard pnQRPA, fails at a critical value of the particle-particle interaction strength, while VP2 works in the complementary interval.

## Method
The authors study a model many-body Hamiltonian describing paired protons and paired neutrons interacting through monopole particle-hole and monopole particle-particle interactions, restricted to a single j shell. They apply time-dependent variational principles using four distinct trial functions: coherent states of the Weyl group (VP1, equivalent to standard pnQRPA), coherent states of SU(2) (VP2), and two additional forms (VP3, VP4). The double beta decay transition amplitude for Fermi-type transitions is calculated using the harmonic modes from VP1 and VP2, and the results are compared with exact solutions and with boson expansion methods (Holstein-Primakoff and Schwinger type) that include anharmonic effects.

## Key numbers
- The critical value of the re-scaled particle-particle interaction strength k' where the pnQRPA (VP1) breaks down is k' = 1.05 MeV for the daughter nucleus harmonic mode.
- The SU(2) mode (VP2) in the mother nucleus has non-vanishing energy only for k' values larger than 1.2 MeV for chi' = 0.0 and 1.3 MeV for chi' = 0.5.
- The transition amplitude for small k' (k' < 0.8 MeV) and large k' (k' > 1.5 MeV) is almost constant.
- At the k' value where the pnQRPA amplitude equals zero, the exact result for the transition amplitude is about 0.9 MeV^{-1}.
- The energy shift Delta E is taken equal to 1 MeV for small k' values; for k' >= 1.3 MeV, half the difference between static ground state energies of mother and daughter nuclei is added.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Raduta et al., 2000) on stabilizing the ground state in the region of large particle-particle interaction strength by introducing the particle-particle interaction first in the mean field and then in the pnQRPA process. The paper addresses the well-known drawback of standard pnQRPA (Vogel, 1986; Civitarese et al., 1987) that the transition amplitude vanishes at a certain g_pp and the formalism collapses shortly after, despite this being the region where agreement with experiment is found. The results enable a unified description of two-neutrino double beta decay for both small and large strengths of the particle-particle interaction, and the authors show that the dynamic ground states from VP1 and VP2 can be approximated by solutions of a variational principle, which is a step toward finding an approach where the RPA ground state is a variational solution.
