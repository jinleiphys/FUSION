# Pole Approximation for the pion eletromagnetic form factor within light-front dynamics

> We carefully investigate the reliability of the propagator Pole Approximation, i.e, the approximation of retaining only the propagator in the evaluation of the Mandelstam covariant expression for the eletromagnetic current of the pion. Different frames are analyzed, in order to find the most suitable one for calculating the pion form factor within the proposed approximation. It turns out that approximation is more accurate in the frame where q^+ is maximal. The relevance of the Pole Approximation is briefly discussed in view of calculations of hadrons factors basead wave functions generate by dynamical models.

## Key claim

The propagator Pole Approximation (PA) for the pion electromagnetic form factor is reliable only when evaluated in a frame where the plus component of the momentum transfer \(q^+\) is maximal; in the Drell-Yan frame (\(q^+=0\)) the approximation works very badly, contradicting the common assumption that the valence contribution alone suffices.

## Method

The authors compute the pion electromagnetic form factor from the Mandelstam covariant expression for the current in the impulse approximation, using a symmetric analytic model for the pion-quark vertex function with a regulator mass. They compare the exact result with the Pole Approximation, which retains only the poles from the constituent quark propagators (neglecting vertex poles), for three kinematic frames defined by \(\alpha = 0^\circ\) (Drell-Yan, \(q^+=0\)), \(45^\circ\), and \(90^\circ\) (maximal \(q^+\)). The form factor is extracted from the plus component of the current, and the PA includes both a valence contribution (spectator quark pole) and a non-valence pair-term contribution.

## Key numbers

* Quark mass \(m_q = 0.220\) GeV, regulator mass \(m_R = 0.60\) GeV, pion mass \(m_\pi = 0.140\) GeV; the regulator mass is chosen to fit the experimental pion decay constant \(f_\pi^{\text{exp}} = 92.4\) MeV.
* The pion form factor is calculated for momentum transfer up to \(q^2 = 10\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).
* For \(\alpha = 90^\circ\) (maximal \(q^+\)), the PA (valence plus non-valence) qualitatively reproduces the exact result; for \(\alpha = 0^\circ\) (Drell-Yan), the PA fails badly.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Pacheco et al. (2004, 2005), who proposed evaluating hadron electromagnetic form factors by replacing the vertex function with a dynamical wave function while neglecting the vertex analytical structure. The present study tests the reliability of that propagator Pole Approximation using an analytic covariant model for the pion (Pacheco et al., 2002). The results enable future calculations of hadron form factors from wave functions generated by dynamical models, provided the frame with maximal \(q^+\) is chosen.
