# Three models for charged hadron nuclear modification from light to heavy ions

> In July 2025 the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) collided $^{16}$O$^{16}$O and $^{20}$Ne$^{20}$Ne isotopes in a quest to understand the physics of ultrarelativistic light ion collisions. One of the key motivations for this run is to discover partonic energy loss in systems with a small quark-gluon plasma (QGP). In this letter we combine a BDMPS-Z based model, a path-length based energy loss prescription, and JEWEL together with two realistic geometries of the $^{16}$O and $^{20}$Ne isotopes. The different sizes of the ions affect the energy loss in characteristically different ways depending on the model.

# Three models for charged hadron nuclear modification from light to heavy ions

## Key claim
The three models (BDMPS-Z based Simple model, path-length based prescription, and JEWEL) all predict that the nuclear modification factor ratio NeNe/OO differs significantly between PGCM and NLEFT nuclear structure calculations, by about a factor two when looking at (R_AA - 1), but the absolute R_AA values for oxygen and neon collisions differ substantially between the Simple model and the path-length approach despite both being fitted to the same lead data.

## Method
The authors implemented three independent energy-loss frameworks: (1) the Simple model, which solves the BDMPS-Z emission equations in the harmonic oscillator approximation for a dynamic medium with EPPS16 nuclear PDFs and a single fitted parameter (q-hat) from PbPb data; (2) a path-length based approach using Trajectum hydrodynamic profiles and a power-law spectrum with energy loss proportional to the integral of T^3 along the path, calibrated to five CMS PbPb centrality classes; (3) the JEWEL Monte Carlo event generator coupled to Trajectum hydrodynamic backgrounds via a public interface, using default parameters and EPPS21 nuclear PDFs. All three models used nuclear structure input from both Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) and the Projected Generator Coordinate Method (PGCM) for 16O and 20Ne isotopes.

## Key numbers
- The Simple model's single free parameter (q-hat) is fitted to minimum bias charged hadron R_AA at 54 GeV in PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV
- The path-length approach uses five CMS PbPb centrality classes up to 70% and 10 different parameter settings from the Trajectum posterior
- JEWEL results used 20 hydrodynamic events for both jets and hadrons
- The NeNe/OO ratio difference between PGCM and NLEFT is approximately a factor of two when expressed as (R_AA - 1)
- The path-length approach shows significantly lower R_AA bands for both OO and NeNe compared to the Simple model, despite both fitted to the same lead data

## Context
This work extends the Simple model of Huss (2020) to include 20Ne systems and updates the Trento profiles for both isotopes, while the path-length approach follows van der Schee (2023). The JEWEL-Trajectum interface from Kolbe (2023) is applied to light ions for the first time. The nuclear structure inputs from NLEFT (Lee 2008, Lahde 2019) and PGCM (Frosini 2021, Yao 2018) produce differences that Giacalone (2024) previously found in mean transverse momentum calculations. The results enable experimental discrimination between energy-loss models using the upcoming LHC light-ion data, and the ratio measurements can help constrain nuclear structure calculations since both PGCM and NLEFT are known to deviate from accurately measured charge radii by similar but opposite amounts.
