# Quartet excitations and cluster spectra in light nuclei

> The relation of quarteting and clustering in atomic nuclei is discussed based on symmetry-considerations. This connection enables us to predict a complete high-energy cluster spectrum from the description of the low-energy quartet part. As an example the $^{28}$Si nucleus is considered, including its well-established ground-state region, the recently proposed superdeformed band, and the high-lying molecular resonances.

# Quartet excitations and cluster spectra in light nuclei

## Key claim
The multichannel dynamical symmetry (MUSY) allows the complete high-energy cluster spectrum of 28Si to be predicted from the low-energy quartet model spectrum by simple projection, without any additional fitting to the cluster states. The authors qualify that the symmetry cannot be decided a priori but must be tested by comparing its consequences with experimental data.

## Method
The authors apply a semimicroscopic algebraic quartet model (SAQM) with a U(3) dynamical symmetry Hamiltonian to describe the low-energy quartet excitations of 28Si, fitting three parameters to experimental data. They then use the multichannel dynamical symmetry (MUSY) to project the 12C+16O and 24Mg+4He cluster spectra from the quartet spectrum by taking the intersection of the quartet and cluster model spaces via U(3) triple product selection rules, with the Wildermuth condition determining the lowest-allowed relative motion quantum numbers.

## Key numbers
- The harmonic oscillator strength for 28Si: ħω = 12.11 MeV, obtained from the systematics ħω = 45A^(-1/3) - 25A^(-2/3) MeV
- Fitted Hamiltonian parameters: a = -0.133 MeV, b = 0.000444 MeV, d = 1.003 MeV
- The B(E2) parameter α² = 0.366 W.u., fitted to the experimental 2⁺₁ → 0⁺₁ transition of 13.2 W.u.
- The symmetry-breaking ratio sb = 13% for the predicted spectrum including both low-energy and high-energy parts; sb = 12% when resonances are included in fitting with weight 0.1
- For 24Mg+4He clusterization, the lowest allowed relative motion quantum number n_R = 8
- For 16O+12C clusterization, values of n_R below 16 are excluded

## Context
This work extends the multichannel dynamical symmetry framework previously applied to 28Si by Cseh and collaborators (2015) to connect the quartet model with cluster spectra, using fewer parameters than earlier MUSY studies. The SAQM is a symmetry-governed truncation of the no-core shell model, while the SACM builds on Elliott's SU(3) shell model and the vibron model. The approach enables prediction of high-lying molecular resonances in 28Si from low-energy quartet data, and can be extended to non-alpha-like nuclei and other binary clusterizations such as 20Ne+8Be.
