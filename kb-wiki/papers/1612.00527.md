# Probing the resonance in the Dirac equation with quadruple-deformed potentials by complex momentum representation method

> Resonance plays critical roles in the formation of many physical phenomena, and many techniques have been developed for the exploration of resonance. In a recent letter [Phys. Rev. Lett. 117, 062502 (2016)], we proposed a new method for probing single-particle resonances by solving the Dirac equation in complex momentum representation for spherical nuclei. Here, we extend this method to deformed nuclei with theoretical formalism presented. We elaborate numerical details, and calculate the bound and resonant states in $^{37}$Mg. The results are compared with those from the coordinate representation calculations with a satisfactory agreement. In particular, the present method can expose clearly the resonant states in complex momentum plane and determine precisely the resonance parameters for not only narrow resonances but also broad resonances that were difficult to obtain before.

## Key claim
The complex momentum representation (CMR) method, previously demonstrated for spherical nuclei, is extended to axially deformed nuclei and can determine resonance parameters for both narrow and broad resonances, including broad resonances that were difficult to obtain with other methods such as the complex scaled method (CSM) or analytic continuation in the coupling constant (ACCC) approach.

## Method
The Dirac equation with quadruple-deformed Woods-Saxon scalar and vector potentials is transformed into momentum representation and expanded in a coupled-channel basis labeled by total angular momentum projection \(\Omega\) and parity. The resulting coupled integral equations are discretized using Gauss-Legendre quadrature on a contour in the complex momentum plane, yielding a symmetric matrix that is diagonalized to obtain bound and resonant states simultaneously. The method is applied to the deformed nucleus \(^{37}\)Mg, and results are compared with coordinate-representation ACCC calculations from Xu (2015).

## Key numbers
- Momentum integral truncated at \(k_{\text{max}} = 4.0\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), with Gauss-Legendre grid number \(N_l = 120\) and coupled-channel number \(N_c = 8\).
- For \(\Omega^\pi = 1/2^-\) states in \(^{37}\)Mg with \(\beta_2 = 0.4\), the resonant state \(1/2[301]\) remains at its original position in the complex \(k\) plane regardless of contour choice; four different contours were tested.
- For \(\beta_2 = -0.2\), fifteen resonant states are exposed in the complex momentum plane for states \(\Omega^\pi = 1/2^\pm, 3/2^\pm, \dots, 9/2^\pm\).
- At \(\beta_2 = 0\) (spherical), four resonant states appear: \(2p_{1/2}\), \(2d_{5/2}\), \(1f_{5/2}\), and \(1g_{9/2}\).
- The resonant level \(5/2[402]\) disappears at large prolate deformation as its width increases rapidly.

## Context
This work extends the CMR method from spherical nuclei (Fang et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 117, 062502, 2016) to deformed nuclei, building on prior relativistic CSM studies for spherical (Guo, 2010) and deformed (Shi, 2014) systems. It overcomes limitations of the CSM, which is only effective for rotation angles \(\theta < \tan^{-1}(\pi a/R)\) and struggles with broad resonances, and of the ACCC approach (Xu, 2015), which did not obtain results for the \(2d_{5/2}\) resonant states. The method enables systematic study of single-particle resonances in deformed nuclei, including broad resonances relevant to exotic phenomena such as halos.
