# Possible Formation of a Perfect Fluid in $pp$, $p$-Pb, Xe-Xe and Pb-Pb Collisions at the Large Hadron Collider Energies: A Color String Percolation Approach

> Isothermal compressibility ($κ_{\rm T}$) is an important thermodynamic observable which gives information about the deviation of a system from perfect fluid behavior. In this work, for the first time we have estimated the isothermal compressibility of QCD matter formed in high energy hadronic and nuclear collisions using color string percolation model (CSPM), where we investigate the change in $κ_{\rm T}$ as a function of final state charged particle multiplicity and initial percolation temperature across various collision species. The estimated initial percolation temperature for different collision systems at different collision energies helps us to have a better understanding of the system at the initial phase of evolution. The comparison of the CSPM results for isothermal compressibility with that for the well known fluids, indicates that the matter formed in heavy-ion collisions might be the {\it closest perfect fluid} found in nature. This estimation complements the well-known observation of minimum shear viscosity to entropy density ratio for a possible QGP medium created in heavy-ion collision experiments. A threshold of pseudorapidity density of charged particles, $\langle dN_{\rm ch}/dη\rangle \geq 20 $ in the final state event multiplicity is observed, after which one may look for a possible QGP formation at the LHC energies.

## Key claim
Using the color string percolation model (CSPM), the authors estimate that the isothermal compressibility of QCD matter formed in high-energy collisions is very small, suggesting it may be the "closest perfect fluid" found in nature, but this conclusion is conditional on a threshold of pseudorapidity density of charged particles, ⟨dNch/dη⟩ ≥ 20, after which QGP formation is possible.

## Method
The authors apply the color string percolation model (CSPM) to fit the soft part (pT range 0.12–1.0 GeV/c) of charged-particle transverse momentum spectra from pp, p-Pb, Xe-Xe, and Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies. From the fitted percolation density parameter ξ and the color suppression factor F(ξ), they derive the initial percolation temperature T(ξ) and then compute the isothermal compressibility κT using an expression that relates pressure, energy density, trace anomaly, and the CSPM parameters.

## Key numbers
- The critical string density for percolation phase transition is ξc = 1.2, corresponding to a critical temperature Tc = 167.7 ± 2.8 MeV.
- The threshold for possible QGP formation is ⟨dNch/dη⟩ ≥ 20, above which the percolation parameter ξ exceeds ξc.
- The isothermal compressibility of water at room temperature is ~6.62×10⁴² fm³/GeV, and of mercury is ~5.33×10⁴¹ fm³/GeV, both much larger than the values obtained for the most central Pb-Pb collisions.
- The single-string average transverse momentum squared is √⟨p²T⟩₁ = 207.2 ± 3.3 MeV.

## Context
This work extends prior CSPM studies of deconfinement phase transitions (Braun 2015) and complements measurements of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (Kovtun 2004, Biro 2011, Plumari 2011) that indicate QGP is nearly a perfect fluid. It also builds on earlier estimates of isothermal compressibility using hadron resonance gas (HRG) and excluded-volume HRG models (Khuntia 2018) and on experimental upper limits from the ALICE collaboration (ALICE 2021). The results enable searches for QGP droplets in high-multiplicity pp collisions at LHC energies.
