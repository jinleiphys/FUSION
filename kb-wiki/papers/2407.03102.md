# Droplets of Bosons at a Narrow Resonance

> We consider bosons interacting through a narrow $s$-wave resonance. Such a resonance is characterized by an infinite scattering length and a large and negative effective range $r_0$. We argue that any number $N\ge3$ of bosons can form a self-bound cluster with the binding energy per particle increasing as $N^2$ for $1\ll N\ll (-r_0/a_\text{bg})^{1/2}$, where $a_\text{bg}$ is the background scattering length (between atoms and molecules). In the opposite limit $N\gg (-r_0/a_\text{bg})^{1/2}$, bosons form droplets with binding energy per particle saturating to a constant value independent of the particle number. The stability of clusters and droplets when the interaction is detuned from the resonance is also studied.

## Key claim

The authors argue that any number \(N\ge 3\) of bosons interacting through a narrow \(s\)-wave resonance can form a self-bound cluster, with the binding energy per particle scaling as \(N^2\) for small clusters and saturating to a constant for large droplets; the existence of the bound state is conditional on the detuning parameter \(\nu\) lying within a finite range that widens as \(N^2\) for small clusters.

## Method

The authors model the narrow resonance using an effective nonrelativistic quantum field theory with an atom field \(\psi\) and a molecular field \(\phi\), where the resonance parameters (scattering length \(a\) and effective range \(r_0\)) are encoded in the coupling \(\alpha = \sqrt{4\pi/(-r_0)}\) and detuning \(\nu = -2/[(-r_0)a]\). For large \(N\), they perform a variational mean-field calculation using a \(\cosh^{-1}\) profile ansatz for the condensates, minimizing the energy functional under the particle-number constraint. For very large \(N\), they include background scattering terms (atom-atom, atom-molecule, molecule-molecule) and analyze the Landau free energy to find the first-order phase transition between the self-bound liquid and the vacuum.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per particle for small clusters (zero detuning): \(E_0 = -0.0347\, N^3 \, \hbar^2/(m r_0^2)\) from a five-parameter variational calculation.
- Optimal atomic fraction at zero detuning: \(c = (\sqrt{17}-1)/6 \approx 0.521\).
- Upper and lower critical detuning for the \(N\)-body bound state: \(\nu_+(N) \approx 0.191 N^2\), \(\nu_-(N) \approx -0.140 N^2\) (in units where \(m=1\), \(-r_0=1\)).
- For the three-body bound state (trimer), the detuning range is \(\nu_+(3) \approx 8.72\) and \(\nu_-(3) \approx -0.366\).
- For the cesium resonance at \(B=19.849(2)\,\text{G}\): effective range ratio \(r_0/a_\text{bg} = -320(20)\), background scattering length \(a_\text{bg}=163(1)a_0\), molecule-molecule scattering length \(a_{22}=220(30)a_0\).
- Crossover between small-cluster and large-droplet regimes occurs at \(N \sim (r_0/a_\text{bg})^{1/2} \sim 20\) for the cesium system.

## Context

This work extends the study of self-bound bosonic droplets, which were first known for \(^4\text{He}\) clusters (Whaley 1994, Slenczka and Toennies) and later formalized for short-range attractive interactions (Bulgac 2001, Hammer 2004). It builds on Petrov's (2015) prediction of quantum-stabilized droplets in bosonic mixtures and their experimental realization (Ferrier-Barbut 2016, Cabrera 2018). The paper specifically addresses the narrow-resonance regime, where the three-body problem was solved by Nishida (2012) and Griesshammer (2023), and where a "mutually trapped state" was previously suggested (Cusack 2001). The results enable future studies of few-body bound states (four-body problem via zero-range calculations of Platter 2004) and Monte Carlo simulations (Bazak 2018), as well as experimental searches in ultracold cesium near the narrow Feshbach resonance at 19.849 G.
