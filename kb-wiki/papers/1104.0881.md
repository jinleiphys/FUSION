# The Rapidity Renormalization Group

> We introduce a systematic approach for the resummation of perturbative series which involve large logarithms not only due to large invariant mass ratios but large rapidities as well. Series of this form can appear in a variety of gauge theory observables. The formalism is utilized to calculate the jet broadening event shape in a systematic fashion to next to leading logarithmic order. An operator definition of the factorized cross section as well as a closed form of the next-to leading log cross section are presented. The result agrees with the data to within errors.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a systematic renormalization group framework (the Rapidity Renormalization Group) to resum perturbative series containing large logarithms from both large invariant mass ratios and large rapidities, and applies it to jet broadening at next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) order; the abstract's claim that "the result agrees with the data to within errors" glosses over the fact that the NLL calculation leaves sizable theory uncertainties and that the agreement is only "reasonable" given large experimental error bars, with NNLL corrections expected to reduce theory errors considerably.

## Method

The authors formulate a factorization theorem for the jet broadening event shape in Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET), where the cross section is written as a convolution of hard, jet, and soft functions. To regulate rapidity divergences that arise when soft and collinear modes have comparable invariant masses, they introduce a new regulator parameter η and an associated arbitrary scale ν, analogous to the dimensional regularization scale μ, and derive renormalization group equations in ν (the rapidity scale) alongside the standard μ evolution. The resummation is performed in Laplace transform space, using two-loop cusp anomalous dimensions, and the NLL resummed cross section for total jet broadening is compared to e⁺e⁻ data at 130 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The NLO singular cross section for total jet broadening is given by dσ/dB_T = σ₀ (α_s(μ) C_F)/(π B_T) (-3 - 4 log B_T), where σ₀ is the Born cross section.
- The NLL resummed cross section is dσ/dB_T = (σ₀/B_T) [U_H(Q², μ_Q, μ) / (Γ(2ω_s) e^{2γ_E ω_s})] (Q B_T/μ)^{2ω_s}, with ω_s = 2 Γ_c[α_s(μ)] log(ν/ν₀).
- The soft function rapidity anomalous dimension in Laplace space is γ_ν^S(b,b') = -2 Γ_c [log(b μ e^{γ_E}) + log(b' μ e^{γ_E})].
- The jet function at one loop contributes singular terms proportional to ln(√s e₀/(2μ)) [3 + 4 ln(ν/Q^±)].
- Non-perturbative power corrections in the small B_T region scale as Λ_QCD/(B_T Q) and are estimated at 20-30%.

## Context

This work extends the SCET factorization framework (Bauer et al., 2001; Bauer, Fleming, Pirjol, Stewart, 2002) to observables where soft and collinear modes have comparable invariant masses, a regime where previous factorization proofs for angularity observables (Bauer et al., 2008) were known to fail as the parameter a approaches 1 (jet broadening). The rapidity renormalization group provides a systematic method to resum rapidity logarithms that were previously handled with ad hoc regulators, enabling precision calculations for jet broadening and other observables such as transverse momentum distributions. The authors note that the pioneering work on jet broadening resummations (Catani et al., 1992) used the coherent branching formalism and that later work (Salam, 1999) identified neglected recoil effects; the present formalism addresses these issues and can be systematically improved to NNLL order for precision α_s determinations, analogous to thrust analyses (Abbate et al., 2011).
