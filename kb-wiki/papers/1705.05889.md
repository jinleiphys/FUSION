# First simultaneous extraction of spin-dependent parton distributions and fragmentation functions from a global QCD analysis

> We perform the first global QCD analysis of polarized inclusive and semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering and single-inclusive $e^+e^-$ annihilation data, fitting simultaneously the parton distribution and fragmentation functions using the iterative Monte Carlo method. Without imposing SU(3) symmetry relations, we find the strange polarization to be very small, consistent with zero for both inclusive and semi-inclusive data, which provides a resolution to the strange quark polarization puzzle. The combined analysis also allows the direct extraction from data of the isovector and octet axial charges, and is consistent with a small SU(2) flavor asymmetry of the polarized sea.

## Key claim
The paper reports the first simultaneous global QCD analysis of polarized inclusive and semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (DIS and SIDIS) and single-inclusive \(e^+e^-\) annihilation (SIA) data to extract both spin-dependent parton distribution functions (PDFs) and fragmentation functions (FFs). The key result is that the strange quark polarization is very small and consistent with zero when the SU(3) flavor symmetry constraint on the octet axial charge is not imposed, resolving the "strange quark polarization puzzle."

## Method
The analysis uses the iterative Monte Carlo (IMC) method at next-to-leading order (NLO) in the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme, fitting polarized PDFs (\(\Delta u^+\), \(\Delta d^+\), \(\Delta s^+\), \(\Delta \bar{u}\), \(\Delta \bar{d}\), \(\Delta g\)) and FFs for \(\pi^\pm\) and \(K^\pm\) simultaneously to 1855 data points from DIS, SIDIS, and SIA. Template functions parameterize PDFs and FFs at the input scale \(Q_0^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\), with kinematic cuts \(Q^2 > 1\) GeV\(^2\) and \(W^2 > 10\) GeV\(^2\) to avoid higher-twist contributions.

## Key numbers
- Total \(\chi^2\) per datum for the combined fit: \(1969.7 / 1855 \approx 1.06\).
- For SIDIS \(\pi^\pm\) data: \(\chi^2\) per datum = \(68.5 / 80\).
- For SIDIS \(K^\pm\) data: \(\chi^2\) per datum = \(49.3 / 71\).
- Strange quark first moment: \(\Delta s^+(Q_0^2) = -0.03(10)\).
- Octet axial charge: \(a_8 = 0.46(21)\), compared to the SU(3) symmetric value \(0.586(31)\).
- Triplet axial charge: \(a_3 = 1.24(4)\), in agreement with the neutron weak decay value \(g_A = 1.269(3)\).
- Total spin carried by quarks and antiquarks: \(\Delta \Sigma(Q_0^2) = 0.36(9)\).
- Isovector sea polarization: \(\Delta \bar{u} - \Delta \bar{d} = 0.05(8)\).
- For the HERMES deuteron \(K^-\) data, \(\chi^2\) increases from 5.7 to 18.5 for 9 data points when using a negative \(\Delta s^+\) from the JAM15 analysis.

## Context
This work extends the JAM15 analysis (Ethier et al., 2015) of inclusive DIS by adding SIDIS and SIA data to simultaneously constrain PDFs and FFs, and contrasts with earlier global fits such as DSSV09 (de Florian et al., 2009) and LSS10 (Leader et al., 2010) that imposed SU(3) symmetry and found a negative strange polarization. The results enable future studies of SU(3) breaking in the octet axial charge, improved determinations of sea quark asymmetries, and extensions to NNLO and small-\(x\) corrections, as well as combined analyses of unpolarized and polarized PDFs.
