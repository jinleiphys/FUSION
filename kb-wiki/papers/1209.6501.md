# Low Energy Neutron Production by Inverse-beta decay in Metallic Hydride Surfaces

> It has been recently argued that inverse-beta nuclear transmutations might occur at an impressively high rate in a thin layer at the metallic hydride surface under specific conditions. In this note we present a calculation of the transmutation rate which shows that there is little room for such a remarkable effect.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that the inverse-beta decay rate of electrons on protons in metallic hydride surfaces is about 300 times smaller than previously claimed by Widom and collaborators, and that the required electron mass renormalization factor needed to reach threshold is likely unattainable for bound electrons in realistic condensed matter conditions.

## Method

The authors compute the decay rate for the reaction \(\tilde{e}p \to n\nu_e\) using two independent methods: first, by treating the electron-proton bound state analogously to the Van Royen-Weisskopf calculation of pion decay in the quark model, with the decay amplitude derived from the Fermi Lagrangian via Fierz transformation; second, by using the standard formula \(\Gamma = |\psi(0)|^2 v \sigma\) relating the decay rate to the wavefunction at the origin and the unpolarized cross section for electron capture. Both methods yield the same result, which includes the mandatory factor \(|\psi(0)|^2\) that was absent in the earlier work.

## Key numbers

- Threshold mass renormalization factor: \(\beta_0 = (M_n - M_p)/m_e \approx 2.59\)
- For \(\beta = 20\), the corrected decay rate is \(\Gamma = 1.8 \times 10^{-3}\) Hz, compared to the earlier claimed value of 0.56 Hz (a factor of about 300 smaller)
- For a more moderate value \(\beta = 5.2\) (approximately \(2\beta_0\)), the decay rate is \(\Gamma = 6.9 \times 10^{-7}\) Hz
- Using the plasma frequency \(\Omega_p = 0.1\) eV (a current estimate), the calculated \(\beta\) is 1.01, below threshold
- Using \(\Omega_p = 0.8\) eV (the value used by Widom and collaborators), the calculated \(\beta\) is 1.8, still below threshold

## Context

This paper directly contradicts the claims of Widom and collaborators (2012) that low-energy neutrons could be produced at high rates on metallic hydride surfaces via inverse-beta decay. The authors show that the earlier calculation omitted the essential factor \(|\psi(0)|^2\) (the probability density for electron and proton to be at the same point), which reduces the rate by orders of magnitude. The work extends the standard treatment of electron K-capture and positronium decay (Landau and Lifshitz) to this specific reaction, and suggests that a more detailed analysis of attainable \(\beta\) values is needed before any definite conclusions can be drawn about this phenomenon.
