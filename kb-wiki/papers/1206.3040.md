# Quasifission at extreme sub-barrier energies

> With the quantum diffusion approach the behavior of the capture cross-section is investigated in the reactions $^{92,94}$Mo + $^{92,94}$Mo, $^{100}$Ru + $^{100}$Ru, $^{104}$Pd + $^{104}$Pd, and $^{78}$Kr + $^{112}$Sn at deep sub-barrier energies which are lower than the ground state energies of the compound nuclei. Because the capture cross section is the sum of the complete fusion and quasifission cross sections, and the complete fusion cross section is zero at these sub-barrier energies, one can study experimentally the unique quasifission process in these reactions after the capture.

## Key claim

At bombarding energies below the ground-state energy of the compound nucleus (E_c.m. < -Q), the complete fusion cross section is exactly zero, so the measured capture cross section consists purely of quasifission. This allows experimental isolation of the quasifission process without contamination from fusion-fission, provided the chosen reaction has a sufficiently small difference between the Coulomb barrier height and the ground-state energy (V_b + Q).

## Method

The authors applied the quantum diffusion approach, treating the collision of two nuclei with a single collective variable (the relative distance) and including fluctuation and dissipation effects. The nucleus-nucleus potential was calculated using a double-folding formalism with a Skyrme-type density-dependent effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, and nuclear deformations were incorporated through quadrupole deformation parameters and mutual orientations. Capture cross sections were computed for five symmetric or near-symmetric reactions at deep sub-barrier energies where E_c.m. < -Q.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction $^{92}$Mo + $^{92}$Mo, the sub-barrier energy relative to the Coulomb barrier is V_b - E_c.m.^0 = 13 MeV, and the calculated capture cross section at E_c.m. = E_c.m.^0 is σ_cap = 0.2 nb.
- For $^{94}$Mo + $^{94}$Mo, V_b - E_c.m.^0 = 19.4 MeV, and σ_cap at E_c.m.^0 = 5.1 nb.
- For $^{104}$Pd + $^{104}$Pd, V_b - E_c.m.^0 = 14.8 MeV, and σ_cap at E_c.m.^0 = 0.7 mb.
- For $^{100}$Ru + $^{100}$Ru, V_b - E_c.m.^0 = 21.8 MeV, and σ_cap at E_c.m.^0 = 24.4 μb.
- For $^{78}$Kr + $^{112}$Sn, V_b - E_c.m.^0 = 18 MeV, and σ_cap at E_c.m.^0 = 2.3 μb.

## Context

This work extends the quantum diffusion approach previously applied by the same group (Sargsyan et al., EPJSub1, EPJSub2) to study sub-barrier capture in medium-mass systems, now focusing on the extreme sub-barrier regime where complete fusion is energetically forbidden. The paper identifies $^{104}$Pd + $^{104}$Pd, $^{100}$Ru + $^{100}$Ru, and $^{78}$Kr + $^{112}$Sn as optimal candidates for future experiments to measure pure quasifission, which would provide cleaner information than higher-energy data where fusion-fission competes (as noted in earlier work by Volkov, Schroder, and others). The results enable experimental tests of quasifission dynamics at very low angular momenta (J < 10) and low excitation energies.
