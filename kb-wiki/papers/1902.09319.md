# Empirical Evidence of Isospin Memory in Compound Nuclear Fission

> We present empirical evidence of isospin dependence in the compound nuclear fission cross-sections and fission widths, which suggests that the compound nucleus (CN) possibly retains the memory of the isospin when it is formed. We examine the idea, first proposed by Yadrovsky [1], for three pairs of reactions where experimental data of fission cross section at various excitation energies are available. One of the pairs of reactions is the same as used by Yadrovsky i.e. $^{209}$Bi($p$, f) and $^{206}$Pb($α$, f) leading to the CN $^{210}$Po but with an improved experimental data set. The other two pairs of reaction sets are, $^{185}$Re($p$, f) and $^{182}$W($α$, f) leading to the CN $^{186}$Os and, $^{205}$Tl($p$, f) and $^{202}$Hg($α$, f) leading to the CN $^{206}$Pb. An observable difference between the fission branching ratios in two different isospin states suggests that the CN seems to remember its isospin at the point of formation. This possibility is further supported by another method, where additional empirical evidence for four CN, viz. $^{210}$Po, $^{209}$Bi, $^{207}$Bi, and $^{198}$Hg, is obtained from the experimental data in Zhukova et al. [2]. Further, the data also suggest a possible new signature of the weakening of CN process and gradual transition to non-compound processes as the energy rises. Fresh experimental efforts as proposed, are required to confirm these findings.

## Key claim

The paper presents empirical evidence that the compound nucleus (CN) "possibly retains the memory of the isospin when it is formed," based on observed differences in fission branching ratios from two different isospin states of the same CN. The authors hedge by stating this conclusion is "a distinct possibility requiring further experimentation and analysis," and note that the results are valid only up to excitation energies where fission proceeds mainly via compound nuclear processes.

## Method

The authors analyzed three pairs of proton and alpha induced reactions leading to the same compound nucleus but populated in different isospin states: 209Bi(p,f) and 206Pb(alpha,f) forming 210Po; 185Re(p,f) and 182W(alpha,f) forming 186Os; and 205Tl(p,f) and 202Hg(alpha,f) forming 206Pb. They calculated fission branching ratios for the two isospin states using experimental fission cross-sections and CN formation cross-sections from the PACE4 statistical code. In a second empirical approach, they directly used fissility data from Zhukova et al. for four compound nuclei (210Po, 209Bi, 207Bi, 198Hg) without PACE4 calculations.

## Key numbers

* For 210Po at excitation energy 27.5 MeV: fission branching ratio for T0+1/2 state = 6.14 x 10^-5, for T0-1/2 state = 9.67 x 10^-6 (difference of order 10)
* For 210Po at excitation energy 28.6 MeV: fission branching ratio for T0+1/2 state = 3.96 x 10^-4, for T0-1/2 state = 2.83 x 10^-5
* For 210Po, crossing of fission cross-sections occurs near excitation energy approximately 29 MeV, where the fission branching ratio for T0+1/2 becomes negative
* For 186Os, the fission branching ratio for T0+1/2 is approximately ten times greater than for T0-1/2 state below the crossing near excitation energy 40 MeV
* For 206Pb, below excitation energy 50-60 MeV, there is a difference of order one hundred between fission branching ratios from the two isospin states
* The ratio of alpha to proton fissility was found to be about 0.5 at lower energies, becoming 1 around 28-30 MeV and more than 1 at higher energies

## Context

This work extends the idea first proposed by Yadrovsky (1975) for 209Bi(p,f) and 206Pb(alpha,f) reactions forming 210Po, using improved experimental data sets from Ignatyuk et al. and Moretto et al. The authors had previously applied isospin conservation to reproduce fission fragment distributions in heavy ion and thermal neutron induced reactions (Jain, Swati, 2015-2018). The results support theoretical work by Lane and Soper (1962) showing isospin may remain a good quantum number in heavy neutron-rich nuclei, and recent calculations by Loc, Auerbach and Colo showing isospin mixing remains very small in heavy nuclei. The observed crossing where fission branching ratios become negative provides a possible new signature of the transition from compound nuclear to non-compound processes at higher energies.
