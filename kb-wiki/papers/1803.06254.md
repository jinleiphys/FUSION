# Superfluid liquid crystals: pasta phases in neutron star crusts

> The pasta phases predicted to occur near the inner boundary of the crust of a neutron star resemble liquid crystals, a smectic A in the case of sheet-like nuclei (lasagna) and the columnar phase in the case of rod-like nuclei (spaghetti). An important difference compared with usual liquid crystals is that the nucleons are superfluid. We develop the hydrodynamic equations for this system and use them to study collective oscillations. Nucleon superfluidity leads to important qualitative differences in the spectra of these oscillations and also increases their frequencies compared with ordinary liquid crystals. We discuss a number of directions for future work.

# Superfluid liquid crystals: pasta phases in neutron star crusts

## Key claim

The pasta phases (lasagna and spaghetti) in neutron star crusts behave like superfluid liquid crystals, where nucleon superfluidity leads to qualitatively different collective oscillation spectra compared to ordinary liquid crystals, with the key difference being that the structure does not move with the local velocity of the matter, allowing permeation (counterflow) that prevents mode velocities from vanishing for certain wave vector directions.

## Method

The authors developed hydrodynamic equations for the pasta phases by treating them as liquid crystals (smectic A for lasagna, columnar for spaghetti) with superfluid neutrons and protons, using a two-fluid model with an anisotropic normal density tensor. They linearized the equations of motion around a uniform equilibrium state, assumed incompressibility to isolate the effects of superfluidity on low-frequency modes, and derived dispersion relations for collective oscillations by combining the continuity equation, momentum conservation, and the Josephson relation for the superfluid phase.

## Key numbers

- The Debye screening length of electrons is given by λ_D ≈ (π/4α)^(1/2) ħ/p_Fe, where α = e^2/ħc is the fine structure constant and p_Fe is the electron Fermi momentum
- The coherence length of nucleon superfluids is ~ ħv/πΔ, where v is the nucleon Fermi velocity and Δ the pairing gap
- For lasagna, the second sound velocity squared is c_2^2 = B(n/n^n - cos^2θ) cos^2θ, where θ is the angle between wave vector and sheet normal, B is an elastic constant, n is total density, and n^n is normal density
- For spaghetti, the second sound velocity squared is c_2^2 = B(n/n^n) sin^2θ (1 - (n^n/n) sin^2θ), where sinθ = k_x/k
- For spaghetti, the transverse mode velocity squared is c_t^2 = (B_t/(m n^n)) sin^2θ
- The heat capacity due to collective modes varies as T^2 for lasagna and T^(5/2) for spaghetti at low temperatures, larger than ordinary phonons (T^3)

## Context

This work extends the theory of collective oscillations in pasta phases beyond the assumption of undistorted pasta (DiGallo, Oertel, and Urban) by incorporating elastic deformations of the nuclear structures. It builds on the liquid crystal theory of de Gennes (1969) and Martin, Parodi, and Pershan, and generalizes the two-fluid hydrodynamics of superfluid neutron star matter (CJP, Chamel, and Reddy) to anisotropic pasta phases. The results enable future calculations of heat capacity, mechanical properties, and gravitational wave emission from rotating neutron stars, though numerical predictions require improved calculations of elastic constants (CJP and Potekhin) and superfluid densities (Watanabe and CJP).
