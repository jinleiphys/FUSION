# Forward & Far-Forward Heavy Hadrons with JETHAD: A High-energy Viewpoint

> Inspired by the recent finding that semi-inclusive detections of heavy hadrons exhibit fair stabilization patterns in high-energy resummed distributions against (missing) higher-order corrections, we review and extend our studies on the hadroproduction of light and heavy hadrons tagged in forward and far-forward rapidity ranges. We analyze the NLL/NLO+ behavior of rapidity rates and angular multiplicities via the JETHAD method, where the resummation of next-to-leading energy logarithms and beyond is consistently embodied in the collinear picture. We explore kinematic regions that are within LHC typical acceptances, as well as novel sectors accessible thanks the combined tagging of a far-forward light or heavy hadron at future Forward Physics Facilities and a of central particle at LHC experiments via a precise timing-coincidence setup.

## Key claim

The paper claims that semi-inclusive detections of heavy hadrons exhibit "natural stability" in high-energy resummed distributions against missing higher-order corrections, a feature that emerges specifically when using variable-flavor number scheme collinear fragmentation functions for heavy-flavored particles, and that this stabilization is a basic property connected to the inclusive emission of any heavy-flavored particle.

## Method

The author employs the JETHAD method, a hybrid high-energy and collinear factorization framework based on the Balitsky-Fadin-Kuraev-Lipatov (BFKL) formalism, to compute next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL/NLO+) rapidity rates and angular multiplicities for forward-plus-backward hadroproduction of identified hadrons. The analysis considers two rapidity configurations: symmetric tagging of both particles within current LHC detector acceptances, and asymmetric tagging where a light or charmed hadron is detected at future Forward Physics Facilities while a bottomed hadron is simultaneously detected in the barrel of an LHC detector via a timing-coincidence setup.

## Key numbers

* Rapidity-interval rates and angular distributions are computed at NLL/NLO+ accuracy for processes involving a charged pion or charged D* meson as the first hadron and a singly b-flavored particle (sum of noncharmed B mesons and Lambda_b^0 baryons) as the second hadron.
* The analysis covers kinematic regions within LHC typical acceptances at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV, referencing CMS data from Khachatryan (2016) and CMS (2021) for Mueller-Navelet jet studies.
* The asymmetric FPF+LHC configuration accesses very large rapidity intervals, where the interplay between BFKL and threshold resummations becomes relevant, with the impact of double BFKL-plus-threshold logarithms for central Higgs-boson rates being small at LHC energies but becoming "quite relevant" at the nominal energies of the Future Circular Collider.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies on semi-hard hadroproduction of light and heavy hadrons, including analyses of Lambda_c baryons (Celiberto, 2021), singly bottomed hadrons (Celiberto, 2021), vector quarkonia (Celiberto, 2022-2024), B_c mesons (Celiberto, 2022-2024), and XQq tetraquarks (Celiberto, 2023). It builds upon the Mueller-Navelet framework for light jets (Mueller, 1986) and the hybrid factorization approach developed for light-flavored di-jet and hadron-plus-jet studies (Celiberto, 2015-2020). The proposed FPF+LHC coincidence setup takes inspiration from a prospective FPF+ATLAS study (Celiberto, 2022) within the Forward Physics Facilities program (Anchordoqui, 2021; Feng, 2022), and enables future investigations of the connection between BFKL and threshold resummations, as well as QCD studies at future facilities including the Electron-Ion Collider and Future Circular Collider.
