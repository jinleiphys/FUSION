# Kaon Photoproduction in a Confining and Covariant Diquark Model

> Baryons are modeled as bound states of scalar or axialvector diquarks and a constituent quark which interact through quark exchange. This description results as an approximation to the relativistic Faddeev equation for three quarks. The corresponding effective Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved, and fully four-dimensional wave functions for both octet and decuplet baryons are obtained. Confinement of quarks and diquarks is incorporated by modifying their propagators. Results are given for kaon photoproduction which is one of the applications currently under investigation in this model.

## Key claim

Kaon photoproduction near threshold can be described within a covariant diquark–quark model of baryons, but the total cross section depends very little on the details of the baryon wave functions and instead shows a pronounced dependence on how confinement is parameterized into the quark and diquark propagators.

## Method

The authors approximate the relativistic Faddeev equation for three quarks by treating baryons as bound states of a constituent quark and either scalar or axialvector diquarks, with the attractive interaction provided by quark exchange. They solve the resulting four-dimensional homogeneous Bethe–Salpeter equation in ladder approximation, incorporating confinement by modifying the quark and diquark propagators. For kaon photoproduction, they compute the dominant diagrams in which only the scalar diquark contributes to the proton–lambda overlap and the kaon does not couple to the diquark.

## Key numbers

- Octet and decuplet masses calculated with two parameter sets: Set I (weakly confining propagators) gives M_Λ = 1.123 GeV, M_Σ = 1.134 GeV, M_Ξ = 1.307 GeV, M_Σ* = 1.373 GeV, M_Ξ* = 1.545 GeV, M_Ω = 1.692 GeV; Set II (strongly confining propagators) gives M_Λ = 1.133 GeV, M_Σ = 1.140 GeV, M_Ξ = 1.319 GeV, M_Σ* = 1.380 GeV, M_Ξ* = 1.516 GeV, M_Ω = 1.665 GeV; experimental values are 1.116, 1.193, 1.315, 1.384, 1.530, 1.672 GeV respectively.
- Constituent quark masses used: m_u = 0.5 GeV for both sets; m_s = 0.65 GeV (Set I) and 0.63 GeV (Set II).
- The total cross section for pγ → KΛ is reproduced nicely for photon energies E < 1.5 GeV, but the model results do not fall off fast enough for higher energies.
- The lambda polarization is calculated for the energy range 0.9–1.1 GeV; the model reproduces the change in sign but falls short of experimental values.

## Context

This work extends the covariant diquark–quark model of baryons developed by the same group (Oettel 1998) to the reaction pγ → KΛ, which has been measured with high precision by the SAPHIR collaboration. The approach contrasts with isobar resonance models (Hab 1999, Tia 1999) and coupled channel calculations (Kai 1999) that describe data well but do not connect to baryon substructure. The results enable future studies that combine this model with Dyson–Schwinger approaches to constrain the nonperturbative quark propagator in the timelike region, and can be extended to strangeness production in proton–proton collisions.
