# Gauge-invariant two- and three- density correlators

> Gauge-invariant spatial correlations between two and three quarks inside a hadron are measured within quenched and unquenched QCD. These correlators provide information on the shape and multipole moments of the pion, the rho, the nucleon and the $Δ$.

# Gauge-invariant two- and three-density correlators

## Key claim

Gauge-invariant spatial correlations between two and three quarks inside hadrons, measured in both quenched and unquenched QCD, provide information on hadron shape and multipole moments; however, the nucleon shows no statistically significant deformation despite intrinsic deformation possibly present for a given background gauge field, because as a spin-1/2 particle any intrinsic deformation averages to zero over the gauge configuration ensemble.

## Method

Equal-time gauge-invariant density-density correlators were computed on the lattice using Wilson fermions, with charge density operator $\Gamma=\gamma_0$ and matter density operator $\Gamma={\bf 1}$. For mesons, two-density correlators were evaluated; for baryons, three-density correlators involving two relative distances were computed using fast Fourier transforms, with the all-to-all propagator contribution checked to be small by comparing integrated one-particle densities with two-density correlators. Simulations were performed in the quenched approximation at $\beta=6.0$ on a $16^3\times32$ lattice, and with two degenerate flavors of dynamical Wilson fermions at similar lattice spacing.

## Key numbers

- Quenched simulation at $\beta=6.0$ on $16^3\times32$ lattice with $\kappa=0.153$ ($m_\pi/m_\rho=0.81$, 220 configurations) and $\kappa=0.154$ ($m_\pi/m_\rho=0.78$, 220 configurations)
- Unquenched simulation with two dynamical flavors at $\kappa_{sea}=0.156$ ($m_\pi/m_\rho=0.83$, 150 configurations) and $\kappa_{sea}=0.157$ ($m_\pi/m_\rho=0.76$, 200 configurations)
- Pion form factor vector dominance mass extracted: 0.57 GeV at $\kappa=0.153$, 0.59 GeV at $\kappa=0.1554$, 0.61 GeV at $\kappa=0.155; linear extrapolation in $m_\pi^2$ gives $m\sim0.65$ GeV, within 15% of the rho meson mass
- Three-density charge correlator computed in quenched approximation using 30 configurations at $\kappa=0.15$ and $\kappa=0.154$

## Context

This work extends the approach of Green (2001) by providing gauge-invariant density correlators (unlike Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes) for both quenched and unquenched QCD, enabling direct comparison with experimental photoproduction measurements of nucleon and $\Delta$ deformation reported by the Athens group. The method enables extraction of form factors via the Wilcox (1997) relation and allows investigation of pion cloud contributions to hadron deformation, with the all-to-all propagator technique from Alexandrou, de Forcrand, and Tsapalis (2000) used to check systematic effects. The results enable future studies with lighter quarks and larger lattices to reliably extrapolate to the chiral limit.
