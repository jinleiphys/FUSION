# Delta properties in the rainbow-ladder truncation of Dyson-Schwinger equations

> We present a calculation of the three-quark core contribution to nucleon and Delta-baryon masses and Delta electromagnetic form factors in a Poincare-covariant Faddeev approach. A consistent setup for the dressed-quark propagator, the quark-quark, quark-'diquark' and quark-photon interactions is employed, where all ingredients are solutions of their respective Dyson-Schwinger or Bethe-Salpeter equations in a rainbow-ladder truncation. The resulting Delta electromagnetic form factors concur with present experimental and lattice data.

## Key claim

The rainbow-ladder truncation of Dyson-Schwinger equations, when implemented with an appropriate input scale, produces consistently overestimated "quark-core" contributions to nucleon and Delta masses; these core masses are expected to be reduced by 20-30% through chiral (pion-cloud) corrections, while the Delta electromagnetic form factors show good agreement with lattice data and quark model analyses.

## Method

The authors solved a quark-diquark Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) for the Delta baryon, where all ingredients (dressed-quark propagator, diquark amplitudes, and diquark propagators) were obtained from their respective Dyson-Schwinger or Bethe-Salpeter equations in a rainbow-ladder truncation. The effective quark-gluon coupling used a frequently employed ansatz with an infrared scale Lambda and width parameter eta, and the electromagnetic current was constructed via impulse approximation with seagull terms and transverse rho-meson pole contributions to satisfy Ward-Takahashi identities.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass at physical u/d-quark mass (fixed-scale model, Lambda=0.72 GeV): M_N = 0.94 GeV
- Delta mass at physical u/d-quark mass (fixed-scale model): M_Delta = 1.28 GeV
- Quark-core nucleon mass (core model, Lambda=0.98 GeV): M_N^core = 1.26(2) GeV
- Quark-core Delta mass (core model): M_Delta^core = 1.73(5) GeV
- Delta+ magnetic moment: G_M1(0) = 3.64(16)
- Delta+ electric quadrupole moment: G_E2(0) = -1.32(16)
- Delta+ magnetic octupole moment: G_M3(0) = -0.26(4)
- Electric quadrupole form factor zero-crossing at Q^2/M_Delta^2 ~ 0.6

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Dyson-Schwinger studies of nucleon and Delta properties (Nicmorus 2008, Eichmann 2008) by providing a consistent rainbow-ladder treatment of all ingredients. The approach identifies the rainbow-ladder truncation with the "quark core" of chiral effective field theory, following Eichmann (2008), where pion-cloud corrections are expected to reduce the overestimated core masses. The results enable future studies of the N-Delta-gamma transition and can be improved by implementing explicit chiral corrections or solving the full three-quark Faddeev equation without the diquark approximation.
