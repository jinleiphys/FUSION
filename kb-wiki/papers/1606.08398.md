# Axial anomaly and energy dependence of hyperon polarization in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We address the issue of energy and charge dependence of global polarization of $Λ$ hyperons in peripheral $Au-Au$ collisions recently observed by the STAR collaboration at RHIC. We compare different contributions to the anomalous mechanism relating polarization to vorticity and hydrodynamic helicity in QCD matter. We stress that the suppression of the gravitational anomaly contribution in strongly correlated matter observed in lattice simulations confirms our earlier prediction of rapid decrease of polarization with increasing collision energy. Our mechanism leads to polarization of $\bar Λ$ of the same sign and larger magnitude than the polarization of $Λ$. The energy and charge dependence of polarization is suggested as a sensitive probe of fine details of QCD matter structure.

## Key claim

The observed decrease of Λ hyperon polarization with increasing collision energy in peripheral Au-Au collisions is explained by the suppression of the gravitational anomaly contribution (proportional to T²) in strongly correlated QCD matter, combined with the rapid decrease of the strange chemical potential at higher energies; the mechanism predicts Λ̅ polarization of the same sign but larger magnitude than Λ polarization.

## Method

The authors relate the strange axial charge Q₅ˢ, induced by the axial anomaly through the triangle diagram, to the hydrodynamic helicity H = ∫ d³x (v · w) where v is velocity and w is vorticity. They use the mean-value theorem to express the axial charge as Q₅ˢ = (⟨μ²γ²⟩ N_c H)/(2π²), then convert this classical charge to a quantum average polarization per Λ hyperon by dividing by the number of Λ particles and applying Lorentz transformations from the laboratory frame to the hyperon rest frame.

## Key numbers

- The average polarization was first roughly estimated by dividing Q₅ˢ by the number of Λ hyperons, leading to a value of about 1%, later confirmed by more detailed simulations compatible with STAR data.
- The chiral vorticity coefficient is c_V = (μ_s² + μ_A²)/(2π²) + T²/6, where the T²/6 term (gravitational anomaly contribution) is suppressed by one order of magnitude at high temperatures in lattice simulations.
- The NICA energy range is specified as √s_NN = 4–11 GeV, where the assumption that Λ mass is large relative to its momentum provides a lower bound estimate of polarization.

## Context

This work extends the earlier prediction of polarization decrease with energy from Rogachevsky (2010) and the anomalous mechanism relating polarization to vorticity and helicity from Baznat (2013). It addresses the preliminary STAR collaboration results from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan (Lisa, 2016) showing decreasing polarization with energy. The suppression of the gravitational anomaly contribution in strongly correlated matter, observed in lattice simulations by Braguta (2016), is used to explain the rapid decrease of polarization, and the mechanism enables using energy and charge dependence of polarization as a probe of QCD matter structure at future facilities like NICA and FAIR.
