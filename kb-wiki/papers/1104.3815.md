# Charm diffusion in a pion gas implementing unitarity, chiral and heavy quark symmetries

> We compute the charm drag and diffusion coefficients in a hot pion gas, such as is formed in a Heavy Ion Collision after the system cools sufficiently to transit into the hadron phase. We fully exploit Heavy Quark Effective Theory (with both D and D* mesons as elementary degrees of freedom during the collision) and Chiral Perturbation Theory, and employ standard unitarization to reach higher temperatures. We find that a certain friction and shear diffusion coefficients are almost p-independent at fixed temperature which simplifies phenomenological analysis. At the higher end of reliability of our calculation, T about 150 MeV, we report a charm relaxation length of some 40 fm, in agreement with the model estimate of He, Fries and Rapp. The momentum of a 1-GeV charm quark decreases about 50 MeV per Fermi when crossing the hadron phase.

## Key claim

The authors compute charm drag and diffusion coefficients in a hot pion gas using both Chiral Perturbation Theory and Heavy Quark Effective Theory, and extend the calculation to higher temperatures by implementing unitarization. The abstract's claim of "almost p-independent" coefficients is qualified: the friction and shear diffusion coefficients are nearly independent of the squared momentum \(p^2\) at fixed temperature, not of momentum itself.

## Method

The authors construct a leading-order and next-to-leading-order chiral Lagrangian for \(D\) and \(D^*\) mesons interacting with pions, incorporating heavy quark symmetry constraints. They compute tree-level scattering amplitudes for \(D\pi \to D\pi\), \(D^*\pi \to D\pi\), \(D\pi \to D^*\pi\), and \(D^*\pi \to D^*\pi\) channels, then unitarize these amplitudes to extend their validity to higher temperatures. The transport coefficients (drag force \(F\), and diffusion coefficients \(\Gamma_0\) and \(\Gamma_1\)) are obtained by integrating the scattering amplitudes over the pion thermal distribution within the Fokker-Planck formalism, treating both \(D\) and \(D^*\) mesons as stable propagating degrees of freedom in the hadron gas.

## Key numbers

- Charm relaxation length \(\lambda_c \simeq 40\) fm at temperature \(T \simeq 150\) MeV, in agreement with the model estimate of He, Fries and Rapp.
- The momentum of a 1-GeV charm quark decreases by about 50 MeV per Fermi when crossing the hadron phase.
- The \(D\) meson mass is taken as \(M_D \simeq 1867\) MeV (charge-averaged), and the \(D^*\) meson mass as \(M_{D^*} = 2008.5\) MeV.
- The \(D^*\) meson vacuum width is estimated at 1 MeV (charged) and \(\Gamma \le 2\) MeV (neutral), corresponding to a mean lifetime of order 100-200 fm.
- The \(D_0\) resonance mass is \(2360 \pm 40\) MeV with width \(270 \pm 50\) MeV; the \(D_1(2430)\) resonance mass is \(2427 \pm 40\) MeV with width \(380 \pm 150\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative approach of Laine (2011) by adding unitarization to reach higher temperatures, and improves upon the phenomenological cross-section estimates of He, Fries and Rapp (2011) by using a theoretically grounded effective Lagrangian. It also differs from Ghosh et al. (2011) by including unitarity constraints to avoid overestimating cross-sections at higher energies. The results enable more reliable input for hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions, particularly for understanding how the hadron phase affects charm quark observables such as nuclear suppression factors and elliptic flow.
