# Complex heavy-quark potential and Debye mass in a gluonic medium from lattice QCD

> We improve and extend our study of the complex in-medium heavy quark potential and its Debye mass $m_D$ in a gluonic medium with a finer scan around the deconfinement transition and newly generated ensembles closer to the thermodynamic limit. On the lattices with larger physical volume, Re[V] shows signs of screening, i.e. a finite $m_D$, only in the deconfined phase, reminiscent of a genuine phase transition. Consistently Im[V] exhibits nonzero values also only above $T_C$. We compare the behavior of Re[V] with the color singlet free-energies that have been used historically to extract the Debye mass. An effective coupling constant is computed to assess the residual influence of the confining part of the potential at $T>0$. Our previous finding of a gradual screening of Re[V] around $T_C$ on finer lattices is critically reassessed and interpreted to originate from finite volume artifacts that affect the deployed $β=7$, $ξ_b=3.5$ parameter set at $N_s=32$.

## Key claim

The complex in-medium heavy quark potential and its Debye mass show screening behavior only in the deconfined phase on lattices with larger physical volume, contradicting the authors' previous finding of gradual screening around the critical temperature on finer lattices, which is now reinterpreted as originating from finite volume artifacts.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations of quenched SU(3) Yang-Mills theory using the naive Wilson plaquette action on anisotropic lattices with inverse coupling β=6.1 and bare anisotropy ξb=3.2108, corresponding to a spatial lattice spacing of a_s=0.097 fm and a physical volume of L=3.1 fm. They extracted the complex in-medium heavy quark potential from a Bayesian spectral reconstruction of Wilson line correlators in Coulomb gauge, fitting the lowest-lying skewed-Lorentzian peak in the reconstructed spectrum to obtain the real and imaginary parts. The Debye mass was determined by fitting the real part of the potential to a generalized Gauss-law ansatz that combines a Coulombic contribution with a string contribution, using a complex permittivity from hard-thermal loop perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

* Debye mass at T=1.4 T_C: m_D = 603 ± 24 MeV
* Debye mass at T=1.27 T_C: m_D = 430 ± 42 MeV
* Debye mass at T=1.08 T_C: m_D = 273 ± 72 MeV
* Debye mass at T ≈ 1.0 T_C: m_D = 150 ± 16 MeV
* Debye mass at T=0.93 T_C: m_D = 14 ± 8 MeV
* Debye mass at T=0.88 T_C and below: m_D = 0 ± 10 MeV (consistent with zero)
* Vacuum parameters from Cornell fit: α_S = 0.272 ± 0.016, σ = 0.215 ± 0.003 GeV², c = 1.38 ± 0.014 GeV
* Lattice spacing: a_s = 0.097 fm = 4 a_τ
* Critical temperature: T_C^{β=6.1} = 290 MeV
* Physical volume: L = 3.1 fm
* Non-perturbative fit parameters for m_D: κ_1 = 1.97 ± 0.44, κ_2 = -1.09 ± 0.26

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Burnier and Rothkopf, 2015) of the complex in-medium heavy quark potential by using larger physical volumes and a finer temperature scan around the deconfinement transition. The results contradict earlier findings of gradual screening around T_C on finer lattices with smaller volumes, which are now attributed to finite volume artifacts. The extracted Debye mass is systematically lower than values obtained from fitting Yukawa-type ansaetze to color singlet free energies (Kaczmarek, 2005), and the comparison shows that the color singlet free energies exhibit stronger screening than the actual in-medium potential. The work enables future continuum extrapolations of the Debye mass and provides a framework for comparing lattice results with resummed perturbation theory and dimensionally reduced QCD (Kajantie, 1997).
