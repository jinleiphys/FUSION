# Catalytic phi meson production in heavy-ion collisions

> The phi meson production on hyperons, pi Y --> phi Y and anti-kaons bar-K N--> phi Y is argued to be a new efficient source of phi mesons in a nucleus-nucleus collision. These reactions are not suppressed according to Okubo-Zweig-Izuka rule in contrast to the processes with non-strange particles in the entrance channels, pi B and BB with B=N,Delta. A rough estimate of the cross sections within a simple hadronic model shows that the cross sections of pi Y-->phi Y and bar-K N-->phi Y reactions can exceed that of the pi N--> phi N reaction by factors 50 and 60, respectively. In the hadrochemical model for nucleus-nucleus collisions at SIS and lower AGS energies we calculate the evolution of strange particle populations and phi meson production rate due to the new processes. It is found that the catalytic reactions can be operative if the maximal temperature in nucleus-nucleus collisions is larger than 130 MeV and the collision time is larger than 10 fm. A possible influence of the catalytic reactions on the centrality dependence of the phi yield at AGS energies and the phi rapidity distributions at SPS energies is discussed.

## Key claim
The authors propose that phi meson production via catalytic reactions on hyperons (πY → φY) and antikaons (K̄N → φY) can be a new efficient source of φ mesons in heavy-ion collisions, provided the maximal temperature exceeds 130 MeV and the collision time exceeds 10 fm. The abstract's claim of cross section enhancements by factors of 50 and 60 is explicitly qualified as a rough estimate from a simple hadronic model, not a precise measurement.

## Method
The authors estimate cross sections for πY → φY and K̄N → φY reactions using a simple hadronic model with a Lagrangian based on U(1) symmetry associated with strangeness conservation, with coupling constants fixed from φ → K̄K decay and meson-baryon interaction analyses. They then implement a hadrochemical model for nucleus-nucleus collisions at SIS and lower AGS energies, solving a differential equation for kaon density evolution with a scaling fireball expansion parameterization, to calculate the evolution of strange particle populations and φ meson production rates.

## Key numbers
- The cross sections of πY → φY and K̄N → φY reactions can exceed that of the πN → φN reaction by factors of approximately 50 and 60, respectively, at Δs^(1/2) ~ 100 MeV
- The catalytic reactions can be operative if the maximal temperature in nucleus-nucleus collisions is larger than 130 MeV and the collision time is larger than 10 fm
- The experimental cross section for πN → φN is approximately 0.03 mb
- The ratio of ω to φ meson production cross section in πN reactions is σ(πN → ωN)/σ(πN → φN) ~ 75
- At SIS energies, the FOPI collaboration reported ⟨φ⟩/⟨K⁻⟩ ≈ (0.44 ± 0.16 ± 0.22) or (1.7 ± 0.6 ± 0.85) depending on assumed source temperature (130 MeV or 70 MeV)
- The HADES experiment with Ar+KCl collisions at 1.756 A GeV gives ⟨φ⟩/⟨K⁻⟩ ≈ 0.37 ± 0.13
- The lower bound for σ(πΛ → φY) at 2 GeV/A is 23 mb, decreasing to 1 mb at 10 GeV/A
- The lower bound for σ(K̄N → φY) at 4 GeV/A is 5.2 mb, decreasing to 0.9 mb at 10 GeV/A

## Context
This work extends prior hadrochemical models of φ meson production, particularly addressing puzzles from FOPI (Mangiarotti 2003) and HADES (Agakichev 2009) experiments at SIS energies where standard transport models (Chung 1997, Kampfer 2002) and the UrQMD model (Bleicher 1999) systematically underestimate φ yields. The catalytic mechanism differs from the strangeness coalescence process (K̄K → φ) proposed by Ko (1991) because strangeness does not hide inside the φ but stays in the system, and the presence of K mesons is unnecessary. The work enables improved modeling of φ production across SIS, AGS, and SPS energies, and could help explain the centrality dependence of φ yields and rapidity distributions observed by the AGS (Back 2004) and SPS (NA49 Afanasiev 2000, NA50 Alessandro 2003) collaborations.
