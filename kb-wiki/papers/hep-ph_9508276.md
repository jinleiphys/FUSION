# Charmonium Composition and Nuclear Suppression

> We study charmonium production in hadron-nucleus collisions through the intermediate next-to-leading Fock space component $|(\bar{c}c)_8 g>$, formed by a colour octet $\bar{c}c$ pair and a gluon. We estimate the size of this state and show that its interaction with nucleons accounts for the observed charmonium suppression in nuclear interactions.

## Key claim

The observed suppression of charmonium (J/psi, psi', chi) in hadron-nucleus collisions can be explained by the interaction of the intermediate next-to-leading Fock state |(c cbar)_8 g> with nucleons, provided this state has a size of about 0.25 fm and an inelastic nucleon cross section of 6-7 mb, rather than the previously assumed hadronic-size colour octet with a cross section of 20-50 mb.

## Method

The authors decompose the J/psi wave function into Fock components, identifying the next-to-leading term |(c cbar)_8 g> as a colour-octet c cbar pair plus a gluon. They estimate the intrinsic transverse size of this state from the uncertainty relation using the confinement scale Lambda_QCD and the charm quark mass, then compute its inelastic cross section with nucleons by scaling the J/psi-nucleon cross section (from short-distance QCD and photoproduction) by the colour factor 9/4. This cross section is used in an exponential survival probability formula as a function of path length through nuclear matter, which is compared to existing data on J/psi, psi', and Upsilon production in p-A and S-U collisions.

## Key numbers

*   Intrinsic size of the |(c cbar)_8 g> state: r_8 = 0.25 fm, derived from r_8 = 1/sqrt(2 m_c Lambda) with m_c the charm quark mass and Lambda = 0.2 GeV.
*   Inelastic (c cbar)_8 g - nucleon cross section: sigma_{(c g) N} = 6-7 mb, obtained as (9/4) times the J/psi-nucleon cross section sigma_{psi N} = 2.5-3 mb.
*   Mean free path of the (c cbar)_8 g state in nuclear matter: lambda = 10 fm, from lambda = 1/(n_0 sigma) with n_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}.
*   Inelastic (b bbar)_8 g - nucleon cross section: sigma_{(b g) N} = 2 mb, scaled by sqrt(m_c/m_b) relative to the charm case.
*   J/psi and psi' production ratio in p-A collisions: psi'/(J/psi) = 0.15, independent of target mass A.
*   J/psi suppression in p-A collisions is described by sigma_{(c g) N} = 6 mb; Upsilon suppression by sigma_{(b g) N} = 2 mb, using average path length L_A = (3/4)[(A-1)/A][1.12 A^{1/3}].

## Context

This work extends the colour octet model of Bodwin, Braaten, and Lepage (1995) and the non-perturbative treatment of Kharzeev and Satz (1993) by providing a specific physical picture for the colour-octet state that propagates through the nucleus. It contradicts earlier assumptions (Kharzeev and Satz 1993; Piller, Mutzbauer, and Weise 1992; Dolejsi and Hufner 1992) that the colour octet c cbar pair has hadronic size (20-50 mb cross section) and that shadowing (Gupta and Satz 1992; Kharzeev and Satz 1994) is the dominant suppression mechanism. The result enables a unified description of J/psi, psi', and Upsilon suppression in p-A collisions and shows that the additional psi' suppression observed in S-U collisions (NA38 experiment) requires a later-stage confined hadronic medium, while the J/psi suppression in S-U is fully accounted for by early (c cbar)_8 g interactions with normal nuclear matter.
