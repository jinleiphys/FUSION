# Spinodal Instabilities in Nuclear Matter in a Stochastic Relativistic Mean-Field Approach

> Spinodal instabilities and early growth of baryon density fluctuations in symmetric nuclear matter are investigated in the basis of stochastic extension of relativistic mean-field approach in the semi-classical approximation. Calculations are compared with the results of non-relativistic calculations based on Skyrme-type effective interactions under similar conditions. A qualitative difference appears in the unstable response of the system: the system exhibits most unstable behavior at higher baryon densities around $ρ_{b}=0.4 ~ρ_{0}$ in the relativistic approach while most unstable behavior occurs at lower baryon densities around $ρ_{b}=0.2 ~ρ_{0}$ in the non-relativistic calculations

## Key claim

Spinodal instabilities in symmetric nuclear matter exhibit a qualitative difference between relativistic and non-relativistic calculations: the relativistic approach shows most unstable behavior at higher baryon densities around ρ_b = 0.4 ρ_0, while non-relativistic calculations show most unstable behavior at lower densities around ρ_b = 0.2 ρ_0. This result is obtained using the original Walecka model without scalar meson self-interaction, which gives a compressibility of 540 MeV and an effective nucleon mass M* = 0.541 M.

## Method

The authors employed a stochastic extension of the relativistic mean-field approach in the semi-classical approximation, using the linearized relativistic Vlasov equation coupled to linearized field equations for scalar and vector mesons. They solved the dispersion relation ε(k,ω) = 0 to obtain growth rates of unstable collective modes, and used the initial fluctuation correlation function to compute the spectral intensity of baryon density fluctuations and the equal-time baryon density correlation function. Calculations were performed for symmetric nuclear matter at temperature T = 5 MeV using the Walecka model with standard coupling ratios g_v^2(M/m_v)^2 = 273.8 and g_s^2(M/m_s)^2 = 357.4.

## Key numbers

- Most unstable behavior occurs at baryon density ρ_b = 0.4 ρ_0 in the relativistic approach, versus ρ_b = 0.2 ρ_0 in non-relativistic calculations (at T = 5 MeV)
- Growth rates of most unstable modes at ρ_b = 0.4 ρ_0 are nearly a factor of two larger in relativistic calculations than in non-relativistic calculations
- Most unstable wave numbers in relativistic calculations are concentrated around k = 0.6 fm^{-1} at both ρ_b = 0.2 ρ_0 and ρ_b = 0.4 ρ_0
- In non-relativistic calculations, most unstable modes shift to k = 0.8 fm^{-1} at ρ_b = 0.2 ρ_0 and to k = 0.5 fm^{-1} at ρ_b = 0.4 ρ_0
- Spectral intensity at the most unstable mode (k = 0.6 fm^{-1}) grows by about a factor of ten at ρ_b = 0.2 ρ_0 and about a factor of six at ρ_b = 0.4 ρ_0 over t = 40 fm/c in relativistic calculations
- Correlation length of baryon density fluctuations is approximately 3.0 fm at both densities in both relativistic and non-relativistic calculations
- The original Walecka model parameters give binding energy per nucleon 15.75 MeV at saturation density, effective nucleon mass M* = 0.541 M, and compressibility 540 MeV; the Skyrme interaction used for comparison gives compressibility 201 MeV

## Context

This work extends the stochastic mean-field approach previously applied with Skyrme-type effective interactions (Ayik et al., 2009) to a relativistic framework based on the Walecka model. The results show a qualitative difference from non-relativistic calculations in the density dependence of spinodal instabilities, which the authors attribute partly to the large compressibility (540 MeV) and small effective mass (M* = 0.541 M) of the original Walecka model. The findings enable future investigations of spinodal dynamics in symmetric and charge-asymmetric nuclear matter using improved relativistic models that include scalar meson self-interactions and rho mesons, allowing exploration over a wider range of nuclear compressibility and effective mass values.
