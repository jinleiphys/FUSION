# Extracting excited states from lattice QCD: the Roper resonance

> We present a new method for extracting excited states from a single two-point correlation function calculated on the lattice. Our method simply combines the correlation function evaluated at different time slices so as to ``subtract'' the leading exponential decay (ground state) and to give access to the first excited state. The method is applied to a quenched lattice study (volume = 24^3 x 64, beta = 6.2, 1/a = 2.55 GeV) of the first excited state of the nucleon using the local interpolating operator O = [uT C gamma5 d] u. The results are consistent with the identification of our extracted excited state with the Roper resonance N'(1440). The switching of the level ordering with respect to the negative-parity partner of the nucleon, N*(1535), is not seen at the simulated quark masses and, basing on crude extrapolations, is tentatively expected to occur close to the physical point.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new method for extracting the first excited state from a single two-point lattice correlation function by constructing a modified correlator that subtracts the ground-state contribution, and applies it to quenched lattice QCD. The extracted excited state is consistent with the Roper resonance N'(1440), but the authors note that the level ordering between the positive-parity first excited state and the negative-parity partner N*(1535) is not seen at the simulated quark masses and, based on crude extrapolations, is tentatively expected to occur close to the physical point.

## Method

The method constructs a modified correlator \(\widehat{G}(t) \equiv G(t+1)G(t-1) - G^2(t)\) from a single two-point correlation function \(G(t)\). This combination cancels the diagonal terms (ground state) in the exponential expansion, leaving a leading decay governed by the sum of the masses of the ground state and first excited state. The ground-state mass \(aM_N\) is extracted from the plateau of the effective mass of \(G(t)\), and the sum \(aM_N + aM_{N'}\) is extracted from the plateau of the effective mass of \(\widehat{G}(t)\), allowing the first excited state mass \(aM_{N'}\) to be determined. The method is applied to 450 quenched gauge configurations with Wilson fermions and non-perturbative Clover improvement at \(\beta = 6.20\) on a \(24^3 \times 64\) lattice.

## Key numbers

*   Lattice volume: \(24^3 \times 64\), \(\beta = 6.2\), inverse lattice spacing \(a^{-1} = 2.55(3)\) GeV.
*   Critical hopping parameter: \(k_c = 0.13588(2)\).
*   Bare quark masses used: \(m_q \in \{47, 82, 117, 153, 189\}\) MeV.
*   Pseudoscalar meson masses: \(M_{PS} \in \{0.513, 0.686, 0.831, 0.960, 1.08\}\) GeV.
*   At \(k = 0.1332\): nucleon mass \(aM_N = 0.7534 \pm 0.0027\), first excited state mass \(aM_{N'} = 1.189 \pm 0.0181\), ratio \(M_{N'}/M_N = 1.578 \pm 0.025\).
*   At \(k = 0.1352\): nucleon mass \(aM_N = 0.4832 \pm 0.0054\), first excited state mass \(aM_{N'} = 0.825 \pm 0.053\), ratio \(M_{N'}/M_N = 1.71 \pm 0.11\).
*   Negative-parity ground-state mass at \(k = 0.1332\): \(aM_{N^*} = 0.926 \pm 0.009\); at \(k = 0.1352\): \(aM_{N^*} = 0.697 \pm 0.026\).

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of baryon excited states, addressing the long-standing puzzle of the level ordering between the Roper resonance N'(1440) and the negative-parity N*(1535). The method provides an alternative to Bayesian approaches (Lepage 2001, maximum entropy method) and variational matrix methods (UKQCD Collaboration, Leinweber 2000) that require multiple interpolating operators. The results are compared directly with those of Gockeler (2004) using smeared operators, showing agreement for both \(aM_N\) and \(aM_{N^*}\). The method enables future studies at lower quark masses to resolve the level-switching question and to distinguish the Roper from the higher N(1710) resonance.
