# Nucleon resonance parameters from Roy-Steiner equations

> A reliable determination of the pole parameters and residues of nucleon resonances is notoriously challenging, given the required analytic continuation into the complex plane. We provide a comprehensive analysis of such resonance parameters accessible with Roy-Steiner equations for pion-nucleon scattering - a set of partial-wave dispersion relations that combines the constraints from analyticity, unitarity, and crossing symmetry - most prominently of the $Δ(1232)$ resonance. Further, we study the Roper, $N(1440)$, resonance, which lies beyond the strict domain of validity, in comparison to Padé approximants, comment on the role of subthreshold singularities in the $S$-wave, and determine the residues of the $f_0(500)$, $ρ(770)$, and $f_0(980)$ resonances in the $t$-channel process $ππ\to\bar NN$. The latter allows us to test - for the first time fully model independently in terms of the respective residues - universality of the $ρ(770)$ couplings and the Goldberger-Treiman relation expected if the scalars behaved as dilatons, in both cases revealing large deviations from the narrow-resonance limit.

## Key claim

The paper provides a comprehensive determination of nucleon resonance parameters using Roy-Steiner equations for pion-nucleon scattering, but the Roper N(1440) resonance lies beyond the strict domain of validity of these equations, requiring Padé approximants for its extraction, and the interpretation of an S-wave subthreshold singularity remains an open question.

## Method

The authors analytically continue the coupled system of Roy-Steiner partial-wave dispersion relations for pion-nucleon scattering into the complex plane, using input from pion-pion and pion-nucleon phase shifts, elasticities, and scattering lengths from pionic atoms. For the s-channel, they determine resonance poles by searching for zeros of the inverse S-matrix on the second sheet, while for the t-channel they extract residues of the pi pi -> N Nbar amplitudes at the known resonance poles of the f0(500), rho(770), and f0(980). The Roper resonance is accessed via Padé approximants after benchmarking the method on the Delta(1232).

## Context

This work extends the successful application of partial-wave dispersion relations from the mesonic sector (pion-pion scattering, where Roy equations resolved the f0(500) controversy as in Caprini (2005) and Garcia-Martin (2011)) to the baryonic sector via Roy-Steiner equations for pion-nucleon scattering. It provides the first fully model-independent determination of the rho(770) couplings to the nucleon and tests of the Goldberger-Treiman relation for scalar resonances, finding large deviations from narrow-resonance limits that challenge dilaton interpretations of the f0(500) as proposed by Crewther (2013) and Zwicky (2023). The results enable improved predictions for neutrino-nucleus cross sections at next-generation oscillation experiments and provide benchmark values for lattice QCD calculations of nucleon resonance properties.
