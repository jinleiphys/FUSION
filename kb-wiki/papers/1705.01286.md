# Experimental Determination of $η$/$s$ for Finite Nuclear Matter

> We present, for the first time, simultaneous determination of shear viscosity ($η$) and entropy density ($s$) and thus, $η/s$ for equilibrated nuclear systems from $A$ $\sim$ 30 to $A$ $\sim$ 208 at different temperatures. At finite temperature, $η$ is estimated by utilizing the $γ$ decay of the isovector giant dipole resonance populated via fusion evaporation reaction, while $s$ is evaluated from the nuclear level density parameter (${a}$) and nuclear temperature ($T$), determined precisely by the simultaneous measurements of the evaporated neutron energy spectra and the compound nuclear angular momenta. The transport parameter $η$ and the thermodynamic parameter $s$ both increase with temperature resulting in a mild decrease of $η$/$s$ with temperature. The extracted $η$/$s$ is also found to be independent of the neutron-proton asymmetry at a given temperature. Interestingly, the measured $η$/$s$ values are comparable to that of the high-temperature quark-gluon plasma, pointing towards the fact that strong fluidity may be the universal feature of the strong interaction of many-body quantum systems.

# Experimental Determination of η/s for Finite Nuclear Matter

## Key claim

The paper reports the first simultaneous experimental determination of shear viscosity (η) and entropy density (s) for equilibrated finite nuclear matter across a range of nuclei from A ~ 30 to A ~ 208, finding that η/s values (2.5–6.5 ℏ/4πk_B) are comparable to those of the high-temperature quark-gluon plasma. However, the authors note that the apparent independence of η/s from nuclear size and neutron-proton asymmetry may be an artifact of using the same zero-temperature viscosity η(0) = 1u for all nuclei, and that large errors prevent definitive conclusions.

## Method

Shear viscosity was extracted from measured giant dipole resonance (GDR) energies and widths using the formalism of Dang (2011), which relates η to the GDR width via the Green-Kubo relation with a Lorentzian photoabsorption cross section. Entropy density was calculated from the nuclear level density parameter a(T) and nuclear temperature T, determined simultaneously by measuring evaporated neutron energy spectra and compound nuclear angular momenta in light-ion-induced (α) fusion reactions on targets of 27Al, 93Nb, 115In, and 197Au. The GDR was populated via fusion evaporation reactions and its γ decay was measured using the LAMBDA spectrometer, while angular momenta were measured with a 50-element multiplicity filter.

## Key numbers

- η/s values for finite nuclear matter are confined in the range (2.5–6.5) ℏ/4πk_B over the temperature range ~ (0.8–2.1) MeV
- At the lowest available temperatures, η/s remains within (5.1–6.5) ℏ/4πk_B for all nuclei studied
- At the highest available temperatures, η/s remains within (4.6–6.1) ℏ/4πk_B for all nuclei studied
- Zero-temperature shear viscosity η(0) was taken as 1u (where 1u = 10^{-23} MeV·s·fm^{-3}), with lower bound 0.55u and upper bound 1.25u
- Accepted ground state GDR widths Γ_GDR(0) were 7.5 MeV for 31P, 5.5 MeV for 97Tc, 4.5 MeV for 119Sb, and 3.5 MeV for 201Tl
- Nuclear density ρ = 0.16 fm^{-3} was used for entropy density calculations
- The damping factor γ for shell effect depletion was 0.054 MeV^{-1} with an uncertainty of 0.020 MeV^{-1}
- Ground state shell correction values ΔS were -2.23 MeV (31P), -0.20 MeV (97Tc), 0.22 MeV (119Sb), and -8.31 MeV (201Tl)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Auerbach and Shlomo (2009), who first studied η/s in relation to giant resonance damping within the Fermi liquid drop model (FLDM), and the formalism of Dang (2011) relating shear viscosity to GDR width via the Green-Kubo relation. The experimental results confirm the prediction of Auerbach and Shlomo (2009) that strong fluidity is a universal characteristic of strong interaction in many-body nuclear systems, with measured η/s values comparable to those of the quark-gluon plasma (Schafer 2009). The study enables future investigations of the high-temperature limit of η/s for finite nuclear systems as proposed by Dang (2011), and calls for further studies to determine whether the apparent independence of η/s from nuclear size and neutron-proton asymmetry is genuine or an artifact of the analysis.
