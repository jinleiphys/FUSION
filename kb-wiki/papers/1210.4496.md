# Towards the phase diagram of dense two-color matter

> We study two-color QCD with two flavors of Wilson fermion as a function of quark chemical potential mu and temperature T. We find evidence of a superfluid phase at intermediate mu and low T where the quark number density and diquark condensate are both very well described by a Fermi sphere of nearly-free quarks disrupted by a BCS condensate. Our results suggest that the quark contribution to the energy density is negative (and balanced by a positive gauge contribution), although this result is highly sensitive to details of the energy renormalisation. We also find evidence that the chiral condensate in this region vanishes in the massless limit. This region gives way to a region of deconfined quark matter at higher T and mu, with the deconfinement temperature, determined from the renormalised Polyakov loop, decreasing only very slowly with increasing chemical potential. The quark number susceptibility chi_q does not exhibit any qualitative change at the deconfinement transition. We argue that this is because chi_q is not an appropriate measure of deconfinement for 2-color QCD at high density.

## Key claim

The paper finds evidence for a superfluid phase at intermediate quark chemical potential and low temperature in two-color QCD, where the quark number density and diquark condensate are well described by a Fermi sphere of nearly-free quarks disrupted by a BCS condensate; however, the result that the quark contribution to the energy density is negative is highly sensitive to details of the energy renormalisation.

## Method

The authors perform lattice simulations of two-color QCD with two flavors of Wilson fermion, using a Hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm and a diquark source term to stabilise simulations. They map the phase diagram in the (chemical potential, temperature) plane by computing order parameters including the diquark condensate, Polyakov loop, chiral condensate, and quark number susceptibility, extrapolating results to zero diquark source where possible. Energy densities are computed using an anisotropic lattice formalism with Karsch coefficients derived from fits to ensembles with varying bare anisotropies.

## Key numbers

- Deconfinement temperature at zero chemical potential: \(T_d = 217(23)\) MeV, determined from the renormalised Polyakov loop on \(16^3 \times N_\tau\) lattices at \(\beta=1.9, \kappa=0.168\)
- Lattice spacing values range from 0.107(3) fm to 0.229(3) fm across \(\beta=1.7-2.1\) and various \(\kappa\) values, determined from string tension \(\sqrt{\sigma}=440\) MeV
- Critical hopping parameters \(\kappa_c\) (where \(m_\pi^2=0\)) range from 0.18226(8) at \(\beta=1.7\) to 0.15935(8) at \(\beta=2.1\)
- Pion-to-rho mass ratios \(m_\pi/m_\rho\) range from 0.61(5) to 0.946(1) across the simulated parameter sets
- Temperatures studied: 47 MeV (\(N_\tau=24\)), 70 MeV (\(N_\tau=16\)), 94 MeV (\(N_\tau=12\)), and 141 MeV (\(N_\tau=8\)) at \(\beta=1.9, \kappa=0.168\)

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of two-color QCD by Hands et al. (2006, 2010) which found evidence for a transition to deconfined quark matter at high chemical potential but left the precise nature unclear, and by Kogut et al. (2001) and Hands et al. (2007) which studied the excitation spectrum including Goldstone bosons. The paper also addresses the possibility of a quarkyonic phase proposed by McLerran (2007), which earlier work by Hands et al. (2010) could not conclusively identify due to distortions from a non-zero diquark source. The results enable future controlled extrapolations to the continuum and chiral limits, and provide first-principles nonperturbative constraints for model calculations of high-density QCD-like theories.
