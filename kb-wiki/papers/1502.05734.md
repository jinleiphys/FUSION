# Quarkonium formation time in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We calculate the quarkonium formation time in relativistic heavy-ion collisions from the space-time correlator of heavy quark vector currents in a hydrodynamics background with the initial nonequilibrium stage expanding only in the longitudinal direction. Using in-medium quarkonia properties determined with the heavy quark potential taken to be the free energy from lattice calculations and the fact that quarkonia can only be formed below their dissociation temperatures due to color screening, we find that $Υ$(1S), $Υ$(2S), $Υ$(3S), $J/ψ$ and $ψ^\prime$ are formed, respectively, at 1.2, 6.6, 8.8, 5.8, and 11.0 fm/c after the quark pair are produced in central Au+Au collisions at the top energy of Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC), and these times become shorter in semi-central collisions. We further show, as an example, that including the effect of formation time enhances appreciably the survivability of $Υ$(1S) in the produced hot dense matter.

# Quarkonium formation time in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The formation times of quarkonia in relativistic heavy-ion collisions are significantly longer than their vacuum values, and the effect of including these formation times enhances the survivability of quarkonia in the hot dense matter; however, the result depends on the magnitude and temperature dependence of the quarkonium thermal decay widths, which are presently not well determined.

## Method

The authors compute the quarkonium formation time from the space-time correlator of heavy quark vector currents, using in-medium quarkonium properties (mass, wavefunction at origin, and continuum threshold) obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation with the heavy quark potential taken as the lattice free energy. The time-dependent temperature of the quark-gluon plasma is obtained from a 2+1 ideal hydrodynamic model with boost invariance, assuming an initial thermalization time of 0.6 fm/c and longitudinal-only expansion in the pre-equilibrium stage, with the average temperature weighted by the number of binary collisions in the transverse plane from a Glauber model.

- Upsilon(3S) formation time: 8.8 fm/c in central (0-5%) Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV

- Upsilon(2S) formation time: 2.6 fm/c in semi-central (50-60%) Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV
- Upsilon(3S) formation time: 3.8 fm/c in semi-central (50-60%) Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV

- Vacuum formation time of Upsilon(1S): 0.32 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Song 2013) of quarkonium formation time in a constant-temperature QGP to the more realistic case of a time-dependent temperature from a hydrodynamic model, using the same method based on space-time correlators of heavy quark vector currents originally developed for vacuum by Kharzeev (1999). The results are relevant for interpreting quarkonium suppression data from RHIC and LHC experiments, as the formation time affects whether a heavy quark pair forms a bound state inside or outside the hot medium; the authors note that for excited states and charmonia in central collisions, the formation times are so long that regeneration from thermalized heavy quarks may dominate production.
