# The QCD critical point hunt: emergent new ideas and new dynamics

> The QCD critical point is a landmark point on the QCD phase diagram, with potential connections to a plethora of deep questions on the properties of thermal nuclear matter. Future heavy-ion collision experiments, in particular, the second phase of beam energy scan (BES-II), will explore the QCD phase diagram with an unprecedented precision and would potentially discover the QCD critical point. In this short review, I discuss and summarize recent new ideas, such as `rapidity scan', and new theoretical developments, in particular in the qualitative characterization and quantitative descriptions of the critical fluctuations in the expanding fireball. Those new ideas and developments together would enhance the prospect of locating the QCD critical point.

# The QCD critical point hunt: emergent new ideas and new dynamics

## Key claim
The QCD critical point may be discovered through new observables and theoretical frameworks that account for off-equilibrium effects, but the success of this hunt depends critically on the fireball passing close enough to the critical point and on the ability to model the emergent Kibble-Zurek dynamics that replace equilibrium critical scaling in expanding fireballs.

## Method
The paper reviews recent theoretical developments including the "rapidity scan" method, which bins cumulants of proton multiplicity as a function of spatial rapidity at fixed beam energy to map small regions of the QCD phase diagram. It also presents the "hydro+" framework, which couples wavenumber-dependent two-point correlation functions of the specific entropy density (treated as additional slow variables) to conventional hydrodynamic equations, with the generalized pressure depending on energy density, baryon density, and the critical fluctuation amplitude.

## Key numbers
- The chiral pseudo-critical temperature T_c is reported as 156.5 MeV with an errorbar of 1.5 MeV (Steinbrecher 2018).
- The hierarchy of length scales near the critical point is l_mic << l_KZ << l_max, where l_KZ is the Kibble-Zurek length and the microscopic relaxation time is much shorter than the expansion time of the fireball (approximately 10 fm).
- The rescaled proton fourth cumulant o_4_sigma (defined as kappa_4_N / N_p) is negative for small rapidity y_max but becomes positive at larger y_max, as computed in a simplified setup with bin width Delta y = 0.4.

## Context
This review extends the line of work on QCD critical point phenomenology that began with equilibrium critical scaling predictions (Stephanov 2007, Fukushima 2010) and the beam energy scan program at RHIC. It addresses the "sign puzzle" where preliminary STAR data on proton skewness contradicts equilibrium expectations (Mukherjee 2015), and introduces the Kibble-Zurek framework (Mukherjee 2016) to explain off-equilibrium effects. The hydro+ framework (Stephanov 2017) provides a deterministic alternative to stochastic fluctuating hydrodynamics (Kapusta 2011, Singh 2018) and chiral fluid dynamics (Herold 2013, Nahrgang 2016), enabling quantitative modeling of critical fluctuations coupled to bulk evolution. Future BES-II data and rapidity scan measurements could test the predicted off-equilibrium scaling and constrain the location of the critical point.
