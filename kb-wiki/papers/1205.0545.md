# Critical endpoint for deconfinement in matrix and other effective models

> We consider the position of the deconfining critical endpoint, where the first order transition for deconfinement is washed out by the presence of massive, dynamical quarks. We use an effective matrix model, employed previously to analyze the transition in the pure glue theory. If the param- eters of the pure glue theory are unaffected by the presence of dynamical quarks, and if the quarks only contribute perturbatively, then for three colors and three degenerate quark flavors this quark mass is very heavy, m_de \sim 2.5 GeV, while the critical temperature, T_de, barely changes, \sim 1% below that in the pure glue theory. The location of the deconfining critical endpoint is a sensitive test to differentiate between effective models. For example, models with a logarithmic potential for the Polyakov loop give much smaller values of the quark mass, m_de \sim 1 GeV, and a large shift in T_de \sim 10% lower than that in the pure glue theory.

## Key claim

If the parameters of the pure glue theory are unaffected by the presence of dynamical quarks, and if the quarks only contribute perturbatively, then for three colors and three degenerate quark flavors the deconfining critical endpoint occurs at a very heavy quark mass of about 2.5 GeV, with the critical temperature barely changing (about 1% below that in the pure glue theory).

## Method

The authors use an effective matrix model for the Polyakov loop, previously employed to analyze the deconfining transition in pure SU(3) glue theory, and add dynamical quarks through their one-loop perturbative contribution to the background-field-dependent potential. They compute the deconfining critical endpoint by finding the quark mass at which the first-order deconfining phase transition becomes second order, using one-parameter and two-parameter matrix models tuned to lattice data for the pure glue interaction measure, as well as polynomial and logarithmic Polyakov loop models for comparison.

## Key numbers

- For the one-parameter matrix model with three degenerate flavors: critical temperature T_de = 0.995 T_d, critical quark mass m_de = 2167 MeV (for one flavor)
- For the two-parameter matrix model with three degenerate flavors: T_de = 0.990 T_d, critical quark mass m_de = 1836 MeV (for one flavor)
- For the zero-parameter matrix model: T_de = 0.973 T_d, critical quark mass m_de = 1536 MeV (for one flavor)
- For the logarithmic Polyakov loop model: T_de = 0.90 T_d, critical quark mass m_de ~ 1 GeV
- For the polynomial Polyakov loop model: T_de = 0.996 T_d, critical quark mass m_de ~ 3.5 GeV
- All results assume T_d = 270 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous matrix model studies by Meisinger, Miller, and Ogilvie (2001) and Dumitru, Guo, and collaborators (2010, 2012), using models whose parameters are tuned to reproduce lattice data on the pure SU(3) glue interaction measure. The results sharply differentiate between classes of effective models: matrix and polynomial Polyakov loop models predict heavy critical quark masses (2-3.5 GeV), while logarithmic Polyakov loop models (Fukushima 2003, Roessner, Ratti, and Weise 2006) predict light masses (~1 GeV). The authors suggest that lattice simulations with an external background field could resolve which prediction is correct, and note that recent Wuppertal-Budapest Collaboration results (Borsanyi 2012) with dynamical charm quarks could address the question.
