# Quantum Statistical Effects on Fusion Dynamics of Heavy-Ions

> In order to describe the fusion of two very heavy nuclei at near barrier energies, a generalized Langevin approach is proposed, which incorporates the quantum statistical fluctuations in accordance with the fluctuation and dissipation theorem. It is illustrated that the quantum statistical effects introduce an enhancement of the formation of compound nucleus, though the quantum enhancement is somewhat less pronounced as indicated in the previous calculations.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a generalized Langevin approach that incorporates quantum statistical fluctuations into the description of heavy-ion fusion at near-barrier energies, and finds that these quantum effects enhance the compound nucleus formation probability at low temperatures, though the enhancement is somewhat less pronounced than in the authors' previous calculations using a density-matrix-based Fokker-Planck approach.

## Method

The authors model the evolution of the elongation coordinate along the conditional saddle from the sticking configuration to compound nucleus formation using a single collective variable coupled linearly to intrinsic nucleonic degrees of freedom. They derive a generalized Langevin equation with a memory-dependent friction term and a correlated random force whose autocorrelation satisfies the quantum fluctuation-dissipation theorem, then solve analytically for the Gaussian joint probability distribution of the collective coordinate and momentum. The formation probability is computed as the integral of the distribution over the elongation coordinate beyond the saddle point, using parameters for the collision of Ca-48 with U-238.

## Key numbers

* The curvature of the conditional saddle is taken as hbar*Omega = 1.0 MeV, and the reduced friction coefficient is beta = 5 x 10^21 s^-1.
* The height of the conditional saddle is set to 4.0 MeV for the initial position q0.
* Calculations are performed at temperatures T = 0.5 MeV, 1.0 MeV, and 5.0 MeV, with a cutoff energy Delta = 15 MeV.
* The typical single-particle energy spacing is Delta = approximately 10 MeV, much larger than the collective frequency hbar*Omega = 1.0 MeV.
* The damping width of single-particle states eta is such that hbar*Omega is less than or equal to eta for low-frequency collective motion.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous density-matrix-based generalized Fokker-Planck approach (Ayik et al., 2004, 2005) by reformulating the problem in a generalized Langevin framework that is more suitable for numerical simulations on complex potential energy surfaces. The paper also incorporates a modification of the diffusion coefficients by the friction mechanism that was not included in the earlier Fokker-Planck treatment. The results are relevant to the synthesis of superheavy elements via heavy-ion fusion at near-barrier energies, where nuclear temperatures are of order 0.5-1.0 MeV and quantum statistical effects become important.
