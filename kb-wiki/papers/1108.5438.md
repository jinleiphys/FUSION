# The physics potential of proton-nucleus collisions at the TeV scale

> The LHC brings nuclear collisions to the TeV scale for the first time and the first data show the qualitative differences of this new regime. The corresponding phase-space available encompasses completely uncharted regions of QCD in which high-density or high-temperature domains can be identified. Proton-nucleus runs are essential for a complete interpretation of the data and for the study of new regimes dominated by large occupation numbers in the hadronic wave function. I comment here the physics opportunities for p+Pb runs at the LHC and d+Au runs at RHIC and the corresponding needs in view of the new Pb+Pb data from the LHC.

# The physics potential of proton-nucleus collisions at the TeV scale

## Key claim
Proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC serve a dual purpose: benchmarking cold nuclear matter backgrounds for interpreting nucleus-nucleus data, and accessing new QCD regimes dominated by large gluonic densities in the hadron wave function. The paper argues that a p+Pb run at the LHC is essential for a complete interpretation of the Pb+Pb data, but notes that the actual kinematic reach is normally limited by the process under study and smaller than the total phase space plotted.

## Method
The author reviews the physics opportunities for p+Pb runs at the LHC and d+Au runs at RHIC, comparing kinematic reach across facilities. The paper examines specific observables (Z/W production, quarkonia suppression, high-pT particle production, jet reconstruction) and categorizes them by the relative size of cold versus hot nuclear matter effects. Existing data from RHIC d+Au collisions and first LHC Pb+Pb results are used to illustrate what benchmarking from p+A collisions would clarify.

## Key numbers
- p+Pb collisions at top LHC energy reach sqrt(s_NN) ≈ 8.8 ATeV, with a rapidity shift Δy ≈ 0.46
- Estimated luminosity for a p+Pb run: L ≈ 1.5 × 10^29 cm^{-2} s^{-1}
- Assuming 10^6 s running time, integrated luminosity ≈ 0.1 pb^{-1}
- Expected yield: 4000 Z bosons decaying to dileptons per unit rapidity in p+Pb at sqrt(s)=8.8 TeV; W yield is a factor of 10 larger
- RHIC d+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 AGeV
- Previous experiments at CERN SPS (sqrt(s) ≈ 17-27 AGeV) and Fermilab fixed target (sqrt(s) ≈ 39 AGeV)
- At the LHC, p+Pb can reach x values of order 10^{-5}, within saturation region estimates
- The energy jump from RHIC d+Au to top LHC p+Pb is a factor of more than 40

## Context
This paper extends the discussion of cold nuclear matter effects from earlier work at RHIC, SPS, and Fermilab into the new TeV-scale regime opened by the LHC. It builds on the LHC proton-nucleus feasibility report (Salgado 2011) and references the NLO global fits EPS09 (Eskola 2009), HKN07 (Hirai 2007), and nDS (de Florian 2003) for nuclear parton distribution functions. The paper enables future p+Pb runs at the LHC by identifying which observables (Z/W production, quarkonia, jets) most need benchmarking, and it connects to the theoretical Color Glass Condensate formalism (Balitsky-Kovchegov equations at NLO) as a framework for interpreting saturation signals.
