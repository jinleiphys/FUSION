# Symmetry energy of warm nuclear systems

> The temperature dependence of the symmetry energy and symmetry free energy coefficients of infinite nuclear matter and of finite nuclei is investigated. For infinite matter, both these coefficients are found to have a weaker dependence on temperature at densities close to saturation; at low but homogeneous densities, the temperature dependence becomes stronger. For finite systems, different definitions of symmetry energy coefficients are encountered in the literature yielding different values. A resolution to this problem is suggested from a global liquid-drop-inspired fit of the energies and free energies of a host of nuclei covering the entire periodic table. The hot nucleus is modeled in a subtracted finite-temperature-Thomas-Fermi framework, with dynamical surface phonon coupling to nucleonic motion plugged in. Contrary to infinite nuclear matter, a substantial change in the symmetry energy coefficients is observed for finite nuclei with temperature.

## Key claim

The paper finds that for infinite nuclear matter, the symmetry energy and symmetry free energy coefficients have a weak temperature dependence at saturation density, but a stronger dependence at low densities. For finite nuclei, contrary to infinite matter, a substantial change in the symmetry energy coefficients with temperature is observed, but the exact values depend on the definition used (local density approximation vs. difference method), and the authors propose a liquid-drop-inspired global fit to resolve this ambiguity.

## Method

The authors model hot nuclei using a subtracted finite-temperature Thomas-Fermi (FTTF) framework with three Skyrme-class interactions (SkM*, SLy4, and SK255). For finite nuclei, they incorporate dynamical surface phonon coupling to nucleonic motion through a phenomenological energy-mass term. They calculate energies and free energies for 69 spherical and near-spherical nuclei with masses 36 ≤ A ≤ 218 and charges 14 ≤ Z ≤ 92, then perform a four-parameter liquid-drop fit to extract volume and surface symmetry coefficients as functions of temperature.

## Key numbers

- At saturation density ρ₀, the symmetry energy coefficient e_sym shows very weak temperature dependence for all three interactions (SkM*, SLy4, SK255)
- At subsaturation density ρ = 0.1ρ₀, e_sym shows a slow fall with temperature, while f_sym shows a comparatively more prominent rise
- At T = 0, the surface symmetry parameter β_E or β_F lies between approximately 12-13 MeV for the three interactions, in consonance with the empirical value
- The phenomenological value of e_sym^s(T=0) is approximately 50-58 MeV, and e_sym^v(T=0)/β_E(T=0) is in the range of approximately 2.4 ± 0.4
- The symmetry entropy coefficient s_sym at very low densities is approximately -1/2

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of symmetry energy in homogeneous nuclear matter using mean-field approximations (Xu, De, Samaddar, Mou) and earlier finite-temperature Thomas-Fermi calculations for specific atomic masses (De 2012). It addresses the ambiguity in defining symmetry coefficients for finite nuclei that arises from the difference method used by Dean (1995) and the local density approximation used by Samaddar (2007). The paper enables more reliable modeling of warm nuclear systems for applications in core-collapse supernova simulations, where the temperature dependence of symmetry energy affects electron capture rates and the equation of state of hot neutron star matter.
