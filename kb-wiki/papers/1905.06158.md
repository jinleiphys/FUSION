# Rossby modes in neutron stars as sources of gravitational waves

> In the present work, we explore the Rossby mode instabilities in neutron stars as sources of gravitational waves. The intensity and time evolution of the emitted gravitational waves in terms of the amplitude of the strain tensor are estimated in the slow rotation approximation using $β$-equilibrated neutron star matter obtained from density dependent M3Y effective interaction. For a wide range of neutron star masses, the fiducial gravitational and various viscous time scales, the critical frequencies and the time evolutions of the frequencies are calculated. The dissipative mechanism of the Rossby modes is considered to be driven by the shear viscosity along the boundary layer of the solid crust-liquid core interface as well as in the core and the bulk viscosity. It is found that neutron stars with slower frequency of rotation, for the same mass, radius and surface temperature, are expected to emit gravitational waves of higher intensity.

## Key claim
The paper estimates gravitational wave strain from Rossby (r-mode) instabilities in neutron stars using a specific nuclear equation of state (DDM3Y), finding that slower-rotating neutron stars of the same mass, radius, and surface temperature emit higher-intensity gravitational waves, a conditional result that depends on the assumed saturation mechanism for the r-mode amplitude.

## Method
The authors model r-mode instabilities in slowly rotating neutron stars with a rigid crust, using β-equilibrated neutron star matter from the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective interaction. They compute fiducial gravitational and viscous time scales (shear viscosity in the core and at the crust-core boundary layer, plus bulk viscosity) and solve for the critical angular frequency where instability sets in. The r-mode amplitude is constrained via two independent methods, spin equilibrium and thermal equilibrium, using observed spin frequencies and core temperatures of Low Mass X-ray Binaries (LMXBs) and Millisecond Radio Pulsars (MSRPs) from Haensel et al. (2012). The gravitational wave strain tensor amplitude is then calculated from the saturated r-mode amplitude.

## Key numbers
- Gravitational wave strain tensor amplitude \(h_0\) is of order \(10^{-28}\) for the \(l=2\) r-mode (stated in the Introduction).
- Saturation baryonic number density \(\rho_0 = 0.1533\) fm\(^{-3}\) and saturation energy per baryon \(\epsilon_0 = -15.26 \pm 0.52\) MeV for the DDM3Y EoS.
- Critical angular velocity \(\Omega_c\) is obtained from the condition \(1/\tau(\Omega_c, T)=0\) for the \(l=2\) mode; the Keplerian angular velocity limit is \(\Omega_K \approx \frac{2}{3}\Omega_0\).
- Observed spin frequencies of LMXBs and MSRPs range from 182 Hz (Swift J1756-2508) to 620 Hz (4U 1608-52), with core temperatures from \(<0.11 \times 10^8\) K (SAX J1808.4-3658) to \(4.55 \times 10^8\) K (4U 1608-52) (Table I).
- Shear viscosity coefficients: \(\eta_V^{ee} = 6 \times 10^6 \rho^2 T^{-2}\) g cm\(^{-1}\) s\(^{-1}\) and \(\eta_V^{nn} = 347 \rho^{9/4} T^{-2}\) g cm\(^{-1}\) s\(^{-1}\).
- Bulk viscosity expression: \(\xi_{BV} = 6 \times 10^{-59} \left(\frac{l+1}{2}\right)^2 \left(\frac{\text{Hz}}{\Omega}\right)^2 \left(\frac{\rho}{\text{g cm}^{-3}}\right)^2 \left(\frac{T}{\text{K}}\right)^6\) g cm\(^{-1}\) s\(^{-1}\).

## Context
This work extends prior studies of r-mode instabilities in neutron stars (Lindblom 1998, 2000; Owen 1998) by using the DDM3Y equation of state and comparing two saturation mechanisms for the r-mode amplitude (spin equilibrium and thermal equilibrium) as applied to observed LMXBs and MSRPs from Haensel et al. (2012). The results show that all observed neutron stars lie in the stable r-mode region, consistent with the absence of detected gravitational waves from r-modes. The calculations enable future comparisons with Advanced LIGO sensitivity thresholds and constrain the nuclear equation of state through r-mode observables.
