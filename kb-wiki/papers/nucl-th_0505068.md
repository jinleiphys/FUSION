# A covariant model of the electromagnetic current for the study of two-body scalar systems

> We present a procedure to derive a covariant electromagnetic current operator for a system made up by two scalars constituents. Using different wave functions we fitted their parameters to the experimental data of the pion form factor, obtainig great discrepancy at low momentum transfer. Introducing the Vector Meson Dominance corrective factor, we obtained a better fit to the data.

# A covariant model of the electromagnetic current for the study of two-body scalar systems

## Key claim

The authors derive a gauge-invariant, conserved electromagnetic current for a two-scalar constituent system using a covariant impulse approximation, but find that the model alone produces large discrepancies with experimental pion form factor data at low momentum transfer; only after introducing a phenomenological Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) corrective factor does the model achieve a better fit to the data.

## Method

The authors start from a relativistic wave equation for two scalar constituents in the rest frame, then transform it to an arbitrary observation reference frame using on-shell constituent momenta. They derive a conserved electromagnetic current by subtracting the wave equation for initial and final states, obtaining a kinetic contribution that takes the form of an impulse approximation where the spectator constituent's momentum remains unchanged. For numerical application to the pion form factor, they use Gaussian, Hulten, and modified Hulten wave functions, and introduce a VMD corrective factor that multiplies the bare electromagnetic vertex by a function involving the vector meson propagator.

## Key numbers

- The VMD factor uses the rho meson mass: \( m_V = m_\rho = 0.77 \, \text{GeV}/c^2 \), with parameters \( \beta_V = 0.865 \) and \( n = 2 \).
- For the modified Hulten wave function with VMD, the fitted parameters are: \( a = 0.70 \), \( b = 0.55 \), \( C = 0.76 \, (\text{GeV}/c)^{-3/2} \), \( \alpha = 0.9 \, \text{GeV}/c \), and \( \beta = 0.6 \, \text{GeV}/c \).
- For the standard Hulten wave function without VMD, the parameters are \( \alpha = 0.1 \, \text{GeV}/c \) and \( \beta = 0.4 \, \text{GeV}/c \).

## Context

This work extends the covariant impulse approximation method previously introduced by the same authors (Acero, De Sanctis, Sandoval, earlier work) for scattering processes on composite systems. The model correctly reproduces the high momentum transfer ("partonic") behavior of the pion form factor but fails at low momentum transfer, which the authors attribute to the highly non-perturbative nature of the bound state at large spatial distances. The same procedure is currently being applied to the nucleon as a three-quark relativistic composite system (De Sanctis, ongoing work) and is intended for future application to a model with two spin-1/2 quark-antiquark particles.
