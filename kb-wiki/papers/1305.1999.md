# Structure of Near-Threshold s-Wave Resonances

> We study the structure of two-body s-wave bound states as well as resonances in the threshold energy region. We focus on the single-channel scattering where the scattering length and the effective range are given by real numbers. It is shown that, in the energy region where the effective range expansion is valid, the properties of resonances are constrained only by the position of the pole. We find that the compositeness defined through the analytic continuation of the field renormalization constant is pure imaginary and normalized for resonances. We discuss the interpretation of this quantity by examining the structure of the hadron resonance Lambda_c(2595) in the pi Sigma_c scattering. We show that the Lambda_c(2595) resonance requires an unnaturally large effective range and hence it is not likely a pi Sigma_c molecule.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for near-threshold s-wave resonances where the effective range expansion is valid, the compositeness (defined via analytic continuation of the field renormalization constant) becomes purely imaginary and normalized, and the resonance properties are constrained only by the pole position. The claim is hedged by the condition that this applies only in the energy region where the effective range expansion is valid and for single-channel scattering with real scattering length and effective range.

## Method

The author employs the effective range expansion truncated at the k² term for single-channel s-wave scattering, expressing the amplitude in terms of the scattering length a and effective range r_e. The pole positions in the complex momentum plane are derived from these threshold parameters, and the field renormalization constant Z (and compositeness X = 1-Z) is calculated from the pole positions. A single-channel model for πΣ_c scattering based on chiral dynamics is used to test the effective range expansion and to analyze the Λ_c(2595) resonance, comparing the physical pole position with that obtained from a natural renormalization scheme.

## Key numbers

- The Λ_c(2595) resonance has central values of E_R = 0.67 MeV (excitation energy relative to πΣ_c threshold) and Γ_R = 2.59 MeV (width)
- The scattering length and effective range deduced from the physical Λ_c(2595) pole are a = 10.5 fm and r_e = -19.5 fm
- In the natural renormalization scheme, the corresponding values are E_R = 33.70 MeV, Γ_R = 133.31 MeV, a = 0.9 fm, and r_e = -4.6 fm
- For the chiral model with C=4, the effective range is r_e ∼ -4.8 fm; for C=1, r_e ∼ -18.3 fm
- The compositeness of the Λ_c(2595) resonance is given by X̄ = 0.608, with Z = 1 - 0.608i

## Context

This work extends the compositeness analysis of Weinberg (1965) for bound states to the case of resonances, using the effective range expansion as a model-independent framework. The analysis contradicts the interpretation of the Λ_c(2595) as a πΣ_c hadronic molecule, which would be expected from a dynamically generated resonance in chiral dynamics as studied by Ikeda (2011) and Hyodo (2011). The framework enables the structure analysis of any near-threshold resonance, such as ⁸Be in αα scattering, by relating observable threshold parameters to the resonance compositeness.
