# Uncertainty Quantification for Neutrino Opacities in Core-Collapse Supernovae and Neutron Star Mergers

> We perform an extensive study of the correlations between the neutrino-nucleon inverse mean free paths (IMFPs) and the underlying equation of states (EoSs). Strong interaction uncertainties in the neutrino mean free path are investigated in different density regimes. The nucleon effective mass, the nucleon chemical potentials, and the residual interactions in the medium play an important role in determining neutrino-nucleon interactions in a density-dependent manner. We study how the above quantities are constrained by an EoS consistent with (i) nuclear mass measurements, (ii) proton-proton scattering phase shifts, and (iii) neutron star observations. We then study the uncertainties of both the charged current and the neutral current neutrino-nucleon inverse mean free paths due to the variation of these quantities, using Hartree-Fock+random phase approximation method. Finally, we calculate the Pearson correlation coefficients between (i) the EoS-based quantities and the EoS-based quantities; (ii) the EoS-based quantities and the IMFPs; (iii) the IMFPs and the IMFPs. We find a strong impact of residual interactions on neutrino opacity in the spin and spin-isospin channels, which are not well constrained by current nuclear modelings.

## Key claim

The paper systematically quantifies the uncertainties in neutrino-nucleon inverse mean free paths (IMFPs) arising from uncertainties in the nuclear equation of state (EoS), finding that residual interactions in the spin and spin-isospin channels have a strong impact on neutrino opacity but are not well constrained by current nuclear modeling, with the analysis limited to densities up to 0.2 fm$^{-3}$.

## Method

The authors employ a Hartree-Fock plus random phase approximation (HF+RPA) method to compute both charged current and neutral current neutrino-nucleon inverse mean free paths at finite temperature. They derive density-dependent residual interactions from a virial equation of state at low densities and from Skyrme-type interactions at higher densities, where the Skyrme models are constrained by nuclear mass measurements, proton-proton scattering phase shifts, and neutron star observations. Pearson correlation coefficients are calculated between EoS-based quantities, between EoS-based quantities and IMFPs, and between IMFPs at different densities.

## Key numbers

- The virial residual interactions at T=10 MeV are: $f_{0,\mathrm{virial}} = -2.03 \times 10^{-4}$ MeV$^{-2}$, $f_{0,\mathrm{virial}}' = 1.19 \times 10^{-4}$ MeV$^{-2}$, $g_{0,\mathrm{virial}} = 2.88 \times 10^{-6}$ MeV$^{-2}$, and $g_{0,\mathrm{virial}}' = 7.31 \times 10^{-5}$ MeV$^{-2}$
- The virial coefficients at T=10 MeV are: $b_{n,0}=0.463$, $b_{n,1}=-0.152$, $b_{pn,0}=0.725$, $b_{pn,1}=1.130$, with free-gas values $b_{n,0}^{\mathrm{free}}=0$, $b_{n,1}^{\mathrm{free}}=-1/(4\sqrt{2})$, $b_{pn,0}^{\mathrm{free}}=b_{pn,1}^{\mathrm{free}}=0$
- The high-density limit considered in this study is 0.2 fm$^{-3}$
- The dynamic response functions are computed at T=10 MeV with momentum transfer $q=3T=30$ MeV at densities $n=0.15$, $0.005$, and $10^{-4}$ fm$^{-3}$

## Context

This work extends earlier HF+RPA calculations of neutrino opacities by Reddy (1997, 1998), Burrows (1998), and Navarro (1999), which did not systematically study the uncertainties from density-dependent nuclear interactions. It also builds on the virial equation of state approach used by Horowitz (2016) for neutral current neutrino-nucleon scattering near the neutrino sphere, and on lattice effective field theory calculations by Alexandru (2019, 2020) that provide ab-initio static structure factors. The results enable improved modeling of neutrino transport in core-collapse supernova simulations and neutron star merger simulations by identifying which EoS-based quantities most strongly influence opacities at different densities.
