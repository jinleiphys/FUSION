# Microscopic aspects of $γ$-softness in atomic nuclei

> The microscopic origin of the $γ$-softness (fluctuations in the triaxiality parameter $γ$ of the nuclear shape) observed in atomic nuclei is studied in the framework of the triaxial projected shell model approach, which is based on the deformed mean-field picture with multi-quasiparticle configuration space. It is demonstrated that the coupling to quasiparticle excitations drives the system from a $γ$-rigid to a $γ$-soft pattern. As an illustrative example for a $γ$-soft nucleus, a detailed study has been performed for the $^{104}$Ru nucleus. The experimental energies and a large sample of measured $E2$ matrix elements available for this nucleus are reproduced quite accurately. The shape invariant analysis of the calculated $E2$ matrix elements elucidates the $γ$-soft nature of $^{104}$Ru.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM), which mixes multi-quasiparticle configurations with a fixed deformation, reproduces the experimental energies and E2 matrix elements of the γ-soft nucleus ¹⁰⁴Ru, showing that γ-softness emerges from quasiparticle admixtures rather than from a collective Bohr Hamiltonian with a shallow potential.

## Method

The authors employed the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) approach, which diagonalizes a shell model Hamiltonian (containing monopole pairing, quadrupole pairing, and quadrupole-quadrupole interactions) in a basis of angular-momentum projected multi-quasiparticle states generated from a triaxially deformed mean field. The basis included the 0-quasiparticle vacuum, two-quasiproton, two-quasineutron, and four-quasiparticle configurations, with fixed deformation parameters ε=0.258 and ε'=0.150 (γ=30.2°). The calculated E2 matrix elements were analyzed using shape invariants to quantify the triaxiality fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameters: ε=0.258 and ε'=0.150, corresponding to a triaxiality parameter γ=30.2°
- Effective charges: 1.5e for protons and 0.5e for neutrons; spin g-factors reduced by a factor of 0.75
- For the ground band, TPSM values of ⟨Q²⟩ ≈ 1.0 (e b)², corresponding to an effective deformation β=0.28
- For the γ-band, TPSM values of ⟨Q²⟩ ≈ 0.7 (e b)², corresponding to β=0.27
- For the ground band, ⟨cos 3δ⟩ = 0.6 corresponds to δ = 18°, with dispersion σ⟨cos 3δ⟩ ≈ 0.3, indicating 70% of the distribution within 9° < δ < 24°
- QCBH dispersion σ⟨cos 3δ⟩ ≈ 0.4 corresponds to a distribution with 70% in the range 0° < δ < 26°
- For the 0⁺₂ state, shape invariant indicates δ = 26°, with 60% component of the K=0 two h₁₁/₂ quasineutron configuration
- For the 0⁺₃ state, 95% component of the K=0 two g₉/₂ quasiproton configuration
- Quasiparticle admixtures to the γ-band reach about 20% for the I=7 state

## Context

This work extends the analysis of γ-softness from the staggering parameter S(I) (studied in Ref. JS21 for a large set of nuclei) to the more direct criteria of shape invariants derived from E2 matrix elements, following the Coulomb excitation experiment on ¹⁰⁴Ru by JS06. The TPSM results are compared with the quadrupole collective Bohr Hamiltonian (QCBH) approach of JS06, which also reproduced the data but overestimated the γ-softness. The paper demonstrates that γ-softness emerges from mixing a relatively small number of quasiparticle configurations, providing a microscopic alternative to the collective Bohr Hamiltonian and the interacting boson model (IBM) of Otsuka (1987) and Nomura (2008).
