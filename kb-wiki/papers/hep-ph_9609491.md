# Nonhermitean Random Matrix Models : a Free Random Variable Approach

> Using the standard concepts of free random variables, we show that for a large class of nonhermitean random matrix models, the support of the eigenvalue distribution follows from their hermitean analogs using a conformal transformation. We also extend the concepts of free random variables to the class of nonhermitean matrices, and apply them to the models discussed by Ginibre-Girko (elliptic ensemble) and Mahaux-Weidenmüller (chaotic resonance scattering).

# Nonhermitean Random Matrix Models: a Free Random Variable Approach

## Key claim

For a large class of nonhermitean random matrix models, the support of the eigenvalue distribution in the complex plane can be derived from the cuts of the corresponding hermitean analog via a conformal transformation, provided the resolvent remains holomorphic; the method does not directly access the nonholomorphic interior of the eigenvalue islands.

## Method

The authors apply Voiculescu's free random variable formalism, specifically the R-transform for addition and S-transform for multiplication of random matrices, to construct resolvents for hermitean ensembles. They then analytically continue the coupling constant (g → ig) to obtain the nonhermitean case, and use a conformal mapping (w = z − 2G(z)) to relate the hermitean spectral cuts to the boundary of the holomorphic region in the complex eigenvalue plane. For the full nonholomorphic interior, they generalize the Blue's function to a 2×2 matrix formalism.

## Key numbers

- For the elliptic ensemble (Ginibre-Girko model), the boundary of the eigenvalue support is an ellipse with axes 2/√(1+g²) and 2g²/√(1+g²); for g²=1 this reduces to a circle (radius √2 for GUE, radius 1 for GOE).
- For the resonance scattering model (Mahaux-Weidenmüller) with fixed channel ratio m=0.25, the critical coupling for structural change in the hermitean analog is g*²(m) = 1/(1 − ∛m)³, yielding g* ≈ 4.44 for m=0.25.
- For the f-case (constrained transition matrix elements), the critical coupling is g*²_f(m) = [∛(1−m) + ∛m]³, which varies between 1 and 4 and is symmetric around m=0.5.
- The spectral density for the product matrix Γ = A²P (with rectangular A of aspect ratio m = M/N) is ν(λ) = (1−m)δ(λ) + (1/2π)√[4m/λ − ((1−m)/λ − 1)²].

## Context

This work extends the free random variable formalism of Voiculescu (1991) and Zee (1994) from hermitean to nonhermitean random matrices. It provides an alternative to the replica method used by Haake (1992) and the supersymmetric method used by Sommers (1993) for analyzing the Mahaux-Weidenmüller resonance scattering model and the Ginibre-Girko elliptic ensemble. The approach enables straightforward computation of eigenvalue supports for nonhermitean ensembles without requiring replica or supersymmetric techniques, and can be applied to chaotic scattering, quantum dots, and nuclear dynamics.
