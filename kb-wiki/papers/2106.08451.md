# Regarding the distribution of glue in the pion

> Understanding why the scale of emergent hadron mass is obvious in the proton but hidden in the pion may rest on mapping the distribution functions (DFs) of all partons within the pion and comparing them with those in the proton; and since glue provides binding in quantum chromodynamics, the glue DF could play a special role. Producing reliable predictions for the proton's DFs is difficult because the proton is a three valence-body bound-state problem. As sketched herein, the situation for the pion, a two valence-body problem, is much better, with continuum and lattice predictions for the valence-quark and glue DFs in agreement. This beginning of theory alignment is timely because experimental facilities now either in operation or planning promise to realise the longstanding goal of providing pion targets, thereby enabling precision experimental tests of rigorous theory predictions concerning Nature's most fundamental Nambu-Goldstone bosons.

## Key claim

Continuum and lattice predictions for the pion's valence-quark and glue distribution functions are now in agreement, but this alignment is conditional on the inclusion of next-to-leading-logarithmic threshold resummation effects in the hard-scattering kernels used for data fitting; without these effects, phenomenological fits disagree with QCD-based predictions and with each other.

## Method

The authors used continuum Schwinger function methods (CSMs) to solve QCD's gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations, obtaining a parameter-free prediction for the pion's valence-quark distribution at the hadron scale, defined as the screening mass m_G = 0.331(2) GeV derived from QCD's process-independent effective charge. They then evolved these distributions to ζ = 2 GeV using one-loop DGLAP equations integrated with the same process-independent effective charge, producing predictions for valence, sea, and glue distributions. These continuum predictions were compared with lattice QCD results from Sufian (2019) for the valence distribution and Fan (2021) for the glue distribution, as well as with phenomenological fits from Gluck (1999), Barry (2018), Novikov (2020), and the NLO+NLL analysis of Aicher (2010).

## Key numbers

- Hadron scale ζ_H = m_G = 0.331(2) GeV, defined from the screening mass in QCD's process-independent effective charge
- At ζ = 2 GeV, the pion's light-front momentum fractions are: ⟨2x q^π_ζ2⟩ = 0.48(4) for valence quarks, ⟨x S^π_ζ2⟩ = 0.11(2) for sea quarks, ⟨x g^π_ζ2⟩ = 0.41(2) for glue
- Valence-quark Mellin moments at ζ = 2 GeV: ⟨x q^π_ζ2⟩ = 0.24(2), ⟨x^2 q^π_ζ2⟩ = 0.094(13), ⟨x^3 q^π_ζ2⟩ = 0.047(08)
- The pion's valence-quark DF at large x behaves as (1-x)^β with β > 2, contradicting phenomenological fits that yield β ≈ 1
- The process-independent effective charge interpolation uses Λ_QCD = 0.234 GeV with parameters a_0 = 0.104(1) GeV^2, a_1 = 0.0975 GeV^2, b_0 = 0.121(1) GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the continuum QCD approach to pion structure developed by Chang (2013), Cui (2020), and Roberts (2021), and provides the first direct comparison between continuum and lattice predictions for the pion's glue distribution, using the recent lattice results of Fan (2021). The agreement between continuum and lattice methods for both valence and glue distributions supports the perspective on emergent hadron mass from Roberts (2016), where the pion's small mass arises from a cancellation between positive one-body-dressing content and negative binding energy. The paper enables future experimental tests at facilities such as JLab and AMBER@CERN, which promise to provide pion targets for precision measurements, and highlights the need for improved data analyses that include next-to-leading-logarithmic threshold resummation effects.
