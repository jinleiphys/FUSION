# Dynamics and Stability of Chiral Fluid

> Starting from the linear sigma model with constituent quarks we derive the chiral fluid dynamics where hydrodynamic equations for the quark fluid are coupled to the equation of motion for the order-parameter field. In a static system at thermal equilibrium this model leads to a chiral phase transition which, depending on the choice of the quark-meson coupling constant, could be a crossover or a first order one. We investigate the stability of the chiral fluid in the static and expanding backgrounds by considering the evolution of perturbations with respect to the mean-field solution. In the static background the spectrum of plane-wave perturbations consists of two branches, one corresponding to the sound waves and another to the sigma-meson excitations. For large couplings these two branches "cross" and the excitation spectrum acquires exponentially growing modes. The stability analysis is also done for the Bjorken-like background solution by explicitly solving the time-dependent differential equation for perturbations in the eta-space. In this case the growth rate of unstable modes is significantly reduced.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in chiral fluid dynamics derived from the linear sigma model, the coupling between the quark fluid and the chiral order parameter can produce exponentially growing unstable modes in the excitation spectrum when the quark-meson coupling constant is large enough to produce a first-order phase transition, but only under the condition that the background state is metastable (on segments AB or DE of the thermodynamic potential) rather than at the global minima.

## Method

The authors start from the linear sigma model with constituent quarks and derive chiral fluid dynamics by coupling ideal hydrodynamic equations for the quark fluid to the equation of motion for the sigma field (the chiral order parameter). They linearize the coupled equations around a static hydrostatic background and around a Bjorken-like expanding background, then solve the resulting dispersion relations for plane-wave perturbations. For the expanding case, they solve the time-dependent differential equation for perturbations in the eta-space explicitly.

## Key numbers

- For the linear sigma model parameters: pion mass \(m_{\pi}=138\) MeV, pion decay constant \(f_{\pi}=93\) MeV, sigma mass in vacuum \(m_{\sigma}=600\) MeV, and the quark-meson coupling constant \(g=3.3\) (crossover) or \(g=4.5\) (first-order transition).
- For \(g=4.5\), the first-order phase transition occurs between temperatures \(T_{\mathrm{D}}=122.7\) MeV and \(T_{\mathrm{B}}=132.1\) MeV, with the transition temperature \(T_{\mathrm{C}}=128.5\) MeV where the two minima have equal height.
- For \(g=4.5\) at metastable points B and D, unstable modes appear for wave numbers \(k < k_{\mathrm{max}} \sim 2\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- For \(g=3.3\) (crossover), no imaginary part appears in the dispersion relation at any temperature studied (130, 140, 150, and 160 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the chiral fluid dynamics model first proposed by Mishustin et al. (2004) and further developed by Paech, Davesne, Pratt, and Kodama. It builds on the mean-field thermodynamics of the linear sigma model studied by Scavenius et al. (2001) and addresses the critical slowing down phenomenon near phase transitions originally discussed by van Hove. The stability analysis in a Bjorken-like expanding background shows that the growth rate of unstable modes is significantly reduced compared to the static case, which is relevant for understanding phase transition dynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies.
