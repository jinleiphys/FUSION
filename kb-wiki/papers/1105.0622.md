# Nonequilibrium chiral fluid dynamics including dissipation and noise

> We present a consistent theoretical approach for the study of nonequilibrium effects in chiral fluid dynamics within the framework of the linear sigma model with constituent quarks. Treating the quarks as an equilibrated heat bath we use the influence functional formalism to obtain a Langevin equation for the sigma field. This allows us to calculate the explicit form of the damping coefficient and the noise correlators. For a selfconsistent derivation of both the dynamics of the sigma field and the quark fluid we have to employ the 2PI (two-particle irreducible) effective action formalism. The energy dissipation from the field to the fluid is treated in the exact formalism of the 2PI effective action where a conserved energy-momentum tensor can be constructed. We derive its form and comment on approximations generating additional terms in the energy-momentum balance of the entire system.

## Key claim

The paper presents a consistent theoretical framework for studying nonequilibrium effects in chiral fluid dynamics by deriving a Langevin equation for the sigma field with explicit damping and noise terms, but the approach is restricted to a first qualitative analysis that neglects pion degrees of freedom and the Polyakov loop, with the quarks treated as an equilibrated heat bath.

## Method

The authors apply the influence functional formalism to the linear sigma model with constituent quarks, perturbatively integrating out the quark degrees of freedom to obtain a Langevin equation for the sigma field with explicit damping coefficient and noise correlators. For a selfconsistent derivation that includes the back reaction on the quark fluid and energy-momentum conservation, they employ the two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action formalism, from which a conserved energy-momentum tensor for the entire system can be constructed.

## Key numbers

* The vacuum pion decay constant is set to \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV and the pion mass to \(m_\pi = 138\) MeV, with the coupling \(\lambda^2 = 20\) yielding a vacuum sigma mass of \(m_\sigma \approx 604\) MeV.
* The quark-meson coupling constant is \(g = 3.3\), giving a constituent quark mass in vacuum of \(m_q = 306.9\) MeV.
* For the first order phase transition scenario at \(g = 5.5\), the critical temperature is \(T_c = 123.27\) MeV and the spinodal temperature is \(T_{\rm sp} = 108\) MeV.
* For the second order phase transition scenario at \(g = 3.63\), the critical temperature is \(T_c = 139.88\) MeV and the sigma mass at the critical point decreases to \(m_\sigma = 26.6\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends existing chiral fluid dynamic models by Mishustin (1998), Paech (2003), and Scavenius (1999), which propagated chiral fields using classical equations of motion without dissipation or noise, by consistently including damping and stochastic terms. The formalism enables future numerical studies of nonequilibrium phenomena at the chiral phase transition in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the effects of critical slowing down and supercooling, and provides a foundation for including pion degrees of freedom and a two-fluid dynamic description in subsequent work.
