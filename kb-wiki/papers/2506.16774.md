# Inclusive quasielastic (anti-)neutrino nucleus scattering within the Standard Model and beyond

> We derive fully general expressions for the inclusive (anti-)neutrino-induced nuclear quasielastic production of a strange or charmed baryon $Y$, considering all dimension-six new physics operators relevant to the semileptonic $q\to q'\ell ν_\ell$ transition with both left- and right-handed neutrino fields. We illustrate the formalism by applying it to the $Λ_c$ production in neutrino nucleus scattering. The nuclear response is computed using a state-of-the-art {\it ab initio} spectral function, based on realistic nuclear many-body wavefunctions obtained via coupled-cluster method and a nuclear Hamiltonian derived from chiral effective field theory. We also highlight the role played by the final state interactions between the produced $Y$ hyperon and the residual nuclear system, which can obscure potential new physics signals in this (anti-)neutrino nucleus reaction. Our results improve upon previous studies that neglected nuclear corrections and uncertainties -- effects we show to be comparable to, or even larger than, those expected from new physics.

## Key claim

The authors derive fully general expressions for inclusive quasielastic (anti-)neutrino-nucleus scattering producing strange or charmed baryons, including all dimension-six new physics operators, and demonstrate that nuclear corrections and uncertainties, previously neglected in studies such as Kong (2023), are comparable to or larger than the expected new physics signals, potentially preventing any new physics discovery claim in this reaction.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Hamiltonian containing all dimension-six vector, axial-vector, scalar, pseudoscalar, tensor, and pseudotensor operators for the semileptonic quark transition, with both left- and right-handed neutrino fields. They compute the nuclear response using a state-of-the-art ab initio hole spectral function based on realistic nuclear many-body wavefunctions obtained via the coupled-cluster method with a chiral effective field theory Hamiltonian. The formalism is illustrated for the specific case of Lambda_c production in nu_tau-nucleus quasielastic scattering, including final state interactions between the produced hyperon and the residual nuclear system.

## Key numbers

* The neutrino threshold energy for the reaction nu_tau n -> Lambda_c tau^- is approximately 8.3 GeV; for the nu_mu n -> Lambda_c mu^- reaction it is approximately 2.6 GeV.
* At threshold for the nu_tau n -> Lambda_c tau reaction, q^2 is approximately -3.68 GeV^2, while the lattice QCD form factors from Meinel (2017) are evaluated for q^2 greater than approximately -0.36 GeV^2.
* The scaled cross-section results are a factor of two smaller than those reported in Yan (2024), which the authors suspect arises from an error in that reference.

## Context

This work extends the nucleon-level analysis of Kong (2023), who proposed the quasielastic neutrino scattering process nu_tau n -> tau^- Lambda_c as a probe of new physics Wilson coefficients for the d-to-c transition, but neglected nuclear effects. The authors incorporate realistic nuclear targets and nuclear structure effects through hole spectral functions, comparing predictions from a state-of-the-art spectral function from Sobczyk (2023) with those based on the local Fermi gas approximation. The results highlight that nuclear uncertainties must be controlled before such reactions can serve as clean probes for beyond-Standard-Model physics, and the formalism enables future studies of other final baryons with arbitrary spin-parity.
