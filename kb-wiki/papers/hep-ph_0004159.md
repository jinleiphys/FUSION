# Relaxing Near the Critical Point

> Critical slowing down of the relaxation of the order parameter is relevant both in early the universe and in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. We study the relaxation rate of the order parameter in an O(N) scalar theory near the critical point to model the non-equilibrium dynamics of critical fluctuations near the chiral phase transition.A lowest order perturbative calculation (two loops in the coupling lambda) reveals the breakdown of perturbation theory for long-wavelength fluctuations in the critical region and the emergence of a hierarchy of scales with hard q>T, semisoft T >> q >> lambda T and soft lambda T>>q loop momenta which are widely separated for weak coupling. The non-perturbative resummation in the large N limit reveals the renormalization of the interaction and the crossover to an effective 3D-theory for soft momenta.The effective 3D coupling goes to the Wilson-Fisher 3D fixed point in the soft limit.The relaxation rate of the order parameter for wave vectors lambda T >>k>> k_{us} or near the critical temperature lambda T>>m_T>>k_{us} with the ultra soft scale k_{us} = [(lambda T)/(4pi)] exp[-(4pi/lambda)] is dominated by classical semisoft loop momentum leading to Gamma(k,T) = lambda T/(2 pi N). For wavectors k<< k_{us} the damping rate is dominated by hard loop momenta and given by Gamma(k,T)=4 pi T/[3N ln(T/k)]. Analogously, for homogeneous fluctuations in the ultracritical region m_T<<k_{us} the damping rate is given by Gamma_0(m_T,T)=4 pi T/[3N ln(T/m_T)]. Thus critical slowing down emerges for ultrasoft fluctuations where the rate is lambda-independent. The strong coupling regime and the shortcomings of the quasiparticle interpretation are discussed.

## Key claim

Critical slowing down of the order parameter relaxation emerges for ultrasoft fluctuations where the damping rate becomes independent of the coupling constant lambda, contradicting the perturbative expectation that the rate would diverge as lambda^2 T^2/k in the long-wavelength limit.

## Method

The authors studied the relaxation rate of long-wavelength fluctuations of the order parameter in an O(N) scalar field theory near the critical point, modeling the non-equilibrium dynamics of critical fluctuations near the chiral phase transition. They first performed a two-loop perturbative calculation that revealed the breakdown of perturbation theory for soft momenta, then implemented a non-perturbative resummation via the large N limit to obtain the damping rate to next-to-leading order in 1/N. The analysis identified a hierarchy of scales for loop momenta: hard (q greater than or equal to T), semisoft (T much greater than q much greater than lambda T), and soft (lambda T much greater than q).

## Key numbers

- The ultrasoft scale is given by k_us = (lambda T)/(4 pi) exp[-(4 pi)/lambda]
- For wavevectors lambda T much greater than k much greater than k_us, the relaxation rate is Gamma(k,T) = lambda T/(2 pi N)
- For wavevectors k much less than k_us, the damping rate is Gamma(k,T) = 4 pi T/[3N ln(T/k)]
- For homogeneous fluctuations in the ultracritical region m_T much less than k_us, the damping rate is Gamma_0(m_T,T) = 4 pi T/[3N ln(T/m_T)]
- The two-loop perturbative result for the classical soft momentum contribution is Gamma_cl(k,T) = (lambda^2 T^2)/(8 pi k) (N+2)/N^2

## Context

This work extends the study of critical slowing down from classical critical phenomena (Forster, 1975; Hohenberg and Halperin, 1977) to a quantum field theory model relevant for the QCD chiral phase transition, which has been argued to belong to the O(4) universality class (Rajagopal and Wilczek, 1993; Pisarski and Wilczek, 1984). The results enable quantitative predictions for non-equilibrium dynamics near the QCD critical point that could be tested in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and in early universe cosmology, and the authors compare their hierarchy of scales to that found in non-abelian plasmas via the hard thermal loop approximation (Le Bellac, 1996; Braaten and Pisarski, 1990; Yaffe, 1995).
