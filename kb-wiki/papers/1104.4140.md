# Alpha-cluster structure and density wave in oblate nuclei

> Pentagon and triangle shapes in Si-28 and C-12 are discussed in relation with nuclear density wave. In the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics calculations, the $K^π=5^-$ band in Si-28 and the $K^π=3^-$ band in C-12 are described by the pentagon and triangle shapes, respectively. These negative-parity bands can be interpreted as the parity partners of the $K^π=0^+$ ground bands and they are constructed from the parity-asymmetric-intrinsic states. The pentagon and the triangle shapes originate in 7alpha and 3alpha cluster structures, respectively. In a mean-field picture, they are described also by the static one-dimensional density wave at the edge of the oblate states. In analysis with ideal alpha cluster models using Brink-Bloch cluster wave functions and that with a simplified model, we show that the static edge density wave for the pentagon and triangle shapes can be understood by spontaneous breaking of axial symmetry, i.e., the instability of the oblate states with respect to the edge density wave. The density wave is enhanced in the Z=N nuclei due to the proton-neutron coherent density waves, while it is suppressed in Z\ne N nuclei.

## Key claim

The pentagon shape in ²⁸Si and triangle shape in ¹²C, which originate from 7α and 3α cluster structures respectively, can be understood as static one-dimensional density waves at the edge of oblate nuclear states arising from spontaneous breaking of axial symmetry; this density wave is enhanced in Z=N nuclei due to proton-neutron coherent density waves but suppressed in Z≠N nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations for ²⁸Si and ¹²C without assuming any cluster structure, using the MV1 central force and G3RS spin-orbit force. They analyzed the single-particle wave functions from the AMD results in a Hartree-Fock representation to identify parity-mixed orbits with pentagon and triangle shapes. They also analyzed ideal α-cluster states using Brink-Bloch cluster wave functions and a simplified particle-hole model to study the instability of oblate states with respect to edge density waves.

## Key numbers

- The 5⁻ state in ²⁸Si is at 9.70 MeV (experimental band head of the Kπ=5⁻ band)
- The 3⁻ state in ¹²C is at 9.64 MeV (experimental band head of the Kπ=3⁻ band)
- In the AMD pentagon orbits of ²⁸Si, about 5% negative-parity component is mixed into the dominant positive-parity component (|c'|²∼0.05)
- In the AMD triangle orbits of ¹²C, about 6% negative-parity component is mixed (|c'|²∼0.06)
- The Gaussian width parameter ν=0.15 fm⁻² was used for ²⁸Si and ν=0.175 fm⁻² for ¹²C
- The spin-orbit force strengths were uI=-uII=2800 MeV
- The calculated B(E2) value for the 2⁺₁→0⁺₁ transition in ²⁸Si is 10.6 W.u. (experimental: 12.7 W.u.)
- The calculated B(E2) value for the 6⁻₁→5⁻₁ transition in the Kπ=5⁻₁ band of ²⁸Si is 14.4 W.u. (experimental: 17 W.u.)
- In the Brink-Bloch 7α analysis, the energy minimum occurs at d²/6∼0.1, indicating ∼10% mixing of negative-parity components in the pentagon orbits

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of negative-parity rotational bands in light nuclei, specifically the Kπ=5⁻ band in ²⁸Si reported by Glatz (1981) and the Kπ=3⁻ band in ¹²C, from the perspective of spontaneous symmetry breaking and density waves. The authors connect the cluster model description using Brink-Bloch wave functions (Brink 1966) with a mean-field picture of static one-dimensional density waves, analogous to the Overhauser density wave in nuclear matter (Overhauser 1960). The analysis enables a unified understanding of α-cluster structures in oblate nuclei and provides a mechanism for why cluster structures are suppressed in neutron-rich nuclei, which can guide future studies of cluster formation in exotic nuclei.
