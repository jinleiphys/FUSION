# The $Λ$-$Σ$ coupling effect in the neutron-rich $Λ$-hypernucleus $_Λ^{10}$Li by microscopic shell model

> We investigate the structure of the neutron-rich $Λ$-hypernucleus $_Λ^{10}$Li by using microscopic shell-model calculations considering a $Λ$-$Σ$ coupling effect. The calculated $Σ$-mixing probability in the $_Λ^{10}$Li ground state is found to be about 0.34 % which is coherently enhanced by the $Λ$-$Σ$ coupling configurations, leading to the energy shift 0.28 MeV which is about 3 times larger than that in $_Λ^{7}$Li. The importance of the $Σ$ configuration obtained by the $ΣN$ interaction and the potentiality of the neutron-rich environment are discussed.

## Key claim

The Σ-mixing probability in the neutron-rich hypernucleus $_Λ^{10}$Li is about 0.34%, which is coherently enhanced by Λ-Σ coupling configurations, leading to an energy shift of 0.28 MeV that is about 3 times larger than that in $_Λ^{7}$Li, but this result depends on the ΣN effective interaction which still has ambiguities.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic shell-model calculations for $_Λ^{10}$Li using a multi-configuration approach with four nucleons inert in the $^4$He core and (A-5) valence nucleons moving in p-shell orbits, with the Λ or Σ hyperon assumed to be in the lowest $0s_{1/2}$ orbit. They used the Cohen-Kurath (8-16) 2BME for the NN interaction and YN effective interactions based on the NSC97e,f potentials, treating the Λ-Σ coupling as a perturbation to calculate Σ-mixing probabilities and energy shifts via first-order perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- Σ-mixing probability in the $_Λ^{10}$Li ground state: $P_Σ = 0.345\%$ (renormalized $P_Σ^{(R)} = 0.344\%$)
- Energy shift for $_Λ^{10}$Li ground state: $\Delta E = 0.280$ MeV
- Energy shift for $_Λ^{7}$Li ground state: $\Delta E = 0.085$ MeV (comparable to Millener's result of 0.078 MeV)
- Energy shift for $_Λ^{11}$B ground state: $\Delta E = 0.073$ MeV (comparable to Millener's result of 0.066 MeV)
- Σ-mixing probability breakdown for $_Λ^{10}$Li ground state: $P_{Σ^-} = 0.183\%$, $P_{Σ^0} = 0.159\%$, $P_{Σ^+} = 0.002\%$
- Energy difference between Σ and Λ ground states: $E_{g.s.}^Σ - E_{g.s.}^Λ = 69.3$ MeV
- Fermi-type coupling contribution to Σ-mixing: $\sum |D^F|^2 = 0.144\%$
- Gamow-Teller-type coupling contribution: $\sum |D^{GT}|^2 = 0.098\%$
- Combined Fermi and Gamow-Teller contribution: $0.350\%$ (close to full calculated $P_Σ = 0.345\%$)

## Context

This work extends the shell-model calculations of Gal, Soper and Dalitz (1970s) and Millener (2007) for p-shell Λ-hypernuclei to the neutron-rich case $_Λ^{10}$Li, which was first measured by Saha et al. (2005) via the double-charge exchange reaction $(\pi^-, K^+)$ on a $^{10}$B target. The results address the discrepancy between experimental production cross sections and theoretical calculations by Tretyakova and Lanskoy (2003), suggesting the importance of Σ-mixing in neutron-rich hypernuclei. This work enables future studies of hyperon behavior in neutron-excess environments relevant to neutron star equation of state softening, as discussed by Baldo (2000).
