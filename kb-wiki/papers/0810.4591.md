# The $풑$-$풖$ coupling effect in the neutron-rich $풑$-hypernucleus $_풑^{10}$Li by microscopic shell model

> We investigate the structure of the neutron-rich $풑$-hypernucleus $_풑^{10}$Li by using microscopic shell-model calculations considering a $풑$-$풖$ coupling effect. The calculated $풖$-mixing probability in the $_풑^{10}$Li ground state is found to be about 0.34 % which is coherently enhanced by the $풑$-$풖$ coupling configurations, leading to the energy shift 0.28 MeV which is about 3 times larger than that in $_풑^{7}$Li. The importance of the $풖$ configuration obtained by the $풖N$ interaction and the potentiality of the neutron-rich environment are discussed.

## Key claim

The 풖-mixing probability in the neutron-rich hypernucleus $_풑^{10}$Li is about 0.34%, which is coherently enhanced by 풑-풖 coupling configurations, leading to an energy shift of 0.28 MeV that is about 3 times larger than that in $_풑^{7}$Li, but this result depends on the 풖N effective interaction which still has ambiguities.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic shell-model calculations for $_풑^{10}$Li using a multi-configuration approach with four nucleons inert in the $^4$He core and (A-5) valence nucleons moving in p-shell orbits, with the 풑 or 풖 hyperon assumed to be in the lowest $0s_{1/2}$ orbit. They used the Cohen-Kurath (8-16) 2BME for the NN interaction and YN effective interactions based on the NSC97e,f potentials, treating the 풑-풖 coupling as a perturbation to calculate 풖-mixing probabilities and energy shifts via first-order perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- 풖-mixing probability in the $_풑^{10}$Li ground state: $P_풖 = 0.345\%$ (renormalized $P_풖^{(R)} = 0.344\%$)
- Energy shift for $_풑^{10}$Li ground state: $\Delta E = 0.280$ MeV
- Energy shift for $_풑^{7}$Li ground state: $\Delta E = 0.085$ MeV (comparable to Millener's result of 0.078 MeV)
- Energy shift for $_풑^{11}$B ground state: $\Delta E = 0.073$ MeV (comparable to Millener's result of 0.066 MeV)
- 풖-mixing probability breakdown for $_풑^{10}$Li ground state: $P_{풖^-} = 0.183\%$, $P_{풖^0} = 0.159\%$, $P_{풖^+} = 0.002\%$
- Energy difference between 풖 and 풑 ground states: $E_{g.s.}^풖 - E_{g.s.}^풑 = 69.3$ MeV
- Fermi-type coupling contribution to 풖-mixing: $\sum |D^F|^2 = 0.144\%$
- Gamow-Teller-type coupling contribution: $\sum |D^{GT}|^2 = 0.098\%$
- Combined Fermi and Gamow-Teller contribution: $0.350\%$ (close to full calculated $P_풖 = 0.345\%$)

## Context

This work extends the shell-model calculations of Gal, Soper and Dalitz (1970s) and Millener (2007) for p-shell 풑-hypernuclei to the neutron-rich case $_풑^{10}$Li, which was first measured by Saha et al. (2005) via the double-charge exchange reaction $(\pi^-, K^+)$ on a $^{10}$B target. The results address the discrepancy between experimental production cross sections and theoretical calculations by Tretyakova and Lanskoy (2003), suggesting the importance of 풖-mixing in neutron-rich hypernuclei. This work enables future studies of hyperon behavior in neutron-excess environments relevant to neutron star equation of state softening, as discussed by Baldo (2000).
