# Relativistic Mean-Field Approach in Nuclear Systems

> A new scheme to study the properties of finite nuclei is proposed based on the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach starting from a bare nucleon-nucleon interaction. The relativistic structure of the nucleon self-energies in nuclear matter depending on density, momentum and isospin asymmetry are determined through a subtracted T-matrix technique and parameterized, which makes them easily accessible for general use. The scalar and vector potentials of a single particle in nuclei are generated via a local density approximation (LDA). The surface effect of finite nuclei can be taken into account by an improved LDA (ILDA), which has successfully been applied in microscopic derivations of the optical model potential for nucleon-nucleus scattering. The bulk properties of nuclei can be determined in a self-consistent scheme for nuclei all over the nuclear mass table. Calculated binding energies agree very well with the empirical data, while the predicted values for radii and spin-orbit splitting of single-particle energies are about 10 \% smaller than the experimental data. Basic features of more sophisticated DBHF calculations for finite nuclei are reproduced.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new, simple scheme to compute bulk properties of finite nuclei using a parameterized Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) self-energy in a local density approximation (LDA) with an improved surface treatment (ILDA). The scheme reproduces binding energies very well, but the predicted charge radii and spin-orbit splittings are systematically about 10% and 15% smaller than experimental data, respectively, a discrepancy shared with more sophisticated DBHF calculations.

## Method

The nucleon self-energy in asymmetric nuclear matter was computed using the DBHF approach with the Bonn A one-boson-exchange potential and a subtracted T-matrix technique, then parameterized as a function of density, single-particle energy, and isospin asymmetry. For finite nuclei, the scalar and vector potentials were obtained via a local density approximation (LDA) and iterated self-consistently in the Dirac equation. Surface effects were included by folding the LDA potentials with a Gaussian of width \(t\) (the Improved LDA, ILDA), with \(t\) adjusted to reproduce binding energies.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon for \(^{16}\)O: ILDA gives \(-7.97\) MeV, experiment gives \(-7.98\) MeV.
- Binding energy per nucleon for \(^{208}\)Pb: ILDA gives \(-7.88\) MeV, experiment gives \(-7.87\) MeV.
- Charge radius for \(^{16}\)O: ILDA gives 2.46 fm, experiment gives 2.70 fm (about 9% smaller).
- Charge radius for \(^{208}\)Pb: ILDA gives 5.08 fm, experiment gives 5.50 fm (about 8% smaller).
- Spin-orbit splitting \(\Delta E_{\pi p}^{ls}\) for \(^{16}\)O: ILDA gives 5.0 MeV, experiment gives 6.3 MeV (about 21% smaller).
- The ILDA surface correction to binding energy per nucleon follows \((E^\text{ILDA}-E^\text{LDA})/A = 17.2 \times A^{-1/3} - 2.5\) MeV.
- The Gaussian width parameter \(t\) is parameterized as \(t = 1.35 - 0.13 A^{1/3} - 0.17 (N-Z)/A\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the local-density approach previously used to derive the CTOM optical model potential for nucleon-nucleus scattering (Ruirui Xu et al., 2016) to the self-consistent calculation of bound-state properties. It contrasts with the direct DBHF calculations for finite nuclei by Shen et al. (2017), which are more computationally demanding but yield similar discrepancies in radii and spin-orbit splitting. The ILDA scheme enables rapid evaluation of bulk properties across the nuclear mass table and can be extended to deformed nuclei.
