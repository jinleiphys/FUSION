# Equivalence of model space techniques and the renormalization group for a separable model problem

> Lee-Suzuki similarity transformations and Krencigowa-Kuo folded diagrams are two common methods used to derive energy independent model space effective interactions for nuclear many-body systems. We demonstrate that these methods are equivalent to a Renormalization Group (RG) analysis of a separable potential model. The effective low-momentum potentials V_{eff} are shown to give the same scaling equation that RG arguments predict. We find the new result that the different model space techniques considered in this paper yield a unique low-momentum V_{eff} when applied to the toy model problem.

# Equivalence of model space techniques and the renormalization group for a separable model problem

## Key claim

For a separable potential toy model, the Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation and the Krenciglowa-Kuo folded diagram methods yield the same low-momentum effective potential as a Wilsonian renormalization group analysis, and all methods of solving the decoupling equation give a unique low-momentum V_eff when applied to this specific model problem. This uniqueness result is surprising because it is known that for bound state (shell model) problems, different model space techniques often converge to different V_eff's.

## Method

The authors constructed a separable potential model Hamiltonian H = H_0 + g|η⟩⟨η| and considered infinitesimal decimations of the momentum-space cutoff from Λ to Λ−δΛ, where the model space P consists of states with momenta k ≤ Λ−δΛ and the excluded Q-space consists of states with momenta in the shell Λ−δΛ < k ≤ Λ. They derived analytic expressions for the wave operator ω from both the Andreozzi-Lee-Suzuki (ALS) and Andreozzi-Krenciglowa-Kuo (AKK) iteration schemes in the limit δΛ→0, and compared these to a renormalization group analysis where the coupling g is allowed to run with Λ such that the half-on-shell T-matrix from the Lippmann-Schwinger equation is independent of the UV cutoff.

## Key numbers

- The model space cutoff is defined as Λ/s, where s > 1, and the excluded Q-space consists of momenta in the shell Λ/s < k ≤ Λ; for infinitesimal decimations, Λ/s = Λ − δΛ.
- The ALS wave operator for infinitesimal decimations is ω(Λ,p) = −g η_Λ η_p / (Λ² − p²) = −V_eff(Λ,p) / (Λ² − p²).
- The scaling equation derived from both model space methods is ∂/∂Λ V_eff(p',p) = Λ² V_eff(p',Λ) V_eff(Λ,p) / (Λ² − p²).
- The same scaling equation is obtained from the RG analysis by setting ∂_Λ T⁻¹ = 0, where the half-on-shell T-matrix is 1/⟨k'|T(ω=k²)|k⟩ = 1/(g η_k' η_k) − (1/(η_k' η_k)) ∫₀^Λ q² η_q²/(k²−q²) dq.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study of Fields, Vary, and Gupta (1996) on effective interactions for simple model problems, but adopts a Wilsonian view where the UV regulator is kept finite and V_eff scales as the model space boundary is varied, rather than sending the regulator to infinity. The results show quantitative similarity between nuclear shell-model effective interaction methods and modern renormalization group techniques, following the spirit of Haxton and Song (2000) who sought to formulate a rigorous shell model using RG concepts. The paper enables the application of model space techniques to low-energy nuclear physics within the Effective Field Theory framework of Weinberg (1990), Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998), and Epelbaum, Glöckle, and Meißner (1998), and the authors state that generalization to realistic NN potentials (Paris, Bonn-A, V-18) will appear in a forthcoming paper.
