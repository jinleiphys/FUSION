# Renormalization of the SU(2)-symmetric model of hadrodynamics

> It is proved that the SU(2)-symmetric model of hadrodynamics can well be set up on the gauge-invariance principle. The quantization of the model can readily be performed in the Lagrangian path-integral formalisms by using the Lagrangian undetermined multiplier method. Furthermore, it is shown that the quantum theory is invariant with respect to a kind of BRST-transformations. From the BRST-symmetry of the theory, the Ward-Takahashi identities satisfied by the generating functionals of full Green functions, connected Green functions and proper vertex functions are successively derived. As an application of the above Ward-Takahashi identities, the Ward-Takahashi identities obeyed by the propagators and various proper vertices are derived. Based on these identities, the propagators and vertices are perfectly renormalized. Especially, as a result of the renormalization, the Slavnov-Taylor identity satisfied by renormalization constants is natually deduced. To demonstrate the renormalizability of the theory, the one-loop renormalization of the theory is carried out by means of the mass-dependent momentum space subtraction and the renormalization group approach, giving an exact one-loop effective coupling constant and one-loop effective nucleon, pion and $ρ-$meson masses.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the SU(2)-symmetric model of hadrodynamics, previously considered non-gauge-invariant and unrenormalizable due to the rho-meson mass term, can be consistently set up on the gauge-invariance principle and perfectly renormalized, provided the Lorentz condition is imposed as a constraint from the beginning and the gauge-invariance is examined from the action in the physical subspace rather than from the Lagrangian.

## Method

The authors quantize the SU(2) hadrodynamics model using the Lagrangian path-integral formalism with the Lagrange undetermined multiplier method, incorporating the Lorentz condition as a constraint and introducing ghost fields via a ghost equation that eliminates residual gauge degrees of freedom. They derive BRST-transformations for all fields, including a modified transformation for the ghost field C^a that acquires a mass term proportional to sigma^2 in general gauges, and from the BRST symmetry they derive Ward-Takahashi identities for generating functionals of full Green functions, connected Green functions, and proper vertex functions. Using these identities, they renormalize the propagators and vertices, and perform a one-loop renormalization using mass-dependent momentum space subtraction and the renormalization group approach.

## Key numbers

* The rho-meson mass m_rho appears in the Lagrangian as the coefficient of the term (1/2) m_rho^2 A^{a mu} A_mu^a, and the pion mass m_pi appears in the term -(1/2) m_pi^2 pi^2.
* The parameter sigma^2 = alpha m_rho^2 is introduced, where alpha is the gauge parameter; in the Landau gauge (alpha = 0), sigma = 0 and the ghost transformation reduces to the form similar to QCD.
* The one-loop renormalization yields an exact one-loop effective coupling constant and one-loop effective nucleon, pion, and rho-meson masses.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier papers (Su and Wang, 2005-2006) which argued that a non-Abelian massive gauge field theory with equal gauge boson masses can be set up on gauge-invariance without the Higgs mechanism, and it generalizes the U(1)-symmetric hadrodynamics (Su and Wang, 2005) to the SU(2) case. The paper directly contradicts the previous conclusion of unrenormalizability of the model (references 4-9 in the text), which the authors attribute to incorrect quantum theories that did not eliminate unphysical degrees of freedom via appropriate constraints. The derived Ward-Takahashi identities and the Slavnov-Taylor identity for renormalization constants enable systematic renormalization of the model and provide a foundation for practical calculations using renormalization group equations.
