# Study of the azimuthal asymmetry in heavy ion collisions combining initial state momentum orientation and final state collective effects

> In the present work we investigate the source of azimuthal asymmetry for nuclear collision using a model that contemplates particles produced in the initial hard collisions and the collective effects described by a Blast-Wave like expansion. The latter is described by the relaxation time approximation of the Boltzmann transport equation. The parameters regarding collective flow and asymmetry are fitted by the experimental data from $p_T$ spectrum and $v_2$ for PbPb and XeXe collisions at different centrality classes. As a by-product the ratio of final elliptic flow with the initial anisotropy, $v_2/ε_2$, and the average transverse momentum are predicted.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a combined model incorporating initial-state hard scattering (with azimuthal asymmetry from dipole orientation) and final-state collective effects (via a Blast-Wave-like expansion in the relaxation time approximation of the Boltzmann transport equation) can simultaneously describe the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) and elliptic flow \(v_2\) for PbPb and XeXe collisions at LHC energies, but only for centralities above 5-10% because the initial distribution alone cannot generate sufficient asymmetry for very central collisions.

## Method

The authors model charged pion production using \(k_T\)-factorization with a QCD color dipole amplitude that includes an angular modulation parameter \(a_r\) to introduce initial-state azimuthal asymmetry. The final-state collective expansion is described by the relaxation time approximation of the Boltzmann transport equation, where the equilibrium distribution follows a Boltzmann-Gibbs-Blast-Wave model with an anisotropic velocity profile \(\rho = \rho_0 + \rho_a \cos(2\phi_s)\) and a spatial anisotropy parameter \(s_2\). The parameters \(T_{eq}\), \(\beta_s\), \(\rho_a\), \(s_2\), and the ratio \(t_f/t_r\) are fitted simultaneously to ALICE data for the \(p_T\) spectrum and \(v_2\) in PbPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV and XeXe collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=5.44\) TeV across multiple centrality classes.

## Key numbers

- For PbPb collisions at 5-10% centrality: \(T_{eq} = 0.229\) GeV, \(\beta_s = 0.504\), \(\rho_a = 0.0386\), \(s_2 = 0.0590\), \(t_f/t_r = 2.05\), \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 0.990\)
- For PbPb collisions at 50-60% centrality: \(T_{eq} = 0.090\) GeV, \(\beta_s = 0.911\), \(\rho_a = 0.202\), \(s_2 = 0.164\), \(t_f/t_r = 0.89\), \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 1.122\)
- For XeXe collisions at 5-10% centrality: \(T_{eq} = 0.190\) GeV, \(\beta_s = 0.671\), \(\rho_a = 0.0351\), \(s_2 = 0.057\), \(t_f/t_r = 2.03\), \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 0.556\)
- For XeXe collisions at 50-60% centrality: \(T_{eq} = 0.151\) GeV, \(\beta_s = 0.767\), \(\rho_a = 0.0771\), \(s_2 = 0.324\), \(t_f/t_r = 0.31\), \(\chi^2/\text{dof} = 0.098\)
- Initial asymmetry parameter \(a_r\) for PbPb: \(0.668 \pm 0.068\) at 5-10% centrality, decreasing to \(0.114 \pm 0.015\) at 60-70% centrality
- Initial asymmetry parameter \(a_r\) for XeXe: \(0.70 \pm 0.42\) at 5-10% centrality, decreasing to \(0.189 \pm 0.057\) at 50-60% centrality
- Average radial flow \(\langle \beta_r \rangle\) for PbPb at 5-10% centrality: \(0.336 \pm 0.024\)
- The \(p_T\) spectrum and \(v_2\) were fitted in the interval \(0.1 < p_{T_h} < 8\) GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous approach (Moriggi et al., 2020; Moriggi et al., 2022) that described light hadron spectra in PbPb collisions and small systems using the same \(k_T\)-factorization formalism with nuclear shadowing. It addresses the \(R_{AA} \otimes v_2\) puzzle, where parton energy loss models tuned to describe \(R_{AA}\) underestimate \(v_2\) at intermediate transverse momentum, as noted by Molnar (2013), Noronha-Hostler (2016), and others. The approach contrasts with hydrodynamic models (Romatschke, 2007; Song, 2010; Gale, 2012) that work well at low \(p_T \lesssim 2\) GeV and jet quenching models (Wang, 1992; Vitev, 2002) that work at high \(p_T \gtrsim 10\) GeV, by providing a unified description in the intermediate region using weak coupling methods from saturation physics. The work enables further investigation into how initial-state effects versus final-state collective effects contribute to elliptic flow, and can be extended to include energy loss or color transparency models at higher \(p_T\).
