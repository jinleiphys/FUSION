# Roles of quark-pair correlations in baryon structure and non-leptonic weak transitions of hyperon

> Roles of quark-pair correlations in the baryon structure and the hyperon non-leptonic weak decay are studied within the non-relativistic constituent quark model. We construct the SU(3) ground state baryons by solving the three body problem rigorously with the confinement force and the short range spin-dependent attraction. We emphasize the importance of the $s=0$ quark-quark correlation to reproduce the $ΔI=1/2$ enhancement of the hyperon decay, and demonstrate that resulting static properties as well as the decay amplitudes agree with the experiments, if we deal with the $s=0$ correlation properly. Special attention is also put on the consequences of the SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry breaking due to the $s=0$ correlation. Calculated magnetic moments are the almost same as the naive SU(6) predictions in spite of the existence of the strong correlations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that including a short-range spin-dependent attraction specifically for spin-0 quark pairs in a non-relativistic constituent quark model can reproduce the observed enhancement of $\Delta I=1/2$ non-leptonic hyperon decay amplitudes, but the calculated magnetic moments remain almost unchanged from naive SU(6) predictions despite the strong correlations.

## Method

The authors solved the non-relativistic three-body problem for SU(3) ground-state baryons using the coupled-rearrangement-channel variational method with Gaussian basis functions, incorporating a confinement force (harmonic oscillator) and a spin-dependent Gaussian attraction that acts only on spin-0 quark pairs. They fixed model parameters (confinement strength $K=0.005$ GeV$^3$, range $\beta=0.5$ fm, strength $C_{SS}/m_u^2=1.4$ GeV) to reproduce the nucleon and $\Delta$ masses, proton radius, and the $\Sigma^+ \to n\pi^+$ weak decay amplitude. Weak decay matrix elements were evaluated using the baryon pole approximation with the PCAC soft-pion theorem.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses: $m_u = m_d = 330$ MeV, $m_s = 510$ MeV
- For the nucleon with a spin-0 quark pair: average Jacobi distances $\bar r = 0.92$ fm and $\bar R = 0.97$ fm; for spin-1 pair: $\bar r = 1.1$ fm and $\bar R = 0.81$ fm
- Weak matrix element $\langle n | {\cal H}_W | \Lambda \rangle = -0.946 \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$ with correlation, versus $-0.310 \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$ without
- Weak matrix element $\langle p | {\cal H}_W | \Sigma^+ \rangle = 2.86 \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$ with correlation, versus $0.762 \times 10^{-2}$ GeV$^3$ without
- Ratio $\langle p | H_W | \Sigma^+ \rangle / \langle n | H_W | \Lambda \rangle = -3.02$ with correlation, compared to SU(6) value $-2.4494$
- Parity-conserving weak amplitudes (in $10^{-7}$ units): $\Sigma^+_0$ pole = 24.0, total = 26.1 (exp. 26.24); $\Sigma^+_+$ pole = 43.3, total = 43.3 (exp. 41.83); $\Lambda^0_0$ pole = $-3.82$, total = $-8.84$ (exp. $-15.61$)
- Magnetic moments: proton full = 2.75 (exp. 2.79); neutron full = $-1.78$ (exp. $-1.91$); $\Lambda$ full = $-0.60$ (exp. $-0.61$); $\Sigma^+$ full = 2.67 (exp. 2.46); $\Sigma^-$ full = $-1.05$ (exp. $-1.16$); $\Xi^0$ full = $-1.40$ (exp. $-1.25$)

## Context

This work extends earlier constituent quark model studies (e.g., Harmonic Oscillator and MIT bag models) that had failed to reproduce the absolute magnitude of non-leptonic hyperon decay amplitudes, typically giving only about half the experimental values. The authors build on the observation by Suzuki (1999) that weak matrix elements are sensitive to short-range quark-quark correlations, and on theoretical arguments for strong spin-0 diquark correlations. The results enable future studies of SU(6) symmetry breaking in baryon structure, showing that static properties like magnetic moments remain robust while local wave function features and weak decay amplitudes are significantly modified.
