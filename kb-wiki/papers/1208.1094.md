# Spin-dependent WIMP scattering off nuclei

> Chiral effective field theory (EFT) provides a systematic expansion for the coupling of WIMPs to nucleons at the momentum transfers relevant to direct cold dark matter detection. We derive the currents for spin-dependent WIMP scattering off nuclei at the one-body level and include the leading long-range two-body currents, which are predicted in chiral EFT. As an application, we calculate the structure factor for spin-dependent WIMP scattering off 129,131Xe nuclei, using nuclear interactions that have been developed to study nuclear structure and double-beta decays in this region. We provide theoretical error bands due to the nuclear uncertainties of WIMP currents in nuclei.

# Spin-dependent WIMP scattering off nuclei

## Key claim
Chiral effective field theory predicts that two-body currents reduce the isovector part of the spin-dependent WIMP-nucleus structure factor by 25-55% compared to one-body calculations alone, with the range providing an estimate of theoretical uncertainties from nuclear currents. This reduction is significant for momentum transfers below about 100 MeV, but the effect is smaller at higher momentum transfers due to a momentum-dependent modification of the pseudoscalar part that has opposite sign.

## Method
The authors derived one-body and leading long-range two-body axial-vector currents for spin-dependent WIMP-nucleus scattering using chiral effective field theory up to order Q^3. They applied these currents to calculate the axial-vector structure factors for 129Xe and 131Xe using large-scale shell-model calculations with the gcn5082 interaction in a valence space comprising the 0g7/2, 1d5/2, 1d3/2, 2s1/2, and 0h11/2 orbitals on top of a 100Sn core, with the 129Xe calculation restricted to at most 3 particles in the 1d3/2, 2s1/2, and 0h11/2 orbitals (matrix dimension 3.5 × 10^8). The two-body currents were included via normal ordering, treating them as density-dependent one-body currents in a Fermi gas approximation for the core.

## Key numbers
- For 129Xe: ⟨S_n⟩ = 0.329, ⟨S_p⟩ = 0.010; for 131Xe: ⟨S_n⟩ = -0.272, ⟨S_p⟩ = -0.009 (spin expectation values at zero momentum transfer)
- Two-body currents reduce the isovector structure factor S11 by 25-55% for momentum transfers p ≲ 100 MeV (u ≲ 0.7)
- The axial coupling form factor scale Λ_A = 1040 MeV; pion mass m_π = 138.04 MeV; pion decay constant F_π = 92.4 MeV; g_πpn = 13.05
- Nuclear densities used for two-body current estimates: ρ = 0.10 to 0.12 fm^{-3}, giving I(ρ, P=0) = 0.58 to 0.60
- Magnetic moments calculated: μ = -0.72 μ_N for 129Xe and 0.86 μ_N for 131Xe, agreeing within ~20% with experiment
- For 131Xe at p ~ 250 MeV, the relative effect of chiral two-body currents is ~10-15% smaller than at p ~ 100 MeV

## Context
This work extends chiral EFT calculations of weak neutral currents, previously applied to neutrino breakup of the deuteron for SNO and to Gamow-Teller and double-beta decays (Menendez 2011), to spin-dependent WIMP-nucleus scattering. It provides the first calculation of spin-dependent WIMP currents in nuclei including leading long-range two-body currents from chiral EFT. The results differ substantially from earlier calculations by Ressell and Dean (1997) and Toivanen et al. (2009), which used smaller valence spaces and less tested nuclear interactions; the present work uses the largest valence spaces accessible and interactions tested against nuclear structure and double-beta decay data. The predicted reduction of the isovector structure factor should be included in experimental limits on WIMP couplings from direct detection experiments such as XENON.
