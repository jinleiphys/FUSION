# Magnetoviscosity of relativistic plasma

> Using first-principles quantum field-theoretical methods, we investigate the shear and bulk viscosities of strongly magnetized relativistic plasmas. The analysis is performed within the weak-coupling approximation and utilizes known results for the fermion damping rates in the Landau-level representation, $Γ_{n}(p_{z})$, which are dominated by one-to-two and two-to-one processes in the presence of a strong magnetic field. The transverse and longitudinal components of the viscosities are derived using Kubo's linear response theory. Our results reveal a pronounced anisotropy in both shear and bulk viscosities induced by the magnetic field. In the case of an electron-positron plasma, where the weak-coupling approximation is well justified, the dimensionless longitudinal shear viscosity $η_{\parallel}/T^3$ increases rapidly with the magnetic field strength, while the transverse component $η_{\perp}/T^3$ decreases and can even drop below the KSS bound at sufficiently large fields. In contrast, both the dimensionless longitudinal and transverse bulk viscosities, $ζ_{\perp}/T^3$ and $ζ_{\parallel}/T^3$, initially rise from small values, reach a maximum, and then gradually decrease toward zero. We find that the bulk viscosity is highly sensitive to the longitudinal and transverse components of the sound velocity, particularly at high magnetic fields, indicating that its quantitative values should be interpreted with caution. We also calculate an additional cross viscosity, which is negative and whose magnitude increases with the magnetic field strength. Finally, we discuss the physical implications of these magnetoviscosity results in the contexts of magnetar physics and the strongly magnetized quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Using first-principles quantum field theory within the weak-coupling approximation, the authors show that strong magnetic fields induce pronounced anisotropy in both shear and bulk viscosities of relativistic plasmas, with the transverse shear viscosity potentially dropping below the Kovtun-Son-Starinets (KSS) bound at sufficiently large fields, but the quantitative values of bulk viscosity should be interpreted with caution due to high sensitivity to the longitudinal and transverse components of the sound velocity.

## Method

The authors applied Kubo's linear response theory to express shear, bulk, and cross viscosity coefficients in terms of retarded correlation functions of the energy-momentum tensor, using the spectral representation of fermion propagators in the Landau-level basis. The fermion damping rates in the Landau-level representation, dominated by one-to-two and two-to-one processes, were incorporated into the spectral functions. The analytical expressions were derived for an electron-positron (QED) plasma and extended to a quark-gluon (QCD) plasma, with contributions from charged fermions only, excluding gauge boson contributions.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless longitudinal shear viscosity η∥/T³ increases rapidly with magnetic field strength, while the transverse component η⊥/T³ decreases and can drop below the KSS bound at sufficiently large fields.
- Both dimensionless longitudinal and transverse bulk viscosities, ζ⊥/T³ and ζ∥/T³, initially rise from small values, reach a maximum, and then gradually decrease toward zero.
- The cross viscosity ζ× is negative and its magnitude increases with magnetic field strength.
- The electron mass is taken as m ≈ 0.511 MeV for the QED plasma calculations.
- The photon damping rate used for parametric estimates is Γγ ∼ α|eB|/T, giving ηγ ∼ T⁵/(α|eB|).

## Context

This work extends the foundational developments in relativistic magnetohydrodynamics from Huang (2011), Critelli (2014), Finazzo (2016), Grozdanov (2016), Hattori (2017), Hernandez (2017), and Hattori (2022), who analyzed how strong magnetic fields modify viscous coefficients. The authors show that contrary to the conclusion of Hattori (2017) that only the longitudinal component of bulk viscosity is nontrivial within leading-log and lowest Landau level approximations, contributions from higher Landau levels are essential even in extremely strong fields, making the transverse bulk viscosity nonzero. The results enable more consistent incorporation of magnetic field effects into theoretical descriptions of magnetar physics and the strongly magnetized quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions.
