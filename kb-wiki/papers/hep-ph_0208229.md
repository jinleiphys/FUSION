# Photon Production from Nonequilibrium Disoriented Chiral Condensates in a Spherical Expansion

> We study the production of photons through the non-equilibrium relaxation of a disoriented chiral condensate formed in the expanding hot central region in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. It is found that the expansion smoothes out the resonances in the process of parametric amplification such that the non-equilibrium photons are dominant to the thermal photons over the range 0.2-2 GeV. We propose that to search for non-equilibrium photons in the direct photon measurements of heavy-ion collisions can be a potential test of the formation of disoriented chiral condensates.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in an expanding spherical geometry, the resonances from parametric amplification in photon production from disoriented chiral condensates (DCCs) are smoothed out, making non-equilibrium photons dominant over thermal photons in the 0.2–2 GeV range, but this conclusion depends on the specific assumption of spherical boost-invariant hydrodynamical expansion and the choice of initial DCC amplitude.

## Method

The authors model the DCC as an O(N+1) linear sigma model with an effective Lagrangian that includes both the anomalous U_A(1) π⁰γγ vertex and a vector-meson-mediated (ω meson) π⁰γγ interaction. They implement the Schwinger-Keldysh closed-time-path formalism in a conformally flat Robertson-Walker metric approximating spherical boost-invariant expansion, solve the large-N equations of motion for the mean fields and mode functions self-consistently, and compute the photon spectral number density. For comparison, they also compute equilibrium thermal photon emission from an expanding quark-gluon plasma and hadronic matter using a Bjorken-like spherical hydrodynamical model with a first-order phase transition, incorporating two-loop HTL rates for the QGP phase and hadronic reactions (ππ→ργ, πρ→πγ, ω→πγ, ρ→ππγ) for the hadronic phase.

## Key numbers

- Non-equilibrium photons dominate over thermal photons in the energy range **0.2–2 GeV**.
- Initial π⁰ mean field amplitude set to **ζ(0) = 0.3 fm⁻¹** with initial derivative **ζ̇(0) = 0** and σ mean field **φ(0) = 0**.
- Phenomenological parameters used: **m_σ ≈ 600 MeV**, **f_π ≈ 93 MeV**, **λ ≈ 4.5**, **T_c ≈ 200 MeV**, **h ≈ (120 MeV)³**, **m_V ≈ 782 MeV**, **λ_V ≈ 0.36**.
- Ultraviolet momentum cutoff **Λ ≈ 1 GeV**.
- Initial temperature **T_i > 200 MeV** and initial proper time **τ_i** (value not specified numerically in the text).
- Effective degeneracy for hadron gas parametrized as **g_H(T) ∼ 13 (T/fm⁻¹)^δ** with **δ ≈ 3.4**.

## Context

This work extends the earlier studies of photon production from DCCs by Boyanovsky et al. (2000) and by two of the present authors (Lee and Ng, 2001), which used a simple instantaneous "quench" scenario from an initial temperature above T_c to zero temperature without expansion. The inclusion of spherical hydrodynamical expansion modifies the parametric amplification dynamics and spinoidal instabilities found in those quench calculations. The results enable experimental searches for non-equilibrium photons in direct photon measurements at RHIC and LHC as a potential test of DCC formation, complementing the soft-pion signature that has been sought but not seen at CERN SPS (Aggarwal et al., 2000).
