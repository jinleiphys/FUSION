# Liquid-gas instability and superfluidity in nuclear matter

> We study effects of the medium polarization on superfluidity in symmetric nuclear matter in a relativistic formalism. An effect of the liquid-gas instability is emphasized. We examine two types of decomposition of the nucleon propagator; the standard Feynman-density and the particle-hole-antiparticle ones. In both cases, the medium polarization effect is determined by a characteristic cancellation among the σ, the longitudinal ω, and the σ-ωmixed polarizations. The instability leads to increase of pairing gap. Around the saturation density that is free from the instability the medium polarization enhances pairing gap in the former case whereas reduces in the latter. At the lowest density that is also free from the instability the gap increases in both cases.

## Key claim

The medium polarization effect on the pairing gap in symmetric nuclear matter depends on the decomposition of the nucleon propagator used: the standard Feynman-density (FD) decomposition enhances the gap around saturation density, while the particle-hole-antiparticle (pha) decomposition reduces it there; at the lowest density free from the liquid-gas instability, the gap increases in both cases, but only qualitative discussion is possible in the instability region itself.

## Method

The author uses a relativistic σ-ω model (quantum hadrodynamics) and calculates the RPA meson propagator from the Dyson equation including σ, longitudinal ω, σ-ω mixed, and transverse ω polarizations at zero energy transfer. Two decompositions of the nucleon propagator are examined: the standard Feynman-density (FD) decomposition and the particle-hole-antiparticle (pha) decomposition. The resulting RPA interaction is inserted into the relativistic Nambu-Gor'kov gap equation for the ¹S₀ pairing channel, with a form factor (Λ = 7.26 fm⁻¹) introduced at vertices to modulate the high-momentum part of the tree-level one-boson-exchange interaction.

## Key numbers

- The liquid-gas instability in symmetric nuclear matter occurs in the Fermi momentum range 0.3 fm⁻¹ < k_F < 1.3 fm⁻¹ in the FD case.
- The form factor parameter used to modulate the high-momentum interaction is Λ = 7.26 fm⁻¹, determined by minimizing χ² over 11 values of k_F from 0.2 to 1.2 fm⁻¹.
- The cutoff parameter ε_cut = 0.65 is chosen to regularize the calculation in the instability region, maintaining full variation of ε_L and ε_T around saturation density.
- The upper bound of the momentum integrations in the gap equation is set to 20 fm⁻¹.
- The nucleon mass M = 939 MeV, m_σ = 550 MeV, m_ω = 783 MeV, g_σ² = 91.64, and g_ω² = 136.2 are adopted.

## Context

This work extends relativistic studies of medium polarization effects on superfluidity in symmetric nuclear matter, following the approach of Batista et al. (2002) and Chen et al. (2001), and addresses the liquid-gas instability discussed by Furnstahl and Horowitz (1988) and Furnstahl et al. (1988). The results are compared with non-relativistic calculations by Schulze et al. (1996) and Heiselberg (1992), and with the relativistic calculation of Batista et al. (2002). The study enables future inclusion of vacuum polarization and extension to the liquid-gas coexistent phase.
