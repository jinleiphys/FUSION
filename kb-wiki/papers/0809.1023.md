# On Finite Width of Quark Gluon Plasma Bags

> Within an exactly solvable model I discuss an influence of the medium dependent finite width of QGP bags on their equation of state. It is shown that inclusion of such a width allows one to naturally resolve two conceptual problems of the QGP statistical description. On the basis of the proposed simple kinetic model for a sequential decay of heavy QGP bags formed in high energy elementary particle collisions it is argued that by measuring the energy dependence of life time of these bags it is possible to distinguish the case of critical point existence from the case of tricritical point.

## Key claim

Inclusion of a medium-dependent finite width for QGP bags within an exactly solvable model resolves two conceptual problems of the QGP statistical description, and the energy dependence of the lifetime of heavy QGP bags formed in elementary particle collisions can distinguish between the existence of a critical point and a tricritical point, depending on the value of the Fisher exponent τ.

## Method

The author constructs a finite width model (FWM) where the mass-volume-surface spectrum of QGP bags includes a Hagedorn-like mass spectrum with a Gaussian attenuation around the bag mass Bv, with a volume-dependent Gaussian width Γ(v). The isobaric partition is analyzed to find its rightmost singularities, and a simple kinetic model for sequential two-particle decay of heavy QGP bags is developed, using the Hagedorn mass spectrum and nonrelativistic approximations to derive the lifetime of a protofireball as a function of its mass.

## Key numbers

- The minimal resonance width at zero temperature is estimated as Γ_R(V₀, T=0) ≈ 600 MeV.
- The width at the Hagedorn temperature is Γ_R(V₀, T=T_H) = √12 × Γ_R(V₀, T=0) ≈ 2000 MeV.
- At temperatures below half the Hagedorn temperature, the QGP bag pressure acquires a linear T dependence: p⁻(T < 0.5 T_H) = -T B²/(2γ²), seen in lattice QCD data in the range T ∈ [202.5; 419.09] MeV.
- The lifetime of a protofireball of mass M_F is given by t_τ≠3/2(M_F) ≈ (Γ₀⁻¹)/(q(τ - 3/2)) √(M₀/E_min) [E_min^(3/2-τ)/M_F^(3/2-τ) - E_min^(3/2-τ)/M₀^(3/2-τ)] and t_τ=3/2(M_F) ≈ (Γ₀⁻¹)/q ln[M_F/M₀], where Γ₀ ≈ 600 MeV and M₀ ≈ 2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the finite width model (FWM) developed in Bugaev (2008) and Reggeons (2008) to address the Hagedorn-Mott resonance model of Blaschke (2004) and the HBT puzzles at RHIC energies discussed by Wong. It enables experimental distinction between critical point and tricritical point scenarios in the QCD phase diagram by measuring the energy dependence of protofireball lifetimes in elementary particle collisions, and it explains the deficit of heavy hadronic resonances in the Particle Data Group compared to the exponential mass spectrum used to describe the QGP equation of state.
