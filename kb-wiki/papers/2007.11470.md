# Perturbative Approach to Effective Shell-Model Hamiltonians and Operators

> The aim of this work is to present an overview of the derivation of the effective shell-model Hamiltonian and decay operators within many-body perturbation theory, and to show the results of selected shell-model studies based on their utilisation. More precisely, we report some technical details that are needed by non-experts to approach the derivation of shell-model Hamiltonians and operators starting from realistic nuclear potentials, in order to provide some guidance to shell-model calculations where the single-particle energies, two-body matrix elements of the residual interaction, effective charges and decay matrix elements, are all obtained without resorting to empirical adjustments. On the above grounds, we will present results of studies of double-beta decay of heavy-mass nuclei where shell-model ingredients are derived from theory, so to assess the reliability of such a way to shell-model investigations. Attention will be also focussed on the relevant aspects that are connected to the behavior of the perturbative expansion, whose knowledge is needed to establish limits and perspectives of this approach to nuclear structure calculations.

## Key claim

This review demonstrates that effective shell-model Hamiltonians and decay operators can be derived from realistic nuclear potentials using many-body perturbation theory without empirical adjustments, but the reliability of this approach depends on the behavior of the perturbative expansion and its convergence properties, particularly regarding the intruder-state problem.

## Method

The authors present the formal derivation of effective shell-model Hamiltonians and decay operators using the Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation and the Q-box vertex function within energy-independent linked-diagram perturbation theory. They describe the Krenciglowa-Kuo (KK) and Lee-Suzuki (LS) iterative techniques for solving the decoupling equation in degenerate model spaces, and the extended Kuo-Krenciglowa (EKK) method and Z-box vertex function for non-degenerate model spaces. The approach employs the reaction matrix G derived from realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials as interaction vertices in the perturbative expansion of the Q-box and Theta-box vertex functions.

## Key numbers

- The core-polarization diagram at second order contributed about 30% of the first-order two-body matrix element for open-shell nuclei 18O and 42Sc outside doubly-closed cores 16O and 40Ca, respectively (Bertsch 1965)
- The first successful shell-model calculation starting from the free nucleon-nucleon Hamada-Johnston potential was performed by Kuo and Brown (1966), using reaction-matrix G and including terms up to second order in G for sd-shell nuclei
- The TBMEs derived by Kuo and Brown (1966) for sd-shell and later for fp-shell (Kuo 1968a) became the backbone of empirical effective Hamiltonians such as USD (Brown 1988) and KB3G (Caurier 2005, Poves 2001)

## Context

This work extends the perturbative approach to effective shell-model Hamiltonians pioneered by Bertsch (1965) and Kuo and Brown (1966), and formalized through folded-diagram expansion by Brandow (1967) and Kuo (1971). It contrasts with phenomenological approaches (Elliott 1969, Talmi 2003) and non-perturbative methods such as valence-space in-medium SRG (Morris 2015), shell-model coupled cluster (Sun 2018), and no-core shell-model with a core (Lisetskiy 2008, 2009; Dikmen 2015; Smirnova 2019). The review enables practitioners to perform shell-model calculations where single-particle energies, two-body matrix elements, effective charges, and decay matrix elements are all derived from theory, with applications to double-beta decay of heavy-mass nuclei such as 130Te and 136Xe.
