# Neutral weak currents in pion electroproduction on the nucleon

> Parity violating asymmetry in inclusive scattering of longitudinally polarized electrons by unpolarized protons with $π^0$ or $π^+$ meson production, is calculated as a function of the momentum transfer squared $Q^2$ and the total energy $W$ of the $πN$-system. This asymmetry, which is induced by the interference of the one-photon exchange amplitude with the parity-odd part of the $Z^0$-exchange amplitude, is calculated for the $γ^*(Z^*)+p\to N+π$ processes ($γ^*$ is a virtual photon and $Z^*$ a virtual Z-boson) considering the $Δ$-contribution in the $s-$channel, the standard Born contributions and vector meson ($ρ$ and $ω$) exchanges in the $t-$channel. Taking into account the known isotopic properties of the hadron electromagnetic and neutral currents, we show that the P-odd term is the sum of two contributions. The main term is model independent and it can be calculated exactly in terms of fundamental constants. It is found to be linear in $Q^2$. The second term is a relatively small correction which is determined by the isoscalar component of the electromagnetic current. Near threshold and in the $Δ$-region, this isoscalar part is much smaller (in absolute value) than the isovector one: its contribution to the asymmetry depend on the polarization state (longitudinal or transverse) of the virtual photon.

## Key claim

The parity-violating asymmetry in inclusive pion electroproduction on the nucleon can be separated into a dominant, model-independent term linear in \(Q^2\) that depends only on fundamental constants (\(G_F\), \(\alpha\), \(\sin^2\theta_W\)), plus a small correction from the isoscalar component of the electromagnetic current that is negligible near threshold and in the \(\Delta\)-region.

## Method

The authors calculated the parity-violating asymmetry for \(\vec e + p \to e + N + \pi\) (\(\pi^0\) or \(\pi^+\)) using the interference of one-photon exchange with the parity-odd part of \(Z^0\)-exchange. They included Born terms in the \(s\)-, \(t\)-, and \(u\)-channels, \(\Delta\)-isobar excitation in the \(s\)-channel, and vector meson (\(\rho\) and \(\omega\)) exchanges in the \(t\)-channel, using a pseudoscalar \(\pi NN\) interaction and a Rarita-Schwinger description for the \(\Delta\). The asymmetry was expressed in terms of the isovector and isoscalar components of the hadronic electromagnetic current, with the isoscalar correction \(\Delta^{(s)}\) evaluated from the interference of the isoscalar current with the full electromagnetic current.

## Key numbers

- The model-independent isovector contribution to the asymmetry is \(A = -\frac{G_F |k^2|}{2\sqrt{2}\pi\alpha} [1 - 2\sin^2\theta_W]\), with \(\frac{G_F}{2\sqrt{2}\pi\alpha} = 1.8 \times 10^{-4}\).
- The Weinberg angle is \(\sin^2\theta_W = 0.23124(24)\).
- The electron coupling constants are \(g_a^{(e)} = 1\) and \(g_v^{(e)} = 1 - 4\sin^2\theta_W \simeq 0.076\).
- The isoscalar Born contribution is suppressed relative to the isovector by \(|\mu_p + \mu_n| / |\mu_p - \mu_n| = |1.79 - 1.91| / (1.79 + 1.91) \approx 10^{-2}\).
- The \(\rho\)-exchange coupling constants are \(g_{\rho NN}^2 / 4\pi = 0.55\) and \(\kappa_\rho = 3.7\); the \(\omega\)-exchange constants are \(g_{\omega NN}^2 / 4\pi = 20\) and \(\kappa_\omega = 0\).
- The \(\rho\pi\gamma\) coupling is suppressed relative to \(\omega\pi\gamma\) by \(g_{\rho\pi\gamma} \simeq (1/3) g_{\omega\pi\gamma}\) at the electromagnetic vertex and \(g_{\rho NN} \simeq (1/6) g_{\omega NN}\) at the strong vertex.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of parity-violating asymmetries in pion electroproduction by Nagl (1982) and Joul (1980) using quasi-two-body models with stable \(\Delta\) isobars, and by Haberzettl and Doyle (1995) who included Born terms and a Rarita-Schwinger \(\Delta\) field. The present calculation improves upon Haberzettl and Doyle (1995) by including \(\omega\)- and \(\rho\)-exchange in the \(t\)-channel, using a different \(\Delta\) parametrization, and providing a physical explanation for the linear \(Q^2\) dependence and energy independence of the asymmetry. The results enable precise predictions for parity-violating asymmetries in pion electroproduction that can be used to correct elastic scattering measurements (such as those by the SAMPLE, HAPPEX, and G0 collaborations) for pion contamination, and to study the axial-vector transition form factor \(G_{N\Delta}^A\) independently of PCAC.
