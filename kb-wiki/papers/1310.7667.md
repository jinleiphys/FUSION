# Low-energy enhancement of magnetic dipole radiation

> Magnetic dipole strength functions have been deduced from averages of a large number of $M1$ transition strengths calculated within the shell model for the nuclides $^{90}$Zr, $^{94}$Mo, $^{95}$Mo, and $^{96}$Mo. An enhancement of $M1$ strength toward low transition energy has been found for all nuclides considered. Large $M1$ strengths appear for transitions between close-lying states with configurations including proton as well as neutron high-$j$ orbits that re-couple their spins and add up their magnetic moments coherently. The $M1$ strength function deduced from the calculated $M1$ transition strengths is compatible with the low-energy enhancement found in ($^3$He,$^3$He') and $(d,p)$ experiments. The present work presents for the first time an explanation of the experimental findings.

## Key claim
Shell-model calculations show that the low-energy enhancement of the dipole strength function observed in experiments on Mo isotopes is caused by M1 transitions between close-lying excited states, where proton and neutron high-j orbits re-couple their spins and add up their magnetic moments coherently. The authors state that this M1 radiation accounts for the observed enhancement, but note that uncertainties in calculating the low-energy E1 strength leave room for additional mechanisms.

## Method
The authors performed large-scale shell-model calculations using the code RITSSCHIL with a model space of proton orbits (0f5/2, 1p3/2, 1p1/2, 0g9/2) and neutron orbits (0g9/2, 1d5/2, 0g7/2) relative to a 68Ni core. They calculated reduced transition probabilities B(M1) for over 14000 transitions per parity in 90Zr, 94Mo, 95Mo, and 96Mo, sorting them into 100 keV bins of transition energy. The M1 strength functions were then derived by multiplying the average B(M1) values by the level density at the initial state energy, using level densities obtained by counting calculated levels in 1 MeV intervals.

## Key numbers
- For 90Zr: exponential fit parameters for positive (negative) parity B(M1) are B0 = 0.36 (0.58) μ²N and TB = 0.33 (0.29) MeV
- For 94Mo: exponential fit parameters for positive (negative) parity B(M1) are B0 = 0.32 (0.16) μ²N and TB = 0.35 (0.51) MeV
- For 95Mo: exponential fit parameters for positive (negative) parity B(M1) are B0 = 0.23 (0.12) μ²N and TB = 0.39 (0.58) MeV
- For 96Mo: exponential fit parameters for positive (negative) parity B(M1) are B0 = 0.20 (0.13) μ²N and TB = 0.41 (0.50) MeV
- The M1 strength function exponential parameters f0 for 90Zr, 94Mo, 95Mo, and 96Mo are 34, 37, 39, and 55 × 10⁻⁹ MeV⁻³ respectively, with Tf values of 0.50, 0.50, 0.51, and 0.48 MeV
- For the 2⁺₃ → 2⁺₁ transition in 94Mo: calculated Eγ = 884 keV and B(M1) = 0.96 μ²N, compared with experimental values of 1196 keV and 0.56(5) μ²N

## Context
This work provides the first microscopic explanation for the low-energy enhancement of the dipole strength function previously observed in (³He,³He') experiments on Mo isotopes by Guttormsen et al. (2005) and confirmed in a ⁹⁴Mo(d,p)⁹⁵Mo experiment by Wiedeking et al. (2012). The mechanism is related to the same spin re-coupling that generates "shears bands" manifesting magnetic rotation, as discussed by Frauendorf (2001), and the mixed-symmetry configurations of the interacting boson model observed in ⁹⁴Mo and ⁹⁶Mo. The authors predict that the low-energy M1 enhancement should appear in the same nuclear regions where magnetic rotation is expected, including near the neutron drip line around (Z,N) = (22,48), (26,52), (34,80), and (64,118), and note that such enhancement could increase astrophysical (n,γ) rates for the r-process by more than a factor of 10.
