# Covariant spectator theory of quark-antiquark bound states: Mass spectra and vertex functions of heavy and heavy-light mesons

> We use the covariant spectator theory with an effective quark-antiquark interaction, containing Lorentz scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector contributions, to calculate the masses and vertex functions of, simultaneously, heavy and heavy-light mesons. We perform least-square fits of the model parameters, including the quark masses, to the meson spectrum and systematically study the sensitivity of the parameters with respect to different sets of fitted data. We investigate the influence of the vector confining interaction by using a continuous parameter controlling its weight. We find that vector contributions to the confining interaction between 0% and about 30% lead to essentially the same agreement with the data. Similarly, the light quark masses are not very tightly constrained. In all cases, the meson mass spectra calculated with our fitted models agree very well with the experimental data. We also calculate the mesons wave functions in a partial wave representation and show how they are related to the meson vertex functions in covariant form.

## Key claim
The covariant spectator theory (CST) with an effective quark-antiquark interaction containing scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector contributions can simultaneously describe heavy and heavy-light meson mass spectra, but vector contributions to the confining interaction between 0% and about 30% yield essentially the same agreement with data, meaning the Lorentz structure of confinement is not tightly constrained by the mass spectrum alone.

## Method
The authors solved the one-channel covariant spectator equation (1CSE) for quark-antiquark bound states with unequal masses, using a kernel that combines a linear confining potential (with adjustable scalar+pseudoscalar versus vector mixing via a continuous parameter y), a one-gluon-exchange term, and a constant term. They performed least-square fits of the model parameters (quark masses, coupling strengths σ, α_s, C, and the mixing parameter y) to experimental meson masses, and then calculated the meson wave functions in a partial-wave representation to extract vertex functions in covariant form.

## Key numbers
- Vector contributions to the confining interaction between 0% and about 30% lead to essentially the same agreement with experimental data.
- The meson mass spectra calculated with fitted models agree very well with experimental data across a range from D mesons below 2 GeV up to bottomonium above 10 GeV.
- The linear confining kernel strength σ, the one-gluon-exchange strength α_s, and the constant C are the three adjustable coupling parameters of the interaction model.
- A Pauli-Villars regularization cutoff Λ is introduced to ensure convergence of loop integrations.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous CST calculations (Leitao 2017) by introducing a vector component in the confining interaction through a mixing parameter y, and by treating quark masses as adjustable fit parameters rather than fixing them ad-hoc. It contrasts with lattice QCD and Dyson-Schwinger-Bethe-Salpeter approaches, which have difficulties describing higher excited meson states (Meinel 2010, Lewis 2012, Fischer 2015). The results enable future systematic studies of the Lorentz structure of confinement and provide covariant vertex functions that can be used in Faddeev-type three-quark calculations of baryons.
