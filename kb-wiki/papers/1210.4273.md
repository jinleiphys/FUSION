# Three-body forces: From cold atoms to nuclei

> It is often assumed that few- and many-body systems can be accurately described by considering only pairwise two-body interactions of the constituents. We illustrate that three- and higher-body forces enter naturally in effective field theories and are especially prominent in strongly interacting quantum systems. We focus on three-body forces and discuss examples from atomic and nuclear physics. In particular, we highlight the importance and the challenges of three-nucleon forces for nuclear structure and reactions, including applications to astrophysics and fundamental symmetries.

# Three-body forces: From cold atoms to nuclei

## Key claim

Three-body forces arise naturally in effective field theories and are quantitatively significant in strongly interacting quantum systems, contributing on the order of 20% to binding energies of light nuclei, but their precise determination remains challenging because many low-energy observables are insensitive to the spin-isospin structure of three-nucleon forces once the triton binding energy is fitted.

## Method

The authors review the formulation of three-body forces within chiral effective field theory (ChPT), where the expansion parameter is \(Q/\Lambda_{\chi}\) with \(Q \sim m_{\pi}\) and \(\Lambda_{\chi} \approx 1\) GeV, and derive the leading three-nucleon force at order \(Q^3\) containing two-pion-exchange, one-pion-exchange–short-range (D-term), and short-range (E-term) topologies. They determine the low-energy constants \(c_i\) from pion-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon data, and fix \(c_D\) and \(c_E\) by fitting the \(^3\)H binding energy together with additional few-nucleon observables such as the doublet neutron-deuteron scattering length, the \(^4\)He binding energy, or the \(^3\)H beta decay half-life. They then compare chiral EFT predictions for few-nucleon binding energies and nucleon-deuteron scattering to experimental data and to results from phenomenological forces.

## Key numbers

- Contribution of three-nucleon forces to binding energies of light nuclei is of the order 20% (citing Pieper (2001)).
- Pion decay constant \(f_{\pi} = 92.4\) MeV.
- Chiral symmetry breaking scale \(\Lambda_{\chi} \approx 1\) GeV.
- Cutoffs in state-of-the-art chiral EFT applications are restricted to \(\Lambda \lesssim 450 - 600\) MeV.
- For \(^4\)He with cutoff \(\Lambda = 450\) MeV: binding energy \(B = 27.65\) MeV, \(\langle V_{\rm NN} \rangle = -84.56\) MeV, \(\langle V_{\rm 3NF} \rangle = -1.11\) MeV (1.3% of NN contribution).
- For \(^4\)He with cutoff \(\Lambda = 600\) MeV: binding energy \(B = 28.57\) MeV, \(\langle V_{\rm NN} \rangle = -93.73\) MeV, \(\langle V_{\rm 3NF} \rangle = -6.83\) MeV (7.2% of NN contribution).
- Experimental \(^4\)He binding energy is 28.30 MeV.
- Chiral EFT calculations up to order \(Q^3\) are useful only up to nucleon laboratory energies of \(\sim 100\) MeV.
- When three-nucleon forces are included in SRG evolution of \(^4\)He, the remaining \(\lambda\) variation is of the order of 50 keV for \(\lambda \gtrsim 1.5\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- Explicit calculations using four-nucleon forces give 200–300 keV for \(^4\)He.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on three-nucleon forces from phenomenological models such as the Fujita-Miyazawa (1957) two-pion-exchange 3NF and the Tucson-Melbourne (Coon, 1978) family to a systematic chiral EFT framework developed by Weinberg (1990), Epelbaum (2005, 2008), and Machleidt (2011). It highlights that phenomenological NN-only calculations show model dependence in \(^3\)H and \(^4\)He binding energies (Polyzou, 1990; Amghar, 1995), and that consistent NN+3N forces from chiral EFT are required to resolve discrepancies in low-energy nucleon-deuteron scattering observables such as the analyzing power \(A_y\) (Miller, 2007). The work enables next-generation calculations at order \(Q^4\) (Bernard, 2007, 2011) and applications to larger nuclei and nuclear matter using SRG-evolved interactions (Jurgenson, 2009; Roth, 2011; Hebeler, 2012).
