# An integral equation for distorted wave amplitudes

> We derive a new integral equation that allows the calculation of the scattering or annihilation amplitude of two particles subjected to two potentials when the corresponding amplitude for one potential only is known. We assume that scattering or annihilation occurs through one of the potentials, while the other potential affects the particle wave functions. Our expression is valid for any choice of the distorting potential and for any particle model. Our technique does not require the expansion of the amplitude into partial waves, and allows the study of models that are generally difficult to solve by means of the Schroedinger equation.

# An integral equation for distorted wave amplitudes

## Key claim

The authors derive an exact integral equation that relates the scattering or annihilation amplitude for two particles subject to two potentials (a distorting potential and a transition potential) to the amplitude when only the transition potential is present, without requiring a partial wave expansion and without assuming the distorting potential is weak. The derivation is presented for non-relativistic two-particle initial states but claims validity for any choice of distorting potential and any particle model.

## Method

The authors start from the Schrödinger equation for two particles in the presence of both a distorting potential V̂ and a transition potential Ŵ, cast it into Lippmann-Schwinger form, and insert a completeness relation for the eigenstates of Ŵ alone. This yields an integral equation for the full scattering state |ψ⁺_α⟩ in terms of the states |ψ^{W+}_α⟩ (solutions with Ŵ only) and the matrix elements V^W_{γα} = ⟨ψ^{W+}_γ|V̂|ψ^{W+}_α⟩. Multiplying by the final state ⟨φ_β| gives the main result: an integral equation for the distorted amplitude M⁺_{βα} in terms of the undistorted amplitude M⁰_{βα}. The authors also present the Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) simplification and show that for spin-independent potentials the equation separates into independent sets for each total spin S, involving Wigner D-matrices.

## Key numbers

- Reduced mass μ = m₁m₂/(m₁+m₂) for the two initial particles in the center-of-mass frame.
- Non-relativistic energy E_α = p²/(2μ) for the two-particle initial state.
- The completeness relation for the Ŵ eigenstates is normalized as ⟨ψ^{W+}_α|ψ^{W+}_{α'}⟩ = (2π)³δ(α - α'), where δ includes Kronecker deltas for discrete quantum numbers.
- The DWBA equation for spin-singlet (S=0) particles reduces to M⁺_{00}(p) = M⁰_{00}(p) + ∫ d³k/(2π)³ [Ṽ(p-k) M⁺_{00}(k)]/(E_p - E_k + iε), where Ṽ is the Fourier transform of the distorting potential.

## Context

This work extends the standard Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) formalism (Davydov, Weinberg) by providing an exact integral equation that does not require the distorting potential to be weak. The authors note that the equation is the non-relativistic limit of sums of ladder diagrams in quantum field theory, connecting to earlier work on Sommerfeld enhancement (Sommerfeld) and dark matter annihilation (Hisano). The approach avoids partial wave expansion, which is advantageous when s-waves are suppressed or p-wave resonances occur. The authors cite Iengo for a specific Yukawa potential case that their general equation subsumes, and state that numerical studies of their Eq. (4) will appear in a separate paper (Visinelli).
