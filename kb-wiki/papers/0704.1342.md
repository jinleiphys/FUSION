# Two-pion-exchange contributions to the pp\to ppπ^0 reaction

> Our previous study of the near-threshold pp\to ppπ^0 reaction based on a hybrid nuclear effective field theory is further elaborated by examining the momentum dependence of the relevant transition operators. We show that the two-pion exchange diagrams give much larger contributions than the one-pion exchange diagram, even though the former is of higher order in the Weinberg counting scheme. The relation between our results and an alternative counting scheme, the momentum counting scheme, is also discussed.

## Key claim

The two-pion-exchange (TPE) diagrams for the near-threshold \(pp\to pp\pi^0\) reaction give much larger contributions than the one-pion-exchange (1\(\pi\)-Resc) diagram, even though the TPE diagrams are of higher order in the Weinberg counting scheme; however, the "leading parts" of these TPE diagrams (the most divergent terms in the momentum counting scheme) cancel among themselves, and the significant contributions come from the "sub-leading" parts.

## Method

The authors derived the transition operators for \(pp\to pp\pi^0\) at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB\(\chi\)PT), categorized into Types I–IV (two-pion-exchange) and Types V–VII (vertex correction). They used a fixed kinematics approximation (FKA) to simplify the operators and decomposed the TPE amplitudes into a leading term (\(t_1\), proportional to \(|\vec{k}|\)) and sub-leading terms (\(t_2\), \(t_3\), and \(\delta t\)). They evaluated the TPE contributions in both plane-wave approximation (PWBA) and distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) at \(T_{lab}=281\) MeV, using the Nijm93 potential with a Gaussian momentum regulator (\(\Lambda_G\) between 500 and 1000 MeV/\(c\)) and \(V_{low-k}\) potentials derived from the CD-Bonn potential with cutoffs \(\Lambda_{low-k}=4\) and \(5\) fm\(^{-1}\).

## Key numbers

* The sum of the plane-wave matrix element ratios \(\sum_K R^{\,\star}_K = -4.65\), indicating that the TPE contributions (with the \(t_1\) term subtracted) are more important than the 1\(\pi\)-Resc contribution in plane-wave approximation.
* In DWBA with the Nijm93 potential and \(\Lambda_G=500\) MeV/\(c\), the summed TPE contribution \(J = -0.70\) compared to the 1\(\pi\)-Resc contribution \(J = 0.18\), giving a factor of \(\sim 3.9\) larger magnitude.
* In DWBA with the Nijm93 potential and \(\Lambda_G=1000\) MeV/\(c\), the summed TPE contribution \(J = -0.71\) compared to the 1\(\pi\)-Resc contribution \(J = 0.23\), giving a factor of \(\sim 3.1\) larger magnitude.
* In DWBA with \(V_{low-k}\) (CD-4, \(\Lambda_{low-k}=4\) fm\(^{-1}\)), the summed TPE contribution \(J = -0.65\) compared to the 1\(\pi\)-Resc contribution \(J = 0.23\), giving a factor of \(\sim 2.8\) larger magnitude.
* In plane-wave approximation, the summed TPE contribution \(J = -0.37\) compared to the 1\(\pi\)-Resc contribution \(J = 0.080\), giving a factor of \(\sim 4.6\) larger magnitude.
* The characteristic momentum scale of the reaction is \(p \simeq \sqrt{m_N m_\pi} \simeq 360\) MeV/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends the HB\(\chi\)PT study of the \(pp\to pp\pi^0\) reaction by Kim et al. (1999, DKMS) and addresses the cancellation among TPE diagrams pointed out by Hanhart and Kaiser (2002) and Hanhart (2004). The results confirm that the sub-leading parts of the TPE diagrams are significant, consistent with the phenomenological success of the heavy-meson exchange mechanism of Lee and Riska (1993). The findings enable a more complete calculation of the \(pp\to pp\pi^0\) cross section, including counter terms and other diagrams, to be compared with experimental data.
