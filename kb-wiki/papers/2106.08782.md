# The Radial Excited Heavy Mesons

> In this paper, the first radial excited heavy pseudoscalar and vector mesons ($η_c(2S)$, $ψ(2S)$, $B_c(2S)$, $B^*_c(2S)$, $η_b(2S)$, $\varUpsilon(2S)$) are studied in the Dyson-Schwinger equation and Bethe-Salpeter equation approach. It is showed that the effective interactions of the radial excited states are harder than that of the ground states. With the interaction well determined by fitting the masses and leptonic decay constants of $ψ(2S)$ and $\varUpsilon(2S)$, the first radial excited heavy mesons could be quantitatively described in the rainbow ladder approximation. The masses and leptonic decay constants of $η_c(2S)$, $B_c(2S)$, $B^*_c(2S)$ and $η_b(2S)$ are predicted.

## Key claim
The effective interaction between quark and antiquark in radial excited heavy mesons is harder (more strength at high momentum transfer and less at low momentum transfer) than in ground-state mesons; by refitting the interaction parameters to the masses and leptonic decay constants of psi(2S) and Upsilon(2S), the rainbow-ladder Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach can quantitatively describe all first radial excited heavy pseudoscalar and vector mesons, not just the two used for fitting.

## Method
The quark propagator is solved from the Dyson-Schwinger gap equation and the meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitude from the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation, both in the rainbow-ladder (RL) approximation. The effective gluon interaction is modeled as a sum of an infrared Gaussian term and a perturbative ultraviolet term; for radial excited states the infrared term is modified by two free parameters per flavor (alpha_f for width, eta_f for strength), which are fixed by fitting the masses and leptonic decay constants of psi(2S) and Upsilon(2S) to experimental values. Because the radial excited meson masses lie beyond the contour border where the quark propagator can be directly evaluated, the eigenvalue and decay constant are extrapolated to the physical mass using rational-function fits of order N_o = 1, 2, 3.

## Key numbers
- Mass of eta_c(2S): 3.618(25)(3) GeV (experiment: 3.638 GeV)
- Mass of B_c(2S): 6.874(9)(6) GeV (experiment: 6.872 GeV)
- Mass of B*_c(2S): 6.926(12)(6) GeV (no precise experimental value available)
- Mass of eta_b(2S): 9.989(13)(3) GeV (experiment: 9.999 GeV)
- Leptonic decay constant of psi(2S): -0.208(5)(0) GeV (fitted to experimental value -0.208 GeV)
- Leptonic decay constant of Upsilon(2S): -0.352(4)(0) GeV (fitted to experimental value -0.352 GeV)
- Leptonic decay constant of eta_c(2S): -0.158(8)(4) GeV
- Leptonic decay constant of B_c(2S): -0.174(5)(4) GeV
- Leptonic decay constant of B*_c(2S): -0.216(9)(4) GeV
- Leptonic decay constant of eta_b(2S): -0.345(6)(1) GeV
- Largest mass deviation from experiment: 20 MeV (for eta_c(2S))
- Systematic error of the spectrum: estimated 20 MeV

## Context
This work extends the DSBSE approach previously applied to ground-state mesons (Chen 2019) and to scalar and axial-vector mesons (Chen 2021), where a universal effective interaction was found adequate. The author and collaborators (Chang 2020) had shown that using the ground-state interaction for radial excited heavy mesons yields masses 1% too low and leptonic decay constants 12% too low for Upsilon(2S) and 42% too low for psi(2S). The present paper corrects this by introducing separate interaction parameters for excited states, enabling precise predictions for eta_c(2S), B_c(2S), B*_c(2S), and eta_b(2S) that can guide future experimental searches, particularly for the B*_c(2S) mass which is not yet well determined.
