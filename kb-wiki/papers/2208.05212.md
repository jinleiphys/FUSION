# Centrality and Transverse Spherocity dependent study of charged-particle production in Xe-Xe collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.44 TeV using PYTHIA8 Angantyr and AMPT models

> Transverse Spherocity is an event structure variable which provide an effective way to disentangle the data into hard and soft components of the processes corresponding to events with small and large numbers of multi-parton interactions (MPI), respectively. Recent experimental results in small systems from the LHC suggest the importacnce of transverse spherocity variable in the classification of the events. In this contribution, we have studied the dynamics of identified particle production in Xe-Xe collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.44 TeV using A Multi-Phase Transport Model (AMPT) and the recently developed Angantyr model, which is incorporated within PYTHIA8. A study of the transverse momentum spectra of the dentified particles are presented for soft (isotropic) and hard (jetty-like) events in different centrality intervals.

## Key claim

Transverse spherocity can effectively separate hard (jetty-like) and soft (isotropic) components of particle production in Xe-Xe collisions at 5.44 TeV, but the crossing point where jetty events dominate over isotropic events depends on both centrality and the choice of event generator (AMPT versus PYTHIA8 Angantyr), with the latter lacking assumptions of a thermalised medium.

## Method

The authors generated approximately 0.5 million Xe-Xe collision events at √sNN = 5.44 TeV using both the AMPT model (with string melting, version 2.26t7) and the PYTHIA8 Angantyr model. Events were classified into centrality bins using impact parameter for AMPT and summed transverse energy (∑ET) in pseudorapidity interval [-0.8, 0.8] for PYTHIA8. Transverse spherocity (So) was computed event-by-event, and the extreme 20% of the spherocity distribution defined jetty (low So) and isotropic (high So) event classes for each centrality bin. Transverse momentum (pT) spectra of pions, kaons, and protons were then analysed at mid-rapidity (|η| < 0.8) for these event classes.

## Key numbers

- Charged-particle pseudorapidity density dNch/dη was compared with ALICE experimental data for Xe-Xe at √sNN = 5.44 TeV; AMPT describes data fairly well at low centralities with a slight overestimate at high centralities, while PYTHIA8 overestimates data increasingly in higher centrality bins.
- For the 0-10% centrality bin, the crossing point (pT where jetty and isotropic event yields become equal) for pions is 5.83 GeV/c in AMPT and 2.61 GeV/c in PYTHIA8; for kaons it is 5.97 GeV/c (AMPT) and 3.56 GeV/c (PYTHIA8); for protons it is 6.09 GeV/c (AMPT) and 3.63 GeV/c (PYTHIA8).
- For the 60-70% centrality bin, the crossing point for pions is 3.9 GeV/c in AMPT and 1.73 GeV/c in PYTHIA8; for kaons it is 3.99 GeV/c (AMPT) and 1.83 GeV/c (PYTHIA8); for protons it is 4.1 GeV/c (AMPT) and 1.88 GeV/c (PYTHIA8).
- Centrality bins were defined using impact parameter ranges for AMPT (e.g., 0-10%: b = 0 to 4.507 fm) and summed transverse energy ranges for PYTHIA8 (e.g., 0-10%: ∑ET = 1031.47 to 2000 GeV).
- Transverse spherocity cuts for jetty and isotropic events vary with centrality; for 0-10% in AMPT, jetty events have So in [0, 0.87] and isotropic in [0.95, 1]; for PYTHIA8, jetty in [0, 0.93] and isotropic in [0.97, 1].

## Context

This work extends the use of transverse spherocity, previously applied in small systems by ALICE (2019), CMS (Chatrchyan), and ATLAS (Abelev), to heavy-ion Xe-Xe collisions for the first time. The study compares two models: AMPT, which assumes formation of a hot thermalised medium with collective expansion, and PYTHIA8 Angantyr, which superposes multiple pp collisions without medium formation, following the approach of Bierlich (2018). The results enable experimental investigation of event topology dependence of system dynamics in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC and RHIC, particularly regarding jet production mechanisms and the role of collectivity.
