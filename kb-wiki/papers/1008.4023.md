# Jet quenching in hot strongly coupled gauge theories revisited: 3-point correlators with gauge-gravity duality

> Previous studies of high-energy jet stopping in strongly-coupled plasmas have lacked a clear gauge-theory specification of the initial state. We show how to set up a well-defined gauge theory problem to study jet stopping in pure {\cal N}=4 super Yang Mills theory (somewhat analogous to Hofman and Maldacena's studies at zero temperature) and solve it by using gauge-gravity duality for real-time, finite-temperature 3-point correlators. Previous studies have found that the stopping distance scales with energy as E^{1/3} (with disagreement on the gauge coupling dependence). We do find that none of the jet survives beyond this scale, but we find that almost all of our jet stops at a parametrically smaller scale proportional to (E L)^{1/4}, where L is the size of the space-time region where the jet is initially created.

## Key claim

The paper finds that for a well-defined gauge-theory initial state (a localized, high-energy R-charge excitation created by an external source), almost all of the jet's conserved charge stops at a parametrically smaller scale proportional to \((E L)^{1/4}\) (where \(L\) is the initial source size), not at the previously claimed \(E^{1/3}\) scale; however, the \(E^{1/3}\) scale does emerge as the furthest distance any non-negligible fraction of the charge propagates before stopping and thermalizing.

## Method

The authors set up a well-defined gauge-theory problem in pure \(\mathcal{N}=4\) super Yang Mills theory at large \(N_c\) and large 't Hooft coupling \(\lambda\), where a classical external source \(\mathcal{A}_{\text{cl}}^\mu\) couples to the SU(4) R-charge currents and creates a high-energy excitation with nearly light-like momentum \((\omega,0,0,\omega)\) and a smooth envelope of size \(L\). They compute the late-time R-charge density response \(\langle j^{(3)0}(x)\rangle_{\mathcal{A}_{\text{cl}}}\) using gauge-gravity duality, evaluating real-time, finite-temperature retarded 3-point correlators via Witten diagrams in AdS\(_5\)-Schwarzschild space, with the bulk-to-boundary propagators for the 5-dimensional SU(4) gauge fields.

## Key numbers

* The R-charge diffusion constant is \(D = 1/(2\pi T)\).
* The maximal stopping distance (furthest non-exponentially-suppressed charge deposition) scales as \((x_3)_{\text{max}} \sim E^{1/3} / T^{4/3}\).
* The dominant stopping distance (where almost all charge is deposited) scales as \((x_3)_{\text{dominant}} \sim (E L)^{1/4} / T\).
* Numerical constants from the first quasi-normal mode: \(\numpole \simeq 0.927\) and \(\numres \simeq 3.2\).
* The constant \(\num \equiv \Gamma^2(1/4) / (2\pi)^{1/2}\).

## Context

This work extends the studies of jet stopping in strongly-coupled plasmas by Gubser, Hatta-Iancu-Mueller (HIM), and Chesler et al., which all found an \(E^{1/3}\) scaling but lacked a clear gauge-theory specification of the initial state. The authors provide a complete gauge-theory definition of the problem (analogous to Hofman and Maldacena's zero-temperature studies) and show that the Hatta-Iancu-Mueller (2010) scale \(E^{1/3}\) is only the maximal stopping distance, while a new \(E^{1/4}\) scale dominates the charge deposition. The result enables future work on jet stopping with well-defined initial states and clarifies the role of R-charge conservation in the gravity dual calculation.
