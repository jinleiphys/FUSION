# Density functional theory for self-bound systems

> The density functional theory is extended to account for self-bound systems. To this end the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem is formulated for the intrinsic density and a Kohn-Sham like procedure for an $N$--body system is derived using the adiabatic approximation to account for the center of mass motion.

## Key claim

The Hohenberg-Kohn theorem can be formulated for the intrinsic (translationally invariant) density of a self-bound system, and a Kohn-Sham-like procedure can be derived using an adiabatic (Born-Oppenheimer-type) approximation for the center-of-mass coordinate, which breaks translational symmetry and introduces corrections that vanish in the infinite-particle limit.

## Method

The author first proves the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem for the intrinsic density \(n_{in}(\mathbf{r})\) of an \(N\)-body system with two-body forces and an intrinsic one-body potential, following the standard reductio ad absurdum argument. A Kohn-Sham-like energy functional is then constructed, and the resulting Hamiltonian is separated into single-particle orbitals by expanding the one-body potential \(\varphi(\mathbf{r}-\mathbf{R})\) in a Taylor series around the center-of-mass coordinate \(\mathbf{R}\), replacing center-of-mass operators by their expectation values, and retaining only leading-order corrections. The resulting closed set of equations is solved self-consistently in the Hartree approximation, with the center-of-mass second moment \(\langle R_a R_b \rangle\) expressed in terms of the single-particle orbitals.

## Key numbers

- The leading center-of-mass correction to the single-particle potential in the local density approximation is \(\delta \varphi(\mathbf{r}) = \frac{1}{2} \langle R_a R_b \rangle \, \partial^2_{ab} \varphi(\mathbf{r})\).
- For a collapsing system (size independent of \(N\)), the center-of-mass expansion behaves as a \(1/N\) series.
- For non-collapsing systems such as nuclei, where the rms radius grows as \(\sqrt[3]{N}\), the expansion converges as \(1/\sqrt[3]{N}\) unless the potential scales with the rms matter radius.
- In the harmonic oscillator example, the center-of-mass correction terms exactly cancel the center-of-mass energy, recovering the correct internal Hamiltonian.

## Context

This work extends the density functional theory framework to self-bound systems, addressing a limitation noted by Engel (2006) and Giruad (2006) that the standard single-particle density vanishes for a freely moving self-bound system. The author follows the path of deriving the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem for the intrinsic density, as suggested by Engel (2006), rather than adding an external confining potential as considered by Giruad (2006). The result enables the application of Kohn-Sham methods to finite self-bound systems like atomic nuclei, bridging the gap between formal DFT and practical nuclear structure calculations using Skyrme-type functionals.
