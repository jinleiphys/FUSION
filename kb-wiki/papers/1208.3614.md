# Simulation of Light Antinucleus-Nucleus Interactions

> Creations of light anti-nuclei (anti-deuterium, anti-tritium, anti-He3 and anti-He4) are observed by collaborations at the LHC and RHIC accelerators. Some cosmic ray experiments are aimed to find the anti-nuclei in cosmic rays. To support the experimental studies of the anti-nuclei a Monte Carlo simulation of anti-nuclei interactions with matter is implemented in the Geant4 toolkit. The implementation combines practically all known theoretical approaches to the problem of antinucleon-nucleon interactions.

## Key claim

The authors implement a Monte Carlo simulation of light antinucleus-nucleus interactions in the Geant4 toolkit that combines the Glauber approach for cross sections with the quark-gluon string model for multiparticle production, but they acknowledge that the energy dependence of the annihilation cross sections required parameterization because they "did not find an acceptable solution" from first principles.

## Method

The authors calculate antinucleus-nucleus cross sections using the Glauber eikonal approach with parameterized energy dependencies of antinucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes. For multiparticle production in antinucleon-nucleon interactions, they implement a set of quark flow diagrams (annihilation, string creation, and Fritiof-type processes) with parameterized cross sections and the LUND string fragmentation algorithm in the FTF generator of Geant4. For antiproton-nucleus interactions, they apply Abramovsky-Gribov-Kancheli (AGK) cutting rules with finite energy corrections and include secondary particle cascading via the binary cascade model.

## Key numbers

- The model is valid between 100 MeV/c and 1 TeV/c per antinucleon.
- Absorption cross sections for anti-deuterons are calculated and compared with data at momenta of 13.3 and 25 GeV/c.
- The parameterization for annihilation cross section (process "a") is given as sigma_a = 16/(sqrt(s-4m^2)) * [(s-4m^2)^(-0.175) + 3.125*(1-1.88/sqrt(s))] in mb.
- The threshold for process "b" is set at M_th = 2.172 GeV, with sigma_b = 3.13 + 140*(M_th - sqrt(s))^2.5 mb below threshold and sigma_b = 6.8/sqrt(s) mb above.
- The parameterization for process "g" (Fritiof-type) is sigma_g = 35*(1 - 2.1/sqrt(s)) mb.
- The finite energy correction introduces a maximum multiplicity nu_max = [p_lab/p_0] + 1 with p_0 approximately 2 GeV/c.
- Charged particle multiplicity distribution is shown for anti-deuteron + Ta interactions at P_d = 6.1 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the Glauber approach previously applied to antiproton-nucleus scattering by Franco and Glauber (1960s) and Dalkarov and Karmanov, and builds on earlier anti-deuteron cross section calculations by Buck et al. and Ludmila. The implementation enables cosmic ray experiments (PAMELA, BESS, AMS, CAPRICE) and LHC/RHIC collaborations (STAR, ALICE) to simulate antinucleus interactions with detector materials, supporting the interpretation of anti-deuterium, anti-tritium, anti-He3, and anti-He4 observations.
