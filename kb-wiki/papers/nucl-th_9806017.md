# On Complete Sets of Polarization Observables

> A new criterion is developed which provides a check as to whether a chosen set of polarization observables is complete with respect to the determination of all independent $T$-matrix elements of a reaction of the type $a+b\to c+d+...$. As an illustrative example, this criterion is applied to the longitudinal observables of deuteron electrodisintegration.

# On Complete Sets of Polarization Observables

## Key claim
The paper develops a new criterion that can check whether a chosen set of polarization observables is sufficient to determine all independent T-matrix elements of a reaction, but the solution generally contains discrete ambiguities that require additional observables to resolve.

## Method
The authors reformulate the hermitean forms of polarization observables into real quadratic forms in the real and imaginary parts of the T-matrix elements, constructing m×m matrices (where m = 2n−1 for n independent T-matrix elements). They then test whether the Jacobian determinant of the system of quadratic equations is nonvanishing by examining determinants built from the columns of the observable matrices; if at least one such determinant is nonzero, the set is complete up to discrete ambiguities. The criterion is applied to the longitudinal observables of deuteron electrodisintegration, where the 6 independent longitudinal T-matrix elements require 11 observables for a complete set.

## Key numbers
- For a reaction with independent T-matrix elements, the number of real parameters needed for complete information is 2n−1 (since one overall phase is undetermined).
- For deuteron electrodisintegration, the total number of T-matrix elements is 3×3×4 = 36, reduced by parity conservation to n = 18 independent elements; of these, 6 are associated with the longitudinal current density component.
- For the longitudinal observables, a complete set requires 11 observables selected from 36 linearly independent ones.
- The numerical test used a specific internal excitation energy of E_np = 100 MeV and momentum transfer q_cm^2 = 5 fm^−2 for various n-p angles θ_np.

## Context
This work extends the necessity criterion of Simonius (1967), which excluded certain subsets from being complete but was not specific enough to identify complete sets. The authors build on prior formal treatments of polarization observables in deuteron electrodisintegration by DmG89 and ArL93, and on explicit construction methods used for pion photoproduction by BaD75 and KeW96. The criterion enables experimentalists to select which polarization observables to measure for complete determination of reaction amplitudes, though discrete ambiguities remain and must be resolved by measuring additional observables. The authors note that a forthcoming paper will extend the analysis to include transverse and interference observables.
