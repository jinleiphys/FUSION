# Relativistic (Lattice) Boltzmann Equation with Non-Ideal Equation of State

> The relativistic Boltzmann equation for a single particle species generally implies a fixed, unchangeable equation of state that corresponds to that of an ideal gas. Real-world systems typically have more complicated equation of state which cannot be described by the Boltzmann equation. The present work derives a 'Boltzmann-like' equation that gives rise to a conserved energy-momentum tensor with an arbitrary (but thermodynamically consistent) equation of state. Using this, a Lattice Boltzmann scheme for diagonal metric tensors and arbitrary equations of state is constructed. The scheme is verified for QCD in the Milne metric by comparing to viscous fluid dynamics.

# Relativistic (Lattice) Boltzmann Equation with Non-Ideal Equation of State

## Key claim

The standard relativistic Boltzmann equation for a single particle species forces an ideal-gas equation of state; by introducing a medium-dependent (temperature- and density-dependent) quasiparticle mass and adding a compensating term to the energy-momentum tensor, one can construct a "Boltzmann-like" kinetic equation that reproduces any thermodynamically consistent non-ideal equation of state, provided the thermodynamic consistency condition is promoted to hold also out of equilibrium.

## Method

The author replaces the fixed particle mass in the standard relativistic Boltzmann equation with a temperature- and chemical-potential-dependent mass M(T,μ), motivated by quasiparticle descriptions of hot QCD. To maintain thermodynamic consistency, a function B(T,μ) is added to the energy-momentum tensor definition, and the Boltzmann equation is modified by a term (1/2) ∂_μ M² ∂^μ_(p) f. The resulting "Boltzmann-like" equation is then discretized for diagonal metric tensors using a lattice Boltzmann scheme: momentum space is sampled at discrete nodes constructed from orthogonal polynomials in the rapidity variable ξ and angular variables, with weights chosen to exactly reproduce the equilibrium integrals. The scheme is verified for QCD in the Milne metric by comparing the evolution of the energy density to viscous fluid dynamics.

## Key numbers

- For QCD with N_c=3, N_f=3, the degrees of freedom factor is set to Z = (π⁴/180)[4(N_c²−1) + 7 N_c N_f].
- The shear viscosity coefficient is given by η = (τ_R/15T) Z ∫ d³p/(2π)³ [(M²−E²)²/E²] e^{−E/T}, where τ_R is the relaxation time.
- The bulk viscosity coefficient is ζ = (τ_R/9T) Z ∫ d³p/(2π)³ p² [−M² + (1−3c_s²)E² + 3c_s² M T dM/dT] / E² e^{−E/T}.
- In the massless limit, η = τ_R (ε+P)/5.
- The hydrodynamic shear relaxation time τ_π equals the relaxation time τ_R of the collision term.

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle approach used in medium-dependent mass models for QCD (Peshier 1999, Kraemmer 2003) and the non-relativistic lattice Boltzmann methods for non-ideal equations of state (non-id1 through non-id4) to a fully relativistic kinetic framework. It provides a concrete algorithm for simulating systems with arbitrary equations of state using a single "virtual" particle species, which is computationally cheaper than multi-species approaches (Blattel 1988, Bozek 1998, Plumari 2010, Bluhm 2010). The scheme enables relativistic lattice Boltzmann simulations of heavy-ion collisions with realistic QCD equations of state, verified against viscous fluid dynamics in Milne coordinates.
