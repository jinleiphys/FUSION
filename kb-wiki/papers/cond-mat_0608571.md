# Bosonic molecules in rotating traps

> We present a variational many-body wave function for repelling bosons in rotating traps, focusing on rotational frequencies that do not lead to restriction to the lowest Landau level. This wave function incorporates correlations beyond the Gross-Pitaevskii (GP) mean field approximation, and it describes rotating boson molecules (RBMs) made of localized bosons that form polygonal-ring-like crystalline patterns in their intrinsic frame of reference. The RBMs exhibit characteristic periodic dependencies of the ground-state angular momenta on the number of bosons in the polygonal rings. For small numbers of neutral bosons, the RBM ground-state energies are found to be always lower than those of the corresponding GP solutions, in particular in the regime of GP vortex formation.

## Key claim

For small numbers of neutral bosons, the rotating boson molecule (RBM) ground-state energies are always lower than those of the corresponding Gross-Pitaevskii (GP) solutions, even in the regime of GP vortex formation and even for weakly repelling bosons as the interaction strength \(R_\delta\) approaches zero.

## Method

The authors construct a variational many-body wave function for \(N\) repelling bosons in rotating toroidal or harmonic traps using \(N\) displaced Gaussian orbitals centered at the vertices of a regular polygon. They first build an unrestricted Bose Hartree-Fock (UBHF) permanent that breaks circular symmetry, then restore good total angular momentum via projection techniques, yielding the projected (PRJ) RBM wave function. The ground-state energy and angular momentum are computed as functions of the reduced rotational frequency \(\Omega/\omega_0\) and interaction strength \(R_\delta\), and compared to mean-field Gross-Pitaevskii solutions.

## Key numbers

- For \(N=8\) neutral bosons in a toroidal trap with \(r_0=3 l_0\) and \(R_\delta=50\), the ground-state angular momenta vary in steps of \(N=8\) units (magic angular momenta \(L=kN\) with integer \(k\)).
- For \(N=6\) neutral bosons in a harmonic trap with \(R_\delta=50\), the ground-state angular momenta vary in steps of \(N-1=5\) units, indicating a \((1,5)\) two-ring structure.
- For \(N=6\) neutral bosons in a harmonic trap with \(R_\delta=50\), the GP ground-state energy remains above the RBM curve for the whole range \(0 \leq \Omega/\omega_0 \leq 1\).
- For \(N=6\) neutral bosons in a harmonic trap rotating at \(\Omega/\omega_0=0.85\), the GP energy remains above the RBM curve even as \(R_\delta \rightarrow 0\).
- The GP total angular momentum for \(N=6\) bosons is quantized (\(L_z=0\) or \(L_z=6\)) only for \(0 \leq \Omega/\omega_0 \leq 0.42\); above that it takes non-integer values.

## Context

This work extends the concept of rotating electron molecules (REMs) studied in quantum dots under high magnetic fields (Yannouleas and Landau, 2000; 2002) to neutral and charged bosons in rotating traps, introducing rotating boson molecules (RBMs) as an alternative to the Gross-Pitaevskii mean-field description. The RBM wave function incorporates correlations beyond the GP approximation and predicts that for small boson numbers, crystalline molecular states are energetically favored over vortex-bearing Bose-Einstein condensates, even for weak interactions. This enables experimental tests using rotating optical lattices (Greiner et al., 2001) and detection via spatial extent measurements or Hanbury Brown-Twiss-type experiments.
