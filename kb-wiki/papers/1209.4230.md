# Relation between total cross sections from elastic scattering and alpha-induced reactions: the example of 64Zn

> The total reaction cross section is related to the elastic scattering angular distribution by a basic quantum-mechanical relation. We present new experimental data for alpha-induced reaction cross sections on 64Zn which allow for the first time the experimental verification of this simple relation at low energies by comparison of the new experimental reaction data to the result obtained from 64Zn(alpha,alpha)64Zn elastic scattering.

# Relation between total cross sections from elastic scattering and alpha-induced reactions: the example of 64Zn

## Key claim

The total reaction cross section derived from elastic scattering angular distributions via the standard quantum-mechanical partial-wave relation (Eq. 1) is experimentally identical to the sum of measured alpha-induced reaction cross sections for 64Zn at low energies around the Coulomb barrier, with a ratio r = 1.02 ± 0.17. This is the first experimental confirmation of this basic relation for alpha-induced reactions at low energies, contrary to earlier higher-energy studies that had found ratios between 1.1 and 1.5.

## Method

The cross sections of the 64Zn(alpha,gamma)68Ge, 64Zn(alpha,p)67Ga, and 64Zn(alpha,n)67Ge reactions were measured using the activation method at the ATOMKI cyclotron, with alpha-beam energies between 5.8 and 12.4 MeV in the center-of-mass frame. The summed experimental cross sections at 12.37 MeV were combined with estimated contributions from inelastic scattering and two-particle emission channels (calculated using the ECIS and TALYS codes) to obtain the total reaction cross section, which was then compared to the total reaction cross section derived from reanalysis of 64Zn(alpha,alpha)64Zn elastic scattering data at 12.4 MeV from Di Pietro et al. (2004).

## Key numbers

- Ratio r = 1.02 ± 0.17 between total reaction cross section from elastic scattering (610 ± 80 mb) and from summed reaction channels (596 ± 62 mb) at Ec.m. = 12.37 MeV
- 64Zn(alpha,p)67Ga cross section at Ec.m. = 12.37 MeV: 344 ± 40 mb
- 64Zn(alpha,n)67Ge cross section at Ec.m. = 12.37 MeV: 179 ± 19 mb
- 64Zn(alpha,gamma)68Ge cross section at Ec.m. = 12.37 MeV: 1.81 ± 0.19 mb
- Total reaction cross section from elastic scattering reanalysis: 610 ± 80 mb at Ec.m. = 12.4 MeV
- Summed cross section of three measured channels at 12.37 MeV: 525 ± 57 mb
- Estimated inelastic scattering cross section at 12.37 MeV: 69 ± 25 mb
- Total reaction cross section at 11.24 MeV: 428 ± 44 mb

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to verify the quantum-mechanical relation between elastic scattering and total reaction cross sections, which had failed at higher energies (above the Coulomb barrier) where transmission experiments by Auce (1994) and Ingemarsson (2000) gave inconsistent results with ratios r between 1.1 and 1.5. The present study uses the activation technique rather than transmission measurements, avoiding systematic uncertainties of the latter, and focuses on the low-energy regime relevant for astrophysical p-process nucleosynthesis. The result validates the use of elastic scattering data to determine the alpha-nucleus optical potential for predicting (alpha,gamma) capture cross sections, which had proven difficult for heavy targets above A ≈ 100 in previous work by Somorjai (1998), Gyurky (2006), and others. The authors recommend a new transmission experiment with simultaneous elastic and inelastic scattering measurements at exactly matched energies to achieve a more precise confirmation above the Coulomb barrier.
