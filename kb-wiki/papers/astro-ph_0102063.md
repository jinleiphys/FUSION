# The likelihood of GODs' existence: Improving the SN1987a constraint on the size of large compact dimensions

> The existence of compact dimensions which are accessible only to gravity represents an intriguing possible solution to the hierarchy problem. At present the strongest constraint on the existence of such compact Gravity-Only Dimensions (GODs) comes from SN1987a. Here we report on the first self-consistent simulations of the early, neutrino-emitting phase of a proto-neutron star which include energy losses due to the coupling of the Kaluza-Klein modes of the graviton which arise in a world with GODs. We compare the neutrino signals from these simulations to that from SN1987a and use a rigorous probabilistic analysis to derive improved bounds for the radii of such GODs. We find that the possibility that there are two compact extra dimensions with radii larger than 0.66 $μ$m is excluded at the 95% confidence level---as is the possibility that there are three compact extra dimensions larger than 0.8 nm.

## Key claim
The paper reports that the possibility of two compact extra dimensions with radii larger than 0.66 μm is excluded at the 95% confidence level, and three compact extra dimensions larger than 0.8 nm are similarly excluded, based on the first self-consistent simulations of proto-neutron star evolution that include Kaluza-Klein graviton energy losses and a rigorous probabilistic analysis of the SN 1987a neutrino data.

## Method
The authors performed self-consistent simulations of the early, neutrino-emitting phase of a proto-neutron star (PNS) using a numerical code that includes energy losses due to the coupling of Kaluza-Klein (KK) graviton modes arising from compact Gravity-Only Dimensions (GODs). They employed the most recent calculations of KK-graviton emissivities from Hanhart et al. (2000), which are based on low-energy theorems relating emissivities to measured nucleon-nucleon cross sections, and compared the simulated neutrino signals to the data from SN 1987a using a Bayesian likelihood analysis to marginalize over uncertain parameters such as PNS mass and anti-neutrino temperature.

## Key numbers
- For two compact extra dimensions (n=2): the bound on the coefficient a₂ is 0.022 MeV/baryon/s, corresponding to a radius R₂ ≤ 0.66 μm at the 95% confidence level.
- For three compact extra dimensions (n=3): the bound on the coefficient a₃ is 0.0077 MeV/baryon/s, corresponding to a radius R₃ ≤ 8 × 10⁻⁴ μm (0.8 nm) at the 95% confidence level.
- The KK-graviton emissivity for n=2 is given by dE/dt = a₂ (n_B/n₀) (T/10 MeV)^5.42 χ(X_n,X_p) MeV/baryon/s, with a₂ = 5.1 × 10⁴ R₂² and R₂ in mm.
- The KK-graviton emissivity for n=3 is given by dE/dt = a₃ (n_B/n₀) (T/10 MeV)^6.5 χ(X_n,X_p) MeV/baryon/s, with a₃ = 1.4 × 10¹⁶ R₃³ and R₃ in mm.
- The most likely model (maximum likelihood) occurs at a baryonic mass of M = 1.5 M⊙, a₂ = 0, and anti-neutrino temperature T_ν̄ = 1.1 T_ν⁰, where T_ν⁰ is the reference temperature from the optical-depth prescription.

## Context
This work extends previous order-of-magnitude bounds on GOD radii from Arkani-Hamed et al. (1999) and the improved analysis by Cullen and Perelstein (1999), who found R < 0.3 μm (n=2) and R < 0.4 nm (n=3) using the simple Raffelt criterion. The authors' earlier emissivity calculations (Hanhart et al., 2000) gave bounds of R < 0.71 μm (n=2) and R < 0.85 nm (n=3). The present work improves upon these by performing the first self-consistent PNS simulations with KK-graviton energy losses and using a rigorous Bayesian likelihood analysis, which obviates the need for subjective choices of fiducial temperature and density. The bounds are significantly more stringent than any foreseeable collider bounds and roughly a factor of hundred more stringent than tests of sub-millimeter gravity for two extra dimensions. The formalism developed here can be applied to other exotic particle energy-loss mechanisms in future supernova neutrino observations.
