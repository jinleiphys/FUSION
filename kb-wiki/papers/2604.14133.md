# AI-assisted modeling and Bayesian inference of unpolarized quark transverse momentum distributions from Drell-Yan data

> We present an extraction of unpolarized quark transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions (TMD PDFs) from Drell-Yan data within a Bayesian inference framework, incorporating artificial intelligence at multiple stages of the analysis. Our analysis is performed at ${\rm N^3LO}$ in perturbative QCD combined with ${\rm N^4LL}$ resummation accuracy. We first employ an AI-driven iterative procedure to explore and rank candidate functional forms for the nonperturbative contributions to TMD PDFs at the initial scale, as well as for the Collins-Soper evolution kernel, using $χ^2$ fits and physics constraints. To enable efficient Bayesian inference, we construct a surrogate model for TMD cross sections by training a machine-learning emulator over the parameter space, replacing computationally expensive repeated evaluations and allowing scalable sampling with an affine-invariant Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) ensemble. Using this framework, we perform a global analysis of Drell-Yan data from fixed-target, RHIC, and LHC experiments and extract TMD PDFs with quantified uncertainties. We compare the results with those obtained using the replica method and highlight differences in the resulting uncertainty estimates.

## Key claim

The paper presents an extraction of unpolarized quark TMD PDFs from Drell-Yan data using Bayesian inference with AI-assisted modeling, but the uncertainty estimates depend on the inference methodology: the Bayesian approach yields different uncertainty estimates compared to the traditional replica method, highlighting that the choice of statistical framework affects the reliability of uncertainty quantification in TMD extractions.

## Method

The analysis employs TMD factorization at N3LO perturbative QCD combined with N4LL resummation accuracy, using an AI-driven iterative procedure to explore and rank candidate functional forms for the nonperturbative Sudakov factor and Collins-Soper kernel. A machine-learning emulator (surrogate model) is trained over the parameter space to replace computationally expensive repeated cross-section evaluations, enabling scalable Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling for Bayesian inference. The framework is applied to a global analysis of 465 data points from fixed-target (E288, E605, E772), RHIC (STAR), and LHC (ATLAS, CMS, LHCb) Drell-Yan experiments, and a parallel replica-method analysis is performed within the same theoretical setup for direct comparison.

## Key numbers

* The analysis includes 465 total data points from Drell-Yan experiments after applying the kinematic cut q_T^max / Q_min < 0.2 to retain only the low-q_T region.
* The perturbative accuracy reaches N3LO for the hard function and matching coefficients, with the Collins-Soper kernel and cusp anomalous dimension computed to 4-loop and 5-loop order respectively.
* The nonperturbative parameterization contains 9 fit parameters: {c_0, c_1, B_NP, lambda_1, lambda_2, lambda_3, A_NP, x_0, sigma_x}.
* The b_max parameter in the b* prescription is set to b_0 GeV^{-1}, where b_0 = 2e^{-gamma_E}, so that the matching scale freezes at mu_* = 1 GeV at large b.
* The collider datasets span center-of-mass energies from 510 GeV (STAR) to 13000 GeV (CMS 13 TeV), with dilepton mass ranges from 46-150 GeV for ATLAS 8 TeV and 60-120 GeV for CMS and LHCb measurements.

## Context

This work extends the line of TMD phenomenology established by the ART extractions (Moos et al., 2025) and the neural-network parametrization approach (Bacchetta et al., 2025), while introducing Bayesian inference as an alternative to the Monte Carlo replica method commonly used in TMD analyses. The comparison between Bayesian and replica uncertainty estimates provides insight into the robustness of extracted TMD PDF uncertainties and clarifies the regimes where each framework may be more reliable, enabling future joint analyses that combine experimental data with lattice QCD inputs (Avkhadiev et al., 2025) within a unified probabilistic description.
