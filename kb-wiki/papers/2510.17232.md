# Nonlocality effect in $α$ decay half-lives for even-even nuclei within a two potential approach

> In this paper, we carefully look at the $α$ -decay half-lives of 196 even-even nuclei using a two-potential approach that is made better by taking into account an alpha particle's effective mass that changes with coordinates. The result shows that the accuracy of this model has been improved after considering effective mass for the alpha particle. Furthermore, considering $α$ decay energies derived from three mass models, namely the Weizsacker-Skyrme-4 (WS4) mass model, the relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov theory mass model, and the FRDM (2012), we extend this model to predict the $α$ -decay half-lives of Z = 118 and 120 isotopes. Finally, we carefully study the predicted $α$ decay energies and half-lives of Z = 118 and 120 isotopes and discuss the shell structure of superheavy nuclei. We found that the shell effect is obvious at N = 178 and at N = 184 in the WS4 mass model and FRDM(2012), while the shell effect is only obvious at N = 184 in the relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov theory mass model.

# Nonlocality effect in α decay half-lives for even-even nuclei within a two potential approach

## Key claim
Including a coordinate-dependent effective mass for the alpha particle (reflecting nonlocality of the particle-nucleus interaction) improves the accuracy of the two-potential approach for calculating α-decay half-lives of even-even nuclei, but the improvement is modest: the standard deviation decreases from 0.573 to 0.522, an improvement of approximately 9%. The shell effect at N = 184 appears consistently across three mass models, while the shell effect at N = 178 appears only in the WS4 and FRDM(2012) models, not in the relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov theory mass model.

## Method
The authors modified the standard two-potential approach (TPA) by replacing the free alpha-particle mass with a coordinate-dependent effective mass m* = m / [1 − ρ(r)], where ρ(r) is defined as the derivative of a Woods-Saxon function with parameters RS, aS, and a global adjustment parameter ρS = 0.595. They calculated α-decay half-lives for 196 even-even nuclei using experimental Qα values from NUBASE2020 and AME2020, then extended the model to predict half-lives for Z = 118 and 120 isotopes using Qα values from three mass models: WS4, relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory, and FRDM(2012). The preformation probability Pα was computed using the cluster formation model of Ahmed et al. (2013).

## Key numbers
- Standard deviation σ between calculated and experimental log10(T1/2) for 196 even-even nuclei: σ = 0.573 without effective mass (ρS = 0); σ = 0.522 with effective mass (ρS = 0.595); improvement of approximately 9%.
- At r = RS, the effective mass decreases by approximately 13% relative to the free mass.
- For 292118: QαRCHB = 10.962 MeV, log10(T1/2)RCHB = −1.24 s; QαWS4 = 12.237 MeV, log10(T1/2)WS4 = −4.48 s; QαFRDM = 12.382 MeV, log10(T1/2)FRDM = −4.67 s.
- For 304118: QαRCHB = 12.652 MeV, log10(T1/2)RCHB = −5.45 s; QαWS4 = 13.118 MeV, log10(T1/2)WS4 = −6.47 s; QαFRDM = 13.382 MeV, log10(T1/2)FRDM = −6.95 s.
- For 306120: QαRCHB = 13.582 MeV, log10(T1/2)RCHB = −6.71 s; QαWS4 = 13.776 MeV, log10(T1/2)WS4 = −7.17 s; QαFRDM = 14.262 MeV, log10(T1/2)FRDM = −8.00 s.
- For 308120: QαRCHB = 13.072 MeV, log10(T1/2)RCHB = −5.56 s; QαWS4 = 12.962 MeV, log10(T1/2)WS4 = −5.72 s; QαFRDM = 12.962 MeV, log10(T1/2)FRDM = −5.81 s.

## Context
This work extends the study of Medeiros et al. (2022), who incorporated effective mass into the semiclassical WKB method for α decay, by applying the same nonlocality concept to the two-potential approach (TPA) originally developed by Gurvitz (1987). The TPA with effective mass is then used to predict half-lives for superheavy nuclei with Z = 118 and 120, complementing prior predictions from the Coulomb and proximity potential model (Santhosh, 2012), density-dependent cluster model (Xu, 2005), and universal decay law (Qi, 2009). The results enable comparison of shell effects at N = 178 and N = 184 across different mass models, providing guidance for future experimental searches for new superheavy isotopes.
