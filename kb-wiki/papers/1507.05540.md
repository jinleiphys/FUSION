# Initial state fluctuations from mid-peripheral to ultra-central collisions in a event-by-event transport approach

> We have developed a relativistic kinetic transport approach that incorporates initial state fluctuations allowing to study the build up of elliptic flow $v_2$ and high order harmonics $v_3$, $v_4$ and $v_5$ for a fluid at fixed $η/s(T)$. We study the effect of the $η/s$ ratio and its T dependence on the build up of the $v_n(p_T)$ for two different beam energies: RHIC for Au+Au at $\sqrt{s}=200 \,GeV$ and LHC for $Pb+Pb$ at $\sqrt{s}=2.76 \,TeV$. We find that for the two different beam energies considered the suppression of the $v_n(p_T)$ due to the viscosity of the medium have different contributions coming from the cross over or QGP phase. Our study reveals that only in ultra-central collisions ($0 - 0.2 \%$) the $v_n(p_T)$ have a stronger sensitivity to the T dependence of $η/s$ in the QGP phase and this sensitivity increases with the order of the harmonic n. Moreover, the study of the correlations between the initial spatial anisotropies $ε_n$ and the final flow coefficients $v_n$ shows that at LHC energies there is more correlation than at RHIC energies. The degree of correlation increases from peripheral to central collisions, but only in ultra-central collisions at LHC, we find that the linear correlation coefficient $C(n,n) \approx 1$ for $n=2,3,4$ and $5$. This suggests that the final correlations in the ($v_n$,$v_m$) space reflect the initial correlations in the ($ε_n$,$ε_m$) space.

## Key claim

The study reveals that only in ultra-central collisions (0-0.2% centrality) the differential anisotropic flows v_n(p_T) have a stronger sensitivity to the temperature dependence of eta/s in the QGP phase, and this sensitivity increases with the order of the harmonic n; at LHC energies the linear correlation coefficient C(n,n) approaches approximately 1 for n=2,3,4 and 5, suggesting that final correlations in (v_n, v_m) space reflect initial correlations in (epsilon_n, epsilon_m) space.

## Method

The authors developed a relativistic kinetic transport approach solving the Boltzmann equation with a Monte Carlo Glauber model for event-by-event initial state fluctuations, using the test particle method and stochastic collision integral. The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s(T) was fixed locally in space and time by determining the total cross section via Chapman-Enskog theory, allowing study of three different eta/s(T) parametrizations: constant 4pi eta/s=1, an increasing eta/s in the crossover region, and a linear eta/s proportional to T in the QGP phase. Simulations were performed for Au+Au at sqrt(s)=200 GeV and Pb+Pb at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV across multiple centrality classes with 500 events per centrality class and up to 10^3 events for correlation studies.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, the initial time of simulation was set to tau_0=0.6 fm/c; for Pb+Pb at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV, tau_0=0.3 fm/c
- The Gaussian width for smearing nucleon positions was fixed to sigma_xy=0.5 fm
- The nucleon-nucleon cross section used was sigma_NN=4.2 fm^2 for RHIC and sigma_NN=7.0 fm^2 for LHC
- For mid-peripheral collisions (b=7.5 fm) at RHIC, initial temperature reached approximately T=300 MeV; at LHC, approximately T=400 MeV
- Convergence was achieved with a grid cell transverse area A_T=0.12 fm^2 and N_test=2x10^6 test particles per event
- For mid-peripheral collisions (20-30% centrality) at RHIC, the increase of eta/s at low temperature reduced v_2(p_T) by about 17% and v_4(p_T) by about 30-40%
- At LHC for the same centrality, the increase of eta/s at low temperature reduced v_4(p_T) by about 5-10%, while v_2(p_T) was almost unaffected
- At RHIC, the triangular flow v_3(p_T) showed a reduction of about 25% due to increased eta/s at low temperature
- At LHC, v_3 and v_4 showed deviations of about 10% from changing eta/s(T), compared to less than 5% for v_2
- The linear correlation coefficient C(n,n) was approximately 1 for n=2,3,4 and 5 only in ultra-central collisions at LHC

## Context

This work extends previous transport studies of elliptic flow without event-by-event fluctuations (Plumari 2013, Plumari 2014) to include high-order harmonics v_3, v_4, and v_5 for the first time in a cascade approach with initial state fluctuations. The results are consistent with viscous hydrodynamic calculations (Schenke 2011, Gale 2012) showing that small eta/s values are needed, but the transport approach avoids assumptions about viscous corrections delta f and allows self-consistent kinetic freeze-out. The study enables future determination of the temperature dependence of eta/s by comparing v_n(p_T) across RHIC and LHC energies, particularly in ultra-central collisions where the sensitivity to the QGP phase eta/s is strongest.
