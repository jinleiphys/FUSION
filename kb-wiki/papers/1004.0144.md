# Relativistic Effects in Nuclear Matter and Nuclei

> The status of relativistic nuclear many-body calculations of nuclear systems to be built up in terms of protons and neutrons is reviewed. In detail, relativistic effects on several aspects of nuclear matter such as the effective mass, saturation mechanism, and the symmetry energy are considered. This review will especially focus on isospin asymmetric issues, since these aspects are of high interest in astrophysical and nuclear structure studies. Furthermore, from the experimental side these aspects are experiencing an additional boost from a new generation of radioactive beam facilities, e.g. the future GSI facility FAIR in Germany or SPIRAL2 at GANIL/France. Finally, the prospects of studying finite nuclei in microscopic calculations which are based on realistic $NN$ interactions by including relativistic effects in calculations of low momentum interactions are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations, unlike non-relativistic approaches, can reproduce the empirical saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter (energy per nucleon around -16 MeV at density ~0.16 nucleon/fm⁻³) using realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions without introducing phenomenological three-nucleon forces, provided the density-dependent modification of nucleon Dirac spinors in the medium is accounted for.

## Method

The authors survey the DBHF approach, which solves a coupled set of equations: the relativistic Bethe-Salpeter equation for the in-medium T-matrix in the ladder approximation, the Dyson equation for the dressed nucleon propagator, and the Hartree-Fock self-energy expressed as traces over the T-matrix and propagator. The self-energy is decomposed into scalar (Σ_s), time-like vector (Σ_0), and space-like vector (Σ_v) components, leading to effective in-medium masses and momenta via the Dirac equation. The review also covers the V_low-k renormalization approach, where a cutoff Λ ≈ 2 fm⁻¹ yields a unique low-momentum NN interaction, and compares results from non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF), self-consistent Green's function (SCGF), and relativistic mean-field (RMF) methods.

## Key numbers

- Empirical saturation energy per nucleon of symmetric nuclear matter: about -16 MeV, at saturation density ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 nucleon/fm⁻³.
- Non-relativistic BHF calculations using realistic NN interactions produce saturation points on the "Coester band," with energies per nucleon ranging from about -10 MeV (at correct density) to values near -16 MeV (at densities roughly twice ρ₀).
- The scalar part of the relativistic self-energy in DBHF is strongly attractive (around -300 MeV), partially compensated by a repulsive time-like vector component from ω-meson exchange, yielding a net single-particle energy of about -40 MeV.
- The V_low-k cutoff is set at Λ = 2 fm⁻¹, corresponding to the pion threshold in NN scattering.
- The hard-core radius in early local NN potentials is r_c ≈ 0.4 fm.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on relativistic nuclear many-body theory initiated by Brockmann and Machleidt (1990), Anastasio et al. (1983), Serot and Walecka (1986), and Ter Haar and Malfliet (1987), among others. It contrasts the success of DBHF in reproducing nuclear matter saturation with the persistent failure of non-relativistic approaches (BHF, SCGF, coupled cluster, variational, Quantum Monte Carlo) using only two-nucleon forces, which require empirical three-nucleon forces to match experiment. The paper discusses how incorporating relativistic effects into the V_low-k approach (van Dalen and Müther, 2009) may bridge ab initio DBHF calculations and phenomenological relativistic mean-field models, enabling future microscopic studies of finite nuclei and neutron-rich systems relevant to facilities like FAIR and SPIRAL2.
