# Proof of the orthogonal--Pin duality

> This article contains the proof of a theorem on orthogonal-Pin duality that was cited without proof in a previous article in this journal.

# Proof of the orthogonal–Pin duality

## Key claim

The paper proves that the fermion Fock space decomposes into a direct sum of tensor products of irreducible representations of the orthogonal Lie algebra $\mathfrak{o}(d)$ and the Pin group $\mathrm{Pin}(2k)$, with the duality established via a character calculation rather than relying on a first fundamental theorem of invariant theory for Pin groups, which does not exist.

## Method

The author constructs two commuting representations on the fermion Fock space $\Phi$: a number-conserving representation of the orthogonal Lie algebra $\mathfrak{o}(d)$ and a number-non-conserving representation of $\mathfrak{o}(2k)$. Using a previously proven $\mathfrak{o}(d)$–$\mathfrak{o}(2k)$ duality theorem (Neergård 2019), which decomposes $\Phi$ into irreducible $\mathfrak{o}(d) \otimes \mathfrak{o}(2k)$ modules, the author extends the $\mathfrak{o}(2k)$ representation to a representation of the Pin group $\mathrm{Pin}(2k)$ by constructing an explicit operator $\sigma$ that implements the action of a reflection element $\alpha = a_1^\dagger - a_1$ on the Fock space. The action of $\sigma$ on the highest weight states $\phi_D$ is computed, showing that the $\mathfrak{o}(2k)$ modules $\Psi_\mu$ carry irreducible representations of $\mathrm{Pin}(2k)$ with the appropriate highest weight assignments.

## Key numbers

- The single-kind state space $\mathcal S$ has dimension $d$, and there are $k$ fermion kinds, giving a $dk$-dimensional space $\mathcal S \otimes \mathcal K$ and a Clifford algebra $\mathrm{Cl}(2dk)$.
- The operator $\sigma$ satisfies $\sigma^2 = (-1)^\Omega$ with $\Omega = \lfloor d/2 \rfloor$, and the normalization constant $c = i^\Omega$ is chosen so that $\rho(\alpha)^2 = \rho(-1) = (-1)^d$.
- For even $d$, $\rho(-1) = 1$, so the $\mathrm{Pin}(2k)$ representation factors through $\mathrm{O}(2k)$.
- The duality decomposition $\Phi = \bigoplus \mathrm{X}_\lambda \otimes \Psi_\mu$ sums over pairs $(\lambda, \mu)$ whose Young diagrams fill a $d/2 \times k$ frame without overlap, as illustrated for $(d,k) = (12,4)$ with $\lambda = (-1,1,2,2,3,4)$ and $\mu = (0,0,0,1,1,2,4,5)$.

## Context

This work provides the proof of a theorem cited without proof in Neergård (2021) and previously available only in a preprint (Neergård 2020a). It extends the $\mathfrak{o}(d)$–$\mathfrak{o}(2k)$ duality of Neergård (2019) to a duality between $\mathfrak{o}(d)$ and $\mathrm{Pin}(2k)$, analogous to the $\mathrm{O}(d)$–$\mathfrak{o}(2k)$ duality discussed in Neergård (2021, 2020b). The result proves the purely fermionic case of a theorem by Cheng, Kwon, and Wang (2010) on dual Fock space representations of a Lie superalgebra and a Pin group, without relying on a first fundamental theorem of invariant theory for Pin groups, which Howe (1994) notes does not exist. The three duality theorems ($\mathfrak{o}(d)$–$\mathfrak{o}(2k)$, $\mathrm{O}(d)$–$\mathfrak{o}(2k)$, and $\mathfrak{o}(d)$–$\mathrm{Pin}(2k)$) are shown to be equivalent, all ultimately based on a character calculation analogous to Helmers's (1961) proof of symplectic-symplectic duality.
