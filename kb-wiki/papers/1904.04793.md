# Bayesian averaging of computer models with domain discrepancies: a nuclear physics perspective

> This article studies Bayesian model averaging (BMA) in the context of competing expensive computer models in a typical nuclear physics setup. While it is well known that BMA accounts for the additional uncertainty of the model itself, we show that it also decreases the posterior variance of the prediction errors via an explicit decomposition. We extend BMA to the situation where the competing models are defined on non-identical study regions. Any model's local forecasting difficulty is offset by predictions obtained from the average model, thus extending individual models to the full domain. We illustrate our methodology via pedagogical simulations and applications to forecasting nuclear observables, which exhibit convincing improvements in both the BMA prediction error and empirical coverage probabilities.

## Key claim

The paper shows that Bayesian model averaging (BMA) not only accounts for model uncertainty but also reduces the posterior mean square error (PMSE) of predictions compared to any individual model, and extends BMA to handle competing models defined on non-identical input domains, a situation common in nuclear physics where different models cover different regions of the nuclear chart.

## Method

The authors derive a decomposition of the PMSE for BMA predictions, proving that the BMA posterior mean estimator is optimal among all convex combinations of individual model posterior means in the L2 sense under the posterior distribution. They then develop a "domain-corrected BMA" procedure that adjusts model weights when models are defined on different subsets of the data, replacing missing model predictions with a geometric average over models that do produce predictions, computed via evidence integrals and posterior predictive distributions.

## Key numbers

- The relative gain of BMA over the best individual model is expressed as r²_BMA = p(M1|y)² × (ŷ1* - ŷ2*)² / E[(ŷ2* - y*)²|y] for the two-model case, where p(M1|y) is the posterior probability of model 1 and ŷ1*, ŷ2* are posterior means
- The PMSE of the BMA predictor is smaller than the PMSE of any individual model's posterior mean estimator, with the reduction quantified by p₂²(ŷ1* - ŷ2*)² for model 1 and p₁²(ŷ1* - ŷ2*)² for model 2
- In the domain-corrected framework, the evidence for a model M_k with domain subset y^(k) is proportional to p(y^(k)|M_k) × p(y^(-k)|y^(k)) × π(M_k), where y^(-k) is the complement of y^(k) in the full data

## Context

This work extends the classical BMA framework of Kass and Raftery (1995) and Geweke (1999) to address a gap in the literature: no principled approach existed for averaging models with overlapping but non-identical domains, a situation arising in nuclear physics where ab initio models cover lighter nuclei while energy density functionals cover the full nuclear chart, as discussed by Klupfel (2008) and Kortelainen (2010). The method enables more honest uncertainty quantification for nuclear observables such as masses and radii when multiple models with different coverage regions are available, and could apply to time series with missing data or different time scales in other fields.
