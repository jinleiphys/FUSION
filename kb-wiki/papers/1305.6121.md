# Direct photon production and interferometry in $\sqrt{s}_{NN}$=200 GeV Au+Au and in $\sqrt{s}_{NN}$=2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions

> Direct photon production, in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 GeV Au+Au and in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions, are studied in a hydrodynamic model. Ideal hydrodynamic model, initialised to reproduce experimentally charged particle spectra in RHIC and LHC energy, also reproduces the PHENIX and ALICE measurements for the direct photon spectra in Au+Au collisions at RHIC and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC. The model however produces less elliptic flow than in experiment. Discrepancy between experiment and hydrodynamic simulation is comparatively less in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC than in Au+Au collisions at RHIC. We also studied direct photon correlation and determined the HBT radii. In 0-10%-50-60% collisions, HBT radii in Au+Au or in Pb+Pb collisions do not show large centrality dependence. Energy dependence of the HBT radii for direct photons is also not large.

## Key claim

An ideal hydrodynamic model that reproduces charged particle spectra at RHIC and LHC also reproduces the direct photon spectra measured by PHENIX and ALICE, but it systematically underpredicts the elliptic flow of direct photons; the discrepancy between experiment and simulation is smaller for Pb+Pb collisions at LHC than for Au+Au collisions at RHIC.

## Method

The author solved ideal (non-viscous) relativistic hydrodynamic equations with boost-invariance using the AZHYDRO-KOLKATA code, initializing the fluid at τ_i = 0.6 fm with a Glauber-model transverse energy density profile (hard-scattering fraction f = 0.25 for Au+Au at 200 GeV, f = 0.9 for Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV). Direct photon production rates from a hadronic gas (parametrized from Turbide 2003) and from a quark-gluon plasma (leading-order rates from Arnold 2001) were convolved with the space-time evolution of the fluid. HBT correlation functions for two photons were computed via Monte-Carlo sampling for transverse momenta 0 ≤ k_T ≤ 1 GeV, and the outward (R_out) and sideward (R_side) radii were extracted from the half-widths of the correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- Central energy density ε_0 = 30.0 GeV/fm³ and central temperature T = 370 MeV for Au+Au at √s_NN = 200 GeV; ε_0 = 125 GeV/fm³ and T = 530 MeV for Pb+Pb at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV.
- Inverse slope parameter from exponential fit to direct photon spectra (p_T range 1–2 GeV) in 0–10% centrality: 219 MeV for Au+Au at 200 GeV; 260 MeV for Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV.
- Inverse slope parameter in 70–80% centrality: 185 MeV for Au+Au at 200 GeV; 202 MeV for Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV.
- From RHIC to LHC (energy increase factor ~14), R_out increases by 30–50% in 10–20% to 50–60% centrality; R_side decreases by ~10–20% in central 0–10% and 10–20% collisions and increases by ~30–40% in peripheral 20–30% to 50–60% collisions.
- The ratio R_out/R_side increases by ~10–45% from RHIC to LHC.

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic analyses of charged particle spectra and elliptic flow by the same author (Chaudhuri 2009, 2008, 2011) to direct photon observables at both RHIC and LHC energies. It directly compares with PHENIX measurements (Afanasiev 2012, Adare 2011) for Au+Au at 200 GeV and ALICE measurements (Lohner 2012) for Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV. The finding that ideal hydro reproduces photon spectra but underpredicts elliptic flow, especially at RHIC, highlights the need for dissipative (viscous) hydrodynamic treatments and further theoretical work on photon production mechanisms.
