# Phenomenological Scaling Relations for SQM Stars with a Massive s-Quark in Gravitationally Strong Magnetic Fields under the Spherical Symmetry Approximation

> Scaling relations with the bag constant parameter are investigated for Strange Quark Matter (SQM) stars in the presence of gravitationally strong magnetic fields minimally coupled with matter, considering both massless and massive strange quark scenarios. Assuming a simple model for such coupling under the approximation of spherical symmetry, a phenomenological scaling formula for the maximum mass of stars is derived as a function of the surface magnetic field and the strange quark mass. This formula is applicable for all formally admissible values of the free parameters, and strict scaling with the bag constant is retained only for a vanishing strange quark mass. As a byproduct of this study, the mathematical structure of the equation of state for the relativistic SQM model with a massive strange quark is revisited without approximations. It is observed that the contribution of the electron term to the energy density cannot be neglected in the theoretical limit of large strange quark masses. Consequently, the maximum mass of SQM stars increases with sufficiently large strange quark mass, contrasting with the behavior observed for low strange quark masses.

## Key claim

The paper derives a phenomenological scaling formula for the maximum mass of Strange Quark Matter (SQM) stars as a function of surface magnetic field and strange quark mass, but strict scaling with the bag constant is retained only for a vanishing strange quark mass; the formula is applicable for all formally admissible values of the free parameters only if a dimensionless parameter involving the bag constant and the strange quark mass remains constant.

## Method

The authors model SQM stars under spherical symmetry with gravitationally strong magnetic fields introduced at the level of the stress-energy tensor, assuming the electromagnetic energy density is proportional to the energy density of SQM matter. They reformulate the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations using baryon number density as the independent integration variable and solve the resulting system of first-order differential equations numerically. The equation of state for SQM with a massive strange quark is revisited without approximations, using a parametric description with the fractional electron density as the auxiliary parameter, and the minimum energy condition is imposed under charge neutrality and fixed baryon number density constraints.

## Key numbers

- The reference bag constant adopted is B_ref = 57.81068 MeV/fm^3, chosen so that a symmetric SQM star with this bag constant has a maximum mass of 2 M_sun (assuming M_sun = 1.98892 x 10^33 g)
- The magnetic field strength with energy density equal to B_ref is H_ref = sqrt(8 pi B_ref) = 1.525735 x 10^18 Gs
- The dimensionless parameter b relating bag constant and strange quark mass is b = 11.98351 x (B/B_60) x (m_150/m_s)^4, where B_60 = 60 MeV/fm^3 and m_150 = 150 MeV/c^2
- The scale for magnetic field strength is h_o = 3.175000 x 10^17 Gs at the reference mass m_f = 150 MeV/c^2
- The scale for stellar mass is m_o = 74.18451 M_sun at the reference mass m_f = 150 MeV/c^2
- The scale for stellar radius is r_o = 109.5708 km at the reference mass m_f = 150 MeV/c^2

## Context

This work extends the scaling relations previously investigated by Zdunik (2000) for SQM stars with a massive strange quark, and continues the authors' prior work (Bratek, 2023) which examined symmetric SQM star models with magnetic fields. The study demonstrates how the scaling symmetry with the bag constant is broken by introducing the strange quark mass, and shows that the electron contribution to the energy density cannot be neglected in the limit of large strange quark masses, leading to an increase in maximum mass with sufficiently large strange quark mass that contrasts with the behavior at low strange quark masses. The results enable phenomenological modeling of SQM stars with magnetic fields across a range of admissible parameter values.
