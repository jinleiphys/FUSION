# Proton-Proton and Proton-Neutron Correlations in Medium-Weight Nuclei: Role of the Tensor Force within a Many-Body Cluster Expansion

> A detailed analysis of the effect of tensor correlations on one- and two-body densities and momentum distributions of complex nuclei is presented within a linked cluster expansion providing reliable results for the ground state properties of nuclei calculated with realistic interactions.

## Key claim

The tensor force produces a large enhancement of proton-neutron (pn) over proton-proton (pp) two-nucleon momentum distributions at relative momenta above 1.5 fm⁻¹ in medium-weight nuclei (¹²C, ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca), and this effect can be reliably described within a lowest-order linked cluster expansion using realistic interactions.

## Method

The authors used a linked cluster expansion in lowest order of the correlation operator η̂ᵢⱼ = f̂²ᵢⱼ − 1 to compute one-body and two-body densities and momentum distributions for ¹²C, ¹⁶O, and ⁴⁰Ca. The correlated ground state wave function was constructed from a mean-field Slater determinant multiplied by a product of correlation operators f̂(ij) that include central, spin, isospin, and tensor components derived from the V8′ realistic interaction. Single-particle wave functions were taken in both harmonic oscillator and Woods-Saxon forms, with parameters fixed by minimizing the ground state energy.

## Key numbers

- For ¹²C, the ratio of pn to pp pairs in the nucleus is 12/5; for ¹⁶O it is 16/7; for ⁴⁰Ca it is 40/19.
- At relative momentum k_rel ≥ 1.5 fm⁻¹, the momentum distribution of pn pairs is much larger than that of pp pairs in all three nuclei.
- In carbon, over 74% of protons with momenta above 275 MeV/c were found to be members of an np correlated pair (citing prior experimental analysis).
- The ratio of ¹²C(e,e′pp)X to ¹²C(e,e′p)X events for proton missing momenta above 300 MeV/c is 9.5 ± 2% (citing prior experiment E01-015).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of tensor-force effects on two-nucleon momentum distributions from few-body systems and light nuclei (A < 8), previously studied with Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) by Rocco and collaborators, to medium-weight nuclei up to A=40. The results confirm the factorization approximation of the two-nucleon momentum distribution used by Ciofi degli Atti and Simula (1996) to construct one-nucleon spectral functions, and provide theoretical support for experimental observations of strong pn dominance in short-range correlations from the EVA/BNL and JLab E01-015 experiments. The approach enables parameter-free calculations of short-range correlations in nuclei that can be compared with planned measurements of ⁴He(e,e′pp), (e,e′pn), and (e,e′pp) ratios at missing momenta of 500–875 MeV/c.
