# 2PI effective action for gauge theories: Renormalization

> We discuss the application of two-particle-irreducible (2PI) functional techniques to gauge theories, focusing on the issue of non-perturbative renormalization. In particular, we show how to renormalize the photon and fermion propagators of QED obtained from a systematic loop expansion of the 2PI effective action. At any finite order, this implies introducing new counterterms as compared to the usual ones in perturbation theory. We show that these new counterterms are consistent with the 2PI Ward identities and are systematically of higher order than the approximation order, which guarantees the convergence of the approximation scheme. Our analysis can be applied to any theory with linearly realized gauge symmetry. This is for instance the case of QCD quantized in the background field gauge.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the two-particle-irreducible (2PI) effective action for QED can be renormalized order by order in a loop expansion, but only by introducing new counterterms (such as a photon mass term and four-photon couplings) that have no analog in standard perturbation theory; these extra counterterms are consistent with the 2PI Ward identities and are systematically of higher order than the approximation order, ensuring the convergence of the scheme.

## Method

The authors consider QED in the linear covariant gauge using dimensional regularization, working with the 2PI effective action as a functional of the full fermion and photon propagators. They classify all local counterterms of mass dimension ≤ 4 consistent with the 2PI gauge symmetry at two-loop order, then show how to renormalize the photon and fermion propagators obtained as saddle points of the 2PI functional. The renormalization of the four-photon vertex is handled via a ladder (Bethe-Salpeter) equation, with the extra counterterms absorbing non-transverse divergences that appear at finite loop order.

## Key numbers

- At L-loop order in the 2PI loop expansion, the non-perturbative propagators agree with the perturbative expansion of the exact propagators up to and including order e^{2(L-1)}.
- The extra counterterms δg₁ and δg₂ are shown to be of order e^{2L} when suitable renormalization conditions are imposed, guaranteeing convergence to the exact theory as L → ∞.
- The photon polarization tensor at L-loop order satisfies k^μ Π̄_{μν}(k) = O(e^{2L}), meaning transversality is violated only at higher order than the approximation.

## Context

This work extends the 2PI renormalization program previously developed for scalar theories (van Hees and Knoll, 2002; Berges, 2005) and fermionic theories (Reinosa and Serreau, 2005) to the case of gauge theories, specifically QED. It addresses a long-standing obstacle to applying 2PI techniques to gauge theories by showing that gauge-dependent contributions are parametrically suppressed and that renormalization is possible. The analysis applies directly to any theory with linearly realized gauge symmetry, such as QCD in the background field gauge, and enables practical non-perturbative calculations in thermal and non-equilibrium QED.
