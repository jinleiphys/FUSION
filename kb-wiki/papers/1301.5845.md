# Enhanced J/Psi-pair production from double parton scatterings in nucleus-nucleus collisions at the Large Hadron Collider

> A generic expression of double-parton scattering cross sections in high-energy nucleus-nucleus (A-A) collisions is derived as a function of the corresponding single-parton hard cross sections and of the A-A event centrality. We consider the case of prompt-J/psi production in lead-lead (Pb-Pb) at the CERN Large Hadron Collider and find that about 20% (35%) of the J/psi events in minimum-bias (most central) collisions contain a second J/psi from double parton interactions. In Pb-Pb at 5.5 TeV, in the absence of final-state effects, about 240 double-J/psi events are expected per unit midrapidity and per inverse-nanobarn in the dilepton decay modes. The implications of double-J/psi production on the interpretation of the observed J/psi suppression in A-A collisions are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives a generic expression for double-parton scattering (DPS) cross sections in nucleus-nucleus (A-A) collisions and finds that about 20% (35%) of J/psi events in minimum-bias (most central) lead-lead collisions at the LHC contain a second J/psi from double parton interactions, with the caveat that these estimates assume no final-state effects such as quark-gluon plasma suppression or regeneration.

## Method

The authors derive the DPS cross section in A-A collisions by summing three contributions: partons from the same nucleon pair (scaled by A^2), partons from one nucleon in one nucleus interacting with two different nucleons in the other nucleus, and partons from two different nucleons in both nuclei. They apply this formalism to prompt J/psi production in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV, using single-parton J/psi cross sections from the color evaporation model (CEM) with EPS09 nuclear PDF modifications, and evaluate the centrality dependence via the nuclear overlap function T_AA(b).

## Key numbers

- About 20% of J/psi events in minimum-bias Pb-Pb collisions contain a second J/psi from DPS; about 35% in most central collisions.
- In Pb-Pb at 5.5 TeV, about 240 double-J/psi events are expected per unit midrapidity and per inverse-nanobarn in the dilepton decay modes (assuming no final-state effects).
- The effective A-A normalization cross section for DPS in Pb-Pb is sigma_eff,AA = 1.5 nb.
- The nuclear overlap function at b=0 for Pb-Pb is T_AA(0) = 30.4 mb^-1 (using Fermi-Dirac density) or 31.5 mb^-1 (hard-sphere approximation).
- The DPS enhancement factor in Pb-Pb compared to nucleon-nucleon collisions is of order 200, dominated by interactions of partons from two different nucleons in both nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the DPS formalism previously developed for proton-nucleus collisions by d'Enterria and Snigirev (2012) to nucleus-nucleus collisions, and provides the first quantitative estimate of double-J/psi production in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC. The results have implications for interpreting the observed J/psi suppression in A-A collisions, as DPS contributions could mimic reduced suppression if not properly accounted for in models combining dissociation and regeneration (e.g., Andronic 2007, Zhao 2011). The paper enables future experimental searches for double-J/psi events in Pb-Pb data at the LHC to validate the DPS predictions and refine the interpretation of quarkonium measurements in heavy-ion collisions.
