# Charged Particle Ratio Fluctuation as a Signal for QGP

> In this letter we argue that the event-by-event fluctuations of the ratio of the positively charged and the negatively charged pions provides a signal of quark-gluon plasma. The fact that quarks carry fractional charges is ultimately responsible for this distinct signal.

# Charged Particle Ratio Fluctuation as a Signal for QGP

## Key claim

The event-by-event fluctuation of the ratio of positively charged to negatively charged pions can serve as a distinct signal for quark-gluon plasma formation, provided the detected phase space is a small sub-system of the whole and the original quarks and gluons stay in the system during or after hadronization (rapidity intervals satisfying y_total >> y_accept >> 1). The signal relies on the fact that quarks carry fractional charges, making the fluctuation in the charge ratio about a factor of 3-4 smaller for QGP than for a hadron gas.

## Method

The authors derive an expression for the event-by-event fluctuation of the charge ratio R = N_+/N_- in terms of the net charge fluctuation, showing that <N_ch><delta R^2> = 4 <delta Q^2>/<N_ch>. They compute this quantity for a thermal pion gas (where the fundamental charge unit is 1) and for a quark-gluon plasma (where the fundamental charge unit is 1/3), using both a non-interacting thermal gas model and lattice QCD results for the quark number susceptibility and entropy density from Gottlieb et al. (1997). The analysis assumes entropy conservation during hadronization and that the detected phase space is a small subsystem of the whole collision.

## Key numbers

- For a thermal pion gas: D_had = <N_ch><delta R^2> ≈ 4 (from Eq. 13), reduced to ≈ 3 when resonance contributions are included (Eq. 21)
- For a non-interacting thermal QGP: D_QGP ≈ 0.75 (Eq. 17), more than a factor of 5 smaller than the pion gas value
- From lattice QCD calculations (Gottlieb et al. 1997) with 2 flavors of dynamic quarks: D_lat ≈ 1 (Eq. 20), still 4 times smaller than the pion gas result
- The quark number susceptibilities from lattice are close to the non-interacting thermal gas limit: chi_S ≈ chi_NS ≈ 2 T^2 at high temperature
- The lattice calculation gives total entropy density sigma_qg ≈ 12 T^3 (Eq. 19), with quark entropy density about one half of the non-interacting thermal fermion value (alpha ≈ 1/2)
- At RHIC, the expected average net charge to charged multiplicity ratio is <Q>/<N_ch> ∼ 5%
- For STAR at RHIC: y_total ≈ 10, y_accept ≈ 3

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Jeon and Koch, 1999) on multiplicity ratio fluctuations as a probe of QGP, and is related to similar ideas by Baym and collaborators (1999). The proposal provides a direct experimental test of lattice QCD calculations of quark number susceptibilities, specifically those by Gottlieb et al. (1997). The measurement is expected to be feasible with the STAR detector at RHIC within the first few days of data collection. The paper also notes that a similar approach using strangeness-to-anti-strangeness ratio fluctuations could provide additional handles on QGP formation, and that a preprint by Asakawa, Heinz and Muller addresses related issues.
