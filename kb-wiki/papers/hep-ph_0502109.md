# Excited hadrons as a signal for quark-gluon plasma formation

> At the quark-hadron transition, when quarks get confined to hadrons, certain orbitally excited states, namely those which have excitation energies above the respective $L = 0$ states of the same order as the transition temperature $T_c$, may form easily because of thermal velocities of quarks at the transition temperature. We propose that the ratio of multiplicities of such excited states to the respective $L = 0$ states can serve as an almost model independent signal for the quark-gluon plasma formation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. For example, the ratio $R^*$ of multiplicities of $D_{SJ}^{*\pm}(2317)(J^P = 0^+)$ and $D_S^{*\pm}(2112)(J^P = 1^-)$ when plotted with respect to the center of mass energy of the collision $\sqrt{s}$ (or vs. centrality/number of participants), should show a jump at the value of $\sqrt{s}$ beyond which the QGP formation occurs. This should happen irrespective of the shape of the overall plot of $R^*$ vs. $\sqrt{s}$. Recent data from RHIC on $Λ^*/Λ$ vs. N$_{part}$ for large values of N$_{part}$ may be indicative of such a behavior, though there are large error bars. We give a list of several other such candidate hadronic states.

## Key claim

The ratio of multiplicities of specific orbitally excited hadronic states to their respective \(L = 0\) states should show a jump enhancement at the center-of-mass energy (or centrality) where quark-gluon plasma formation begins, provided the orbital excitation energy is of order \(\alpha T_c\) with \(\alpha\) roughly in the range 1–3. This jump should occur irrespective of the overall shape of the ratio versus energy curve, though the authors note that existing RHIC data on \(\Lambda^*/\Lambda\) versus number of participants show only a possible slight increase within large error bars.

## Method

The authors propose that at the quark-hadron transition, thermal velocities of quarks and antiquarks (of order \(T_c\)) can directly contribute to orbital excitation of hadrons when the excitation energy matches the available thermal kinetic energy. They argue that for hadronization from a deconfined QGP state, specific orbitally excited states with excitation energies close to \(\alpha T_c\) will form with enhanced rates compared to hadronization from systems that never reach \(T_c\). The signal is the ratio \(R^* = N(M^*)/N(M)\) of multiplicities of an orbitally excited meson to its \(L = 0\) partner, plotted as a function of \(\sqrt{s}\) or number of participants, which should exhibit an abrupt jump at the threshold for QGP formation.

## Key numbers

* The transition temperature \(T_c\) is taken to be approximately 170 MeV.
* The parameter \(\alpha\) (fraction of thermal kinetic energy converted to orbital excitation) is estimated to lie in the range 1–3, with a more probable range of 1–2, giving \(\alpha T_c\) in the range 150–500 MeV (more probably 150–350 MeV).
* The average thermal kinetic energy per degree of freedom for quarks at hadronization is about \(\frac{1}{2}T_c\), so the combined thermal energy of a quark-antiquark pair usable for orbital excitation is roughly \(T_c\) to \(2T_c\).
* The candidate meson pair \(D_{SJ}^{*\pm}(2317)(J^P = 0^+)\) and \(D_S^{*\pm}(2112)(J^P = 1^-)\) has an excitation energy of 205 MeV (2317 – 2112 MeV), which falls within the estimated \(\alpha T_c\) range.

## Context

This work extends the search for QGP signals beyond traditional approaches such as J/\(\psi\) suppression (J/\(\psi\) suppression, proposed by Matsui and Satz, 1986) and thermal model analyses of hadron abundances (Braun-Munzinger et al., 2003; Becattini et al., 2004). The proposed signal is intended to be nearly model-independent, relying only on the defining property of deconfinement at \(T_c\), unlike other signals that depend sensitively on plasma evolution details. The authors note that existing RHIC data on \(\Lambda^*(1520)/\Lambda\) versus number of participants (reported by the STAR Collaboration, 2004) show a decreasing trend but may contain a slight increase at large \(N_{part}\) that could be consistent with their prediction, though the error bars are large. The work enables experimental searches for a common jump in \(R^*\) across multiple hadronic states as a definitive signature of QGP formation, and potentially provides an independent determination of \(T_c\) from the excitation energies of the states that exhibit the jump.
