# Twin Stars in General Relativity and Extended Theories of Gravity

> We explore gravity-independent equations of state for neutron stars, particularly focusing on twin stars. Examining four categories, we emphasize their behavior in both General Relativity and Palatini gravity. Additionally, we discuss a subcategory of type I, which, in the context of General Relativity, does not exhibit twin star phenomena, yet demonstrates this phenomenon in modified gravity. Furthermore, we briefly address challenges associated with the negative trace of the energy-momentum tensor, prevalent in both theories.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that certain equations of state which do not produce twin stars (two neutron stars with equal mass but different radii) in General Relativity can exhibit this phenomenon when analyzed in Palatini f(R) gravity, specifically for a subcategory of type I phase transitions.

## Method
The authors constructed gravity-independent equations of state using chiral Effective Field Theory at N3LO for low densities (0.05-0.34 fm^-3) and perturbative QCD at N3LO for high densities (above 40 nuclear saturation densities), with interpolation connecting these regimes at n=1.5 nuclear saturation densities. They classified twin stars into four categories based on the masses of the hadronic and hybrid branch maxima, then solved the modified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for both General Relativity and Palatini f(R) gravity to compare the resulting mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass of the stiffest equation of state (EoSmax): 3.365 solar masses
- Maximum mass of EoS1: 2.75 solar masses
- Maximum mass of EoS2: 2.44 solar masses
- Mass gap range for type I twin stars: 0.01 to 0.34 solar masses
- Mass gap range for type II twin stars: 0.1 to 1.3 solar masses
- Latent heat range for type I twin stars: 0.094 to 0.149
- Latent heat range for type II twin stars: 0.12 to 0.22
- Critical energy density jumps for type I twin stars in EoSmax: 220 to 491 MeV/fm^3 at pressure points between 62 and 97 MeV/fm^3
- Energy density at which negative trace appears: 348 MeV/fm^-3 for EoSmax, 523 MeV/fm^-3 for EoS1, 694 MeV/fm^-3 for EoS2
- Weak-field limit constraint on Palatini parameter beta: absolute value less than 2 times 10^8 m^2

## Context
This work extends the classification of twin stars from Christian (2017) by examining how these configurations behave in Palatini f(R) gravity rather than only in General Relativity. The authors use gravity-independent equations of state developed in their prior work (Lope-Oter, 2019; Lope-Oter, 2023) that avoid astrophysical constraints dependent on General Relativity, enabling cleaner tests of modified gravity theories. The results enable future observational discrimination between General Relativity and extended theories of gravity through twin star detections, and highlight challenges with negative trace of the energy-momentum tensor that appear in both theories.
