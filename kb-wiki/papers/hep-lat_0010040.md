# Flavor and Quark Mass Dependence of QCD Thermodynamics

> We calculate the transition temperature in 2 and 3-flavor QCD using improved gauge and staggered fermion actions on lattices with temporal extent Nt=4. We find Tc=173(8)MeV and 154(8)MeV for nf=2 and 3, respectively. In the case of 3-flavor QCD we present evidence that the chiral critical point, i.e. the second order endpoint of the line of first order chiral phase transitions, belongs to the universality class of the 3d Ising model.

# Flavor and Quark Mass Dependence of QCD Thermodynamics

## Key claim

The transition temperature in 2-flavor QCD is 173(8) MeV and in 3-flavor QCD is 154(8) MeV, but these values carry statistical errors only; systematic errors from remaining cut-off effects and the chiral extrapolation ansatz are expected to be of similar magnitude, and calculations on lattices with larger temporal extent are still needed to control them.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with a Symanzik improved gauge action and a p4-improved staggered fermion action (fat 1-link terms) on 16^3 × 4 lattices to determine pseudo-critical couplings β_c(m_q) for 2 and 3-flavor QCD over a quark mass range 0.025 ≤ m_q ≤ 1.0. They set the temperature scale by computing the string tension and vector meson mass on 16^4 lattices at the same β_c values, and extrapolated T_c to the chiral limit using fits guided by universality scaling expectations.

## Key numbers

- T_c = 173 ± 8 MeV for n_f = 2 (chiral limit, statistical error only)
- T_c = 154 ± 8 MeV for n_f = 3 (chiral limit, statistical error only)
- T_c/m_ρ = 0.225 ± 0.010 for n_f = 2 and 0.20 ± 0.01 for n_f = 3 in the chiral limit
- The smallest quark mass simulated corresponds to m_PS ≈ 350 MeV
- For 3-flavor QCD, the chiral critical point (second order endpoint) occurs at β_c(0) = 3.258(4)
- T_c in 3-flavor QCD is about 10% smaller than for n_f = 2 over a wide range of quark masses
- Systematic cut-off effects from unimproved actions on N_τ = 4 lattices are about 10%

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of finite-temperature QCD by Karsch et al. (2000) using improved actions to reduce the large cut-off effects that had caused significant discrepancies between standard staggered and Wilson fermion results for T_c. The 2-flavor result agrees well with a calculation using improved Wilson fermions by Ali Khan et al. (2000). The finding that the chiral critical point of 3-flavor QCD belongs to the 3d Ising universality class supports a conjecture by Gavai et al. (1994). The results enable more reliable comparisons with bag model and percolation model predictions, and provide a baseline for future calculations on larger lattices (N_τ > 4) needed to control systematic errors.
