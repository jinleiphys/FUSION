# Flow at AGS Energies: A Barometer for High Density Effects?

> Preliminary data on transverse energy `flow' and event asymmetries reported by the E877(814) collaborations are compared to ARC model calculations for Au+Au at full AGS beam energy. ARC triple differential cross-sections for protons and pions are presented. Proton flow is produced in ARC, with the maximum in-plane momentum about 120 MeV/c. For central events the directed momentum for pions is near zero, consistent with experiment. Pion momentum opposite to the nucleons' is evident in a peripheral sample, however, indicating that this pion `anti-flow' involves absorption by `spectator' matter. `Squeeze-out' of protons in central events at mid-rapidity is suggested by the ARC distributions.

# Flow at AGS Energies: A Barometer for High Density Effects?

## Key claim

The ARC cascade model reproduces the observed proton flow and the near-zero pion directed flow in central Au+Au collisions at AGS beam energy, but the absolute flow magnitude is more sensitive to the treatment of two-body scattering orbits at 11.6 GeV/c than at 2 GeV/c, suggesting that the cascade description of flow is viable but that the potential contribution becomes more important at higher energy.

## Method

The authors compared preliminary data from the E877/E814 collaborations on transverse energy azimuthal asymmetry and directed transverse momentum with calculations from the ARC (A Relativistic Cascade) model for Au+Au collisions at the full AGS beam energy of 11.6 GeV/c per nucleon. They computed the first and second moments v1 and v2 of the azimuthal transverse energy distribution from ARC events, simulating the segmentation of the participant calorimeter (PCAL) with sixteen bins in phi over the pseudorapidity interval 1.85 < eta < 4.5. Triple differential cross-sections for protons and pions were generated for central and peripheral event samples, using an energy-dependent interpolation between repulsive orbits at low energies (below approximately 300 MeV) and a 50/50 mixture of repulsive and attractive orbits at high energies (above approximately 500 MeV) to model the transition from real potential scattering to diffractive scattering.

## Key numbers

- Maximum in-plane proton directed momentum <P_x> ~ 120 MeV/c produced in ARC for Au+Au at full AGS beam energy
- For central events (10% of PCAL E_T), pion <P_x> is near zero, consistent with experiment
- The dispersion in the event plane determined from PCAL is on the order of 60 degrees (FWHM) from experiment
- The E_T spectrum calculated directly from ARC agrees with experiment at better than 10% level for all but the most peripheral events
- At BEVALAC energies, purely repulsive orbits produced a less than 10% increase in flow; at AGS energies, the choice of orbits can produce as much as 60% change in <P_x> for a central cut
- The average pion number per inelastic collision at AGS energies is 3.8

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous successful application of ARC to Bevalac data on sideways flow (Kahana et al., 1995) to the higher AGS energy regime, where pion production becomes dominant and the two-body optical theorem is violated. The calculations are compared to preliminary results from the E877/E814 collaborations (Barrette et al., 1994) and to earlier asymmetry measurements from E866 (Abbott et al., 1993). The paper suggests that measurements at intermediate beam momenta of 2-8 GeV/c, such as those planned with the EOS TPC at the AGS, could reveal a reduction in flow if quark-gluon plasma formation occurs at high baryon density, with the BEVALAC and full AGS results providing the baseline flow values at the endpoints.
