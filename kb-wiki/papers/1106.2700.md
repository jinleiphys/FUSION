# On critical scaling at the QCD N_f=2 chiral phase transition

> We investigate the critical scaling of the quark propagator of N_f=2 QCD close to the chiral phase transition at finite temperature. We argue that it is mandatory to take into account the back-reaction effects of pions and the sigma onto the quark to observe critical behavior beyond mean field. On condition of self-consistency of the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation we extract the scaling behavior for the quark propagator analytically. Crucial in this respect is the correct pion dispersion relation when the critical temperature is approached from below. Our results are consistent with the known relations for the quark condensate and the pion decay constant from universality. We verify the analytical findings also numerically assuming the critical dispersion relation for the Goldstone bosons.

## Key claim

The authors argue that including back-reaction effects of pions and the sigma meson onto the quark propagator is mandatory to observe critical scaling beyond mean field in the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation; the scaling behavior emerges only under the condition of self-consistency and with the correct pion dispersion relation when the critical temperature is approached from below.

## Method

The authors perform an analytical scaling analysis of the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) for N_f=2 QCD at finite temperature, including meson back-coupling via one-meson exchange diagrams for pions and the sigma meson. They extract the scaling exponent for the scalar dressing function B by requiring self-consistency of the DSE, and verify the analytical findings numerically by solving the truncated quark DSE with a scaling ansatz for the transverse pion decay constant f_s.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature determined from the scalar quark dressing function: T_c = 162.5 ± 2.5 MeV (using all Matsubara frequencies in pion propagators)
- Critical temperature with only zeroth Matsubara frequency and sigma meson included: T_c = 157 ± 2.5 MeV
- Scaling exponent for the scalar dressing function B: x = ν/2 with ν ≈ 0.73 (O(4) universality class value)
- Scaling region extends approximately 10 MeV below T_c (deviations from scaling set in at roughly T_c - T ≈ 10 MeV)
- The chiral condensate scales as ⟨ψ̄ψ⟩ ∼ t^{ν/2}, consistent with the scalar dressing function

## Context

This work extends previous Dyson-Schwinger studies that had only observed critical scaling at the mean field level (Roberts 2000, Blank 2010) by including meson back-reaction effects along the lines of zero-temperature studies by Fischer (2008). The results are consistent with the O(4) universality class predictions for the chiral phase transition in N_f=2 QCD, supporting the scenario originally suggested by Pisarski and Wilczek (1984). The analysis provides a basis for further functional method studies of the chiral phase transition and connects scaling properties in effective theories to the behavior of fundamental quark propagators.
