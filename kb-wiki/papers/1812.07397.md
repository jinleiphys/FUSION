# Charmonium decay widths in magnetized matter

> We study the partial decay widths of the charmonium states ($J/ψ$, $ψ(3686)$, $ψ(3770)$, $χ_{c0}$, $χ_{c2}$) to $D\bar D$ ($D^+D^-$ or $D^0\bar {D^0}$) in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter, in the presence of strong magnetic fields. The in-medium partial decay widths of charmonium states to $D\bar D$ are calculated within a light quark--antiquark pair creation model, namely the $^3P_0$ model, using the in--medium masses of the charmonia as well as $D$ and $\bar D$ mesons in the magnetized nuclear matter obtained within a chiral effective model. The presence of a magnetic field leads to Landau quantization of the energy levels of the proton in the nuclear medium. The effects of magnetic field and isospin asymmetry on the charmonium decay widths to $D\bar D$ are found to be quite prominent. The effects of the anomalous magnetic moments have also been taken into consideration for obtaining the in-medium masses of these heavy flavour mesons, used to calculate the partial decay widths of the charmonium states. The medium modifications of the charmonium decay widths can have observable consequences on the production of the charmed mesons in high energy asymmetric heavy ion collision experiments.

## Key claim

The partial decay widths of charmonium states to $D\bar D$ in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter are significantly modified by strong magnetic fields, with the effects depending on the specific charmonium state and the inclusion of anomalous magnetic moments of nucleons; the abstract's claim of "quite prominent" effects is qualified by the observation that the medium modifications of the dilaton field give only marginal modifications to the meson masses compared to the frozen glueball approximation.

## Method

The authors compute in-medium partial decay widths of five charmonium states ($J/\psi$, $\psi(3686)$, $\psi(3770)$, $\chi_{c0}$, $\chi_{c2}$) to $D^+D^-$ and $D^0\bar{D^0}$ using the $^3P_0$ light quark-antiquark pair creation model, with in-medium masses obtained from a chiral SU(3) effective model that includes a scalar dilaton field simulating the gluon condensate. The coupled equations of motion for the scalar fields ($\sigma$, $\zeta$, $\delta$) and the dilaton field $\chi$ are solved self-consistently in the mean-field approximation for given baryon density, isospin asymmetry parameter, and magnetic field strength, including Landau quantization of proton energy levels and the anomalous magnetic moments of nucleons.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strengths considered: $eB = 4 m_\pi^2$ and $eB = 8 m_\pi^2$, where $m_\pi$ is the pion mass
- Charm quark mass taken as $m_c = 1.95$ GeV
- Coupling strength $\gamma = 0.33$ in the $^3P_0$ model, chosen to reproduce vacuum decay widths of $\psi(3770)$ to $D^+D^-$ and $D^0\bar{D^0}$
- Harmonic oscillator strength $\beta_D = 0.31$ GeV for daughter $D(\bar D)$ mesons
- $\beta$ values for charmonium states: 0.52 GeV ($J/\psi$, 1S), 0.38 GeV ($\psi(3686)$, 2S), 0.37 GeV ($\psi(3770)$, 1D), 0.44 GeV ($\chi_{c0}$, 1P), 0.41 GeV ($\chi_{c2}$, 1P)
- Vacuum masses: $\chi_{c0}$ = 3414.7 MeV, $\chi_{c2}$ = 3556.17 MeV
- In isospin-symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density $\rho_0$ with $eB=4 m_\pi^2$ including AMM: $D^+$ mass = 1813.6 MeV, $D^0$ mass = 1787 MeV; at $4\rho_0$: $D^+$ mass = 1557 MeV, $D^0$ mass = 1526.5 MeV
- For $\bar D$ mesons at $\rho_0$ with $eB=4 m_\pi^2$ including AMM: $D^-$ mass = 1864 MeV, $\bar{D^0}$ mass = 1837.8 MeV; at $4\rho_0$: $D^-$ mass = 1740.2 MeV, $\bar{D^0}$ mass = 1711.5 MeV
- Anomalous magnetic moment values: $\kappa_p = 3.5856$, $\kappa_n = -3.8263$
- Vacuum dilaton field value: $\chi_0 = 409.77$ MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of charmonium decay widths in hadronic matter using the $^3P_0$ model by Friman and collaborators, and the authors' own prior work on charmonium and open charm mesons in magnetized matter using the chiral effective model. The study incorporates for the first time the self-consistent medium modification of the dilaton field (rather than the frozen glueball approximation used in earlier work by the same group) and includes anomalous magnetic moment effects. The results are relevant for interpreting charmed meson production in noncentral heavy-ion collisions at RHIC (where $eB \sim 2 m_\pi^2$) and LHC (where $eB \sim 15 m_\pi^2$), and could help distinguish magnetic field effects from other medium modifications in asymmetric collision systems.
