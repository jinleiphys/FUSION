# Neutrino emissivity of $^{3}P_{2}$-$^{3}F_{2}$ superfluid cores in neutron stars

> The influence of the admixture of the $^{3}F_{2}$ state onto collective spin oscillations and neutrino emission processes in the triplet superfluid neutron liquid is studied in the BCS approximation. The eigen mode of spin oscillations with $ω\simeq\sqrt{58/35}Δ$ is predicted to exist in the triplet superfluid neutron condensate besides the already known mode $ω\simeqΔ/\sqrt{5}$. Excitation of the high-frequency spin oscillations in the condensate occurs through the tensor interactions between quasiparticles. Neutrino energy losses through neutral weak currents are found to consist of three separate contributions caused by a recombination of broken Cooper pairs and by weak decays of the collective modes of spin oscillations. Neutrino decays of the low-frequency spin waves can play an important role in the cooling scenario of neutron stars. Weak decays of the high-frequency oscillations that occur only if the tensor forces are taken into account in the pairing interactions does not modify substantially the total energy losses. Simple expressions are suggested for the total neutrino emissivity.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that, in a triplet superfluid neutron condensate with an admixture of the \(^{3}F_{2}\) state, a high-frequency collective spin oscillation mode exists at \(\omega \simeq \sqrt{58/35}\,\Delta\) in addition to the previously known low-frequency mode at \(\omega \simeq \Delta/\sqrt{5}\); however, the neutrino decays of this high-frequency mode do not substantially modify the total neutrino energy losses, and the low-frequency spin-wave decays can play an important role in neutron-star cooling only when tensor forces are included in the pairing interaction.

## Method

The author studies collective spin oscillations and neutrino emission in a \(^{3}P_{2}\)-\(^{3}F_{2}\) superfluid neutron liquid using the BCS approximation, with the pairing interaction including both spin-orbit and tensor forces. The ground state is taken as the unitary triplet with projection \(m_j = 0\), and the angle-average approximation is applied to the anisotropic energy gap. The dispersion of spin-density oscillations is derived by analyzing the poles of the anomalous three-point vertices in the linear response to an external axial-vector field, and the neutrino emissivity from pair-breaking and formation (PBF) processes and spin-wave decays (SWD) is calculated via neutral weak currents.

## Key numbers

* The low-frequency spin oscillation mode has energy \(\omega \simeq \Delta / \sqrt{5}\) in the triplet superfluid neutron condensate.
* The high-frequency spin oscillation mode has energy \(\omega \simeq \sqrt{58/35}\,\Delta\) (approximately \(1.287\,\Delta\)).
* The neutrino emissivity from PBF and SWD processes is evaluated for temperatures \(T \ll T_c\), where \(T_c\) is the critical condensation temperature; the low-frequency spin-wave decays can be important at the lowest temperatures when other mechanisms are suppressed by superfluidity.

## Context

This work extends the author’s previous studies of collective spin oscillations in a pure \(^{3}P_{2}\) superfluid neutron liquid (Leinson 2009, 2010a, 2010b), where only the low-frequency mode \(\omega = \Delta/\sqrt{5}\) was considered. By including the admixture of the \(^{3}F_{2}\) state via tensor forces, the paper predicts a new high-frequency mode and shows that the total neutrino emissivity consists of three contributions: recombination of broken Cooper pairs and decays of both low- and high-frequency spin waves. The results enable more accurate cooling models for neutron stars with triplet superfluid cores, particularly at very low temperatures where PBF processes are strongly quenched.
