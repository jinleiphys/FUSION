# Elliptic flow in intermediate energy heavy ion collisions and in-medium effects

> We investigate elliptic flow in heavy ion collisions at intermediate energies. In doing this, we implement and use a lattice-Hamiltonian model of the nuclear interaction and we also study the effect of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections that follow consistently from the momentum-dependence of the nuclear mean field.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a self-consistent, parameter-free calculation of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections, derived from the momentum-dependence of the nuclear mean field, improves agreement with experimental elliptic and directed flow data compared to using free-space cross sections, but only at lower bombarding energies (200 and 400 MeV/A) where the momentum-dependence near the Fermi surface is steepest.

## Method

The authors implemented a Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model using a lattice-Hamiltonian solution for the mean field dynamics, with a momentum-dependent single-particle potential parameterized for nuclear matter. They calculated the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section self-consistently from the effective mass derived from the mean field's momentum-dependence, using Fermi's Golden Rule and assuming the transition matrix element is unchanged from vacuum. The model was applied to simulate Bi+Bi and Au+Au collisions at laboratory bombarding energies from 25 to 1000 MeV/A, and the resulting directed and elliptic flow observables were compared to experimental data from the FOPI collaboration and other measurements.

## Key numbers

- Mean field parameters: A = -322.0 MeV, B = 352.5 MeV, sigma = 12/11, C = 62.75 MeV, Lambda = 1.58 p_f^0, giving u(p=0) = -72.4 MeV, u(p=p_f) = -51.4 MeV, u(p->infinity) = +30.5 MeV at saturation density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Effective mass at Fermi surface: m*/m = 0.67; nuclear matter compressibility: K = 210 MeV
- Isospin parameter D = 32 MeV (within empirical range 34 +/- 4 MeV)
- Lattice parameters: N_ens = 100, delta x = 1.00 fm
- Directed flow for Au+Au at 400 MeV/A: free space cross section over-predicts flow magnitude; in-medium cross section shows good agreement with data at all impact parameters
- Elliptic flow anisotropy ratio for Bi+Bi: self-consistent in-medium correction is significant only at 200 and 400 MeV/A, not at 700 and 1000 MeV/A
- Elliptic flow excitation function for Au+Au: in-medium cross section better reproduces measured ellipticity coefficient for fragments with A=1,2,3 over the energy range 150-800 MeV/A

## Context

This work extends prior BUU transport studies of collective flow in intermediate energy heavy ion collisions, particularly building on the momentum-dependent lattice Hamiltonian approach of Persram and Gale (1998) and Danielewicz et al. (1998). It addresses the long-standing question of in-medium modifications to nucleon-nucleon cross sections, which had previously been treated with constant scaling factors (Bertsch et al., 1987; Ogilvie et al., 1990) or simple density-dependent parameterizations (Westfall et al., 1993; Klakow et al., 1993). The self-consistent approach reveals that these simpler ansatze oversimplify the momentum and density dependence, and the results enable more precise tests of transport models against flow data, while identifying phase-space regions (high transverse momentum) where the BUU model performs less satisfactorily.
