# Separable Expansions of V_{low} for 2- and 3-Nucleon Systems

> We present an alternative organizational scheme for developing effective theories of 2- and 3-body systems that is systematic, accurate, and efficient with controlled errors. To illustrate our approach we consider the bound state and scattering properties of the 2- and 3-nucleon systems. Our approach combines the computational benefits of using separable potentials with the improved convergence properties of potentials evolved with a renormalization group procedure. Long ago Harms showed that any potential can be expanded in a series of separable terms, but this fact is only useful if the expansion can be truncated at low order. The separable expansion provides an attractive organizational scheme that incorporates the two body bound state in the leading term while allowing for systematic corrections thereafter. We show that when applied to a renormalization group-evolved potential, the separable expansion converges rapidly, with accurate results for both 2- and 3-body scattering processes using only two separable terms.

# Separable Expansions of V_{low} for 2- and 3-Nucleon Systems

## Key claim

The authors show that combining a Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) evolution of the bare nucleon-nucleon potential with a separable expansion (Harms expansion) yields rapid convergence for both 2- and 3-body scattering processes, requiring only two separable terms to achieve accurate results, whereas the separable expansion of the bare potential converges more slowly and suffers from non-physical negative imaginary phase shifts in 3-body applications.

## Method

The authors first evolve the bare Malfliet-Tjon (MT-III) potential in the ^3S_1 channel using the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) flow equation dH/ds = [[T,H],H] from a high-momentum cutoff of 16 fm^{-1} down to 2 fm^{-1}. They then apply the Harms separable expansion to both the bare potential V_0 and the SRG-evolved potential V_{low}, solving the eigenvalue problem V G_0(-B) |psi_n> = lambda_n |psi_n> to obtain form factors and eigenvalues. The resulting separable T-matrices are used to compute 2-body phase shifts and, via Faddeev equations, the 3-body nd quartet (^4S_{3/2}) scattering phase shifts.

## Key numbers

* Deuteron binding energy for MT-III potential: B = 2.23995 MeV, corresponding to gamma = 0.232405 fm^{-1}
* For the bare potential V_0, the Harms eigenvalues include lambda_1 = 1.0000 (bound state), lambda_2 = 0.159190, and a large negative eigenvalue lambda_3 = -1.8917
* For the SRG-evolved potential V_{low} (Lambda_{low} = 2 fm^{-1}), the Harms eigenvalues are lambda_1 = 1.0000, lambda_2 = 0.11937, and lambda_3 = 0.024633, with no large negative eigenvalues
* The rank-2 separable expansion of V_{low} reproduces the exact 2-body T-matrix out to about 400 MeV/c
* The effective range expansion parameters fitted from the exact MT-III phase shifts are: scattering length a = 5.505 fm, effective range r_0 = 1.891 fm, and shape parameter v_4 = 0.110 fm^3
* The phenomenological dipole form factor with beta = 1.281 fm^{-1} predicts r_0 = 1.897 fm and v_4 = 0.170 fm^3

## Context

This work extends the approach of Bogner, Furnstahl, and Perry (2006-2007) who showed how the SRG drives the matrix elements of the bare NN interaction to the diagonal and applied it to a 2-state model 3-body problem. It contrasts with the work of Kamada et al. (2005) who used a phenomenological separable expansion in Legendre polynomials for low-momentum effective potentials, requiring as many as 16 terms for convergence. The method enables systematic, controlled calculations of 3-body scattering that avoid the catastrophic failures of unitarity (negative imaginary phase shifts) seen in low-order effective range expansion treatments, and provides a computationally simpler alternative to full Faddeev calculations with realistic microscopic potentials such as those by Huber et al. (1993) and Kievsky et al. (2004).
