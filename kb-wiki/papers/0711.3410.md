# Self-consistent coupled-channel approach to $D$ and $\bar D$ in hot dense matter

> A self-consistent coupled-channel approach is used to study the properties of $D$ and $\bar D$ mesons in hot dense matter. The starting point is a broken SU(4) s-wave Tomozawa-Weinberg $DN$ ($\bar DN$) interaction supplemented by an attractive isoscalar-scalar term. The Pauli blocking effects, baryon mean-field bindings, and $π$ and open-charm meson self-energies are incorporated in dense matter at finite temperature. In the $DN$ sector, the dynamically generated $\tildeΛ_c$ and $\tildeΣ_c$ resonances remain close to their free space position while acquiring a remarkable width because of the thermal smearing of Pauli blocking. Therefore, the $D$ meson spectral density shows a single pronounced quasiparticle peak close to the free mass, that broadens with increasing density, and a low energy tail associated to smeared $\tildeΛ_c N^{-1}$, $\tildeΣ_c N^{-1}$ configurations. In the $\bar DN$ case, the low-density approximation to the repulsive $\bar D$ self-energy is found unreliable already at subsaturation densities. From this study we speculate the possible existence of $D$-mesic nuclei. We also discuss the consequences for $J/Ψ$ suppression at FAIR.

## Key claim

The dynamically generated $\tilde\Lambda_c$ and $\tilde\Sigma_c$ resonances in the $DN$ sector remain close to their free-space positions at finite temperature, but acquire a remarkable width due to thermal smearing of Pauli blocking; the low-density approximation to the repulsive $\bar D$ self-energy is found unreliable already at subsaturation densities.

## Method

The authors solve a self-consistent coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation for the $DN$ and $\bar DN$ $T$-matrix, using a broken SU(4) $s$-wave Tomozawa-Weinberg interaction supplemented by an attractive isoscalar-scalar term ($\Sigma_{DN}$). Medium modifications at finite temperature are incorporated by including Pauli blocking, baryon mean-field bindings from a temperature-dependent $\sigma$-$\omega$ model, and $\pi$ and open-charm meson self-energies in the intermediate propagators. The $D$ and $\bar D$ self-energies are obtained self-consistently by summing the in-medium $T$-amplitudes over the thermal nucleon Fermi distribution.

## Key numbers

- At $T=100$ MeV and saturation density $\rho_0=0.17\ {\rm fm^{-3}}$, the $\tilde\Lambda_c$ resonance lies at 2579 MeV and the $\tilde\Sigma_c$ at 2767 MeV for model A (with $\Sigma_{DN}$ term); for model B (without $\Sigma_{DN}$ term), $\tilde\Lambda_c$ is at 2602 MeV and $\tilde\Sigma_c$ at 2807 MeV.
- For model A (B) at $T=0$ and $\rho_0$, the $D$ meson feels an attractive optical potential of $-12$ ($-18$) MeV, while the $\bar D$ feels a repulsive potential of $11$ ($20$) MeV.
- The $\bar D N$ scattering lengths for model A are $a^{I=0}=0.61$ fm and $a^{I=1}=-0.26$ fm; for model B they are $a^{I=0}=0$ fm and $a^{I=1}=-0.29$ fm.
- The $D$ meson spectral function at $T=0$ and $\rho_0$ shows a low-energy $\tilde\Lambda_c N^{-1}$ excitation that moves up by about 50 MeV when mean-field binding of baryons is included.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature self-consistent coupled-channel study of Mizutani and Ramos (2006) to finite temperature, incorporating baryon mean-field bindings and pion self-energy dressing. It contrasts with earlier predictions by Tsushima et al. (1999) of possible $D$-mesic nuclei, finding instead that the repulsive $\bar D$-nucleus optical potential at zero momentum rules out strongly bound $\bar D^0$ nuclear systems, while the moderate attraction and large width of the $D$ meson optical potential rule out bound $D$ nuclear states. The results suggest that $J/\Psi$ suppression at FAIR is unlikely to come from $D$ or $\bar D$ mass modifications directly, but rather from absorption of excited charmonia ($\chi_{c\ell}(1P)$ or $\Psi'$) by multi-nucleon processes.
