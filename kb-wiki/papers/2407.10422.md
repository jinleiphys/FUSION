# A Semi-Empirical Formula for Two-Neutrino Double-Beta Decay

> We propose a semi-empirical formula (SEF) for calculating the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for two-neutrino double-beta decay. The SEF's dependence on the proton and neutron numbers, the pairing, isospin, and deformation properties of the initial and final nuclei is inspired by the insights offered by nuclear many-body methods and trends in experimental data. Compared with the previous phenomenological and nuclear models, the SEF yields the best agreement with the experimental NMEs. Its stability and predictive power are cross-validated, and predictions are provided for nuclear systems of experimental interest.

# A Semi-Empirical Formula for Two-Neutrino Double-Beta Decay

## Key claim

The proposed semi-empirical formula (SEF) achieves the best agreement with experimental nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for two-neutrino double-beta decay compared to previous phenomenological and nuclear models, but the fit is based on only ten direct counter experiments and radiochemical observations, and geochemical measurements are excluded from the fitting due to potential systematic uncertainties.

## Method

The authors constructed a semi-empirical formula for the two-neutrino double-beta decay NME that depends on the proton-to-neutron ratio of the final nucleus, the isospin of the final nucleus, the product of experimental proton and neutron pairing gaps of the initial and final nuclei, and the ratio of the smaller to larger quadrupole deformation parameters of the initial and final nuclei. The three free parameters (alpha, gamma, sigma) were determined by minimizing chi-squared against experimental NMEs derived from measured half-lives and precisely computed phase-space factors. Leave-one-out cross-validation was performed to assess stability and predictive power.

## Key numbers

- The best-fit parameters for the SEF are alpha = 46.94, gamma = 0.22, and sigma = 4.90, obtained from fitting N = 10 two-neutrino double-beta decay transitions.
- The SEF yields a reduced chi-squared of chi-squared_nu = 43 with 7 degrees of freedom, compared to values of 5651, 24981, and 17632 for the three previous phenomenological models by Ren (2014), Rajan (2018), and Pritychenko (2023), respectively.
- The SEF predicts a half-life of 1.5 x 10^24 years for the two-neutrino double-beta decay of 128Te, compared to the geochemical experimental value of 2.49 x 10^24 years.
- The SEF predicts half-lives of 3.1 x 10^20 years for 110Pd, 1.9 x 10^21 years for 124Sn, and 4.0 x 10^25 years for 134Xe.
- The experimental NME for 100Mo is 0.2019 +/- 0.0016, and the SEF predicts 0.199.
- The experimental NME for 136Xe is 0.0174 +/- 0.0002, and the SEF predicts 0.016.

## Context

This work extends the line of phenomenological models for two-neutrino double-beta decay NMEs previously proposed by Ren (2014), Rajan (2018), and Pritychenko (2023), which the authors show have significantly larger reduced chi-squared values. The SEF is inspired by insights from nuclear many-body methods including the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA), the nuclear shell model (NSM), the interacting boson model (IBM), and the projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) method. The formula enables predictions for nuclear systems of experimental interest, including 110Pd, 124Sn, and 134Xe, which are candidates for near-future measurements, and suggests that pairs of isotopes differing by two neutrons (such as 128,130Te and 134,136Xe) have NME ratios around 2, contradicting Pontecorvo's assumption (1968) that such pairs have nearly equal NMEs.
