# QCD from a Large N Perspective

> The 1/N expansion of QCD can be used to calculate properties of nucleons and Delta baryons, such as masses, magnetic moments, and pion couplings. The predictions of the 1/N expansion are in excellent agreement with the experimental data. The 1/N expansion also provides an understanding of the relation between the quark model, the Skyrme model, and QCD. This talk reviews some of the recent developments.

# QCD from a Large N Perspective

## Key claim

The 1/N_c expansion of QCD reveals a contracted SU(4) symmetry in the baryon sector that becomes exact in the N_c → ∞ limit, and this symmetry explains why many quark model and Skyrme model predictions for baryon properties (masses, magnetic moments, pion couplings) agree with experiment to order 1/N_c or 1/N_c^2, with the caveat that the expansion parameter is actually J/N_c and therefore only reliable for baryons with spin J held fixed as N_c → ∞.

## Method

The author derives consistency conditions for baryon axial couplings by requiring that the leading-order pion-baryon scattering amplitude from pole graphs (order N_c) must cancel to satisfy unitarity, yielding commutation relations [X^{ia}, X^{jb}] = 0 that define a contracted SU(4) algebra. Higher-order consistency conditions from multi-pion scattering and baryon mass splittings are then solved to determine the form of 1/N_c corrections, showing that the first correction to axial coupling ratios vanishes and that baryon mass splittings must be proportional to J^2/N_c.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant f_π is of order √N_c, and the axial coupling g_A is of order N_c.
- In the non-relativistic quark model, g_A = (N_c + 2)/3, giving 5/3 for N_c = 3.
- The 1/N_c^2 mass relation (not suppressed by SU(3) breaking) works to about 11% accuracy.
- The isospin-averaged baryon masses can be understood to a fractional error of 0.001 (∼1 MeV) using 1/N_c and SU(3) symmetry.
- The SU(3) breaking parameter ε is approximately 0.3.
- The Δ⁺ → p transition moment relation (order 1/N_c^2) works to 30%.
- The quantity Δu + Δd − 2Δs is shifted from its SU(3) symmetric value of 0.6 to 0.3 ± 0.1.

## Context

This review extends the large N_c QCD framework of 't Hooft (1974) and Witten (1979) to the baryon sector, building on consistency conditions first derived by Dashen and Manohar (1993) and Gervais and Sakita (1984). The work unifies the non-relativistic quark model, the Skyrme model, and the chiral bag model by showing they all share the same contracted SU(4) symmetry in the large N_c limit. The results enable systematic calculations of baryon properties directly from QCD without model assumptions, and the combination with baryon chiral perturbation theory (Jenkins, 1992; Jenkins and Lebed, 1995) resolves the sigma-term puzzle and explains why including the Δ baryon converts chiral perturbation theory from a strong to a weak coupling expansion.
