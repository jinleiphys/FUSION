# Hyperon polarization in kaon photoproduction from the deuteron

> We analyze the reaction gamma + d --> K^+ + Lambda (Sigma) + N using the Nijmegen soft core hyperon-nucleon interactions NSC97f and NSC89 and a recently updated production operator for the gamma + N --> K^+ + Lambda(Sigma) processes. Significant effects of the YN final state interaction are found near both the K^+ Lambda N and K^+ Sigma N thresholds.

# Hyperon polarization in kaon photoproduction from the deuteron

## Key claim
The hyperon-nucleon final state interaction produces significant and model-dependent effects on hyperon polarization observables in kaon photoproduction from the deuteron, particularly near the K⁺ΛN and K⁺ΣN thresholds, but only for the specific kinematic condition of kaon scattering at 0 degrees.

## Method
The authors solved a coupled set of integral equations for the reaction γ + d → K⁺ + Λ(Σ) + N after partial-wave decomposition in momentum space, using the Nijmegen soft-core hyperon-nucleon interactions NSC97f and NSC89 and a recently updated production operator for the elementary γ + N → K⁺ + Λ(Σ) processes. The deuteron state was generated using the Nijmegen 93 NN interaction, and the K⁺ meson interaction with the nucleon and hyperon in the final states was ignored.

## Key numbers
- Inclusive cross section increased up to 86% by final state interaction near the K⁺ΛN threshold at photon lab energy Eγ = 1.3 GeV and θK⁺ = 0°
- K⁺ΛN threshold located at pK = 977.30 MeV/c; K⁺ΣN threshold located at pK = 869.14 MeV/c
- Two pronounced peaks in inclusive cross section appear around pK = 945 MeV/c and pK = 809 MeV/c due to quasi-free Λ and Σ processes
- Exclusive calculations performed at pK⁺ = 870 MeV/c (just below K⁺ΣN threshold) and pK⁺ = 865 MeV/c (just above K⁺ΣN threshold) at Eγ = 1.3 GeV
- Λ recoil polarization in plane wave impulse approximation is almost one, but final state interactions cause large deviations with different predictions between NSC97f and NSC89

## Context
This work extends previous studies of hyperon-nucleon interactions using hyperon production processes, motivated by the difficulty of performing direct hyperon-nucleon scattering experiments. The authors had previously found that various meson-theoretical YN interactions generate S-matrix poles around the ΛN and ΣN thresholds (Miyagawa, year not specified), and the present study examines whether these pole structures produce visible effects in photoproduction observables. The results enable future comparison with inclusive d(e,e'K⁺)YN experiments already performed at TJNAF Hall C and d(γ,K⁺Y)N data being analyzed in Hall B, and suggest this reaction is a promising candidate for investigating the elementary production operator.
