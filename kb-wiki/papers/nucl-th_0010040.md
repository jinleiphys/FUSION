# Asymptotic Behavior of Nucleon Electromagnetic Form Factors in Space- and Time-Like Regions

> We compare the existing data about electromagnetic form factors of the proton in the time-like region of momentum transfer (up to $q^2\simeq$ 14 (GeV/c)$^2$), with the corresponding data in the space-like region. From the constrains given by the Phragmèn-Lindelöf theorem, it turns out that the asymptotic regime can not be reached simultaneously for both form factors (electric and magnetic) in the considered region of momentum transfer. The angular dependence for the annihilation processes, such as $\bar{p}+p\leftrightarrow e^++e^-$, is sensitive to the asymptotic properties of form factors and to the two-photon physics in the time-like region.

## Key claim

The asymptotic regime for proton electromagnetic form factors cannot be reached simultaneously for both electric and magnetic form factors in the momentum transfer region up to approximately 14 (GeV/c)^2; at least one form factor remains far from asymptotic behavior even if the other may have reached it.

## Method

The authors compare existing experimental data for proton electromagnetic form factors in the time-like region (from the annihilation process p-bar + p -> e+ + e-) with data in the space-like region (from elastic e-p scattering). They apply constraints from the Phragmèn-Lindelöf theorem, which requires that space-like and time-like form factors become equal at asymptotically high momentum transfer, and reanalyze the time-like data under four different assumptions about the relative behavior of the electric and magnetic form factors.

## Key numbers

- Time-like data extend up to s ≈ 14 (GeV/c)^2 for the process p-bar + p -> e+ + e-
- Space-like magnetic form factor data follow a dipole behavior G_M/μ_p = 1/[1 + q^2/(0.71 (GeV/c)^2)]^2 up to q^2 ≈ 31 (GeV/c)^2
- Independent determination of both G_M and G_E from unpolarized e-p scattering has been done up to q^2 = 8.7 (GeV/c)^2
- New polarization data at q^2 = 3.5 (GeV/c)^2 show G_E deviating from G_M/μ_p by up to 50%
- The time-like magnetic form factor can be parametrized with a deviation parameter m_nd^2 = 3.6 ± 0.9 (GeV/c)^2 from the usual dipole dependence
- Extrapolation indicates the Phragmèn-Lindelöf theorem will be satisfied by the magnetic form factor only for s(q^2) ≥ 20 (GeV/c)^2
- The mass parameter for the electric proton form factor is m^2 ≈ 5.3 (GeV/c)^2 and for the magnetic N→Δ transition is m^2 ≈ 6.1 (GeV/c)^2

## Context

This work extends the discussion of where asymptotic behavior for nucleon form factors is reached, following the quark counting rules of Brodsky and Farrar (1973) and Matveev et al. (1973), and the helicity conservation hypothesis of Lepage and Brodsky (1980). The analysis contradicts earlier claims by Bistrovic et al. (1993) that the Phragmèn-Lindelöf theorem is inapplicable, instead showing that the asymptotic regime is simply not yet reached in the measured region. The results enable future measurements of the angular asymmetry in p-bar + p ↔ e+ + e- to separate electric and magnetic form factors and test for two-photon exchange contributions, as well as motivate extending measurements to higher momentum transfer.
