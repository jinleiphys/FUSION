# Baryon Number Fluctuations in Quasi-particle Model

> Baryon number fluctuations are sensitive to the QCD phase transition and QCD critical point. According to the Feynman rules of finite-temperature field theory, we calculated various order moments and cumulants of the baryon number distributions in the quasi-particle model of quark gluon plasma. Furthermore, we compared our results with the experimental data measured by the STAR experiment at RHIC. It is found that the experimental data can be well described by the model for the colliding energies above 30 GeV and show large discrepancies at low energies. It can put new constraint on qQGP model and also provide a baseline for the QCD critical point search in heavy-ion collisions at low energies.

## Key claim

The quasi-particle model of quark-gluon plasma (qQGP) can describe the experimental data for baryon number fluctuation observables \(S\sigma\) and \(\kappa\sigma^2\) for colliding energies above 30 GeV, but shows large discrepancies at lower energies, suggesting that low-energy data may contain physics (such as the QCD critical point) not included in the model.

## Method

The authors calculated the first four moments of the baryon number distribution (mean, variance, skewness, and kurtosis) using finite-temperature field theory and Feynman rules applied to a quasi-particle model of QGP. In this model, quarks and gluons are treated as non-interacting quasi-particles with temperature- and chemical-potential-dependent thermal masses, and the moments are obtained directly from the expectation values of powers of the quark number operator without taking derivatives of the partition function, thereby avoiding thermodynamic inconsistency. The results were compared to net-proton multiplicity distribution data from the STAR experiment at RHIC for Au+Au collisions at various center-of-mass energies.

## Key numbers

- The model reproduces experimental data for \(S\sigma\) and \(\kappa\sigma^2\) for colliding energies **above 30 GeV**; for \(S\sigma\) the model result is approximately **0.1** less than the experimental data at the maximum deviation in this region.
- For \(\kappa\sigma^2\), the model describes the experimental data well for energies above 30 GeV.
- The calculations use a quark rest mass \(m_{q0} = 6.5\) MeV for up and down quarks, with \(\mathcal{N}_f = 2\) flavors.
- The critical temperature used is \(T_c = 175\) MeV.
- The parameter \(\Lambda_T/T_c\) is varied around 0.7 (specifically 0.85, 0.7, 0.6 for one parameter set and 0.7, 0.63, 0.58 or 0.57 for another), and the parameter \(a\) takes two values: \(a = (1.91/2.91)^2\) and \(a = (1/\pi)^2\).

## Context

This work extends the quasi-particle model of QGP, originally proposed by Peshier et al. to study the non-ideal equation of state from lattice QCD, to the calculation of higher-order baryon number susceptibilities and fluctuation observables. It builds on prior studies of second-order quark-number susceptibilities in the qQGP model (e.g., at finite chemical potential and temperature) and addresses the lack of higher-order susceptibility calculations. The results provide a baseline for QCD critical point searches in low-energy heavy-ion collisions, complementing experimental observations from the STAR collaboration that show non-monotonic behavior in \(\kappa\sigma^2\) of net-proton distributions, which is consistent with proximity to a critical point.
