# Probing the QCD Critical Point with Higher Moments of Net-proton Multiplicity Distributions

> Higher moments of event-by-event net-proton multiplicity distributions are applied to search for the QCD critical point in the heavy ion collisions. It has been demonstrated that higher moments as well as moment products are sensitive to the correlation length and directly connected to the thermodynamic susceptibilities computed in the Lattice QCD and Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model. In this paper, we will present measurements for kurtosis ($κ$), skewness ($S$) and variance ($σ^{2}$) of net-proton multiplicity distributions at the mid-rapidity ($|y|<0.5$) and $0.4<p_{T}<0.8$ GeV/$c$ for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=19.6, 39, 62.4, 130 and 200 GeV, Cu+Cu collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=22.4, 62.4 and 200 GeV, d+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 GeV and p+p collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=62.4 and 200 GeV. The moment products $κσ^{2}$ and $S σ$ of net-proton distributions, which are related to volume independent baryon number susceptibility ratio, are compared to the Lattice QCD and HRG model calculations. The $κσ^{2}$ and $S σ$ of net-proton distributions are consistent with Lattice QCD and HRG model calculations at high energy, which support the thermalization of the colliding system. Deviations of $κσ^{2}$ and $S σ$ for the Au+Au collisions at low energies from HRG model calculations are also observed.

## Key claim

Higher moments of net-proton multiplicity distributions are sensitive to the correlation length and can probe the QCD critical point, but the observed deviations from Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model calculations at low beam energies (particularly at √sNN = 39 GeV and below) are not yet conclusive due to limited statistics and could alternatively arise from non-applicability of the grand canonical ensemble or chiral phase transitions rather than the critical point itself.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration measured event-by-event net-proton multiplicity distributions (proton minus antiproton) at mid-rapidity (|y| < 0.5) and transverse momentum 0.4 < pT < 0.8 GeV/c using the Time Projection Chamber (TPC) detector. They calculated the mean (M), variance (σ²), skewness (S), and kurtosis (κ) for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 19.6, 39, 62.4, 130, and 200 GeV; Cu+Cu collisions at 22.4, 62.4, and 200 GeV; d+Au collisions at 200 GeV; and p+p collisions at 62.4 and 200 GeV. The moment products κσ² and Sσ, which are volume-independent and directly related to baryon number susceptibility ratios (χB⁽⁴⁾/χB⁽²⁾ and χB⁽³⁾/χB⁽²⁾), were compared to Lattice QCD and HRG model calculations.

## Key numbers

* For Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200, 130, and 62.4 GeV, the moment products κσ² and Sσ are consistent with Lattice QCD and HRG model calculations (HRG predicts κσ² = 1 and Sσ = tanh(μB/T)).
* For Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 39 GeV, Sσ and κσ² deviate from HRG model calculations (smaller than predicted).
* Lattice QCD calculations (with Nτ = 6 and Tc = 175 MeV) show a negative value for κσ² at √sNN = 19.6 GeV.
* The Sσ of net-proton distributions for various systems (p+p, d+Au, Cu+Cu, Au+Au) at fixed energy follows a power-law dependence on charged particle density dNch/dη, with fitted parameters α = 3.669 ± 0.1358, β = 0.0853 ± 0.003927, γ = -0.6796 ± 0.00693 (χ²/ndf = 34.08/37).
* The typical correlation length near the QCD critical point in heavy ion collisions is estimated to be about 2–3 fm.

## Context

This work extends the search for the QCD critical point using higher moments of conserved quantities, as proposed theoretically by Stephanov, Rajagopal, and Shuryak, and builds on earlier STAR measurements (PRL 2010, SQM 2009). The results are compared to Lattice QCD calculations (Cheng et al. 2009) and the Hadron Resonance Gas model. The observed deviations at low energies motivate further data collection at √sNN = 19.6 GeV (planned for 2011) and enable future comparisons with model calculations predicting negative kurtosis when approaching the critical point from the crossover side (as in linear σ model calculations).
