# Fully integrated transport approach to heavy ion reactions with an intermediate hydrodynamic stage

> We present a coupled Boltzmann and hydrodynamics approach to relativistic heavy ion reactions. This hybrid approach is based on the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport approach with an intermediate hydrodynamical evolution for the hot and dense stage of the collision. Event-by-event fluctuations are directly taken into account via the non-equilibrium initial conditions generated by the initial collisions and string fragmentations in the microscopic UrQMD model. After a (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic evolution, the hydrodynamical fields are mapped to hadrons via the Cooper-Frye equation and the subsequent hadronic cascade calculation within UrQMD proceeds to incorporate the important final state effects for a realistic freeze-out. This implementation allows to compare pure microscopic transport calculations with hydrodynamic calculations using exactly the same initial conditions and freeze-out procedure. The effects of the change in the underlying dynamics - ideal fluid dynamics vs. non-equilibrium transport theory - will be explored. The freeze-out and initial state parameter dependences are investigated for different observables. Furthermore, the time evolution of the baryon density and particle yields are discussed. We find that the final pion and proton multiplicities are lower in the hybrid model calculation due to the isentropic hydrodynamic expansion while the yields for strange particles are enhanced due to the local equilibrium in the hydrodynamic evolution. The results of the different calculations for the mean transverse mass excitation function, rapidity and transverse mass spectra for different particle species at three different beam energies are discussed in the context of the available data.

## Key claim

The paper presents a hybrid transport+hydrodynamics approach that embeds a (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic stage within the UrQMD transport model, allowing direct comparison of pure microscopic transport and hydrodynamic calculations using identical initial conditions and freeze-out procedures. The authors find that final pion and proton multiplicities are lower in the hybrid model due to isentropic hydrodynamic expansion, while strange particle yields are enhanced due to local equilibrium in the hydrodynamic evolution.

## Method

The authors couple the UrQMD transport model to a (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic evolution for the hot and dense stage of heavy ion collisions. Initial conditions are generated event-by-event from UrQMD's non-equilibrium initial collisions and string fragmentations, with hadrons represented as Gaussian distributions on a spatial grid. After the hydrodynamic evolution using the SHASTA algorithm with a hadron gas equation of state, the hydrodynamic fields are mapped to hadrons via the Cooper-Frye equation, and subsequent hadronic cascade within UrQMD proceeds for final state effects and freeze-out.

## Key numbers

- Initial transition time for hydrodynamic evolution is calculated as \(t_{start}=2R\sqrt{2m_N/E_{\rm lab}}\), with a minimum of 1 fm/c; for Pb+Pb at \(E_{\rm lab}=40A\) GeV, \(t_{start}=2.83\) fm
- Maximum initial energy density reaches 6 GeV/fm\(^3\) and baryon number density around 8 times nuclear ground state density for central Pb+Pb at \(E_{\rm lab}=40A\) GeV
- Hydrodynamic grid uses cell size \((0.2\ {\rm fm})^3\) with 200 cells in each direction and timesteps of \(dt=0.08\) fm
- Gaussian width for smearing particles to hydrodynamic fields is \(\sigma=1\) fm
- Hydrodynamic evolution stops when energy density drops below five times nuclear ground state energy density (\(\sim 730\) MeV/fm\(^3\))
- Calculations cover beam energies from \(E_{\rm lab}=2-160A\) GeV

## Context

This work extends previous hybrid approaches such as NEXSpheRIO (Paiva et al., Aguiar et al.) and the QGSM-based hybrid by Toneev et al., as well as the pioneering UrQMD-hydrodynamics coupling studies by Dumitru, Bass, Soff, Nonaka, and Steinheimer. Unlike earlier micro+macro approaches that rely on complete separation of initial conditions, hydrodynamic evolution, and transport calculation, this implementation allows direct comparison between pure transport and hydrodynamic calculations with identical initial conditions and freeze-out. The purely hadronic calculations provide a baseline for future studies employing different equations of state, including those with a phase transition to quark-gluon plasma.
