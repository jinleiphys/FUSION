# Crossed channel analysis of quark and gluon generalized parton distributions with helicity flip

> Quark and gluon helicity flip generalized parton distributions (GPDs) address the transversity quark and gluon structure of the nucleon. In order to construct a theoretically consistent parametrization of these hadronic matrix elements, we work out the set of combinations of those GPDs suitable for the ${\rm SO}(3)$ partial wave (PW) expansion in the cross-channel. This universal result will help to build up a flexible parametrization of these important hadronic non-perturbative quantities, using for instance the approaches based on the conformal PW expansion of GPDs such as the Mellin-Barnes integral or the dual parametrization techniques.

## Key claim

The paper identifies the specific combinations of quark and gluon helicity flip generalized parton distributions (GPDs) that are suitable for a partial wave expansion in the cross-channel SO(3) basis, providing a theoretically consistent parametrization framework for these hadronic matrix elements, but the analysis is performed under the approximation of neglecting threshold corrections (setting β=1) and requires further study to handle kinematical singularities in the direct channel.

## Method

The authors analytically continue the N-th Mellin moments of quark and gluon helicity flip GPDs to the cross-channel (t-channel of DVCS), then compute the corresponding matrix elements in the nucleon-antinucleon center-of-mass frame for definite helicity configurations. By projecting out operator helicity combinations and comparing with the Wigner d-functions governing the polar angle dependence, they determine which combinations of GPDs expand in Legendre polynomials P'_J(cosθ) and their derivatives. The method follows the approach of Sec. 4.2 of Diehl (2003) and provides an independent cross-check of the J^PC selection rules established by Hagler (2004) and Chen and Ji (2004) using the method of Ji and Lebed (2000).

## Key numbers

- For the aligned helicity configuration (λ'=λ) of nucleon and antinucleon, the combinations τ \tilde{H}_T^q - (1/2) E_T^q and -H_T^q + τ \tilde{H}_T^q - (1/2) E_T^q are to be expanded in P'_J(1/ξ), where τ ≡ Δ²/(4m²)
- For the opposite helicity configuration (|λ'-λ|=1), the combinations H_T^q + τ \tilde{H}_T^q ± τ \tilde{E}_T^q are to be expanded in P'_J(1/ξ) + (1 ∓ ξ)/ξ P''_J(1/ξ)
- For even Mellin moments (N=0,2,...) of the non-singlet (C=-1) quark helicity flip combinations, only odd J values are consistent with T-invariance, with the highest possible J = N+1
- For N=0, the allowed exchanges are J^PC = 1^{--} and J^PC = 1^{+-}; for N=2, the allowed exchanges are J^PC = 1^{--}, 3^{--} and J^PC = 1^{+-}, 3^{+-}

## Context

This work extends the SO(3) partial wave analysis previously applied to unpolarized and polarized GPDs (Diehl, 2003; Polyakov, 2008; Semenov-Tian-Shansky, 2010) to the helicity flip sector, which had not been treated in the literature. The results enable construction of flexible parametrizations for quark and gluon helicity flip GPDs using either the Mellin-Barnes transform technique (Kumericki, 2007) or the dual parametrization approach (Polyakov, 2002, 2008), both of which rely on double partial wave expansions in conformal and SO(3) partial waves. The analysis also provides the first explicit treatment of the equivalent parametrization of gluon helicity flip GPDs using the dual gluon field strength operator, which was previously overlooked in the literature.
