# Tetraquark mixing model is superior to meson molecules in explaining two light-meson nonets

> In this work, we compare the tetraquark mixing model and meson molecules in describing the two physical nonets in the $J^P=0^+$ channel, the light nonet [$a_0 (980)$, $K_0^* (700)$, $f_0 (500)$, $f_0 (980)$] and the heavy nonet [$a_0 (1450)$, $K_0^* (1430)$, $f_0 (1370)$, $f_0 (1500)$]. In particular, we focus on whether successful aspects of the tetraquark mixing model that apply to all members of each nonet can be reproduced from a model of meson molecules. By combining two mesons in the lowest-lying pseudoscalar nonet, we construct SU$_f$(3) molecular nonets that can be tested for the two physical nonets. This molecular approach can make two flavor nonets just as the tetraquark mixing model but this model has some difficulties in describing the universal features of the two nonets %Because of this, this molecular model cannot reproduce successful aspects of the tetraquark mixing model, such as mass splitting between the two nonets, and enhancement or suppression of the coupling strengths of the two nonets into two pseudoscalar mesons. We also compare the fall-apart modes of the tetraquark mixing model and the two-meson modes from the molecular model. A clear distinction can be seen by the two-pion modes in the isovector resonances. The two-pion modes appear in the molecular model, but not in the tetraquark mixing model. The absence of the two-pion modes is supported by the experimental decay modes of the isovector resonances.

## Key claim

The tetraquark mixing model, which describes the two scalar meson nonets as mixtures of two tetraquark types, is superior to a meson molecular model constructed from two pseudoscalar mesons because the molecular model cannot reproduce the universal features of the two nonets, such as the mass splitting between them and the enhancement or suppression of their coupling strengths into two pseudoscalar mesons.

## Method

The authors constructed SU$_f$(3) molecular nonets by combining two mesons from the lowest-lying pseudoscalar nonet (π, K, η, η′) using a tensor method, generating two-meson multiplets from the product (1_f ⊕ 8_f) ⊗ (1_f ⊕ 8_f). They then compared the resulting two-meson decay modes and coupling patterns with those predicted by the tetraquark mixing model, where the two physical nonets arise from mixing two tetraquark types (|000⟩ and |011⟩) that differ in color and spin configurations. The comparison focused on whether the molecular model could reproduce the universal enhancement of couplings in the light nonet and suppression in the heavy nonet that arises from the relative sign difference in the tetraquark mixing.

## Key numbers

- The tetraquark mixing parameters are α ≈ √(2/3) and β ≈ 1/√3, fixed by diagonalization of the color-spin interaction.
- The overall coupling factor for the light nonet is (β/√12 + α/√2) ≈ 0.744, while for the heavy nonet it is (−α/√12 + β/√2) ≈ 0.173, showing universal enhancement in the light nonet and suppression in the heavy nonet.
- The tetraquark mixing model gives a hyperfine mass of approximately ⟨V_CS⟩ ≈ −500 MeV for the light nonet, explaining masses below 1 GeV, and approximately ⟨V_CS⟩ ≈ −20 MeV for the heavy nonet, explaining masses near 4m_q.
- The molecular model from 8_f ⊗ 8_f produces only one nonet (1 ⊕ 8) rather than two, and the two-meson modes in the molecular model (e.g., π⁰π⁺ − π⁺π⁰ for isovector states) differ from those in the tetraquark mixing model.

## Context

This work extends the tetraquark mixing model developed by Kim and collaborators (2016-2022) for the two scalar meson nonets and tests whether meson molecular models, previously applied to individual members like a₀(980) and f₀(980) as K̅K molecules by Weinstein (1990) or Branz (2007-2008), can describe the full nonet structure. The authors show that the molecular model cannot produce two distinct nonets with the observed mass splitting and coupling patterns, and that the two-pion decay modes predicted for isovector resonances in the molecular model are absent in the tetraquark mixing model, a distinction supported by experimental decay data. This comparison helps determine whether the two nonets in the Particle Data Group are tetraquarks or hadronic molecules, and enables future experimental tests of the two-pion decay modes to distinguish between the models.
