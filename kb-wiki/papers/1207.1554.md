# EoS for massive neutron stars

> Using relativistic Hartree-Fock approximation, we investigate the properties of the neutron-star matter in detail. In the present calculation, we consider not only the tensor coupling of vector mesons to octet baryons and the form factors at interaction vertexes but also the internal (quark) structure change of baryons in dense matter. The relativistic Hartree-Fock calculations are performed in two ways: one is the calculation with the coupling constants determined by SU(6) (quark model) symmetry, the other is with the coupling constants based on SU(3) (flavor) symmetry. For the latter case, we use the latest Nijmegen (ESC08) model. Then, it is very remarkable that the particle composition of the core matter in SU(3) symmetry is completely different from that in SU(6) symmetry. In SU(6) symmetry, all octet baryons appear in the density region below $\sim 1.2$ fm$^{-3}$, while, in the ESC08 model, only the Ξ^- hyperon is produced. Furthermore, the medium modification of the internal baryon structure hardens the equation of state for the core matter. Taking all these effects into account, we can obtain the maximum neutron-star mass which is consistent with the recently observed mass, 1.97 \pm 0.04 M_\sun (PSR J1614-2230). We therefore conclude that the extension from SU(6) symmetry to SU(3) symmetry in the meson-baryon couplings and the internal baryon-structure variation in matter certainly enhance the mass of neutron star. Furthermore, the effects of the form factor at vertex and the Fock contribution including the tensor coupling due to the vector mesons are indispensable to describe the core matter. In particular, the Fock term is very vital in reproducing the preferable value of symmetry energy, a_4 (\simeq 30 - 40 MeV), in nuclear matter.

## Key claim

Using relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approximation that includes tensor coupling of vector mesons, form factors at interaction vertices, and internal baryon structure change, the maximum neutron-star mass can be made consistent with the observed mass of PSR J1614-2230 (1.97 ± 0.04 M☉), but only when the meson-baryon coupling constants are extended from SU(6) (quark model) symmetry to SU(3) (flavor) symmetry using the Nijmegen ESC08 model.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Hartree-Fock calculations for neutron-star matter including octet baryons (p, n, Λ, Σ⁺⁰⁻, Ξ⁰⁻) and leptons (e⁻, μ⁻), using three different frameworks: Quantum Hadrodynamics with nonlinear σ terms (QHD+NL), the Quark-Meson Coupling (QMC) model, and the Chiral Quark-Meson Coupling (CQMC) model. The calculations incorporated tensor couplings of ω and ρ mesons to baryons, dipole form factors at interaction vertices (with cutoff Λ_B² = 0.71 GeV²), and the internal quark structure change of baryons in dense matter via the scalar-density ratio C_B(σ̄). Two symmetry schemes for meson-baryon coupling constants were compared: SU(6) (quark model) symmetry and SU(3) (flavor) symmetry based on the Nijmegen ESC08 model.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron-star mass consistent with PSR J1614-2230: 1.97 ± 0.04 M☉
- Normal nuclear matter density: n₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³
- In SU(6) symmetry, all octet baryons appear below density ∼1.2 fm⁻³
- Symmetry energy a₄ ≈ 30–40 MeV in nuclear matter
- Tensor coupling ratios: κ_ωN = f_ωN/g_ωN = −0.12, κ_ρN = f_ρN/g_ρN = 3.7
- Pion-nucleon coupling: f_πN²/4π = 0.08
- Cutoff parameter for form factors: Λ_B² = 0.71 GeV²
- Hyperon potential depths at n₀: U_Λ ≈ −30 MeV, U_Σ ≈ +30 MeV, U_Ξ ≈ −15 MeV
- Free baryon masses: M_N = 939 MeV, M_Λ = 1116 MeV, M_Σ = 1193 MeV, M_Ξ = 1313 MeV
- Meson masses: m_σ = 550 MeV, m_ω = 783 MeV, m_π = 138 MeV, m_ρ = 770 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic mean-field (RMF) and relativistic Hartree (RH) calculations by Todd-Rutel and Tews (2004), Schramm (2003), Shen (2002), Bednarek (2004), and Weissenborn (2012) that included nonlinear meson self-interactions to push hyperon thresholds to higher densities. The authors' earlier work (Miyatsu 2012) briefly reported effects of Fock terms, tensor coupling, and baryon structure variation using QHD, QMC, and CQMC models. The present paper fully investigates these effects in RHF approximation and extends the analysis to SU(3) symmetry using the Nijmegen ESC08 model (Rijken, Stoks, Yamamoto 2010). The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron-star cores that can accommodate the observed 2 M☉ neutron stars while including hyperonic degrees of freedom.
