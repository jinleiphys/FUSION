# Low-energy collective excitations in the neutron star inner crust

> We study the low-energy collective excitations of the neutron star inner crust, where a neutron superfluid coexists with a Coulomb lattice of nuclei. The dispersion relation of the modes is calculated systematically from a microscopic theory including neutron band structure effects. These effects are shown to lead to a strong mixing between the Bogoliubov-Anderson bosons of the neutron superfluid and the longitudinal crystal lattice phonons. In addition, the speed of the transverse shear mode is greatly reduced as a large fraction of superfluid neutrons are entrained by nuclei. Not only does the much smaller velocity of the transverse mode increase the specific heat of the inner crust, but it also decreases its electron thermal conductivity. These results may impact our interpretation of the thermal relaxation in accreting neutron stars. Due to strong mixing, the mean free path of the superfluid mode is found to be greatly reduced. Our results for the collective mode dispersion relations and their damping may also have implications for neutron star seismology.

## Key claim
The paper shows that neutron band-structure effects in the neutron star inner crust cause strong mixing between Bogoliubov-Anderson bosons of the neutron superfluid and longitudinal crystal lattice phonons, and that entrainment of superfluid neutrons by nuclei greatly reduces the speed of transverse shear modes. These effects depend on the specific microscopic composition of the crust and are calculated using a consistent microscopic theory rather than the hydrodynamic approximations of prior work.

## Method
The authors solve linearized hydrodynamic equations for small perturbations of densities and currents in the inner crust, incorporating neutron band-structure effects from a microscopic model. The crust composition is taken from ETFSI (extended Thomas-Fermi with Strutinsky-Integral) calculations using the Skyrme force BSk14, and neutron band-structure calculations determine the superfluid density and entrainment. The resulting dispersion relations for collective modes are obtained in the long-wavelength limit, and damping is estimated by coupling lattice phonon dissipation to the superfluid mode through the mixing parameter.

## Key numbers
- Transverse shear mode speed \(v_t\) is reduced to \(0.24 \times 10^{-2}c\) at density \(n = 0.02\) fm\(^{-3}\) (with entrainment), compared to \(0.66 \times 10^{-2}c\) without entrainment
- Mixing parameter \(g_{\rm mix}\) ranges from \(2.11 \times 10^{-2}c\) at \(n = 0.0003\) fm\(^{-3}\) to \(6.34 \times 10^{-2}c\) at \(n = 0.08\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- The low-velocity longitudinal eigenmode speed \(v_-\) varies from \(1.02 \times 10^{-2}c\) at \(n = 0.0003\) fm\(^{-3}\) to \(3.84 \times 10^{-2}c\) at \(n = 0.08\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- The high-velocity longitudinal eigenmode speed \(v_+\) varies from \(5.56 \times 10^{-2}c\) at \(n = 0.0003\) fm\(^{-3}\) to \(11.4 \times 10^{-2}c\) at \(n = 0.08\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- The ratio of mean free path of the low-velocity mode to that of the unmixed longitudinal lattice phonon \(\lambda_-/\lambda_\ell\) is 32.3 at \(n = 0.0003\) fm\(^{-3}\) and decreases to 1.37 at \(n = 0.08\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- The ion-sphere radius is approximately \(r_{\rm I} \approx 75 ( (A_{\rm cell}/1000) / \rho_{12} )^{1/3}\) fm
- The Debye wave number is \(q_D \approx 0.03 ( \rho_{12} / (A_{\rm cell}/1000) )^{1/3}\) fm\(^{-1}\)

## Context
This work extends the hydrodynamic approach of Epstein (1988) by including long-range perturbations on the superfluid flow induced by the crystal lattice, which were previously neglected. It builds on the entrainment calculations of Chamel (2005, 2012) and the theoretical framework for coupled superfluid-solid dynamics developed by Andersson (2009), Pethick (2010), and Cirigliano (2011). The results enable more accurate modeling of thermal relaxation in accreting neutron stars observed in soft x-ray transients such as KS 1731-260, MXB 1659-29, XTE J1701-462, and EXO 0748-676, and may have implications for neutron star seismology and the interpretation of quasiperiodic oscillations in soft-gamma ray repeaters.
