# Integral Equation Results for the 4He(e,e'p)3H Reaction at High Missing Momenta

> The two-fragment electrodisintegration of 4He into proton and triton is calculated in Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA). The three- and four-nucleon wave functions involved are obtained by solving the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) integral equations, with the Malfliet-Tjon potential as the underlying NN-interaction. Our results are in remarkable agreement with the experimental data and, in contrast to alternative approaches, do not exhibit any dip in the five-fold differential cross section at a missing momentum of ~ 450 MeV/c.

## Key claim

The authors show that a Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) calculation using wave functions from the exact Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) integral equations yields remarkable agreement with experimental data for the 4He(e,e'p)3H reaction, and crucially does not exhibit the characteristic dip in the five-fold differential cross section at a missing momentum of approximately 450 MeV/c that appears in other PWIA approaches.

## Method

The authors solve the exact three- and four-nucleon AGS integral equations, using the W-matrix method and energy-dependent pole approximation (EDPA), with the Malfliet-Tjon potential as the underlying NN interaction. The resulting bound state wave functions for 3H and 4He are then used in a PWIA calculation of the five-fold differential cross section for the two-fragment electrodisintegration process 4He(e,e'p)3H, employing the McVoy-van Hove electromagnetic Hamiltonian with dominant electric operators.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of 3H: 8.595 MeV, obtained from the AGS equations with the Malfliet-Tjon potential
- Binding energy of 4He: 30.1 MeV, obtained from the AGS equations with the Malfliet-Tjon potential
- The characteristic dip in the PWIA cross section appears at a missing momentum of approximately 450 MeV/c in other approaches, but is absent in the AGS-based calculation
- At missing momenta less than 300 MeV/c, all calculations show good agreement with experimental data
- In the region 300 MeV/c less than Q less than 600 MeV/c, the Laget results underestimate the cross section by a factor of 4 and the Schiavilla results by a factor of 2
- The calculations are performed for the kinematics with omega = 215 MeV from the NIKHEF data and for the Saclay kinematics

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the two-fragment photodisintegration of the alpha particle by Ellerkmann, Sandhas, Sofianos, and Fiedeldey (1996) to the electrodisintegration case. It directly contradicts the results of Laget (1994), Schiavilla (1990), and Howell (1997), all of whom found a dip in the PWIA cross section at approximately 450 MeV/c that disagrees with experimental data. The authors attribute their success to the use of AGS wave functions that fully incorporate the coupling between (2+2)-channels and (3+1)-channels in the four-body system, a feature not fully included in other approaches. This work enables future inclusion of final state interactions in a rigorous way within the same AGS framework, as well as investigation of sensitivity to the input NN potential.
