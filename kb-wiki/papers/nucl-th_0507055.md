# The Strange Star Surface: A Crust with Nuggets

> We reexamine the surface composition of strange stars. Strange quark stars are hypothetical compact stars which could exist if strange quark matter was absolutely stable. It is widely accepted that they are characterized by an enormous density gradient ($~10^{26}$ g/cm$^4$) and large electric fields at surface. By investigating the possibility of realizing a heterogeneous crust, comprised of nuggets of strange quark matter embedded in an uniform electron background, we find that the strange star surface has a much reduced density gradient and negligible electric field. We comment on how our findings will impact various proposed observable signatures for strange stars.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the surface of a strange star is likely a heterogeneous crust of strange quark matter nuggets embedded in an electron gas, rather than a bare quark surface with a steep density gradient and large electric fields, but this conclusion depends on the surface tension between strange quark matter and vacuum being sufficiently small (approximately less than 36 MeV/fm² for typical parameters).

## Method

The authors perform a model-independent thermodynamic analysis of the quark-electron system near zero pressure, expanding the quark pressure to second order in the electron chemical potential to identify conditions where a mixed phase (quark nuggets plus electrons) is energetically favored over homogeneous quark matter. They then use a bag model with parameters B=65 MeV/fm³ and strange quark mass m_s=150 MeV to compute the equation of state of the mixed phase, and solve the general relativistic equations for hydrostatic equilibrium to determine the crust thickness.

## Key numbers

- The critical electron chemical potential at the transition to the mixed phase is μ_e^c ≃ 18 MeV, corresponding to a critical pressure P_c ≃ 1.2 × 10⁻⁴ MeV/fm³.
- The energy density inside the quark nuggets is ε₀ ≃ 283 MeV/fm³ for the chosen bag model parameters.
- The Newtonian estimate for the crust radial extent is ΔR ≃ 100 meters for a 1.4 M_⊙ star with radius 10 km; the general relativistic calculation gives ΔR ≃ 40 meters.
- The Gibbs free energy gain per quark in the mixed phase (neglecting finite-size costs) is Δg ≃ 0.4 MeV per quark for m_s=150 MeV and n=1/fm³.
- The condition for the mixed phase to be favored in the bag model is σ ≲ 36 (m_s/150 MeV)³ (m_s/μ) MeV/fm²; for m_s=150 MeV and μ≃300 MeV this gives σ ≲ 36 MeV/fm², while Berger and Jaffe estimate σ ≃ 8 MeV/fm² for these parameters.
- The fractional mass in the crust is M_crust/M_star ∼ 10⁻³ to 10⁻².

## Context

This work contradicts the conventional picture of a bare strange star surface with a steep density gradient (∼10²⁶ g/cm⁴) and large electric fields, originally proposed by Alcock et al. (1986). It extends the concept of a mixed phase of quark drops and nuclear matter in hybrid stars, as studied by Glendenning (1992), to the surface of strange stars. The proposed crust shares features with the quark-alpha crust scenario of Benvenuto and Michel (1988) but is fundamentally different. The findings enable new calculations of observable signatures such as photon radiation, pulsar glitches via star-quakes, and thermal evolution of strange stars, and suggest that color-flavor-locked (CFL) quark matter, studied by Alford et al. (1998), would not have such a crust.
