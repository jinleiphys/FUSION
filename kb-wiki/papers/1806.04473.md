# Isospin mixing and Coulomb mixing in ground states of even-even nuclei

> In this work, the Coulomb mixing and the isospin mixing in the ground states of even-even nuclei are evaluated in perturbation theory. The calculation of the isospin mixing is performed by using the connection to isovector monopole resonance properties. The uncertainty in the results that depends on different choices of the Skyrme interactions is shown. While Coulomb mixing turns out to be large in the ground states of heavy nuclei, isospin mixing is very small.

# Isospin mixing and Coulomb mixing in ground states of even-even nuclei

## Key claim

The authors evaluate isospin mixing and Coulomb mixing in the ground states of even-even nuclei using perturbation theory, finding that while Coulomb mixing can be large (up to nearly 40% in heavy nuclei), isospin mixing remains very small (typically below 1%) because it is suppressed by a factor of 1/(T+1) in nuclei with neutron excess. The results depend on the choice of Skyrme interaction, but the qualitative conclusion that isospin mixing is small holds across all 12 parameter sets studied.

## Method

The Coulomb mixing was calculated using the full isovector part of the Coulomb potential in a Hartree-Fock plus random phase approximation (HF-RPA) framework. The isospin mixing was computed by exploiting the connection to the isovector monopole (IVM) resonance: the authors used the charge-exchange HF-pnRPA to calculate transition strengths of the IVM operator Q^(IV)_(+1) in the (N+1, Z-1) nucleus, then related these to the T+1 states in the parent nucleus using Coulomb displacement energies obtained from Skyrme-HF calculations. The ratio between the total Coulomb strength and the total IVM strength was used to avoid uncertainties from the choice of the nuclear radius parameter r_0.

## Key numbers

- For N=Z nuclei, Coulomb/isospin mixing ranges from 0.62% (SkI2, using V_C^(IV) operator) to 1.15% (SKP, using Q^(IV)_0 operator) in 40Ca; from 0.98% (SKO) to 2.00% (SKP) in 56Ni; and from 4.13% (SKO) to 6.82% (SKP) in 100Sn.
- For N>Z nuclei with the SIII interaction, Coulomb mixing in 208Pb is 29.90% while isospin mixing is only 0.29%.
- For 208Pb with the SKP interaction, Coulomb mixing reaches 38.50% while isospin mixing is 0.32%.
- For 78Ni (T=14), isospin mixing ranges from 0.02% (SKO) to 0.05% (SGII, SKM*, SAMi0) across interactions.
- The symmetry potential V_1 derived from the isospin splitting of the IVM resonance is around 100 MeV for most nuclei, e.g., 106.14 MeV for 48Ca with SIII and 89.25 MeV for 208Pb with SIII.
- The direct Coulomb displacement energy for 208Pb ranges from 18.98 MeV (SK255) to 19.93 MeV (LNS).

## Context

This work extends the earlier formalism of Auerbach (1983) that connected isospin mixing to the isovector monopole resonance, by applying it with 12 modern Skyrme interactions (including SIII, SGII, SKM*, SkP, SkI2, SLy4, SKO, SKO', LNS, SK255, BSk17, and SAMi0) rather than the few interactions used previously. The results confirm that the isospin mixing in ground states is small even when Coulomb mixing is large, consistent with the expectation from the 1/(T+1) suppression factor. The calculated IVM resonance properties and symmetry potentials can be compared to the Green function method results of Auerbach (1977) and provide input for future experimental searches for the IVM resonance, such as those attempted by the authors cited in the paper.
