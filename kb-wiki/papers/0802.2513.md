# Oscillations of Mossbauer neutrinos

> We calculate the probability of recoilless emission and detection of neutrinos (Mossbauer effect with neutrinos) taking into account the boundedness of the parent and daughter nuclei in the neutrino source and detector as well as the leptonic mixing. We show that, in spite of their near monochromaticity, the recoillessly emitted and captured neutrinos oscillate. After a qualitative discussion of this issue, we corroborate and extend our results by computing the combined rate of $\barν_e$ production, propagation and detection in the framework of quantum field theory, starting from first principles. This allows us to avoid making any a priori assumptions about the energy and momentum of the intermediate-state neutrino. Our calculation permits quantitative predictions of the transition rate in future experiments, and shows that the decoherence and delocalization factors, which could in principle suppress neutrino oscillations, are irrelevant under realistic experimental conditions.

## Key claim

The authors show that Mossbauer neutrinos do oscillate despite their extremely small energy uncertainty, because the momentum uncertainty of the neutrino source (determined by the spatial localization of the emitting nucleus) is large enough to ensure coherent emission of different mass eigenstates; the decoherence and delocalization factors that could suppress oscillations are irrelevant under realistic experimental conditions.

## Method

The authors calculate the combined rate of antineutrino production, propagation and detection for the tritium-helium-3 system using a quantum field theoretical approach with external wave packets, where the parent and daughter nuclei are treated as bound in harmonic oscillator potentials to represent their confinement in a crystal lattice. They compute the transition amplitude from first principles using coordinate-space Feynman rules for the diagram describing neutrino emission and absorption, without making a priori assumptions about the energy or momentum of the intermediate-state neutrino. The authors also employ a quantum mechanical intermediate wave packet formalism for comparison, and separately calculate the rates for recoilless neutrino production and detection including Lorentzian linewidths.

## Key numbers

- Natural linewidth for tritium decay: 1.17 x 10^{-24} eV; experimentally achievable Mossbauer linewidth: approximately 10^{-11} eV
- Neutrino energy in bound state tritium decay: 18.6 keV; achievable relative linewidth: approximately 10^{-15}
- For Delta m^2 = Delta m_{31}^2 approximately 2.5 x 10^{-3} eV^2, the energy difference between mass eigenstates is approximately 7 x 10^{-8} eV, much larger than the linewidth gamma
- The momentum uncertainty sigma_p from source localization is approximately 10 keV, giving 2p sigma_p much greater than Delta m^2
- Oscillation length L^{osc} = 4 pi E / Delta m^2 approximately 20 m for the relevant mass splitting
- The enhancement factor for the detection cross section compared to non-resonant capture can be as large as 10^{12} for gamma_S + gamma_D approximately 10^{-11} eV
- The recoil-free fraction factor X_S = 8 (eta_S + 1/eta_S)^{-3} exp(-p^2 / sigma_{pS}^2) with sigma_{pS}^2 = m_H omega_{H,S} + m_He omega_{He,S}
- The atomic wave function overlap factor kappa_S = kappa_D = 512/729 approximately 0.7 in the hydrogen-like approximation

## Context

This work resolves a controversy raised by Bilenky (2006) about whether Mossbauer neutrinos would oscillate, which depended on whether equal energies or equal momenta were assumed for different neutrino mass eigenstates. The paper extends earlier proposals by Raghavan (2005, 2006) for a tritium-helium-3 Mossbauer neutrino experiment, and provides the first QFT calculation of rates for bound-state beta decay and stimulated electron capture with nuclei bound to a crystal lattice. The results enable quantitative predictions for future experiments that could detect neutrinos with 100 g scale detectors, search for oscillations driven by theta_13 at baselines of only 10 m, determine the neutrino mass hierarchy without matter effects, and study gravitational redshift of neutrinos.
