# Accessing GPDs through the exclusive photoproduction of a $ γ$-meson pair

> We consider the exclusive photo-production of a photon-meson pair with a large invariant mass, working in the QCD factorisation framework. Explicitly, we consider a $ ρ$-meson or a charged $ π$ in the final state. This process gives access both to chiral-even GPDs and chiral-odd GPDs, which are not well-known experimentally, especially the latter ones. The computation is performed at leading order and leading twist. We discuss the prospects of measuring them in experiments, focusing on the kinematics at the JLab 12-GeV experiment, and pPb ultra-peripheral collisions at LHC. In particular, the latter gives access to the small $ ξ$ regime of GPDs.

## Key claim

Exclusive photoproduction of a photon-meson pair (with a $\rho$ or charged $\pi$ in the final state) can access both chiral-even and chiral-odd generalized parton distributions (GPDs) at leading twist, but the computation is performed only at leading order and leading twist, and the results depend on the choice of distribution amplitude model (asymptotic versus holographic) and GPD parametrization (standard versus valence scenario).

## Method

The amplitude for $\gamma + N \rightarrow \gamma + m + N'$ (with $m = \rho^{0,\pm}_{L,T}, \pi^{\pm}$) is factorized into a hard part computed from 20 Feynman diagrams at leading order, convolved with a nucleon GPD and a meson distribution amplitude (DA). The convolution over the DA momentum fraction $z$ is performed analytically, while the convolution over the GPD momentum fraction $x$ is performed numerically. The fully differential cross-section is expressed as a function of $-u'$, $-t$, and $M^2_{\gamma m}$, with $-t$ set to its minimum value and a dipole form factor $F_H(t)$ applied for the $t$-dependence.

## Key numbers

- The decay constant for the $\rho^0$ meson is $f_{\rho^0} = 213$ MeV.
- The cuts employed are $-u', -t' > 1$ GeV$^2$ and $-t < 0.5$ GeV$^2$, which ensure $M^2_{\gamma m} > 1$ GeV$^2$.
- The dipole form factor uses $C = 0.71$ GeV$^2$.
- For $\rho^0_L$ production at $S_{\gamma N} = 20$ GeV$^2$, using the holographic DA gives a fully differential cross-section roughly twice that of the asymptotic DA.
- The single differential cross-section for $\rho^0_L$ peaks around $M^2_{\gamma \rho} \approx 3$ GeV$^2$, with the peak position approximately independent of $S_{\gamma N}$ for $S_{\gamma N} \leq 2000$ GeV$^2$.
- The cross-section $\sigma_{\gamma \rho^0_L}$ as a function of $S_{\gamma N}$ peaks at around 20 GeV$^2$.
- Counting rates for pPb UPCs at LHC (luminosity $1.2$ pb$^{-1}$) without $S_{\gamma N}$ cut: $\rho^0_L$: $8.7$–$16 \times 10^3$ events; $\rho^+_L$: $4.8$–$10 \times 10^3$ events; $\pi^+$: $1.6$–$9.2 \times 10^3$ events.
- Counting rates with $S_{\gamma N} \geq 300$ GeV$^2$ cut: $\rho^0_L$: $3.6$–$7.2 \times 10^2$ events; $\rho^+_L$: $1.9$–$5.6 \times 10^2$ events; $\pi^+$: $1.0$–$3.1 \times 10^2$ events.
- At $S_{\gamma N} = 300$ GeV$^2$, the region of maximum cross-section corresponds to $\xi \approx 5 \times 10^{-3}$; at $S_{\gamma N} = 2000$ GeV$^2$, $\xi \approx 7.5 \times 10^{-4}$.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Boussarie (2016) and Duplancic (2018) for photoproduction of a photon-meson pair, providing updated cross-section calculations and counting rate estimates for both JLab 12-GeV and LHC pPb ultra-peripheral collisions. The process complements deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and deeply virtual meson production by giving access to chiral-odd GPDs at leading twist, which are poorly constrained experimentally. The results enable experimental searches at JLab 12-GeV and LHC, with the latter specifically accessing the small-$\xi$ regime of GPDs, and the authors note that NLO corrections will be needed to distinguish between different DA models.
