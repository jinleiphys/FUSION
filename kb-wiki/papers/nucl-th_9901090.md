# Microscopic Calculations of Weak Interaction Rates of Nuclei in Stellar Environment for A = 18 to 100

> We report here the microscopic calculation of weak interaction rates in stellar matter for 709 nuclei with A = 18 to 100 using a generalized form of proton-neutron quasiparticle RPA model with separable Gamow-Teller forces. This is the first ever extensive microscopic calculation of weak rates calculated over a wide temperature-density grid which includes 10^7 \leq T(K) \leq 30 \times 10^9 and 10 \leq ρY_{e} (gcm^{-3}) \leq 10^{11}, and over a larger mass range. Particle emission processes from excited states, previously ignored, are taken into account, and are found to significantly affect some $β$ decay rates. The calculated capture and decay rates take into consideration the latest experimental energy levels and $ft$ value compilations. Our calculation of electron capture and $β$-decay rates, in the $fp$-shell, show considerable differences with a recently reported shell model diagonalization approach calculation.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first extensive microscopic calculation of stellar weak interaction rates using the proton-neutron quasiparticle RPA (pn-QRPA) model for 709 nuclei with A = 18 to 100, covering a wider mass range and temperature-density grid than previous work, while also including particle emission processes from excited states that were previously ignored. The authors report that their calculated electron capture and beta-decay rates in the fp-shell show considerable differences compared to a recently reported shell model diagonalization approach calculation, with the direction and magnitude of these differences depending on nuclear type and stellar conditions.

## Method

The authors employed a generalized proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase-approximation (pn-QRPA) model with separable Gamow-Teller forces, considering a model space up to 7 major shells. They calculated 12 different stellar rates for each parent nucleus, including electron and positron capture rates, beta-minus and beta-plus decay rates, (anti)neutrino energy loss rates, and probabilities and energy rates of beta-delayed proton and neutron emission, considering parent excitation energies up to the particle decay threshold (minimum of proton and neutron separation energies). The calculations incorporated the latest experimental energy levels and ft value compilations, and were performed over a temperature-density grid spanning 10^7 ≤ T(K) ≤ 30 × 10^9 and 10 ≤ ρY_e (g cm^{-3}) ≤ 10^11.

## Key numbers

- 709 nuclei with mass numbers A = 18 to 100 were calculated
- Temperature range: 10^7 ≤ T(K) ≤ 30 × 10^9
- Density range: 10 ≤ ρY_e (g cm^{-3}) ≤ 10^11
- For electron capture on ^56Ni at ρ_7 = 4.32 and T_9 = 3.26: QRPA rate = 9.9 × 10^{-3} s^{-1}, compared to shell model rate of 1.3 × 10^{-2} s^{-1} and FFN rate of 7.4 × 10^{-3} s^{-1}
- For electron capture on ^55Co at ρ_7 = 4.32 and T_9 = 3.26: QRPA rate = 8.0 × 10^{-2} s^{-1}, compared to shell model rate of 1.6 × 10^{-3} s^{-1} and FFN rate of 8.4 × 10^{-2} s^{-1}
- For beta decay of ^56Fe at ρ_7 = 5.86 and T_9 = 3.40: QRPA rate = 5.5 × 10^{-13} s^{-1}, compared to shell model rate of 3.9 × 10^{-11} s^{-1} and FFN rate of 2.3 × 10^{-10} s^{-1}
- For beta decay of ^58Co at ρ_7 = 4.32 and T_9 = 3.26: QRPA rate = 2.7 × 10^{-11} s^{-1}, compared to shell model rate of 2.7 × 10^{-6} s^{-1} and FFN rate of 1.2 × 10^{-6} s^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the earlier pn-QRPA calculations of terrestrial beta decay half-lives by Staudt et al. (1990) and Hirsch et al. (1993) to stellar conditions, and supersedes the pioneering tabulations of Fuller, Fowler, and Newman (FFN, 1980) for 226 nuclei with A = 21 to 60, as well as the sd-shell calculations of Oda et al. (1994) for A = 17 to 39. The results enable improved modeling of presupernova core evolution, supernova collapse dynamics, and r-process nucleosynthesis, and provide a benchmark for comparison with the shell model diagonalization approach of Langanke and Martinez-Pinedo (1998), which was limited to truncated model spaces of one major shell and parent excited states of only a few MeV.
