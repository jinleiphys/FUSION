# Particle productions and anisotropic flows from the AMPT model for Cu+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV

> We use the string melting version of a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model to study Cu+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV. The rapidity distributions of identified hadrons show asymmetric dependences on rapidity. In addition, elliptic and triangular flows at mid-rapidity from the AMPT model for pions, kaons, and protons agree reasonably with the experimental data up to $p_{T}\sim1$ GeV$/c$. We then investigate the forward/backward asymmetry of $v_2$ and $v_3$. We find that these anisotropic flows are larger on the Au-going side than the Cu-going side, while the asymmetry tends to go away in very peripheral collisions. We also make predictions on transverse momentum spectra of identified hadrons and longitudinal decorrelations of charged particles, where the average decorrelation of elliptic flow in asymmetric Cu+Au collisions is found to be stronger than that in Au+Au collisions.

## Key claim

The string melting AMPT model with the new quark coalescence can reproduce the elliptic and triangular flows of identified hadrons at mid-rapidity in Cu+Au collisions at 200 GeV only up to transverse momentum of about 1 GeV/c, and the forward/backward asymmetry of these flows is largest in semi-central (20-30%) collisions rather than in central collisions.

## Method

The authors used the string melting version of the AMPT model with a new quark coalescence mechanism, where each quark is free to coalesce into either a meson or a baryon based on distance to potential partners, removing the previous separate conservation constraints. They simulated Cu+Au collisions at 200 GeV with Lund string fragmentation parameters a=0.55 and b=0.15 GeV^-2, strong coupling constant alpha_s=0.33, and a parton cross section of 1.5 mb, terminating the hadron cascade at a global time of 300 fm/c. Anisotropic flow coefficients v2 and v3 were calculated using the event-plane method with three-subevent resolution corrections, matching the analysis methods of the PHENIX and STAR Collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Mean rapidity of pi+ in central (0-5%) Cu+Au collisions is -0.43, shifting to -0.24 in semi-central (20-30%) collisions
- Mean rapidity of Lambda in central collisions is -0.78, shifting to -0.42 in semi-central collisions
- Ratio of average v2 in backward pseudorapidity (-3.9 < eta < -3) over forward pseudorapidity (3 < eta < 3.9) is 1.30 +/- 0.03 for 0-10% centrality, 1.35 +/- 0.02 for 20-30%, 1.22 +/- 0.02 for 40-60%, and 1.14 +/- 0.03 for 60-80%
- Ratio of average v3 in backward over forward pseudorapidity is 1.42 +/- 0.08 for 0-10%, 1.43 +/- 0.10 for 20-30%, 1.34 +/- 0.15 for 40-60%, and 0.71 +/- 0.36 for 60-80%
- Charged particle multiplicity density dN_ch/deta in backward pseudorapidity (-3.9 < eta < -3) is 207.8 +/- 0.5 for 0-10% centrality and 12.2 +/- 0.1 for 60-80%
- Ratio of backward/forward multiplicity density is 1.67 +/- 0.01 for 0-10% and 1.22 +/- 0.02 for 60-80%

## Context

This work extends previous AMPT model studies of Cu+Au collisions (Lin 2015, He 2015) that used the old quark coalescence and overestimated flows at low pT, and it provides predictions for observables not yet measured by the PHENIX and STAR Collaborations. The results enable comparisons between asymmetric Cu+Au collisions and symmetric Au+Au collisions to study initial-state geometry effects and longitudinal decorrelations, where the average decorrelation of elliptic flow in Cu+Au is found to be stronger than in Au+Au collisions. The study also builds on hydrodynamic predictions for Cu+Au collisions (Bozek 2012) and on measurements of longitudinal flow decorrelations at LHC (CMS 2015, ATLAS 2017) and RHIC (STAR 2019).
