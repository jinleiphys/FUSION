# Enhancement of low-${m_t}$ kaons in AGS heavy-ion collisions

> In the relativistic transport model, we show that the recently observed enhancement of low-$m_t$ kaons ($K^+$ and $K^-$) in Si+Pb collisions at AGS can be explained if a density isomer is introduced in the nuclear equation-of-state.

## Key claim

The observed enhancement of low-$m_t$ kaons and antikaons in Si+Pb collisions at 14.6 (GeV/c)/nucleon can be explained if a density isomer (secondary minimum) is introduced in the nuclear equation-of-state around 5.5 times normal nuclear matter density, which slows the fireball expansion enough for kaons to be cooled by their attractive mean-field potentials.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic transport model based on the sigma-omega model, simulating the expansion stage of a fireball initially at 6 times normal nuclear matter density and 170 MeV temperature. They introduced a density-dependent vector coupling constant to create a density isomer at 5.5 times saturation density, and propagated kaons and antikaons through their mean-field potentials including both scalar and vector interactions, with in-medium modifications to the antikaon annihilation cross section.

## Key numbers

- Initial fireball density: 6 times normal nuclear matter density (rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}), temperature 170 MeV
- Density isomer secondary minimum at 5.5 rho_0 with binding energy of about 2 MeV
- Nuclear matter parameters: saturation density 0.16 fm^{-3}, binding energy 15.96 MeV, nucleon effective mass m*/m = 0.77, incompressibility 380 MeV
- Kaon sigma term Sigma_KN = 350 MeV, kaon decay constant f_K = 93 MeV
- Final low-m_t kaon component apparent temperature: about 40 MeV (from exponential fit for m_t - m_K < 0.02 GeV)
- Final low-m_t antikaon component apparent temperature: about 30 MeV
- High-m_t component apparent temperature for both kaons and antikaons: about 140-145 MeV

## Context

This work extends the approach of Koch (1994), who attempted to explain low-m_t kaon enhancement using an attractive kaon potential but required an unrealistic reduction of the vector coupling constant that produced nuclear matter with saturation at 0.6 fm^{-3} and binding energy of about 250 MeV. The authors instead introduce a density isomer mechanism, building on the theoretical possibility of abnormal nuclear matter states proposed by Lee and Wick (1974) and developed by Boguta (1982) using a relativistic mean-field theory with delta degrees of freedom. This enables more realistic comparisons with E814 collaboration data (1994) and suggests the need for a complete dynamical model including rapidity cuts and additional degrees of freedom.
