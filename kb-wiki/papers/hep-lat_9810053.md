# Instantons, the QCD Vacuum, and Hadronic Physics

> A large body of evidence from lattice calculations indicates that instantons play a major role in the physics of light hadrons. This evidence is summarized, and recent results concerning the instanton content of the SU(3) vacuum, instanton contributions to the static potential, and a new class of instanton solutions at finite temperature are reviewed.

# Instantons, the QCD Vacuum, and Hadronic Physics

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations provide substantial evidence that instantons play a major role in light hadron physics, but the precision with which one can apply semiclassical concepts is intrinsically limited because cooling procedures used to extract instantons from lattice configurations necessarily modify the instanton distribution to some extent.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD studies that extract instanton content from gauge configurations using several cooling and smoothing methods: standard cooling (iteratively minimizing the Wilson action locally), underrelaxed cooling (APE smearing), improved cooling (minimizing an improved action with five plaquette terms to prevent instanton shrinkage), renormalization group cycling, and simultaneous relaxation. Instantons are identified by peaks in the topological charge density Q(x) assuming additivity, with filters applied to assure appropriate shape and avoid counting fluctuations. Vacuum correlation functions for hadron currents are calculated on cooled and uncooled configurations and compared with the instanton liquid model.

## Key numbers

- Topological susceptibility χ^(1/4) for SU(3): 187(14) MeV from underrelaxed cooling (Smith+98), 192(5) MeV from APE smearing (Ahasenfratz+98), 185(9) MeV from improved cooling (DeForcrand+98), 197(4) MeV from spectral flow (Edwards+98)
- Extrapolated average instanton size ρ̄_extrap = 0.39(5) fm, consistent with the instanton liquid model
- Average instanton size after cooling to density N/V ≈ 3.2 fm⁻⁴: ρ̄_(N/V) = 0.54(5) fm
- Instanton number densities range from 55 fm⁻⁴ to 0.3 fm⁻⁴ depending on cooling steps
- Chiral condensate ⟨ψ̄ψ⟩_(m_q→0) from low eigenvalue fits: 0.0114 at β=5.65 and 0.002 at β=5.725 (Hetrick+98)
- For a 16⁴ lattice, 64 of 786,432 eigenmodes reproduce most of the rho peak strength and 128 modes reproduce full pion strength in the vector correlation function (Ivanenko+98)
- Lattice slices narrow in the time direction show ⟨Q²⟩ up to twice as large as slices narrow in one spatial direction (Hetrick+98)

## Context

This review summarizes lattice evidence supporting the instanton liquid model developed by Shuryak (1982), Diakonov and Petrov (1986), and Schäfer and Shuryak (1998), which provides a phenomenology of instanton-mediated quark propagation. The work extends earlier analytic efforts that could not proceed beyond the dilute instanton gas approximation (Callan, Dashen, and Gross, 1978). The results confirm that instantons account for the chiral condensate via the Banks-Casher relation (Banks and Casher, 1980) and the η′ mass via the Veneziano-Witten formula (Witten, 1979; Veneziano, 1979). The findings enable quantitative study of how instantons produce the observed channel dependence of hadron correlation functions and may explain the proton spin crisis through helicity transfer from valence quarks to gluons and sea quarks.
