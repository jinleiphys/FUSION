# Schwinger-Dyson approach to light pseudoscalars

> We review briefly some of the successes of Schwinger-Dyson (SD) approach to the physics of quarks and hadrons, primarily light pseudoscalar mesons including eta and eta'. The main purpose of this paper is to point out that SD results on eta and eta' mesons can be formulated and given also in the two-mixing-angle scheme.

## Key claim
The Schwinger-Dyson (SD) approach to light pseudoscalar mesons, including η and η′, can be formulated in the two-mixing-angle scheme of decay constants, even though the ladder-approximated SD–Bethe-Salpeter (BS) equations cannot include gluon anomaly effects directly; the anomaly is instead parameterized through the anomalous shift of the η0 mass, which is suppressed as 1/Nc.

## Method
The authors solved the rainbow-ladder-approximated SD equation for dressed light-quark propagators (u, d, s) using an effective gluon propagator (the Munczek-Jain Ansatz), and then employed the same interaction in the ladder-approximated BS equations for quark-antiquark bound-state amplitudes. The gluon anomaly was included only by parameterizing the anomalous mass shift of the η0 state. The resulting η–η′ mass matrix and decay constants were then expressed in the nonstrange-strange (NS–S) basis, and the two mixing angles θ8 and θ0 were derived from the single NS–S state mixing angle φ and the decay constants fNS and fS via the relations of Feldmann, Kroll, and Stech (FKS).

## Key numbers
- The preferred NS–S state mixing angle from the SD-BS approach is φ ≈ 42°.
- The ratio y = fNS / fS = 0.689, corresponding to fS = 1.451 fπ (just 3% above the theoretical FKS prediction).
- The derived decay-constant-mixing angles are θ8 = −22° and θ0 = −2.3°, close to the FKS theoretical results (θ8 = −21.0°, θ0 = −2.7°).
- The effective η8–η0 state-mixing angle θ ≈ (θ8 + θ0)/2 ≈ −12°.
- The octet and singlet decay constants are f8 = 1.318 fπ and f0 = 1.170 fπ.

## Context
This work extends the Schwinger-Dyson approach to η–η′ mixing, previously applied by the same authors (Klabucar 1997, Kekez 2000), by showing its consistency with the two-mixing-angle scheme of Feldmann, Kroll, and Stech (1998, 1999). The results agree with phenomenological and lattice determinations of the η–η′ mass matrix. The approach enables calculation of meson decay constants (such as fπ) from quark substructure, rather than treating them as input parameters, and can be extended to processes far from the chiral limit, such as η, η′ → γγ.
