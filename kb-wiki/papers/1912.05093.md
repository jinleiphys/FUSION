# Strange quark stars within proper time regularized (2+1)-flavor NJL model

> In this work we use the equation of state (EOS) of (2+1)-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to study the structure of the strange quark star. With a new free parameter $α$, the Lagrangian is constructed by two parts, the original NJL Lagrangian and the Fierz transformation of it, as $\mathcal{L}=(1-α)\mathcal{L}_{NJL}+α\mathcal{L}_{Fierz}$. To determine the range of $α$, we compare the binding energies in the 2-flavor and (2+1)-flavor cases. We also consider the constraints of chemical equilibrium and electric charge neutrality in the strange quark star and choose six representative EOSs with different $α$ and $B$ (bag constant) to study their influence on the structure of the strange quark star. As a result, we find that a larger $α$ and a smaller $B$ corresponds to a heavier star with a stiffer EOS. Furthermore, the heaviest strange quark star is in agreement with not only the recent mass observation of PSR J0740+6620 and the X-ray observations on radius measurements, but also the constraint on tidal deformability of GW170817.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that within a modified (2+1)-flavor NJL model with a mixing parameter α between the original Lagrangian and its Fierz transformation, strange quark stars can satisfy multiple observational constraints simultaneously, but only for a specific combination of α and bag constant B (α=0.94, B^(1/4)=117 MeV), and only under the condition that the strange quark matter is more stable than non-strange matter for small α values.

## Method

The authors construct a Lagrangian as a linear combination of the original NJL Lagrangian and its Fierz transformation, weighted by a free parameter α. They apply the mean-field approximation and proper time regularization (PTR) to derive the equation of state (EOS) for (2+1)-flavor quark matter at zero temperature, imposing chemical equilibrium and electric charge neutrality conditions. The TOV equations are then solved to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities for strange quark stars, using six representative EOSs with different α values (0, 0.5, 0.94, 0.95, 0.97) and bag constants B^(1/4)=117 MeV and 130 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of 2.057 solar masses for the EOS with α=0.94 and B^(1/4)=117 MeV, with corresponding radius about 11.20 km
- The bag constant is taken as (117 MeV)^4 and (130 MeV)^4 for comparison
- Sound velocities for all six representative EOSs are smaller than 0.7 times the speed of light
- For α values from 0 to 0.97, the critical chemical potential where u,d quark number density becomes nonzero is about 200 MeV; for s quarks, about 320 MeV
- Parameter set used: m_u=3.4 MeV, m_s=104 MeV, Λ_UV=1330 MeV, G=1.51×10^(-6) MeV^(-2), K=2.75×10^(-14) MeV^(-5), producing M_u=197 MeV and M_s=364 MeV

## Context

This work extends the modified NJL model approach previously applied to 2-flavor non-strange quark stars by Wang et al. (2019) and subsequent studies (PhysRevD.100.043018, PhysRevD.100.123003) to the (2+1)-flavor case for strange quark stars. It addresses the long-standing question from Witten's strange quark matter hypothesis (PhysRevD.30.272) about whether 3-flavor or 2-flavor quark matter is more stable, finding that the answer depends on α and baryon density. The results enable future comparisons with multi-messenger observations including pulsar masses from PSR J0740+6620 and PSR J0348+0432, X-ray radius measurements from NICER, and tidal deformability constraints from GW170817.
