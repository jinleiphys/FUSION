# A Pomeron Approach to Hadron-Nucleus and Nucleus-Nucleus "Soft" Interactions at High Energy

> We formulate a generalization of the Glauber formalism for hadron-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions based on the Pomeron approach to high energy interactions. Our treatment is based on two physical assumptions (i.e. two small parameters) : (i) that only sufficiently small distances contribute to the Pomeron structure; and (ii) the triple Pomeron vertex $G_{3P}/g_{P-N} \ll 1$ (where $g_{P-N}$ is the Pomeron-nucleon vertex) is small. A systematic method is developed for calculating the total, elastic and diffractive dissociation cross sections as well as the survival probability of large rapidity gap processes and inclusive observables, both for hadron - nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions. Our approach suggests saturation of the density of the produced hadrons in nucleus-nucleus collisions, the value of the saturation density turns out to be large and depends on the number of nucleons in the lightest nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the Glauber-Gribov formalism for hadron-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions by incorporating Pomeron interactions, specifically the triple Pomeron vertex, under the assumptions that only sufficiently small distances contribute to the Pomeron structure and that the ratio \(G_{3P}/g_{P-N}\) is small. The approach suggests saturation of produced hadron density in nucleus-nucleus collisions, with the saturation density depending on the number of nucleons in the lightest nucleus.

## Method

The authors develop a systematic method based on summing "fan" diagrams of Pomeron exchanges, using a small parameter \(\kappa_A\) (defined in terms of the atomic number \(A\), Pomeron-nucleon vertex \(g_{PN}\), triple Pomeron vertex \(G_{3P}\), and the Pomeron intercept \(\Delta\)) to classify Reggeon diagrams. They treat the Pomeron exchange and all Pomeron-Pomeron interaction vertices as delta functions in impact parameter space, concentrating all \(b_t\) dependence in the Pomeron-hadron vertices. The method yields analytic expressions for total, elastic, and diffractive dissociation cross sections, as well as survival probabilities for large rapidity gap processes, for both hadron-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key numbers

- The Pomeron trajectory intercept is \(\alpha_P(0) = 1 + \Delta\) with \(\Delta \ll 1\), and the slope \(\alpha_P' = 0.25\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) (compared to the Reggeon slope \(\alpha_R' = 1\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\)).
- The nuclear radius is parametrized as \(R_A = A^{2/3} \cdot 17\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\).
- The triple Pomeron vertex ratio \(G_{3P}/(g_{PN} \cdot \Delta)\) is estimated from experimental data to be approximately 1/4 to 1/8.
- The mean transverse momentum of partons in the Pomeron is assumed to be \(\langle p_t \rangle \geq 1\ \text{GeV}\).
- The gluon confinement radius in the Additive Quark Model is \(R_G \approx 0.1\ \text{fm}\), much smaller than the hadron radius \(R_h \approx 1\ \text{fm}\).

## Context

This work extends the Glauber-Gribov approach (Glauber, 1959; Gribov, 1969) for hadron-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions by including Pomeron interactions that were previously neglected, particularly the triple Pomeron vertex. The approach builds on the Schwimmer parameter \(\kappa_A\) (Schwimmer, 1970) and uses the BFKL Pomeron (Balitsky, Fadin, Kuraev, Lipatov, 1975-1978) for the general structure of perturbative QCD at high energy. The formalism enables calculations of observables relevant for RHIC experiments, including total cross sections, diffractive dissociation, and survival probabilities for large rapidity gap processes, and provides a bridge between the Glauber-Gribov approach and high-density QCD (Gribov, Levin, Ryskin, 1983; Mueller, Qiu, 1986).
