# Locking Internal and Space-Symmetries: Relativistic Vector Condensation

> Internal and Lorentz symmetries are necessarily linked when considering non scalar condensates. Here I review vectorial type condensation due to a non zero chemical potential associated to some of the global conserved charges of the theory. The phase structure is very rich since three distinct phases exists depending on the value assumed by one of the zero chemical potential vector self interaction terms. In a certain limit of the couplings and for large chemical potential the theory is not stable. This limit corresponds to a gauge type limit often employed to economically describe the ordinary vector mesons self interactions in QCD. Our analysis is relevant since it leads to a number of physical applications not limited to strongly interacting theories at non zero chemical potential.

# Locking Internal and Space-Symmetries: Relativistic Vector Condensation

## Key claim
Vector condensation driven by a nonzero chemical potential for global charges can produce three distinct phases depending on the value of the zero-chemical-potential vector self-interaction term λ′, but in the gauge-type limit where λ = λ′ (often used to describe vector meson self-interactions in QCD), the theory becomes unstable at large chemical potential with no stable minimum.

## Method
The author considers an effective Lagrangian for a relativistic massive vector field in the adjoint of SU(2) in 3+1 dimensions, including mass, quartic self-interactions, and a possible δ term. A nonzero chemical potential μ is introduced for the T³ charge, and the vacuum structure is explored via a variational ansatz for the charged vector fields φμ, with the neutral component ψμ set to zero. The potential is minimized as a function of the condensate amplitude σ and phase α, and the resulting symmetry breaking patterns and Goldstone boson types are classified according to the sign of λ′.

## Key numbers
- The unbroken phase occurs when μ ≤ m, with minimum at σ = 0.
- In the polar phase (λ′ > 0), the condensate amplitude is σ² = (μ² − m²) / [4(λ − λ′)].
- In the apolar phase (λ′ < 0), the condensate amplitude is σ² = (μ² − m²) / [2(2λ − λ′)].
- In the gauge limit λ = λ′, the potential becomes V = 2σ⁴λ sin²α + 2(m² − μ²)σ², yielding only a saddle point at α = ±π/2 with σ² = (μ² − m²)/(2λ) when μ > m.
- For the external magnetic field case, the potential minimum occurs at α = π/2 and σ = (eH − m²)/(2λ) when eH > m.

## Context
This work reviews and extends the author’s prior analysis of vector condensation (Sannino 2002) and builds on earlier studies of vector condensation in various contexts, including Linde (1979) on W boson condensation in superdense fermionic matter, Ambjørn and Olesen (1989) on vector condensation in strong magnetic fields, and Manton (1979) and Hosotani (1988) on gauge field condensation in extra dimensions. The results are directly applicable to two-color QCD at nonzero baryon chemical potential, where vector condensation was predicted by Lenaghan and Sannino (2001) and Sannino (2001), and recent lattice studies by Alles et al. (2002) appear to support this prediction. The analysis enables future lattice studies at high isospin chemical potential to determine the correct description of vector self-interactions at zero chemical potential.
