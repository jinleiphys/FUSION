# New perspective on cold fusion reactions: A microscopic description

> A microscopic framework that combines the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach with the fusion by diffusion (FBD) model is proposed to investigate the synthesis mechanism of superheavy nuclei (SHN). For the reaction $^{48}\text{Ca}+^{208}\text{Pb}$, the calculated evaporation-residue cross section (ERCS) reproduces the experimental data reasonably well. The method enables self-consistent extraction of the fusion injection point and inner barrier from HFB potential-energy surfaces (PES), thereby incorporating nuclear structure effects while eliminating phenomenological tuning at the fusion stage. For cold-fusion reactions, the PES features a hyperasymmetric valley driven by shell effects. This $^{208}$Pb anchored valley connects the entrance channel to compound nucleus formation and provides an exit channel for cluster decay. We further investigate the cold-fusion reactions $^{54}\text{Cr}+^{208}\text{Pb}$ and $^{58}\text{Fe}+^{208}\text{Pb}$, obtaining a near-exponential decrease of $P_{\text{CN}}$ with compound-nucleus charge $Z$, consistent with established systematics. This approach demonstrates a self-consistent framework that can reduce uncertainties in the fusion stage of SHN production.

## Key claim

The paper claims that combining Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the fusion-by-diffusion (FBD) model can self-consistently predict fusion probabilities for cold-fusion superheavy element synthesis without phenomenological tuning, but this demonstration is limited to reactions on a $^{208}$Pb target and the agreement with experimental evaporation-residue cross sections is described as "reasonably well" rather than exact.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Skyrme HFB calculations using the SkM* energy density functional to construct multidimensional potential-energy surfaces (PES) for the compound nucleus $^{256}$No, with constraints on quadrupole ($Q_{20}$), octupole ($Q_{30}$), and hexadecapole ($Q_{40}$) moments. From the PES, they extracted the injection point (the intersection of the fusion path and cluster-decay path) and the inner fusion barrier height $B$ self-consistently, then used these as inputs to the Smoluchowski diffusion equation within the FBD model to compute the compound-nucleus formation probability $P_{\text{CN}}$. The capture cross sections were calculated using the Hill-Wheeler formula with empirical barrier distributions, and survival probabilities were obtained from a statistical model.

## Key numbers

* For the reaction $^{48}\text{Ca}+^{208}\text{Pb}$, the inner fusion barrier height extracted from the HFB PES is $B = 0.25$ MeV.
* The injection point for $^{256}\text{No}$ is located at ($Q_{20}=155$ b, $Q_{30}=50$ b$^{3/2}$).
* The $Q$ value for the reaction $^{208}\text{Pb}(^{48}\text{Ca},xn)^{256-x}\text{No}$ equals -152.87 MeV.
* The calculated fusion probability $P_{\text{CN}}$ shows a near-exponential decrease with compound-nucleus charge $Z$ for the reactions $^{48}\text{Ca}+^{208}\text{Pb}$, $^{54}\text{Cr}+^{208}\text{Pb}$, and $^{58}\text{Fe}+^{208}\text{Pb}$ at an excitation energy $E^{*}=15$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the fusion-by-diffusion approach of Swiatecki (2005) and Cap (2011) by replacing the phenomenological macroscopic-microscopic potential-energy surfaces with fully microscopic HFB calculations, following earlier efforts by Sekizawa (2019) and Guo-Lu (2022, 2023) who used time-dependent Hartree-Fock to determine the injection point but still relied on macroscopic-microscopic inner barriers. The framework unifies the description of cold fusion and cluster decay through the same $^{208}$Pb-anchored hyper-asymmetric valley on the PES, as previously studied in cluster decay by Warda (2011, 2018) and Giuliani (2023). This self-consistent approach enables predictions for unmeasured reactions such as $^{54}\text{Cr}+^{208}\text{Pb}$ and $^{58}\text{Fe}+^{208}\text{Pb}$, and can be extended to incorporate pairing and finite-temperature effects in future work.
