# Relativistic interpretation on the nature of nuclear tensor force

> The spin-dependent nature of the nuclear tensor force is studied in details within the relativistic Hartree-Fock approach. The relativistic formalism for the tensor force is supplemented with an additional Lorentz-invariant tensor formalism in $σ$-scalar channel, so as to take into account almost fully the nature of the tensor force brought about by the Fock diagrams in realistic nuclei. Specifically, the tensor sum rules are tested for the spin and pseudo-spin partners with/without nodes, to further understand the tensor force nature within relativistic model. It is shown that the interference between two components of nucleon spinors brings distinct violations on the tensor sum rules in realistic nuclei , which is mainly due to the opposite sign on $κ$ quantities of the upper and lower components as well as the nodal difference. Even though, the sum rules can be precisely reproduced if taking the same radial wave functions for the spin/pseudo-spin partners in addition to neglecting the lower/upper components, revealing clearly the nature of tensor force.

## Key claim

The relativistic formalism for the nuclear tensor force in the sigma-scalar channel requires supplementation with a higher-rank Lorentz-invariant tensor term (rank 3) to fully capture the tensor nature brought by the Fock diagrams; without this supplementation, the spin-dependent feature from the sigma-scalar coupling is overestimated. The tensor sum rules are violated in realistic nuclei when using full Dirac spinors, but can be precisely reproduced when the lower components of the spinors are neglected and the same radial wave functions are assumed for spin/pseudo-spin partners.

## Method

The authors used the density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) approach with the PKA1 functional to calculate the contributions of Fock diagrams to spin-orbit splittings in spin-partner states. They tested the tensor sum rules for spin and pseudo-spin partner states in the nuclei ⁴⁸Ca, ⁹⁰Zr, and ²⁰⁸Pb, comparing results obtained with full Dirac spinors, with lower components omitted, and with the additional assumption of identical radial wave functions for partner states.

## Key numbers

- For the nodeless neutron orbits in ⁴⁸Ca, the sum rule V_sum values for the sigma-S coupling with full Dirac spinors range from -0.731 × 10⁻¹ MeV to -3.409 × 10⁻¹ MeV, comparable to the interaction matrix elements themselves.
- When lower components are neglected for the same system, the sum rule deviations reduce to relative deviations of a few percent, with V_sum values between -0.132 × 10⁻¹ MeV and 0.298 × 10⁻¹ MeV.
- When both lower components are neglected and identical radial wave functions are assumed for spin partners, the sum rule is reproduced with V_sum less than or equal to 10⁻⁶ MeV.
- For the pseudo-spin partner states in ⁹⁰Zr with full Dirac spinors, the sum rule is violated completely, with V_sum values one order of magnitude larger than the interaction matrix elements (e.g., 3.433 × 10⁻¹ MeV for the 1p̃ channel).
- When upper components are neglected for pseudo-spin partners, the sum rule agreement improves, with V_sum values in units of 10⁻⁴ MeV (e.g., 0.698 × 10⁻⁴ MeV).
- With both upper components neglected and identical radial wave functions assumed, the pseudo-spin sum rule is reproduced with V_sum less than or equal to 10⁻⁹ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic formalism for nuclear tensor force components in Fock diagrams developed by Jiang et al. (2015), who had previously tested the tensor sum rules under the approximation of neglecting lower Dirac spinor components and assuming identical radial wave functions. The present study reveals that the lower components of Dirac spinors, which have opposite sign on the kappa quantum number and different nodal structure compared to the upper components, cause distinct violations of the tensor sum rules in realistic nuclei. This enables a deeper understanding of the relativistic nature of the tensor force and its role in spin-orbit splitting and pseudo-spin symmetry within covariant density functional theory.
