# Application of the nuclear equation of state obtained by the variational method to core-collapse supernovae

> The equation of state (EOS) for hot asymmetric nuclear matter which is constructed with the variational method starting from the Argonne v18 and Urbana IX nuclear forces is applied to spherically symmetric core-collapse supernovae (SNe). We first investigate the EOS of isentropic beta-stable SN matter, and find that the matter with the variational EOS is more neutron-rich than that with the Shen EOS. Using the variational EOS for uniform matter supplemented by the Shen EOS of non-uniform matter at low densities, we perform general-relativistic spherically symmetric simulations of core-collapse SNe with and without neutrino transfer, starting from a presupernova model of 15 solar mass. In the adiabatic simulation without neutrino transfer, the explosion is successful, and the explosion energy with the variational EOS is larger than that with the Shen EOS. In the case of the simulation with neutrino transfer, the shock wave stalls and then the explosion fails, as in other spherically symmetric simulations. The inner core with the variational EOS is more compact than that with the Shen EOS, due to the relative softness of the variational EOS. This implies that the variational EOS is more advantageous for SN explosions than the Shen EOS.

## Key claim

The variational equation of state (EOS) based on realistic nuclear forces (Argonne v18 and Urbana IX) is softer and produces a more compact inner core than the Shen EOS, which is advantageous for supernova explosions in adiabatic simulations; however, in the more realistic simulation with neutrino transfer, the shock wave stalls and the explosion fails, as in other spherically symmetric simulations.

## Method

The authors performed general-relativistic spherically symmetric hydrodynamic simulations of core-collapse supernovae starting from a 15 solar mass presupernova model, using a variational EOS for uniform matter at baryon mass densities above 10^14.3 g/cm^3 and the Shen EOS for non-uniform matter at lower densities. They ran two sets of simulations: an adiabatic case without neutrino transfer (weak reactions switched off, fixed electron fraction) and a more realistic case with neutrino transfer including weak reactions. The variational EOS was constructed using a cluster variational method with the Argonne v18 two-body potential and Urbana IX three-body potential, with parameters tuned to reproduce empirical saturation properties.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density n0 = 0.16 fm^-3, saturation energy E0/N = -16.09 MeV, incompressibility K = 245 MeV, symmetry energy Esym = 30.0 MeV for the variational EOS
- Incompressibility of the Shen EOS is K = 281 MeV, compared to 245 MeV for the variational EOS
- Symmetry energy of the Shen EOS is 36.9 MeV at saturation density, compared to 30.0 MeV for the variational EOS
- Maximum mass of cold neutron stars with the variational EOS is 2.22 solar masses
- Critical baryon mass density for causality violation is rho_c = 1.5 x 10^15 g/cm^3 (n_c = 0.93 fm^-3) for Y_l = 0.3 and S = 1
- Central density at bounce with the variational EOS is 5.2 x 10^14 g/cm^3
- Explosion energy in the adiabatic simulation with the variational EOS is 1.7 x 10^51 erg, compared to 1.5 x 10^51 erg with the Shen EOS
- Shock wave reaches the edge of the iron core 11 ms after bounce with the variational EOS, 1 ms faster than with the Shen EOS

## Context

This work extends the application of microscopic nuclear EOSs based on realistic bare nuclear forces (Argonne v18 and Urbana IX) from cold neutron star studies to core-collapse supernova simulations, which previously relied on phenomenological EOSs such as the Shen EOS (Shen et al., 1998) based on relativistic mean field theory or the Lattimer-Swesty EOS (Lattimer and Swesty, 1991) based on Skyrme-type interactions. The variational EOS results are compared directly with the Shen EOS, and the authors note that the variational EOS is softer (lower incompressibility and symmetry energy), leading to a more compact inner core and larger explosion energy in adiabatic simulations. This work enables future multi-dimensional simulations and the inclusion of non-uniform matter treatments based on the same microscopic framework.
