# Low-energy multipole response in nuclei at finite temperature

> The multipole response of nuclei at temperatures T=0-2 MeV is studied using a self-consistent finite-temperature RPA (random phase approximation) based on relativistic energy density functionals. Illustrative calculations are performed for the isoscalar monopole and isovector dipole modes and, in particular, the evolution of low-energy excitations with temperature is analyzed, including the modification of pygmy structures. Both for the monopole and dipole modes, in the temperature range T=1-2 MeV additional transition strength appears at low energies because of thermal unblocking of single-particle orbitals close to the Fermi level. A concentration of dipole strength around 10 MeV excitation energy is predicted in $^{60,62}$Ni, where no low-energy excitations occur at zero temperature. The principal effect of finite temperature on low-energy strength that is already present at zero temperature, e.g. in $^{68}$Ni and $^{132}$Sn, is the spreading of this structure to even lower energy and the appearance of states that correspond to thermally unblocked transitions.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that finite temperature (T=1-2 MeV) induces additional low-energy multipole strength in nuclei via thermal unblocking of single-particle orbitals, and that this effect can create novel low-energy dipole modes in nuclei like $^{60,62}$Ni where no such excitations exist at zero temperature, while in nuclei with pre-existing pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) structure (e.g., $^{68}$Ni, $^{132}$Sn) the main effect is spreading of strength to even lower energies.

## Method

The authors formulated a fully self-consistent finite-temperature relativistic random phase approximation (FTRRPA) based on the DD-ME2 relativistic energy density functional, solving the RPA equations by diagonalization in the single-nucleon basis from finite-temperature relativistic mean-field (FTRMF) calculations. The configuration space included particle-hole, particle-particle, and hole-hole pairs, as well as configurations built from occupied positive-energy states and empty negative-energy states from the Dirac sea. Discrete transition strengths were averaged with a Lorentzian distribution of width $\Gamma = 1$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- For $^{60}$Ni at T=2 MeV: the main low-energy dipole peak is at 9.71 MeV, exhausting 1.54% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) sum rule
- For $^{62}$Ni at T=2 MeV: two low-energy dipole peaks at 9.78 MeV (1.2% TRK) and 10.03 MeV (1.5% TRK)
- For $^{132}$Sn at T=2 MeV: additional low-energy single-particle transitions appear in the interval E=2-7 MeV, with specific states at 3.75 MeV, 4.54 MeV, 4.92 MeV, and 6.12 MeV
- The main PDR peak in $^{132}$Sn shifts only slightly from 7.75 MeV at T=0 to 7.77 MeV at T=2 MeV
- For $^{56}$Ni at T=2 MeV: the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) strength distribution is lowered by approximately 1 MeV
- For $^{56}$Ni at T=2 MeV: the isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) main peak is lowered by approximately 2 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous finite-temperature RPA studies based on schematic interactions (Civitarese 1984, Vautherin 1984) and Skyrme effective interactions (Sagawa 1984) by implementing a fully self-consistent relativistic framework with medium-dependent meson-nucleon couplings. The results are relevant for astrophysical r-process nucleosynthesis calculations, as neutron capture rates computed with the Hauser-Feshbach model are sensitive to the fine structure of low-energy dipole transitions at finite temperature (Litvinova 2009). The predictions for PDR in $^{68}$Ni at zero temperature were recently confirmed experimentally (Wieland 2009), and the PDR in $^{132}$Sn was confirmed in Coulomb dissociation experiments (Adrich 2005).
