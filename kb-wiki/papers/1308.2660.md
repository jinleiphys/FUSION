# Chiral condensate at finite density using chiral Ward identity

> In order to study partial restoration of the chiral symmetry at finite density, we investigate the density corrections of the chiral condensate up to next-leading order of density expansion using the chiral Ward identity and an in-medium chiral perturbation theory. In our study, we assume that all the in-vacuum quantities for the pion, the nucleon and the pi N interaction are determined and focus on density expansion of the in-medium physical quantities. We perform diagrammatic analysis of the correlation functions which provide the in-medium chiral condensate. This density expansion scheme shows that medium effects to the chiral condensate beyond the linear density come from density corrections to the pi N sigma term as a result of the interactions between pion and nucleon in nuclear matter. We also discuss that higher density contributions beyond order of rho^2 cannot be fixed only by the in-vacuum pi N dynamics but we need NN two-body dynamics in vacuum to fix divergence appearing in the calculation of the rho^2 dependence of the chiral condensate with the pi N dynamics.

## Key claim

The authors show that the in-medium chiral condensate can be calculated up to next-to-leading order in density using the chiral Ward identity and an in-medium chiral perturbation theory, but they find that contributions beyond order \(\rho^{2}\) cannot be fixed solely by in-vacuum pion-nucleon dynamics; a nucleon-nucleon two-body contact interaction, which can be fixed in vacuum, is required to remove divergences appearing at \(\rho^{2}\) order.

## Method

The authors use the chiral Ward identity to relate the in-medium quark condensate to correlation functions of the axial vector current and pseudoscalar density in the soft limit. They evaluate these correlation functions diagrammatically using an in-medium chiral perturbation theory that expands in both chiral order and Fermi sea insertions, treating non-interacting Fermi gas of nucleons as the asymptotic state and assuming all in-vacuum quantities for the pion, nucleon, and \(\pi N\) interaction are already determined by experiment.

## Key numbers

* The leading order contribution to the in-medium chiral condensate ratio is \(\frac{ \langle \bar{u}u + \bar{d}d \rangle^*_{\rm LO}}{\langle \bar{u}u + \bar{d}d \rangle_0} = - \frac{\sigma_{\pi N}}{f_\pi^2 m_\pi^2} \rho \left( 1- \frac{3 k_F^2}{10 m_N^2} \right)\), using the sigma term \(\sigma_{\pi N} = 45\ \mathrm{MeV}\), the pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92.4\ \mathrm{MeV}\), and the pion mass \(m_\pi = 138\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The Fermi momentum at normal nuclear density is \(k_F = 270\ \mathrm{MeV}\), which is approximately \(2 m_\pi\).
* The linear density term includes a Fermi motion correction of \(1 - 3 k_F^2 / (10 m_N^2)\), which is an \(O(\rho^{5/3})\) correction.

## Context

This work extends the model-independent linear density relation for the in-medium quark condensate derived by Drukarev and Levin (1991) and Cohen (1991), and the sum rule approach using the chiral Ward identity by Jido, Hatsuda, and Kunihiro (1998). It provides a systematic framework to calculate higher density corrections beyond the linear approximation, using the in-medium chiral perturbation theory formulation of Oset (1999) and Meissner, Oller, and Wirzba (2002). The results enable more precise theoretical determination of the density dependence of the quark condensate, which is needed to interpret experimental signals of partial chiral symmetry restoration from pionic atom spectra and low-energy pion-nucleus scattering.
