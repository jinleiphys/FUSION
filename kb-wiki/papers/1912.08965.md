# Data-driven extraction of heavy quark diffusion in quark-gluon plasma

> Heavy quark production provides a unique probe of the quark-gluon plasma transport properties in heavy ion collisions. Experimental observables like the nuclear modification factor $R_{\rm AA}$ and elliptic anisotropy $v_{2}$ of heavy flavor mesons are sensitive to the heavy quark diffusion coefficient. There now exist an extensive set of such measurements, which allow a data-driven extraction of this coefficient. In this work, we make such an attempt within our recently developed heavy quark transport modeling framework (Langevin-transport with Gluon Radiation, LGR). A question of particular interest is the temperature dependence of the diffusion coefficient, for which we test a wide range of possibility and draw constraints by comparing relevant charm meson data with model results. We find that a relatively strong increase of diffusion coefficient from crossover temperature $T_c$ toward high temperature is preferred by data. We also make predictions for Bottom meson observables for further experimental tests.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a relatively strong increase of the heavy quark spatial diffusion constant from the crossover temperature \(T_c\) toward higher temperatures is preferred by experimental data, but this conclusion is hedged by the fact that only two optimized models (Model-A and Model-B) were identified from a linear ansatz \(2\pi TD_s(T) \approx \alpha(T/T_c) + \beta\), and the extracted temperature dependence still carries large uncertainty for \(T \gtrsim 2T_c\).

## Method

The authors employed a Langevin-transport with Gluon Radiation (LGR) framework to model heavy quark evolution in quark-gluon plasma, using a 3+1 dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamics for the medium background. They parameterized the spatial diffusion constant with a linear temperature dependence \(2\pi TD_s(T) = \alpha(T/T_c) + \beta\), scanned 25 combinations of \((\alpha, \beta)\) over a wide range (\(0 \le \alpha \le 9\), \(-8.5 \le \beta \le 4\)), and performed a \(\chi^2\) analysis comparing model predictions with experimental data for D-meson \(R_{\rm AA}\) and \(v_2\) from Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV and 5.02 TeV. Hadronization was treated via a dual approach combining fragmentation and heavy-light coalescence.

## Key numbers

* The two optimized models from the LGR framework are Model-A with \((\alpha, \beta) = (3, -1)\) and Model-B with \((\alpha, \beta) = (6.5, -5.5)\), both achieving total \(\chi^2/d.o.f \sim 1.10-1.11\).
* Model-A gave \(\chi^2/d.o.f = 1.09\) for \(R_{\rm AA}\) and 1.26 for \(v_2\); Model-B gave \(\chi^2/d.o.f = 1.15\) for \(R_{\rm AA}\) and 0.77 for \(v_2\).
* A small value \(2\pi TD_s \simeq (2\sim 4)\) is preferred near \(T_c\).
* For bottom quarks, a constant factor of 0.85 was used to relate the spatial diffusion constant to that of charm quarks: \(2\pi TD_s({\rm bottom}) = 0.85 \times 2\pi TD_s({\rm charm})\).

## Context

This work extends prior heavy quark transport modeling by the same group (Li and Liao, 2019) and other frameworks such as TAMU (He et al., 2013), PHSD (Song et al., 2016), LTB (Cao et al., 2016), POWLANG (Beraudo et al., 2011), BAMPS (Fochler et al., 2010), and CUJET3 (Xu et al., 2018). The data-driven extraction of the diffusion coefficient's temperature dependence enables comparison with lattice QCD calculations (Banerjee et al., 2012; Ding et al., 2012; Kaczmarek et al., 2014) and Bayesian analyses (LIDO, 2018), and provides predictions for bottom meson observables at LHC energies for future experimental tests.
