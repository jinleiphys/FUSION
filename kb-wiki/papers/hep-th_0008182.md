# A New Expansion of the Heisenberg Equation of Motion with Projection Operator

> We derive a new expansion of the Heisenberg equation of motion based on the projection operator method proposed by Shibata, Hashitsume and Shingū. In their projection operator method, a certain restriction is imposed on the initial state. As a result, one cannot prepare arbitrary initial states, for example a coherent state, to calculate the time development of quantum systems. In this paper, we generalize the projection operator method by relaxing this restriction. We explain our method in the case of a Hamiltonian both with and without explicit time dependence. Furthermore, we apply it to an exactly solvable model called the damped harmonic oscillator model and confirm the validity of our method.

## Key claim

The authors derive a new expansion of the Heisenberg equation of motion using the projection operator method of Shibata, Hashitsume, and Shingu, but without imposing the restriction \(PL_E = 0\) that limited previous formulations to initial states where the environment density matrix is an eigenstate of the environment Hamiltonian or diagonal in that basis.

## Method

The authors start from the Heisenberg equation and introduce generic projection operators \(P\) and \(Q\) satisfying \(P^2 = P\), \(Q = 1-P\), and \(PQ = QP = 0\). They derive a time-convolutionless (TCL) equation by solving for \(e^{iL(t-t_0)}Q\) and substituting back, obtaining an expression that avoids memory integrals. The key innovation is to relax the condition \(PL_E = 0\) used in prior work, instead using only the weaker relations \(QL_0 Q = QL_0\) and \(Q e^{iL_0 t} Q = Q e^{iL_0 t}\), which hold for the chosen projection operator \(P O = \mathrm{Tr}_E[\rho_E O]\). The formalism is developed for both time-independent and explicitly time-dependent Hamiltonians, and the resulting expansion is applied to the damped harmonic oscillator model to test its validity.

## Key numbers

* For the damped harmonic oscillator model, the exact time evolution of \(A_k^\dagger(t)\) is \(A_k^\dagger(t) = e^{i\Omega_k t} \left( \cosh(\Gamma_k t) A_k^\dagger(0) + \sinh(\Gamma_k t) B_k(0) \right)\), where \(\Omega_k\) and \(\Gamma_k\) are parameters of the Hamiltonian.
* The first-order perturbative expansion from the new method yields \(\frac{d}{dt}\langle A_k^\dagger(t) \rangle = i\Omega_k \langle A_k^\dagger(t) \rangle + \Gamma_k^2 t \langle A_k^\dagger(t) \rangle + e^{i\Omega_k t} \Gamma_k \langle B_k(0) \rangle\), which matches the exact equation expanded to lowest order in \(\Gamma_k\).
* Including the next-order term gives \(\frac{d}{dt}\langle A_k^\dagger(t) \rangle = i\Omega_k \langle A_k^\dagger(t) \rangle + \Gamma_k^2 t \langle A_k^\dagger(t) \rangle + e^{i\Omega_k t} \Gamma_k \left( 1 - \frac{1}{2} \Gamma_k^2 t^2 \right) \langle B_k(0) \rangle\), which matches the exact equation expanded to the appropriate order in \(\Gamma_k\).
* The previous projection operator method (Shibata, Hashitsume, Shingu) gives \(\frac{d}{dt}\langle A_k^\dagger(t) \rangle = i\Omega_k \langle A_k^\dagger(t) \rangle + \Gamma_k^2 t \langle A_k^\dagger(t) \rangle + \Gamma_k \langle B_k(0) \rangle\), which lacks the factor \(e^{i\Omega_k t}\) and is only consistent when \(\langle B_k(0) \rangle = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the projection operator method of Shibata, Hashitsume, and Shingu (1980s) by removing the restriction \(PL_E = 0\) that prevented the use of general initial states such as coherent states. The new expansion enables the study of time evolution with arbitrary initial density matrices that factorize into system and environment parts, which is important for describing phase transitions and other phenomena requiring coherent states. The method is compared to the Uchiyama-Shibata projection operator method (recent, 1999), which imposes different restrictions on the operator whose time evolution is calculated rather than on the projection operator itself.
