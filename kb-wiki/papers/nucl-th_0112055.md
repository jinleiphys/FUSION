# Coherent Interactions in Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

> At the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) at Brookhaven National Laboratory and at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) at CERN, particles will be produced in coherent and diffractive nuclear interactions. In extremely peripheral nuclear collisions (b$>$2R), coherent interactions occur at very high rates and are dominated by photon-Pomeron or photon-meson processes. In these reactions, the photon and the Pomeron/meson from the electromagnetic and nuclear fields couple coherently to all nucleons. The rates for photonuclear interactions are roughly two orders of magnitude larger than for two-photon interactions at comparable center-of-mass energies.

## Key claim

Coherent photonuclear (photon-Pomeron/meson) interactions in ultra-peripheral nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC and LHC have cross sections roughly two orders of magnitude larger than two-photon interactions at comparable center-of-mass energies, but only when the impact parameter exceeds twice the nuclear radius (b > 2R) and the momentum transfer is small enough (Q < 1/R) for the fields to couple coherently to all nucleons.

## Method

The author uses the Weizsacker-Williams method of equivalent photons to compute the photon flux from relativistic heavy ions, integrating over impact parameters with a hadronic interaction probability calculated from a Glauber model. For vector meson production, the photonuclear cross section is derived from the Vector Meson Dominance Model, using measured gamma-nucleon cross sections from HERA and fixed-target experiments as input to a Glauber model for the nuclear cross section. Two-photon production cross sections are calculated from the two-photon width and mass of the meson, with a nuclear form factor including a skin thickness parameter of 0.7 fm.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC (Au+Au, gamma=108.4), coherent vector meson production cross sections: rho0 = 590 mb, omega = 59 mb, phi = 39 mb, J/Psi = 0.29 mb
- At LHC (Pb+Pb, gamma=2940), coherent vector meson production cross sections: rho0 = 5200 mb, omega = 490 mb, phi = 460 mb, J/Psi = 32 mb
- Coherent vector meson cross sections are roughly 10% of the total inelastic hadronic Au+Au cross section at RHIC, rising to 50% for Pb+Pb at LHC
- Two-photon production cross sections are between one and two orders of magnitude smaller than photonuclear production up to photon energies of 10^4 GeV
- Probability of mutual Coulomb dissociation reaches about 35% in a grazing Au+Au collision at RHIC
- Cross sections for vector meson production in coincidence with nuclear breakup: rho0 = 42 mb, omega = 4 mb, phi = 3 mb, J/Psi = 0.29 mb in Au+Au at RHIC (reduced by roughly a factor of 10 compared with no breakup)
- Angular deflection of ions: theta ~ 0.175/(gamma * A^(4/3)), which is a few microradians at RHIC and a few tenths of a microradian at LHC

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for coherent electromagnetic interactions in heavy-ion collisions, building on the Weizsacker-Williams method and Vector Meson Dominance Model applied to nucleus-nucleus systems. The calculations use input from HERA and fixed-target experiments on gamma-nucleon cross sections, and the formalism for Coulomb dissociation probabilities follows Baltz, Chasman, and White (BCW). The paper enables experimental studies at RHIC (where the STAR collaboration has already demonstrated feasibility) and future measurements at LHC, including novel phenomena such as two-source interference in vector meson production and multiple excitations in strong electromagnetic fields that are not accessible in e+e- or eA collisions.
