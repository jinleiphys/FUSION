# $π^+π^-$ Emission from Coherent $ρ^0$ Propagation in Nuclei

> Cross sections for the $π^+π^-$ events arising due to the decay of $ρ^0$ meson have been calculated. This rho meson is considered to produce coherently in the $(p,p^\prime)$ reaction on a nucleus. The distorted wave functions for the continuum particles and the rho meson propagator are described by the eikonal form. The sensitivity of the cross section on the pion emission angle as well as on the beam energy has been investigated. The cross sections for the $ρ^0$ decaying inside and outside the nucleus are compared. In addition, the coherent and incoherent contributions to the cross section due to $ρ^0$ inside and outside decay amplitudes are presented. The initial and final state interactions for this reaction have been studied. The nuclear medium effect on the $ρ^0$ propagation through the nucleus is also explored.

# π⁺π⁻ Emission from Coherent ρ⁰ Propagation in Nuclei

## Key claim

Cross sections for π⁺π⁻ events from coherent ρ⁰ production in (p,p′) reactions on ¹²C and ²⁰⁸Pb are calculated using a Glauber-based eikonal model, with the ρ⁰ propagation restricted to the forward direction only. The calculation shows that the interference between inside-nucleus and outside-nucleus decay amplitudes is important, and that the ρ⁰-nucleus interaction itself attenuates the peak cross section by only about 8.8% for carbon and 12% for lead, contrary to the much larger effects from initial and final state interactions.

## Method

The T-matrix for the reaction factorizes into production, propagation, and decay of the ρ⁰ meson, with the ρ⁰ produced coherently in the (p,p′) reaction on a nucleus. Distorted wave functions for protons and pions, as well as the ρ⁰ propagator, are described in eikonal form using optical potentials constructed via the "tρ" approximation. The ρ⁰ propagation is restricted to the forward direction, and the cross section for the energy transfer (E_p − E_p′) distribution is calculated for ¹²C and ²⁰⁸Pb targets at beam energies of 2.5, 3.0, and 3.5 GeV, with the p′ emission angle fixed at 1°.

## Key numbers

- Peak cross section for ¹²C at 2.5 GeV beam energy and θ_π⁺ = 25°: 82.5 μb/(GeV² sr³) at E_p − E_p′ = 1.64 GeV
- Peak cross section for ¹²C at 3.5 GeV beam energy and θ_π⁺ = 25°: 0.75 mb/(GeV² sr³) at E_p − E_p′ = 1.74 GeV
- Peak cross section for ¹²C at 3.0 GeV beam energy and θ_π⁺ = 25°: 0.4 mb/(GeV² sr³) at E_p − E_p′ = 1.7 GeV
- For ¹²C at 3.5 GeV and θ_π⁺ = 20°: inside-nucleus decay peak 0.12 mb/(GeV² sr³) at E_p − E_p′ = 1.98 GeV; outside-nucleus decay peak ~1.46 mb/(GeV² sr³) at E_p − E_p′ = 2.12 GeV (outside dominates by factor ~12)
- For ¹²C at 3.5 GeV: coherent contribution peak ~2.17 mb/(GeV² sr³) at E_p − E_p′ = 2.08 GeV; incoherent contribution peak ~1.57 mb/(GeV² sr³) at E_p − E_p′ = 2.12 GeV (coherent exceeds incoherent by factor ~1.4)
- For ²⁰⁸Pb at 3.5 GeV and θ_π⁺ = 15°: inside and outside decay peaks both ~65 μb/(GeV² sr³); coherent contribution peak 0.22 mb/(GeV² sr³) at E_p − E_p′ = 2.19 GeV; incoherent contribution peak 0.11 mb/(GeV² sr³) at E_p − E_p′ = 2.2 GeV (interference enhances by factor 2)
- For ¹²C at 3.5 GeV: plane wave peak 25.54 mb/(GeV² sr³) at E_p − E_p′ = 2.02 GeV; initial and final state interactions together reduce peak by factor ~10.7 to 2.38 mb/(GeV² sr³) at E_p − E_p′ = 2.07 GeV; ρ⁰-nucleus interaction further reduces peak by 8.8% to 2.17 mb/(GeV² sr³)
- For ²⁰⁸Pb at 3.5 GeV: initial state interaction alone reduces main peak by factor ~179; projectile and ejectile distortions together reduce by factor ~379; ρ⁰-nucleus interaction reduces cross section by 12%

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of in-medium vector meson properties to coherent ρ⁰ production in (p,p′) reactions, complementing earlier calculations using the Vector Dominance Model by Kondratyuk et al. (2002) and the Resonance Model for ρN scattering amplitudes. The approach is related to the semiclassical BUU transport model calculations by Effenberger et al. (1999) for dilepton production and the Intranuclear Cascade model by Golubeva et al. (2001), but uses a fully quantum-mechanical Glauber framework. The results enable comparison with planned experiments at TJNAF (e⁺e⁻ radiation), INS Tokyo (π⁺π⁻ emission), and existing KEK-PS E325 data on e⁺e⁻ yields in p+A reactions, providing predictions for the energy transfer spectra where medium modifications of the ρ⁰ should be observable.
