# The three-dimensional distribution of quarks in momentum space

> We present the distribution of unpolarized quarks in a transversely polarized proton in three-dimensional momentum space. Our results are based on consistent extractions of the unpolarized and Sivers transverse momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs).

## Key claim

The paper presents the first fully consistent extraction of the three-dimensional momentum-space distribution of unpolarized quarks in a transversely polarized proton, where both the unpolarized and Sivers transverse momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs) are obtained using the same TMD framework and evolution formalism, rather than combining separate extractions with different assumptions.

## Method

The authors extract the Sivers TMD \(f_{1T}^{\perp}\) by fitting experimental data for single transverse-spin asymmetries from Semi-Inclusive Deep-Inelastic Scattering (SIDIS) and vector-boson production, using the same Collins-Soper-Sterman (CSS) TMD framework and next-to-leading-logarithmic (NLL) evolution as the Pavia17 extraction of the unpolarized TMD \(f_1\). The Sivers function is parametrized at an initial scale \(Q_0 = 1\) GeV in terms of its first \(k_T\)-moment and a nonperturbative transverse-momentum shape, with the unpolarized TMD and fragmentation functions taken from the Pavia17 fit. The fit uses the bootstrap method with 200 replicas of 125 data points from HERMES, COMPASS, JLab, and STAR, including only the \(x\) projection of SIDIS asymmetries and the rapidity projection of STAR data.

## Key numbers

- The fit includes 125 data points: 30 from HERMES, 82 from COMPASS (32 from 2009 analysis, 50 from 2017 analysis), 6 from JLab, and 7 from STAR.
- The initial scale for the Sivers function parametrization is \(Q_0 = 1\) GeV.
- The \(b_*\)-prescription uses \(b_{\mathrm{bmax}} = 2 e^{-\gamma_E} \; \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\) and \(b_{\mathrm{min}} = 2 e^{-\gamma_E} / Q\).
- The total number of free parameters in the fit is 17.
- Data selection cuts include \(Q^2 > 1.4\) GeV\(^2\), \(0.20 < z < 0.74\), and \(P_{hT} < \min[0.2Q,\,0.7Qz] + 0.5\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Pavia17 extraction of the unpolarized TMD (Bacchetta et al., 2017) by consistently adding the Sivers TMD within the same CSS-based TMD framework, at variance with earlier Sivers extractions such as Anselmino et al. (2005, 2008) and Echevarria et al. (2014) that used different formalisms. The result provides a benchmark for future multi-dimensional quark distribution studies at facilities like Jefferson Lab and the Electron-Ion Collider, and enables comparisons with later consistent extractions such as Echevarria et al. (2020) and Bury et al. (2021).
