# Interaction Matrix Element Fluctuations in Ballistic Quantum Dots: Dynamical Effects

> We study matrix element fluctuations of the two-body screened Coulomb interaction and of the one-body surface charge potential in ballistic quantum dots, comparing behavior in actual chaotic billiards with analytic results previously obtained in a normalized random wave model. We find that the matrix element variances in actual chaotic billiards typically exceed by a factor of 3 or 4 the predictions of the random wave model, for dot sizes commonly used in experiments. We discuss dynamical effects that are responsible for this enhancement. These dynamical effects have an even more striking effect on the covariance, which changes sign when compared with random wave predictions. In billiards that do not display hard chaos, an even larger enhancement of matrix element fluctuations is possible. These enhanced fluctuations have implications for peak spacing statistics and spectral scrambling for quantum dots in the Coulomb blockade regime.

## Key claim

The matrix element variances of the two-body screened Coulomb interaction and one-body surface charge potential in actual chaotic billiards exceed the predictions of the normalized random wave model by a factor of 3 or 4 for dot sizes commonly used in experiments (kL between 30 and 70), and this enhancement arises from dynamical effects that are not captured by the random wave model.

## Method

The authors numerically computed single-electron wave functions for modified quarter-stadium billiard geometries (with a parabolic bump to eliminate bouncing-ball modes) using a plane wave method with singular value decomposition, for Dirichlet and Neumann boundary conditions. They evaluated the variance of diagonal two-body interaction matrix elements vαβ (modeled as a contact interaction) and one-body surface charge potential matrix elements, averaging over energy windows of constant momentum width ΔkL = 10 for kL values between 30 and 70. The numerical results were compared with analytic predictions from a normalized random wave model and with semiclassical calculations incorporating contributions from trajectories that bounce off the billiard boundaries.

## Key numbers

- Matrix element variances in actual chaotic billiards exceed random wave model predictions by a factor of 3 or 4 for dot sizes with kL between 30 and 70 (corresponding to approximately 150-800 electrons in the dot)
- For the modified quarter-stadium billiard with parameter a=0.25, the enhancement factor for vαβ variance over the random wave prediction is approximately 3-5 in the range 30 ≤ kL ≤ 70
- The Lyapunov exponent λ takes values 0.69 ≤ λTB ≤ 0.74 for a=1.00 and 0.55 ≤ λTB ≤ 0.56 for a=0.25 (with bump size 0.1 ≤ s ≤ 0.2)
- The classical correlation decay rate λ*TB is 0.15-0.20 for a=1.00 and 0.095-0.13 for a=0.25
- Adding one-bounce effects to the random wave correlator increases the predicted vαβ variance by approximately 30-40% in the energy range of interest
- The dynamical contribution to the variance appears to follow a power law ~ (kL)^{-1.15}, not the expected (kL)^{-2} behavior

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analytic results using a normalized random wave model (Kaplan and Alhassid, 2008) for interaction matrix element fluctuations in ballistic quantum dots, and addresses discrepancies between theory and experiments on peak spacing distributions in quantum dots (Patel et al., 1998; Luscher et al., 2001). The enhanced fluctuations have implications for peak spacing statistics and spectral scrambling in the Coulomb blockade regime, and may help explain the absence of bimodality in peak spacing distributions that cannot be accounted for by the exchange interaction term of the universal Hamiltonian (Kurland et al., 2000; Aleiner et al., 2002). The results also show that in billiards with mixed dynamics or marginally stable bouncing-ball modes, even larger (order of magnitude) enhancement of fluctuations is possible.
