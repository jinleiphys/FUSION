# Low-energy three-body charge transfer reactions with Coulomb interaction in the final state

> Three-body charge transfer reactions with Coulomb interaction in the final state are considered in the framework of coordinate-space integro-differential Faddeev-Hahn-type equations within two- and six-state close coupling approximations. The method is employed to study direct muon transfer in low-energy collisions of the muonic hydrogen H$_μ$ by helium (He$^{++}$) and lithium (Li$^{+++}$) nuclei. The experimentally observed isotopic dependence is reproduced.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a full quantum-mechanical Faddeev-Hahn-type approach, incorporating the final-state Coulomb interaction directly into the equations, can reproduce the experimentally observed isotopic dependence of low-energy muon-transfer rates for collisions of muonic hydrogen with helium and lithium nuclei, but only within a two- or six-state close-coupling approximation that may not be fully converged.

## Method

The authors solve coordinate-space integro-differential Faddeev-Hahn-type equations for the three-body system (two heavy nuclei and one muon) by expanding the wave function components in hydrogen-like bound states on each center (initial and final channels). After partial-wave projection for total angular momentum L=0, the resulting coupled one-dimensional integro-differential equations are discretized using a trapezoidal rule for integrals and a three-point rule for derivatives, then solved by Gauss elimination. The final-state Coulomb repulsion between clusters is included as a distortion potential U_C in the equations, ensuring correct asymptotic Coulomb wave behavior in the transfer channel.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of muon-transfer reactions beyond the two-state molecular Born-Oppenheimer approximation (Matveev 1973, W 1992) and a semiclassical Faddeev-type model (Sultanov 1999) by providing a full quantum-mechanical solution of Faddeev-Hahn-type equations with explicit final-state Coulomb interaction. The method reproduces the experimentally observed isotope effect (decreasing transfer rate from ¹H to ²H) for helium and lithium targets, which had been measured for heavier nuclei like oxygen, neon, and argon (Mulhauser 1993, Schell 1993). The approach is expected to be applicable to heavier targets with Z₁ ≥ 4, and also to electron rearrangement collisions such as H(1s) + He⁺⁺ → H⁺ + He⁺(1s) (Hose 1997).
