# Rapidity regulators in the semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan processes

> We study the semi-inclusive limit of the deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan (DY) processes in soft collinear effective theory. In this regime so-called threshold logarithms must be resummed to render perturbation theory well behaved. Part of this resummation occurs via the Dokshitzer, Gribov, Lipatov, Altarelli, Parisi (DGLAP) equation, which at threshold contains a large logarithm that calls into question the convergence of the anomalous dimension. We demonstrate here that the problematic logarithm is related to rapidity divergences, and by introducing a rapidity regulator can be tamed. We show that resumming the rapidity logarithms allows us to reproduce the standard DGLAP running at threshold as long as a set of potentially large non-perturbative logarithms are absorbed into the definition of the parton distribution function (PDF). These terms could, in turn, explain the steep fall-off of the PDF in the endpoint. We then go on to show that the resummation of rapidity divergences does not change the standard threshold resummation in DY, nor do our results depend on the rapidity regulator we choose to use.

# Rapidity regulators in the semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan processes

## Key claim

The large logarithm that appears in the DGLAP anomalous dimension at threshold is related to rapidity divergences and can be tamed by introducing a rapidity regulator, but only if a set of potentially large non-perturbative logarithms are absorbed into the definition of the parton distribution function (PDF). The resummation of rapidity divergences does not change the standard threshold resummation in Drell-Yan, and the results are independent of the choice of rapidity regulator.

## Method

The authors use soft collinear effective theory (SCET) to study deep inelastic scattering (DIS) and Drell-Yan (DY) processes in the semi-inclusive threshold region where Bjorken-x approaches one. They match QCD onto SCET_I at the hard scale Q, then onto SCET_II at the intermediate scale, introducing an eta regulator to handle rapidity divergences that arise when separating collinear and soft modes. They calculate the collinear and soft functions to one-loop order for both DIS and DY, perform zero-bin subtractions, and verify consistency through the renormalization group. They also repeat the calculations using an alternative delta regulator to demonstrate regulator independence.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order nonsinglet anomalous dimension in Mellin moment space is given by gamma_ns^(0) = -(alpha_s(mu)/(4 pi))^(n+1)[A_0 ln(nu_c nu_s/(Q^2/bar(N))) - B_0], where A_0 = 16/3 approximately 5.3 and B_0 = 4 at order n=0
- The collinear rapidity scale nu_c is approximately Q, and the soft rapidity scale nu_s is approximately Q/bar(N), where bar(N) = N e^(gamma_E)
- The threshold region where dynamical enhancement remains effective extends down to moderate values tau approximately 0.2, where tau=1 represents the true endpoint
- The one-loop collinear counterterm contains a 1/epsilon term with coefficient (3/4 + ln(nu/(bar(n) dot p))) multiplied by alpha_s C_F/pi
- The one-loop soft counterterm contains a 1/epsilon term with coefficient [(1/z)_+ - ln(nu/Q) delta(z)] multiplied by alpha_s C_F/pi

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of DIS in the endpoint region (Fleming, 2012) by demonstrating that the rapidity-separated PDF approach also reproduces the standard threshold resummation in Drell-Yan processes. It addresses the problematic large logarithm in the DGLAP anomalous dimension at threshold identified by Moch (2004), showing it arises from rapidity divergences rather than being a genuine perturbative issue. The paper uses the rapidity regulator formalism developed by Chiu (2011, 2012) and compares with the alternative approach of Hoang (2015) for zero-bin subtractions, finding that the DIS results agree between methods but DY requires the Hoang approach for consistency. This work enables a consistent treatment of threshold resummation in both DIS and DY within the SCET framework, with implications for PDF extraction at large x and for precision calculations of Higgs boson and beyond-standard-model production at hadron colliders.
