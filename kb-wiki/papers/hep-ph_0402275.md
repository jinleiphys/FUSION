# J/psi Absorption in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We present a new calculation of the pi-J/psi dissociation cross sections within the Constituent Quark-Meson Model recently introduced. To discuss the absorption of J/psi in heavy-ion collisions, we assume the J/psi to be produced inside a thermalized pion gas, as discussed by Bjorken, and introduce the corrections due to absorption by nuclear matter as well. We fit the absorption length of the J/psi to the data obtained at the CERN SPS by the NA50 Collaboration for Pb-Pb collisions. Collisions of lower centrality allow us to determine the temperature and the energy density of the pion gas. For both these quantities we find values close to those indicated by lattice gauge calculations for the transition to a quark-gluon plasma. A simple extrapolation to more central collisions, which takes into account the increase of the energy deposited due to the increased nucleon flux, fails to reproduce the break in J/psi absorption indicated by NA50, thus lending support to the idea that an unconfined quark-gluon phase may have been produced. This conclusion could be sharpened by analysing in a similar way, as a function of centrality, other observables such as strange particle production.

## Key claim

The authors test whether J/psi suppression in heavy-ion collisions can be explained entirely by hadronic absorption in a thermalized pion gas, and find that a simple extrapolation of the pion-gas model to more central collisions fails to reproduce the sharp break in the NA50 data, lending support to the idea that an unconfined quark-gluon plasma phase may have been produced.

## Method

The authors compute pi-J/psi dissociation cross sections into D, D*, and D*D* final states using tree-level diagrams within the Constituent Quark-Meson Model, with coupling constants determined from that model. They then take a thermal average of these cross sections over a perfect Bose gas of pions with vanishing chemical potential, and fit the resulting J/psi absorption length to NA50 Pb-Pb collision data as a function of the linear size of the fireball, including corrections for nuclear absorption using a cross section of 4.3 mb from p-A collisions.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear absorption cross section: sigma_nucl = 4.3 +/- 0.6 mb, obtained from p-A collisions
- Best-fit fireball temperature (including nuclear absorption): T = 225 +/- 15 MeV
- Corresponding energy density at T = 225 MeV: epsilon = 0.32 GeV/fm^3
- Pion number density at T = 225 MeV: rho = 0.0828 fm^{-3}
- Thermal average absorption rate at T = 225 MeV: <rho sigma>_T = 0.070 fm^{-1}
- Energy threshold for pi-J/psi reaction: E_pi = 800 MeV
- Coupling constant g_{J/psi D D*} = 4.05 GeV^{-1}
- Coupling constant g_{J D D pi} = 234 GeV^{-2} (with form factor scale Lambda_2 = 0.8 GeV)
- Coupling constant g_{pi D D*} = 13 (with form factor scale Lambda_1 = 1.1 GeV)
- For central Pb-Pb collisions: A(b=0)/S(b=0) = 1.5 fm^{-2}
- Energy density estimate for Pb-Pb with L = 5 fm: epsilon = 0.95 GeV/fm^3 * (2 fm / ct)
- Energy density estimate for Pb-Pb with L = 2R = 13 fm: epsilon = 1.35 GeV/fm^3 * (2 fm / ct)

## Context

This work extends the Constituent Quark-Meson Model introduced by the same authors (Maiani et al., 2004) to compute J/psi absorption cross sections, and applies it to the Bjorken picture of heavy-ion collisions (Bjorken, 1983). The results are compared with previous calculations using SU(4) symmetry, Vector Meson Dominance, and QCD Sum Rules (Rapp et al., 2003; Cina et al., various). The analysis uses NA50 Pb-Pb collision data (NA50 Collaboration, 1999) and p-A absorption cross sections (NA50, 2003). The temperature found (225 MeV) is compared with lattice QCD critical temperatures (Karsch, 2002) of about 180 MeV for n_f = 2 and 260 MeV for SU(3) gauge theory. The failure of the hadronic model to reproduce the break in the NA50 data at l > 5 fm suggests the need for a quark-gluon plasma interpretation, and the authors propose that similar analysis of strange particle production as a function of centrality could sharpen this conclusion.
