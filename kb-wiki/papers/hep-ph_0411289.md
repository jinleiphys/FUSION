# Finite-Temperature Yang-Mills Theory in Landau Gauge

> The gluon and ghost propagators in Landau Gauge Yang-Mills Theory are investigated. Self-consistent solutions are obtained from their equations of motion above and below the presumed phase transition. Gluon confinement is manifest in these solutions and can be read off the infrared behavior of the gluon and ghost propagators. Confinement prevails below the presumed phase transition. Above and in the infinite temperature limit, a qualitative change is observed: The chromoelectric sector exhibits screening, while long-range chromomagnetic interactions, mediated by soft modes, are still observed. At least part of the gluon spectrum is still confined. These findings agree with corresponding lattice results.

# Finite-Temperature Yang-Mills Theory in Landau Gauge

## Key claim
The gluon and ghost propagators in Landau gauge Yang-Mills theory show that gluon confinement persists at all temperatures, including above the phase transition and in the infinite-temperature limit, based on self-consistent solutions of Dyson-Schwinger equations. The transverse (chromomagnetic) gluon remains confined at all temperatures, while the longitudinal (chromoelectric) sector exhibits screening above the phase transition, yet at least part of the gluon spectrum is still confined.

## Method
The authors solved a truncated set of Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) for the gluon and ghost propagators in Landau gauge pure Yang-Mills theory at four temperature regimes: T=0, T<T_c, T>T_c, and T→∞. Calculations at T<T_c used a toroidal discretized space-time, while the other regimes were treated in the continuum; for T→∞ the theory becomes dimensionally reduced to a 3d Yang-Mills theory plus an adjoint Higgs field, where the transverse gluon dressing Z corresponds to the chromomagnetic 3d gluon and the longitudinal dressing H corresponds to the chromoelectric Higgs.

## Key numbers
- The ghost dressing function diverges at zero momentum at all temperatures, indicating long-range forces persist even at T→∞.
- Below the phase transition, the transverse gluon propagator satisfies the confinement criterion lim_{q→0} D(q^2)=0 and becomes steeper in the infrared with increasing temperature.
- In the high-temperature phase, the transverse gluon shows no qualitative difference from the confined phase, with surprisingly small temperature dependence.
- The longitudinal gluon dressing H at T<T_c becomes shallower with temperature in the infrared but still exhibits confinement; at T>T_c and T→∞, H shows screening and behaves similar to a massive particle.

## Context
This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger approach to finite-temperature Yang-Mills theory, building on prior zero-temperature DSE studies by Fischer (2002) and the authors' own work at T<T_c (Gruter, 2004), T>T_c (Maas, PhD thesis), and T→∞ (Maas, 2004). The results agree with corresponding lattice calculations by Cucchieri (2001, 2003) and confirm that the Gribov-Zwanziger confinement scenario applies at all temperatures. The findings demonstrate that the high-temperature phase is far from trivial and may have significant impact on thermodynamic properties near the phase transition and on experimentally accessible observables.
