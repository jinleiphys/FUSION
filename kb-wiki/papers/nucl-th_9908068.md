# Constraints on vector meson photoproduction spin observables

> Extraction of spin observables from vector meson photoproduction on a nucleon target is described. Starting from density matrix elements in the vector meson's rest frame, we transform to spin observables in the photon-nucleon c.m. frame. Several constraints on the transformed density matrix and on the spin observables follow from requiring that the angular distribution and the density matrix be positive definite. A set of constraints that are required in order to extract meaningful spin observables from forthcoming data are enunciated.

## Key claim

The paper formulates a procedure for extracting meaningful spin observables from vector meson photoproduction data, but the key hedge is that the vector meson's vector polarization cannot be measured from the decay angular distribution, only the tensor polarizations can be extracted; furthermore, the authors demonstrate that some older data (Aachen et al., 1968) violate basic positivity constraints and must be rejected.

## Method

The authors start from density matrix elements measured in the vector meson rest frame, transform them to the photon-nucleon center-of-mass frame using the relative velocity vector of the two decay mesons, and then project out the tensor polarizations \(T^V_{20}, T^V_{21}, T^V_{22}\) using spherical harmonics. They derive linear constraints from requiring the decay angular distribution to be positive definite and quadratic constraints from Schwarz inequalities and eigenvalue conditions on the spin density matrix, then test these constraints against published Aachen et al. data for \(\gamma + p \rightarrow \rho^0 + p\).

## Key numbers

- Four cases of the Aachen et al. data were rejected because they did not satisfy the positivity constraints.
- For a realistic case from Aachen et al. at \(E_\gamma = 3\) GeV and \(\Theta = 70^\circ\), the tensor polarizations are \(T_{20} = -0.72\), \(T_{21} = -0.21\), \(T_{22} = 0.19\).
- The linear constraint \(1 - \sqrt{2} T_{20} \ge 0\) follows from evaluating the decay angular distribution at \(\bar{\theta} = 0\).
- The linear constraint \(1 + \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} T_{20} \ge 0\) follows from evaluating at \(\bar{\theta} = \pi/2, \bar{\phi} = \pi/4\).
- The quadratic constraint \(9 (P^V_y)^2 + 12 (T^V_{21})^2 \le 8 (1 - \sqrt{2} T^V_{20}) (1 + \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} T^V_{20})\) follows from the Schwarz inequality \(|\rho_{10}|^2 \le \rho_{11} \rho_{00}\).
- The quadratic constraint \(9 (P^V_y)^2 + 12 (T^V_{21})^2 \le 4 (1 - \sqrt{2} T^V_{20}) (1 + \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} T^V_{20} - \sqrt{3} T^V_{22})\) follows from the condition \(x_2 \ge 0\) on the eigenvalues of the density matrix.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis by Kloet, Chiang, and Tabakin (1998) on the angular distribution of pseudoscalar decay mesons from vector meson photoproduction, and builds on constraints derived by Daboul (1967) using Schwarz inequalities and by Minnaert (1966) using positivity of the density matrix eigenvalues. The paper enables the analysis of forthcoming data from Thomas Jefferson Laboratory with polarized photon beams, where the derived constraints should be incorporated directly into the fitting procedure to ensure that extracted spin observables satisfy basic physical requirements.
