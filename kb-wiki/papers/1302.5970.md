# Next-to-leading order thermal photon production in a weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma

> We compute the next-to-leading order O(g) correction to the thermal photon production rate in a QCD plasma. The NLO contributions can be expressed in terms of gauge invariant condensates on the light cone, which are amenable to novel sum rules and Euclidean techniques. We expect these technologies to be generalizable to other NLO calculations. For the phenomenologically interesting value of alpha_s=0.3, the NLO correction represents a 20% increase and has a functional form similar to the LO result.

## Key claim

The next-to-leading order (NLO) O(g) correction to the thermal photon production rate in a weakly coupled QCD plasma is computed; for the phenomenologically interesting value of alpha_s = 0.3, the NLO correction represents a 20% increase and has a functional form similar to the leading-order result, though the partial NLO corrections from different kinematic regions are large and of both signs, nearly canceling in the full result.

## Method

The photon production rate is computed at NLO by summing contributions from three distinct kinematic regions: the soft region, the collinear region, and the semi-collinear region. The soft region contributions are evaluated using sum rules derived from analytic properties of HTL-resummed propagators and vertices, while the collinear region requires resummation of ladder diagrams via a Schrodinger-type equation to account for the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect, with NLO corrections arising from an O(g) shift in the asymptotic mass m_infinity and a one-loop O(g) correction to the soft scattering kernel C(q_perp). The semi-collinear region, where the quark momentum scaling satisfies p^+ ~ T, p^- ~ gT, and p_perp ~ sqrt(g)T, contributes at NLO due to a cancellation between diagrams that renders this phase space region O(g) relative to leading order.

## Key numbers

- For alpha_s = 0.3, the NLO correction represents a 20% increase over the leading-order result.
- The leading-order rate is proportional to A(k) = 2 alpha_EM (m_infinity/k) n_F(k) sum_s d_R q_s^2, where m_infinity = g^2 T^2 C_F/4 and for QCD with uds quarks equals 4 alpha_EM n_F(k) g^2 T^2/(3k).
- The parameter kappa = m_infinity/m_D^2 = 3 C_F/[4(C_A + T_R N_f)] = 2/(6+N_f) for QCD.
- The hard region function C_hard(k/T) is approximated by (1/2) ln(2k/T) + 0.041 T/k - 0.3615 + 1.01 e^{-1.35 k/T} for 0.2 < k/T.
- The collinear region function C_coll^LO(k/T, kappa) is approximated by (1/sqrt(kappa))[0.316 ln(12.18+T/k)/(k/T)^{3/2} + 0.0768(k/T)/sqrt(1+k/(16.27 T))] for 0.2 < k/T < 50.

## Context

This work extends the leading-order calculation of thermal photon production by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2001), who computed the complete LO rate including LPM resummation, and follows the NLO calculation of heavy quark diffusion by Caron-Huot (2008), which found very large NLO corrections. The NLO photon rate is simpler than the heavy quark diffusion calculation because hard modes experience soft modes at lightlike separations, enabling the use of analyticity and Euclidean methods; this technical development makes it more likely that other transport coefficients, such as shear viscosity, can be computed beyond leading order with reasonable effort.
