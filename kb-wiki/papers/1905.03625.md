# Critical end points in (2+1)-flavor QCD with imaginary chemical potential

> We present here the results from an ongoing determination of the critical quark mass in simulations of (2+1)-flavor QCD with an imaginary chemical potential. Studies with unimproved actions found the existence of a critical quark mass value at which the crossover transition ends on a second order phase transition and becomes first order for smaller values of the quark mass for the case of both vanishing and imaginary chemical potential. We use the Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) action and perform calculations in the Roberge-Weiss (RW) plane, where the value of the critical mass is expected to be largest. The lowest quark mass value used in our simulation corresponds to the pion mass $m_π$, down to $40$ MeV. Contrary to calculations performed with unimproved actions we find no evidence for the occurrence of first order transitions at the smallest quark mass values explored so far. Moreover we also show that the chiral observables are sensitive to the RW transition. Our results also indicate that the RW transition and chiral transition could coincide in the chiral limit.

## Key claim

Using the Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) action, the authors find no evidence for a first-order phase transition at the Roberge-Weiss endpoint for light quark masses down to a pion mass of 40 MeV, contrary to earlier studies with unimproved staggered fermions that had found first-order transitions at similar or larger pion masses.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations of (2+1)-flavor QCD with the HISQ action and a tree-level improved Symanzik gauge action on lattices with temporal extent Nτ=4 and spatial extents Nσ=8, 12, 16, 24, and 32. They set the imaginary chemical potential to μ/T=π/3 (the Roberge-Weiss plane) and varied the light-to-strange quark mass ratio m_l/m_s from 1/27 (physical) down to 1/320, corresponding to pion masses from 135 MeV down to 40 MeV. Finite-size scaling analyses of the order parameter (absolute value of the imaginary part of the Polyakov loop), its susceptibility, and the Binder cumulant were performed using universal 3-d Z(2) scaling functions.

## Key numbers

- Lowest quark mass ratio used: m_l/m_s = 1/320, corresponding to a Goldstone pion mass of 40 MeV
- Phase transition temperature at physical quark masses (m_l/m_s=1/27): T_c = 200.6 MeV (with ΔT_c = 0.18 MeV)
- Phase transition temperature at m_l/m_s=1/40 (m_π=110 MeV): T_c = 199.37 MeV (ΔT_c = 0.23 MeV)
- Phase transition temperature at m_l/m_s=1/160 (m_π=55 MeV): T_c = 195.23 MeV (ΔT_c = 0.27 MeV)
- Phase transition temperature at m_l/m_s=1/320 (m_π=40 MeV): T_c = 195.36 MeV (ΔT_c = 0.29 MeV)
- For a second-order Z(2) transition, the susceptibility peak height scales as N_σ^{γ/ν} = N_σ^{1.96}, not as N_σ^3 (which would indicate first order)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the QCD phase diagram with imaginary chemical potential, which had found first-order transitions at the Roberge-Weiss endpoint for sufficiently small quark masses using unimproved staggered fermions (deForcrand and Philipsen, 2010; Philipsen and collaborators, 2016; Cuteri and collaborators, 2015). The results contradict those earlier findings and are consistent with calculations using other improved actions, such as the stout action (Bonati and collaborators, 2018), which also found no first-order transition for pion masses above 50 MeV. The work enables future determination of whether the chiral transition and the Roberge-Weiss transition coincide in the chiral limit, and whether a first-order region exists at even smaller quark masses than those explored here.
