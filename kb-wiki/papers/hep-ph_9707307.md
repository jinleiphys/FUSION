# Suppression of PiNN* Coupling and Chiral Symmetry

> Meson-baryon couplings between positive and negative parity baryons are investigated using two point correlation functions in the soft meson limit. We find that the pi NN^* coupling vanishes due to chiral symmetry, while the eta NN^* coupling remains finite. We perform an analysis based on the algebraic method for SU(2) and SU(3) chiral symmetry, and find that baryon axial charges play an essential role for vanishing coupling constants.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $\pi NN^*$ coupling constant vanishes in the soft-meson limit due to chiral symmetry, while the $\eta NN^*$ coupling remains finite; this result depends on the assumption that the positive and negative parity baryons are coupled by the same type of interpolating field and form a parity doublet.

## Method

The authors compute two-point correlation functions between the vacuum and a one-pion state in the soft-pion limit using operator product expansion (OPE) up to dimension eight. They analyze the chiral transformation properties of baryon interpolating fields under $SU(2)_R \times SU(2)_L$ and $SU(3)_R \times SU(3)_L$, showing that the $\pslash \gamma_5$ term in the correlation function vanishes when the positive and negative parity baryons carry the same axial charge. The analysis is extended to $SU(3)$ baryons by decomposing the interpolating fields into chiral multiplets and examining the commutation relations with axial charges.

## Key numbers

* $\gpNR \sim 0.7$ and $\geNR \sim 2$ are extracted from experimental decay widths of $N(1535)$, compared to $\gpNN \sim 13$ for the $\pi NN$ coupling.
* The $\pi NN^*$ coupling vanishes ($\gpNR = 0$) in the soft-meson limit due to chiral symmetry, while $\geNR$ remains nonzero.
* For the $SU(3)$ case, the $\pi NN^*$ coupling vanishes for the $(p,n)$ isospin doublet, but the $\pi \Xi \Xi^*$ coupling does not vanish because $J^8_\Xi$ and $J^3_\Xi$ carry axial charges with opposite sign.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QCD sum rule study of negative parity baryon masses (Jido, Kodama and Oka, 1996), where mass splitting between parity doublets was attributed to nonzero quark condensate. The result that $\gpNR$ vanishes while $\geNR$ remains finite is consistent with the observed decay properties of $N(1535)$, which has comparable branching ratios to $\pi N$ and $\eta N$ despite phase space differences. The analysis builds on the classification of hadron interpolating fields by chiral symmetry (Cohen and Ji, 1996) and contrasts with an alternative approach by Kim and Lee (1997) using derivative-containing interpolating fields that do not treat positive and negative parity baryons as a parity doublet.
