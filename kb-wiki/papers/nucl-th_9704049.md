# Nuclear medium modifications of the NN interaction via quasielastic ($\vec p,\vec p '$) and ($\vec{p},\vec{n}$) scattering

> Within the relativistic PWIA, spin observables have been recalculated for quasielastic ($\vec p,\vec p '$) and ($\vec p,\vec n$) reactions on a $^{40}$Ca target. The incident proton energy ranges from 135 to 300 MeV while the transferred momentum is kept fixed at 1.97 fm^{-1}. In the present calculations, new Horowitz-Love--Franey relativistic NN amplitudes have been generated in order to yield improved and more quantitative spin observable values than before. The sensitivities of the various spin observables to the NN interaction parameters, such as (1) the presence of the surrounding nuclear medium, (2) a pseudoscalar versus a pseudovector interaction term, and (3) exchange effects, point to spin observables which should preferably be measured at certain laboratory proton energies, in order to test current nuclear models. This study also shows that nuclear medium effects become more important at lower proton energies ($\leq$ 200 MeV). A comparison to the limited available data indicates that the relativistic parametrization of the NN scattering amplitudes in terms of only the five Fermi invariants (the SVPAT form) is questionable.

## Key claim

The relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) with new Horowitz-Love-Franey (HLF) amplitudes yields improved quantitative predictions for quasielastic spin observables, but the comparison to limited available data shows that the standard SVPAT parametrization of NN scattering amplitudes in terms of only five Fermi invariants is questionable, and the data give mixed signals regarding whether a pseudoscalar or pseudovector form of the pion-nucleon vertex is preferred.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) calculations for quasielastic (p,p') and (p,n) reactions on a 40Ca target at fixed momentum transfer of 1.97 fm^-1, using newly generated Horowitz-Love-Franey (HLF) relativistic NN amplitudes with energy-dependent coupling constants and cutoff parameters. They systematically compared spin observables calculated with effective nucleon masses (medium-modified) versus free masses, pseudoscalar versus pseudovector pion-nucleon coupling, and with versus without exchange contributions, presenting results as difference graphs over incident proton energies from 135 to 300 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer fixed at 1.97 fm^-1 for the systematic study on 40Ca
- Incident proton energy range: 135 to 300 MeV
- Centroid of quasielastic peak at ω ≈ 80 MeV for the 40Ca calculations
- New HLF parameters generated between 80 and 195 MeV in 5-MeV intervals
- Theoretical uncertainty from HLF parameter fits on polarization transfer observables always smaller than 0.04
- For 12C(p,n) at 186 MeV and momentum transfer 1.1 fm^-1: centroid of quasielastic peak at ω ≈ 50 MeV, reaction Q-value of -18.6 MeV
- For 12C(p,p') at 290 MeV and momentum transfer 1.97 fm^-1: centroid of quasielastic peak at ω ≈ 80 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous qualitative studies (Hillhouse 1994, 1995) by performing quantitative calculations with improved HLF parameters and the Maxwell parametrization (Maxwell 1996), addressing the failure of nonrelativistic Schrodinger-based models (Tessarowicz 1988) to describe quasielastic analyzing powers. The results enable experimental tests of nuclear medium modifications at energies below 200 MeV where no complete published data sets exist, and point toward the need for full relativistic distorted-wave descriptions and relativistic random-phase approximation treatments (Horowitz and Piekarewicz 1994) to resolve ambiguities between pseudoscalar and pseudovector pion coupling suggested by the limited (p,p') and (p,n) data.
