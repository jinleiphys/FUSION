# Examination of the lattice QCD-motivated strong attractive $ΩN$ potentials in the $Ω^- n p$ system

> Within the framework of the Faddeev equations in configuration space, we examine the $Ω^{-} np$ system, employing strongly attractive lattice HAL QCD and Yukawa-type meson exchange potentials for the $ΩN$ interaction. Our formalism incorporates the attractive Coulomb force between the $Ω^{-}$ and proton, treating the system as three non-identical particle pairs (the $ABC$ model). In this study, we assess the impact of the Coulomb interaction on the system and compare our results with recent $ΩNN$ ($AAC$ model) calculations obtained using various approaches. The $ABC$ model yields low-energy characteristics for the \(ΩNN\) system that differ from previous calculations. The Coulomb potential has a marginal perturbative effect on the $AAC$ system, shifting the three-body binding energy by the Coulomb energy of the two-body $BC$ subsystem, but only slightly deviating the spatial configuration from isosceles triangle symmetry. These effects are primarily driven by the strong \(ΩN\) interaction. We demonstrate that the large binding energy of the $Ω^{-} np$ system arises from the short-range behavior of the $ΩN$ potentials.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the large binding energy of the \(\Omega^{-} np\) system arises specifically from the short-range behavior of the \(\Omega N\) potentials, not from the medium-range attraction, and that the Coulomb interaction between \(\Omega^{-}\) and proton has only a marginal perturbative effect on the three-body binding energy, shifting it by approximately the Coulomb energy of the two-body subsystem.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations in configuration space for the \(\Omega^{-} np\) system, treating it as three non-identical particles (the \(ABC\) model) to incorporate the Coulomb force between \(\Omega^{-}\) and proton. They employed two types of \(\Omega N\) potentials: the lattice HAL QCD potential (parameter sets \(V_{L1\Omega N}\) and \(V_{L2\Omega N}\)) and a Yukawa-type meson exchange potential (\(V_{Yu\Omega N}\)), together with the Malfliet-Tjon (MT) and Afnan-Tang (ATS3) \(NN\) potentials for the nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Key numbers

- The \(\Omega^{-} n\) binding energy with the \(V_{L1\Omega N}\) potential is \(-1.2909\) MeV, with scattering length \(5.8\) fm and effective range \(1.0\) fm.
- The \(\Omega^{-} p\) binding energy with the same potential is \(-2.1595\) MeV, with the Coulomb contribution \(\Delta_C = -0.85\) MeV.
- The \(\Omega^{-} n\) binding energy with the \(V_{Yu\Omega N}\) potential is \(-0.3385\) MeV, with scattering length \(10.2\) fm and effective range \(0.75\) fm.
- The deuteron binding energy obtained with the MT potential is \(-2.2302\) MeV, with root mean square distance \(3.98\) fm.
- The \(\Omega d\) three-body binding energy in the \(AAC\) model (no Coulomb) with \(V_{L1\Omega N}\) and MT potentials is \(-19.582\) MeV, in agreement with the \(-19.6\) MeV result from Garcilazo and Valcarce.
- The \(\Omega d\) binding energy with \(V_{Yu\Omega N}\) and MT potentials is \(-16.772\) MeV, compared to \(-16.34\) MeV from Garcilazo and Valcarce.
- The quantum well width for \(V_{L1\Omega N}\) is \(1.76\) fm with minimal depth \(-26\) MeV; for \(V_{Yu\Omega N}\) it is \(2.6\) fm with minimal depth \(-12\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev calculations of Garcilazo and Valcarce (2016) and the hyperspherical function calculations of Zhang (2022) and ESE (2023) for the \(\Omega NN\) system, which used the \(AAC\) model with two identical nucleons and omitted the Coulomb force. By introducing the \(ABC\) model, the authors rigorously include the Coulomb interaction between \(\Omega^{-}\) and proton, enabling a direct comparison of binding energies and spatial configurations. The results enable future studies of \(\Omega NN\) systems with different strangeness and charge states, and clarify that the short-range part of the \(\Omega N\) potential is the dominant driver of the large three-body binding energy.
