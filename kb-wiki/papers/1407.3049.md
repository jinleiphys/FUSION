# Description of $α$-cluster tail in $^{8}$Be and $^{20}$Ne: Delocalization of $α$ cluster because of quantum penetration

> We analyze the $α$-cluster wave functions in cluster states of $^8$Be and $^{20}$Ne by comparing the exact relative wave function obtained by the generator coordinate method (GCM) with various types of trial functions. For the trial functions, we adopt the fixed range shifted Gaussian of the Brink-Bloch (BB) wave function, the spherical Gaussian with the adjustable range parameter of the spherical Thosaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke (sTHSR), the deformed Gaussian of the deformed THSR (dTHSR), and a function with the Yukawa tail (YT). The quality of the description of the exact wave function with a trial function is judged by the squared overlap between the trial function and the GCM wave function. The better result is obtained with the sTHSR wave function than the BB wave function, and further improvement can be done with the dTHSR wave function because these wave functions can describe the outer tail better. The YT wave function gives almost the equal quality to or even better quality than the dTHSR wave function indicating that the outer tail of $α$ cluster states is characterized by the Yukawa-like tail rather than the Gaussian tail. In the weakly bound $α$ cluster states with the small $α$ separation energy and the low centrifugal and Coulomb barriers, the outer tail part is the slowly damping function described well by the quantum penetration through the effective barrier. This outer tail characterizes the almost zero-energy free $α$ gas behavior, i.e., the delocalization of cluster.

## Key claim

The outer tail of α-cluster wave functions in weakly bound cluster states of ⁸Be and ²⁰Ne is characterized by a Yukawa-like tail rather than a Gaussian tail, and this tail arises from quantum penetration through the effective barrier, meaning the delocalization of the α cluster is an obvious consequence of quantum penetration in low-ℓ states with small separation energy and low Coulomb barrier, not necessarily a new physical concept.

## Method

The author performed generator coordinate method (GCM) calculations for the ²α and ¹⁶O+α cluster systems to obtain exact relative wave functions for ⁸Be and ²⁰Ne. She then compared these exact wave functions with several single-parameter or two-parameter trial functions, the Brink-Bloch (BB) shifted Gaussian with fixed range, the spherical THSR (sTHSR) Gaussian with adjustable range, the deformed THSR (dTHSR) Gaussian, and a Yukawa-tail (YT) function, by computing the squared overlap between the antisymmetrized relative wave function of each trial function and the GCM wave function. The radial curvature of the relative wave functions was also analyzed to quantify the damping behavior of the outer tail.

## Key numbers

- For ²⁰Ne(1⁻), the single dTHSR wave function achieves a squared overlap of 99.98% with the exact GCM solution.
- The sTHSR wave function gives better squared overlap than the BB wave function for the cluster states studied, and the dTHSR gives further improvement.
- The YT wave function gives quality equal to or better than the dTHSR wave function for states such as ²⁰Ne(0⁺) and ²⁰Ne(2⁺₁).

## Context

This work extends the THSR approach developed by Tohsaki, Horiuchi, Schuck, and Röpke (2001) and applied to ⁸Be and ²⁰Ne by Funaki (2002, 2003) and Zhou (2012, 2013), who proposed the "container picture" of delocalized clusters. The paper clarifies that the success of the dTHSR wave function in reproducing exact GCM solutions arises because the projected deformed Gaussian can mimic a Yukawa-like tail when the effective barrier is low, rather than indicating a fundamentally new cluster gas state. This enables a more precise understanding of which aspects of cluster wave functions are physically meaningful versus artifacts of the chosen functional form.
