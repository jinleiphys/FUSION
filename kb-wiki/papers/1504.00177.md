# Thermodynamics of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter from chiral effective field theory

> The density and temperature dependence of the nuclear symmetry free energy is investigated using microscopic two- and three-body nuclear potentials constructed from chiral effective field theory. The nuclear force models and many-body methods are benchmarked to properties of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter in the vicinity of the saturation density as well as the virial expansion of the neutron matter equation of state at low fugacities. The free energy per particle of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter is calculated assuming a quadratic dependence of the interaction contributions on the isospin asymmetry. The spinodal instability at subnuclear densities is examined in detail.

## Key claim

The symmetry free energy of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter is calculated assuming a quadratic dependence of the interaction contributions on the isospin asymmetry parameter δ², while the free Fermi gas contribution contains large quartic and higher powers of δ that are explicitly quantified; the authors note that future research will address the accuracy of this quadratic approximation in greater detail.

## Method

The free energy per particle of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter was calculated using the Kohn-Luttinger-Ward many-body perturbation series up to second order, employing N3LO two-body and N2LO three-body chiral low-momentum interactions with resolution scales Λ=414 MeV and Λ=450 MeV. The symmetry free energy was obtained as the difference between the free energy per particle in pure neutron matter and isospin-symmetric nuclear matter, and the free energy of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter was constructed assuming a quadratic dependence on the isospin asymmetry parameter δ. The spinodal instability region was examined by analyzing the thermodynamic stability conditions as functions of density, temperature, and isospin asymmetry.

## Key numbers

- Zero-temperature saturation energy of symmetric nuclear matter: -15.79 MeV (n3lo414) and -15.50 MeV (n3lo450)
- Saturation density: 0.171 fm⁻³ (n3lo414) and 0.161 fm⁻³ (n3lo450)
- Incompressibility K: 223 MeV (n3lo414) and 244 MeV (n3lo450)
- Critical temperature of liquid-gas phase transition in symmetric nuclear matter: 17.4 MeV (n3lo414) and 17.2 MeV (n3lo450)
- Critical density: 0.066 fm⁻³ (n3lo414) and 0.064 fm⁻³ (n3lo450)
- Critical pressure: 0.33 MeV fm⁻³ (n3lo414) and 0.32 MeV fm⁻³ (n3lo450)
- Critical compressibility factor Zc ≈ 0.29 for both interaction sets
- Neutron-neutron scattering length as ≈ -19 fm
- At densities near nuclear saturation density, deviations of the symmetry internal energy from its zero-temperature value are below 0.5 MeV
- Zero-temperature endpoint of the low-density part of the spinodal located at ρ ≈ 2×10⁻⁴ fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter (Wellenhofer et al., 2014) to isospin-asymmetric systems using chiral effective field theory interactions. The results are benchmarked against the virial expansion of Horowitz and Schwenk (2005) for low-density neutron matter and compared to quantum Monte Carlo simulations by Wlazlowski et al. (2014) and Gezerlis and Carlson (2007), as well as to variational calculations by Akmal et al. (1998). The symmetry energy results are consistent with empirical constraints from isobaric analog states and neutron skins (Danielewicz, 2014) and with calculations by Drischler et al. (2014). This work enables the development of a chiral effective field theory thermodynamic equation of state across temperatures, densities, and isospin asymmetries relevant for astrophysical phenomena and heavy-ion collisions.
