# Correlation of the neutron star crust-core properties with the slope of the symmetry energy and the lead skin thickness

> The correlations of the crust-core transition density and pressure in neutron stars with the slope of the symmetry energy and the neutron skin thickness are investigated, using different families of relativistic mean field parametrizations with constant couplings and non-linear terms mixing the $σ$, $ω$ and $ρ$-meson fields. It is shown that the modification of the density dependence of the symmetry energy, involving the $σ$ or the $ω$ meson, gives rise to different behaviors: the effect of the $ω$-meson may also be reproduced within non-relativistic phenomenological models, while the effect of the $σ$-meson is essentially relativistic. Depending on the parametrization with $σ-ρ$ or $ω-ρ$ mixing terms, different values of the slope of the symmetry energy at saturation must be considered in order to obtain a neutron matter equation of state compatible with results from chiral effective field theory. This difference leads to different pressures at the crust-core transition density. A linear correlation between the transition density and the symmetry energy slope or the neutron skin thickness of the $^{208}$Pb nucleus is obtained, only when the $ω$-meson is used to describe the density dependence of the symmetry energy. A comparison is made between the crust-core transition properties of neutron stars obtained by three different methods, the Relativistic Random Phase Approximation (RRPA), the Vlasov equation and Thermodynamical method. It is shown that the RRPA and the Vlasov methods predict similar transition densities for $pne$ $β$-equilibrium stellar matter.

## Key claim

The correlation between the neutron star crust-core transition density and the slope of the symmetry energy or the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb is linear only when the ω-meson is used to describe the density dependence of the symmetry energy (the F2ρ family); when the σ-meson is used instead (the Fρ family), the transition density remains almost unchanged for low values of L or Δrnp, contradicting the linear trend reported in earlier work.

## Method

The authors used three different methods to determine the crust-core transition density and pressure in β-equilibrium neutron star matter at zero temperature: the Relativistic Random Phase Approximation (RRPA), the Vlasov equation formalism, and the Thermodynamical method. They employed two families of relativistic mean-field parametrizations (Fρ and F2ρ) that include non-linear σ-ρ or ω-ρ mixing terms, respectively, with the coupling strengths varied to produce a range of symmetry energy slopes and neutron skin thicknesses for 208Pb while keeping isoscalar properties fixed to the BKA22 model.

## Key numbers

- The Fρ family yields symmetry energy slopes L ranging from 108.9 MeV (Fρ-1) down to 70.1 MeV (Fρ-7), with corresponding 208Pb neutron skin thicknesses Δrnp from 0.280 fm to 0.179 fm.
- The F2ρ family yields L values from 97.3 MeV (F2ρ-1) down to 46.0 MeV (F2ρ-7), with Δrnp from 0.269 fm to 0.152 fm.
- Crust-core transition densities ρtrans for the Fρ family range from 0.0489 fm−3 (Fρ-1) to 0.0582 fm−3 (Fρ-7) as calculated with the Vlasov method.
- Crust-core transition densities ρtrans for the F2ρ family range from 0.0525 fm−3 (F2ρ-1) to 0.0805 fm−3 (F2ρ-7) as calculated with the Vlasov method.
- The saturation density for all models is ρ0 = 0.148 fm−3, with binding energy E/A = −16.08 MeV and incompressibility K = 228.63 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of correlations between neutron star crust-core properties and nuclear symmetry energy parameters, following earlier work by Horowitz (2001) and Vidana (2009) that established correlations between transition density, neutron skin thickness, and the slope L. The authors show that the choice of meson mixing term (σ-ρ versus ω-ρ) qualitatively changes these correlations, with the σ-ρ family producing a purely relativistic effect not reproducible by non-relativistic Skyrme forces, while the ω-ρ family behaves similarly to Skyrme interactions. The results enable more precise constraints on the equation of state by requiring that future models simultaneously reproduce both the crust-core transition properties and the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb.
