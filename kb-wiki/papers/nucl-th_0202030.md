# Complete relativistic equation of state for neutron stars

> We construct the equation of state (EOS) in a wide density range for neutron stars using the relativistic mean field theory. The properties of neutron star matter with both uniform and non-uniform distributions are studied consistently. The inclusion of hyperons considerably softens the EOS at high densities. The Thomas-Fermi approximation is used to describe the non-uniform matter, which is composed of a lattice of heavy nuclei. The phase transition from uniform matter to non-uniform matter occurs around $0.06 \rm{fm^{-3}}$, and the free neutrons drip out of nuclei at about $2.4 \times 10^{-4}\ \rm{fm^{-3}}$. We apply the resulting EOS to investigate the neutron star properties such as maximum mass and composition of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a complete relativistic equation of state (EOS) for neutron stars across a wide density range using the same relativistic mean field (RMF) framework for both uniform and non-uniform matter, but the results for hyperon thresholds and fractions are sensitive to the adopted hyperon couplings derived from the quark model, which may overbind Λ hyperons and cause an earlier appearance of Λ hyperons than would be constrained by experimental hypernuclei data.

## Method

The author uses the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with the TM1 parameter set to describe uniform neutron star matter containing the full baryon octet (n, p, Λ, Σ⁺, Σ⁰, Σ⁻, Ξ⁰, Ξ⁻) plus electrons and muons in β-equilibrium. For non-uniform matter at low densities, the Thomas-Fermi approximation is employed, modeling matter as a body-centered-cubic (BCC) lattice of spherical heavy nuclei immersed in an electron gas with or without free neutrons, with the optimal state at each density determined by minimizing the energy density. The phase transition between non-uniform and uniform matter is identified by comparing the energy densities of the two phases.

## Key numbers

- Phase transition from uniform matter to non-uniform matter occurs around 0.06 fm⁻³
- Free neutrons drip out of nuclei at about 2.4 × 10⁻⁴ fm⁻³
- Muons appear when the electron chemical potential exceeds the muon rest mass at n_B ≈ 0.11 fm⁻³
- Hyperons first appear at n_B ≈ 0.27 fm⁻³ (Σ⁻ is the first hyperon, followed by Λ at n_B ≈ 0.29 fm⁻³)
- Maximum mass of neutron stars including hyperons is around 1.6 M_⊙
- Maximum mass of neutron stars without hyperons is around 2.2 M_⊙
- The surface energy parameter F_0 = 70 MeV·fm⁵ is used in the Thomas-Fermi calculation
- Hyperon masses used: m_Λ = 1116 MeV, m_Σ = 1193 MeV, m_Ξ = 1313 MeV
- Strange meson masses used: m_σ* = 975 MeV, m_φ = 1020 MeV

## Context

This work extends the RMF approach of Sugahara and Toki (1994) with the TM1 parameter set, which was successful for finite nuclei, to construct a consistent EOS for neutron stars across all density ranges within a single relativistic framework. It contrasts with earlier composite EOS approaches that connected relativistic high-density EOS with non-relativistic low-density EOS from works such as Baym, Bethe, and Pethick (1971) and Negele and Vautherin (1973), which introduced discontinuities and inconsistencies. The resulting EOS enables self-consistent studies of neutron star structure including crust properties, and the author notes that future work should investigate the dependence on hyperon couplings and extend the approach to more microscopic theories such as the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach.
