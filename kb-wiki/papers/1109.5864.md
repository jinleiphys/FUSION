# Three-dimensional imaging of the nucleon in momentum space

> Transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs) are studied in the framework of quark models. Results for the six T-even TMDs are obtained from the overlap of three-quark light-cone wave functions, using both the chiral quark-soliton model and a light-cone constituent quark model. Furthermore, quark model relations among TMDs are reviewed and their physical origin is discussed in terms of rotational-symmetry properties of the nucleon state in its rest frame.

## Key claim

The six T-even transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs) are not all independent in the chiral quark-soliton model and light-cone constituent quark model; they satisfy three flavor-independent relations and one flavor-dependent relation, which have a geometrical origin in rotational symmetry of the nucleon state in its rest frame, but these relations are not expected to hold identically in QCD.

## Method

The authors calculated the six T-even TMDs from the overlap of three-quark light-cone wave functions using two distinct quark models: the light-cone version of the chiral quark-soliton model (LCχQSM) and a light-cone constituent quark model (LCCQM). They then derived the model relations among TMDs by working at the amplitude level, expressing the quark-quark correlator in a net-polarization basis and imposing spherical symmetry in the canonical-spin basis, connected to the light-cone helicity basis via a rotation.

## Key numbers

- The flavor-dependent relation for a proton target is given by \(\mathcal D^u f_1^u + g_{1L}^u = 2 h_1^u\) with \(\mathcal D^u = 2/3\) for u quarks and \(\mathcal D^d = -1/3\) for d quarks.
- The spin-flavor factors for a proton target are \(N^u = 2\) and \(N^d = 1\) for unpolarized TMDs, and \(P^u = 4/3\) and \(P^d = -1/3\) for polarized TMDs.
- The transverse moments are defined as \(j^{(0)}(x) \equiv \int d^2k_\perp\, j(x,\vec k_\perp^2)\), \(j^{(1/2)}(x) \equiv \int d^2k_\perp\, (k_\perp/M)\, j(x,\vec k_\perp^2)\), and \(j^{(1)}(x) \equiv \int d^2k_\perp\, (k_\perp^2/2M^2)\, j(x,\vec k_\perp^2)\).

## Context

This work extends previous model calculations of TMDs in the bag model (Avakian 2010, Avakian 2008), quark-diquark models (Jakob 1997, She 2009, Zhu 2011), and the covariant parton model (Efremov 2009), showing that the observed relations among TMDs arise from common assumptions: impulse approximation with no explicit gluons, a rotation between light-cone and canonical quark polarizations, and spherical symmetry of the target. The results enable phenomenological parametrizations of TMDs for fitting experimental data from semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan processes, and provide a framework for testing the accuracy of these approximate relations against future lattice QCD calculations (Hagler 2009, Musch 2010).
