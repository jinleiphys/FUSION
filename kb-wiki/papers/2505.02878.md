# Pole-Expansion of Two-Hadron Imaginary-Time Correlation Function -a new method of analysis for unstable states in lattice QCD-

> We analyze the pole expansion of the two-hadron imaginary-time correlation function. We first explain the general idea that the imaginary-time correlation function is expressed as a sum of the pole terms, the Mittag-Leffler expansion, in terms of the uniformization variable, which makes the S-matrix single-valued. We then derive explicit expressions of the pole expansion for the single-channel ($ρ$ meson) and two-channel ($Λ(1405)$) examples and demonstrate that the pole expansion actually holds employing phenomenological models, the vector-dominance model for the $ρ$ meson and the chiral unitary model for $Λ(1405)$. From this observation we propose the pole expansion as a method to extract information of unstable states such as masses and widths from the two-hadron imaginary-time correlation functions obtained by lattice QCD simulations.

## Key claim

The imaginary-time correlation function of two hadrons can be expressed as a Mittag-Leffler expansion in terms of a uniformization variable, parametrized solely by pole positions and residues of unstable states, provided that left-hand cuts and three-or-more-body channels are neglected and the correlation function is assumed to be meromorphic in that variable.

## Method

The authors derive explicit pole-expansion formulas for the imaginary-time correlation function in single-channel (rho meson) and two-channel (Lambda(1405)) systems using the uniformization variable that makes the S-matrix single-valued. They demonstrate the validity of the expansion by comparing the exact correlation functions from phenomenological models (vector-dominance model for the rho meson and chiral unitary model for Lambda(1405)) with the sum of pole contributions, showing near-perfect agreement for both the spectral functions and the imaginary-time correlation functions.

## Key numbers

* Rho meson pole position in the uniformization variable k: k = ±0.354 - 0.039i GeV, corresponding to complex energy E = 0.760 ∓ 0.073i GeV (mass 0.760 GeV, width 0.160 GeV)
* Rho meson residue r^{ππρ} for |q|=0: r^{ππρ} = ±28.93 + 2.27i GeV^{-4}; for |q|=1 GeV: r^{ππρ} = ∓0.571 + 0.101i GeV^{-4}
* Pion mass used in the vector-dominance model: m_π = 0.140 GeV; coupling constant g = 6.05
* Lambda(1405) pole positions in the uniformization variable z: Λ1 at z = ±0.760 + 0.154i (E = 1.435 ∓ 0.010i GeV), Λ2 at z = ±0.413 + 0.395i (E = 1.386 ∓ 0.072i GeV), Λ3 at z = ±1.72 - 1.49i (E = 1.397 ∓ 0.131i GeV)
* Chiral unitary model pion decay constant: f = 0.104 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of the pole-expansion method for unstable states in coupled channels (Yamada 2020, Yamada 2021, Yamada 2023) and provides a new alternative to the Luscher method (Luscher 1985, 1986) and the HALQCD method (Ishii 2006, Aoki 2009) for extracting resonance parameters from lattice QCD simulations. The method directly relates imaginary-time correlation functions to masses and widths of unstable states without the arbitrary K-matrix parametrization assumptions used in recent coupled-channel lattice studies (BaryonScatteringBaSc 2023). The next step is to validate the method against actual lattice QCD data and to investigate finite-volume effects in the pole expansion.
