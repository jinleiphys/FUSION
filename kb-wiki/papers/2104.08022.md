# Anisotropic flow decorrelation in heavy-ion collisions with event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics

> Decorrelation of the elliptic flow in rapidity is calculated within a hybrid approach which includes event-by-event viscous fluid dynamics and final state hadronic cascade model. The simulations are performed for Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass collision energies of 27 and 200 GeV per nucleon pair, as well as various colliding systems at 72 GeV per nucleon pair. Initial conditions determined by an extended Monte Carlo Glauber model show better agreement with experimental data than initial conditions from the UrQMD transport model. We show how the observed decorrelation is connected with the decorrelation of initial state spatial anisotropies. We also study how the effect is increased by the final state hadronic cascade.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the longitudinal decorrelation of elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies (27 and 200 GeV per nucleon pair) can be described by a hybrid viscous hydrodynamics model, but only when using initial conditions from an extended Monte Carlo Glauber model (GLISSANDO 2) rather than from the UrQMD transport model; the UrQMD initial state produces too strong decorrelation due to its overly local rapidity structure.

## Method

The authors used the event-by-event (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic code vHLLE coupled with the UrQMD hadronic cascade for the final state, employing two different initial condition models: UrQMD transport model and the 3D Monte Carlo Glauber model (GLISSANDO 2) with a triangular longitudinal entropy profile and baryon charge deposition. They simulated Au+Au collisions at 27 and 200 GeV per nucleon pair for the 10-40% centrality interval, and also Pb+Ti, Pb+W, and Pb+C collisions at 72 GeV per nucleon pair as predictions for the proposed AFTER fixed-target experiment. The flow decorrelation was quantified using the factorization ratio r_n, and the contributions from flow magnitude decorrelation versus flow angle decorrelation were separated.

## Key numbers

* For Au+Au collisions at 27 GeV per nucleon pair in 10-40% centrality, the factorization ratio r_2 with GLISSANDO initial conditions agrees with preliminary STAR data within uncertainties, while UrQMD initial conditions produce much stronger decorrelation.
* For Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV per nucleon pair in 10-40% centrality, GLISSANDO initial conditions describe the preliminary STAR data within uncertainties, while neither initial state reproduces the triangular pseudorapidity dependence of v_2 seen in experimental data.
* The shear viscosity over entropy density ratio (eta/s) was set to 0.12 for 27 GeV and 0.08 for both 72 and 200 GeV per nucleon pair.
* The starting proper time tau_0 was 1.0 fm/c for 27 GeV, 0.7 fm/c for 72 GeV, and 0.4 fm/c for 200 GeV per nucleon pair.
* For the AFTER experiment predictions at 72 GeV per nucleon pair, the event-plane resolution correction factor R decreased from 0.846 (GLISSANDO) or 0.838 (UrQMD) for Pb+W to 0.532 (GLISSANDO) or 0.460 (UrQMD) for Pb+C.

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic studies of flow decorrelation at LHC and top RHIC energies (Bozek 2017, Wu 2018, Pang 2015) down to the lower energies of the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program, where finite baryon density becomes important. The study confirms that even at 27 GeV per nucleon pair, flow angle decorrelation dominates over flow magnitude decorrelation, consistent with findings at higher energies. The results enable predictions for the proposed AFTER fixed-target experiment at the LHC and demonstrate that the final-state hadronic cascade increases the elliptic flow but also reduces the decorrelation, contrary to earlier hydrodynamic calculations without hadronic rescatterings.
