# Generalized thermodynamic relations for perfect spin hydrodynamics

> Generalized thermodynamic relations are introduced into the framework of a relativistic perfect spin hydrodynamics. They allow for consistent treatment of spin degrees of freedom, including the use of spin tensors whose structure follows from microscopic calculations. The obtained results are important for establishing consistency between different formulations of spin hydrodynamics and form the basis for introducing dissipative corrections.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard scalar thermodynamic relations used in perfect spin hydrodynamics (Eqs. with energy density, pressure, temperature, entropy density, chemical potential, and baryon density) are insufficient for a consistent treatment of spin degrees of freedom; instead, generalized tensor forms of thermodynamic relations are required, which include terms orthogonal to the fluid flow that are not captured by simply multiplying scalar relations by the flow four-velocity.

## Method

The authors critically reexamine the thermodynamic relations used in perfect spin hydrodynamics for spin-1/2 particles, starting from the standard scalar relations and their tensor extensions that include the spin polarization tensor and spin density tensor. They then derive the entropy current from a kinetic theory model using a classical treatment of spin with a spin-dependent phase-space distribution function in local equilibrium, and compare the resulting expression with the phenomenological tensor relations. By analyzing the decomposition of the baryon current, energy-momentum tensor, and spin tensor into components parallel and orthogonal to the fluid flow, they show that the scalar relations must be replaced by two scalar equations and that the tensor relations require a generalized form with a particle four-current replacing the pressure term.

## Key numbers

- The spin tensor decomposition includes scalar functions A, A1, and A3 (Eq. spint_GLW), where the case A3=0 reproduces the standard Weyssenhoff form and the case A=A1 (with A3=0) gives S^{mu nu} = A omega^{mu nu}, which has been excluded as leading to unstable behavior in first-order and second-order dissipative spin hydrodynamics (citing Daher 2024).
- The spin integration measure dS = (m/(pi s)) d^4s delta(s.s + s^2) delta(p.s) with normalization integral dS = 2 reflecting two possible orientations for spin-1/2 (s^2 = 3/4).
- The entropy current derived from kinetic theory (Eq. Hmu2) has the form S^mu = T^{mu alpha} beta_alpha - (1/2) omega_{alpha beta} S^{mu, alpha beta} - xi N^mu + calN^mu, where calN^mu = coth(xi) N^mu, replacing the term P beta^mu in the standard tensor relation.
- The generalized scalar relations yield two equations: bar epsilon + coth(xi) bar n T = T bar sigma + mu bar n + bar s T (Eq. scalarG1) and P_t + coth(xi) n_t T = T sigma_t + mu n_t + s_t T (Eq. scalarG2), where the second equation vanishes in the spinless case.

## Context

This work extends the framework of relativistic spin hydrodynamics by addressing inconsistencies between formulations based on phenomenological thermodynamic relations (e.g., Hattori 2019, Fukushima 2020) and those based on kinetic theory (e.g., Florkowski 2017, Florkowski 2018). The generalized thermodynamic relations enable a consistent treatment of spin tensors with structures derived from microscopic calculations, similar to how anisotropic relativistic magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) requires richer tensor structures (PhysRevE.51.4901). The results form the basis for introducing dissipative corrections in spin hydrodynamics and establish a connection with the quantum statistical approach of Becattini (2023), which the authors view as complementary top-down and bottom-up methods that should converge in practical applications to heavy-ion collision data.
