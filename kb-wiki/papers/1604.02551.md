# Low-energy theorems for nucleon-nucleon scattering at $M_π=450$ MeV

> We apply the low-energy theorems to analyze the recent lattice QCD results for the two-nucleon system at a pion mass of $M_π\simeq 450$ MeV obtained by the NPLQCD collaboration. We find that the binding energies of the deuteron and dineutron are inconsistent with the low-energy behavior of the corresponding phase shifts within the quoted uncertainties and vice versa. Using the binding energies of the deuteron and dineutron as input, we employ the low-energy theorems to predict the phase shifts and extract the scattering length and the effective range in the $^3S_1$ and $^1S_0$ channels. Our results for these quantities are consistent with those obtained by the NPLQCD collaboration from effective field theory analyses but are in conflict with their determination based on the effective-range approximation.

## Key claim

The low-energy theorems (LETs) for nucleon-nucleon scattering, which exploit the analytic structure of the scattering amplitude from one-pion exchange, reveal that the NPLQCD lattice QCD results at a pion mass of 450 MeV for the deuteron and dineutron binding energies are inconsistent with the corresponding phase shifts within the quoted uncertainties, and vice versa.

## Method

The authors solved the Kadyshevsky integral equation with a leading-order potential consisting of one-pion exchange and a single contact interaction, renormalized by adjusting the contact term to reproduce the input scattering length or binding energy. They generalized the LETs to unphysical pion masses by using lattice QCD results for the pion-mass dependence of the axial coupling \(g_A\), the pion decay constant \(F_\pi\), and the nucleon mass \(m_N\), and extended the analysis to next-to-leading order by including a heavy-meson-exchange term with strength \(\beta\) adjusted to reproduce the empirical effective range at the physical pion mass.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy at \(M_\pi \simeq 450\) MeV from NPLQCD: \(B_d = 14.4\ ({}^{+3.2}_{-2.6})\) MeV.
- At the lowest lattice momentum \(k \simeq 122\) MeV, the NPLQCD phase shift in the \(^3S_1\) channel is \(\delta = 38\ ({}^{+13}_{-11})\ ({}^{+23}_{-16})\) degrees, while the LETs predict \(\delta = 111 (\pm 5)\) degrees.
- NPLQCD effective range parameters in the \(^3S_1\) channel: \((M_\pi a)^{-1} = -0.04\ ({}^{+0.07}_{-0.10})\ ({}^{+0.08}_{-0.17})\) and \(M_\pi r = 7.8\ ({}^{+2.2}_{-1.5})\ ({}^{+3.5}_{-1.7})\).
- The LETs predict only positive values of the scattering length in the \(^3S_1\) channel from the NPLQCD deuteron binding energy, in disagreement with the negative central value reported by NPLQCD.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous application of low-energy theorems to nucleon-nucleon scattering (Baru 2015) and applies them to the recent NPLQCD lattice results at \(M_\pi = 450\) MeV (Orginos 2015). The findings contradict the NPLQCD determination of scattering parameters based on the effective-range approximation, while being consistent with the NPLQCD results from effective field theory analyses. The LETs provide a model-independent consistency check for lattice QCD calculations of two-nucleon systems and can help resolve the puzzle of conflicting results between the NPLQCD and HAL QCD collaborations regarding bound states at unphysical pion masses.
