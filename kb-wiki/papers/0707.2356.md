# Identified hadron spectra in Pb-Pb at sqrt(s) = 5.5 TeV: hydrodynamics+pQCD predictions

> 2 pages, 2 figures. Contribution to "Workshop on Heavy Ion Collisions at the LHC: Last Call for Predictions", Geneva, Switzerland, 14 May - 8 Jun 2007. To appear in J.Phys.G

## Key claim
The paper presents predictions for identified hadron spectra in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV using a combined hydrodynamics and perturbative QCD approach, but the hydrodynamical contribution dominates only up to p_T approximately 4 GeV/c in central collisions and 1.5 GeV/c in peripheral collisions, with the crossing point increasing with hadron mass.

## Method
The authors used cylindrically symmetric boost-invariant 2+1-D relativistic ideal hydrodynamics with initial conditions fixed for Pb-Pb at impact parameter b = 0 fm, using a Glauber prescription for other centralities. The initial entropy density was set to s_0 = 1120 fm^{-3} (energy density epsilon_0 approximately 650 GeV/fm^3) at formation time tau_0 = 0.1 fm/c to reproduce expected final hadron multiplicity dN_ch/deta approximately 1300. Above p_T approximately 3 GeV/c, hadron production from (mini)jet fragmentation was computed from collinearly factorized pQCD cross sections at NLO accuracy, using CTEQ6.5M PDFs with nDSg nuclear shadowing corrections and AKK fragmentation functions modified with BDMPS quenching weights.

## Key numbers
- Initial entropy density: s_0 = 1120 fm^{-3} (corresponding to energy density epsilon_0 approximately 650 GeV/fm^3)
- Expected final hadron multiplicity: dN_ch/deta approximately 1300 at the LHC
- Formation time: tau_0 = 0.1 fm/c
- Critical temperature for phase transition: T_crit approximately 170 MeV
- Freeze-out temperature: T_fo approximately 120 MeV
- Chemical potential: mu_B approximately 5 MeV (almost baryon-free system)
- Number of incoherent nucleon-nucleon collisions: N_coll = 1670 for 0-10% central, N_coll = 12.9 for 60-90% peripheral
- BDMPS energy loss scale: omega_c approximately 50 GeV (from expected energy dependence of quenching parameter, compared to omega_c approximately 20 GeV at RHIC)
- Nuclear modification factor R_PbPb approximately 0.1 at p_T = 10 GeV/c and approximately 0.5 at p_T = 100 GeV/c in central Pb-Pb
- Hydro-pQCD crossing point: p_T approximately 4 GeV/c in central Pb-Pb, p_T approximately 1.5 GeV/c in peripheral Pb-Pb

## Context
This work extends the hydrodynamics+pQCD approach previously tested at RHIC energies for Au-Au collisions (d'Enterria, 2005) to LHC energies, using an empirical extrapolation of hadron multiplicities from RHIC measurements (Adler, 2004). The predictions enable comparison with future LHC heavy-ion data to test the combined hydrodynamic and parton energy loss framework, and highlight the need to include recombination effects for protons at intermediate p_T (up to approximately 5 GeV/c in central collisions).
