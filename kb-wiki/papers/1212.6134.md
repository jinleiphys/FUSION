# Isospin symmetry breaking and baryon-isospin correlations from Polyakov$-$Nambu$-$Jona-Lasinio model

> We present a study of the 1+1 flavor system of strongly interacting matter in terms of the Polyakov$-$Nambu$-$Jona-Lasinio model. We find that though the small isospin symmetry breaking brought in through unequal light quark masses is too small to affect the thermodynamics of the system in general, it may have significant effect in baryon-isospin correlations and have a measurable impact in heavy-ion collision experiments.

## Key claim

The small isospin symmetry breaking from unequal up and down quark masses, while too small to affect bulk thermodynamics, can produce significant non-zero baryon-isospin correlations that may be measurable in heavy-ion collision experiments, with the correlations scaling linearly with the quark mass difference.

## Method

The authors used the 1+1 flavor Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model in the mean-field approximation, introducing unequal current quark masses for u and d quarks via a mass matrix with parameters m1 (average mass) and m2 (mass difference). They computed off-diagonal susceptibilities in the baryon-isospin sector from chemical potential derivatives of the thermodynamic potential, studying their temperature and baryon chemical potential dependence for three representative values of m2.

## Key numbers

- Average quark mass m1 fixed at 0.0055 GeV; three representative values of m2 = (md - mu)/2 were studied
- The second-order off-diagonal susceptibility χ11^BI shows an almost linear scaling with m2, demonstrated for m2 values up to 0.0015 GeV
- At T ~ 2Tc, χ11^BI is positive and slowly decreases with increasing μB; near Tc it drops sharply to zero, becomes negative, then slowly approaches zero again
- The ratio R2 = χ11^BI/χ2^B decreases monotonically with temperature and approaches zero above Tc
- For freezeout at √sNN ~ 8 GeV (T ~ 0.140 GeV, μB ~ 0.420 GeV), both numerator and denominator of R2 are within measurable regime for future facilities

## Context

This work extends previous studies of fluctuations and correlations in the isospin symmetric limit within the PNJL model (Ray et al., 2010-2012) and lattice QCD studies of isospin breaking effects (Gavai and Gupta, 2003; RM123 Collaboration, 2011; UKQCD Collaboration, 2011). It also builds on chiral perturbation theory analyses of isospin breaking in quark condensates (Nicola and Torres, 2011, 2012) and NJL model studies of isospin symmetry breaking (Frank, Isgur, and Klevansky, 2007). The work enables experimental extraction of quark mass asymmetry from heavy-ion collision data and provides a model-independent observable (the ratio of fourth-order correlators) to distinguish partonic from hadronic matter.
