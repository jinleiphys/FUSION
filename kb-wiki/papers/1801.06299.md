# Kaon Structure in the confining Nambu-Jona-Lasino Model

> The elastic electromagnetic form factors in the space-like region and valence quark distribution functions for the K+ meson are calculated using the confining Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with the help of the proper-time regularization scheme, which simulates quark confinement. In this model framework, the dynamics information on the nonperturbative aspects is obtained from quark propagators and bound state amplitudes via Bethe-Salpeter equations. We found that the results on the kaon form factors and valence quark distribution functions are qualitatively in excellent agreement with the existing kaon data as well as the perturbation QCD prediction at higher $Q^2$.

# Kaon Structure in the Confining Nambu-Jona-Lasinio Model

## Key claim
The confining Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with proper-time regularization produces kaon elastic form factors and valence quark distribution functions that are in qualitative agreement with existing data and perturbative QCD predictions at high $Q^2$, but the model's prediction for the $x$-dependence of the $u_{K^+}/u_{\pi^+}$ ratio differs from much of the data in the valence region, possibly due to the absence of momentum dependence in standard NJL Bethe-Salpeter vertices.

## Method
The author solved the gap equation for dressed quark masses and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for quark-antiquark correlations in the confining NJL model using the proper-time regularization scheme, which introduces an infrared cutoff to simulate confinement. The kaon elastic form factors were computed from the matrix element of the electromagnetic current including dressed quark-photon vertices, and the valence quark distribution functions were extracted using moments and the Ward identity. The distribution functions were evolved from a model scale of $Q_0^2 = 0.16$ GeV$^2$ to $Q^2 = 16$ GeV$^2$ using next-to-leading order DGLAP evolution equations.

## Key numbers
- The kaon form factor monopole fit gives $\Lambda^2_K = 0.687$ GeV$^2$.
- The strange quark component becomes dominant in the $K^+$ elastic form factor for $Q^2 \geq 1.6$ GeV$^2$.
- At the model scale $Q_0^2 = 0.16$ GeV$^2$, the momentum fractions carried by quarks in the $K^+$ are $\langle x\,u\rangle = 0.42$ and $\langle x\,s\rangle = 0.58$.
- The ratio $u_{K^+}/u_{\pi^+} \to 0.434 \simeq M_u^2/M_s^2$ as $x \to 1$ at $Q^2 = 16$ GeV$^2$.
- The ratio $u_{K^+}(x)/s_{K^+}(x)$ approaches $0.37$ as $x \to 1$ at $Q^2 = 16$ GeV$^2$.

## Context
This work extends the confining NJL model calculations of Hutauruk, Cloet, and Thomas (2016) for pion and kaon form factors and parton distribution functions, and Ninomiya, Bentz, and Cloet (2015) for dressed quark mass dependence of meson form factors. The results are compared to experimental data from the NA7 Collaboration (Amendolia et al., 1986) for kaon form factors and from Conway et al. (1989) for pion valence quark distributions, as well as Drell-Yan data from Badier et al. (1980) for the $K^-/\pi^-$ structure function ratio. The calculations enable future comparisons with expected data from CLAS, JPARC, COMPASS, and the future Electron Ion Collider to study flavor symmetry breaking and gluon distributions in the kaon.
