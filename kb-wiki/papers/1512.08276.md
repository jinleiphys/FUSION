# Pre-equilibrium dynamics and heavy-ion observables

> To bracket the importance of the pre-equilibrium stage on relativistic heavy-ion collision observables, we compare simulations where it is modeled by either free-streaming partons or fluid dynamics. These cases implement the assumptions of extremely weak vs. extremely strong coupling in the initial collision stage. Accounting for flow generated in the pre-equilibrium stage, we study the sensitivity of radial, elliptic and triangular flow on the switching time when the hydrodynamic description becomes valid. Using the hybrid code iEBE-VISHNU we perform a multi-parameter search, constrained by particle ratios, integrated elliptic and triangular charged hadron flow, the mean transverse momenta of pions, kaons and protons, and the second moment $\langle p_T^2\rangle$ of the proton transverse momentum spectrum, to identify optimized values for the switching time $τ_s$ from pre-equilibrium to hydrodynamics, the specific shear viscosity $η/s$, the normalization factor of the temperature-dependent specific bulk viscosity $(ζ/s)(T)$, and the switching temperature $T_\mathrm{sw}$ from viscous hydrodynamics to the hadron cascade UrQMD. With the optimized parameters, we predict and compare with experiment the $p_T$-distributions of $π$, $K$, $p$, $Λ$, $Ξ$ and $Ω$ yields and their elliptic flow coefficients, focusing specifically on the mass-ordering of the elliptic flow for protons and Lambda hyperons which is incorrectly described by VISHNU without pre-equilibrium flow.

## Key claim

The pre-equilibrium stage of a heavy-ion collision, modeled as either free-streaming partons (weak coupling) or fluid dynamics (strong coupling), significantly affects final observables; the paper finds that an extended pre-equilibrium stage increases radial flow and flatters transverse momentum spectra, but the specific mass-ordering of elliptic flow between protons and Lambda hyperons is incorrectly described by the VISHNU model without pre-equilibrium flow, and this ordering is largely eliminated (though not inverted) by the hadronic rescattering stage.

## Method

The authors used the hybrid code iEBE-VISHNU, modeling the pre-equilibrium stage as free-streaming massless partons with a variable switching time τ_s to viscous hydrodynamics (VISH2+1), followed by a hadron cascade (UrQMD) at a switching temperature T_sw. They performed a multi-parameter search over τ_s, specific shear viscosity η/s, bulk viscosity normalization factor, and T_sw, constrained by nine experimental observables from 10-20% central Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 A TeV, using Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling to identify optimized parameters and then ran 400 event-by-event simulations with fluctuating initial conditions to predict transverse momentum distributions and elliptic flows.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit switching time τ_s = 2.233 fm/c (mean 1.889 fm/c, 95% confidence interval 1.187 - 2.591 fm/c)
- Best-fit specific shear viscosity η/s = 0.135 (mean 0.143, 95% C.I. 0.124 - 0.161)
- Best-fit switching temperature T_sw = 133.4 MeV (mean 134.0 MeV, 95% C.I. 128.5 - 151.1 MeV)
- Best-fit bulk viscosity normalization factor = 3.998 (mean 3.277, 95% C.I. not available)
- Experimental constraints used: integrated elliptic flow ⟨v₂^ch⟩ = 0.0782 ± 0.0019; integrated triangular flow ⟨v₃^ch⟩ = 0.0316 ± 0.0008; mean transverse momenta ⟨p_T⟩ for pions (0.542 ± 0.018 GeV/c), kaons (0.825 ± 0.028 GeV/c), protons (1.311 ± 0.043 GeV/c); second moment ⟨p_T²⟩ for protons (2.085 ± 0.070 GeV²/c²); particle ratios (π⁺/K⁺ = 6.691 ± 0.670, π⁺/p = 21.667 ± 2.292, π⁺/Λ = 26.765 ± 3.639)

## Context

This work extends the hybrid hydrodynamic+cascade approach of Shen (2014) by systematically including pre-equilibrium flow effects, which were previously neglected in VISHNU simulations. It builds on earlier studies of pre-equilibrium dynamics by Kolb (2002), Broniowski (2008), Schenke (2012), van der Schee (2013), and Romatschke (2015). The results enable improved model-to-data comparisons for heavy-ion collisions at the LHC, particularly for the mass-ordering of elliptic flow between protons and Lambda hyperons, which was incorrectly described by Zhu (2015) without pre-equilibrium flow.
