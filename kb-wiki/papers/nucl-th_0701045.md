# Dihadron Tomography of High-Energy Nuclear Collisions in NLO pQCD

> Back-to-back dihadron spectra in high-energy heavy-ion collisions are studied within the next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD parton model with jet quenching incorporated via modified jet fragmentation functions due to radiative parton energy loss in dense medium. The experimentally observed appearance of back-to-back dihadrons at high $p_T$ is found to originate mainly from jet pairs produced close and tangential to the surface of the dense matter. However, a substantial fraction of observed high $p_T$ dihadrons also comes from jets produced at the center of the medium after losing finite amount of energy. Consequently, the suppression factor of such high-$p_T$ hadron pairs is found to be more sensitive to the initial gluon density than the single hadron spectra that are dominated by surface emission. A simultaneous $χ^2$-fit to both the single and dihadron spectra can be achieved within a narrow range of the energy loss parameters $ε_0=1.6-2.1$ GeV/fm. Because of the flattening of the initial jet production spectra, high $p_T$ dihadrons at the LHC energy are found to be more robust as probes of the dense medium.

## Key claim

Back-to-back dihadron spectra at high transverse momentum are more sensitive to the initial gluon density than single hadron spectra, because a substantial fraction (about 25%) of observed high-pT dihadrons still comes from jets produced at the center of the medium after losing finite energy, whereas single hadron spectra are dominated by surface emission. However, if the initial gluon density were to increase further, dihadron spectra at RHIC would also become dominated by surface emission and lose sensitivity.

## Method

The authors employed a next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD parton model with a Monte Carlo based program using two-cutoff parameters to isolate soft and collinear divergences. Jet quenching was incorporated via effective medium-modified fragmentation functions that include radiative parton energy loss parameterized as a path integral over the gluon density distribution in a 1-d expanding medium, with the energy loss parameter ε0 proportional to the initial gluon density ρ0. The spatial distribution of initial jet production points was given by the nuclear overlap function T_AB(b,r), and the calculations were compared to PHENIX and STAR data for central Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Energy loss parameter range from simultaneous χ²-fit to both single and dihadron spectra: ε0 = 1.6–2.1 GeV/fm
- Nuclear modification factor R_AA for single π⁰ spectra in NLO pQCD calculated with ε0 = 1.48, 1.68, and 2.08 GeV/fm (from top to bottom) for central Au+Au (0–10%) at √s = 200 GeV
- Factorization scale for dihadron spectra chosen as μ = 1.2 M, where M² = (p₁+p₂)², with NLO results compared to d+Au data at √s = 200 GeV
- Dihadron suppression factor I_AA calculated for pT_trig = 8–15 GeV and zT = 0.45–0.95 in central Au+Au at √s = 200 GeV
- At LHC energy √s = 5.5 TeV, model estimate gives ε0 ≈ 5 GeV/fm for central Pb+Pb collisions
- About 25% of dihadron contribution comes from dijets near the center of the overlapped region

## Context

This work extends the lowest-order (LO) pQCD parton model study by Wang (2003) to NLO, and addresses the fragility of single hadron suppression as a probe of initial gluon density, as noted by Eskola and collaborators. The finding that dihadron spectra are more sensitive to the initial gluon density than single hadron spectra confirms earlier suggestions by Dainese and Renk. The simultaneous χ²-fit to both single and dihadron spectra provides convincing evidence for the jet quenching description, and the results enable future LHC measurements to use dihadron tomography as a robust probe of the dense medium.
