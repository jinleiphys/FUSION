# Proton-proton correlations in distinguishing the two-proton emission mechanism of $^{23}$Al and $^{22}$Mg

> The proton-proton momentum correlation functions ($C_{pp}(q)$) for kinematically complete decay channels of $^{23}$Al $\rightarrow$ p + p + $^{21}$Na and $^{22}$Mg $\rightarrow$ p + p + $^{20}$Ne have been measured at the RIKEN RI Beam Factory. From the very different correlation strength of $C_{pp}(q)$ for $^{23}$Al and $^{22}$Mg, the source size and emission time information were extracted from the $C_{pp}(q)$ data by assuming a Gaussian source profile in the correlation function calculation code (CRAB). The results indicated that the mechanism of two-proton emission from $^{23}$Al was mainly sequential emission, while that of $^{22}$Mg was mainly three-body simultaneous emission. By combining our earlier results of the two-proton relative momentum and the opening angle, it is pointed out that the mechanism of two-proton emission could be distinguished clearly.

## Key claim

The proton-proton momentum correlation function \(C_{pp}(q)\) can distinguish between sequential and three-body simultaneous two-proton emission mechanisms, but only when both source size and emission time are extracted together; the abstract's claim of "distinguishing clearly" depends on combining the correlation function with earlier relative-momentum and opening-angle analyses.

## Method

The experiment used a 135\(A\) MeV \({}^{28}\)Si primary beam at the RIKEN RI Beam Factory to produce secondary beams of \({}^{23}\)Al (57.4\(A\) MeV) and \({}^{22}\)Mg (53.5\(A\) MeV) incident on a carbon target. Proton-proton momentum correlation functions were constructed via the event-mixing technique with an iterative calculation to remove residual correlations, and the data were fitted using the CRAB code assuming a Gaussian source profile \(S(r,t) \sim \exp(-r^2/2r_0^2 - t/\tau)\) to extract the source size parameter \(r_0\) and the emission lifetime \(\tau\).

## Key numbers

* For the inclusive channel of \({}^{23}\)Al \(\rightarrow\) p + p + X, the source size parameter was \(r_0 = 3.15\)–\(3.25\) fm (rms radius \(R_{\text{rms}} = 5.46\)–\(5.63\) fm).
* For the inclusive channel of \({}^{22}\)Mg \(\rightarrow\) p + p + X, the source size parameter was \(r_0 = 2.9\)–\(3.0\) fm.
* For the kinematically complete channel \({}^{23}\)Al \(\rightarrow\) p + p + \({}^{21}\)Na, the source size was \(r_0 = 3.9\)–\(4.7\) fm; with time included, the best-fit ranges were \(r_0 = 1.2\)–\(2.8\) fm and \(\tau = 600\)–\(2450\) fm/c.
* For the kinematically complete channel \({}^{22}\)Mg \(\rightarrow\) p + p + \({}^{20}\)Ne, the source size was \(r_0 = 2.35\)–\(2.45\) fm; with time included, the best-fit ranges were \(r_0 = 2.2\)–\(2.4\) fm and \(\tau = 0\)–\(50\) fm/c.
* For the channel \({}^{23}\)Al \(\rightarrow\) p + p + \({}^{20}\)Ne (where one proton was not detected), the source size was \(r_0 = 3.1\)–\(3.3\) fm; with time included, the best-fit ranges were \(r_0 = 0.4\)–\(2.0\) fm and \(\tau = 350\)–\(1950\) fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the earlier relative-momentum and opening-angle analysis of the same two-proton emission channels by Fang et al. (2015), which could not distinguish sequential from three-body simultaneous emission. By applying two-particle intensity interferometry to two-proton emitters for the first time, the authors show that the emission time difference extracted from \(C_{pp}(q)\) clearly separates the sequential decay of \({}^{23}\)Al (\(\tau > 600\) fm/c) from the simultaneous decay of \({}^{22}\)Mg (\(\tau < 50\) fm/c). The method enables future studies of two-proton emission mechanisms in other proton-rich nuclei near the drip line.
