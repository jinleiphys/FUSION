# The Sivers Asymmetry in Hadronic Dijet Production

> We study the single spin asymmetry in the back-to-back dijet production in transversely polarized proton-proton collisions. Such an asymmetry is generated by the Sivers functions in the incoming polarized proton. We propose a QCD formalism in terms of the transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions, which allow us to resum the large logarithms that arise in the perturbative calculations. We make predictions for the Sivers asymmetry of hadronic dijet production at the kinematic region that is relevant to the experiment at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). We further compute the spin asymmetries in the selected positive and negative jet charge bins, to separate the contributions from $u$- and $d$-quark Sivers functions. We find that both the sign and size of our numerical results are roughly consistent with the preliminary results from the STAR collaboration at the RHIC.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a QCD formalism for the Sivers asymmetry in back-to-back dijet production at RHIC, finding that both the sign and size of their numerical results are "roughly consistent" with preliminary STAR collaboration data, though the formalism assumes a factorized form that may be subject to TMD factorization breaking effects from Glauber gluon exchanges.

## Method

The authors develop a factorized QCD formalism within the soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) framework, expressing the spin-dependent cross section in terms of transverse momentum dependent (TMD) parton distribution functions, including the Sivers function for the polarized proton. They perform Fourier transforms to impact-parameter space to resum large logarithms, derive process-dependent hard functions in color matrix form for both unpolarized and polarized scattering, and compute the Sivers asymmetry by taking the ratio of the spin-dependent to unpolarized differential cross sections. The formalism incorporates jet functions for anti-kT jets with radius R, collinear-soft functions, and leading-logarithmic non-global logarithm resummation.

## Key numbers

* The partonic Mandelstam variables are defined as $\hat{s} = 4P_\perp^2 \cosh^2(\Delta y/2)$, $\hat{t} = -2P_\perp^2 e^{-\Delta y/2}\cosh(\Delta y/2)$, and $\hat{u} = -2P_\perp^2 e^{\Delta y/2}\cosh(\Delta y/2)$, where $P_\perp$ is the average transverse momentum of the two jets and $\Delta y = y_c - y_d$ is the rapidity difference.
* The matching coefficient for the Sivers function onto the collinear Qiu-Sterman function at NLO is given by $C^T_{q\leftarrow q'}(x, \mu_b) = \delta_{qq'}[\delta(1-x) + (\alpha_s(\mu_b)/2\pi)(-1/(2N_c))(1-x)]$.
* The initial scale for TMD evolution is $\mu_b = b_0/b$ with $b_0 = 2e^{-\gamma_E}$.
* The spin-dependent cross section contains a $\sin(\phi_q - \phi_S)$ modulation, where $\phi_q$ and $\phi_S$ are the azimuthal angles of the transverse momentum imbalance and the proton spin, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for Sivers asymmetry in dijet production originally proposed by Boer and Vogelsang (2003) for RHIC, and builds upon recent developments in QCD resummation for back-to-back dijet production by Sun, Yuan, and collaborators (2014, 2015) and for vector boson-jet production by Buffing, Chien, and collaborators (2018-2020). The formalism enables comparison with ongoing STAR collaboration measurements using a charge-weighting method to separate u- and d-quark Sivers function contributions, and provides a starting point for studying TMD factorization breaking effects from Glauber gluon exchanges as discussed by Collins and Rogers (2007, 2010).
