# Initial-state temperature of light meson emission source from squared momentum transfer spectra in high-energy collisions

> The squared momentum transfer spectra of light mesons, $π^0$, $π^+$, $η$, and $ρ^0$, produced in high-energy virtual photon-proton ($γ^{*} p$) $\rightarrow {\rm meson + nucleon}$ process in electron-proton ($ep$) collisions measured by the CLAS Collaboration are analyzed by the Monte Carlo calculations, where the transfer undergoes from the incident $γ^*$ to emitted meson or equivalently from the target proton to emitted nucleon. In the calculations, the Erlang distribution from a multi-source thermal model is used to describe the transverse momentum spectra of emitted particles. Our results show that the average transverse momentum ($\langle p_T\rangle$) and the initial-state temperature ($T_i$) increase from lower squared photon virtuality ($Q^2$) and Bjorken variable ($x_B$) to higher one. This renders that the excitation degree of emission source, which is described by $\langle p_T\rangle$ and $T_i$, increases with increasing of $Q^2$ and $x_B$.

## Key claim

The average transverse momentum and initial-state temperature of the emission source increase with higher squared photon virtuality and Bjorken variable, indicating that the excitation degree of the system grows with the hardness of the reaction, but this conclusion is based on a Monte Carlo transformation from transverse momentum to squared momentum transfer spectra using the Erlang distribution from a multi-source thermal model, not on direct temperature measurements.

## Method

The authors used the Erlang distribution from a multi-source thermal model to describe transverse momentum spectra of emitted particles, then applied a Monte Carlo method to transform these spectra into squared momentum transfer distributions for the processes gamma* p -> meson + nucleon. The squared momentum transfer spectra of pi0, pi+, eta, and rho0 mesons produced in electron-proton collisions measured by the CLAS Collaboration were fitted to extract the average transverse momentum contributed by each participant parton and the number of participant partons, from which the average transverse momentum and initial-state temperature were calculated using a color string percolation method with a single string assumption.

## Key numbers

- For gamma* p -> pi0 p at Q2 in (4.0,4.6) GeV2 and xB in (0.48,0.58): Ti = 0.666 +/- 0.020 GeV, with ns = 5 partons
- For gamma* p -> pi+ n at Q2 = 4.35 GeV2 and xB = 0.55: Ti = 0.523 +/- 0.008 GeV, with ns = 5 partons
- For gamma* p -> eta p at Q2 in (4.0,4.6) GeV2 and xB in (0.48,0.58): Ti = 0.776 +/- 0.020 GeV, with ns = 5 partons
- For gamma* p -> rho0 p at Q2 in (5.1,5.6) GeV2 and xB in (0.64,0.70): Ti = 1.053 +/- 0.025 GeV, with ns = 3 partons
- For gamma* p -> pi0 p at Q2 in (1.0,1.5) GeV2 and xB in (0.10,0.15): Ti = 0.409 +/- 0.005 GeV, with ns = 3 partons
- The initial-state temperature values range from approximately 0.279 GeV (pi+ n at Q2=1.75 GeV2, xB=0.25) to 1.053 GeV (rho0 p at highest Q2 and xB)

## Context

This work extends the multi-source thermal model approach previously applied to nucleus-nucleus collisions (Liu, 2008; Liu and Li, 2003) to the smaller system of electron-proton collisions, specifically analyzing CLAS Collaboration data (2009, 2012, 2014, 2017) for exclusive meson production. The results enable systematic comparison of initial-state temperatures across different meson species and kinematic regimes, and provide a method to extract temperature information from squared momentum transfer spectra when transverse momentum spectra are not directly available.
