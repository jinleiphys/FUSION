# Corrections to nuclear energies and radii in finite oscillator spaces

> We derive corrections to the ground-state energies and radii of atomic nuclei that result from the limitations of finite oscillator spaces.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytical corrections to nuclear ground-state energies and radii that arise from the finite spatial extent of harmonic-oscillator basis spaces, assuming that ultraviolet (momentum-space) convergence has already been achieved. The key hedge is that the corrections are derived under the assumption that the UV condition (interaction momentum cutoff smaller than the basis UV cutoff) is satisfied, so that the dominant remaining error comes from the infrared (coordinate-space) truncation.

## Method

The authors adapt Luscher's finite-box correction method to the oscillator basis, treating the maximum radial extent L of the oscillator space as an effective box size with Dirichlet boundary conditions. They use a linear energy expansion of the radial wave function around the infinite-space solution, evaluate it at the boundary r = L where the truncated wave function must vanish, and extract the leading exponential correction to the energy. For radii, they expand the squared-radius expectation value using the same asymptotic wave function forms, yielding a correction proportional to powers of (2kL) times exp(-2kL), where k is determined by the nucleon separation energy. The results are tested on a one-dimensional toy model and applied to coupled-cluster calculations of 16O and no-core full-configuration (NCFC) calculations of the halo nucleus 6He.

## Key numbers

- For 16O ground-state energy: extrapolated value E_infinity ≈ -122.6 MeV from fits to coupled-cluster data with oscillator spaces up to N=12,14 and frequencies 42 ≤ hbar-Omega/MeV ≤ 76, using UV cutoffs above 1100 MeV.
- For 16O radius: extrapolated point-proton radius r ≈ 2.34 fm from fits to the squared-radius data with k_infinity fixed from the energy fit.
- For 6He ground-state energy: extrapolated value E_infinity ≈ -29.87 MeV from NCFC data with UV cutoff above 660 MeV and hbar-Omega ≥ 24 MeV, yielding a two-neutron separation energy of about 0.95 MeV.
- For 6He neutron radius: extrapolated value r ≈ 2.40 fm at next-to-leading order from NCFC data, in reasonable agreement with experimental deductions from Alkhazov (1997).
- For 6He combined UV+IR fit: extrapolated E_infinity ≈ -29.84 MeV using all Nmax = 6–10 data points with the combined formula.

## Context

This work extends the insights of Coon et al. (2012) on infrared and ultraviolet cutoffs induced by truncated oscillator bases, providing a theoretical foundation for empirical extrapolation schemes previously used by Hagen (2007), Bogner (2008), Forssen (2008), Maris (2009), and Roth (2009). The derived exponential corrections explain the empirical exponential decay observed by Coon et al. (2012) and enable reliable extrapolations for weakly bound halo nuclei such as 6He, where the Gaussian falloff of the oscillator basis cannot capture the extended tail in achievable model spaces. The results also enable uncertainty quantification for finite-basis errors in ab initio nuclear structure computations, and can be extended to other long-range observables sensitive to the tail of the nuclear density.
