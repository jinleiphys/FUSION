# Fractionized Skyrmions in Dense Compact-Star Matter

> The hadronic matter described as a skyrmion matter embedded in an FCC crystal is found to turn into a half-skyrmion matter with vanishing (in the chiral limit) quark condensate and {\it non-vanishing} pion decay constant {$f_π$} at a density $n_{1/2}$ lower than or near the critical density $n_c$ at which hadronic matter changes over to a chiral symmetry restored phase with possibly deconfined quarks. When hidden local gauge fields and a dilaton scalar of spontaneously broken scale symmetry with decay constant $f_χ$ are incorporated, this half-skyrmion phase is characterized by $f_χ\approx f_π\neq 0$ with the hidden gauge coupling $g\neq 0$ but $\ll 1$. While chiral symmetry is restored {\it globally} in this region in the sense that space-averaged, $\la\bar{q}q\ra$ vanishes, quarks are still confined in massive hadrons and massless pions. This phase is shown to play a crucial role in the model for a smooth transition from a soft EoS at low density to a stiffer EoS at high density, the changeover taking place at $n_{1/2} \sim 2n_0$. It resembles the "quarkyonic phase" predicted in large $N_c$ QCD and represents the "hadronic freedom" regime which figures as a doorway to chiral restoration. The fractionization of skyrmion matter into half-skyrmion matter has a tantalizing analogy to what appears to happen in condensed matter in (2+1) dimensions where half-skyrmions or "merons" enter as relevant degrees of freedom at the interface.

## Key claim

The paper claims that skyrmion matter embedded in an FCC crystal undergoes a topology change to a half-skyrmion phase at a density \(n_{1/2}\) lower than or near the critical density \(n_c\) for chiral restoration, but this half-skyrmion phase is characterized by a globally vanishing quark condensate while the pion decay constant remains non-zero and quarks remain confined, meaning chiral symmetry is only globally restored.

## Method

The authors construct a scale-invariant hidden local symmetry (sHLS) Lagrangian that includes pions, the lowest-lying vector mesons (\(\rho, \omega\)) as hidden local gauge fields, and a scalar dilaton field \(\phi\) (identified with \(f_0(500)\)) as a pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone boson of spontaneously broken scale symmetry. They place skyrmions generated from this Lagrangian into a face-centered-cubic (FCC) crystal and study the topology change as the lattice size is decreased (density increased), using collective quantization to compute the symmetry energy and soliton mass as functions of density.

## Key numbers

- The topology change from skyrmions to half-skyrmions occurs at a density \(n_{1/2} \sim 2 n_0\), where \(n_0\) is normal nuclear matter density.
- In the half-skyrmion phase, the hidden gauge coupling \(g\) satisfies \(g \neq 0\) but \(g \ll 1\).
- In the half-skyrmion phase, the dilaton decay constant satisfies \(f_\chi \approx f_\pi \neq 0\).
- For the HLS\((\pi,\rho,\omega)\) case (full hidden local gauge invariance with \(U(2)\) symmetry), the baryon mass ratio satisfies \(m_N^\star/m_N \approx f_\pi^\star/f_\pi\) throughout the density range explored, with the baryon mass dropping smoothly up to \(n_{1/2}\) and then stabilizing to a density-independent constant.
- In the same case, the baryon mass can be written as \(m_N^\star/m_N \approx A + \Delta(\langle \bar{q}q \rangle)\) with \(A \sim (0.7-0.9)\) and \(\Delta(0)=0\), meaning 70-90% of the baryon mass remains non-zero when the quark condensate averages to zero.

## Context

This work extends the skyrmion crystal approach to dense baryonic matter previously developed by two of the authors (Lee and Rho) in the first volume of "The Multifaceted Skyrmion," and builds on the topology change from skyrmions to half-skyrmions described by Park and Vento in the same volume. The half-skyrmion phase resembles the "quarkyonic phase" predicted in large \(N_c\) QCD and represents the "hadronic freedom" regime as a doorway to chiral restoration. The approach enables a smooth transition from a soft equation of state at low density to a stiffer equation of state at high density, relevant for compact-star interiors, and has a tantalizing analogy to half-skyrmions (merons) appearing in condensed matter systems in (2+1) dimensions.
