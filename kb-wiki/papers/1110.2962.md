# Triply Heavy Baryons and Heavy Quark Spin Symmetry

> We study the semileptonic $b\to c$ decays of the lowest-lying triply-heavy baryons made from $b$ and $c$ quarks in the limit $m_b, m_c \gg Λ_\mathrm{QCD}$ and close to the zero recoil point. The separate heavy quark spin symmetries strongly constrain the matrix elements, leading to single form factors for $ccb\to ccc$, $bbc\to ccb$, and $bbb\to bbc$ baryon decays. We also study the effects on these systems of using a $Y$-shaped confinement potential, as suggested by lattice QCD results for the interaction between three static quarks.

## Key claim

The authors derive heavy quark spin symmetry (HQSS) relations for semileptonic \(b\to c\) decays of triply-heavy baryons, showing that near the zero recoil point the matrix elements reduce to single form factors for each decay channel, but these relations are only approximate and hold at the level of 20-30% for some decay width ratios due to deviations from the strict zero recoil approximation.

## Method

The authors construct baryon wave functions using the trace formalism with tensor products of Dirac matrices and spinors, respecting heavy quark spin symmetry in the limit \(m_b, m_c \gg \Lambda_\mathrm{QCD}\). They then evaluate the Isgur-Wise functions \(\eta(w)\), \(\chi(w)\), and \(\xi(w)\) using a nonrelativistic constituent quark model with the AL1 interquark potential, solving the three-body problem variationally with Gaussian basis functions. The decay widths are computed from the HQSS-constrained hadronic tensors combined with the leptonic tensor, using \(|V_{cb}| = 0.0410\) and \(G_F = 1.16637(1) \times 10^{-11}\,\mathrm{MeV}^{-2}\).

## Key numbers

- Mass of \(\Omega^*_{bbb}\): 14398 MeV (variational, AL1 potential); lattice QCD gives \(14371 \pm 12\) MeV (Meinel 2010)
- Mass of \(\Xi^*_{bbc}\): 11245 MeV; mass of \(\Xi_{bbc}\): 11214 MeV
- Mass of \(\Xi^*_{ccb}\): 8046 MeV; mass of \(\Xi_{ccb}\): 8018 MeV
- Mass of \(\Omega^*_{ccc}\): 4799 MeV (variational, AL1 potential)
- Using a \(Y\)-shaped confinement potential instead of \(\Delta\)-shaped increases masses by roughly 26, 34, 40, and 48 MeV for \(bbb\), \(bbc\), \(ccb\), and \(ccc\) systems respectively
- The ratio \(\Gamma(\Xi_{ccb}\to \Omega^*_{ccc}) / [\frac85\Gamma(\Xi^*_{ccb}\to \Omega^*_{ccc})] \approx 1\) holds at the 20-30% level
- The ratio \(\Gamma(\Xi_{bbc}\to \Xi_{ccb}) / [\frac{25}{8}\Gamma(\Xi_{bbc}\to \Xi^*_{ccb})] \approx 1\) holds at the 5.5% level
- The ratio \(2\Gamma(\Xi^*_{bbc}\to \Xi_{ccb}) / \Gamma(\Xi_{bbc}\to \Xi^*_{ccb}) \approx 1\) holds at the 3.4% level
- Maximum \(w\) values: \(w_{\mathrm{max}} \approx 1.125\) for \(bcc\to ccc\), \(\approx 1.06\) for \(bbc\to bcc\), \(\approx 1.03\) for \(bbb\to bbc\) transitions
- Differential decay widths peak near \(w \approx 1.005\), \(1.009\), and \(1.025\) for \(bbb\), \(bbc\), and \(ccb\) decays respectively

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous HQSS studies of single and doubly heavy baryons (Albertus 2003, Albertus 2006, Hernandez 2007) to triply-heavy systems, deriving for the first time HQSS relations for their semileptonic decays. It builds on earlier mass predictions from bag models (Hasenfratz 1980), nonrelativistic constituent quark models (Silvestre-Brac 1996, Vijande 2004, Roberts 2007), relativistic three-quark models (Martynenko 2007), pNRQCD (Brambilla 2005, Brambilla 2009), QCD sum rules (Zhang 2009), and lattice QCD (Meinel 2010). The results enable approximate model-independent predictions for decay width ratios and absolute widths, which could be tested at the LHC where \(10^4\)--\(10^5\) triply heavy baryon events are expected per 10 fb of integrated luminosity (Chen 2011).
