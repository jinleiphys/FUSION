# Asymmetry parameter for nonmesonic hypernuclear decay

> We derive general explicit expressions for a shell-model calculation of the intrinsic asymmetry parameter, $a_Λ$, in nonmesonic weak hypernuclear decay. They include the effects of three-body kinematics in the final states of the decay and correctly treat the contribution of transitions originating from single-proton states beyond the s-shell. The expressions are particularized to the cases of $^5_Λ$He and $^{12}_Λ$C and used for the calculation of $a_Λ$ in several one-meson-exchange models. Besides the strictly local approximation usually adopted for the transition potential, we also consider the addition of the first-order nonlocality terms. We find values for $a_Λ$ ranging from -0.62 to -0.24, in qualitative agreement with other theoretical estimates but in contradiction with some recent experimental determinations.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general shell-model expression for the intrinsic asymmetry parameter \(a_\Lambda\) in nonmesonic hypernuclear decay that correctly includes three-body kinematics and contributions from single-proton states beyond the s-shell, finding values ranging from \(-0.62\) to \(-0.24\) that are in qualitative agreement with other theoretical estimates but in contradiction with some recent experimental determinations.

## Method

The authors derive general expressions for the vector hypernuclear asymmetry \(A_V\) and the intrinsic asymmetry parameter \(a_\Lambda\) using spectroscopic amplitudes rather than spectroscopic factors, working within the weak-coupling model and extreme shell model for the specific cases of \(^5_\Lambda\)He and \(^{12}_\Lambda\)C. They compute the decay strengths and asymmetry parameter by integrating over the available phase space with three-body kinematics, using a Moshinsky transformation and short-range correlations, and evaluate the transition potential in several one-meson-exchange models both in the strictly local approximation and with first-order nonlocality corrections.

## Key numbers

- For \(^5_\Lambda\)He, the calculated \(a_\Lambda\) values range from \(-0.2449\) (strictly local \(\pi+\rho\) model) to \(-0.6153\) (corrected \((\pi,\eta,K)\) model), compared to experimental values of \(0.24 \pm 0.22\) (KEK-PS E278) and \(0.11 \pm 0.08 \pm 0.04\) (KEK-PS E462 preliminary).
- For \(^{12}_\Lambda\)C, the calculated \(a_\Lambda\) values range from \(-0.2379\) (strictly local \(\pi+\rho\) model) to \(-0.5975\) (corrected \((\pi,\eta,K)\) model), compared to experimental values of \(-0.9 \pm 0.3\) (KEK-PS E160) and \(-0.44 \pm 0.32\) (KEK-PS E508 preliminary).
- The \(\omega_0\) and \(\omega_1\) decay strengths are given in units of the free \(\Lambda\) decay constant \(\Gamma_\Lambda^{(0)} = 2.50 \times 10^{-6}\) eV; for example, in the complete \((\pi,\eta,K)+(\rho,\omega,K^*)\) model for \(^5_\Lambda\)He, \(\omega_0 = 0.5352\) and \(\omega_1 = -0.2739\) (strictly local), and \(\omega_0 = 0.5526\) and \(\omega_1 = -0.2974\) (with corrections).
- The first-order nonlocality corrections increase the absolute values of \(\omega_\kappa\) by 20–30% but affect \(a_\Lambda\) by only 5–10%.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical calculations of the asymmetry parameter in nonmesonic hypernuclear decay, which had mostly used an approximate formula valid only for s-shell hypernuclei, by providing a general formalism applicable to both \(^5_\Lambda\)He and \(^{12}_\Lambda\)C. The results are compared with earlier OME model calculations by Dubach (1996), Parreno (1997), and Barbero (2002), and with experimental data from KEK experiments by Ajimura (1992, 2000) and Maruta (2005). The paper enables more precise comparisons between theory and experiment for the asymmetry parameter, which is sensitive to parity-violating amplitudes and may help discriminate between different models of the weak hypernuclear decay mechanism.
