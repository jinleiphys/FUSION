# Heavy Baryons with Strangeness in a Soliton Model

> We present results from a chiral soliton model calculation for the spectrum of baryons with a single heavy quark (charm or bottom) and non-zero strangeness. We treat the strange components within a three flavor collective coordinate quantization of the soliton that fully accounts for light flavor symmetry breaking. Heavy baryons emerge by binding a heavy meson to the soliton. The dynamics of this heavy meson is described by the heavy quark effective theory with finite mass effects included.

## Key claim

The paper presents a chiral soliton model calculation for the spectrum of baryons containing a single heavy quark (charm or bottom) and non-zero strangeness, treating light flavor symmetry breaking exactly via the Yabu-Ando diagonalization method rather than perturbatively, and including finite mass corrections to the heavy quark effective theory.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral soliton stabilized by light vector mesons (rho and omega), then quantize the soliton using three-flavor collective coordinates that fully account for SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking. Heavy baryons are formed by binding a heavy meson (charm or bottom) to the soliton, with the heavy meson dynamics described by a Lagrangian that extends heavy quark effective theory to include finite mass effects. The bound state wavefunctions for the heavy meson are obtained in both P-wave and S-wave channels, and the collective coordinate Hamiltonian is diagonalized exactly using the Yabu-Ando approach for the strangeness-changing angle, yielding mass formulas that depend on the symmetry breaking parameter x.

## Context

This work extends the bound state approach to heavy baryons originally developed by Callan and Klebanov (1985) for hyperons, and later applied to heavy baryons by Rho, Goldhaber, and collaborators (1992) without heavy spin-flavor symmetry, and by Momen, Schechter, and Subbaraman (1993) with perturbative symmetry breaking. The present calculation improves upon prior work by Schechter and collaborators (1995) and Harada and collaborators (1997) by including strangeness degrees of freedom, treating SU(3) symmetry breaking exactly via the Yabu-Ando diagonalization method (1987) rather than perturbatively, and using parameters from an actual soliton model calculation rather than fitting. The results enable comparison with experimental candidates from the Particle Data Group (2014) and with other approaches such as quark models (Roberts, 2007; Lipkin, 2011), quark-diquark models (Ebert, 2007), QCD sum rules (Zhang, 2008), and lattice QCD (Brown, 2014).
