# A three-fermion Salpeter equation

> We write a 3D equation for three fermions by combining the three two-body potentials obtained by the reductions of the corresponding two-fermion Bethe-Salpeter equations to equivalent 3D equations, putting the spectator fermion on the mass shell. In this way, the cluster-separated limits are still exact, and the Lorentz invariance / cluster separability requirement is automatically satisfied, provided no supplementary approximation, like the Born approximation, is made. The use of positive free-energy projectors in the chosen reductions of the two-fermion Bethe-Salpeter equations prevents continuum dissolution in our 3D three-fermion equation. The potentials are hermitian and depend only slowly on the total three-fermion energy. The one high-mass limits are approximately exact. In view of a possible perturbation calculation, correcting the remaining discrepancies with the three-fermion Bethe-Salpeter equation, we succeeded in deriving our 3D equation from an approximation of the three-fermion Bethe-Salpeter equation, in which the three-body kernel is neglected and the two-body kernels approached by positive-energy instantaneous expressions, with the spectator fermion on the mass shell. The neglected terms are transformed into corrections to the 3D equation. A comparison is made with Gross' spectator model.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a three-dimensional (3D) equation for three fermions by combining three two-body potentials, each derived from a reduction of the corresponding two-fermion Bethe-Salpeter equation with the spectator fermion placed on mass shell; the resulting equation satisfies Lorentz invariance and cluster separability exactly only if no supplementary approximation (such as the Born approximation) is made, and the use of positive-energy projectors prevents continuum dissolution.

## Method

The author starts from the two-fermion Bethe-Salpeter equation and performs a 3D reduction using a constraint that fixes the relative energy (choosing the constraint based on the first-order equations, \(p_0 = s\), with the Breit or Salpeter propagator). For the three-fermion case, the three two-body potentials \(V_{12}, V_{23}, V_{31}\) are combined into a single 3D equation, with each potential evaluated at the partial energy \(P_0 - h_k\) (the spectator fermion on mass shell). Positive-energy projectors \(\Lambda^{++}_{ij}\) are inserted around each two-body potential to prevent continuum dissolution, yielding the final equation \(\psi = (P_0 - S)^{-1} (\Lambda^{++}_{12} V_{12} \Lambda^{++}_{12} + \Lambda^{++}_{23} V_{23} \Lambda^{++}_{23} + \Lambda^{++}_{31} V_{31} \Lambda^{++}_{31}) \psi\). The derivation is also related back to the three-fermion Bethe-Salpeter equation by neglecting the three-body kernel and approximating the two-body kernels as positive-energy instantaneous expressions, with the neglected terms transformed into corrections.

## Key numbers

- The free propagator \(G_0\) is approximated by \(G_\delta = \delta(p_0 - s) A G_B\), where \(G_B = -2i\pi (P_0 - S)^{-1} \beta_1 \beta_2\) (Breit propagator) and \(A\) can be \(\tau\), 1, or \(\Lambda^{++}\).
- The operator \(\tau\) is defined as \(\tau = \frac{1}{2}(\tau_1 + \tau_2)\) with \(\tau_i = h_i / \sqrt{h_i^2}\), and the projectors are \(\Lambda_i^\pm = (E_i \pm h_i) / (2E_i)\).
- In the continuum dissolution analysis, the energy of a \((+,-)\) two-body system in the rest frame is \(E_1 - E_2 = (m_1^2 - m_2^2) / (\sqrt{\vec p^2 + m_1^2} + \sqrt{\vec p^2 + m_2^2})\), lying between \(m_1 - m_2\) and zero.
- For three-fermion asymptotically free states, the energy \(E_3 - E_1 - E_2\) lies between \(-\infty\) and \((m_3 - m_1 - m_2) \theta(m_3 - m_1 - m_2)\).
- In the heavy-mass limit where \(m_3 \to \infty\), the equation becomes \(W_{12} \psi = (h_1 + h_2 + \Lambda^{++}_{12} V_{12} \Lambda^{++}_{12} + \Lambda^+_2 V^+_2 \Lambda^+_2 + \Lambda^+_1 V^+_1 \Lambda^+_1) \psi\), with \(V^+_2\) given by the series \(V^+_2 = V_2 + V_2 (\Lambda^-_2 / (W_{12} - h_1 - h_2)) V_2 + \dots\).

## Context

This work extends the 3D reduction techniques for the two-fermion Bethe-Salpeter equation (Salpeter (1952), Breit) to the three-fermion case, addressing the cluster separability and Lorentz invariance requirements that are automatic in the two-body problem but nontrivial for three bodies. The approach is compared with Gross' spectator model (Gross, 1982) and builds on prior work by Bijtebier (1997) on 3D reductions and the Sazdjian propagator (Sazdjian, 1987). The resulting equation enables perturbative calculations by providing a manageable 3D starting point, with the neglected terms from the full Bethe-Salpeter equation transformed into corrections, and it opens the way to studying three-fermion bound states (e.g., in QED or QCD) while avoiding the continuum dissolution problem.
