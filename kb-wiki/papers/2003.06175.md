# Uncertainty of three-nucleon continuum observables arising from uncertainties of two-nucleon potential parameters

> Propagation of uncertainties from two-nucleon potential parameters to three-nucleon observables, that is statistical errors for the neutron-deuteron elastic scattering and the deuteron breakup reaction at neutron laboratory energies up to 200 MeV is investigated. To that end we use the chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction with the semi-local momentum-space regularization at various orders of the chiral expansion, exploiting knowledge of the covariance matrix of its parameters. For both reactions we compare statistical uncertainties for chiral predictions with the uncertainties obtained in the same way but for the semi-phenomenological One-Pion-Exchange two-nucleon force. In addition for the elastic scattering we show also the truncation errors arising from restriction to a given order of chiral predictions, estimated among others within the Bayesian method, and the cutoff dependence of chiral predictions. We find that the resulting statistical uncertainty is smaller than the truncation errors for the chiral force at lower orders of the chiral expansion. At the higher orders of the chiral expansion and at low energies the statistical errors exceed the truncation ones but at intermediate and higher energies truncation errors are more important. Overall, magnitudes of the theoretical uncertainties are small and amount up to 0.5\%-4\%, depending on the observable and energy. We also find that the magnitudes of statistical uncertainties for the chiral and semi-phenomenological potentials are similar and that the dependence of predictions on the regularization parameter values is important at all investigated energies.

## Key claim

The statistical uncertainties arising from two-nucleon potential parameters propagate to three-nucleon continuum observables and are generally smaller than truncation errors at lower chiral orders, but at higher orders and low energies the statistical errors can exceed the truncation errors, while at intermediate and higher energies truncation errors dominate.

## Method

The authors solved the three-nucleon Faddeev equations in momentum-space partial wave basis using the chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction with semi-local momentum-space regularization (SMS) at orders from NLO to N4LO+. They sampled 50 sets of potential parameters from a multivariate normal distribution using the known covariance matrix, computed observables for each set, and used the half-spread of the central 68% of predictions (1/2 Δ68%) as the estimator of statistical uncertainty. Truncation errors were estimated using both the method of Epelbaum et al. (2015) and a Bayesian approach following Furnstahl et al. (2015) and Melendez et al. (2017).

## Key numbers

- Statistical uncertainties for elastic nd scattering and deuteron breakup observables amount to 0.5%-4% depending on observable and energy
- For the deuteron vector analyzing power iT11 at E=65 MeV and θc.m.=90°, the relative difference between statistical and truncation error widths reaches a few percent
- At E=135 MeV and θc.m.=90° for iT11, the truncation error exceeds the statistical uncertainty by about 84%
- At E=200 MeV and θc.m.=90° for iT11, the truncation error exceeds the statistical uncertainty by about 92%
- For the spin-transfer coefficient Kyy'y'(d-n) at θc.m.=145°, the 1/2 Δ68% reaches 0.22% (N2LO) and 0.24% (N4LO) of the prediction at E=65 MeV, 0.30% (N2LO) and 0.43% (N4LO) at E=135 MeV, and 0.27% (N2LO) and 0.74% (N4LO) at E=200 MeV
- For the spin-transfer coefficient Ky'y'(d-n) at E=200 MeV and θc.m.=147.5°, the difference between N2LO and N4LO predictions amounts to approximately 60%, while N2LO statistical uncertainties reach 0.27% and N4LO reach 0.88%

## Context

This work extends the uncertainty propagation study of Skibiński et al. (2018), which used the semi-phenomenological OPE-Gaussian potential for elastic nd scattering, to chiral interactions and to the deuteron breakup reaction. It provides the first quantitative comparison of statistical uncertainties from NN potential parameters with truncation errors for a chiral force in three-nucleon continuum observables, using the SMS potential family of Reinert et al. (2018). The results enable more complete uncertainty quantification in few-nucleon calculations and show that the cutoff dependence of chiral predictions remains important at all investigated energies up to 200 MeV.
