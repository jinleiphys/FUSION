# Equation of State for SU(3) Gauge Theory via the Energy-Momentum Tensor under Gradient Flow

> The energy density and the pressure of SU(3) gauge theory at finite temperature are studied by direct lattice measurements of the renormalized energy-momentum tensor obtained by the gradient flow. Numerical analyses are carried out with $Î˛=6.287$--$7.500$ corresponding to the lattice spacing $a= 0.013$--$0.061\,\mathrm{fm}$. The spatial (temporal) sizes are chosen to be $N_s= 64$, $96$, $128$ ($N_Ď„=12$, $16$, $20$, $22$, $24$) with the aspect ratio, $5.33 \le N_s/N_Ď„ \le 8$. Double extrapolation, $a\rightarrow 0$ (the continuum limit) followed by $t\rightarrow 0$ (the zero flow-time limit), is taken using the numerical data. Above the critical temperature, the thermodynamic quantities are obtained with a few percent precision including statistical and systematic errors. The results are in good agreement with previous high-precision data obtained by using the integral method.

## Key claim

The energy density and pressure of SU(3) gauge theory at finite temperature can be obtained with a few percent precision (including statistical and systematic errors) by direct lattice measurements of the renormalized energy-momentum tensor constructed via the gradient flow, provided a double extrapolation (continuum limit followed by zero flow-time limit) is performed.

## Method

The authors performed lattice simulations of SU(3) gauge theory using the Wilson plaquette gauge action, generating gauge configurations via a pseudo heat bath algorithm mixed with over-relaxation. They measured the renormalized energy-momentum tensor from flowed gauge fields using the short flow-time expansion, then extracted the energy density and pressure from its thermal averages. The double extrapolation was carried out by first taking the continuum limit (a to 0) at fixed flow time t using a 1/N_tau^2 fit, then extrapolating the continuum results to zero flow time (t to 0) using a linear fit.

## Key numbers

* Lattice spacing range: a = 0.013 to 0.061 fm, corresponding to beta = 6.287 to 7.500
* Spatial lattice sizes: N_s = 64, 96, 128; temporal sizes: N_tau = 12, 16, 20, 22, 24
* Aspect ratio range: 5.33 <= N_s/N_tau <= 8
* Temperature range studied: 0.93 <= T/T_c <= 2.69
* At T/T_c = 1.68, the trace anomaly Delta/T^4 = 1.201(19)(+10/-0)(0) and entropy density s/T^3 = 5.285(35)(+44/-0)(18), where the first error is statistical, the second is systematic from fit range choice, and the third is from Lambda_MSbar uncertainty
* At T/T_c = 2.69, s/T^3 = 5.914(32)(+70/-0)(18)
* Critical coupling after infinite volume extrapolation: beta_c = 6.33552(47) at N_tau = 12
* Reference scale: w_0 T_c = 0.25244(17)
* Lambda parameter: w_0 Lambda_MSbar = 0.2154(5)(11)

## Context

This work extends the gradient flow method for calculating thermodynamic quantities from the energy-momentum tensor, first tested in SU(3) gauge theory by Asakawa (2013) with coarser lattices (a = 0.041 to 0.11 fm) where the double extrapolation could not be performed. The results agree well with previous high-precision data obtained using the integral method by Boyd (1996) and Borsanyi (2012). This approach enables future studies of correlation functions and transport coefficients of the quark-gluon plasma with a firm theoretical basis.
