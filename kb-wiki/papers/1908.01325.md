# On the stability of two-flavor and three-flavor quark stars

> Following our recenty proposed self-consistent mean-field approximation approach, we have done some researches on the chiral phase transition of strong interaction matter within the framework of Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. The chiral susceptibility and equation of state (EOS) are computed in this work for both two-flavor and three-flavor quark matter for contrast. The Pauli-Villars scheme, which can preserve gauge invariance, is used in this paper. Moreover, whether the three-flavor quark matter is more stable than the two-flavor quark matter or not in quark stars is discussed in this work. In our model, when the bag constant are the same, the two-flavor quark matter has a higher pressure than the three-flavor quark matter, which is different from what Witten proposed in his pioneering work.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, within their self-consistent mean-field NJL model using Pauli-Villars regularization, two-flavor quark matter is more stable than three-flavor quark matter when the bag constant is the same, contradicting Witten's original conjecture based on the MIT bag model. However, the authors explicitly note that this result is model-dependent because the two-flavor and three-flavor models are different models with different parameter sets, and there is no physical basis requiring them to share the same parameter α.

## Method

The authors compute the chiral susceptibility and equation of state (EOS) for both two-flavor and three-flavor quark matter within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, using a self-consistent mean-field approximation that mixes the original Lagrangian with its Fierz-transformed form via a parameter α. The Pauli-Villars regularization scheme, which preserves gauge invariance, is employed instead of the common three-momentum cutoff. The gap equations and effective chemical potentials are solved at zero temperature, and the pressure and energy density are obtained by integrating the number density, with the bag constant treated as a free parameter in the empirical range (100 MeV)^4 to (200 MeV)^4.

## Key numbers

- For the two-flavor model, the fitted parameters are: current quark mass m_u = 10.0 MeV, cutoff Λ = 778 MeV, and coupling constant g = 9.64 × 10^{-6} MeV^{-2}, obtained by fitting m_π = 135 MeV and f_π = 94 MeV.
- For the three-flavor model, the fitted parameters are: m_u = 11.8 MeV, m_s = 327.8 MeV, Λ = 743.3 MeV, gΛ² = 5.885, and KΛ⁵ = 175.5, obtained by fitting m_π = 138 MeV, f_π = 92 MeV, m_K = 495 MeV, and m_η' = 958 MeV.
- The chiral phase transition occurs when the quark chemical potential exceeds approximately 308 MeV, a model-independent result that quarks can be excited from the vacuum only when the chemical potential is comparable to the constituent quark mass.
- For a two-flavor quark star with α = 0.95 and B = (120 MeV)^4, the tidal deformability for a 1.4 M_sun star is 557; for a three-flavor quark star with α = 0.8 and the same B, it is 570, both below the LIGO/Virgo constraint of 580.
- For two-flavor quark stars, α must lie in the interval 0.91–0.95 to simultaneously satisfy the two-solar-mass constraint and the tidal deformability constraint (with B = (120 MeV)^4).
- The chemical potentials corresponding to maximum mass for two-flavor stars with α = 0.91, 0.93, 0.95 are 734 MeV, 744 MeV, and 754 MeV respectively; for three-flavor stars with α = 0.75, 0.78, 0.80 they are 765 MeV, 780 MeV, and 793 MeV respectively.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous self-consistent mean-field approach (Wang et al. 2019, Zhao et al. 2019) from two-flavor to three-flavor quark matter, and replaces the three-momentum cutoff with the Pauli-Villars scheme to avoid restrictions on maximal quark star mass. The result that two-flavor matter is more stable than three-flavor matter contradicts Witten's (1984) original conjecture based on the MIT bag model, but agrees with a recent study by Holdom et al. (2018). The paper enables future constraints on the parameter α and the bag constant B using astronomical observations of neutron star masses, radii, and tidal deformabilities from LIGO/Virgo.
