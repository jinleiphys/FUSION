# Incoherent pion photoproduction on the deuteron with polarization observables II: Influence of final state rescattering

> Incoherent pion photoproduction on the deuteron is studied for photon energies from threshold up to 1 GeV with special emphasis on polarization observables. The elementary $γN\toπN$ amplitude is taken from the MAID model. The influence of final state interactions on total and semi-exclusive cross sections $\vec d(\vecγ,π)NN$ is investigated by including complete rescattering in the final $NN$- and $πN$-subsystems. For charged pion-production the influence of $NN$-rescattering is moderate whereas $πN$-rescattering is almost negligible. Much stronger influences of $NN$-rescattering are seen in neutral pion production, which is due to the elimination of a significant spurious coherent contribution in the impulse approximation. Sizeable effects are also found in some of the beam, target and beam-target asymmetries of the differential cross section.

## Key claim

The influence of final-state rescattering on incoherent pion photoproduction on the deuteron is systematically evaluated, with the key finding that the large apparent effect of NN-rescattering in neutral pion production arises primarily from removing a spurious coherent contribution in the impulse approximation rather than from genuine dynamical rescattering, while for charged pion production the rescattering effects remain moderate throughout the energy range studied.

## Method

The authors compute the T-matrix for the reaction γ + d → π + N + N using the impulse approximation (IA) as a starting point, then add complete rescattering in the final NN and πN subsystems via partial-wave expansions of realistic separable potentials (Paris potential for NN, a separable representation from NoB90 for πN). The elementary γN → πN amplitude is taken from the MAID model, and the formalism includes full treatment of beam, target, and beam-target polarization observables following the correct expressions derived in ArF05. The calculation covers photon energies from threshold up to 1 GeV, with results presented for total cross sections, semi-exclusive differential cross sections, and various polarization asymmetries.

## Key numbers

- For charged pion production, FSI reduces total cross sections by "a few percent" at energies near and above the Δ-resonance maximum
- At Eγ = 500 MeV, the IA cross section for π⁻ production at θπ = 0° is approximately 10 times larger than the prediction of LeS00
- The NN-rescattering calculation uses the Paris potential fitted to NN-scattering data up to nucleon lab kinetic energies of TNN = 330 MeV, but remains valid up to TNN = 500 MeV where inelasticity parameters are still small
- For π⁰ production at Eγ = 250 MeV, the modified IA (removing spurious coherent contribution) shows the largest FSI influence on the beam asymmetry Σ^l
- The vector asymmetry σ₀ T̄₁₀^c for circularly polarized photons, governing the GDH sum rule, shows weak sensitivity to FSI for both π⁻ and π⁰ channels

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (ArF05) on the correct derivation of polarization observables for incoherent pion photoproduction on the deuteron, and addresses discrepancies with earlier calculations by Darwish et al. (2004, 2005) which used incorrect expressions for polarization observables and found different FSI effects. The paper clarifies the role of final-state interactions in view of conflicting results between Laget (1978, 1981), Levchuk et al. (2000), and Darwish et al. (2003), particularly regarding whether FSI increases or decreases charged pion production cross sections in the Δ-resonance region. The results enable reliable calculations of polarization observables for the GDH sum rule and provide a benchmark for future experimental studies at facilities such as MAMI and ELSA.
