# Coherent multiple scattering effect in DIS

> We present a QCD calculation of the coherent multiple scattering effect on single hadron production in semi-inclusive lepton-nucleus deep inelastic scattering (DIS). We show that the destructive interference of coherent multiple scattering leads to suppression in hadron productions. The suppression is similar to the expectations of energy loss calculations. We compare our calculation with experimental data.

# Coherent Multiple Scattering Effect in DIS

## Key claim

The paper claims that destructive interference from coherent multiple scattering of a quark in a nuclear medium, without any induced gluon radiation, can suppress single hadron production in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (DIS). The suppression is equivalent to a shift in the fragmentation function's momentum fraction variable, but the authors note that at large \(z_h\) the simple resummed theory overestimates the suppression compared to HERMES data, requiring a \((1-z_h)\) formation-time correction to bring the calculation into better agreement.

## Method

The authors perform a perturbative QCD calculation of single hadron production in semi-inclusive lepton-nucleus DIS, factorizing the cross section into leptonic and hadronic tensors. They use the leading-pole approximation to extract the \(A^{1/3}\)-enhanced twist-4 contributions from double and higher-order coherent scatterings of the struck quark with partons in the nuclear medium at the same impact parameter in the Breit frame. The sum over \(n\) scatterings is resummed to all orders, yielding a closed-form result where the net effect is a shift \(\Delta z = z_h \xi^2 (A^{1/3}-1)/Q^2\) in the fragmentation function's momentum fraction.

## Key numbers

- The shift in the fragmentation function variable is \(\Delta z = z_h \xi^2 (A^{1/3}-1)/Q^2\), where \(\xi^2\) is a characteristic scale of quark interaction with the nuclear medium.
- The coherent multiple scattering effects are relevant for \(x_B \ge x_c \sim 0.1\); for \(x_B < x_c\) an additional term proportional to \(x_B \frac{d}{dx_B}\phi_q(x_B,Q^2)\) appears.
- The suppression ratio \(R_M = R^A/R^D\) is compared with HERMES data for pion production on nuclear targets; the resummed theory curves are much steeper than the data at large \(z_h\).
- The formation-time correction multiplies \(\Delta z\) by a factor \((1-z_h)\), reflecting that hadron formation time is proportional to \((1-z_h)\).

## Context

This work extends the QCD treatment of multiple scattering in nuclei developed by Qiu and Vitev (2003) and by the authors themselves (Guo and Li, 2005), applying it to single hadron production in DIS. The calculated suppression from coherent multiple scattering is complementary to the medium-induced radiative energy loss mechanism (Wang and others, 1990s–2000s) that is commonly invoked to explain jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions. The result enables future studies of the interplay between collisional and radiative energy loss in \(p+A\) and \(A+A\) collisions, though the authors note that beyond leading order the separation of these effects is scheme-dependent and requires further work.
