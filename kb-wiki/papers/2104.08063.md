# Microscopic description of quadrupole-octupole coupling in neutron-rich actinides and superheavy nuclei with the Gogny-D1M energy density functional

> The interplay between quadrupole and octupole degrees of freedom is discussed in a series of neutron-rich actinides and superheavy nuclei with $92 \le$ Z $\le 110$ and $186 \le$ N $\le 202$. In addition to the static Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach, dynamical beyond-mean-field correlations are taken into account via both parity restoration and symmetry-conserving Generator Coordinate Method calculations based on the Gogny-D1M energy density functional. Physical properties such as correlation energies, negative-parity excitation energies as well as reduced transition probabilities $B(E1)$ and $B(E3)$ are discussed in detail. It is shown that, for the studied nuclei, the quadrupole-octupole coupling is weak and to a large extent the properties of negative parity states can be reasonably well described in terms of the octupole degree of freedom alone.

## Key claim

For neutron-rich actinides and superheavy nuclei with Z between 92 and 110 and N between 186 and 202, the coupling between quadrupole and octupole degrees of freedom is weak, and the properties of negative-parity states can be reasonably well described using only the octupole degree of freedom, despite the softness of the mean-field potential energy surfaces along the octupole direction.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny-D1M energy density functional to build two-dimensional mean-field potential energy surfaces (MFPESs) as functions of quadrupole (Q20) and octupole (Q30) moments. Beyond-mean-field correlations were incorporated through parity projection of the intrinsic HFB states and through symmetry-conserving two-dimensional Generator Coordinate Method (2D-GCM) calculations that mix both quadrupole and octupole configurations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum HFB octupole correlation energy ΔE_CORR,HFB = 1.8 MeV, found in ²⁹⁰Cf
- For ²⁹⁴No, the energy difference between the local Q30 = 2.0 b³/² and global Q30 = 7.0 b³/² minima in the positive-parity projected potential energy surface is 652 keV
- For ²⁹⁶No, the energy difference between the global reflection-symmetric minimum (Q20 = 22 b, Q30 = 0) and the local reflection-asymmetric minimum (Q20 = 10 b, Q30 = 7 b³/²) is 210 keV
- For A = 294, a quadrupole deformation Q20 = 10 b is equivalent to β2 = 0.141, and an octupole deformation Q30 = 1 b³/² is equivalent to β3 = 0.021
- Octupole-deformed HFB ground states are found with Q30,GS values in the range 1 b³/² ≤ Q30,GS ≤ 7 b³/² for specific isotopes across the studied chains
- For ²⁹⁴No, the third minimum at (Q20 = 22 b, Q30 = 2 b³/²) is only 14 keV above the absolute minimum in the positive-parity projected potential energy surface

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quadrupole-octupole coupling in Sm and Gd isotopes (Rodriguez-Guzman and Robledo, 2012) and in actinide nuclei around N ≈ 134 (Rodriguez-Guzman and Robledo, 2020) to the neutron-rich actinide and low-Z superheavy region. The results confirm the existence of an island of octupole-deformed ground states predicted by relativistic EDF calculations (Agbemava et al., 2016, 2017) and Skyrme-EDF calculations (Erler et al., 2012), though with differences in the precise location and extension in the (Z,N)-plane. The findings enable improved modeling of fission recycling in neutron star mergers by providing microscopic predictions for the octupole properties of nuclei that determine the fate of heavy-element nucleosynthesis.
