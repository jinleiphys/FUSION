# Study of the $D^*ρ$ system using QCD sum rules

> In this talk I present a study of the $D^* ρ$ system made by using the method of QCD sum rules. Considering isospin and spin projectors, we investigate the different configurations and obtain three $D^*$ mesons with isospin $I=1/2$, spin $S=0$, $1$, $2$ and with masses $2500\pm 67$ MeV, $2523\pm60$ MeV, and $2439\pm119$ MeV, respectively. The last state can be related to $D^*_2(2460)$ (spin 2) listed by the Particle Data Group, while one of the first two might be associated with $D^*(2640)$, whose spin-parity is unknown. In the case of $I=3/2$ we also find evidences of three states with spin 0, 1 and 2, respectively, with masses $2467\pm82$ MeV, $2420\pm128$ MeV, and $2550\pm56$ MeV.

## Key claim

The authors use QCD sum rules to predict the masses of possible D* rho molecular states, finding three states with isospin I=1/2 and spins 0, 1, and 2, and three states with isospin I=3/2 and spins 0, 1, and 2, though the results carry uncertainties of 56-128 MeV and the interpretation relies on the assumption of relative angular momentum L=0 between the two vector mesons.

## Method

The authors evaluate two-point correlation functions for currents representing the D* rho system, projecting onto definite isospin (I=1/2 and 3/2) and spin (S=0, 1, 2) using covariant projectors. The correlation function is computed in the operator product expansion (OPE) including condensates up to dimension 7 (perturbative, quark condensate, gluon condensate, mixed condensate, four-quark condensate, three-gluon condensate, and mixed quark-gluon condensate). The OPE result is matched to a phenomenological parametrization assuming a single narrow resonance plus a continuum, and the mass is extracted using sum rule stability criteria.

## Key numbers

- D* meson with I=1/2, S=0: mass 2500 +/- 67 MeV
- D* meson with I=1/2, S=1: mass 2523 +/- 60 MeV
- D* meson with I=1/2, S=2: mass 2439 +/- 119 MeV (related to D*_2(2460) from the Particle Data Group)
- D* meson with I=3/2, S=0: mass 2467 +/- 82 MeV
- D* meson with I=3/2, S=1: mass 2420 +/- 128 MeV
- D* meson with I=3/2, S=2: mass 2550 +/- 56 MeV
- Charm quark mass used: m_c = 1.23 +/- 0.05 GeV
- Quark condensate value: <q q> = -(0.23 +/- 0.03)^3 GeV^3
- Gluon condensate value: <g^2 G^2> = (0.88 +/- 0.25) GeV^4

## Context

This work extends a previous study of the D* rho system using effective field theories by Raquel (2013), which predicted J^P=2^+ for D*_2(2460) and J^P=1^+ for D*(2640), and also predicted a new J^P=0^+ state near 2600 MeV. The QCD sum rule results confirm the spin-2 assignment for D*_2(2460) and provide mass predictions for the other states, though with significant uncertainties. The I=3/2 states would be exotic (not possible as simple q qbar mesons) and their existence would indicate molecular or tetraquark structure, motivating further experimental searches at facilities like Belle, BaBar, and CLEO.
