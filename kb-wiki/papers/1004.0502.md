# Systematic study of the energy of vanishing flow: Role of equations of state and cross sections

> We present a systematic study of the energy of vanishing flow by considering symmetric colliding nuclei (between $^{12}$C and $^{238}$U) at normalized impact parameters using variety of equations of state (with and without momentum dependent interactions) as well as different nucleon-nucleon cross sections. A perfect power law mass dependence is obtained in all the cases which passes through calculated points nicely. Further, the choice of impact parameter affects the energy of vanishing flow drastically, demanding a very accurate measurement of the impact parameter. However, the energy of vanishing flow is less sensitive towards the equation of state as well as its momentum dependence.

## Key claim

The energy of vanishing flow (EVF) follows a perfect power-law mass dependence for symmetric colliding nuclei when the impact parameter is fixed and the asymmetry of reacting partners is removed, but the EVF is less sensitive to the equation of state and its momentum dependence than to the impact parameter.

## Method

The authors used the quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) model to simulate symmetric reactions of eight systems from \(^{12}\)C+\(^{12}\)C to \(^{238}\)U+\(^{238}\)U at reduced impact parameters \(b/b_{max}=0.4\) and 0.2. They employed hard, soft, and hard with momentum-dependent interactions (HMD) equations of state, combined with constant nucleon-nucleon cross sections of 40 and 55 mb, and extracted the EVF by straight-line interpolation of transverse flow values at incident energies between 30 and 250 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{12}\)C+\(^{12}\)C with Hard\(^{55}\) at \(b/b_{max}=0.4\), the EVF is 143 MeV/nucleon; at \(b/b_{max}=0.2\), it reduces to 76 MeV/nucleon.
- For \(^{12}\)C+\(^{12}\)C with Soft\(^{55}\) at \(b/b_{max}=0.4\), the EVF is 158 MeV/nucleon.
- The average difference between Hard and HMD equations of state (for \(\sigma = 40\) and 55 mb) is 15%; between hard and soft equations of state, it is 26%; between hard equation of state at \(b/b_{max}=0.4\) and 0.2, it is 42%.
- Measured EVF for \(^{40}\)Ar+\(^{45}\)Sc varies from \(84\pm 7\) MeV/nucleon to \(104\pm 5\) MeV/nucleon between \(b/b_{max}=0.28\) and 0.48 (experimental data from Pak (1997)).
- The power-law exponent \(\tau\) is close to \(-1/3\) in all cases except for Hard\(^{40}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier mass-dependence studies of EVF by Westmeier (1993), Magestro (2000), and Sood (2004), which reported deviations of up to 18-23 MeV/nucleon from a power law due to varying impact parameters and asymmetric colliding nuclei. By restricting the analysis to symmetric systems at fixed reduced impact parameters, the authors obtain a clean power law and show that impact parameter uncertainty can mask the sensitivity to the equation of state. The results enable more precise experimental extraction of the nuclear equation of state by demanding accurate impact parameter measurement, and they clarify that momentum-dependent interactions have a stronger effect for lighter nuclei and peripheral reactions.
