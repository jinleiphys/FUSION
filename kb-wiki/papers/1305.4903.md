# Two-Nucleon Systems in a Finite Volume: (I) Quantization Conditions

> The quantization condition for interacting energy eigenvalues of the two-nucleon system in a finite cubic volume is derived in connection to the nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes. This condition is derived using an auxiliary (dimer) field formalism that is generalized to arbitrary partial waves in the context of non-relativistic effective field theory. The quantization condition presented gives access to the scattering parameters of the two-nucleon systems with arbitrary parity, spin, isospin, angular momentum and center of mass motion, from a lattice QCD calculation of the energy eigenvalues. In particular, as it includes all non-central interactions, such as the two-nucleon tensor force, it makes explicit the dependence of the mixing parameters of nucleon-nucleon systems calculated from lattice QCD when there is a physical mixing among different partial-waves, e. g. S-D mixing in the deuteron channel. We provide explicit relations among scattering parameters and their corresponding point group symmetry class eigenenergies with orbital angular momentum l smaller than or equal to 3, and for center of mass boost vectors of the form 2π(2n_1, 2n_2, 2n_3)/L, 2π(2n_1, 2n_2, 2n_3+1)/L and 2π(2n_1+1, 2n_2+1, 2n_3)/L. L denotes the special extent of the cubic volume and n_1,n_2,n_3 are integers. Our results are valid below inelastic thresholds up to exponential volume corrections that are governed by the pion mass.

## Key claim

The paper derives quantization conditions for two-nucleon systems in a finite cubic volume that connect lattice QCD energy eigenvalues to nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes, including all non-central interactions such as the tensor force. The results are valid below inelastic thresholds and up to exponential volume corrections governed by the pion mass, and they explicitly account for physical mixing among different partial waves (e.g., S-D mixing in the deuteron channel).

## Method

The authors use an auxiliary (dimer) field formalism generalized to arbitrary partial waves within non-relativistic effective field theory, extending the standard Luscher method to nucleon-nucleon systems with spin, isospin, and center-of-mass motion. They derive the quantization condition by summing bubble diagrams for the dimer propagator in a finite cubic volume, then reduce the infinite-dimensional determinant condition to finite-size blocks by exploiting the irreducible representations of the cubic (O), tetragonal (D4), and orthorhombic (D2) point groups for systems with center-of-mass momentum P=0, P=2π/L(0,0,1), and P=2π/L(1,1,0), respectively. Explicit relations are provided for orbital angular momentum l ≤ 3 and total angular momentum J ≤ 4, ignoring scattering in partial waves with l ≥ 4.

## Key numbers

- The quantization conditions are derived for boost vectors of the form 2π(2n₁,2n₂,2n₃)/L, 2π(2n₁,2n₂,2n₃+1)/L, and 2π(2n₁+1,2n₂+1,2n₃)/L, where L is the spatial extent of the cubic volume and n₁,n₂,n₃ are integers.
- For systems with total angular momentum J ≤ 4, ignoring scattering in partial waves with l ≥ 4, the largest block in the reduced quantization condition has dimension 9×9.
- Explicit relations are provided for orbital angular momentum l ≤ 3 and for center-of-mass boost vectors corresponding to three distinct symmetry classes.
- The quantization conditions are tabulated for all spin and isospin channels with total angular momentum J ≤ 4, covering both positive and negative parity sectors.

## Context

This work extends the original Luscher method (Luscher, 1991) for scalar particles to nucleon-nucleon systems with spin, isospin, and tensor-force-induced partial-wave mixing, building on prior generalizations for meson-baryon systems (Bernard, 2008) and the first nucleon-nucleon quantization conditions at zero momentum (Ishizuka, 2009). The formalism enables first-principle lattice QCD extraction of scattering parameters including the S-D mixing parameter in the deuteron channel, and it provides the necessary framework for extending finite-volume three-body calculations (Briceno, 2012; Kreuzer, 2008-2012) to include higher partial-wave mixing in two-particle subsystems.
