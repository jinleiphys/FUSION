# Two-body currents in inclusive electron scattering

> The longitudinal and transverse structure functions are calculated for inclusive electron scattering from ${}^{12}$C and ${}^{40}$Ca in the quasielastic and dip region. The microscopic model presented here incorporates two-body currents derived from one-pion exchange and intermediate $Δ$ excitation. The reaction mechanism involves both one-nucleon and two-nucleon knockout processes. It is demonstrated that, even for quasielastic kinematics, two-body currents give a substantial contribution to the transverse structure function. Furthermore, the observed excess of transverse strength in the dip region between the quasielastic and delta peak can be partially ascribed to direct two-nucleon knockout following photoabsorption on a two-body current.

## Key claim

Even for quasielastic kinematics, two-body currents derived from one-pion exchange and intermediate Δ excitation give a substantial contribution to the transverse structure function, and the observed excess of transverse strength in the dip region can be partially ascribed to direct two-nucleon knockout following photoabsorption on a two-body current. The authors note that the calculations overestimate the measured longitudinal and transverse strength to the same degree when compared to the Saclay data, and that additional many-body effects like short-range correlations and relativistic effects would reduce both response functions similarly, leaving the transverse-to-longitudinal ratio almost unaffected.

## Method

The authors performed a non-relativistic continuum Hartree-Fock plus random phase approximation (HF+RPA) calculation for inclusive electron scattering from ¹²C and ⁴⁰Ca, incorporating one-body and two-body nuclear currents. The two-body currents were derived from a non-relativistic reduction of Feynman diagrams with one exchanged pion (seagull and pion-in-flight terms) and intermediate Δ(1232) excitation, with the nuclear charge operator unaffected by two-body contributions. The reaction mechanism included both one-nucleon knockout (e,e'N) and two-nucleon knockout (e,e'2N) processes, with the one-body response folded using a phenomenological spreading function to account for damping effects from higher-order excitations.

## Key numbers

- For ¹²C(e,e') at q=400 MeV/c and q=550 MeV/c, and for ⁴⁰Ca(e,e') at q=370 MeV/c, the longitudinal and transverse structure functions were calculated and compared with experimental data from Saclay and MIT-Bates.
- The two-body currents induce an extra 20-30% of strength into the transverse channel in the quasielastic region.
- The two-body current contribution to one-nucleon knockout is relatively more important for the smallest momentum transfers considered (q=400 MeV/c for ¹²C, q=370 MeV/c for ⁴⁰Ca).
- Two-nucleon knockout comes into play beyond ω=100 MeV in the dip region.
- The coupling constants adopted were f²_πNN/4π = 0.079, f²_πNΔ/4π = 0.37, and f²_γNΔ = 0.014.
- The hadronic formfactor cutoff parameter Λ_π was set to 1200 MeV, with standard values lying in the range 800-1250 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of inclusive electron scattering that used the impulse approximation within a Fermi Gas Model (Moniz 1969) or simple shell-model approaches, and addresses the long-standing problem of simultaneously describing the total cross section and separated structure functions. The authors compare their results with the microscopic calculations of Carlson and Schiavilla (1992) for ⁴He(e,e'), who found virtual pion exchange essential, and with Amaro et al. (1994) for ¹²C and ⁴⁰Ca, who found negligible two-body current contributions to one-nucleon knockout, a discrepancy the authors attribute to the lack of short-range correlations in both models, though they note explicit inclusion of SRC is expected to reduce the MEC strength by only about 10%. The work enables future studies to incorporate short-range correlations and relativistic effects to achieve a complete description of inclusive electron scattering from complex nuclei.
