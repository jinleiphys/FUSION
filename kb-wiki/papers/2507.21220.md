# Gravitational form factors of the nucleon in the Skyrme model based on scale-invariant chiral perturbation theory

> We investigate the role of the QCD scale anomaly in the gravitational form factors of the nucleon -- particularly the $D(t)$ form factor -- as well as the associated stress distribution and internal forces, using a Skyrme model based on the scale-invariant chiral perturbation theory. A distinctive feature of this model is the inclusion of both the pion and the scalar meson, which respectively capture the effects of the current quark mass and gluonic quantum contributions to the scale anomaly. By varying the mass of the scalar meson, we evaluate the sensitivity of the gluonic scale anomaly to the nucleon properties. We find that the gluonic scale anomaly plays a crucial role in satisfying the stability conditions of the nucleon and provides an internal confining force. Moreover, we also evaluate the momentum-transfer dependence of $D(t)$, which closely reproduces the lattice QCD results. With an appropriate choice of the anomalous dimension associated with the quark mass, its forward-limit value (i.e., the D-term) also matches the lattice data well.

## Key claim

The gluonic scale anomaly, modeled via a scalar meson field in scale-invariant chiral perturbation theory, is essential for satisfying the nucleon's mechanical stability conditions and provides the dominant confining force inside the nucleon; the model's momentum-transfer dependence of the D(t) form factor closely reproduces lattice QCD results, and with an appropriate choice of the anomalous dimension associated with the quark mass, the forward-limit D-term also matches lattice data.

## Method

The authors employ a Skyrme model based on scale-invariant chiral perturbation theory (sChPT), incorporating both a pion field and a scalar meson field to capture the current quark mass and gluonic quantum contributions to the QCD scale anomaly, respectively. The scalar meson couples to the dilatation current via the partially conserved dilatation current (PCDC) relation, and the Skyrme term stabilizes the soliton solution identified as the nucleon (skyrmion). By varying the scalar meson mass, decay constant, and anomalous dimension parameter, the authors control the strength of the scale anomaly and compute the gravitational form factors, stress distributions, and internal forces from the static energy-momentum tensor in the Breit frame.

## Key numbers

- The scale anomaly contributes exactly 25% of the nucleon mass: \(\hat M_N = \frac{1}{4}M_N\) (Eq. MN/4).
- The dynamical (traceless) part contributes 75% of the nucleon mass: \(\bar M_N = \frac{3}{4}M_N\) (Eq. 3MN/4).
- The spatial integral of the anomalous pressure satisfies \(4\pi\int_0^\infty dr\,r^2 \hat{p}(r) = -\frac{1}{4}M_N\) (Eq. hatp).
- The spatial integral of the dynamical pressure satisfies \(4\pi\int_0^\infty dr\,r^2 \bar{p}(r) = \frac{1}{4}M_N\) (Eq. barp).
- The D-term is negative (\(D < 0\)) as a necessary condition for mechanical stability (Eq. D-term condition).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier Skyrme model analysis (Fujii 2025) by systematically varying the parameters controlling the scale anomaly strength, performing a detailed mechanical stability analysis, and computing the full momentum-transfer dependence of D(t) for comparison with lattice QCD results. The study builds on Ji's (1994) mass decomposition framework showing the scale anomaly contributes to the nucleon mass, and on the experimental extraction of pressure profiles (Burkert 2018) and shear force distributions (Burkert 2021, Duran 2022). The results enable future investigations of how internal stress distributions change under external conditions such as finite temperature or density, where the scale anomaly strength is expected to vary.
