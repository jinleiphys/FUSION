# Benchmark calculations of electromagnetic sum rules with a symmetry-adapted basis and hyperspherical harmonics

> We demonstrate the ability to calculate electromagnetic sum rules with the \textit{ab initio} symmetry-adapted no-core shell model. By implementing the Lanczos algorithm, we compute non-energy weighted, energy weighted, and inverse energy weighted sum rules for electric monopole, dipole, and quadrupole transitions in $^4$He using realistic interactions. We benchmark the results with the hyperspherical harmonics method and show agreement within $2σ$, where the uncertainties are estimated from the use of the many-body technique. We investigate the dependence of the results on three different interactions, including chiral potentials, and we report on the $^4$He electric dipole polarizability calculated in the SA-NCSM that reproduces the experimental data and earlier theoretical outcomes. We also detail a novel use of the Lawson procedure to remove the spurious center-of-mass contribution to the sum rules that arises from using laboratory-frame coordinates. We further show that this same technique can be applied in the Lorentz integral transform method, with a view toward studies of electromagnetic reactions for light through medium-mass nuclei.

## Key claim

The symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) can reliably compute electromagnetic sum rules for 4He, with results agreeing within 2σ of hyperspherical harmonics (HH) benchmarks, where the uncertainty σ is estimated from the many-body technique rather than from the interaction.

## Method

The authors implemented the Lanczos sum rule (LSR) method using SA-NCSM wave functions to compute non-energy weighted (m0), energy weighted (m1), and inverse energy weighted (m-1) sum rules for electric monopole, dipole, and quadrupole transitions in 4He. They benchmarked these results against the hyperspherical harmonics (HH) method using the same realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions (JISP16, N3LO-EM, and NNLOopt), and employed a novel application of the Lawson procedure to remove spurious center-of-mass contributions arising from laboratory-frame coordinates.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of 4He with JISP16: SA-NCSM extrapolated value agrees with HH result, with uncertainties smaller than plot markers
- Monopole m0 for JISP16: HH = 22.68(1) fm4, NCSM = 22.74(1) fm4, SA-NCSM = 22.57(8) fm4
- Dipole m0 for JISP16: HH = 0.8583(1) e2fm2, NCSM = 0.8581(1) e2fm2, SA-NCSM = 0.8566(7) e2fm2
- Dipole m1/m0 for JISP16: HH = 48.179(9) MeV, NCSM = 48.147(6) MeV, SA-NCSM = 48.24(4) MeV
- Dipole m-1/m0 for JISP16: HH = 0.02655(1) MeV-1, NCSM = 0.026574(9) MeV-1, SA-NCSM = 0.02644(8) MeV-1
- Quadrupole m0 for JISP16: HH = 14.731(3) e2fm4, NCSM = 14.78(1) e2fm4, SA-NCSM = 14.62(6) e2fm4
- Dipole m0 for N3LO-EM (NN only): NCSM = 0.95(3) e2fm2, SA-NCSM = 0.94(2) e2fm2
- Dipole m0 for NNLOopt (NN only): NCSM = 0.8394(3) e2fm2, SA-NCSM = 0.837(1) e2fm2
- SA-NCSM model space dimensions reduced by factors of 2-13 compared to full NCSM (e.g., 0+ state: NCSM at Nmax=16 has 135,475 dimensions, SA-NCSM ⟨6⟩16 has 14,902 dimensions)

## Context

This work extends the Lanczos sum rule method (previously applied in coupled-cluster theory by Miorelli (2016) and Hagen (2016)) to the symmetry-adapted no-core shell model, enabling first-principles sum rule calculations for open-shell nuclei up through medium masses. The benchmark against hyperspherical harmonics for 4He validates the approach, and the demonstration that SA-NCSM reproduces full NCSM results with much smaller model spaces (as previously shown for structure observables by Dytrych (2012) and Launey (2016)) opens the path to studying electromagnetic reactions in heavier nuclei where exact HH solutions are unavailable.
