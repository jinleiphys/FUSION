# Folding model study of the elastic $Œ±+ Œ±$ scattering at low energies

> The folding model analysis of the elastic $Œ±+ Œ±$ scattering at the incident energies below the reaction threshold of 34.7 MeV (in the lab system) has been done using the well-tested density dependent versions of the M3Y interaction and realistic choices for the $^4$He density. Because the absorption is negligible at the energies below the reaction threshold, we were able to probe the $Œ±+ Œ±$ optical potential at low energies quite unambiguously and found that the $Œ±+ Œ±$ overlap density used to construct the density dependence of the M3Y interaction is strongly distorted by the Pauli blocking. This result gives possible explanation of a long-standing inconsistency of the double-folding model in its study of the elastic $Œ±+ Œ±$ and $Œ±$-nucleus scattering at low energies using the same realistic density dependent M3Y interaction.

## Key claim

The long-standing inconsistency of the double-folding model in describing elastic alpha+alpha scattering at low energies with density-dependent M3Y interactions can be resolved if the overlap density used to construct the density dependence is dynamically distorted by Pauli blocking, rather than treated as a static sum of the two ground-state densities.

## Method

The authors performed double-folding model calculations of the real optical potential for elastic alpha+alpha scattering at laboratory energies below the reaction threshold of 34.7 MeV, using the BDM3Y1 and CDM3Y6 density-dependent versions of the M3Y interaction. Three approximations for the overlap density entering the density dependence were compared: the frozen density approximation (FDA), the fully antisymmetrized overlap density (AOD), and a new dynamic Pauli distortion (DPD) procedure that reduces the local densities when the sum of the two Fermi momenta exceeds the local relative nucleon momentum. The folded potentials were then used in optical model calculations with the code PTOLEMY to describe elastic scattering data at energies from 3 to 29.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the double-folding model studies of Satchler and Love (1979) and Khoa et al. (1995, 1997, 2000, 2007) by addressing a known inconsistency: density-dependent M3Y interactions successfully describe alpha-nucleus scattering but fail for alpha+alpha scattering, where only density-independent M3Y interactions work (Avrigeanu et al., 2003). The authors' dynamic Pauli distortion procedure provides a physical explanation for this discrepancy and enables consistent application of the same density-dependent interaction to both systems. The approach contrasts with the resonating group method of Buck et al. (1977) and the fish-bone model of Day (2011), which treat Pauli effects more rigorously but with simpler interactions.
