# Nuclear physics from lattice simulations

> We review recent lattice QCD activities with emphasis on the impact on nuclear physics. In particular, the progress toward the determination of nuclear and baryonic forces (potentials) using Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions is presented. We discuss major challenges for multi-baryon systems on the lattice: (i) signal to noise issue and (ii) computational cost issue. We argue that the former issue can be avoided by extracting energy-independent (non-local) potentials from time-dependent NBS wave functions without relying on the ground state saturation, and the latter cost is drastically reduced by developing a novel "unified contraction algorithm." The lattice QCD results for nuclear forces, hyperon forces and three-nucleon forces are presented, and physical insights are discussed. Comparison to results from the traditional Luescher's method is given, and open issues to be resolved are addressed as well.

## Key claim
The HAL QCD method can extract energy-independent (non-local) nuclear potentials from time-dependent Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions without requiring ground state saturation, thereby avoiding the exponential signal-to-noise degradation that plagues traditional lattice approaches for multi-baryon systems.

## Method
The authors compute NBS wave functions on the lattice from four-point correlation functions of nucleon operators, then define a non-local potential through the Schrodinger equation that these wave functions satisfy. In practice, they employ a derivative expansion of the potential to extract central, tensor, and spin-orbit components from the coupled-channel Schrodinger equations. For hyperon systems, they construct two-baryon operators with appropriate Clebsch-Gordan coefficients to project onto irreducible representations of flavor SU(3), and extend the method to coupled-channel systems above inelastic threshold by considering multiple NBS wave functions at different energies.

## Key numbers
- Scattering length in the 1S0 channel at m_pi = 0.70 GeV: a(1S0) = 1.6(1.1) fm, compared to the experimental value of approximately 20 fm
- Pion masses studied for two-nucleon forces: 701, 570, and 411 MeV, with corresponding nucleon masses of 1584, 1412, and 1215 MeV
- Lattice spacing a = 0.091 fm for the N_f = 2+1 clover fermion configurations on a 32^3 x 64 lattice (2.9 fm box)
- For parity-odd forces: m_pi = 1.1 GeV, m_N = 2.2 GeV on a 16^3 x 32 lattice with a^{-1} = 1.269(14) GeV
- In the flavor SU(3) limit at M_ps = 469 MeV, the flavor-singlet channel shows a single bound H-dibaryon state with binding energy of 20-50 MeV, decreasing for lighter quark masses
- Computational cost reduction factors from the unified contraction algorithm: factor of 192 for 3H and 3He, factor of 20736 for 4He, and factor of O(10^11) for 8Be without assuming isospin symmetry

## Context
This work extends the HAL QCD method (Ishii 2006, Aoki 2009) for extracting hadronic potentials from lattice QCD, which provides an alternative to the traditional Luscher finite-volume method (Luscher 1990) that requires ground state saturation and becomes impractical for multi-baryon systems. The time-dependent extension presented here avoids the signal-to-noise problem identified by Lepage (1989), where the signal-to-noise ratio decays as exp[-A(m_N - 3m_pi/2)(t-t_0)] for A-nucleon systems. The results enable first-principles calculations of nuclear forces, hyperon forces, and three-nucleon forces directly from QCD, which can be used as inputs for many-body nuclear calculations and astrophysical applications such as neutron star structure and supernova explosions.
