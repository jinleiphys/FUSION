# Nuclear symmetry energy effects on liquid-gas phase transition in hot asymmetric nuclear matter

> The liquid-gas phase transition in hot asymmetric nuclear matter is investigated within relativistic mean-field model using the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy constrained from the measured neutron skin thickness of finite nuclei. We find symmetry energy has a significant influence on several features of liquid-gas phase transition. The boundary and area of the liquid-gas coexistence region, the maximal isospin asymmetry and the critical values of pressure and isospin asymmetry all of which systematically increase with increasing softness in the density dependence of symmetry energy. The critical temperature below which the liquid-gas mixed phase exists is found higher for a softer symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy, constrained by neutron skin thickness data, significantly influences the liquid-gas phase transition in hot asymmetric nuclear matter, with a softer symmetry energy systematically increasing the phase coexistence region, critical pressure, critical isospin asymmetry, and critical temperature.

## Method

The authors used two relativistic mean-field (RMF) models, NL3 and FSUGold, with the isovector coupling \(\Lambda_v\) varied from 0.0 to 0.03 to generate different density dependences of the symmetry energy, all adjusted to reproduce \(E_{\rm sym}(\overline\rho,T=0) = 25.67\) MeV (NL3) or 26.00 MeV (FSUGold) at an average density corresponding to \(k_F=1.15\) fm\(^{-1}\). They solved the Gibbs conditions for phase equilibrium (equal chemical potentials and pressures in the two phases) to construct the binodal surface and determine critical points, maximal asymmetries, and critical temperatures for the liquid-gas phase transition at finite temperature.

## Key numbers

- The saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter is \(\rho_0 \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The symmetry energy at saturation density and zero temperature is \(E(\rho_0, T=0) = 32 \pm 4\) MeV.
- For the NL3 set at \(T=10\) MeV, the critical pressure and isospin asymmetry are \((P_c, \alpha_c) = (0.210, 0.652)\) for \(\Lambda_v=0.0\), \((0.276, 0.741)\) for \(\Lambda_v=0.02\), and \((0.331, 0.797)\) for \(\Lambda_v=0.03\).
- For the FSUGold set at \(T=10\) MeV, the critical pressure and isospin asymmetry are \((P_c, \alpha_c) = (0.209, 0.638)\) for \(\Lambda_v=0.0\), \((0.266, 0.725)\) for \(\Lambda_v=0.02\), and \((0.303, 0.789)\) for \(\Lambda_v=0.03\).
- The critical temperature for symmetric nuclear matter (\(\alpha=0\)) in the FSUGold model is \(T_c = 14.7\) MeV.
- For the softest symmetry energy (\(\Lambda_v=0.03\)) in FSUGold, pure neutron matter (\(\alpha=1\)) can exhibit liquid-gas phase transition at \(T \leq T_c = 2\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of liquid-gas phase transition in asymmetric nuclear matter (Mueller, Qian, Wang) by using symmetry energy constrained from neutron skin thickness data (Sharma) rather than unconstrained model predictions. It also builds on isospin diffusion and isoscaling analyses (Chen, BALi08, Baran; Shetty) that constrained the symmetry energy at zero temperature. The results enable predictions for future experiments with radioactive ion beams (Brown, Horowitz) to probe symmetry energy effects on phase transitions in neutron-rich matter.
