# Transverse Momentum Dependence of Hanbury-Brown/Twiss Correlation Radii

> The transverse momentum dependence of Hanbury-Brown/Twiss (HBT) interferometry radii for 2-body correlation functions provides experimental access to the collective dynamics in heavy-ion collisions. We present an analytical approximation scheme for these HBT radii which combines the recently derived model-independent expressions with an approximate determination of the saddle point of the emission function. The method is illustrated for a longitudinally boost-invariant hydrodynamical model of a heavy ion collision with freeze-out on a sharp hypersurface. The analytical approximation converges rapidly to the width of the numerically computed correlation function and reproduces correctly its exact transverse momentum dependence. However, higher order corrections within our approximation scheme are essential, and the previously published lowest order results with simple $m_{\perp}$ scaling behaviour are quantitatively and qualitatively unreliable.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that previously published lowest-order analytical approximations for the transverse momentum dependence of HBT radii, which predict simple $m_{\perp}$ scaling behavior, are quantitatively and qualitatively unreliable; higher-order corrections within the authors' approximation scheme are essential for correctly reproducing the exact transverse momentum dependence.

## Method

The authors develop an analytical approximation scheme that combines model-independent expressions for HBT radii with an approximate determination of the saddle point of the emission function, using a longitudinally boost-invariant hydrodynamical model with freeze-out on a sharp hypersurface at constant proper time $\tau_0$. They perform the $\eta$-integration analytically in terms of modified Bessel functions, then carry out the transverse integrations by saddle point integration, expanding the Bessel functions asymptotically for large arguments $a(r) = m_{\perp} \text{ch}\, \eta_t(r)/T$ and keeping terms up to third non-leading order.

## Key numbers

- For a fit of the exact correlation function with the simple analytical expression from Makhlin and Sinyukov (1988) for the longitudinal HBT radius $R_l$, the estimate for the decoupling time is too large by a factor $\simeq 2$.
- The longitudinal HBT radius expression $R_l^2 = \tau_0^2 T/m_{\perp}$ (Makhlin and Sinyukov 1988) is the leading term of an expansion for large $m_{\perp}/T$; keeping only this lowest order term is an insufficient approximation for realistic values of $T$ and $m_{\perp}$.
- Convergence of the asymptotic expansion of the Bessel functions is reached at low orders $p \leq 3$ in all cases where the approximation scheme works.
- For a weak linear transverse flow with $\eta = \eta_f (r/R)$, $\eta_f \ll 1$, the denominator in the geometric parts of $R_o^2$ and $R_s^2$ contains $m_{\perp}/T + 1/2$ rather than $m_{\perp}/T$ as in Chapman, Scotto and Heinz (1995).

## Context

This work extends the model-independent HBT formalism of Chapman, Scotto and Heinz (1995) and Herrmann and Bertsch (1995) by providing a more accurate analytical approximation scheme that goes beyond the simple scaling laws proposed by Makhlin and Sinyukov (1988), Csörgő and Lørstad (1994), and Akkelin and Sinyukov (1995). The paper shows that the finite duration of particle emission contributes to $R_o^2 - R_s^2$ even for sharp freeze-out at constant proper time, a feature missed by earlier approximations, and that for parabolic transverse flow profiles the approximation scheme fails completely, indicating the need for numerical evaluation of HBT radii in quantitative analyses.
