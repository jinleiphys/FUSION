# The radiation pattern of a QCD antenna in a dilute medium

> Radiative interferences in the multi-parton shower is the building block of QCD jet physics in vacuum. The presence of a hot medium made of quarks and gluons is expected to alter this interference pattern. To study these effects, we derive the gluon emission spectrum off a color-correlated quark-antiquark pair (antenna) traversing a colored medium to first order in the medium density. The resulting induced gluon distribution is found to be governed by the hardest scale of the problem. In our setup, this can either be the inverse antenna transverse size, $r_\perp^{-1}$, or the scale related to the transverse color correlation length in the medium, which is given by the Debye mass $m_D$. This emerging scale opens the angular phase space of emissions off the antenna compared to the vacuum case and gives rise to a typical transverse momentum of the medium-induced emitted gluons, $<k_\perp^2>_{\text{med}}\sim \text{max}(r_\perp^{-1},m_D)$. Above the hard scale interference effects suppress the spectrum resulting in the restoration of vacuum coherence.

## Key claim

The medium-induced gluon emission spectrum off a quark-antiquark antenna is governed by the hardest scale among the inverse antenna transverse size and the Debye mass, which opens the angular phase space compared to vacuum and gives a typical transverse momentum of emitted gluons; above this hard scale, interference effects suppress the spectrum and restore vacuum coherence.

## Method

The authors derived the gluon emission spectrum off a color-correlated quark-antiquark pair (antenna) traversing a dilute colored medium to first order in the medium density, using classical Yang-Mills equations in light-cone gauge with a static medium modeled by a Yukawa-type potential. The medium was treated as a Gaussian white noise of color charges with a Debye screening mass, and the calculation included both independent emission terms and novel interference contributions from the antenna structure.

## Key numbers

- The typical transverse momentum of medium-induced emitted gluons is given by \(\langle k_\perp^2 \rangle_{\text{med}} \sim \max(r_\perp^{-1}, m_D)\), where \(r_\perp = \theta_{q\bar q} L\) is the antenna transverse size and \(m_D\) is the Debye mass.
- In the "dipole" regime where \(r_\perp \ll m_D^{-1}\), the hard scale is \(Q_{\text{hard}} = r_\perp^{-1}\) and the decoherence parameter \(\Delta_{\text{med}} \propto r_\perp^2\) reveals color transparency.
- In the "saturation" regime where \(r_\perp \gg m_D^{-1}\), the hard scale is \(Q_{\text{hard}} = m_D\) and the decoherence parameter saturates at its maximal value.
- The medium transport parameter is defined as \(\hat q = \alpha_s C_A n_0 m_D^2\), where \(n_0\) is the one-dimensional medium density.

## Context

This work extends the study of color coherence effects in QCD jet physics, which in vacuum leads to strict angular ordering of emissions (Dokshitzer 1991, Mueller 1981, Ermolaev 1981), to the case of a deconfined medium. It builds on earlier calculations of medium-induced gluon radiation off a single emitter (Baier 1996, Zakharov 1996, Wiedemann 2000) and on the authors' prior work on the quark-antiquark antenna in medium (Mehtar-Tani 2010, Mehtar-Tani 2011). The results enable a consistent treatment of multiple emissions in jet quenching phenomenology, moving beyond heuristic approaches that assume independent emissions (Baier 2001, Salgado 2003) or modify vacuum splitting functions (Guo 2000, Majumder 2004, Armesto 2007).
