# BHAC-QGP: three-dimensional MHD simulations of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, II. Application to Au-Au collisions

> We present BHAC-QGP, a new numerical code to simulate the evolution of matter created in heavy-ion collisions. BHAC-QGP is based on the Black Hole Accretion Code (BHAC), which has been designed to model astrophysical processes through the solution of the equations of general-relativistic magnetohydrodynamics. Like the mother code, BHAC-QGP uses Adaptive Mesh Refinement (AMR), which allows for a dynamic adjustment of the resolution in regions of the computational domain where a particularly high accuracy is needed. We here discuss a number of applications of BHAC-QGP to Au-Au collisions at Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) energies and show that the code is able to reproduce results of other simulations of these scenarios, but with much higher accuracy.

## Key claim

BHAC-QGP reproduces results of other simulations of Au-Au collisions at RHIC energies but with much higher accuracy, enabled by its Adaptive Mesh Refinement (AMR) capability; however, the paper presents only thermal spectra based on particle masses and degeneracies rather than full hadronic afterburner calculations, so no direct comparison to experimental data is yet attempted.

## Method

The code solves the equations of ideal general-relativistic magnetohydrodynamics in time-dependent Milne coordinates, using the optical Glauber model with a two-component parametrization (hard and soft processes) to initialize the three-dimensional energy-density distribution. Initial magnetic fields are computed by integrating over all charged particles using solutions of Maxwell's equations for a medium with constant electric conductivity and chiral magnetic conductivity, then the Cooper-Frye prescription with the CORNELIUS method is applied to compute particle spectra and anisotropic flow coefficients from the freeze-out hypersurface.

## Key numbers

- Au-Au collisions at center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s_{\text{NN}}} = 200~\text{GeV}$ (RHIC energy)
- For impact parameter $b \sim 10~\text{fm}$, the magnetic field is of order $e B \approx 10~m_{\pi}^2 \approx 6 \times 10^{18}~\text{G}$, where $m_{\pi} \approx 0.14~\text{GeV}$
- Chiral symmetry restoration occurs at temperatures $T > 170~\mathrm{MeV}$ (from lattice-QCD calculations)
- Electric conductivity of static QGP: $\sigma_E = \left(5.8 \pm 2.9\right) \dfrac{T}{T_C} ~\text{MeV}$ (from lattice-QCD in quenched approximation)
- Initial magnetic field shown for $b = 10~\text{fm}$ with $\sigma_E = 5.80~\text{MeV}$ and $\sigma_{\chi} = 0~\text{MeV}$ (top row) and $\sigma_E = 5.80~\text{MeV}$, $\sigma_{\chi} = 1.50~\text{MeV}$ (bottom row)
- Beam rapidity defined via $Y_B := \dfrac{1}{2} \, \text{ln}\!\left(\dfrac{1 + v}{1 - v}\right)$
- Nuclear radius parameterization: $R \approx (1.12 \, A^{1/3} - 0.86 \, A^{-1/3})~\text{fm}$
- Hard processes defined as $p_T > 1.0~\text{GeV}$

## Context

This work extends the application of the Black Hole Accretion Code (BHAC), originally designed for astrophysical general-relativistic MHD, to heavy-ion collision physics as BHAC-QGP. It builds on prior MHD simulations of heavy-ion collisions such as those by Del Zanna (2013), Karpenko (2013), and Schenke (2010), and on calculations of electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions by Tuchin (2013) and Li (2016). The code enables future studies of chiral effects (CME, CSE, CMW) and the influence of finite electric conductivity on magnetic field evolution in the QGP, with planned extensions to include hadronic afterburners for direct comparison to experimental data.
