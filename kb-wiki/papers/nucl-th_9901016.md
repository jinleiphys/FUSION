# Experimental and Theoretical Search for a Phase Transition in Nuclear Fragmentation

> Phase transitions of small isolated systems are signaled by the shape of the caloric equation of state e^*(T), the relationship between the excitation energy per nucleon e^* and temperature. In this work we compare the experimentally deduced e^*(T) to the theoretical predictions. The experimentally accessible temperature was extracted from evaporation spectra from incomplete fusion reactions leading to residue nuclei. The experimental e^*(T) dependence exhibits the characteristic S-shape at e^* = 2-3 MeV/A. Such behavior is expected for a finite system at a phase transition. The observed dependence agrees with predictions of the MMMC-model, which simulates the total accessible phase-space of fragmentation.

## Key claim

The experimentally deduced caloric equation of state e*(T_app) from 701 MeV 28Si + 100Mo incomplete fusion reactions exhibits a characteristic S-shape at excitation energies e* = 2-3 MeV/A, which is expected for a finite isolated system undergoing a first-order phase transition; however, the experimental data show no backbending, likely because the source mass and charge change slightly with excitation energy, smearing out the full S-shape with backbending predicted by the MMMC model.

## Method

The experiment used 701 MeV 28Si projectiles on 100Mo targets, detecting heavy evaporation residues at forward angles and charged particles (protons, deuterons, tritons, alpha particles) plus neutrons in concentric 4π detectors. Excitation energy of the source was deduced from linear momentum reconstruction, and raw particle spectra were fitted with a three-moving-source prescription using a surface-evaporating Maxwellian to extract the apparent temperature T_app. The experimental e*(T_app) curves were compared to predictions from the Berlin microcanonical statistical multifragmentation model (MMMC), which simulates the total accessible phase space for decaying nuclei using Monte Carlo Metropolis importance sampling, with the freeze-out radius set to the standard value of 2.2A^(1/3) fm and a software filter selecting events with one big residue (A_res >= 90).

## Key numbers

* The S-shape anomaly in e*(T_app) appears at excitation energies e* = 2-3 MeV/A for all four particle types (protons, deuterons, tritons, alpha particles).
* The source mass grows from 105 to 122 nucleons and charge from 47 to 54 from lowest to highest excitation energy.
* The MMMC model overestimates proton multiplicity by approximately a factor of 1.3 and underestimates alpha multiplicity by a factor of 2.
* The IMF multiplicities in the selected decay channels are less than 0.1, meaning more than 90% of selected events (both experiment and model) have no IMF.
* The angular momentum of the source ranges from L = 18.2 to 48.8 hbar, but the anomaly in the caloric equation of state exists also at L = 0.

## Context

This work extends earlier experimental observations by Pochodzalla (1995) and Hauger (1996) showing structure in the e*(T) curve, but the authors argue (along with Natowitz (1995) and Ma (1997), and in opposition to Lee (1997)) that those earlier curves may reflect changing source mass rather than a phase transition. The results support the MMMC model predictions of Gross (1995) and show agreement with earlier data from Wada (1989) for similar compound nucleus masses (109 to 128). The work enables future experiments covering a larger range of excitation energies with strictly constant source mass and charge to confirm the phase transition interpretation, and raises the question of whether dynamics beyond the single freeze-out configuration of MMMC are needed to explain differences between particle types.
