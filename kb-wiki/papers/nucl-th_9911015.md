# Comparison of Strangeness Production between A+A and p+p Reactions from 2 to 160 AGeV

> The measured K$^+/π^+$ ratios from heavy-ion reactions are compared with the K$^+/π^+$ ratios from p+p reactions over the energy range 2-160 AGeV. The K/$π$ enhancement in heavy-ion reactions is largest at the lower energies, consistent with strangeness production in secondary scattering becoming relatively more important than initial collisions near the kaon production threshold. The enhancement decreases steadily from 4 to 160 AGeV, suggesting that the same enhancement mechanism of hadronic rescattering and decay of strings may be applicable over this full energy range. Based on existing data, the mid-rapidity K$^+/π^+$ ratio is predicted to be $0.27\pm0.05$ for the forthcoming Pb+Pb reactions at 40 AGeV/c.

# Comparison of Strangeness Production between A+A and p+p Reactions from 2 to 160 AGeV

## Key claim
The K/π enhancement in heavy-ion reactions decreases steadily from 4 to 160 AGeV, and the data are consistent with the same enhancement mechanism of hadronic rescattering and decay of strings being applicable over this full energy range, but the authors note this argument is far from establishing that the same mechanism *is* at work, as particle production can smoothly increase while the reaction mechanism evolves between two scenarios.

## Method
The authors compiled inclusive K⁺ and π⁺ yields from p+p reactions over the energy range √s = 3–20 GeV using published data, fitting them with piecewise parameterizations (low-energy form from Sib and high-energy form from Rossi et al.). They then compared these parameterized p+p K⁺/π⁺ ratios with measured mid-rapidity K⁺/π⁺ ratios from central Au+Au reactions at 2–10.7 AGeV (E895 collaboration) and central Pb+Pb reactions at 158 AGeV (NA49 collaboration), computing the enhancement as the double ratio (K⁺/π⁺)_AA / (K⁺/π⁺)_pp.

## Key numbers
- K⁺/π⁺ ratio in Au+Au at 2 AGeV: 0.0271 ± 0.0015 ± 0.0014
- K⁺/π⁺ ratio in Au+Au at 10.7 AGeV: 0.202 ± 0.005 ± 0.010
- K⁺/π⁺ ratio in Pb+Pb at 157 AGeV/c: 0.19 ± 0.01
- K⁺/π⁺ ratio from p+p reactions tends towards 0.08 at higher energies
- Predicted mid-rapidity K⁺/π⁺ ratio for Pb+Pb at 40 AGeV/c: 0.27 ± 0.05
- Interpolated K⁺/π⁺ enhancement at 40 AGeV/c: 3.3 ± 0.5
- Fit parameters for enhancement decrease from 4–160 AGeV: a = 8.2, b = 0.49 in equation (K⁺/π⁺)_AA/(K⁺/π⁺)_pp = a/(√s − √s₀)^b
- Strangeness suppression factor γ_s for p+p reactions: 0.2 across a broad range of energies
- Strangeness suppression factor γ_s for heavy-ion reactions at 160 AGeV: 0.6
- Strangeness suppression factor γ_s for heavy-ion reactions at 10 AGeV: 0.9–1.0 (consistent with no suppression)

## Context
This work extends the comparison of strangeness production between heavy-ion and proton-proton reactions originally motivated by the search for the quark-gluon plasma (QGP), following the observation by experiments with Si beams at 14.6 AGeV/c that the K⁺/π⁺ ratio in heavy-ions was four to five times larger than in p+p reactions. The authors build on transport calculations by Sorge (1990), Pang (1992), and Li (1995) that showed hadronic rescattering could qualitatively reproduce measured strangeness yields, and on statistical model analyses by Becattini showing that p+p reactions require γ_s = 0.2 while heavy-ion reactions at 160 AGeV require γ_s = 0.6 and at 10 AGeV require γ_s = 0.9–1.0. The work enables a data-driven prediction for the upcoming Pb+Pb measurement at 40 AGeV/c at the SPS, where a measured result below the predicted 0.27 ± 0.05 would imply a minimum in strangeness enhancement and potentially indicate an additional mechanism (such as QGP formation) turning on at higher energies.
