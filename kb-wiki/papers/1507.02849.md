# Relativistic second-order dissipative hydrodynamics at finite chemical potential

> Starting from the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation and employing a Chapman-Enskog like expansion for the distribution function close to equilibrium, we derive second-order evolution equations for the shear stress tensor and the dissipative charge current for a system of massless quarks and gluons. The transport coefficients are obtained exactly using quantum statistics for the phase space distribution functions at non-zero chemical potential. We show that, within the relaxation time approximation, the second-order evolution equations for the shear stress tensor and the dissipative charge current can be decoupled. We find that, for large values of the ratio of chemical potential to temperature, the charge conductivity is small compared to the coefficient of shear viscosity. Moreover, we show that in the relaxation-time approximation, the limiting behaviour of the ratio of heat conductivity to shear viscosity is qualitatively similar to that obtained for a strongly coupled conformal plasma.

# Relativistic second-order dissipative hydrodynamics at finite chemical potential

## Key claim
The authors derive second-order evolution equations for shear stress tensor and dissipative charge current for massless quarks and gluons using a Chapman-Enskog-like expansion, showing that within the relaxation-time approximation these equations can be decoupled. The claim is conditional on the relaxation-time approximation with a common relaxation time for all particle species, and the results are obtained exactly using quantum statistics for the phase-space distribution functions at non-zero chemical potential.

## Method
The authors start from the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation and employ an iterative Chapman-Enskog expansion for the non-equilibrium distribution function close to local thermodynamic equilibrium. They compute the first-order constitutive relations for shear stress tensor and dissipative charge current, then take comoving derivatives of these expressions to obtain second-order evolution equations, performing momentum integrations using quantum statistics (Fermi-Dirac for quarks and anti-quarks, Bose-Einstein for gluons) at finite quark chemical potential.

## Key numbers
- Shear viscosity first-order coefficient: β_π = (ε + P)/5, where ε is energy density and P is pressure
- Charge diffusion first-order coefficient: β_n = J₁₀⁺/3 − n²T/(ε+P), where J₁₀⁺ = (g_q/6)T³ + (g_q/(2π²))Tμ²
- Equilibrium energy density: ε₀ = [(4g_g+7g_q)π²/120]T⁴ + (g_q/4)T²μ² + (g_q/(8π²))μ⁴
- Equilibrium net quark number density: n₀ = (g_q/6)T²μ + (g_q/(6π²))μ³
- Equilibrium entropy density: s₀ = [(4g_g+7g_q)π²/90]T³ + (g_q/6)Tμ²
- Quark degeneracy factor: g_q = 6N_f (with N_f the number of flavors)
- Gluon degeneracy factor: g_g = 16
- In the limit of large μ/T, the ratio κ_q/η follows κ_q/η = C π²T/μ² with C = 5/3, independent of number of flavors
- In the limit of small μ/T, C = 37/27 for two-flavor and C = 95/81 for three-flavor QGP

## Context
This work extends the Chapman-Enskog approach previously applied to systems without conserved charges (Jaiswal 2013, Jaiswal 2014, Chattopadhyay 2014, Florkowski 2015) to include finite chemical potential with quantum statistics. It contrasts with the moment method of Grad and the Israel-Stewart formulation, which the authors note contains inconsistencies. The derived decoupled evolution equations enable implementation in viscous hydrodynamic codes for heavy-ion collisions at lower energies (RHIC low-energy scan, FAIR) where baryon chemical potential is significant, and the results show qualitative similarity to the strongly coupled conformal plasma calculations of Son (2006) and Jain (2009).
