# 3H and 3He calculations without angular momentum decomposition

> Results for the three nucleon (3N) bound state carried out using the "three dimensional" (3D) formalism are presented. In this approach calculations are performed without the use of angular momentum decomposition and instead rely directly on the 3D degrees of freedom of the nucleons. In this paper, for the first time, 3D results for $^{3}$He bound state with the inclusion of the screened Coulomb potential are compared to $^{3}$H calculations. Additionally, using these results, matrix elements of simple current operators related to the description of beta decay of the triton are given. All computations are carried out using the first generation of NNLO two nucleon (2N) and 3N forces from the Bochum - Bonn group.

# 3H and 3He calculations without angular momentum decomposition

## Key claim
For the first time, the "three dimensional" (3D) formalism, which avoids angular momentum decomposition and works directly with the 3D momentum degrees of freedom, is extended to compute the 3He bound state including a screened Coulomb potential, and these results are compared to 3H calculations. The method is demonstrated using first-generation NNLO two-nucleon and three-nucleon forces from the Bochum-Bonn group, with the caveat that the numerical grid sizes remain modest (16 or 17 points per dimension) and the results show some numerical artifacts in the first run that are reduced in the second run.

## Method
The Faddeev equation is solved in operator form without angular momentum decomposition, representing three-nucleon states via sets of scalar functions of Jacobi momenta and the angle between them. The energy-dependent linear operator A(E) is constructed from the free propagator, the two-nucleon potential (split into strong NN and screened Coulomb parts for 3He), and the three-nucleon force; its matrix representation is built using the Arnoldi algorithm with N = 80 or 110 basis vectors. The bound state energy is found by scanning energies until the eigenvalue closest to 1 is obtained, with a linear fit used to extrapolate to lambda = 1.

## Key numbers
- 3H bound state energy: extrapolated to E = -8.4043 MeV (eigenvalue lambda = 0.99998, second run)
- 3He bound state energy: extrapolated to E = -7.72654 MeV (eigenvalue lambda = 0.99997, second run, screening radius R = 10 fm)
- 3H expectation values (with 3N force, second run): kinetic energy 32.91 MeV, strong 2N potential -40.45 MeV, 3N force -0.7848 MeV, sum -8.329 MeV
- 3He expectation values (with 3N force, second run): kinetic energy 32.36 MeV, strong 2N potential -39.91 MeV, screened Coulomb 0.6929 MeV, 3N force -0.774 MeV, sum -7.628 MeV
- 3H expectation values (without 3N force, second run): kinetic energy 31.32 MeV, strong 2N potential -39.29 MeV, sum -7.973 MeV
- 3He expectation values (without 3N force, second run): kinetic energy 30.73 MeV, strong 2N potential -38.7 MeV, screened Coulomb 0.6953 MeV, sum -7.268 MeV
- Fermi current matrix elements for particle 1: both non-zero values are 0.332825 (for m_i = m_f = -1/2 and m_i = m_f = 1/2)
- Expectation value of (1 + tau_3)/2: 0.666395 for 3He, 0.333533 for 3H

## Context
This work extends the 3D formalism previously applied to 3H by Topolnicki (2017) and others, adding the screened Coulomb interaction for 3He and including the T = 3/2 isospin component. It also improves the numerical treatment of the three-nucleon force by decoupling integrals so they can be reused across different energies. The results enable future calculations with more modern chiral potentials and more complicated current operators for triton beta decay, moving beyond the simple single-nucleon current models presented here.
