# Generic size dependences of pairing in ultrasmall systems: electronic nano-devices and atomic nuclei

> Average, global pairing behaviours of electronic devices, like films, wires, and grains are studied with semiclassical methods, as Weyl and Thomas-Fermi approximations, in the ultrasmall, i.e., quantal regime, which exhibits strong quantum fluctuations and shell effects. This is the case also for superfluid nuclei. Results, mostly analytic, are elaborated for average size dependences, ready for easy use also in other circumstances. Comparisons with experimental results are given where possible.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the average size dependence of pairing in ultrasmall systems (metallic nano-devices and atomic nuclei) can be captured by semiclassical Thomas-Fermi and Weyl approximations, with the key hedge being that the BCS/Anderson approximation is valid only for steep mean-field potentials (like Woods-Saxon or box potentials) and fails for soft potentials such as harmonic oscillator traps used for cold atoms.

## Method

The authors employ semiclassical Thomas-Fermi (TF) and Weyl approximations to derive analytic expressions for the density of states (DOS) and the pairing gap in metallic films, wires, and grains, as well as in atomic nuclei. They solve the gap equation in the BCS/Anderson approximation using semiclassical level densities and size-dependent pairing matrix elements, and compare the resulting average gaps with quantal Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) solutions. The validity of the BCS approximation is tested by examining the effective potential pocket formation at the surface for different mean-field potential steepness parameters.

## Key numbers

* For a film of thickness L = 10 nm, the quantal and semiclassical DOS show "perfect average" agreement (Fig. 2, using Cadmium parameters from Shanenko (2007)).
* The semiclassical chemical potential for a film follows μ(L) = μ_B (1 + π/(2k_F^B L_M)), where μ_B is the bulk chemical potential and k_F^B is the bulk Fermi momentum.
* The Fermi momentum for a film follows k_F(L) = k_F^B (1 + π/(4k_F^B L_M)), leading to a 4-5% correction in the product ṽ_0 g_F^sc(μ) for L = 1 nm in Cadmium.
* The semiclassical gap formula is Δ/Δ_B = exp[-1/(|v_0|g_F^B(1 + π/(4k_F^B L_M)))] exp(1/(|v_0|g_F^B)), where Δ_B is the bulk gap.
* For Woods-Saxon potentials, the effective potential forms a surface pocket for width parameter a = 11 fm (with R_0 = 43.32 fm and |U_0| = 1200 MeV), but no pocket for a = 1 fm (R_0 = 22.12 fm).

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical approach to pairing previously applied to ultra-thin films by Pastore, Schuck, and Viñas (grain, year not specified) to other nano-devices (wires and grains) and atomic nuclei. The paper provides analytic formulas for the average size dependence of pairing, complementing exact numerical solutions of the HFB/BdG equations by Shanenko (2006-2011), Peeters, and others. The results enable easy estimation of average pairing gaps in ultrasmall systems without full numerical solution, and explain the empirical nuclear law Δ ∝ 12/√A (Bohr and Mottelson, 1969; Ring and Schuck, 1980) in terms of semiclassical size effects.
