# Velocity-induced Heavy Quarkonium Dissociation using the gauge-gravity correspondence

> Using the gauge-gravity duality we have obtained the potential between a heavy quark and an antiquark pair, which is moving perpendicular to the direction of orientation, in a strongly-coupled supersymmetric hot plasma. For the purpose we work on a metric in the gravity side, {\em viz.} OKS-BH geometry, whose dual in the gauge theory side runs with the energy and hence proves to be a better background for thermal QCD. The potential obtained has confining term both in vacuum and in medium, in addition to the Coulomb term alone, usually reported in the literature. As the velocity of the pair is increased the screening of the potential gets weakened, which may be understood by the decrease of effective temperature with the increase of velocity. The crucial observation of our work is that beyond a critical separation of the heavy quark pair, the potential develops an imaginary part which is nowadays understood to be the main source of dissociation. The imaginary part is found to vanish at small $r$, thus agrees with the perturbative result. Finally we have estimated the thermal width for the ground and first excited states and found that non-zero rapidities lead to an increase of thermal width. This implies that the moving quarkonia dissociate easier than the static ones, which agrees with other calculations. However, the width in our case is larger than other calculations due to the presence of confining terms.

## Key claim

Using the gauge-gravity duality with the OKS-BH metric (which accounts for renormalization group flow and is therefore a better background for thermal QCD than pure AdS), the authors find that the potential between a heavy quark and antiquark moving perpendicular to their orientation includes a confining term both in vacuum and in medium, and that beyond a critical separation the potential develops an imaginary part whose magnitude increases with velocity, implying that moving quarkonia dissociate more easily than static ones.

## Method

The authors employ the AdS/CFT correspondence to compute the Wilson loop expectation value for a heavy quark-antiquark pair moving through a strongly-coupled supersymmetric hot plasma, using the OKS-BH metric which incorporates RG flow and is asymptotically AdS. They boost the system to the rest frame of the pair, parametrize the Nambu-Goto action for the string worldsheet, and obtain the real and imaginary parts of the potential by analytically continuing string configurations beyond a critical separation, expanding in small velocity and small metric coefficients. The thermal width for ground and first excited charmonium states is then estimated from the imaginary part of the potential.

## Key numbers

* The critical separation for the static case in pure AdS is given as r_c = 0.87 z_h (where z_h is the black hole horizon position).
* The real and imaginary parts of the potential are plotted at a constant temperature of 400 MeV with coefficients A_2 and B_2 both taken as 0.24.
* The thermal width for the charmonium ground state is shown to increase with both temperature and velocity, with non-zero rapidities leading to an increase of thermal width compared to the static case.

## Context

This work extends previous holographic calculations of the heavy quark potential in strongly-coupled plasmas, which used pure AdS black hole metrics (Maldacena 1998, Brandhuber 1998, Rey 1998) and thus described conformal gauge theories unlike QCD. The authors improve upon calculations for moving pairs in pure AdS backgrounds (Liu 2006, Chernicoff 2006, Avramis 2006, Caceres 2006, Rajagopal) and upon the thermal worldsheet fluctuation method (Noronha 2013, Aliakbari 2014) by using the OKS-BH geometry that accounts for RG flow. The work also extends the authors' previous calculation for a static pair (Patra 2015) to the moving case. The finding that the thermal width is larger than in other calculations due to the presence of confining terms enables more realistic modeling of quarkonium dissociation in heavy-ion collisions.
