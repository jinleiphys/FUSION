# Low Mass Dilepton Rate from the Deconfined Phase

> We discuss low mass dilepton rates ($\le 1$ GeV) from the deconfined phase of QCD using both perturbative and non-perturbative models and compare with those from lattice gauge theory and in-medium hadron gas. Our analysis suggests that the rate at very low invariant mass ($ M\le 200$ MeV) using the nonperturbative gluon condensate in a semiempirical way within the Green function dominates over the Born-rate and independent of any uncertainty associated with the choice of the strong coupling in perturbation theory. On the other hand the rate from $ρ-q$ interaction in the deconfined phase is important between 200 MeV $\le M \le 1 GeV as it is almost of same order of the Born-rate as well as in-medium hadron gas rate. Also the higher order perturbative rate, leaving aside its various uncertainties, from HTL approximation becomes reliable at $M\ge 200$ MeV and also becomes comparable with the Born-rate and the lattice-rate for $M\ge 500$ MeV, constraining on the broad resonance structures in the dilepton rate at large invariant mass. We also discuss the lattice constraints on the low mass dilepton rate. Furthermore, we discuss a more realistic way to advocate the quark-hadron duality hypothesis based on the dilepton rates from QGP and hadron gas than it is done in the literature.

## Key claim

The paper argues that non-perturbative dilepton production rates from the deconfined phase are essential at low invariant mass (below 1 GeV), with the gluon condensate rate dominating below 200 MeV and the rho-q interaction rate being comparable to the Born rate between 200 MeV and 1 GeV, though the authors note that perturbative rates carry significant uncertainties from the choice of strong coupling and that lattice results cannot yet rule out structures like van Hove singularities at low mass.

## Method

The authors calculated dilepton production rates from the quark-gluon plasma using several approaches: lowest-order perturbative Born rate from q-qbar annihilation, Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) perturbation theory at one-loop and two-loop order, a non-perturbative model incorporating the gluon condensate from lattice QCD into quark Green functions, and a phenomenological rho-q interaction model using Vector Meson Dominance. They compared these rates with lattice gauge theory results from quenched QCD using the maximum entropy method and with in-medium hadron gas rates from the literature.

## Key numbers

- Born rate at zero momentum and M much less than T: 1.90 x 10^-8 (dimensionless, from equation for dR/d^4xd^4P)
- Thermal quark mass: m_q = gT/sqrt(6), with thermal gluon mass m_g^2 = 8m_q^2/3
- Gluon condensate rate calculated at T = 2T_c with T_c approximately 165 MeV, dominating over Born rate for M/T_c less than or equal to 2 (M less than or equal to 330 MeV)
- Rho-q coupling G_rho approximately 6, obtained from G_rho = sqrt(8 m_rho^2 G_2)
- Lattice results at T = 1.5T_c and 3T_c show vector spectral functions vanishingly small for M/T less than or equal to 4 due to reconstruction cut-off
- HTL alpha_s-correction dominates over Born term for invariant masses below 300 MeV at T = 200 MeV and E = 1 GeV
- Two-loop HTL rate dominates over Born-term for M/m_q less than or equal to 2 in the perturbative regime (g less than or equal to 1)

## Context

This work extends earlier perturbative calculations by Aurenche et al. (1998) and non-perturbative studies using gluon condensates by Schaefer and Thoma (1999) and Mustafa, Schaefer, and Thoma (2000), while addressing the unresolved issue of low-mass dilepton emission from the deconfined phase at SPS and RHIC energies. The analysis enables a more realistic assessment of quark-hadron duality by comparing QGP and hadron gas rates, and provides guidance for future lattice QCD calculations to improve the low-mass regime where current lattice results from Karsch et al. suffer from finite volume and cut-off artefacts.
