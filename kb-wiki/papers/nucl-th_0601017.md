# Molecular dynamics simulation of strongly coupled QCD plasmas

> The properties of a strongly interacting quark plasma are investigated by molecular dynamics method including non-abelian quark-quark potential. Our main goal is to study the thermalization process in this system. We find an interesting resonance-like behaviour: at a characteristic time close to the inverse plasma frequency the quark plasma is heated up substantially via energy transfer from quark potential energy into one particle kinetic energy. Color rotation mechanism enhances the effectivity of this heating process, leading to a very fast thermalization with high temperature.

# Molecular dynamics simulation of strongly coupled QCD plasmas

## Key claim

The authors find a resonance-like heating effect in a strongly interacting quark plasma simulated via molecular dynamics: when the characteristic time for reassigning the color-dependent pair interactions (tau_D) is close to the inverse plasma frequency (approximately 0.19 fm/c), the system heats up substantially via energy transfer from potential to kinetic energy, and this heating is enhanced by an additional color rotation mechanism. The claim is explicitly conditional on the choice of tau_D and tau_C parameters, and the effect disappears in the limiting cases of very fast or very slow reassignment.

## Method

The authors perform classical molecular dynamics simulations of a quark plasma where quarks interact via a non-abelian Coulomb-like potential V = <lambda_i * lambda_j> * alpha / r_ij, with the color factor <lambda_i * lambda_j> taking values of +1/3 (for equal-colored pairs, always repulsive) or randomly either +1/3 or -2/3 (for different-colored pairs, repulsive or attractive with equal probability). Gluons are treated as a background field that provides an infinite heat reservoir, periodically reassigns the random color-pair interaction values (with characteristic time tau_D), and exchanges color states between quarks (with characteristic time tau_C). The simulation uses periodic boundary conditions with Ewald summation, a soft-core cutoff at 0.1 fm, and fixed input parameters: density n = 10 quarks/fm^3, initial temperature T_0 = 200 MeV, effective quark mass m_q = 300 MeV/c^2, and coupling alpha = 1.

## Key numbers

- Plasma frequency derived as omega_P^2 = 4*pi*alpha*n/m_q, giving inverse plasma frequency 1/omega_P approximately 0.19 fm/c
- Input density: n = 10 quarks/fm^3
- Initial temperature: T_0 = 200 MeV
- Effective quark mass: m_q = 300 MeV/c^2
- Interaction coupling strength: alpha = 1
- Soft-core cutoff radius: 0.1 fm
- Color rotation enhances energy transfer by 10 to 20 percent (for tau_C = tau_D with N_C = 50% or 100%)
- Wigner-Seitz radius: a_WS = 0.3628 fm (used for pair-correlation function comparison)

## Context

This work extends classical molecular dynamics methods, previously applied to one-component plasmas (OCP) and strongly coupled plasmas (MD, OCP), to the non-abelian quark-quark interaction relevant for the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma indicated by RHIC data and lattice QCD calculations (sQCD, QCDquasi). The approach is complementary to lattice QCD and massive quasi-particle models, enabling qualitative study of thermalization and clusterization processes that other methods cannot address. The observed structural similarity of the repulsive equal-colored component to classical OCP in the gas phase (Gamma around unity) suggests strong non-ideality, and the onset of clusterization at higher tau_D values connects to discussions of quark matter clustering (Shuryak, Fisher).
