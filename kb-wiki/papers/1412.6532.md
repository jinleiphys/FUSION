# Thermal model for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}_{NN} = 2.76$ TeV with explicit treatment of hadronic ground states

> Various explanations of the anomalous proton to pion ratio at the LHC are discussed. The special emphasis is set on the Cracow thermal model with single freeze-out. This model allows to get a good agreement for both the mean hadron multiplicities and the spectra. Moreover, the values of the fit parameters indicate the possibility of pion Bose condensation in the most central collisions at the LHC. Therefore, a modification of the thermal framework is proposed that explicitly allows for the condensation in the ground state. The generalised model makes a link between equilibrium and non-equilibrium thermal models. It also suggests that the pion condensation may be formed in the central collisions.

## Key claim

The Cracow thermal model with single freeze-out, when extended to allow explicit pion Bose condensation in the ground state (BEC model), can simultaneously explain the anomalous proton-to-pion ratio and the low-\(p_T\) enhancement of pions in Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}_{NN} = 2.76\) TeV, but the existence of condensation depends on the unmeasured low-\(p_T\) spectrum of neutral pions.

## Method

The author uses the Cracow single-freeze-out model with a Hubble-like flow and the Cooper-Frye formula, implementing primordial distributions with non-equilibrium fugacity factors \(\gamma_q\) and \(\gamma_s\) for light and strange quarks. The fit parameters (volume, temperature, \(\gamma_q\), \(\gamma_s\)) are taken from prior fits to hadron abundances using the SHARE code, and the model is extended by adding an explicit Bose condensate term for the zero-momentum state of pions (BEC model). The spectra and yields are computed for pions, kaons, protons, and other hadrons in several centrality bins and compared to ALICE data.

## Key numbers

- The critical value for pion condensation is \(\gamma_q^{\rm critic} = \exp(m_{\pi^0} / (2T))\), corresponding to a pion chemical potential \(\mu_\pi \simeq 134\) MeV, very close to the \(\pi^0\) mass \(m_{\pi^0} \simeq 134.98\) MeV.
- In the equilibrium (EQ) model, the temperature is nearly constant between 150–160 MeV across centralities.
- In the non-equilibrium (NEQ) and BEC models, the temperature in most central collisions is lower than in EQ, while in very peripheral collisions the BEC temperature approaches the EQ temperature.
- The \(\gamma_q\) and \(\gamma_s\) values in BEC are always smaller than in NEQ; at high centrality both approach unity.
- The measured \(\pi^0\) spectrum exists only for \(p_T \gtrsim 700\) MeV, which gives only about 1/3 of the total expected \(\pi^0\) multiplicity.

## Context

This work extends the Cracow single-freeze-out model (Broniowski et al., 2001; Rybczynski et al., 2012) and the non-equilibrium hadronization approach (Petran et al., 2013) to explicitly include pion Bose condensation, linking equilibrium and non-equilibrium thermal models. It addresses the long-standing proton anomaly at LHC energies, where equilibrium thermal models (Stachel et al., 2013) fail to describe proton abundances, and where hydrodynamic models (Gale et al., 2012; Molnar et al., 2014) struggle with the low-\(p_T\) pion enhancement. The results enable future experimental tests: measuring the low-\(p_T\) charged and neutral pion spectra would confirm or rule out the onset of pion condensation in central Pb+Pb collisions.
