# Quenched Λ spin-orbit splitting by relativistic Fock diagram in single-Λ hypernuclei

> We extend the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) theory to study the structure of single-$Λ$ hypernuclei. The density dependence is taken in both meson-nucleon and meson-hyperon coupling strengths, and the induced $Λ$-nucleon ($ΛN$) effective interactions are determined by fitting $Λ$ separation energies to the experimental data for several single-$Λ$ hypernuclei. The equilibrium of nuclear dynamics described by the RHF model in normal atomic nuclei, namely, the balance between nuclear attractive and repulsive interactions, is then found to be drastically changed in single-$Λ$ hypernuclei, revealing a different role of Fock terms via $Λ$ hyperon from the nucleon exchange. Since only one hyperon exists in a single-$Λ$ hypernucleus, the overwhelmed $ΛN$ and $ΛΛ$ attractions via the Hartree than the $ΛΛ$ repulsion from the Fock terms require an alternation of meson-hyperon coupling strengths in RHF to rebalance the effective nuclear force with the strangeness degree of freedom, leading to an improved description of $Λ$ Dirac mass and correspondingly a systematically reduced $σ$-$Λ$ coupling strength $g_{σΛ}$ in current models as compared to those relativistic mean-field (RMF) approaches without Fock terms. As a result, the effective $Λ$ spin-orbit coupling potential in the ground state of hypernuclei is suppressed, and these RHF models predict correspondingly a quenching effect in $Λ$ spin-orbit splitting in comparison with the RMF cases. Furthermore, the $Λ$ spin-orbit splitting could decrease efficiently by evolving the hyperon-relevant couplings $g_{σΛ}$ and $g_{ωΛ}$ simultaneously, where to reconcile with the empirical value the RHF models address a larger parameter space of meson-hyperon couplings.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including Fock exchange terms in relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) theory, as opposed to the simpler relativistic mean-field (RMF) approximation, systematically reduces the effective \(\sigma\)-\(\Lambda\) coupling strength \(g_{\sigma\Lambda}\) and thereby quenches the \(\Lambda\) spin-orbit splitting in single-\(\Lambda\) hypernuclei, provided the coupling strengths are refitted to reproduce experimental \(\Lambda\) separation energies.

## Method

The authors extended the density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) theory to include the \(\Lambda\) hyperon degree of freedom, with meson-nucleon and meson-hyperon coupling strengths taken as functions of baryon density. The \(\Lambda\)-nucleon effective interactions were determined by fitting the calculated \(\Lambda\) separation energies to experimental data for several single-\(\Lambda\) hypernuclei. The ground-state properties of spherical hypernuclei were then computed self-consistently using the RHF formalism, and the results were compared to those from relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations without Fock terms.

## Key numbers

* The \(\Lambda\) spin-orbit splitting predicted by RHF models is systematically reduced (quenched) compared to RMF predictions, with the quenching effect arising from the inclusion of Fock diagrams.
* For the ground state of single-\(\Lambda\) hypernuclei where the \(\Lambda\) occupies the \(1s_{1/2}\) orbit, a relation \(E_{\sigma,\Lambda\Lambda}^E = -E_{\sigma,\Lambda\Lambda}^D / 2\) holds between the Fock and Hartree contributions from the \(\sigma\) meson.
* The \(\Lambda\) spin-orbit splitting can decrease efficiently by simultaneously evolving the hyperon-relevant couplings \(g_{\sigma\Lambda}\) and \(g_{\omega\Lambda}\), and the RHF models address a larger parameter space of meson-hyperon couplings to reconcile with the empirical value.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approach, previously successful for normal atomic nuclei (Long 2006, Long 2008, Geng 2019), to single-\(\Lambda\) hypernuclei. It contrasts with relativistic mean-field (RMF) models (Brockmann 1977, Glendenning 1991, Sugahara 1994) that lack Fock terms and therefore predict larger \(\Lambda\) spin-orbit splittings. The findings enable improved descriptions of hypernuclear structure and may help resolve the hyperon-puzzle in neutron star equations of state by providing more realistic hyperon-nucleon interactions.
