# Exploring the Neutrino Mass Hierarchy from Isotopic Ratios of Supernova Nucleosynthesis Products in Presolar Grains

> We study the nucleosynthesis in a core-collapse supernova model including newly calculated neutrino-induced reaction rates with both collective and Mikheyev-Smirnov-Wolfenstein (MSW) neutrino-flavor oscillations considered. We show that the measurement of a pair of $^{11}$B/$^{10}$B and $^{138}$La/$^{139}$La or $^6$Li/$^7$Li and $^{138}$La/$^{139}$La in presolar grains that are inferred to have originated from core-collapse supernovae could constrain the neutrino mass hierarchy. The new shell-model and the model of quasi-particle random phase approximation in the estimate of three important neutrino-induced reactions, $ν+^{16}$O, $ν+^{20}$Ne, and $ν+^{138}$Ba are applied in our reaction network. The new rates decrease the calculated $^{7}$Li/$^{6}$Li ratio by a factor of five compared with the previous study. More interestingly, these new rates result in a clear separation of the isotopic ratio of $^{11}$B/$^{10}$B between normal and inverted mass hierarchies in the O/Ne, O/C, and C/He layers where $^{138}$La abundance depends strongly on the mass hierarchy. In these layers, the sensitivity of the calculated abundances of $^{10,11}$B and $^{6,7}$Li to the nuclear reaction uncertainties is also tiny. Therefore, we propose that the $^{11}$B/$^{10}$B vs. $^{138}$La/$^{139}$La and $^6$Li/$^7$Li vs. $^{138}$La/$^{139}$La in type X silicon carbide grains sampled material from C/He layer can be used as a new probe to constrain the neutrino mass hierarchy.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that measuring correlated isotopic ratios of $^{11}$B/$^{10}$B and $^{138}$La/$^{139}$La (or $^6$Li/$^7$Li and $^{138}$La/$^{139}$La) in type X silicon carbide presolar grains that originated from core-collapse supernovae could constrain the neutrino mass hierarchy, but only if the grains sample material from the C/He layer where the C/O ratio exceeds unity and the separation between hierarchies is clearest.

## Method

The authors performed nucleosynthesis calculations in a 20 $M_{\odot}$ core-collapse supernova model with metallicity $Z = Z_{\odot}/4$, using newly calculated neutrino-induced reaction rates for $\nu+^{16}$O and $\nu+^{20}$Ne from shell-model calculations (Su18) and a QRPA calculation for $^{138}$Ba$(\nu_e,e^-)^{138}$La. They included both collective neutrino flavor oscillations and the Mikheyev-Smirnov-Wolfenstein (MSW) effect to modify the neutrino energy spectra, then folded these spectra with energy-dependent cross sections to compute isotopic yields of $^6$Li, $^7$Li, $^{10}$B, $^{11}$B, and $^{138}$La across five distinct layers of the supernova ejecta.

## Key numbers

- The new $\nu+^{16}$O and $\nu+^{20}$Ne reaction rates decrease the calculated $^7$Li/$^6$Li ratio by a factor of five compared with previous studies using Hoffmann and Woosley (1992) rates.
- The measured $^{11}$B/$^{10}$B ratio in X grains is $4.68 \pm 0.31$ and the $^7$Li/$^6$Li ratio is $11.83 \pm 0.29$, both with 1$\sigma$ errors, compared to solar values of $(^{11}B/^{10}B)_{sol}=4.04$ and $(^7Li/^6Li)_{sol}=12.06$.
- The total neutrino energy is $3 \times 10^{53}$ erg with an exponential decay timescale of 3 s; initial neutrino temperatures are 3.2 MeV for $\nu_e$, 5.0 MeV for $\bar{\nu}_e$, and 6.0 MeV for $\nu_x$.
- The $^{12}$C$(\nu_e,e^-)^{12}$N$_{g.s.}$ and $^{12}$C$(\nu,\nu')^{12}$C* reaction cross sections have $\pm10\%$ and $\pm20\%$ uncertainties for CC and NC channels, respectively.
- The $^{138}$La/$^{139}$La ratio shows a noticeable disparity between normal and inverted hierarchies in the O/Ne layer (region I) and O/C layer (region II), with clear separation in $^{11}$B/$^{10}$B vs. $^{138}$La/$^{139}$La appearing in the mass region between 3.58 and 3.90 $M_{\odot}$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Ko (2020) and Ko (2022) that showed the $^{138}$La/$^{11}$B ratio is sensitive to the neutrino mass hierarchy, and by Yoshida (2006, 2008) who demonstrated that charged-current reactions depend strongly on the mass hierarchy through MSW effects. The new calculations incorporate updated neutrino-induced reaction rates from Suzuki (2018) for $\nu+^{16}$O and $\nu+^{20}$Ne, which were not included in earlier nucleosynthesis studies, and show that these rates produce a clearer separation between hierarchies in the $^{11}$B/$^{10}$B ratio. The work enables future measurements of Li, B, and La isotopes in presolar X SiC grains to serve as a direct probe of the neutrino mass hierarchy, complementing terrestrial experiments like NO$\nu$A which favor normal hierarchy at 1.96$\sigma$ confidence level.
