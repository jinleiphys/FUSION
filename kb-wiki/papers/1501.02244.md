# The Resonance Scattering Phenomenon of Fast Negatively Charged Particles in a Single Crystal

> The energy spectrum of the extended attractive potential of a crystallographic row for negatively charged particles has quasi-bound states. It follows that a negatively charged particle with small transversal momentum component ($p_{\bot} R <<1$) may undergo resonance scattering. Thus the resonance scattering phenomenon can be observed in a single crystal, when fast electrons move with a small glancing angle ($θ_0 << 1/pR$) to a crystallographic axis. The calculated results for the electrons and angular widths of resonance peaks are consistent with experimental data.

# The Resonance Scattering Phenomenon of Fast Negatively Charged Particles in a Single Crystal

## Key claim
Resonance scattering of fast negatively charged particles (pR >> 1) can occur in a single crystal when the transverse momentum component satisfies p⊥R << 1, contradicting the usual assumption that resonance scattering in elastic collisions requires slow particles (pR << 1). The phenomenon arises from quasi-bound states in the energy spectrum of the extended attractive potential of a crystallographic row.

## Method
The authors solved the Dirac equation for a fast particle in the continuum potential of a crystallographic row with longitudinal length L and transverse radius R, decomposing the wave function inside the interaction field into eigenfunctions of the transverse motion. Using continuity conditions at the boundaries and the optical theorem, they derived a Breit-Wigner formula for the total cross section, with resonance conditions expressed in terms of the glancing angle θ0 relative to the crystallographic axis.

## Key numbers
- Electron energy: E = 15 MeV, scattered by a silicon single crystal with thickness L = 1.4 μm along the (111) crystallographic axis.
- Silicon potential parameters: U0 = 23 eV, R⁻¹ = me²Z¹/³ = 9.0 × 10³ eV.
- Central peak position: θ0 ≈ 0.02°.
- Lateral resonance peak position: θ0 res ≈ 0.1°.
- Angular width of lateral peak: Γ ≈ 0.5 × θ0 res.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies on fast electron scattering in single crystals, where the importance of quasi-bound states was first noted (Kovalev and Kalashnikov, 1979, referencing prior work from 1979 and earlier). The observation that the scattering process has a resonance nature was first made in a prior study (Kovalev and Kalashnikov, 1979, referencing work [5] from the original paper). The calculated results show satisfactory agreement with experimental data (Kovalev and Kalashnikov, 1979, referencing experimental data from [7]), enabling further investigation of resonance scattering phenomena for fast negatively charged particles in crystalline materials.
