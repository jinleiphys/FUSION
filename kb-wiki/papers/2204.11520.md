# Impact of Charge Symmetry Breaking on Gluon and Sea Quark Distributions in the Pion and Kaon

> In this exploratory study, I present, for the first time, the implications of the charge symmetry breaking (CSB) that arise from the $u$ and $d$ quark mass differences on gluon and sea quark distribution functions of the pion and kaon in the framework of the Nambu--Jona-Lasino (NJL) model, which is a quark-level chiral effective theory of QCD, with the help of the proper-time regularization scheme to simulate color confinement of QCD. From the analysis, one finds that the charge symmetry (CS) gluon distribution for the pion has a good agreement with the prediction results obtained from the recent lattice QCD simulation and JAM global fit QCD analysis at a higher scale of $Q^2 =$ 5 GeV$^2$. The size of the CSB effects on gluon and sea quark distributions for the pion with the realistic ratios of $m_u/m_d = 0.5$ at $Q^2 =$ 5 GeV$^2$ are respectively estimated by 1.3\% and 2.0\% at $x \simeq 1$ in comparison with those for $m_u/m_d = 1.0$, while those for the kaon are approximately about 0.3\% and 0.5\% at $ x \simeq 1$, respectively. A remarkable result is found that the CSB effects on gluon distribution for the kaon are smaller than that for the pion, which has a similar prediction result as that for the CS case.

## Key claim

This exploratory study presents, for the first time, the implications of charge symmetry breaking (CSB) arising from u and d quark mass differences on gluon and sea quark distribution functions of the pion and kaon within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model; the CSB effects are estimated at the specific quark mass ratio m_u/m_d = 0.5 and at a high scale of Q^2 = 5 GeV^2, with the condition that these effects are most pronounced at high x values near x ≃ 1.

## Method

The author used the three-flavor NJL model, a quark-level chiral effective theory of QCD, with the proper-time regularization scheme to simulate color confinement. Valence quark distributions for the pion and kaon were computed from the model at an initial scale Q_0^2 = 0.16 GeV^2, where gluons and sea quarks are absent; gluon and sea quark distributions were then dynamically generated by evolving the valence distributions to Q^2 = 5 GeV^2 using next-to-leading order (NLO) DGLAP QCD evolution equations.

## Key numbers

- The CSB effect on gluon distributions for the pion with m_u/m_d = 0.5 at Q^2 = 5 GeV^2 is 1.3% at x ≃ 1, compared to the charge-symmetric case m_u/m_d = 1.0.
- The CSB effect on sea quark distributions for the pion with m_u/m_d = 0.5 at Q^2 = 5 GeV^2 is 2.0% at x ≃ 1, compared to m_u/m_d = 1.0.
- The CSB effect on gluon distributions for the kaon with m_u/m_d = 0.5 at Q^2 = 5 GeV^2 is approximately 0.3% at x ≃ 1, compared to m_u/m_d = 1.0.
- The CSB effect on sea quark distributions for the kaon with m_u/m_d = 0.5 at Q^2 = 5 GeV^2 is approximately 0.5% at x ≃ 1, compared to m_u/m_d = 1.0.
- For the pion with m_u/m_d = 0.0 (maximal CSB), the CSB effect on gluon distributions is 5.5% and on sea d-quark distributions is 6.0% at x ≃ 0.6 to x ≃ 1 at Q^2 = 5 GeV^2.
- The charge-symmetric (m_u/m_d = 1.0) gluon distribution for the pion at Q^2 = 5 GeV^2 agrees with predictions from lattice QCD simulation and JAM global fit QCD analysis.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of CSB effects on valence quark distributions and electromagnetic form factors of the pion and kaon within the NJL model by Hutauruk (2018, 2019), which did not examine gluon or sea quark distributions. The results provide estimates of CSB effects on partonic structure that could inform future experiments at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), the Electron-Ion Collider in China (EicC), COMPASS++/AMBER at CERN-SPS, and SoLiD at JLab12, as well as parity-violating deep inelastic scattering and pion-induced Drell-Yan reactions.
