# Photoproduction of $K^{+}Λ$ with a Regge-plus-resonance model

> Making use of the hybrid Regge-plus-resonance model, we investigate the process of kaon photoproduction off the proton target. We present a new model whose free parameters were adjusted to data in and above the resonance region and which provides an acceptable description of experimental data. The overwhelming majority of nucleon resonances selected in this analysis overlaps with those selected in our previous analyses and also with the Bayesian analysis with the Regge-plus-resonance model, which we deem to be dependable. A novel feature of our model consists in applying a different scheme for gauge-invariance restoration, which results in a need for implementing a contact current. As we further reveal, the choice of the gauge invariance restoration scheme as well as the choice of either pseudoscalar or pseudovector coupling in the strong vertex play a significant role for cross-section predictions at forward angles where data are scarce.

## Key claim

The authors present a new Regge-plus-resonance (RPR) model for kaon photoproduction that provides an acceptable description of experimental data, but they emphasize that the choice of gauge-invariance restoration scheme and the choice of pseudoscalar versus pseudovector coupling in the strong vertex significantly affect cross-section predictions at forward kaon angles, where experimental data are scarce.

## Method

The authors constructed a hybrid Regge-plus-resonance model combining Reggeized t-channel exchanges of K+(494) and K*(892) trajectories with s-channel nucleon resonance exchanges. They employed a gauge-invariance restoration method based on generalized Ward-Takahashi identities with a contact current, contrasting this with the commonly used Guidal-Laget-Vanderhaeghen (GLV) method. Free parameters were adjusted to experimental data using the MINUIT least-squares fitting code, first fitting high-energy data (W > 2.36 GeV) for Regge background parameters, then adding resonance-region data.

## Key numbers

- The model includes 14 nucleon resonances with spins up to 5/2, with masses and widths adjusted within Particle Data Group limits; for example, the S11(1535) resonance was assigned mass 1520 MeV and width 110 MeV, while the P13(1900) was assigned mass 1900 MeV and width 400 MeV
- Two new D15 partial-wave states were introduced: N(2060)5/2- with mass 2130 MeV and width 350 MeV, and N(2570)5/2- with mass 2570 MeV and width 250 MeV
- The high-energy fitting dataset included 230 cross-section data points from CLAS 2010 for W > 2.36 GeV, 95 points from CLAS 2005 for laboratory photon energy >= 2.505 GeV, 20 points from LEPS 2018 for laboratory photon energy >= 2.55 GeV, and 305 hyperon polarization data points from CLAS 2010 for W > 2.23 GeV
- The resonance-region fitting added 1247 cross-section data points from CLAS 2010 for W < 2.36 GeV
- The background cutoff parameter Lambda_bgr was set to 1.5 GeV for calculations with pseudoscalar coupling

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous isobar model analyses of K+Lambda production (Bydzovsky and Skoupil, 2016, 2018) and the Ghent group's Regge-plus-resonance model (RPR-2011) by introducing a different gauge-invariance restoration scheme based on Haberzettl et al. (2006). The model enables more reliable predictions of hypernucleus production cross sections, which require accurate descriptions of the elementary photoproduction process at forward kaon angles. The authors note that their resonance selection overlaps substantially with their previous analyses and with Bayesian analyses using the RPR framework.
