# Full major-shell calculation for states that were degenerate in a single-j-shell calculation

## Key claim

In a full $fp$-shell calculation using the FPD6 interaction, states that were degenerate in a single-$j$-shell calculation with $T=0$ two-body matrix elements set to zero are no longer degenerate; most, but not all, of the resulting energy splitting comes from the $T=0$ part of the interaction, with two exceptions for low-angular-momentum states where configuration mixing is significant.

## Method

The authors performed full $fp$-shell calculations using the FPD6 interaction for selected high-spin states in $^{43}$Sc ($^{43}$Ti), $^{44}$Ti, $^{45}$Ti, $^{46}$V, and $^{47}$V. They compared these results with calculations using T0FPD6, an interaction obtained by setting the $T=0$ two-body matrix elements to zero while keeping the $T=1$ ones unchanged, and with experimental excitation energies from the National Nuclear Data Center.

## Key numbers

- In $^{44}$Ti, the $(9^+_1 - 10^+_1)$ splitting is 1.214 MeV for FPD6 but only 0.094 MeV for T0FPD6.
- In $^{43}$Sc, the $(17/2)^-_1$ state has excitation energy 4.380 MeV with FPD6 and 3.671 MeV with T0FPD6, compared to the experimental value 4.360 MeV.
- In $^{44}$Ti, the $10^+_1$ state has excitation energy 7.614 MeV with FPD6 and 6.360 MeV with T0FPD6, compared to the experimental value 7.671 MeV.
- In $^{46}$V, the $12^+_1$ state has excitation energy 7.841 MeV with FPD6 and 8.276 MeV with T0FPD6, compared to the experimental value 8.268 MeV.
- In $^{47}$V, the $(29/2)^-_1$ state has excitation energy 11.848 MeV with FPD6 and 9.412 MeV with T0FPD6, compared to the experimental value 10.7685 MeV.
- In $^{47}$V, the $(29/2)^-_1 - (31/2)^-_1$ splitting is 0.780 MeV for FPD6 and 0.072 MeV for T0FPD6, compared to the experimental value 0.765 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous single-$j$-shell calculations by Escuderos, Robinson, and Zamick (2005) that identified degeneracies in $^{43}$Sc, $^{44}$Ti, $^{45}$Ti, $^{46}$V, and $^{47}$V when $T=0$ matrix elements were set to zero, with some degeneracies attributed to a partial dynamical symmetry and others labeled as degeneracy without symmetry. The full $fp$-shell results provide benchmarks for determining the correct $T=0$ effective interaction, complementing earlier studies by Robinson, Escuderos, and Zamick (2005) on yrast spectra and $B(E2)$ values, and by Robinson and Zamick (2002) on Gamow-Teller matrix elements and scissors mode strength. The authors note that high-spin splittings are the simplest to test experimentally, though some data are missing for $^{45}$Ti and $^{46}$V.
