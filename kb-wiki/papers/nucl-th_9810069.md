# The Reaction $pp \to ppϕ$ and the Validity of the OZI Rule

> The reaction $pp \to pp ϕ$ is investigated within a relativistic meson-exchange model of hadronic interactions. The possibility of extracting the $NNϕ$ coupling constant from a study of this process is explored. A combined analysis of $ω-$ and $ϕ$-meson production in proton-proton collisions utilizing recent data on these reactions is carried out and yields values for $g_{NNϕ}$ that are compatible with the OZI rule.

## Key claim

The combined analysis of ω- and φ-meson production in proton-proton collisions yields values for the NNφ coupling constant that are compatible with the OZI rule, but the extracted coupling constant is not uniquely determined; rather it ranges from g_NNφ = -0.163 to g_NNφ = -1.40, with the OZI-predicted value being approximately -(0.60 ± 0.15).

## Method

The reaction pp → ppφ was described within a relativistic meson-exchange model using the Distorted Wave Born Approximation, with the Bonn B NN interaction for final-state interactions and no explicit initial-state interaction. The dominant production mechanisms considered were the nucleonic current and the φρπ meson-exchange current; the NNφ coupling constant was extracted by requiring the nucleonic current contribution to reproduce the φ-meson angular distribution measured by the DISTO collaboration at T_lab = 2.85 GeV. A combined analysis of ω- and φ-meson production was performed, using total cross sections for pp → ppω from Saclay data and the φ angular distribution from DISTO, assuming common form factors and SU(3) relations between corresponding parameters.

## Key numbers

- The total cross section for pp → ppφ at T_lab = 2.85 GeV is estimated as σ ≈ 0.3 μb, obtained by multiplying the DISTO ratio σ(pp→ppφ)/σ(pp→ppω) with the interpolated ω-production total cross section.
- The extracted NNφ coupling constant ranges from g_NNφ = -0.163 to g_NNφ = -1.40, depending on model parameters.
- The OZI-rule prediction from SU(3) flavor symmetry gives g_NNφ = -3 g_NNρ sin(α_v) ≈ -(0.60 ± 0.15), using g_NNρ = 2.63–3.36 and α_v ≈ 3.8°.
- The NNω coupling constant is fixed to the SU(3) value g_NNω = 9, based on g_NNρ from the literature.
- The tensor-to-vector coupling ratio κ_φ is assumed equal to κ_ω, with -0.5 ≤ κ_ω ≤ 0.5.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of ω-meson production in pp collisions (Nakayama et al., 1998) to the φ-meson channel, motivated by unexpectedly large φ/ω ratios observed in antiproton-proton annihilation at rest (compiled by Ellis, 1995) and preliminary DISTO data showing a ratio about 8 times larger than the OZI estimate. The analysis enables a test of whether the observed φ enhancement implies an intrinsic ss̅ component in the nucleon, as argued by Ellis (1995), or can be explained without OZI violation, as in the two-step mechanism of Locher et al. The results show that a dynamical model is necessary before drawing conclusions about OZI rule validity.
