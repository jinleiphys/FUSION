# Many-body characterization of topological superconductivity: The Richardson-Gaudin-Kitaev chain

> What distinguishes trivial from topological superluids in interacting many-body systems where the number of particles is conserved? Building on a class of integrable pairing Hamiltonians, we present a number-conserving, interacting variation of the Kitaev model, the Richardson-Gaudin-Kitaev chain, that remains exactly solvable for periodic and antiperiodic boundary conditions. Our model allows us to identify fermionic parity switches that distinctively characterize topological superconductivity in interacting many-body systems. Although the Majorana zero-modes in this model have only a power-law confinement, we may still define many-body Majorana operators by tuning the flux to a fermion parity switch. We derive a closed-form expression for an interacting topological invariant and show that the transition away from the topological phase is of third order.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an exactly solvable, number-conserving, interacting variation of the Kitaev chain (the Richardson-Gaudin-Kitaev chain) that retains topological superconductivity despite having only power-law confined Majorana zero-modes, and shows that the topological phase transition is of third order.

## Method

The authors define a Hamiltonian with momentum-dependent single-particle spectrum ε_k = -2t₁ cos k - 2t₂ cos 2k and an odd-parity pairing interaction η_k = sin(k/2)√(t₁ + 4t₂ cos²(k/2)). The model belongs to the hyperbolic family of exactly solvable Richardson-Gaudin pairing Hamiltonians, allowing eigenstates to be constructed via Richardson-Gaudin equations. The authors solve these equations numerically for systems up to L ≈ 2000 sites at quarter filling, and perform thermodynamic limit extrapolations to obtain the phase diagram, fermion parity switches, and topological invariant.

## Key numbers

- The Read-Green coupling (critical line) is g_c = G^φ_{n=1} L/2, which separates the topological (weak-pairing) from trivial (strong-pairing) phases.
- The Moore-Read coupling occurs at g_M = G^φ_{n=M} L/2, with g_M^{-1} = 1 - ρ in the thermodynamic limit.
- The Read-Green boundary in the thermodynamic limit is g_c^{-1} = 1 - 2ρ.
- For the antiperiodic chain with ρ = 1/4, the blocked momentum k₀ reaches k₀ = π/L at g₀ ≈ 1.1936.
- The ground state energy density in the thermodynamic limit is e₀ = -2t₊ρ - (4/g)Δ² + (4/π)∫₀^π η²_k v²_k dk.
- The inverse compressibility in the thermodynamic limit takes the simple forms χ(0) = -8μ and χ(2π) = 8|μ| for g > g₀.

## Context

This work extends the mean-field Kitaev chain (Kitaev 2001) into the interacting, number-conserving regime by constructing an exactly solvable model based on Richardson-Gaudin integrable pairing Hamiltonians (Richardson 1963, Gaudin 1976, Dukelsky 2001, Ortiz 2005). The model enables the first demonstration of fermion parity switches as a criterion for topological superconductivity beyond mean-field, and shows that the topological phase transition is third-order, matching the behavior found for the two-dimensional chiral p-wave superconductor (Rombouts 2010, Lerma 2011). The long-range pairing interaction may be relevant for chains of magnetic nanoparticles on superconducting substrates (Nadj-Perge 2013, Pientka 2013).
