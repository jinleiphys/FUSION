# Equation of state of hypernuclear matter: tuning hyperon--scalar-meson couplings

> We discuss to which extent the modifications of the hyperon--scalar-meson coupling constants affect the equation of state (EoS) hypernuclear matter. The study is carried out within a relativistic density functional theory. The nucleonic matter is described in terms of a density-dependent parametrization of nucleon-meson couplings, whereas the hyperon--meson couplings are deduced from the octet model. We identify the parameter space of hyperon-meson couplings for which massive stellar configurations with $M\le 2.25 M_{\odot}$ exist. We also discuss the EoS at finite temperatures with and without of a trapped neutrino component and show that neutrinos stiffen the EoS and change qualitatively the composition of stellar matter.

# Equation of state of hypernuclear matter: tuning hyperon–scalar-meson couplings

## Key claim
By tuning the hyperon–scalar-meson coupling constants within the constraints of the octet model, hypernuclear equations of state can produce stellar configurations with masses up to 2.25 solar masses, but only when hyperons are coupled to scalar mesons more weakly than predicted by the SU(6) quark model; the existence of such massive hyperonic stars depends on the specific choice of coupling ratios.

## Method
The study uses a relativistic density functional theory with a density-dependent parametrization (DD-ME2) for nucleon–meson couplings. Hyperon–meson couplings are derived from the SU(3) octet model, with the hyperon–scalar-meson couplings varied within constraints from the octet mixing relation and the Nijmegen Soft Core potential. The equation of state is computed at zero and finite temperature (50 MeV) both with and without trapped neutrinos, and the resulting stellar mass–radius relations are obtained by solving the Tolman–Oppenheimer–Volkoff equations.

## Key numbers
- Maximum stellar mass of 2.25 solar masses is achievable for certain hyperon–scalar-meson coupling choices.
- The observational lower bound on maximum mass is 1.97 solar masses (from pulsar observations).
- At finite temperature (T = 50 MeV), the lepton fraction is varied in the range 0.1 ≤ Y_L ≤ 0.4.
- Hyperon–scalar-meson coupling ratios x_{Hσ} = g_{Hσ}/g_{Nσ} are varied in the ranges: 0.26 ≤ x_{Σσ} ≤ 0.66 (with x_{Λσ} = 0.58) and 0.26 ≤ x_{Λσ} ≤ 0.66 (with x_{Σσ} = 0.448).
- The stiffest hypernuclear EoS uses x_{Σσ} = 0.448 and x_{Λσ} = 0.52.
- A mass–radius constraint from observations gives M = 1.76 solar masses and R ≥ 12.5 km at the 2σ level.

## Context
This work addresses the "hyperonization puzzle", the tension between the softening of the equation of state caused by hyperon appearance and the observed two-solar-mass pulsars (Demorest et al., 2010; Antoniadis et al., 2013). It extends prior relativistic density functional studies (Glendenning, 1982; Schaffner-Bielich, 2007) by systematically varying hyperon–scalar-meson couplings within octet model constraints, showing that weaker hyperon–scalar couplings than those from the SU(6) quark model can reconcile hyperonic stars with observations. The finite-temperature results with trapped neutrinos are relevant for proto-neutron star evolution and enable future comparisons with cooling and deleptonization simulations.
