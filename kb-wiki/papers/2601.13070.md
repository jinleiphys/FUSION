# Lattice-QCD validation of hadron mass and trace-anomaly decomposition sum rules

> We present the first lattice-QCD validation of multiple sum rules associated with quark-gluon decomposition of hadron mass by computing all components from first principles. We achieve this through nonperturbative renormalization of the QCD energy-momentum tensor, including its trace, in a gradient-flow scheme, followed by continuum extrapolations, two-loop matching to the $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$ scheme, and zero-flow-time extrapolations. These ingredients enable a direct and simultaneous verification, in a common renormalization scheme and scale, of multiple energy-density-based and trace-based mass decomposition sum rules proposed in the literature. We demonstrate the framework for the $η_c$ and $J/ψ$ charmonia using three fine lattice spacings with a physical strange-quark and near-physical up- and down-quark masses. We present the first lattice-QCD results for the gravitational form factor $\bar{C}$. We find sizable gluonic contributions to charmonia masses at the hadronic scale, $\sim 15\%$ in the Lorcé and Metz-Pasquini-Rodini decompositions. The trace-anomaly contribution in the Ji sum rule is $\sim 6\%$, while the gluonic component of the trace anomaly in the Hatta-Rajan-Tanaka sum rule is $\sim 35\%$. The method is general and can be straightforwardly adopted for lattice-QCD calculations of mass and spin decompositions as well as gravitational form factors of other hadrons and nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first lattice-QCD validation of multiple hadron mass decomposition sum rules by computing all constituent components from first principles, but the validation is demonstrated only for the charmonia states eta_c and J/psi, and the results depend on the specific renormalization scheme (MSbar at 2 GeV) and the gradient-flow procedure used.

## Method

The authors computed the QCD energy-momentum tensor (EMT) components nonperturbatively using lattice QCD with three 2+1-flavor HISQ gauge ensembles at lattice spacings a = 0.06, 0.05, and 0.04 fm. They employed gradient-flow renormalization to control operator mixing and power-divergent subtractions, followed by continuum extrapolations, two-loop perturbative matching to the MSbar scheme at mu = 2 GeV, and zero-flow-time extrapolations. Hadronic matrix elements for eta_c and J/psi were extracted from two-point and three-point correlation functions using Gaussian-smeared sources and sinks, with excited-state effects controlled through two-exponential and summation fits.

## Key numbers

- Gluonic contributions to charmonia masses at the hadronic scale: approximately 15% in the Lorce and Metz-Pasquini-Rodini decompositions
- Trace-anomaly contribution in the Ji sum rule: approximately 6%
- Gluonic component of the trace anomaly in the Hatta-Rajan-Tanaka sum rule: approximately 35%
- Charm quark contribution to eta_c mass: M_c^HRT = 1918 (+53/-54) MeV, M_c^Lor = 2536 (+25/-48) MeV, M_c^Ji + M_m^Ji = 2542 (+26/-40) MeV
- Gluon contribution to eta_c mass: M_g^HRT = 953 (+168/-167) MeV, M_g^Lor = 426 (+111/-100) MeV, M_g^Ji = 188 (+102/-91) MeV
- Trace-anomaly contribution to eta_c mass: M_a^Ji = 231 (+45/-44) MeV
- Charm quark contribution to J/psi mass: M_c^HRT = 1776 (+51/-117) MeV, M_c^Lor = 2617 (+35/-110) MeV, M_c^Ji + M_m^Ji = 2614 (+40/-78) MeV
- Gluon contribution to J/psi mass: M_g^HRT = 1144 (+134/-129) MeV, M_g^Lor = 455 (+82/-85) MeV, M_g^Ji = 179 (+78/-82) MeV
- Trace-anomaly contribution to J/psi mass: M_a^Ji = 277 (+43/-45) MeV
- Gravitational form factor C(0) for eta_c: total = -0.010(18) at mu = 2 GeV in MSbar
- Gravitational form factor C(0) for J/psi: total = 0.030(30) at mu = 2 GeV in MSbar
- Longitudinal momentum sum rule A_c(0) + A_g(0) = 1.000(46) for eta_c and 0.997(43) for J/psi

## Context

This work extends previous lattice-QCD studies that were restricted to selected traceless components of the EMT (Hackett 2023, Pefkou 2021, Alexandrou 2019) by providing the first direct computation of all components including the trace, enabling validation of multiple decomposition schemes proposed by Ji (1994, 1995), Lorce (2017), Metz-Pasquini-Rodini (2020), and Hatta-Rajan-Tanaka (2018). The methodology enables future first-principles calculations of mass and spin decompositions as well as gravitational form factors for other hadrons and nuclei, providing complementary nonperturbative input to experimental programs at Jefferson Lab and the future Electron-Ion Collider.
