# Thermodynamics of an exactly solvable confining quark model

> The grand partition function of a model of confined quarks is exactly calculated at arbitrary temperatures and quark chemical potentials. The model is inspired by a softly BRST-broken version of QCD and possesses a quark mass function compatible with nonperturbative analyses of lattice simulations and Dyson-Schwinger equations. Even though the model is defined at tree level, we show that it produces a nontrivial and stable thermodynamic behaviour at any temperature or chemical potential. Results for the pressure, the entropy and the trace anomaly as a function of the temperature are qualitatively compatible with the effect of nonperturbative interactions as observed in lattice simulations. The finite density thermodynamics is also shown to contain nontrivial features, being far away from an ideal gas picture.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a tree-level quark model with softly broken BRST symmetry produces nontrivial and stable thermodynamic behavior at any temperature or chemical potential, qualitatively compatible with nonperturbative lattice QCD results, despite being defined at tree level and lacking explicit interactions beyond the nonlocal mass term.

## Method

The authors start from a QCD-inspired action with soft BRST breaking in the quark sector, integrate out auxiliary fields to obtain a nonlocal quark action with a momentum-dependent mass function, and then compute the exact grand canonical partition function using the Matsubara imaginary-time formalism. The partition function is separated into a zero-chemical-potential part (expressed as a sum of four terms each formally identical to the local free fermion case) and a finite-chemical-potential part (evaluated numerically or analytically in the zero-temperature limit). Thermodynamic quantities such as pressure, entropy density, energy density, and trace anomaly are then derived from the partition function.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum mass function parameters fitted to lattice data from Parappilly (2005) are: M3 = 0.196 GeV^3, m^2 = 0.639 GeV^2, and m0 = 0.014 GeV, with chi^2/dof = 1.18.
- The trace anomaly shows a peak around T approximately 0.15 GeV at zero chemical potential.
- The bag model comparison uses a bag constant B = (0.145 GeV)^4.
- The pressure is normalized by the Stefan-Boltzmann limit for free massless fermions: P_SB(T,mu) = N_c N_f [7 pi^2 T^4/180 + mu^2 T^2/6 + mu^4/(12 pi^2)].

## Context

This work extends the soft BRST-breaking approach previously applied to pure gauge systems (Zwanziger 2004, Fukushima 2013) into the quark sector, building on the renormalizable quark model with BRST breaking introduced by Baulieu (2009) and Dudal (2013). The model's propagator was shown to fit lattice data from Parappilly (2005) and Dyson-Schwinger analyses by Bhagwat (2003). The paper contrasts with the nonlocal Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (General 2000, Benic 2012), which exhibits unstable thermodynamic behavior, and with the Polyakov-loop extended NJL and Quark Meson models (Fukushima 2003, Ratti 2005), where quarks remain asymptotic states rather than confined ones. The results enable further study of the relation between confinement and chiral symmetry breaking through the lens of BRST symmetry breaking.
