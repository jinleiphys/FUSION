# g_{πΛΣ} and g_{KΣΞ} from QCD sum rules

## Key claim

The coupling constants g_{πΛΣ} and g_{KΣΞ} are calculated using QCD sum rules with the three-point function method, finding that SU(3) symmetry breaking effects in the leading-order operator product expansion (OPE) side are less than 5% for both couplings. However, the pattern of SU(3) breaking appears different from that predicted by SU(3) relations, and the results are sensitive to the inclusion of continuum model corrections, which can increase the couplings by up to nearly 50%.

## Method

The author uses the three-point correlation function method in QCD sum rules, constructing interpolating fields for the octet baryons (Λ, Σ, Ξ) and pseudoscalar meson currents (π⁰, K⁻). The sum rules are derived after Borel transformation, with SU(3) breaking effects included via the strange quark mass m_s = 150 MeV and the ratio of quark condensates γ = ⟨s̄s⟩/⟨q̄q⟩ - 1 ≈ -0.2. The coupling constants are evaluated at the Borel mass M = (M_Λ + M_Σ)/2 for g_{πΛΣ} and similarly for g_{KΣΞ}, with the pole-continuum transition terms neglected in this exploratory work.

## Key numbers

- g_{πΛΣ} = 7.53 (without continuum model) for ⟨q̄q⟩ = -(0.230 GeV)³, f_π = 133 MeV, m_s = 150 MeV
- g_{πΛΣ} = 10.79 (with continuum model, s₀ = 2.756 GeV²) for the same input parameters
- g_{KΣΞ} = -7.02 (without continuum model) for ⟨q̄q⟩ = -(0.230 GeV)³, f_K = 160 MeV, m_s = 150 MeV
- g_{KΣΞ} = -10.22 (with continuum model, s₀ = 2.856 GeV²) for the same input parameters
- Variation of g_{πΛΣ} is within 0.3% when ⟨s̄s⟩ = 0.6 ⟨q̄q⟩, and less than 0.8% for m_s = 180 MeV
- Variation of g_{KΣΞ} is within 0.5% when ⟨s̄s⟩ = 0.6 ⟨q̄q⟩, but changes to -8.55 for m_s = 180 MeV
- For ⟨q̄q⟩ = -(0.250 GeV)³, g_{πΛΣ} = 7.13 and g_{KΣΞ} = -8.75

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule calculations of Choe, Cheoun, and Lee (1996) for g_{KNΛ} and g_{KNΣ} to the hyperon couplings g_{πΛΣ} and g_{KΣΞ}, using the same three-point function methodology. The results are compared to SU(3) symmetry predictions using de Swart's convention (1963) with α_D = 0.64 from Ratcliffe (1996-1997) and g_{πNN} = 13.43 from Ericson et al. (1996), as well as experimental fits from Timmermans, Rijken, and de Swart (1995) and Nagels et al. (1979)/Dumbrajs et al. (1983). The author notes that the omission of continuum model contributions, as done in previous calculations, appears too crude, and that full quantitative analysis along the lines of Leinweber (1997) would require inclusion of higher-order power corrections and pole-continuum transition terms.
