# Heavy Holographic Exotics: Tetraquarks as Efimov States

> We provide a holographic description of non-strange multiquark exotics as compact topological molecules by binding heavy-light mesons to a tunneling configuration in D8-D$\bar 8$ that is homotopic to the vacuum state with fixed Chern-Simons number. In the tunneling process, the heavy-light mesons transmute to fermions. Their binding is generic and arises from a trade-off between the dipole attraction induced by the Chern-Simons term and the U(1) fermionic repulsion. In the heavy quark limit, the open-flavor tetraquark exotics $QQ\bar q\bar q$ and $\bar Q\bar Q qq$, emerge as bound Efimov states in a degenerate multiplet $IJ^π=(00^+ , 01^+)$ with opposite intrinsic Chern-Simons numbers $\pm \frac 12$. The hidden-flavor tetraquark exotics such as $Q\bar Q q\bar q$, $QQ\bar Q\bar q$ and $QQ\bar Q\bar Q$ as compact topological molecules are unbound. Other exotics are also discussed.

## Key claim

In the heavy quark limit, open-flavor tetraquark exotics $QQ\bar q\bar q$ and $\bar Q\bar Q qq$ emerge as bound Efimov states in a degenerate multiplet $IJ^\pi=(00^+ , 01^+)$ with opposite intrinsic Chern-Simons numbers $\pm \frac 12$, but only when the tunneling configuration carries fractional Chern-Simons number (not unit topological charge); hidden-flavor tetraquarks such as $Q\bar Q q\bar q$, $QQ\bar Q\bar q$ and $QQ\bar Q\bar Q$ are unbound as compact topological molecules.

## Method

The authors construct a holographic description using the D4-D8-D$\bar 8$ setup with one heavy probe brane, where heavy-light mesons are attached to an O(4) symmetric tunneling gauge configuration (a sphaleron-like solution on the instanton-anti-instanton valley) that carries fixed Chern-Simons number and is homotopic to the vacuum. They solve the Dirac-Born-Infeld and Chern-Simons actions in the double limit of large $N_c$ and strong coupling $\lambda$, then derive the effective Hamiltonian on the moduli space of the topological molecule formed by heavy mesons bound to the tunneling configuration, treating the heavy meson fields as fermionic zero modes after transmutation.

## Key numbers

* The sphaleron energy at the turning point is $E_0 = 3\pi^2/g_\kappa^2$ for $k=0$, while the instanton energy is $E_1=0$ for $k=1$; the holographic mass ratio at the sphaleron to instanton point is $M_0/M_1 = 3\pi/(8\sqrt{2}) < 1$.
* The Chern-Simons number at the turning point satisfies $N_k(1-N_k) = \frac14 (E_k/E_0)^{1/2}$, with $N_1=1$ (instanton topological charge) and $N_0=1/2$ (sphaleron Chern-Simons number).
* The coefficient $\alpha_0(k)$ is maximally repulsive at $k=0$ and vanishes at the self-dual point $k=1$; $\alpha_1(k)$ is attractive for all $k$ with limiting value $\alpha_1(1) = -1/8$; $\alpha_2(k)$ is repulsive throughout with limiting value $\alpha_2(1) = 1/3$.

## Context

This work extends the holographic heavy-light framework developed by Liu, Zahed and collaborators (2018) for heavy baryons as instanton-heavy meson bound states, and applies it to multiquark exotics using tunneling configurations rather than stable instantons. The approach provides a geometrical realization of the Skyrme model approach to pentaquarks (Marek, 2003) and the bound state approach of Skyrme with heavy mesons (Pentarho, 2004), without the shortcomings of derivative expansion. The results enable a first-principles holographic treatment of compact tetraquarks as Efimov-like states, contrasting with the hadronic molecule interpretation of hidden-flavor exotics such as $X(3872)$ and $Z_c(3900)^\pm$ reported by BELLE, BESIII, and LHCb.
