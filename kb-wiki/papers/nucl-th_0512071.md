# On the estimate of the sigma^(I = 1)_(KN)(0)-term value from the energy level shift of kaonic hydrogen in the ground state

> Using the experimental data on the energy level shift of kaonic hydrogen in the ground state (the DEAR Collaboration, Phys. Rev. Lett. 94, 212302 (2005)) and the theoretical value of the energy level shift, calculated within the phenomenological quantum field theoretic approach to the description of strong low-energy anti-K N and anti-K NN interactions developed at Stefan Meyer Institut fuer subatomare Physik in Vienna, we estimate the value of the sigma^(I = 1)_(KN)(0)-term of low-energy anti-K N scattering. We get sigma^(I = 1)_(KN)(0) = (433 +/- 85) MeV. This testifies the absence of strange quarks in the proton structure.

## Key claim

Using the DEAR Collaboration's experimental data on the kaonic hydrogen ground-state energy level shift and the authors' own theoretical calculation of that shift, the value of the sigma^(I=1)_(KN)(0)-term is estimated as (433 ± 85) MeV, which is about a factor of 2 larger than the chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) estimate of (200 ± 50) MeV obtained with dimensional regularization.

## Method

The authors used the Deser-Goldberger-Baumann-Thirring (DGBTT) formula relating the kaonic hydrogen energy level shift to the S-wave K^-p scattering amplitude at threshold. They calculated the real part of this amplitude up to next-to-leading order in chiral expansion, including contributions from the sigma-term and a matrix element of time-ordered axial currents, evaluated in the chiral and heavy-baryon limits using current algebra and PCAC. The sigma-term was then extracted by equating the theoretical energy level shift expression to the experimental value from the DEAR Collaboration.

## Key numbers

- Experimental energy level shift of kaonic hydrogen ground state: (-193 ± 37) + i(125 ± 56) eV (DEAR Collaboration, 2005)
- Leading-order contribution to the energy level shift from the S-wave scattering length: epsilon^(0)_(1s) = (238 ± 21) eV
- Contribution from the axial-current matrix element to the energy level shift: delta epsilon_(1s) = -260 eV
- Extracted sigma^(I=1)_(KN)(0)-term: (433 ± 85) MeV
- S-wave scattering length of elastic K^-p scattering at leading order: a^(K^-p)_0 = (-0.54 ± 0.05) fm
- Reduced mass of K^-p pair: mu = 323.48 MeV (using m_K = 493.68 MeV, m_p = 938.27 MeV)
- PCAC constant: F_pi = 92.4 MeV
- Squared slope parameter of proton electromagnetic form factor: M^2_V = 0.71 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous phenomenological quantum field theoretic model for low-energy anti-K N and anti-K NN interactions (Ivanov et al., 2003-2005) by applying it to extract the sigma-term from the DEAR Collaboration data (2005). The result (433 ± 85) MeV is about twice the ChPT estimate of (200 ± 50) MeV obtained with dimensional regularization (Borasoy, 1993), but agrees qualitatively with ChPT using cut-off regularization. The extracted sigma-term, combined with the sigma_(pi N)(0)-term of (61+2/-4) MeV from pionic hydrogen data (PSI Collaboration), yields a strange-quark content parameter y = -0.21 ± 0.23, consistent with the absence of strange quarks in the proton structure. The SIDDHARTA Collaboration's planned experiments with improved precision are expected to reduce the experimental uncertainty.
