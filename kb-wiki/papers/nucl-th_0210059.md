# Astrophysical reaction rate for $α(αn,γ)$$^{9}$Be by photodisintegration

> We study the astrophysical reaction rate for the formation of $^{9}$Be through the three body reaction $α(αn,γ)$. This reaction is one of the key reactions which could bridge the mass gap at A = 8 nuclear systems to produce intermediate-to-heavy mass elements in alpha- and neutron-rich environments such as r-process nucleosynthesis in supernova explosions, s-process nucleosynthesis in asymptotic giant branch (AGB) stars, and primordial nucleosynthesis in baryon inhomogeneous cosmological models. To calculate the thermonuclear reaction rate in a wide range of temperatures, we numerically integrate the thermal average of cross sections assuming a two-steps formation through a metastable $^{8}$Be. Off-resonant and on-resonant contributions from the ground state in $^{8}$Be are taken into account. As input cross section, we adopt the latest experimental data by photodisintegration of $^{9}$Be with laser-electron photon beams, which covers all relevant resonances in $^{9}$Be. We provide the reaction rate for $α(αn,γ)^{9}$Be in the temperature range from T$_{9}$=10$^{-3}$ to T$_{9}$=10$^{1}$ both in the tabular form and in the analytical form. The calculated reaction rate is compared with the reaction rates of the CF88 and the NACRE compilations. The CF88 rate is valid at $T_{9} > 0.028$ due to lack of the off-resonant contribution. The CF88 rate differs from the present rate by a factor of two in a temperature range $T_{9} \geq 0.1$. The NACRE rate, which adopted different sources of experimental information on resonance states in $^{9}$Be, is 4--12 times larger than the present rate at $T_{9} \leq 0.028$, but is consistent with the present rate to within $\pm 20 %$ at $T_{9} \geq 0.1$.

## Key claim

The paper provides a new calculation of the astrophysical reaction rate for the three-body reaction α(αn,γ)⁹Be based on photodisintegration data, covering a wider temperature range (T₉ = 10⁻³ to 10¹) than previous work, and including both off-resonant and on-resonant contributions from the ground state in ⁸Be; the authors note that the contribution through the ⁵He + α channel is not included due to insufficient experimental information, so the rate may be incomplete at high temperatures above T₉ ≈ 4.

## Method

The authors numerically integrated the thermal average of cross sections assuming a two-step formation through metastable ⁸Be (α + α ⇌ ⁸Be(n,γ)⁹Be), using the formulation of Nomoto (1985) for the triple-alpha reaction. They adopted photoneutron cross sections for ⁹Be measured with laser-electron photon beams (LEPBs) at energies from 1.69 to 6.11 MeV, added data points near the neutron threshold with γ-ray flux corrections, performed a new least-squares analysis to extract Breit-Wigner resonance parameters for the 1/2⁺, 5/2⁺, and 5/2⁻ states, and converted the photoneutron cross sections into neutron capture cross sections of ⁸Be via the detailed-balance theorem.

## Key numbers

- The reaction rate is provided for temperatures from T₉ = 10⁻³ to T₉ = 10¹ (T₉ is temperature in units of 10⁹ K).
- The CF88 rate differs from the present rate by a factor of two at T₉ ≥ 0.1.
- The NACRE rate is 4–12 times larger than the present rate at T₉ ≤ 0.028.
- The NACRE rate is consistent with the present rate to within ±20% at T₉ ≥ 0.1.
- The CF88 rate is valid only at T₉ > 0.028 due to lack of the off-resonant contribution.
- The 5/2⁻ state at Eₓ = 2.4294 MeV ± 1.3 keV has a total width Γ = 0.77 keV ± 0.15 keV; a 7% branching ratio to the n + ⁸Be decay channel was adopted.
- The B(E1↓) for the 1/2⁺ state from photodisintegration is 0.106⁺⁰·⁰¹⁹₋₀·₀₁₆ e²fm², a factor of two larger than electron scattering results (0.050 ± 0.020 and 0.054 ± 0.004 e²fm²).
- The B(E1↓) for the 5/2⁺ state from photodisintegration is about 4 times larger than the electron scattering value (0.010 ± 0.008 e²fm²).
- The B(M1↓) for the 5/2⁻ state from photodisintegration is about half the electron scattering value (0.536 ± 0.060).

## Context

This work extends the brief report of Utsunomiya et al. (2001) by adding data near the neutron threshold, including off-resonant contributions, and providing tabulated and analytical reaction rates over a wider temperature range. It updates the reaction rates from the CF88 compilation (Caughlan and Fowler, 1988) and the NACRE compilation (Angulo et al., 1999), which used different experimental sources for resonance states in ⁹Be. The new rate enables more accurate nucleosynthesis calculations for r-process in supernova neutrino-driven winds, s-process in AGB stars, and primordial nucleosynthesis in inhomogeneous cosmological models, though the authors note that future experiments on the ⁵He + α decay channel (e.g., β-delayed particle decay of ⁹Li) are needed to evaluate the rate at high temperatures above T₉ ≈ 4.
