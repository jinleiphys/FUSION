# Simulating dense QCD matter with ultracold atomic boson-fermion mixtures

> We delineate, as an analog of two-flavor dense quark matter, the phase structure of a many-body mixture of atomic bosons and fermions in two internal states with a tunable boson-fermion attraction. The bosons b correspond to diquarks, and the fermions f to unpaired quarks. For weak b-f attraction, the system is a mixture of a Bose-Einstein condensate and degenerate fermions, while for strong attraction composite b-f fermions N, analogs of the nucleon, are formed, which are superfluid due to the N-N attraction in the spin-singlet channel. We determine the symmetry breaking patterns at finite temperature as a function of the b-f coupling strength, and relate the phase diagram to that of dense QCD.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a mixture of ultracold atomic bosons and fermions with a tunable attraction can serve as an analog for the transition from color-superconducting two-flavor quark matter to superfluid hadronic matter in dense QCD, but notes that the analogy is incomplete because the gluonic attraction in QCD depends on baryon density (preventing tuning of coupling at fixed density) and chiral symmetry breaking plays an important role in the QCD transition.

## Method

The authors analyze a nonrelativistic Hamiltonian for a mixture of single-component bosons (b) and two-component fermions (f) with equal populations, using a tunable attractive boson-fermion interaction. They compute the phase structure at weak coupling via induced interactions (fermion-density fluctuations mediating boson-boson attraction, and boson-density fluctuations mediating fermion-fermion attraction) and at strong coupling via formation of composite boson-fermion fermions (N) whose effective N-N scattering length is estimated from a T-matrix diagram. The symmetry breaking patterns are classified by the continuous U(1) symmetries of the Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- For a Rb-K mixture with mass ratio mb/mf = 2, the induced boson-boson scattering length is k_F a_bfb ≈ -11 η^{-2}, where η ≡ -1/(n^{1/3} a_bf) and k_F = (3π^2 n)^{1/3}.
- For mb/mf = 2 and n^{1/3} a_bb = 10^{-2}, the critical η for mechanical instability of the Bose gas is η_c^{b-BEC} ≈ 21 at T=0 and η_c^{b-BEC} ≈ 2.2 near T_c(b-BEC).
- For mb/mf = 2, the induced fermion-fermion scattering length is k_F a_fbf ≈ -4.6 ln(1+x^2) η^{-2}, with x = sqrt(3π/4 k_F a_bb).
- For mb/mf = 2, fermionic BCS superfluidity emerges when η < η_c^{f-BCS} = 2.1 [ln(1+x^2)/(k_F a_ff)]^{1/2}.
- For mb/mf = 2, the composite fermion scattering length is k_F a_N ≈ -7.0 Γ/|η|, where 0.76 < Γ(a_bf/r_0) < 1 for 1 < a_bf/r_0 < ∞.

## Context

This work extends the analogy between ultracold atomic systems and dense QCD previously explored in BEC-BCS crossover studies (Regal, Baym 2008) and phenomenological diquark models (diquarks). It specifically models the transition from two-flavor color superconductivity (2SC) at high density to superfluid nuclear matter at low density, building on earlier work by Hatsuda (2006) and Baym (2008). The results suggest that the phase boundary between b-BEC and N-BCS phases in cold atoms is analogous to the boundary between color-superconducting quark matter and superfluid hadronic matter, and the analysis enables future experimental studies of dense QCD phenomena using ultracold atomic mixtures such as Rb-K.
