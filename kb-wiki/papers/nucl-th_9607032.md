# Competition Between Particle-Hole and Particle-Particle Correlations in Forbidden Electron Capture: the Case of $^{123}$Te

> The K-electron capture half-life of $^{123}$Te has been recently measured to be $t^K_{exp}=2.4\times 10^{19}$ yr, and constitutes the longest half-life ever measured in a single $β$-transition of any nuclear species. We have calculated this second unique forbidden transition within the framework of the proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation, making use of Skyrme-type effective interactions. A strong cancellation effect between particle-hole and particle-particle correlations is found. The model, without any renormalization of the force, provides a lower limit for the K-electron capture half-life of $\sim 10^{17}$ yr, which unambiguously rules out the old experimental values of $10^{13} - 10^{14}$ yr. A few percent increase of the particle-particle matrix elements of the Skyrme interaction allows to reproduce the experimental findings.

## Key claim
The K-electron capture half-life of $^{123}$Te, measured as $2.4 \times 10^{19}$ yr, can be reproduced within the proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation using Skyrme-type interactions only after introducing a 6% renormalization of the particle-particle matrix elements; without this renormalization the model provides a lower limit of approximately $10^{17}$ yr, which rules out older experimental values of $10^{13} - 10^{14}$ yr but still falls two orders of magnitude short of the measured value.

## Method
The authors calculated the second unique forbidden electron capture transition from $^{123}$Te (ground state, $J^\pi = 1/2^+$) to $^{123}$Sb (ground state, $J^\pi = 7/2^+$) by reducing the problem to the transition between the even-even core $^{122}$Te ($0^+$) and the lowest $3^+_1$ state in $^{122}$Sb, factoring out the spectator neutron configuration. They employed the proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) with a Woods-Saxon mean field, BCS pairing correlations, and the SGII Skyrme interaction, introducing a multiplicative parameter $G_{pp}$ to renormalize the particle-particle matrix elements obtained via the Pandya transformation. Experimental data from one-nucleon transfer reactions were used to constrain quasi-particle energies and occupation factors, and the $3^+$ strength was averaged over states within 0.2 MeV of excitation energy and multiplied by the spectroscopic factors 0.78 and 0.16.

## Key numbers
- Experimental K-electron capture half-life of $^{123}$Te: $t^K_{\rm exp} = 2.4 \times 10^{19}$ yr, the longest half-life ever measured in a single beta transition of any nuclear species.
- Lower limit for the K-electron capture half-life from the model without renormalization ($G_{pp}=1$): $t^K_{1/2} = 1.3 \times 10^{17}$ yr.
- Old experimental values ruled out by the calculation: $10^{13} - 10^{14}$ yr.
- Renormalization factor required to reproduce the experimental half-life: $G_{pp} = 1.06$ (a 6% increase of particle-particle matrix elements).
- Empirical nuclear matrix element extracted from the experimental half-life: $|\langle f | \hat{T}^+_{3,2,1} | i \rangle|^2 = 1.8 \times 10^{-3}$ fm$^4$.
- Q-value of the reaction: $Q_V = 0.0513$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the study of cancellation effects between particle-hole and particle-particle correlations, previously investigated in other nuclear channels (Mot, Bes, Bro), to the proton-neutron channel in a forbidden electron capture transition. The calculation uses the pn-QRPA framework with Skyrme interactions (SGII parameters from VauBei) and incorporates experimental data from one-nucleon transfer experiments (078, 016) to constrain the nuclear structure. The results enable future studies of similar forbidden transitions and provide a benchmark for testing effective interactions in the particle-particle channel, while highlighting the need for improved understanding of the nuclear interaction beyond the Pandya transformation.
