# Pygmy dipole response of proton rich argon beyond the random phase approximation

> The occurrence of a pygmy dipole resonance in proton rich Ar-32 and Ar-34 is studied using the unitary correlator operator method interaction Vucom, based on Argonne V18. Predictions from the random phase approximation (RPA) and the shell model in a no-core basis are compared. It is found that the inclusion of configuration mixing up to two-particle--two-holes broadens the pygmy strength slightly and reduces sensibly its strength, as compared to the RPA predictions. For Ar-32 a clear peak associated with a pygmy resonance is found. For Ar-34, the pygmy states are obtained close to the giant dipole resonance and mix with it.

## Key claim

The paper finds that including configuration mixing up to two-particle–two-hole (2p2h) states, beyond the random phase approximation (RPA), broadens the pygmy dipole strength slightly and reduces its strength sensibly; for Ar-32 a clear pygmy peak is found, while for Ar-34 the pygmy states lie close to the giant dipole resonance and mix with it.

## Method

The authors employed the unitary correlator operator method interaction V_UCOM, based on the Argonne V18 potential, to perform consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) plus RPA calculations and no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for Ar-32 and Ar-34. The RPA equations were solved in a harmonic oscillator basis truncated to up to 24 major shells, while the shell model diagonalizations used a truncated HF basis of the six lowest shells and included up to 2p2h configurations. The isovector dipole operator corrected for center-of-mass displacement was applied, and the resulting B(E1) strength was folded with a Lorentzian of width 1 MeV.

## Key numbers

* For Ar-32 at the RPA level (N_max >= 15), the total isovector dipole strength within the first 40 MeV of excitation energy is 8.15 ± 0.10 e²fm², with a centroid of 22.15 ± 0.15 MeV.
* For Ar-32, the RPA predicts a pygmy strength of about 1.0 e²fm² below 12 MeV; including 2p2h shell model correlations reduces this to 0.49 e²fm².
* For Ar-34 at the RPA level (N_max = 15), the total dipole strength in the interval 0–40 MeV is 8.69 e²fm², corresponding to 161% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) sum rule, with a centroid of 23.3 MeV.
* For Ar-34, the pygmy strength integrated up to 14 MeV is 0.80 e²fm² in RPA; including 2p2h shell model correlations reduces the low-energy strength to 0.62 e²fm².
* The RPA calculation for Ar-32 exhausts about 153% of the TRK sum rule; the 2p2h shell model calculation reduces this to 137%.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) in neutron-rich nuclei to the proton-rich side, specifically Ar-32 and Ar-34. It builds on RQRPA calculations by Paar et al. (2005) that suggested a proton PDR in medium-mass nuclei, and on studies of particle-vibration coupling beyond RPA by Litvinova et al. (2007) for neutron-rich isotopes. The comparison of RPA and shell model predictions using the same V_UCOM interaction enables an assessment of correlation effects beyond RPA, and the results can be tested by planned experiments aimed at observing low-energy dipole response in proton-rich argon isotopes (Boretzky et al., 2006).
