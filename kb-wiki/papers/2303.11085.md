# Investigation of spectroscopic factors of deeply-bound nucleons in drip-line nuclei with the Gamow shell model

> Spectroscopic factors involving well bound nucleons in light nuclei are calculated with standard shell model, no-core shell model and Gamow shell model. Continuum coupling is included exactly in the Gamow shell model, due to the use of the Berggren basis, which contains bound, resonance and scattering states. Conversely, it is absent from standard and no-core shell models, where a basis of harmonic oscillator states is used. As the A - 1 nuclei for which spectroscopic factors are calculated are either weakly bound or unbound, coupling to continuum is prominent, even though the A nuclei are well bound. It is then showed that Gamow shell model can properly reproduce experimental data and is a predictive tool for detailed nuclear structure at drip-line, contrary to standard and no-core shell model.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that continuum coupling, rather than short-range correlations or the specific choice of nucleon-nucleon interaction, is the dominant mechanism responsible for the systematic overestimation of spectroscopic factors in theoretical calculations, provided that the parent nuclei involved are weakly bound or unbound.

## Method

The authors calculated spectroscopic factors and overlap functions for nucleon addition reactions on A=6-8 nuclei (He, Li, Be, B isotopes) using three different many-body frameworks: the standard shell model (SM) with a single harmonic oscillator major shell and the Cohen-Kurath interaction, the no-core shell model (NCSM) with the Daejeon16 realistic interaction in Nhw spaces up to Nmax=10, and the Gamow shell model (GSM) using a Berggren basis with a Woods-Saxon core potential and Furutani-Horiuchi-Tamagaki residual interaction. The GSM calculations employed the cluster orbital shell model formalism to remove center-of-mass excitations and included spdf partial waves with discretized scattering continua.

## Key numbers

* The widths of the unbound parent nuclei used in the calculations: 6Be has a width of about 90 keV, 7He has a width of about 200 keV (calculated) versus 150 keV (experimental), and 7B has a width of about 770 keV (calculated) versus 800 keV (experimental)
* The typical error in reproducing experimental spectra with both NCSM and GSM is about 500 keV to 1 MeV
* NCSM calculations were performed in Nhw spaces with Nmax running from 0 to 10, with hw = 15 MeV
* The GSM valence space used 30 discretized scattering points for each partial wave in the Berggren basis, with six harmonic oscillator states (n=0,...,5) for the d3/2 and f partial waves

## Context

This work extends the investigation of spectroscopic factor quenching in drip-line nuclei, previously studied with coupled-cluster calculations by PhysRevLett.107.032501, by directly comparing standard shell model, no-core shell model, and Gamow shell model approaches. The results show that continuum coupling, not short-range correlations as often assumed (POLLS 1995), is the primary cause of spectroscopic factor reduction. The paper enables future work using the newly developed ab-initio Gamow shell model with a core (Hu 2020, Zhang 2022) to combine realistic interactions with proper continuum treatment for heavier systems.
