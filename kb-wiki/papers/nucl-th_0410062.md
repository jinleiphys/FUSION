# Chiral SU(3) dynamics and $Λ$-hyperons in the nuclear medium

> We present a novel approach to the density dependent mean field and the spin-orbit interaction of a $Λ$-hyperon in a nuclear many-body system, based on flavor-SU(3) in-medium chiral perturbation theory. The leading long-range $ΛN$-interaction arises from kaon exchange and from two-pion exchange with a $Σ$-hyperon in the intermediate state. The empirical $Λ$-nucleus potential depth of about $-28 $MeV is well reproduced with a single cutoff scale, $\bar Λ= 0.7 $GeV, effectively representing all short-distance (high-momentum) dynamics not resolved at scales characteristic of the nuclear Fermi momentum. This value of $\barΛ$ is remarkably consistent with the one required to reproduce the empirical saturation point of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter in the same framework. The smallness of the $Λ$-nuclear spin-orbit interaction finds a natural (yet novel) explanation in terms of an almost complete cancellation between short-range contributions (properly rescaled from the known nucleonic spin-orbit coupling strength) and long-range terms generated by iterated one-pion exchange with intermediate $Σ$-hyperons. The small $ΣΛ$-mass difference figures prominently in this context.

# Chiral SU(3) dynamics and Λ-hyperons in the nuclear medium

## Key claim
The empirical Λ-nucleus potential depth of about -28 MeV is reproduced using a single cutoff scale of 0.7 GeV, consistent with that required for nuclear matter saturation, but only when the cutoff is adjusted to fit the data rather than predicted from first principles. The smallness of the Λ-nuclear spin-orbit interaction is explained by an almost complete cancellation between short-range contributions (rescaled from the nucleonic spin-orbit coupling strength) and long-range terms from iterated one-pion exchange with intermediate Σ-hyperons, though the short-range contribution carries significant uncertainty.

## Method
The authors calculate the density-dependent mean field and spin-orbit interaction of a Λ-hyperon in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter using flavor-SU(3) in-medium chiral perturbation theory at leading order. Long-range contributions come from one-kaon exchange (Fock diagram) and two-pion exchange with Σ-hyperons in the intermediate state (Hartree diagrams and Pauli-blocking corrections), with the ΣΛ-mass difference of 77.5 MeV treated as a small scale in the energy denominators. Short-distance dynamics not resolved at nuclear Fermi momentum scales are absorbed into a single momentum cutoff parameter Λ̅ = 0.7 GeV, which effectively represents a contact interaction.

## Key numbers
- Λ-nucleus potential depth at saturation density: U_Λ(k_f0) = -28.15 MeV, decomposed as +4.17 MeV (kaon exchange) -39.77 MeV (iterated pion exchange with ΣN states) +7.46 MeV (Pauli-blocking correction)
- Cutoff scale: Λ̅ = 0.70 GeV, consistent with Λ̅ = 0.65 GeV needed for nuclear matter saturation in the same framework
- ΣΛ-mass difference: M_Σ - M_Λ = 77.5 MeV, corresponding to Δ ≈ 285 MeV in the formalism
- Nuclear matter saturation density: ρ_0 = 0.16 fm⁻³, with Fermi momentum k_f0 = 263 MeV
- Λ-nuclear spin-orbit coupling strength at saturation: U_Λls(k_f0) = (24.8 C_ls - 16.70 + 1.64) MeV·fm², where C_ls is varied between 1/2 and 2/3
- Empirical p₃/₂ - p₁/₂ spin-orbit splitting in Λ¹³C: (152 ± 65) keV, compared to about 6 MeV in ordinary p-shell nuclei
- Coupling constants: g_KNΛ = 14.25, g_πΛΣ = 12.12, g_πN = 13.2, with D = 0.84 and F = 0.46
- Pion decay constant: f_π = 92.4 MeV; average kaon mass: m_K = 496 MeV; average pion mass: m_π = 138 MeV; mean baryon mass: M_B = 1047 MeV

## Context
This work extends in-medium chiral perturbation theory from the nucleonic sector (Kaiser and Weise, earlier work on nuclear matter saturation) to hypernuclear physics, specifically addressing the long-standing puzzle of the weak Λ-nuclear spin-orbit interaction. Prior attempts using one-boson exchange ΛN potentials (Dover, Hiyama, Millener, and others) tended to overestimate the spin-orbit splitting significantly. The approach contrasts with relativistic scalar-vector mean field models that required a strong tensor coupling of the ω-meson to the Λ-hyperon (proposed by Jennings and others). The results enable a unified chiral EFT description of both nucleonic and hyperonic in-medium interactions, with the absence of Δ(1232)-isobar three-body mechanisms for the Λ (which cancel the long-range spin-orbit contribution for nucleons, as shown by Kaiser and others) providing a natural explanation for the observed small spin-orbit splittings in Λ-hypernuclei.
