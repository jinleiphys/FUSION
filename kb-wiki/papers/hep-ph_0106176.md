# Degrees of Freedom and the Deconfining Phase Transition

> There is a sharp increase in the relative number of degrees of freedom at the deconfining phase transition. Characterizing this increase using the Polyakov Loop model, we find that for a nearly second order deconfining phase transition, the medium-induced energy loss turns on rapidly above T_c, proportional to the relative number of degrees of freedom. Further, energy loss is logarithmically dependent on the screening mass, and thus is sensitive to nearly critical scattering.

# Degrees of Freedom and the Deconfining Phase Transition

## Key claim

For a nearly second order deconfining phase transition (as in QCD with three colors), the medium-induced energy loss of a fast parton turns on rapidly above T_c, proportional to the relative number of degrees of freedom n(T), and is logarithmically sensitive to the screening mass m_ℓ, which enhances energy loss near T_c by about 50% compared to estimates using a fixed screening mass.

## Method

The authors use the Polyakov Loop model as a mean field theory for the relative pressure n(T) = p(T)/p_ideal, where p_ideal is the pressure of an ideal quark-gluon plasma. They compute the critical energy scale E_cr = m_ℓ² L² / λ (where λ is the mean free path and L the medium thickness) and derive the medium-induced energy loss ΔE in both the LPM regime (E < E_cr) and the factorization regime (E > E_cr), expressing results in terms of n(T) and the Polyakov loop mass m_ℓ.

## Key numbers

- The ideal gas coefficient for QCD with three flavors is c_QGP = 47 1/2 degrees of freedom, versus c_π = 3 for pions in the hadronic phase, an increase of more than a factor of ten.
- For quenched QCD, lattice simulations find n(T) ∼ 0.8 by T ∼ 2 T_c, with a sharp bump in (e-3p)/e_ideal at ∼ 1.1 T_c.
- For three colors at T_c⁺, the Polyakov loop mass satisfies √Z_s m_ℓ/T ∼ 0.3, giving b_3 ∼ 0.45.
- At T = 2 T_c, m_ℓ ∼ 2.5 T and m_mag ∼ 6 T (static magnetic glueball mass).
- The critical energy E_cr ∼ 20 GeV × n(T) (T/T_c)³; for QCD with n(T_c) ∼ 0.1, E_cr ∼ 2 GeV near T_c, rising to ∼ 160 GeV by 2 T_c.
- For E = 25 GeV, L = 5 fm, T = T_c, the logarithm log(2E/(L m_ℓ²)) changes from ∼ 2.3 (fixed m_ℓ) to ∼ 3.5 (including n¹/⁴ scaling), an increase of about 50%.
- For quenched QCD, the energy ratio at T_c is r ≡ e(T_c⁺)/e_ideal(T_c) ∼ 1/3, giving n(T) ∼ t and ℓ_0(T) ∝ t¹/⁴ for small reduced temperature t = T/T_c - 1.

## Context

This work extends the Polyakov Loop model of Pisarski (2000) and Dumitru and Pisarski (2001) to study how the deconfining phase transition affects observable quantities like parton energy loss. It contrasts the weakly first order transition for three colors (where the Polyakov loop changes significantly between T_c and 2 T_c) with a strongly first order transition (as for four or more colors, studied by Ohta and Wingate, 1999-2001), where the loop jumps to near one at T_c. The analysis builds on the energy loss formalism of Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, and Schiff (1997) and Gyulassy, Levai, and Vitev (2001), and suggests that energy loss turns on before dilepton production (which scales as n²) as temperature increases above T_c, providing a sequential series of experimental signatures for the deconfined phase at RHIC.
