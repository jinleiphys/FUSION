# Further evidence for three-nucleon spin-orbit interaction in isotope shifts of $Z=\mathrm{magic}$ nuclei

> It was pointed out [Phys. Rev. C \textbf{91}, 021302(R)] that the isotope shifts of the Pb nuclei, the kink at $N=126$ in particular, can be well described by the Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations if a density-dependent LS interaction derived from the $3N$ interaction is incorporated. Effects of the density-dependence in the LS channel on the isotope shifts are extensively investigated for the Ca, Ni and Sn isotopes, using the semi-realistic M3Y-P6 interaction and its LS modified variant M3Y-P6a, as in the Pb case. It is found that almost equal charge radii between $^{40}$Ca and $^{48}$Ca are reproduced, as well as the isotope shifts in a long chain of the Sn nuclei, owing to the density-dependence in the LS channel. A kink is predicted at $N=82$ for the isotope shifts of the Sn nuclei, in clear contrast to the interactions without the density-dependence.

# Further evidence for three-nucleon spin-orbit interaction in isotope shifts of Z=magic nuclei

## Key claim

The density-dependent LS interaction derived from the three-nucleon (3N) interaction, when incorporated into Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations, reproduces the nearly equal charge radii of 40Ca and 48Ca and describes isotope shifts across long chains of Sn nuclei, with a predicted kink at N=82 for Sn, but the chiral effective-field theory results have not been completely followed in quantitative respects because the theory is not yet convergent at densities near or above saturation density.

## Method

Spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations were performed for Ca, Ni, and Sn isotopes using the semi-realistic M3Y-P6 interaction and its LS-modified variant M3Y-P6a, which incorporates a density-dependent LS term of zero-range form derived from 3N interaction effects. The strength parameter w1 in M3Y-P6a was fitted to reproduce the n0i spin-orbit splitting at 208Pb as obtained with M3Y-P6, while all other parameters remained identical between the two interactions. Results were compared with those from the Gogny-D1M interaction and a modified version D1Ma that includes density-dependent LS terms.

## Key numbers

- The n0d splitting at 40Ca decreases by only 4% when replacing M3Y-P6 by M3Y-P6a
- For M3Y-P6a, w1 = 742 MeV·fm^8 with d1 = 1.0 fm^3; for d1 = 2.0 fm^3, w1 = 842 MeV·fm^8
- The mean-square radius of neutrons occupying the 0f7/2 orbit decreases by 0.23 fm^2 at 40Ca when switching from M3Y-P6 to M3Y-P6a
- The mean-square radius of n0f7/2 increases from 40Ca to 48Ca by only 0.035 fm^2 with M3Y-P6a, compared to 0.365 fm^2 with M3Y-P6
- With D1M (D1Ma), n1g9/2 lies lower than n0i11/2 by 1.4 MeV (1.2 MeV) in 208Pb
- Root-mean-square charge radii with M3Y-P6a: 40Ca = 3.487 fm (μp) or 3.496 fm (ep); 208Pb = 5.464 fm (μp) or 5.470 fm (ep)
- Neutron-skin thickness difference between M3Y-P6 and M3Y-P6a: 0.011 fm larger at 68Ni, 0.014 fm smaller at 48Ca, and 0.001 fm or less at 208Pb and 132Sn

## Context

This work extends the author's previous finding (Nakada 2015) that density-dependent LS interactions from 3N forces reproduce the kink at N=126 in Pb isotope shifts, to Ca, Ni, and Sn isotopes. It addresses long-standing problems in nuclear structure where non-relativistic approaches with Skyrme interactions failed to reproduce isotope shift systematics that relativistic mean-field approaches (Sharma et al. 1994, 1995) could partially describe. The results enable future experimental tests of the predicted kink at N=82 in Sn isotopes and suggest that the 3N LS interaction may simultaneously resolve the origin of spin-orbit splitting and isotope shift puzzles, while also motivating readjustment of central channel parameters in heavier nuclei.
