# Rapid structural change in low-lying states of neutron-rich Sr and Zr isotopes

> The rapid structural change in low-lying collective excitation states of neutron-rich Sr and Zr isotopes is tudied by solving a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian with parameters determined by both relativistic mean-field and non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations using the PC-PK1 and SLy4 forces respectively. Pair correlations are treated in BCS method with either a separable pairing force or a density-dependent zero-range force. The isotope shifts, excitation energies, electric monopole and quadrupole transition strengths are calculated and compared with corresponding experimental data. The calculated results with both the PC-PK1 and SLy4 forces exhibit a picture of spherical-oblate-prolate shape transition in neutron-rich Sr and Zr isotopes. Compared with the experimental data, the PC-PK1 (or SLy4) force predicts a more moderate (or dramatic) change in most of the collective properties around N=60. The underlying microscopic mechanism responsible for the rapid transition is discussed.

## Key claim
The paper shows that both the relativistic PC-PK1 and non-relativistic SLy4 energy density functionals predict a spherical-oblate-prolate shape transition in neutron-rich Sr and Zr isotopes around N=60, but the PC-PK1 force yields a more moderate change in collective properties while the SLy4 force yields a more dramatic change, with neither calculation fully reproducing the rapid increase in the first 2+ excitation energy at 96Zr.

## Method
The authors solved a five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) for quadrupole vibrations and rotations, with collective potential, mass parameters, and moments of inertia determined from constrained relativistic mean-field (RMF)+BCS calculations using the PC-PK1 force with a separable pairing force, and from constrained Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF)+BCS calculations using the SLy4 force with a density-dependent zero-range pairing force. The collective Hamiltonian was diagonalized to obtain excitation energies, transition strengths, and collective wave functions for low-lying states in even-even Sr and Zr isotopes from N=50 to N=64.

## Key numbers
- Excitation energy E_x(2^+_1) decreases from N=58 to N=60 in both Sr and Zr isotopes, with the SLy4 force predicting a more dramatic drop than PC-PK1; the sudden increase in E_x(2^+_1) at 96Zr is not reproduced by either calculation.
- The ratio R_{4/2} = E_x(4^+_1)/E_x(2^+_1) approaches the rotational limit of 3.33 for prolate deformed isotopes near N=60 in SLy4 calculations, while PC-PK1 gives values closer to the vibrational limit of 2.00.
- Electric monopole transition strength ρ^2(E0:0^+_2→0^+_1) peaks at 96Sr and 100Zr in both calculations; for 98Sr, the SLy4 force underestimates the experimental value by approximately a factor of two, while PC-PK1 overestimates it by approximately a factor of two.
- The SLy4 force predicts a stronger neutron spin-orbit splitting than PC-PK1 by a factor of about 1.1 for all states in 96Sr and 98Sr.
- The squared proton radii from SLy4 are systematically larger than those from PC-PK1 by about 0.5%.

## Context
This work extends previous mean-field studies of shape transitions in Sr and Zr isotopes (Bonche 1985, Guzman 2010, Xiang 2012) by including dynamic correlation effects from quadrupole fluctuations via the 5DCH approach. It contrasts with the Gogny D1S-based 5DCH calculations of Delaroche (2010), which also gave a moderate shape transition, and with large-scale shell model calculations (Sieja 2009) that reproduced low-spin data for 90-98Zr but failed for 100Zr. The results enable systematic comparison of relativistic and non-relativistic energy functionals for describing rapid shape changes, and highlight the need for further beyond-mean-field investigations to reproduce the sharp increase in E_x(2^+_1) at 96Zr.
