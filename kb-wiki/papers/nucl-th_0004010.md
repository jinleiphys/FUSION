# Particles in classically forbidden area, neutron skin and halo, and pure neutron matter in Ca isotopes

> The nucleon density distributions and the thickness of pure neutron matter in Ca isotopes were systematically studied using the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model (SHF) from the $β$-stability line to the neutron drip-line. The pure neutron matter, related with the neutron skin or halo, was shown to depend not only on the Fermi levels of the neutrons but also on the orbital angular momentum of the valence neutrons. New definitions for the thickness of pure neutron matter are proposed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the thickness of pure neutron matter (neutron skin or halo) in Ca isotopes depends not only on the Fermi levels of the neutrons but also on the orbital angular momentum of the valence neutrons, with low-\(l\) orbits (such as \(s\) and \(p\)) being essential for halo formation even when the separation energy is small.

## Method

The authors performed spherical Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations using the SIII force parameter set for Ca isotopes from \(^{40}\)Ca to \(^{70}\)Ca, employing a filling approximation for open-shell nuclei. They computed nucleon density distributions, single-particle energies, and potential barriers, and proposed new definitions for the thickness of pure neutron matter based on integrated density radii (\(R_{N=1}\), \(R_{N=0.1}\), and \(R_{Z=0.1}\)).

## Key numbers

- The difference between experimental and calculated binding energies for Ca isotopes is less than 3 MeV, which is less than 1% of the experimental values (approximately 400 MeV).
- The energy gap between the \(1f5/2\) and \(1g9/2\) orbitals is about 3 MeV, compared to 5 MeV between \(2s1/2\) and \(1f7/2\), and 7 MeV between \(1f7/2\) and \(2p3/2\).
- The difference in potential barrier height between \(f\) orbits and \(p\) orbits is around 4.5 MeV for all Ca isotopes studied.
- The thickness \(t_n = R_{N=0.1} - R_{Z=0.1}\) shows a dramatic increase from \(^{50}\)Ca to \(^{54}\)Ca, then becomes constant after \(^{54}\)Ca.
- The thickness \(t_n = R_{N=1} - R_{Z=0.1}\) becomes positive after \(^{54}\)Ca, implying a neutron skin or halo in nuclei beyond that isotope.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of neutron skins and halos in drip-line nuclei using mean-field approaches, such as those by Dobaczewski, Hamamoto, Nazarewicz, and Sheikh (1994) and by Meng and Ring (1996). The paper provides a systematic SHF study of Ca isotopes and proposes new quantitative definitions for the thickness of pure neutron matter that are more sensitive to shell effects than the conventional difference of neutron and proton root-mean-square radii. The results enable future work that includes pairing, continuum, and deformation effects, which the authors note are important and will be addressed in subsequent studies.
