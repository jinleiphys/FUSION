# Electromagnetic moments of ground and excited states calculated in heavy odd-N open-shell nuclei

> Within nuclear DFT, we calculated spectroscopic magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments for various quasiparticle configurations of odd-$N$, even-$Z$, $83\leq{}N\leq125$ nuclei ranging from gadolinium to osmium. By tagging the blocked quasiparticles with single-particle states of the semi-magic dysprosium isotope, we efficiently computed 22 prolate and 22 oblate states for each of the 154 nuclei and tracked them across the entire major neutron shell. We compared this extensive set of theoretical results with experimental data for 82 states in the region. Breaking rotational, time-reversal, and signature symmetries, we aligned the intrinsic angular momenta along the axis of axial symmetry, thereby enabling full shape- and spin-self-consistent polarizations. The spectroscopic moments were then obtained by restoring rotational symmetry. We conducted a detailed analysis of the pattern of agreement and disagreement between theory and experiment in individual nuclei. For the magnetic dipole moments, agreement with the data varies and is characterized by an overall average and RMS deviation of 0.11 $μ_N$ and 0.35 $μ_N$, respectively. For the electric quadrupole moments, a good corresponding agreement of 0.16 b and 0.29 b was observed.

## Key claim

Within nuclear density functional theory (DFT), the authors calculated spectroscopic magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole moments for 22 prolate and 22 oblate quasiparticle configurations in each of 154 odd-N, even-Z nuclei (gadolinium to osmium, N=83-125), finding that agreement with experimental data varies and is characterized by average deviations of 0.11 μ_N (magnetic dipole) and 0.16 b (electric quadrupole), though the paper's abstract does not emphasize that 408 of 6,776 calculations did not converge and 233 cases produced duplicated solutions requiring a second round of calculations.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Skyrme functional UNEDF1, breaking rotational, time-reversal, and signature symmetries to align intrinsic angular momenta along the axial symmetry axis. They tagged blocked quasiparticles using single-particle states from the semi-magic nucleus 192Dy, applied constraints on total axial intrinsic quadrupole moments, and restored rotational symmetry via angular-momentum projection (AMP) to obtain spectroscopic moments. For each of the 154 nuclei, they computed 22 prolate and 22 oblate states, requiring 6,776 independent self-consistent calculations, with a second round of calculations using tag states defined in the specific nucleus to address non-converged and duplicated solutions.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic dipole moments: overall average deviation of 0.11 μ_N and RMS deviation of 0.35 μ_N when comparing 82 theoretical states with experimental data.
- Electric quadrupole moments: overall average deviation of 0.16 b and RMS deviation of 0.29 b when comparing with experimental data.
- 154 nuclei studied with neutron numbers 83 ≤ N ≤ 125 and proton numbers 64 ≤ Z ≤ 76 (gadolinium to osmium).
- 22 prolate and 22 oblate tag states (44 total) used for each nucleus, originating from spherical orbitals 2f7/2, 1h9/2, 3p3/2, 2f5/2, 3p1/2, and 1i13/2.
- 408 out of 6,776 calculations did not converge; 233 cases produced duplicated solutions; after a second round, 246 non-converged and 56 missed solutions remained.
- Constrained total axial intrinsic quadrupole moments of Q20tot = ±0.4 b used for initial deformation, with final self-consistent calculations using Q20tot = ±10 b as starting points.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous DFT methodology for calculating electromagnetic moments (Sassarini et al., 2022; Bonnard et al., 2023; Wibowo et al., 2025) from near-doubly-magic and open-shell tin-to-lead nuclei to a systematic study of deformed odd-N rare-earth nuclei. It contrasts with phenomenological approaches such as the Nilsson model (Nilsson, 1955) and Woods-Saxon calculations (Ringo, 1980), which required effective charges and effective g-factors, and with prior DFT calculations by Bally et al. (2014, 2022, 2023) and Ryssens et al. (2022) that used triaxial shapes with conserved signature symmetry. The results enable future systematic calculations of electromagnetic moments across the nuclear chart using the hfodd code, covering both weak-coupling and strong-coupling regimes.
