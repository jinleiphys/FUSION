# Compatibility of localized wave packets and unrestricted single particle dynamics for cluster formation in nuclear collisions

> Antisymmetrized molecular dynamics with quantum branching is generalized so as to allow finite time duration of the unrestricted coherent mean field propagation which is followed by the decoherence into wave packets. In this new model, the wave packet shrinking by the mean field propagation is respected as well as the diffusion, so that it predicts a one-body dynamics similar to that in mean field models. The shrinking effect is expected to change the diffusion property of nucleons in nuclear matter and the global one-body dynamics. The central $\xenon+\tin$ collisions at 50 MeV/nucleon are calculated by the models with and without shrinking, and it is shown that the inclusion of the wave packet shrinking has a large effect on the multifragmentation in a big expanding system with a moderate expansion velocity.

## Key claim

The paper presents a generalized framework for antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) that allows a finite coherence time for unrestricted mean field propagation before decoherence into localized wave packets, thereby respecting both the diffusion and the shrinking of phase-space distributions predicted by coherent mean field evolution. The authors show that including wave packet shrinking has a large effect on multifragmentation in central xenon-tin collisions at 50 MeV/nucleon, particularly on the expansion velocity and cluster size distribution.

## Method

The authors extend AMD by introducing two time parameters: the coherence time τ (duration of coherent mean field propagation before decoherence into Gaussian wave packets) and the mean field branching time τ_mf (when the mean field is calculated from individual wave packets rather than the averaged distribution). The mean field propagation is solved via a Vlasov equation with a Gaussian-Gaussian approximation, where diffusing components beyond the original wave packet width are converted into stochastic fluctuations of the centroid, while shrinking components are preserved in the shape variables. At decoherence time τ, the shape variables are reset to the minimum width (1/4 in phase space units), and the stochastic centroids become the physical wave packet positions. The new model AMD/DS uses (τ, τ_mf) = (large, 0), while the earlier AMD/D corresponds to (0, 0). Central collisions of 129Xe + 120Sn at 50 MeV/nucleon are calculated with both models and compared to INDRA experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Width parameter ν = 0.16 fm^{-2} for the Gaussian wave packets
- Central 129Xe + 120Sn collisions at 50 MeV/nucleon are calculated
- AMD/D overestimated clusters with Z = 4, 5, 6 and underestimated clusters with Z ≥ 15; these problems are solved by AMD/DS

## Context

This work extends the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics approach previously developed by Ono and collaborators (Ono, 1995; Ono, 1996) by incorporating wave packet shrinking effects that were missing in the earlier AMD/D model. The framework unifies molecular dynamics models (which assume fixed Gaussian wave packets) and mean field (TDHF-like) models (which allow unrestricted single-particle motion but cannot describe cluster formation) as limiting cases of the parameters (τ, τ_mf). The approach is related to the work of Ohnishi and Randrup (1996, 1997) on quantum fluctuations in wave packet molecular dynamics, but provides a dynamical origin for the fluctuations. The comparison with INDRA experimental data demonstrates that AMD/DS resolves discrepancies in cluster production that existed in AMD/D, enabling more reliable predictions for multifragmentation in heavy-ion collisions.
