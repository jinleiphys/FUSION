# Collectivity Embedded in Complex Spectra: Example of Nuclear Double-Charge Exchange Modes

> The mechanism of collectivity coexisting with chaos is investigated on the quantum level. The complex spectra are represented in the basis of two-particle two-hole states describing the nuclear double-charge exchange modes in $^{48}$Ca. An example of $J^π=0^-$ excitations shows that the residual interaction, which generically implies chaotic behavior, under certain specific and well identified conditions may create transitions stronger than those corresponding to the pure mean-field picture. Therefore, for this type of excitations such an effect is not generic and in most cases the strength of transitions is likely to take much lower values, even close to the Porter-Thomas distributed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a collective transition stronger than the mean-field prediction can appear in the complex 2p2h spectrum of nuclear double-charge exchange modes, but this effect is not generic; it requires specific conditions including correlations among matrix elements, nonuniformities in the density of states, and a proper matching of interaction strength, and in most cases the transition strengths are much lower and may even follow a Porter-Thomas distribution.

## Method

The authors diagonalized the nuclear Hamiltonian in a subspace of 2286 two-particle two-hole (2p2h) states describing double-charge exchange excitations in $^{48}$Ca, using a Woods-Saxon mean field and a density-dependent zero-range residual interaction. They computed the transition-strength distribution for $J^\pi=0^-$ excitations generated by a two-phonon operator coupling a dipole and a spin-quadrupole mode, and analyzed the information entropy, spectral rigidity, and off-diagonal matrix element distributions to characterize the interplay between collectivity and chaos.

## Key numbers

- The collective state appears at 49.1 MeV, with a transition strength more than a factor of 2 stronger than any unperturbed transition, shifted about 10 MeV higher in energy.
- About 96% of the collective strength originates from off-diagonal contributions $S^{od}_{F_{\alpha}}(n)$.
- The model space contains N=2286 2p2h states for the $J^\pi=0^-$ DCX excitations in $^{48}$Ca.
- Multiplying off-diagonal matrix elements by 0.7 produces a transition at 46 MeV that is another factor of 2 stronger than the original collective state.
- For the case with off-diagonal elements multiplied by 3.0, the total entropy of the strength distribution is ${\cal I}=6.82$, compared to ${\cal I}=7.0$ for a Porter-Thomas distribution with the same number of states.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of chaos in nuclear spectra, particularly the finding by Drozdz et al. (1994) that GOE-level fluctuations appear in the 2p2h space, and the Brown-Bolsterli schematic model (1959) for collectivity on the 1p1h level. The results show that collectivity can coexist with chaos under specific conditions, analogous to stochastic resonance, and suggest that most two-phonon giant resonances will have transition strengths significantly below the mean-field prediction, potentially creating an illusion of collectivity through concentration of strength at similar energies. The findings are relevant for understanding double-charge exchange reactions and for distinguishing conventional nuclear effects from possible dibaryon contributions as suggested by Bilger et al. (1993).
