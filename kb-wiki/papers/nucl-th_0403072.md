# Fragmentation or Recombination at High p_T?

> All hadronization processes, including fragmentation, are shown to proceed through recombination. The shower partons in a jet turn out to play an important role in describing the p_T spectra of hadrons produced in heavy-ion collisions. Due to the recombination of the shower partons with the soft thermal partons, the structure of jets produced in AA collisions is not the same as that of jets produced in pp collisions.

# Fragmentation or Recombination at High \(p_T\)?

## Key claim
All hadronization processes, including what is conventionally called fragmentation, actually proceed through recombination of partons. The crucial new element is that shower partons from a hard jet can recombine with soft thermal partons from the medium, a process that dominates hadron production in the moderate \(p_T\) range of 2–9 GeV/c in heavy-ion collisions and makes jet structure in AA collisions fundamentally different from that in pp collisions.

## Method
The authors express the fragmentation function (FF) as a convolution of a joint distribution of shower partons with a recombination function, and use known FFs from parametrizations to determine the shower parton distributions (SPDs). They then decompose the parton distribution for pion production into four components: thermal-thermal (TT), thermal-shower (TS), shower-shower from one jet (SS), and shower-shower from two overlapping jets ((SS)₂). The TS component is identified as the dominant contribution in the moderate \(p_T\) region for central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV.

## Key numbers
- The moderate \(p_T\) range where thermal-shower recombination dominates is \(2 < p_T < 9\) GeV/c.
- The parameter \(\xi = 0.07\) represents the fraction of hard partons that escape the dense medium to produce showers, determined by fitting the high-\(p_T\) spectrum.
- The nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}(p_T) \approx 0.2\) for \(p_T > 4\) GeV/c is larger than \(\xi\) because the denominator (scaled pp spectrum from fragmentation) is smaller than the TS recombination component.
- For trigger particles in the range \(3 < p_T^{\rm trig} < 4\) GeV/c, the STAR collaboration measured \(I_{AA} \approx 1.5\), where a value of 1 would indicate identical jet structures in AA and pp collisions.

## Context
This work extends the recombination model previously developed by Hwa and Yang (2003) and others, which had already explained many features of hadron production in heavy-ion collisions. The new addition of thermal-shower recombination contradicts the conventional fragmentation picture that treats hard and soft partons as noninteracting components. The formalism has also been applied to d+Au collisions to explain the Cronin effect (Cronin et al., 1975) as a final-state interaction rather than initial-state \(k_T\) broadening (Accardi and Arleo, 2002). This work enables more precise quantification of how jet structure differs between AA and pp collisions, and suggests that the separation of partons into soft and hard components is an oversimplification.
