# Inclusive tau lepton hadronic decay in the framework of dispersive approach to QCD

> Based on talks given at the 12th Workshop on Nonperturbative QCD (10 - 13 June 2013, Paris, France) and the 16th Lomonosov Conference on Elementary Particle Physics (22 - 28 August 2013, Moscow, Russia); 4 pages, 1 figure

# Inclusive Tau Lepton Hadronic Decay in the Framework of Dispersive Approach to QCD

## Key claim
The dispersive approach to QCD, which incorporates nonperturbative constraints from dispersion relations and a nonvanishing hadronic production threshold, can describe the inclusive tau lepton hadronic decay data from ALEPH and OPAL in both vector and axial-vector channels, but only when using a specific model for the spectral density, as no unique complete expression for the spectral density is currently known.

## Method
The author employs the dispersive approach to QCD, which provides integral representations for the hadronic vacuum polarization function, the R-ratio of electron-positron annihilation into hadrons, and the Adler function, with the spectral density modeled as the sum of a one-loop perturbative term and an intrinsically nonperturbative term proportional to Lambda^2/sigma. The QCD scale parameter Lambda is then extracted by fitting this framework to the updated ALEPH and OPAL experimental data on inclusive tau lepton hadronic decay in both vector and axial-vector channels at the one-loop level with three active flavors.

## Key numbers
- Vector channel: QCD scale parameter Lambda = 408 +/- 30 MeV from ALEPH data and 409 +/- 53 MeV from OPAL data within the dispersive approach.
- Axial-vector channel: QCD scale parameter Lambda = 418 +/- 35 MeV from ALEPH data and 409 +/- 61 MeV from OPAL data within the dispersive approach.
- Vector channel: perturbative approach gives Lambda = 434 (+117/-127) MeV from ALEPH data and 445 (+201/-230) MeV from OPAL data.
- Axial-vector channel: perturbative approach yields no solution for the QCD scale parameter.

## Context
This work extends the dispersive approach to QCD developed by Nesterenko and collaborators (2012, 2013) to inclusive tau lepton hadronic decay, contrasting with the massless Analytic Perturbation Theory (APT) approach of Shirkov and Solovtsov (1997) and subsequent modifications, which either treat only the total sum of vector and axial-vector terms or the vector term alone. The dispersive approach also shows agreement with low-energy lattice simulation data for the hadronic vacuum polarization function, and the extracted Lambda values being nearly identical in both channels supports the self-consistency of the method. Future work could apply this framework to other processes or refine the spectral density model.
