# Oscillations of the static meson fields at finite baryon density

> The spatial dependence of static meson correlation functions at finite baryon density is studied in the Nambu -- Jona - Lasinio model. In contrast to the finite temperature case, we find that the correlation functions at finite density are not screened but exhibit long-range oscillations. The observed phenomenon is analogous to the Friedel oscillations in a degenerate electron gas.

## Key claim

At finite baryon density, static meson correlation functions in the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model are not screened but exhibit long-range oscillations, analogous to Friedel oscillations in a degenerate electron gas; this behavior contrasts with the exponential screening found at finite temperature and arises from the existence of a sharp Fermi surface.

## Method

The authors compute static meson correlation functions in the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model using the imaginary-time formalism at finite chemical potential, separating vacuum and matter parts. The zeroth-order correlation functions are evaluated in the random-phase approximation, and the Fourier transform to coordinate space is performed by deforming the integration contour in the complex momentum plane to isolate contributions from poles, vacuum cuts, and matter cuts. For higher densities where the NJL approach breaks down, the correlation function is computed in perturbative QCD keeping only the leading term.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass in vacuum: \(M_0 = 376\) MeV.
- Pion dynamic mass in vacuum: \(m_{dyn,\pi} = 138\) MeV.
- Sigma dynamic mass in vacuum: \(m_{dyn,\sigma} = 760\) MeV.
- Quark condensate in vacuum: \(\langle \overline{q} q \rangle^{1/3} = -214\) MeV.
- Pion decay constant: \(f_\pi = 94\) MeV.
- Pion–nucleon sigma term from the model: \(\Sigma_{\pi N} = 18\) MeV.
- At nuclear saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.17\ \text{fm}^{-3}\), the quark condensate is reduced by 15%.
- In perturbative QCD, the oscillation period is \(\delta r = \pi / p_F\), where \(p_F\) is the quark Fermi momentum.
- For the pseudoscalar channel at finite density, oscillations appear for chemical potentials in the range \(376\ \text{MeV} \leq \mu \leq 415\ \text{MeV}\), with period decreasing as \(\mu\) increases.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of meson screening masses at finite temperature in the NJL model (Florkowski and Friman, 1995) to finite baryon density, complementing lattice QCD results that are unavailable at non-zero density. The oscillatory behavior is analogous to Friedel oscillations in a degenerate electron gas (Fetter and Walecka, 1971) and contrasts with the exponential screening found at high temperature in lattice simulations (DeTar and Kogut, 1987; Gottlieb et al., 1987; Gavai et al., 1989; Born et al., 1991). The results enable further study of in-medium hadron properties and the chiral phase transition at finite density, though the NJL approach breaks down at moderate densities, requiring perturbative QCD for higher densities.
