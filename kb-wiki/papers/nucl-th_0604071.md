# Thermal Dimuon Yields at NA60

> Dilepton emission rates from a hadronic gas at finite temperature and baryon density are completely constrained by broken chiral symmetry in a density expansion. The rates can be expressed in terms of vacuum correlations which are measured in $e^+e^-$ annihilation, $τ$ decays and photo-reactions on nucleons and nuclei. In this paper, the theoretical results are summarized and the total dimuon yield is calculated by integrating the dimuon rates over the space-time history of a fireball based on hydrodynamic calculations with CERN SPS conditions. The resulting dimuon yield is in good agreement with the recent measurements reported by NA60.

# Thermal Dimuon Yields at NA60

## Key claim

The thermal dimuon yields from a hadronic gas constrained by broken chiral symmetry, combined with perturbative or non-perturbative QGP rates and integrated over a hydrodynamic fireball evolution, reproduce the excess dimuon data reported by NA60 for In-In collisions at 158 GeV/nucleon, but only under the condition that the matter is dilute; in central collisions the model over-predicts the yield at the rho peak by approximately 50-60%, indicating that higher-order terms in the virial expansion may be needed.

## Method

The dilepton emission rates were computed using a chiral reduction formalism that expresses the thermal current-current correlation function in terms of vacuum spectral functions measured in e+e- annihilation, tau decays, and photo-reactions on nucleons and nuclei, expanded to first order in meson and nucleon density. These rates were integrated over the space-time history of a fireball obtained from a (2+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic simulation with boost invariance, using an equation of state with a hadronic phase (ideal gas of SU(3) multiplets), a mixed phase, and a QGP phase, with initial conditions set by a Glauber model to match CERN SPS conditions for In-In collisions. The final dimuon yields were folded with the NA60 acceptance function and compared to the excess data in four centrality windows.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature: T_c ≈ 170 MeV
- Freeze-out temperature: T_f.o. = 130 MeV
- Initial proper time: τ_0 = 1.0 fm/c
- Speed of sound in mixed phase: c_s^2 = 0.05c
- Speed of sound in QGP phase: c_s^2 = 0.33c
- Entropy per participant per unit spatial rapidity: C_s = 8.06
- Net baryon number per participant per unit spatial rapidity: C_nB = 0.191
- Baryon density to entropy ratio: n_B/s = 0.0238
- Energy density contour for QGP to mixed phase transition: e_Q = 1.70 GeV/fm^3
- Energy density contour for mixed to hadronic phase transition: e_H = 0.50 GeV/fm^3
- In semi-central collisions, the theoretical rates over-predict the yield at the rho peak by approximately 50%
- In central collisions, the theoretical rates over-predict the yield at the rho peak by approximately 60%
- The non-perturbative QGP rate is about a factor of two larger than the perturbative rate in the low mass region (below approximately 0.4 GeV)

## Context

This work extends the model-independent analysis of dilepton emission rates constrained by broken chiral symmetry, previously applied to CERES (NA45) dielectron data by Dusling, Teaney, and Zahed (2005), to the higher-statistics NA60 dimuon data from In-In collisions at 158 GeV/nucleon. The approach uses the chiral reduction formalism developed in earlier papers by the same authors (2005) and the hydrodynamic code of Teaney (2001). The results contradict earlier attempts to explain the CERES excess using effective Lagrangians with medium modification (Rapp, 1997; Li and Gale, 1998) or dropping vector meson masses (Brown and Rho, 1991), as those model-independent rates were unable to account for the NA45 excess. The present work enables future investigation of higher-order terms in the virial expansion for central collisions where the diluteness assumption may break down, and provides a baseline for comparing non-perturbative QGP effects from gluon condensates.
