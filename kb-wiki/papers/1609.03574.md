# Dispersive treatment of $K_S\toγγ$ and $K_S\toγ\ell^+\ell^-$

> We analyse the rare kaon decays $K_S \to γγ$ and $K_S \to γ\ell^+\ell^-$ $(\ell = e \mbox{ or } μ)$ in a dispersive framework in which the weak Hamiltonian carries momentum. Our analysis extends predictions from lowest order $SU(3)_L\times SU(3)_R$ chiral perturbation theory ($χ$PT$_3$) to fully account for effects from final-state interactions, and is free from ambiguities associated with extrapolating the kaon off-shell. Given input from $K_S \to ππ$ and $γγ^{(*)}\toππ$, we solve the once-subtracted dispersion relations numerically to predict the rates for $K_S \to γγ$ and $K_S \to γ\ell^+\ell^-$. In the leptonic modes, we find sizeable corrections to the $χ$PT$_3$ predictions for the integrated rates.

## Key claim
The paper extends lowest-order chiral perturbation theory (χPT₃) predictions for \(K_S\to\gamma\gamma\) and \(K_S\to\gamma\ell^+\ell^-\) by fully accounting for final-state interactions (FSI) via a dispersive framework, while avoiding ambiguities from off-shell kaon extrapolations. The authors find that FSI improve agreement with experiment for \(K_S\to\gamma\gamma\) and produce sizeable corrections to χPT₃ predictions for the leptonic modes.

## Method
The authors construct a dispersive representation for \(K_S\to\gamma\gamma^*\) by allowing the weak Hamiltonian to carry momentum, then decompose the amplitude into scalar functions free from kinematic singularities. They solve once-subtracted dispersion relations numerically using input from \(K_S\to\pi\pi\) (via Omnès functions and the empirical \(I=0\) amplitude \(A_0\)) and from \(\gamma\gamma^{(*)}\to\pi\pi\) (using the coupled-channel analysis of García-Martín and Moussallam for the helicity partial wave \(h_{0,++}^0\)). The subtraction constant for \(K_S\to\gamma\gamma\) is fixed by matching to χPT₃ at a point below \(\pi\pi\) threshold where FSI are absent.

## Key numbers
- Branching ratio \(\mathrm{BR}(K_S \to \gamma\gamma)_{\chi\mathrm{PT}_3} = 2.0 \times 10^{-6}\) (lowest-order chiral prediction).
- Experimental average \(\mathrm{BR}(K_S \to \gamma\gamma) = (2.63\pm 0.17)\times 10^{-6}\) (Agashe 2014).
- Ratio \(\Gamma(K_S\to\gamma e^+e^-)/\Gamma(K_S\to\gamma\gamma)|_{\chi\mathrm{PT}_3} = 1.6\times 10^{-2}\) (lowest-order chiral prediction).
- Ratio \(\Gamma(K_S\to\gamma\mu^+\mu^-)/\Gamma(K_S\to\gamma\gamma)|_{\chi\mathrm{PT}_3} = 3.8\times 10^{-4}\) (lowest-order chiral prediction).
- Empirical \(I=0\) amplitude \(A_0 = (2.704\pm0.001)\times 10^{-7}\) GeV (Cirigliano 2011).
- Octet coupling \(G_8 = 9.1 \times 10^{-6}\) GeV\(^{-2}\) at \(O(p^2)\).
- Pion decay constant \(F_\pi = 92.2\) MeV (Agashe 2014).
- Subtraction point for dispersion relation: \(s_0 = -0.098\) GeV\(^2\).
- Cutoff for dispersion integral: \(\Lambda = 1.2\) GeV\(^2\) (benchmark value).

## Context
This work extends the dispersive framework developed by Büchler et al. (2001) and Colangelo et al. (2001) for \(K\to\pi\pi\) to the rare radiative decays \(K_S\to\gamma\gamma\) and \(K_S\to\gamma\ell^+\ell^-\). It improves upon lowest-order χPT₃ predictions (D'Ambrosio 1986, Goity 1986, Ecker 1987) by incorporating \(\pi\pi\) rescattering effects via Omnès functions, and avoids off-shell extrapolation ambiguities present in earlier work by Kambor et al. (1993). The results are relevant for ongoing experiments such as NA62 and KLOE-2, which may be sensitive to \(K_S\) branching ratios down to \(O(10^{-9})\).
