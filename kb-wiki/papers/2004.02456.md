# Recent Progress in Basis Light-front Quantization

> Basis Light-front Quantization (BLFQ) is a nonperturbative approach to quantum field theory. In this paper, we report our recent progress in applying BLFQ to the positronium system in QED and to the meson and the baryon system in QCD. We present preliminary results on the mass spectrum, light-front wave functions and other observables of these systems, where one dynamical gauge boson is retained for the positronium and meson systems.

## Key claim

BLFQ provides a nonperturbative, first-principles approach to bound states in QED and QCD, but the results presented for positronium, mesons, and baryons are preliminary and rely on basis truncation (retaining only the two leading Fock sectors) and, for QCD systems, the addition of phenomenological confining potentials and parameter tuning.

## Method

The authors solve the light-front Hamiltonian eigenvalue problem for positronium (QED) and heavy/light mesons and baryons (QCD) using Basis Light-front Quantization (BLFQ). For positronium and mesons, they retain the two leading Fock sectors (e.g., \(|e^+e^-\rangle + |e^+e^-\gamma\rangle\) for positronium; \(|q\bar{q}\rangle + |q\bar{q}g\rangle\) for mesons) and apply Fock-sector dependent renormalization for fermion masses. For QCD mesons, they add a phenomenological confining potential in both longitudinal and transverse directions to the \(|q\bar{q}\rangle\) sector, and for baryons they use an effective interaction in the \(|qqq\rangle\) sector with pairwise one-gluon-exchange and confining interactions.

## Key numbers

- Positronium: artificially increased electromagnetic coupling constant \(\alpha = 0.075\) used for numerical convenience; binding energy spectrum calculated at \(N_{\rm max} = K-1 = 28\).
- Charmonium: charm quark kinetic mass \(m_c = 1.561\) GeV, interaction mass \(m'_c = 4.343\) GeV, gluon mass \(m_g = 0.4\) GeV, confining strength \(\kappa = 1.14\) GeV, strong coupling \(g = 2.3\); spectra at \(N_{\rm max} = K-1 = 8\).
- Bottomonium: bottom quark kinetic mass \(m_b = 4.767\) GeV, interaction mass \(m_f = 15\) GeV, gluon mass \(m_g = 0.4\) GeV, \(\kappa = 1.948\) GeV, \(g = 1.6\); spectra at \(N_{\rm max} = K-1 = 8\).
- Light mesons: \(m_u = m_d = 0.33\) GeV, \(m'_u = m'_d = 3.38\) GeV, \(\kappa = 0.77\) GeV, \(g = 1.74\).
- Pion PDF: initial scale \(\mu_0^2 = 0.31\) GeV\(^2\) evolved to \(\mu^2 = 16\) GeV\(^2\) via DGLAP equations.
- Baryon: ground state spin-1/2 nucleon and spin-3/2 \(\Delta(1232)\) masses calculated; \(\Delta(1232)\) mass is about 170 MeV smaller than the experimental value.

## Context

This work extends the BLFQ approach from earlier effective-interaction calculations (e.g., Wiecki (2014) for positronium with one-photon-exchange; Li (2017) and Tang (2018) for mesons with one-gluon-exchange) to include dynamical gauge bosons (photons/gluons) in the Fock space for positronium and mesons. For baryons, it provides an initial calculation using an effective interaction in the \(|qqq\rangle\) sector. The results enable future studies of hadron structure beyond the valence sector, including parton distribution functions and form factors, and can be improved by increasing basis sizes and including higher Fock sectors.
