# Roles of triaxiality and residual interaction in signature inversions of A~130 odd-odd nuclei

> Rotational bands with (neutron h_11/2)^1 (proton h_11/2)^1 configurations are studied using a particle-rotor model in which a proton and a neutron quasiparticles interacting through a zero-range force are coupled with a triaxial rotor. It is shown for 124Cs that one can reproduce the signature dependence of energy and B(M1)/B(E2) ratio best when one takes into account gamma-deformations with irrotational-flow moment of inertia in addition to the proton-neutron interaction proposed by Semmes and Ragnarsson. Including both effects, a systematic calculation of signature splittings is performed for Cs, La isotopes and N=75 isotones to be compared with experiments. Discussions are also done on the deficiencies of the cranking model concerning its applicability to signature inversion phenomena in odd-odd nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper shows that signature inversion in odd-odd nuclei near A~130 can be reproduced best by combining a zero-range proton-neutron residual interaction (as proposed by Semmes and Ragnarsson) with a triaxial rotor having an irrotational-flow moment of inertia and negative gamma deformation, rather than using either factor alone or using a gamma-reversed moment of inertia.

## Method

A particle-triaxial-rotor model was used in which one proton quasiparticle and one neutron quasiparticle in h11/2 orbitals are coupled to a triaxial rotor, with a zero-range delta interaction between the unpaired nucleons. The Hamiltonian was diagonalized in a subspace of one proton h11/2 orbital, one neutron h11/2 orbital, and rotor states with K=0,2,4,6. Systematic calculations of signature splittings were performed for Cs isotopes (A=120-128), La isotopes (A=124-130), and N=75 isotones, using parameters for quadrupole deformation from neighboring even-even nuclei and gamma values from the ratio E(2+_gamma)/E(2+_gr).

## Key numbers

- For 124Cs, the best reproduction of signature dependence of energy and B(M1)/B(E2) ratio was achieved with gamma = -23 degrees (negative-gamma rotor) combined with the Semmes-Ragnarsson interaction (u0 = -7.2 MeV, u1 = -0.8 MeV)
- The irrotational-flow moment of inertia parameter Ec(2+) was set to 0.20 MeV for 124Cs and 0.15 MeV for 120Cs in the intensive study
- For the systematic calculation, a Coriolis attenuation factor rho = 0.7 was used for all nuclei
- The k=2 component of the pn interaction has strength -11.2 MeV for hhconfig configurations, compared to -7.7 MeV for the standard QQ force in A=124 nuclei (a factor of 1.5 stronger)
- The range of fluctuation in the two-body matrix element g_J versus J is 3.4 MeV for Vpn and 1.1 MeV for V_QQ (a factor of 3.0)
- For 124Cs, the GM splitting from the spin-dependent interaction is very small: only 3% difference in spin-singlet/triplet probabilities between configurations with Omega_p*Omega_n > 0 and < 0
- For 120Cs, I_inv coincides with experiment when u0 = -7.2 MeV, but the slope is twice as small as the experimental trend
- For 124Cs, I_inv and slope are reproduced with u0 = -5.4 MeV when using axial symmetry only
- With gamma = +18 degrees and no pn interaction, I_inv and slope are reproduced for 124Cs
- With gamma = -23 degrees and Vpn (u0 = -7.2 MeV), the agreement with experiment for 124Cs is very good
- For 120Cs, the calculated I_inv is smaller than experiment by Delta I = -1.8
- For 126Cs, the calculated I_inv is larger than experiment by Delta I = 2.0
- The abrupt reversion of sign between A=126 and A=128 Cs is not reproduced
- For La isotopes, I_inv increases as A increases in both experiment and calculation

## Context

This work extends the particle-rotor model framework of Semmes and Ragnarsson (1990, 1992), who applied a zero-range pn interaction to 152Eu and 120Cs, by systematically including triaxiality with irrotational-flow moment of inertia. It contradicts the earlier approach of Hamamoto (1986), who needed a QQ interaction ten times stronger than standard to reproduce signature inversion in 157Ho, and the work of Ikeda and Shimano (1989), who required a gamma-reversed moment of inertia. The paper also points out deficiencies of the cranking model for signature inversion phenomena, specifically the non-unique correspondence between signature and parity of I at low spins and the inability to treat K-band mixing in 2qp configurations. The results enable systematic calculations of signature splittings across isotopic and isotonic chains in the A~130 region, though the model fails to reproduce the sign reversal between Cs and La isotopes.
