# Nucleus-nucleus potential with shell-correction contribution

> The full relaxed-density potential between spherical nuclei is considered as a sum of the macroscopic and shell-correction contributions. The macroscopic part of the potential is related to a nucleus-nucleus potential obtained in the framework of the extended Thomas-Fermi approach with the Skyrme and Coulomb forces and the relaxed-density ansatz for evaluation of proton and neutron densities of interacting nuclei. A simple prescription for the shell-correction part of the total potential is discussed. The parameters of the shell-correction and macroscopic parts of the relaxed-density potential are found by fitting the empirical barrier heights of the 89 nucleus-nucleus systems as well as macroscopic potentials evaluated for 1485 nucleus-nucleus systems at 12 distances around touching points.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the full nucleus-nucleus potential between spherical nuclei can be accurately described as a sum of a macroscopic part (derived from the extended Thomas-Fermi approach with Skyrme and Coulomb forces) and a shell-correction part, with the hedge that the shell-correction contribution is significant only near the touching point and is exponentially suppressed at larger separations, and that its energy dependence matters only above the barrier.

## Method

The macroscopic part of the potential was computed using the non-local extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) energy-density functional with Skyrme (SLy4) and Coulomb forces, combined with a relaxed-density ansatz for the proton and neutron densities of interacting nuclei. The shell-correction contribution was parametrized using a Fermi-function form (radius \(R_{\rm sh}\) and diffuseness \(d_{\rm sh}\)) multiplied by the sum of the shell-correction energies of the two isolated nuclei, which were obtained from the difference between experimental binding energies and a liquid-drop mass formula. The parameters of both the macroscopic Woods-Saxon form and the shell-correction part were fitted by minimizing a function that included 17820 macroscopic potential values (1485 systems at 12 distances around the touching point) and 89 empirical barrier heights.

## Key numbers

- The macroscopic potential was evaluated for 1485 nucleus-nucleus systems at 12 distances around the touching point, yielding 17820 data points.
- The fit used 89 empirical barrier heights from Refs. [duttp, nbdhgmh, mintjw, gg].
- The Woods-Saxon strength parameters: \(v_1 = -27.190\) MeV fm\(^{-1}\), \(v_2 = -0.93009\) MeV fm\(^{-1/2}\).
- The diffuseness parameters: \(d_1 = 0.78122\) fm, \(d_2 = -0.20535\) fm\(^2\).
- The liquid-drop mass formula (Eq. 17) described experimental binding energies of 3353 nuclei with a root mean error of 2.49 MeV.
- The shell-correction energy of \(^{208}\)Pb evaluated via Eqs. (16)-(17) is \(-10.620\) MeV.
- The reduction parameter for the energy dependence of the shell-correction contribution is \(a = 0.35\) MeV\(^{-1}\), determined from the \(^{16}\)O + \(^{208}\)Pb reaction.

## Context

This work extends the macroscopic-microscopic approach of Strutinsky (1967, 1972) to the nucleus-nucleus potential, which had previously been applied only to atomic masses, fission, and cluster decay. It contrasts with phenomenological potentials (e.g., Bass (1973, 1977), Christensen and Winther (1976), and the proximity potential) that ignore shell effects entirely. The resulting potential enables improved modeling of heavy-ion fusion cross sections, especially the hindrance at deep sub-barrier energies, as previously studied for the \(^{16}\)O + \(^{208}\)Pb system by Denisov (2014).
