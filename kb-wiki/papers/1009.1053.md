# Charge Fluctuations from the Chiral Magnetic Effect in Nuclear Collisions

> We derive a nonlocal effective Lagrangian for the chiral magnetic effect. An electric field is generated by winding number fluctuations of the nonabelian gauge field in the presence of a strong magnetic field. We estimate the magnitude of charge asymmetry fluctuations with respect to the reaction plane induced by the chiral magnetic effect in relativistic heavy ion collisions to be less than $10^{-6}$, several orders of magnitude smaller than the signal observed in the STAR experiment.

## Key claim

The authors estimate that charge asymmetry fluctuations with respect to the reaction plane induced by the chiral magnetic effect in relativistic heavy ion collisions are less than 10⁻⁶, which is several orders of magnitude smaller than the signal observed in the STAR experiment; this estimate is conditional on assuming one winding number transition per volume of approximately (0.5 fm)³ during the presence of the strong magnetic field.

## Method

The authors derive a nonlocal effective Lagrangian for the chiral magnetic effect by relating the chiral magnetic conductivity to the axial anomaly and the winding number fluctuations of the SU(3) gauge field. They then compute the induced electric field by minimizing the total field energy density including nonlinear QED effects, and relate the resulting momentum shift of charged particles to the final-state charge asymmetry using a thermal spectrum modified by collective flow. The numerical estimate uses parameters for Au+Au collisions at RHIC, including a magnetic field lifetime τ_B ≈ 2R/γ_cm, a temperature T ≈ 350 MeV during the magnetized phase, and a freeze-out temperature T_f ≈ 150 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Estimated charge asymmetry fluctuation Δ^± ≈ 3.5 × 10⁻⁷ × (b/R), where b is the impact parameter and R = 7 fm is the nuclear radius
- Induced electric field magnitude |e E_min| ≈ (6 MeV)² × (b/R) for Z = 79 and R = 7 fm
- Magnetic field lifetime τ_B ≈ 2R/γ_cm with γ_cm = 100 at RHIC
- Number of independent winding number domains N = πR²/ρ̄² ≈ 600, with domain size ρ̄ ≈ 0.5 fm
- Freeze-out temperature T_f ≈ 150 MeV and collective flow velocity |v| ≈ 0.5c
- Nonlinear QED effect reduces induced electric field substantially, with f(B) ≈ 10 for B = (100 MeV)²

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Fukushima, Kharzeev, and Warringa (2008) on the chiral magnetic effect by constructing a nonlocal effective Lagrangian and including the back reaction of the induced electric field. The estimate contradicts the magnitude of charge correlations observed in the STAR experiment (Abelev 2009), suggesting that the observed signal may have a different origin. The paper identifies several avenues for improvement, including more reliable calculations of winding number fluctuation density from color glass condensate interactions (Asakawa 2010) and sphaleron transition rates in pre-equilibrated quark-gluon plasma (Kharzeev 2001), as well as lattice QCD simulations of winding number fluctuations in strong magnetic fields (Buividovich 2009, Abramczyk 2009).
