# Nonextensive statistical effects on nuclear astrophysics and many-body problems

> Density and temperature conditions in many stellar core (like the solar core) imply the presence of nonideal plasma effects with memory and long-range interactions between particles. This aspect suggests the possibility that the stellar core could not be in a global thermodynamical equilibrium but satisfies the conditions of a metastable state with a stationary (nonextensive) power law distribution function among ions. The order of magnitude of the deviation from the standard Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution can be derived microscopically by considering the presence of random electrical microfields in the stellar plasma. We show that such a nonextensive statistical effect can be very relevant in many nuclear astrophysical problems.

# Nonextensive statistical effects on nuclear astrophysics and many-body problems

## Key claim
The solar core and similar stellar plasmas may not be in global thermodynamic equilibrium but in a metastable state described by a power-law distribution function, with deviations from the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution that are small (q=0.990) but can produce significant effects in nuclear astrophysical reaction rates, particularly for the CNO cycle and resonant fusion reactions.

## Method
The authors derive a deformation parameter δ = (1-q)/2 from microscopic considerations of random electrical microfields in stellar plasmas, relating it to the plasma parameter Γ and the pair-correlation function. They then apply the resulting nonextensive distribution to calculate modified nuclear reaction rates for the pp chain, CNO cycle, and resonant reactions such as ¹²C+¹²C fusion, expressing the nonextensive correction as a multiplicative factor to the classical reaction rate.

## Key numbers
- Plasma parameter in the solar core: Γ⊙ ≃ 0.1
- Global equilibrium values obtained from free energy minimization: n* ≃ 2.74 × 10⁻¹⁴ fm⁻³, kB T* ≃ 5 keV, R* ≈ 0.2 R⊙
- Deformation parameter for the solar core: |δ| ≈ 0.005, corresponding to q = 0.990 (with α = 0.55, Γ ∼ 0.1)
- Nonextensive CNO correction ranges from 37% to more than 53%
- For carbon white dwarf plasma at T = 8 × 10⁸ K and ρ = 2 × 10⁹ g/cm³ (Γ ≃ 5.6), the deformation parameter is set to |δ| = 10⁻³
- Resonant screening correction reduces the ¹²C+¹²C rate by 11 orders of magnitude at the resonant energy of 400 keV

## Context
This work extends the application of Tsallis nonextensive thermostatistics (Tsallis 1988, Borges 2004) to stellar plasmas, building on the authors' previous studies (Lavagno and Quarati 2001, Ferro et al.) of metastable states in electron-nuclear plasmas. The paper connects microscopic plasma properties (random electrical microfields) to macroscopic nuclear reaction rates, enabling quantitative predictions for solar neutrino fluxes, Jupiter energy production, lithium abundance, and white dwarf evolution. The approach also parallels recent applications to high-energy nuclear collisions at SPS and RHIC facilities.
