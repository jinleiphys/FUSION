# Neutron-proton scattering with lattice chiral effective field theory at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order

> We present a new lattice formulation of chiral effective field theory interactions with a simpler decomposition into spin channels. With these interactions the process of fitting to the empirical scattering phase shifts is simplified, and the resulting lattice phase shifts are more accurate than in previous studies. We present results for the neutron-proton system up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order for lattice spacings of $1.97$, $1.64$, $1.32$, and $0.99~{\rm fm}$. Our results provide a pathway to $\textit{ab initio}$ lattice calculations of nuclear structure, reactions, and thermodynamics with accurate and systematic control over the chiral nucleon-nucleon force.

## Key claim
The paper presents a new lattice formulation of chiral effective field theory interactions with a simpler decomposition into spin channels, which simplifies fitting to empirical scattering phase shifts and produces more accurate lattice phase shifts than previous studies, but only for neutron-proton scattering and only up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) for lattice spacings of 1.97, 1.64, 1.32, and 0.99 fm.

## Method
The authors constructed a lattice Hamiltonian with short-range interactions organized by chiral order (Q0, Q2, Q4) and long-range one-pion and two-pion exchange interactions, using a new operator decomposition based on spherical harmonics and spin-orbit Clebsch-Gordan coefficients. They computed neutron-proton scattering phase shifts and mixing angles from radial wave functions extracted from lattice simulations, fitting low-energy constants to the Nijmegen partial wave analysis (NPWA) data. For coarse lattice spacings (1.97 and 1.64 fm) the two-pion exchange potential was omitted and absorbed into contact terms, while for finer spacings (1.32 and 0.99 fm) it was included explicitly.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacings used: 1.97, 1.64, 1.32, and 0.99 fm
- For a = 1.97 fm: C0 = -0.175 l.u., sL = 0.070 l.u., sNL = 0.080 l.u.
- For a = 1.64 fm: C0 = -0.100 l.u., sL = 0.109 l.u., sNL = 0.122 l.u.
- For a = 1.32 fm: C0 = -0.045 l.u., sL = 0.170 l.u., sNL = 0.186 l.u.
- For a = 0.99 fm: C0 = -0.015 l.u., sL = 0.265 l.u., sNL = 0.283 l.u.
- Deuteron binding energy used in fits: Eb(Exp) = 2.224575 MeV with error Delta E_Exp = 9 x 10^-6 MeV
- Galilean invariance restoration coefficient for a = 1.97 fm: C_GIR = -0.0658
- Lattice cutoffs: 314 MeV for a = 1.97 fm, 377 MeV for a = 1.64 fm; regularization parameter b_pi = 0.25 lattice units, giving Lambda = 300 MeV for a = 1.32 fm and Lambda = 400 MeV for a = 0.99 fm
- Energy range for fits at coarse spacings: E_lab <= 50 MeV, using five points at 1, 5, 10, 25, and 50 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous nuclear lattice simulations using chiral effective field theory (Epelbaum 2011, Epelbaum 2012, Epelbaum 2013, Elhatisari 2015, Elhatisari 2016, Elhatisari 2017) by introducing a simpler spin-channel decomposition that solves problems with rotational invariance breaking at higher chiral orders. The new interactions enable more accurate phase shifts than earlier lattice studies and provide a pathway to ab initio lattice calculations of nuclear structure, reactions, and thermodynamics with systematic control over the chiral nucleon-nucleon force, building on the SU(4) symmetry treatment from Elhatisari (2017) and the scattering method from Lu (2015).
