# QCD Predictions for Meson Electromagnetic Form Factors at High Momenta: Testing Factorization in Exclusive Processes

> We report the first lattice QCD computation of pion and kaon electromagnetic form factors, $F_M(Q^2)$, at large momentum transfer up to 10 and 28 $\mathrm{GeV}^2$, respectively. Utilizing physical masses and two fine lattices, we achieve good agreement with JLab experimental results at $Q^2 \lesssim 4~\mathrm{GeV}^2$. For $Q^2 \gtrsim 4~\mathrm{GeV}^2$, our results provide $\textit{ab-initio}$ QCD benchmarks for the forthcoming experiments at JLab 12 GeV and future electron-ion colliders. We also test the QCD collinear factorization framework utilizing our high-$Q^2$ form factors at next-to-next-to-leading order in perturbation theory, which relates the form factors to the leading Fock-state meson distribution amplitudes. Comparisons with independent lattice QCD calculations using the same framework demonstrate, within estimated uncertainties, the universality of these nonperturbative quantities.

## Key claim

This work presents the first lattice QCD computation of pion and kaon electromagnetic form factors at momentum transfers up to 10 GeV² and 28 GeV² respectively, providing ab-initio QCD benchmarks for future experiments; the agreement with JLab experimental results is limited to Q² ≲ 4 GeV², and the test of QCD collinear factorization at higher Q² relies on comparisons with independent lattice calculations of meson distribution amplitudes within estimated uncertainties.

## Method

The authors extracted bare meson form factors from two-point and three-point correlation functions computed on two HotQCD gauge ensembles with lattice spacings 0.076 fm and 0.04 fm, using physical pion and kaon masses. They employed optimized boosted sources with Gaussian-smeared quark fields in Coulomb gauge, the All Mode Averaging technique for statistics, and two-state fits to the ratio of three-point to two-point functions to extract ground-state bare matrix elements, which were then converted to physical form factors using the vector current renormalization factor Z_V.

## Key numbers

- Pion form factor F_π⁺(Q²) values from the a = 0.076 fm lattice: 0.252(5) at Q² = 1.56 GeV², 0.176(8) at 2.34 GeV², 0.120(9) at 3.90 GeV², 0.073(11) at 6.50 GeV², 0.056(10) at 9.35 GeV²
- Kaon form factor F_K⁺(Q²) values from the a = 0.076 fm and 0.04 fm lattices: 0.967(14) at Q² = 0.025 GeV², 0.207(3) at 2.34 GeV², 0.135(2) at 3.95 GeV², 0.088(1) at 6.50 GeV², 0.063(3) at 9.35 GeV², 0.025(4) at 23.45 GeV², 0.019(3) at 28.12 GeV²
- Maximum momentum transfer reached: 9.61 GeV² for pion (a = 0.076 fm lattice) and 28.15 GeV² for kaon (a = 0.04 fm lattice)
- Pion mass: 140(1) MeV for a = 0.076 fm ensemble, 134(3) MeV for a = 0.04 fm ensemble; kaon mass: 498(1) MeV and 497(4) MeV respectively
- Ground-state kaon energy extracted from two-point functions agrees with dispersion relation E = sqrt(m_K² + |P|²) with m_K = 0.497 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of pion and kaon electromagnetic form factors, which were restricted to low Q² (Boyle 2008, JLQCD 2009, Bali 2013, Alexandrou 2021), and pion calculations up to 6 GeV² with large uncertainties using the Feynman-Hellmann method (QCDSF 2017). The results provide benchmarks for the ongoing JLab 12 GeV program (Dudek 2012, Arrington 2021) and future electron-ion colliders (Arrington 2021, Anderle 2021), and enable tests of QCD collinear factorization at NNLO (Chen 2023) by comparing with independent lattice determinations of meson distribution amplitudes (RQCD 2019, Detmold 2021, LatticeParton 2022, Gao 2022, Baker 2024, Cloet 2024).
