# Surveying exotic pentaquarks with the typical $QQqq\bar{q}$ configuration

> As a hot issue, exploring exotic pentaquarks is full of challenges and opportunities for both theorist and experimentalist. In this work, we focus on a type of pentaquark with the $QQqq\bar{q}$ ($Q=b,c$; $q=u,d,s$) configuration, where their mass spectrum is estimated systematically. Especially, our result indicates that there may exist some stable or narrow exotic pentaquark states. Obviously, our study may provides valuable information for further experimental search for the $QQqq\bar{q}$ pentaquarks. With the running of LHCb and forthcoming Belle II, we have a reason to believe that these predictions present here can be tested.

## Key claim

The authors estimate that some doubly heavy pentaquark states with the \(QQqq\bar{q}\) configuration (\(Q=b,c\); \(q=u,d,s\)) may be stable or narrow against strong decay, but they note that the model is an oversimplification and that accurate masses require further dynamical calculations, so the predictions are rough estimates rather than precise values.

## Method

The authors adopt a color-magnetic interaction (CMI) model, constructing flavor-color-spin wave functions for the \(QQqq\bar{q}\) systems using diquark-diquark-antiquark bases and diagonalizing the Hamiltonian to obtain mass splittings. They extract the effective quark masses and coupling constants \(C_{ij}\) from the masses of conventional hadrons, assuming the same quark-quark interactions across different systems, and then estimate the pentaquark masses by adding the CMI expectation values to a common effective mass term.

## Key numbers

* For the \((ccnn)^{I=1}\bar{q}\) and \((bbnn)^{I=1}\bar{q}\) states in the first class, the \(J^P = \frac{5}{2}^-\) state has a CMI expectation value of \(\frac{2}{3}(4\alpha+\beta+2\lambda+2\nu)\), where the variables \(\alpha, \beta, \lambda, \nu\) are defined in terms of the coupling constants \(C_{ij}\).
* For the \((ccnn)^{I=0}\bar{q}\) and \((bbnn)^{I=0}\bar{q}\) states in the second class, the \(J^P = \frac{5}{2}^-\) state has a CMI expectation value of \(\frac{1}{3}(3\tau-\alpha+5\beta-2\lambda+10\nu)\).
* For the \((cbnn)^{I=1}\bar{q}\) and \((cbss)\bar{q}\) states in the third class, the \(J^P = \frac{5}{2}^-\) Hamiltonian is a \(2\times2\) matrix with diagonal entries \(\frac{1}{3}[2(4\alpha+\beta+2\lambda+2\nu)]\) and \(\frac{1}{3}[5\beta+10\lambda-2\nu-\alpha-3\theta]\).
* For the \((ccns)\bar{q}\) and \((bbns)\bar{q}\) states in the fifth class, the \(J^P = \frac{5}{2}^-\) Hamiltonian is a \(2\times2\) matrix with diagonal entries \(\frac{2}{3}(4\alpha+\beta+2\lambda+2\nu)\) and \(\frac{1}{3}(3\tau-\alpha+5\beta-2\lambda+10\nu)\).

## Context

This work extends the study of doubly heavy multiquark systems, following the observation of the doubly charmed baryon \(\Xi_{cc}\) by LHCb (Aaij 2017) and the hidden-charm pentaquark-like resonances \(P_c(4380)\) and \(P_c(4450)\) (Aaij 2015). The authors use a CMI model similar to that applied to tetraquark states by Liu (2004) and relate their approach to the heavy diquark-antiquark symmetry discussed by Cohen (2006). The predictions are intended to guide experimental searches at LHCb and Belle II, and to help distinguish compact pentaquark states from baryon-meson molecules.
