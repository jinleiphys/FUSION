# On Calculation of the Neutron Charge Radius

> We show that the anomalous quark magnetic moments and relativistic effects in the nucleon wave function result in the correct value of the neutron charge radius.

## Key claim
The paper shows that including anomalous quark magnetic moments (with values close to those already found in the literature) together with relativistic effects in the nucleon wave function can reproduce the experimental neutron charge radius, but the calculation uses a simplified wave function with only one of sixteen possible spin structures and an oscillator parametrization, so the author notes this does not exclude other contributions.

## Method
The nucleon form factors were calculated in explicitly covariant light-front dynamics using the plus-component of the current, with the nucleon wave function constructed from a scalar coupling spin structure and an oscillator momentum part. The two-loop (five-dimensional) integrals were evaluated numerically, and the anomalous quark magnetic moments were introduced into the quark electromagnetic vertex analogously to the nucleon vertex, neglecting their Q² dependence.

## Key numbers
- Experimental neutron charge radius squared: \( r^2_{En} = -0.113 \pm 0.005 \, \text{fm}^2 \)
- Foldy term contribution: \( r^2_{\text{Foldy}} = -0.126 \, \text{fm}^2 \) (from neutron magnetic moment \( \mu_n = -1.91 \))
- Best-fit anomalous quark magnetic moments: \( \kappa_u = 0.13 \), \( \kappa_d = -0.11 \) (with quark mass \( m = 340 \, \text{MeV} \), oscillator parameter \( \beta = 700 \, \text{MeV/c} \))
- Calculated neutron charge radius with these parameters: \( r^2_{En} = -0.125 \, \text{fm}^2 \) (with \( r^2_1 = 0.002 \, \text{fm}^2 \), \( r^2_{\text{Foldy}} = -0.127 \, \text{fm}^2 \))
- Calculated neutron magnetic moment: \( \mu_n = -1.92 \); calculated proton magnetic moment: \( \mu_p = 3.09 \)

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations that used the Melosh wave function and found a cancellation between \( r^2_1 \) and \( r^2_{\text{Foldy}} \) in the \( 1/m^2 \) order, leading to an underestimated neutron charge radius (Isgur, 1990s; Boffi and collaborators). The author shows that with a different wave function (scalar coupling rather than Melosh) and including anomalous quark magnetic moments, the experimental value can be reproduced, in contrast to numerical calculations by Frederico (1990s) and others that still underestimated the radius. This enables further refinement by incorporating additional spin structures and exchange currents within the nucleon.
