# q-Boson approach to multiparticle correlations

> An approach is proposed enabling to effectively describe, for relativistic heavy-ion collisions, the observed deviation from unity of the intercept λ(measured value corresponding to zero relative momentum {\bf p} of two registered identical pions or kaons) of the two-particle correlation function C(p,K). The approach uses q-deformed oscillators and the related picture of ideal gas of q-bosons. In effect, the intercept λis connected with deformation parameter q. For a fixed value of q, the model predicts specific dependence of λon pair mean momentum {\bf K} so that, when |{\bf K}|\gsim 500 - 600 MeV/c for pions or when |{\bf K}|\gsim 700 - 800 MeV/c for kaons, the intercept λtends to a constant which is less than unity and determined by q. If q is fixed to be the same for pions and kaons, the intercepts λ_πand λ_K essentially differ at small mean momenta {\bf K}, but tend to be equal at {\bf K} large enough (|{\bf K}|\gsim 800MeV/c) where the effect of resonance decays can be neglected. We argue that it is of basic interest to check in the experiments on heavy ion collisions: (i) the exact shape of dependence λ= λ({\bf K}), and (ii) whether for |{\bf K}| \gsim 800 MeV/c the resulting λ_πand λ_K indeed coincide.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the deviation of the two-particle correlation intercept lambda from unity in relativistic heavy-ion collisions can be described by a q-deformed boson model, and predicts that at large pair mean momenta (above 500-600 MeV/c for pions or 700-800 MeV/c for kaons) the intercept tends to a constant less than one determined by the deformation parameter q, rather than approaching unity as expected from resonance decay explanations alone.

## Method

The authors model the system of pions or kaons produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions as an ideal gas of q-bosons, using q-deformed commutation relations with a complex deformation parameter q = exp(i theta). They compute the thermal q-deformed distribution function and the two-particle correlation function from the q-oscillator algebra, obtaining an expression for the intercept lambda as a function of the deformation angle theta, temperature T, particle mass m, and pair mean momentum |K|.

## Key numbers

* For pions (mass 139.57 MeV) at zero mean momentum, the critical temperatures corresponding to the two special values of the dimensionless variable w = beta omega are T_0 = 290.0 MeV (for w_0 = 0.481) and T'_0 = 200.5 MeV (for w'_0 = 0.696).
* At w = 2.0 (curve E) for pions with |K| = 0, the lowest temperature is T_E = 69.8 MeV.
* The intercept lambda tends to a constant less than unity when |K| is above 500-600 MeV/c for pions or above 700-800 MeV/c for kaons.
* At |K| approximately 800 MeV/c, the intercepts lambda_pi and lambda_K are predicted to coincide.
* The asymptotic limit at large w gives lambda = 2 cos(theta) - 1, with the minimum lambda on the "small deformation" interval being approximately 0.33.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier treatment in Anchishkin, Gavrilik, Iorgov (2000) of q-deformed oscillators applied to two-particle correlations, and contrasts with the commonly accepted explanation that the reduced intercept arises solely from resonance decays as discussed by Heiselberg (1996) and Wiedemann and Heinz (1997). The model enables experimental tests at SPS at CERN and RHIC at BNL to determine whether the deformation parameter q can serve as a fundamental characteristic of the hot, short-lived hadronic system, and whether the predicted asymptotic behavior of lambda at large momenta reveals memory effects from the fireball dynamics including possible quark-gluon plasma phase and hadronization.
