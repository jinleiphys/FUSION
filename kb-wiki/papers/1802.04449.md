# Pseudorapidity distribution and decorrelation of anisotropic flow within CLVisc hydrodynamics

> Studies of fluctuations and correlations of soft hadrons and hard and electromagnetic probes of the dense and strongly interacting medium require event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations of high-energy heavy-ion collisions that are computing intensive. We develop a (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic model -- CLVisc that is parallelized on Graphics Processing Unit (GPU) using Open Computing Language (OpenCL) with 60 times performance increase for space-time evolution and more than 120 times for the Cooper-Frye particlization relative to that without GPU parallelization. The pseudo-rapidity dependence of anisotropic flow $v_n(η)$ are then computed in CLVisc with initial conditions given by the A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model, with energy density fluctuations both in the transverse plane and along the longitudinal direction. Although the magnitude of $v_n(η)$ and the ratios between $v_2(η)$ and $v_3(η)$ are sensitive to the effective shear viscosity over entropy density ratio $η_v/s$, the shape of the $v_{n}(η)$ distributions in $η$ do not depend on the value of $η_v/s$. The decorrelation of $v_n$ along the pseudo-rapidity direction due to the twist and fluctuation of the event-planes in the initial parton density distributions is also studied. The decorrelation observable $r_n(η^a, η^b)$ between $v_n\{-η^a\}$ and $v_n\{η^a\}$ with the auxiliary reference window $η^b$ is found not sensitive to $η_v/s$ when there is no initial fluid velocity. For small $η_v/s$, the initial fluid velocity from mini-jet partons introduces sizable splitting of $r_n(η^a, η^b)$ between the two reference rapidity windows $η^b \in [3, 4]$ and $η^b \in [4.4, 5.0]$, as has been observed in experiment. The implementation of CLVisc and guidelines on how to efficiently parallelize scientific programs on GPUs are also provided.

## Key claim

The paper claims that while the magnitude of pseudorapidity-dependent anisotropic flow \(v_n(\eta)\) and the ratios between \(v_2(\eta)\) and \(v_3(\eta)\) are sensitive to the effective shear viscosity over entropy density ratio \(\eta_v/s\), the shape of the \(v_n(\eta)\) distributions in \(\eta\) does not depend on the value of \(\eta_v/s\); additionally, the decorrelation observable \(r_n(\eta^a, \eta^b)\) is not sensitive to \(\eta_v/s\) when there is no initial fluid velocity, but for small \(\eta_v/s\), the initial fluid velocity from mini-jet partons introduces a sizable splitting of \(r_n(\eta^a, \eta^b)\) between the two reference rapidity windows \(\eta^b \in [3, 4]\) and \(\eta^b \in [4.4, 5.0]\), as observed in experiment.

## Method

The authors developed CLVisc, a (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic model parallelized on Graphics Processing Units (GPUs) using OpenCL, and solved the second-order Israel-Stewart hydrodynamic equations with a Kurganov-Tadmor algorithm. Initial conditions were provided by the A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model, which includes energy density fluctuations both in the transverse plane and along the longitudinal direction. The pseudorapidity dependence of anisotropic flow \(v_n(\eta)\) and the decorrelation observable \(r_n(\eta^a, \eta^b)\) were computed, and the effects of shear viscosity and initial fluid velocity on these longitudinal observables were investigated.

## Key numbers

* GPU parallelization achieved a 60 times performance increase for space-time evolution and more than 120 times for the Cooper-Frye particlization relative to the same code without GPU parallelization.
* The decorrelation observable \(r_n(\eta^a, \eta^b)\) was computed for two reference rapidity windows: \(\eta^b \in [3, 4]\) and \(\eta^b \in [4.4, 5.0]\).
* The smooth particle spectra calculation used tabulated bins of \(N_Y \times N_{p_T} \times N_{\phi} = 41 \times 15 \times 48\).
* The thermal density \(n_i\) in the co-moving frame was computed numerically by one-dimensional integration over momentum from 0 to \(100T\).

## Context

This work extends prior event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations that required intensive computing resources, such as those using MPI parallelization in MUSIC (Schenke et al., 2010) and earlier GPU-accelerated hydrodynamics like SHASTA (Gerhard et al., 2012) and GPU-VH (Bazow et al., 2016). The CLVisc model enables efficient (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic simulations with longitudinal fluctuations, which are necessary to study observables like event-plane twisting and decorrelation along pseudorapidity, and it provides a fast solver for future big data analyses using machine learning and deep learning techniques in heavy-ion collisions.
