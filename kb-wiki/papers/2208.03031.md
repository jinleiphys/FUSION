# Properties of Hot Quark Matter with Neutrino Confinement In the NJL Model

> The thermodynamic characteristics of hot $β$-equilibrium three-flavor quark matter with neutrino confinement are studied in terms of the local SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, which also accounts for the 't Hooft interaction which leads to mixing of quark flavors. For different temperatures $T \in [20 ÷100]$ MeV and baryon number densities $n_B \in [0 ÷1.8]$ fm$^{-3}$ the constituent quark masses, quark condensates, and relative contributions of individual types of particles to the pressure and chemical potentials of the constituent particles are determined. In order to determine the role of neutrinos in the hot quark matter, the pressures and energies in states with and without neutrinos are compared. Keywords: hot quark matter: neutrino confinement: NJL model: equation of state.

# Properties of Hot Quark Matter with Neutrino Confinement In the NJL Model

## Key claim
The thermodynamic characteristics of hot beta-equilibrium three-flavor quark matter with neutrino confinement are studied using the local SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model including the 't Hooft interaction, but the applicability of the results for proto-quark star cores depends on whether the assumed relative lepton charge of 0.4 is justified under the specific conditions of supernova evolution.

## Method
The authors solve the gap equations for constituent quark masses in the mean-field approximation of the SU(3) NJL model with 't Hooft interaction, using a fixed ultraviolet cutoff momentum Lambda = 602.3 MeV and coupling constants G = 1.835/Lambda^2 and K = 12.36/Lambda^5. For temperatures T in [20, 100] MeV and baryon number densities n_B in [0, 1.8] fm^{-3}, they compute constituent quark masses, quark condensates, partial pressures, chemical potentials, and the equation of state for electrically neutral beta-equilibrium matter containing u, d, s quarks and all lepton species except tau leptons, with fixed lepton charges Y_{L_e} = 0.4, Y_{L_mu} = 0, and Y_{L_tau} = 0.

## Key numbers
- Temperature range studied: T in [20, 100] MeV
- Baryon number density range: n_B in [0, 1.8] fm^{-3}
- Current quark masses used: m_{0u} = m_{0d} = 5.5 MeV, m_{0s} = 140.7 MeV
- NJL model parameters: Lambda = 602.3 MeV, G = 1.835/Lambda^2, K = 12.36/Lambda^5
- Fixed lepton charge: Y_{L_e} = 0.4 (ratio of combined lepton and baryon charges)
- For n_B approximately 0.2 to 0.6 fm^{-3}, the u quark mass M_u increases by up to a factor of two when temperature rises to T = 100 MeV
- Electron chemical potential mu_e exceeds 300 MeV for n_B > 0.7 fm^{-3}
- Electron neutrino chemical potential mu_{nu_e} reaches values of 450 MeV
- For T = 20 MeV, pressures with and without neutrinos differ by approximately 25 percent; for T = 100 MeV, this difference falls below 5 percent when plotted as P = P(epsilon, T)
- Muon chemical potential becomes negative above n_B approximately 1.25 fm^{-3} at T = 20 MeV and above n_B = 0.87 fm^{-3} at T = 100 MeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier study (Hajyan and Alaverdyan, Ref. 1) which derived the equation of state, composition, and speed of sound for hot strange quark matter with neutrino confinement in the NJL model. The results contrast with those from the MIT quark bag model (Ref. 15), where the pressure as a function of energy density depends weakly on temperature; in the NJL model, this dependence is strong regardless of neutrino confinement. The findings are relevant for modeling proto-neutron star cores with quark matter, where the TOV equations determine stellar structure, and the question of whether neutrino confinement occurs depends on the detailed time-dependent supernova explosion problem.
