# Deciphering Azimuthal Correlations in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We discuss various sources of azimuthal correlations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The integral measure Phi is applied to quantify the correlations. We first consider separately the correlations caused by the elliptic flow, resonance decays, jets and transverse momentum conservation. An effect of randomly lost particles is also discussed. Using the PYTHIA and HIJING event generators we produce a sample of events which mimic experimental data. By means of kinematic cuts and particle's selection criteria, the data are analyzed to identify a dominant source of correlations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that azimuthal correlations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, as quantified by the integral measure \(\Phi_\phi\), are dominated by the rather trivial effect of transverse momentum conservation rather than by more dynamically interesting mechanisms such as elliptic flow, resonance decays, or jets, at least for the collision energies and systems studied with the PYTHIA and HIJING event generators.

## Method

The authors first used toy models to compute the contribution to the azimuthal correlation measure \(\Phi_\phi\) from elliptic flow, resonance decays, dijets, and transverse momentum conservation separately, both analytically and via Monte Carlo simulations. They then generated minimum-bias events with the PYTHIA event generator for proton-proton collisions at SPS and RHIC energies and with the HIJING event generator for nucleus-nucleus collisions (p-p, C-C, Si-Si, Pb-Pb) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 17.3\) GeV, computing \(\Phi_\phi\) for charged particles and applying kinematic cuts to identify the dominant correlation source.

## Key numbers

- The magnitude of \(\Phi_\phi\) measured in Pb-Pb collisions at 158\(A\) GeV is of order 0.01 radian.
- For the toy model where all particles come from back-to-back resonance decays, \(\Phi_\phi = \frac{1 - \sqrt{2}}{\sqrt{6}} \pi \approx -0.531\).
- The measure \(\Phi_\phi\) vanishes for a fixed correlation angle \(\Delta \phi = \pi \left(1 - \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\right) \approx 1.328\).
- In PYTHIA simulations of p-p collisions, \(\Phi_\phi\) was computed at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 6.27, 7.62, 8.73, 12.3, 17.3\) GeV (SPS) and 19.6, 62.4, 130, 200 GeV (RHIC), and was found to be negative and weakly dependent on collision energy.
- In HIJING simulations of nucleus-nucleus collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 17.3\) GeV, \(\Phi_\phi\) was almost independent of the mass number of colliding nuclei (p-p, C-C, Si-Si, Pb-Pb).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of the integral correlation measure \(\Phi\), previously applied to transverse momentum and electric charge fluctuations (Gazdzicki 1992, Anticic 2003, Alt 2004), to azimuthal correlations as suggested by Mrowczynski (1999). The study provides model simulations to interpret experimental data from the NA49 and NA61 Collaborations (Cetner 2010). The key finding that transverse momentum conservation dominates the correlation signal in PYTHIA and HIJING events enables future searches for more exotic phenomena such as critical fluctuations at phase boundaries or plasma instabilities from the early stage of collisions.
