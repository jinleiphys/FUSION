# Production rates for hadrons, pentaquarks $Θ^+$ and $Θ^{*++}$, and di-baryon $(ΩΩ)_{0^{+}}$ in relativistic heavy ion collisions by a quark combination model

> The hadron production in relativistic heavy ion collisions is well described by the quark combination model. The mixed ratios for various hadrons and the transverse momentum spectra for long-life hadrons are predicted and agree with recent RHIC data. The production rates for the pentaquarks $Θ^+$, $Θ^{*++}$ and the di-baryon $(ΩΩ)_{0^{+}}$ are estimated, neglecting the effect from the transition amplitude for constituent quarks to form an exotic state.

## Key claim

The quark combination model (QCM) can estimate upper limits for the production rates of exotic hadrons such as pentaquarks and di-baryons in relativistic heavy ion collisions, but these estimates neglect the effect of the transition amplitude for constituent quarks to form an exotic state and assume the transition amplitude as unity.

## Method

The authors used a quark combination model based on a quark combination rule where all quarks and anti-quarks are stochastically ordered in rapidity and combine into hadrons according to color-singlet and color-anti-triplet attraction. The model incorporates SU_f(3) symmetry with a strangeness suppression factor lambda_s, and the production weights for baryon multiplets satisfy the relations P_10 = P_1' and P_8 = 2P_10. The input parameters (total quark and anti-quark numbers, net quark number, and lambda_s) were determined by fitting the total charged multiplicity and the anti-proton to proton ratio from RHIC data at 130 and 200 AGeV.

## Key numbers

- At 130 AGeV, the predicted yield of Theta^+ is 1.16 per rapidity and 7.97 per event; at 200 AGeV, it is 1.30 per rapidity and 8.93 per event.
- At 130 AGeV, the predicted yield of Theta^{*++} is 1.86 per rapidity and 12.75 per event; at 200 AGeV, it is 2.08 per rapidity and 14.29 per event.
- At 130 AGeV, the predicted yield of (Omega Omega)_{0+} is 2.70 x 10^{-5} per rapidity and 1.86 x 10^{-4} per event; at 200 AGeV, it is 3.67 x 10^{-5} per rapidity and 2.52 x 10^{-4} per event.
- The ratio (Omega Omega)_{0+} / Omega^- is 6.79 x 10^{-5} at 130 AGeV and 7.20 x 10^{-5} at 200 AGeV.
- The total number of quarks and anti-quarks was determined as 7400 at 130 AGeV and 8900 at 200 AGeV, with net quark numbers of 420 and 360 respectively.
- The strangeness suppression factor lambda_s was 0.5 at 130 AGeV and 0.6 at 200 AGeV.
- The ratio of decuplet to octet baryon production weights is P_10/P_8 = 0.5, giving an experimental ratio R_exp ~ 0.3.

## Context

This work extends the quark combination model, previously applied to electron-positron and proton-proton/anti-proton collisions by Xie and collaborators, to relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC energies. The predictions for pentaquark yields agree with estimates from statistical models by Randrup (2003) and Letessier (2003). The di-baryon (Omega Omega)_{0+} is of particular interest because chiral SU(3) model calculations by Zhang and collaborators found it to be a deeply bound state with binding energy around 100 MeV, and its large strangeness makes heavy ion collisions at RHIC the best place to study its production due to enhanced strangeness production.
