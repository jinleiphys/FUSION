# Generalized parton distributions for weakly bound systems from light-front quantum mechanics

> We present generalized parton distributions for weakly bound systems on the light cone in order to build intuition about the light-front formalism. Physics at the crossover is reviewed in terms of the light-cone Fock space representation. Furthermore, we link light-cone Fock components to the equal (light-cone) time projection of the covariant Bethe-Salpeter amplitude. Continuity of the distributions arises naturally in this weak binding model.

# Generalized parton distributions for weakly bound systems from light-front quantum mechanics

## Key claim
Generalized parton distributions (GPDs) for weakly bound systems on the light cone are continuous at the crossover kinematic region \(x = \zeta\) due to simple relations between Fock components at vanishing plus-momentum fraction, but this continuity arises naturally only in the weak binding model presented; in QCD the relations between light-cone Fock components at small-\(x\) are far richer.

## Method
The author constructs a two-body bound state model in the ladder approximation, starting from a covariant Bethe-Salpeter framework and projecting onto the light cone by integrating out the light-front energy dependence. The GPD is extracted from the integrand of the electromagnetic form factor by inserting a delta function to fix the plus-momentum of the struck quark, then performing the \(k^-\) integral to express results in terms of light-front wavefunctions in light-front time-ordered perturbation theory. Contributions are computed to first order in the coupling, yielding two-body overlaps for \(x > \zeta\) and four-to-two overlaps for \(x < \zeta\).

## Key numbers
- The beam-spin asymmetry \(\Im \mathcal{M}\) is proportional to \(\frac{\sqrt{1-\zeta}}{1 - \frac{\zeta}{2}} H(\zeta,\zeta,t) - \frac{\zeta^2}{4 ( 1 - \frac{\zeta}{2}) \sqrt{1 - \zeta}} E(\zeta,\zeta,t)\), where \(\zeta\) is the skewness variable and \(t\) is the momentum transfer.
- The sum over Fock component overlaps for the imaginary part of the Compton amplitude includes a factor \(\sqrt{1 - \zeta}^{2 - n}\) for an \(n\)-body Fock component.
- The valence wavefunction \(\psi_3(\ldots,x_j=0,\ldots) = 0\) at vanishing plus-momentum fraction \(x_j = 0\) due to the kinetic term and interaction in the bound-state equation.

## Context
This work extends the light-front Fock space representation of GPDs developed by Diehl (2000) and the Bethe-Salpeter reduction scheme of Sales (1999) to demonstrate continuity properties in a simple model. The author previously applied this reduction scheme to bound-state matrix elements of the electromagnetic current (Tiburzi, 2002). The paper shows that valence quark models (such as those used by Tiburzi in 2001) are insufficient for modeling GPDs because they force the imaginary part of the Compton amplitude to vanish at the crossover, and that the popular factorized Ansatz \(H(x, \zeta=0, t; Q^2) \sim q(x;Q^2) F(t)\) using the dipole form factor is too simple. This work enables future construction of phenomenological GPD models from light-front wavefunctions, though the author notes that continuity and polynomiality conditions remain serious constraints, and the sum rule relating the zeroth moment of the GPD to the electromagnetic form factor has yet to be demonstrated from Fock component relations.
