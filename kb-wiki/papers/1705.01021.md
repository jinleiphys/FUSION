# Baryon susceptibilities from a holographic equation of state

> A black hole holographic model is used to mimic the behavior of the quark gluon plasma at finite temperature and density. This model reproduces lattice data at $μ_B\!=\!0$ and displays critical behavior at large densities. High order baryon susceptibilities are used to extract freeze-out points by comparing those susceptibilities with the corresponding net-protons distribution measured by STAR. Possible experimental signatures of the critical end point are discussed.

# Baryon susceptibilities from a holographic equation of state

## Key claim
A black hole holographic model that reproduces lattice QCD data at zero baryon chemical potential and displays a critical endpoint at large densities can be used to extract freeze-out temperatures and chemical potentials from STAR net-proton data, but the extracted freeze-out chemical potentials at low collision energies are smaller than those from traditional thermal fits, possibly because the model lacks strangeness and electric charge chemical potentials and does not include acceptance cuts.

## Method
The authors compute baryon number susceptibilities (up to fourth order) from a refined holographic black hole model by taking derivatives of the pressure with respect to baryon chemical potential. They compare the susceptibility ratios chi1/chi2 and chi3/chi2 to corresponding moments of net-proton distributions measured by STAR across collision energies sqrt(s) = 7.7 to 200 GeV, and determine freeze-out parameters as the point of closest approach between the curves in the temperature-chemical potential plane.

## Key numbers
- The ratio chi4/chi2 at muB = 500 MeV shows a peak three times larger than the ratio chi3/chi2 at the same chemical potential.
- Freeze-out parameters are extracted for collision energies sqrt(s) = 7.7 GeV and sqrt(s) = 19.6 GeV, with the freeze-out chemical potentials at these energies found to be smaller than those from traditional thermal fits.
- The model predicts chi4/chi2 values along the freeze-out line that follow the trend of experimental data at low sqrt(s) and show a hint of non-monotonic behavior.

## Context
This work extends the holographic approach of Gubser (2008) and DeWolfe (2010, 2011) by using a refined black hole model from Finazzo (2016) that better matches lattice data at zero chemical potential. It builds on the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC searching for the QCD critical endpoint, and follows the method of comparing baryon susceptibilities to experimental moments as done by Borsanyi (2014), Alba (2014), and Noronha-Hostler (2016). The results enable future studies incorporating acceptance cuts and decays, and comparison with preliminary STAR data with higher transverse momentum cuts (0.4 to 2.0 GeV) from Luo (2015).
