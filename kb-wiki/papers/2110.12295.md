# A simple model to include initial-state and hot-medium effects in the computation of quarkonium nuclear modification factor

> Quarkonium suppression is one of the more useful observables to obtain information about the hot medium created in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. In this manuscript, we discuss a simple way to implement both the initial-state effects and the hot-medium evolution, and to compute the quarkonium nuclear modification factor if the survival probability for a bound state at a given energy density is known. Based on the Glauber model, the temperature of the evolving medium and the centrality dependence of the nuclear modification factor will be analysed.

## Key claim

The paper provides a simple, analytically tractable method to incorporate both initial-state nuclear shadowing effects and hot-medium evolution into computations of the quarkonium nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\), under the condition that the survival probability for a bound state at a given energy density is already known from other calculations.

## Method

The authors use the optical Glauber model with a Woods-Saxon nuclear density profile to describe the collision geometry. Initial-state shadowing is implemented via an analytical model based on triple-Pomeron exchanges (Capella 2011), which modifies the density of binary collisions. The initial energy density of the medium is taken proportional to the pion multiplicity, which is itself computed from the shadowing-corrected Glauber model; the initial temperature is then obtained as \(T_0 \propto \epsilon_i^{1/4}\). The quarkonium nuclear modification factor is computed by integrating over the transverse plane the product of the shadowing-corrected binary collision density and a medium-induced survival probability \(S_{med}\), which depends on the local initial temperature.

## Key numbers

* For PbPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV, the inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section is taken as \(\sigma = 70\) mb.
* The Woods-Saxon parameters for lead are \(c = 6.62\) fm and \(\xi = 0.546\) fm.
* The triple-Pomeron coupling constant is \(C = 0.023\) fm\(^2\) and the Pomeron intercept is \(\Delta = 0.13\) (giving \(\alpha_P(0) = 1.13\)).
* For the most central PbPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV, the central temperature \(T_{00}\) is estimated to be 502 MeV.
* At impact parameter \(b = 0\) fm, the number of participants is \(N_{part} = 405 \pm 1\), the number of binary collisions is \(N_{coll} = (211 \pm 1) \times 10\), the initial-state-only nuclear modification factor is \(R_{AA}^{I} = 0.649 \pm 0.003\), and the ratio of average temperature to central temperature is \(\langle T_{HQ} \rangle / T_{00} = 0.954 \pm 0.002\).
* At impact parameter \(b = 10\) fm, \(N_{part} = 91 \pm 1\), \(N_{coll} = 231 \pm 2\), \(R_{AA}^{I} = 0.74 \pm 0.01\), and \(\langle T_{HQ} \rangle / T_{00} = 0.809 \pm 0.003\).

## Context

This work extends the analytical shadowing model of Capella (2011) by coupling it to a Glauber-model description of the collision geometry and a Bjorken-like evolution of the medium temperature. It provides a framework that can be combined with any existing quarkonium-medium interaction model, such as the pNRQCD-based computations of Brambilla (2016, 2020) or the gap model of Blaizot (2021), to include initial-state effects in a straightforward way. The method enables systematic studies of how shadowing modifies the centrality dependence of \(R_{AA}\) and how it interacts non-trivially with hot-medium suppression, which is relevant for interpreting LHC heavy-ion data on bottomonium and charmonium.
