# Short-range nuclear effects on axion emissivities by nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung

> The rates of axion emission by nucleon-nucleon (NN) bremsstrahlung are reconsidered by taking into account the NN short range correlations. The analytical formulas for the neutron-neutron (nn), proton-proton (pp) and neutron-proton (np) processes with the inclusion of the full momentum dependence of an one- and two- pion exchange nuclear potentials, in the non-degenerate limit, are explicitly given. We find that the two-pion exchange (short range) effects can give a significant contribution to the emission rates, and are temperature dependent. Other short range nuclear effects like effective nucleon mass, polarization effects and use of correlated wave functions, are discused as well. The trend of all these nuclear effects is to diminish the corresponding axion emission rates. Further, we estimate that the values of the emission rates calculated with the inclusion of all these effects can differ from the corresponding ones derived with constant nuclear matrix elements by a factor of $\sim 24$. This leads to an uncertainty factor of $\sim 4.9$ when extracting bounds of the axion parameters

# Short-range nuclear effects on axion emissivities by nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung

## Key claim

The inclusion of two-pion exchange (short-range) effects, simulated by rho-meson exchange, significantly reduces axion emission rates from nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung compared to calculations using only one-pion exchange potentials, and the total reduction from all short-range nuclear effects (including effective nucleon mass, polarization effects, and correlated wave functions) can change the emission rates by a factor of approximately 24 relative to calculations using constant nuclear matrix elements. This reduction is temperature-dependent and most pronounced at lower temperatures.

## Method

The authors computed axion emission rates for neutron-neutron, proton-proton, and neutron-proton bremsstrahlung processes in the non-degenerate limit using Fermi's Golden Rule, incorporating the full momentum dependence of nuclear matrix elements from both one-pion exchange and two-pion exchange (simulated by rho-meson exchange) potentials. They expressed the spin-summed squared matrix elements as a sum of a constant high-momentum term plus correction terms for one-pion and two-pion contributions, then performed multidimensional numerical integrations (including a five-dimensional integral for the two-pion correction) using Gaussian quadratures. The calculations were done for two values of the rho meson mass: the free mass of 770 MeV and an effective mass of 600 MeV appropriate for dense nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- The relative correction to the axion emissivity from one-pion exchange effects alone is approximately 30% at T ~ 75 K, while including both one-pion and two-pion exchange effects gives a correction of approximately 57% at the same temperature (for the nn/pp processes with effective rho mass 600 MeV).
- At T ~ 25 K, the combined one-pion and two-pion exchange corrections reach approximately 97% of the emissivity calculated in the high-momentum limit.
- The axion emission rates calculated with all short-range nuclear effects (full momentum dependence of one-pion and two-pion exchange, effective nucleon mass, polarization effects, correlated wave functions) can differ from those derived with constant nuclear matrix elements by a factor of approximately 24.
- This factor of 24 in emission rates translates to an uncertainty factor of approximately 4.9 when extracting bounds on axion parameters (mass, coupling constants, decay constant).
- The effective nucleon mass in dense nuclear matter at densities around 3 times nuclear saturation density can be approximately 0.5 times the rest mass, leading to an overestimate of emission rates by a factor of approximately 6 if neglected.
- The typical nucleon momentum in the relevant temperature range is (3m_N T)^(1/2), and at T ~ few MeV the momentum exchange terms are only a few times larger than the pion mass.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations (Stoica and Pastrav, 2009) that included full momentum dependence of one-pion exchange potentials for axion emission rates, by now incorporating two-pion exchange effects simulated by rho-meson exchange. It builds on the foundational calculations of Iwamoto (1984) for the degenerate limit and Brinkmann and Turner (1988) for the non-degenerate limit using constant nuclear matrix elements, as well as the work of Ericson and Mathiot (1989) who investigated short-range nuclear effects on axion emission from supernova SN1987A. The results enable more accurate bounds on axion parameters (mass, coupling constants, decay constant) from neutron star cooling observations, and quantify the systematic uncertainties introduced by nuclear medium effects that were previously estimated only qualitatively.
