# Hadron Correlation in Jets on the Near and Away Sides of High-p_T Triggers in Heavy-ion Collisions

> Correlation between trigger and associated particles in jets produced on near and away sides of high-p_T triggers in heavy-ion collisions is studied. Hadronization of jets on both sides is treated by thermal-shower and shower-shower recombination. Energy loss of semihard and hard partons traversing the nuclear medium is parametrized in a way that renders good fit of the single-particle inclusive distributions at all centralities. The associated hadron distribution in the near-side jet can be determined showing weak dependence on system size because of trigger bias. The inverse slope increases with trigger momentum in agreement with data. The distribution of associated particle in the away-side jet is also studied with careful attention given to antitrigger bias that is due to the longer path length that the away-side jet recoiling against the trigger jet must propagate in the medium to reach the opposite side. Centrality dependence is taken into account after determining a realistic probability distribution of the dynamical path length of the parton trajectory within each class of centrality. For symmetric dijets with p_T^{trig}=p_T^{assoc}(away) it is shown that the per-trigger yield is dominated by tangential jets. The average parton momentum for the recoil jet is shown to be always larger than that of the trigger jet for fixed p_T^{trig} and centrality and for any measurable p_T^{assoc}(away). With the comprehensive treatment of dijet production described here it is possible to answer many questions regarding the behavior of partons in the medium under conditions that can be specified on measurable hadron momenta.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the observed dihadron correlations in heavy-ion collisions can be quantitatively described by a recombination model that includes thermal-shower and shower-shower hadronization, with energy loss parametrized as a simple exponential damping factor proportional to path length. The key qualifier is that this description works only after subtracting ridge contributions on the near side and double-hump background on the away side, and relies on a single fitted parameter βL = 2.9 for central collisions.

## Method

The authors use the recombination model with three components: thermal-thermal (TT), thermal-shower (TS), and shower-shower (SS) recombination to describe pion production. Hard parton distributions are taken from a parametrization with shadowing for Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV, and energy loss is modeled by an exponential damping factor G(q,k,t) = q δ(q - k e^{-βt}) where βL is the quenching parameter. For near-side correlations, the trigger bias is studied by averaging over all hard-scattering points; for away-side correlations, a joint distribution F'_i(q,q') accounts for the recoil parton traveling distance L-t in the opposite direction. The per-trigger yields are computed by integrating over the trigger momentum bin and normalizing by the single-pion distribution.

## Key numbers

- βL = 2.9, the single fitted parameter used to reproduce the inclusive π⁰ distribution for 0-10% centrality Au+Au at √s = 200 GeV over the range 2 < p_T < 13 GeV/c
- Thermal parameters for 0-10% centrality: C = 23.2 GeV⁻¹, T = 0.317 GeV
- Near-side suppression factor Γ_near(p_T) saturates at 0.85 for high p_T, meaning on average only 15% of parton energy is lost on the near side
- Average ⟨βt⟩ for p_T > 4 GeV/c is between 0.18 and 0.2, implying ⟨t⟩/L ≈ 0.065 (thickness of near-side layer ~13% of L)
- ⟨k⟩ ≈ 2 p_T and ⟨q⟩ ≈ 1.6 p_T for p_T > 3 GeV/c on the near side
- Away-side suppression factor Γ_away(p_t,p_b) at the symmetry point p_t = p_b is constant at 0.24, consistent with e^{-βL/2} = e^{-1.45} ≈ 0.235
- For p_t = p_b = 4 GeV/c, ⟨k'⟩ = 4.7 ⟨k⟩, rising to approximately 9 p_t, with 76% of parton energy lost before exiting
- Inverse slope T_a of near-side associated pions increases with trigger momentum, matching data from Refs. (J. Phys. G and Phys. Rev. C) for 2 < p_a < 4 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the recombination model developed by Hwa and Yang in earlier papers (Hwa and Yang 2002, 2003, 2004) to treat dihadron correlations in heavy-ion collisions, incorporating the discovery of the ridge structure. It builds on the formalism of thermal-shower and shower-shower recombination that interpolates between thermal production at low p_T and fragmentation at high p_T. The paper enables quantitative predictions for trigger and antitrigger biases in both near-side and away-side jets, and provides a framework for calculating centrality-dependent yields that can be compared to PHENIX and STAR data. The approach contrasts with opacity expansion methods (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev 2000) and other energy-loss formalisms by using a simple exponential damping factor that is validated by fitting inclusive spectra.
