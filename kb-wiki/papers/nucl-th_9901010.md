# Spin-Polarizabilities of the Nucleon in Chiral Soliton Model with Dispersion Relation

> We calculate the spin-polarizabilities of the nucleon by means of dispersion relation, where the pion photoproduction amplitude predicted by chiral soliton model is utilized. We consider the $Nπ$ and $Δπ$ channels in the pion-photoproduction amplitude, and also evaluate the contribution of the anomalous term of the $π^0γγ$ process. In a narrow decay-width limit of the $Δ$ particle the result coincides with that in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) except for the interference part of the electric and magnetic amplitudes. A comparison with the multipole analysis is given.

## Key claim

The spin-polarizabilities of the nucleon calculated via dispersion relations using chiral soliton model pion photoproduction amplitudes coincide with heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) results in the narrow decay-width limit of the Delta particle, except for the interference part of the electric and magnetic amplitudes, which is not included in HBChPT.

## Method

The authors computed the imaginary parts of Compton scattering structure functions using unitarity with intermediate Npi and Deltapi channels, where the pion photoproduction amplitudes were taken from the chiral soliton model. They then applied fixed-t dispersion relations to extract the spin-polarizabilities gamma_1 through gamma_4 from the non-Born parts of the amplitudes, including an anomalous contribution from the pi0-gamma-gamma process via the Wess-Zumino-Witten term. The Delta resonance was treated with a finite width to avoid divergent dispersion integrals, and the narrow-width limit was taken for comparison with HBChPT.

## Key numbers

* Forward spin-polarizability from Npi-loop plus Delta-pole in HBChPT: gamma_0 = (4.44 - 3.66) x 10^{-4} fm^4 = 0.78 x 10^{-4} fm^4
* Multipole analysis yields gamma_0 = -1.34 x 10^{-4} fm^4 for the proton and -0.38 x 10^{-4} fm^4 for the neutron (Sandorfi 1994)
* Delta width expression: Gamma_Delta = (1/6pi) (G_{Delta N pi} / 2M)^2 q^3, yielding 145 MeV at q = 227 MeV
* Electric part contribution to polarizabilities: alpha^{N,e} = (e^2/4pi) (10 G_{NNpi}^2)/(192 pi M^2 m_pi), beta^{N,e} = (1/10) alpha^{N,e}
* Forward spin-polarizability from electric part: gamma_0^{N,e} = (e^2/4pi) (G_{NNpi}^2)/(24 pi^2 M^2 m_pi^2)
* Magnetic part in narrow-width limit: beta^{N,m} = (e^2/4pi) (mu_V^2/M^2)(1/Delta), gamma_0^{N,m} = -(e^2/4pi) (mu_V^2/(2M^2))(1/Delta^2)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of the forward spin-polarizability gamma_0 (Tanushi 1998) to the full set of four independent spin-polarizabilities gamma_1 through gamma_4, following the formalism of Ragusa. It builds on HBChPT calculations by Bernard et al. (1992) and Hemmert et al. (1997, 1998), and on the multipole analysis of Sandorfi (1994). The chiral soliton model provides a natural framework where the nucleon and Delta(1232) are treated as rotational states of the same soliton, in contrast to HBChPT where the Delta is included as an explicit degree of freedom. The results enable comparison between the soliton model approach and both HBChPT and experimental multipole analyses for the spin-dependent electromagnetic response of the nucleon.
