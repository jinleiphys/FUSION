# Longitudinal form factors of $A\,\leq\,10$ nuclei in a chiral effective field theory approach

> In this work, we present the elastic electron scattering longitudinal form factors of $A\le 10$ nuclei computed in a variational Monte Carlo approach. We employ the Norfolk family of local chiral interactions and a consistent electromagnetic charge operator. Our calculations are compared both to data and past theoretical evaluations. This work represents, to our knowledge, the first exact many-body calculation of longitudinal form factors in the $7\,\leq\,A\,\leq\,10$ mass range. Finally, we identify $^9$Be and $^{10}$B as candidate targets for renewed experimental interest, as they exhibit the potential to provide more stringent constraints on the theoretical models.

# Longitudinal form factors of $A \leq 10$ nuclei in a chiral effective field theory approach

## Key claim
The authors present the first exact many-body calculation of longitudinal form factors in the $7 \leq A \leq 10$ mass range using variational Monte Carlo with chiral effective field theory interactions, but note that no single Norfolk model fully captures the magnitude of the second peak in $^3$He data, and that model dependence becomes stark at higher momentum transfers for $^9$Be and $^{10}$B.

## Method
The authors solved the many-body Schrödinger equation using the variational Monte Carlo method with the Norfolk family (NV2+3) of local chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions, employing a consistent electromagnetic charge operator expanded through N3LO in chiral effective field theory. They computed the longitudinal form factor $F_L^2(q)$ for nuclei with $3 \leq A \leq 10$ by evaluating multipole contributions $C_l(q)$ from the charge operator, including one-body operators at LO and N2LO and two-body operators at N3LO. The calculations were compared to experimental data and past theoretical evaluations using different NV2+3 model classes varying in cutoff parameters, energy fitting ranges, and three-body force fitting procedures.

## Key numbers
- For $^3$He, two-body charge operators at N3LO provide corrections that lower the position of the diffraction minimum in $q$ and add strength to the subsequent peak; the N4LO corrections were found in prior work to provide on average less than 0.4% correction for momentum transfers in the range $[0,5]$ fm$^{-1}$.
- For $^7$Li, two-body current effects become sizeable beyond approximately 3 fm$^{-1}$, changing $F_L^2(q)$ by $\gtrsim 50\%$ in that region.
- For $^9$Be, the $C_2(q)$ multipole is necessary to reproduce data from Glickman (1991) in the range $1.6$ fm$^{-1} \lesssim q \lesssim 2.6$ fm$^{-1}$.
- For $^{10}$B, beyond $q \approx 3$ fm$^{-1}$, the NV2+3-IIb$^{\star}$ model predicts an $F_L^2(q)$ that is orders of magnitude smaller than the NV2+3-Ia$^{\star}$ model.
- The NV2+3 model classes use cutoffs $[R_L,R_S]=[1.2$ fm$,0.8$ fm$]$ for "soft" (class a) and $[R_L,R_S]=[1.0$ fm$,0.7$ fm$]$ for "hard" (class b) interactions.

## Context
This work extends the authors' prior studies of magnetic moments and form factors in $3 \leq A \leq 10$ systems (Chambers-Wall, 2024) to longitudinal form factors, and represents the first calculation of this observable in the $7 \leq A \leq 10$ range using accurate many-body methods with interacting nucleon degrees of freedom. The results build on previous variational Monte Carlo calculations using phenomenological potentials (Wiringa, 1991; Schiavilla, 1990) and Faddeev calculations using the Argonne $v_{18}$ plus Urbana IX interaction (Golak, 2005), while also extending hyperspherical harmonics calculations using different chiral EFT interactions (Marcucci, 2015). The work enables future direct comparisons of computed elastic electron scattering cross sections to experimental data, avoiding the kinematic-dependent Rosenbluth separation procedure, and identifies $^9$Be and $^{10}$B as candidate targets for renewed experimental interest at momentum transfers $q \gtrsim 2.8$ fm$^{-1}$ to further constrain nuclear structure models.
