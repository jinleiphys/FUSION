# Overview from lattice QCD

> 10 pages LaTeX with 5 epsf figures, Plenary talk presented at ``Nuclear and Particle physics with CEBAF at Jefferson Lab'', Dubrovnik, November 3-10, 1998 v2: cosmetic changes

## Key claim

Lattice QCD can compute the light hadron spectrum in the quenched approximation to within a few percent uncertainty, but the quenched spectrum deviates from experiment at that accuracy; first unquenched results with two flavours of light sea quarks indicate such differences.

## Method

The author reviews lattice QCD results on confinement, string breaking, glueballs, hybrid mesons, and light hadron spectroscopy, focusing on simulations that evaluate path integrals stochastically. Results are extrapolated to the continuum limit (a → 0) at fixed physical volume, then to infinite volume, and finally to the physical light quark mass using chiral perturbation theory motivated parametrisations.

## Key numbers

- The electric field distribution between SU(2) quarks separated by r = 15a ≈ 1.2 fm was measured at lattice spacing a ≈ 0.081 fm, with the scale derived from √κ = 440 MeV for the string tension.
- Penetration depth λ = 0.15(2) fm and Ginzburg-Landau coherence length ξ = 0.25(3) fm were obtained, giving λ/ξ = 0.59(13) < 1/√2, corresponding to a weak type I superconductor.
- The lightest spin-exotic hybrid meson has quantum numbers J^(PC) = 1^(-+) and a mass between 1.8 and 2 GeV.
- The lightest spin-exotic glueball state 2^(+-) lies well above 4 GeV.
- In the quenched light hadron spectrum, no mass comes out wrong by more than 10% compared to experiment.

## Context

This talk reviews lattice QCD results presented at a 1998 conference, extending the quenched spectrum calculations of the GF11 collaboration (1994) and the CP-PACS collaboration, and the quenched glueball spectrum work of Morningstar and Peardon. It enables future unquenched studies of hadron spectroscopy, glueball mixing with quark-model states, and tests of chiral perturbation theory predictions such as quenched chiral logarithms reported by the CP-PACS collaboration.
