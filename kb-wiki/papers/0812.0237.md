# Quark and Gluon Condensates in Isospin Matter

> Applying the Hellmann-Feynman theorem to a charged pion gas, the quark and gluon condensates at low isospin density are determined by precise pion properties. At intermediate density around $ f_π^2m_π$, from both the estimation for the dilute pion gas and the calculation with Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model, the quark condensate is strongly and monotonously suppressed, while the gluon condensate is enhanced and can be larger than its vacuum value. This unusual behavior of the gluon condensate is universal for Bose condensed matter of mesons. Our results can be tested by lattice calculations at finite isospin density.

# Quark and Gluon Condensates in Isospin Matter

## Key claim
While it is generally believed that density effects reduce condensates, the gluon condensate at finite isospin density can be enhanced and even become larger than its vacuum value, which is qualitatively different from the case at finite baryon density. This unusual behavior is attributed to repulsive meson-meson interactions and is claimed to be universal for Bose condensed matter of mesons.

## Method
The authors apply the Hellmann-Feynman theorem to a dilute charged pion gas described by Lee-Huang-Yang (LHY) theory to derive model-independent expressions for the quark and gluon condensates at low isospin density. For intermediate densities, they employ the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model at mean field level, solving the gap equations and density equations self-consistently to obtain the condensates as functions of isospin density.

## Key numbers
- Vacuum quark condensate: ⟨q̄q⟩₀ ≃ −(250 MeV)³
- Vacuum gluon condensate: ⟨G²⟩₀ ≃ (360 MeV)⁴, with G² ≡ αₛ/π G^a_{μν} G_a^{μν}
- Pion–pion s-wave scattering length in I=2 channel: a = m_π/(16π f_π²) ≃ 0.043 m_π⁻¹
- Density scale: ρ₀ = f_π² m_π, approximately equal to nuclear saturation density (ρ₀ ≃ 1.1 ρ_sat)
- At ρ_I = ρ₀, the quark condensate is reduced by about 45%
- In the NJL model, the gluon condensate turns to increase at ρ_I ≃ 0.6 ρ₀ and becomes larger than its vacuum value at ρ_I > 1.14 ρ₀
- In the LHY theory, the gluon condensate turns to increase at ρ_I ≃ ρ₀ and becomes larger than its vacuum value at ρ_I > 2 ρ₀
- For kaon matter, the turning density where the gluon condensate starts to increase is ρ_S ≃ f_K² m_K, with lattice QCD result m_K a_K = 0.352

## Context
This work extends the study of in-medium condensates from finite baryon density (Cohen, 1995) to finite isospin density, where lattice simulations are feasible without the fermion sign problem (Lattice, 2001). The results contradict the expectation from baryon matter that both condensates are monotonically suppressed, and instead predict a non-monotonous gluon condensate that can exceed its vacuum value. The findings enable future lattice tests at finite isospin density and suggest a connection to the BEC-BCS crossover and quarkyonic matter, with possible extension to kaon condensation.
