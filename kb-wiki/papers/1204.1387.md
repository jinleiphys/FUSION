# New Wrinkles on an Old Model: Correlation Between Liquid Drop Parameters and Curvature Term

> The relationship between the volume and surface energy coefficients in the liquid drop A^{-1/3} expansion of nuclear masses is discussed. The volume and surface coefficients in the liquid drop expansion share the same physical origin and their physical connection is used to extend the expansion with a curvature term. A possible generalization of the Wigner term is also suggested. This connection between coefficients is used to fit the experimental nuclear masses. The excellent fit obtained with a smaller number of parameters validates the assumed physical connection.

## Key claim

The volume and surface energy coefficients in the liquid drop model should not be treated as independent parameters; they share the same physical origin and are approximately equal, but this equality only becomes apparent when a curvature term proportional to \(A^{1/3}\) is included in the leptodermous expansion.

## Method

The authors fit a liquid drop formula with volume, surface, curvature, Coulomb, asymmetry, and pairing terms to 2076 experimental nuclear masses corrected for shell effects using the Strutinsky procedure as implemented by Möller et al. They performed four fits: (A) independent volume and surface coefficients without curvature; (B) forcing \(a_v = a_s\) without curvature; (C) independent volume and surface with a free curvature term; (D) forcing \(a_v = a_s\) with a curvature term. The asymmetry dependence was treated as an isospin expectation value \(\langle T^2 \rangle = |I|(|I|+2)/4\), which naturally introduces a linear Wigner-like term without an extra parameter.

## Key numbers

- Fit D (with \(a_v = a_s\) and curvature): \(a_v = a_s = 15.264(4)\) MeV, \(a_r = 3.60(3)\) MeV, \(\chi^2 = 0.54\)
- Fit A (traditional independent coefficients, no curvature): \(a_v = 15.597(7)\) MeV, \(a_s = 17.32(2)\) MeV, \(\chi^2 = 0.58\)
- Fit C (independent coefficients with curvature): \(a_v = 15.25(3)\) MeV, \(a_s = 15.17(17)\) MeV, \(a_r = 3.8(3)\) MeV, \(\chi^2 = 0.54\)
- From the geometric model, the predicted curvature coefficient is \(a_r \simeq (3/8) a_s\); using fit D values this gives a nucleon radius \(r_n = 0.566(5)\) fm, compared to the experimental value of 0.84 fm
- When the isospin exponent \(x\) was allowed to vary freely (fit with \(\langle I^2 \rangle = |I|(|I|+x)\)), the result was \(x = 1.51(3)\), corresponding to a congruence energy of 41.3(8) MeV, consistent with the 42 MeV reported by Myers and Swiatecki (1974)

## Context

This work extends the liquid drop model of Myers and Swiatecki (1974) by demonstrating that the volume and surface coefficients are physically connected and approximately equal, resolving a long-standing discrepancy in traditional fits. The curvature term, analogous to the Tolman correction for surface tension in molecular fluids, had previously yielded ambiguous results in nuclear systems (Myers and Swiatecki 1974; various later studies). The authors show that including this term stabilizes the fit and enables a consistent geometric interpretation. The results enable improved predictions for fission saddle-point configurations and for cluster yields in the Fisher model near the critical temperature.
