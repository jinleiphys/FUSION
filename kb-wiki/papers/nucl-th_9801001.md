# Competition between standard and exotic double beta decays

> We discuss the contributions of higher order terms in weak Hamiltonian to the standard two-neutrino double beta decay. The formalism for the unique first forbidden transitions has been developed, and it is shown that they can alter the two-electron energy spectrum. Yet, their effect is too small to screen the detection of exotic neutrinoless double beta decays, which are candidates for testing the physics beyond the standard model.

## Key claim

The higher-order (parity-forbidden) contributions to standard two-neutrino double-beta decay alter the two-electron energy spectrum, but their effect is too small (at the level of 10^-6) to mask the detection of exotic neutrinoless double-beta decays, and the unique forbidden transitions (J^pi = 2^-) do not interfere with the detection of neutrinoless events because their spectral deviation occurs only at low energies, not near the Q-value endpoint.

## Method

The authors developed the formalism for unique first-forbidden (U) transitions (J^pi = 2^-) in two-neutrino double-beta decay, deriving the transition amplitude and decay rate using the second-order Fermi golden rule with a non-relativistic hadronic current. They evaluated the nuclear matrix elements within the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) for three nuclei (76Ge, 82Se, 100Mo) and computed the corresponding kinematical factors and half-lives, comparing them with those from the charged Majoron (CM) model.

## Key numbers

- For 76Ge: the allowed+non-unique matrix element |M_A + M_NU| = 0.042 (dimensionless), while the unique matrix element |M_U| = 1.0 x 10^-5; the kinematical factor G_A = 5.39 x 10^-20 y^-1 and G_U = 2.10 x 10^-19 y^-1.
- The half-life ratio T_U / T_(A+NU) is approximately (RQ/4)^-4 ~ 10^6 for the unique transitions, where R is the nuclear radius and Q the decay energy.
- For 76Ge: the calculated half-life for allowed+non-unique transitions is T_(A+F) = 1.1 x 10^22 y, while for unique transitions T_U = 4.7 x 10^28 y.
- For the charged Majoron model with M_+ -> infinity, the half-life for 76Ge is T_CM = 1.6 x 10^25 y; with M_+ ~ M_- (M = 100 MeV), T_CM = 1.3 x 10^29 y.
- The experimental half-life limit for neutrinoless double-beta decay in 76Ge improved from T_0nu > 2 x 10^16 y (1952) to T_0nu > 1.2 x 10^25 y (1998).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of non-unique forbidden transitions (Barbero 1995) by developing the formalism for unique (J^pi = 2^-) transitions in two-neutrino double-beta decay, which had not been done before. It contradicts the assumption of Civitarese and Suhonen (1996) that unique transitions contribute only quadratically and are negligible, by providing the full derivation and numerical evaluation. The results enable experimentalists to confidently distinguish standard physics backgrounds from exotic decays such as neutrinoless double-beta decay and Majoron-emitting decays, particularly for the GENIUS project and other upcoming high-sensitivity experiments.
