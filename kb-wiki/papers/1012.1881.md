# Evaluating Results from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider with Perturbative QCD and Hydrodynamics

> We review the basic concepts of perturbative quantum chromodynamics (QCD) and relativistic hydrodynamics, and their applications to hadron production in high energy nuclear collisions. We discuss results from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) in light of these theoretical approaches. Perturbative QCD and hydrodynamics together explain a large amount of experimental data gathered during the first decade of RHIC running, although some questions remain open. We focus primarily on practical aspects of the calculations, covering basic topics like perturbation theory, initial state nuclear effects, jet quenching models, ideal hydrodynamics, dissipative corrections, freeze-out and initial conditions. We conclude by comparing key results from RHIC to calculations.

## Key claim

Perturbative QCD and relativistic hydrodynamics together explain a large amount of experimental data from the first decade of RHIC running, but the authors note that some questions remain open, and the review emphasizes that a consistent description of leading parton energy loss has not yet been achieved despite its apparent simplicity.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework of collinear factorization in perturbative QCD for hard probes (high-transverse-momentum hadrons, jets, and photons) and the formalism of ideal and viscous relativistic hydrodynamics for bulk particle production. It then applies these tools to interpret key RHIC observables including single particle spectra, elliptic flow, hadron correlations, and direct photon measurements, comparing experimental data to calculations from leading-order pQCD and (2+1)- and (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic models with various initial conditions and freeze-out procedures.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density in central head-on collisions at top RHIC energy has a conservative lower bound of approximately 3 GeV/fm^3, above the estimated critical energy density for the phase transition.
- The bulk of produced particles at RHIC have transverse momenta P_T smaller than approximately 2 GeV/c and show signatures of collective behavior.
- Hydrodynamics describes more than 98% of particles in a collision.
- Hadrons with transverse momenta of 5 GeV/c or more in elementary p+p or p+anti-p collisions are created through single hard scattering of two partons.
- The saturation scale Q_s for gold nuclei at RHIC energies is estimated to be smaller than 2 GeV.
- Perturbative QCD methods work down to Q^2 approximately 1 GeV^2 in some applications.
- Parton distribution parameterizations are available in a range of about 10^{-5} < x < 0.5 for protons and neutrons.

## Context

This review extends the line of work applying pQCD factorization (as developed by Mueller (1981), Ellis (1982), Collins (1985, 1989), and Qiu (1990)) and relativistic hydrodynamics to heavy ion collisions, building on earlier hydrodynamic modeling that used (2+1)-dimensional ideal fluid dynamics with simple initial conditions. The paper enables quantitative measurements of the QCD equation of state, the order of the phase transition, the existence of a critical point, and shear viscosities, while noting that measurement of bulk viscosity and relaxation times remains elusive.
