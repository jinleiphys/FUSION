# Thermodynamic and Transport Properties of Matter Formed in $pp$, $p$-Pb, Xe-Xe and Pb-Pb Collisions at the Large Hadron Collider using Color String Percolation Model

> To have a better understanding of the matter formed in ultra-relativistic collisions, using the Color String Percolation Model (CSPM), we have estimated initial energy density, mean free path, squared speed of sound, shear viscosity to entropy density ratio ($η/s$), bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio and bulk modulus for $pp$, $p$-Pb, Xe-Xe and Pb-Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). These observables are studied as a function of final state charged particle multiplicity density and initial state percolation temperature. The results from this work are compared with the results from well known QCD models and with the transport properties of matter found in day-to-day life. These observables allow us to conclude an array of important findings about the matter formed in high energy collisions at the LHC. This work gives a threshold of charged particle multiplicity $\langle dN_{ch}/dη\rangle \simeq$ 20, after which there are indications of change in the dynamics of the systems. The critical initial energy density and temperature as estimated by lattice QCD, are observed to be achieved for events with final state multiplicity $\langle dN_{ch}/dη\rangle \geq$ 20. In addition, for these high-multiplicity events, the estimated $η/s$ are comparable with that is observed for heavy-ion collisions, which is an indication of a strongly coupled Quark Gluon Plasma. This makes LHC high-multiplicity $pp$ collision events as probable candidates for the search of QGP droplets.

## Key claim

Using the Color String Percolation Model (CSPM), the authors find that for events with charged particle multiplicity density ⟨dNch/dη⟩ ≥ 20, the estimated shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s) becomes comparable to that observed in heavy-ion collisions, suggesting that high-multiplicity pp collisions at the LHC may produce strongly coupled Quark-Gluon Plasma droplets, but only above this multiplicity threshold, which the paper identifies as the point where system dynamics change.

## Method

The authors fit the softer part (0.12–1.0 GeV/c) of transverse momentum spectra from pp, p-Pb, Xe-Xe, and Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies using the Color String Percolation Model to extract the color suppression factor F(ξ) and percolation density parameter ξ. From these, they compute initial percolation temperature, initial energy density via Bjorken 1-D hydrodynamics, mean free path, shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, squared speed of sound, bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio, and bulk modulus as functions of charged particle multiplicity density normalized by nuclear overlap area and of initial percolation temperature normalized by critical temperature.

## Key numbers

- Critical percolation density: ξc ≈ 1.2
- Critical/hadronization temperature: Tc = 167.7 ± 2.8 MeV (from Becattini (2010))
- Threshold charged particle multiplicity: ⟨dNch/dη⟩ ≈ 20, above which indications of change in system dynamics appear
- Squared speed of sound approaches the ideal massless gas limit of cs² = 1/3 for most central heavy-ion collisions
- Mean free path saturates around 1 fm at high charged particle multiplicity density
- η/s values for high-multiplicity events approach the KSS bound (≈ 1/4π) and are comparable to strongly interacting QGP predictions from Hirano (2005)

## Context

This work extends the Color String Percolation Model framework previously applied by Braun (2015) and Sahoo (2017) to study thermodynamic and transport properties across multiple collision systems at the LHC. The results are compared with lattice QCD predictions from Karsch (2007) and Bazavov (2009), the excluded volume hadron resonance gas model from Tiwari (2011), and the AdS/CFT conjecture from Kovtun (2004). The finding that high-multiplicity pp collisions produce η/s values comparable to heavy-ion collisions supports the possibility of QGP droplet formation in small systems, enabling further searches for QGP signatures in pp collisions at the LHC.
