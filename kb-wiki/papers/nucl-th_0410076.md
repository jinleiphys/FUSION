# Solving the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation

> We develop an advanced method of solving homogeneous and inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equations by using the expansion over the complete set of 4-dimensional spherical harmonics. We solve Bethe-Salpeter equations for bound and scattering states of scalar and spinor particles for the case of one meson exchange kernels. Phase shifts calculated for the scalar model are in agreement with the previously published results. We discuss possible manifestations of separability for one meson exchange interaction kernels.

# Solving the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation

## Key claim
The method of expanding the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude and interaction kernel over a complete set of 4-dimensional spherical harmonics can be extended from bound states to scattering states (inhomogeneous equations) for both scalar and spinor particles, provided the Wick rotation is performed and the resulting 1-dimensional integral equations are solved numerically. The authors claim that the approach shows rapid convergence and that the resulting solutions exhibit a separability property that can be represented by simple analytical fitting functions, though this separability is demonstrated only for the one meson exchange kernel in ladder approximation.

## Method
The authors solve the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for scalar and spinor particles by first performing a Wick rotation to Euclidean space, then expanding the amplitude and kernel over 4-dimensional hyperspherical harmonics (Gegenbauer polynomials and spherical harmonics). This reduces the 4-dimensional integral equation to an infinite set of 1-dimensional integral equations, which are truncated after a few terms and solved numerically using Gaussian quadrature to convert the integrals into a system of linear equations. For the spinor case in the $^1S_0$ channel, a new set of spin-angular matrices is introduced to enable analytical Gegenbauer decomposition, and the resulting scalar coefficients are transformed back to the standard physical basis.

## Key numbers
- Coupling constant: $g^2/(4\pi) = 4\pi$, with $m = \mu = 1$ GeV and $\hat p = 0.77$ GeV/c for the scalar model numerical calculations.
- For the scalar homogeneous equation with $l=0$, bound state mass $M = 1.9$ GeV, $m = 1$ GeV, $\mu = 0.1$ GeV; the leading coefficient $c_0^0 = 0.17904$ with $\beta_{00} = 0.39736$ GeV.
- For the spinor case: $m = 0.938$ GeV, $\mu = 0.14$ GeV, $\sqrt{s} = 2m + 0.1$ GeV.
- Phase shifts are calculated for $l=0$ and $l=1$ for coupling constant values $\lambda = 0.7, 1, 3, 5, 7$ (where $g^2/(4\pi) = 4\pi\lambda$); for $\lambda = 0.7$ there are no bound states, for $\lambda = 1, 3, 5$ there is one bound state, and for $\lambda = 7$ there are two bound states in the $l=0$ channel.
- Separable kernel parameters (rank 3) for the scalar homogeneous equation: $\lambda_{11} = 0.19613$ GeV$^5$, $\lambda_{22} = 0.13734$ GeV$^5$, $\lambda_{33} = -0.01644$ GeV$^5$, $\lambda_{12} = -0.09675$ GeV$^5$, $\lambda_{13} = 0.00236$ GeV$^5$, $\lambda_{23} = -0.00034$ GeV$^5$.

## Context
This work extends the hyperspherical expansion method previously applied to bound states of scalar particles by the same group (fb-tjon, 2004) to inhomogeneous (scattering) equations for both scalar and spinor particles. It also generalizes the approach of Tjon (pr-tjon) who treated the inhomogeneous BS equation for spinor particles with a 2-dimensional Gaussian mesh, which the authors argue can suffer from under- or overestimation due to the mapping procedure. The results agree with those of Tjon (pr-tjon) for phase shifts and with the authors' own earlier one-iteration approximation results (our_brkp) for the spinor case. The method enables calculation of half-off-shell T-matrices needed for describing final state interactions in processes such as charge-exchange reactions on deuteron, deuteron break-up, and electro- and photo-disintegration of deuteron. The authors also suggest a general form for constructing separable kernels based on the observed fitting properties of the solutions.
