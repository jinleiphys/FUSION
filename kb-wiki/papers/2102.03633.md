# High Energy Behaviour of Light Meson Photoproduction

> We evaluated recent CLAS Collaboration measurements for the $90^\circ$ meson photoproduction off the nucleon using a tagged photon beam spanning the energy interval $s = 3 - 11$ GeV$^2$. The results are compared with the "Quark Counting Rules" predictions.

## Key claim

The authors evaluate whether the Quark Counting Rules (QCR) prediction of $d\sigma/dt \propto s^{-7}$ for light meson photoproduction at $90^\circ$ holds for CLAS data in the energy range $s = 3 - 11$ GeV$^2$, finding general consistency but noting that the QCR requires higher energies and larger $|t|$ and $|u|$ before it can provide a valid description, with oscillations observed at low energies indicating limitations.

## Method

The authors compiled differential cross section measurements from the CLAS Collaboration for 14 light meson photoproduction reactions off the nucleon at a meson production angle of $90^\circ$ in the center-of-mass frame, spanning $s = 3 - 11$ GeV$^2$. They performed power-law fits of the form $d\sigma/dt \propto s^{-(n-2)}$ to extract the exponent $(n-2)$ for each reaction, and compared these values to the QCR prediction of $(n-2) = 7$ for the case where the photon is treated as a point-like particle with one constituent. For the $\gamma p \to \pi^+ n$ analysis, they also included JLab Hall A and SLAC measurements.

## Key numbers

* For $\gamma p \to \pi^0 p$ over $s = 5.9 - 11.1$ GeV$^2$ and $|t| = 2.1 - 4.7$ GeV$^2$, the best-fit power is $(n-2) = 6.89 \pm 0.26$.
* For $\gamma p \to \pi^+ n$ over $s = 6.3 - 14.9$ GeV$^2$ and $|t| = 2.3 - 6.6$ GeV$^2$, the best-fit power is $(n-2) = 7.14 \pm 0.22$.
* For $\gamma n \to \pi^- p$ over $s = 4.0 - 11.3$ GeV$^2$ and $|t| = 0.2 - 4.6$ GeV$^2$, the best-fit power is $(n-2) = 7.29 \pm 0.14$.
* For $\gamma p \to \eta p$ over $s = 3.2 - 9.6$ GeV$^2$ and $|t| = 0.6 - 3.8$ GeV$^2$, the best-fit power is $(n-2) = 7.02 \pm 0.16$.
* For $\gamma p \to \eta' p$ over $s = 4.2 - 9.3$ GeV$^2$ and $|t| = 0.8 - 2.6$ GeV$^2$, the best-fit power is $(n-2) = 6.92 \pm 0.22$.
* For $\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda$ over $s = 4.0 - 8.0$ GeV$^2$ and $|t| = 0.3 - 2.9$ GeV$^2$, the best-fit power is $(n-2) = 7.28 \pm 0.06$.
* For $\gamma p \to K^+ \Sigma^0$ over $s = 5.2 - 8.0$ GeV$^2$ and $|t| = 0.3 - 2.8$ GeV$^2$, the best-fit power is $(n-2) = 7.12 \pm 0.21$.
* For $\gamma p \to \omega p$ over the full range $s = 3.5 - 8.1$ GeV$^2$ and $|t| = 0.3 - 2.9$ GeV$^2$, the best-fit power is $(n-2) = 6.80 \pm 0.11$; for the higher energy range $s = 5.0 - 8.1$ GeV$^2$, the best-fit power is $(n-2) = 8.80 \pm 0.06$.
* For $\gamma p \to \rho^0 p$ over $s = 7.0 - 8.0$ GeV$^2$ and $|t| = 2.3 - 2.9$ GeV$^2$, the best-fit power is $(n-2) = 7.9 \pm 0.3$.
* For $\gamma p \to \phi p$ over $s = 4.0 - 7.5$ GeV$^2$ and $|t| = 0.6 - 2.4$ GeV$^2$, the best-fit power is $(n-2) = 6.86 \pm 0.22$.
* The average value for pseudoscalar-meson photoproduction is $<(n-2)> = 7.06 \pm 0.15$.

## Context

This work extends the study of Quark Counting Rules (Brodsky and Farrar 1973; Matveev, Muradyan, and Tavkhelidze 1973) to light meson photoproduction using the extensive CLAS dataset from Jefferson Lab, building on earlier SLAC measurements by Anderson et al. (1976) and previous CLAS analyses by Battaglieri et al. (2001, 2002) and Dey (2014). The results enable future tests at higher energies with the JLab12 program and the Electron-Ion Collider, and highlight the need for more data on vector-meson photoproduction (especially $\rho$ and $\omega$) over a wider energy range to clarify the role of meson spin and helicity conservation violations.
