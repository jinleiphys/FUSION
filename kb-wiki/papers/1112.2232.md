# Nucleon to Delta electromagnetic transition in the Dyson-Schwinger approach

> We study the N-Delta-gamma transition in the Dyson-Schwinger approach. The nucleon and Delta baryons are treated as quark-diquark bound states, where the ingredients of the electromagnetic transition current are computed self-consistently from the underlying dynamics in QCD. Although our approach does not include pion-cloud effects, we find that the electric and Coulomb quadrupole form-factor ratios R_EM and R_SM show good agreement with experimental data. This implies that the deformation from a spherical charge distribution inside both baryons can be traced back to the appearance of p waves in the nucleon and Delta bound-state amplitudes which are a consequence of Poincare covariance. On the other hand, the dominant transition amplitude, i.e. the magnetic dipole transition form factor, underestimates the data by ~25% in the static limit whereas agreement is achieved at larger momentum transfer, which is consistent with missing pion-cloud contributions. We furthermore find that the static properties of the form factors are not very sensitive to a variation of the current-quark mass.

## Key claim

The electric and Coulomb quadrupole form-factor ratios R_EM and R_SM show good agreement with experimental data despite the absence of pion-cloud effects, implying that the deformation from a spherical charge distribution in both baryons originates from p waves in the nucleon and Delta bound-state amplitudes as a consequence of Poincare covariance; however, the magnetic dipole transition form factor underestimates experimental data by approximately 25% in the static limit, consistent with missing pion-cloud contributions.

## Method

The nucleon and Delta baryons were treated as quark-diquark bound states within the Dyson-Schwinger approach, using a rainbow-ladder truncation for the quark-quark kernel that describes a dressed gluon exchange between quarks. The electromagnetic transition current was computed self-consistently from the underlying QCD dynamics, with the quark propagator, diquark amplitudes, and baryon bound-state amplitudes all obtained from solutions of their respective Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations. The effective coupling used as input was taken from Maris and Tandy (1999) with an infrared scale Lambda = 0.72 GeV and a width parameter eta varied in the range 1.6 to 2.0.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic dipole transition form factor underestimates experimental data by approximately 25% in the static limit (Q^2 = 0).
- The infrared scale Lambda was set to 0.72 GeV via the experimental pion decay constant.
- The width parameter eta was varied in the range 1.6 to 2.0 to reflect model uncertainty.
- Experimental data for the N to Delta gamma transition exist up to Q^2 approximately 8 GeV^2.
- The electric and Coulomb quadrupole form-factor ratios R_EM and R_SM show good agreement with experimental data despite the absence of pion-cloud effects.

## Context

This work extends previous quark-diquark investigations of nucleon and Delta baryon masses and form factors by Eichmann, Nicmorus, and collaborators (2008-2011), completing the study of quark-core contributions to these properties. The approach contrasts with constituent quark models where d waves are required to produce nonzero quadrupole ratios (Becchi 1965, Isgur 1981), and with chiral effective field theory (Pascalutsa 2005, Gail 2005), dynamical reaction models such as the Sato-Lee model (Sato 2000), and lattice QCD calculations (Alexandrou 2007, 2010). The results enable future inclusion of pion-cloud effects to resolve the remaining 25% discrepancy in the magnetic dipole transition at low momentum transfer.
