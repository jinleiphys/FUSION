# Parity-violating nucleon-nucleon interactions: what can we learn from nuclear anapole moments?

> Knowledge about parity-violating effects both from theory and experiment is reviewed. Further information that could be obtained from measurements of nuclear anapole moments is discussed.

## Key claim

Knowledge about parity-violating nucleon-nucleon interactions from both theory and experiment is reviewed, and the paper argues that measurements of nuclear anapole moments could provide valuable information on the strength of the neutron-nucleus parity-violating force, which is currently not directly determined; however, the author notes that medium effects in complex nuclei may complicate the interpretation, and the comparison of anapole moment measurements with other data could help quantify these corrections.

## Method

The paper reviews the meson-exchange description of parity-violating nucleon-nucleon forces, focusing on single-meson exchanges (pi, rho, omega) and two-pion exchange contributions, and discusses phenomenological approaches based on five S-P transition amplitudes extended to include a sixth parameter (the h^1_pi coupling). It then examines existing experimental data on parity-violating asymmetries in pp scattering at 13.6, 45, and 221 MeV, radiative transitions in complex nuclei (F-19, K-41, Lu-175, Ta-181), and the F-18(0- to 1+) transition, extracting fitted values for the strength parameters X_pp, X_N^p, and the isovector constraint on X_N^p - X_N^n.

## Key numbers

*   X_pp ≈ 0.9 × 10^{-6}: fitted strength of the parity-violating pp force, derived from measurements of the parity-violating asymmetry A_L in pp scattering at 13.6 and 45 MeV.
*   X_N^p ≈ 3.4 × 10^{-6}: fitted strength of the proton-nucleus parity-violating force, obtained from analysis of parity-violating effects in odd-proton systems including p-alpha scattering and radiative transitions in F-19, K-41, Lu-175, and Ta-181.
*   |X_N^p - X_N^n| ≤ 1.4 × 10^{-6}: upper limit on the difference between proton-nucleus and neutron-nucleus parity-violating force strengths, derived from the analysis of the F-18(0- to 1+) transition.
*   From the above limit, the range for the neutron-nucleus force strength is: 2.0 × 10^{-6} ≤ X_N^n ≤ 4.2 × 10^{-6} (or 2.6 to 4.8 × 10^{-6} under an alternative assumption).
*   |h_pi^1| ≤ 1.3 × 10^{-7}: upper limit on the pion parity-violating coupling constant, assuming the isovector contribution is dominated by single-pion exchange.
*   From pp scattering analysis at 13.6, 45, and 221 MeV, the extracted couplings are: 10^7 h^{pp}_rho = -22.3 and 10^7 h^{pp}_omega = +5.2 (Carlson et al., 2002).

## Context

This review extends the earlier comprehensive review by Desplanques (1998) on parity-violating nucleon-nucleon interactions and discusses recent developments including the effective-field theory approach of Zhu et al. (2005) and the analysis of pp scattering data by Carlson et al. (2002). The paper addresses the failure of single-meson exchange models to provide a consistent description of all data, particularly the discrepancy in the P-3_2 - D-1_2 transition at 221 MeV, and proposes that measurements of nuclear anapole moments in odd-neutron nuclei (such as n-alpha scattering) could provide direct information on the neutron-nucleus force strength X_N^n, complementing existing data from odd-proton systems like Cs-133 (Wood et al., 1997).
