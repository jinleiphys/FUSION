# The Concealed Voter Model is in the Voter Model universality class

> The Concealed Voter Model (CVM) is an extension of the original Voter Model, where two different opinions compete until consensus is reached. In the CVM, agents express an opinion not only publicly, they also hold a private opinion, which they may disclose or change. In this paper I derive the critical exponents of both models via renormalised field theory and show that both belong to the compact directed percolation universality class.

## Key claim

The paper derives the critical exponents of both the Voter Model (VM) and the Concealed Voter Model (CVM) via renormalised field theory and claims that both models belong to the compact directed percolation universality class, with the caveat that the CVM's additional processes (externalisation and internalisation) do not alter the universality class from that of the original VM.

## Method

The author uses the Doi-Peliti path integral formalism to construct a field theory for both the VM and the CVM, starting from their master equations on a d-dimensional lattice. The action functional is derived in the continuum limit, and the critical exponents are calculated via renormalisation group methods using dimensional regularisation at d=2-epsilon, with the renormalisation of couplings determined by summing loop diagrams exactly. The survival probability is computed from tree-level diagrams and then renormalised to obtain the asymptotic scaling.

## Key numbers

- The expected number of particles at criticality scales as n(t) proportional to t^eta with eta = 0 in all dimensions
- The mean square spread of particles scales as R^2(t) proportional to t^z with z = 1 in all dimensions
- The survival probability exponent delta = 1 - epsilon/2 for dimensions d less than 2, where epsilon = 2 - d
- The survival probability exponent delta = 1 for dimensions d greater than or equal to 2
- The critical dimension is d_c = 2, where the coupling chi becomes marginal
- At the infrared-stable fixed point, the Wilson gamma-functions give gamma_s = gamma_chi = epsilon for epsilon greater than 0

## Context

This work extends the known results for the Voter Model universality class, previously established by Dickman (1995), Dornic (2001), and Munoz (1997), to the Concealed Voter Model introduced by Gastner (2018). The paper uses a path-integral approach similar to that of Howard (1998) for computing persistence probabilities. The result enables future studies of opinion dynamics models with private and public opinions to be classified within the same universality class as the standard Voter Model.
