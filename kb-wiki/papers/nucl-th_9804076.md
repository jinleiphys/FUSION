# Structure and decay properties of spin-dipole giant resonances within a semimicroscopical approach

> A semimicroscopical approach is applied to calculate: (i) strength functions for the charge-exchange spin-dipole giant resonances in the 208Pb parent nucleus; (ii) partial and total branching ratios for the direct proton decay of the resonance in 208Bi. The approach is based on continuum-RPA calculations of corresponding reaction-amplitudes and phenomenological description of the doorway-state coupling to many-quasiparticle configurations. The only adjustable parameter needed for the description is found by comparison of the calculated and experimental total widths of the resonance. Other model parameters used in calculations are taken from independent data. The calculated total branching ratio is found to be in reasonable agreement with the experimental value.

## Key claim

The semimicroscopical approach, which combines continuum-RPA calculations with a phenomenological description of doorway-state coupling to many-quasiparticle configurations, yields a calculated total branching ratio for the direct proton decay of the spin-dipole giant resonance in 208Bi (b = 16.1%) that is in reasonable agreement with the experimental value (b_exp = 14.1 ± 4.2%), using only the doorway-state spreading width as an adjustable parameter.

## Method

The authors performed continuum-RPA (CRPA) calculations of reaction amplitudes for the charge-exchange spin-dipole giant resonances (SDR) in 208Pb, using a phenomenological mean field and the isovector part of the Landau-Migdal particle-hole interaction. The CRPA results were Breit-Wigner parameterized to extract doorway-state parameters (energies, partial strengths, and escape widths), and then the doorway-state coupling to many-quasiparticle configurations was treated phenomenologically by introducing a spreading width, which was the only adjustable parameter, fixed by matching the calculated total width of the SDR(-) to the experimental total width of 8.4 MeV. Energy-averaged partial cross sections for proton decay were then calculated, accounting for penetrability changes over the broadened resonances, and branching ratios were obtained by integrating these cross sections over the excitation energy interval.

## Key numbers

- Total branching ratio for direct proton decay of SDR(-) in 208Bi: b = 16.1%, compared to experimental value b_exp = 14.1 ± 4.2% (Akimune et al., 1995).
- Total proton width of SDR(-): calculated Gamma_tot_up = 1.35 MeV, compared to experimental value Gamma_tot_up_exp = 1.18 ± 0.35 MeV (Akimune et al., 1995).
- Experimental total width of SDR(-) used to fix the spreading width: Gamma_exp = 8.4 MeV (Akimune et al., 1995).
- Doorway-state spreading width determined for the chosen interval (omega_1(-) = 17 MeV, omega_2(-) = 35 MeV): Gamma_down = 4.7 MeV.
- Calculated mean excitation energy of SDR(-) in 208Bi: omega_m(-) = 23.1 MeV, compared to experimental value omega_exp(-) = 21.1 ± 0.8 MeV (Akimune et al., 1995).
- Calculated mean excitation energy of SDR(+) in 208Tl: omega_m(+) = 19.2 MeV.
- Strength of the isovector spin-isospin part of Landau-Migdal interaction: g' = 0.76 (dimensionless, multiplied by 300 MeV fm^3).
- Strength of the isovector non-spin part of Landau-Migdal interaction: f' = 1.0 (dimensionless, multiplied by 300 MeV fm^3).
- For J^pi = 0-: one doorway state exhausts 85% of the total strength.
- For J^pi = 1-: one doorway state exhausts 81% of the total strength.
- For J^pi = 2-: eight doorway-state resonances exhaust 91% of the total strength.

## Context

This work extends the semimicroscopical approach previously applied to the Gamow-Teller resonance (GTR) by Moukhai et al. (1995) and Chekmurur et al. (1995) to the spin-dipole giant resonance (SDR) in 208Bi, and refines the earlier GTR calculations. The calculated total branching ratio for SDR(-) proton decay is compared to experimental data from Akimune et al. (1995), who also provided the GTR partial proton widths used for comparison. The results enable future detailed comparisons with experimental partial proton branching ratios and separated SDR spin components, and provide a predicted excitation energy for the SDR(+) in 208Tl to guide experimental searches.
