# Nuclear mean field from chiral pion-nucleon dynamics

> Using the two-loop approximation of chiral perturbation theory, we calculate the momentum and density dependent single particle potential of nucleons in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter. The contributions from one- and two-pion exchange diagrams give rise to a potential depth for a nucleon at rest of $U(0,k_{f0}) = -53.2 $MeV at saturation density. The momentum dependence of the real part of the single particle potential $U(p,k_{f0})$ is non-monotonic and can be translated into a mean effective nucleon mass of $\bar M^* \simeq 0.8 M$. The imaginary part of the single particle potential $W(p,k_f)$ is generated to that order entirely by iterated one-pion exchange. The resulting half width of a nucleon hole-state at the bottom of the Fermi sea comes out as $W(0,k_{f0})=29.7 $MeV. The basic theorems of Hugenholtz-Van-Hove and Luttinger are satisfied in our perturbative two-loop calculation of the nuclear mean field.

## Key claim

Using the two-loop approximation of chiral perturbation theory, the authors calculate the momentum- and density-dependent single particle potential of nucleons in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter, finding that chiral one- and two-pion exchange alone can reproduce realistic nuclear binding, saturation, and single particle properties with only one adjustable cut-off parameter. The abstract glosses over the fact that the calculation is perturbative and restricted to momenta below about 300 MeV, and that higher-order contributions remain unexplored.

## Method

The single particle potential (real part U and imaginary part W) was derived from the nuclear matter energy density functional by adding a test nucleon of fixed momentum to the filled Fermi sea, using the two-loop approximation of chiral perturbation theory. Contributions from one-pion exchange (Fock diagram), iterated one-pion exchange (Hartree and Fock diagrams), and irreducible two-pion exchange were computed analytically, with power divergences regularized by a single momentum cut-off scale. The calculation was performed for isospin-symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density, with the cut-off fine-tuned to reproduce the empirical binding energy per particle.

## Key numbers

- Potential depth for a nucleon at rest at saturation density: U(0,k_f0) = -53.2 MeV
- Mean effective nucleon mass: M* = 0.82 M (from maximal potential increase Delta U = 8.8 MeV in momentum interval 0 to k_f0)
- Half width of a nucleon hole-state at the bottom of the Fermi sea: W(0,k_f0) = 29.7 MeV
- Saturation density: rho_0 = 0.178 fm^{-3} (Fermi momentum k_f0 = 272.7 MeV)
- Binding energy per particle: -E(k_f0) = 15.26 MeV
- Nuclear matter compressibility: K = 255 MeV
- Cut-off scale: Lambda = 646.3 MeV
- Asymmetry energy (from prior work): A_0 = 33.8 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous chiral perturbation theory treatment of nuclear matter (Kaiser, Fritsch, Weise, 2001) from bulk properties to single particle properties, demonstrating that the same framework reproduces the empirical optical model potential depth (about -52 MeV from phenomenological fits) and effective nucleon mass (0.7-0.8 M from shell model analyses). The results are compared with Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations using the Paris NN-potential (Grange, 1987) which gave U(0,k_f0) about -64 MeV and W(0,k_f0) about 40 MeV, and with relativistic Dirac-Brueckner calculations using the Bonn-A potential (Rolf, 1994) which found a deeper potential of about -80 MeV. The approach enables systematic improvement through higher-order chiral calculations and provides a foundation for understanding nuclear mean field from fundamental QCD-based dynamics.
