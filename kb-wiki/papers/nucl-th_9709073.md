# Nuclear kaon dynamics

> An effective low energy Lagrangian density is applied to nuclear $K^-$-dynamics. The free parameters, local s-wave couplings and SU(3)-symmetry constrained range terms are adjusted to describe elastic and inelastic $K^-$-nucleon scattering data. The propagation and decay of the $Λ(1405)$-resonance and the $Λ(1405)$-nucleon hole state is studied self consistently with respect to the $K^-$-propagation in isospin symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a self-consistent treatment of the kaon propagator and the Λ(1405) resonance in nuclear matter is necessary because the Λ(1405) mass shift results from two competing effects, repulsive Pauli blocking and attractive feedback from a decreased kaon mass, and that previous work (Koch, Waas1, Waas2) that omitted this self-consistency gave quantitatively different predictions for the K⁻ spectral density.

## Method

The author constructs an effective low-energy Lagrangian density with local s-wave couplings and SU(3)-symmetry constrained range terms, then fits the free parameters to elastic and inelastic K⁻-nucleon scattering data using a coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation in the I=0 and I=1 channels. The kaon self energy in nuclear matter is evaluated in the nucleon gas approximation, with the in-medium kaon-nucleon scattering amplitude computed self-consistently by replacing the vacuum kaon-nucleon loop with an in-medium loop that uses the dressed kaon propagator. The coupled set of integral equations for the kaon self energy and the scattering amplitude is solved iteratively until convergence.

## Key numbers

- K⁺-nucleon scattering lengths: a_{K⁺N}^{(0)} ≈ 0.02 fm and a_{K⁺N}^{(1)} ≈ -0.32 fm (from Martin)
- At nuclear saturation density (k_F ≈ 265 MeV), the K⁺-mass shift increases from 28 MeV to 35 MeV when the next-to-leading order k_F⁴ correction is included (about 20% increase)
- K⁻-nucleon scattering lengths from fit: a_{K⁻N}^{(I=0)} ≈ (-1.76 + i 0.60) fm and a_{K⁻N}^{(I=1)} ≈ (0.35 + i 0.69) fm
- At k_F = 300 MeV, self-consistent calculation gives Δ m_{K⁻} ≈ -140 MeV and Γ_{K⁻} ≈ 35 MeV, compared to Δ m_{K⁻} ≈ -141 MeV and Γ_{K⁻} ≈ 31 MeV from the free-space amplitude, and Δ m_{K⁻} ≈ -123 MeV and Γ_{K⁻} ≈ 29 MeV from the Pauli-blocked amplitude
- The sum rule for the kaon spectral density holds at the 5% accuracy level
- Cutoff parameter λ = 0.7 GeV used to regularize loop functions

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Koch, Waas1, and Waas2 on K⁻ propagation in nuclear matter by treating the K⁻ state and the Λ(1405) resonance self-consistently, rather than using the Pauli-blocked amplitude alone. The paper also builds on the chiral Lagrangian approach of Kaiser for describing K⁻-nucleon scattering and the Λ(1405) resonance. The results enable more reliable predictions for kaonic atom data (Gal) and for the in-medium behavior of kaons in dense nuclear matter, though the author notes that p-wave K⁻N-interactions (Kolomeitsev) not included here may modify the results.
