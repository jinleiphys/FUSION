# Reexamining charm versus bottom quark energy loss inside a color-deconfined medium

> The general intuition that heavier partons suffer weaker energy loss inside a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) medium is critically re-examined. Within a linear Boltzmann transport model that includes both Yukawa and string types of interactions between heavy quarks and the QGP, we find that while the radiative energy loss is suppressed by the parton mass, heavier partons can experience stronger string potential scatterings with the medium. Their competition may result in less energy loss of bottom quarks than charm quarks at low transverse momentum ($p_\mathrm{T}$) but an inverse order at high $p_\mathrm{T}$. Our model calculation shows a weaker nuclear modification on bottom particles than charm particles at low $p_\mathrm{T}$, as observed by both RHIC and LHC experiments, but predicts an opposite hierarchy at high $p_\mathrm{T}$. A larger momentum space transport coefficient ($\hat{q}$) and a smaller spatial diffusion coefficient ($D_\mathrm{s}$) are found for bottom quarks than for charm quarks.

## Key claim

The general intuition that heavier partons always suffer weaker energy loss inside a quark-gluon plasma is challenged: within a linear Boltzmann transport model that includes both Yukawa and string interactions, bottom quarks may lose less energy than charm quarks at low transverse momentum but more energy at high transverse momentum, depending on the competition between the string interaction and the dead cone effect.

## Method

The authors use a (3+1)-D viscous hydrodynamic model (CLVisc) to simulate the QGP medium and a linear Boltzmann transport (LBT) model to evolve heavy quark phase-space distributions. Elastic scatterings are described by a Cornell-type potential with a Yukawa term (perturbative) and a string term (non-perturbative), with matrix elements evaluated for both heavy quark–light parton and heavy quark–gluon channels. Inelastic scatterings use a higher-twist formalism for medium-induced gluon emission, and hadronization is performed via a hybrid fragmentation-coalescence model.

## Key numbers

- Bare quark masses: 1.27 GeV for charm quarks, 4.19 GeV for bottom quarks.
- Coupling parameters: \(\alpha_\mathrm{s}=0.27\), string strength \(\sigma=0.45\) GeV\(^2\); screening mass parameters \(a=0.20\) GeV, \(b=2.0\), \(a_s=0\), \(b_s=0.10\) GeV.
- Initial proper time of QGP evolution: \(\tau_0=0.6\) fm/\(c\); freeze-out temperature: \(T_\mathrm{c}=165\) MeV.
- Crossing of \(R_\mathrm{AA}\) between \(D\) and \(B\) mesons occurs around \(p_\mathrm{T}\approx 20\) GeV/\(c\) in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=200\) GeV.
- In static medium at \(T=170\) MeV, bottom quarks lose more energy than charm quarks above \(p_\mathrm{T}^\mathrm{init}\approx 27\) GeV.
- Oscillator frequencies for hadronization: \(\omega_c=0.24\) GeV for charm hadrons, \(\omega_b=0.14\) GeV for bottom hadrons.

## Context

This work extends earlier LBT calculations that included only perturbative interactions (Cao 2016) and builds on the Cornell-type potential approach of Xing (2021). It challenges the standard mass hierarchy predicted by perturbative QCD (Cao 2017) and provides a mechanism for the crossing pattern previously seen in the CUJET calculation (Xu 2014, Xu 2015) but not clearly explained. The results enable predictions for upcoming sPHENIX data and more precise LHC measurements to constrain non-perturbative string interactions between heavy quarks and the QGP.
