# Non-conformality and non-perfectness of fluid near phase transition

> We investigate the thermodynamic and transport properties of the real scalar field theory at weak as well as strong couplings in the Hartree approximation of Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) formalism. To our surprise, we find that near phase transition, all the thermodynamic and transport properties of the simplest real scalar model at certain strong coupling agree well with the lattice results of the complex QCD system. We also demonstrate that the system near phase transition is non-conformal, and behaves as a non-perfect fluid.

## Key claim

The authors show that the simplest real scalar field theory with Z(2) symmetry breaking, when computed at strong coupling (b=30) in the Hartree approximation of the CJT formalism, reproduces all thermodynamic and transport properties of the complex QCD system near phase transition, despite the model's simplicity and the mean-field approximation's known limitations in describing critical phenomena.

## Method

The authors use the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) formalism at finite temperature in the Hartree approximation, which resums tadpole diagrams self-consistently while neglecting exchange diagrams. They solve the gap equations for the scalar condensate and mass, then compute the equation of state, speed of sound, trace anomaly, specific heat, and bulk viscosity from the effective potential and its temperature derivatives. The bulk viscosity is obtained from a low-energy theorem relating it to the specific heat and trace anomaly, with a scale parameter ω₀.

## Key numbers

- At weak coupling (b=0.3): η/s ≈ 2065, ζ/s ≈ 0, Δ/d = 0.018, c_s² = 0.312, p_T/ε_T = 0.32 at T_c
- At strong coupling (b=30): η/s = 0.146, ζ/s = 0.5–2.0, Δ/d = 0.48, c_s² = 0.03, p_T/ε_T = 0.07 at T_c
- The strong-coupling result for p_T/ε_T (0.07) matches the lattice QCD result for both pure gluon and two-flavor systems
- The strong-coupling η/s (0.146) falls within the lattice QCD range of 0.1–0.2 for purely gluonic plasma
- The strong-coupling ζ/s (0.5–2.0) agrees with the lattice result of 0.5–2.0 for pure gluon system and 0.25–1.0 for two-flavor QCD
- The trace anomaly peak Δ/d = 0.48 at T_c for b=30 is close to the lattice two-flavor result Δ/(d_G+d_Q) ≈ 0.4 at T_max

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous finding (Li and Huang, 2008) that η/s in the real scalar model behaves similarly to water, helium, and nitrogen across phase transitions. It directly compares with lattice QCD results for pure gluon systems (LAT-etas, 2005; LAT-xis-KT, 2007; LAT-xis-Meyer, 2007) and two-flavor QCD (LAT-EOS-Nf2, 2006), as well as with the PNJL model (EOS-PNJL-Weise, 2006; EOS-PNJL-Ray, 2007) and AdS/CFT black hole duals (Gubser-EOS, 2008). The results challenge the applicability of conformal AdS/CFT methods to strongly coupled QGP near T_c, since the system is highly non-conformal there, and suggest that bulk viscosity corrections and non-conformal equations of state should be included in hydrodynamic simulations of heavy ion collisions.
