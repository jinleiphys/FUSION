# Precise Determination of Electroweak Parameters in Neutrino-Nucleon Scattering

> A systematic error in the extraction of $\sin^2 θ_W$ from nuclear deep inelastic scattering of neutrinos and antineutrinos arises from higher-twist effects arising from nuclear shadowing. We explain that these effects cause a correction to the results of the recently reported significant deviation from the Standard Model that is potentially as large as the deviation claimed, and of a sign that cannot be determined without an extremely careful study of the data set used to model the input parton distribution functions.

## Key claim

The deviation from the Standard Model reported by the NuTeV collaboration may be significantly affected by higher-twist nuclear shadowing corrections that were not accounted for in the analysis; these corrections are potentially as large as the claimed deviation, but their sign cannot be determined without a careful reanalysis of the parton distribution functions used.

## Method

The authors estimate the size of nuclear shadowing corrections to the extraction of sin^2 theta_W by modeling the effect of vector meson dominance (VMD) contributions to charged current and neutral current neutrino-nucleus cross sections on iron. They derive expressions for the nuclear ratios R^nu_A and R^nubar_A that include correction factors epsilon and epsilon_bar, and use published results for the x and Q^2 dependence of the VMD shadowing effect to estimate these corrections for the kinematic range of the NuTeV experiment.

## Key numbers

- The correction factor epsilon (for neutrino charged current interactions) is approximately 0.041 at x=10^{-2} and Q^2=5 GeV^2
- At a typical NuTeV value of Q^2=10 GeV^2 and x=0.05, epsilon=0.006 and epsilon_bar=0.012
- The nuclear corrected ratios R^nu_A and R^nubar_A would be smaller than those reported by NuTeV by 0.003 and 0.006 respectively
- The total error reported by NuTeV for R^nu_A is 0.0013 and for R^nubar_A is 0.00272, meaning the shadowing corrections are larger than these quoted errors by a factor of two or three

## Context

This paper extends the work of Boros et al. (1999) on VMD contributions to nuclear shadowing in neutrino interactions and applies it to the NuTeV result reported by Zeller et al. (2002). The authors note that charge symmetry breaking effects, as discussed by Miller, Nefkens and Slaus (1990) and others, can account for about a third to a half of the deviation, while nuclear parton distribution modifications (the EMC effect) remain a challenge. The paper enables a more careful treatment of nuclear corrections in precision electroweak measurements and suggests that a "cocktail" of effects may be required before concluding the NuTeV result demonstrates a deficiency of the Standard Model.
