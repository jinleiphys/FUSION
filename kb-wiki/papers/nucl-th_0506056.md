# Charge and matter distributions and form factors of light, medium and heavy neutron-rich nuclei

> Results of charge form factors calculations for several unstable neutron-rich isotopes of light, medium and heavy nuclei (He, Li, Ni, Kr, Sn) are presented and compared to those of stable isotopes in the same isotopic chain. For the lighter isotopes (He and Li) the proton and neutron densities are obtained within a microscopic large-scale shell-model, while for heavier ones Ni, Kr and Sn the densities are calculated in deformed self-consistent mean-field Skyrme HF+BCS method. We also compare proton densities to matter densities together with their rms radii and diffuseness parameter values. Whenever possible comparison of form factors, densities and rms radii with available experimental data is also performed. Calculations of form factors are carried out both in plane wave Born approximation (PWBA) and in distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA). These form factors are suggested as predictions for the future experiments on the electron-radioactive beam colliders where the effect of the neutron halo or skin on the proton distributions in exotic nuclei is planned to be studied and thereby the various theoretical models of exotic nuclei will be tested.

## Key claim

The paper provides theoretical predictions for charge form factors of neutron-rich exotic nuclei across a wide mass range (He, Li, Ni, Kr, Sn), calculated using both plane wave Born approximation (PWBA) and distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA), with the caveat that the large-scale shell-model (LSSM) densities for light stable isotopes (⁴He and ⁶Li) do not appear reliable due to the use of harmonic-oscillator wave functions, while the predictions for heavier nuclei and neutron-rich isotopes are presented as guidance for future electron-radioactive beam collider experiments.

## Method

For light isotopes (⁶,⁸He and ¹¹Li), point-proton and point-neutron densities were obtained from large-scale shell-model (LSSM) calculations using Woods-Saxon single-particle wave function bases (except for ⁴He and ⁶Li where harmonic-oscillator bases were used). For medium and heavy isotopes (Ni, Kr, Sn), densities were calculated using deformed self-consistent mean-field Skyrme HF+BCS (HFB) method with density-dependent SG2 effective interactions in a large harmonic-oscillator basis of 11 major shells. Charge form factors were computed from these densities using both PWBA and DWBA (by numerical solution of the Dirac equation at electron energies of 250 MeV or 540 MeV), including the Sachs proton and neutron electric form factors from the Friedrich parametrization and a center-of-mass correction factor.

## Key numbers

- Calculated matter rms radius R_m for ⁶He: 2.621 fm; for ⁸He: 2.670 fm; for ¹¹Li: 2.945 fm (LSSM densities)
- Calculated charge rms radius R_ch for ⁴He: 2.153 fm (LSSM) versus experimental 1.696(14) fm from Vries (1987) and 1.695 fm from Burov (1977)
- Calculated charge rms radius R_ch for ⁶Li: 2.647 fm (LSSM) versus experimental 2.57(10) fm from Pato (2003)
- Calculated charge rms radius R_ch for ¹³²Sn: 4.740 fm (HF+BCS)
- Difference ΔR = R_m - R_p for ⁶He: 0.676 fm; for ⁸He: 0.746 fm; for ¹¹Li: 0.707 fm
- Difference ΔR = R_m - R_p for ⁷⁴Ni: 0.138 fm; for ⁹⁴Kr: 0.136 fm; for ¹³²Sn: 0.122 fm
- Neutron contribution to charge form factor of ¹³²Sn is 10-20% in the q-range 1.5 to 2 fm⁻¹
- Charge form factor calculations performed for momentum transfers q < 4 fm⁻¹

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Antonov (2004) from light halo nuclei (He, Li) to medium and heavy neutron-rich isotopes (Ni, Kr, Sn), using more realistic LSSM densities with exponential asymptotic behavior rather than the semi-phenomenological Gaussian-tail densities of Tanihata (1992) and the COSMA model used previously. The predictions are intended for future electron-radioactive beam collider experiments at facilities such as GSI (Darmstadt) and RIKEN (MUSES), where the charge form factors can test theoretical models of exotic nuclei and probe how neutron halo or skin affects proton distributions. The isotopes ⁵⁶Ni, ⁶²Ni, ⁷⁴Ni, ⁸²Kr, ⁹²Kr, ⁹⁴Kr, ¹¹⁸Sn, ¹²⁶Sn, and ¹³²Sn were chosen because they have been indicated as first candidates for charge density determination at the RIKEN electron-radioactive-ion collider, as noted by Suda (2001) and Katayama (2003).
