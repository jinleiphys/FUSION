# Leading order track functions in a hot and dense QGP

> We study the modifications to the fragmentation pattern of partons into charged particles in the presence of a hot and dense Quark Gluon Plasma. To this end, we analyze the perturbative renormalization group equations of the track functions, which describe the energy fraction carried by charged hadrons. Focusing on pure Yang-Mills theory, we compute the lowest order moments of the medium-modified track functions, which are found to be sensitive to the reduced phase space for emissions in the medium and to energy loss. We use the extracted moments to calculate the Energy Energy Correlator (EEC) on tracks in the collinear limit. The EEC on medium-evolved tracks does not differ qualitatively from the EEC on vacuum tracks despite being sensitive to the color decoherence transition and suppressing the distribution due to quenching, as seen in other jet observables.

# Leading order track functions in a hot and dense QGP

## Key claim

The medium-modified track functions do not qualitatively change the shape of the Energy Energy Correlator (EEC) distribution on tracks compared to vacuum track functions, despite being sensitive to the color decoherence transition and suppressing the distribution due to quenching; however, this conclusion is drawn under several simplifying assumptions including pure Yang-Mills theory, a homogeneous and isotropic medium, and leading-order evolution.

## Method

The authors compute the leading-order perturbative renormalization group equations for track functions in pure Yang-Mills theory, incorporating three medium effects: reduced phase space for vacuum-like emissions (modeled via two different phase space constraints), medium-induced bremsstrahlung radiation (using the BDMPS-Z/ASW approximation), and radiative energy loss (modeled via a mean energy loss distribution). They extract the lowest-order moments of the medium-modified track functions and use them to calculate the Energy Energy Correlator on tracks in the collinear limit, comparing results with vacuum track functions.

## Key numbers

- The characteristic medium angular scale is defined as $\theta_c \equiv 2/\sqrt{\hat q_{\rm eff} L^3}$, with $\hat q_{\rm eff} = \hat q (1-z+z^2)$ the effective jet quenching transport coefficient in Yang-Mills theory.
- The characteristic medium time scale is $t_c = \sqrt{2z(1-z)p_t/\hat q_{\rm eff}}$.
- For the anomalous dimension with energy loss, the lower integration limit is set to $2\langle \varepsilon \rangle/p_t$, where $\langle \varepsilon \rangle \sim \hat q L^2$ is the mean energy loss.
- The EEC results are shown for jet energies $p_t = 50$ GeV and $p_t = 200$ GeV.
- Mean energy loss values used in the EEC calculation are $\langle \varepsilon \rangle = 0.4$ GeV and $\langle \varepsilon \rangle = 1$ GeV.
- The medium length is taken as $L = 10$ fm and jet energy $p_t = 15$ GeV for the anomalous dimension including medium-induced radiation.
- The initial condition for the track function is taken as $T(x,\mu_0) = 252 x^2 (1-x)^6$ at $\mu_0 = p_t$, following Chang (2013).

## Context

This work extends the study of track functions, originally introduced by Chang (2013) for vacuum evolution, to the heavy ion context for the first time. It builds on prior calculations of the in-medium EEC by Andres (2023) and Barata (2023) that neglected hadronic-level matching, and on the BDMPS-Z/ASW phenomenological model for jet quenching (Zakharov 1996, Wiedemann 1999, Baier 1994, Salgado 2003). The results enable future phenomenological studies of jet substructure in heavy ion collisions, though the authors note the need for improved modeling of in-medium matrix elements and phase space constraints at finite energy fractions.
