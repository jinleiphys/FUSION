# Nuclear modification of forward $D$ production in pPb collisions at the LHC

> We study nuclear modification factors for single $D$ meson and semileptonic decay lepton $l$ ($=e,μ$) production in minimum bias proton-nucleus (p$A$) collisions at the LHC in the color-glass-condensate (CGC) framework at leading order in strong coupling. In our numerical computations, transverse momentum ($k_\perp$) dependent multi-point Wilson line correlators are employed for describing target nucleus for p$A$ and proton for pp. The projectile proton is treated with unintegrated gluon distribution function, which is also $k_\perp$-dependent. The rapidity evolutions of these functions in the small Bjorken $x$ region are taken into account by solving running coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation at leading logarithmic accuracy. For simplicity, we employ Kartvelishvili's type fragmentation function and a simple model for lepton energy distribution from seileptonic decay, respectively, to compute differential cross sections for $D$ and $l$ production. The gluon saturation scale inside the heavy nucleus is enhanced and dependent on $x$, which we take into account by replacing the initial saturation scale in the BK equation with a larger value for the heavy nucleus. We show that the saturation effect leads to perceptible nuclear suppression of $D$ production at forward rapidity. Our numerical results predict similar nuclear suppressions in p$A$ collisions for forward $l$ production at lower transverse momentum $p_\perp<2\;{\rm GeV}$. Numerical tables on the nuclear modifications of $D$ and $l$ are listed in this note.

# Nuclear modification of forward D production in pPb collisions at the LHC

## Key claim
The color-glass-condensate (CGC) framework at leading order predicts perceptible nuclear suppression of D meson production at forward rapidity in pPb collisions, with similar suppression predicted for semileptonic decay leptons at low transverse momentum (p⊥ < 2 GeV), but these predictions depend on the choice of initial saturation scale for the heavy nucleus and the heavy quark mass, and the calculations are restricted to leading order with the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov equation for rapidity evolution.

## Method
The authors compute nuclear modification factors for single D meson and semileptonic decay lepton production in minimum bias pPb collisions using the CGC framework at leading order in strong coupling, employing transverse-momentum-dependent multi-point Wilson line correlators for the target nucleus and unintegrated gluon distribution functions for the projectile proton. The rapidity evolution of these functions in the small Bjorken x region is described by solving the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) equation at leading logarithmic accuracy, with the initial saturation scale for the heavy nucleus enhanced relative to the proton by a factor cA^(1/3). Kartvelishvili-type fragmentation functions (α=3.5) and a simple model for lepton energy distribution from semileptonic decay are used to compute differential cross sections.

## Key numbers
- Initial saturation scale for the proton: Q_s0,p^2 = 0.1597 GeV^2 (with γ=1.118, Λ=0.241 GeV, C=2.47 from HERA DIS fits)
- For Pb nucleus, initial saturation scale variation: Q_s0,A^2 = (2-4) × Q_s0,p^2
- For Q_s0,A^2 = 3 Q_s0,p^2 with γ=1.118, the effective nuclear radius constraint gives R_A = 7.80 R_p
- For Q_s0,A^2 = 2 Q_s0,p^2, R_A = 9.79 R_p; for Q_s0,A^2 = 4 Q_s0,p^2, R_A = 6.64 R_p
- Fragmentation function parameter: α = 3.5 for D meson (Kartvelishvili form)
- D meson mass: M_D = 1.86 GeV; Kaon mass: M_K = 0.497 GeV
- Lepton production suppression predicted for p⊥ < 2 GeV at forward rapidity
- Rapidity dependence of R_pA integrated over p⊥ up to 10 GeV is provided in tables

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous CGC calculations of quarkonium production (Fujii 2013), open heavy-flavor meson production (Fujii 2013), and decay lepton production (Fujii 2015) in pA collisions. The results are compared with ALICE data on D meson production at mid rapidity (Adam 2015) and LHCb preliminary data at forward rapidity (LHCb 2016), as well as ALICE muon production data at forward rapidity (Acharya 2017). The paper enables quantitative comparisons between CGC predictions and available LHC data on heavy-flavor production, providing numerical tables for nuclear modification factors. The authors note that full next-to-leading order CGC calculations and inclusion of Sudakov logarithms are necessary for more precise comparisons, and that centrality-dependent studies would be a natural next step.
