# Thermodynamic Analogy for Structural Phase Transitions

> We investigate the relationship between ground-state (zero-temperature) quantum phase transitions in systems with variable Hamiltonian parameters and classical (temperature-driven) phase transitions in standard thermodynamics. An analogy is found between (i) phase-transitional distributions of the ground-state related branch points of quantum Hamiltonians in the complex parameter plane and (ii) distributions of zeros of classical partition functions in complex temperatures. Our approach properly describes the first- and second-order quantum phase transitions in the interacting boson model and can be generalized to finite temperatures.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the distribution of branch points of quantum Hamiltonians in the complex parameter plane near the critical point is quantitatively similar to the distribution of zeros of classical partition functions in complex temperatures, with the order of the quantum phase transition determined by the density of these branch points approaching the real axis.

## Method

The authors analyze the interacting boson model (IBM) Hamiltonian with two essential parameters, studying both first-order and second-order quantum phase transitions between spherical and deformed nuclear shapes. They define a "specific heat" analog derived from a fictitious partition function constructed from the partial discriminant of the Hamiltonian, which measures the density of branch points on the ground-state Riemann sheet. The scaling factor Omega is set proportional to (n-1) where n is the Hilbert space dimension, analogous to volume scaling in thermodynamic phase transitions.

## Key numbers

- For the IBM second-order quantum phase transition (chi=0), the critical parameter value is lambda_c = 0.8
- For the first-order quantum phase transition (chi not equal to 0), the critical parameter value is lambda_c = (4 + (2/7)chi^2)/(5 + (2/7)chi^2), giving lambda_c = 0.8181... for |chi| = sqrt(7)/2
- The maximal value of the normalized specific heat for the second-order transition converges to an asymptotic behavior proportional to N^2 for boson numbers N > 300
- The asymptotic limit of the specific heat from the standard definition (Eq. heat1) for the second-order transition is A_infinity = 12.5
- The dimension of the J=0 subspace in IBM scales as n ~ N^2/12 for large boson numbers

## Context

This work extends the Yang-Lee theory of phase transitions (Yang, 1952) to zero-temperature quantum phase transitions by establishing an analogy between branch points of quantum Hamiltonians and zeros of partition functions. The approach builds on earlier work by Heiss (2000) on exceptional points and by Cejnar et al. on quantum phase transitions in the interacting boson model. The method enables a unified description of quantum phase transitions in the lambda x T parameter space and can be generalized to finite temperatures.
