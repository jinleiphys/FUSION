# Change of nuclear configurations in the neutrinoless double-$Î²$ decay of $^{130}$Te $\rightarrow$ $^{130}$Xe and $^{136}$Xe $\rightarrow$ $^{136}$Ba

> The change in the configuration of valence protons between the initial and final states in the neutrinoless double-$Î²$ decay of $^{130}$Te $\rightarrow$ $^{130}$Xe and of $^{136}$Xe $\rightarrow$ $^{136}$Ba has been determined by measuring the cross sections of the ($d$,$^3$He) reaction with 101-MeV deuterons. Together with our recent determination of the relevant neutron configurations involved in the process, a quantitative comparison with the latest shell-model and interacting-boson-model calculations reveals significant discrepancies. These are the same calculations used to determine the nuclear matrix elements governing the rate of neutrinoless double-$Î²$ decay in these systems.

## Key claim

The change in valence proton configurations between parent and daughter nuclei in the neutrinoless double-beta decay of 130Te to 130Xe and 136Xe to 136Ba has been measured, and comparison with shell-model and interacting-boson-model calculations reveals significant discrepancies, meaning the same calculations used to determine nuclear matrix elements for the decay rate are not reproducing the measured occupancy changes.

## Method

The (d,3He) proton-removal reaction was measured at 101 MeV incident deuteron energy using the Grand Raiden spectrometer at the Research Center for Nuclear Physics, Osaka University, on targets of 128,130Te, 130,132,134,136Xe, and 136,138Ba. Angular distributions were measured at six angles for 130Te and at three angles for all other targets, and distorted-wave Born approximation calculations using the finite-range code Ptolemy were used to extract spectroscopic factors, which were normalized to the expected total proton occupancy above Z=50 (2 for Te, 4 for Xe, 6 for Ba) using a common normalization factor of 0.61.

## Key numbers

* Proton occupancy change for 130Te to 130Xe: 0g7/2 = 1.05(23), 1d = 0.68(12), 2s1/2 = 0.20(2), 0h11/2 = 0.13(4) nucleons, total = 2.06(26) nucleons
* Proton occupancy change for 136Xe to 136Ba: 0g7/2 = 0.93(14), 1d = 0.77(9), 2s1/2 = 0.14(2), 0h11/2 = 0.22(7) nucleons, total = 2.06(18) nucleons
* The common normalization factor used for spectroscopic factor extraction was 0.61, with an rms spread of 0.03 across eight independent normalizations
* The uncertainty on summed strength for any given orbital is estimated to be less than or equal to 0.1 nucleons
* The systematic uncertainty on absolute cross sections is less than or equal to 10%, and on relative cross sections less than or equal to 6%

## Context

This work extends the approach previously applied to the 76Ge to 76Se system by Schiffer et al. (2008) and Kay et al. (2009), where neutron and proton occupancies were measured and used to constrain nuclear matrix element calculations, reducing discrepancies between models by almost a factor of 2. The present results for 130Te to 130Xe and 136Xe to 136Ba, combined with neutron vacancy data from Kay et al. (2013) for the 130Te to 130Xe system, provide experimental constraints that can be used to test and improve the shell-model calculations of Neacsu and Horoi (2015) and Menendez et al. (2009), the interacting-boson model calculations of Kotila et al. (2013), and the quasiparticle random-phase approximation calculations of Suhonen and Civitarese (2010), all of which show discrepancies with the measured occupancy changes.
