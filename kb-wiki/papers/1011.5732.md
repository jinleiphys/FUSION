# A new microscopic nucleon-nucleon interaction derived from relativistic mean field theory

> A new microscopic nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction has been derived for the first time from the popular relativistic mean field theory (RMFT) Lagrangian. The NN interaction so obtained remarkably relate to the inbuilt fundamental parameters of RMFT. Furthermore, by folding it with the RMFT-densities of cluster and daughter nuclei to obtain the optical potential, it's application is also examined to study the exotic cluster radioactive decays, and results obtained found comparable with the successfully used M3Y phenomenological effective NN interactions. The presently derived NN-interaction can also be used to calculate a number of other nuclear observables.

# A new microscopic nucleon-nucleon interaction derived from relativistic mean field theory

## Key claim

The authors derive a new effective nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction, called R3Y, from the linear relativistic mean field theory (RMFT) Lagrangian using only the sigma, omega, and rho meson fields, and show that when folded with RMFT densities to study exotic cluster radioactive decays, the resulting preformation probabilities are closer to the phenomenological Blendowske-Walliser formula than those obtained with the standard M3Y interaction, though the agreement is within two to three orders of magnitude.

## Method

The authors start from the linear Walecka-type RMFT Lagrangian and, in the one-meson-exchange limit for a heavy static baryonic medium, sum the scalar (sigma, delta) and vector (omega, rho) meson potentials to obtain an effective NN interaction expressed as a sum of Yukawa terms with coupling constants and meson masses as parameters. They then use this R3Y interaction in a double folding model with spherical RMFT-HS densities to compute the nucleus-nucleus optical potential for cluster-daughter systems, and apply the preformed cluster model (PCM) to calculate the WKB penetrability P and empirically deduce the cluster preformation probability P0 from experimental decay constants for six trans-lead cluster decays with 208Pb as the daughter.

## Key numbers

- For the HS parameter set, the R3Y interaction is: v_eff(r) = 11956 × e^(-3.97r)/(4r) + 4099 × e^(-3.90r)/(4r) - 6882 × e^(-2.64r)/(4r), with strengths in MeV and ranges in fm.
- For the L1 parameter set, the R3Y interaction is: v_eff(r) = 9967 × e^(-3.97r)/(4r) - 6660 × e^(-2.79r)/(4r).
- The M3Y effective interaction used for comparison is: v_eff(r) = 7999 × e^(-4r)/(4r) - 2134 × e^(-2.5r)/(2.5r).
- For 222Ra decaying via 14C (Q = 33.050 MeV), the penetrability P is 1.728 × 10^(-25) with M3Y+EX and 2.277 × 10^(-24) with R3Y+EX; the empirical preformation factor P0^(c(emp)) is 1.044 × 10^(-08) (M3Y+EX) and 7.921 × 10^(-10) (R3Y+EX).
- For 238Pu decaying via 30Mg (Q = 76.824 MeV), P is 1.873 × 10^(-30) (M3Y+EX) and 1.185 × 10^(-25) (R3Y+EX); P0^(c(emp)) is 1.984 × 10^(-18) (M3Y+EX) and 3.136 × 10^(-23) (R3Y+EX).

## Context

This work extends the long-standing use of phenomenological effective NN interactions, particularly the M3Y interaction derived from G-matrix fits to the Reid-Elliott soft-core potential by Satchler and Love (1979), by instead deriving an NN interaction directly from the RMFT Lagrangian of Walecka (1981), Serot and Walecka (1986), and Reinhard (1989). The resulting R3Y interaction is applied to cluster radioactivity within the preformed cluster model of Gupta and collaborators (1989, 1994), and the empirical preformation factors are compared to the Blendowske-Walliser phenomenological formula (1988). The work enables future replacement of the M3Y interaction with a microscopically motivated R3Y interaction for calculating nuclear observables, and suggests a path toward unifying RMFT with cluster decay studies through inclusion of nonlinear meson terms.
