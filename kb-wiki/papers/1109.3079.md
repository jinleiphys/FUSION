# Surface properties of neutron-rich exotic nuclei: A source for studying the nuclear symmetry energy

> We study the correlation between the thickness of the neutron skin in finite nuclei and the nuclear symmetry energy for isotopic chains of even-even Ni, Sn, and Pb nuclei in the framework of the deformed self-consistent mean-field Skyrme HF+BCS method. The symmetry energy, the neutron pressure and the asymmetric compressibility in finite nuclei are calculated within the coherent density fluctuation model using the symmetry energy as a function of density within the Brueckner energy-density functional. The mass dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy and the neutron skin thickness are also studied together with the role of the neutron-proton asymmetry. A correlation between the parameters of the equation of state (symmetry energy and its density slope) and the neutron skin is suggested in the isotopic chains of Ni, Sn, and Pb nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates a correlation between neutron skin thickness and the symmetry energy, symmetry pressure, and asymmetric compressibility in finite nuclei across isotopic chains, but this correlation is approximate and linear only within specific isotopic chains, with the behavior showing a kink or inflection point at double-magic nuclei such as \(^{78}\)Ni and \(^{208}\)Pb.

## Method

The authors performed deformed self-consistent mean-field Skyrme HF+BCS calculations using SLy4, SG2, Sk3, and LNS parametrizations to obtain proton and neutron densities for even-even Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopes. They then applied the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) with the Brueckner energy-density functional to compute the symmetry energy, neutron pressure, and asymmetric compressibility for finite nuclei by weighting the corresponding nuclear matter quantities with a weight function derived from the nuclear density distributions.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy values for Ni isotopes calculated with CDFM lie in the range 26.8–27.8 MeV; for Sn isotopes 27.3–28.8 MeV; for Pb isotopes 28.7–29.1 MeV (using SLy4 force).
- The symmetry energy values for Ni isotopes from all four Skyrme forces are approximately in the range 27–29 MeV.
- The ratio of volume to surface symmetry energy used in the liquid-drop model comparison is \(y_s = 1.7\).
- The liquid-drop model symmetry energy \(a_a\) from Eq. (35a) with \(S_V = 27\) MeV and \(y_s = 1.7\) gives values in the interval 19–21 MeV for nuclei between \(^{78}\)Ni and \(^{208}\)Pb.
- The neutron skin thickness for Ni isotopes shows an inflection point at the double-magic nucleus \(^{78}\)Ni (\(N=50\)).

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Sarriguren et al. (2007) on neutron skin in deformed HF+BCS calculations and by Warda et al. (2010) on the bulk and surface nature of neutron skin formation in Sn and Pb isotopes. The CDFM approach provides a method to connect nuclear matter properties to finite nuclei, complementing liquid-drop model analyses by Danielewicz (2003) and Diep et al. (2007). The results enable further constraints on the density dependence of the symmetry energy from measurements of neutron skin thickness in neutron-rich isotopes accessible at radioactive beam facilities.
