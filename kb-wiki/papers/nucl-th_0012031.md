# Constructing Point Form Mass Operators from Interaction Lagrangians

> Starting from an interaction Lagrangian formed out of local fields, an interacting four-momentum operator is constructed by integrating the interaction Lagrangian over the forward hyperboloid. Such a four-momentum operator has the property that the components commute among themselves; however, when the Fock space on which the four-momentum operator acts is truncated, the components no longer commute among themselves. By modifying matrix elements of the four-momentum operator on the truncated space, Bakamjian-Thomas mass operatorsare constructed which restore the Poincare relations. Examples for a simple Lagrangian are given.

## Key claim

The paper constructs interacting mass operators for few-body relativistic quantum mechanics by taking matrix elements of vertex operators (products of local fields at the space-time point zero) on velocity states of a truncated Fock space, then multiplying by a form factor that depends only on the magnitude of the mass difference between initial and final states; this procedure restores the Poincaré commutation relations that are lost when the Fock space is truncated.

## Method

The author uses point form relativistic quantum mechanics, where all interactions are placed in the four-momentum operator and Lorentz transformations are kinematic. The four-momentum operator is written as the product of a mass operator and a four-velocity operator (the Bakamjian-Thomas construction in point form). Interacting mass operators are constructed from velocity-state matrix elements of Lorentz scalar densities \(\mathcal{H}(0)\) (products of local fields at the origin), multiplied by a form factor \(f(\Delta m)\) that depends on the magnitude of the mass difference between initial and final many-particle states, ensuring the operator is well-defined on the truncated Fock space. The method is illustrated for a scalar nucleon-pion vertex \(\mathcal{H}(x)=g\Psi^\dagger(x)\Psi(x)\phi(x)\) in baryon number one and two sectors.

## Key numbers

- Bare nucleon mass \(m_N(0)\) and physical nucleon mass \(m_N\) are related by the integral equation \(g^2 \int \frac{d^3 k}{\omega_N(0)\omega_\pi} \frac{|f(\omega_N(0)+\omega_\pi-m_N(0))|^2}{m_N-\omega_N(0)-\omega_\pi} = m_N - m_N(0)\), where \(\omega_N(0)=\sqrt{m_N^2(0)+\vec{k}^2}\) and \(\omega_\pi=\sqrt{m_\pi^2+\vec{k}^2}\).
- For baryon number two, the two-particle to three-particle transition kernel is \(<\vec{k}_1\vec{k}_2|K|\vec{k}> = f(\omega_1+\omega_2+\omega_\pi-2\omega) (\delta^3(\vec{k}+\vec{k}_1)+\delta^3(\vec{k}-\vec{k}_1)+\delta(\vec{k}+\vec{k}_2)+\delta(\vec{k}-\vec{k}_2))\), where \(\omega=\sqrt{m_N^2(0)+\vec{k}^2}\) and \(\omega_i=\sqrt{m_N^2(0)+\vec{k}_i^2}\).
- The form factor \(f_\tau(\Delta m)\) derived from integrating over the forward hyperboloid is given by \(f_\tau(\Delta m)=-\pi^2\tau(\Delta m)^{-2}\alpha^{-1}\partial/\partial\alpha [\alpha^{-1}J_1(\Delta m\tau\alpha)+i\alpha^{-1}N_1(\Delta m\tau\alpha)]_{\alpha=1}\) with \(\alpha:=\sqrt{v\cdot v}\), where \(J_1\) and \(N_1\) are Bessel functions.

## Context

This work extends the Bakamjian-Thomas procedure (Bakamjian and Thomas, 1953) for constructing Poincaré-covariant mass operators to include particle production via vertex interactions from local field theory, addressing the loss of relativity when Fock space is truncated. It is intermediate between relativistic direct interaction theories for a fixed number of particles and full quantum field theory, and is reminiscent of the Lee model (Lee, 1954) and its relativistic generalization by Fuda (1990) using front form. The formalism enables calculation of bound states (e.g., deuteron mass \(m_D\)) and scattering amplitudes (e.g., pion-nucleon scattering) from coupled-channel mass operator eigenvalue equations, with open questions remaining about cluster separability properties and the dependence of mass renormalization on truncation.
