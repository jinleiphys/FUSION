# Single-particle spectral density of the unitary Fermi gas: Novel approach based on the operator product expansion, sum rules and the maximum entropy method

> Making use of the operator product expansion, we derive a general class of sum rules for the imaginary part of the single-particle self-energy of the unitary Fermi gas. The sum rules are analyzed numerically with the help of the maximum entropy method, which allows us to extract the single-particle spectral density as a function of both energy and momentum. These spectral densities contain basic information on the properties of the unitary Fermi gas, such as the dispersion relation and the superfluid pairing gap, for which we obtain reasonable agreement with the available results based on quantum Monte-Carlo simulations.

## Key claim

The authors derive a general class of sum rules for the imaginary part of the single-particle self-energy of the unitary Fermi gas using the operator product expansion, and then extract the single-particle spectral density via the maximum entropy method, obtaining reasonable agreement with quantum Monte-Carlo results for the dispersion relation and superfluid pairing gap, but only for momenta down to the Fermi momentum where the OPE remains valid.

## Method

The authors first compute the operator product expansion (OPE) for the single-particle Green's function of the unitary Fermi gas at zero temperature, keeping operators up to momentum dimension 5, which yields an expression for the self-energy in terms of the Bertsch parameter and the contact density. They then derive sum rules relating weighted integrals of the imaginary part of the self-energy to the OPE expression, using Borel kernels of the form \(\mathcal{K}_n(\omega, M) = (\omega/M)^n e^{-\omega^2/M^2}\). Finally, they apply the maximum entropy method (MEM) to extract the most probable form of the imaginary part of the self-energy from these sum rules without assuming a specific functional form, and obtain the real part via the Kramers-Kronig relation to compute the single-particle spectral density.

## Key numbers

- Bertsch parameter \(\xi = 0.372(5)\) from quantum Monte-Carlo simulations (Carlson) and \(\xi = 0.370(5)(8)\) from experiment (Ku, Zurn)
- Dimensionless contact density \(\zeta = 3.40(1)\) from quantum Monte-Carlo simulations (Gandolfi) and \(\zeta = 3.33(7)\) from experiment (Hoinka)
- The imaginary part of the function \(L(x)\) is non-zero only in the interval \(1/3 < x < 1\) where \(x = k_0/\epsilon_k\)
- The OPE expression for the self-energy in the unitary limit includes terms proportional to \(\epsilon_F^{3/2}\), \(\epsilon_F^2\), and \(\epsilon_F^{5/2}\)

## Context

This work extends the OPE analysis of the single-particle Green's function for the unitary Fermi gas previously carried out by Nishida (2012), which showed good agreement with quantum Monte-Carlo simulations for momenta as small as the Fermi momentum. The method adapts techniques from QCD sum rules, originally developed by Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979), to the non-relativistic context of ultracold atomic gases, and uses the maximum entropy method as proposed by Gubler (2013) for QCD sum rules. The results enable a first-principles calculation of the single-particle spectral density without relying on mean-field assumptions, and the two-peak structure found in the imaginary part of the self-energy leads to a BCS-Bogoliubov-like dispersion relation with a nonzero superfluid pairing gap.
