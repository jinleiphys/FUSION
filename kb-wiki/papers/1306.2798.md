# Correlated D-meson decays competing against thermal QGP dilepton radiation

> The QGP that might be created in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions is expected to radiate thermal dilepton radiation. However, this thermal dilepton radiation interferes with dileptons originating from hadron decays. In the invariant mass region between the $ϕ$ and $J/Ψ$ peak ($1\,$GeV$\lesssim M_{\ell^+ \ell^-} \lesssim 3 \,$GeV) the most substantial background of hadron decays originates from correlated D$\bar{\mathrm{D}}$-meson decays. We evaluate this background using a Langevin simulation for charm quarks. As background medium we utilize the well-tested UrQMD-hybrid model. The required drag and diffusion coefficients are taken from a resonance approach. The decoupling of the charm quarks from the hot medium is performed at a temperature of $130\,$MeV and as hadronization mechanism a coalescence approach is chosen. This model for charm quark interactions with the medium has already been successfully applied to the study of the medium modification and the elliptic flow at FAIR, RHIC and LHC energies. In this proceeding we present our results for the dilepton radiation from correlated D$\bar{\mathrm{D}}$ decays at RHIC energy in comparison to PHENIX measurements in the invariant mass range between 1 and 3 GeV using different interaction scenarios. These results can be utilized to estimate the thermal QGP radiation.

# Correlated D-meson decays competing against thermal QGP dilepton radiation

## Key claim

The correlated D-meson decay background in the invariant mass region between 1 and 3 GeV at RHIC energy may be smaller than previously assumed when using physically motivated drag and diffusion coefficients, leaving room for thermal QGP radiation to account for the difference between the model and PHENIX data; however, the scenario with no medium interactions matches the data quite well, so the thermal contribution is inferred from the deficit of the physical-interaction scenario rather than directly observed.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic Langevin simulation for charm-quark propagation through a hydrodynamic medium provided by the UrQMD-hybrid model, with drag and diffusion coefficients taken from a resonance approach assuming D-meson resonances in the QGP. Charm quarks were decoupled from the hot medium at a temperature of 130 MeV and hadronized via a coalescence mechanism. The invariant mass spectrum of dielectrons from correlated D-meson decays was computed for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV and compared to PHENIX measurements under three scenarios: no interaction, physical thermalization, and extreme thermalization with coefficients multiplied by a factor of 20.

## Key numbers

- Invariant mass range studied: 1 GeV to 3 GeV (between the phi and J/psi peaks)
- Collision system: Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, minimum bias (0-92% centrality)
- Decoupling temperature for charm quarks: 130 MeV
- Rapidity cut applied: |y| < 0.35
- Electron momentum cut applied: p_T^e = 0.2 GeV/c
- Initial charm-quark p_T distribution parameters: A_1 = 2.0/GeV^2, A_2 = 0.1471/GeV^2, A_3 = 21.0
- Extreme thermalization scenario used drag and diffusion coefficients multiplied by a factor of 20

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Langevin-based studies of charm-quark medium modification and elliptic flow at FAIR, RHIC, and LHC energies (Lang 2012, Lang 2013) to the specific problem of the dilepton background from correlated D-meson decays. The results provide an updated estimate of the hadronic background that must be subtracted from PHENIX dilepton measurements (Adare 2009) to isolate thermal QGP radiation, and suggest that the physical-interaction scenario leaves a larger gap to data than the no-interaction scenario, implying a significant thermal contribution in the 1-3 GeV invariant mass window. The approach enables future comparisons at LHC energies and with other experimental data to further constrain the thermal QGP signal.
