# Negative Coupling $ϕ^4$ on the Lattice

> Triviality of $ϕ^4$ theory in four dimensions can be avoided if the bare coupling constant is negative in the UV. Theories with negative coupling can be put on the lattice if the integration domain for $ϕ(x)$ is contour-deformed from the real to the complex domain. In 0+1d (quantum mechanics), one can recover results from $\mathcal{PT}$-symmetric quantum mechanics in this way. In this work, I report on an attempt to put negative coupling $ϕ^4$ theory in 4 dimensions on the lattice.

## Key claim
Triviality of ϕ⁴ theory in four dimensions can be avoided if the bare coupling constant is negative in the UV, but the author reports only on an attempt to put negative coupling ϕ⁴ theory on the lattice in 4d, with results limited to tiny lattices (Nσ = 2, 3 and Nτ = 1) due to computational constraints.

## Method
The author defines a contour-deformed partition function for scalar ϕ⁴ theory on a lattice with Nτ × Nσ³ sites, where the integration contour for each ϕ(x) is deformed into the complex plane at an angle α = π/4 to render the path integral convergent for negative coupling. Direct numerical integration is performed on small lattices (2³ × 1, 2⁴, and 3³ × 1) for bare lattice coupling g and fixed mass m² = 1 in lattice units. The reduced partition function is compared to the Stefan-Boltzmann limit and to large-N O(N) model results from Romatschke (2022, 2023) and Weller (2023).

## Key numbers
- Energy levels for the PT-symmetric quantum mechanical Hamiltonian: E₀ ≈ 0.930546, E₁ ≈ 3.781896, E₂ ≈ 7.435067, E₃ ≈ 11.628327, with higher levels given by the WKB formula E_n = [3√2(n+1/2)/Γ²(1/4)]^(4/3) π².
- The 0d toy model partition function for negative coupling is Z⁰_C(g) = √2 Γ(5/4) g^(-1/4).
- The relation between bare lattice coupling g and lattice spacing a from the large-N O(N) model is g = -2π² / ln(T_c a), with T_c the critical temperature.
- Direct numerical integration is limited to oscillatory integrals of up to roughly 30 dimensions, restricting lattice sizes to Nσ³ × Nτ of 2³ × 1, 2⁴, and 3³ × 1.

## Context
This work extends the contour deformation approach for negative coupling scalar field theory from 0d and 1d (quantum mechanics) to 4d on the lattice, following pioneering lattice studies by Lawrence, Oh and Yamauchi (2022) in 1d and 2d and further 1d work by Lawrence (2023) connecting to PT-symmetric quantum mechanics of Bender (1998). The 4d results are compared qualitatively to large-N O(N) model predictions from Romatschke (2022, 2023) and Weller (2023), which indicate asymptotic freedom and a second-order phase transition. The work is limited to tiny lattices and requires larger simulations (especially Nτ > 1) for conclusive interpretations.
