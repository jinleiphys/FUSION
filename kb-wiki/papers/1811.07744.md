# Elucidating the multiplicity dependence of J/$ψ$ production in proton-proton collisions with PYTHIA8

> A study of prompt and non-prompt J/$ψ$ production as a function of charged-particle multiplicity in inelastic proton--proton (pp) collisions at a centre-of-mass energy of $\sqrt{s}$ = 13 TeV based on calculations using the PYTHIA8 Monte Carlo is reported. Recent experimental data shows an intriguing stronger-than-linear increase of the self-normalized J/$ψ$ yield with multiplicity; several models, based on initial or final state effects, have been able to describe the observed behaviour. In this paper, the microscopic reasons for this behaviour, like the role of multiple parton interactions, colour reconnections and auto-correlations are investigated. It is observed that the stronger-than-linear increase and the transverse momentum ($p_{\rm T}$) dependence, contrary to what is predicted by the other available models, can be attributed to auto-correlation effects only. In absence of auto-correlation effects, the increase of the yield of J/$ψ$ with multiplicity -- and in general for all hard processes -- is weaker than linear for multiplicities exceeding about three times the mean multiplicity. The possibility of disentangling auto-correlation effects from other physical phenomena by measuring the charged-particle multiplicity in different pseudo-rapidity and azimuthal regions relative to the J/$ψ$ direction is investigated. In this regard, it is suggested to extend the experimental measurements of J/$ψ$ production as a function of the charged-particle multiplicity by determining the multiplicity in several azimuthal regions and in particular in the Transverse region with respect to the direction of the J/$ψ$ meson.

## Key claim
The stronger-than-linear increase of self-normalized J/psi yield with charged-particle multiplicity in pp collisions at 13 TeV, and its transverse momentum dependence, can be attributed entirely to auto-correlation effects in PYTHIA8 simulations; in the absence of such effects, the yield increase becomes weaker than linear for multiplicities exceeding about three times the mean multiplicity.

## Method
The authors used PYTHIA8 (version 8.230, Monash 2013 tune) to simulate non-diffractive pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV, generating J/psi mesons forced to decay in the dielectron channel. They systematically switched off multiple parton interactions (MPI) and colour reconnections (CR) to isolate their contributions, and studied J/psi yields as a function of charged-particle multiplicity measured in different pseudorapidity and azimuthal regions (Toward, Transverse, Away) relative to the J/psi direction.

## Key numbers
- Average charged-particle multiplicity at mid-rapidity (|eta| < 1) for full PYTHIA8 simulation: 6.04, with RMS 6.46
- Average multiplicity with MPI off: 2.57 (RMS 2.04); with CR off: 8.44 (RMS 10.25)
- Colour reconnection reduces average multiplicity by about 30%
- Non-prompt J/psi relative contribution rises from about 10% at pT = 0 to above 40% at pT ~ 16 GeV/c
- Cluster collapse contribution to total J/psi yield: between about 8% and 4% depending on pT
- About 65% of beauty quarks producing non-prompt J/psi come from primary perturbative scattering, about 20% from gluon splitting in hard scattering, about 15% from gluon splitting in initial or final-state radiation
- Average number of MPI saturates at 3.3 times the mean value in most central collisions (b=0)
- MPI-CR-baseline is approximately linear for Nch / mean(Nch) < 3 and weaker than linear above

## Context
This work extends experimental observations from ALICE at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV (ALICE, 2012) and CMS for Upsilon(nS) mesons (CMS, 2014), which showed stronger-than-linear increase of charmonium yields with multiplicity. It contradicts models attributing the behavior to percolation mechanisms, higher Fock states (Kopeliovich, 2013), color glass condensate effects, or multi-pomeron interactions (EPOS3), by showing that PYTHIA8 can reproduce the effect through auto-correlations alone. The findings enable experimental tests by measuring multiplicity in the Transverse azimuthal region relative to the J/psi direction, which would be insensitive to auto-correlation effects and could reveal genuine physical phenomena.
