# Baryon - Antibaryon Production by Disordered Chiral Condensates

> We investigate the production of baryons and antibaryons in the central rapidity region of high energy nuclear collisions within the framework of the Skyrme model taking into account the effects of explicit chiral symmetry breaking. We argue that the formation of disordered chiral condensates may lead to enhanced baryon-antibaryon production at low transverse momentum.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the formation of disordered chiral condensates (DCC) in high-energy nuclear collisions may lead to enhanced baryon-antibaryon production at low transverse momentum, with the enhancement factor depending on the magnitude of explicit chiral symmetry breaking terms in the effective potential.

## Method

The authors use the linear sigma model with explicit chiral symmetry breaking terms to study Skyrmion formation via the Kibble mechanism, where the chiral field settles into randomly oriented domains of correlation length ξ during a phase transition. They estimate the probability of forming partial-winding Skyrmion configurations that can evolve into full integer-winding baryons, first for the case without symmetry breaking and then for the case with a tilted potential that favors θ = 0. The analysis uses tetrahedral domain decomposition in three spatial dimensions, with the geodesic rule governing field variation between vertices.

## Key numbers

- Skyrmion formation probability per unit volume without explicit symmetry breaking: P ≈ 0.08 ξ⁻³, giving about 20 Skyrmions in a volume of 250 fm³ with ξ = 1 fm
- Skyrmion formation probability per unit volume with tilted potential: P′ ≈ 0.33 ξ⁻³, giving about 82 baryons and antibaryons in a volume of 250 fm³ with ξ = 1 fm
- Probability of partial Skyrmion formation in a suitable region (four elementary domains) without symmetry breaking: about 0.05
- Numerical simulation probability for partial texture formation: about 0.04
- For the two-dimensional analog, the probability of Skyrmion formation per unit area is ξ⁻²/(6√3)

## Context

This work extends the Kibble mechanism for topological defect formation (Kibble, 1976) to baryon production in the Skyrme model, building on earlier proposals by Anselm (1988), Blaizot and Krzywicki (1989), and Bjorken, Kowalski and Taylor (1993) for disoriented chiral condensates. It corrects earlier overestimates of Skyrmion production by Tomaras (1989) and Ellis (1990) that did not properly account for boundary conditions, and incorporates explicit chiral symmetry breaking that was absent in those studies. The results enable experimental searches for enhanced baryon-antibaryon pairs at low transverse momentum in heavy-ion collisions, particularly in the central rapidity region where net baryon number is small.
