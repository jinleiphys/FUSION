# Kaon-baryon coupling schemes and kaon condensation in hyperon-mixed matter

> Possible coexistence of kaon condensation and hyperons in highly dense matter [the ($Y+K$) phase] is investigated on the basis of the relativistic mean-field theory combined with the effective chiral Lagrangian. Two coupling schemes for the $s$-wave kaon-baryon interaction are compared regarding the onset density of kaon condensation in the hyperon-mixed matter and equation of state for the developed ($Y+K$) phase: One is the contact interaction scheme related to the nonlinear effective chiral Lagrangian. The other is the meson-exchange scheme, where the interaction vertices between the kaon field and baryons are described by exchange of mesons (sigma, sigma^* mesons for scalar coupling, and omega, rho, phi mesons for vector coupling). It is shown that in the meson exchange scheme, the contribution from the nonlinear scalar self-interaction gives rise to a repulsive effect for kaon effective energy, pushing up the onset density of kaon condensation as compared with the case of the contact interaction scheme. In general, the difference of kaon-baryon dynamics between the contact interaction scheme and the meson-exchange scheme relies on the specific forms of the nonlinear self-interacting meson terms. It is shown that the nonlinear self-interacting term is not relevant to repulsive energy leading to stiffening of the equation of state at high densities and that it cannot be compensated with large attractive energy due to the appearance of the ($Y$+$K$) phase in the case of the contact interaction scheme. We also discuss in the contact interaction scheme what effects are necessary so as to make the equation of state with (Y+K) phase stiff enough to be consistent with recent observations of massive neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper compares two coupling schemes for the s-wave kaon-baryon interaction in hyperon-mixed dense matter and finds that the meson-exchange scheme pushes up the onset density of kaon condensation compared to the contact interaction scheme, with the nonlinear scalar self-interaction term providing an extra repulsive contribution to the kaon effective energy in the meson-exchange scheme that is absent in the contact interaction scheme.

## Method

The authors combine relativistic mean-field theory with the effective chiral Lagrangian to describe the coexistence of kaon condensation and hyperons in dense matter. They compare two schemes for the s-wave kaon-baryon interaction: the contact interaction scheme derived from the nonlinear effective chiral Lagrangian, and the meson-exchange scheme where kaon-baryon interaction vertices are mediated by scalar mesons (sigma, sigma*) and vector mesons (omega, rho, phi). The baryon-baryon interaction is described within the relativistic mean-field framework including nonlinear scalar self-interaction terms for the sigma meson, with meson-baryon coupling constants determined from nuclear matter properties and hypernuclear experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Onset density for kaon condensation in hyperon-mixed matter is estimated at baryon number density rho_B = (3-4) rho_0, where rho_0 = 0.153 fm^{-3} is the normal nuclear density, for the KN sigma term Sigma_Kn = (300-400) MeV in the contact interaction scheme
- The KN sigma term values used are Sigma_Kn = 300 MeV and 400 MeV, corresponding to a_3 = -0.89 and a_3 = -1.1 respectively
- For Sigma_Kn = 300 MeV, the derived sigma terms for other baryons are: Sigma_Kp = Sigma_KXi- = 369 MeV, Sigma_KLambda = 380 MeV, Sigma_KSigma- = 300 MeV
- For Sigma_Kn = 400 MeV, the derived sigma terms are: Sigma_Kp = Sigma_KXi- = 469 MeV, Sigma_KLambda = 480 MeV, Sigma_KSigma- = 400 MeV
- The scalar meson-hyperon couplings are determined from hyperon potential depths: V_Lambda^N = -27 MeV, V_Sigma-^N = +23.5 MeV, V_Xi-^N = -14 MeV
- The sigma* meson coupling for Lambda is set to g_sigma*Lambda = 7.2, reproducing the theoretical Lambda-Lambda separation energy B^th_LambdaLambda(^11_LambdaLambdaBe) = 20.7 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of kaon condensation in neutron stars by Muto (2008) and others, and addresses the problem that both kaon condensates and hyperon mixing lead to significant softening of the equation of state, making it difficult to reconcile with observations of massive neutron stars exceeding 2 solar masses (PSR J1614-2230 at 1.928 solar masses, PSR J0348+0432 at 2.01 solar masses, PSR J0740+6620 at 2.08 solar masses). The paper builds on the relativistic mean-field approach of Glendenning (1985) and others, and the chiral model framework previously applied to multi-antikaonic bound states by Muto, Maruyama, and Tatsumi (2009). The results enable future work incorporating universal three-baryon repulsive forces as an alternative to the nonlinear scalar self-interaction term to achieve stiff enough equations of state consistent with neutron star observations.
