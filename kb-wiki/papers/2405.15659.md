# Spin-down of a pulsar with a yielding crust

> In light of the discovery of the long-period radio pulsar PSR J0901-4046, it is interesting to revisit a question about how the magnetized neutron star slows down its rotation. In the case of a weak or liquid outer crust, the mechanism of spin-down becomes unclear because the braking stress cannot then be transmitted from the surface to the main bulk of the star. We show that even if the outer crust does not withstand the surface electromagnetic forces creating the braking torque, the stellar spin-down does not stop, and the matter rearranges so that the necessary electromagnetic forces form in more deep and rigid layers capable of withstanding these forces. The spin-down rate remains the same and corresponds to the transformation of the rotational energy of the neutron star into the energy of the generated relativistic electron-positron plasma. The solid iron surface of ultrastrongly magnetized PSR J0901-4046 appears not to be yielding and withstands the braking stress without breaking.

# Spin-down of a pulsar with a yielding crust

## Key claim
Even if a neutron star's outer crust yields to the electromagnetic braking forces and cannot transmit torque from the surface to the stellar interior, the spin-down does not stop; the matter rearranges into a force-free magnetic configuration that channels the electric current into deeper, rigid layers, and the resulting braking torque remains identical to that of a star with a solid surface.

## Method
The author derives the braking torque from Ampere forces acting on the polar cap currents, then considers the case where the outer crust breaks under these forces. Using the force-free condition (j × B = 0) for the yielding matter, the electric current is shown to flow inward along magnetic tubes until reaching rigid layers, where it spreads radially. The torque integral is evaluated over the boundary between yielding and solid matter, yielding the same expression as for a solid surface. The result is verified via energy conservation and Poynting flux balance.

## Key numbers
- PSR J0901-4046 has rotation period P ≈ 75.9 s and period derivative Ṗ ≈ 2.25 × 10⁻¹³.
- Standard magnetic dipole estimate gives surface field 10¹⁴ G, but the author's earlier work (Sob'yanin 2023) gives B ≈ 3.2 × 10¹⁶ G (3.2 TT) for this pulsar.
- The polar cap radius is R_pc ≈ 17 m, and the total polar cap current is I ≈ 56 MA.
- Linear current density at the polar cap edge is i ≈ 0.53 MA/m.
- Ampere force shear stress on the solid surface is τ ≈ 1.7 EPa.
- The shear modulus of the iron crust is μ ∼ 10²⁵ Ba (1 YPa), giving shear strain γ = τ/μ ∼ 10⁻⁶.
- The critical strain for crust breaking is γ_cr ∼ 0.1 (Horowitz and Kadau 2009; Hoffman and Heyl 2012), so γ ≪ γ_cr.

## Context
This work addresses a puzzle raised by the discovery of the ultra-slowly rotating radio pulsar PSR J0901-4046 (Caleb et al. 2022) and similar objects GLEAM-X J162759.5-523504.3 and GPM J1839-10 (Hurley-Walker et al. 2022, 2023). The standard vacuum magnetic-dipole spin-down model (Deutsch 1955; Pacini 1968) is inapplicable for plasma-filled magnetospheres. The author extends earlier work on polar gap electrodynamics (Sob'yanin 2023) by showing that even a weak or liquid crust cannot halt spin-down, and demonstrates that PSR J0901-4046's solid iron surface withstands the braking stress without breaking. The result applies to any neutron star with active polar caps.
