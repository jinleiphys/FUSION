# The roles of nucleon resonances in $Λ$(1520) photoproduciton off proton

> In this work the roles of the nucleon resonances in the $Λ$(1520) photoproduction off proton target are investigated within the effective Lagrangian method. Besides the Born terms,including contact term, $s$-, $u$- and $K$ exchanged $t$-channels, vector meson $K^*$ exchanged $t-$channel is considered in our investigation, which is negligible at low energy and important at high energy. The important nucleon resonances predicted by the constituent quark model (CQM) are considered and the results are found well comparable with the experimental data. Besides the dominant $D_{13}(2080)$, the resonance $[\textstyle{5\over 2}^-]_2(2080)$ predicted by the CQM is found important to reproduce the experimental data. Other nucleon resonances are found to give small contributions in the channel considered in this work. With all important nucleon resonances predicted by CQM, the prediction of the differential cross section at the energy up to 5.5 GeV are presented also, which can be checked by the future CLAS experimental data.

## Key claim

The nucleon resonance $D_{13}(2080)$ is the dominant resonance in $\Lambda(1520)$ photoproduction, but a second resonance $[\textstyle{5\over 2}^-]_2(2080)$ predicted by the constituent quark model (CQM) is also essential to reproduce experimental data, while other nucleon resonances give only small contributions.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian method to calculate the $\gamma p \to K^+\Lambda(1520)$ reaction, including Born terms (contact term, $s$-channel, $K$-exchanged $t$-channel) and a $K^*$-exchanged $t$-channel with Reggeized treatment at high energies. The coupling constants for nucleon resonances were determined from the radiative and strong decay amplitudes predicted by the constituent quark model of Capstick and Roberts, rather than treated as free fitting parameters.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant $g_{KN\Lambda^*} = 10.5$ was obtained from the experimental decay width of $\Lambda^* \to NK$ in PDG.
- The cut-off parameter for Born terms was set to $\Lambda = 0.6$ GeV; for $K^*$-exchange, $\Lambda_V = 0.8$ GeV; for nucleon resonances, $\Lambda_R = 1$ GeV.
- The $\chi^2$ value for the full model fit to LEPS10 and SLAC data was 1.38; turning off $D_{13}(2080)$ increased $\chi^2$ to 12.42, while turning off $[\textstyle{5\over 2}^-]_2(2080)$ increased it to 4.01.
- The mass of $D_{13}(2080)$ was adopted as 2150 MeV (larger than the PDG value of 2080 MeV), following Xie et al. (2010).
- The decay widths for all nucleon resonances were set to 200 MeV.
- Predictions for differential cross sections were presented for photon energies up to 5.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of $\Lambda(1520)$ photoproduction beyond earlier studies by Nam et al. (2010) and Xie et al. (2010), who considered only the $D_{13}(2080)$ resonance. It incorporates all nucleon resonances predicted by the constituent quark model of Capstick and Roberts (1992, 1998), addressing the confusion around nucleon resonances near 2.1 GeV. The predictions for energies up to 5.5 GeV can be tested by future CLAS experimental data from the $eg3$ and $g11$ runs.
