# Electromagnetic form factor of pion in the field theory inspired approach

> A new formula for the pion form factor F_pi is proposed which takes into account the pseudoscalar meson loops and the mixing among rho(770), rho(1450), and rho(1700). The expression possesses correct analytical properties and can be used in both timelike and spacelike kinematical regions. The comparison is made with the existing experimental data on F_pi collected by the detectors SND, CMD-2, KLOE, and BaBaR restricted to energies below 1 GeV. A good description of all four data sets is obtained. In the spacelike region, upon substituting the resonance parameters found in the timelike one, the formula gives F_pi, in agreement with the measurements of the NA7 Collaboration.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a new expression for the pion form factor that incorporates pseudoscalar meson loops and mixing among three rho resonances, achieving good agreement with four experimental datasets below 1 GeV, but the analysis is restricted to energies below 1 GeV and only includes pi+pi- and K anti-K loop contributions, with heavier decay modes of rho(1450) and rho(1700) neglected.

## Method
The authors constructed a field-theory-inspired expression for the pion form factor F_pi(s) using an effective Lagrangian with SU(3) invariant interactions of vector resonances with pseudoscalar meson pairs. They included diagonal and nondiagonal polarization operators from pi+pi- and K anti-K loops calculated via dispersion relations, and incorporated mixing among rho(770), rho(1450), and rho(1700) through a 3x3 inverse propagator matrix, plus rho(770)-omega(782) mixing. The free parameters were determined by fitting the bare cross section for e+e- -> pi+pi- from SND, CMD-2, KLOE, and BaBaR data restricted to sqrt(s) below 1 GeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier approaches to the pion form factor, including the simple vector dominance model of Kroll, Lee, and Zumino (1967) and the Gounaris-Sakurai formula (1968), which lacked correct analytical properties or the ability to handle mixing of multiple rho resonances. It also improves upon earlier work by Achasov and Kozhevnikov (1997, 1997a) that included strong isovector mixing but satisfied the normalization condition only approximately. The new expression enables continuation to the spacelike region, where it agrees with NA7 Collaboration measurements (Amendolia et al., 1986), and allows calculation of the pion charge radius and pi-pi scattering phase shifts in the vector-isovector channel.
