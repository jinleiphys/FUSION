# Microscopic Description of Isospin Mixing Pairing Correlations in the Framework of an Algebraic Sp(4) Model

> We explore isospin mixing beyond that due to the Coulomb interaction in the framework of an exactly solvable microscopic sp(4) algebraic approach. Specifically, we focus on the isospin non-conserving part of the pure nuclear pairing interaction. The outcome of this study shows the significance of the pairing charge dependence and its role in mixing isospin multiplets of pairing-governed isobaric analog 0+ states in light and medium mass nuclei, especially in nuclei with equal numbers of protons and neutrons. The model reveals possible, but still extremely weak, non-analog beta-decay transitions and estimates their relative strengths within a shell closure.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the isospin non-conserving part of the pure nuclear pairing interaction can induce isospin mixing among isobaric analog 0+ states, but this mixing is extremely weak (typically less than 0.14%) and likely undetectable with current experimental precision, though it may be comparable to isospin-symmetry breaking corrections for even-even N=Z nuclei near the middle of the shell.

## Method

The authors employ an exactly solvable Sp(4) algebraic model (isomorphic to SO(5)) that extends the isospin-invariant nuclear pairing Hamiltonian to include charge-dependent isotensor terms (H_IM and H_split) while preserving the Sp(4) dynamical symmetry. They apply this model to pairing-governed isobaric analog 0+ states in light and medium mass nuclei (32 ≤ A ≤ 100) where protons and neutrons occupy the same shell, using Coulomb-corrected experimental energies to isolate nuclear effects. The model parameters are fixed from previous fits to 149 nuclei, and the authors compute isospin mixing overlaps and non-analog beta-decay transition matrix elements within a first-order perturbation approximation.

## Key numbers

- The charge dependence of the pure nuclear interaction in the f7/2 level is on average ~2.5% when comparing T0=0 to T0=1 two-body matrix elements in T=1 multiplets
- For the d3/2 level, the isospin mixing parameter F/Ω = 0.007, indicating nearly equal pn, pp, and nn pairing strengths
- For the f7/2 level, the mixing overlap δ_T̃,T for light nuclei in the j=3/2 orbit is 0.0001% for 36Ar (F/Ω = 0.007)
- The isospin mixing between isobaric analog states in the f7/2 level is on average 0.006% (excluding even-even N=Z nuclei)
- The mixing of the first excited 0+ non-analog state due to isospin non-conserving interaction is typically about 0.04% for the f7/2 level
- The δ_IAS correction for the decay 25^48Mn(2) → 24^48Cr(0) is 0.14328%
- The first-order mixing correction for the 23^44V(2) → 22^44Ti(0) decay is 1.000 times the reference value, while for 25^48Mn(2) → 24^48Cr(0) it is 1.452 times the reference
- For the upper fp shell, the 33^64As(2) → 32^64Ge(0) decay has a first-order mixing ratio of 2.301 relative to the 31^60Ga(2) → 30^60Zn(0) decay

## Context

This work extends the Sp(4) algebraic model developed by Sviratcheva, Georgieva, and Draayer (2003, 2004) for describing isobaric analog 0+ states to include isospin mixing from charge-dependent pairing correlations. The study is motivated by experimental evidence of isospin mixing from Hagberg (1994) and others, and by the finding from Zuker, Lenzi, and Martinez-Pinedo (2002) that J=0 pairing correlations dominate the isotensor energy difference within isobaric multiplets. The results are consistent with the 2% charge dependence inferred from free nucleon-nucleon scattering data by Henley (1966) and with the isobaric multiplet mass equation coefficients. The work enables identification of which non-analog beta-decay transitions might be most promising for future experimental searches, particularly decays to ground states of even-even N=Z nuclei near mid-shell, while showing that most such transitions are too weak to be detected with current capabilities.
