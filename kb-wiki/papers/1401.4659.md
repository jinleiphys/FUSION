# Search of double shell closure in the superheavy nuclei using a simple effective interaction

## Key claim
The simple effective interaction (SEI) model predicts that the next double magic nucleus beyond Z=82, N=126 occurs at Z=120 with N=182, but the single-particle energy analysis shows a preferred gap at N=184, which is consistent with Skyrme, Gogny, and RMF(NL3) predictions; the discrepancy arises from overestimation of pairing strength in the SEI model.

## Method
The authors used a newly developed simple effective interaction (SEI) with a Gaussian finite-range term, solved via Hartree-Fock formalism with BCS pairing in a constant-gap approximation, and performed spherical calculations for isotopic chains of Z=82, 114, 120, and 126. Results were compared with relativistic mean field (RMF) theory using the NL3 parameter set, the finite-range droplet model (FRDM), and experimental data for known superheavy nuclei.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy of $^{289}$Fl: 2056.80 MeV (SEI), compared to 2064.87 MeV (RMF/NL3), 2071.04 MeV (FRDM), and 2066.06 MeV (experimental/systematics)
- Binding energy of $^{285}$Fl: 2029.41 MeV (SEI), 2039.19 MeV (RMF/NL3), 2044.12 MeV (FRDM), 2040.03 MeV (experimental/systematics)
- Nuclear matter saturation density for SEI: $\rho_0 = 0.157$ fm$^{-3}$ with binding energy per nucleon -16.0 MeV, symmetry energy 35.0 MeV, and incompressibility 245 MeV
- Neutron single-particle gap at N=184 in $^{304}$120: 1.949 MeV (SEI), 1.4503 MeV (RMF/NL3)
- Proton single-particle gap at Z=120 in $^{304}$120: 1.275 MeV (SEI), 2.1781 MeV (RMF/NL3)
- Neutron and proton gaps for $^{302}$120: approximately 0.6 MeV and 1.663 MeV respectively (SEI)
- Chemical potential for $^{302}$120: $\mu_p$ negative and $\mu_n$ negative, indicating stability; for Z=126, N=182/184 combination, $\mu_p = 1.36$ MeV (positive, indicating instability)

## Context
This work extends earlier predictions of superheavy magic numbers using relativistic mean field calculations (Bhuyan 2012) and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock formalisms, which had suggested Z=120 with N=172/182 as the next closed shell. The SEI model confirms Z=120 as the next proton magic number but finds N=182 as the neutron magic number, differing by two neutrons from the N=184 predicted by Skyrme, Gogny, and RMF(NL3) forces. The study enables further investigation into pairing strength adjustments needed for consistent predictions across different theoretical frameworks in the superheavy region.
