# Can shadowing mimic the QCD phase transition?

> The directed flow of protons is studied in the quark-gluon string model as a function of the impact parameter for S+S and Pb+Pb reactions at 160 AGeV/c. A significant reduction of the directed flow in midrapidity range, which can lead to the development of the antiflow, is found due to the absorption of early emitted particles by massive spectators (shadowing effect). This effect can mimic the formation of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). However, in the absorption scenario the antiflow is stronger for the system of light colliding nuclei than for the heavy ones, while in the case of the plasma creation the effect should be opposite.

## Key claim

The reduction of directed proton flow in midrapidity (antiflow) observed in heavy-ion collisions at SPS energies can be produced by purely geometrical absorption effects (shadowing) in a microscopic string model without any quark-gluon plasma formation; however, the shadowing scenario predicts stronger antiflow for light colliding nuclei (S+S) than for heavy ones (Pb+Pb), which is opposite to the expectation from QGP creation.

## Method

The authors used the quark-gluon string model (QGSM), a microscopic model based on Gribov-Regge theory with string phenomenology and rescatterings, to simulate S+S and Pb+Pb collisions at 160 AGeV/c. They calculated the mean in-reaction-plane transverse momentum of protons as a function of rapidity for various impact parameters, and decomposed the total directed flow into normal flow and antiflow components by separating cells where the product of transverse momentum and rapidity is positive or negative.

## Key numbers

* For S+S collisions at 160 AGeV/c with reduced impact parameter $\tilde{b} \leq 0.3$, antiflow develops in the central rapidity region $|y| \leq 1$
* For Pb+Pb collisions at 160 AGeV/c, flow disappearance occurs at $\tilde{b} = 0.3$ within a narrower region $|y| \leq 0.25$, with distinct antiflow appearing only in peripheral collisions with $\tilde{b} \geq 0.75$
* The maximum impact parameters are $b_{max} = 7.11$ fm for S+S and $b_{max} = 13.27$ fm for Pb+Pb
* The center-of-mass momentum is $p_{c.m.} = 8.65$ and center-of-mass rapidity is $y_{c.m.} = 2.9$
* Both normal flow and antiflow components reach their maximum at $t \cong 2$ fm/c after the beginning of collision
* In S+S collisions with $b \cong 2$ fm ($\tilde{b} \cong 0.3$), antiflow dominates over normal flow in the range $|y| \leq 0.4$
* In Pb+Pb collisions, the effect is most pronounced in peripheral collisions with $b \cong 9$ fm ($\tilde{b} \cong 0.7$) or larger

## Context

This work extends earlier QGSM studies by Bravina (1995) at AGS energies to SPS energies, and contrasts with hydrodynamic simulations by Brachmann et al. (1997, 1999) and Csernai and Rohrich (1999) that attribute similar antiflow signals to QGP formation with a softened equation of state. The results show that the shadowing effect produces a characteristic mass-number dependence opposite to that expected from plasma creation, providing a testable criterion to distinguish between the two scenarios when experimental data on proton directed flow in the central rapidity region become available.
