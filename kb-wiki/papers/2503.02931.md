# Relativistic Navier-Stokes description of the quark-gluon plasma radial flow

> The relativistic viscous hydrodynamic description of the quark-gluon plasma by Müller-Israel-Stewart formulations has been very successful, but despite this success, these theories present limitations regarding well-posedness and causality. In recent years, a well-behaved version of the relativistic Navier-Stokes equations has been formulated, appearing as a promising alternative in which those limitations are absent. Using this novel theory, we perform numerical simulations of a quark-gluon plasma fluid that we use to describe experimental data on the transverse momentum distribution of hadrons from central Pb-Pb collisions measured at the LHC.

## Key claim

The relativistic Navier-Stokes equations, when formulated in a suitable hydrodynamic frame (type II field redefinitions), can describe experimental data on the transverse momentum distribution of hadrons from central Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC, despite the fact that the Landau frame version of these equations is not well-posed or causal.

## Method

The authors performed 1+1 dimensional numerical simulations of the relativistic Navier-Stokes equations (BDNK formulation) for a boost-invariant, axially symmetric quark-gluon plasma fluid in Minkowski spacetime, using a QCD equation of state from the HotQCD collaboration (2014) and shear and bulk viscosities parameterized by the JETSCAPE collaboration. They initialized the evolution with a Woods-Saxon energy density profile and vanishing transverse velocity, with first time derivatives set by the ideal hydrodynamic equations of motion, and evolved the system until freezeout on a constant energy density surface, where they applied the Cooper-Frye prescription to obtain the transverse momentum distribution of hadrons.

## Key numbers

- Initial proper time \(\tau_i = 0.6\) fm/c and initial central energy density \(\epsilon_i = 100\) GeV/fm\(^3\) (corresponding to temperature \(T = 485\) MeV) were used for a representative evolution.
- Freezeout energy density \(\epsilon_f = 0.05\) GeV/fm\(^3\) (freezeout temperature \(T_f = 0.12\) GeV) defined the freezeout surface.
- The evolution was stopped at \(\tau = 23\) fm/c.
- The frame parameters used were \(\{a_1, a_2\} = \{\frac{25}{12 c_s^2}, \frac{100}{75-150 c_s^2 +27 c_s^4}\}\), yielding characteristic velocities \(c_i = \pm 1, \pm \frac{3}{5} c_s^2\).
- The Woods-Saxon parameters for Pb were \(R_0 = 6.62\) fm and \(a_0 = 0.546\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the Bemfica-Disconzi-Noronha-Kovtun (BDNK) formulation of relativistic Navier-Stokes (2017-2020) from theoretical proofs of well-posedness and causality to a practical application in heavy-ion physics, where Müller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) theories (e.g., MUSIC, SONIC, VISHNU) have been the standard tool despite known violations of well-posedness and causality conditions (Plumberg 2021, Chiu 2021, ExTrEMe 2023, Domingues 2024). This proof-of-principle enables future rederivation of transport coefficients using the relativistic Navier-Stokes equations and extension to more realistic scenarios such as non-central collisions and Glauber-type initial conditions.
