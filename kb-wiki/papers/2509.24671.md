# Uncertainties with low-resolution nuclear forces

> Low-resolution nuclear Hamiltonians, obtained from chiral effective field theory (EFT) and softened using renormalization group techniques, have been very successful in nuclear structure theory. The associated EFT truncation uncertainty for these potentials is difficult to quantify. We use singular values decompositions of low-resolution nuclear forces to obtain an operator basis to study Hamiltonian uncertainties for these potentials. We perform Bayesian inference for the singular values and three-body low-energy constants, the free parameters of nuclear Hamiltonians in our framework, using likelihoods based on nucleon-nucleon phase shifts and triton observables to account for the EFT truncation uncertainties in these quantities. We propagate the resulting distribution of Hamiltonians forward to predictions for ground-state properties of $^{24,28}$O and $^{48}$Ca, comparing against other state-of-the-art nuclear structure predictions. Our approach makes it possible to account for EFT uncertainties when using low-resolution potentials, which is important for many ongoing studies in exotic nuclei.

## Key claim
The authors claim that singular value decompositions of low-resolution nuclear forces can recover a linear operator structure that enables Bayesian uncertainty quantification for Hamiltonians that have undergone similarity renormalization group evolution, which otherwise destroys the analytic operator basis needed for such statistical treatments.

## Method
The authors perform singular value decompositions of the N$^3$LO Entem-Machleidt nucleon-nucleon potential evolved to $\lambda = 1.8\,\text{fm}^{-1}$, truncating each partial wave at rank five. They then construct a Bayesian likelihood using nucleon-nucleon phase shifts with EFT truncation uncertainties estimated via the Epelbaum-Krebs-Meissner prescription from the more recent EMN interactions evolved to $\lambda = 2.0\,\text{fm}^{-1}$, combined with triton observables to constrain the three-body low-energy constants $c_D$ and $c_E$. The resulting posterior distribution of Hamiltonians is propagated forward to compute ground-state properties of $^{24,28}$O and $^{48}$Ca.

## Key numbers
- Singular value decomposition truncated at rank $R_{\text{SVD}} = 5$ for each partial wave, with the fifth singular value at least two orders of magnitude smaller than the first
- 17 nucleon-nucleon parameters total: three singular values varied in each of $^1$S$_0$, $^3$S$_1$, $^1$P$_1$, $^3$P$_1$, $^3$P$_2$, and two in $^3$P$_0$
- Phase shift likelihood constructed for energies between $0\,\text{MeV}$ and $200\,\text{MeV}$
- EFT breakdown scale $\Lambda_b = 600\,\text{MeV}$ used in the EKM prescription
- Three-body low-energy constants fixed to $c_1 = -0.81$, $c_3 = -3.2$, $c_4 = 5.4$ from Entem (2003)
- Ground-state properties predicted for $^{24,28}$O and $^{48}$Ca

## Context
This work extends Bayesian uncertainty quantification methods developed for chiral EFT potentials by Furnstahl (2015), Epelbaum (2015), and Wesolowski (2016, 2019) to low-resolution Hamiltonians that have been softened via renormalization group evolution. It builds on singular value decomposition techniques demonstrated by Tichai (2021, 2022, 2023) for reproducing nuclear forces with few terms. The approach enables uncertainty quantification for the widely used 1.8/2.0 (EM) interaction from Hebeler (2011), which has been applied to nuclei across the chart by Hagen (2016), Morris (2018), and Stroberg (2021) but previously lacked a complete treatment of EFT truncation uncertainties. This enables future studies of exotic nuclei where such uncertainty estimates are critical.
