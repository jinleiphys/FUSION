# Dynamical symmetry breaking of $λϕ^4$ in two loop effective potential

> The two loop effective potential of massless $λϕ^4$ theory was presented in several regularization and renormalization prescriptions and the dynamical symmetry breaking solution was obtained in strong coupling situation in several prescriptions except the Coleman-Weinberg prescription. The beta function in the broken phase becomes negative and the UV fixed point turns out to be a strong coupling one, and its numeric value varies with renormalization prescriptions, a detail in difference from the asymptotic free solution in one loop case. The symmetry breaking phase was shown to be an entirely strong coupling phase. The reason of the relevance of the renormalization prescriptions was shown to be due to the non-perturbative nature of the effective potential. We also reanalyzed the two loop effective potential by adopting a differential equation approach basing on the understanding that takes all the QFT's as the ill-defined formulations of the 'low energy' effective theories of a complete underlying theory. Then the relevance of the prescriptions of fixing the local ambiguities to physical properties like symmetry breaking was further emphasized. We also tentatively proposed a rescaling insensitivity argument for fixing the quadratic ambiguities. Some detailed properties of the strongly coupled broken phase and related issues were discussed.

## Key claim

The two-loop effective potential of massless λφ⁴ theory permits a dynamical symmetry breaking solution in the strong-coupling regime for several renormalization prescriptions, but not for the Coleman-Weinberg prescription; the existence and properties of the solution depend nontrivially on the choice of renormalization scheme, a feature the authors attribute to the nonperturbative nature of the effective potential.

## Method

The authors computed the two-loop effective potential for massless λφ⁴ theory using both dimensional regularization (with MS-bar renormalization) and cutoff regularization (with three different renormalization prescriptions: a μ²_Λ scheme simulating MS-bar, the Jackiw prescription, and the Coleman-Weinberg prescription). They solved the first-order condition dV/dφ = 0 to find nonzero minima, analyzed the stability of the symmetry-breaking vacuum via the vacuum energy density, and derived the beta function from renormalization-group invariance of the vacuum energy. They also reanalyzed the effective potential using a differential equation approach that treats quantum field theories as low-energy effective theories of an underlying complete theory.

## Key numbers

- The critical coupling for symmetry breaking in the MS-bar scheme: λ_cr = 4.368; the stable critical coupling: λ̂_cr = 5.2024.
- The critical coupling for symmetry breaking in the μ²_Λ scheme: λ_cr = 5.1152; the stable critical coupling: λ̂_cr = 6.5797.
- The critical coupling for symmetry breaking in the Jackiw scheme: λ_cr = 6.5797; the stable critical coupling: λ̂_cr = 10.698.
- The Coleman-Weinberg scheme has α = 16 + 1/3 = 196/12, which violates the inequality α < 1/12 required for symmetry breaking.
- The beta function in the broken phase approaches β(λ) → -4λ as λ → ∞, and β(λ) → -4(λ - λ̂_cr) as λ → λ̂_cr⁺.
- The effective mass at zero momentum scales as m²_eff(μ²) ∝ μ² in the UV (μ → ∞) and as m²_eff(μ²) ∝ 1/μ⁶ in the IR (μ → 0).

## Context

This work extends the one-loop analysis of Stevenson (1985) and Tarrach (1986), who found a nontrivial (asymptotically free) solution for the one-loop effective potential of massless λφ⁴, by including two-loop corrections. It contrasts with the original Coleman-Weinberg (1973) one-loop analysis, where dynamical symmetry breaking was found in the Coleman-Weinberg scheme; here that scheme fails to produce symmetry breaking at two loops. The results show that the symmetry-breaking phase is entirely a strong-coupling phase with a nonzero UV fixed point, unlike the asymptotic freedom found at one loop. The paper enables further investigation of nonperturbative renormalization issues and suggests that the Higgs mechanism might allow alternative scenarios when explored nonperturbatively, with possible implications for color confinement and symmetry breaking in the Standard Model.
