# Importance of multi-mesonic fusion processes on (strange) antibaryon production

> Sufficiently fast chemical equilibration of (strange) antibaryons in an environment of nucleons, pions and kaons during the course of a relativistic heavy ion collision can be understood by a `clustering' of mesons to build up baryon-antibaryon pairs. This multi-mesonic (fusion-type) process has to exist in medium due to the principle of detailed balance. Novel numerical calculations for a dynamical setup are presented. They show that - at maximum SPS energies - yields of each antihyperon specie are obtained which are consistent with chemical saturated populations of T approximately 150-160 MeV, in line with popular chemical freeze-out parameters extracted from thermal model analyses.

## Key claim

Multi-mesonic fusion-type reactions (clustering of pions and kaons into baryon-antibaryon pairs) are the dominant production mechanism for (strange) antibaryons in a moderately dense hadronic environment, and their inclusion via detailed balance resolves the paradox that thermal models describe antihyperon yields well while transport calculations without these back-reactions fail.

## Method

The author derives a master equation from a Boltzmann-type collision integral that incorporates both forward annihilation and backward multi-mesonic fusion reactions via detailed balance. Numerical rate calculations are performed for an expanding hadronic fireball at SPS and AGS energies, using a parametrized volume expansion (longitudinal Bjorken plus transverse flow) and assuming pions, kaons, and baryons remain approximately in thermal equilibrium while antihyperon densities evolve from zero initial abundance.

## Key numbers

- The thermally averaged annihilation cross section used is ⟨σ_ann v⟩ := σ_0 ≡ 40 mb.
- For SPS conditions (initial temperature T_0 = 190 MeV, entropy per baryon S/A = 30, starting time t_0 = 3 fm/c), the antihyperon yields saturate at an effective temperature T_eff ≈ 150–160 MeV.
- For AGS conditions (T_0 = 150 MeV, S/A = 12, t_0 = 5 fm/c), the anti-Λ yield saturates at T_eff ≈ 120–130 MeV.
- The equilibration timescale for antihyperons is estimated as 1–3 fm/c at initial baryon densities of 1–2 times normal nuclear matter density ρ_0.
- For the SPS case, about 5 times more antihyperons are created during the evolution than finally freeze out (for λ = 1 in σ_eff = λ σ_0).

## Context

This work extends the argument of Rapp and Shuryak (2000) on antiproton chemical equilibration via multi-mesonic processes to strange antibaryons (anti-Λ, anti-Ξ). It contradicts earlier hadronic transport approaches (e.g., Soff et al., 1995) that invoked exotic mechanisms like color ropes or string fusion to explain antihyperon yields, and which had omitted the back-reaction channels required by detailed balance. The results enable a dynamical understanding of why thermal model fits (e.g., Braun-Munzinger et al., 1996) yield chemical freeze-out temperatures around 150–160 MeV at SPS, and suggest that future transport codes must incorporate multi-particle fusion reactions to correctly describe antibaryon production.
