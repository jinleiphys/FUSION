# Lambda(1405,1/2^-) photoproduction from the gamma p -> K^+ Lambda(1405) reaction

> We investigate the photoproduction of Lambda(1405,1/2^-) = Lambda* off the proton target using the effective Lagrangian in the Born approximation. We observed that, depending on the choice of the K* N Lambda* coupling strength, the total cross section becomes 0.1 <~ sigma_Lambda* <~ 0.2 mu b near the threshold and starts to decrease beyond E_gamma ~ 1.6 GeV, and the angular dependence shows a mild enhancement in the forward direction. It turns out that the energy dependence of the total cross section is similar to that shown in the recent LEPS experiment. This suggests that the production mechanism of the Lambda* is dominated by the s-channel contribution.

## Key claim
The photoproduction of Λ(1405) off the proton is dominated by the s-channel contribution, not the t-channel as in ground-state Λ(1116) photoproduction, but this conclusion depends strongly on the choice of form factor scheme; using a four-dimensional gauge-invariant form factor instead produces t-channel dominance and a qualitatively different energy dependence.

## Method
The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach in the Born approximation, computing Feynman diagrams for the s-channel, u-channel, t-channel K-exchange, and t-channel K*-exchange contributions to the γ p → K⁺ Λ(1405) reaction. They used coupling constants derived from the chiral unitary model (χUM) and from the SU(3) quark model, with phenomenological electromagnetic and hadronic form factors treated in a gauge-invariant manner. The K*NΛ* coupling strength was treated as a free parameter, and the cutoff mass for the γΛ*Λ* form factor was varied to probe the size effect of the Λ*.

## Key numbers
- Total cross section near threshold: 0.1 ≲ σ_Λ* ≲ 0.2 μb, depending on the K*NΛ* coupling strength
- Total cross section starts to decrease beyond E_γ ≈ 1.6 GeV
- Coupling constant g_KNΛ* = 3.18 (average of χUM values for the higher pole)
- Anomalous magnetic moment κ_Λ* ≈ 0.4 (from SU(3) quark model and χUM)
- Cutoff masses: Λ_h = 650 MeV for hadronic form factors; Λ_EM = 650 MeV for most EM vertices, but Λ_EM = 300 MeV for the γΛ*Λ* vertex (reflecting larger spatial size of Λ*)
- g_γKK* = 0.388 GeV⁻¹ for neutral decay and 0.254 GeV⁻¹ for charged decay
- Estimated EM charge radius of Λ*: 1.36 fm (phenomenological), 1.8 fm (chiral potential model), 1.48 fm (χUM), about twice the proton radius (~0.86 fm)
- Differential cross section estimated from LEPS data: about 0.4 μb for 1.5 ≲ E_γ ≲ 2.0 GeV and 0.8 ≲ cosθ_cm ≲ 1.0

## Context
This work extends theoretical studies of Λ(1405) photoproduction by Williams (1991), who used crossing symmetry and duality, and by Nacher (1998) and Lutz (2004), who used chiral unitary models for the γ p → K⁺ π Σ process. It directly addresses the recent LEPS experiment by Niiyama (2008), which found a large difference in the Λ*/Σ(1385) production ratio between low and high photon energies. The results suggest that the s-channel dominance, rather than the novel internal structure of the Λ*, is responsible for the observed energy dependence, though the authors note that higher nucleon resonances (e.g., P₁₃(1900), F₁₇(1990), F₁₅(2000), D₁₃(2080), S₁₁(2090), P₁₁(2100), G₁₇(2190), D₁₅(2200)) could alter the conclusions and that work on resonance contributions is in progress.
