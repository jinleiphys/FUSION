# Toroidal States of the $^{12}$C Nucleus

> Among the states of $^{12}$C, there is an important subset of $K$=0 and $K$=$I$ planar intrinsic states in which the intrinsic motion of the nucleons are confined in the planar region defined by the three-alpha cluster or by their generated toroid. The intrinsic nuclear densities of these states are toroidal in nature. We study these $^{12}$C toroidal states from the generator-coordinate viewpoints in both the alpha cluster model and the toroidal shell model. Numerical solutions in the toroidal mean field approximation are examined to pave the way for future extensions and refinements.

# Toroidal States of the $^{12}$C Nucleus

## Key claim
The Hoyle state and many of its higher excited states may be tentatively attributed to toroidal configurations of the $^{12}$C nucleus, based on phenomenological matching of the spectrum with toroidal nucleus signatures; however, in the mean-field approximation with a single Slater determinant, the state at the Hoyle energy lies on an energy slope as a function of the radial parameter and is unstable against radial contraction, requiring calculations beyond the mean field to confirm stability.

## Method
The author studies $^{12}$C toroidal states using the generator-coordinate method in two complementary frameworks: the alpha cluster model, where toroidal wave functions are obtained by projecting triangular three-alpha cluster states over all orientations, and the toroidal shell model, where the basis consists of toroidal single-particle wave functions. In the toroidal mean-field approximation, a single Slater determinant with variational parameters (major radius R, diffuseness d, and deformation a₂) is solved using the Skyrme SkM* interaction, and the effects of a residual octupole-octupole interaction are explored by introducing a sausage deformation parameter σ₃.

## Key numbers
- The ground state variational parameters are (R=0, d=1.49 fm, a₂=-0.089), giving a nuclear density where equidensity surfaces become toroidal for n ≥ 0.21 fm⁻³.
- The provisional toroidal state at the Hoyle energy has variational parameters (R=1.16 fm, d=1.370 fm, a₂=-0.007) with excitation energy E_x = 7.65 MeV.
- With an octupole-octupole interaction strength χ = 0.02 MeV, the energy surface shows a saddle point at (R=3 fm, σ₃ ≈ 0.08) with a barrier height of about 35-40 MeV.
- No energy minimum is found at the Hoyle energy of E_x = 7.654 MeV for a linear residual octupole-octupole interaction with a single Slater determinant.

## Context
This work extends Wheeler's original suggestions (Wheeler, 1950; Wheeler, 1998) that nuclear fluid may assume toroidal shapes under extreme conditions, and applies them specifically to $^{12}$C, which was identified as a favorable candidate due to strong toroidal shell effects (Wong, 1973). The study connects the toroidal concept to the well-studied three-alpha cluster model of $^{12}$C (Wheeler, 1937; Griffin, Hill, and Wheeler, 1957; Kamimura, 1981; Uegaki et al., 1979) and to recent calculations of toroidal high-spin isomers in light alpha-conjugate nuclei (Ichimura, 2012; Staszczak et al., 2014-2016; Cao et al., 2019). The results enable future calculations using the Griffin-Hill-Wheeler equation with a two-body residual interaction and multiple Slater determinants to study the stability and structure of the Hoyle state and its excited states.
