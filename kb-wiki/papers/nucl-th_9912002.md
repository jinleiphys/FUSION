# Neutron Stars: Recent Developments

> Recent developments in neutron star theory and observation are discussed. Based on modern nucleon-nucleon potentials more reliable equations of state for dense nuclear matter have been constructed. Furthermore, phase transitions such as pion, kaon and hyperon condensation, superfluidity and quark matter can occur in cores of neutron stars. Specifically, the nuclear to quark matter phase transition and its mixed phases with intriguing structures is treated. Rotating neutron stars with and without phase transitions are discussed and compared to observed masses, radii and glitches. The observations of possible heavy $\sim 2M_\odot$ neutron stars in X-ray binaries and QPO's require relatively stiff equation of states and restrict strong phase transitions to occur at very high nuclear densities only.

## Key claim

Observations of possible heavy neutron stars (∼2 M⊙) in X‑ray binaries and quasi‑periodic oscillations require a relatively stiff equation of state and restrict strong phase transitions to occur only at very high nuclear densities; however, the interpretation of the QPO data as evidence for an innermost stable orbit is not yet firm because large accretion can slow the accreting matter.

## Method

The author parametrizes the APR98 equation of state for β‑stable pn‑matter with a softness parameter s that smoothly enforces causality (sound speed ≤ c) at high densities, then solves the Tolman‑Oppenheimer‑Volkoff equation with and without rotational corrections. Phase transitions to quark matter are incorporated via either a mixed‑phase (Gibbs) construction or a Maxwell construction, using bag‑model parameters B = 150 and 200 MeV fm⁻³ and α_s = 0.4. The resulting mass‑radius relations are compared to the observed QPO frequency of 4U 1820‑30 (1060 Hz) and to binary‑pulsar masses.

## Key numbers

- Binary‑pulsar masses all lie in the range 1.35 ± 0.04 M⊙ (95% confidence).
- Vela X‑1 mass: (1.9 ± 0.2) M⊙; Cygnus X‑2 mass: (1.8 ± 0.4) M⊙.
- Maximum QPO frequency of 4U 1820‑30: 1060 Hz, giving M ≃ 2.25 M⊙ after correcting for a spin frequency ν_s ≃ 275 Hz.
- For 4U 1608‑52 (ν_QPO = 1125 Hz) and 4U 1636‑536 (ν_QPO = 1228 Hz), the corresponding masses are 2.1 M⊙ and 1.9 M⊙.
- Nuclear saturation density n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³; binding energy per nucleon ℰ₀ = 15.8 MeV.
- Best‑fit symmetry‑energy parameters: S₀ = 32 MeV, γ = 0.6.
- Causality requires s ≳ 0.13; observed 1.4 M⊙ restricts s < 0.4–0.5.
- For B = 150 MeV fm⁻³, the mixed phase begins at 0.51 fm⁻³ and pure quark matter at 1.89 fm⁻³; for B = 200 MeV fm⁻³, mixed phase starts at 0.72 fm⁻³ and pure quark phase at 2.11 fm⁻³.
- Pure quark‑star maximum mass: M_max = 2.0 M⊙ (58 MeV fm⁻³ / B)^{1/2} (for α_s = 0).
- Glitch healing parameter Q: for Vela ∼3% of the moment of inertia is in the rigid component; for Crab ∼96%.

## Context

This review extends the work of Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall (1998) on the APR98 equation of state and the causality‑bound analysis of Kalogera and Baym (1996) by introducing a smooth parametrization that enforces causality at high densities rather than a discontinuous jump to the speed of light. It also builds on the mixed‑phase treatment of Glendenning (1992) and the structure calculations of Heiselberg and collaborators (1999). The results enable tighter constraints on the nuclear equation of state from observed neutron‑star masses and QPO frequencies, and they motivate further searches for first‑order phase transitions via the braking‑index signature in rotating pulsars.
