# Superscaling analysis of quasielastic electron scattering with relativistic effective mass

> We provide a parametrization of a new phenomenological scaling function obtained from a chi-square fit to a selected set of (e,e') cross section data expanding a band centered around the quasielastic peak. We start from a re-analysis of quasielastic electron scattering from nuclear matter within the relativistic mean field model. The cross section depends on the relativistic effective mass of the nucleon, $m_N^*$, and it scales with respect to a new scaling variable, $ψ^*$. This suggests a new superscaling approach with effective mass (SuSAM*) for predicting quasielastic cross sections within an uncertainty band. The model reproduces previously established results on the enhancement of the transverse response function as compared to the traditional relativistic Fermi gas.

## Key claim

The paper provides a new phenomenological scaling function, obtained from a chi-square fit to a selected set of (e,e') cross section data, that maintains scaling with respect to a new scaling variable psi* derived from the relativistic mean field model with an effective nucleon mass M* = 0.8, rather than assuming the traditional scaling function f_L = f_T used in earlier SuSA models.

## Method

The authors re-analyze quasielastic electron scattering from nuclear matter within the relativistic mean field (RMF) model, where the cross section depends on the relativistic effective mass m_N* and scales with a new scaling variable psi*. They select approximately 1000 experimental (e,e') cross section data points for 12C out of about 2500 total, using a density criterion (n >= 25 neighbors within a circle of radius r = 0.1) to isolate quasielastic events. The selected data are fitted with a sum of two Gaussian functions to produce a central phenomenological scaling function f*(psi*) and upper/lower uncertainty bands, which are then used to compute longitudinal and transverse response functions and cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Effective mass ratio M* = m_N*/m_N = 0.8, providing the best scaling behavior of the data.
- Fermi momentum k_F = 225 MeV/c for 12C.
- The selected quasielastic data set contains about 1000 data points out of the approximately 2500 existing data for 12C.
- The transverse scaling function f_T is about 20% larger than the longitudinal scaling function f_L under the CC2 prescription, consistent with earlier RMF results from Caballero (2007).
- The phenomenological scaling function is parametrized as a sum of two Gaussians with coefficients: central values a1 = -0.0465, a2 = 0.469, a3 = 0.633, b1 = 0.707, b2 = 1.073, b3 = 0.202; with min and max band parameters given in Table 1.

## Context

This work extends the superscaling approach (SuSA) originally developed by Donnelly (1999) and Amaro (2004), which assumed f_T = f_L, and the SuSA-v2 model by Gonzalez-Jimenez (2014) that introduced a q-dependent transverse scaling function at the cost of violating scaling. The new SuSAM* model maintains exact scaling with respect to psi* while incorporating the transverse response enhancement seen in RMF calculations by Caballero (2007). The parametrized scaling function and uncertainty band provide a test for theoretical models of quasielastic scattering and can be extended to constrain quasielastic neutrino scattering in ongoing accelerator experiments.
