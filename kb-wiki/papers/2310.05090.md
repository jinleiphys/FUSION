# Impact of choices for center-of-mass correction energy on the surface energy of Skyrme energy density functionals

> In the framework of nuclear energy density functional (EDF) methods, many nuclear phenomena can be related to the deformation of intrinsic states. Their accurate modeling relies on the correct description of the change of nuclear binding energy with deformation. The two most important contributions to the deformation energy have their origin in shell effects and the surface energy coefficient of nuclear matter. In a first step, we build nine series of parametrizations with a systematically varied surface-energy coefficient a_surf for three frequently-used options for the CM correction (none, one-body term only, full one-body and two-body contributions) combined with three values for the isoscalar effective mass m^*_0/m (0.7, 0.8, 0.85) and analyse how well each of these parametrizations can be adjusted to the properties of spherical nuclei and infinite nuclear matter. In a second step, we performed additional fits without the constraint on surface energy, adding one ``best-fit" parametrization to each of the nine series. We then benchmark these parametrizations to the deformation properties of heavy nuclei by means of three-dimensional Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations that allow for non-axial and/or non-reflection symmetric configurations. We perform a detailed correlation analysis between surface and volume properties of nuclear matter using the nine series of parametrizations. The best fits out of each series are then benchmarked on the fission barriers of Pu240 and Hg180, as well as on the properties of deformed states at normal and superdeformation for actinides and nuclei in the neutron-deficient Hg region. (see paper for full abstract)

## Key claim

The optimal value of the surface energy coefficient \(a_{\text{surf}}\) depends strongly on the choice of center-of-mass (c.m.) correction scheme and the isoscalar effective mass \(m^*_0/m\); only when the full one-body and two-body c.m. correction is included does an adjustment protocol constrained only to infinite nuclear matter and spherical nuclei yield realistic surface properties for fission barriers and superdeformed states.

## Method

Nine series of Skyrme energy density functional parametrizations at next-to-leading order (NLO) in gradients were constructed by systematically varying the surface energy coefficient \(a_{\text{surf}}\) for three c.m. correction options (none, one-body only, full one-body and two-body) combined with three values of the isoscalar effective mass \(m^*_0/m\) (0.7, 0.8, 0.85). Each series was adjusted to properties of seven doubly-magic nuclei and infinite nuclear matter using a penalty function, with the saturation density fixed to \(\rho_{\text{sat}} = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\). Additional best-fit parametrizations without the constraint on \(a_{\text{surf}}\) were produced for each of the nine combinations. The parametrizations were then benchmarked via three-dimensional Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations on fission barriers of \(^{240}\text{Pu}\) and \(^{180}\text{Hg}\) and on deformed states in actinides and neutron-deficient Hg nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Surface energy coefficient \(a_{\text{surf}}\) was constrained to target values varying between 15.5 MeV and 20.0 MeV in steps of 0.2 MeV with a tolerance of 0.01 MeV.
- Isoscalar effective mass values used: \(m^*_0/m = 0.70\), 0.80, and 0.85.
- Saturation density fixed to \(\rho_{\text{sat}} = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
- Target values for infinite nuclear matter: energy per nucleon \(\varepsilon_{\text{sat}} = -16.0\ \text{MeV}\) (tolerance \(\pm 0.1\ \text{MeV}\)), symmetry energy coefficient \(J = 32\ \text{MeV}\) (tolerance \(\pm 1\ \text{MeV}\)), slope \(L = 50\ \text{MeV}\) (tolerance \(\pm 5\ \text{MeV}\)).
- Binding energies of seven doubly-magic nuclei from AME20 were used, e.g., \(^{208}\text{Pb}\) at \(-1635.862\ \text{MeV}\) (tolerance \(\pm 1.0\ \text{MeV}\)).
- The difference in binding energy between \(^{56}\text{Ni}\) and \(^{40}\text{Ca}\) was constrained to 141.920 MeV with a tolerance of 1 MeV.
- The power \(\alpha\) of the density dependence was set to \(1/6\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Jodon et al. (2016) on the SLy5sX series, which varied \(a_{\text{surf}}\) with only the one-body c.m. correction, by systematically comparing all three c.m. correction schemes and three effective masses. It confirms earlier indications by Ben00f that the c.m. correction choice impacts surface properties, and clarifies why parametrizations like SkM* (Bartel 1982) and UNEDF1 (Kortelainen 2012) required explicit adjustment to fission data. The results enable the construction of future parametrizations with the full c.m. correction that automatically yield realistic deformation energies, as recently demonstrated by the BSkG2 and BSkG3 mass fits (Ryssens 2022, 2023; Grams 2023).
