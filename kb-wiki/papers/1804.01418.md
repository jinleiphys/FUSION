# Dark Matter Effects On Neutron Star Properties

> We study possible effects of a dark matter (DM) core on the maximum mass of a neutron star (NS), on the mass-radius relation and on the NS tidal deformability parameter $Λ$. We show that all these quantities would in general be reduced in the presence of a DM core. In particular, our calculations indicate that the presence of a DM core with a mass fraction $\sim 5\%$ could affect significantly the interpretation of these NS data as constraints on the nuclear equation of state (EOS), potentially excluding some EOS models on the basis of the measured mass of PSR J0348+0432, while allowing other EOS models to become consistent with the LIGO/Virgo upper limit on $Λ$. Specific scenarios for generating such DM cores are explored in an Appendix.

## Key claim

The presence of a dark matter core with a mass fraction of approximately 5% of the total neutron star mass would generally reduce the maximum mass, radius, and tidal deformability of the neutron star, potentially complicating the interpretation of observational data as constraints on the nuclear equation of state. The authors qualify that these effects depend on the DM mass fraction and self-interaction strength, and that the DM mass fraction is not expected to be universal across all neutron stars.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for two-fluid systems (baryonic matter and dark matter) assuming negligible nongravitational interactions between the two components. They modeled the baryonic component using 11 different nuclear equations of state that reproduce a maximal NS mass exceeding 1.97 solar masses, and modeled the DM component primarily using a Bose-Einstein condensate equation of state for self-interacting bosonic DM, with the DM self-interaction strength parameterized by sqrt(sigma_D)/m_D^3. The tidal deformability Lambda was calculated from the resulting density and pressure profiles using the l=2 tidal Love number formalism.

## Key numbers

- DM core mass fraction of 5% of total NS mass is used as the primary illustrative value throughout the paper
- The observation of PSR J0348+0432 sets a lower bound on maximal NS mass of 1.97 solar masses; an upper bound of 2.33 solar masses is inferred from GW170817 assuming the merger product collapsed to a black hole (Rezzolla 2017)
- For a NS with baryonic mass 1.4 solar masses, the DM core mass is forbidden above approximately 0.3 solar masses by stability requirements
- The LIGO/Virgo 90% confidence level upper limit on tidal deformability is Lambda < 800; a tentative lower bound Lambda > 400 was obtained by Radice (2017) combining optical/infrared and GW observations
- For the H4 EOS with a 1.4 solar mass baryonic component, the transition from DM core to DM halo occurs when log10(M_D/M_N) is approximately -2.18 for sqrt(sigma_D)/m_D^3 = 16/GeV^2
- In the DM halo case with R_D > R_N, maximum tidal deformability values for the H4 EOS are Lambda = O(10^3), exceeding the current 90% confidence level bound of 800

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Ellis 2017) on DM cores in NSs and their potential signatures in postmerger GW frequency spectra, and complements the DM halo model of Nelson (2018) which found an increase in tidal deformability. The paper shows that DM cores with 5% mass fraction could rule out EOS models ALF2, H4, and SLy4 based on the PSR J0348+0432 mass measurement, while allowing EOS models MS1, MS1B, and H4 to become consistent with the LIGO/Virgo upper limit on Lambda. The results enable future GW observations to potentially distinguish between DM core and DM halo scenarios, and suggest that DM effects could complicate the interpretation of NS measurements as constraints on the nuclear EOS.
