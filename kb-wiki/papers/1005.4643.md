# Nonextensive statistical effects in the hadron to quark-gluon phase transition

> We investigate the relativistic equation of state of hadronic matter and quark-gluon plasma at finite temperature and baryon density in the framework of the nonextensive statistical mechanics, characterized by power-law quantum distributions. We study the phase transition from hadronic matter to quark-gluon plasma by requiring the Gibbs conditions on the global conservation of baryon number and electric charge fraction. We show that nonextensive statistical effects play a crucial role in the equation of state and in the formation of mixed phase also for small deviations from the standard Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics.

## Key claim
The paper shows that even small deviations from standard Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics (characterized by the nonextensive parameter \(q>1\)) significantly affect the hadronic and quark-gluon equations of state and the formation of a mixed phase, reducing the critical baryon densities for the onset of deconfinement. The hedge is that these effects are studied only for \(q>1\) and for small deviations from standard statistics, focusing on the regime of lower temperatures and higher baryon chemical potentials than the critical endpoint.

## Method
The authors model hadronic matter using a relativistic mean field theory (Walecka-type) with \(\sigma\), \(\omega\), and \(\rho\) meson fields (GM2 parameter set) and include pions as a quasi-ideal Bose gas. Quark-gluon plasma is described by the MIT bag model with massless up and down quarks and gluons, using a bag constant \(B^{1/4}=190\) MeV. The phase transition is studied by imposing Gibbs conditions for global conservation of baryon number and electric charge fraction (\(Z/A=0.4\)), with particle distributions replaced by \(q\)-deformed Fermi-Dirac and Bose-Einstein distributions from Tsallis nonextensive statistics.

## Key numbers
- At \(T=90\) MeV and \(q=1.10\), the first critical baryon density \(\rho^{I}_{\rm cr}/\rho_0 = 1.91\) (where \(\rho_0=0.153\) fm\(^{-3}\)), compared to 3.41 for \(q=1.00\).
- At \(T=90\) MeV and \(q=1.10\), the first critical baryon chemical potential \(\mu^{I}_{\rm cr}=916\) MeV, compared to 1123 MeV for \(q=1.00\).
- At \(T=120\) MeV and \(q=1.10\), the first critical baryon density \(\rho^{I}_{\rm cr}/\rho_0 = 0.08\), compared to 0.45 for \(q=1.00\).
- At \(T=120\) MeV and \(q=1.10\), the first critical baryon chemical potential \(\mu^{I}_{\rm cr}=184\) MeV, compared to 588 MeV for \(q=1.00\).
- At \(T=60\) MeV and \(q=1.10\), the first critical baryon density \(\rho^{I}_{\rm cr}/\rho_0 = 5.33\), compared to 5.75 for \(q=1.00\).
- The bag constant is set to \(B^{1/4}=190\) MeV.
- Lattice QCD critical temperature is cited as \(T_c \approx 170\) MeV with critical energy density \(\epsilon_c \approx 1\) GeV/fm\(^3\) (Karsch, year not given in text).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of nonextensive effects on the hadronic equation of state using a Walecka-type model (Silva, year not given) and on the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Wilk and collaborators, year not given) by incorporating both hadronic and quark-gluon phases with two conserved charges. It enables future comparisons with data from the CBM experiment at FAIR and low-energy RHIC scans, where the predicted reduction of critical densities due to nonextensive effects could be tested. The authors note that a density- or temperature-dependent bag constant (as in Burgio, Rafelski, and Prasad, years not given) would be needed for a more complete description over a large range of temperature and density.
