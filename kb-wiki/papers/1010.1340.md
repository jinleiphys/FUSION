# Role of non-coplanarity in nuclear reactions using the Wong formula based on the proximity potential

> we assessed Wong's formula for its angular momentum $\ell$-summation and "barrier modification" effects at sub-barrier energies in the dominant fusion-evaporation and capture (equivalently, quasi-fission) reaction cross-sections. For use of the multipole deformations (up to $β_4$) and (in-plane, $Φ$=0$^0$) orientations-dependent proximity potential in fusion-evaporation cross-sections of $^{58}$Ni+$^{58}$Ni, $^{64}$Ni+$^{64}$Ni and $^{100}$Mo, known for fusion hindrance phenomenon in coupled-channels calculations, and the capture cross-sections of $^{48}$Ca+$^{238}$U, $^{244}$Pu and $^{248}$Cm reactions, forming superheavy nuclei, though the simple $\ell$=0 barrier-based Wong formula is found inadequate, its extended version, the $\ell$-summed Wong expression fits very well the above noted capture cross-sections at all center-of-mass energies $E_{c.m.}$'s, but require (additional) modifications of the barriers to fit the fusion-evaporation cross-sections in the Ni-based reactions at below-barrier energies. Some barrier modification effects are shown \cite{kumar09} to be already present in Wong expression due to its inbuilt $\ell$-dependence via $\ell$-summation.

# Role of non-coplanarity in nuclear reactions using the Wong formula based on the proximity potential

## Key claim

The extended, ℓ-summed Wong formula fits capture cross-sections for superheavy nucleus formation at all center-of-mass energies without barrier modification, but fusion-evaporation cross-sections in Ni-based reactions require additional empirical barrier lowering; however, for fission reactions forming ²⁴⁶Bk*, including the non-coplanarity degree of freedom (Φ ≠ 0°) eliminates the need for any barrier modification, whereas the coplanar (Φ = 0°) case still requires it for one entrance channel.

## Method

The authors applied the ℓ-summed Wong formula using a proximity potential with multipole deformations up to β₄ and orientation-dependent potentials, explicitly summing over angular momentum ℓ up to an empirically determined ℓ_max. They compared coplanar (Φ = 0°) and non-coplanar (Φ ≠ 0°) calculations for three reaction types: capture (⁴⁸Ca+²³⁸U), fusion-evaporation (⁶⁴Ni+¹⁰⁰Mo), and fission (¹¹B+²³⁵U and ¹⁴N+²³²Th forming ²⁴⁶Bk*), with empirical barrier lowering ΔV_B^emp applied where needed.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁸Ca+²³⁸U capture, a small deformation β₂₁ = 0.183 was given to ⁴⁸Ca for carrying out Φ-integration, and the fitted ℓ_max(E_c.m.) increased by one-to-two units.
- For ⁶⁴Ni+¹⁰⁰Mo fusion-evaporation, fits required empirical barrier lowering ΔV_B^emp for both Φ = 0° and Φ ≠ 0° cases.
- For ¹¹B+²³⁵U fission forming ²⁴⁶Bk*, the Φ = 0° case required a large ΔV_B^emp at higher energies, which reduced to zero for the Φ ≠ 0° case.
- For ¹⁴N+²³²Th fission forming ²⁴⁶Bk*, a nice fit was obtained for both Φ = 0° and Φ ≠ 0° cases without barrier modification.

## Context

This work extends the earlier assessment by Kumar (2009) of Wong's formula for ℓ-summation and barrier modification effects in fusion-evaporation and capture reactions. It introduces for the first time the non-coplanarity degree of freedom (Φ ≠ 0°) to fission reactions such as ¹¹B+²³⁵U and ¹⁴N+²³²Th studied by Behera (2001). The results enable a unified description of fusion-evaporation, capture, and fission cross-sections within the same ℓ-summed Wong framework, showing that non-coplanarity can account for what previously required empirical barrier adjustments.
