# Correlation functions of impedance and scattering matrix elements in chaotic absorbing cavities

> Wave scattering in chaotic systems with a uniform energy loss (absorption) is considered. Within the random matrix approach we calculate exactly the energy correlation functions of different matrix elements of impedance or scattering matrices for systems with preserved or broken time-reversal symmetry. The obtained results are valid at any number of arbitrary open scattering channels and arbitrary absorption. Elastic enhancement factors (defined through the ratio of the corresponding variance in reflection to that in transmission) are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact analytic expressions for the energy correlation functions of impedance and scattering matrix elements in chaotic cavities with uniform absorption, valid for any number of open channels and any absorption strength, for both preserved and broken time-reversal symmetry. The abstract glosses over the fact that the impedance correlation function is directly related to the two-level cluster function (or its form factor), while the S-matrix correlation function requires a more complicated supersymmetry calculation that is completed here for the unitary symmetry case.

## Method

The authors employ the random matrix approach, replacing the Hamiltonian of the closed chaotic system with a random Hermitian matrix from the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE, for preserved time-reversal symmetry) or Gaussian Unitary Ensemble (GUE, for broken time-reversal symmetry). For the impedance correlation function, the calculation reduces to spectral correlations determined by the two-point cluster function, using a representation in the eigenbasis of the closed system with Gaussian-distributed random coupling amplitudes. For the S-matrix correlation function, the statistical average is performed using the supersymmetry method (nonlinear zero-dimensional supersymmetric sigma-model), with saddle-point integration over a nontrivial manifold parametrized by supermatrices, following the approach of Verbaarschot, Weidenmuller and Zirnbauer (1985) but extended to the unitary symmetry case.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless absorption strength is defined as \(\gamma \equiv 2\pi\Gamma/\Delta\), where \(\Gamma\) is the absorption width and \(\Delta\) is the mean level spacing; \(\gamma \ll 1\) corresponds to weak absorption, \(\gamma \gg 1\) to strong absorption.
- For the impedance elastic enhancement factor \(W_{Z,\beta}\), the limiting values are \(W_{Z,\beta} = 2 + \delta_{1\beta}\) at \(\gamma \ll 1\) and \(W_{Z,\beta} = 1 + \delta_{1\beta}\) at \(\gamma \gg 1\), where \(\delta_{1\beta}=1\) for orthogonal symmetry and 0 for unitary symmetry.
- For unitary symmetry, the impedance enhancement factor is given explicitly as \(W_{Z,2} = 1 + \frac{1}{\gamma}(1 - e^{-\gamma})\).
- For the S-matrix in the many-channel limit with total width \(\gamma_T = \sum_c T_c + \gamma\), the enhancement factor \(W_{S,\beta}\) follows the same expression as \(W_{Z,\beta}\) with \(\gamma\) replaced by \(\gamma_T\); at \(\gamma_T \gg 1\), \(W_{S,\beta} = 2/\beta\).

## Context

This work extends the exact calculation of S-matrix correlation functions by Verbaarschot, Weidenmuller and Zirnbauer (1985) for orthogonal symmetry to the case of unitary symmetry (broken time-reversal symmetry), and provides the first exact calculation of impedance correlation functions at arbitrary absorption and coupling. The results enable quantitative comparison with microwave cavity experiments such as those by Kuhl (2005) on S-matrix statistics and Hemmady (2005) on impedance measurements, and unify the description of these complementary experimental approaches. The impedance correlation function is related to earlier work on reverberation in complex structures by Davy (1987) and Lobkis (2000), and to chaotic photodissociation by Alhassid (1992, 1998).
