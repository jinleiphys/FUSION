# Chiral Vortical Effect for Bosons

> The thermal contribution to the chiral vortical effect is believed to be related to the axial anomaly in external gravitational fields. We use the universality of the spin-gravity interaction to extend this idea to a wider set of phenomena. We consider the Kubo formula at weak coupling for the spin current of a vector field and derive a novel anomalous effect caused by the medium rotation: the chiral vortical effect for bosons. The effect consists in a spin current of vector bosons along the angular velocity of the medium. We argue that it has the same anomalous nature as in the fermionic case and show that this effect provides a mechanism for helicity transfer, from flow helicity to magnetic helicity.

# Chiral Vortical Effect for Bosons

## Key claim
The authors derive a novel anomalous effect for vector bosons, a spin current along the angular velocity of a rotating medium, and argue that this bosonic chiral vortical effect (bCVE) has the same anomalous origin as the thermal chiral vortical effect (tCVE) for fermions, providing a microscopic mechanism to transfer flow helicity into magnetic helicity. The derivation relies on the conjectured connection between the gravitational anomaly and the tCVE, which remains under active discussion.

## Method
The authors compute the Kubo formula at weak coupling for the two-point function of the Chern current and the stress-energy tensor of a massive vector field in the Proca formalism, taking the massless limit at the end. The calculation uses the Matsubara technique with dimensional regularization, expanding in powers of momentum and evaluating the resulting divergent sum over bosonic thermal frequencies via zeta-function regularization. The result is then interpreted as the bCVE conductivity for massless vector bosons.

## Key numbers
- The bCVE conductivity is σ_V = T²/12, yielding a spin current K = (T²/6)Ω, where Ω is the local angular velocity of the medium.
- The fermionic tCVE axial current is J_A^μ = (μ_V² + μ_A²)/(2π²) + T²/6 times the vorticity ω^μ.
- The gravitational anomaly for vector bosons gives ∇_μ K^μ = (1/(96π²)) ε^{μναβ} R^λ_{ρμν} R^ρ_{λαβ}, compared to the fermionic gravitational anomaly coefficient of -1/(192π²).
- The corrected tCVE in the presence of dynamical gauge fields is J_5^μ = (T²/6)(1 + e²/(4π²)) ω^μ.

## Context
This work extends the connection between the gravitational anomaly and the chiral vortical effect, previously established for fermions by Landsteiner et al. (2011), to bosonic fields of arbitrary spin, motivated by the universality of spin-gravity interaction noted by Alvarez-Gaume and Witten (1983) and Dolgov et al. (1987). The bCVE provides a microscopic mechanism for helicity transfer from flow helicity to magnetic helicity, supporting the generalized axial charge conservation picture of Avdoshkin et al. (2014). This enables new studies of anomalous transport in systems such as the quark-gluon plasma, P-odd condensed matter, and cosmological primordial plasma, and connects to ongoing work in chiral kinetic theory by Yamamoto (2017).
