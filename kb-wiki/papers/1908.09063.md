# How to Improve Functionals in Density Functional Theory? ---Formalism and Benchmark Calculation---

> We proposed in Ref. [arXiv:1812.09285v2] a way to improve energy density functionals in the density functional theory based on the combination of the inverse Kohn-Sham method and the density functional perturbation theory. In this proceeding, we mainly focus on the results for the $ \mathrm{Ar} $ and $ \mathrm{Kr} $ atoms.

# How to Improve Functionals in Density Functional Theory?, Formalism and Benchmark Calculation, ## Key claim
The IKS-DFPT method can improve energy density functionals by combining the inverse Kohn-Sham method with density functional perturbation theory, but only if the initial functional is already close enough to the exact functional that the difference can be treated as a small perturbation; otherwise the final results would be unreasonable.

## Method
The authors propose the IKS-DFPT1 method, which uses first-order density functional perturbation theory (the Hellmann-Feynman theorem) to correct an approximate Hartree-exchange-correlation functional. They assume a polynomial form for the first-order correction term, $E^{(1)}_{\mathrm{Hxc}}[\rho] = A \int [\rho(\mathbf{r})]^\alpha d\mathbf{r}$, and determine the parameters $A$ and $\alpha$ by requiring consistency between two expressions for the ground-state energy, one from DFPT and one from the IKS and KS equations, using two different atomic systems as input.

## Key numbers
- For the Ar-Kr pair, the extracted exponent $\alpha = 1.3290958$ (target: $1.3333333$, error $0.318\%$) and the coefficient $\lambda A = -0.7658732$ (target: $-0.7385588$, error $3.70\%$), in Hartree atomic units.
- Across all noble-gas atom pairs (He, Ne, Ar, Kr, Xe, Rn), $\alpha$ is obtained within approximately $1.0\%$ error or better, with the best pair (Xe-Rn) giving $\alpha = 1.3311445$ (error $0.164\%$).
- The coefficient $\lambda A$ is obtained with around $5\%$ errors on average, with the best pair (Xe-Rn) giving $\lambda A = -0.7558229$ (error $2.34\%$).
- The ground-state energy of Ar improves from $-497.3858$ (Hartree only) to $-525.1119$ (IKS-DFPT), compared to the target of $-524.5143$; for Kr, from $-2659.6912$ to $-2748.1434$, compared to the target of $-2746.7828$ (all in Hartree atomic units).
- The accuracy of ground-state energies improves by two to three orders of magnitude, and ground-state densities improve by one to two orders of magnitude after the IKS-DFPT correction.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier proposal (Naito et al., arXiv:1812.09285v2) for improving energy density functionals by combining the inverse Kohn-Sham method (introduced by Wang and Parr, 1992, and by van Leeuwen and Baerends, 1994) with density functional perturbation theory (developed by Baroni et al., 1987, and Gonze, 1989). As a benchmark, the method successfully reproduces the LDA exchange functional (Dirac, 1930) starting from the Hartree functional alone, using noble-gas atoms. The approach is promising for application to nuclear density functional theory, where improving existing functionals is a major goal.
