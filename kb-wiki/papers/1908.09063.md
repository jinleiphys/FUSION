# How to Improve Functionals in Density Functional Theory? ---Formalism and Benchmark Calculation---

> We proposed in Ref. [arXiv:1812.09285v2] a way to improve energy density functionals in the density functional theory based on the combination of the inverse Kohn-Sham method and the density functional perturbation theory. In this proceeding, we mainly focus on the results for the $ \mathrm{Ar} $ and $ \mathrm{Kr} $ atoms.

# How to Improve Functionals in Density Functional Theory?, Formalism and Benchmark Calculation, ## Key claim

The IKS-DFPT method can improve energy density functionals by combining the inverse Kohn-Sham method with density functional perturbation theory, but only if the starting functional is already close enough to the exact functional that the difference can be treated as a small perturbation; otherwise the final results would be unreasonable.

## Method

The authors propose the IKS-DFPT1 method, which uses first-order density functional perturbation theory (the Hellmann-Feynman theorem) to correct an approximate Hartree-exchange-correlation functional. The correction term is assumed to have a simple power-law form \( E^{(1)}_{\mathrm{Hxc}}[\rho] = A \int [\rho(\mathbf{r})]^\alpha \, d\mathbf{r} \), with the two parameters \( A \) and \( \alpha \) determined by solving a functional equation using exact ground-state densities from two different systems. As a benchmark, the Hartree functional is used as the starting approximation and the Hartree plus LDA exchange functional (Hartree-Fock-Slater approximation) as the target to be reproduced, using pairs of isolated noble-gas atoms.

## Key numbers

- For the Ar-Kr pair, the extracted exponent \( \alpha = 1.3290958 \) compared to the target value \( 1.3333333 \), giving an error of \( 0.3\% \); the coefficient \( \lambda A = -0.7658732 \) compared to the target \( -0.7385588 \), giving an error of \( 3.7\% \).
- Across all noble-gas pairs (He, Ne, Ar, Kr, Xe, Rn), \( \alpha \) is obtained within \( 1.0\% \) error or better, while \( \lambda A \) is obtained with around \( 5\% \) errors.
- The best reproduction is for the Xe-Rn pair, with \( \alpha = 1.3311445 \) (error \( 0.164\% \)) and \( \lambda A = -0.7558229 \) (error \( 2.34\% \)).
- The original (Hartree) ground-state energy for Ar is \( -497.3858 \) a.u.; after IKS-DFPT it becomes \( -525.1119 \) a.u., compared to the target \( -524.5143 \) a.u.
- The original (Hartree) ground-state energy for Kr is \( -2659.6912 \) a.u.; after IKS-DFPT it becomes \( -2748.1434 \) a.u., compared to the target \( -2746.7828 \) a.u.
- The Xe-Rn pair reproduces the target exchange energy density within a few percent over the range \( 0.01 \, \mathrm{a.u.} \le r_s \le 100 \, \mathrm{a.u.} \).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier proposal in Naito (2018) for improving energy density functionals by combining the inverse Kohn-Sham method (introduced by Wang and Parr in 1993 and by van Leeuwen and Baerends in 1994) with density functional perturbation theory (developed by Gonze and others in the late 1980s and 1990s). The benchmark demonstrates that the LDA exchange functional (originally formulated by Dirac in 1930) can be accurately reproduced from the Hartree functional using only two atomic systems. The method is promising for application to nuclear density functional theory, where improving existing functionals is a major goal, and could be extended to heavier systems and more complex functionals.
