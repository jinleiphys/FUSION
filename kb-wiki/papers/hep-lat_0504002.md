# Flavor Twisted Boundary Conditions and the Nucleon Axial Current

> With twisted boundary conditions on the quark fields, we study nucleon matrix elements of the axial current utilizing twisted heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. One can explore the momentum transfer dependence of the axial form factors more easily than by using ordinary lattice quantized momenta alone. As examples, we derive expressions for the nucleon axial radius and pseudoscalar form factor.

# Flavor Twisted Boundary Conditions and the Nucleon Axial Current

## Key claim

Twisted boundary conditions on valence quark fields allow continuous variation of momentum transfer in nucleon axial matrix elements on a fixed lattice, but the method introduces modified finite-volume corrections that can be controlled using partially twisted heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory; the axial radius and pseudoscalar form factor are largely insensitive to these corrections at leading order.

## Method

The author develops partially twisted heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory for lattice QCD calculations where valence and ghost quarks satisfy twisted boundary conditions while sea quarks remain periodic. The effective theory is used to derive tree-level expressions for the nucleon axial radius and pseudoscalar form factor, showing how the momentum transfer induced by twisting angles allows access to these quantities even at zero lattice momentum. The calculation involves constructing the appropriate baryon and meson fields with twisted boundary conditions, then computing the axial current matrix elements in the partially quenched framework.

## Key numbers

- The axial radius is given by `<r_A^2> = 6 n_A / Lambda_chi^2 [1 + O(m_phi^2 / Lambda_chi^2, m_phi^2 / M_N^2)]`, where `n_A` is a low-energy constant and `Lambda_chi` is the chiral scale.
- The pseudoscalar form factor is `G_P(q^2) = (2 M_N)^2 ( g_A / (q^2 - m_pi^2) - 2 n_A / Lambda_chi^2 ) [1 + O(m_phi^2 / Lambda_chi^2, q^2 / Lambda_chi^2, m_phi^2 / M_N^2, q^2 / M_N^2)]`.
- At zero lattice momentum, the momentum transfer is `q = (theta^u - theta^d - 2 pi n) / L`, where `theta^u` and `theta^d` are the twist angles for u and d quarks, `n` is an integer vector, and `L` is the lattice size.
- The induced momentum transfer from twisting is `q = 2 pi n / L + delta theta / L`, where `delta theta` is the difference in twist angles of the flavors changed.

## Context

This work extends the use of twisted boundary conditions in lattice QCD, previously applied to meson systems by Bedaque (2004), de Divitiis (2004), and Sachrajda (2004), into the baryon sector using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. The approach builds on prior chiral perturbation theory calculations of nucleon axial form factors by Bernard (1994, 1998) and Borasoy (1998), and enables lattice determinations of the axial radius and pseudoscalar form factor without requiring multiple lattice volumes or momentum extrapolations. The results provide a clean test case for twisted quark techniques and allow confrontation with experimental data on these observables.
