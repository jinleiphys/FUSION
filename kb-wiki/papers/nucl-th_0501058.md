# Comparative variational studies of $0^+$ states in three-$α$ models

> Three variational approaches, the hyperspherical-harmonics, Gaussian-basis and Lagrange-mesh methods involving different coordinate systems, are compared in studies of $0^+$ bound-state energies in 3$α$ models. Calculations are performed with different versions of the shallow Ali-Bodmer potential (with and without Coulomb) and with the deep Buck-Friedrich-Wheatley potential. All three methods yield very accurate energies. Their advantages and drawbacks are evaluated. The implications of the disagreement between the obtained results and the experimental $^{12}$C energies in $3α$ models with Coulomb interaction are discussed.

# Comparative variational studies of $0^+$ states in three-$\alpha$ models

## Key claim
All three variational methods (hyperspherical-harmonics, Gaussian-basis, and Lagrange-mesh) yield very accurate and mutually consistent energies for $0^+$ bound states in 3$\alpha$ models, but regardless of the choice of local $\alpha$-$\alpha$ potential (shallow Ali-Bodmer or deep Buck-Friedrich-Wheatley), the model fails to reproduce the experimental $^{12}$C ground-state energy when the Coulomb interaction is included.

## Method
Three variational approaches were applied to the 3$\alpha$ bound-state problem using different coordinate systems: the Lagrange-mesh method (LMM) in perimetric coordinates, the hyperspherical-harmonics method (HHM) on a Lagrange mesh, and the variational Gaussian-basis method (VGM) in Jacobi coordinates. Calculations were performed with the shallow Ali-Bodmer potential (versions ABd$_0$ and ABd, with and without Coulomb) and with the deep Buck-Friedrich-Wheatley (BFW) potential, the latter requiring elimination of forbidden states via orthogonalising pseudopotentials with a projection parameter $\Lambda$.

## Key numbers
- ABd$_0$ potential without Coulomb: ground-state energy $-5.12205$ MeV (VGM with 372 Gaussians), $-5.1215$ MeV (VGM with 280 Gaussians); excited state $-1.3523$ MeV (372 Gaussians), $-1.341$ MeV (280 Gaussians). LMM accuracy better than displayed digits; VGM error about $2 \times 10^{-6}$ MeV; HHM accuracy $10^{-3}$ MeV for ground state.
- ABd$_0$ potential with Coulomb: ground-state energy $-0.61733886$ MeV (LMM, with AB Coulomb parameter), $-0.57238115$ MeV (point Coulomb). LMM accuracy better than $10^{-8}$ MeV; VGM accuracy $10^{-5}$ MeV; HHM accuracy $10^{-3}$ MeV.
- ABd potential without Coulomb: ground-state energy about $-6.42$ MeV (VGM and HHM agree within $10^{-3}$ MeV); with Coulomb: about $-1.6$ MeV.
- BFW potential with Coulomb: ground-state energy extrapolated to $-0.27$ MeV (HHM, $\Lambda = 10^9$ MeV); VGM with 280 Gaussians gives close agreement within 1-2% for each $\Lambda$ value.
- BFW potential two-body forbidden-state energies: $E(0_1^+)=-72.625691755$ MeV, $E(0_2^+)=-25.618638588$ MeV, $E(2^+)=-22.000501732$ MeV.
- Experimental $^{12}$C ground-state energy: $-7.275$ MeV (Audi and Wapstra 1993).

## Context
This work extends earlier 3$\alpha$ calculations by Visschers and Van Wageningen (1971) and by Tursunov (2001), resolving the apparent lack of convergence in the latter by demonstrating that accurate forbidden-state wave functions are essential. The results contradict the possibility of describing $^{12}$C with local $\alpha$-$\alpha$ potentials, confirming that microscopic 3$\alpha$-cluster models (Kamimura 1981, Descouvemont and Baye 1987) are necessary for realistic description. The study enables future work on non-local interactions, as explored by Fujiwara, Suzuki, and Miyagawa (2002), and provides benchmark energies for testing other three-body methods.
