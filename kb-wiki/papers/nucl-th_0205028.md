# R_{out}/R_{sid} and Opacity at RHIC

> One of the most dramatic results from the first RHIC run are the STAR results for pi+- pi+- interferometry. They showed that the ratio of the so-called R_{out} and R_{sid} radii seem to decrease below unity for increasing transverse momentum of the pair (K_T). This was subsequently confirmed by PHENIX, which also extended the K_T range of the measurements. We consider here the effects of opacity of the nuclei on this ratio, and find that such a small value is consistent with surface emission from an opaque source.

## Key claim

The observed decrease of the HBT radius ratio \(R_{out}/R_{sid}\) below unity at RHIC is consistent with surface emission from an opaque source, in contrast to the transparent volume emission models that predict \(R_{out}/R_{sid} > 1\). The authors find that opacity naturally produces a ratio that can be less than one, but they acknowledge that their model does not reproduce the correct absolute HBT radii or their transverse momentum dependence.

## Method

The authors implement a covariant current ensemble formalism to compute two-particle HBT correlation functions, using a model where particles are emitted from the surface of a cylinder with \(1+1\) dimensional longitudinal Bjorken expansion. The system evolves through a Quark-Gluon Plasma phase, a mixed phase at critical temperature \(T_c = 175\) MeV, and a pion gas phase until decoupling at \(T_f = 150\) MeV, with surface emission treated as blackbody radiation modulated by an opacity parameter \(\kappa\). The emitted flux fractions from the surface and from the final volumetric decoupling are computed by integrating entropy and energy conservation equations over proper time.

## Key numbers

- For \(\kappa = 1\): \(\tau_0 = 0.160\) fm/c, \(\tau_c = 1.54\) fm/c, \(\tau_h = 5.73\) fm/c, \(\tau_f = 6.97\) fm/c; surface emission fraction \({\cal S}/{\cal N} = 0.844\), volumetric emission fraction \({\cal V}/{\cal N} = 0.156\).
- For \(\kappa = 0.5\): \(\tau_0 = 0.160\) fm/c, \(\tau_c = 1.75\) fm/c, \(\tau_h = 8.37\) fm/c, \(\tau_f = 10.5\) fm/c; surface emission fraction \({\cal S}/{\cal N} = 0.758\), volumetric emission fraction \({\cal V}/{\cal N} = 0.242\).
- Initial temperature \(T_0 \sim 411\) MeV obtained from multiplicity constraint with \(\Gamma = 3.6\) and \(R_T\) from nuclear radius.
- In the opaque cylinder model without longitudinal expansion, effective radii from Gaussian fits give \(R^{eff}_{sid} \approx 0.53 R_T\) and \(R^{eff}_{out} = 0.22 R_T\); in the transparent case \(R^{eff}_{out} \approx 0.61 R_T\).
- The ratio \(R_{out}/R_{sid} \sim \Delta t (K_T/E_K)/R_T\) in the opaque case, which can be less than one; in the transparent case \(R_{out}/R_{sid} \sim \sqrt{\Delta t^2 (K_T/E_K)^2 + R_T^2}/R_T\), always larger than one.

## Context

This work extends the surface emission model of Heiselberg and Vischer (1999) and the later work of Heinz and Tomasik (2001) to RHIC energies, providing an explanation for the STAR and PHENIX results showing \(R_{out}/R_{sid} < 1\) that contradicted earlier expectations from volume emission models. The approach contrasts with hydrodynamic computations by Heinz and Kolb (2002) by treating surface emission explicitly rather than volumetric decoupling. The model enables future work to incorporate time-dependent system radii and transverse flow to improve the description of absolute HBT radii and their \(K_T\) dependence.
