# Contribution to muon g-2 from the \pi0γand ηγintermediate states in the vacuum polarization

> Using new experimental data, we have calculated the contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon from the \pi0γand ηγintermediate states in the vacuum polarization with high precision: aμ(\pi0γ)+aμ(ηγ)=(54.7\pm 1.5)\times 10^{-11}. We have also found the small contribution from e+e-\pi0, e+e-ηand μ+μ-\pi0 intermediate states equal to 0.5\times 10^{-11}.

# Contribution to muon g-2 from the π⁰γ and ηγ intermediate states in the vacuum polarization

## Key claim
Using new experimental data from SND and CMD-2, the authors calculate the combined contribution of π⁰γ and ηγ intermediate states to the muon anomalous magnetic moment as (54.7 ± 1.5) × 10⁻¹¹, which is 27% larger and 2.5 times more precise than the prior calculation by Ynduráin (2001). The result accounts for 1.37 of the projected error of the E821 experiment at Brookhaven National Laboratory.

## Method
The authors evaluate the dispersion integral for the muon anomalous magnetic moment using experimental cross-section data for e⁺e⁻ → π⁰γ from SND (600 MeV to 1039 MeV) and for e⁺e⁻ → ηγ from CMD-2 (720 MeV to 1040 MeV). For energies below these ranges, they use theoretical formulas: a vector-meson-dominance model for π⁰γ (normalized to the π⁰ → γγ decay width) and a ρ-resonance model for ηγ. The contributions from e⁺e⁻π⁰, e⁺e⁻η, and μ⁺μ⁻π⁰ intermediate states are computed using a relation involving the γ* → e⁺e⁻ decay width and the π⁰γ cross-section.

## Key numbers
- aμ(π⁰γ) = (46.2 ± 0.6 ± 1.3) × 10⁻¹¹ for √s from 600 MeV to 1039 MeV, from SND data
- aμ(π⁰γ) = 1.3 × 10⁻¹¹ for √s below 600 MeV, from theoretical formula
- aμ(ηγ) = (7.1 ± 0.2 ± 0.3) × 10⁻¹¹ for √s from 720 MeV to 1040 MeV, from CMD-2 data
- aμ(ηγ) = 0.1 × 10⁻¹¹ for √s below 720 MeV, from ρ-resonance model
- Combined aμ(π⁰γ) + aμ(ηγ) = (54.7 ± 0.6 ± 1.4) × 10⁻¹¹, with statistical and systematic errors added in quadrature
- aμ(e⁺e⁻π⁰) + aμ(μ⁺μ⁻π⁰) + aμ(e⁺e⁻η) = 0.5 × 10⁻¹¹
- Total aμ(hadrons + γ) = (97.3 ± 2.1) × 10⁻¹¹, including contributions from π⁺π⁻γ and other hadron+γ states from Ynduráin (2001) and Jegerlehner (2001)

## Context
This work extends the calculation of hadronic light-by-light and vacuum polarization contributions to the muon g-2, specifically improving upon the earlier calculation by Ynduráin (2001) which gave a lower value with larger uncertainty. The improved precision is enabled by new experimental data from the SND and CMD-2 detectors at Novosibirsk. The result is directly relevant for interpreting the Brookhaven E821 experiment, as the combined contribution accounts for 65% of the reached experimental accuracy of 150 × 10⁻¹¹. The authors note that the errors in their result are negligible for any foreseeable future (g-2)μ measurement.
