# A dynamical collective calculation of supernova neutrino signals

> We present the first calculations with three flavors of collective and shock wave effects for neutrino propagation in core-collapse supernovae using hydroynamical density profiles and the S matrix formalism. We explore the interplay between the neutrino-neutrino interaction and the effects of multiple resonances upon the time signal of positrons in supernova observatories. A specific signature is found for the inverted hierarchy and a large third neutrino mixing angle and we predict, in this case, a dearth of lower energy positrons in Cherenkov detectors midway through the neutrino signal and the simultaneous revelation of valuable information about the original fluxes. We show that this feature is also observable with current generation neutrino detectors at the level of several sigmas.

# A dynamical collective calculation of supernova neutrino signals

## Key claim

For the inverted hierarchy and a large third neutrino mixing angle (sin²θ₁₃ ≥ 10⁻⁴), the positron event rate in Cherenkov detectors shows a characteristic dip at lower energies and a bump at higher energies midway through the supernova neutrino signal, observable at the level of several sigmas in current-generation detectors like Super-Kamiokande.

## Method

The authors performed three-flavor neutrino evolution calculations in two steps: first evolving neutrino wavefunctions through the inner supernova region using hydrodynamical density profiles with neutrino-neutrino interactions (single-angle approximation) and Vμτ loop corrections, then solving the evolution operator through the outer mantle using S matrices rather than probabilities. The density profiles included both a forward and reverse shock from hydrodynamical simulations, matched to the inner profiles, and the final flux on Earth was calculated accounting for decoherence but not Earth matter effects.

## Key numbers

- Solar mass-squared difference Δm²₁₂ = 8 × 10⁻⁵ eV² and atmospheric |Δm²₂₃| = 3 × 10⁻³ eV² used as best-fit oscillation parameters
- Two values of θ₁₃ considered: sin²θ₁₃ = 10⁻⁴ (large, representing the adiabatic regime) and sin²θ₁₃ = 10⁻⁸ (small, non-adiabatic regime)
- Neutrino luminosities decrease as Lν = Lν₀ × exp(-t/τ) with Lν₀ = 10⁵² erg·s⁻¹ and τ = 3.5 s
- Average neutrino energies: ⟨Eνₑ⟩ = 12 MeV, ⟨Eν̅ₑ⟩ = 15 MeV, ⟨Eνₓ⟩ = 18 MeV
- The adiabatic and non-adiabatic spectra cross at Eν = 20 MeV
- For Super-Kamiokande (22.5 ktons), a bump above 25 MeV is distinguishable at 3.5 sigma and a dip between 10-19 MeV at 1 sigma, using 0.5 s time bins
- Supernova assumed at 10 kpc from Earth

## Context

This work extends prior calculations that separately treated neutrino-neutrino collective effects (Pantaleone 1992, Samuel 1993, Duan 2005) and shock-wave-induced multiple resonances (Schirato 2002, Tomas 2004, Fogli 2004, Kneller 2005, Dasgupta 2005) by combining both effects in a three-flavor framework with matched density profiles and S-matrix propagation. The predicted dip in low-energy positrons qualitatively agrees with the observed gap in Supernova 1987A data (Hirata 1987, Bionta 1987, Alekseev 1987), and the signature may be robust against turbulence based on the work of Fogli (2006). The results enable future galactic supernova observations to determine the neutrino mass hierarchy and constrain θ₁₃ beyond the reach of terrestrial experiments.
