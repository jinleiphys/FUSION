# Lattice QCD equation of state : improving the differential method

> We propose an improvement of the differential method for the computation of the equation of state of QCD from lattice simulations. In contrast to the earlier differential method our technique yields positive pressure for all temperatures including in the transition region. Employing it on temporal lattices of 8, 10 and 12 sites and by extrapolating to zero lattice spacing we obtained the pressure, energy density, entropy density, specific heat and speed of sound in quenched QCD for 0.9 < T/Tc < 3. A comparison of our results is made with those from the dimensional reduction approach and a conformal symmetric theory at high-temperature.

## Key claim

The authors propose a "t-favoured scheme" modification of the differential method for computing the QCD equation of state on the lattice, which yields positive pressure for all temperatures including the transition region, even when using one-loop order perturbative couplings. This contrasts with the earlier differential method (s-favoured scheme) that produced negative pressure near the critical temperature.

## Method

The method formulates QCD on an asymmetric lattice with different spatial (a_s) and temporal (a_tau) lattice spacings, setting the scale by the temporal spacing a = a_tau (t-favoured scheme) rather than the spatial spacing. Using the Wilson action for pure gauge SU(3) theory, the authors compute the pressure, energy density, entropy density, specific heat, and speed of sound from plaquette expectation values on temporal lattices of 8, 10, and 12 sites, then extrapolate to zero lattice spacing (continuum limit) using linear fits in a^2 proportional to 1/N_tau^2. The specific heat and speed of sound are obtained through the conformal measure formalism involving variances and covariances of plaquettes.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range studied: 0.9 ≤ T/T_c ≤ 3.0
- Temporal lattice sizes used: N_tau = 8, 10, and 12
- At β = 6.55, one-loop order perturbative and non-perturbative Karsch coefficients c_i' differ by approximately 20%
- At β = 6.0, the difference between perturbative and non-perturbative c_i' increases to approximately 80%
- Maximum integrated autocorrelation time for plaquettes: about 12 sweeps (T=0 run at β=6.0); minimum: 3 sweeps (T=3T_c run for N_tau=12)
- The interaction measure Δ/T^4 is identical for both the integral method and the t-favoured scheme

## Context

This work extends the differential method originally devised by Engels et al. (two decades prior) for computing the QCD equation of state on the lattice, which suffered from negative pressure near T_c. The integral method introduced by Engels et al. and Boyd et al. avoided perturbative couplings but required the assumption of homogeneity and could not easily compute fluctuation measures. The t-favoured scheme enables calculation of specific heat and speed of sound through operator methods, providing tests for models attempting to explain lattice data (such as quasi-particle models, resummation approaches, and effective models) and has direct relevance to hydrodynamic studies of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC.
