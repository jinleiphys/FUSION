# Model for hypernucleus production in heavy ion collisions

> We estimate the production cross sections of hypernuclei in projectile like fragment (PLF) in heavy ion collisions. The discussed scenario for the formation cross section of hypernucleus is: (a) Lambda particles are produced in the participant region but have a considerable rapidity spread and (b) Lambda with rapidity close to that of the PLF and total momentum (in the rest system of PLF) up to Fermi motion can then be trapped and produce hypernuclei. The process (a) is considered here within Heavy Ion Jet Interacting Generator HIJING-BBbar model and the process (b) in the canonical thermodynamic model (CTM). We estimate the production cross-sections for light hypernuclei for C + C at 3.7 GeV total nucleon-nucleon center of mass energy and for Ne+Ne and Ar+Ar collisions at 5.0 GeV. By taking into account explicitly the impact parameter dependence of the colliding systems, it is found that the cross section is different from that predicted by the coalescence model and large discrepancy is obtained for 6_He and 9_Be hypernuclei.

# Model for hypernucleus production in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The authors estimate hypernucleus production cross sections in projectile like fragments (PLF) using a hybrid model combining HIJING/B-Bar v2.0 for Lambda production in the participant region with a canonical thermodynamic model (CTM) for Lambda attachment to fragments, finding that cross sections differ from coalescence model predictions, with large discrepancies for 6_He and 9_Be hypernuclei, particularly when impact parameter dependence is explicitly included.

## Method

The authors first generate events using the HIJING/B-Bar v2.0 model to obtain the average number of Lambda particles per event within a rapidity window of (y_proj - 0.05) to (y_proj + 0.05) and total momentum below 250 MeV/c in the PLF rest frame, for various impact parameters. They then apply the canonical thermodynamic model (CTM) with three particle types (neutron, proton, Lambda) to calculate the average number of hypernuclei produced at temperatures of 5 MeV and 8 MeV, using experimental ground state and excited state energies for hypernuclei with mass number a < 11 and a liquid-drop formula for heavier species. The inclusive cross section is obtained by integrating over impact parameter the product of the average Lambda number and the average hypernucleus occupation number.

## Key numbers

* For C + C collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 3.7 GeV, the total number of events with one Lambda particle produced in the projectile rapidity region is 13.05 x 10^(-4), compared to 26.6 x 10^(-4) from UrQMD calculations at lower energy (2A GeV in laboratory system) with a larger rapidity cut
* Increasing the rapidity cut delta y from 0.05 to 0.5 results in a factor of 6-8 increase in the average value of <n_Lambda(b)>
* For Ne + Ne central collisions (b = 3 fm) at sqrt(s_NN) = 5 GeV, using a mean string tension kappa = 3 GeV/fm instead of 2 GeV/fm results in a modest increase of less than 3-5% in <n_Lambda(b)>
* For C + C central collisions (b = 3 fm), increasing sqrt(s_NN) from 3.7 GeV to 5 GeV gives a 32% increase in <n_Lambda(b)>, while increasing from 5 GeV to 10 GeV gives a factor of 2.75 increase
* Inclusive cross sections for C + C at sqrt(s_NN) = 3.7 GeV range from 0.00008 microbarn (9_Li at T = 5 MeV) to 26.88 microbarn (7_Li at T = 5 MeV), with coalescence model predictions ranging from 0.05 microbarn (9_Li) to 2.58 microbarn (5_He)
* For 7_Li at T = 5 MeV, the CTM prediction is 26.88 microbarn versus 0.24 microbarn from coalescence; for 9_Be at T = 5 MeV, the CTM prediction is 22.3 microbarn versus 2.48 microbarn from coalescence

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of hypernucleus production in relativistic heavy ion collisions, which were first proposed by Kerman and Weiss (1973) and subsequently studied via coalescence models by Wakai (1988), Asai (1984), Sano (1994), and Sato (1981) at lower center-of-mass energies (sqrt(s_NN) = 2.7-3.1 GeV). The present calculations at higher energies (3.7-5.0 GeV) use different models for Lambda production and attachment, and are motivated by upcoming experimental programs at FAIR (GSI, Germany) and NICA (JINR, Dubna, Russia) which aim to measure hypernuclei in the energy range sqrt(s_NN) = 2.7-9.4 GeV. The results enable comparison between the coalescence model and the canonical thermodynamic approach, and suggest that relative populations of lighter hypernuclei could serve as a thermometer for the PLF temperature.
