# Excited-State Quantum Phase Transitions in Bosonic Lattice Systems

> Concentrating on bosonic lattice systems, we ask whether and how Excited State Quantum Phase Transition (ESQPT) singularities occur in condensed matter systems with ground state QPTs. We study in particular the spectral singularities above the ground-state phase diagram of the boson Hubbard model. As a general prerequisite, we point out the analogy between ESQPTs and van Hove singularities (vHss).

## Key claim

The paper argues that Excited-State Quantum Phase Transitions (ESQPTs) in bosonic lattice systems are analogous to van Hove singularities (vHss) in condensed matter, and that ESQPT singularities occur in the boson Hubbard model above its ground-state phase diagram, specifically in bands with low numbers of particles or holes in the infinite-lattice limit.

## Method

The author studies the boson Hubbard model on a one-dimensional lattice, analyzing the level density (density of states) of multi-particle bands as a function of excitation energy. The spectral singularities are identified by examining the stationary points of the energy dispersion relation for systems with varying numbers of bosons, and the analogy between the classical Hamiltonian integral for level density and the quasimomentum integral for density of states is used to classify singularity types.

## Key numbers

- For \(\nu = 1\) particle in the free-hopping limit (\(U = V = 0\), \(t = 1\), \(\mu = 0\)), inverse square-root singularities \(\overline{\mathrm{DoS}}(E) \approx 1/\sqrt{|E - E_s|}\) occur at both band-edges \(E_s = \pm 1\).
- For \(\nu = 2\) particles, a logarithmic singularity \(\overline{\mathrm{DoS}} \approx \log{|E - E_s|}\) occurs at mid-band \(E_s = 0\).
- For \(\nu = 3\) and 4 particles, singularities occur in the derivative \(\partial \overline{\mathrm{DoS}}/\partial E\), of inverse-square-root and logarithmic type respectively.
- In general, singularities occur in derivatives of order \(\lfloor(\nu-1)/2\rfloor\), and are of inverse-square-root type for odd \(\nu\) and logarithmic type for even \(\nu\).

## Context

This work extends the concept of ESQPTs, previously studied in zero-dimensional systems like atomic nuclei and molecules (Cejnar 2006, Caprio 2007), to the spatially extended boson Hubbard model, drawing a formal analogy to van Hove singularities originally identified in crystal vibrations (van Hove 1953) and electronic spectra. The results enable further study of ESQPT phases, the nature of singularities in higher-lying bands with overtone contributions, and more complex lattice types, as well as potential connections to non-equilibrium thermodynamics and transport.
