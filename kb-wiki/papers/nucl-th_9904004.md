# One-Nucleon Effective Generators of the Poincare Group derived from a Field Theory: Mass Renormalization

> We start from a Lagrangian describing scalar "nucleons" and mesons which interact through a simple vertex. Okubo's method of unitary transformation is used to describe a single nucleon dressed by its meson cloud. We find an expression for the physical mass of the nucleon being correct up to second order in the coupling constant. It is then verified that this result is the same as the corresponding expression found by Feynman techniques. Finally we also express the three boost operators in terms of the physical nucleon mass. Doing so we find expressions for all the ten generators of Poincaré transformations for the system of one single dressed nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that, for a single scalar "nucleon" dressed by its meson cloud, the effective generators of the Poincaré group (Hamiltonian and boost operators) can be expressed in terms of the physical nucleon mass rather than the bare mass, correct to second order in the coupling constant, and that this mass renormalization preserves the Poincaré algebra.

## Method

The authors start from a Lagrangian describing charged scalar nucleons and uncharged scalar mesons interacting via a simple vertex. They apply Okubo's method of unitary transformation to block-diagonalize the ten Poincaré generators, separating the one-nucleon subspace from the rest of Fock space. The operator A that achieves this block diagonalization is solved perturbatively to first order in the coupling constant g, and the effective Hamiltonian and boost operators in the one-nucleon subspace are computed to order g². The resulting mass shift is compared to the standard Feynman diagram calculation, and the momentum independence of the mass shift is verified.

## Key numbers

* The physical mass m is expanded as m = m₀ + m₂ + O(g³), where m₁ = 0 and m₂ is given by the integral: m₂ = -g²/[8m₀(2π)³] ∫ d³q {1/(E_q ω_{p+q}) [1/(ω_{p+q} + E_p + E_q) + 1/(ω_{p+q} - E_p + E_q)]}, with E_q = √(m₀² + q²) and ω_k = √(μ₀² + k²)
* The mass shift m₂ is shown to be independent of the external momentum p, despite the apparent p-dependence in the integrand
* The Feynman calculation yields m₂^F = -1/(2m₀) Σ(m₀²) + O(g⁴), which is proven equal to the Hamiltonian result after contour integration

## Context

This work extends the formal framework for N-nucleon effective Poincaré generators developed by the same authors in a previous study (Krüger and Glöckle, 1998) to the specific case of one nucleon. It applies Okubo's method of unitary transformation (Okubo, 1954) to achieve block diagonalization, following the approach outlined in Glöckle and Müller (1996). The paper verifies that the mass renormalization obtained in the Hamiltonian formalism matches the standard Feynman diagram result, and it establishes that the renormalized boost operators take the free-particle form with the physical mass. This work enables the next step of investigating two interacting nucleons, where renormalization effects at order g⁴ will appear and the preservation of the Poincaré algebra must be verified.
