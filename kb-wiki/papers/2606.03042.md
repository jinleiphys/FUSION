# Chiral Quark Soliton Model And Nucleon Parton Distribution Functions

> The chiral quark soliton model (CQSM) is an effective quark model of baryons maximally taking account of the most important feature of low-energy QCD, i.e., the spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking of the QCD vacuum and the associated appearance of Nambu--Goldstone pions. It shares many common features with the famous Skyrme model in that the baryons are viewed as rotating hedgehog objects in both models. Despite many similarities, it turned out that the CQSM can give more realistic predictions on most baryon observables. Above all, a decisive advantage of the CQSM over the Skyrme-like models is that it can handle non-local quark--quark correlations in baryons, which is absolutely impossible within the framework of effective meson theories. This feature is decisively important for making theoretical predictions on the quark distribution functions inside the nucleon, which are defined as nucleon matrix elements of bilinear quark operators with light-cone separation. In the present paper, we try to elucidate why and how the CQSM can give successful predictions for a variety of types of nucleon quark distribution functions, especially for the flavor asymmetry of the unpolarized and longitudinally polarized sea-quark (anti-quark) distribution functions in the nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the chiral quark soliton model (CQSM) can give successful predictions for a variety of nucleon quark distribution functions, particularly the flavor asymmetry of unpolarized and longitudinally polarized sea-quark distributions, because it can handle non-local quark–quark correlations in baryons, which is impossible within effective meson theories like the Skyrme model. The hedge is that these predictions are given at a low-energy model scale thought to be around 600 MeV and then evolved to high energy scales where experimental data exist.

## Method

The CQSM is formulated as a relativistic mean-field theory where quarks move in a hedgehog-shaped pion mean field with nontrivial topology, incorporating vacuum polarization of negative-energy Dirac-sea quark orbitals to account for pionic quark–antiquark excitation modes. The nucleon is constructed by placing N_c = 3 quarks into the valence orbital and all negative-energy Dirac-sea orbitals, with the pion field determined self-consistently via a Hartree problem solved numerically using the Kahana–Ripka–Soni method. Parton distribution functions are computed as nucleon matrix elements of bilinear quark operators with light-cone separation, evaluated in the nucleon rest frame using Lorentz-boost invariance, with the collective rotational motion of the hedgehog treated via the cranking method.

## Key numbers

- The model scale is thought to be around 600 MeV.
- The CQSM predicts the isovector axial-vector coupling constant g_A^(3) approximately as 0.8 (zeroth order in Omega) + 0.4 (first order in Omega) = 1.2, compared to the empirical value of 1.27.
- The Skyrme model prediction for g_A^(3) is around 0.6 to 0.8.
- The CQSM predicts the quark spin fraction Delta Sigma approximately 0.35 at the model scale of around 600 MeV.
- The isoscalar unpolarized distribution u(x) + d(x) from valence quarks has a peak around x approximately 0.25.

## Context

This work extends the CQSM framework originally proposed by Diakonov, Petrov, and Pobilitsa (1988) based on the instanton-liquid picture of the QCD vacuum, and builds on earlier reviews of the model. It contrasts with the Skyrme model (Witten, 1983; Adkins, Nappi, and Witten, 1983) by showing that the CQSM resolves the longstanding underestimation of the isovector axial-vector coupling constant g_A and can handle non-local quark correlations needed for parton distribution functions. The results enable further studies of generalized form factors, Ji's angular momentum sum rule (Ji, 1997), and semi-empirical analyses of nucleon spin contents with attention to scale dependencies.
