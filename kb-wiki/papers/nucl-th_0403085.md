# QCD motivated three-body force and light nuclei

> The need for the three-body forces (3BF) for reproduction of physical properties of light nuclei has been evident for some time. These 3BF are doing quite well for light nuclei, but there seems to be scope for other 3BF which may arise from other physical considerations not included so far. Here we discuss a QCD motivated new 3BF which should be included in studies of nuclei, in particular the light one3s. We perform variational Monte Carlo calculations including this new 3BF and discuss improvements brought in thereby for light nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a new three-body force (3BF) derived from QCD and quark-model considerations, implemented as an infinitely repulsive hard core with two variational parameters, produces small but non-negligible improvements in the variational ground-state energies of light nuclei (A=3,4) beyond what the standard Urbana-IX 3BF already provides.

## Method

The authors performed variational Monte Carlo calculations for ³H, ³He, and ⁴He using the Argonne v18 two-nucleon potential and the Urbana-IX three-nucleon potential, then added a new QCD-motivated three-body force V^Q_ijk. This force was implemented as an infinite repulsion when all three inter-nucleon distances are ≤ λ₁ and the total perimeter R_ijk ≤ λ₂, with λ₁ and λ₂ treated as variational parameters tuned to reproduce experimental ground-state energies and radii.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴He, the variational parameters are λ₁ = 0.55 fm and λ₂ = 1.2 fm; for ³He, λ₁ = 0.63 fm and λ₂ = 1.5 fm; for ³H, λ₁ = 0.62 fm and λ₂ = 1.5 fm (Table 1).
- The calculated point-proton radius for ⁴He is 1.466 fm (experimental: 1.47 fm); for ³He it is 1.766 fm (experimental: 1.77 fm); for ³H it is 1.613 fm (experimental: 1.60 fm) (Table 2).
- With V^Q_ijk included, the total ground-state energy for ⁴He is -27.77(1) MeV (experimental: -28.30 MeV); for ³He it is -7.54(1) MeV (experimental: -7.72 MeV); for ³H it is -8.32(1) MeV (experimental: -8.48 MeV) (Table 3).
- Including V^Q_ijk improves the variational ground-state energy by about -0.04 MeV for ⁴He, -0.03 MeV for ³He, and -0.01 MeV for ³H compared to calculations without it (Tables 3 and 4).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Gibson (year not given) and Pudliner et al. (1997) that established the need for three-body forces in light nuclei, and by Wiringa et al. (1991) on variational Monte Carlo methods. The new force is motivated by earlier quark-model work by Abbas (1986, 1988) on hidden-color components in multiquark systems, where 9-quark and 12-quark configurations have 97.6% and 99.8% hidden color respectively. The results suggest that QCD effects manifest through this three-body force in light nuclei, and the authors recommend its inclusion in calculations for dense and compact nuclear systems, with future extension to a four-body force for ⁴He.
