# The Oklo Constraints on Alpha-Decay Half-Lives

> The Oklo data constrain the depth of the nuclear potential well to a small margin of error, determined by various unknowns, such as the reactor temperature. However, we show that even these small variations could allow the U-238 half-life to vary by more than one order of magnitude.

# The Oklo Constraints on Alpha-Decay Half-Lives

## Key claim
Although the Oklo natural reactor data provide stringent limits on variations in the Sm-149 neutron absorption cross-section, these constraints do not translate into equally stringent limits on nuclear alpha-decay half-lives; even small variations in the nuclear potential well depth allowed by the Oklo data could allow the U-238 half-life to vary by more than one order of magnitude.

## Method
The authors used elemental concentration data from Oklo reactor samples to infer the Sm-149 absorption cross-section for neutrons, following procedures similar to Damour and Dyson (1996) and Fujii et al. (2000). They solved coupled linear differential equations for isotope concentrations to obtain the Sm-149 cross-section, then related this cross-section to the depth of a square-well nuclear potential via the Breit-Wigner resonance shape. Using the Pierronne and Marquez complex eigenvalue method implemented in Mathematica notebooks, they calculated the U-238 alpha-decay constant as a function of well depth, examining how small changes in well depth affect the number of nodes in the alpha particle wavefunction.

## Key numbers
- The integrated Sm-149 absorption cross-section from Oklo data is 90.3 ± 7.4 kilobarns (one standard deviation statistical error).
- The samarium resonant energy is 0.0958325 eV with a corresponding one standard deviation spread of ±0.0102 eV.
- The U-238 half-life of 4.47 × 10^9 years is reproduced by a square-well potential depth V0 of 110.5 MeV.
- A change in the number of nodes of the alpha particle wavefunction causes the decay constant to change by a factor of 13.5 (more than one order of magnitude).

## Context
This work extends the approach of Shlyakhter (1976) and Damour and Dyson (1996), who used Oklo data to constrain time variation of fundamental constants, but shows that the constraints on alpha-decay half-lives are much weaker than previously assumed. The authors build on the complex eigenvalue method of Pierronne and Marquez for calculating alpha-decay half-lives and on the Mathematica implementations of Chaffin, Banks, Gothard, and Tuttle. The results suggest that future searches for time variation of the strong coupling constant must account for the possibility of discontinuous changes in alpha-decay rates due to node-count changes in the alpha particle wavefunction.
