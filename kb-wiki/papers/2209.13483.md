# Rotating Solar Models in Agreement with Helioseismic Results and Updated Neutrino Fluxes

> Standard solar models (SSMs) constructed in accordance with old solar abundances are in reasonable agreement with seismically inferred results, but SSMs with new low-metal abundances disagree with the seismically inferred results. The constraints of neutrino fluxes on solar models exist in parallel with those of helioseismic results. The solar neutrino fluxes were updated by Borexino Collaboration. We constructed rotating solar models with new low-metal abundances where the effects of enhanced diffusion and convection overshoot were included. A rotating model using OPAL opacities and the Caffau abundance scale has better sound-speed and density profiles than the SSM with the old solar abundances and reproduces the observed $p$-mode frequency ratios $r_{02}$ and $r_{13}$. The depth and helium abundance of the convection zone of the model agree with the seismically inferred ones at the level of $1¤â$. The updated neutrino fluxes are also reproduced by the model at the level of $1¤â$. The effects of rotation and enhanced diffusion not only improve the model's sound-speed and density profiles but bring the neutrino fluxes predicted by the model into agreement with the detected ones. Moreover, the calculations show that OP may underestimate opacities for the regions of the Sun with $T\gtrsim5\times10^{6}$ K by around $1.5\%$, while OPAL may underestimate opacities for the regions of the Sun with $2\times10^{6}$ K $\lesssim T \lesssim 5\times10^{6}$ K by about $1-2\%$.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing solar modeling problem, where standard solar models with low-metal abundances (e.g., Asplund 2009, AGSS09) disagree with helioseismic results and neutrino flux constraints (Bahcall 2004, Basu 2004, Serenelli 2009). Previous attempts to reconcile low-Z models with helioseismology included increased opacity (Bahcall 2004, Serenelli 2009), enhanced neon abundance (Bahcall 2005), mass accretion (Castro 2007, Guzik 2010, Zhang 2019), and convection overshoot (Montesinos 2006, Yang 2019). The present work shows that combining rotation, enhanced diffusion, and modest opacity adjustments can produce a model (Copal11ri) that simultaneously satisfies both helioseismic constraints and the updated Borexino neutrino fluxes (Borexino 2018, 2020), suggesting that the solar abundance problem may be resolved by a combination of multiple small physical effects rather than a single large correction.
