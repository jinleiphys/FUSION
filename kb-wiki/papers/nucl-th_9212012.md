# High-energy pion-nucleus elastic scattering

> We investigate theoretical approaches to pion--nucleus elastic scattering at high energies (300 $\le T_π\le$ 1 GeV). A ``model--exact'' calculation of the lowest--order microscopic optical model, carried out in momentum space and including the full Fermi averaging integration, a realistic off--shell pion--nucleon scattering amplitude and fully covariant kinematics, is used to calibrate a much simpler theory. The simpler theory utilizes a local optical potential with an eikonal propagator and includes the Coulomb interaction and the first Wallace correction, both of which are found to be important. Comparisons of differential cross sections out to beyond the second minimum are made for light and heavy nuclei. Particularly for nuclei as heavy as $^{40}$Ca, the eikonal theory is found to be an excellent approximation to the full theory. (8 Figures available from authors)

## Key claim

The paper calibrates a simple eikonal model (local optical potential with eikonal propagator) against a "model-exact" momentum-space calculation of the lowest-order microscopic optical potential for pion-nucleus elastic scattering at high energies (300 MeV to 1 GeV), finding that the eikonal theory is an excellent approximation for heavy nuclei like Ca-40, but the agreement is less quantitative for light nuclei like C-12.

## Method

The authors performed a "model-exact" calculation of the lowest-order microscopic optical potential in momentum space, including full Fermi averaging integration, realistic off-shell pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes, and fully covariant kinematics. They then constructed a simpler eikonal theory using a local optical potential with an eikonal propagator, including the Coulomb interaction and the first Wallace correction. The differential cross sections from both approaches were compared for pi+ and pi- scattering from C-12 and Ca-40 at various energies between 400 and 800 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Energy range studied: 300 MeV to 1 GeV (pion kinetic energy T_pi)
- At 1 GeV, the pion wavelength is 1 fm (about the size of a single nucleon)
- The two-body total cross section above the Delta_33 resonance is less than 30 mb, about 15% of that on resonance
- Ratio of second-order to first-order optical potential: R = 0.04 at 500 MeV and R = 0.02 at 1 GeV
- For Ca-40 at 800 MeV/c, both the location of the minima and the magnitude of the cross section at forward angles show good agreement between eikonal and model-exact calculations
- For C-12, the minima are slightly shifted between the two approaches
- The r.m.s. radius for C-12 from Hartree-Fock calculation is 2.365 fm, while the measured value is 2.297 fm (requiring a 3% scaling factor of 0.9715)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pion-nucleus scattering above the Delta_33 resonance region, which had received much less attention than scattering on and below resonance (Johnson and Ernst, 1992). The authors note that a previous similar investigation by Arima, Masutani, and Seki (1991) used a factorized approximation in momentum-space calculations and did not consider the full nonlocality of the optical potential, and also used Glauber multiple scattering theory rather than the simpler eikonal model examined here. The paper enables simpler theoretical calculations for high-energy pion-nucleus scattering that can be used to study nuclear structure and to search for unconventional phenomena such as mesonic current contributions or modified nucleon properties in the nuclear medium.
