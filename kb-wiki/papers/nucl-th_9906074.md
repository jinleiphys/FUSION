# Fluctuations of particle ratios and the abundance of hadronic resonances

> In this letter we will argue that the event-by-event fluctuations of the ratio of positively over negatively charged pions provides a measurements of the number of rho and omega mesons right after hadronization. This finding can be utilized to put the hypothesis of chemical equilibration in relativistic heavy ion collisions to a test.

## Key claim

The event-by-event fluctuation of the π⁺/π⁻ ratio is sensitive to the number of primordial ρ⁰ and ω mesons present right after hadronization/chemical freeze-out, not at thermal freeze-out, because the pion number does not change during the hadronic phase (relaxation times ~100 fm/c versus system lifetime ~10 fm/c). This provides a test of the chemical equilibration hypothesis, but the authors hedge that charge exchange processes and acceptance cuts could introduce small corrections.

## Method

The authors derive a general formalism for event-by-event fluctuations of particle ratios, accounting for volume fluctuations (which cancel in ratios), thermal fluctuations, and correlations from resonance decays. They apply this to the π⁺/π⁻ ratio using a hadron resonance gas model with mesons and baryons up to ~1.5 GeV, with temperature T=170 MeV, baryon chemical potential μ_b=270 MeV, and strangeness chemical potential μ_s=74 MeV, using particle yields from the particle data book and NA49 data for total pion multiplicity.

## Key numbers

- For a pion gas at T=170 MeV, the quantum-statistical correction gives w_π = 1.13
- For a typical resonance of mass ~1 GeV at T=170 MeV, (w_R^T - 1) < 10⁻²
- The correlation term 2D_+- is 70% of the individual contributions D_++ or D_--
- The individual pion fluctuations are close to the statistical limit: D_++ ≃ D_-- ≃ 1.09
- The total fluctuation of the π⁺/π⁻ ratio is σ_+-² = 0.0128, giving σ_+- = 0.113
- The ratio of total fluctuations to purely statistical fluctuations is σ_+-² / (σ_+-^stat)² = 0.70 (a 30% reduction)
- For the K/π ratio, σ_K/π² / (σ_K/π^stat)² = 1.04, with σ_K/π = 0.17
- The total pion density at freeze-out is n_+^tot = n_-^tot = 0.20 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the statistical model approach of Braun-Munzinger et al. (1999) and Becattini et al. (1999), who inferred chemical equilibrium from single-particle yields, by proposing a new observable sensitive to resonance abundances at chemical freeze-out. It enables experimental tests of the chemical equilibration hypothesis using event-by-event fluctuation measurements from NA49 and other heavy-ion experiments, and suggests that values of σ_+-/σ_+-^stat significantly larger than 1 would indicate new physics such as Quark-Gluon Plasma bubble formation.
