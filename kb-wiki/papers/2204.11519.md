# Quantum kinetic theory for dynamical spin polarization from QED-type interaction

> We investigate the dynamical spin polarization of a massless electron probing an electron plasma in locally thermal equilibrium via the Moller scattering from the quantum kinetic theory. We derive an axial kinetic equation delineating the dynamical spin evolution in the presence of the collision term with quantum corrections up to $\mathcal{O}(\hbar)$ and the leading-logarithmic order in coupling by using the hard-thermal-loop (HTL) approximation, from which we extract the spin-polarization rate induced by the spacetime gradients of the medium. When the electron probe approaches local equilibrium, we further simplify the collision term into a relaxation-time expression. Our kinetic equation may be implemented in the future numerical simulations for dynamical spin polarization.

## Key claim
The authors derive a quantum kinetic equation for dynamical spin polarization of a massless electron probe scattering off an electron plasma in local thermal equilibrium via Moller scattering, obtaining the spin-polarization rate induced by spacetime gradients of the medium (thermal vorticity, shear strength, and gradient of chemical potential over temperature) up to order ℏ and leading-logarithmic order in coupling, with the caveat that the result applies only when the probe approaches local equilibrium and the collision term can be simplified into a relaxation-time expression.

## Method
The authors start from the QED Lagrangian for massless fermions and use the Wigner-function approach with the Keldysh formalism to derive Kadanoff-Baym equations up to order ℏ. They compute the collision kernels for 2-2 scattering (t-channel only) using the hard-thermal-loop (HTL) approximation and leading-logarithmic order in the electric coupling e, assuming the medium electrons are in local equilibrium while the probe electron may be out of equilibrium. The vector and axial self-energies are derived from photonic self-energies, and the axial kinetic equation is extracted from the collision term with quantum corrections.

## Key numbers
* Spin-polarization rate is derived from collision terms up to order ℏ (the leading quantum correction) and leading-logarithmic order in coupling e, using the HTL approximation.
* The axial distribution function in local equilibrium is given by N_{A,leq}^{<}(x,p) = -(ℏ/2) N_{V,leq}^{<}(x,p) N_{V,leq}^{>}(x,p) Ω_{μν} S_{(u)}^{μν}, where Ω_{μν} is the thermal vorticity tensor.
* The axial-vector Wigner function in local equilibrium contains terms proportional to ∂_ν(βμ_V), the thermal shear tensor ξ_{αν}, and the dual thermal vorticity tensor Ω̃^{ρξ}, each multiplied by ℏ.

## Context
This work extends the quantum kinetic theory approach for spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions, addressing the "sign problem" of local spin polarization observed in STAR experiments (STAR, 2019) where global equilibrium models fail to describe longitudinal spin polarization. It builds on prior work by Yang (2020) for collision kernels in weakly coupled QCD and by Hidaka (2017) for chiral kinetic theory, and provides a microscopic foundation for spin hydrodynamics approaches (e.g., Hattori, 2019; Fukushima, 2020). The derived kinetic equation may be implemented in future numerical simulations for dynamical spin polarization in quark-gluon plasmas.
