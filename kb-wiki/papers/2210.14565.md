# (Anti)kaon condensation in strongly magnetized dense matter

> Recent observations of several massive pulsars, with masses near and above $2~M_\odot$, point towards the existence of matter at very high densities, compared to normal matter that we are familiar with in our terrestrial world. This leads to the possibility of appearance of exotic degrees of freedom other than nucleons inside the core of the neutrons stars (NS). Another significant property of NSs is the presence of high surface magnetic field, with highest range of the order of $\sim~10^{16}$ G. We study the properties of highly dense matter with the possibility of appearance of heavier strange and non-strange baryons, and kaons in presence of strong magnetic field. We find that the presence of a strong magnetic field stiffens the matter at high density, delaying the kaon appearance and, hence, increasing the maximum attainable mass of NS family.

## Key claim

The presence of a strong magnetic field stiffens the equation of state at high density, which delays the onset of (anti)kaon condensation and increases the maximum attainable mass of neutron stars, provided the magnetic field strength does not exceed approximately 10^17 G (beyond which spherical symmetry assumptions break down).

## Method

The authors used a density-dependent relativistic mean field (DD-RMF) model to describe dense matter containing nucleons, hyperons, Delta-resonances, and (anti)kaon condensates under a strong magnetic field, with Landau quantization for charged particles. They employed three parametrizations (DD-ME2, DD-2, and DD-MEX) and constrained the model by requiring consistency with observed mass-radius relations from pulsars and tidal deformability limits from the GW170817 event. The magnetic field was modeled using a universal profile derived from Einstein-Maxwell field equations, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were used to compute neutron star structure for fields up to 10^17 G.

## Key numbers

- Maximum masses for matter containing nucleons, hyperons, Delta-resonances, and (anti)kaon condensates: 2.192 M_sun (DD-MEX) and 2.120 M_sun (DD-ME2) with standard symmetry energy parameters
- With increased symmetry energy (L_sym = 70 MeV, E_sym = 38.1 MeV), maximum masses reduce to 2.156 M_sun (DD-ME2) and 2.088 M_sun (DD-MEX)
- Nuclear saturation density n_0 = 0.152 fm^-3 for DD-ME2 and DD-MEX, 0.149065 fm^-3 for DD-2
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: -16.14 MeV (DD-ME2), -16.02 MeV (DD-2), -16.097 MeV (DD-MEX)
- Compression modulus K_0: 250.89 MeV (DD-ME2), 242.70 MeV (DD-2), 267.059 MeV (DD-MEX)
- Symmetry energy E_sym: 32.30 MeV (DD-ME2), 32.73 MeV (DD-2), 32.269 MeV (DD-MEX)
- Slope parameter L_sym: 51.253 MeV (DD-ME2), 54.966 MeV (DD-2), 49.576 MeV (DD-MEX)
- (Anti)kaon optical potential chosen as U_Kbar = -130 MeV
- Magnetic field profile used up to 10^17 G; spherical TOV equations not used above 10^17 G
- Surface magnetic field of magnetars: 10^14 - 10^16 G

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron star matter containing hyperons and Delta-resonances without (anti)kaon condensates (Li 2018, Li 2019) and with (anti)kaon condensates (PhysRevD.103.063004) to include strong magnetic fields. It also extends studies of magnetized matter with (anti)kaon condensates but without hyperons or Delta-resonances (PhysRevC.77.045804, 2022IJMPE..3150050K). The novel contribution is the simultaneous inclusion of hyperons, Delta-resonances, and (anti)kaon condensates under strong magnetic fields, using density-dependent couplings that satisfy observational constraints from massive pulsars and gravitational wave events. The work enables future studies of magnetar structure with complete exotic degrees of freedom and provides constraints on the symmetry energy parameters from combined nuclear experiment and astrophysical data.
