# Scalar kappa meson in K* photoproduction

> We propose that the scalar $κ(800)$ meson may play an important role in $K^*$ photoproduction. In the reactions of $γp \to K^{*+} Λ$ and $γp \to K^{*0} Σ^+$, we consider the production mechanisms including $t$-channel $K^*$, $K$, $κ$ exchanges, s-channel $N$, $Δ$ diagrams, and $u$-channel $Λ$, $Σ$, $Σ^*$ diagrams within the tree level approximation, and find that the $κ$-meson exchange may contribute significantly to $K^*Σ$ photoproduction, while it is rather supplementary in $K^*Λ$ photoproduction. We demonstrate how the observables of $K^*$ photoproduction can be used to constrain the $κ$ meson properties. In particular, the parity asymmetry can separate the $κ$ meson contribution in $K^*$ photoproduction.

## Key claim

The scalar κ(800) meson exchange may contribute significantly to γp → K*0 Σ+ photoproduction, while being supplementary in γp → K*+ Λ production, thereby explaining the similar cross sections observed for these two reactions at forward angles.

## Method

The authors computed tree-level amplitudes for γp → K*+ Λ and γp → K*0 Σ+ including t-channel K*, K, and κ exchanges, s-channel N and Δ diagrams, and u-channel Λ, Σ, and Σ* diagrams. They varied the κ meson mass and coupling constants within ranges from the literature, using monopole or Gaussian form factors at the vertices, and compared the resulting differential and total cross sections to preliminary CLAS data.

## Key numbers

- The ratio R_K = (g^c_{K*Kγ} g_{KNΛ} / √2 g^0_{K*Kγ} g_{KNΣ})² ≈ 1.7², predicting K*+Λ cross section ~3 times larger than K*0Σ+ if only K exchange dominates.
- In model (I): M_κ = 900 MeV, Γ_κ = 550 MeV, |g_{κK*γ}^c g_{κNΛ}| = 1.1 GeV⁻¹, |g_{κK*γ}^c g_{κNΣ}| = 0.7 GeV⁻¹.
- In model (II): M_κ = 750 MeV, Γ_κ = 550 MeV, with the same coupling constants as model (I) but using a Gaussian form factor with Λ_κ = 1.2 GeV.
- Alternative parameter sets that also fit the K*0Σ+ data: M_κ = 900 MeV with |g_{κK*γ}^c g_{κNΣ}| = 1.2 GeV⁻¹, or M_κ = 600 MeV with |g_{κK*γ}^c g_{κNΣ}| = 0.4 GeV⁻¹.
- The coupling constant ratio R_κ = (g^c_{κK*γ} g_{κNΛ} / √2 g^0_{κK*γ} g_{κNΣ})² ≈ 0.3, implying a mild role of κ exchange in K*Λ production.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of γN → K*Λ (Oh and Kim, 2006) and addresses the unexpected similarity in cross sections between K*+Λ and K*0Σ+ photoproduction observed by the CLAS Collaboration (Hleiqawi and Guo, 2005; Guo and Weygand, 2006). The authors propose that the scalar κ meson, whose existence is still controversial (Particle Data Group, 2004), can resolve the discrepancy with the kaon-exchange dominance picture. The results suggest that parity asymmetry and photon beam asymmetry measurements at current experimental facilities can separate the κ meson contribution and constrain its properties.
