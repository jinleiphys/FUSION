# Effect of negative energy components for two-nucleon systems in the relativistic framework with the separable ansatz

> We investigate the electromagnetic properties of the deuteron such as the charge and magnetic form factors by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) with the separable ansatz. In solving the deuteron bound state solution to the BSE, we include negative energy components of $P$-wave in addition to the $^3S_1$ and $^3D_1$ states of positive energy, employing a rank IV separable ansatz. We found that the inclusion of the negative energy components improves systematically the electromagnetic properties which are not described in the conventional non-relativistic impulse approximation.

## Key claim

The inclusion of negative energy $P$-wave components in the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for the deuteron systematically improves the description of electromagnetic form factors and tensor polarizations, which are not well described by the conventional non-relativistic impulse approximation, but the optimal amount of negative energy admixture differs between the charge form factor (best at -3%) and the magnetic form factor (best at -0.5%).

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) for the deuteron bound state using a rank IV separable ansatz that extends the covariant Graz-II potential. They include negative energy components of $^1P_1^e$ and $^1P_1^o$ waves in addition to the positive energy $^3S_1^+$ and $^3D_1^+$ states, while ignoring $^3P_1$, $^3S_1^-$, and $^3D_1^-$ states for simplicity. The deuteron electromagnetic form factors are then computed in the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) using the resulting BS amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The $D$-wave ratio is fixed at 4% for the calculations including negative energy components.
- The $P$-wave negative energy component is controlled by parameter $u_4$, with values $u_4 = -3.5, -5.0, -8.5$ corresponding to $P$-wave ratios of $-0.5\%$, $-1\%$, and $-3\%$, respectively.
- The $g$-function parameters for the $P$-wave are chosen as $\beta_3 = 0.481$ GeV and $\gamma_3 = -15.0$.
- The charge form factor dip location and high-momentum behavior above $Q^2 > 1$ GeV$^2$ are significantly improved with $-3\%$ negative energy ratio.
- The magnetic form factor shows best agreement with $-0.5\%$ negative energy ratio.
- The tensor polarization $t_{20}$ is remarkably improved when the negative energy ratio is $-3\%$.

## Context

This work extends the covariant Graz-II potential approach of Rupp and Tjon (1990), who solved the BSE for deuteron phase shifts but did not include negative energy components. It also builds on non-relativistic studies of deuteron electromagnetic properties by Gari and Hyuga (1976), Tamura et al., Blunden et al., and Wiringa et al., which required meson-exchange currents to explain experimental data. The present relativistic framework naturally incorporates some exchange current effects through the $Z$-graph process via negative energy components, and the results enable improved descriptions of deuteron electromagnetic structure without explicit meson-exchange current modeling.
