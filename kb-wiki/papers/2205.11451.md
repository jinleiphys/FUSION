# Systematic shell model study for $N=82$ and $N=126$ isotones and nuclear isomers

> In the present work, we have done a systematic shell model study of $N=82$ and $N=126$ isotones. For the $N=82$ isotones, we have performed calculations using SN100PN interaction, while for $N=126$ isotones, we have used KHPE interaction. Similarities between these two isotonic chains have been reported, using the strong resemblance between the high-$j$ orbitals. Apart from the nuclear spectroscopic properties, we have also explained different isomeric states in these two regions. In the $N$ =82 region, we have mainly discussed the properties of the $6^+$ and $17/2^+$ isomers, while in the $N=126$ region for $8^+$, $11^-$, $21/2^-$ and $29/2^+$ isomers. We have reported $B(E2)$, $B(E3)$, $g$-factor, and quadrupole moments of the isomeric states for comparison in these two isotonic chains.

## Key claim

The authors perform a systematic shell-model comparison of $N=82$ and $N=126$ isotones, claiming strong similarities between the two isotonic chains based on the correspondence of high-$j$ orbitals, but the results are limited to the specific interactions used (SN100PN for $N=82$ and KHPE for $N=126$) and the comparison is qualitative rather than quantitative across all observables.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed for $N=82$ isotones ($52 \leq Z \leq 60$) using the SN100PN interaction in the 50-82 valence shell (orbitals $1g_{7/2}$, $2d_{5/2}$, $2d_{3/2}$, $3s_{1/2}$, $1h_{11/2}$), and for $N=126$ isotones ($84 \leq Z \leq 92$) using the KHPE interaction in the 82-126 proton valence shell (orbitals $1h_{9/2}$, $2f_{7/2}$, $2f_{5/2}$, $3p_{3/2}$, $3p_{1/2}$, $1i_{13/2}$) with no truncation. The calculations were performed using the KShell code, and results were compared with experimental data from the National Nuclear Data Center (NNDC) for energy levels, $B(E2)$, $B(E3)$, $g$-factors, and quadrupole moments of isomeric states.

## Key numbers

- For $^{134}$Te ($N=82$), a large gap of about 1.2 MeV between ground state $0^+$ and first excited state $2^+$ is reproduced
- For $^{210}$Po ($N=126$), the low-lying yrast states $0^+_1$, $2^+_1$, $4^+_1$, $6^+_1$, $8^+_1$ are constructed by $\pi h_{9/2}^2$ configuration, analogous to $^{134}$Te
- In $^{135}$I ($N=82$), the $17/2^+_1$ state is found to be a member of the $\pi g_{7/2}^2 d_{5/2}$ multiplet with seniority $\nu = 3$
- In $^{211}$At ($N=126$), the $29/2^+_1$ state is a seniority $\nu = 3$ state with $\pi h_{9/2}^2 i_{13/2}$ configuration
- For $^{212}$Rn ($N=126$), the $10^+_1$ state is dominated by $\pi h_{9/2}^4$ configuration with over 95% contribution and is a seniority $\nu = 4$ state
- In $^{214}$Ra ($N=126$), the ground state has $\pi h_{9/2}^4 i_{13/2}^2$ with 24% probability as seniority $\nu = 0$

## Context

This work extends the systematic comparison of $N=82$ and $N=126$ isotones previously explored by Coraggio et al. (various years), Astier et al. (various years), and Blomqvist (various years), who noted universal patterns of nuclear structure in both regions based on the correspondence between high-$j$ orbitals above the $Z=50$ and $Z=82$ shell gaps. The authors note that while experimental indications exist for similar properties between these two isotonic chains, no systematic theoretical results were previously available. This study enables future comparisons of isomeric states and electromagnetic properties across these two magic-number regions, and provides shell-model predictions for nuclei where experimental data are incomplete, particularly for neutron-rich short-lived nuclei in the $N=126$ region.
