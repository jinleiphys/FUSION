# The N=28 shell closure; from N=Z to the neutron drip line

> The evolution of the N=28 neutron shell closure is studied in the N=28 isotones from neutron drip line's $^{40}$Mg to N=Z doubly magic $^{56}$Ni. It is found that the N=28 closure vanishes at Z=12 in favor of a deformed ground state. For Z=14 and Z=16 closed shell and intruder configurations are almost degenerate, leading to highly mixed ground states and to very low lying excited 0$^+$ states. For Z$>$20 the intruder states are always above the closed shell ground states. We examine their structure and their possible existence as collective yrare bands of well defined particle-hole character.

## Key claim

The N=28 neutron shell closure, a spin-orbit-driven "classical" magic number, vanishes at Z=12 (in $^{40}$Mg) where a deformed ground state becomes favored, while for Z=14 and Z=16 the closed-shell and intruder configurations are nearly degenerate, leading to highly mixed ground states and very low-lying excited 0$^+$ states; for Z>20 the intruder states remain above the closed-shell ground states.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model diagonalizations in the $m$-scheme using the Lanczos algorithm (code ANTOINE) and in the J-coupled scheme (code NATHAN) for N=28 isotones ranging from $^{40}$Mg to $^{56}$Ni. For Z$\le$20, the valence space comprised the $pf$-shell for neutrons and the $sd$-shell for protons; for Z$>$20, the full $pf$-shell was used for both neutrons and protons with the KB3G interaction. Intruder configurations were characterized by imposing that two neutrons be outside the 1f$_{7/2}$ neutron orbit, and the Lanczos Strength Function method was employed to project closed-shell and intruder states onto the physical states.

## Key numbers

* In $^{48}$Ca, the intruder configuration is 5.38 MeV less bound than the closed shell; the neutron gap is 4.73 MeV and the intruder gains 4.08 MeV of correlation energy relative to the closed shell.
* In $^{40}$Mg, the intruder state lies 1.75 MeV below the closed shell (E$^*_{2p-2h}$ = -1.75 MeV), with a neutron gap of 3.35 MeV and a correlation energy difference of 8.45 MeV.
* In $^{44}$S, the intruder and closed-shell configurations are nearly degenerate (E$^*_{2p-2h}$ = -0.2 MeV), with a neutron gap of 3.23 MeV and a correlation energy difference of 6.66 MeV.
* In $^{52}$Cr, the first excited 0$^+$ state (dominated by intruder configurations) lies at 2.43 MeV (calculated) versus 2.65 MeV (experimental); the yrare 2p-2h band has B(E2)(2$^+$ $\rightarrow$ 0$^+$) = 238 $e^2$ fm$^4$ and spectroscopic quadrupole moment Q(2$^+$) = -27.7 $e$ fm$^2$.
* In $^{54}$Fe, the yrare 2p-2h band has B(E2)(2$^+$ $\rightarrow$ 0$^+$) = 232 $e^2$ fm$^4$ and Q(2$^+$) = -29.1 $e$ fm$^2$; the bandhead is at 2.77 MeV (calculated) versus 2.56 MeV (experimental).
* In $^{40}$Mg, the ground state is fully dominated by the deformed 2p-2h bandhead; the 2$^+$ state is at 0.81 MeV with Q(2$^+$) = -21 $e$ fm$^2$ and B(E2) = 108 $e^2$ fm$^4$.
* A superdeformed 4p-4h band is predicted in $^{42}$Si at an excitation energy of 5 MeV, with intrinsic quadrupole moment Q$_0$ = 86 $e$ fm$^2$ (deformation $\beta \sim 0.5$), terminating at spin 8$\hbar$.

## Context

This work extends the study of shell-closure evolution far from stability, following earlier observations that harmonic-oscillator closures vanish in neutron-rich regions (e.g., $^{31}$Na and $^{32}$Mg at N=20) and that spin-orbit closures like N=28 were thought more robust. The calculations confirm and refine the findings of Mizusaki et al. (2001) regarding collective yrare bands in $^{52}$Cr and $^{54}$Fe, but refute their prediction of a 4p-4h yrare band in $^{54}$Fe after full mixing. The results enable future experimental searches for shape coexistence and deformed intruder bands in neutron-rich N=28 isotones, particularly the predicted spherical-to-deformed shape transition at $^{40}$Mg and the superdeformed band in $^{42}$Si.
