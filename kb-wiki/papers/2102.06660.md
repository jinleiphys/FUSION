# Lattice QCD equation of state at finite chemical potential from an alternative expansion scheme

> Taylor expansion of the equation of state of QCD suffers from shortcomings at chemical potentials $μ_B \geq (2-2.5)T$. First, one faces difficulties inherent in performing such an expansion with a limited number of coefficients; second, higher order coefficients determined from lattice calculations suffer from a poor signal-to-noise ratio. In this work, we present a novel scheme for extrapolating the equation of state of QCD to finite, real chemical potential that can extend its reach further than previous methods. We present continuum extrapolated lattice results for the new expansion coefficients and show the thermodynamic observables up to $μ_B/T\le3.5$.

## Key claim

The paper presents an alternative expansion scheme for the QCD equation of state at finite baryon chemical potential that replaces the standard Taylor expansion at fixed temperature with a temperature-rescaling approach, claiming this method can extend reliable extrapolations to \(\mu_B/T \le 3.5\) rather than the \(\mu_B/T \lesssim 2-2.5\) limit of Taylor expansions, though the improvement relies on the assumption that finite-density observables can be described by a shifted temperature dependence.

## Method

The authors introduce \(\kappa\) parameters that describe the temperature shift of thermodynamic observables at finite \(\mu_B\), defined via \(T' = T(1 + \kappa_2^{ij}(T) \hat{\mu}_B^2 + \kappa_4^{ij}(T) \hat{\mu}_B^4 + \dots)\). They determine these coefficients using lattice QCD simulations at both zero and imaginary chemical potentials on \(32^3\times8\), \(40^3\times10\), \(48^3\times12\), and \(64^3\times16\) lattices with physical quark masses, performing combined continuum extrapolations and polynomial fits in \(\hat{\mu}_B^2\) to extract \(\kappa_2\) and \(\kappa_4\) for baryon number, baryon-strangeness, and strangeness observables.

## Key numbers

* Continuum extrapolated \(\kappa_2^{BB}(T)\) shows very mild temperature dependence in the transition region, with a value consistent with \(\kappa \approx 0.0205\) from a simple shift analysis on \(48^3\times12\) lattices
* \(\kappa_4^{BB}(T)\) is consistent with zero within errors throughout the entire temperature range studied, showing a separation of almost one order of magnitude from \(\kappa_2^{BB}(T)\)
* The new scheme provides thermodynamic observables up to \(\mu_B/T \le 3.5\), extending beyond the \(\mu_B/T \lesssim 2-2.5\) range where Taylor expansions show convergence issues
* Simulations were performed at imaginary chemical potentials \(\hat{\mu}_B = i n\pi/8\) with \(n \in \{3,4,5,6\}\) in the temperature range \(T = 135-245\) MeV
* The temperature range for \(\mu_B=0\) simulations was \(130-300\) MeV on all lattices, extended to \(500\) MeV on larger volumes

## Context

This work extends the Taylor expansion approach of Borsanyi (2018) and Bazavov (2017) by addressing the pathological non-monotonic behavior that appears in truncated Taylor series at \(\mu_B/T \gtrsim 2-2.5\), which was previously noted in chiral model studies by Friman (2011). The method is closely related to the "lines of constant physics" approach used by Bazavov (2017) but uses the temperature shift as a truncation scheme rather than as a post-processing analysis of Taylor results. The results enable improved equation of state inputs for hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions and can be compared with functional methods such as those of Fu (2021).
