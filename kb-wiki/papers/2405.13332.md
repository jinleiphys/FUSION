# Qualitative explanation of the data on the decays $D^0\to a_0(980)^\pmπ^\mp$ and $D^+\to a_0(980)^{+(0)}π^{0(+)}$ in the four-quark model of the $a_0(980)$ resonance

> It is shown that the values of the ratios $\mathcal{B}(D^0\to a_0(980)^+π^-)/\mathcal{B} (D^0\to a_0(980)^-π^+ )$ and $\mathcal{B}(D^+\to a_0(980)^+π^0)/\mathcal{B} (D^+\to a_0(980)^0 π^+)$, recently measured by the BESIII Collaboration, are naturally explained in the four-quark model of the $a_0(980) $ resonance.

## Key claim

The measured ratios of branching fractions for D-meson decays into a0(980) and pion final states, which contradict standard quark-diagram expectations, are naturally explained if the a0(980) resonance is a four-quark state rather than a conventional quark-antiquark meson, under the simplifying assumption that the double-vacuum-fluctuation amplitude is negligible and the remaining amplitudes are real and same-sign.

## Method

The authors constructed quark-level diagrams for the decays D0 → a0(980)+π−, D0 → a0(980)−π+, D+ → a0(980)+π0, and D+ → a0(980)0π+ in the four-quark model where a0(980) has the symbolic structure u dbar s sbar (for the charged states) or s sbar (u ubar − d dbar)/√2 (for the neutral state). They expressed the two measured branching-fraction ratios in terms of three amplitudes (a, b, e) corresponding to different vacuum-fluctuation mechanisms, then derived a relation between the two ratios by neglecting the double-fluctuation amplitude e and taking a and b as real and same-sign.

## Key numbers

* r_{+/-} = B(D0 → a0(980)+π−) / B(D0 → a0(980)−π+) = 7.5 (+2.5/−0.8) ± 1.7, measured by BESIII
* r_{+/0} = B(D+ → a0(980)+π0) / B(D+ → a0(980)0π+) = 2.6 ± 0.6 ± 0.3, measured by BESIII
* Using r_{+/-} = 7.5 in the derived relation gives r_{+/0} ≈ 2.48
* Using r_{+/0} = 2.6 in the derived relation gives r_{+/-} ≈ 6.93

## Context

This work addresses the discrepancy between the BESIII measurements and the expectation from standard quark-diagram analyses (Cheng, Chiang, and collaborators, 2022; BESIII Collaboration, 2024) that r_{+/-} would be less than 0.05. The authors extend the four-quark model of the a0(980) resonance developed by Jaffe (1977) and Achasov (1980, 1989, 1998, 2011) to D-meson decays, providing a qualitative explanation that previous evidence for the four-quark nature of a0(980) came from other experimental channels. The work enables future studies incorporating final-state interactions between a0(980) and pions, and may allow discrimination between the four-quark and molecular K Kbar models for the a0(980) state.
