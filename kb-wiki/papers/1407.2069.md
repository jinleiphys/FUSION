# Occupation numbers in strongly polarized Fermi gases and the Luttinger theorem

> We study a two-component Fermi gas that is so strongly polarized that it remains normal fluid at zero temperature. We calculate the occupation numbers within the particle-particle random-phase approximation, which is similar to the Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink approach. We show that the Luttinger theorem is fulfilled in this approach. We also study the change of the chemical potentials which allows us to extract, in the limit of extreme polarization, the polaron energy.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the particle-particle random-phase approximation (pp-RPA) satisfies the Luttinger theorem for strongly polarized Fermi gases at zero temperature, but the truncation of the Dyson equation to first order in the self-energy leads to unphysical occupation numbers (wrong sign of the jump or even negative values) for the minority species when the interaction becomes too strong.

## Method

The authors calculate occupation numbers and the in-medium T matrix within the zero-temperature pp-RPA formalism (ladder diagram resummation) for a two-component Fermi gas with contact interaction, using a renormalized coupling constant to handle ultraviolet divergences. The self-energy is treated to first order in the Dyson equation, and the correlation energy density is obtained via coupling-constant integration; chemical potentials are then computed by numerical differentiation of the energy density with respect to density.

## Key numbers

- For \(k_F^\down = k_F^\up/2\) (polarization \(P \approx 0.78\)) and interaction strength \(1/(k_F^\up a) = -0.4\), the occupation numbers appear reasonable, but for \(1/(k_F^\up a) = -0.2\) the jump in the \(\down\) occupation numbers has the wrong sign.
- The Luttinger theorem is satisfied to a precision of \(\sim 10^{-4}\) in the numerical integration of the occupation numbers.
- For the two strongest interactions studied, \(1/(k_F^\up a) = -0.2\) and \(-0.1\), the minority chemical potential \(\mu_\down\) increases with decreasing \(n_\down\) at polarizations \(P < 0.73\) and \(P < 0.85\), respectively.
- To avoid the unphysical sign of the jump in \(n_{\pv}^\down\), one should only consider polarizations \(P > 0.81\) for \(1/(k_F^\up a) = -0.2\) and \(P > 0.94\) for \(1/(k_F^\up a) = -0.1\).

## Context

This work extends the Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink (NSR) approach, which fails in the imbalanced case (producing \(\rho_\up < \rho_\down\) despite \(\mu_\up > \mu_\down\) near unitarity, as noted by Liu and Hu (2006), Parish and Marchetti (2007), and Kashimura and Watanabe (2012)), to the zero-temperature pp-RPA formalism. The authors show that pp-RPA avoids this pathology and satisfies the Luttinger theorem, and they extract the polaron energy in the extreme polarization limit, comparing with results from Combescot et al. (2007) that agree well with quantum Monte-Carlo calculations by Lobo et al. and Pilati et al. The approach enables future self-consistent finite-temperature treatments that include the shift of single-particle energies.
