# Antiscreening of the Ampere force in QED and QCD plasmas

> The static forces between electric charges and currents are modified at the loop level by the presence of a plasma. While electric charges are screened, currents are not. The effective coupling constant at long distances is enhanced in both cases as compared to the vacuum, and by different amounts, a clear sign that Lorentz symmetry is broken. We investigate these effects quantitatively, first in a QED plasma and secondly using non-perturbative simulations of QCD with two light degenerate flavors of quarks.

## Key claim

The static Ampère force between currents in a QED or QCD plasma is not screened but is instead enhanced at long distances relative to the vacuum, quantified by a positive coefficient κ_t; this antiscreening effect is distinct from the Debye screening of electric charges and signals the breaking of Lorentz symmetry in the medium.

## Method

The authors computed the medium-induced modification of the static polarization tensor in the long-wavelength limit, extracting the coefficients κ_ℓ (longitudinal) and κ_t (transverse) from the small-momentum expansion of the matter part of the polarization. For QED, they used one-loop perturbative formulae for a Dirac fermion of mass m; for QCD, they performed non-perturbative lattice Monte Carlo simulations with two degenerate light quark flavors (O(a)-improved Wilson fermions) at T ≈ 250 MeV (T/T_c ≈ 1.2) on a 16×64³ lattice, subtracting the zero-temperature vacuum polarization on a 128×64³ ensemble.

## Key numbers

- For light quarks (m_ud/T = 0.0325(48)(7) in MSbar at 2 GeV), the transverse screening mass M_t^V/(2πT) = 0.943(17), lying below the perturbative prediction of 1 + 0.02980 g² for two massless flavors.
- For strange quarks (m_s/T = 0.3747(30)(86)), M_t^V/(2πT) = 0.973(21).
- The squared coupling of the lightest screening state to the vector current in the transverse channel is |A_1|²/T³ = 6.05 ± 0.78 for light quarks and 5.80 ± 1.10 for strange quarks.
- In QED at T ≈ 0.8 MeV (around the time of neutron beta decay in the early universe), κ_ℓ and κ_t represent a 2–3 permille effect on the inter-proton electromagnetic force.
- The difference κ_ℓ − κ_t is infrared-safe and at leading order in massless QED equals 1/(12π²) plus small corrections.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative analysis of the static polarization tensor in QED (Kapusta 2006) and the leading-order weak-coupling calculation of κ in SU(N) gauge theory (Romatschke 2009) to a non-perturbative lattice QCD determination. The coefficient κ_t also appears as a second-order transport coefficient in the constitutive equation of the electromagnetic current (Hong 2010). The results enable quantitative predictions for the modification of electromagnetic forces in the quark-gluon plasma and in the early universe, and provide a benchmark for future perturbative calculations of screening masses and couplings.
