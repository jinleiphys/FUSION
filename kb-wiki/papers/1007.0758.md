# Systematic Improvement of Parton Showers with Effective Theory

> We carry out a systematic classification and computation of next-to-leading order kinematic power corrections to the fully differential cross section in the parton shower. To do this we devise a map between ingredients in a parton shower and operators in a traditional effective field theory framework using a chain of soft-collinear effective theories. Our approach overcomes several difficulties including avoiding double counting and distinguishing approximations that are coordinate choices from true power corrections. Branching corrections can be classified as hard-scattering, that occur near the top of the shower, and jet-structure, that can occur at any point inside it. Hard-scattering corrections include matrix elements with additional hard partons, as well as power suppressed contributions to the branching for the leading jet. Jet-structure corrections require simultaneous consideration of potential 1 -> 2 and 1 -> 3 branchings. The interference structure induced by collinear terms with subleading powers remains localized in the shower.

## Key claim

The authors develop a systematic effective field theory framework to compute next-to-leading order kinematic power corrections (NLO(λ)) to the fully differential cross section in parton showers, going beyond the leading-log (LL) approximation. The approach overcomes technical obstacles including double counting and distinguishing true power corrections from coordinate choices, but the work focuses on establishing the theoretical framework and computing the power corrections rather than providing an algorithmic implementation.

## Method

The authors construct a chain of soft-collinear effective theories (SCET_i) with decreasing power counting parameters to describe successive emissions in a parton shower, mapping shower ingredients onto operators in the effective theory. They perform matching calculations between QCD and SCET_1, SCET_1 and SCET_2, and SCET_2 and SCET_3 at subleading order in the kinematic expansion parameter λ, classifying corrections as either "hard-scattering" (occurring near the top of the shower) or "jet-structure" (occurring at any point in the shower). The matching computations incorporate reparametrization invariance (RPI) to distinguish kinematic coordinate conventions from genuine power corrections, and they derive anomalous dimensions for subleading operators to encode no-branching probabilities.

## Key numbers

- The power counting parameter λ ≪ 1 governs the kinematic expansion, with collinear momenta scaling as (n·p, p̄, p⊥) ∼ (λ², 1, λ) p̄ and soft momenta scaling as (λ², λ², λ²) Q
- Operators scale as O^(j,k,ℓ) ∼ λ^(j+k+ℓ) where j is the number of quark fields, k is the number of gluon fields, and ℓ is the number of perpendicular derivatives
- The goal is to compute dσ^LO + dσ^NLO(λ) for the fully differential cross section with n final state emissions, where NLO(λ) represents terms suppressed by λ² relative to leading order
- The leading order splitting function for q → qg is P_(q→qg)^(0) = (α_s/2π) C_F (1+z²)/(1-z), where z is the longitudinal momentum fraction

## Context

This work extends the earlier SCET-based parton shower framework of Bauer and Schwartz (2006) by developing a systematic approach to include kinematic power corrections beyond the leading-log approximation. The authors address limitations of traditional parton shower Monte Carlos like Pythia and Herwig, which use the strongly-ordered limit and leading-order splitting functions, and relate their corrections to existing approaches including CKKW-L merging, POWHEG, MC@NLO, and the GenEvA framework. The results enable future implementation of NLO(λ)-corrected parton shower algorithms that account for 1→3 splittings, spin correlations, and interference effects that remain localized in the shower.
