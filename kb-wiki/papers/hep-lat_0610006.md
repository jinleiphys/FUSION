# On the infrared behavior of Green's functions in Yang-Mills theory

> Non-perturbative properties of QCD, such as color confinement, are encoded in the infrared behavior of correlation functions, e.g. propagators and vertices. Various analytic predictions have been suggested for these quantities in various gauges. Here we numerically test these predictions using lattice gauge theory. In particular, we present results for the 2- and 3-point functions for SU(2) Landau-gauge Yang-Mills theory in three and in four dimensions. Special attention is paid to systematic finite-volume effects. The gluon and ghost propagators are also evaluated in the so-called interpolating gauge (between the Landau and the Coulomb gauge), in order to study their gauge-dependence. Finally, we consider these propagators in Landau gauge at finite temperature, with the aim of understanding the effect of the deconfinement phase transition on their infrared behavior. All our results are compatible with the so-called Gribov-Zwanziger confinement scenario.

## Key claim

All lattice results are compatible with the Gribov-Zwanziger confinement scenario, but the true infrared regime is only reached below a momentum scale of about 200 MeV, requiring lattice volumes with linear size N much larger than 50 in lattice units, a condition just reached in 3d but not yet in 4d.

## Method

The authors performed lattice gauge theory simulations of SU(2) Yang-Mills theory in Landau gauge in three and four dimensions, measuring gluon and ghost propagators and the ghost-gluon and three-gluon vertices. They also evaluated the gluon and ghost propagators in the interpolating gauge (between Landau and Coulomb gauges) in 3d, and studied the gluon and ghost propagators in Landau gauge at finite temperature in 4d. Special attention was paid to systematic finite-volume effects by comparing results across multiple lattice volumes.

## Key numbers

- The true infrared regime is reached only below a momentum scale Lambda_I less than or about 200 MeV, requiring lattice volumes with N much greater than 50 for a lattice spacing a of order 1 GeV^-1.
- In 3d Landau gauge, lattice volumes studied ranged from 20^3 (V approximately 3.5 fm^3) up to 140^3 (V approximately 24 fm^3), all at beta=4.2.
- At finite temperature, simulations used beta=2.3 with temperatures T approximately 149 MeV (10x26^3 lattice), T approximately 298 MeV (4x34^3 lattice), and T approximately 597 MeV (2x42^3 lattice), compared to T=0 on a 20^4 lattice.
- For the highest temperature (T approximately 597 MeV), the 3d-transverse gluon propagator shows a clear infrared suppression with a maximum at p approximately 600 MeV.

## Context

This work numerically tests analytic predictions from Dyson-Schwinger equation studies (Alkofer 2000, Fischer, von Smekal, Zwanziger) and renormalization group methods (Gies) against lattice data, extending earlier lattice studies of propagators (Cucchieri 2006, Sternbeck 2005). The results support the Gribov-Zwanziger confinement scenario and the Kugo-Ojima scenario, and suggest that this confinement picture persists at finite temperature. The study of the interpolating gauge (Baulieu 1998, Capri 2005) connects Landau and Coulomb gauge results, while the finite-temperature analysis (Karsch 2001) provides a first step toward understanding how the deconfinement phase transition affects infrared correlation functions.
