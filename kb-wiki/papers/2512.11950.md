# From DGLAP to Sudakov: Precision Predictions for Energy-Energy Correlators

> Correlations in the distribution of energy produced in collider experiments provide a snapshot of the microscopic dynamics of QCD, and its evolution from asymptotically free quarks and gluons, to confined hadrons. There has recently been considerable progress in the interpretation and precision calculation of these correlations, using a specific class of observables called energy correlators (EECs). These observables are most cleanly studied in $e^+e^-$ collisions, where they can be measured over their full angular range. Of particular interest are kinematic limits of the correlator, both collinear, and back-to-back, where the correlator exhibits scaling behaviors governed by specific operators in QCD. Resolving these scalings requires measurements with exceptional angular resolution, which can be achieved by performing measurements on tracks (charged particles). In this paper we perform the first calculation of the track-based EEC over its entire kinematic range, achieving a record precision of of NNLL (collinear) + NNLO (fixed order) + NNNNLL (back-to-back) for the track-based EEC, and additionally incorporate the leading non-perturbative corrections and their resummation, including the Collins-Soper kernel computed using lattice QCD. We describe the breadth of physics probed by this observable, and highlight the impact of different components of our factorization theorem on the final distribution. Combined with recent measurements of the track-based EEC with archival LEP data, our calculation initiates the precision study of track-based observables at LEP, which will lead to new insights into the dynamics of QCD, and the precision extraction of its underlying parameters.

## Key claim

The authors present the first calculation of the track-based energy-energy correlator (EEC) over its entire kinematic range in electron-positron collisions, achieving record precision of NNLL (collinear) + NNLO (fixed order) + NNNNLL (back-to-back), but this precision is contingent on the observable being measured on charged particles (tracks) rather than all particles, which introduces non-perturbative corrections that must be handled through the track function formalism and lattice QCD inputs.

## Method

The calculation combines fixed-order perturbation theory at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) with resummation in two kinematic limits: next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) resummation in the collinear limit (z approaching 0) and next-to-next-to-next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNNNLL) resummation in the back-to-back limit (z approaching 1). The authors incorporate leading non-perturbative corrections using universal parameters extracted from event shape measurements and the Collins-Soper kernel computed from lattice QCD, with these corrections dressed by LL resummation in the collinear limit and NLL resummation in the back-to-back limit.

## Key numbers

* The calculation achieves NNLL precision in the collinear limit, NNLO fixed order, and NNNNLL precision in the back-to-back limit for the track-based EEC
* The energy scale of the collisions is at the Z-boson mass, Q = m_Z
* The observable is a function of a single variable z = 1/2 (1 - cos chi), where chi is the angle between two detectors
* The scale probed by the correlator is mu^2 = Q^2 z in the collinear limit and mu^2 = Q^2 (1-z) in the back-to-back limit
* The track-based EEC satisfies a non-perturbative sum rule: integral of EEC(z) over z equals the average of the squared energy fraction of charged particles divided by Q^2

## Context

This work extends the track function formalism developed by Chang (2013) and applied to energy correlators by Jaarsma, Li, Moult, Waalewijn, and Zhu (2022-2023), enabling multi-loop calculations and high-order resummation in kinematic limits. The calculation is motivated by recent re-analyses of archival LEP data from the ALEPH and DELPHI experiments that achieved measurements of the track-based EEC with extraordinary angular resolution. Combined with these measurements, this work initiates the precision study of track-based observables at LEP and enables new extractions of the strong coupling constant alpha_s, potentially resolving discrepancies between lattice QCD determinations (alpha_s = 0.1184 plus or minus 0.0008) and those from precision e+e- event shapes such as thrust (alpha_s = 0.1136 plus or minus 0.0012).
