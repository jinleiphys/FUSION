# Constraining the viscous freeze-out distribution function with data obtained at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC)

> We investigate the form of the viscous correction to the equilibrium distribution function in the context of a Cooper-Frye freeze out prescription for viscous hydrodynamic simulations of relativistic heavy ion collisions. The standard quadratic ansatz used by all groups for the case of shear viscosity is found to be disfavored by experimental data for v4/(v2)^2 at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider, and is unlikely to be correct for the hadron resonance gas present at freeze out. Instead, data for v2(pt) along with v4/(v2)^2 favor a momentum dependence between linear and quadratic.

## Key claim

The standard quadratic ansatz for the viscous correction to the equilibrium distribution function (δf ∝ p² f₀) used by all groups in viscous hydrodynamic simulations is disfavored by experimental data for v₄/(v₂)² at RHIC, and the correct momentum dependence for the hadron resonance gas at freeze out likely lies between linear and quadratic.

## Method

The authors performed 2+1 dimensional conformal viscous hydrodynamic simulations with both Glauber- and CGC-type initial conditions, a constant value of η/s, a realistic equation of state, and a Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription including resonance feed down. They replaced the standard quadratic ansatz for δf (χ ∝ p²) with a linear ansatz (χ ∝ p) and an intermediate power law (χ ∝ p¹·⁵), then compared the resulting differential elliptic flow v₂(pₜ) and the ratio v₄/(v₂)² to PHENIX experimental data at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- For an observed pion with pₜ in the range 1–4 GeV, the momentum in the fluid frame p is in the range 0.4–1.7 GeV; a collision with a thermal pion at T = 0.14 GeV then occurs at √s in the range 0.4–1 GeV.
- The viscous Glauber calculation used η/s = 0.08 and initial temperature Tᵢ = 0.333 GeV; the CGC calculation used η/s = 0.16 and Tᵢ = 0.299 GeV.
- Experimental data for v₄/(v₂)² were scaled down by a factor of 1.37 to account for fluctuations.
- All hadrons and resonances up to approximately 2 GeV were included in the calculation.

## Context

This work extends the viscous hydrodynamic framework of Luzum (2008) and addresses the open question raised by Dusling (2009) regarding the correct form of the viscous correction δf for the hadron resonance gas at freeze out. The quadratic ansatz, previously used by all groups and correct for simple systems like φ⁴ theory, is shown to be inconsistent with RHIC data. The results enable future work to extract microscopic information about hadronic cross sections from hydrodynamic comparisons to data, and suggest that improved calculations could constrain the freeze-out dynamics of the hadron resonance gas.
