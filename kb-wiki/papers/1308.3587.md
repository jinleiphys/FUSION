# Combined analysis of the $K^{+}K^{-}$ interaction using near threshold $pp \to ppK^+K^-$ data

> The $K^{+}K^{-}$ final state interaction was investigated based on both the $K^{+}K^{-}$ invariant mass distributions measured at excess energies of Q = 10 and 28 MeV and the near threshold excitation function for the $pp \to ppK^{+}K^{-}$ reaction. The $K^+K^-$ final state enhancement factor was parametrized using the effective range expansion. The effective range of the $K^+K^-$ interaction was estimated to be: $\mathrm{Re}(b_{K^{+}K^{-}}) = -0.1 \pm 0.4_{stat} \pm 0.3_{sys} \mathrm{fm}$ and $\mathrm{Im}(b_{K^{+}K^{-}}) = 1.2^{+0.1_{stat}+0.2_{sys}}_{-0.2_{stat}-0.0_{sys}} \mathrm{fm}$, and the determined real and imaginary parts of the $K^+K^-$ scattering length amount to: $|\mathrm{Re}(a_{K^{+}K^{-}})| = 8.0^{+6.0_{stat}}_{-4.0_{stat}} \mathrm{fm}$ and $\mathrm{Im}(a_{K^{+}K^{-}}) = 0.0^{+20.0_{stat}}_{-5.0_{stat}} \mathrm{fm}$.

## Key claim

The $K^+K^-$ final state interaction is significant near the kinematical threshold of the $pp \to ppK^+K^-$ reaction, but the fit is only weakly sensitive to the scattering length; the effective range of the interaction is estimated for the first time, while the scattering length remains poorly constrained.

## Method

The authors performed a combined fit to both the total cross section excitation function (near threshold) and the differential invariant mass distributions (Goldhaber plots) at excess energies Q = 10 and 28 MeV from the COSY-11 experiment. The final state interaction was factorized into enhancements in the $pp$, $pK^-$, and $K^+K^-$ subsystems, with the $K^+K^-$ term parametrized using the effective range expansion; the $pK^-$ scattering length was fixed to the mean of values from the literature ($a_{pK^-} = (-0.65 + 0.78i)$ fm). A $\chi^2$ statistic combining Neyman's $\chi^2$ for the excitation function and a Poisson likelihood for the Goldhaber plots was minimized with the $K^+K^-$ scattering length $a_{K^+K^-}$ and effective range $b_{K^+K^-}$ as free complex parameters.

## Key numbers

- Real part of the $K^+K^-$ effective range: $\mathrm{Re}(b_{K^{+}K^{-}}) = -0.1 \pm 0.4_{stat} \pm 0.3_{sys}$ fm.
- Imaginary part of the $K^+K^-$ effective range: $\mathrm{Im}(b_{K^{+}K^{-}}) = 1.2^{+0.1_{stat}+0.2_{sys}}_{-0.2_{stat}-0.0_{sys}}$ fm.
- Absolute value of the real part of the $K^+K^-$ scattering length: $|\mathrm{Re}(a_{K^{+}K^{-}})| = 8.0^{+6.0_{stat}}_{-4.0_{stat}}$ fm.
- Imaginary part of the $K^+K^-$ scattering length: $\mathrm{Im}(a_{K^{+}K^{-}}) = 0.0^{+20.0_{stat}}_{-5.0_{stat}}$ fm.
- Goodness of fit: $\chi^2/ndof = 1.30$ (five parameters varied, statistical uncertainties at 70% confidence level).

## Context

This work extends the earlier COSY-11 analysis (PhysRevC) by combining Goldhaber plot data with the near-threshold excitation function from ANKE and DISTO measurements, improving the precision of the $K^+K^-$ interaction parameters. The results confirm that the $pp \to ppK^+K^-$ reaction is dominated by the $pK^-$ interaction (likely via $\Lambda(1405)$ production) rather than scalar meson resonances, and that the $K^+K^-$ interaction is relatively weak. The authors note that higher-statistics data from ANKE at COSY or from $e^+e^- \to K^+K^-\gamma$ reactions at KLOE-2 will be needed for a precise determination of the kaon-antikaon scattering length.
