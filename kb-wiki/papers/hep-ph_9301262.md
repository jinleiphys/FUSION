# Nuclear Gluon Distributions in a Parton Model

> Gluon distributions in the carbon and tin nuclei are investigated by using a $Q^2$ rescaling model with parton recombination effects. We obtain strong shadowings in the small $x$ region due to the recombinations. The ratio $G_A(x)/G_N(x)$ in the medium $x$ region is typically 0.9 for medium size nuclei. At large $x$, the ratio becomes large due to gluon fusions from different nucleons. Comparisons with recent New Muon Collaboration data for $G_{Sn} (x)/ G_C (x)$ indicate that more accurate experimental data are needed for testing the model.

## Key claim

Gluon distributions in carbon and tin nuclei are investigated using a Q^2 rescaling model that includes parton recombination effects, producing strong shadowing at small x and enhancement at large x; however, the author states that more accurate experimental data are needed for testing the model, indicating that the comparison with New Muon Collaboration data is not yet conclusive.

## Method

The author applies a Q^2 rescaling model to nuclear parton distributions, incorporating parton recombination effects to modify the gluon distributions in carbon and tin nuclei. The ratio G_A(x)/G_N(x) is calculated as a function of Bjorken x, and the results are compared with data from the New Muon Collaboration for the ratio G_Sn(x)/G_C(x).

## Context

This work extends the Q^2 rescaling approach to nuclear parton distributions by adding parton recombination effects, which produce strong shadowing at small x and gluon fusion enhancement at large x. The comparison with New Muon Collaboration data for tin-to-carbon gluon ratios suggests the model is promising but requires more precise experimental measurements to be validated. The approach enables further theoretical work on nuclear gluon distributions and their role in understanding nuclear structure functions in deep inelastic scattering.
