# Magnetism in Dense Quark Matter

> We review the mechanisms via which an external magnetic field can affect the ground state of cold and dense quark matter. In the absence of a magnetic field, at asymptotically high densities, cold quark matter is in the Color-Flavor-Locked (CFL) phase of color superconductivity characterized by three scales: the superconducting gap, the gluon Meissner mass, and the baryonic chemical potential. When an applied magnetic field becomes comparable with each of these scales, new phases and/or condensates may emerge. They include the magnetic CFL (MCFL) phase that becomes relevant for fields of the order of the gap scale; the paramagnetic CFL, important when the field is of the order of the Meissner mass, and a spin-one condensate associated to the magnetic moment of the Cooper pairs, significant at fields of the order of the chemical potential. We discuss the equation of state (EoS) of MCFL matter for a large range of field values and consider possible applications of the magnetic effects on dense quark matter to the astrophysics of compact stars.

## Key claim

An external magnetic field can significantly modify the ground state of cold dense quark matter even when the field is much weaker than the baryon chemical potential, because the color superconductor is characterized by multiple scales (the superconducting gap, the gluon Meissner mass, and the chemical potential) and different physical effects emerge when the field becomes comparable to each of these scales.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical mechanisms by which a rotated magnetic field (the long-range electromagnetic field that penetrates the color superconductor) affects spin-zero color superconductivity in the Color-Flavor-Locked (CFL) phase at asymptotically high densities. They analyze the gap equations derived from Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) effective models, considering how the magnetic field modifies the density of states at the Fermi surface and leads to the splitting of the CFL gap into distinct gaps for pairs of charged and neutral quarks. The symmetry breaking patterns and low-energy effective theories are compared between the CFL and magnetic-CFL (MCFL) phases.

## Key numbers

- The maximum interior magnetic field for a quark star, estimated from the binding energy of iron nuclei (939 MeV), is approximately \(1.5 \times 10^{20}\) G.
- The threshold field for the effective CFL to MCFL crossover is \(\widetilde{e}\widetilde{B}_{MCFL} = 12 \Delta_{CFL}^2\); for \(\Delta_{CFL} \sim 15\) MeV this gives \(\widetilde{e}\widetilde{B}_{MCFL} \sim 10^{16}\) G.
- For NJL model parameters with \(x \equiv g^2 \mu^2 / \Lambda^2 \pi^2 \sim 0.3\) and \(y \equiv \widetilde{e}\widetilde{B}/\mu^2 = 3/2\), the neutral gap \(\Delta \sim 0.2\,\Delta_B\); for \(x \sim 1\), \(\Delta \sim 0.5\,\Delta_B\).
- The CFL gap used as a reference scale is \(\Delta_{CFL} = 25\) MeV at \(\mu = 500\) MeV.
- Surface magnetic fields of magnetars are observed in the range \(10^{14} - 10^{16}\) G.
- Maximum interior fields for gravitationally bound stars with nuclear matter are \(\sim 10^{18}\) G, while for stars with quark matter cores the maximum is \(\sim 10^{19} - 10^{20}\) G.

## Context

This review extends the authors' prior work on the magnetic CFL phase (Ferrer and de la Incera, 2006-2012) and the discovery of a spin-one condensate associated with the magnetic moment of Cooper pairs (Ferrer and de la Incera, 2010). It contrasts the magnetic catalysis of color superconductivity with the magnetic catalysis of chiral symmetry breaking studied by Gusynin, Miransky, and Shovkovy (1995-1999). The results enable predictions for the equation of state and observable signatures of strongly magnetized compact stars, particularly magnetars, and raise the open question of whether the CFL-MCFL crossover exhibits singularities analogous to Mott transitions in condensed matter.
