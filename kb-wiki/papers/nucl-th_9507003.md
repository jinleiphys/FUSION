# On the Numerical Solution of the Time-Dependent Schroedinger Equation

> An algorithm for the numerical solution of the Schrödinger equation in the case of a time dependent potential is proposed. Our simple modification upgrades the well known method of Koonin while negligibly increasing the computing time. In the presented test the accuracy is enhanced by up to an order of magnitude.

# On the Numerical Solution of the Time-Dependent Schrödinger Equation

## Key claim

The authors propose a simple modification to Koonin's standard algorithm for solving the time-dependent Schrödinger equation with an explicitly time-dependent potential, which includes terms depending on the time derivative of the potential (V-dot) to achieve consistency up to second order in the time step. In the presented test case, this modification enhances accuracy by up to an order of magnitude while negligibly increasing computing time, but the improvement is demonstrated only for a specific analytically solvable harmonic oscillator potential.

## Method

The authors start from Koonin's alternating direction implicit method for solving the space-discretized Schrödinger equation on a cylindrical grid, then modify the time propagation operator by including terms proportional to the time derivative of the potential (V-dot) evaluated at time t_n. The algorithm proceeds by solving two sets of tridiagonal matrix equations sequentially: first for an intermediate quantity w^(n), then for g^(n+1), where the V-dot terms are computed from the potential values at consecutive time steps as (V^(n) - V^(n-1))/Δt. The method is tested against an analytically solvable harmonic oscillator potential with a linear time-dependent term.

## Key numbers

- For the test with ω = 2, Δt = 1/60, 60 time steps, grid N_ρ = N_z = 64, and Δρ = Δz = 0.25, the relative error of the real part for ℓ=0 is 10% with the standard algorithm versus 0.4% with the modified algorithm.
- For ℓ=0, the relative error of the imaginary part is 4% (standard) versus 0.2% (modified).
- For ℓ=2, the relative error of the imaginary part is 40% (standard) versus 5% (modified).
- For ℓ=3, the relative error of the real part is 20% (standard) versus 4% (modified).

## Context

This work extends the numerical method of Koonin, Davies, Maruhn-Rezwani, Feldmeier, Krieger, and Negele (1977) for solving the time-dependent Schrödinger equation, which neglected the V-dot terms in the time expansion. The modification is motivated by applications to ternary fission (a fission process accompanied by alpha-particle emission), with results to be published separately. The algorithm enables more accurate simulations of time-dependent quantum systems such as atomic or nuclear collisions, nuclear fission, and fusion, where collective coordinates lead to an effective one-body Schrödinger equation with a time-dependent potential.
