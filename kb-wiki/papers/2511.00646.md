# Implication of multimessenger observations on the relativistic mean-field equation of state of dense nuclear matter and skin thickness of nuclei

> The composition and properties of infinite nuclear matter under extreme conditions of temperature and pressure remain incompletely understood. In this work, we constrain the equation of state (EoS) of nuclear matter - constructed within the framework of the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model - by combining results from chiral effective field theory and multimessenger observations of neutron stars. Using the saturation properties of nuclear matter, we generate a wide ensemble of EoS, which are subsequently constrained within a Bayesian framework. The resulting posterior distributions provide tight bounds on both the saturation parameters and the coupling constants of the RMF model. Our results indicate that the GW170817 event and the latest NICER observation favor a relatively soft EoS, leading to lower crust-core transition densities and thinner neutron star crusts. The radius of a $1.4\,M_\odot$ neutron star is tightly constrained to $12.508_{-0.241}^{+0.257}$ km, while the maximum mass reaches $2.174_{-0.123}^{+0.174}\,M_\odot$. Furthermore, our analysis reveals that the $ω$-$ρ$ coupling, which governs the density dependence of the symmetry energy, becomes increasingly significant under successive astrophysical constraints. Finally, the predicted neutron skin thickness of $^{48}$Ca agrees well with the CREX measurement, whereas that of $^{208}$Pb remains in tension with PREX-II. In contrast to earlier studies, we do not observe a clear correlation between the neutron skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb and the symmetry energy slope parameter $L$.

## Key claim

The authors find that combining chiral effective field theory (χ-EFT) with multimessenger observations (GW170817 and NICER) within a Bayesian framework favors a relatively soft equation of state for dense nuclear matter, but they explicitly note that, in contrast to earlier studies, they do not observe a clear correlation between the neutron skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb and the symmetry energy slope parameter L.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with six saturation properties (ρ₀, E₀, M*/M, K, Esym, L) as free parameters, drawing priors from nuclear theory and experiments. They generate an ensemble of equations of state, then apply Bayesian inference via nested sampling (PyMultinest) to impose sequential constraints: χ-EFT up to 1.12ρ₀, the maximum mass of PSR J0740+6620, tidal deformability from GW170817, and mass-radius data from four NICER pulsars (PSR J0030+0451, PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0437-4715, PSR J0614-3329). The posterior distributions of the RMF coupling parameters are then used to compute the neutron skin thickness of ⁴⁸Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb for comparison with CREX and PREX-II measurements.

## Context

This work extends Bayesian inference studies of the nuclear equation of state (e.g., Biswas (2019), Malik (2022), Char (2023)) by using RMF model parameters directly tied to saturation properties rather than abstract couplings, and by incorporating the latest NICER measurements including PSR J0437-4715 (Choudhury 2024) and PSR J0614-3329 (Mauviard 2025). The predicted neutron skin thickness of ⁴⁸Ca agrees with the CREX measurement, while that of ²⁰⁸Pb remains in tension with PREX-II, and the authors find no clear correlation between the ²⁰⁸Pb skin thickness and the symmetry energy slope L, contradicting earlier studies such as Vinas (2013) and Reinhard (2016). This enables future work to explore more complex density dependence of the symmetry energy or limitations in existing nuclear models.
