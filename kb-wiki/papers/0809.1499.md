# Heavy Quark Diffusion with Relativistic Langevin Dynamics in the Quark-Gluon Fluid

> The relativistic diffusion process of heavy quarks is formulated on the basis of the relativistic Langevin equation in Itô discretization scheme. The drag force inside the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is parametrized according to the formula for the strongly coupled plasma obtained by the AdS/CFT correspondence. The diffusion dynamics of charm and bottom quarks in QGP is described by combining the Langevin simulation under the background matter described by the relativistic hydrodynamics. Theoretical calculations of the nuclear modification factor R_{AA} and the elliptic flow v_{2} for the single electrons from the charm and bottom decays are compared with the experimental data from the relativistic heavy ion collisions. The R_{AA} for electrons with large transverse momentum (p_{T} > 3 GeV) indicates that the drag force from the QGP is as strong as the AdS/CFT prediction.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) for single electrons from heavy quark decays with transverse momentum above 3 GeV is consistent with a drag force as strong as the AdS/CFT prediction, but only under the assumption that the dimensionless drag coefficient \(\gamma\) is treated as a free parameter and that the Langevin approach is applicable only where the condition \(\tau_B \gg \tau_m\) holds.

## Method

The authors formulated a relativistic Langevin equation in the Itô discretization scheme for charm and bottom quarks diffusing in a quark-gluon plasma background. The background QGP fluid was evolved using (3+1)-dimensional ideal relativistic hydrodynamics with a bag model equation of state, and the drag force on heavy quarks was parametrized as \(\Gamma = \gamma T^2 / M\) with the diffusion constant determined by a generalized fluctuation-dissipation relation. The Langevin simulation was run from initial heavy quark distributions generated by PYTHIA 6.4 until freezeout, after which hadronization and semileptonic decays into electrons were computed using PYTHIA.

## Key numbers

* Relaxation time for charm quarks at \(T = 210\) MeV: 22 fm for \(\gamma = 0.30\), 6.7 fm for \(\gamma = 1.0\), and 2.2 fm for \(\gamma = 3.0\).
* Relaxation time for bottom quarks at \(T = 210\) MeV: 72 fm for \(\gamma = 0.30\), 21 fm for \(\gamma = 1.0\), and 7.2 fm for \(\gamma = 3.0\).
* Effective QGP lifetime at impact parameter \(b = 3.1\) fm: 9.8 fm for freezeout condition \(f_0 = 0\), 5.9 fm for \(f_0 = 0.5\), and 4.5 fm for \(f_0 = 1\).
* Effective QGP lifetime at impact parameter \(b = 5.5\) fm: 8.7 fm for \(f_0 = 0\), 5.2 fm for \(f_0 = 0.5\), and 4.0 fm for \(f_0 = 1\).
* The AdS/CFT-motivated drag coefficient estimate: \(\Gamma_{\rm AdS/CFT} = (2.1 \pm 0.5) T^2 / M\).
* The averaged temperature experienced by heavy quarks lies between 200 MeV and 220 MeV across a wide range of initial \(p_T\) and \(\gamma\) values.

## Context

This work extends earlier perturbative QCD calculations of heavy quark energy loss (Djordjevic 2006, Wicks 2007) which suffer from poor convergence of the weak-coupling expansion at RHIC temperatures (Moore 2008), and instead adopts a drag force parametrization motivated by the AdS/CFT correspondence (Herzog 2006, Gubser 2006, Teaney 2006). By combining relativistic Langevin dynamics with a hydrodynamic background, the paper enables quantitative comparison of heavy quark diffusion models with RHIC data on single electron \(R_{AA}\) and \(v_2\), and provides a framework for extracting the effective drag coefficient \(\gamma\) from experimental observables.
