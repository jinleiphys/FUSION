# Dilute Nuclear Matter in Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We use chiral perturbation theory to compute the effective nucleon propagator in an expansion about low density in the chiral limit. We neglect four-nucleon interactions and focus on pion exchange. Evaluating the nucleon self-energy on its mass shell to leading order, we show that the effective nucleon mass increases by a small amount. We discuss the relevance of our results to the structure of compact stars.

## Key claim

Using chiral perturbation theory to compute the nucleon self-energy on its mass shell (rather than at zero momentum as in prior work), the effective nucleon mass increases by a small amount at low density, but this result is a minor point of principle because the mass shift is dominated by contact terms, not single pion exchange.

## Method

The authors computed the nucleon self-energy from single pion exchange at one-loop order using the imaginary time formalism, evaluating it on the mass shell in the chiral limit and neglecting four-nucleon interactions. They isolated the density-dependent contribution from the pole at \(k_0 = i(\mu - E_k)\) that switches sign at the Fermi surface, integrating only over momenta below the Fermi momentum. The calculation was performed in an expansion about low density, with the natural expansion parameter \(p_f^3/(m f_\pi^2)\).

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless correction from single pion exchange at normal nuclear matter density (\(p_f \approx 270\) MeV) is \(2\Sigma_0 \approx 0.04\).
- In the chiral limit, \(\Sigma_0 = \frac{g_A^2}{16\pi^2} \frac{p_f^3}{m f_\pi^2} = \frac{3 g_A^2}{32} \frac{n_{\rm nucl}}{m f_\pi^2}\), where \(n_{\rm nucl} = 2 p_f^3/(3\pi^2)\).
- The expansion parameter for the pion propagator from RPA resummation is \(\approx 2\) at nuclear matter densities, requiring \(p_f \lesssim 70\) MeV for chiral perturbation theory to be valid, corresponding to densities \(\sim 1/64\) of normal nuclear matter.
- The pion decay constant used is \(f_\pi \approx 93\) MeV, and the axial vector coupling constant is \(g_A \approx 1.2\).

## Context

This work extends the calculation of Savage and Wise (2001), who computed baryon mass shifts at zero momentum using chiral perturbation theory and found all masses decrease with density; the present authors instead evaluate the self-energy on the mass shell, where chiral logarithms appear. The results are similar to computations by Horowitz and Serot, by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner, and by Meissner, Oller, and Wirzba. The paper shows that while nucleon self-energy corrections are tiny at nuclear densities, the pion propagator requires RPA resummation, limiting the applicability of chiral perturbation theory for the nuclear equation of state to very low densities, leaving open the possibility of a hadronic phase with small pressure needed for a new class of quark stars.
