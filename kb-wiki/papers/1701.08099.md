# Cooper pairs in the Borromean nuclei $^6$He and $^{11}$Li using continuum single particle level density

> A Borromean nucleus is a bound three-body system which is pairwise unbound because none of the two-body subsystem interactions are strong enough to bind them in pairs. As a consequence, the single-particle spectrum of a neutron in the core of a Borromean nucleus is purely continuum, similarly to the spectrum of a free neutron, but two valence neutrons are bound up in such a core. Most of the usual approaches do not use the true continuum to solve the three-body problem but use a discrete basis, like for example, wave functions in a finite box. In this paper the proper continuum is used to solve the pairing Hamiltonian in the continuum spectrum of energy by using the single particle level density devoid of the free gas. It is shown that the density defined in this way modulates the pairing in the continuum. The partial-wave occupation probabilities for the Borromean nuclei $^6$He and $^{11}$Li are calculated as a function of the pairing strength. While at the threshold strength the $(s_{1/2})^2$ and $(p_{3/2})^2$ configurations are equally important in $^6$He, the $(s_{1/2})^2$ configuration is the main one in $^{11}$Li. For very small strength the $(s_{1/2})^2$ configuration becomes the dominant in both Borromean nuclei. At the physical strength, the calculated wave function amplitudes show a good agreement with other methods and experimental data which indicates that this simple model grasps the essence of the pairing in the continuum.

## Key claim

The paper shows that using the continuum single-particle level density (CSPLD) to solve the pairing Hamiltonian for Borromean nuclei captures the essence of pairing correlations in the continuum, but the model's success depends on the subtraction of the free-gas density to remove the divergent contribution from unbound states.

## Method

The author models each Borromean nucleus as an inert core plus two valence neutrons interacting through a constant pairing force, with the single-particle spectrum consisting entirely of continuum states. The continuum is handled using the CSPLD, defined as the derivative of the partial-wave phase shift with respect to energy, which replaces the discrete box-discretized basis used in most prior approaches. The ground-state energy and partial-wave occupation probabilities are obtained by solving the dispersion relation and probability amplitude equations derived from the pairing Hamiltonian in the continuum representation, with the pairing strength adjusted to reproduce the experimental binding energies.

## Key numbers

- Experimental ground-state energies used to fix the pairing strength: \(E_{Exp} = -0.973\) MeV for \(^6\)He (Tilley 2002) and \(E_{Exp} = -0.369\) MeV for \(^{11}\)Li (Wang 2012).
- Physical pairing strength for \(^6\)He with \(\varepsilon_{max}=9\) MeV: \(G_{ph}^{He} = 1.427\) MeV; for \(^{11}\)Li: \(G_{ph}^{Li} = 0.553\) MeV.
- At the physical strength for \(^6\)He, the \((p_{3/2})^2\) configuration dominates with \(X^2_{p_{3/2}} = 0.8770\), while \((s_{1/2})^2 = 0.0853\).
- At the physical strength for \(^{11}\)Li, the \((p_{1/2})^2\) configuration is \(X^2_{p_{1/2}} = 0.6501\) and \((s_{1/2})^2 = 0.3324\).
- Threshold pairing strength (where the system becomes unbound) for \(^6\)He: \(G_{th} \simeq 0.55\) MeV; for \(^{11}\)Li: \(G_{th} \simeq 0.005\) MeV.
- Energy cutoff \(\varepsilon_{max} = 8.884\) MeV derived from the neutron-neutron effective range \(r_{nn}=2.75\) fm, rounded to 9 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the use of the continuum single-particle level density, previously applied to pairing in Refs. (Id Betan 2012), to the specific Borromean nuclei \(^6\)He and \(^{11}\)Li. It contrasts with approaches that discretize the continuum using a finite box (Esbensen 1997, Hagino 2005, Fortunato 2014) or use scattering wave functions directly (Singh 2016), and it shows that the simple constant pairing interaction modulated by the CSPLD reproduces wave function amplitudes comparable to more elaborate methods such as complex scaling (Myo 2014) and the cluster model (Zhukov 1993). The results enable future studies of how the near-threshold antibound state in \(^{10}\)Li affects pairing correlations and other observables like dipole transitions.
