# Phase transition and properties of compact star

> We investigate the phase transition to a deconfined phase and the consequences in the formation of neutron stars. We use the recently proposed effective field theory motivated relativistic mean field theory for hadron and the MIT Bag model and color-flavor locked (CFL) phase for the quark matter in order to get the appropriate equation of state. The properties of star are then calculated. The differences between unpaired and CFL quark matter are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a mixed phase of hadronic and quark matter can exist in neutron star interiors, but only after adjusting the hadronic model's incompressibility from 215 MeV to 300 MeV and effective mass ratio from 0.664 to 0.7, because the original G2 parameter set of the effective field theory motivated relativistic mean field (E-RMF) model did not yield any mixed phase.

## Method

The authors constructed equations of state (EOS) for neutron star matter by combining an E-RMF hadronic model (including the full baryon octet) with either unpaired quark matter (UQM) described by the MIT bag model or color-flavor locked (CFL) quark matter. They applied Gibbs criteria for phase equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions to build a mixed-phase EOS at zero temperature, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to compute stellar properties.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass for E-RMF+UQM: 1.44 solar masses, with radius 9.35 km
- Maximum mass for E-RMF+CFL: 1.35 solar masses, with radius 9.33 km
- Central energy density for maximum mass star: 31.0 x 10^14 g cm^-3 (UQM) and 32.0 x 10^14 g cm^-3 (CFL)
- Baryonic mass: 1.62 solar masses (UQM) and 1.51 solar masses (CFL)
- Gravitational redshift: 0.35 (UQM) and 0.32 (CFL)
- Bag constant used: B^(1/4) = 170 MeV for UQM, B^(1/4) = 188 MeV for CFL
- Gap parameter for CFL phase: Delta = 100 MeV
- Quark masses: m_u = m_d = 5.0 MeV, m_s = 150 MeV
- Hadronic model parameters: incompressibility K = 300 MeV, effective mass M*/M = 0.7

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hybrid neutron stars by using an E-RMF hadronic model (Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang, 1996-1997) that includes nonlinear scalar-vector and vector-vector interactions, combined with both unpaired and color-flavor locked quark matter phases. The results are compared to observational constraints from the X-ray binary EXO0748-676 (Cottam et al., 2002) which gave a redshift of 0.35, and from the neutron star 1E 1207.4-5209 (Sanwal et al., 2002). The calculated mass-to-radius ratios and redshifts agree with these observations, and the maximum masses are consistent with other field theoretical models.
