# Big Bang Nucleosynthesis results refined via the Trojan Horse Method

> This work presents the Trojan Horse Method (THM) as a powerful technique for measuring nuclear reaction cross sections at astrophysical energies. We then explore the impact of THM-derived reaction rates on the predictions of Standard Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (SBBN) using the PRIMAT code. Primordial abundances are shown for the single rate impact and, for the first time, also for all the THM rates together. The result shows significant differences with the use of THM rates, which in some cases goes in the direction of improving the agreement with the observations with respect to the use of only reaction rates from direct data, especially for the $^7$Li and deuterium abundances, which are still open issues for SBBN.

## Key claim

The paper claims that using Trojan Horse Method (THM) reaction rates for six key reactions in Standard Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (SBBN) calculations, both individually and collectively, produces primordial abundances that show "significant differences" compared to using only direct reaction rates, with some changes "going in the direction of improving the agreement with the observations", but the authors hedge that this improvement is partial and that further measurements are needed to confirm whether THM rates can resolve the deuterium tension and the Cosmological Lithium Problem.

## Method

The authors used the PRIMAT code (version 0.2.2) to compute primordial abundances from the twelve-reaction SBBN network, replacing direct reaction rates one-by-one with THM-derived rates for six reactions: ²H(d,n)³He, ²H(d,p)³H, ³He(n,p)³H, ³He(d,p)⁴He, ⁷Li(p,α)⁴He, and ⁷Be(n,p)⁷Li. They then performed a combined calculation replacing all six rates simultaneously, using Monte Carlo sampling with lognormal probability density functions to estimate uncertainties as 15% and 86% quantiles.

## Key numbers

- Combined THM rates yield Y_p = 0.24699^{+0.00005}_{-0.00006} for ⁴He mass fraction, compared to the observed value Y_p = 0.2453 ± 0.0034 from Aver
- THM ²H(d,n)³He and ²H(d,p)³H rates raise D/H × 10⁵ to 2.61 ± 0.13, a 7.7% increase over the direct-rate result
- Combined THM rates give (⁷Li+⁷Be)/H × 10¹⁰ = 4.78, a 13% reduction from the direct-rate abundance
- THM ³He(n,p)³H rate alone gives (³He+T)/H × 10⁵ = 0.97 ± 0.10; THM ³He(d,p)⁴He gives 1.14 ± 0.22; combined THM rates give 1.06 ± 0.11
- ⁶Li/H × 10¹⁴ = 1.27373 with all THM rates (PRIMAT direct: 1.18269; Coc2012 reference: 1.23)
- ⁹Be/H × 10¹⁹ = 11.92813 with all THM rates (PRIMAT direct: 8.78250; Coc2012 reference: 9.60)
- ¹¹B/H × 10¹⁶ = 2.56751 with all THM rates (PRIMAT direct: 3.25468; Coc2012 reference: 3.05)
- CNO/H × 10¹⁶ = 7.69346 with all THM rates (PRIMAT direct: 7.82870; Coc2012 reference: 7.43)

## Context

This work extends previous THM applications to BBN by Spartà (2021), which used only four THM rates, and by Pizzone (2023) and Hayakawa (2021), which used earlier PRIMAT versions with single THM rates. The paper addresses the Cosmological Lithium Problem (CLiP), the factor-of-three discrepancy between predicted and observed ⁷Li abundances, and the recent deuterium tension raised by Mossa (2020), who found D/H = (2.439 ± 0.037) × 10⁵ with a 1.8σ difference from CMB expectations. The results enable future inclusion of additional THM measurements (such as ⁷Be(n,α)⁴He and ³H(d,n)⁴He) and motivate more precise rate determinations to test whether THM can resolve these long-standing SBBN discrepancies.
