# Massive Meson Fluctuation in NJL Model

> Based on the self-consistent scheme beyond mean-field approximation in the large $N_c$ expansion, including current quark mass explicitly, a general scheme of SU(2) NJL model is developed. To ensure the quark self-energy expanded in the proper order of $N_c$, an approximate internal meson propagator is deduced, which is in order of $O(1/N_c)$. In our scheme, adopting the method of external momentum expansion, all the Feynman diagrams are calculated in a unified way by only expanding the quark propagator. Our numerical results show, that different from the mean field approximation in which the explicitly chiral symmetry breaking is invisible, the effect of finite pion mass can be seen clearly when beyond mean-field approximation.

## Key claim

The paper develops a self-consistent scheme beyond mean-field approximation in the SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model that includes explicit chiral symmetry breaking via a finite current quark mass. The key advance is that, unlike the mean-field approximation where the effect of finite pion mass is invisible, the beyond-mean-field treatment reveals the explicit chiral symmetry breaking effect through the separation of curves for massive and massless quark cases, but only when the internal meson propagator is truncated to leading order \(O(1/N_c)\) to maintain proper ordering in the large-\(N_c\) expansion.

## Method

The authors extend the self-consistent scheme of Dmitrašinović et al. (1995) to include explicit chiral symmetry breaking in the SU(2) NJL model. They use the large-\(N_c\) expansion to organize the quark self-energy and meson polarization function into leading and subleading orders, and derive an approximate internal meson propagator of order \(O(1/N_c)\) by neglecting subleading corrections to the RPA propagator. All Feynman diagrams are calculated using external momentum expansion of the quark propagator only, and divergent integrals are regulated with Pauli-Villars regularization for quark loops and a covariant cutoff \(\Lambda_b\) for meson loops.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass fixed to \(m_{\pi}=139\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and pion decay constant to \(f_{\pi}=92.4\ \mathrm{MeV}\) as input observables.
* Quark condensate constrained to the empirical range \(-300\ \mathrm{MeV} < \frac{1}{2}\langle\bar{q}q\rangle^{1/3} < -200\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* For \(z = \Lambda_b/\Lambda_f = 1.5\) in the plateau region, the constituent quark mass range is \(m = 0.47 \pm 0.1\ \mathrm{GeV}\), current quark mass \(m_0 = 8.9 \pm 0.30\ \mathrm{MeV}\), quark cutoff \(\Lambda_f = 0.615 \pm 0.002\ \mathrm{GeV}\), and dimensionless coupling \(G\Lambda_f^2 = 4.8 \pm 0.9\).
* For \(z = 1\) in the plateau region, the constituent quark mass range is \(m = 0.39 \pm 0.05\ \mathrm{GeV}\), current quark mass \(m_0 = 7.78 \pm 0.20\ \mathrm{MeV}\), quark cutoff \(\Lambda_f = 0.71 \pm 0.01\ \mathrm{GeV}\), and \(G\Lambda_f^2 = 4.7 \pm 0.5\).
* Mesonic corrections to quark self-energy and pion polarization reach 30–40% for \(m\) in the range \(0.3\ \mathrm{GeV} < m < 0.5\ \mathrm{GeV}\) and \(1 < z < 1.5\).

## Context

This work extends the chirally symmetric self-consistent approximation of Dmitrašinović et al. (1995) and the effective action method of Kleinert and Van den Bossche (1995) to include explicit chiral symmetry breaking via a finite current quark mass. The scheme enables systematic calculation of meson cloud effects on quark properties and pion observables beyond the mean-field (Hartree plus RPA) approximation, and provides a framework for computing pion-pion scattering lengths that could be tested by the DIRAC experiment.
