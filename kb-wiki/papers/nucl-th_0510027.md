# Understanding the rapidity dependence of the elliptic flow and the HBT radii at RHIC

> The pseudo-rapidity dependence of the elliptic flow at various excitation energies measured by the PHOBOS Collaboration in Au+Au collisions at RHIC is one of the surprising results that has not been explained before in terms of hydrodynamical models. Here we show that these data are in agreement with theoretical predictions and satisfy the universal scaling relation predicted by the Buda-Lund hydrodynamical model, based on exact solutions of perfect fluid hydrodynamics. We also show a theoretical prediction on the rapidity and transverse momentum scaling of the HBT radii measured in heavy ion collisions, based on the Buda-Lund model.

# Understanding the rapidity dependence of the elliptic flow and the HBT radii at RHIC

## Key claim
The PHOBOS data on the pseudorapidity dependence of elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, which had resisted description by hydrodynamical models, are shown to satisfy the universal scaling relation predicted by the Buda-Lund hydrodynamical model based on exact solutions of perfect fluid hydrodynamics, provided that the fit parameters (space-time eccentricity and longitudinal elongation) are extracted from the data and the remaining parameters are fixed to values that do not influence the fit quality.

## Method
The authors used the ellipsoidally symmetric generalization of the Buda-Lund hydrodynamical model, which describes an expanding ellipsoid with principal axes X, Y, Z and their expansion rates. They performed a saddle point integration in transverse momentum and a change of variables to pseudorapidity to evaluate single-particle invariant spectra, then calculated v2(eta) from this distribution, matching the PHOBOS measurement procedure. The fit parameters were space-time eccentricity (epsilon) and longitudinal elongation (Delta eta), while central freeze-out temperature T0, temperature gradient parameter a, and average transverse flow u_t were fixed at T0=175 MeV, a=1.19, and u_t=1.64.

## Key numbers
- Space-time eccentricity (epsilon) extracted from PHOBOS data increases with collision energy: 0.294 +/- 0.029 at sqrt(s_NN)=19.6 GeV, 0.349 +/- 0.008 at 62.4 GeV, 0.376 +/- 0.005 at 130 GeV, and 0.394 +/- 0.006 at 200 GeV.
- Longitudinal elongation (Delta eta) extracted from PHOBOS data increases with collision energy: 1.70 +/- 0.25 at sqrt(s_NN)=19.6 GeV, 2.16 +/- 0.05 at 62.4 GeV, 2.46 +/- 0.04 at 130 GeV, and 2.56 +/- 0.04 at 200 GeV.
- Fit quality (chi^2/NDF) values: 1.84/11 at 19.6 GeV, 20.1/13 at 62.4 GeV, 34.8/15 at 130 GeV, and 27.5/15 at 200 GeV.
- Fixed parameters: T0=175 MeV, a=1.19, u_t=1.64.

## Context
This work extends the Buda-Lund hydrodynamical model, previously successful in describing BRAHMS, PHENIX, PHOBOS, and STAR data on single particle spectra and HBT radii at sqrt(s_NN)=130 GeV (Ster, 2003) and 200 GeV (Csanad, 2003), to the pseudorapidity dependence of elliptic flow that had resisted description by other hydrodynamical models. The results provide quantitative evidence for perfect fluid behavior extending far from mid-rapidity in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, and the authors predict a universal scaling of HBT radii with the scaling variable mbar_t that could be tested by future measurements of rapidity and transverse momentum dependent HBT radii.
