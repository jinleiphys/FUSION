# pp <-> pi+ d process at low energy: Interplay between s-- and p-wave mechanisms

> The large variety of experimental data around the pion-production threshold are compared with a meson-exchange isobar model which includes the pion-nucleon interaction in s-- and p-waves. Theoretical results obtained with two different NN potentials (Bonn and Paris) indicate that the behavior of the excitation function at threshold is sensitive to the details of the NN correlations. The complete model presented, while developed originally to reproduce the reaction around the Delta resonance, is shown to describe well the integral (Coulomb-corrected) cross-section at threshold along with its angular distribution. At low energies the angular dependence of the analyzing power Ay0 is well reproduced also. Finally, the energy dependence of the analyzing power for theta=90 from threshold up to the Delta resonance is considered and discussed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a meson-exchange isobar model originally developed to describe the $pp \leftrightarrow \pi^+ d$ reaction around the Delta resonance can also reproduce the integral cross-section, angular distributions, and analyzing power $A_{y0}$ at low energies near threshold, but only if both s-wave and p-wave pion-nucleon mechanisms are included; the model does not include heavy-meson exchange currents or off-shell isoscalar piN effects, and the results are sensitive to which NN potential (Bonn or Paris) is used.

## Method

The authors calculate the $pp \leftrightarrow \pi^+ d$ amplitude using a distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) framework with a meson-exchange isobar model. The s-wave mechanism is based on a piN rescattering process described by a K-matrix with isoscalar and isovector (rho-exchange) components, including unitary effects via the Heitler equation. The p-wave mechanism is dominated by Delta-rescattering via pi- and rho-exchange transition potentials, with the Delta resonance width included. The NN initial and final state interactions are treated with either the Bonn B or Paris potential, and all meson-baryon vertices include monopole form factors with a single adjustable cutoff parameter $\Lambda_B$ set to reproduce the cross-section at the resonance peak.

## Key numbers

- The parameter $\alpha$ in the expansion $\sigma(\eta)=\alpha\eta+\beta\eta^3$ is reduced by 40% when switching from the Bonn to the Paris potential, while $\beta$ increases by 30%.
- By $\eta=0.4$ (where $\eta$ is the pion center-of-mass momentum in units of pion masses), the p-wave contribution amounts to roughly 50% of the total cross section.
- The cutoff parameter $\Lambda_B$ for the piNN and piNDelta vertices is set to approximately 0.7 GeV to reproduce the cross-section at the resonance peak.
- The angular distributions are compared with data for $\eta$ values ranging from 0.062 up to 0.634.
- The analyzing power $A_{y0}$ is shown at $\eta=0.15$ and $\eta=0.21$ for comparison with data.
- Coulomb distortion effects on $A_{y0}(90^\circ)$ are estimated to be of order $|\delta A_{y0}(90)|=0.04$ at $\ln(\eta)\simeq -1.4$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of the $pp \leftrightarrow \pi^+ d$ reaction around the Delta resonance (Canton 1996, Dortmans 1997) down to threshold energies, and is motivated by the debate over the missing strength in near-threshold pion production seen in the $pp\rightarrow \pi^0 pp$ reaction (Mayer 1990-1992, Bondar 1996). The paper directly addresses the interplay between s-wave mechanisms (dominated by isovector piN rescattering via rho-exchange) and p-wave mechanisms (dominated by Delta-rescattering), which had been treated separately in prior work by Horowitz (1993), Niskanen (1996), and Hanhart (1995, 1997). The model enables extension to few-nucleon systems such as $pd\rightarrow \pi^+ t$ in the energy range from threshold to the Delta resonance, as already attempted by Canton (1997).
