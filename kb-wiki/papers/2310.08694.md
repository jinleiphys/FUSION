# The intermediate neutron capture process: IV. Impact of nuclear model and parameter uncertainties

> We investigate both the systematic and statistical uncertainties associated with theoretical nuclear reaction rates of relevance during the i-process and explore their impact on the i-process elemental production, and subsequently on the surface enrichment, for a low-mass low-metallicity star during the early AGB phase. We use the TALYS reaction code (Koning et al. 2023) to estimate both the model and parameter uncertainties affecting the photon strength function and the nuclear level densities, hence the radiative neutron capture rates. The STAREVOL code (Siess et al. 2006) is used to determine the impact of nuclear uncertainties on the i-process nucleosynthesis in a 1 $M_{\odot}$ [Fe/H] = - 2.5 model star during the proton ingestion event in the early AGB phase. A large nuclear network of 1160 species coherently coupled to the transport processes is solved to follow the i-process nucleosynthesis. We find that the non-correlated parameter uncertainties lead the surface abundances uncertainties of element with $Z\geq 40$ to range between 0.5 and 1.0 dex, with odd-$Z$ elements displaying higher uncertainties. The correlated model uncertainties are of the same order of magnitude, and both model and parameter uncertainties have an important impact on potential observable tracers such as Eu and La. Both the correlated model and uncorrelated parameter uncertainties need to be estimated coherently before being propagated to astrophysical observables through multi-zone stellar evolution models. Many reactions are found to affect the i-process predictions and will require improved nuclear models guided by experimental constraints. Priority should be given to the reactions influencing the observable tracers.

## Key claim

The paper finds that both correlated model uncertainties (from different choices of nuclear model for the photon strength function and nuclear level density) and uncorrelated parameter uncertainties (from random variations within a given model) produce surface abundance uncertainties of similar magnitude for i-process elements with Z >= 40, typically ranging between 0.5 and 1.0 dex, with odd-Z elements showing larger uncertainties.

## Method

The authors used the TALYS reaction code (Koning et al. 2023) to generate radiative neutron capture rates for the i-process, varying both the nuclear model (systematic uncertainty, set A) and the model parameters (statistical uncertainty, set B). These rates were then propagated through the STAREVOL stellar evolution code (Siess et al. 2006) for a 1 solar mass, [Fe/H] = -2.5 model star during the early AGB phase, solving a network of 1160 nuclear species coupled to transport processes to follow the i-process nucleosynthesis.

## Key numbers

- Surface abundance uncertainties for elements with Z >= 40 range between 0.5 and 1.0 dex for non-correlated parameter uncertainties
- The largest surface abundance uncertainty found is for Uranium (Z=92): 2.90 dex (set A) and 1.37 dex (set B) for U-235, driven by the Bi-217(n,gamma) reaction with rate uncertainty factors of 57.2 (set A) and 10.0 (set B)
- Thorium-232 shows surface abundance uncertainty of 2.75 dex (set A) and 1.36 dex (set B), also driven by Bi-217(n,gamma)
- Europium-153 surface abundance uncertainty is 1.15 dex (set A) and 0.88 dex (set B), driven by Sm-153(n,gamma) with rate uncertainty factors of 12.5 (set A) and 5.5 (set B)
- Barium-137 surface abundance uncertainty is 1.34 dex (set A) and 1.95 dex (set B), driven by Cs-137(n,gamma) with rate uncertainty factors of 15.4 (set A) and 78.4 (set B)
- Lanthanum-139 surface abundance uncertainty is 0.59 dex (set A) and 0.77 dex (set B), driven by Ba-139(n,gamma) with rate uncertainty factors of 10.3 (set A) and 9.0 (set B)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the intermediate neutron capture process (i-process) by quantifying for the first time both systematic and statistical nuclear uncertainties in a coherent framework, using the TALYS code rather than relying on a single nuclear model. The results identify specific neutron capture reactions that most strongly affect observable tracers in carbon-enhanced metal-poor (CEMP) stars, such as Eu and La, providing guidance for future experimental nuclear physics measurements. The authors recommend that priority be given to improving the nuclear models for reactions influencing these observable tracers, particularly those involving unstable nuclei far from stability where experimental constraints are most needed.
