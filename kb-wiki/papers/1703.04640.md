# The many-nucleon theory of nuclear collective structure and its macroscopic limits: an algebraic perspective

> The nuclear collective models introduced by Bohr, Mottelson and Rainwater, together with the Mayer-Jensen shell model, have provided the central framework for the development of nuclear physics. This paper reviews the microscopic evolution of the collective models and their underlying foundations. In particular, it is shown that the Bohr-Mottelson models have expressions as macroscopic limits of microscopic models that have precisely-defined expressions in many-nucleon quantum mechanics. Understanding collective models in this way is especially useful because it enables the analysis of nuclear properties in terms of them to be revisited and reassessed in the light of their microscopic foundations.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how the Bohr-Mottelson collective models can be understood as macroscopic limits of microscopic many-nucleon models, specifically the symplectic shell model, and argues that this perspective allows nuclear properties to be reassessed in light of their microscopic foundations, though the macroscopic limits are approached only to an extraordinarily high level of accuracy in medium-to-heavy nuclei, not universally.

## Method

The authors construct a microscopic theory of nuclear collective dynamics by identifying the essential observables (quadrupole moments, angular momenta, and kinetic energy) and expressing them as elements of the Sp(3,R) Lie algebra, which is the smallest Lie algebra containing both the nuclear quadrupole moments and the kinetic energy. They then show how the Bohr model emerges as a hydrodynamic limit of this algebra by contracting the commutation relations when the monopole moment is treated as nearly constant, and how the unified model with vorticity arises by including angular momentum operators to form the GCM(3) algebra. The symplectic model is then presented as a bridge between the shell model and the collective model, with the many-nucleon Hilbert space expressed as a sum of symplectic-model spaces carrying representations of the Sp(3,R) group.

## Key numbers

* The monopole and quadrupole observables in the contracted Bohr model satisfy commutation relations \([\hat q_0, \hat p_0] = i\hbar \hat I + O(\epsilon^2)\) and \([\hat q_{2\mu}, \hat p^{2\nu}] = i\hbar \delta_\mu^\nu + O(\epsilon)\), where \(\epsilon\) is a small parameter defined such that \(4\epsilon^2 = 1/\langle \hat Q_0\rangle\) with \(\langle \hat Q_0\rangle\) the mean monopole moment.
* The Sp(3,R) Lie algebra is spanned by operators \(\hat Q_{ij} = \sum_{n=1}^A \hat x_{ni} \hat x_{nj}\), \(\hat K_{ij} = \sum_{n=1}^A \hat p_{ni} \hat p_{nj}\), \(\hbar \hat L_{ij} = \sum_{n=1}^A (\hat x_{ni}\hat p_{nj} - \hat x_{nj}\hat p_{ni})\), and \(\hat P_{ij} = \sum_{n=1}^A (\hat x_{ni} \hat p_{nj} + \hat p_{ni} \hat x_{nj})\).
* The giant-resonance raising and lowering operators \(\hat{\mathcal{A}}_{ij}\) and \(\hat{\mathcal{B}}_{ij}\) carry \(2\hbar\omega\) excitations, with the monopole and quadrupole giant-resonance operators expressed as spherical tensors with normalization factors such as \(\sqrt{1/6}\) for \(\hat{\mathcal{A}}_0\) and \(\sqrt{1/12}\) for \(\hat{\mathcal{A}}_{2,0}\).

## Context

This review extends the earlier work by Tomonaga (1955), Miyazima and Tamura (1956), and Elliott (1958) on microscopic collective models, and it builds on the algebraic rotor model of Ui (1970) and the GCM(3) model of Weaver, Biedenharn, and Cusson (1973, 1976). The paper shows that the symplectic model, developed by Rosensteel and Rowe (1977, 1980) and Rowe (1985), provides a formally precise many-nucleon theory that contains both the Bohr-Mottelson unified model and the SU(3) model as submodels, enabling future work to analyze low-energy beta- and gamma-vibrational states of rotational nuclei from a microscopic perspective.
