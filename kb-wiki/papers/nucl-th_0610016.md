# Search for missing baryon resonances via associated strangeness photoproduction

> Differential cross-section and single polarization observables in the process gamma p --> K^+ Lambda are investigated within a constituent quark model and a dynamical coupled-channel formalism. The effects of two new nucleon resonances and of the K*(892)- and K1(1270)-exchanges are briefly presented.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether the effects of two new nucleon resonances (a third $S_{11}$ and a $D_{13}$) in the reaction $\gamma p \rightarrow K^+ \Lambda$ can be distinguished from the effects of $t$-channel $K^*(892)$ and $K1(1270)$ exchanges, finding that the new resonances produce features not mimicked by the $t$-channel contributions, thereby supporting the duality hypothesis within the model's approximations.

## Method

The authors used a chiral constituent quark model for the direct $\gamma p \rightarrow K^+ \Lambda$ channel, combined with a dynamical coupled-channel formalism for the $\pi N \rightarrow \pi N$, $\pi N \rightarrow K Y$, and $K Y \rightarrow K Y$ reactions. They performed three separate minimizations to a fitted data base of 1029 data points: a full model including all known resonances plus $t$-channel $K^*$ and $K1$ exchanges plus two new resonances; a configuration with the $t$-channel exchanges switched off; and a configuration with the two new resonances switched off.

## Key numbers

* The fitted data base contains 1029 data points from SAPHIR, JLab, and GRAAL, spanning $E_{\gamma}^{lab}$ from 0.912 GeV to 2.575 GeV and center-of-mass energy W from 1.6 GeV to 2.4 GeV.
* The full model yields a $\chi^2_{d.o.f}$ of 1.83; switching off the $t$-channel $K^*$ and $K1$ exchanges increases $\chi^2_{d.o.f}$ to 3.48; switching off the new resonances increases $\chi^2_{d.o.f}$ to 5.25.
* The extracted mass and width for the new $S_{11}$ resonance are $M = 1.820$ GeV and $\Gamma = 240$ MeV.
* The extracted mass and width for the new $D_{13}$ resonance are $M = 1.920$ GeV and $\Gamma = 160$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous coupled-channel formalism (Saghai et al., 2006) by explicitly including $t$-channel $K^*(892)$ and $K1(1270)$ exchanges, which were previously treated only via a background approximation based on the duality hypothesis. The results enable future studies to disentangle $s$-channel resonance contributions from $t$-channel effects in the higher-energy region (W $\ge$ 2.4 GeV), and the extracted resonance parameters are compatible with findings from other works (Saghai et al., 2006; Mart, 2006).
