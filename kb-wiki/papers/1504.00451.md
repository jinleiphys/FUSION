# Exotic Phases in Magnetars

> Neutron stars feature extremely high magnetic fields with deduced field strengths of $10^{15}$ G in the case of magnetars and potentially much higher values inside of the star. In this context we consider the appearance of $ρ^-$ meson condensation taking into account the effect of the magnetic field. The results show that, depending on parameters, such a condensation in magnetized neutron stars might (just) occur.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether \(\rho^-\) meson condensation can occur in magnetar cores under the combined influence of high magnetic fields and dense matter, finding that such condensation might (just) occur depending on the choice of a free coupling parameter \(g\) and only for magnetic field strengths at the upper end of hypothetically possible values (up to \(7 \times 10^{18}\) G).

## Method

The authors adopt a standard relativistic mean-field (RMF) model (GM3 parameterization) for neutron star matter, including magnetic field effects by summing over nucleonic and leptonic Landau levels. They compute the effective energy of the \(\rho^-\) meson, including a field-dependent term coupling the rho meson to the scalar \(\sigma\) field, and compare it to the electron Fermi energy to determine the critical density for condensation onset. The nucleon-\(\rho\) coupling \(g_{N\rho}\) is readjusted for each choice of the free parameter \(g\) to keep the symmetry energy fixed at \(a_{sym} = 32.5\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strength considered: \(B = 7 \times 10^{18}\) G, at the upper end of hypothetically possible values for the center of neutron stars.
- Critical density for \(\rho^-\) condensation onset: approximately \(4.4 \times \rho_0\) (where \(\rho_0\) is nuclear saturation density) for coupling parameter \(g = -6\).
- Maximum neutron star mass without condensation: \(M_{max} = 2.13\,M_\odot\).
- Condensation onset at about \(5 \times \rho_0\) corresponds to an increase of 20 MeV in the slope parameter \(L\) compared to the original model.
- Surface magnetic fields of magnetars: of the order of \(10^{15}\) G.
- Maximum core magnetic field estimates from virial theorem and 2-D calculations: several times \(10^{18}\) G to perhaps \(10^{19}\) G.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of kaon condensation in neutron stars (Kaplan, year not given) and vector meson condensation in strong magnetic fields proposed by Schramm (year not given) and Chernodub (year not given). It also builds on earlier investigations of \(\rho\) meson condensation in neutron stars without magnetic fields by Voskresensky (year not given) and Kolomeitsev (year not given). The results suggest that \(\rho^-\) condensation might occur in highly magnetized stars, potentially softening the equation of state and limiting stable star masses, which could be relevant for interpreting observations of two-solar-mass neutron stars (Demorest, year not given; Antonidis, year not given). Future work could explore consequences for neutron star cooling and the role of different model parameterizations.
