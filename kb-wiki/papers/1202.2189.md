# Scaling quark gluon plasma by HBT interferometry with lepton pairs

> We study the intensity interferometry with lepton pairs for nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. It is argued that the invariant mass dependence of HBT radii extracted from the correlation functions of dilepton pairs can be used as an efficient tool to scale the size and life time of the quark gluon plasma expected to be formed in nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC. Quantitatively different magnitudes of HBT radii are obtained at RHIC and LHC indicating stronger radial flow at LHC.

## Key claim

The invariant mass dependence of HBT radii extracted from dilepton-pair correlation functions can be used as an efficient tool to scale the size and lifetime of the quark gluon plasma formed in nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC, with quantitatively different magnitudes of HBT radii obtained at the two energies indicating stronger radial flow at LHC.

## Method

The authors computed the Bose-Einstein correlation function for two identical lepton pairs using a relativistic hydrodynamic model with cylindrical symmetry and boost invariance along the longitudinal direction, with initial conditions constrained by hadronic multiplicity. The correlation functions were evaluated for different average invariant mass windows (⟨M⟩ = 0.3, 0.5, 0.7, 1.2, 1.6, and 2.5 GeV) for 0-5% central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, and the HBT radii Rside and Rout were extracted by fitting the correlation functions to a Gaussian form.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature Ti = 290 MeV and thermalization time τi = 0.6 fm for RHIC; Ti = 640 MeV and τi = 0.1 fm for LHC
- Critical temperature Tc = 175 MeV, chemical freeze-out temperature Tch = 170 MeV, and kinetic freeze-out temperature Tfo = 120 MeV for both RHIC and LHC
- For LHC with ⟨M⟩ = 500 MeV and kT = 1 GeV, the differential number of pairs per event is 0.138 × 10^(-3), yielding approximately 2.7 × 10^6 total pairs from 2 × 10^10 events
- For LHC with ⟨M⟩ = 1.02 GeV and kT = 1 GeV, the probability to get two pairs per event is 10^(-8), requiring roughly 10^8 events for interferometry

## Context

This work extends the use of electromagnetic probes as thermometers and flowmeters (as reviewed in the electromagnetic probes literature) to intensity interferometry with lepton pairs, building on prior HBT interferometry studies with hadrons (HBT review by Heinz and collaborators). The approach enables scaling the size and lifetime of QGP across different collision energies by using the invariant mass dependence of HBT radii, which the authors argue can serve as a clock for heavy ion collisions since large M corresponds to early times and small M (~mρ) correlates to late times.
