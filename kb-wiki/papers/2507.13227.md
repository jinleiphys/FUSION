# Effect of Dark matter and $σ$-cut potential on radial and non-radial oscillation modes in neutron stars

> We study the mesonic nonlinear (NL) interaction equation of state (EoS) employing the relativistic mean-field model and investigate the effect of $σ$-cut potential (NL-$σ$ cut) and dark matter (NL DM) on the non-radial and radial oscillation modes of neutron stars. For NL-$σ$ cut, we include the $σ$-cut potential $U_{cut} (σ)$ to study its effect. For the dark matter, we use the neutron decay anomaly model. For each model, we investigate two extreme EoSs, stiff and soft, that cover the entire allowed parameter range from the given model, consistent with the current astrophysical constraints. The EoS and the stellar properties, such as mass and radius, are calculated, and the effect of $σ$-cut and DM is discussed. Both non-radial and radial oscillation modes are computed in the general relativistic framework. We study the non-radial $f$ and $p_1$ mode frequency, damping time, and some qusi-universal relations connecting the frequencies of the $f$-mode to the average density and compactness. The analysis showed that the $f$ and $p_1$ mode frequencies at both 1.4~$M_{\odot}$ and the maximum mass configuration are higher in the NL DM model compared to the NL and NL-$σ$ models. The consistent alignment between our prior parameterizations and current calculations strongly supports the existence of quasi-universal relations that hold true irrespective of the particular matter components involved. For the radial oscillations, we compute 10 lowest-order modes ($f$, $p$), study the radial perturbations as well as the large frequency separation with NL-$σ$ cut and NL DM EoS, showing that the microphysics involved in the NS EoS is imprinted on the frequency separation between different nodes.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how adding a σ-cut potential (which stiffens the equation of state at high densities) or dark matter (via a neutron decay anomaly model) modifies both radial and non-radial oscillation modes of neutron stars, finding that the f-mode and p1-mode frequencies are higher in the dark matter model compared to the standard nonlinear and σ-cut models, while quasi-universal relations connecting mode frequencies to stellar compactness remain valid regardless of the microphysical composition.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic mean-field (RMF) framework with nonlinear mesonic interactions (NL model), then separately add either a logarithmic σ-cut potential (NL-σ cut model) or a dark matter component via the neutron decay channel n→χ+φ with fermionic dark matter self-interactions (NL DM model). For each model, they select two extreme parameter sets (stiff and soft) that satisfy current astrophysical constraints from pulsar masses, NICER, and GW170817. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for stellar structure, then compute non-radial f and p1 modes using full general relativistic perturbation theory (solving the coupled linearized Einstein equations) and radial modes by solving the coupled differential equations for displacement and pressure perturbation.

## Key numbers

- For the NL DM model, the dark matter self-interaction strength Gχ is set to 420 fm² (soft) and 767 fm² (stiff), with a dark matter particle mass mχ = 938.0 MeV
- The σ-cut potential uses parameters α = mπ⁴ and β = 120, with fs = 0.57 (soft) and fs = 0.50 (stiff)
- The f-mode and p1-mode frequencies at both 1.4 M⊙ and the maximum mass configuration are higher in the NL DM model compared to the NL and NL-σ models
- The neutron decay anomaly involves two distinct neutron lifetime measurements: τbottle = 879.6 ± 0.6 s and τbeam = 888.0 ± 2.0 s, differing by 4σ
- The dark matter fraction constraints from prior work include fχ* = 1.6 ± 0.4% near PSR J0348+0432 and fχ* = 1.35 ± 0.35% around PSR J0740+6620, with an upper limit of fχ ≤ 20%
- The lower limit on dark matter self-interaction strength is Gχ > 1.6 fm² from the tidal deformability constraint Λ1.4M⊙ > 70, with an upper bound of Gχ ≤ 943 fm² from galactic core-cusp constraints

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Bayesian analysis (Thakur 2024) that compared NL, NL-σ cut, and NL DM models against nuclear and astrophysical constraints, where the NL-σ cut model showed the highest Bayesian evidence excluding PREX-II data. It builds on the σ-cut potential technique introduced by Maslov (2015) for stiffening the equation of state above twice saturation density, and on the neutron decay anomaly dark matter model developed by Motta (2018) and Shirke (2023). The study enables future gravitational wave detections by third-generation detectors like Cosmic Explorer and Einstein Telescope to discriminate between competing microphysical scenarios through observed oscillation frequencies, and confirms that quasi-universal relations connecting f-mode frequency to average density and compactness (Andersson 1997, Lau 2009) remain robust even with exotic matter components.
