# Nuclear Matter on a Lattice

> We investigate nuclear matter on a cubic lattice. An exact thermal formalism is applied to nucleons with a Hamiltonian that accommodates on-site and next-neighbor parts of the central, spin- and isospin-exchange interactions. We describe the nuclear matter Monte Carlo methods which contain elements from shell model Monte Carlo methods and from numerical simulations of the Hubbard model. We show that energy and basic saturation properties of nuclear matter can be reproduced. Evidence of a first-order phase transition from an uncorrelated Fermi gas to a clustered system is observed by computing mechanical and thermodynamical quantities such as compressibility, heat capacity, entropy and grand potential. We compare symmetry energy and first sound velocities with literature and find reasonable agreement.

# Nuclear Matter on a Lattice

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a lattice-based, exact thermal Monte Carlo method using a simplified Hamiltonian with on-site attraction and next-neighbor repulsion can reproduce the saturation energy and density of symmetric nuclear matter, but only under the condition that the potential parameters are chosen to avoid the sign problem, which forces an on-site attraction that contradicts the usual short-range repulsion of realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials.

## Method

The authors discretize three-dimensional space onto a cubic lattice with spacing 1.842 fm and apply a Hubbard-Stratonovitch transformation to reduce the many-body grand canonical partition function to an integral over auxiliary fields, which is then evaluated using the Metropolis algorithm. The Hamiltonian includes kinetic energy and a Skyrme-like potential with on-site and next-neighbor central and spin-exchange interactions, with parameters fitted to reproduce nuclear matter saturation properties. Calculations are performed on a 4x4x4 lattice with 11520 auxiliary fields, using a Trotter decomposition with time step 0.01 MeV^{-1} over a temperature range of 3.0 to 100 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 1.842 fm, tuned so that quarter-filling corresponds to saturation density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Half-filling of the lattice occurs at rho = 2 rho_0 = 0.32 fm^{-3}
- Central potential parameters: V_c^{(0)} = -181.5 MeV fm^3 (on-site attraction), V_c^{(2)} = 37.8 MeV fm^5 (next-neighbor repulsion)
- Spin-exchange parameters: V_sigma^{(0)} = -31.25 MeV fm^3, V_sigma^{(2)} = 0.0 MeV fm^5
- Critical temperature for the first-order phase transition: T_c ~ 15 MeV
- Temperature range of the investigation: 3.0 MeV <= T <= 100 MeV
- Energy of the full lattice state: E/A = 5.96 MeV
- The system shows a maximum in compressibility at T ~ 14 MeV

## Context

This work extends the shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) methods developed by Johnson, Gladysz-Dziadus, Ormand, Alhassid, and others to infinite nuclear matter in coordinate space, combining elements from numerical simulations of the Hubbard model in condensed matter physics. It contrasts with classical lattice gas calculations by Kuo, Campi, and Pan that treat kinetic terms by hand or via Maxwell-Boltzmann sampling, and with ground-state Bethe-Brueckner-Goldstone calculations using realistic potentials such as Argonne AV14 and AV18. The approach enables the first exact thermal treatment of nuclear matter on a lattice with a Hamiltonian that includes both kinetic and potential terms, allowing direct observation of clustering and the liquid-gas phase transition, though the authors note that a full treatment with realistic potentials like AV18 remains computationally infeasible at the time of publication.
