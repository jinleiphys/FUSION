## Key claim

The authors derive minimal Fock-state expansions for both the pion and photon wave functions in light-cone formalism and show that the calculated $\gamma^{\ast}\gamma \to \pi^{0}$ transition form factor agrees with experimental data at low and moderately high energy scales, while also demonstrating that the $\gamma^{\ast}\pi^{0}\to \gamma$ and $\gamma^{\ast}\gamma \to \pi^{0}$ transition form factors are physically different but intrinsically related.

## Method

The authors derive the quark-antiquark wave functions of the pion and photon using the light-cone Fock expansion of minimal Fock-states, employing both the Melosh-Wigner rotation method from the light-cone quark model and a full relativistic field theory treatment of the interaction vertex. They then calculate the transition form factors for $\gamma^{\ast}\pi^{0}\to \gamma$ and $\gamma^{\ast}\gamma \to \pi^{0}$ processes by evaluating matrix elements of the vector current between the pion and photon wave functions, using the Brodsky-Huang-Lepage (BHL) prescription for the momentum space wave function of the pion. The Drell-Yan-West assignment is employed to handle the final state wave functions in the $q^{+}=0$ frame.

## Key numbers

- The $\pi^{0}\to\gamma\gamma$ decay width $\Gamma(\pi\to\gamma\gamma)$ is related to the form factor at $Q^{2}=0$ by $|F_{\pi\to\gamma\gamma}(0)|^{2}=64\pi\Gamma(\pi\to\gamma\gamma)/[(4\pi\alpha)^{2}M_{\pi}^{3}]$, where $\alpha$ is the QED coupling constant and $M_{\pi}$ is the pion mass.
- The leading order behavior of $F_{\gamma^{\ast}\pi\to\gamma^{\ast}}(Q^{2},Q^{\prime 2})$ in the limits $Q^{2}\to\infty$ and $Q^{\prime 2}\to\infty$ is given by $4\sqrt{3}(e_{u}^{2}-e_{d}^{2})\int_{0}^{1}\mathrm{d}x\,\phi_{\pi}(x)[1/(xQ^{\prime 2}+(1-x)Q^{2})]$.
- The number of colors is taken as $n_{c}=3$ in the color and flavor decomposition of the $\pi^{0}$ state.

## Context

This work extends the light-cone formalism approach to hadronic wave functions previously developed by Brodsky (1980, 2001) for QED composite systems, and applies it to both pion and photon Fock-state expansions simultaneously. The results connect to prior calculations of transition form factors using perturbative QCD by Brodsky-Lepage (1980) and Cao-Huang-Ma (1996), as well as light-front quark model calculations by Kroll-Raulfs (1996) for $\gamma^{\ast}\pi^{0}\to\gamma$ and by Hwang (2001) for $\gamma^{\ast}\gamma\to\pi^{0}$. The work enables a clearer understanding of the differences between the two transition processes, which can be measured in experiments such as $e+\pi^{0}\to e+\gamma$ versus $e^{+}+e^{-}\to e^{+}+e^{-}+\pi^{0}$ at Cornell (CLEO, 1998) and DESY (1991).
