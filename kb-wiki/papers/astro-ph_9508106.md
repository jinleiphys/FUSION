# Effects of Random Density Fluctuations on Matter-Enhanced Neutrino Flavor Transitions in Supernovae and Implications for Supernova Dynamics and Nucleosynthesis

> We calculate the effects of random density fluctuations on two-neutrino flavor transformations ($ν_{τ(μ)} \rightleftharpoons ν_e$) in the post-core-bounce supernova environment. In particular, we follow numerically the flavor evolution of neutrino states propagating through a stochastic field of density fluctuations. We examine the approach to neutrino flavor depolarization, and study the effects of this phenomenon in both the early shock reheating epoch and the later r-process nucleosynthesis epoch. Our results suggest that significant fluctuation-induced neutrino flavor depolarization effects occur in these environments only when the zero-order (without density fluctautions) evolution of the neutrino states includes adiabatic propagation through resonances (mass level crossings). In the shock reheating epoch, depolarization effects from fluctuations with amplitudes larger than 0.05% of the local matter density can cause an increase in the heating rate of the material behind the shock compared to the case with no neutrino flavor transformation - but this corresponds to a significant decrease in this quantity relative to the case with adiabatic neutrino transformation. If r-process nucleosynthesis is to occur during the late stages of supernova evolution, then the requirement of neutron-rich conditions excludes a region of neutrino mass-squared difference and vacuum mixing angle ($δm^2,\ \sin^22θ$) parameter space for neutrino flavor transformation. We find that in the presence of stochastic fluctuations, this excluded region is not significantly altered even for random fluctuations with an amplitude of 1% of the local matter density.

# Effects of Random Density Fluctuations on Matter-Enhanced Neutrino Flavor Transitions in Supernovae and Implications for Supernova Dynamics and Nucleosynthesis

## Key claim

Significant fluctuation-induced neutrino flavor depolarization effects in post-core-bounce supernovae occur only when the zero-order (without density fluctuations) evolution of neutrino states includes adiabatic propagation through resonances (mass level crossings). For the r-process nucleosynthesis epoch, the region of neutrino mass-squared difference and vacuum mixing angle parameter space excluded by the requirement of neutron-rich conditions is not significantly altered even for random fluctuations with an amplitude of 1% of the local matter density, but during the shock reheating epoch, fluctuations with amplitudes larger than 0.05% of the local matter density can reduce the heating rate by up to 45% relative to the adiabatic MSW case.

## Method

The authors numerically integrated the two-neutrino flavor evolution density matrix equation for neutrino states propagating through a stochastic field of density fluctuations modeled as Gaussian colored noise with an exponential correlation function. They developed a "ladder" approximation yielding a differential equation for the averaged transition probability valid when the correlation length is small compared to the resonance width, and compared this approximation to direct numerical averaging of the stochastic evolution equations. The survival probabilities were then used to compute the electron fraction and heating rates for the shock reheating epoch at time post bounce approximately 0.15 s and the r-process nucleosynthesis epoch at time post bounce approximately 3-16 s.

## Key numbers

- For the r-process epoch, a 1% rms fluctuation in matter density decreases the electron fraction from 0.5 to approximately 0.495 for points on the boundary of the excluded MSW parameter space; a 0.5% fluctuation gives a decrease of about 0.4%; a 0.1% fluctuation produces essentially no change in the excluded region.
- For the shock reheating epoch, fluctuations with rms amplitude of 0.05% of the local matter density can reduce the heating rate by up to 45% from the adiabatic MSW case, diminishing the MSW-induced increase in supernova explosion energy from approximately 30-60% to approximately 20%.
- The correlation length of maximal effect is approximately tau_c = L_res/pi = (delta m^2 sin 2theta / 4E)^{-1}, which for one example with delta m^2 = 10^2 eV^2, sin^2 2theta = 10^{-3}, and E = 33 MeV gives tau_c^0 = 832.6 cm.
- For the r-process boundary example with E_nu = 25 MeV and delta m^2 approximately 900 eV^2, the conversion probability is approximately 25% and the density scale height at resonance is H approximately 0.5 km.
- The average neutrino energies used are: <E_nu_e> approximately 11 MeV, <E_nu_bar_e> approximately 16 MeV, and <E_nu_tau> approximately 25 MeV.
- To obtain neutron-rich conditions (Y_e < 0.5) requires P(nu_e -> nu_e) > 64%; to obtain Y_e < 0.45 requires less than 15% flavor conversion.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of MSW neutrino flavor transformations in supernovae by Fuller et al. (1995) and Wilson and Mayle (1995) on shock reheating, and by Qian et al. (1995) on r-process nucleosynthesis constraints, by incorporating the effects of random density fluctuations modeled as colored noise. The authors build on the semi-quantitative approach to neutrino oscillations in inhomogeneous matter developed in Ref. [6] and the delta-correlated Gaussian noise treatment of Ref. [10], extending these to the more realistic case of colored noise with finite correlation length. The results enable future work to assess whether random density fluctuations could significantly alter the predicted neutrino heating of the stalled shock if MSW transitions prove necessary for successful supernova explosions, while showing that the r-process exclusion region in neutrino mixing parameter space is robust against such fluctuations.
