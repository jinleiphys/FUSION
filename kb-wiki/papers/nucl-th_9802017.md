# Unified model of the relativistic piNN and gamma-piNN systems

> We present a unified description of the relativistic piNN and gamma-piNN systems where the strong interactions are described non-perturbatively by four-dimensional integral equations. Our formulation obeys two and three-body unitarity and is strictly gauge invariant with photons coupled to all possible places in the strong interaction model. At the same time our formulation is free from the overcounting problems plaguing four-dimensional descriptions of piNN and gamma-piNN systems. The accurate nature of our description is achieved through the use of the recently introduced gauging of equations method.

## Key claim

The paper presents a unified, gauge-invariant description of the relativistic πNN and γπNN systems using four-dimensional integral equations, claiming to be free from the overcounting problems that have plagued previous four-dimensional formulations. The key qualifier is that the gauge invariance and consistency are achieved specifically through the recently introduced "gauging of equations method," and the authors note that the resulting expressions, while formally correct, are not the most convenient for numerical calculations.

## Method

The authors derive Bethe-Salpeter-type integral equations for the πNN system, first for distinguishable nucleons and then for indistinguishable nucleons using antisymmetrization operators. They then apply their gauging of equations method to these integral equations to obtain electromagnetic transition currents for processes such as γd → πNN, γd → NN, and NN → γNN, as well as deuteron electromagnetic form factors. An alternative form of the πNN equations is presented that uses a "free" Green function without two-body interactions, which simplifies the gauging procedure and yields a compact expression for the gauged Green function matrix.

## Key numbers

- The πNN equations are formulated as 4×4 matrix Bethe-Salpeter equations for distinguishable nucleons, and 3×3 matrix equations for indistinguishable nucleons.
- The kernel V^d_NN for distinguishable nucleons contains a subtraction term Δ defined as Δ = W_ππ + W^d_πN + W_NN + X + Y^d, where each term corresponds to specific two-body amplitudes.
- The gauged Green function matrix is given by G^μ = G(M^μ + V_t^μ)G, where M^μ corresponds to impulse approximation photon coupling and V_t^μ corresponds to interaction currents.
- The deuteron bound state electromagnetic current is given by j^μ = Ψ̄(M^μ + V_t^μ)Ψ, where Ψ satisfies Ψ = G_0 V_t Ψ.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous gauging of equations method, which was first applied to the covariant three-nucleon problem (Blankleider and Kvinikhidze, 1995-1997) and later to the three-dimensional spectator equation for three-nucleons. The πNN equations themselves were derived earlier by the same authors (1995) and independently by Phillips and Afnan (1995), correcting inconsistencies in earlier attempts by Taylor (1966), Tucciarone (1969), Broido (1969), Avishai (1971), and Haberzettl (1994) that had overcounting or undercounting of perturbation diagrams. The formalism enables gauge-invariant calculations of deuteron electromagnetic form factors with all meson exchange currents included, and can be applied to processes such as pion photoproduction, electroproduction, deuteron photodisintegration, and nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung above pion production threshold.
