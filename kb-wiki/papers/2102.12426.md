# Hybrid star properties from an extended linear sigma model

> The equation of state provided by effective models of strongly interacting matter should comply with the restrictions imposed by current astrophysical observations on compact stars. Using the equation of state given by the (axial-)vector meson extended linear sigma model, we determine the mass-radius relation and study whether these restrictions are satisfied under the assumption that most of the star is filled with quark matter. We study the dependence of the mass-radius relation on the parameters of the model.

# Hybrid star properties from an extended linear sigma model

## Key claim

Using the equation of state from the (axial-)vector meson extended linear sigma model (eLSM) for the quark phase, hybrid star masses above 2 solar masses can be obtained within the permitted radius window of 11.0–12.5 km at 2 solar masses, but only for a narrow range of the vector coupling parameter \(g_v\) and the hadron-quark crossover transition density \(\bar\rho\); the authors caution that the hadron-quark phase transition is introduced in a very ad hoc way and the work should be regarded as a first attempt.

## Method

The authors construct a hybrid star equation of state by combining three nuclear equations of state (BPS for the outer crust, NV for the inner crust, and TNI2u or TNI3u for the outer core) with a quark-phase equation of state from the \(N_f=2+1\) flavor eLSM, which includes a Yukawa coupling \(-g_v\sqrt{6}\bar\Psi\gamma_\mu V_0^\mu\Psi\) to a vector meson at mean-field level. The hadron and quark phases are connected via a hyperbolic-tangent crossover interpolation with transition density \(\bar\rho\) and width \(\Gamma\). The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved with a fourth-order Runge-Kutta integrator to obtain mass-radius sequences for various values of \(g_v\), \(\bar\rho\), and \(\Gamma\).

## Key numbers

- The vector meson mass in the model is \(m_v = 871.9\) MeV.
- The maximum compact star mass with \(g_v=0\) is approximately \(1.5\,M_\odot\).
- The parameter set \(g_v = 2.5\), \(\Gamma = 0\)–\(1\), \(\bar\rho = 2\rho_0\)–\(3\rho_0\) with TNI2u nuclear interaction (compressibility \(K=250\) MeV) fulfills the mass-radius constraints of neutron star observations.
- The experimental tidal deformability constraint is \(70 < \Lambda_{1.4M_\odot} < 580\).
- The permitted radius window at \(2\,M_\odot\) is \(11.0\)–\(12.5\) km.

## Context

This work extends the eLSM equation of state from Kovacs (2016) by adding a vector meson coupling to quarks, and applies it to hybrid star structure for the first time. It follows the observational constraints from the two-solar-mass neutron star of Demorest (2010) and the GW170817 merger event (2018). The hadronic equations of state used (BPS, NV, TNI2u/TNI3u) are standard in the literature. The results enable future improvements beyond mean-field approximation and a self-consistent derivation of the phase transition parameters from the model itself.
