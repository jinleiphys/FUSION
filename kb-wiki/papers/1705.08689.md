# Manifestations of the $a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)$ mixing in $D^0\to K^0_Sπ^+π^-$ and $D^0\to K^0_Sηπ^0$ decays

> Possible manifestations of the isospin-breaking mixing of the $a^0_0(980)$ and $f_0(980)$ resonances are analyzed in the $D^0\to K^0_S π^+π^-$ and $D^0\to K^0_Sηπ^0$ decays. It is shown that the $a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)$ mixing has the most influence on the $π^+π^-$ mass spectrum in the decay $D^0\to K^0_Sπ^+π^-$. Owing to the $a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)$ mixing, the $f_0(980)$ peak in $D^0 \to K^0_Sf_0(980)\to K^0_S π^+π^-$ can experience distortions comparable with its size. The effect essentially depends on the relative phase between the amplitudes of the $D^0\to K^0_Sf_0(980)$ and $D^0\to K^0_Sa^0_0(980)$ transitions.

## Key claim

The isospin-breaking mixing between the \(a^0_0(980)\) and \(f_0(980)\) resonances can produce distortions of the \(f_0(980)\) peak in \(D^0\to K^0_S\pi^+\pi^-\) that are comparable with its size, but the effect depends essentially on the relative phase between the amplitudes of the \(D^0\to K^0_Sf_0(980)\) and \(D^0\to K^0_Sa^0_0(980)\) transitions.

## Method

The authors model the \(\pi^+\pi^-\) and \(\eta\pi^0\) mass spectra in \(D^0\to K^0_S\pi^+\pi^-\) and \(D^0\to K^0_S\eta\pi^0\) decays using coupled Breit-Wigner propagators for the \(f_0(980)\) and \(a^0_0(980)\) resonances, including a mixing amplitude \(\Pi_{a^0_0f_0}(m)\) that describes the \(a^0_0(980)\to(K^+K^-+K^0\bar K^0)\to f_0(980)\) transition. The normalization constants \(C_1\) and \(C_2\) are fixed using CLEO and Particle Data Group branching ratio measurements, and the mass spectra are computed as functions of the relative phase \(\varphi\) between the two decay amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Branching ratio for \(D^0\to K^0_Sf_0(980)\to K^0_S\pi^+\pi^-\): \((1.23^{+0.40}_{-0.24})\times10^{-3}\)
- Branching ratio for \(D^0\to K^0_Sa^0_0(980)\to K^0_S\eta\pi^0\): \((6.6\pm2.0)\times10^{-3}\)
- Incoherent contribution from \(a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)\) mixing to the \(\pi^+\pi^-\) channel: approximately \(1.7\%\)
- Incoherent contribution from \(a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)\) mixing to the \(\eta\pi^0\) channel: approximately \(0.17\%\)
- Maximal constructive interference in the \(\pi^+\pi^-\) channel occurs at phase \(\varphi\approx 245^\circ\); maximal destructive interference at \(\varphi\approx 70^\circ\)
- Maximal constructive interference in the \(\eta\pi^0\) channel occurs at phase \(\varphi\approx 110^\circ\); maximal destructive interference at \(\varphi\approx 290^\circ\)
- The integral effect from mixing can reach about \(11\%\) of the \(f_0(980)\) branching ratio
- The mixing can narrow the \(f_0(980)\) peak by about 1.5 times and increase its height up to 60%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of \(a^0_0(980)-f_0(980)\) mixing in \(D^+_s\to\eta\pi^0\pi^+\) decays (Achasov and Shestakov, 2017) to the \(D^0\) meson system. The predictions are testable with existing data from CLEO, BaBar, and Belle, which have collected up to 1,231,731 \(D^0\to K^0_S\pi^+\pi^-\) candidates. The observed narrowness of the \(f_0(980)\) peak (width less than 25 MeV) in BaBar (2008) and Belle (2014) data may already indicate the mixing effect, though interference with other intermediate states cannot be excluded. Detection of the predicted distortions would clarify the production and decay mechanisms of light scalar mesons in weak three-body hadronic \(D^0\) decays.
