# Lattice study of pion-pion scattering using Nf=2+1 Wilson improved quarks with masses down to their physical values

> We use 2HEX smeared gauge configurations generated with an $\mathrm{N}_\mathrm{f}\mathrm{=2+1}$ clover improved Wilson action to investigate $ππ$ scattering in the $ρ$ channel. The range of lattice spacings (0.054 to 0.12 fm) and space-like extents (32 and 48) allows us to extract the scattering parameters through the volume dependence of the $ππ$-state energies according to Lüscher's formalism. The pion masses (134 to 300 MeV) are light enough to allow the decay of the rho and the level repulsion observed indicates that our data are sensitive to the interaction. We analyse our data with a multi-channel GEVP variational formula. Our results are in good agreement with the experimental values and consistent with a weak pion mass dependence of the $ρππ$ coupling constant.

## Key claim

The paper reports that lattice QCD results for the rho meson resonance parameters (mass and coupling) are in good agreement with experimental values and consistent with a weak pion mass dependence of the rho-pi-pi coupling constant, based on preliminary analysis of six gauge ensembles with pion masses ranging from 134 to 300 MeV.

## Method

The authors used Nf=2+1 clover improved Wilson fermions with 2HEX smeared gauge configurations at lattice spacings from 0.054 to 0.12 fm and spatial extents of 32 and 48. They extracted pi-pi scattering phase shifts in the I=J=1 channel from the volume dependence of two-pion state energies using Luscher's formalism, employing a multi-channel GEVP variational method with up to five independent operators (one rho operator and four pi-pi operators with different momenta) and stochastic propagators for the contractions.

## Key numbers

* Pion masses studied: 134 to 300 MeV, with the lowest ensemble at m_pi = 134 MeV (physical point)
* Lattice spacings: 0.054 to 0.12 fm (inverse scales a^{-1} = 1.697(6) GeV at beta=3.31 and 2.561(26) GeV at beta=3.61)
* Spatial extents: 32 and 48 lattice sites, with m_pi L values ranging from 3.87 to 5.65
* Extracted rho-pi-pi coupling constant at the physical point: g_{rho pi pi}^{phys} = 6.2 +/- 0.24
* Number of gauge ensembles analyzed: 6 independent ensembles
* Bootstrap samples: 2000, with approximately 20% discarded when fit relative error exceeded 100%

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD studies of the Budapest-Marseille-Wuppertal collaboration (Durr 2010) to pi-pi scattering in the rho channel, using the same gauge configurations that previously reached physical pion masses. The analysis follows Luscher's finite-volume formalism (Luscher 1986, 1991) and the variational method (Luscher 1990) for extracting excited state energies. The results enable future combination with lattice QCD+QED methods (Borsanyi 2014) to investigate isospin breaking effects in rho resonance parameters, which remain controversial (Particle Data Group 2014).
