# Pseudorapidity dependence of short-range correlations from a multi-phase transport model

> Using a multi-phase transport model (AMPT) that includes both initial partonic and hadronic interactions, we study neighboring bin multiplicity correlations as a function of pseudorapidity in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7-62.4$ GeV. It is observed that for $\sqrt{s_{NN}} < $19.6GeV Au+Au collisions, the short-range correlations of final particles have a trough at central pseudorapidity, while for $\sqrt{s_{NN}} > $19.6GeV AuAu collisions, the short-range correlations of final particles have a peak at central pseudorapidity. Our findings indicate that the pseudorapidity dependence of short-range correlations should contain some new physical information, and are not a simple result of the pseudorapidity distribution of final particles. The AMPT results with and without hadronic scattering are compared. It is found that hadron scattering can only increase the short-range correlations to some level, but is not responsible for the different correlation shapes for different energies. Further study shows that the different pseudorapidity dependence of short-range correlations are mainly due to partonic evolution and the following hadronization scheme.

# Pseudorapidity dependence of short-range correlations from a multi-phase transport model

## Key claim
The pseudorapidity dependence of short-range correlations in Au+Au collisions exhibits a qualitative change at approximately 19.6 GeV: a trough at central pseudorapidity for energies below 19.6 GeV and a peak at central pseudorapidity for energies above 19.6 GeV. This behavior is not a simple consequence of the pseudorapidity distribution of final particles, and the authors argue it arises primarily from partonic evolution and the subsequent quark coalescence hadronization scheme, rather than from hadronic scattering.

## Method
The authors used the AMPT model with string melting mechanism to simulate Au+Au collisions at six beam energies from 7.7 to 62.4 GeV, with a parton cross section of 10 mb. They computed the neighboring-bin multiplicity correlation C(η) = ⟨n_η n_{η+δη}⟩/(⟨n_η⟩⟨n_{η+δη}⟩) − 1 over the pseudorapidity range [-2, +2] divided into 20 bins of width δη = 0.2. They compared results from the full evolution (including hadronic scattering via the ART model) with results obtained just after quark coalescence but before hadronic scattering, and separately examined the parton-level correlations at different evolution times.

## Key numbers
- Beam energies studied: √s_NN = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, and 62.4 GeV
- Transition energy: approximately 19.6 GeV; below this energy short-range correlations show a trough at central pseudorapidity, above this energy they show a peak at central pseudorapidity
- Bin width used for correlation analysis: δη = 0.2 over the pseudorapidity range [-2, +2] (20 bins)
- Parton cross section in simulations: 10 mb
- Partonic evolution time points examined: initial state ("before ZPC"), τ = 2 fm/c into ZPC evolution, and "before hadronization" (freeze-out of partons)

## Context
This work extends the study of short-range correlations from pp collisions (where they are attributed to cluster decay, as in SRC-pp1 and SRC-pp2) to Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies, where few such studies had been done. It contrasts with the well-investigated long-range forward-backward correlations studied by the STAR collaboration (LRC-STAR) and in elementary collisions (LRC-pp, LRC-review). The findings enable future experimental tests at RHIC and the LHC to determine whether the observed energy-dependent shape change in short-range correlations can serve as a signature of partonic evolution and the quark coalescence mechanism in heavy-ion collisions.
