# Sigma Exchange in the Nonmesonic Decays of Light Hypernuclei and Violation of the Delta I=1/2 Rule

> Nonmesonic weak decays of s-shell hypernuclei are analyzed in microscopic models for the Lambda N to NN weak interaction. A scalar-isoscalar meson, sigma, is introduced and its importance in accounting the decay rates, n/p ratios and proton asymmetry is demonstrated. Possible violation of the Delta I=1/2 rule in the nonmesonic weak decay of Lambda is discussed in a phenomenological analysis and several useful constraints are presented. The microscopic calculation shows that the current experimental data indicate a large violation of the Delta I=1/2 rule, although no definite conclusion can be derived due to large ambiguity of the decay rate of {^4_Lambda H}.

## Key claim

The paper argues that introducing a scalar-isoscalar sigma meson exchange into the microscopic model for the Lambda N to NN weak transition can resolve the proton asymmetry puzzle in the nonmesonic weak decay of light hypernuclei, and that current experimental data indicate a large violation of the Delta I=1/2 rule, although no definite conclusion can be drawn due to the large ambiguity in the decay rate of 4_Lambda H.

## Method

The authors constructed microscopic models for the Lambda N to NN weak interaction including one-pion exchange (OPE), one-kaon exchange (OKE), one-sigma exchange (OSE), and a direct-quark (DQ) transition potential. They performed a phenomenological analysis parameterizing the decay rates of s-shell hypernuclei in terms of spin-singlet and spin-triplet strengths R_NJ, and then determined the weak coupling constants A_sigma and B_sigma for the sigma meson by fitting to experimental data on total decay rates, n/p ratios, and proton asymmetry for 5_Lambda He, 4_Lambda He, and 4_Lambda H.

## Key numbers

- The strong coupling constant for the sigma meson is taken as g_NNsigma = 13.16, the same as the strong pi NN coupling.
- The sigma meson mass is 550 MeV, with a cutoff mass of 1200 MeV for the form factor.
- For the ME model, the parity-violating coupling B_sigma is determined to be 1.2 (central value) with a range from -1.2 to 4.4; for the DQ+ model, B_sigma = 6.6 (central value) with a range from 4.2 to 9.8.
- For the ME model, the parity-conserving coupling A_sigma has two candidates: 3.8 and -1.7; for the DQ+ model, A_sigma has two candidates: 3.9 and -1.0.
- The phenomenological analysis without the Delta I=1/2 constraint yields kappa = R_n0/R_p0 = 0.27 +/- 0.21 for the new data set (Set II), compared to the Delta I=1/2 prediction of kappa = 2.
- The average nucleon density at the position of Lambda is rho_5 = 0.045 fm^{-3} for 5_Lambda He and rho_4 = 0.020 fm^{-3} for A=4 hypernuclei (Set II).

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Sasaki, Izaki, and Oka (2001, 2002) that used OPE+OKE+DQ models, which overestimated total decay rates and failed to reproduce the proton asymmetry. The introduction of sigma exchange is motivated by effective field theory analyses by Jun (1998) and Park (2001) that suggested the need for central interactions. The phenomenological analysis follows the approach of Alberico et al. (2002) who examined the Delta I=1/2 rule in s-shell hypernuclei. The results enable future precise measurements of 4_Lambda H decays to definitively test the Delta I=1/2 rule in nonmesonic weak decays.
