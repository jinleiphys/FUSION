# Chiral Hall Effect and Chiral Electric Waves

> We investigate the vector and axial currents induced by external electromagnetic fields and chemical potentials in chiral systems at finite temperature. Similar to the normal Hall effect, we find that an axial Hall current is generated in the presence of the electromagnetic fields along with an axial chemical potential, which may be dubbed as the "chiral Hall effect"(CHE). The CHE is related to the interactions of chiral fermions and exists with the a nonzero axial chemical potential. We argue that the CHE could lead to nontrivial charge distributions at different rapidity in asymmetric heavy ion collisions. Moreover, we study the chiral electric waves(CEW) led by the fluctuations of the vector and axial chemical potentials along with the chiral electric separation effect(CESE), where a density wave propagates along the applied electric field. Combining with the normal/chiral Hall effects, the fluctuations of chemical potentials thus result in Hall density waves. The Hall density waves may survive even at zero chemical potentials and become non-dissipative. We further study the transport coefficients including the Hall conductivities, damping times, wave velocities, and diffusion constants of CEW in a strongly coupled plasma via the AdS/CFT correspondence.

## Key claim

The authors propose a "chiral Hall effect" (CHE) where an axial Hall current is generated perpendicular to both applied electric and magnetic fields in chiral systems with a nonzero axial chemical potential, and they argue this effect could lead to nontrivial charge distributions at different rapidity in asymmetric heavy ion collisions, though they emphasize this is a qualitative discussion requiring future numerical hydrodynamic studies.

## Method

The authors derive the chiral Hall effect from the difference in Hall conductivities of right-handed and left-handed fermions in the presence of an axial chemical potential, using both classical force-balance arguments and the Sakai-Sugimoto (SS) model of AdS/QCD to compute transport coefficients. They then formulate evolution equations for density fluctuations that combine the chiral electric separation effect (CESE) with the chiral Hall effect to obtain chiral electric waves (CEW), solving linearized dispersion relations to extract wave velocities, damping times, and diffusion constants.

## Key numbers

- The damping time for charge density fluctuations in QGP is estimated as τ_c ~ 7.6 fm/c for μ_V = μ_A = 0 at T = 200 MeV, based on holographic calculations giving eσ_v ~ 26 MeV
- For μ_V = 4T and μ_A = 0 at T = 200 MeV, the damping time is τ_c ~ 5.5 fm/c, with eσ_v ~ 36 MeV
- From lattice calculations, the DC conductivity of static QGP is eσ_v ~ 5.8 T/T_c MeV, giving damping times τ_c ~ 17–34 fm/c for T ~ T_c–2T_c
- The strong electric field in asymmetric collisions (e.g., Cu+Au) can have magnitude m_π^2, where m_π is the pion mass

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of CESE in the SS model (Pu 2014) and builds on the chiral magnetic effect (CME) proposed by Kharzeev (2007) and the chiral separation effect (CSE) by Fukushima (2008). The chiral electric waves generalize the chiral magnetic waves (CMW) introduced by Kharzeev (2010) to the case of electric fields, and the chiral Hall effect provides a new mechanism for charge and chirality separation along the beam direction in heavy ion collisions, complementing the quadrupole distributions predicted from combining CME and CESE by Huang (2013).
