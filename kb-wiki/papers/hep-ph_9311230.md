# Bounds for Bose-Einstein Correlation Functions

> Bounds for the correlation functions of identical bosons are discussed for the general case of a Gaussian density matrix. In particular, for a purely chaotic system the two-particle correlation function must always be greater than one. On the other hand, in the presence of a coherent component the correlation function may take values below unity. The experimental situation is briefly discussed.

## Key claim

For a Gaussian density matrix, a purely chaotic source always yields a two-particle Bose-Einstein correlation function greater than or equal to one, while a partially coherent source can produce values below unity; however, the lower bound for charged bosons is 2/3 and for neutral bosons (photons, pi0) is 1/3, not zero.

## Method

The authors derive general bounds for two-particle Bose-Einstein correlation functions using the Gaussian density matrix formalism, expressing all multiparticle inclusive distributions in terms of the chaotic component D(k,k), the coherent component |I(k)|^2, and the normalized current correlators d_rs and tilde-d_rs. ## Key numbers

- For a purely chaotic source: C_2(k1,k2) >= 1 (lower bound)
- For a partially coherent source of identical charged bosons: C_2(k1,k2) >= 2/3
- For a partially coherent source of neutral bosons (pi0, photons): C_2(k1,k2) >= 1/3
- For charged bosons in the conventional treatment: 1 <= C_2(k1,k2) <= 2
- For neutral bosons in the conventional treatment: C_2(k1,k2) <= 3

## Context

This work extends the earlier upper-bound analysis of Andreev, Plumer, and Weiner (1993) for Gaussian density matrices to include lower bounds, and provides a quantum-statistical critique of the calculation by Kapusta et al. (1993) for photons from expanding hadronic matter, which found C_2 values below unity for a purely chaotic system. The results enable a third experimental criterion for detecting coherence in Bose-Einstein correlations (alongside the intercept criterion of Gyulassy et al. (1979) and the two-exponent structure of Weiner (1989)), and clarify that the observation of C_2 < 1 in e+e- collisions may indicate coherence rather than final-state interactions.
