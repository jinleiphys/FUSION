# Meson decay in a corrected $3^P_0$ model

> Extensively applied to both light and heavy meson decay and standing as one of the most successful strong decay models is the $3^P_0$ model, in which $q\bar{q}$ pair production is the dominant mechanism. The pair production can be obtained from the non-relativistic limit of a microscopic interaction Hamiltonian involving Dirac quark fields. The evaluation of the decay amplitude can be performed by a diagrammatic technique for drawing quark lines. In this paper we use an alternative approach which consists in a mapping technique, the Fock-Tani formalism, in order to obtain an effective Hamiltonian starting from same microscopic interaction. An additional effect is manifest in this formalism associated to the extended nature of mesons: bound-state corrections. A corrected $3^P_0$ is obtained and applied, as an example, to $b_{1}\toωπ$ and $a_{1}\toρπ$ decays.

## Key claim

The Fock-Tani formalism, when applied to the $^3P_0$ model of meson decay, yields bound-state corrections that modify the pair-creation interaction strength, producing a "corrected $^3P_0$ model" (C$^3P_0$) that can reproduce experimental decay widths and D/S ratios for light meson decays with slightly different parameters than the standard $^3P_0$ model.

## Method

The authors start from the microscopic $q\bar{q}$ pair-creation interaction Hamiltonian $H_I = 2m_q\gamma\int d\vec{x}\,\bar{\psi}(\vec{x})\psi(\vec{x})$ and apply the Fock-Tani unitary transformation to obtain an effective Hamiltonian in an enlarged Fock space where mesons are represented by "ideal" bosonic operators. The transformed Hamiltonian is expanded in powers of the meson wave function $\Phi$, yielding a leading-order $^3P_0$ amplitude at third order and bound-state corrections at fifth order, which are encoded in the bound-state kernel $\Delta(\rho\tau;\lambda\nu) = \Phi^{\rho\tau}_\alpha \Phi^{*\lambda\nu}_\alpha$. The corrected decay amplitude is then evaluated for specific decays using simple harmonic oscillator wave functions.

## Key numbers

- For $b_1 \rightarrow \omega\pi$: the C$^3P_0$ model with $\gamma=0.539$ and $\beta=0.396$ GeV yields $\Gamma = 142$ MeV (experimental: 142 MeV) and $D/S = 0.273$ (experimental: $0.277 \pm 0.027$)
- For $a_1 \rightarrow \rho\pi$: the C$^3P_0$ model with $\gamma=0.539$ and $\beta=0.396$ GeV yields $\Gamma = 543$ MeV (experimental range: 250 to 600 MeV) and $D/S = -0.113$ (experimental: $-0.108 \pm 0.016$)
- The standard $^3P_0$ model optimum fit for $b_1$ data uses $\gamma=0.506$ and $\beta=0.397$ GeV
- Meson masses used: $M_\pi = 138$ MeV, $M_\rho = 775$ MeV, $M_{a_1} = 1230$ MeV, $M_{b_1} = 1229$ MeV, $M_\omega = 782$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the Fock-Tani formalism, previously applied to hadron-hadron scattering with constituent interchange (da Silva et al., 1997; Hadjimichef et al., 1997), to meson decay processes. It builds on the extensive $^3P_0$ model surveys by Barnes et al. (1996-2002), who found optimum parameters $\gamma \approx 0.5$ and $\beta \approx 0.4$ GeV for light 1S and 1P decays but noted overestimates for higher-L states. The corrected model enables systematic inclusion of bound-state effects in decay calculations and can be extended to study dense hadronic mediums where the wave function is not an eigenstate of the microscopic Hamiltonian.
