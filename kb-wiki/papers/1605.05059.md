# What do we know about neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements?

> The detection of neutrinoless double-beta decay will establish the Majorana nature of neutrinos. In addition, if the nuclear matrix elements of this process are reliably known, the experimental lifetime will provide precious information about the absolute neutrino masses and hierarchy. I review the status of nuclear structure calculations for neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements, and discuss some key issues to be addressed in order to meet the demand for accurate nuclear matrix elements.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) from different calculations still disagree by a factor of two to three, the main limitations of the calculations have been identified and work is in progress to obtain improved NMEs, but two-body current corrections to the transition operator are usually neglected and their effect could be sizeable.

## Method

The paper reviews and compares NME calculations from five different nuclear structure frameworks: the shell model (Strasbourg-Madrid, Tokyo, and Michigan groups), the interacting boson model (IBM-2), the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (Jyvaskyla and Tubingen groups), and non-relativistic and relativistic energy density functional methods. It analyzes the impact of configuration space size, pairing correlations (proton-proton, neutron-neutron, and isoscalar proton-neutron), quadrupole correlations from deformation, and two-body current corrections derived from chiral effective field theory on the computed NMEs.

## Key numbers

- NME differences between calculations are about a factor of two to three, or three to four units across the ten isotopes studied.
- Expanding the shell model configuration space for 48Ca from one major harmonic-oscillator shell to two major shells increases the NME by about 30%.
- The two-body current corrections reduce shell model NMEs by about 35%, with a relatively large error band from uncertainties in chiral EFT couplings.
- The typical momentum transfer in neutrinoless double-beta decay is q ~ 200 MeV due to the virtual nature of the neutrinos.
- A recent shell model study suggests isoscalar pairing correlations could reduce NMEs by as much as 50%.

## Context

This review extends the ongoing discussion of NME calculations for neutrinoless double-beta decay, comparing results from the shell model (Caurier 2005, Menendez 2009, Iwata 2016, Neacsu 2016), interacting boson model (Barea 2015), QRPA (Simkovic 2013, Hyvarinen 2015), and energy density functional methods (Vaquero 2013, Yao 2015). The work identifies that ab initio calculations based on chiral effective field theory will soon become available for these decays, and that two-body current corrections (Menendez 2011) must be included for accurate NMEs.
