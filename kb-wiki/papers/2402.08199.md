# A=3 (e,e') $x_B \geq 1$ cross-section ratios and the isospin structure of short-range correlations

> We study the relation between measured high-$x_B$, high-$Q^2$, Helium-3 to Tritium, $(e,e')$ inclusive-scattering cross-section ratios and the relative abundance of high-momentum neutron-proton ($np$) and proton-proton ($pp$) short-range correlated (SRC) nucleon pairs in three-body ($A=3$) nuclei. Analysis of this data using a simple pair-counting cross-section model suggested a much smaller $np/pp$ ratio than previously measured in heavier nuclei, questioning our understanding of $A=3$ nuclei and, by extension, all other nuclei. Here we examine this finding using spectral-function-based cross-section calculations, with both an \textit{ab initio} $A=3$ spectral function and effective Generalized Contact Formalism (GCF) spectral functions using different nucleon-nucleon interaction models. The \textit{ab initio} calculation agrees with the data, showing good understanding of the structure of $A=3$ nuclei. An 8\% uncertainty on the simple pair-counting model, as implied by the difference between it and the \textit{ab initio} calculation, gives a factor of 5 uncertainty in the extracted $np/pp$ ratio. Thus we see no evidence for the claimed ``unexpected structure in the high-momentum wavefunction for hydrogen-3 and helium-3''.

## Key claim

The measured Helium-3 to Tritium inclusive $(e,e')$ cross-section ratios agree with a full *ab initio* spectral-function calculation using the AV18 interaction, showing no evidence for the previously claimed "unexpected structure in the high-momentum wavefunction for hydrogen-3 and helium-3." An 8% uncertainty in the simple pair-counting model, as implied by the difference between it and the *ab initio* calculation, gives a factor of 5 uncertainty in the extracted $np/pp$ SRC pair ratio, eliminating the discrepancy with previous results.

## Method

The authors compared measured high-$x_B$, high-$Q^2$ Helium-3 to Tritium inclusive $(e,e')$ cross-section ratios to theoretical calculations using an *ab initio* spectral function for $A=3$ calculated with the AV18 interaction, as well as effective Generalized Contact Formalism (GCF) spectral functions using different nucleon-nucleon interaction models (AV4', Norfolk, N2LO). The factorized cross-section approximation (Eq. 4) was used, where the inclusive cross section equals the spectral function integrated over all states within experimental acceptance and summed over proton and neutron knockout, accounting for final state interactions between spectator nucleons but not with the leading nucleon.

## Key numbers

- The *ab initio* calculation differs from the simple pair-counting model by 8% for the same $np/pp$ SRC ratio of 11.5 (taken from AV18-based Quantum Monte-Carlo calculations)
- An 8% uncertainty in the simple pair-counting model gives a factor of 5 uncertainty in the extracted $np/pp$ ratio, spanning from 2.5 to 12
- The GCF cross-section ratio calculation using the AV18 interaction is about 15% larger than the exact AV18 spectral-function calculation
- Chiral EFT potential cross-section ratios agree with the AV18 result to about 5%, while their corresponding $np/pp$ ratios vary by a factor of 2.5
- The tensor-less AV4' potential gives a cross-section ratio that is 10% to 15% smaller than AV18, but an $np/pp$ ratio that is four times smaller
- At $Q^2 \approx 1.9$ GeV$^2$, the calculation shows significant ($\ge 20\%$) contributions to the cross-section from high-momentum low-energy nuclear states in the $1.4 \le x_B \le 1.7$ region
- Even at the highest $x_B$, the $^3$He cross-section includes a $\sim10\%$ contribution from two-body breakup (e.g., $(e,e')pd$) not associated with SRC-pair breakup

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Helium-3 and Tritium inclusive $(e,e')$ scattering measurements by Li (2022), which used a simple pair-counting model to extract an $np/pp$ SRC pair ratio of $4.34^{+0.49}_{-0.40}$ and claimed evidence for unexpected structure in the $A=3$ high-momentum wavefunction. The authors show that the data are consistent with *ab initio* calculations using the AV18 interaction from Atti and Kaptari (2005), contradicting that claim. The work enables future studies to focus on varied experimental measurements and state-of-the-art theoretical calculations with quantified uncertainties, rather than relying on inclusive cross-section ratios to precisely quantify the $np/pp$ SRC-pair ratio.
