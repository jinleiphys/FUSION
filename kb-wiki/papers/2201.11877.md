# New many-body method using cluster expansion diagrams with tensor-optimized antisymmetrized molecular dynamics

> We propose a new many-body method based on the correlation functions, in which the multiple products of the correlation functions are expanded into the many-body diagrams using the cluster expansion method and every diagram is independently optimized in the total-energy variation. We apply this idea to the tensor-optimized antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (TOAMD) using the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction and show the results of the $s$-shell nuclei within the triple products of the correlation functions of tensor and central-types. We evaluate the effect of the independent optimization of the many-body diagrams on the solutions. It is found that the triple products provides the sizable effect in the present scheme, which results in the good reproduction of the total energy and the Hamiltonian components of nuclei with respect to the few-body calculations.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new many-body method (New-TOAMD) in which the cluster expansion diagrams arising from multiple products of correlation functions are treated as independent basis states with their own variational weights, rather than keeping the fixed symmetry-factor relations among diagrams. For s-shell nuclei, this independent optimization provides a modest but noticeable improvement in total energy (0.2 MeV for He-4) compared to the previous TOAMD approach where diagram weights were fixed.

## Method

The authors start from an antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) wave function and multiply it by two-body correlation functions of tensor type (F_D) and central type (F_S), then consider up to triple products of these correlation functions (F^3). Each multiple product is expanded into irreducible many-body diagrams via cluster expansion; in the new scheme, each diagram (two-body, three-body, four-body, etc.) is treated as an independent basis state with its own variational coefficient, rather than keeping the symmetry-factor relations among diagrams. The correlation functions are expanded in Gaussians (up to N_G=9 for single, 7 for double, 3 for triple products), and the total wave function is optimized by solving a generalized eigenvalue problem for the Hamiltonian with the bare AV6' and AV8' nucleon-nucleon interactions.

## Key numbers

- For H-3 with AV6' potential: final total energy -7.95 MeV, matching the GFMC value of -7.95(1) MeV
- For H-3: triple correlation functions (F^3 terms) contribute only 0.03 MeV to the total energy beyond the F^2 level (-7.92 MeV)
- For He-4 with AV6' potential: final total energy -25.83 MeV, close to the GFMC value of -26.15(2) MeV (within about 0.3 MeV)
- For He-4: the F^3 terms contribute about 0.6 MeV to the total energy beyond the F^2 level (-25.27 MeV)
- For He-4: New-TOAMD gives total energy -25.83 MeV compared to previous TOAMD -25.63 MeV, a difference of 0.2 MeV
- For He-4: Gaussian range parameter nu = 0.22 fm^{-2} for the AMD wave function
- For H-3: Gaussian range parameter nu = 0.10 fm^{-2}
- Maximum basis number for He-4: around 10,000 basis states with (N_G1,N_G2,N_G3) = (9,7,3)

## Context

This work extends the tensor-optimized antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (TOAMD) method previously developed by Myo et al. (2015-2021) from double products of correlation functions (F^2) to triple products (F^3), and introduces a new treatment where cluster expansion diagrams are independently optimized rather than keeping fixed symmetry factors. The results for s-shell nuclei approach the accuracy of Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations by Pieper et al. (2001) and Wiringa et al. (2002), and improve upon the Jastrow method (Jastrow, 1955) which uses common correlation functions for all nucleon pairs. This enables future application to p-shell nuclei and nuclear matter calculations with bare nucleon-nucleon interactions.
