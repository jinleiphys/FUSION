# Jet and Dijet Rates in AB Collisions

> Jet and dijet rates in AB collisions at LHC energies are computed using a NLO Monte Carlo code. Uncertainties due to nuclear modifications of nucleon parton densities are analyzed. Predictions for different AB collisions at LHC energies are presented.

## Key claim
Jet and dijet rates in AB collisions at LHC energies are computed using a next-to-leading-order (NLO) Monte Carlo code, but the results consider only "initial state" effects (nuclear modifications of parton densities and isospin) and explicitly exclude any energy loss or jet quenching, so they serve as a baseline reference whose failure would indicate new physics.

## Method
The authors used an NLO Monte Carlo code adapted to include isospin effects and nuclear modifications of nucleon parton distribution functions (PDFs) via the EKS98 parameterization, with MRST98 central gluon PDFs as the nucleon baseline. Jet reconstruction employed the \(k_T\)-clustering algorithm with \(D=1\), a factorization/renormalization scale \(\mu = E_T/2\), and kinematic cuts of \(|\eta_i| < 2.5\) and \(E_{Ti} > 20\) GeV for single jets, with \(E_{T1} > 20\) GeV and \(E_{T2} > 15\) GeV for dijet angular distributions. No centrality dependence or underlying event subtraction was included; the study focused on PbPb at 5.5 TeV and ArAr at 6.3 TeV per nucleon in the LHC lab frame.

## Key numbers
- In PbPb collisions at 5.5 TeV with luminosity \(5 \times 10^{26} \, \text{cm}^{-2}\text{s}^{-1}\), expected jet yields per month are \(2.2 \times 10^7\) jets at \(E_{Ti} \sim 50\) GeV (cross section 1 \(\mu\)b/(AB)) and \(2.2 \times 10^3\) jets at \(E_{Ti} \sim 250\) GeV (cross section \(10^{-4}\) \(\mu\)b/(AB)).
- For PbPb at 5.5 TeV, a sample of \(10^3\) jets is theoretically accessible up to \(E_{Ti} \sim 275\) GeV in a one-month run at the same luminosity, corresponding to a cross section of \(4.5 \times 10^{-5}\) \(\mu\)b/(AB).
- Nuclear PDF modifications (EKS98) increase jet cross sections by \(\sim 3\%\) on transverse momentum distributions, up to \(\sim 5\%\) on pseudorapidity distributions, and up to \(\sim 10\%\) on dijet angular distributions; isospin effects are negligible.
- In PbPb at 5.5 TeV, the average number of nucleon-nucleon collisions involved in jet production is 1.6 for minimum bias and 2.4 for central collisions at \(E_{T0} = 20\) GeV, dropping to 1.0 for both at \(E_{T0} = 100\) and 200 GeV (using \(\sigma(E_{T0}) = 70\), 0.1, and 0.006 \(\mu\)b respectively).
- For ArAr at 6.3 TeV, expected event rates per month per \(\mu\)b/(AB) are \(1.6 \times 10^8\) at luminosity \(10^{29} \, \text{cm}^{-2}\text{s}^{-1}\) and \(4.8 \times 10^6\) at \(3 \times 10^{27} \, \text{cm}^{-2}\text{s}^{-1}\); for PbPb at 5.5 TeV, the rate is \(2.2 \times 10^7\) at \(5 \times 10^{26} \, \text{cm}^{-2}\text{s}^{-1}\).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous NLO study of jet and dijet rates in pA collisions (Accardi et al., 2002) to symmetric AB collisions, providing a baseline for initial-state effects without jet quenching. The results are intended as a reference for future LHC measurements; deviations would indicate the presence of new physics such as parton energy loss in a quark-gluon plasma, as proposed by the jet quenching and tomography frameworks (Baier et al., 1997; Gyulassy et al., 2000). The study enables systematic comparisons with experimental data from CMS and other detectors, and highlights the need for future eA collider experiments to clarify the centrality dependence of nuclear PDF modifications.
