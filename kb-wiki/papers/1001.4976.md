# Nuclear halo structure and pseudo-spin symmetry

> Nuclear halo structure and restoration of relativistic symmetry are studied within the framework of the relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) theory. Giant halos as well as ordinary ones are found in Cerium isotopes close to the neutron drip line. Bridged by T=0 {channel}, the restoration of pseudo-spin symmetry (PSS) plays an essential role in stabilizing the neutron halo structures. The Fock terms, especially the $ρ$-tensor couplings, not only play significant role in the PSS restoration but also present substantial contributions to the T=0 {channel}, from which is well demonstrated the necessity of Fock terms.

# Nuclear halo structure and pseudo-spin symmetry

## Key claim

Giant halos and ordinary halos are found in Cerium isotopes near the neutron drip line, but only when pseudo-spin symmetry (PSS) is properly conserved on the proton side; the stability of these halo structures depends on the consistency between neutron shell evolution and proton PSS conservation, which is mediated by the T=0 channel of the nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) calculations using the density-dependent meson-nucleon coupling parameter set PKA1, with the Gogny pairing force D1S in the pairing channel. They computed neutron and proton densities, canonical single-particle energies, occupation probabilities, and neutron skin thickness for Cerium isotopes approaching the neutron drip line, and analyzed the two-body interaction matrix elements between proton and neutron valence orbits to study the coupling between PSS and halo formation.

## Key numbers

- The neutron drip line calculated with PKA1 is N=140 for Cerium isotopes, whereas calculations with PKO1 and DD-ME2 predict a shorter drip line at N=126.
- The neutron matter radius in 198Ce is r_n = 6.2 fm.
- In 198Ce, the halo orbits are nu4s_{1/2}, nu3d_{5/2}, and nu3d_{3/2}; isotopes from 192Ce to 198Ce have substantial numbers of neutrons beyond r=16 fm.
- The neutron shell gap at N=126 (Delta E = E_nu2g_{9/2} - E_nu1i_{13/2}) varies with neutron number and differs between models; PKA1 gives a smaller gap than PKO1 and DD-ME2.
- Fock terms contribute about 30% of the total T=0 two-body interaction matrix elements, with approximately 2/3 of the Fock contribution coming from the tensor rho couplings.
- Example matrix elements: V_{ab} for a=pi2d_{5/2} and b=nu1i_{13/2} is -0.257 MeV; for a=pi1g_{7/2} and b=nu1i_{13/2} is -0.386 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) studies of halo phenomena in Li (Meng, 1996), Ne (Poschl, 1997; Lalazissis, 1998), Na (Lalazissis, 1998; Meng, 1998), Ca (Meng, 2002), and Zr (Meng, 1998; Grasso, 2006) by incorporating Fock terms through the RHFB framework. The authors show that the tensor forces from rho and pi meson exchanges, missing in Hartree approaches, are essential for proper PSS conservation and for stabilizing halo structures in Cerium isotopes. This enables future studies of halo phenomena in heavier neutron-rich nuclei where Fock terms and PSS conservation may be critical.
