# Pseudospin symmetry and the relativistic harmonic oscillator

> A generalized relativistic harmonic oscillator for spin 1/2 particles is studied. The Dirac Hamiltonian contains a scalar $S$ and a vector $V$ quadratic potentials in the radial coordinate, as well as a tensor potential $U$ linear in $r$. Setting either or both combinations $Σ=S+V$ and $% Δ=V-S$ to zero, analytical solutions for bound states of the corresponding Dirac equations are found. The eigenenergies and wave functions are presented and particular cases are discussed, devoting a special attention to the nonrelativistic limit and the case $Σ=0$, for which pseudospin symmetry is exact. We also show that the case $U=Δ=0$ is the most natural generalization of the nonrelativistic harmonic oscillator. The radial node structure of the Dirac spinor is studied for several combinations of harmonic-oscillator potentials, and that study allows us to explain why nuclear intruder levels cannot be described in the framework of the relativistic harmonic oscillator in the pseudospin limit.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that exact pseudospin symmetry (Σ = 0) in a relativistic harmonic oscillator with scalar, vector, and tensor potentials yields analytical bound-state solutions, but the radial node structure of the Dirac spinor in this limit prevents the description of nuclear intruder levels, contrary to what might be expected from pseudospin symmetry in realistic nuclear mean fields.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation for spin-1/2 particles with a generalized Hamiltonian containing a scalar potential S and vector potential V both quadratic in the radial coordinate r, plus a tensor potential U linear in r. They obtain analytical solutions for two special cases: Δ = V − S = 0 and Σ = S + V = 0, by reducing the coupled first-order Dirac equations to second-order differential equations that become exactly solvable harmonic-oscillator-type equations. The eigenenergies and wave functions are derived using generalized Laguerre polynomials, and the radial node structure of the upper and lower spinor components is analyzed for various combinations of the potentials.

## Key numbers

* For the Δ = 0 case, the nonrelativistic limit yields the energy expression E = (2n + l + 3/2)√(ω₁² + ω₂²) + (κ − 1/2)ω₂, where ω₁ and ω₂ are the frequencies of the sum and tensor potentials, respectively.
* For the Σ = 0 case, the energy equation is E² − m² + (2κ̃ − 1)mω₂ = (2ñ + l̃ + 3/2)√(2m(E − m)ω₁² + 4m²ω₂²), where κ̃ = −κ and l̃ is the pseudoorbital angular momentum quantum number.
* For the Δ = 0 case with ω₁ = 2, ω₂ = 1, and m = 10, the energy spectrum shows nondegenerate levels (e.g., the 2s₁/₂, 1d₃/₂, and 1d₅/₂ states have distinct energies).
* For the Σ = 0 case with ω₁ = 2 and ω₂ = 0, the ground state is the 0̃s̃₁/₂ state; for ω₂ ≳ 0.3, the 0̃p̃₁/₂ state becomes the ground state.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of relativistic harmonic oscillators by Kukulin et al. and Cheng et al., who considered vector-scalar harmonic potentials with Δ = 0 or Σ = 0, and by Ginocchio, who solved harmonic oscillators for Δ = 0 applied to antinucleons. The paper generalizes these by including a tensor potential U linear in r alongside the quadratic scalar and vector potentials. The analysis of radial node structure explains why nuclear intruder levels cannot be described by a relativistic harmonic oscillator with exact pseudospin symmetry (Σ = 0), a finding relevant for understanding pseudospin symmetry in nuclear mean fields as studied by Ginocchio and others. The results enable further investigation of pseudospin symmetry in more realistic nuclear potentials beyond the harmonic oscillator.
