# Diffractive deeply virtual Compton scattering

> Diffractive deeply virtual Compton scattering (DiDVCS) is the process $γ^*(- Q^2) + N \rightarrow ρ^0 + γ^* (Q'^2)+ N'$, where N is a nucleon or light nucleus, in the kinematical regime of large rapidity gap between the $ρ^0$ and the final photon-nucleus system, and in the generalized Bjorken regime where both photon virtualities $Q^2$ and $ Q'^2$ are large. We show that this process has the unique virtue of combining the large diffractive cross sections at high energy with the tomographic ability of deeply virtual Compton scattering to scrutinize the quark and gluon content of nucleons and light nuclei. Its study at an electron-ion collider would enlighten the internal structure of hadrons.

## Key claim

Diffractive deeply virtual Compton scattering (DiDVCS) combines the large diffractive cross sections at high energy with the tomographic ability of deeply virtual Compton scattering to probe the quark and gluon content of nucleons and light nuclei, but the authors note in a later addition that a subsequent phenomenological study indicates the process seems very difficult to measure at future electron-ion colliders with their nominal luminosities.

## Method

The authors calculate the scattering amplitude for the process γ*(−Q²) + N → ρ⁰ + γ*(Q'²) + N' in the forward limit and large rapidity gap regime, using a k_T-factorization approach with two-Reggeized-gluon exchange in the t-channel. They factorize the amplitude into two impact factors: a known γ* → ρ impact factor and a newly calculated N → γ* N' impact factor expressed in terms of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) in the ERBL region (−ξ < x < ξ). The amplitude is then expressed in terms of diffractive Compton form factors ℋ^d(ξ,t) and ℰ^d(ξ,t), with the d²k and du integrations performed analytically for the asymptotic shape of the ρ meson distribution amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The ρ⁰ meson decay constant used is f_ρ⁰ = 216 MeV.
- The numerical estimate of the diffractive Compton form factors is shown at Q² = Q'² = 4 GeV² and t = −0.1 GeV².
- The amplitude scales like S_γN/(Q³ Q') f(Q/Q', ξ) at fixed t.
- The coupling constant C in the diffractive Compton form factor expression contains a factor 2⁹ π³ α_em C_F² α_S² f_ρ / (√2 Q Q' (N_c² − 1)).

## Context

This work extends the collinear factorization framework of deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and timelike Compton scattering (TCS), which have been shown to provide the best tool for 3-dimensional imaging of nucleon structure (Burkardt, 2000; Ralston, 2001; Diehl, 2002; Dupre, 2016). The process combines the diffractive electroproduction mechanism studied in the Regge-inspired k_T-factorization approach with BFKL evolution (Fadin, 1975; Kuraev, 1977; Balitsky, 1978) and the GPD-based tomography program. The authors note that the N → γ* N' impact factor is a new object, and the resulting diffractive Compton form factors are unique in that they probe GPDs only in the ERBL region (−ξ < x < ξ) with no contribution from gluon GPDs due to C-parity. The process is proposed for study at future electron-ion colliders, though a subsequent phenomenological study (Cosyn and Pire, 2021) suggests it may be very difficult to measure with nominal luminosities.
