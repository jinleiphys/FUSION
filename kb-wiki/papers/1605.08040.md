# Monte Carlo study of real time dynamics

> Monte Carlo studies involving real time dynamics are severely restricted by the sign problem that emerges from highly oscillatory phase of the path integral. In this letter, we present a new method to compute real time quantities on the lattice using the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism via Monte Carlo simulations. The key idea is to deform the path integration domain to a complex manifold where the phase oscillations are mild and the sign problem is manageable. We use the previously introduced "contraction algorithm" to create a Markov chain on this alternative manifold. We substantiate our approach by analyzing the quantum mechanical anharmonic oscillator. Our results are in agreement with the exact ones obtained by diagonalization of the Hamiltonian. The method we introduce is generic and in principle applicable to quantum field theory albeit very slow. We discuss some possible improvements that should speed up the algorithm.

## Key claim

The authors present a new Monte Carlo method for computing real-time correlators using the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism, where the path integration domain is deformed to a complex manifold via holomorphic gradient flow to mitigate the sign problem; however, the method is currently very slow and in principle applicable to quantum field theory only with further improvements.

## Method

The method complexifies the real field variables and uses holomorphic gradient flow to deform the integration domain from ℝ^N to a complex manifold Γ where the imaginary part of the action varies slowly. A Markov chain on Γ is generated via the "contraction algorithm," which parametrizes points on Γ by their pre-images in ℝ^N under the flow and uses a Metropolis algorithm with proposals adapted to the local curvature of the effective action. The phase from both the action and the Jacobian determinant is reweighted at the measurement stage.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing a = 0.2, with n₁ = 12 and n₂ = 2, giving t_max = 2.2 and β = 0.8.
- Parameters: ω = 1, λ = 24, and flow time T_flow = 0.2.
- The method achieved t_max ≈ 3β (approximately 2.4 in real time units) without the convergence issues of complex Langevin.
- Results required 3 × 10⁷ Metropolis steps for the presented correlators.

## Context

This work extends the Lefschetz thimble approach (Cristoforetti 2012, 2013; Fujii 2013; Tanizaki 2014) by avoiding the need to locate critical points and their thimbles a priori. It directly addresses the failure of the complex Langevin method (Berges 2005, 2006; Mizutani 2008) for real-time separations larger than the inverse temperature β, which is critical for computing transport coefficients in strongly coupled systems. The method enables future computation of real-time correlators in quantum field theory, provided improvements in proposal efficiency and Jacobian estimation are developed.
