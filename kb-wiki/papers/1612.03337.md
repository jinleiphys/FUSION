# Effect of meson cloud on the jet nuclear modification factor in $pA$ collisions

> We study the effect of the nucleon meson cloud on centrality dependence of the jet nuclear modification factor $R_{pA}$. We find that the meson-baryon Fock components may lead to a noticeable deviation of $R_{pA}$ from unity. Our results for $R_{pA}$ show the same tendency as that observed by ATLAS in $p+Pb$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV. The meson cloud suppresses the central jet events and enhances the peripheral jet events. But quantitatively the effect is somewhat smaller than in the data.

# Effect of meson cloud on the jet nuclear modification factor in pA collisions

## Key claim

The meson-baryon Fock components of the proton (with total weight up to 40%) can produce a noticeable deviation of the jet nuclear modification factor R_pA from unity in p+Pb collisions, suppressing central jet events and enhancing peripheral jet events; however, the quantitative effect is somewhat smaller than observed by ATLAS at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV, suggesting additional mechanisms are needed.

## Method

The author models the physical proton as a superposition of a bare nucleon and meson-baryon Fock states (primarily pi N, pi Delta, rho N, rho Delta), using the infinite momentum frame picture with a dipole form factor (Lambda=1.3 GeV) for the pion-nucleon vertex. The jet cross section is separated into one-body and meson-baryon contributions, and the underlying event activity in pA collisions is simulated within a Monte-Carlo Glauber wounded nucleon model that incorporates the meson cloud. The centrality dependence of R_pA arises because the charged multiplicity distributions for minimum-bias soft events and for jet events differ, due to the different probabilities of N+A versus MB+A collisions in jet production.

## Key numbers

- Total weight of meson-baryon Fock states in the physical nucleon: W_MB = 0.4 (40%)
- Dipole form factor cutoff parameter: Lambda = 1.3 GeV
- The meson spectrum is strongly peaked near fractional longitudinal momentum x ~ 0.3
- The transverse mean-square radius of the bare proton is reduced by a factor a ~ 0.88 due to the meson cloud
- The virtuality scale in alpha_s uses cQ with c = 0.265, following the PYTHIA event generator
- Collision energy: sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV for p+Pb collisions

## Context

This work extends the parton-level correlation mechanism proposed by Skokov (2012) for explaining the ATLAS centrality dependence of R_pPb, and addresses the same data as the Monte-Carlo analyses by Armesto (2015) and Majumder (2015) using PYTHIA and HIJING, which achieved only crude agreement. The meson cloud approach is conceptually similar to the partonic mechanism but uses nonperturbative meson-baryon Fock components, which have been studied in deep inelastic scattering by Thomas (1983) and others for flavor dependence of nucleon PDFs and the Gottfried sum rule. The results suggest that a better agreement with ATLAS data may require combining the meson cloud effect with correlations between hard and soft partons in the bare constituents, or including nonperturbative quark-gluon-pion anomalous chromomagnetic interactions related to instantons as discussed by Kochelev (2016).
