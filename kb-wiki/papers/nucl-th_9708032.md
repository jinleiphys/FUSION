# Large-basis shell-model calculation of 10C->10B Fermi matrix element

> We use a $4\hbarΩ$ shell-model calculation with a two-body effective interaction derived microscopically from the Reid93 potential to calculate the isospin-mixing correction for the 10C->10B superallowed Fermi transition. The effective interaction takes into account the Coulomb potential as well as the charge dependence of T=1 partial waves. Our results suggest the isospin- mixing correction $δ_{C}\approx 0.1 %$, which is compatible with previous calculations. The correction obtained in those calculations, performed in a $0\hbarΩ$ space, was dominated by deviation from unity of the radial overlap between the converted proton and the corresponding neutron. In the present calculation this effect is accommodated by the large model space. The obtained $δ_{C}$ correction is about a factor of four too small to obtain unitarity of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix with the present experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors use a large-basis shell-model calculation including up to 4ħΩ excitations to compute the isospin-mixing correction δC for the 10C→10B superallowed Fermi transition, finding δC ≈ 0.08-0.1% from the 4ħΩ calculation directly, but estimating a more realistic value of δC ≈ 0.12(3)% after accounting for expected increases from larger model spaces. This correction is about a factor of four too small to restore unitarity of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix with current experimental data.

## Method

The authors performed no-core shell-model calculations for A=10 nuclei in model spaces allowing up to 2ħΩ and 4ħΩ excitations, using a two-body effective interaction derived microscopically from the Reid93 nucleon-nucleon potential via the Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation method. The effective interaction included the Coulomb potential and the charge dependence of T=1 partial waves by deriving separate effective interactions for pn, pp, and nn systems, and employed a multi-valued effective interaction approach where different interactions were used for different ħΩ excitations. The Fermi matrix element M_F = ⟨10B,0+1|T_-|10C,0+1⟩ was computed in the m-scheme using the Many-Fermion-Dynamics Code, with dimensions of 581,740 for 10B and 430,137 for 10C in the 4ħΩ space.

## Key numbers

- Isospin-mixing correction δC ≈ 0.08-0.1% from the 4ħΩ calculation directly, with a final estimate of δC ≈ 0.12(3)% after accounting for expected increases from larger model spaces
- The 4ħΩ calculation used N_max = 8 for the two-nucleon model space, while the 2ħΩ calculation used N_max = 6
- The experimental ground state splitting between 10C and 10Be is 4.66 MeV; calculated values were 4.68, 4.83, and 4.94 MeV for ħΩ = 14, 15.5, and 17 MeV, respectively
- The splitting between the 0+1 states of 10C and 10B (experimentally 2.69 MeV) was overestimated by 8%, 11%, and 14% for ħΩ = 14, 15.5, and 17 MeV, respectively
- δC increased by approximately 0.03% when going from the 2ħΩ to the 4ħΩ model space
- Previous published value by Ormand and Brown (1995) was δC ≈ 0.15(9)%, composed of about 0.04% from configuration mixing and 0.09% from radial overlap mismatch

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model calculations of isospin-mixing corrections for superallowed Fermi transitions, which were performed in a 0ħΩ (single major oscillator shell) space and required separate treatment of configuration mixing (δ_IM) and radial overlap mismatch (δ_RO) contributions. The authors unify these two effects within a single large-basis calculation, using a microscopically derived effective interaction rather than phenomenological interactions. The results are compatible with the earlier estimates of Ormand and Brown (1995) but do not resolve the deviation from CKM matrix unitarity, and the authors note that preliminary data from Fujikawa (1996) for 10C suggested a smaller Ft value that might indicate an unaccounted-for correction.
