# Constraining models of initial state with $v_2$ and $v_3$ data from LHC and RHIC

> We present a combined analysis of elliptic and triangular flow data from LHC and RHIC using viscous relativistic hydrodynamics. Elliptic flow $v_2$ in hydrodynamics is proportional to the participant eccentricity $\varepsilon_2$ and triangular flow is proportional to the participant triangularity $\varepsilon_3$, which means $v_n=C_n\varepsilon_n$, where $C_n$ is the linear response coefficient in harmonic n. Experimental data for $v_2$ and $v_3$ combined with hydrodynamic calculations of $C_n$ thus provide us with the rms values of initial anisotropies $\varepsilon_2$ and $\varepsilon_3$. By varying free parameters in the hydro calculation (in particular the shear viscosity), we obtain an allowed band in the (rms $\varepsilon_2$, rms $\varepsilon_3$) plane. Comparison with Monte-Carlo models of the initial state allows us to exclude several of these models. We illustrate that the effect of changing the granularity of the initial state is similar to changing the medium properties, making these effects difficult to disentangle.

## Key claim

By combining experimental elliptic and triangular flow data with viscous hydrodynamic calculations, the authors extract the root-mean-square values of initial-state eccentricity and triangularity, and show that the ratio of these moments can exclude several Monte Carlo models of the initial state even when the shear viscosity is treated as an unknown free parameter.

## Method

The authors performed 2+1D viscous relativistic hydrodynamic calculations with varying shear viscosity (η/s from 0 to 0.24), thermalization time (0.5 fm/c and 1 fm/c), freeze-out ansatz (linear and quadratic), and weighting scheme (energy-density vs. entropy-density) to compute the linear response coefficients C₂ and C₃. They then used Eq. (1) to extract the root-mean-square values of the initial participant eccentricity ε₂ and triangularity ε₃ from experimental integrated flow data from ALICE (LHC) and PHENIX (RHIC), and compared these extracted values with predictions from five Monte Carlo initial-state models: MC-Glauber, MC-KLN, MCrcBK, DIPSY, and IP Glasma.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity η/s was varied from 0 to 0.24 in steps of 0.04 across all hydrodynamic calculations.
- Thermalization time was varied between 0.5 fm/c and 1 fm/c.
- For the MC-Glauber model, the Gaussian smearing width σ was varied over values 0 fm, 0.4 fm, 0.8 fm, and 1.2 fm.
- The extracted rms eccentricities were found to obey the relation √⟨ε₂²⟩ / (√⟨ε₃²⟩)^k = C, with k = 0.6 for LHC and k = 0.5 for RHIC.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Alver et al. (2010) and Adare et al. (2011), who noted that some initial-state models could not simultaneously fit v₂ and v₃ data even when tuning η/s. The authors provide a systematic method to constrain initial-state models by extracting the allowed band in the (rms ε₂, rms ε₃) plane, enabling direct comparison with models such as MC-Glauber (PHOBOS, Miller et al. 2007), MC-KLN (Drescher et al. 2007), MCrcBK (Dumitru et al. 2012), DIPSY (Flensburg et al. 2011), and IP Glasma (Schenke et al. 2012). The results show that MC-Glauber and MC-KLN are excluded at LHC energies, while MC-KLN and MCrcBK are excluded at RHIC energies.
