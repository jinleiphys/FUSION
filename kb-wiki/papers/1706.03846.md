# Nucleon-deuteron scattering with the JISP16 potential

> The nucleon-nucleon J-matrix Inverse Scattering Potential JISP16 is applied to elastic nucleon-deuteron (Nd) scattering and the deuteron breakup process at the lab. nucleon energies up to 135 MeV. The formalism of the Faddeev equations is used to obtain 3N scattering states. We compare predictions based on the JISP16 force with data and with results based on various NN interactions: the CD Bonn, the AV18, the chiral force with the semi-local regularization at the 5th order of the chiral expansion and with low-momentum interactions obtained from the CD Bonn force as well as with the predictions from the combination of the AV18 NN interaction and the Urbana IX 3N force. JISP16 provides a satisfactory description of some observables at low energies but strong deviations from data as well as from standard and chiral potential predictions with increasing energy. However, there are also polarization observables at low energies for which the JISP16 predictions differ from those based on the other forces by a factor of two. The reason for such a behavior can be traced back to the P-wave components of the JISP16 force. At higher energies the deviations can be enhanced by an interference with higher partial waves and by the properties of the JISP16 deuteron wave function. In addition, we compare the energy and angular dependence of predictions based on the JISP16 force with the results of the low-momentum forces obtained with different values of the momentum cutoff parameter. We found that such low-momentum forces can be employed to interpret the Nd elastic scattering data only below some specific energy which depends on the cutoff parameter. Since JISP16 is defined in a finite oscillator basis, it has properties similar to low momentum interactions and its application to the description of Nd scattering data is limited to a low momentum transfer region.

## Key claim

JISP16 provides a satisfactory description of some nucleon-deuteron scattering observables at low energies but shows strong deviations from data and from standard and chiral potential predictions with increasing energy; however, even at low energies some polarization observables differ from other force predictions by a factor of two, traced to the P-wave components of the JISP16 force.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations in momentum space for elastic nucleon-deuteron scattering and deuteron breakup, using the JISP16 nucleon-nucleon potential defined in a finite harmonic oscillator basis. They compared JISP16 predictions with results from the CD Bonn, AV18, chiral N4LO (semi-local regularization, R=0.9 fm), AV18+Urbana IX, and low-momentum interactions derived from CD Bonn via the Okubo unitary transformation with cutoff parameters from 1.5 to 5.0 fm^-1. Calculations included all partial waves with j ≤ 4 and J ≤ 25/2.

## Key numbers

- JISP16 deuteron ground state energy: -2.2246 MeV (experimental value -2.22457 MeV)
- JISP16 triton ground state energy: -8.369 MeV (experimental value -8.482 MeV)
- JISP16 3S1 deuteron state probability: 96.02%; 3D1 probability: 3.98%
- At E=13 MeV, JISP16 differential cross section predictions are approximately 10% below other overlapping predictions around the minimum
- At E=65 MeV, JISP16 cross section predictions are approximately 15% above data around the minimum
- JISP16 deuteron analyzing power iT11 at E=5 MeV and E=13 MeV is about twice as large as predictions from other interactions
- JISP16 3S1 deuteron wave function becomes negligible above p ≈ 2.5 fm^-1, while other potentials approach zero above p=5 fm^-1
- JISP16 triton kinetic energy expectation value: 27.399 MeV; AV18: 46.805 MeV; chiral N4LO: 49.124 MeV

## Context

This work extends tests of the JISP16 potential, previously successful in describing nuclear structure up to 16O and nucleon-alpha scattering, into the three-nucleon continuum. It contrasts with high-precision potentials like CD Bonn (Machleidt 2001) and AV18 (Wiringa 1995) that require explicit three-nucleon forces (e.g., Urbana IX) to describe binding energies and higher-energy scattering. The results show that JISP16, despite reproducing the triton binding energy without a three-nucleon force, fails to describe many Nd scattering observables, especially polarization observables, even at low energies. The comparison with low-momentum interactions (Fujii 2004) shows that JISP16's applicability is limited to low momentum transfer, similar to such softened interactions.
