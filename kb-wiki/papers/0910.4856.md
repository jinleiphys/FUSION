# Radial Flow from Electromagnetic Probes and Signal of Quark Gluon Plasma

> A first attempt has been made to extract the evolution of radial flow from the analysis of the experimental data on electromagnetic probes experimentally measured at SPS and RHIC energies. The $p_T$ spectra of photons and dileptons measured by WA98 and NA60 collaborations respectively at CERN-SPS and the photon spectra obtained by PHENIX collaboration at BNL-RHIC have been used to constrain the theoretical models, rendering the outcome of the analysis largely model independent. We argue that the variation of the radial velocity with invariant mass is indicative of a phase transition from initially produced partons to hadrons at SPS and RHIC energies.

## Key claim
The variation of radial flow velocity with invariant mass of lepton pairs, extracted from the ratio of photon to dilepton transverse momentum spectra, is indicative of a phase transition from partons to hadrons at SPS and RHIC energies, but the extraction relies on the assumption that the absolute normalization of the dilepton spectra for lower mass windows is not essential for determining the flow slope.

## Method
The authors used relativistic hydrodynamics with longitudinal boost invariance and cylindrical symmetry to model the space-time evolution of the fireball, with initial conditions constrained by measured hadronic multiplicities and the equation of state taken from lattice QCD (MILC collaboration). They calculated thermal photon and dilepton production rates from both the quark-gluon plasma and hadronic phases, including chemical non-equilibrium effects via hadronic chemical potentials, and compared the results to experimental data from WA98 (photons at SPS), NA60 (dileptons at SPS), and PHENIX (photons and dileptons at RHIC). The radial flow velocity was extracted by parametrizing the ratio of photon to dilepton spectra as a function of transverse momentum and invariant mass, using effective temperature parameters to solve for the average radial velocity and average temperature.

## Key numbers
- Initial temperature for Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=17.3 GeV (0-6% centrality): T_i=200 MeV with thermalization time tau_i=1.0 fm and dN/dy=700.
- Initial temperatures for Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV: T_i=227 MeV (0-20% centrality, dN/dy=496), T_i=203 MeV (20-40% centrality, dN/dy=226), T_i=200 MeV (minimum bias, dN/dy=184), all with tau_i=0.6 fm.
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature: T_f=140 MeV for all hadrons.
- Effective temperatures from ratio parametrization at SPS: T_eff=263 MeV for M=0.75 GeV and T_eff=243 MeV for M=1.2 GeV.
- Radial flow velocity at SPS is larger in the hadronic phase (around the rho meson mass) and smaller in the QGP phase (below rho peak and above phi peak); at RHIC, radial flow velocities are larger than at SPS due to higher initial pressure.

## Context
This work extends prior efforts to extract radial flow from hadronic spectra (Heinz, Hung, Hirano; Pasi, Teaney for reviews) by instead using electromagnetic probes, which carry information from all stages of the fireball evolution. The ratio method builds on earlier proposals by Sinha, JKN1, and JKN2 to cancel model-dependent uncertainties in individual spectra. The non-monotonic variation of radial flow with invariant mass is interpreted as a signature of the QGP-to-hadron phase transition, similar to the behavior observed for elliptic flow of photons as a function of p_T (v2photon). The results enable future simultaneous measurements of photons and dileptons in heavy ion collisions to quantify the time evolution of collectivity and test the equation of state from lattice QCD.
