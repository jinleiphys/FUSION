# Isospin effects in a covariant transport approach to spallation reactions: Analysis of $p+Fe$ and $Pb$ reactions at 0.8, 1.2 and 1.6 GeV

> We have investigated the influence of different non-linear relativistic mean field models ($NL$, $NLρ$ and $NLρδ$) on spallation neutrons for p+Fe and Pb reactions at 0.8, 1.2 and 1.6 GeV by means of a relativistic Boltzmann Uehling Uhlenbeck (RBUU) approach plus a statistical multifragmentation (SM) decay model. We find that the "evaporation shoulder", i.e. the neutron energy spectrum from 3 to 30 MeV, almost for any emission angle is quite sensitive to the isospin part of the mean field. For the more neutron-rich Pb-target the evaporation component can be directly related to the low density behavior on the symmetry energy in the thermal expansion phase of the excited compound system. It turns out that the spallation data for the reactions under study are shown to be more consistent with RBUU+SM employing the $NLρ$ effective lagrangian.

## Key claim

The "evaporation shoulder" (neutron energies from 3 to 30 MeV) is sensitive to the isospin part of the relativistic mean field, and for the neutron-rich Pb target this component can be directly related to the low-density behavior of the symmetry energy during the thermal expansion phase of the excited compound system. The spallation data are more consistent with the RBUU+SM model employing the NLρ effective lagrangian, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific choice of transition time (75 fm/c) and the fixed symmetry coefficient (γ_sym = 25 MeV) in the statistical multifragmentation model.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) transport model with three different non-linear relativistic mean field parameter sets (NL, NLρ, and NLρδ) to simulate the dynamical stage of p+Fe and p+Pb reactions at 0.8, 1.2, and 1.6 GeV. The transport stage was run up to a transition time of 75 fm/c, after which a statistical multifragmentation (SM) decay model was applied as an afterburner to describe the evaporation of neutrons from the excited prefragments. The calculations used 500 events per initial condition with 40 test particles per nucleon, and the results were compared to experimental double differential cross sections of emitted neutrons at angles from 0° to 160°.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy at saturation density is fixed to 30.7 MeV for both NLρ and NLρδ models.
- The NL model (no isovector mesons) gives an incompressibility K = 200 MeV, nucleon effective mass m* = 0.83M, and saturation density ρ₀ = 0.148 fm⁻³.
- The transition time t_tr = 75 fm/c was chosen based on analysis showing that calculations with t_tr from 60 to 100 fm/c yield equivalent results for neutron energies E_n ≤ 30 MeV.
- The symmetry coefficient γ_sym = 25 MeV was kept fixed in the SM code, with tests showing that varying γ_sym from 5 to 25 MeV only caused a slight increase of low energy neutrons at E_n < 10 MeV for γ_sym = 5 MeV.
- For p+Pb at 1.2 GeV, nearly 20 nucleons are emitted at earlier times (between 20 and 40 fm/c) in the NLρ calculations.
- The quasi-inelastic peak is centered around 873 MeV at 0° and 760 MeV at 10°.

## Context

This work extends previous non-relativistic QMD studies by Gaitanos et al. (2008) that showed the evaporation component of neutron spectra is most sensitive to the symmetry potential, but here using a fully covariant relativistic framework with explicit isospin dependence in both the mean field and collision integral. The results enable discrimination between different density dependencies of the symmetry energy below saturation, with the NLρ model (giving a stiff, roughly linear increase of symmetry energy below saturation) providing the best agreement with data for the neutron-rich Pb target. The approach contrasts with simpler intranuclear cascade models that neglect mean field dynamics, and the findings are relevant for applications in accelerator technology, nuclear waste transmutation, and astrophysics.
