# $Ω_c$ excited states: a molecular approach with heavy-quark spin symmetry

> The LHCb Collaboration has recently discovered five excited $Ω_c$ states with masses between 3 and 3.1 GeV, four of them corroborated by the Belle Collaboration. We analyse the dynamical generation of these states within a molecular baryon-meson model that is consistent with both chiral and heavy-quark spin symmetries. Earlier predictions within this model found five $Ω_c$ states with masses below 3 GeV. Thus, in order to study the possible identification of any of these states with the experimental ones in the correct energy region, we explore two different regularization schemes, that is, a modified regularization subtraction method and a cutoff regularization scheme. We find that at least three of the dynamically generated states can be identified with the experimental ones and have spin-parity $J=1/2^-$ or $J=3/2^-$

# Ω_c excited states: a molecular approach with heavy-quark spin symmetry

## Key claim
At least three of the five excited Ω_c states recently observed by LHCb (and corroborated by Belle) can be identified as dynamically generated molecular states with spin-parity J=1/2^- or J=3/2^-, provided the regularization scheme is adjusted from earlier predictions that placed all five states below 3 GeV.

## Method
The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the on-shell approximation using an s-wave baryon-meson potential derived from a SU(6)_lsf × HQSS extension of the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction, involving pseudoscalar and vector mesons together with low-lying 1/2^+ and 3/2^+ baryons. They compare two regularization schemes for the ultraviolet-divergent loop functions: a one-subtraction method at a scale μ and a common sharp-cutoff regulator Λ in momentum space, varying these parameters to shift the predicted masses upward into the experimental energy region.

## Key numbers
- Five Ω_c states were predicted earlier in Romanets (2012) with masses below 3 GeV: 2810.9 MeV (J=1/2), 2814.3 MeV (J=3/2), 2884.5 MeV (J=1/2), 2941.6 MeV (J=1/2), 2980.0 MeV (J=3/2)
- Using one-subtraction regularization with α=1.16, states d and e shift to 2999.9 MeV (J=1/2, width 0.06 MeV) and 3036.3 MeV (J=3/2, width 0 MeV), near experimental Ω_c(3000) at 3000.4 MeV and Ω_c(3050) at 3050.2 MeV
- Using a common UV cutoff Λ=1090 MeV, three states match experiment: state c at 2994.26 MeV (J=1/2, width 1.85 MeV) near Ω_c(3000); state b at 3048.7 MeV (J=3/2, width 0.0 MeV) near Ω_c(3050); state d at 3116.81 MeV (J=1/2, width 3.72 MeV) near Ω_c(3119) or Ω_c(3090)
- The LHCb experimental masses are: Ω_c(3000), Ω_c(3050), Ω_c(3066), Ω_c(3090), Ω_c(3119), plus a broad structure at 3188 MeV

## Context
This work extends the molecular baryon-meson model of Romanets (2012) and Garcia-Recio (2015) that previously predicted five Ω_c states below 3 GeV, by exploring alternative regularization schemes to match the LHCb discoveries (Aaij 2017) and Belle confirmations (Yelton 2017). The results differ from Montana (2017), who identified two J=1/2 states at 3050 and 3090 MeV, and Debastiani (2017), who found two J=1/2 states plus a J=3/2 state at 3119 MeV, due to different regularization and interaction matrices. The identification of states b and c with J=3/2 and J=1/2 places them in the same SU(6)_lsf × HQSS multiplets as the Λ_c(2595) and Λ_c(2625) resonances and the Λ_b(5912) and Λ_b(5920) narrow resonances found by LHCb (Aaij 2012).
