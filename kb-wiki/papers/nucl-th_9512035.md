# Isoscalar Roper Excitation in $p(α, α')$ Reactions in the $10 - 15$ $GeV$ Region

> Recent experiments at Saturne at $4$ $GeV$ showed that the $(α,α')$ reaction on the proton shows two distinctive peaks, which were associated to $Δ$ projectile excitation and Roper target excitation. A subsequent theoretical analysis has shown that this picture is qualitatively correct but there are important interference effects between the two mechanisms. Futhermore, at this energy the ratio of strengths for the Roper and $Δ$ peak is about $1/4$. In the present paper we show that by going to the $10 - 15$ $GeV$ region the interference effects become negligible, the signal for the Roper excitation is increased by more than an order of magnitude and the ratio of cross sections at the peaks for Roper and $Δ$ excitation becomes of the order of unity, thus making this range of energies ideal for studies of isoscalar Roper excitation.

## Key claim

By increasing the alpha-particle kinetic energy from 4 GeV to the 10-15 GeV region, the interference between the Delta projectile excitation and Roper target excitation mechanisms becomes negligible, the Roper signal increases by more than an order of magnitude, and the ratio of cross sections at the Roper and Delta peaks becomes of order unity, making this energy range ideal for studying isoscalar Roper excitation.

## Method

The authors used the same theoretical model previously applied to analyze the 4 GeV Saturne data, which includes pion and rho exchange with Landau-Migdal corrections for the Delta excitation mechanism and an effective sigma exchange (incorporating omega meson exchange and nuclear correlations) for the isoscalar Roper excitation mechanism. They evaluated the double differential cross section for the p(alpha,alpha') reaction at alpha kinetic energies of 10 GeV and 15 GeV, including the Roper decay into pi N (65% of the free width) and pi pi N channels, with the helium-4 nuclear form factor incorporating eikonal distortion of proton and pion waves.

## Key numbers

- At 4 GeV, the ratio of strengths for the Roper peak to the Delta peak is about 1/4.
- At 15 GeV, the cross section for Roper excitation is increased by more than an order of magnitude compared to the 4 GeV case.
- At 10 GeV, the Roper and Delta peaks each have a strength of about 4 mb/(sr MeV).
- At 15 GeV, the ratio of the squared helium form factor at 15 GeV to that at 4.2 GeV is about 4 to 5 at an energy transfer omega of 550 MeV (where the Roper peak appears), while the increase is moderate at omega around 200 MeV (where the Delta peak appears).
- The Roper resonance decays into pi N with a branching fraction of about 65% of its free width.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical analysis of Hirenzaki et al. (1995) of the Saturne experiment at 4 GeV (Morsch et al., 1992), where two peaks were identified as Delta projectile excitation and Roper target excitation. The proposed higher-energy experiments could be performed at the Synchrophasotron of Dubna (up to 4 GeV/A) or the new Nuclotron (up to 6 GeV/A), and would provide new information about the Roper resonance, which quark models have difficulty explaining and which has been suggested as a monopole (breathing mode) excitation of the nucleon by Morsch et al. (1992).
