# Dilepton production in nucleon-nucleon collisions reexamined

> We present a fully relativistic and gauge invariant framework for calculating the cross sections of dilepton production in nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) collisions which is based on the meson-exchange approximation for the $NN$ scattering amplitudes. Predictions of our model are compared with those of other covariant models that have been used earlier to describe this reaction. Our results are also compared with those of the semiclassical models which are employed to get the input elementary cross sections in the transport model calculations of the dilepton production in nucleus-nucleus collisions. It is found that cross sections obtained within the semiclassical and quantum mechanical models differ noticeably from each other.

## Key claim

The cross sections for dilepton production in nucleon-nucleon collisions calculated within semiclassical models (soft photon approximation and Dalitz decay model) differ noticeably from those predicted by a fully relativistic, gauge-invariant quantum mechanical model, and there is no overall multiplicative factor that differentiates the results of various quantum mechanical models from each other.

## Method

The authors constructed a fully relativistic and gauge-invariant framework based on the meson-exchange approximation for nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes, using a pseudoscalar (PS) nucleon-nucleon-pion vertex to avoid ambiguities from contact terms required with pseudovector coupling. They computed Feynman diagrams for dilepton emission before, after, and during the nucleon-nucleon collision, including contributions from intermediate nucleons and delta isobars, and restored gauge invariance in the presence of hadronic form factors via the Ward-Takahashi identity and a modified meson current. The results were compared with the models of de Jong et al. (1996) and Kaptari & Kämpfer (2006), as well as with semiclassical soft photon approximation (SPA) and Dalitz decay (DDD) models.

## Key numbers

- The authors' pp bremsstrahlung cross sections are lower than those of Kaptari & Kämpfer (2006) by factors of 2–4 for dilepton invariant mass values below 0.6 GeV at both 1.04 GeV and 2.09 GeV beam energies.
- The pn bremsstrahlung cross sections of Kaptari & Kämpfer (2006) are larger than the authors' by factors ranging between 2 and 3 at both 1.04 GeV and 2.09 GeV beam energies.
- The delta isobar contributions of Kaptari & Kämpfer (2006) are larger than the authors' by factors of approximately 2 at smaller invariant mass values at 1.04 GeV beam energy.
- The authors' cross sections are about 10% larger than those reported in their earlier work (Shyam 2003).
- The de Jong et al. (1996) pp results are larger than the authors' for invariant mass M < 0.25 GeV at 1.04 GeV beam energy, and almost identical at 2.09 GeV in that mass region; for M > 0.25 GeV, the de Jong et al. results are smaller at both energies.

## Context

This work addresses the "DLS-puzzle" – the persistent discrepancy between transport model calculations and dilepton yields measured by the DLS experiment in nucleus-nucleus collisions at 1–2 GeV/nucleon – by reexamining the elementary nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung cross sections that serve as input to those transport models. The authors extend the earlier covariant model of Shyam (2003) by using a pseudoscalar pion-nucleon coupling to avoid gauge-invariance ambiguities present in the pseudovector-based model of Kaptari & Kämpfer (2006), and compare with the T-matrix approach of de Jong et al. (1996). The results enable proper interpretation of upcoming HADES data on elementary pp and pn dilepton production and provide guidance for transport model calculations of dilepton production in nucleus-nucleus collisions.
