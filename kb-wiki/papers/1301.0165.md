# Is anisotropic flow really acoustic?

> The flow harmonics for charged hadrons ($v_{n}$) and their ratios $(v_n/v_2)_{n\geq 3}$, are studied for a broad range of transverse momenta ($p_T$) and centrality ($\text{cent}$) in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 2.76$ TeV. They indicate characteristic scaling patterns for viscous damping consistent with the dispersion relation for sound propagation in the plasma produced in the collisions. These scaling properties are not only a unique signature for anisotropic expansion modulated by the specific shear viscosity ($η/s$), they provide essential constraints for the relaxation time, a distinction between two of the leading models for initial eccentricity, as well as an extracted $\left< η/s \right>$ value which is insensitive to the initial geometry model. These constraints could be important for a more precise determination of $η/s$.

## Key claim

The observed viscous damping of flow harmonics \(v_n\) and their ratios \((v_n/v_2)_{n\geq 3}\) in Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 2.76\) TeV follows scaling patterns consistent with the dispersion relation for sound propagation, but this conclusion is conditional on a parametrized viscous hydrodynamical framework and the validation of specific exponential damping forms (e.g., \(\propto \exp(-\beta' n^2)\)) that the paper tests against data.

## Method

The authors analyzed charged hadron flow harmonics \(v_n(p_T,\text{cent})\) measured by the ATLAS collaboration for Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 2.76\) TeV, using both event-plane and two-particle correlation methods. They divided the measured \(v_n\) by eccentricity moments \(\varepsilon_n\) computed from Monte Carlo Glauber simulations, then fit the resulting \(v_n/\varepsilon_n\) versus harmonic number \(n\) with an exponential form \(\propto \exp(-\beta' n^2)\) to extract the \(p_T\)-dependent viscous coefficient \(\beta'(\tilde{p}_T)\). They also performed simultaneous fits of the ratios \(v_n/v_2\) for \(n=3,4,5\) using Eq. (8) to extract \(\beta\), \(\alpha\), and the eccentricity ratios \(\varepsilon_n/\varepsilon_2\), and validated the system-size dependence via \(\ln(v_n/\varepsilon_n)\) versus \(1/\bar{R}\). Finally, they calibrated \(\beta\) against viscous hydrodynamical calculations with known \(\eta/s\) values to extract an average specific shear viscosity.

## Key numbers

- Extracted \(\alpha \sim 0.58\) and \(\beta \sim 0.12\) from fits to \(\beta'\) vs. \(p_T\) for the 20-30% centrality selection (Fig. 1e).
- Extracted \(\left< 4\pi\eta/s \right> \sim 2.2 \pm 0.2\) from the calibration of \(\beta\) against viscous hydrodynamical calculations for 0.1% central Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 2.76\) TeV (Fig. 4c).
- The \(v_n\) data used are for Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 2.76\) TeV, covering a broad range of transverse momenta (up to \(p_T \alt 3-4\) GeV/c) and centrality selections (e.g., 20-30% and 0-5% central).

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of anisotropic flow as an acoustic phenomenon driven by pressure gradients, building on prior viscous hydrodynamic calculations (e.g., Schenke (2011), Gale (2012)) that reproduce \(v_n\) measurements with small \(\eta/s\) values. It provides a data-driven method to extract constraints on the relaxation time \(\tau_R\) and to distinguish between the MC-Glauber and MC-KLN models for initial eccentricity, which have been a major source of theoretical uncertainty in \(\eta/s\) extractions (e.g., Song (2008), Luzum (2012)). The extracted \(\left< \eta/s \right>\) value, insensitive to the initial geometry model, enables more precise determination of the specific shear viscosity and can guide future hydrodynamic calculations.
