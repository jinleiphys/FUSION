# Cosmological CP odd axion field as the coherent Berry's phase of the Universe

> We consider a dark matter (DM) model offering a very natural explanation of the observed relation, $Ω_{\rm dark} \sim Ω_{\rm visible}$. This generic consequence of the model is a result of the common origin of both types of matter (DM and visible) which are formed during the same QCD transition. The masses of both types of matter in this framework are proportional to one and the same dimensional parameter of the system, $Λ_{\rm QCD}$. The focus of the present work is the detail study of the dynamics of the $\cal{CP}$-odd coherent axion field $a(x)$ just before the QCD transition. We argue that the baryon charge separation effect on the largest possible scales inevitably occurs as a result of merely existence of the coherent axion field in early Universe. It leads to preferential formation of one species of nuggets on the scales of the visible Universe where the axion field $a(x)$ is coherent. A natural outcome of this preferential evolution is that only one type of the visible baryons remains in the system after the nuggets complete their formation. This represents a specific mechanism on how the baryon charge separation mechanism (when the Universe is neutral, but visible part of matter consists the baryons only) replaces the conventional "baryogenesis" scenarios.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a coherent, Universe-scale CP-odd axion field inevitably generates a baryon charge separation effect before the QCD transition, leading to preferential formation of one species of quark nuggets; this asymmetry is claimed to be of order one and not sensitive to the axion mass or initial conditions, but the authors introduce an unknown coefficient c(T) of order one to parameterize the effect, acknowledging that precise dynamical computation is a hard problem of strongly coupled QCD at nonzero theta.

## Method

The authors study the dynamics of the coherent axion field θ(t) before the QCD transition, modeled as a coherent superposition of axions at rest with oscillatory behavior described by θ(t) ∼ C t^{-3/4} cos ∫ ω_a(t') dt'. They analyze how this CP-odd background field affects the evolution of quark nuggets and anti-nuggets formed from closed N_DW=1 axion domain wall bubbles, using analytical estimates and numerical computations of the difference in baryon charge accretion between nuggets and anti-nuggets. The disparity is parameterized by the coefficient c(T) in the relation B_antinuggets = c(T) · B_nuggets, and the authors argue that |c(T)| ∼ 1 is a robust consequence independent of the axion mass m_a(T) or the initial value θ_0.

## Key numbers

- The observed ratio Ω_dark ≈ 5 · Ω_visible corresponds to |B_visible| : |B_nuggets| : |B_antinuggets| ≈ 1 : 2 : 3, with net baryonic charge zero.
- The nuggets carry very large baryon charge |B| ≳ 10^{25}, corresponding to an axion mass m_a ≈ 10^{-4} eV.
- The effective interaction cross-section per mass is σ/M ∼ 10^{-10} cm^2/g, comparable to conventional WIMPs values.
- The damping time scale τ for bubble oscillations is of order τ ∼ 10^{-4} s, during which the Universe temperature drops by approximately a factor of ∼3.
- The formation temperature of nuggets is T_form ≈ 41 MeV, with chemical potential μ > μ_c in the colour superconducting phase.
- The QCD transition occurs at T_c ∼ 170 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Liang, 2016) on the evolution of individual quark nuggets, providing quantitative analysis of the global CP-violating baryon charge separation that was mentioned qualitatively in that earlier paper. The model builds on the original proposal (Zhitnitsky, 2002; Oaknin, 2003) that dark matter consists of quark nuggets formed during the QCD transition, and that matter-antimatter asymmetry results from CP-violating charge separation rather than conventional baryogenesis. The framework contrasts with conventional axion dark matter mechanisms (such as misalignment or domain wall decay) which are highly sensitive to parameters as Ω_axion ∼ θ_0^2 m_a^{-7/6}, whereas the present model's relation Ω_dark ∼ Ω_visible is claimed to be insensitive to such parameters. The work enables further study of how the coherent axion field generates the observed dark matter density without fine-tuning.
