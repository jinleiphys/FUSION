# Rapid Thermalization by Baryon Injection in Gauge/Gravity Duality

> Using the AdS/CFT correspondence for strongly coupled gauge theories, we calculate thermalization of mesons caused by a time-dependent change of a baryon number chemical potential. On the gravity side, the thermalization corresponds to a horizon formation on the probe flavor brane in the AdS throat. Since heavy ion collisions are locally approximated by a sudden change of the baryon number chemical potential, we discuss implication of our results to RHIC and LHC experiments, to find a rough estimate of rather rapid thermalization time-scale t_{th} < 1 [fm/c]. We also discuss universality of our analysis against varying gauge theories.

## Key claim

Using the AdS/CFT correspondence, the authors calculate that meson thermalization caused by a time-dependent change of baryon number chemical potential occurs on a timescale \(t_{\rm th} < 1\) fm/c for RHIC parameters, but this estimate applies only to the meson sector on a probe flavor brane and does not include glueball thermalization or back-reaction effects.

## Method

The authors model heavy ion collisions as a sudden change in baryon number chemical potential, encoded in the gravity dual by throwing fundamental strings (F-strings) from the AdS boundary into the bulk. They solve the Born-Infeld action on a probe D7-brane in \(AdS_5 \times S^5\) geometry to obtain a time-dependent gauge field configuration, then compute the effective metric for scalar meson fluctuations and locate the apparent horizon on the D7-brane, which signals thermalization in the dual field theory.

## Key numbers

* For RHIC parameters (Au-Au, \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \sim 200\) GeV, \(\gamma \sim 100\), \(n_{\rm B} \sim 2\gamma n_{\rm N}\) with \(n_{\rm N}\sim 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(1/w \sim 0.1\) fm/c), the thermalization time-scale is \(t_{\rm th} < 1\) fm/c.
* For LHC parameters (Pb-Pb, \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.7\) TeV, \(\gamma \sim 1000\), \(1/w \sim 0.01\) fm/c), the thermalization time-scale is \(t_{\rm th} \lesssim \mathcal{O}(0.1)\) fm/c.
* The generic thermalization time-scale scaling is \(t_{\rm th} \sim \left( \lambda / (n_{\rm B}^2 w^k) \right)^{1/(6+k)}\) for \(k \ge 0\), where \(\lambda\) is the 't Hooft coupling, \(n_{\rm B}\) is the maximum baryon density, and \(w\) is the inverse variation timescale of the baryon chemical potential.
* For the baryonic matter formation example, the thermalization time seen by the boundary observer is \(t_{\rm th} \sim \left( \lambda / (n_{\rm B}^2 w^2) \right)^{1/8}\) when \(t_{\rm th} w \lesssim 1\), or \(t_{\rm th} \sim \left( \lambda / n_{\rm B}^2 \right)^{1/6}\) when \(1/w \ll \left( \lambda / (n_{\rm B}^2 w^2) \right)^{1/8}\).

## Context

This work extends holographic thermalization studies, previously focused on glueball sectors (Janik 2006, Chesler 2008, Bhattacharyya 2009), to the meson sector on a probe flavor brane using the D3-D7 system of Karch and Katz (2002). It provides a framework for computing thermalization due to baryon charge injection, which had not been proposed before, and the resulting rapid thermalization timescale (\(t_{\rm th} < 1\) fm/c) is consistent with hydrodynamic simulation requirements (\(t_{\rm th} < 2\) fm/c) from Hirano (2001), Kolb (2000), Teaney (2001), Huovinen (2001), and Heinz (2002). The results enable future studies of flavor thermalization in more realistic QCD-like theories and suggest universality across varying gauge theories.
