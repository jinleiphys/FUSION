# Fractals, non-extensive statistics and QCD

> In this work we analyse how scaling properties of Yang-Mills field theory manifest as self-similarity of truncated n-point functions by scale evolution. The presence of such structures, which actually behaves as fractals, allow for recurrent non-perturbative calculation of any vertex. Some general properties are indeed independent of the perturbative order, what simplifies the non-perturbative calculations. We show that for sufficiently high perturbative orders a statistical approach can be used, the non extensive statistics is obtained, and the Tsallis index, $q$, is deduced in terms of the field theory parameters. The results are applied to QCD in the one-loop approximation, where $q$ can be calculated, resulting in a good agreement with the value obtained experimentally. We discuss how this approach allows to understand some intriguing experimental findings in high energy collisions, as the behavior of multiplicity against collision energy, long-tail distributions and the fractal dimension observed in intermittency analysis.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the scaling properties of Yang-Mills field theory lead to self-similar (fractal) structures in truncated n-point functions, and that for sufficiently high perturbative orders this fractal behavior gives rise to Tsallis non-extensive statistics, with the Tsallis index q derived from field theory parameters rather than being a free parameter.

## Method

The authors analyze how the Callan-Symanzik equation and renormalization group scaling of Yang-Mills fields produce self-similarity in vertex functions across different energy scales. They develop a statistical description of partonic states by considering the trace of the evolution operator and counting configurations of effective partons with fixed particle number N after n interactions. The self-similarity condition forces the energy distribution to follow a q-exponential function, and the beta function for the effective coupling is derived and compared to the standard QCD beta function at one-loop order.

## Key numbers

- The Tsallis index q = 1.14 is calculated from QCD parameters using N_c = 3 and N_f/2 = 3, giving (11/3)c_1 - (4/3)c_2 = 7
- The experimentally determined value from data analysis is q = 1.14 ± 0.01, showing agreement between theory and experiment
- The beta function derived from the fractal approach gives β_g = -(1/(16π²))(1/(q-1)) g^{Ñ+1} with Ñ = 2
- The effective coupling shows asymptotic freedom with g → 0 as μ → ∞, and the beta function peaks around μ/λ_o = 40

## Context

This work extends the concept of thermofractals introduced by Deppman (2016) and studied in detail by Deppman, Frederico, Megias, and Menezes, which connects fractal structures to non-extensive statistics. It also relates to Hagedorn's self-consistent thermodynamics for high energy collisions (Hagedorn, 1965) and its extension to non-extensive statistics by Deppman (2012). The approach enables understanding of experimental findings in high energy collisions including multiplicity dependence on collision energy, long-tail distributions, and fractal dimensions observed in intermittency analysis, while providing a first-principles derivation of the Tsallis index from QCD parameters.
