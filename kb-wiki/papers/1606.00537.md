# Study for (anti)hypertriton and light (anti)nuclei productions in high energy collisions at 200 GeV

> We use the parton and hadron cascade (PACIAE) model and the dynamically constrained phase-space coalescence (DCPC) model to investigate the productions of (anti)hypertriton and light (anti)nuclei generated by 0-10% centrality $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C, $^{24}$Mg+$^{24}$Mg, $^{40}$Ca+$^{40}$Ca, and $^{64}$Cu+$^{64}$Cu collisions at $\sqrt{S_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV with $|y|$ $<$ 1.5 and $p_{T}$ $<$ 5. We study the yield ratios of the antiparticle to particle and the rapidity distributions of the different (anti)nuclei. We find that the amounts of antimatter produced are significantly lower than that of the corresponding particles, the results of theoretical models are well consistent with the PHOBOS data. The yield ratios of the particle to antiparticle in different transverse momentum regions are also given, and we find that the ratios are raise with the increase of the transverse momentum.

## Key claim

The PACIAE + DCPC model reproduces the PHOBOS experimental data for antiparticle-to-particle yield ratios in light nucleus-nucleus collisions at 200 GeV, but the abstract's claim of "well consistent" results applies specifically to the ratios of hadrons (pions, kaons, protons, etc.) and not to the (anti)hypertriton and light (anti)nuclei yields, which are purely model predictions with no experimental comparison provided.

## Method

The authors used the parton and hadron cascade (PACIAE) model to simulate the final-state particles from 0-10% central collisions of four symmetric systems (C+C, Mg+Mg, Ca+Ca, Cu+Cu) at 200 GeV. They then applied the dynamically constrained phase-space coalescence (DCPC) model to form (anti)hypertriton and light (anti)nuclei from the final-state nucleons, hyperons, and their antiparticles, imposing constraints on invariant mass and spatial separation (diameter D0) of the constituent particles. Model parameters (K factor, strange production parameters parj(1), parj(2), parj(3)) were tuned by fitting PHENIX data for p and pbar in Cu+Cu at 62.4 GeV and STAR data for Lambda and anti-Lambda in Cu+Cu at 200 GeV.

- Integrated yield dN/dy for deuteron in Cu+Cu at 200 GeV: 3.835E-02; for antideuteron: 2.397E-02
- Integrated yield dN/dy for hypertriton in Cu+Cu at 200 GeV: 2.783E-06; for antihypertriton: 1.207E-06
- Integrated yield dN/dy for triton in Cu+Cu at 200 GeV: 4.181E-06; for antitriton: 2.163E-06
- Integrated yield dN/dy for He-3 in Cu+Cu at 200 GeV: 3.731E-06; for anti-He-3: 1.780E-06
- Integrated yield dN/dy for proton in Cu+Cu at 200 GeV: 7.48; for antiproton: 4.82
- Integrated yield dN/dy for Lambda in Cu+Cu at 200 GeV: 4.75; for anti-Lambda: 3.58
- Fitted model parameters: K = 3, parj(1) = 0.15, parj(2) = 0.45, parj(3) = 0.65; mass uncertainty Delta m = 0.0003 (units not specified but GeV implied)
- Rapidity range: |y| < 1.5; transverse momentum range: 0 < pT < 5 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies using the PACIAE + DCPC model, which had predicted light nuclei yields in pp collisions at 7 and 14 TeV (Li et al., 2015) and investigated hypernuclei production in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV (Li et al., 2015). It directly follows the STAR Collaboration's 2010 measurement of 70 +/- 17 antihypertritons and 157 +/- 30 hypertritons in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, and the 2011 STAR report of 18 He-4 counts. The results enable predictions for antimatter production yields in smaller collision systems (C+C through Cu+Cu) that could be tested by future RHIC or LHC fixed-target experiments, and provide baseline calculations for understanding the production of strangelets and hyperon-nucleon interactions relevant to neutron star physics.
