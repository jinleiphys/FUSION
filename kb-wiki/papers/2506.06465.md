# Perturbative QCD reveals the softening of matter in the cores of massive neutron stars

> The cores of neutron stars (NSs) contain the densest matter in the universe. Rapid advancements in neutron-star observations allow unprecedented empirical access to cold, ultra-dense Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) matter. The combination of these observations with theoretical calculations has revealed previously inaccessible features of the equation of state (EoS) and the QCD phase diagram. In this thesis, I demonstrate how perturbative-QCD calculations at asymptotically high baryon densities provide robust constraints on the EoS at neutron-star densities. The method for constraint propagation is based solely on thermodynamical causality, stability, and consistency of the EoS. By constructing a large ensemble of EoSs using Gaussian processes regression and incorporating it into a Bayesian inference of EoS, I demonstrate that the novel pQCD constraints go beyond those obtained from current astrophysical observations alone, forcing the EoS to soften at the maximum densities of stable neutron stars. This softening of the EoS can be interpreted as an indication of approximate conformal symmetry restoration, a sign of a first-order phase transition (FOPT), or potentially both. I show that the conformal symmetry restoration is consistent with the hypothesis of quark matter cores inside the most massive NSs. Although current astrophysical data and theoretical inputs cannot definitively distinguish between the two scenarios, they slightly favor the occurrence of a phase transition of some kind - whether a crossover to quark matter or a destabilizing FOPT - in the cores of the most massive neutron stars.

## Key claim

Perturbative QCD calculations at asymptotically high baryon densities provide robust constraints on the equation of state at neutron-star densities that go beyond those obtained from current astrophysical observations alone, forcing the equation of state to soften at the maximum densities of stable neutron stars. This softening can be interpreted as either approximate conformal symmetry restoration, a sign of a first-order phase transition, or potentially both, though current data slightly favor the occurrence of a phase transition of some kind in the cores of the most massive neutron stars.

## Method

The author constructed a large ensemble of equations of state using Gaussian process regression and incorporated them into a Bayesian inference framework, propagating perturbative QCD constraints from asymptotically high densities down to neutron-star densities using only thermodynamic causality, stability, and consistency conditions. The equations of state were constrained by chiral effective field theory calculations at low densities (up to 1.1-2 times nuclear saturation density), perturbative QCD calculations at high densities (above approximately 40 times nuclear saturation density), and astrophysical observations including NICER X-ray data, radio pulsar mass measurements, and gravitational wave tidal deformability constraints from LIGO/Virgo.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: n_sat = 0.16/fm^3
- Perturbative QCD calculations are applied at chemical potentials mu >= 2.6 GeV, corresponding to number densities n_pQCD >= 40 n_sat
- The renormalization scale parameter X = 3*Lambda_bar/(2*mu) is varied in the range X in [1/2, 2] to estimate theoretical uncertainties
- Color superconducting gap estimates at non-asymptotic densities range from 50-150 MeV
- Neutron star period stability measured to 10^-10 to 10^-21 precision in pulsar observations

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kurkela (2014) and Annala (2019) who attempted to interpolate between chiral effective field theory and perturbative QCD limits, but goes beyond by systematically demonstrating that pQCD constraints nontrivially affect the equation of state inference even though neutron stars collapse at densities much lower than 40 n_sat. The framework enables quantitative assessment of whether quark matter cores exist inside the most massive neutron stars and whether a first-order phase transition occurs within the density range reached in neutron star cores, building on the model-agnostic equation of state generation methods developed by Kurkela (2014) and others.
