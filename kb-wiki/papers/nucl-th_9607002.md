# Relations between fusion cross sections and average angular momenta

> We study the relations between moments of fusion cross sections and averages of angular momentum. The role of the centrifugal barrier and the target deformation in determining the effective barrier radius are clarified. A simple method for extracting average angular momentum from fusion cross sections is demonstrated using numerical examples as well as actual data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the average angular momentum \(\langle \ell \rangle\) can be reliably extracted from fusion cross section data using integral relations, provided the energy dependence of the effective barrier radius is properly accounted for, this dependence arises from both the centrifugal barrier (via a higher-order expansion of the transmission probability) and target deformation (via a phenomenological parametrization), and neglecting it leads to systematic underestimation of \(\langle \ell \rangle\).

## Method

The authors derive an integral expression linking \(\langle \ell \rangle\) to moments of the fusion cross section, starting from a partial-wave expansion and an improved approximation for the \(\ell\)-wave transmission probability that includes the next-to-leading-order term in \(\ell(\ell+1)\). They then incorporate target deformation effects using a two-level orientation model and introduce a phenomenological Fermi-function parametrization for the energy-dependent effective barrier radius \(\rho_0(E)\). The method is tested against numerical calculations from the IBMFUS code (which uses the interacting boson model and WKB penetrabilities) and applied to experimental data for the \(^{16}\text{O}+^{154}\text{Sm}\) system.

## Key numbers

- For the \(^{16}\text{O}+^{154}\text{Sm}\) system with a spherical target, the \(s\)-wave barrier height is \(V_{B0} \approx 59\ \text{MeV}\); the error in approximating \(q_1\) and \(q_2\) at 70 MeV is less than \(0.05\%\).
- In the spherical case, the effective radius parametrization \(R(E) = \eta R_B + (1-\eta) R_C\) works with \(\eta = 0.78\).
- For the deformed case, the width parameter \(W\) in the Fermi-function fit to \(\rho_0(E)\) is \(2 \pm 0.3\), with a mild increase with \(\beta\).
- For the \(^{16}\text{O}+^{154}\text{Sm}\) system at 65 MeV, the correction to the barrier distribution \(D(E')\) from the improved barrier-position expansion is about \(12\%\) at 55 MeV.
- The constant-radius assumption underestimates \(\langle \ell \rangle\) by about one unit of \(\hbar\) for the deformed case, while the improved method agrees well with IBMFUS calculations at all energies.

## Context

This work extends earlier integral-relation approaches by Balantekin (1986) and Das (1991), which assumed a constant effective radius and were limited by poor subbarrier data or global fitting that washed out angular momentum features. It also contrasts with the barrier-distribution method of Rowley (1991), which requires differentiation of data and is model-dependent. The results enable a model-independent consistency check between fusion cross sections and measured average angular momenta, as demonstrated for the \(^{16}\text{O}+^{154}\text{Sm}\) system using data from Wei (1991) and Bies (1993).
