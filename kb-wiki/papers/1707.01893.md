# Richardson's solutions in the real- and complex-energy spectrum

> The constant pairing Hamiltonian holds exact solutions worked out by Richardson in the early Sixties. This exact solution of the pairing Hamiltonian regained interest at the end of the Nineties. The discret complex-energy states had been included in the Richardson's solutions by Hasegawa et al. [1]. In this contribution we reformulate the problem of determining the exact eigenenergies of the pairing Hamiltonian when the continuum is included through the single particle level density. The solutions with discret complex-energy states is recovered by analytic continuation of the equations to the complex energy plane. This formulation may be applied to loosely bound system where the correlations with the continuum-spectrum of energy is really important. Some details are given to show how the many-body eigenenergy emerges as sum of the pair-energies.

## Key claim

This paper reformulates the exact Richardson solution of the constant pairing Hamiltonian to include continuum states via the single-particle level density, recovering the discrete complex-energy representation of Hasegawa (2003) by analytic continuation of the equations to the complex energy plane; the derivation is pedagogical, showing how the many-body eigenenergy emerges as a sum of pair-energies, but the approach introduces some arbitrariness depending on the chosen density.

## Method

The author starts from the constant pairing Hamiltonian in a spherical-box representation, adopts the ansatz wavefunction of Von Delft (1999) for a 2n-body system, and solves the many-body Schrodinger equation by evaluating the commutator [H, product of pair-creation operators]. The condition that "dangerous" terms cancel yields the Richardson equations (Richardson, 1963). The formal limit R → ∞ replaces the discrete sum over single-particle states by an integral over the single-particle level density, using the Beth-Uhlenbeck (1937) density expressed in terms of phase-shift derivatives. Analytic continuation of the resonant part of the density to the complex energy plane converts the resonant-continuum integral into a pole term, recovering the complex-energy Richardson equations of Hasegawa (2003) and Id Betan (2012).

## Key numbers

- The many-body eigenenergy for a 2n-body system is given by ℰ_n = Σ_{ν=1}^n E_{J_ν}, where E_{J_ν} are the pair-energies (Eq. 6).
- The Richardson equations in the real continuum representation (Eq. 12) are: 1 - (G/2) Σ_{j_b σ} 1/(2ε_b - E_ν) - (G/2) ∫_0^∞ dε g(ε)/(2ε - E_ν) + 2G Σ_{ν≠ν'} 1/(E_ν - E_{ν'}) = 0.
- The complex-energy Richardson equations (Eq. 14) add a term -(G/2) Σ_{j_r σ} 1/(2ε_r - E_ν) for resonant states at complex energy ε_r = ε_r - i Γ_r/2, plus background integrals g_{Bckg}(ε) and g_{CxBckg}(ε).
- The resonant density is approximated by a Lorentzian: g_{Res}(ε) ≈ Σ_{j_r σ} (1/π) (Γ_r/2) / [(ε - ε_r)^2 + (Γ_r/2)^2].

## Context

This work extends the exact Richardson solution (Richardson, 1963) for the constant pairing Hamiltonian to include continuum correlations, complementing the pedagogical derivation of Von Delft (1999) and the complex-energy treatment of Hasegawa (2003) and Id Betan (2012). By using the Beth-Uhlenbeck (1937) density based on phase-shift derivatives, the approach naturally separates resonant from non-resonant continuum, enabling application to loosely bound systems where continuum correlations are important. The pole approximation (ignoring background densities) recovers the complex-energy Richardson equations of Hasegawa (2003), and the imaginary part of the resulting eigenenergy provides a measure of the approximation's quality.
