# Theoretical estimates of cross sections for neutron-nucleus collisions

> We construct an analytical model derived from nuclear reaction theory and having a simple functional form to demonstrate the quantitative agreement with the measured cross sections for neutron induced reactions. The neutron-nucleus total, reaction and scattering cross sections, for energies ranging from 5 to 700 MeV and for several nuclei spanning a wide mass range are estimated. Systematics of neutron scattering cross sections on various materials for neutron energies upto several hundred MeV are important for ADSS applications. The reaction cross sections of neutrons are useful for determining the neutron induced fission yields in actinides and pre-actinides. The present model based on nuclear reaction theory provides good estimates of the total cross section for neutron induced reaction.

# Theoretical estimates of cross sections for neutron-nucleus collisions

## Key claim

The authors construct an analytical model based on the nuclear Ramsauer effect, derived from optical model and WKB approximations, that provides global fits to neutron total cross sections for energies from 5 to 700 MeV across a wide mass range, but the model's quantitative agreement is demonstrated primarily for total cross sections, with scattering and reaction cross sections shown as predictions without direct experimental validation for most nuclei.

## Method

The authors use a nuclear Ramsauer model where the forward scattering amplitude is summed over partial waves up to a maximum angular momentum, assuming the complex phase shift is independent of angular momentum. The real part of the phase shift is calculated using a WKB approximation for a square well potential, while the attenuation factor is derived from the imaginary potential and the average chord length of a neutron passing through the nucleus (4R/3). The real potential includes isoscalar, isovector, and energy-dependent terms, and the imaginary potential is parametrized with a constant term plus an energy-dependent term proportional to sqrt(E+V). Model parameters are obtained by nonlinear least-squares fitting to experimental neutron total cross section data.

## Key numbers

- Energy range: 5 to 700 MeV for neutron incident energies
- Nuclear radius parameter r0 fitted to 1.378 A^gamma fm, where gamma = 7.93 x 10^-3
- Real potential parameters: V0 = 46.51, V1 = 6.74, V2 = -117.52, V_E = -3.22 (all in MeV)
- Imaginary potential parameters: W0 = 5.293 MeV, W_E = 33.88 x 10^-2
- Beta0 = 0.5928, alpha0 fixed at 0.2929
- Channel radius parametrization coefficients: r10 = -22.98 x 10^-3, r11 = 10.27 x 10^-2, r2 = 23.22 x 10^-2
- Calculations performed at neutron incident energy intervals of 1 MeV
- Total, scattering, and reaction cross sections plotted for 15 target nuclei: 238U, 232Th, 209Bi, 208Pb, 197Au, 186W, 184W, 182W, 181Ta, 93Nb, 90Zr, 59Co, 55Mn, 40Ca, and 31P, plus predictions for 239Pu

## Context

This work extends the nuclear Ramsauer model approach previously applied by Mukhopadhyay, Lahiri and Basu (2010) by treating the imaginary part of the nuclear potential explicitly rather than through an ad-hoc attenuation parameter. The model provides an alternative to global optical potential calculations such as those by Koning and Delaroche (2003), which are limited to energies up to 150-200 MeV. The results are intended for applications in accelerator-driven sub-critical systems (ADSS) neutronics, radioactive ion beam (RIB) production, and as inputs to Hauser-Feshbach calculations and intranuclear cascade codes such as MCNPX.
