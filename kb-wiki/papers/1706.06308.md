# Improved version of simplified method for including tensor effect in cluster models

> The tensor contribution can be directly incorporated in the cluster model in a simplified way. In conventional $α$ cluster models, the contribution of the non-central interactions exactly cancels because of the antisymmetrization effect and spatial symmetry of $α$ clusters. The mixing of breaking components of $α$ clusters to take into account the spin-orbit and tensor effects is needed. Previously we proposed a simplified method to include the spin-orbit effect, and also for the tensor part, a simplified model to directly take into account the contribution of the tensor interaction (called SMT) was introduced; however the contribution of the tensor interaction was quite limited. Here we improve SMT, which is called $i$SMT. Using newly proposed $i$SMT, the contribution of the tensor interaction in $^4$He is more than $-40$ MeV, four times larger than the previous version. The method is applied to four-$α$ cluster structure of $^{16}$O. In $^{16}$O, the tensor contribution is also large, and this is coming from the finite size effect for the distances among $α$ clusters with a tetrahedral configuration.

## Key claim

The improved Simplified Model for Tensor contribution (iSMT) achieves a tensor interaction contribution in $^4$He of more than $-40$ MeV, four times larger than the previous SMT version, by shifting the imaginary part of Gaussian center parameters (which corresponds to nucleon momentum) rather than the real part (spatial displacement).

## Method

The authors start with an $\alpha$ cluster in a $(0s)^4$ configuration and introduce 21 Slater determinants where the imaginary part of the Gaussian center parameters for spin-up protons and spin-down (or spin-up) neutrons are shifted along the $z$-axis by distances $d = 0$ to $10$ fm. These basis states are superposed using the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) to include 2p2h excitations with high momentum components. The Hamiltonian uses the Tohsaki interaction for the central part, G3RS for the spin-orbit part, and the Furutani interaction for the tensor part.

## Key numbers

- Tensor contribution in $^4$He with iSMT: $-41.56$ MeV, compared to $-9.40$ MeV for previous SMT and $0.0$ MeV for the $(0s)^4$ configuration
- Total energy of $^4$He with iSMT: $-50.64$ MeV, compared to $-32.85$ MeV for SMT and $-27.50$ MeV for $(0s)^4$
- Kinetic energy of $^4$He with iSMT: $71.96$ MeV, an increase of about $25$ MeV from the $(0s)^4$ value of $46.65$ MeV
- In $^{16}$O with tetrahedral four-$\alpha$ configuration: tensor matrix element of $-32.7$ MeV at $\alpha$-$\alpha$ distance of $2$ fm; energy decrease due to tensor interaction about $20$ MeV around the lowest energy point

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Simplified Model for Tensor contribution (SMT) (Itagaki et al., 2006) and the Antisymmetrized Quasi-Cluster Model (AQCM) framework for including spin-orbit effects. The iSMT method addresses the limitation that SMT gave only about $-10$ MeV tensor contribution in $^4$He, whereas ab initio calculations (Kamada et al., 2001) and Tensor Optimized Shell Model (TOSM) calculations (Myo et al., 2005-2012) show much larger tensor effects requiring high-momentum 2p2h components. The method enables systematic inclusion of tensor effects in cluster model descriptions of nuclei like $^{16}$O, where the tensor interaction enhances clustering stability, contrary to earlier suggestions (Ogawa et al., 2006, 2011) that tensor contribution is suppressed in $^{16}$O at the shell model limit.
