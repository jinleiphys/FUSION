# Watson's theorem and the $NΔ(1232)$ axial transition

> We present a new determination of the $NΔ$ axial form factors from neutrino induced pion production data. For this purpose, the model of Hernandez {\it et al.} [Phys. Rev. D76, 033005 (2007)] is improved by partially restoring unitarity. This is accomplished by imposing Watson's theorem on the dominant vector and axial multipoles. As a consequence, a larger $C_5^A(0)$, in good agreement with the prediction from the off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation, is now obtained.

## Key claim

By imposing Watson's theorem on the dominant vector and axial multipoles in the Hernandez et al. (2007) model, the extracted value of the axial coupling \(C_5^A(0)\) increases to \(1.12 \pm 0.11\), bringing it into agreement within \(1\sigma\) with the off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation prediction of \(1.15-1.2\), whereas previous fits without unitarization gave values around \(1.00 \pm 0.11\) that were \(2\sigma\) below the GTR.

## Method

The authors partially restore unitarity in the HNV model of Hernandez et al. (2007) by introducing two Olsson phases, \(\Psi_V\) and \(\Psi_A\), which modify the relative phase between the \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance and the nonresonant background contributions in the \(W^+p \to \pi^+ p\) amplitude. These phases are fixed by requiring that the dominant vector multipole (the magnetic \(M_{1+}\)) and the dominant axial multipole (the \(WN\) \(s\)-wave) independently satisfy Watson's theorem, meaning their phases equal the \(P_{33}\) \(\pi N\) phase shift \(\delta_{P_{33}}(s)\) from the SAID WI08 partial wave analysis. The model is then fitted to the ANL flux-averaged \(d\sigma/dQ^2\) differential cross section data (with a \(W_{\pi N} < 1.4\) GeV cut) and the BNL integrated cross sections at three lowest neutrino energies (0.65, 0.9, and 1.1 GeV), including deuterium target corrections and flux normalization uncertainties (20% for ANL, 10% for BNL).

## Key numbers

- \(C_5^A(0) = 1.12 \pm 0.11\) from the unitarized fit A to ANL and BNL data, compared to the GTR prediction of \(C_5^A(0) = 1.15-1.2\).
- \(M_{A\Delta} = (953.7 \pm 62.6)\) MeV from fit A, using a dipole form \(C_5^A(q^2) = C_5^A(0)/(1-q^2/M_{A\Delta}^2)^2\).
- The Gaussian correlation coefficient \(r\) between \(C_5^A(0)\) and \(M_{A\Delta}\) is \(-0.08\) for fit A.
- The \(\chi^2/\text{dof}\) for fit A is 0.46.
- The previous fit IV from Hernandez et al. (2010) without unitarization gave \(C_5^A(0) = 1.00 \pm 0.11\) and \(M_{A\Delta} = 0.93 \pm 0.07\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the model of Hernandez et al. (2007, 2010) for neutrino-induced pion production by partially restoring unitarity via Watson's theorem, which was previously only exactly satisfied in the dynamical coupled-channels model of Sato et al. (2003). The improved model resolves a \(2\sigma\) discrepancy between the extracted \(C_5^A(0)\) and the off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation, and provides a simpler, portable framework that can be implemented in event generators for neutrino oscillation experiments such as MiniBooNE and MINERvA.
