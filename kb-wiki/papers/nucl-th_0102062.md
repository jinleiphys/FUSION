# Systematic calculation of s-wave pion and kaon self-energies in asymmetric nuclear matter

> We calculate the pion self-energies in asymmetric nuclear matter in the two-loop approximation of chiral perturbation theory. We find three types of corrections beyond the well-known linear density approximation. The resulting s-wave potential (or equivalent mass-shift) of a $π^-$ in the center of a heavy nucleus like Pb turns out to be $U_{π^-}=Δm_{π^-} \simeq 14$ MeV, about half of what is needed to form the deeply bound and narrow pionic atom-states recently observed at GSI. The potential for a $π^+$ under the same conditions is $U_{π^+}=Δm_{π^+} \simeq -1$ MeV. In the same way we calculate the mass-shifts of $K^+$-mesons in symmetric nuclear matter and neutron matter and find an increase of the $K^+$-mass by 9% and 5%, respectively. As a further application we give an analytical result for the third order term in the scattering length expansion of the in-medium self-energy of an interacting light-boson/heavy-fermion system.

## Key claim

The s-wave optical potential for a π⁻ in the center of a heavy nucleus like Pb is calculated to be about 14 MeV, which is only half of what is needed to form the deeply bound and narrow pionic atom-states recently observed at GSI, indicating that the two-loop chiral perturbation theory calculation does not fully account for the required repulsion.

## Method

The authors calculate pion and kaon self-energies in asymmetric nuclear matter using two-loop chiral perturbation theory, working with on-shell mesons at zero three-momentum. They use in-medium nucleon propagators with separate proton and neutron Fermi momenta to treat isospin-asymmetric matter, and evaluate diagrams with two medium insertions that produce corrections beyond the linear density approximation. The calculation includes the Ericson-Ericson double scattering term generalized to asymmetric matter, a new term from ππ-interaction with two virtual pions absorbed on nucleons, and small relativistic corrections.

## Key numbers

- π⁻ mass-shift in asymmetric nuclear matter (density 0.165 fm⁻³, N/Z=1.5): Δmπ⁻ = 13.8 MeV (sum of contributions: 9.5 MeV from linear density, -1.0 MeV relativistic, 6.0 MeV double scattering, -0.7 MeV from ππ-interaction)
- π⁺ mass-shift under same conditions: Δmπ⁺ = -1.2 MeV (contributions: -7.5, +1.0, +6.0, -0.7 MeV)
- π⁰ mass-shift under same conditions: Δmπ⁰ = 6.1 MeV (contributions: 1.0, 0.0, 5.7, -0.6 MeV)
- K⁺ mass-shift in symmetric nuclear matter at density 0.165 fm⁻³: ΔmK = 45 MeV (30 MeV from linear density, 15 MeV from double scattering)
- K⁺ mass-shift in pure neutron matter at same density: ΔmK = 25 MeV (19 MeV from linear density, 6 MeV from double scattering)
- Empirical πN scattering T-matrices from pionic hydrogen: T⁻πN = (1.847 ± 0.086) fm, T⁺πN = (-0.045 ∓ 0.088) fm

## Context

This work extends the linear density approximation for pion-nucleus optical potentials (Ericson and Ericson, 1966) by providing a systematic chiral perturbation theory calculation up to two-loop order, including explicit dependence on proton and neutron densities. The results are directly relevant to the deeply bound pionic atom states observed at GSI by Yamazaki et al. (1996) and Gilg et al. (2000), and the finding that the calculated repulsion is only half of what is needed suggests that three-loop effects from pion absorption (πNN → NN) or in-medium renormalization of the pion decay constant may be required. The K⁺ results confirm earlier model calculations by Waas et al. (1996) and agree with the real part of the double scattering term from Lutz (1998), while correcting a claimed imaginary part.
