# Hollow nuclear matter

> It is generally considered that an atomic nucleus is always compact. Based on the isospin-dependent Boltzmann nuclear transport model, here I show that large block nuclear matter or excited nuclear matter may both be hollow. And the size of inner bubble in these matter is affected by the charge number of nuclear matter. Existence of hollow nuclear matter may have many implications in nuclear or atomic physics or astrophysics as well as some practical applications.

# Hollow nuclear matter

## Key claim

Based on the isospin-dependent Boltzmann nuclear transport model, large block nuclear matter (superheavy nuclei with mass number ~900) or excited nuclear matter (such as ⁷⁹Au with 5 MeV per nucleon excitation energy) may both be hollow, but the bubble configuration does not appear in relatively light ground-state nuclei such as ¹⁹⁷₇₉Au unless excitation energy is provided.

## Method

The author used the isospin-dependent Boltzmann nuclear transport model with Skyrme-type parametrization for the isoscalar mean field (incompressibility coefficient K = 230 MeV), a symmetry potential with γ = 0.3, and in-medium nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections factorized by a medium correction factor. Simulations were performed with 1000 test-particles per nucleon, initializing nucleon coordinates randomly in a sphere of radius R = 1.2A^(1/3) and momenta randomly in the Fermi sea, to study the time evolution of density distributions for hypothetical superheavy nuclei and for ¹⁹⁷₇₉Au both in ground state and with excitation energy, as well as in head-on ¹⁹⁷₇₉Au + ¹⁹⁷₇₉Au collisions.

## Key numbers

- The hypothetical superheavy nucleus ⁹⁰⁰₁₀₀X shows a bubble configuration appearing after 50 fm/c in the compact nucleus.
- The hypothetical superheavy nucleus ⁹⁰⁰₄₀₀X shows a bubble configuration appearing after 40 fm/c in the compact nucleus.
- For the relatively light nucleus ¹⁹⁷₇₉Au, no bubble configuration appears in the ground state, but a bubble appears after 30 fm/c when given an average excitation energy of 5 MeV per nucleon.
- In head-on ¹⁹⁷₇₉Au + ¹⁹⁷₇₉Au collisions at incident beam energy 35 MeV per nucleon, bubble configuration of compressed nuclear matter appears after 90 fm/c.
- The incompressibility coefficient used is K = 230 MeV; varying it to 200 MeV produced relatively large changes in the results, while varying the symmetry energy parameter γ from 0.3 to 1.5 produced very small effects.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of bubble nuclei using the liquid drop model (Wilson 1946; Siemens and Bethe 1967; Wong 1973), self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with the Gogny interaction (Decharge 1999, 2003) which found stable bubble solutions for superheavy nuclei with 250 ≤ Z ≤ 280 and 780 ≤ A ≤ 920, and transport simulations of nuclear collisions (Bauer 1992). The results show that bubble formation depends on both mass number and excitation energy, with the inner bubble size affected by charge number, and suggest that the radial velocity of nucleons in the center of the reaction system can be used to probe bubble formation in heavy-ion collisions. The existence of hollow nuclear matter may have implications for quantum many-body theory, atomic hyperfine structure, and neutron star configurations.
