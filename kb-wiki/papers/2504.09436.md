# Time-dependent random phase approximation for particle-number fluctuations and correlations in deep-inelastic collisions of $^{144}$Sm+$^{144}$Sm and $^{154}$Sm+$^{154}$Sm

> The fluctuation-dissipation mechanism underlying non-equilibrium transport in low-energy heavy-ion reactions remains unclear. Although the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) method provides a reasonable description of average reaction outcomes and one-body dissipation, it is known to significantly underestimate fluctuations of observables. The purpose of this work is to investigate deep-inelastic collisions of 144Sm+144Sm and 154Sm+154Sm with microscopic mean-field approaches and to show a predominant role of one-body dissipation as well as one-body fluctuations and correlation in low-energy heavy-ion reactions. Three dimensional TDHF calculations are carried out for 144Sm+144Sm at Ecm=500 MeV and 154Sm+154Sm at Ecm=485 MeV for a range of impact parameters with Skyrme SLy5 energy density functional. Backward time evolutions are performed as well to evaluate fluctuations and correlation in nucleon numbers within time-dependent random phase approximation (TDRPA). With TDRPA we calculate mass- and charge-number fluctuations, as well as the correlation between neutron and proton transfers, for each impact parameter. We demonstrate that TDRPA quantitatively reproduces the experimental σ_{AA}^2-TKEL distributions, whereas it systematically underestimates the charge fluctuation, σ_{ZZ}. The double-differential cross sections of reaction products are calculated, showing good agreement with the experimental data. We confirm a long-thought characteristic property that the closed-shell structure limits nucleon transfer at small energy losses, based on our microscopic TDHF and TDRPA calculations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that time-dependent random phase approximation (TDRPA), built on top of time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) mean-field dynamics, quantitatively reproduces experimental mass-number fluctuation distributions in deep-inelastic collisions, but systematically overestimates charge-number fluctuations, indicating that one-body dissipation and fluctuations are the predominant mechanism while leaving room for refinements in the energy density functional or missing two-body processes.

## Method

Three-dimensional TDHF calculations were performed for symmetric collisions of 144Sm+144Sm at Ec.m.=500 MeV and 154Sm+154Sm at Ec.m.=485 MeV across a range of impact parameters using the Skyrme SLy5 energy density functional. Backward time evolutions were then carried out within the TDRPA framework to evaluate neutron-number fluctuations, proton-number fluctuations, and the neutron-proton correlation for each impact parameter, following the Balian-Veneroni variational approach. For the deformed 154Sm system, three initial orientations (Tip-Tip, Side-Side, and Tip-Side) were considered, though only the Tip-Tip configuration preserved the symmetry required for valid TDRPA calculations.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energies: 500 MeV for 144Sm+144Sm and 485 MeV for 154Sm+154Sm
- Impact parameter range: 0 to approximately 6 fm, with 50 simulations per system at 0.2 fm increments
- Contact time threshold: minimum density between colliding nuclei exceeding 0.08 fm^-3
- Time step: 0.2 fm/c; mesh spacing: 0.8 fm; box grid: 30x30x30 for static calculations, 70x30x70 for dynamics
- Deformation parameter for 154Sm: beta_2 approximately 0.32 from calculation (experimental value 0.2925(25) from PhysRevLett.134.022503)
- TKE uncertainty assigned for cross-section calculations: sigma_E = 5 MeV, matching experimental resolution
- The experimental ratio of sigma^2_AA between the two systems shows weak TKEL dependence with a mean value of 0.82, deviating from (144/154)^2 approximately 0.87
- TDRPA calculations used epsilon = 10^-3 after testing 10^-2, 10^-3, and 10^-4

## Context

This work extends prior TDHF studies that accurately describe average reaction outcomes but severely underestimate fluctuations of observables (RevModPhys.54.913). It builds on the Balian-Veneroni formalism (PhysRevLett.47.1353, BALIAN1984301, BALIAN1992351) and earlier TDRPA applications (PhysRevLett.120.022501, PhysRevC.102.014620) by providing systematic comparison with experimental data from Wu (1981) and Hildenbrand (1983) for Sm+Sm systems. The results enable future investigations into the energy density functional dependence of charge fluctuations and the role of two-body dissipation mechanisms (Tohyama 2016, Wen 2018) that may be missing in the present TDRPA framework.
