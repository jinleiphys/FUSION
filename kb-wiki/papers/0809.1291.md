# Di-neutron correlation in soft octupole excitations of neutron-rich Ni isotopes beyond N=50

> We investigate low-lying octupole response of neutron-rich Ni isotopes beyond the N=50 shell closure using the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mean-fields and the continuum quasi-particle random phase approximation. Performing detailed numerical analyses employing the Skyrme parameter set SLy4 and a density-dependent delta interaction of the mixed type, we show that a neutron mode emerges above the neutron separation energy as a consequence of the weak binding of neutrons and it exhibits strong influences of the di-neutron correlation.

# Di-neutron correlation in soft octupole excitations of neutron-rich Ni isotopes beyond N=50

## Key claim

The authors show that a neutron octupole mode emerges above the neutron separation energy in neutron-rich Ni isotopes, and this mode exhibits strong di-neutron correlation effects; however, the strength of this mode is smaller than that of the coexisting surface vibrational mode by about a factor of ten, and the di-neutron correlation is sensitive to the choice of effective pairing interaction.

## Method

The authors perform fully self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the SLy4 parameter set to describe the ground states of neutron-rich Ni isotopes, then apply the continuum quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA) to compute the octupole response. The residual interaction in the particle-hole channel is treated via a Landau-Migdal approximation to the Skyrme interaction, while a density-dependent delta interaction of the mixed type is used for the pairing channel.

## Key numbers

- The sharp surface vibrational peak in $^{84}$Ni appears at $E \approx 4.16$ MeV, carrying an isoscalar octupole strength of $6.74 \times 10^5$ fm$^6$, exhausting 29% of the classical sum-rule value
- The broad neutron mode in $^{84}$Ni carries an integrated isoscalar octupole strength of $5.85 \times 10^4$ fm$^6$, corresponding to 1.7% of the classical sum-rule value
- The one-neutron separation energies for $^{80,82,84,86}$Ni are 2.79, 2.54, 1.86, and 1.32 MeV respectively; the two-neutron separation energies are 3.84, 3.26, 2.37, and 1.37 MeV respectively
- The neutron Fermi energy in $^{84}$Ni is -1.183 MeV for SLy4, compared to -0.722 MeV for the Woods-Saxon model and -2.338 MeV for SkM$^*$
- The electric dipole strength integrated above the one-neutron separation energy in $^{84}$Ni is $B(E1) = 2.23$ $e^2$fm$^2$, with an energy-weighted sum of 4.0% of the classical TRK sum rule

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of di-neutron correlation in soft dipole excitations (Serizawa and Matsuo, 2005) to the octupole channel, and goes beyond earlier RPA studies of neutron-rich nuclei (Hagino and Sagawa, 2001; Zang et al., 2003; Yoshida, 2001; Peru et al., 2004) that did not include pair correlations. The results demonstrate that di-neutron correlation is a generic feature appearing in multiple multipole channels, not only in dipole excitations, and that the neutron mode becomes more pronounced as the neutron drip line is approached. The sensitivity to the effective pairing interaction, particularly the stronger di-neutron correlation with mixed-type density-dependent pairing compared to volume pairing, provides guidance for future experimental searches for these modes in neutron-rich nuclei.
