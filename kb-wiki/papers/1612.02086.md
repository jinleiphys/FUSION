# Cluster states in stable and unstable nuclei

> In this contribution, I will discuss two topics related to the clustering of atomic nuclei. The first is dual character of the ground state. Recently, it was pointed out that the ground states of atomic nuclei have dual character of shell and cluster implying that both of the single-particle excitation and cluster excitation occur as the fundamental excitation modes. The isoscalar monopole and dipole transitions are regarded as the triggers to induce the cluster excitation. By referring the Hartree-Fock and antisymmetrized molecular dynamics calculations, the dual character and those transitions are explained. The second is the linear-chain state of $^{16}{\rm C}$ in which the linearly aligned 3$α$ particles are sustained by the valence neutrons. The rather convincing evidences for this exotic state are very recently reported by several experiments. Here, I introduce a couple of theoretical analysis and predictions.

## Key claim

The ground states of atomic nuclei have a "dual character of shell and cluster," meaning that the degrees-of-freedom of cluster excitation are embedded even in a pure shell model ground state, and both single-particle and cluster excitations are possible as fundamental excitation modes; this is not a claim that the ground state itself has a developed cluster structure, but rather that the mathematical equivalence of shell model and cluster model wave functions at small inter-cluster distance (Bayman-Bohr theorem) implies the potential for cluster excitation.

## Method

The author analyzed the overlap between Hartree-Fock ground states of \(^{8}\)Be, \(^{16}\)O, and \(^{20}\)Ne and Bloch-Brink cluster model wave functions using several Skyrme parameter sets (SkI3, SkI4, Sly6, SkM*), calculating both the single-Bloch-Brink overlap \(\mathcal{O}_{BB}\) and the generator coordinate method (GCM) overlap \(\mathcal{O}_{GCM}\) from superpositions of Bloch-Brink wave functions. For \(^{20}\)Ne, the author then derived analytical expressions for isoscalar (IS) monopole and dipole transition matrix elements from the ground state to nodal and angular excited cluster states, rewriting the transition operators in terms of internal cluster coordinates and the inter-cluster coordinate.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{8}\)Be, the single Bloch-Brink overlap with the Hartree-Fock ground state ranges from 71% (SkM*) to 82% (SkI3), with optimal inter-cluster distances \(R\) from 2.20 fm (SkM*) to 2.70 fm (SkI3); the GCM overlap reaches 82% (SkM*) to 98% (SkI3).
- For \(^{16}\)O, the overlap between the Hartree-Fock ground state and a tetrahedral 4\(\alpha\) Bloch-Brink wave function is 96% for all Skyrme parameter sets, with the tetrahedron side length only 0.01 fm.
- For \(^{20}\)Ne, the 5\(\alpha\) Bloch-Brink configuration has overlap from 36% (SkM*) to 53% (SkI3) with the Hartree-Fock ground state, with inter-cluster distance \(R\) from 1.59 fm (SkM*) to 1.91 fm (SkI3); the \(\alpha\)+\(^{16}\)O configuration has overlap from 36% (SkM*) to 52% (SkI3) with \(R\) from 2.4 fm (SkM*) to 2.7 fm (SkI3).
- The \(0^+_4\) state of \(^{20}\)Ne is observed as a broad resonance around 8.7 MeV, and the \(1^-_1\) state is observed at 5.8 MeV; both have large \(\alpha\) width.

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of the Bayman-Bohr theorem (Bayman and Bohr, 1958) from a criticism of cluster models to evidence for the dual character of shell and cluster, building on the analysis of Maruhn et al. (2006) who studied Hartree-Fock ground states of light nuclei. The finding that IS monopole and dipole transitions activate cluster degrees-of-freedom follows from earlier work by Yamada (2008) on monopole excitations and Kimura (2016) on dipole excitations. The discussion of linear-chain states in \(^{16}\)C connects to experimental reports from Freer (2014), Koyama (2015), and others, and to theoretical studies by Maruhn (2010), Suhara (2011), and Kimura (2014).
