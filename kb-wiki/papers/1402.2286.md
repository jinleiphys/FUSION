# The Magneto-Sono-Luminescence and its signatures in photon and dilepton production in heavy ion collisions

> The matter produced in the early stages of heavy ion collisions consists mostly of gluons, and is penetrated by coherent magnetic field produced by spectator nucleons. The fluctuations of gluonic matter in an external magnetic field couple to real and virtual photons through virtual quark loops. We study the resulting contributions to photon and dilepton production that stem from the fluctuations of the stress tensor $T_{μν}$ in the background of a coherent magnetic field $\vec{B}$. Our study extends significantly the earlier work by two of us and Skokov, in which only the fluctuations of the trace of the stress tensor $T_{μμ}$ were considered (the coupling of $T_{μμ}$ to electromagnetic fields is governed by the scale anomaly). In the present paper we derive more general relations using the Operator Product Expansion (OPE). We also extend the previous study to the case of dileptons which offers the possibility to discriminate between various production mechanisms. Among the phenomena that we study are Magneto-Sono-Luminescence (MSL, the interaction of magnetic field $\vec{B}(x,t)$ with the sound perturbations of the stress tensor $δT_{μν}(x,t)$) and Magneto-Thermo-Luminescence (MTL, the interaction of $\vec{B}(x,t)$ with smooth average $<T_{μν}>$). We calculate the rates of these process and find that they can dominate the photon and dilepton production at early stage of heavy ion collisions. We also point out the characteristic signatures of MSL and MTL that can be used to establish their presence and to diagnose the produced matter.

# The Magneto-Sono-Luminescence and its signatures in photon and dilepton production in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The authors propose that photon and dilepton production in heavy ion collisions can be dominated at early times by two new mechanisms, Magneto-Sono-Luminescence (MSL) and Magneto-Thermo-Luminescence (MTL), which couple the coherent magnetic field from spectator nucleons to fluctuations of the gluonic stress tensor via virtual quark loops, extending beyond the earlier trace-only treatment of Basar, Kharzeev, and Skokov (2012). The claim is conditional on the magnetic field being present primarily at early times (lifetime ~0.15 fm at RHIC) and on the stress tensor fluctuations having non-negligible momentum components.

## Method

The authors derive an effective local Lagrangian coupling two gluons to two photons using two complementary approaches: tensor meson dominance via the f2(1270) decay to two photons, and the Operator Product Expansion (OPE) treating the photon momentum as large compared to gluon momenta. They then compute the dilepton production rates for MTL (using the smooth average stress tensor of the medium) and MSL (using the spectral density of stress tensor fluctuations), incorporating the space-time dependence of the magnetic field parameterized by a Gaussian profile and a Lorentzian time decay.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strength at RHIC: eB ~ 0.2 GeV^2 = 10 m_pi^2 at impact parameter b = 10 fm, where it has a maximum.
- Magnetic field lifetime parameter at RHIC: t_B ≈ 0.15 fm.
- Effective temperature from magnetic field lifetime: T_eff = (2 t_B)^{-1} ≈ 0.66 GeV at RHIC.
- Initial QGP temperature for comparison: T_i ≈ 0.35 GeV.
- Tensor meson f2(1270) decay constant: f_f ≈ 108 MeV.
- Tensor meson f2(1270) decay width to two photons: Γ(f2 → γγ) ≈ 3 keV.
- Effective ggγγ coupling from hadronic approach: g_T ≈ (1.87 GeV)^{-4}.
- Intermediate mass dilepton window: m_phi ≈ 1 GeV < M < m_psi ≈ 3 GeV.
- Speed of sound in QGP: c_s ≈ 1/√3.

## Context

This work extends the earlier BKS approach of Basar, Kharzeev, and Skokov (2012), which considered only the trace of the stress tensor coupled to the magnetic field via the scale anomaly, by including the full stress tensor T_μν and extending the analysis to dilepton production. The mechanisms provide a possible explanation for the "large photon elliptic flow puzzle" observed by PHENIX at RHIC (Adare 2011) and ALICE at LHC (Lohner 2012), where conventional photon sources cannot account for the unexpectedly large second azimuthal coefficient v_2. The results enable new diagnostic tools using dilepton mass and polarization to discriminate between production mechanisms and to probe the early-stage gluonic matter and magnetic field in heavy ion collisions.
