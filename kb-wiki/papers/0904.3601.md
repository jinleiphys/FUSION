# Progress and Issues in the Electromagnetic Production of Kaon on the Nucleon

> 12 pages, 7 figures, invited talk at International Symposium on Strangeness in Hypernuclear and Hadronic Systems (SENDAI08), Sendai, Japan, December 15-18, 2008

# Progress and Issues in the Electromagnetic Production of Kaon on the Nucleon

## Key claim
The paper argues that despite decades of study, kaon photo- and electroproduction on the nucleon remain less understood than pion production, and that current experimental data sets (particularly from CLAS and SAPHIR) are mutually inconsistent in forward and backward angular regions, preventing a firm conclusion on the extraction of "missing resonances" and reliable extraction of resonance parameters.

## Method
The paper reviews single-channel isobaric models, chiral perturbation theory, coupled-channels analyses, Regge and hybrid models, and multipole analyses applied to the six isospin channels of kaon photoproduction on the nucleon. It compares model predictions against experimental data from SAPHIR, CLAS, LEPS, and GRAAL collaborations, and examines the effects of hadronic form factors, beam-recoil polarization data, and the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule on model fits.

## Key numbers
- A global scaling factor of 15% is required to remove the discrepancy between SAPHIR and CLAS data at forward kaon angles.
- The second peak in the total cross section for $\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda$ appears at $W \approx 1700$ MeV and 1900 MeV.
- The $D_{13}$ resonance mass extracted from both SAPHIR and CLAS data lies between 1911 and 1936 MeV.
- The GRAAL collaboration measured $O_x$ and $O_z$ observables from threshold up to $E_\gamma = 1.5$ GeV.
- For hypernuclear photoproduction of the hypertriton, the differential cross section is only realistic for experimental measurement at $0^\circ \le \theta_K \le 20^\circ$, while at $\theta_K \approx 25^\circ$ the cross section is practically zero.

## Context
This review extends the line of work initiated by Kawaguchi and Moravcsik (over 50 years ago) and revived by Adelseck, Bennhold, and Wright, who started the modern era of phenomenological models in $K^+\Lambda$ photoproduction. The paper highlights that the data discrepancy between CLAS and SAPHIR hinders reliable extraction of resonance parameters, and that forward-angle data are critical for hypernuclear calculations (e.g., hypertriton production). It recommends that a combination of CLAS, LEPS, and old data sets is preferred for fitting, while CLAS and SAPHIR data should not be combined, especially at forward angles. The paper also notes that the concept of hadronic form factors in meson photoproduction needs to be revisited, and that measurements of the longitudinal cross section in electroproduction at $W \ge 2.4$ GeV are needed to extract the kaon electromagnetic form factor.
