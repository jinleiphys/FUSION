# New eigen-mode of spin oscillations in the triplet superfluid condensate in neutron stars

> The eigen mode of spin oscillations with $ω\simeq \sqrt{58/35}Δ$ is predicted to exist besides already known spin waves with $ω\simeqΔ/\sqrt{5}$ in the triplet superfluid neutron condensate in the inner core of neutron stars. The new mode is kinematically able to decay into neutrino pairs through neutral weak currents. The problem is considered in BCS approximation for the case of $^{3}P_{2}-^{3}F_{2}$ pairing with a projection of the total angular momentum $m_{j}=0$ which is conventionally considered as preferable one at supernuclear densities.

# New eigen-mode of spin oscillations in the triplet superfluid condensate in neutron stars

## Key claim

The paper predicts a new collective spin oscillation mode with frequency ω ≃ √(58/35)Δ in the triplet superfluid neutron condensate, in addition to the previously known spin wave with ω ≃ Δ/√5. This prediction holds specifically for the case of ³P₂-³F₂ pairing with total angular momentum projection mⱼ = 0, which is conventionally considered the preferred state at supernuclear densities, and is derived within the BCS approximation.

## Method

The author extends the decomposition of the order parameter from l=1 only to include both l=1 and l=3 partial waves (³P₂ and ³F₂ channels), solving the Dyson equations for anomalous axial-vector vertices in the ladder approximation at zero momentum transfer (q=0). The eigen-frequencies are obtained from the poles of the vertex functions by diagonalizing the interaction matrix and using angle-averaged energy gap approximations, with the coupling constants dropping out of the solutions so that the frequencies depend only on the partial gap amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The previously known spin wave frequency: ω ≃ Δ/√5 (approximately 0.447Δ)
- The new spin wave frequency: ω ≃ √(58/35)Δ (approximately 1.287Δ)
- For the realistic ratio Δ₃ ≃ 0.17Δ₁ at the Fermi surface (from Khodel), the two degenerate modes have ω₋ = 0.42Δ and ω₊ = 1.19Δ
- The new mode has excitation energy ω(q=0) < 2Δ, making it kinematically able to decay into neutrino pairs

## Context

This work extends previous studies of spin modes in ³P₂ superfluid neutron liquid by Leinson (2009, 2010), which were restricted to l=1 excitations and found only the ω = Δ/√5 mode. The results cannot be directly applied from earlier work on p-wave superfluid ³He with central interactions (Maki, Combescot, Wölfle) because neutron pairing is dominated by spin-orbit interaction. The new mode enables additional neutrino emission through neutral weak currents at low temperatures (T ≪ T꜀), potentially extending the domain where volume neutrino emission dominates surface gamma radiation in neutron star cooling, as previously demonstrated for the lower branch by Leinson (2010).
