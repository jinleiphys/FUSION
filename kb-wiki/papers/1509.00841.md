# Hybrid star structure with the Field Correlator Method

> We explore the relevance of the color-flavor locking phase in the equation of state (EoS) built with the Field Correlator Method (FCM) for the description of the quark matter core of hybrid stars. For the hadronic phase, we use the microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) many-body theory, and its relativistic counterpart, i.e. the Dirac-Brueckner (DBHF). We find that the main features of the phase transition are directly related to the values of the quark-antiquark potential $V_1$, the gluon condensate $G_2$ and the color-flavor superconducting gap $Δ$. We confirm that the mapping between the FCM and the CSS (constant speed of sound) parameterization holds true even in the case of paired quark matter. The inclusion of hyperons in the hadronic phase and its effect on the mass-radius relation of hybrid stars is also investigated.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the color-flavor locking (CFL) phase can be incorporated into the Field Correlator Method (FCM) for quark matter, and that the mapping between the FCM and the Constant Speed of Sound (CSS) parameterization remains valid even with paired quark matter, though the accessible parameter region depends on the value of the superconducting gap Δ.

## Method

The authors constructed hybrid star equations of state by combining hadronic matter described with the non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) and relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) many-body theories with quark matter described by the Field Correlator Method (FCM) extended to include color-flavor locking via an additional pressure term P_cfl = Δ² μ_B² / (3π²). The hadron-quark phase transition was treated as first-order using Maxwell construction, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved to obtain mass-radius relations for hybrid stars, with the FCM parameters V₁ (quark-antiquark potential), G₂ (gluon condensate), and Δ (CFL gap) treated as free parameters.

## Key numbers

* The gluon condensate G₂ from QCD sum rules is 0.012 GeV⁴, with an uncertainty of about 50%
* The critical temperature T_c = 147 ± 5 MeV or T_c = 154 ± 9 MeV yields V₁(T_c) ≤ 0.15 GeV from Eq. (tcritical)
* A fit to lattice data suggests V₁(T_c) ∼ 0.5 - 0.6 GeV with G₂ ≃ 0.003 - 0.004 GeV⁴
* V₁(T_c) ∼ 0.5 - 0.6 GeV corresponds to V₁(T=0) ≃ 0.8 - 0.9 GeV
* The CFL gap Δ is expected in the range 10-100 MeV in the region of interest for neutron stars
* The largest maximum mass in the study (V₁ = 200 MeV, Δ = 100 MeV, G₂ = 0.01 GeV⁴) is compatible with the observed mass (2.01 ± 0.04) M⊙ in PSR J0348+0432
* The DBHF approach including hyperons yields a maximum neutron-star mass of 2.08 M⊙
* The saturation density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends previous investigations using different quark models (Baldo 2006, 2011Chen) and the authors' prior FCM studies (Baldo 2008, Plumari 2013) by incorporating the color-flavor locking phase and exploring the mapping onto the CSS parameterization developed by Alford (2013). The inclusion of hyperons in the hadronic phase addresses the "hyperon puzzle" where hyperonic three-body forces may be needed to reconcile the existence of massive neutron stars with the softening caused by hyperon appearance. The results enable future model-independent constraints on quark matter parameters from neutron star observations.
