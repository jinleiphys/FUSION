# Ground-state properties of superheavy $Z=122$ isotopes within the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum

> The ground-state properties of superheavy $Z = 122$ isotopes are investigated using the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc). Bulk properties, including binding energies, Fermi energies, nucleon separation energies, quadrupole deformations, and root-mean-square radii, are calculated. The results are compared with those obtained from the relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory. By examining the dependence on the angular-momentum cutoff and the effects of triaxial and octupole deformations, a strategy for determining the ground states is suggested. Furthermore, based on an analysis of the Fermi and nucleon separation energies, the proton and neutron drip lines for $Z = 122$ isotopes are determined within both the DRHBc and RCHB frameworks. The possible magic numbers $N=184$, 258, and 350 are also suggested. Finally, the evolution of single-particle levels, deformation, charge and neutron radii as well as average pairing gaps with increasing neutron number, is discussed.

## Key claim

The ground states of superheavy $Z=122$ isotopes are determined using the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc), but the identification of the true ground state requires careful examination of angular-momentum cutoff convergence and the effects of triaxial and octupole deformations; the previously recommended strategy of selecting the lowest minimum within $|\beta_2| < 0.3$ is superseded, as minima at large oblate deformation ($\beta_2 \sim -0.45$) can be the actual ground state.

## Method

The authors performed deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations in continuum (DRHBc) using the PC-PK1 density functional for the entire $Z=122$ isotopic chain from $N=171$ to $N=352$, solving the RHB equations in a Dirac Woods-Saxon basis with a box size of 20 fm and angular momentum cutoff $J_{\rm max}=31/2~\hbar$. For comparison, spherical relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) calculations were also performed. To validate ground-state assignments, the authors examined the dependence on angular-momentum cutoff ($J_{\rm max}=23/2$, $31/2$, $33/2~\hbar$) and used multi-dimensionally constrained covariant density functional theory (MDC-CDFT) to test the effects of triaxial and octupole deformations on the potential energy curves of $^{384}$Ubb.

## Key numbers

- The proton drip-line nucleus is $^{304}$Ubb ($N=182$) according to DRHBc, while RCHB indicates $^{303}$Ubb ($N=181$); isotopes with $N \le 178$ are unbound in both models based on negative two-proton separation energy.
- The neutron drip-line for $Z=122$ isotopes is $^{442}$Ubb ($N=320$) in DRHBc, whereas RCHB predicts $^{472}$Ubb ($N=350$).
- Possible neutron magic numbers $N=184$, $258$, and $350$ are identified from abrupt changes in neutron Fermi energy, two-neutron separation energy drops, and two-neutron shell gap peaks.
- The pairing strength used is $V_0 = -300$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^3$ with saturation density $\rho_{\rm sat}=0.152$ fm$^{-3}$; the DWS basis box is $R_{\rm box}=20$ fm with mesh $\Delta r=0.1$ fm and energy cutoff $E_\mathrm{cut}=300$ MeV.
- The quadrupole deformation $\beta_2$ reaches highly oblate values of approximately $-0.4$ to $-0.5$ in the neutron ranges $N=188$-$214$ and $N=263$-$291$.

## Context

This work extends the DRHBc mass table calculations previously performed up to $Z=120$ (Guo 2024) into the superheavy region $Z=122$, which has a temporary IUPAC name "Unbibium" (symbol Ubb). The study is part of the DRHBc Mass Table Collaboration's effort to cover the entire nuclear chart including superheavy nuclei with $121 \le Z \le 136$. The results confirm the possible neutron magic numbers $N=184$, $258$, and $350$ found in earlier DRHBc studies (Du 2024, Liu 2024, Pan 2025, Wu 2025) and identify a "stability peninsula" phenomenon similar to that observed in the $50 \le Z \le 70$ region (Pan 2021) and $100 \le Z \le 120$ region (Zhang 2021, He 2021, He 2024). The work enables future experimental searches for $Z=122$ isotopes and provides theoretical guidance for the synthesis of elements beyond $Z=120$.
