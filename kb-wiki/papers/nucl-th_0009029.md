# Can we determine the nuclear equation of state from heavy ion collisions?

> We discuss the problems involved in extracting the nuclear equation-of-state from heavy-ion collisions. We demonstrate that the equation of state becomes effectively softer in non-equilibrium and this effect is observable in terms of collective flow effects. Thus, non-equilibrium effects must be included in transport descriptions on the level of the effective mean fields. A comparison with transverse momentum, rapidity, and centrality selected flow data show the reliability and limitations of the underlying interaction which was derived from microscopic Dirac-Brueckner (DB) results.

## Key claim

The nuclear equation of state (EOS) becomes effectively softer in non-equilibrium heavy-ion collisions compared to its equilibrium form, and this softening is observable in collective flow; therefore, non-equilibrium effects must be included in transport descriptions at the level of effective mean fields before conclusions about the equilibrium EOS can be drawn from heavy-ion data.

## Method

The authors performed transport (BUU-type) calculations for Au+Au collisions using Dirac-Brueckner (DB) self-energies in two approximations: the Local Density Approximation (LDA), which assumes equilibrated nuclear matter at the local total density, and the Local phase space Configuration Approximation (LCA), which assumes a two-Fermi-sphere (colliding nuclear matter) configuration with a given relative velocity. They compared calculated directed flow \(F_y/A\) and elliptic flow \(v_2\) as functions of beam energy, as well as rapidity distributions at 400 AMeV, against experimental data from the EOS and FOPI collaborations.

## Key numbers

- The quadrupole moment of the energy-momentum tensor \(Q_{zz}\) and central density (in units of \(\rho_{\rm sat}\)) are shown for central Au+Au collisions at 0.6 and 6.0 A.GeV, demonstrating significant phase-space anisotropy during the compression phase.
- The reduced EOS for colliding nuclear matter (CNM) is shown for relative velocities \(v_{rel}=0.8\) and \(v_{rel}=0.99\), corresponding to beam energies of 0.6 and 6.0 GeV per nucleon, respectively, as a function of total density.
- The equilibrium Skyrme parameterisations used as reference have incompressibilities K = 200 MeV (soft) and K = 380 MeV (hard).
- The elliptic flow \(v_2\) for semi-central Au+Au reactions shows a transition from small \(v_2\) at 0.2 A.GeV to maximal out-of-plane flow (\(v_2 < 0\)) at 0.4 A.GeV, with a subsequent transition back to in-plane flow around 4 A.GeV.
- Rapidity distributions for Au+Au collisions at 400 AMeV are shown for centrality bins selected by the ERAT ratio (transverse to longitudinal total momentum).

## Context

This work extends earlier transport studies using Skyrme parameterisations (Stoecker 1986) and addresses the problem that the EOS is an equilibrium concept while heavy-ion collisions are highly non-equilibrium processes. The authors build on the Kardanoff-Baym equations and previous attempts to improve semi-classical and quasi-particle approximations (Cei). The results show that the difference between LDA and LCA calculations is of the same magnitude as the difference between a soft and hard EOS (Dani 1998), and that including non-equilibrium effects considerably improves agreement with data from the EOS and FOPI collaborations (Andronic 1999, Pinkenburg 1999). This enables more reliable extraction of the equilibrium EOS from heavy-ion flow observables.
