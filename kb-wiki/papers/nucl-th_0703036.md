# Neutrino-nucleus interactions at low energies within Fermi-liquid theory

> Cross sections are calculated for neutrino scattering off heavy nuclei at energies below 50 MeV. The theory of Fermi liquid is applied to estimate the rate of neutrino-nucleon elastic and inelastic scattering in a nuclear medium in terms of dynamic form factors. The cross sections, obtained here in a rather simple way, are in agreement with the results of the other much more sophisticated nuclear models. A background rate from the solar neutrino interactions within a large Ge detector is estimated in the above-mentioned approach. The knowledge of the rate is in particular rather important for new-generation large-scale neutrino experiments.

## Key claim

The authors apply Fermi-liquid theory (FLT) to calculate neutrino-nucleus cross sections at energies below 50 MeV, finding that their simpler approach yields results in agreement with more sophisticated nuclear models, but only within a factor of about 2 at higher energies (5–50 MeV) and with closer agreement below 5 MeV.

## Method

The authors compute the vector and axial-vector dynamic form factors for symmetric nuclear matter within Fermi-liquid theory by solving Dyson-type equations for the response functions, using Landau parameters taken from the literature at saturation density. They then estimate the neutrino-nucleus cross section for slightly asymmetric nuclei (71Ge and 40Ar) by relating the neutrino mean free path in nuclear matter to the cross section via the nuclear volume, summing elastic (neutral current) and absorption (charged current) contributions. The response functions include the spin-zero-sound collective mode contribution as a pole contribution.

## Key numbers

*   The Fermi weak coupling constant is given as \(G_{\rm F}=1.166\times 10^{-5}\) GeV\(^{-2}=1.436\times 10^{-49}\) erg cm\(^3\).
*   For free nucleons, the weak coupling constants are: \(C_V^p=0.08\), \(C_V^n=-1.0\), \(C_A^p=1.23\), \(C_A^n=-1.23\); for charged currents, \(D_V=1.0\), \(D_A=1.23\).
*   The saturation nuclear density used is \(n=0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
*   The nucleon radius is taken as about 0.8 fm.
*   The Landau parameters used are: \(F_0=-0.4\), \(F_0^\prime=0.2\), \(G_0=0.19\), \(G_0^\prime=0.78\), \(F_1=-0.66\), \(F_1^\prime=0.14\), \(G_1=0.48\), \(G_1^\prime=0.08\).
*   For solar neutrinos with energies less than 5 MeV, the FLT results in total cross section values close to the shell model calculation of Klapdor (1986).
*   At 5 < \(E_\nu\) < 50 MeV, the FLT results differ from the RPA (Kolbe 2003) and the simple shell model (Klapdor 1986) by a factor of 2.
*   The estimated total number of neutrino events from the \(^8\)B flux in a 1-ton 71Ge detector over one month is about 20–30 events per month (240–360 per year); for a 100-kg detector, about 2–3 events per month (24–36 per year).

## Context

This work extends the application of Fermi-liquid theory, previously used to calculate neutrino mean free paths in dense neutron matter (Chris1) and two-component nuclear matter (Chris2), to the calculation of neutrino-nucleus cross sections for finite heavy nuclei at low energies. The results are compared with the nuclear shell model calculations of Klapdor (1986) and the RPA calculations of Kolbe (2003), showing agreement within a factor of 2. The approach enables a simple estimate of the solar neutrino-induced background in large-scale detectors for double beta decay and dark matter experiments, which the authors note is important for experiments like those searching for double beta decay and dark matter.
