# Thermal Dilepton Production from Dropping rho based on the Vector Manifestation

> We study the pion electromagnetic form factor and the dilepton production rate in hot matter using the hidden local symmetry theory as an effective field theory for pions and rho mesons. In this framework, the chiral symmetry restoration is realized as the vector manifestation (VM) in which the massless vector meson becomes the chiral partner of the pion, giving a theoretical support to the dropping rho a la Brown-Rho scaling. In the VM the vector dominance (VD) is strongly violated near the phase transition point associated with the dropping $ρ$. We show that the effect of the violation of the VD substantially suppresses the dilepton production rate compared with the one predicted by assuming the VD together with the dropping rho.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the vector dominance (VD) of the pion electromagnetic form factor is strongly violated near the chiral phase transition point when the rho meson mass drops according to the vector manifestation (VM), and this violation substantially suppresses the thermal dilepton production rate compared to predictions that assume VD together with a dropping rho mass.

## Method

The authors used hidden local symmetry (HLS) theory as an effective field theory for pions and rho mesons, incorporating intrinsic temperature effects through Wilsonian matching to QCD at a matching scale of 1.1 GeV. They determined the temperature dependence of the bare parameters (gauge coupling g and parameter a) by requiring that the difference between axial-vector and vector current correlators vanishes at the critical temperature, then evolved these parameters via renormalization group equations and added hadronic thermal loop corrections to compute the pion form factor and dilepton production rate.

## Key numbers

- The rho meson mass at zero temperature is taken as m_rho = 775.8 MeV, with pion decay constant f_pi = 92.46 MeV
- The critical temperature is set to T_c = 170 MeV, with the flash temperature T_f = 0.7 T_c (119 MeV)
- The bare parameter a at zero temperature is a(Lambda) = 1.31, evolving to a = 1 at the critical point; the bare gauge coupling g(Lambda) = 3.63 at T=0, evolving to zero at T_c
- The Wilsonian matching at T=0 yields a(0) = 1.94, g(m_rho) = 6.02, and z_3(m_rho) = -3.87 x 10^(-3) for the on-shell parameters
- The matching scale is Lambda = 1.1 GeV with Lambda_QCD = 0.4 GeV

## Context

This work extends the vector manifestation framework developed by Harada and Yamawaki (2001) and applies it to finite temperature, providing a theoretical basis for the Brown-Rho scaling of the rho meson mass. The results contradict naive dropping rho models that assume vector dominance holds near the phase transition, and the authors note that the strong violation of VD may help explain the NA60 Collaboration dimuon spectrum data (2006) which appears difficult to reconcile with a simple dropping rho scenario. The analysis enables more realistic calculations of dilepton spectra in heavy-ion collisions by incorporating both intrinsic temperature effects from QCD matching and hadronic thermal corrections.
