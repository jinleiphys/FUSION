# Forward $J/ψ$ production in proton-nucleus collisions at high energy

> Inclusive production of $J/ψ$ mesons, especially at forward rapidities, is an important probe of small-x gluons in protons and nuclei. In this paper we re-evaluate the production cross sections in the Color Glass Condensate framework, where the process is described by a large x gluon from the probe splitting into a quark pair and eikonally interacting with the target proton or nucleus. Using a standard collinear gluon distribution for the probe and an up to date dipole cross section fitted to HERA data to describe the target we achieve a rather good description of the cross section in proton-proton collisions, although with a rather large normalization uncertainty. More importantly, we show that generalizing the dipole cross section to nuclei in the Glauber approach results in a nuclear suppression of $J/ψ$ production that is much closer to the experimental data than claimed in previous literature.

# Forward J/ψ Production in Proton-Nucleus Collisions at High Energy

## Key claim

The authors show that when the dipole cross section is generalized to nuclei using a Glauber approach with a proton transverse area consistent with DIS fits, the resulting nuclear suppression of J/ψ production is much closer to experimental data than claimed in previous CGC calculations (Fujii et al., 2013), though the proton-proton cross section normalization has a rather large uncertainty.

## Method

The calculation uses the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework in the hybrid formalism, where a collinear gluon from the projectile proton splits into a quark-antiquark pair that eikonally interacts with the target via Wilson lines. The dipole cross section is obtained from a running coupling BK equation fit to HERA DIS data (Lappi et al., 2013), and the nuclear dipole cross section is constructed via a Glauber model using the Woods-Saxon nuclear density with no additional parameters. The color evaporation model with F_J/ψ = 0.06 converts c-cbar pairs below the D-meson threshold into J/ψ mesons.

## Key numbers

- F_J/ψ = 0.06 (nonperturbative constant for J/ψ formation in color evaporation model)
- F_Υ = 0.04 (nonperturbative constant for Υ formation)
- Charm quark mass varied between 1.2 and 1.5 GeV; bottom quark mass varied between 4.5 and 4.8 GeV
- D meson mass M_D = 1.864 GeV; B meson mass M_B = 5.280 GeV
- Dipole fit parameters: Q_s0^2 = 0.060 GeV^2, C^2 = 7.2, e_c = 18.9, σ_0/2 = 16.36 mb (χ^2/d.o.f. = 1.15)
- Woods-Saxon parameters: d = 0.54 fm, R_A = (1.12 A^{1/3} - 0.86 A^{-1/3}) fm
- Factorization scale varied between M_⊥/2 and 2M_⊥ where M_⊥ = sqrt(M^2 + P_T^2)
- Transverse momentum integration for integrated cross sections goes up to P_T = 15 GeV
- Center-of-mass energies: 7 TeV for pp, 5 TeV for pPb, and RHIC energies for comparison

## Context

This work extends the CGC framework for forward J/ψ production previously used by Fujii et al. (2013), which predicted much stronger nuclear suppression than observed at the LHC. The key improvement is using the dipole cross section parametrization from Lappi et al. (2013), which properly treats the proton transverse area from DIS fits and relates proton and nuclear dipole cross sections via Glauber model without an independent nuclear saturation scale. This enables nuclear modification ratios that approach unity at high transverse momentum, resolving a major discrepancy with ALICE and LHCb data. The results can be compared with cold nuclear matter energy loss calculations (Arleo et al., 2012) and NLO collinear calculations using nuclear PDFs such as EPS09 (Eskola et al., 2009), and future work could combine this approach with NRQCD hadronization (Kang et al., 2013; Ma et al., 2014).
