# Elliptic Flow at Large Viscosity

> In this contribution we present an alternative scenario for the large elliptic flow observed in relativistic heavy ion collisions. Motivated by recent results from Lattice QCD on flavor off-diagonal susceptibilities we argue that the matter right above $T_{c}$ can be described by single-particle dynamics in a repulsive single-particle potential, which in turn gives rise to elliptic flow. These ideas can be tested experimentally by measuring elliptic flow of heavy quarks, preferably via the measurement of $J/Ψ$ elliptic flow.

## Key claim

The observed large elliptic flow in relativistic heavy ion collisions can be explained by single-particle dynamics in a repulsive density-dependent potential, without requiring a short mean free path or small shear viscosity, contrary to the standard interpretation that the matter is a nearly perfect fluid.

## Method

The author develops a schematic model using the Vlasov equation with non-relativistic kinematics and a Gaussian spatial distribution, where the single-particle potential is proportional to the local density of light degrees of freedom. Heavy quarks are propagated in the potential generated by light quarks, ignoring their contribution to the potential. The model is solved analytically to leading order in the initial spatial eccentricity, and a more realistic relativistic transport calculation is also performed.

## Key numbers

- Lattice QCD results show the pressure right above \(T_c\) is approximately 15% below that of a free gas of massless quarks and gluons
- The schematic model uses an initial temperature of \(T = 300\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for the calculations shown
- The elliptic anisotropy \(v_{2,light}(u) = \frac{1}{2}\epsilon\frac{U_0}{T}\frac{1}{u^2}\left[1-\exp(-u^2)(u^2+1)\right]\) depends on kinetic energy \(u = \frac{mv^2}{2T}\), initial eccentricity \(\epsilon\), and initial potential strength \(U_0\)
- Heavy quark elliptic anisotropy relates to light quark anisotropy by \(v_{2,heavy}(v) = \frac{1+\gamma}{2}\,v_{2,light}(v)\) where \(\gamma = m_{light}/M_{heavy}\)

## Context

This work presents an alternative to the standard interpretation that the large elliptic flow at RHIC requires a strongly coupled, nearly perfect fluid with very small shear viscosity, as supported by AdS-CFT correspondence (Son, 2004) and viscous hydrodynamic calculations (Luzum; Song). The author builds on Lattice QCD results (Cheng, 2008) showing flavor-off-diagonal susceptibilities consistent with quasi-particle behavior (Koch, 2005) and higher order baryon number susceptibilities (Ejiri, 2005). The model predicts that integrated \(v_2\) for heavy quarks and \(J/\Psi\) should be considerably smaller than for pions, which can be tested experimentally to distinguish this scenario from hydrodynamic predictions.
