# Non-perturbative insights into the spectral properties of QCD at finite temperature

> In quantum field theories at finite temperature spectral functions describe how particle systems behave in the presence of a thermal medium. Although data from lattice simulations can in principle be used to determine spectral function characteristics, existing methods rely on the extraction of these quantities from temporal correlators, which requires one to circumvent an ill-posed inverse problem. In these proceedings we report on a recent approach that instead utilises the non-perturbative constraints imposed by field locality to extract spectral function information directly from spatial correlators. In particular, we focus on the application of this approach to lattice QCD data of the spatial pseudo-scalar meson correlator in the temperature range $220-960 \, \text{MeV}$, and outline why this data supports the conclusion that there exists a distinct pion state above the chiral pseudo-critical temperature $T_{\!\text{pc}}$.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a distinct pion state exists above the chiral pseudo-critical temperature \(T_{\!\text{pc}}\), based on a novel non-perturbative approach that extracts spectral function information directly from spatial correlators rather than temporal ones, thereby avoiding the ill-posed inverse problem. The hedge is that this conclusion is supported by lattice QCD data in the temperature range \(220-960 \, \text{MeV}\) and is consistent with the expectations of chiral spin symmetry, but the analysis only captures the two lowest-lying states (pion and its first radial excitation) and may be modified by higher-excited states not resolved by the data.

## Method
The authors applied the non-perturbative constraints from field locality, which yield a thermal spectral representation for the spatial correlator \(C(x_3)\). They performed fits to lattice QCD data of the spatial pseudo-scalar meson correlator from Rohrhofer (2019) using a two-exponential ansatz \(C_{\text{PS}}(x_3) = A e^{-m_{\pi}^{\text{scr}} x_3} + B e^{-m_{\pi^{*}}^{\text{scr}} x_3}\) at six temperatures (220, 320, 380, 480, 660, and 960 MeV). From the fit parameters, they derived the explicit form of the spectral function \(\rho_{\text{PS}}(\omega,\mathbf{p})\) via the thermal spectral representation, and validated the result by predicting the temporal correlator at \(T=220\) MeV and comparing with lattice data from Rohrhofer (2019).

## Key numbers
- Temperature range studied: \(220-960 \, \text{MeV}\), corresponding to approximately \(1.2 \, T_{\!\text{pc}}\) and above.
- At \(T=220\) MeV, the pion component of the spectral function gives a pronounced peak; the peak location is \(\omega_{\pi}^{\text{peak}} = \sqrt{m_{\pi}^{2} + \tfrac{1}{3}\gamma_{\pi}^{2}}\) with \(m_{\pi}=140\) MeV.
- The temporal correlator prediction shows quantitative agreement with lattice data for \(\tau \gtrsim 0.0007 \, \text{MeV}^{-1} \approx m_{\pi^{*}}^{-1}\) at \(T=220\) MeV.
- The \(\pi^{*}\) features a distinct peak up to around \(T=660\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the axiomatic framework of local quantum field theory at finite temperature developed by Bros (1992, 1995, 1998, 1996, 2001) and applies it to lattice QCD data from Rohrhofer (2019). It contrasts with standard methods that extract spectral functions from temporal correlators via ill-posed inverse problems, as reviewed by Asakawa (2000) and Meyer (2011). The results enable a non-perturbative analytic description of the QCD phase diagram and can be generalized to meson states with higher spin, different hadronic states, and regimes with non-vanishing baryon density.
