# On the definition of fragmentation functions and the violation of sum rules

> We point out a problem with the formulation and derivations of sum rules for quark fragmentation functions that impacts their validity in QCD, but which potentially points toward an improved understanding of final states in inclusive hard processes. Fragmentation functions give the distribution of final-state hadrons arising from a parton exiting a hard scattering, and the sum rules for momentum, electric charge, etc express conservation of these quantities. The problem arises from a mismatch between the quark quantum numbers of the initial quark and the fact that all observed final-state hadrons are confined bound states with color zero. We point that, in a confining theory like QCD, the Wilson line in the operator definition of a fragmentation function entails that the final state in a fragmentation function includes a bound state in the external field generated by the Wilson line. We justify this with the aid of general features of string hadronization. The anomalous bound states are restricted to fractional momentum $z=0$. They tend to invalidate sum rules like the one for charge conservation when applied to the fragmentation functions inferred from experimental data, but not the momentum sum rule. We propose to exploit our ideas in future studies as a way to relate the ffs extracted from inclusive cross sections to more detailed non-perturbative descriptions of final state hadronization. We also describe scenarios wherein the traditional sum rules might remain approximately valid with a reasonably high degree of accuracy.

# On the definition of fragmentation functions and the violation of sum rules

## Key claim

The standard sum rules for quark fragmentation functions (charge, hadron number) are not guaranteed identities in QCD because the Wilson line in the operator definition forces the final state to include bound states of the Wilson line with orphan quarks at fractional momentum z=0, which carry the quark quantum numbers that cannot appear in purely hadronic final states. The momentum sum rule remains valid because the z=0 bound states contribute zero due to the explicit factor of z in the integrand.

## Method

The authors analyze the operator definition of fragmentation functions, showing that the initial quark state created by a light-front creation operator has fractional quantum numbers (e.g., electric charge) that cannot be matched by any purely hadronic final state in a confining theory. They demonstrate that the Wilson line required for gauge invariance acts as an external color source, and using the string hadronization model of Casher, Kogut and Susskind (1974), they show that the final state must contain an "orphan" quark that binds to the Wilson line at z=0. They then derive modified sum rules that include deficit fragmentation functions for these bound states.

## Key numbers

- The momentum sum rule becomes: sum over hadrons h of integral from 0 to 1 of z times d_{h/j}(z) = 1 minus sum over bound states b of integral from 0 to 1 of z times d_{b/j}(z); the deficit term vanishes for delta-function bound states at z=0
- For the charge sum rule with only u, d, s active flavors, the sum of quark charges is zero and the standard charge sum rule is recovered
- For the charge sum rule with charm included, the correction term equals e/6, giving: sum over hadrons h of Q_h times integral d_{h/j}(z) approximately equals Q_j minus e/6
- In the meson-dominance approximation with n_f active flavors, the orphan quark coefficients satisfy c_{j,j'} approximately equals 1/n_f
- The hadron number sum rule becomes: sum over hadrons h of integral d_{h/j}(z) equals average multiplicity minus 1 (in the no-baryon approximation)

## Context

This paper extends and challenges the foundational sum rule derivations for fragmentation functions by Collins (1981) and the hadron number sum rule by Majumder (2004). It identifies a fundamental paradox in the operator definition that previous proofs of sum rules had overlooked: the mismatch between quark quantum numbers of the initial state and the color-singlet hadronic final states. The work enables future connections between fragmentation functions extracted from inclusive cross sections and non-perturbative descriptions of hadronization, and it raises questions about sum rule applications in dihadron fragmentation functions (Pitonyak, 2023), fracture functions (Trentadue, 1993), and Monte Carlo event generators (Andersson, 1998; Kerbizi, 2018).
