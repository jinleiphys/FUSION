# Holographic Photon Production in Heavy Ion Collisions

> The thermal-photon emission from strongly coupled gauge theories at finite temperature is calculated using holographic models for QCD in the Veneziano limit (V-QCD). The emission rates are then embedded in hydrodynamic simulations combined with prompt photons from hard scattering and the thermal photons from hadron gas to analyze the spectra and anisotropic flow of direct photons at RHIC and LHC. The results from different sources responsible for the thermal photons in QGP including the weakly coupled QGP (wQGP) from perturbative calculations, strongly coupled $\mathcal{N}=4$ super Yang-Mills (SYM) plasma (as a benchmark for reference), and Gubser's phenomenological holographic model are then compared. It is found that the direct-photon spectra are enhanced in the strongly coupled scenario compared with the ones in the wQGP, especially at high momenta. Moreover, both the elliptic flow and triangular flow of direct photons are amplified at high momenta for V-QCD and the SYM plasma. The results are further compared with experimental observations.

## Key claim

The holographic V-QCD and Gubser-Nellore models, which incorporate strong coupling and conformal symmetry breaking, predict enhanced direct-photon spectra and amplified elliptic and triangular flow at high transverse momenta compared to weakly coupled perturbative QCD calculations, though the models still underestimate the experimentally observed anisotropic flow.

## Method

The thermal-photon emission rates were computed from the spectral functions of vector current correlators in two bottom-up holographic QCD models (V-QCD and the Gubser-Nellore model) at finite temperature, using the gauge/gravity duality. These rates were then embedded in hydrodynamic simulations of heavy ion collisions, combined with contributions from prompt photons from hard scattering and thermal photons from hadron gas, to calculate the spectra and anisotropic flow of direct photons at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- The direct-photon spectra are enhanced in the strongly coupled scenario compared with weakly coupled QGP, especially at high momenta above approximately 2-3 GeV.
- The elliptic flow (v2) and triangular flow (v3) of direct photons are amplified at high momenta for V-QCD and N=4 SYM plasma.
- For pT in the range 2-4 GeV, thermal photons from the QGP phase are responsible for the direct-photon anisotropic flow.
- The holographic models improve agreement with experimental observations in spectra but still underestimate the flow.

## Context

This work extends prior holographic calculations of photon production in strongly coupled N=4 SYM plasma (Caron-Huot, 2006) and the Gubser-Nellore phenomenological model (Gubser and Nellore, 2008; Finazzo, 2013) to the more sophisticated V-QCD framework that includes flavor dynamics and chiral symmetry breaking. It addresses the puzzle of large direct-photon anisotropic flow measured at RHIC (Adare, 2011) and LHC (Lohner, 2012), which previous simulations using perturbative QCD rates with hard-thermal-loop resummation (Arnold, 2001; Shen, 2013) under-predicted. The results enable future comparisons between holographic and lattice QCD approaches to electromagnetic probes in the quark-gluon plasma.
