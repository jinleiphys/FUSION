# Application of the operator product expansion to the short distance behavior of nuclear potentials

> We investigate the short distance behavior of nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials defined through Bethe-Salpeter wave functions, by perturbatively calculating anomalous dimensions of 6-quark operators in QCD. Thanks to the asymptotic freedom of QCD, 1-loop computations give certain exact results for the potentials in the zero distance limit. In particular the functional form of the S-state central NN potential at short distance $r$ is predicted to be a little weaker than $r^{-2}$. On the other hand, due to the intriguing character of the anomalous dimension spectrum, perturbative considerations alone can not determine whether this potential is repulsive or attractive at short distances. A crude estimation suggests that the force at short distance is repulsive, as found numerically in lattice QCD. A similar behavior is found for the tensor potential.

## Key claim

The short-distance behavior of the nucleon-nucleon (NN) central potential for S-states is predicted to diverge as a little weaker than \(r^{-2}\), specifically as \((-\log r)^{\beta^{SI}-1}/r^2\), but perturbative QCD alone cannot determine whether the potential is repulsive or attractive at short distances; a crude non-perturbative estimate using the non-relativistic quark model suggests repulsion, consistent with lattice QCD results.

## Method

The authors apply the operator product expansion (OPE) to equal-time Bethe-Salpeter wave functions for two nucleons in QCD, factorizing the short-distance behavior into perturbatively calculable coefficient functions and non-perturbative matrix elements of local 6-quark operators. They compute the one-loop anomalous dimensions of these 6-quark operators (and of 3-quark nucleon operators) using dimensional regularization in the MS scheme, then solve the renormalization group equations to obtain the leading logarithmic behavior of the coefficient functions as \(r \to 0\). The analysis focuses on operators without derivatives, which dominate at short distances for S-wave states.

## Key numbers

- The anomalous dimension exponent for the S-state central NN potential with spin \(S=0\), isospin \(I=1\) is \(\beta^{01} = -6/(33-2N_f)\), where \(N_f\) is the number of quark flavors.
- The anomalous dimension exponent for the S-state central NN potential with \(S=1\), \(I=0\) is \(\beta^{10} = -2/(33-2N_f)\).
- The one-loop coefficient \(d\) appearing in the 3-quark operator anomalous dimensions is \(d = 1/(96\pi^2)\).
- The one-loop beta function coefficient is \(\beta_0 = (1/(16\pi^2))[(11/3)N - (2/3)N_f]\) with \(N=3\) colors.

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD approach to NN potentials introduced by Ishii, Aoki, and Hatsuda (2007), which numerically reproduced a repulsive core at short distances but lacked a theoretical explanation. The OPE analysis provides a first-principles QCD framework for the short-distance behavior, analogous to the authors' earlier successful application to the Ising field theory in 2 dimensions (Aoki, Balog, Weisz, 2009). The results enable future non-perturbative determinations of the sign and magnitude of the potential from lattice calculations of the relevant 6-quark operator matrix elements.
