# Interplay between Spin Polarization and Color Superconductivity in High Density Quark Matter

> Here, it is suggested that a four-point interaction of the tensor type may lead to spin polarization in quark matter at high density. It is found that the two-flavor superconducting phase and the spin polarized phase correspond to distinct local minima of a certain generalized thermodynamical potential. It follows that the transition from one to the other phase occurs passing through true minima with both a spin polarization and a color superconducting gap. It is shown that the quark spin polarized phase is realized at rather high density, while the two-flavor color superconducting phase is realized at a lower density region.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that a tensor-type four-point interaction between quarks can lead to spin polarization in quark matter at high density, and that the two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phase and the spin polarized phase correspond to distinct local minima of a generalized thermodynamic potential, with the transition between them passing through true minima that exhibit both a spin polarization and a color superconducting gap.

## Method

The authors use a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model extended with a tensor-type four-point interaction and a quark-pair interaction derived by Fierz transformation from the original NJL Lagrangian. They treat the system in the mean field approximation with a BCS state, introducing order parameters for spin polarization (F) and the two-flavor color superconducting gap (Δ). The thermodynamic potential is derived at zero temperature, and the gap equations are solved numerically to compare the stability of the spin polarized phase (F≠0, Δ=0) and the 2SC phase (F=0, Δ≠0) as functions of quark chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The phase transition between the 2SC phase and the spin polarized phase occurs at a critical chemical potential μ_c = 0.442 GeV.
- Up to a baryon number density ρ_B = 4.73 ρ_0, where ρ_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3} is normal nuclear matter density, the color superconducting phase is realized.
- The three-momentum cutoff parameter Λ = 0.631 GeV is adopted.
- The tensor-type coupling strength is G = 20.0 GeV^{-2}, and the quark-pair coupling strength is G_c = 6.6 GeV^{-2}.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Tsue et al., 2012) which showed that a tensor-type four-point interaction can induce spin polarization in quark matter even in the chiral symmetric phase, where earlier work by Tatsumi (2000) using pseudovector-type interactions had found spin alignment disappears for massless quarks. The paper also builds on the NJL model treatment of color superconductivity by Kitazawa et al. (2002) and investigates the competition between the spin polarized phase and the two-flavor color superconducting phase, which is relevant for understanding the interior of compact stars such as magnetars.
