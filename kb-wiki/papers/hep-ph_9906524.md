# Testing the Meson Cloud Model in Inclusive Meson Production

> We have applied the Meson Cloud Model to calculate inclusive momentum spectra of pions and kaons produced in high energy proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions. For the first time these data are used to constrain the cloud cut-off parameters. We show that it is possible to obtain a reasonable description of data, especially the large $x_F$ ($x_F \geq 0.2$) part of the spectrum and at the same time describe (partially) the E866 data on $\bar d - \bar u$ and $\bar d / \bar u$. We also discuss the relative strength of the $πN$ and $πΔ$ vertices. We find out that the corresponding cut-off parameters should be both soft and should not differ by more than 200 MeV from each other. An additional source (other than the meson cloud) of sea antiquark asymmetry, seems to be necessary to completely explain the data. A first extension of the MCM to proton nucleus collisions is discussed.

# Testing the Meson Cloud Model in Inclusive Meson Production

## Key claim

The Meson Cloud Model (MCM) can provide a reasonable description of inclusive pion and kaon spectra in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions at large $x_F$ ($x_F \geq 0.2$) and low $p_T$ ($p_T \leq 0.3$ GeV), but an additional source of sea antiquark asymmetry beyond the meson cloud is required to fully explain the E866 data on $\bar d - \bar u$ and $\bar d / \bar u$.

## Method

The authors decompose the physical proton into a sum of virtual meson-baryon ($MB$) Fock states, including $\pi N$, $\pi \Delta$, and kaon-hyperon configurations. They compute inclusive meson production cross sections using three mechanisms: direct production (the cloud meson escapes), meson-initiated reactions (the cloud meson interacts inelastically with the target), and baryon-initiated reactions (the cloud baryon interacts inelastically). The splitting functions for the $MB$ states are calculated using dipole form factors with cut-off parameters $\Lambda_{oct}$ and $\Lambda_{dec}$ for octet and decuplet vertices respectively, and the elementary cross sections for $M p \rightarrow M^+ X$ and $B p \rightarrow M^+ X$ are taken from experimental data or approximated. The same splitting functions are used to compute the antiquark distributions $\bar d(x)$ and $\bar u(x)$ via convolution with pion valence quark distributions.

## Key numbers

- Cut-off parameters tested: Case I: $\Lambda_{oct} = 0.96$ GeV, $\Lambda_{dec} = 0.77$ GeV; Case II: $\Lambda_{oct} = 0.87$ GeV, $\Lambda_{dec} = 1.0$ GeV
- The octet and decuplet cut-off parameters should not differ by more than 200 MeV from each other
- Pion multiplicity from the cloud: $n_{\pi} \simeq 0.17$ for both cases I and II
- Absorption factor $K_{abs} = 0.4$ for pion production and $K_{abs} = 0.8$ for kaon production
- Baryon-proton cross section taken as constant $\sigma^{B p} = 38$ mb
- Gottfried integral values: $S_G = 0.261$ (case I) and $S_G = 0.314$ (case II), compared to NMC value $0.235 \pm 0.026$
- For proton-nucleus collisions, the nuclear dependence exponent $\alpha = 0.72$ is used
- Medium effects approximated by baryon mass modification $M_B^* \simeq 0.8 M_B$

## Context

This work extends the Meson Cloud Model approach of Sullivan (1972) and subsequent developments by Kumano, Holtmann, Speth, and collaborators, who used the MCM to study the Gottfried Sum Rule violation and parton distributions. It directly tests the cut-off parameter choices from the E866 analysis by Peng et al. (1998), which favored $\Lambda_{\pi N} \geq \Lambda_{\pi \Delta}$, against the alternative by Melnitchouk, Speth, and Thomas (1998), who found $\Lambda_{\pi N} \leq \Lambda_{\pi \Delta}$. The inclusion of indirect (rescattering) production mechanisms goes beyond earlier works by Holtmann et al. (1994) and Dolejsi et al. (1995) that considered only direct production. The results enable future studies of medium effects in proton-nucleus collisions and constrain the role of non-perturbative mechanisms in sea quark asymmetry.
