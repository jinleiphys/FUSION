# Pomeron-LQCD model of $J/Ψ$ photo-production on the nucleon

> Based on the vector meson dominance assumption, a Hamiltonian model has been developed to investigate $J/Ψ$ photo-production reaction on the nucleon by using the $J/Ψ$-nucleon potential extracted from a lattice QCD calculation of Phys. Rev. D{\bf 82}, 091501 (2010). It is found that the predicted total cross sections are comparable to the recent data of $J/Ψ$ photo-production reaction from Jefferson Laboratory. The model is then extended to include the two-gluon exchange amplitude modeled by Donnachie and Lanshoff within Regge Phenomenology. The resulting Pomeron-LQCD model can then explain the data up to invariant mass $W=$ 300 GeV. Future improvements needed to reduce the uncertainties of the predictions are discussed. The need of an accurate extraction of $J/Ψ$-N potential at short distances from LQCD is illustrated.

# Pomeron-LQCD model of J/Ψ photo-production on the nucleon

## Key claim

The predicted total cross sections from a VMD-LQCD model using the J/Ψ-nucleon potential extracted from lattice QCD are comparable to recent Jefferson Laboratory data, but only after including an off-shell factor F^off (0.75 for pot-1, 0.41 for pot-2) to account for the q^2-dependence of the VMD coupling constant; the model must be extended with a Pomeron-exchange amplitude to describe data up to W=300 GeV.

## Method

The author constructs a Hamiltonian model based on vector meson dominance (VMD) using a J/Ψ-nucleon potential v_LQCD(r) extracted from lattice QCD calculations by Kawanai and Sasaki (2010), parameterized as a Yukawa form v(r) = v_0 * exp(-αr)/r. The V+N → V+N scattering amplitude is obtained by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with this potential, and the photo-production amplitude is then evaluated. The model is extended to a Pomeron-LQCD model by adding the two-gluon exchange amplitude from the Donnachie-Landshoff Regge phenomenology, using the two-potential formula of reaction theory to combine the non-perturbative LQCD potential with the perturbative Pomeron-exchange amplitude.

## Key numbers

- J/Ψ mass M_V = 3096.91 MeV; VMD coupling constant f_V = 11.2 (determined from J/Ψ → e+e- decay width)
- LQCD potential parameters: v_0 = (-0.06, -0.11) and α = (0.3, 0.5) GeV for pot-1 and pot-2 respectively
- Pomeron parameters for heavy quarks: β_c = 0.32 GeV^{-1}, α_0 = 1.25, α'_P = 0.25 GeV^{-2}, μ_0^2 = 1.1 GeV^2
- Off-shell factor F^off = 0.75 for pot-1 and F^off = 0.41 for pot-2 needed to fit JLab data
- Model describes data up to invariant mass W = 300 GeV
- Lattice spacing ~0.1 fm limits accuracy of potential at r < 0.4 fm

## Context

This work extends the Pomeron-exchange model of Donnachie and Landshoff (1992) and the earlier formulations by Oh, Lee, and Wu to incorporate a J/Ψ-nucleon potential from lattice QCD calculations by Kawanai and Sasaki (2010). The resulting Pomeron-LQCD model bridges non-perturbative LQCD input with perturbative Regge phenomenology, enabling description of J/Ψ photo-production from threshold to high energies. Future improvements require more accurate LQCD extraction of the J/Ψ-N potential at short distances (r < 0.4 fm) and a QCD-based prediction of the off-shell factor F^off, such as from the qq-bar loop model explored by Pich.
