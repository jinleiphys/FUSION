# Primordial Lithium Puzzle and the Axion Quark Nugget Dark Matter Model

> Astrophysics today faces a number of mysteries which defiant their resolutions in spite of drastic improvements in instrumental design, better technique being developed, and gradual improvements in theoretical and computation methods over last decades. Primordial Lithium Puzzle is known to stay with us for at least two decades, and it is very likely that its final resolution will require some fundamentally new ideas, novel frameworks and a non-conventional paradigms. We propose that Primordial Lithium Puzzle finds its natural resolution within the so-called Axion Quark Nugget (AQN) dark matter model. This model was invented long ago as a natural explanation of the observed ratio $Ω_{\rm dark} \sim Ω_{\rm visible}$ without any references to BBN physics. In this new paradigm, in contrast with conventional WIMP (Weakly Interacting Massive Particle) framework the dark matter (DM) takes the form of the macroscopically large quark nuggets without requiring any new fields beyond the standard model physics, except for the axion. The time evolution of these AQNs in primordial soup at $T\sim 20$ KeV suggests a strong suppression of the abundances of nuclei with high charges $Z\geq 3$. This suppression mechanism represents the resolution of the primordial lithium abundance within AQN dark matter scenario.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the Primordial Lithium Puzzle, the discrepancy between predicted and observed abundances of \(^7\)Li, can be resolved within the Axion Quark Nugget (AQN) dark matter model, specifically through the capture and annihilation of nuclei with charge \(Z \geq 3\) by negatively charged antinuggets at temperatures around \(T \sim 20\) KeV, while leaving lighter nuclei unaffected.

## Method
The authors analyze the time evolution of AQNs in the primordial plasma at \(T \sim 20\) KeV, focusing on the electrostatic attraction between positively charged nuclei (such as \(^7\)Be and \(^7\)Li) and negatively charged antinuggets. They apply the Thomas-Fermi model to describe the electrosphere structure of the nuggets, including the density profile of positrons, and estimate the capture probability for high-Z nuclei, which scales exponentially with nuclear charge \(Z\). The suppression of \(^7\)Li abundance arises from annihilation of these captured nuclei inside the antinugget cores.

## Key numbers
- The predicted abundance of \(^7\)Li from standard Big Bang Nucleosynthesis is about **3 times larger** than the observed value, as cited from the Particle Data Group.
- The suppression mechanism operates at a temperature of **\(T \sim 20\) KeV**, where the positron number density in the plasma becomes comparable to the baryon number density.
- The typical baryon charge of an AQN ranges from **\(B \sim 10^{23}\) to \(B \sim 10^{28}\)**, with a mean value \(\langle B \rangle \sim 10^{26}\) used for numerical estimates.
- The baryon-to-photon ratio is \(\eta \sim 5 \times 10^{-10}\), and the formation temperature of nuggets is \(T_{\rm form} \simeq 41\) MeV.
- The lepton chemical potential at the nugget surface is approximately **\(\mu \approx 25\) MeV**, based on QCD estimates from the color superconducting phase.

## Context
This work extends the AQN dark matter model, previously applied to explain the galactic 511 KeV line (Oaknin 2004, Zhitnitsky 2006) and solar corona heating (Zhitnitsky 2017, Raza 2018), to the long-standing Primordial Lithium Puzzle, which has resisted resolution for over two decades. The proposal contrasts with conventional WIMP-based dark matter scenarios and with prior solutions classified as astrophysical, nuclear physics, or beyond-Standard-Model approaches, as reviewed in a recent BBN review paper (2018). If confirmed, this mechanism would provide a unified framework linking dark matter, baryogenesis, and light-element abundances.
