# Experimental study of $^{53}$Cr via the $(d,pγ)$ reaction

> Excited states in $^{53}$Cr were studied via the $^{52}$Cr$(d,pγ)$ reaction up to the neutron-separation threshold. Proton-$γ$ angular correlations and $γ$ decay branching ratios were measured in particle-$γ$ coincidences between the Super-Enge Split-Pole Spectrograph (SE-SPS) and CeBr$_3$ Array (CeBrA) demonstrator of the John D. Fox Accelerator Laboratory at Florida State University. Previous spin-parity assignments from a $(d,p)$ singles experiment at the SE-SPS are supported and $γ$-ray transitions in $^{53}$Cr reported. We firmly assign higher-lying excited states to $^{53}$Cr because overlapping excited states and contaminants could be identified better due to the complementary $γ$-decay information. We also correct some of the previously reported excitation energies and present a reanalysis of previously measured $^{52}$Cr$(d,p){}^{53}${Cr} angular distributions guided by the complementary $γ$-ray information. Based on this reanalysis, the fragmentation of the neutron $2p_{3/2}$, $2p_{1/2}$, $1f_{5/2}$, $1g_{9/2}$, and $2d_{5/2}$ single-particle strengths is reassessed for $^{53}$Cr. A comparison to the corresponding strengths in $^{55}$Fe is presented.

## Key claim

The paper uses complementary gamma-ray information from a $(d,p\gamma)$ experiment to reassign and correct excitation energies and spin-parity assignments for states in $^{53}$Cr, particularly for higher-lying states above 4.1 MeV where overlapping states and contaminants had previously caused ambiguities in $(d,p)$ singles experiments.

## Method

The experiment employed the $^{52}$Cr$(d,p\gamma)$ reaction at 16 MeV deuteron energy using the Super-Enge Split-Pole Spectrograph (SE-SPS) and CeBr$_3$ Array (CeBrA) demonstrator at Florida State University. Proton-gamma coincidences were measured at a laboratory scattering angle of 37 degrees with two magnetic field settings (8.3 kG and 7.5 kG), and gamma-ray gates on known transitions were used to filter states belonging to $^{53}$Cr. Previously measured $(d,p)$ angular distributions from Ril23a were reanalyzed using the coupled-channels program CHUCK3 with adiabatic distorted wave approximation, guided by the new gamma-decay information.

## Key numbers

- Neutron separation energy for $^{53}$Cr: $S_n = 7939.12(14)$ keV
- SE-SPS energy resolution for protons: approximately 50 keV (FWHM)
- Solid-angle acceptance of SE-SPS: $\Delta \Omega = 4.6$ msr
- Gamma-decay intensities for the 2657-keV state: 42(7) for the 1366-keV transition and 100(7) for the 1650-keV transition, in agreement with adopted values of 39(10) and 100(10)
- Gamma-decay intensities for the 4135-keV state: 100(17) for the 1810-keV transition, 84(15) for the 3121-keV transition, and 25(8) for the 4119-keV transition
- Multipole-mixing ratio for the 2417-keV $9/2^+ \rightarrow 7/2^-_1$ transition from the 3707-keV state: $\delta = 0.07^{+0.05}_{-0.04}$, confirming dominant $E1$ character
- Excitation energy of a newly observed state at approximately 7891 keV from $(d,p)$ singles data

## Context

This work extends previous $(d,p)$ singles studies of $^{53}$Cr by Ril23a and the broader program mapping single-particle strengths in $N=29$ isotones at the SE-SPS, including studies of $^{51}$Ti, $^{55}$Fe, and $^{52}$V. The complementary gamma-ray information enables better identification of overlapping states and contaminants, correcting excitation energies reported in Ril23a and providing a reassessment of the fragmentation of neutron $2p_{3/2}$, $2p_{1/2}$, $1f_{5/2}$, $1g_{9/2}$, and $2d_{5/2}$ single-particle strengths. The results are compared to the $N=29$ isotone $^{55}$Fe studied by Ril22a and to predictions from covariant density functional theory calculations using the FSUGarnet, RMF022, and RMF028 functionals.
