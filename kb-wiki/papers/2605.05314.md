# First next-to-next-to-leading-order extraction of fragmentation functions for Lambda hyperons

> We present MAPFF1.0_Lambda, the first global analysis at next-to-next-to-leading order in perturbative QCD of the collinear unpolarised fragmentation functions of Lambda hyperons. The fit is based on data from single-inclusive electron-positron annihilation, and from both neutral-current and -- for the first time -- charged-current semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering. We have adopted a statistical framework based on Monte Carlo sampling and parametrised fragmentation functions in terms of a neural network. The fragmentation function set comprises a total of seven independent parton flavours, allowing for the first independent determination of valence-quark distributions. Our analysis offers new insights into the hadronisation mechanism of strange baryons and establishes a baseline for future phenomenological and experimental investigations.

## Key claim

The paper presents MAPFF1.0_Lambda, the first global extraction of collinear unpolarised fragmentation functions for Lambda hyperons at next-to-next-to-leading order in perturbative QCD, achieving separate determination of valence-quark distributions for the first time by including charged-current semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering data.

## Method

The authors performed a global fit to single-inclusive electron-positron annihilation data and both neutral-current and charged-current semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering data, using a Monte Carlo sampling framework with neural network parametrisation of seven independent parton flavours (u, d, s valence quarks, light sea quarks, charm, bottom, and gluon). The fit was carried out at both NLO and NNLO accuracy in perturbative QCD, with the NNLO SIDIS coefficient functions taken from Bonino (2024, 2025) and implemented in the APFEL++ library. The parametrisation scale was set to 5 GeV, and the NNPDF31 parton distribution set was used for SIDIS cross section calculations.

## Key numbers

- Global chi-squared per data point of 1.09 for 241 total data points at NNLO
- 185 SIA data points included from experiments spanning center-of-mass energies from 10 GeV to 91.2 GeV
- 56 SIDIS data points included, comprising 41 neutral-current and 15 charged-current measurements
- 100 Monte Carlo replicas generated for the final fragmentation function set
- 97 free parameters in the neural network architecture (one hidden layer with 10 nodes, 7 output nodes)
- Minimum Q-squared cut of 1 GeV-squared applied to all datasets
- SIA data restricted to z greater than 0.075 for energies below the Z mass, and z greater than 0.02 at the Z mass
- SIDIS data restricted to z greater than 0.1 for cross sections differential in x_F or x_P, with stricter cuts for z-differential data (z greater than 0.3 for Lambda, z greater than 0.2 for anti-Lambda)

## Context

This work extends previous Lambda fragmentation function extractions by the DSV group (deFlorian, 1997) at NLO, the AKK group (Albino, 2005, 2008) at NLO, and the NPC23 group (Gao, 2025) at NLO, all of which extracted only the sum of Lambda and anti-Lambda and did not achieve separate valence-quark distributions. The inclusion of charged-current SIDIS data for the first time enables independent determination of valence u, d, and s quark fragmentation functions, providing new insights into the hadronisation mechanism of strange baryons and establishing a baseline for future transverse-momentum-dependent analyses that require collinear fragmentation functions as input.
