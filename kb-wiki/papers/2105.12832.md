# Nuclear superfluidity at finite temperature

> The equation of motion for the two-fermion two-time correlation function in the pairing channel is considered at finite temperature. Within the Matsubara formalism, the Dyson-type Bethe-Salpeter equation (Dyson-BSE) with the frequency-dependent interaction kernel is obtained. Similarly to the case of zero temperature, it is decomposed into the static and dynamical components, where the former is given by the contraction of the bare interaction with the two-fermion density and the latter is represented by the double contraction of the four-fermion two-time correlation function, or propagator, with two interaction matrix elements. The dynamical kernel with the four-body propagator, being formally exact, requires approximations to avoid generating prohibitively complicated hierarchy of equations. We focus on the approximation where the dynamical interaction kernel is truncated on the level of two-body correlation functions, neglecting the irreducible three-body and higher-rank correlations. Such a truncation leads to the dynamical kernel with the coupling between correlated fermionic pairs, which can be interpreted as emergent bosonic quasibound states, or phonons, of normal and superfluid nature. The latter ones are, thus, the mediators of the dynamical superfluid pairing. In this framework, we obtained the closed system of equations for the fermionic particle-hole and particle-particle propagators. This allows us to study the temperature dependence of the pairing gap beyond the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer approximation, that is implemented for medium-heavy nuclear systems. The cases of 68Ni and 44,46Ca are discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The paper derives a formally exact Dyson-type Bethe-Salpeter equation for the two-fermion correlation function in the pairing channel at finite temperature, but the numerical results for the pairing gap are obtained under the approximation that the dynamical interaction kernel is truncated at the level of two-body correlation functions, neglecting irreducible three-body and higher-rank correlations, and using an effective monopole-monopole force for the static kernel rather than a full bare interaction.

## Method

The authors start from the equation of motion for the two-fermion two-time correlation function (particle-particle propagator) in the Matsubara imaginary-time formalism, obtaining a Dyson-type Bethe-Salpeter equation with a frequency-dependent interaction kernel. The kernel is decomposed into a static part (from the bare interaction contracted with the two-fermion density) and a dynamical part (from double contraction of the four-fermion propagator with two interaction matrix elements). The dynamical kernel is approximated by a cluster decomposition retaining only two-body correlation functions, which leads to coupling between correlated fermionic pairs interpreted as emergent bosonic phonons. The resulting closed system of equations for particle-hole and particle-particle propagators is then reduced to a pairing gap equation of BCS form but with a more complicated interaction kernel containing both static and dynamical components, and solved numerically for finite nuclei using a relativistic mean-field basis with the NL3 parametrization and finite-temperature relativistic random phase approximation for the phonon vertices.

## Key numbers

* For the critical temperature of the superfluid-to-normal phase transition, the BCS theory predicts T_c ~ 0.6 Delta(T=0), where Delta(T=0) is the zero-temperature pairing gap.
* The pairing gap equation (Eq. 20) has the form Delta_1(T) = - sum_2 V_{1bar1 2bar2} Delta_2(T)(1-2f_2(T))/(2E_2), where f_2(T) = 1/(exp(E_2/T)+1) and E_2 = sqrt((tilde_epsilon_2 - tilde_lambda)^2 + Delta_2^2).
* The numerical implementation is applied to the medium-heavy nuclear systems 68Ni and 44,46Ca, using the NL3 parametrization of the relativistic mean field.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature formalism of Litvinova and Schuck (2020) for the particle-particle propagator to finite temperature using the Matsubara formalism, building on the equation-of-motion framework for correlation functions developed by Dukelsky, Roepke, and Schuck (1998) and others. It goes beyond the standard Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) theory by including dynamical effects from induced pairing mediated by collective phonons, similar to how particle-vibration coupling was shown to contribute significantly to the pairing gap in phenomenological frameworks by Barranco, Broglia, Gori et al. (1999) and Idini, Potel, Barranco et al. (2015). The approach enables systematic study of how the temperature dependence of the induced pairing differs from the simple BCS temperature dependence, with potential applications to nuclear reactions in stellar environments such as electron capture in collapsing stars and r-process nucleosynthesis in neutron star mergers.
