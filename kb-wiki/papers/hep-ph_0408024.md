# AAC analysis of polarized parton distributions with uncertainties

> We report recent studies of the Asymmetry Analysis Collaboration (AAC) on polarized parton distribution functions (PDFs). Using the data on the spin symmetry A_1 in deep inelastic lepton scattering, we investigate optimum polarized PDFs. Their uncertainties are estimated by the Hessian method. The uncertainties are large for the polarized antiquark and gluon distributions. We discuss the role of accurate SLAC-E155 proton data on the determination of the PDFs. The obtained distributions are compared with other parametrization results.

## Key claim
The AAC03 analysis finds that including the accurate SLAC-E155 proton data significantly reduces the uncertainties of all polarized parton distributions, including the gluon, due to error correlations between the polarized antiquark and gluon distributions; however, the uncertainties for the polarized gluon distribution remain so large that even a zero gluon contribution is allowed at the current stage.

## Method
The authors performed a global chi-squared fit of spin asymmetry A_1 data from deep inelastic lepton-nucleon scattering experiments (EMC, SMC, SLAC-E130, E142, E143, E154, E155, and HERMES) to determine polarized parton distribution functions (PDFs) at an initial scale Q_0^2 = 1 GeV^2. The polarized PDFs were parameterized using a functional form multiplied by the unpolarized GRV98 PDFs, with parameters for u_v, d_v, flavor-symmetric antiquarks, and gluon distributions optimized via the CERN MINUIT program. Uncertainties were estimated using the Hessian method with a Delta chi-squared value of 12.647 for eleven parameters, corresponding to a one-sigma error range.

## Key numbers
- The Hessian uncertainty analysis used Delta chi-squared = 12.647, corresponding to a one-sigma confidence level for eleven fit parameters.
- The initial scale for PDF evolution was Q_0^2 = 1 GeV^2.
- The analysis included data from eight experiments: EMC, SMC, SLAC-E130, E142, E143, E154, E155, and HERMES.

## Context
This work extends the previous AAC00 analysis (Hirai, Kumano, Saito 2000) by incorporating the accurate SLAC-E155 proton data, which significantly reduced the uncertainty bands for all polarized PDFs. The results are compared with other contemporary parametrizations including GRSV (Gluck, Reya, Stratmann, Vogelsang), LSS (Leader, Sidorov, Stamenov), and BB (Blumlein, Bottcher), finding general agreement within the uncertainty bands. The analysis enables future experimental planning, particularly for prompt-photon measurements at RHIC to better constrain the polarized gluon distribution.
