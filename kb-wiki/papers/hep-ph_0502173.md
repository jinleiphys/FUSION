# Quark-model study of few-baryon systems

> We review the application of non-relativistic constituent quark models to study one, two and three non-strange baryon systems. We present results for the baryon spectra, potentials and observables of the NN, N$Δ$, $ΔΔ$ and NN$^*(1440)$ systems, and also for the binding energies of three non-strange baryon systems. We make emphasis on observable effects related to quark antisymmetry and its interplay with quark dynamics.

## Key claim

This review argues that non-relativistic constituent quark models incorporating chiral potentials provide the most successful and consistent microscopic description of both the baryon spectrum and the baryon-baryon interaction, but the authors acknowledge that the connection to QCD is not clearly established and there is no sound systematics to obtain corrections.

## Method

The authors review the chiral constituent quark model (CCQM) where the quark-quark potential combines a linear confining interaction, a one-gluon exchange (OGE) potential derived from the non-relativistic reduction of QCD, and pseudoscalar (pion) and scalar (sigma) exchange potentials derived from a chiral Lagrangian incorporating spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. Two-baryon wave functions are constructed using harmonic oscillator approximations for single baryons, with the full six-quark antisymmetrizer applied to account for quark Pauli blocking. The model parameters are fitted from the NN interaction and the non-strange baryon spectrum, and then applied without further adjustment to study NN, NΔ, ΔΔ, and NN*(1440) systems, as well as three-baryon binding energies.

## Key numbers

* Constituent quark mass: m_q = 313 MeV (from NN scattering) or 350 MeV (from nucleon magnetic moments)
* Harmonic oscillator parameter: b = 0.518 fm (determined from simultaneous study of S-wave NN phase shifts and deuteron properties)
* Strong coupling constant: α_s = 0.485 (for r_0 = 0.25 fm, adjusted to reproduce the Δ–N mass difference)
* Confinement strength: a_c = 67.0 MeV fm⁻¹
* Chiral coupling constant: α_ch = 0.0269 (derived from πNN coupling constant f²_πNN/4π = 0.078)
* Pseudoscalar (pion) mass: m_PS = 0.7 fm⁻¹ (identified with physical pion mass)
* Scalar (sigma) mass: m_S = 3.42 fm⁻¹ (from PCAC relation m²_S = m²_PS + 4m²_q)
* Chiral symmetry breaking scale: Λ = 4.2 fm⁻¹ (fixed from pp → nΔ⁺⁺ reaction)
* Regularization parameter: r_0 = 0.25 fm

## Context

This review extends the line of non-relativistic constituent quark models that began with Dalitz (1965) and the OGE-based models of de Rujula, Georgi and Glashow (1975), and addresses the failure of earlier hybrid quark models (Oka 1983, Fujiwara 1986) to provide sufficient medium-range NN attraction. The CCQM described here, developed by Oka and collaborators (1990) and Fernandez and collaborators (1993), successfully reproduces NN phase shifts and deuteron properties while also predicting baryon spectra with the same parameter set. The review enables parameter-free studies of other baryon-baryon interactions (NΔ, ΔΔ, NN*(1440)) and three-baryon systems including the triton, treating nucleons and resonances on the same footing.
