# Tunneling time in coupled-channel systems

> In present work, we present a couple-channel formalism for the description of tunneling time of a quantum particle through a composite compound with multiple energy levels or a complex structure that can be reduced to a quasi-one-dimensional multiple-channel system.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the two-component (complex) tunneling time formalism from single-channel elastic scattering to coupled-channel systems that include inelastic effects, showing that when inelasticity is significant the Büttiker tunneling time is no longer given by the energy derivative of the scattering phase shift but instead by the energy derivative of the transmission amplitude phase, which depends on both the inelasticity parameter η and the scattering phase shifts.

## Method

The authors develop a formal coupled-channel scattering theory using Lippmann-Schwinger equations and the Møller operator formalism, deriving a generalized Friedel formula and a Muskhelishvili-Omnès representation for the determinant of the transmission amplitude matrix in multi-channel systems. They then construct an exactly solvable two-channel model with contact interactions (delta-function potentials) representing an electron scattering off a composite compound with ground and excited states, where the two channels are coupled by a strength parameter g. The traversal time is defined as the integrated trace of the full Green's function matrix over the barrier region, and explicit expressions are derived for both diagonal (same-channel) and off-diagonal (cross-channel) components of the complex tunneling time.

## Key numbers

- The inelasticity parameter η ranges from η = 1 (elastic case) to η = 0 (totally inelastic case), as defined in the text.
- The diagonal transmission amplitude in channel i is given by t_ii = √(1 + η² + 2η cos(2δ_i)) e^{iφ_i}/2, where φ_i = tan⁻¹[η sin(2δ_i)/(η cos(2δ_i) + 1)].
- The Büttiker tunneling time in the i-th individual channel is τ₁^{(ii)} = (μ_i/k_i)(∂φ_i/∂k_i) + (μ_i/k_i²)[4k_iL + η sin(2δ_i + 2k_iL) - sin(2k_iL)]/2, where L is the half-size of the compound.
- The off-diagonal component Im[τ_E^{(12)}] = 2√(μ₁μ₂/k₁k₂) √(1-η²)/(k₁+k₂) × sin(δ₁+δ₂+(k₁+k₂)L/2) sin((k₁+k₂)L/2), which can be negative in contrast to the always-positive diagonal components.

## Context

This work extends the two-component tunneling time formalism developed by Büttiker and Landauer (PhysRevB.27.6178, PhysRevLett.49.1739) and later elaborated by Gasparian and collaborators (PhysRevB.47.2038, PhysRevB.51.6743, PhysRevA.54.4022, PhysRevA.107.032210) from single-channel elastic scattering to multi-channel inelastic systems. The formalism enables proper treatment of inelastic effects in tunneling experiments such as attoclock measurements (Kheifets 2020, Ramos 2020, PhysRevLett.127.133001) and provides a mechanism to generate effective complex potentials via Hamiltonian projection (MUGA2004357), which may explain the persistence of the Hartman effect in non-Hermitian scattering systems. The authors note that long-range Coulomb effects, crucial for attoclock tunneling ionization time experiments, are not yet included and require future work.
