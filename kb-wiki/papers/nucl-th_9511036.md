# Roper Excitation in Alpha-Proton Scattering

> We study the Roper excitation in the $(α,α')$ reaction. We consider all processes which may be relevant in the Roper excitation region, namely, Roper excitation in the target, Roper excitation in the projectile, and double $Δ$ excitation processes. The theoretical investigation shows that the Roper excitation in the proton target mediated by an isoscalar exchange is the dominant mechanism in the process. We determine an effective isoscalar interaction by means of which the experimental cross section is well reproduced. This should be useful to make predictions in related reactions and is a first step to construct eventually a microscopic $NN \rightarrow NN^*$ transition potential, for which the present reaction does not offer enough information.

## Key claim

The Roper excitation in the proton target, mediated by an effective isoscalar exchange, is the dominant mechanism in the (α,α') reaction, but the extracted isoscalar interaction should not be interpreted as a literal σ-meson exchange; it is simply a parameterization of the effective T=0 exchange channel that accounts for σ, ω, and correlation effects.

## Method

The authors calculated the (α,α') reaction on a proton target at intermediate energies by considering five mechanisms: Δ excitation in the projectile (already evaluated in prior work), Roper excitation in the target via isoscalar exchange, Roper excitation in the projectile decaying to πN, Roper excitation in the projectile decaying to ππN, and double Δ excitation. They used a theoretical model with σ-meson exchange as a parameterization of the isoscalar interaction, with the σNN coupling taken from the Bonn potential and the σNN* coupling treated as a free parameter fitted to reproduce the experimental cross section. The Roper width was modeled including both πN and ππN decay channels, and the interference between the target Roper and projectile Δ amplitudes was included.

## Key numbers

- The Roper mass used in the best fit: M* = 1430 MeV
- The Roper width at the pole in the best fit: Γ*(s=M*²) = 300 MeV
- The effective σNN* coupling from the best fit: g²_σNN*/4π = 1.33
- The strength of the Roper excitation at its peak after subtracting the Δ background: approximately 0.25 mb/sr/MeV
- The σ-meson mass used in the model: m_σ = 550 MeV
- The σ form factor cutoff: Λ_σ = 1700 MeV
- The σNN coupling from the Bonn potential: g²_σNN/4π = 5.69
- The Roper mass used in the initial model: M* = 1440 MeV
- The Roper width used in the initial model: Γ*(s=M*²) = 350 MeV
- The ratio of coupling constants suppressing projectile Roper relative to projectile Δ excitation: (f'/f*)⁴ = 2.4 × 10⁻³

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of the (α,α') reaction on protons previously performed for Δ excitation by the same authors in ref. [5] (1995), which provided the background subtraction necessary to isolate the Roper signal. The experimental data were taken from ref. [1] (Morsch et al.) and reanalyzed with a more precise background subtraction in ref. [9]. The paper enables future studies of N* excitation in nuclei using the (α,α') reaction as a tool to investigate modifications of N* properties in nuclear medium, and provides a first step toward constructing a microscopic NN → NN* transition potential, though the present reaction alone does not offer enough information for that purpose.
