# Local equilibrium in heavy ion collisions. Microscopic model versus statistical model analysis

> The assumption of local equilibrium in relativistic heavy ion collisions at energies from 10.7 AGeV (AGS) up to 160 AGeV (SPS) is checked in the microscopic transport model. Dynamical calculations performed for a central cell in the reaction are compared to the predictions of the thermal statistical model. We find that kinetic, thermal and chemical equilibration of the expanding hadronic matter are nearly approached late in central collisions at AGS energy for $t \geq 10$ fm/$c$ in a central cell. At these times the equation of state may be approximated by a simple dependence $P \cong (0.12-0.15) ε$. Increasing deviations of the yields and the energy spectra of hadrons from statistical model values are observed for increasing energy, 40 AGeV and 160 AGeV. These violations of local equilibrium indicate that a fully equilibrated state is not reached, not even in the central cell of heavy ion collisions at energies above 10 AGeV. The origin of these findings is traced to the multiparticle decays of strings and many-body decays of resonances.

## Key claim

Local equilibrium (kinetic, thermal, and chemical) is nearly approached in a central cell of heavy ion collisions at AGS energy (10.7 AGeV) for times t >= 10 fm/c, but increasing deviations from statistical model predictions are observed at higher SPS energies (40 AGeV and 160 AGeV), indicating that a fully equilibrated state is not reached even in the central cell at energies above 10 AGeV.

## Method

The authors used the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) microscopic transport model to simulate central heavy ion collisions at 10.7 AGeV (Au+Au), 40 AGeV (Pb+Pb), and 160 AGeV (Pb+Pb). They extracted energy density, baryon density, and strangeness density from a central cubic cell of volume 125 fm^3 and used these as input to a statistical model of an ideal hadron gas containing the same 55 baryon and 32 meson species. The hadron yields, energy spectra, pressure, and temperature from the dynamical UrQMD calculations were compared to the equilibrium statistical model predictions at times from 10 to 18 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- Kinetic equilibrium is reached at t = 8-10 fm/c for all three energies, with pressure components agreeing within 5% accuracy
- The equation of state in the central cell follows P = (0.12-0.15) epsilon, with values of 0.12 at AGS, 0.13 at 40 AGeV, and 0.15 at 160 AGeV
- Entropy per baryon ratio S/A = s/rho_B is approximately 12 at AGS energy and 38 +/- 2 at SPS energy
- Baryon density in the central cell at late stages is not larger than 0.15 fm^{-3} for all three reactions
- At AGS energy, about 70% of total energy is carried by baryons; at SPS, more than 70% is in the mesonic sector
- The crossing times for the Lorentz-contracted nuclei are 5.46 fm/c (10.7 AGeV), 2.9 fm/c (40 AGeV), and 1.44 fm/c (160 AGeV)
- At 160 AGeV, nucleon "temperature" from UrQMD is 30-40 MeV below the statistical model temperature, and pion "temperature" is 50-60 MeV below
- Pions at SPS energy undergo about 1.6-1.7 elastic collisions while baryons suffer more than 20 strong interactions

## Context

This work extends earlier studies using QGSM, RQMD, and UrQMD models that found no thin or thick freeze-out layer in heavy ion collisions (Bravina 1995, Sorge 1996, UrQMD 1998). It contradicts the assumption of local equilibrium commonly made in hydrodynamic models such as those based on Landau's theory (Landau 1953, Landau and Belenkij 1956). The finding that chemical equilibrium is not achieved, particularly for pions and kaons at higher energies, enables further investigation of the peculiar nonequilibrium state of hot hadronic matter and suggests that statistical model fits to experimental data may yield "apparent" temperatures that are too high, potentially reaching into the quark-gluon plasma phase transition region predicted by the MIT bag model.
