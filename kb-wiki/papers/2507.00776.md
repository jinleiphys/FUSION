# Hybrid Quark Stars with Quark-Quark Phase Transitions

> We explore the possibility of phase transitions between different quark matter phases occurring within quark stars, giving rise to the hybrid quark stars (HybQSs). First, we obtain the generic phase diagram of possible mass-radius relation forms for the HybQSs. Then, utilizing a well-established general parameterization of interacting quark matter, we construct quark star models featuring sharp first-order quark-quark phase transitions of various types, in contrast to the hadron-quark transition in conventional hybrid stars. We systematically investigate how recent observations, such as the pulsar mass measurement $M_{\rm TOV}\gtrsim2M_{\odot}$ and the GW170817's tidal deformability bound $Λ_{1.4M_{\odot}}<800$, constrain the viable parameter space. We also identified twin stars in some of the HybQS parameter space. Furthermore, we found that the quark-quark phase transitions in hybrid quark stars may also cause the deviation from the approximate universal relation between the dominant postmerger frequency $f_{\rm peak}$ and tidal deformability $Λ_{1.35M_{\odot}}$, which was previously believed to only be caused by the hadron-quark phase transitions in hybrid neutron stars. This work unveils new possibilities of phase transitions and the resulting new types of compact stars in realistic astrophysical scenarios.

# Hybrid Quark Stars with Quark-Quark Phase Transitions

## Key claim

The paper proposes that phase transitions between different quark matter phases (rather than between hadronic and quark matter) can occur within quark stars, forming hybrid quark stars (HybQSs), and that such quark-quark phase transitions may cause deviations from the approximate universal relation between postmerger gravitational wave frequency and tidal deformability, a signature previously attributed only to hadron-quark transitions in hybrid neutron stars.

## Method

The authors construct quark star models using a well-established parameterized interacting quark matter equation of state (EOS) with parameters representing the bag constant B, color-superconducting gap, strange quark mass, and perturbative QCD corrections. They consider Maxwell constructions for sharp first-order phase transitions between different quark matter phases (unpaired two-flavor, 2SC, unpaired three-flavor, 2SC+s, and CFL) and solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations to obtain mass-radius relations. They then apply astrophysical constraints from pulsar mass measurements and the GW170817 tidal deformability bound to identify viable parameter space, and compute f-mode frequencies to test deviations from universal relations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass constraint: M_TOV >= 2 solar masses, from pulsar measurements (demorest2010shapiro)
- Tidal deformability constraint: Lambda(1.4 solar masses) < 800, from the GW170817 event (LIGOScientific:2017vwq)
- For absolutely stable strange quark matter, the bag constant must satisfy B < (162.8 MeV)^4 approximately 91 MeV/fm^3
- For absolutely stable up-down quark matter, the bag constant must satisfy B < (144.38 MeV)^4 approximately 56.8 MeV/fm^3
- The critical energy density jump for stable hybrid branches follows Delta_rho_crit / rho_trans = 1/2 + (3/2)(p_trans / rho_trans)
- For the unpaired 2f to unpaired 3f transition, B_crust less than approximately 46 MeV/fm^3 is determined by the Lambda(1.4 solar masses) = 800 constraint
- The maximum deviation of f(2 solar masses) from the universal relation reaches 220 Hz, corresponding to a deviation in f_peak of about 368 Hz
- The fitted universal relation between f_peak and f(2 solar masses) is f_peak = 1.675 * f(2 solar masses) - 1054 Hz

## Context

This work extends the study of phase transitions in compact stars beyond the conventional hadron-quark hybrid star scenario explored by Alford (2013) and bauswein (2019). Unlike previous work by Alford (2017), Li (2023), and Sedrakian (2023) that considered quark-quark transitions as secondary transitions within hadronic hybrid stars, this paper investigates quark stars that are entirely self-bound by quark matter. The results enable future gravitational wave observations to potentially distinguish between different types of phase transitions in compact stars, and the authors identify twin star solutions in some parameter regions, similar to those found in hadron-quark hybrid star studies by Alford (2013).
