# Contact interaction in the nonrelativistic pion absorption operator

## Key claim

The paper proposes a consistent scheme for obtaining the nonrelativistic operator of pion absorption by an NN-pair that includes chiral scalar exchange, and shows that the contact term in the absorption operator arising from the scalar exchange gives a contribution that is more than one order of magnitude larger than the contribution from Z-diagrams for reactions such as pp → dπ⁺ near threshold.

## Method

The authors start from a chirally invariant Lagrangian containing nucleon, pion, and chiral scalar fields, then apply a unitary transformation to convert from pseudoscalar to pseudovector πNN coupling. They derive the nonrelativistic limit of the pion absorption amplitude on a nucleon caused by scalar-isoscalar interaction, separating the amplitude into terms corresponding to nucleon propagation and contact terms, and show that the total contribution coincides with leading terms from a Foldy–Wouthuysen transformation for a nucleon in a scalar potential.

## Key numbers

- The contact term contribution to the absorption amplitude is proportional to −σ(p − p′ − q)/4m², while the leading πSNN term from the relativistic amplitude gives σ(p − p′)/2m², indicating the contact term is diminished by a factor of 1/2 near threshold where pion momentum is small.
- For the reaction pp → dπ⁺ near threshold, the contact term contribution from the S-scalar exchange was found to be more than one order of magnitude larger than the contribution from Z-diagrams, as previously shown by Maekava and da Rocha (1999).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Maekava and Robilotta (1998) and Maekava and da Rocha (1999), who showed that including a chiral scalar meson in the effective Lagrangian successfully describes three-nucleon interactions and near-threshold pion production in NN collisions. The paper demonstrates that the nonrelativistic reduction of the absorption operator must consistently include contact terms arising from the scalar exchange, and notes that these contact terms have been previously overlooked in treatments using static propagators. The formalism enables improved calculations of reactions such as pp → dπ⁺ and pp → NNππ near threshold, where the authors note that Roper N₁₁-resonance excitation must also be considered, following their own earlier work (Zaikin and Osipchuk, 1999).
