# The nuclear pseudospin symmetry along an isotopic chain

> We investigate the isospin dependence of pseudospin symmetry in the chain of tin isotopes (from $^{120}$Sn until $^{170}$Sn). Using a Woods-Saxon parametrization of the nuclear potential for these isotopes we study in detail the effect of the vector-isovector $ρ$ and Coulomb potentials in the energy splittings of neutron and proton pseudospin partners in the isotopic chain. We conclude that the realization of nuclear pseudospin symmetry does not change considerably with the mass number, and is always favored for neutrons. We also find that the $ρ$ potential accounts for essentially all the pseudospin isospin asymmetry observed and that the Coulomb potential plays a negligible role in this asymmetry. This can be explained by the dynamical nature of pseudospin symmetry in nuclei, namely the dependence of the pseudospin splittings on the shape of the nuclear mean-field potential.

## Key claim

The paper claims that pseudospin symmetry in tin isotopes does not change considerably with mass number and is always favored for neutrons, but this conclusion is hedged by the fact that it applies specifically to the Sn isotopic chain from \(^{120}\)Sn to \(^{170}\)Sn and relies on a Woods-Saxon parametrization fitted to a self-consistent relativistic mean-field calculation, not on direct experimental data.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation for nucleons using a Woods-Saxon parametrization of the nuclear potential \(\Sigma = V + S\) for the Sn isotopic chain, separating the potential into a central part, a vector-isovector \(\rho\) potential, and a Coulomb potential (for protons only). They computed the energy splittings of two pseudospin doublets, \([3s_{1/2} - 2d_{3/2}]\) and \([2d_{5/2} - 1g_{7/2}]\), for neutrons and protons as a function of mass number \(A\) from 120 to 170, and then selectively removed the \(\rho\) and Coulomb potentials to isolate their contributions.

## Key numbers

- For \(A = 150\), the energy splitting of the \([3s_{1/2}, 2d_{3/2}]\) doublet is 0.49 MeV for neutrons and 1.19 MeV for protons.
- For \(A = 150\), the energy splitting of the \([2d_{5/2}, 1g_{7/2}]\) doublet is 1.97 MeV for neutrons and 3.58 MeV for protons.
- Removing the \(\rho\) potential for neutrons at \(A = 150\) increases the \([3s_{1/2}, 2d_{3/2}]\) splitting to 0.86 MeV (from 0.49 MeV) and the \([2d_{5/2}, 1g_{7/2}]\) splitting to 2.76 MeV (from 1.97 MeV).
- Removing the \(\rho\) potential for protons at \(A = 150\) decreases the \([3s_{1/2}, 2d_{3/2}]\) splitting to 0.86 MeV (from 1.19 MeV) and the \([2d_{5/2}, 1g_{7/2}]\) splitting to 2.87 MeV (from 3.58 MeV).
- Removing the Coulomb potential for protons at \(A = 150\) changes the \([3s_{1/2}, 2d_{3/2}]\) splitting to 1.13 MeV (from 1.19 MeV) and the \([2d_{5/2}, 1g_{7/2}]\) splitting to 3.38 MeV (from 3.58 MeV).
- The central potential depth is \(\Sigma_{o\,c} = -69.94\) MeV, with radius \(R_c = 1.21 A^{1/3}\) fm and diffuseness \(a_c = 0.13 A^{1/3}\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous quantitative analysis of Coulomb and \(\rho\) potentials in pseudospin asymmetry (Lisboa, Malheiro, Alberto, 2003; PRC67) by examining the entire Sn isotopic chain rather than isolated nuclei. It builds on the relativistic interpretation of pseudospin symmetry by Ginocchio (1997) and the dynamical character established by Alberto et al. (2001) and by Marcos et al. (2001). The results enable future studies of pseudospin symmetry in other isotopic chains and confirm that the \(\rho\) potential, not the Coulomb potential, is the dominant source of isospin asymmetry in pseudospin doublets.
