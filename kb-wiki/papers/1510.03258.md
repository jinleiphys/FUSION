# A Monte Carlo algorithm for simulating fermions on Lefschetz thimbles

> A possible solution of the notorious sign problem preventing direct Monte Carlo calculations for systems with non-zero chemical potential is to deform the integration region in the complex plane to a Lefschetz thimble. We investigate this approach for a simple fermionic model. We introduce an easy to implement Monte Carlo algorithm to sample the dominant thimble. Our algorithm relies only on the integration of the gradient flow in the numerically stable direction, which gives it a distinct advantage over the other proposed algorithms. We demonstrate the stability and efficiency of the algorithm by applying it to an exactly solvable fermionic model and compare our results with the analytical ones. We report a very good agreement for a certain region in the parameter space where the dominant contribution comes from a single thimble, including a region where standard methods suffer from a severe sign problem. However, we find that there are also regions in the parameter space where the contribution from multiple thimbles is important, even in the continuum limit.

## Key claim

The authors propose a Monte Carlo algorithm for sampling a single Lefschetz thimble that avoids the numerically unstable downward gradient flow by instead using the stable upward flow, but they find that the single-thimble approximation fails in regions of parameter space where multiple thimbles contribute significantly, even in the continuum limit.

## Method

The algorithm parametrizes points on the thimble (far points) by their images near the critical point (near points) after a fixed flow time T along the stable upward gradient flow. A Metropolis Markov chain generates near points with a probability distribution that includes the Jacobian determinant of the flow map, and observables are computed from the corresponding far points weighted by the residual phase of the Jacobian. The tangent space at the near points is approximated by the positive eigenvectors of the real symmetric matrix constructed from the Hessian of the action at the critical point.

## Key numbers

- The algorithm is tested on a 0+1 dimensional Thirring model with N lattice sites, where N = beta/a is an even number.
- The lattice action uses a coupling 1/(2 hat g^2) multiplying the sum over t of (1 - cos z_t), with hat g^2 = g^2 a.
- The fermion matrix K contains the chemical potential hat mu = mu a and mass hat m = m a.
- The exact partition function is given analytically: Z = e^{-N alpha} / 2^{N-1} [ I_1^N(alpha) cosh(N hat mu) + I_0^N(alpha) cosh(N sinh^{-1}(hat m)) ].
- The exact charge density is: <n> = (1/N) (partial/partial hat mu) log Z = [ I_1^N(alpha) sinh(N hat mu) ] / [ I_1^N(alpha) cosh(N hat mu) + I_0^N(alpha) cosh(N sinh^{-1}(hat m)) ].

## Context

This work extends the Lefschetz thimble approach to a fermionic model with a sign problem, following earlier proposals for bosonic theories by Cristoforetti (2012, 2013) and others. The algorithm differs from prior Metropolis-based thimble sampling by Mukherjee (2013) by including the Jacobian in the effective action used for updating rather than as a reweighting factor. The model was previously used to test complex Langevin dynamics by Aarts (2008) and Pawlowski (2013, 2014), and hybrid Monte Carlo on Lefschetz thimbles by Fujii (2015). The results enable future application to higher-dimensional fermionic theories but also highlight the open problem of multi-thimble sampling.
