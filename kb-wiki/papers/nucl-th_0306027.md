# Hadron production in heavy ion collisions: Fragmentation and recombination from a dense parton phase

> We discuss hadron production in heavy ion collisions at RHIC. We argue that hadrons at transverse momenta P_T < 5 GeV are formed by recombination of partons from the dense parton phase created in central collisions at RHIC. We provide a theoretical description of the recombination process for P_T > 2 GeV. Below P_T = 2 GeV our results smoothly match a purely statistical description. At high transverse momentum hadron production is well described in the language of perturbative QCD by the fragmentation of partons. We give numerical results for a variety of hadron spectra, ratios and nuclear suppression factors. We also discuss the anisotropic flow v_2 and give results based on a flow in the parton phase. Our results are consistent with the existence of a parton phase at RHIC hadronizing at a temperature of 175 MeV and a radial flow velocity of 0.55c.

## Key claim

Hadrons with transverse momenta between 2 and 5 GeV/c in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC are formed by recombination of partons from a dense parton phase, rather than by fragmentation, with the transition between the two mechanisms occurring between 4 and 6 GeV/c depending on hadron species; the authors argue that recombination always wins over fragmentation for an exponentially falling parton spectrum, but fragmentation takes over for a power-law spectrum.

## Method

The authors develop a non-relativistic recombination model using overlap integrals between parton states and hadron wave functions, then generalize to a relativistic Wigner function formalism with light cone coordinates. They parametrize the parton phase at hadronization with a temperature of 175 MeV and radial flow velocity of 0.55c, compute hadron spectra from recombination of two quarks into mesons and three quarks into baryons, and compare these results with standard pQCD fragmentation using KKP fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers

- Hadronization temperature: 175 MeV
- Radial flow velocity: 0.55c
- Recombination dominates for P_T < 5 GeV/c; fragmentation dominates above 4-6 GeV/c depending on hadron species
- For an exponential parton spectrum w = exp(-P_T/T), the ratio of recombination to fragmentation scales as exp[-(P_T/T)(1 - 1/<z>)] where <z> is approximately 0.6 for valence quark fragmentation into pions
- The leading-order meson spectrum from recombination is proportional to C_M w(P_T/2)^2; the leading-order baryon spectrum is proportional to C_B w(P_T/3)^3
- The direct p/pi^0 ratio from recombination counting degeneracies is approximately 2, compared to approximately 0.2 from pQCD fragmentation
- Nuclear suppression factor R_AA below 4 GeV/c is close to 1 for protons and lambdas, while it is about 0.3 for pions
- The ratio of protons over positively charged pions is equal or above 1 for P_T > 1.5 GeV/c and approximately constant up to 4 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the recombination concept previously applied to forward hadron production in p+p collisions (Das Hwa 1977) and to leading particle effects in fixed-target experiments (E791 1996), and builds on recent recombination approaches to RHIC data (Fries et al. 2003, Greco et al. 2003). The paper provides a quantitative framework that explains the species-dependent nuclear suppression and large baryon/meson ratios observed by PHENIX and STAR collaborations, and enables systematic calculations of hadron spectra, ratios, and elliptic flow v_2 from a common parton phase description. The approach contrasts with purely perturbative energy loss models (Gyulassy and Wang 1994, Baier et al. 2001) that cannot explain the different behavior of baryons and mesons without species-dependent non-perturbative fragmentation functions.
