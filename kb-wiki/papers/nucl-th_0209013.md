# Break-up and electromagnetic response of light weakly-bound dicluster systems

> This study is focused on the break-up and electromagnetic response of light weakly-bound dicluster nuclei. The cluster picture in the case of $ ^7$Li is shown to be a very good approximation and in this framework we calculate nuclear structure observables. We solve the Schrödinger equation for the relative motion both for discrete and continuum states and this automatically takes into a proper account the role of resonances. A concentration of strength in the low energy continuum, solely due to the weakly-bound nature of the bound states is seen and explained as a favourable matching between the wavelengths of the initial and final states. Finally preliminary results on form factors are briefly outlined and their microscopic derivation as well as utilization in reaction studies is discussed.

# Break-up and electromagnetic response of light weakly-bound dicluster systems

## Key claim
The cluster picture of ⁷Li as an alpha-plus-triton dicluster provides a very good approximation for calculating nuclear structure observables, and a concentration of electromagnetic strength in the low-energy continuum arises solely from the weakly-bound nature of the bound states, explained by favourable wavelength matching between initial and final states.

## Method
The Schrödinger equation for the relative motion of the two clusters (treated as frozen spherical charge and mass distributions) was solved for both discrete and continuum states, including Coulomb and nuclear interactions. The nuclear interaction used a Woods-Saxon shape whose depth was adjusted to reproduce the correct energy for bound and resonant states. Wavefunctions were obtained that differ slightly from those of the Resonating Group Method (RGM) but have very similar spatial extension.

## Key numbers
- Electric quadrupole transition from the 3/2⁻ ground state to the 1/2⁻ excited bound state: B(E2) = 7.55 e²fm⁴, very close to measured values.
- Magnetic dipole transition from the 3/2⁻ ground state to the 1/2⁻ excited bound state: B(M1) = 2.45 μ², very close to measured values.
- Nuclear break-up remains dominant at a separation distance of 14 fm, about double the sum of radii in a reaction with a heavy target.

## Context
This work extends the dicluster description of ⁷Li previously used by several authors (Walliser, cited as Wal) and builds on the explanation of low-energy strength concentration given by Dasgupta and collaborators (Das2, Das1). The calculated charge and matter radii, as well as electric and matter quadrupole intrinsic moments, agree well with experimental data. The microscopic derivation of form factors aims to clarify the dependence on inter-ion distance and continuum energy, and to assess the relevance of nuclear break-up in reaction studies currently under investigation.
