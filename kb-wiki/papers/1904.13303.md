# Application of an ab-initio S-matrix to data analysis of transfer to the continuum reactions populating 11Be

> Recently, the bound and continuum spectrum of 11Be has been calculated within the ab-initio no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) method successfully reproducing the parity inversion in the ground state. The continuum spectrum obtained is in agreement with known experimental levels. The S-matrix contained in the NCSMC continuum wave functions of the n+10Be system is used in this work for the first time in a Transfer-to-the-Continuum (TC) reaction calculation. The TC approach is applied to study the excitation energy spectrum of 11Be measured in the 9Be(18O,16O)11Be reaction at 84 MeV. Previously known levels are confirmed and theoretical and experimental evidence for a 9/2+ state at Ex=5.8 MeV is given, whose configuration is thought to be 10Be(2+)+n(d5/2).

# Application of an ab-initio S-matrix to data analysis of transfer to the continuum reactions populating 11Be

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that an ab-initio S-matrix from the No-Core Shell Model with Continuum (NCSMC) can replace phenomenological optical potentials in Transfer-to-the-Continuum (TC) reaction calculations, reproducing the excitation energy spectrum of 11Be from the 9Be(18O,16O)11Be reaction at 84 MeV. However, the absolute theoretical cross sections are reliable only at the order-of-magnitude level, and the calculation required renormalization by a factor of 3.2 after folding with experimental resolution to match the data.

## Method

The authors performed a two-neutron transfer experiment using an 18O beam at 84 MeV incident energy on a 9Be target, with 16O ejectiles momentum-analyzed by the MAGNEX magnetic spectrometer over laboratory angles between 3 and 10 degrees. The reaction was modeled as two independent sequential single-neutron transfers: first populating bound states of 10Be (ground state, 2+1 at 3.4 MeV, and 2+2 at 6 MeV), then transferring a second neutron to continuum states of 11Be using the TC model. The S-matrix for the n+10Be system was obtained from NCSMC calculations using the chiral N2LOSAT NN+3N interaction, with a phenomenological adjustment (NCSMC-pheno) to reproduce experimental thresholds and resonance positions.

## Key numbers

- Incident beam energy: 84 MeV for 18O6+ on 9Be target
- Target thickness: 200 +/- 10 microgram/cm2 self-supporting 9Be
- Energy resolution: approximately 500 keV (FWHM)
- Angular resolution: 0.3 degrees
- Absolute cross section uncertainty: about 10%
- Experimental cross section for the 1.783 MeV d5/2 state: sigma_exp = 359 +/- 35 microbarn; theoretical: sigma_th = 175 microbarn
- Strong absorption radius: R_s = 1.4(A_c^(1/3) + A_t^(1/3))
- Impact parameter range from classical trajectory: 7 < b_c < 8 fm
- Experimental one-neutron transfer cross sections used: 2.1 mb (10Be_gs), 2.2 mb (10Be_2+1), 3.2 mb (10Be_2+2)
- NCSMC-pheno calculation predicts 9/2+ resonance at 5.59 MeV; experimental candidate observed at 5.3 MeV (with respect to n+10Be threshold) and at Ex = 5.8 MeV in the excitation energy spectrum

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the 9Be(18O,17O)10Be reaction (Bonaccorso et al., prior work) by adding a second neutron transfer to populate 11Be. It applies the NCSMC S-matrix from the ab-initio structure calculations of 11Be by the same group (Navratil et al., 2016; AC in the text) to replace phenomenological optical potentials previously used for n+9Be (Bonaccorso et al., prior work with DOM potentials). The approach enables the study of exotic nuclei at stable beam facilities, providing complementary information to radioactive ion beam experiments, and specifically confirms a 9/2+ state at Ex = 5.8 MeV with a [10Be(2+1) ⊗ (1d)5/2]9/2+ stretched configuration predicted by the NCSMC calculations.
