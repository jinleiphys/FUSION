# Bulk viscosity and conformal symmetry breaking in the dilute Fermi gas near unitarity

> The dilute Fermi gas at unitarity is scale invariant and its bulk viscosity vanishes. We compute the leading contribution to the bulk viscosity when the scattering length is not infinite. A measure of scale breaking is provided by the ratio $(P-\frac{2}{3}{\cal E})/P$, where $P$ is the pressure and ${\cal E}$ is the energy density. In the high temperature limit this ratio scales as $\frac{zλ}{a}$, where $z$ is the fugacity, $λ$ is the thermal wave length, and $a$ is the scattering length. We show that the bulk viscosity $ζ$ scales as the second power of this parameter, $ζ\sim (\frac{zλ}{a})^2 λ^{-3}$.

## Key claim

The bulk viscosity of a dilute Fermi gas near unitarity scales as the square of the conformal symmetry breaking parameter, specifically \(\zeta \sim (z\lambda/a)^2 \lambda^{-3}\) in the high temperature limit, where the measure of scale breaking is the ratio \((P-\frac{2}{3}{\cal E})/P \sim z\lambda/a\).

## Method

The authors compute the bulk viscosity using a diagrammatic approach to derive the fermion self-energy and quasi-particle properties, then match these to a quasi-particle Boltzmann equation solved via the Chapman-Enskog procedure. The linearized Boltzmann equation is solved by expanding the off-equilibrium distribution function in generalized Laguerre (Sonine) polynomials, taking moments to determine the coefficients, and evaluating the dissipative part of the bulk stress from the scale-breaking part of the quasi-particle energy.

## Key numbers

* Bulk viscosity coefficient: \(\zeta = \frac{1}{24\sqrt{2}\pi}\lambda^{-3} \left(\frac{z\lambda}{a}\right)^2\), where \(\lambda\) is the thermal de Broglie wavelength, \(z\) is the fugacity, and \(a\) is the scattering length.
* In terms of density: \(\frac{\zeta}{n} = \frac{1}{9\sqrt{2\pi}} \frac{1}{(k_Fa)^2} \left( \frac{T_F}{T}\right)^{5/2}\), where \(k_F=(3\pi^2n)^{1/3}\) and \(T_F=k_F^2/(2m)\).
* Convergence of the Laguerre polynomial expansion: the numerical prefactor \(k\) in \(\zeta=k\lambda^{-3}(z\lambda/a)^2\) converges as \(k = 9.378\times 10^{-3}\) (N=2), \(9.739\times 10^{-3}\) (N=3), and \(9.771\times 10^{-3}\) (N=4).
* The second virial coefficient near unitarity: \(\delta b_2 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \left\{ 1+\frac{2}{\sqrt{\pi}} \frac{1}{\sqrt{mT}a} + \ldots \right\}\).

## Context

This work extends the understanding of transport in nearly conformal non-relativistic fluids, building on the observation by Son (2005) that bulk viscosity vanishes at unitarity due to scale invariance. It provides the first calculation of the leading bulk viscosity contribution away from unitarity in the high-temperature regime, analogous to Weinberg's (1971) relation for relativistic gases where \(\zeta \sim \eta (c_s^2-c^2/3)^2\). The result enables experimental searches for bulk viscosity in ultracold Fermi gases, with estimates suggesting \(\zeta/n\) could be as large as 0.5 near \(k_Fa\sim 1\) and \(T\sim 2T_c\), and connects to sum rules derived by Taylor and Randeria (2010) and Hofmann (2011).
