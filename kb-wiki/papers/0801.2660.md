# Cluster formation in asymmetric nuclear matter: semi-classical and quantal approaches

> The nuclear-matter liquid-gas phase transition induces instabilities against finite-size density fluctuations. This has implications for both heavy-ion-collision and compact-star physics. In this paper, we study the clusterization properties of nuclear matter in a scenario of spinodal decomposition, comparing three different approaches: the quantal RPA, its semi-classical limit (Vlasov method), and a hydrodynamical framework. The predictions related to clusterization are qualitatively in good agreement varying the approach and the nuclear interaction. Nevertheless, it is shown that i) the quantum effects reduce the instability zone, and disfavor short-wavelength fluctuations; ii) large differences appear bewteen the two semi-classical approaches, which correspond respectively to a collisionless (Vlasov) and local equilibrium description (hydrodynamics); iii) the isospin-distillation effect is stronger in the local equilibrium framework; iv) important variations between the predicted time-scales of cluster formation appear near the borders of the instability region.

## Key claim

The paper shows that while quantal RPA, its semi-classical Vlasov limit, and a hydrodynamical framework give qualitatively consistent predictions for cluster formation in asymmetric nuclear matter via spinodal decomposition, the quantal effects reduce the instability zone and disfavor short-wavelength fluctuations, and large differences in predicted time scales appear near the borders of the instability region.

## Method

The authors studied finite-size density instabilities in infinite asymmetric nuclear matter using Skyrme density functionals (SLy230a, SGII, RATP) at finite temperature. They compared three approaches: (1) a fully quantal random-phase approximation (RPA) from linearized time-dependent Hartree-Fock; (2) its semi-classical limit, the Vlasov method (collisionless); and (3) a hydrodynamical framework assuming local equilibrium, derived from Thomas-Fermi free-energy curvature and Euler/continuity equations. The instability growth rates and spinodal contours were computed as functions of density, asymmetry, and wave number.

## Context

This work extends the study of liquid-gas spinodal instabilities in nuclear matter, previously treated thermodynamically (e.g., Bertsch and Finn, early 1980s), to finite-size fluctuations relevant for multifragmentation in heavy-ion collisions (Borderie, Rivet, Trautmann, early 2000s) and clusterization in neutron-star crusts (Ravenhall, Lassaut, Watanabe, Horowitz). By comparing quantal RPA, Vlasov, and hydrodynamical methods, the paper disentangles quantum effects from collisionless versus local-equilibrium dynamics. It enables more precise modeling of fragment formation in spinodal decomposition and provides constraints on the density dependence of the symmetry energy from the shape of the spinodal contour.
