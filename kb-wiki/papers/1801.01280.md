# Two-nucleon emitters within a pseudostate method: The case of $^6$Be and $^{16}$Be

> Background: Since the first experimental observation, two-nucleon radioactivity has gained renewed attention over the past fifteen years. The $^6$Be system is the lightest two-proton ground-state emitter, while $^{16}$Be has been recently proposed to be the first two-neutron ground-state emitter ever observed. A proper understanding of their properties and decay modes requires a reasonable description of the three-body continuum. Purpose: Study the ground-state properties of $^6$Be and $^{16}$Be within a general three-body model and investigate their nucleon-nucleon correlations in the continuum. Method: The pseudostate (PS) method in hyperspherical coordinates, using the analytical transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis for three-body systems, is used to construct the $^6$Be and $^{16}$Be ground-state wave functions. These resonances are approximated as a stable PS around the known two-nucleon separation energy. Effective core-$N$ potentials, constrained by the available experimental information on the binary subsystems $^5$Li and $^{15}$Be, are employed in the calculations. Results: The ground state of $^{16}$Be is found to present a strong dineutron configuration, with the valence neutrons occupying mostly an $l=2$ state relative to the core. The results are consistent with previous $R$-matrix calculations for the actual continuum. The case of $^6$Be shows a clear symmetry with respect to its mirror partner, the two-neutron halo $^6$He: The diproton configuration is dominant, and the valence protons occupy an $l=1$ orbit. Conclusions: The PS method is found to be a suitable tool in describing the properties of unbound $\text{core}+N+N$ ground states. For both $^{16}$Be and $^6$Be, the results are consistent with previous theoretical studies and confirm the dominant dinucleon configuration. This favors the picture of a correlated two-nucleon emission.

## Key claim

The pseudostate (PS) method in hyperspherical coordinates, using the analytical transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis, can reliably approximate three-body resonances as stable pseudostates around the known two-nucleon separation energy, provided the basis parameters are chosen to give a low level density that isolates the resonant state from the discretized continuum.

## Method

The three-body Schrodinger equation for core+N+N systems was solved by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in a THO basis in hyperspherical coordinates, truncating the expansion at maximum hypermomentum Kmax=30 and imax=15 hyperradial excitations per channel. Effective core-N potentials with Woods-Saxon geometries were adjusted to reproduce the known binary subsystem resonances in 15Be and 5Li, and a Gaussian three-body force was added to reproduce the experimental two-nucleon separation energies. The ground-state resonances of 16Be and 6Be were identified by varying the THO basis parameters gamma and b to find stable eigenstates near the known resonance energies, and the spatial correlations of the valence nucleons were analyzed from the probability distribution in Jacobi coordinates.

## Key numbers

- 16Be two-neutron separation energy: S2n = -1.35(10) MeV (from Spyrou 2012)
- 6Be two-proton separation energy: S2p = -1.372(5) MeV (from Tilley 2002)
- 16Be ground-state resonance energy found at approximately 1.3 MeV in the PS calculation
- 15Be ground state: d5/2 state at 1.8(1) MeV above neutron threshold, width 0.58(20) MeV (from Snyder 2013)
- 5Li ground state: p3/2 state unbound by 1.96(5) MeV, width 1.5 MeV (from Tilley 2002)
- 16Be ground state: 75% of the norm comes from relative lx=0 components between valence neutrons; in the Jacobi set where core and neutron are related by x, the d-wave probability is 85%, s-wave 10%, p-wave 4%
- 16Be ground state: 81% of the n-14Be partial wave content is d5/2

## Context

This work extends the pseudostate method previously applied to bound three-body systems and resonances in 6He and 9Be (Casal 2013, Descouvemont 2015) to the unbound two-nucleon emitters 16Be and 6Be. The results are consistent with R-matrix calculations for the 16Be continuum by Lovell (2017) and confirm the dominant dineutron configuration in 16Be and diproton configuration in 6Be, supporting the picture of correlated two-nucleon emission. The approach enables the study of three-body resonances in systems with Coulomb interactions, where exact continuum calculations are difficult, and opens the possibility of studying mirror symmetry between 6Be and the two-neutron halo 6He.
