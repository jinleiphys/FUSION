# Charmonium suppression in fixed target proton-nucleus collisions

> In this article, we perform a systematic investigation of the cold nuclear matter (CNM) effects, operative on charmonium ($J/ψ$, $ψ(2S)$) production, in fixed target proton-nucleus (p+A) collisions. Influence on charmonium production cross section due to the interplay of three different plausible CNM effects namely the initial-state parton energy loss, nuclear shadowing, and final-state absorption of the resonant states, are evaluated in detail. The available data on charmonium production in fixed target p+A collision experiments from SPS, Fermilab and HERA-B are examined for this purpose. The beam energy dependence of the observed $J/ψ$ production patterns are utilized to anticipate level of "normal" absorption in the upcoming proton induced collisions by the NA60+ experiment at CERN SPS and the CBM experiment at FAIR SIS100 accelerator facilities.

## Key claim
The paper systematically evaluates the interplay of three cold nuclear matter effects, initial-state parton energy loss, nuclear shadowing, and final-state absorption, on charmonium production in fixed target proton-nucleus collisions, and uses the beam energy dependence of observed J/ψ production patterns to predict the level of "normal" absorption for the upcoming NA60+ and CBM experiments, but only for data sets where the average formation length of J/ψ mesons in the target rest frame fits within the nuclear absorption scenario.

## Method
The authors adopt the Color Evaporation Model (CEM) at leading order to calculate charmonium production cross sections, convoluting parton-level subprocesses (gluon fusion and quark-antiquark annihilation) with the CT18ANLO free proton PDF and EPPS21NLO nuclear PDF. Initial-state parton energy loss is modeled using two parameterizations, the Brodsky-Hoyer (BH) formalism with linear path length dependence and the BDMPS approach with quadratic path length dependence, with parameters α = 0.467 ± 0.053 GeV/fm and β = 0.068 ± 0.008 GeV/fm² for quarks, scaled by 9/4 for gluons. Final-state absorption of charmonium states is treated within the Glauber model framework, and the analysis selects only those fixed target p+A data sets from SPS, Fermilab, and HERA-B where the kinematic conditions favor nuclear absorption as the dominant final-state dissociation mechanism.

## Key numbers
- Charm quark mass set to m_c = 1.3 GeV/c² and D meson mass to m_D = 1.87 GeV for the invariant mass window of closed c-cbar pairs (2m_c to 2m_D).
- Quark energy loss parameter α = 0.467 ± 0.053 GeV/fm for the BH linear path length dependence model.
- Quark energy loss parameter β = 0.068 ± 0.008 GeV/fm² for the BDMPS quadratic path length dependence model.
- Gluon energy loss taken as 9/4 times the quark energy loss, based on color factors (3 for gluon-gluon interactions, 3/4 for quark-gluon interactions).
- Mean path length for initial-state energy loss inside the target nucleus given by <L>_A = (3/4)R_A, with R_A = R_0 A^(1/3) assuming uniform matter density.

## Context
This work extends previous analyses of charmonium suppression in p+A collisions by Arleo (2006), Lourenco (2008), and others, who studied nuclear absorption and shadowing effects but did not systematically incorporate initial-state parton energy loss. The study builds on the authors' prior analysis of Drell-Yan reactions (Giri 2025) to determine the energy loss parameters now applied to charmonium production. The results provide a baseline for "normal" cold nuclear matter absorption that will enable the NA60+ experiment at CERN SPS and the CBM experiment at FAIR SIS100 to identify anomalous suppression signatures potentially indicative of quark-gluon plasma formation in future heavy-ion collisions.
