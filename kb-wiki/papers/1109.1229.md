# Chiral QCD sum rules for open charm mesons

> QCD sum rules for chiral partners in the open-charm meson sector are presented at nonzero baryon net density or temperature. We focus on the differences between pseudo-scalar and scalar as well as vector and axial-vector D mesons and derive the corresponding Weinberg type sum rules. This allows for the identification of such QCD condensates which drive the non-degeneracy of chiral partners in lowest order of the strong coupling alpha_s and which therefore may serve as "order parameters" for chiral restoration (or elements thereof).

## Key claim

The paper derives Weinberg-type QCD sum rules for the differences between chiral partners (pseudo-scalar vs. scalar, vector vs. axial-vector) in the open-charm meson sector, showing that in lowest order of the strong coupling \(\alpha_s\) the non-degeneracy of these partners is driven by QCD condensates that can serve as order parameters for chiral restoration, but only when one quark is light and the other is heavy, otherwise the relevant condensates or their Wilson coefficients vanish.

## Method

The authors perform an operator product expansion (OPE) for the difference of current-current correlators of chiral partners, using the background field method in fixed-point gauge for the gluon field. They project the resulting expressions onto the Clifford algebra basis, evaluate the quark propagator traces, and handle infrared divergences by introducing non-normal ordered condensates. The OPE results are then Borel-transformed and expanded in inverse powers of the Borel mass to obtain moment sum rules relating spectral integrals to QCD condensates.

## Key numbers

- The chiral condensate \(\langle \bar q q \rangle\) appears in the sum rules multiplied by the charm quark mass \(m_c\), giving a term \(-2 m_c \langle \bar q q \rangle\) in the first moment (Eq. 4.3a).
- The mixed condensate \(\langle \bar q g \sigma \mathcal{G} q \rangle\) contributes to the third moment as \(+ m_c \langle \bar q g \sigma \mathcal{G} q \rangle\) (Eq. 4.3b).
- A medium-specific combination \(\langle \Delta \rangle \equiv \langle \bar q g \sigma \mathcal{G} q \rangle - 8 \langle \bar q D_0^2 q \rangle\) appears in the third and fifth moments with coefficients \(- m_c \langle \Delta \rangle\) and \(-3 m_c^3 \langle \Delta \rangle\) respectively (Eqs. 4.3b, 4.3c).
- The Borel-transformed sum rule (Eq. 4.2) contains the factor \(e^{-m_c^2/M^2}\) multiplying condensate terms with explicit \(m_c\) and Borel mass \(M\) dependence.

## Context

This work extends the Weinberg-type chiral sum rules originally developed for light vector and axial-vector mesons in vacuum (Weinberg, 1967; Das et al., 1967) and at finite temperature (Kapusta and Shuryak, 1993) to the open-charm meson sector. It generalizes earlier QCD sum rule studies of individual D mesons (Reinders et al., 1984; Hilger et al., 2008) by focusing on chiral partner differences, which isolate chirally odd condensates. The results are directly applicable to future experimental programs at FAIR (CBM and PANDA collaborations) and can be transferred to open bottom mesons by replacing \(m_c \to m_b\).
