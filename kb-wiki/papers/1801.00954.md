# Properties of superheavy isotopes Z=120 and isotones N=184 within the Skyrme-HFB model

> We study the nuclear properties of even-even superheavy $Z$=120 isotopes and $N$=184 isotones with the Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB)approach. Within this model we examine the deformation energy surfaces and two paths to fission: a reflection-symmetric path with elongated fission fragments (sEF) and a reflection-asymmetric path corresponding to elongated fission fragments (aEF). Furthermore, we explore the energy surfaces in the region of very large oblate deformations with toroidal nuclear density distributions. While the energy surfaces of toroidal $Z$=120 isotopes and $N$=184 isotones do not possess energy minima without angular momenta,local energy minima (toroidal high spin isomeric states) appear for many of these superheavy nuclei with specific angular momenta about the symmetry axis. We have theoretically located the toroidal high spin isomers (THSIs) of $^{302}$Og$_{184}$,$^{302}120_{182}$, $^{306}120_{186}$, and $^{306}122_{184}$.

## Key claim

The authors find that while toroidal nuclear density distributions for Z=120 isotopes and N=184 isotones do not form energy minima without angular momentum, local energy minima (toroidal high spin isomeric states) appear for many of these superheavy nuclei when endowed with specific angular momenta about the symmetry axis.

## Method

The authors used the constrained and cranked Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach with the SkM* force and a density-dependent mixed pairing interaction, solving the equality-constrained problem via an augmented Lagrangian method with the symmetry-unrestricted code HFODD. They examined deformation energy surfaces for reflection-symmetric and reflection-asymmetric fission paths, and explored energy surfaces in the region of very large oblate deformations with toroidal density distributions. The cranked Skyrme-HF model (without pairing) was applied to locate toroidal high spin isomers under the constraint of angular momentum about the symmetry axis.

## Key numbers

- The fission barrier heights for Z=120 isotopes reach a maximal value of approximately 10 MeV for N=180 and N=182.
- For N=184 isotones, the inner-barrier height reaches a maximum value of approximately 10 MeV for Z=114 to Z=124.
- The energy difference between the ground state and the point where biconcave and toroidal solutions have the same energy reaches 54.6 MeV for the isotope 302120182.
- For N=184 isotones, this energy difference decreases rapidly from 78.1 to 38.7 MeV as the number of protons increases from Z=106 to Z=126.
- The THSI 302Og184 has isomers at Iz=30, 143, and 208 hbar, with excitation energies E* of 70.5, 93.0, and 107.2 MeV respectively.
- The THSI 306122184 has isomers at Iz=46, 60, and 256 hbar, with excitation energies E* of 54.3, 56.0, and 109.7 MeV respectively.
- The THSI 302120182 has an isomer at Iz=111 hbar with excitation energy E* of 76.6 MeV.
- The THSI 306120186 has isomers at Iz=117 and 218 hbar, with excitation energies E* of 76.5 and 102.0 MeV respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies by Staszczak et al. (2017) and Kosior et al. (2017) that examined energy surfaces of superheavy Z=120 isotopes and N=184 isotones, where the THSI 304120184 was first located with Iz=81 and 208 hbar. The present study finds that toroidal high spin isomers appear to be rather common in the superheavy nuclei region, occurring in neighboring even-even N=184 isotones with Z=118 and 122, as well as Z=120 isotopes with N=182 and 186. These results enable further theoretical and experimental searches for toroidal high-spin isomeric states in the superheavy element region.
