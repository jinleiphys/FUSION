# Baryon Mapping of Quark Systems

> We discuss a mapping procedure from a space of colorless three-quark clusters into a space of elementary baryons and illustrate it in the context of a three-color extension of the Lipkin model recently developed. Special attention is addressed to the problem of the formation of unphysical states in the mapped space. A correspondence is established between quark and baryon spaces and the baryon image of a generic quark operator is defined both in its Hermitian and non-Hermitian forms. Its spectrum (identical in the two cases) is found to consist of a physical part containing the same eigenvalues of the quark operator in the cluster space and an unphysical part consisting only of zero eigenvalues. A physical subspace of the baryon space is also defined where the latter eigenvalues are suppressed. The procedure discussed is quite general and applications of it can be thought also in the correspondence between systems of 2n fermions and n bosons.

## Key claim

The mapping procedure from colorless three-quark clusters to elementary baryons produces a baryon-image operator whose spectrum consists of a physical part (identical to the quark operator eigenvalues in the cluster space) and an unphysical part consisting only of zero eigenvalues, but only when the cluster states are linearly dependent (Omega greater than 1); in the linearly independent case (Omega=1) no unphysical states appear.

## Method

The author establishes a "simple" correspondence between the vector space of colorless three-quark cluster states (created by operators F-dagger) and the vector space of elementary baryon states (created by operators f-dagger) for the three-color extension of the Lipkin model. For a generic Hermitian quark operator acting in the cluster space, the baryon image operator is defined in both non-Hermitian and Hermitian forms by requiring that all eigenvalues of the quark operator in the cluster space be reproduced as eigenvalues of the image operator in the baryon space. The physical subspace of the baryon space is identified where unphysical zero eigenvalues are suppressed and matrix elements between corresponding basis states are preserved.

## Key numbers

- For Omega=2 (two-cluster case), the total number of states in the cluster space is N=52, while the number of orthonormal states after diagonalizing the overlap matrix is N-bar=20.
- The one-body baryon image Hamiltonian for Omega=1 contains terms with coefficients 3*Delta/2, -12*chi_2/Omega, and -36*chi_3/Omega^2, where Delta is the level spacing and chi_2, chi_3 are coupling constants.
- The image Hamiltonian for general Omega contains up to Omega-body terms, even if the original quark Hamiltonian is at most one-body.

## Context

This work extends the mapping procedure previously developed by Catara and Sambataro (1991) from cases with linearly independent cluster states to cases with linearly dependent states, using the three-color extension of the Lipkin model developed by Pittel, Arias, Dukelsky and Frank (1994) as a testbed. The approach differs from earlier mapping procedures by Nadjakov (1990), Pittel, Engel, Dukelsky and Ring (1990), and Meyer (1991), which followed the Belyaev-Zelevinsky method and showed a preference toward special forms of Hamiltonians. The procedure is general enough to be applicable to the correspondence between systems of 2n fermions and n bosons.
