# Fixed-target charmonium production and pion parton distributions

> We investigate how charmonium hadroproduction at fixed-target energies can be used to constrain the gluon distribution in pions. Using nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) formulation, the $J/ψ$ and $ψ(2S)$ cross sections as a function of longitudinal momentum fraction $x_F$ from pions and protons colliding with light targets, as well as the $ψ(2S)$ to $J/ψ$ cross section ratios, are included in the analysis. The color-octet long-distance matrix elements are found to have a pronounced dependence on the pion parton distribution functions (PDFs). This study shows that the $x_F$ differential cross sections of pion-induced charmonium production impose strong constraints on the pion's quark and gluon PDFs. In particular, the pion PDFs with larger gluon densities provide a significantly better description of the data. It is also found that the production of the $ψ(2S)$ state is associated with a larger quark-antiquark contribution, compared with $J/ψ$.

## Key claim

Charmonium hadroproduction at fixed-target energies can strongly constrain the gluon distribution in pions, but only if the color-octet long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) are refitted simultaneously with the pion PDFs; the pion PDFs with larger gluon densities provide a significantly better description of the data, though the absolute normalization of the cross sections requires additional normalization parameters to achieve good fits.

## Method

The authors employed the nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) framework at leading order to compute the $x_F$-differential cross sections for $J/\psi$ and $\psi(2S)$ production in pion-nucleon and proton-nucleon collisions, including contributions from $q\bar{q}$ annihilation, gluon-gluon fusion, and $qG$ subprocesses. They performed global fits of the color-octet LDMEs $\langle \mathcal{O}_{8}^{H}[^{3}S_{1}] \rangle$ and $\Delta_8^{H}$ for $J/\psi$ and $\psi(2S)$ to 164 pion-induced and 82 proton-induced data points, using four different pion PDF sets (SMRS, GRV, JAM, and xFitter) and including normalization parameters for each differential dataset.

## Key numbers

- The reduced $\chi^2$/ndf for the fit (FIT) using SMRS pion PDFs was 1.9, compared to 5.7 for the reference calculation (REF) using LDMEs from total cross section fits only.
- For the pion-induced $x_F$ data, $\chi^2$/ndp values after fitting were 1.8 (SMRS), 2.4 (GRV), 5.9 (JAM), and 4.5 (xFitter), showing that SMRS and GRV provide better descriptions than JAM and xFitter.
- The fitted LDME $\langle \mathcal{O}_{8}^{J/\psi}[^{3}S_{1}] \rangle$ for SMRS was 0.0259 $\pm$ 0.0023 GeV$^3$, compared to the reference value of 0.0690 GeV$^3$.
- The fitted LDME $\Delta_8^{J/\psi}$ for SMRS was 0.0560 $\pm$ 0.0016 GeV$^3$, compared to the reference value of 0.0250 GeV$^3$.
- The beam momenta of the datasets covered the range 39.5 to 515 GeV/$c$, corresponding to $\sqrt{s}$ values from 8.6 to 31.1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies limited to total cross sections of charmonium production (Hsieh 2021) by including $x_F$-dependent differential cross sections and $\psi(2S)$ to $J/\psi$ ratios in the global fit. It builds on the NRQCD framework developed by Beneke (1996) and uses four pion PDF sets: SMRS (Sutton 1991), GRV (Gluck 1991), JAM (Barry 2018, Cao 2021, Barry 2021), and xFitter (Novikov 2020). The results demonstrate that charmonium production data can discriminate between different pion gluon distributions, enabling future global analyses to incorporate these data for more precise pion PDF extractions.
