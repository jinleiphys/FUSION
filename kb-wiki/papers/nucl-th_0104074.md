# Spectator Response to the Participant Blast

> The interplay between spectator and participant matter in heavy-ion collisions is investigated in the context of a microscopic transport model. Transport simulations show that flow patterns for the participant matter are strongly influenced by the presence of the nearby spectator matter. However, the influence is mutual. During the explosion of the participant zone, the spectator matter acquires a transverse momentum $<P^x /A>$ that shows sensitivity to the nuclear incompressibility and to the momentum dependence of the nuclear mean field (MF). An observed change in the net average momentum per nucleon, $Δ|< {\bf P}/A >|$, can be associated with the momentum dependence of the MF. For a momentum-dependent MF and a low impact parameter in a heavy system, the spectators may emerge faster than in the initial state, accelerated by the violent participant explosion. The average excitation energy and the mass of the spectators, in contrast to the momentum, show little sensitivity to the nuclear equation of state.

## Key claim

Transport simulations show that spectator matter acquires a transverse momentum during the participant explosion that is sensitive to both nuclear incompressibility and the momentum dependence of the nuclear mean field, but the average excitation energy and mass of the spectators show little sensitivity to the nuclear equation of state. For a momentum-dependent mean field and low impact parameter in a heavy system, spectators may emerge faster than in the initial state, accelerated by the participant explosion.

## Method

The authors performed Boltzmann-Uhlenbeck-Uehling (BUU) transport simulations of heavy-ion collisions, using both momentum-dependent (MD) and momentum-independent (MI) mean fields with different stiffness parameters. They simulated $^{124}$Sn + $^{124}$Sn reactions at beam energies from 250 MeV/nucleon to 1 GeV/nucleon at impact parameters $b = 5-7$ fm, and $^{197}$Au + $^{197}$Au collisions at 1 GeV/nucleon, tracking the time evolution of spectator density, transverse momentum, excitation energy, and mass.

## Key numbers

- In 800 MeV/nucleon $^{124}$Sn + $^{124}$Sn at $b=5$ fm, the high-density stage with central density $\rho_c > 1.5 \rho_0$ lasts about 13 fm/c, while the elapsed time from initial impact to complete separation of target and projectile is approximately 40 fm/c.
- For $^{197}$Au + $^{197}$Au at 1 GeV/nucleon and $b=8$ fm, the elliptic flow parameter $v_2$ changes sign from positive to negative at approximately 7 fm/c, with the most rapid density decline ending at approximately 16 fm/c.
- In the $b=6$ fm Au+Au simulation with hard momentum-dependent (HM) mean field and free cross sections, the gain in longitudinal momentum per nucleon contributes 17 out of 24 MeV/c of the gain in net spectator momentum per nucleon; with reduced cross sections, the longitudinal gain contributes about 4 out of 8 MeV/c.
- A simple estimate for Sn + Sn at 800 MeV/nucleon gives spectator transverse momentum per nucleon $P^x/A \simeq 50$ MeV/c, consistent with simulation magnitudes.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of elliptic flow sensitivity to the nuclear equation of state by Danielewicz (2000) and Larionov (2000), and complements long-time spectator evolution studies by Gaitanos and Wolter (1999). The results suggest that spectator transverse and longitudinal momentum measurements can provide information on the high-density nuclear equation of state comparable to that from elliptic flow analysis, and enable future combined analyses where spectator properties initialize statistical decay calculations for complete reaction descriptions.
