# A new exact solution of the relativistic Boltzmann equation and its hydrodynamic limit

> We present an exact solution of the relativistic Boltzmann equation for a system undergoing boost-invariant longitudinal and azimuthally symmetric transverse flow ("Gubser flow"). The resulting exact non-equilibrium dynamics is compared to 1st- and 2nd-order relativistic hydrodynamic approximations for various shear viscosity to entropy density ratios. This novel solution can be used to test the validity and accuracy of different hydrodynamic approximations in conditions similar to those generated in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first exact solution of the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation (RTA) for a system undergoing simultaneous longitudinal and transverse expansion (Gubser flow), and uses this solution to test the accuracy of first-order (Navier-Stokes) and second-order (Israel-Stewart) hydrodynamic approximations for various shear viscosity to entropy density ratios.

## Method

The authors impose Gubser flow symmetry (boost-invariant longitudinal expansion plus azimuthally symmetric transverse flow) on the RTA Boltzmann equation for a conformal, massless system. By transforming to de Sitter coordinates \(dS_3 \otimes R\), the partial differential equation reduces to a simple ordinary differential equation in the de Sitter time coordinate \(\rho\), which is solved exactly via an integral equation. The resulting exact non-equilibrium distribution function is used to compute the energy-momentum tensor, and the temperature and shear stress evolution is compared to ideal hydrodynamics, first-order Navier-Stokes theory, and second-order Israel-Stewart theory.

## Key numbers

* For specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\), second-order Israel-Stewart theory provides the best description of the exact kinetic solution for both temperature and shear stress \(\bar \pi^\varsigma_\varsigma\) as functions of de Sitter time \(\rho\).
* For \(\eta/s = 10/(4\pi)\), the free-streaming limit provides the best approximation to the exact solution, followed by second-order Israel-Stewart theory as the best hydrodynamic approximation.
* First-order Navier-Stokes theory exhibits qualitatively wrong behavior for the shear stress \(\bar \pi^\varsigma_\varsigma\) at negative \(\rho\) values for both \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\) and \(\eta/s = 10/(4\pi)\).
* The ideal fluid solution for the energy density in de Sitter space is \(\hat\varepsilon(\rho) = \hat\varepsilon(\rho_0) / (\cosh\rho)^{8/3}\).
* The Navier-Stokes result for the shear stress component is \((\bar\pi^\varsigma_\varsigma)_{\rm NS} = \frac{4}{3} \frac{\eta/s}{\hat T} \tanh\rho\).

## Context

This work extends previous exact solutions of the RTA Boltzmann equation for systems with only longitudinal boost-invariant expansion (Bjorken flow) by Baym (1984) and Florkowski et al. (2013, 2014) to include simultaneous transverse expansion. The solution enables testing of hydrodynamic approximations under conditions more realistic for quark-gluon plasma formed in heavy-ion collisions, where transverse flow is responsible for observed collective flow signals. The authors compare their exact results to the Gubser flow solutions for Navier-Stokes theory (Gubser 2010, 2011) and Israel-Stewart theory (Marrochio et al. 2013), finding that second-order viscous hydrodynamics performs much better than first-order Navier-Stokes theory.
