# Delta and Omega electromagnetic form factors in a covariant three-body approach

> We study baryons as three-body systems using the QCD degrees of freedom in the framework of covariant Bethe-Salpeter equations. The interaction among quarks is reduced to a vector-vector interaction via a single dressed-gluon exchange (Rainbow-Ladder truncation). The formalism allows for the study of the hadron spectrum as well as their internal properties. We will present the calculation of electromagnetic properties of spin-3/2 baryons. The model independent features of our results are assessed using two different models for the dressings.

## Key claim
The paper presents a calculation of the electromagnetic form factors of spin-3/2 baryons (Delta and Omega) using a covariant three-body Bethe-Salpeter approach, but the results are model-dependent and quantitatively unsatisfactory for the Omega magnetic moment, likely due to the neglect of mesonic dressing effects in the Rainbow-Ladder truncation.

## Method
The authors solve the covariant Faddeev equation for three-quark bound states using a Rainbow-Ladder truncation, where the quark-quark interaction is reduced to a single dressed-gluon exchange with a vector-vector structure. The quark propagators are obtained from the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation in the Rainbow truncation, and the quark-photon vertex is solved from its inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation. Two different models for the effective interaction (Maris-Tandy model and a model designed for the U_A(1) anomaly) are compared to assess model dependence.

## Key numbers
- Delta mass: 1.22 GeV for both models I and II (experimental value: 1.232 +/- 0.002 GeV)
- Delta charge radius squared: 0.50 fm^2 (model I) and 0.61 fm^2 (model II), compared to lattice values ranging from 0.279 to 0.373 fm^2 at various pion masses
- Delta dimensionless charge radius times mass squared: 0.75 (model I) and 0.91 (model II), compared to lattice values 0.726 to 0.858
- Delta magnetic dipole moment G_M1(0): 2.38 (model I) and 2.77 (model II), compared to experimental value 3.54 (+4.59/-4.72)
- Delta electric quadrupole moment G_E2(0): -0.67 (model I) and -0.99 (model II), compared to lattice values -0.87 to -1.68
- Omega mass: 1.65 GeV (model I) and 1.80 GeV (model II), compared to experimental value 1.672 GeV
- Omega charge radius squared: 0.27 fm^2 for both models, compared to lattice values 0.338 to 0.355 fm^2
- Omega magnetic dipole moment G_M1(0): -2.41 (model I) and -2.71 (model II), compared to experimental value -3.52 +/- 0.09
- Omega electric quadrupole moment G_E2(0): 0.54 (model I) and 0.75 (model II), compared to lattice values 0.838 to 0.959

## Context
This work extends the covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach previously applied to nucleons (Eichmann 2009) to spin-3/2 baryons, providing a unified quantum-field-theoretical description of meson and baryon properties. The results are compared to lattice QCD calculations by Alexandrou (2009, 2010) at various pion masses and to experimental data for the Delta and Omega magnetic moments. The authors note that more sophisticated truncation schemes successfully applied to mesonic bound states (Fischer 2007, 2008; Chang 2009) could in principle be extended to baryons to improve the quantitative agreement.
