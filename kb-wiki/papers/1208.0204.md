# Quarkonium Phenomenology in Vacuum

> Broad meson phenomenology in vacuum is discussed in a single model containing scalar, pseudoscalar, vector and axial-vector states both in the non-strange and strange sectors. Emphasis is given to search for quark-antiquark (quarkonium) states in the scalar and axial-vector channels. In particular, the work examines the question whether the scalar quark-antiquark states are found in the energy region below or above 1 GeV in the physical spectrum. Contrarily to the naive expectation, the mentioned states are found in the region above 1 GeV. Additionally, the axial-vector isotriplet state $a_1(1260)$ is described as a quarkonium. These results have consequences for the building of order parameters for the chiral-symmetry breaking of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD).

## Key claim

Contrary to the naive expectation that scalar quark-antiquark states lie below 1 GeV, this work finds that scalar quarkonium states are located in the energy region above 1 GeV in the physical spectrum, and the axial-vector isotriplet state a1(1260) is described as a quarkonium state within a single model containing scalar, pseudoscalar, vector and axial-vector mesons.

## Method

The author employs a linear sigma model with three quark flavors (Nf=3) that includes scalar, pseudoscalar, vector and axial-vector mesons, with global chiral invariance. The model is used to compute meson masses and decay widths in vacuum, focusing on the identification of quark-antiquark (quarkonium) states in the scalar and axial-vector channels. The analysis determines whether scalar quarkonium states appear below or above 1 GeV by comparing model predictions to the physical meson spectrum.

## Key numbers

- Scalar quarkonium states are found in the energy region above 1 GeV, contrary to the naive expectation of a sub-1 GeV location.
- The axial-vector isotriplet state a1(1260) is described as a quarkonium state within the model.
- The work discusses scalar mesons f0(600), a0(980), f0(1370) and a0(1450) in the context of quarkonium interpretation (no explicit masses or widths for these states are given in the provided text beyond their names).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies using a linear sigma model with vector mesons and global chiral invariance (Parganlija, Giacosa, Rischke 2010) to include axial-vector states and address the long-standing question of whether light scalar mesons are quarkonium or multi-quark states. The results have consequences for building order parameters for chiral-symmetry breaking in QCD and challenge the conventional tetraquark or molecular interpretations of scalar mesons below 1 GeV proposed by Jaffe (1977) and others. The findings enable further studies of the chiral partner structure of mesons and the nature of the QCD vacuum.
