# Structure of neutron-rich He $Λ$ hypernuclei using the cluster orbital shell model

> We calculated the energy spectra of the neutron-rich He $Λ$ hypernuclei with $A=6$ to 9 within the framework of an $α+ Λ+Xn$ ($X=1$--4) cluster model using the cluster orbital shell model. The employed constituent particles reproduce their observed properties. For resonant states of core nuclei such as $^5$He, $^6$He, and $^7$He, the complex scaling method is employed to obtain energies and decay widths. The calculated ground states of $^6_Λ$He and $^7_Λ$He are in good agreement with published data. The energy levels of $^8_Λ$He and $^9_Λ$He are predicted. In $^9_Λ$He, we find one deeply bound state and two excited resonant states, which are proposed to be produced at the Japan proton accelerator research complex (J-PARC) by the double-charge-exchange reaction $(π^-, K^+)$ using a $^9$Be target.

## Key claim

The authors predict the energy levels of neutron-rich He Λ hypernuclei up to A=9 using a cluster model, finding that in ⁹_ΛHe there is one deeply bound state and two narrow resonant states; however, the calculated Λ separation energy for ⁸_ΛHe (5.95 MeV) is significantly less bound than the emulsion data value (7.17 ± 0.7 MeV), which the authors attribute to the large experimental uncertainty and small number of events.

## Method

The authors employ an α + Λ + Xn (X=1–4) cluster orbital shell model (COSM) with Gaussian basis states, treating the α particle as a rigid core and the valence neutrons and Λ as single-particle states in a jj-coupling scheme. For resonant states of the core nuclei (⁵He, ⁶He, ⁷He) and hypernuclei, the complex scaling method is applied to obtain resonance energies and decay widths. The α–neutron interaction uses the Kanada-Kaneko-Nagata-Nomoto potential, the neutron-neutron interaction uses a modified Minnesota potential, and the Λ–neutron interaction uses the phenomenological ORG single-range Gaussian potential, with the α–Λ potential folded from the Λ–neutron interaction to reproduce the observed B_Λ = 3.12 MeV in ⁵_ΛHe.

## Key numbers

- ⁶_ΛHe ground state (1⁻): calculated B_Λ = 4.10 MeV, compared to experimental B_Λ = 4.18 ± 0.10 MeV
- ⁷_ΛHe ground state (1/2⁺): calculated B_Λ = 5.51 MeV, compared to experimental B_Λ = 5.55 ± 0.10 (stat.) ± 0.11 (sys.) MeV
- ⁷_ΛHe first excited state: calculated B_Λ = 3.34 MeV (3/2⁺) and 2.87 MeV (5/2⁺), compared to experimental B_Λ = 3.65 ± 0.20 (stat.) ± 0.11 (sys.) MeV
- ⁸_ΛHe ground state (1⁻): calculated B_Λ = 5.95 MeV, compared to experimental B_Λ = 7.17 ± 0.7 MeV from emulsion data
- ⁹_ΛHe ground state (1/2⁺): calculated B_Λ = 7.09 MeV, with two excited resonant states (3/2⁺ and 5/2⁺) having B_Λ = 2.49 MeV and 2.33 MeV, respectively, and decay widths less than 0.1 MeV
- ⁸He ground state: calculated binding energy −3.21 MeV, compared to experimental −3.11 MeV
- ⁶He two-neutron separation energy: calculated 0.975 MeV (reproduced by adjusting the Minnesota potential repulsive strength to 173.7 MeV from the original 200 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous cluster model studies of ⁵_ΛHe, ⁶_ΛHe, and ⁷_ΛHe (Hiyama 1996, 2009, 2015) to heavier neutron-rich hypernuclei, and contrasts with the no-core shell model calculations of Wirth (2018) which did not focus on reproducing observed binding energies. The predictions for ⁹_ΛHe are motivated by planned experiments at J-PARC using the double-charge-exchange reaction (π⁻, K⁺) on a ⁹Be target, and if observed, the binding energy would help constrain ΛN–ΣN coupling models, complementing studies of ⁴_ΛH and ⁴_ΛHe (Hiyama 2001, Nogga, Nemura).
