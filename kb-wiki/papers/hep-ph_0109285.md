# Relativistic Three-Quark Bound States in Separable Two-Quark Approximation

> Baryons as relativistic bound states in 3-quark correlations are described by an effective Bethe-Salpeter equation when irreducible 3-quark interactions are neglected and separable 2-quark correlations are assumed. We present an efficient numerical method to calculate the nucleon mass and its covariant wave function in this quantum field theoretic quark-diquark model with quark-exchange interaction. Expanding the components of the spinorial wave function in terms of Chebyshev polynomials, the four-dimensional integral equations are in a first step reduced to a coupled set of one-dimensional ones. This set of linear and homogeneous equations defines a generalised eigenvalue problem. Representing the eigenvector corresponding to the largest eigenvalue, the Chebyshev moments are then obtained by iteration. The nucleon mass is implicitly determined by the eigenvalue, and its covariant wave function is reconstructed from the moments within the Chebyshev approximation.

## Key claim

The paper presents an efficient numerical method for solving the relativistic quark-diquark Bethe-Salpeter equation for the nucleon, but the method is restricted to a specific range of the momentum partitioning parameter \(\eta\) due to singularities in propagators, and the Chebyshev expansion converges more slowly near the boundaries of this range.

## Method

The authors reduce the four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter integral equations for the nucleon as a quark-diquark bound state to coupled one-dimensional equations by expanding the scalar components of the spinorial wave function in Chebyshev polynomials of the first kind. The resulting system defines a generalized eigenvalue problem, where the eigenvalue \(\lambda(P^2)\) is iteratively solved for a given test nucleon mass, and a bisection method in the bound state mass variable determines the correct mass corresponding to chosen quark and diquark parameters. The separable two-quark correlations are restricted to scalar and axialvector diquark channels with dipole form factors for the diquark-quark vertices.

## Key numbers

* The nucleon mass \(M_n\) is implicitly determined by the condition \(\lambda(P^2) = 1/g_s^2\) at \(P^2 = -M_n^2\), where \(g_s\) is the scalar diquark coupling constant.
* The momentum partitioning parameter \(\eta\) is restricted to the interval \(\eta \in [1 - m_{sc}/M_n, \, m_q/M_n]\) when \(m_{ax} \ge m_{sc} > m_q\), with additional bounds \(\eta \in [\frac{1}{2}(1 - m_q/M_n), \, \frac{1}{2}(1 + m_q/M_n)]\) and \(\eta \in [\frac{1}{3}(1 - 2\lambda_n/M_n), \, \frac{1}{3}(1 + 2\lambda_n/M_n)]\).
* Typical running time is between one and ten minutes on a 1.2 GHz AMD K7 processor, depending on parameters and desired accuracy.
* Memory required to execute with typical data is 10 MB.

## Context

This work extends the covariant quark-diquark model for baryons developed in earlier studies by Oettel (2000), Alkofer (2001), and others, which itself builds on the Faddeev approach to three-quark bound states using separable two-quark correlations introduced by Cahill (1989) and Reinhardt (1990). The numerical method enables calculation of the nucleon mass and covariant wave function without non-relativistic reductions, providing input for covariant calculations of nucleon observables such as form factors. The Chebyshev expansion technique is closely related to hyperspherical harmonic expansions used in the Wick-Cutkosky model, as reviewed by Nakanishi (1988).
