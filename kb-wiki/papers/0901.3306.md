# Two and three nucleon K- absorption in nuclei

> We analyze the peaks in the (Lambda p) and (Lambda d) invariant mass distributions, observed in recent FINUDA experiments and claimed to be signals of deeply bound kaonic states, and find them to be naturally explained in terms of K- absorption by two or three nucleons leaving the rest of the original nuclei as spectator. For reactions on heavy nuclei, the subsequent interactions of the particles produced in the primary absorption process with the residual nucleus play an important role. Thus at present there is no experimental evidence of deeply bound K- states in nuclei. However some new physics can be extracted from the data of FINUDA experiments.

## Key claim
The peaks in the (Λp) and (Λd) invariant mass distributions observed by FINUDA, previously claimed as evidence for deeply bound kaonic states, are naturally explained by K- absorption on two or three nucleons with the rest of the nucleus acting as a spectator, plus final-state interactions; therefore, there is currently no experimental evidence for deeply bound K- states in nuclei.

## Method
The authors performed calculations of K- absorption on two nucleons (for the Λp channel) and three nucleons (for the Λd channel) in light nuclei, using a model that includes the primary absorption process and subsequent final-state interactions (rescattering) of the produced particles (Λ, p, d) with the residual nucleus. For the Λp case, they simulated K- absorption in a mixture of 12C, 6Li, and 7Li targets; for the Λd case, they modeled absorption in 6Li. The calculated invariant mass and angular distributions were compared directly to FINUDA experimental data.

## Key numbers
- For the Λp peak in light nuclei (51% 12C, 35% 6Li, 14% 7Li), the contribution from reactions with 12C is about 99% of the total, due to the two orders of magnitude higher overlap integral ∫ d⃗r |Ψ_K-(⃗r)|² ρ_A²(r) in 12C compared to Li.
- The FINUDA collaboration originally interpreted the Λp peak as a K-pp bound state with 115 MeV binding and 67 MeV width.
- The FINUDA collaboration interpreted the Λd peak from 6Li as a bound K-NNN state with 58 MeV binding and 37 MeV width.
- The Λd invariant mass spectrum from 12C targets in the same FINUDA experiment does not show a clear peak.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier calculations (Magas 2006, Magas 2008) that reinterpreted FINUDA peaks as absorption processes rather than bound states, contradicting the original FINUDA claims (Agnello 2005, 2007). It agrees with the suggestion from a KEK experiment (Suzuki 2007) that the Λd peak could be a signature of three-body absorption. The results enable future experiments to use separate-nucleus targets to verify the absorption dynamics model, rather than searching for deeply bound kaonic states.
