# Spin polarization phenomena in dense nuclear matter

> Spin polarized states in nuclear matter with an effective nucleon-nucleon interaction are studied for a wide range of isospin asymmetries and densities. Based on a Fermi liquid theory, it is shown that there are a few possible scenarios of spin ordered phase transitions: (a) nuclear matter undergoes at some critical density a phase transition to a spin polarized state with the oppositely directed spins of neutrons and protons (Skyrme SLy4 and Gogny D1S interactions); (b) at some critical density, a spin polarized state with the like-directed neutron and proton spins appears (Skyrme SkI5 interaction); (c) nuclear matter under increasing density, at first, undergoes a phase transition to the state with the opposite directions of neutron and proton spins, which goes over at larger density to the state with the same direction of nucleon spins (Skyrme SkI3 interaction).

# Spin polarization phenomena in dense nuclear matter

## Key claim

Spin polarized states in nuclear matter can appear via several distinct phase transition scenarios depending on the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction used, with the critical densities and ordering types (ferromagnetic versus antiferromagnetic) varying strongly between Skyrme SLy4, SkI5, SkI3, and Gogny D1S parametrizations.

## Method

The author employed Fermi liquid theory to solve self-consistent equations for the single particle energy expansion coefficients in spin and isospin space, using the normal Fermi liquid amplitudes U0 through U3. Neutron and proton spin polarization parameters were computed as functions of density at zero temperature for symmetric nuclear matter and varying isospin asymmetries, using four different effective nucleon-nucleon interactions: Skyrme SLy4, SkI3, SkI5, and Gogny D1S.

## Key numbers

- Critical density of antiferromagnetic spin instability in symmetric nuclear matter for SLy4 force: 0.33 fm^-3
- Critical density of ferromagnetic instability in neutron matter for SLy4 force: 0.59 fm^-3
- Critical density of ferromagnetic spin state in neutron matter for SkI5 force: 0.28 fm^-3
- Critical density of ferromagnetic spin instability in symmetric nuclear matter for SkI5 force: 0.43 fm^-3
- Critical density for ferromagnetic ordering in symmetric nuclear matter for SkI3 force: 0.910 fm^-3
- Critical density for antiferromagnetic ordering in symmetric nuclear matter for SkI3 force: 0.917 fm^-3
- Critical density of spin instability in neutron matter for SkI3 force: 0.37 fm^-3
- Antiferromagnetic spin order parameter arises at density 3.8 times nuclear saturation density (3.8 x 0.16 fm^-3) for D1S Gogny force
- Totally antiferromagnetically polarized state forms at 4.3 times nuclear saturation density for D1S Gogny force

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of spin instability in nuclear matter using effective interactions, referencing calculations by Kutschera and Wojcik (1994) and by Isayev (prior work) that predicted spin instability at densities from saturation density up to six times saturation density. It contrasts with models using realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, cited as Pandharipande, Garde, and Smith (prior work) and others, where ferromagnetic phase transitions appear suppressed well above saturation density. The results highlight the need for a new generation of energy functionals with properly constrained time-odd parts at high densities, potentially informed by data on magnetic field decay in isolated neutron stars.
