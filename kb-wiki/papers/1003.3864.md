# Higher order QED in high mass e+ e- pairs production at RHIC

> Lowest order and higher order QED calculations have been carried out for the RHIC high mass e+ e- pairs observed by PHENIX with single ZDC triggers. The lowest order QED results for the experimental acceptance are about two standard deviations larger than the PHENIX data. Corresponding higher order QED calculations are within one standard deviation of the data.

## Key claim
Lowest-order QED calculations for high-mass electron-positron pair production at RHIC, when restricted to the PHENIX experimental acceptance, exceed the data by about two standard deviations; including higher-order QED corrections brings the theory to within one standard deviation of the measured cross sections.

## Method
The authors computed the cross section for ultraperipheral Au+Au collisions at RHIC (γ = 107) by integrating over impact parameter the product of the pair production probability P_ee(b), the probability that at least one nucleus undergoes Coulomb dissociation P_1x(b), and a hadronic survival factor exp[-P_nn(b)]. The perturbative and higher-order QED pair probabilities were calculated using an analytical elastic form factor derived from a Woods-Saxon charge distribution, while the Coulomb dissociation probability was unitarized from a phenomenological photodissociation model and the hadronic interaction probability was computed via the Glauber model with a total hadronic cross section of 52 mb.

## Key numbers
- For the invariant mass range 2.0–2.8 GeV/c², the PHENIX data give dσ/dm dy = 0.95 ± 0.31 μb/GeV; the perturbative QED calculation gives 1.69 μb/GeV (about 2σ high), and the higher-order QED calculation gives 1.24 μb/GeV (within 1σ).
- For 2.0–2.3 GeV/c²: data = 1.43 ± 0.61 μb/GeV; perturbative QED = 2.60 μb/GeV; higher-order QED = 1.90 μb/GeV.
- For 2.3–2.8 GeV/c²: data = 0.65 ± 0.30 μb/GeV; perturbative QED = 1.14 μb/GeV; higher-order QED = 0.83 μb/GeV.
- The fraction of events with |y_pair| < 0.35 and both e⁺ and e⁻ within |η| < 0.35 is 1.10% for the full mass range, 1.11% for 2.0–2.3 GeV/c², and 1.08% for 2.3–2.8 GeV/c².
- The Woods-Saxon parameters used for Au are R = 6.38 fm and a = 0.535 fm; the hard sphere radius in the form factor is r = 6.5 fm with Yukawa range a = 0.7 fm.

## Context
This work extends the higher-order QED methodology previously applied by Baltz (2008) to STAR data at lower invariant masses (140–265 MeV/c²) to the PHENIX measurement at higher masses (2.0–2.8 GeV/c²). The calculations are compared directly with the Starlight Monte Carlo (using the Weizsacker-Williams equivalent-photon approximation), which agrees well with the data. The authors argue that the perturbative QED result is higher than the equivalent-photon result because the smooth form factor allows more high-momentum photons than the sharp nuclear-radius cutoff used in Starlight; a simple luminosity estimate suggests the two-photon luminosity would increase by 44% over the sharp cutoff in the STAR mass range. The work enables refined tests of higher-order QED in strong electromagnetic fields at relativistic heavy-ion colliders.
