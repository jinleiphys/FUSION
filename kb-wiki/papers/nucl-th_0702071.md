# Enhancement of $B(E2)$ and low excitation of the second 0$^+$ state near N=40 in Ge isotopes

> The long-standing problem of dramatic structure change near N=40 in Ge isotopes is investigated by means of large-scale shell model calculation. The analysis of simulated calculations suggests a possible understanding of the problem in terms of rapid increase in the $g_{9/2}$ proton and neutron occupation. The observed variation in excitation of the second $0^+$ state in $^{70,72,74}$Ge appears to correlate closely with the $g_{9/2}$ occupations induced by strong proton-neutron interactions. The enhancement of the $g_{9/2}$ occupancies is probably due to correlations in the $1g2d3s$ shell.

## Key claim

The observed dramatic structure change near N=40 in Ge isotopes, specifically the enhancement of B(E2) and the low excitation of the second 0+ state, can be understood in terms of a rapid increase in g9/2 proton and neutron occupation, but only if the effective single-particle energy gap between the g9/2 and p1/2 orbits is reduced and the quadrupole matrix element for the g9/2 orbit is enlarged to mimic effects from the missing 2d1g7/2 3s orbits.

## Method

Large-scale shell model calculations were performed in the (p3/2, f5/2, p1/2, g9/2) model space using an extended pairing-plus-quadrupole (P+QQ) Hamiltonian with A-dependent interaction strengths and monopole correction terms. To simulate the missing contributions from the 2d1g7/2 3s orbits, the authors introduced an effective quadrupole matrix element Q_eff(g9/2 g9/2) = η Q(g9/2 g9/2) with η = 1 + 0.05(N-32), and systematically varied the single-particle energy separation ε_g9/2 - ε_p1/2 to study the resulting changes in B(E2) and excitation energies for 66-74Ge.

## Key numbers

* For 72Ge (N=40), the calculation required ε_g9/2 to be lowered to approximately 1.75 MeV (from the original value of 2.5 MeV) to reproduce observed B(E2) and E_x(0_2+).
* For 70Ge, ε_g9/2 needed to be approximately 2.0 MeV; for 74Ge, approximately 1.5 MeV.
* With the adjusted parameters, the calculated g9/2 occupation numbers for the ground state of 72Ge were ⟨n_g9/2^π⟩ ∼ 0.5 and ⟨n_g9/2^ν⟩ ∼ 2.5.
* The standard calculation (without renormalization) gave ⟨n_g9/2^ν⟩ ∼ 0.23 for 72Ge, while the SMMC calculation of Langanke et al. gave ⟨n_g9/2^ν⟩ > 2.
* Effective charges used were e_eff^π = 1.5e and e_eff^ν = 0.5e for the standard calculation; an alternative set e_eff^π = 1.55e and e_eff^ν = 0.97e was tested but could not reproduce the enhancement at N=40.

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing puzzle of structure change in Ge isotopes near N=40, first observed in (p,t) and (t,p) reaction studies by Ardouin, Lebrun, and Mordechai in the 1970s-1980s, and later highlighted by the B(E2) measurements of Padilla-Rodal et al. (2005) at Oak Ridge HRIBF. The calculations extend earlier shell model work by Hasegawa, Kaneko, and collaborators on 64-68Ge, and contrast with the Shell Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) approach of Langanke et al. (2003) which used a larger model space including the 1g2d3s shell. The results suggest that a full two-major-shell calculation including both the 1f2p and 1g2d3s shells is needed for a complete microscopic explanation, as also indicated by Caurier et al. for Cr and Fe isotopes.
