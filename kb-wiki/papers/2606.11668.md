# Application of the Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov Theory to WIMP-Nucleus Interactions in 40Ar

> WIMP scattering from 40Ar is investigated using a self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach. Nuclear form factors relevant to dark matter direct detection are calculated from the resulting one-body density matrix elements and compared with shell-model predictions. Good agreement is found for the spin-independent response, while significant differences are observed for the spin-orbit response due to variations in single-particle occupancies. The effects of particle-number projection are shown to be small for 40Ar. These results demonstrate the sensitivity of certain dark matter response channels to the underlying nuclear structure model and establish a framework for extending mean-field calculations to nuclei beyond the reach of large-scale shell-model studies.

## Key claim

The Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach yields spin-independent form factors for WIMP-40Ar scattering in good agreement with shell-model predictions, but the spin-orbit response shows significant differences due to variations in single-particle occupancies between the two nuclear structure models. The authors present this as a proof-of-principle for spherical 40Ar, noting that particle-number projection effects are small for this nucleus, and that the framework is intended for future extension to deformed nuclei beyond the reach of large-scale shell-model calculations.

## Method

The authors used the code HFBTHO with the SLy4 Skyrme energy density functional and a mixed surface-volume pairing interaction (V0 = -310 MeV, gamma = 1, V1 = 0.5) to compute the ground-state wavefunction of 40Ar in the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory. The one-body density matrix elements (OBDMEs) were extracted in the spherical harmonic oscillator basis and input into the Mathematica package of Anand et al. (2013) to compute nuclear form factors for the spin-independent (M) and spin-orbit (Phi'') response operators. For comparison, shell-model OBDMEs were obtained from NuShellX calculations in the sdpf valence space using four different interactions (SDPF-NR, SDPF-U, EPQQM, SDPF-MU). Particle-number projection was performed using the Fomenko expansion with 15 gauge angle points.

## Key numbers

- Pairing strength V0 = -310 MeV was used for both protons and neutrons, with a pairing cutoff energy of 60 MeV, based on a fit to the experimental neutron pairing gap of 120Sn by Chen et al.
- The harmonic oscillator length parameter b is given by b = 1/sqrt(m_N omega) approximately equal to sqrt(41.467/(45A^{-1/3} - 25A^{-2/3})) fm.
- 15 gauge angle points were used in the Fomenko expansion for particle-number projection, sufficient for the calculation.
- The target nucleus 40Ar has Z=18 protons and N=22 neutrons, with ground-state spin-parity J_i^pi = 0+.

## Context

This work extends the standard shell-model approach to WIMP-nucleus form factors, previously applied by Fitzpatrick et al. (2012), Anand et al. (2013), and Abdel Khaleq et al. (2023, 2024), by introducing a self-consistent mean-field method that does not assume an inert core. The HFB framework is intended to enable calculations for deformed nuclei such as germanium isotopes (relevant to SuperCDMS, EDELWEISS, CDEX) and xenon isotopes (relevant to XENONnT, XLZD) that are beyond the reach of large-scale shell-model studies. The authors note that future work will apply this approach to deformed nuclei, where symmetry restoration (angular momentum and parity projection) will be necessary.
