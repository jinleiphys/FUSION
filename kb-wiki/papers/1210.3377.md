# What can we learn from dijets? A systematic study with VNI/BMS

> We present a systematic study of dijet suppression at RHIC using the VNI/BMS parton cascade code. We examine the medium modification of the dijet asymmetry $A_j$ and the energy distribution within the dijets. Understanding the sensitivity of these observables to properties of the deconfined medium and to experimental factors is vital if dijets are to be useful for QCD tomography. VNI/BMS provides a controllable test-bed with sufficient complexity to model jet modification without confounding results with fluctuations from hydrodynamics and hadronization. Dijets are examined under the modification of: the jet transport coefficient $\hat{q}$; the path length of leading and sub-leading jets; jet cone angle and the jet-medium interaction mechanism. We find that $A_j$ is very sensitive to the distance traveled by the secondary jet, the jet shape is dominated by $\hat{q}$ and the nature of the interaction mechanism.

## Key claim

The dijet asymmetry \(A_j\) is very sensitive to the distance traveled by the sub-leading jet and to the jet transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\), but the authors note that the leading jet shows a strong surface bias and loses very little energy, making its transverse energy a reasonable proxy for its initial energy at RHIC scales.

## Method

The authors used the VNI/BMS parton cascade code in a static uniform-medium mode, modeling the medium as a torus of a given radius with periodic boundary conditions. Dijet events were generated from PYTHIA-8 pp collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) GeV, and anti-kt jet reconstruction was applied with a minimum jet momentum of \(p_{t,\rm min} = 5\) GeV. The jet evolution was recorded without reference to a particular jet definition, allowing the influence of varying jet cone angles and medium conditions to be studied systematically.

## Key numbers

- Dijet asymmetry \(A_j\) was studied for medium temperatures of 250 MeV and 350 MeV, with the higher temperature leading to a depletion of jets with small modification and shifting the distribution toward higher \(A_j\) values.
- The medium radius was fixed at \(R_{med} = 5\) fm for the main analysis, with dijet propagation lengths generated as chords centered on uniformly sampled hard-collision vertices within this torus.
- The strong coupling constant for scatterings was held fixed at \(\alpha_s = 0.3\) for the baseline, though its variation was explored.
- The vacuum jet distribution falls off very quickly; jets with an initial leading jet transverse energy \(E_{t,\ell} > 65\) GeV are extremely rare at RHIC.
- The minimum momentum of an identified jet was fixed at \(p_{t,\rm min} = 5\) GeV, with \(\Delta |\eta_{12}| < 1.1\) and \(\Delta \phi_{12} > \pi / 2\) between the two reconstructed jets.

## Context

This work extends the study of dijet suppression from LHC energies (where dijets with \(E_t \sim 100-200\) GeV have been observed by ATLAS (Aad, 2010) and CMS (Chatrchyan, 2011)) down to the RHIC kinematic window of \(E_t \sim 15-65\) GeV. The authors note that recent work by Renk (2012) indicated that dijet asymmetry results may be relatively insensitive to the fine details of jet energy loss, suggesting \(A_j\) may be unsuitable for tomographic purposes; this study aims to test whether more differential dijet observables at RHIC can provide valuable insights. The results enable future jet measurements at RHIC, particularly with proposed PHENIX upgrades and continuing STAR jet reconstruction development, to probe the nature of jet interactions with the quark-gluon plasma.
