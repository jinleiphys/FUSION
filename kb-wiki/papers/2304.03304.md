# Back-to-back inclusive dijets in DIS at small $x$: Gluon Weizsäcker-Williams distribution at NLO

> In JHEP 11 (2022) 169, we performed the first complete computation of the back-to-back inclusive di-jet cross-section in Deeply Inelastic Scattering (DIS) at small $x_{\rm Bj}$ to next-to-leading order (NLO) in the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory (CGC EFT). We demonstrate here that for di-jets with relative transverse momentum $P_\perp$ and transverse momentum imbalance $q_\perp$, to leading power in $q_\perp/P_\perp$, the cross-section for longitudinally polarized photons can be fully factorized into the product of a perturbative impact factor and the nonperturbative Weizsäcker-Williams (WW) transverse momentum dependent (TMD) gluon distribution to NLO accuracy. The impact factor can further be expressed as the product of a universal soft factor which resums Sudakov double and single logs in $P_\perp/q_\perp$ and a coefficient function given by a remarkably compact analytic expression. We show that in the CGC EFT the WW TMD satisfies a kinematically constrained JIMWLK renormalization group evolution in rapidity. This factorization formula is valid to all orders in $Q_s/q_\perp$ for $q_\perp, Q_s \ll P_\perp$, where $Q_s$ is the semi-hard saturation scale that grows with rapidity.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the next-to-leading order (NLO) back-to-back inclusive dijet cross-section in Deeply Inelastic Scattering (DIS) at small Bjorken $x$ can be fully factorized into a perturbative impact factor and the nonperturbative Weizsäcker-Williams (WW) transverse momentum dependent (TMD) gluon distribution, provided the hierarchy $q_\perp, Q_s \ll P_\perp$ holds, where $q_\perp$ is the dijet transverse momentum imbalance, $P_\perp$ is the relative transverse momentum, and $Q_s$ is the saturation scale. This factorization resolves apparent violations of TMD factorization at NLO that were previously observed in the dense saturated regime where $q_\perp \sim Q_s$.

## Method

The authors computed all real and virtual NLO corrections to the back-to-back inclusive dijet cross-section in the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory (CGC EFT), using the formalism developed in Caucal (2021) and Caucal (2022). They performed a correlation expansion in the limit $P_\perp \gg q_\perp, Q_s$ to isolate the leading power contribution, and demonstrated that the color correlators from diagrams where the gluon crosses the shockwave reduce to the WW TMD distribution. The impact factor was separated into a universal soft factor resumming Sudakov double and single logarithms in $P_\perp/q_\perp$ and a coefficient function containing finite $\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s)$ terms.

## Key numbers

- The factorization formula is valid to all orders in $Q_s/q_\perp$ for $q_\perp, Q_s \ll P_\perp$, where $Q_s$ is the semi-hard saturation scale that grows with rapidity.
- The LO hard factor for longitudinally polarized photons is $\mathcal{H}_{\rm LO}^{\lambda=\textrm{L},ij}(\Pt) = 16z_1^3z_2^3Q^2\frac{\Pt^i\Pt^j}{(\Pt^2+\bar Q^2)^4}$, where $\bar Q^2 = z_1z_2 Q^2$.
- The LO hard factor for transversely polarized photons is $\mathcal{H}_{\rm LO}^{\lambda=\textrm{T},ij}(\Pt) = z_1z_2(z_1^2+z_2^2)\left\{\frac{\delta^{ij}}{(\Pt^2+\bar Q^2)^2}-\frac{4\bar Q^2\Pt^i\Pt^j}{(\Pt^2+\bar Q^2)^4}\right\}$.
- The LO longitudinally polarized cross-section contains the factor $\frac{8(z_1z_2)^3Q^2\Pt^2}{(\Pt^2+\bar Q^2)^4}$ multiplying the WW TMD distributions.

## Context

This work extends the leading-order factorization derived by Dominguez (2011) for back-to-back dijets in DIS to next-to-leading order accuracy, confirming that TMD factorization holds in the correlation limit even when saturation effects are significant ($q_\perp \sim Q_s$). It resolves the apparent factorization violation observed in Caucal (2022) and Taels (2022) for photo-produced dijets, where color correlators from gluons crossing the shockwave appeared to have a complicated structure different from the WW gluon TMD. The results enable robust NLO predictions for the extraction of WW TMD distributions at the Electron-Ion Collider, including the unpolarized and linearly polarized gluon distributions, and provide the foundation for numerical studies of the interplay between Sudakov suppression and gluon saturation effects.
