# Born series for s-wave scattering length and some exact results

> In these notes the Born series for the $s$-wave scattering $a_0$ is calculated for a class of central potentials $V(r)$ up to sixth order in a dimensionless coupling strength $g$. Examples of exponentially decaying potentials as well truncated potentials involving a single length-scale $a$ are considered. In certain favorable cases the exact result for the $g$-dependent $s$-wave scattering length $a_0=A_0(g) a$ can be given in terms of special functions. The poles of $A_0(g)$ at increasing positive values of $g$ correspond to the thresholds, where $s$-wave bound-states occur successively. A scattering problem, where $A_0(g)$ is solvable in terms of elementary functions, is also presented.

# Born series for s-wave scattering length and some exact results

## Key claim

The Born series for the s-wave scattering length can be computed up to sixth order in a dimensionless coupling strength g for a class of central potentials, and in certain favorable cases the exact g-dependent scattering length can be expressed in terms of special functions; however, the series has a finite radius of convergence determined by the threshold for the first s-wave bound state, and closed-form expressions exist only in rare cases.

## Method

The author computes the Born series for the s-wave scattering length a₀ by expanding in powers of a dimensionless coupling strength g, using a potential ansatz V(r) = -ħ²g/(2ma²) f(r/a) with profile function f(x). The expansion coefficients cⱼ are evaluated as multiple integrals involving the profile function f(x) and an "electrostatic potential" Φ(x) derived from f(x). For each potential, the exact scattering length A₀(g) = a₀/a is obtained by solving the radial Schrödinger equation at zero energy and matching the logarithmic derivative at the boundary (for truncated potentials) or at infinity (for exponentially decaying potentials).

## Key numbers

- Yukawa potential: Born series coefficients c₁ = 1, c₂ = 1/2, c₃ = ln(4/3) = 0.28768207, c₄ = ln(32/27) = 0.16989904, c₅ = 0.100941276, c₆ = 0.06006048; first three bound-state thresholds at g₁ = 1.679808, g₂ = 6.44726045, g₃ = 14.34202607
- Exponential potential f(x) = e⁻ˣ: Born series A₀(g) = 2g + (5/4)g² + (23/27)g³ + (677/1152)g⁴ + (7313/18000)g⁵ + (218491/777600)g⁶; exact expression A₀(g) = πN₀(2√g)/J₀(2√g) - ln g - 2γ_E for g>0; first six thresholds at g₁ = 1.445796, g₂ = 7.617816, g₃ = 18.72175, g₄ = 34.76007, g₅ = 55.73308, g₆ = 81.64084
- Spherical potential well: Born series A₀(g) = g/3 + (2/15)g² + (17/315)g³ + (62/2835)g⁴ + (1382/155925)g⁵ + (21844/6081075)g⁶; exact result A₀(g) = -1 + (1/√g)tan√g for g>0; first six thresholds at g₁ = 2.467401, g₂ = 22.20661, g₃ = 61.68503, g₄ = 120.9027, g₅ = 199.8595, g₆ = 298.5555
- Squared Lorentz potential f(x) = 1/(1+x²)²: exact result A₀(g) = -√(1+g) cot(π√(1+g)/2) for g>-1; Born series coefficients c₁ = π/4 = 0.7853982, c₂ = π/16 = 0.1963495; thresholds at gⱼ = 4j²-1 giving g₁ = 3, g₂ = 15, g₃ = 35, g₄ = 63, g₅ = 99, g₆ = 143
- Truncated 1/r² potential with discontinuity: exact result A₀(g) = (1-√(1-4g))/(1+√(1-4g)) for g ≤ 1/4; boundary value A₀(1/4) = 0.6095845

## Context

This work extends the author's previous momentum-space calculation of the two-loop coefficient for the Yukawa potential (Kaiser, earlier work) and provides novel three-loop and higher coefficients. The paper systematically computes Born series up to sixth order for a wide range of central potentials, including Yukawa, exponential, spherical well, truncated Coulomb, oscillator, linear, and cosinus-shaped potentials, as well as potentials with power-law drop-offs. The exact analytical results for the s-wave scattering length in several cases (exponential potential, spherical well, squared Lorentz potential, truncated 1/r² potential) enable precise determination of bound-state thresholds and provide benchmarks for testing numerical methods. The work enables future studies of the convergence properties of the Born series and the relationship between scattering length and bound-state formation in short-range potentials.
