# The pion form factor in improved lattice QCD

> We calculate the electromagnetic form factor of the pion in lattice gauge theory. The non-perturbatively improved Sheikoleslami-Wohlert lattice action is used together with the O(a) improved current. The form factor is compared to results for other choices for the current and features of the structure of the pion deduced from the 'Bethe-Salpeter wave function' are discussed.

## Key claim

The non-perturbatively O(a) improved lattice action and improved current yield a pion form factor that is systematically lower than the one obtained with the conserved Noether current, with the difference reaching about 25% at the largest momentum transfer considered; the form factor for the lightest pion mass (541 MeV) is substantially lower than in previous work where m_pi was about 1 GeV.

## Method

The authors performed quenched lattice QCD simulations on a 24^3 x 32 lattice at beta=6 using the non-perturbatively O(a) improved Sheikoleslami-Wohlert clover action with c_SW=1.769. They calculated two- and three-point correlation functions for the pion, projecting onto definite three-momenta with |p_i|^2 = |p_f|^2 = 2 in units of the minimal momentum, and extracted the form factor by simultaneously fitting all parameters (energies, Z-factors, and F(Q^2)) from 100 configurations using a single elimination jackknife procedure.

## Key numbers

- For the lightest quark mass (kappa=0.1343), the form factor from the improved current is about 25% lower than from the conserved current at the largest momentum transfer considered.
- Pion masses achieved: 0.515(2) lattice units (approx. 671 MeV), 0.357(2) lattice units, and 0.288(2) lattice units (approx. 541 MeV) for kappa values 0.13230, 0.13380, and 0.1343 respectively.
- Fitted vector meson masses m_V from monopole fits: 0.597(14), 0.496(15), and 0.470(19) in lattice units for the three kappa values.
- RMS radii from form factor: 4.23(10), 4.94(15), and 5.21(21) in lattice units for the three kappa values.
- RMS radii from Bethe-Salpeter wavefunction: 2.530(2), 2.615(2), and 2.629(2) in lattice units.
- The charge conservation condition was satisfied to at least 1 ppm for all configurations.

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of the pion form factor by Martinelli and Sachrajda and by Draper et al., who used the Wilson action, by implementing a non-perturbatively O(a) improved action and current. The results are compared to a monopole form factor suggested by vector meson dominance, using rho masses interpolated from Bowler et al., and the Bethe-Salpeter wavefunction approach of Chu, Hecht, Gupta, Lacock, and Schmidt is used to extract RMS radii for comparison. The method enables future calculations to push further toward the physical pion mass with reduced discretisation errors.
