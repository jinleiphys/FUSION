# Extracting the bulk viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma

> We investigate the implications of a nonzero bulk viscosity coefficient on the azimuthal momentum anisotropy of ultracentral relativistic heavy ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider. We find that, with IP-Glasma initial conditions, a finite bulk viscosity coefficient leads to a better description of the flow harmonics in ultracentral collisions. We then extract optimal values of bulk and shear viscosity coefficients that provide the best agreement with flow harmonic coefficients data in this centrality class.

# Extracting the bulk viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim
A nonzero bulk viscosity coefficient improves the description of flow harmonics in ultracentral heavy ion collisions at the LHC when using IP-Glasma initial conditions, but the extracted optimal values depend on the specific parametrization of the bulk correction to the distribution function and the centrality class considered.

## Method
The authors simulate Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies in the 0-1% centrality class using IP-Glasma initial conditions with a thermalization time of 0.4 fm, evolving the system with relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics via the MUSIC hydrodynamic code. They employ relaxation-time equations for bulk viscous pressure and shear-stress tensor derived from kinetic theory, parametrize the shear viscosity as η = a s and the bulk viscosity as ζ = b × η (1/3 - c_s²)², and compute final-state hadrons via the Cooper-Frye procedure with nonequilibrium corrections to the distribution function from the relaxation time approximation.

## Key numbers
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s = 0.15 with bulk parameter b = 15 provides almost equally good agreement with CMS flow harmonics as η/s = 0.22 without bulk viscosity, representing a 32% reduction in η/s.
- For η/s = 0.18 and b = 45, the bulk correction to the distribution function increases v₂{2} by 4%, v₃{2} by 8%, v₄{2} by 20%, v₅{2} by 57%, and v₆{2} by 117%.
- The best description of the mean transverse momentum of pions ⟨p_T^π⟩ occurs for b = 20-40 when η/s = 0.15, compared to ALICE data in the 0-5% centrality class with a lower transverse momentum cutoff of 0.12 GeV.
- Calculations with bulk viscosity used a freezeout temperature of T_FO = 140 MeV, while shear-only calculations used T_CF = 150 MeV and T_FO = 103 MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous hydrodynamic studies of ultracentral heavy ion collisions that considered only shear viscosity (Gale 2012, Denicol 2014) by including bulk viscous effects, which are expected from QCD's nonconformal nature. The IP-Glasma initial conditions incorporate nucleon-nucleon correlations found important for central collisions by Denicol (2014). The results enable future extraction of temperature-dependent transport coefficients and highlight the need for systematic studies of the bulk correction to the distribution function, as different momentum dependences can yield rather different results for observables like mean transverse momentum.
