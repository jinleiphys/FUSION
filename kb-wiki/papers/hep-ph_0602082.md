# Gluon self-energy in the color-flavor-locked phase

> We calculate the self-energies and the spectral densities of longitudinal and transverse gluons at zero temperature in color-superconducting quark matter in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase. We find a collective excitation, a plasmon, at energies smaller than two times the gap parameter and momenta smaller than about eight times the gap. The dispersion relation of this mode exhibits a minimum at some nonzero value of momentum, indicating a van Hove singularity.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the gluon self-energy and spectral densities in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase at zero temperature, finding a collective plasmon excitation at energies below twice the gap parameter and momenta below about eight times the gap, with a dispersion relation exhibiting a minimum at nonzero momentum indicating a van Hove singularity.

## Method

The authors computed the one-loop gluon self-energy components (Π⁰⁰, Πᵗ, Πˡ, and Π⁰ⁱp̂ᵢ) in the CFL phase using the quark-loop approximation, neglecting gluon-loop contributions which are suppressed at temperatures below the critical temperature. The imaginary parts were evaluated analytically in terms of elliptic functions, while the real parts were obtained numerically via dispersion integrals. From these, the spectral densities for longitudinal and transverse gluons were constructed, and the dispersion relations for collective excitations were determined by solving for the poles of the gluon propagators.

## Key numbers

- The light plasmon collective mode has a mass m_coll ≃ 1.35 φ, in rough agreement with the value m_coll ≃ 1.362 φ from a prior prediction (plasmon reference)
- The light plasmon dispersion relation has a minimum at momentum p ≃ 1.33 φ, indicating a van Hove singularity
- The light plasmon excitation exists for energies below 2 φ and momenta below approximately 8 φ
- The gluon mass parameter is m_g = 8 φ (used in figures)
- The singlet gap is approximately twice the octet gap: φ₁ ≃ 2 φ₈ ≡ 2 φ
- Octet-octet excitations require gluon energies larger than 2 φ; octet-singlet excitations require p₀ ≥ 3 φ
- A peak-like structure appears at p₀ = E_p⁸⁸ = √(p² + 4φ²) = √20 φ and at p₀ = E_p¹⁸ = √(p² + 9φ²) = 5 φ

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the gluon self-energy in the 2SC phase (2cf, dirkigor) to the CFL phase, where all eight gluons become massive via the Anderson-Higgs mechanism. The calculation confirms the existence of a low-energy collective excitation (the "light plasmon") predicted in earlier work (plasmon, casalbuoni) and provides the full energy-momentum dependence of the gluon self-energy, enabling further studies of transport properties and electromagnetic response in color-superconducting quark matter.
