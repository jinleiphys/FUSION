# Probing the Nuclear Neutron Skin by Low-Energy Dipole Modes

> Dipole excitations below the neutron threshold in neutron rich Sn isotopes are studied theoretically in the Quasiparticle-Phonon Model with HFB single particle input. Of special interest are the low-lying two-phonon 1- states and the Pygmy Dipole Resonance (PDR). The evolution of low-energy dipole excitations with neutron excess is investigated over the Sn isotopic chain including the experimentally unknown regions close to 132Sn. A dependence of the PDR strengths and centroid energies on the neutron skin thickness is found. Despite significant multi-phonon contributions to mean energies and transition strengths, the PDR states retain their one-phonon character. The fragmentation pattern is reduced with increasing neutron excess towards the N=82 shell closure which will be of advantage for future experimental work.

## Key claim

The paper claims a correlation between the total Pygmy Dipole Resonance (PDR) strength and centroid energies and the neutron skin thickness in neutron-rich Sn isotopes, but the analysis is performed at the level of theoretical calculations rather than direct experimental measurement, and the experimental neutron skin data used for comparison were normalized to theoretical predictions, so only the relative mass dependence is considered significant.

## Method

The authors used the Quasiparticle-Phonon Model (QPM) with Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) single-particle input to calculate dipole excitations below the neutron threshold in Sn isotopes from A=120 to A=132. The QPM wave functions included up to three-phonon configurations built from a basis of QRPA states, allowing separation of two-phonon quadrupole-octupole 1- states from genuine PDR states. The mean-field part was treated semi-microscopically using Wood-Saxon potentials adjusted to reproduce binding energies, charge radii, and proton-neutron RMS radius differences from previous HFB calculations.

## Key numbers

- The lowest 1- states in 120-130Sn have excitation energies ranging from 3.203 MeV (120Sn) to 4.115 MeV (128Sn), with the 1-1 states being about 90% two-phonon quadrupole-octupole configurations.
- For 124Sn, the experimental PDR centroid energy is 6.5 MeV with total B(E1) strength of 0.345(43) e2fm2; the QPM calculation gives 6.641 MeV and 0.398 e2fm2.
- The total PDR strength increases with mass number from 0.142 e2fm2 (QRPA, 120Sn) to 0.360 e2fm2 (QRPA, 132Sn), while centroid energies decrease from 6.839 MeV to 5.900 MeV.
- The PDR contribution to the Energy Weighted Sum Rule (EWSR) ranges from 0.185% (120Sn) to 0.380% (132Sn).
- A sudden increase in slope of PDR strength versus neutron skin thickness occurs beyond A=126, where the proton-neutron RMS radius difference δr exceeds 0.2 fm.

## Context

This work extends previous QPM studies of low-energy dipole strength in 208Pb (Tsoneva, Lenske, Stoyanov, 2003) and earlier investigations of PDR in stable nuclei with small and moderate neutron excess. The results are compared to experimental data from Nuclear Resonance Fluorescence measurements by Pon2 and to charge-exchange reaction data on neutron skin thickness by Krasznahorkay et al. (Sn-skin1, Sn-skin2). The paper enables future experimental work by predicting that the reduced fragmentation pattern approaching N=82 will facilitate measurements of PDR strength distributions in exotic Sn isotopes, which could provide information on nuclear shapes far from stability.
