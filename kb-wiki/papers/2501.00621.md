# Uncertainties in tellurium-based dark matter searches stemming from nuclear structure uncertainties

> Using tellurium dioxide as a target, we calculate uncertainties on 90% upper confidence limits of Galilean effective field theory (Galilean EFT) couplings to a weakly-interacting massive particle (WIMP) dark matter candidate due to uncertainties in nuclear shell models. We find that these uncertainties in naturally-occurring tellurium isotopes are comparable across the different Galilean EFT couplings to uncertainties in xenon, with some reaching over 100%. We also consider the effect these nuclear uncertainties have on estimates of the annual modulation of dark matter from these searches, finding that the uncertainties in the modulation amplitude are proportional to the non-modulating upper confidence limit uncertainties. We also show that the determination of the modulation phase is insensitive to changes in the nuclear model for a given isotope.

## Key claim

The paper quantifies how uncertainties in nuclear shell-model calculations propagate into 90% upper confidence limits on Galilean EFT coupling coefficients for WIMP-tellurium interactions in CUORE, finding that these uncertainties can exceed 100% for spin-dependent operators in odd-mass tellurium isotopes, while the phase of the annual modulation signal is insensitive to nuclear model variations for a given isotope.

## Method

The authors performed Gaussian ensemble Monte Carlo simulations using two different shell-model Hamiltonians (GCN and JJ55) for tellurium isotopes, constructing probability distributions for one-body density matrix elements from the differences between the two models. For each of 2500 Monte Carlo iterations, randomized density matrices were input into the dmscatter code to compute differential event rates, from which 90% upper confidence limits on EFT couplings were derived using the Feldman-Cousins method. For oxygen-16, three different wave function models were compared: a simple closed-shell Slater determinant, and no-core shell-model calculations using Daejeon16 and Entem-Machleidt N3LO interactions.

## Key numbers

- For tellurium isotopes, nuclear model uncertainties on 90% upper confidence limits for spin-dependent EFT operators reach over 100% in some cases, with operators 3, 12, and 15 showing small uncertainties below 5%
- For neutron couplings in tellurium, uncertainties reach up to 25% for 123Te across spin-dependent operators
- The CUORE detector efficiency is taken as a constant 0.883, the product of trigger efficiency (99%), data selection efficiency (90%), and anti-coincidence selection efficiency (99.2%)
- For oxygen-16, the experimental root-mean-square radius is 2.59(7) fm, and the harmonic oscillator parameter b was varied between 1.315 fm and 1.764 fm across different models
- The minimum event rate for a 90% confidence level positive detection is taken as 2.3 events/ton-year
- WIMP masses were considered in the range 10-1000 GeV, with a specific calculation shown at 69.5193 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of nuclear structure uncertainties in xenon-based dark matter searches (Heimsoth 2023) to tellurium dioxide targets as used in the CUORE experiment, which primarily searches for neutrinoless double beta decay. The results enable direct comparison of nuclear model uncertainties between xenon-based experiments (XENON1T, XENONnT) and tellurium-based experiments (CUORE), and show that the annual modulation phase determination is robust against nuclear structure uncertainties, supporting the use of CUORE to test the DAMA/LIBRA annual modulation signal.
