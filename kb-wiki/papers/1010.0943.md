# Axial anomaly, Dirac sea, and the chiral magnetic effect

> Gribov viewed the axial anomaly as a manifestation of the collective motion of charged fermions with arbitrarily high momenta in the vacuum. In the presence of an external magnetic field and a chirality imbalance, this collective motion becomes directly observable in the form of the electric current - this is the chiral magnetic effect (CME). I give an elementary introduction into the physics of CME, and discuss some recent developments.

# Axial anomaly, Dirac sea, and the chiral magnetic effect

## Key claim
The chiral magnetic effect (CME) arises from the collective motion of charged fermions at all momenta, including arbitrarily high momenta in the vacuum, and this topological origin protects the effect from being modified by quantum corrections even at strong coupling. The derivation relies on the existence of an external magnetic field and a chirality imbalance that can originate from non-electromagnetic sources such as strong interactions with non-Abelian gauge field configurations of non-trivial topological content.

## Method
The paper derives the CME current by considering the energy balance of chirality generation in the presence of an auxiliary electric field, then taking the limit of vanishing electric field. A more rigorous derivation sums over all Landau levels of charged fermions in a magnetic field, where the lowest Landau level (LLL) is chiral and non-degenerate in spin, while all excited Landau levels cancel in pairs. The effective theory is formulated as Maxwell-Chern-Simons (axion) electrodynamics, where a pseudo-scalar field theta couples to the Chern-Simons current, and the Euler-Lagrange equations yield modified Maxwell equations that produce the CME current.

## Key numbers
- Rate of chirality generation: d^4 Q_5 / (dt dV) = (e^2 / 2 pi^2) (E dot B)
- CME current density: J = (e^2 mu_5 / 2 pi^2) B, where mu_5 = (mu_R - mu_L)/2 is the chiral chemical potential
- For N_f=3 flavor case, the CME electromagnetic current scales as 2/3 (up to overall factor N_c mu_5 B / (2 pi^2)) and the baryon current is zero
- For N_f=2 flavor case, the CME electromagnetic current scales as 5/9 and the baryon current scales as 1/9 (up to the same overall factor)
- For the chiral vortical effect (CVE) with N_f=3, the electromagnetic current is zero and the baryon current scales as 1 (up to overall factor N_c mu_5 mu omega / pi^2)
- For the CVE with N_f=2, the electromagnetic current scales as 1/3 and the baryon current scales as 2/3 (up to the same overall factor)

## Context
This work extends Gribov's interpretation (Gribov, 1981) of the axial anomaly as collective motion of high-momentum fermions in the Dirac sea, and builds on earlier discussions of related phenomena in primordial electroweak plasma (Giovannini, 1997) and quantum wires. The paper connects the CME to Maxwell-Chern-Simons electrodynamics previously developed by Wilczek (1987), Carroll (1989), and Sikivie (1984), and discusses experimental tests at RHIC by the STAR and PHENIX Collaborations. The results enable new tests distinguishing CME from CVE based on the flavor dependence of anomalous triangle diagrams, with predictions that baryon number separation should increase at lower collision energies where baryon chemical potential is larger.
