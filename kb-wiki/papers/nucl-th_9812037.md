# Rigidity and Normal Modes in Random Matrix Spectra

> We consider the Gaussian ensembles of random matrices and describe the normal modes of the eigenvalue spectrum, i.e., the correlated fluctuations of eigenvalues about their most probable values. The associated normal mode spectrum is linear, and for large matrices, the normal modes are found to be Chebyshev polynomials of the second kind. We contrast this with the behaviour of a sequence of uncorrelated levels, which has a quadratic normal mode spectrum. The difference in the rigidity of random matrix spectra and sequences of uncorrelated levels can be attributed to this difference in the normal mode spectra. We illustrate this by calculating the number variance in the two cases.

# Rigidity and Normal Modes in Random Matrix Spectra

## Key claim
The normal mode spectrum of Gaussian random matrix ensembles is linear (eigenvalues λ_k = -kN), while a sequence of uncorrelated levels has a quadratic normal mode spectrum; this difference in dispersion relations directly explains the logarithmic growth of the number variance for random matrices versus the linear growth for uncorrelated levels. The authors emphasize that their results are neither new nor exact, but rather offer a new way to regard fluctuations in random matrix spectra.

## Method
The authors compute the normal modes of the Gaussian orthogonal, unitary, and symplectic ensembles by expanding the joint eigenvalue probability distribution around its maximum in a quadratic approximation. They diagonalize the curvature matrix C (the Hessian of log P_Nβ) to obtain eigenvalues λ_k = -kN and eigenvectors that become Chebyshev polynomials of the second kind in the large-N limit. They then use these normal modes to calculate the asymptotic form of the number variance Σ²(L) for both the Gaussian ensembles and, by an analogous treatment of the correlation matrix D, for a sequence of uncorrelated Poisson-distributed levels.

## Key numbers
- Eigenvalues of the curvature matrix C for Gaussian ensembles: λ_k = -kN, where k runs from 1 to N.
- Average level density in the large-N limit: ρ(x) = (N/π) √(2 - x²).
- Number variance for Gaussian ensembles (large L): Σ_β²(L) ≈ (2/(βπ²)) (ln L + ln(π/2) + γ), where γ is Euler's constant.
- Number variance for uncorrelated levels (Poisson): Σ²(L) = L exactly, and approximately L - (1/(2π³L)) sin(2πL) - 1/π² for large L.
- Hardest normal mode eigenvalue for uncorrelated levels (after rescaling): approximately -(8/π)² N², comparable to λ_N = -N² for random matrices.
- Soft mode spectrum for uncorrelated levels: approximately -2k² for small k, versus -kN (linear) for random matrices.

## Context
This work extends Dyson's (1962) Coulomb gas analogy for random matrix eigenvalues by providing a normal-mode analysis of the joint probability distribution. The authors show that the linear dispersion relation for random matrix normal modes parallels that of phonons in elastic solids, supporting Dyson's characterization of the Coulomb gas as a "crystal." The quadratic dispersion for uncorrelated levels leads to a "gas" interpretation. The normal-mode approach offers a complementary perspective to standard results from Mehta's (1991) random matrix theory and provides a practical method for extracting soft modes from numerical simulations of ensembles that resist analytic treatment.
