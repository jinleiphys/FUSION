# On the application of symmetry breaking and its restoration to treat pairing correlation in finite nuclei

> An alternative approach to symmetry restoration within Energy Density Functional, the Symmetry-Conserving EDF is discussed. In this approach, the energy is directly written in terms of the degrees of freedom encoded in the one-, two-... body density matrices of the state having good quantum numbers. The SC-EDF framework is illustrated within Projection After and Before variation applied to particle number restoration.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the standard Multi-Reference Energy Density Functional (MR-EDF) approach to symmetry restoration can be reinterpreted as a functional of the one-body and two-body density matrices of the projected state, and that this Symmetry-Conserving EDF (SC-EDF) framework avoids the spurious terms and regularization difficulties that plague conventional MR-EDF, provided the energy is written directly in terms of the projected state's observables rather than transition densities between rotated states.

## Method

The authors derive the SC-EDF energy functional for particle number restoration by expressing the energy directly in terms of the occupation numbers and two-body density matrix of the symmetry-restored state, rather than using transition densities between the original and rotated quasi-particle states as in standard MR-EDF. They apply this framework to both Projection After Variation (PAV) and Variation After Projection (VAP) for particle number restoration in finite nuclei, using Skyrme energy density functionals (parameterizations SIII and SLy4) with a delta pairing interaction.

## Key numbers

- For the krypton isotopic chain, VAP with particle number restoration gives a pairing energy of 4.1 MeV for $^{76}$Kr at the neutron sub-shell closure N=40, where HFB or BCS approaches yield zero pairing energy in the neutron channel.
- The net energy gain from VAP for $^{76}$Kr is 1 MeV after accounting for mean-field rearrangement.
- The SC-EDF approach applied to PAV with the SIII Skyrme parameterization yields results strictly equivalent to the regularized MR-EDF for $^{18}$O deformation energy curves.
- For the SLy4 parameterization, which contains non-integer powers of the density, the SC-EDF produces smooth potential energy curves for $^{18}$O, whereas standard MR-EDF cannot be regularized and shows steps and divergences.

## Context

This work extends the ongoing debate about the validity of configuration mixing within EDF methods, particularly the regularization issues raised by Lacroix (2009), Bender (2009), and Duguet (2009), and the need to clarify symmetry breaking and restoration in density functional theory discussed by Duguet (2010). The SC-EDF framework directly addresses the three difficulties identified in prior work: regularization of MR-EDF, the choice of density in density-dependent interactions, and the proper behavior under symmetry transformations. The approach enables future applications to other symmetry restorations, such as rotational invariance, and provides a foundation for VAP calculations that can capture pairing correlations even in closed-shell nuclei where BCS/HFB approaches fail.
