# Expanding proton dripline by employing a number of muons

> Through mean-field calculations, we demonstrate that, in a large $Z$ nucleus binding multiple muons, these heavy leptons localize within a few dozen femtometers of the nucleus. The mutual Coulomb interactions between the muons and the protons can lead to a substantial decrease in proton chemical potential, surpassing 1 MeV. These findings allow for expanding the proton-dripline on the nuclear chart in principle, suggesting the possible production of nuclei with Z around 120.

## Key claim

Through mean-field calculations, the authors demonstrate that binding multiple muons to a large-Z nucleus can lower the proton chemical potential by more than 1 MeV, which in principle could expand the proton dripline and allow production of nuclei with Z around 120. The claim is conditional on the muons being present in sufficient numbers (up to 20 studied) and on the assumption that the muon lifetime of 2 microseconds is long enough relative to nuclear timescales for such systems to be experimentally realized.

## Method

The authors performed spherical Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations for N=106 isotones with Z ranging from 82 to 92, incorporating muons as non-relativistic point particles that interact with protons and each other only through Coulomb forces. The self-consistent mean-field equations were solved iteratively for both nucleons and muons, using the Skyrme force II parameterization from Vautherin (1972), with a radial lattice extending to 60 fm and a lattice constant as small as 0.08 fm. Nuclear pairing interactions were ignored, and spherical symmetry was assumed.

## Key numbers

- The energy of the muon 1s1/2 orbital for Z=82 goes from -10.39 MeV at N_mu=1 to -9.10 MeV at N_mu=20.
- The muon 1s1/2 single-particle energy at N_mu=1 decreases from -10.39 MeV at Z=82 to -12.14 MeV at Z=92.
- Adding one muon reduces the proton energy level by about 0.2 MeV; adding 20 muons reduces it by about 1.7 MeV.
- For Z=87, the Coulomb potential of muons near the center is 0.2, 0.8, 1.3, 1.5, and 1.7 MeV for N_mu = 1, 5, 10, 15, and 20 respectively.
- The proton center density varies by less than 3% across the isotones; the change in proton density due to different numbers of muons is about 0.3%.
- Increasing N_mu from 1 to 20 changes the Coulomb potential of the proton by less than 0.1%.
- The muon Coulomb potential increases by about 3.5% as Z increases from 82 to 92.
- The proton Coulomb potential increases by about 8% as Z increases from 82 to 92.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing study of muonic atoms, where muons are captured by atoms and used to probe nuclear properties such as ground state spin, magnetic dipole moments, and electric quadrupole moments (Wu 1969, Knecht 2020, Measday 2001). Prior theoretical work on muonic atoms and ions (Borie 1982, Dong 2011) focused on atomic physics and quantum electrodynamics rather than the possibility of stabilizing proton-rich nuclei. The present study suggests that muons could enable the production of superheavy nuclei with Z around 120, which would otherwise be unbound due to Coulomb repulsion. The authors note that experimentally generating a nucleus with multiple muons is technically very challenging given the muon lifetime of 2 microseconds, but the timescale is still long compared to the typical nuclear timescale of 10^-22 seconds.
