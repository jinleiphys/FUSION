# Spin-isospin nuclear response using the existing microscopic Skyrme functionals

> Our paper aims at providing an answer to the question whether one can reliably describe the properties of the most important spin-isospin nuclear excitations, by using the available non-relativistic Skyrme energy functionals. Our method, which has been introduced in a previous publication devoted to the Isobaric Analog states, is the self-consistent Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA). The inclusion of pairing is instrumental for describing a number of experimentally measured spherical systems which are characterized by open shells. We discuss the effect of isoscalar and isovector pairing correlations. Based on the results for the Gamow-Teller resonance in $^{90}$Zr, in $^{208}$Pb and in few Sn isotopes, we draw definite conclusions on the performance of different Skyrme parametrizations, and we suggest improvements for future fits. We also use the spin-dipole resonance as a benchmark of our statements.

## Key claim

The paper assesses whether existing non-relativistic Skyrme energy functionals can reliably describe spin-isospin nuclear excitations, concluding that while some parameter sets perform adequately for Gamow-Teller resonance energies, the collectivity (fraction of sum rule strength) varies significantly and reveals deficiencies in certain forces like SLy5, which produces less collective wavefunctions due to smaller residual interaction matrix elements.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) calculations based on Hartree-Fock plus BCS ground states, using a zero-range density-dependent pairing force for open-shell Sn isotopes. They solved the QRPA matrix equations in a discretized continuum with box boundary conditions, treating the spin-gradient (J^2) terms of the Skyrme functional differently for type-I forces (included in residual interaction but not mean field) versus type-II forces (included everywhere), and analyzed the Gamow-Teller and isovector spin-dipole responses for 90Zr, 208Pb, and several Sn isotopes using the SIII, SGII, SLy5, and SkO' parameter sets.

## Key numbers

- The GTR peak energy in 208Pb varies by only 60 keV between SLy4 and SLy5, but varies by 0.5 MeV when J^2 terms are added to the SLy4 mean field
- Removing the J^2 term contribution from the p-h interaction changes the GTR peak energy by about 2 MeV
- The GTR in 208Pb and Sn isotopes exhausts approximately 60-70% of the Ikeda sum rule for SIII, SGII, and SkO', while SLy5 shows reduced collectivity
- The spin-orbit splitting changes by up to 400 keV when J^2 terms are included in the mean field
- In 90Zr, the discrepancy between the present calculations and those of Bender (2005) amounts to about 1.2 MeV from the choice of reference energy and another approximately 1 MeV from J^2 term treatment, with about 1 MeV remaining unexplained

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent QRPA method previously introduced by the authors for Isobaric Analog States (Fracasso, 2005) to Gamow-Teller and spin-dipole resonances, and directly compares with the HFB-based QRPA calculations of Bender (2005) for the GTR, finding different results due to treatment of J^2 terms and energy reference choices. The study enables future fits of Skyrme parameters to include GT data as additional constraints, particularly to address the anomalous behavior of SLy5, and provides a microscopic benchmark for the spin-dipole response which has been previously calculated only with phenomenological approaches like Bertsch (1981) and Auerbach (1980).
