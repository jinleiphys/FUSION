# Topological characterization of neutron star crusts

> Neutron star crusts are studied using a classical molecular dynamics model developed for heavy ion reactions. After the model is shown to produce a plethora of the so-called "pasta" shapes, a series of techniques borrowed from nuclear physics, condensed matter physics and topology are used to craft a method that can be used to characterize the shape of the pasta structures in an unequivocal way.

# Topological characterization of neutron star crusts

## Key claim
The combination of curvature and Euler number (Minkowski functionals) applied to nucleon configurations from classical molecular dynamics simulations can uniquely classify the shapes of nuclear pasta structures in neutron star crusts, including a previously unreported "jungle gym" phase. The authors hedge that the absolute magnitude of these topological measures depends on the overall size of the structure (i.e., number of particles used), so data must be normalized for comparison.

## Method
The authors used a classical molecular dynamics model (CMD) with Pandharipande two-body potentials and screened Coulomb interactions to simulate infinite nuclear matter in periodic boxes of 2000 or 3000 nucleons at densities from 0.01 fm⁻³ to 0.16 fm⁻³, temperatures from 0.1 to 1.0 MeV, and proton fractions x=0.3 and x=0.5. Clusters were identified using the Minimum Spanning Tree algorithm with a 3.0 fm clusterization radius, and topological characterization was performed by digitizing nucleon positions onto a cubic grid and computing Minkowski functionals (volume, area, mean breadth, and Euler characteristic) using the algorithm of Michielsen and De Raedt.

## Key numbers
- Equilibrium density of the Pandharipande potential: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Binding energy at equilibrium: E(ρ₀) = 16 MeV/nucleon
- Compressibility: 250 MeV ("Medium") or 535 MeV ("Stiff")
- Simulation densities ranged from ρ = 0.01 fm⁻³ (about ρ₀/15) to ρ₀
- Temperatures studied: T = 0.1 to 1.0 MeV
- Proton fractions: x = Z/A = 0.5 (1000 neutrons, 1000 protons) or 0.3 (2000 neutrons, 1000 protons)
- Clusterization radius: r_cl = 3.0 fm
- Potential cutoff radius: r_c = 5.4 fm
- Screening length: λ = 10 fm
- Nearest neighbor distance from pair correlation function: r ≈ 1.7 fm at all densities studied
- Percolation densities at T=0.1 MeV: ρ ≈ 0.03 fm⁻³ for x=0.3, ρ ≈ 0.024 fm⁻³ for x=0.5

## Context
This work extends dynamical simulations of nuclear pasta using molecular dynamics approaches previously developed for heavy ion reactions by Pandharipande (1979) and applied to neutron star crusts by Horowitz and collaborators (2004-2008). It addresses the problem that previous characterizations relied on visual inspection of snapshots (Watanabe 2010) or static models using Minkowski functionals in a different methodology (Watanabe 2009). The topological classification method enables quantitative comparison between different theoretical models and provides a tool to systematically map the phase diagram of neutron star crust matter, which the authors plan to apply to study the role of Coulomb interactions and the equation of state in pasta formation.
