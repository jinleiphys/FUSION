# Constraints on spin observables

> Positivity constrains the allowed domain for sets of spin observables in exclusive or inclusive reactions. Examples are given for strangeness-echange reactions and photoproduction.

# Constraints on spin observables

## Key claim
Positivity of the density matrix imposes model-independent constraints on the allowed domain for sets of spin observables in exclusive and inclusive reactions, which can restrict triples of observables to volumes as small as 1/3 of the full hypercube even when no pair of observables is individually constrained.

## Method
The authors derive inequalities and identities for spin observables from the positivity requirement of the density matrix in various reaction channels. They analyze exclusive reactions such as spin-0 spin-1/2 elastic scattering and the reaction pp to Lambda anti-Lambda, as well as inclusive reactions like gamma + p to Lambda + K and general a + b to anything. The allowed domains for triples of observables are mapped by randomly generating fictitious amplitudes and then rigorously establishing the boundaries.

## Key numbers
- For pi-N elastic scattering, the three independent observables satisfy the identity P_n^2 + A^2 + R^2 = 1, meaning each observable is constrained to [-1,+1] but the triple lies on the unit sphere.
- For the reaction pp to Lambda anti-Lambda, the inequalities D_nn^2 + C_mm^2 <= 1 and D_nn^2 + C_ll^2 <= 1 constrain the transverse polarization transfer.
- For triples of observables in both exclusive and inclusive reactions, the allowed domain can be a tetrahedron whose volume is only 1/3 of the cube [-1,+1]^3.
- For inclusive reactions a + b to anything, the helicity and transversity asymmetries satisfy |Delta sigma_T| <= sigma_tot + Delta sigma_L/2.

## Context
This work extends the earlier studies of spin observable domains initiated by Doncel, Minnaert, Michel and others. The authors apply positivity constraints to modern data from the PS185 collaboration at CERN's LEAR facility for pp to Lambda anti-Lambda, and to recent photoproduction data for gamma + p to Lambda + K. The results enable model-independent tests of consistency among spin measurements and identify which yet-unknown observables will best distinguish between competing theoretical models, such as Yukawa-type meson exchange models versus quark-based models with qqbar annihilation and ssbar creation.
