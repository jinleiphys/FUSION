# Many-body correlations in Semiclassical Molecular Dynamics and Skyrme forces for symmetric Nuclear Matter

> Constraint Molecular dynamics CoMD calculations have been performed for symmetric nuclear matter (NM) by using a simple effective interactions of the Skyrme type. The set of parameter values reproducing common accepted saturation properties of nuclear matter have been obtained for different degree of stiffness characterizing the iso-vectorial potential density dependence. A comparison with results obtained in the limit of the Semi-Classical Mean Field approximation performed using the same kind of interaction put in evidence the role played by the many-body correlations present in the model explaining also the noticeable differences obtained in the parameter values in the two cases

## Key claim

Constraint Molecular Dynamics (CoMD) calculations for symmetric nuclear matter using Skyrme-type interactions produce energy-density functionals that differ significantly from the Semi-Classical Mean Field Approximation (Se-MFA) even when the same elementary interaction is used, because the localized wave packets in CoMD preserve two-body correlations that are averaged out in mean-field approaches.

## Method

The author performed CoMD simulations of symmetric nuclear matter with 1600 and 3560 nucleons enclosed in spherical volumes at densities between 0.7 and 1.2 times saturation density, using a Skyrme-type effective interaction with isoscalar, three-body, and isovector terms. The overlap integrals per nucleon, correlation coefficients, and kinetic energies were extracted from the minimum-energy configurations obtained via a cooling-warming procedure with Pauli constraint, then corrected for surface effects using a finite-size scaling relation. An iterative procedure was used to find the Skyrme parameter sets that reproduce the reference saturation properties (density 0.165 fm^-3, binding energy -16 MeV, compressibility 220 MeV) within the CoMD framework, with convergence after 2-3 iterations.

## Key numbers

- Reference saturation density: 0.165 fm^-3
- Reference binding energy at saturation: -16 MeV
- Reference compressibility modulus: 220 MeV
- Reference symmetry energy at saturation: 28.6 MeV (Se-MFA case, T4 = 32 MeV)
- Se-MFA parameter values: T0 = -263 ± 1.3 MeV, T3 = 208 ± 1 MeV, σ = 1.25 ± 0.02
- CoMD calculations performed at densities from 0.7 ρ0 to 1.2 ρ0 in steps of 0.1 ρ0
- System sizes: A1 = 1600 and A2 = 3560 particles
- Wave packet width σr of order 1-2 fm, with localization volume of order 2-10 fm^3
- Convergence criterion for iterative procedure: parameter changes less than ±5% between steps
- For T4 = 0 MeV, finite α values remain due to Pauli correlations in the interacting system

## Context

This work extends the comparison between mean-field based approaches (like the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport models of BNV, VUU, BUU type) and molecular dynamics models (QMD, CoMD, FMD, AMD) by quantifying how many-body correlations alter the effective energy-density functional even when the same Skyrme-type elementary interaction is used. The results show that the overlap integral per nucleon deviates from a simple linear density dependence and that the isovector interaction produces a bias term Ebias proportional to the correlation coefficient α, which depends on both density and the stiffness parameter γ of the isovector form factor. This enables future work on asymmetric nuclear matter and provides a framework for understanding why molecular dynamics and mean-field approaches require different effective interaction parameters to reproduce the same saturation properties.
