# Microscopic calculations of isospin-breaking corrections to superallowed beta-decay

> The superallowed beta-decay rates that provide stringent constraints on physics beyond the Standard Model of particle physics are affected by nuclear structure effects through isospin-breaking corrections. The self-consistent isospin- and angular-momentum-projected nuclear density functional theory is used for the first time to compute those corrections for a number of Fermi transitions in nuclei from A=10 to A=74. The resulting leading element of the CKM matrix, |V_{ud}|= 0.97447(23), agrees well with the recent result by Towner and Hardy [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 77}, 025501 (2008)].

## Key claim

The self-consistent isospin- and angular-momentum-projected nuclear density functional theory is used for the first time to compute isospin-breaking corrections to superallowed Fermi beta-decay transitions, yielding a value of |V_ud| = 0.97447(23) that agrees with the result of Towner and Hardy (2008); however, the confidence level of the calculated corrections is low, primarily due to the poor spectroscopic quality of the Skyrme SV energy density functional used.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent nuclear density functional theory calculations using the Skyrme SV parametrization, with simultaneous isospin and angular-momentum projection applied to DFT states that break both rotational and isospin symmetries. The Hamiltonian, including the isospin-invariant kinetic energy, Skyrme nuclear interaction, and isospin-breaking Coulomb force, was rediagonalized in a good-angular-momentum and good-isospin basis to remove spurious isospin mixing while retaining physical mixing from the Coulomb interaction. Calculations were performed with the DFT solver HFODD (v2.48q) using 10 oscillator shells for A < 40, 12 shells for 40 ≤ A < 62, and 14 shells for A ≥ 62, with systematic errors from basis truncation not exceeding about 10%.

## Key numbers

- |V_ud| = 0.97447(23), extracted from averaging over 13 superallowed beta transitions from A=10 to A=74
- Average corrected ft-value: overline{cal F}t = 3070.4(9) s
- CKM unitarity test: |V_ud|^2 + |V_us|^2 + |V_ub|^2 = 1.00031(61), implying unitarity satisfied to 0.1% precision
- Isospin-breaking correction delta_C for A=10: 0.559(56)%
- Isospin-breaking correction delta_C for A=74: 2.06(21)%
- Isospin-breaking correction delta_C for A=38: approximately 10% (excluded from V_ud calculation)
- Reduced chi-squared value of 5.2 for the set of delta_C values, compared to 0.4 for Towner and Hardy (2008) shell-model results

## Context

This work extends the approach of Towner and Hardy (2008), who used the nuclear shell model with mean-field radial wave functions, by applying a fully self-consistent isospin- and angular-momentum-projected density functional theory framework that contains no adjustable parameters and simultaneously describes symmetry breaking, configuration mixing, and long-range Coulomb polarization. The results provide a microscopic benchmark for future improvements, particularly through the development of regularizable energy density functionals and inclusion of pairing, which are needed to overcome the limitations of the SV parametrization used here.
