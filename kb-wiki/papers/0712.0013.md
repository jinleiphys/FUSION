# Coulomb interactions within Halo Effective Field Theory

> I present preliminary results of effective field theory applied to nuclear cluster systems, where Coulomb interactions play a significant role.

## Key claim

Halo EFT can be applied to alpha-alpha and proton-alpha systems where Coulomb forces are important, but the alpha-alpha system requires a more fine-tuned power counting than naively expected because the scattering length is incredibly large (a0 ~ 10^3 fm), and the inverse scattering length is very sensitive to cancellations between strong and electromagnetic contributions.

## Method

For alpha-alpha scattering at low energies (E_LAB less than about 3 MeV), the author developed a power counting that treats Coulomb interactions non-perturbatively, yielding an amplitude proportional to -1/a0 + r0 k^2/2 - 2H(eta)/a_B plus subleading terms. For proton-alpha scattering at low energies (E_LAB less than about 4 MeV), the formalism was extended to include P-waves, adopting the same power counting from BHvK (2005) where the P_1/2 wave does not contribute up to next-to-leading order.

## Key numbers

- Alpha-alpha S-wave resonance at E_R = 184 KeV with width Gamma_R = 11 eV (the 8Be ground state)
- Alpha-alpha scattering length a0 ~ 10^3 fm, implying power counting a0 ~ M_hi / M_lo^2 and effective range r0 ~ 1/M_hi
- Alpha-alpha Bohr radius a_B = 2/(m_alpha Z_alpha^2 alpha_em) approximately 137/(2 m_alpha)
- Proton-alpha P_3/2 resonance around E_LAB ~ 2.3 MeV

## Context

This work extends Halo EFT, introduced by BHvK (2005) for neutron-alpha scattering, to charged cluster systems (alpha-alpha and proton-alpha) that are relevant for describing heavier nuclear systems. The alpha-alpha system shows that when Coulomb is turned off, the 8Be system exhibits conformal invariance and the corresponding 3-body system 12C acquires an exact Efimov spectrum, as noted in the EFT review literature. The results enable a systematic EFT description of nuclear cluster systems with Coulomb interactions, with the alpha-alpha fine-tuning remaining an open question.
