# Virtual Compton scattering off spin-zero particles at low energies

> We discuss the low-energy behavior of the virtual Compton scattering amplitude off a spin-zero target. We first compare various methods of obtaining a low-energy expression based either on the soft-photon approximation or the use of Ward-Takahashi identities. We point out that structure-dependent terms are defined with respect to a low-energy approximation of the pole terms which commonly is separated from the full amplitude. We derive a general expression for the structure-dependent terms in an expansion in terms of the momenta $k_1$ and $k_2$ of the initial and final virtual photon, respectively, up to and including terms of order ${\cal O}(k^4)$. At order ${\cal O}(k^2)$ two terms appear which are related to the usual electric and magnetic polarizabilities of real Compton scattering. At order ${\cal O}(k^4)$ we find nine new structures of which five can only be determined using virtual photons.

## Key claim

The low-energy virtual Compton scattering amplitude for a spin-zero target can be expanded in terms of the photon momenta, but the definition of the structure-dependent terms (such as polarizabilities) is not unique: it depends on the specific choice of the low-energy approximation for the pole terms that is separated from the full amplitude, so different separation schemes (soft-photon approximation vs. Ward-Takahashi approaches) lead to different numerical values for the same physical constants.

## Method

The authors compared two methods for deriving a low-energy expression of the virtual Compton scattering amplitude off a charged spin-zero particle (e.g., a pion): the soft-photon approximation (SPA) and an approach using Ward-Takahashi (WT) identities. They constructed the full amplitude as a sum of a singular pole part and a remainder, imposed gauge invariance and crossing symmetry, and expanded the structure-dependent remainder in powers of the photon momenta \(k_1\) and \(k_2\) up to order \(\mathcal{O}(k^4)\). The general tensor structure was solved order by order using the constraints from gauge invariance, crossing symmetry, charge conjugation, and time reversal invariance.

## Key numbers

- At order \(\mathcal{O}(k^2)\), two structure-dependent constants appear, related to the electric and magnetic polarizabilities of real Compton scattering via \(\bar{\alpha} = -\frac{e^2}{8\pi m}(g_0 + 8m^2 \tilde{c}_1)\) and \(\bar{\beta} = \frac{e^2 g_0}{8\pi m}\), where \(e^2/4\pi \approx 1/137\) and \(m\) is the pion mass.
- At order \(\mathcal{O}(k^4)\), nine new structure-dependent constants are found, of which four (\(g_{2a}, g_{2c}, \tilde{c}_{3a}, \tilde{c}_{3c}\)) can in principle be determined from real Compton scattering, while the other five require virtual photons.
- If charge conjugation or time reversal symmetry is violated, one additional structure appears at \(\mathcal{O}(k^3)\).

## Context

This work extends the low-energy theorem for real Compton scattering off the proton (Low, 1954; Gell-Mann and Goldberger, 1954) to virtual photons for spin-zero targets, and generalizes the virtual Compton scattering formalism previously developed for nucleons (Guichon et al., 1995; Scherer et al., 1996). The paper clarifies that the separation of structure-dependent terms from the pole terms is not unique, which has implications for the definition of generalized polarizabilities measured in experiments such as \(e + p \rightarrow e + p + \gamma\). The results enable future extractions of these polarizabilities from virtual Compton scattering data on pions or spin-zero nuclei, provided the chosen low-energy approximation scheme is specified.
