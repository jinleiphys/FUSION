# State densities of heavy nuclei in the static-path plus random-phase approximation

> Nuclear state densities are important inputs to statistical models of compound-nucleus reactions. State densities are often calculated with self-consistent mean-field approximations that do not include important correlations and have to be augmented with empirical collective enhancement factors. Here, we benchmark the static-path plus random-phase approximation (SPA+RPA) to the state density in a chain of samarium isotopes $^{148-155}$Sm against exact results (up to statistical errors) obtained with the shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method. The SPA+RPA method incorporates all static fluctuations beyond the mean field together with small-amplitude quantal fluctuations around each static fluctuation. Using a pairing plus quadrupole interaction, we show that the SPA+RPA state densities agree well with the exact SMMC densities for both the even- and odd-mass isotopes. For the even-mass isotopes, we also compare our results with mean-field state densities calculated with the finite-temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approximation. We find that the SPA+RPA repairs the deficiencies of the mean-field approximation associated with broken rotational symmetry in deformed nuclei and the violation of particle-number conservation in the pairing condensate. In particular, in deformed nuclei the SPA+RPA reproduces the rotational enhancement of the state density relative to the mean-field state density.

## Key claim

The static-path plus random-phase approximation (SPA+RPA) yields state densities for samarium isotopes 148-155Sm that agree well with exact shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) results, provided the method is applied above its breakdown temperature and the ground-state energy is extracted via a partition function extrapolation method.

## Method

The authors applied the SPA+RPA to a configuration-interaction shell model Hamiltonian with pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole interactions, using a Monte Carlo method to integrate over the static auxiliary fields (deformation parameters β₂, γ, and pairing gaps Δp, Δn). They computed the canonical partition function via number-parity projection combined with a saddle-point approximation for particle-number projection, then obtained the state density through the saddle-point approximation of the inverse Laplace transform of the partition function. For the even-mass isotopes, they compared SPA+RPA results with finite-temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) mean-field calculations.

## Key numbers

- State densities were calculated for the chain of samarium isotopes 148-155Sm, covering the crossover from vibrational to rotational collectivity.
- The SPA+RPA canonical entropy and state density agreed well with SMMC results for both even-mass and odd-mass isotopes in this chain.
- For even-mass deformed isotopes, the SPA+RPA reproduced the rotational enhancement of the state density relative to the HFB mean-field density.
- The SPA+RPA entropy remained nonnegative at low temperatures, whereas the HFB pairing phase exhibited unphysical negative entropy.
- The SPA+RPA enhancement factors relative to HFB densities were in good agreement with those extracted from SMMC and consistent with a crossover from pairing-dominated collectivity to rotational collectivity.

## Context

This work extends prior SPA+RPA applications to heavy nuclei with realistic interactions, which had previously been limited to erbium isotopes with pure pairing forces (Rossignoli, 1998), molybdenum isotopes with pairing forces (Kaneko, 2006), and ⁵⁶Fe with a pairing plus quadrupole interaction (Kaneko, 2007). The benchmark against SMMC results for samarium isotopes, which were previously studied by Ozen (2013) and others, demonstrates that SPA+RPA can serve as a complementary method to SMMC, particularly for interactions where SMMC suffers from a sign problem. The approach enables microscopic calculations of state densities in heavy nuclei without requiring empirical collective enhancement factors.
