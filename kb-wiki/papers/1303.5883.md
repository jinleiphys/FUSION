# Single-Particle Momentum Distributions of Efimov States in Mixed-Species Systems

> We solve the three-body bound state problem in three dimensions for mass imbalanced systems of two identical bosons and a third particle in the universal limit where the interactions are assumed to be of zero-range. The system displays the Efimov effect and we use the momentum-space wave equation to derive formulas for the scaling factor of the Efimov spectrum for any mass ratio assuming either that two or three of the two-body subsystems have a bound state at zero energy. We consider the single-particle momentum distribution analytically and numerically and analyse the tail of the momentum distribution to obtain the three-body contact parameter. Our finding demonstrate that the functional form of the three-body contact term depends on the mass ratio and we obtain an analytic expression for this behavior. To exemplify our results, we consider mixtures of Lithium with either two Caesium or Rubium atoms which are systems of current experimental interest.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for mass-imbalanced three-body systems (two identical bosons and a distinguishable particle), the asymptotic single-particle momentum distribution contains a subleading $q^{-5}$ term that does not cancel for general mass ratios, unlike the equal-mass case where it cancels exactly; this means the functional form of the three-body contact parameter depends on the mass ratio.

## Method

The authors solve the three-body bound state problem in momentum space using subtracted Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian integral equations with zero-range interactions, deriving the spectator functions $\chi_{AA}$ and $\chi_{AB}$ for an $AAB$ system. They analytically compute the asymptotic forms of the single-particle momentum distributions $n(q_B)$ and $n(q_A)$ by expanding the four contributing terms ($n_1$ through $n_4$) in the large-momentum limit, and numerically evaluate the results for specific mass ratios corresponding to $^6$Li-$^{133}$Cs-$^{133}$Cs and $^6$Li-$^{87}$Rb-$^{87}$Rb mixtures.

## Key numbers

- Scaling factor $s = 2.00588$ for $^{133}$Cs-$^{133}$Cs-$^6$Li ($\mathcal{A}=6/133$) when all three subsystems have resonant interactions
- Scaling factor $s = 1.68334$ for $^{87}$Rb-$^{87}$Rb-$^6$Li ($\mathcal{A}=6/87$) when all three subsystems have resonant interactions
- Scaling factor $s = 1.98572$ for $^{133}$Cs-$^{133}$Cs-$^6$Li with no $AA$ interaction
- Scaling factor $s = 1.63454$ for $^{87}$Rb-$^{87}$Rb-$^6$Li with no $AA$ interaction
- Leading-order contact coefficient $C/\kappa_0 = 0.0274$ for $^{133}$Cs-$^{133}$Cs-$^6$Li
- Leading-order contact coefficient $C/\kappa_0 = 0.0211$ for $^{87}$Rb-$^{87}$Rb-$^6$Li
- For equal masses ($\mathcal{A}=1$), the normalized contact coefficient $3(2\pi)^3 C/\kappa_0 = 53.197$, compared to the "exact" value 53.097 from Castin and Werner (2011)
- The $q^{-5}$ non-oscillatory term cancels exactly at $\mathcal{A} = 0.2$, $1$, and $1.57$

## Context

This work extends the universal relations framework of Tan (2008) and the three-body contact concept introduced by Braaten et al. (2011) and Castin and Werner (2011) from equal-mass three-boson systems to mass-imbalanced $AAB$ systems. The authors show that the cancellation of the $q^{-5}$ term found by Castin and Werner (2011) for three identical bosons does not hold for general mass ratios, which has implications for interpreting momentum distribution measurements in cold atom mixtures such as $^6$Li-$^{133}$Cs and $^6$Li-$^{87}$Rb. The results enable experimental probes to discriminate between identical and non-identical three-body correlations in mixed-species cold gas experiments.
