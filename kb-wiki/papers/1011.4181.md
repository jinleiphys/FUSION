# Transport coefficients of gluonic fluid

> The shear ($η$) and bulk ($ζ$) viscous coefficients have been evaluated for a gluonic fluid. The elastic, $gg \rightarrow gg$ and the inelastic, number non-conserving, $gg\rightarrow ggg$ processes have been considered as the dominant perturbative processes in evaluating the viscous co-efficients to entropy density ($s$) ratios. Recently the processes: $gg \rightarrow ggg$ has been revisited and a correction to the widely used Gunion-Bertsch (GB) formula has been obtained. The $η$ and $ζ$ have been evaluated for gluonic fluid with the formula derived recently. The sensitivity of the quantity, $η/s$ on the running coupling constant is also discussed. At $α_s=0.3$ we get $η/s=0.24$ which is close to the value obtained from the analysis of the elliptic flow at RHIC experiments.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio for a gluonic fluid approaches the AdS/CFT lower bound of approximately 1/4π only at large values of the strong coupling constant (α_s ~ 0.47), while at α_s = 0.3 the value is η/s = 0.24, which is close to the value extracted from elliptic flow analysis at RHIC but above the bound.

## Method

The authors evaluated shear (η) and bulk (ζ) viscosities for a gluonic fluid using perturbative QCD, considering both elastic (gg → gg) and inelastic number-non-conserving (gg → ggg) processes. For the inelastic process, they used a recently corrected matrix element (Das-Alam, 2010) that modifies the widely used Gunion-Bertsch formula, and computed η via the procedure of Chen et al. (2007) and ζ via a relaxation-time approach with temperature-dependent sound velocity from lattice QCD.

## Key numbers

- η/s = 0.24 at α_s = 0.3, close to the value obtained from elliptic flow analysis at RHIC experiments
- η/s = 0.12 at α_s = 0.3 (stated later in the text as an alternative value)
- η/s = 1/4π (≈ 0.08) at α_s = 0.47, reaching the AdS/CFT lower bound
- ζ/s ≈ 0.15 at α_s ≈ 0.3, comparable to η/s at the same coupling
- Typical η/s values expected at RHIC (T ≈ 300 MeV, α_s ≈ 0.23) and LHC (T ≈ 700 MeV, α_s ≈ 0.17) are 0.177 and 0.29 respectively

## Context

This work extends prior perturbative QCD calculations of transport coefficients by Xu and Greiner (2008) and Chen et al. (2007), who used the Gunion-Bertsch formula for gg → ggg, by incorporating a correction to that matrix element that becomes significant at lower temperatures (higher coupling). The results enable more precise modeling of the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and the authors note that lattice QCD calculations by Meyer (2007) for pure SU(3) gauge theory also find η/s close to the AdS/CFT bound.
