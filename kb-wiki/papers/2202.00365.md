# A puzzle on isomeric configurations in and around $N=126$ closed shell

> The puzzle of finding consistent nuclear configurations for explaining both the decay probabilities and moments of the ${9/2}^-$, ${8}^+$ and ${21/2}^-$ isomers in and around $N=126$ closed shell has been approached in the generalized seniority scheme. Though $h_{9/2}$ is the dominant orbital near Fermi energy, the role of configuration mixing from the surrounding $f_{7/2}$ and $i_{13/2}$ orbitals is found to be very important for the consistent explanation of all the isomeric properties such as the $B(E2)$ rates, $Q-$moments and $g-$factors. The structural behavior of the closed shell $N=126$ isotonic isomers turns out to be very similar to the $N=124$ and $N=128$ isotonic isomers which have two neutron-holes and two neutron-particles, respectively. This is due to the pairing symmetries of nuclear many-body Hamiltonian. As a confirmation, the microscopic shell model occupancies are also calculated for these isomers in $N=126$ chain which support the generalized seniority results. Additional arguments using the systematics of odd-proton ${9/2}^-$ states in Tl ($Z=81$), Bi ($Z=83$), At ($Z=85$) and Fr ($Z=87$) isotopes are also presented.

# A puzzle on isomeric configurations in and around $N=126$ closed shell

## Key claim

The paper resolves the puzzle of finding a consistent multi-j configuration for the ${9/2}^-$, ${8}^+$, and ${21/2}^-$ isomers in and around the $N=126$ closed shell by using the generalized seniority scheme with a proton $h_{9/2} \otimes f_{7/2} \otimes i_{13/2}$ configuration, but only when the mixing of neighboring $f_{7/2}$ and $i_{13/2}$ orbitals is restricted to about 29% occupancy (corresponding to pair degeneracy $\Omega=7$) to account for the non-degeneracy of the multi-j environment where $h_{9/2}$ is the dominant orbital.

## Method

The authors applied the generalized seniority scheme with a multi-j proton configuration $\{h_{9/2} \otimes f_{7/2} \otimes i_{13/2}\}$ above the $Z=82$ core, using a restricted occupancy model where the dominant $h_{9/2}$ orbital carries full occupancy while neighboring $f_{7/2} \otimes i_{13/2}$ orbitals are limited to 4 particles (29% mixing), yielding an effective pair degeneracy $\Omega=7$. They calculated $B(E2)$ rates, $Q$-moments, and $g$-factors for the ${9/2}^-$, ${8}^+$, and ${21/2}^-$ isomers in $N=124$, $N=126$, and $N=128$ isotonic chains, and validated the generalized seniority occupancies against microscopic shell model calculations using the Kuo-Herling interaction for $N=126$ isotones.

## Key numbers

- The best results for decays and moments are found with $A=1$ and $B=0.18$, giving total occupancy of 14 (full $h_{9/2}$ plus 4 from $f_{7/2} \otimes i_{13/2}$) and pair degeneracy $\Omega=7$, corresponding to $h_{9/2}$ probability of about 71% and $f_{7/2} \otimes i_{13/2}$ probability of about 29%.
- The $B(E2)$ value for the $8^+$ isomer in $^{216}$Th ($Z=90$, $N=126$) is 0.000656 W.U. (estimated assuming 26 keV $E_\gamma$ and 126 $\mu$s half-life), which fits the GS$^*$ trend but contradicts pure seniority predictions.
- The $g$-factor for the ${9/2}^-$ isomer in $^{209}$Bi ($Z=83$, $N=126$) is +0.9093(4) n.m., while the pure Schmidt proton $h_{9/2}$ value is 0.58 n.m.; the GSSM$^*$(p) calculation yields 0.92 n.m., close to experimental data.
- The $Q$-moment for the ${9/2}^-$ isomer in $^{209}$Bi ($Z=83$, $N=126$) is -0.418(6) b, and for $^{207}$Bi ($Z=83$, $N=124$) is -0.62(4) b.
- The $g$-factor for the $8^+$ isomer in $^{216}$Ra ($Z=88$, $N=128$) is {−}0.1(3) n.m., which lies near the GSSM(n) trend calculated using multi-j neutron $g_{9/2} \otimes i_{11/2} \otimes j_{15/2}$ configuration, indicating a change in active nucleons.

## Context

This work extends the generalized seniority approach previously applied to $v=1$, ${13/2}^+$, $v=2$, ${12}^+$, and $v=3$, ${33/2}^+$ isomers in Hg, Pb, and Po isotopic chains (Maheshwari 2021) to the $N=126$ isotonic region. It contradicts the earlier pure seniority interpretation of Ressler (2005) for $8^+$ isomers in $N=120,122,124,126$ isotones, which could not explain the recently measured low $B(E2)$ values in $^{214,216}$Th (Zhou 2021, Zhang 2019). The results enable predictions for unmeasured moments in $N=128$ isotones and suggest that future measurements of $Q$-moments for $8^+$ isomers and $g$-factors for ${21/2}^-$ isomers in Bi isotopes will test the configuration mixing picture.
