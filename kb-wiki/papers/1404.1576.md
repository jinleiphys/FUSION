# A study of the parity-odd nucleon-nucleon potential

> We investigate the parity-violating nucleon-nucleon potential as obtained in chiral effective field theory. By using resonance saturation we compare the chiral potential to the more traditional one-meson exchange potential. In particular, we show how parameters appearing in the different approaches can be compared with each other and demonstrate that analyses of parity violation in proton-proton scattering within the different approaches are in good agreement. In the second part of this work, we extend the parity-violating potential to next-to-next-to-leading order. We show that generally it includes both one-pion- and two-pion-exchange corrections, but the former play no significant role. The two-pion-exchange corrections depend on five new low-energy constants which only become important if the leading-order weak pion-nucleon constant $h_π$ turns out to be very small.

## Key claim

The parity-violating nucleon-nucleon potential derived from chiral effective field theory at next-to-next-to-leading order includes both one-pion-exchange and two-pion-exchange corrections, but the former play no significant role; the two-pion-exchange corrections depend on five new low-energy constants which only become important if the leading-order weak pion-nucleon constant \(h_\pi\) turns out to be very small.

## Method

The authors constructed the parity-violating nucleon-nucleon potential up to next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory, extending previous work that had only reached next-to-leading order. They used resonance saturation techniques to express the low-energy constants of the chiral potential in terms of the parameters of the traditional one-meson exchange (DDH) model, and compared the two frameworks by calculating their contributions to the longitudinal analyzing power in proton-proton scattering at several beam energies (13.6, 45, and 221 MeV lab-energy).

## Key numbers

- The LO OPE potential uses \(F_\pi = 92.4\) MeV, \(m_\pi = 139.57\) MeV, and \(g_A = 1.29\) (taking into account the Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy).
- The DDH model uses strong couplings \(g_\omega = 8.4\), \(g_\rho = 2.8\), \(\chi_S = -0.12\), and \(\chi_V = 3.70\), with meson masses \(m_\rho \simeq m_\omega \simeq 780\) MeV.
- The DDH `best' value for \(h_\pi\) is \(4.6 \times 10^{-7}\), with a range \((0.0 \rightarrow 11) \times 10^{-7}\); the KMW estimate gives \(h_\pi = 1.0 \times 10^{-7}\).
- The bound from \({}^{18}\)F gamma-ray emission gives \(|h_\pi| < 1.3 \times 10^{-7}\).
- The DDH `best' values for the contact LECs \(C_i\) (dimensionless) are: \(C_0 = 4.7 \times 10^{-6}\), \(C_1 = 1.2 \times 10^{-6}\), \(C_2 = -2.2 \times 10^{-6}\), \(C_3 = 1.0 \times 10^{-6}\), \(C_4 = 0.25 \times 10^{-6}\).
- The DDH range for the \(pp\) LAP analysis gives \(-66 \times 10^{-7} \leq h_\rho^{pp} \leq -18 \times 10^{-7}\) and \(+40 \times 10^{-7} \geq h_\omega^{pp} \geq -10 \times 10^{-7}\) at 90% confidence level.

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach to parity violation in nucleon-nucleon interactions beyond the next-to-leading order calculations of Zhu, Kaiser, and others, and provides a systematic comparison with the traditional DDH model of Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (1979). The resonance saturation technique used to relate the chiral low-energy constants to the DDH parameters follows methods previously applied to parity-conserving potentials by Epelbaum (2001) and Berengut (2013). The results enable future analyses of parity-violating observables to incorporate higher-order corrections, which become relevant if the weak pion-nucleon coupling \(h_\pi\) is as small as suggested by model estimates from Kaiser and Meissner and by the first lattice-QCD calculation.
