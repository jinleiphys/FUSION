# On the linearly polarized gluon distributions in the color dipole model

> We show that the linearly polarized gluon distributions appear in the color dipole model as we derive the full cross sections of the DIS dijet production and the Drell-Yan dijet ($γ^*$ jet correlation) process. Together with the normal Weizsäcker-Williams gluon distribution, the linearly polarized one will contribute to the DIS dijet production cross section as the coefficient of the $\cos(2 Δϕ) $ term in the correlation limit. We also derive the exact results for the cross section of the Drell-Yan dijet process, and find that the linearly polarized dipole gluon distribution which is identical to the normal dipole gluon distribution involves in the cross section. The results obtained in this paper agree with the previous transverse momentum dependent factorization study. We further derive the small-$x$ evolution of these linearly polarized gluon distributions and find that they rise as $x$ gets small at high energy.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that linearly polarized gluon distributions arise naturally in the color dipole model for both DIS dijet production and Drell-Yan dijet processes, but only when the azimuthal orientation of the outgoing partons is not averaged over; the contribution from these distributions appears specifically as the coefficient of the cos(2Δφ) term in the correlation limit where the dijets are nearly back-to-back.

## Method

The authors derived the full cross sections for DIS dijet production and Drell-Yan dijet (γ* jet correlation) processes in the color dipole model (Color Glass Condensate formalism) by expanding the quadrupole amplitude in the correlation limit where the transverse separation of the quark-antiquark pair is small. They decomposed the operator expression involving the field strength tensor into a trace part (conventional Weizsäcker-Williams gluon distribution) and a traceless part (linearly polarized gluon distribution), then derived the small-x evolution equations for both types of linearly polarized gluon distributions using the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation for the dipole type and the quadrupole evolution for the Weizsäcker-Williams type.

## Key numbers

- For q⊥² ≫ Q_sg², the linearly polarized Weizsäcker-Williams gluon distribution xh⊥^(1)(x,q⊥) ≃ (α_s A C_F N_c)/(π² q⊥²), which is identical to xG^(1)(x,q⊥) and scales like A (number of nucleons)
- For Λ² ≪ q⊥² ≪ Q_sg², xh⊥^(1)(x,q⊥) ≃ (α_s A C_F N_c)/(π² Q_sg²), which is approximately constant and scales like A^(2/3) since Q_sg² ∼ A^(1/3)
- In the low q⊥² region, the ratio xG^(1)(x,q⊥)/xh⊥^(1)(x,q⊥) ≃ ln(q⊥²/Λ²) ln(Q_sg²/q⊥²) ≫ 1
- For the Drell-Yan dijet process, the linearly polarized dipole gluon distribution xh⊥^(2)(x,q⊥) equals the normal dipole gluon distribution xG^(2)(x,q⊥) exactly

## Context

This work extends the color dipole model calculations of Dominguez et al. (2010, 2011) for dijet production, which previously averaged over the azimuthal orientation of outgoing partons and thus did not reveal the linearly polarized gluon contributions. The results agree completely with the transverse momentum dependent (TMD) factorization study of Metz et al. (2011). The paper enables experimental extraction of the linearly polarized Weizsäcker-Williams gluon distribution through the cos(2Δφ) modulation in DIS dijet production, and shows that the linearly polarized dipole gluon distribution can be probed in Drell-Yan dijet measurements in pA collisions.
