# Neutron Drops and Skyrme Energy-Density Functionals

> The J$^π$=0$^+$ ground state of a drop of 8 neutrons and the lowest 1/2$^-$ and 3/2$^-$ states of 7-neutron drops, all in an external well, are computed accurately with variational and Green's function Monte Carlo methods for a Hamiltonian containing the Argonne $v_{18}$ two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon potentials. These states are also calculated using Skyrme-type energy-density functionals. Commonly used functionals overestimate the central density of these drops and the spin-orbit splitting of 7-neutron drops. Improvements in the functionals are suggested.

# Neutron Drops and Skyrme Energy-Density Functionals

## Key claim

Commonly used Skyrme-type energy-density functionals overestimate both the central density of 8-neutron drops and the spin-orbit splitting in 7-neutron drops, but the authors suggest that adding a density-gradient term and separating two- and three-body contributions to the spin-orbit potential can resolve these discrepancies, though the gradient term alone cannot simultaneously fit the binding energy and density profile.

## Method

Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) and Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) methods were used to compute the ground state of 8-neutron drops (Jπ=0+) and the lowest 1/2− and 3/2− states of 7-neutron drops, all confined in a Woods-Saxon external well (V₀ = −20 MeV, R = 3 fm, a = 0.65 fm). The Hamiltonian included the Argonne v₁₈ two-nucleon potential (approximated by a charge-symmetric v′₈ version with perturbative corrections) and the Urbana IX three-nucleon potential. The same states were then calculated using four Skyrme-type energy-density functionals (Skyrme 1′, SkM, FPS, and FPS21) and compared to the GFMC benchmarks.

## Key numbers

- GFMC ground-state energy of ⁸n(0⁺): −37.6(3) MeV
- GFMC energies of ⁷n(1/2⁻) and ⁷n(3/2⁻): −32.3(2) MeV and −31.2(2) MeV, respectively
- Spin-orbit splitting in ⁷n from GFMC: 1.1 ± 0.3 MeV; from CVMC: 1.4 ± 0.1 MeV
- Single-neutron 1s state in the external well alone: bound at −5.73 MeV
- CVMC energies with improved wave function Ψ′_V: −35.6(1) MeV for ⁸n(0⁺), −31.2(1) MeV for ⁷n(1/2⁻), −29.7(1) MeV for ⁷n(3/2⁻)
- Unmodified FPS21 spin-orbit splitting in ⁷n: 3 MeV; modified FPS21 with gradient term: 2.2 MeV
- Proposed two-body spin-orbit parameter: W⁽²⁾_ℓs = 61 MeV·fm⁵; three-body parameter: W⁽³⁾_ℓs = 745 MeV·fm⁸

## Context

This work extends ab initio calculations of light nuclei (Pudliner 1995, for A ≤ 6) and cluster variational Monte Carlo studies of ¹⁶O (Pieper 1992) and spin-orbit splitting in ¹⁵N (Pieper 1993) to pure neutron systems. It provides the first exact microscopic benchmarks for neutron drops, which can constrain Skyrme energy-density functionals used in neutron-star crust and neutron-rich nuclei studies (e.g., the FPS and SkM models). The results enable future CVMC calculations of larger neutron drops to build a database for fitting improved Skyrme functionals that separate two- and three-body spin-orbit contributions, as suggested by the finding that three-nucleon forces contribute significantly to the spin-orbit splitting in ¹⁵N but negligibly in ⁷n.
