# Light quark masses, chiral condensate and quark-gluon condensate in quenched lattice QCD with exact chiral symmetry

> We determine several quantities in quenched lattice QCD with exact chiral symmetry. For 100 gauge configurations generated with Wilson gauge action at $ β= 6.0 $ on the $ 16^3 \times 32 $ lattice, we compute quenched quark propagators for 13 bare quark masses. The pion decay constant is extracted from the pion propagator, and from which the inverse lattice spacing is determined to be $ a^{-1} = 1.979(6) $ GeV. The parameters ($ C, δ, B $) in the pseudoscalar meson mass formula in quenched chiral perturbation theory (q$χ$PT) to one-loop order are determined. Further, we measure the index (topological) susceptibility of these 100 gauge configurations, $ χ_t = (175 \pm 6 {MeV})^4 $, from which we obtain an estimate of the mass of $ η' $ in q$χ$PT, and the coefficient of quenched chiral logarithm, both in good agreement with the values determined from the pion masses, as well as with the theoretical estimates. With our values of $ C, δ, B $, the experimental inputs of pion and kaon masses, and the pion decay constant, we determine the light quark masses: $ m_{u,d} = 4.1 \pm 0.3 $ MeV, and $ m_s = 92 \pm 9 $ MeV, in the $ \bar{MS} $ scheme at scale $ μ= 2 $ GeV. Also, we determine the quark condensate $<\bar q q > = -(250 \pm 3 {MeV})^3 $, and the quark-gluon condensate $ g < \bar q σ_{μν} F_{μν} q > = -(434 \pm 4 {MeV})^5 $, in the $ \bar{MS} $ scheme at scale 2 GeV.

## Key claim

The paper determines light quark masses, the chiral condensate, and the quark-gluon condensate in quenched lattice QCD using a formulation that preserves exact chiral symmetry, but the results are obtained in the quenched approximation (no dynamical quarks) and rely on one-loop perturbative renormalization to convert lattice results to the MSbar scheme at 2 GeV.

## Method

The authors generated quenched quark propagators for 13 bare quark masses on 100 gauge configurations with Wilson gauge action at beta=6.0 on a 16^3 x 32 lattice, using the overlap Dirac operator implemented via the optimal domain-wall fermion formulation with 16th-order Zolotarev rational approximation. They extracted the pion mass and decay constant from pion propagator fits, determined the parameters of quenched chiral perturbation theory (qchiPT) including the quenched chiral logarithm coefficient delta, and measured the topological susceptibility via the spectral flow method. Using these lattice-determined qchiPT parameters together with experimental pion and kaon masses and the pion decay constant, they computed the light quark masses and condensates.

## Key numbers

- Inverse lattice spacing: a^{-1} = 1.979(6) GeV, corresponding to a = 0.0997(3) fm
- Quenched chiral logarithm coefficient from pion mass fit: delta = 0.164(13)
- Topological susceptibility: chi_t = (175 +/- 6 MeV)^4
- eta' mass in qchiPT from topological susceptibility: m_eta' = 813 +/- 51 MeV
- Light quark masses in MSbar at 2 GeV: m_{u,d} = 4.1 +/- 0.3 MeV (statistical) +/- 1.0 MeV (systematic)
- Strange quark mass in MSbar at 2 GeV: m_s = 92 +/- 9 MeV (statistical) +/- 16 MeV (systematic)
- Quark condensate in MSbar at 2 GeV: <\bar q q> = -(250 +/- 3 MeV)^3
- Quark-gluon condensate in MSbar at 2 GeV: g <\bar q sigma_{munu} F_{munu} q> = -(434 +/- 4 MeV)^5

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by the same group (Chiu and Hsieh, 2002) on smaller 8^3 x 24 lattices at beta=5.8 to a larger volume and smaller lattice spacing, confirming the consistency of the quenched chiral logarithm coefficient delta from both pion mass fits and topological susceptibility measurements. The results enable comparison with the current lattice world average for light quark masses as reviewed by Wittig (2002) and with the strange quark mass determination of Giusti et al. (2001). The paper also provides a benchmark for future unquenched (dynamical fermion) calculations that would properly address chiral symmetry breaking without the quenched approximation.
