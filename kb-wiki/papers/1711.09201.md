# Parameter free predictions within the proxy-SU(3) model

> Using a new approximate analytic parameter-free proxy-SU(3) scheme, we make predictions of shape observables for deformed nuclei, namely beta and gamma deformation variables, and compare them with empirical data and with predictions by relativistic and non-relativistic mean-field theories. Furthermore, analytic expressions are derived for B(E2) ratios within the proxy-SU(3) model, free of any free parameters, and/or scaling factors. The predicted B(E2) ratios are in good agreement with the experimental data for deformed rare earth nuclides.

# Parameter free predictions within the proxy-SU(3) model

## Key claim
The proxy-SU(3) model provides parameter-free predictions for nuclear shape observables (beta and gamma deformation variables) and B(E2) ratios that agree well with experimental data for deformed rare earth nuclei, but the model is only valid for well-deformed nuclei and shows failures at the beginning and end of the deformed region where nuclei are not sufficiently deformed.

## Method
The authors connected the SU(3) irreducible representation quantum numbers (lambda, mu) to the collective model deformation variables beta and gamma using established formulas, then computed beta and gamma for rare earth nuclei using only proton number Z and neutron number N as input. For B(E2) ratios within ground state bands and gamma-1 bands, they derived analytic expressions using SU(3) to SO(3) coupling coefficients, with all other factors canceling in the ratio, and set the effective charge for neutrons to zero.

## Key numbers
- Beta deformation predictions from proxy-SU(3) lie within the error bars of the D1S Gogny mean ground state deformation for most nuclei, with exceptions at N=84 in Gd-Hf, at N=120 and 122 in Gd and Dy, and at N=122 in Er
- Gamma deformation predictions from proxy-SU(3) show exceptions at the first three points (N=84, 86, 88) in Gd-Yb and at the last three points (N=118, 120, 122) in W-Pt
- The gamma deformation at the HFB energy minimum jumps suddenly to values close to 60 degrees near the end of the shell (N=116-122)
- B(E2) ratios were calculated for the proxy-SU(3) irreps (54,12), (52,14), and (50,10), accommodating nuclei including 168Er, 160Gd, 162Dy, 166Er, 172Er, and 156Gd
- Agreement between proxy-SU(3) predictions and experimental B(E2) data is excellent for 156Gd, 162Dy, and 166Er, while in 168Er three points are missed

## Context
This work extends the proxy-SU(3) model introduced in earlier papers (Martinou et al., 2015; Bonatsos et al., 2017) by providing detailed comparisons to D1S Gogny force calculations tabulated by Delaroche et al. (2010) and to relativistic mean-field theory predictions by Lalazissis et al. The parameter-free B(E2) ratio predictions improve upon earlier approaches that required effective charges or scaling factors, and the results enable future work on spectra of heavy deformed nuclei using three- and four-body terms to break degeneracy between ground state and gamma-1 bands, as well as B(M1) transition rate calculations using techniques from the pseudo-SU(3) scheme.
