# The $J/ψDD^*$ vertex

> We employ QCD sum rules to calculate the $J/ψDD^*$ form factors and coupling constant by studying the three-point $J/ψD^*D$ correlation function. We find that the momentum dependence of the form factor depends on the off-shell meson. We get a value for the coupling which is in agreement with estimates based on constituent quark model.

# The J/psi DD* vertex

## Key claim

Using QCD sum rules to compute the J/psi DD* form factors and coupling constant, the authors find that the momentum dependence of the form factor depends on which meson is taken off-shell, and they obtain a coupling value consistent with constituent quark model estimates but roughly half the value from vector meson dominance plus relativistic potential model calculations.

## Method

The authors evaluate the three-point J/psi D* D correlation function using QCD sum rules, performing a double Borel transformation to improve the matching between the phenomenological and OPE sides. They compute the form factor separately for each choice of off-shell meson (D, D*, or J/psi), fitting the QCD sum rule results with either a monopole form (for off-shell J/psi) or a Gaussian form (for off-shell D and D*), and then extrapolate to the off-shell meson pole to extract the coupling constant.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant: g_{psi DD*} = (3.48 +/- 0.76) GeV^{-1}
- For comparison: ref. OSL gives 8.02 +/- 0.62 GeV^{-1}; ref. DEA gives 4.05 +/- 0.25 GeV^{-1}
- Input parameters: m_c = 1.3 GeV, m_D = 1.87 GeV, m_{D*} = 2.01 GeV, m_psi = 3.1 GeV
- Decay constants: f_D = (170 +/- 10) MeV, f_{D*} = (240 +/- 20) MeV, f_{J/psi} = (405 +/- 15) MeV
- Quark condensate: <qbar q> = -(0.23)^3 GeV^3
- Continuum thresholds: Delta_s = Delta_u = 0.5 GeV
- Borel stability window: 7 <= M^2 <= 11 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QCD sum rule studies of the D*Dpi vertex (Navarra et al., 2000, 2002), DDrho vertex (Matheus et al., 2002), and J/psi DD vertex (Bracco et al., 2001) to the J/psi D* D vertex, which is important for evaluating J/psi dissociation cross sections by pions and rho mesons in heavy-ion collisions. The result is compatible with the constituent quark meson model of Deandrea et al. (2003) but disagrees by about a factor of two with the vector meson dominance plus relativistic potential model of Oh et al. (2001), a difference that can lead to cross sections differing by more than an order of magnitude.
