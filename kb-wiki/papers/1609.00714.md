# Analytical On-shell Calculation of Low Energy Higher Order Scattering

> We demonstrate that the use of analytical on-shell methods involving calculation of the discontinuity across the t-channel cut associated with the exchange of a pair of massless particles (photons or gravitons) can be used to evaluate one-loop contributions to electromagnetic and gravitational scattering, with and without polarizability, reproducing via simple algebraic manipulations, results obtained previously, generally using Feynman diagram techniques. In the gravitational case the use of factorization permits a straightforward and algebraic calculation of higher order scattering without consideration of ghost contributions or of triple-graviton couplings, which made previous evaluations considerably more arduous.

# Analytical On-shell Calculation of Low Energy Higher Order Scattering

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that analytical on-shell methods, based on calculating the discontinuity across the t-channel cut from massless particle exchange, can reproduce known one-loop electromagnetic and gravitational scattering amplitudes via simple algebraic manipulations, without needing to evaluate full Feynman diagrams. The method is claimed to be considerably simpler than conventional Feynman diagram techniques, particularly for gravitational scattering where it avoids ghost contributions and triple-graviton couplings.

## Method

The author uses unitarity to compute the discontinuity of the scattering amplitude across the t-channel cut associated with two-photon or two-graviton exchange, then reconstructs the full amplitude via dispersion relations. For electromagnetic scattering, the t-channel Compton helicity amplitudes for spinless particles are analytically continued to imaginary momentum, and the angular-averaged integrals over the intermediate two-particle phase space are performed. For gravitational scattering, the gravitational Compton amplitude is obtained from the electromagnetic Compton amplitude via factorization, eliminating the need to evaluate triple-graviton vertices or ghost diagrams.

## Key numbers

- Electromagnetic scattering of two charged spinless particles: effective potential $V_2^{em}(r) = -\frac{\alpha_{em}^2(m_A+m_B)}{2r^2} - \frac{7\alpha_{em}^2\hbar}{6\pi r^3}$, where $\alpha_{em}=e^2/4\pi$ is the fine structure constant
- Charged-neutral polarizable scattering: effective potential $V_2^{N-em}(r) = -\frac{\alpha_{em}\alpha_E^A}{2r^4} + \alpha_{em}\frac{(11\alpha_E^A+5\beta_M^A)\hbar}{4\pi m_B r^5}$, where $\alpha_E^A$ and $\beta_M^A$ are electric and magnetic dipole polarizabilities
- Two polarizable systems (Casimir-Polder form): $V_2^{NN-em}(r) = \frac{-23(\alpha_E^A\alpha_E^B+\beta_M^A\beta_M^B)+7(\alpha_E^A\beta_M^B+\alpha_E^B\beta_M^A)}{4\pi r^7}$
- Gravitational scattering of two masses: $V_2^{grav}(r) = -\frac{3G^2 m_A m_B (m_A+m_B)}{r^2} - \frac{41 G^2 m_A m_B \hbar}{10\pi r^3}$
- Gravitational scattering of a mass and a polarizable system (new result): $V_2^{N-grav}(r) = -\frac{3G m_B^2 \alpha_G^A}{32\pi}\left(\frac{1}{r^6} + \frac{163}{7}\frac{\hbar}{\pi m_B r^7}\right)$, where $\alpha_G^A$ is the quadrupole polarizability
- Two polarizable gravitational systems: $V_2^{NN-grav}(r) = -\frac{3987}{1024}\frac{\alpha_G^A\alpha_G^B}{\pi^3 r^{11}}$
- Massless particle scattering off a massive target: $V_2^{0-grav}(r) = \frac{15}{4}\frac{G^2 m_B^2 E}{r^2} - \frac{(15-3/5)}{4\pi}\frac{G m_B E \hbar}{r^3} - \frac{12 G^2 m_B E \hbar}{\pi r^3}\ln\frac{r}{r_0}$, where $E$ is the laboratory frame energy of the massless particle

## Context

This work extends the on-shell unitarity method previously applied to electromagnetic scattering by Feinberg and Sucher (1988, 1992) to the gravitational case, reproducing results obtained via Feynman diagram calculations by Donoghue (1994), Khriplovich and Kirilin (2003), and Bjerrum-Bohr et al. (2003). The method enables simpler algebraic calculation of higher-order gravitational scattering amplitudes without ghost contributions or triple-graviton couplings, and produces a new result for the interaction of a point mass with a polarizable gravitational system. The approach is complementary to the covariant on-shell methods used by Bjerrum-Bohr et al. (2014, 2016), which require expansion to obtain low-energy forms.
