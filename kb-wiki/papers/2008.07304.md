# Collective nuclear vibrations and initial state shape fluctuations in central Pb+Pb collisions: resolving the $v_2$ to $v_3$ puzzle

> We have studied, for the first time, the influence of the collective quantum effects in the nuclear wave functions on the azimuthal anisotropy coefficients $ε_{2,3}$ in the central Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC energies. With the help of the energy weighted sum rule we demonstrate that the classical treatment with the Woods-Saxon nuclear density overestimates the mean square quadrupole moment of the $^{208}$Pb nucleus by a factor of $\sim 2.2$. The Monte-Carlo Glauber simulation of the central Pb+Pb collisions accounting for the restriction on the quadrupole moment leads to $ε_2/ε_3\approx 0.8$ which allows to resolve the $v_2$-to-$v_3$ puzzle.

# Collective nuclear vibrations and initial state shape fluctuations in central Pb+Pb collisions: resolving the $v_2$ to $v_3$ puzzle

## Key claim

The classical Woods-Saxon nuclear density treatment overestimates the mean square quadrupole moment of $^{208}$Pb by a factor of approximately 2.2 compared to the quantum treatment using the energy weighted sum rule; when this quantum restriction is applied in Monte-Carlo Glauber simulations of central Pb+Pb collisions, the ratio $\epsilon_2/\epsilon_3 \approx 0.8$ resolves the previously puzzling discrepancy between hydrodynamic predictions and experimental data for $v_2/v_3$ in ultra-central collisions.

## Method

The author performed Monte-Carlo Glauber simulations of central ($b=0$) Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ and $5.02$ TeV, sampling nucleon positions both with the standard Woods-Saxon distribution and with a modified distribution that squeezes the squared quadrupole moment by the factor $r\approx 2.2$ to match the quantum mean square quadrupole moment obtained from the energy weighted sum rule for the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance. The initial entropy density in the transverse plane was computed using a two-component wounded nucleon and binary collision model with Gaussian smearing, and the rms eccentricity coefficients $\epsilon_2\{2\}$ and $\epsilon_3\{2\}$ were extracted from $5\times 10^5$ simulated events.

## Key numbers

- The classical Woods-Saxon treatment overestimates the mean square quadrupole moment of $^{208}$Pb by a factor of $r \approx 2.2$ relative to the quantum EWSR calculation using the ISGQR centroid energy $E_c \approx 11.9$ MeV.
- For the quantum (squeezed) version at $2.76$ TeV: $\epsilon_2\{2\} = 0.0946$ (with meson-baryon component) and $0.0983$ (without); $\epsilon_3\{2\} = 0.118$ (with) and $0.121$ (without); ratio $\epsilon_2\{2\}/\epsilon_3\{2\} = 0.802$ (with) and $0.812$ (without).
- For the quantum version at $5.02$ TeV: $\epsilon_2\{2\} = 0.0939$ (with meson-baryon) and $0.0977$ (without); $\epsilon_3\{2\} = 0.117$ (with) and $0.12$ (without); ratio $\epsilon_2\{2\}/\epsilon_3\{2\} = 0.802$ (with) and $0.814$ (without).
- For the standard Woods-Saxon (unsqueezed) version at $2.76$ TeV: $\epsilon_2\{2\} = 0.107$ (with) and $0.112$ (without); $\epsilon_3\{2\} = 0.118$ (with) and $0.121$ (without); ratio $0.907$ (with) and $0.926$ (without).
- Combining the quantum $\epsilon_2/\epsilon_3 \approx 0.8$ with hydrodynamic linear response coefficients $k_2/k_3 \approx 1.2-1.4$ yields $v_2/v_3 \approx 0.96-1.12$, compared to the ALICE experimental value $v_2/v_3 \approx 1.08 \pm 0.05$ in the limit $c \to 0$.

## Context

This work extends the standard Monte-Carlo Glauber approach (as implemented in GLISSANDO by Broniowski et al. and the wounded nucleon model by Kharzeev and Nardi) by incorporating quantum collective effects from nuclear vibrations, which had been previously ignored in heavy-ion initial condition modeling. The $v_2$-to-$v_3$ puzzle arose because hydrodynamic calculations (Niemi et al., Heinz et al., Luzum et al.) predicted $v_2/v_3 > 1$ while experiments at ALICE and CMS found $v_2$ close to $v_3$ in ultra-central Pb+Pb collisions; earlier attempts to resolve this by modifying viscosity coefficients (Gale et al.), the equation of state, or initial conditions had not succeeded. The present result enables more accurate event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations and may be important for interpreting collisions of deformed nuclei such as $^{197}$Au and $^{238}$U at RHIC and future NICA experiments, as well as for event shape engineering techniques.
