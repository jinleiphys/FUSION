# Log-Periodic Oscillations in the Photo Response of Efimov Trimers

> The photoassociation of Efimov trimer, composed of three identical bosons, is studied utilizing the multipole expansion. For identical particles the leading contribution comes from the r^2 s-mode operator and from the quadrupole d-mode operator. Log-periodic oscillations are found in the photoassociation response function, both near the energy threshold for the leading s-wave reaction, and in the high frequency tail for all partial waves.

## Key claim

Log-periodic oscillations appear in the photoassociation response function of Efimov trimers composed of three identical bosons, both near the energy threshold for the leading s-wave reaction and in the high frequency tail for all partial waves, with the s-wave oscillations having a normalized amplitude of approximately 8.475%.

## Method

The authors used a multipole expansion of the photon-atom interaction Hamiltonian, keeping terms up to order \(k^5\) where \(k\) is the photon wavenumber, and applied the zero-range approximation in the hyperspherical adiabatic framework to describe three identical bosons at the unitary point (infinite two-body scattering length). They solved the hyperangular equation for \(L=0\) and \(L=2\) partial waves to obtain the eigenvalues \(\nu_0 \approx 1.00624i\) and \(\nu_2 \approx 2.82334\), then evaluated the hyperradial integrals for the \(r^2\) (s-mode) and quadrupole (d-mode) transition matrix elements connecting bound Efimov trimers to continuum scattering states.

## Key numbers

- The imaginary eigenvalue for the \(L=0\) Efimov trimer at unitarity is \(\nu_0 \approx 1.00624i\), leading to a discrete Efimov spectrum with ratio \(\epsilon_n/\epsilon_0 = e^{-2\pi n/|\nu_0|} \approx 515^{-n}\).
- The lowest non-trivial eigenvalue for \(L=2\) at unitarity is \(\nu_2 \approx 2.82334\).
- The normalized amplitude of the log-periodic oscillations in the s-wave matrix element near threshold is \(B_3 \approx 8.475\%\).
- The s-wave association is peaked at relative momentum \(q = \kappa/2\) and the d-wave association is peaked at \(q = \kappa\), where \(\kappa = \sqrt{-\epsilon}\) for the bound state and \(q = \sqrt{\epsilon}\) for the continuum state.

## Context

This work extends the multipole analysis of radio-frequency association previously applied to dimers and bosonic trimers by Bazak, Liverts, and Barnea (2012) and Liverts, Bazak, and Barnea (2012, 2013), providing analytic results at the unitary point using zero-range potentials. The predicted log-periodic oscillations offer a new fingerprint of Efimov physics that could be observed in photoassociation experiments with ultracold bosonic atoms such as \(^7\)Li, following the experimental demonstrations of Efimov trimers by Zaccanti et al. (2009), Pollack et al. (2009), and Gross et al. (2009, 2011).
