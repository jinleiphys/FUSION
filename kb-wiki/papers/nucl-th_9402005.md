# Can the $Σ^- nn$ System be Bound?

> Motivated by the $Σ$-hypernuclear states reported in ($K^-,π^{\pm}$) experiments, we have explored the possibility that there exists a particle-stable $Σ^- nn$ bound state. For the Jülich Ã hyperon-nucleon, realistic-force model, our calculations yield little reason to expect a positive-parity bound state in either the $J = \frac{1}{2}$ or the $J = \frac{3}{2}$ channels.

## Key claim

The authors find that the Jülich Ã hyperon-nucleon potential model, which is the most attractive realistic model available and therefore most likely to support a bound state, does not produce a particle-stable Σ⁻nn bound state in either the J=1/2 or J=3/2 positive-parity channels; a strength factor greater than 1.7 is required to achieve binding.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations in momentum space for the Σ⁻nn three-body system, using the Jülich Ã hyperon-nucleon interaction (an energy-independent one-boson-exchange model) for the ΣN forces and the Nijmegen one-boson-exchange potential for the nn interaction. Baryon-baryon interactions were included in all partial waves with j ≤ 1 and positive parity. The search for bound states was performed by varying the overall interaction strength until binding was achieved.

## Key numbers

- The Jülich Ã model gives a Σ⁺p spin-singlet scattering length aˢ = -2.26 fm and effective range rˢ₀ = 5.22 fm, and a spin-triplet scattering length aᵗ = -0.76 fm and effective range rᵗ₀ = 0.78 fm.
- The total interaction must be multiplied by a factor greater than 1.7 to produce binding in the J=1/2⁺ channel.
- No bound state was found for either J=1/2⁺ or J=3/2⁺.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis by Dover and Gal (1982), who argued that T=2 ΣNN states like Σ⁻nn are not expected to be bound due to the strong spin-isospin dependence of the ΣN interaction, and follows the rekindled interest from Hayano et al. (1989, 1991) who observed narrow structure in ⁴He(K⁻,π⁻) reactions interpreted as a bound ⁴_ΣHe hypernucleus. The results contradict the possibility of a bound Σ⁻nn state even with the most attractive realistic potential model, and suggest that experimental searches for such a state are unlikely to succeed; the authors note that Garcilazo (1987) had already argued on the basis of rank-one separable potentials that the system is unbound.
