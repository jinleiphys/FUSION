# Matching issue in quasi parton distribution approach

> In recent years, the quasi parton distribution has been introduced for extracting the parton distribution functions from lattice QCD simulations. The quasi and standard distribution share the same perturbative collinear singularity and the renormalized quasi distribution can be factorized into the standard distribution with a perturbative matching factor. The quasi parton distribution is known to have power-law UV divergences, which do not exist in the standard distribution. We discuss in this talk the nonperturbative renormalization scheme for the power divergence. We also demonstrate the perturbative matching of the quasi quark distribution between continuum and lattice at the one-loop.

## Key claim

The quasi parton distribution (quasi-PDF) approach can be used to extract standard parton distribution functions (PDFs) from lattice QCD, but the power-law ultraviolet divergences inherent in quasi-PDFs must be subtracted nonperturbatively; the authors propose a subtraction scheme using the static quark potential and demonstrate one-loop perturbative matching between continuum and lattice calculations, though they note that other nonperturbative renormalization schemes like RI/MOM might be preferable for better accuracy.

## Method

The authors propose a nonperturbative renormalization scheme for the quasi-quark distribution operator by subtracting the linear power divergence using the static quark potential \(V(R)\) obtained from Wilson loop measurements on the lattice. They then perform a one-loop perturbative calculation of the matching factor between continuum and lattice for the power-divergence-subtracted non-local operator, using naive fermions for the lattice side and introducing link smearings (HYP1 and HYP2) for the Wilson line operator, with mean-field improvement applied to improve convergence in the coupling expansion.

## Key numbers

- The static potential one-loop expression is \(V(R) = -g^2 C_F \frac{1}{4\pi R} + g^2 C_F \int_{k_{\perp 0}} \frac{1}{k_{\perp 0}^2} + \mathcal{O}(g^4)\), where \(C_F = 4/3\).
- For \(\delta z = 0\), the one-loop continuum integrals yield \(I_{\text{vertex}}(\delta z=0) = 2\ln(\mu/\lambda)\), \(I_{\text{sail}}(\delta z=0) = 0\), and \(I_{\text{tadpole}}^{\text{subt}}(\delta z=0) = 0\), where \(\mu\) is the two-dimensional UV cutoff scale and \(\lambda\) is the IR regulator.
- The one-loop matching coefficients for the unsmeared case show a flat \(\delta z\) dependence at large \(\delta z\) after linear divergence subtraction, with the total coefficient being small for the HYP1 and HYP2 smeared cases compared to the unsmeared case.

## Context

This work extends the quasi-PDF approach introduced by Ji (2013) and the large momentum effective theory by Ji (2014), as well as the QCD collinear factorization approach to the relation between normal and renormalized quasi-PDFs by Ma (2014). The paper addresses two major uncertainties in lattice calculations of quasi-PDFs that were noted in earlier simulations by Chen (2016) and Alexandrou (2016): the existence of power divergences and the matching between continuum and lattice. The proposed nonperturbative subtraction scheme using the static quark potential, building on work by Musch (2010), and the one-loop matching calculation provide a foundation for future lattice QCD simulations to extract PDFs with better control over systematic uncertainties, though the authors note that other renormalization techniques like RI/MOM may offer better accuracy.
