# Necessary conditions for causality from linearized stability at ultra-high boosts

> In this work, we provide a novel method to constrain the causal parameter space of a relativistic hydrodynamic system exclusively from its linear stability analysis at non-zero momenta. Our approach exploits the Lorentz-invariant stability property of causal theories. In boosted frames, the dispersion relation exhibits a feature that we call ``$γ$-suppression,'' whereby the higher-order terms in the wavenumber expansion are increasingly suppressed beyond leading order at large boosts. As a consequence, at near-luminal values of Lorentz boost, stability criteria at the spatially homogeneous limit are sufficient to identify the region of the parameter space that satisfies the necessary conditions of causality, even at non-zero momenta. After presenting the general hydrodynamic framework, we test the method in conformal Müller-Israel-Stewart theory and show that it provides an efficient way of deriving the necessary conditions of causality while remaining within the low-energy regime of hydrodynamic validity.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the necessary conditions for causality in a relativistic hydrodynamic theory can be derived solely from linear stability analysis at the spatially homogeneous limit (k→0), provided one considers ultra-high Lorentz boosts (v→1), because at such boosts the dispersion relation exhibits "γ-suppression" that makes higher-order wavenumber terms negligible.

## Method

The authors perform a Lorentz transformation of the dispersion relation from the local rest frame to a boosted frame, showing that the boosted mode frequency ω can be expressed as a power series in k/γ rather than in k. They then apply the frame-invariant stability condition Im(ω) ≤ |Im(k)| to the conformal Müller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) theory, analyzing both shear and sound channels at various boost velocities v from 0 to 0.999, for both k=0 and non-zero complex k values up to O(k⁶).

## Key numbers

- The analysis uses boost velocities v = 0, v = 0.8, and v = 0.999 for the k=0 case, and v = 0, v = 0.76, and v = 0.999 for the non-zero k case in the shear channel
- For the sound channel at k=0, boost velocities v = 0, v = 0.8, and v = 0.999 are used
- The non-zero wavenumber used for the shear channel analysis is k = 0.394 + 0.1 i
- The dispersion series is truncated at O(k⁶) for the non-zero k analysis
- The sound speed in the conformal MIS theory is 1/√3, appearing in the leading-order hydrodynamic sound mode coefficient

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous approach in Roy (2023) where the causal parameter space was derived at the spatially homogeneous limit (k→0) using stability invariance. It builds on the framework established by Gavassino (2021, 2023) showing that causality is a necessary condition for covariant stability, and by Hoult (2023) and Heller (2022) on the connection between non-hydrodynamic modes and causality. The method provides an alternative to the traditional asymptotic causality condition (group velocity at infinite wavenumber) which operates outside the domain of validity of hydrodynamics as a low-energy effective theory.
