# Exclusive pion-induced Drell-Yan process at J-PARC for accessing the nucleon GPDs and soft nonfactorizable mechanism

> Generalized parton distributions (GPDs) encoding multidimensional information of hadron partonic structure appear as the building blocks in a factorized description of hard exclusive reactions. The nucleon GPDs have been accessed by deeply virtual Compton scattering and deeply virtual meson production with lepton beam. A complementary probe with hadron beam is shown to be the exclusive pion-induced Drell-Yan process, $π^- p \to μ^+ μ^- n$, as demonstrated by recent theoretical advances on describing this process in terms of QCD factorization as the partonic subprocess convoluted with the nucleon GPDs and the pion distribution amplitudes, and by the feasibility study for its measurement via a spectrometer at the High Momentum Beamline being constructed at J-PARC in Japan. We also discuss the possible soft partonic mechanisms beyond the QCD factorization framework, and present an estimate of the soft mechanisms at J-PARC kinematics, making use of dispersion relations and quark-hadron duality. Realization of the measurement of the exclusive pion-induced Drell-Yan process at J-PARC will provide a new test of QCD descriptions of a novel class of hard exclusive reactions, and also offer the possibility of experimentally accessing nucleon GPDs at large timelike virtuality.

# Exclusive pion-induced Drell-Yan process at J-PARC for accessing the nucleon GPDs and soft nonfactorizable mechanism

## Key claim

The exclusive pion-induced Drell-Yan process π⁻ p → μ⁺ μ⁻ n at J-PARC can access nucleon generalized parton distributions (GPDs) at large timelike virtuality, but the soft nonfactorizable mechanism beyond QCD factorization may dominate the cross section at J-PARC kinematics, giving a cross section larger by a factor of about 5 than the leading-order QCD factorization prediction.

## Method

The author derives the QCD factorization formula at leading order for the exclusive Drell-Yan process, expressing the cross section as a convolution of the pion distribution amplitude, nucleon GPDs (specifically the pseudoscalar GPDs H̃ and Ẽ), and a hard partonic subprocess with gluon exchange. To estimate the soft nonfactorizable mechanism, the author uses light-cone QCD sum rules combined with dispersion relations and quark-hadron duality, treating the initial pion leg as off-shell and matching the operator product expansion for the off-forward virtual Compton amplitude to the pion pole residue. The cross sections are evaluated using the BMP2001 parameterization for the nucleon GPDs and compared with updated estimates using the GK2013 parameterization.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order QCD factorization cross section dσ_L/(dt dQ'²) for π⁻ p → γ* n at Q'² = 5 GeV² and τ = 0.2 is at the pb level.
- The updated cross section using the GK2013 GPD parameterization is enhanced compared to the BMP2001 result at the same kinematics (Q'² = 5 GeV², τ = 0.2).
- The soft nonfactorizable mechanism gives a cross section larger by a factor of about 5 than the QCD factorization result at Q'² = 5 GeV², |t| = 0.2 GeV², and τ = 0.2.
- The secondary pion beam at J-PARC has moderately high energies of about 15 to 20 GeV.
- The threshold parameter q_th² = 0.7 GeV² is used in the light-cone sum rule estimate.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical description of exclusive Drell-Yan processes initiated by Berger, Diehl, and Pire (2001), who first estimated the cross section using the leading-order QCD factorization formula. The author provides an updated estimate using the GK2013 GPD parameterization determined from HERMES data for π⁺ electroproduction, and performs a feasibility study for measurement with the E50 spectrometer at the J-PARC High Momentum Beamline. The soft nonfactorizable mechanism, analogous to the Feynman mechanism known to be important for the pion electromagnetic form factor as studied by Braun et al. (1999), is estimated here for the first time for exclusive Drell-Yan and found to be significant at J-PARC kinematics, enabling future experimental tests of the interplay between factorizable and nonfactorizable QCD mechanisms.
