# Low-lying S-wave and P-wave Dibaryons in a Nodal Structure Analysis

> The dibaryon states as six-quark clusters of exotic QCD states are investigated in this paper. With the inherent nodal surface structure analysis, the wave functions of the six-quark clusters (in another word, the dibaryons) are classified. The contribution of the hidden color channels are discussed. The quantum numbers of the low-lying dibaryon states are obtained. The States $[ΩΩ]_{(0,0^{+})}$, $[ΩΩ]_{(0,2^{-})}$, $[Ξ^{*}Ω]_{(1/2,0^{+})}$, $[Σ^{*}Σ^{*}]_{(0,4^{-})}$ and the hidden color channel states with the same quantum numbers are proposed to be the candidates of dibaryons, which may be observed in experiments.

# Low-lying S-wave and P-wave Dibaryons in a Nodal Structure Analysis

## Key claim
The authors propose that dibaryon states can be identified by analyzing the inherent nodal surface (INS) structure of six-quark clusters, and that only wave-function components which are nodelessly accessible to the regular octahedron and regular pentagon pyramid configurations are favorable for binding. The states $[\Omega\Omega]_{(0,0^{+})}$, $[\Omega\Omega]_{(0,2^{-})}$, $[\Xi^{*}\Omega]_{(1/2,0^{+})}$, $[\Sigma^{*}\Sigma^{*}]_{(0,4^{-})}$ and hidden-color channel states with the same quantum numbers are proposed as candidates for experimentally observable dibaryons, but the authors note that for strangeness values $-1$, $-2$, and $-3$, the coupling between symmetry basis and physics basis states is so complicated that it remains unclear whether stable dibaryons exist.

## Method
The authors extended Harvey's algebraic framework for classifying six-quark states to include strange quarks ($u$, $d$, $s$), analyzing the symmetry properties under the group chain SU$_{CFS}(18)\supset$SU$_C$(3)$\otimes$SU$_F$(3)$\otimes$SU$_S$(2). They applied the inherent nodal surface (INS) analysis method, previously developed for few-body electron and nucleon systems, to determine which orbital wave-function components with specific spatial permutation symmetries $[f]_O$ and angular momenta $L^\pi$ are nodelessly accessible to the two most symmetric spatial configurations (regular octahedron and regular pentagon pyramid). By combining the INS accessibility constraints with the requirement of color-singlet states ($[f]_C=[2\,2\,2]$) and the assumption that low-lying dibaryons are built from ground-state baryons with flavor-spin symmetry $[f]_{FS}=[3]$, they identified the quantum numbers (strangeness, isospin, spin, total angular momentum) of the favorable S-wave ($L^\pi=0^+$) and P-wave ($L^\pi=1^-$) states.

## Key numbers
- The $[\Omega\Omega]_{(0,0^{+})}$ dibaryon (strangeness $-6$) may have binding energy $E_b = 45$ to $115$ MeV, based on prior SU(3) chiral quark model and QDCSM calculations cited in the paper.
- The $\Xi^{*}\Omega$ dibaryon with $S=0$ (strangeness $-5$) may have binding energy $E_b = 80.0$ to $92.4$ MeV, based on prior chiral quark model calculations cited in the paper.
- The $\Xi\Omega$ dibaryon with $S=1$ (strangeness $-5$) may have binding energy $E_b = 26.2$ to $32.9$ MeV, based on prior chiral quark model calculations cited in the paper.
- Narrow dibaryon candidates with masses 2122 MeV, 2150 MeV, and $1956\pm6$ MeV have been proposed in prior pp scattering experiments, but the authors note that a more recent experiment (Brod02) does not support these results.
- The P-wave states $[\Omega\Omega]_{(0,2^{-})}$ and $[\Sigma^{*}\Sigma^{*}]_{(0,4^{-})}$ are identified as the lowest P-wave candidates, with possible $J^\pi$ values of $2^{-}$ and $4^{-}$ respectively.

## Context
This work extends the algebraic classification of six-quark states by Harvey (Harvey) to include strange quarks, and applies the inherent nodal surface analysis method developed by Bao and collaborators (Bao) for few-body systems to the dibaryon problem. The results are consistent with numerical calculations from the SU(3) chiral quark model (Zhang and Yu, 2000) and the quark-delocalization and color-screening model (Pang et al., 2002), which predicted binding energies for $\Omega\Omega$ and $\Xi^{*}\Omega$ dibaryons. The analysis provides a model-independent symmetry-based framework that can guide experimental searches for dibaryons in heavy ion collisions, particularly at RHIC energies where strangeness production is enhanced, and suggests that the long-sought H particle (strangeness $-2$) may be difficult to observe due to complicated mixing between symmetry basis states.
