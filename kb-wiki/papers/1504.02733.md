# Lepton-pair production in ultraperipheral collisions at AFTER@LHC

> We investigate the potentialities offered by the study of lepton-pair production in ultraperipheral collisions at a fixed-target experiment using the proton and ion LHC beams. In these collisions, exclusive or semi-exclusive lepton-pair production can be used as luminosity monitor as well as a check of the equivalent-photon approximation, via the measurement of the Bethe-Heitler cross section. It can also serve as a probe of the inner hadron structure via the measurement of the lepton-pair azimuthal asymmetry which is sensitive to the timelike virtual Compton scattering. We also briefly discuss the possibility offered by the study of eta(c) production. Finally, we outline the possibilities for lepton-pair production by Pomeron-Odderon fusion in exclusive pp and pA collisions.

## Key claim

The paper investigates the feasibility of studying lepton-pair production in ultraperipheral collisions at a fixed-target experiment using LHC beams (AFTER@LHC), claiming that such measurements can serve as a luminosity monitor, a check of the equivalent-photon approximation, and a probe of hadron structure via timelike Compton scattering, but only if the experimental conditions (rapidity coverage, beam energies, and target thickness) are sufficient to access the relevant kinematic regions.

## Method

The authors computed photon fluxes from protons and lead ions using the equivalent-photon approximation with impact-parameter-dependent flux formulas, integrating over impact parameters with minimum values set by the sum of the colliding hadron radii. They then calculated Bethe-Heitler dilepton production cross sections by convolving these fluxes with the Breit-Wheeler formula, and compared their results with the Starlight Monte Carlo code for consistency. The rapidity dependence of the produced dileptons was derived by transforming the photon flux as a function of the final-state particle rapidity, and the joint photon-photon luminosity was computed for double-photon fusion processes.

## Key numbers

* For pp collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=115 GeV, the total dimuon cross section is 15.0 nb (Breit-Wheeler) compared to 16.5 nb from Starlight; for pPb collisions, the cross section is 100 microbarn (scaled by Z^2).
* The differential cross section for dilepton production at Q=2 GeV is 0.14 nb/GeV for pp, 200 nb/GeV for pPb, 77 nb/GeV for Pbp, and 7000 nb/GeV for PbPb collisions.
* The eta_c production cross section via photon-photon fusion is 8.8 nb for pp at sqrt(s_NN)=115 GeV, yielding an expected 10^4 eta_c per year with the yearly luminosity of 2.0 x 10^4 pb^-1 yr^-1.
* For pPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=115 GeV, the eta_c cross section is 0.4 nb at y=0 in the hadron-hadron cms, yielding 1.8 x 10^6 eta_c per year.
* The maximum photon-nucleon cms energy for pp collisions at AFTER@LHC is 44 GeV when the proton is the photon emitter, and for pPb collisions it is 19 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of ultraperipheral collisions from collider-mode experiments at RHIC (STAR, PHENIX) and the LHC (ALICE) to the fixed-target configuration AFTER@LHC, where a 7 TeV proton or 2.76 TeV lead beam hits a stationary target. The authors note that earlier fixed-target attempts at the SPS with InIn collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=17 GeV were inconclusive due to limited photon-nucleon cms energies below 3 GeV. The paper enables future experimental searches for timelike Compton scattering and odderon-pomeron fusion processes, and provides a benchmark for comparing the Drees-Zeppenfeld photon flux (appropriate for ep collisions) with the impact-parameter-based flux for hadron-hadron collisions, particularly through eta_c and chi_c2 production measurements.
