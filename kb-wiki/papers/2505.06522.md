# Phenomenology of baryon dynamics with directed flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> This thesis aims to elucidate the role of initial baryon stopping and its diffusion in heavy-ion collisions (HIC) using hydrodynamic model. In this regard, we have studied the observable-directed flow ($v_1$) of identified hadrons, particularly the $v_1$ of baryons and antibaryons, as well as the splitting observed between them in detail. We propose a new ansatz for the initial baryon distribution. By employing this initial baryon deposition model alongside a tilted energy distribution as inputs to a hybrid framework, we successfully describe the rapidity-odd $v_1$ of identified hadrons, including the elusive baryon-antibaryon splitting of $v_1$ across a wide range of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$. Our model, incorporating baryon stopping and it's subsequent diffusion within a relativistic hydrodynamic framework and employing a crossover equation of state derived from lattice QCD calculations, establishes a non-critical baryonic baseline. Moreover, we demonstrate that recent STAR measurements of the centrality and system-size dependence of $v_1$ splitting between oppositely charged hadrons-attributed to electromagnetic field effects-are significantly influenced by background contributions from baryon stopping and its diffusion. Furthermore, we show that the rapidity dependence of the splitting of the rapidity-even component of $v_1$ between $p$ and $\bar{p}$ is highly sensitive to the initial baryon deposition scheme. If measured experimentally, this could constraint the rapidity dependence of the initial baryon deposition profile. Moreover, it could offer valuable phenomenological insights into the baryon junction picture and help refine constraints on the baryon diffusion coefficient of the medium. Notably, utilizing this phenomenologically successful baryon deposition model, we present the first estimation of the baryon diffusion coefficient for the strongly interacting QCD matter created in HIC.

# Phenomenology of baryon dynamics with directed flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The observed sign change in the centrality dependence of directed flow splitting between protons and antiprotons, previously attributed primarily to electromagnetic field effects, can be reproduced by a hydrodynamic model that includes only baryon stopping and baryon diffusion, without any electromagnetic field contributions. The model demonstrates that baryon inhomogeneity and diffusion produce a background signal that must be disentangled before electromagnetic field effects can be isolated.

## Method
The author employs a hybrid hydrodynamic framework with a tilted energy distribution and a new two-component initial baryon deposition model, using a crossover equation of state (NEoS-BQS) derived from lattice QCD. Hydrodynamic simulations are performed for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 27 GeV and 200 GeV, with and without baryon diffusion (controlled by parameter C_B), and the resulting directed flow v_1 of identified hadrons is computed using the Cooper-Frye particlization routine including resonance decays.

## Key numbers
- Magnetic field magnitude: approximately 10^18 Gauss in mid-central collision events, estimated via Biot-Savart law
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 27 GeV, hydrodynamic simulations use initial time tau_0 = 1.2 fm, energy density epsilon_0 = 2.4 GeV/fm^3, with baryon tilt parameter omega = 0.17 (C_B=0 case) or omega = 0.11 (C_B=1 case)
- The model with baryon diffusion (C_B=1) reproduces the sign change in Delta(dv_1/dy) for p-pbar from positive in central collisions to negative in peripheral collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 27 GeV
- For the system size study at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, simulations are performed for Cu+Cu, Ru+Ru, and Au+Au collisions, with the energy tilt parameter eta_m adjusted independently for each system

## Context
This work extends the author's previous studies (Parida 2022) on baryon stopping effects in directed flow and builds on the baryon deposition model developed in earlier chapters. It directly addresses the STAR collaboration measurements (STAR 2023) of charge-dependent directed flow splitting, which had been interpreted as electromagnetic field signatures. The results contradict the interpretation that the centrality dependence of Delta v_1 is solely due to electromagnetic fields, as argued by Gursoy (2014, 2018) and others. The work also engages with the STAR collaboration's system-size dependence measurements presented at SQM 2024 (Taseer 2024). The model enables future extraction of the baryon diffusion coefficient for strongly interacting QCD matter and motivates experimental measurement of Lambda-AntiLambda directed flow splitting to further constrain baryon stopping dynamics.
