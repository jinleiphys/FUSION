# Hadronic and Electromagnetic Interactions of Quarkonia

> We examine the hadronic interactions of quarkonia, focusing on the decays psi(2s)->psi pi pi and upsilon(2s)-> upsilon pi pi. The leading gluonic operators in the multipole expansion are matched onto the chiral lagrangian with the coefficients fit to available data, both at tree-level and loop-level in the chiral expansion. A comparison is made with naive expectations loosely based on the large-$N_c$ limit of QCD in an effort to determine the reliability of this limit for other observables, such as the binding of $\ps$ to nuclei. Crossing symmetry is used to estimate the cross-section for inelastic $π\ps\toπ\pss$ scattering, potentially relevant for heavy ion collisions. The radiative decays psi(2s)->psi pi pi gamma and upsilon(2s)-> upsilon pi pi gamma are determined at tree-level in the chiral lagrangian. Measurement of such decays will provide a test of the multipole and chiral expansions. We briefly discuss decays from the upsilon(3s) and also the contribution from pions to the electromagnetic polarizability of quarkonia.

## Key claim

The multipole expansion and chiral perturbation theory can be systematically combined to describe hadronic and electromagnetic decays of quarkonia, but the reliability of the large-\(N_c\) limit for estimating the coefficients \(C_E\) and \(C_B\) is uncertain, especially for off-diagonal transitions like \(\psi(2S)\to J/\psi\,\pi\pi\) where the energy release is comparable to the gap between color-singlet and color-octet states.

## Method

The leading gluonic operators in the multipole expansion (chromo-electric \({\cal O}_E\) and chromo-magnetic \({\cal O}_B\)) were matched onto the chiral Lagrangian for pions. The coefficients \(C_E\) and \(C_B\) for the \(\psi(2S)\to J/\psi\) and \(\Upsilon(2S)\to\Upsilon(1S)\) transitions were fit to the measured differential decay spectra and partial widths of \(\psi(2S)\to J/\psi\,\pi^+\pi^-\) and \(\Upsilon(2S)\to\Upsilon(1S)\,\pi^+\pi^-\), both at tree-level and at one-loop level in the chiral expansion. Crossing symmetry was then used to estimate the inelastic \(\pi J/\psi\to\pi\psi(2S)\) cross section, and the radiative decays \(\psi(2S)\to J/\psi\,\pi^+\pi^-\gamma\) and \(\Upsilon(2S)\to\Upsilon(1S)\,\pi^+\pi^-\gamma\) were computed at tree-level.

## Key numbers

- Partial width \(\Gamma(\psi(2S)\to J/\psi\,\pi^+\pi^-) = 90\pm 10\ {\rm keV}\) (from PDG evaluation).
- Partial width \(\Gamma(\Upsilon(2S)\to\Upsilon(1S)\,\pi^+\pi^-) = 8.1\pm 1.3\ {\rm keV}\) (from PDG evaluation).
- Tree-level fit for charmonium: \(C_E^{(\psi)} = 10.5\pm 0.6\ {\rm GeV}^{-3}\), \(C_B^{(\psi)} = 3.5\pm 0.5\ {\rm GeV}^{-3}\).
- Tree-level fit for bottomonium: \(C_E^{(\Upsilon)} = 2.6\pm 0.4\ {\rm GeV}^{-3}\), \(C_B^{(\Upsilon)} = 1.3\pm 0.3\ {\rm GeV}^{-3}\).
- One-loop fit (with \(w_4=0\)): \(C_E^{(\psi)} = 7.8\pm 0.4\ {\rm GeV}^{-3}\), \(C_B^{(\psi)} = 1.3\pm 0.3\ {\rm GeV}^{-3}\); \(C_E^{(\Upsilon)} = 2.0\pm 0.2\ {\rm GeV}^{-3}\), \(C_B^{(\Upsilon)} = 0.75\pm 0.14\ {\rm GeV}^{-3}\); counterterm \(w_2(\Lambda_\chi) = -6.9\pm 0.4\).
- Large-\(N_c\) estimate for diagonal charmonium: \(C_E^{[(1S),(1S)]\psi} \sim 4.7\ {\rm GeV}^{-3}\), \(C_E^{[(2S),(2S)]\psi} \sim 341\ {\rm GeV}^{-3}\).
- Large-\(N_c\) estimate for diagonal bottomonium: \(C_E^{[(1S),(1S)]\Upsilon} \sim 0.48\ {\rm GeV}^{-3}\), \(C_E^{[(2S),(2S)]\Upsilon} \sim 35\ {\rm GeV}^{-3}\).
- Inelastic \(\pi J/\psi\to\pi\psi(2S)\) cross section near threshold is of order \(10^{-2}\ {\rm mb}\).
- Maximum photon energy in \(\psi(2S)\to J/\psi\,\pi^+\pi^-\gamma\) is \(E_\gamma^{\rm max.} = 309\ {\rm MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral Lagrangian approach to quarkonia-pion interactions previously developed by Guralnik and Rothstein (1992) and Moxhay and Uy (1992), and compares the extracted coefficients with large-\(N_c\) estimates from Peskin (1979) and Bhanot and Peskin (1979). The results enable estimates of inelastic \(\pi J/\psi\) scattering as a background to \(J/\psi\) suppression in heavy-ion collisions, and propose radiative decays \(\psi(2S)\to J/\psi\,\pi^+\pi^-\gamma\) and \(\Upsilon(2S)\to\Upsilon(1S)\,\pi^+\pi^-\gamma\) as tests of the combined multipole and chiral expansions. The extracted coefficients also inform predictions for the binding of \(J/\psi\) to nuclei, previously estimated by Luke, Manohar, and Savage (1992).
