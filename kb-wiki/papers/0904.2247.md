# Angular bremsstrahlung during $α$-decay, emission from the internal region and unified formula of the bremsstrahlung probability

> Model of angular bremsstrahlung of photons emitted during $α$-decay is presented. A special emphasis is given on development of unified formalism of matrix elements in the dipole and multipolar approaches. A probability of the emission of photons calculated on the basis of the multipole model without any normalization on experimental data (i. e. in absolute scale) is found at $90^{\circ}$ of the angle $\vartheta_{αγ}$ between directions of motion of the $α$-particle (with its tunneling under barrier) and emission of photons to be in a good agreement with the newest experimental data for the $^{210}{\rm Po}$, $^{214}{\rm Po}$, and $^{226}{\rm Ra}$ nuclei. The spectrum for $^{244}{\rm Cm}$ is found at $\vartheta_{αγ} = 25^{\circ}$ to be in satisfactory agreement with high limit of errors of experimental data of Japanese group. A comparative analysis for the spectra calculated for $^{210}{\rm Po}$ by the multipole and dipole approaches in the absolute scale and with normalization on experimental data is performed. The emission of photons from the internal well in the dipole approach is found to be not small while the multipolar approach does not show such a strong dependence. Distribution of the bremsstrahlung probability on the numbers of protons and nucleons of the $α$-decaying nucleus in selected region close to $^{210}{\rm Po}$ is obtained. An unified formula of the bremsstrahlung probability during the $α$-decay of arbitrary nucleus expressed directly through the $Q_α$-value and numbers $A_{p}$, $Z_{p}$ of nucleons and protons of this nucleus is proposed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a fully quantum multipole model of angular bremsstrahlung during alpha-decay that can calculate photon emission probabilities in absolute scale (without normalization to experimental data) and achieves good agreement with data for several nuclei, while also showing that emission from the internal nuclear region before the barrier is not negligible in the dipole approach but does not show such strong dependence in the multipolar approach.

## Method

The author develops a fully quantum formalism for the bremsstrahlung matrix element using multipole expansion of the photon vector potential into electric and magnetic multipoles, with the alpha-decay treated in a spherically symmetric approximation. Wave functions of the decaying system are separated into radial and angular components, and the matrix elements are calculated using gradient formulas and angular integrals over vector spherical harmonics, with the angle between the alpha-particle direction and photon emission direction explicitly included. The model uses realistic alpha-nucleus potentials and calculates bremsstrahlung probabilities without any normalization to experimental data, then compares these absolute-scale predictions with measured spectra for several nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For the nuclei 210Po, 214Po, and 226Ra, the multipole model at angle theta_alphagamma = 90 degrees shows good agreement with experimental data without normalization
- For 244Cm at theta_alphagamma = 25 degrees, the calculated spectrum shows satisfactory agreement with the high limit of errors of experimental data from the Japanese group (Kasagi, 1997)
- Agreement between theory and experiment for 214Po and 226Ra is achieved up to 765 keV
- The unified formula covers alpha-active nuclei from 106Te up to nuclei with A_p = 266 and Z_p = 109, for photon energies from 50 keV up to 900 keV

## Context

This work extends the fully quantum multipole approach to bremsstrahlung during alpha-decay that was initiated by Tkalya (1999) and further developed by Maydanyuk (2003), providing a more accurate spatial description than the dipole approximation used by Papenbrock and Bertsch (1998). The model enables prediction of bremsstrahlung spectra for arbitrary alpha-decaying nuclei without experimental normalization, and the proposed unified formula expressed through the Q_alpha-value and nucleon/proton numbers allows estimation of bremsstrahlung probability across a wide range of nuclei. The angular formalism developed here addresses the question raised in discussions between Eremin (2000) and Kasagi (2000) about whether angular analysis of bremsstrahlung can provide new information about alpha-decay dynamics.
