# Statistical properties of fragment isospin in multifragmentation

> It is shown that the chemical equilibrium condition of the system can be unambiguously identified by analyzing the isospin evolution of fragments produced in nuclear multifragmentation process. As far as the chemical equilibrium is established, the isotope production can be used for finding properties of nuclear matter and fragments in the freeze-out volume, for example, via the isoscaling phenomenon.

## Key claim

Chemical equilibrium in nuclear multifragmentation can be unambiguously identified by analyzing the isospin evolution of fragments, but the paper shows that this identification requires studying the evolution of the N/Z ratio together with the fragment charge distribution, and that the effect of increasing N/Z of intermediate mass fragments (IMFs) within the energy range of the phase transition can only be explained by the "increasing" trend characteristic of chemical equilibrium.

## Method

The author applies the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM), which assumes statistical equilibrium of highly excited nuclear systems at a low-density freeze-out stage (density less than approximately one third of normal nuclear density). The model treats all breakup channels composed of nucleons and excited fragments, conserving mass, charge, momentum and energy, with fragment free energies parameterized as a sum of bulk, surface, Coulomb and symmetry energy contributions using standard liquid-drop parameters. Both grand-canonical and microcanonical Markov-chain versions of the SMM are used to calculate fragment isospin distributions and isoscaling behavior.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out density condition: \(\rho \lesssim \rho_0/3\), where \(\rho_0 \approx 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\) is normal nuclear density
- Standard SMM parameters: binding energy \(W_0 = 16\) MeV, surface coefficient \(B_0 = 18\) MeV, critical temperature \(T_c = 18\) MeV, symmetry energy coefficient \(\gamma = 25\) MeV, Coulomb parameter with \(r_0 = 1.17\) fm
- Multifragmentation threshold excitation energy: \(E_{th} \approx 3\) MeV/nucleon
- Excitation energy range studied for Au sources: \(E_x = 3\)–8 MeV/nucleon
- Isoscaling parameter \(\beta_{t3}\) from five light-particle-induced reactions on \(^{112}\)Sn and \(^{124}\)Sn targets decreases from 1.08 to 0.68 with increasing incident energy
- Isoscaling slope parameters from Markov-chain SMM calculations for hot fragments at \(E_x/A = 2, 5, 8\) MeV: \(\beta_{t3} = 1.74, 1.23, 1.09\); for cold fragments: \(\beta_{t3} = 1.33, 0.95, 0.84\)
- Extracted symmetry energy coefficient from isoscaling analysis: \(\gamma \simeq 22.5\) MeV
- Equation for chemical potential differences (Eq. 6) is accurate within 3% in grand-canonics for temperatures \(T \gtrsim 5\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) framework developed by Bondorf et al. (1995) and Botvina et al. (1985, 1987) to systematically analyze fragment isospin evolution and chemical equilibrium conditions. It directly addresses the isoscaling phenomenon reported by Tsang et al. (2001) and provides a theoretical foundation connecting isoscaling parameters to the symmetry energy coefficient. The paper contradicts mean-field approaches (Muller, year not specified) that predict separation into neutron-rich gas and isospin-symmetric liquid during the phase transition, instead showing that chemical equilibrium in finite systems yields few free neutrons and neutron-rich large fragments. The results enable extraction of nuclear matter symmetry energy at subnuclear densities from experimental data, with direct applications to astrophysical systems such as supernovae and neutron stars where chemical equilibrium is believed to be established.
