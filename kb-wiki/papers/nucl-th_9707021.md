# Strangeness in ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions

> I discuss strangeness production in nucleus-nucleus reactions at ultrarelativistic energies (up to 200 AGeV). In these reactions matter may be created with densities and temperatures in the transition region between quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and hadron gas. Strange anti-baryon enhancement at 200 AGeV and probably even more so at 10 AGeV signals importance of interactions beyond hadron gas dynamics. The systematics of strangeness production indicates that energy and baryon density are key variables while the size of the production volume plays no visible role. Analysis of strangeness appears useful to explore thermalization, flow and the post-equilibrium stage in ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions.

# Strangeness in ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions

## Key claim

Strangeness enhancement, particularly of strange anti-baryons, is not as clearly linked to quark-gluon plasma formation as initially thought; the systematics indicate that energy and baryon density are the key variables while production volume plays no visible role, and the observed enhancement may be explained by string fusion into color ropes or by hadronic rescattering without requiring a QGP phase transition.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental data from BNL-AGS (Au beams up to 12 AGeV) and CERN-SPS (Pb beam at 158 AGeV) and compares them to transport model calculations using RQMD (with color rope formation and hadronic rescattering), VENUS (with statistical fragmentation of high-density droplets), and parton cascade models. The author analyzes strange hadron yields, particularly anti-baryon ratios, and examines the sequential freeze-out of different hadron species to study the post-equilibrium stage of nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: up to 200 AGeV at CERN-SPS and up to nearly 12 AGeV at BNL-AGS
- The $\bar{\Lambda}/\bar{p}$ ratio in Si+Au and Au+Au at 10-15 AGeV is suggested to be on the order of 5-6 based on preliminary data
- In RQMD calculations for Pb(160AGeV)+Pb, the $\overline{\Xi}$ yield increases by a factor of 7.7 compared to the NN mode, and would increase by a factor of 13.3 without anti-baryon absorption
- Effective temperatures of more than 300 MeV characterize initial hadronic interactions at CERN energy in RQMD
- Chemical freeze-out temperature extracted from fireball fits to S(200AGeV)+W data is close to 190 MeV
- Strangeness suppression parameter in Cleyman's fit is 0.7, roughly a factor of 2 larger than in $pp$ or $e^+e^-$ fits
- RQMD predicts $\Phi$ meson slope parameter of 225 MeV compared to proton slope of 280 MeV in central Pb(160AGeV)+Pb collisions
- $\Phi$ mesons decouple from the system about 12 fm/c earlier than nucleons in RQMD calculations
- Hadron density at chemical freeze-out is approximately 1 fm$^{-3}$
- Parton cascade model generates too many pions with $p_t > 3$ GeV by a factor of 5 compared to WA98 data

## Context

This review extends the discussion of strangeness as a QGP signature originally suggested by Koch (1986) and examines the subsequent decade of experimental and theoretical work. The paper contrasts the equilibrium fireball approach of Cleyman (1997) and Bebie et al. (1992) with transport models like RQMD (Sorge, 1992), VENUS (Werner, 1993), and the parton cascade model (Geiger, 1997). The work enables future studies of sequential freeze-out to probe the equation of state in the phase transition region, and identifies p+A collisions at CERN-SPS energies and heavy-ion collisions at lower beam energies (around 10 AGeV) as the most promising directions to distinguish between production mechanisms.
