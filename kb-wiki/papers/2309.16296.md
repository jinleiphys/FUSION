# Production properties of deuterons, helions and tritons via an analytical nucleon coalescence method in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV

> We improve a nucleon coalescence model to include the coordinate-momentum correlation in nucleon joint distributions, and apply it to Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV to study production properties of deuterons ($d$), helions ($^3$He) and tritons ($t$). We give formulas of the coalescence factors $B_2$ and $B_3$, and naturally explain their behaviors as functions of the collision centrality and the transverse momentum per nucleon $p_T/A$. We reproduce the transverse momentum spectra, averaged transverse momenta and yield rapidity densities of $d$, $^3$He and $t$, and find the system effective radius obtained in the coalescence production of light nuclei behaves similarly to Hanbury Brown-Twiss interferometry radius. We particularly give expressions of yield ratios $d/p$, $^3$He$/d$, $t/p$, $^3$He$/p$, $d/p^{2}$, $^3$He$/p^3$, $t/^3$He and argue their nontrivial behaviors can be used to distinguish production mechanisms of light nuclei.

## Key claim

The improved nucleon coalescence model, which includes coordinate-momentum correlation in nucleon joint distributions, can quantitatively describe the rising behavior of coalescence factors \(B_2\) and \(B_3\) as functions of transverse momentum per nucleon in Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV, a feature that the earlier coordinate-momentum factorization assumption (Wang 2020) could only reproduce qualitatively with underestimated growth.

## Method

The authors extended their previous analytic nucleon coalescence model (Zhao 2022, Wang 2022) by incorporating coordinate-momentum correlation in the joint distributions of nucleon pairs and triplets, using a Gaussian form for the relative coordinate distribution with width parameter \(C=4\). They applied the model to Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV, using experimental proton and neutron spectra as inputs, and parameterized the system effective radius \(R_f(p_T)\) as a function of the cube root of charged particle pseudorapidity density and a power law of the transverse mass, with parameters \(a\) and \(b\) fixed by fitting deuteron and \(^3\)He spectra in the most central collisions.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square radii used: \(R_d = 2.1421\) fm for deuterons, \(R_{^3\mathrm{He}} = 1.9661\) fm for helions, \(R_t = 1.7591\) fm for tritons (from Angeli 2013).
- Free parameters for \(R_f(p_T)\) in Pb-Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV: \((a,b) = (0.67, -0.25)\) for deuterons and \((0.60, -0.25)\) for \(^3\)He and tritons.
- Spin degeneracy factors: \(g_d = 3/4\), \(g_{^3\mathrm{He}} = 1/4\), \(g_t = 1/4\).
- The width parameter \(C\) is set to 4, following Mrowczynski (2016).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analytic coalescence model (Zhao 2022, Wang 2022, Wang 2020) by adding coordinate-momentum correlation, which was missing in the earlier factorization assumption. The model successfully reproduces the transverse momentum spectra, averaged transverse momenta, and yield rapidity densities of deuterons, helions, and tritons measured by ALICE (2015, 2017) at the LHC. The system effective radius extracted from light nuclei coalescence behaves similarly to Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) interferometry radii, and the analytic expressions for yield ratios such as \(d/p\), \(^3\)He\(/d\), and \(t/^3\)He are argued to distinguish between thermal and coalescence production mechanisms.
