# Systematics of $α$--nucleus optical potentials

> Double--folded optical $α$--nucleus potentials can be used to calculate elastic scattering cross sections in a wide mass-- and energy region. Because of the systematic behavior of the potential parameters we are able to obtain reliable optical potentials for astrophysically relevant reactions even without scattering data in low--energy region. As example we analyze the capture reaction ${^{144}{\rm Sm}}(α,γ){^{148}{\Gd}}$.

## Key claim

Double-folded alpha-nucleus optical potentials, whose parameters show systematic behavior across a wide mass and energy range, can provide reliable potentials for astrophysically relevant reactions even in the low-energy region where no scattering data exist, though the volume integrals for the imaginary part retain a strong mass dependence that prevents a single universal parametrization.

## Method

The real part of the optical potential was constructed using the double-folding model of Kobos et al. (1984), folding nucleon densities from nuclear charge distributions with a density-dependent effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, and scaling the depth by a normalization factor lambda fitted to elastic scattering data and cluster-state energies. The imaginary part was parametrized with a Woods-Saxon form. Volume integrals per interacting nucleon pair were extracted for both real and imaginary parts from fits to elastic scattering data on targets from light (He-4, O-16, Ca-40) to intermediate and heavy nuclei (Ge-70, Zr-90, Pr-141, Sm-144, Pb-208) at energies above 15 MeV, and the energy dependence was modeled including a dispersive contribution linking real and imaginary parts.

## Key numbers

- For intermediate and heavy nuclei, the volume integral of the real part follows a linear extrapolation at low energies: J_R/(A_P * A_T) = (320 + 0.67 E_CM) MeV fm^3, where E_CM is in MeV.
- The volume integrals of the real part have a maximum around E_CM = 30 MeV and decrease slightly at lower and higher energies.
- For Pb-208 at E_CM = 10 MeV, the volume integral of the imaginary part J_I can range between 10 and 60 MeV fm^3 depending on nuclear structure (doubly magic vs. deformed).
- For the capture reaction Sm-144(alpha,gamma)Gd-148 at E_alpha = 9.5 MeV, the astrophysical S-factor calculated with the folding potential is 7.8 x 10^27 MeV b, compared to 2.3 x 10^28 MeV b for an equivalent square well with radius 8.75 fm and 2.3 x 10^27 MeV b for radius 8.01 fm.
- The reaction rate at T_9 = 2.5 using the folding potential is 1.27 x 10^-15 cm^3 mol^-1 s^-1, about one-third of the value for the equivalent square well with radius 8.75 fm (3.75 x 10^-15 cm^3 mol^-1 s^-1).
- The resulting Sm-146/Sm-144 ratio is about 0.22, consistent with cosmochemical data of 0.1 to 0.7 from Prinzhofer et al. (1989).

## Context

This work extends the systematic double-folding approach for alpha-nucleus potentials, previously applied to light nuclei by Abele and Staudt (1993), to intermediate and heavy nuclei (A >= 70) relevant for the p-process nucleosynthesis described by Woosley and Howard (1978). The folding potentials reduce the ambiguity of earlier phenomenological approaches such as the equivalent square well used by Woosley and Howard (1990), which gave cross sections differing by a factor of ten for two different radius choices. The results enable more reliable calculation of (gamma,alpha) photodisintegration cross sections and reaction rates for p-process isotopes, and the method can be extended to unstable nuclei using densities from relativistic mean-field theory (e.g., the NLSH parameter set).
