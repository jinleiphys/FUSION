# Deformed one-quasiparticle states in covariant density functional theory

> Systematic investigation of the accuracy of the description of the energies of deformed one-quasiparticle states has been performed in covariant density functional theory in actinide and rare-earth mass regions. The sources of the discrepancies between theory and experiment are analyzed. Although some improvements in the description of ground state configurations and one-quasiparticle spectra can be achieved by better parametrization of the relativistic mean field Lagrangian, the analysis suggests that spectroscopic quality of their description can be achieved only in theoretical framework which takes into account particle-vibration coupling.

## Key claim

Although some improvements in the description of ground state configurations and one-quasiparticle spectra can be achieved by better parametrization of the relativistic mean field Lagrangian, the analysis suggests that spectroscopic quality of their description can be achieved only in a theoretical framework which takes into account particle-vibration coupling.

## Method

The authors performed systematic cranked relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (CRHB) calculations for 44 nuclei in the actinide region (Z=89-100) and 31 odd-proton nuclei in the rare-earth region, using the NL1 and NL3* parametrizations of the relativistic mean field Lagrangian with the D1S Gogny force in the pairing channel. They calculated the energies of one-quasiparticle configurations by blocking the odd particle in a fully self-consistent way, then compared these theoretical energies to adopted experimental one-quasiparticle band-head energies from evaluated data.

## Key numbers

- In the actinide region, only approximately 33% of one-quasiparticle states are described with an accuracy better than 200 keV, and approximately 22% with an accuracy between 200 and 400 keV.
- For some states, the deviation of calculated energy from experimental one exceeds 1 MeV and can be close to 1.4 MeV.
- With the NL1 parametrization in odd-proton rare-earth nuclei, the energies of 44% of the states are described with an accuracy better than 200 keV.
- The percentage of ground states whose structure is correctly reproduced is 38% for actinides with NL3*, 45% for actinides with NL1, and 48% for rare-earth with NL1.
- After rescaling by the Lorentz effective mass, more than 75% of states are described with an accuracy better than 400 keV.
- On average, only 6.8% of calculated states have gamma-deformation in the range 1 degree to 17 degrees; in the actinide region, 43% of triaxial configurations have the dominant nu[622]3/2 structure and 38% have the nu[501]1/2 structure.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of single-particle spectra in a few actinide nuclei within covariant DFT (Afanasjev, 2004) and the global survey of ground state configurations in odd-mass nuclei (Bonneau, Quentin, and Minkov, 2007). It provides the first systematic statistical analysis of the accuracy of one-quasiparticle energy descriptions across deformed nuclei in the DFT framework, and it enables future development of spectroscopic-quality energy density functionals by demonstrating that particle-vibration coupling must be incorporated, as has already been shown to improve descriptions in spherical nuclei (Litvinova and Afanasjev, 2011).
