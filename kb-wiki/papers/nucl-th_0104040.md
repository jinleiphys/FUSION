# Microscopic Reaction Dynamics at SPS and RHIC

> The current status of transport theoretical models applicable to the physics of the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider is reviewed. The time evolution of microscopic reaction dynamics - from early, hard, partonic rescattering up to soft hadronic interactions close to freeze-out is analyzed and key observables linked to the different reaction stages are discussed.

# Microscopic Reaction Dynamics at SPS and RHIC

## Key claim
Transport theoretical models can connect experimental observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions to the time evolution and reaction dynamics, potentially revealing the formation of a transient deconfined Quark-Gluon-Plasma phase; however, no single transport approach currently available can cover the entire time evolution of a collision at RHIC in one self-consistent manner.

## Method
The paper reviews multiple transport theoretical approaches, Classical Yang-Mills theory, Lattice Gauge Transport, the Parton Cascade Model (PCM) using leading-order pQCD cross sections, Nuclear Fluid Dynamics (NFD), and hadronic/string transport models (e.g., RQMD, UrQMD), and analyzes their ranges of applicability across different reaction stages. The author also presents results from hybrid "hydro plus micro" calculations that combine NFD with a microscopic hadronic cascade, and examines balance functions, flavor evolution, and freeze-out dynamics using these frameworks.

## Key numbers
- In central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, early parton rescattering occurs at average squared momentum transfer ⟨Q²⟩ ≈ 7 GeV², decreasing to about 4.5 GeV² by the time hadronization sets in (between t_cm ≈ 0.5 fm/c and t_cm ≈ 4.5 fm/c).
- Partonic reaction dynamics are dominated by gluon-gluon scattering (≈43%), followed by gluon fusion (≈27%) and quark-gluon scattering (≈26%); quark-quark and quark-antiquark scattering contribute at the few percent level.
- At SPS, antiproton multiplicity is reduced by 40-50% due to baryon-antibaryon annihilation in the hadronic phase.
- Ω baryons suffer on average only one hadronic interaction before freeze-out, while nucleons and Λ baryons suffer approximately 5-6 collisions.
- For central Au+Au collisions at √s = 130A GeV, preliminary STAR data gives R_out/R_side ≈ 1.1 at small K_T.

## Context
This review extends the line of work on transport theory for relativistic heavy-ion collisions, building on prior approaches such as the Parton Cascade Model (PCM), Nuclear Fluid Dynamics (Bjorken, Dumitru and Rischke), and hadronic transport models (RQMD, UrQMD). The hybrid hydro+micro approach was pioneered by Huovinen and collaborators and later taken up by Teaney and others. The paper enables future tests of whether a long-lived hadronic soft-rescattering stage associated with QGP formation and hadronization is observed at RHIC, particularly through HBT measurements at higher K_T.
