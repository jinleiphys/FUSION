# Non-collapsing quasiparticle random phase approximation for nuclear double-beta decay

> We show how the longstanding problem of the collapse of the charge-exchange QRPA near the physical value of the force strength can be circumvented. This is done by including the effect of ground state correlations into the QRPA equations of motion. The corresponding formalism, called renormalized QRPA, is briefly outlined and its consequences are discussed in the framework of a schematic model for the two-neutrino double beta decay in the $^{100}Mo \rightarrow\, ^{100}Ru$ system. The question of the conservation of the Ikeda sum rule is also addressed within the new formalism.

## Key claim

The renormalized QRPA (RQRPA), which includes ground state correlations in the equations of motion, avoids the collapse of the charge-exchange QRPA near the physical value of the particle-particle force strength; however, the two-neutrino double-beta decay matrix element still passes through zero in the RQRPA, albeit at a somewhat higher value of the coupling strength.

## Method

The authors modify the standard QRPA by incorporating ground state correlations into the equations of motion, leading to a renormalized QRPA (RQRPA) formalism. They introduce renormalized two-quasiparticle operators and a generalized quasiboson approximation, resulting in a nonlinear system of coupled equations for the amplitudes that must be solved self-consistently together with modified BCS conditions. The formalism is applied to a schematic single-mode model (SMM) for the two-neutrino double-beta decay in the ¹⁰⁰Mo → ¹⁰⁰Ru system, using a delta-force interaction with separate strength constants for particle-hole, particle-particle, and pairing channels.

## Key numbers

- The physical value of the particle-particle coupling ratio is t ≅ 1.5 (where t = v_t^pp / v_s^pair).
- The QRPA collapses close to t = 1.5, while in the RQRPA the energy decreases asymptotically when t → ∞.
- The unperturbed energy ω₀ = -[G(pp;0) + G(nn;0)]/4.
- The particle-hole strength parameters used are v_s^ph = 55 and v_t^ph = 92, with v_s^pair = 55 (in units of MeV fm³).
- The active nucleon numbers are N_p = 2 and N_n = 2 for the initial state (¹⁰⁰Mo), and N_p = 4 and N_n = 0 for the final state (¹⁰⁰Ru).

## Context

This work extends the charge-exchange QRPA approach to double-beta decay calculations, which had been plagued by the collapse of the approximation near physical force strengths (Krmpotic 1994). The RQRPA formalism was originally introduced by Rowe (1968) and recently applied by Catara et al. (1993, 1994) to charge-conserving collective states. The paper addresses the longstanding problem that the QRPA collapse makes the theoretical estimation of both two-neutrino and neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements uncertain, and the RQRPA provides a framework where the dynamical calculation no longer collapses, potentially enabling more reliable upper limits for the effective neutrino mass. The authors note that a similar study was performed by Toivanen and Suhonen (1995).
