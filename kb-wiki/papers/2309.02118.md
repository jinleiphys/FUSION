# Hadronization dynamics from the spectral representation of the gauge invariant quark propagator

> Using the spectral representation of the quark propagator we study the Dirac decomposition of the gauge invariant quark propagator, whose imaginary part describes the hadronization of a quark as this interacts with the vacuum. We then demonstrate the formal gauge invariance of the so-called jet mass, that is of the coefficient of the chiral-odd part of the gauge invariant propagator, that can be expressed in any gauge as the first moment of the chiral-odd quark spectral function. This is therefore revealed to be a \textit{bona fide} QCD observable encoding aspects of the dynamical mass generation in the QCD vacuum, and is furthermore experimentally measurable in specific twist-3 longitudinal-transverse asymmetries in DIS and in semi-inclusive electron-positron collisions. In light-like axial gauges, we also obtain a new sum rule for the spectral function associated with the gauge fixing vector. We finally present a gauge-dependent formula that connects the second moment of the chiral-even coefficient of the quark spectral function to invariant mass generation and final state rescattering in the hadronization of a quark. Finding twist-4 experimental observables sensitive to this quantity is left for future work.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the jet mass, defined as the first moment of the chiral-odd quark spectral function, is a gauge-invariant QCD observable that encodes dynamical mass generation during hadronization, but the decomposition of the jet virtuality into invariant mass and transverse broadening contributions is gauge-dependent and only their sum is gauge invariant.

## Method

The authors use the Kallen-Lehmann spectral representation of the quark propagator to study the Dirac decomposition of the gauge invariant quark propagator (the inclusive jet correlator), whose discontinuity describes the hadronization of a quark interacting with the QCD vacuum. They perform calculations in both light-like axial gauges and generic gauges, projecting the jet correlator onto specific Dirac matrices to extract its twist-2, twist-3, and twist-4 coefficients, and derive sum rules for the quark spectral functions by enforcing gauge invariance of the jet correlator.

## Key numbers

* The jet mass sum rule: \( M_j = \int_{0}^{\infty} dp^2 \;\sqrt{p^2}\;\rho_1(p^2) \), where \(M_j\) is the gauge-invariant jet mass and \(\rho_1\) is the chiral-odd quark spectral function.
* The chiral-even spectral function sum rule: \( 1 = \int_{0}^{\infty}dp^{2}\rho_3(p^2) \), which follows from canonical commutation relations.
* The axial spectral function sum rule: \( 0 = \int_{0}^\infty dp^2\,p^2\, \rho_0(p^2) \), a new result for the spectral function associated with the gauge fixing vector.
* The jet virtuality decomposition: \( K_j^2 = \mu_j^2 +\tau_j^2 \), where \(\mu_j^2 = \int_0^\infty dp^2 \,p^2\, \rho_3(p^2) \) is the bare invariant mass and \(\tau_j^2\) is the transverse broadening from final state rescattering; only the sum \(K_j^2\) is gauge invariant.

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed by Accardi, Signori, and collaborators (2017-2023) for studying hadronization through the gauge invariant quark propagator, which replaces the free quark line in inclusive DIS and semi-inclusive electron-positron annihilation at large Bjorken x due to kinematic restrictions. The jet mass \(M_j\) couples to the chiral-odd transversity distribution \(h_1(x)\) in twist-3 longitudinal-transverse spin asymmetries in DIS, and to the chiral-odd twist-3 fragmentation function \(H_1^\Lambda(z)\) in semi-inclusive \(e^+e^-\) annihilation with self-polarizing \(\Lambda\) hadrons, as explored by the Belle II collaboration (2022). The derived sum rules provide tools to verify nonperturbative calculations of the quark propagator, such as those by Horak (2022), Falcao (2022), Solis (2019), and Duarte (2022), though these have only been carried out in covariant gauges.
