# Holographic baryons, dense matter and neutron star mergers

> The gauge/gravity duality, combined with information from lattice QCD, nuclear theory, and perturbative QCD, can be used to constrain the equation of state of hot and dense QCD. I discuss an approach based on the holographic V-QCD model. I start by reviewing the results from the construction of the V-QCD baryon as a soliton of the gauge fields in the model. Then I discuss implementing nuclear matter in the model by using a homogeneous approach. The model predicts a strongly first order phase transition from nuclear to quark matter with a critical endpoint. By using the model in state-of-the-art simulations of neutron star binaries with parameters consistent with GW170817, I study the formation of quark matter during the merger process.

## Key claim

The V-QCD holographic model, combined with low-density nuclear theory and a van der Waals extrapolation for temperature dependence, predicts a strongly first-order phase transition from nuclear to quark matter with a critical endpoint; this transition is almost completely described by the holographic model apart from weak temperature dependence in the nuclear matter phase, and the resulting hybrid equations of state are consistent with all known theoretical and observational constraints including GW170817.

## Method

The author constructs baryons in the V-QCD model as solitons of non-Abelian gauge fields, solving the nonlinear partial differential equations via a relaxation method with an ansatz depending on the holographic coordinate and a single spatial radial coordinate. Nuclear matter is then implemented using a homogeneous approximation with a discontinuity in the bulk to produce nonzero baryon number, and the resulting zero-temperature equation of state is combined with the Hempel-Schaffner-Bielich model at low densities, the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall model near saturation density, and a van der Waals model for temperature dependence in the dense nuclear matter phase. The hybrid equations of state are used in 3+1 dimensional general relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of neutron star binary mergers with parameters consistent with GW170817, using the FUKA spectral code for initial data and the FIL code for binary evolution.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: \(n_s \approx 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Maximum neutron star mass constraint: \(M_\mathrm{max} \gtrsim 2 M_\odot\) from Shapiro delay measurements of J0348+0432 and J0740+6620
- Tidal deformability bound from GW170817: \(\Lambda \lesssim 580\) at 90% confidence level, translating to an upper bound of about 13.5 km for the radius of a \(1.4 M_\odot\) neutron star
- V-QCD nucleon mass: \(M_N \simeq 1170\) MeV (experimental: 940 MeV)
- V-QCD \(\Delta\) baryon mass: \(M_\Delta \simeq 1260\) MeV (experimental: 1234 MeV)
- Critical point temperature range: \(110~\mathrm{MeV} \lesssim T_c \lesssim 130~\mathrm{MeV}\)
- Critical point baryon chemical potential range: \(480~\mathrm{MeV} \lesssim \mu_{bc} \lesssim 580~\mathrm{MeV}\)
- Speed of sound in high-density nuclear matter phase: \(c_s^2 > 1/3\)

## Context

This work extends the V-QCD holographic approach developed by Jarvinen and collaborators (2011 onward) for modeling QCD thermodynamics, building on earlier holographic baryon constructions in hard wall models by Pomarol and collaborators (2007-2008) and in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model by Kim, Hata, and Bolognesi (2006-2013). The hybrid equations of state enable state-of-the-art neutron star merger simulations that can study quark matter production during the merger process, which was previously inaccessible in models with controlled predictions for the nuclear-to-quark matter phase transition. The results can be compared with future gravitational wave observations and electromagnetic counterparts to constrain the QCD equation of state at intermediate densities where no first-principles methods are available.
