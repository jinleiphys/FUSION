# On the possible existence of a soft dipole mode in 8He

> With an extreme neutron-to-proton ratio of N/Z=3, 8He provides an ideal laboratory for the study of a variety of exotic phenomena, such as the emergence of a soft dipole mode that is dominated by transitions into the continuum. In this contribution, a covariant density functional theory (DFT) framework is used to compute ground-state properties and the dipole response of 8He. Although 8He is admittedly too light for DFT to be applicable, the great merit of the approach is that the spurious contamination associated with the center-of-mass motion is guaranteed to decouple from the physical response. Given that a strong mixing between the isoscalar and isovector dipole modes is expected for a system with such a large neutron-proton asymmetry as 8He, the narrow structures that emerged at low energies in the isovector dipole response are attributed to the shift of the spurious strength to zero (or near zero) excitation energy. Thus, the theoretical framework implemented here disfavors the emergence of a soft dipole mode in 8He.

## Key claim

Using a covariant density functional theory (DFT) framework, the author argues that narrow low-energy structures seen in the isovector dipole response of 8He are contaminants from the spurious center-of-mass mode, not a genuine soft dipole mode; the theoretical framework "disfavors" the emergence of such a mode, in agreement with some experiments (Iwata 2000, Holl 2021) but in disagreement with others (Markenroth 2001, Golovkov 2008) and a recent ab initio calculation (Bonaiti 2021).

## Method

The author employed a covariant density functional theory (DFT) framework, using three energy density functionals (RMF016/FSUGarnet, RMF022, and RMF028/FSUGold2) that share the same isoscalar properties but differ in their isovector properties, calibrated to different assumed neutron skin thicknesses of 208Pb. Ground-state properties were obtained from self-consistent Kohn-Sham equations, and the dipole response was computed using a self-consistent random-phase approximation (RPA) that ensures the spurious center-of-mass state decouples from physical modes by shifting its strength to zero excitation energy.

## Key numbers

* The classical energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) for 8He is 22.2 MeV fm²; the uncorrelated dipole response gave 22.4, 22.6, and 22.8 MeV fm² for RMF016, RMF022, and RMF028, respectively.
* After removing spurious strength, the correlated (RPA) energy-weighted sum m₁ was 16.37, 16.70, and 16.84 fm² MeV for RMF016, RMF022, and RMF028, respectively.
* The electric dipole polarizability αD was estimated as 0.262, 0.242, and 0.220 fm³ for RMF016, RMF022, and RMF028, respectively.
* The experimental one-neutron separation energy of 8He is 2.535(8) MeV; the RMF016 model predicted a p₃/₂ neutron orbital energy of 1.714 MeV, RMF022 predicted 2.740 MeV, and RMF028 predicted 3.784 MeV.
* The experimental charge radius of 8He is 1.929(26) fm; the three models predicted 1.998, 1.981, and 1.970 fm, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the application of covariant DFT to a very light, neutron-rich nucleus (8He, N/Z=3), a system for which DFT is "admittedly too light" to be applicable. It directly addresses a controversy between experiments that identified a soft dipole mode at 3-4 MeV (Markenroth 2001, Meister 2002, Golovkov 2008, Grigorenko 2009) and those that did not (Iwata 2000, Holl 2021), as well as a recent ab initio coupled-cluster calculation (Bonaiti 2021) that reported low-energy dipole strength around 5 MeV. The author argues that the self-consistent RPA treatment of the center-of-mass motion, which is guaranteed to decouple spurious strength, provides a unique advantage over other approaches for interpreting the low-energy dipole response.
