# Strangeness S=-3 and -4 baryon-baryon interactions in chiral EFT

> 4 pages, 2 figures, Contribution to the proceedings of the 12th International Conference on Meson-Nucleon Physics and the Structure of the Nucleon (MENU2010), May 31-June 4, 2010, College of William and Mary, Williamsburg, Virginia

## Key claim

The paper reports on leading-order (LO) chiral effective field theory predictions for baryon-baryon interactions in the strangeness S=-3 and S=-4 sectors, but the results are strongly cut-off dependent and therefore not yet reliable; a next-to-leading-order (NLO) calculation is needed before firm conclusions can be drawn, particularly regarding the possible existence of bound states.

## Method

The authors derived baryon-baryon potentials at leading order in chiral effective field theory using SU(3) flavor symmetry constraints, consisting of four-baryon contact terms without derivatives and one-pseudoscalar-meson exchanges. The scattering amplitudes were obtained by solving a regularized Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the LO potential, with a regulator function exp[-(p'^4 + p^4)/Lambda^4] applied to remove high-energy components. The five low-energy coefficients (LECs) that enter the S=-3 and S=-4 sectors were fixed from a previous study of the YN (S=-1) sector, allowing genuine predictions for the higher-strangeness channels.

## Key numbers

- The LO chiral EFT interaction for S=-3 and S=-4 depends on five contact terms that also enter the YN interaction.
- A clear cusp effect is visible in the Xi0Sigma- cross section at p_lab approximately 106 MeV/c, at the opening of the Xi-Sigma0 channel.
- No sizeable cusp effects are observed in the Xi0Lambda cross section around p_lab = 690 MeV/c, at the opening of the XiSigma channels.
- The Xi0Lambda singlet scattering length a_s ranges from -33.5 fm (Lambda=550 MeV) to 9.07 fm (Lambda=700 MeV), changing sign within the cut-off range.
- The Xi0Lambda singlet effective range r_s ranges from 1.00 fm (Lambda=550 MeV) to 0.84 fm (Lambda=700 MeV).
- The Xi0Lambda triplet scattering length a_t ranges from 0.33 fm (Lambda=550 MeV) to 0.31 fm (Lambda=700 MeV).
- The Xi0Sigma+ singlet scattering length a_s ranges from 4.28 fm (Lambda=550 MeV) to 2.74 fm (Lambda=700 MeV).
- The XiXi singlet scattering length a_s ranges from 3.92 fm (Lambda=550 MeV) to 2.47 fm (Lambda=700 MeV).
- For cut-off Lambda = 700 MeV, the Xi0Lambda system shows a bound state with binding energy -0.43 MeV.
- The cut-off range considered is Lambda = 550 to 700 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Juelich group's previous chiral EFT studies of the NN interaction (Entem and Machleidt, 2003; Epelbaum et al., 2005) and the S=-1 and S=-2 baryon-baryon sectors (Polinder et al., 2006, 2007; Haidenbauer et al., 2007, 2009, 2010) to the S=-3 and S=-4 channels. The predictions are compared with results from the Nijmegen potential models (NSC97a, NSC97f) and the model by Fujiwara et al. (fss2). The strong cut-off dependence of the singlet scattering lengths, especially in the Xi0Lambda channel where a virtual bound state transforms into a real bound state within the cut-off range, indicates that an NLO calculation is required before reliable conclusions can be drawn about the possible existence of bound states in these systems.
