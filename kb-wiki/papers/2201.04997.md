# Strangeness $S = -2$ baryon-baryon interactions and femtoscopic correlation functions in covariant chiral effective field theory

> We study the baryon-baryon interactions with strangeness $S = -2$ and corresponding momentum correlation functions in leading order covariant chiral effective field theory. The relevant low energy constants are determined by fitting to the latest HAL QCD simulations, taking into account all the coupled channels. Extrapolating the so-obtained strong interactions to the physical point and considering both quantum statistical effects and the Coulomb interaction, we calculate the $ΛΛ$ and $Ξ^-p$ correlation functions with a spherical Gaussian source and compare them with the recent experimental data. We find remarkable agreement between our predictions and the experimental measurements by using the source radius determined in proton-proton correlations, which demonstrates the consistency between theory, experiment, and lattice QCD simulations. Moreover, we predict the $Σ^+Σ^+$, $Σ^+Λ$, and $Σ^+Σ^-$ interactions and corresponding momentum correlation functions. We further investigate the influence of the source shape and size of the hadron pair on the correlation functions studied and show that the current data are not very sensitive to the source shape. Future experimental measurement of the predicted momentum correlation functions will provide a non-trivial test of not only SU(3) flavor symmetry and its breaking but also the baryon-baryon interactions derived in covariant chiral effective field theory.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that their leading-order covariant chiral effective field theory interactions for strangeness \(S=-2\) baryon-baryon systems, fitted to HAL QCD lattice simulations at a pion mass of 146 MeV and extrapolated to the physical point, yield \(\Lambda\Lambda\) and \(\Xi^-p\) correlation functions in remarkable agreement with experimental data from ALICE when using the source radius determined from proton-proton correlations, thereby showing consistency between theory, experiment, and lattice QCD.

## Method

The authors determined 12 low-energy constants (LECs) for the \(S\)-wave contact potentials by fitting to the latest HAL QCD phase shifts for \(\Lambda\Lambda\) and \(\Xi N\) channels (with \(t/a = 12\)) at a pion mass of 146 MeV, considering all coupled channels. They then solved the coupled-channel Kadyshevsky equation to obtain reaction amplitudes and scattering wave functions, and computed momentum correlation functions using the Koonin–Pratt formula with a static spherical Gaussian source, including quantum statistical effects for identical particles and the Coulomb interaction for charged pairs. The theoretical correlation functions were spin-averaged with appropriate weights and compared to experimental data from \(p\)–Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 5.02\) TeV and \(p\)–\(p\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 13\) TeV.

## Key numbers

*   The \(\Lambda\Lambda\) scattering length from the full coupled-channel result is \(a_s^{\Lambda\Lambda} \approx -0.77\) fm and the effective range is \(r_s^{\Lambda\Lambda} \approx 3.83\) fm.
*   The source radius used for comparison with \(p\)–Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 5.02\) TeV is \(R = 1.427\) fm, determined from proton-proton correlations.
*   The source radius used for comparison with \(p\)–\(p\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 13\) TeV is \(R = 1.182\) fm, determined from proton-proton correlations.
*   The hadronization temperature used to estimate channel weights is \(T^* = 154\) MeV.
*   The total \(\chi^2/{\rm d.o.f.}\) for the fit to the HAL QCD phase shifts is approximately 0.34.

## Context

This work extends the covariant chiral effective field theory framework, previously applied to nucleon-nucleon and strangeness \(-1\) to \(-4\) systems (KaiWen 2016, Liu 2021), to the strangeness \(-2\) sector using the latest HAL QCD lattice simulations (Sasaki 2020). It improves upon a prior study by Kamiya et al. (Ohnishi 2021) by including complete coupled-channel effects (especially \(\Sigma\Lambda\) and \(\Sigma\Sigma\) channels), using interactions extrapolated to the physical point rather than at \(m_\pi = 146\) MeV, and predicting \(\Sigma^+\Sigma^+\), \(\Sigma^+\Lambda\), and \(\Sigma^+\Sigma^-\) correlation functions for the first time. The results enable future experimental tests of SU(3) flavor symmetry and its breaking through measurements of these predicted correlation functions.
