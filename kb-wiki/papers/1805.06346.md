# Heavy Quarkonium Dissociation by Thermal Gluons at Next-to-leading Order in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Using the chromo-electric dipole coupling Hamiltonian from QCD multipole expansion, we derive the dissociation cross sections of heavy quarkonia by thermal gluons at next-to-leading order (NLO, also known as inelastic parton scattering dissociation) in the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) in the framework of second order quantum mechanical perturbation theory. While suffering divergence (infrared and soft-collinear divergences) in vacuum, the cross sections thus derived become finite in the QGP as rendered by the finite thermal gluon masses. In contrast to the leading order (LO, also known as gluo-dissociation) counterparts rapidly dropping off with increasing incident gluon energy, the NLO cross sections exhibits finite value toward high energies because of new phase space being opened up. We then carry out a full calculation of the dissociation rates for various charmonia and bottomonia within a non-relativistic in-medium potential model. The NLO process is shown to dominate the dissociation rate toward high temperatures when the binding energies of heavy quarkonia become smaller relative to the Debye screening mass.

## Key claim

The paper derives next-to-leading order (NLO) dissociation cross sections for heavy quarkonia by thermal gluons in the quark-gluon plasma, showing that while these cross sections diverge in vacuum due to infrared and soft-collinear divergences, they become finite in the QGP because of finite thermal gluon masses. The NLO process dominates the dissociation rate at high temperatures when binding energies become small relative to the Debye screening mass, removing an artifact of the leading-order (gluo-dissociation) approximation.

## Method

The authors used the chromo-electric dipole coupling Hamiltonian from QCD multipole expansion and second-order quantum mechanical perturbation theory to derive NLO dissociation cross sections for the process g + Ψ → g + Q + Q̅. They computed transition amplitudes from singlet-to-octet and octet-to-octet vertices (V_SO and V_OO) and from the three-gluon vertex (V_3g), then evaluated the cross sections numerically using temperature-dependent bound state wave functions and binding energies from a non-relativistic in-medium potential model with color screening.

## Key numbers

- Gluon thermal mass taken as m_g(T) = √(3/4) g_s T with fixed g_s = 2.3 for N_f = 3 light flavors and N_c = 3 colors
- Debye screening mass parameterization: m_D/T = -4.058 + 6.32·(T/T_c - 0.885)^0.1035 with T_c = 172.5 MeV
- Vacuum Cornell potential parameters: Coulomb coupling α = 4/3 α_s = 0.471, string tension σ = 0.192 GeV²
- Heavy quark masses: m_c = 1.320 GeV, m_b = 4.746 GeV
- J/ψ mass = 3.097 GeV, Υ(1S) mass = 9.460 GeV
- For vacuum cross section comparison, an effective gluon mass m_g = 600 MeV was used

## Context

This work extends the LO gluo-dissociation mechanism (Peskin 1979, Kharzeev 1994, Wong 2001) and the "quasi-free" NLO scattering scenario proposed by Grandchamp (2001). It builds on the imaginary part of the heavy quark potential derived from Landau damping (Laine 2006, Beraudo 2007) and confirmed by lattice QCD (Burnier 2014), and on the pNRQCD framework at finite temperature (Brambilla 2008, 2013). The results enable more accurate transport calculations of heavy quarkonium yields and momentum spectra in the QGP, particularly at high temperatures where the NLO process dominates over LO gluo-dissociation.
