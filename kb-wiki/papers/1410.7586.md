# Search for baryonium and the physics at FAIR

> The existence of nucleon-antinucleon quasi-bound state is indicated in two decay modes of the J/psi meson studied by BES. We discuss an explanation in a fairly traditional Paris-potential model of the nucleon-antinucleon interactions. A broad, S-wave and a narrow P-wave quasi-bound states are predicted by this model. Some existing experimental evidence and possible verifications in the future are indicated.

## Key claim

The BES Collaboration's observation of two maxima in the \(p\overline{p}\) invariant mass spectrum from \(J/\psi\) decay, named \(X(1859)\) and \(X(2170)\), can be explained by a Paris-potential model of nucleon-antinucleon interactions that predicts a broad S-wave quasi-bound state and a narrow P-wave quasi-bound state, but the threshold enhancement could alternatively be due to a virtual state as predicted by the Jülich potential.

## Method

The authors extended final-state interaction (FSI) calculations from Ref. [LOI05] to cover the full photon spectrum in \(J/\psi \rightarrow \gamma p\overline{p}\) decays, assuming the photon is emitted before baryons are formed. The FSI was calculated using half-off-shell \(T\) matrices generated by the Paris potential, with a Gaussian source function of radius \(R\) (0.28 fm at maximal photon momentum) used to describe the creation of the \(\gamma p\overline{p}\) state. The radius was allowed to vary weakly with photon energy, changing from 0.28 fm at \(k \sim 1.2\) GeV to 0.39 fm at \(k = 0\).

## Key numbers

* The Paris potential generates a broad quasi-bound state in the \(^{11}S_0\) wave with binding energy \(-4.8\) MeV and width \(\Gamma/2 = 26\) MeV (total width 52 MeV).
* The Paris potential generates a narrow quasi-bound state in the \(^{33}P_1\) wave with binding energy \(-4.5\) MeV and width \(\Gamma/2 = 9.0\) MeV (total width 18 MeV).
* The Gaussian source radius used in the FSI calculation varies from 0.28 fm at maximal photon momentum \(k \sim 1.2\) GeV to 0.39 fm at \(k = 0\).
* For standard nuclei, the ratio of antiproton capture rates on neutrons to protons \(C(n\overline{p})/C(p\overline{p})\) at extreme nuclear peripheries (density \(\sim 10^{-3} \rho_0\)) ranges from 2.6(3) for \(^{96}\)Zr to 5.0(6) for \(^{124}\)Sn.
* For nuclei with small proton binding, the ratio drops to 0.5(1) for \(^{106}\)Cd and 0.79(14) for \(^{112}\)Sn at extreme peripheries.

## Context

This work extends the FSI calculations of Loiseau et al. (2005) and contrasts with the Jülich potential approach of Haidenbauer et al. (2006), which attributes the threshold enhancement to a virtual state rather than a bound state. The results enable future experimental searches for baryonium at FAIR/FLAIR, including fine-structure splitting in light antiprotonic atoms (\({}^{1}\)H, \({}^{2}\)H, \({}^{3}\)H, \({}^{3}\)He, \({}^{4}\)He) to probe subthreshold amplitudes down to \(-40\) MeV, and studies of mesons emitted from antiproton annihilations at nuclear peripheries.
