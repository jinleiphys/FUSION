# B decays into radially excited charmed mesons

> It has been recently argued that some longstanding problems in semileptonic B decays can be solved provided the branching ratio for the $B\to D^{\prime(\ast)}$ semileptonic decays are large enough. We have studied these decays in a constituent quark model which has been successful in describing semileptonic and non-leptonic B decays into orbitally excited charmed mesons. Our results do not confirm the hypothesis of large branching ratios for the $B\to D^{\prime(\ast)}$ semileptonic decays. In addition, we calculate the non-leptonic $B\to D'π$ decays which can provide an independent test of the form factors involved in the $B\to D^{\prime(\ast)}$ reactions.

## Key claim

The authors find that the branching ratios for \(B^{+}\to D^{\prime(\ast)}l^{+}\nu_{l}\) decays are much smaller than the \(\sim 1\%\) needed to explain the discrepancy between inclusive and exclusive semileptonic \(B\) decay rates, contrary to the hypothesis of Bernlochner (2012). Their results show that the sum of the two semileptonic branching ratios is \((0.109\pm0.016)\%\), a factor of ten smaller than the required value.

## Method

The authors use a constituent quark model with a Hamiltonian that includes one-gluon exchange, confinement, and Goldstone-boson exchange interactions, previously applied to heavy meson spectra and decays. They compute the form factors \(h_{+}\), \(h_{-}\), \(h_{V}\), \(h_{A1}\), \(h_{A2}\), and \(h_{A3}\) for the \(B\to D^{\prime(\ast)}\) transitions, then integrate the differential decay widths over the kinematic range to obtain branching ratios. For the non-leptonic decay \(\bar{B}^{0}\to D^{\prime+}\pi^{-}\), they use the factorization approximation to relate the branching ratio to the form factor \(f_{+}(0)\).

## Key numbers

- Sum of semileptonic branching ratios: \(\mathcal{B}(B^{+}\to D^{\prime(\ast)}l^{+}\nu_{l}) = (0.109 \pm 0.016)\%\).
- Individual branching ratios: \(\mathcal{B}(B^{+}\to D^{\prime}l^{+}\nu_{l}) = (0.012 \pm 0.006)\%\) and \(\mathcal{B}(B^{+}\to D^{\prime\ast}l^{+}\nu_{l}) = (0.097 \pm 0.015)\%\).
- Ratio of non-leptonic branching fractions: \(\mathcal{B}(\bar{B}^{0}\to D^{\prime+}\pi^{-}) / \mathcal{B}(\bar{B}^{0}\to D^{+}\pi^{-}) = 0.011 \pm 0.004\).
- Predicted \(\mathcal{B}(\bar{B}^{0}\to D^{\prime+}\pi^{-}) \sim 3 \times 10^{-5}\).
- Form factors at maximum recoil: \(F(w_{\rm max}) = 0.2\) and \(G(w_{\rm max}) = 0.1\), compatible with the light cone sum rule estimates of Bernlochner (2012) (\(F(w_{\rm max}) = 0.25 \pm 0.15\), \(G(w_{\rm max}) = 0.15 \pm 0.1\)).
- Strong decay width of \(D(2550)\) (as \(2\,0^{-}\)): total \(132.07\) MeV, with \(D^{\ast}\pi\) channel at \(131.90\) MeV.
- Strong decay width of \(D^{\ast}(2600)\) (as \(2\,1^{-}\)): total \(96.91\) MeV, with \(D^{\ast}\pi\) channel at \(54.10\) MeV and \(D\pi\) channel at \(10.84\) MeV.
- Ratio of branching fractions for \(D^{\ast}(2600)\) decays: \(\mathcal{B}(D^{\ast}(2600)^{0} \to D^{+}\pi^{-}) / \mathcal{B}(D^{\ast}(2600)^{0} \to D^{\ast+}\pi^{-}) = 0.20\), compared to the experimental value \(0.32 \pm 0.02 \pm 0.09\) from BaBar.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous constituent quark model studies of semileptonic and non-leptonic \(B\) decays into orbitally excited charmed mesons (Segovia 2011). It directly tests the hypothesis of Bernlochner (2012) that large branching ratios for \(B\to D^{\prime(\ast)}\) decays could resolve the \(1.45\%\) discrepancy between inclusive and exclusive semileptonic \(B\) decay rates, and also address the "\(1/2\) vs \(3/2\)" puzzle. The results contradict the larger branching ratios found by Ebert (1999) using a relativistic quark model, but are consistent with earlier heavy quark effective theory calculations by Suzuki (1993) and the ISGW2 model by Scora (1995). The predicted non-leptonic decay \(\bar{B}^{0}\to D^{\prime+}\pi^{-}\) at the \(3\times 10^{-5}\) level is suggested as an experimentally accessible test of the form factors at \(B\)-factories or LHCb.
