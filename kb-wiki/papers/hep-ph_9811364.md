# Quark Propagation in a Quark-Gluon Plasma with Gluon Condensate

> We present a calculation of the thermal quark propagator taking the gluon condensate above the critical temperature into account. The quark dispersion relation following from this propagator, describing two massive modes, is discussed.

# Quark Propagation in a Quark-Gluon Plasma with Gluon Condensate

## Key claim

The thermal quark propagator and its dispersion relation, computed by combining non-perturbative lattice input for the gluon condensate above $T_c$ with perturbative methods, yields two massive quark modes whose effective mass is approximately $1.15\,T$ between $1.1\,T_c$ and $4\,T_c$, but this result is derived from pure gauge lattice data and assumes the lowest Matsubara mode approximation, which is valid only when the quark momentum is not much larger than $T_c$.

## Method

The authors compute the quark self-energy at finite temperature using the imaginary time formalism, expanding the quark propagator for small loop momenta $k\ll p$ and keeping only the lowest Matsubara mode ($k_0=0$). They relate moments of the non-perturbative gluon propagator (with perturbative parts subtracted to avoid double counting with Hard Thermal Loop results) to the chromoelectric and chromomagnetic condensates, which are extracted from lattice measurements of the plaquette expectation values $\Delta_\sigma$ and $\Delta_\tau$ in pure SU(3) gauge theory. The quark dispersion relations are then obtained from the poles of the propagator decomposed into helicity eigenstates.

## Key numbers

- The effective quark mass is $m_{eff} = 1.15\,T$ for temperatures between $T=1.1\,T_c$ and $4\,T_c$, independent of the coupling $g$.
- The electric condensate $(\alpha_s/\pi)\langle \mathbf{E}^2\rangle_T$ increases approximately as $T^{3.5}$ between $T=1.1\,T_c$ and $4\,T_c$.
- The zero-temperature gluon condensate is $\langle G^2\rangle_{T=0} = (2.5 \pm 1.0)\, T_c^4$.
- For large momenta $p\gg T$, the $p_0^+$ mode approaches $p_0^+ = p + c_1$ with $c_1 = [(2\pi/9)\alpha_s (\langle \mathbf{E}^2\rangle_T + \langle \mathbf{B}^2\rangle_T/5)]^{1/4}$.
- For small momenta $p\rightarrow 0$, the dispersion behaves as $p_0^\pm = m_{eff} \pm c_2\, p$ with $c_2 = (3/4)\langle \mathbf{E}^2\rangle_T/(\langle \mathbf{E}^2\rangle_T + \langle \mathbf{B}^2\rangle_T)$.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum-rule approach of Lavelle and Schaden (1988) from zero temperature to finite temperature, combining it with lattice results for the thermal gluon condensate from Boyd et al. (1996). The resulting dispersion relations show two massive branches similar to those from Hard Thermal Loop resummation (Braaten and Pisarski, 1990; Braaten, Pisarski, and Yuan, 1991), including a plasmino mode with a minimum, but the effective mass here is proportional to $T$ near $T_c$ rather than to $gT$ as in the HTL case. The results enable calculations of dilepton and photon production rates from a quark-gluon plasma using the non-perturbative quark propagator, and suggest that a similar treatment for the gluon propagator could address the static magnetic screening problem.
