# Photoproduction at collider energies: from RHIC and HERA to the LHC

> We present the mini-proceedings of the workshop on ``Photoproduction at collider energies: from RHIC and HERA to the LHC'' held at the European Centre for Theoretical Studies in Nuclear Physics and Related Areas (ECT*, Trento) from January 15 to 19, 2007. The workshop gathered both theorists and experimentalists to discuss the current status of investigations of high-energy photon-induced processes at different colliders (HERA, RHIC, and Tevatron) as well as preparations for extension of these studies at the LHC. The main physics topics covered were: (i) small-$x$ QCD in photoproduction studies with protons and in electromagnetic (aka. ultraperipheral) nucleus-nucleus collisions, (ii) hard diffraction physics at hadron colliders, and (iii) photon-photon collisions at very high energies: electroweak and beyond the Standard Model processes. These mini-proceedings consist of an introduction and short summaries of the talks presented at the meeting.

## Key claim

The workshop mini-proceedings summarize the current status and future prospects of high-energy photon-induced processes at HERA, RHIC, and Tevatron, with preparations for extending these studies to the LHC, covering small-x QCD, hard diffraction, and photon-photon collisions for electroweak and beyond-Standard-Model physics.

## Method

The workshop gathered 35 theorists and experimentalists for 30 presentations organized around three main topics: (i) small-x QCD in photoproduction with protons and in ultraperipheral nucleus-nucleus collisions, (ii) hard diffraction physics at hadron colliders, and (iii) photon-photon collisions at very high energies. The proceedings consist of an introduction and short summaries of each talk, including relevant references, rather than full-fledged papers.

## Key numbers

* Maximum quasi-real photon energies from ultra-relativistic beams range from approximately 3 GeV at RHIC (sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV) to approximately 2000 GeV at the LHC (sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5-14 TeV)
* Photon fluxes from lead or gold nuclei are Z^2 ≈ 7000 times larger than those from electron or proton beams at the same energies, due to Z ≈ 80 proton charges
* CDF measured the cross section for exclusive p-pbar -> p + e+e- + pbar at sqrt(s) = 1.96 TeV as 1.6 (+0.5/-0.3)(stat) ± 0.3(syst) pb, consistent with the theoretical prediction of 1.71 ± 0.01 pb
* The observation of exclusive gamma-gamma -> e+e- at CDF had a significance of 5.5 sigma, with 16 candidate events observed against 1.9 ± 0.3 expected background events
* For exclusive vector meson production at LHC, the accessible W(gamma N) range is 30 GeV to 1 TeV, exceeding HERA by a factor of 10 in both gamma-p and gamma-A scattering
* The energy range for large-t vector meson photoproduction at LHC covers s(dipole-parton) from 10^3 to 10^6 GeV^2, where the cross section is expected to change by a factor of at least 20

## Context

This workshop extends the line of photoproduction studies from HERA ep collider measurements of QCD parton structure and evolution at low-x, and from RHIC ultraperipheral collisions of gold nuclei producing rho, J/psi, and e+e- pairs. The discussions build on prior work by Frankfurt, Strikman, and Zhalov (2002) on color transparency and black disk regime in heavy ion collisions, and by Klasen (2002) on hard photoproduction theory. The meeting enables preparations for LHC studies of photon-hadron and photon-photon processes at energies one order of magnitude larger than previous colliders, probing parton distributions at still lower x values and opening windows to electroweak and beyond-Standard-Model physics.
