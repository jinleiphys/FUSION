# Out-of-equilibrium Chiral Magnetic Effect via Kubo formulas

> In this proceedings article, we present the first steps towards the determination of the out-of-equilibrium conductivity of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) in the presence of strong interactions. Using linear response theory, we obtain an analytical expression for the spectral function associated with this effect at one-loop in perturbation theory. In addition, we provide a first estimate of the CME conductivity by calculating the associated Euclidean correlator using quenched Wilson fermions and dynamical staggered fermions in physical Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) simulations. In particular, we focus on the midpoint of the correlator, which can be used as a proxy of the full conductivity. We present results in a wide range of temperatures, showing how this observable is suppressed at low temperatures, while at high temperatures it approaches the perturbation theory prediction.

# Out-of-equilibrium Chiral Magnetic Effect via Kubo formulas

## Key claim
The out-of-equilibrium CME conductivity, estimated via the midpoint of the Euclidean correlator, is severely suppressed at temperatures below the QCD crossover transition temperature (T_c = 155 MeV) and approaches the one-loop perturbation theory prediction at high temperatures, contradicting the conclusions of Buividovich (2024) using two-color QCD simulations.

## Method
The authors used linear response theory to derive a Kubo formula relating the out-of-equilibrium CME conductivity to the spectral function, which was computed at one-loop in perturbation theory using free fermion propagators in a magnetic background. Lattice QCD simulations were performed using two fermion discretizations: quenched Wilson fermions with m_pi ≈ 750 MeV and dynamical staggered fermions at the physical point (2+1 flavors), focusing on the midpoint of the Euclidean CME correlator as a proxy for the full conductivity.

## Key numbers
- The CME midpoint observable C_CME^MP is suppressed below the QCD crossover temperature T_c = 155 MeV, with a pronounced increase above this temperature.
- In the quenched Wilson simulations, suppression begins around the quenched transition temperature T_c^qnch ≈ 270 MeV.
- The analytical one-loop result for the spectral function contains a term proportional to m^2/(π^2) * tanh(|ω|/(4T)) / (ω sqrt(ω^2 - 4m^2)) for ω^2 > 4m^2.
- In the massless limit m/T → 0, the coefficient α(m/T) approaches 1/(2π^2).
- The free-field analytical midpoint value is given by C_CME^MP(m/T) = 1/(2π^2) ∫_0^∞ dp [1 + cosh(sqrt(p^2 + (m/T)^2))]^{-1}.

## Context
This work extends the study of out-of-equilibrium CME conductivity from non-interacting fermions (Kharzeev 2009, Hou 2011, Banerjee 2022) and two-color QCD (Buividovich 2024) to full QCD with physical quark masses. The results disagree with the conclusions of Buividovich (2024) regarding the behavior below T_c. The observed temperature dependence closely resembles that of the equilibrium Chiral Separation Effect (CSE) conductivity found by Brandt (2023), suggesting the CME and CSE correlators may be equal at the midpoint even non-perturbatively. The work enables future spectral reconstruction techniques to extract the full frequency-dependent conductivity.
