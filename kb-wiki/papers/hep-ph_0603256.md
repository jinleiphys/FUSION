# Susceptibilities near the QCD (tri)critical point

> Based on the proper-time renormalization group approach, the scalar and the quark number susceptibilities in the vicinity of possible critical end points of the hadronic phase diagram are investigated in the two-flavor quark-meson model. After discussing the quark-mass dependence of the location of such points, the critical behavior of the in-medium meson masses and quark number density are calculated. The universality classes of the end points are determined by calculating the critical exponents of the susceptibilities. In order to numerically estimate the influence of fluctuations we compare all quantities with results from a mean-field approximation. It is concluded that the region in the phase diagram where the susceptibilities are enhanced is more compressed around the critical end point if fluctuations are included.

## Key claim

The paper uses the proper-time renormalization group (PTRG) approach to show that, when fluctuations are included, the region in the QCD phase diagram where susceptibilities are enhanced is more compressed around the critical endpoint (CEP) than mean-field approximations predict, though the mean-field approximation itself fails to produce a tricritical point (TCP) in the chiral limit for the chosen parameter set.

## Method

The authors study the two-flavor quark-meson model, first in a mean-field approximation where meson fields are replaced by their expectation values and only quark/antiquark fluctuations are retained, then using the proper-time renormalization group (PTRG) method to include mesonic fluctuations. They compute the grand canonical potential, solve the gap equation for the sigma-field expectation value, and calculate in-medium meson masses, quark number density, scalar susceptibility, and quark number susceptibility as functions of temperature T and quark chemical potential μ. Critical exponents are extracted from power-law fits near the CEP.

## Key numbers

* CEP location in mean-field approximation: T_c = 91.4 MeV, μ_c = 223 MeV for physical pion mass M_π = 138 MeV, constituent quark mass M_q = 300 MeV, sigma mass M_σ = 600 MeV, and pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV.
* Critical exponent ε for the quark number susceptibility χ_q along a path parallel to the μ-axis at fixed T_c: ε = 0.64 ± 0.02, consistent with the mean-field prediction ε = 2/3 (since δ = 3).
* For a massless free quark gas, the quark number susceptibility limit is χ_q^free = (ν_q/6)[T² + 3μ²/π²] with ν_q = 12 (2 flavors × 3 colors × 2 spin states).
* Reducing the pion mass to M_π = 69 MeV (half the physical value) causes the CEP to disappear in mean-field, with first-order transition for all T and μ.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous PTRG analysis of the quark-meson model (Schaefer 2005) by focusing on the size of critical regions around the TCP and CEP. It contrasts with mean-field studies such as those by Scavenius (2001) and Fujii (2004), which fail to reproduce the expected O(4) universality class in the chiral limit. The results enable more realistic estimates of the critical region size for heavy-ion collision experiments searching for the CEP, complementing lattice QCD studies by Fodor (2002) and Forcrand (2002) that suffer from the fermion sign problem at finite μ.
