# Meson Properties in the 1/N_c-corrected NJL model

> Properties of mesons are investigated within the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. We include meson-loop corrections, which are generated via a systematic 1/N_c-expansion in next-to-leading order. We show that our scheme is consistent with chiral symmetry, in particular with the Goldstone theorem and the Gell-Mann Oakes Renner relation. The numerical part focuses on the pion and the rho-meson sector. For the latter the 1/N_c-corrections are crucial in order to include the dominant rho -> pi pi-decay channel, while the leading-order approximation only contains unphysical q\bar q-decay channels. We show that a satisfactory description of the pion electromagnetic form factor can be obtained. Similarities and differences to hadronic models are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including meson-loop corrections via a systematic \(1/N_c\) expansion up to next-to-leading order in the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model yields a consistent chiral-symmetry-preserving scheme that can simultaneously fit pion properties and the \(\rho\)-meson spectral function, provided the meson-loop cutoff \(\Lambda_M\) is chosen such that the constituent quark mass is large enough to push the unphysical \(q\bar q\) threshold above the \(\rho\)-meson peak.

## Method

The authors start from a two-flavor NJL Lagrangian with scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector four-fermion interactions. They compute the quark self-energy and meson polarization functions in a strict \(1/N_c\) expansion: leading order corresponds to the Hartree approximation for quarks and the random phase approximation (RPA) for mesons, while next-to-leading order includes meson-loop corrections (quark self-energy diagrams and four types of polarization diagrams involving triangle and box quark loops). The RPA meson propagators are regularized with a quark-loop cutoff, and the meson loops are regularized with an independent cutoff \(\Lambda_M\). The scheme is shown analytically to satisfy the Goldstone theorem and the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation up to next-to-leading order. Numerically, parameters are fixed by fitting the pion decay constant \(f_\pi\), the quark condensate \(\langle \bar\psi\psi \rangle\), and the \(\rho\)-meson mass and width.

## Key numbers

* Constituent quark mass \(m \sim 300\) MeV (typical value cited for the NJL model, used as reference for the unphysical \(q\bar q\) threshold).
* \(\rho\)-meson mass: 770 MeV (physical value used as target for the fit).
* Leading-order pion decay constant \(f_\pi^{(0)} \sim 100\)–150 MeV (range used to estimate the constituent quark mass from the effective \(\rho\pi\pi\) coupling constant).
* Effective \(\rho\pi\pi\) coupling constant in the static limit: \(g^2_{\rho\pi\pi} = 6 m^2 / f_\pi^{(0)2}\), yielding \(m \approx \sqrt{6} f_\pi^{(0)}\) (estimate for the constituent quark mass from hadronic models).

## Context

This work extends earlier NJL studies that were limited to leading order in \(1/N_c\) (e.g., the original papers by Nambu and Jona-Lasinio and reviews by Vogl, Klevansky, and Hatsuda), where the \(\rho\)-meson could only decay into unphysical \(q\bar q\) channels. It builds on symmetry-conserving \(1/N_c\) expansion schemes proposed by Dmitrašinović et al. (1995) and Nikolov et al. (1996), but differs by performing a strict expansion without self-consistent iteration of the gap equation, avoiding tachyonic RPA pions in the chiral limit. The numerical results enable a direct comparison with hadronic models (e.g., vector dominance models by Brown and Herrmann, and Chanfray and Schuck) and provide a framework for future studies of medium modifications of vector mesons and dilepton production in hot/dense matter.
