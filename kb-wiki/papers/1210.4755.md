# Odd parity bottom-flavored baryon resonances

> The LHCb Collaboration has recently observed two narrow baryon resonances with beauty. Their masses and decay modes look consistent with the quark model orbitally excited states Lambda_b(5912) and Lambda*_b(5920), with quantum numbers J^P=1/2^- and 3/2^-, respectively. We predict the existence of these states within a unitarized meson-baryon coupled-channel dynamical model, which implements heavy-quark spin symmetry. Masses, quantum numbers and couplings of these resonances to the different meson-baryon channels are obtained. We find that the resonances Lambda^0_b(5912) and Lambda^0_b(5920) are heavy-quark spin symmetry partners, which naturally explains their approximate mass degeneracy. Corresponding bottom-strange baryon resonances are predicted at Xi_b(6035.4) (J^P=1/2^-) and Xi_b(6043.3) (J^P=3/2^-). The two Lambda_b and two Xi_b resonances complete a multiplet of SU(3)-flavor times heavy-quark spin symmetry.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the two narrow bottom baryon resonances observed by LHCb, Lambda_b(5912) and Lambda_b*(5920), are dynamically generated molecular states in a unitarized meson-baryon coupled-channel model, rather than conventional quark-model orbitally excited states, and that they form a heavy-quark spin symmetry (HQSS) doublet which naturally explains their approximate mass degeneracy.

## Method

The authors use a unitarized meson-baryon coupled-channel dynamical model with s-wave tree-level contact interactions derived from an extended Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction that incorporates both chiral symmetry (for Goldstone bosons) and heavy-quark spin symmetry (for bottom hadrons). The Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved with a renormalized two-particle propagator, and poles of the scattering amplitude on the second Riemann sheet are identified as resonances, with masses, widths, and couplings extracted from the pole positions.

## Key numbers

- Mass of Lambda_b(5912) predicted at 5910.1 MeV (J^P = 1/2^-), compared to LHCb experimental value 5911.95 +/- 0.12 (stat) +/- 0.03 (syst) +/- 0.66 (Lambda_b mass) MeV
- Mass of Lambda_b*(5920) predicted at 5921.5 MeV (J^P = 3/2^-), compared to LHCb experimental value 5919.76 +/- 0.07 (stat) +/- 0.02 (syst) +/- 0.66 (Lambda_b mass) MeV
- Predicted mass for Xi_b(1/2^-) resonance at 6035.4 MeV
- Predicted mass for Xi_b*(3/2^-) resonance at 6043.3 MeV
- Estimated width for Lambda_b(5912) around 8 keV and for Lambda_b*(5920) around 12 keV, consistent with experimental upper limits of 0.82 MeV and 0.72 MeV at 95% confidence level
- Experimental ratio of production cross sections sigma(pp -> Lambda_b*(5920)) / sigma(pp -> Lambda_b(5912)) = 3.0 +/- 1.0, compared to the HQSS expectation of 2

## Context

This work extends the unitarized coupled-channel approach previously applied to light-flavor and charm baryon resonances (Garcia-Recio 2008, Romanets 2012, Gamermann 2010) to the bottom sector, where the model successfully predicted the Lambda_c(2595) and Lambda_c(2625) states. The results contradict quark-model predictions by Capstick and Isgur (1986) and Garcilazo (2007) that treat these states as orbitally excited three-quark configurations, instead supporting a molecular interpretation. The predicted Xi_b and Xi_b* resonances at 6035.4 MeV and 6043.3 MeV complete a SU(3)-flavor times HQSS multiplet and provide targets for future LHCb searches.
