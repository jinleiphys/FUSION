# Probing chiral-even and chiral-odd leading twist quark generalised parton distributions through the exclusive photoproduction of a $ γρ$ pair

> We extend our studies of a new class of $2 \to 3$ exclusive processes using the collinear factorisation framework by considering the exclusive photoproduction of a $γ\,ρ$ pair, in the kinematics where the pair has a large invariant mass, {and the outgoing $ ρ$-meson has a sufficiently large transverse momentum to not resonate with the nucleon}. We cover the whole kinematical range from medium energies in fixed target experiments to very large energies of colliders, by considering the experimental conditions of JLab~12-GeV, COMPASS, future EIC and LHC (in ultra-peripheral collisions) cases. One of the main interest in studying the present process is that it provides access to both chiral-even and chiral-odd GPDs, depending on the polarisation of the outgoing $ρ$-meson, both at leading twist. Our analysis covers both neutral and charged $ ρ$-mesons. We find that the order of magnitude of the obtained cross sections are sufficiently large for a dedicated experimental analysis to be performed, especially at JLab, for both longitudinally and transversely polarised $ρ$. Furthermore, we compute the linear photon beam polarisation asymmetry {which is} sizeable for a longitudinally polarised meson. These predictions are obtained for both asymptotic distribution amplitude (DA) and the \textit{holographic} DA.

## Key claim

The exclusive photoproduction of a $\gamma\rho$ pair with large invariant mass provides access to both chiral-even and chiral-odd leading-twist quark generalised parton distributions (GPDs), depending on the polarisation of the outgoing $\rho$-meson, with cross sections sufficiently large for dedicated experimental analysis at JLab, COMPASS, future EIC, and LHC in ultra-peripheral collisions, provided the kinematical cuts $-u',-t' > 1$ GeV$^2$ and $-t < 0.5$ GeV$^2$ are applied to ensure the hard scale and avoid resonance regions.

## Method

The authors compute the leading-order collinear factorisation amplitude for the $2 \to 3$ process $\gamma + N \to \gamma + N' + \rho$, using 20 Feynman diagrams organised into four QED gauge-invariant sets, with the hard scale provided by the large relative transverse momentum of the outgoing photon and $\rho$-meson. The chiral-even contribution is separated into vector and axial GPD sectors (structures $SS$ and $SP$), while the chiral-odd contribution uses transversity GPDs, with the $\rho$-meson distribution amplitudes taken in both asymptotic and holographic forms; the GPDs are constructed from GRV-98 and GRSV-2000 PDFs with a dipole $t$-dependence and fixed factorisation scale $\mu_F^2 = 10$ GeV$^2$.

## Key numbers

- The kinematical cuts $-u', -t' > 1$ GeV$^2$ and $-t < 0.5$ GeV$^2$ ensure $M^2_{\gamma\rho} > 2$ GeV$^2$ and $M^2_{\rho N'} > 3.11$ GeV$^2$, pushing the $\rho N'$ invariant mass well above the $\Delta$ baryon mass squared of $m_\Delta^2 \approx 1.52$ GeV$^2$.
- The $\rho$-meson decay constants used are $f_\rho^\parallel = 216$ MeV for the longitudinally polarised case and $f_\rho^\bot = 160$ MeV for the transversely polarised case.
- The asymptotic distribution amplitude is $\phi^{\rm as}(z) = 6z(1-z)$ and the holographic distribution amplitude is $\phi^{\rm hol}(z) = \frac{8}{\pi}\sqrt{z(1-z)}$, both normalised to 1.
- The normalisation coefficient for the chiral-even amplitude is $C^\parallel = -\frac{4}{9} f_\rho^\parallel \, \alpha_{\rm em} \, \alpha_s \, \pi^2$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of $2 \to 3$ exclusive processes in collinear factorisation (Duplancic 2022, Boussarie 2016, Duplancic 2018) from neutral $\rho^0$ to charged $\rho^\pm$ mesons, and from chiral-even GPDs to include chiral-odd (transversity) GPDs. The factorisation theorem for this process was recently proved by Qiu (2022), and the results enable experimental extraction of the poorly known chiral-odd GPDs at JLab 12-GeV, COMPASS, the future EIC, and LHC ultra-peripheral collisions, complementing DVCS and meson electroproduction studies.
