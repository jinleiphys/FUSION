# Unification of the physics of nucleons and nuclei

> I outline an ambitious program which aims to achieve a unified description of nucleon and nuclear properties based on one chiral effective field theory.

## Key claim

The paper outlines an ambitious program to achieve a unified description of nucleon and nuclear properties from a single chiral effective field theory (EFT), but acknowledges that the scheme is still under development and its ultimate range in mass number, photon virtuality, and pion energy remains to be determined.

## Method

The author proposes using chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) as the working tool, organizing the effective Lagrangian into purely mesonic, meson–nucleon, and nucleonic sectors, each with a chiral expansion in powers of a small parameter Q (small momenta and quark masses). For systems with baryon number larger than one, an additional non-perturbative resummation is required to generate large scattering lengths and shallow nuclear bound states. The approach is applied to electron scattering off nucleons and deuterium, and to pion–nucleon and pion–deuteron scattering, using systematic power counting to estimate theoretical uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- The dimension-two low-energy constant \(c_4\) from \(\mathcal{L}_{\pi N}^{(2)}\) is determined to be \(c_4 = 3.36 \ldots 3.73\,\text{GeV}^{-1}\) from \(\pi N\) scattering partial-wave analyses; resonance saturation gives \(c_4 = (1.9 + 0.1 + 1.6)\,\text{GeV}^{-1} = 3.6\,\text{GeV}^{-1}\).
- The neutron electric form factor \(G_E^n(Q^2)\) is described accurately for \(Q^2 \lesssim 0.4\,\text{GeV}^2\) using a fourth-order calculation with infrared regularization.
- The deuteron binding momentum is \(\gamma = 45\,\text{MeV}\), much smaller than the pion mass \(M_\pi \simeq 140\,\text{MeV}\).
- The real part of the \(\pi d\) scattering length from PSI is \(\text{Re}\,a_{\pi d} = -0.0261 (\pm 0.0005)\,M_\pi^{-1}\).
- The S-wave \(\pi N\) scattering lengths determined from pionic hydrogen and deuterium are \(a^{-} = [0.0936 (\pm 0.0011)]\,M_\pi^{-1}\) and \(a^{+} = [-0.0029 (\pm 0.0009)]\,M_\pi^{-1}\) (using the most recent systematic determination of the pionic hydrogen level shift).

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT approach from single-nucleon systems (pion–nucleon scattering, nucleon form factors) to few-nucleon systems (deuteron electromagnetic form factors, pion–deuteron scattering), building on earlier developments by Weinberg, Beane (2003), and Epelbaum (2003). It enables a systematic framework for precision calculations of light nuclei with controlled theoretical uncertainties, and the author notes that extension to systems with \(A=3,4\) is under way, while open questions remain about when to include explicit degrees of freedom such as the delta or vector mesons.
