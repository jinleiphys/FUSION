# Quantum Transport Theory

> The problem of thermalization of a quark -- gluon plasma is addressed in the framework of thermal field theory. Within a simple approximation, the full quantum relaxation problem is solved and compared to the Boltzmann solution. Memory effects and a slowdown of the relaxation process are the results, they can be partially described by using a generalized kinetic equation without quasi-particle approximation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that quantum memory effects cause a significant slowdown of the thermalization process in a quark-gluon plasma compared to the standard Boltzmann equation prediction, but this result is conditional on a simple approximation where the gluon background is treated as externally driven and the back-reaction of quarks on the gluon distribution is neglected.

## Method

The author solves the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the quark propagator within thermal field theory, using a 2x2 matrix formalism (Schwinger-Keldysh or Thermo Field Dynamics) and a Wigner representation with gradient expansion. The quark spectral function is approximated by a Lorentzian with time-dependent width and mass parameters, and the gluon self-energy is modeled as a time-dependent step function representing instantaneous or fast heating. The resulting quantum transport equation is solved for the generalized distribution function of slow quarks and compared to the Boltzmann equation solution and a generalized kinetic equation truncated to first order in the gradient expansion.

## Key numbers

- The time to reach approximately 86% of the equilibrium quark occupation number is 14.7 fm/c in the quantum transport case, compared to 8.2 fm/c in the Boltzmann case (for parameters g=0.12, T_i=1 MeV, T_f=200 MeV, m=10 MeV).
- The delay time in the quantum transport solution is almost doubled relative to the delay time in the spectral width parameter gamma_t.
- The heating times considered are instantaneous (Lambda=0) and fast (Lambda=4 fm/c).
- The initial gluon temperature is T_i=1 MeV and the final temperature is T_f=200 MeV.
- The quark mass is m=10 MeV and the coupling is g=0.12.

## Context

This work extends the non-equilibrium quantum field theory approach to thermalization, building on the Narnhofer-Thirring theorem (Narnhofer and Thirring, 1983) which states that interacting systems at finite temperature cannot be described by particles with a sharp dispersion law. The results contradict the standard quasi-particle approximation used in Boltzmann transport theory and support earlier work by Danielewicz (1984) and Henning (1993) on quantum relaxation and memory effects. The paper enables future investigation of whether quantum memory effects substantially hinder thermalization of a quark-gluon plasma over long time scales, though the author notes that realistic estimates must account for cooling of the bosonic background.
