# S-wave Pairing of $Λ$ Hyperons in Dense Matter

> In this work we calculate the $^1S_0$ gap energies of $Λ$ hyperons in neutron star matter. The calculation is based on a solution of the BCS gap equation for an effective G-matrix parameterization of the $Λ-Λ$ interaction with a nuclear matter background, presented recently by Lanskoy and Yamamoto. We find that a gap energy of a few tenths of MeV is expected for $Λ$ Fermi momenta up to about 1.3 fm$^{-1}$. Implications for neutron star matter are examined, and suggest the existence of a $Λ$ $^1S_0$ superfluid between the threshold baryon density for $Λ$ formation and the baryon density where the $Λ$ fraction reaches $15-20%$.

# S-wave Pairing of $\Lambda$ Hyperons in Dense Matter

## Key claim

The authors calculate that a $^1S_0$ superfluid gap of a few tenths of MeV exists for $\Lambda$ hyperons in neutron star matter, but they explicitly state that these results must be treated as preliminary estimates because the effective interaction is based on hypernuclei experiments at nuclear densities near $\rho_0$ and does not include relativistic corrections or particle-hole correlations.

## Method

The authors solve the BCS gap equation for the $^1S_0$ channel using a three-range Gaussian G-matrix parameterization of the $\Lambda-\Lambda$ interaction in a nuclear matter background, taken from Lanskoy and Yamamoto (1997) and derived from Nijmegen OBE potentials. The single-particle energies are calculated with first-order Hartree-Fock corrections in the decoupling approximation, using the bare $\Lambda$ mass of 1115.6 MeV. The gap equation is solved iteratively with exponential spacing of integration points around the $\Lambda$ Fermi momentum.

## Key numbers

- Gap energy of a few tenths of MeV is expected for $\Lambda$ Fermi momenta up to about 1.3 fm$^{-1}$.
- Maximum gap energy of 0.8-0.9 MeV is achieved for a $\Lambda$ fraction of about 5% of the total baryon population.
- The $\Lambda$ $^1S_0$ superfluid is expected to exist between the threshold baryon density for $\Lambda$ formation (about 2.5 $\rho_0$, where $\rho_0 \approx 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$) and the baryon density where the $\Lambda$ fraction reaches 15-20%.
- The critical temperature $T_c$ is related to the zero-temperature gap by $k_B T_c \approx 0.57 \Delta_F(T=0)$.
- For a nuclear matter density of $\rho_N = 2.5 \rho_0$, the gap energy for a given $k_F(\Lambda)$ increases with increasing $\rho_N$.

## Context

This work extends the study of baryon pairing in neutron stars, which previously focused on nucleon pairing (Migdal, 1959; Takatsuka, 1971; Hoffberg, 1970; and more recent works by Wambach, Ainsworth, and Pines, and by Elgaroy et al.), to the case of $\Lambda$ hyperons. The authors use the G-matrix parameterization of Lanskoy and Yamamoto (1997), which reproduces the experimental bond energy $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 4.9 \pm 0.7$ MeV from doubly-strange hypernuclei observed in KEK experiments. The results enable future studies of hyperon direct Urca cooling suppression in neutron stars, which has been identified as potentially too rapid by Schaab et al., Haensel et al., and Nomoto et al. if hyperons are not superfluid.
