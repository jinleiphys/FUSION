# Chemical freeze-out parametrization with mean field repulsive hadron resonance gas model

> We have examined the chemical freeze-out surface of the heavy-ion collision experiments within an interacting hadron resonance gas model. By considering repulsive interaction among hadrons in the mean-field level, we have suitably parameterized the freeze-out surface by fitting the yield data of mid-rapidity for the most central collision, for the collision energy available in AGS, RHIC (BES), and LHC programs. To suitably account for the repulsive interaction among mesons and (anti-) baryons, we have introduced phenomenological parameters $K_M$ and $K_B$ in the freeze-out parametrization. Although a finite value of these two parameters seem to be necessary to have an improved normalized \emph{chi-square}, the effect on the rest of the parameters like temperature and relevant chemical potentials seem to be within the standard variance.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including mean-field repulsive interactions among hadrons (with separate strength parameters for mesons, \(K_M\), and baryons, \(K_B\)) improves the normalized chi-square fit to experimental hadron yield data across a wide range of collision energies, although the extracted freeze-out temperature and chemical potentials remain within standard variance of the ideal hadron resonance gas results.

## Method

The authors fit mid-rapidity hadron yield data (\(dN/dY\)) for the most central collisions from AGS, RHIC (Beam Energy Scan), and LHC experiments using a mean-field hadron resonance gas (MFHRG) model. The repulsive interaction is incorporated by shifting the chemical potential of each species by \(\mu_{\text{eff},i} = \mu_i - K n\), with separate mean-field coefficients \(K_M\) for mesons and \(K_B\) for baryons and anti-baryons. The freeze-out parameters (temperature \(T\), baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B\), strange chemical potential \(\mu_S\), charge chemical potential \(\mu_Q\), and the freeze-out volume) are extracted via \(\chi^2\) minimization, with \(\mu_S\) and \(\mu_Q\) fixed by strangeness neutrality and a fixed net baryon-to-charge ratio.

## Key numbers

* For \(K_M = 0\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\), a non-zero \(K_B\) is required to achieve a better \(\chi^2\) per degree of freedom; the extracted \(K_B\) values range from approximately 100 to 800 MeV fm\(^{-3}\) (considering variances) across the collision energies studied.
* For the three fixed values of \(K_M\) (0, 50, and 100 MeV fm\(^{-3}\)), the freeze-out temperature saturates around 160 MeV at higher RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies, which is approximately 5 MeV higher than the ideal HRG result.
* The freeze-out volume (presented as freeze-out radius) shows a non-monotonic behavior with collision energy and increases for higher values of \(K_M\) and \(K_B\).

## Context

This work extends previous applications of the mean-field repulsive HRG model, where earlier studies (Sollfrank, 1996) fixed \(K_B = 450\) MeV fm\(^{-3}\) for S + Au collisions at CERN-SPS, and recent lattice QCD studies (Huovinen, 2017; Kadam, 2019) constrained \(K_B\) from charge susceptibilities. The present analysis provides, for the first time, the collision energy dependence of both \(K_M\) and \(K_B\) by fitting experimental yield data from AGS through LHC energies, enabling improved parameterization of the chemical freeze-out surface for future heavy-ion collision studies.
