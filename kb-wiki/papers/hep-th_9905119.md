# Reduction of relativistic three-body kinematics

> The Klein-Gordon system describing three scalar particles without interaction is cast into a new form, by transformation of the momenta. Two redundant degrees of freedom are eliminated; we are left with a covariant equation for a reduced wave function with three-dimensional arguments. This new formulation of the mass-shell constraints is equivalent to the original KG system in a sector characterized by positivity of the energies and, if the mass differences are not too large, by a moderately relativistic regime. Then, mutual interaction is introduced and produces a tractable model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the free Klein-Gordon system for three scalar particles can be transformed into an equivalent covariant equation with three-dimensional arguments, eliminating two redundant degrees of freedom, but this equivalence holds only in a sector characterized by positivity of all energies and, if mass differences are not too large, by a moderately relativistic regime.

## Method

The author starts with the three Klein-Gordon equations for noninteracting scalar particles in momentum representation, then introduces heliocentric variables (choosing particle 1 as reference) to define relative momenta \(y_2, y_3\) conjugate to relative coordinates. A point transformation in momentum space is constructed that maps the old relative momenta to new ones \(y'_2, y'_3\) such that the difference equations \(P_{12}\cdot y_{12} = P\cdot y'_{12}\) and \(P_{13}\cdot y_{13} = P\cdot y'_{13}\) hold, while keeping the spatial projections orthogonal to total momentum unchanged. The transformation is solved in closed form, and its invertibility is analyzed via a graphic analysis of a quartic equation, establishing a unique admissible solution under specified bounds on the parameters \(\sigma\) and \(\epsilon\).

## Key numbers

* The positive-energy conditions require \(\xi < 1/3\), \(\eta < 1/3\), and \(-1/3 < \xi + \eta\), where \(\xi = y_2 \cdot P / P^2\) and \(\eta = y_3 \cdot P / P^2\).
* The transformation is invertible provided \(\sigma\) lies in the open interval \((-3/4, 1/2)\) and \(\epsilon\) lies in the open interval \((-1/2, 1/2)\).
* For vanishing \(\epsilon\) (e.g., equal masses \(m_2 = m_3\)), the solution for \(X = \xi + \eta\) is \(X^+ = (2/3)(-1 + \sqrt{1 + \sigma})\).
* The leading-order expansion for small \(\sigma\) and \(\epsilon\) gives \(S = \sigma/3 + O(\sigma^2, \epsilon^2, \epsilon\sigma)\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic constraint dynamics approach of Sazdjian (1985, 1988) for eliminating relative times in \(n\)-body systems, specializing to the three-body case where a simplification not possible for \(n>3\) exists. The paper addresses the difficulty that the standard procedure for eliminating relative time in the two-body case fails for three particles, even for free motion. The result enables the introduction of mutual interactions to produce a tractable model with a reasonable nonrelativistic limit, at least for three equal masses.
