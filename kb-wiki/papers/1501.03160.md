# Neutrino-Nucleus Interactions at the LBNF Near Detector

> The reaction mechanisms for neutrino interactions with an $^{40}Ar$ nucleus with the LBNF flux are calculated with the Giessen-Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport-theoretical implementation of these interactions. Quasielastic scattering, many-body effects, pion production and absorption and Deep Inelastic Scattering are discussed; they all play a role at the LBNF energies and are experimentally entangled with each other. Quasielastic scattering makes up for only about 1/3 of the total cross section whereas pion production channels make up about 2/3 of the total. This underlines the need for a consistent description of the neutrino-nucleus reaction that treats all channels on an equal, consistent footing. The results discussed here can also serve as useful guideposts for the Intermediate Neutrino Program.

## Key claim

At LBNF energies, quasielastic scattering (CCQE) and pion production are experimentally entangled and cannot be separated by experimental means alone; any measurement of CCQE alone must rely on an event generator to subtract pion production channels, and the signal-to-background ratio for CCQE is only about 1:2, so uncertainties in the pion-channel description become amplified when extracting CCQE cross sections.

## Method

The author used the Giessen-Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport-theoretical framework to calculate neutrino interactions on an ⁴⁰Ar target, using the incoming flux expected for the Long-Baseline Neutrino Facility (LBNF). The framework treats nucleons semiclassically as particles bound in a coordinate- and momentum-dependent potential well with a local Thomas-Fermi momentum distribution, encodes theoretical transition rates for reactions on static nucleons, and Lorentz-boosts them according to the nucleon's momentum in the Fermi sea. Final state interactions are treated by solving approximately the Kadanoff-Baym equations of transport theory.

## Key numbers

- Quasielastic scattering makes up only about 30% of the total cross section, while pion production channels make up about 2/3 of the total.
- At the peak of the total cross section, around Q² = 0.2 GeV², CCQE, Δ excitation, and deep inelastic scattering (DIS) contribute roughly in equal parts to the total.
- At Q² > 0.3 GeV², DIS becomes the dominant component.
- In the 0π, 1p, Xn event sample, true 1-body CCQE makes up about 80% of the total cross section.
- The pion kinetic energy spectrum shows a peak at about 80 MeV.
- The 2p2h component contributes only at the 5% level for small Q², becoming comparable to other channels only at Q² = 1.5 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the author's prior GiBUU-based studies of neutrino-nucleus reactions (Mosel 2012, Leitner 2009, Buss 2011) to the higher energies of the LBNF, and it addresses the same entanglement of reaction channels previously identified at lower energies (Kim 1996, Leitner 2010). The results contradict the interpretation of large axial mass values (M_A ≈ 1.2–1.3 GeV) from experiments like T2K (Abe 2014) and MINOS (Adamson 2014) as being due to in-medium physics; instead, the author argues these values arise because the generators used (e.g., NEUT) lack a 2p2h component. The paper calls for development of a new precision-era generator, building on experience from existing generators and transport theory, and notes that upcoming experiments from the Intermediate Neutrino Program (MicroBooNE, MINERνA, CAPTAIN) could help verify generator ingredients.
