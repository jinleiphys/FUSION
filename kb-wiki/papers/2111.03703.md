# Transverse momentum dependent distributions in dijet and heavy hadron pair production at EIC

> We discuss the measurement of gluon transverse momentum distribution (TMD) in dijet and heavy hadron pair (HHP) production in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering. The factorization of these processes in impact parameter space shows the appearance of a specific new soft factor matrix element on top of angular a complex valued anomalous dimensions. We show in detail how these features can be treated consistently and we discuss a scale prescription for the evolution kernel of the dijet soft function. As a result we obtain phenomenological predictions for unpolarized and angular modulated cross-sections for the electron-ion collider (EIC) using current available information on unpolarized TMD.

## Key claim

The paper provides phenomenological predictions for gluon transverse momentum distributions (TMDs) in dijet and heavy hadron pair production at the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), but these predictions rely on the condition that the transverse momentum imbalance is much smaller than the average transverse momentum of the final particles (|r_T| << p_T) and that the partonic Mandelstam variables have no hierarchy (ŝ ∼ |t̂| ∼ |û|).

## Method

The authors factorize the cross-sections for dijet and heavy hadron pair production in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering in impact parameter (b) space, then Fourier transform back to momentum space by performing an angular integration over the angle φ_b between the impact parameter vector and the jet direction. They treat the angular-dependent and complex-valued anomalous dimensions that appear in the soft function, collinear-soft function, and heavy meson jet function by separating the evolution kernel into an angular-dependent part and an angle-independent part, showing that imaginary parts cancel upon angular integration. The phenomenological predictions are obtained using the Artemide code with existing unpolarized TMD extractions from Drell-Yan and semi-inclusive DIS experiments.

## Key numbers

- The factorization condition |r_T| << p_T is satisfied for p_T in the range [5, 40] GeV in the central rapidity region
- The rapidity scales that minimize hard logs are ζ₁ = p_T² and ζ₂ = 1
- The heavy quark jet function involves two parametrically different scales: μ₊ = m_Q and μ_𝒥 = m_Q × (r_T/p_T), requiring resummation of large logs
- The dijet soft function at NLO in the MS scheme for the (γ*g)-channel contains terms proportional to C_F and C_A with coefficients involving ln²(B μ² e^{2γ_E}/(-A_b)) and Li₂(1+A_b)

## Context

This work extends the factorization framework developed by del Castillo et al. (2020) for dijet and hadron pair production at electron-ion colliders to probe gluon TMDs. It builds upon previous studies by Dominguez et al. (2010) and Hatta et al. (2019) for dijet production, and by Zhu et al. (2013), Zhang et al. (2017), and Boer et al. (2010) for heavy-meson pair production. The paper enables future experimental measurements at EIC by providing theoretical predictions for unpolarized and angular-modulated cross-sections, using the TMD evolution kernel extracted from Drell-Yan and semi-inclusive DIS experiments by Scimemi and collaborators (2019).
