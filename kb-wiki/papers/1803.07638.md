# Photoproduction of light vector mesons in Xe-Xe ultraperipheral collisions at the LHC and the nuclear density of Xe-129

> We make predictions for cross sections of $ρ$ and $ϕ$ vector meson photoproduction in ultraperipheral Xe-Xe collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.44$ TeV. Analyzing the momentum transfer distribution of $ρ$ mesons in this process, we explore the feasibility of extracting the nuclear density of $^{129}$Xe, which is needed in searches for dark matter with Xenon-based detectors.

# Photoproduction of light vector mesons in Xe-Xe ultraperipheral collisions at the LHC and the nuclear density of Xe-129

## Key claim

The momentum transfer distribution of coherent ρ meson photoproduction in Xe-Xe ultraperipheral collisions at the LHC can be used to extract the nuclear matter density of ¹²⁹Xe, provided that high statistics and momentum resolution are achieved and that the "0n0n" channel (no forward neutron emission) is selected to suppress incoherent background. The authors hedge that the theoretical uncertainty from inelastic nuclear shadowing affects the cross section magnitude but not the shape of the t-dependence, so it does not affect the positions of diffractive minima.

## Method

The authors apply the modified vector meson dominance Gribov-Glauber model (mVMD-GGM), previously used for Au-Au and Pb-Pb UPCs, to predict coherent and incoherent ρ and φ photoproduction cross sections in Xe-Xe collisions at √sNN = 5.44 TeV. The nuclear density of ¹²⁹Xe is calculated using the spherical Hartree-Fock-SkyrmeIII model with BCS pairing, and the photon flux is computed including electromagnetic excitation channels (0n0n, 0nXn, XnXn) using the Starlight Monte Carlo approach. The t-dependence of the coherent cross section is analyzed for two-parameter Fermi distributions with different nuclear radii to demonstrate sensitivity to the nuclear matter distribution.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair: √sNN = 5.44 TeV for Xe-Xe collisions at the LHC
- RMS mass radius of ¹²⁹Xe from Hartree-Fock-SkyrmeIII model: 4.818 fm; charge RMS radius: 4.77 fm (compared to experimental value 4.7831 ± 0.0043 fm from isotope shift measurements)
- Effective nuclear radius RA = 5.8 fm (distance where mass density drops to half its maximum), compared to the parametrization RA = 1.112 A¹/³ - 0.86 A⁻¹/³ fm giving 5.45 fm
- Ratio of forward target-dissociative to elastic ρ photoproduction cross sections on the proton: dσ(γp → ρY)/dt / dσ(γp → ρp)/dt ≈ 0.1–0.12, with slope ratio B_diss/B_el ≈ 0.25, leading to σ(γp → VY) ≈ 0.5 σ(γp → Vp)
- γ-V coupling constants: fρ²/4π = 2.01 for ρ, fφ²/4π = 13.7 for φ
- NN correlation length: lc = -0.74 fm
- Low-energy coherence correction applies for WγN ≤ O(√(2RA mN) MV) ≈ 5 GeV
- Two-parameter Fermi distribution parameters tested: a = 0.54 fm with RA = 6.1 fm and RA = 5.45 fm

## Context

This work extends the mVMD-GGM formalism of Frankfurt (2015), which successfully described coherent ρ photoproduction in Au-Au UPCs at RHIC (Adler, 2002) and Pb-Pb UPCs at the LHC (Adam, 2015), to the new Xe-Xe system. The predictions provide the nuclear mass number A dependence of nuclear shadowing in light vector meson photoproduction and can be compared to future LHC data. The nuclear matter distribution of ¹²⁹Xe is of key importance for dark matter experiments searching for WIMPs with Xenon-based detectors (Uchida, 2014; Vietze, 2014; Fieguth, 2018), where only charge radii from isotope shift measurements (Libert, 2007) and the charge density of ¹³²Xe from electron scattering at SCRIT (Tsukada, 2017) were previously available. The authors note that alternative approaches exist, including the color dipole model with saturation effects (Ivanov, 2007; Goncalves, 2011; Santos, 2014; Klusek-Gawenda, 2016) and the Starlight Monte Carlo generator (Klein, 2016), but argue that their approach predicts a two-bump rapidity distribution for ρ due to sub-leading Reggeon exchange, unlike the bell-shaped distribution from dipole models.
