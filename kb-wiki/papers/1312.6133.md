# The photon-pion transition form factor: incompatible data or incompatible models?

> The elastic and $γ\to π$ transition form factors of the pion along with its usual static observables are calculated within a light-front field approach to the constituent quark model. The focus of this exercise in a simple model is on a unified description of all observables with one singly parametrized light-front wave function to detect possible discrepancies in experimental data, in particular the contentious large momentum-squared data on the transition factor as reported by BaBar and Belle. We also discuss the relation of a small to vanishing pion charge radius with an almost constant pion distribution amplitude and compare our results with those obtained in a holographic light-front model.

# The photon-pion transition form factor: incompatible data or incompatible models?

## Key claim

Using a single, uniformly parametrized light-front wave function to describe all pion observables simultaneously, the authors find that the BaBar and Belle data for the $\gamma^*\gamma \to \pi^0$ transition form factor above $Q^2 \simeq 20$ GeV$^2$ cannot be reconciled within their model, which favors the Belle results; however, by adjusting the wave function parameter they can partially reproduce the BaBar rising trend, but only at the cost of an unrealistically small pion charge radius and a too-hard elastic form factor.

## Method

The authors compute the elastic and $\gamma \to \pi$ transition form factors of the pion, along with its weak decay constant and charge radius, using a light-front constituent quark model with a constant dressed quark mass. The pion bound-state wave function is parametrized in two forms (Gaussian and hydrogen-atom models) with a single scale parameter $r_\mathrm{nr}$, which is fixed by fitting the experimental weak decay constant $f_\pi = 92.4$ MeV; no further adjustments are made for individual observables. The transition form factor is obtained from the triangle diagram in impulse approximation, and the elastic form factor is extracted from the electromagnetic current matrix element.

## Key numbers

- Weak decay constant fixed to $f_\pi = 92.4$ MeV (experimental value from Beringer (2012)).
- For the Gaussian model with constituent quark mass $M = 220$ MeV and $r_\mathrm{nr} = 0.345$ fm, the charged pion charge radius is $\langle r_\pi^2 \rangle^{1/2} = 0.637$ fm (experimental value $0.672 \pm 0.008$ fm).
- For the same Gaussian model, the neutral pion charge radius is $\langle r_{\pi^0}^2 \rangle^{1/2} = 0.683$ fm.
- For the hydrogen model with $M = 220$ MeV and $r_\mathrm{nr} = 0.593$ fm, the charged pion charge radius is $\langle r_\pi^2 \rangle^{1/2} = 0.795$ fm.
- When the Gaussian wave function parameter is adjusted to partially match the BaBar rising trend, the charge radius decreases to $\langle \sqrt{r_\pi^2} \rangle \simeq 0.4$ fm.
- The asymptotic pQCD prediction is $Q^2 F_{\gamma \pi^0} = 2 f_\pi$ in the limit $Q^2 \to \infty$.
- The transition form factor at zero momentum transfer is $F_{\gamma\pi^0}(0) = 1/(4\pi^2 f_\pi)$.

## Context

This work extends the light-front quark model approach of de Melo (2002, 2003, 2005) and El-Bennich (2012) to provide a unified description of all pion observables with a single wave function, directly addressing the tension between the BaBar data (Aubert 2009) and Belle data (Uehara 2012) for the $\gamma^*\gamma \to \pi^0$ transition form factor at large momentum transfer. The results are compared with the holographic light-front model of Brodsky (2011) and with a contact-interaction Bethe-Salpeter treatment by Roberts (2010). The authors conclude that their model cannot simultaneously accommodate both data sets without producing inconsistent values for the charge radius and elastic form factor, suggesting an inherent incompatibility in the experimental data rather than a failure of the model.
