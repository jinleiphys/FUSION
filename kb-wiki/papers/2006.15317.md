# Entrainment effects in neutron-proton mixtures within the nuclear-energy density functional theory. II. Finite temperatures and arbitrary currents

> Mutual entrainment effects in hot neutron-proton superfluid mixtures are studied in the framework of the self-consistent nuclear energy-density functional theory. The local mass currents in homogeneous or inhomogeneous nuclear systems, which we derive from the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations at finite temperatures, are shown to have the same formal expression as the ones we found earlier in the absence of pairing at zero temperature. Analytical expressions for the entrainment matrix are obtained for application to superfluid neutron-star cores. Results are compared to those obtained earlier using Landau's theory. Our formulas, valid for arbitrary temperatures and currents, are applicable to various types of functionals including the Brussels-Montreal series for which unified equations of state have been already calculated, thus laying the ground for a fully consistent microscopic description of superfluid neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact analytical expressions for the entrainment matrix in hot neutron-proton superfluid mixtures within the self-consistent nuclear energy-density functional theory, valid for arbitrary temperatures and currents, showing that the local mass currents have the same formal expression as those found earlier in the absence of pairing at zero temperature.

## Method

The authors solve the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) equations for homogeneous nuclear matter with stationary nucleon currents in the normal rest frame, using plane-wave single-particle wavefunctions and assuming uniform superfluid velocities. They derive the mass currents and momentum densities from the TDHFB solutions, then obtain the entrainment matrix elements by expressing the vector field I_q in terms of the superfluid velocities V_q through a self-consistent system of equations. The results are compared to those obtained earlier using Landau's theory of Fermi liquids.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous zero-temperature analysis (Chamel and Allard, 2019) within the self-consistent nuclear energy-density functional theory to finite temperatures and arbitrary currents. It contrasts with earlier calculations using Landau's theory of Fermi liquids (Gusakov, 2005; Gusakov, 2009b), which are not self-consistent and cannot be easily applied to inhomogeneous systems such as neutron-star crusts. The results are applicable to various functionals including the Brussels-Montreal series, enabling a fully consistent microscopic description of superfluid neutron-star dynamics, including neutron-star oscillations where temperature dependence of the entrainment matrix may be significant (Dommes and Kantor, 2019; Kantor, 2020).
