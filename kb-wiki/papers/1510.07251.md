# Towards nature of the X(3872) resonance

> We construct spectra of decays of the resonance X(3872) with good analytical and unitary properties which allows to define the branching ratio of the X(3872) \to D^{*0}\bar D^0 + c.c. decay studying only one more decay, for example, the X(3872)\toπ^+π^- J/ψ(1S) decay, and show that our spectra are effective means of selection of models for the resonance X(3872). Then we discuss the scenario where the X(3872 resonance is the c\bar c = χ_{c1}(2P) charmonium which "sits on" the D^{*0}\bar D^0 threshold. We explain the shift of the mass of the X(3872) resonance with respect to the prediction of a potential model for the mass of the χ_{c1}(2P) charmonium by the contribution of the virtual D^*\bar D+c.c. intermediate states into the self energy of the X(3872) resonance. This allows us to estimate the coupling constant of the X(7872) resonance with the D^{*0}\bar D^0 channel, the branching ratio of the X(3872) \to D^{*0}\bar D^0 + c.c. decay, and the branching ratio of the X(3872) decay into all non-D^{*0}\bar D^0 + c.c. states. We predict a significant number of unknown decays of X(3872) via two gluons:X(3872)\to gluons\ gluon\to hadrons.

# Towards nature of the X(3872) resonance

## Key claim

The X(3872) resonance can be interpreted as the χc1(2P) charmonium state that sits on the D*0D̅0 threshold, with the 50 MeV mass shift relative to potential model predictions explained by the contribution of virtual D*D̅ + c.c. intermediate states to the self-energy of the resonance, provided that the coupling to the D*0D̅0 channel is strong enough to produce a branching ratio of approximately 0.3 for that decay.

## Method

The authors construct decay spectra for the X(3872) resonance with good analytical and unitary properties using an effective Lagrangian approach where the S-wave dominates in the X(3872) → D*0D̅0 + c.c. decay. They fit the Belle data on the π+π−J/ψ(1S) and D*0D̅0 invariant mass distributions using a propagator that includes the self-energy contribution from D*D̅ loops, with a cutoff regularization Λ. The mass shift of the χc1(2P) charmonium relative to potential model predictions is then calculated from the renormalization condition involving the loop contributions.

## Key numbers

- Mass of X(3872): mX = 3871.84 MeV, equal to mD*0 + mD0 = m+
- Total width of X(3872) into all non-(D*0D̅0+c.c.) channels: Γ < 1.2 MeV at 90% CL
- Mass shift relative to potential model prediction: mX − mχc1(2P) = −Δ ≈ −50 MeV
- Fitted coupling: gA2/8π = 1.4−1+5 GeV2 (from fit to Belle data)
- Branching ratio X(3872) → D*0D̅0 + c.c.: BR = 0.6−0.1+0.02
- Branching ratio X(3872) → D*+D− + c.c.: BR = 0.31−0.16+0.13
- Branching ratio X(3872) → Others (non-D*0D̅0 states): BR = 0.1−0.1+0.3
- For the charmonium scenario with Δ ≈ 50 MeV and Λ = 10 GeV: gA2/8π ≈ 0.2 GeV2 and BR(X → D0D̅*0 + c.c.) ≈ 0.3
- Ratio of production cross sections: 0.74 < σ(pp → X(3872) + anything)/σ(pp → ψ(2S) + anything) < 2.1
- Ratio of radiative decays: BR(X → γ ψ(2S))/BR(X → γ J/ψ) = 2.46 ± 0.7, with CX = 136.78 ± 38.89
- For comparison, χb1(2P): BR(χb1(2P) → γ Υ(2S))/BR(χb1(2P) → γ Υ(1S)) = 2.16 ± 0.28, with Cχb1(2P) = 80 ± 10.37

## Context

This work extends the charmonium interpretation of the X(3872) resonance, challenging the dominant molecular or four-quark interpretations that arose after the Belle and LHCb discoveries. The authors argue against the mixing scenario of a giant D*D̅ molecule with compact χc1(2P) charmonium (as considered by Kalashnikova and Bugg, and by Badalian et al.) on the grounds that the transition probability scales as the square root of the volume ratio Vχc1(2P)/VX(3872) > 1/100, making such mixing negligible. The paper enables experimental searches for two-gluon decays of X(3872) into non-D*0D̅0 states and for the analogous χb1(2P) → ρ Υ(1S) decay as decisive tests of the charmonium scenario.
