# Deformation around neutron-rich Cr isotopes in axially symmetric Skyrme-Hatree-Fock-Bogoliubov method

> We analyse deformation mechanism in neutron-rich Cr, Fe and Ti isotopes with N=32-44 by means of a Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mean-field code employing a two-dimensional mesh representation in the cylindrical coordinate system. Evaluating systematically the quadrupole deformation energy, we show that the Skyrme parameter set SkM* gives a quadrupole instability around the neutron number N$\sim$38-42 in Cr isotopes, where the deformation energy curve suggests a transitional behavior with a shallow minimum extending to a large prolate deformation. Roles of a deformed N=38 gap and the position of the neutron g9/2 orbit are analysed in detail.

## Key claim

Using the Skyrme parameter set SkM*, the authors find that neutron-rich Cr isotopes with N=38-42 exhibit a quadrupole instability and transitional behavior with a shallow minimum extending to large prolate deformation, but the energy difference between spherical and deformed minima is small (about 290 keV in 62Cr), suggesting that a well-developed static deformation is not realized and a large amplitude quadrupole motion of transitional character is expected.

## Method

The authors developed an axially symmetric Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) code using a two-dimensional mesh representation in cylindrical coordinates, with an 11-point formula for differential operators and a trapezoidal formula for integrals. They performed constrained HFB calculations using the mass quadrupole operator to obtain deformation energy curves, and analyzed the single-particle energies via Nilsson diagrams to understand the deformation mechanism in neutron-rich Cr, Fe, and Ti isotopes with N=32-44.

## Key numbers

- The ground state quadrupole deformation parameter beta for 62Cr is 0.257, for 64Cr is 0.262, and for 66Cr is 0.247, obtained with SkM*
- The energy difference between the spherical configuration (beta=0) and the quadrupole deformed minimum in 62Cr is about 290 keV
- The occupation number of the neutron g9/2 orbit is 2.98 in 62Cr, 3.85 in 64Cr, and 4.39 in 66Cr
- The observed 2+1 energy in 62Cr is 446 keV, and the observed E4/E2 ratio in 62Cr is 2.65
- The neutron subshell gap N=40 between the f5/2 and g9/2 orbits in the spherical region is larger for SLy4 than for SkM* by about 2 MeV
- The deformed N=38 gap in the prolate region occurs around beta=0.25-0.5 for SkM*
- The level crossing of the down-sloping nu g9/2 Omega=3/2 orbit and the up-sloping orbit from nu f5/2 and nu p1/2 occurs at beta ~0.20 in 62Cr, compared to beta ~0.26 in 76Sr

## Context

This work extends self-consistent mean-field calculations of ground state deformation in neutron-rich Cr isotopes, which had been performed using Skyrme-HFB (Stoitsov 2003, Goriely 2005) and relativistic mean-field models (Lalazissis 1999), by providing a detailed microscopic analysis of the deformation mechanism. The results are consistent with experimental observations of decreasing 2+1 energies in Cr isotopes (Sorlin 2003) and the E4/E2 ratio of 2.65 in 62Cr (RIKEN experiment), and the deformation mechanism is related to the well-known prolate deformation in proton-rich Sr and Zr isotopes with N~Z=38-40 (Bonche 1985). The sensitivity to the Skyrme parameter set provides a constraint for future improvements of nuclear energy density functionals.
