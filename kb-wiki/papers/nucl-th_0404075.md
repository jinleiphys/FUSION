# Influence of electron capture and Coulomb explosion on electron screening in low energy nuclear reactions in laboratories

> We discuss the effects of electron capture by the projectile and the Coulomb explosion of a molecular projectile on the electron screening in low energy nuclear reactions in laboratory. Using the idea of equilibrium charge, we show that the electron capture of projectile leads to a screening energy which significantly exceeds the adiabatic limit in the simple consideration for the D(d,p)T reaction and provides a possibility to explain the large screening energy claimed in the analysis of experimental data. We then show that the Coulomb explosion can result in a large apparent screening energy as large as that encountered in the analysis of ^3He(d,p)^4He reactions induced by the molecular D_2^+ and D_3^+ projectiles at very low energies.

# Influence of electron capture and Coulomb explosion on electron screening in low energy nuclear reactions in laboratories

## Key claim

Electron capture by the projectile and Coulomb explosion of molecular projectiles can produce apparent screening energies that exceed the adiabatic limit and match experimentally reported values, but the authors note that the Coulomb explosion results depend on assuming prompt explosion by electron loss, and that more detailed studies are needed before a definite conclusion can be drawn.

## Method

The authors use the concept of equilibrium charge to estimate the probability that a projectile captures an electron before reacting with the target, then compute the effective screening energy by averaging over charge states weighted by their probabilities using a formula that accounts for the nonlinear dependence of the tunneling enhancement factor on screening energy. For molecular beams, they model the energy spread from Coulomb explosion of D2+ and D3+ projectiles and compute the apparent screening energy that would be inferred from the resulting cross section enhancement.

## Key numbers

- For D(d,p)T at E=1.62 keV, electron capture yields an effective screening energy U_e^(c)=35.7 eV, compared to U_e=22.0 eV without capture and the experimental value 25±5 eV.
- For ^3He(d,p)^4He at E=5.01 keV, electron capture yields U_e^(c)=117.5 eV, compared to U_e=119.1 eV without capture and the experimental value 219±7 eV.
- For D(^3He,p)^4He at E=4.22 keV, electron capture yields U_e^(c)=105.3 eV, compared to U_e=70.6 eV without capture and the experimental value 109±9 eV.
- For the ^3He(d,p)^4He reaction with D3+ beam at E=5.01 keV, Coulomb explosion yields an apparent screening energy U_e^(a)=213.0 eV, nearly matching the experimental value 219±7 eV.
- The equilibrium charge estimate gives a 90% probability that a deuteron becomes neutral D at 1.62 keV, and 10-20% probability for a deuteron to capture an electron at 5.01 keV.

## Context

This work extends earlier discussions of electron screening in low-energy nuclear reactions, where a persistent puzzle was that experimentally inferred screening energies exceeded the adiabatic limit for all systems studied (Rolfs and collaborators, 1995). The authors previously showed (Kato and Takigawa, 2003) that tunneling effects could produce screening exceeding the adiabatic limit but by a negligibly small amount. The present paper revisits electron capture effects first considered by Bracci and collaborators (1990) and Coulomb explosion effects first considered by Engstler and collaborators (1988), which had been omitted in most subsequent work. The results suggest that Coulomb explosion could resolve the discrepancy for the ^3He(d,p)^4He reaction, but the authors caution that earlier studies by McClure (1969), van der Weg and collaborators (1976, 1977, 1978), and Vogler and collaborators (1968) found that Coulomb explosion is much gentler than assumed here, making further experimental and theoretical examination necessary.
