# Self-consistent conversion of a viscous fluid to particles

> Comparison of hydrodynamic and "hybrid" hydrodynamics+transport calculations to heavy-ion data inevitably requires the conversion of the fluid to particles. For dissipative fluids the conversion is ambiguous without additional theory input complementing hydrodynamics. We obtain self-consistent shear viscous phase space corrections from linearized Boltzmann transport theory for a gas of hadrons. These corrections depend on the particle species, and incorporating them in Cooper-Frye freezeout affects identified particle observables. For example, with additive quark model cross sections,proton elliptic flow is larger than pion elliptic flow at moderately high $p_T$ in $Au+Au$ collisions at RHIC. This is in contrast to Cooper-Frye freezeout with the commonly used "democratic Grad" ansatz that assumes no species dependence. Various analytic and numerical results are also presented for massless and massive two-component mixtures to aid the interpretation. Self-consistent viscous corrections for each species are tabulated in Appendix F for convenient inclusion in pure hydrodynamic and hybrid calculations.

## Key claim

Shear viscous phase space corrections obtained self-consistently from linearized Boltzmann transport theory for a gas of hadrons depend on particle species, in contrast to the commonly used "democratic Grad" ansatz that assumes no species dependence; this species dependence affects identified particle observables such as elliptic flow.

## Method

The authors solve the linearized Boltzmann transport equation for a multicomponent hadron gas to obtain shear viscous phase space corrections of the form δf_i = f_eq_i χ_i(|p̃|) P^{μν} X_{μν}, where χ_i are scalar functions determined by extremizing a variational functional Q[χ] derived from the collision terms. For practical implementation, they adopt the Grad approximation (χ_i = constant) and compute the species-dependent coefficients for massless and massive two-component mixtures, as well as for a multicomponent hadronic gas with additive quark model cross sections, tabulating the results in Appendix F.

## Key numbers

- For a one-component massless system in Grad approximation, the shear viscosity is η_s = 6T/5σ_TOT
- For a one-component nonrelativistic system in Grad approximation, the shear viscosity is η_s^{Grad} = (5√π/16) √(mT)/σ_TOT
- In the 0+1D Bjorken expansion test with five different scenarios (inverse Knudsen numbers K_A ranging from 2.2 to 6, K_B from 1.1 to 3.8), linearized transport captures the ratio of dissipative corrections c_B/c_A with better than 10% accuracy compared to fully nonlinear transport
- The democratic Grad ansatz predicts c_i = 1 for all species, while self-consistent calculations give species-dependent values that differ significantly (e.g., in scenario a) with K_A=4.4, K_B=2.2, the asymptotic ratio c_B/c_A deviates from unity)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies that considered massless quarks and gluons (Dusling, 2010) or hadronic mixtures with only two species (Molnar, 2012) to a full multicomponent hadronic gas with realistic cross sections. The self-consistent approach provides species-dependent viscous corrections that can be incorporated into pure hydrodynamic and hybrid hydrodynamics+transport calculations (such as VISHNU), replacing the "democratic Grad" ansatz that ignores microscopic dynamics. The results enable more realistic modeling of identified particle observables like elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies.
