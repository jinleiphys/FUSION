# Internal particle width effects on the the triangle singularity mechanism in the study of the $η(1405)$ and $η(1475)$ puzzle

> In this article, the analyticity of triangle loop integral with complex masses of internal particles is discussed in a new perspective, base on which we obtain the explicit width dependence of the absorptive part of the triangle amplitude. We reanalyze the decay pattern of $η(1405/1475)$ with the width effects included in the triangle singularity (TS) mechanism. Based on the present experimental information, we provide a self-consistent description of the $K\bar{K}π$, $ηππ$, and $3π$ decay channels for $η(1405/1475)$. Our results confirm the claim that the TS mechanism plays a decisive role in the understanding of the $η(1405)$ and $η(1475)$ puzzle. Namely, the observed differences of $η$ resonances within the mass region of $1.40\sim 1.48$ GeV are originated from the same state. For the isospin violated process $J/ψ\toγη(1405/1475)\to f_0(980)π\to 3π$, we identify an additional contribution to the $a_0(980)-f_0(980)$ mixing via the TS mechanism.

## Key claim

The observed differences between the η(1405) and η(1475) signals in different decay channels originate from a single underlying state, with the triangle singularity (TS) mechanism providing the decisive kinematic explanation, provided that the finite width of the internal K* meson is properly included in the loop integral; the authors confirm that the TS mechanism remains dominant even when these width effects are accounted for, contrary to earlier suggestions that the non-zero K* width could significantly suppress the decay rate.

## Method

The authors analytically derived the absorptive part of the triangle loop integral with complex masses for internal particles, specifically introducing a finite width for the K* meson in its propagator. They then applied this formalism to reanalyze the decay patterns of η(1405/1475) into the K̄Kπ, ηππ, and 3π channels, including the isospin-violating process J/ψ → γ η(1405/1475) → f0(980)π → 3π. The analytic expression for the scalar triangle integral was obtained using the Spence (dilogarithm) function formalism of 't Hooft and Veltman, and the motion of singularities in the complex plane was traced to determine how the finite width shifts the triangle singularity condition.

## Key numbers

- The isospin breaking ratio Γ(η(1405) → f0π → π⁺π⁻π⁰)/Γ(η(1405) → a0π → ηπ⁰π⁰) is measured to be approximately 17.9%, nearly one order of magnitude larger than that produced by a0(980)-f0(980) mixing alone.
- The triangle singularity condition requires the internal K* mass m1, K mass m2, and K̄ mass m3 to satisfy kinematic constraints with s1 > (m1 + m3)², s3 > (m2 + m3)², and √s2 < m1 - m2, where s1, s2, s3 are the squared invariant masses of the external particles.
- The critical values for the triangle singularity region are given by s1c = (m1 + m3)² + (m3/m1)[(m1 - m2)² - s2] and s3c = (m2 + m3)² + (m3/m1)[(m1 - m2)² - s2].
- The η(1405/1475) mass region under study is 1.40 to 1.48 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the triangle singularity mechanism proposed by Wu et al. (2011, 2012) for explaining the η(1405)/η(1475) puzzle, which was later questioned by Achasov et al. (2015) regarding the suppression effects of the K* width. The authors provide a quantitative resolution by deriving the explicit width dependence of the absorptive part of the triangle amplitude, confirming that the TS mechanism remains decisive. This enables a self-consistent description of the three main decay channels and identifies an additional contribution to a0(980)-f0(980) mixing via the TS mechanism in the isospin-violating 3π channel, which can be tested by future high-statistics experiments at BESIII.
