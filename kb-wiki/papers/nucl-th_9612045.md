# A Giant Halo at the Neutron Drip Line

> Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theory in coordinate space is used to describe the chain of even-even Zirconium isotopes reaching from Zr116 to the drip line nucleus Zr140. Pairing correlations are taken into account by a density dependent force of zero range. For neutron numbers larger than the magic number N=82 a giant neutron halo outside the core of Zr122 is observed. It is formed by up to six neutrons.

# A Giant Halo at the Neutron Drip Line

## Key claim
Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in coordinate space predicts that zirconium isotopes near the neutron drip line develop a giant neutron halo containing up to six neutrons outside the closed core of 122Zr, in contrast to previously observed halos which contain only one or two neutrons. This prediction depends on the proper treatment of the continuum and its coupling to bound orbits through pairing correlations, which earlier BCS-based calculations could not reliably describe.

## Method
The authors solved the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) equations in coordinate space using the shooting method with a spherical box of radius up to 30 fm, employing the NLSH parameter set for the mean-field Lagrangian. Pairing correlations were treated with a density-dependent zero-range force, with its strength adjusted to reproduce the pairing energy of a calculation using the finite-range Gogny force D1S in 116Zr. The calculations were performed for even-even zirconium isotopes from 116Zr to the drip-line nucleus 140Zr, with 20 radial wavefunctions per spin-parity channel corresponding to a cutoff energy of approximately 120 MeV.

## Key numbers
- The neutron drip line for zirconium is reached at 140Zr (N=100).
- The neutron chemical potential crosses the continuum limit at N=100 for 140Zr.
- For a box radius of 30 fm, results for binding energies and matter radius agree within 0.1% accuracy compared to calculations with larger boxes.
- The number of neutrons in the continuum (halo) increases from 2 at N=84 to approximately 6 at N=100 (140Zr).
- The neutron density is reliably reproduced up to r=25 fm, where it has decreased to 10^-6 fm^-3.

## Context
This work extends the RHB method previously applied to 11Li by Meng and Ring (1996) to heavier neutron-rich nuclei, demonstrating that multi-neutron halos can occur beyond light p-shell nuclei. It contradicts the limitations of earlier BCS-approximation calculations by Sharma, Lalazissis, Hillebrandt, and Ring (1994), which used an oscillator basis expansion and could not properly treat the continuum, leading to unbound neutron gas artifacts. The prediction enables experimental searches for collective phenomena in low-density neutron matter and motivates further theoretical studies of halo formation in medium-mass nuclei near the drip line.
