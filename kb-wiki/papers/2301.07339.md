# The Self-energy of Nucleon for the Photoproduction of Pion in the Low-energy Region

> The scattering amplitude of the photoproduction of $π^{+}$ is calculated using the helicity formalism. The pseudovector coupling $π$-N interaction with the non-perturbative term is applied to the perturbative expansion. The parameters for the self-energy are taken from the results of the $π$-N scattering in our previous study.

# The Self-energy of Nucleon for the Photoproduction of Pion in the Low-energy Region

## Key claim
The scattering amplitude for photoproduction of π⁺ is calculated using a non-perturbative pseudovector π-N interaction, but the resulting cross section only reaches the correct volume at laboratory energy ~0.3 GeV and does not decrease above the resonance region, meaning the self-energy parameters suitable for the threshold region (determined from π-N scattering) differ from those needed at intermediate energies.

## Method
The helicity formalism was used to compute the scattering amplitude for γ + p → π⁺ + n. The pseudovector coupling π-N interaction was expanded perturbatively with a non-perturbative term containing the nucleon self-energy Σ(p), whose coefficients c₁(p²) and c₂(p²) were taken from a previous study of π-N elastic scattering using matrix inversion. The renormalized propagator was approximated to first order in (p²−M²), and the resulting helicity amplitudes were integrated over the scattering angle to obtain partial-wave elements up to J=3/2 in the low-energy region.

## Key numbers
- The pseudovector coupling constant f is reduced about 20% from the standard value f ∼ 1, to f ∼ 0.8, to simultaneously fit the anomalous magnetic moments of proton and neutron.
- The anomalous magnetic moments used are κ_p = 1.79 and κ_n = −1.91 (experimental values).
- The branching ratio for Δ(1232) decay to helicity λ = 3/2 is calculated as 0.75 under the x ≈ q₀ approximation, compared to the experimental value 0.78–0.79.
- Including the δa′ correction up to O(z³) yields a branching ratio of 0.78.
- The cross section σ is calculated at laboratory energy ∼ 0.3 GeV.
- The self-energy parameters α and β are small (α, β ≤ 1) compared to those from low-energy π-N scattering.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous study (Kinpara) of π-N elastic scattering using a non-perturbative method with matrix inversion, applying the same self-energy formalism to photoproduction. The calculation connects to the Δ(1232) resonance region and enables study of helicity-dependent decay branching ratios. The approach contrasts with the pseudoscalar coupling model (which gives cross sections about half the experimental value) and with dispersion-theoretical methods, which the author suggests would be effective for understanding the cross section decrease above the resonance region.
