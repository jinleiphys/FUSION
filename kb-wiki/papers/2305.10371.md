# Baryon diffusion coefficient of the strongly interacting medium

> We propose that the transverse momentum ($p_T$) differential splitting of directed flow ($Δv_1$) between proton and anti-proton can serve as a sensitive observable to extract the baryon diffusion coefficient ($κ_B$) of the hot and dense strongly interacting matter produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions. We use relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics framework with Glauber model based initial condition for the energy as well as baryon deposition that is calibrated to capture the rapidity dependence of charged particle multiplicity, net proton yield as well as the elusive $v_1$ splitting between proton and anti-proton. We employ the commonly used kinetic theory motivated ansatz: $κ_B= C_B \frac{n_B}{T} \left( \frac{1}{3} \text{coth}\left(\frac{μ_B}{T} \right) - \frac{n_BT}{ε+P} \right)$ where $n_B$, $ε$, $P$, $T$ and $μ_B$ are baryon number density, energy density, pressure, temperature and baryon chemical potential respectively while $C_B$ is an arbitrary constant which is largely unknown for the Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) medium. We find that the variation of $Δv_1$ with $p_T$ is strongly influenced by the choice of $C_B$. Further, we find that the recent STAR measurement of the centrality dependence of the rapidity slope of $Δv_1$ prefers $0.5<C_B<1.5$.

# Baryon diffusion coefficient of the strongly interacting medium

## Key claim
The transverse momentum differential splitting of directed flow between proton and anti-proton is a sensitive observable for extracting the baryon diffusion coefficient of the QCD medium, but the extraction relies on a specific kinetic theory motivated ansatz with an unknown constant C_B, and the recent STAR measurement of the centrality dependence of the rapidity slope of this splitting only constrains C_B to the range 0.5 < C_B < 1.5, not a precise value.

## Method
The authors use a relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics framework (MUSIC code) with a tilted initial condition for energy density and a participant-plus-binary-collision initial condition for net baryon density, calibrated to reproduce charged particle multiplicity, net proton yield, and directed flow of identified hadrons at sqrt(s_NN) = 27 GeV Au+Au collisions. The baryon diffusion coefficient is parametrized as kappa_B = C_B * (n_B/T) * (1/3 coth(mu_B/T) - n_B T/(epsilon + P)), where C_B is a free constant, and the hydrodynamic evolution is followed by particleization (iSS) and a hadronic afterburner (UrQMD).

## Key numbers
- The analysis is performed for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 27 GeV.
- Four values of the constant C_B are studied: 0, 0.5, 1.0, and 1.5.
- The STAR measurement of the centrality dependence of the rapidity slope of Delta v_1 between proton and anti-proton prefers 0.5 < C_B < 1.5.
- The rapidity acceptance used for the p_T differential v_1 analysis is 0 < y < 0.6.
- The centrality range where model results are considered most reliable is (0 - 40)%, where contributions from strong electromagnetic fields are expected to be small.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies (Parida 2022) that developed a suitable initial condition for baryon density at Beam Energy Scan energies, which successfully described identified hadron v_1 and the observed v_1 splitting between protons and anti-protons. It builds on the relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics framework of Denicol (2018) and the MUSIC code of Schenke (2010, 2011), and uses the kinetic theory motivated ansatz for kappa_B from Denicol (2018). The results enable future extraction of the full 3x3 diffusion matrix for baryon, strangeness, and electric charge, as recently studied by Fotakis (2021, 2022) and others, by applying similar observables constructed from hadrons with appropriate quantum numbers.
