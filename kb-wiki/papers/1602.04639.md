# Medium polarization in asymmetric nuclear matter

> The influence of the core polarization on the effective nuclear interaction of asymmetric nuclear matter is calculated in the framework of the induced interaction theory. The strong isospin dependence of the density and spin density fluctuations is studied along with the interplay between the neutron and proton core polarizations. Moving from symmetric nuclear matter to pure neutron matter the crossover of the induced interaction from attractive to repulsive in the spin singlet state is determined as a function of the isospin imbalance.The density range in which it occurs is also determined. For the spin triplet state the induced interaction turns out to be always repulsive. The implications of the results for the neutron star superfluid phases are shortly discussed.

## Key claim

The induced interaction in asymmetric nuclear matter exhibits a crossover from attractive to repulsive in the spin singlet state as the system moves from symmetric nuclear matter to pure neutron matter, but this crossover only occurs below a density threshold of about 0.09 fm⁻³; above that density the induced interaction is repulsive at any asymmetry.

## Method

The authors solved the induced interaction theory equations in the Landau limit using the LNS potential, a Skyrme parametrization of the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock energy density functional. The particle-hole irreducible interaction was calculated as a 2×2 matrix in isospin space, with the polarization propagator computed as a bubble series where vertex insertions between bubbles are expressed in terms of the full particle-hole interaction. Calculations were performed for the spin singlet and spin triplet states at momentum transfers q=0 and q=2k_F across a wide density range for three values of the symmetry parameter β=(N-Z)/A.

## Key numbers

- The density threshold ρ_c for the crossover of the induced interaction from attractive to repulsive in the spin singlet state is about 0.09 fm⁻³.
- At ρ=0.2 fm⁻³ in β-stable nuclear matter, the p-p effective interaction in the spin singlet state was found to be -1.2 MeV fm³ from the present study, compared to -14.2 MeV fm³ from Baldo (2014).
- The induced interaction was calculated for three values of the symmetry parameter β: 0 (symmetric nuclear matter), and two positive values including β=0.9 (almost pure neutron matter).

## Context

This work extends the study of Cao (2006) from symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter to asymmetric nuclear matter, investigating the interplay between density and spin density fluctuations in the context of neutron star superfluidity. The results are relevant for calculating the three superfluid states expected in neutron stars: the low-density n-n ¹S₀ pairing in the crust, the high-density n-n ³PF₂ pairing in the inner core, and the ¹S₀ p-p pairing in β-stable matter. The authors note that their p-p results differ significantly from Baldo (2014) due to different approximations, particularly the missing three-body force in that earlier work, and that calculations including both self-energy corrections from Dong (2013) and the induced interaction on the same footing are in progress.
