# Quasiradial oscillations of rotating hybrid neutron stars

> We investigate fundamental quasiradial oscillations in slow-rotation approximation of pure and hybrid neutron stars, employing equations of state of nuclear matter from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory or the relativistic mean field model, and of quark matter from the Dyson-Schwinger quark model, performing a Gibbs construction for the mixed phase in hybrid stars. Characteristic differences between neutron-star and hybrid-star fundamental quasiradial oscillation frequencies during spin-down are pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how slow rotation modifies the fundamental quasiradial oscillation frequencies of hybrid neutron stars (with a quark matter core) compared to pure neutron stars, finding characteristic differences during spin-down that could serve as observational signatures of a hadron-quark phase transition, though the results depend on the specific equation of state models employed.

## Method

The authors computed equilibrium configurations of slowly rotating neutron stars using Hartle's perturbative framework up to third order in angular velocity, solving the TOV equations for nonrotating backgrounds and then adding rotational corrections. For nuclear matter they employed the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) theory with the Argonne V18 potential and three-body forces, and the relativistic mean field (RMF) Shen 2020 model; for quark matter they used the Dyson-Schwinger model (DSM) with a Gaussian-type effective interaction. The hadron-quark phase transition was treated via the Gibbs construction, producing a mixed phase with continuous pressure and energy density, and the quasiradial oscillation frequencies were obtained by solving the Sturm-Liouville eigenvalue problem with rotational shifts computed from a variational integral.

## Key numbers

- The Dyson-Schwinger model uses current quark masses \(m_u = m_d = 0\) and \(m_s = 115\ \text{MeV}\), with a bag constant \(B_\text{DS} = 90\ \text{MeV}\,\text{fm}^{-3}\)
- Nuclear saturation density is \(\rho_0 \approx 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\)
- The paper reports that for a \(1.4\ M_\odot\) star with the BHF+DSM equation of state, the fundamental quasiradial oscillation frequency at zero rotation is approximately \(2.0\ \text{kHz}\) (extracted from figures, though the text does not provide explicit tabulated values for all models)
- The rotational shift in oscillation frequency scales as \(\omega^2 = \omega_0^2 - \omega_2^2 + \mathcal{O}(\Omega^4)\), where \(\omega_2^2\) is computed from the integral in Eq. (e:sigma2)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of radial oscillations in nonrotating hybrid stars (Sun 2021) by incorporating slow rotation, and builds on the Hartle formalism for rotating relativistic stars (Hartle 1967, 1968, 1973) and the quasiradial oscillation framework (Hartle 1975). It contrasts with earlier studies of rotating neutron star oscillations (Datta 1998, Font 2002) by specifically focusing on the hadron-quark phase transition via Gibbs construction and using modern equations of state from BHF theory and the Dyson-Schwinger model. The results enable future comparisons with gravitational wave observations from spinning-down neutron stars, potentially identifying signatures of quark matter cores through the evolution of oscillation frequencies.
