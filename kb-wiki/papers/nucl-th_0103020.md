# Elastic electron-deuteron scattering in chiral effective field theory

> We calculate elastic electron-deuteron scattering in a chiral effective field theory approach for few-nucleon systems based on a modified Weinberg power counting. We construct the current operators and the deuteron wave function at next-to-leading (NLO) and next-to-next-to-leading (NNLO) order simultaneously within a projection formalism. The leading order comprises the impulse approximation of photons coupling to point-like nucleons with an anomalous magnetic moment. At NLO, we include renormalizations of the single nucleon operators. To this order, no unknown parameters enter. At NNLO, one four-nucleon-photon operator appears. Its strength can be determined from the deuteron magnetic moment. We obtain not only a satisfactory description of the deuteron structure functions and form factors measured in electron-deuteron scattering but also find a good convergence for these observables.

## Key claim

Elastic electron-deuteron scattering can be described in chiral effective field theory at next-to-leading (NLO) and next-to-next-to-leading (NNLO) order with good convergence, but the accuracy is limited to momentum transfers of about Q ≃ 0.4 GeV because the description of single-nucleon form factors restricts the applicability.

## Method

The authors constructed current operators and deuteron wave functions simultaneously within a projection formalism based on a modified Weinberg power counting. They derived the effective electromagnetic current from a chiral effective Hamiltonian by gauging the free and interaction Hamiltonians, including one-body terms (impulse approximation with anomalous magnetic moments at leading order), renormalizations of single-nucleon operators at NLO, and one four-nucleon-photon magnetic operator at NNLO whose strength was fixed from the deuteron magnetic moment. The bound and scattering states were obtained by solving a regularized Lippmann-Schwinger equation with an exponential regulator (cut-off Λ = 600 MeV).

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: LO = 2.224 MeV, NLO = 2.224 MeV, experimental = 2.22456612(12) MeV
- Deuteron magnetic moment: LO = 0.828 μ_N, NLO = 0.852 μ_N, experimental = 0.8574382284(94) μ_N
- Deuteron quadrupole moment: LO = 0.265 fm², NLO = 0.276 fm², experimental = 0.2859(3) fm²
- LEC L_2 (magnetic four-nucleon-photon interaction) = 2.68 × 10⁻² GeV⁻²
- LO contact term C̃_{3S1} = 0.0427 × 10⁴ GeV⁻²; NLO contact terms: C̃_{3S1} = -0.0368 × 10⁴ GeV⁻², C_{3S1} = 0.186 × 10⁴ GeV⁻⁴, C_{3S1-3D1} = -0.190 × 10⁴ GeV⁻⁴
- Momentum transfer range considered: Q from 0 to 400-500 MeV

## Context

This work extends the approach of Phillips and Cohen (2000) by constructing current operators and wave functions simultaneously from the same Hamiltonian and by including single-nucleon form factor corrections at NLO, making the calculation sensitive to nucleon structure. It contrasts with the perturbative calculation of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1999), where the NLO corrections are sizeably larger and one free parameter enters from the four-nucleon-photon interaction due to different power counting. The results enable future work on N³LO corrections (where meson-exchange currents first appear) and on electron scattering from three- and four-body systems using the wave functions of Epelbaum et al. (2000).
