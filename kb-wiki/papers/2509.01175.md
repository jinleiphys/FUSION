# $w$-mode oscillation of neutron star in a new relativistic hybrid model

> We investigate how the pulsation frequencies of axial gravitational-wave modes ($w$-modes) in a non-rotating neutron star depend on its composition, particularly when including quarkyonic matter and fermionic dark matter. These modes emerge from the coupling between the star's fluid component and the gravitational field of general relativity, which are highly damped and characterized by complex frequencies with comparable real and imaginary parts. Using a relativistic mean field formalism for the nucleonic component, we modeled the neutron star's interior, while the exterior is analyzed through the complex-coordinate method to determine the $w$-modes. Our study employs a realistic equation of state, based on different physical assumptions and covering a broad area of observational constraints, starting from finite nuclei to nuclear matter with extreme conditions. The numerical findings demonstrate that axial $w$-modes provide valuable insights into the properties of neutron star matter, highlighting their significance in probing the star's internal structure.

# w-mode oscillation of neutron star in a new relativistic hybrid model

## Key claim

The paper claims that axial gravitational-wave w-modes in non-rotating neutron stars are sensitive probes of exotic interior compositions, specifically quarkyonic matter and fermionic dark matter, but this sensitivity is conditional: the imaginary part of the frequency (damping rate) shows a much stronger enhancement than the real part when comparing a DM-admixed quarkyonic equation of state to a simple polytropic baseline, with the fundamental mode damping increasing by more than 230%.

## Method

The authors solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations for the equilibrium neutron star structure using a relativistic mean field (IOPB-I) parametrization for the nucleonic sector, with quarkyonic matter modeled as a crossover transition at density n_t = 0.3 fm^{-3} and confinement scale Lambda_cs = 800 MeV, and fermionic neutralino dark matter coupled via the Higgs portal with Fermi momentum k_f^{DM} = 0.03 GeV. The exterior spacetime perturbations are reduced to the Zerilli equation, which is solved using the complex-coordinate phase-amplitude method of Anderson et al. to find quasinormal mode frequencies as singularities of the outgoing-to-incoming amplitude ratio.

## Key numbers

- For the DM-admixed quarkyonic star, the fundamental w-mode (w_1) has dimensionless frequency Re(omega M) = 0.521303 and Im(omega M) = 0.192982, compared to the polytropic case with Re(omega M) = 0.466165 and Im(omega M) = 0.057644.
- The fifth overtone (w_5) for the DM-admixed quarkyonic star has Re(omega M) = 1.794486 and Im(omega M) = 0.380952, versus the polytropic case with Re(omega M) = 1.353383 and Im(omega M) = 0.283208.
- The real part of the w-mode frequency increases by approximately 12-33% when moving from the polytropic to the DM-admixed quarkyonic EOS, while the imaginary part rises by 35-70% for higher modes and by more than 230% for the fundamental mode w_1.
- The quality factor Q = Re(omega M) / (2 |Im(omega M)|) ranges from approximately 2-4 for the polytropic EOS and 1.3-2.3 for the DM-admixed quarkyonic EOS.
- The damping ratio R = |Im(omega M)| / Re(omega M) ranges from 0.12-0.26 for the polytropic EOS and 0.21-0.37 for the DM-admixed quarkyonic EOS.
- The DM-admixed quarkyonic model predicts a maximum neutron star mass of approximately 2.33 solar masses.
- The spacing between successive overtone real parts is approximately constant at about 0.30 in dimensionless units.

## Context

This work extends the study of axial w-modes, originally formulated by Andersson (1998) and Kokkotas (1999), to include the quarkyonic matter concept proposed by McLerran and Reddy and extended by Tinaqi and Lattimer to include beta equilibrium, combined with fermionic dark matter. The results are constrained by multi-messenger observations including the gravitational-wave event GW170817 (Abbott et al., 2017) and NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451 (Riley et al., 2019; Miller et al., 2019), which require a 1.4 solar mass neutron star radius below 13.5 km and masses above 2 solar masses. The findings suggest that future gravitational-wave detection of w-modes could discriminate between purely hadronic stars and those containing quarkyonic cores or dark matter admixtures, with the quality factor and damping ratio serving as observational discriminants.
