# Cluster radioactivity preformation probability of trans-lead nuclei in the scheme of NpNn

> In the present work, the cluster radioactivity preformation probability Pc in the scheme of NpNn for the effective number of the valence particles (holes) in trans-lead nuclei has been systematically investigated. This quantity has been explored in the simplified parametrization of NpNn as well as the multiplication NpNnI of this product with the isospin asymmetry I. The calculations for Pc are both performed in microscopic and model-dependent way. Within the microscopic approach, based on our previous work [Chin. Phys. C 47,014101 (2023)], Pc is calculated in cluster formation model (CFM) combined with the exponential relationship of Pc to the alpha decay preformation probability P alpha when the mass number of the emitted cluster Ac less than 28. While Ac greater than 28, Pc is obtained through the charge-number dependence of Pc on the decay products proposed by Ren et al. [Phys. Rev. C 70,034304 (2004)]. In the model-dependent approach, Pc is extracted through the ratios from calculated cluster radioactivity half-lives in the framework of unified fission model (UFM) proposed by Dong et al. [Eur. Phys. J. A 41,197 (2009)] to experimental ones. Both of the results show Pc in logarithmic form are linear to NpNn as well as NpNnI. For comparison, the parent-mass-number dependence analytical formula as well as the model proposed by K. Wei and H. F. Zhang [Phys. Rev. C 96,021601(R)(2017)] are also used. Furthermore, the preformation mechanic for cluster radioactivity has also been discussed.

## Key claim

The cluster radioactivity preformation probability \(P_c\) in trans-lead nuclei, expressed in logarithmic form, is linearly proportional to both the product of valence proton and neutron numbers \(N_p N_n\) and the product multiplied by isospin asymmetry \(N_p N_n I\), with the linear relationship holding for results obtained from both microscopic (cluster formation model) and model-dependent (unified fission model) approaches.

## Method

The authors calculated \(P_c\) using two approaches. In the microscopic approach, for emitted clusters with mass number \(A_c \leq 28\), \(P_c\) was obtained from the alpha decay preformation probability \(P_\alpha\) via an exponential relationship within the cluster formation model (CFM); for \(A_c \geq 28\), \(P_c\) was obtained using the charge-number dependence formula of Ren et al. (2004). In the model-dependent approach, \(P_c\) was extracted from the ratio of calculated cluster radioactivity half-lives (computed using the unified fission model (UFM) of Dong et al. (2009)) to experimental half-lives. The resulting \(\log_{10} P_c\) values were then plotted against \(N_p N_n\) and \(N_p N_n I\) to test for linear correlations, using the doubly magic core (\(Z_0=82, N_0=126\)) to define valence particle/hole numbers.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{221}\)Fr decaying to \(^{207}\)Tl + \(^{14}\)C, \(\log_{10} P_c\) from CFM is \(-3.204\) and from UFM is \(-6.420\), with \(N_p=5\) and \(N_n=8\).
* For \(^{242}\)Cm decaying to \(^{208}\)Pb + \(^{34}\)Si, \(\log_{10} P_c\) from CFM is \(-11.366\) and from UFM is \(-16.511\), with \(N_p=14\) and \(N_n=20\).
* For \(^{222}\)Ra decaying to \(^{208}\)Pb + \(^{14}\)C, \(\log_{10} P_c\) from CFM is \(-3.037\) and from UFM is \(-5.547\), with \(N_p=6\) and \(N_n=8\).
* For \(^{236}\)Pu decaying to \(^{208}\)Pb + \(^{28}\)Mg, \(\log_{10} P_c\) from CFM is \(-7.689\) and from UFM is \(-14.132\), with \(N_p=12\) and \(N_n=16\).
* The experimental half-life \(\log_{10} T_{1/2}^{\text{exp}}\) for \(^{226}\)Ra decaying to \(^{212}\)Pb + \(^{14}\)C is 21.20 (units: seconds, implied by context), with \(Q_c = 28.20\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the \(N_p N_n\) scheme, previously applied to alpha decay preformation probability by Seif et al. (2011) and Deng et al. (2017), to cluster radioactivity preformation probability for trans-lead nuclei. It builds on the authors' prior work (Chin. Phys. C 47, 014101, 2023) and uses the cluster formation model combined with the exponential relationship to alpha decay preformation probability for lighter clusters, and the charge-number dependence formula of Ren et al. (2004) for heavier clusters. The results are compared with the parent-mass-number dependence analytical formula and model of Wei and Zhang (2017). This study enables a simpler parameterization of cluster preformation probabilities based on valence nucleon numbers, potentially aiding in probing nuclear shell effects in the trans-lead region.
