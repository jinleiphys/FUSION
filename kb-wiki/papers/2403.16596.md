# Limits on an improved action for contact effective field theory in two-body systems

> We consider a possible resummation of subleading effects in two-body systems with a large scattering length as described by a short-range effective field theory (EFT). In particular, we investigate the consequences of a resummation of part of the range corrections. Explicit calculations of the two-body phase shifts and charge form factor indicate that, except for extreme choices, resummations do not alter the convergence of the EFT expansion and are often beneficial at lowest orders. We have considered the expansion when the regulator cutoff is removed as well as when it is finite, and find that the cutoff is not an important factor for resummations. Our results connect with other works where the partial resummation is induced by potentials with finite cutoffs or interaction ranges.

## Key claim

The authors investigate whether partially resumming effective-range corrections into the leading-order (LO) action of short-range effective field theory (EFT) for two-body systems with a large scattering length disrupts renormalization or power counting. They find that except for extreme choices of the resummation parameter, such resummations do not alter the convergence of the EFT expansion and are often beneficial at lowest orders, though the convergence radius remains largely unaffected.

## Method

The authors consider a toy underlying theory where the effective range expansion (ERE) is truncated at order \(k^6\) (shape parameters \(\nu_2\) and \(\nu_3\)), with parameters taken from spin-triplet neutron-proton scattering. They construct an improved EFT by splitting the dimensionless effective-range parameter \(\alpha = r_0/(2a_0)\) into a resummed part \(x\alpha\) treated nonperturbatively at LO and a perturbative remainder \((1-x)\alpha\), using energy-dependent contact interactions to avoid the Wigner bound. They compare the EFT expansion of phase shifts, bound-state pole positions, and charge form factors up to N\(^5\)LO against the toy theory, both in the contact limit (cutoff removed) and with a finite delta-shell regulator.

## Key numbers

* The dimensionless ERE parameters used for the toy underlying theory (spin-triplet np): \(10\,\alpha = 1.617\), \(10^{4}\,\nu_2 = 2.512\), \(10^{4}\,\nu_3 = 1.437\), with binding momentum \(\gamma = \kappa a_0 = 1.254\) (Table 1).
* For the unimproved expansion (\(x=0\)), the EFT converges at least up to reduced momentum \(\eta \approx 4.5\), beyond which N\(^5\)LO deviates from the toy theory.
* For the improved expansion with \(x=1\) (full effective range at LO), convergence is greatly enhanced up to \(\eta \approx 3\), after which \(\nu_2\) and \(\nu_3\) contributions become essential.
* The perturbative regime for the NLO correction to phase shifts, quantified by \(\xi_\delta(x,\eta) < 1\), holds for \(0 \lesssim x \lesssim 2\) over a wide range of \(\eta\); at \(x \approx 8\) and \(\eta \approx 1\), \(\xi_\delta \ge 1\) indicating breakdown (Figure 3).
* For the bound-state pole expansion, convergence fails for \(x \gtrsim 1.32\), where corrections become oscillatory and non-convergent.
* For the expansion around the pole position, \(\xi_\delta'(\eta' \rightarrow 0) > 1\) for \(x \gtrsim 2.9648\), reducing the resummability range compared to the threshold expansion.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of partial resummation of range effects in short-range EFT, previously shown beneficial in few-body systems by Contessi (2023) using local auxiliary potentials. It connects to earlier work by Phillips (1999) on resummation of the full effective range, and addresses the Wigner bound issue identified by Phillips (1996) and Beane (1997) for momentum-dependent interactions. The results enable systematic improvement of LO calculations in few- and many-body systems without compromising renormalizability, relevant to the stability problem of nuclei beyond the alpha particle in both Pionless EFT (Stetcu 2006, Contessi 2017) and Chiral EFT (Yang 2020).
