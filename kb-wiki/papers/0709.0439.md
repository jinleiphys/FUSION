# Cluster Model for Near-barrier Fusion Induced by Weakly Bound and Halo Nuclei

> The influence on the fusion process of coupling transfer/breakup channels is investigated for the medium weight $^{6,7}$Li+$^{59}$Co systems in the vicinity of the Coulomb barrier. Coupling effects are discussed within a comparison of predictions of the Continuum Discretized Coupled-Channels model. Applications to $^{6}$He+$^{59}$Co induced by the borromean halo nucleus $^{6}$He are also proposed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that Continuum Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) calculations can provide a consistent description of elastic scattering, breakup, total fusion, and transfer for reactions induced by weakly bound nuclei, but the evidence for a "breakup threshold anomaly" in the $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co system remains inconclusive due to poor precision of elastic scattering data at sub-barrier energies.

## Method

The authors performed CDCC calculations using the FRESCO code for the $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co reaction, modeling $^{6}$Li as an $\alpha$-$d$ two-body cluster. They included couplings to resonant and non-resonant continuum states up to 8 MeV and compared the results to experimental elastic scattering, breakup, and total fusion data. For the $^{6}$He+$^{59}$Co system, they adopted a dineutron cluster model ($^{6}$He = $^{4}$He+$^{2}$n) with couplings to resonant and non-resonant continuum states up to f-waves.

## Key numbers

- Total calculated breakup cross sections for $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co were between 3.7% and 9.7% of the total reaction cross sections, small compared to total fusion cross sections.
- Sequential breakup of $^{6}$Li via the 2.18 MeV $3^+$ state gave a CDCC cross section of 22.5 mb, compared to an experimental value of $45 \pm 10$ mb at 41 MeV.
- The total $^{6}$Li $\rightarrow$ $\alpha + d$ breakup cross section was approximately 80 mb.
- The CDCC calculations predicted much larger total fusion cross sections for $^{6}$He+$^{59}$Co compared to $^{4}$He+$^{59}$Co and $^{6}$Li+$^{59}$Co, with inclusion of breakup couplings notably increasing the total fusion cross section for all energies.

## Context

This work extends earlier CDCC studies by Diaz-Torres (2003) and Beck (2003, 2006, 2007) on $^{6,7}$Li+$^{59}$Co reactions, and addresses conflicting experimental results on whether the halo of $^{6}$He enhances fusion probability, as reported by Raabe (2004), Di Pietro (2004), Navin (2004), DeYoung (2005), and Penionzhkevich (2007). The paper enables future four-body CDCC models, as described by Moro (2007), to provide a more accurate $\alpha$-n-n description of $^{6}$He and to quantify the role of 1n- and 2n-transfer channels in fusion reactions.
