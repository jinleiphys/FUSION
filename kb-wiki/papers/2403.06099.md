# Investigating $D^0$ meson production in $p-$Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV with a multi-phase transport model

> We study the production of $D^0$ meson in $p$+$p$ and $p-$Pb collisions using the improved AMPT model considering both coalescence and independent fragmentation of charm quarks after the Cronin broadening are included. After a detailed discussion of the improvements implemented in the AMPT model for heavy quark production, we show that the modified AMPT model can provide good description of $D^0$ meson spectra in $p-$Pb collisions, the $Q_{\rm pPb}$ data at different centrality and $R_{\rm pPb}$ data in both mid- and forward (backward) rapidities. We also studied the effects of nuclear shadowing and parton cascade on the rapidity dependence of $D^{0}$ meson production and $R_{\rm pPb}$. Our results indicate that having the same strength of the Cronin (i.e $δ$ value) obtained from the mid-rapidity data leads to a considerable overestimation of the $D^0$ meson spectra and $R_{\rm pPb}$ data at high $p_{T}$ in the backward rapidity. As a result, the $δ$ is determined via a $χ^2$ fitting of the $R_{\rm pPb}$ data across various rapidities. This work lays the foundation for a better understanding of cold-nuclear-matter (CNM) effects in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The improved AMPT model can describe D0 meson production and nuclear modification factors in p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, but only if the Cronin effect strength parameter delta is determined via chi-squared fitting across different rapidities rather than using a single value from mid-rapidity data, which would overestimate the backward rapidity results.

## Method

The authors extracted charm quarks directly from HIJING initial conditions rather than from string melting, applied transverse momentum broadening (Cronin effect) to initial Q-Qbar pairs using a Gaussian smearing with width parameter w that depends on the number of primary nucleon-nucleon collisions, and implemented a two-component hadronization scheme combining coalescence (for low-pT charm quarks) with independent fragmentation via PYTHIA's Peterson fragmentation function (for high-pT charm quarks). The Cronin strength parameter delta was extracted from chi-squared fits to the R_pPb data across different rapidity intervals, and separate cross sections were used for light quark interactions (0.5 mb) versus heavy quark interactions (1.5 mb).

## Key numbers

- The Cronin effect parameter delta was determined via chi-squared fitting across rapidities, with smaller values needed at backward/forward rapidities compared to the mid-rapidity value of 5.0 used in previous work
- The coalescence probability for charm quarks to form D0 mesons is higher at mid-rapidity compared to forward/backward regions, especially at low pT below 0.5 GeV/c
- The Gaussian width parameter w0 for p+p collisions is 0.375 GeV/c, calculated from Lund fragmentation parameters aL=0.8 and bL=0.7 GeV-2
- The coalescence criteria parameters were set to pr=0.9 fm and pm=0.5 from chi-squared fits to D0 meson pT spectra in p+p collisions at 5.02 TeV
- The Peterson fragmentation parameter epsilon_Q was set to 0.05 for charm quarks and 0.005 for bottom quarks

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous AMPT model studies (Zhang 2022, Zhang 2023) that addressed the R_pA and v2 puzzle of D0 mesons in p-Pb collisions at LHC energies. The improved model incorporates modern nuclear parton distribution functions and local nuclear scaling for Lund parameters (Zhang 2021), and builds upon the HVQMNR code approach (Vogt 2018, 2019, 2021) where Cronin broadening is also required to describe quarkonium distributions. The results enable better quantification of cold-nuclear-matter effects in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, separating initial-state nuclear effects from final-state QGP interactions.
