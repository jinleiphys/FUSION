# Quadrupole moments of baryons

> Quadrupole moments of decuplet baryons and the octet-decuplet transition quadrupole moments are calculated using Morpurgo's general QCD parameterization method. Certain relations among the decuplet and the octet to decuplet transition quadrupole moments are derived. These can be used to predict the $Δ$ quadrupole moments which are difficult to measure.

## Key claim

The paper calculates decuplet baryon quadrupole moments and octet-decuplet transition quadrupole moments using Morpurgo's general QCD parameterization method, which is model-independent regarding the quark-quark interaction. The results depend on the assumption that three-body (C) terms in the charge operator are smaller than two-body (B) terms, with the ratio |C/B| estimated to be at most 0.3.

## Method

The authors use Morpurgo's general parameterization (GP) method, which formally defines QCD operators and eigenstates and reduces matrix elements to evaluations in a basis of auxiliary three-quark states with orbital angular momentum L=0. The electric quadrupole operator is expanded in spin-flavor space as a sum of two-body and three-body terms with unknown parameters B and C, and matrix elements are calculated between spin-flavor wave functions of octet and decuplet baryons. The constant B is determined from the experimental neutron charge radius, while C is set to zero for numerical evaluation based on evidence from the GP method and the 1/N_c expansion.

## Key numbers

- Neutron charge radius used as input: r_n^2 = -0.113(3) fm^2 (from Kop95)
- For the SU(3) symmetry limit (r=1) with C=0: Q(Delta^-) = +0.113 fm^2, Q(Delta^+) = -0.113 fm^2, Q(Delta^++) = -0.226 fm^2
- For the SU(3) symmetry limit (r=1) with C=0: Q(p -> Delta^+) = -0.080 fm^2, Q(n -> Delta^0) = -0.080 fm^2
- With quadratic SU(3) breaking (r=0.6) and C=0: Q(Omega^-) = 0.041 fm^2
- With cubic SU(3) breaking (r=0.6) and C=0: Q(Omega^-) = 0.024 fm^2
- Experimental N -> Delta transition quadrupole moments: Q^{exp}_{p->Delta^+} = -0.105(16) fm^2 (Bla97) and Q^{exp}_{p->Delta^+} = -0.085(13) fm^2 (Bec97)

## Context

This work extends previous quark model calculations by Buchmann (1997) and Henley (2001) that found relations between the neutron charge radius, the Delta^+ quadrupole moment, and the p -> Delta^+ transition quadrupole moment, and it provides a more model-independent framework using Morpurgo's GP method. The results are compared with a group theoretical analysis by Lebed (1995), and the paper's relations reproduce all of Lebed's relations when three-body operators are neglected. The predictions enable experimental determination of baryon non-sphericity through proposed measurements of the Omega^- quadrupole moment via X-ray spectroscopy in heavy nuclei or crystal polarization, and through Primakoff reactions for octet-decuplet transition quadrupole moments.
