# Mechanism of proton-$^3{\rm He} $ elastic backward scattering at intermediate energy

> We provide systematic analysis of the differential cross section of the proton-$^3{\rm He}$ elastic scattering at $θ_{\rm cm}=180^{\circ}$ and at $T_p<700$ MeV. Three mechanisms are discussed: 2N pair exchange in the triplet and singlet spin states, pion exchange and direct mechanism. It is shown that t Tp>150 MeV tree-body structure, including triplet and singlet states of the 2N pair, becomes of great importance for understanding energy dependence of the reaction observables. Prediction for the differential cross section and the polarization correlation $C_{00nn} which can be studied now in experiment are given. PACS number(s): 21.30.Cb, 21.30.-x, 25.40.Cm, 25.55.Ci

## Key claim
The paper shows that at incident proton energies above 150 MeV, the three-body structure of 3He (including both triplet and singlet scattering states of the 2N pair) becomes essential for understanding the energy dependence of the differential cross section and spin observables, contradicting the simpler cluster (deuteron-plus-proton) approximation that works only at lower energies.

## Method
The authors calculate the differential cross section at θ_cm = 180° for p-3He elastic scattering up to T_p = 700 MeV by summing amplitudes from three mechanisms: 2N-pair exchange (in both triplet and singlet spin states), pion-exchange (PI) via a two-loop diagram using elastic πd scattering amplitudes from the Virginia group partial wave analysis, and a direct (DIR) mechanism using on-shell pp elastic scattering amplitudes from the Saclay-Geneva partial wave analysis. The 3He wave function is parametrized with five partial wave components from the Paris and CD-Bonn potentials, and relativistic corrections are applied using infinite-momentum-frame dynamics.

## Key numbers
- The effective number of deuterons in 3He is N_d = 1.39 for the Paris potential and N_d = 1.36 for the CD-Bonn potential.
- At internal momentum q < 300 MeV/c, the triplet 2N pair exists mainly as a real deuteron; at higher q the non-bound (np) pair contribution becomes essential.
- The mean squared momentum in the pair at q < 0.7 GeV/c is ⟨p²⟩ < 0.1 (GeV/c)².
- The Fermi motion of the deuteron in 3He is characterized by ⟨p_d²⟩^{1/2} = 41.3 MeV/c.
- The pion form factor cutoff is Λ = 1300 MeV with f²_πNN/4π = 0.08.
- The DIR mechanism gives sizable contribution only at T_p > 1 GeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of elastic backward scattering on light nuclei, particularly the one-deuteron-exchange model of Lesniak (year not given) which fails above 200 MeV, and the singlet-pair-exchange model of Kobushkin (1993) which also fails at intermediate energy. The authors improve upon previous pion-exchange estimates by Berthet (year not given) and Uzikov (2002) by using a T-invariant two-loop diagram with elastic πd scattering amplitudes from the Virginia group (Arndt, year not given), avoiding the double-counting and non-T-invariant issues of earlier triangle-diagram approaches. The predictions for the polarization correlation C_00nn at RCNP energies (200-400 MeV) can now be tested experimentally, and the model enables future inclusion of distortion effects and angular dependence.
