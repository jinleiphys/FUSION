# A Time Dependent Multi-Determinant approach to nuclear dynamics

> We study a multi-determinant approach to the time evolution of the nuclear wave functions (TDMD). We employ the Dirac variational principle and use as anzatz for the nuclear wave-function a linear combination of Slater determinants and derive the equations of motion. We demonstrate explicitly that the norm of the wave function and the energy are conserved during the time evolution. This approach is a direct generalization of the time dependent Hartree-Fock method. We apply this approach to a case study of ${}^6Li$ using the N3LO interaction renormalized to 4 major harmonic oscillator shells. We solve the TDMD equations of motion using Krylov subspace methods of Lanczos type. We discuss as an application the isoscalar monopole strength function.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a Time-Dependent Multi-Determinant (TDMD) approach as a direct generalization of time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) to multiple Slater determinants, but the method is explicitly exploratory and limited by the fact that the number of Slater determinants needed for convergence is large and the gauge freedom in the single-particle wave functions must be fixed to obtain unique solutions.

## Method

The authors employ the Dirac variational principle with an ansatz for the nuclear wave function as a linear combination of Slater determinants, each built from different time-dependent single-particle wave functions. They derive equations of motion of the form \( i L \dot\psi = R \) where \(L\) is a large Hermitian matrix related to the time derivative of the norm, and solve these equations using Krylov subspace methods of Lanczos type (Direct Lanczos method) combined with a rank-4 Runge-Kutta integration. The method is applied to \(^6\)Li using the N3LO nucleon-nucleon interaction renormalized to 4 major harmonic oscillator shells via the Lee-Suzuki method.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy for \(^6\)Li with \(N_w=1\) (TDHF): \(-15.933\) MeV
- Ground-state energy for \(^6\)Li with \(N_w=15\): \(-22.911\) MeV
- Ground-state energy for \(^6\)Li with \(N_w=25\): \(-24.033\) MeV
- Ground-state energy for \(^6\)Li with \(N_w=200\) after angular momentum and parity projection: \(-28.672\) MeV
- Extrapolated ground-state energy for \(^6\)Li: \(-28.774\) MeV
- For 3 Slater determinants, 36 Lanczos iterations are needed; for 5 Slater determinants, about 50 iterations are required
- Time step for real-time evolution: \(10^{-3}\) to \(10^{-4}\) MeV\(^{-1}\)
- Typical real-time evolution up to 50 to 100 MeV\(^{-1}\)

## Context

This work extends the Hybrid Multi-Determinant (HMD) variational approach of Puddu (refs. [9]-[11]) to time-dependent problems, and is distinct from the Multiconfiguration Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (MCTDHF) method used in quantum chemistry by Meyer et al. (refs. [18]-[20]), where all Slater determinants share a common time-dependent single-particle basis. The TDMD approach enables the evaluation of strength functions via the boost method for light nuclei starting from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, with the ultimate goal of extending ab-initio methods to time-dependent nuclear dynamics.
