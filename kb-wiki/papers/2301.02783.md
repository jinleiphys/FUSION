# Can a pure neutron state of matter exist?

## Key claim
A pure four-neutron (tetraneutron) resonance is highly likely to exist, based on the agreement between a 2022 knockout reaction experiment and state-of-the-art *ab initio* calculations, though the observed peak in the missing mass spectrum could still manifest as a possible coupling among the 4n system and other ingredients surrounding the reaction channel.

## Method
The paper reviews the theoretical and experimental status of multineutron systems, focusing on a 2022 experiment using the knockout reaction ⁸He(p, p⁴He) at high energy, where the alpha core was removed from ⁸He by a target proton, leaving a four-neutron system. The energy spectrum of the 4n system was obtained by missing mass reconstruction from detecting the knocked-out alpha particle and the scattered proton. The experimental results are compared with *ab initio* no-core shell model (NCSM) and no-core Gamow shell model (NCGSM) calculations that treat continuum coupling using the complex-momentum Berggren ensemble.

## Key numbers
- The 2022 experiment observed a peak at an energy of 2.37 ± 0.38 (statistical) ± 0.44 (systematic) MeV near the 4n threshold, suggesting a resonance-like structure.
- The extracted resonance width was Γ = 1.75 ± 0.22 (stat.) ± 0.30 (sys.) MeV, corresponding to a lifetime of (3.8 ± 0.8) × 10⁻²² seconds.
- The dineutron (2n) is unbound by only about 100 keV, but no 2n resonance is possible because the two neutrons stay in the l=0 s-wave with zero centrifugal barrier.

## Context
This work extends the long-standing search for multineutron states that began about 60 years ago with speculation about the tetraneutron. It follows a 2016 double-charge-exchange reaction experiment at RIKEN (Kisamori et al., 2016) that indicated the possibility of a tetraneutron resonance but with large experimental uncertainty. The 2022 result shows excellent agreement with the *ab initio* NCGSM prediction (Li et al., 2019), and the calculations also suggest a possible three-neutron (3n) resonance with lower energy and narrower width. Further experimental and theoretical studies are necessary, including direct detection of the 4n system and measurement of neutron momentum correlations.
