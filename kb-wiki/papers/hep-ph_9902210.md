# Effects of the Nuclear Correlations on the Neutrino-Oxygen Interactions

> We perform a calculation of the absolute charged current neutrino- oxygen events rates relevant in the atmospheric neutrino experiments. The inclusive reaction cross-section is split into exclusive channels, which are classified according to the number of Čerenkov rings they produce. The model includes the effects of residual interaction in a RPA scheme with both nucleon-hole and Delta-hole excited states and the effects of (np-nh) excitations (n=2,3). Our result is that although the flavor ratio $μ/e$ remains almost unaffected by the nuclear effects considered here and often neglected in the Monte-Carlo simulations, the absolute events rates are subject to important modifications.

## Key claim

Although the flavor ratio μ/e remains almost unaffected by the nuclear effects considered (maximum effect less than 10%), the absolute event rates are subject to important modifications, with the total one-Cerenkov-ring event yield enhanced by approximately 20-30% relative to the NN quasi-elastic channel alone.

## Method

The inclusive charged-current neutrino-oxygen cross section was calculated by splitting it into exclusive channels classified by the number of Cerenkov rings they produce, using a Random Phase Approximation (RPA) scheme with both nucleon-hole and Delta-hole excited states. The model included effects of (np-nh) excitations (n=2,3) and used polarization propagators evaluated in a semi-classical approximation with a local Fermi momentum derived from experimental nuclear density, solving RPA equations in the ring approximation with a π + ρ + δ-function effective interaction.

## Key numbers

- The Delta width at resonance is around 130 MeV, close to the free case value
- The Landau-Migdal parameters used: f' = 0.6, g'_NN = 0.7, g'_NΔ = g'_ΔN = 0.5, g'_ΔΔ = 0.5
- The maximal reduction of the NN quasi-elastic channel from RPA effects is of order 10%
- At the maximum value of the yields, the enhancement of the total yield relative to the NN quasi-elastic one is around 30%
- The maximum enhancement factor from the full analysis is approximately 20%
- The coherent pion channel contributes less than 2% of the total ν_e + ν̄_e event yield
- The ratio of ratios R_μ/e(NN q.e.) / R_μ/e(Total) ranges from 1.060 at k' = 100 MeV/c to 1.001 at k' = 400 MeV/c
- The fraction of non-pionic ΔΔ channels over the pionic one is around 50%

## Context

This work extends calculations by Engel and Vogel (1990s) who treated quasi-elastic scattering with shell structure and RPA correlations, and by Kim, Schramm, and Horowitz (1990s) who used a relativistic Walecka-type model for the Delta resonance region. The paper shows that (np-nh) channels, previously neglected in Monte-Carlo simulations for atmospheric neutrino experiments, significantly enhance absolute event rates while leaving the flavor ratio nearly unchanged, enabling improved interpretation of Super-Kamiokande, IMB, and Soudan-2 data.
