# Shell-model predictions for double-Lambda hypernuclei

> It is shown how the recent shell-model determination of Lambda-N spin dependent interaction terms in Lambda hypernuclei allows for a reliable deduction of Lambda-Lambda separation energies in Lambda-Lambda hypernuclei across the nuclear p shell. Comparison is made with the available data, highlighting 11(Lambda-Lambda)Be and 12(Lambda-Lambda)Be which have been suggested as possible candidates for the KEK-E373 Hida event.

# Shell-model predictions for double-Lambda hypernuclei

## Key claim
The shell-model determination of Lambda-N spin-dependent interaction terms from recent gamma-ray measurements in single-Lambda hypernuclei allows reliable deduction of Lambda-Lambda separation energies across the nuclear p shell, but the authors acknowledge that the shell model cannot correctly reproduce the ground-state Lambda separation energy in 9-Lambda-Be due to the loose structure of its particle-unstable 8-Be core, requiring an alternative estimate for 10-Lambda-Lambda-Be.

## Method
The authors apply a shell-model formalism using Eq. (1): B_LL(SM) = 2*B_bar_Lambda + <V_LL>_SM, where B_bar_Lambda is the (2J+1)-averaged Lambda separation energy in the single-Lambda hypernuclear ground-state doublet, and <V_LL>_SM = 0.67 ± 0.17 MeV is the Lambda-Lambda interaction shell-matrix element derived from 6-Lambda-Lambda-He. The spin-dependent Lambda-N interaction parameters (Delta, S_Lambda, S_N, T) are taken from fits to observed gamma-ray transitions in p-shell Lambda hypernuclei, with separate parameter sets for A=7-9 and A=11-16. For 10-Lambda-Lambda-Be, an alternative formula Eq. (2) is used to account for the loose 8-Be core structure.

## Key numbers
- Delta B_LL(6-Lambda-Lambda-He) = 0.67 ± 0.17 MeV, defined as B_LL minus 2*B_Lambda(5-Lambda-He)
- <V_LL>_SM = 0.67 ± 0.17 MeV, the Lambda-Lambda interaction shell-model matrix element in the (1s_Lambda)^2 configuration
- Lambda-N spin-dependent parameters for A=7-9: Delta = 0.430 MeV, S_Lambda = -0.015 MeV, S_N = -0.390 MeV, T = 0.030 MeV
- Lambda-N spin-dependent parameters for A=11-16: Delta = 0.330 MeV, S_Lambda = -0.015 MeV, S_N = -0.350 MeV, T = 0.024 MeV
- B_LL(SM) for 10-Lambda-Lambda-Be = 14.97 ± 0.22 MeV, compared to experimental 14.94 ± 0.13 MeV (Demachi-Yanagi event)
- B_LL(SM) for 13-Lambda-Lambda-B = 23.21 ± 0.21 MeV, compared to experimental 23.3 ± 0.7 MeV (KEK-E176 event)
- B_LL(SM) for 11-Lambda-Lambda-Be = 18.40 ± 0.28 MeV, compared to experimental 17.53 ± 0.71 MeV (KEK-E176 G2) and 20.83 ± 1.27 MeV (KEK-E373 Hida)
- B_LL(SM) for 12-Lambda-Lambda-Be = 20.72 ± 0.20 MeV, compared to experimental 22.48 ± 1.21 MeV (KEK-E373 Hida)
- B_LL(SM) for 12-Lambda-Lambda-B = 20.85 ± 0.20 MeV, compared to experimental 20.60 ± 0.74 MeV (KEK-E176 G3)
- Spin-independent matrix element V_bar(SM) = -1.06 ± 0.03 MeV derived from Lambda hypernuclear systematics
- Precision of B_LL values extracted by this method estimated at about 0.2 MeV

## Context
This work extends the shell-model approach of Millener (2010) for single-Lambda hypernuclei to double-Lambda hypernuclei, using spin-dependent Lambda-N interaction parameters derived from gamma-ray measurements by Tamura (2010). It provides predictions that agree with cluster-model calculations by Hiyama (2010) for 11-Lambda-Lambda-Be, while extending coverage to heavier species beyond the cluster model's computational reach. The results rule out both 11-Lambda-Lambda-Be and 12-Lambda-Lambda-Be assignments for the KEK-E373 Hida event reported by Nakazawa (2010), and suggest that alternative interpretations of the KEK-E176 event as 11-Lambda-Lambda-Be (G2) or 12-Lambda-Lambda-B (G3) cannot be excluded. This enables systematic prediction of Lambda-Lambda separation energies across the p shell using experimentally constrained Lambda hypernuclear inputs.
