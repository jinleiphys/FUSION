# Inverse-Reynolds-Dominance approach to transient fluid dynamics

> We consider the evolution equations for the bulk viscous pressure, diffusion current and shear tensor derived within second-order relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics from kinetic theory. By matching the higher order moments directly to the dissipative quantities, all terms which are of second order in the Knudsen number Kn vanish, leaving only terms of order $\mathcal{O}(\textrm{Re}^{-1} \textrm{Kn})$ and $\mathcal{O}(\textrm{Re}^{-2})$ in the relaxation equations, where $\textrm{Re}^{-1}$ is the inverse Reynolds number. We therefore refer to this scheme as the Inverse-Reynolds-Dominance (IReD) approach. The remaining (non-vanishing) transport coefficients can be obtained exclusively in terms of the inverse of the collision matrix. This procedure fixes unambiguously the relaxation times of the dissipative quantities, which are no longer related to the eigenvalues of the inverse of the collision matrix. In particular, we find that the relaxation times corresponding to higher-order moments grow as their order increases, thereby contradicting the \textit{separation of scales} paradigm. The formal (up to second order) equivalence with the standard DNMR approach is proven and the connection between the IReD transport coefficients and the usual DNMR ones is established.

## Key claim

The paper presents a reformulation of second-order relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics (the Inverse-Reynolds-Dominance or IReD approach) in which all terms of second order in the Knudsen number Kn are eliminated by construction, leaving only terms of order O(Re⁻¹Kn) and O(Re⁻²) in the relaxation equations; the authors claim formal equivalence to the standard DNMR approach up to second order, but find that relaxation times for higher-order moments grow with their order, contradicting the separation-of-scales paradigm.

## Method

Starting from the Boltzmann equation and the exact equations of motion for irreducible moments of the single-particle distribution function, the authors apply a power-counting scheme in Knudsen number Kn and inverse Reynolds number Re⁻¹. Instead of diagonalizing the linearized collision matrix as in DNMR, they match higher-order moments directly to the dissipative quantities (bulk viscous pressure Π, diffusion current n^μ, shear tensor π^{μν}) using asymptotic matching conditions that express thermodynamic forces in terms of dissipative quantities. This procedure eliminates all O(Kn²) terms (the K-terms in DNMR) by reabsorbing them into modified transport coefficients and relaxation times in the J-terms, yielding relaxation equations that contain only O(Re⁻¹Kn) and O(Re⁻²) contributions.

## Context

This work extends the DNMR formulation of relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics (Denicol 2012, Molnar 2014) by introducing an alternative truncation scheme that eliminates O(Kn²) terms. The approach builds on an asymptotic matching scheme proposed by Denicol (2012) for multiple dynamical moments and by Jan for multicomponent fluids, with a non-relativistic analogue in the work of Struchtrup (2004). The IReD approach enables a formulation where no diagonalization of the linearized collision kernel is needed and all transport coefficients are expressed exclusively in terms of the inverse collision matrix, potentially simplifying practical calculations in heavy-ion collisions and relativistic astrophysics.
