# Charmonium production in relativistic proton-nucleus collisions : What will we learn from the negative x_F region ?

> We study the nuclear medium effects on the $c\bar{c}$ time evolution and charmonium production, in a relativistic proton-nucleus collision. In particular, we focus on the fragmentation region of the nucleus where the formation length of the charmonium mesons is shorter than the size of the nucleus. Little is known on the nuclear effects in this region. We use a quantum-mechanical model which includes a realistic potential for the $c\bar{c}$ system and an imaginary potential to describe the collisions of the $c\bar{c}$ with the nucleons. The imaginary potential introduces a transition amplitude among the charmonium states and produces an interference pattern on the charmonium survival probability, which is particulartly important for $ψ'$. Our results on the suppression factors are compared with data from the NA50 and E866/NuSea Collaborations. Predictions are given for the suppression of $J/ψ,~ψ',~χ_c$ as a function of the nuclear mass and in the negative $x_F$ region, where data will be available soon.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in the fragmentation region of the nucleus (negative x_F), where the charmonium formation length is shorter than the nuclear size, the coupling between charmonium eigenstates via an imaginary potential produces an interference pattern in the survival probability that is particularly important for ψ', and that this effect has been neglected in prior models.

## Method

The authors use a quantum-mechanical model where the c-cbar pair is bound by a realistic potential (from Buchmüller and Tye) and evolves in time according to the Schrödinger equation with an added imaginary potential that depends on the dipole charmonium-nucleon cross section and the nuclear density. The time-dependent wave function is expanded on the basis of charmonium eigenstates, leading to a system of coupled-channel differential equations for the amplitudes. The initial conditions are fixed by fitting the ψ'/J/ψ ratio in pp collisions at 450 GeV, and the suppression factors are computed by integrating the survival probability over all paths through the nucleus.

## Key numbers

- The ratio |c_{2,0}/c_{1,0}|^2(0) = 0.22 ± 0.03, determined from the experimental ratio B_{ψ'→μμ}σ^{pp→ψ'}/B_{J/ψ→μμ}σ^{pp→J/ψ} = (1.60 ± 0.04)% in pp collisions at 450 GeV.
- The Gaussian width parameter β = 1.33 ± 0.5 GeV, used for the initial wave function of all charmonium states.
- The dominant oscillation frequency in the coupled-channel equations is ω = (E_{2,ℓ} - E_{1,ℓ})/γ, where γ is the Lorentz factor of the c-cbar pair.
- The total "J/ψ" suppression is composed as S^{"J/ψ"}_{pA} = 0.62 S^{J/ψ}_{pA} + 0.30 S^{χ_c}_{pA} + 0.08 S^{ψ'}_{pA}.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of charmonium suppression in proton-nucleus collisions by Kharzeev and Satz (1991) and Kopeliovich and collaborators (1995), which did not include the coupling between charmonium states via an imaginary potential. The model is compared to data from the NA50 Collaboration at CERN SPS (450 GeV protons) and the E866/NuSea Collaboration at Fermilab (800 GeV protons). The predictions for the negative x_F region are intended for upcoming measurements by the HERA-B Collaboration at DESY and the PHENIX Collaboration at RHIC, which will provide new constraints on the charmonium-nucleon cross section and the poorly known initial conditions for charmonium production.
