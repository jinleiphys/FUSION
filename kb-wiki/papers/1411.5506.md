# Phase Space Factors for Double Beta Decay: an up-date

> We give a complete, up-date list of the phase space factors (PSF) for beta-beta-, beta+beta+, EC beta+ and ECEC double beta decay (DBD) modes, in all nuclei of interest and possible transitions to final states. In calculation, the Coulomb distortion of the electron wave functions is treated by solving numerically the Dirac equation with inclusion of the finite nuclear size and electron screening effects. In addition to the previous calculations we use a Coulomb potential derived from a realistic proton density distribution in nucleus, improve the precision of the numerical routines used to solve the Dirac equations and to integrate the PSF expressions, and use recently reported Q-values. These ingredients proved to be important, leading in many cases to significant differences as compared to the present available PSF values, which are discussed as well. Accurate values of the PSF are necessary ingredients both for theorists, to improve the DBD lifetime predictions and constraint the neutrino parameters, and for experimentalists to plan their set-ups.

## Key claim

The authors provide an updated, complete list of phase space factors (PSF) for all double beta decay modes, claiming that using a Coulomb potential derived from a realistic proton density distribution, improved numerical precision, and updated Q-values leads to significant differences compared to previous calculations, particularly for heavier nuclei and decays with small Q-values.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation numerically to obtain relativistic electron and positron wave functions, including finite nuclear size and electron screening effects. They used a Coulomb potential derived from a realistic proton density distribution obtained from Woods-Saxon single-particle wave functions, rather than the uniform charge distribution approximation. The PSF integrals were computed using Gauss-Legendre quadrature with 32 points, and the radial wave functions were interpolated with spline functions at up to 49 energy points across the Q-value interval.

## Key numbers

- For $\beta^-\beta^-$ decay, differences between the authors' $G_{0\nu}$ values and those of Kotila and Iachello (2012) are within 3% for transitions to ground states, with exceptions for $^{110}Te$ (18% difference) and $^{130}Te$ (38% difference)
- For $G_{2\nu}$ in $\beta^-\beta^-$ decay, differences are within 7% except for $^{128}Te$ (20% difference) and $^{238}U$ (the authors' result is 7 times larger than Kotila and Iachello's)
- For $\beta^+\beta^+$ decay modes, PSF values agree with Kotila and Iachello within 3-12% for $G_{0\nu}$ and within 6-23% for $G_{2\nu}$
- For $EC\beta^+$ decay, differences between the authors' results and Kotila and Iachello's are within 35% for most isotopes, with some cases reaching about 70%
- For $ECEC$ decay, differences in $G_{2\nu}$ values are within 30-55% for most nuclei, with significantly larger differences for heavier nuclei
- Using updated Q-values from Audi (2012) instead of those used by Kotila and Iachello produced differences of 3-4% between the two sets of results

## Context

This work extends the calculations of Kotila and Iachello (2012, 2013), who first recalculated PSF using exact Dirac electron wave functions with finite nuclear size and screening effects. The authors improve upon that work by using a realistic proton density distribution for the Coulomb potential, higher numerical precision, and updated Q-values from Audi (2012). The updated PSF values are necessary for improving double beta decay lifetime predictions and constraining neutrino parameters, as well as for experimental planning.
