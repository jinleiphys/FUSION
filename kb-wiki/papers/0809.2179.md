# Energy Loss in Stochastic Abelian Medium

> Energy losses of fast charged particles in randomly spatially inhomogeneous stationary medium are considered. Analytical results for effective dielectric permittivity are presented.

# Energy Loss in Stochastic Abelian Medium

## Key claim
Energy losses of fast charged particles in a randomly inhomogeneous stationary medium can be computed analytically using an effective dielectric permittivity tensor, even when the non-random medium has no significant imaginary part and thus no intrinsic energy losses. The results are derived in the abelian approximation under the condition that the fluctuation magnitude squared times the correlation scale times frequency is small (sigma^2 (w a) << 1).

## Method
The authors model a stationary medium with random spatial inhomogeneities by writing the dielectric permittivity as epsilon(r) = epsilon_0 (1 + xi(r)), where xi(r) is a zero-mean Gaussian random field characterized by an exponential binary correlation function g(r) = sigma^2 exp(-r/a). They compute the effective dielectric permittivity tensor epsilon_ij(w,k) in the one-loop approximation via the polarization tensor Pi_ij(w,k), then evaluate the average energy loss per unit length dW/dz using the work done by the average electric field on the charged particle.

## Key numbers
- For epsilon_0 = 0.7 (sub-Cherenkov regime) and sigma = 0.3, longitudinal losses dominate over transverse losses as a function of the dimensionless variable pa.
- For epsilon_0 = 1.1 (Cherenkov regime present) and sigma = 0.3, transverse losses are significantly amplified and dominate over longitudinal losses.
- At fixed particle momentum pa = 1 and epsilon_0 = 0.7, the energy loss shows a pronounced rapid growth with increasing fluctuation magnitude sigma.

## Context
This work extends the standard theory of energy loss in homogeneous media, as described in B82 and GT92, to the case of random inhomogeneities. It is motivated by the finding of GRZ97 that the parton system created in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions exhibits violent event-by-event fluctuations of local partonic energy-momentum density. The results enable future generalization to the case of quark-gluon plasma, as announced in the forthcoming publication KL08.
