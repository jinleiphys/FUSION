# Neutron capture of 26Mg at thermonuclear energies

> The neutron capture cross section of 26Mg was measured relative to the known gold cross section at thermonuclear energies using the fast cyclic activation technique. The experiment was performed at the 3.75 MV Van-de-Graaff accelerator, Forschungszentrum Karlsruhe. The experimental capture cross section is the sum of resonant and direct contributions. For the resonance at E(n,lab) = 220 keV our new results are in disagreement with the data from Weigmann et al. An improved Maxwellian averaged capture cross section is derived from the new experimental data taking into account s- and p-wave capture and resonant contributions. The properties of so-called potential resonances which influence the p-wave neutron capture of 26}Mg are discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The new experimental data for the 220 keV resonance in 26Mg(n,γ)27Mg yield a resonance strength of ωγ = 1.34 ± 0.24 eV, which is a factor of about 2.8 lower than the previous result from Weigmann et al. (1976), and the authors argue that the direct capture model must be applied with great care because potential resonances can cause the calculated cross section to vary by orders of magnitude depending on the potential strength parameter.

## Method

The neutron capture cross section of 26Mg was measured relative to the gold standard using the fast cyclic activation technique at the 3.75 MV Van-de-Graaff accelerator at Forschungszentrum Karlsruhe. Neutrons were produced via the 7Li(p,n)7Be reaction at six different neutron energy spectra, and the induced activity of 27Mg (half-life 9.458 min) was detected via its 843.76 keV and 1014.44 keV γ-rays using a high-purity germanium detector. The experimental capture cross sections were analyzed using a direct capture model with folding potentials, and the resonance strength of the 220 keV resonance was extracted by subtracting the calculated direct capture contribution from the measured average cross section.

## Key numbers

- Resonance strength of the 220 keV resonance: ωγ = 1.34 ± 0.24 eV (derived from the new measurement), compared to ωγ = 3.75 ± 0.16 eV from Weigmann et al. (1976)
- Measured average capture cross section at En,lab = 208.3 keV: σ = 0.735 ± 0.040 mb
- Non-resonant direct capture contribution at En,lab = 208.3 keV: σDC = 0.095 ± 0.010 mb
- Resonant contribution after subtracting DC: σ = 0.64 ± 0.05 mb
- Thermal capture cross section of 26Mg: σthermal = 39.0 ± 0.8 mb (from Wagemans et al. 1992)
- Spectroscopic factors derived from thermal capture: C²S(3/2⁻, 3560 keV) = 0.360 ± 0.054 and C²S(1/2⁻, 4827 keV) = 0.260 ± 0.039
- Neutron scattering length: bfree = 4.725 ± 0.15 fm
- Half-life of 27Mg: T1/2 = 9.458 min
- γ-ray energies from 27Mg decay: 843.76 keV (branching 71.8%) and 1014.44 keV (branching 28.0%)

## Context

This work extends the measurements of 26Mg neutron capture at thermonuclear energies beyond the earlier study by Weigmann et al. (1976), which only determined resonance properties for four resonances and neglected p-wave direct capture contributions. The new results are relevant for astrophysical nucleosynthesis scenarios including inhomogeneous Big Bang models and s-process nucleosynthesis in asymptotic giant branch stars. The paper enables improved Maxwellian averaged capture cross sections for 26Mg and provides a cautionary discussion about potential resonances in direct capture calculations that applies to neutron capture studies of other light nuclei.
