# There and Sharp Again: The Circle Journey of Nucleons and Energy Deposition

> A central question in high-energy nuclear phenomenology is how the geometry of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formed in relativistic nuclear collisions is precisely shaped. In our understanding of such processes, two features are especially crucial for the determination of the QGP geometry, respectively, the nucleon size and the energy deposition scheme. This contribution reports on the (circular) evolution of such features in state-of-the-art model incarnations of heavy-ion collisions over the past seven years. Ideas for future directions of investigation are pointed out.

## Key claim

The nucleon size and energy deposition scheme in initial-state models of heavy-ion collisions have undergone a "circular" evolution over the past seven years, with Bayesian analyses initially favoring small nucleons (≈0.45 fm) and a (T_A T_B)^{2/3} energy deposition, then shifting to large nucleons (≈0.96 fm) and smooth profiles, before returning to small nucleons (≈0.5 fm) and the same energy deposition when the total inelastic nucleus-nucleus cross section is properly constrained.

## Method

The author reviews the evolution of initial-state parametrizations in the TRENTo and IP-Glasma models as inferred from Bayesian analyses of Pb+Pb and Au+Au collision data at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, comparing the nucleon size parameter w, the generalized mean parameter p (where dS/dy ∝ ((T_A^p + T_B^p)/2)^{1/p}), and the resulting initial energy density profiles. The analysis traces how successive Bayesian analyses by the Duke group (2016, 2018, 2019), JETSCAPE (2020), and Trajectum (2020-2022) changed the preferred values of these parameters, and how the inclusion of the total inelastic cross section σ_AA constraint in Trajectum-22 restored the small nucleon size.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon size w = 0.45 fm from Duke-16 Bayesian analysis of 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb data, consistent with the two-gluon radius of 0.50 fm from HERA diffractive J/Ψ production (Caldwell and Kovalsky, 2010)
- Nucleon size w = 0.96 fm from Duke-19 Bayesian analysis, doubling the 2016 estimate
- Nucleon size w = 1.12 fm from JETSCAPE Bayesian analysis (2020) using Grad-type viscous corrections at freeze-out
- Nucleon size w ≈ 0.5 fm from Trajectum-22 after enforcing the σ_AA constraint, with q ≈ 4/3 and p ≈ 0 yielding dE/dy ∝ (T_A T_B)^{2/3}
- Constituent width w_q = 0.11 fm from data-driven analysis of incoherent J/Ψ production at HERA (Mäntysaari and Schenke, 2016), implemented in IP-Glasma
- Constituent width w_q ≈ 0.4 fm in Trajectum-22 with 3-4 constituents per nucleon
- Maximum specific bulk viscosity (ζ/s)_max ≈ 0.03 in Duke-19 with smooth profiles, compared to (ζ/s)_max ≈ 0.1 with IP-Glasma initial conditions

## Context

This work reviews the circular evolution of initial-state modeling from Duke-16 (Moreland et al., 2014) through Duke-19 (Bernhard et al., 2019), JETSCAPE (2020), and Trajectum (Nijs et al., 2020-2022), showing how Bayesian analyses initially converged on small nucleons consistent with IP-Glasma (Schenke et al., 2012) and HERA diffractive data, then shifted to large nucleons producing smooth profiles, before returning to small nucleons when the total cross section constraint was applied. The resolution enables future Bayesian analyses to include multi-particle correlation observables like ρ(v_n², ⟨p_t⟩) for tighter constraints, and suggests that high-energy data could be used to infer the neutron skin of ²⁰⁸Pb, connecting to neutron star physics (PREX, 2021; Reed et al., 2021).
