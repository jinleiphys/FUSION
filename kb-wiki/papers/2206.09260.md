# Non-radial oscillations and global stellar properties of anisotropic compact stars using realistic equations of state

> In this work, we have made a systematic study of how the gravitational wave frequency of the fundamental mode from compact stars is affected by anisotropic effects using realistic equations of state. Our study is an extension of the seminal research performed by Doneva [Phys. Rev. D 85 (2012) 124023], where a polytropic equation of state was used. To achieve our objective, we considered compact stars which were built by using equations of state in the framework of a relativistic mean field theory for the case of hadronic stars and in the framework of the MIT model for the case of quark stars. In order to obtain some pertinent information that could give us the possibility to detect the anisotropy in compact stars, we also studied and analized the behaviour of various global stellar quantities, e.g., gravitational redshift, stellar mass, radius, among others. We concluded that the anisotropic effects can have important consequences, which are strongly related to the anisotropic parameter and the equation of state of high density matter. Additionally, a comparison with observational data has been made and we have shown that the anisotropic parameter $λ$ can be used as a tuning parameter to reproduce mass and radius observational data of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The anisotropic parameter λ can serve as a tuning parameter to reproduce mass and radius observational data of neutron stars, but the effects of anisotropy on gravitational wave frequencies and global stellar properties are strongly dependent on both the value of λ and the specific equation of state used for high-density matter.

## Method

The authors solved the generalized Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for static, spherically symmetric compact stars with an anisotropic pressure profile σ = λ p (1 − e^(−2Λ)), where λ is a dimensionless anisotropy parameter. They computed non-radial oscillation frequencies in the Cowling approximation (neglecting metric perturbations) for quadrupolar (l=2) fundamental modes, using the relativistic mean field theory with GM1 and NL3 parametrizations for hadronic stars and the MIT bag model with B = 60 and 90 MeV fm^(−3) for quark stars.

## Key numbers

- For hadronic stars with the GM1 model, anisotropy does not affect stars with masses below 0.3 M⊙ and radii greater than 13.2 km; for the NL3 model, the threshold is masses below 0.3 M⊙ and radii greater than 14.5 km
- For hadronic stars with the GM1 parametrization, fundamental mode frequencies for masses below 1.5 M⊙ show little difference from isotropic stars; above 1.5 M⊙, frequencies change significantly with |λ|
- For quark stars with bag constant B = 60 MeV fm^(−3), fundamental mode frequencies range from 2.3 to 3.0 kHz
- For quark stars, the maximum fundamental mode frequency is approximately 3.6 kHz and the maximum normalized frequency ω√(R³/M) is approximately 1.4 kHz
- For hadronic stars, gravitational redshift increases linearly for masses below approximately 1.5 M⊙, becoming independent of λ in that regime
- For quark stars with B = 60 MeV fm^(−3), gravitational redshift increases linearly for masses below approximately 1.4 M⊙; for B = 90 MeV fm^(−3), the threshold is approximately 1.1 M⊙
- For hadronic stars, central densities below 200 MeV fm^(−3) show no visible change in mass with λ; for quark stars with B = 60 MeV fm^(−3), central densities below 300 MeV fm^(−3) show no considerable change

## Context

This work extends the seminal study by Doneva (2012), who used a polytropic equation of state to study non-radial oscillations of anisotropic neutron stars in the Cowling approximation. The authors replace the polytropic EoS with realistic equations of state from relativistic mean field theory (GM1 and NL3 parametrizations) for hadronic stars and the MIT bag model for quark stars, enabling comparison with observational mass and radius data from NICER and other experiments. The results provide a framework for using future third-generation gravitational wave detectors to potentially detect anisotropic effects in compact stars through deviations in fundamental mode frequencies.
