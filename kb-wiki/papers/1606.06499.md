# Mass dependence and isospin dependence of short-range correlated pairs

> The target-mass number dependence of nucleon-nucleon pairs with short-range correlations is explored in a physically transparent geometrical model within a zero-range approximation. The observed $A$ dependence of 2-nucleon ejection cross sections in $(e,e')$ reactions is found to reflect the mass dependence of nuclear density distributions. A parametrization of this $A$ dependence is given. The $A$ dependence of proton-proton vs.\ proton-neutron pairs relative to $^{12}$C is also analyzed in this model. It can be understood using simple combinatorics without any additional isospin dependence.

# Mass dependence and isospin dependence of short-range correlated pairs

## Key claim
The observed target-mass-number (A) dependence of two-nucleon ejection cross sections in (e,e') reactions is a simple consequence of nuclear geometry and combinatorics, not requiring any additional isospin dependence beyond simple statistical counting. The authors show that the A-dependence reflects the mass dependence of nuclear density distributions, with deviations from linearity explained by surface effects.

## Method
The authors developed a geometrical model using a zero-range approximation (ZRA) where the probability density for finding a nucleon pair is proportional to the integral of the square of the single-particle density over nuclear volume. They computed the average nucleon density using experimental density distributions from DeJager (1987) for nuclei between 12C and 208Pb, and compared the resulting A-dependence to the normalization of correlated many-body wave functions from Vanhalst (2014). The number of pp and pn pairs relative to 12C was then obtained by multiplying the pair-probability density with simple combinatorial factors based on proton and neutron numbers.

## Key numbers
- Average nucleon density parametrization: ⟨ρ⟩(A) = 0.145 − 0.147 A^(−1/3) fm^(−3), valid for nuclei between 12C and 208Pb
- Pair-probability density: P_NN(A) = 0.145 A − 0.147 A^(2/3) fm^(−3)
- Combinatorial ratios for 12C: C_pp = 5/22 (about 23% of all pairs are pp) and C_pn = 6/11 (about 55% of all pairs are pn)
- Predicted ratio of short-range pairs in 40Ar vs. 12C: approximately 4.2

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of the Ghent group (Vanhalst 2011, 2012, 2014; Colle 2015), who used correlation operators applied to harmonic oscillator wave functions, by showing that the observed A-dependence can be understood through simple nuclear geometry without sophisticated many-body theory. The results are independent of the specific interaction (electromagnetic or weak) and are therefore applicable to neutrino-nucleus interactions, particularly relevant for future LAr detectors exploring 40Ar. The paper also connects to the experimental observation from Subedi (2008) of predominance of pn over pp processes in 12C, showing that this does not appear in the target mass-dependence.
