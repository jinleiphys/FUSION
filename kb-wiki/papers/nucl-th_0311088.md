# Inelastic electron-nucleus scattering and scaling at high inelasticity

> Highly inelastic electron scattering is analyzed within the context of the unified relativistic approach previously considered in the case of quasielastic kinematics. Inelastic relativistic Fermi gas modeling that includes the complete inelastic spectrum - resonant, non-resonant and Deep Inelastic Scattering - is elaborated and compared with experimental data. A phenomenological extension of the model based on direct fits to data is also introduced. Within both models, cross sections and response functions are evaluated and binding energy effects are analyzed. Finally, an investigation of the second-kind scaling behavior is also presented.

## Key claim

The paper extends the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model, previously applied only to quasielastic (QE) kinematics, to cover the entire inelastic spectrum (resonant, non-resonant, and deep inelastic scattering), but the authors caution that a complete understanding of all reaction mechanisms (including 2p-2h meson-exchange current effects) is not yet achieved, making detailed quantitative comparison with experimental data premature.

## Method

The authors derive the inelastic hadronic tensor for electron-nucleus scattering within the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model, integrating over the Fermi sea of nucleon momenta and over the invariant mass of the final hadronic state. They incorporate phenomenological fits of single-nucleon inelastic structure functions (the Bodek et al. parameterization) to describe the resonant and deep inelastic regions. A binding energy shift is introduced by modifying the energy-conserving delta function, and an extended relativistic Fermi gas (ERFG) model is constructed by replacing the RFG scaling function with a phenomenological universal scaling function fitted to quasielastic data from Jourdan (1996).

## Key numbers

- The inelastic scaling variable is defined as \(\psi_X \equiv \text{sign}(\lambda - \tau\rho) \sqrt{(\epsilon_0(\rho)-1)/(\epsilon_F-1)}\), where \(\epsilon_F = \sqrt{1 + \eta_F^2}\) and \(\eta_F = p_F/m_N\).
- The integration limits for the inelasticity parameter \(\rho\) are \(\rho_1 = \max\{(\lambda\epsilon_F - \kappa\eta_F)/\tau, \rho_{\text{thresh}}\}\) and \(\rho_2 = (\lambda\epsilon_F + \kappa\eta_F)/\tau\), with \(\rho_{\text{thresh}} = 1 + \mu_\pi(\mu_\pi+2)/(4\tau)\) and \(\mu_\pi = m_\pi/m_N\).
- The resonant mass range is \(m_N + m_\pi \le W_X \le m_N + \omega - E_S\), where \(E_S\) is the separation energy.
- The width of the inelastic peak for a given \(\mu_X\) is \(\Delta\lambda \simeq 2\kappa\eta_F / \sqrt{4\kappa^2 + \mu_X^2}\), which grows with \(\kappa\) and decreases with \(\mu_X\).
- The RFG scaling function is \(f(\psi_X') = (3/4)(1 - \psi_X'^2)\theta(1 - \psi_X'^2)\), which is replaced in the ERFG model by the phenomenological \(f_{\text{univ}}(\psi_X')\) fitted to data from Jourdan (1996).

## Context

This work extends the unified relativistic approach previously applied to quasielastic scattering (Alberico et al., 1988; Donnelly et al., 1998) to the highly inelastic region, including resonance excitation (Amaro et al., 1999; Alvarez-Ruso et al., 2003) and deep inelastic scattering. The RFG model is related to traditional convolution models (Bodek and Ritchie, 1981; Simula, 1995) but correctly incorporates a relativistic nuclear spectral function. The study enables future investigations of second-kind scaling behavior and provides a framework for adding 2p-2h meson-exchange current contributions (De Pace et al., 2003) to achieve a complete description of inclusive electron-nucleus scattering.
