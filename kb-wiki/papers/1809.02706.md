# Consistent account of deuteron-induced reactions on Cr up to 60 MeV

> Purpose: Accurate new measurements of low-energy deuteron-induced reaction cross sections for natural Cr target can enhance the related database and the opportunity for an unitary and consistent account of the involved reaction mechanisms. Methods: The activation cross sections of $^{51,52,54}$Mn, $^{51}$Cr, and $^{48}$V nuclei for deuterons incident on natural Cr at energies up to 20 MeV, were measured by the stacked-foil technique and high resolution gamma spectrometry using U-120M cyclotron of the Center of Accelerators and Nuclear Analytical Methods (CANAM) of the Nuclear Physics Institute of the Czech Academy of Sciences (NPI CAS). They as well as formerly available data for deuteron interactions with Cr isotopes up to 60 MeV are the object of an extended analysis of all processes from elastic scattering until the evaporation from fully equilibrated compound system, but with a particular attention given to the BU and DR mechanisms. Results: The new measured activation excitation functions proved essential for the enrichment of the deuteron database, while the theoretical analysis of all available data strengthens for the first time their consistent account provided that (i) a suitable BU and DR assessment is completed by (ii) the assumption of PE and CN contributions corrected for decrease of the total-reaction cross section due to the leakage of the initial deuteron flux towards BU and DR processes. Conclusions: The suitable description of nuclear mechanisms involved within deuteron-induced reactions on chromium, taking into account especially the BU and DR direct processes, is validated by an overall agreement of the calculated and measured cross sections including particularly the new experimental data at low energies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a consistent account of deuteron-induced reactions on natural chromium up to 60 MeV is achieved for the first time, provided that (i) a suitable breakup (BU) and direct reaction (DR) assessment is completed by (ii) the assumption of pre-equilibrium (PE) and compound-nucleus (CN) contributions corrected for the decrease of the total-reaction cross section due to the leakage of the initial deuteron flux towards BU and DR processes.

## Method

Activation cross sections for production of 51,52,54Mn, 51Cr, and 48V from deuterons on natural Cr were measured up to 20 MeV using the stacked-foil technique and high-resolution gamma spectrometry at the U-120M cyclotron. These new data, together with previously available data up to 60 MeV, were analyzed using an extended theoretical framework including elastic breakup (EB) and breakup fusion (BF) parametrizations, DWBA calculations for stripping and pick-up reactions via the FRESCO code, and statistical PE+CN calculations via TALYS-1.9 corrected for the direct-interaction flux leakage.

- New measured cross sections for Cr(d,xn)52Mng at 19.45 MeV: 143.17 mb (uncertainty 831)
- New measured cross sections for Cr(d,x)51Cr at 19.45 MeV: 41.93 mb (uncertainty 459)
- New measured cross sections for Cr(d,x)48V at 19.45 MeV: 0.661 mb (uncertainty 40)
- Beam energy accuracy: 1%, with FWHM of 1.8%
- Charge measurement uncertainty: 5%
- Foil thickness uncertainty: 2%
- HPGe detector efficiency uncertainty: 2%
- Natural Cr isotopic composition: 50Cr (4.345%), 52Cr (83.789%), 53Cr (9.501%), 54Cr (2.365%)
- Normalization factor of 0.75 used for the spin cut-off parameter of 52Mn to describe the isomeric cross section ratio

## Context

This work extends a series of recent analyses (Avrigeanu et al., 2014-2017) looking for consistent inclusion of deuteron breakup contributions within activation cross-section calculations for various target nuclei. The paper addresses the long-standing problem that reaction cross sections recommended for high-priority elements (e.g., in TENDL-2017) are still based on data fits with low predictive power, rather than on nuclear modeling that properly accounts for breakup and direct reactions. The validated approach enables more reliable predictions for deuteron-induced reactions needed for fusion materials research (ITER, IFMIF) and nuclear data libraries (FENDL).
