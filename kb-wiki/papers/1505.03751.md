# Quarteting and spin-aligned proton-neutron pairs in heavy N=Z nuclei

> We analyze the role of maximally aligned isoscalar pairs in heavy $N=Z$ nuclei by employing a formalism of quartets. Quartets are superpositions of two neutrons and two protons coupled to total isospin $T=0$ and given $J$. The study is focused on the contribution of spin-aligned pairs carrying the angular momentum $J=9$ to the structure of $^{96}$Cd and $^{92}$Pd. We show that the role played by the $J=9$ pairs is quite sensitive to the model space and, in particular, it decreases considerably by passing from the simple $0g_{9/2}$ space to the more complete $1p_{1/2}$,$1p_{3/2}$,$0f_{5/2}$,$0g_{9/2}$ space. In the latter case the description of these nuclei in terms of only spin-aligned $J=9$ pairs turns out to be unsatisfactory while an important contribution, particularly in the ground state, is seen to arise from isovector $J=0$ and isoscalar $J=1$ pairs. Thus, contrary to previous studies, we find no compelling evidence of a spin-aligned pairing phase in $^{92}$Pd.

## Key claim
The contribution of spin-aligned isoscalar $J=9$ pairs to the structure of $^{96}$Cd and $^{92}$Pd is strongly dependent on the model space; in the full $fpg$ space, the role of these pairs decreases considerably, and the authors find no compelling evidence of a spin-aligned pairing phase in $^{92}$Pd, contrary to previous studies.

## Method
The authors employ a quartet model (QM) where quartets are superpositions of two neutrons and two protons coupled to total isospin $T=0$ and a given angular momentum $J$. For $^{96}$Cd (two proton holes and two neutron holes relative to $^{100}$Sn) a single quartet describes the system exactly; for $^{92}$Pd (four proton holes and four neutron holes) the wave function is a superposition of two-quartet states built from the quartets of $^{96}$Cd. Calculations are performed in three model spaces: the $0g_{9/2}$ space ($g$), the $1p_{1/2}0g_{9/2}$ space ($pg$), and the full $1p_{3/2}0f_{5/2}1p_{1/2}0g_{9/2}$ space ($fpg$), using the SLGT0, F-FIT, and JUN45 interactions respectively. Approximate schemes restrict the pairs forming the quartets: QM$_{SA}$ uses only isoscalar $J=9$ pairs; QM$_{IV}$ uses isovector pairs with one $J=0$ pair; QM$_{R}$ mixes spin-aligned and isovector components; QM$_{J1}$ adds isoscalar $J=1$ pairs.

## Key numbers
- For $^{96}$Cd in the $g$ space, the squared overlap $\langle$QM$|$QM$_{SA}\rangle^2$ for the ground state is 0.91; in the $fpg$ space it decreases to 0.56.
- For $^{96}$Cd in the $g$ space, the QM$_{SA}$ ground state correlation energy is underestimated by about 400 keV compared to the exact QM value.
- For $^{92}$Pd, the QM ground state is composed by more than 99% of $J=0$ quartets in all three model spaces.
- Experimental energies for $^{92}$Pd yrast states: E(2$^+$) = 0.874 MeV, E(4$^+$) = 1.786 MeV, E(6$^+$) = 2.536 MeV; the QM spectra reproduce these well in all model spaces.
- For $^{92}$Pd in the $fpg$ space, the QM$_{J1}$ scheme generates a ground state energy basically identical to that of the QM$_R$ scheme.

## Context
This work extends the quartet model previously applied to $sd$ shell nuclei (Sambataro and Sandulescu, 2014) to the heavy $N=Z$ nuclei $^{96}$Cd and $^{92}$Pd. It directly contradicts the interpretation of Cederwall et al. (2011) and Qi et al. (2011) that the low-lying yrast states of $^{92}$Pd show evidence of a spin-aligned pairing phase dominated by isoscalar $J=9$ pairs. The results also contrast with earlier shell-model studies (Isack et al., Coraggio et al., Zamick et al.) and multi-step shell model calculations (Xu et al.) that were limited to the $0g_{9/2}$ orbit. The findings enable a more complete understanding of the role of isovector $J=0$ and isoscalar $J=1$ pairs in these nuclei, and suggest that future studies should use larger model spaces to assess pairing correlations.
