# Mechanisms of high energy polarized photoproduction of $π^{-}Δ^{++}$

> We present an amplitude analysis of high-energy polarized photoproduction of $π^-Δ^{++}$ within a Regge exchange framework. A Regge amplitude model incorporating $π$, $ρ$, $b_1$, and $a_2$ trajectory exchanges is fit simultaneously to spin density matrix elements measured by the GlueX experiment at photon energies of $E_γ= 8.2$--$8.8$ GeV and differential cross section data from SLAC. By including SDME data, the fit constrains not only the magnitudes but also the relative phases of the helicity amplitudes. The results confirm the dominance of pion exchange at small momentum transfer, while natural parity exchanges become significant at larger $t$. We analytically continue the $s$-channel amplitude to the $t$-channel, taking care of the kinematical singularities, and isolate the dynamical residues at the meson poles. The extracted $πNΔ$ coupling constant is found to be consistent with the value obtained from the decay width of the $Δ(1232)$. For the $ρNΔ$, $b_1 NΔ$, and $a_2 NΔ$ vertices, first extractions of the relevant coupling constants are provided.

## Key claim

The paper confirms the dominance of pion exchange at small momentum transfer in high-energy polarized photoproduction of π⁻Δ⁺⁺, while natural parity exchanges (ρ, a₂) become significant at larger |t|; the extracted πNΔ coupling constant is consistent with the value obtained from the Δ(1232) decay width, and first extractions of the ρNΔ, b₁NΔ, and a₂NΔ coupling constants are provided.

## Method

A Regge amplitude model incorporating π, ρ, b₁, and a₂ trajectory exchanges was fit simultaneously to spin density matrix elements (SDMEs) measured by the GlueX experiment and differential cross section data from SLAC. The s-channel helicity amplitudes were analytically continued to the t-channel, removing kinematical singularities via parity-conserving helicity amplitudes (PCHAs), to isolate the dynamical residues at the meson poles. Absorption corrections were implemented for unnatural exchange amplitudes using the poor man's absorption (PMA) scheme.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy range: Eγ = 8.2–8.8 GeV (GlueX experiment)
- χ²/dof = 153.6/140 for the combined fit to 160 data points
- π-exchange slope parameter: bπ = 1.20 ± 0.16 GeV⁻²
- b₁-exchange slope parameter: b_{b₁} = -0.80 ± 0.19 GeV⁻²
- Natural exchange slope parameter: bN = -0.51 ± 0.15 GeV⁻²
- πNΔ coupling extracted from the π-exchange residue, consistent with the Δ(1232) decay width value
- Regge trajectory slopes: α' = 0.9 GeV⁻² for all trajectories
- Pion mass used: mπ = 0.1395 GeV; ρ meson mass used: mρ = 0.775 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier Lagrangian-based Regge models for γp → π⁻Δ⁺⁺ (Barbour 1974, Clark 1974, Gotsman 1969, Yu 2016, Joint Physics Analysis Center 2017) that successfully described cross sections and beam spin asymmetry but did not constrain relative phases of helicity amplitudes. The inclusion of high-precision SDME data from GlueX (2024) resolves this ambiguity. The results enable future studies of hybrid meson production, particularly the π₁(1600), in charge-exchange photoproduction at GlueX, and provide the first extraction of ρNΔ, b₁NΔ, and a₂NΔ coupling constants for use in other reactions.
