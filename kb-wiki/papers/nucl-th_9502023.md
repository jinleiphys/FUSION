# Thermal Properties of a Hot Pion Gas beyond the Quasiparticle Approximation

> Within the Matsubara formalism we derive expressions for the pion self-energy and the two-pion propagator in a hot pion gas. These quantities are used to selfconsistently calculate the in-medium $ππ$ amplitude beyond the quasiparticle approximation (QPA). The results are shown to differ significantly from QPA-based calculations. We also examine the impact of chiral constraints on the $ππ$ interaction in a chirally improved version of the Jülich $ππ$ model.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that full off-shell calculations of the in-medium pion self-energy and two-pion propagator in a hot pion gas yield results that differ significantly from quasiparticle approximation (QPA) calculations, particularly for temperatures above approximately 150 MeV where the QPA becomes unreliable.

## Method

The authors use the Matsubara formalism to derive expressions for the pion self-energy and the two-pion propagator in a hot pion gas in thermal and chemical equilibrium (mu_pi = 0). These quantities are used to self-consistently calculate the in-medium pi-pi amplitude by numerical iteration, starting from the vacuum pi-pi model of Lohse et al. and including all partial waves up to J=2 and all isospins. A chirally improved version of the Julich pi-pi model is also constructed by introducing pi-pi contact interactions from the gauged nonlinear sigma model of Weinberg (1966) and using an on-mass-shell prescription for the off-shell values of the pseudopotentials.

## Key numbers

- The QPA becomes unreliable for temperatures above T approximately 150 MeV, near the chiral restoration temperature T_c.
- For the chirally improved model, the s-wave scattering lengths obtained are a^{00} = 0.21 m_pi^{-1} and a^{02} = -0.028 m_pi^{-1}, in reasonable agreement with experimental analysis.
- In the sigma channel (JI=00), considerable enhancement over the vacuum curve is found for CMS energies below E approximately 450 MeV with increasing temperature.
- At T=200 MeV, some accumulation of subthreshold strength is found in the sigma channel, almost entirely due to thermal excitations (the B-contribution).
- The additional cutoff parameters in the contact terms of the chirally improved model are chosen to be slightly below 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Brueckner-type calculation for a thermal pion gas (Rapp and Wambach, 1994) by going beyond the quasiparticle approximation, which was found to be unreliable above T approximately 150 MeV. The study is motivated by anticipated experiments at RHIC that will create a dense meson gas primarily consisting of pions at temperatures of the order of the pion mass. The results can serve as input for transport models simulating the time evolution of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions near freeze-out, and the chirally improved model builds on the analysis of pi-pi correlations in cold nuclear matter by Ainsworth et al. (1991).
