# Microscopic Calculations of Stellar Weak Rates for sd- and fp-Shell Nuclei for Astrophysical Applications

> Proton neutron quasiparticle RPA is used for the first time to calculate weak interaction rates for sd and fp shell nuclei at high temperatures and densities. The calculated rates take into consideration the latest experimental energy levels and ft value compilations. Particle emission processes from excited states are taken into account. The calculation is done for 700 nuclei with mass number ranging from 18 to 100. The astrophysical applications of the calculated rates are highlighted.

## Key claim
The paper claims that proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase-approximation (pn-QRPA) theory is used for the first time to calculate weak interaction rates for sd- and fp-shell nuclei in stellar matter at high temperatures and densities, but the actual hedge is that the calculated rates for A=24 systems differ from earlier gross theory rates by two orders of magnitude, and the authors state that a precise calculation of stellar evolution is necessary to determine the quantitative impact of their new rates.

## Method
The authors used the pn-QRPA framework to calculate beta-decay and electron-capture transition probabilities between parent and daughter nuclear states, incorporating experimental energy levels and ft-value compilations. The phase space integrals were evaluated using Fermi-Dirac distribution functions for electrons and positrons at given temperature and density, with Fermi energies determined iteratively from the electron number density. The total rate per nucleus was obtained by summing over all initial and final states weighted by thermal occupation probabilities, and the formalism was extended to include beta-delayed particle emission and neutrino energy loss rates.

## Key numbers
- The calculation covers 700 nuclei with mass numbers ranging from 18 to 100.
- The constant D in the rate formula is taken as 6295 s, and the axial-vector to vector coupling constant ratio (g_A/g_V) is taken as 1.254.
- For the A=24 systems at the relevant stellar conditions (log T = 8.4-9.4 in Kelvins, log ρ = 9.3-10.4 in g cm^{-3}), the authors' electron capture rates for ^{24}Mg differ by two orders of magnitude compared to the gross theory rates.
- The temperature and density ranges for the O+Ne+Mg core evolution are given as log T = 8.4-9.4 (Kelvins) and log ρ = 9.3-10.4 (g cm^{-3}).

## Context
This work extends the pn-QRPA approach previously applied to terrestrial beta-decay half-lives by Bender et al., Staudt et al. (1990), and Muto et al. to stellar weak interaction rates for the first time, particularly for fp-shell nuclei. The calculated rates differ significantly from earlier gross theory rates used by Miyaji and Nomoto (1987) and from the rates of Oda et al. (1994) and Fuller et al. (1982), which could affect simulations of the evolution of 8-10 solar mass stars and the associated r-process nucleosynthesis. The authors suggest that ranked lists of important nuclei for stellar evolution, previously compiled by Aufderheide et al. (1990-1994), may change when using the new rates.
