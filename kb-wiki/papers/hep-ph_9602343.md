# Statistical Properties of the Linear Sigma Model

> The statistical equilibrium properties of the linear sigma model are studied, with a view towards characterizing the field configurations employed as initial conditions for numerical simulations of the formation of disoriented chiral condensates in high-energy nuclear collisions. The field is decomposed into its spatial average (the order parameter) and the fluctuations (the quasi- particles) and enclosed in a rectangular box with periodic boundary conditions. The quantized quasi-particle modes are described approximately by Klein-Gordon dispersion relations containing an effective mass that depends on both the temperature and the magnitude of the order parameter. The thermal fluctuations are instrumental in shaping the effective potential governing the order parameter, and the evolution of its statistical distribution with temperature is discussed, as is the behavior of the associated effective masses. As the system is cooled the field fluctuations subside, causing a smooth change from the high-temperature phase in which chiral symmetry is approximately restored towards the normal phase. Of practical interest is the fact that the equilibrium field configurations can be sampled in a simple manner, thus providing a convenient means for specifying the initial conditions in dynamical simulations of the non-equilibrium relaxation of the chiral field. The corresponding correlation function is briefly considered and used to calculate the spectral strength of radiated pions. Finally, by propagating samples of initial configurations by the exact equation of motion, it has been ascertained that the treatment is sufficiently accurate to be of practical utility.

## Key claim

The paper provides a practical, approximate method for sampling statistical equilibrium configurations of the linear sigma model that can serve as initial conditions for dynamical simulations of disoriented chiral condensate formation, but the treatment is limited to the mean-field approximation and assumes thermal equilibrium for the quasi-particle degrees of freedom.

## Method

The chiral field is decomposed into its spatial average (the order parameter) and spatial fluctuations (quasi-particles), confined in a rectangular box with periodic boundary conditions. The quasi-particle modes are described by Klein-Gordon dispersion relations with effective masses that depend self-consistently on both temperature and the order parameter magnitude, and the coupled gap equations for these masses are solved to obtain the statistical equilibrium properties. The partition function is then constructed by integrating over the quasi-particle degrees of freedom, treating them as an ideal Bose-Einstein gas, yielding a statistical weight for the order parameter that incorporates both the effective potential and the entropy of the fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- The model parameters are fixed by \(f_\pi = 92\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(m_\pi = 138\ \mathrm{MeV}/c^2\), and \(m_\sigma = 600\ \mathrm{MeV}/c^2\), yielding \(\lambda = 20.14\), \(v = 86.71\ \mathrm{MeV}\), and \(H = (120.55\ \mathrm{MeV})^3\).
- The critical temperature in the thermodynamic limit is \(T_c = 122.63\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- For a cubic box of side \(L = 8\ \mathrm{fm}\), the critical temperature increases to \(154\ \mathrm{MeV}\); for \(L = 5\ \mathrm{fm}\), it increases to \(172\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- At high temperatures, the effective mass at \(\phi_0 = 0\) becomes \(\mu_0 c^2 \approx 1.59\ T\) for \(T \gg v\).

## Context

This work extends the mean-field treatment of the linear sigma model used in dynamical studies of disoriented chiral condensates by Rajagopal (1993), Gavin (1995), and others, providing a systematic framework for generating initial field configurations rather than relying on ad hoc choices. The method enables more realistic numerical simulations of the non-equilibrium relaxation of the chiral field following high-energy nuclear collisions, and the author verifies by propagating samples with the exact equation of motion that the approximate treatment is sufficiently accurate for practical utility.
