# Thermodynamics of hot strong-interaction matter from ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions

> Collisions between heavy atomic nuclei at ultra-relativistic energies are carried out at particle colliders to produce the quark-gluon plasma, a state of matter where quarks and gluons are not confined into hadrons, and colour degrees of freedom are liberated. This state is thought to be produced as a transient phenomenon before it fragments into thousands of particles that reach the particle detectors. Despite two decades of investigations, one of the big open questions is to obtain an experimental determination of the temperature reached in a heavy-ion collision, and a simultaneous determination of another thermodynamic quantity, such as the entropy density, that would give access to the number of degrees of freedom. Here we obtain the first such determination, utilizing state-of-the-art hydrodynamic simulations. We define an effective temperature, averaged over the space-time evolution of the medium. Then, using experimental data, we determine this temperature, the corresponding entropy density and speed of sound in the matter created in lead-lead collisions at the Large Hadron Collider. Our results agree with first-principles calculations from lattice quantum chromodynamics and confirm that a deconfined phase of matter is indeed produced.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first experimental determination of the temperature, entropy density, and speed of sound in the quark-gluon plasma created in lead-lead collisions, using an effective temperature averaged over the space-time evolution of the medium rather than the initial temperature.

## Method

The authors define an effective temperature \(T_{\rm eff}\) and effective volume \(V_{\rm eff}\) as those of a uniform fluid at rest that would have the same total energy and total entropy as the fluid at freeze-out, using integrals over the freeze-out hypersurface from hydrodynamic simulations. They carry out ideal and viscous hydrodynamic simulations of Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, using both a soft equation of state from lattice QCD and a stiff equation of state, and compare the effective temperature to the mean transverse momentum \(\langle p_t\rangle\) of charged particles. The entropy density is extracted from the charged multiplicity and effective volume, while the speed of sound is obtained from the relative variation of \(\langle p_t\rangle\) and \(dN_{\rm ch}/d\eta\) with collision energy.

## Key numbers

* Effective temperature in central Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02\) TeV: \(T_{\rm eff}=222\pm 9\) MeV = \((2.58\pm 0.10)\times 10^{12}\) K, obtained from \(\langle p_t\rangle=681\) MeV and the proportionality \(\langle p_t\rangle=3.07~T_{\rm eff}\).
* Entropy density at \(T_{\rm eff}\): \(s(T_{\rm eff})=20\pm 5\) fm\(^{-3}\), giving \(s(T_{\rm eff})/T_{\rm eff}^3=14\pm 3.5\).
* Speed of sound squared: \(c_s^2(T_{\rm eff})=0.24\pm 0.04\), derived from the ratio \(d\ln\langle p_t\rangle/d\ln(dN_{\rm ch}/d\eta)\) using data at 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV.
* Back-of-the-envelope estimate of degrees of freedom: \(g\sim 30\), using \(T_{\rm eff}=222\) MeV, \(V_{\rm eff}\approx 780\) fm\(^3\), and \(dN_{\rm ch}/d\eta\approx 2000\).

## Context

This work extends decades of speculation (Van Hove, 1982; Campanini, 2011) that the temperature in the hydrodynamic phase is related to the mean transverse momentum, but provides the first quantitative determination using state-of-the-art hydrodynamic simulations. The results agree with ab-initio lattice QCD calculations (Borsanyi, 2013) and confirm that a deconfined phase of matter is produced. The method enables experimental access to QCD thermodynamics at nonzero temperature, and the authors note that extending the analysis to RHIC energies would probe QCD thermodynamics at other temperatures and at nonzero baryon chemical potential.
