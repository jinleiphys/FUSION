# A Bayesian analysis of light-front models and the nucleon's charmed sigma term

> We present the results of a recent analysis to study the nucleon's charm sigma term, $σ_{c\overline{c}}$. We construct a minimal model in terms of light-front variables and constrain the range of possibilities using extant knowledge from deeply inelastic scattering (DIS) and Bayesian parameter estimation, ultimately computing $σ_{c\overline{c}}$ in an explicitly covariant manner. We find a close correlation between a possible nonperturbative component of the charm structure function, $F^{c\overline{c}}_{2,\, \mathrm{IC}}$, and $σ_{c\overline{c}}$. Independent of prescription for the covariant relativistic quark-nucleon vertex, we determine $σ_{c\overline{c}}$ under several different scenarios for the magnitude of intrinsic charm (IC) in DIS, namely $\langle x \rangle_{c+\overline{c}} = 0.1\%$, $0.35\%$, and $1\%$, obtaining for these $σ_{c\overline{c}} = 4 \pm 4$, $12 \pm 13$, and $32 \pm 34$ MeV, respectively. These results imply the existence of a reciprocity between the IC parton distribution function (PDF) and $σ_{c\overline{c}}$ such that new information from either DIS or improved determinations of $σ_{c \overline{c}}$ could significantly impact constraints to the charm sector of the proton wave function.

## Key claim

The authors find a close correlation between a possible nonperturbative component of the charm structure function and the nucleon's charm sigma term, such that new information from either deeply inelastic scattering or improved determinations of the sigma term could significantly impact constraints on the charm sector of the proton wave function. The results are conditional on the assumed magnitude of intrinsic charm in DIS, with the sigma term values varying substantially across different scenarios.

## Method

The authors construct a minimal light-front model in which the nucleon's charm content resides in a leading 5-quark Fock state, with the interacting charm quark dressed by a scalar spectator of fixed mass. They constrain the model parameters using Bayesian Markov-chain Monte Carlo simulations with pseudo-data corresponding to three different scenarios for the intrinsic charm PDF first moment, then compute the charm sigma term in an explicitly covariant manner using standard Feynman parameter techniques.

## Key numbers

- For the intrinsic charm momentum fraction scenario ⟨x⟩_{c+anti-c} = 0.1%, the charm sigma term is σ_{c anti-c} = 4 ± 4 MeV
- For ⟨x⟩_{c+anti-c} = 0.35%, the charm sigma term is σ_{c anti-c} = 12 ± 13 MeV
- For ⟨x⟩_{c+anti-c} = 1%, the charm sigma term is σ_{c anti-c} = 32 ± 34 MeV
- The high-x behavior of the light-front PDFs is ∼ (1-x)^7, consistent with a 4-quark spectator state, verified by obtaining n_s = 3.99 ± 0.08 from the counting rule analysis
- The charm quark mass input prior is centered at m_c = 1.3 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier treatments of the nucleon's strange quark content (Hobbs 2014, Hobbs 2016) and bare valence quark content (Cloet 2012) to the charm sector, using light-front wave functions as a common framework. The results connect to lattice QCD determinations of the charm sigma term (Freeman 2012, Gong 2013, Abdel-Rehim 2016) which give values in the range 25-125 MeV, and to QCD global analyses of intrinsic charm PDFs (Jimenez-Delgado 2014, Ball 2016, Rojo 2017, Hou 2017). The analysis enables future reciprocal constraints between DIS measurements of intrinsic charm and determinations of the charm sigma term, which could provide guidance to ongoing direct WIMP searches where the charm sigma term plays a decisive role in determining the WIMP-nucleon interaction strength in some beyond-Standard-Model scenarios (Hill 2013).
