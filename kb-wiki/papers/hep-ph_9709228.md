# Charm -- a thermometer of the mixed phase

> A charmed quark experiences drag and diffusion in the quark-gluon plasma, as well as strong interaction with the plasma surface. Our simulations indicate that charmed quarks created in heavy ion collisions will be trapped in the mixed phase and will come to equilibrium in it. Their momentum distribution will thus reflect the temperature at the confinement phase transition.

# Charm -- a thermometer of the mixed phase

## Key claim

Charmed quarks created in heavy ion collisions will be trapped in the mixed phase and come to equilibrium at the confinement transition temperature, so that their final D meson transverse momentum distribution reflects the transition temperature; however, the mean momentum lies above the thermal average due to transverse flow of the droplet fluid, meaning the thermometer is only a rough one.

## Method

The authors simulate the evolution of a charmed quark created in a quark-gluon plasma that cools to the mixed phase, using a Langevin process for diffusion in the plasma and a cascade hydrodynamics code (Bertsch, Gong, and McLerran 1988) that models the mixed phase as droplets of fixed temperature which radiate and absorb pions. When a charmed quark reaches a droplet surface, a flux tube with tension 0.16 GeV² is stretched; if the tube fissions (with a rate per unit length between 0.5 and 2.5 fm⁻²), the quark hadronizes into a D meson outside the droplet, otherwise it is reflected back. D mesons that escape are tracked through collisions with pions and possible reabsorption by droplets.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperature \(T^* = 150\) MeV and \(T^* = 200\) MeV are compared; the D transverse momentum shows sensitivity to a 50 MeV temperature difference that rises above the uncertainty in model parameters.
- Mean thermal \(p_\perp\) of a D meson at \(T^* = 150\) MeV is 820 MeV.
- Flux tube tension \(\sigma = 0.16\) GeV²; fission rate per unit length \(d\Gamma/d\ell = 0.5\) to \(2.5\) fm⁻².
- Droplet radius parameter \(r_0 = 1.0\) or \(1.5\) fm.
- Between 40% and 80% of charmed quarks are trapped inside their original droplets until droplet breakup.
- Recapture by droplets affects equilibration of 10% to 40% of particles.
- A charmed quark needs about 350 MeV to emerge from a droplet as a meson.

## Context

This work extends the Langevin-based treatment of heavy quark diffusion in the quark-gluon plasma developed by Svetitsky (1988, 1989) by adding a detailed simulation of the mixed phase using the droplet cascade model of Bertsch, Gong, and McLerran (1988). It builds on the Bjorken (1983) picture of longitudinal boost-invariant expansion. The results suggest that D meson transverse momentum can serve as a rough thermometer of the confinement phase transition, complementing the information obtained from J/ψ suppression, and motivate experimental measurement of open charm at RHIC.
