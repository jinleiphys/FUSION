# Spin-Flavor van der Waals Forces and NN interaction

> We study the Nucleon-Nucleon interaction in the Born-Oppenheimer approximation at second order in perturbation theory including the Δ resonance as an intermediate state. The potential resembles strongly chiral potentials computed either via soliton models or chiral perturbation theory and has a van der Waals like singularity at short distances which is handled by means of renormalization techniques. Results for the deuteron are discussed.

## Key claim

The Nucleon-Nucleon (NN) interaction can be represented as a spin-flavor van der Waals force derived in the Born-Oppenheimer approximation at second order in perturbation theory, with the Δ resonance as an intermediate state; the resulting potential reproduces the Skyrme soliton model result exactly and is very similar to chiral potentials from chiral perturbation theory, but the short-distance van der Waals singularity requires renormalization.

## Method

The authors compute the NN potential using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation at second order in perturbation theory, replacing the dipole-dipole interaction of atomic physics with the One-Pion-Exchange (OPE) potential and including the Δ resonance as an intermediate excited state. The resulting potential is treated with renormalization via boundary conditions to handle the short-distance van der Waals singularity, and the deuteron is solved by fixing its binding energy, D/S ratio, and scattering length.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy fixed to B_d = 2.224575 MeV
- D/S ratio fixed to η = 0.0256
- ^3S_1 scattering length fixed to a_{^3S_1} = 5.419 fm
- For N_c = 3: A_S = 0.873(8) fm^{-1/2}, r_m = 1.945(14) fm, Q_d = 0.2712(1) fm^2, P_D = 7.3(1.2)%, ⟨r^{-1}⟩ = 0.468(8), a_{^3D_1} = 6.56(5) fm^5, a_{E_1} = 1.549(1) fm^3
- For N_c → ∞: A_S = 0.886(9) fm^{-1/2}, r_m = 1.973(18) fm, Q_d = 0.2789(12) fm^2, P_D = 6.8(1.4)%, ⟨r^{-1}⟩ = 0.44(1), a_{^3D_1} = 6.471(9) fm^5, a_{E_1} = 1.689(3) fm^3
- The axial coupling g_A is varied between 1.26 and 1.29, and the estimate errors correspond to these extreme values

## Context

This work extends the molecular analogy for nuclear forces previously discussed by Ruiz Arriola (2009) within chiral soliton models, showing that the NN potential from the Born-Oppenheimer approximation reproduces the Skyrme model result of Walet (1992) exactly and matches the chiral two-pion-exchange potential at NLO-Δ from Kaiser (1998) and Krebs (2007) at short distances. The approach enables a unified description of two- and three-nucleon forces, with the three-body force containing the old Fujita-Miyazawa force (1957), and suggests a workable scheme for multi-nucleon forces once renormalization of singular three-body problems is achieved.
