# Emergent four-body parameter in universal two-species bosonic systems

> The description of unitary few-boson systems is conceptually simple: only one parameter -- the three-body binding energy -- is required to predict the binding energies of clusters with an arbitrary number of bosons. Whether this correlation between the three- and many-boson systems still holds for two species of bosons for which only the inter-species interaction is resonant depends on how many particles of each species are in the system. For few-body clusters with species $A$ and $B$ and a resonant $AB$ interaction, it is known that the emergent $AAB$ and $ABB$ three-body scales are correlated to the ground-state binding energies of the $AAAB$ and $ABBB$ systems, respectively. We find that this link between three and four bodies is broken for the $AABB$ tetramer whose binding energy is neither constrained by the $AAB$ nor by the $ABB$ trimer. From this de-correlation, we predict the existence of a scale unique to the $AABB$ tetramer. In our explanation of this phenomenon, we understand the $AABB$ and $AAAB$/$ABBB$ tetramers as representatives of two different universal classes of $N$-body systems with distinct renormalization-group and discrete-scaling properties.

## Key claim

The authors find that the binding energy of the $AABB$ tetramer (a four-body cluster with two species of bosons where only the inter-species interaction is resonant) is not constrained by either the $AAB$ or $ABB$ trimer binding energies, breaking the correlation between three- and four-body scales that holds for the $AAAB$ and $ABBB$ tetramers. This decorrelation implies the existence of a scale unique to the $AABB$ tetramer, which the authors explain by classifying the $AABB$ and $AAAB$/$ABBB$ tetramers as representatives of two different universal classes of $N$-body systems (the "circle" and "dandelion" classes) with distinct renormalization-group and discrete-scaling properties.

## Method

The authors model the two-species bosonic systems using zero-range contact interactions regularized with a Gaussian cut-off function, with the two-body coupling calibrated to approximate a unitary scattering length. They introduce a three-body potential to prevent the Thomas collapse and renormalize it separately for the 3-circle and 3-dandelion systems to reproduce a ground-state binding energy of $B_3=0.01$ (in units where $\hbar=c=m=1$). Ground-state energies for three-, four-, and five-body representatives of the circle and dandelion classes are calculated using the stochastic variational method, with benchmark checks against the refined-resonating-group method, over a range of cut-off radii $R_c$ to test convergence or collapse.

## Key numbers

- The discrete scaling factor for the 3-dandelion (equal masses) is $(1986.1)^2$, while for the 3-circle it is $(22.7)^2$.
- For the four-body dandelion (representing $AAAB$/$ABBB$), the ratio $B_4^{\rm dand} / B_3^{\rm dand} \simeq 11$ in the large cut-off limit.
- For the four-body circle (representing $AABB$), the ratio $B_4^{\rm circ} / B_3^{\rm circ} \simeq 0.2$ in the large cut-off limit.
- For the five-body dandelion, $B_5^{\rm dand} / B_3^{\rm dand} \simeq 30$; for the five-body circle, $B_5^{\rm circ} / B_3^{\rm circ} \simeq 0.06$.
- The three-body ground-state binding energy is set to $B_3=0.01$ (in units where $\hbar=c=m=1$).
- Cut-off ranges used: $R_c \in [0.01,1]$ for dandelions and $R_c \in [0.1,10]$ for circles.

## Context

This work extends the study of universal few-body systems with resonant interactions, originally established for identical bosons by Efimov (1970) and later for two-species three-body systems (Tango configurations) as observed experimentally with ${}^{87}{\rm Rb}$ and ${}^{41}{\rm K}$ trimers by Ferlaino et al. (2008). The authors build on prior numerical results for $AAAB$ clusters by Blume et al. (2012) and for $ABBB$ clusters by Blume et al. (2014), which showed that those tetramer binding energies are correlated to the trimer binding energies. The present work contradicts the naive expectation that this correlation extends to the $AABB$ system, and instead predicts that the $AABB$ tetramer requires its own four-body force at leading order, in contrast to the four-boson system where such a force is subleading (as shown by Platter et al. (2011) and Bazak et al. (2018)). The results enable experimental tests using heteronuclear systems such as ${}^{85}{\rm Rb}$-${}^{87}{\rm Rb}$ or comparisons of $B_4^{AABB} / B_3^{AAB}$ ratios across systems with different mass imbalances.
