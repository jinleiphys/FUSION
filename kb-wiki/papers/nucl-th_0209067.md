# Selected Topics in Near Threshold Pion Photoproduction and Compton Scattering off Nucleons

> Some open topics in the field of low energy photon-nucleon interactions are discussed--which in my opinion are of interest both for obtaining new information on nucleon structure as well as for precision tests of our theoretical understanding of chiral dynamics. In particular, I discuss p-wave multipoles in charged pion photoproduction off protons as well as the role of spin-dependent effects in (unpolarized) Compton scattering off nucleons. There the concept of dynamical spin polarizabilities is found to be essential for the understanding of differential Compton cross sections above 120 MeV cms photon energy.

# Selected Topics in Near Threshold Pion Photoproduction and Compton Scattering off Nucleons

## Key claim
The concept of dynamical spin polarizabilities, energy-dependent multipole structures that reduce to static polarizabilities in the zero-energy limit, is essential for understanding unpolarized differential Compton cross sections above 120 MeV cms photon energy, and these dynamical quantities can be extracted from existing unpolarized data without requiring polarized beams, provided the spin-independent dipole polarizabilities are already known.

## Method
The author reviews the multipole expansion for pion photoproduction, truncating at p-wave level to obtain three bi-linear combinations (A, B, C) of the four complex low-energy multipoles E0+, P1, P2, P3 from the unpolarized differential cross section. For Compton scattering, the author defines dynamical polarizabilities as energy-dependent functions derived from the multipole expansion at l=1, and compares predictions from dispersion analysis, leading-one-loop heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (ChPT), and leading-one-loop small-scale expansion (SSE) with explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers
- The low-energy expansion of the Compton cross section truncated at omega^2 is valid only up to approximately 70 MeV photon energy, whereas most Compton data start above this range.
- A truncation of the multipole expansion for Compton scattering at l=1 is consistent with all existing unpolarized Compton data on the proton up to cms photon energies of 170 MeV.
- At l=1, there are 6 dynamical structures to determine: the electric and magnetic dipole polarizabilities alpha_E1(omega), beta_M1(omega), plus four dynamical dipole spin-polarizabilities gamma_M1M1(omega), gamma_E1E1(omega), gamma_M1E2(omega), gamma_E1M2(omega).
- Sensitivity to the dynamical dipole spin polarizabilities in unpolarized Compton scattering appears in the photon energy region above 120 MeV (cms).
- For charged pion photoproduction, existing predictions for p-wave multipoles show discrepancies with dispersion relation analyses of up to 30%.

## Context
This paper extends the theoretical framework for low-energy photon-nucleon interactions beyond the static polarizabilities (alpha_E, beta_M) that have been the focus of most prior work, as summarized by Olmos (2002) for Compton data. The concept of dynamical polarizabilities was introduced by Hemmert and collaborators (2002) to address the mismatch between the low-energy expansion (valid only below ~70 MeV) and the energy range of actual data. The work builds on heavy baryon ChPT calculations by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1997) for neutral pion photoproduction, and on the SSE framework of Hemmert, Holstein, and Kambor (1998) that includes explicit Delta resonance degrees of freedom. The author suggests that experimental mapping of the energy dependence of dynamical polarizabilities could resolve discrepancies between chiral effective field theories and dispersion analyses for mixed dipole spin-polarizabilities, and that combining the SSE framework with a chiral potential ansatz may clarify puzzling results in Compton scattering off the deuteron reported by Bentz (2002).
