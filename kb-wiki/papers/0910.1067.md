# Radiation of an electric charge in the field of a magnetic monopole

> We consider the radiation of photons from quarks scattering on color-magnetic monopoles in the Quark-Gluon Plasma. We consider a temperature regime $T\gsim2T_c$, where monopoles can be considered as static, rare objects embedded into matter consisting mostly of the usual "electric" quasiparticles, quarks and gluons. The calculation is performed in the classical, non-relativistic approximation and results are compared to photon emission from Coulomb scattering of quarks, known to provide a significant contribution to the photon emission rates from QGP. The present study is a first step towards understanding whether this scattering process can give a sizeable contribution to dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions. Our results are encouraging: by comparing the magnitudes of the photon emission rate for the two processes, we find a dominance in the case of quark-monopole scattering. Our results display strong sensitivity to finite densities of quarks and monopoles.

## Key claim

The paper argues that soft photon emission from quarks scattering on color-magnetic monopoles in the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) could be as large as previously accounted mechanisms, but only under the specific condition that finite densities of quarks and monopoles provide natural cutoffs on impact parameters; without such cutoffs, Coulomb scattering dominates.

## Method

The authors computed classical, non-relativistic photon radiation from an electric charge moving on a conical trajectory in the Coulomb-like magnetic field of a static monopole, using the dipole approximation and ignoring back reaction. They integrated the Fourier-transformed acceleration over impact parameter with cutoffs set by monopole density (from lattice data) and quark density (from a PNJL model), then compared the resulting energy radiated per unit volume to that from quark-quark and quark-antiquark Coulomb scattering in the same approximations.

## Key numbers

- Monopole density at T ~ 2 T_c: n_M ~ 0.02 GeV^3, giving b_max ~ 1.8 GeV^{-1} (from lattice data scaled from SU(2) to SU(3) by a factor 2).
- Monopole core radius: r_M ~ 0.15 fm = 0.78 GeV^{-1}, used as b_min.
- Quark thermal mass at T = 2 T_c: m ~ 0.8 T ~ 0.3 GeV (interpolated from lattice results at 1.5 T_c and 3 T_c).
- Quark density: n_q ~ 2.8 T^3 ~ 0.12 GeV^3, giving b_max ~ 1 GeV^{-1} for Coulomb scattering.
- Typical quark velocity range considered: v ~ 0.5 to 0.7.
- Strong coupling used: alpha_s = 0.8.
- Ratio of total radiated energy (quark-monopole vs Coulomb scattering) shows dominance of monopole scattering for finite b_max, with the ratio depending on v; for v = 0.7 the ratio is above 1 for omega below about 0.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the "magnetic scenario" for QGP proposed by Liao and Shuryak (2006), which suggested that near-T_c matter is dominated by monopoles and explained the low viscosity observed at RHIC. It also builds on lattice evidence for physical monopoles from DeGrand and Toussaint (1980) and D'Alessandro and D'Elia (2007). The calculation is a first step toward understanding whether quark-monopole scattering can contribute to the dilepton excess observed by the NA50/60 experiments at CERN SPS in the low-mass region below the rho meson mass (0.77 GeV), which remains unexplained by standard hadronic cocktail and QGP annihilation contributions. The authors plan a full quantum and relativistic treatment as the next step.
