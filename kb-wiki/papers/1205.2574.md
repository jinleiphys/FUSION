# Virial expansion coefficients in the harmonic approximation

> The virial expansion method is applied within a harmonic approximation to an interacting N-body system of identical fermions. We compute the canonical partition functions for two and three particles to get the two lowest orders in the expansion. The energy spectrum is carefully interpolated to reproduce ground state properties at low temperature and the non-interacting large temperature limit of constant virial coefficients. This resembles the smearing of shell effects in finite systems with increasing temperature. Numerical results are discussed for the second and third virial coefficients as function of dimension, temperature, interaction, and the transition temperature between low and high energy limits.

## Key claim

The virial expansion coefficients for identical fermions can be computed within a harmonic approximation to the Hamiltonian, but the coefficients diverge at high temperature unless a temperature-dependent cutoff is introduced to force the interacting spectrum to approach the non-interacting spectrum at large excitation energy.

## Method

The authors compute the canonical partition functions \(Q_2\) and \(Q_3\) for two and three identical spin-polarized fermions by exact diagonalization of a harmonic-oscillator Hamiltonian whose one- and two-body terms are replaced by quadratic forms. The two-body interaction frequency \(\omega_{in}\) and constant shift \(V_S\) are fitted to reproduce the exact two-body ground state energy and spatial extension in a harmonic trap. To obtain finite virial coefficients at high temperature, the interaction frequency and energy shift are multiplied by cutoff functions \(F(T)\) and \(G(T)\) that vanish as \(1/T^D\) and \(1/T^{D-1}\) respectively, with a single cutoff parameter \(T_0\).

## Key numbers

* For \(\Delta b_2\) in two dimensions at high temperature, the leading divergence is \(T^2\left(\frac{1}{4\Theta_r^2}-\frac{1}{4\Theta_0^2}\right)\) before regularization, where \(\Theta_r = \hbar\omega_r/k_B\) and \(\Theta_0 = \hbar\omega_0/k_B\).
* For \(\Delta b_2\) in three dimensions at high temperature, the leading divergence is \(T^3\left(\frac{1}{2\Theta_r^3}-\frac{1}{2\Theta_0^3}\right)\) before regularization.
* After regularization with the cutoff \(F(T) = (T_0/(T+T_0))^D\), the high-temperature limit of \(\Delta b_2\) in two dimensions is \(-\frac{N}{8}\frac{\omega_{in}^2}{\omega_0^2}\frac{(k_B T_0)^2}{(\hbar\omega_0)^2} - \frac{V_S k_B T_0}{4(\hbar\omega_0)^2}\) for \(N=2\).
* After regularization, the high-temperature limit of \(\Delta b_2\) in three dimensions is \(-\frac{3N}{8}\frac{\omega_{in}^2}{\omega_0^2}\frac{(k_B T_0)^3}{(\hbar\omega_0)^3} - \frac{V_S (k_B T_0)^2}{2(\hbar\omega_0)^3}\) for \(N=2\).
* The critical temperature for washing out shell gaps is estimated as \(k_B T \approx \hbar\omega_r/(2\pi^2)\), giving a factor of about 19.7.

## Context

This work extends the harmonic approximation method previously applied to few-body thermodynamics by Armstrong et al. (2012) to the virial expansion formalism, which is usually formulated via the scattering S-matrix as in Dashen, Ma, and Bernstein (1969) and applied to cold atomic gases by LeClair (2012) and others. The approach enables computation of virial coefficients for fermions in a harmonic trap without solving the full scattering problem, but requires a cutoff to handle the unphysical divergence arising from the harmonic potential's non-vanishing nature at high energy. The method is intended for future applications to cold atomic gases, nuclear physics, and condensed-matter systems.
