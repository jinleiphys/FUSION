# P- and T-violating Schiff moment of the Mercury nucleus

> The Schiff moment of the $^{199}$Hg nucleus was calculated using finite range P- and T-violating weak nucleon-nucleon interaction. Effects of the core polarization were considered in the framework of RPA with effective residual forces.

# P- and T-violating Schiff moment of the Mercury nucleus

## Key claim
The Schiff moment of $^{199}$Hg is calculated using finite-range P- and T-violating weak nucleon-nucleon interaction with core polarization effects included via RPA, yielding values that are significantly smaller in the isoscalar and isotensor channels than previous contact-interaction calculations, but the finite-range effects alone are not very significant, the dominant reduction comes from core polarization.

## Method
The Schiff moment operator was renormalized using RPA with effective residual strong forces (Migdal-type interaction), treating the weak interaction perturbatively. The single-particle basis was obtained from a partially self-consistent Woods-Saxon mean field including continuum states. The weak interaction was the finite-range pion-exchange P- and T-odd potential with isoscalar, isovector, and isotensor couplings. The spurious center-of-mass component was subtracted analytically using contour integration in the complex frequency plane.

## Key numbers
- For $^{199}$Hg, the total Schiff moment is $S = -0.0004\, gg_0 - 0.055\, gg_1 + 0.009\, gg_2$ [e fm$^3$] (Eq. 20).
- After rescaling to compare with contact interaction conventions: $S = (-0.01\, g\tilde{g}_0 - 0.86\, g\tilde{g}_1 + 0.14\, g\tilde{g}_2) \times 10^{-8}$ [e fm$^3$] (Eq. 22).
- Without core polarization but with finite-range interaction: $S = -1.35 \times 10^{-8}\, g(\tilde{g}_0 + \tilde{g}_1 - 2\tilde{g}_2)$ [e fm$^3$] (Eq. 24).
- With contact interaction and no core polarization: $S = -0.96 \times 10^{-8}\, g(\tilde{g}_0 + \tilde{g}_1 - 2\tilde{g}_2)$ [e fm$^3$] (Eq. 23).
- The spurious dipole mode was suppressed to $\omega_0 = 0.1$ keV using $f_{in} = 0.3935$, $f_{ex} = -2.6$, $f' = 1.07$.
- Pairing gap for $^{199}$Hg: $\Delta_n = 0.69$ MeV from mass differences.
- Core polarization renormalization for $^{209}$Bi changed the valence proton contribution by approximately 15%.

## Context
This work extends earlier simplified calculations of the Schiff moment for $^{199}$Hg that used contact (zero-range) weak interactions without many-body nuclear structure effects, such as those by Flambaum and Ginges (2002) and by Flambaum, Khriplovich, and Sushkov (1986). It also builds on the study of polarization effects from coupling to the isoscalar dipole compression mode by Haxton and Henley (2000). The results show that core polarization effects are large and must be included for reliable extraction of P- and T-violating nuclear interaction parameters from atomic electric dipole moment measurements of $^{199}$Hg.
