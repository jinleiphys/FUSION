# High Spin Spectroscopy and Shape Evolution in 105Cd

> High spin states in 105Cd were studied using 16O beam on 92Mo reaction at an incident beam energy of 75 MeV. The level scheme of 105Cd has been observed up to an excitation energy of 10.8 MeV with the addition of 30 new gamma transitions to the previous work. Spin and parity for most of the reported levels are assigned from the DCO ratios and linear polarization measurements. The microscopic origin of the investigated band structures is discussed in the context of triaxial projected shell model. The energies of observed positive and negative parity bands agree with the predictions of the TPSM by considering triaxial deformation for the observed excited band structures. The shape evolution with increasing angular momentum is explained in the framework of Cranked Shell Model and the Total Routhian Surface calculations.

# High Spin Spectroscopy and Shape Evolution in 105Cd

## Key claim
The level scheme of 105Cd has been extended to an excitation energy of approximately 10.8 MeV with 30 new gamma transitions, and the observed band structures agree with triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) predictions only when triaxial deformation is considered for the excited band structures, not simply axial symmetry.

## Method
High-spin states in 105Cd were populated via the 92Mo(16O, 2pn)105Cd fusion-evaporation reaction at a beam energy of 75 MeV using the 14UD Pelletron accelerator at TIFR, Mumbai. Gamma-ray coincidence events were detected using the Indian National Gamma Array (INGA) comprising fifteen Compton-suppressed clover Ge detectors, and more than two billion gamma-gamma and higher-fold coincidence events were recorded. Spin and parity assignments were made using directional correlation orientation (DCO) ratios and integrated polarization directional correlation from oriented nuclei (IPDCO) measurements, and the band structures were analyzed within the cranked shell model (CSM), total Routhian surface (TRS) calculations, and the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM).

## Key numbers
- Level scheme observed up to spin Jpi = (47/2-) and excitation energy approximately 10.8 MeV
- 30 new gamma-ray transitions identified and placed in the level scheme
- Beam energy of 75 MeV used for the 92Mo(16O, 2pn)105Cd reaction
- Target thickness of 1 mg/cm2 on 10 mg/cm2 Au backing
- More than two billion gamma-gamma and higher-fold coincidence events recorded
- The 539 keV transition in band B4 used as reference with relative intensity set to 100%
- Dynamic moment of inertia for the yrast negative parity band remains constant at approximately 30 hbar2 MeV-1 (from prior work cited in the paper)
- Energy of the 1293 keV transition reassigned from E2 to M1 character based on measured R_DCO = 0.57(4) and IPDCO = -0.19(5)
- The 1064 keV transition measured with R_DCO = 0.65(9) and IPDCO = 0.05(6), consistent with E1 character
- The 1009 keV transition measured with R_DCO = 0.79(10) and IPDCO = -0.12(4), consistent with M1 character

## Context
This work extends the high-spin spectroscopy of 105Cd beyond the previous studies by Regan et al. and Jerrestam et al., which had established the level scheme up to Jpi = 47/2- for negative parity states but had limited information on positive parity and non-yrast negative parity states. The new band structures and spin-parity assignments, particularly the reassignment of the 1293 keV transition from E2 to M1 and the identification of band B2, modify the understanding of shape evolution in this nucleus. The results enable systematic comparisons with lighter odd-A Cd isotopes such as 103Cd and 107Cd, and test the triaxial projected shell model predictions for transitional nuclei near the N = Z = 50 shell closure.
