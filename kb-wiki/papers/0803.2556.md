# A new Godunov-type numerical code with a non-linear Riemann solver for equations of relativistic hydrodynamics

> We present a second-order upwind numerical scheme for equations of relativistic hydrodynamics with a source term. A new non-linear Riemann solver is constructed. Solution of a Riemann problem on a cells boundary is based on exact relations in case of zero tangential velocities. In this sense our solver is "exact". In case of non-zero tangential velocities a reasonable approximation is made that allows to avoid solution of very complicated exact equations. The scheme is tested on several one- and three-dimensional solutions demonstrating a good performance for both strong shocks and strong rarefactions.

## Key claim

The authors present a second-order Godunov-type numerical scheme for relativistic hydrodynamics that uses a Riemann solver which is "exact" (based on exact relations) only in the case of zero tangential velocities; for non-zero tangential velocities a reasonable approximation is made to avoid solving the complicated exact equations.

## Method

The scheme is a second-order upwind Godunov-type method following Falle & Komissarov (2000), using a non-linear Riemann solver that first constructs a pure shock-wave solution via Newton's method and then checks whether each wave should be replaced by a simple rarefaction wave based on the sign of the fluid velocity relative to the shock front in the rest frame. For one-dimensional flows the solver is exact, while for multi-dimensional flows with non-zero tangential velocities an approximate treatment is employed. Second-order accuracy in space is achieved by linear reconstruction inside cells with a slope limiter, and second-order in time via a predictor-corrector step using an intermediate solution at half the time step.

## Key numbers

- The scheme is second-order accurate in both space and time.
- The Riemann solver handles systems of 5 equations (for 3D) or 3 equations (for 1D) for the primitive variables (energy density, baryon number density, and velocity components).
- The condition for causality is given as \(\sqrt{\mbox{Q}_x^2+\mbox{Q}_y^2+\mbox{Q}_z^2}<\mbox{Q}_1\).
- The speed of sound is defined as \(\mbox{c}_s\left( \varepsilon,\mbox{n}\right)=\sqrt{\left. \partial \mbox{p}/\partial \varepsilon \right|_{\mbox{s}}}\).
- The shock speed in the rest frame of the fluid ahead is given by \(\mbox{v}^{\pm}_{12}\left(\varepsilon_1, \mbox{n}_1, \varepsilon_2, \mbox{n}_2 \right)= \pm \sqrt{ \frac{\left( \mbox{p}_2 - \mbox{p}_1 \right) \left( \varepsilon_2 + \mbox{p}_1 \right)}{\left( \varepsilon_2 - \varepsilon_1 \right) \left( \varepsilon_1 + \mbox{p}_2 \right)}}\).

## Context

This work extends the Godunov-type approach of Falle & Komissarov (2000) from one- and two-dimensional to three-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamics, incorporating a source term for applications such as ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions. It contrasts with approximate Riemann solvers like Roe-type (Eulderink & Mellema, 1995) and HLLE methods (Schneider et al., 1993; Duncan & Hughes, 1994) by providing an exact treatment for one-dimensional flows, and with two-shock approximations (Balsara, 1994; Dai & Woodward, 1994) by correctly handling rarefaction waves. The code is intended for simulating relativistic flows in astrophysics (active galactic nuclei, gamma-ray bursts) and heavy-ion collisions, where strong shocks and rarefactions must be treated reliably.
