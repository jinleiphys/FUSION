# Extra S11 and P13 in the Hypercentral Constituent Quark Model

> We report on the recent results of the hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (hCQM). The model contains a spin independent three-quark interaction which is inspired by Lattice QCD calculations and reproduces the average energy values of the SU(6) multiplets. The splittings within each multiplet are obtained with a SU(6)-breaking interaction, which can include also an isospin dependent term. All the 3- and 4-stars resonances are well reproduced. Moreover, as all the Constituent Quark models, the hCQM predicts ``missing'' resonances ({\em e.g.} extra $S11$ and $P13$ states) which can be of some help for the experimental identification of new resonances. The model provides also a good description of the medium $Q^2$-behavior of the electromagnetic transition form factors. In particular the calculated helicity amplitude $A_{1/2}$ for the $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance agrees very well with the recent CLAS data. More recently, the elastic nucleon form factors have been calculated using a relativistic version of the hCQM and a relativistic quark current.

# Extra S11 and P13 in the Hypercentral Constituent Quark Model

## Key claim

The hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (hCQM) predicts five "missing" resonances below 1900 MeV, including extra S11 and P13 states, which may aid experimental identification of new resonances; recent analyses show indications for a third S11 and a third P13 with masses comparable to the hCQM predictions, though these states have not yet received experimental confirmation.

## Method

The authors use a three-quark Hamiltonian with an SU(6)-invariant hypercentral potential (linear plus Coulomb-like, dependent only on the hyperradius) inspired by lattice QCD, plus a perturbative SU(6)-breaking hyperfine term. The potential parameters are fitted to the baryon spectrum, with the hyperfine strength fixed by the Delta-Nucleon mass difference. A generalized interaction including isospin-dependent and spin-isospin-dependent terms is also introduced to improve the reproduction of resonances such as the Roper. Electromagnetic transition form factors and helicity amplitudes are calculated using non-relativistic quark currents, while elastic nucleon form factors are computed with a relativistic version of the model including boosts and a relativistic quark current.

## Key numbers

- Quark mass taken as 1/3 of the nucleon mass
- Potential parameters: α = 1.61 fm⁻², τ = 4.59
- hCQM predicts 5 missing resonances with energies below 1900 MeV
- S11(1535) helicity amplitude A₁/₂ calculated with hCQM agrees well with CLAS data, with discrepancies at low Q²
- For the S11 states: PDG lists 1535 and 1650 MeV; hCQM predicts 1507, 1574, 1887, 1937 MeV; hCQM with isospin term predicts 1524, 1688, 1861, 2008 MeV
- For the P13 states: PDG lists 1720 and 1900 MeV; hCQM predicts 1797, 1835, 1853, 1863 MeV; hCQM with isospin term predicts 1848, 1816, 1894, 1939 MeV
- For D13 states: PDG lists 1520 and 1700 MeV; hCQM predicts 1526, 1606, 1899, 1969 MeV
- For P33 states: PDG lists 1232, 1600, 1920 MeV; hCQM predicts 1240, 1727, 1843, 1856, 2104 MeV
- Relativistic calculation of the ratio between electric and magnetic proton form factors reaches almost 50% decrease, not far from recent TJNAF data

## Context

This work extends the hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (hCQM) previously developed by the same group, which uses a hypercentral potential inspired by lattice QCD calculations (Bali, 2000). The model reproduces all 3- and 4-star resonances from the Particle Data Group, similar to the Capstick model (Capstick, 1992) and the Goldstone Boson Exchange model (Olofsson, 1998; Plessas, 2000), but with the advantage of predicting missing resonances that may guide experimental searches. The results enable future experimental identification of new S11 and P13 states, as suggested by analyses from Saghai (2001) and Ripani (2003), and provide a framework for calculating electromagnetic properties of baryons including transition form factors and elastic nucleon form factors.
