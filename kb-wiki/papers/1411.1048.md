# A unified description of the reaction dynamics from pp to pA to AA collisions

> There is little doubt that in heavy ion collisions at the LHC and RHIC, we observe a hydrodynamically expanding system, providing strong evidence for the formation of a Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) in the early stage of such collisions. These observations are mainly based on results on azimuthal anisotropies, but also on particle spectra of identified particles, perfectly compatible with a hydrodynamic evolution. Surprisingly, in p-Pb collisions one observes a very similar behavior, and to some extent even in p-p. We take these experimental observations as a strong support for a unified approach to describe proton-proton (p-p), proton-nucleus (p-A), and nucleus-nucleus (A-A) collisions, with a plasma formation even in tiny systems as in p-p scatterings.

## Key claim
The authors argue that experimental observations of collective flow signatures in p-Pb and even p-p collisions strongly support a unified approach where a Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) is formed in all collision systems, from p-p to A-A, with the same hydrodynamic description applied to each.

## Method
The EPOS3 model is employed, combining a Gribov-Regge multiple scattering approach (Parton-Based Gribov-Regge Theory, PBGRT) with DGLAP parton ladders and a saturation scale to generate initial conditions. A core-corona separation is performed at an early proper time, where the core part undergoes a viscous hydrodynamic expansion (with η/s=0.08) followed by statistical hadronization via a Cooper-Frye procedure and a final-state hadronic cascade using UrQMD.

## Key numbers
- The viscous hydrodynamic evolution uses a shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s = 0.08.
- Proton transverse momentum spectra in p-Pb at 5.02 TeV are compared for four multiplicity classes with mean values of 8, 84, 160, and 235 charged tracks (within |η|<2.4).
- The Λ/Ks ratio in p-Pb at 5.02 TeV shows a peak structure with a maximum around 2-3 GeV/c, more pronounced for the 0-5% highest multiplicity class compared to 60-80%.
- The elliptical flow coefficient v2 for pions and protons in p-Pb shows a mass splitting in the pt range of 1-1.5 GeV/c, where the proton result is clearly below the pion one.

## Context
This work extends the hydrodynamic description of heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies to smaller systems, motivated by experimental ridge structures observed in p-p by CMS (2010) and in p-Pb by ALICE (2012), CMS (2012), and ATLAS (2013). The EPOS3 model is compared against QGSJETII (no flow), AMPT (partonic cascade), and EPOS LHC (parametrized flow), showing that only the full hydrodynamic treatment reproduces the multiplicity-dependent hardening of spectra and the Λ/Ks peak structure. This enables a unified framework for all collision systems, supporting QGP formation even in p-p scatterings.
