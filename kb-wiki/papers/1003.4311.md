# Octupole deformation properties of the Barcelona-Catania-Paris energy density functionals

> We discuss the octupole deformation properties of the recently proposed Barcelona-Catania-Paris (BCP) energy density functionals for two sets of isotopes, those of radium and barium, where it is believed that octupole deformation plays a role in the description of the ground state. The analysis is carried out in the mean field framework (Hartree- Fock- Bogoliubov approximation) by using the axially symmetric octupole moment as a constraint. The main ingredients entering the octupole collective Hamiltonian are evaluated and the lowest lying octupole eigenstates are obtained. In this way we restore, in an approximate way, the parity symmetry spontaneously broken by the mean field and also incorporate octupole fluctuations around the ground state solution. For each isotope the energy of the lowest lying $1^{-}$state and the $B(E1)$ and $B(E3)$ transition probabilities have been computed and compared to both the experimental data and the results obtained in the same framework with the Gogny D1S interaction, which are used here as a well established benchmark. Finally, the octupolarity of the configurations involved in the way down to fission of $^{240}$Pu, which is strongly connected to the asymmetric fragment mass distribution, is studied. We confirm with this thorough study the suitability of the BCP functionals to describe octupole related phenomena.

# Octupole deformation properties of the Barcelona-Catania-Paris energy density functionals

## Key claim

The BCP energy density functionals are suitable for describing octupole-related phenomena in nuclei, but the claim is conditional on the comparison being made primarily against the Gogny D1S interaction as a benchmark rather than against experimental data alone; the authors state that BCP results "look very similar" to Gogny D1S results for potential energy curves and single-particle structures, with the main quantitative differences arising from the higher effective mass (1 versus 0.7) and lower pairing correlations in BCP1.

## Method

The authors performed axially symmetric Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using a constrained octupole moment Q3 = r^3 Y_30 to generate potential energy curves for radium isotopes (A=216 to 232) and barium isotopes (A=140 to 150). They then constructed a one-dimensional collective Schrodinger equation using the Generator Coordinate Method with Gaussian Overlap Approximation, incorporating collective inertias from the adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock (ATDHF) cranking approximation and zero-point energy corrections, to compute the lowest 1- state energies and B(E1) and B(E3) transition probabilities. The octupole properties of the fission valley of 240Pu were also studied.

## Key numbers

- The depth of the octupole deformed minimum reaches its maximum value of 1.5 MeV for the nucleus 222Ra, which is considered the strongest octupole deformed nucleus in the radium chain.
- The BCP functionals give rms deviations for ground-state energies of 161 even-even spherical nuclei of 1.77 MeV (BCP1) and 2.06 MeV (BCP2), compared to 1.71 MeV for Skyrme SLy4, 2.41 MeV for Gogny D1S, and 3.58 MeV for relativistic NL3 parametrization.
- The BCP functional is a factor between 6 to 10 faster computationally than Gogny D1S, with comparable computational cost to Skyrme.
- For 224Ra, the self-consistent quadrupole minimum at Q3=0 occurs at Q2=8.12 b for Gogny D1S and 7.88 b for BCP1.
- The octupole deformed self-consistent minimum in 224Ra is located at around Q3=4 b^(3/2).
- For protons in 224Ra, a K=5/2- level at Q3=0 lies at -4.5 MeV in the D1S case and at -4.2 MeV in the BCP case.
- The zero-point energy correction varies by typically around half an MeV in the interval between Q3=0 b^(3/2) and Q3 approximately 5 b^(3/2).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of quadrupole deformation properties using BCP functionals (Robledo 2008) to the octupole degree of freedom, comparing results against the well-established Gogny D1S interaction (Decharge 1980, Berger 1984) which had been used in earlier octupole studies of radium isotopes (Egido 1989, 1990, 1991). The BCP functionals are inspired by the same principles as the Fayans functionals (Fayans 1998, 2001) and are aimed at describing not only masses and radii but also the low-lying spectrum across the nuclide chart. The results enable future work implementing a self-consistent blocking procedure for odd-A nuclei in the BCP framework, which the authors state is under way.
