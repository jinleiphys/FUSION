# Quasielastic scattering with the Relativistic Green's Function approach

> A relativistic model for quasielastic (QE) lepton-nucleus scattering is presented. The effects of final-state interactions (FSI) between the ejected nucleon and the residual nucleus are described in the relativistic Green's function (RGF) model where FSI are consistently described with exclusive scattering using a complex optical potential. The results of the model are compared with experimental results of electron and neutrino scattering.

# Quasielastic scattering with the Relativistic Green's Function approach

## Key claim
The Relativistic Green's Function (RGF) model describes final-state interactions (FSI) in inclusive quasielastic lepton-nucleus scattering using the same complex optical potential as in exclusive scattering, but with the imaginary part redistributing flux across all channels rather than absorbing it. The model produces cross sections that agree with MiniBooNE charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) data without requiring an increased nucleon axial mass, though the use of phenomenological optical potentials prevents disentangling the role of different reaction processes.

## Method
The authors compute inclusive electron and neutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections within the relativistic impulse approximation (IA) using the RGF model, where the hadron tensor components are expressed via a spectral representation of the single-particle optical model Green's function. This representation involves a biorthogonal expansion in eigenfunctions of the non-Hermitian optical potential and its Hermitian conjugate, requiring matrix elements of the same type as in relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) but for both the optical potential and its conjugate. Bound nucleon states are self-consistent Dirac-Hartree solutions from a relativistic mean-field approach, and two parameterizations of the relativistic optical potential are used: the energy-dependent and A-dependent (EDAD1) and the energy-dependent but A-independent (EDAI) complex phenomenological potentials.

## Key numbers
- For $^{16}$O$(e,e^{\prime})$: beam energy $\varepsilon = 1080$ MeV, scattering angle $\vartheta = 32^{\circ}$; for $^{56}$Fe$(e,e^{\prime})$: beam energy $\varepsilon = 2020$ MeV, scattering angle $\vartheta = 20^{\circ}$ (comparison with experimental data shown in Fig. 1)
- Standard value of the nucleon axial mass used in all neutrino calculations: $M_A = 1.03$ GeV/$c^2$
- MiniBooNE CCQE flux-averaged double-differential cross sections on $^{12}$C shown for three bins of $\cos\vartheta_{\mu}$: $0.4 \leq \cos\vartheta_{\mu} \leq 0.5$, $0.7 \leq \cos\vartheta_{\mu} \leq 0.8$, and $0.8 \leq \cos\vartheta_{\mu} \leq 0.9$, as a function of muon kinetic energy $T_{\mu}$
- MiniBooNE neutral-current elastic (NCE) data reported for $Q^2$ range up to approximately 1.65 (GeV/$c)^2$
- RGF-EDAI cross sections for antineutrino scattering are larger than data up to $E_{\bar\nu} \approx 1.5$ GeV, while RGF-EDAD1 gives better agreement

## Context
This work extends the RGF model, originally developed for quasielastic electron scattering and successfully tested against electron data (Anghinolfi 1996 for $^{16}$O; Fe56 data), to neutrino-nucleus scattering. The model addresses the longstanding discrepancy where relativistic Fermi gas and other IA-based models (Benhar 2010, Butkevich 2010, Juszczak 2010) underestimate MiniBooNE CCQE cross sections unless the axial mass is artificially increased. The RGF results are larger than those from relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA) and relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation using only the real part of the optical potential (rROP). The authors note that multinucleon excitations (Benhar 2010, Martini 2010, Nieves 2011) and two-body currents (Amaro 2011) may also contribute significantly, and that a better phenomenological optical potential fulfilling dispersion relations would improve the model.
