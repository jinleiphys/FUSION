# Exploring behaviors of partonic matter forming in early stage of $pp$ and nucleus-nucleus collisions at ultra-relativistic energies

> The parton yield, (pseudo)rapidity distribution, and transverse momentum distribution in partonic matter assumed forming in the early stage of $pp$ and nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC energy ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 GeV) and LHC energy ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=5.5 TeV for nucleus-nucleus, 5.5 and/or 14 TeV for $pp$) are comparatively investigated with parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE. It turned out that the different parton and anti-parton spectra approach to be similar with reaction energy increasing from RHIC to LHC. We have argued that if the partonic matter forming in Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy is strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma, the one forming in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy might approach the real (free) quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The partonic matter forming in the early stage of Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy (√sNN=5.5 TeV) might approach a real (free) quark-gluon plasma (fQGP), whereas the partonic matter forming in Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy (√sNN=200 GeV) is a strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP). This conclusion is hedged by the qualifier that the study is based on model calculations with the PACIAE code, not on experimental data.

## Method

The authors used the parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE (based on PYTHIA) to simulate pp and nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. Nucleus-nucleus collisions were decomposed into nucleon-nucleon collisions according to collision geometry with Woods-Saxon nucleon distributions, and each nucleon-nucleon collision was further decomposed into parton-parton collisions using leading-order pQCD cross sections with a K factor of 3 and a parton colour screen mass of μ=0.63 GeV. Simulations were ended at the parton evolution (rescattering) stage, before hadronization, to study the properties of the partonic matter.

## Key numbers

- Gluon fraction of total parton yield: 0.420 in pp at RHIC (200 GeV), 0.422 in Au+Au at RHIC (200 GeV), 0.648 in pp at LHC (14 TeV), and 0.636 in Pb+Pb at LHC (5.5 TeV)
- Gluon momentum fraction of total parton momentum: 0.165 in pp at RHIC, 0.179 in Au+Au at RHIC, 0.420 in pp at LHC, and 0.415 in Pb+Pb at LHC
- In pp at RHIC (200 GeV): d yield = 2.09, u yield = 3.98, s yield = 0.123, g yield = 4.82; anti-d yield = 0.237, anti-u yield = 0.234, anti-s yield = 0.106
- In pp at LHC (14 TeV): d yield = 5.25, u yield = 7.08, s yield = 1.19, g yield = 39.8; anti-d yield = 3.47, anti-u yield = 3.47, anti-s yield = 1.17
- In Au+Au at RHIC (200 GeV): d yield = 330, u yield = 294, s yield = 13.5, g yield = 516; anti-d yield = 27.3, anti-u yield = 27.5, anti-s yield = 12.7
- In Pb+Pb at LHC (5.5 TeV): d yield = 244, u yield = 226, s yield = 39.6, g yield = 1351; anti-d yield = 112, anti-u yield = 112, anti-s yield = 39.5
- In pp at LHC, the pT distributions of d and anti-d (and u and anti-u) become identical for pT ≥ 2 GeV/c
- In Pb+Pb at LHC, the pT distributions of d and anti-d (and u and anti-u) become identical for pT ≥ 2 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the coalescence (recombination) model approach (Biro, Csiz, Lin, Hwo, Fries, Molnar, various years) and the v2 quark-number scaling observations (STAR Collaboration, 2003; PHENIX Collaboration, various years) by directly simulating partonic matter properties rather than extracting them from hadron spectra. The study uses the PACIAE model (Sa, 2007) to compare pp and nucleus-nucleus collisions, finding that the similarity between parton and anti-parton spectra increases with energy from RHIC to LHC. This enables future experimental searches for free quark-gluon plasma at LHC energies, particularly through measurements of gluon momentum fraction, anti-parton to parton ratios, and flavor balance in the partonic matter.
