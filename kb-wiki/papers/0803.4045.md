# Electromagnetic Characteristics of States $^{172}Yb$}

> To describe the ground (gr) and excited states of even-even deformed nuclei with the phenomenological model, which takes into account the mixing ground $(gr)$, $0^{+}_{2}(β_1)$--, $0^{+}_{3}(β_2)$--, $2^{+}_{1}(γ_1)$--, $2^{+}_{2}(γ_2)$-- and $K^π=1^{+}_ν$ rotational bands the calculation has been done for the isotope $^{172}Yb$. The energy specterum is described. The reduced probability of electric quadrupole, monopole and dipole magnetic transitions from the states $0^{+}_ν$ and $2^{+}_ν$ - bands to the ground $(gr)$ state band is calculated. A non adiabatic effect in the energy and electromagnetic characteristics is discussed.

## Key claim

The phenomenological model that mixes ground, two beta, two gamma, and isovector rotational bands can describe the energy spectrum and electromagnetic transitions in \(^{172}\text{Yb}\), but the agreement depends on fitting several free parameters (bandhead energies, Coriolis matrix elements, and transition operator constants) to experimental data; the model specifically reproduces non-adiabatic effects such as \(E0\) transitions from \(\gamma\)-bands and \(M1\) transitions from \(\beta\)-bands, which are forbidden in the adiabatic approximation.

## Method

The authors diagonalize a Hamiltonian that includes rotational energy (parameterized via the Harris formula with moments of inertia \(J_0\) and \(J_1\)) and Coriolis coupling among five rotational bands: ground (\(K^\pi=0^+\)), two \(\beta\)-vibrational (\(0^+_2, 0^+_3\)), two \(\gamma\)-vibrational (\(2^+_1, 2^+_2\)), and a \(K^\pi=1^+_\nu\) band. The wave functions from the diagonalization are used to compute reduced transition probabilities \(B(E2)\), \(B(E0)\), and \(B(M1)\), as well as multipole mixing ratios \(\delta(E2/M1)\), with intrinsic matrix elements \(m_K\) and \(m'_K\) fitted to experimental data from prior work.

## Key numbers

- Intrinsic quadrupole moment: \(Q_0 = 791\ \text{fm}^2\).
- Inertia parameters: \(J_0 = 36.37\) (units not given, presumably \(\hbar^2/\text{MeV}\)) and \(J_1 = 127.49\) (units not given).
- Bandhead energies: \(\omega_{\gamma_1} = 1.395\ \text{MeV}\), \(\omega_{\gamma_2} = 1.530\ \text{MeV}\).
- Coriolis matrix elements: \((j_x)_{\text{gr},1_\nu} = 0.106\), \((j_x)_{\beta_1,1_\nu} = 1.167\), \((j_x)_{\beta_2,1_\nu} = 0.778\), \((j_x)_{\gamma_1,1_\nu} = 0.525\), \((j_x)_{\gamma_2,1_\nu} = 0.625\) (dimensionless).
- \(E2\) transition parameters: \(m_{\beta_1} = 12.65\ e\cdot\text{fm}^2\), \(m_{\beta_2} = 0.374\ e\cdot\text{fm}^2\), \(m_{\gamma_1} = 1.5\ e\cdot\text{fm}^2\), \(m_{\gamma_2} = 1.5\ e\cdot\text{fm}^2\), \(m_{1_1} = 14.0\ e\cdot\text{fm}^2\), \(m_{1_2} = 9.0\ e\cdot\text{fm}^2\).
- \(E0\) transition parameters: \(m'_{0_2} = 2.154\ e\cdot\text{fm}^2\), \(m'_{0_3} = 0.641\ e\cdot\text{fm}^2\).
- Example \(B(E2)\): \(2\gamma_1 \rightarrow 0\text{gr}\) experimental = \(75(6)\ e^2\text{fm}^4\), theoretical = \(54\ e^2\text{fm}^4\).
- Example \(X_I(E0/E2)\): \(0\beta_1 \rightarrow 0\text{gr}\) experimental = \(0.028(4)\), theoretical = \(0.029\).
- Example \(\delta(E2/M1)\): \(2\gamma_1 \rightarrow 2\text{gr}\) experimental = \(-5.1^{+1.1}_{-1.6}\), theoretical = \(-3.5\).

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological mixing model of Usmanov (2005) to the specific isotope \(^{172}\text{Yb}\), which had been experimentally studied in \((n,\gamma)\) reactions by Bekzhanov (2005) and in Coulomb excitation by Fahlander (2005). The calculations reproduce measured \(B(E2)\) values from Suber (2005) and multipole mixing ratios from Gresswell (2005) and Kracikova (2005), and they explain \(K\)-forbidden \(E0\) transitions from \(\gamma\)-bands as arising from Coriolis mixing with \(\beta\)-bands. The results enable future tests of the model on other deformed rare-earth nuclei and on higher-spin states.
