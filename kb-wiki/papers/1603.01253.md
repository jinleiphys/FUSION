# Chiral Electroweak Currents in Nuclei

> The development of the chiral dynamics based description of nuclear electroweak currents is reviewed. Gerald E. (Gerry) Brown's role in basing theoretical nuclear physics on chiral Lagrangians is emphasized. Illustrative examples of the successful description of electroweak observables of light nuclei obtained from chiral effective field theory are presented.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how chiral effective field theory (χEFT) provides a systematic framework for deriving nuclear electroweak currents, emphasizing that the phenomenological success of exchange current operators in light nuclei (A=2–10) can be understood within this approach, though the derivation of nuclear operators from transition amplitudes is not uniquely defined because of off-the-energy-shell ambiguities, which are immaterial as the resulting operators are unitarily equivalent.

## Method

The authors review the historical development of chiral Lagrangian-based nuclear currents, starting from Gerry Brown's early work on meson-exchange models and the Chemtob-Rho derivation of exchange currents, then describe the modern χEFT approach using time-ordered perturbation theory (TOPT) to derive nuclear potentials and electroweak current operators up to one-loop level. The method involves expanding transition amplitudes in powers of Q/Λχ (where Λχ ~ 1 GeV), separating irreducible from reducible contributions, and constructing nuclear operators that match the T-matrix order by order when iterated in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation.

## Key numbers

- The pion exchange current contribution accounts for the ~10% difference between calculated and experimental values for the thermal neutron capture cross section on protons [1H(n,γ)2H]
- The exchange current contribution to the calculated cross section for thermal neutron capture on 3He is about 5 times larger than that from the sum of the three nucleon magnetic moments
- The axial exchange current increased the cross section of the reaction 1H(p,e+ νe)2H by 1.5% and that of the reaction 3He(p,e+ νe)4He by almost a factor 5 (in calculations using Argonne v14 NN interaction)
- χEFT-based calculations found the axial exchange current enhances the cross section for 1H(p,e+ νe)2H by ~4% relative to single-nucleon currents
- Pion-exchange mechanisms yield enhancements of the axial charge of order 45–60%, with total enhancement in heavy nuclei of order 85–100% depending on the interaction model
- The calculated magnetic moment of the deuteron would have a node close to momentum transfer values ~6 GeV/c without the ρπγ exchange current, in disagreement with experiment

## Context

This review extends the line of work initiated by Weinberg (1990) on applying χEFT to nuclear physics, building on earlier meson-exchange current calculations by Chemtob and Rho (1971) and the pioneering work of Gerry Brown. The paper summarizes derivations by Park et al. (1996, 2003) and more recent TOPT-based derivations by the present authors (Pastore et al., 2009, 2011; Piarulli et al., 2013; Baroni et al., 2016) and Kolling et al. (2009, 2011). The framework enables parameter-free calculations of electroweak observables in light nuclei and provides a systematic path for improving nuclear interaction and current models to higher orders in the chiral expansion.
