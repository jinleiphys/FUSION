# Robustness of the proxy-SU(3) symmetry in atomic nuclei and the role of the next highest weight irreducible representation

> The proxy-SU(3) symmetry predicts, in a parameter-free way, the collective deformation variables beta and gamma in even-even atomic nuclei away from closed shells based on the highest weight irreducible representations (irreps) of SU(3) in the relevant proton and neutron shells, which are the most symmetric irreps allowed by the Pauli principle and the short-range nature of the nucleon-nucleon interactions. The special cases in which the use of the next highest weight irrep of SU(3) becomes necessary are pointed out and numerical results are given for several regions of the nuclear chart, which can be used as input for irrep-mixing calculations.

# Robustness of the proxy-SU(3) symmetry in atomic nuclei and the role of the next highest weight irreducible representation

## Key claim

The proxy-SU(3) symmetry's parameter-free predictions for the collective deformation variables beta and gamma are robust for most nuclei, but when the highest weight irreducible representation (irrep) becomes fully symmetric (mu=0, boson-like), the predictions for gamma deviate substantially from empirical values; in these special cases, involving the next highest weight (nhw) irrep and taking the average of the hw and nhw predictions dramatically improves agreement with data.

## Method

The authors computed the highest weight (hw) and next highest weight (nhw) SU(3) irreducible representations for valence protons and neutrons in five regions of the nuclear chart (Z=36-80, N=36-122) using the code UNTOU3. They then mapped the SU(3) quantum numbers (lambda, mu) to the collective deformation variables beta and gamma via established relations (Castanos 1988, Draayer 1989), and compared the hw and nhw predictions to empirical gamma values derived from the Davydov model relation using experimental energy ratios of the 2_2^+ and 2_1^+ states.

## Key numbers

- The hw irrep with mu=0 occurs at M = 2, 6, 12, 20, 30, 42, 56 valence particles (protons or neutrons) within a given shell.
- The nhw irreps with mu=0 occur only for M = 8 and 10 nucleons, never coinciding with hw irreps having mu=0.
- When the hw irrep is (lambda, mu) with mu >= 4, the nhw irrep is (lambda+2, mu-4); the quantity lambda^2+lambda*mu+mu^2+3lambda+3mu+3 (proportional to beta^2) is reduced by 6(mu-1) when using the nhw irrep instead of the hw irrep.
- Large discrepancies between hw and nhw gamma predictions occur at nucleon numbers 40, 56, 62, 70, 88, 94, 102, 112, corresponding to 6, 12, 20, 30 valence nucleons above magic numbers.
- The constant r_0 used in the beta mapping is 0.87, obtained from a fit over a wide range of nuclei (DeVries 1987, Stone 2014).
- In the rare earth region, the scaling factor for beta is A/76, where S_p=32 (50-82 shell) and S_n=44 (82-126 shell).

## Context

This work extends the proxy-SU(3) symmetry framework (Bonatsos 2017a, 2017b, 2023) by addressing discrepancies that arise when the highest weight irrep is fully symmetric (mu=0), a situation analogous to the boson-like irreps in the Interacting Boson Model (Arima 1978, Iachello 1987). The paper shows that in contrast to the IBM-1 SU(3) limit, where the gamma band and ground state band belong to different irreps requiring symmetry breaking, the proxy-SU(3) hw irrep with mu >= 4 can accommodate both the ground state band (K=0) and the gamma_1 band (K=2) within the same irrep, resolving a long-standing issue. The calculated beta and gamma values from both hw and nhw irreps are tabulated for five nuclear regions and can serve as input for more elaborate irrep-mixing calculations, with extension to actinides and superheavy nuclei (Martinou 2017b, Sarantopoulou 2017) identified as a straightforward next step.
