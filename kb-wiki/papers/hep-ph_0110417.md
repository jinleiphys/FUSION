# Probing Gluons in Nuclei: the Case of $η'$

> Using the recently proposed $gg --> eta'$ effective vertex, we investigate the production of $η'$ from gluon fusion in $pA$ collisions. We show that measuring $η'$ production cross-section at moderate $x_{η'}$ yields direct information on the small $x$ gluon distribution function of the nucleus. At RHIC, the smallest accessible $x$ turns out to be $O(10^{-5})$ and at LHC, it is $O(10^{-8})$. Therefore, $η'$ is an excellent probe of the Color Glass Condensate.

## Key claim

Measuring the $\eta'$ production cross-section in $pA$ collisions at moderate $x_{\eta'}$ can directly probe the small-$x$ gluon distribution function of the nucleus, provided the gluon distribution in the proton is known from HERA; the authors caution that collinear factorization for this process has not been rigorously proven and that the natural scale ($M_{\eta'}^2$) is not very high, so the results are most reliable for ratios of cross sections rather than absolute values.

## Method

The authors calculate the $\eta'$ production cross-section in $pA$ collisions using a recently proposed effective $gg\eta'$ vertex (Atwood and Soni, 1997) with on-shell form factor $H_0 \approx 1.8\ \text{GeV}^{-1}$. They apply collinear factorization to relate the measured $\eta'$ cross-section to the nuclear gluon distribution $G^A(x_2, Q_f^2)$, then extend the formalism to include intrinsic transverse momentum in the nucleus (modeled as a Gaussian with width given by the saturation scale $Q_s$) and in the proton. Numerical predictions are made using two parameterizations of nuclear gluon shadowing (EKS and BQV) for RHIC and LHC kinematics.

## Key numbers

- $\eta'$ mass: $M = 0.958\ \text{GeV}$ (compared to $\eta$ mass $M_\eta = 0.547\ \text{GeV}$)
- On-shell $gg\eta'$ form factor: $H_0 \equiv H(0,0,M^2) \approx 1.8\ \text{GeV}^{-1}$
- At RHIC ($\sqrt{s}=200\ \text{GeV}$), the smallest accessible $x_-$ (gluon momentum fraction in the nucleus) is $2.2\times 10^{-5}$; at LHC, it is $3.2\times 10^{-8}$
- For an $\eta'$ at rapidity $y=3.04$ at RHIC: $x_+ = 0.10$ and $x_- = 2.3\times 10^{-4}$
- For an $\eta'$ at rapidity $y=6.35$ at LHC: $x_+ = 0.1$ and $x_- = 3.0\times 10^{-7}$
- With intrinsic momentum $P_t = 1\ \text{GeV}$ at RHIC: accessible $x_2$ range $3.3\times 10^{-5} < x_2 < 0.007$
- With intrinsic momentum $P_t = 5\ \text{GeV}$ at LHC: accessible $x_2$ range $1.7\times 10^{-7} < x_2 < 9.3\times 10^{-4}$
- Estimated total $\eta'$ production cross-section is about 3--5% of $\pi^0$ cross-section; the $\eta'\to\gamma\gamma$ branching ratio is about 2%
- Estimated branching-ratio-times-cross-section is on the order of $A\sigma_{\pi^0}/1000 \sim 1\ \text{mb}$

## Context

This work extends the use of the $gg\eta'$ effective vertex, originally proposed by Atwood and Soni (1997) to explain the $B\to\eta'$ branching fraction, to the context of $pA$ collisions as a probe of nuclear gluon distributions at small $x$. It builds on earlier studies of $\eta'$ production from a hadronizing quark-gluon plasma by Jeon (2001) and on the Color Glass Condensate framework for high gluon density QCD (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1994; Kovchegov, 1996). The paper enables direct extraction of the nuclear gluon distribution function at $x$ values orders of magnitude smaller than those accessible in fixed-target DIS experiments at CERN and Fermilab ($x \sim 10^{-2}$), potentially revealing the nature of nuclear shadowing and saturation effects.
