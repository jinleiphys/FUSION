# Nuclear Structure Features of Gamow-Teller Excitations

> It is widely accepted that nuclear Gamow-Teller transitions are quenched; shell-model calculations also showed a clear anticorrelation between the Gamow-Teller strength and the transition rate of the collective quadrupole excitation from the ground state. We discuss the physics beyond this observation. It is based on the existence of spin-orbit coupling that is responsible for the non-zero probabilities of Gamow-Teller transitions in self-conjugate nuclei (N = Z). The shell-model calculations in the f p-space demonstrate the effects of the gradual artificial removal of the spin-orbit coupling that influences Gamow-Teller and quadrupole modes in opposite way. The realistic spin-orbit splitting moves the cumulative Gamow-Teller strength up and leads to stronger fragmentation; both trends are discussed in terms of simple symmetry arguments. Along with this process, the Gamow-Teller operator excites, in addition to the main line of L = 0 states, states with L = 2 which should be added, with the interference terms, to account for the total strength.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the well-known quenching of Gamow-Teller (GT) strength is not primarily due to subnuclear degrees of freedom, but rather follows naturally from nuclear structure effects, specifically the spin-orbit coupling which creates an anticorrelation between GT strength and low-lying quadrupole (E2) strength; the authors qualify that the universal GT sum rule is fulfilled in shell-model calculations only through many very weak transitions that are difficult to observe experimentally.

## Method

The authors performed exact shell-model calculations in the fp-shell space using the FPD6pn interaction for 44Ti and 46Ti, systematically reducing the spin-orbit splitting between the f7/2 and f5/2 orbitals from its realistic value of 6.5 MeV down to zero. They computed the total GT excitation probability B- from the ground state and the quadrupole excitation rate B(E2;0+→2+) for the lowest collective quadrupole state as functions of the spin-orbit splitting, and analyzed the cumulative GT strength distribution as a function of excitation energy in the daughter nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The realistic spin-orbit splitting in the FPD6pn interaction is 6.5 MeV between f5/2 and f7/2 levels and 2 MeV between p3/2 and p1/2 levels.
- For 44Ti with full spin-orbit splitting (row 0 of Table 1), B- = 1.26 and B(E2) = 699 Weisskopf units; when spin-orbit splitting is reduced to zero (row 12), B- drops to 0.09 while B(E2) rises to 909 Weisskopf units.
- For 46Ti with full spin-orbit splitting, B- = 6.90 and B(E2) = 781 Weisskopf units; the Ikeda sum rule gives B- - B+ = 3(N-Z) = 6 for this nucleus.
- The average spin-orbit coupling strength is estimated as h̄ ≈ -20/A2/3 MeV, giving a collective quadrupole energy shift Δω ≈ -60/(A2/3N) MeV, of order 200-300 keV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier observation by Auerbach, Zamick, and Zheng (1993) of anticorrelation between GT strength and quadrupole strength, providing a physical explanation based on spin-orbit coupling, isospin invariance, and fermionic antisymmetry. The authors connect their findings to the long-standing GT quenching problem discussed by Wilkinson (1973), Gaarde (1981), and Osterfeld (1992), and show that the sum rule saturation proceeds through many weak transitions as stressed by Klein (1985). The results suggest that experimental multipole decomposition of charge-exchange reactions, which typically extracts only L=0 strength, may miss L=2 components excited by the GT operator, raising questions about the evaluation of experimental GT quenching.
