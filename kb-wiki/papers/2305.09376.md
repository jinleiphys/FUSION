# Configuration mixing and intertwined quantum phase transitions in odd-mass niobium isotopes

> Nuclei in the $Z\!\approx\!40,N\!\approx\!60$ region have one of the most complicated structural evolution across the nuclear chart, with coexisting shapes arising from different mixed configurations. In such a region, it is difficult to investigate odd-mass nuclei. In this paper a new algebraic framework is introduced, the interacting boson-fermion model with configuration mixing. Using this framework, with a boson core and a proton in the $1f_{5/2},2p_{3/2},2p_{1/2},1g_{9/2}$ orbits, a calculation is carried out to understand the structural evolution of the odd-mass niobium isotopes $(Z=41)$ with neutron number 52-62. The calculated results are compared to energy levels, two neutron separation energies, $E2$ and $M1$ transition rates, and to quadrupole and magnetic moments. The detailed analysis discloses the effects of an abrupt crossing of states between normal and intruder configurations (Type II QPT), which is accompanied by a gradual evolution from spherical- to deformed-core shapes within the intruder configuration (Type I QPT), where both types of QPTs occur around the critical point of neutron number 60. The identification of both types of QPTs in the same chain of isotopes provides an empirical manifestation of intertwined quantum phase transitions (IQPTs) in odd-mass nuclei and the relevance of IQPTs to the niobium chain.

## Key claim

The paper claims that both Type I (gradual shape-phase transition within a single configuration) and Type II (abrupt crossing between normal and intruder configurations) quantum phase transitions occur in the same chain of odd-mass niobium isotopes, providing an empirical manifestation of intertwined quantum phase transitions (IQPTs) in odd-mass nuclei, with both types occurring around neutron number 60.

## Method

The authors introduce the interacting boson-fermion model with configuration mixing (IBFM-CM), a new algebraic framework that couples a boson core (representing valence nucleon pairs) with a single proton occupying the \(1f_{5/2}, 2p_{3/2}, 2p_{1/2}, 1g_{9/2}\) orbits. They perform calculations for odd-mass niobium isotopes (\(Z=41\)) with neutron numbers 52–62, using boson parameters taken from previous calculations of the adjacent even-even zirconium cores, and compare results to experimental energy levels, two-neutron separation energies, \(E2\) and \(M1\) transition rates, and quadrupole and magnetic moments.

## Key numbers

* The ground state \(9/2^+_1\) of \(^{93-97}\)Nb arises from weak coupling of the \(\pi(1g_{9/2})\) proton to the \(0^+_{1;A}\) state of \(^{92-96}\)Zr.
* For \(^{93}\)Nb, the quintuplet of states built on the \(2^+_{1;A}\) of \(^{92}\)Zr has a center of gravity (CoG) of 0.976 MeV, close to the observed 0.935 MeV of the \(2^+_1\) in \(^{92}\)Zr.
* For \(^{95}\)Nb, the quintuplet built on the \(2^+_{1;A}\) of \(^{94}\)Zr has a CoG of 0.9776 MeV, close to the observed 0.919 MeV of the \(2^+_1\) in \(^{94}\)Zr.
* The \(E2\) transitions from quintuplet states to the ground state in \(^{93-97}\)Nb are comparable to the \(2^+_{1;A}\to0^+_{1;A}\) transitions in \(^{92-96}\)Zr (6.4(6), 4.9(3), 2.3(3) W.u., respectively).
* In \(^{93}\)Nb, the \(M1\) transitions \(7/2^+_1 \to 9/2^+_1\) and \(11/2^+_1 \to 9/2^+_1\) have \(B(M1)\) of 0.099(8) and 0.085(6) W.u., while intra-quintuplet \(M1\) transitions \(5/2^+_1 \to 7/2^+_1\), \(9/2^+_3 \to 11/2^+_1\), and \(9/2^+_3 \to 7/2^+_1\) have \(B(M1)\) of 0.160(12), 0.73(18), and 0.16(3) W.u.
* The energy difference \(E(0^+_{2;B})-E(9/2^+_2)\) increases from 0.3 MeV in \(^{93}\)Nb and 0.212 MeV in \(^{95}\)Nb to 0.422 MeV in \(^{97}\)Nb.

## Context

This work extends the concept of intertwined quantum phase transitions (IQPTs), previously demonstrated in even-even zirconium isotopes by Gavrielov (2019, 2020, 2022) within the IBM-CM framework, to odd-mass nuclei for the first time using the newly developed IBFM-CM. The niobium chain exhibits the same configuration mixing scenario as the adjacent zirconium cores, where the sudden onset of deformation near neutron number 60 arises from the proton-neutron interaction between \(\pi(1g_{9/2})\) and \(\nu(1g_{7/2})\) spin-orbit partners, as established by Federman (1977, 1979) and Heyde (1985, 1987). The framework enables future studies of configuration mixing and QPTs in other odd-mass nuclei in this region, such as the technetium or rhodium isotopes.
