# Intermediate-Distance String Effects in Wilson Loops\\ via Boundary Action

> The density profile of the QCD flux tube is investigated within the framework of the Lüscher-Weisz (LW) string action with two boundary terms. The transverse action profile and potential between static quarks are considered using Wilson's loop overlap formalism at zero temperature in SU(2) Yang-Mills theory. We find the predictions of the LW string matching the lattice data for the width of the energy-density and $Q\bar{Q}$ potential up to a small color-source separation of $R=0.32$\,fm.

## Key claim
The Lüscher-Weisz (LW) string action with two boundary terms (b₂ and b₄) can match lattice data for the QCD flux-tube width and static quark-antiquark potential down to color-source separations as small as R = 0.32 fm, which is substantially shorter than the distances where the free (non-boundary-corrected) effective string theory works.

## Method
The authors performed fits of the LW string potential (including boundary corrections at orders b₂ and b₄) to SU(2) Yang-Mills lattice data from Bali et al. (1994) at three lattice couplings (β = 2.50, 2.635, 2.74). They extracted the boundary parameters b₂ and b₄ by fitting the static Q̅Q potential and the mean-square width of the flux tube, using Wilson loop overlap formalism at zero temperature. The fits were evaluated over various intervals of color-source separation R, with χ² per degree of freedom used to assess goodness of fit.

## Key numbers
- The LW string with two boundary terms matches lattice data down to a minimal color-source separation of R = 0.32 fm (at β = 2.5, fit interval [4a,8a] corresponding to R = 0.3246 fm; at β = 2.63, fit interval [6a,12a] corresponding to R = 0.3304 fm).
- At β = 2.5, the fit of V_{nℓo}^{b2,b4} over R ∈ [4a,8a] yields χ²_{d.o.f.} = 0.38; the fit of V_{nnℓo}^{b2,b4} over the same interval yields χ²_{d.o.f.} = 0.31.
- At β = 2.63, the fit of V_{nℓo}^{b2,b4} over R ∈ [6a,12a] yields χ²_{d.o.f.} = 0.17; the fit of V_{nnℓo}^{b2,b4} over the same interval yields χ²_{d.o.f.} = 0.15.
- At β = 2.74, the fit of V_{nℓo}^{b2,b4} over R ∈ [7a,16a] yields χ²_{d.o.f.} = 1.06; the fit of V_{nnℓo}^{b2,b4} over the same interval yields χ²_{d.o.f.} = 0.83.
- The continuum boundary parameter b₂ᶜ extracted from the best fits ranges from approximately 0.6×10⁻³ to 1.4×10⁻³ across the three lattices.
- The lattice spacings used are a = 0.0826(14) fm (β = 2.50), a = 0.0541(2) fm (β = 2.635), and a = 0.0408(2) fm (β = 2.74).

## Context
This work extends the effective string theory (EST) approach of Lüscher and Weisz (2002) and the boundary-term formalism of Billó et al. (2012) by applying both b₂ and b₄ boundary corrections simultaneously to the static potential and flux-tube width. It addresses the known deviations from the free EST at intermediate distances (0.3–0.6 fm) that were observed in earlier lattice studies by Bali et al. (1994) and others. The results enable future lattice studies to extract nonuniversal boundary parameters for different gauge groups and to test whether the boundary-corrected LW string can describe the flux-tube profile at even shorter distances or at finite temperature.
