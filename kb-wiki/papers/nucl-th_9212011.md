# Colour Transparency in Hadronic Basis

> The dynamics of nuclear transparency in hard nuclear reactions is studied by an expansion of the correlator of the hard scattering operator on a hadronic basis. Colour transparency appears as an effect of interference between the amplitudes corresponding to the final state interaction of different intermediate baryonic states. We find that colour transparency occurs even at moderate momentum transfers if one chooses the missing momentum of the struck nucleon by appropriate kinematics. In general, the role of the Fermi-motion is found to be crucial; without it, any effect of anomalous nuclear transparency would be impossible.

# Colour Transparency in Hadronic Basis

## Key claim
Colour transparency can occur even at moderate momentum transfers if the missing momentum of the struck nucleon is chosen by appropriate kinematics; the Fermi motion of nucleons is crucial, and without it any effect of anomalous nuclear transparency would be impossible.

## Method
The authors expand the correlator of the hard scattering operator on a hadronic basis, restricting to two baryonic states (the proton and the Roper resonance N(1440) with the same quantum numbers). The final state interaction is described by a system of two coupled eikonal equations with an effective optical potential that includes both elastic interactions and diffractive transitions between the two baryonic states. The cross section is obtained by coherently summing contributions from all points inside the nucleus, weighted by the bound state wavefunction and the momentum distribution of protons in the nucleus.

## Key numbers
- The mass of the lowest baryonic excitation with proton quantum numbers (Roper resonance N(1440)) is 1.44 GeV, with a width of 350 MeV.
- The mass spacing between the proton and the first baryonic excitation is approximately 300 MeV.
- The total cross section used for the optical potential is 40 mb.
- Numerical calculations are performed for the ²⁷Al target nucleus, using a Wood-Saxon density with radius 1.1·A^(1/3) fm and diffuseness 0.5 fm.
- Transparency is computed for momentum transfers q = 4, 8, and 40 GeV as functions of missing momentum p_m (in units of Fermi momentum p_f).
- The longitudinal momentum transfer in the 2 → 1 process is given by q_L ≃ (m_k² − m²)/(2p).

## Context
This work extends the theoretical understanding of colour transparency beyond the naive picture of a compact state expanding in the nuclear medium, which had failed to explain the oscillatory pattern observed in the only dedicated experiment at the time (the (p,2p) experiment of reference [11]). The authors clarify the fundamental role of Fermi motion, a conclusion that appeared simultaneously in work by Jennings and Kopeliovich (1992) and in the authors' own previous work (1992). The approach enables a dynamical treatment of the transition between normal nuclear attenuation and colour transparency, and predicts that transparency maxima occur at specific missing momenta related to the probability of exciting baryonic resonances.
