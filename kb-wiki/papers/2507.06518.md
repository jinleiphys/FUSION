# Chiral symmetry breaking and pion condensation in the early universe

> We determine the possible trajectories the universe may have followed in the QCD phase diagram during the QCD epoch. We focus on the roles of chiral symmetry breaking and pion condensation under high imbalances in lepton asymmetry. Adopting the quark-meson model as an effective description of QCD at finite temperature, charge and baryon chemical potentials we show that, for sufficiently large but physically motivated asymmetries, the universe may have entered the pion condensation phase through a first-order phase transition, followed by a second-order phase transition when exiting it. Such a first-order phase transition represents a new possible source of primordial gravitational waves during the QCD epoch.

## Key claim

For sufficiently large but physically motivated imbalances in individual lepton asymmetries (specifically $|l_e + l_\mu| \gtrsim 0.1$), the universe may have entered a pion condensation phase during the QCD epoch through a first-order phase transition, followed by a second-order phase transition when exiting it, rather than following the standard trajectory along the temperature axis.

## Method

The authors employed the two-flavor quark-meson (QM) model in the mean-field approximation to describe the QCD sector at finite temperature, baryon chemical potential, and charge chemical potential. They solved the conservation equations for baryon number, electric charge, and lepton flavor asymmetries simultaneously with the minimization of the QM free energy with respect to the chiral and pion condensates, determining the cosmic trajectories across the QCD phase diagram for different values of the lepton asymmetry imbalance $|l_e + l_\mu|$ in the range 0.01-0.4.

## Key numbers

- Baryon asymmetry from CMB and BBN observations: $b = (8.7 \pm 0.06) \times 10^{-11}$
- Tricritical point in the QM model phase diagram located at $T_{tc} \approx 124$ MeV and $\mu_{Qc} \approx 186$ MeV ($\mu_I \approx 93$ MeV)
- Threshold for entering pion condensation phase: $|l_e + l_\mu| \gtrsim 0.1$, with slightly smaller values ($\sim 0.085$) still generating pion condensation for a small temperature range
- First-order phase transition occurs for $|l_e + l_\mu| > 0.1$ when entering the pion condensation phase
- Pion decay constant used for parameter fixing: $f_\pi = 92$ MeV; pion mass: $m_\pi = 138$ MeV; sigma mass: $m_\sigma = 700$ MeV
- Chiral crossover temperature at $\mu_B=0$ and $\mu_Q=0$: $T \approx 160$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Wygas (2018) and Vovchenko (2020), who first predicted pion condensation in the early universe under high lepton asymmetries using an effective mass model matched to lattice data. The authors provide a complementary treatment using the quark-meson model, which allows simultaneous discussion of chiral symmetry breaking and pion condensation, evaluation of condensates along cosmic trajectories, and reveals a tricritical point at high $\mu_Q$ not present in the earlier effective mass model. The results enable investigation of first-order phase transitions as a new possible source of primordial gravitational waves during the QCD epoch, and motivate further lattice QCD studies at high charge chemical potentials.
