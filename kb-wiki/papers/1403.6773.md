# Evidence for tetrahedral symmetry in 16O

> We derive the rotation-vibration spectrum of a 4-alpha configuration with tetrahedral symmetry, T_d, and show evidence for the occurrence of this symmetry in the low-lying spectrum of 16O. All vibrational states with A, E and F symmetry appear to have been observed, as well as the rotational bands with L(P)=0(+), 3(-), 4(+), 6(+) on the A states, and part of the rotational bands built on the E, F states. We derive analytic expressions for the form factors and B(EL) values of the ground state rotational band and show that the measured values support the tetrahedral symmetry of this band.

# Evidence for tetrahedral symmetry in 16O

## Key claim

The low-lying states of 16O can be described as rotation-vibration of a 4-alpha cluster with tetrahedral (T_d) symmetry, with the ground state band showing unbroken T_d symmetry in both energies and electromagnetic transitions, while the vibrational bands show evidence of symmetry breaking.

## Method

The authors employed the algebraic cluster model (ACM) based on a U(10) algebra, which describes four-body clusters using three vector bosons (representing Jacobi coordinates) and an auxiliary scalar boson. They derived a Hamiltonian for a tetrahedral configuration with T_d symmetry, obtained approximate energy formulas via semiclassical methods in the large-N limit, and computed form factors and B(EL) values for the ground state rotational band. The model was compared to the experimental spectrum of 16O from nuclear data sheets.

## Key numbers

- Ground state rotational band moment of inertia parameter: kappa_1 = 0.511 MeV, giving rotational energies E = 0.511 * L(L+1) MeV
- A-vibration (breathing mode) rotational band: E = 0.463 * L(L+1) MeV
- All three vibrational modes (A, E, F) have comparable energies at approximately 6 MeV
- Extracted tetrahedral radius parameter beta = 2.0 fm from the first minimum in the elastic form factor
- B(E3; 3_1^- -> 0_1^+): theoretical 181 e^2fm^6, experimental 205 +/- 10 e^2fm^6
- B(E4; 4_1^+ -> 0_1^+): theoretical 338 e^2fm^8, experimental 378 +/- 133 e^2fm^8
- B(E6; 6_1^+ -> 0_1^+): theoretical 8245 e^2fm^12, no experimental value available
- Gamma-ray energy for 3_1^-: theoretical 6132 keV, experimental 6130 keV
- Gamma-ray energy for 4_1^+: theoretical 10220 keV, experimental 10356 keV
- Gamma-ray energy for 6_1^+: theoretical 21462 keV, experimental 21052 keV

## Context

This work extends the algebraic cluster model previously applied to 3-alpha clusters in 12C with D_3h symmetry (Bijker and Iachello, 2000, 2002) to 4-alpha clusters in 16O. The suggestion of tetrahedral 4-alpha structure in 16O dates back to Dennison (1954), Kameny (1956), Brink (1965), and Robson (1978), but clear signatures could not previously be identified. The analysis of B(EL) values provides stronger evidence for T_d symmetry than the energy spectrum alone. The results enable further studies of symmetry breaking in vibrational bands and comparison with ab initio lattice calculations by Epelbaum et al. (2014), which confirmed the tetrahedral ground state but suggested a square configuration for excited states.
