# The Nucleon Sigma Term from Threshold Parameters

> A new sum rule determines the nucleon sigma term by the πN elastic scattering threshold parameters alone. This "threshold" value of σ(2μ^2) provides an independent check of existing values obtained from global dispersive analyses, and also provides a realistic error assignment. Employing a recent analysis of low energy (T_π< 100 MeV) πN data our sum rule implies σ(2μ^2) = 71\pm 9 MeV.

# The Nucleon Sigma Term from Threshold Parameters

## Key claim

The nucleon sigma term at the Cheng-Dasher point, σ(2μ²), can be determined using only πN elastic scattering threshold parameters via a new sum rule, yielding a value of 71 ± 9 MeV. This result depends on a specific recent analysis of low-energy (T_π < 100 MeV) πN data and assumes that the remainder function R from the on-shell theorem is small and model-dependent at the level of less than 2 MeV.

## Method

The author derives a sum rule by subtracting two fixed-t dispersion relations for the crossing-even amplitude D⁺(ν,t), one evaluated at t = 2μ² and one at t = 0, which eliminates the need for global phase shift analyses. The resulting expression for the on-shell amplitude D̅(2μ²) is written entirely in terms of s-wave and p-wave scattering lengths, the s-wave effective range parameter C⁺, and a small integral over the imaginary part of the amplitude that is dominated by the Δ(1232) resonance. The sigma term then follows from the on-shell theorem, σ(2μ²) = F_π² D̅(2μ²) − (0.46 MeV), where the small correction comes from a Δ-resonance model.

## Key numbers

- σ(2μ²) = 71 ± 9 MeV, obtained from the sum rule using threshold parameters from a recent low-energy analysis (Woolcock et al., 1999)
- The Δ-resonance correction to the on-shell theorem is Δ_R = 0.46 MeV, with an estimated uncertainty |Δ_R| < 2 MeV
- The continuum integral I = 0.21 ± 0.02, with an asymptotic contribution I_ASY ≃ 0.01
- The combined Born and continuum term gives μ g²/M · (r/(1−r²))² − I ≃ −0.06 ± 0.02
- The ratio r = μ/2M ≃ 0.0744
- The numerical coefficient relating λ₂ to λ₀ is 1.047, and λ₂ = 1.036 λ₀
- Threshold parameters used (in units of μ): a₀₊⁽⁺⁾ = 0.0035 ± 0.0026, a₀₊⁽¹/²⁾ = 0.179 ± 0.006, a₀₊⁽³/²⁾ = −0.079 ± 0.003, C⁽⁺⁾ = −0.116 ± 0.026, a₁₋⁽⁺⁾ = −0.063 ± 0.003, a₁₊⁽⁺⁾ = 0.135 ± 0.002

## Context

This work extends the long-standing debate between "large" and "small" sigma term values, where theoretical estimates from chiral perturbation theory (based on the GMOR model with quark mass ratios) predict σ(0) ∼ 25–40 MeV, while experimental determinations via the on-shell theorem historically yield ∼ 65 MeV. The new sum rule provides an independent check of global dispersive analyses such as those by Hohler (1971) and Gasser, Leutwyler, and Sainio (1991), and enables a more reliable error assignment by using only data near threshold rather than global phase shift fits. The result σ(2μ²) = 71 ± 9 MeV reinforces the discrepancy between experiment and conventional chiral perturbation theory, and is consistent with recent global calculations by Pavan, Arndt, Strakovsky, and Workman (1999).
