# Isospin- and momentum-dependent effective interactions for the baryon octet and the properties of hybrid stars

> The isospin- and momentum-dependent MDI interaction, which has been extensively used in intermediate-energy heavy-ion reactions to study the properties of asymmetric nuclear matter, is extended to include the nucleon-hyperon and hyperon-hyperon interactions by assuming same density, momentum and isospin dependence as for the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The parameters in these interactions are determined from the empirical hyperon single-particle potentials in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density. The extended MDI interaction is then used to study in the mean-field approximation the equation of state of hypernuclear matter and also the properties of hybrid stars by including the phase transition from the hypernuclear matter to the quark matter at high densities. In particular, the effects of attractive and repulsive $Σ$N interactions and different values of symmetry energies on the hybrid star properties are investigated.

# Isospin- and momentum-dependent effective interactions for the baryon octet and the properties of hybrid stars

## Key claim

The isospin- and momentum-dependent MDI interaction, previously used only for nucleons, is extended to all baryon octet members (N, Λ, Σ, Ξ) by assuming the same functional form for density, momentum, and isospin dependence, with parameters fitted to empirical hyperon single-particle potentials at saturation density. The resulting model can describe both attractive and repulsive ΣN interactions, but the authors caution that the hyperon-hyperon interaction parameters are only a baseline and more sophisticated treatments are needed once in-medium hyperon properties are better understood.

## Method

The authors extend the finite-range Gogny-based MDI interaction to include nucleon-hyperon and hyperon-hyperon interactions by assuming proportional scaling of all interaction parameters (A, A', B, C) from the nucleon-nucleon case, with scaling factors f_{bb'} determined by fitting empirical single-particle potentials at saturation density. They then apply the extended interaction in the mean-field approximation to compute the equation of state of hypernuclear matter under β-equilibrium and charge neutrality, and use the Gibbs construction with an MIT bag model for the quark phase to study hybrid star properties, including the hadron-quark phase transition.

## Key numbers

- Λ single-particle potential in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density: U^{(N)}_Λ(ρ_N = ρ_0) = -30 MeV
- Ξ single-particle potential at saturation density: U^{(N)}_Ξ(ρ_N = ρ_0) = -18 MeV
- Σ single-particle potential at saturation density: U^{(N)}_Σ(ρ_N = ρ_0) = ±30 MeV (attractive or repulsive cases)
- Hyperon-hyperon interaction strength fitted to: U^{(Y')}_Y(ρ_{Y'} = ρ_0) ~ -40 MeV
- Saturation density of normal nuclear matter: ρ_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Scaling factors: f_{NΛ} = 1, f_{NΞ} = 0.73, f_{ΛΛ} = 0.63, f_{ΛΣ} = 1.25, f_{ΛΞ} = 1.25, f_{ΣΣ} = 0.49, f_{ΣΞ} = 1, f_{ΞΞ} = 0.53
- For repulsive ΣN interaction (MDI-Hyp-R): B_{NΣ} = 2.33 B_{NN} and A'_{NΣ} = 2.33 A'_{NN}
- Symmetry potential at zero momentum at ρ_0: nucleon ~34 MeV, Ξ ~12 MeV, Σ (attractive) ~17 MeV, Σ (repulsive) ~9 MeV
- At ρ = 3ρ_0 with x=0: nucleon symmetry potential ~53 MeV, Ξ ~19 MeV, Σ (attractive) ~26 MeV, Σ (repulsive) ~2 MeV
- At ρ = 3ρ_0 with x=-1: nucleon symmetry potential ~174 MeV, Ξ ~63 MeV, Σ (attractive) ~87 MeV, Σ (repulsive) ~143 MeV
- Quark masses: m_u = m_d = 0, m_s = 150 MeV
- Example bag constant used: B^{1/4} = 180 MeV

## Context

This work extends the MDI interaction framework of Das et al. (2003) and Chen et al. (2005), which was previously used to constrain the nuclear symmetry energy from isospin diffusion data and to study neutron star properties assuming only nucleons, electrons, and muons (Xu et al., 2009a, 2009b). The extended model enables studies of hypernuclear matter and hybrid stars with hyperons, complementing approaches such as the relativistic mean-field model (Schaffer et al., 1993, 1996, 2000), Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory (Schulze et al., 1995, 1998; Baldo et al., 1998, 2000; Vidana et al., 2000), and phenomenological models (Balberg et al., 1997; Banik et al., 2000). The single-particle potentials for Λ and Σ are close to those from chiral effective field theory (Pol et al., 2006) but more repulsive than G-matrix calculations using the Nijmegen or Jülich models at high densities. The authors note that the charged Σ and Ξ hyperon ratios in heavy-ion collisions could serve as probes of the high-density symmetry energy, following the suggestion of Li et al. (2005).
