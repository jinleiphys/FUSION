# Comparison between chiral and meson-theoretic nucleon-nucleon potentials through (p,p') reactions

> We use proton-nucleus reaction data at intermediate energies to test the emerging new generation of chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials. Predictions from a high quality one-boson-exchange (OBE) force are used for comparison and evaluation. Both the chiral and OBE models fit NN phase shifts accurately, and the differences between the two forces for proton-induced reactions are small. A comparison to a chiral model with a less accurate NN description sets the scale for the ability of such models to work for nuclear reactions.

# Comparison between chiral and meson-theoretic nucleon-nucleon potentials through (p,p') reactions

## Key claim

Chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials that accurately reproduce NN phase shifts up to 300 MeV can produce predictions for proton-nucleus reactions at 200 MeV that are nearly indistinguishable from those of high-quality one-boson-exchange potentials, provided the chiral model includes sufficient flexibility (contact terms through fourth order) to achieve high-precision phase-shift reproduction. A less accurate chiral model (NNLO without fourth-order contact terms) fails dramatically for the same reactions, establishing that precise phase-shift reproduction is an essential prerequisite for reliable reaction predictions.

## Method

The authors performed distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) calculations for 200-MeV proton elastic and inelastic scattering on 40Ca and 16O, using two chiral NN potentials (Entem-Machleidt at N3LO and Epelbaum et al. at NLO and NNLO) and the CD-Bonn one-boson-exchange potential for comparison. Medium modifications were included via Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) density dependence, and the resulting density-dependent G-matrices were transformed into Yukawa representations for use in the DWIA reaction codes LEA (natural parity) and DWBA86 (unnatural parity).

## Key numbers

- Incident proton energy: 200 MeV for all reactions studied
- Elastic scattering on 40Ca: cross section (ratio to Rutherford) and analyzing power compared for chiral and CD-Bonn potentials, with BHF medium effects included
- Inelastic scattering to the 3- state in 40Ca at 3.736 MeV: cross section and analyzing power compared for chiral and CD-Bonn potentials
- Unnatural-parity transition to the 4-, T=1 state in 16O at 18.98 MeV: cross section and polarization observables (DNN, DSS) compared; chiral potential showed better agreement with DNN and DSS data than CD-Bonn
- The NLO and NNLO potentials of Epelbaum et al. produced predictions that "differ dramatically from the measurements" for the 4- transition, with differences "exceeding by an order of magnitude" those between the Entem-Machleidt chiral potential and CD-Bonn
- The Entem-Machleidt chiral potential reproduces NN phase shifts "excellently at energies below 300 MeV" and was fit to the Nijmegen phase shift solution using 46 model parameters

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Sammarruca, Stephenson, and Jiang (1999) and Sammarruca et al. (2000) that used (p,p') reactions to test conventional NN potentials, and provides the first tests of a high-precision chiral NN potential (Entem and Machleidt, 2001) for proton-nucleus reactions. The results demonstrate that chiral effective field theory can now produce NN potentials of sufficient quality for nuclear reaction calculations at intermediate energies, enabling future work to explore the predictive power of chiral potentials beyond the 200 MeV range and to investigate short-range ambiguities that remain due to the flexibility of contact terms.
