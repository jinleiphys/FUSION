# Delays, Unstable Particles, and Transport Theory

> 12 pages, Latex, 5 figures, Talk given at the XXV Int. Workshop on Gross Properties of Nuclei and Nuclear Excitations, "QCD Phase Transitions", Jan. 13-18, 1997

## Key claim

Delays associated with elementary interaction processes, both in the forward and scattered waves, affect the density of states and thermodynamic properties of a system; the unphysically long lifetimes of broad resonances near threshold in transport simulations arise specifically from forcing mean-field effects onto scattering delays rather than treating them separately.

## Method

The authors analyze wave-packet scattering in a spherical chamber of radius R, computing the average time spent by the packet within the chamber and comparing it to the free-flight time. They derive expressions for the delay time in terms of the phase of the scattering amplitude, showing that the forward delay time is related to the real part of the forward scattering amplitude and thus to the optical potential. They then connect these delays to thermodynamic properties via the Beth-Uhlenbeck formula, and apply the formalism to Breit-Wigner resonances and the πN Δ-resonance using measured phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- For billiard-ball scattering, the average delay time is Δτ_s = -(4/3)(d/v), yielding a repulsive excluded volume correction to pressure P = nT + n²T(2πd³/3).
- For repulsive impact-parameter scattering, the average delay time is Δτ_s = -(π/3)(d/v), which is π/4 of the billiard-ball value.
- For a Breit-Wigner resonance at E = E_R, the scattering delay time is Δτ_s = 2/Γ, twice the naively expected lifetime of 1/Γ.
- The width of the Δ-resonance near threshold behaves as Γ ∝ (E - m_π - m_N)^(3/2).
- The scattering cross section for πN → Δ near threshold behaves as σ ∝ (E - m_π - m_N)^(1/2).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier investigation (Danielewicz and Pratt, 1996) to establish links between scattering prescriptions in transport models and macroscopic system properties. It addresses sensitivity problems found in simulations by Halbert (1981), where density variations exceeded a factor of 2 between billiard-ball and impact-parameter scattering, and by Gyulassy (1982), where flow angles varied by nearly a factor of 2. The paper enables proper treatment of broad resonances like the Δ in transport simulations, resolving the unphysical long lifetimes near threshold that arise when mean-field effects are incorrectly forced onto scattering delays.
