# Diabatic and Adiabatic Collective Motion in a Model Pairing System

> Large amplitude collective motion is investigated for a model pairing Hamiltonian containing an avoided level crossing. A classical theory of collective motion for the adiabatic limit is applied utilising either a time-dependent mean-field theory or a direct parametrisation of the time-dependent Schrödinger equation. A modified local harmonic equation is formulated to take account of the Nambu-Goldstone mode. It turns out that in some cases the system selects a diabatic path. Requantizing the collective Hamiltonian, a reasonable agreement with an exact calculation for the low-lying levels are obtained for both weak and strong pairing force. This improves on results of the conventional Born-Oppenheimer approximation.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for a model pairing Hamiltonian with an avoided level crossing, the adiabatic large amplitude collective motion (ALACM) theory can yield a diabatic collective path in some cases, and that requantizing the resulting collective Hamiltonian gives reasonable agreement with exact low-lying level energies for both weak and strong pairing forces, improving on the conventional Born-Oppenheimer approximation.

## Method

The authors apply the theory of adiabatic large amplitude collective motion (ALACM) to a model pairing Hamiltonian with a two-level system (N=Ω=2) and a harmonic oscillator core. They derive classical Hamiltonians using both a time-dependent mean-field (BCS) approximation and a direct parametrization of the exact time-dependent Schrödinger equation, then formulate a constrained local harmonic equation (CLHE) to handle the Nambu-Goldstone mode arising from particle-number symmetry breaking. The collective path is determined iteratively by solving the local harmonic equations, and the resulting collective Hamiltonian is requantized to compute low-lying energy levels.

## Key numbers

- For the two-level system with N=Ω=2, the potential in the BCS parametrization has stationary points at ξ = ±1 and, for |ε| < G, at ξ = −sgn(ε)/√2 (√(1+|ε|/G) ± √(1−|ε|/G)).
- In the exact parametrization, the mass parameter is B_val(ξ) = G / sin(2ξ) and the potential is V(ξ) = 2ε cos(2ξ) − G(1 + sin(2ξ)).
- The two stationary points of the exact potential in 0 ≤ ξ ≤ π satisfy tan(2ξ) = −G/(2ε).
- The single-particle energies are ε_α(q) = (−1)^(α+1)(χq − ε_0), with the two levels crossing at q = ε_0/χ.

## Context

This work extends the ALACM theory developed by Klein, Walet, and Do Dang (1991) to systems with pairing interactions and level crossings, which had not been previously studied with this approach. The model, introduced by Fukui, Matsuo, and Matsuyanagi (1991) for shape mixing, serves as a testbed for understanding whether an adiabatic theory can describe diabatic dynamics, a question raised by Hill and Wheeler (1953) and discussed by Bertsch (1988) in the context of nuclear shape change via pair hopping. The results enable future application of the CLHE formalism to more realistic nuclear systems where pairing and level crossings are important.
