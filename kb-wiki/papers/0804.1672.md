# Criterion for Bose-Einstein condensation in traps and self-bound systems

> The internal one-particle density matrix is discussed for Bose-Einstein condensates with finite number of particles in a harmonic trap. The outcome of the digonalization of the density matrix depends on the choice of the internal coordinates: The Pethick-Pitaevskii-type internal density matrix, whose analytical eigenvalues and eigenfunctions are evaluated, yields a fragmented condensate, while the Jacobi-type internal density matrix leads to an ideal condensate. We give a criterion for the choice of the internal coordinates: In the macroscopic limit the internal density matrix should have eigenvalues and eigenfunctions of an ideal Bose-Einstein condensate, this being a very physical condition for cases where the system is also an ideal Bose condensation in the laboratory frame. One choice fulfilling this boundary condition is given by the internal Jacobi coordinates, while the internal coordinates with respect to the center of mass do not satisfy the condition. Based on our criterion, a general definition of the internal one-particle density matrix is presented in a self-bound system, consisting of interacting bosons.

## Key claim

The internal one-particle density matrix for a Bose-Einstein condensate with a finite number of particles in a harmonic trap yields different results depending on the choice of internal coordinates: the Pethick-Pitaevskii-type internal density matrix gives a fragmented condensate, while the Jacobi-type internal density matrix gives an ideal condensate. The authors propose a criterion that in the macroscopic limit the internal density matrix should have the same eigenvalues and eigenfunctions as an ideal Bose-Einstein condensate in the laboratory frame, which the Jacobi coordinates satisfy but the Pethick-Pitaevskii coordinates do not.

## Method

The authors analytically diagonalized two forms of the internal one-particle density matrix for an ideal Bose-Einstein condensate of N spinless particles in a harmonic trap: the Pethick-Pitaevskii-type, which uses coordinates relative to the center of mass, and the Jacobi-type, which uses Jacobi coordinates. They compared the eigenvalues and eigenfunctions of both density matrices and examined their behavior in the macroscopic limit (N approaching infinity) against the known result for an ideal condensate in the laboratory frame.

## Key numbers

- For the PP-type internal density matrix, the occupation probability summed over all magnetic substates for angular momentum L in the macroscopic limit is given by Lambda^(L)_(N=infinity) = (2L+1)(2-sqrt(2))(3-2*sqrt(2))^L, which depends on L and thus indicates fragmentation.
- For the Jacobi-type internal density matrix, the occupation probability is Lambda^(L)_N = delta_(L0), meaning 100% occupancy of the zero-node S-wave state independent of N.
- The PP-type internal density matrix has the analytical form rho_(int,PP)^(1)(q,q') = (N/(N-1))^(3/2) (2nu/pi)^(3/2) exp[-(3N-2)/(2(N-1)) nu(q^2+q'^2) + (N-2)/(N-1) nu q·q'].
- The Jacobi-type internal density matrix has the analytical form rho_(int,J)^(1)(xi,xi') = ((N-1)/N * 2nu/pi)^(3/2) exp[-(N-1)/N nu(xi^2+xi'^2)].

## Context

This work resolves a controversy raised by Pethick and Pitaevskii (2000), who proposed using internal coordinates relative to the center of mass to define an internal one-particle density matrix for Bose-Einstein condensates, and by Gajda (2006), who noted that the Pethick-Pitaevskii criterion is incomplete for attractive systems with large center-of-mass uncertainty. The paper extends the discussion to self-bound systems such as alpha-particle condensates in nuclei, where the Hoyle state in carbon-12 and the 0+_6 state in oxygen-16 have been studied by Tohsaki (2001), Funaki (2003, 2008), and others. The Jacobi-type definition has already been successfully applied to study alpha-particle condensation in self-conjugate nuclei near the n-alpha disintegration threshold.
