# The stopping of low energy ions in reactions of astrophysical interest

> he velocity dependence of the stopping power of swift protons and deuterons in low energy collisions with hydrogen and helium gas targets is investigated with the numerical solution of the time-dependent Schroedinger coupled-channels equations using molecular orbital wavefunctions. At low projectile energies the stopping is mainly due to nuclear stopping, charge exchange of the electron, and excitation of the lowest levels in the target. The second and third mechanisms dominate at E < 200 eV. At lower energies it is also shown that a threshold effect is responsible for a quick drop of the energy loss. This investigation sheds more light on the long standing electron screening problem in fusion reactions of astrophysical interest.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the standard extrapolation of electronic stopping power (proportional to projectile velocity, S ~ v) fails at very low energies (below about 1 keV), and that a threshold effect causes a rapid drop in energy loss for p+He and d+He systems, which may help resolve the longstanding discrepancy between theoretical and experimental electron screening in astrophysical fusion reactions.

## Method
The author solves the time-dependent Schroedinger coupled-channels equations using molecular orbital wavefunctions for p+H and p+He systems. For p+H, only the two lowest molecular states (1sσ and 2pσ) are included, with energies calculated via Teller's two-center method in elliptical coordinates. For p+He, Slater-type orbitals and Hartree-Fock equations are used to construct molecular orbitals, and configuration-interaction with double excitations is included; the dynamical evolution is solved starting from 15 a.u. internuclear separation.

## Key numbers
- For p+H at E_p → 0, the charge exchange cross section becomes constant at σ = 37.88 × 10⁻¹⁶ cm².
- For p+H in the energy range 100 eV to 1 keV, the stopping power best fit yields S ~ v_p^1.35, contrasting with the standard S ~ v extrapolation.
- For p+He, the threshold energy for atomic excitations and charge exchange is E_p^thres ~ 9 keV, derived from the 1s²(¹S₀) → 1s2s(³S) transition energy ΔE = 18.7 eV.
- For d+³He, the threshold deuteron energy is of order 18 keV, and the numerical calculation agrees well with experimental data for E_d > 20 keV.
- The damping of charge exchange oscillations in p+He is described by a width Γ ≈ 5 eV for the band of states.

## Context
This work extends the two-center molecular orbital approach of Teller (1930) and the dynamical calculations of Grande and Schwietz (1993) to low-energy stopping power, addressing the electron screening problem in fusion reactions noted by Rolfs and collaborators (1987, 1995). The threshold effect, first reported by Formicola (2000) for d+³He, is shown to cause a rapid drop in electronic stopping at low energies, which may explain why the screening effect observed in experiments like ³He(d,p)⁴He is about twice the theoretical expectation. The results call for improved theoretical and experimental studies of energy loss at extremely low energies relevant to stellar fusion.
