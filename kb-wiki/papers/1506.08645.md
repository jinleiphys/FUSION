# Energy bursts from deconfinement in high-mass twin stars

> We estimate the energy reservoir available in the deconfinement phase transition induced collapse of a neutron star to its hybrid star mass twin on the "third family" branch, using a recent equation of state of dense matter. The available energy corresponding to the mass-energy difference between configurations is comparable with energies of the most violent astrophysical burst processes. An observational outcome of such a dynamical transition might be fast radio bursts, specifically a recent example of a FRB with a double-peak structure in its light curve.

# Energy bursts from deconfinement in high-mass twin stars

## Key claim

The energy reservoir available from the deconfinement phase transition induced collapse of a high-mass neutron star to its hybrid star mass twin is comparable to the most violent astrophysical burst processes, and such a dynamical transition might explain fast radio bursts with a double-peak structure in their light curve, specifically FRB121002. The authors qualify that this scenario applies specifically to high-mass stars near the maximum mass on the "third family" branch, unlike previous estimates for typical 1.4 solar mass stars.

## Method

The authors used the DD2 hadronic equation of state with excluded volume correction from quark substructure, combined with a NJL equation of state with multiquark interactions for quark matter, employing a Maxwell construction for the phase transition. They computed the gravitational mass versus baryonic mass relation for non-rotating stars and identified the mass-energy difference between the last stable hadronic configuration and the corresponding hybrid star configuration at the same baryonic mass, assuming baryon mass conservation during the dynamical collapse.

## Key numbers

- The energy reservoir ΔE available in the transition amounts to roughly 10^51 erg.
- The radius change ΔR in the transition is between 1 km and 1.5 km for the coupling constant range considered.
- The double-peak FRB121002 has a light curve peak separation of about 5 ms.
- The dynamical collapse timescale is about 1 ms.
- The maximum observed star mass constraint is 2 solar masses, as measured for PSR J0348+0432 and PSR J1614-2230.
- Typical gamma ray burst energies are 10^48 to 10^50 erg per second.

## Context

This work extends previous estimates of the energy reservoir for deconfinement phase transitions in typical 1.4 solar mass neutron stars by Zdunik (2005) and others, now applied to high-mass stars using the equation of state from Bejger (2015) that allows for a "third family" of compact stars near the maximum mass. The scenario provides an alternative to the direct neutron star collapse to a black hole model for fast radio bursts proposed by Falcke and Rezzolla (2014), and specifically offers an explanation for the double-peak structure observed in FRB121002 by Champion (2015), where the corequake transition to a metastable twin hybrid star generates the first peak before ultimate collapse to a black hole produces the second peak.
