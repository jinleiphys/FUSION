# Medium mass fragments production due to momentum dependent interactions

> The role of system size and momentum dependent effects are analyzed in multifragmenation by simulating symmetric reactions of Ca+Ca, Ni+Ni, Nb+Nb, Xe+Xe, Er+Er, Au+Au, and U+U at incident energies between 50 MeV/nucleon and 1000 MeV/nucleon and over full impact parameter zones. Our detailed study reveals that there exist a system size dependence when reaction is simulated with momentum dependent interactions. This dependence exhibits a mass power law behavior.

# Medium mass fragments production due to momentum dependent interactions

## Key claim
The multiplicity of medium mass fragments (MMFs) produced in symmetric heavy-ion collisions scales with projectile mass according to a power law when momentum dependent interactions (MDI) are included, but the power law exponent τ does not have a unique value and depends on incident energy and impact parameter.

## Method
The authors used the quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) model to simulate symmetric reactions of seven systems (Ca+Ca, Ni+Ni, Nb+Nb, Xe+Xe, Er+Er, Au+Au, and U+U) at incident energies from 50 to 1000 MeV/nucleon across full impact parameter zones. The phase space was clusterized using the minimum spanning tree (MST) method with a binding distance of 4 fm, and reactions were evolved for 200 fm/c using both soft and soft momentum dependent (SMD) equations of state with compressibility K = 200 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Seven symmetric reaction systems were simulated: Ca+Ca, Ni+Ni, Nb+Nb, Xe+Xe, Er+Er, Au+Au, and U+U.
- Incident energies ranged from 50 MeV/nucleon to 1000 MeV/nucleon.
- Medium mass fragments were defined as fragments with mass number A satisfying 5 ≤ A ≤ 9.
- The MST clustering condition required |r_i - r_j| ≤ 4 fm.
- The Yukawa potential parameter m = 1.5 fm, t3 = -6.66 MeV, t4 = 1.57 MeV, and t5 = 5×10^{-4} MeV^{-2}.
- The compressibility used was K = 200 MeV.
- Each reaction was simulated for 100 events.
- The reaction time was chosen as t = 200 fm/c.
- Scaled impact parameter values examined were b̂ = 0.0, 0.3, 0.6, and 0.9.

## Context
This work extends the earlier study by Singh and Puri (2002) which predicted a power law for system size effects in multifragmentation using a static equation of state, by now incorporating momentum dependent interactions. The study connects to experimental work by the MSU miniball and ALADIN groups (Ogilvie 1991, Bowman 1992) which varied reaction asymmetry, and to the plastic ball (Gutbrod 1990) and FOPI experiments (Schaffner 1997) which used symmetric colliding nuclei. The results enable systematic scaling predictions for MMF production across different system sizes in heavy-ion collisions.
