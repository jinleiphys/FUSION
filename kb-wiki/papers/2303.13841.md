# Systematic analysis of the nuclear absorption effect on the cross section of the knockout reaction

> Recent studies on nucleon and alpha knockout reactions have shown that the distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) is a simple and accurate method to describe these reactions. As it has been argued for decades, the nuclear absorption is one of the most important ingredients of the DWIA calculation. In this work, we systematically investigate the absorption effects on the cross sections of the nucleon and alpha knockout reactions. To do this, we calculate the ratio of the cross sections of the DWIA and plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) and examine its dependence on the mass number and single-particle orbital of the knocked-out particles. We will discuss the specific characteristics of the absorption effect for each reaction.

## Key claim

The nuclear absorption effect in knockout reactions, quantified by the ratio of distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) to plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) cross sections, depends systematically on target mass number and the radial quantum number \(n\) of the knocked-out particle; the DW-PW ratio generally decreases for heavier nuclei and increases for larger \(n\), with the \(n\)-dependence being more significant than the orbital angular momentum \(l\)-dependence.

## Method

The authors calculated the ratio of total cross sections from DWIA and PWIA (the DW-PW ratio) for both \((p,pN)\) nucleon knockout and \((p,p\alpha)\) alpha knockout reactions. For \((p,pN)\), they considered proton beams at 50 MeV and 200 MeV on 11 target nuclei from \(^{12}\)C to \(^{208}\)Pb, using Woods-Saxon single-particle wave functions, Koning-Delaroche optical potentials, and Franey-Love effective NN interactions. For \((p,p\alpha)\), they considered 101.5 MeV protons on 13 target nuclei from \(^{12}\)C to \(^{208}\)Pb, using Woods-Saxon bound-state wave functions for the alpha particle with \(l=0\) and radial quantum numbers \(n=2,4,6,8,11\), Avrigeanu optical potentials for the emitted alpha, and a folding model for the \(p\)-\(\alpha\) differential cross section.

## Key numbers

- For \((p,pN)\) at 50 MeV, the DW-PW ratio decreases with increasing target mass number across all orbitals studied (0p, 0d, 0f, 0g, 1p, 2s, 2p).
- For \((p,pN)\) at 200 MeV, the DW-PW ratio values are "noticeably larger" than at 50 MeV, consistent with easier penetration at higher incident energy.
- For \((p,p\alpha)\) at 101.5 MeV, the DW-PW ratio shows similar mass number and \(n\) dependence as for \((p,pN)\).
- The 2p neutron orbital in \((p,pn)\) at 50 MeV shows an exceptional behavior where the DW-PW ratio does not monotonically decrease with \(A\); the 2p neutron radius \(r_{\rm C}\) varies from 6.16 fm (\({}^{186}\)Os) to 6.37 fm (\({}^{208}\)Pb), a stronger \(A\)-dependence than for 2s orbitals.
- For the 2s proton orbital, \(r_{\rm C}\) values are 5.25 fm (\({}^{166}\)Er), 5.35 fm (\({}^{186}\)Os), and 5.42 fm (\({}^{208}\)Pb).
- For the 2s neutron orbital, \(r_{\rm C}\) values are 5.77 fm (\({}^{166}\)Er) and 5.86 fm (\({}^{186}\)Os).

## Context

This work extends the discussion of absorption effects in Wakasa (2017), which showed the DW-PW ratio for only a small number of cases in Fig. 31 of that review. The present systematic study covers a wider range of target nuclei and single-particle orbitals for both nucleon and alpha knockout, providing reference DW-PW ratios that can be used to estimate experimental knockout cross sections from elementary-process cross sections prior to actual measurements. The results enable future systematic knockout reaction experiments to better account for nuclear absorption when extracting spectroscopic information.
