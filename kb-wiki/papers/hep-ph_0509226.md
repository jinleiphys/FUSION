# The turbulent spectrum created by non-Abelian plasma instabilities

> Recent numerical work on the fate of plasma instabilities in weakly-coupled non-Abelian gauge theory has shown the development of a cascade of energy from long to short wavelengths. This cascade has a steady-state spectrum, analogous to the Kolmogorov spectrum for turbulence in hydrodynamics or for energy cascades in other systems. In this paper, we theoretically analyze processes responsible for this cascade and find a steady-state spectrum f_k ~ k^-2, where f_k is the phase-space density of particles with momentum k. The exponent -2 is consistent with results from numerical simulations. We also discuss implications of the emerging picture of instability development on the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario for (extremely high energy) heavy ion collisions, emphasizing fundamental questions that remain to be answered.

# The turbulent spectrum created by non-Abelian plasma instabilities

## Key claim

The steady-state spectrum of the energy cascade from long to short wavelengths in weakly-coupled non-Abelian gauge theory plasma instabilities follows $f_k \sim k^{-2}$ (where $f_k$ is the phase-space density of particles with momentum $k$), but this result is provisional on the assumption that multiple scattering off the non-perturbative infrared background is the dominant process, and the analysis is restricted to moderately anisotropic hard particle distributions with a single relevant soft scale.

## Method

The authors theoretically analyze the cascade of energy from unstable soft modes at momentum scale $m_\text{soft}$ toward higher momenta by comparing the rates of competing processes: large-angle $2\to2$ scattering and multiple scattering of high-momentum particles off the non-perturbative background field with wavenumber $m_\text{soft}$. They determine the spectral index $\nu$ by balancing the rate at which energy flows out the ultraviolet end of the cascade against the constant rate of energy input from the infrared end, using the Boltzmann equation in the classical field limit where occupation numbers satisfy $1 \ll f_k \ll 1/g^2$.

## Key numbers

- The steady-state spectrum exponent is $\nu = 2$, giving $f_k \sim (1/g^2) (m_\text{soft}/k)^2$, consistent with numerical simulations that extract $\nu = 2 \pm 0.2$ from Coulomb-gauge spectra.
- The ultraviolet cutoff of the cascade grows as $\Lambda(t) \propto t^{1/2}$.
- The classical treatment ($f_k \gg 1$ for $k \ll \Lambda$) is valid for times $t \lesssim 1/(g^2 m_\text{soft})$.
- For the bottom-up thermalization scenario with extreme anisotropy, the authors estimate an upper bound on the anisotropy ratio $p_z/p_\perp \sim 1/(Q_s \tau)^{1/8}$, compared to Bodeker's estimate of $1/(Q_s \tau)^{1/4}$ and the original Baier-Mueller-Schiff-Son result of $1/(Q_s \tau)^{1/3}$.

## Context

This work extends numerical studies of non-Abelian plasma instability growth in 3+1 dimensions (Arnold, Lenaghan, Moore 2003; Rebhan, Romatschke, Strickland 2004, 2005) by providing a theoretical explanation for the observed cascade spectrum. The analysis is analogous to investigations of thermalization during reheating after inflation (Son 1996; Tkachev 1996, 1997), though the spectral index and dominant processes differ. The results have implications for the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario of Baier, Mueller, Schiff, and Son (2001), which did not account for plasma instabilities. The paper identifies open questions about how extremely anisotropic distributions affect instability development, noting that Bodeker's (2005) analysis of instability effects on bottom-up thermalization may need revision because it assumed $O(1)$ anisotropy while predicting parametrically large anisotropy.
