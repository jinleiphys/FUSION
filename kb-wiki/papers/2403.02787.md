# Examination of STAR fixed-target data on directed flow at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 3 and 4.5 GeV

> We present results of simulations of directed flow of various hadrons in Au+Au collisions at collision energies of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 3 and 4.5 GeV. Simulations are performed within the model three-fluid dynamics (3FD) and the event simulator based on it (THESEUS). The results are compared with recent STAR data. The directed flows of various particles provide information on dynamics in various parts and at various stages of the colliding system depending on the particle. However, the information on the equation of state is not always directly accessible because of strong influence of the afterburner stage or insufficient equilibration of the matter. It is found that the crossover scenario gives the best overall description of the data. This crossover EoS is soft in the hadronic phase. The transition into QGP in Au+Au collisions occurs at collision energies between 3 and 4.5 GeV, at baryon densities $n_B \geq 4 n_0$ and temperatures $\approx 150$ MeV. In-medium effects in the directed flow of (anti)kaons are discussed.

## Key claim

The crossover equation of state (EoS) provides the best overall description of STAR fixed-target directed flow data at both 3 and 4.5 GeV, but the information on the EoS is not always directly accessible because of strong influence of the afterburner stage or insufficient equilibration of the matter, meaning the EoS signal is partially obscured by later-stage dynamics.

## Method

The authors performed simulations of Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies of 3 and 4.5 GeV per nucleon pair using the three-fluid dynamics (3FD) model, which treats the projectile, target, and fireball as separate interacting fluids, and also used the THESEUS event simulator that extends the 3FD output with a UrQMD afterburner stage for final-state interactions. The calculated directed flow of various hadrons was compared with recent STAR fixed-target data to test different equations of state, including a crossover scenario and a first-order phase transition scenario.

## Key numbers

- The transition into quark-gluon plasma in Au+Au collisions occurs at collision energies between 3 and 4.5 GeV, at baryon densities n_B >= 4 n_0 (where n_0 is normal nuclear density) and temperatures approximately 150 MeV
- The freeze-out energy density used in the 3FD model was epsilon_frz = 0.4 GeV/fm^3, chosen for best reproduction of secondary particle yields
- The soft EoS with incompressibility K = 210 MeV was found preferable from earlier KaoS and FOPI data analysis at collision energies E_lab <= 2A GeV (sqrt(s_NN) <= 2.7 GeV)

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of STAR Beam Energy Scan directed flow data (STAR, 2014) and the subsequent theoretical studies by Ivanov and Soldatov (2015, 2016) that indicated a crossover deconfinement transition, now applied to the newer STAR fixed-target data at 3 and 4.5 GeV. The results contradict recent transport model studies by Nara and collaborators (2020, 2022), Oliinychenko and collaborators (2023), and Steinheimer and collaborators (2022) that reported stiff EoSs were preferable at 3 GeV, while supporting earlier AGS-era findings by Danielewicz and collaborators (2002) and Russkikh and Ivanov (2006) that favored a soft EoS. The work enables more precise determination of the QCD phase diagram at high baryon density and constrains the density range where deconfinement begins.
