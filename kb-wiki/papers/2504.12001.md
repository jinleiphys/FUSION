# Charge radii of neutron-rich scandium isotopes and the seniority symmetry in the $0f_{7/2}$ shell

> Nuclear charge radii of neutron-rich $^{47-49}$Sc isotopes were measured using collinear laser spectroscopy at CERN-ISOLDE. The new data reveal that the charge radii of scandium isotopes exhibit a distinct trend between $N=20$ and $N=28$, with $^{41}$Sc and $^{49}$Sc isotopes having similar values, mirroring the closeness of the charge radii of $^{40}$Ca and $^{48}$Ca. Theoretical models that successfully interpret the radii of calcium isotopes could not account for the observed behavior in scandium radii, in particular the reduced odd-even staggering. Remarkably, the inclusion of the new $^{49}$Sc radius data has unveiled a similar trend in the charge radii of $N=28$ isotones and $Z=20$ isotopes when adding the neutrons atop the $^{40}$Ca core and the protons atop the $^{48}$Ca core, respectively. We demonstrate that this trend is consistent with the prediction of the seniority model.

## Key claim

The charge radii of scandium isotopes between N=20 and N=28 follow a seniority-limit pattern that is nearly identical for protons in the 0f7/2 shell (N=28 isotones) and neutrons in the 0f7/2 shell (Z=20 isotopes), but current state-of-the-art nuclear theories including Fayans density functional theory and valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group calculations fail to fully reproduce the observed scandium radii trend, particularly the reduced odd-even staggering.

## Method

Collinear laser spectroscopy at the COLLAPS setup at CERN-ISOLDE was used to measure hyperfine structure spectra of 44-49Sc isotopes via the 3d4s 3D1 → 3d4p 3F2 transition at 364.3 nm. Isotope shifts were extracted from the center-of-gravity of each spectrum relative to the stable reference isotope 45Sc, and changes in mean-square charge radii were deduced using atomic factors F and KMS calculated with the relativistic Fock-space coupled cluster method and configuration interaction with many-body perturbation theory.

- Charge radius of 49Sc: 3.505(1)(35) fm
- Charge radius of 48Sc: 3.512(1)(27) fm
- Charge radius of 47Sc: 3.526(1)(18) fm
- Charge radius of 46Sc: 3.538(1)(10) fm
- Charge radius of 44Sc: 3.542(1)(10) fm
- Reference charge radius of 45Sc: 3.5459(25) fm
- Field-shift factor F for the 3d4s 3D1 → 3d4p 3F2 transition: -373(12) MHz/fm2
- Mass-shift factor KMS for the 3d4s 3D1 → 3d4p 3F2 transition: 595(50) GHz·u
- Seniority-model fit parameters for Z=20 isotopes: a=0.132(7) fm2, b=-0.017(1) fm2, c=0.112(10) fm2
- Seniority-model fit parameters for N=28 isotones: a=0.148(9) fm2, b=-0.018(1) fm2, c=0.127(11) fm2

## Context

This work extends the charge radius measurements of scandium isotopes from the previous study around N=20 (Sc-radii-prl2023) up to N=28, completing the trend between the two magic numbers. The results reveal that 41Sc and 49Sc have nearly identical charge radii, mirroring the closeness of 40Ca and 48Ca radii first observed in 1984 (Ca-radii-1984). The data enable a direct comparison of the seniority coupling scheme for protons in the 0f7/2 shell (N=28 isotones) versus neutrons in the same orbital (Z=20 isotopes), showing that the monopole polarization effects are very similar for both cases. The failure of current ab initio VS-IMSRG calculations with the ΔNNLOGO chiral interaction to reproduce these trends, despite their success for potassium isotopes (K-radii-np2021), highlights the need for further theoretical developments in many-body methods.
