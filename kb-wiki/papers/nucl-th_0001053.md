# Effects of Dense Matter on Hadron Production in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The intrinsic transverse momentum distribution of partons in the nucleon can be used to explain a large amount of high-$p_T$ hadron and photon production data in high-energy nucleon-nucleon collisions at energies $\sqrt{s} \approx 20$ to 1800 GeV. However, proton-nucleus experiments at energies $\approx 30$ GeV show an extra enhancement (Cronin effect) in the yield of photons and mesons compared to a simple extrapolation of the proton-proton data. This enhancement is due to the effect of the dense hadronic matter encountered by the projectile proton in the nuclear environment. We discuss the origin and the properties of the nuclear enhancement.

# Effects of Dense Matter on Hadron Production in Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The Cronin effect (enhanced yield of photons and mesons in proton-nucleus collisions compared to scaled proton-proton data) can be explained by an increase in the width of the parton transverse momentum distribution as the projectile traverses dense nuclear matter, but only if one assumes that the projectile proton breaks up in a hard or semi-hard collision, with the saturating prescription (at most one effective broadening collision) providing reasonable agreement with data.

## Method

The authors performed a perturbative QCD calculation of hard-particle production cross sections using the factorization theorem, incorporating parton distribution functions (MRST98 NLO set), fragmentation functions (NLO parameterization), and a Gaussian transverse momentum distribution for partons. They introduced a phenomenological broadening term for proton-nucleus collisions where the average squared transverse momentum is increased by a factor C multiplied by a function of the number of nucleon-nucleon collisions at a given impact parameter, and tested a saturating prescription that limits the number of effective broadening collisions to at most one.

## Key numbers

- The E609 collaboration extracted a width of ⟨k_T⟩ = (0.9 ± 0.2) GeV/c for the transverse momentum distribution of partons in the proton from dijet events at 400 GeV/c.
- The saturating prescription uses a coefficient C^{sat} = 1.2 GeV^2, determined at √s ≈ 30 GeV.
- The calculation covers the center-of-mass energy range 18 GeV ≤ √s ≤ 65 GeV for proton-proton collisions.
- The K factor was set to K = 2 as an approximation for corrections beyond the Born term.
- The scales were fixed with Λ_MSbar (n_f=4) = 300 MeV and Q = Q̂ = p_T/2.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological approach of Wang and collaborators (1997-1998) who described the Cronin effect in terms of increased transverse momentum width, but addresses the problem that the required coefficient C becomes target-dependent when all projectile collisions are counted. The authors propose a saturating prescription that gives a target-independent value of C^{sat} = 1.2 GeV^2, which simultaneously describes both pion production data from Antreasyan (1979) in pW collisions at √s = 27.4 GeV and photon production data from Apanasevich (1998) in pBe collisions at √s = 31.6 GeV. The model requires systematic experimental studies up to √s = 200 GeV to be useful for RHIC predictions.
