# Shell-model study of $β^+$/EC-decay half-lives for $Z = 21-30$ nuclei

> In the present work, we have reported $β^+$/EC-decay half-lives for $Z = 21 -30$ nuclei using large-scale shell-model. %and N $\leq$ A recent study shows that some proton-rich nuclei in this region belong to the island of inversion. We have performed calculations for these nuclei using KB3G effective interaction, while for Ni, Cu, and Zn nuclei we have used JUN45 effective interaction in the $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ model space. The calculated quenching factors for $fp$ and $f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}$ space using KB3G and JUN45 effective interactions are also reported. Shell-model results of $β$-decay half-lives, excitation energies, log$ft$ values, and branching fractions are discussed and compared with the available experimental data. We have obtained a reasonable agreement with the available data.

## Key claim

The paper reports shell-model calculations of β⁺/EC-decay half-lives for proton-rich nuclei with Z = 21–30, claiming "reasonable agreement" with experimental data, but the agreement is qualified by the use of empirically determined quenching factors (q = 0.719 ± 0.050 for the fp space and q = 0.743 ± 0.030 for the f5/2pg9/2 space) that are necessary to bring theoretical Gamow-Teller strengths into line with measured values.

## Method

Large-scale shell-model calculations were performed using the KB3G effective interaction for nuclei in the fp model space (Z = 21–30) and the JUN45 effective interaction for Ni, Cu, and Zn nuclei in the f5/2pg9/2 model space. The shell-model code NuShellX@MSU was used for diagonalization of energy matrices. The authors computed β⁺/EC-decay half-lives, excitation energies, logft values, and branching fractions for allowed Fermi and Gamow-Teller transitions, and compared these with experimental data from the ENSDF database and published literature.

## Key numbers

- Quenching factor q = 0.719 ± 0.050 for the fp model space using KB3G effective interaction
- Quenching factor q = 0.743 ± 0.030 for the f5/2pg9/2 model space using JUN45 effective interaction
- After excluding outlier data points, quenching factors become q = 0.669 ± 0.020 (fp space) and q = 0.768 ± 0.030 (f5/2pg9/2 space)
- The constant κ = 6289 s used in the half-life formula, taken from Patrignani and the Particle Data Group (2016)
- The axial-vector coupling constant gA = 1.270 used in the calculations
- For ⁴¹Sc decay to ⁴¹Ca(7/2⁻): theoretical logft = 3.768 versus experimental logft = 3.452, with branching fraction 99.97% (theoretical) versus 99.96% (experimental)
- For ⁴²Ti decay to ⁴²Sc(1⁺): theoretical logft = 3.216 versus experimental logft = 3.495, with branching fraction 80.23% (theoretical) versus 56.06% (experimental)

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies by Kumar and Srivastava (2016) on β⁻-decays of fp and fpg shell nuclei to the β⁺/EC-decay channel for proton-rich nuclei in the same region. The study is motivated by recent experimental measurements at radioactive ion beam facilities (GANIL, GSI, ISOLDE/CERN) that have identified an island of inversion around N = 40, as discussed by Recchia et al. (2012) and Otsuka et al. (2020). The calculations provide systematic theoretical estimates needed to complement experimental data from Orrigo et al. (2016), Molina et al. (2015), and Dossat et al. (2007), and enable future comparisons with ab initio approaches such as those reported by Gysbers et al. (2019).
