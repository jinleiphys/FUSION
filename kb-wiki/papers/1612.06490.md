# Amplitude analysis and the nature of the Zc(3900)

> The microscopic nature of the XYZ states remains an unsettled topic. We show how a thorough amplitude analysis of the data can help constraining models of these states. Specifically, we consider the case of the Zc(3900) peak and discuss possible scenarios of a QCD state, virtual state, or a kinematical enhancement. We conclude that current data are not precise enough to distinguish between these hypotheses, however, the method we propose, when applied to the forthcoming high-statistics measurements should shed light on the nature of these exotic enhancements.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that current experimental data on the Zc(3900) peak are not precise enough to distinguish between a genuine QCD resonance, a virtual state, or a kinematical enhancement from a triangle singularity, but the proposed amplitude analysis framework will be able to discriminate these scenarios when applied to forthcoming high-statistics measurements.

## Method
The authors constructed a coupled-channel amplitude model for the reactions Y(4260) → J/ψ π π and Y(4260) → D D* π, treating all particles as spinless in S-wave. They parametrized the scattering matrix using a K-matrix approach and included triangle singularities from cross-channel exchanges (D1(2420), D0(2400), f0(980), σ) via dispersion relations. Four scenarios were tested: a Flatté K-matrix without triangle singularities (scenario III), a Flatté K-matrix with triangle singularities (scenario IIItr), a constant K-matrix with triangle singularities (scenario IV), and a constant K-matrix with a penalty forcing the pole far from the peak region to isolate triangle singularity effects (scenario tr). The model was fitted to 566 experimental data points from BESIII on mass projections of J/ψ π and D D* at center-of-mass energies of 4.23 GeV and 4.26 GeV.

## Key numbers
- The Zc(3900) peak appears at √s ∼ 3900 MeV in the J/ψ π and D D* mass distributions.
- The Y(4260) resonance mass is 4260 MeV, used as the fixed center-of-mass energy for the primary BESIII dataset.
- The D1(2420) resonance is the best candidate to produce a triangle cusp, with its mass of 2420 MeV.
- The f0(980) and σ parameters used in the fit are M_f0 = 920 MeV, Γ_f0 = 223 MeV, M_σ = 112 MeV, Γ_σ = 906 MeV, determined from a preliminary fit to the ππ distribution.
- The full dataset contains 566 experimental points; four points below the isospin-symmetric D D* threshold were removed.
- The incoherent background in the experimental analyses is approximately 15% of events and flat.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical discussion on the nature of XYZ states, specifically the Zc(3900), which was discovered simultaneously by BESIII (Ablikim 2013) and Belle (Liu 2013). Previous studies by Wang (2013) analyzed the J/ψ ππ Dalitz distribution with both an anomalous threshold and a resonance, concluding the cusp alone was insufficient. Similar analyses were performed by Albaladejo (2015, 2016) considering comparable scenarios. The paper's method enables future high-statistics data from BESIII and other facilities to determine whether the Zc(3900) is a compact QCD state, a hadron molecule, a virtual state, or a kinematic effect from triangle singularities.
