# Counterintuitive Behaviors in Configuration Mixing

> By starting with a simple configurations of 2 neutrons in a single j shell we hare able to learn the effects of high lying configurations on physical properties such as nuclear magnetic g factors. Some counterintuitive results are found when both high lying spin orbit partners are allowed to admix with the basic configuration.

## Key claim

By starting with a simple two-neutron system in a single \(j\) shell and admixing high-lying spin-orbit partner configurations, the authors find that including the \(h_{9/2}h_{9/2}\) configuration does not cancel the effect of the \(h_{11/2}h_{11/2}\) admixture on the magnetic \(g\) factor, but instead enhances it, a result that is counterintuitive and goes beyond first-order perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors construct a model of two neutrons starting from the \([g_{7/2}g_{7/2}]^{J}\) configuration and admix the \([h_{11/2}h_{11/2}]^{J}\), \([h_{11/2}h_{9/2}]^{J}\), and \([h_{9/2}h_{9/2}]^{J}\) configurations. They use a surface delta interaction (SDI) with strength \(C_{0} = -0.200\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and vary the single-particle energy splitting \(E\) between the \(g_{7/2}\) orbital and the higher \(h\) orbitals. The \(g\) factors for \(J=2^{+}\), \(4^{+}\), and \(6^{+}\) states are calculated for several cases: including only \([h_{11/2}h_{11/2}]\) (Case 1), adding \([h_{9/2}h_{9/2}]\) (Case 2), and including all four configurations (Case 3), as well as a case where the \(h_{9/2}\) energy is raised relative to \(h_{11/2}\) (Case 4).

## Key numbers

*   Free single-particle \(g\) factor for a neutron in the \(g_{7/2}\) shell: \(+0.42511\) (from Equation 2).
*   Free single-particle \(g\) factor for a neutron in the \(h_{11/2}\) shell: \(-0.34782\) (from Equation 1).
*   Free single-particle \(g\) factor for a neutron in the \(h_{9/2}\) shell: \(+0.34782\) (from Equation 2).
*   For \(J=2^{+}\) with \(E=+0.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\), the \(g\) factor in Case 1 (only \(h_{11/2}h_{11/2}\) admixture) is \(0.2870\); in Case 2 (adding \(h_{9/2}h_{9/2}\)) it is \(0.2497\), a further reduction.
*   For \(J=2^{+}\) with \(E=+0.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\), the \(g\) factor in Case 3 (all four configurations) is \(0.2015\).
*   The \(g\) factor for the mixed \([h_{11/2}h_{9/2}]^{J}\) configuration is \(-3.8260/(J(J+1))\), yielding \(-0.6377\) for \(J=2\), \(-0.1913\) for \(J=4\), and \(-0.0911\) for \(J=6\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous SDI-based \(g\) factor calculations for nuclei such as \(^{86}\mathrm{Kr}\) and \(^{112}\mathrm{Sn}\) (Zamick, Yu) and is motivated by the frustration in large-scale shell model calculations, such as those by Kumbartzki et al., where high-lying configurations cannot be included due to model space size. The simple two-neutron system allows the authors to explore the qualitative effects of these missing configurations, providing insight into what might be missing in more complex shell model calculations.
