# Description of shape coexistence in $^{96}$Zr based on the collective quadrupole Bohr Hamiltonian

> Experimental data on $^{96}$Zr indicate coexisting spherical and deformed structures with small mixing amplitudes. We investigate the properties of the low-lying collective states of $^{96}$Zr based on the collective quadrupole Bohr Hamiltonian. The $β$-dependent collective potential having two minima -- spherical and deformed, is fixed so to describe experimental data in the best way.Good agreement with the experimental data on the excitation energies, $B(E2)$ and $B(M1)$ reduced transition probabilities is obtained. It is shown that the low-energy structure of $^{96}$Zr can be reproduced in a satisfactory way in the geometrical model with a potential function supporting shape coexistence. However, the excitation energy of the $2^+_2$ state can be reproduced only if the rotation inertia coefficient is taken five times smaller then the vibrational one in the region of the deformed well. It is shown also that shell effects are important for the description of the $B(M1;2^+_2 \rightarrow 2^+_1)$ value. An indication on the influence of the pairing vibrational mode on the $ρ^2 (0^+_2 \rightarrow 0^+_1)$ value is obtained.

## Key claim

The low-energy structure of $^{96}$Zr can be reproduced satisfactorily by a geometrical collective model with a potential that supports shape coexistence, but only if the rotational inertia coefficient in the deformed well is taken five times smaller than the vibrational one, and if shell-model effects are incorporated into the M1 transition operator.

## Method

The authors constructed a collective quadrupole Bohr Hamiltonian with a $\beta$-dependent potential having two minima (spherical and deformed), fixed phenomenologically to best describe experimental data. They separated the $\gamma$ degree of freedom, assumed axial symmetry, and solved the resulting Schrödinger equation for the $0^+_{1,2}$ and $2^+_{1,2}$ states. The rotational inertia coefficient $b_{\rm rot}$ was treated as a free parameter and set to 0.2 relative to the vibrational coefficient.

## Context

This work extends the geometrical collective model approach to shape coexistence in $^{96}$Zr, following experimental evidence from Kremer (2016) showing high purity of coexisting spherical and deformed states. The authors build on earlier findings by Jolos (2007, 2008) that in well-deformed axially symmetric nuclei the vibrational inertia coefficient can exceed the rotational one by 4-10 times. The paper shows that while the collective model can reproduce most observables, the $\rho^2(0^+_2 \rightarrow 0^+_1)$ value is underestimated by a factor of about 6, suggesting the need to include pairing vibrational modes as discussed by Iwasaki. The M1 transition strength could only be reproduced by incorporating a $\beta$-dependent $g_R$ factor that accounts for shell-model values, highlighting the importance of microscopic effects beyond the purely geometrical model.
