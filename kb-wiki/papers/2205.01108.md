# Brief history of the pion-nucleon sigma term

> The pion-nucleon sigma term is a quantity involved in many important aspects of particle and nuclear physics. In this review I show its origin and how it is connected to important questions as the origin of mass of the ordinary matter, studies of dark matter detection and nucleosynthesis. I mention the most common methods used to obtain the sigma term, and comment on the extracted values. As it is shown, the accepted value of the sigma term has been moving from relatively low ($\sim 40$ MeV) to larger ones ($\sim 60$ MeV) until today, where this controversy still persist.

# Brief history of the pion-nucleon sigma term

## Key claim

The accepted value of the pion-nucleon sigma term has shifted over time from approximately 40 MeV to approximately 60 MeV, but this controversy persists because different methods (dispersion relations, chiral effective field theory, lattice QCD, and SU(3) breaking models) yield values that disagree, and the discrepancy is tied to the difficulty of accurately determining the isoscalar scattering length a⁺₀₊ and the scalar form factor correction Δ_σ.

## Method

The paper reviews the historical development of the pion-nucleon sigma term, defined as σ_πN = (m̂/2m_N) ⟨N|ūu + d̄d|N⟩, tracing its origin from current algebra and the Cheng-Dashen theorem. It surveys five methodological approaches: (1) models of SU(3) breaking relating σ_πN to baryon octet mass splittings; (2) fixed-t dispersion relations evaluating the D⁺ amplitude at the Cheng-Dashen point (ν=0, t=2M_π²); (3) sum rules connecting threshold parameters (particularly a⁺₀₊) to σ_πN; (4) chiral effective field theory using the low-energy constant c₁ from the Lagrangian term c₁⟨χ₊⟩ψ̄ψ; and (5) lattice QCD computing the nucleon mass dependence on quark mass via the Hellmann-Feynman theorem.

## Key numbers

- The sigma term value has moved from approximately 40 MeV to approximately 60 MeV over time, with the controversy persisting.
- The correction Δ_R in the modified Cheng-Dashen theorem is approximately 2 MeV (Bernard, 1996).
- The scalar form factor correction Δ_σ ≡ σ(t=2M_π²) – σ_πN was originally calculated as approximately 14 MeV (Pagels, 1972).
- In the Gasser-Leutwyler-Sainio evaluation, the combination Δ_D – Δ_σ = –3.3(2) MeV, with the curvature term Δ_D and scalar form factor correction nearly canceling.
- In the Roy-Steiner equation determination, Δ_D – Δ_σ = 1.8(2) MeV (Hoferichter, 2015).
- For a sigma term of 45 MeV, the chiral condensate ratio ⟨Ψ|q̄q|Ψ⟩/⟨0|q̄q|0⟩ vanishes at ρ ≈ 3ρ₀ (nuclear saturation density); for σ_πN = 60 MeV, it vanishes at ρ ≈ 2ρ₀.
- The strange quark content relation uses m_s/2m̂ ≈ 13, and σ₀ from octet baryon masses gives approximately 30 MeV if the Zweig rule holds.
- The Olsson sum rule expresses Σ = f_π² D⁺(0,2M_π²) in terms of threshold parameters: f_π⁻²Σ = 14.5 a⁺₀₊ – 5.06 (a⁽¹/²⁾₀₊)² – 10.13 (a⁽³/²⁾₀₊)² – 5.55 C⁺ – 0.06 a⁽⁺⁾₁₋ + 5.70 a⁺₁₊ – (0.08±0.03) [in M_π⁻¹].

## Context

This review extends the long-standing controversy on the sigma term value that began with Cheng (1975), who noted that assuming a negligible strange quark content (Zweig rule) would give σ_πN ≈ 30 MeV from octet baryon mass splittings, while dispersion relation analyses by Gasser, Leutwyler, and Sainio (1990) and later Hoferichter (2015) using Roy-Steiner equations found larger values near 60 MeV. The paper connects the sigma term to the origin of nucleon mass via the trace of the energy-momentum tensor, to dark matter detection cross sections (where Ellis (2008) showed that the range of sigma term values changes spin-independent cross sections by approximately 50%), and to nuclear thermodynamics where the chiral condensate restoration density depends linearly on σ_πN. The review enables future work by identifying that precise determinations of the isoscalar scattering length a⁺₀₊ from pionic atom spectroscopy (Gotta, 2008; Baru, 2011) are the key to resolving the discrepancy between phenomenological determinations.
