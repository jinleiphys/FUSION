# Microscopic Studies on Two-Phonon Giant Resonances

> A new class of giant resonances in nuclei, namely double giant resonances, is discussed. They are giant resonances built on top of other giant resonances. Investigation on their properties, together with similar studies on low-lying two-phonon states, should give an answer on how far the harmonic picture of boson-type excitations holds in the finite fermion systems like atomic nuclei. The main attention in this review is paid to double giant dipole resonances (DGDR) which are observed in relativistic heavy ion collisions with very large cross sections. A great experimental and theoretical effort is underway to understand the reaction mechanism which leads to the excitation of these states in nuclei, as well as the better microscopic understanding of their properties. The Coulomb mechanism of the excitation of single and double giant resonances in heavy ion collision at different projectile energies is discussed in details. A contribution of the nuclear excitation to the total cross section of the reaction is also considered. The Coulomb excitation of double resonances is described within both, the second-order perturbation theory approach and in coupled-channels calculation. The properties of single and double resonances are considered within the phenomenologic harmonic vibrator model and microscopic quasiparticle-RPA approach. For the last we use the Quasiparticle-Phonon Model (QPM) the basic ideas and formalism of which are presented. The QPM predictions of the DGDR properties (energy centroids, widths, strength distributions, anharmonicities and excitation cross sections) are compared to predictions of harmonic vibrator model, results of other microscopic calculations and experimental data available.

## Key claim

This review argues that double giant resonances, particularly the double giant dipole resonance (DGDR), can be used to test how well the harmonic picture of boson-type excitations holds in finite fermion systems like atomic nuclei, but the authors emphasize that a great experimental and theoretical effort is still underway to understand both the reaction mechanism and the microscopic properties of these states.

## Method

The authors review the Coulomb excitation mechanism for single and double giant resonances in relativistic heavy ion collisions using both second-order perturbation theory and coupled-channels calculations, with the reaction treated semiclassically via straight-line trajectories at high energies and via curved trajectories with recoil corrections at intermediate energies. The nuclear structure properties of single and double resonances are calculated within the Quasiparticle-Phonon Model (QPM), a microscopic quasiparticle-RPA approach, and compared to predictions from the phenomenological harmonic vibrator model and to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- DGDR excitation cross sections in relativistic heavy ion collisions are predicted to be of order a few hundred millibarns (from Baur and Bertulani, 1986)
- The GDR in 208Pb is observed at 13.5 MeV in inelastic scattering experiments with 17O at 22A MeV and 84A MeV, and with 36Ar at 95A MeV
- The adiabaticity parameter for intermediate energy collisions is given by ξ(R) = ω_fi R / (γ v), where the Lorentz factor γ is about 1.1 for collisions around 100A MeV
- The half-distance of closest approach in a head-on collision is given by a_0 = Z_1 Z_2 e^2 / (m_0 v^2), where m_0 is the reduced mass

## Context

This review extends the work of Baur and Bertulani (1986), who first showed that DGDR excitation cross sections in relativistic heavy ion collisions could be large enough to observe, and builds on experimental programs at GSI (Emling, 1994; Aumann, 1998) and elsewhere (Beene et al., 1990, 1993) that have made DGDR an active field. The paper enables future microscopic tests of anharmonicity in multi-phonon states by comparing QPM predictions for energy centroids, widths, strength distributions, and excitation cross sections against the harmonic vibrator model and experimental data.
