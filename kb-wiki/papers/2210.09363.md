# Effective model for pure Yang-Mills theory on $\mathbb{T}^2\times \mathbb{R}^2$ with Polyakov loops

> We investigate the phase diagram and thermodynamics of $SU(N)$ pure Yang-Mills theory on a manifold $\mathbb{T}^2\times \mathbb{R}^2$ with an effective model that includes two Polyakov loops along two compactified directions. We find that a rich phase structure can appear owing to the spontaneous breaking of two center symmetries for $N=2$ and $3$. Thermodynamic quantities are obtained in the model and compared with recent lattice results. It is shown that two Polyakov loops play significant roles in thermodynamics on $\mathbb{T}^2\times \mathbb{R}^2$.

## Key claim

The effective model with two Polyakov loops predicts a rich phase structure on the $\mathbb{T}^2\times \mathbb{R}^2$ manifold, but the authors explicitly state that their model fails to reproduce the lattice results for thermodynamic quantities even qualitatively, suggesting that the simple separable ansatz for the potential term requires improvement.

## Method

The authors extend the "model-B" approach of Meisinger (2001) by introducing constant background gauge fields along both the temporal and one spatial compactified direction, yielding two Polyakov-loop order parameters $P_\tau$ and $P_x$. The total free energy combines the one-loop perturbative contribution from massless gauge fields with a non-perturbative potential term based on the Haar measure from strong-coupling expansion, using a separable ansatz that sums independent contributions from each compactified direction. The expectation values of the Polyakov loops are determined by minimizing the free energy for $N=2$ and $N=3$ gauge groups.

## Key numbers

- For $N=2$, the critical temperature on $\mathbb{S}^1\times \mathbb{R}^3$ is $T_c^\infty = 1/((2/3)^{1/3}R) \approx 1/(0.874R)$, with a second-order phase transition.
- For $N=2$ at the symmetric point $L_\tau = L_x = L$, the second-order transition occurs at $L T_c^\infty = (3\ln 2/\pi)^{1/3} \approx 0.871$.
- For $N=3$, the critical temperature on $\mathbb{S}^1\times \mathbb{R}^3$ is $T_c^\infty \approx 1/(0.733R)$, with a first-order phase transition.
- For $N=3$ at the symmetric point $L_\tau = L_x = L$, the first-order transition occurs at $L T_c^\infty \approx 0.991$.
- The thermodynamic comparison with lattice data is performed at $T/T_c^\infty = 2.10$ for $N=3$.

## Context

This work extends the effective model approach of Meisinger (2001) for pure Yang-Mills theory on $\mathbb{S}^1\times \mathbb{R}^3$ to the manifold $\mathbb{T}^2\times \mathbb{R}^2$ with two compactified directions. The study is motivated by lattice simulations of Kitazawa (2019) which showed that pressure anisotropy in $SU(3)$ Yang-Mills theory with a spatial compactification is remarkably suppressed near the critical temperature compared to free field theory. The present model fails to reproduce these lattice results, indicating that the separable ansatz for the Polyakov-loop potential needs modification, and the authors suggest that successful modeling of the $\mathbb{T}^2\times \mathbb{R}^2$ system could provide deeper understanding of the theory on $\mathbb{S}^1\times \mathbb{R}^3$.
