# Nodal trajectories of spin observables and kaon photoproduction dynamics

> Spin observables for the reaction $γp \rightarrow K^+ Λ$ are examined using three recent dynamical models and are compared to the general features of such observables deduced earlier by Fasano, Tabakin and Saghai. These general features, such as the energy dependence of spin observables and the location of nodes in their angle dependence, are realized. Several instructive surprises, which occur in this comparison to the conjectures of Fasano et al., are then discussed. The sensitivity of spin observables to isobar and {\it t-}channel dynamics is analyzed and suggestions for selecting experiments which provide important dynamical information are presented.

## Key claim

The general rules for spin observables in kaon photoproduction, previously conjectured by Fasano, Tabakin and Saghai (FTS) based on helicity and multipole truncation arguments, are largely realized in three specific dynamical models, but several instructive surprises emerge, most notably that the photon asymmetry observable Σ can develop a double nodal structure at unexpectedly low energies due to J=5/2 strength arising from t-channel (kaon exchange) mechanisms rather than from explicit J=5/2 baryon resonances.

## Method

The authors computed the full set of 16 spin observables (cross-section plus 15 spin observables) for the reaction γp → K⁺Λ using three isobaric dynamical models: Adelseck-Saghai (AS), Williams-Ji-Cotanch (WJC), and Saclay-Lyon (SALY). They compared the angular dependence and energy evolution of these observables, particularly the location of sign-changing nodes as a function of incident photon energy, against the general helicity rules (h1–h6) and multipole rules (m1–m10) previously formulated by Fasano, Tabakin and Saghai (FTS).

## Key numbers

- The reaction threshold is Eγ^lab = 0.911 GeV; observables were examined at Eγ^lab = 0.920 GeV (near threshold) and Eγ^lab = 1.40 GeV.
- The AS model fits existing data up to Eγ^lab ≤ 1.5 GeV with χ²/N_d.f. ≈ 1.4.
- The WJC and SALY models cover photon energies up to Eγ^lab ≤ 2.1 GeV.
- The SALY model includes a J=3/2 resonance N*(1720) 1(3/2⁺) at about Eγ^lab = 1.1 GeV, corresponding to √s = 1.716 GeV in the center-of-mass frame.
- For the SALY model, the observable P develops a node starting at 180° at 0.96 GeV; for WJC, a node in P starts at 180° at 1.47 GeV.
- The observable Σ shows a bifurcation (two nodes) for the AS model at low energies and for WJC at higher energies (around 1.4 GeV), while SALY shows a single node moving rapidly from 180° to 0° in the region of its J=3/2 resonances.
- All models display a bifurcation in the observable E at about 1.2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier theoretical framework of Fasano, Tabakin and Saghai (FTS, 1992) by testing their conjectured rules against three specific dynamical models (AS, WJC, SALY) that include explicit baryon resonances and t-channel kaon exchanges. The paper identifies that the photon asymmetry Σ provides a particularly sensitive test of resonance versus t-channel dynamics and of the duality relationship between s-channel resonances and t-channel exchanges. The results enable experimentalists at facilities such as ELSA, CEBAF, and GRAAL to select which spin observables, especially Σ, P, H, and T, are most informative for extracting resonance parameters and distinguishing between competing dynamical mechanisms.
