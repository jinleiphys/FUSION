# Long-Range $N-J/ψ$ Interaction from an Operator Product Expansion Perspective

> A recent lattice QCD study has shown that the $N-J/ψ$ potential is attractive at all distances, and its long-range tail is well described by two-pion exchange. Here, we study to what extent the long-range part of the attraction can be reproduced from the perspective of the operator product expansion (OPE). This is accomplished by extracting the leading-order four-quark operator that couples to two pions and calculating its contribution to the $J/ψ$ mass in nuclear matter, to linear order in density, within the QCD sum rule framework. Using previous estimates of the four-quark operators for the chiral symmetric and breaking parts, we obtain a mass decrease that is smaller in magnitude but qualitatively consistent with the attraction obtained in the lattice QCD calculation. By expressing the interaction in terms of four-quark operators, we can analyze the effects of chiral symmetry restoration in dense matter on the masses of the $J/ψ$ and other mesons composed of heavy quarks.

## Key claim

The long-range attractive part of the nucleon-\(J/\psi\) potential, previously attributed to two-pion exchange in lattice QCD, can be qualitatively reproduced from the operator product expansion perspective using QCD sum rules, but the resulting mass shift is smaller in magnitude than the lattice result; the sign of the shift depends critically on isolating the pion-coupled component of the four-quark condensate via Fierz transformation.

## Method

The authors use QCD sum rules to compute the in-medium mass shift of the \(J/\psi\) meson at normal nuclear matter density, working to leading order in density and retaining operators up to mass dimension 6. They extract the leading-order four-quark operator that couples to two-pion states by applying the equation of motion to convert dimension-6 gluon operators into four-quark operators, then perform a Fierz transformation to project out the pseudoscalar and axial-vector channels that couple to pions. The density-dependent part of the four-quark condensate is estimated using previous results on chiral symmetric and breaking components, parameterized by a factor \(\kappa\) that accounts for deviations from the vacuum saturation hypothesis.

## Key numbers

* The spin-averaged energy shift from the two-pion exchange potential extracted from lattice QCD is \(\Delta m_{J/\psi}^{TPE} = -5\) MeV, using potential strengths \(\alpha^{(S=3/2)}_{J/\psi}=22\) and \(\alpha^{(S=1/2)}_{J/\psi}=23\) MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^2\) with \(r_{min}=1.0\) fm, \(r_{max}=1.8\) fm, and nuclear density \(\rho_0=0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\).
* The QCD sum rule analysis yields an attractive mass shift of 0.36–0.90 MeV for \(\kappa\) values in the range 2–5 with \(\alpha_{s,\text{IR}}=0.7\).
* The density-dependent part of the quark condensate squared is \(\langle\bar{u}u\rangle^{2}_{\Delta\rho} = -1.38 \times 10^{-4}\) GeV\(^6\) at \(\rho=\rho_0\), using \(\sigma_{\pi N}=39.7\) MeV and \(\langle \bar{u}u \rangle_{0}=(-0.246\) GeV\()^3\).
* The vacuum gluon condensates used are \(G_0 = 0.012\) GeV\(^4\) and \(G_3 = 0.0467\) GeV\(^6\); the charm quark mass is \(m_c = 1.262\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule analyses of the \(J/\psi\) mass in nuclear matter by Klingl (1999), Hayashigaki (1999), and S.S. Kim (2001), which predicted a modest downward mass shift of about 5–10 MeV at normal nuclear density from gluon condensates. It directly addresses the recent lattice QCD finding by Lyu (2022, 2025) that the nucleon-\(J/\psi\) interaction is attractive at all distances with a long-range tail dominated by two-pion exchange, and the dispersive analysis by Wu (2025) finding an attractive scattering length \(|a_{NJ/\psi}|\gtrsim 0.16\) fm. By expressing the interaction in terms of four-quark operators, the work enables analysis of how chiral symmetry restoration in dense matter affects the masses of heavy quark mesons, and identifies that higher-order corrections to the four-quark Wilson coefficient (parameterized by \(\kappa\)) are important for quantitative agreement with lattice data.
