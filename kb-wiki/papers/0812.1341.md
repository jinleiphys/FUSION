# The Quest for the Nuclear Equation of State

> The present status of the efforts to determine the nuclear equation of state from results of heavy ion reactions and from astrophysical observations is reviewed

## Key claim

This review paper summarizes the status of efforts to determine the nuclear equation of state from heavy ion reactions and astrophysical observations, concluding that heavy ion data are only compatible with a soft equation of state (compressibility modulus K around 240 MeV) but cautioning that this value was extracted from a highly excited non-equilibrium system and conclusions about the compressibility modulus at large densities and low temperature must be drawn with great caution.

## Method

The authors review transport theory simulations (quantum molecular dynamics and Boltzmann equation approaches) that simulate heavy ion reactions from initial separation to final detection, using parameters for two- and three-body interactions characterized by a compressibility modulus (soft: K=250 MeV; hard: K=380 MeV). They compare simulation results for K+ meson production in heavy ion collisions (particularly subthreshold production via ΔN → N K+ Λ) and in-plane flow observables against experimental data, and also review astrophysical constraints from neutron star structure equations (Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations) and core-collapse supernova simulations.

## Key numbers

- The volume compressibility modulus from non-relativistic and relativistic approaches is around K = 240 MeV, with a possible uncertainty of 30% due to underestimated surface compressibility effects.
- The Weizsäcker mass formula parameters: a_V = 15.75 MeV (volume energy), a_S = 17.8 MeV (surface energy), a_C = 0.710 MeV (Coulomb energy), a_A = 23.7 MeV (symmetry energy), a_P = 23.7 MeV (pairing energy).
- At normal nuclear matter density ρ0, the Fermi wave number is k_F = 1.42 fm^{-1} and the average internuclear distance is about three times the hard core radius (0.4-0.5 fm).
- The binding energy per nucleon at ρ0 is E/A = -15.75 MeV from the Weizsäcker formula.
- The energy needed for K+ production in the NN center of mass system is 671 MeV.
- The Hulse-Taylor pulsar mass is (1.4414 ± 0.0002) M⊙.
- A free neutron gas supports a maximum neutron star mass of only 0.7 M⊙.
- Heavy ion reactions reach densities up to 3ρ0, with K+ mesons in Au+Au collisions at 1.5 AGeV produced at around 2ρ0.

## Context

This review extends earlier work on the nuclear equation of state by summarizing results from transport theory simulations (IQMD, UrQMD, QMD, HSD, RBUU approaches) and comparing with astrophysical constraints from neutron star observations and supernova simulations. The paper discusses how the K+ meson production observable has converged across different simulation programs (as shown in the Trento 2003 workshop and Fuchs 2006 studies), and how the extracted compressibility modulus agrees with that from nuclear volume oscillation studies (Mon 1987, Shl 1993, Col 1994, Vre 1995) but notes this agreement may be accidental. The review enables future work on constraining the equation of state at high densities through combined heavy ion and astrophysical observations, particularly with upcoming facilities like FAIR at GSI (operational in 2013) and through gravitational wave detection from neutron star mergers.
