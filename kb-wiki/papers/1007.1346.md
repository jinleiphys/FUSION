# Effective chiral restoration in the rho'-meson in lattice QCD

> In simulations with dynamical quarks it has been established that the ground state rho in the infrared is a strong mixture of the two chiral representations (0,1)+(1,0) and (1/2,1/2)_b. Its angular momentum content is approximately the 3S1 partial wave which is consistent with the quark model. Effective chiral restoration in an excited rho-meson would require that in the infrared this meson couples predominantly to one of the two representations. The variational method allows one to study the mixing of interpolators with different chiral transformation properties in the non-perturbatively determined excited state at different resolution scales. We present results for the first excited state of the rho-meson using simulations with n_f=2 dynamical quarks. We point out, that in the infrared a leading contribution to rho'= rho(1450) comes from (1/2,1/2)_b, in contrast to the rho. Its approximate chiral partner would be a h_1(1380) state. The rho' wave function contains a significant contribution of the 3D1 wave which is not consistent with the quark model prediction.

# Effective chiral restoration in the rho'-meson in lattice QCD

## Key claim
The first excited state of the rho-meson, rho(1450), is dominated in the infrared by the (1/2,1/2)_b chiral representation rather than being a pure 3S1 radial excitation as predicted by the quark model, indicating a smooth onset of effective chiral restoration. This conclusion is based on the ratio of vector to pseudotensor couplings at large smearing radii, though the authors note they cannot reliably extrapolate to the 1 fm scale with only two smearing widths.

## Method
The authors use the variational method on a cross-correlation matrix of four interpolating operators (two Dirac structures with two different Gaussian smearing widths of approximately 0.34 fm and 0.67 fm) on 16^3 x 32 lattices with n_f=2 dynamical Chirally Improved fermions and Luscher-Weisz gauge action. They extract the ratio a_V/a_T of vector to pseudotensor couplings for both the ground state and first excited rho-meson at three different pion masses (323, 469, and 526 MeV) and study the dependence of this ratio on the smearing radius R, which sets the resolution scale.

## Key numbers
- Ground state rho at R=0.67 fm: ratio a_V/a_T approaches approximately 1.2, corresponding to a wave function of 0.997|3S1> - 0.073|3D1>
- Three data sets: Set A with m_pi = 526(7) MeV and m_rho = 911(11) MeV; Set B with m_pi = 469(5) MeV and m_rho = 870(10) MeV; Set C with m_pi = 323(5) MeV and m_rho = 795(15) MeV
- Lattice spacings: 0.1507(17) fm (Set A), 0.1500(12) fm (Set B), 0.1440(12) fm (Set C)
- For rho(1450) at large R, the ratio a_V/a_T is very small (could be positive or negative in sign), implying a leading contribution from (1/2,1/2)_b representation
- Assuming vanishing ratio at R ~ 1 fm gives partial wave content sqrt(1/3)|3S1> - sqrt(2/3)|3D1>

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous lattice studies of chiral representations in the ground state rho-meson (Glozman, Lang, Limmer 2009) and their broader excited hadron spectroscopy program with dynamical fermions (Engel et al. 2010, Gattringer et al. 2008). It directly tests the conjecture of effective chiral restoration in excited hadrons proposed by Cohen and Glozman (2000s), which predicts that highly excited states should approximately decouple from the quark condensate and belong to single chiral representations. The results contradict the quark model prediction that rho(1450) is a 3S1 radial excitation (Isgur and Godfrey 1985, Particle Data Group), and instead support the existence of a chiral partner relation between rho(1450) and h1(1380). The next excited state, rho(1700), is predicted to be dominated by the (0,1)+(1,0) representation with a1(1640) as its chiral partner.
