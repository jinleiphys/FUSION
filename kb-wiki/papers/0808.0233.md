# Cluster formation in compact stars: relativistic versus Skyrme models

> We present various properties of nuclear and compact-star matter, comparing the predictions from two kinds of phenomenological approaches: relativistic models (both with constant and density-dependent couplings) and non-relativistic Skyrme-type interactions. We mainly focus on the liquid-gas instabilities that occur at sub-saturation densities, leading to the decomposition of the homogeneous matter into a clusterized phase. Such study is related to the description of neutron-star crust (at zero temperature) and of supernova dynamics (at finite temperature).

## Key claim

The paper compares relativistic and non-relativistic nuclear models to show that, while both reproduce similar qualitative features of cluster formation in compact-star matter, they exhibit significant quantitative differences in the isovector channel that affect predictions for neutron-star crust properties and supernova dynamics, particularly regarding the symmetry energy and its density dependence.

## Method

The authors used the Vlasov formalism to study dynamic spinodal instabilities in nuclear matter at zero temperature, comparing predictions from five Skyrme-type non-relativistic interactions (SIII, SGII, SLy230a, NRAPR, LNS) and five relativistic models (NL3, NLδ, TW, DD-ME2, DDHδ). They calculated the dispersion relation for density fluctuations and identified the instability regions as a function of baryon density and isospin asymmetry, analyzing both thermodynamic and dynamic spinodal boundaries.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ₀ ranges from 0.145 fm⁻³ (SIII) to 0.175 fm⁻³ (LNS) for Skyrme models, and from 0.148 fm⁻³ (NL3) to 0.160 fm⁻³ (NLδ) for relativistic models
- Symmetry energy at saturation a_s(ρ₀) ranges from 26.9 MeV (SGII) to 33.4 MeV (LNS) for Skyrme models, and from 25.1 MeV (DDHδ) to 37.4 MeV (NL3) for relativistic models
- Slope parameter L(ρ₀) ranges from 9.9 MeV (SIII) to 61.5 MeV (LNS) for Skyrme models, and from 48.6 MeV (DDHδ) to 118.3 MeV (NL3) for relativistic models
- Symmetry incompressibility K_sym(ρ₀) ranges from -394 MeV (SIII) to -98 MeV (SLy230a) for Skyrme models, and from -125 MeV (TW) to 127 MeV (NLδ) for relativistic models
- The dynamic spinodal instability region is approximated using q = 80 MeV transferred momentum for all models

## Context

This work extends previous separate studies of spinodal instabilities in relativistic models (inst04, inst06, umodes06, umodes06a, umodes08) and Skyrme models (JMPC-PRC67, CD-A2, CD-A3) by providing a direct quantitative comparison between the two approaches. The study is related to the description of neutron-star crust (at zero temperature) and supernova dynamics (at finite temperature), and complements equilibrium approaches such as the virial equation of state (Horowitz and Schwenk, 2006) and pasta-phase calculations. The results enable identification of which nuclear-matter properties most need experimental constraints from facilities like FAIR@GSI and SPIRAL2@GANIL.
