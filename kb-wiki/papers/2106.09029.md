# Polarization tensor of magnetized quark-gluon plasma at nonzero baryon density

> We derive a general expression for the absorptive part of the one-loop photon polarization tensor in a strongly magnetized quark-gluon plasma at nonzero baryon chemical potential. To demonstrate the application of the main result in the context of heavy-ion collisions, we study the effect of a nonzero baryon chemical potential on the photon emission rate. The rate and the ellipticity of photon emission are studied numerically as a function the transverse momentum (energy) for several values of temperature and chemical potential. When the chemical potential is small compared to the temperature, the rates of the quark and antiquark splitting processes (i.e., $q\rightarrow q +γ$ and $\bar{q}\rightarrow \bar{q} +γ$, respectively) are approximately the same. However, the quark splitting gradually becomes the dominant process with increasing the chemical potential. We also find that increasing the chemical potential leads to a growing total photon production rate but has only a small effect on the ellipticity of photon emission. The quark-antiquark annihilation ($q+\bar{q}\rightarrow γ$) also contributes to the photon production, but its contribution remains relatively small for a wide range of temperatures and chemical potentials investigated.

## Key claim
The paper derives a general expression for the absorptive part of the one-loop photon polarization tensor in a strongly magnetized quark-gluon plasma at nonzero baryon chemical potential, extending previous zero-chemical-potential results. The authors find that increasing the chemical potential leads to a growing total photon production rate but has only a small effect on the ellipticity of photon emission, with the quark splitting process gradually becoming dominant over antiquark splitting as chemical potential increases.

## Method
The authors start from the Landau level representation of the fermion Green's function in a magnetic field and compute the one-loop photon polarization tensor at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential using the Matsubara formalism. They extract the absorptive part of the retarded polarization tensor by analytic continuation and use it to calculate the differential photon emission rate and ellipticity via standard formulas. Numerical evaluations are performed for a two-flavor quark-gluon plasma with light quark masses of 5 MeV, summing over up to 1000 Landau levels for magnetic field strengths of |eB| = m_pi^2 and |eB| = 5 m_pi^2 at temperatures of 0.2 GeV and 0.35 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Quark masses are set to m = 5 MeV for both u and d flavors.
- Magnetic field strengths used: |eB| = m_pi^2 (approximately 3.08 x 10^18 G) and |eB| = 5 m_pi^2 (approximately 1.54 x 10^19 G), where m_pi = 0.135 GeV.
- Temperatures studied: T = 0.2 GeV and T = 0.35 GeV.
- Chemical potential values compared: mu = 0, mu = 0.1 GeV, mu = 0.2 GeV, and mu = 1 GeV.
- Transverse momentum range: from k_T,min = 0.01 GeV to k_T,max = 1 GeV with step Delta k_T = 0.01 GeV.
- At its peak value, the total photon emission rate at mu = 0.2 GeV is only about 40% larger than at mu = 0 for the parameters studied.
- Maximum number of Landau levels included in sums: n_max = 1000.

## Context
This work extends the zero-chemical-potential results for the photon polarization tensor in a magnetized plasma from Wang (2020) and Wang (2021) to the case of nonzero baryon chemical potential. The study is relevant for heavy-ion collision phenomenology, where the produced quark-gluon plasma carries a nonzero baryon chemical potential as an unavoidable artifact of the initial baryon charge from colliding ions, as noted by Bass (1998). The results enable quantitative predictions for direct photon emission from magnetized quark-gluon plasma under conditions more realistic for heavy-ion experiments, and the authors suggest that qualitative features of photon emission could provide a measure of the magnetic field strength in deconfined matter.
