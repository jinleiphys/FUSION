# Phi meson production at RHIC

> The production of phi mesons in Au+Au collisions at RHIC and their propagation in a hot and dense nuclear medium is studied within the microscopic quark-gluon string model. The inverse slope parameter of the transverse mass distribution agrees well with that extracted from the STAR data, while the absolute yield of phi is underestimated by a factor 2. It appears that the fusion of strings alone cannot increase the phi yield either. Less than 30% of detectable phi's experience elastic scattering, this rate is insufficient for the full thermalization of phi. The directed flow of phi at |y|<2 demonstrates strong antiflow behavior, whereas its elliptic flow rises up to about 3.5% in the same rapidity interval. As a function of transverse momentum it rises linearly with increasing p_t, in agreement with the STAR data, and saturates at p_t > 2 GeV/c.

## Key claim

The quark-gluon string model (QGSM) reproduces the inverse slope parameter of the phi meson transverse mass distribution in agreement with STAR data, but underestimates the absolute phi yield by a factor of 2, and string fusion alone cannot resolve this discrepancy.

## Method

The authors used the microscopic quark-gluon string model (QGSM) to simulate Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 130 AGeV at RHIC, tracking phi meson production, propagation, and freeze-out. They compared the model results to STAR experimental data for transverse mass spectra, yields, and anisotropic flow parameters, and also tested the string fusion model (SFM) to examine whether string fusion could increase the phi yield.

## Key numbers

- Inverse slope parameter T from QGSM: 378 MeV (central 0-11%), 349 MeV (11-45%), 370 MeV (45-85%); experimental values: 379 +/- 50 MeV, 369 +/- 73 MeV, 417 +/- 75 MeV for the same centrality bins
- QGSM underestimates absolute phi yield by a factor of 2 compared to STAR data
- Less than 30% of detectable phi mesons experience at least one elastic rescattering before freeze-out
- About 51% of detectable phi mesons decay after decoupling from the fireball
- Elliptic flow of phi at midrapidity: v2 = 3.3 +/- 1.1%
- Elliptic flow of phi rises up to about 3.5% in the rapidity interval |y| < 2
- Phi elliptic flow rises linearly with p_t and saturates at p_t > 2 GeV/c
- In the string fusion model, phi yield for 0-11% centrality: 5.71 (no fusion), 3.60 (fusion), 4.54 (fusion plus rescattering); experimental value: 5.73 +/- 0.37

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of phi meson production at SPS energies using the UrQMD model (Soff, 2001) and addresses the "phi meson puzzle" observed by NA49 and NA50 at SPS and by PHENIX at RHIC, where phi yields extracted from di-kaon and dimuon channels disagree. The results enable further investigation of phi meson thermalization and flow as signatures of the quark-gluon plasma, and point to the need for including OZI-violation mechanisms and increased strangeness content in nucleons to match experimental yields.
