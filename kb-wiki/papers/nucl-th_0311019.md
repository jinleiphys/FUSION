# On the density dependent hadron field theory at finite temperature and its thermodynamical consistency

> In this work we study in a formal way the density dependent hadron field theory at finite temperature for nuclear matter. The thermodynamical potential and related quantities, as energy density and pressure are derived in two different ways. We first obtain the thermodynamical potential from the grand partition function, where the Hamiltonian depends on the density operator and is truncated at first order. We then reobtain the thermodynamical potential by calculating explicitly the energy density in a Thomas-Fermi approximation and considering the entropy of a fermi gas. The distribution functions for particles and antiparticles are the output of the minimization of the thermodynamical potential. It is shown that in the mean field theory the thermodynamical consistency is achieved. The connection with effective chiral lagrangians with Brown-Rho scaling is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper formally demonstrates that in the density dependent hadron field theory (DDHFT) at finite temperature, the mean field approximation (MFA) and the Thomas-Fermi approximation (TFA) yield identical thermodynamic potentials and distribution functions, thereby proving thermodynamic consistency is achieved in the mean field theory.

## Method

The authors first derive the thermodynamic potential from the grand partition function, expanding the density-dependent Hamiltonian to first order in the density operator around the equilibrium mean density. They then rederive the thermodynamic potential by calculating the energy density in the Thomas-Fermi approximation and using the entropy of a Fermi gas, minimizing the potential to obtain the particle and antiparticle distribution functions. The two approaches are compared to verify thermodynamic consistency, and the connection to effective chiral Lagrangians with Brown-Rho scaling is discussed.

## Key numbers

- The scalar field equation yields \( m_\phi^2 \phi_0 - \Gamma_\phi(\rho_0) \rho_s = 0 \), where \(\rho_s\) is the thermal scalar density.
- The vector field equations give \( m_\omega^2 \omega_0 - \Gamma_\omega(\rho_0) \rho_0 = 0 \) and \( m_\rho^2 b_0 - \frac{\Gamma_\rho(\rho_0)}{2} \rho_3 = 0 \), with \(\rho_0\) the baryon density and \(\rho_3\) the isovector density.
- The distribution functions for particles and antiparticles are \( f_{i\pm} = 1 / \left( 1 + \exp[(E^*({\mathbf p}) \mp \mu^*_i)/T] \right) \), where \( E^*({\mathbf p}) = \sqrt{{\mathbf p}^2 + M^{*2}} \) and \( M^* = M - \Gamma_\phi(\rho_0) \phi_0 \).
- The pressure in the Thomas-Fermi approach is given by \( P = \frac{1}{3\pi^2} \sum_{i=p,n} \int dp \frac{{\mathbf p}^4}{\sqrt{{\mathbf p}^2 + M^{*2}}} (f_{i+} + f_{i-}) - \frac{m_\phi^2}{2} \phi_0^2 (1 + 2 \frac{\rho}{\Gamma_\phi} \frac{\partial \Gamma_\phi}{\partial \rho}) + \frac{m_\omega^2}{2} \omega_0^2 (1 + 2 \frac{\rho}{\Gamma_\omega} \frac{\partial \Gamma_\omega}{\partial \rho}) + \frac{m_\rho^2}{2} b_0^2 (1 + 2 \frac{\rho}{\Gamma_\rho} \frac{\partial \Gamma_\rho}{\partial \rho}) \).

## Context

This work extends the density dependent hadron field theory formalism of Florkowski and Lenske (1997) and Typel and Wolter (1999) to finite temperature, proving thermodynamic consistency that was previously assumed in calculations by Delfino et al. (1999). The formalism enables consistent finite-temperature studies of nuclear matter within DDHFT and can be directly applied to effective chiral Lagrangians with Brown-Rho scaling, such as those proposed by Song et al. (1997), for which the zero-temperature thermodynamics has been studied by Rikovska-Stone et al. (2000).
