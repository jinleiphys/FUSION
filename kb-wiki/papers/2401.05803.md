# Impact of the molecular resonances on the 12C+12C fusion reaction rate

> The properties of the low-energy 12C+12C molecular resonances, which potentially enhance the fusion reaction rate at low temperatures, have been investigated by a full-microscopic nuclear model employing various nuclear energy density functionals. We show that some density functionals plausibly describe the observed high-spin 12C+12C molecular resonances and predict many 0+ and 2+ resonances at low energies, which enhance the reaction rate. We also discuss how the uncertainty in the nuclear energy density functionals propagates to that of the reaction rate.

## Key claim

Some nuclear energy density functionals (Gogny-type) predict low-energy \(0^+\) and \(2^+\) molecular resonances that enhance the \(^{12}\mathrm{C}+^{12}\mathrm{C}\) fusion reaction rate at low temperatures, while others (Skyrme-type) do not produce such low-lying resonances and yield a hindered rate; the authors interpret the region between these two sets of predictions as the constraint imposed by current nuclear density functionals.

## Method

The authors employed the generator coordinate method (GCM) with antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) to describe \(^{12}\mathrm{C}+^{12}\mathrm{C}\) molecular resonances, including coupling to the \(\alpha+^{20}\mathrm{Ne}\) and \(p+^{23}\mathrm{Na}\) exit channels and compound \(^{24}\mathrm{Mg}\) states. They used five nuclear energy density functionals (Gogny D1S and D1M*, Skyrme SkM*, SLy4, and SIII) and calculated resonance parameters via the \(R\)-matrix theory and Breit-Wigner formula, evaluating reduced width amplitudes (RWAs) for six decay channels to obtain partial widths and the modified astrophysical \(S^*\)-factor.

## Key numbers

* The D1M* functional yields a \(2^+\) resonance at 0.40 MeV with total width 0.14 MeV and a \(0^+\) resonance at 0.93 MeV with total width 0.47 MeV; the \(2^+\) resonance at 1.06 MeV has total width 0.10 MeV.
* The SkM* functional yields no resonances below 2.42 MeV; its lowest \(2^+\) resonance is at 2.42 MeV with total width 0.36 MeV.
* The \(0^+\) resonance at 0.93 MeV (D1M*) has a reduced width amplitude for the \(^{12}\mathrm{C}+^{12}\mathrm{C}\) channel of 4.8% at channel radius 6 fm; the \(2^+\) resonance at 1.06 MeV has 1.1% at 7 fm.
* The isoscalar monopole transition strength for the D1M* \(0^+\) resonance at 0.93 MeV is 0.39 Weisskopf units; the isoscalar quadrupole transition strength for the \(2^+\) resonance at 1.06 MeV is 2.34 W.u.
* The reaction rates from Gogny functionals are close to the CF88 estimation, while Skyrme functionals give rates comparable to the hindrance model; the THM reaction rate is significantly higher than the region bounded by these two families.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous AMD study (Taniguchi 2021) by employing multiple density functionals and including full channel coupling to assess the uncertainty in the \(^{12}\mathrm{C}+^{12}\mathrm{C}\) fusion reaction rate. It directly addresses the long-standing discrepancy between the standard CF88 rate (Caughlan and Fowler 1988), the hindrance model (Jiang 2007), and the enhanced rate from the Trojan Horse Method (Tumino 2018). The results provide a nuclear-theory-based constraint on the reaction rate that can be tested by future experiments, particularly those searching for \(0^+\) and \(2^+\) resonances via isoscalar transitions, as recently demonstrated by Adsley (2022) who discovered a \(0^+\) resonance at 1.38 MeV.
