# Accounting for the analytical properties of the quark propagator from Dyson-Schwinger equation

> An approach based on combined solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter (BS) and Dyson-Schwinger (DS) equations within the ladder-rainbow approximation in the presence of singularities is proposed to describe the meson spectrum as quark antiquark bound states. We consistently implement into the BS equation the quark propagator functions from the DS equation, with and without pole-like singularities, and show that, by knowing the precise positions of the poles and their residues, one is able to develop reliable methods of obtaining finite interaction BS kernels and to solve the BS equation numerically. We show that, for bound states with masses $M < 1$ GeV, there are no singularities in the propagator functions when employing the infrared part of the Maris-Tandy kernel in truncated BS-DS equations. For $M >1 $ GeV, however, the propagator functions reveal pole-like structures. Consequently, for each type of mesons (unflavored, strange and charmed) we analyze the relevant intervals of $M$ where the pole-like singularities of the corresponding quark propagator influence the solution of the BS equation and develop a framework within which they can be consistently accounted for. The BS equation is solved for pseudo-scalar and vector mesons. Results are in a good agreement with experimental data. Our analysis is directly related to the future physics programme at FAIR with respect to open charm degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for meson masses below 1 GeV, the quark propagator functions from the Dyson-Schwinger equation have no singularities when using the infrared part of the Maris-Tandy kernel, but for meson masses above 1 GeV the propagator functions develop pole-like singularities that must be accounted for to obtain stable numerical solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation.

## Method

The authors solve the truncated Dyson-Schwinger equation in the rainbow-ladder approximation using a hyperspherical harmonics decomposition to obtain the quark propagator functions sigma_s and sigma_v for u/d, s, and c quarks. They then analyze the analytical properties of these propagators in the complex Euclidean momentum plane defined by the Bethe-Salpeter equation integration domain, using Rouche's theorem and graphical representations of inverse propagators as vortex fields. For the region where propagators are analytic (Re p^2 > 0), they parametrize the functions using rational complex functions with 12 parameters fitted via the Levenberg-Marquardt algorithm, while for the region with singularities (Re p^2 < 0 for M > 1 GeV), they develop methods to split the singular functions into analytic and pole-containing parts.

## Key numbers

- Meson mass threshold for pole appearance: M = 1 GeV; below this value no singularities appear in propagator functions
- Maximum meson mass considered: M = 3.5 GeV
- Interaction kernel parameters: omega = 0.5 GeV and D = 16 GeV^{-2}
- Bare quark masses used: 5 MeV (u/d), 115 MeV (s), and 1000 MeV (c)
- Fit interval for propagator parametrization: -0.15 (GeV/c)^2 < k^2 < 10 (GeV/c)^2
- Three terms (12 parameters) sufficient for accurate approximation of each propagator function

## Context

This work extends the Maris-Tandy model (Maris and Tandy, 2003) for meson spectroscopy, which successfully describes light mesons but encounters difficulties for mesons with masses above 1 GeV containing at least one light quark. The authors build on their previous investigation of singularities in quark propagators (Dorkin, Kaptari, and Kampfer, 2014) to develop a consistent framework for solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the presence of pole-like singularities. The analysis is directly relevant to the future physics program at FAIR concerning open charm degrees of freedom and complements other approaches such as instanton-based models (Dorokhov) and separable interaction models.
