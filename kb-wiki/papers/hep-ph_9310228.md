# How to Make Large Domains of Disoriented Chiral Condensate

> Rajagopal and Wilczek have proposed that relativistic nuclear collisions can generate domains in which the chiral condensate is disoriented. If sufficiently large ({\it i.e.} nucleus sized), such domains can yield measurable fluctuations in the number of neutral and charged pions. However, by numerical simulation of the zero-temperature two-flavor linear sigma model, we find that domains are essentially {\it pion} sized. Nevertheless, we show that large domains can occur if the effective mesons masses are much lighter.

# How to Make Large Domains of Disoriented Chiral Condensate

## Key claim
While Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993) proposed that relativistic nuclear collisions can generate nucleus-sized domains of disoriented chiral condensate (DCC), numerical simulations of the zero-temperature two-flavor linear sigma model show that domains are essentially pion-sized under the standard quench scenario. Large domains can occur only if the effective meson masses are much lighter than the physical pion and sigma masses.

## Method
The authors performed numerical simulations of the classical equations of motion for the O(4) linear sigma model on a three-dimensional 10² × 40 lattice with spacing a = 1 fm, using a leap-frog algorithm. They initialized the fields as Gaussian random variables with ⟨Φ⟩ = 0, ⟨Φ²⟩ = v²/4, and ⟨Φ̇²⟩ = v²/1 fm², following the quench approximation of Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993). They compared a strongly coupled case (λ = 20, physical meson masses) to an extremely weakly coupled case (λ = 10⁻⁴, very light meson masses), measuring the pion correlation function and the distribution of the neutral pion fraction r₃ = nπ⁰/(nπ⁺ + nπ⁰ + nπ⁻) from 500 independent configurations.

## Key numbers
- For strong coupling (λ = 20): mπ = 135 MeV, mσ = 600 MeV, fπ = 92.5 MeV; the spinodal time τsp ∼ √2/mσ ≈ 0.5 fm; domains are estimated to be ∼ 1.4 fm in size, comparable to the pion Compton wavelength.
- For weak coupling (λ = 10⁻⁴): mπ = 0.3 MeV, mσ = 1.8 MeV, fπ = 92.5 MeV; domains grow large enough to produce a DCC-like distribution of r₃ at t = 150 fm.
- DCC-like behavior disappears for couplings exceeding λ ∼ 10⁻² on the 10² × 40 lattice; for a gold nucleus-sized system, domain-like behavior requires λ < 8/(vR)² ∼ 1.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993) by quantitatively estimating domain sizes and the neutral pion fraction distribution, using the analytical framework of Boyanovsky et al. (1993) from inflationary cosmology. The results contradict the expectation that the quench mechanism alone can produce large DCC domains with physical meson masses. The paper enables future work on DCC formation near a critical point in the QCD phase diagram, where a light field would naturally appear, as the authors indicate in forthcoming papers.
