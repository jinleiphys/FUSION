# Influence of quark masses and strangeness degrees of freedom on inhomogeneous chiral phases

> Several model calculations of the QCD phase structure at nonzero temperature and density suggest that in certain regions of the phase diagram inhomogeneous condensates are favored over homogeneous ones. In particular, in a two-flavor NJL model in the chiral limit the would-be first-order boundary associated with the chiral phase transition is entirely covered by an inhomogeneous phase, characterized by a spatially modulated chiral condensate, whose tip coincides exactly with the chiral critical point. In this contribution we discuss how this result is altered by the inclusion of nonzero quark masses and strange quarks in the model.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in a two-flavor NJL model away from the chiral limit, the tip of the inhomogeneous phase (the "pseudo-Lifshitz point") exactly coincides with the critical endpoint of the first-order chiral phase transition, but that this coincidence breaks down when strange quarks are included with a non-vanishing Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft (KMT) interaction.

## Method

The authors perform a Ginzburg-Landau (GL) expansion of the mean-field thermodynamic potential for the NJL model, expanding about a spatially constant but temperature- and chemical-potential-dependent mass for the two-flavor case, and about a two-flavor restored solution for the three-flavor case. They derive analytic expressions for the GL coefficients up to fourth order in the fluctuating fields and gradient terms, and use the conditions for the critical endpoint (CEP), Lifshitz point (LP), and pseudo-Lifshitz point (PLP) to relate their locations in the phase diagram.

## Key numbers

* In the two-flavor NJL model with nonzero quark masses, the PLP (where both amplitude and wave number of spatial modulations vanish) coincides exactly with the CEP, characterized by the conditions α₂ = α₃ = 0 for the CEP and α₂ = α_{4,b} = 0 for the PLP.
* In the three-flavor NJL model with massless up and down quarks (m_ℓ = 0) but massive strange quarks (m_s ≠ 0), the LP and TCP split when the KMT coupling K ≠ 0, because the quartic coefficient for the light condensate gains an additional term -γ₃²/(4β₂) while the gradient coefficient α_{4,b} does not.
* For K = 0 (no flavor mixing), the three-flavor α coefficients reduce to the two-flavor chiral-limit expressions, and the TCP and LP coincide as in the two-flavor chiral limit.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of inhomogeneous chiral phases in the NJL model, particularly the finding by Nickel (2009) that in the two-flavor chiral limit the Lifshitz point coincides with the tricritical point, and the numerical evidence by Nickel (2009) that the inhomogeneous region covers the entire first-order boundary for nonzero quark masses. The authors' GL analysis provides an analytic proof of the CEP-PLP coincidence for two flavors (Buballa and Carignano, 2018) and extends the analysis to three flavors, where the coupling through the KMT interaction (mimicking the axial anomaly) causes the LP and TCP to split. This result has implications for whether inhomogeneous phases could be studied at μ=0 on the lattice, which would require the LP to reach the temperature axis in the three-flavor chiral limit.
