# Inferring the initial condition for the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation

> We apply Bayesian inference to determine the posterior likelihood distribution for the parameters describing the initial condition of the small-$x$ Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution equation at leading logarithmic accuracy. The HERA structure function data is found to constrain most of the model parameters well. In particular, we find that the HERA data prefers an anomalous dimension $γ\approx 1$ unlike in previous fits where $γ>1$ which led to e.g. the unintegrated gluon distribution and the quark-target cross sections not being positive definite. The determined posterior distribution can be used to propagate the uncertainties in the non-perturbative initial condition when calculating any other observable in the Color Glass Condensate framework. We demonstrate this explicitly for the inclusive quark production cross section in proton-proton collisions and by calculating predictions for the nuclear modification factor for the $F_2$ structure function in the EIC and LHeC/FCC-he kinematics.

## Key claim

The HERA structure function data constrains the anomalous dimension of the Balitsky-Kovchegov initial condition to γ ≈ 0.95–1.05, in contrast to previous fits where γ > 1 led to unintegrated gluon distributions and quark-target cross sections that were not positive definite.

## Method

The authors performed Bayesian inference using Gaussian Process Emulators and Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling to determine the posterior likelihood distribution for five parameters describing the initial condition of the Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution equation at leading logarithmic accuracy with running coupling corrections. The dipole amplitude was fitted to HERA reduced cross section data in the region 2.0 GeV² < Q² < 50.0 GeV², using the full experimental covariance matrix including correlations among 162 systematic uncertainty sources. The posterior distribution was then used to propagate uncertainties to inclusive quark production in proton-proton collisions and to predictions for the nuclear modification factor for the F₂ structure function at the EIC and LHeC/FCC-he kinematics.

## Key numbers

- Anomalous dimension γ = 1.01_{-0.03}^{+0.04} (median with 95% credible interval) in the 5-parameter fit, compared to γ ≈ 1.1–1.2 in previous fits by Lappi (2013) and Albacete (2010, 2009)
- Initial saturation scale Q_{s,0}² = 0.067_{-0.015}^{+0.041} GeV² (5-parameter case median)
- Proton transverse area σ₀/2 = 14.0_{-1.2}^{+1.3} mb (5-parameter case median)
- Running coupling scale C² = 4.72_{-1.58}^{+2.52} (5-parameter case median)
- Saturation scale Q_s² at x_Bj = 0.01: 0.288 GeV² (5-parameter case median)
- χ²/d.o.f. = 1.016 for the 5-parameter fit to 403 HERA datapoints
- Average relative difference between Gaussian Process Emulator and actual model calculation: 0.047%
- Light quark mass fixed at m_q = 0.14 GeV
- Strong coupling frozen to α_s = 0.7 in the infrared, with Λ_QCD = 0.241 GeV
- BK evolution initiated at x₀ = 0.01

## Context

This work extends previous leading-order fits of the BK initial condition by Lappi (2013), Albacete (2010), and Albacete (2009) by providing, for the first time, a statistically rigorous uncertainty estimate using Bayesian inference. The determined posterior distribution enables propagation of initial condition uncertainties to any observable calculated in the Color Glass Condensate framework, which previous fits lacked. The authors demonstrate this by calculating inclusive quark production in proton-proton collisions and the nuclear modification factor for F₂ at the EIC and LHeC/FCC-he kinematics, where no predictions with consistent BK evolution from HERA data previously existed (except work by Armesto in 2022).
