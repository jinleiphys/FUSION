# The $ψ$(3770) resonance and its production in $\bar pp \to D \bar D$

> The production of a $D\bar D$ meson-pair in antiproton-proton ($\bar p p$) annihilation close to the production threshold is investigated, with special emphasis on the role played by the $ψ$(3770) resonance. The study is performed in a meson-baryon model where the elementary charm production process is described by baryon exchange. Effects of the interactions in the initial and final states are taken into account rigorously, where the latter involves also those due to the $ψ$(3770). The predictions for the $D\bar D$ production cross section are in the range of 30 -- 250 nb, the contribution from the $ψ$(3770) resonance itself amounts to roughly 20 -- 80 nb.

## Key claim

The ψ(3770) resonance produces a sizeable enhancement in the \(\bar p p \to D \bar D\) cross section around the resonance energy, with the contribution from the resonance itself estimated at roughly 20–80 nb, but this result depends on the specific choice of the initial-state \(\bar p p\) interaction and on the exact mass of the ψ(3770) (which has an uncertainty of about 6 MeV).

## Method

The reaction amplitude for \(\bar p p \to D \bar D\) was obtained within the distorted-wave Born approximation, using a meson-baryon model where the elementary charm production process is described by baryon exchange (Λ_c, Σ_c). Effects of initial-state (\(\bar p p\)) and final-state (\(D \bar D\)) interactions were included rigorously by solving Lippmann-Schwinger type scattering equations, with the ψ(3770) resonance incorporated as a bare resonance in the direct \(D \bar D\) potential whose bare mass and coupling were adjusted to reproduce the physical pole at \(E = (3773 - i\,13.6)\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- Total \(D \bar D\) production cross section in the near-threshold region: 30–250 nb.
- Contribution from the ψ(3770) resonance itself: roughly 20–80 nb.
- The ψ(3770) resonance mass used in the main calculation: 3773 MeV (with an alternative value of 3779 MeV also considered, which reduces the resonance contribution by about 15–20%).
- For the \(D^+ D^-\) channel, the cross section at the ψ(3770) peak estimated from experimental data using a Breit-Wigner formula yields either 3–18 nb or around 350 nb, depending on the BESIII analysis of the \(e^+ e^- \to \bar p p\) cross section.
- A QCD-based perturbative estimate gives \(\sigma_{\bar p p \to D^+ D^-} \approx 0.2\) nb due to the ψ(3770).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation (Haidenbauer and Krein, 2014) of \(\bar p p \to D \bar D\) that did not include the ψ(3770) resonance, which is a well-established p-wave resonance seen in \(e^+ e^- \to D \bar D\) (Ablikim, 2008; Anashin, 2007). The study complements the Jülich meson-baryon model for \(\bar p p \to K \bar K\) (Mull, 1991) and follows the same SU(4) symmetry strategy used by the authors for \(D N\) and \(\bar D N\) interactions (Haidenbauer, 2007; Haidenbauer, 2008) and for \(\bar p p \to \bar \Lambda_c^- \Lambda_c^+\) (Haidenbauer and Krein, 2010). The results suggest that the ψ(3770) signal should be measurable in dedicated experiments at the FAIR facility with the PANDA detector.
