# Odd-even mass differences from self-consistent mean-field theory

> We survey odd-even nuclear binding energy staggering using density functional theory with several treatments of the pairing interaction including the BCS, Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov, and the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov with the Lipkin-Nogami approximation. We calculate the second difference of binding energies and compare with 443 measured neutron energy differences in isotope chains and 418 measured proton energy differences in isotone chains. The particle-hole part of the energy functional is taken as the SLy4 Skyrme parametrization and the pairing part of the functional is based on a contact interaction with possible density dependence. An important feature of the data, reproduced by the theory, is the sharp gap quenching at magic numbers. With the strength of the interaction as a free parameter, the theory can reproduce the data to an rms accuracy of about 0.25 MeV. This is slightly better than a single-parameter phenomenological description but slightly poorer than the usual two-parameter phenomenological form C/A^alpha . The following conclusions can be made about the performance of common parametrization of the pairing interaction: (i) there is a weak preference for a surface-peaked neutron-neutron pairing, which might be attributable to many-body effects; (ii) a larger strength is required in the proton pairing channel than in the neutron pairing channel; (iii) pairing strengths adjusted to the well-known spherical isotope chains are too weak to give a good overall fit to the mass differences.

## Key claim

The paper claims that self-consistent mean-field theory with a density-dependent pairing interaction can reproduce experimental odd-even staggering (OES) of nuclear binding energies to an rms accuracy of about 0.25 MeV, which is slightly better than a single-parameter phenomenological description but slightly poorer than the usual two-parameter phenomenological form C/A^alpha. The authors note that their conclusions must be tentative because they do not consider all mechanisms that generate OES, such as time-odd fields and neutron-proton pairing.

## Method

The authors performed global calculations of odd-even mass differences using density functional theory with the SLy4 Skyrme parametrization for the particle-hole channel and a zero-range density-dependent contact interaction for the pairing channel. They employed three treatments of pairing: BCS (using the ev8 code on a 3D Cartesian mesh), Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB, using the hfbtho code with an axial 2D basis), and HFB with the Lipkin-Nogami approximation (HFB+LN). The odd-A nuclei were treated using the filling approximation, blocking one orbital near the Fermi level, and the pairing strength was adjusted to minimize the average residual in the Delta^(3)_o data sets for neutrons and protons independently.

## Key numbers

- The experimental data set contains 443 measured neutron energy differences in isotope chains and 418 measured proton energy differences in isotone chains, drawn from the 2003 atomic mass evaluation with experimental errors less than 200 keV
- The theory reproduces the data to an rms accuracy of about 0.25 MeV with the pairing strength as a free parameter
- The experimental averages and variances for neutron and proton OES are 1.04 +/- 0.31 MeV and 0.96 +/- 0.27 MeV, respectively
- The largest experimental neutron OES is 1.87 MeV at (N,Z) = (21,16); the smallest is 0.32 MeV at (N,Z) = (125,82)
- The largest experimental proton OES is 2.07 MeV at (N,Z) = (12,9); the smallest is 0.31 MeV at (N,Z) = (126,81)
- For HFB calculations, increasing the pairing strength by 20% from the value fitted to 120Sn causes the average neutron pairing gap to increase by a factor of 2.3
- The phenomenological parametrization Delta = c/A^alpha with c = 4.66 MeV (neutrons) or 4.31 MeV (protons) and alpha = 0.31 gives an rms residual of 0.25 MeV on both data sets
- Effective pairing strengths for BCS with mixed pairing: V0_nn = 700.0 MeV-fm^3, V0_pp = 755.0 MeV-fm^3
- Effective pairing strengths for HFB with mixed pairing: V0_nn = 318.1 MeV-fm^3, V0_pp = 352.0 MeV-fm^3
- Effective pairing strengths for HFB+LN with mixed pairing: V0_nn = 300.5 MeV-fm^3, V0_pp = 332.6 MeV-fm^3

## Context

This work extends previous density functional theory surveys of nuclear masses (Ben03w, Sto06, Ber07) by focusing specifically on odd-even staggering and testing multiple pairing treatments (BCS, HFB, HFB+LN) against a large global data set. The authors note that many previous studies examined only individual isotope chains (e.g., Dob01c, yu03 for the Z=50 chain) or inferred OES from average HFB gap parameters rather than calculated binding energy differences (Hil02). The results provide a benchmark for assessing future improvements in pairing theory and show that pairing strengths adjusted to spherical isotope chains like 120Sn are too weak to give a good overall fit to the mass differences, highlighting the need for global surveys rather than chain-specific fits.
