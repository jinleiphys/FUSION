# On the maximum mass of hyperonic neutron stars

> Chiral Lagrangian and quark-meson coupling models of hyperon matter are used to estimate the maximum mass of neutron stars. Our relativistic calculations include, for the first time, both Hartree and Fock contributions in a consistent manner. Being related to the underlying quark structure of baryons, these models are considered to be good candidates for describing the dense core of neutron stars. Taking account of the known experimental constraints at saturation density, the equations of state deduced from these relativistic approaches cannot sustain a neutron star with a mass larger than 1.6-1.66 $M_\odot$.

## Key claim
The maximum mass of neutron stars containing hyperons, calculated using chiral Lagrangian and quark-meson coupling models that consistently include both Hartree and Fock contributions, cannot exceed 1.6–1.66 solar masses when constrained by known experimental saturation properties; this range is conditional on the models reproducing empirical nuclear matter saturation properties and hyperon interaction potentials.

## Method
The authors performed relativistic mean-field calculations in the Hartree–Fock approximation for two classes of models: a chiral Lagrangian model and the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. They included scalar (s), omega, delta, rho, and pi mesons coupled to nucleons and hyperons (Lambda, Sigma, Xi), with hyperon coupling constants determined from SU(6) symmetry and adjusted to reproduce empirical non-relativistic hyperon potentials at saturation density. The Fock contributions were treated consistently in the relativistic framework for the first time, and the equations of state were used to solve the Tolman–Oppenheimer–Volkoff equations for neutron star structure.

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass for chiral Hartree–Fock models with hyperons: 1.55–1.60 solar masses (MC1-HF/NY: 1.58, MC2-HF/NY: 1.60, MC3-HF/NY: 1.55, MC4-HF/NY: 1.55)
- Maximum mass for QMC models with hyperons: 1.66–2.00 solar masses (QMC700/NY: 2.00, QMC-HF2/NY: 1.97, QMC-HF3/NY: 1.66)
- Observed mass of PSR J1614-2230: 1.97 ± 0.04 solar masses
- Incompressibility values: chiral HF models K0 ≈ 274–276 MeV; QMC700 K0 = 338 MeV; QMC-HF3 K0 = 285 MeV
- Hyperon non-relativistic potentials at saturation: Lambda = -30 MeV, Sigma = +30 MeV, Xi = -18 MeV
- Effect of rotation (3.15 ms period) increases maximum mass by no more than 0.02 solar masses

## Context
This work extends previous relativistic mean-field studies of hyperonic neutron stars (Schafner-Bielich 2002, Wu 2011) and Brueckner–Hartree–Fock calculations (Baldo 2000, Schulze 2006, Schulze 2011) by including, for the first time, consistent relativistic Hartree and Fock contributions. The results contradict the original QMC700 model (RGM08) which predicted a maximum mass of 2.00 solar masses, showing that this was due to an overestimated incompressibility. The findings suggest that hyperonic matter cannot explain the observed 1.97 solar mass pulsar PSR J1614-2230, reinforcing the need for alternative models such as deconfined quark matter.
