# Identifying exotic hidden-charm pentaquarks

> The LHCb Collaboration at the Large Hadron Collider at CERN discovered two pentaquark states $P_c(4380)$ and $P_c(4450)$. These two hidden-charm states are interpreted as the loosely bound $Σ_c(2455)D^*$ and $Σ_c^*(2520)D^*$ molecular states in the boson exchange interaction model, which provides an explanation for why the experimental width of $P_c(4450)$ is much narrower than that of $P_c(4380)$. The discovery of the new resonances $P_c(4380)$ and $P_c(4450)$, indeed, opens a new page for hadron physics. The partners of $P_c(4380)$ and $P_c(4450)$ should be pursued in future experiments.

## Key claim

The LHCb pentaquarks \(P_c(4380)\) and \(P_c(4450)\) can be identified as loosely bound \(\Sigma_c(2455)\bar{D}^*\) and \(\Sigma_c^*(2520)\bar{D}^*\) molecular states, respectively, under the one-pion-exchange model, provided that the cutoff parameter \(\Lambda\) is tuned to reproduce their masses; the model also predicts that both states have negative parity, which may conflict with the LHCb preference for opposite parities.

## Method

The authors constructed effective potentials for the \(\Sigma_c(2455)\bar{D}^*\) and \(\Sigma_c^*(2520)\bar{D}^*\) systems using one-pion-exchange (OPE) interactions derived from effective Lagrangians respecting heavy quark symmetry and chiral symmetry. They introduced a monopole form factor with a cutoff \(\Lambda\) to regularize the potential, then solved the Schrodinger equation to find bound-state solutions. The cutoff was adjusted separately for each system to reproduce the experimental masses of \(P_c(4380)\) and \(P_c(4450)\).

## Key numbers

- \(P_c(4380)\) mass: \(4380 \pm 8 \pm 29\ \mathrm{MeV}\), width: \(205 \pm 18 \pm 86\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- \(P_c(4450)\) mass: \(4449.8 \pm 1.7 \pm 2.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\), width: \(39 \pm 5 \pm 19\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Cutoff \(\Lambda = 2.35\ \mathrm{GeV}\) used for the \(\Sigma_c\bar{D}^*\) system to reproduce \(P_c(4380)\)
- Cutoff \(\Lambda = 1.77\ \mathrm{GeV}\) used for the \(\Sigma_c^*\bar{D}^*\) system to reproduce \(P_c(4450)\)
- Predicted binding energy of \(-80\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for the \(\Sigma_c\bar{D}^*\) system with \((I=3/2, J=1/2)\) (isospin partner of \(P_c(4380)\))
- Predicted binding energy of \(-28\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for the \(\Sigma_c^*\bar{D}^*\) system with \((I=3/2, J=1/2)\) (isospin partner of \(P_c(4450)\))
- Pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 132\ \mathrm{MeV}\) used in the Lagrangians
- Coupling constants: \(g = 0.59 \pm 0.07 \pm 0.01\) (from \(D^*\) width), \(g_1 = 0.94\) (fixed from prior work)

## Context

This work extends the one-boson-exchange model previously applied to hidden-charm molecular baryons by Yang et al. (2011) to the specific pentaquark candidates reported by the LHCb Collaboration. It provides a dynamical calculation supporting the molecular interpretation of \(P_c(4380)\) and \(P_c(4450)\), which were first observed in \(\Lambda_b \to J/\psi p K\) decays. The study enables experimental searches for isospin partners via \(\Delta(1232)J/\psi\) final states and predicts analogous hidden-bottom and \(B_c\)-like pentaquark systems for future LHCb investigations.
