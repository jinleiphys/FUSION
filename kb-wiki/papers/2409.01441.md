# Parton distribution functions and transverse momentum dependence of heavy mesons

> The leading Fock state light-front wave functions of heavy quarkonia and $D$ and $B$ mesons are obtained from the projections of their Bethe-Salpeter wave functions on the light front. We compute therefrom their leading-twist time-reversal even transverse momentum distributions and parton distribution functions. Mirroring the behavior of parton distribution amplitudes, the support of both distributions is increasingly narrower and shifted towards larger $x$ as a function of the meson mass. The dependence of $x$ and $\k_\perp^2$ of the transverse distributions does not factorize into separate functions, and their fall-off with $\k_\perp^2$ is much slower than that of light mesons.

# Parton distribution functions and transverse momentum dependence of heavy mesons

## Key claim

The leading Fock-state light-front wave functions (LFWFs) of heavy pseudoscalar mesons (D, D_s, B, B_s, B_c, eta_c, eta_b) are obtained from Bethe-Salpeter wave functions computed within a Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter framework that preserves the axialvector Ward identity, and their transverse momentum distributions (TMDs) and parton distribution functions (PDFs) are derived; the x and k_perp^2 dependence of the TMDs does not factorize, and the fall-off with k_perp^2 is much slower than for light mesons, with the effect becoming more pronounced as meson mass increases.

## Method

The authors compute k_perp^2-dependent moments of the LFWF from Euclidean-space Bethe-Salpeter wave functions using a complex conjugate pole parametrization for dressed quark propagators and a Nakanishi-type integral representation for the Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, both fitted to numerical DSE-BSE solutions in rainbow-ladder truncation. The LFWF is reconstructed by minimizing the sum of relative differences between calculated moments and those from an algebraic parametrization (Eq. LFWFs-par) with up to m_max=5 moments, using separate functional forms for light mesons (phi_L = x^alpha xbar^beta) and heavy mesons (phi_H = 4 x xbar exp[-4 alpha x xbar - beta(x - xbar)]). The TMD is obtained from the LFWFs via Eq. TMD-def and the PDF by integrating over transverse momentum up to Lambda_perp^2 = 8 GeV^2.

## Key numbers

- Model scale determined to be Q_0 = 0.53 GeV by matching the pion valence quark momentum fraction 2⟨x⟩_v = 0.47(2) at Q^2 = 4 GeV^2 from pi N Drell-Yan analysis
- LFWF reconstruction uses m_max = 5 moments and n = 4 in the algebraic parametrization for all mesons
- TMDs exhibit an inflection point from concave to convex function at k_perp ≈ 0.3 GeV for all mesons studied
- PDFs are integrated up to Lambda_perp^2 = 8 GeV^2, with results unchanged for Lambda_perp^2 ≥ 8 GeV^2
- PDFs are fitted with Gegenbauer expansion up to j_m = 17 (Eq. PDFfit)
- Pion valence PDF evolved from Q_0 = 0.53 GeV to Q = 4 GeV using NLO DGLAP evolution via QCDNUM and compared with LO analysis from Conway (1989)

## Context

This work extends earlier DSE-BSE calculations of TMDs for the pion and kaon (Shi, 2018; Shi, 2023) and for heavy quarkonia (Kou, 2023) to the first TMD and PDF calculations of D, D_s, B, B_s, and B_c mesons using a functional approach. The LFWFs of D and B mesons were previously extracted from Mellin moments using an algebraic ansatz for the Bethe-Salpeter wave function (Almeida-Zamora, 2023); the present work uses a full DSE-BSE calculation that preserves the axialvector Ward identity. The results enable future studies of transverse-momentum dependence in heavy meson production amplitudes and provide input for phenomenological analyses of heavy meson structure.
