# Nuclear reaction studies of unstable nuclei using relativistic mean field formalisms in conjunction with Glauber model

> We study nuclear reaction cross-sections for stable and unstable projectiles and targets within Glauber model, using densities obtained from various relativistic mean field formalisms. The calculated cross-sections are compared with the experimental data in some specific cases. We also evaluate the differential scattering cross-sections at several incident energies, and observe that the results found from various densities are similar at smaller scattering angles, whereas a systematic deviation is noticed at large angles. In general, these results agree fairly well with the experimental data.

## Key claim
The authors show that nuclear reaction cross-sections calculated with the Glauber model using densities from the extended relativistic mean field (E-RMF) formalism with the G2 parameter set generally agree well with experimental data, but the agreement is better at higher incident energies and the E-RMF densities reproduce differential cross-sections more accurately than the standard RMF (SIG-OM) densities, particularly at large scattering angles.

## Method
The authors calculated ground-state densities for stable and unstable nuclei (Li, Be, B, C isotopes) using both the standard relativistic mean field (RMF) formalism with the SIG-OM parameter set and the extended RMF (E-RMF) formalism with the G2 parameter set. These densities were fitted to sums of Gaussian functions and used as input to the Glauber model to compute total reaction cross-sections and elastic differential scattering cross-sections for various projectile-target combinations at incident energies from 30 to 2200 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers
- For the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C system, the E-RMF calculated total reaction cross-sections agreed excellently with experimental data across almost all incident energies, with the best agreement at higher energies.
- At 800 MeV/nucleon, total reaction cross-sections were calculated for Li isotopes ($^{6-11}$Li), Be isotopes ($^{7-14}$Be), and B isotopes ($^{8-17}$B) on a $^{12}$C target, and compared with experimental data from Ozawa (2001), Tanihata (1996), and Tanihata (1985).
- For the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C system, differential cross-sections were calculated at 30 MeV/nucleon and 85 MeV/nucleon incident energies, with E-RMF densities reproducing experimental data better than RMF densities at 85 MeV/nucleon.
- The averaged nucleon-nucleon cross-sections $\sigma_{NN}$ used in the profile function range from 19.6 mb at 30 MeV/nucleon to 4.335 mb at 2200 MeV/nucleon.

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Shukla (2005) that calculated reaction cross-sections with densities from various interactions, and applies the RMF and E-RMF formalisms (Patra 1991, Furnstahl 1996, Serot 1997) to the Glauber model (Glauber 1959) for both stable and unstable nuclei. The results enable predictions for future radioactive ion beam experiments with unstable targets, such as $^{11}$Li+$^{11}$Li, $^{11}$Be+$^{11}$Be, $^{14}$Be+$^{14}$Be, and $^{17}$B+$^{17}$B systems, which are not yet experimentally feasible but are being planned at facilities like RIKEN and the Institute of Modern Physics, CAS.
