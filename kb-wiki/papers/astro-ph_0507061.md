# Medium Effects in Cooling of Neutron Stars and $3P_2$ Neutron Gap

> We study the dependence of the cooling of isolated neutron stars on the magnitude of the $3P_2$ neutron gap. It is demonstrated that our ``nuclear medium cooling'' scenario is in favor of a suppressed value of the $3P_2$ neutron gap.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the "nuclear medium cooling" scenario, which incorporates in-medium effects on the pion propagator and vertices, favors a suppressed value of the $3P_2$ neutron pairing gap, contrary to the enhanced gap suggested by Khodel et al. (2004) based on strong pion softening.

## Method

The authors incorporate in-medium effects into the cooling code by using a Landau-Migdal Fermi liquid approach to compute the in-medium NN interaction amplitude, including explicit treatment of nucleon particle-hole loops, Delta isobar-nucleon hole loops, and the in-medium pion propagator. They then calculate the neutrino emissivities of medium-modified Urca (MMU), medium nucleon bremsstrahlung (MNB), and medium pair breaking and formation (MPBF) processes, and compare cooling curves for different values of the $3P_2$ neutron gap against observational data.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: $n_0 \simeq 0.5 m_{\pi}^3$, where $m_{\pi}=140$ MeV
- Baryon density range considered: $7 n_0 \gtrsim n \gtrsim 0.5 n_0$
- Dimensional normalization factor: $C_0 = \pi^2 /[m_N p_{FN} (n_0 )] \simeq 300$ MeV$\cdot$ fm$^3$ $\simeq 0.77 m_{\pi}^{-2}$
- Landau-Migdal parameter values from Migdal (1967): $f\simeq 0.25$, $f^{\prime} \simeq 1$, $g\simeq 0.5$, $g^{\prime} \simeq 1$
- Landau-Migdal parameter values from Speth and Tzeng (1998), Fayans and Zawischa (1995), and Borzov et al. (1984): $f\simeq 0$, $f^{\prime} \simeq 0.5 \div 0.6$, $g\simeq 0.05\pm 0.1$, $g^{\prime} \simeq 1.1 \pm 0.1$

## Context

This work extends the "nuclear medium cooling" scenario developed by Voskresensky and Senatorov (1984, 1986, 1987) and applied in cooling codes by Schaab et al. (1997) and Blaschke et al. (2004). It directly addresses the contradiction between the suppressed $3P_2$ neutron gaps calculated by Schwenk and Friman (2003) and the enhanced gaps predicted by Khodel et al. (2004). The paper enables discrimination between competing gap calculations through comparison with observed neutron star cooling data.
