# The Gamow-Teller response within Skyrme random-phase approximation plus particle-vibration coupling

> Although many random-phase approximation (RPA) calculations of the Gamow-Teller (GT) response exist, this is not the case for calculations going beyond the mean-field approximation. We apply a consistent model that includes the coupling of the GT resonance to low-lying vibrations, to nuclei of the $fp$ shell. Among other motivations, our goal is to see if the particle-vibration coupling can redistribute the low-lying GT$^+$ strength that is relevant for electron-capture processes in core-collapse supernova. We conclude that the lowering and fragmentation of that strength are consistent with the experimental findings and validate our model. However, the particle-vibration coupling cannot account for the quenching of the total value of the low-lying strength.

# The Gamow-Teller response within Skyrme random-phase approximation plus particle-vibration coupling

## Key claim
The inclusion of particle-vibration coupling (PVC) within a Skyrme random-phase approximation (RPA) framework lowers and fragments the low-lying GT$^+$ strength in $fp$-shell nuclei, making the results consistent with experimental findings; however, PVC alone cannot account for the total quenching of the low-lying strength, meaning the missing strength must arise from other mechanisms such as coupling to $\Delta$ isobars or higher-order configurations.

## Method
The authors performed Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations using Skyrme interactions, then solved the RPA equations in a discrete particle-hole (p-h) configuration space with a 100 MeV cutoff and a 21 fm box. They extended the model beyond RPA by constructing doorway states consisting of p-h excitations coupled to collective phonons ($2^+$, $3^-$, $4^+$), using the same Skyrme interaction consistently for all vertices. The effective Hamiltonian was diagonalized in the RPA basis to obtain complex eigenvalues whose imaginary parts give the spreading width, and the GT strength function was extracted from the response function.

## Key numbers
- For $^{60}$Ni with SGII, the Ikeda sum rule $m_0(\text{GT}^-) - m_0(\text{GT}^+)$ reaches 11.998 at the RPA level with the 100 MeV cutoff.
- At the RPA-plus-PVC level, the Ikeda sum rule integrated up to 45 MeV gives 11.87 (SGII), 11.89 (SLy5), and 11.83 (SkM*) for $^{60}$Ni.
- For $^{56}$Ni, the sum rule value integrated up to 45 MeV is -0.10 (SGII), -0.07 (SLy5), and -0.11 (SkM*).
- For $^{208}$Pb with SGII, the sum rule integrated up to 70 MeV is 129.29.
- The $2_1^+$ phonon energy in $^{60}$Ni is calculated as 1.820 MeV (SGII), 1.157 MeV (SLy5), and 2.212 MeV (SkM*), compared to the experimental value of 1.333 MeV.
- The $B(E2; 0 \rightarrow 2)$ for the $2_1^+$ state in $^{60}$Ni is $3.611 \times 10^2$ e$^2$fm$^4$ (SGII), $3.562 \times 10^2$ e$^2$fm$^4$ (SLy5), and $2.457 \times 10^2$ e$^2$fm$^4$ (SkM*), compared to the experimental value of $8.780 \times 10^2$ e$^2$fm$^4$.

## Context
This work extends earlier RPA calculations of the GT response (Fracasso 2007) by implementing a consistent PVC scheme that includes the full central Skyrme interaction at the vertex, going beyond the approximation used in Colo (1994) which retained only the $V_0$ part of the $t_0$ and $t_3$ terms. The study is motivated by the need for accurate electron-capture rates in core-collapse supernova simulations, where previous HF-RPA calculations (Paar 2009) predicted too-high threshold energies for GT$^+$ transitions compared to shell-model results (Dean 1998, Langanke 2001). The present PVC approach enables a more realistic description of the low-lying GT$^+$ strength fragmentation, but the remaining quenching problem points to the need for including $\Delta$-isobar coupling or more complex configurations in future work.
