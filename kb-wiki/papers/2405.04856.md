# Effect of dark matter interaction on hybrid star in the light of the recent astrophysical observations

> We have explored the effect of dark matter interaction on hybrid star (HS) in the light of recent astrophysical observational constraints. The presence of dark matter is assumed to be there in both the hadron as well as the quark sector. The dark matter particle interacts with both hadron and quark matter through the exchange of a scalar as well as a vector meson. The equation of state (EOS) of the hadron part is computed using the NL3 version of the relativistic mean field(RMF) model, whereas the quark part is taken care of using the well-known MIT Bag model with the vector interaction. We investigate the effect of the dark matter density and the mass of the dark matter particle on various observables like mass, radius, tidal deformability of the dark matter admixed hybrid star(DMAHS). In this study, we have noted an intriguing aspect that is the speed of sound in the DMAHS is insensitive to both the mass as well as the density of dark matter. We also observe a striking similarity in the variation of transition mass and its corresponding radius, as well as the maximum mass of neutron stars, with dark matter density and mass. We employ observational constraints from neutron stars to narrow down the allowed range of the parameters of dark matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the speed of sound in dark matter admixed hybrid stars is insensitive to both the mass and the density of dark matter, and that the transition mass, transition radius, and maximum mass all follow a strikingly similar pattern of variation with dark matter parameters, but these conclusions are conditional on the specific NL3 parametrization for hadronic matter and the MIT Bag model with vector interaction for quark matter, using Maxwell construction for the phase transition.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid star equations of state using the relativistic mean field (RMF) model with NL3 parametrization for the hadronic phase and the MIT Bag model with vector interaction (bag constant B^{1/4}=160 MeV, vector coupling G_V=0.35 fm^{-2}) for the quark phase, connected via Maxwell construction with local charge neutrality. Fermionic dark matter is uniformly distributed and interacts with both hadron and quark matter through exchange of scalar (phi) and vector (xi) mesons, with dark matter Fermi momenta ranging from 0.01 to 0.06 GeV and dark matter particle masses from 0.1 to 100 GeV, using a single-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation.

## Context

This work extends the study of dark matter admixed compact stars from purely hadronic configurations (Kain 2021, Giangrandi 2022) to hybrid stars with a quark core, building on the approach of Lenzi (2022). The authors use the same scalar-vector interaction model for dark matter as Sen (2021) and Guha (2021), but apply it to both hadronic and quark phases simultaneously. The results enable constraints on dark matter particle mass and Fermi momentum from multi-messenger astrophysical observations including NICER radius measurements of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, the gravitational wave event GW170817, and the light compact object HESS J1731-347.
