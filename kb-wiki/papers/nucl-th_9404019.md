# Microscopic mass formulae

> By assuming the existence of a pseudopotential smooth enough to do Hartree-Fock variations and good enough to describe nuclear structure, we construct mass formulae that rely on general scaling arguments and on a schematic reading of shell model calculations. Fits to 1751 known binding energies for N,Z$\geq 8$ lead to RMS errors of 614 keV with 14 parameters and 388 keV with 28 parameters. The latter is easily reduced to a 20 parameter form at 423 keV.

# Microscopic mass formulae

## Key claim

By assuming a nucleon-nucleon pseudopotential smooth enough for Hartree-Fock calculations and good enough to describe nuclear structure, the authors construct mass formulae that replace the traditional local-versus-global dichotomy with a monopole-multipole separation of the Hamiltonian. The RMS errors quoted in the abstract (614 keV with 14 parameters, 388 keV with 28 parameters) are achieved only for nuclei with N,Z ≥ 8 and rely on iterative fitting that initializes and resets a deformation flag δ_d until convergence.

## Method

The authors separate the nuclear Hamiltonian into a monopole part H_m (responsible for saturation and shell formation) and a multipole part H_M (responsible for correlations, pairing, and deformation). They construct mass formulae from symmetric quadratic combinations of isoscalar and isovector operators built from number and spin-orbit variables, scaled by A-dependent factors derived from the oscillator frequency ω. Fits to 1751 known binding energies are performed by iteratively setting a deformation flag δ_d (0 for spherical, 1 for deformed) and minimizing the difference between calculated and experimental energies.

## Key numbers

- RMS error of 614 keV for a 14-parameter fit to 1751 binding energies (N,Z ≥ 8)
- RMS error of 388 keV for a 28-parameter fit to the same data set
- RMS error of 423 keV for a 20-parameter fit (reduced from the 28-parameter form)
- For the 28-parameter fit, RMS error for deformed nuclei (δ_d=1) is 302 keV, smaller than the RMS error for spherical nuclei
- Number of deformed cases (δ_d=1) is 423 for the 14-parameter fit, 367 for the 28-parameter fit, and 329 for the 20-parameter fit
- The Coulomb radius extracted from fits gives r ≈ 1.235 R_c, close to a previously fitted value of r ≈ 1.225 R_c (Duflo, 1994)
- The spin-orbit scaling parameter ξ is set to 0.42 in R_c = [A(1 − ξ(2T/A)²)]^{1/3}
- The intruder promotion number JU is set to 4 and found to be optimal

## Context

This work extends the monopole-multipole separation approach previously developed by Zuker (1994) and Dufour and Zuker (1993), and builds on shell model spectroscopy results from Abzouzi, Caurier, and Zuker (1991) that achieved RMS errors below 300 keV in the p and sd shells. The authors aim to improve upon existing mass models: the droplet model (FRDM) of Möller, Nix, Myers, and Swiatecki (1993) and the ETFSI Thomas-Fermi calculations of Pearson, Aboussir, Dutta, Nayak, and Farine (1991), which have RMS errors around 700 keV and are insufficiently precise for terrestrial applications, while also surpassing local formulae such as those of Masson and Jänecke (1988) that require hundreds of parameters and cannot safely reach the drip lines needed for r-process nucleosynthesis calculations.
