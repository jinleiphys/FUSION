# The Fubini-Furlan-Rosetti sum rule and related aspects in light of covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We analyze the Fubini-Furlan-Rosetti sum rule in the framework of covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory to leading one-loop accuracy and including next-to-leading order polynomial contributions. We discuss the relation between the subtraction constants in the invariant amplitudes and certain low-energy constants employed in earlier chiral perturbation theory studies of threshold neutral pion photoproduction off nucleons. In particular, we consider the corrections to the sum rule due to the finite pion mass and show that below the threshold they agree well with determinations based on fixed-t dispersion relations. We also discuss the energy dependence of the electric dipole amplitude E_{0+}.

## Key claim

The Fubini-Furlan-Rosetti sum rule, which is exact in the chiral limit of QCD, receives finite pion mass corrections that can be systematically calculated in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory; the authors show that within this framework the sum rule for the proton is fulfilled within 8%, consistent with the expected size of chiral corrections proportional to the small parameter μ = Mπ/mN ≃ 1/7.

## Method

The authors analyze the FFR sum rule using infrared regularization of covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory, calculating the invariant amplitudes A_i for neutral pion photoproduction off nucleons to leading one-loop accuracy (third order in the chiral expansion) and including next-to-leading order polynomial contributions (fourth order). They compute the discrepancy function Δ_N(ν, t_thr) and the electric dipole amplitude E_0+ for the proton, fitting the subtraction constants to simultaneously describe the discrepancy function, the threshold region E_0+ data, and the P-wave slopes at threshold.

## Key numbers

- Proton anomalous magnetic moment in the chiral limit: κ̊_p = 2.37 (in nuclear magnetons)
- Neutron anomalous magnetic moment in the chiral limit: κ̊_n = -2.84 (in nuclear magnetons)
- FFR sum rule result for proton: κ̊_p + Δ_p(ν = ν_thr, t = t_thr) = 2.18, fulfilling the sum rule within 8%
- Threshold multipoles (in units of 10^{-3}/M_{π+} for E_0+ and 10^{-3}/M_{π+}^2 for P-waves): E_0+ = -1.19, P̅_1 = 9.67, P̅_2 = -9.6, P̅_3 = 11.45
- D-wave slope: D̅ = 0.66 × 10^{-3}/M_{π+}^3
- Coefficients in pion mass expansion of Δ_p at threshold: α_p = 2.19 GeV^{-1}, β_p = -22.3 GeV^{-2}
- Best-fit subtraction constants: A_1^ct = (191.3 ν_B + 220 ν^2) GeV^{-2}, A_2^ct = -54.9 GeV^{-4}, A_3^ct = -155 ν GeV^{-3}, A_4^ct = 181.5 GeV^{-3}

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the FFR sum rule by Pasquini, Drechsel, and Tiator (2004), who considered pion mass corrections using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory and found an incorrect curvature of the discrepancy function at low energies due to shifted pole positions in the 1/m_N expansion. The covariant formulation avoids these artifacts and provides a correct description of the energy dependence below threshold. The results enable systematic determination of low-energy constants from dispersive representations in the unphysical region, with future work planned to include complete fourth-order loop corrections and fits to MAMI data.
