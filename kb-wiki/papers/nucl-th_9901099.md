# Multifragmentation of non-spherical nuclei

> The shape influence of decaying thermalized source on various characteristics of multifragmentation as well as its interplay with effects of angular momentum and collective expansion are first studied and the most pertinent variables are proposed. The analysis is based on the extension of the statistical microcanonical multifragmentation model.

## Key claim

The shape of a thermalized nuclear source at freeze-out influences multifragmentation observables, but this effect is only clearly visible in angular distributions and global event-shape variables, not in fragment-size distributions, and it is strongly modulated by the source's angular momentum and collective expansion.

## Method

The authors extended the microcanonical Metropolis Monte Carlo (MMMC) multifragmentation model of the Berlin group (Gross 1990) to treat the decaying source as an axial ellipsoid with conserved volume, parameterized by the axis ratio R = R_x/R_z. They simulated the decay of ¹⁹⁷Au at excitation energy 6 A·MeV and angular momentum L = 40 ħ, with freeze-out density ρ ≈ ρ₀/6, for prolate (R=0.6) and oblate (R=1.667) shapes, and added collective radial expansion via a blast velocity profile v_b(r) = v₀ (r/R₀)^α.

## Key numbers

- Source: ¹⁹⁷Au at excitation energy 6 A·MeV and angular momentum L = 40 ħ, corresponding to central Xe+Sn collisions at 50 A·MeV.
- Freeze-out density: ρ ≈ ρ₀/6, giving spherical source radius R_sys = 12.8 fm.
- Ellipsoid axis ratios studied: R = 0.6 (prolate) and R = 1.667 (oblate).
- Blast velocity used: v_b = 0.08 c with exponent α = 2 for the main calculations.
- Experimental mean kinetic energy per nucleon of IMF's: 5.2 ± 0.1 MeV/nucleon, reproduced with v_b = 0.088 c (prolate) or v_b = 0.063 c (oblate).
- For the 'frozen' configuration (ψ = 0), the prolate shape produces strong forward-backward peaks in the Z_max angular distribution, while the oblate shape produces sideward emission at θ_cm = π/2.
- Collective expansion (α = 2) enhances the deformation effect in the Θ_flow distribution.

## Context

This work extends the statistical multifragmentation models of Gross (1990, 1997) and Bondorf (1996) by relaxing the assumption of a spherical freeze-out source, motivated by shape instabilities from angular momentum (Swiatecki, 1982; Faber and Ploszajczak, 1991) and dynamical compression/rarefaction zones. It enables experimental extraction of source deformation and time-scale information (ψ-averaging limits) from angular distributions of the largest fragment, flow angle distributions, and the Z-dependence of average kinetic energy, using data from the INDRA collaboration for Xe+Sn collisions at 50 A·MeV.
