# Forward quark jet-nucleus scattering in a light-front Hamiltonian approach

> We investigate the scattering of a quark jet on a high-energy heavy nucleus using the time-dependent light-front Hamiltonian approach. We simulate a real-time evolution of the quark in a strong classical color field of the relativistic nucleus, described as the Color Glass Condensate. We study the sub-eikonal effect by letting the quark jet carry realistic finite longitudinal momenta, and we find sizeable changes on the transverse coordinate distribution of the quark. We also observe the energy loss of the quark through gluon emissions in the $\ket{q}+\ket{qg}$ Fock space. This approach provides us with an opportunity to study scattering processes from non-perturbative aspects.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that sub-eikonal corrections from finite longitudinal momentum produce sizeable changes in the quark's transverse coordinate distribution during scattering off a Color Glass Condensate nucleus, and that energy loss occurs via gluon emissions in the truncated Fock space of |q⟩ + |qg⟩. The claim is hedged by the restriction to a specific Fock-space truncation and to a classical background field description of the nucleus.

## Method
The authors use time-dependent basis light-front quantization (tBLFQ) to simulate the real-time evolution of a quark jet scattering on a classical color field representing a high-energy heavy nucleus. The nucleus is described by the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism with a stochastic color charge density satisfying a local correlation relation. The quark state is evolved via the light-front Schrödinger equation in the interaction picture, discretized on a 3-dimensional lattice with periodic transverse boundary conditions, first in the single-quark Fock sector |q⟩ and then in the expanded |q⟩ + |qg⟩ sector including one dynamical gluon.

## Key numbers
- Quark longitudinal momentum values studied: p⁺ = ∞ (eikonal limit) and p⁺ = 10 GeV for the sub-eikonal comparison.
- Transverse lattice parameters: N⊥ = 18 sites per side, L⊥ = 50 GeV⁻¹, giving lattice spacing a = L⊥/N⊥.
- Background field extent along x⁺: Lη = 50 GeV⁻¹, discretized into Nη = 4 layers (|q⟩ sector) or Nη = 8 layers (|q⟩ + |qg⟩ sector).
- Gluon mass parameter: mg = 0.1 GeV; quark mass: mq = 4.2 GeV (in the |q⟩ + |qg⟩ calculation).
- Color charge correlation strength: g²μ̃ = 0.486 GeV⁻³/² (|q⟩ sector) and g²μ̃ = 0.018 GeV⁻³/² (|q⟩ + |qg⟩ sector).
- Initial quark state in the |q⟩ + |qg⟩ calculation: p⁺ = 4.5 × 100 GeV, with total longitudinal quantum number Kmax = 4.5.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous study (Li, 2020) of sub-eikonal non-perturbative corrections to quark-nucleus scattering by including one dynamical gluon in the Fock space, building on the tBLFQ formalism developed by Zhao (2013). The approach enables access to the intermediate-time wavefunction of the quark during scattering, which is not available in standard eikonal approximations, and opens the door to studying non-perturbative aspects of jet-nucleus scattering processes beyond the leading Fock sector.
