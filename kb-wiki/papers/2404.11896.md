# Systematic shell-model analysis of $2νββ$ decay of $^{76}$Ge and $^{96}$Zr to the ground and excited states of $^{76}$Se and $^{96}$Mo

> In this work, we have studied the $2νββ$ decay of $^{76}$Ge and $^{96}$Zr isotopes utilizing large-scale shell-model calculations. The GWBXG effective interaction has been employed in the calculation of $2νββ$-decay nuclear matrix elements (NMEs). We have tested the effective interaction by comparing the predicted spectroscopic properties, such as energy spectra and transition probabilities, with the available experimental data. The variation of cumulative NMEs with respect to the $1^+$ state energies of the intermediate nucleus is also studied, corresponding to $0^+_{\rm g.s.}\rightarrow0^+_{\rm g.s.}$, $0^+_{\rm g.s.}\rightarrow0^+_{2}$, and $0^+_{\rm g.s.}\rightarrow2^+_{1}$ transitions between the parent and grand-daughter nuclei. The effective values of axial-vector coupling strength ($g_A^{\rm eff}$) are calculated using the predicted NMEs and experimental half-lives for $0^+_{\rm g.s.}\rightarrow0^+_{\rm g.s.}$ transitions. The extracted half-lives for $0^+_{\rm g.s.}\rightarrow0^+_{2}$, and $0^+_{\rm g.s.}\rightarrow2^+_{1}$ transitions using the shell-model predicted NMEs are consistent with the recent experimental data. The comparison of the shell-model predicted NMEs with previous NMEs available in the literature is discussed. Also, the computed branching ratios for the $2νββ$ decay of $^{76}$Ge and both the $2νββ$ and single-$β$ decay of $^{96}$Zr are reported corresponding to the calculated $g_A^{\rm eff}$ values.

# Systematic shell-model analysis of $2\nu\beta\beta$ decay of $^{76}$Ge and $^{96}$Zr to the ground and excited states of $^{76}$Se and $^{96}$Mo

## Key claim

The shell-model calculations using the GWBXG effective interaction reproduce the experimental half-lives for ground-state-to-ground-state $2\nu\beta\beta$ transitions in $^{76}$Ge and $^{96}$Zr, but only after extracting an effective axial-vector coupling strength ($g_A^{\rm eff}$) that differs significantly between the two nuclei: $0.677\pm0.004$ for $^{76}$Ge versus $1.17^{+0.02}_{-0.03}$ for $^{96}$Zr. The predicted half-lives for transitions to excited states ($0^+_2$ and $2^+_1$) are notably higher than current experimental lower limits, providing sensitivity targets for future experiments.

## Method

Large-scale shell-model calculations were performed using the GWBXG effective interaction, which operates in a model space comprising the $0f_{5/2}$, $1p_{3/2}$, $1p_{1/2}$, and $0g_{9/2}$ proton orbitals and the $1p_{1/2}$, $0g_{9/2}$, $0g_{7/2}$, $1d_{5/2}$, $1d_{3/2}$, and $2s_{1/2}$ neutron orbitals. Truncations were applied: for $^{76}$Ge, $1p-1h$ excitations across $N=50$ to neutron orbitals above $N=50$ were included; for $^{96}$Zr, specific proton and neutron configuration partitions were imposed. The NUSHELLX code was used for Gamow-Teller matrix element calculations and KSHELL for energy spectra and transition probabilities. The cumulative nuclear matrix elements were computed as functions of $1^+$ state excitation energies in the intermediate nuclei ($^{76}$As and $^{96}$Nb) up to saturation.

## Key numbers

- $^{76}$Ge $0^+_{\rm g.s.}\rightarrow^{76}$Se $0^+_{\rm g.s.}$: $|M_{2\nu}| = 0.2285$, extracted $g_A^{\rm eff} = 0.677\pm0.004$, experimental half-life $(2.022\pm0.018_{\rm stat}\pm0.038_{\rm syst})\times10^{21}$ yr (GERDA experiment)
- $^{76}$Ge $0^+_{\rm g.s.}\rightarrow^{76}$Se $0^+_2$: $|M_{2\nu}| = 0.2414$, predicted half-life $1.276\times10^{24}$ yr, experimental lower limit $>6.2\times10^{21}$ yr
- $^{76}$Ge $0^+_{\rm g.s.}\rightarrow^{76}$Se $2^+_1$: $|M_{2\nu}| = 0.0045$, predicted half-life $5.877\times10^{26}$ yr, experimental lower limit $>1.1\times10^{21}$ yr
- $^{96}$Zr $0^+_{\rm g.s.}\rightarrow^{96}$Mo $0^+_{\rm g.s.}$: $|M_{2\nu}| = 0.0599$, extracted $g_A^{\rm eff} = 1.17^{+0.02}_{-0.03}$, experimental half-life $(2.35\pm0.14_{\rm stat}\pm0.16_{\rm syst})\times10^{19}$ yr (NEMO-3)
- $^{96}$Zr $0^+_{\rm g.s.}\rightarrow^{96}$Mo $0^+_2$: $|M_{2\nu}| = 0.0159$, predicted half-life $1.292\times10^{22}$ yr, experimental lower limit $>3.1\times10^{20}$ yr
- $^{96}$Zr $0^+_{\rm g.s.}\rightarrow^{96}$Mo $2^+_1$: $|M_{2\nu}| = 7.4\times10^{-4}$, predicted half-life $1.334\times10^{24}$ yr, experimental lower limit $>4.1\times10^{19}$ yr
- First $1^+$ state contributes 55.8% of final NME for $^{76}$Ge $0^+_{\rm g.s.}\rightarrow0^+_{\rm g.s.}$ transition and 99.5% for $^{96}$Zr $0^+_{\rm g.s.}\rightarrow0^+_{\rm g.s.}$ transition (single-state dominance)
- Cumulative NME for $^{76}$Ge $0^+_{\rm g.s.}\rightarrow0^+_{\rm g.s.}$ peaks at 4.35 MeV with value 0.2671, then saturates at 0.2285 near 12 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous shell-model studies of $2\nu\beta\beta$ decay (Patel 2024) to the nuclei $^{76}$Ge and $^{96}$Zr, motivated by recent experimental data from the GERDA and NEMO-3 collaborations. The results are compared with earlier shell-model calculations by Kostensalo (2021), which did not include $1p-1h$ excitations across $N=50$ and yielded a smaller NME (0.168) for $^{76}$Ge. The present calculations also contrast with the interacting boson model (Nomura), Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory (Dhiman), and (renormalized) quasiparticle random-phase approximation approaches (Toivanen, Stoica), which produce NMEs ranging from 0.034 to 0.879 for the same transition. The extracted $g_A^{\rm eff}$ values and predicted half-lives for excited-state transitions provide benchmarks for future experimental searches and sensitivity estimates.
