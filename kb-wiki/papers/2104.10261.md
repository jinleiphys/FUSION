# Semileptonic $B_c \to η_c, J/ψ$ transitions

> Using a systematic, symmetry-preserving continuum approach to the Standard Model strong-interaction bound-state problem, we deliver parameter-free predictions for all semileptonic $B_c \to η_c, J/ψ$ transition form factors on the complete domains of empirically accessible momentum transfers. Working with branching fractions calculated therefrom, the following values of the ratios for $τ$ over $μ$ final states are obtained: $R_{η_c}=0.313(22)$ and $R_{J/ψ}=0.242(47)$. Combined with other recent results, our analysis confirms a $2σ$ discrepancy between the Standard Model prediction for $R_{J/ψ}$ and the single available experimental result.

## Key claim

Using a symmetry-preserving continuum Schwinger function method (CSM) with no free parameters for the form factors, the authors predict the ratios \(R_{\eta_c}=0.313(22)\) and \(R_{J/\psi}=0.242(47)\); combined with other recent results, this confirms a \(2\sigma\) discrepancy between the Standard Model prediction for \(R_{J/\psi}\) and the single available experimental result from LHCb (Aaij 2017).

## Method

The authors compute the semileptonic \(B_c \to \eta_c, J/\psi\) transition form factors by solving the Bethe-Salpeter and gap equations within rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation of the continuum Schwinger function method, using a two-parameter interaction kernel that preserves one-loop renormalisation group behaviour of QCD. Because direct computation at the physical \(b:c\) current-mass ratio \(r_{b:c}=4.32\) is prevented by singularities in the complex integration domain, they compute form factors for fictitious meson systems with varying current-mass ratios and extrapolate to the physical point using the statistical Schlessinger point method (SPM). The procedure is repeated from two complementary perspectives (varying the initial-state or final-state quark mass) and the results are combined.

## Key numbers

* \(R_{\eta_c} = 0.313(22)\): ratio of branching fractions for \(B_c^+ \to \eta_c \tau \nu\) over \(B_c^+ \to \eta_c \mu \nu\).
* \(R_{J/\psi} = 0.242(47)\): ratio of branching fractions for \(B_c^+ \to J/\psi \tau \nu\) over \(B_c^+ \to J/\psi \mu \nu\).
* \(m_{B_c} = 6.270(4)\ \text{GeV}\): SPM-extrapolated mass of the \(B_c\) meson, consistent with the directly computed value \(6.27\ \text{GeV}\) and the empirical value \(6.2749(8)\ \text{GeV}\).
* \(m_{B_c^\ast} = 6.392(23)\ \text{GeV}\), \(m_{B_{c0}^\ast} = 6.760(25)\ \text{GeV}\), \(m_{B_{c1}} = 6.866(23)\ \text{GeV}\): SPM predictions for the masses of the vector, scalar, and axial-vector \(B_c\) excited states.
* Mean absolute relative error between the framework's predictions for static properties of heavy pseudoscalar mesons and empirical values is 3.6\%.

## Context

This work extends the continuum Schwinger function method (CSM) previously applied to semileptonic transitions of \(\pi\), \(K\), \(D_{(s)}\) mesons (Yao 2020) to the \(B_c \to \eta_c, J/\psi\) system, providing parameter-free predictions that complement lattice QCD results (Harrison 2020) and a range of continuum studies (Tran 2018, Issadykov 2018, Wang 2018, Leljak 2019, Hu 2019, Zhou 2019). The analysis confirms the \(2\sigma\) tension between the Standard Model prediction for \(R_{J/\psi}\) and the LHCb measurement (Aaij 2017), motivating further experimental and theoretical scrutiny of lepton universality in \(b\)-quark decays.
