# Primordial black hole capture, gravitational wave beats, and the nuclear equation of state

> Primordial black holes (PBHs), if captured by neutron stars (NSs), would emit a characteristic gravitational wave (GW) signal as they orbit inside the host star. We identify a specific and qualitatively new feature of these signals, namely quasi-periodic beats caused by the precession of noncircular PBH orbits. We demonstrate numerically and analytically that the beat frequency depends rather sensitively on the NS structure, so that hypothetical future observations with next-generation GW detectors would provide valuable constraints on the nuclear equation of state.

## Key claim

Primordial black holes (PBHs) captured on noncircular orbits inside neutron stars (NSs) produce quasiperiodic gravitational wave (GW) beats due to orbital precession, and the beat frequency depends sensitively on the NS structure and nuclear equation of state (EOS), but only if such signals are observed by next-generation GW detectors and the orbital radius and eccentricity can be determined independently.

## Method

The authors modeled PBHs as test masses moving on geodesics in the gravitational field of relativistic, static, spherically symmetric NSs constructed from polytropic EOSs with adiabatic exponents Gamma ranging from 1.75 to infinity. They solved the relativistic geodesic equations numerically for orbits starting at a fraction of the stellar radius with reduced angular momentum, and computed the leading-order GW signals using the quadrupole formalism. They also derived analytic expressions for the pericenter advance by perturbing nearly circular orbits near the stellar center, expanding the effective potential to fourth order in radius.

## Key numbers

- For a NS with mass M* = 1.4 solar masses and compaction GM*/(c^2 R*) = 1/6, the orbital GW frequency f_orb^GW ranges from 2.62 kHz (Gamma = infinity) to 5.56 kHz (Gamma = 1.75) for nearly circular orbits at R0/R* = 0.05.
- The GW beat frequency f_beat^GW ranges from 1.38 Hz (Gamma = infinity) to 28.7 Hz (Gamma = 1.75) for the same orbits.
- The pericenter advance Delta_phi for nearly circular orbits at R0/R* = 0.05 ranges from 0.00164 radians (Gamma = infinity) to 0.0161 radians (Gamma = 1.75) in general relativity.
- The number of GW cycles per beat cycle N_GW ranges from 1904 (Gamma = infinity) to 194 (Gamma = 1.75).
- For the example orbits shown (R_frac = 0.6, l_frac = 0.1), the pericenter advance was 0.0255 (Gamma = infinity), 0.0597 (Gamma = 3), and 0.127 (Gamma = 2) over a time span Delta t = 2000 M* (about 14 ms for M* = 1.4 solar masses).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of PBH capture by NSs, which examined circular orbits (Zou and Huang 2022; Gao et al. 2023) and the inspiral process (Horowitz and Reddy 2019), by identifying the qualitatively new feature of GW beats from noncircular orbits. The authors show that the precession rate is dominated by relativistic effects rather than Newtonian density nonuniformity, contrary to what a Newtonian treatment would suggest. The results enable future observations with next-generation detectors such as the Einstein Telescope, Cosmic Explorer, or NEMO to constrain the nuclear EOS by comparing observed beat frequencies with those computed for candidate EOS models, provided the orbital parameters can be determined from the inspiral signal and the host star's compaction.
