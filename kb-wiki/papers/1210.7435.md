# Influence of pions and hyperons on stellar black hole formation

> We present numerical simulations of stellar core-collapse with spherically symmetric, general relativistic hydrodynamics up to black hole formation. Using the CoCoNuT code, with a newly developed grey leakage scheme for the neutrino treatment, we investigate the effects of including pions and Λ-hyperons into the equation of state at high densities and temperatures on the black hole formation process. Results show non-negligible differences between the models with reference equation of state without any additional particles and models with the extended ones. For the latter, the maximum masses supported by the proto-neutron star are smaller and the collapse to a black hole occurs earlier. A phase transition to hyperonic matter is observed when the progenitor allows for a high enough accretion rate onto the proto-neutron star. Rough estimates of neutrino luminosity from these collapses are given, too.

## Key claim

Including pions or Λ-hyperons in the equation of state for core-collapse simulations reduces the maximum mass supported by the proto-neutron star and causes earlier black hole formation, but the authors note that their simple grey leakage scheme for neutrino treatment does not allow extraction of an energy-resolved neutrino signal and that the results depend on the progenitor's accretion rate.

## Method

The authors performed spherically symmetric general relativistic hydrodynamic simulations using the CoCoNuT code with a newly developed grey leakage scheme for neutrino treatment. They extended the Lattimer and Swesty equation of state with K=220 MeV (LS220) by adding either a free pion gas (LS220+π) or Λ-hyperons via the Balberg and Gal model with parameterization 220BG (LS220+Λ), and simulated collapse for two different 40 M⊙ progenitors (low-metallicity u40 and solar-metallicity WWs40) up to black hole formation detected by an apparent horizon finder.

## Key numbers

- Maximum gravitational mass of cold neutron stars: 2.06 M⊙ for LS220, 1.95 M⊙ for LS220+π, and 1.91 M⊙ for LS220+Λ
- Maximum baryonic mass of cold neutron stars: 2.40 M⊙ for LS220, 2.27 M⊙ for LS220+π, and 2.22 M⊙ for LS220+Λ
- Radius at maximum mass for cold neutron stars: 10.67 km for LS220, 9.94 km for LS220+π, and 9.28 km for LS220+Λ
- Radius at M=1.4 M⊙ for cold neutron stars: 12.71 km for LS220, 12.03 km for LS220+π, and 12.41 km for LS220+Λ
- Central density at maximum mass for cold neutron stars: 1.09 fm−3 for LS220, 1.26 fm−3 for LS220+π, and 1.47 fm−3 for LS220+Λ
- The fiducial model (lsu40) took 152 minutes for a single-processor run simulating 871 ms of physical time

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Ishizuka et al. (2008), Sumiyoshi et al. (2009), and Nakazato et al. (2012) who added pions and hyperons to the HShen equation of state, by applying similar extensions to the other commonly used Lattimer and Swesty equation of state. The results enable comparison of how different nuclear equations of state affect black hole formation timescales and proto-neutron star properties, and the authors note that their extensions of the LS EoS can fulfill the constraint from the 2 M⊙ neutron star measurement (Demorest et al., 2010) together with available experimental constraints on hyperon couplings, unlike some earlier hyperonic EoSs.
