# Atom-dimer p-wave resonance for fermionic mixtures with different masses

> We show that, near a Feshbach resonance, a strong p-wave resonance is present at low energy in atom-dimer scattering for $^6$Li-$^{40}$K fermionic mixtures. This resonance is due to a virtual bound state, in the atom-dimer system, which is present at this low energy. When the mass ratio between the two fermionic elements is increased, this virtual bound state goes to a known real bound state which appears when the mass ratio reaches 8.17. This resonance should affect a number of physical properties. These include the equation of state of unbalanced mixtures at very low temperature but also the equation of state of balanced mixtures at moderate or high temperature. The frequency and the damping of collective modes should also provide a convenient way to evidence this resonance. Finally it should be possible to modify the effective mass of one the fermionic species by making use of an optical lattice. This would allow to study the strong dependence of the resonance as a function of the mass ratio of the two fermionic elements.

# Atom-dimer p-wave resonance for fermionic mixtures with different masses

## Key claim

Near a Feshbach resonance, a strong p-wave resonance appears at low energy in atom-dimer scattering for ⁶Li-⁴⁰K fermionic mixtures, arising from a virtual bound state that becomes a real bound state when the mass ratio reaches 8.17. The resonance is present even for the experimentally relevant mass ratio of 6.64, which is below the threshold for a true bound state, and its strength grows rapidly as the mass ratio approaches 8.17.

## Method

The authors solved the full Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian integral equations for fermion-dimer scattering with unequal masses, projecting onto s-wave and p-wave components. They computed the scattering amplitudes for various mass ratios below the bound-state threshold of 8.17, using reduced variables with wavevectors in units of 1/a, and extracted the modulus of the scattering amplitudes and total cross sections as functions of the outgoing momentum k₀.

## Key numbers

- The first p-wave bound state appears at mass ratio m↑/m↓ = 8.17 (Kartavtsev and Malykh, 2007); the authors find the homogeneous integral equation gives a solution at m↑/m↓ = 8.172, in full agreement.
- For the ⁶Li-⁴⁰K mixture (mass ratio 6.64), the p-wave resonance peak occurs around k₀ ≃ 0.45 (in reduced units with a=1).
- The s-wave atom-dimer scattering length at k₀=0 increases from 1.179 for equal masses to 2.099 for m↑/m↓ = 8.
- The total scattering cross section at mass ratio 6.64 displays a strong resonance feature, while for mass ratios up to 4 the cross section is almost featureless.
- Reaching the bound-state mass ratio of 8.17 by tuning the ⁴⁰K effective mass in an optical lattice requires a lattice depth parameter s = 2.45 (for λ ~ 500 nm).

## Context

This work extends the study of three-body bound states in fermionic mixtures by Kartavtsev and Malykh (2007), who found that p-wave bound states appear for mass ratios above 8.17. The authors show that even below this threshold, virtual bound states produce strong resonances that affect atom-dimer scattering, particularly for the experimentally relevant ⁶Li-⁴⁰K mixture (mass ratio 6.64). This contrasts with the equal-mass case where p-wave contributions are negligible and the atom-dimer vertex can be approximated as a constant. The results enable predictions for the equation of state of unbalanced mixtures at low temperature, balanced mixtures at moderate or high temperature, and collective mode properties. The authors also propose using optical lattices to tune the effective mass ratio and explore the resonance systematically.
