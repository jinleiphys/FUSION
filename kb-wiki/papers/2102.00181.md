# Dibaryon with highest charm number near unitarity from lattice QCD

> A pair of triply charmed baryons, $Ω_{ccc}Ω_{ccc}$, is studied as an ideal dibaryon system by (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD with nearly physical light-quark masses and the relativistic heavy quark action with the physical charm quark mass. The spatial baryon-baryon correlation is related to their scattering parameters on the basis of the HAL QCD method. The $Ω_{ccc}Ω_{ccc}$ in the ${^1S_0}$ channel taking into account the Coulomb repulsion with the charge form factor of $Ω_{ccc}$ leads to the scattering length $a^{\rm C}_0\simeq -19~\text{fm}$ and the effective range $r^{\rm C}_{\mathrm{eff}}\simeq 0.45~\text{fm}$. The ratio $r^{\rm C}_{\mathrm{eff}}/a^{\rm C}_0 \simeq -0.024$, whose magnitude is considerably smaller than that of the dineutron ($-0.149$), indicates that $Ω_{ccc}Ω_{ccc}$ is located in the unitary regime.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $\Omega_{ccc}\Omega_{ccc}$ dibaryon system in the ${^1S_0}$ channel, when Coulomb repulsion between the doubly-charged baryons is included using a realistic charge form factor, sits in the unitary regime just above threshold with a large negative scattering length, rather than being a bound state as the strong interaction alone would produce.

## Method

The authors used (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD on a $96^4$ lattice with nearly physical light-quark masses ($m_\pi \simeq 146$ MeV) and the relativistic heavy quark action for the physical charm quark. They applied the time-dependent HAL QCD method to extract the $\Omega_{ccc}\Omega_{ccc}$ central potential $V(r)$ in the ${^1S_0}$ channel from the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave function, then solved the Schrodinger equation to obtain scattering phase shifts and low-energy scattering parameters via effective range expansion. Coulomb repulsion was incorporated using a dipole form factor for $\Omega_{ccc}^{++}$ with charge radius $r_d = 0.410$ fm from prior lattice QCD studies.

## Key numbers

- Scattering length from strong interaction only: $a_0 = 1.57(0.08)(^{+0.12}_{-0.04})$ fm
- Effective range from strong interaction only: $r_{\mathrm{eff}} = 0.57(0.02)(^{+0.01}_{-0.00})$ fm
- Binding energy from strong interaction only: $B = 5.68(0.77)(^{+0.46}_{-1.02})$ MeV
- Root-mean-square distance of bound state from strong interaction: $\sqrt{\langle r^2\rangle} = 1.13(0.06)(^{+0.08}_{-0.03})$ fm
- Scattering length with Coulomb repulsion: $a^{\rm C}_0 = -19(7)(^{+7}_{-6})$ fm
- Effective range with Coulomb repulsion: $r^{\rm C}_{\mathrm{eff}} = 0.45(0.01)(^{+0.01}_{-0.00})$ fm
- Ratio $r^{\rm C}_{\mathrm{eff}}/a^{\rm C}_0 = -0.024(0.010)(^{+0.006}_{-0.014})$, compared to the dineutron value of $-0.149$
- Lattice spacing $a \simeq 0.0846$ fm, lattice cutoff $a^{-1} \simeq 2.333$ GeV, lattice size $La \simeq 8.1$ fm
- Pion mass $m_\pi \simeq 146$ MeV, kaon mass $m_K \simeq 525$ MeV
- Interpolated $\Omega_{ccc}$ mass: $m_{\Omega_{ccc}} = 4795.6(0.7)$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD Collaboration's previous lattice studies of dibaryon systems, particularly the $\Omega\Omega$ system studied by Gongyo et al. (2018), to the highest charm number sector ($C=6$). The $\Omega_{ccc}\Omega_{ccc}$ system was originally proposed by Bjorken (1985) as an ideal system for studying heavy-baryon interactions. The results show that this system is even closer to unitarity than the dineutron, and the authors suggest that future experimental measurements of pair-momentum correlations in high-energy $pp$, $pA$, and $AA$ collisions could detect this near-threshold state. The work also motivates future lattice studies of the $\Omega_{bbb}\Omega_{bbb}$ system to reveal the quark mass dependence of scattering parameters.
