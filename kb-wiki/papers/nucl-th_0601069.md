# Dynamical model of electroweak pion production in the resonance region

> In this report, we will briefly review the dynamical model of pion electroweak production reactions in the $Δ$ resonance region and report on our study of neutrino-nucleus reactions based on this model.

# Dynamical model of electroweak pion production in the resonance region

## Key claim

The dynamical model of pion electroweak production, which includes both non-resonant and resonant mechanisms, can describe most available data on pion electroproduction and neutrino reactions on the nucleon in the Δ resonance region, but the authors note that further studies on the propagation of the Δ-particle in nuclei are needed to fully explain nuclear effects beyond Fermi-averaging and Pauli blocking.

## Method

The authors construct an effective few-body Hamiltonian in a subspace spanned by πN, Δ, and γN states using a unitary transformation method, separating non-resonant interactions (nucleon exchange, t-channel meson exchange) from resonant Δ excitation. The resulting coupled equations yield a pion photoproduction amplitude with a non-resonant term and a resonant term containing a dressed γN→Δ vertex that incorporates pion cloud effects. The model is extended to weak pion production by relating the weak charged and neutral currents to the vector and axial-vector currents, with the axial NΔ form factor determined from the SU(6) quark model relation and no further adjustable parameters.

## Key numbers

- The non-resonant contribution to the p(ν,e⁻π⁺)p cross section amounts to 15% at W=1.2 GeV and Q²=0.1 (GeV/c)²
- The dipole form factor parameter for the proton is m_V²=0.71 GeV²
- The correction factor R_SL for NΔ form factors uses a=0.154 GeV⁻² and b=0.166 GeV⁻², deduced from (e,e'π) data at Q²=2.8 and 4 GeV²
- The nucleon axial-vector dipole mass is m_A=1.02 GeV
- The inclusive electron scattering on ¹²C is compared with data at E_e=0.96 GeV and 1.1 GeV at θ=37.5°

## Context

This work extends the dynamical model previously developed by two of the authors (Sato and Lee) for photo- and electro-production of pions (Sato and Lee, 2001; Sato and Lee, 2002) to neutrino-induced reactions (Sato and Lee, 2003; Sato and Lee, 2004). The model addresses the long-standing puzzle that quark model predictions for the NΔ magnetic dipole transition form factor G_M are about 40% lower than empirical values, explaining this discrepancy through pion cloud effects. The first application to neutrino-nucleus reactions on ¹²C incorporates Fermi-averaging and Pauli effects, and the results are compared with electron scattering data from Sealock (1989). The authors note that more detailed studies of nuclear effects, such as those by Benhar et al. (2005) and Nieves et al. (2005), are needed to improve the description of the dip region between quasi-elastic and delta-excitation peaks.
