# A symmetry conserving description of odd-nuclei with the Gogny force

> We present an approach for the calculation of odd-nuclei with exact self-consistent blocking and particle number and angular momentum projection with the finite range density dependent Gogny force. As an application we calculate the nucleus $^{31}$Mg at the border of the $N=20$ inversion island. We evaluate the ground state properties, the excited states and the transition probabilities. In general we obtain a good description of the measured observables.

## Key claim

The authors present a symmetry-conserving mean-field approach for odd nuclei that combines exact self-consistent blocking with particle number projection (PN-VAP) and an approximate angular momentum projection (AM-PAV) over a grid of deformation parameters \((\beta,\gamma)\), rather than performing a full variation after projection for angular momentum. The method reproduces the experimental ordering of levels in \(^{31}\mathrm{Mg}\) but produces a stretched spectrum, which the authors attribute to the absence of residual correlations such as large-amplitude fluctuations.

## Method

The starting point is a self-consistent blocked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) wave function for an odd-even nucleus, with the Gogny D1S interaction. Particle number projection is performed in the variation after projection (PN-VAP) approach, while angular momentum projection is carried out after variation (AM-PAV) on a grid of \((\beta,\gamma)\) deformation points. For each angular momentum \(I\) and parity, the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation is solved at each \((\beta,\gamma)\) point to obtain the lowest energy, and the minimum across the grid defines the physical state for that spin and parity.

## Key numbers

* The ground state of \(^{31}\mathrm{Mg}\) (positive parity) is found at \(\beta = 0.61\), \(\gamma = 13.9^\circ\), with an absolute energy of \(-246.840\) MeV for the \(I^\pi = 1/2^+\) state.
* The excitation energy of the \(3/2^+_1\) state is 0.287 MeV, the \(5/2^+_1\) state is 1.049 MeV, and the \(7/2^+_1\) state is 1.827 MeV relative to the \(1/2^+\) ground state.
* The magnetic moment of the \(1/2^+\) ground state is calculated as \(-0.935\ \mu_N\), compared to the experimental value \(-0.88355(15)\ \mu_N\).
* The \(B(E2; 5/2^+_1 \rightarrow 1/2^+_1)\) transition is calculated as 117 \(e^2\mathrm{fm}^4\), compared to the experimental value 61(7) \(e^2\mathrm{fm}^4\).
* The \(B(E2; 7/2^-_1 \rightarrow 3/2^-_1)\) transition is calculated as 88 \(e^2\mathrm{fm}^4\), compared to the experimental value 68(5) \(e^2\mathrm{fm}^4\).

## Context

This work extends symmetry-conserving beyond-mean-field (BMF) methods, previously applied to even-even nuclei with the Gogny force (e.g., Rodriguez and Egido, 2010; Borrajo and Egido, 2015), to odd-mass nuclei. It contrasts with earlier angular-momentum-projected calculations for odd-A nuclei that used small valence spaces or Skyrme forces (e.g., Bally et al., 2014). The approach enables the study of shape coexistence and intruder configurations in odd nuclei near the \(N=20\) inversion island, but the authors note that the stretched spectrum indicates the need to incorporate generator-coordinate-method (GCM) fluctuations for quantitative spectroscopy.
