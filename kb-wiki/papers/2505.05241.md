# Insights into Neutron Star Matter: EoS Models and Observations

> The equation of state (EoS) for neutron stars is a crucial topic in astrophysics, nuclear physics, and quantum chromodynamics (QCD), influencing their structure, stability, and observable properties. This review classifies EoS models into hadronic matter, hybrid, and quark matter models, analyzing their assumptions, predictions, and constraints. While hadronic models characterize nucleonic matter, potentially including contributions from hyperons or mesons, hybrid models introduce phase transitions to quark matter, and quark models hypothesize the presence of deconfined quark matter cores or entirely quark-composed stars. By synthesizing results from recent theoretical and observational studies, this review aims to offer a comprehensive understanding of the methodologies used in constructing neutron star EoS, their implications, and future directions.

## Key claim

This review paper classifies and compares neutron star equation of state (EoS) models into hadronic, hybrid, and quark matter categories, but does not present a single unified claim or new result; instead it synthesizes existing theoretical and observational constraints, noting that no single model is universally accepted and that each class has distinct limitations, such as the hyperon puzzle in hadronic models or the need for additional repulsive interactions in quark models to reach observed masses above 2 solar masses.

## Method

The authors conducted a literature review categorizing neutron star EoS models into three main classes: hadronic matter models (including relativistic mean-field and non-relativistic potential approaches), quark matter models (Nambu-Jona-Lasinio, functional renormalization group, and holographic V-QCD), and hybrid models (quark-hadron crossover and first-order phase transition models). They also discuss chiral effective field theory with many-body perturbation theory and Bayesian uncertainty quantification using Gaussian process regression. The review compares model predictions against observational constraints from gravitational wave event GW170817, NICER X-ray timing measurements, and pulsar mass measurements.

## Key numbers

- Hadronic RMF models predict neutron star radii in the range of 12–13 km for a 1.4 solar mass star, consistent with NICER observations
- The symmetry energy at saturation density is estimated as S_v = 31.7 ± 1.1 MeV, with slope L = 59.8 ± 4.1 MeV (from chiral EFT with Bayesian uncertainty quantification)
- The squared speed of sound at twice nuclear saturation density from chiral EFT at N3LO is c_s^2(2n_0) ≈ 0.10 ± 0.07
- The energy per particle in pure neutron matter and symmetric nuclear matter are highly correlated with Pearson correlation coefficient ρ ≈ 0.94
- V-QCD holographic models predict neutron star masses above 2.2 solar masses
- QHC21 hybrid crossover models support neutron star masses around 2.1 solar masses while maintaining consistency with NICER radius constraints and GW170817 tidal deformability constraints

## Context

This review extends the line of work from Oppenheimer and Volkoff (1939) who introduced the first relativistic EoS based on a free neutron Fermi gas, through Akmal et al. (1998) who developed the APR model, to more recent approaches such as the QHC21 model by Kojo et al. (2022) and the NMMA framework by Pang et al. (2023). The paper synthesizes constraints from GW170817 and NICER observations of pulsar J0740+6620, and discusses open questions including the hyperon puzzle, the nature of the hadron-quark phase transition, and the potential existence of strange quark stars.
