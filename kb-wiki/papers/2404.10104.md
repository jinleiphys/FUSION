# Chiral phase transition in soft-wall AdS/QCD with scalar-dilaton coupling

> The chiral phase boundary of nuclear matter is expected to have a critical point where the rapid crossover of lattice methods at zero chemical potential becomes a first-order phase transition. Phenomenological models based on the AdS/CFT correspondence, known as AdS/ QCD, have succeeded in capturing many features of nuclear matter, with recent progress in producing the expected critical point. We study a model that produces a critical point in the chiral phase diagram by introducing a coupling between the scalar chiral field and the dilaton. We examine the effect of the scalar-dilaton coupling on the critical point. We also study the zero-temperature chiral dynamics, which must allow for spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking in the limit of zero quark mass. We find that when the scalar-dilaton coupling is large enough to ensure correct zero-temperature chiral dynamics, a critical point is present only if the quark mass is greater than 12.8 MeV.

## Key claim

When the scalar-dilaton coupling is large enough to ensure correct zero-temperature chiral dynamics (spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking in the chiral limit), a critical point in the chiral phase diagram is present only if the quark mass is greater than 12.8 MeV.

## Method

The authors use a soft-wall AdS/QCD model with a 5D AdS-Reissner-Nordström black hole metric to incorporate finite temperature and chemical potential. They introduce a coupling term λ₁Φ|X|² between the dilaton field and the scalar chiral field, which gives the chiral field an effective mass that runs with energy scale. The chiral condensate σ is obtained by solving the equation of motion for the chiral field VEV numerically, using a near-horizon integration method, and extracting m_q and σ from the UV asymptotic expansion via the AdS/CFT dictionary.

## Key numbers

- The scalar-dilaton coupling must satisfy λ₁ ≥ 6.0 to achieve spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking in the chiral limit (m_q → 0) for both two-flavor and three-flavor cases.
- The minimum quark mass that allows a critical point when λ₁ = 6.0 is m_q = 12.8 MeV.
- The confinement scale is set by μ_g = 440 MeV.
- The scalar potential parameters are λ₄ = 4.2 and γ = -22.6 (with γ → 6√2 λ₃).
- The AdS/CFT dictionary sets the chiral field mass m₅² = -3.

## Context

This work extends prior soft-wall AdS/QCD models that achieved correct chiral dynamics at zero chemical potential using a UV-modified dilaton (Chelabi 2016, Bartz 2018) but did not produce a critical point. It builds on the approach of Fang (2019) who showed that scalar-dilaton coupling can introduce a critical point, and Fang (2018) who used the running mass to reproduce the Columbia plot. The results constrain the parameter space for holographic QCD models seeking to describe the chiral phase diagram, and the authors plan to extend the analysis to 2+1 flavor results with unequal quark masses and to combine the chiral transition with the deconfinement phase transition using dynamical Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton models (Yang 2022).
