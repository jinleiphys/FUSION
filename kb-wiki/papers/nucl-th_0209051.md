# Positive pion absorption on 3He using modern trinucleon wave functions

> We study pion absorption on 3He employing trinucleon wave functions calculated from modern realistic NN interactions (Paris, CD Bonn). Even though the use of the new wave functions leads to a significant improvement over older calculations with regard to both cross section and polarization data, there are hints that polarization data with quasifree kinematics cannot be described by just two-nucleon absorption mechanisms.

# Positive pion absorption on ³He using modern trinucleon wave functions

## Key claim

Even with improved, fully antisymmetrized trinucleon wave functions from modern NN interactions, the polarization data for quasifree pion absorption on ³He at 250 MeV cannot be fully described by two-nucleon absorption mechanisms alone, suggesting either an energy-dependent mechanism not present in two-nucleon reactions or active participation of the spectator nucleon.

## Method

The authors used Faddeev-calculated trinucleon wave functions from the CD Bonn and Paris NN potentials, parametrized as a sum of separable terms (two-term fits) to allow correlation between pair and spectator momenta. They embedded standard two-nucleon absorption mechanisms, direct production, Δ-isobar excitation, πN s-wave rescattering, and heavy meson exchange, into ³He, computing differential cross sections and proton polarizations for quasifree kinematics at pion kinetic energies of 120 and 250 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The total cross section difference between CD Bonn and Paris potential results is about 10% after adjusting the s-wave rescattering form factor to reproduce the analyzing power dip in pp→dπ⁺ at 515 MeV; without adjustment the difference would be 20–30%.
- The cross section overestimates data by up to 30% at 206 MeV.
- The πN coupling constant used is f²/4π = 0.076; the πΔN coupling constant is f*²/4π = 0.35.
- The s-wave rescattering cut-off mass was set to Λ = 3mπ for CD Bonn, 4mπ for Paris, and 5mπ for Reid soft core, adjusted to reproduce the dip in pp→dπ⁺ analyzing power at 515 MeV.
- All partial wave amplitudes up to J = 5 were included, found sufficient even at 800 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier quasifree absorption calculations by Quasid (1980s) and Quasids (1990s) that used phenomenological range-corrected deuteron wave functions or single-term separable Faddeev amplitudes from Hajduk et al. (HGS). It directly addresses the energy-dependent discrepancy found by Maytal-Beck and Aclander (1990s), where polarization data at 120 and 250 MeV could only be reproduced with different models for each energy. The new parametrization from Schneider et al. (2002) of fully antisymmetrized wave functions improves agreement but still cannot simultaneously describe both energies, pointing toward the need for three-body mechanisms or spectator participation. This enables future studies of three-nucleon absorption and the inverse production reactions pd→³He π⁰ and pd→³H π⁺.
