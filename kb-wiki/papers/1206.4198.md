# Resonances and fluctuations in the statistical model

> We describe how the study of resonances and fluctuations can help constrain the thermal and chemical freezeout properties of the fireball created in heavy ion collisions. This review is based on [1-5].

# Resonances and fluctuations in the statistical model

## Key claim
The study of short-lived resonances and event-by-event fluctuations can help constrain whether the statistical model's success in describing hadron yields reflects genuine chemical equilibration at hadronization, or merely phase-space dominance, by testing whether the energy dependence of resonance-to-stable-particle ratios follows the smooth rise predicted by equilibrium models or exhibits a dip characteristic of non-equilibrium scenarios such as supercooling.

## Method
The author reviews the statistical hadronization model with both equilibrium (γq=γs=1) and non-equilibrium (γq, γs fitted) assumptions, using the SHARE code to compute resonance-to-stable-particle ratios (e.g., K*/K, Σ*/Λ, Λ(1520)/Λ) as functions of collision energy from AGS through RHIC. A dynamical model combining resonance decay with rescattering and collective expansion is used to estimate how post-hadronization interactions modify detectable resonance abundances. Fluctuation observables (scaled variances of particle ratios) are computed from the partition function and compared with mixed-event subtraction to isolate physics signals from detector acceptance effects.

## Key numbers
- Chemical freeze-out temperature in absolute equilibrium fits: T ~ 160–170 MeV at highest heavy-ion energies, decreasing to ~50 MeV at lowest reaction energies.
- The non-equilibrium scenario predicts a dip in resonance-to-stable ratios near 30 A GeV, corresponding to the "kink" in multiplicity per participant and the "horn" in particle yield ratios.
- The fluctuation-based estimate of K*0/K− agrees with direct measurements within error bars of 10–15%, suggesting rescattering and regeneration either cancel or are negligible.
- Transport model estimates of rescattering effects on resonance abundances are ~40%, larger than the 10–15% margin from fluctuation comparisons.

## Context
This review extends the statistical model debate between phase-space dominance (Hagedorn, early work) and genuine equilibration (Becattini, Castorina) by focusing on observables that can discriminate between equilibrium fits (equil_energy, 2006) and non-equilibrium fits (gammaq_energy, 2006). The work builds on prior resonance studies (ratio1, ratio2) and fluctuation analyses (prcfluct, westfall) to propose that simultaneous measurement of resonance ratios and fluctuation observables can determine whether chemical and thermal freeze-out are close together (sudden hadronization) or separated by a significant rescattering phase. The method enables future energy scans at FAIR, RHIC, and NICA to test for non-monotonic behavior indicative of deconfinement or supercooling.
