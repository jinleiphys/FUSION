# Strange Fluctuations at RHIC

> Robust statistical observables can be used to extract the novel isospin fluctuations from background contributions in K-short K-plus measurements in nuclear collisions. To illustrate how this can be done, we present new HIJING and UrQMD computations of these observables.

# Strange Fluctuations at RHIC

## Key claim
Robust statistical observables can extract novel isospin fluctuations from background contributions in $K_S^0K^\pm$ measurements, but only if the fraction of kaons from disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) domains and the number of domains per kaon are such that the dynamic isospin observable $\nu_{\rm dyn}$ becomes positive; the signal is not guaranteed for all collision centralities.

## Method
The authors compute the dynamic isospin observable $\nu_{\rm dyn}$ for kaons using HIJING, HIJING/$B\overline{B}$, and UrQMD event generators for Au+Au collisions at 200 AGeV in the rapidity range $-0.5<y<0.5$, with 300,000 events for HIJING variants and 50,000 for UrQMD. They compare these simulations to a wounded nucleon model (WNM) normalized to pp simulations, and then estimate the effect of adding DCC contributions using a threshold model for the DCC kaon fraction $\beta$ as a function of impact parameter.

## Key numbers
- For a single DCC domain, the isospin variance $\nu \rightarrow 4/3$.
- The thermal (uncorrelated) statistical variance is $\nu_{\rm stat} = 4/\langle N_K\rangle$, where $N_K$ is the total kaon number.
- For $n \gg 1$ DCC domains, the isospin variance from DCC is $\nu_{\rm dcc} = 4/3n$.
- The dynamic isospin observable including both DCC and thermal contributions is $\nu_{\rm dyn} = 4\beta(\beta/3n - 1/\langle N_K\rangle)$, where $\beta$ is the fraction of kaons from DCC.
- In the DCC scenario estimate, 10 domains contribute kaons in the rapidity range $-0.5 < y < 0.5$, with impact parameter threshold $b_0 \sim 6$ fm and DCC fraction $\beta_0 \sim 20\%$.
- The DCC domain size is about 2 fm, consistent with WA98 results showing no evidence of large DCC domains.

## Context
This work extends the earlier proposal by Gavin and Kapusta (2002) that DCC can produce kaon isospin fluctuations, building on Kapusta and Wong's explanation of $\Omega$ and $\overline\Omega}$ baryon enhancement at the CERN SPS. The authors use event generator simulations (HIJING and UrQMD) to establish baseline fluctuations without DCC, finding that isospin fluctuations distinguish between these models while charge fluctuations do not. This enables experimental searches for DCC signals using statistical observables even when individual DCC domains are too small to detect in single-event analyses, as was the case for WA98 pion measurements.
