# Cracking hadron and nuclear collisions open with ropes and string shoving in PYTHIA8

> Enhancement in strangeness yields in heavy-ion collisions is regarded as one of the attributes of the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) along with jet quenching and final-state collectivity. To reproduce these effects in the MC implementation of the Lund string model in \pythia, the inclusion of non-perturbative mechanisms like string shoving and rope hadronization is crucial. In this proceeding, we discuss our latest implementation of these mechanisms and the preliminary results for the strangeness enhancement in high-multiplicity p-p, p-Pb and Xe-Xe. We conclude with remarks on the ongoing development of the model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that implementing both string shoving and rope hadronization within the PYTHIA8/Angantyr framework using a new "parallel frame" formalism can produce strangeness enhancement in high-multiplicity p-p, p-Pb, and Xe-Xe collisions, but the results are preliminary and the model requires further tuning of fragmentation parameters before quantitative agreement with data is achieved.

## Method

The authors implement string shoving and rope hadronization in PYTHIA8 by boosting every pair of string pieces to a Lorentz frame (the "parallel frame") where they lie in parallel planes, enabling calculation of the repulsive shoving force and spatial overlap for rope formation. When strings overlap in transverse space, their color charges combine into higher multiplets forming ropes with increased effective string tension, which modifies the production probability of strange quarks via the tunneling mechanism. The strings then hadronize separately but with modified fragmentation parameters influenced by the effective string tension, and the shoving force imparts additional transverse momentum to hadrons.

## Key numbers

- For p-p collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV, the ratio of (Lambda + anti-Lambda) to (pi+ + pi-) increases with charged particle multiplicity, with ropes and shoving producing higher baryon yields than default PYTHIA
- For p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV and Xe-Xe collisions at 5.44 TeV, the (Lambda + anti-Lambda)/(pi+ + pi-) yield ratios increase monotonously when going from p-p to p-Pb to Xe-Xe
- The yield ratios with the new rope implementation for p-Pb and Xe-Xe are 20% more compared to default Angantyr at lowest multiplicities and increase with charged particle multiplicity N_ch
- The shoving force parameter g is set to 0.25 or 1.0 in different comparisons, with g being a tunable parameter of order 1

## Context

This work extends the Lund string model in PYTHIA8 by implementing string interactions previously studied in the DIPSY event generator (Bierlich 2014, 2016) and earlier simplified shoving models (Bierlich 2017, 2020). The new parallel frame formalism allows treatment of all strings including those from high-pT partons, which was not possible in earlier implementations that only considered strings parallel to the beam axis. The approach aims to reproduce Quark-Gluon Plasma signatures such as strangeness enhancement and collective flow in both small (p-p) and large (A-A) collision systems within a single framework, with ongoing work to compare to experimental data from ALICE and to study jet quenching in small systems.
