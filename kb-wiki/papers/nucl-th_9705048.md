# Vector mesons in medium with finite three momentum

> We formulate a QCD sum rule to find the three momentum dependence of the peak position of the vector meson spectral density in nuclear medium. We find less than 2 % (0.1 %) shift of the peak position at nuclear matter density and at ($q =0.5$GeV/c) for the $ρ,ω(ϕ$) mesons. However, at higher density and three momentum, its effect becomes non-negligible. The relevance of our result in the dilepton spectrum in A-A and p-A reaction is discussed.

# Vector mesons in medium with finite three momentum

## Key claim

The paper formulates a QCD sum rule to calculate the three-momentum dependence of vector meson spectral densities in nuclear matter, finding that at nuclear matter density and moderate three-momentum (q = 0.5 GeV/c) the shift in peak position is less than 2% for rho and omega mesons and less than 0.1% for phi mesons, but that at higher densities and momenta the effect becomes non-negligible and could affect dilepton spectra in heavy-ion reactions.

## Method

The author constructs a QCD sum rule for the transverse and longitudinal polarization components of the vector current correlation function at finite three-momentum, expanding to leading order in q^2 and linear order in nuclear density. The operator product expansion is truncated at dimension 6, including twist-2 and twist-4 operators, with nucleon matrix elements taken from deep inelastic scattering data and previous estimates. The spectral density is modeled with a pole plus continuum, allowing the pole residue, mass-squared, and continuum threshold to acquire q^2-dependent corrections, and the parameters are determined by a Borel sum rule fit.

## Key numbers

- At nuclear matter density and q = 0.5 GeV/c: less than 2% shift in peak position for rho and omega mesons; less than 0.1% shift for phi mesons
- Transverse rho meson: a = -0.065 (coefficient for q^2 dependence of mass-squared), f = 0.137, s = -0.008, Borel interval 1 to 2.3 GeV^2
- Transverse omega meson: a = -0.040, f = 0.120, s = 0.009, Borel interval 1.3 to 2.5 GeV^2
- Longitudinal rho and omega mesons: a = 0.021 (0.061 without scattering term), f = 0.068 (-0.042 without scattering term), s = 0.027 (0.042 without scattering term), Borel interval 2.5 to 3.5 GeV^2
- Transverse phi meson: a = 0.004, f = 0.010, s = 0.009, Borel interval 0.9 to 2.0 GeV^2
- Longitudinal phi meson: a = 0.009, f = -0.001, s = 0.009, Borel interval 2.0 to 3.0 GeV^2
- Vacuum parameters for rho and omega: m_V^2 = 0.77^2 GeV^2, F = 1.48, S_0 = 1.43 GeV^2
- Vacuum parameters for phi: m_phi^2 = 1.02^2 GeV^2, F = 2.19, S_0 = 2.1 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach to vector mesons in nuclear matter, previously applied at zero three-momentum by Hatsuda and Lee (1992) and others, by including the leading finite-momentum effects that break Lorentz invariance. The results are consistent with effective hadronic model calculations such as the resonance-nucleon hole contribution studied by Friman and Pirner (1997) and the nucleon-hole contribution from Walecka-type models. The paper enables more realistic modeling of dilepton spectra in A-A and p-A reactions, particularly for the CERES collaboration data, by providing the momentum-dependent shift that must be disentangled from the scalar mass shift predicted by chiral symmetry restoration scenarios.
