# The glasma initial state at the LHC

> We present results from numerical Classical Yang Mills calculations of the initial stage Glasma field configurations in a relativistic heavy ion collision. We compute the initial gluon multiplicity from RHIC to LHC energies. The initial conditions for the classical field computation are taken from a dipole model parametrization tested on HERA data, meaning that all the parameters, including the normalization and the value of the saturation scale, are fixed from DIS data.

## Key claim

The initial gluon multiplicity in heavy ion collisions can be predicted from RHIC to LHC energies using Classical Yang-Mills calculations with initial conditions fixed entirely from HERA deep inelastic scattering data, but the prediction depends on the choice of dipole cross section parametrization (IPsat or bCGC) and on an assumed value for the constant \(c_x\) relating the momentum fraction \(x\) to the saturation scale and collision energy.

## Method

The authors performed numerical Classical Yang-Mills (CYM) simulations of the Glasma initial state in relativistic heavy ion collisions, using Wilson line correlators from two dipole model parametrizations (IPsat and bCGC) that were constrained by HERA diffractive data. The Wilson lines were constructed numerically under a nonlocal Gaussian approximation, and the gluon multiplicity was computed on a \(512^2\) lattice at proper time \(\tau = 1.6/Q_s\). The saturation scale \(Q_s\) was determined from the dipole models, and the gluon liberation coefficient \(c\) was extracted from the ratio of gluon multiplicity to \(Q_s^2\).

## Key numbers

- Saturation scale at RHIC (200 A GeV): \(Q_s \approx 1.1\) GeV (IPsat) and \(Q_s \approx 1.0\) GeV (bCGC) at median impact parameter.
- Saturation scale at LHC (5500 A GeV): \(Q_s \approx 1.9\) GeV (IPsat) and \(Q_s \approx 1.6\) GeV (bCGC).
- Gluon multiplicity per unit rapidity at RHIC (200 A GeV): \(\mathrm{d}N_g/\mathrm{d}\eta = 1000\) (IPsat) and 850 (bCGC).
- Gluon multiplicity per unit rapidity at LHC (5500 A GeV): \(\mathrm{d}N_g/\mathrm{d}\eta = 2900\) (IPsat) and 2200 (bCGC).
- Gluon liberation coefficient \(c \approx 1.1\) for both IPsat and bCGC models, within 10% of the MV model result.
- Ratio of saturation scale to color charge density parameter: \(Q_s / (g^2 \mu) \approx 0.57\) in the MV model implementation used.

## Context

This work extends earlier Classical Yang-Mills calculations of gluon production in heavy ion collisions (Krasnitz 1998, Krasnitz 2001, Lappi 2003) by replacing the phenomenological MV model initial conditions with dipole cross section parametrizations (IPsat from Kowalski and Teaney 2003, bCGC from Kowalski 2006) that are directly constrained by HERA deep inelastic scattering data. The results enable LHC predictions for charged particle multiplicities that can discriminate between different models of high-energy QCD evolution (IPsat with DGLAP evolution versus bCGC with BK evolution), and the authors note that further work is needed on impact parameter dependence, running coupling effects, and pomeron loop effects.
