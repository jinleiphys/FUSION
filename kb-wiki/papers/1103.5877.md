# Effect of the momentum dependence of nuclear symmetry potential on pion-/pion+ ratio in heavy-ion collisions

> In the framework of the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model, effect of the momentum dependence of nuclear symmetry potential on pion-/pion+ ratio in the neutron-rich reaction 132Sn+124Sn at a beam energy of 400 MeV/nucleon is studied. We find that the momentum dependence of nuclear symmetry potential affects the compressed density of colliding nuclei, numbers of produced pion- and pion+, as well as the value of pion-/pion+ ratio. The momentum dependent nuclear symmetry potential increases the compressed density of colliding nuclei, numbers of produced resonances delta(1232), N*(1440), pion- and pion+, as well as the value of pion-/pion+ ratio.

## Key claim
The momentum dependence of the nuclear symmetry potential increases the compressed density of colliding nuclei and the yields of pions and resonances, leading to a higher \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\) ratio. The effect on the ratio is about 7.4%, and the momentum dependence makes the inferred symmetry energy softer when comparing to experimental data.

## Method
The authors used the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model to simulate the central reaction \(^{132}\mathrm{Sn}+^{124}\mathrm{Sn}\) at a beam energy of 400 MeV/nucleon. They kept the isoscalar potential fixed and the symmetry energy fixed, while switching between a momentum-dependent symmetry potential (MDI) and a momentum-independent symmetry potential (MIDI). The model includes pion production and absorption channels via \(\Delta(1232)\) and \(N^{*}(1440)\) resonances, with in-medium reduced NN elastic scattering cross sections and free-space inelastic cross sections.

## Key numbers
- Maximum central baryon density reached is about 2 times normal nuclear matter density (\(\rho_0\)).
- The momentum dependence of the nuclear symmetry potential increases charged pion yields by about 30%.
- The effect of the momentum dependence on the \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\) ratio is about 7.4%.
- The beam energy is 400 MeV/nucleon for the reaction \(^{132}\mathrm{Sn}+^{124}\mathrm{Sn}\).

## Context
This work extends prior studies using the \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\) ratio to probe the high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy, as done by Li et al. (2002), Yong et al. (2006), and Xiao et al. (2009). It specifically addresses that most transport models use different momentum-dependent interactions among nucleons, and that the momentum dependence of the symmetry potential was seldom considered in such analyses. The results suggest that if Xiao et al. (2009) used a momentum-independent symmetry potential, their inferred symmetry energy would be even softer. The study enables more precise extraction of the symmetry energy at suprasaturation densities by showing that the momentum dependence must be included in transport model comparisons with data.
