# Microscopic study of tetrahedrally symmetric nuclei by an angular-momentum and parity projection method

> We study the properties of the nuclear rotational excitations with hypothetical tetrahedral symmetry by employing the microscopic mean-field and residual-interaction Hamiltonians with angular-momentum and parity projection method; we focus on the deformed nuclei with tetrahedral doubly-closed shell configurations. We find that for pure tetrahedral deformation the obtained excitation patterns satisfy the characteristic features predicted by group-representation theory applied to the tetrahedral symmetry group. We find that a gradual transition from the approximately linear to the characteristic rigid-rotor, parabolic energy-vs.-spin dependence occurs as a function of the tetrahedral deformation parameter. The form of this transition is compared with the similar well-known transition in the case of quadrupole deformation.

## Key claim

The authors find that for pure tetrahedral deformation, the calculated excitation patterns satisfy the characteristic features predicted by group-representation theory for the tetrahedral symmetry group, but this result is conditional on the Coriolis perturbation being small enough that the original mean-field tetrahedral symmetry is not destroyed.

## Method

The authors use a deformed Woods-Saxon mean-field potential with pure tetrahedral (α₃₂) deformation, combined with monopole pairing and a small Coriolis coupling term, to generate HFB-type product states. They then apply angular-momentum, parity, and particle-number projection techniques within the Hill-Wheeler formalism, using a spherically-symmetric two-body Hamiltonian with separable multipole interactions (F-type and G-type) to go beyond the mean-field approximation while preserving tetrahedral symmetry.

## Key numbers

- Tetrahedral magic numbers (same for neutrons and protons): 16, 20, 32, 40, 56–68, 70, 90–94, 112, and 136/142
- For the projection calculation, the model space truncation parameter ε = 10⁻⁶ gives projected energies stable within 1 keV (six digits of accuracy for absolute energy)
- The small Coriolis coupling used is ℏω_rot = 0.010 MeV, sufficient to break time-reversal invariance and introduce |ΔK| = 1 K-mixing
- For the pairing cut-off function: Λ_up = Λ_low = 1.2 ℏω and d_cut = 0.2 ℏω with ℏω = 41/A^(1/3) MeV
- The ratio of quadrupole to monopole pairing strength is g₂^τ/g₀^τ = 13.6
- For ¹⁶⁰Yb: neutron pairing gap Δ_n = 1.265 MeV, proton pairing gap Δ_p = 1.370 MeV; ground state deformations α₂₀ = 0.194 and α₄₀ = 0.031

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of tetrahedral symmetry in nuclei using the microscopic-macroscopic method (LD94) and Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations (TYM98, MTY98, YMM01), and builds on the authors' own previous development of efficient projection techniques (TS12). The paper focuses on three doubly-closed tetrahedral-shell configurations: ¹⁶⁰Yb (Z=70, N=90), ¹¹⁰Zr, and ²²⁶Th (Z=90, N=136), and enables future calculation of electromagnetic transition probabilities as characteristic signatures for experimental identification of tetrahedral symmetry in nature.
