# Searching for small droplets of hydrodynamic fluid in proton--proton collisions at the LHC

> In this paper, we investigate the hydrodynamic collectivity in high-multiplicity events of proton-proton collisions at $\sqrt{s}=$ 13 TeV, using iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model with three different initial conditions, namely, HIJING, super-MC and TRENTo. With properly tuned parameters, hydrodynamic simulations with each initial model give reasonable descriptions of the measured two-particle correlations, including the integrated and $p_{\rm T}$-differential flow for all charged and identified hadrons. However, the hydrodynamic simulations fail to describe the negative value of the four-particle cumulant $c_2^v\{4\}$ as measured in experiments. Further investigations show that the non-linear response between the elliptic flow $v_2$ and the initial eccentricity $\varepsilon_2$ becomes significant in the small p-p systems. This leads to a large deviation from linear eccentricity scaling and generates additional flow fluctuations, which results in a positive $c_2^v\{4\}$ even with a negative $c_2^\varepsilon\{4\}$ from the initial state. We also presented the first hydrodynamic calculations of multi-particle mixed harmonic azimuthal correlations in p-p collisions, such as normalized asymmetric cumulant $nac_n\{3\}$, normalized Symmetric-Cumulant, $nsc_{2,3}\{4\}$ and $nsc_{2,4}\{4\}$. Although many qualitative features are reproduced by the hydrodynamic simulations with chosen parameters, the measured negative $nsc_{2,3}\{4\}$ cannot be reproduced. The failure of the description of negative $c_2\{4\}$ and $nsc_{2,3}\{4\}$ triggers the question on whether hydrodynamics with a fundamentally new initial state model could solve this puzzle, or hydrodynamics itself might not be the appreciated mechanism of the observed collectivity in p-p collisions at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper shows that hydrodynamic simulations of high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at 13 TeV, using three different initial condition models, fail to reproduce the experimentally observed negative sign of the four-particle cumulant \(c_2\{4\}\) and the negative normalized symmetric cumulant \(nsc_{2,3}\{4\}\), raising the question of whether hydrodynamics itself is the correct mechanism for the observed collectivity in these small systems, or whether a fundamentally new initial state model could resolve the discrepancy.

## Method

The authors used the iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model, which combines 2+1D viscous hydrodynamics with the UrQMD hadron cascade, to simulate proton-proton collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=13\) TeV. They implemented three different initial condition models: HIJING, super-MC, and TRENTo, each with several parameter sets tuned to approximately reproduce the measured \(p_T\)-spectra and \(v_2\{2\}\) data. The simulations computed two-particle correlations, four-particle cumulants, and multi-particle mixed harmonic azimuthal correlations, and the results were compared to experimental data from CMS and ATLAS.

## Key numbers

- The four-particle cumulant \(c_2\{4\}\) from hydrodynamic simulations is always positive in the high-multiplicity region, failing to reproduce the negative values measured in experiments; for example, with TRENTo initial condition Para-III, the initial state \(c_2^\varepsilon\{4\}\) is \(-92.4 \pm 1.5 \times 10^{-4}\) but the final state \(c_2^v\{4\}\) remains positive.
- For HIJING initial condition Para-III, the initial state \(c_2^\varepsilon\{4\}\) is \(-22.0 \pm 6.0 \times 10^{-4}\), yet the final state \(c_2^v\{4\}\) is positive after hydrodynamic evolution.
- For super-MC initial condition Para-I, the initial state \(c_2^\varepsilon\{4\}\) is \(-32.0 \pm 3.3 \times 10^{-4}\), but the final state \(c_2^v\{4\}\) is positive.
- The normalized symmetric cumulant \(nsc_{2,3}\{4\}\) measured by ATLAS is negative and cannot be reproduced by the hydrodynamic simulations with any of the three initial condition models.

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic studies of small systems, such as the authors' earlier work with HIJING initial conditions (Zhao 2017), which also found a positive \(c_2\{4\}\) that disagreed with experiment. The paper systematically tests whether different initial condition models (super-MC from Welsh 2016 and TRENTo from Moreland 2018) could resolve this sign problem, and finds that none do. The failure to reproduce negative \(c_2\{4\}\) and \(nsc_{2,3}\{4\}\) challenges the interpretation that the observed collectivity in proton-proton collisions necessarily arises from hydrodynamic flow, and motivates further investigation into whether alternative mechanisms (such as initial-state effects from color glass condensate or transport models) might be more appropriate.
