# Crossing symmetry and phenomenological widths in effective Lagrangian models of the pion photoproduction process

> We investigate the importance of crossing symmetry in effective field models and the effects of phenomenological nucleon resonance widths on the paradigmatic case of pion photoproduction. We use reaction models containing four star resonances up to 1.8 Gev ($Δ$(1232), N(1440), N(1520), N(1535), $Δ$(1620), N(1650), $Δ$(1700), and N(1720)) with different prescriptions for crossed terms and widths, to fit the latest world database on pion photoproduction. We compare $χ^2$ results from selected multipoles and fits. The $χ^2$ is highly dependent on the fulfillment of crossing symmetry and the inclusion of $u$ channels.

## Key claim

The quality of fits to pion photoproduction data, as measured by χ², depends strongly on whether the model preserves crossing symmetry by including the resonance width in the u-channel propagator, not just in the s-channel; models that omit the u-channel or treat it inconsistently yield χ² values 50% or more larger than the best crossing-symmetric model.

## Method

The authors constructed five effective Lagrangian models for pion photoproduction, all containing the same set of four-star nucleon resonances up to 1.8 GeV (Δ(1232), N(1440), N(1520), N(1535), Δ(1620), N(1650), Δ(1700), N(1720)) but differing in how they treat the resonance width in the s- and u-channels and whether the u-channel is included at all. They fitted the parameters of each model independently to the energy-independent SAID multipole solution up to 1.2 GeV laboratory photon energy, using spin-3/2 couplings from the Pascalutsa scheme to avoid pathologies, and compared the resulting χ² values.

## Key numbers

- Model I (crossing-symmetric, energy-dependent width in both s and u channels) achieved the best fit, with χ²/χ²(Model I) = 1.00 by definition.
- Model V (traditional scheme: width in s channel only, zero width in u channel) gave χ²/χ²(Model I) = 1.18.
- Model II (crossing-symmetric, constant width in both channels) gave χ²/χ²(Model I) = 1.35.
- Model IV (energy-dependent width, s channel only, no u channel) gave χ²/χ²(Model I) = 1.51.
- Model III (constant width, s channel only, no u channel) gave the worst fit at χ²/χ²(Model I) = 1.85.
- The intrinsic E2/M1 ratio (EMR) of the Δ(1232) from all fits was consistent with lattice QCD results: EMR = (-1.93 ± 0.94)% at Q² = 0.1 GeV² and m_π = 0, and EMR = (-1.40 ± 0.60)% at Q² = 0.1 GeV² and m_π = 370 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous crossing-symmetric model (Fernandez-Ramirez 2006a, 2006b) by systematically comparing it against four alternative prescriptions for resonance widths and u-channel treatment, including the traditional scheme of Benmerrouche (1991) and Garcilazo (1993) that omits the width in the u-channel. The results demonstrate that crossing symmetry is not merely formal but quantitatively important for describing pion photoproduction data, and they identify the M_{1-}^{3/2} multipole as particularly sensitive to the u-channel contribution. The bare amplitudes from the crossing-symmetric model are intended for use in calculations of pion photoproduction from nuclei, where the elementary reaction model must be theoretically sound.
