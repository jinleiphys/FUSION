# Visible narrow cusp structure in $Λ_c^+\to p K^- π^+$ enhanced by triangle singularity

> A resonance-like structure as narrow as 10 MeV is observed in the $K^-p$ invariant mass distributions in $Λ_c^+\to p K^- π^+$ at Belle. Based on the large data sample of about 1.5 million events and the small bin width of just 1 MeV for the $K^-p$ invariant mass spectrum, the narrow peak is found precisely lying at the $Λη$ threshold. While lacking evidence for a quark model state with such a narrow width at this mass region, we find that this narrow structure can be naturally identified as a threshold cusp but enhanced by the nearby triangle singularity via the $Λ$-$a_0(980)^+$ or $η$-$Σ(1660)^+$ rescatterings.

# Visible narrow cusp structure in $\Lambda_c^+\to p K^- \pi^+$ enhanced by triangle singularity

## Key claim

The narrow (10 MeV) resonance-like structure observed by Belle in the $K^-p$ invariant mass spectrum of $\Lambda_c^+\to p K^- \pi^+$ at 1663 MeV is not a genuine quark-model resonance, but a threshold cusp at the $\Lambda\eta$ threshold that is enhanced and narrowed by a nearby triangle singularity from $\Lambda$-$a_0(980)^+$ or $\eta$-$\Sigma(1660)^+$ rescattering loops.

## Method

The authors construct triangle-diagram amplitudes for $\Lambda_c^+\to p K^- \pi^+$ via two intermediate rescattering sequences: $\Lambda_c^+\to \Lambda a_0(980)^+$ followed by $a_0(980)^+\to \eta\pi^+$ and $\eta\Lambda\to \Lambda(1670)\to K^-p$; and $\Lambda_c^+\to \eta\Sigma(1660)^+$ followed by $\Sigma(1660)^+\to \Lambda\pi^+$ and $\eta\Lambda\to \Lambda(1670)\to K^-p$. The triangle singularity location is computed using the Coleman-Norton theorem, and the loop integrals are evaluated with complex masses for the unstable intermediate particles ($a_0(980)$ and $\Sigma(1660)$) to regularize the logarithmic singularity. The $K^-p$ invariant mass distributions are then calculated and compared to the observed narrow peak at the $\Lambda\eta$ threshold.

## Key numbers

- The narrow structure observed by Belle has a mass of 1663 MeV and a width of 10 MeV, based on a data sample of about $1.452\times 10^6$ events with a bin width of 1 MeV for the $K^-p$ invariant mass spectrum.
- The $\Lambda\eta$ mass threshold is approximately 1663.5 MeV, coinciding with the peak position.
- For the $a_0(980)$-loop triangle singularity to lie on the physical boundary, the intermediate mass $m_2$ must fall in the range 1.06 to 1.17 GeV; for the $\Sigma(1660)$-loop, the range is 1.70 to 1.74 GeV.
- The $a_0(980)$ mass range considered is 960 to 1000 MeV with width 50 to 100 MeV; the $\Sigma(1660)$ mass range is 1630 to 1690 MeV with width 40 to 200 MeV.
- The upper limit for the coupling $|g_A|^2$ is estimated as $0.32\ \text{GeV}^{-1}/\tau_{\Lambda_c}$ for the $\Lambda a_0^+$ channel and $3.14\ \text{GeV}^{-1}/\tau_{\Lambda_c}$ for the $\Sigma(1660)\eta$ channel.
- The $\Lambda(1670)$ resonance has a PDG-averaged mass of about 1670 MeV and width of about 35 MeV, with mass range 1660 to 1680 MeV and width range 25 to 50 MeV.

## Context

This work explains the narrow structure observed by Belle in $\Lambda_c^+\to p K^- \pi^+$ (reported by Shen and Gabyshev at workshops) as a triangle-singularity-enhanced cusp, rather than a genuine hyperon resonance. It extends the known phenomenon of threshold cusps, such as the classical $\pi\pi$ cusp in $K^\pm\to \pi^\pm\pi^0\pi^0$ decays observed by the NA48/2 collaboration (Batley 2005) and the cusp in $\gamma p\to \pi^0 p$ at the $\pi^+ n$ threshold (Bernstein 1996). The authors note that a chiral unitary approach (Miyahara 2015) found that two-body unitarity cuts alone do not produce narrow structures at the $\Lambda\eta$ threshold, motivating the inclusion of triangle singularities. The work enables future experimental tests at BESIII, Belle-II, and LHCb, and predicts a similar cusp structure in $\Lambda_c\to \Sigma\pi\pi$ decays.
