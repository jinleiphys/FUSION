# Transition magnetic moments of $J^P=\frac{3}{2}^+$ decuplet to $J^P=\frac{1}{2}^+$ octet baryons in the chiral constituent quark model

> In light of the developments of the chiral constituent quark model ($χ$CQM) in studying low energy hadronic matrix elements of the ground-state baryons, we have extended this model to investigate their transition properties. The magnetic moments of transitions from the $J^P=\frac{3}{2}^+$ decuplet to $J^P=\frac{1}{2}^+$ octet baryons have been calculated with explicit valence quark spin, sea quark spin and sea quark orbital angular momentum contributions. Since the experimental data is available for only a few transitions, we have compared our results with the results of other available models. The implications of other complicated effects such as chiral symmetry breaking and SU(3) symmetry breaking arising due to confinement of quarks have also been discussed.

# Transition magnetic moments of decuplet to octet baryons in the chiral constituent quark model

## Key claim

The chiral constituent quark model (χCQM) can describe transition magnetic moments from the J^P = 3/2^+ decuplet to the J^P = 1/2^+ octet baryons, but the model's predictions agree well with experimental data only for the Δ → p transition (3.87 μN vs. 3.46 ± 0.03 μN extracted from helicity amplitudes), while for Σ*+ → Σ+ the result (2.60 μN) is roughly half the experimental value (4.45 μN), indicating the model's limitations for certain channels.

## Method

The transition magnetic moments were calculated from the matrix element ⟨B_{1/2^+}, S_z=1/2| μ_z |B_{3/2^+}, S_z=1/2⟩ using SU(6) spin-flavor wavefunctions for the octet and decuplet baryons. The total magnetic moment was decomposed into three contributions: valence quark spin polarization, sea quark spin polarization from Goldstone boson fluctuations (π, K, η, η'), and sea quark orbital angular momentum contributions. The transition probabilities for Goldstone boson emission were fixed from deep inelastic scattering data as P_π = 0.12, P_K = 0.06, P_η = 0.04, and P_η' = 0.03.

## Key numbers

- Δ → p transition magnetic moment: 3.87 μN (total), compared to experimental value of -3.43 μN from PDG and extracted value of 3.46 ± 0.03 μN from helicity amplitudes
- Σ*+ → Σ+ transition: 2.60 μN (total), compared to PDG value of 4.45 μN
- Σ*0 → Λ transition: 3.35 μN (total), compared to PDG value of 3.69 μN
- Σ*0 → Σ0 transition: 1.02 μN (total), no experimental data available
- Σ*- → Σ- transition: -1.27 μN (total), experimental upper bound < 0.85 μN
- Ξ*0 → Ξ0 transition: 2.83 μN (total), experimental upper bound < 5.39 μN
- Ξ*- → Ξ- transition: -1.02 μN (total), experimental upper bound < 5.39 μN
- Valence contribution to Δ → p: 2.83 μN; sea contribution: -0.60 μN; orbital contribution: 1.65 μN

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model (χCQM), previously applied to octet and decuplet baryon magnetic moments by Dahiya (2018) and collaborators, to decuplet-to-octet transition properties. The results are compared with predictions from chiral perturbation theory (χPT), light cone QCD sum rules (LCQCDSR), large-N_c chiral perturbation theory, relativistic quark model (Rel-QM), QCD sum rules (QCDSR), lattice QCD, chiral quark model (χQM), effective mass quark model (EMQM), meson cloud model (MCM), and U-spin approaches. The model enables decomposition of transition magnetic moments into valence, sea spin, and orbital contributions, showing that orbital contributions dominate when the number of u, d, or s quarks is larger, while equal numbers of quark flavors show more variation.
