# The few scales of nuclei and nuclear matter

> The well-known correlations of low-energy three and four-nucleon observables with a typical three-nucleon scale (e.g., the Tjon line) is extended to light nuclei and nuclear matter. Evidence for the scaling between light nuclei binding energies and the triton one are pointed out. We argue that the saturation energy and density of nuclear matter are correlated to the triton binding energy. The available systematic nuclear matter calculations indicate a possible band structure representing these correlations.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the binding energies of light nuclei and the saturation properties (energy per nucleon and Fermi energy) of infinite nuclear matter are correlated with the triton binding energy, extending the Tjon-line concept to many-body systems. They hedge that this scaling is a conjecture based on qualitative arguments, and that the empirical nuclear matter values disagree with the general trend of the correlation, suggesting that three-body correlations (not yet included in the analyzed calculations) might bring the results into agreement.

## Method

The authors analyze published nuclear matter binding energies per nucleon and Fermi energies (saturation densities) from calculations using various two-nucleon potentials, where the triton binding energy varies while two-nucleon low-energy observables (deuteron binding and singlet virtual-state energy) are kept fixed. They plot these nuclear matter results against the triton binding energy and also in dimensionless form (binding per nucleon divided by triton binding versus Fermi energy divided by triton binding) to test for linear correlations. The analysis uses data extracted from Ref. [mac] (Mackellar et al.) and compares with empirical nuclear matter values.

## Key numbers

- The Tjon line parametrization: \(B_\alpha = 4.72 (B_t - 2.48)\) MeV, which for the experimental triton binding \(B_t^{exp} = 8.48\) MeV gives \(B_\alpha^{exp} = 28.32\) MeV.
- Empirical nuclear matter values: binding energy per nucleon \(B_A/A = 16\) MeV and Fermi energy \(E_F = 37.8\) MeV.
- The scaling function \(B_A/A\) versus \(B_t\) is "quite linear" over an interval of about 2 MeV around the triton binding energy.
- The dimensionless plot of \(B_A/(A B_t)\) versus \(E_F/B_t\) shows a "clear linear correlation" across the data points.

## Context

This work extends the well-known Tjon line correlation between the triton and alpha-particle binding energies (Tjon 1975) to light nuclei and nuclear matter, and connects it to the Coester band (Coester et al. 1970) which correlates nuclear matter saturation energy and density from different two-nucleon potentials. The authors argue that the three-body scale (triton binding) dominates, generalizing the universal scaling found in three-nucleon systems (Phillips plot, Efimov physics) to many-nucleon systems. The paper suggests that future realistic calculations with controlled three-body forces could verify whether a single three-body scale suffices to describe both light nuclei and nuclear matter, and notes that Braaten and Hammer (2003) conjectured that QCD places nuclear physics near the Thomas-Efimov limit.
