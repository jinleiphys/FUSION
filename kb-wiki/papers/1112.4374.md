# Analytic continuation of functional renormalization group equations

> Functional renormalization group equations are analytically continued from imaginary Matsubara frequencies to the real frequency axis. On the example of a scalar field with O(N) symmetry we discuss the analytic structure of the flowing action and show how it is possible to derive and solve flow equations for real-time properties such as propagator residues and particle decay widths. The formalism conserves space-time symmetries such as Lorentz or Galilei invariance and allows for improved, self-consistent approximations in terms of derivative expansions in Minkowski space.

## Key claim

The paper develops a method to analytically continue functional renormalization group flow equations from imaginary Matsubara frequencies to the real frequency axis at the level of the flow equations themselves, rather than continuing the final result at the macroscopic scale \(k=0\); this approach is restricted to close-to-equilibrium situations accessible via linear response theory.

## Method

The author starts from the Wetterich equation for the flowing action \(\Gamma_k[\phi]\) in Euclidean space and performs the analytic continuation of the flow equations for the inverse propagator \(P(p) = G(p)^{-1}\) by hand, using the known analytic structure of the propagator (Källén-Lehmann spectral representation) and a suitable class of infrared regulator functions that depend on spatial momentum only. The formalism is applied to a scalar field with \(O(N)\) symmetry, where the flow equations are solved within a derivative expansion truncation in Minkowski space, extracting real-time properties such as propagator residues and particle decay widths.

## Key numbers

- The propagator near a resonance is expanded as \(P_1 = Z (-p_0^2 + \vec p^2 + m^2) + \cdots\) and \(P_2 = Z \gamma^2 + \cdots\), where \(\gamma^2 = m \Gamma\) and \(\Gamma \ll m\) yields a Breit-Wigner form.
- The spectral representation of the Euclidean propagator is \(G(p) = \int_0^\infty d \mu^2 \; \rho(\mu^2) \frac{1}{p^2+\mu^2}\) with real and non-negative spectral weight \(\rho(\mu^2)\geq 0\).
- The inverse propagator close to the real frequency axis takes the form \(P(p) = P_1(p_0^2-\vec p^2) - i s(p_0) P_2(p_0^2-\vec p^2)\) with \(s(p_0) = \text{sign}(\text{Re}\; p_0) \; \text{sign}(\text{Im}\; p_0)\).

## Context

This work extends the functional renormalization group formalism from Euclidean space (Wetterich 1993) to real-time properties, providing an alternative to approaches based on the Schwinger-Keldysh closed time contour (Gasenzer 2008, Berges 2008) or numerical Padé approximant techniques (Dupuis 2009, Sinner 2009, Schmidt and Enss). The method enables self-consistent approximations where real-time information (e.g., decay widths) feeds back into the flow equations, improving calculations of thermodynamic and spectral properties while preserving Lorentz or Galilei invariance.
