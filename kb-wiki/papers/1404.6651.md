# Disentangle the nature of resonances in coupled-channel models

> We present several possible hadronic states found in coupled-channel models within the on-shell approximation. The interaction potential is constructed as a sum of the tree-level Feynman diagrams calculated with the effective Lagrangians. Based on the recent empirical data, we illustrate the possible existence of several baryonic and mesonic states with definite quantum numbers in the model. We give their properties for the purpose of further study and discuss the potential of finding them in future experiments.

## Key claim

The paper argues that several hadronic states, including the baryonic \(F_{35}(2000)\) and various Bottomonium-like mesons, can be identified or predicted within coupled-channel models using an on-shell approximation, but the evidence for the \(F_{35}(2000)\) remains tentative because its contribution to the total cross section is tiny and it may be of non-resonant nature, acting as background in \(\pi N\) reactions.

## Method

The author solves the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the on-shell approximation, reducing it to a K-matrix form for each partial wave. The interaction kernel \(V\) is constructed from tree-level Feynman diagrams using effective Lagrangians that respect chiral symmetry at low energies, and the propagator matrix \(G\) is computed as a two-meson loop function using dimensional regularization. For baryons, the model includes channels such as \(\gamma N\), \(\pi N\), \(2\pi N\), \(\eta N\), \(\omega N\), \(K\Lambda\), and \(K\Sigma\) up to 2000 MeV; for mesons, the model includes Vector-Vector, Vector-Pseudoscalar, and Pseudoscalar-Pseudoscalar interactions in the bottom sector.

## Key numbers

- The \(F_{35}(2000)\) resonance is found with a mass of 2160 MeV and a width of 313 MeV, compared to 2015(24) MeV and 500(52) MeV from the KSU survey.
- The fitted helicity amplitudes for \(F_{35}(2000)\) are \(A_{1/2} = 18\) GeV\(^{-1/2}\) and \(A_{3/2} = -23\) GeV\(^{-1/2}\), while the KSU survey gives \(-16(81)\) GeV\(^{-1/2}\) and \(158(32)\) GeV\(^{-1/2}\).
- In the bottom sector, the pole positions for the \(0^+(0^{++})\) state in the VV channel is \(10650.2 \pm 1.9\) MeV (real part) with an imaginary part of \(11 \pm 7\) MeV; the \(0^-(1^{+-})\) state in the VV channel has a pole at \(10471.1 \pm 42.3\) MeV (real) with \(1 \pm 1\) MeV (imaginary).
- The subtraction constant \(\alpha_l\) is determined to be \(-3.12 \pm 0.08\) for channels with two heavy vector mesons and \(-1.65 \pm 0.05\) for other cases.
- The estimated production cross section for \(\gamma N \to N Z_b\) reactions is \(\sigma_{Zb} \lesssim 0.5\) nb.

## Context

This work extends coupled-channel analyses of baryon resonances, such as those by Penner (2002) and the KSU survey (2012), by focusing on the \(F_{35}(2000)\) state in \(K\Sigma\) photoproduction and its role in polarization observables. For mesons, it builds on the discovery of the \(Z_b(10610)\) and \(Z_b(10650)\) states by the Belle collaboration (2011) and uses heavy quark flavor symmetry to predict additional Bottomonium-like states, contrasting with approaches that emphasize pion exchange (Hosaka, 2012; Sun, 2011; Li, 2013) versus vector-meson dominance. The predictions enable future experimental searches in photo-induced reactions at high-luminosity accelerators.
