# The symmetry energy at subnuclear densities and nuclei in neutron star crusts

> We examine how the properties of inhomogeneous nuclear matter at subnuclear densities depend on the density dependence of the symmetry energy. Using a macroscopic nuclear model we calculate the size and shape of nuclei in neutron star matter at zero temperature in a way dependent on the density dependence of the symmetry energy. We find that for smaller symmetry energy at subnuclear densities, corresponding to larger density symmetry coefficient L, the charge number of nuclei is smaller, and the critical density at which matter with nuclei or bubbles becomes uniform is lower. The decrease in the charge number is associated with the dependence of the surface tension on the nuclear density and the density of a sea of neutrons, while the decrease in the critical density can be generally understood in terms of proton clustering instability in uniform matter.

# The symmetry energy at subnuclear densities and nuclei in neutron star crusts

## Key claim

The charge number of nuclei in neutron star crusts and the critical density at which inhomogeneous matter becomes uniform are both controlled by the density dependence of the symmetry energy, specifically the density symmetry coefficient L, with the caveat that these conclusions are drawn from a macroscopic nuclear model that ignores pairing, shell effects, fluctuations, and electron screening.

## Method

The authors constructed a macroscopic nuclear model using a Thomas-Fermi approach with parametrized nucleon density distributions, where the bulk energy per nucleon was expressed as a function of neutron and proton densities using potential energy densities for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter. They determined the EOS parameters by fitting calculated charge numbers, mass excesses, and root-mean-square charge radii to empirical data for stable nuclei, then extended the model to describe five phases of inhomogeneous matter (spherical nuclei, cylindrical nuclei, planar nuclei, cylindrical bubbles, spherical bubbles) in Wigner-Seitz cells at zero temperature under beta equilibrium and charge neutrality.

## Key numbers

- The saturation density n0 was constrained to 0.14–0.17 fm^{-3}, the saturation energy w0 to -16 ± 1 MeV, the symmetry energy coefficient S0 to 25–40 MeV, and the gradient coefficient F0 to 66 ± 6 MeV fm^5.
- The rms deviations of calculated masses from measured values were ~3–5 MeV, comparable to a Weizsacker Bethe formula; rms deviations of calculated charge radii were about 0.06 fm, comparable to the A^{1/3} law.
- For the standard EOS model E (L ≈ 40 MeV), the pasta phases appear between ~0.06 and ~0.09 fm^{-3}.
- The density at which spherical nuclei reach volume fraction u = 1/8 was approximately 0.06 fm^{-3}, nearly independent of L.
- The onset density of proton clustering n(Q) decreased with increasing L, with the difference between n(Q) and the u = 1/8 density vanishing near L = 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the macroscopic nuclear model of Oyamatsu (1993) and Oyamatsu and Iida (2003) to include a neutron gas, systematically examining how the density dependence of the symmetry energy affects crust properties. It contrasts with earlier investigations by Watanabe et al. (2000) which used a fixed parametrization based on Siemens and Pandharipande (1976), and it explains why some nuclear models (e.g., LRP, Chen, SLy) predict the absence of bubbles and nonspherical nuclei due to their relatively high pressure for pure neutron matter. The results enable predictions for neutron star thermal conductivity, neutrino emissivity, and vortex-nucleus interactions, while noting that real crustal matter includes defects and nonequilibrium effects beyond this study's scope.
