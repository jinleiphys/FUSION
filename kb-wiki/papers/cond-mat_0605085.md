# Anomalous specific heat jump in a two-component ultracold Fermi gas

> The thermodynamic functions of a Fermi gas with spin population imbalance are studied in the temperature-asymmetry plane in the BCS limit. The low temperature domain is characterized by anomalous enhancement of the entropy and the specific heat above their values in the unpaired state, decrease of the gap and eventual unpairing phase transition as the temperature is lowered. The unpairing phase transition induces a second jump in the specific heat, which can be measured in calorimetric experiments. While the superfluid is unstable against a supercurrent carrying state, it may sustain a metastable state if cooled adiabatically down from the stable high-temperature domain. In the latter domain the temperature dependence of the gap and related functions is analogous to the predictions of the BCS theory.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that a two-component ultracold Fermi gas with spin population imbalance in the BCS regime can exhibit a second, anomalous jump in the specific heat at a lower critical temperature, but this second jump occurs within a temperature domain where the homogeneous superfluid is metastable against a supercurrent-carrying state.

## Method

The authors study the thermodynamic functions of a uniform gas of fermionic atoms in two hyperfine states of $^6$Li, approximating the pairing interaction by a zero-range force characterized by the $s$-wave scattering length. They solve the gap equation and compute the free energy, entropy, and specific heat as functions of temperature for fixed density asymmetries, using the BCS mean-field formalism with a contact interaction and an ultraviolet cutoff.

## Key numbers

- The total density of $^6$Li atoms is fixed at $\rho=3.8\times10^{12}$ cm$^{-3}$, corresponding to a Fermi momentum $k_F = 4.83\times10^4$ cm$^{-1}$ and $k_F a_S = -0.558$ for the spin-symmetric system at $T=0$.
- The scattering length in units of the Bohr radius is $a_S/a_B = -2160$ for the two hyperfine states of $^6$Li.
- For asymmetry $\alpha = 0.02$, the specific heat at $T=0$ is $c_V(T=0) = 4.5$ (in units of $10^2$ nK and $10^{-4}$ cm).
- At large asymmetries (e.g., $\alpha \ge 0.04$), the reentrance effect sets in, with the gap non-zero only in a finite temperature domain bounded by two critical temperatures.
- The upper critical temperature asymptotics follow $\Delta(\alpha) \sim [T_c(\alpha)(T_c(\alpha)-T)]^{1/2}$, and the lower critical temperature asymptotics follow $\Delta(\alpha) \sim [T^*_c(\alpha)(T-T^*_c(\alpha))]^{1/2}$.

## Context

This work extends the theory of asymmetric superconductors (ASC) to ultracold Fermi gases, building on prior studies of population imbalance in metallic superconductors (LO, FF), nuclear systems (Sedrakian, Nozieres, Muther), and high-density QCD (Alford, Bowers, Nardulli). The results enable calorimetric experiments on ultracold gases to test the predicted reentrance effect and the second specific heat jump, which would be a signature of the unpairing phase transition at low temperatures.
