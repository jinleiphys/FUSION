# Quarkonium dissociation in a PNJL quark plasma

> We investigate the Mott effect for heavy quarkonia due to Debye screening of the heavy quark potential in a plasma of massless quarks and antiquarks. The influence of residual color correlation is investigated by coupling the light quark sector to a temporal gauge field driven by the Polyakov loop potential. This leads to an increase of the Mott dissociation temperatures for quarkonia states which stabilizes in particular the excited states, but has marginal effect on the ground states. The temperature dependence of binding energies suggests that the dissciation of the charmonium (bottomonium) ground state by thermal activation sets in at temperatures of 200 MeV (250 MeV).

## Key claim
The inclusion of Polyakov-loop dynamics (PNJL model) increases the Mott dissociation temperatures for excited quarkonium states compared to calculations without this coupling, but has only a marginal effect on ground states. The dissociation of charmonium and bottomonium ground states by thermal activation is suggested to occur at temperatures of 200 MeV and 250 MeV, respectively, which are below the Mott temperatures derived from the Schrödinger equation.

## Method
The authors calculated the Debye mass in a PNJL quark plasma by evaluating the one-loop longitudinal polarization function with massless quarks coupled to a homogeneous static gluon background field via the Polyakov loop. They then solved the Schrödinger equation for charmonium and bottomonium states using a plasma Hamiltonian with a screened Cornell potential, where the screening mass depends on the Polyakov loop variable. The temperature dependence of the Polyakov loop was taken from a nonlocal PNJL model (Blaschke 2007).

## Key numbers
- Debye mass integral: \( I(1) = 1 \) for deconfinement (\(\Phi = 1\)), \( I(0) = 1/9 \) for confinement (\(\Phi = 0\))
- Cornell potential parameters fitted to vacuum spectroscopy: \(\alpha_s = 0.471\) (equivalently \(3\alpha/4\)), string tension \(\sigma = 0.192~\text{GeV}^2\), charm quark mass \(m_c = 1.94~\text{GeV}\), bottom quark mass \(m_b = 5.1~\text{GeV}\)
- Mott dissociation temperature for charmonium ground state by thermal activation: 200 MeV
- Mott dissociation temperature for bottomonium ground state by thermal activation: 250 MeV
- Lattice QCD extracted Mott temperature for \(J/\psi\): \( T^{\rm Mott}_{J/\psi} \approx 1.9 T_c \) (cited from literature)
- Potential model Mott temperature for \(J/\psi\): \( T^{\rm Mott}_{J/\psi} \approx 1.2 T_c \) (cited from literature)

## Context
This work extends the standard one-loop calculation of the Debye mass in thermal quantum field theory (Le Bellac; Beraudo 2007) by incorporating the PNJL model (Ratti 2005; Hansen 2006) to account for residual color correlations in the deconfined phase. The results address the disagreement between lattice QCD spectral function analyses (Asakawa 2000) and potential model calculations (Satz 2005) regarding Mott temperatures for quarkonia. The findings enable future studies of heavy quarkonium dissociation kinetics and the role of imaginary parts of heavy-quark potentials (Beraudo 2007), and suggest that even the tightly bound \(\Upsilon\) may dissociate at temperatures accessible at RHIC.
