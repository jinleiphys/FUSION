# The (K-,p) reaction on nuclei with in-flight kaons

> We perform a theoretical study of the spectrum of protons with kinetic energies of around 600 MeV, emitted following the interaction of 1 GeV/c kaons with nuclei. A recent experimental analysis of this (K-,p) reaction on 12C, based on the dominant quasielastic process, has suggested a deeply attractive kaon nucleus potential. Our Monte Carlo simulation considers, in addition, the one-and two-nucleon K- absorption processes producing hyperons that decay into πN pairs. We find that this kaon in-flight reaction is not well suited to determine the kaon optical potential due, essentially, to the limited sensitivity of the cross section to its strength, but also to unavoidable uncertainties from the coincidence requirement applied in the experiment. A shallow kaon nucleus optical potential obtained in chiral models is perfectly compatible with the observed spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the (K-,p) reaction with in-flight kaons is not well suited to determine the depth of the kaon-nucleus optical potential, contrary to a previous experimental analysis by Kishimoto (2007) that claimed evidence for a deeply attractive potential of about 200 MeV. The authors show that a shallow potential of about 60 MeV from chiral models is perfectly compatible with the observed spectrum when one- and two-nucleon absorption processes and experimental coincidence requirements are properly accounted for.

## Method

The authors performed a Monte Carlo simulation of the (K-,p) reaction on 12C, treating the nucleus as a density distribution of nuclear matter through which kaons and produced particles propagate. The simulation included quasielastic processes (K- N → K- N), one-nucleon absorption (K- N → π Λ, K- N → π Σ), and two-nucleon absorption (K- NN → Σ N, K- NN → Λ N), followed by weak decays of hyperons into πN pairs. The effect of a kaon optical potential was implemented, and events were selected containing a proton with kinetic energy 500-700 MeV within an angle of 4.1 degrees in the lab frame, matching the experimental conditions of Kishimoto (2007).

## Key numbers

- The kaon optical potential tested was V_opt = (-60, -60)ρ/ρ0 MeV (shallow chiral potential) and V_opt = (-200, -60)ρ/ρ0 MeV (deep potential)
- The probability per unit length for two-nucleon absorption was taken as 20% that of one-body absorption, giving C_abs ≈ 6 fm^5
- Protons were selected in a kinetic energy range of 500-700 MeV within an angle of 4.1 degrees in the lab frame
- The initial kaon momentum was 1 GeV/c, producing protons with kinetic energies around 600 MeV

## Context

This work extends the debate on the kaon-nucleus optical potential, which has two competing views: shallow potentials (about 60 MeV at nuclear matter density) from chiral dynamics models by Lutz (1998), Angels (2005), and Tolos (2006), versus deeply attractive phenomenological potentials (about 600 MeV) advocated by Akaishi (2002). The paper directly contradicts the analysis of Kishimoto (2007), which claimed evidence for a 200 MeV deep potential from the same (K-,p) reaction data. The results enable future experiments to recognize that the bare spectrum, rather than the coincidence-required spectrum, would be a more valuable observable for determining the kaon optical potential.
