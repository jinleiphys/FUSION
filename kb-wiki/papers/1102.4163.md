# Molecule model for deeply bound and broad kaonic nuclear clusters

> A molecule model is proposed for the description of the properties of the kaonic nuclear cluster (KNC) anti-KNN with the structure N(ant-KN)_(I = 0) and quantum numbers I(J^P) = 1/2(0^-), the large binding energy B^(\exp)_(anti-KNN) = 103(6) MeV and the width Gamma^(\exp)_(anti-KNN) = 118(13) MeV, observed recently by the DISTO Collaboration. The theoretical values of the binding energy B^(th)_(anti-KNN) = 118 MeV, the width Gamma^(th)_(anti-KNN) = 142 MeV of the KNC anti-KNN and the density n_(anti-KNN) = 2.71 n_0, where n_0= 0.17 fm^(-3) is the normal nuclear density, reproduce well the large experimental values. They are calculated with the trial harmonic oscillator wave functions by using chiral Lagrangians, accounting for all self-energy terms, contributing to the masses of the kaonic nuclear clusters (anti-KN)_(I = 0) and anti-KNN. In addition the high Lambda*p sticking probability in the pp reaction at the kinetic energy T_p = 2.85 GeV of the incident proton is explained.

## Key claim

The molecule model, going beyond the tree-approximation by including self-energy loop corrections from chiral Lagrangians, reproduces the large experimental binding energy and width of the \(\bar{K}NN\) kaonic nuclear cluster observed by the DISTO Collaboration, but only when the frequency parameter \(\Omega_{\Lambda^*N} = 105.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\) is chosen to optimize the ratio \(B/\Gamma\) to 0.83, compared to the experimental ratio of 0.87(11).

## Method

The authors model the \(\bar{K}NN\) cluster as a nucleon bound to a \((\bar{K}N)_{I=0}\) pair (the \(\Lambda^*\) resonance), using trial harmonic oscillator wave functions parameterized by two frequencies \(\Omega_{\Lambda^*}\) and \(\Omega_{\Lambda^*N}\) for the \(\bar{K}N\) and \(N(\bar{K}N)\) correlations, respectively. They calculate binding energies and widths using chiral Lagrangians with derivative meson-baryon couplings, including all self-energy loop corrections from intermediate states \((\bar{K}N)_{I=0}\), \(\Lambda^0\eta\), and \((\Sigma\pi)_{I=0}\) for the \(\bar{K}N\) subsystem, and from \(N\Lambda^0\eta\), \(N(\Sigma\pi)_{I=0}\), \(N(\Sigma\pi)_{I=1}\), and \(N\Lambda^0\pi\) states for the full \(\bar{K}NN\) system.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of \(\bar{K}NN\): theoretical \(B^{\mathrm{th}}_{\bar{K}NN} = 118\ \mathrm{MeV}\), compared to experimental \(B^{\mathrm{exp}}_{\bar{K}NN} = 103(6)\ \mathrm{MeV}\) from the DISTO Collaboration.
- Width of \(\bar{K}NN\): theoretical \(\Gamma^{\mathrm{th}}_{\bar{K}NN} = 142\ \mathrm{MeV}\), compared to experimental \(\Gamma^{\mathrm{exp}}_{\bar{K}NN} = 118(13)\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Density of \(\bar{K}NN\): \(n_{\bar{K}NN} = 2.71\,n_0\), where \(n_0 = 0.17\ \mathrm{fm^{-3}}\) is normal nuclear density, consistent with the DISTO estimate \(\sim 3\,n_0\).
- Root mean square radius of \(\bar{K}NN\): \(R_{{^2_{\bar{K}}}{\mathrm{H}}} = 0.89\ \mathrm{fm}\).
- Binding energy of the \((\bar{K}N)_{I=0}\) subsystem: \(B_{{^1_{\bar{K}}}{\mathrm{H}}} = 29\ \mathrm{MeV}\), with width \(\Gamma_{{^1_{\bar{K}}}{\mathrm{H}}} = 36.4\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Frequency parameters: \(\Omega_{\Lambda^*} = 59.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(\Omega_{\Lambda^*N} = 105.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Sticking probability of the \(\Lambda^*N\) pair into \(\bar{K}NN\): \(P \sim 0.94\) for relative momentum \(p \sim 111\ \mathrm{MeV}/c\) (corresponding to diameter \(D = 1.78\ \mathrm{fm}\)).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier molecule model (Ivanov et al., 2009) from the tree-approximation to include self-energy loop corrections, enabling reproduction of the deeply bound \(\bar{K}NN\) state observed by the DISTO Collaboration (2010). The model contradicts the \(SU(3)\) coupled-channel approach with chiral dynamics (predicting shallow binding of about \(20\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for \(\bar{K}NN\)) and the coupled-channel Faddeev equation approach, which cannot describe the two-body non-pionic decay modes or the high momentum transfer reaction channels. The results enable predictions for partial decay widths into \(N\Lambda^0\), \(N\Sigma^+\), and \(N\Sigma^0\) channels, and explain the high \(\Lambda^*p\) sticking probability observed in the \(pp \to K^+ p \Lambda^0\) reaction at \(T_p = 2.85\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
