# Heavy baryons with Strangeness in Soliton Models

> We present some recent results from soliton model calculations for the spectrum of baryons with a single heavy quark.The model comprises chiral symmetry for light flavors and (approximate) heavy spin-flavor symmetry for the heavy quarks. We focus on flavor symmetry breaking for strangeness degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

The paper presents a chiral soliton model calculation for the spectrum of baryons with a single heavy quark (charm or bottom), claiming that all parameters in the mass formula are obtained from a single model Lagrangian except for a moderate uncertainty in the strength of light flavor symmetry breaking. The abstract glosses over the fact that the model systematically overestimates binding energies and underestimates mass splittings within a given heavy quark sector, requiring the symmetry breaking parameter to be tuned within a range (x ~ 20-30) to achieve acceptable agreement with data.

## Method

The authors start from a chiral soliton model with pseudoscalar and vector meson fields (rho and omega) that stabilizes the soliton without higher derivative terms, then augment it by coupling mesons with a single heavy quark (charm or bottom) constructed to reproduce heavy spin-flavor symmetry in the infinite heavy meson mass limit. The soliton produces an attractive potential for heavy mesons, and bound state solutions are found with energy eigenvalue omega; collective coordinates for flavor orientation in SU(3) are quantized, and flavor symmetry breaking between non-strange and strange quarks is included via a non-perturbative Yabu-Ando treatment of the operator O_sb. The resulting mass formula combines the classical soliton energy, moments of inertia for isospace and strangeness rotations, heavy meson binding energy, hyperfine splitting, and symmetry breaking contributions.

## Key numbers

- For x=25, the predicted mass difference M_Omega_c - M_Lambda_c = 463 MeV, while the empirical value is 409 MeV.
- For x=30, the predicted mass difference M_Omega_c - M_Lambda_c = 523 MeV, compared to the empirical 409 MeV.
- The predicted mass difference between Sigma and Xi baryons for j=1/2 positive parity changes from 101 MeV (strange) to 23 MeV (charm) to 6 MeV (bottom) at x=25; the observed trend is 125, 14, -17 MeV.
- For Lambda_c relative to the nucleon, the predicted mass difference is 1230 MeV (x=25) and 1233 MeV (x=30), while the empirical value is 1347 MeV.
- For Lambda_b relative to the nucleon, the predicted mass difference is 4391 MeV (x=25) and 4394 MeV (x=30), while the empirical value is 4681 MeV.
- The parity splitting for charmed baryons is underestimated by about 50 MeV compared to data.

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral soliton model calculations for heavy baryons with strangeness by Momen (1993) and builds on the soliton model framework reviewed by Weigel (2008). The model also reproduces the spectrum and static properties of light baryons as shown by Park (1991). The results enable future comparison with experimental data for negative parity heavy baryons and bottom baryons where data are currently sparse, and suggest that kinematical corrections (such as substituting the reduced mass in the bound state problem) could improve the overestimated binding energies inherited from exact heavy flavor symmetry.
