# On the atomic resonances in the $0 ν2EC$ transitions

> The nuclear method to discover Majorana neutrinos is the neutrinoless double $ β$ decay. An interesting alternative is offered by the inverse process, neutrinoless radiative double electron capture, accompanied by a photon emission. Two different mechanisms seem plausible: the magnetic type radiation by an initial electron and the resonant electric type radiation by the final atom. The physical background for these processes is calculated.

# On the atomic resonances in the $0\nu2EC$ transitions

## Key claim

The neutrinoless radiative double electron capture process, accompanied by photon emission, may offer a viable alternative to neutrinoless double beta decay for discovering Majorana neutrinos, provided the transition energy $Q$ is close to the $2P-1S$ atomic level difference in the final atom (within about 1 keV), which enables resonant enhancement of the capture rate.

## Method

The authors calculate the signal-to-background ratio for two distinct radiation mechanisms in neutrinoless double electron capture: magnetic-type radiation emitted by an initial electron before capture, and resonant electric-type radiation emitted by the final atom after two $1S$ electrons are captured virtually. The physical background from the competing two-neutrino radiative double electron capture process is computed using phase space integrals, with the photon energy distribution $W(q)$ derived from the leptonic and nuclear matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- For the magnetic-type transition with $Q = 1$ MeV and neutrino mass $m_\nu = 1$ eV, the ratio of neutrinoless to two-neutrino radiative rates is $R_{0\nu/2\nu} = 5 \times 10^{-5}$.
- For the same parameters and energy resolution $D = 3$ keV, the signal-to-background ratio is $R_{s/b} = 2 \times 10^6$.
- For the resonant capture case with $Q - Q_{res} = 1$ keV, the signal-to-background ratio is $R_{s/b} \approx 10^4$.
- For the resonant case where $Q < Q_{res}$ or $|Q - E_{res}| < \Gamma^r$ (direct hit into the atomic line), the signal-to-background ratio reaches $R_{s/b} \approx 10^9$.
- The radiative width $\Gamma^r$ of the two-electron-hole atom for the $2P \rightarrow 1S$ transition lies in the range $0.05-0.10$ keV.
- Practical interest requires $|Q_{res} - Q| < 1$ keV for resonance enhancement.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of neutrinoless double electron capture first proposed by Winter (1955) and later by Bernabeu et al. (1983), and builds on the authors' own earlier calculations (Sujkowski and Wycech, 2002, 2003). The resonant enhancement mechanism, where the $0\nu2EC$ transition energy matches the $K_\alpha$ atomic transition in the final atom, offers a path to experimentally feasible searches for Majorana neutrinos. The results enable identification of candidate nuclei with suitable energy matching and show that the physical background from two-neutrino processes can be effectively suppressed, particularly in the resonant regime.
