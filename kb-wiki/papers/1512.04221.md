# A non-boost-invariant solution of relativistic hydrodynamics in 1+3 dimensions

> We present a new solution of relativistic hydrodynamics in 1+3 dimensions which depends on both the transverse coordinate and rapidity. At early times the flow expands dominantly longitudinally in a non-boost-invariant manner, and at late times it expands nearly spherically. These two regimes are shown to be related by symmetry. The effect of viscosity is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new analytic solution of relativistic hydrodynamics in 1+3 dimensions that depends on both transverse coordinate and rapidity, but the solution is only exact in the asymptotic early-time (longitudinal-dominated) and late-time (nearly spherical) regimes; the intermediate regime requires perturbation theory and has no closed analytic expression.

## Method

The authors start from ideal relativistic hydrodynamics with a conformal equation of state (p = ε/3) and perform a Weyl transformation to map Minkowski space to a product space dS₂ × H₂ (two-dimensional de Sitter space times two-dimensional hyperbolic space). They construct a flow ansatz with two velocity components in this space and solve the hydrodynamic equations asymptotically for ρ⊥ → −∞ (early times) and ρ⊥ → +∞ (late times), then develop a perturbative expansion in powers of e^{±2ρ⊥} to connect the two regimes. Viscous corrections are treated by solving the Navier-Stokes equation to linear order in the shear viscosity.

## Key numbers

* The early-time energy density decays exponentially in rapidity as σ ∼ e^{−3|η|} and ε ∼ e^{−4|η|} at large |η|.
* The late-time energy density decreases as ε ∼ 1/t⁸, in contrast to the Landau estimate ε ∼ 1/t⁴.
* The expansion parameter for the perturbative series is e^{±2ρ⊥} cosh²η⊥, which scales as t²/L² for t ≪ L and L²/t² for t ≫ L, where L is the nuclear transverse size.
* The shear tensor components are O(e^{±2ρ⊥}) due to a cancellation, and the viscous correction to the energy density enters at O(e^{±7ρ⊥}) in the perturbative expansion.

## Context

This work extends the boost-invariant Gubser solution (Gubser, 2010) by introducing rapidity dependence, and contrasts with both the Bjorken boost-invariant solution (Bjorken, 1982) and the Khalatnikov-Landau 1+1-dimensional solution (Landau, 1953; Khalatnikov, 1954). Unlike previous interpolation attempts (Srivastava, 1992; Pratt, 2008; Bialas, 2007; Nagy, 2007; Beuf, 2008; Wong, 2008; Gubser, 2012; Wong, 2014) that assumed infinite transverse extent, this solution incorporates full 1+3-dimensional dynamics. The solution enables analytic modeling of low-energy heavy-ion collisions where boost invariance is violated and the transition from longitudinal to spherical expansion occurs.
