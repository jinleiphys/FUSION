# Testing superscaling predictions in electroweak excitations of nuclei

> Superscaling analysis of electroweak nuclear response functions is done for momentum transfer values from 300 to 700 MeV/c. Some effects, absent in the Relativistic Fermi Gas model, where the superscaling holds by construction, are considered. From the responses calculated for the 12C, 16O and 40Ca nuclei, we have extracted a theoretical universal superscaling function similar to that obtained from the experimental responses. Theoretical and empirical universal scaling functions have been used to calculate electron and neutrino cross sections. These cross sections have been compared with those obtained with a complete calculation and, for the electron scattering case, with the experimental data.

# Testing superscaling predictions in electroweak excitations of nuclei

## Key claim

The authors show that a theoretical universal scaling function, constructed by including finite-size effects, collective excitations, meson exchange currents, and final state interactions beyond the Relativistic Fermi Gas model, can reproduce electron and neutrino cross sections in kinematic regions where momentum transfer exceeds about 500 MeV/c, but the scaling approach fails at lower momentum transfers where surface and collective effects dominate.

## Method

The authors calculated longitudinal and transverse nuclear response functions for 12C, 16O, and 40Ca using a continuum shell model with final state interactions modeled after Botrugno and Co' (2005), random phase approximation (RPA) with two different residual interactions, and meson exchange currents including one-pion exchange diagrams with virtual Delta excitation. They extracted theoretical universal scaling functions from these responses and compared them with empirical scaling functions obtained from experimental electron scattering data of Jourdan (1996), then used both to compute electron and neutrino cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer range studied: 300 to 700 MeV/c
- For experimental longitudinal scaling functions at 570 MeV/c: scaling quality index D = 0.101 +/- 0.009 and R = 0.079 +/- 0.017 (used as threshold for acceptable scaling: R < 0.096 and D < 0.11)
- For experimental transverse scaling functions at 300 MeV/c: D = 0.223 +/- 0.004 and R = 0.165 +/- 0.017
- Shell model scaling functions fulfill scaling of first kind down to 400 MeV/c
- RPA effects become negligible at q > 600 MeV/c when calculated with finite-range interaction
- Scaling of first kind conserved with final state interactions down to q = 450 MeV/c
- Meson exchange current effects become relevant for q ~ 600 MeV/c
- For neutrino total cross sections on 16O at 200 MeV: scaling prediction about 20% larger than full calculation; at 300 MeV: difference about 7%

## Context

This work extends the superscaling analysis of Donnelly and Sick (1999) and Maieron et al. (2002) by testing whether physics beyond the Relativistic Fermi Gas model (which satisfies superscaling by construction) preserves scaling properties. The authors use the empirical scaling function from Jourdan (1996) data and compare with their own theoretical universal function. The results enable neutrino cross section predictions in the quasielastic region using scaling functions, but show that such predictions are unreliable for neutrino energies below 200 MeV where collective excitations dominate. The approach is limited by the absence of real Delta resonance excitation and pion production mechanisms in the models.
