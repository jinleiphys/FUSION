# Hanbury Brown - Twiss Interferometry in High Energy Nuclear and Particle Physics

> I review recent applications of two-particle intensity interferometry in high energy physics, concentrating on relativistic heavy ion collisions. By measuring hadronic single-particle spectra and two-particle correlations in hadron-hadron or heavy-ion collisions, the size and dynamical state of the collision fireball at freeze-out can be reconstructed. I discuss the relevant theoretical methods and their limitations. By applying the formalism to recent pion correlation data from Pb+Pb collisions at CERN we demonstrate that the collision zone has undergone strong transverse growth before freeze-out (by a factor 2 in each direction), and that it expands both longitudinally and transversally. From the thermal and flow energy density at freeze-out the energy density at the onset of transverse expansion can be estimated from conservation laws. It comfortably exceeds the critical value for the transition to color deconfined matter.

## Key claim

The paper argues that two-particle intensity interferometry (HBT) can reconstruct the size and dynamical state of the collision fireball at freeze-out, but that this reconstruction is necessarily model-dependent because the mass-shell constraint prevents direct inversion of the Fourier transform linking the correlation function to the emission function. The claim that the energy density at the onset of transverse expansion "comfortably exceeds the critical value for the transition to color deconfined matter" is based on estimates from conservation laws applied to the thermal and flow energy density at freeze-out, not on direct measurement.

## Method

The author reviews the theoretical formalism of two-particle intensity interferometry for relativistic heavy ion collisions, deriving the emission function S(x,K) as the Wigner transform of the source density matrix and showing how single-particle spectra and two-particle correlation functions are expressed as integrals over this emission function. The Yano-Koonin-Podgoretskii (YKP) parametrization is used to analyze the K-dependence of HBT radii, which separates spatial and temporal aspects of the source and allows identification of transverse collective flow through its breaking of M_perp-scaling. The formalism is applied to recent pion correlation data from Pb+Pb collisions at CERN.

## Key numbers

- The collision zone underwent transverse growth by a factor of 2 in each direction before freeze-out, based on pion correlation data from Pb+Pb collisions at CERN
- The energy density at the onset of transverse expansion "comfortably exceeds the critical value" for the transition to color deconfined matter (no specific numerical value given for either the estimated or critical energy density)
- The YKP radius parameters show perfect M_perp-scaling in thermal models without transverse collective flow; only non-zero transverse flow breaks this scaling, causing an explicit dependence on the particle rest mass

## Context

This review extends the work of Pratt (1984) and Makhlin and Sinyukov (1988) on HBT interferometry for heavy-ion collisions, particularly the recognition that collective expansion induces a K-dependence of the correlation function. The paper builds on earlier analyses showing that hadron production data at AGS and SPS energies can be understood within a hydrodynamical model with thermalized momentum distributions at freeze-out (Lee and Heinz, 1989; Stachel, 1994). The work enables future high-quality measurements of YKP correlation radii for both pions and kaons to provide model-independent identification of transverse flow, while also controlling for resonance decay contributions.
