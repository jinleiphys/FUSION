# Reexamining the neutron skin thickness within a density dependent hadronic model

> We calculate the skin thickness, binding energy, surface energy and density profiles within the Dirac solution of a density dependent hadronic model and compare with the NL3 parametrization results of the non-linear Wallecka model. The asymmetry for polarized electron scattering is also obtained using a partial wave expansion calculation. Results for 208Pb, 40Ca, 48Ca, 66Ni and 90Zr are shown and discussed.

# Reexamining the neutron skin thickness within a density dependent hadronic model

## Key claim
The neutron skin thickness and electron scattering asymmetry calculated with the full Dirac equation solution and partial wave expansion confirm that density dependent hadronic (DDH) models and the constant-coupling NL3 model give different results, but the model dependence remains difficult to extract from small momentum transfer data. The DDH model parametrizations (TW and DDME1) produce neutron skin thicknesses that fall within the experimental linear correlation with proton-neutron asymmetry, while NL3 results for very asymmetric nuclei lie outside the upper limit of that correlation.

## Method
The authors solved the Dirac equation for nucleons in finite nuclei using a harmonic oscillator basis expansion and the linear variational method, with meson fields obtained from Klein-Gordon equations and the Coulomb field from Green's function method. They calculated proton and neutron density profiles, mean-square radii, and neutron skin thickness for five nuclei using both the DDH model (with TW and DDME1 parametrizations) and the NL3 parametrization of the non-linear Walecka model. The asymmetry for polarized electron scattering was computed via a partial wave expansion method solving the Dirac equation for the electron, incorporating both electromagnetic and weak neutral-current potentials.

- For 208Pb with the DDH model (TW parametrization, Dirac solution): neutron skin thickness = 0.18 fm (from Table I, inferred from text discussion)
- For 208Pb with the NL3 model: neutron skin thickness = 0.28 fm (from Table I, inferred from text discussion)
- The PREX experiment at Jefferson Laboratory aims to measure the 208Pb neutron radius with accuracy less than 0.05 fm at momentum transfer q ≈ 0.4 fm⁻¹
- Elastic electron scattering cross sections were calculated at incident energies of 502 MeV for 208Pb and 757.5 MeV for 48Ca
- The asymmetry calculations used an incident electron energy of E = 800 MeV
- The weak neutral current constants are χp ≈ 0.04 and χn = -0.5
- The experimental linear fit for neutron skin thickness versus asymmetry is θ = (-0.04 ± -0.03) + (1.01 ± 0.15)δ, where δ = (N-Z)/A

## Context
This work extends the earlier study by Peles et al. (2005) which used Thomas-Fermi approximation for densities and Plane Wave Born Approximation for the asymmetry, by instead solving the Dirac equation exactly and using partial wave expansion for electron scattering. The authors compare their results with experimental data from Frois (for 208Pb) and Belicoso (for 48Ca), and with the experimental linear correlation between neutron skin thickness and proton-neutron asymmetry reported by Trzcinska et al. The results are directly relevant to the PREX experiment at Jefferson Laboratory, and the comparison between Thomas-Fermi and Dirac density profiles helps establish the accuracy of Thomas-Fermi approximations used in neutron star and supernova simulations.
