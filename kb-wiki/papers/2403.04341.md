# Momentum correlation of light nuclei in Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.0 $\sim$ 7.7 GeV

> Within the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics model (UrQMD) coupled with nucleon coalescence model and Mini-Spanning-Tree model, the yields of light nuclei have been stimulated in Au + Au collisions over an energy range of \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.0\sim7.7\ \rm{GeV}\) and the momentum correlation functions of light nuclei pairs have been calculated by both the Lednický-Lyuboshitz and the Correlation After Burner methods. We compare the yields of light nuclei and their momentum correlation functions at midrapidity in this energy region with experimental data. It is found that there are differences between the results of the two models, and the coalescence method seems less valid at low collision energy. Furthermore, both the peak values of proton-proton correlation functions and the transition point of elliptic flows from out-of-plane to in-plane emission show a turning point around 3-4 GeV, which suggests that there is a relation between momentum correlation function and collective flow of particles.

## Key claim

Within the UrQMD transport model coupled with nucleon coalescence and Mini-Spanning-Tree (MST) models, the momentum correlation functions of light nuclei in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.0 \sim 7.7\) GeV show a turning point around 3-4 GeV, which the authors suggest is related to the transition of elliptic flow from out-of-plane to in-plane emission; however, the coalescence method appears less valid at low collision energy, and the two cluster-formation models produce differences in both yields and correlation functions.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD microscopic transport model with a hard density-dependent Skyrme equation-of-state (below \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.3\) GeV) to simulate Au+Au collisions. Light nuclei (deuteron, triton, \(^3\)He) were formed via two methods: a coalescence model using Wigner phase-space densities with Monte Carlo sampling (with two sampling orders, coal_1 and coal_2, to enforce baryon number conservation), and a Mini-Spanning-Tree (MST) algorithm with spatial and momentum cuts (\(\Delta r = 3.575\) fm, \(\Delta p = 0.285\) GeV/\(c\)). Momentum correlation functions for particle pairs (p-p, d-d, t-t, p-d) were calculated using both the Lednicky-Lyuboshitz (LL) model and the Correlation After Burner (Crab) model, incorporating Coulomb and strong final-state interactions.

## Key numbers

* Deuteron Wigner function parameter \(b = 2.26\) fm; triton \(b = 1.61\) fm; \(^3\)He \(b = 1.74\) fm (from root-mean-square radii).
* MST cluster formation criteria: \(\Delta r = 3.575\) fm, \(\Delta p = 0.285\) GeV/\(c\).
* A time cut of 50 fm/\(c\) was found optimal for MST light nuclei production in the energy range studied.
* The peak of the proton-proton correlation function occurs at relative momentum \(k^* \approx 20\) MeV/\(c\).
* The turning point in both p-p correlation peak values and elliptic flow \(v_2\) transition (from negative to positive) occurs between \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.0\) and 4.0 GeV.
* At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.4\) GeV, MST yields lower values for deuteron, triton, and \(^3\)He than experimental data; at 7.7 GeV, MST yields more light nuclei than the coalescence model, especially at higher rapidity.
* The d-d correlation function at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.0\) GeV shows a slight increase in MST results around \(k^* = 50 \sim 150\) MeV/\(c\).
* The p-p correlation function at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.2\) GeV shows a slight increase in MST results in the \(k^* = 50 \sim 100\) MeV/\(c\) region.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of light nuclei production and HBT correlations in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies, specifically in the \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.0 \sim 7.7\) GeV range relevant to the RHIC Beam Energy Scan II (BES-II) and fixed-target programs. It compares two cluster-formation approaches (coalescence and MST) coupled with UrQMD, building on prior work by Chen (2003) on Wigner-function coalescence, Aichelin (1991) on MST methods, and Lednicky and Lyuboshitz (1981) on correlation functions. The results are compared with preliminary STAR and HADES experimental data. The observed connection between the p-p correlation peak and the elliptic flow \(v_2\) transition suggests a relation between momentum correlation functions and collective flow, enabling future studies that use correlation functions as probes of reaction dynamics and source properties in this energy regime.
