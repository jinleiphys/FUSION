# Isospin splitting of the Dirac mass probed by the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory in the full Dirac space

> The isospin splitting of the Dirac mass obtained with the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) theory is thoroughly investigated. From the perspective in the full Dirac space, the long-standing controversy between the momentum-independence approximation (MIA) method and the projection method on the isospin splitting of the Dirac mass in asymmetric nuclear matter (ANM) is analyzed in detail. We find that, the \textit{assumption procedure} of the MIA method, which assumes that the single-particle potentials are momentum independent, is not a sufficient condition that directly leads to the wrong sign of the isospin splitting of the Dirac mass, while the \textit{extraction procedure} of the MIA method, which extracts the single-particle potentials from the single-particle potential energy, leads to the wrong sign. By approximately solving the set of equations involved in the \textit{extraction procedure}, a formal expression of the Dirac mass is obtained. The wrong isospin splitting of the Dirac mass is mainly caused by that the \textit{extraction procedure} forcely assumes the momentum dependence of the single-particle potential energy to be a quadratic form where the strength is solely determined by the constant scalar potential.

## Key claim

The momentum-independence approximation (MIA) method fails in asymmetric nuclear matter not because it assumes momentum-independent single-particle potentials (the *assumption procedure*), but because its *extraction procedure* forcibly assumes the momentum dependence of the single-particle potential energy to be a quadratic form whose strength is determined solely by the constant scalar potential.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) calculations in the full Dirac space, including both positive-energy and negative-energy states, using the Bonn A nucleon-nucleon interaction. They separately tested the two procedures of the MIA method, the *assumption procedure* (assuming momentum-independent scalar and vector potentials) and the *extraction procedure* (extracting constant potentials from single-particle potential energies at two selected momenta), by applying each procedure within the full Dirac space framework and comparing the resulting Dirac masses and binding energies to the full self-consistent results.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear saturation density \(\rho = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) with Bonn A interaction, the full Dirac space RBHF calculation yields \(M^*_{D,n} - M^*_{D,p} < 0\) for all asymmetry parameters \(\alpha\), consistent with the projection method but opposite to the MIA method.
- For the *extraction procedure* with momenta chosen at \(0.7k^\tau_F\) and \(k^\tau_F\), the extracted dimensionless function values are \(f_n(k_n) \approx 0.6\) and \(f_p(k_p) \approx 0.8\) for asymmetric nuclear matter at \(\alpha = 0.5\) and \(\rho = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\), leading to \(M^*_{D,n} - M^*_{D,p} > 0\).
- The binding energies per nucleon for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter calculated with the *assumption procedure* in the full Dirac space are very close to the full self-consistent RBHF results (shown in Fig. 2, no explicit numerical values given in text).

## Context

This work extends the recent self-consistent RBHF calculations in the full Dirac space by Wang et al. (2021, 2022), which resolved the long-standing controversy between the MIA method and the projection method regarding the sign of the isospin splitting of the Dirac mass. The paper clarifies that the MIA method's failure in asymmetric nuclear matter, first noted by Ulrych (1997), originates specifically from its extraction procedure rather than its momentum-independence assumption. This understanding enables improved treatment of isovector properties in nuclear matter and neutron star calculations, building on the full Dirac space framework developed by Wang et al. (2021, 2022).
