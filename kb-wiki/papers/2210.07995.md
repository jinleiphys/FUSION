# Single diffractive hard exclusive processes for the study of generalized parton distributions

> Generalized parton distributions (GPDs) are important nonperturbative functions that provide tomographic images of partonic structures of hadrons. We introduce a type of exclusive processes, to be referred to as single diffractive hard exclusive processes (SDHEPs). We discuss the necessary and sufficient conditions for SDHEPs to be factorized into GPDs. We demonstrate that the SDHEP is not only sufficiently generic to cover all known processes for extracting GPDs, but is also well motivated for the search of new processes for the study of GPDs. We examine the sensitivity of the SDHEP to the parton momentum fraction $x$ dependence of GPDs.

## Key claim

The paper introduces single diffractive hard exclusive processes (SDHEPs) as a generic class of 2→3 exclusive reactions that can be factorized into generalized parton distributions (GPDs) under the condition that the final-state particles' transverse momenta are much larger than the square root of the momentum transfer to the diffracted hadron, with the caveat that the factorization proof requires the soft-end suppression assumption for the meson distribution amplitudes involved.

## Method

The authors analyze the QCD factorization structure of SDHEPs by decomposing the scattering amplitude into contributions from different exchanged partonic channels between the diffractive hadron transition and the hard 2→2 exclusive subprocess, with the leading-power contribution coming from the n=2 channels (quark-antiquark or two-gluon exchange). They examine the necessary and sufficient conditions for factorization by working in the center-of-mass frame of the colliding particles, requiring the hard scale Q (set by the transverse momentum of final-state particles) to be much larger than the square root of the momentum transfer t, and demonstrate that the factorization follows from organizing pinch singularities in loop momentum integrals and applying collinear approximations to separate the hadronic subgraph from the hard part.

## Key numbers

- The condition for factorization requires q_{1T} ~ q_{2T} >> sqrt(-t), where q_{1T} and q_{2T} are the transverse momenta of the two final-state particles and t = (p-p')^2 is the momentum transfer to the diffracted hadron
- The n=1 (virtual photon exchange) channel is power enhanced by a factor of Q/sqrt(-t) compared to the n=2 (GPD) channels, with the leading power of the n=1 amplitude scaling as 1/sqrt(-t) versus 1/Q for the n=2 channels
- The collinear momentum scaling in the factorization region is k_i^mu ~ (1, lambda^2, lambda) Q, where lambda = sqrt(-t)/Q << 1
- The factorization error is of order lambda = sqrt(-t)/Q, with the approximation valid up to terms suppressed by powers of lambda

## Context

This work generalizes the diphoton production process in diffractive pion-nucleon scattering studied by Qiu and Yu (2022) to a broader class of exclusive processes, and provides a unified framework that covers all known GPD-extraction processes including deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) introduced by Ji (1996) and Radyushkin (1997), deeply virtual meson production (DVMP) from Brodsky (1994) and Frankfurt (1995), timelike Compton scattering (TCS) from Berger (2001), and exclusive Drell-Yan processes. The SDHEP formalism enables the search for new processes with enhanced sensitivity to the parton momentum fraction x dependence of GPDs, which has been the most difficult variable to extract experimentally due to the convolution nature of GPD factorization at the amplitude level.
