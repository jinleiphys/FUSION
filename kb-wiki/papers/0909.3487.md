# Microscopic calculation of the equation of state of nuclear matter and neutron star structure

> We present results for neutron star models constructed with a new equation of state for nuclear matter at zero temperature. The ground state is computed using the Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) technique, with nucleons interacting via a semi-phenomenological Hamiltonian including a realistic two-body interaction. The effect of many-body forces is included by means of additional density-dependent terms in the Hamiltonian. In this letter we compare the properties of the resulting neutron-star models with those obtained using other nuclear Hamiltonians, focusing on the relations between mass and radius, and between the gravitational mass and the baryon number.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new equation of state for neutron-star matter computed using the Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) technique, where many-body forces are included via density-dependent terms rather than explicit three-body potentials; the resulting maximum neutron-star mass is reduced from approximately 2.5 solar masses (obtained with previous AV8'+UIX calculations) to about 2.2 solar masses, and the authors note that inclusion of hyperons or deconfined quarks at high densities would likely lower the maximum mass further.

## Method

The authors used the AFDMC technique to compute the ground-state energy of symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) with 28 nucleons and pure neutron matter (PNM) with 66 neutrons in periodic boxes, employing the Argonne AV6' two-body potential. Many-body forces were incorporated through density-dependent terms of the form used in the Lagaris-Pandharipande (LP) model, with three free parameters refitted to reproduce the saturation density, binding energy, and compressibility of SNM. The resulting equation of state was extended to beta-equilibrium matter including electrons and muons, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were integrated to obtain neutron-star mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, binding energy per particle E₀ = -16.0 MeV, compressibility K ≈ 240 MeV
- Symmetry energy parameters: C_s = 31.3 MeV, γ_s = 0.64
- Maximum neutron-star mass for beta-equilibrium matter: approximately 2.2 solar masses (M⊙)
- Maximum neutron-star mass for PNM with AV8'+UIX (previous work): approximately 2.5 M⊙
- Central density at maximum mass for beta-equilibrium matter: ρ_c ≈ 1.2 fm⁻³
- Muons begin to appear at ρ ≈ 0.133 fm⁻³
- Density-dependent term parameters from AFDMC: γ₁ = 0.10 fm³, γ₂ = -750 fm⁶, γ₃ = 13.9 fm³
- Parametrization coefficients for SNM energy: a = 520.0 MeV fm⁶, b = -1297.4 MeV fm⁹, γ = -2.213 fm³

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic calculations of neutron matter using AFDMC by Gandolfi et al. (2009) and addresses limitations of the FHNC/SOC approximation used in the original LP model by Lagaris and Friedman (1981). The authors argue that the density-dependent approach, which was largely abandoned in favor of explicit three-body potentials like UIX, should be revisited because FHNC/SOC overbinds symmetric nuclear matter. The resulting equation of state enables comparison with observational constraints from binary pulsars, particularly the mass-radius relation for PSR J0737-3059, and provides a baseline for future inclusion of hyperons and quark matter at high densities.
