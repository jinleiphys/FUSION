# Transverse Asymmetry in Nucleon Deuteron Scattering in Pionless Effective Field Theory

> In this dissertation we apply the pionless effective field theory to low energy neutron deuteron elastic scattering process. We calculated some of the polarization observables in neutron deuteron scattering to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading-order, in particular the transverse asymmetry $A_y$. All of the previous calculations have the same characteristic feature of under-predicting this observable, this is known as the $A_y$-puzzle. At this order of the pionless EFT new two-body $P$-wave interaction terms enter into the Lagrangian. This interaction terms give crucial contributions to the $A_y$ observable. By varying the interaction coefficients within the allowed error estimates of the pionless EFT we find results that at this order are consistent with the experimental data. Our conclusion is that the $A_y$-puzzle is likely to be solved within the next few orders of the \EFT. Other observables in neutron-deuteron scattering process are also calculated and are in good agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The transverse asymmetry \(A_y\) in low-energy neutron-deuteron elastic scattering, calculated to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading-order (N3LO) in pionless effective field theory (EFT), can be brought into agreement with experimental data by varying the two-body \(P\)-wave interaction coefficients within the estimated theoretical error band, suggesting that the long-standing \(A_y\)-puzzle is likely to be resolved within the next few orders of the EFT rather than requiring new physics.

## Method

The author applied pionless effective field theory (EFT) to neutron-deuteron elastic scattering, calculating the transition matrix \(M\) by solving integral equations numerically. The transverse asymmetry \(A_y\) was computed using the density-matrix formalism, where \(A_y(\theta) = \Tr(M\sigma_2 M^\dagger) / \Tr(MM^\dagger)\), with contributions from new two-body \(P\)-wave interaction terms (\({}^3P_J\) channels) entering at N3LO. The \(C_2^{{}^3P_J}\) coefficients were varied within a \(\pm 15\%\) band around their central values to estimate theoretical uncertainties, and the denominator of \(A_y\) was kept at N2LO (resummed) to match the experimental peak position.

## Key numbers

*   The theoretical error on the N2LO total unpolarized cross section at \(E_n = 3.0\) MeV is estimated as 6% of the leading-order (LO) result, based on a power-counting parameter \(Q/\Lambda_{\not{\pi}} \sim 0.3\) and the fact that cross sections are proportional to the square of amplitudes.
*   The contribution to \(A_y\) from the two-body \(SD\)-mixing term is about three orders of magnitude smaller than the contributions from the two-body \({}^3P_J\) interactions.
*   At \(E_n = 3.0\) MeV, the maximum of \(A_y\) occurs around \(\theta = 105^\circ\), which coincides with the minimum of the unpolarized cross section.
*   The \(C_2^{{}^3P_J}\) interaction coefficients were varied within a \(\pm 15\%\) band around their central values (given in Eq. (4.34) of the dissertation) to produce the theoretical curves for \(A_y\).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of polarization observables in nucleon-deuteron scattering, which all under-predicted the \(A_y\) observable (the "\(A_y\)-puzzle"), as noted in prior potential model calculations such as those by Kievsky et al. (1996) using AV18+UR. By applying pionless EFT to N3LO, the author shows that the new two-body \(P\)-wave interactions can account for the missing strength, enabling future higher-order calculations (e.g., at N4LO with new three-body \(P\)-wave interactions) to test whether the puzzle is fully resolved within the EFT framework. The results also enable comparisons with \(pd\) scattering data, where Coulomb effects must be included for forward angles.
