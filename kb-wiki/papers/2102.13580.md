# Microscopic analysis of low-energy spin and orbital magnetic dipole excitations in deformed nuclei

> A low-energy magnetic dipole $(M1)$ spin-scissors resonance (SSR) located just below the ordinary orbital scissors resonance (OSR) was recently predicted in deformed nuclei within the Wigner Function Moments (WFM) approach. We analyze this prediction using fully self-consistent Skyrme Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) method. Skyrme forces SkM*, SVbas and SG2 are implemented to explore SSR and OSR in $^{160,162,164}$Dy and $^{232}$Th. Accuracy of the method is justified by a good description of M1 spin-flip giant resonance. The calculations show that isotopes $^{160,162,164}$Dy indeed have at 1.5-2.4 MeV (below OSR) $I^πK=1^+1$ states with a large $M1$ spin strength ($K$ is the projection of the total nuclear moment to the symmetry z-axis). These states are almost fully exhausted by $pp[411\uparrow, 411\downarrow]$ and $nn[521\uparrow, 521\downarrow]$ spin-flip configurations corresponding to $pp[2d_{3/2}, 2d_{5/2}]$ and $nn[2f_{5/2}, 2f_{7/2}]$ structures in the spherical limit. So the predicted SSR is actually reduced to low-orbital (l=2,3) spin-flip states. Following our analysis and in contradiction with WFM spin-scissors picture, deformation is not the principle origin of the low-energy spin $M1$ states but only a factor affecting their features. The spin and orbital strengths are generally mixed and exhibit the interference: weak destructive in SSR range and strong constructive in OSR range. In $^{232}$Th, the $M1$ spin strength is found very small. Two groups of $I^π=1^+$ states observed experimentally at 2.4-4 MeV in $^{160,162,164}$Dy and at 2-4 MeV in $^{232}$Th are mainly explained by fragmentation of the orbital strength. Distributions of nuclear currents in QRPA states partly correspond to the isovector orbital-scissors flow but not to spin-scissors one.

## Key claim

The low-energy spin-scissors resonance (SSR) predicted by the Wigner Function Moments approach is actually reduced to ordinary low-orbital (l=2,3) spin-flip states; deformation is not the principal origin of these low-energy spin M1 states but only a factor affecting their features, in contradiction with the WFM spin-scissors picture.

## Method

Fully self-consistent Skyrme Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) calculations were performed for the axially deformed nuclei 160,162,164Dy and 232Th using three Skyrme forces (SkM*, SVbas, and SG2). The mean field and pairing were computed with the code SKYAX using a two-dimensional grid in cylindrical coordinates, with pairing described by zero-range pairing interaction included at the iterative HF-BCS level. QRPA was implemented in matrix form with a large configuration space extending single-particle spectra up to 30 MeV, and spurious admixtures from rotational invariance violation were removed using the SEBRPA technique.

## Key numbers

- In 160,162,164Dy, states with IπK=1+1 at 1.5-2.4 MeV (below OSR) exhibit large M1 spin strength
- For 162Dy with SG2 force: spin state at 2.06 MeV has B(M1)_spin = 0.46 μN², B(M1)_orbital = 0.03 μN², total B(M1) = 0.27 μN²
- For 162Dy with SG2 force: spin state at 2.36 MeV has B(M1)_spin = 0.26 μN², B(M1)_orbital ≈ 0 μN², total B(M1) = 0.28 μN²
- For 162Dy with SG2 force: orbital state at 3.44 MeV has B(M1)_orbital = 0.86 μN², B(M1)_spin = 0.01 μN², total B(M1) = 1.07 μN²
- In 162Dy (SG2), total summed M1 strength at 0-4 MeV is 5.09 μN² (orbital 2.88 μN², spin 1.07 μN²)
- In 164Dy, experimental total M1 strength at 0-4 MeV reaches 6.17 μN² (Mar_exp_NRF_2005)
- Interference factor R in SSR range (0-2.4 MeV) for 162Dy: 0.73 (SG2), 0.57 (SVbas), 0.95 (SkM*)
- Interference factor R in OSR range (2.4-4 MeV) for 162Dy: 1.42 (SG2), 1.47 (SVbas), 1.45 (SkM*)
- Equilibrium deformation β for 162Dy: 0.346 (SG2), 0.345 (SVbas), 0.351 (SkM*), experimental 0.341(3)
- In 232Th, M1 spin strength is found very small

## Context

This work extends the microscopic understanding of low-energy magnetic dipole excitations in deformed nuclei, directly contradicting the Wigner Function Moments prediction of a spin-scissors resonance by Balbutsev, Molodtsova, and Schuck (2011-2020). The results agree with earlier Quasiparticle-Phonon Nuclear Model calculations by Soloviev (1996) for Dy isotopes, which also predicted low-energy 1+ states with spin-flip character. The analysis enables a reinterpretation of experimental data from nuclear resonance fluorescence experiments by Wesselborg (1988) and Margraf (2005), and Oslo (γ,n) experiments by Renstrom (2018), showing that low-energy 1+ states below 2.7 MeV in rare-earth nuclei are ordinary spin-flip excitations rather than a new deformation-induced scissors mode.
