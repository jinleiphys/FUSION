# Scaling Law in Cluster Decay

> A recently proposed scaling law for the decay time of alpha particles is generalized for cluster decay. It is shown that for the decay of even-even parents, $logT_{1/2}$ depends linearly on the scaling variable S=(Z$_{c}$Z$_{d})^{0.6}/\sqrt{Q_{c}}$ and on the square root of the reduced mass of cluster and daughter.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes a previously proposed universal scaling law for alpha decay half-lives to cluster decay, showing that for even-even parents, the logarithm of the half-life depends linearly on the scaling variable S = (Z_c Z_d)^0.6 / sqrt(Q_c) and also on the square root of the reduced mass of the cluster and daughter. The authors present this as a "model independent" description, but the fit parameters and the exponent on the reduced mass (x = 0.416) are determined empirically from a limited dataset of 11 cluster decays and 119 alpha decays, and the authors caution that extrapolation to heavier clusters must be taken with caution.

## Method

The authors collected experimental half-lives for alpha and cluster decays from even-even parents, including data for 14C, 24Ne, 28Mg, 20O, 32Si, and 34Si clusters. They performed a least-squares fit of the data to a proposed scaling law of the form logT_1/2 = (a1 * mu^x + b1) * [(Z_c Z_d)^y / sqrt(Q) - 7] + (a2 * mu^x + b2), where mu is the reduced mass, and determined the six parameters a1, b1, a2, b2, x, and y from the fit.

## Key numbers

- For the 119 alpha decays and 11 cluster decays from even-even parents, the fit gave parameters a1 = 9.1, b1 = -10.2, a2 = 7.39, b2 = -23.2, x = 0.416, and y = 0.613, with a root-mean-square deviation of 0.34 in logT_1/2.
- For the heavy clusters only, the root-mean-square deviation was 0.58 in logT_1/2.
- For the 14C chain: logT_1/2^exp = 11.02 from 222Ra, 15.9 from 224Ra, and 21.33 from 226Ra.
- For the 24Ne chain: logT_1/2^exp = 20.41 from 232U, 24.64 from 230Th, and 25.24 from 234U.
- For the 28Mg chain: logT_1/2^exp = 21.68 from 236Pu and 25.75 from 234U.
- The fitted coefficients C1 and C2 for each cluster type were: 6.3 and -7.3 for 4He; 17.4 and 8.0 for 14C; 20.7 and 19.1 for 24Ne; 27.1 and 21.7 for 28Mg.
- The fitted lines for C1 and C2 as functions of sqrt(mu) were: C1 = 6.3 sqrt(mu) - 6.2, C2 = 9.8 sqrt(mu) - 26.9.
- For the decay of 256No emitting 48Ca, the scaling law predicted logT_1/2 = 27.9, while a model-dependent result from another reference gave 18.9.
- For the decay of 118Ba into 12C and 106Sn, the scaling law gave logT_1/2 = 18.0, compared to a model-dependent prediction of 21.3 from another reference.

## Context

This work extends the universal scaling law for alpha decay proposed by Brown (1992) to cluster decay, providing a model-independent description that contrasts with earlier model-dependent approaches such as those by Poenaru et al. (1985) and Blendowske and Walliser (1988). The scaling law enables predictions of cluster decay half-lives for new decays, such as 12C from 220Ra and 222Th, and 18O from 226Th, and highlights the need for experimental data in the mass region around 256No and for light parents like 118Ba to test the extrapolation.
