# Turbulent thermalization process in heavy-ion collisions at ultrarelativistic energies

> The non-equilibrium evolution of heavy-ion collisions is studied in the limit of weak coupling at very high energy employing lattice simulations of the classical Yang-Mills equations. Performing the largest classical-statistical simulations to date, we find that the dynamics of the longitudinally expanding plasma becomes independent of the details of the initial conditions. After a transient regime dominated by plasma instabilities and free streaming, the subsequent space-time evolution is governed by a nonthermal fixed point, where the system exhibits the self-similar dynamics characteristic of wave turbulence. This allows us to distinguish between different kinetic scenarios in the classical regime. Within the accuracy of our simulations, the scaling behavior found is consistent with the ``bottom-up" thermalization scenario.

## Key claim

The authors find that the non-equilibrium evolution of a longitudinally expanding non-Abelian plasma at weak coupling becomes independent of the details of the initial conditions after a transient regime, and that the observed self-similar scaling behavior is consistent with the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario within the accuracy of their simulations.

## Method

The authors performed classical-statistical lattice simulations of SU(2) Yang-Mills theory in 3+1 dimensions with longitudinal expansion, solving the classical Hamilton equations of motion on a spatial lattice with periodic boundary conditions. Initial conditions were formulated at proper time Qτ₀ = 100 using a quasi-particle parametrization with variable initial occupancy n₀ and anisotropy ξ₀, and the gluon distribution function was extracted from Coulomb gauge-fixed fields using Fock state projection.

## Key numbers

- The scaling exponent for the longitudinal hard momentum scale was extracted as 2γ = 0.67 ± 0.07, averaged over data points for Qτ ≥ 650
- The transverse hard momentum scale exponent satisfied |β| < 0.06 for 800 ≤ Qτ ≤ 2000
- The combination α - 3β - γ approached -1 with residual deviation |α(τ) - 3β(τ) - γ(τ) + 1| < 0.05 for 800 ≤ Qτ ≤ 2000
- The extracted scaling exponents from kinetic theory analysis were α = -2/3, β = 0, and γ = 1/3
- Self-similar behavior was observed for Qτ in the range from 750 to 4000

## Context

This work extends the understanding of thermalization in heavy-ion collisions by resolving which kinetic scenario is realized in the weak-coupling limit, comparing the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario of Baier, Mueller, Schiff and Son (2001) against alternative scenarios such as those of Bodeker (2005) and the Kurkela-Moore (2011) approach. The results demonstrate that the first stage of the bottom-up scenario emerges as a non-thermal fixed point of the evolution, enabling future studies to connect classical-statistical dynamics at early times to kinetic theory descriptions at later times.
