# Double-Dipole Excitations in 40Ca

> The double-dipole strength distribution in $^{40}$Ca is calculated microscopically within a model space of 1p1h - and 2p2h excitations. Anharmonic effects in the centroid energies of the $0^+$- and $2^+$ components are found to be small, in agreement with experimental observation. Firm conclusions about the spreading width cannot be drawn, as yet, due to computational limitations in the number of 2p2h states.

## Key claim

Anharmonic effects in the centroid energies of the double-dipole resonance components in 40Ca are small, but firm conclusions about the spreading width cannot be drawn due to computational limitations in the number of 2p2h states included in the model space.

## Method

The authors diagonalized a model Hamiltonian in a space of 1p1h and 2p2h excitations for 40Ca, using a Woods-Saxon mean field and a density-dependent zero-range residual interaction. The single-dipole and double-dipole strength distributions were computed from the expansion coefficients and eigenvalues, with continuum excitations treated by discretization through expansion in a harmonic oscillator basis.

## Key numbers

- The GDR centroid energy in 40Ca was calculated at 19.7 MeV, compared to 20.3 MeV from the global A-dependence formula E_GDR = 31.2 A^{-1/3} + 20.6 A^{-1/6}
- The 0+ component of the DGDR centroid energy was 38.67 MeV and the 2+ component was 38.38 MeV, compared to 2 x E_GDR = 39.34 MeV
- The ratio of variances sigma_GDR / sigma_DGDR was 1.61 for the 0+ component and 1.24 for the 2+ component (sigma_GDR evaluated in a 10-30 MeV window, sigma_DGDR in a 30-50 MeV window)
- The model space truncated at 45 MeV excitation energy contained 1067 states for the 0+ component and 4144 states for the 2+ component of the DGDR

## Context

This work extends earlier second RPA calculations by Lauritsch and Reinhard (1990) using a separable residual interaction, and particle-vibration coupling calculations by Ponomarev et al. (1994), by performing a full diagonalization that avoids diagrammatic selections and fully respects the Pauli principle. The results enable future calculations of Coulomb excitation cross sections for direct comparison with experimental data on the double-giant-dipole resonance observed in several nuclei, as reviewed by Emling (1994).
