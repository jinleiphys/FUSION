# Bosonization and even Grassmann variables

> A new approach to bosonization in relativistic field theories and many-body systems, based on the use of fermionic composites as integration variables in the Berezin integral defining the partition function of the system, is tested. The method is applied to the study of a simplified version of the BCS model.

## Key claim

The paper tests a new bosonization method based on using fermionic composites as integration variables in the Berezin integral, but the authors explicitly state that a general procedure for deriving the free action of the composites from the constituent action remains to be established; the method is applied only to a simplified version of the BCS model where the composite action can be obtained, but whether this result can be generalized remains an open question.

## Method

The authors introduce even Grassmann variables representing fermion pairs as new integration variables in the Berezin integral defining the partition function, then determine the free action for these composites and evaluate their propagators. They apply this approach to the pairing model, a simplified version of BCS theory describing fermions in a single angular momentum level j interacting pairwise when coupled to zero total angular momentum. The free action for the composites is derived from the fermionic action, and the correlation functions of spin-zero pairs are evaluated in the path integral formalism to extract the energy spectrum.

## Key numbers

- The energy spectrum for states of n spin-zero pairs (seniority zero states) is given by E_n = n[2e - (Omega - n + 1)g_P], where e is the fermion energy, Omega is the number of m-substates, and g_P is the pairing coupling constant.
- The parameter xi = (Omega + 1)g_P / (2e) characterizes the system; the authors confine to xi < 1 where the ground state is trivial.
- The free pair propagator for t1 < t2 yields <phi_n(t2) phi*_n(t1)> = x_n^{2(t2-t1)} / [1 + e^{-beta e_n}]^2 in the continuum limit, where x_n = 1 - tau e_n.
- The monopole interaction shifts the pair energy from 2e to 2e + R, where R is the radial integral of the monopole term.

## Context

This work extends the bosonization approach introduced by Palumbo (1994) for handling fermionic composites in field theory and many-body systems, contrasting with the standard Stratonovitch-Hubbard transformation used in many-body theory. The method is tested on the pairing model, whose spectrum was previously known from the Hamiltonian formalism of Rowe (1970). The authors note that the free action for the composites has the same structure as the strong coupling mesonic action of QCD studied by Kawamoto and Smit (1981) and Kluberg-Stern et al. (1983), suggesting that the absence of time evolution in the wave operator is a general property of even composites rather than a strong-coupling artifact. The approach enables evaluation of composite propagators at zeroth order and handles cases where the fermionic determinant expansion required by the Stratonovitch-Hubbard method would fail (when xi is not much less than 1).
