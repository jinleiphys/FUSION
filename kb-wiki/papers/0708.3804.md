# Free Quarks and Antiquarks versus Hadronic Matter

> Meson-meson reactions A(q_1 \bar{q}_1) + B(q_2 \bar{q}_2) to q_1 + \bar{q}_1 + q_2 + \bar{q}_2 in high-temperature hadronic matter are found to produce an appreciable amount of quarks and antiquarks freely moving in hadronic matter and to establish a new mechanism for deconfinement of quarks and antiquarks in hadronic matter.

# Free quarks and antiquarks versus hadronic matter

## Key claim
Meson-meson reactions of the type A(q1 qbar1) + B(q2 qbar2) → q1 + qbar1 + q2 + qbar2 in high-temperature hadronic matter can produce an appreciable number of freely moving quarks and antiquarks, establishing a new "collisional deconfinement" mechanism. However, this mechanism only becomes significant near the critical temperature (T ~ 0.174 GeV) and produces negligible free quarks below T = 0.14 GeV.

## Method
The authors calculated unpolarized cross sections for meson-meson reactions producing four free quarks/antiquarks using a one-gluon-exchange transition amplitude with a temperature-dependent potential derived from lattice QCD results. They solved master rate equations for number densities of free quarks, pions, rho mesons, kaons, and K* mesons in static hadronic matter, starting from thermal equilibrium at t=0 fm/c with zero initial free quark density, considering the dominant meson species at RHIC (π, ρ, K, K*).

## Key numbers
- At T=0.174 GeV (chiral limit), the ratio of free-quark number density to total hadronic matter density reaches 0.87 at t=0.5 fm/c
- At T=0.174 GeV (constituent quark masses), the ratio of free-quark number density to total hadronic matter density reaches 0.33 at t=0.5 fm/c
- At T=0.16 GeV, the ratio of free-quark number density to total hadronic matter density is 0.11 at t=0.5 fm/c
- At T=0.14 GeV, the ratio of free-quark number density to total hadronic matter density is only 0.02 at t=0.5 fm/c
- In the chiral limit at T=0.174 GeV, about 75% of the four hadron species dissociate by t=1.7 fm/c and 90% by t=4.7 fm/c
- With constituent quark masses at T=0.174 GeV, about 70% of hadrons dissociate by t=5.0 fm/c and 90% by t=16.0 fm/c
- The critical temperature used is T_c = 0.175 GeV
- Constituent quark masses: 0.334 GeV for up/down quarks, 0.575 GeV for strange quarks

## Context
This work extends studies of deconfinement in hadronic matter, contrasting with lattice QCD approaches that examine deconfinement through energy density, chiral condensate, and screening mass of heavy quark-antiquark pairs (Karsch, various years). The temperature-dependent potential used is based on lattice gauge results from Karsch et al. and has previously been applied to calculate dissociation cross sections of J/ψ, χc1, and χc2 with pions (Xu et al., various years) in the quark-interchange mechanism. The paper enables further study of collisional deconfinement as a dynamical process complementary to the thermal QCD phase transition, though the authors note they have not yet included quark-antiquark annihilation processes.
