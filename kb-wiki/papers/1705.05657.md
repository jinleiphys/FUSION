# Magneto-Vortical evolution of QGP in heavy ion collisions

> The interplay of magnetic field and thermal vorticity in a relativistic ideal fluid might generate fluid vorticity during the fluid evolution provided the flow fields and the entropy density of the fluid is inhomogeneous \cite{Mahajan:2010}. Exploiting this fact and assuming large magnetic Reynolds number we study the evolution of generalised magnetic field ($\hat{B}$) which is defined as a combination of the usual magnetic field ($\vec{B}$) and relativistic thermal vorticity ($ω^{μν}$), in a 2(space)+1(time) dimensional isentropic evolution of Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) with longitudinal boost invariance. The temporal evolution of $\hat{B}$ is found to be different than $\vec{B}$ , and the $\hat{B}$ evolution also depends on the position of the fluid along the beam direction (taken along the z axis) with respect to the mid-plane $z=0$. Further it is observed that the transverse components ($\hat{B_{x}}$, $\hat{B_{y}}$) evolve differently around the mid-plane.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a relativistic ideal fluid with inhomogeneous flow fields and entropy density, the interplay of magnetic field and thermal vorticity can generate fluid vorticity, leading to a generalized magnetic field whose temporal evolution differs from the usual magnetic field and depends on the fluid's position along the beam direction relative to the mid-plane.

## Method

The authors studied a 2(space)+1(time) dimensional isentropic evolution of Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) with longitudinal boost invariance, assuming large magnetic Reynolds number (ideal MHD regime). They derived an analytic solution for the generalized magnetic field, defined as a combination of the usual magnetic field and relativistic thermal vorticity, by solving the relativistic magneto-vortical evolution equation with a Gaussian transverse entropy density profile and Bjorken flow.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic Reynolds number estimate for QGP at top RHIC energy: \(R_m \sim 560\), using fluid velocity \(v \sim 0.5c\), electrical conductivity \(\sigma_e \sim 6T/e^2\) at temperature \(T=300\) MeV, magnetic permeability \(\mu=1\), and characteristic length \(l=10\) fm.
- Initial magnetic field value used in numerical results: \(B^0_x = B^0_y = m_\pi^2\) (pion mass squared).
- Parameter values used in numerical results: entropy density \(s=100\) MeV\(^3\), number density \(n=0.4\) MeV\(^3\), temperature \(T=200\) MeV, Lorentz factors \(\gamma=3\) and \(\gamma=10\), initial transverse coordinates \(x_0=y_0=0.1\) fm, initial time \(t_0=0.1\) fm, Gaussian widths \(\sigma_x=\sigma_y=4.2\) fm, speed of sound squared \(c_s^2=1/3\), and initial longitudinal positions \(z_0=0.1\) fm and \(z_0=-0.1\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the analytic solution of magnetic field evolution in 2+1 dimensional QGP expansion found by Deng et al. (2012) by incorporating both magnetic field and fluid vorticity effects, as well as relativistic corrections. The study enables future investigations into the rapidity dependence of the chiral magnetic effect and related phenomena, though the authors note several assumptions (ideal fluid velocity field, zero viscosity, ideal gas equation of state, neglect of magnetic field back-reaction) that require improvement in subsequent work.
