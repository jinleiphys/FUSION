# Tensor-polarized twist-3 parton distribution functions $f_{LT}(x)$ for the spin-1 deuteron by using twist-2 relations

> Tensor-polarized twist-3 parton distribution functions (PDFs) $f_{LT}(x)$ are calculated for the spin-1 deuteron by using twist-2 relations, which are similar to the Wandzura-Wilczek relation and the Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule in the spin-1/2 nucleon, together with tensor-polarized twist-2 PDFs $f_{1LL}(x)$. The PDFs are shown for $f_{LT}(x)$ at $Q^2 =2.5$ GeV$^2$, where the tensor-polarized PDFs $f_{1LL}(x)$ are provided. The $x$-dependence of $f_{LT}(x)$ is similar to $f_{1LL}(x)$, and the magnitude of $f_{LT}(x)$ is roughly of the order of $f_{1LL}(x)$. In experiments at the Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility (JLab), higher-twist effects could be sizable because $Q^2$ values are not very large in comparison with the hadronic scale of 1 GeV$^2$. Therefore, the JLab experiments could provide a good opportunity to investigate the twist-3 distributions $f_{LT}(x)$ in addition to the twist-2 ones $f_{1LL}(x)$. Furthermore, these tensor-polarized PDFs could be investigated at future Electron-Ion Colliders (EICs) and hadron accelerator facilities such as the Fermi National Accelerator Laboratory (Fermilab), the Nuclotron-based Ion Collider fAcility (NICA), and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC).

## Key claim

The tensor-polarized twist-3 parton distribution functions \(f_{LT}(x)\) for the spin-1 deuteron are calculated from the twist-2 distributions \(f_{1LL}(x)\) using a Wandzura-Wilczek-like relation that neglects dynamical twist-3 terms, so the results represent only the twist-2 contribution to \(f_{LT}(x)\) and not the full twist-3 function.

## Method

The authors use the Wandzura-Wilczek-like relation \(f_{LT}^{\, q+}(x_D)= \frac{3}{2} \int^1_{x_D} \frac{dy_D}{y_D} \, f_{1LL}^{\, q+}(y_D)\) derived from nonlocal operator product expansion, together with a parametrization of the tensor-polarized twist-2 PDFs \(\delta_T q^D(x)\) and \(\delta_T \bar q^D(x)\) at \(Q^2 = 2.5\) GeV\(^2\) from an earlier analysis that explains the HERMES \(b_1\) data. The twist-2 distributions \(f_{1LL}^{\, q/D+}(x)\) are obtained from these parametrized PDFs via \(f_{1LL}^{\, q/D+}(x) = -\frac{2}{3} [\delta_T q^D(x) + \delta_T \bar q^D(x)]\), and then the twist-3 distributions \(f_{LT}^{\, q/D+}(x)\) are computed by numerical integration of the WW-like relation.

## Key numbers

* The tensor-polarized PDFs \(\delta_T q^D(x)\) and \(\delta_T \bar q^D(x)\) are given at \(Q^2 = 2.5\) GeV\(^2\), the average \(Q^2\) of the HERMES data.
* The parametrization parameters for \(\delta_T w(x) = a x^b (1-x)^c (x_0-x)\) are: \(a = 0.221\), \(\alpha_{\bar q} = 3.20\), \(b = 0.648\), \(c = 1\), \(x_0 = 0.221\).
* The BC-like sum rule integrals for \(f_{2LT}^{\, q/D+}(x)\) become of the order of \(10^{-5}\) when integrated from \(x_{\min} = 10^{-9}\).
* The distributions are shown for \(x\) in the range \(0 \le x \le 2\), where the Bjorken variable \(x = Q^2/(2 M_N \nu)\) is used.

## Context

This work extends the Wandzura-Wilczek relation and Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule, originally developed for spin-1/2 nucleons, to the spin-1 deuteron case. It provides the first numerical estimate of the tensor-polarized twist-3 distributions \(f_{LT}(x)\), which could be measured in upcoming JLab experiments on the tensor-polarized deuteron (JLab \(b_1\) proposal) and at future facilities such as the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), Fermilab, NICA, and LHC. The results enable experimental searches for higher-twist effects in inclusive DIS, semi-inclusive DIS, and Drell-Yan processes with tensor-polarized deuteron targets.
