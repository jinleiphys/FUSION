# Combined Constraints on the Equation of State of Dense Neutron-Rich Matter from Terrestrial Experiments and Observations of Neutron Stars

> Within the parameter space of equation of state (EOS) of dense neutron-rich matter limited by existing constraints mainly from terrestrial nuclear experiments, we investigate how the neutron star maximum mass $M_{\rm{max}}>2.01\pm0.04$ M$_\odot$, radius $10.62<R_{\rm{1.4}}< 12.83$ km and tidal deformability $Λ_{1.4}\leq800$ of canonical neutron stars all together constrain the EOS of dense neutron-rich nucleonic matter. While the 3-D parameter space of $K_{\rm{sym}}$ (curvature of nuclear symmetry energy), $J_{\rm{sym}}$ and $J_0$ (skewness of the symmetry energy and EOS of symmetric nuclear matter, respectively) are narrowed down significantly by the observational constraints, more data are needed to pin down the individual values of $K_{\rm{sym}}$, $J_{\rm{sym}}$ and $J_0$ with quantified uncertainties. The $J_0$ largely controls the maximum mass of neutron stars. While the EOS with $J_0=0$ is sufficiently stiff to support neutron stars as massive as 2.37 M$_{\odot}$, to support the hyperthetical ones as massive as 2.74 M$_{\odot}$ (composite mass of GW170817) requires $J_0$ to be larger than its currently known maximum value of about 400 MeV and beyond the causality limit. The upper limit on the tidal deformability of $Λ_{1.4}=800$ from the recent observation of GW170817 is found to provide upper limits on some EOS parameters consistent with but far less restrictive than the existing constraints of other observables studied.

## Key claim

Within the parameter space of the equation of state (EOS) of dense neutron-rich matter already limited by terrestrial nuclear experiments, the authors investigate how the combined astrophysical constraints of neutron star maximum mass, radius, and tidal deformability further narrow the allowed 3-dimensional parameter space of the curvature of the symmetry energy (\(K_{\rm{sym}}\)), the skewness of the symmetry energy (\(J_{\rm{sym}}\)), and the skewness of the symmetric nuclear matter EOS (\(J_0\)), though they note that more data are needed to pin down individual values with quantified uncertainties.

## Method

The authors parameterize the EOS of symmetric nuclear matter and the symmetry energy using expansions in density up to third order, treating the coefficients \(K_{\rm{sym}}\), \(J_{\rm{sym}}\), and \(J_0\) as free parameters to be constrained. They fix the incompressibility \(K_0\), symmetry energy at saturation \(E_{\rm{sym}}(\rho_0)\), and slope parameter \(L\) at their most probable empirical values, then explore intersections of constant surfaces for \(M_{\rm{max}}=2.01\) M\(_\odot\), \(R_{\rm{1.4}}=10.62\) km, \(R_{\rm{1.4}}=12.83\) km, and \(\Lambda_{1.4}=800\) in the 3-D parameter space of \(K_{\rm{sym}}\), \(J_{\rm{sym}}\), and \(J_0\). The crust-core transition density and pressure are calculated using a thermodynamical approach, and the core EOS is constructed for non-rotating, charge-neutral neutron stars consisting of \(npe\mu\) particles at \(\beta\)-equilibrium.

## Key numbers

*   The neutron star maximum mass constraint used is \(M_{\rm{max}} > 2.01 \pm 0.04\) M\(_\odot\).
*   The radius constraint for a 1.4 M\(_\odot\) canonical neutron star is \(10.62 < R_{\rm{1.4}} < 12.83\) km.
*   The tidal deformability constraint for a 1.4 M\(_\odot\) neutron star is \(\Lambda_{1.4} \leq 800\).
*   An EOS with \(J_0 = 0\) can support a neutron star as massive as 2.37 M\(_\odot\).
*   Supporting a hypothetical neutron star as massive as 2.74 M\(_\odot\) (the composite mass of GW170817) requires \(J_0\) to be larger than about 400 MeV, which is beyond the causality limit.
*   The currently known uncertainty ranges for the EOS parameters are: \(-400 \leq K_{\rm{sym}} \leq 100\) MeV, \(-200 \leq J_{\rm{sym}} \leq 800\) MeV, and \(-800 \leq J_0 \leq 400\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous constraints on the EOS of dense neutron-rich matter from terrestrial nuclear experiments by incorporating simultaneous astrophysical constraints from neutron star masses, radii, and the tidal deformability from the GW170817 event. It builds upon prior analyses that constrained the incompressibility \(K_0\) (Shlomo 2006, Piekarewicz 2010), the symmetry energy at saturation \(E_{\rm{sym}}(\rho_0)\) and its slope \(L\) (Li 2013, Oertel 2017), and the high-density behavior of symmetric nuclear matter from heavy-ion collisions (Danielewicz 2002). The study enables future work to combine more independent observables to determine the individual values of \(K_{\rm{sym}}\), \(J_{\rm{sym}}\), and \(J_0\) with quantified uncertainties.
