# Two neutron decay from the $2_1^+$ state of $^6$He

> Decay mode of the $2_1^+$ resonant state of $^6$He populated by the $^6$He breakup reaction by $^{12}$C at 240 MeV/nucleon is investigated. The continuum-discretized coupled-channels method is adopted to describe the formation of the $2_1^+$ state, whereas its decay is described by the complex-scaled solutions of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation. From analysis of invariant mass spectra with respect to the $α$-$n$ and $n$-$n$ subsystems, coexistence of two decay modes is found. One is the simultaneous decay of two neutrons correlating with each other and the other is the emission of two neutrons to the opposite directions. The latter is found to be free from the final state interaction and suggests existence of a di-neutron in the $2_1^+$ state of $^6$He.

## Key claim

The $2_1^+$ resonant state of $^6$He decays via two coexisting modes: simultaneous emission of two neutrons that correlate with each other (di-neutron decay) and emission of two neutrons to opposite directions (democratic decay). The democratic decay mode is found to be free from final state interactions and suggests existence of a di-neutron in the $2_1^+$ state, but the authors note that the di-neutron decay peak is formed by final state interactions in the decay process rather than direct emission of a pre-existing di-neutron.

## Method

The formation of the $2_1^+$ state of $^6$He was described using the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) for the $^6$He breakup reaction by $^{12}$C at 240 MeV/nucleon, treating the system as an $\alpha+n+n+{}^{12}$C four-body system. The decay of the $2_1^+$ resonance into $\alpha$ and two neutrons was described using complex-scaled solutions of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation (CSLS). The double differential breakup cross sections with respect to the relative energies of the $\alpha$-$n$ and $n$-$n$ subsystems were calculated to analyze the decay modes.

## Key numbers

- The $2_1^+$ resonance of $^6$He was obtained at 0.98 MeV above the three-body threshold with a decay width of 0.27 MeV in the present calculation.
- The resonant part of $d\sigma/d\varepsilon_{\alpha\mbox{-}n}$ shows a peak around 0.5 MeV, about half the total energy of the $2_1^+$ state.
- The nonresonant part of $d\sigma/d\varepsilon_{\alpha\mbox{-}n}$ shows a peak around 0.7 MeV corresponding to the energy of $^5$He.
- In $d\sigma/d\varepsilon_{n\mbox{-}n}$, a first peak appears around 0.2 MeV suggesting di-neutron decay, and a second peak around 0.8 MeV suggesting democratic decay.
- The $2_1^+$ state was populated by $^6$He breakup on $^{12}$C at 240 MeV/nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the CSLS analysis of Kikuchi et al. (2009, 2010) on the Coulomb breakup of $^6$He by $^{208}$Pb, which found dominance of sequential decay through $^5$He for the $1^-$ nonresonant state. The present study incorporates a more realistic reaction model (CDCC) to describe the formation of the $2_1^+$ resonance, in contrast to the earlier work that simplified the breakup process by a one-step electric dipole transition. The findings enable future unified studies of formation and decay of particle-unbound states in three-body systems, and the democratic decay mode provides a new probe of di-neutron correlations in nuclear structure.
