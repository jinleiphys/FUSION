# The Long and Short of Nuclear Effective Field Theory Expansions

> Nonperturbative effective field theory calculations for NN scattering seem to break down at rather low momenta. By examining several toy models, we clarify how effective field theory expansions can in general be used to properly separate long- and short-range effects. We find that one-pion exchange has a large effect on the scattering phase shift near poles in the amplitude, but otherwise can be treated perturbatively. Analysis of a toy model that reproduces 1S0 NN scattering data rather well suggests that failures of effective field theories for momenta above the pion mass can be due to short-range physics rather than the treatment of pion exchange. We discuss the implications this has for extending the applicability of effective field theories.

# The Long and Short of Nuclear Effective Field Theory Expansions

## Key claim

Nonperturbative effective field theory calculations for nucleon-nucleon scattering appear to break down at rather low momenta, but this failure can be due to short-range physics rather than the treatment of pion exchange, provided the expansion is properly reordered around the true scattering length or pole position rather than the short-distance scattering length.

## Method

The authors analyze several quantum mechanical toy models of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, first with only short-range interactions and then with a delta-shell potential at radius 1/m_pi as a caricature of one-pion exchange. They compare two expansion schemes, the Weinberg approach (iterating the potential to all orders) and the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) approach (expanding perturbatively about the critical point of infinite scattering length), and derive a general algorithm for fixing the low-energy constants in the KSW expansion by expanding each contact coupling in powers of the small momentum scale Q.

## Key numbers

- The toy pion strength parameter Lambda_pi is taken as 500 MeV to make the delta-shell potential comparable to one-pion exchange, which is approximately 30% of the critical value needed to give a bound state (critical coupling g_pi = 1).
- The pion mass is m_pi = 140 MeV, the pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV, and the axial coupling g_A = 1.29, giving the scale Lambda_NN = 16 pi f_pi^2 / (g_A^2 M) ≈ 300 MeV.
- In all examples, the scattering length is tuned to a = -23 fm, similar to the 1S0 channel of nucleon-nucleon scattering.
- The leading-order KSW amplitude has a pole at p_star = i / (short-distance scattering length), while the exact amplitude has a pole at p_star ≈ i / a; the difference between 1/(short-distance scattering length) and 1/a is of order Q^2.

## Context

This work extends the debate between the Weinberg power-counting scheme (Weinberg, 1990) and the alternative KSW expansion (Kaplan, Savage, and Wise, 1998) for nuclear effective field theory. The authors clarify that both schemes differ only by terms considered higher order in the KSW approach when the renormalization scale is chosen sufficiently high, and that the KSW expansion requires reordering around the true scattering length to avoid failure near poles in the amplitude. The analysis enables a systematic procedure for fixing low-energy constants in the KSW expansion, which is expected to be valuable for many-nucleon systems where iterating extended interactions to all orders is numerically intensive.
