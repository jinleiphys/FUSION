# Consistent analysis of neutral- and charged-current (anti)neutrino scattering off carbon

> Good understanding of the cross sections for (anti)neutrino scattering off nuclear targets in the few-GeV energy region is a prerequisite for the correct interpretation of results of ongoing and planned oscillation experiments. To clarify a possible source of disagreement between recent measurements of the cross sections on carbon, we analyze the available data within an approach based on the realistic spectral function of carbon, treating neutral-current elastic (NCE) and charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) processes on equal footing. We show that the axial mass from the shape analysis of the MiniBooNE data is in good agreement with the results reported by the BNL E734 and NOMAD Collaborations. However, the combined analysis of the NCE and CCQE data does not seem to support the contribution of multinucleon final states being large enough to explain the normalization of the MiniBooNE-reported cross sections.

## Key claim

The spectral function approach, using an effective axial mass of 1.23 GeV to account for multinucleon contributions, reproduces the shape of MiniBooNE neutral-current elastic and charged-current quasielastic cross sections but underestimates their absolute normalization by 20%; this discrepancy is more likely due to underestimated flux uncertainty in the MiniBooNE data than to large contributions from two- and multinucleon final states.

## Method

The author analyzed neutral-current elastic (NCE) and charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) neutrino and antineutrino scattering off carbon using a spectral function approach based on the local density approximation, treating both interaction channels on equal footing. The hole spectral function combined shell structure with correlation contributions from uniform nuclear matter, and an effective axial mass of 1.23 GeV was applied to account for multinucleon processes. The calculated total cross sections and flux-averaged differential cross sections dσ/dQ² were compared to data from the BNL E734, MiniBooNE, and NOMAD experiments.

## Key numbers

- MiniBooNE CCQE event sample: 146070 events passing cuts; NCE event sample: 94531 candidate events surviving cuts
- MiniBooNE average neutrino energy: 788 MeV; BNL E734 mean neutrino energy: 1.3 GeV, mean antineutrino energy: 1.2 GeV
- NOMAD mean neutrino energy: 25.9 GeV, mean antineutrino energy: 17.6 GeV; total of 14021 neutrino and 2237 antineutrino CCQE events recorded
- Spectral function calculations underestimate MiniBooNE-reported cross sections by a factor of 1.2, consistent with the MiniBooNE normalization discrepancy of 1.21 ± 0.24
- NOMAD axial mass from total CCQE cross section: 1.05 ± 0.02 (stat) ± 0.06 (syst) GeV; from shape analysis of double-track events: 1.07 ± 0.06 (stat) ± 0.07 (syst) GeV
- Spectral function approach yields NOMAD total cross sections higher by ~6% than the NOMAD best fit, within the ~8% (neutrino) and ~11% (antineutrino) systematic uncertainties
- Scattering off correlated nucleon pairs yields ~6% of the inclusive cross section in NOMAD kinematics
- MiniBooNE flux possibly underestimated by 9.2 ± 3.5% according to Nieves et al. (2011) using M_A = 1.077 GeV in a local relativistic Fermi gas model

## Context

This work extends the spectral function approach of Benhar et al. (2005, 2006) for carbon, previously validated against electron scattering data, to simultaneously describe NCE and CCQE neutrino scattering. It addresses the discrepancy between MiniBooNE and NOMAD cross section measurements, which had been attributed by some authors to multinucleon final states. The analysis enables a consistent comparison of axial mass determinations from shape versus total cross section analyses, and suggests that the MiniBooNE flux uncertainty, rather than nuclear effects, may reconcile the data, a possibility also raised by Kopp (2007) and supported by the flux underestimation found by Nieves et al. (2011).
