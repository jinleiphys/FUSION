# Mass measurement of 56Sc reveals a small A=56 odd-even mass staggering, implying a cooler accreted neutron star crust

> We present the mass excesses of 52-57Sc, obtained from recent time-of-flight nuclear mass measurements at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory at Michigan State University. The masses of 56Sc and 57Sc were determined for the first time with atomic mass excesses of -24.85(59)(+0 -54) MeV and -21.0(1.3) MeV, respectively, where the asymmetric uncertainty for 56Sc was included due to possible contamination from a long-lived isomer. The 56Sc mass indicates a small odd-even mass staggering in the A = 56 mass-chain towards the neutron drip line, significantly deviating from trends predicted by the global FRDM mass model and favoring trends predicted by the UNEDF0 and UNEDF1 density functional calculations. Together with new shell-model calculations of the electron-capture strength function of 56Sc, our results strongly reduce uncertainties in model calculations of the heating and cooling at the 56Ti electron-capture layer in the outer crust of accreting neutron stars. We found that, in contrast to previous studies, neither strong neutrino cooling nor strong heating occurs in this layer. We conclude that Urca cooling in the outer crusts of accreting neutron stars that exhibit superbursts or high temperature steady-state burning, which are predicted to be rich in A=56 nuclei, is considerably weaker than predicted. Urca cooling must instead be dominated by electron capture on the small amounts of adjacent odd-A nuclei contained in the superburst and high temperature steady-state burning ashes. This may explain the absence of strong crust Urca cooling inferred from the observed cooling light curve of the transiently accreting x-ray source MAXI J0556-332.

## Key claim

The mass measurement of 56Sc reveals a small odd-even mass staggering in the A=56 mass chain, which, when combined with new shell-model calculations of the electron-capture strength function, rules out both the strong neutrino cooling predicted by the HFB-21 mass model and the strong heating predicted by the FRDM mass model at the 56Ti electron-capture layer in accreting neutron star crusts. The result is that neither strong cooling nor strong heating occurs in this layer, with heating limited to less than 0.02 MeV per accreted nucleon, contingent on the 1+ ground state assignment for 56Sc being correct or thermally populated.

## Method

Time-of-flight mass measurements were performed at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory at Michigan State University, using a 140 MeV/u 82Se beam fragmented on a beryllium target, with fragments transmitted through the A1900 fragment separator and the S800 spectrograph. The ion rest mass was determined from time-of-flight measured over a 60.6 m flight path and magnetic rigidity determined from position measurements, calibrated using 20 reference nuclei with masses known to better than 100 keV. New shell-model calculations of the 56Sc electron-capture strength function were performed using the GXPF1A Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- Atomic mass excess of 56Sc: -24.85(59)(+0 -54) MeV, where the asymmetric uncertainty accounts for possible contamination from a long-lived isomer with predicted excitation energy of 540 keV
- Atomic mass excess of 57Sc: -21.0(1.3) MeV, determined for the first time
- QEC staggering for 56Sc: 2.3(+1.3 -2.4) MeV (using FRDM 56Ca mass) or 2.1(+1.3 -2.4) MeV (using HFB-21 56Ca mass)
- Heating from the 56Ti electron-capture layer: less than 0.02 MeV per accreted nucleon, with possible weak cooling of 0.002 MeV per nucleon
- Measurement precision: delta m/m approximately 1x10^-5

## Context

This work extends the time-of-flight mass measurement program at NSCL (Matoš 2012, Estradé 2011) and directly addresses the large uncertainties highlighted by Schatz (2013) regarding the 56Ti electron-capture layer in accreting neutron star crusts. The results rule out the rapid increase in odd-even staggering predicted by the FRDM mass model (Möller 1995) and favor the more constant staggering predicted by energy density functional calculations (Kortelainen 2010, 2012). The finding that Urca cooling in A=56-dominated crusts is much weaker than previously predicted may explain the absence of strong crust Urca cooling inferred from the cooling light curve of MAXI J0556-332 (Homan 2014, Deibel 2015), and now makes it crucial to reliably predict the abundance of odd-A nuclei produced in thermonuclear burning on accreting neutron stars.
