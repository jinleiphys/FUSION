# A theory of baryon resonances at large N_c

> At large number of colors, N_c quarks in baryons are in a mean field of definite space and flavor symmetry. We write down the general Lorentz and flavor structure of the mean field, and derive the Dirac equation for quarks in that field. The resulting baryon resonances exhibit an hierarchy of scales: The crude mass is O(N_c), the intrinsic quark excitations are O(1), and each intrinsic quark state entails a finite band of collective excitations that are split as O(1/N_c). We build a (new) theory of those collective excitations, where full dynamics is represented by only a few constants. In a limiting (but unrealistic) case when the mean field is spherically-and flavor-symmetric, our classification of resonances reduces to the SU(6) classification of the old non-relativistic quark model. Although in the real world N_c is only three, we obtain a good accordance with the observed resonance spectrum up to 2 GeV.

## Key claim

The authors develop a new theory of baryon resonances based on the large-\(N_c\) limit, where the mean field in baryons spontaneously breaks separate \(SU(3)_{\text{flavor}}\) and \(SO(3)_{\text{space}}\) symmetry down to a combined \(SU(2)_{\text{iso+space}}\) symmetry, producing rotational bands around each intrinsic quark level. Although the real world has \(N_c = 3\), the authors claim good accordance with the observed resonance spectrum up to 2 GeV, with the hedgehog symmetry of the mean field being the key distinguishing feature from the nonrelativistic quark model.

## Method

The authors write down the general Lorentz and flavor structure of the mean mesonic field (scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, axial, and tensor) compatible with the \(SU(2)_{\text{iso+space}}\) hedgehog symmetry, and derive the Dirac equation for \(u,d\) and \(s\) quarks in that field. They classify intrinsic quark levels by grand spin \(K^P\) for \(u,d\) quarks and by \(J^P\) for \(s\) quarks, then construct the theory of collective rotational excitations by quantizing slow rotations in flavor and ordinary space, treating the rotational bands as arising from a given intrinsic one-quark excitation.

## Key numbers

* The crude baryon mass is of order \(N_c\), intrinsic quark excitations are of order \(1\), and collective excitation splittings are of order \(1/N_c\).
* The string tension used in the illustrative example is \(\sigma = (0.44\ \text{GeV})^2\).
* The observed resonance spectrum is reproduced up to 2 GeV.
* Decays with transition from one quark level to another are of order \(1\); decays inside the same rotational band are of order \(1/N_c^2\).
* Total widths of baryons in the rotational band of the ground state (e.g., \(\Delta\)-resonance) are of order \(1/N_c^2\), while total widths of all excited baryons are of order \(1\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark-soliton model of Diakonov (1986) by removing the previous limitation that the mean field is exclusively pseudoscalar, and focuses on baryon resonances rather than ground states. It provides a bridge between three-quark models (which ignore \(Q\bar Q\) pairs) and solitonic approaches such as the Skyrme model (Skyrme 1961) and holographic QCD (Sakai 2004), enabling a unified description of light and heavy baryon spectra. The approach enables extraction of the intrinsic quark Dirac spectrum from experimental baryon data rather than from a specific dynamical model.
