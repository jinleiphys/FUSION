# Solving Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space

> We develop a new method of solving Bethe-Salpeter (BS) equation in Minkowski space. It is based on projecting the BS equation on the light-front (LF) plane and on the Nakanishi integral representation of the BS amplitude. This method is valid for any kernel given by the irreducible Feynman graphs. For massless ladder exchange, our approach reproduces analytically the Wick-Cutkosky equation. For massive ladder exchange, the numerical results coincide with the ones obtained by Wick rotation.

## Key claim

The method solves the Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space without Wick rotation by combining a light-front projection with the Nakanishi integral representation, and is valid for any kernel given by irreducible Feynman graphs, though the paper only demonstrates numerical results for the ladder kernel.

## Method

The authors project the Bethe-Salpeter equation onto the light-front plane and substitute the Nakanishi integral representation for the BS amplitude, which removes the singularities present in the original Minkowski-space equation. This yields an equivalent integral equation for the weight function \(g(\gamma,z)\), which is solved by expanding on a spline basis over a compact domain \(\Omega=[0,\gamma_{max}]\times[-1,+1]\) and solving the resulting generalized eigenvalue matrix equation. The BS amplitude and light-front wave function are then reconstructed from the weight function using the Nakanishi representation.

- A value \(\varepsilon=10^{-4}\) is sufficient to ensure 4-digit stability on \(\alpha\) for a system with \(B=1.0\) and \(\mu=0.5\) (Figure 1).
- The accuracy in eigenvalues is reached with \(N_{\gamma}=1\), meaning the solution is mostly one-dimensional.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kusaka and Williams (1995) who used the Nakanishi representation but only for the ladder kernel, and contrasts with the Wick rotation method of Wick (1954) and Cutkosky (1954) which loses the original BS amplitude in Minkowski space. The method enables calculation of physical observables like form factors that require the Minkowski-space amplitude, and the authors state it will be applied to the cross-box kernel in a subsequent paper.
