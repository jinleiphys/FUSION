# Effects of elastic and inelastic NN scattering cross sections on pion-/pion+ ratios in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies

> Based on the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model and the scaling model according to nucleon effective mass, effects of elastic and inelastic NN scattering cross sections on pion-/pion+ in the neutron-rich reaction of Ca48+Ca48 at a beam energy of 400 MeV/nucleon are studied. It is found that cross-section effects of both NN elastic and inelastic scatterings affect Delta_1232, pion- and pion+ production, as well as the value of pion-/pion+.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that both elastic and inelastic nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering cross sections, when reduced in-medium via a scaling model based on nucleon effective mass, significantly affect the \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\) ratio in neutron-rich heavy-ion collisions. The abstract glosses over the condition that the reduction factor \(R_{medium}\) depends on density, momentum, and isospin asymmetry, and that the effect on \(\pi^{-}\) production is more pronounced than on \(\pi^{+}\) due to the Coulomb force partially canceling the reduction for proton-proton pairs.

## Method
The study uses the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model with a momentum-dependent mean-field potential. The in-medium NN elastic scattering cross section is reduced from its free-space value by a factor \(R_{medium} = (\mu_{NN}^{\ast}/\mu_{NN})^{2}\), where \(\mu_{NN}^{\ast}\) and \(\mu_{NN}\) are the reduced masses in medium and free space, respectively. The same reduction factor is applied to the NN inelastic scattering cross section for simplicity, and the \(NR \rightarrow NN\) cross section is determined by detailed balance. The reaction simulated is \(^{48}\text{Ca} + ^{48}\text{Ca}\) at a beam energy of 400 MeV/nucleon, with three cases compared: full free NN cross sections (ff), reduced elastic plus free inelastic (mf), and full reduced cross sections (mm).

## Key numbers
- The reduction factor \(R_{medium}\) for nn, np, and pp colliding pairs is shown at densities of 0.5, 1.0, and 2.0 times normal nuclear matter density \(\rho_0\) and momenta of 50, 100, and 200 MeV/c, with asymmetry \(\delta = 0.2\).
- At \(\rho/\rho_0 = 2.0\) and momentum \(p = 200\) MeV/c, the reduction factor \(R_{medium}\) for nn pairs increases rapidly with asymmetry \(\delta\), while for pp pairs it decreases slowly with \(\delta\).
- For a central Au+Au reaction at 400 MeV/nucleon, using the full reduced NN cross sections (mm) compared to the reduced elastic plus free inelastic case (mf), the \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\) ratio increases by about 7% and the charged-pion multiplicity increases by about 30%.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of pion production as a probe of the high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy, as discussed by LiBA02, Yong06, and Xiao09. It specifically addresses the uncertainty in in-medium NN scattering cross sections, which previous transport models often treated as free-space values or used different forms, as noted by DiToro2, Lar01, and Lar03. The results indicate that cross-section effects must be carefully disentangled from symmetry energy effects before extracting the high-density symmetry energy from \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\) ratios, and they motivate parallel investigations of pre-equilibrium nucleon emission and N/Z ratios.
