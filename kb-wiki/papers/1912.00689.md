# Heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient from the lattice

> We report progress towards computing the heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient from the lattice correlator of two chromoelectric fields attached to a Polyakov loop in pure SU(3) gauge theory. Using a multilevel algorithm and tree-level improvement, we study the behavior of the diffusion coefficient as a function of temperature in the wide range $1.1 < T/Tc < 10^4$.

# Heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient from the lattice

## Key claim
The heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient kappa divided by T^3 shows a clear temperature dependence across the range 1.1 < T/Tc < 10^4 in pure SU(3) gauge theory, decreasing at higher temperatures as expected from perturbation theory, but the extraction relies on a simple ansatz for the spectral function and the results are presented as ranges rather than precise values.

## Method
The authors computed the Euclidean correlator of two chromoelectric fields attached to a Polyakov loop on the lattice using the Wilson gauge action with a multilevel algorithm and tree-level improvement. They performed simulations at six temperatures (T/Tc = 1.1, 1.5, 3, 6, 10, 10^4) on lattices with temporal extents Nt = 12, 16, 20, 24 and spatial volume 48^3, then took the continuum limit using the three largest Nt values. The heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient kappa was extracted by fitting a spectral function ansatz consisting of a linear transport term at low frequencies matched to the NLO vacuum spectral function at high frequencies.

## Key numbers
- Temperature range studied: 1.1 < T/Tc < 10^4
- At T = 1.1 Tc: 2.28 < kappa/T^3 < 3.57
- At T = 1.5 Tc: 1.99 < kappa/T^3 < 2.69
- At T = 3 Tc: 1.05 < kappa/T^3 < 2.26
- At T = 6 Tc: 0 < kappa/T^3 < 1.5
- At T = 10 Tc: 0 < kappa/T^3 < 0.91
- At T = 10^4 Tc: 0 < kappa/T^3 < 0.39
- Scale setting parameter: Tc sqrt(t0) = 0.2489(14)
- Renormalization coefficient Z_E = 1 + 0.13771856909427574(1) g0^2

## Context
This work extends previous lattice studies of the heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient in pure SU(3) gauge theory, which had either considered only a single temperature (Francis 2015) or a narrow temperature range (Banerjee 2011), and found no significant temperature dependence of kappa/T^3. The results agree with existing determinations at low temperature from Meyer (2010), Francis (2011), Banerjee (2011), Francis (2015), and Brambilla (2019). The observed temperature dependence is phenomenologically important because a constant kappa/T^3 fails to simultaneously explain the elliptic flow parameter v2 for heavy quarks and the nuclear modification factor, as noted by Rapp (2018). The next step is to refine the spectral function ansatz and assess the systematic uncertainty from the scale choice in the NLO matching.
