# Modified Trento initial condition and its impact on collective flows and global polarization in Cu+Au collisions

> Collective flow coefficients and spin polarization are valuable probes of the geometry and flow velocity field of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Using a modified TRENTo initial condition coupled to a (3+1)-dimensional (D) viscous hydrodynamic model CLVisc, we study the directed flow and elliptic flow coefficients of hadrons, together with the global polarization of $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ hyperons in asymmetric Cu+Au collisions. We extend the 2D TRENTo model to the 3D space, and find that the initial tilted geometry of the QGP fireball with respect to the longitudinal direction leads to a decrease of directed flow from positive to negative values with increasing pseudorapidity, an enhancement of elliptic flow at forward and backward pseudorapidities, and a non-monotonic dependence of global polarization on the transverse momentum of hyperons. The initial longitudinal flow velocity gradient further enhances the values of directed flow and global polarization. Our model calculation provides a satisfactory description of the rapidity, transverse momentum, and centrality dependences of the directed flow of charged hadrons in Cu+Au collisions for the first time, and proposes that such asymmetric heavy-ion collisions create a better environment for studying the initial tilted geometry and longitudinal flow field of the QGP than symmetric Au+Au collisions do.

# Modified TRENTo initial condition and its impact on collective flows and global polarization in Cu+Au collisions

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a modified 3D TRENTo initial condition coupled with (3+1)-D viscous hydrodynamics can describe the directed flow of charged hadrons in Cu+Au collisions at 200 GeV for the first time, but only when both a tilted initial geometry and a non-zero longitudinal flow velocity gradient are included; the tilted geometry alone causes directed flow to decrease from positive to negative with increasing pseudorapidity, while the longitudinal velocity gradient further enhances both directed flow and global polarization.

## Method

The authors extended the 2D TRENTo model to 3D by introducing a longitudinal tilt parameter H_t that generates a counterclockwise tilt of the QGP fireball in the reaction plane, and a longitudinal flow velocity parameter f_v that controls the fraction of local center-of-mass rapidity deposited into the QGP medium. They coupled this initial condition to the (3+1)-D viscous hydrodynamic model CLVisc with the NEOS-BQS equation of state, using specific shear viscosity C_eta_v = 0.08 and baryon diffusion coefficient C_B = 0.4, and computed hadron spectra via the Cooper-Frye formalism with resonance decays included.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass nucleon-nucleon collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV for Cu+Au collisions
- Woods-Saxon radius parameters: R_0 = 4.20 fm for Cu (A=63) and R_0 = 6.38 fm for Au (A=197), both with diffuseness d_0 = 0.546 fm and average nucleon density rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}
- TRENTo parameters: nucleon width w = 0.5 fm, fluctuation parameter k = 1, entropy deposition exponent p = 1 (wounded nucleon model)
- Tilt parameter: H_t = 0.8b/fm, where b is impact parameter; longitudinal tilt function parameter eta_t = 8.0
- Longitudinal flow velocity parameter: f_v = 0.05 (non-zero, beyond Bjorken flow)
- Hydrodynamic starting time: tau_0 = 0.6 fm; specific shear viscosity C_eta_v = 0.08; baryon diffusion coefficient C_B = 0.4
- Freezeout energy density: epsilon_frz = 0.4 GeV/fm^3
- Center-of-mass rapidity of QGP in Cu+Au collisions: y_CM = 0.34 for 0-10% centrality, decreasing to 0.11 for 60-80% centrality
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section: sigma_NN_inel = 42 mb at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV

## Context

This work extends the 3D TRENTo initial condition approach previously applied to Au+Au collisions by Jiang et al. (2021, 2023) to asymmetric Cu+Au collisions, where the initial geometry differs fundamentally from symmetric systems. The study builds on earlier proposals by Bozek et al. (2010, 2012, 2016) that a tilted QGP fireball explains rapidity-odd directed flow, and by Shen et al. (2020) and Ryu et al. (2021) on the importance of longitudinal flow velocity gradients. The authors note that while predictions for v_1 in Cu+Au collisions existed from Bozek et al. (2012), Nakamura et al. (2022), and transport models by Chen et al. (2005) and Voronyuk et al. (2014), no satisfactory description of experimental data from STAR (2017) had been achieved prior to this work. The results suggest that asymmetric Cu+Au collisions provide a better environment than symmetric Au+Au collisions for constraining the initial tilted geometry and longitudinal flow field of the QGP.
