# Pion Valence-Quark TMD from Continuum Schwinger Function Methods and Gaussian GTMD

> We employ the continuum Schwinger function method to investigate the unpolarized valence-quark transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution function (TMD) of the pion at the hadron scale. The first seventeen generalized Mellin-transverse moments, constructed from lightlike and transverse vectors, are computed and found to be well described by a factorized ansatz, in which the longitudinal component coincides with the distribution function (DF) and the transverse momentum follows a Gaussian form. The Gaussianity relation between the mean and mean-squared transverse momenta is satisfied with approximately $99\%$ accuracy in our numerical results, with the mean-squared transverse momentum equal to $0.231\,\text{GeV}^2$. Using the extracted TMD, we test the hypothesis that the quark's transverse spatial distribution also follows a Gaussian form and find that the resulting electromagnetic form factor is in good agreement with existing data. These results indicate that the intrinsic transverse-momentum and transverse-spatial distributions of valence quarks in the pion can be accurately approximated by a Gaussian ansatz, supporting its use in phenomenological analyses and experimental fits.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the intrinsic transverse-momentum distribution of valence quarks in the pion can be accurately approximated by a Gaussian form at the hadron scale, with the caveat that this conclusion is established only for the nonperturbative region where \(k_T \lesssim \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\), and the perturbative \(1/k_T^2\) tail is excluded from the analysis.

## Method

The authors employed the continuum Schwinger function method (CSM) within the rainbow-ladder truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations to compute the first seventeen generalized Mellin-transverse moments \(\mathcal{M}^{n,m}\) of the pion's unpolarized valence-quark TMD at the hadron scale \(\mu_H = 0.331\) GeV. The moments were evaluated using the dressed-quark propagator and pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude obtained self-consistently from the RL kernel with parameters \(D\omega = (0.82\ \text{GeV})^3\) and \(\omega = 0.5\) GeV. A factorized ansatz separating longitudinal and transverse dependence was tested, with the longitudinal component matching the valence-quark DF and the transverse component following a Gaussian form.

## Key numbers

- Mean-squared transverse momentum: \(\langle k_T^2 \rangle = 0.231\ \text{GeV}^2\) at the hadron scale \(\mu_H = 0.331\) GeV.
- Gaussianity ratio \(R_{0,1} = 0.991\), \(R_{1,1} = 0.993\), \(R_{2,1} = 0.998\), indicating deviations from a perfect Gaussian transverse distribution at the level of approximately \(1\%\) or less.
- Mean transverse momentum: \(\langle k_T \rangle = 0.422\ \text{GeV}\) for \(n=0\).
- Higher-order ratios: \(R_{0,3} = 1.037\), \(R_{1,3} = 1.015\), \(R_{2,3} = 0.934\) (deviations within \(\sim 7\%\)); \(R_{0,4} = 1.181\) (deviation \(\sim 18\%\)), \(R_{1,4} = 0.970\) (deviation \(\sim 3\%\)).
- DF parametrization parameter: \(\rho_{\text{DF}} = 0.061\).
- TMD parametrization parameters \(\rho_m\) for \(m=0\) through \(4\): \(\{0.061, 0.145, 0.227, 0.297, 0.340\}\).

## Context

This work extends the continuum Schwinger function method from describing the pion's longitudinal structure (DF and DA) to its three-dimensional transverse structure, providing a QCD-based derivation of the Gaussian transverse-momentum dependence that has been empirically successful in phenomenological analyses of SIDIS and Drell-Yan data (Anselmino 2005, Schweitzer 2010, Anselmino 2015). The result that the Gaussian form emerges dynamically from nonperturbative QCD interactions supports its continued use in phenomenological fits and experimental analyses, while the computed \(\langle k_T^2 \rangle = 0.231\ \text{GeV}^2\) at the hadron scale provides a baseline for future studies incorporating TMD evolution to higher scales for comparison with experimental data.
