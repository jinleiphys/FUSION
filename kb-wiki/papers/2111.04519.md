# Explaining the Cabibbo Angle Anomaly

> The Cabibbo-Cobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) matrix parametrizes the misalignement between the up- and down-quark mass basis in the Standard Model (SM). The observation of first row CKM unitarity violation has recently emerged as a new anomaly of the SM, known as the "Cabibbo Angle Anomaly" (CAA). With current measurements, comparing the elements $V_{ud}$ and $V_{us}$ extracted from beta and kaon decays respectively, the tension with the SM prediction amounts to $\sim$$3\,σ$. Recently, it has been pointed out that this anomaly can also be seen as a discrepancy in the determination of the Fermi constant from muon decay vs $β$ and K decays, once CKM unitarity is assumed. In fact, possible explanations in terms on New Physics fall under two broad classes: contributions to $β$ decay and/or to $μ$ decay. In this proceedings, we discuss these solutions in terms of gauge invariant dimension 6 operators in SMEFT and simplified extensions of the Standard Model. The latter could introduce correlations with other anomalies in the SM, pointing to new and interesting directions for model building.

## Key claim

The observed 3σ deficit in first-row CKM unitarity (the Cabibbo Angle Anomaly) can be explained by new physics affecting either beta decay, muon decay, or both; the proceedings systematically identify which gauge-invariant dimension-6 SMEFT operators and simplified models can provide viable solutions while respecting existing constraints.

## Method

The author classifies possible new-physics explanations into four operator classes: four-fermion operators in muon decay, four-fermion operators in beta decay, modified W–quark couplings, and modified W–lepton couplings. For each class, the analysis identifies which specific SMEFT dimension-6 operators survive constraints from Michel parameters, lepton-flavor-violating decays, meson mixing, electroweak precision observables, and LEP/ATLAS searches. The study then maps these operators onto simplified models (scalar singlets, vector bosons, vector-like fermions, and leptoquarks) and checks for correlations with other flavor anomalies.

## Key numbers

- First-row CKM unitarity sum: |V_ud|² + |V_us|² + |V_ub|² = 0.9985(5), a 3σ tension with the Standard Model prediction.
- For the four-fermion operator Q_ℓℓ^(2112) in muon decay, the CAA prefers C_ℓℓ ≈ −(8 TeV)⁻².
- For the four-fermion operator Q_ℓq^(3)1111 in beta decay, the CAA prefers C_ℓq^(3) ≈ (10 TeV)⁻².
- For the modified W–quark coupling operator Q_ϕq^(3)11, data prefer C_ϕq^(3)11 ≈ −(9 TeV)⁻².

## Context

This proceedings paper extends the analysis of the Cabibbo Angle Anomaly first identified by Belfatto (2019), Grossman (2019), and Coutinho (2019) by performing a systematic SMEFT operator analysis and mapping to simplified models. The work builds on the compilation of Hardy (2020) for superallowed beta decays and the PDG review (Zyla 2020), and it connects the CAA to other flavor anomalies including b→sℓℓ transitions (Capdevila 2020), τ→μνν decays, and Z→bb data, suggesting possible common new-physics explanations.
