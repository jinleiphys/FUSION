# Baryon productions and collective flow of relativistic heavy-ion collisions in the AGS, SPS, RHIC and LHC energy regions($\sqrt{s_{NN}}\leq5$GeV to 5.5 TeV)

> The features of net baryon productions and collective flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at energies reached at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC), BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC), CERN Super Proton Synchrotron (SPS) and BNL Alternating Gradient Synchrotron (AGS) with the model of Non-Uniform Flow Model(NUFM) are systematically studied in this paper. Especially we predict the feature of net baryon productions and collective flow at LHC $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=5500 GeV basing on the detailed study of that at RHIC $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=62.4 and 200GeV. The dependencies of the features of baryon stopping and collective flow on the collision energies and centralities are investigated.

## Key claim

The Non-Uniform Flow Model (NUFM), when corrected for baryon number conservation, can simultaneously describe net proton rapidity distributions from AGS through RHIC energies and predict a deep central dip at LHC energies, with the non-uniformity parameter \(e\) decreasing from 0.82 at AGS to 0.19 at LHC, indicating increasing transparency.

## Method

The authors applied the NUFM, which assumes fireballs are distributed non-uniformly in longitudinal rapidity via an ellipticity parameter \(e\) (0 = maximally non-uniform, 1 = uniform), to fit experimental net-proton rapidity distributions from AGS (Au+Au at \(E_{\rm lab}=2\)–10.8 GeV), SPS (Pb+Pb at 158 GeV), and RHIC (Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=62.4\) and 200 GeV). They then extrapolated the model to LHC (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5500\) GeV) using a fitted logarithmic relation between central rapidity density and beam rapidity, while enforcing baryon number conservation.

## Key numbers

*   At AGS (\(E_{\rm lab}=10.8\) GeV, Au+Au): \(e=0.82\), \(y_{e0}=1.300\), net proton distribution peaks at mid-rapidity.
*   At SPS (\(E_{\rm lab}=158\) GeV, Pb+Pb): \(e=0.61\), \(y_{e0}=2.340\), a dip begins to appear at mid-rapidity.
*   At RHIC (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, Au+Au): \(e=0.31\), \(y_{e0}=5.320\), showing a deep central dip.
*   At LHC (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5500\) GeV, Pb+Pb): predicted \(e=0.19\), \(y_{e0}=7.880\), with a predicted central rapidity density \(dN/dy|_{y=0} \approx 3.63\) (about 1/4 of the value at RHIC 200 GeV).
*   Average rapidity loss \(<\delta y>\) increases from 0.9499 at AGS (10.8 GeV) to 2.3021 at RHIC (200 GeV) and is predicted to be 3.5724 at LHC.
*   The longitudinal flow parameter \(<\beta\gamma>_{\rm L}\) rises from 0.6967 at AGS (10.8 GeV) to 7.7894 at RHIC (200 GeV) and is predicted to be 32.7912 at LHC.

## Context

This work extends the NUFM approach of Feng et al. (previous papers) by correcting for baryon number conservation, which significantly changes the predicted rapidity distributions at RHIC and LHC compared to earlier results. It systematically covers the transition from baryon stopping at AGS to baryon transparency at LHC, complementing models such as the Glauber-type multiple collision approach of Wong (1994) and the thermal/collective flow models of Teaney, Hirano, and others. The predictions for LHC provide a baseline for future experimental measurements of net baryon distributions and collective flow at the highest collision energies.
