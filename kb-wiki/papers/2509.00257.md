# Nonradial oscillations of stratified neutron stars with solid crusts: Mode characterization and tidal resonances in coalescing binaries

> Dynamical tides of neutron stars in the late stages of binary inspirals provide a viable probe into dense matter through gravitational waves, and potentially trigger electromagnetic precursors. We model the tidal response as a set of driven harmonic oscillators, where the natural frequencies are given by the quasinormal modes of a nonrotating neutron star. These modes are calculated in general relativity by applying linear perturbation theory to stellar models that include a solid crust and compositional stratification. For the mode spectrum, we find that the canonical interface mode associated with the crust-core boundary vanishes in stratified neutron stars and is replaced by compositional gravity modes with mixed gravity-interfacial character, driven primarily by strong buoyancy in the outer core. We also find that fluid modes such as the core gravity mode and the fundamental mode can penetrate the crust, and we establish a criterion for such penetration. Regarding the tidal interaction, we find that transfer of binding energy to oscillations is dominated by the fundamental mode despite its frequency being too high to resonate with the tidal forcing. In general, we find that lower-frequency modes induce gravitational-wave phase shifts smaller than $\sim 10^{-3},\rm rad$ for the equation of state we consider. We discover that nonresonant fundamental and crustal shear modes can trigger crust breaking already near the first gravity-mode resonance, while gravity-mode resonance concentrates strain at the base of the crust and may marginally crack it. These results suggest that both resonant and nonresonant excitations can overstress the crust and may channel energy into the magnetosphere prior to merger, potentially powering electromagnetic precursors. Our work represents an important step toward realistic modeling of dynamical tides of neutron stars in multimessenger observations.

## Key claim

The canonical interface mode associated with the crust-core boundary vanishes in stratified neutron stars and is replaced by compositional gravity modes with mixed gravity-interfacial character, driven primarily by strong buoyancy in the outer core; nonresonant fundamental and crustal shear modes can trigger crust breaking already near the first gravity-mode resonance, while gravity-mode resonance concentrates strain at the base of the crust and may marginally crack it.

## Method

The authors computed quasinormal modes of nonrotating neutron stars in general relativity by applying linear perturbation theory to stellar models built with the unified TW99 equation of state, which includes both a solid crust and compositional stratification. They solved the perturbed Einstein equations for polar parity perturbations using the relativistic Lagrangian perturbation formalism, incorporating a Hookian elastic solid model for the crust with shear modulus estimated from a body-centered cubic lattice. The mode spectrum was obtained by searching for frequencies where the amplitude of ingoing gravitational waves at the stellar surface vanishes, and the tidal response was modeled as a set of driven harmonic oscillators using these quasinormal mode frequencies.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of the TW99 EOS: \(M_{\rm TOV} = 2.08\,M_\odot\), consistent with the most massive pulsars observed to date
- For a \(1.35\,M_\odot\) neutron star: radius \(R_{1.35} = 12.29\,\mathrm{km}\) and dimensionless tidal deformability \(\Lambda_{1.4} = 510.0\)
- Shear speed in the crust: \(10^{-3}\) to \(10^{-2}\) of the speed of light for densities \(\sim 10^{8}\) to \(10^{14}\,\rm g/cm^3\)
- Lower-frequency modes induce gravitational-wave phase shifts smaller than \(\sim 10^{-3}\,\rm rad\) for the equation of state considered
- Crust-core interface density: \(\rho_{\rm cc}\simeq 1.03\times 10^{14}\,\rm g/cm^3\); neutron drip density: \(\rho_{\rm nd} \simeq 3.0 \times 10^{11}\,\mathrm{g/cm^{3}}\)
- Weak reaction timescales: modified Urca \(\tau_{\mathrm{M}} \sim 2\,\text{month}/T_9^6\) and direct Urca \(\tau_{\mathrm{D}} \sim 20\,\mathrm{s}/T_9^4\), with \(T_9 \lesssim 0.01\) for inspiralling neutron stars, much longer than oscillation periods of \(10^{-4}\) to \(10^{-1}\,\mathrm{s}\)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron star dynamical tides by using a unified equation of state that includes both a solid crust and compositional stratification, whereas earlier works such as Yu (2016), Counsell (2024), and Kuan (2021) often treated these aspects separately or introduced stratification phenomenologically. The authors employ full relativistic linear perturbation theory without approximations such as the Cowling approximation or Newtonian perturbations on relativistic backgrounds, improving upon earlier mode calculations. The results enable more realistic modeling of dynamical tides in multimessenger observations of coalescing neutron star binaries, particularly for predicting gravitational-wave phase shifts and electromagnetic precursors from crust breaking, building on the mechanism first proposed for interface modes by Tsang (2011) and later extended to gravity and fundamental modes.
