# Dilepton production and off-shell transport dynamics at SIS energies

> Dilepton production in nucleus-nucleus collisions at 1-2 A GeV as well as in elementary pp and pd reactions is studied within the microscopic HSD transport approach which includes the off-shell dynamics of vector mesons explicitly. The study addresses additionally the production of $π^0$ and $η$ mesons since their Dalitz decays provide a sizeable contribution to the dilepton invariant mass spectra up to about 0.5 GeV. Our transport results agree with the TAPS experimental data on $π$ and $η$ multiplicities in C+C collisions from 0.8 to 2 A GeV. We find that the 'DLS-puzzle' - which addresses an underestimation of the $e^+e^-$ yield in the mass range from 0.2 to 0.5 GeV in C+C and Ca+Ca collisions - may be solved when incorporating a stronger bremsstrahlung contribution in line with recent OBE calculations. Moreover, the HSD results with 'enhanced' bremsstrahlung cross sections agree very well with the HADES experimental data for the dilepton mass spectra for C+C at 1 and 2 A GeV, especially when including a collisional broadening in the vector-meson spectral functions. Detailed predictions for dilepton spectra from pp and pn/pd reactions at 1.25 GeV, 2.2 GeV and 3.5 GeV are presented which will allow to verify/falsify the larger bremsstrahlung contributions from the experimental side in the near future.

## Key claim

The 'DLS-puzzle', a long-standing underestimation of the dilepton yield in the mass range 0.2 to 0.5 GeV in C+C and Ca+Ca collisions at 1-2 A GeV, may be resolved when incorporating a stronger bremsstrahlung contribution in line with recent one-boson-exchange (OBE) calculations, provided that collisional broadening in the vector-meson spectral functions is also included.

## Method

The authors employed the microscopic HSD (Hadron-String-Dynamics) transport approach, which includes explicit off-shell dynamics for vector mesons (rho, omega, phi) according to the off-shell transport dynamics formalism. They incorporated novel NN bremsstrahlung cross sections from recent OBE calculations, updated eta production cross sections, and implemented in-medium spectral functions for vector mesons using either collisional broadening alone or a dropping-mass scenario combined with collisional broadening. The model was applied to pp, pd, C+C, and Ca+Ca collisions at SIS energies (1-2 A GeV) and compared to TAPS, DLS, and HADES experimental data.

## Key numbers

* The transport results agree with TAPS experimental data on pi and eta multiplicities in C+C collisions from 0.8 to 2 A GeV.
* The collisional broadening coefficient for the rho meson is alpha_coll approximately 150 MeV, and for the omega meson approximately 70 MeV, extracted from the vector-meson collision rate in C+C and Ca+Ca reactions at 1 and 2 A GeV.
* The in-medium pole mass shift is parametrized as M_0^*(rho_N) = M_0 / (1 + alpha * rho_N / rho_0) with alpha approximately 0.16 for the rho and alpha approximately 0.12 for the omega meson, where rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}.
* Predictions are presented for dilepton spectra from pp and pn/pd reactions at 1.25 GeV, 2.2 GeV, and 3.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous HSD and UrQMD transport studies (Ernst 1998, Bratkovskaya 1998, 1999) that systematically underestimated the DLS data for C+C and Ca+Ca at 1 A GeV, a discrepancy known as the 'DLS-puzzle' since 1999. The study builds on OBE bremsstrahlung calculations by Kaptari and Kampfer (2006) and off-shell transport dynamics developed by Cassing and Juchem (2000, 2001). The results enable future experimental verification or falsification of the enhanced bremsstrahlung contributions using HADES data for elementary pp and pd reactions at 1.25, 2.2, and 3.5 GeV.
