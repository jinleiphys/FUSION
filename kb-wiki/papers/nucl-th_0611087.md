# Lattice Simulations for Light Nuclei: Chiral Effective Field Theory at Leading Order

> We discuss lattice simulations of light nuclei at leading order in chiral effective field theory. Using lattice pion fields and auxiliary fields, we include the physics of instantaneous one-pion exchange and the leading-order S-wave contact interactions. We also consider higher-derivative contact interactions which adjust the S-wave scattering amplitude at higher momenta. By construction our lattice path integral is positive definite in the limit of exact Wigner SU(4) symmetry for any even number of nucleons. This SU(4) positivity and the approximate SU(4) symmetry of the low-energy interactions play an important role in suppressing sign and phase oscillations in Monte Carlo simulations. We assess the computational scaling of the lattice algorithm for light nuclei with up to eight nucleons and analyze in detail calculations of the deuteron, triton, and helium-4.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that lattice simulations of light nuclei using chiral effective field theory at leading order are feasible and produce reasonable binding energies, but only when the lattice cutoff is kept below the chiral symmetry breaking scale (approximately 1.2 GeV), which introduces finite-cutoff errors of the form \((p/\Lambda)^{k_1}(\Lambda/\Lambda_\chi)^{k_2}\) that cannot be removed by a continuum limit.

## Method

The authors formulated the leading-order chiral effective Lagrangian on a Euclidean space-time lattice, including instantaneous one-pion exchange and S-wave contact interactions, with higher-derivative contact interactions added to adjust the S-wave scattering amplitude at higher momenta. They used auxiliary fields (Hubbard-Stratonovich transformations) to render the contact interactions quadratic in nucleon fields, and employed an \(O(a^4)\)-improved action for the nucleon kinetic energy to reduce finite-cutoff errors. The path integral was evaluated via Monte Carlo sampling, with the lattice action constructed to ensure positivity of the path integral in the limit of exact Wigner SU(4) symmetry for any even number of nucleons, thereby suppressing sign and phase oscillations.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: approximately 2.2 MeV (from lattice simulations at leading order, with specific lattice parameters; exact value depends on lattice spacing and volume).
- Triton binding energy: approximately 8.5 MeV (from lattice simulations at leading order, with specific lattice parameters).
- Helium-4 binding energy: approximately 28.3 MeV (from lattice simulations at leading order, with specific lattice parameters).
- Physical constants used: nucleon mass \(m = 938.92\) MeV, pion mass \(m_\pi = 138.08\) MeV, pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV, nucleon axial charge \(g_A = 1.26\).
- Lattice cutoff scale: \(\Lambda_\chi = 4\pi f_\pi \simeq 1.2\) GeV (the scale of spontaneous chiral symmetry breakdown).

## Context

This work extends the nuclear lattice approach previously applied to neutron matter at nonzero temperature (Lee, 2004) and to the triton in pionless effective field theory (Borasoy, 2005), now incorporating one-pion exchange and contact interactions from chiral effective field theory at leading order. It provides a framework that can be systematically improved by including higher-order chiral interactions and enables future studies of larger nuclei and nuclear matter within the same formalism, with a clear connection to the underlying symmetries of QCD.
