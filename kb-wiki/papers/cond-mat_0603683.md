# Sound Velocity and Meissner Effect in Light-heavy Fermion Pairing Systems

> In the frame of a four fermion interaction theory, we investigated the collective excitation in light-heavy fermion pairing systems. When the two species of fermions posses different masses and chemical potentials but keep the same Fermi surface, we found that the sound velocity in superfluids and the Meissner mass or inverse penetration depth in superconductors have the same mass ratio dependence as the ratio of the transition temperature to the zero temperature gap.

## Key claim
For light-heavy fermion pairing systems with equal Fermi surfaces but unequal masses, the sound velocity in superfluids and the inverse penetration depth in superconductors share the same mass-ratio dependence as the ratio of the transition temperature to the zero-temperature gap, specifically in the weak-coupling BCS regime.

## Method
The authors start from a four-fermion interaction Lagrangian with attractive coupling and perform a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce an auxiliary boson field. After imposing equal Fermi surfaces via matching chemical potentials, they derive the mean-field gap equation and transition temperature. They then integrate out gapped fermionic degrees of freedom to obtain an effective action for the phase fluctuation (Goldstone mode), expanding to quadratic order in the phase field to extract the sound velocity. For the charged case, they compute the London penetration depth from the current correlation function.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of transition temperature to zero-temperature gap: \( T_c / \Delta_0 = (e^\gamma / \pi) \times (2\sqrt{\alpha} / (1+\alpha)) \), where \(\alpha = m_b/m_a\) and \(\gamma\) is the Euler constant.
- For the mixed \(^6\)Li and \(^{40}\)K system, the critical temperature is about \(\sqrt{1/7}\) of that for a pure \(^6\)Li system (since \(\alpha \approx 7\)).
- Sound velocity in the superfluid: \( v_s = (v_F / \sqrt{3}) \times (2\sqrt{\alpha} / (1+\alpha)) \), recovering \( v_s = v_F / \sqrt{3} \) for equal masses (\(\alpha=1\)) and tending to zero as \(\alpha \to \infty\).
- Inverse London penetration depth for charged fermions with \(Q_a = Q_b = 1\): \( \lambda_L^{-1} = \sqrt{4\pi n e^2 / m} \times (2\sqrt{\alpha} / (1+\alpha)) \), recovering the standard result for \(\alpha=1\) and diverging to infinity (ideal type-II superconductor) as \(\alpha \to \infty\).
- At low temperature, the specific heat \(c \propto T^3 / v_s^3\); for the mixed \(^6\)Li/\(^{40}\)K system, the specific heat is about 17 times that of a pure \(^6\)Li system at fixed density.

## Context
This work extends the study of breached pairing (interior gap) in light-heavy fermion systems, previously proposed by Baldo et al. (2000) and others, by focusing on the equal-Fermi-surface case where the breached state is ruled out. It builds on the finding by Caldas (2004) that the ratio \(T_c/\Delta_0\) depends only on the mass ratio \(\alpha\). The results enable experimental tests in cold-atom mixtures such as \(^6\)Li and \(^{40}\)K, where measuring the specific heat or penetration depth could verify the predicted mass-ratio dependence. The authors note that for gapless states (breached pairing or LOFF states), the effective theory would yield imaginary sound velocity and penetration depth, as discussed in color superconductivity by Huang and Ren (2005).
