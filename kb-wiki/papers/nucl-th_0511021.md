# NeXSPheRIO results on elliptic flow at RHIC and connection with thermalization

> Elliptic flow at RHIC is computed event-by-event with NeXSPheRIO. Reasonable agreement with experimental results on $v_2(η)$ is obtained. Various effects are studied as well: reconstruction of impact parameter direction, freeze out temperature, equation of state (with or without crossover), emission mecanism.

# NeXSPheRIO results on elliptic flow at RHIC and connection with thermalization

## Key claim

Event-by-event hydrodynamics with NeXSPheRIO can reproduce the shape of elliptic flow $v_2(\eta)$ at RHIC without invoking lack of thermalization, contrary to the suggestion of Hirano et al. that the disagreement between data and smooth-hydrodynamics calculations might be due to incomplete thermalization.

## Method

The authors used the NeXSPheRIO code, which combines the NeXus event generator for fluctuating initial conditions with the SPheRIO smoothed-particle hydrodynamics solver. They ran many events with different initial conditions, computed $v_2(\eta)$ using the known impact parameter angle $\phi_b$, and compared results obtained with different freeze-out temperatures, equations of state (first-order transition versus crossover), and emission mechanisms (freeze-out versus continuous emission). They also examined the effect of reconstructing the impact parameter direction as done experimentally.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature of 135 MeV is favored by comparisons of $v_2(\eta)$ and $d^2N/p_t dp_t$ distributions.
- The theoretical $v_2^b(\eta)$ computed with known impact parameter angle lies below the observed $v_2^{obs}(\eta)$ obtained with reconstructed reaction plane; the observed $v_2^{obs}(\eta)$ can be larger than $v_2^b(\eta)$ due to sine terms in the Fourier decomposition of the azimuthal distribution.
- Smooth initial conditions (averaged over 30 NeXus events) produce a bumpy $v_2$ structure, while event-by-event initial conditions yield a smoother $v_2(\eta)$ shape that agrees with Phobos data.

## Context

This work extends the event-by-event hydrodynamical approach previously applied by the same group to particle distributions, kaon effective temperatures, and HBT interferometry at RHIC. It directly addresses the discrepancy noted by Hirano et al. (2004) between smooth-hydrodynamics calculations and experimental $v_2(\eta)$ data, and contrasts with the partial-thermalization model of Heinz and Kolb. The results show that fluctuating initial conditions, rather than lack of thermalization, can resolve the shape disagreement, a conclusion similar to that reached by Hirano using color glass condensate initial conditions with a cascade afterburner.
