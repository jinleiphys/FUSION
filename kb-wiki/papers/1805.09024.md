# Dynamically integrated transport approach for heavy-ion collisions at high baryon density

> We develop a new dynamical model for high energy heavy-ion collisions in the beam energy region of the highest net-baryon densities on the basis of non-equilibrium microscopic transport model JAM and macroscopic 3+1D hydrodynamics by utilizing a dynamical initialization method. In this model,dynamical fluidization of a system is controlled by the source terms of the hydrodynamic fields. In addition, time dependent core-corona separation of hot regions is implemented. We show that our new model describes multiplicities and mean transverse mass in heavy-ion collisions within a beam energy region of $3<\sqrt{s_{NN}}<30$ GeV. Good agreement of the beam energy dependence of the $K^+/π^+$ ratio is obtained, which is explained by the fact that a part of the system is not thermalized in our core-corona approach.

## Key claim

The authors develop a new hybrid model that dynamically couples the JAM microscopic transport model with 3+1D ideal hydrodynamics via source terms, and show that it describes multiplicities and mean transverse mass in heavy-ion collisions at beam energies between 3 and 30 GeV. The key hedge is that the good agreement of the K+/pi+ ratio is achieved not by assuming full thermalization but by the core-corona approach in which part of the system remains non-thermalized in both space and time.

## Method

The JAM hadronic cascade model (based on hadron and string degrees of freedom) is coupled to ideal hydrodynamics through source terms J^mu and rho that control the dynamical fluidization of the system. Particles are converted into fluid elements when they decay from strings or resonances and the local energy density exceeds a fluidization energy density e_f (default 0.5 GeV/fm^3), implementing a time-dependent core-corona separation; leading particles from string fragmentation are not converted. The hydrodynamic equations are solved with the HLLE algorithm in three spatial dimensions using the EOS-Q equation of state (first-order phase transition between massless quark-gluon phase and hadronic gas with baryon-density-dependent repulsive potential), and fluid elements are converted back to particles via the Cooper-Frye formula at particlization energy density e_p = 0.5 GeV/fm^3.

## Key numbers

* Beam energy range studied: 3 < sqrt(s_NN) < 30 GeV
* Default fluidization energy density e_f = 0.5 GeV/fm^3 (also tested at 0.8 and 1.0 GeV/fm^3)
* Default particlization energy density e_p = 0.5 GeV/fm^3
* Gaussian smearing width sigma = 0.5 fm for particle-to-fluid conversion
* Cell size Delta x = Delta y = Delta z = 0.3 fm; time step Delta t = 0.15 fm/c
* Bag constant B^{1/4} = 235 MeV in EOS-Q; repulsive potential parameter K = 0.45 GeV fm^3 for baryons
* At sqrt(s_NN) = 17.3 GeV, fluid fraction reaches about 70% at t = 6 fm/c in central Pb+Pb collisions
* At sqrt(s_NN) = 6.4 GeV, the sum of energy densities from particles and fluid reaches about 3 GeV/fm^3 at 5 fm/c at the coordinate origin

## Context

This work extends the dynamical initialization approach previously applied to jet energy loss (Okai 2017) and to string-based initialization for RHIC-BES energies (Shen 2017) by simultaneously solving the nonequilibrium transport with hydrodynamics. It contrasts with the UrQMD hybrid model (Steinheimer 2007, Petersen 2008) which uses a fixed switching time, and with three-fluid dynamics (3FD) (Ivanov 2005, 2013) which overestimates strange particles at low energies. The model enables future incorporation of a first-order chiral phase transition in nonequilibrium real-time dynamics, and may help discriminate equations of state relevant for neutron star mergers at densities (5-10) rho_0.
