# Power counting in chiral effective field theory and nuclear binding

> Chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT), as originally proposed by Weinberg, promises a theoretical connection between low-energy nuclear interactions and quantum chromodynamics (QCD). However, the important property of renormalization-group (RG) invariance is not fulfilled in current implementations and its consequences for predicting atomic nuclei beyond two- and three-nucleon systems has remained unknown. In this work we present a first and systematic study of recent RG-invariant formulations of $χ$EFT and their predictions for the binding energies and other observables of selected nuclear systems with mass-numbers up to $A =16$. Specifically, we have carried out ab initio no-core shell-model and coupled cluster calculations of the ground-state energy of $^3$H, $^{3,4}$He, $^{6}$Li, and $^{16}$O using several recent power-counting (PC) schemes at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO), where the subleading interactions are treated in perturbation theory. Our calculations indicate that RG-invariant and realistic predictions can be obtained for nuclei with mass number $A \leq 4$. We find, however, that $^{16}$O is either unbound with respect to the four $α$-particle threshold, or deformed, or both. Similarly, we find that the $^{6}$Li ground-state resides above the $α$-deuteron separation threshold. These results are in stark contrast with experimental data and point to either necessary fine-tuning of all relevant counterterms, or that current state-of-the-art RG-invariant PC schemes at LO in $χ$EFT lack necessary diagrams -- such as three-nucleon forces -- to realistically describe nuclei with mass number $A>4$.

## Key claim

RG-invariant chiral effective field theory interactions can produce realistic binding energies for nuclei with mass number A ≤ 4, but fail to bind 16O and 6Li correctly, suggesting that current leading-order power-counting schemes either require fine-tuning of counterterms or lack necessary three-nucleon forces for heavier systems.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) and coupled cluster (CC) calculations of ground-state energies for 3H, 3,4He, 6Li, and 16O using several recent RG-invariant power-counting schemes at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO), with subleading interactions treated in perturbation theory via a Hellmann-Feynman procedure. The LO potentials included one-pion exchange plus contact terms in S-waves and promoted P-wave contact terms, with low-energy constants calibrated to reproduce experimental scattering lengths, deuteron binding energy, and Nijmegen phase shifts at specific laboratory energies.

## Key numbers

- 3H and 3He ground-state energies at LO and NLO in MWPC agree with experiment nearly exactly, with at most 5% relative differences between different calibration strategies for the 3P2-3F2 channel
- 4He shows first signs of RG-invariant description in MWPC, with larger uncertainties from infrared extrapolations using oscillator frequencies ℏΩ ∈ [35,75] MeV in 25 major oscillator shells
- 16O is either unbound with respect to the four α-particle threshold, or deformed, or both
- 6Li ground-state resides above the α-deuteron separation threshold
- Reliable results for 6Li and 16O obtained only up to Λ ≈ 650 MeV and 600 MeV respectively, due to computational challenges with larger cutoffs

## Context

This work extends the few-body Faddeev-type calculations of 3H and 3He by Nogga (2005) and Song (2017) to heavier nuclei up to A=16 using RG-invariant chiral EFT formulations. The results contradict the successful descriptions of light nuclei achieved with Weinberg power counting (WPC) interactions, which lack RG invariance. The findings enable systematic investigation of whether missing three-nucleon forces or alternative power-counting schemes can resolve the binding problem for A>4 systems, and highlight the need for developing RG-invariant interactions that include three-nucleon forces at leading order.
