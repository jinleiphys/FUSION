# Revisiting inclusive production of J/psi and Upsilon in high-energy gamma-gamma collisions

> The impact of Next-to-Leading Order (NLO) QCD corrections to the differential distributions of J/psi and Upsilon mesons produced inclusively in gamma-gamma collisions is discussed for the kinematical conditions of LEP II for DELPHI and at the future Circular Electron-Positron Collider (CEPC). We take into account all sizeable contributions at LO in v^2 in NRQCD factorisation: 1) pure QED process gamma + gamma -> J/psi(3S^1_1) + γup to alpha^3 alpha_s, 2) single-resolved-photon contributions up to alpha_s^4, 3) gamma+gamma -> J/psi + c c-bar up to alpha^2 alpha_s and 4) gamma+gamma -> J/psi + ggg up to alpha^2 alpha_s^3. We will also discuss the pure QED process as a contribution to the exclusive production in ultra-peripheral heavy-ion collisions (UPC) at the LHC.

## Key claim
The Colour-Singlet Model (CSM) predictions for inclusive J/psi production in gamma-gamma collisions, when including all sizeable direct and single-resolved-photon contributions, may resolve the "LEP II J/psi production puzzle" where the NLO NRQCD calculation fails to reproduce DELPHI data; however, the normalisation of the DELPHI data itself remains unpublished and is therefore uncertain.

## Method
The authors computed all sizeable Colour-Singlet contributions to inclusive J/psi and Upsilon production in gamma-gamma collisions at leading order in v^2 in NRQCD factorisation. They used Wolfram Mathematica packages (FeynArts, FeynCalc, FormCalc) for amplitude generation, KIRA for one-loop integral reduction, LoopTools for numerical evaluation, and CUBA Monte-Carlo routines for phase-space integration; the multileg Born-order contributions were computed with HELAC-Onia. The cross sections were obtained by convolving the hard-scattering part with the Weizsacker-Williams photon flux and, for resolved-photon contributions, with the GRVGHO photon PDF set.

## Key numbers
- For LEP II DELPHI kinematics: sqrt(s_ee) = 197 GeV, with rapidity y in [-2,2] and maximum scattering angle theta_max = 25 mrad
- For CEPC kinematics: sqrt(s_ee) = 250 GeV, with rapidity y in [-2,2] and theta_max = 25 mrad
- Radial wave function squared for J/psi: |R_J/psi(0)|^2 = 1.25 GeV^3, with charm quark mass m_c = 1.5 GeV
- Radial wave function squared for Upsilon(1S): |R_Upsilon(1S)(0)|^2 = 7.5 GeV^3, with bottom quark mass m_b = 4.75 GeV
- Feed-down contributions: 20% from psi' decay for J/psi; 12.5% from Upsilon(2S) and 2.2% from Upsilon(3S) for Upsilon(1S)
- For exclusive J/psi+gamma production in ultra-peripheral PbPb collisions at the LHC: cross section estimated at O(10) nb for sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV, with expected O(10) events at integrated luminosity L=13 fb^-1

## Context
This work revisits the "LEP II J/psi production puzzle" identified by the DELPHI experiment (DELPHI, 2003), where the NLO NRQCD calculation (PhysRevD.84.051501) fails to reproduce the experimental data while the LO calculation (Klasen, 2001) succeeded. The authors extend the CSM calculations of Chen (2016) for the direct-photon gamma+gamma -> J/psi + c cbar process by adding all other sizeable CS contributions for the first time. The results provide first predictions for CSM one-loop QED direct-photon and single-resolved-photon contributions at CEPC kinematics for both J/psi and Upsilon, and demonstrate that exclusive J/psi+gamma production is measurable in ultra-peripheral heavy-ion collisions at the LHC.
