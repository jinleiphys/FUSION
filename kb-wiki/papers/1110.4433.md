# Charged bottomonium-like structures $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$

> The observation of two charged bottomonium-like structures $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ has stimulated extensive studies of the properties of $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$. In this talk, we briefly introduce the research status of $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ combined with our theoretical progress.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed charged bottomonium-like structures Zb(10610) and Zb(10650) can be explained either as B*B and B*B* molecular states or as kinematic enhancements arising from the Initial Single Pion Emission (ISPE) mechanism in Upsilon(5S) decays, rather than necessarily being genuine exotic resonances.

## Method

The authors review the Belle Collaboration data on Upsilon(5S) decays to Upsilon(nS) pi+ pi- and hb(mP) pi+ pi-, and analyze three competing decay mechanisms: direct dipion transition, rescattering via intermediate sigma(600), and contributions from intermediate Zb states. They perform a dynamical calculation using the One-Boson-Exchange (OBE) model with pi, rho, omega, and sigma meson exchanges to compute effective potentials for B(*)B(*) molecular systems under the Breit approximation, including both S-wave and D-wave interactions. Additionally, they introduce the ISPE mechanism where a single pion emitted from Upsilon(5S) produces a low-momentum B(*)B(*) pair that rescatters into final states.

## Key numbers

- Zb(10610) and Zb(10650) are enhancement structures observed around 10610 MeV and 10650 MeV, respectively, close to the BB* and B*B* thresholds
- For Zb(10610) as a molecular state with cutoff Lambda = 2.3 GeV, the binding energy is -1.64 MeV and the root-mean-square radius is 1.31 fm
- For Zb(10650) as a molecular state with cutoff Lambda = 2.4 GeV, the binding energy is -3.31 MeV and the root-mean-square radius is 0.95 fm
- The branching ratios of Upsilon(5S) to Upsilon(1S) pi+ pi- and Upsilon(2S) pi+ pi- are larger than dipion-transition rates between lower Upsilon family members by two orders of magnitude
- The Upsilon(5S) resonance is studied at sqrt(s) = 10.87 GeV

## Context

This work extends the study of anomalous Upsilon(5S) hidden-bottom dipion decays first reported by Belle (2007), which showed unexpectedly large branching ratios. The authors build on earlier rescattering mechanism calculations by Meng (2007) and tetraquark state proposals by Ali (2009), and address inconsistencies in the cos(theta) distribution of Upsilon(5S) to Upsilon(2S) pi+ pi- that could not be explained by either the tetraquark picture or rescattering alone. The ISPE mechanism prediction of charged charmonium-like structures near D D* and D* D* thresholds provides a testable prediction for BESIII, Belle, BaBar, and future Belle-II experiments.
