# Effect of Wigner energy on the symmetry energy coefficient in nuclei

> The nuclear symmetry energy coefficient (including the coefficient $a_{\rm sym}^{(4)}$ of $I^{4}$ term) of finite nuclei is extracted by using the differences of available experimental binding energies of isobaric nuclei. It is found that the extracted symmetry energy coefficient $a^{*}_{\rm sym}(A,I)$ decreases with increasing of isospin asymmetry $I$, which is mainly caused by Wigner correction, since $e^{*}_{\rm sym}$ is the summation of the traditional symmetry energy $e_{\rm sym}$ and the Wigner energy $e_{\rm W}$. We obtain the optimal values $J=30.25\pm0.10$ MeV, $a_{\rm ss}=56.18\pm1.25$ MeV, $a_{\rm sym}^{(4)}=8.33\pm1.21$ MeV and the Wigner parameter $x=2.38\pm0.12$ through the polynomial fit to 2240 measured binding energies for nuclei with $20 \leq A \leq 261$ with an rms deviation of 23.42 keV. We also find that the volume symmetry coefficient $J\simeq 30$ MeV is insensitive to the value $x$, whereas the surface symmetry coefficient $a_{\rm ss}$ and the coefficient $a_{\rm sym}^{(4)}$ are very sensitive to the value of $x$ in the range $1\leq x\leq 4$. The contribution of $a_{\rm sym}^{(4)}$ term increases rapidly with increasing of isospin asymmetry $I$. For very neutron-rich nuclei, the contribution of $a_{\rm sym}^{(4)}$ term will play an important role.

## Key claim

The extracted symmetry energy coefficient of finite nuclei decreases with increasing isospin asymmetry, which is mainly caused by the Wigner energy correction rather than by the traditional symmetry energy alone; the extracted quantity \(e^{*}_{\rm sym}\) is the sum of the traditional symmetry energy and the Wigner energy.

## Method

The authors extracted the symmetry energy coefficient (including the \(I^{4}\) term) from differences of experimental binding energies of isobaric nuclei in the AME2012 mass table, after subtracting the Coulomb energy. By fitting the average difference \(\overline{\Delta e_{\rm sym}^{*}}\) from Eq. (14) to more than 2200 measured binding energies for nuclei with \(20 \leq A \leq 261\), they obtained optimal parameters for the volume symmetry coefficient \(J\), surface symmetry coefficient \(a_{\rm ss}\), the \(I^{4}\) coefficient \(a_{\rm sym}^{(4)}\), and the Wigner parameter \(x\).

## Key numbers

- Volume symmetry coefficient \(J = 30.25 \pm 0.10\) MeV, obtained from a polynomial fit to 2240 measured binding energies for nuclei with \(20 \leq A \leq 261\).
- Surface symmetry coefficient \(a_{\rm ss} = 56.18 \pm 1.25\) MeV from the same fit.
- Coefficient of the \(I^{4}\) term \(a_{\rm sym}^{(4)} = 8.33 \pm 1.21\) MeV from the same fit.
- Wigner parameter \(x = 2.38 \pm 0.12\) from the same fit.
- Root-mean-square deviation of the fit: 23.42 keV.
- For \(x\) in the range \(1 \leq x \leq 4\), \(a_{\rm ss}\) varies from 38.72 MeV to 65.85 MeV, and \(a_{\rm sym}^{(4)}\) varies from \(-6.98\) MeV to 16.56 MeV.
- For mass number \(A=168\), the average contribution ratios of the \(a_{\rm sym}^{(2)}\) term, Wigner term, and \(a_{\rm sym}^{(4)}\) term are 87.92%, 8.27%, and 3.81%, respectively, over the isospin asymmetry range \(I = 0.07\) to 0.5.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Liu et al. (2010), who extracted the mass-dependent symmetry energy coefficient via two-parameter parabola fitting, by including both the isospin dependence and the \(I^{4}\) term, and by explicitly incorporating the Wigner energy. The authors show that the Wigner energy and the symmetry energy are intertwined, as noted by Isack (2006), and that neglecting the Wigner correction leads to unreliable values for \(a_{\rm sym}^{(4)}\). The results enable improved mass formulas for neutron-rich nuclei and provide constraints on the symmetry energy parameters relevant for nuclear astrophysics and heavy-ion collisions.
