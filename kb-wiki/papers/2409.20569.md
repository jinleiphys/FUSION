# Sokolov--Ternov effect in rotating systems

> We study electromagnetic radiation by electrically charged fermions embedded in a rotating medium in an external magnetic field. We compute the dependence of the radiation intensity on the angular velocity $Ω$ of the rotating medium for fermion polarizations along and opposite the magnetic field direction in intense subcritical fields. The polarization dependence of the photon radiation results in the Sokolov--Ternov effect -- the radiative polarization of fermions. We study the dependence of the degree of polarziation on $Ω$. We found that rotation significantly changes the degree of polarization. We show that the rotating quark-gluon plasma acquires a finite magnetic moment that exhibits complex dependence on $Ω$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that rotation significantly modifies the Sokolov–Ternov radiative polarization of fermions in a magnetic field, but only in the regime where the magnetic field strength dominates over the rotation rate (|qB| >> Ω²), allowing the causal boundary (light cylinder) to be ignored; the degree of polarization η shows strong dependence on both the magnitude and sign of the angular velocity Ω.

## Method

The authors compute the photon radiation rate and intensity for charged fermions in a constant magnetic field with uniform rotation, using exact solutions of the Dirac equation in cylindrical coordinates and leading-order perturbation theory for photon emission. The fermion wavefunctions are eigenstates of the magnetic moment operator, and the photon wavefunctions are eigenfunctions of the curl operator in the radiation gauge. Numerical integration over the photon emission angle θ and summation over final fermion states yields the polarization-dependent radiation rates, from which the degree of polarization η and induced magnetization ℳ are extracted.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strengths studied: |qB| = 10⁻³ and 10⁻² (in units of fermion mass squared, with ℏ=c=1)
- Fermion energy range: E = 1–15 (in units of fermion mass M=1)
- Angular velocities studied: |Ω| = 10⁻⁶ and 10⁻⁵ (in units of fermion mass)
- Parameter χ = |qB|Ev (evaluated at Ω=0) ranges from 0.01 to 0.15
- The semiclassical degree of polarization in the high-energy, χ≪1 limit is η₀ = 8√3/15 = 0.924
- Classical synchrotron intensity (Ω=0): W_Cl* = (2/3)α|qB|²E², where α is the fine-structure constant
- Classical synchrotron rate (Ω=0): ẇ_Cl* = (5√3/6)α|qB|
- Causality bound on quantum numbers: n,a ≪ N_caus = |qB|/(2Ω²)

## Context

This work extends the original Sokolov–Ternov effect (Sokolov 1963) from storage rings to rotating systems relevant to quark-gluon plasma produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where both strong magnetic fields (Kharzeev 2007, Skokov 2009) and high vorticity (STAR 2017) are present. The authors build on their previous calculations of synchrotron radiation intensity in rotating systems (Buzzegoli 2022, Buzzegoli 2023) and note that at later stages of heavy-ion collisions where qB ≪ Ω², causality effects from the light cylinder boundary must be included (Buzzegoli 2024). The results enable future finite-temperature calculations of magnetization in quark-gluon plasma and motivate development of semiclassical approximations that include rotation effects.
