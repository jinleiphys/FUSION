# Probing colored glass via $q\bar{q}$ photoproduction

> In this paper, we calculate the cross-section for the photoproduction of quark-antiquark pairs in the peripheral collision of ultra-relativistic nuclei, by treating the color field of the nuclei within the Color Glass Condensate model. We find that this cross-section is sensitive to the saturation scale $Q_s^2$ that characterizes the model. In particular, the transverse momentum spectrum of the produced pairs could be used to measure the properties of the color glass condensate.

## Key claim

The cross-section for photoproduction of quark-antiquark pairs in peripheral ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions is sensitive to the saturation scale \(Q_s^2\) of the Color Glass Condensate model, and the transverse momentum spectrum of the produced pairs exhibits a maximum near \(Q_s\), but this sensitivity is strongest for quarks whose mass is close to the saturation scale.

## Method

The authors calculate the average number of \(q\bar{q}\) pairs produced per peripheral collision by treating the electromagnetic interaction to lowest order (leading logarithmic approximation) and the interactions with the classical color field of one nucleus to all orders in the field, using the eikonal approximation for the quark propagator in the color field. The retarded quark propagator in the combined electromagnetic and color background is expressed in terms of separate QED and QCD scattering matrices, and the ensemble average over the hard color sources is performed using a Gaussian weight for the covariant-gauge source \(\widetilde{\rho}\). The impact parameter dependence is integrated from \(2R\) to \(\gamma/m\) to obtain the leading logarithmic contribution to the total cross-section.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale \(Q_s^2\) is expected to be of order \(1\ \text{GeV}\) at RHIC and \(2-3\ \text{GeV}\) at LHC.
- For a large nucleus of radius \(R = 6\ \text{fm}\), the inverse radius is \(1/2R \sim 17\ \text{MeV}\), which sets the scale for coherent photon transverse momentum.
- The multiplicity at large impact parameter decreases like \(1/b^2\), leading to a logarithmic divergence in the cross-section when integrated over impact parameter.
- The upper cutoff for the impact parameter integral is \(b = \gamma/m\), where \(m\) is the quark mass and \(\gamma\) is the Lorentz factor of the electromagnetic source.

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate model of McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) to a new observable, photoproduction of \(q\bar{q}\) pairs in peripheral collisions, which is more directly observable than the gluon production studied in central AA collisions by Kovner, McLerran, and Weigert (1995) and by Krasnitz and Venugopalan (1999). The process is sensitive to the gluon distribution via the correlator \(\langle U(0)U^\dagger(\mathbf{x}_\perp)\rangle\) and offers a way to measure the saturation scale \(Q_s\), complementing earlier work on structure functions by McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and on photoproduction as a probe of gluon distributions by Barone and collaborators (1993).
