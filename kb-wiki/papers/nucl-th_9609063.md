# QCD Vacuum Changes in Nuclei

> In this talk, I discuss how the changes in the QCD vacuum induced by increasing nuclear matter density affect nuclear properties under normal as well as extreme conditions. The quark condensate which is the order parameter for the mode in which chiral symmetry is manifested is expected to change as matter density or temperature is changed. The topics discussed are ``BR scaling," its connection to the structure of nuclei in Landau's Fermi liquid theory and a variety of consequences on such nuclear properties as effective nucleon mass, nuclear gyromagnetic ratios, $g_A^\star$, axial-charge transitions in nuclei and on the fluctuations of nuclear matter into the strange-flavor direction as observed in heavy-ion collisions. I will also present a simple explanation of the recent dilepton data in the CERN-CERES heavy-ion experiments involving densities greater than that of nuclear matter and of the Indiana results on the longitudinally polarized proton scattering from heavy nuclei, evidencing dropping vector-meson masses in medium which can be interpreted as signaling an aspect of chiral symmetry restoration in dense medium. The test of BR scaling provides a bridge between the physics of extreme conditions (e.g. relativistic heavy-ion collisions) and the physics of normal conditions which has conventional descriptions, thereby setting the stage for formulating many-body theory for nuclear matter starting from an effective chiral Lagrangian.

# QCD Vacuum Changes in Nuclei

## Key claim

The paper argues that the changes in the QCD vacuum with increasing nuclear matter density can be captured by a universal scaling factor Φ(ρ) that relates in-medium hadron masses to their free-space values, and that this "BR scaling" provides a bridge between normal nuclear properties and extreme conditions such as heavy-ion collisions. The claim is hedged by the recognition that the precise connection between the Landau effective mass (defined at the Fermi surface) and the scaling mass (defined for a nucleon propagating in the modified vacuum) is not fully understood, though they are expected to be approximately equal.

## Method

The author derives BR scaling from an effective chiral Lagrangian incorporating the trace anomaly of QCD, where a scalar field interpolating the gluon condensate is integrated out to yield density-dependent parameters. The scaling is then connected to Landau Fermi-liquid theory by relating the universal scaling factor Φ to the Landau parameter F₁ through the ω-meson exchange contribution, with the pion-exchange Fock term treated as a perturbation. Predictions for nuclear observables are made by fixing Φ(ρ₀) = 0.78 from QCD sum-rule calculations of the ρ-meson mass in medium, then computing effective nucleon mass, axial-charge transitions, effective axial-vector coupling, orbital gyromagnetic ratios, and kaon properties.

## Key numbers

- Φ(ρ₀) = 0.78, extracted from the QCD sum-rule result m_ρ*/m_ρ = 0.78 ± 0.08 for the ρ-meson mass at nuclear matter density
- (f_π*/f_π)²(ρ₀) ≈ 0.63 from the in-medium Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation using Σ_{πN} ≈ 45 MeV
- Landau effective nucleon mass m_N*/m_N = 0.69(7) at ρ₀, compared to QCD sum-rule result 0.67 ± 0.05
- Pion-exchange contribution to the Landau parameter: (1/3) F̃₁^π ≈ -0.153 at nuclear matter density (p_F ≈ 2m_π)
- Axial-charge ratio ε_{MEC} = 1.9 ± 0.1 predicted for heavy nuclei (lead region), compared to experimental range 1.8–2.0
- Effective axial-vector coupling g_A* = 1.0(0) at ρ₀
- Orbital gyromagnetic ratio correction δg_l = 0.22(7)τ₃ at ρ₀, compared to δg_l^{proton} = 0.23 ± 0.03 from the dipole sum rule in ²⁰⁹Bi
- Kaon potential prediction: S_{K⁻} + V_{K⁻} ≲ -200 MeV, compared to K-mesic atom result -200 ± 20 MeV at ρ ≈ 0.97ρ₀ from Friedman, Gal and Batty

## Context

This work extends the BR scaling conjecture first proposed by Brown and Rho (1991) by providing a detailed connection to Landau Fermi-liquid theory through the work of Shankar and Polchinski on renormalization-group flow in Fermi systems. It builds on prior calculations of in-medium hadron masses from QCD sum rules by Jin and collaborators, and on the in-medium Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation. The paper enables a unified description of diverse nuclear phenomena: it explains the CERN-CERES dilepton excess in heavy-ion collisions (Li, Ko and Brown, 1995) through dropping ρ-meson mass, the Indiana polarized proton scattering results through scaling vector-meson masses, Warburton's axial-charge measurements in heavy nuclei, and the quenching of g_A observed in heavy nuclei. The kaon condensation prediction at 2–3 times normal density connects to compact-star physics, though the author notes that chiral perturbation theory constraints from Waas, Rho and Weise suggest corrections may prevent condensation at such low density unless additional four-Fermi interactions are included.
