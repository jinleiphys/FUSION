# A Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model of quantum chromodynamics and hadron structure

> In this review paper, I present a study of the structure of the hadrons computed in the covariant Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model as the chiral effective quark theory of QCD. I describe how the NJL model is treated to imitate the spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and confinement QCD properties. The consistency for the parton distribution functions and electromagnetic form factors, as internal structure observables, in comparison with existing data and other theoretical predictions, is also shown. The implications of mimicking the QCD properties in the NJL model for hadron structure observables, as well as the relevance of the results to EIC, EicC, and COMPASS/AMBER future experiments, are discussed.

## Key claim

The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, when treated with the Schwinger proper time regularization scheme including an infrared cutoff, can mimic both dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and a crude form of color confinement, and its predictions for pion and kaon parton distribution functions and electromagnetic form factors are consistent with existing data and other theoretical models, though the model lacks quark momentum dependence present in approaches like the Dyson-Schwinger Equation.

## Method

The author reviews the covariant NJL model as a chiral effective quark theory of QCD, using the Schwinger proper time regularization scheme with ultraviolet cutoff Lambda_UV = 645 MeV and infrared cutoff Lambda_IR = 240 MeV to cure divergences and implement confinement. The dynamical quark mass M_q = 400 MeV is obtained from a gap equation, and the model is applied to compute the parton distribution functions and electromagnetic form factors of the pi+ and K+ mesons. The PDF results are evolved from an initial scale Q0^2 = 0.16 GeV^2 to Q^2 = 5 GeV^2 using NLO DGLAP QCD evolution for comparison with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Dynamical quark mass M_q = 400 MeV, with ultraviolet cutoff Lambda_UV = 645 MeV and infrared cutoff Lambda_IR = 240 MeV
- QCD scale Lambda_QCD = 200-300 MeV, consistent with experimental results
- Pion and kaon PDFs computed at Q^2 = 5 GeV^2, evolved from initial scale Q0^2 = 0.16 GeV^2
- Moments of PDFs computed at renormalization scales Q^2 = 0.16, 4, 8, 12, 16, and 20 GeV^2
- The pi+ and K+ electromagnetic form factors at higher Q^2 satisfy the quark counting rule approximately 1/Q^2
- Critical coupling for chiral symmetry breaking given by G_critical = pi^2 / [3 (Lambda^2_UV - Lambda^2_IR)]

## Context

This review extends the author's prior work on the NJL model (Hutauruk 2016, Hutauruk 2018) and compares results with the Dyson-Schwinger Equation approach (Maris 2000) and lattice QCD simulations. The results are positioned as predictions relevant for future experiments at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), the Electron-ion collider in China (EicC), COMPASS/AMBER++ at CERN, and the upgraded JLAB 22 GeV, which are expected to provide precise data on gluon distributions and hadron structure. The paper notes that lattice QCD can currently compute only a few moments of the pion and kaon PDFs, and that higher moments from lattice calculations are needed.
