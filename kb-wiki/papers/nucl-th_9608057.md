# Strong decays of qqq baryons

> We study strong decays of nonstrange baryons by making use of the algebraic approach to hadron structure. Within this framework we derive closed expressions for decay widths in an elementary-meson emission model and use these to analyze the experimental data for N* -> N + pi, N* -> Delta + pi, N* -> N + eta, Delta* -> N + pi, Delta* -> Delta + pi and Delta* -> Delta + eta decays.

## Key claim

The paper claims that strong decays of nonstrange baryons into pions follow the expected pattern of conventional quark-model dynamics, but that the observed large eta width of the N(1535)S11 resonance cannot be explained by a conventional q^3 baryon state and instead suggests the presence of an exotic configuration (e.g., a quasi-bound meson-baryon resonance or a q^4 q̅ state).

## Method

The authors use an algebraic approach based on U(7) ⊗ SU_sf(6) ⊗ SU_c(3) symmetry to derive closed-form expressions for strong decay widths in an elementary-meson emission model. They calculate helicity amplitudes and partial wave amplitudes for decays of nonstrange baryon resonances into Nπ, Δπ, Nη, Δπ, Δη, and Δη channels, using a collective model with a distribution function g(β) = β² e^{-β/a} / 2a³. The two parameters g and h in the transition operator and the scale parameter a are determined from a least-squares fit to the Nπ partial widths of 3- and 4-star resonances, excluding the S11 resonances.

## Key numbers

*   The fitted parameters are g = 1.164 GeV⁻¹ and h = -0.094 GeV⁻¹, with a scale parameter a = 0.232 fm.
*   The scale parameter a = 0.232 fm extracted from the strong decay fit equals the value extracted from electromagnetic couplings in prior work (Bijker, Iachello, Leviatan, 1994).
*   For the missing resonances below 2 GeV, the calculated Nπ widths for the P13 states are 31 MeV and 56 MeV, with the difference due to the k values entering the elementary partial wave amplitude W₁(k).
*   The momentum k carried by the eta meson is smaller than that carried by the pion due to the mass difference, suppressing eta decay widths relative to pion decays.

## Context

This work extends the algebraic approach to hadron structure previously applied to mass spectra and electromagnetic couplings (Bijker, Iachello, Leviatan, 1994) to the analysis of strong decays. It provides an alternative to nonrelativistic quark model calculations (Koniuk and Isgur, 1980) and relativized quark model calculations (Capstick and Roberts, 1993), which use harmonic oscillator bases and include hyperfine mixing. The results enable analysis of upcoming experimental data from CEBAF, MAMI, and ELSA, particularly for (e,e'π) and (e,e'η) reactions, and suggest that the N(1535)S11 resonance's large eta width requires investigation of unconventional configurations beyond the q³ model space.
