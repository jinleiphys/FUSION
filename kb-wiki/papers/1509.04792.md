# In-medium and isospin effects on particle production near threshold energies in heavy-ion collisions

> Dynamics of pseudoscalar mesons ($π$, $η$, $K$ and $\overline{K}$ ) and hyperons ($Λ$ and $Σ$) produced in heavy-ion collisions near threshold energies has been investigated within the Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model. The in-medium modifications on particle production in dense nuclear matter are implemented in the model through corrections on the elementary cross sections and by inclusion of the meson (hyperon)-nucleon potentials, in which the isospin effects are considered. It is found that the transportation of particles are influenced with the in-medium corrections. The total number of pions is reduced with an isospin, density and momentum dependent pion-nucleon potential. However, the ratios of charged pions is enhanced with inclusion of the potential. The production of eta in the domain of mid-rapidities and high momenta is sensitive to the $η$-nucleon potential, but weakly depends on symmetry energy. The attractive antikaon-nucleon potential enhances the subthreshold $\overline{K}$ production and also influences the structure of phase-space distributions. Dynamics of etas, kaons, antikaons and hyperons is also influenced by the pion potential because of collisions between pions and nucleons (resonances). The impacts of mean-field potentials on particle dynamics are investigated, such as the phase-space distributions from rapidity and transverse momentum spectra, inclusive invariant spectra, collective flows etc.

# In-medium and isospin effects on particle production near threshold energies in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model, including in-medium modifications to elementary cross sections and meson (hyperon)-nucleon potentials with isospin dependence, shows that the total number of pions is reduced by an isospin-, density-, and momentum-dependent pion-nucleon potential, while the charged-pion ratio is enhanced; however, the extraction of the high-density symmetry energy from particle production is complicated by the interplay of mean-field potentials and threshold-energy corrections, and the eta production is sensitive to the eta-nucleon potential but only weakly dependent on the symmetry energy.

## Method
The LQMD transport model was used to simulate heavy-ion collisions near threshold energies, incorporating hadron-hadron collisions, resonance decays, and mean-field potentials for baryons, mesons (pions, etas, kaons, antikaons), and hyperons. In-medium effects were implemented via corrections to elementary cross sections (scaled by effective masses) and by including density- and momentum-dependent optical potentials for pions (phenomenological ansatz and Delta-hole model), etas (chiral perturbation theory), kaons and antikaons (chiral Lagrangian approach), and hyperons (light-quark counting rule). The symmetry energy was varied among soft, linear, hard, and supersoft forms to study its influence on particle production.

## Key numbers
- The pion-nucleon potential reduces the total pion multiplicity in central Au+Au collisions compared to calculations without the potential, with the phenomenological approach giving a stronger reduction than the Delta-hole model.
- The charged-pion ratio (pi-/pi+) is enhanced by the pion-nucleon potential, particularly with the phenomenological isoscalar part, in central Au+Au collisions at 300 MeV/nucleon.
- The eta optical potential at zero momentum and saturation density is -94 MeV, based on chiral perturbation theory with parameters a_eta = 280 MeV and kappa = 0.4 fm.
- The kaon-nucleon potential is repulsive at 27.8 MeV and the antikaon-nucleon potential is attractive at -100.3 MeV at saturation density for isospin symmetric matter.
- The effective mass ratio m*_K/m_K = 1.056 and m*_anti-K/m_anti-K = 0.797 at normal baryon density in isospin symmetric nuclear matter.
- The slope parameters L for the symmetry energy are 203.7 MeV (hard), 124.9 MeV (linear), 85.6 MeV (soft), and 74.7 MeV (supersoft), with corresponding curvature parameters K_sym of 448 MeV, -24.5 MeV, -83.5 MeV, and -326 MeV.
- The K-/K+ ratio in Ni+Ni reactions is enhanced by the attractive antikaon-nucleon potential, with a deeply attractive K-N potential of -110 +/- 15 MeV and a weakly repulsive K+N potential of 25 +/- 10 MeV extracted from heavy-ion collisions.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of in-medium hadron properties in dense nuclear matter using transport models, particularly building on the LQMD framework developed by Feng et al. (2010, 2012) and earlier work on kaon production as a probe of the high-density equation of state by Aichelin (1985) and the KaoS collaboration (Sturm et al., 2001). The paper addresses ongoing debates about the interpretation of charged-pion ratios in constraining the high-density symmetry energy, as discussed by Xiao et al. (2009) and Feng et al. (2010), and the role of pion optical potentials studied by Xu et al. (1993) and Fuchs et al. (1997). The results enable more precise extraction of the high-density symmetry energy and in-medium potentials from future heavy-ion collision experiments, particularly through combined analysis of neutral particles (pi0, eta) and strange particles (kaons, antikaons, hyperons).
