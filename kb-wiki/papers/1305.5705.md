# Infrared dynamics in de Sitter space from Schwinger-Dyson equations

> We study the two-point correlator of an O(N) scalar field with quartic self-coupling in de Sitter space. For light fields in units of the expansion rate, perturbation theory is plagued by large logarithmic terms for superhorizon momenta. We show that a proper treatment of the infinite series of self-energy insertions through the Schwinger-Dyson equations resums these infrared logarithms into well defined power laws. We provide an exact analytical solution of the Schwinger-Dyson equations for infrared momenta when the self-energy is computed at two-loop order. The obtained correlator exhibits a rich structure with a superposition of free-field-like power laws. We extract mass and field-strength renormalization factors from the asymptotic infrared behavior. The latter are nonperturbative in the coupling in the case of a vanishing tree-level mass.

## Key claim

The Schwinger-Dyson equations, when solved exactly for infrared momenta with the self-energy computed at two-loop order, resum the large infrared logarithms that plague perturbation theory into well-defined power laws, but the two-loop approximation for the self-energy is insufficient for the case of a perturbatively massless field, where nonperturbative schemes such as 1/N-expansion or self-consistent 2PI approximations are required.

## Method

The authors study an O(N) scalar field with quartic self-coupling on the expanding Poincaré patch of de Sitter space, using the physical momentum (p-)representation for correlators. They compute the nonlocal self-energy at two-loop order in terms of the massive propagator G_M, then solve the Schwinger-Dyson equations for infrared momenta by converting them into a one-dimensional integro-differential equation for the spectral function ρ(x). The exact analytical solution is obtained in closed form using a kernel method, yielding explicit expressions for both the spectral and statistical two-point functions.

## Key numbers

- The parameter controlling the nonlocal self-energy corrections is σ_ρ = (λ²/ε²) × (N+2)/(24N²) × Γ²(d/2)/(4π^{d+2}), where ε ≈ M²/d ≪ 1 for light fields.
- The effective infrared exponent is given by ν̃ = √(ν² + σ_ρ/(4ε²)), where ν = d/2 - ε.
- The deep infrared behavior yields a field-strength renormalization factor Z = (ν̃ + ν)/(2ν̃) and an effective mass ratio M̄²/M² = 2 - ν̃/ν.
- The dynamical mass m_dyn²/M² = (4/3)(1 - ν̃²/(4ν²)) differs from the infrared mass M̄.
- For a massive field with c_dλ ≪ m_dS⁴ ≪ 1, the perturbative expansion gives Z = 1 - (3λ̄²)/(2(N+2)) and M̄² = m_dS²(1 + λ̄ - (N+5)/(N+2)λ̄²), where λ̄ = c_dλ/m_dS⁴.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of infrared effects in de Sitter space using stochastic methods (Starobinsky 1994), Euclidean approaches (Hu 1985, Rajaraman 2010), and 2PI techniques (Garbrecht 2011, Prokopec 2011). The exact analytical solution of the Schwinger-Dyson equations goes beyond what was obtained with stochastic or Euclidean approaches, revealing the detailed structure of two-point correlators from horizon to deep superhorizon scales. The results enable future studies using nonperturbative approximation schemes such as 1/N-expansion or self-consistent 2PI approximations for the massless case where the two-loop approximation breaks down.
