# Probing hadronization with flavor correlations of leading particles in jets

> We study nonperturbative flavor correlations between pairs of leading and next-to-leading charged hadrons within jets at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC). We introduce a charge correlation ratio observable $r_c$ that distinguishes same- and opposite-sign charged pairs. Using Monte Carlo simulations with different event generators, $r_c$ is examined as a function of various kinematic variables for different combinations of hadron species, and the feasibility of such measurements at the EIC is demonstrated. The precision hadronization study we propose will provide new tests of hadronization models and hopefully lead to improved quantitative, and perhaps eventually analytic, understanding of nonperturbative QCD dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a charge correlation ratio observable $r_c$ for leading and next-to-leading charged hadrons within jets, and demonstrates through Monte Carlo simulations that this observable can distinguish between different hadronization models (PYTHIA and Herwig) at the EIC, with the caveat that the feasibility depends on achieving sufficient particle identification (3$\sigma$ $\pi/K$ separation up to 10 GeV) and detector resolution.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of deep inelastic scattering (DIS) events at the EIC using PYTHIA 6.428 and Herwig 7.1.5 event generators, with event selection requiring $Q^2 > 50$ GeV$^2$. Jets were reconstructed using the anti-$k_t$ algorithm with $R=1.0$ using FastJet 3, including particles with transverse momenta above 0.2 GeV and pseudorapidity $-1.5 \le \eta \le 3.5$, selecting jets with $p_T^{\rm jet} > 5$ GeV. The charge correlation ratio $r_c$ was computed as a function of formation time $t_{\rm form}$, relative transverse momentum $k_\perp$, DIS $Q^2$, and jet $p_T^{\rm jet}$ for different hadron species pairs ($\pi^\pm$, $K^\pm$, $p\bar{p}$).

## Key numbers

* Event selection imposed $Q^2 > 50$ GeV$^2$; 10 million events were analyzed, corresponding to approximately one percent of the expected integrated luminosity at the EIC.
* Jets were reconstructed with $R=1.0$; particles with transverse momenta above 0.2 GeV and pseudorapidity $-1.5 \le \eta \le 3.5$ were included; jets with $p_T^{\rm jet} > 5$ GeV were selected.
* The formation time $t_{\rm form}$ distributions for leading dihadrons peak between 1 and 10 fm and decrease at large ($\gtrsim 10$ fm) and small ($\lesssim 1$ fm) formation time.
* For large formation time where dynamics is dominated by non-perturbative physics, the correlation strength is flat and corresponds to dihadrons with $k_\perp \lesssim 200$ MeV.
* The correlation $r_c$ decreases in absolute value as $k_\perp$ increases on the scale of 1-2 GeV.
* The EIC goal for particle identification is 3$\sigma$ $\pi/K$ separation up to 10 GeV in momentum.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of charge correlations in hadron production, including the charge density ratio introduced by the TASSO experiment (1985) and the closely-related "balance function" used in heavy ion studies by ALICE (2013). The paper specifically contrasts the Lund string model (implemented in PYTHIA) and the cluster model (implemented in Herwig) for hadronization. The proposed measurements at the EIC would provide new tests of hadronization models and could lead to improved quantitative understanding of nonperturbative QCD dynamics, with the EIC's unique capability for precise $\pi/K$ separation at high momentum compared to existing data from RHIC (STAR, sPHENIX), LHC (ALICE, CMS, ATLAS), HERA (H1), and LEP (DELPHI, ALEPH).
