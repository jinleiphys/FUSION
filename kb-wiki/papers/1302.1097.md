# The angular momentum dependence of nuclear optical potentials

> The nuclear optical model potential (OMP) is generally assumed to be independent of the orbital angular momentum, $l$, of the interacting nuclei. Nucleon-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus interactions are customarily $l$ independent in calculations of nuclear elastic scattering and in standard reaction codes. The evidence for various forms of $l$ dependence of OMPs is reviewed and the importance of implementing these forms is evaluated. Existing arguments and evidence for $l$ dependence are reviewed and new arguments and calculations are introduced. The relationship is examined between (i) $l$ dependence, and, (ii) the undularity (waviness) of $l$-independent potentials that are $S$-matrix equivalent to $l$-dependent potentials. Such undularity is a property of the dynamic polarisation potential (DPP) generated by the coupling to reaction channels, or by coupling to excited states of the target or projectile nuclei. Various examples, particularly involving weakly bound projectile nuclei, are presented. Such undularity also occurs in $l$-independent potentials that have been found in model independent fits to precise wide angular range elastic scattering angular distributions. Cases of such phenomenological undularity, for both light and heavy ions, are referenced and shown to be related to undulatory properties of the dynamic polarisation potentials (DPPs) arising from channel coupling. Other forms of $l$ dependence, that could be standard options in direct reaction codes, are noted. Of particular importance are $l$ dependencies arising from full antisymmetrization. The case is made that reaction-induced $l$ dependence is a general property of nucleon-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus interactions and represents a valid extension of the nuclear optical model.

## Key claim

The nuclear optical model potential (OMP) is generally assumed to be independent of orbital angular momentum $l$, but the paper argues that reaction-induced $l$ dependence is a general property of nucleon-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus interactions, representing a valid extension of the optical model. The key hedge is that for any $l$-dependent potential there always exists an $l$-independent potential with the same complex S-matrix, but this $l$-independent representation will never have a smooth Woods-Saxon-like form and is generally undulatory (wavy).

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical arguments for $l$ dependence from Feshbach theory, self-energy approaches, and the resonating group model (RGM), then examines dynamic polarisation potentials (DPPs) generated by coupling to reaction channels or excited states. The method involves performing coupled channel (CC), coupled reaction channel (CRC), or continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) calculations to obtain elastic channel S-matrices, then applying iterative-perturbative (IP) S-matrix-to-potential inversion to extract local $l$-independent representations of the DPPs. These inverted potentials are compared with phenomenological potentials from precision fits to elastic scattering angular distributions.

## Key numbers

- For 30.3 MeV protons on $^{40}$Ca, coupling to neutron pickup channels generated a DPP with a change in volume integral of $\Delta J_{\rm R} = -7.44$ MeV fm$^3$ (per nucleon), with the rms radius increased by 0.034 fm.
- For 15.66 MeV protons on $^{8}$He, an $l$-dependent potential with a real part factor $(1.0 + 0.05(-1)^l)$ was studied, and its $l$-independent equivalent exhibited undulations including a small excursion into emissivity in the imaginary part.
- For 52 MeV deuterons on $^{40}$Ca, coupling effects were shown to produce DPPs with undulatory character typical for energies well above the Coulomb barrier.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of parity dependence in nucleon-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus scattering, including the RGM-based predictions of Baye (1986) that Majorana term strength decreases with target mass, and the phenomenological parity-dependent fits for systems such as $\alpha$ + $^{20}$Ne by Michel and Reidemeister. The review connects the undulatory features found in precision fits to elastic scattering data (e.g., for $^{40}$Ca and $^{16}$O) with DPPs arising from channel coupling, as studied by Rawitscher (1987), Buck (1963), Perey (1963), and others. The paper enables future work on implementing $l$-dependent OMPs as standard options in direct reaction codes, and on exploiting precise elastic scattering data to extract information about reaction dynamics that is lost in standard global potentials.
