# Generation of strong magnetic fields in hybrid and quark stars driven by the electroweak interaction of quarks

> We study the generation of strong large scale magnetic fields in compact stars containing degenerate quark matter with unbroken chiral symmetry. The magnetic field growth is owing to the magnetic field instability driven by the electroweak interaction of quarks. In this system we predict the enhancement of the seed magnetic field $10^{12}\,\text{G}$ to the strengths $(10^{14}-10^{15})\,\text{G}$. In our analysis we use the typical parameters of the quark matter in the core of a hybrid star or in a quark star. We also apply of the obtained results to model the generation of magnetic fields in magnetars.

# Generation of strong magnetic fields in hybrid and quark stars driven by the electroweak interaction of quarks

## Key claim
The electroweak interaction of quarks in degenerate quark matter with unbroken chiral symmetry can amplify a seed magnetic field of 10^12 G to strengths of (10^14-10^15) G, but this mechanism requires that the chiral symmetry is restored (quarks are effectively massless), which is possible only in the core of hybrid stars or in quark stars, not in ordinary neutron stars.

## Method
The author derived the induced electric current in quark matter from the chiral magnetic effect, incorporating the electroweak interaction potentials V_{5q} for u and d quarks. A system of kinetic equations for the magnetic helicity density spectrum, magnetic energy density spectrum, and chiral imbalances mu_{5u} and mu_{5d} was established and solved numerically, using a Kolmogorov initial spectrum for the magnetic energy density and accounting for the temperature-dependent electric conductivity of degenerate quark matter. The saturation of the magnetic field was enforced through energy conservation between the thermal energy of background fermions and the magnetic energy, introducing a quenching factor.

## Key numbers
- Seed magnetic field: B_0 = 10^12 G (typical for a young pulsar)
- Saturated magnetic field for initial temperature T_0 = 10^8 K: B_sat ≈ 1.1 × 10^14 G
- Saturated magnetic field for initial temperature T_0 = 10^9 K: B_sat ≈ 1.1 × 10^15 G
- Total quark number density: n_0 = 1.8 × 10^38 cm^-3
- Electroweak potentials: V_{5u} = 4.5 eV, V_{5d} = 2.9 eV (for n_0 = 1.8 × 10^38 cm^-3)
- Mean chemical potential: mu_0 = 346 MeV; mu_u = 0.69 mu_0, mu_d = 0.87 mu_0
- Helicity flip rates: Gamma_u = 2.98 × 10^-10 mu_0, Gamma_d = 5.88 × 10^-12 mu_0
- Electric conductivity parameter: sigma_0 = 3.15 × 10^22 s^-1
- Stellar radius: R = 10 km, giving k_min = 2 × 10^-11 eV
- Saturation time for T_0 = 10^8 K: t_sat ≤ 10 hours
- Saturation time for T_0 = 10^9 K: t_sat ≤ 10^2 minutes

## Context
This work extends the chiral magnetic effect mechanism previously developed by Dvornikov (2016) and Dvornikov and Semikoz (2015) for neutron stars, where electrons were the active particles. The key advance is applying the mechanism to quark matter in hybrid stars or quark stars, where chiral symmetry is restored for light u and d quarks at densities achievable in stellar cores, as shown by Dexheimer and Schramm (2010). The author notes that the earlier electron-based mechanism is unlikely to apply in neutron stars because electrons have nonzero mass and the chiral symmetry restoration density for electrons (n ~ 10^46 cm^-3) is far higher than neutron star densities. This work enables a new model for magnetar magnetic field generation, with growth times much shorter than in the electron-nucleon case, and suggests that hybrid stars or quark stars could become magnetars.
