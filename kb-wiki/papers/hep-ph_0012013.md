# Quark description of nuclear matter

> We discuss the role of an adjoint chiral condensate for color superconducting quark matter. Its presence leads to color-flavor locking in two-flavor quark matter. Color is broken completely as well as chiral symmetry in the two-flavor theory with coexisting adjoint quark-antiquark and antitriplet quark-quark condensates. The qualitative properties of this phase match the properties of ordinary nuclear matter without strange baryons. This complements earlier proposals by Schafer and Wilczek for a quark description of hadronic phases. We show for a class of models with effective four-fermion interactions that adjoint chiral and diquark condensates do not compete, in the sense that simultaneous condensation occurs for sufficiently strong interactions in the adjoint chiral channel.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that simultaneous condensation in the adjoint chiral (color-octet quark-antiquark) and color-antitriplet diquark channels in two-flavor quark matter leads to complete breaking of color and chiral symmetry, producing a phase whose qualitative properties match ordinary nuclear matter without strange baryons. However, the author emphasizes that a strong attraction in the adjoint chiral channel is a necessary condition which may not be realized in the two-flavor theory and for which no control is claimed.

## Method

The author constructs a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio-type model with effective four-fermion interactions including a color-antitriplet diquark channel and a color-octet quark-antiquark channel, with a three-momentum cutoff of 0.7 GeV. The mean-field thermodynamic potential is derived as a function of the adjoint chiral gap parameter chi and the diquark gap parameter Delta, and the coupled gap equations are solved to find the ground state as a function of quark chemical potential mu and the ratio of couplings G_chi/G_Delta. The diquark coupling G_Delta is fixed to produce a gap of 125 MeV at mu = 500 MeV when G_chi = 0.

## Key numbers

* Diquark gap of 125 MeV at mu = 500 MeV for G_chi = 0 (used to fix the diquark coupling)
* At a first-order phase transition point (mu = 475 MeV, G_chi/G_Delta = 2.78, m = 0), the 2CFL phase has chi = 187 MeV and Delta = 88 MeV, while the 2SC phase has chi = 0 and Delta = 120 MeV
* The chemical potential range studied is from approximately 350 MeV to approximately 500 MeV
* The cutoff in three-momentum space is Lambda = 0.7 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier proposals by Schafer and Wilczek (1999) for a quark description of hadronic phases, complementing their continuity argument between quark matter and hypernuclear matter. The paper contrasts with prior studies by Berges and Rajagopal (1999) and by Chiku and Hatsuda (1998) which found strong competition and no simultaneous condensation in the standard color-singlet quark-antiquark and antitriplet diquark channels. The result enables a possible quark description of ordinary nuclear matter without strange baryons, though the author notes that a strong attraction in the adjoint chiral channel is a necessary condition whose realization in the two-flavor theory is not established.
