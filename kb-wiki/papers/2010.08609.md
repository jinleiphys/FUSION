# Ab initio benchmarks of neutrinoless double beta decay in light nuclei with a chiral Hamiltonian

> We report ab initio benchmark calculations of nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for neutrinoless double-beta ($0νββ$) decays in light nuclei with mass number ranging from $A=6$ to $A=22$. We use the transition operator derived from light-Majorana neutrino exchange and evaluate the NME with three different methods: two variants of in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) and importance-truncated no-core shell model (IT-NCSM). The same two-plus-three-nucleon interaction from chiral effective field theory is employed, and both isospin-conserving ($ΔT=0$) and isospin-changing ($ΔT=2$) transitions are studied. We compare our resulting ground-state energies and NMEs to those of recent ab initio no-core shell model and coupled-cluster calculations, also with the same inputs. We show that the NMEs of $ΔT=0$ transitions are in good agreement among all calculations, at the level of 10%. For $ΔT=2$, relative deviations are more significant in some nuclei. The comparison with the exact IT-NCSM result allows us to analyze these cases in detail, and indicates the next steps towards improving the IMSRG-based approaches. The present study clearly demonstrates the power of consistent cross-checks that are made possible by ab initio methodology. This capability is crucial for providing meaningful many-body uncertainties in the NMEs for the $0νββ$ decays in heavier candidate nuclei, where quasi-exact benchmarks are not available.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for isospin-conserving (ΔT=0) neutrinoless double-beta decays in light nuclei (A=6 to A=22) agree among three different ab initio methods at the 10% level, but for isospin-changing (ΔT=2) transitions the relative deviations are more significant in some nuclei, indicating that the IMSRG-based approaches require further improvement.

## Method

The authors employed a chiral two-plus-three-nucleon interaction (EM1.8/2.0) from chiral effective field theory and evaluated the NME using three methods: two variants of the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG), the valence-space IMSRG (VS-IMSRG) and the in-medium generator coordinate method (IM-GCM), and the importance-truncated no-core shell model (IT-NCSM). The transition operator was derived from light-Majorana neutrino exchange, and both isospin-conserving (ΔT=0) and isospin-changing (ΔT=2) transitions were studied for nuclei with mass numbers from A=6 to A=22.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energies and NMEs were compared across methods for nuclei with A=6 to A=22
- NMEs for ΔT=0 transitions agree among all calculations at the level of 10%
- The chiral NN interaction was used at N3LO with a momentum cutoff of 500 MeV/c, evolved via SRG to a resolution scale of λ=1.8/fm
- The 3N interaction was constructed with a chiral cutoff of Λ=2.0/fm
- For the 3N interaction, all matrix elements involving states with e1+e2+e3 > 14 were discarded, where ei=2ni+ℓi

## Context

This work extends previous ab initio calculations of 0νββ-decay NMEs that were performed for 48Ca, 76Ge, and 82Se using IMSRG and coupled-cluster methods (Yao 2020, Belley 2020, Novario 2020). It also builds on earlier NCSM calculations for 6,8,10He, 14C, and 22O (Novario 2020) and quantum Monte Carlo calculations for 6,8,10He and 10,12Be (Pastore 2018, Wang 2019). The benchmarks in light nuclei enable systematic cross-checks that are crucial for providing meaningful many-body uncertainties in NMEs for heavier candidate nuclei where quasi-exact benchmarks are not available.
