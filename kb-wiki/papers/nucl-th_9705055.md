# Boost covariant gluon distributions in large nuclei

> It has been shown recently that there exist analytical solutions of the Yang-Mills equations for non-Abelian Weizsäcker-Williams fields which describe the distribution of gluons in large nuclei at small x. These solutions however depend on the color charge distribution at large rapidities. We here construct a model of the color charge distribution of partons in the fragmentation region and use it to compute the boost covariant momentum distributions of wee gluons. The phenomenological applications of our results are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a model for the color charge distribution of partons in the nuclear fragmentation region that makes the previously derived analytical solutions of the Yang-Mills equations for non-Abelian Weizsäcker-Williams fields boost covariant, but the authors caution that for realistically sized nuclei the results are semi-quantitative and qualitative at best because the typical energy scale is only marginally large enough for weak coupling calculations to be valid.

## Method

The authors model the color charge squared per unit area per unit rapidity, \(\mu^2(\eta, Q_0^2)\), by starting from a nuclear rest frame picture where the color charge squared per unit longitudinal distance is constant within the nucleus, then boosting to the infinite momentum frame. This yields an expression for \(\mu^2\) that depends on a single free parameter \(\kappa\), the average color charge squared per nucleon integrated over one unit of rapidity. The resulting integrated color charge \(\chi(y, Q_0^2)\) is then inserted into the previously derived analytical solution for the classical gluon distribution function, which is obtained by solving the classical Yang-Mills equations for static light-cone sources and averaging over a Gaussian color charge distribution.

## Key numbers

- For a nucleus with \(A=200\), the fragmentation region momentum fraction threshold is \(x_R \sim 0.025\), derived from \(x_R \sim A^{-1/3}/(\sqrt{2}\, r_0 m_N)\) with \(r_0=1.12\) fm and \(m_N\) the nucleon mass.
- The typical energy scale at central rapidities is estimated to be \(300\)–\(400\) MeV at RHIC energies and \(750\)–\(900\) MeV at LHC energies.
- The classical gluon distribution in coordinate space is given by \({dN\over {d^2 x_t d\eta}}={4 \over g^2}{(N_c^2-1)\over {N_c x_t^2}}\left(1-\left(x_t^2 \Lambda_{QCD}^2\right)^{{g^4 N_c\over {16\pi}}\chi(\eta,Q^2)x_t^2}\right)\).
- The color charge squared per unit area \(\chi(y, Q_0^2)\) is constant at \(2 R \rho_0 \kappa\) for \(y<y_R\) and decays as \(2 R\rho_0 \kappa e^{y_R-y}\) for \(y>y_R\).

## Context

This work extends the analytical solutions for non-Abelian Weizsäcker-Williams fields previously obtained by McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and by Jalilian-Marian, Kovner, McLerran, and Weigert (1997), and builds upon the nuclear rest frame analysis of Kovchegov (1996). The model provides the initial conditions for the Wilson renormalization group equations that evolve the color charge \(\chi\) to higher \(Q^2\) and smaller rapidities, enabling future calculations of nuclear gluon shadowing and other phenomenological observables relevant for deep inelastic scattering experiments off nuclei at HERA and for ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions.
