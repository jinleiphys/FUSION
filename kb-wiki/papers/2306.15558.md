# Collider constraints on massive gravitons coupling to photons

> We study the discovery potential of massive graviton-like spin-2 particles coupled to standard model fields, produced in photon-photon collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) as well as in electron-positron ($e^+e^-$) collisions, within an effective theory with and without universal couplings. Our focus is on a massive graviton G coupled to the electromagnetic field, which decays via $\mathrm{G}\to γγ$ and leads to a resonant excess of diphotons over the light-by-light scattering continuum at the LHC, and of triphoton final states at $e^+e^-$ colliders. Based on similar searches performed for pseudoscalar axion-like particles (ALPs), and taking into account the different cross sections, $γγ$ partial widths, and decay kinematics of the pseudoscalar and tensor particles, we reinterpret existing experimental bounds on the ALP-$γ$ coupling into G-$γ$ ones. Using the available data, exclusion limits on the graviton-photon coupling are set down to $g_{\mathrm{G}γγ}\approx 1$--0.05~TeV$^{-1}$ for masses $m_\mathrm{G} \approx 100$~MeV--2~TeV. Such bounds can be improved by factors of 100 at Belle~II in the low-mass region, and of 4 at the HL-LHC at high masses, with their expected full integrated luminosities.

## Key claim
The paper derives new exclusion limits on the coupling of massive spin-2 graviton-like particles to photons by reinterpreting existing experimental bounds originally set for pseudoscalar axion-like particles (ALPs), accounting for the different cross sections, diphoton partial widths, and decay kinematics of the two particle types. The limits depend on whether one assumes a photophilic scenario (100% branching fraction to diphotons) or a more realistic universal-coupling scenario where the graviton decays to all Standard Model particles.

## Method
The authors recast existing experimental searches for ALPs coupling to photons from LHC ultraperipheral collisions (PbPb at 5.02 TeV and pp at 14 TeV) and from electron-positron colliders (Belle II, BES-III, LEP) into bounds on the graviton-photon coupling. They generate simulated ALP and graviton events using the gamma-UPC code and MadGraph5_aMC@NLO with effective field theory Lagrangians, apply the same experimental selection criteria, and use the ratio of cross sections and branching fractions to translate ALP coupling limits into graviton coupling limits.

## Key numbers
* Exclusion limits on the graviton-photon coupling are set down to g_{Gγγ} ≈ 1–0.05 TeV^{-1} for graviton masses m_G ≈ 100 MeV–2 TeV.
* In PbPb UPCs at 5.02 TeV, assuming photophilic scenario, graviton production cross sections are about five times larger than ALP ones due to spin counting; with universal couplings (B_{G→γγ} ≈ 0.05), graviton cross sections are about four times smaller than ALP ones.
* The diphoton branching fraction for a graviton with universal couplings is B_{G→γγ} ≈ 44.4% at m_G = 100 MeV, 6.1% at 5 GeV, 5% at 100 GeV, 4.3% at 1 TeV, and 4.2% at 2 TeV.
* Future improvements: bounds can be improved by factors of 100 at Belle II in the low-mass region and by a factor of 4 at the HL-LHC at high masses with their expected full integrated luminosities.

## Context
This work extends the study of massive spin-2 particles produced via photon fusion at colliders, building on exploratory work by Atwood (1999), Ahern (2000), Fichet (2014), Inan (2018), and Shao (2022). It directly reinterprets experimental ALP search results from CMS (2018), ATLAS (2023), TOTEM (2021), Belle II (2020), BESIII (2022), and LEP experiments (Baillargeon 1995). The results enable future searches for massive graviton-like particles at the HL-LHC and Belle II, and connect to broader theoretical frameworks including extra-dimensional models (ADD, Randall-Sundrum) and bimetric gravity theories.
