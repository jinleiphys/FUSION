# Chiral symmetry breaking in continuum QCD

> We present a quantitative analysis of chiral symmetry breaking in two-flavour continuum QCD in the quenched limit. The theory is set-up at perturbative momenta, where asymptotic freedom leads to precise results. The evolution of QCD towards the hadronic phase is achieved by means of dynamical hadronisation in the non-perturbative functional renormalisation group approach. We use a vertex expansion scheme based on gauge-invariant operators and discuss its convergence properties and the remaining systematic errors. In particular we present results for the quark propagator, the full tensor structure and momentum dependence of the quark-gluon vertex, and the four-fermi scatterings.

## Key claim

This work presents the first closed, self-consistent and quantitative computation for quenched continuum QCD in the vacuum using the functional renormalisation group, with no additional model input beyond the fundamental parameters of QCD (the strong coupling and current quark masses set at perturbative momentum scales). The quantitative agreement with lattice QCD results is achieved only when including the full momentum dependence of all eight tensor structures of the quark-gluon vertex together with their gauge-invariant partner structures in higher vertices, and the authors note that a naive inclusion of the quark-gluon vertex basis without these partner structures would produce considerably different results.

## Method

The authors solve the matter sector of quenched QCD using the functional renormalisation group (FRG) with a vertex expansion scheme based on gauge-invariant operators, using Yang-Mills FRG results for gluon and ghost propagators from Fischer et al. (2008) and FP as input. They include a fully momentum-dependent quark propagator and quark-gluon vertex (all eight tensor structures in Landau gauge), dynamically generated four-fermi interactions in a Fierz-complete basis, and employ dynamical hadronisation to introduce effective mesonic degrees of freedom (pions and sigma) that remove the resonant four-fermi channel. Modified Slavnov-Taylor identities (mSTIs) are used to include higher quark-gluon interactions (two-quark-two-gluon and two-quark-three-gluon vertices) as gauge-invariant completions of the non-classical quark-gluon vertex tensor structures.

## Key numbers

- Current quark mass set at 20 GeV: M_q(20 GeV) = 1.3 MeV
- Strong coupling at 20 GeV: alpha_s(20 GeV) = 0.21
- All running couplings from different vertices (quark-gluon, ghost-gluon, three-gluon) coincide down to momenta of 4 GeV
- Dimensionful quantities from lattice results of Bowman et al. (2004, 2005) are rescaled by a factor of 0.91 to match the scales of Sternbeck et al. (2006) and the FRG results
- All but the resonant pion four-fermi channel constitute subleading contributions with a quantitative effect of less than 5 percent
- The critical gauge coupling for chiral symmetry breaking is shown as alpha_crit in Fig. 13 (numerical value not given in text)

## Context

This work extends the functional renormalisation group approach to QCD from earlier studies by Braun (2008, 2009) and provides the foundation, together with a related study of the unquenched system by Braun (2014), for a quantitative FRG framework for QCD (the fQCD collaboration). The results agree very well with quenched lattice QCD results from Bowman et al. (2005) for the quark propagator, and the findings on the relative importance of quark-gluon vertex tensor structures agree with recent Dyson-Schwinger studies by Hopfer et al. (2013), Williams (2014), and Aguilar et al. (2014). The work enables future quantitative studies of QCD at finite chemical potential, where mismatches in thermal/density fluctuation scales lead to large systematic errors as detailed by Helmboldt et al. (2014).
