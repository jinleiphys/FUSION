# Kinetics of polarization in non-relativistic scattering

> An approach is developed, which essentially simplifies derivation of kinetic equation for polarization in non-relativistic scattering. This approach is applicable for collision of projectile particles with a target for any spins of colliding particles. The most detailed consideration is performed for the case of spin 1/2 projectile particle interacting with spin 1/2 particle of a target. The solution of the kinetic equation for the case of zero initial polarization is presented.

## Key claim

The paper develops a simplified approach for deriving the kinetic equation describing spin evolution in non-relativistic scattering, applicable for any spins of colliding particles. The approach is restricted to the case where projectiles remain in the beam after scattering (small-angle scattering), and the mass of the first particle is much larger than the mass of the second particle.

## Method

The authors start from the quantum Liouville equation and express the time evolution of spin operators using wave packets normalized to unity, introducing a convergence factor \(e^{-\lambda r}\) to handle integration by parts. They derive a general kinetic equation for the expectation value of an arbitrary spin operator, expressed in terms of the scattering amplitude operator \(F\) and the density matrix of the system. The method is then applied explicitly to the cases of a spin-1/2 projectile scattering from a spin-0 target and from a spin-1/2 target, yielding closed-form solutions for the polarization evolution.

## Key numbers

- For \(S_2=0\) (spinless target), the relaxation rate is \(\omega = vN \int d\Omega_{\bm n} \, \nu^2 |f_1|^2\), where \(\nu = [\bm n \times \bm n_0]\) and \(f_1\) is the spin-flip scattering amplitude.
- For \(S_2=1/2\) with zero beam polarization (\(\bm\zeta_2=0\)), the polarization decays as \(\bm\zeta_1(t) = [\bm\zeta_1(0)-\bm n_0(\bm\zeta_1(0)\cdot\bm n_0)] e^{A_1 t} + \bm n_0(\bm\zeta_1(0)\cdot\bm n_0) e^{(A_1+B_1) t}\), where \(A_1<0\) and \(A_1+B_1<0\).
- For \(S_2=1/2\) with beam polarization parallel to \(\bm n_0\), the solution includes an oscillatory term with frequency \(\omega = (A_2+B_2)\zeta_2\).
- For \(S_2=1/2\) with beam polarization perpendicular to \(\bm n_0\), the solution includes an oscillatory term with frequency \(\Omega = \sqrt{A_2^2 \zeta_2^2 - B_1^2/4}\).

## Context

This work extends the kinetic equation approach of Pipkin (1964), who first derived the equation for the density matrix via the quantum Liouville equation and applied it to spin-exchange optical pumping, and of Baryshevsky (1996), who re-derived and applied it to spin evolution. It also builds on the work of Milstein and Strakhovenko (2005), who investigated polarization buildup during interaction of stored protons with a polarized target using cross sections for spin-flip and spin-non-flip transitions. The present approach simplifies the derivation for arbitrary spins and provides explicit solutions for the spin-1/2 case, enabling future generalization to more complicated scattering scenarios.
