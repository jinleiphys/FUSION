# Bulk viscous corrections to screening and damping in QCD at high temperatures

> Non-equilibrium corrections to the distribution functions of quarks and gluons in a hot and dense QCD medium modify the "hard thermal loops" (HTL). The HTLs determine the retarded, advanced, and symmetric (time-ordered) propagators for gluons with soft momenta as well as the Debye screening and Landau damping mass scales. We compute such corrections to a thermal as well as to a non-thermal fixed point.The screening and damping mass scales are sensitive to the bulk pressure and hence to (pseudo-) critical dynamical scaling of the bulk viscosity in the vicinity of a second-order critical point. This could be reflected in the properties of quarkonium bound states in the deconfined phase and in the dynamics of soft gluon fields.

## Key claim

Bulk viscous corrections to the quark and gluon distribution functions modify the Debye screening mass and Landau damping mass scales in a hot QCD medium, but the effect is isotropic and preserves the functional form of the hard thermal loop (HTL) self-energies; the magnitude of the correction depends on the specific model for the non-equilibrium distribution and on the power-law exponent \(a\) of the momentum dependence in the bulk viscous distortion.

## Method

The authors compute the retarded (advanced) and symmetric (time-ordered) gluon self-energies in the HTL approximation, starting from a distribution function that adds an isotropic bulk-viscous correction \(\delta_{\rm bulk}f(k)\) to the ideal thermal or non-thermal fixed-point distribution. For the thermal fixed point, they parameterize \(\delta_{\rm bulk}f(k) = (k/T)^a \Phi\, f_{\rm id}(k)(1\pm f_{\rm id}(k))\) and evaluate the resulting shifts to the Debye mass scales \(m_{R,D}^2\) and \(m_{F,D}^2\) to linear order in \(\Phi\), with explicit coefficients for fermion and gluon loops. For the non-thermal fixed point, they use a distortion inspired by anisotropic hydrodynamics, \(f(p) = f_{\rm id}\bigl(\sqrt{p^2+m^2(1+\tilde{\Phi})}/T\bigr)\), expand to linear order in \(\Phi\), and compute the corresponding mass shifts. They then obtain the resummed longitudinal gluon propagator and the static heavy-quark potential (real and imaginary parts) in the presence of these bulk-viscous corrections.

## Key numbers

* For the thermal fixed point with \(a=1\) and \(\tilde{\mu}=0\), the coefficient for the shift of the retarded Debye mass from the quark loop is \(c_R^{(q)}(a=1)=2\) and from the gluon loop is \(c_R^{(g)}(a=1)=2\); for the symmetric self-energy, \(c_F^{(q)}(a=1)=3\) and \(c_F^{(g)}(a=1)=3\) (Table I).
* For the thermal fixed point with \(a=2\) and \(\tilde{\mu}=0\), \(c_R^{(q)}(a=2)=54\zeta(3)/\pi^2\) and \(c_R^{(g)}(a=2)=36\zeta(3)/\pi^2\); for the symmetric self-energy, \(c_F^{(q)}(a=2)=108\zeta(3)/\pi^2\) and \(c_F^{(g)}(a=2)=72\zeta(3)/\pi^2\) (Table I).
* For the thermal fixed point with \(a=3\) and \(\tilde{\mu}=0\), \(c_R^{(q)}(a=3)=14\pi^2/5\) and \(c_R^{(g)}(a=3)=8\pi^2/5\); for the symmetric self-energy, \(c_F^{(q)}(a=3)=7\pi^2\) and \(c_F^{(g)}(a=3)=4\pi^2\) (Table I).
* The real part of the static potential is \(\mathrm{Re}\, V(r) = - (g^2 C_F)/(4\pi r)\, e^{-r\sqrt{m_{R,D}^2+\delta m_{R,D}^2}} + 2F_Q\), where the free-energy term \(2F_Q = - (g^2 C_F)/(4\pi) \sqrt{m_{R,D}^2+\delta m_{R,D}^2}\).
* The imaginary part of the static potential is \(\mathrm{Im}\, V(r) = - (g^2 C_F T)/(4\pi) \, (m_{F,D}^2+\delta m_{F,D}^2)/(m_{R,D}^2+\delta m_{R,D}^2) \, \phi(\hat{r})\), with \(\hat{r} \equiv r\sqrt{m_{R,D}^2+\delta m_{R,D}^2}\) and \(\phi(\hat{r})\) defined by an integral over \(z\).

## Context

This work extends the HTL framework for non-equilibrium corrections to the gluon self-energy, which previously focused on shear-viscous (anisotropic) corrections (Dumitru 2007, Dumitru 2009, Burnier 2009), to include isotropic bulk-viscous corrections. The bulk viscosity \(\zeta\) in QCD at high temperatures was computed to leading order by Arnold (2006), who found it to be very small (\(\zeta/\eta \sim \alpha_s^4\)), but lattice results (Bazavov 2014) show a large trace anomaly near \(T\sim\Lambda_{\rm QCD}\), suggesting that \(\zeta\) may grow near the confinement-deconfinement temperature (Kharzeev and Tuchin). The results enable phenomenological studies of heavy quarkonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions that incorporate bulk-viscous effects on the heavy-quark potential, complementing existing shear-viscous calculations (Strickland 2011, Krouppa 2015, Krouppa 2016).
