# Resonance propagation in heavy-ion scattering

> The formalism developed by Jain and Kundu for the propagation of a resonance in the nuclear medium has been modified to the case of heavy-ion collisions at relativistic energies. The formalism includes coherently the contribution to the observed di-lepton production from the decay of the resonance inside as well as outside the nuclear medium. The calculated results are presented for the $ρ$-meson production. It is observed that, in general, the shape, magnitude and peak position of the coherently summed invariant mass distribution is much different from that obtained by summing the inside and outside contributions incoherently. Therefore, while inferring the modification of hadron properties produced in heavy-ion collisions from experiments, it is important that in theoretical calculations one includes the decay of the propagating hadron from inside and outside the heavy-ion system coherently. We also find that the mass distribution is sensitive to the amount of the medium modification of the $ρ$-meson.

# Resonance propagation in heavy-ion scattering

## Key claim

The shape, magnitude, and peak position of the coherently summed invariant mass distribution for di-lepton production from a propagating resonance in heavy-ion collisions differs substantially from the incoherent sum of inside-nucleus and outside-nucleus decay contributions; this difference increases with resonance speed and is larger for smaller nuclear systems, meaning that any inference of in-medium hadron modification from experimental data must include coherent summation of both decay contributions.

## Method

The authors extended the Jain and Kundu (1996) formalism for resonance propagation in proton-nucleus collisions to the case of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, assuming a resonance is produced when one nucleon from the projectile and one from the target collide. They derived an eikonal approximation for the resonance propagator including a complex optical potential, split the decay amplitude into inside-nucleus and outside-nucleus contributions using a sharp-surface approximation, and computed the invariant mass distribution of the decay products for a rho meson produced in Pb+Pb and S+Au collisions at two different resonance velocities.

## Key numbers

- Free width of the rho meson taken as 150 MeV.
- Optical potential for the rho meson in nuclear matter: V/v_R = -(45 + i64) MeV, derived using sigma_T^{rho N} = 38 mb, alpha = 0.7, and rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}.
- At 0.9c rho speed in Pb+Pb collisions, the inside decay spectrum shows a shift toward lower mass by about 70 MeV.
- The width of the summed cross-section in Pb+Pb at 0.6c broadens to about twice the free width (approximately 300 MeV).
- Outside decay probability reaches up to 10% for Pb+Pb and 25% for S+Au systems, integrated over kinematically allowed invariant mass.
- Mass shifts of 50, 100, and 150 MeV were explored for the Pb+Pb system at 0.9c rho speed.

## Context

This work extends the resonance propagation formalism of Jain and Kundu (1996), originally developed for proton-nucleus collisions, to the case of heavy-ion collisions at relativistic energies. The paper addresses the same experimental observations of di-lepton production in heavy-ion collisions reported by Agakichiev et al. (1995) and others, where in-medium hadron modification is inferred from invariant mass spectra. The formalism enables more accurate theoretical calculations for interpreting heavy-ion data by demonstrating that coherent summation of inside and outside decay contributions is essential, and that the shape of the rho invariant mass distribution is highly sensitive to the magnitude of in-medium mass modification, with large mass shifts (approximately 100 MeV and beyond) producing very broad distributions similar to those seen in heavy-ion experiments.
