# The Regulated GeAs Cycles with the New $^{63}$Ga(p,$γ$)$^{64}$Ge and $^{64}$Ge(p,$γ$)$^{65}$As Reaction Rates and Their Impact on the GS 1826$-$24 Clocked Bursts and SAX J1808.4$-$3658 Photospheric Radius Expansion Bursts

> The $^{63}$Ga(p,$γ$)$^{64}$Ge and $^{64}$Ge(p,$γ$)$^{65}$As thermonuclear reactions connect the ZnGa and GeAs cycles by diverting the flow of the rapid proton capture process from $^{63}$Ga to $^{65}$As. Changes in these two reaction rates regulate the ZnGa and GeAs cycles and may affect the modeled properties matching with the observed counterparts of a type I X-ray burster. We implement the latest $^{63}$Ga(p,$γ$)$^{64}$Ge and $^{64}$Ge(p,$γ$)$^{65}$As reaction rates to the state-of-the-art self-consistent one-dimensional multi-zone thermo-hydrodynamic code, KEPLER, to study the influence of these new reaction rates on the models of the GS 1826$-$24 clocked burster and SAX J1808.4$-$3658 photospheric radius expansion burster. Both new reaction rates obtained by Lu et al. [Phys. Rev. C 110, 065804 (2024)] are determined from complementing the experimental input with the nuclear spectroscopic information deduced from the full pf-shell space configuration-interaction shell-model calculations. By constraining the models on reproducing the observed burst peak, light-curve profile, fluence, and recurrence time, we find that the impact of the newly measured proton thresholds and respective proton-capture reactions on the burst light-curve profile of the GS 1826$-$24 clocked burster is, in fact, not as significant as claimed by Zhou et al. [Nat. Phys. 19, 1091 (2023)]. With or without the inclusion of the newly determined reaction rate of the highly influential $^{22}$Mg($α$,p)$^{25}$Al reaction, the impact of the new $^{63}$Ga(p,$γ$)$^{64}$Ge and $^{64}$Ge(p,$γ$)$^{65}$As reaction rates on SAX J1808.4$-$3658 photospheric radius expansion bursts is evident. Our finding indicates that the models reproducing the 2002 October epoch of SAX J1808.4$-$3658 photospheric radius expansion burster is more sensitive to the uncertainties of thermonuclear reaction rates.

# The Regulated GeAs Cycles with the New $^{63}$Ga(p,$\gamma$)$^{64}$Ge and $^{64}$Ge(p,$\gamma$)$^{65}$As Reaction Rates and Their Impact on the GS 1826$-$24 Clocked Bursts and SAX J1808.4$-$3658 Photospheric Radius Expansion Bursts

## Key claim

The impact of the newly measured proton thresholds and respective proton-capture reactions on the burst light-curve profile of the GS 1826$-$24 clocked burster is not as significant as claimed by Zhou et al. (2023), contrary to what the abstract's general statement about "may affect" might suggest. The new $^{63}$Ga(p,$\gamma$)$^{64}$Ge and $^{64}$Ge(p,$\gamma$)$^{65}$As reaction rates do show evident impact on SAX J1808.4$-$3658 photospheric radius expansion bursts, but the models reproducing the 2002 October epoch of this burster are more sensitive to the uncertainties of thermonuclear reaction rates overall.

## Method

The authors implemented the latest $^{63}$Ga(p,$\gamma$)$^{64}$Ge and $^{64}$Ge(p,$\gamma$)$^{65}$As reaction rates from Lu et al. (2024), which were determined by complementing experimental input with nuclear spectroscopic information from full pf-shell space configuration-interaction shell-model calculations, into the self-consistent one-dimensional multi-zone thermo-hydrodynamic code KEPLER. They modeled the GS 1826$-$24 clocked burster and SAX J1808.4$-$3658 photospheric radius expansion burster, constraining the models to reproduce observed burst peak, light-curve profile, fluence, and recurrence time. The study examined the influence both with and without inclusion of the newly determined $^{22}$Mg($\alpha$,p)$^{25}$Al reaction rate.

## Key numbers

- The mass fractions of accreted H and He for GS 1826$-$24 clocked bursts are approximately 0.7 and 0.28, respectively, with an accretion rate of about 0.1 times the Eddington-limited accretion rate ($\dot{m}_\mathrm{Edd}=8.8\times10^4$ g cm$^{-2}$ s$^{-1}$).
- The $^{63}$Ga(p,$\gamma$)$^{64}$Ge reaction rate is up to 4 orders of magnitude higher than the $^{63}$Ga(p,$\alpha$)$^{60}$Zn reaction rate at temperatures $T=0.5$-$1.6$ GK.
- Alternative paths via $^{64}$Ga(p,$\gamma$)$^{65}$Ge and $^{65}$Ga(p,$\gamma$)$^{66}$Ge reactions have strengths up to a factor of approximately 13 higher than $^{63}$Ga(p,$\gamma$)$^{64}$Ge at $T=0.5$-$1.6$ GK.

## Context

This work extends the sensitivity studies on X-ray burst models performed by Cyburt et al. (2016) and Jacobs et al. (2018), who identified $^{63}$Ga(p,$\gamma$)$^{64}$Ge as the fourth key influential (p,$\gamma$) reaction affecting energy release and burst light-curve profiles of the GS 1826$-$24 clocked burster. It contradicts the stronger claim made by Zhou et al. (2023) regarding the significance of these reactions for GS 1826$-$24. The study uses the KEPLER code following the approach of Heger (2007) and Woosley (2004), which provides self-consistent one-dimensional multi-zone thermohydrodynamics including convection and mixing, unlike the post-processing models of Parikh et al. (2008, 2009) or the one-zone models of Schatz et al. (2001, 2017) and Kimura et al. (2025). The findings enable refined modeling of SAX J1808.4$-$3658 PRE bursts and highlight the need for improved reaction rate uncertainties in the mass region $A=60$-$64$.
