# Pairing enhancement in a two-neutron transfer process through the continuum

> Enhancement, due to constructive interference through the many possible reaction channels, occurs in two-neutron transfer reactions from a bound system $A$ to a weakly-bound system ($A$+2), whenever the intermediate system is unbound and consists of a continuum with a resonant state. We elucidate this phenomenon in the case of $^6$He, modeled as two neutrons in the orbitals of an intermediate $^5$He nucleus, comparing the realistic case of an unbound ($A$+1) system with an artificially bound case. A properly modeled continuum in these calculations is found to be crucial and leads to significant enhancement due to pairing correlations.

## Key claim

Enhancement in two-neutron transfer cross-sections due to pairing correlations occurs when the intermediate ($A$+1) system is unbound and its continuum includes a resonant state, but the magnitude of this enhancement depends on the bombarding energy and is most pronounced at higher beam energies where more continuum states can be populated.

## Method

The authors modeled the sequential two-neutron transfer reaction $^{18}$O($^4$He,$^6$He)$^{16}$O, treating $^6$He as two neutrons in orbitals of an intermediate $^5$He nucleus. They used a transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis to discretize the continuum of $^5$He into pseudostates, and compared three scenarios: a realistic unbound $^5$He with a resonance near 0.7 MeV, and two artificially bound cases with neutron separation energies of 1 MeV and 0.1 MeV. One-neutron transfer form factors were computed in the prior representation, and two-neutron transfer cross-sections were obtained by diagonalizing a Hamiltonian with a contact delta pairing interaction, with the strength adjusted to reproduce the $^6$He ground state energy at -0.975 MeV.

## Key numbers

- One-neutron transfer total cross-section for the continuum case at 21 MeV beam energy: 2.08 mb.
- One-neutron transfer total cross-section for the continuum case at 100 MeV beam energy: 5.95 mb.
- Two-neutron transfer cross-section for the continuum case at 21 MeV beam energy: 0.94 $\mu$b (with pairing) versus 0.51 $\mu$b (unperturbed, $g=0$), giving an enhancement ratio of 1.84.
- Two-neutron transfer cross-section for the continuum case at 100 MeV beam energy: 44 $\mu$b (with pairing) versus 8.6 $\mu$b (unperturbed), giving an enhancement ratio of 5.12.
- Two-neutron transfer cross-section for the bound case ($S_n$ = 0.1 MeV) at 21 MeV beam energy: 6.95 $\mu$b (with pairing) versus 6.89 $\mu$b (unperturbed), giving an enhancement ratio of 1.01.
- Two-neutron transfer cross-section for the bound case ($S_n$ = 0.1 MeV) at 100 MeV beam energy: 147 $\mu$b (with pairing) versus 125 $\mu$b (unperturbed), giving an enhancement ratio of 1.18.
- The resonance state in the continuum of $^5$He was fixed at 0.6912 MeV above zero, close to the experimental $p_{3/2}$ resonance at 0.789 MeV (FCS14PRC).
- The pairing interaction strength $g$ for the continuum case was 10430 MeV-fm$^{-3}$ at 21 MeV and 7827 MeV-fm$^{-3}$ at 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends studies of two-neutron transfer reactions in light exotic nuclei, particularly for Borromean systems like $^6$He, where the intermediate nucleus $^5$He is unbound. It builds on prior work by Oertzen (1991) on pairing enhancement in heavy semi-magic nuclei and by various authors (TAB08PRL, MMV21PRC, VMH15AIP) on the role of continuum couplings. The results are directly relevant to understanding the structure and halo properties of $^6$He (KO00EPJ, TT00PRC, Sferrazza) and could inform future studies of BEC- and BCS-like behavior in the excited states of $^6$He.
