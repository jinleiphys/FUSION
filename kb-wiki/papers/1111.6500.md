# The α-Decay Chains of the $^{287, 288}115$ Isotopes using Relativistic Mean Field Theory

> We study the binding energy, root-mean-square radius and quadrupole deformation parameter for the synthesized superheavy element Z = 115, within the formalism of relativistic mean field theory. The calculation is dones for various isotopes of Z = 115 element, starting from A = 272 to A = 292. A systematic comparison between the binding energies and experimental data is made.The calculated binding energies are in good agreement with experimental result. The results show the prolate deformation for the ground state of these nuclei. The most stable isotope is found to be 282115 nucleus (N = 167) in the isotopic chain. We have also studied Qα and Tα for the α-decay chains of $^{287, 288}$115.

## Key claim

The relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with the NL3 parameter set reproduces the binding energies and alpha-decay properties of the superheavy isotopes 287115 and 288115 in reasonable agreement with experimental data, but the calculated half-lives differ from experiment by factors of roughly 2 to 1000 depending on the specific decay step, and the RMF results systematically overestimate the pairing energy compared to empirical values.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic mean field (RMF) formalism with the NL3 force parameter set to solve the coupled Dirac and meson field equations self-consistently for the isotopic chain of Z=115 from A=272 to A=292. From the resulting binding energies, they computed the Qα values using the relation Qα(N,Z) = BE(N,Z) - BE(N-2,Z-2) - BE(2,2), where BE(2,2)=28.296 MeV for the alpha particle, and then estimated the alpha-decay half-lives using the phenomenological formula of Parkhomenko and Sobiczewski. The calculations were performed for the full alpha-decay chains of 287115 and 288115, and results were compared with the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM), calculations by Silisteanu et al. (2010) and Samanta et al. (2008), and experimental data from Oganessian et al. (2004).

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of 287115: 2056.3 MeV (RMF), compared to experimental Qα of 10.74 MeV and experimental half-life of 32(+155/-14) ms; RMF gives Qα = 11.304 MeV and T1/2 = 0.0158 s
- Binding energy of 288115: 2063.0 MeV (RMF), compared to experimental Qα of 10.61 MeV and experimental half-life of 87(+105/-30) ms; RMF gives Qα = 10.81 MeV and T1/2 = 0.288 s
- Most stable isotope in the chain is 282115 (N=167) with binding energy 2022.8 MeV and quadrupole deformation β2 = 0.182 (prolate)
- For 290115, two nearly degenerate solutions exist: β2 = 0.103 with BE = 2074.53 MeV and β2 = -0.176 with BE = 2074.38 MeV, indicating shape coexistence
- Quadrupole deformation parameters for the chain range from β2 = 0.255 (A=272) to β2 = 0.060 (A=292), all prolate in RMF, while FRDM predicts a shape change to oblate at A=286-288

## Context

This work extends the application of relativistic mean field theory to the superheavy element Z=115, which was synthesized via the fusion-evaporation reaction 243Am + 48Ca by Oganessian et al. (2004). The study compares RMF results with the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) of Moller et al. (1997) and with calculations by Silisteanu et al. (2010) and Samanta et al. (2008). The finding that 282115 is the most stable isotope differs from the FRDM prediction of 286115 as most stable, and the RMF results show all isotopes are prolate deformed while FRDM predicts a transition to oblate shapes at higher neutron numbers. The work enables further theoretical investigation of the island of stability near Z=114-120 and N=172-184, and provides predictions for alpha-decay half-lives that can guide future experiments.
