# Neutron Skin size dependence of the nuclear binding energy

> The nuclear binding energy is studied using a finite temperature density functional theory. A Skyrme interaction is used in this work. Volume, surface, and symmetry energy contributions to the binding energy are investigated. The case of neutron skin is considered in detail. The neutron skin modifies the mass $A$ dependence of various terms and $I$ dependence of the skin thickness is proportional to $I$ for the case of same central density.

## Key claim

The neutron skin size dependence of the nuclear binding energy is studied using a finite temperature density functional theory with Skyrme interactions, showing that the ratio of surface symmetry energy to volume symmetry energy for the neutron-skin-dependent part is much larger (approximately 10) than the corresponding ratio for the neutron-skin-independent part (approximately 1.5), indicating that a large part of the symmetry energy originates from the different spatial distributions of neutrons and protons.

## Method

The authors used a finite temperature density functional theory with a Skyrme interaction, taking the density distribution as a Saxon-Wood form with equal diffuseness for protons and neutrons but allowing different radii. They expanded the total energy around the case of equal neutron and proton radii (R_n = R_p) to first order in the neutron skin size t = R_n - R_p, and extracted the skin-dependent coefficients for volume, surface, volume symmetry, and surface symmetry energy terms by minimizing the Helmholtz free energy for beta-stable nuclei. Three Skyrme parameter sets were used: SLy4 (m*/m = 0.69), SkM* (m*/m = 0.79), and SkM (m* = m), covering a range of effective masses.

## Key numbers

- Volume binding energy coefficient E_V(T=0) ≈ -15.308 MeV (SLy4), -15.127 MeV (SkM*), -15.310 MeV (SkM(m*=m))
- Surface energy coefficient E_S(T=0) ≈ 20.008 MeV (SLy4), 18.756 MeV (SkM*), 18.303 MeV (SkM(m*=m))
- Volume symmetry energy coefficient S_V(T=0) ≈ 31.113 MeV (SLy4), 29.655 MeV (SkM*), 19.685 MeV (SkM(m*=m))
- Surface symmetry energy coefficient S_S(T=0) ≈ -41.035 MeV (SLy4), -43.596 MeV (SkM*), -33.175 MeV (SkM(m*=m))
- Neutron-skin-dependent volume energy coefficient E_V(T=0)_sk ≈ 5.520 MeV/fm (SLy4), 5.584 MeV/fm (SkM*), 6.981 MeV/fm (SkM(m*=m))
- Neutron-skin-dependent surface energy coefficient E_S(T=0)_sk ≈ -17.077 MeV/fm (SLy4), -17.261 MeV/fm (SkM*), -21.691 MeV/fm (SkM(m*=m))
- Neutron-skin-dependent volume symmetry energy coefficient S_V(T=0)_sk ≈ 63.203 MeV/fm (SLy4), 62.787 MeV/fm (SkM*), 63.366 MeV/fm (SkM(m*=m))
- Neutron-skin-dependent surface symmetry energy coefficient S_S(T=0)_sk ≈ -711.559 MeV/fm (SLy4), -706.246 MeV/fm (SkM*), -611.848 MeV/fm (SkM(m*=m))
- Ratio S_S(T)_sk / S_V(T)_sk ≈ 10 for the neutron-skin-dependent part, compared to S_S(T)/S_V(T) ≈ 1.5 for the skin-independent part
- For an assumed t/A^(1/3) = 0.1, the ratio of total surface symmetry energy to total volume symmetry energy becomes approximately 3

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Lee and Mekjian, 2010) which assumed equal neutron and proton radii (R_n = R_p) and thus missed neutron skin effects; here they fully incorporate the neutron skin size dependence by expanding the energy to first order in t = R_n - R_p. The results connect to the broader effort to extract the volume symmetry energy from finite nuclei for application to neutron star physics, as discussed by Danielewicz et al. (2006) and Prakash et al. (1997). The finding that the surface symmetry energy has a much larger neutron-skin dependence than the volume symmetry energy enables improved mass formula fits that include odd powers of the isospin asymmetry I, potentially allowing extraction of neutron skin information from binding energy systematics.
