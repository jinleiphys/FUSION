# Proton Holography -- Discovering Odderon from Scaling Properties of Elastic Scattering

> We investigate the scaling properties of elastic scattering data at ISR and LHC energies and find that the significance of an Odderon observation is larger than the discovery threshold of 5$σ$. As an unexpected by-product of these investigations, for certain experimentally relevant cases, we also conjecture the possibility of proton holography with the help of elastic proton-proton scattering.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the Odderon effect in elastic proton-proton and proton-antiproton scattering is discovered with a statistical significance exceeding the 5σ threshold, based on scaling properties of differential cross-section data at ISR and LHC energies. The claim is hedged by the condition that the scaling function \(H(x)\) is energy-independent for \(pp\) collisions in the 2.76–8.0 TeV range but differs significantly from the \(p\bar p\) scaling function at 1.96 TeV, and that systematic errors must be properly accounted for.

## Method

The authors define a scaling function \(H(x) = \frac{1}{B(s) \sigma_{\rm el}(s)} \frac{d\sigma}{dt}\) with \(x = -t B(s)\), where \(B(s)\) is the nuclear slope and \(\sigma_{\rm el}(s)\) the elastic cross-section. They compare the \(H(x)\) scaling functions for elastic \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 2.76\) and 7.0 TeV (TOTEM data) with that for elastic \(p\bar p\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 1.96\) TeV (D0 data), using confidence level analysis to quantify the statistical significance of the difference. For proton holography, they apply a model-independent Lévy series expansion to reconstruct the \(t\)-dependent nuclear phase from differential cross-section data, validating the method by reproducing the \(\rho_0\) value measured in the Coulomb-Nuclear Interference region.

## Key numbers

- The confidence level of agreement between the \(H(x)\) scaling functions for \(pp\) at \(\sqrt{s} = 7.0\) TeV and \(p\bar p\) at \(\sqrt{s} = 1.96\) TeV is at most \(CL = 3.7 \times 10^{-8}\%\), corresponding to a statistical significance of at least \(6.26\sigma\) for the Odderon signal.
- For \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 2.76\) and 7.0 TeV, the \(H(x,s)\) scaling function is energy-independent at a confidence level of \(CL = 99\%\) (statistical errors) and \(CL = 100\%\) (systematic errors included).
- The phase reconstruction using a 4th-order Lévy expansion converges up to \(-t \lessapprox 0.9\) GeV\(^2\) at \(\sqrt{s} = 13.0\) TeV and up to \(-t \lessapprox 0.5\) GeV\(^2\) at \(\sqrt{s} = 7.0\) TeV.
- The lowest \(|t|\) value analyzed is \(-t \ge 0.00515\) GeV\(^2\) at \(\sqrt{s} = 7\) TeV, outside the Coulomb-Nuclear Interference region.

## Context

This work extends the search for the Odderon, a crossing-odd contribution to elastic scattering proposed by Lukaszuk and Nicolescu (1973), and builds on the scaling analysis of Csorgo et al. (2019) that showed \(H(x)\) scaling holds for \(pp\) collisions at ISR energies (23.5–62.5 GeV) and LHC energies (2.76–7.0 TeV) but with different scaling functions. The proton holography method extends earlier phase reconstruction work by Nagy et al. (1978) at ISR energies, which used the Phillips-Barger model and neglected Odderon contributions, and provides a model-independent Lévy series approach that connects the phase at the diffractive dip to the CNI region, resolving the "Cul-de-Sac" problem discussed by Dremin (2019). This enables future \(t\)-dependent phase measurements in elastic \(pp\) and \(p\bar p\) scattering at TeV energies.
