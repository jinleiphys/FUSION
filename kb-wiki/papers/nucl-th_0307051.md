# Pairing effects on the collectivity of quadrupole states around 32Mg

> The first 2+ states in N=20 isotones including neutron-rich nuclei 32Mg and 30Ne are studied by the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov plus quasiparticle random phase approximation method based on the Green's function approach. The residual interaction between the quasiparticles is consistently derived from the hamiltonian density of Skyrme interactions with explicit velocity dependence. The B(E2) transition probabilities and the excitation energies of the first 2+ states are well described within a single framework. We conclude that pairing effects account largely for the anomalously large B(E2) value and the very low excitation energy in 32Mg.

## Key claim

Pairing correlations, rather than static deformation, are the essential microscopic mechanism that accounts for the anomalously large B(E2) value and very low excitation energy of the first 2+ state in 32Mg and 30Ne, within a spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov plus quasiparticle random phase approximation framework.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations to obtain spherical ground states for N=20 isotones, then solved the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) equations in coordinate space using a Green's function approach. The residual interaction between quasiparticles was consistently derived from the same Skyrme Hamiltonian density (SkM* and SkP parameter sets) including all explicit momentum-dependent terms, and a density-dependent zero-range pairing interaction was used with strength adjusted to reproduce the experimental neutron pairing gap in 30Ne.

## Key numbers

- B(E2) value in 32Mg: 15.0 +/- 2.5 single-particle units (experimental)
- B(E2) value in 24Mg: 21.0 +/- 5.8 single-particle units (experimental)
- B(E2) value in 34Mg: 19.2 +/- 3.8 single-particle units (experimental)
- Energy ratio E(4+)/E(2+) in 32Mg: 2.6 (experimental)
- Energy ratio E(4+)/E(2+) in 24Mg: 3.0 (experimental)
- Energy ratio E(4+)/E(2+) in 34Mg: 3.2 (experimental)
- Neutron pairing gap in 30Ne: 1.26 MeV (experimental, used to fix pairing strength)
- Neutron pairing strength for SkM*: V_pair = -418 MeV fm^-3
- Neutron pairing strength for SkP: V_pair = -400 MeV fm^-3
- N=20 shell gap in 40Ca: 4.2 MeV (HF with SkM*)
- N=20 shell gap in 30Ne: 3.4 MeV (HF with SkM*)
- N=16 shell gap in 40Ca: 2.4 MeV (HF with SkM*)
- N=16 shell gap in 30Ne: 4.0 MeV (HF with SkM*)
- Average number of neutrons in fp shell in 30Ne: approximately 0.8 (HFB)
- Average number of neutrons in fp shell in 32Mg: approximately 0.5 (HFB)

## Context

This work extends earlier mean-field studies (e.g., Terasaki and Flocard (1997), Rutz and collaborators (1999)) that found spherical ground states for 32Mg and 30Ne, and contrasts with shell model calculations (e.g., Warburton and collaborators (1990), Utsuno and collaborators (1999)) that emphasized 2p-2h configurations across N=20 without distinguishing pairing from deformation. The results also address discrepancies in angular-momentum projected generator coordinate method calculations with the Gogny force (Rodriguez-Guzman and collaborators (2002, 2003)), which gave excitation energies somewhat higher than experiment (about 1.5 MeV in 32Mg and about 2.1 MeV in 30Ne). The work enables future studies that combine both pairing and deformation effects in a unified framework for neutron-rich nuclei around N=20.
