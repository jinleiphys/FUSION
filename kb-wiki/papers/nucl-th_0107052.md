# Relativistic corrections in (gamma,N) knockout reactions

> We develop a fully relativistic DWIA model for photonuclear reactions using the relativistic mean field theory for the bound state and the Pauli reduction of the scattering state which is calculated from a relativistic optical potential. Results for the 12C(gamma,p) and 16O(gamma,p) differential cross sections and photon asymmetries are displayed in a photon energy range between 60 and 257 MeV, and compared with nonrelativistic DWIA calculations. The effects of the spinor distortion and of the effective momentum approximation for the scattering state are discussed. The sensitivity of the model to different prescriptions for the one-body current operator is investigated. The off-shell ambiguities are large in (gamma,p) calculations, and even larger in (gamma,n) knockout.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a fully relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) model can describe the direct knockout (DKO) mechanism as the leading contribution to (gamma,p) cross sections at low photon energies, in contrast to nonrelativistic calculations which require additional meson exchange currents (MEC); however, this conclusion is heavily qualified by the finding that off-shell ambiguities in the one-body current operator produce large variations in the results, especially for (gamma,n) knockout where the DKO mechanism alone is insufficient.

## Method

The authors developed a fully relativistic DWIA model using Dirac-Hartree bound state wave functions from relativistic mean field theory and a Pauli reduction of the scattering state calculated from a relativistic optical potential. They computed differential cross sections and photon asymmetries for 12C(gamma,p) and 16O(gamma,p) reactions over a photon energy range of 60 to 257 MeV, comparing results with nonrelativistic DWIA calculations using the same bound state wave functions (upper components of the relativistic spinors) and optical potentials. Three different current-conserving (cc1, cc2, cc3) prescriptions for the one-body electromagnetic current operator were tested, and the same spectroscopic factors from prior (e,e'p) analysis (0.56 for 12C, 0.70 for 16O) were applied.

## Key numbers

* Spectroscopic factors applied: 0.56 for 12C and 0.70 for 16O, obtained from fitting RDWIA (e,e'p) results to data.
* Photon energy range investigated: 60 to 257 MeV for (gamma,p) reactions; nonrelativistic calculations were not extended above 200 MeV.
* Missing momentum values explored in the considered energy range: between about 200 and 1000 MeV/c.
* For 16O(gamma,p) at 60 MeV, RDWIA results with the cc2 current reproduced the magnitude and shape of experimental data, while nonrelativistic results were always smaller than data.
* For 12C(gamma,p), RDWIA calculations with the cc2 current overestimated the data by a factor of 2.
* For 16O(gamma,n), RDWIA calculations with the cc1 current at 150 and 200 MeV fit the data, but this was attributed to unreliable off-shell effects; cc2 (and cc3, which coincide for neutrons) results largely underestimated experimental cross sections.

## Context

This work extends prior relativistic DWIA analyses of (gamma,p) reactions by Lotz (1993) and Johansson (1993) to a wider energy range and to (gamma,n) knockout, while also providing a consistent comparison with nonrelativistic DWIA calculations using identical bound state and optical potential ingredients. The authors had previously applied the same RDWIA framework to (e,e'p) reactions (Meucci, 2001) and here investigate how relativistic effects differ in photonuclear reactions where larger missing momenta are probed. The results enable future work to incorporate meson exchange currents and delta-excitation mechanisms within a relativistic framework to resolve the large off-shell ambiguities, particularly for (gamma,n) reactions where the DKO mechanism alone is inadequate.
