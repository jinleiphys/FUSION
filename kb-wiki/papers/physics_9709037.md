# Bound States and Scattering Processes in the ^4He_3 Atomic System

> We present a mathematically rigorous method for solving three-atomic bound state and scattering problems. The method is well suited for applications in systems where the inter-atomic interaction is of a hard-core nature. It has been employed to obtain the ground- and excited-state energies for the Helium trimer and to calculate, for the first time, the scattering phase shifts and wave-functions for the He atom-He dimer at ultra-low energies.

## Key claim

The paper presents a mathematically rigorous method for solving three-atomic bound state and scattering problems that is specifically designed for systems with hard-core inter-atomic interactions, and demonstrates its application to the helium trimer; however, the method's success is qualified by the need for extremely large computational resources (grids up to 600 knots, cut-off radii up to 600 angstroms, and up to 5 Gb of memory) and calculation times of ten or more hours per phase shift point when including two partial waves.

## Method

The authors solved the two-dimensional integro-differential Faddeev equations in configuration space for total angular momentum L=0, incorporating a hard-core version of the boundary-condition model that requires the wave function to vanish on the core boundary. They employed a finite-difference algorithm with grids in hyperradius and hyperangle variables, using the HFDHE2 and HFD-B potentials of Aziz and co-workers as the He-He interaction, and calculated both bound state energies and scattering phase shifts for the (2+1 to 2+1) and (2+1 to 1+1+1) processes at ultra-low energies.

## Key numbers

- Dimer bound state energy: -0.8301 mK for HFDHE2 potential, -1.6854 mK for HFD-B potential
- He-He scattering length: 124.7 angstrom for HFDHE2 potential, 88.6 angstrom for HFD-B potential
- Ground state energy of helium trimer with HFD-B potential including l=0 and l=2 partial waves: 0.131 K
- Excited state energy of helium trimer with HFD-B potential including l=0 and l=2 partial waves: 2.8 mK
- Atom-dimer scattering length for HFD-B potential: 145 +/- 5 angstrom when including both l=0 and l=2 partial waves
- Inverse wave number for trimer excited state with HFD-B potential including both l=0 and l=2: approximately 89 angstrom
- Computational requirements: cut-off radius up to 400-600 angstrom, grids with up to 600 knots in both hyperradius and hyperangle, storage up to 5 Gb

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of the helium trimer using Faddeev equations (Nakaichi-Maeda and Lim, 1983; Cornelius and Gloeckle, 1986; Carbonell, Gignoux, and Merkuriev, 1993) and variational/Monte Carlo methods (McMillan, 1983; Pandharipande et al., 1983; Rick, Lynch, and Doll, 1991), and provides the first calculation of scattering phase shifts for He atom-He dimer at ultra-low energies. The method enables future studies of various three-atomic systems with hard-core interactions, including calculations of cross-sections and recombination rates, and addresses open questions about Efimov states in the helium trimer system.
