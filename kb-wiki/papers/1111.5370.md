# Status of the QCD phase diagram from lattice calculations

> The present knowledge of the QCD phase diagram based on simulations of lattice QCD is summarised. The main questions are whether there is a critical point in the QCD phase diagram and whether it is related to a chiral phase transition. It is shown that QCD at imaginary chemical potentials has a rich phase structure, which can be determined in a controlled way without sign problem and which severely constrains the phase structure at real chemical potentials.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while some lattice QCD signals (reweighting, Taylor expansion radius-of-convergence, canonical ensemble) are consistent with a critical point in the QCD phase diagram, their systematic uncertainties do not yet permit a definite conclusion for physical QCD in the continuum; following the chiral critical surface with controlled systematics instead shows that the chiral phase transition weakens with moderate chemical potential, which would lead away from the physical point.

## Method

The author reviews lattice QCD simulations at imaginary chemical potential (which has no sign problem) and uses analytic continuation to constrain the real-chemical-potential phase diagram. The chiral critical surface is mapped by computing the Binder cumulant of the chiral condensate or Polyakov loop as a function of quark mass and chemical potential, extracting the curvature of the critical line via Taylor expansion or direct derivative evaluation. For the deconfinement critical surface in the heavy-quark regime, tricritical scaling from the 3d Z(3) Potts model is applied.

## Key numbers

- Curvature of the pseudo-critical temperature: kappa = 0.059(2)(4) from improved staggered quarks on N_t=4,8 lattices with physical strange quark mass and light quarks extrapolated to chiral limit assuming O(4) scaling (Tc/μ collaboration, 2010)
- Curvature of the pseudo-critical temperature: kappa = 0.066(20) from continuum-extrapolated simulations on N_t=6,8,10 lattices with physical quark masses (Tc/w collaboration, 2011)
- For N_f=3 on N_t=4 lattices, the chiral critical surface curvature coefficients: c_1 = -3.3(3), c_2 = -47(20) (Philipsen and collaborators, 2010)
- For non-degenerate quarks with physical strange quark mass on N_t=4: c_1 = -39(8) for the light quark critical mass (Philipsen and collaborators, 2011)
- Reweighting method at physical quark masses on N_t=4 lattices gives a critical point at mu_B^E ~ 360 MeV (Fodor and Katz, 2004)
- Canonical ensemble on 6^3 x 4 lattices with m_pi ~ 700-800 MeV gives mu_B/T ~ 2.6 at the critical point (Liu and collaborators, 2011)
- Ratio of critical pion masses on N_t=4 vs N_t=6 for N_f=3: m_pi(N_t=4)/m_pi(N_t=6) ~ 1.8

## Context

This review extends and systematizes the lattice QCD phase diagram program, contrasting the reweighting approach of Fodor and Katz (2004) and the Taylor expansion method of Gavai and Gupta (2008) with the chiral critical surface mapping by Philipsen and collaborators (2010, 2011). The key new insight is that the critical structures at imaginary chemical potential (Roberge and Weiss, 1984) severely constrain the real-chemical-potential phase diagram, showing that the chiral transition weakens with mu rather than strengthening toward a critical point. This enables future work to distinguish whether a critical point exists at larger chemical potential (mu_B > 500 MeV) or belongs to physics unrelated to chiral symmetry breaking.
