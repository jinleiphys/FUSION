# Analytically Solvable Mean-Field Potential for Stable and Exotic Nuclei

> Slater determinants built from the single-particle wave functions of the analytically solvable Ginocchio potential are used to approximate the self-consistent Hartree-Fock solutions for the ground states of nuclei. The results indicate that the Ginocchio potential provides a good parametrization of the nuclear mean field for a wide range of nuclei, including those at the limit of particle stability.

## Key claim

Slater determinants built from the analytically solvable Ginocchio potential, with parameters chosen variationally, can reproduce self-consistent Hartree-Fock results for ground-state energies, density distributions, and rms radii to within 2% for binding energies and 0.35% for neutron rms radii, for both stable nuclei and nuclei at the limit of particle stability.

## Method

The authors constructed Slater determinants from single-particle eigenstates of the Ginocchio potential, which depends on four parameters (scaling, shape, effective mass, and depth). They minimized the expectation value of a Skyrme Hamiltonian (using the SI parametrization) with respect to these parameters in two schemes: G4 with global parameters for all states, and G8 with separate parameters for neutrons and protons. They compared the resulting ground-state energies, rms radii, and density distributions to full self-consistent Hartree-Fock (SCHF) calculations and to variational calculations using harmonic-oscillator wave functions (HOHF), for a set of doubly-magic nuclei including 16O, 40Ca, 56Ni, 78Ni, and 208Pb.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of 208Pb: G8 gives -1609.2 MeV, SCHF gives -1641.1 MeV (within 2% of SCHF)
- Ground-state energy of 78Ni: G8 gives -649.0 MeV, SCHF gives -660.7 MeV
- Ground-state energy of 16O: G8 gives -130.0 MeV, SCHF gives -131.5 MeV
- Neutron rms radius of 208Pb: G8 gives 5.47 fm, SCHF gives 5.49 fm (deviation 0.35% or less)
- Proton rms radius of 208Pb: G8 gives 5.38 fm, SCHF gives 5.38 fm
- Neutron rms radius of 78Ni: G8 gives 4.05 fm, SCHF gives 4.07 fm
- Proton rms radius of 78Ni: G8 gives 3.86 fm, SCHF gives 3.85 fm

## Context

This work extends the use of the analytically solvable Ginocchio potential (Ginocchio 1985) as a flexible alternative to the harmonic-oscillator potential for shell-model calculations, addressing the need for improved mean-field descriptions of exotic nuclei far from stability. The authors show that their variational Ginocchio-Hartree-Fock method reproduces self-consistent Hartree-Fock results much better than the harmonic-oscillator approach, particularly in the nuclear surface region, and can accommodate the increased surface diffuseness expected for neutron-rich nuclei as suggested by Dobaczewski et al. (1996) and Fukunishi et al. (1993). This enables the extension of shell-model methodology to weakly-bound exotic nuclei and opens the possibility of applying the approach with more realistic effective interactions, including finite-range forces where the self-consistent HF potential is difficult to cast in local form.
