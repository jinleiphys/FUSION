# Evolution of Inhomogeneous Condensates: Self-consistent Variational Approach

> We establish a self-consistent variational framework that allows us to study numerically the non-equilibrium evolution of non-perturbative inhomogeneous field configurations including quantum backreaction effects. After discussing the practical merits and disadvantages of different approaches we provide a closed set of local and renormalizable update equations that determine the dynamical evolution of inhomogeneous condensates and can be implemented numerically. These incorporate self-consistently the backreaction of quantum fluctuations and particle production. This program requires the solution of a self-consistent inhomogeneous problem to provide initial Cauchy data for the inhomogeneous condensates and Green's functions. We provide a simple solvable ansatz for such an initial value problem for the Sine-Gordon and phi^4 quantum field theories in one spatial dimension. We compare exact known results of the Sine Gordon model to this simple ansatz. We also study the linear sigma model in the large N limit in three spatial dimensions as a microscopic model for pion production in ultrarelativistic collisions. We provide a solvable self- consistent ansatz for the initial value problem with cylindrical symmetry. For this case we also obtain a closed set of local and renormalized update equations that can be numerically implemented. A novel phenomenon of spinodal instabilities and pion production arises as a result of a Klein paradox for large amplitude inhomogeneous condensate configurations.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a self-consistent variational framework for numerically studying the non-equilibrium evolution of non-perturbative inhomogeneous field configurations including quantum backreaction effects, but the numerical implementation is deferred to a forthcoming article, so the present work only provides the theoretical setup, initial value problem solutions, and renormalized update equations.

## Method

The authors implement Gaussian trial wave functionals in Dirac's variational principle to derive Time Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) equations, then reformulate these as local equal-time Green's function equations for the field expectation value φ_c, the connected two-point function G, and the mixed D and K kernels. They also develop a covariant mean-field approach using mode function expansions that satisfy a Schrodinger-like eigenvalue problem at t=0, and apply this to the Sine-Gordon and φ^4 theories in 1+1 dimensions and the linear sigma model in the large N limit in 3+1 dimensions with cylindrical symmetry.

## Key numbers

- For the Sine-Gordon model in 1+1 dimensions, the authors provide a solvable ansatz for the initial value problem using a Schrodinger operator whose spectrum is exactly known; no specific numerical values are given for this case.
- For the linear sigma model in the large N limit in 3+1 dimensions, the paper reports a novel phenomenon of spinodal instabilities and pion production arising from a Klein paradox for large amplitude inhomogeneous condensate configurations; no specific numerical values are given.
- The paper states that the update equations are "local and renormalizable" and can be "numerically implemented on current computers," but provides no explicit numerical results from simulations.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of non-equilibrium dynamics in spatially homogeneous systems (Boyanovsky et al., 1996; Cooper et al., 1996) to the inhomogeneous case, and generalizes earlier semiclassical treatments of disoriented chiral condensates (Rajagopal, 1995; Gavin, 1995; Asakawa, 1995) by incorporating quantum backreaction self-consistently. The framework enables future numerical studies of relaxation of large-amplitude inhomogeneous initial states, particle production, and the emergence of hydrodynamic descriptions from microscopic quantum field theory, with particular relevance to modeling relativistic heavy ion collisions.
