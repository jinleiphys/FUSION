# Hadron momentum spectra from analytical solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics

> We present analytical solution of relativistic hydrodynamics for a system having cylindrical symmetry with boost-invariant longitudinal expansion and Hubble-like transverse expansion. We also consider analytical solution for Hubble-like spherically expanding system. For these two cases, we calculate analytical expression for transverse momentum spectra of hadrons, at constant temperature freeze-out hypersurface using Cooper-Frye prescription. We compare our results for transverse momentum spectra with experimental results from Large Hadron Collider and CERN SPS where one expects cylindrical and spherical geometry of the fireball, respectively. In the case of low-energy collisions with spherical geometry, we calculate rapidity spectra and compare with the results from CERN SPS.

## Key claim
The paper presents fully analytical solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics for both spherically and cylindrically expanding fireballs, deriving closed-form expressions for hadron momentum spectra at a constant-temperature freeze-out hypersurface. The key qualifier is that these solutions assume scaling (Hubble-like) velocity profiles and a constant speed of sound, which limits their validity to regimes where both spatial coordinates and time are large compared to the initial system size.

## Method
The authors solved the ideal (non-dissipative) relativistic hydrodynamic equations for two symmetric geometries: spherical expansion with radial velocity \(v_r = r/t\), and cylindrical expansion with boost-invariant longitudinal flow \(v_z = z/t\) and Hubble-like transverse flow \(v_\rho = \rho/t\). They assumed a constant speed of sound equation of state \(p = c_s^2 \varepsilon\) and used the method of characteristics to obtain analytical temperature and entropy density profiles. The Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription was then applied on a constant-temperature hypersurface (which corresponds to constant proper time \(\tau_3\)) to derive analytical expressions for the transverse momentum spectra of hadrons, with the final integrals performed numerically.

## Key numbers
- Temperature evolution: \(T(\tau_3) = T_0 (\tau_{30}/\tau_3)^{3c_s^2}\), where \(\tau_3 = \sqrt{t^2 - r^2}\) (spherical) or \(\sqrt{t^2 - \rho^2 - z^2}\) (cylindrical), and \(c_s\) is the constant speed of sound.
- For spherical expansion, the average radial velocity at freeze-out is \(\langle v_r \rangle = \frac{1 + \nu^2 \log(\nu^2/(1+\nu^2))}{\sqrt{1+\nu^2} - \nu^2 \log[\nu/(\sqrt{1+\nu^2}-1)]}\), where \(\nu = \tau_{3f}/R\).
- For cylindrical expansion, the average transverse velocity at freeze-out is \(\langle v_T \rangle = \sqrt{1+\nu^2} - \nu^2 \log[\nu/(\sqrt{1+\nu^2}-1)]\).
- Both average velocities reach a maximum value of 1 when \(\nu = 0\) and decrease monotonically with increasing \(\nu\).

## Context
This work extends the family of analytical hydrodynamic solutions previously explored by Csorgo (2003) and Lin (2009), and builds on the scaling solution approach of Baym (1983) for nuclear collisions. The derived analytical spectra provide a direct, fully hydrodynamic alternative to phenomenological blast-wave models (such as those by Schnedermann (1993) and Retiere (2003)) that parameterize freeze-out variables ad hoc. The results enable systematic comparison with experimental data from the LHC (cylindrical geometry) and CERN SPS (spherical geometry), and can serve as benchmarks for testing numerical hydrodynamic simulation codes.
