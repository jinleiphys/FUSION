# Peripherality of breakup reactions

> The sensitivity of elastic breakup to the interior of the projectile wave function is analyzed. Breakup calculations of loosely bound nuclei (8B and 11Be) are performed with two different descriptions of the projectile. The descriptions differ strongly in the interior of the wave function, but exhibit identical asymptotic properties, namely the same asymptotic normalization coefficient, and phase shifts. Breakup calculations are performed at intermediate energies (40-70 MeV/nucleon) on lead and carbon targets as well as at low energy (26 MeV) on a nickel target. No dependence on the projectile description is observed. This result confirms that breakup reactions are peripheral in the sense that they probe only the external part of the wave function. These measurements are thus not directly sensitive to the total normalization of the wave function, i.e. spectroscopic factor.

## Key claim

Elastic breakup reactions of loosely bound nuclei are peripheral with respect to the internal coordinate of the projectile (the core-fragment distance), meaning they probe only the asymptotic tail of the projectile wave function and are insensitive to its interior; this holds for both Coulomb- and nuclear-dominated breakup at intermediate and low energies, except possibly for resonant final states.

## Method

Breakup calculations for the proton-halo nucleus 8B and the neutron-halo nucleus 11Be were performed using two descriptions of each projectile that differ strongly in the interior of the wave function but have identical asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) and phase shifts, obtained via supersymmetric transformations (Baye 1987). The calculations used the dynamical eikonal model for Coulomb breakup on lead at 44 MeV/nucleon and for nuclear-dominated breakup on carbon at 68 MeV/nucleon, and the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) technique for low-energy breakup on nickel at 26 MeV.

## Key numbers

* Breakup cross section of 8B on Pb at 44 MeV/nucleon: total breakup cross section is 0.874 b for the deep potential and 0.869 b for its supersymmetric partner; the difference between the two calculations is about 0.5%.
* Inelastic cross section to the spurious deeply bound state in 8B: 8 microbarns, five orders of magnitude below the total breakup cross section.
* For 11Be on C at 68 MeV/nucleon, the two descriptions differ by 8% in the peak at 1.3 MeV, which is due to a d5/2+ resonance.
* Breakup of 8B on Ni at 26 MeV: the two descriptions produce angular distributions that are indistinguishable.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of peripherality in breakup reactions beyond earlier studies (Typel and Baur 2004, 2005; Capel and Nunes 2006) by explicitly testing sensitivity to the interior of the projectile wave function using phase-equivalent potentials. It contradicts the common practice of extracting spectroscopic factors from breakup data (Nakamura 1994, Palit 2002, Pramanik 2003, Fukuda 2004) and instead supports the view that only the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) can be reliably extracted (Trache, Carstoiu, and Gagliardi 2001, 2004). The results enable a clearer separation of model-dependent uncertainties in breakup analyses, showing that spectroscopic factors extracted from such measurements contain an error due to the unconstrained interior of the projectile description.
