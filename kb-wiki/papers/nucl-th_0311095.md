# Electromagnetic interactions of three-body systems in the covariant spectator theory

> We derive a complete Feynman diagram expansion for the elastic form factor and the three-body photo and electrodisintegration of the three-body bound state using the covariant spectator theory. We show that the equations obtained are fully consistent with bound-state equations and the normalization condition previously derived for the covariant three-body bound state, and that the results conserve current.

## Key claim

The paper derives a complete, gauge-invariant Feynman diagram expansion for electromagnetic interactions of three-body bound states in the covariant spectator theory, showing that the resulting currents are fully consistent with previously derived bound-state equations and normalization conditions, but only when three-body forces of relativistic origin are neglected.

## Method

The authors use a diagrammatic method based on the covariant spectator formalism to construct the three-body current by coupling photons to all propagators and momentum-dependent couplings in the infinite series of Feynman diagrams that define the scattering equations. They first gauge the spectator propagator and two-body scattering amplitudes, then use the bound-state and scattering equations to eliminate off-shell spectator contributions, and finally derive Ward-Takahashi identities for the resulting "core" diagrams to prove current conservation.

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator formalism previously applied to NN scattering and the deuteron (Gross 1969, Buck and Gross 1979) to three-body electromagnetic interactions, providing the diagrammatic derivation that complements the algebraic approach of Kvinikhidze and Blankleider (1997) and agrees with results by Adam and Van Orden (2003). The derived currents are being applied to study the three-body electrodisintegration process ³He(e,e'pp)n recently measured at Jefferson Laboratory (Weinstein 2003), and the results enable consistent relativistic calculations of three-body form factors and breakup amplitudes that conserve current.
