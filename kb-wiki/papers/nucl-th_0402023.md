# Nuclear halo and its scaling laws

> We have proposed a procedure to extract the probability for valence particle being out of the binding potential from the measured nuclear asymptotic normalization coefficients. With this procedure, available data regarding the nuclear halo candidates are systematically analyzed and a number of halo nuclei are confirmed. Based on these results we have got a much relaxed condition for nuclear halo occurrence. Furthermore, we have presented the scaling laws for the dimensionless quantity $<r^{2}>/R^{2}$ of nuclear halo in terms of the analytical expressions of the expectation value for the operator $r^{2}$ in a finite square-well potential.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the condition for nuclear halo occurrence is much more relaxed than previously thought: halo can occur for \(B_p A^{2/3} < 10\) MeV, whereas the earlier condition from Jensen and Riisager (2000) required \(B_p A^{2/3} < 2\) MeV for s- and p-states. This relaxed condition is based on a new procedure to extract the probability of a valence particle being outside the binding potential from measured asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs).

## Method

The authors extract the probability \(P\) that a valence particle lies outside the binding potential radius \(R\) by fitting single-particle Woods-Saxon wave functions to experimentally measured nuclear asymptotic normalization coefficients \(C_{A_c N lj}^A\) and spectroscopic factors \(S_{lj}^{(sp)}\). For each choice of diffuseness parameter \(a_0\) (0.50-0.70 fm), the radius parameter \(r_0\) is varied to minimize \(\chi_p^2\) between the scaled wave function and the asymptotic Whittaker function form. The weighted average probability over minima for different \(a_0\) is then computed. Separately, the authors derive analytical scaling laws for the dimensionless quantity \(\langle r^2 \rangle / R^2\) using expectation values of \(r^2\) in a finite square-well potential for \(l=0\), \(l=1\), and \(l=2\) states.

## Key numbers

* The well-known s-wave halos in \(^{11}\text{Li}\), \(^{11}\text{Be}\) and the p-wave halo in \(^{11}\text{Be}\) have \(B_p A^{2/3}\) values of 1.48 MeV, 2.49 MeV, and 0.89 MeV, respectively, which are below or close to the earlier limit of 2 MeV.
* The new relaxed condition for halo occurrence is \(B_p A^{2/3} < 10\) MeV (Equation 10).
* For the \(2s\) excited state in \(^{13}\text{C}\), the nuclear ANC used is \(C_{A_c N lj}^A = 1.84 \pm 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-1/2}\) and the spectroscopic factor is \(S_{lj}^{(sp)} = 0.95\).
* The scaling laws yield the following conditions for halo: \(\langle r^2 \rangle / R^2 \geq 1.5\) for \(2s\) states and \(\langle r^2 \rangle / R^2 \geq 1.9\) for \(1p\) states.
* For \(l=2\) states, the probability \(P\) is less than 40% in the limit \(\chi \rightarrow 0\), making halo unlikely.

## Context

This work extends the earlier occurrence conditions for nuclear halos proposed by Jensen and Riisager (2000), which set a stricter binding-energy threshold. The authors systematically analyze halo candidates using experimental ANCs, confirming several halo nuclei and providing a much relaxed condition (\(B_p A^{2/3} < 10\) MeV). The scaling laws for \(\langle r^2 \rangle / R^2\) derived from a finite square-well potential improve upon earlier scaling laws from Fedorov et al. (1993) and Riisager et al. (2000), which were independent of the quantum number \(n\). The results enable more reliable identification of two-body halo nuclei and provide a framework for predicting halo structure in weakly bound systems.
