# Transverse electron scattering response function of 3He in the quasi-elastic peak region and beyond with Delta isobar degrees of freedom

> The 3He transverse electron scattering response function R_T(q,omega) is calculated in the quasi-elastic peak region and beyond for momentum transfers q = 500, 600 and 700 MeV/c. Distinct from our previous work for these kinematics where we included meson exchange currents and relativistic corrections we now additionally include Delta isobar currents (Delta-IC). The Delta-IC contribution increases the quasi-elastic peak height by about 5% and leads to an excellent agreement with experimental data in the whole peak region. In addition it is shown that effects due to the three-nucleon force largely cancel those due to the Delta-IC in the peak region. Finally, we have found that Delta-IC are important for three-body break-up reactions in the so-called dip region. This could explain why in a previous study of such a reaction, where Delta degrees of freedom were not included, no agreement between experimental and theoretical results could be obtained.

# Transverse electron scattering response function of 3He in the quasi-elastic peak region and beyond with Delta isobar degrees of freedom

## Key claim
The inclusion of Delta isobar currents (Delta-IC) increases the quasi-elastic peak height of the transverse response function R_T(q,omega) for 3He by about 5% at momentum transfers q = 500, 600, and 700 MeV/c, producing excellent agreement with experimental data. However, effects from the three-nucleon force (3NF) largely cancel those from the Delta-IC in the peak region, meaning the net Delta contribution is only visible when the 3NF is also included.

## Method
The authors calculated the transverse electron scattering response function R_T(q,omega) for 3He using the Lorentz integral transform method, which rigorously includes final state interactions. The nuclear current operator included the non-relativistic one-body nucleon current with first-order relativistic corrections, meson exchange currents (MEC), and newly added Delta isobar currents (Delta-IC) computed in impulse approximation. Calculations were performed in the active nucleon Breit (ANB) frame and then transformed to the laboratory frame, using the Argonne V18 NN potential and the Urbana IX three-nucleon force.

## Key numbers
- The Delta-IC contribution increases the quasi-elastic peak height by about 5% for momentum transfers q = 500, 600, and 700 MeV/c.
- At q_ANB = 500 and 600 MeV/c, the 3NF reduces the quasi-elastic peak height by about 5%, nearly perfectly canceling the Delta-IC increase in the peak region.
- At q_ANB = 700 MeV/c, the Delta-IC contribution is slightly larger than the 3NF effect.
- For the T_f = 3/2 isospin channel (three-body break-up only), the total Delta effect increases R_T at omega_int = 250 MeV by 82% at q_ANB = 500 MeV/c, 45% at q_ANB = 600 MeV/c, and 21% at q_ANB = 700 MeV/c.
- For the T_f = 1/2 channel, the increases at the same omega_int are 33%, 26%, and 13% at q_ANB = 500, 600, and 700 MeV/c, respectively.

## Context
This work extends previous calculations by the same group (Efros et al., 2010; Efros et al., 2011) that included MEC and relativistic corrections but not Delta-IC, and which slightly underestimated the experimental quasi-elastic peak height. It contrasts with the coupled-channel calculation by Deltuva (2004), which found almost no Delta effects at q = 500 MeV/c in the quasi-elastic peak region; the present work reconciles this by showing that 3NF and Delta-IC effects largely cancel. The finding that Delta-IC are important for three-body break-up reactions in the dip region suggests that including Delta degrees of freedom could resolve discrepancies between theory and experiment for the 3He(e,e'pn) reaction studied by Middleton et al. (2009), where neither 3NF nor Delta-IC were included.
