# Electromagnetic interactions for the two-body spectator equations

> This paper presents a new non-associative algebra which is used to (i) show how the spectator (or Gross) two-body equations and electromagnetic currents can be formally derived from the Bethe-Salpeter equation and currents if both are treated to all orders, (ii) obtain explicit expressions for the Gross two-body electromagnetic currents valid to any order, and (iii) prove that the currents so derived are exactly gauge invariant when truncated consistently to any finite order. In addition to presenting these new results, this work complements and extends previous treatments based largely on the analysis of sums of Feynman diagrams.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new non-associative operator algebra that formally derives the spectator (Gross) two-body equations and electromagnetic currents from the Bethe-Salpeter equation and currents, proving that the resulting currents are exactly gauge invariant when truncated consistently to any finite order, provided the strong kernel and electromagnetic current operator are both truncated to the same finite order.

## Method

The authors develop a non-associative operator algebra to handle products of singular operators, specifically the on-shell projector operators that arise when separating the Bethe-Salpeter propagator into on-shell and off-shell contributions. They use this algebra to derive explicit expressions for the Gross two-body electromagnetic currents valid to all orders in the perturbation expansion of the kernel, and demonstrate gauge invariance through Ward-Takahashi identities for both one-body and two-body currents.

## Context

This work extends previous treatments of electromagnetic interactions in the spectator formalism (Gross, 1969; Gross, 1987) that were based largely on analysis of sums of Feynman diagrams, providing a formal algebraic derivation complementary to those diagrammatic approaches. The paper complements and extends recent work on normalization conditions for the three-body vertex function (Adam, Van Orden, and Gross, 1997) and lays the foundation for extension of three-body Gross equations developed by Stadler and Gross (1997). The formalism is designed to enable derivation of the electromagnetic current operator for the three-body Gross equations in a forthcoming paper, and the results agree with independent discussions by Kvinikhidze and Blankleider (1997).
