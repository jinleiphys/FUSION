# Reexamination of microscopic optical potentials based on multiple scattering theory

> Microscopic optical potentials have been successful in describing nucleon-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus scattering. Some essential ingredients of the framework, however, have not been examined in detail. Applicability of the microscopic folding model is systematically investigated. Effect of an antisymmetrization factor (ASF) appearing in multiple scattering theory, theoretical uncertainty regarding the local density approximation (LDA), and the validity of a prescription for nonlocality, the Brieva-Rook (BR) localization, of the microscopic potential, are quantitatively estimated for nucleon-nucleus scattering; investigation on the ASF is carried out for also deuteron-nucleus scattering. A single folding model with the Melbourne g-matrix interaction and the SLy4 Skyrme-type Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubiv (SLy4-HFB) density is employed for evaluating a nucleon-nucleus microscopic optical potential. Deuteron-nucleus scattering is described by the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method incorporating the microscopic proton-nucleus and neutron-nucleus potentials. The ASF is found to affect proton total reaction cross sections for a 12C target below 200 MeV by about 10%. Effect of the ASF on total reaction cross sections is negligibly small if a target nucleus is heavy or scattering energy is above 200 MeV; elastic cross sections are hardly affected by the ASF for all the reaction systems considered. Below 65 MeV, still the BR localization works quite well. However, at energies below about 50 MeV, the LDA becomes less accurate for evaluating elastic cross sections at backward angles. This is the case also for the total reaction cross sections of p-12C below about 200 MeV. The microscopic model is applicable to nucleon-nucleus scattering above 25 MeV for target nuclei in a wide range of mass numbers. Deviation of calculated results from experimental data is less than about 10%.

## Key claim

The microscopic folding model using the Melbourne g-matrix interaction and SLy4-HFB density, with the local density approximation (LDA) and Brieva-Rook (BR) localization, is applicable to nucleon-nucleus scattering above 25 MeV for a wide range of target masses, with deviations from experimental data less than about 10%; however, the LDA becomes less accurate below about 50 MeV for elastic cross sections at backward angles and for total reaction cross sections of p-12C below about 200 MeV.

## Method

A single folding model with the Melbourne g-matrix interaction and SLy4 Skyrme-type Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (SLy4-HFB) density was used to evaluate a nucleon-nucleus microscopic optical potential. The effect of an antisymmetrization factor (ASF) from multiple scattering theory, the theoretical uncertainty of the local density approximation (LDA), and the validity of the Brieva-Rook (BR) localization prescription for nonlocality were quantitatively estimated for nucleon-nucleus scattering. Deuteron-nucleus scattering was described by the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) incorporating the microscopic proton-nucleus and neutron-nucleus potentials.

## Key numbers

- The ASF affects proton total reaction cross sections for a 12C target below 200 MeV by about 10%.
- The microscopic model is applicable to nucleon-nucleus scattering above 25 MeV for target nuclei in a wide range of mass numbers.
- Deviation of calculated results from experimental data is less than about 10%.
- For p-12C at 30 MeV, the total reaction cross section with the nonlocal potential is 441.0 mb, and with the localized potential is 452.2 mb.
- For p-12C at 65 MeV, the total reaction cross section with the nonlocal potential is 313.5 mb, and with the localized potential is 318.6 mb.
- For p-12C at 100 MeV, the total reaction cross section with the nonlocal potential is 241.9 mb, and with the localized potential is 244.6 mb.
- For p-208Pb at 30 MeV, the total reaction cross section with the nonlocal potential is 1697.9 mb, and with the localized potential is 1758.2 mb.
- For p-208Pb at 65 MeV, the total reaction cross section with the nonlocal potential is 1925.3 mb, and with the localized potential is 1945.2 mb.
- For p-208Pb at 100 MeV, the total reaction cross section with the nonlocal potential is 1758.2 mb, and with the localized potential is 1766.7 mb.

## Context

This work systematically extends the microscopic folding model approach of the Melbourne group (Amos et al., 2000) by quantitatively examining the effects of the antisymmetrization factor, the local density approximation, and the Brieva-Rook localization, which had not been investigated in detail, especially at energies below 65 MeV and above 200 MeV. The findings enable more reliable application of the microscopic framework to scattering of unstable nuclei, where phenomenological densities are unavailable, and highlight the need for a new approach beyond the g-matrix folding model for scattering at energies below about 25 MeV.
