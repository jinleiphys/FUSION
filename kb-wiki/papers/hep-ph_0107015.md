# Ioffe Time in Double Logarithmic Approximation

> We analyze the light cone (Ioffe) time structure of the gluon distribution function in the double logarithmic approximation. We show that due to QCD evolution Ioffe equation is modified. The characteristic light cone time of the gluons does not increase as fast with increasing energy (decreasing Bjorken x) as predicted by the parton distributions exhibiting Bjorken scaling due to the increase of the transverse momenta of the gluons in the DGLAP ladder.

## Key claim

The characteristic light-cone (Ioffe) time of gluons in the double logarithmic approximation (DLA) of DGLAP evolution does not grow as fast with decreasing Bjorken \(x\) as the naive Ioffe equation \(l_c \propto 1/(m_N x)\) would predict, because the evolution simultaneously increases the gluons' transverse momenta, shortening their coherence length.

## Method

The authors compute the Ioffe-time representation \(f(u,\nu)\) of the gluon distribution function by performing an inverse Fourier transform of the DLA DGLAP-evolved gluon structure function, first with a fixed strong coupling constant and then with a running coupling. They extract the characteristic Ioffe time \(u^*\) by finding the maximum of the exponent in the saddle-point approximation of \(f(u,\nu)\). They then translate this into the coherence length \(l_c\) in the target rest frame and compare it to the nuclear diameter to estimate the onset of non-linear effects.

## Key numbers

* For the fixed-coupling DLA case, the characteristic Ioffe time is \(u^*_{fc} = (1/z_0) \sqrt{\nu/\nu_0}\), leading to a coherence length that grows as \(\sqrt{s}\) rather than linearly with \(s\).
* For the running-coupling DLA case, the characteristic Ioffe time is approximately \(u^* \approx (1/z_0) \left( 2 \nu m_N z_0 / \Lambda^2 \right)^{1 - 1/\ln(\ln(2\nu m_N z_0/\Lambda^2)/\ln(2\nu_0 m_N z_0/\Lambda^2))}\), with \(\Lambda = 0.3\ \text{GeV}\) and \(z_0 = 0.1\).
* In the running-coupling case, the coherence length is roughly half of the maximum \(1/(2 m_N z)\) throughout the energy range considered, and the energy required for the coherence length to reach a nuclear diameter of \(14\ \text{fm}\) is about \(30\%\) higher than the naive Ioffe estimate.
* For the fixed-coupling case, the nuclear diameter is reached at about \(s \approx 30000\ \text{GeV}^2\).

## Context

This work extends the classic analysis of Ioffe (1969) and Gribov, Ioffe, and Pomeranchuk (1965), who derived the coherence length \(l_c \propto 1/(m_N x)\) under the assumption of exact Bjorken scaling. It also builds on later studies by Frankfurt and Strikman (1988) and others that used the Ioffe representation with scaling parton distributions. The authors show that QCD scaling violations, specifically DGLAP evolution in the double logarithmic approximation, significantly reduce the coherence length compared to the scaling prediction, thereby slowing the onset of nuclear shadowing and parton saturation in nuclei as previously discussed by Gribov (1970), Gribov, Levin, and Ryskin (1983), and Mueller (1990). The results are limited to the small-\(x\), large-\(Q^2\) region where the DLA is valid, and the authors call for further studies with more realistic parton densities.
