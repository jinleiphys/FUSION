# Neutrino-Nucleus Reactions

> Neutrino-nucleus reactions as applied to astrophysics are reviewed.

## Key claim

This review paper surveys theoretical treatments of neutrino-nucleus reactions relevant to astrophysical neutrino detection, but the authors note that for the most thoroughly studied case (the A=12 triad), the model-independent elementary-particle treatment is only valid for neutrino energies up to approximately 100 MeV, and that even for the best-studied neutrino-deuteron reactions, discrepancies of up to 20% existed between different calculations until very recently.

## Method

The authors organize their review by calculational method: they describe "ab initio" calculations using realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials for light nuclei (A ≤ 4), the elementary-particle treatment (EPT) which parametrizes nuclear form factors from experimental data, the empirical effective operator method, the use of (p,n) reactions as calibrators of Gamow-Teller matrix elements, and the Fermi gas model for high-energy neutrino-nucleus reactions. For each approach, they critically examine the approximations made and compare results from different groups.

## Key numbers

- The Homestake chlorine detector observed 2.1 ± 0.9 SNU, compared to the standard solar model prediction of 7.9 ± 2.6 SNU (1 SNU = 10⁻³⁷ events per target atom per second)
- The Kamiokande experiment observed a neutrino flux 0.46 ± 0.05(stat) ± 0.06(syst) times the standard solar model prediction
- The GALLEX collaboration reported 83 ± 19(1σ) ± 8(syst) SNU, and the SAGE collaboration reported 58⁺¹⁷₋₂₄ ± 14(syst) SNU, both below the standard solar model prediction of 132⁺²⁰₋₁₇ SNU for a ⁷¹Ga target
- The average cross section for νₑ + d → e⁻ + p + p from μ⁺ decay is 5.4 × 10⁻⁴¹ cm², consistent with the observed value (5.2 ± 1.8) × 10⁻⁴¹ cm²
- For the A=12 triad, the magnetic form factor F_M = (1.516 ± 0.016) × 10⁻³ MeV⁻¹ from the γ-decay width Γ_γ = 38.5 ± 0.8 MeV, and the axial form factor F_A = 0.711 ± 0.024 from β-decay ft-values
- The pp reaction astrophysical S-factor at zero energy is S(E=0) = 4.00 × 10⁻²⁵ MeV b, with derivative dS(E)/dE|_(E=0) = 4.07(1 ± 0.051) × 10⁻²⁵ MeV b
- The hep reaction S-factor at zero energy is S(E=0) = 1.3 × 10⁻²³ MeV b, smaller by a factor of approximately 6 than earlier estimates

## Context

This review extends the work of Bahcall (1989) and Fukugita (1987) on astrophysical neutrinos, and critically compares calculations for neutrino-deuteron reactions by Ying, Haxton and Henley (1992), Tatara, Kohyama and Kubodera (1992), and Doi and Kubodera (1992), showing that the best available estimates agree within 2% after correcting earlier discrepancies. The paper enables reliable cross section predictions for the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory (SNO), which aims to separately detect charged-current and neutral-current reactions on deuterium to test the Mikheyev-Smirnov-Wolfenstein (MSW) effect as a solution to the solar neutrino problem.
