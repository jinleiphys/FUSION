# Structure effects of the two protons - two neutrons correlations

> The proton-neutron 2 particles - 2 holes correlations in superfluid nuclei, described by an effective, Josephson-type interaction ~[(P+N-)+(P-N+)], are studied within a boson approximation. The strength of this interaction is estimated using the symmetry energy for an even-even system of nucleons lying on the same degenerate level. The RPA vacuum is obtained explicitly, and the electroexcitation to isovector monopole states of oscillatory pair exchange between the proton and neutron superfluids is briefly discussed.

# Structure effects of the two protons - two neutrons correlations

## Key claim
The paper proposes that a proton-neutron 2 particles-2 holes mean-field, arising from a four-body isospin quadrupole interaction, may become energetically favored in superfluid nuclei and contribute to the symmetry energy, but this effect is demonstrated only within a boson approximation for a single degenerate level and requires an additional interaction constant G₄ to be determined from experiment.

## Method
The author linearizes a four-particle Hamiltonian in the proton-neutron 2p-2h channel using an isospin quadrupole interaction separable in that channel. The strength of the 2p-2h mean-field is estimated by comparing the resulting ground-state energy increase with the symmetry energy from the Weizsäcker mass formula, first for a single j=3/2 level and then in the limit of large degeneracy using a boson representation of the pairing operators. The RPA vacuum for the low-energy monopole states is constructed explicitly, and the electroexcitation cross-section for these states is derived.

## Key numbers
- The symmetry energy coefficient from the Weizsäcker formula is given as k_w = 28.1/A MeV.
- In the large-degeneracy boson approximation, the interaction constant is estimated as G₄ ~ 112/A² MeV; for ³⁶Ar this gives G₄ ~ 0.08 MeV.
- The low-energy monopole oscillation frequency is given as ω_v = 8Δ√(k_w δ₀)/G, where Δ is the pairing gap.
- The isovector breathing oscillations from charge-exchange reactions are cited at an excitation energy of 170/A^(1/3) MeV, providing a contrast to the predicted lower-energy monopole.
- The condition for a stable p-n 2p-2h mean-field at N=Z is G₄ > G/8, where G is the pairing interaction constant.

## Context
This work extends earlier treatments of alpha-particle type correlations in nuclei following the BCS theory, particularly the quadrupling Hamiltonian approach of previous authors and the author's own prior work on a single j=3/2 level (Grigorescu, 1990). The paper generalizes the boson approximation to larger degeneracy and discusses electroexcitation of isovector monopole states as a possible experimental signature, contrasting these with the higher-energy isovector breathing modes observed in charge-exchange reactions (cited as work from 2007). The results enable future experimental searches for low-energy monopole resonances in superfluid nuclei using electron scattering.
