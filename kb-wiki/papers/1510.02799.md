# Structure of the neutral pion and its electromagnetic transition form factor

> The $γ^\ast γ\to π^0$ transition form factor, $G(Q^2)$, is computed on the entire domain of spacelike momenta using a continuum approach to the two valence-body bound-state problem in relativistic quantum field theory: the result agrees with data obtained by the CELLO, CLEO and Belle Collaborations. The analysis unifies this prediction with that of the pion's valence-quark parton distribution amplitude (PDA) and elastic electromagnetic form factor, and demonstrates, too, that a fully self-consistent treatment can readily connect a pion PDA that is a broad, concave function at the hadronic scale with the perturbative QCD prediction for the transition form factor in the hard photon limit. The normalisation of that limit is set by the scale of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, which is a crucial feature of the Standard Model. Understanding of the latter will thus remain incomplete until definitive transition form factor data is available on $Q^2>10\,$GeV$^2$.

# Structure of the neutral pion and its electromagnetic transition form factor

## Key claim
The $\gamma^\ast \gamma \to \pi^0$ transition form factor, $G(Q^2)$, computed using a continuum Dyson-Schwinger equation approach, agrees with data from CELLO, CLEO, and Belle collaborations, and is expected to approach the perturbative QCD asymptotic limit either from below or only marginally exceed it, with logarithmic approach to the ultraviolet limit in either case. The analysis unifies this prediction with the pion's valence-quark parton distribution amplitude and elastic electromagnetic form factor, but definitive data on $Q^2 > 10$ GeV$^2$ is still needed to fully understand dynamical chiral symmetry breaking.

## Method
The $\gamma^\ast \gamma \to \pi^0$ transition form factor was computed using the leading-order (rainbow-ladder) truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations, expressed via perturbation theory integral representations for the dressed quark propagator and pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, with an Ansatz for the dressed photon-quark vertex that satisfies the Ward-Green-Takahashi identity. The pion Bethe-Salpeter wave function was evolved with the resolution scale using ERBL evolution equations, interpolating between a broad, concave parton distribution amplitude at the hadronic scale and the conformal-limit form at high momentum transfer.

## Key numbers
- The chiral-limit Abelian anomaly fixes $2 f_\pi^0 G(Q^2=0) = 1$, where $f_\pi^0 \approx 0.09$ GeV is the chiral-limit value of the charged pion's leptonic decay constant.
- The perturbative QCD asymptotic prediction is $Q^2 G(Q^2) \approx 4\pi^2 f_\pi$ for $Q^2 > Q_0^2$, with $\Lambda_{\rm QCD} \approx 0.3$ GeV.
- The Euclidean constituent-quark mass used in the vertex Ansatz is $M_E = 0.46$ GeV, and the momentum redistribution parameter in the vertex is ${\mathpzc s}_0 = 1.9$.
- The pion PDA at $\zeta_B \approx 2$ GeV takes the form $\varphi_\pi(x;\zeta_B) \approx (8/\pi) \sqrt{x(1-x)}$, while the conformal-limit PDA is $\varphi^{\rm cl}(x) = 6x(1-x)$.
- The VMD Ansatz yields an asymptotic limit of $m_\rho^2/[2f_\pi]$, which is 90% of the perturbative QCD result $4\pi^2 f_\pi$.
- The scale for progression to perturbative QCD in the charged-pion form factor is $Q^2 \approx 8$ GeV$^2$.

## Context
This work extends the continuum Dyson-Schwinger equation approach previously applied to the charged-pion elastic form factor by Chang et al. (2013) and the pion light-front wave function by Chang et al. (2013), now unifying the neutral pion transition form factor with the same framework. The results contradict the BaBar collaboration data (Aubert, 2009) which showed values above the perturbative QCD limit on $Q^2 \gtrsim 10$ GeV$^2$, and instead support the Belle collaboration data (Uehara, 2012) which agree with the asymptotic prediction. The analysis enables future comparisons with high-$Q^2$ experimental data to resolve the controversy surrounding the BaBar measurements and to test the role of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in setting the normalization of the hard-photon limit.
