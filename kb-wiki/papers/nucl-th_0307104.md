# Chaos and rotational damping in particle-rotor model

> The onset of chaos and the mechanism of rotational damping are studied in an exactly soluble particle-rotor model. It is shown that the degree of chaoticity as inferred from the statistical measures is closely related to the onset of rotational damping obtained using the model Hamiltonian.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates a clear correspondence between the degree of chaoticity (inferred from spectral statistics) and the onset of rotational damping in an exactly soluble particle-rotor model with conserved angular momentum, but the results are limited to a model space of a deformed single-j shell and cannot be directly compared with experimental data.

## Method
The authors constructed a particle-rotor model (PRM) with an axially-symmetric core and valence particles in a deformed single-j shell of i13/2, interacting through a two-body delta-interaction. They solved the Hamiltonian exactly for six valence particles with standard parameters (G=0.45 MeV, θ=24 ħ² MeV⁻¹) at two deformation values (κ=2.5 and 6.0 MeV, corresponding to normal and superdeformed shapes). Spectral rigidity statistics (Δ₃) were computed for different angular momenta and excitation-energy bins, and B(E2) transition probabilities were calculated to evaluate rotational damping through the branching parameter n.

## Key numbers
- Deformation parameter κ=2.5 MeV corresponds to β≈0.28 for the i13/2 shell; κ=6.0 MeV corresponds to superdeformation.
- Two-body interaction strength G=0.45 MeV; core moment of inertia θ=24 ħ² MeV⁻¹.
- For normal deformation (κ=2.5 MeV) at low spin I=10 and 12, Δ₃ values follow GOE statistics at all excitation energies, indicating fully chaotic motion.
- For normal deformation at high spin I=60, results with all eigenvalues deviate from GOE, indicating less chaotic motion.
- For superdeformation (κ=6.0 MeV) at low spin I=10 and 12, motion is less chaotic than normal deformation at low excitation energy, but tends toward chaotic at higher excitation.
- Branching parameter n for transition Ii=12→If=10 with κ=2.5 MeV and no core quadrupole moment exceeds 2 even at very low excitation energy; with Q0=2.5 barn, n remains below 2 for almost all first set of states.
- For transition Ii=62→If=60 with κ=6.0 MeV, averaged n for eigen-states 41-50 was 6.83 (Q0=0) and 1.50 (Q0=5.0 barn); with enhanced interaction (factor of 2), n increased to 8.20 and 1.65 respectively.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of rotational damping using cranked mean-field models by Abe (1990), Matsuo et al. (1993), and Yoshida and Matsuo (1997), which did not conserve angular momentum. The authors use an exactly soluble particle-rotor model with conserved angular momentum, avoiding mean-field approximations that could introduce different errors in calculations of rotational damping and statistical distributions. The results enable future studies of observable consequences of quantum chaos in rotating nuclei, particularly the connection between chaotic motion and electromagnetic transition fragmentation.
