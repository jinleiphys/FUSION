# Medium-induced broadening and softening of a parton shower

> The modifications of the angular and transverse momentum distributions of quarks and gluons inside a parton shower due to the presence of a medium are studied within an analytical description that reduces to the modified leading logarithmic approximation (MLLA) of QCD in the absence of medium.

## Key claim

The medium-induced modifications of parton showers are modeled by enhancing the singular $1/z$ parts of the leading-order parton splitting functions by a constant factor $1+f_{\rm med}$, which mimics increased soft gluon radiation while allowing controlled analytical calculations; the results show angular broadening, transverse momentum broadening, and softening of the $k_\perp$ spectrum, but the author notes the modeling is "admittedly crude" and does not include momentum transfer from the medium to the jet.

## Method

The author modifies the DGLAP parton splitting functions $P_{gq}(z)$ and $P_{gg}(z)$ by multiplying the $1/z$ terms by a constant factor $1+f_{\rm med}$, leaving the other splitting functions unchanged. Using the MLLA formalism with angular ordering, the author computes the angular distribution of hadron multiplicities inside a jet via Eq. (4) and the double differential single-particle distribution $d^2N/(dx\,d\ln k_\perp)$ via Eq. (8), then integrates over $\ell=\ln(1/x)$ to obtain the transverse momentum distribution. The calculations are performed for gluon and quark jets at energies typical for RHIC (40 GeV) and LHC (100 GeV), with the infrared cutoff $Q_0=\Lambda_{\rm QCD}\simeq250$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- For RHIC energy ($Y_{\Theta_0}=5.1$), the medium enhancement factor $f_{\rm med}=0.8$ is adopted, which allows reproduction of a nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}\approx 0.2$.
- For LHC energy ($Y_{\Theta_0}=6$), the medium enhancement factor $f_{\rm med}=1$ is chosen.
- The LPHD constant is taken as ${\cal K}^h=0.2$.
- The ratio of multiplicities above a $k_\perp$ cutoff in medium-distorted versus vacuum jets is larger than 1 up to $k_\perp^{\rm cut}\simeq 10-15$ GeV/$c$, and is systematically larger for quark than for gluon jets.

## Context

This work extends the analytical approach of Borghini (2005) for medium-modified parton showers, which modeled the medium as an enhancement of soft gluon radiation, and provides analytical reference calculations against which Monte-Carlo simulations such as Q-Pythia (Armesto, 2008) and Jewel (Zapp, 2008) can be tested. The results confirm the angular broadening seen in Q-Pythia simulations and the softening of the $k_\perp$ spectrum also observed there, while noting that the "usual" transverse momentum broadening from momentum transfer between the jet and medium (Baier, 1996; Salgado, 2003) is not included in this model. The formalism enables future studies of how both effects combine, and provides predictions for the multiplicity above a $k_\perp$ cutoff that could be tested experimentally at RHIC and LHC.
