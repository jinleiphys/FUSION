# Heavy-quark symmetries in the light of nonperturbative QCD approaches

> We critically review the validity of heavy-quark spin and flavor symmetries in heavy-light decay constants, form factors and effective couplings obtained within a nonperturbative framework, the ingredients of which are all motivated by Dyson-Schwinger equations studies of QCD. Along the way, we make new predictions for two effective nonphysical couplings: g_{DsDK} = 24.1+2.5/-1.6 and g_{BsBK} = 33.3+4.0/-3.7.

# Heavy-quark symmetries in the light of nonperturbative QCD approaches

## Key claim
Heavy-quark spin and flavor symmetries, which emerge only in the leading term of a 1/m_Q expansion of the QCD Lagrangian, are broken by nonperturbative corrections of order Λ_QCD/m_Q and α_s that are significant for charm quarks; the heavy-quark effective theory (HQET) is a poor guide to charm physics because the charm quark lies in an uncomfortable mass region neither really heavy nor light, whereas for bottom quarks HQET is a more sensible tool but still requires proper treatment of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in the light quark.

## Method
The authors use a nonperturbative framework based on Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) for dressed quark propagators and Bethe-Salpeter equations (BSEs) for meson bound-state amplitudes, employing rainbow-ladder truncation with momentum-dependent quark mass functions. They compute heavy-light meson decay constants, transition form factors, and strong couplings by evaluating loop integrals with dressed propagators and Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, and compare these full results to the predictions obtained in the heavy-quark limit using the Isgur-Wise function formalism.

- Strong coupling g_B*Bπ = 30.0 (+3.2/-1.4) using simple heavy constituent propagator, giving ĝ = 0.37 (+0.04/-0.02)

## Context
This work extends DSE-based studies of heavy mesons by Ivanov (1997-2007), El-Bennich (2009-2011), and Maris (2005) to critically test heavy-quark symmetry predictions. The authors compare their results with lattice QCD simulations by Becirevic (2009-2012), Albertus (2010), and Dimopoulos (2011), as well as with experimental data from the Particle Data Group (Amsler 2008). The paper demonstrates that corrections to the heavy-quark symmetry limit are less than 30% in b→c transitions but can be as large as a factor of 2 in c→d transitions, and that the rainbow-ladder truncation fails to reproduce experimental decay constants for charm mesons, motivating the need for improved truncation schemes beyond this approximation.
