# Are there monopoles in the quark-gluon plasma?

> Monopole-like objects have been identified in multiple lattice studies, and there is now a significant amount of literature on their importance in phenomenology. Some analytic indications of their role, however, are still missing. The 't Hooft-Polyakov monopoles, originally derived in the Georgi-Glashow model, are an important dynamical ingredient in theories with extended supersymmetry ${\cal N} = 2,\,4$, and help explain the issues related with electric-magnetic duality. There is no such solution in QCD-like theories without scalar fields. However, all of these theories have instantons and their finite-$T$ constituents known as instanton-dyons (or instanton-monopoles). The latter leads to semiclassical partition functions, which for ${\cal N} = 2,\,4$ theories were shown to be identical ("Poisson dual") to the partition function for monopoles. We show how, in a pure gauge theory, the semiclassical instanton-based partition function can also be Poisson-transformed into a partition function, interpreted as the one of moving and rotating monopoles.

## Key claim

The authors show that the semiclassical partition function for instanton-dyons in pure gauge theory (without scalar fields) can be Poisson-transformed into a partition function that is interpretable as describing moving and rotating monopoles, providing an indirect analytic argument for the existence of monopole-like objects in the quark-gluon plasma even though no explicit monopole solution exists in QCD-like theories.

## Method

The authors start from the finite-temperature instanton-dyon (caloron) solutions in SU(2) pure gauge theory, which split into time-independent M dyons and time-twisted L_n dyons with winding number n around the Matsubara circle. They write the semiclassical partition function as a sum over all winding numbers n of Boltzmann factors with action proportional to |2πn − ω|, where ω is the electric holonomy parameter. They then apply the Poisson summation formula to transform this sum into a dual form, which they interpret as a sum over monopole states with integer electric charge q, with an action that depends logarithmically on q.

## Key numbers

* The semiclassical action parameter S₀ = 8π²/g₀² is taken to be approximately 10, based on instanton-dyon ensemble simulations that used S₀ ranging from 5 to 13.
* The monopole mass in theories with extended supersymmetry is M = (4π/g²)φ, where φ is the scalar vacuum expectation value.
* The instanton-dyon action for a configuration with winding n is S_n = (4π/g²)|2πn/β − v|, where v is the VEV of A₄ at large distances.
* The Poisson-transformed monopole action for small electric charge q ≪ 4π/g₀² is approximately S(q) ≈ 2 log(4π/g₀²) + q²(g₀²/4π)².

## Context

This work extends the Poisson duality relation previously established for theories with extended supersymmetry N = 2, 4 by Dorey and collaborators (2000) and by Poppitz and Unsal (2011, 2012), where the partition function for 't Hooft-Polyakov monopoles was shown to be identical to the instanton-dyon partition function. The paper provides an analytic foundation for the phenomenological "magnetic scenario" of the quark-gluon plasma developed by Liao and Shuryak (2006-2008), which assumes non-condensed monopoles as quasiparticles that affect transport properties like shear viscosity and jet quenching. The result enables further study of how multiple rotational states of monopoles might affect their Bose-Einstein condensation at the deconfinement transition temperature T_c and their contributions to kinetic properties of the QGP.
