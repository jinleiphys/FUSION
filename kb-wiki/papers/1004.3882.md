# Constraints on the inner edge of neutron star crusts from relativistic nuclear energy density functionals

> The transition density $n_t$ and pressure $P_t$ at the inner edge between the liquid core and the solid crust of a neutron star are analyzed using the thermodynamical method and the framework of relativistic nuclear energy density functionals. Starting from a functional that has been carefully adjusted to experimental binding energies of finite nuclei, and varying the density dependence of the corresponding symmetry energy within the limits determined by isovector properties of finite nuclei, we estimate the constraints on the core-crust transition density and pressure of neutron stars: $0.086 \ {\rm fm}^{-3} \leq n_t < 0.090 \ {\rm fm}^{-3}$ and $0.3\ {\rm MeV \ fm}^{-3} < P_t \leq 0.76 \ {\rm MeV \ fm}^{-3}$.

## Key claim

The transition density and pressure at the inner edge of a neutron star crust are constrained to narrow intervals ($0.086 \ {\rm fm}^{-3} \leq n_t < 0.090 \ {\rm fm}^{-3}$ and $0.3\ {\rm MeV \ fm}^{-3} < P_t \leq 0.76 \ {\rm MeV \ fm}^{-3}$) when the density dependence of the symmetry energy is varied within limits determined by isovector properties of finite nuclei, rather than by an unrestricted variation of the slope parameter and curvature parameter of the symmetry energy.

## Method

The authors used the thermodynamical method, which locates the transition density by finding where the uniform neutron-proton-electron matter becomes unstable against separation into two coexisting phases, equivalent to the condition $C_I(n_t)=0$ in their formalism. They employed the relativistic nuclear energy density functional DD-PC1, which was previously adjusted to experimental binding energies of 64 axially deformed nuclei, and varied the density dependence of the symmetry energy by modifying either the symmetry energy at subsaturation density $\langle S_2 \rangle$ (at $n=0.12$ fm$^{-3}$) or the symmetry energy at saturation density $a_4$, while keeping the isoscalar properties fixed.

## Key numbers

- Transition density range: $0.086 \ {\rm fm}^{-3} \leq n_t < 0.090 \ {\rm fm}^{-3}$
- Transition pressure range: $0.3\ {\rm MeV \ fm}^{-3} < P_t \leq 0.76 \ {\rm MeV \ fm}^{-3}$
- Saturation density of the DD-PC1 functional: $n_0 = 0.152$ fm$^{-3}$
- Symmetry energy at saturation for DD-PC1: $a_4 = 33$ MeV
- Nuclear matter compression modulus: $K_{nm} = 230$ MeV
- Volume energy coefficient range constrained by binding energies: $-16.04$ MeV $\leq a_v \leq -16.08$ MeV
- Range of $\langle S_2 \rangle$ (symmetry energy at $n=0.12$ fm$^{-3}$) constrained by finite nuclei: $27.6$ MeV $\le \langle S_2 \rangle \le 28.6$ MeV
- An increase of $3.5\%$ in $\langle S_2 \rangle$ leads to an increase of $1.5\%$ in $n_t$ and a decrease of $16-20\%$ in $P_t$

## Context

This work extends the analysis of core-crust transition properties using relativistic mean-field models, following the approach of Horowitz and Piekarewicz (2001) who used RPA with RMF interactions to study the correlation between neutron-skin thickness and transition density. The results are compared with calculations using the MDI Gogny interaction by Xu et al. (2009), the Akmal-Pandharipande-Ravenhall EOS (1998) with the $A18+\delta v+UIX^*$ interaction, and DBHF calculations with the Bonn B potential by Sammarruca et al. (2009). The constrained values lie close to the result of Akmal et al. (1998) and within the region determined by current uncertainty in the density dependence of the symmetry energy, enabling more precise predictions for crust properties such as the crustal fraction of the moment of inertia relevant to pulsar glitch observations.
