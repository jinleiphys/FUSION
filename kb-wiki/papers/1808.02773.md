# Extraction of $γn\toπN$ observables from deuteron-target data

> An examination is conducted on a commonly used procedure for extracting (un)polarized $γn\to π^-p$ and $γn\to π^0n$ observables from $d(γ,π^-)pp$ and $d(γ,π^0)pn$ data, using a model that consists of the impulse term and the final-state interaction (FSI) terms due to nucleon- and pion-exchange. Recent experimental and theoretical analyses used an extraction method that does not impose a cut on the final $πN$ invariant mass $W$. I demonstrate that the use of this method can result in the $γn\to πN$ observables that are seriously distorted by the nucleon Fermi motion, and that one can efficiently avoid this problem by imposing a cut on $W$. It is also shown that the use of kinematical cuts of recent experimental analyses can still leave in the selected samples substantial FSI effects that must be corrected in extracting the $γn \toπN$ cross sections. In terms of the nucleon- and pion-exchange mechanisms, I give the first qualitative explanation of the FSI corrections, obtained in a recent MAMI experiment, for extracting $γn\to π^0 n$ cross sections.

## Key claim

The commonly used extraction formula that does not impose a cut on the final πN invariant mass W can produce γn→πN observables seriously distorted by nucleon Fermi motion, and even when kinematical cuts from recent experimental analyses are applied, substantial final-state interaction (FSI) effects remain that must be corrected.

## Method

The author constructed a model for d(γ,π)NN including impulse, N-exchange, and π-exchange mechanisms, using the ANL-Osaka model for γN→πN and πN→πN elementary amplitudes and the CD-Bonn potential for the deuteron wave function and NN→NN amplitudes. He generated "data" from this model, applied kinematical cuts from recent JLab analyses (Cuts A, B, and C from Table 1), and extracted γn→πN observables using two different formulas: one with a W-cut [Eq. (4)] and one without [Eq. (6)]. The extracted observables were compared to the free γn→πN observables calculated with the same elementary amplitudes to quantify distortions from Fermi motion and FSI.

## Key numbers

- At Eγ=300 MeV (W̄=1200 MeV), extracted γn→πN cross sections using Eq. (6) (no W-cut) are significantly smaller than free cross sections due to averaging over W=1180-1210 MeV across the Δ(1232) peak
- At Eγ=1000 MeV (W̄=1660 MeV), extracted cross sections using Eq. (6) reproduce free cross sections fairly well for cosθ<0.5 but show visible deviations for cosθ>0.5
- For γn→π0n at Eγ=300 MeV and cosθ∼-1, the FSI reduction factor RFSI is approximately 0.74 for γn→π0n versus 0.81 for γp→π0p
- The π-exchange FSI effect in d(γ,π0)pn reduces the spectrum for proton momenta pp≲190 MeV and enhances it for pp≳190 MeV before kinematical cuts are applied

## Context

This work extends the theoretical studies of Tarasov et al. (JLab1, Tara, Tara-1) who estimated FSI corrections for extracting γn→πN cross sections but neglected the π-exchange mechanism for d(γ,π0)pn and used an extraction formula that did not include a W-cut. The author provides the first qualitative explanation of the FSI corrections obtained in the MAMI experiment (MAMI1, MAMI6) for γn→π0n cross sections, showing that π-exchange FSI plays a crucial role. The results enable more accurate extraction of γn→πN observables from deuteron-target data at facilities like Jefferson Laboratory and MAMI, and demonstrate that polarization asymmetries Σ, E, and G are reasonably safe from FSI distortions when extracted with the W-cut formula.
