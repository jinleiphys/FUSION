# The centrality dependence of elliptic flow, the hydrodynamic limit, and the viscosity of hot QCD

> We show that the centrality and system-size dependence of elliptic flow measured at RHIC are fully described by a simple model based on eccentricity scaling and incomplete thermalization. We argue that the elliptic flow is at least 25% below the (ideal) ``hydrodynamic limit'', even for the most central Au-Au collisions. This lack of perfect equilibration allows for estimates of the effective parton cross section in the Quark-Gluon Plasma and of its viscosity to entropy density ratio. We also show how the initial conditions affect the transport coefficients and thermodynamic quantities extracted from the data, in particular the viscosity and the speed of sound.

## Key claim

The centrality and system-size dependence of elliptic flow at RHIC can be described by a simple model based on eccentricity scaling and incomplete thermalization, but the elliptic flow is at least 25% below the ideal hydrodynamic limit even for the most central Au-Au collisions, contrary to the common interpretation that RHIC data saturates the hydrodynamic limit.

## Method

The authors fit PHOBOS data for Au-Au and Cu-Cu collisions using a formula relating scaled elliptic flow (v2/epsilon) to the Knudsen number K = lambda/R, where lambda is the mean free path and R is the transverse size. They compare two types of initial conditions: a Glauber model with 80% participant and 20% binary-collision scaling, and a Color-Glass Condensate (CGC) approach incorporating gluon saturation and eccentricity fluctuations. The fit parameters yield the hydrodynamic limit v2_hydro/epsilon and the effective partonic cross section sigma.

## Key numbers

- v2_hydro/epsilon = 0.30 +/- 0.02 for Glauber initial conditions
- v2_hydro/epsilon = 0.22 +/- 0.01 for CGC initial conditions
- Partonic cross section sigma = 4.3 +/- 0.6 mb for Glauber initial conditions
- Partonic cross section sigma = 5.5 +/- 0.5 mb for CGC initial conditions (increasing to 7.6 +/- 0.7 mb when accounting for the softer equation of state)
- Viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s = 0.19 for Glauber initial conditions
- Viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s = 0.11 for CGC initial conditions
- In central Au-Au collisions, v2 reaches 70% of the hydrodynamic limit for Glauber initial conditions and 75% for CGC initial conditions
- Mean free path lambda = 0.60 fm for Glauber and 0.34 fm for CGC initial conditions

## Context

This work extends the approach of Bhalerao (2005) by applying the Knudsen number scaling formula to actual RHIC data and comparing two different models of initial conditions. It contradicts the widespread interpretation (e.g., Tannenbaum 2006) that RHIC data fully saturates the hydrodynamic limit, showing instead that significant dissipative corrections remain. The extracted viscosity values are close to the lattice estimate of Meyer (2007) for SU(3) gluodynamics (eta/s = 0.134 +/- 0.033 at T = 1.65 Tc) and agree with estimates from heavy quark energy loss and elliptic flow (phenixQ), transverse momentum correlations (Gavin and Aziz), and bounds on entropy production (DMN). The results enable future hydrodynamic simulations to use the isolated v2_hydro/epsilon values to test realistic equations of state from lattice QCD.
