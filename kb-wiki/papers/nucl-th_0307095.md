# Medium influence of the nucleon nucleon cross section on the fragmentation

> Based on an isospin dependent quantum molecular dynamics model we studied the influence of a medium correction of an isospin dependent nucleon nucleon cross section on the fragmentation at the intermediate energy heavy ion collisions. We found that the medium correction from an isospin dependent nucleon nucleon cross section increases the dependence of the fragmentation on the isospin effect of in-medium nucleon nucleon cross section, at the same time, the momentum dependent interaction (MDI) produces also an important role for enhancing the influence of the medium correction on the isospin effect of two-body collisions in the fragmentation process. In this case, the dependence of the multiplicity of intermediate mass fragments $N_{imf}$ on the isospin effect of two-body collision due to the medium correction and MDI, is a probe for extracting the information on the isospin dependent nucleon cnucleon cross section in the medium correction.

# Medium influence of the nucleon nucleon cross section on the fragmentation

## Key claim

The medium correction of an isospin dependent nucleon nucleon cross section (with density parameter alpha = -0.2) enhances the dependence of the multiplicity of intermediate mass fragments on the isospin effect of two-body collisions, and the momentum dependent interaction (MDI) plays an important role in further enhancing this medium correction effect.

## Method

The authors used an isospin dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model with Skyrme-Hatree-Fock initial density distributions (SKM* parameter set) to simulate intermediate energy heavy ion collisions. They compared calculations using an isospin dependent in-medium nucleon nucleon cross section (sigma_iso, where sigma_np is larger than sigma_nn = sigma_pp) versus an isospin independent one (sigma_noiso, where all cross sections are equal), with and without medium correction (alpha = -0.2 versus alpha = 0.0 in the density-dependent cross section formula), and with and without momentum dependent interaction (MDI versus NOMDI).

## Key numbers

- The free neutron-proton cross section is about a factor of 3 times larger than the free proton-proton or free neutron-neutron cross section below 400 MeV.
- The empirical density dependent nucleon nucleon cross section in medium uses the formula sigma_NN = (1 + alpha * rho/rho_0) * sigma_free_NN, with alpha = -0.2 found to reproduce flow data.
- The multiplicity of intermediate mass fragments N_imf is defined as fragments with charge numbers from 3 to 13.
- Calculations were performed at beam energy of 100 MeV/nucleon and impact parameter of 4 fm for the time evolution studies, and at beam energy of 150 MeV/nucleon and b = 4.0 fm for the density ratio studies.
- The impact parameter averaged multiplicity <N_imf>_b was evaluated at equilibrium time 200 fm/c for the systems 76Kr + 76Kr and 112Sn + 40Ca.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same group (Liu et al., 2003) on isospin effects in nuclear stopping and fragmentation using the IQMD model, which itself builds on the quantum molecular dynamics model of Aichelin (1991) and others. The study addresses the open question of how the in-medium nucleon nucleon cross section and the symmetry potential of the mean field affect heavy ion collision dynamics, as reviewed by Li et al. (2001). The results suggest that the multiplicity of intermediate mass fragments can serve as a probe for extracting information on the isospin dependent nucleon nucleon cross section in the medium correction.
