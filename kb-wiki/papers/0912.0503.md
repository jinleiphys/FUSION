# Garvey-Kelson Relations for Nuclear Charge Radii

> The Garvey-Kelson relations (GKRs) are algebraic expressions originally developed to predict nuclear masses. In this letter we show that the GKRs provide a fruitful framework for the prediction of other physical observables that also display a slowly-varying dynamics. Based on this concept, we extend the GKRs to the study of nuclear charge radii. The GKRs are tested on 455 out of the approximately 800 nuclei whose charge radius is experimentally known. We find a rms deviation between the GK predictions and the experimental values of only 0.01 fm. This should be contrasted against some of the most successful microscopic models that yield rms deviations almost three times as large. Predictions - with reliable uncertainties - are provided for 116 nuclei whose charge radius is presently unknown.

## Key claim

The Garvey-Kelson relations, originally developed for nuclear masses, can be extended to predict nuclear charge radii with a root-mean-square deviation of only 0.0097 fm when tested on 455 nuclei, provided that the underlying observable displays a slowly-varying dynamics with small quantum fluctuations.

## Method

The authors applied the two Garvey-Kelson relations, which are linear combinations of six neighboring nuclei, to the nuclear charge radius observable instead of masses. For each nucleus, up to 12 possible estimates were averaged to produce a GK prediction, which was then compared against the experimental charge radii from Angeli's 2004 compilation. A global statistical analysis using a Dipole probability density function was performed to attach reliable confidence intervals to the predictions.

## Key numbers

- RMS deviation between GK predictions and experimental values for 455 nuclei: 0.0097 fm
- RMS deviation for the microscopic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model HFB-8: 0.0275 fm (for 782 nuclei with Z>=8 and N>=8)
- RMS deviation for the HFBCS model with MSk7 interaction on Tin isotopes: 0.0082 fm; GK relations on the same Tin isotopes: 0.0031 fm
- Dipole PDF parameters for the distribution of differences between GK prediction and experiment: mean mu = 3.633 x 10^{-3} fm, standard deviation sigma = 4.554 x 10^{-3} fm
- Confidence intervals for the Dipole PDF: CI(1) = 0.818 (82% confidence within +/- 1 sigma), CI(2) = 0.960 (96% confidence within +/- 2 sigma)
- Standard deviation of experimental errors from Dipole fit: sigma_exp = 9.427 x 10^{-3} fm
- Largest deviation between GK prediction and experiment: about 0.06 fm (for a handful of nuclei)
- 331 out of 455 GK predictions fell within experimental error
- Predictions provided for 116 nuclei whose charge radius is presently unknown

## Context

This work extends the Garvey-Kelson relations, originally developed by Garvey and Kelson (1966, 1969) for nuclear masses and recently revitalized by Barea et al. (2005, 2008) and Morales et al. (2009), to the observable of nuclear charge radii. The GK approach is compared against macroscopic-microscopic models such as those by Myers (1983), Duflo (1994), and Buchinger et al. (1994, 2001, 2005), as well as microscopic mean-field models including HFB-8 by Buchinger et al. (2005), BSk17 by Goriely et al. (2009), and D1M by Goriely et al. (2009). The results enable reliable predictions for 116 unmeasured charge radii and suggest future applications to other finite quantum systems such as helium and metal clusters.
