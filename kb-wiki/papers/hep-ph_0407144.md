# Is it possible to construct the Proton Structure Function by Lorentz-boosting the Static Quark-model Wave Function?

> The energy-momentum relations for massive and massless particles are E = p^2/2m and E = pc respectively. According to Einstein, these two different expressions come from the same formula E = \sqrt{(cp)^2 + m^2 c^4}. Quarks and partons are believed to be the same particles, but they have quite different properties. Are they two different manifestations of the same covariant entity as in the case of Einstein's energy-momentum relation? The answer to this question is YES. It is possible to construct harmonic oscillator wave functions which can be Lorentz-boosted. They describe quarks bound together inside hadrons. When they are boosted to an infinite-momentum frame, these wave functions exhibit all the peculiar properties of Feynman's parton picture. This formalism leads to a parton distribution corresponding to the valence quarks, with a good agreement with the experimentally observed distribution.

# Is it possible to construct the Proton Structure Function by Lorentz-boosting the Static Quark-model Wave Function?

## Key claim
It is possible to construct harmonic oscillator wave functions that can be Lorentz-boosted and, when boosted to an infinite-momentum frame, produce a valence quark parton distribution that agrees well with experimentally observed data. The claim is conditional on using a specific set of covariant harmonic oscillator solutions that are localized and normalizable in both space and time coordinates, rather than the non-normalizable solutions originally proposed by Feynman et al. (1971).

## Method
The authors use a Lorentz-invariant harmonic oscillator equation to describe the internal motion of two quarks bound inside a hadron. They construct space-time wave functions for the hadron at rest, then apply Lorentz boosts along the longitudinal (z) direction using the boost parameter eta, which transforms the wave functions into a Lorentz-squeezed form. The momentum-energy wave functions are obtained by Fourier transformation of the space-time wave functions, and the resulting parton distribution is calculated from the infinite-momentum frame limit and compared to experimental data.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of external interaction time to internal oscillation time is given by T_ext/T_osc = exp(-2 eta); for a 900 GeV proton from the Fermilab accelerator, this ratio becomes 10^{-6}.
- The uncertainty product for light-cone variables satisfies <u^2><q_u^2> = 1/4 and <v^2><q_v^2> = 1/4, demonstrating Lorentz invariance of Planck's constant.
- The product of longitudinal position and momentum widths yields <z^2><q_z^2> = (1/4)[cosh(2 eta)]^2, which increases with velocity and is not a conjugate pair in the moving frame.

## Context
This work extends the covariant harmonic oscillator formalism developed by Feynman, Kislinger, and Ravndal (1971) and further elaborated by Kim and Noz in previous work (1986). It directly addresses the apparent paradox between the quark model (with a finite number of bound quarks) and Feynman's parton picture (1969), which describes hadrons at high velocity as collections of many free, nearly massless partons. The formalism enables a unified description where the static quark-model wave function and the parton distribution function emerge as two limits of the same covariant quantum bound state, and it provides a calculable valence quark distribution that can be compared with experimental data from high-energy scattering experiments.
