# Tetrahedral symmetry in Zr nuclei: Calculations of low-energy excitations with Gogny interaction

> We report on the results of the calculations of the low energy excitation patterns for three Zirconium isotopes, viz. $^{80}$Zr$_{40}$, $^{96}$Zr$_{56}$ and $^{110}$Zr$_{70}$, reported by other authors to be doubly-magic tetrahedral nuclei (with tetrahedral magic numbers $Z$=40 and $N$=40, 56 and 70). We employ the realistic Gogny effective interactions using three variants of their parametrisation and the particle-number, parity and the angular-momentum projection techniques. We confirm quantitatively that the resulting spectra directly follow the pattern expected from the group theory considerations for the tetrahedral symmetric quantum objects. We also find out that, for all the nuclei studied, the correlation energy obtained after the angular momentum projection is very large for the tetrahedral deformation as well as other octupole deformations. The lowering of the energies of the resulting configurations is considerable, i.e. by about 10 MeV or even more, once again confirming the significance of the angular-momentum projections techniques in the mean-field nuclear structure calculations.

## Key claim

The authors confirm quantitatively that the low-energy excitation spectra of three Zirconium isotopes (⁸⁰Zr, ⁹⁶Zr, and ¹¹⁰Zr) follow the pattern expected from group theory for tetrahedral-symmetric quantum objects, but they hedge that this confirmation relies on the specific choice of Gogny interaction parametrizations (D1S, D1N, D1M) and on the use of particle-number, parity, and angular-momentum projection techniques.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with three variants of the Gogny effective interaction (D1S, D1N, D1M) using a harmonic oscillator basis expansion. They applied quadratic constraints on multipole moment operators, primarily the axial-quadrupole operator \(\hat{Q}_{20}\) and the tetrahedral operator \(\hat{Q}_{32}\), and then carried out full three-dimensional angular-momentum, parity, and particle-number projections from the constrained HFB states, solving the Hill-Wheeler equation to obtain energy eigenvalues.

## Key numbers

- The tetrahedral ground-state minimum for ⁸⁰Zr lies approximately 0.6 MeV below the spherical-shape configuration energy (using D1S, D1N, and D1M parametrizations with \(N_{\rm osc}^{\rm max}=12\)).
- The correlation energy after angular-momentum projection is very large for tetrahedral and other octupole deformations, lowering configuration energies by about 10 MeV or more.
- For the tetrahedral-symmetry minimum in ⁸⁰Zr with D1S, the geometrical symmetry relation for rank-7 moments is verified to six-digit accuracy: \(Q_{76} = -Q_{72} \times \sqrt{11/13}\).
- The model space used primarily had \(N_{\rm osc}^{\rm max}=12\) (13 shells) or 8 (9 shells), with energy differences between spherical and prolate minima larger by about 1 MeV for \(N_{\rm osc}^{\rm max}=8\) compared to \(N_{\rm osc}^{\rm max}=16\).
- The single-particle energy gaps for \(Z=N=40\) in ⁸⁰Zr are very large, causing pairing correlations to vanish for both neutrons and protons.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Tagami et al. (2013) that used a schematic separable-type interaction with a Woods-Saxon mean-field potential, now employing the more realistic Gogny effective interactions. It also builds on the prediction by Dudek (2002) that ⁸⁰Zr, ⁹⁶Zr, and ¹¹⁰Zr are doubly-magic tetrahedral nuclei. The results enable future experimental searches for tetrahedral symmetry in these nuclei, particularly through measurement of octupole (E3) transitions, since collective E1 and E2 transitions are expected to be negligible under exact tetrahedral symmetry.
