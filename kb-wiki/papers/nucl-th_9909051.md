# Nucleon-Nucleon Correlations and Two-Nucleon Currents in Exclusive ($e,e'NN$) Reactions

> The contributions of short-range nucleon-nucleon (NN) correlations, various meson exchange current (MEC) terms and the influence of $Δ$ isobar excitations (isobaric currents, IC) on exclusive two-nucleon knockout reactions induced by electron scattering are investigated. The nuclear structure functions are evaluated for nuclear matter. Realistic NN interactions derived in the framework of One-Boson-Exchange model are employed to evaluate the effects of correlations and MEC in a consistent way. The correlations correlations are determined by solving the Bethe-Goldstone equation. This yields significant contributions to the structure functions W_L and W_T of the (e,e'pn) and (e,e'pp) reactions. These contributions compete with MEC corrections originating from the $π$ and $ρ$ exchange terms of the same interaction. Special attention is paid to the so-called 'super parallel' kinematics at momentum transfers which can be measured e.g. at MAMI in Mainz.

## Key claim

Short-range nucleon-nucleon correlations, meson exchange currents, and isobar currents all contribute to exclusive two-nucleon knockout reactions, but their relative importance depends sensitively on the chosen kinematics; in particular, the "super parallel" kinematic setup suppresses competing two-body currents and therefore provides a clean window to study correlation effects, especially in the longitudinal structure function.

## Method

The authors evaluated nuclear structure functions for infinite nuclear matter at saturation density using the Bonn A one-boson-exchange potential. Short-range correlations were treated by solving the Bethe-Goldstone equation for the G matrix, while meson exchange currents from pion exchange and isobar currents from Delta resonance excitations were computed with consistent coupling constants and form factors. The calculations were performed for exclusive (e,e'pp) and (e,e'pn) reactions under various "super parallel" kinematic conditions, with final-state interactions included at the mean-field level using an effective mass approximation.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: Fermi momentum k_F = 1.35 fm^{-1}
- Effective mass m* = 623 MeV and single-particle potential U = -86.8 MeV, derived from the Bonn A potential
- Pion-nucleon coupling constant f_{\pi NN} = 1.005, f_{\pi N\Delta} = 2 f_{\pi NN}, and f_{\gamma N\Delta} = 0.12
- Cutoff mass for pion-nucleon form factor: Lambda_pi = 1.3 GeV
- Photon energy omega = 215 MeV used in the "super parallel" kinematic studies
- For asymmetric energy distribution in (e,e'pp): T_{p,1} = 156 MeV (parallel to q) and T_{p,2} = 33 MeV (antiparallel to q)
- For symmetric energy distribution in (e,e'pp): T_{p,1} = 116 MeV and T_{p,2} = 73 MeV
- Structure functions for (e,e'pn) reactions are roughly 10 times larger than for (e,e'pp) reactions
- Correlation contributions alone are enhanced by roughly a factor of 7 when comparing (e,e'pn) with (e,e'pp)
- Bonn A potential yields a deuteron D-state probability of 4.38 percent
- For the alternative kinematics with T_p = T_n = 70 MeV and theta_p' = theta_n' = 30 degrees, photon energy omega = 230 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of two-nucleon knockout reactions by providing a consistent framework that treats correlations, meson exchange currents, and isobar currents on equal footing using the same nucleon-nucleon interaction. It builds on the approach of Giusti et al. (1998) for finite nuclei like O-16, but uses infinite nuclear matter to isolate short-range effects from surface and finite-size effects. The results enable experimentalists at facilities like MAMI in Mainz to design kinematic setups that maximize sensitivity to specific reaction mechanisms, and the authors note that future work should include final-state interactions beyond the mean-field approximation, as demonstrated in Monte Carlo simulations by Gil, Nieves, and Oset (1997).
