# Theoretical neutron-capture cross sections for r-process nucleosynthesis in the $^{48}$Ca region

> We calculate neutron capture cross sections for r-process nucleosynthesis in the $^{48}$Ca region, namely for the isotopes $^{40-44}$S, $^{46-50}$Ar, $^{56-66}$Ti, $^{62-68}$Cr, and $^{72-76}$Fe. While previously only cross sections resulting from the compound nucleus reaction mechanism (Hauser-Feshbach) have been considered, we recalculate not only that contribution to the cross section but also include direct capture on even-even nuclei. The level schemes, which are of utmost importance in the direct capture calculations, are taken from quasi-particle states obtained with a folded-Yukawa potential and Lipkin-Nogami pairing. Most recent deformation values derived from experimental data on $β$-decay half lives are used where available. Due to the consideration of direct capture, the capture rates are enhanced and the "turning points" in the r-process path are shifted to slightly higher mass numbers. We also discuss the sensitivity of the direct capture cross sections on the assumed deformation.

## Key claim

The inclusion of direct capture (DC) in addition to the compound nucleus (Hauser-Feshbach) mechanism enhances neutron-capture rates for nuclei in the ⁴⁸Ca region, shifting the "turning points" in the r-process path to slightly higher mass numbers; however, the DC contribution is sensitive to the assumed deformation and can vanish entirely when the target and final nucleus have different deformations, as seen in the case of ⁴⁴S.

## Method

The authors calculated neutron-capture cross sections for even-even isotopes of S, Ar, Ti, Cr, and Fe using both the compound nucleus (Hauser-Feshbach) mechanism and direct capture to bound states. Quasi-particle levels were computed from a folded-Yukawa potential with Lipkin-Nogami pairing, and deformation values were taken from experimental β-decay half-lives where available or from a theoretical mass formula. The direct capture cross sections were obtained using the code TEDCA, with spectroscopic factors derived from occupation probabilities, and the compound nucleus cross sections were computed with the statistical model code SMOKER.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁰S: DC cross section = 0.4246 mb, CN cross section = 0.0851 mb, total = 0.5097 mb at 30 keV (c.m.) Maxwellian average; DC contributes 83% of total.
- For ⁴²S: DC = 0.9466 mb, CN = 0.0202 mb, total = 0.9668 mb; DC contributes 98% of total.
- For ⁴⁴S with deformation ε₂ = −0.20: DC = 0.0000 mb, CN = 0.0044 mb, total = 0.0044 mb; DC contributes 0%.
- For ⁴⁴S with deformation ε₂ = +0.30: DC = 0.014 mb, CN = 0.0044 mb, total = 0.0184 mb; DC contributes 76%.
- For ⁴⁶Ar: DC = 0.5295 mb, CN = 0.1203 mb, total = 0.6498 mb; DC contributes 81%.
- For ⁷⁶Fe: DC = 0.0050 mb, CN = 3×10⁻⁶ mb, total = 0.0050 mb; DC contributes >99%.
- Neutron-capture half-lives were computed at neutron number densities of 3×10¹⁹ cm⁻³ (S, Ar) and 6×10²⁰ cm⁻³ (Ti, Cr, Fe).

## Context

This work extends previous r-process network calculations by Sorlin (1993) and Kratz (1995) that considered only compound nucleus cross sections, by adding direct capture contributions for even-even nuclei in the ⁴⁸Ca region. The authors used deformation parameters inferred from β-decay half-lives measured by Böhmer (1995) and the QRPA parameter study of Böhmer et al., and employed the theoretical mass formula of Möller, Nix, Myers, and Swiatecky (1995). The results enable more accurate r-process network calculations with varying neutron densities to determine isotopic abundances and ratios, particularly for the Ca-Ti-Cr-Fe isotopic anomalies found in meteoritic inclusions.
