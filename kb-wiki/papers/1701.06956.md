# Momentum anisotropy effects for quarkonium in a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma below the melting temperature

> In the early stages of heavy-ion collisions, the hot QCD matter expands more longitudinally than transversely. This imbalance causes the system to become rapidly colder in the longitudinal direction and a local momentum anisotropy appears. In this paper, we study the heavy-quarkonium spectrum in the presence of a small plasma anisotropy. We work in the framework of pNRQCD at finite temperature. We inspect arrangements of non-relativistic and thermal scales complementary to those considered in the literature. In particular, we consider temperatures larger and Debye masses smaller than the binding energy, which is a temperature range relevant for presently running LHC experiments. In this setting we compute the leading thermal corrections to the binding energy and the thermal width induced by quarkonium gluo-dissociation.

## Key claim
For a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma below the melting temperature, where the binding energy exceeds the Debye mass, the presence of a small momentum anisotropy modifies the quarkonium binding energy and thermal width, but the sign and magnitude of the effect depend crucially on whether the normalization factor \(N(\xi)=\sqrt{1+\xi}\) is included in the anisotropic distribution function.

## Method
The authors work within potential non-relativistic QCD (pNRQCD) at finite temperature, assuming a Coulombic quarkonium with hierarchy \(m \gg m\alpha_s \gg \pi T \gg m\alpha_s^2 \gg m_D,\Lambda_{\rm QCD}\). They integrate out the temperature scale \(\pi T\) to match pNRQCD onto pNRQCD\(_{\rm HTL}\), obtaining the thermal correction to the singlet potential from a self-energy diagram with loop momentum \(\sim \pi T\). The binding-energy scale \(m\alpha_s^2\) is then integrated out to compute the thermal width from gluo-dissociation, using an anisotropic distribution function \(f^{\rm B}(\mathbf{q},\xi) = N(\xi)\,f^{\rm B}_{\rm iso}\left(\sqrt{\mathbf{q}^2+\xi(\mathbf{q}\cdot\mathbf{n})^2}\right)\) with anisotropy parameter \(\xi>0\).

- Hierarchy considered: \(m \approx 5\) GeV, \(m\alpha_s \approx 1.5\) GeV, \(m\alpha_s^2 \approx 0.5\) GeV for \(\Upsilon(1S)\), with \(T \lesssim 2T_c \approx 0.3\) GeV for bottomonium.
- Binding-energy shift for a \(1S\) bottomonium state: shown for \(\xi=0\), \(\xi=0.5\), and \(\xi=1\) in Fig. 2, with the shift proportional to \(T^2\).
- Thermal width for a \(1S\) bottomonium state: shown for \(\xi=0\), \(\xi=0.5\), and \(\xi=1\) in Fig. 3, with the width proportional to \(\alpha_s^3 T\).
- Anisotropy functions at \(\xi=0.5\): \(\mathcal{F}_1=1.067\), \(\mathcal{F}_2=1.383\), \(\mathcal{F}_3=0.114\), \(\mathcal{G}_1=1.141\), \(\mathcal{G}_2=0.041\) (from Table I).
- For \(\xi \le 1\), differences between thermal widths of \(1P\) and \(2S\) bottomonium states are at most 1% of the \(2S\) thermal width.

## Context
This work extends the isotropic pNRQCD study of Brambilla et al. (2010) to include momentum anisotropy, complementing earlier anisotropic studies by Dumitru et al. (2007, 2009) and Burnier et al. (2009) that used hard thermal loop resummation for temperatures above the inverse radius. The hierarchy considered (\(m\alpha_s^2 \gg m_D\)) is relevant for bottomonium at LHC temperatures below the melting temperature, where gluo-dissociation dominates the thermal width. The results enable phenomenological studies of quarkonium suppression and polarization in anisotropic QGP evolution models.
