# Interacting hadron resonance gas model in magnetic field and the fluctuations of conserved charges

> In this paper we discuss the interacting hadron resonance gas model in presence of a constant external magnetic field. The short range repulsive interaction between hadrons are accounted through van der Waals excluded volume correction to the ideal gas pressure. Here we take the sizes of hadrons as $r_π$ (pion radius) $= 0$ fm, $r_K$ (kaon radius) $= 0.35$ fm, $r_m$ (all other meson radii) $= 0.3$ fm and $r_b$ (baryon radii) $= 0.5$ fm. We analyse the effect of uniform background magnetic field on the thermodynamic properties of interacting hadron gas. We especially discuss the effect of interactions on the behaviour of magnetization of low temperature hadronic matter. The vacuum terms have been regularized using magnetic field independent regularization scheme. We find that the magnetization of hadronic matter is positive which implies that the low temperature hadronic matter is paramagnetic. We further find that the repulsive interactions have very negligible effect on the overall magnetization of the hadronic matter and the paramagnetic property of the hadronic phase remains unchanged. We have also investigated the effects of short range repulsive interactions as well as the magnetic field on the baryon and electric charge number susceptibilities of hadronic matter within the ambit of excluded volume hadron resonance gas model.

# Interacting hadron resonance gas model in magnetic field and the fluctuations of conserved charges

## Key claim
The authors find that low-temperature hadronic matter is paramagnetic (positive magnetization) and that short-range repulsive interactions between hadrons have a very negligible effect on the overall magnetization, leaving the paramagnetic property unchanged. This conclusion holds specifically within the excluded-volume hadron resonance gas model using magnetic field independent regularization, and the authors note that the thermal part of magnetization can become negative in certain temperature ranges even though the total magnetization remains positive.

## Method
The authors extend the ideal hadron resonance gas (HRG) model by incorporating short-range repulsive interactions via a van der Waals excluded-volume correction, yielding a transcendental equation for the pressure. They compute thermodynamic quantities and conserved charge susceptibilities (baryon number and electric charge) for a gas of hadrons with spin less than 3/2 in a constant external magnetic field, using the magnetic field independent regularization (MFIR) scheme to handle divergent vacuum terms. The hadron hard-core radii are taken as r_pi = 0 fm, r_K = 0.35 fm, r_m (all other mesons) = 0.3 fm, and r_b (baryons) = 0.5 fm, following the prescription of Bugaev (2000).

## Key numbers
- Hadron hard-core radii: pion radius = 0 fm, kaon radius = 0.35 fm, all other meson radii = 0.3 fm, baryon radii = 0.5 fm.
- At mu_B = 300 MeV and eB = 0.2 GeV^2, the pressure increase at finite mu_B relative to zero mu_B is about 16% for HRG and 9% for EVHRG.
- At mu_B = 300 MeV and eB = 0, the energy density increase at finite mu_B relative to zero mu_B is about 25% for HRG, while for EVHRG it does not change.
- At mu_B = 300 MeV and eB = 0.2 GeV^2, the energy density increase at finite mu_B relative to zero mu_B is about 19% for HRG and 8% for EVHRG.
- Pressures for HRG and EVHRG models are almost identical up to T ~ 0.1 GeV for eB = 0 and up to T ~ 0.12 GeV for eB = 0.2 GeV^2.

## Context
This work extends the ideal hadron resonance gas model in magnetic fields studied by Endrodi (2013) and the excluded-volume hadron resonance gas model developed by Rischke (1991) and Bugaev (2000), which previously described hadron yield ratios and the Strangeness Horn behavior. The authors incorporate for the first time the combined effects of magnetic field and repulsive interactions on the equation of state and conserved charge fluctuations. This enables more realistic modeling of the hadronic phase in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where strong magnetic fields (B ~ m_pi^2) are created, and provides a baseline for comparison with lattice QCD results on susceptibilities near the crossover temperature.
