# Anisotropic pressure of magnetized quark matter with anomalous magnetic moment

> We investigate magnetic field $(eB)$ dependence of constituent quark mass, the longitudinal and transverse pressure as well as the magnetization and magnetic susceptibility of strongly interacting quark matter. We employ the two-flavour Polyakov Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with the inclusion of the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of the quarks at finite temperature $(T)$ and finite quark chemical potential $(μ_q)$ capturing different stages of chiral phase transition. We find that the transverse pressure, magnetization and magnetic susceptibility become highly oscillatory for large values of $eB$ in the chiral symmetry broken phase. However the oscillations cease to occur at higher values of $T$ and $μ_q$ when chiral symmetry is (partially) restored and the anisotropic nature of the pressure becomes significant even at smaller values of $eB$. As the inclusion of AMM of the quarks leads to inverse magnetic catalysis of the transition temperature we observe that the variations of transverse pressure, magnetization and magnetic susceptibility are significantly modified in the vicinity of the chiral transition temperature. Furthermore, above the chiral transition temperature the magnetic susceptibility is found to remain positive for a wide range of $eB$ indicating a paramagnetic character of the strongly interacting quark matter. Finally, we have also examined the magnetism of strongly interacting matter in the quarkyonic phase. The obtained results could be useful for a magnetohydrodynamic evolution of hot and dense matter created in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of quarks in the two-flavour Polyakov Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model leads to inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) of the chiral transition temperature, which significantly modifies the magnetic field dependence of transverse pressure, magnetization, and magnetic susceptibility, particularly in the vicinity of the chiral transition temperature; the oscillations in these quantities at large eB vanish at higher temperatures and chemical potentials where chiral symmetry is partially restored.

## Method

The authors employed the two-flavour Polyakov Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with the inclusion of the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of quarks, using a smooth cutoff regularization scheme with parameters fixed to reproduce vacuum values of pion decay constant (92.4 MeV), pion mass (138 MeV), and chiral condensate. They solved the coupled gap equations for the constituent quark mass, Polyakov loop, and its conjugate self-consistently, then computed the longitudinal pressure, transverse pressure, magnetization, and magnetic susceptibility as functions of magnetic field strength eB at three different temperatures representing the chiral symmetry broken phase (T = 150 MeV), near the transition (T = 230 MeV), and above the transition (T = 260 MeV), for both zero and finite quark chemical potential (μ_q = 0 and 200 MeV).

## Key numbers

- Vacuum parameters: Λ = 620 MeV, GΛ² = 2.2, m₀ = 5.5 MeV, yielding f_π = 92.4 MeV, m_π = 138 MeV, and chiral condensate ⟨ūu⟩^{1/3} = -245.7 MeV
- AMM values: κ_u = 0.29016 GeV⁻¹, κ_d = 0.35986 GeV⁻¹ (following Fayazbakhsh 2014)
- At eB = 0 and μ_q = 0: chiral transition temperature T₀^{ch} ≈ 229 MeV; deconfinement transition temperature T₀^{decon} ≈ 217 MeV
- At T = 150 MeV (chiral symmetry broken phase): constituent quark mass increases with eB for both zero and non-zero AMM, with marginally smaller magnitude in the finite AMM case
- At T = 230 MeV (near transition) and μ_q = 0: constituent quark mass increases with eB without AMM but decreases with eB when AMM is included
- At T = 260 MeV (above transition): inclusion of AMM results in further decrease of constituent quark mass as a function of eB

## Context

This work extends previous studies of magnetized quark matter using the PNJL model (Ratti 2005, Ratti 2006) by incorporating the anomalous magnetic moment of quarks, which has been shown to produce inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) in earlier works (Fayazbakhsh 2014, Chaudhuri 2019, Chaudhuri 2020, Ghosh 2020). The results are relevant for magnetohydrodynamic evolution of hot and dense matter created in heavy-ion collisions, where strong magnetic fields of order ~10¹⁸ Gauss are generated (Kharzeev 2007, Skokov 2009), and for understanding the equation of state of compact stars such as neutron stars and quark stars (Chatterjee 2014, Ferrer 2010, Strickland 2012). The finding that magnetic susceptibility remains positive above the chiral transition temperature, indicating paramagnetic character, contrasts with lattice QCD results (Bali 2012, Bali 2020) that show diamagnetism at low temperatures and paramagnetism at high temperatures, and with earlier NJL model studies (Xu 2020, Lin 2022) that failed to reproduce the diamagnetic behavior in the chiral symmetry broken phase.
