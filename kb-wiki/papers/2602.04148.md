# Mapping Nuclear Deformation with Differential Radial Flow in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> In relativistic heavy-ion collisions, the radial flow of the fireball, usually characterized by transverse momentum spectra of final-state particles, encodes essential information about the hot and dense nuclear matter created in the collisions. However, the response of radial flow, including its $p_T$-differential structure $v_0(p_T)$ and longitudinal fluctuations $v_0(η)$, to intrinsic nuclear deformation remains unexplored. Using realistic $(3+1)$-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic calculations with Trento-3D initial conditions, we investigate how nuclear deformation affects the differential radial flow. We observe a clear, positive correlation between quadrupole deformation $β_2$ and radial flow: both magnitudes of $v_0$ and $v_0(p_T)$ are enhanced in central collisions when $β_2$ is increased. In contrast, the Pearson coefficient $ρ(n(p_T), [p_T])$ exhibits a universal step-like behavior across all collision systems and centralities. Further analysis of longitudinal decorrelation of radial flow reveals a rich structure: in central collisions, large $β_2$ tends to suppress the decorrelation, whereas hexadecapole deformation $β_4$ tends to enhance it. Such decorrelation effect increases toward peripheral collisions. Our results demonstrate that precise measurements of radial flow, spanning transverse momentum and longitudinal dependences, can provide powerful, complementary constraints on nuclear deformation in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions.

# Mapping Nuclear Deformation with Differential Radial Flow in Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim
Using (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations with Trento-3D initial conditions, the authors demonstrate that quadrupole deformation β₂ enhances both the integrated radial flow v₀ and its pT-differential form v₀(pT) in central and mid-central collisions, while the Pearson correlation coefficient ρ(n(pT), [pT]) shows a universal step-like behavior largely insensitive to nuclear structure. The longitudinal decorrelation of radial flow exhibits a richer structure, with large β₂ suppressing decorrelation and hexadecapole deformation β₄ enhancing it in central collisions.

## Method
The authors performed event-by-event (3+1)-dimensional CLVisc viscous hydrodynamic simulations with Trento-3D initial conditions for Au+Au and U+U collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV. They varied the quadrupole deformation parameter β₂ (0, 0.14, 0.20, 0.28) and hexadecapole deformation β₄ (0, 0.093) in the Woods-Saxon distribution of uranium nuclei, while keeping gold nuclei spherical. The hydrodynamic evolution included temperature-dependent shear and bulk viscosities, with particlization via the Cooper-Frye formula at Tfrz = 0.15 GeV, followed by resonance decays.

## Key numbers
- Quadrupole deformation values studied: β₂ = 0, 0.14, 0.20, 0.28; hexadecapole values: β₄ = 0, 0.093
- Nuclear radius parameters: R₀,Au = 6.38 fm, aAu = 0.535 fm; R₀,U = 6.67 fm, aU = 0.44 fm
- Gaussian nucleon width fixed at 0.59 fm; Gamma fluctuation parameter k = 2.0; number of hotspots Nq = 3 with width 0.3 fm
- Initial proper time τ₀ = 0.6 fm; freeze-out temperature Tfrz = 0.15 GeV
- Longitudinal cumulant coefficients: μ₀ = 0, σ₀ = 2.0, γ₀ = 6.0
- The STAR Collaboration extracted ²³⁸U deformation parameters as β₂^U = 0.297 ± 0.015 and γ_U = 8.5° ± 4.8° from experimental data
- Nuclear deformation changes the mean transverse momentum by less than 1% in the authors' setup

## Context
This work extends prior studies of nuclear deformation using anisotropic flow (STAR Collaboration, 2024) and mean transverse momentum fluctuations (Jia, 2021) by introducing differential radial flow observables as complementary probes. The authors build on the recently proposed v₀(pT) observable (Schenke, 2020) and its experimental measurements by ATLAS (2025) and ALICE (2025) collaborations, which previously focused on QGP properties rather than nuclear structure. The results enable future constraints on nuclear deformation parameters from radial flow measurements at RHIC and the LHC, and suggest extending such studies to asymmetric collision systems like Pb+Ne and Pb+O to probe light-nuclei structure.
