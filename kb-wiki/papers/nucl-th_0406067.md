# Pion Source in Au+Au Collisions at $\sqrt(S_NN)$=130 GeV

> The modified model emission function S(x,p), with a uniform radial expansion mode and mass reduction, is used to study the single particle spectra and two particle correlation of pions. The calculated HBT radii and one particle spectra are compared with the data from $\sqrt(S_NN)$=130 GeV Au+Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). We see the main features of the data are reproduced, Ro/Rs decreases with increasing Kt and we have a kinetic freeze out temperature T=150 MeV.

## Key claim

The modified emission function, incorporating a uniform radial expansion mode (independent of radial position) and an effective pion mass reduction, can simultaneously reproduce the main features of both the single-particle spectra and the HBT radii from Au+Au collisions at 130 GeV, including the experimentally observed decrease of \(R_{o}/R_{s}\) with increasing \(K_{T}\), a feature that standard hydrodynamic models fail to reproduce.

## Method

The author modifies a standard Gaussian emission function \(S(x,p)\) by replacing the Boltzmann distribution with a Bose-Einstein distribution, assuming a uniform radial flow rapidity profile (\(\eta_t = C\), independent of radius), and treating the pion mass as an adjustable parameter to account for temperature-dependent mass reduction. The model parameters are then fitted to the measured transverse momentum spectra of \(\pi^{-}\) and the HBT radii (\(R_s\), \(R_o\), \(R_l\)) from PHENIX and STAR data for central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 130\) GeV.

## Key numbers

- Fitted effective pion mass: \(m_\pi = 40\) MeV.
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature: \(T = 150\) MeV.
- Uniform transverse flow rapidity: \(\eta_t = 0.55\).
- Transverse Gaussian source size: \(R = 5.2\) fm.
- Longitudinal Gaussian width in space-time rapidity: \(\Delta\eta = 5.6\).
- Mean proper emission time: \(\tau_0 = 6\) fm/c.
- Emission duration: \(\Delta\tau = 1\) fm/c.
- The transverse momentum spectrum of \(\pi^{-}\) is fitted over a broad range \(P_T < 2.2\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the basic model of Akkelin and Heinz (2002) by addressing the long-standing problem that hydrodynamic models predict \(R_o/R_s > 1\) increasing with \(K_T\), while experiments (PHENIX, STAR) show a decreasing trend. The modified model enables a simultaneous description of spectra and correlations, suggesting that chemical and kinetic freeze-out may occur near the same temperature (\(\sim 150\) MeV). The author notes that a detailed calculation including resonance decay contributions is necessary for further improvement.
