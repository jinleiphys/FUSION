# Liquid-gas phase transition and Coulomb instability of asymmetric nuclear systems

> We use a chiral SU(3) quark mean field model to study the properties of nuclear systems at finite temperature. The liquid-gas phase transition of symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter is discussed. For two formulations of the model the critical temperature, $T_c$, for symmetric nuclear matter is found to be 15.8 MeV and 17.9 MeV. These values are consistent with those derived from recent experiments. The limiting temperatures for finite nuclei are in good agreement with the experimental points.

## Key claim

The chiral SU(3) quark mean field model, with parameters fixed from zero-temperature nuclear properties, predicts critical temperatures for symmetric nuclear matter of 15.82 MeV and 17.9 MeV for two formulations of the effective nucleon mass, both consistent with the experimentally extracted value of 16.6 ± 0.86 MeV.

## Method

The authors extended their chiral SU(3) quark mean field model, which treats quarks confined in baryons interacting with scalar and vector meson mean fields, to finite temperature and density. They computed the thermodynamic potential for nuclear matter and applied the Gibbs conditions for phase equilibrium, including the stability conditions on the free energy, to determine the liquid-gas coexistence curves for both symmetric and asymmetric infinite nuclear matter. For finite nuclei, they added Coulomb and surface tension contributions to the pressure and chemical potentials to find the limiting temperature above which nuclei cannot exist in equilibrium with vapor.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for symmetric nuclear matter: 15.82 MeV (square root ansatz for effective nucleon mass) and 17.9 MeV (linear definition of effective nucleon mass).
- Experimental reference value for critical temperature of infinite nuclear matter: 16.6 ± 0.86 MeV, from Natowitz et al. (2002).
- For asymmetric nuclear matter with the square root ansatz, the system can only be in the gas phase at any temperature when the asymmetry parameter α exceeds 0.88.
- Parameterizations of the limiting temperature for finite nuclei (valid for 10 ≤ A ≤ 208): (T_lim/T_c) = 0.611 − 0.00193A + 3.32 × 10⁻⁶ A² (square root case) and (T_lim/T_c) = 0.591 − 0.00203A + 3.80 × 10⁻⁶ A² (linear case).
- Surface tension parameterization: γ(T) = (1.14 MeV fm⁻²) × [1 + 3T/(2T_c)] × [1 − T/T_c].

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) quark mean field model, previously successful at zero temperature for nuclear matter, finite nuclei, and hypernuclei (Wang et al., 2003-2004), to finite temperature. It follows the thermodynamic approach of Glendenning (1985) and Muller and Serot (1995) for asymmetric nuclear matter phase transitions, and the results for limiting temperatures of finite nuclei are compared with experimental data from Natowitz et al. (2002). The calculated critical temperatures are consistent with those from the quark meson coupling model and the SU(2) chiral effective model (around 15-16 MeV), and are lower than those from the Walecka model (above 20 MeV) and the stiff Skyrme interaction SK1 (near 20 MeV). The results enable predictions for the Coulomb instability of finite nuclei and the behavior of asymmetric nuclear matter in heavy-ion collisions.
