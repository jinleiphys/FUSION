# Meson exchange currents in a relativistic model for electromagnetic one nucleon emission

> We analyze the role of meson exchange currents (MEC) in photon- and electron-induced one nucleon emission reactions in a fully relativistic model. The relativistic mean field theory is used for the bound state and the Pauli reduction for the scattering state. Direct 1-body and exchange 2-body terms in the nuclear current are considered. Results for the 12C(gamma,p) and 16O(gamma,p) differential cross sections and photon asymmetries are displayed in an energy range between 60 and 196 MeV. The 2-body seagull current affects the cross section less than in nonrelativistic analyses. In the case of the 16O(gamma,n) differential cross section MEC effects are large but not sufficient to reproduce the data. MEC have a small effect on (e,e'p) calculations.

## Key claim

The seagull (contact) meson exchange current enhances the relativistic direct knockout cross section for photon-induced reactions, but this enhancement is smaller than in nonrelativistic analyses and remains insufficient to reproduce the measured (gamma,n) cross section on 16O; for electron-induced reactions the effect is small and visible only at high missing momenta.

## Method

The authors performed fully relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) calculations for photon- and electron-induced one-nucleon knockout from 12C and 16O. The bound state wave functions were obtained from relativistic mean field theory, the scattering state was treated via the Pauli reduction scheme with scalar and vector optical potentials (EDAD1), and the nuclear current included both a 1-body (direct knockout) term and a 2-body seagull (contact) term derived from the pseudovector pi-N Lagrangian with one-pion exchange. Three different current-conserving (cc1, cc2, cc3) prescriptions for the 1-body off-shell current were compared.

## Key numbers

* 16O(gamma,p) cross section at E_gamma = 60 MeV: the pure 2-body (seagull) contribution is one order of magnitude lower than the 1-body (direct knockout) contribution.
* 16O(gamma,p) at E_gamma = 60 MeV: spectroscopic factor Z(p1/2) = 0.71 applied.
* 12C(gamma,p) at E_gamma = 58.4 MeV: spectroscopic factor Z(p3/2) = 0.56 applied.
* Photon energies studied: range between 60 and 196 MeV.
* Pion mass used: m_pi = 140 MeV; cutoff Lambda = 1250 MeV in the pion propagator; coupling constant f^2/(4pi) = 0.079.
* 16O(e,e'p) at constant proton energy E_p = 90 MeV in center-of-mass system (parallel kinematics) and at Q^2 = 0.8 (GeV/c)^2 in constant (q,omega) kinematics.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic DWIA approach previously applied to (e,e'p) and (gamma,p) reactions by Meucci et al. (2001) and by Johansson et al. (1998) to include meson exchange currents, specifically the seagull diagram. It contrasts with nonrelativistic calculations by Benenti et al. (1997) and Co et al. (1999), which found larger MEC effects and required MEC to reproduce (gamma,p) data. The results suggest that for (gamma,n) reactions, more complicated mechanisms beyond the seagull current (such as rescattering processes or the pion-in-flight and Delta-isobar currents) are needed to match experimental data, and that the full set of MEC contributions should be included in future relativistic calculations.
