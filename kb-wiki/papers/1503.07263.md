# Gluon Transport Equations with Condensate in the Small Angle Approximation

> We derive the set of kinetic equations that control the evolution of gluons in the presence of a condensate. We show that the dominant singularities remain logarithmic when the scattering involves particles in the condensate. This allows us to define a consistent small angle approximation.

## Key claim

The paper derives kinetic equations for gluon evolution in the presence of a Bose condensate, showing that the dominant singularities in the collision kernel remain logarithmic (not linear) when scattering involves condensate particles, which allows a consistent small-angle (leading-log) approximation to be defined.

## Method

The authors start from the Boltzmann-Nordheim equation for gluon-gluon elastic scattering with a matrix element regulated by a Debye screening mass \(m_D\) and a gluon mass \(m\). They insert a two-component distribution function \(f(\p) = n_c (2\pi)^3 \delta^{(3)}(\p) + g(\p)\) into the collision integral, separate the five distinct contributions (one with no condensate particles, four with one condensate particle), and expand each contribution in the small scattering angle limit. They isolate singular integrals over the small energy variable \(\epsilon = E - m\) near zero momentum, using the assumed small-\(p\) behavior \(g(p) \simeq T^*/\epsilon + V^*\), and then combine all contributions to cancel linear divergences \(\sim 1/m_r\), retaining only logarithmic divergences \(\sim \ln(\Lambda/m_r)\).

## Key numbers

* The matrix element squared for gluon-gluon scattering is \(\mid {\cal M} \mid^2 \approx -\kappa [ s u/(t - m_D^2)^2 + t s/(u - m_D^2)^2 ]\) with \(\kappa \equiv 128\pi^2 \alpha_s^2 N_c^2\).
* The leading-log coefficient for condensate-related scattering is \(L_c \equiv (2\kappa / 32\pi) \ln(\Lambda / m_r)\) where \(m_r \equiv m_D^2 / (2m)\).
* The constant factor in the regular-part current at small momentum is \(\kappa_0 = 36\pi \alpha_s^2 \mathcal{L}\).
* The singular integrals yield linear terms \(\sim 1/m_r\) and logarithmic terms \(\sim \ln(\Lambda/m_r)\); the linear terms cancel exactly when \(C_1\) is combined with \(C_3^t + C_4^u\) and \(C_2\) with \(C_3^u + C_4^t\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of gluon evolution using the Boltzmann-Nordheim equation (Blaizot 2011, Blaizot 2013), where Bose condensation was predicted for dense gluon systems in heavy-ion collisions. Prior treatments ignored condensate-dependent terms in the scattering kernel; this paper includes those terms to describe evolution beyond the onset of condensation. The resulting coupled equations for the regular distribution \(g(p)\) and condensate density \(n_c\) enable future numerical studies of condensate growth and thermalization, and the authors verify that the equations satisfy particle number and energy conservation.
