# Search for the QCD Critical Point in High Energy Nuclear Collisions: A Status Report

> We review recent results of net-proton multiplicity fluctuations from STAR experiment, aiming to locate the QCD critical point in high-energy nuclear collisions at RHIC. We show net-proton number cumulant and proton number factorial cumulant ratios up to fourth order using experimental data from RHIC BES-II Au+Au collisions in collider mode and fixed-target mode. The comparison is made between experimental data and non-critical model calculations from Lattice QCD, HRG, hydrodynamic simulations and transport model UrQMD. In addition, we discuss initial volume fluctuation effect, which plays significant role in fixed-target energies. Finally, an outlook on experimental research on the QCD critical point in future experiments will be presented.

# Search for the QCD Critical Point in High Energy Nuclear Collisions: A Status Report

## Key claim
The observed maximum deviation of the net-proton cumulant ratio C4/C2 from non-critical baseline calculations at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 GeV, with significance levels of 2 to 5 sigma, is consistent with theoretical expectations for proximity to a QCD critical point, but alternative explanations related to dynamical baryon transport, finite-size effects, and acceptance dependencies cannot be ruled out due to the finite and non-equilibrium nature of heavy-ion collisions.

## Method
The STAR experiment measured event-by-event net-proton multiplicity distributions in Au+Au collisions across sqrt(s_NN) = 3-200 GeV using the Time Projection Chamber and Time-of-Flight detectors for proton identification with purity exceeding 99%. Higher-order cumulants (C1 through C4) and factorial cumulants (kappa1 through kappa4) of net-proton and proton multiplicity distributions were constructed, with centrality bin width correction applied and analytical efficiency corrections assuming binomial detector response. Comparisons were made to non-critical model calculations including Lattice QCD, Hadron Resonance Gas with canonical ensemble, hydrodynamic simulations with excluded-volume corrections, and the UrQMD transport model.

## Key numbers
- Net-proton cumulant ratio C4/C2 shows a maximum deviation from non-critical baselines at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 GeV in 0-5% central Au+Au collisions, with significance levels of 2 to 5 sigma
- Measurements span center-of-mass energies sqrt(s_NN) = 3-200 GeV, corresponding to baryon chemical potential mu_B approximately 25-750 MeV at chemical freeze-out
- BES-II collider-mode data were taken at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 9.2, 11.5, 14.6, 17.3, 19.6, and 27 GeV; fixed-target data at sqrt(s_NN) = 3.0, 3.2, 3.5, and 3.9 GeV
- Proton identification purity exceeds 99% using combined TPC and TOF detector information
- Transverse momentum range for all measurements is 0.4 < pT < 2.0 GeV/c
- Rapidity window for collider data is |y| < 0.5; for fixed-target data is -0.5 < y < 0 in the center-of-mass frame
- BES-II reference multiplicity distributions use extended pseudo-rapidity acceptance |eta| < 1.6, compared to |eta| < 1.0 in BES-I

## Context
This review extends the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC (STAR Collaboration, 2010-2021) by presenting new results from BES-II and fixed-target data down to sqrt(s_NN) = 3 GeV, building on earlier cumulant measurements from BES-I (STAR, 2020-2022). The observed non-monotonic energy dependence of C4/C2 follows the critical point signature predicted by Stephanov (2011). The work enables future searches at the Compressed Baryonic Matter experiment at FAIR and the Multi-Purpose Detector at NICA (both expected around 2028), as well as the High Intensity Heavy-ion Facility under construction in China. A new centrality-independent method to suppress initial volume fluctuation effects (Wang, 2025) is presented as essential for interpreting low-energy fixed-target data where volume fluctuations are significant.
