# First Evidence of Axial Shape Asymmetry and Configuration Coexistence in $^{74}$Zn: Suggestion for a Northern Extension of the $N=40$ Island of Inversion

> The excited states of $N=44$ $^{74}$Zn were investigated via $γ$-ray spectroscopy following $^{74}$Cu $β$ decay. By exploiting $γ$-$γ$ angular correlation analysis, the $2_2^+$, $3_1^+$, $0_2^+$ and $2_3^+$ states in $^{74}$Zn were firmly established. The $γ$-ray branching and $E2/M1$ mixing ratios for transitions de-exciting the $2_2^+$, $3_1^+$ and $2_3^+$ states were measured, allowing for the extraction of relative $B(E2)$ values. In particular, the $2_3^+ \to 0_2^+$ and $2_3^+ \to 4_1^+$ transitions were observed for the first time. The results show excellent agreement with new microscopic large-scale shell-model calculations, and are discussed in terms of underlying shapes, as well as the role of neutron excitations across the $N=40$ gap. Enhanced axial shape asymmetry (triaxiality) is suggested to characterize $^{74}$Zn in its ground state. Furthermore, an excited $K=0$ band with a significantly larger softness in its shape is identified. A shore of the $N=40$ ``island of inversion'' appears to manifest above $Z=26$, previously thought as its northern limit in the chart of the nuclides.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that the $N=40$ island of inversion extends north of $Z=26$ (iron) into zinc ($Z=30$), based on evidence that the ground state of $^{74}$Zn involves more neutron excitations across the $N=40$ shell gap than the $0_2^+$ state, contrary to the previous assumption that the island's northern limit was at $Z=26$.

## Method

Excited states of $^{74}$Zn were populated via $\beta$ decay of $^{74}$Cu produced at the TRIUMF-ISAC1 facility, with $\gamma$-ray spectroscopy performed using the GRIFFIN spectrometer equipped with 12 Compton-suppressed HPGe clover detectors. $\gamma$-$\gamma$ angular correlation analysis was used to firmly assign spins of the $2_2^+$, $3_1^+$, $0_2^+$, and $2_3^+$ states, and branching ratios and $E2/M1$ mixing ratios were measured to extract relative $B(E2)$ values. Large-scale shell-model calculations using the LNPS effective interaction in a valence space comprising the $pf$ shell for protons and the $1p_{3/2}0f_{5/2}1p_{1/2}0g_{9/2}1d_{5/2}$ orbitals for neutrons were performed and compared with experimental results.

## Key numbers

- The $2_2^+$ state at 1670.07(20) keV decays to the $0_1^+$ state with a relative $B(E2)$ of 9.24(19) (normalized to 100 for the $2_2^+ \to 2_1^+$ transition).
- The $3_1^+$ state at 2099 keV has a mixing ratio $\delta(E2/M1) = -0.57^{+0.06}_{-0.07}$ for the $3_1^+ \to 2_1^+$ transition, with a second solution of $-2.7(5)$.
- The $2_3^+ \to 0_2^+$ transition at 359.2(6) keV has a relative $B(E2)$ of 100(20) (normalized to 100 for that transition).
- From shell-model calculations, $\beta = 0.23$ for the ground-state band and the $\gamma$ band, and $\beta = 0.20$ for the band built on the $0_2^+$ state.
- From Kumar quadrupole sum rules: $\langle \beta \rangle = 0.24$ and $\langle \gamma \rangle = 24^\circ$ for the ground state; $\langle \beta \rangle = 0.22$ and $\langle \gamma \rangle = 20^\circ$ for the $0_2^+$ state.
- Fluctuations: $\sigma_{\beta} (0_1^+) = \pm 0.04$, $\sigma_{\gamma} (0_1^+) = (+11^\circ, -13^\circ)$; $\sigma_{\beta} (0_2^+) = \pm 0.04$, $\sigma_{\gamma} (0_2^+) = (+12^\circ, -20^\circ)$.
- The $B(E2;0_2^+ \to 2_1^+) = 0.58$ W.u. and $B(E2;2_3^+ \to 0_1^+) = 0.03$ W.u. from shell-model calculations indicate weak mixing between the two bands.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of triaxiality in $^{66,70}$Zn (Rocchini 2021, Calinescu 2021) and in the isotone $^{76}$Ge (Ayangeakaa 2019), where shape invariants pointed to rigid triaxial character. The identification of a coexisting $K=0$ band in $^{74}$Zn bridges the gap between the Ge and Ni isotopes, where such structures are well established. The finding that the $N=40$ island of inversion extends above $Z=28$ contradicts the previous assumption that its northern limit was at $Z=26$ in Fe nuclei (Rother 2011), and enables future direct reaction studies and measurements of absolute quadrupole and monopole transition strengths in this mass region.
