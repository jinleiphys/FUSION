# Baryon Spin and Emergent Hadron Mass

> This thesis describes the use of Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) to study baryon bound states in QCD. In this work, octet baryon axial form factors are calculated using a symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector $\times$ vector contact interaction (SCI). The baryons are considered as quark-plus-interacting-diquark bound states, whose structure (wave function) is obtained by solving a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation. Since it preserves symmetries, all consequences of partial conservation of the axial current are manifest. For instance, one finds that octet baryon axial properties are consistent with only minor violations of SU(3)-flavour symmetry, being interpreted as a dynamical consequence of emergent hadron mass (EHM). Considering neutral axial currents, the SCI delivers predictions for the flavour separation of octet baryon axial charges and, consequently, produces values for the associated SU(3) singlet, triplet, and octet axial charges. The results indicate that, at the hadron scale $ζ_{\mathcal{H}}$, valence degrees of freedom carry approximately $50\%$ of an octet baryon's total spin. Proton structure is one of the principal topics in hadron physics. Its study is expected to reveal key features of both the origin of mass and strong interaction dynamics. This work therefore extended the above analyses to an examination of in-proton parton helicity (spin) distribution functions (DFs). Using Ansätze for hadron-scale proton polarised valence quark DFs, predictions are delivered for all proton polarised DFs at the measurement scale $ζ_{\rm C}^2 = 3\,$GeV$^2$. The pointwise behaviour of the predicted DFs and, consequently, their moments, shows good agreement with results inferred from data. Based on these results, one finds that experimental measurements of the proton flavour-singlet axial charge should return a value $a_0^{\rm E}(ζ_{\rm C}) = 0.35(2)$.

## Key claim

Using a symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector × vector contact interaction (SCI) within a quark-plus-interacting-diquark framework for baryons, the author calculates octet baryon axial form factors and finds that valence degrees of freedom carry approximately 50% of an octet baryon's total spin at the hadron scale ζ_ℋ, with the results interpreted as a dynamical consequence of emergent hadron mass (EHM) rather than large SU(3)-flavour symmetry violations.

## Method

The author solves Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equations for octet baryons (proton, Λ, Σ⁺) treating them as quark-plus-interacting-diquark bound states, using a symmetry-preserving implementation of a vector × vector contact interaction (SCI). Axial form factors are computed from six classes of baryon current diagrams (bystander quark, diquark strike, diquark transition, exchanged quark, and two seagull terms) that guarantee Ward-Green-Takahashi identities. For proton polarized parton distribution functions (DFs), Ansätze for hadron-scale polarized valence quark DFs are evolved to the measurement scale ζ_C² = 3 GeV².

## Key numbers

- At the hadron scale ζ_ℋ, valence degrees of freedom carry approximately 50% of an octet baryon's total spin.
- The predicted proton flavour-singlet axial charge at the measurement scale is a_0^E(ζ_C) = 0.35(2).
- Predictions for all proton polarized DFs are delivered at the measurement scale ζ_C² = 3 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) approach to baryon structure, building on the quark-diquark framework and the SCI methodology. The Faddeev equation kernels are modifications of results from Chen (2012) in the SU(3)-flavour symmetry limit. The diquark current calculations follow the procedure detailed in Chen (2021). The results enable future experimental tests of the proton flavour-singlet axial charge prediction and further exploration of emergent hadron mass (EHM) as the origin of baryon spin structure.
