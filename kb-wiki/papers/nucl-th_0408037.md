# Nonperturbative QCD Phenomenology and Light Quark Physics

> Recent progress in modeling QCD for hadron physics through truncated Dyson-Schwinger equations is reviewed. Special emphasis is put upon comparison of dressed quark propagators and the dressed quark-gluon vertex with lattice-QCD results.

## Key claim

Truncated Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) can reproduce quenched lattice-QCD data for the quark propagator and quark-gluon vertex, but the infrared enhancement required for the vertex in the quark propagator analysis is a factor of 6 larger than what the direct vertex calculation yields, indicating that the non-Abelian term dominates the vertex dressing.

## Method

The author reviews the DSE approach to nonperturbative QCD, where the dressed-quark propagator is solved from a gap equation coupled to a truncated kernel. An effective coupling is constructed from the quenched lattice gluon propagator and an infrared-enhanced vertex factor, with the vertex determined by requiring the DSE to reproduce lattice quark propagator data. For the quark-gluon vertex itself, a nonperturbative model is built from dressed versions of the one-loop Abelian and non-Abelian diagrams, using existing rainbow-ladder DSE kernels without introducing new parameters.

## Key numbers

- The vertex factor v(k^2_s) = 13.0 at an effective infrared scale k^2_s ~ 0.04 GeV^2 for the case considered in relation to lattice data
- The DSE-assisted chiral limit gives the quenched-QCD chiral condensate -⟨q̄q⟩⁰_{1 GeV} = (0.19 GeV)³, a factor of two smaller than the empirical value (0.24 ± 0.01 GeV)³
- The linearly-extrapolated lattice data estimate for the chiral condensate is (0.270 ± 0.027 GeV)³, which is 40% too large compared to the DSE-assisted chiral limit
- The quenched lattice data underestimates the chiral f_π⁰ by 30%
- In the 1⁺⁺ (a₁/f₁) axialvector channel, 1-loop vertex dressing adds 30 MeV of attraction to the ladder kernel result
- In the 1⁺⁻ (b₁/h₁) axialvector channel, vertex dressing produces a repulsive effect of 290 MeV above the ladder kernel result, yielding a value close to the experimental m_{b₁}
- The infrared enhancement λ₁(k²_s) ~ 2.3 at k²_s ~ 0.04 GeV² from the direct vertex calculation, a factor of 6 smaller than what the quark propagator analysis required

## Context

This review extends the DSE modeling program of Maris and Tandy (1999) and Maris (2003) for meson phenomenology by comparing with lattice-QCD results from Bowman (2002) for the quark propagator and Skullerud (2003) for the quark-gluon vertex. The work enables systematic chiral extrapolation of lattice data and provides a framework for going beyond the rainbow-ladder truncation, as demonstrated by the axialvector meson calculations of Watson (2004). The vertex model of Bhagwat (2004) shows that the non-Abelian three-gluon vertex diagram dominates the infrared vertex enhancement, consistent with the color factor amplification by -N_c² over the Abelian term.
