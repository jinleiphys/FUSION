# Theoretical Perspective on Quarkonia from SPS via RHIC to LHC

> The objective of this paper is to assess the current theoretical understanding of the extensive set of quarkonium observables (for both charmonia and bottomonia) that have been attained in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions over two orders of magnitude in center-of-mass energy. We briefly lay out and compare the currently employed theoretical frameworks and their underlying transport coefficients, and then analyze excitation functions of quarkonium yields to characterize the nature of the varying production mechanisms. We argue that an overall coherent picture of suppression and regeneration mechanisms emerges which enables to deduce insights on the properties of the in-medium QCD force from SPS via RHIC to LHC, and forms a basis for future quantitative studies.

## Key claim
The paper argues that a coherent picture of quarkonium suppression and regeneration in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions emerges across a factor of ~300 in center-of-mass energy, but this interpretation depends on the assumption that transport models with in-medium binding energies can reliably disentangle the two mechanisms, with regeneration being the only conceivable explanation for the markedly different behavior of J/psi and Upsilon(2S) yields at LHC energies.

## Method
The authors compare theoretical calculations from transport approaches (specifically the TAMU rate-equation framework and Tsinghua transport approach) that solve a Boltzmann or rate equation for quarkonium phase-space distributions, incorporating in-medium binding energies, gluo-dissociation and quasi-free dissociation mechanisms, and heavy-quark fugacity factors. They analyze excitation functions of the nuclear modification factor R_AA for inclusive J/psi, Upsilon(1S), and Upsilon(2S) production at mid-rapidity across collision energies from SPS (17 GeV) via RHIC (39, 62, 200 GeV) to LHC (2.76 and 5.02 TeV), comparing to data from NA50, PHENIX, STAR, ALICE, and CMS.

## Key numbers
- J/psi R_AA increases from ~0.3 at SPS to ~0.8 at top LHC energy (5.02 TeV), interpreted as strong increase in regeneration
- Upsilon(2S) R_AA at LHC is ~5 times smaller than J/psi R_AA, despite comparable vacuum binding energies
- Upsilon(1S) and Upsilon(2S) R_AA decrease from RHIC to LHC, with regeneration contributing ~0.1 in R_AA for both states
- In minimum-bias Pb-Pb at LHC, regeneration amounts to ~25% of Upsilon(1S) yield and >50% of Upsilon(2S) yield
- Initial temperatures extracted from bulk observables: T0_SPS ~ 240 MeV, T0_RHIC ~ 350 MeV, T0_LHC ~ 550 MeV
- Screening radii estimated: r_scr[SPS] ~ 0.7 fm, r_scr[RHIC] ~ 0.5 fm, r_scr[LHC] ~ 0.25 fm
- Charm-quark relaxation time constraint from regeneration: tau_c < 5 fm, translating to spatial diffusion coefficient D_s(2pi T) < 4-9
- Nuclear absorption cross section for J/psi at 17.3 GeV: sigma_abs ~ 7.5 mb from NA60 pA measurements
- Ratio r_AA = <p_T^2>_AA / <p_T^2>_pp softens from ~1.5 at SPS to ~1 at RHIC to ~0.5 at LHC

## Context
This work extends the transport-model approach to quarkonium production previously developed by the TAMU group (Zhao 2011, Du 2017) and the Tsinghua group (Liu 2010, Zhou 2014), and compares with the statistical hadronization model (Gazdzicki 1999, Andronic 2006, Braun-Munzinger 2009). The paper synthesizes data from SPS (NA50), RHIC (PHENIX, STAR), and LHC (ALICE, CMS) to argue that the in-medium QCD force can be inferred from the melting pattern: confining force remnants survive at SPS (holding J/psi but melting psi(2S)), screening sets in at RHIC (melting J/psi and Upsilon(2S)), and color-Coulomb screening occurs at LHC (strongly suppressing Upsilon(1S)). The results enable future quantitative studies connecting open heavy-flavor diffusion coefficients to quarkonium transport parameters, and motivate measurements of open charm/bottom cross sections at 10% precision to control regeneration predictions at the 20% level.
