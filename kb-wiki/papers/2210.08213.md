# Elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energy: the role of impact parameter, mean field potential, and collision term

> Within the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model, by reverse tracing nucleons that are finally emitted at mid-rapidity (|$y_0$| < 0.1) in the entire reaction process, the time evolution of elliptic flow ($v_2$) of these traced nucleons produced in Au+Au collisions at beam energy of 0.4 GeV$/$nucleon with different impact parameters ($b$) is studied. The initial value of $v_2$ is positive and increases with $b$, then it decreases as time passes and tends to saturate at a negative value. It is found that nucleon-nucleon collisions always depress the value of $v_2$ (enhance the out-of-plane emission), while the nuclear mean field potential may slightly raise the value of $v_2$ during the expansion stage in peripheral reactions. The related density mostly probed by $v_2$ of nucleons at mid-rapidity is found to be $\sim$ 60% of the maximum density reached during the collisions.

## Key claim

The paper quantitatively disentangles the contributions of the nuclear mean field potential and nucleon-nucleon collisions to the elliptic flow of nucleons emitted at mid-rapidity in Au+Au collisions at 0.4 GeV per nucleon, finding that the mean field potential dominates the early evolution while collisions dominate later, and that the density most probed by this elliptic flow is only about 60% of the maximum density reached during the collision.

## Method

Using the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model, the authors simulated Au+Au collisions at 0.4 GeV per nucleon for impact parameters b = 2, 4, 6, 8, and 10 fm. They reverse-traced free nucleons finally emitted at mid-rapidity (|y0| < 0.1) and recorded the change in their momenta due to the mean field potential and nucleon-nucleon scattering separately at each time step (1 fm/c), allowing calculation of the time-dependent contributions Δv2^mf(t) and Δv2^coll(t) to the elliptic flow.

## Key numbers

- The average number of traced free nucleons per event ranges from 27.5 (b = 2 fm) to 4.2 (b = 10 fm) for Au+Au at 0.4 GeV per nucleon.
- The saturation time tsat (when |dΔv2/dt| < 0.0001 c/fm) increases from 26 fm/c (b = 4 fm) to 45 fm/c (b = 10 fm).
- The Δv2-weighted average density ⟨ρ⟩_{v2} is 0.206 fm^{-3} for b = 4 fm and 0.145 fm^{-3} for b = 10 fm.
- The density most probed by v2 of nucleons at mid-rapidity is (0.9 to 1.3)ρ0, which is about 60% of the maximum density reached during the collisions.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of elliptic flow formation in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions, building on transport model studies such as Le et al. (2016) which used force-weighted densities, and Russotto et al. (2016) which analyzed elliptic flow sensitivity to the density-dependent symmetry energy. The quantitative decomposition of mean field and collision contributions enables more precise constraints on the nuclear equation of state and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections from experimental elliptic flow data, and the finding that v2 probes densities lower than the maximum density informs the interpretation of symmetry energy studies using elliptic flow ratios.
