# Synthesis of superheavy elements beyond Z=118

> We investigate the cold fission/fusion paths of superheavy nuclei within the two center shell model, in order to find the best projectile-target combinations of their production. The fission/fusion yields are estimated by using the semiclassical approach. We predict several asymmetric combinations of relative long living fragments, which can be used in fusion experiments of superheavy nuclei with Z>118.

## Key claim

The authors predict several asymmetric projectile-target combinations for synthesizing superheavy elements with Z>118, specifically for the hypothetical compound nucleus 300120X, by identifying cold fission valleys where the penetrability ratio is comparable to or larger than that of experimentally successful reactions, but they note that many candidate combinations involve fragments with short half-lives.

## Method

The authors used the Super Asymmetric Two Center Shell Model with a Woods-Saxon potential to compute the potential energy surface for binary combinations of the hypothetical compound nucleus 300120X. They calculated the driving potential as the sum of nuclear and Coulomb inter-fragment potentials minus the Q-value, then estimated penetrabilities using the semiclassical WKB approximation. The macroscopic energy was obtained from the Yukawa-plus-exponential model extended to binary systems, with Strutinsky shell corrections and pairing corrections included.

## Key numbers

- For the hypothetical compound nucleus 300120X, the penetrability ratio R = P(A2)/P(alpha) for 48Ca + 252Fm is 2.1 × 10^(-26)
- The penetrability ratio increases by nine orders of magnitude from 72Zn (R = 3.7 × 10^(-20)) to 74Zn (R = 5.7 × 10^(-11))
- The combination 76Ge + 224Ra has R = 8.1 × 10^(-11), with 76Ge having a half-life of 10^(25) years and 224Ra having a half-life of 3.7 days
- For comparison, the experimentally measured reaction 208Pb + 70Zn → 278112X has R = 5.0 × 10^(-11)
- The experimentally measured reaction 48Ca + 244Cm → 292116X has R = 1.7 × 10^(-24)
- The combination 98Sr + 202Pb has the highest penetrability ratio in the table at R = 1.1 × 10^(-2)

## Context

This work extends the fragmentation theory approach of Sandulescu (1976) and the Pb potential valley concept of Gupta (1977) to predict new projectile-target combinations for synthesizing elements beyond Z=118, building on the success of 48Ca-induced reactions that produced elements up to Z=118 (Oganessian 2004). The authors use a more sophisticated microscopic approach than their earlier work (Delion 2007), incorporating a Woods-Saxon two-center shell model with fragment deformations, and their results suggest that combinations near the Pb valley (Sr+Pb) may be promising for future fusion experiments.
