# Higgs Mechanism with Type-II Nambu-Goldstone Bosons at Finite Chemical Potential

> When the spontaneous symmetry breaking occurs for systems without Lorentz covariance, there arises possible mismatch, $N_{\rm NG} < N_{\rm BG}$, between numbers of Nambu-Goldstone (NG) bosons ($N_{\rm NG}$) and the numbers of broken generators ($N_{\rm BG}$. In such a situation, so-called type-II NG bosons emerge. We study how the gauge bosons acquire masses through the Higgs mechanism under this mismatch by employing gauge theories with complex scalar field at finite chemical potential and by enforcing "charge" neutrality. To separate the physical spectra from unphysical ones, the $R_ξ$ gauge is adopted. Not only massless NG bosons but also massive scalar bosons generated by the chemical potential are absorbed into spatial components of the gauge bosons. Although the chemical potential induces a non-trivial mixings among the scalar bosons and temporal components of the gauge bosons, it does not affect the structure of the physical spectra, so that the total number of physical modes is not modified even for $N_{\rm NG} < N_{\rm BG}$.

## Key claim

The Higgs mechanism remains consistent even when the number of Nambu-Goldstone (NG) bosons is smaller than the number of broken generators due to the presence of type-II NG bosons at finite chemical potential; the total number of physical degrees of freedom is conserved despite non-trivial mixings induced by the chemical potential among scalar bosons and temporal components of gauge bosons.

## Method

The authors study gauge theories with complex scalar fields at finite chemical potential, enforcing "charge" neutrality via non-Abelian external sources. They adopt the $R_{\xi}$ gauge to separate physical from unphysical spectra, and analyze the mass spectra at tree level for gauged SU(2), U(2) (Glashow-Weinberg-Salam type), and SU(3) models, taking the gauge parameter to infinity at the end.

## Key numbers

- In the gauged SU(2) model, the gauge bosons acquire a mass $M = \frac{g}{2} \sqrt{(\mu^2 + m^2)/\lambda}$, where $g$ is the gauge coupling, $\mu$ the chemical potential, $m$ the scalar mass parameter, and $\lambda$ the quartic coupling.
- The Higgs scalar $\psi'$ retains a mass $\sqrt{6\mu^2 + 2m^2}$, unchanged by the chemical potential-induced mixing.
- In the U(2) model without gauge coupling, the type-II NG boson has dispersion $E \propto p^2$ at low momentum, while the type-I NG boson has $E \propto p$; the Nielsen-Chadha relation holds as $N_{\rm I} + 2 \times N_{\rm II} = 1 + 2 \times 1 = N_{\rm BG} = 3$.
- The total physical degrees of freedom in the gauged SU(2) model is 10, conserved regardless of the Higgs mechanism at finite $\mu$.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the Higgs mechanism to systems with type-II NG bosons, which arise when Lorentz covariance is broken by a chemical potential, as in the Heisenberg ferromagnet (Nielsen and Chadha, 1975) and kaon condensation in the color-flavor-locked phase of dense QCD (Miransky and collaborators, 2001). The authors build on the U(2) model of Miransky and collaborators and contrast their approach with that of Gusynin and collaborators (2003), who did not impose non-Abelian charge neutrality. The results enable further study of the Higgs mechanism in nonrelativistic systems such as the Heisenberg ferromagnet and in toy models for two-flavor color superconductivity in dense QCD.
