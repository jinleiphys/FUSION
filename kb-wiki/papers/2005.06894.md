# Very heavy flavored dibaryons

> We explore the possibility of very heavy dibaryons with three charm quarks and three beauty quarks, $bbbccc$, using a constituent model which should drive to the correct solution in the limit of hadrons made of heavy quarks. The six-body problem is treated rigorously, in particular taking into account the orbital, color and spin mixed-symmetry components of the wave function. Unlike a recent claim based on lattice QCD, no bound state is found below the lowest dissociation threshold.

## Key claim

Contrary to a recent lattice QCD claim by Junnarkar and Mathur (2019), no bound state of the $bbbccc$ hexaquark exists below the lowest dissociation threshold when the six-body problem is treated rigorously with full antisymmetrization and inclusion of mixed-symmetry orbital, color, and spin components.

## Method

The authors performed a variational six-body calculation using a standard constituent quark model with pairwise color-octet exchange interactions, including a chromoelectric term (linear-plus-Coulomb) and a chromomagnetic spin-spin term with Gaussian smearing. The wave function was constructed in a $(bbb)-(ccc)$ cluster basis with full antisymmetrization imposed separately on the three identical $b$ quarks and three identical $c$ quarks, systematically including color-singlet and color-octet clusters, mixed-symmetry spin states, and mixed-symmetry orbital components for total spins $S=0$ through $S=3$.

## Key numbers

- The lowest dissociation threshold $\Omega_{bbb}(3/2^+) + \Omega_{ccc}(3/2^+)$ has a mass of 19.082 GeV.
- The $bbb$ baryon mass is 14.253 GeV (spin 3/2); the $ccc$ baryon mass is 4.829 GeV (spin 3/2).
- The $bbc$ baryon masses are 11.162 GeV (spin 3/2) and 11.137 GeV (spin 1/2); the $ccb$ baryon masses are 8.023 GeV (spin 3/2) and 7.972 GeV (spin 1/2).
- The $S=3$ state with mixed-symmetry color and orbital components has an energy of 19.869 GeV, compared to 19.098 GeV for the lowest component.
- The $S=1$ state with mixed-symmetry orbital-color-spin components has an energy of 19.838 GeV, compared to 19.098 GeV for the lowest component.
- In a toy model with non-identical $b$-type and $c$-type quarks (relaxing antisymmetrization), a binding of about 100 MeV is obtained.

## Context

This work directly contradicts the lattice QCD prediction of Junnarkar and Mathur (2019), who reported deeply bound dibaryons for $(ccb)(cbb)$ and similar heavy-flavor configurations. The result extends the long history of stable multiquark predictions that fail under full calculation, such as Jaffe's $H$ dibaryon (1976) which did not survive breaking of flavor SU(3) or dilution of chromomagnetic terms. The paper enables future studies to focus on the role of string confinement versus pairwise additive potentials, and highlights that antisymmetrization constraints are the critical factor preventing binding in the $bbbccc$ system.
