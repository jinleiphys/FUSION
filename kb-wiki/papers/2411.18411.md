# Re examination of \b{eta} decay in Hg, Pb and Po Isotopes

> This study re examines the effect of nuclear deformation on the calculated Gamow Teller (GT) strength distributions of neutron deficient (178 192Hg, 185 194Pb and 196 206Po) nuclei. The nuclear ground state properties and shape parameters were calculated using the Relativistic Mean Field model. Three different density dependent interactions were used in the calculation. Estimated shape parameters were later used within the framework of deformed proton-neutron quasi random phase approximations model, with a separable interaction, to calculate the GT strength distributions, half lives and branching ratios for these neutron deficient isotopes. It was concluded that half lives and GT strength distributions vary considerably with change in shape parameter.

## Key claim

The study re-examines the effect of nuclear deformation on calculated Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions and beta-decay half-lives for neutron-deficient Hg, Pb, and Po isotopes, concluding that half-lives and GT strength distributions vary considerably with changes in the shape parameter, but that the specific deformation parameter set used significantly affects the agreement with experimental data.

## Method

The authors first calculated ground state properties and deformation parameters (beta_2) for 178-192Hg, 185-194Pb, and 196-206Po using the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model with three density-dependent functionals (D3C, DD-ME2, and DD-PC1). These deformation parameters, along with a second set from the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) and a third set from Skyrme HF+BCS+QRPA for even-even nuclei, were then used as input to the deformed proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model with a separable interaction to calculate GT strength distributions, branching ratios, and beta-decay half-lives.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies per nucleon calculated with the D3C functional showed a maximum deviation of around 0.15 MeV from experimental data for isotopic chains of Hg, Pb, and Po.
- For 206Po, the beta_2 values were -0.04651 (D3C), -0.04562 (DD-PC1), -0.04521 (DD-ME2), and -0.01481 (FRDM); the ratios of calculated to measured half-lives were 10.10, 0.89, 0.19, and 1.01 respectively.
- The D3C interaction for 206Po resulted in a total GT strength of 0.0006 and centroid of 1.7 MeV, whereas DD-PC1 gave 0.0049 and 1.39 MeV, producing a factor of 55 difference in half-life between the two interactions.
- For 185Pb, all predicted beta-decay half-life values were around a factor of 70 larger than the measured value.
- The FRDM calculated beta_2 values for 186Pb and 188Pb were almost fifteen times smaller than experimental results.
- For 190Hg, up to two orders of magnitude difference in total GT strength was found between FRDM and other model calculations.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Morelonne et al. (2006), who used a deformed Skyrme HF+BCS+QRPA approach and found that only GT strength distributions altered significantly with beta_2 values, concluding that neither half-lives nor summed strengths were good observables for deformation effects. The present study re-examines those findings using multiple deformation parameter sets from RMF calculations, FRDM (Moller 2016), and Skyrme HF+BCS+QRPA, finding that half-lives do vary considerably with deformation. The results enable more refined predictions of beta-decay properties for neutron-deficient nuclei relevant to shape coexistence studies, particularly for the Hg, Pb, and Po isotopic chains where shape coexistence has been experimentally observed in work by Marsh et al. (2018), Ojala et al. (2022), and Kesteloot et al. (2015).
