# K*-couplings for the antidecuplet excitation

> We estimate the coupling of the K* vector meson to the N-->Theta+ transition employing unitary symmetry, vector meson dominance, and results from the GRAAL Collaboration for eta photoproduction off the neutron. Our small numerical value for the coupling constant is consistent with the non-observation of the Theta+ in recent CLAS searches for its photoproduction. We also estimate the K*-coupling for the N-->Sigma* excitation, with Sigma* being the Sigma-like antidecuplet partner of the Theta+-baryon.

## Key claim

The coupling constants for the \(K^*\) vector meson to the \(N \to \Theta^+\) and \(N \to \Sigma^*\) transitions are small, which is consistent with the null results for \(\Theta^+\) photoproduction from the CLAS Collaboration, but the authors note that the CLAS analysis procedure might not be sensitive enough to reveal the \(\Theta^+\) if it exists, given the rough theoretical estimates and various uncertainties.

## Method

The authors used unitary \(SU(3)_F\) symmetry to relate the tensor coupling constants for the \(N \to \Theta^+\) and \(N \to \Sigma^*\) transitions to the \(\rho^0 N N^*\) coupling, where \(N^*\) is the nucleon-like antidecuplet partner. They then employed vector meson dominance (VMD) to connect the \(\rho^0 N N^*\) tensor coupling to the transition magnetic moment \(\mu(n^* \to n)\), which was extracted from GRAAL Collaboration data on \(\eta\) photoproduction off the neutron. Numerical values for the \(K^*\) couplings were obtained by combining the \(SU(3)_F\) relations with the extracted \(\mu(n^* \to n)\) range and the known \(\rho^0\) meson-photon coupling \(g_V\).

## Key numbers

- Transition magnetic moment: \(|\mu(n^* \to n)| = (0.13 - 0.37)\,\mu_N\), where \(\mu_N = e/(2m_N)\) is the nuclear magneton.
- Tensor coupling for \(\rho^0 N N^*\): \(|f_2(\rho^0\,n\,n^\ast)| = |f_2(\rho^0\,p\,p^\ast)| \approx (0.45 - 1.28)\).
- Tensor coupling for \(K^* N \Theta^+\): \(|f_2(K^{\ast 0}\,p\,\Theta^+)| = |f_2(K^{\ast +}\,n\,\Theta^+)| = (1.10 - 3.14)\).
- Tensor coupling for \(K^* N \Sigma^*\): \(|f_2(K^{\ast -}\,p\,\Sigma^{\ast 0})| = (0.45 - 1.28)\) and \(|f_2({\overline K}^{\ast 0}\,p\,\Sigma^{\ast +})| = (0.64 - 1.81)\).
- Predicted total cross sections for \(\Theta^+\) photoproduction, using the lower bound of the coupling (1.10), are \(\sigma_{tot}(\gamma p \to \overline{K}^0 \Theta^+) < 0.22\) nb and \(\sigma_{tot}(\gamma n \to K^- \Theta^+) < 1\) nb, assuming a \(\Theta^+\) width of 1 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for \(\Theta^+\) photoproduction by providing the first estimates of the \(K^* N \Theta\) tensor coupling, which had been a free parameter in earlier models such as those by Titov (2005) and Liu (2003). The results support the tensor-dominance approach used by Polyakov (2004) and Kwee (2005), in contrast to the vector-coupling assumption in other calculations. The small coupling values are consistent with the null results from the CLAS Collaboration (2005, 2006) and suggest that future searches for the \(\Theta^+\) may require higher sensitivity or different kinematic regions.
