# Jet-Tagged Back-Scattering Photons For Quark Gluon Plasma Tomography

> Several sources of direct photons are known to contribute to the total photon yield in high energy nuclear collisions. All of these photons carry characteristic and important information on the initial nuclei or the hot and dense fireball created in the collision. We investigate the possibility to separate photons from back-scattering of high momentum quarks off quark gluon plasma from other sources. Their unique kinematics can be utilized through high energy jet triggers on the away-side. We discuss the basic idea and estimate the feasibility of such a measurement at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim
Jet-triggered back-scattering photons from fast partons interacting with quark-gluon plasma can be separated from other direct photon sources by using high-energy jet triggers on the away-side, with the signal producing a characteristic enhancement in nuclear modification factors just below the trigger jet energies.

## Method
The authors used the JETPHOX code to calculate jet-photon and jet-hadron yields at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) for the underlying hard process and background from initial hard and fragmentation photons. They employed the PPM code to propagate leading jet partons of the away-side jet inside a longitudinally boost-invariant fireball with L-squared-dependent energy loss (sLPM model) and converted them to photons using the Compton back-scattering and annihilation rate formula. Trigger jets were fixed in windows around midrapidity, and photon spectra were calculated at midrapidity in 30-degree wide sectors around the away-side.

## Key numbers
- For central Au+Au collisions at RHIC (sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV) with a jet trigger window of E_T = 30-35 GeV, back-scattering photons with energy loss show a distinct shoulder toward smaller momenta compared to the trigger window.
- For central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC (sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV) with a jet trigger window of E_T = 100-105 GeV, the same characteristic signal is observed.
- The back-scattering rate is parametrically proportional to T^2 ln(1/T), making it sensitive to medium temperature.
- Back-scattering photons shine particularly bright in the intermediate P_T range around 4 GeV/c.
- Fragmentation photons are concentrated at low z (less than or approximately 0.3), where z = E_gamma / E_parent jet.

## Context
This work extends prior calculations of jet-medium photons by Fries (2002) and Fries (2005) which estimated the rate of Compton back-scattering and annihilation photons from jets interacting with the medium. It addresses the difficulty noted in earlier work by Turbide (2007) that single inclusive photon spectra and azimuthal asymmetry v2 measurements have been insufficient to quantitatively isolate jet-medium photons. The proposed jet-trigger technique enables future experimental separation of back-scattering photons at RHIC and LHC, with the peak height reflecting a quadratic temperature dependence and the peak shift sensitive to parton energy loss.
