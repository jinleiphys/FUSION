# JLab spectral functions of argon in NuWro and their implications for MicroBooNE

> The Short-Baseline Neutrino program in Fermilab aims to resolve the nature of the low-energy excess events observed in LSND and MiniBooNE, and analyze with unprecedented precision neutrino interactions with argon. These studies require reliable estimate of neutrino cross sections, in particular for charged current quasielastic scattering (CCQE). Here, we report updates of the NuWro Monte Carlo generator that, most notably, bring the state-of-the-art spectral functions to model the ground state properties of the argon nucleus, and improve the accuracy of the cross sections at low energies by accounting for the effects of the nuclear Coulomb potential. We discuss these developments in the context of electron and neutrino interactions, by comparing updated NuWro predictions to experimental data from Jefferson Laboratory Hall A and MicroBooNE. The MicroBooNE CCQE data are described with the $χ^2$ per degree of freedom of 0.7, compared with 1.0 in the local Fermi gas model. The largest improvement is observed for the angular distributions of the produced protons, where the $χ^2$ reduces nearly by half. Being obtained using the axial form factor parametrization from MINERvA, our results indicate a~consistency between the CCQE measurements in MINERvA and MicroBooNE.

## Key claim

The updated NuWro Monte Carlo generator, implementing state-of-the-art spectral functions for argon and accounting for Coulomb effects, describes MicroBooNE CCQE-dominated data with a chi-squared per degree of freedom of 0.7, compared with 1.0 for the local Fermi gas model, indicating consistency between CCQE measurements in MINERvA and MicroBooNE when using the MINERvA axial form factor parametrization.

## Method

The authors implemented proton and neutron spectral functions for argon, recently determined from coincidence electron-scattering experiments at Jefferson Laboratory Hall A, into the NuWro Monte Carlo generator. They also incorporated corrections for the nuclear Coulomb potential using the effective momentum approximation (EMA') and accounted for nuclear recoil energy. The updated predictions were compared to inclusive electron-scattering data from JLab and to MicroBooNE CCQE-dominated (CC1p0pi) differential cross sections for muon production angle, proton production angle, muon momentum, and proton momentum.

## Key numbers

- MicroBooNE CCQE-dominated data described with chi-squared per degree of freedom of 0.7 (0.68 combined, 18.4/27) using spectral functions, compared with 1.0 (0.96 combined, 26.0/27) using the local Fermi gas model
- Largest improvement observed for proton angular distributions: chi-squared per degree of freedom of 0.66 (4.6/7) for spectral functions versus 1.27 (8.9/7) for local Fermi gas
- Coulomb potential values for argon: V_max = 9.6 MeV and average V_C = 7.3 MeV
- For 200-MeV muon neutrinos, Coulomb effects increase differential CCQE cross section dsigma/dE_mu by 5%-15%; at 600 MeV, increase is 1%-3%
- Nuclear recoil decreases differential CCQE cross section dsigma/dE_mu by 0.8%-0.9% at 200 MeV and 0.2% at 600 MeV
- Shift between proton spectral function of titanium and neutron spectral function of argon estimated as S = -1.6 MeV
- Average Fermi momentum for Pauli blocking: p_bar_F = 220 MeV
- CCQE interactions comprise 95.0% of signal CC1p0pi events in spectral function approach (versus ~96% in GENIE)
- Electron-scattering quasielastic peak position shifted by about 30 MeV compared to JLab data

## Context

This work extends the approach of Franco-Patino et al. (2021), who showed that Fermi gas and independent-particle shell model predictions overestimate MicroBooNE data, and Butkevich (2021), who calculated CC1p0pi cross sections within relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) with chi-squared per degree of freedom of 1.12-1.36. The updated NuWro generator now incorporates the state-of-the-art argon spectral functions from JLab experiments E12-14-012 (Dai et al., 2018; Jefferson Lab Hall A, 2022), replacing the previous oversimplified estimate. The results enable improved neutrino interaction simulations for the Short-Baseline Neutrino program at Fermilab, particularly for low-energy excess searches, and call for an updated analysis of the full MicroBooNE dataset to further constrain the axial form factor and nuclear model.
