# Structure and composition of inner crust of neutron stars from Gogny interactions

> The detailed knowledge of the inner crust properties of neutron stars might be important to explain different phenomena such as pulsar glitches or the possibility of an {\it r-process} site in neutron star mergers. It has been shown in the literature that quantal effects like shell correction or pairing may play a relevant role to determine the composition of the inner crust of the neutron star. In this paper we construct the equation of state of the inner crust using the finite-range Gogny interactions, where the mean field and the pairing field are calculated with same interaction. We have used the semiclassical Variational Wigner-Kirkwood method along with shell and pairing corrections calculated with the Strutinsky integral method and the BCS approximation, respectively. Our results are compared with those of some popular models from the literature. We report a unified equation of state of the inner crust and core computed with the D1M* Gogny force, which was specifically fabricated for astrophysical calculations.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a unified equation of state for the inner crust of neutron stars using finite-range Gogny interactions, where the mean field and pairing field are calculated with the same interaction, but the results are obtained within a restricted variational approach using trial Fermi-type densities rather than a fully self-consistent solution, and the authors note that the semiclassical energy per particle varies very slowly with proton number, making the determination of the minimal energy configuration a delicate numerical problem.

## Method

The authors use the Variational Wigner-Kirkwood (VWK) semiclassical method up to \(\hbar^2\) order with trial neutron and proton densities of Fermi type in spherical Wigner-Seitz cells, then add proton shell corrections via the Strutinsky integral method and proton pairing correlations in the BCS approximation using the same Gogny interaction. For each average density from 0.0003 fm\(^{-3}\) to 0.08 fm\(^{-3}\), they search for the optimal proton number \(Z\) and neutron number \(N\) that satisfy \(\beta\)-equilibrium (\(\mu_n = \mu_p + \mu_e\)) and minimize the energy per particle, scanning even proton numbers from \(Z=14\) to \(Z=100\).

## Key numbers

* The inner crust region spans average densities from approximately 0.0003 fm\(^{-3}\) (neutron drip) to about 0.08 fm\(^{-3}\) (transition to homogeneous core).
* For the D1M* force at average density \(\rho_{\rm av}=0.07\) fm\(^{-3}\), the global minimum energy configuration occurs at proton number \(Z=40\).
* At \(\rho_{\rm av}=0.0006\) fm\(^{-3}\), conventional magic numbers \(Z=20, 28, 40, 50\) appear, with a hint of a local minimum at \(Z=58\).
* At \(\rho_{\rm av}=0.00891\) fm\(^{-3}\), the appearance of magic numbers like \(Z=28\) or 50 becomes blurred.
* At \(\rho_{\rm av}=0.0475\) fm\(^{-3}\), magic numbers \(Z=28\) and 50 are completely washed away.
* For average densities up to approximately 0.08 fm\(^{-3}\), the global minimum appears at \(Z=40\); at the highest density the minimum shifts to \(Z=92\).
* The D1M* force predicts a maximum neutron star mass of about \(2M_{\odot}\), consistent with observational data from Demorest (2010), Antoniadis (2013), and Cromartie (2019).
* The D1M interaction reproduces more than 2000 experimental masses with a root-mean-square deviation of 798 keV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies using modified D1M Gogny forces (Gonzalez 2017, 2018; Vinas 2018a) that were mainly applied to the neutron star core, providing for the first time a unified equation of state of the inner crust and core computed with the same Gogny interaction. The approach follows the microscopic-macroscopic method pioneered by the Brussels-Montreal group (Pearson 2012, Onsi 2008, Fantina 2013) using Skyrme forces, but with the advantage that Gogny forces describe both mean-field and pairing with the same interaction. The results enable more realistic calculations of neutron star properties such as mass-radius relationships, moment of inertia, and tidal deformability using a consistent crust-core equation of state rather than the polytropic approximation (\(P = a + b\epsilon^{4/3}\)) used previously.
