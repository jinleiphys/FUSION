# Potential analysis of holographic Schwinger effect in the magnetized background

> We study the holographic Schwinger effect with magnetic field at RHIC and LHC energies by using the AdS/CFT correspondence. We consider both weak and strong magnetic field cases with $B\ll T^2$ and $B\gg T^2$ solutions respectively. Firstly, we calculate separating length of the particle pairs at finite magnetic field. It is found that for both weak and strong magnetic field solutions the maximum value of separating length decreases with the increase of magnetic field , which can be inferred that the virtual electron-positron pairs become real particles more easily. We also find that the magnetic field reduces the potential barrier and the critical field for the weak magnetic field solution, thus favors the Schwinger effect. With strong magnetic field solution, the magnetic field enhances the Schwinger effect when the pairs are in perpendicular to the magnetic field although the magnetic field increases the critical electric field.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for weak magnetic fields (B << T^2), the magnetic field reduces both the potential barrier and the critical electric field, thus favoring the Schwinger effect; for strong magnetic fields (B >> T^2), the magnetic field enhances the Schwinger effect when the particle pairs are perpendicular to the magnetic field, even though the magnetic field increases the critical electric field in that regime.

## Method

The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence with a 5-dimensional Einstein-Maxwell system to model a magnetized background dual to N = 4 super Yang-Mills theory. They perform a potential analysis by calculating the separating length of test particle pairs using a probe D3-brane at a finite radial position, deriving the total potential from the Nambu-Goto action and the critical electric field from the Dirac-Born-Infeld (DBI) action. The analysis is carried out separately for the weak magnetic field regime (B << T^2) using perturbative black hole solutions and for the strong magnetic field regime (B >> T^2) using BTZ x T^2 black hole solutions, considering both transverse and parallel orientations of the particle pairs relative to the magnetic field direction.

## Key numbers

* For weak magnetic field at T = 0.3 GeV, the critical electric field E_c decreases as B increases from 0.01 GeV^2 to 0.08 GeV^2 (Fig. 4b).
* For strong magnetic field at T = 0.15 GeV, the critical electric field E_c increases with B for values of 0.1 GeV^2, 0.15 GeV^2, and 0.2 GeV^2 (Fig. 8).
* The separating length maximum decreases with increasing magnetic field for both weak field (B = 0.01 GeV^2 and 0.08 GeV^2 at T = 0.3 GeV, Fig. 1b) and strong field (B = 0.1 GeV^2 and 0.15 GeV^2 at T = 0.15 GeV and T = 0.2 GeV, Fig. 6).
* The separating length maximum decreases with increasing temperature for B = 0.02 GeV^2 at T = 0.2 GeV, 0.25 GeV, and 0.3 GeV (Fig. 2).
* The Hawking temperature for the weak field solution is given by T = r_h/pi - B^2/(6 pi r_h^3).
* The Hawking temperature for the strong field solution is given by T = 3 r_h/(2 pi).

## Context

This work extends the holographic study of the Schwinger effect to include a magnetic field that affects the background geometry, building on the potential analysis approach developed by Sato and collaborators (2013) for confining theories. It differs from earlier treatments by Sato (2013) and Hashimoto (2014) that added magnetic fields via circular Wilson loops without modifying the geometry, instead using the magnetized Einstein-Maxwell system of DHoker and collaborators (2009). The results for the strong field regime show a qualitatively different behavior (critical field increasing with magnetic field) compared to the weak field regime, which the authors attribute to the different way the magnetic field is incorporated. The work is motivated by the strong magnetic fields (up to 15 m_pi^2) produced in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and aims to provide insight for future experimental observation of the Schwinger effect.
