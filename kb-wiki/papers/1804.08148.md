# Matching of transverse momentum dependent distributions at twist-3

> We derive the leading order matching of the quark generated polarized transverse momentum dependent (TMD) distributions onto the collinear functions at small values of the transverse distance. Starting from the very definition of the TMD operator and performing the light-cone operator product expansion up to twist-3 order, we evaluate each distribution directly in position space. We primarily consider the cases of Sivers, Boer-Mulders and worm-gear functions. The effects of the TMD process dependence on the matching are explicitly shown. We also discuss the moments of TMD distributions which can be relevant for lattice calculations.

## Key claim

The paper derives the leading-order matching of quark-generated polarized transverse momentum dependent (TMD) distributions onto collinear functions at small transverse distance, explicitly showing that the process dependence of TMD operators (future-pointing Wilson lines for SIDIS versus past-pointing for Drell-Yan) manifests in the operator product expansion (OPE) and contributes not only to P-odd distributions but also to P-even distributions.

## Method

The authors perform a light-cone operator product expansion (OPE) of the TMD operator in position space up to twist-3 order, expanding in powers of the transverse separation \(\vec b\) and keeping terms linear in \(\vec b\). They evaluate the matrix elements of the resulting two-point and three-point light-cone operators (quark-quark and quark-gluon-quark correlators) using standard parameterizations of collinear parton distribution functions, applying QCD equations of motion to relate twist-3 PDFs to twist-2 and three-point PDFs. The matching is computed at leading order in \(\alpha_s\) (tree level) for the Sivers, Boer-Mulders, and worm-gear functions, with the process dependence entering through the different integration limits in the three-point operator terms for Drell-Yan versus SIDIS kinematics.

## Key numbers

* The matching is performed at leading order in \(\alpha_s\) (order \(\alpha_s^0\)), i.e., at tree level, with no explicit numerical coefficients from higher-order corrections reported.
* The OPE expansion is carried out up to linear order in the transverse position \(\vec b\), with terms of order \(O(\vec b^2)\) neglected.
* The three-point PDFs \(T(x_1,x_2,x_3)\), \(\Delta T(x_1,x_2,x_3)\), \(\delta T_\epsilon(x_1,x_2,x_3)\), and \(\delta T_g(x_1,x_2,x_3)\) are defined with a factor of the hadron mass \(M\) to make them dimensionless.
* The first-moment relations \(\int_{-1}^1 dx\, g_1(x) = \int_{-1}^1 dx\, g_T(x)\) and \(\int_{-1}^1 dx\, h_1(x) = \int_{-1}^1 dx\, h_L(x)\) are given as exact sum rules (Burkhardt-Cottingham sum rule).

## Context

This work extends the matching of TMD distributions beyond the twist-2 level, which was uniformly known at one-loop (Gutierrez-Reyes et al., 2017) and partially at two-loop (Echevarria et al., 2015, 2016; Gutierrez-Reyes et al., 2018), to twist-3 accuracy for quark TMDPDFs. An early study of twist-3 matching was provided by Bacchetta et al. (2008) prior to the modern factorization theorem formulation. The results enable systematic phenomenological studies of spin-dependent TMD distributions at small transverse distances and can be applied to generalized transverse momentum distributions (GTMDs) and Wigner functions (Meissner et al., 2009; Lorce, 2011), as well as to lattice calculations via the moments of worm-gear functions discussed in the paper.
