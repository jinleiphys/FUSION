# Three Body Force in Peripheral $Nα$ Scattering

> The exchange of a single pion does not contribute to $Nα$ scattering, due to the isoscalar nature of the target. Therefore peripheral $Nα$ scattering may be used to probe existing models for the two-pion exchange component of the $NN$ potential. In a recent work we have considered this possibility using an effective $Nα$ interaction based on two-body forces only. In the present communication we discuss the role played by the two-pion exchange three-nucleon force, since its range is comparable to the two-pion exchange potential (TPEP).

## Key claim

Peripheral Nα scattering can be used to probe the two-pion exchange component of the NN potential, and the three-body force contributes a small effect of about 5% of the two-body interaction, preserving the ability of this scattering to discriminate the mid-range part of the NN potential.

## Method

The authors computed an effective Nα potential by averaging a two-pion exchange three-nucleon force over the α-particle ground state, which was assumed to be an S-wave with a gaussian structure. The three-body force was taken from Coelho, Das and Robilotta (1983), where the intermediate πN scattering is described within chiral symmetry. The resulting effective three-body potential was then used to calculate Nα phase shifts for the D5/2 and H11/2 partial waves as functions of laboratory energy.

## Key numbers

- The strength coefficients for the three-body force are Cs = 0.9204, Cp = -2.0082, and C'p = -0.6722
- The three-body force effect is approximately 5% of the two-body interaction, estimated by replacing the three-body potential with ε times the central component of the effective chiral potential, where ε ≈ 0.0485
- The term proportional to C'p drops out of the effective three-body potential due to the isoscalar nature of the α particle

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Barreiro, Higa, Lima and Robilotta, 1998) which considered peripheral Nα scattering using an effective interaction based on two-body forces only. The present communication adds the two-pion exchange three-nucleon force, whose range is comparable to the two-pion exchange potential. The three-body force used (Coelho, Das and Robilotta, 1983) and the chiral potential (Robilotta and da Rocha, 1997) are built within the same theoretical framework, allowing consistent comparison. The small size of the three-body effect means that peripheral Nα scattering remains a viable probe of the mid-range NN potential.
