# Possible chiral doublets in $^{60}$Ni

> The open problem on whether or not the chirality exists in doublet bands M1 and M4 in light-mass even-even nucleus $^{60}$Ni is studied by adopting the recently developed fully quantal four-$j$ shells triaxial particle rotor model. The corresponding experimental energy spectra, energy differences between doublet bands, and the available $B(M1)/B(E2)$ values are successfully reproduced. The analyses on the basis of the angular momentum components, the azimuthal plots, and the $K$-plots suggest that the chiral modes exist at $I\geq 12\hbar$ in doublet bands M1 and M4.

## Key claim

The chirality in doublet bands M1 and M4 of the light-mass even-even nucleus $^{60}$Ni is supported by the fully quantal four-$j$ shells triaxial particle rotor model, but only for spins $I \geq 12\hbar$; at $I \leq 11\hbar$ there is no indication of chirality.

## Method

The authors applied the recently developed fully quantal four-$j$ shells triaxial particle rotor model (PRM) to the configuration $\pi(1f_{7/2})^{-1}(2p_{3/2})^1\otimes \nu(1g_{9/2})^1(1f_{5/2})^{-1}$ in $^{60}$Ni. The PRM Hamiltonian was diagonalized in the laboratory frame with total angular momentum as a good quantum number, using deformation parameters $\beta=0.27$ and $\gamma=22^\circ$, a moment of inertia $\mathcal{J}_0 = 9.0~\hbar^2/\textrm{MeV}$, and a Coriolis attenuation factor $\xi=0.98$. The angular momentum geometry was analyzed via expectation values of angular momentum components, azimuthal plots of the total angular momentum orientation, and $K$-plots (probability distributions of angular momentum components on the three principal axes).

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameters used: $\beta=0.27$ and $\gamma=22^\circ$ (fixed in the PRM calculation).
- Moment of inertia: $\mathcal{J}_0 = 9.0~\hbar^2/\textrm{MeV}$.
- Coriolis attenuation factor: $\xi=0.98$.
- Chiral modes are suggested to exist at $I \geq 12\hbar$; no chirality is indicated at $I \leq 11\hbar$.
- At $I=14\hbar$, the azimuthal plots show two peaks for both bands: for band M1 at $(\theta \sim 60^\circ, \varphi \sim 60^\circ)$ and $(\theta \sim 60^\circ, \varphi \sim -60^\circ)$; for band M4 at $(\theta \sim 55^\circ, \varphi \sim 50^\circ)$ and $(\theta \sim 55^\circ, \varphi \sim -50^\circ)$.
- The energy splitting $\Delta E$ between the doublet bands is less than 400 keV in the static chirality region, with a value of approximately 400 keV at $I=13$ and $14\hbar$.

## Context

This work addresses the open problem of whether chirality exists in the doublet bands M1 and M4 of $^{60}$Ni, which was previously suggested by tilted axis cranking covariant density functional theory (TAC-CDFT) calculations by Zhao et al. (2011). The present study extends that work by using a fully quantal particle rotor model that can describe both bands simultaneously, unlike the mean-field TAC approach which could only describe band M1. The results enable further experimental efforts to extend the level scheme of band M4 (currently only reaching $I=13\hbar$) and to extract electromagnetic transition data, and encourage searches for chirality or multiple chirality in the $A\sim 60$ mass region and lighter-mass regions.
