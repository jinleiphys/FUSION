# Nonlocal Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with wavefunction renormalization at finite temperature and chemical potential

> We study the phase diagram of strongly interacting matter in the framework of a non-local SU(2) chiral quark model which includes wave function renormalization and coupling to the Polyakov loop. Both non-local interactions based on the frequently used exponential form factor, and on fits to the quark mass and renormalization functions obtained in lattice calculations are considered. Special attention is paid to the determination of the critical points, both in the chiral limit and at finite quark mass. In particular, we study the position of the Critical End Point as well as the value of the associated critical exponents for different model parameterizations.

# Nonlocal Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with wavefunction renormalization at finite temperature and chemical potential

## Key claim

The inclusion of wave function renormalization (WFR) in a nonlocal PNJL model shifts the Critical End Point (CEP) toward lower temperatures and higher chemical potentials compared to models without WFR, with the effect being particularly pronounced when using lattice-inspired form factors. The authors claim that the CEP position is highly sensitive to both the parametrization choice and the current quark mass, but that the critical exponents at the CEP consistently take mean-field values without evidence of a "two straight lines" behavior in the specific heat.

## Method

The authors extend a nonlocal SU(2) chiral quark model by including both a scalar-isoscalar current (generating momentum-dependent quark mass) and a "momentum" current (generating wave function renormalization), coupled to the Polyakov loop via a constant background color field in the Polyakov gauge. They work in the mean-field approximation using the Matsubara formalism, regularizing the divergent thermodynamic potential by subtracting the free quark contribution and adding a regularized free term. Three parametrizations are compared: Set A (exponential form factor, no WFR), Set B (exponential form factors with WFR, fitted to reproduce f_pi = 92.4 MeV, m_pi = 139 MeV, and Z(0) = 0.7), and Set C (lattice-inspired form factors fitted to reproduce lattice quark mass and renormalization functions). The gap equations for the mean fields sigma_1, sigma_2, and Phi are solved simultaneously, and the critical points are located using Landau expansion (for the tricritical point in the chiral limit) and a system of equations including second and third derivatives of the thermodynamic potential (for the CEP at finite quark mass).

## Key numbers

* For Set C (lattice-inspired) in the chiral limit: T_TCP = 196.4 MeV, mu_TCP = 136.2 MeV (Table II, approximate values from text context).
* For Set A (exponential, no WFR) in the chiral limit: T_TCP = 204.8 MeV (text states this value explicitly).
* For Set A (exponential, no WFR) at mu=0 in the chiral limit: T_c(0) = 209 MeV (from the approximate analytic formula, consistent with numerically found value).
* For finite quark mass, critical temperatures at mu=0 across all parametrizations are within the range 210-215 MeV.
* For finite quark mass, critical chemical potentials at T=0 across all parametrizations are in the range 298-322 MeV.
* For Set C at finite quark mass: mu_c(T=0) approximately 310 MeV (text states "mu_c(T=0) approximately 310 MeV").
* The critical exponents at the CEP for Set C are gamma_ch = gamma_q = alpha = 2/3 (mean-field values), verified numerically.
* The power-law behavior of Delta T_CEP and Delta mu_CEP as a function of m near the chiral limit yields a slope of 0.40 +/- 0.01 in log-log plots, consistent with the expected exponent 2/5.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nonlocal chiral quark models with Polyakov loop coupling (Contrera 2007, Blaschke 2007, Hell 2008) by incorporating wave function renormalization, following the approach of Noguera (2005, 2008) for describing the T=mu=0 quark propagator. The authors compare their results with the local PNJL model (Fukushima 2003, Ratti 2005, Roessner 2006) and with nonlocal models without WFR (GomezDumm 2006). The lattice-inspired parametrization (Set C) is based on fits to lattice QCD calculations of quark mass and renormalization functions (Parappilly 2005, Furui 2006), which indicate that WFR can be of order 30% or more at zero momentum. The work enables more reliable extrapolations of the QCD phase diagram into regions inaccessible to lattice calculations, particularly for determining the CEP location and its dependence on model parameters.
