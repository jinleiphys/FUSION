# Gluon saturation and entropy production in proton proton collisions

> We study properties of high energy factorisable gluon densities focusing on saturation effects. In particular we show that the property of saturation of unintegrated gluon density allows for introduction of thermodynamical entropy associated with production of gluons. This is due to the observation that saturation scale acts like a mass of a gluon which related with temperature via thermodynamical relations allows for calculations of entropy. We also show that obtained entropy behaves like multiplicity of produced gluons.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the saturation scale of unintegrated gluon densities can be used to define a thermodynamical entropy for produced gluons in proton-proton collisions, and that this entropy is proportional to the gluon multiplicity. The construction relies on interpreting the saturation scale as an effective gluon mass and relating it to temperature via the Unruh effect, with the caveat that the result is valid when the saturation scale is much larger than an unknown constant (chosen as \(\Lambda_{QCD}\)).

## Method

The author uses the Golec-Biernat–Wusthoff (GBW) model for the dipole amplitude in the fundamental color representation, transforms it to the adjoint representation via the large-\(N_c\) relation \(N_G = 2N_Q - N_Q^2\), and obtains the unintegrated gluon density in the adjoint representation. The inclusive gluon production cross section is then calculated using the \(k_T\)-factorization formula, and the result is expanded in the limit where one saturation scale is much smaller than the other. Entropy is derived from the thermodynamic relation \(dE = T dS\), with the temperature given by \(T = Q_s/(2\pi)\) from the Unruh effect, and the effective mass of the gluon system set equal to the saturation scale times the number of gluons.

## Key numbers

* The saturation scale in the adjoint representation is larger than in the fundamental representation: \((Q_s^2)_G = 2 (Q_s^2)_Q\).
* The cross section for inclusive gluon production, when one saturation scale is the smallest scale, is approximated by \(\frac{d\sigma}{dy} = \frac{2 A_{\perp}^2 C_F Q_{s\,1}^2}{\pi^2 \alpha_s}\) (leading term), with subleading corrections of order \(Q_{s\,1}^4 / Q_{s\,2}^2\).
* The entropy of produced gluons is given by \(S = \frac{6 C_F A_{\perp}}{\pi \alpha_s} Q_s^2(x) + S_0\), where \(S_0 = \frac{6 C_F A_{\perp}}{\pi \alpha_s} \mu^2\) is the lowest entropy state.
* The entropy is related to the number of gluons from the unintegrated gluon density as \(S = 12\pi\, n_G(x) + 3\pi\, N_{G\,0}\).
* The temperature is related to the saturation scale by \(T = Q_s(x) / (2\pi)\).

## Context

This work extends the idea of using the Unruh effect in QCD, as developed by Kharzeev (2005) and Castorina (2007), to connect the saturation scale to a temperature and then to entropy. It also builds on the GBW model (Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff, 1998) and the concept of geometrical scaling (Stasto, 2000). The result enables a thermodynamic interpretation of gluon production in the saturation regime and suggests an upper bound on entropy from the saturated part of the gluon distribution, following the observation by Albacete (2008) and Avsar (2010) that the saturation scale itself may saturate at extreme energies.
