# Is J/psi-Nucleon Scattering Dominated by the Gluonic van der Waals Interaction?

> The gluon-exchange contribution to $J/ψ$-nucleon scattering is shown to yield a sizeable scattering length of about -0.25 fm, which is consistent with the sparse available data. Hadronic corrections to gluon exchange which are generated by $ρπ$ and $D\bar D$ intermediate states of the $J/ψ$ are shown to be negligible. We also propose a new method to study $J/ψ$-nucleon elastic scattering in the reaction $π^+ d\to J/ψp p$.

## Key claim

The gluonic van der Waals interaction from two-gluon exchange dominates low-energy J/ψ-nucleon scattering, yielding a scattering length of about -0.25 fm, while hadronic corrections from ρπ and D\(\bar D\) intermediate states are negligible; the authors hedge that the Coulomb approximation for the J/ψ Bohr radius may not be completely reliable but should provide a good estimate.

## Method

The authors computed the forward invariant amplitude for J/ψ-nucleon scattering in first Born approximation using the Luke-Manohar-Savage operator product expansion for two-gluon exchange, then solved the Schroindinger equation with a Gaussian potential of range 0.8 fm to obtain the full scattering length. They estimated meson-exchange contributions by constructing effective hadronic Lagrangians for ρπ and D\(\bar D\) intermediate states, determining coupling constants from the J/ψ→ρπ decay width and from scaling the φ→K\(\bar K\) coupling by the ratio of root-mean-square radii, then computing the resulting J/ψ-nucleon potential via time-ordered perturbation theory and mass-shift derivatives.

## Key numbers

- Born-approximation scattering length a_B = -0.19 fm for J/ψ-nucleon scattering from two-gluon exchange.
- Full scattering length a = -0.24 fm after solving the Schroindinger equation with a Gaussian potential of depth V_0 = 23 MeV and range R = 0.8 fm.
- Total cross section at threshold σ_{ψN} = 4πa^2 ≈ 7 mb, compared to σ_{ψN} = 3.5 ± 0.8 mb ± 0.5 mb from nuclear J/ψ photoproduction at 20 GeV.
- J/ψ→ρπ branching fraction 1.28% of the 87 keV width; coupling g = ε M_ψ with ε = 1.7×10^{-3}.
- D\(\bar D\) coupling g_c^2/(4π) ≈ 0.13, obtained by scaling from g_s^2/(4π) = 1.71 using R_ψ = 0.11 fm and R_φ = 0.4 fm.
- ρπ contribution to J/ψ-nucleon potential ≈ 10^{-5} MeV; D\(\bar D\) contribution ≈ 0.006 × V(D,N), with V(D,N) ≤ about 1 MeV near threshold, giving less than 0.1 MeV.
- Differential cross section dσ_{πN→ψN}/dt at t=0 is 1.6 nb GeV^{-2}; total cross section for π N → ψ N is approximately 1 nb.

## Context

This work extends the operator product expansion analysis of Luke, Manohar, and Savage (1992) for heavy quarkonium-nucleon interactions by providing explicit numerical estimates of the scattering length and demonstrating dominance over hadronic mechanisms. It also builds on Peskin's (1979) result for the color electric polarizability of heavy quarkonium and on the observation by de Teramond and collaborators that the rise of the polarization asymmetry A_{NN} in pp scattering near charm threshold requires a scattering length of similar magnitude. The proposed reaction π^+ d → J/ψ p p enables first direct measurements of J/ψ-nucleon elastic scattering at low energies, testing QCD van der Waals predictions.
