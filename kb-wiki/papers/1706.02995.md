# Optical properties of an anisotropic hot QCD medium

> The present investigation involves explorations on the chromo-dielectric properties of the hot QCD medium produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions in terms of refractive index. The isotropic/equilibrium modelling is done within an effective quasi-particle model of hot QCD medium. The possibilities of negative refraction in the medium are also explored in terms of the Depine-Lakhtakia index. The anisotropic aspects of the hot QCD medium are incorporated by introducing the anisotropy in a particular direction. That makes the medium quite similar to uniaxial crystals, and hence we observe phenomenon of birefringence ( two distinct refractive indices in the anisotropic case). Interestingly, both anisotropy and medium effects play significant roles in deciding the optical properties of the hot QCD/Quark-Gluon-Plasma (QGP) medium.

# Optical properties of an anisotropic hot QCD medium

## Key claim
The chromo-refractive index of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) can become negative in certain frequency ranges, and when momentum anisotropy is introduced along a preferred direction, the medium exhibits birefringence with two distinct refractive indices, analogous to uniaxial crystals. The authors claim these effects depend significantly on both the equation-of-state (EoS) effects incorporated via a quasi-particle model and the degree of anisotropy, but the analysis is restricted to the Abelian limit and small anisotropy parameter ξ.

## Method
The authors compute the gluon polarization tensor within semi-classical linear response transport theory (Boltzmann-Vlasov equation) in the Abelian limit, using isotropic equilibrium distribution functions from the effective fugacity quasi-particle model (EQPM) that encodes hot QCD equation-of-state effects. Anisotropy is introduced by rescaling the isotropic distribution along a fixed direction n̂ via a parameter ξ, and the resulting gluon self-energy is decomposed into four structure functions (α, β, γ, δ) using tensor projectors. From these, the chromo-electric permittivity (ε_L, ε_R) and chromo-magnetic permeability (μ) are extracted, and the refractive indices n_L and n_R are computed as n = √(ε μ), with negative refraction diagnosed via the Depine-Lakhtakia index n_DL for the isotropic case.

## Key numbers
- Debye mass m_D is computed using EQPM for two EoSs: (2+1)-flavor lattice EoS from HotQCD collaboration (LEoS) and 3-loop HTL perturbative EoS; the ideal (non-interacting) case corresponds to z_g → 1, z_q → 1.
- Results are shown at temperature T = 2 T_c with T_c = 0.17 GeV and wave number k = 0.2 GeV.
- Anisotropy parameter ξ is varied; the normalization constant C_ξ = 1 + ξ/3 + O(ξ²) ensures the Debye mass remains unchanged between isotropic and anisotropic cases.
- Birefringence B = |n_L − n_R| is computed as a function of frequency ω for various angles θ_n between the propagation direction and the anisotropy axis.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of the refractive index in QGP by Jiang et al. (2013, 2016), who used kinetic theory with a BGK collisional kernel but treated the QGP as an ideal gas of non-interacting quarks and gluons. The present analysis incorporates both EoS effects (via the EQPM developed by Chandra and collaborators) and momentum anisotropy, which was not extensively studied previously. The collective plasma modes for anisotropic QGP within this approach were previously investigated by Romatschke and Strickland (2003, 2004) and by the same authors in earlier work (Jamal et al., 2017; Kumar et al., 2017). The results enable future exploration of negative refraction and opacity regions in heavy-ion collision environments, and may inform optical analogies for QGP diagnostics.
