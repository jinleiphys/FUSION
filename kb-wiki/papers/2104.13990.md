# A review of quarkonia under strong magnetic fields

> We review the properties of quarkonia under strong magnetic fields. The main phenomena are (i) mixing between different spin eigenstates, (ii) quark Landau levels and deformation of wave function, (iii) modification of $\bar{Q}Q$ potential, and (iv) the motional Stark effect. For theoretical approaches, we review (i) constituent quark models, (ii) effective Lagrangians, (iii) QCD sum rules, and (iv) holographic approaches.

# A review of quarkonia under strong magnetic fields

## Key claim

This review systematically catalogs four main phenomena affecting quarkonia in strong magnetic fields, spin mixing, Landau levels, anisotropic confinement potential deformation, and the motional Stark effect, but notes that many results rely on simplified constituent quark models, with only partial confirmation from QCD sum rules, effective Lagrangians, and holographic approaches. The authors emphasize that in realistic heavy-ion collision experiments, magnetic field effects coexist with finite temperature, density, and vorticity, making isolation of pure magnetic signatures challenging.

## Method

The authors survey theoretical approaches to magnetized quarkonia: (i) constituent quark models solving the Schrödinger equation with a confining potential and magnetic interactions, (ii) effective Lagrangians with pseudoscalar-vector mixing terms, (iii) QCD sum rules using operator product expansions with magnetically modified condensates, and (iv) holographic AdS/CFT approaches using soft wall models and Einstein-Maxwell actions. They review results for S-wave charmonia (η_c, J/ψ) and bottomonia (η_b, Υ), P-wave charmonia (χ_c0, χ_c1, χ_c2, h_c), and excited states, as well as heavy-light mesons (D, B, B_s).

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strength in LHC heavy-ion collisions estimated at |eB| ~ 50 m_π^2 ~ 1 GeV^2 ~ 10^19 Gauss
- Charm and bottom quark masses exceed 1 GeV
- Vorticity strength in RHIC collisions estimated at ω ≈ 9 × 10^21 s^{-1} from Λ hyperon polarization
- Mass shifts of a few MeV for η_c and J/ψ in nuclear matter estimated from gluon condensate modifications via QCD sum rules

## Context

This review extends the line of work on heavy quarkonia under extreme conditions, building on the constituent quark model studies of Alford (2013) that first systematically examined spin mixing in charmonia and bottomonia, and on lattice QCD measurements of anisotropic static potentials by Bonati (2014, 2016). It connects to earlier work on J/ψ suppression in hot QCD matter (Matsui 1986) and the magnetic catalysis of chiral condensates (Klevansky 1989, Gusynin 1994). The review enables future experimental searches for magnetic signatures in heavy-ion collisions at LHC and RHIC, particularly through anisotropic photon emission from radiative decays and modifications of quarkonium production yields.
