# Deuteron production and space-momentum correlations at RHIC

> We present predictions for the formation of (anti)nuclear bound states in Au+Au central collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200A$ GeV. The coalescence afterburner was applied to the freeze-out phase space distributions of nucleons provided by the transport model, RQMD version 2.4. We study the sensitivity of the deuteron spectra to space-momentum correlations. It is found that the deuteron transverse momentum distributions are strongly affected by the nucleon space-momentum correlations.

# Deuteron production and space-momentum correlations at RHIC

## Key claim

Deuteron transverse momentum distributions in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC are strongly affected by nucleon space-momentum correlations, but this sensitivity is demonstrated only through model calculations using RQMD version 2.4 with a coalescence afterburner, not from experimental data. The authors state that if new physics occurs at RHIC, a modification of the space-momentum structure will manifest itself in deuteron yields and transverse momentum distributions, but this remains a prediction.

## Method

The authors used the transport model RQMD version 2.4 to generate freeze-out phase space distributions of nucleons for central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200A GeV, then applied a coalescence afterburner to form deuterons and antideuterons. To isolate the effect of space-momentum correlations, they deliberately altered the relative orientation of each nucleon's transverse radius vector r_T and transverse momentum vector p_T, creating three scenarios: normal RQMD events, a "random" case with no collective flow, and an "aligned" case mimicking maximum flow. They also performed a calculation without rescattering among baryons.

## Key numbers

- Predicted deuteron production rate into STAR TPC acceptance: approximately 1 deuteron per 20 central events
- Predicted antideuteron production rate into STAR TPC acceptance: approximately 1 antideuteron per 100 central events
- Expected trigger rate for STAR Au+Au central collisions: approximately 1 Hz
- In calculations without baryon rescattering, the difference between deuteron and proton transverse momentum is approximately 150 MeV, constant and rapidity independent
- The average transverse momentum for deuterons is about a factor of two higher than for protons in normal RQMD events

## Context

This work extends earlier coalescence studies by Nagle, Sorge, and others (1990s) to RHIC energies, using the RQMD cascade model of Sorge (1995) to predict light cluster production. The paper demonstrates that deuteron spectra are sensitive to collective transverse flow effects, which have been studied previously by NA44 and others in lower-energy heavy-ion collisions. The results provide a baseline for STAR collaboration measurements at RHIC and suggest that deuteron and antideuteron measurements are feasible as "year one" physics at the new collider.
