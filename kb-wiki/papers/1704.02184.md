# Kinetic energy in the collective quadrupole Hamiltonian from the experimental data

> Dependence of the kinetic energy term of the collective nuclear Hamiltonian on collective momentum is considered. It is shown that the fourth order in collective momentum term of the collective quadrupole Hamiltonian generates a sizable effect on the excitation energies and the matrix elements of the quadrupole moment operator. It is demonstrated that the results of calculation are sensitive to the values of some matrix elements of the quadrupole moment. It stresses the importance for a concrete nucleus to have the experimental data for the reduced matrix elements of the quadrupole moment operator taken between all low lying states with the angular momenta not exceeding 4.

## Key claim

The fourth-order term in collective momentum in the Bohr Hamiltonian contributes up to 10% of the total kinetic energy and up to 26% of the energy-weighted sum rule for E2 transitions, but these estimates depend on the sign and magnitude of the reduced matrix element of the quadrupole moment operator for the first 2+ state, which is not well constrained by experiment for some nuclei.

## Method

The authors derived sum-rule expressions for the ground-state average of a double commutator and a fourth-order commutator of the collective Hamiltonian with the quadrupole moment operator, which are independent of the potential energy. They equated these expressions to the corresponding commutators evaluated using a kinetic energy ansatz containing both quadratic and quartic terms in collective momentum, then solved for the coefficient D of the quartic term and the mass parameter B using experimental excitation energies and B(E2) values for low-lying states (0+1, 0+2, 2+1, 2+2, 2+3, 4+1). The analysis was applied to the X(5) nuclei 150Nd, 152Sm, 154Gd and to 110Pd.

## Key numbers

* For 152Sm: s = -1.13, X = 0.09 (9% contribution of the quartic term to the kinetic energy), F = 0.26 (26% contribution to the energy-weighted sum rule).
* For 154Gd: s = -0.49, X = 0.047 (4.7%), F = -0.13 (13%).
* For 110Pd: s = -0.88, X = 0.077 (7.7%), F = -0.22 (22%).
* For 150Nd: s = +0.16, X = 0.018 (1.8%), F = 0.053 (5.3%), but varying the ratio |<2_1||Q_2||2_1>|/|<2_1||Q_2||0_1>| from 1.57 to 1.2 changes s from +0.16 to -1.17.
* In the SU(5) limit of the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) for N=6 (110Pd): s = -0.18, X ≈ 0.02.
* In the SU(3) limit for N=12 (156Gd): s = -0.07, X ≈ 0.01.
* In the O(6) limit for N=6 (196Pt): s = -0.67, X ≈ 0.06.

## Context

This work extends the method of energy-weighted sum rules for E2 transitions developed by Jolos (2007, 2009) to probe the momentum dependence of the kinetic energy in the Bohr Hamiltonian, beyond the usual quadratic approximation. It shows that the quartic term, often neglected under the adiabatic assumption, produces effects comparable to those from deformation-dependent mass tensors considered by Gneuss, Kumar, and Petkov. The results motivate experimental determination of reduced matrix elements of the quadrupole moment operator between all low-lying states with angular momentum up to 4, which would enable more precise tests of collective models and constrain microscopic theories of the mass tensor.
