# Determining the Density of the Sun with Neutrinos

> The discovery of solar neutrinos confirmed that the inner workings of the Sun generally match our theoretical understanding of the fusion process. Solar neutrinos have also played a role in discovering that neutrinos have mass and that they oscillate. We combine the latest solar neutrino data along with other oscillation data from reactors to determine the Sun's density profile. We derive constraints given the current data and show the anticipated improvements with more reactor neutrino data from JUNO constraining the true oscillation parameters and more solar neutrino data from DUNE which should provide a crucial measurement of $hep$ neutrinos.

# Determining the Density of the Sun with Neutrinos

## Key claim
By combining current solar neutrino data with reactor oscillation data from KamLAND, the Sun's density profile can be constrained in a relatively model-independent way, and future measurements from JUNO and DUNE will significantly improve these constraints, particularly through DUNE's anticipated first measurement of *hep* neutrinos which probe larger solar radii.

## Method
The authors construct a chi-squared test statistic combining solar neutrino survival probability measurements from Super-Kamiokande, SNO, Borexino, and gallium experiments (SAGE, GALLEX, GNO) with reactor neutrino data from KamLAND that independently constrains the oscillation parameters theta_12 and Delta_m^2_21. They model the Sun's electron density profile in two ways: a binned model with constant density in three radial bins (r/R_sun in [0,0.05), [0.05,0.1), and [0.1,0.5)) and a parametric fit to a logarithmic linear function of radius. The oscillation probability depends on the matter potential at the neutrino production point through the MSW effect, and different nuclear processes (pp, pep, Be-7, B-8, hep) produce neutrinos at different energies and radii, enabling a differential probe of the density profile.

## Context
This work extends the concept first proposed by Balantekin (1997) of using solar neutrinos to probe the Sun's interior density, and later investigated by Lopes (2013) for future measurements. It builds on recent algorithmic developments by Laber-Smith (2022, 2024) for extracting density information from solar data. The approach is complementary to standard solar models (SSM) and helioseismology, which have limited sensitivity to the inner 5-10% of the Sun's radius where neutrinos are dominantly produced. The results enable future combined analyses with JUNO reactor data and DUNE solar neutrino measurements to provide the first neutrino-based constraints on the Sun's density profile at larger radii through hep neutrinos.
