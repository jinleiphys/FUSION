# Study of Quarkonium properties using SUSYQM method with baryonic chemical potential

> In this article, we employed the Quasi-particle debye mass at finite baryonic chemical potential which can be used in the medium modified heavy quark potential to solve the N-dimensional Schroedinger equation. The bound state solution of the Schroedinger equation using Cornell potential is obtained by Super-Symmetry Quantum Mechanics (SUSYQM) method. The thermodynamical properties of quark matter is calculated by using baryonic chemical potential (mu). We found that the binding energy of quarkonia dissociates more with quasi-particle debye mass in comparison to non-perturbative and leading order debye mass. The medium modified form of potential (real part) has been used to study the thermodynamical properties of quark matter with different equation of states (EoS) (i.e., pressure, energy density and speed of sound) with {mu}. The mass spectra of quarkonia has been also calculated in the N-dimensional space, and compared with the experimental data at N=3. We have also calculated the dissociation temperature (T_D) for the ground states of quarkonium using the dissociation criteria of thermal width.

## Key claim

The authors claim that using a quasi-particle Debye mass (which depends on both temperature and baryonic chemical potential) in a medium-modified Cornell potential leads to stronger dissociation of quarkonium binding energy compared to using non-perturbative or leading-order Debye masses, but this result is conditional on solving the N-dimensional Schrödinger equation via the SUSYQM method and on the specific approximation used for the 1/r term.

## Method

The N-dimensional radial Schrödinger equation was solved analytically using the Supersymmetric Quantum Mechanics (SUSYQM) method with a medium-modified Cornell potential that incorporates a quasi-particle Debye mass dependent on temperature and baryonic chemical potential. The binding energy, mass spectra, and thermal width were computed for charmonium (J/ψ) and bottomonium (Υ) states, and the thermodynamic properties (pressure, energy density, speed of sound) were derived using the real part of the potential with two different equations of state (EoS1: O(g⁵) hot QCD; EoS2: O(g⁶ ln(1/g)) hot QCD) in the quasi-particle description. The dissociation temperature was determined using the criterion that thermal width equals twice the binding energy.

## Key numbers

- String constant σ = 0.184 GeV² used in the Cornell potential.
- For J/ψ at μ = 1000 MeV, N = 3, and Tc = 197 MeV: mass spectra = 3.060 GeV (compared to experimental value 3.096 GeV).
- For Υ at μ = 1000 MeV, N = 3, and Tc = 197 MeV: mass spectra = 9.200 GeV (compared to experimental value 9.460 GeV).
- Dissociation temperature TD for J/ψ at N = 4: 0.7567 (T/Tc) at μ = 1 GeV; 0.7450 at μ = 1.5 GeV; 0.7376 at μ = 2 GeV.
- Dissociation temperature TD for Υ at N = 4: 0.8116 (T/Tc) at μ = 1 GeV; 0.7926 at μ = 1.5 GeV; 0.7808 at μ = 2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of quarkonium properties at finite temperature and zero chemical potential (e.g., using leading-order Debye mass) by incorporating baryonic chemical potential via a quasi-particle Debye mass, which the authors state has not been reported previously. It builds on the SUSYQM method previously applied to solvable potentials (e.g., by Cooper et al. and others) and on quasi-particle models for QCD equation of state (e.g., effective fugacity models by Chandra et al.). The results enable further study of quarkonium dissociation in hot and dense QCD matter relevant to heavy-ion collisions at facilities like RHIC and LHC, and provide a framework for comparing thermodynamic properties with lattice QCD and other phenomenological models such as the Nilima EoS.
