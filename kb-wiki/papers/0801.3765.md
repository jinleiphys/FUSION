# Deformation properties of the BCP energy density functional

> We explore the deformation properties of the newly postulated BCP energy density functional (EDF). The results obtained for three isotope chains of Mg, Dy and Ra are compared to the available experimental data as well as to the results of the Gogny-D1S force. Results for the fission barrier of $^{240}$Pu are also discussed.

## Key claim

The BCP energy density functional, which incorporates a microscopic equation of state with a finite-range surface term, reproduces deformation properties (potential energy surfaces, ground-state deformations, and fission barriers) with quality comparable to the well-established Gogny-D1S force, although systematic differences in deformation energy arise from a slightly lower surface energy coefficient and different effective mass.

## Method

The authors performed axially symmetric (but reflection-asymmetric allowed) Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the gradient method with constraints on the quadrupole moment. The quasiparticle states were expanded in a deformed harmonic oscillator basis with up to 26 shells for fission calculations. Potential energy surfaces (PES) as a function of the quadrupole deformation parameter β₂ were computed for isotope chains of Mg (A=20–40), Dy (A=140–170), and Ra (A=216–236), and the fission barrier of ²⁴⁰Pu was evaluated including the rotational energy correction from the Yoccoz moment of inertia.

## Key numbers

- Surface energy coefficient: a_S = 17.74 MeV for BCP1, 17.84 MeV for BCP2, and 18.2 MeV for Gogny D1S.
- Binding energy of ¹⁶⁰Dy (15-shell basis): -1305.894 MeV (BCP1), -1305.607 MeV (BCP2), -1304.288 MeV (D1S); experimental value is -1309.457 MeV.
- Rotational energy correction for ¹⁶⁰Dy: 2.18 MeV for BCP and 3.16 MeV for D1S.
- Spontaneous fission half-life of ²⁴⁰Pu (without triaxiality): 1.2×10²⁸ s for BCP1, 1.1×10²⁷ s for BCP2, and 1.5×10²⁶ s for D1S.
- Particle-particle correlation energy (measure of pairing) is 30–40% stronger in Gogny-D1S than in BCP.
- Octupole deformation β₃ ≈ 0.15 for ²²⁰–²²⁶Ra isotopes in all calculations.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of the BCP functional (Robledo et al., 2008, "BCP" paper), which had been tested only on spherical nuclei, into the realm of deformed nuclei. The results are systematically compared to the Gogny-D1S force (Berger et al., 1989) as a benchmark. The study enables future work on octupole deformation, triaxiality, collective inertia, and moment of inertia with the BCP functional, as noted by the authors.
