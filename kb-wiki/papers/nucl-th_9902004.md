# Thermodynamical characterization of heavy ion reactions

> An detailed study of the thermodynamical state of nuclear matter in transport calculations of heavy--ion reactions is presented. In particular we determine temperatures from an analysis of the local momentum space distribution on one hand, and from a fit to fragment energy spectra in terms of a blast model with radial flow and temperature on the other. We apply this to spectator and participant matter. In spectator we find regions of spinodal instability with temperatures and densities which are consistent with experiments. In the participant we find different temperatures for different fragment masses, indicating that the fragments are not emitted from a source in thermal and chemical equilibrium.

## Key claim

The paper shows that while spectator matter in heavy-ion reactions approaches a freeze-out configuration consistent with thermal and chemical equilibrium (with temperatures around 5 MeV), participant matter does not yield a unique temperature for all fragment masses, indicating that fragment emission is a dynamical process rather than emission from a source in thermal and chemical equilibrium.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic transport calculations for Au on Au collisions and determined temperatures using two methods: (1) fitting local momentum space distributions to covariant Fermi-Dirac distributions to obtain a local microscopic temperature, and (2) fitting fragment energy spectra generated via a phase space coalescence algorithm to a Siemens-Rasmussen blast model with radial flow and a global temperature. They applied these analyses separately to spectator matter in semi-central collisions and participant matter (fireball) in central collisions.

## Key numbers

- Spectator temperatures approach a constant value of about T ≈ 5 MeV, remaining fairly stable from about 40 fm/c to 80 fm/c, and are rather independent of incident beam energy
- Densities at the instability condition in spectator matter are about 1/3 to 1/2 of saturation density
- The difference between Maxwell-Boltzmann and Fermi-Dirac local temperature determinations is ≤ 5 MeV in the final stages of the collision
- The local temperature from momentum distributions at about 35 fm/c agrees approximately with the blast model fit to A_f = 1 energy spectra

## Context

This work extends transport-based studies of heavy-ion collisions by Gaitanos and collaborators (1998) and Esse and collaborators (1998) that examined non-equilibrium effects and momentum space anisotropies. The spectator analysis is compared directly to experimental results from the ALADIN collaboration, which measured the same T ≈ 5 MeV value using different thermometers. The participant analysis uses the blast model approach of Siemens and Rasmussen (1979) and the FOPI collaboration, and the results question whether a freeze-out configuration in thermodynamical equilibrium is a valid picture for fragment emission from the fireball.
