# Partial-wave analysis of proton Compton scattering data below the pion-production threshold

> Low-energy Compton scattering off the proton is used for determination of the proton polarizabilities. However, the present empirical determinations rely heavily on the theoretical description(s) of the experimental cross sections in terms of polarizabilities. The most recent determinations are based on either the fixed-$t$ dispersion relations (DR) or chiral perturbation theory in the single-baryon sector ($χ$PT). The two approaches obtain rather different results for proton polarizabilities, most notably for $β_{M1}$ (magnetic dipole polarizability). We attempt to resolve this discrepancy by performing a partial-wave analysis of the world data on proton Compton scattering below threshold. We find a large sensitivity of the extraction to a few "outliers", leading us to conclude that the difference between DR and $χ$PT extraction is a problem of the experimental database rather than of "model-dependence". We have specific suggestions for new experiments needed for an efficient improvement of the database. With the present database, the difference of proton scalar polarizabilities is constrainedto a rather broad interval: $α_{E1}-β_{M1} = (6.8\, \ldots \, 10.9)\times 10^{-4}$ fm$^3$, with their sum fixed much more precisely [to $14.0(2)$] by the Baldin sum rule.

## Key claim

The discrepancy between dispersion relation (DR) and chiral perturbation theory (χPT) extractions of proton polarizabilities, particularly the magnetic dipole polarizability βM1, arises from inconsistencies in the experimental database rather than from model dependence; the authors find that the extracted polarizability values are highly sensitive to a few "outlier" data points.

## Method

The authors performed a partial-wave analysis (PWA) of world data on proton Compton scattering below the pion-production threshold (≤ 150 MeV), truncating the multipole expansion of the non-Born amplitude at total angular momentum J = 3/2 and fitting six lowest l = 1 non-Born multipoles parametrized in terms of static polarizabilities. They used two linear relations from forward Compton scattering sum rules (the Baldin sum rule and the forward spin polarizability sum rule) to eliminate two global parameters, and fitted the remaining four static polarizabilities plus four energy-dependent residual functions across 11 energy bins, employing a Monte-Carlo swipe followed by standard minimization.

## Key numbers

- αE1 − βM1 = (6.8 … 10.9) × 10⁻⁴ fm³, a broad interval from the present database
- αE1 + βM1 = (14.0 ± 0.2) × 10⁻⁴ fm³, fixed by the Baldin sum rule
- βM1 ranges from 1.8(3) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (Fit 1, raw database) to 3.8(4) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (Fit 1′′, refined database)
- γ0 = (−0.929 ± 0.044) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴, the forward spin polarizability from sum rules
- χ²/point values: 1.35 (Fit 1), 0.97 (Fit 1₃σ), 0.62 (Fit 1′′)

## Context

This work extends the only previous comprehensive PWA of proton Compton scattering by Pfeil et al. (1974), which focused on the Δ(1232) region, by performing the first PWA below the pion threshold. The analysis attempts to resolve the tension between fixed-t dispersion relation extractions (which yield βM1 ≈ 1.6 × 10⁻⁴ fm³) and chiral perturbation theory predictions (which yield βM1 ≈ 3.9 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ from BχPT). The authors conclude that new precision data at backward angles near 100 MeV are needed to resolve the database inconsistencies, and note that the pilot beam-asymmetry data from Sokhoyan et al. (2016) at MAMI are consistent with all fits but require improved accuracy.
