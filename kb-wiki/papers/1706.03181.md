# Derivation of the gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations at large $N_c$ limit and symmetry preserving truncations

> We develop a framework for deriving Dyson-Schwinger Equations (DSEs) and Bethe-Salpeter Equation (BSE) in QCD at large $N_c$ limit. The starting point is a modified form (with auxiliary fields) of QCD generating functional. This framework provides a natural order-by-order truncation scheme for DSEs and BSE, and the kernels of the equations up to any order are explicitly given. Chiral symmetry (at chiral limit) is preserved in any order truncation, so it exemplifies the symmetry preserving truncation scheme. It provides a method to study DSEs and BSE beyond the Rainbow-Ladder truncation, and is especially useful to study contributions from non-Abelian dynamics (those arise from gluon self-interactions). We also derive the equation for the quark-ghost scattering kernel, and discuss the Slavnov-Taylor identity connecting the quark-gluon vertex, the quark propagator and the quark-ghost scattering kernel.

## Key claim

The paper develops a framework for deriving Dyson-Schwinger Equations (DSEs) and the Bethe-Salpeter Equation (BSE) in QCD specifically at the large \(N_c\) limit, claiming that this provides a natural order-by-order truncation scheme where chiral symmetry is preserved at any truncation order, but only in the chiral limit and only when the truncation is applied consistently through the generating functional rather than to the equations separately.

## Method

The authors start from a modified QCD generating functional that includes auxiliary bilocal fields and external sources for quarks, gluons, and ghosts. They perform a large \(N_c\) expansion, integrate out the gluon fields to obtain an effective action expressed in terms of connected n-gluon Green's functions without inner quark loops, and then derive the quark gap equation, the quark-gluon vertex DSE, and the meson BSE from the same generating functional. The truncation scheme is defined by keeping a finite number of connected gluon Green's functions in the expansion, with the lowest order (keeping only the 2-gluon Green's function) corresponding to the Rainbow-Ladder truncation.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order truncation (Abelian approximation) at large \(N_c\) reduces the quark-gluon vertex to its bare form: \(\Gamma^{\nu,\rho'\sigma'}_{i,\beta\alpha}(z',y',x') = -g(\frac{\lambda_i}{2})_{\beta\alpha}\gamma_{\rho'\sigma'}^{\nu}\delta(z'-y')\delta(z'-x')\).
* The expansion parameter for the gluon Green's functions in the effective action is \((N_c g^2)^{n-1}\), where \(n\) is the number of gluon legs, and the leading term corresponds to \(n=2\).
* The quark propagator is given by \(i\Phi_c = [i\partial\!\!\!/ - M - \Pi_c]^{-1}\), where \(\Pi_c\) is the quark self-energy, and the gap equation sums over \(n \geq 2\) with factors of \((-i)^n (N_c g^2)^{n-1}/(n-1)!\).

## Context

This work extends the bilocal action approach introduced by Cahill and Roberts (1985) for the Global Color Model and later generalized by Wang, Wang, Kwong, and Xu (2000) for chiral perturbation theory, providing a systematic framework for going beyond the Rainbow-Ladder truncation in DSEs and BSE studies. The method enables direct testing of non-Abelian dynamics (gluon self-interactions) in hadron phenomenology, addressing the known inadequacy of Rainbow-Ladder truncation for scalar and axial-vector meson spectra noted by Bhagwat, Qin, Chang, Roberts, and Raya (2010) and others. The framework also derives the quark-ghost scattering kernel within the same formalism, which could help verify the Slavnov-Taylor identity for the quark-gluon vertex and potentially improve vertex modeling beyond the Ball-Chiu (1980) or Curtis-Pennington (1990) approximations used in QED.
