# The MIT bag model in nuclear medium from EQMC and QHD descriptions

> Recent developments in the density dependence of the MIT bag radius and parameters in nuclear matter are discussed. Starting from the MIT bag lagrangian density, the calculations are specialized for symmetric homogeneous isotropic nuclear matter. A simultaneous description of the density dependence of MIT bag model is derived from the Quantum Hadrodynamics and an extended Quark Meson Coupling model. Results are in agreement with those derived from Scaling Model.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the density dependence of MIT bag parameters (radius, bag constant, zero-point energy) can be derived consistently from both Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) and an extended Quark Meson Coupling (EQMC) model, but only when equilibrium conditions between internal bag pressure and external hadronic pressure are imposed. The results agree with the Scaling Model (SM) over a wide density range, but coincide with the Direct Coupling Model (DCM) only for small values of the scalar field.

## Method
The author starts from the MIT bag Lagrangian density with quark and meson fields, applies the Mean Field Approximation (MFA), and derives the bag energy, nucleon mass, and bag pressure. For the nuclear medium, the Zimanyi-Moszkowski (ZM) version of QHD is used to compute the effective nucleon mass and hadronic pressure. The coherence of the two descriptions is enforced by requiring equality of the nucleon masses from both models and equality of the internal bag pressure with the external hadronic pressure, leading to equations that determine the density dependence of the bag radius \(R\), bag constant \(B\), and zero-point energy parameter \(z_0\) via two constant derivatives \(\lambda = dB/d\bar{\sigma}\) and \(\mu = dz_0/d\bar{\sigma}\).

## Key numbers
- Two parameter sets are selected: set I (\(\lambda = -5.28\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(\mu = -0.50\) fm) and set II (\(\lambda = 0\), \(\mu = 1.6\) fm).
- For set II with the ZM model, the bag constant density dependence is fitted by a quadratic polynomial with coefficients \(A_0 = 184.723\) MeV, \(A_1 = -186.42\) MeV, \(A_2 = 112.37\) MeV.
- The best fit to the Scaling Model gives \(k = 3.16\) for the exponent in \(B/B_0 = (M_N^*/M_N)^k\).
- At a nucleon effective mass ratio \(M_N^*/M_N = 0.78\), the bag ratio is \(B/B_0 = 0.54\) and the radius ratio is \(R/R_0 = 1.12\) (set II, ZM model).
- For comparison, the DCM with \(\delta = 3.6\) and \(g_\sigma^q = 2\) gives \(B/B_0 = 0.36\) and \(R/R_0 = 1.27\) at \(M_N^*/M_N = 0.70\).

## Context
This work extends the earlier density-dependent bag model studies by Jin and Jennings (1995), who introduced the Direct Coupling Model and the Scaling Model for the bag constant. By imposing mechanical equilibrium between the bag and the surrounding hadronic matter, the author derives a simultaneous description from QHD and EQMC that is consistent with the Scaling Model over a broad density range. The results enable future calculations of the European Muon Collaboration (EMC) effect and nuclear matter bulk properties using the Zimanyi-Moszkowski model, which is noted as more adequate for describing such properties in the MFA.
