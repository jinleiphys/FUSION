# Has charge symmetry breaking been observed in the d d -> alpha pi0 reaction?

> Estimates are made of the d d -> alpha gamma gamma production cross sections in a model where each neutron-proton pair in the beam and target initiates an n p -> d gamma reaction. This approach, which successfully reproduces observables in two-pion production at intermediate energies, suggests that direct two-photon production could provide a very significant background to the measurement of the charge-symmetry-breaking (CSB) reaction d d -> alpha pi0. A non-vanishing CSB cross section has been reported which might be confused with such two-photon production under the given experimental conditions.

## Key claim

The reported non-vanishing cross section for the charge-symmetry-breaking (CSB) reaction \(dd \to \alpha\pi^0\) may be confused with direct two-photon production (\(dd \to \alpha\gamma\gamma\)) under the given experimental conditions, meaning the evidence for CSB must be treated with great caution rather than taken as confirmed.

## Method

The authors model the \(dd \to \alpha\gamma\gamma\) reaction by assuming each neutron-proton pair in the beam and target independently undergoes an \(np \to d\gamma\) reaction, with the two final deuterons fusing to form an \(\alpha\) particle. The input \(np \to d\gamma\) amplitudes are taken from phenomenological multipoles of Arenhövel, and the formalism follows that previously developed for two-pion production in the \(dd \to \alpha\pi^0\pi^0\) reaction. The predicted missing-mass distributions for the two-photon channel are then compared with the experimental acceptance and the claimed \(\pi^0\) signal from the Saturne experiment.

## Key numbers

- The claimed c.m. differential cross section for \(dd \to \alpha\pi^0\) at \(T_d = 1100\) MeV and \(\theta_{\alpha}^{\text{cm}} = 73^\circ\) is \((1.0 \pm 0.20 \pm 0.25)\) pb/sr, where the first error is statistical and the second systematic.
- The 15 candidate events attributed to \(\alpha\pi^0\) correspond to a cross section of \((1.1 \pm 0.30 \pm 0.20)\) pb/sr.
- The model predicts a c.m. cross section for \(dd \to \alpha\gamma\gamma\) of approximately 8.8 pb/sr when integrated over the experimental acceptance interval \(95 < m_X < 175\) MeV/\(c^2\) at \(\theta_{\alpha}^{\text{cm}} = 73^\circ\).
- At \(T_d = 800\) MeV, the double-differential cross section is estimated as \(d^2\sigma(\alpha\gamma\gamma)/d\Omega\,dm_{\gamma\gamma} \approx 0.11\) pb/(sr MeV/\(c^2\)).
- Near the \(\alpha\pi^0\) threshold (226 MeV), at 10 MeV above threshold in the c.m., the predicted integrated cross section is \(d\sigma(\alpha\gamma\gamma)/dm_{\gamma\gamma} = 3.4\) pb/(MeV/\(c^2\)).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous model for two-pion production in the \(dd \to \alpha\pi^0\pi^0\) reaction (Gardestig, Faldt, and Wilkin, 1998, 1999) to the two-photon channel, questioning the CSB interpretation of the Saturne experiment (Goldzahl et al., 1991). The results suggest that direct two-photon production provides a significant background that was not adequately accounted for, and that future experiments should be performed with better \(\pi^0\) mass resolution, ideally near the \(\alpha\pi^0\) threshold at 226 MeV, or using tensor-polarized deuterons or the angular distribution of the two photons in their rest frame to distinguish signal from background.
