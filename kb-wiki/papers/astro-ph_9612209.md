# The Electron-Screening Correction for the Proton-Proton Reaction

> We test the Salpeter formalism for the electron screening of the solar proton-proton fusion reaction by solving numerically the relevant Schrodinger equation. We evaluate exactly the square of the overlap integral of the two-proton wave function and the deuteron wave function and compare with the usual analytic approximation. The usual WKB solution agrees with the numerical result to $O(10^{-4})$. The WKB approximation should be even more precise for the other nuclear fusion reactions in the $pp$ chain and CNO cycles.

# The Electron-Screening Correction for the Proton-Proton Reaction

## Key claim

The standard WKB analytic treatment of electron screening for the solar proton-proton fusion reaction, originally formulated by Salpeter, agrees with a full numerical solution of the Schrödinger equation to O(10⁻⁴), contradicting a conjecture by Dar that the standard screening treatment could cause an error of order 5% in the proton-proton reaction rate in the Sun.

## Method

The authors solved the radial Schrödinger equation numerically for the two-proton system, first with a pure Coulomb potential and then with a Debye-Hückel screened Coulomb potential, using an exponential nuclear potential that reproduces the measured scattering length and effective range. They evaluated the overlap integral of the two-proton wave function with the McGee deuteron wave function for both potentials, then squared the ratio of the two overlap integrals to determine the screening correction to the cross section. The numerical results were compared with the analytic WKB approximation of Salpeter (1954).

## Key numbers

- The fractional difference between the numerical and WKB screening corrections is O(10⁻⁴) at the relative momentum where the pp reaction occurs (k ≃ 0.016 fm⁻¹).
- For the pp reaction in the solar core, the small parameter x₀ ≃ 0.01, justifying the small-x expansion used in the WKB approximation.
- The Debye radius is given by r_D = ζ⁻¹/² (k_B T a / e²)¹/² a, where a = ρ⁻¹/³ (0.51 × 10⁻⁸ cm) is a measure of interparticle distance.
- The most probable energy of interaction is E₀ = 1.2204 (Z₁² Z₂² A T₆²)¹/³ keV, where T₆ is temperature in units of 10⁶ K.
- The Salpeter enhancement factor is f₀ = exp(0.188 Z₁ Z₂ ζ ρ¹/² T₆⁻³/²).

## Context

This work tests the Salpeter (1954) formalism for electron screening of nuclear fusion reactions, which had been questioned by Dar and Shaviv (1996) who argued that a different screening correction might significantly reduce the discrepancy between observed and predicted solar neutrino fluxes. The numerical verification confirms that the standard WKB treatment is accurate to O(10⁻⁴) for the pp reaction, and the authors argue the approximation should be even more precise for other fusion reactions in the pp chain and CNO cycles. The paper also derives a small correction to Salpeter's result in an appendix, finding it negligibly small (∼0.1%).
