# Solar-neutrino reactions on deuteron in effective field theory

> The cross sections for low-energy neutrino-deuteron reactions are calculated within heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory employing cut-off regularization scheme. The transition operators are derived up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order in the Weinberg counting rules, while the nuclear matrix elements are evaluated using the wave functions generated by a high-quality phenomenological NN potential. With the adoption of the axial-current-four-nucleon coupling constant fixed from the tritium beta decay data, our calculation is free from unknown low-energy constants. Our results exhibit a high degree of stability against different choices of the cutoff parameter, a feature which indicates that, apart from radiative corrections, the uncertainties in the calculated cross sections are less than 1 %.

## Key claim

The cross sections for low-energy neutrino-deuteron reactions are calculated within heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order, with the axial-current-four-nucleon coupling constant fixed from tritium beta decay data, making the calculation free from unknown low-energy constants. The authors claim that, apart from radiative corrections, the uncertainties in the calculated cross sections are less than 1%, a claim that depends on the specific cutoff regularization scheme and the use of phenomenological nuclear wave functions.

## Method

The authors employed heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT) to derive transition operators up to N³LO in Weinberg's counting scheme, using a cut-off regularization scheme with a Gaussian cutoff function. The nuclear matrix elements were evaluated by sandwiching these EFT-controlled transition operators between nuclear wave functions obtained from solving the Schrödinger equation with the Argonne V18 phenomenological NN potential. The unknown low-energy constant d̂^R, which controls the axial-current-four-nucleon contact coupling, was fixed by reproducing the experimental tritium beta-decay rate, and the calculation was limited to the final two-nucleon s-wave (¹S₀) state.

## Key numbers

- For the CC reaction νd → e⁻pp at Eν = 20 MeV, the total cross section σ^{L=0}_{νd} = 15.13 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (with Λ = 600 MeV cutoff).
- For the NC reaction νd → νnp at Eν = 20 MeV, the total cross section σ^{L=0}_{νd} = 6.830 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (with Λ = 600 MeV cutoff).
- The calculated σ^{L=0}_{νd} exhibits only 0.02% variation over a cutoff range of Λ = 500–800 MeV.
- The uncertainty in the ratio ξ due to experimental errors in the tritium beta-decay rate Γᵦᵗ amounts to approximately 0.5%.
- The N³LO two-body operators give a correction of approximately 2.9% for the CC reaction at Eν = 20 MeV, comparable to (mπ/Λ)³ ≈ 1.2%.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to neutrino-deuteron reactions previously applied by Butler, Chen, and Kong (2001), who used the power divergence subtraction scheme and had to adjust one unknown low-energy constant (L₁A) to fit the standard nuclear physics approach results of Nakamura, Sato, Gudkov, and Kubodera (2001). The present calculation achieves a parameter-free determination by fixing the contact coupling from tritium beta-decay data, following the methodology developed for the solar hep process and pp fusion reactions by Park et al. (2001). The results agree within 1% with the most recent standard nuclear physics approach calculation by Nakamura et al. (2002), and the authors suggest that a future "direct" EFT approach (where nuclear wave functions are also obtained within EFT) could provide an independent verification.
