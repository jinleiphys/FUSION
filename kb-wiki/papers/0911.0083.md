# Radiative decay of $K^-p$ system and photoproduction of $Λ(1405)$

> The properties of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance have been investigated from radiative decay of $K^- p\to Yγ$ and photoproduction $γp\to K^+Λ(1405)$ within the framework of the isobar model. For a consistent result with recently measured branching ratios, the axial vector meson $K_1(1270)$ is taken into account. Strong and electromagnetic coupling constants of $Λ(1405)$ are extracted from these branching ratios and are applied to the analysis of $K^+Λ(1405)$ photoproduction. The total and differential cross sections are predicted.

## Key claim

The isobar model with the inclusion of the axial vector meson $K_1(1270)$ exchange can reproduce the recently measured branching ratios for $K^-p\to \Lambda\gamma$ and $K^-p\to \Sigma^0\gamma$, but the extracted coupling constants for $\Lambda(1405)$ are sensitive to the choice of pseudoscalar (PS) versus pseudovector (PV) coupling scheme and the specific value of the magnetic coupling $\kappa_{\Lambda\Lambda_{1405}}$.

## Method

The authors analyzed radiative kaon capture $K^-p\to Y\gamma$ ($Y=\Lambda,\Sigma^0$) and photoproduction $\gamma p\to K^+\Lambda(1405)$ within the isobar model, using t-channel $K_1(1270)$ exchange in addition to Born terms and $K^*$ exchange. Strong and electromagnetic coupling constants of $\Lambda(1405)$ were extracted from the branching ratio data and then applied to predict total and differential cross sections for photoproduction, with s-channel $N^*(1650)$ and $N^*(1710)$ and u-channel $\Lambda(1405)$ exchanges included.

## Key numbers

- Branching ratio $R_{\Lambda\gamma} = (0.86\pm0.07^{+0.10}_{-0.08})\times 10^{-3}$ from the Brookhaven experiment
- Branching ratio $R_{\Sigma^0\gamma} = (1.44\pm0.20^{+0.12}_{-0.10})\times 10^{-3}$ from the Brookhaven experiment
- Previous experimental values: $R_{\Lambda\gamma}=(2.8\pm 0.8)\times 10^{-3}$ and $R_{\Sigma^0\gamma}\leq 4\times 10^{-3}$
- Extracted magnetic coupling $\kappa_{\Lambda\Lambda_{1405}}=-0.34$ (PS scheme) or $\kappa_{\Lambda\Lambda_{1405}}=(-0.38, -0.23)$ (PV scheme) to reproduce $R_{\Lambda\gamma}=0.86$
- Strong coupling constant $g_{Kp\Lambda_{1405}}=3.2$ used for branching ratio calculation
- Pseudopotential of the $K^-p$ system $W_p=(560\pm 135)$ MeV fm$^3$
- Cutoff parameters: 1.2 GeV for non-resonant Born terms, 1.8 GeV for resonances
- Photoproduction cross section magnitude is of order micro barn

## Context

This work extends the isobar model analysis of radiative kaon capture by Burkhardt and Lowe (WF model) and the Type I model of Lowe et al. (2002), which could not reproduce the improved Brookhaven branching ratio measurements. The inclusion of $K_1(1270)$ exchange, motivated by parity and angular momentum conservation, resolves this discrepancy. The extracted coupling constants are then applied to predict $\gamma p\to K^+\Lambda(1405)$ cross sections, which are very scarce experimentally; these predictions are intended to guide future experiments. The authors note that the unitary coupled-channel approach of Ramos and Oset (2000) overestimates the $\Lambda\gamma$ branching ratio ($R_{\Lambda\gamma}=1.58$ without cutoff), indicating the need for further theoretical corrections.
