# Diffraction on nuclei: effects of nucleon-nucleon correlations and inelastic shadowing within an improved Glauber-Gribov approach

> The importance of the effects of nucleon-nucleon (NN) short-range correlations (SRC) and Gribov inelastic shadowing (IS) on various high energy scattering processes involving nuclear targets is demonstrated within an improved Glauber-Gribov approach.

## Key claim

Within an improved Glauber-Gribov approach, nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations (SRC) and Gribov inelastic shadowing (IS) both affect high-energy scattering on nuclear targets, but they act in opposite directions: SRC increase nuclear opacity (making the nucleus appear thicker), while Gribov IS increases nuclear transparency.

## Method

The author and collaborators extended the standard Glauber multiple-scattering formalism by including the two-body correlation function (the "two-body contraction") in the expansion of the squared nuclear wave function, and by summing Gribov inelastic shadowing contributions to all orders using a light-cone dipole approach. Realistic one- and two-body nuclear densities and correlation functions from Alvioli et al. (2008) were used to compute total, elastic, quasi-elastic, inelastic, and diffractive dissociation cross sections for hadron-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus scattering at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

* Total neutron-nucleus cross section for p-208Pb at LHC energies: Glauber alone gives 3850.63 mb; adding SRC increases it to 3885.77 mb; adding both SRC and Gribov IS (q-2q model) gives 3833.26 mb.
* Elastic cross section for p-208Pb at LHC: Glauber alone 1664.76 mb; Glauber+SRC 1690.48 mb; Glauber+SRC+Gribov IS (q-2q model) 1655.70 mb.
* Quasi-elastic cross section for p-208Pb at LHC: Glauber alone 120.92 mb; Glauber+SRC 112.65 mb; Glauber+SRC+Gribov IS (q-2q model) 113.37 mb.
* Number of inelastic collisions N_coll for p-208Pb at RHIC (sigma_in^NN = 42.10 mb): Glauber 4.70; Glauber+SRC 4.65; Glauber+SRC+Gribov IS (q-2q model) 4.75.
* Number of inelastic collisions N_coll for p-208Pb at LHC (sigma_in^NN = 68.30 mb): Glauber 6.88; Glauber+SRC 6.82; Glauber+SRC+Gribov IS (q-2q model) 6.88.

## Context

This work extends the standard Glauber multiple scattering theory (Glauber, 1959) by incorporating short-range correlations from realistic many-body calculations (Pieper, Wiringa, and Pandharipande, 1992) and Gribov inelastic shadowing (Gribov, 1969), which were previously treated separately. The results build on a series of recent papers by Alvioli, Ciofi degli Atti, Kopeliovich, and collaborators (2008-2011) that systematically studied these effects in hadron-nucleus and deuteron-gold scattering. The approach enables more accurate calculations of nuclear cross sections and the number of inelastic collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, with calculations of SRC effects in heavy ion (208Pb-208Pb) collisions reported as in progress.
