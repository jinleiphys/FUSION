# Quark Recombination

> Hadronization is a fundamental process occurring at a distance scale of about $1\,\rm fm \simeq Λ_{QCD}^{-1} $, hence within non-perturbative dynamics. In elementary collisions, like $e^+e^-$, $e^-p$, or $pp$, phenomenological approaches to hadronization have been developed based on vacuum-like dynamics that require the creation of quark-antiquark and/or diquark pairs during the hadronization process. In the 2000s, the idea was developed that in ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus (AA) collisions, which lead to the formation of a partonic medium with large (anti-)quark densities, hadronization can occur through the recombination of in-medium quarks, unlike the situation in $e^+e^-$, $e^-p$, and $pp$. We give an overview of the main features that characterize quark recombination and have enabled a description of several important experimental observables at both RHIC and LHC over the last two decades. We highlight some additional developments and open issues. We specifically discuss the impact of coalescence on the study of heavy-flavor hadronization, including recent developments showing signatures of (the onset of) quark coalescence even in $pp$ collisions at TeV energies. Furthermore, we highlight specific features of hadronization for quarkonium in AA collisions, where it has been possible to develop a dynamical kinetic approach that allows to extract more detailed information about the temperature dependence of the heavy-quark interaction in hot QCD matter.

# Quark Recombination

## Key claim

Quark recombination (coalescence) provides a dominant hadronization mechanism in ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions where high parton densities enable direct formation of hadrons from in-medium quarks, but the paper also presents evidence that signatures of quark coalescence may appear even in proton-proton collisions at TeV energies, particularly in heavy-flavor hadron production, challenging the traditional view that hadronization in small collision systems proceeds exclusively through vacuum-like fragmentation.

## Method

The authors review theoretical approaches to quark recombination across collision systems, including the Wigner-function coalescence formalism (Eqs. 2-3) where hadron spectra are computed as convolutions of quark distribution functions with hadron Wigner functions, and the Resonance Recombination Model (RRM) derived from Boltzmann equations where hadronization proceeds through resonant scattering of quarks into Breit-Wigner meson states with finite widths. For heavy-flavor hadrons, coalescence models are combined with parton transport calculations to compute observables such as nuclear modification factors (R_AA), elliptic flow (v_2), and baryon-to-meson ratios, with comparisons to experimental data from RHIC and LHC.

## Key numbers

- The pseudo-critical temperature for the QCD crossover transition at vanishing baryon chemical potential is 155-160 MeV, as determined by lattice-QCD computations
- The string tension κ is approximately 1 GeV/fm, governing the probability of quark-antiquark pair creation via the Schwinger mechanism exp(-π m²_⟂q/κ)
- The fraction of charm quarks hadronizing into Λ_c in e⁺e⁻ and e⁻p collisions is f(c→Λ_c) ≈ 0.06, while in pp collisions at TeV energies the Λ_c/D⁰ ratio reaches up to ~0.6 at p_T ≈ 1.5 GeV/c
- The Λ_c/D⁰ ratio in AA collisions at RHIC and LHC energies is predicted to be ~1 at intermediate p_T, with a moderate decrease at LHC energies
- The statistical hadronization model with excited charm baryon states beyond PDG listings predicts Λ_c/D⁰ = 0.45-0.57 at hadronization temperature T_H = 160-170 MeV
- The elliptic flow of D mesons from recombination with light quarks was estimated at 5% (assuming vanishing charm v_2) up to 15-20% (assuming charm quarks flowing with the QGP medium) at intermediate p_T in semi-central RHIC collisions

## Context

This review extends the quark recombination framework developed in the early 2000s by Greco (2003), Fries (2003), and Molnar (2003), which successfully explained the enhanced proton-to-pion ratio and quark-number scaling of elliptic flow at RHIC. The paper connects these ideas to the ALCOR model of Biro (1994) and the statistical hadronization model of Braun-Munzinger (2003), while addressing the long-standing criticism of energy conservation violation in coalescence through the RRM approach of Ravagli (2007). The recent observation of large Λ_c/D⁰ ratios in pp collisions by ALICE (2021) challenges standard PYTHIA string fragmentation and motivates extensions such as PYTHIA color-reconnection (Christiansen, 2015) and the inclusion of missing excited charm baryon states in the statistical hadronization model (He, 2019). The review enables future work on extracting the temperature dependence of heavy-quark interactions in hot QCD matter through dynamical kinetic approaches to quarkonium hadronization.
