# Physical mechanism of the (tri)critical point generation

> We discuss some ideas resulting from a phenomenological relation recently declared between the tension of string connecting the static quark-antiquark pair and surface tension of corresponding cylindrical bag. This relation analysis leads to the temperature of vanishing surface tension coefficient of the QGP bags at zero baryonic charge density as T_σ= 152.9 +- 4.5 MeV. We develop the view point that this temperature value is not a fortuitous coincidence with the temperature of (partial) chiral symmetry restoration as seen in the lattice QCD simulations. Besides, we argue that T_σdefines the QCD (tri)critical endpoint temperature and claim that a negative value of surface tension coefficient recently discovered is not a sole result, but should also exist in ordinary liquids at the supercritical temperatures.

## Key claim

The temperature at which the bag surface tension coefficient vanishes, \(T_{\sigma} = 152.9 \pm 4.5\) MeV at zero baryonic charge density, is not a coincidence with the (partial) chiral symmetry restoration temperature from lattice QCD; rather, this temperature defines the QCD (tri)critical endpoint temperature, and negative surface tension coefficients should also exist in ordinary liquids at supercritical temperatures.

## Method

The authors equate the free energy of a static quark-antiquark pair connected by an unbreakable color tube (string tension \(\sigma_{str}\)) with the free energy of an elongated cylindrical bag parameterized by bulk pressure, surface tension, and a Fisher topological term. From this equality they derive a relation \(\sigma_{surf}(T) = \sigma_{str}(T)/(2\pi R) + \frac{1}{2} p_v(T) R\), which allows them to compute the bag surface tension at zero temperature using lattice QCD values for string tension and bag radius. They then use the Hills and Dales Model (HDM) and Fisher droplet model to relate the zero-temperature surface tension to the temperature \(T_{\sigma}\) where the surface tension coefficient changes sign.

## Key numbers

- Temperature of vanishing surface tension coefficient of QGP bags at zero baryonic charge density: \(T_{\sigma} = 152.9 \pm 4.5\) MeV.
- Bag surface tension at zero temperature: \(\sigma_{surf}(T=0) \approx (0.183\ \text{GeV})^3 \approx 157.4\ \text{MeV fm}^{-2}\), derived using lattice QCD values \(R = 0.5\) fm and \(\sigma_{str}(T=0) = (0.42)^2\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- Range for \(T_{\sigma}\) from the HDM parameterization: \([148.4;\ 157.4]\) MeV.
- (Partial) chiral symmetry restoration temperature from the Wuppertal-Budapest group: between \(146 \pm 2 \pm 3\) and \(152 \pm 3 \pm 3\) MeV.
- Cross-over temperature from the Wuppertal-Budapest group: \(T_{co} = 170 \pm 4 \pm 3\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological relation between string tension and bag surface tension introduced in Bugaev et al. (2009) and connects it to the lattice QCD results of the Wuppertal-Budapest group (2009) on chiral symmetry restoration. It builds on the Fisher droplet model (Fisher, 1967), the Hills and Dales Model (Bugaev, 2004, 2005, 2010), and the quark gluon bags with surface tension models (QGBSTM, QGBSTMb, QGBSTM2, QGBSTM2b) to argue that the vanishing surface tension coefficient defines the QCD (tri)critical endpoint. The work enables future lattice QCD simulations to verify the critical endpoint temperature using the derived relation between string tension and surface tension, and suggests that negative surface tension at supercritical temperatures should be investigated in ordinary liquids.
