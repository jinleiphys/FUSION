# Gamma-radiation in non-Markovian Fermi systems

> The gamma-quanta emission is considered within the framework of the non--Markovian kinetic theory. It is shown that the memory effects have a strong influence on the spectral distribution of gamma-quanta in the case of long-time relaxation regime. It is shown that the gamma-radiation can be used as a probe for both the time-reversible hindrance force and the dissipative friction caused by the memory integral.

# Gamma-radiation in non-Markovian Fermi systems

## Key claim
Memory effects in the collision integral strongly influence the spectral distribution of gamma-quanta emitted from heated Fermi systems, but only in the long-time relaxation regime; the gamma-radiation spectrum can serve as a probe for both the time-reversible hindrance force and the dissipative friction caused by the memory integral, provided the relaxation parameter beta is sufficiently large (beta >= 0.5 MeV).

## Method
The authors derive a Langevin equation for shape fluctuations of a Fermi-liquid drop starting from the collisional Landau-Vlasov kinetic equation with a non-Markovian collision integral, including dynamic Fermi-surface distortion up to multipolarity l=2. They then apply the fluctuation-dissipation theorem to relate the random force correlation function to the friction coefficient, and compute the spectral density of electromagnetic radiation from the fluctuating charge density using the classical multipole radiation formula with quantum fluctuations incorporated via the factor E(omega,T) = (hbar omega/2) coth(hbar omega/2T).

## Key numbers
- Relaxation parameter beta = 0.8 MeV is adopted from fitting the isoscalar Giant Quadrupole Resonance (GQR) eigenenergies and widths across the periodic table at T=0.
- The limiting temperature for the liquid-drop stiffness coefficient C_L^(LD) to vanish is T_lim^(LD) = 7.7 MeV for quadrupole deformation (L=2) in ^208Pb.
- The surface energy coefficient is given by b_S = 17.2 * [(T_C^2 - T^2)/(T_C^2 + T^2)]^(5/4) MeV with critical temperature T_C = 18 MeV.
- The Coulomb coefficient is b_C ≈ 0.55 (1 - x_C T^2) MeV with x_C = 0.76 × 10^(-3) MeV^(-2).
- The relaxation time is tau(omega,T) = (4 pi^2 beta hbar) / [(hbar omega)^2 + zeta T^2] with zeta = 4 pi^2 in the Landau prescription; beta ranges between 2.4 and 19.3 from different in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross-section assumptions in prior work.
- The one-body dissipation wall formula gives Gamma_L,one-body ≈ 34.3 L A^(-1/3) MeV; the modified wall formula gives Gamma_L,modif ≈ 73.9 (L-1)^2 L A^(-1) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the non-Markovian kinetic theory of Fermi liquids developed by Abrikosov and Khalatnikov (1959) and Koshcheev (2004) to the problem of gamma-radiation from heated nuclei. It contrasts with the traditional liquid-drop model (Bohr and Mottelson, 1975), which cannot describe GQR energies, and with the one-body dissipation wall-formula approaches of Nix and Sierk (1980) and Blocki and Bodmer (1978), which overestimate or underestimate GQR widths respectively. The results enable using gamma-spectra as experimental probes of the memory-dependent friction coefficient and the relaxation parameter beta, particularly for temperatures above the liquid-drop stability limit where Fermi-surface distortion provides the sole restoring force.
