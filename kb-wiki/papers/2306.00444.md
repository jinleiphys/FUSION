# Chiral Transport Phenomena and Compact Stars

> I will review the main chiral transport phemomena arising in systems made up of (almost) massless fermions associated to the quantum chiral anomaly. These quantum effects might have relevant implications in compact stars, and I will review some relevant works that reveal so. I will also show how a conservation law that has the same form of the chiral anomaly also emerge in perfect classical fluids, which expresses a conservation law of magnetic, fluid and mixed helicities for isentropic fluids, and why this should also be relevant in compact stars.

# Chiral Transport Phenomena and Compact Stars

## Key claim
Chiral transport phenomena arising from the quantum chiral anomaly may have relevant implications for compact stars, but most discussions remain qualitative and based on order-of-magnitude estimates, with much more work needed in this area. Additionally, a classical conservation law analogous to the chiral anomaly equation emerges for isentropic perfect fluids, involving magnetic, fluid, and mixed helicities, which should also be relevant for compact star physics.

## Method
The paper reviews the chiral magnetic effect (CME) and chiral vortical effect (CVE) as dissipationless transport phenomena arising from the quantum chiral anomaly in systems of massless or quasi-massless fermions. It derives the chiral anomaly equations in the presence of chemical potentials and fluid velocity, showing how CME and CVE currents emerge, and discusses chiral kinetic theory (CKT) as a semiclassical framework incorporating Berry curvature corrections to classical transport equations. The author then applies these concepts to compact star scenarios, including magnetic field generation in proto-neutron stars, chiral density generation in pulsar magnetospheres, and pulsar kick mechanisms, before presenting a new helicity conservation law derived from relativistic hydrodynamics for isentropic fluids.

## Key numbers
- The chiral chemical potential in a proto-neutron star could be close to the QCD scale, potentially creating magnetic fields of order B ~ 10^18 G, but later analysis suggests more realistic values of B ~ 10^14 G for high temperature conditions where μ_e >> T.
- The chiral magnetic instability occurs for field wavelengths with k ~ e^2 μ_5, with growth timescale ~ 1/e^4 μ_5 in an electromagnetic plasma and t_ins ~ σ/k e^2 μ_5 in a conductor with electrical conductivity σ.
- The electron chemical potential in a proto-neutron star is μ_e ~ 100 MeV, making electrons quasi-massless in this context.

## Context
This review extends the line of work on chiral transport phenomena from heavy-ion collisions and condensed matter (Weyl/Dirac semimetals) to astrophysical settings, particularly compact stars. It builds on foundational works on the chiral magnetic effect (Fukushima 2008), chiral hydrodynamics (Son and Surowka 2009), and chiral kinetic theory (Son and Yamamoto 2012; Stephanov and Yin 2012; Chen et al. 2012). The paper contrasts the extensive use of the chiral anomaly in cosmology for baryogenesis and primordial magnetic field generation (Joyce and Shaposhnikov 1997; Boyarsky et al. 2011) with the relatively fewer and more qualitative studies in compact star physics. The new helicity conservation law for classical barotropic fluids (Abanov and Wiegmann 2021; Wiegmann and Abanov 2022; Manuel and Torres-Rincon 2022) provides a classical analogue to the quantum chiral anomaly that may require revising previous estimates that assumed the fluid is at rest.
