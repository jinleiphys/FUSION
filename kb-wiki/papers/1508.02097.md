# Partial restoration of isospin symmetry for neutrinoless double beta decay in the deformed nuclear system of $^{150}$Nd

> In this work, we calculate the matrix elements of $0νββ$-decay of $^{150}$Nd with the deformed pn-QRPA method. We adopted the approach introduced by Rodin, Faessler [Phys. Rev. C84, 014322 (2011)] and Simkovic {\it et. al.} [Phys. Rev. C87,045501(2013)] to restore the isospin symmetry by enforcing $M^{2ν}_F=0$. We found that with this restoration, the Fermi matrix elements are reduced by about 30\% while the more important Gamow-Teller matrix elements remains the same. The results of an enlarged model space is also presented, which changes the matrix elements by less than 10\%.

## Key claim

The authors find that enforcing isospin symmetry by setting the two-neutrino double-beta-decay Fermi matrix element to zero reduces the neutrinoless double-beta-decay Fermi matrix elements for the strongly deformed nucleus \(^{150}\)Nd by about 15 to 20%, while the Gamow-Teller matrix elements remain unchanged; this reduction is smaller than the 30-40% reduction previously seen in spherical nuclei.

## Method

The authors use the deformed proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) with a realistic CD-Bonn G-matrix interaction. They restore isospin symmetry by separating the particle-particle residual interaction into T=0 and T=1 components, fitting \(g_{pp}^{T=0}\) to the experimental two-neutrino double-beta-decay Gamow-Teller matrix element and \(g_{pp}^{T=1}\) to enforce \(M_F^{2\nu}=0\). Calculations are performed in two model spaces: a small space of major shells N=4-6 and a larger space N=0-7, with deformation parameters \(\beta_2=0.240\) for \(^{150}\)Nd and \(\beta_2=0.153\) for \(^{150}\)Sm.

## Key numbers

* The Fermi matrix element \(M_F^{0\nu}\) is reduced by about 15 to 20% when isospin symmetry restoration (ISR) is applied, with the largest reduction for \(K^\pi = 0^+\) where it drops to about 1/3 of its original value.
* In the small model space (N=4-6) with \(g_A=1.27\) and Brueckner short-range correlations (src), the total matrix element \(M'^{0\nu}\) is 3.062 with ISR and 3.233 without ISR, a reduction of less than 10%.
* Enlarging the model space from N=4-6 to N=0-7 increases the total matrix element \(M'^{0\nu}\) from 3.062 to 3.367 (with ISR, \(g_A=1.27\), src), an enhancement of about 10%.
* The BCS overlap between initial and final nuclei is taken as 0.52.
* The pairing strengths in the large model space are \(d_{pp}=0.94\) and \(d_{nn}=1.03\) for \(^{150}\)Nd, and \(d_{pp}=0.95\) and \(d_{nn}=1.04\) for \(^{150}\)Sm, giving an average about 5% smaller than \(g_{pp}^{T=1}=1.06\).

## Context

This work extends the isospin restoration formalism developed by Rodin and Faessler (2011) and Simkovic et al. (2013) from spherical nuclei to the strongly deformed system \(^{150}\)Nd. It updates the previous QRPA calculation by Fang, Faessler, and Simkovic (2011) which used equal T=0 and T=1 particle-particle strengths and did not enforce isospin conservation. The results are compared with other methods including Projected HFB (PHFB), Interacting Boson Model (IBM-2), and two Energy Density Functional approaches (NREDF and REDF), which show a spread in matrix elements of a factor of three or more, indicating the need for further cross-method comparisons.
