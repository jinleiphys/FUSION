# Relativistic $<σv_\text{rel}>$ in the calculation of relics abundances: a closer look

> In this paper we clarify the relation between the invariant relativistic relative velocity $V_\text{r}$, the Møller velocity $\bar{v}$, and the non-relativistic relative velocity $v_r$. Adopting $V_{\text{r}}$ as the true physical relative velocity for pair-collisions in a non-degenerate relativistic gas, we show that in the frame co-moving with the gas (i) the thermally averaged cross section times relative velocity $<σv_\text{rel}>$ that appears in the density evolution equation for thermal relics is reformulated only in terms of $V_\text{r}$ and $\mathcal{P}(V_{\text{r}})$ in a manifestly Lorentz invariant form; (ii) the frame-dependent issues of the standard formulation in terms of the Møller velocity, as well as "superluminal" relative velocities, are not present in this formulation. Furthermore, considering the annihilation of dark matter into a particle-antiparticle pair $f\bar{f}$, in the cases $m_f=0$, $m_f=m$ and $m_f \gg m$, we find that the coefficients of the low velocity expansion of $<σV_{\text{r}}>$ admit an exact analytical representation in terms of the Meijer $G$ functions that can be reduced to combinations of modified Bessel functions of the second kind.

# Relativistic $\langle \sigma v_\text{rel}\rangle$ in the calculation of relics abundances: a closer look

## Key claim

The paper argues that the invariant relativistic relative velocity $V_\text{r}$, not the Møller velocity $\bar{v}$ nor the non-relativistic relative velocity $v_r$, is the correct physical relative velocity for pair-collisions in a non-degenerate relativistic gas; using $V_\text{r}$ eliminates the frame-dependence issues and "superluminal" velocity paradoxes present in the standard formulation, while the thermally averaged cross section times relative velocity $\langle \sigma v_\text{rel}\rangle$ can be expressed solely in terms of $V_\text{r}$ and its probability distribution $\mathcal{P}(V_\text{r})$ in a manifestly Lorentz invariant form.

## Method

The author reformulates the relativistic Boltzmann equation collision term using the Lorentz-invariant relative velocity $V_\text{r}$ and the Jüttner distribution for a non-degenerate relativistic gas in equilibrium. The probability density function $\mathcal{P}(V_\text{r})$ for $V_\text{r}$ is derived, and the thermal average $\langle \sigma V_\text{r} \rangle$ is expressed as an integral over this distribution, analogous to the non-relativistic Maxwell-Boltzmann average. For dark matter annihilation into $f\bar{f}$, the low-velocity expansion coefficients of $\langle \sigma V_\text{r} \rangle$ are computed analytically using Meijer $G$ functions and reduced to modified Bessel functions for the cases $m_f=0$, $m_f=m$, and $m_f \gg m$.

## Key numbers

- For $x = m/T$ between 20 and 40 (typical for WIMP freeze-out), the mean relativistic relative velocity $\langle V_\text{r} \rangle$ is always smaller than the non-relativistic Maxwell value $4/\sqrt{\pi x}$, as shown in Fig. 1.
- For constant cross section $\sigma = k$, the exact mean relative velocity is $\langle V_\text{r} \rangle = (4/x) K_3(2x)/K_2^2(x)$.
- For $s$-wave scattering ($n=0$) with $\rho = m_f^2/m^2 = 0$: $\mathcal{K}^{\rho=0}_{n=0}(x) = K_1^2(x)/K_2^2(x)$.
- For $s$-wave scattering with $\rho = 1$: $\mathcal{K}^{\rho=1}_{n=0}(x) = (2/x) K_1(2x)/K_2^2(x)$.
- For $s$-wave scattering with $\rho \gg 1$: $\mathcal{K}^{\rho\gg 1}_{n=0}(x) = \rho K_1^2(\sqrt{\rho} x)/K_2^2(x)$.
- For $p$-wave scattering ($n=1$) with $\rho = 0$: $\mathcal{K}^{\rho=0}_{n=1}(x) = \frac{1}{2}(1 - K_1^2(x)/K_2^2(x))$.
- For $p$-wave scattering with $\rho = 1$: $\mathcal{K}^{\rho=1}_{n=1}(x) = (4/x^2) K_2(2x)/K_2^2(x)$.
- For $p$-wave scattering with $\rho \gg 1$: $\mathcal{K}^{\rho\gg 1}_{n=1}(x) = (\rho^2/2) [K_1^2(\sqrt{\rho} x) + K_2^2(\sqrt{\rho} x)]/K_2^2(x)$.
- The low-velocity expansion for $\rho=0$ reproduces the result of Srednicki, Watkins and Olive (1988) up to $\mathcal{O}(x^{-4})$, e.g., $\langle \sigma V_\text{r} \rangle = \sigma_0^{(0)} + (-3\sigma_0^{(0)} + \frac{3}{2}\sigma_0^{(1)})/x + \dots$.
- For $\rho \gg 1$, the large-$x$ expansion shows exponential suppression: $\langle \sigma V_\text{r} \rangle_{\rho\gg 1} \approx e^{-2x(\sqrt{\rho}-1)}[\sqrt{\rho}(\sigma_0^{(0)} + \rho \sigma_0^{(1)}) + \mathcal{O}(x^{-1})]$.

## Context

This work extends the foundational treatment of thermal relics by Gondolo and Gelmini (1991), who derived the standard single-integral formula for $\langle \sigma \bar{v} \rangle$ using the Møller velocity, and by Srednicki, Watkins and Olive (1988), who provided the non-relativistic low-velocity expansion. The paper resolves conceptual problems in those approaches: the Møller velocity is not Lorentz invariant and can exceed the speed of light in the center-of-mass frame, while the non-relativistic relative velocity $v_r$ also takes unphysical values. By adopting $V_\text{r}$ as the true relativistic relative velocity and deriving its probability distribution, the author provides a manifestly Lorentz-invariant formulation that avoids frame-dependent issues. The exact analytical expressions for the expansion coefficients in terms of Meijer $G$ functions and Bessel functions enable more precise calculations of relic abundances for dark matter models, particularly when relativistic corrections are important at freeze-out temperatures where $x \sim 25$ and thermal velocities $v \sim 0.35$.
