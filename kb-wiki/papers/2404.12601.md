# Study of bottom quark dynamics via non-prompt $D^0$ and $J/ψ$ in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=5.02$ TeV

> We study bottom quark energy loss via the nuclear modification factor ($R_\mathrm{AA}$) and elliptic flow ($v_2$) of non-prompt $D^0$ and $J/ψ$ in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at the LHC. The space-time profile of quark-gluon plasma is obtained from the CLVisc hydrodynamics simulation, the dynamical evolution of heavy quarks inside the color deconfined QCD medium is simulated using a linear Boltzmann transport model that combines Yukawa and string potentials of heavy-quark-medium interactions, the hadronization of heavy quarks is performed using a hybrid coalescence-fragmentation model, and the decay of $B$ mesons is simulated via PYTHIA. Using this numerical framework, we calculate the transverse momentum ($p_\mathrm{T}$) dependent $R_\mathrm{AA}$ and $v_2$ of direct $D$ mesons, $B$ mesons, and non-prompt $D^0$ and $J/ψ$ from $B$ meson decay in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=5.02$ TeV. We find the mass hierarchy of the nuclear modification of prompt $D$ and $B$ mesons depends on their $p_\mathrm{T}$. Both $R_\mathrm{AA}$ and $v_2$ of heavy flavor particles show strong $p_\mathrm{T}$ and centrality dependences due to the interplay between parton energy loss, medium geometry and flow, and hadronization of heavy quarks. Non-prompt $D^0$ and $J/ψ$ share similar patterns of $R_\mathrm{AA}$ and $v_2$ to $B$ mesons except for a $p_\mathrm{T}$ shift during the decay processes. Therefore, future more precise measurements on non-prompt $D^0$ and $J/ψ$ can help further pin down the bottom quark dynamics inside the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The mass hierarchy of nuclear modification between prompt D and B mesons depends on their transverse momentum, with bottom quarks losing less energy than charm quarks at low pT but potentially more energy at high pT due to the interplay between the dead cone effect and non-perturbative string interactions in the LBT-PNP model.

## Method

The space-time evolution of the quark-gluon plasma was obtained from the (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model CLVisc with specific shear viscosity eta/s = 0.16. Heavy quark propagation through the QGP was simulated using a linear Boltzmann transport model that combines Yukawa (perturbative) and string (non-perturbative) potentials for elastic scatterings, plus a higher-twist formalism for inelastic gluon emissions. Hadronization of heavy quarks was performed using a hybrid coalescence-fragmentation model, and B meson decays into non-prompt D0 and J/psi were simulated via PYTHIA.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass taken as M_c = 1.27 GeV; bottom quark mass taken as M_b = 4.19 GeV
- Initial proper time of hydrodynamic evolution: tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c
- QGP freeze-out temperature: T_c = 165 MeV
- Specific shear viscosity of QGP: eta/s = 0.16
- Coupling strength for running coupling: Lambda = 0.2 GeV
- Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV

## Context

This work extends the LBT-PNP model previously developed by Xing et al. (2021) for D meson observables at RHIC and LHC to bottom quark observables, specifically non-prompt D0 and J/psi from B meson decays. It provides theoretical predictions that complement direct B meson measurements from ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS collaborations, which currently have large uncertainties. The results enable future more precise measurements of non-prompt D0 and J/psi to further constrain bottom quark dynamics inside the quark-gluon plasma, particularly the mass dependence of energy loss mechanisms.
