# Theoretical estimate on tensor-polarization asymmetry in proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process

> Tensor-polarized parton distribution functions are new quantities in spin-one hadrons such as the deuteron, and they could probe new quark-gluon dynamics in hadron and nuclear physics. In charged-lepton deep inelastic scattering (DIS), they are studied by the twist-two structure functions $b_1$ and $b_2$. The HERMES collaboration found unexpectedly large $b_1$ values than a naive theoretical expectation based on the standard deuteron model. The situation should be significantly improved in the near future by an approved experiment to measure $b_1$ at JLab (Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility). There is also an interesting indication in the HERMES result that finite antiquark tensor polarization exists. It could play an important role in solving a mechanism on tensor structure in the quark-gluon level. The tensor-polarized antiquark distributions are not easily determined from the charged-lepton DIS; however, they can be measured in a proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process with a tensor-polarized deuteron target. In this article, we estimate the tensor-polarization asymmetry for a possible Fermilab Main-Injector experiment by using optimum tensor-polarized PDFs to explain the HERMES measurement. We find that the asymmetry is typically a few percent. If it is measured, it could probe new hadron physics, and such studies could create an interesting field of high-energy spin physics. In addition, we find that a significant tensor-polarized gluon distribution should exist due to $Q^2$ evolution, even if it were zero at a low $Q^2$ scale. The tensor-polarized gluon distribution has never been observed, so that it is an interesting future project.

## Key claim

The tensor-polarization asymmetry in proton-deuteron Drell-Yan processes is estimated to be typically a few percent, based on tensor-polarized parton distribution functions (PDFs) that were optimized to explain the HERMES measurement of the structure function \(b_1\), but the authors note that the tensor-polarized PDFs are not yet well determined and have large uncertainties.

## Method

The authors used tensor-polarized PDFs extracted from a \(\chi^2\) analysis of HERMES \(b_1\) data at \(Q^2 = 2.5\) GeV\(^2\), with two parameter sets (Set 1 with no tensor-polarized antiquark distributions and Set 2 with finite tensor-polarized antiquark distributions). They then evolved these PDFs via \(Q^2\) evolution to the scale of a proposed Fermilab Main Injector experiment (proton beam energy \(E_p = 120\) GeV) and computed the tensor-polarization asymmetry \(A_{UQ_0}\) in the parton model using the expression \(A_Q = 2 A_{UQ_0} = (2 \overline{V}_T^{UQ_0}) / \overline{W}_T\).

## Key numbers

- The tensor-polarization asymmetry \(A_Q\) is typically a few percent for the Fermilab Main Injector experiment.
- The HERMES data indicate that the magnitude of \(x b_1\) is of the order of \(10^{-3}\).
- The \(\chi^2\) analysis parameters for Set 2 (finite antiquark tensor polarization) are: \(a = 0.221 \pm 0.174\), \(b = 0.648 \pm 0.342\), \(\alpha_{\bar q} = 3.20 \pm 2.75\), \(c = 1\) (fixed), and \(x_0 = 0.221\).
- The \(\chi^2\) analysis parameters for Set 1 (no antiquark tensor polarization) are: \(a = 0.378 \pm 0.212\), \(b = 0.706 \pm 0.324\), \(\alpha_{\bar q} = 0\) (fixed), \(c = 1\) (fixed), and \(x_0 = 0.229\).
- The initial scale for the tensor-polarized PDFs is \(Q_0^2 = 2.5\) GeV\(^2\).
- The dimuon-mass region considered for the Fermilab experiment is \(4^2\) GeV\(^2 < M_{\mu\mu}^2 < 9^2\) GeV\(^2\).
- The center-of-mass energy squared for the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process is \(s = M_p^2 + M_d^2 + 2 M_d E_p\) with \(E_p = 120\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of tensor-polarized parton distribution functions, which were first measured in charged-lepton deep inelastic scattering by the HERMES collaboration (2005) via the structure function \(b_1\), where unexpectedly large values were found compared to the standard deuteron model with D-state admixture. The paper provides the first theoretical estimate of the tensor-polarization asymmetry for a proposed Fermilab Main Injector experiment, building on the formalism for proton-deuteron Drell-Yan processes developed by the same authors in earlier work. The results enable future experimental searches for tensor-polarized antiquark and gluon distributions, which could probe new hadron physics beyond the standard deuteron model, and complement the approved JLab experiment to measure \(b_1\) and the possibility of studies at the future Electron-Ion Collider.
