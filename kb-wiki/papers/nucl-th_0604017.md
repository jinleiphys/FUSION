# Two-pion production in deuteron-deuteron collisions at low energies

## Key claim

The two-step model, which successfully describes the rates of two-pion and eta production in the *pd* → ³He ππ and *dd* → ⁴He η reactions, predicts cross sections for the *dd* → ⁴He ππ reaction that are too low by almost a factor of twenty compared to experiment near threshold. Neither this model nor the double-Delta model can explain the observed production rates, so alternative mechanisms must be sought.

## Method

The authors calculate the *dd* → ⁴He ππ cross section using a two-step model where a pion is first produced via the *dp* → ³He π⁰ reaction, and this pion then creates a second pion through a π⁻p → π⁰π⁰n reaction. The matrix element involves an integral over Fermi momenta of the deuteron and the ³H–p cluster configuration of the alpha particle, with the pion propagator between the two vertices. The *dp* → ³He π⁰ amplitudes are taken from experimental data at Saturne, and the πN → ππN amplitudes are parameterized from the Valencia model. The ⁴He wave function is described using variational Monte Carlo results from the Argonne group, parameterized as a sum of exponentials for the ³H–p overlap.

## Key numbers

- The predicted total cross section for *dd* → ⁴He ππ at an excess energy of Q = 29 MeV is too low by almost a factor of 20 compared to experimental data.
- At Q = 29 MeV, the ratio σ_tot(*dd* → ⁴He ππ) / σ_tot(*pd* → ³He ππ) ≈ (40 nb) / (60 nb) = 2/3.
- The ratio of the squares of the amplitudes for eta production is |f(*dd* → ⁴He η)|² / |f(*pd* → ³He η)|² ≈ 1/50.
- The *dp* → ³He π⁰ amplitudes are parameterized for deuteron kinetic energies in the 0.5–0.8 GeV range, with |A|² and |B|² given as cubic polynomials in T_d (in GeV).
- The πN → ππN amplitude squared is parameterized as a function of Q and x = (m_ππ − 2m_π)/m_π, with coefficients in units of μb/MeV².

## Context

This work extends the two-step model previously applied successfully to the *pd* → ³He ππ reaction (Faldt, Wilkin, and collaborators) and to *dd* → ⁴He η (Faldt and Wilkin, 2002). The double-Delta model of Andersson et al., which describes higher-energy data well, also fails for these low-energy measurements from the CELSIUS/WASA collaboration (Pia Thörngren et al.) and from Chapman et al. The results show that the *dd* → ⁴He ππ cross section near threshold is anomalously large compared to expectations from both existing models, and the authors suggest that final-state interactions of pions with the ⁴He nucleus or modifications to the cluster wave function are unlikely to resolve the discrepancy. New experimental results from CELSIUS are expected to provide more precise data.
