# Testing coalescence and statistical-thermal production scenarios for (anti-)(hyper-)nuclei and exotic QCD objects at energies available at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> We present a detailed comparison of coalescence and thermal-statistical models for the production of (anti-)(hyper-)nuclei in high-energy collisions. For the first time, such a study is carried out as a function of the size of the object relative to the size of the particle emitting source. Our study reveals large differences between the two scenarios for the production of objects with extended wave-functions. While both models give similar predictions and show similar agreement with experimental data for (anti-)deuterons and (anti-)3He nuclei, they largely differ in their description of (anti-)hyper-triton production. We propose to address experimentally the comparison of the production models by measuring the coalescence parameter systematically for different (anti-)(hyper-)nuclei in different collision systems and differentially in multiplicity. Such measurements are feasible with the current and upgraded Large Hadron Collider experiments. Our findings highlight the unique potential of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions as a laboratory to clarify the internal structure of exotic QCD objects and can serve as a basis for more refined calculations in the future.

## Key claim

The paper shows that coalescence and thermal-statistical models give similar predictions for (anti-)deuterons and (anti-)³He, but differ by a factor of 5–6 for (anti-)hyper-triton in central Pb–Pb collisions and by more than a factor of 50 for source radii below 2 fm, with the difference increasing as the source size decreases.

## Method

The authors compare coalescence models using a Gaussian wave-function approximation with a quantum-mechanical correction factor (Wigner formalism) against a statistical-thermal model combined with a blast-wave parameterization for the transverse momentum dependence. They map event multiplicity into source size using a parameterization R = a ⟨dNch/dη⟩^(1/3) with a = 0.473 fm, tuned to reproduce the measured (anti-)deuteron coalescence parameter B₂ in the most central Pb–Pb collisions, and then compare the predicted coalescence parameters B_A for deuterons, ³He, and ³_ΛH with ALICE data across pp and Pb–Pb collision systems.

## Key numbers

- For (anti-)hyper-triton in central Pb–Pb collisions, the coalescence and thermal+blast-wave predictions differ by a factor of 5 to 6.
- For source radii R < 2 fm, the two models differ by more than a factor of 50 for hyper-triton.
- The deuteron binding energy is B_E,d = 2.2 MeV, much smaller than the chemical freeze-out temperature T_chem ≈ 156 MeV and kinetic freeze-out temperature T_kin ≈ 90 MeV.
- The rms charge radius of the deuteron is 2.1413 ± 0.0025 fm; for ³He it is 1.959 ± 0.030 fm; for hyper-triton the rms wave-function radius is 4.9–10.0 fm.
- The coalescence parameter B₂ changes by a factor of about 50 going from pp to central Pb–Pb collisions, but by only a factor of two from pT/A = 0.4 GeV/c to 2.2 GeV/c in central Pb–Pb.
- The ratio ρ of tritium to ³He coalescence parameters is predicted to reach up to a factor of two in small systems.

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons of coalescence and thermal models for light nuclei production (Butler 1963, Kapusta 1980, Sato 1981, Scheibl 1998) by systematically studying the dependence on the object size relative to the source size, a feature enabled by LHC data across pp, p–Pb, and Pb–Pb collisions. The study builds on the observation that the hyper-triton has a much larger wave-function (4.9–10 fm rms) than non-strange A=3 nuclei (about 2 fm), making it a sensitive probe. The results enable future experimental measurements of the coalescence parameter B_A differentially in multiplicity to distinguish the two production scenarios and to constrain the internal structure of exotic QCD objects, as proposed by Mrowczynski (2016) and Bazak (2018) for ⁴He and ⁴Li.
