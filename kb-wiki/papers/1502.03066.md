# Electron-scattering form factors for 6Li in the ab initio symmetry-guided framework

> We present an ab initio symmetry-adapted no-core shell-model description for $^{6}$Li. We study the structure of the ground state of $^{6}$Li and the impact of the symmetry-guided space selection on the charge density components for this state in momentum space, including the effect of higher shells. We accomplish this by investigating the electron scattering charge form factor for momentum transfers up to $q \sim 4$ fm$^{-1}$. We demonstrate that this symmetry-adapted framework can achieve significantly reduced dimensions for equivalent large shell-model spaces while retaining the accuracy of the form factor for any momentum transfer. These new results confirm the previous outcomes for selected spectroscopy observables in light nuclei, such as binding energies, excitation energies, electromagnetic moments, E2 and M1 reduced transition probabilities, as well as point-nucleon matter rms radii.

## Key claim

The symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) with a symmetry-guided space selection reproduces the complete-space electron scattering charge form factor for the ground state of 6Li at any momentum transfer up to q ~ 4 fm−1, using only about 1% of the full model space. However, the 6Li charge radius is not completely converged, so high-precision comparisons with experiment remain for future work.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio no-core shell model calculations for 6Li in a symmetry-adapted basis labeled by SU(3) quantum numbers (λ μ) and intrinsic spins, using the bare JISP16 and NNLOopt nucleon-nucleon interactions. They calculated longitudinal C0 electron scattering form factors in the first-order plane-wave Born approximation from one-body charge density multipole operators, using wavefunctions obtained in complete Nmax=12 spaces and in selected ⟨N⊥max⟩Nmax spaces that include complete basis up through N⊥max along with selected configurations beyond. The form factors were adjusted for finite proton size and had the center-of-mass contribution removed.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer range studied: up to q ~ 4 fm−1
- The selected ⟨6⟩12 model space constitutes about 1% of the complete Nmax=12 model space (approximately 4.3×105 basis states compared to 4.9×107)
- For ℏΩ=20 MeV, the Nmax=12 ground state energy is within 0.54 MeV of the extrapolated result of −31.49(6) MeV
- The SA-NCSM binding energy for the 1+ ground state using JISP16 at ℏΩ=20 MeV is 30.445 MeV (Nmax=12 complete space: 30.951 MeV; experiment: 31.99 MeV)
- The SA-NCSM point-proton rms radius for the 1+ ground state using JISP16 at ℏΩ=20 MeV is 2.112 fm (Nmax=12 complete space: 2.125 fm; experiment deduced from charge radius: 2.43 fm)
- The SA-NCSM electric quadrupole moment for the 1+ ground state using JISP16 at ℏΩ=20 MeV is −0.08 e fm2 (experiment: −0.0818(17) e fm2)
- The SA-NCSM magnetic dipole moment for the 1+ ground state using JISP16 at ℏΩ=20 MeV is 0.839 μN (experiment: 0.822 μN)

## Context

This work extends the SA-NCSM framework previously introduced by Dytrych et al. (2012) from spectroscopy observables (binding energies, excitation energies, electromagnetic moments, transition probabilities, and radii) to electron scattering form factors, confirming that the symmetry-guided space selection preserves accuracy for charge density distributions. The results are compared to Variational Monte Carlo calculations using the AV18 two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon interactions by Wiringa et al. (1998), and to Green's function Monte Carlo results by Pudliner et al. (1997). The study enables future application of the SA-NCSM to heavier nuclei and highly deformed structures, as the symmetry-guided reduction dramatically decreases computational requirements while maintaining accuracy for both structure and reaction observables.
