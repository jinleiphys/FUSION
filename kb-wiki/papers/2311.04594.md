# Fully-heavy baryons $QQQ$ in vacuum and hot QCD medium

> We study the properties of fully-heavy baryons in the vacuum and the hot QCD medium, which is created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Masses and wave functions of $Ω_{ccc}$, $Ω_{ccb}$, $Ω_{bbc}$, and $Ω_{bbb}$ up to the second radial excited states are obtained by solving the three-body Schrödinger equation with Hyperspherical Harmonics method. With parameters completely fixed by fitting quarkonium boundstates in vacuum, we predicted the masses for $1S$, $2S$, and $3S$ states of fully-heavy baryons. We also computed the temperature dependence of baryon masses and the thermal widths in a hot QCD medium. These properties are important to precise study of fully-heavy baryon production in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors predict masses for the 1S, 2S, and 3S states of fully-heavy baryons (Ωccc, Ωccb, Ωbbc, Ωbbb) using a potential model with parameters completely fixed by fitting quarkonium bound states in vacuum, and they compute the temperature dependence of these masses and thermal widths in a hot QCD medium, noting that the spin-spin interaction is a higher-order effect compared to the central interaction.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body Schrödinger equation using the Hyperspherical Harmonics (HH) expansion method with Jacobi coordinates, truncating the expansion to hyperangular quantum number K ≤ 6 for S-wave states. The two-body interaction potential was taken as the Cornell potential (Coulomb plus linear confinement) plus a Gaussian spin-spin term, with parameters fixed by fitting charmonium, bottomonium, and Bc meson masses from experiment. For finite-temperature calculations, they replaced the vacuum potential with two different lattice-QCD-derived complex potentials (potential I from Burnier (2015) and potential II from Bala (2021)) to account for screening and thermal width effects.

## Key numbers

- Ωccc 1S mass: 4.80 GeV (J^P = 3/2^+ state)
- Ωccc 2S mass: 5.31 GeV
- Ωccc 3S mass: 5.74 GeV
- Ωbbb 1S mass: 14.36 GeV (J^P = 3/2^+ state)
- Ωbbb 2S mass: 14.77 GeV
- Ωbbb 3S mass: 15.09 GeV
- Ωccb 1S mass: 8.19 GeV (J^P = 3/2^+) and 8.17 GeV (J^P = 1/2^+)
- Ωbbc 1S mass: 10.96 GeV (J^P = 3/2^+) and 10.87 GeV (J^P = 1/2^+)
- Ωccc 1S root-mean-squared radius: 0.30 fm
- Ωbbb 1S root-mean-squared radius: 0.16 fm
- Charm quark mass parameter: 1.29 GeV; bottom quark mass parameter: 4.7 GeV
- Coulomb strength α: 0.308; string tension σ: 0.15 GeV^2
- QCD phase transition temperature: approximately 160 MeV (from lattice QCD, cited as Bazavov (2011) and Fodor (2004))

## Context

This work extends the potential model approach previously applied to quarkonium (reviewed by Zhao (2020)) to fully-heavy baryons, building on earlier three-body calculations by Silvestre-Brac (1996), Martynenko (2007), Vijande (2015), Liu (2019), Yang (2019), and Shi (2019). The vacuum mass predictions are consistent with lattice QCD results from Padmanath (2013), PACS-CS (2013), Meinel (2012), and Briceno (2012). The finite-temperature study enables more precise predictions for fully-heavy baryon production yields in heavy-ion collisions at LHC, where enhancement relative to proton-proton collisions has been predicted by He (2014), Zhao (2016), Cho (2019), and Andronic (2021).
