# Baryon Masses in Partially Quenched Heavy Hadron Chiral Perturbation Theory

> The masses of baryons containing a heavy quark are calculated to next-to-leading order in partially quenched heavy hadron chiral perturbation theory. Calculations are performed for three light flavors in the isospin limit and additionally for two light non-degenerate flavors. The results presented are necessary for extrapolating lattice QCD and partially quenched lattice QCD calculations of the heavy hadron masses.

## Key claim

The masses of baryons containing a heavy quark are calculated to next-to-leading order in partially quenched heavy hadron chiral perturbation theory, but the results are derived in the heavy-quark limit (infinite heavy quark mass) and are therefore valid only when higher-order corrections in 1/m_Q are neglected.

## Method

The author formulates partially quenched heavy hadron chiral perturbation theory in SU(6|3) for three light flavors in the isospin limit, and separately in SU(4|2) for two non-degenerate light flavors. The masses of s_ell = 0 baryons (the Lambda_Q and Xi_Q states) and s_ell = 1 baryons (the Sigma_Q, Xi'_Q, and Omega_Q states) are computed at one-loop order using dimensional regularization with a modified minimal subtraction scheme, incorporating tree-level contributions from the chiral Lagrangian and loop diagrams involving pseudo-Goldstone mesons and hairpin interactions from the flavor-neutral field.

## Key numbers

- The mass of the s_ell = 0 baryons is expressed as M_Ti = M_0(mu) - M_Ti^(1)(mu) - M_Ti^(3/2)(mu), where M_0(mu) is the renormalized mass in the chiral limit and the superscripts denote orders in m_q
- The mass of the s_ell = 1 baryons is expressed as M_Bi = M_0(mu) + Delta(mu) + M_Bi^(1)(mu) + M_Bi^(3/2)(mu), where Delta(mu) is the renormalized splitting between the T and B baryons in the chiral limit
- The one-loop contribution to the s_ell = 0 baryon masses is M_T^(3/2) = (g_3^2)/(4 pi^2 f^2) [sum_phi A_phi^T F(m_phi, Delta, mu) + sum_{phi phi'} A_{phi phi'}^T F(m_phi, m_{phi'}, Delta, mu)]
- The one-loop contribution to the s_ell = 1 baryon masses contains terms proportional to (g_3^2)/(12 pi^2 f^2) and (g_2^2)/(12 pi f^2), with the latter involving m_phi^3 terms
- The tree-level coefficients m_T for the Lambda_Q and Xi_Q states are m_u and (1/2)(m_u + m_s) respectively
- The tree-level coefficients m_B for the Sigma_Q, Xi'_Q, and Omega_Q states are m_u, (1/2)(m_u + m_s), and m_s respectively

## Context

This work extends the partially quenched chiral perturbation theory framework for heavy baryons from SU(4|2) (previously investigated by Arndt (2003)) to SU(6|3) with three light flavors, building on the heavy hadron chiral perturbation theory developed by Yan (1992), Cho (1992), and Savage (1995). The results enable extrapolation of lattice QCD and partially quenched lattice QCD calculations of heavy hadron masses to physical quark masses, and allow determination of the low-energy constants appearing in the chiral theories from lattice data. The Omega_Q baryon, which does not decay strongly, is identified as an ideal testing ground for obtaining and extrapolating lattice data with dynamical quarks.
