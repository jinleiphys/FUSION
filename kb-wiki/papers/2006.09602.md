# Fractional quantum Hall physics and higher-order momentum correlations in a few spinful fermionic contact-interacting ultracold atoms in rotating traps

> The fractional quantum Hall effect (FQHE) is theoretically investigated, with numerical and algebraic approaches, in assemblies of a few spinful ultracold neutral fermionic atoms, interacting via repulsive contact potentials and confined in a single rapidly rotating two-dimensional harmonic trap. Going beyond the commonly used second-order correlations in the real configuration space, the methodology in this paper will assist the analysis of experimental observations by providing benchmark results for $N$-body spin-unresolved, as well as spin-resolved, momentum correlations measurable in time-of-flight experiments with individual particle detection. Our analysis shows that the few-body lowest-Landau-level (LLL) states with good magic angular momenta exhibit inherent ordered quantum structures in the $N$-body correlations, similar to those associated with rotating Wigner molecules (WMs), familiar from the field of semiconductor quantum dots under high magnetic fields. The application of a small perturbing stirring potential induces, at the ensuing avoided crossings, formation of symmetry broken states exhibiting ordered polygonal-ring structures, explicitly manifest in the single-particle density profile of the trapped particles. Away from the crossings, an LLL state obtained from exact diagonalization of the microscopic Hamiltonian, found to be well-described by a (1,1,1) Halperin two-component variational wavefunction, represents also a spinful rotating WM. Analysis of the calculated LLL wavefunction enables a two-dimensional generalization of the Girardeau one-dimensional 'fermionization' scheme, originally invoked for mapping of bosonic-type wave functions to those of spinless fermions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that few-body spinful fermionic ultracold atoms with contact interactions in a rapidly rotating trap exhibit ordered quantum structures in N-body correlations (rotating Wigner molecules) analogous to those seen in semiconductor quantum dots, but the crystalline order is only fully revealed in higher-order (Nth-order) correlations rather than in the commonly used second-order correlations or single-particle densities, except when a symmetry-breaking stirring potential is applied.

## Method

The authors performed exact diagonalization (configuration interaction) of the microscopic many-body Hamiltonian for N=4 and N=6 spinful fermionic atoms (modeling 6Li) confined to the lowest Landau level (LLL) of a rapidly rotating two-dimensional harmonic trap, with repulsive contact interactions characterized by a dimensionless parameter R_delta=0.4. They computed spin-unresolved and spin-resolved correlation functions up to 4th order in both real configuration space and momentum space (via Fourier transform), and compared the CI-calculated states to the Halperin (1,1,1) two-component variational wavefunction. A small multipole stirring potential (hexadecapolar or quadrupolar) was added to induce avoided crossings and symmetry-broken states.

## Key numbers

- R_delta = 0.4: the dimensionless contact-interaction strength used for all calculations, corresponding to the maximum repulsion between two opposite-spin fermions in the LLL Hilbert space (in units of hbar*omega)
- The condition for validating LLL projection is R_delta < 2, as the energy gap between lowest and first-excited Landau levels is 2*hbar*omega
- For N=4 fermions in the (S=0, Sz=0) spin sector, the magic angular momenta appearing in the relative ground state are L = 2, 4, and 8
- For N=4 fermions in the (S=2, Sz=0) spin sector, the relative ground state has total angular momentum L = 6
- The avoided crossing between L=4 and L=8 states occurs near Omega/omega ~ 0.884 for the hexadecapolar perturbation (m=4)
- Two perturbation strengths were used: cc = 0.0001 (weak) and cc = 0.004 (strong), producing qualitatively different behavior in the expectation value of total angular momentum across the avoided crossing

## Context

This work extends the study of fractional quantum Hall physics from the well-established electronic semiconductor quantum dot systems (Yannouleas and Landman, 2003, 2007) and bosonic ultracold atom systems (Wilkin 2000, Cooper 2003, Popp 2004, Hazzard 2008) to spinful fermionic ultracold atoms with contact interactions. The methodology goes beyond the common second-order correlation analyses (Laughlin 1983, Yannouleas 2003, Barbara 2006) by providing benchmark N-body momentum correlations measurable in time-of-flight experiments with individual particle detection (Jochim 2018, Bergschneider 2019, Preiss 2019, Becher 2020). The results enable experimental verification of FQHE states in finite fermionic systems and demonstrate a two-dimensional generalization of Girardeau's one-dimensional fermionization scheme (Girardeau 1960, Jochim 2012).
