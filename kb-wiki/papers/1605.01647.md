# Hunting for exotic doubly hidden-charm/bottom tetraquark states

> We develop a moment QCD sum rule method augmented by fundamental inequalities to study the existence of exotic doubly hidden-charm/bottom tetraquark states made of four heavy quarks. Using the compact diquark-antidiquark configuration, we calculate the mass spectra of these tetraquark states. There are 18 hidden-charm $cc\bar c\bar c$ tetraquark currents with $J^{PC} = 0^{++}$, $0^{-+}$, $0^{--}$, $1^{++}$, $1^{+-}$, $1^{-+}$, $1^{--}$, and $2^{++}$. We use them to perform QCD sum rule analyses, and the obtained masses are all higher than the spontaneous dissociation thresholds of two charmonium mesons, which are thus their dominant decay modes. The masses of the corresponding hidden-bottom $bb\bar b\bar b$ tetraquarks are all below or very close to the thresholds of the $Υ(1S)Υ(1S)$ and $η_b(1S)η_b(1S)$, except one current of $J^{PC}=0^{++}$. Hence, we suggest to search for the doubly hidden-charm states in the $J/ψJ/ψ$ and $η_c(1S)η_c(1S)$ channels.

## Key claim

The authors develop a moment QCD sum rule method augmented by fundamental inequalities to predict masses of doubly hidden-charm/bottom tetraquark states; they find that all 18 hidden-charm $cc\bar c\bar c$ tetraquark masses lie above the spontaneous dissociation thresholds of two charmonium mesons, while the corresponding hidden-bottom $bb\bar b\bar b$ tetraquark masses lie below or very close to the $\Upsilon(1S)\Upsilon(1S)$ and $\eta_b(1S)\eta_b(1S)$ thresholds except for one $J^{PC}=0^{++}$ current.

## Method

The authors construct 18 local diquark-antidiquark interpolating currents for $cc\bar c\bar c$ and $bb\bar b\bar b$ tetraquarks with quantum numbers $J^{PC}=0^{++}$, $0^{-+}$, $0^{--}$, $1^{++}$, $1^{+-}$, $1^{-+}$, $1^{--}$, and $2^{++}$. They perform moment QCD sum rule analyses at $Q_0^2>0$ to ensure good operator product expansion convergence, using the ratio of moments $r(n,Q_0^2)=M_n(Q_0^2)/M_{n+1}(Q_0^2)$ to extract the lowest-lying resonance mass, and employ Schwarz inequality constraints to identify plateau regions in the $(n,\xi)$ parameter plane.

## Key numbers

- Mass of $bb\bar b\bar b$ tetraquark with $J^{PC}=0^{++}$ (current $J_1$): $18.45 \pm 0.15$ GeV
- Mass of $cc\bar c\bar c$ tetraquark with $J^{PC}=0^{++}$ (current $J_1$): $6.44 \pm 0.15$ GeV
- Mass of $cc\bar c\bar c$ tetraquark with $J^{PC}=1^{++}$ (current $J_{2\mu}^+$): $6.34 \pm 0.19$ GeV
- Mass of $bb\bar b\bar b$ tetraquark with $J^{PC}=1^{++}$ (current $J_{2\mu}^+$): $18.32 \pm 0.18$ GeV
- Mass of $cc\bar c\bar c$ tetraquark with $J^{PC}=2^{++}$ (current $J_{2\mu\nu}$): $6.37 \pm 0.19$ GeV
- Mass of $bb\bar b\bar b$ tetraquark with $J^{PC}=2^{++}$ (current $J_{2\mu\nu}$): $18.32 \pm 0.17$ GeV
- Heavy quark masses used: $m_c(\overline{\rm MS}) = 1.27 \pm 0.03$ GeV, $m_b(\overline{\rm MS}) = 4.18 \pm 0.03$ GeV
- Gluon condensate used: $\langle g_s^2 GG \rangle = (0.48 \pm 0.14)$ GeV$^4$

## Context

This work extends the moment QCD sum rule method, previously successful for charmonium and bottomonium mass spectra (Shifman 1979, Reinders 1985), to the study of doubly hidden-charm/bottom tetraquark systems with four heavy quarks. The results are compatible with earlier predictions from Iwasaki (1975), Chao (1981), Ader (1982), and Lloyd (2004), while the masses for $cc\bar c\bar c$ states are slightly higher and for $bb\bar b\bar b$ states slightly lower than those from the nonrelativistic Schroedinger equation approach of Berezhnoy (2012). The predictions enable experimental searches for these tetraquark states in $J/\psi J/\psi$ and $\eta_c(1S)\eta_c(1S)$ channels at LHCb, D0, CMS, and BelleII.
