# Electric dipole moments of actinide atoms and RaO molecule

> We have calculated the atomic electric dipole moments (EDMs) induced in 229Pa and 225Ac by their respective nuclear Schiff moments S. The results are d(229Pa)= - 9.5 10^{-17} [S/(e fm)] e cm= - 1.1 10^{-20} ηe cm; d(225Ac})=- 8.6 10^{-17} [S/(e fm)] e cm=- 0.8 10^{-21} ηe cm. EDM of 229 Pa is 3 10^4 times larger than 199Hg EDM and 40 times larger than 225Ra EDM. Possible use of actinides in solid state experiments is also discussed. The T,P-odd spin-axis interaction in RaO molecule is 500 times larger than in TlF.

# Electric dipole moments of actinide atoms and RaO molecule

## Key claim

The atomic electric dipole moments (EDMs) of ²²⁹Pa and ²²⁵Ac are calculated to be orders of magnitude larger than previously studied systems, with the ²²⁹Pa EDM being 3×10⁴ times larger than ¹⁹⁹Hg EDM and 40 times larger than ²²⁵Ra EDM, but these results depend on the nuclear Schiff moment values from octupole deformation models and assume the dominant contribution comes from the Ra-like 7s² core, neglecting contributions from high-wave 6d and 5f electrons.

## Method

The atomic EDM induced by the nuclear Schiff moment was calculated for ²²⁹Pa and ²²⁵Ac by scaling from existing atomic calculations for Ra (Z=88) and Pu (Z=94), using the average value for Pa (Z=91) and a similar approach for Ac (Z=89). For the RaO molecule, the T,P-odd spin-axis interaction constant X was estimated by scaling from TlF using the Z²R(Zα) relativistic factor and existing EDM ratio calculations for Ra and Hg, yielding an intermediate estimate of X(RaO)/X(TlF) ≈ 2.2 S(Ra)/S(Tl).

## Key numbers

- ²²⁹Pa atomic EDM: d = -9.5×10⁻¹⁷ [S/(e·fm)] e·cm = -1.1×10⁻²⁰ η e·cm, where η is the dimensionless strength of nucleon P,T-odd interaction in units of the Fermi constant
- ²²⁵Ac atomic EDM: d = -8.6×10⁻¹⁷ [S/(e·fm)] e·cm = -0.8×10⁻²¹ η e·cm
- ²²⁹Pa EDM is 3×10⁴ times larger than ¹⁹⁹Hg atomic EDM (4×10⁻²⁵ η e·cm) and 40 times larger than ²²⁵Ra EDM (2.6×10⁻²² η e·cm)
- RaO molecule T,P-odd spin-axis interaction: ⟨Ψ₀|H_W|Ψ₀⟩ = 1.×10⁵ (S·n) a.u., which is 500 times larger than in TlF
- ²²⁹Pa Schiff moment from Spevak: S = 1.2×10⁻⁴ e·fm³ η
- Ra Schiff moment is 200 times larger than Tl Schiff moment
- Accuracy of Pa EDM result is about 20%

## Context

This work extends the line of research on Schiff moment enhancement in nuclei with octupole deformation, first noted by Spevak (1989) and Haxton and Henley (1983), who found enhanced P,T-violation in ²²⁹Pa due to close opposite-parity levels. The atomic calculations build on existing results for Hg, Xe, Rn, Ra, and Pu from Kozlov (2004) and for TlF from Kozlov (2004) and Petrov (2002). The results enable new experimental searches for CP violation beyond the Standard Model using actinide atoms and RaO molecules, and suggest that solid-state experiments with actinide compounds (such as uranium and thorium) could be about 5 times more sensitive than analogous experiments with rare-earth compounds like gadolinium, as proposed by Lam, Hunter, and Sushkov.
