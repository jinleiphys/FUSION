# The Finite Temperature Pairing Gap of a Unitary Fermi Gas by Quantum Monte Carlo Calculations

> We calculate the one-body temperature Green's (Matsubara) function of the unitary Fermi gas via Quantum Monte Carlo, and extract the spectral weight function $A(p,ω)$ using the methods of maximum entropy and singular value decomposition. From $A(p,ω)$ we determine the quasiparticle spectrum, which can be accurately parametrized by three functions of temperature: an effective mass $m^*$, a mean-field potential $U$, and a gap $Δ$. Below the critical temperature $T_c=0.15\varepsilon_F$ the results for $m^*$, $U$ and $Δ$ can be accurately reproduced using an independent quasiparticle model. We find evidence of a pseudogap in the fermionic excitation spectrum for temperatures up to {$T^*\approx 0.20\varepsilon_{F} > T_c$}.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first *ab initio* evaluation of the one-body temperature propagator of the unitary Fermi gas, free of uncontrolled approximations, and extracts the temperature dependence of the pairing gap and pseudogap. The authors find evidence of a pseudogap in the fermionic excitation spectrum for temperatures up to T* ≈ 0.20εF, which is above the critical temperature Tc = 0.15εF, but note that the singular value decomposition method can reproduce both two- and one-peak structures in this temperature range depending on details of the method, due to finite statistical errors.

## Method
The authors performed Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of the one-body temperature (Matsubara) Green's function for a unitary Fermi gas on an 8³ lattice with periodic boundary conditions and average particle number 50-55, using a Trotter expansion followed by a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation and Metropolis importance sampling. They extracted the spectral weight function A(p,ω) from the Green's function using two independent methods: maximum entropy and singular value decomposition. The quasiparticle spectrum was then parametrized by fitting to a BCS-like dispersion relation with three temperature-dependent parameters: effective mass m*, mean-field potential U, and pairing gap Δ.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature for superfluidity: Tc = 0.15εF
- Pseudogap persists up to T* ≈ 0.20εF, which is above Tc
- Bertsch parameter ξ = 5E/3NεF ≈ 0.40 at unitarity, extracted from T=0 extrapolation
- Statistical errors typically below 1%; systematic errors estimated at about 10-15%
- Calculations performed with 6000 to 10000 uncorrelated samples at each temperature
- Number of imaginary time steps at low temperatures: O(10³)
- Effective range corrections: r₀ = 4/πΛ ≈ 0.4 in lattice units

## Context
This work extends previous T=0 calculations of the pairing gap by Carlson and Chang (2004) and Carlson and Reddy (2005) to finite temperature, and provides the first *ab initio* determination of the temperature dependence of the pairing gap in a unitary Fermi gas. The results contradict the theoretical calculation by Haussmann (2007) based on summing particle-particle ring diagrams, which did not reveal signs of a pseudogap, and support experimental indications of a pseudogap from Jin (2007) and Swinburne (2007). The findings enable future experimental verification using Bragg spectroscopy as advocated by Bruun (2007), and provide a benchmark for other many-body approaches to strongly interacting fermion systems.
