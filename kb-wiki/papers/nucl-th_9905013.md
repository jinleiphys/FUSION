# A New Approach to y-scaling and the Universal Features of Scaling Functions and Nucleon Momentum Distributions

> Some systematic general features of y-scaling structure functions, which are essentially independent of detailed dynamics, are pointed out. Their physical interpretation in terms of general characteristics, such as a mean field description and nucleon-nucleon correlations, is given and their relationship to the momentum distributions illustrated. A new relativistic scaling variable is proposed which incorporates the momentum dependence of the excitation energy of the (A-1) system, with the resulting scaling function being closely related to the longitudinal momemtum distributions and being free from removal-energy scaling violating effects.

# A New Approach to y-scaling and the Universal Features of Scaling Functions and Nucleon Momentum Distributions

## Key claim

The paper claims that the longitudinal momentum distribution extracted from y-scaling data can be decomposed into a model-independent mean-field term and a universal, deuteron-like short-range correlation term, with the exponential slope at large momentum being independent of nuclear mass number A. The authors propose a new relativistic scaling variable that incorporates the average excitation energy of the residual (A-1) system, which they argue removes binding-related scaling violations and establishes a direct link between the scaling function and longitudinal momentum distributions without requiring theoretical binding corrections.

## Method

The authors analyzed the experimentally extracted longitudinal scaling function f(y) for nuclei with A < 56, fitting its behavior across three momentum regimes: a Lorentzian form at small y (controlled by the separation energy), a Gaussian form at intermediate y (dictated by shell-model harmonic oscillator potentials), and an exponential falloff at large y (attributed to short-range nucleon-nucleon correlations). They then derived a new relativistic scaling variable y_CW by replacing the excitation energy E*_{A-1} in the energy conservation equation with its expectation value evaluated using realistic spectral functions from few- and many-body calculations, incorporating both the mean-field excitation energy and the excitation energy from correlated nucleon pairs including their center-of-mass motion.

## Key numbers

- f(0) decreases monotonically with A, from approximately 10^{-2} MeV^{-1} for A=2 to approximately 3 × 10^{-3} MeV^{-1} for ^{56}Fe
- For y near 0, f(y) ~ C_1(α^2 + y^2)^{-1} with α ranging from approximately 45 MeV (A=2) to approximately 140 MeV (A=56), and C_1 ranging from approximately 18 MeV (A=2) to approximately 59 MeV (A=56)
- For 50 MeV ≤ |y| ≤ 200 MeV, f(y) ~ e^{-a^2 y^2} with a ranging from approximately 56 × 10^{-2} MeV^{-1} (A=2) to approximately 45 × 10^{-2} MeV^{-1} (A=56)
- For |y| ≥ 400 MeV, f(y) ~ C_2 e^{-b|y|} with b = 6 × 10^{-3} MeV^{-1}, independent of A; C_2 ranges from 2.5 × 10^{-4} MeV^{-1} (A=2) to 6 × 10^{-4} MeV^{-1} (A=56)
- For ^4He, the decomposition yields S_0 = 0.76 (mean-field normalization) and S_1 = 0.24 (correlation normalization), compared to theoretical values S_0 ~ 0.8 and S_1 ~ 0.2
- The parameters b_A and c_A in the average excitation energy expression range from 17 MeV to 43 MeV and from 3.41 × 10^{-1} to 1.66 × 10^{-1}, for ^3He and Nuclear Matter respectively

## Context

This work extends the y-scaling formalism developed by West and by Ciofi degli Atti and collaborators, which relates the inclusive electron scattering structure function to nucleon momentum distributions. The authors build upon the spectral function approach of Ciofi degli Atti (1990s) and the two-nucleon correlation model of Ji (1990s), proposing a scaling variable that interpolates between the single-particle and correlation-dominated regimes. The analysis confirms predictions from many-body calculations by Pandharipande and collaborators (1990s) and by Frank, Zabolitzky, and collaborators regarding the separation of momentum distributions into mean-field and correlation components. The new variable y_CW enables direct comparison with recent high-Q^2 data from Jefferson Lab (Arrington, late 1990s), potentially allowing separation of binding effects from final state interactions in future analyses.
