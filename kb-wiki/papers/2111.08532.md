# Hydrodynamization times of a holographic fluid far from equilibrium

> We investigate several hydrodynamization times for an ensemble of different far-from-equilibrium solutions of the strongly coupled $\mathcal{N}=4$ Supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma undergoing Bjorken flow. For the ensemble of initial data analyzed in the present work, we find that, with typical tolerances between $3\%$ to $5\%$, the average hydrodynamization time associated with the late time convergence of the pressure anisotropy to the corresponding Borel resummed hydrodynamic attractor is approximately equal to the average hydrodynamization time associated with the Navier-Stokes result, while both are shorter than the average hydrodynamization time associated with second-order hydrodynamics. On the other hand, we find that the entropy density of the different solutions coalesces to second-order hydrodynamics long before entering in the Navier-Stokes regime. A clear hierarchy between the different average hydrodynamization times of the Bjorken expanding fluid is established for the set of analyzed initial data, comprising also some solutions which, whilst satisfying the dominant and the weak energy conditions at the initial time, evolve such as to transiently violate one or both conditions when the fluid is still far from equilibrium. In particular, solutions violating the weak energy condition are generally found to take a longer time to enter in the hydrodynamic regime than the other solutions.

## Key claim

For an ensemble of far-from-equilibrium solutions of strongly coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) Supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma undergoing Bjorken flow, the average hydrodynamization time for convergence of the pressure anisotropy to the Borel resummed hydrodynamic attractor is approximately equal to that for the Navier-Stokes result under typical tolerances of 3% to 5%, while both are shorter than the average hydrodynamization time for second-order hydrodynamics; however, the Borel attractor only provides a clearly better description than Navier-Stokes if one considers very small relative tolerances in the long time regime.

## Method

The authors numerically solved Einstein's equations in a 5D asymptotically anti-de Sitter (AdS) bulk using the characteristic formulation of general relativity with infalling Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates, imposing Bjorken symmetry on the boundary metric. They evolved an ensemble of initial data for the metric anisotropy and the UV coefficient \(a_2(\tau)\) (originally studied in Rougemont (2021)), extracting the pressure anisotropy and non-equilibrium entropy density from the holographically renormalized energy-momentum tensor and the apparent horizon area, respectively. Hydrodynamization times were defined as the times when these observables converged within relative tolerances of 3% to 5% to the Navier-Stokes, second-order hydrodynamic, and Borel resummed attractor predictions.

## Key numbers

- The average hydrodynamization time for the pressure anisotropy to converge to the Borel resummed attractor is approximately equal to that for the Navier-Stokes result under tolerances between 3% and 5%.
- The average hydrodynamization time for the pressure anisotropy to converge to second-order hydrodynamics is longer than both the Borel and Navier-Stokes average times.
- The non-equilibrium entropy density coalesces to second-order hydrodynamics long before it converges to the Navier-Stokes regime, and its average hydrodynamization time for second-order hydrodynamics is considerably smaller than all average hydrodynamization times related to the pressure anisotropy.
- Solutions that transiently violate the weak energy condition are generally found to take a longer time to enter the hydrodynamic regime than other solutions.

## Context

This work extends the high-statistics analysis of holographic Bjorken flow in Jankowski (2014) and the study of energy condition violations in Rougemont (2021), and it directly addresses the proposal in Heller (2015) that the Borel resummed hydrodynamic attractor provides an earlier universal description of far-from-equilibrium dynamics. The results show that for generic initial conditions the Borel attractor does not give a significantly earlier hydrodynamization than Navier-Stokes, contrary to the earlier claim, and establish a clear hierarchy of hydrodynamization times for different observables. This enables future studies to refine the notion of hydrodynamization in strongly coupled plasmas and to test whether similar hierarchies appear in other holographic models or in QCD.
