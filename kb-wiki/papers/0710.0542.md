# Nuclear skin emergence in Skyrme deformed Hartree-Fock calculations

> A study of the charge and matter densities and the corresponding rms radii for even-even isotopes of Ni, Kr, and Sn has been performed in the framework of deformed self-consistent mean field Skyrme HF+BCS method. The resulting charge radii and neutron skin thicknesses of these nuclei are compared with available experimental data, as well as with other theoretical predictions. The formation of a neutron skin, which manifests itself in an excess of neutrons at distances greater than the radius of the proton distribution, is analyzed in terms of various definitions. Formation of a proton skin is shown to be unlikely. The effects of deformation on the neutron skins in even-even deformed nuclei far from the stability line are discussed.

## Key claim

Using deformed Skyrme HF+BCS calculations, the authors show that neutron skin formation in even-even Ni, Kr, and Sn isotopes emerges for neutron-rich isotopes above certain mass thresholds (A>132 in Sn, A>74 in Ni, A>96 in Kr), but the absolute skin thickness values depend strongly on which definition of skin thickness is used, with different criteria producing different numerical results even within the same nuclear model.

## Method

The authors performed deformed self-consistent mean-field Hartree-Fock calculations with Skyrme interactions (SLy4, Sk3, and SG2 parametrizations) and BCS pairing correlations for even-even Ni (A=48-78), Kr (A=70-100), and Sn (A=100-136) isotopes assuming axial symmetry. They computed charge and matter density distributions, root-mean-square radii, and form factors, then extracted neutron skin thicknesses using multiple definitions: differences in rms radii, diffraction radii, Helm model radii, and two density-based criteria (one from Fukuda (1993) and an alternative criterion treating proton and neutron densities symmetrically).

## Key numbers

- For Sn isotopes, the neutron skin thickness defined as ΔR_hs (difference in equivalent hard sphere radii) reaches approximately 0.92 fm for 136Sn using criterion (a), while criterion (b) gives 0.50 fm for the same nucleus
- For Ni isotopes, the neutron skin thickness ΔR^(a) reaches 0.97 fm for 78Ni and ΔR^(b) gives 0.77 fm
- For Kr isotopes, the neutron skin thickness ΔR^(a) reaches 0.90 fm for 100Kr and ΔR^(b) gives 0.62 fm
- The charge rms radii for Sn isotopes were compared with experimental data from laser and muonic atom spectroscopy, with 120Sn used as reference for isotope shift measurements
- The difference between neutron and proton rms radii Δr_np for Sn isotopes was compared with experimental data from (p,p) scattering, antiprotonic atoms, giant dipole resonance method, and spin dipole resonance method

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nuclear sizes from the authors' previous study (Antonov et al., 2005) by performing a more systematic study of a larger set of exotic nuclei and calculating neutron skin thicknesses using multiple definitions within the same microscopic model. The results are compared with relativistic mean-field calculations using NL3 parametrization (Lalazissis et al., 1999), Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with Gogny D1S interaction (Libert et al., 2007), and generator coordinate method calculations (Libert et al., 2007). The study enables systematic comparison of different skin definitions within a single theoretical framework and clarifies how deformation influences skin formation, particularly for Kr isotopes where shape transitions occur.
