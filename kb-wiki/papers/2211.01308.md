# Equation of state of superfluid neutron matter with low-momentum interactions

> In this work, we calculate the ground state energy of pure neutron matter using the renormalization group based low-momentum effective interaction $V_{\text{low-}k}$ in Bogoliubov many-body perturbation theory (BMBPT), which is a perturbative expansion around the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) ground state. In order to capture the low-density behavior of neutron matter, it turns out to be better to use a density dependent cutoff in the $V_{\text{low-}k}$ interaction. Perturbative corrections to the HFB energy up to third order are included. We find that at low densities corresponding to the inner crust of neutron stars, the HFB state that includes pairing is a better starting point for perturbation expansion. It is observed that including the higher order perturbative corrections, the cutoff dependence of the ground state energy is reduced.

## Key claim

The authors show that using a density-dependent cutoff in the low-momentum interaction \(V_{\text{low-}k}\) (scaled as \(\Lambda = f k_F\)) is necessary to capture the correct low-density behavior of neutron matter, and that the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) starting point with Bogoliubov many-body perturbation theory (BMBPT) corrections up to third order yields a well-converged equation of state, but only when the cutoff is density-dependent; with a fixed cutoff, third-order perturbation theory is insufficient to recover the low-density \(\kf a\) expansion.

## Method

The authors start from the realistic AV18 nucleon-nucleon interaction, soften it via renormalization-group techniques to obtain the low-momentum interaction \(V_{\text{low-}k}\) with a smooth exponential regulator, and use it in the HFB approximation to describe superfluid neutron matter with \(^1S_0\) pairing. They then include perturbative corrections to the HFB ground-state energy up to third order within Bogoliubov many-body perturbation theory (BMBPT), computing the momentum integrals via Monte-Carlo integration with importance sampling. The calculations are performed for both fixed cutoff (\(\Lambda = 2\,\text{fm}^{-1}\)) and density-dependent cutoff (\(\Lambda = f k_F\) with scale factor \(f\)), and partial waves up to \(l \leq 6\) are included for convergence.

## Key numbers

* The neutron-neutron scattering length is \(a \approx -18\,\text{fm}\) and the effective range is \(r_{\text{e}} \approx 2.7\,\text{fm}\).
* The HFB ground-state energy density is given by \(\mathcal{E}_{\text{HFB}} = \frac{1}{2\pi^2} \int dk \,k^2\, [v_k^2\,(k^2+\Sigma_{\text{HFB}}(k)) - \Delta_k^2/E_k]\), and the free Fermi gas energy density is \(\mathcal{E}_{\text{FG}} = k_F^5/(10\pi^2 m)\).
* For the density-dependent cutoff \(\Lambda = 2 k_F\), the BMBPT3 results at low densities are in excellent agreement with the fourth-order \(k_F a\) expansion of Wellenhofer (2021) and with Quantum Monte Carlo results of Gezerlis and Carlson (2010) and Gandolfi et al. (2022).
* At \(k_F = 0.1\,\text{fm}^{-1}\) with fixed cutoff \(\Lambda = 2\,\text{fm}^{-1}\), the BMBPT3 correction is about 60% of the BMBPT2 correction, indicating poor convergence; with density-dependent cutoff the BMBPT3 correction is tiny.
* The HFB \(^1S_0\) pairing gap and effective mass are shown for cutoff parameters \(f = \Lambda/k_F = 1.5, 2.0, 2.5\) over the range \(k_F \lesssim 1.4\,\text{fm}^{-1}\).
* Partial waves beyond \(l = 2\) have noticeable effects for \(k_F \gtrsim 0.7\,\text{fm}^{-1}\); including \(l \leq 6\) yields converged HFB results.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of ultracold Fermi gases (Urban et al., 2021) to pure neutron matter, adapting the strategy of using softened interactions with density-dependent cutoffs that was pioneered by Schwenk (2003) and applied to neutron matter by Ramanan and Urban (2018). It is similar in spirit to the work of Coraggio et al. (2013), who used Hartree-Fock plus third-order many-body perturbation theory with different regulators, but here the HFB starting point is essential for capturing pairing effects at low densities relevant to the inner crust of neutron stars. The results enable more reliable calculations of the neutron-matter equation of state for neutron-star crust applications, and the authors note that the remaining discrepancy with Quantum Monte Carlo results of Lovato et al. (2022) at higher densities (\(k_F > 1\,\text{fm}^{-1}\)) may be due to missing three-body forces.
