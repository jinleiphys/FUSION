# The nucleon-nucleon potential beyond the static approximation

> We point out that, due to the use of static nucleon propagators in Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory, the current calculations of the nucleon-nucleon potential miss certain contributions starting at two loops. These contributions give rise to contact interactions, which are both parametrically and numerically more important than the so called NNLO potentials. They show a peculiar dependence on the light quark masses, which should be taken into account when performing chiral extrapolations of lattice data. However, they do not appear to have an impact on phenomenology since they can be absorbed into redefinitions of unknown parameters which are usually fitted to data.

## Key claim

Due to the use of static nucleon propagators in Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT), current calculations of the nucleon-nucleon potential miss certain contributions starting at two loops; these contributions produce contact interactions that are parametrically and numerically more important than the so-called NNLO potentials, but they can be absorbed into redefinitions of unknown parameters fitted to data and therefore do not affect phenomenology.

## Method

The authors applied the strategy of regions (threshold expansions) to analyze individual Feynman diagrams for the nucleon-nucleon potential, using non-relativistic rather than static nucleon propagators. They identified the relevant loop-momentum regions where the kinetic term of the nucleon propagator becomes as important as the energy when integrating out degrees of freedom of energy scale m_pi. The complete two-loop contributions were calculated using the leading-order chiral Lagrangian (pionic, pion-nucleon, and nucleon-nucleon sectors) in the isospin limit, regularized in dimensional regularization.

## Key numbers

- The new two-loop contribution to the potential is proportional to M_N^(3/2) * m_pi^(5/2), with a numerical coefficient involving Gamma(3/4)^2 / (320 * sqrt(2) * pi^(7/2)) for the pure three-pion exchange diagram.
- The correction to the contact term C_S (Delta C_S) ranges from 21.2 to 54.1 (in units of 10^(-5) MeV^(-2)) when using values from Epelbaum (2004) for different cutoff combinations.
- The correction to the contact term C_T (Delta C_T) ranges from -2.8 to -0.3 (in units of 10^(-5) MeV^(-2)) for the same cutoff combinations.
- Input parameters used: g_A = 1.29, F_pi = 92.4 MeV, M_N = 939 MeV, m_pi = 139 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Weinberg approach (Weinberg, 1990, 1991) for calculating the nucleon-nucleon potential by identifying contributions missed when static nucleon propagators are used, as prescribed by standard HBChPT counting rules. The new contributions are related to the "radiation pions" discussed within the KSW approach (Kaplan, 1998; Fleming, 1999) and to fractional powers of the pion mass appearing in pion production off nucleon-nucleon systems (Cohen, 1995; Hanhart, 2000). The results indicate that chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD data for nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths must account for contributions proportional to m_q^(5/4), and that fits to data for the low-energy constants C_S and C_T (such as those in Epelbaum, 2004) need to be redone to incorporate these effects.
