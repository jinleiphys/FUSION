# Quantum Transition from Order to Chaos in the Nuclear Shell Model

> We discuss the role of quantum chaos in atomic nuclei. After reviewing the basic assumptions of the nuclear shell model, we analyze the spectral statistics of the energy levels obtained with realistic shell-model calculations in the fp shell. In particular, for Ca isotopes we observe a transition from order to chaos by increasing the excitation energy and a clear quantum signature of the breaking of integrability by changing the single-particle spacings.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates a transition from order to chaos in Ca isotopes as excitation energy increases, and shows that changing single-particle spacings can break integrability. The abstract's claim of a "clear quantum signature" is hedged by the fact that the Brody parameter only approaches but does not always reach the fully chaotic Wigner limit (ω=1), and the transition is quantified by a continuous parameter rather than a binary classification.

## Method
The author performed realistic shell-model calculations in the fp shell (f7/2, p3/2, f5/2, p1/2) using the code ANTOINE with Lanczos and Householder diagonalization algorithms, assuming 40Ca as an inert core with 4 to 10 valence neutrons. The residual interaction was a minimally modified Kuo-Brown realistic force with monopole improvements, and the spectral statistics of nearest-neighbour spacings were analyzed using the Brody distribution to quantify the degree of chaoticity.

## Key numbers
- Brody parameter ω for the whole spectrum of Ca isotopes ranges from 0.69 (44Ca) to 1.00 (49Ca), with 46Ca, 47Ca, 48Ca, 49Ca, and 50Ca all showing ω ≥ 0.95
- For 48Ca, the Brody parameter increases from ω = 0.54 at 6 MeV excitation energy to ω = 0.93 at 18 MeV excitation energy
- For 49Ca, ω increases from 0.63 at 6 MeV to 0.95 at 18 MeV
- For 50Ca, ω increases from 0.72 at 6 MeV to 0.92 at 18 MeV
- With degenerate single-particle spacings, 44Ca shows ω = 0.85 and 45Ca shows ω = 0.98
- With double-spaced single-particle levels, 44Ca shows ω = 0.29 and 45Ca shows ω = 0.58
- Single-particle energies used (in MeV): ε7/2 = 0, ε3/2 = 2, ε1/2 = 4, ε5/2 = 6.5

## Context
This work extends the study of quantum chaos in nuclear systems from experimental neutron and proton resonance data (where excellent agreement with GOE predictions had been found) to theoretical shell-model calculations in the fp shell. The results enable systematic investigation of how the competition between the integrable one-body mean-field Hamiltonian and the non-linear two-body residual interaction controls the transition to chaos, complementing earlier random matrix theory approaches by Bohigas and collaborators. The use of the Brody parameter as a continuous measure of chaoticity allows quantitative comparison across different isotopes and excitation energies.
