# Nonlocal calculation for nonstrange dibaryons and tribaryons

> We study the possible existence of nonstrange dibaryons and tribaryons by solving the bound-state problem of the two- and three-body systems composed of nucleons and deltas. The two-body systems are $NN$, $NΔ$, and $ΔΔ$, while the three-body systems are $NNN$, $NNΔ$, $NΔΔ$, and $ΔΔΔ$. We use as input the nonlocal $NN$, $NΔ$, and $ΔΔ$ potentials derived from the chiral quark cluster model by means of the resonating group method. We compare with previous results obtained from the local version based on the Born-Oppenheimer approximation.

# Nonlocal calculation for nonstrange dibaryons and tribaryons

## Key claim

The nonlocal chiral quark cluster model predicts bound states in the $N\Delta$ and $\Delta\Delta$ two-body systems and in several three-body systems composed of nucleons and deltas, but the number of bound states is generally smaller than predicted by the earlier local Born-Oppenheimer approximation; the authors emphasize they are studying "which are the best candidates for bound states and the ordering" rather than exact binding energies.

## Method

The authors solve the bound-state problem for two-body ($NN$, $N\Delta$, $\Delta\Delta$) and three-body ($NNN$, $NN\Delta$, $N\Delta\Delta$, $\Delta\Delta\Delta$) systems using nonlocal potentials derived from the chiral quark cluster model via the resonating group method (RGM) in momentum space. For the three-body systems, they use a truncated T-matrix approximation that includes only S-wave two-body amplitudes but incorporates the effect of the tensor force, solving the Faddeev equations for the bound-state problem. The results are compared with previous calculations using local potentials obtained from the Born-Oppenheimer approximation.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: 2.14 MeV (nonlocal model), compared to 3.13 MeV (local model) and 2.225 MeV (exact chiral quark cluster model including $\Delta\Delta$ partial wave $(l_{\Delta\Delta},s_{\Delta\Delta})=(4,3)$)
- $N\Delta$ system: one bound state with $(j,i)=(2,1)$ at 0.141 MeV (nonlocal model); the local model gave a bound state exactly at the $N\Delta$ threshold
- $\Delta\Delta$ system: five bound states with nonlocal interactions (six with local interactions); deepest bound state is $(j,i)=(1,0)$, then $(0,1)$, $(3,0)$, and $(2,1)$
- Triton ($NNN$) binding energy: 6.52 MeV (nonlocal model), 5.76 MeV (local model), compared to 6.58 MeV for Reid soft-core potential in the truncated T-matrix approximation and 8.49 MeV experimental value
- $NN\Delta$ system: two bound states with nonlocal interactions, $(S,I)=({3\over 2},{1\over 2})$ at 0.143 MeV and $({3\over 2},{3\over 2})$ bound; neither bound with local interactions
- $N\Delta\Delta$ system: three bound states with nonlocal interactions: $(S,I)=({1 \over 2},{5\over 2})$ at 0.630 MeV, $({5\over 2},{1\over 2})$ at 8.158 MeV, and $({5 \over 2},{5 \over 2})$ at 0.181 MeV
- $\Delta\Delta\Delta$ system: four bound states with nonlocal interactions (seven with local interactions); the $(S,I)=({1\over 2},{1\over 2})$ state has the largest separation energy

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by the same group (Mota et al., 1999-2001) that used the Born-Oppenheimer approximation to obtain local potentials for baryon-baryon interactions. The nonlocal RGM approach overcomes the Born-Oppenheimer approximation and treats inter-cluster dynamics exactly within the chosen Hilbert space. The method is benchmarked against the three-nucleon bound-state problem using the Reid soft-core potential (Harper et al., 1972; Bertsch et al., 1972). The results enable predictions for dibaryon and tribaryon resonances that could be observed experimentally, with the $N\Delta$ bound state at invariant mass approximately 2.17 GeV corresponding to the well-known $NN$ $^1D_2$ resonance seen in analyses by Arndt et al. (1990s) and Hoshizaki et al. (1980s). The $(j,i)=(3,0)$ $\Delta\Delta$ state would correspond to a new $NN$ resonance in the $^3D_3$ partial wave not previously confirmed.
