# Systematic investigation of the nuclear multiple deformations in U+U collisions with A Multi-Phase Transport model

> Relativistic heavy ion collisions provide a unique opportunity to study the shape of colliding nuclei, even up to higher-order multiple deformations. In this work, several observables that are sensitive to quadrupole and hexadecapole deformations of Uranium-238 in relativistic U+U collisions have been systematically investigated with A Multi-Phase Transport model. We find that the flow harmonic $v_{2}$, the $v_{2}$ and mean transverse momentum correlation, and the three-particle asymmetry cumulant ${\rm ac}_{2}\{3\}$ are sensitive to nuclear quadrupole deformation, while ${\rm ac}_{2}\{3\}$ and nonlinear response coefficient $χ_{4,22}$ are sensitive to nuclear hexadecapole deformation. Our results from transport model studies are in qualitative agreement with previous hydrodynamic studies. The results indicate that the uncertainties of the hexadecapole deformation of Uranium on the quadrupole deformation determination can be reduced by the abundance of correlation observables provided by the relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the nonlinear response coefficient χ₄,₂₂ is an ideal observable for extracting the hexadecapole deformation (β₄) of Uranium-238 because it is sensitive to β₄ while being largely insensitive to quadrupole deformation (β₂), but this conclusion carries the hedge that the effect of β₄ on χ₄,₂₂ in the AMPT transport model is slightly (∼20%) weaker than in previous hydrodynamic simulations, meaning model uncertainties must be quantified for precise extraction.

## Method

The authors used the string melting version of the A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model to simulate U+U collisions at √s_NN = 193 GeV and Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, with three sets of deformation parameters for Uranium (β₂ = 0.282, β₄ = 0.0; β₂ = 0.247, β₄ = 0.1; β₂ = 0.282, β₄ = 0.1) and a reference Au nucleus with β₂ = -0.131, β₄ = 0.0. They computed flow harmonics v₂ and v₄, the mean transverse momentum variance ⟨(δp_T)²⟩, the Pearson correlation coefficient ρ(v₂², [p_T]), the three-particle asymmetry cumulant ac₂{3}, and the nonlinear response coefficient χ₄,₂₂, presenting results as ratios of U+U to Au+Au values across 0-60% centrality.

## Key numbers

- Three sets of Uranium deformation parameters were used: (a) β₂ = 0.282, β₄ = 0.00; (b) β₂ = 0.247, β₄ = 0.10; (c) β₂ = 0.282, β₄ = 0.10, all with R₀ = 6.80 fm and a = 0.615 fm.
- The Au reference nucleus used R₀ = 6.38 fm, a = 0.535 fm, β₂ = -0.131, β₄ = 0.00.
- The AMPT model parameters included parton screening mass μ_D = 3.2032 fm⁻¹, strong coupling α_S = 0.33, and total cross section σ = 1.5 mb.
- The uncertainty in β₄ of 0.1 gives Δβ₂ ≈ 0.035 for the quadrupole deformation uncertainty of Uranium.
- The effect of β₄ on χ₄,₂₂ in AMPT is quantitatively ∼20% weaker than the hydrodynamic response shown in Xu (2024).

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic studies by Xu (2024) that proposed χ₄,₂₂ as a probe of hexadecapole deformation, and addresses ambiguities in β₂ extraction from elliptic flow data at RHIC noted by Ryssens (2023) and the STAR Collaboration (2024). The results enable future precise extraction of both β₂ and β₄ from relativistic heavy ion collisions, with the suggestion that using v₂ for quadrupole and χ₄,₂₂ for hexadecapole deformation can reduce the uncertainty that β₄ introduces into β₂ measurements. The paper also notes that the AMPT model's response to radial flow differs from hydrodynamics, as the [p_T] variance in AMPT is insensitive to nuclear deformation, contrasting with hydrodynamic simulations.
