# Friction Coefficient for Deep-Inelastic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Based on the microscopic model, the friction coefficient for the relative motion of nuclei in deep-inelastic heavy-ion collisions is calculated. The radial dependence of the friction coefficient is studied and the results are compared with those found by other methods. Based on this result, it was demonstrated that the kinetic energy dissipation in deep-inelastic heavy-ion collisions is a gradual process which takes up a significant part of a reaction time. An advantage of the suggested method is that it allows one to consider the relative motion of nuclei and the intrinsic motion self-consistently.

## Key claim

The friction coefficient for deep-inelastic heavy-ion collisions is calculated using a microscopic model that avoids the assumption of fast statistical equilibration of the dissipated kinetic energy, instead solving for the time evolution of single-particle occupation numbers self-consistently with the relative motion.

## Method

The authors start from the linear response theory expression for the radial friction coefficient and solve numerically the coupled equations of motion for the relative distance and the single-particle occupation numbers, using a master equation in the τ-approximation for the residual interaction. The single-particle basis is constructed from the asymptotic wave functions of the noninteracting projectile and target nuclei, and the matrix elements for particle-hole transitions and nucleon exchange are calculated using the Woods-Saxon potential with parameters r₀ = 1.18 fm and a = 0.54 fm. The time step Δt is taken as (0.8–1.0) × 10⁻²² s, and the initial conditions are R(0) = 20 fm with occupation numbers of 1 or 0 for occupied and unoccupied states of the noninteracting nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For the ⁶⁴Zn(440 MeV) + ¹⁹⁶Pt reaction, the radial friction coefficient is calculated as a function of R for the approach phase and compared with results from Gross and Kalinowski (classical model) and from a microscopic model with constant temperature (0.5 MeV to 2 MeV).
- For the ⁶⁴Zn(440 MeV) + ¹⁹⁶Pt and ⁵⁶Fe(480 MeV) + ²⁰⁸Pb reactions, the radial friction coefficient is larger for the approach stage than for the departure stage.
- In the classical model of Gross and Kalinowski, kinetic energy is dissipated during a short time interval of the order of 0.4 × 10⁻²¹ s, while in the present model the dissipation takes significantly longer.
- The effective temperature is given by Θ_K(t) = 3.46 √(E_K*(t) / ⟨A_K(t)⟩), where E_K* is the intrinsic excitation energy and ⟨A_K⟩ is the average mass number.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic approach to nuclear friction by explicitly including the time evolution of single-particle occupation numbers, in contrast to earlier models that assumed a constant temperature corresponding to the total excitation energy (e.g., the approach of Gross and Kalinowski (1978) and the microscopic model of Hofmann and collaborators (1976)). The results support the idea of gradual kinetic energy dissipation argued by Swiatecki (1981) and others, rather than the fast thermalization predicted by classical phenomenological models. The method enables self-consistent treatment of relative and intrinsic motion and can be applied to study the influence of nuclear shell structure on deep-inelastic collisions.
