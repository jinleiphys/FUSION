# A Study of Charge Radii and Neutron Skin Thickness near Nuclear Drip Lines

> We studied the charge radius, rms radius and neutron skin thickness $Œîr_{np}$ in even-even isotopes of Si, S, Ar and Ca and isotones of N =20, 28, 50 and 82. The $Œîr_{np}$ in doubly-magic $^{48}$Ca, $^{68}$Ni, $^{120,132}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb nuclei has also been calculated. Theoretical calculations are done with the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory with the effective Skyrme interactions. Calculated theoretical estimates are in good agreement with the recently available experimental data. The charge radii for Si, S, Ar and Ca isotopes is observed to be minimum at neutron number N =14. The theoretically computed results with UNEDF0 model parameterization of functional are reasonably reproducing the experimental data for $Œîr_{np}$ in $^{48}$Ca, $^{68}$Ni and $^{120,132}$Sn. The energy density functional of UNEDF1 model provides much improved result of $Œîr_{np}$ for $^{208}$Pb.

## Key claim

The paper claims that Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory with Skyrme interactions and UNEDF0/UNEDF1 parameterizations can reproduce experimental charge radii and neutron skin thickness for isotopes of Si, S, Ar, Ca and selected doubly-magic nuclei, but the agreement is qualified: UNEDF0 overestimates neutron skin thickness for lower-mass doubly-magic nuclei by approximately 0.03‚Äì0.1 fm compared to experimental data, while UNEDF1 provides improved results specifically for \(^{208}\)Pb.

## Method

The authors performed axially deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using zero-range Skyrme effective interactions with the harmonic oscillator basis, employing the UNEDF0 and UNEDF1 energy density functional parameterizations. They computed root-mean-square radii, charge radii (using the formula \(R_{ch} = \sqrt{r_p^2 + 0.64}\) fm to account for the finite proton size), and neutron skin thickness \(\Delta r_{np} = \sqrt{r_n^2} - \sqrt{r_p^2}\) for even-even isotopes of Si, S, Ar, Ca and isotones at N = 20, 28, 50, 82, as well as for the doubly-magic nuclei \(^{48}\)Ca, \(^{68}\)Ni, \(^{120,132}\)Sn, and \(^{208}\)Pb.

## Key numbers

- Charge radii for Si, S, Ar, and Ca isotopes show a minimum at neutron number N = 14, with specific minima in \(^{28}\)Si (N = Z = 14), \(^{30}\)S (N = 14, Z = 16), and \(^{32}\)Ar (N = 14, Z = 18).
- Neutron skin thickness \(\Delta r_{np}\) for \(^{28}\)O (N = 20 isotone) is approximately 0.7 fm from the HFB+HO model with UNEDF0.
- For the N = 28 isotone chain, maximum \(\Delta r_{np}\) values range from 0.65 to 0.76 fm for \(^{40}\)Mg and \(^{42}\)Si.
- For N = 50 isotones, \(\Delta r_{np}\) ranges from 0.021 fm in \(^{94}\)Ru to 0.36 fm in \(^{78}\)Ni.
- For N = 82 isotones, \(\Delta r_{np}\) varies from 0.005 fm in \(^{150}\)Er to 0.32 fm in \(^{128}\)Er (UNEDF0).
- Theoretical \(\Delta r_{np}\) for \(^{132}\)Sn is 0.26 fm, comparable to experimental extractions of \(0.23 \pm 0.02\) fm and \(0.29 \pm 0.04\) fm.
- Theoretical \(\Delta r_{np}\) for \(^{208}\)Pb is 0.17 fm, within the experimental range of \(0.15 \pm 0.03\) fm to \(0.22 \pm 0.04\) fm.
- For doubly-magic nuclei with Z/N \(\approx\) 0.7 (\(^{48}\)Ca, \(^{120}\)Sn, \(^{208}\)Pb), \(\Delta r_{np}\) lies in the range 0.16‚Äì0.19 fm.
- For Z/N \(\approx\) 0.5‚Äì0.6 (\(^{42}\)Si, \(^{44}\)S, \(^{132}\)Sn), \(\Delta r_{np}\) exceeds 0.25 fm.
- UNEDF0 overestimates \(\Delta r_{np}\) for \(^{48}\)Ca by approximately 0.01 fm, for \(^{68}\)Ni by approximately 0.09‚Äì0.1 fm compared to experimental data.

## Context

This work extends self-consistent mean-field studies of nuclear radii and neutron skin thickness using Skyrme-HFB theory, building on prior calculations by Angeli (2013) for charge radii and by Centelles (2009), Brown (2017), and Brown (2009) for neutron skin. The results connect to experimental efforts measuring electric dipole polarizability in \(^{208}\)Pb by Tamii (2011), in \(^{120}\)Sn by Hashimoto (2015), and in \(^{68}\)Ni by Rossi (2013), and to the ongoing Darmstadt-Osaka collaboration measurement for \(^{48}\)Ca at Jefferson Laboratory. The findings enable constraints on the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy and its implications for neutron star structure, as discussed by Lattimer (2013) and Hagen (2015).
