# Proton-neutron pairing and binding energies of nuclei close to N=Z line

> We analyse the contribution of isovector and isoscalar proton-neutron pairing to the binding energies of even-even nuclei with $N-Z=0,2,4$ and atomic mass $20 < A <100$. The binding energies are calculated in the mean-field approach by coupling a Skyrme-type functional to an isovector-isoscalar pairing force of zero range. The latter is treated in the framework of quartet condensation model (QCM), which conserves exactly the particle number and the isospin. The interdependence of pairing and deformation is taken into account by performing self-consistent Skyrme-HF+QCM calculations in the intrinsic system. It is shown that the binding energies are not changing much when the isoscalar pairing is switched on. This fact is related to the off-diagonal matrix elements of the pairing force, which is less attractive for the isoscalar force, and to the competition between the isoscalar and isovector pairing channels.

# Proton-neutron pairing and binding energies of nuclei close to N=Z line

## Key claim

The binding energies of even-even nuclei near the N=Z line (with N-Z = 0, 2, 4 and 20 < A < 100) are not changed much when isoscalar (T=0) proton-neutron pairing is switched on, because the off-diagonal matrix elements of the isoscalar pairing force are less attractive than those of the isovector force, and because the isovector and isoscalar pairing channels compete with each other and with the mean field.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations (using the UNE1 functional and the EV8 code) coupled to a zero-range isovector-isoscalar pairing force, treated within the Quartet Condensation Model (QCM) which conserves particle number and isospin exactly. The QCM ground state for N=Z nuclei is a product of isovector quartets (two protons and two neutrons coupled to T=0) plus isoscalar pair condensates; for N>Z nuclei, excess neutron pairs are added. The calculations were done iteratively in an axially deformed intrinsic system, with the pairing force strength V0 = 350 MeV/fm^3 and the ratio w = V0(T=0)/V0(T=1) varied from 0 to 2.0, using 10 neutron and 10 proton single-particle states above the core.

## Key numbers

- For N=Z nuclei with 60 < A < 80, Skyrme-HF results underestimate binding energies by about 3-4 MeV; Skyrme-HF+BCS (nn and pp pairing only) reduces residuals by about 1 MeV compared to HF.
- For 64Ge with V0 = 350 MeV/fm^3 and w = 1.5, the mean field energy increases by about 530 keV when the isoscalar channel is turned on, while the total pairing energy decreases by about the same quantity.
- For 64Ge with w = 2.0, the mean field energy increases by about 540 keV relative to the isovector-only case.
- Pairing gaps extracted from odd-even mass differences are 3.6 MeV for 40Ca and 3.2 MeV for 56Ni.
- For 40Ca and 56Ni, QCM predicts significant pairing correlations even though BCS predicts no pairing for these double magic nuclei.

## Context

This work extends previous QCM studies (Negrea et al., 2019; Negrea et al., 2020) from exactly solvable pairing Hamiltonians to self-consistent mean-field calculations, and addresses the competition between pairing and deformation that was neglected in most earlier HFB calculations (Goodman, 2001; Gezerlis et al., 2011). The results contradict the expectation that isoscalar pairing would significantly increase binding energies, and show that isovector and isoscalar pn pairing coexist in all nuclei studied, including N=Z+4 systems. The approach enables future studies that include proton-neutron pairs with S=1, Sz=±1 and that restore angular momentum via projection.
