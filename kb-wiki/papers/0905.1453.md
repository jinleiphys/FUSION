# Novel two-to-three hard hadronic processes and possible studies of generalized parton distributions at hadron facilities

> We consider a novel class of hard branching hadronic processes a+b->c+d+e, where hadrons c and d have large and nearly opposite transverse momenta and large invariant energy which is a finite fraction of the total invariant energy. We use color transparency logic to argue that these processes can be used to study quark generalized parton distributions (GPDs) for baryons and mesons in hadron collisions hence complementing and adding to the studies of GPDs in the exclusive DIS (deep inelastic scattering) processes. We propose that a number of GPDs can be investigated in hadron facilities such as J-PARC (Japan Proton Accelerator Research Complex) facility and GSI-FAIR (Gesellschaft fur Schwerionenforschung -Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research) project. In this work, the GPDs for the nucleon and for the N->Delta transition are studied in the reaction N+N->N+pi+B, where N, pi, and B are a nucleon, a pion, and a baryon (nucleon or Delta), respectively, with a large momentum transfer between B (or pi) and the incident nucleon. In particular, the Efremov-Radyushkin-Brodsky-Lepage (ERBL) region of the GPDs can be measured in such exclusive reactions. We estimate the cross section of the processes N+N->N+pi+B by using current models for relevant GPDs and information about large angle pi N reactions. We find that it will be feasible to measure these cross sections at the high-energy hadron facilities, and get novel information about the nucleon structure, for example, contributions of quark orbital angular momenta to the nucleon spin. The studies of N->Delta transition GPDs could be valuable also for investigating electromagnetic properties of the transition.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the novel class of hard two-to-three hadronic processes, specifically \(N+N \rightarrow N+\pi+B\), can be used to study generalized parton distributions (GPDs) for the nucleon and the \(N \rightarrow \Delta\) transition in the Efremov-Radyushkin-Brodsky-Lepage (ERBL) region at hadron facilities, provided that the kinematics satisfy the condition that the coherence length for the small-size wave packets is larger than the nucleon size of about 0.8 fm, corresponding to final hadron momenta of at least 3 to 4 GeV/c.

## Method

The authors factorize the cross section for the \(N+N \rightarrow N+\pi+B\) process into a \(N \rightarrow B\) transition amplitude, expressed through nucleonic or \(N \rightarrow \Delta\) GPDs, and a hard meson-nucleon scattering amplitude, using color transparency arguments to justify the dominance of small-size \(q\bar q\) configurations. They estimate the cross section by combining current models for the relevant GPDs, taken from the Bochum group, with parametrizations of large-angle \(\pi N\) scattering data, and evaluate the feasibility for kinematics typical of the J-PARC facility with incident proton momenta of 8 GeV/c or higher.

## Key numbers

- The coherence length for wave packet evolution is estimated as \(l_{coh} = (0.3 \div 0.4\ \text{fm}) \cdot p_h/(\text{GeV}/c)\), and the condition for factorization requires \(l_{coh} > r_N \sim 0.8\ \text{fm}\), which is satisfied for final hadron momenta \(p_c \ge 3 \div 4\ \text{GeV}/c\) and \(p_d \ge 3 \div 4\ \text{GeV}/c\).
- The center-of-mass energy at J-PARC is \(\sqrt{s} \simeq \sqrt{60} \div \sqrt{100}\ \text{GeV}\).
- The invariant energy of the subprocess is \(s' = (1-\alpha)s\), and the meson-nucleon scattering is considered at large angles with \(s_{\pi N} \sim 12\ \text{GeV}^2\) and \(\theta_{cm} \sim 90^\circ\).
- The ratio of \(K^+p \to K^+p\) to \(\pi^+p \to \pi^+p\) cross sections at \(\theta_{cm}=90^\circ\) is close to the square of the wave functions at the origin: \([f_K/f_\pi]^2 \approx 1.4\).

## Context

This work extends the study of GPDs, which are typically accessed in exclusive deep inelastic scattering (DIS) processes at lepton facilities like HERA, to hadron-hadron collisions at facilities such as J-PARC and GSI-FAIR. It builds on the color transparency logic of Brodsky and Mueller and the factorization arguments for exclusive processes by Collins, Frankfurt, and Strikman (1997), and it uses GPD models from the Bochum group. The proposed reactions enable access to the ERBL region of GPDs and to \(N \rightarrow \Delta\) transition GPDs, which are linked to electromagnetic transition form factors studied in low-energy electron experiments and to quark orbital angular momentum contributions to the nucleon spin, as discussed by Ji (1997).
