# Intermediate mass strangelets are positively charged

> For a limited range of parameters, stable strange quark matter may be negatively charged in bulk due to one gluon exchange interactions. However, the reduction in strange quark occupation in the surface layer, which is responsible for surface tension, more than compensates this for intermediate mass strangelets, which therefore always have positive quark charge (e.g. for baryon number between 10^2 and 10^{18} assuming alpha_S=0.9). While details are sensitive to the choice of renormalisation, the general conclusion is not. This rules out a scenario where negatively charged strangelets produced in ultrarelativistic heavy ion colliders might grow indefinitely with potentially disastrous consequences.

# Intermediate mass strangelets are positively charged

## Key claim

Even in the unlikely scenario where bulk strange quark matter is both stable and negatively charged, intermediate mass strangelets (baryon number between 10² and 10¹⁸ for α_S=0.9) always have positive quark charge due to surface depletion of strange quarks, which blocks the growth path that could lead to catastrophic consequences.

## Method

The author uses the MIT bag model with multiple reflection expansion to compute the thermodynamic properties of finite-size strange quark matter spheres, including volume, surface, and curvature contributions to the energy and quark number densities. First-order α_S corrections are included in the volume terms, and the system is minimized at fixed baryon number while maintaining chemical equilibrium via weak interactions, with electrons and positrons included for local charge neutrality.

## Key numbers

- For α_S=0.9, bulk strange quark matter reaches a negative charge per baryon of only −10⁻⁶ (dimensionless ratio)
- For α_S=1.2, bulk strange quark matter reaches a negative charge per baryon of approximately −10⁻³
- Finite size effects increase the total charge per baryon by ΔZ/A ≈ 0.3 A⁻¹/³ ≈ 0.3 R_fm⁻¹
- For α_S=0.9, strangelets are positively charged for baryon numbers between 10² and 10¹⁸ (radii from a few fm up to 10⁶ fm)
- For α_S=1.2, strangelets are positively charged for baryon numbers between 10² and 10⁷

## Context

This work extends the original studies of strange quark matter by Bodmer (1971) and Witten (1984), and the detailed bulk and finite-size calculations by Farhi and Jaffe (1984). Farhi and Jaffe had shown that for sufficiently high α_S, bulk strange matter could become negatively charged due to one-gluon exchange interactions, but noted sensitivity to renormalization. The present paper demonstrates that surface depletion of massive strange quarks, a quantum mechanical effect analogous to the positive surface energy in nuclei, overcomes this bulk negative charge for all intermediate-mass strangelets, thereby ruling out the disaster scenarios discussed by Jaffe, Busza, Sandweiss, and Wilczek (2000) and Dar, De Rujula, and Heinz (1999). The result enables future work to focus on the already-unlikely scenario of negatively charged very small strangelets, which would still be unable to grow through the positively charged intermediate regime.
