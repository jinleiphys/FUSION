# Mott dissociation of D-mesons at the chiral phase transition and anomalous J/Psi suppression

> We investigate the in-medium modification of the charmonium breakup processes due to the Mott-effect for D-mesons at the chiral phase transition. A model calculation for the process J/Psi+pi --> D+D^*+ h.c. is presented which demonstrates that threshold effects in the thermal averaged breakup cross section can be explained as a Mott transition where final state quark-antiquark bound states enter the continuum of resonant states at the QCD phase transition. Applications to heavy-ion collisions within a modified Glauber model scenario and the phenomenon of anomalous J/$ψ$ suppression in the CERN NA50 experiment are addressed.

# Mott dissociation of D-mesons at the chiral phase transition and anomalous J/Psi suppression

## Key claim

The anomalous J/ψ suppression observed in Pb-Pb collisions at CERN NA50 may be explained by a critical enhancement of charmonium breakup cross sections at the chiral/deconfinement phase transition, specifically due to the Mott effect for D-mesons which lowers reaction thresholds when open charm states become unbound.

## Method

The authors calculate the in-medium J/ψ breakup cross section for the process J/ψ + π → D + D* + h.c. in a hot pion gas using a Born collision approximation with temperature-dependent Breit-Wigner spectral functions for the D and D* mesons. The thermal averaged breakup cross section is then applied within a modified Glauber model to compute the J/ψ suppression ratio as a function of transverse energy for Pb-Pb collisions at 158 AGeV.

## Key numbers

- Critical Mott temperatures: T_Mott_D* = 110 MeV, T_Mott_D = 140 MeV
- D* mass: 2.01 GeV; D mass: 1.87 GeV; threshold s0 = 15.05 GeV^2
- Vacuum cross section fit parameters: σ0 = 7.5 × 10^9 mb, λ = 0.9 GeV
- Nuclear absorption cross section for J/ψ in p-A collisions: approximately 3 mb
- D-meson width coefficient: c = 2.67, with Γ_D(T) = c (T - T_Mott_D) Θ(T - T_Mott_D)
- At T = 140 MeV, the D* meson has a half width of about 80 MeV
- Anomalous suppression onset observed at transverse energy E_T ~ 40 GeV in NA50 data

## Context

This work extends the kinetic dissociation approach to charmonium suppression, building on the original quark-gluon plasma screening prediction by Matsui and Satz (1986) and subsequent recognition by Blaizot (1987) that J/ψ Mott dissociation does not occur at deconfinement temperatures. The calculation uses the diagrammatic quark exchange method of Barnes and Swanson (1991) and generalizes finite-temperature Green function techniques from Blaschke et al. (1993, 1995). The results enable a systematic framework for heavy quark bound state diagnostics in heavy-ion collisions, with planned improvements using Dyson-Schwinger equations as applied by Ivanov, Kalinovsky, and Roberts (1999) and Blaschke, Burau, Kalinovsky, and Roberts (2000).
