# Contribution of asymmetric strange-antistrange sea to the Paschos-Wolfenstein relation

> The NuTeV Collaboration reported a value of $\sin^{2}θ_{w}$ measured in neutrino-nucleon deep inelastic scattering, and found that the value is three standard deviations from the standard model prediction. This result is obtained under the assumption that the strange-antistrange sea quarks of nucleons are symmetric, and that the up and down quark distributions are symmetric with the simultaneous interchange of $u$$\leftrightarrow$$d$ and $p$$\leftrightarrow$$n$. We discuss the contribution of asymmetric strange-antistrange sea to the Paschos-Wolfenstein relation in the extraction of weak mixing angle $\sin^{2}θ_{w}$. We also point out that the contribution of asymmetric strange-antistrange sea should remove roughly 30--80% of the discrepancy between the NuTeV result and other determinations of $\sin^{2}θ_{w}$, when using the light-cone meson-baryon model to calculate the contribution of the strange-antistrange sea.

## Key claim

The asymmetry between strange and antistrange quark distributions in the nucleon sea, calculated using the light-cone meson-baryon fluctuation model, should remove roughly 30–80% of the discrepancy between the NuTeV measurement of sin²θ_w and the standard model prediction, depending on the assumed probability (4–10%) of the K⁺Λ Fock state in the proton.

## Method

The authors derived a modified Paschos-Wolfenstein relation that includes a correction term δR⁻_s arising from the integrated asymmetry S⁻ = ∫ x[s(x) – s̄(x)] dx. They calculated the s and s̄ distributions using the light-cone meson-baryon fluctuation model, where the intrinsic strange sea of the proton is represented by a K⁺Λ Fock state, with both Gaussian and power-law wave function forms. The up and down valence quark distributions were computed using a quark-diquark spectator model, and the resulting S⁻ and Q_V values were used to evaluate the shift in sin²θ_w.

## Key numbers

- NuTeV measured sin²θ_w = 0.2277 ± 0.0013 (stat) ± 0.0009 (syst), compared to the standard model value 0.2227 ± 0.0004 from other electroweak processes.
- The calculated integrated strange asymmetry S⁻ ranges from 0.0042 to 0.0106 for the Gaussian wave function (0.0035 to 0.0087 for the power-law wave function), corresponding to K⁺Λ probabilities of 4% and 10%.
- The correction to the Paschos-Wolfenstein relation δR⁻_s ranges from 0.0017 to 0.0041 for the Gaussian wave function (0.0014 to 0.0034 for the power-law wave function).
- The shift in sin²θ_w reduces the discrepancy from 0.005 to 0.0033 (power-law, 4% probability) or 0.0009 (Gaussian, 10% probability).
- The crossover point where s(x) = s̄(x) occurs at x = 0.235.

## Context

This work extends the light-cone meson-baryon fluctuation model of Brodsky and Ma (1992) to address the NuTeV anomaly, where the NuTeV Collaboration (2002) reported a sin²θ_w value three standard deviations above the standard model prediction. The authors find a significant correction from strange-antistrange asymmetry, contradicting the earlier conclusion of Cao and Signal (2003) that the effect is fairly small. This result suggests that future experiments should carefully investigate the strange-antistrange asymmetry to resolve the discrepancy, alongside other corrections such as charge symmetry violation (Londergan and Thomas, 1998) and nuclear effects.
