# Peres lattices in nuclear structure

> A method by Peres is used to draw spectra of the interacting boson model as lattices in the plane of energy versus an arbitrary observable average (or variance). Ordered (disordered) lattices are signatures of regularity (chaos) in both quantum and classical dynamics. The method is also apt to disclose exact or approximate dynamical symmetry.

# Peres lattices in nuclear structure

## Key claim
The Peres lattice method can distinguish regular from chaotic quantum dynamics by visual inspection of individual eigenstates, and is particularly suited to reveal exact or approximate dynamical symmetries in nuclear models. The method works for any choice of observable operator, and regularity of the lattice is expected when the system is integrable based on semiclassical Einstein-Brillouin-Keller (EBK) quantisation arguments.

## Method
The authors construct Peres invariants by taking the infinite time average of an arbitrary operator in the Heisenberg picture, then plot the expectation values of these invariants against energy for eigenstates of the interacting boson model (IBM) Hamiltonian. They examine both the mean values (Peres lattices) and variances of the invariants for states with angular momentum L=0 and N=40 bosons, scanning across the Casten triangle defined by parameters (η,χ). The method is applied to Casimir operators of the U(5), O(6), SU(3), and O(5) groups, and the results are compared with the classical regular fraction of phase space.

## Key numbers
- Total boson number N=40 used for all calculations of L=0 eigenstates
- The Casten triangle vertices correspond to (η,χ) = (1,0) for U(5), (0,0) for O(6), and (0,−√7/2) for SU(3) dynamical symmetries
- Parameter η=0.5 is fixed while χ is varied across the Casten triangle to study mixed regular/chaotic dynamics
- The saddle point energy E_sad and limiting energy E_lim of the classical IBM potential mark major changes in lattice patterns
- A roughly linear dependence of ⟨Q·Q^(SU(3))⟩ on energy is observed below E_sad for χ < −0.8, associated with quasi SU(3) dynamical symmetry

## Context
This work extends the method introduced by Peres (1984) for distinguishing quantum order and chaos, applying it specifically to the interacting boson model of nuclear structure. It builds on prior studies of mixed regular/chaotic dynamics in the Casten triangle by Alhassid and collaborators and by Arcos and collaborators. The method enables identification of approximate dynamical symmetries such as quasi dynamical symmetry (Rowe) and partial dynamical symmetry (Levit and collaborators), and can be applied beyond nuclear physics to any quantum model.
