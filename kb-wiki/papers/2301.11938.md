# Fast Neutrino Flavor Conversion in Core-Collapse Supernovae: A Parametric Study in 1D Models

> We explore the impact of small-scale flavor conversions of neutrinos, the so-called fast flavor conversions (FFCs), on the dynamical evolution and neutrino emission of core-collapse supernovae (CCSNe). In order to do that, we implement FFCs in the spherically symmetric (1D) CCSN simulations of a 20 solar-mass progenitor model parametrically, assuming that FFCs happen at densities lower than a systematically varied threshold value and lead to an immediate flavor equilibrium consistent with lepton number conservation. We find that besides hardening the electron neutrino and antineutrino spectra, which helps the expansion of the shock by enhanced postshock heating, FFCs can cause significant, nontrivial modifications of the energy transport in the SN environment via increasing the heavy-lepton neutrino luminosities. In our non-exploding models this results in extra cooling of the layers around the neutrinospheres, which triggers a faster contraction of the proto-neutron star and hence, in our 1D models, hampers the CCSN explosion. Although our study is limited by the 1D nature of our simulations, it provides valuable insights into how neutrino flavor conversions in the deepest CCSN regions can impact the neutrino release and the corresponding response of the stellar medium.

## Key claim

Fast flavor conversions (FFCs) implemented parametrically in 1D core-collapse supernova simulations of a 20 solar-mass progenitor, assuming immediate flavor equilibrium below a variable density threshold, can harden electron neutrino and antineutrino spectra and increase heavy-lepton neutrino luminosities, but in these non-exploding 1D models the extra cooling around the neutrinospheres triggers faster proto-neutron star contraction and hampers the explosion, contrary to the shock-expansion benefit from spectral hardening alone.

## Method

The authors implemented a schematic, lepton-number-conserving flavor-equilibration scheme in the spherically symmetric (1D) version of the Aenus-Alcar code, using the SFHo equation of state and a two-moment neutrino transport scheme with Minerbo closure. FFCs were assumed to occur instantaneously in all grid cells where the matter density fell below a systematically varied threshold density rho_c (from 10^9 to 10^14 g cm^-3), with full flavor equipartition enforced under conservation of electron lepton number and total neutrino and antineutrino numbers per energy bin, including Pauli blocking limits. The 20 solar-mass progenitor model S20 from Woosley et al. (2007) was used, and simulations were run for at least 0.5 seconds post-bounce.

## Key numbers

- Progenitor mass: 20 solar masses (M_sun), model S20 from Woosley et al. (2007).
- Threshold densities rho_c scanned from 10^9 g cm^-3 to 10^14 g cm^-3 in steps of factors of 10, plus intermediate values at 10^(n+0.5) g cm^-3 for n=9 to 13.
- Radial grid: 640 logarithmically spaced zones up to 10,000 km; energy grid: 15 logarithmically spaced bins up to 400 MeV.
- No explosion occurred in any simulation (neither the no-flavor-conversion reference nor any FFC model) within the simulated post-bounce periods of at least 0.5 seconds.
- The PNS radius was defined at a density of 10^11 g cm^-3.

## Context

This work extends parametric studies of FFCs in CCSNe (e.g., Li et al. 2021, Just et al. 2022) by implementing a schematic flavor-equilibration scheme directly in 1D neutrino-hydrodynamic simulations with feedback on the dynamics, rather than in post-processing or fixed backgrounds. It contrasts with the concurrent work of Nagakura (2023), which solved quantum kinetic equations on a fixed hydrostatic background without dynamical feedback. The results enable future multi-dimensional simulations to assess whether the cooling-induced contraction found here is an artifact of spherical symmetry or a robust effect that could influence the explosion mechanism.
