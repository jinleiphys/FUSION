# Generalized-Seniority Pattern and Thermal Properties in Even Sn Isotopes

> Even tin isotopes of mass number $A = 108 \sim 124$ are calculated with realistic interactions in the generalized-seniority approximation of the nuclear shell model. For each nucleus, we compute the lowest ten thousand states ($5000$ of each parity) up to around $8$ MeV in excitation energy, by allowing as many as four broken pairs. The lowest fifty eigen energies of each parity are compared with the exact results of the large-scale shell-model calculation. The wavefunctions of the mid-shell nuclei show a clear pattern of the stepwise breakup of condensed coherent pairs with increasing excitation energy. We also compute in the canonical ensemble the thermal properties -- level density, entropy, and specific heat -- in relation to the thermal pairing phase transition.

## Key claim

The generalized-seniority truncation to four broken pairs (subspace up to seniority eight) provides an accurate approximation for the low-lying spectrum of even tin isotopes, with the superfluid structure of stepwise pair breakup persisting at higher excitation energies, but the approximation is best near mid-shell and degrades away from it; the eigenstates show sizable generalized-seniority mixing and practically no pure generalized-seniority states exist.

## Method

The authors performed generalized-seniority shell-model calculations for even tin isotopes A=108-124 using a realistic Hamiltonian derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential and renormalized via the G-matrix approach, with parameters fitted to 157 experimental yrast energies in 102-132Sn. They truncated the full shell-model space to the subspace of four broken pairs (up to generalized seniority eight), computed the lowest 5000 eigenstates of each parity up to about 8 MeV excitation energy using a Lanczos method, and compared the lowest 50 eigenenergies of each parity with exact large-scale shell-model results. For the canonical ensemble, they computed level density, entropy, and specific heat by discrete summation over the computed eigenstates.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy errors relative to exact shell model: dE = 3, 4, 7, 10, 9, 7, 3 keV for 110-122Sn respectively
- Hamiltonian matrix dimension in M-scheme: 646,430 (for M=0 including both parities)
- Energy cutoff for computed states: approximately 8 MeV excitation energy
- Temperature range for reliable canonical-ensemble results: up to k_B T ≈ 0.5 MeV
- Pairing phase transition temperature: around k_B T_c ≈ 0.3 MeV
- Generalized-seniority composition of ground state 0+_1: P(s=0) ≈ 0.85, P(s=2) ≈ 0.13
- Generalized-seniority composition of first excited state 2+_1: P(s=1) ≈ 0.82, P(s=2) ≈ 0.08, P(s=3) ≈ 0.09
- Fluctuation Δs in eigenstates: approximately 0.7, decreasing slightly with excitation energy
- Ratio of s=2 to s=1 Fermi surface energies: approximately 1.875 (consistent with 2(1-1/Ω) for Ω=16)

## Context

This work extends the generalized-seniority approach beyond previous calculations that were limited to S=2 (Sandulescu 1997, Dean 2003) or S=4 (Bonsignori 1985, Allaart 1988) with less realistic interactions, achieving convergence for mid-shell nuclei where standard shell-model calculations are most time-consuming. The computed level densities are smaller than experimentally extracted ones for 116,118,122Sn near the neutron separation energy (Agvaanluvsan 2009, Toft 2010, Toft 2011), which the authors attribute to missing cross-shell excitations. The results enable accurate canonical-ensemble thermal property calculations for semi-magic nuclei and suggest the generalized-seniority truncation as an accurate tool for realistic calculations, while cautioning against the use of pure generalized-seniority states for describing seniority isomers as done in Maheshwari (2016) and Morales (2011).
