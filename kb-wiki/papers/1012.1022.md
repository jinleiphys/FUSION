# Non-Abelian dynamics and heavy multiquarks, Steiner-tree confinement in hadron spectroscopy

> A brief review is first presented of attempts to predict stable multiquark states within current models of hadron spectroscopy. Then a model combining flip-flop and connected Steiner trees is introduced and shown to lead to stable multiquarks, in particular for some configurations involving several heavy quarks and bearing exotic quantum numbers.

## Key claim
The paper argues that a confining potential combining flip-flop (pairwise quark-antiquark strings) with connected Steiner trees (generalizing the Y-shape baryon potential) can lead to stable multiquark states, particularly for configurations with several heavy quarks and exotic quantum numbers, even though earlier pairwise colour-additive models typically fail to produce such binding.

## Method
The author reviews the history of multiquark predictions (baryonium, pentaquarks, dibaryons) and the failures of chromomagnetic and colour-additive chromoelectric models. He then introduces a model where the confining potential for tetraquarks is the minimum of a flip-flop term (double meson-like strings) and a connected Steiner tree linking all four quarks, generalizing the Y-shape potential of baryons. The Steiner tree length is estimated using Napoleon's theorem and Melznak points, and the Hamiltonian is bounded to demonstrate stability analytically for limiting mass ratios. Numerical calculations for tetraquarks, pentaquarks, and hexaquarks are performed using hyperspherical or correlated Gaussian expansions.

## Key numbers
- The chromomagnetic model predicted the H dibaryon (uuddss) about 150 MeV below the ΛΛ threshold under SU(3)_f symmetry and equal short-range correlations.
- The same chromomagnetic mechanism predicted a pentaquark (Q̄qqqq) with binding of -150 MeV if Q becomes infinitely heavy.
- For the Coulombic analogue, a system (M⁺,m⁺,M⁻,m⁻) becomes unstable against dissociation into (M⁺,M⁻)+(m⁺,m⁻) if m/M ≲ 2.2.
- The tetraquark (QQq̄q̄) is bound within the Steiner-tree model for any value of the mass ratio M/m, including M=m, while (QqQ̄q̄) loses binding quickly.
- The pentaquark (q̄qqqq) is bound either for equal masses or in the limit where the antiquark or one quark becomes infinitely heavy.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of multiquark stability using pairwise colour-additive potentials (Ader 1981, Heller 1986) and chromomagnetic models (Jaffe 1976, Lipkin 1987, Gignoux 1987), which generally failed to produce stable equal-mass tetraquarks. The Steiner-tree model is supported by lattice QCD (Okiharu 2004) and AdS/QCD (Andreev 2008), and contrasts with earlier pairwise models that penalize multiquark binding due to the non-Abelian colour algebra. The results enable future experimental searches for (QQq̄q̄) tetraquarks combined with (QQq) baryons at heavy-quark facilities.
