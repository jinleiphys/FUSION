# Constraining gluon density of pions at large $x$ by pion-induced $J/ψ$ production

> The gluon distributions of the pion obtained from various global fits exhibit large variations among them. Within the framework of the color evaporation model, we show that the existing pion-induced $J/ψ$ production data, usually not included in the global fits, can impose useful additional constraints on the pion parton distribution functions (PDFs). In particular, these data can probe the pion's gluon densities at large $x$. Existing pion-induced $J/ψ$ data covering a broad range of beam momenta are compared with next-to-leading-order QCD calculations using various sets of pion PDFs. It is found that $J/ψ$ data measured at forward rapidity and at sufficiently high beam momentum are sensitive to the large-$x$ gluon distribution of pions. The current $J/ψ$ data favor the Sutton-Martin-Roberts-Stirling and Gluck-Reya-Vogt pion PDFs, containing significant gluon content at large $x$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that existing pion-induced $J/\psi$ production data, when analyzed within the color evaporation model at next-to-leading order, can impose useful additional constraints on pion gluon densities at large $x$, but the sensitivity depends on the data being measured at forward rapidity and at sufficiently high beam momentum.

## Method

The authors performed next-to-leading-order (NLO) QCD calculations within the color evaporation model (CEM) for pion-induced $J/\psi$ production, comparing the computed $x_F$ differential cross sections to eight datasets from Fermilab and CERN experiments covering beam momenta from 39.5 to 515 GeV/$c$. They used four different pion parton distribution function (PDF) sets (SMRS, GRV, xFitter, and JAM) with CT14nlo nucleon PDFs and EPPS16 nuclear PDFs for nuclear targets, fitting a hadronization factor $F$ for each dataset individually.

## Key numbers

* The gluon first moment at $Q^2 = 9.6$ GeV$^2$ varies from 0.291 (xFitter) to 0.513 (GRV) among the pion PDF sets examined.
* The valence quark momentum fraction at $Q^2 = 9.6$ GeV$^2$ ranges from 0.199 (GRV) to 0.245 (SMRS), with JAM at $0.225 \pm 0.003$ and xFitter at $0.228 \pm 0.009$.
* The hadronization factor $F$ for the total cross section fit varies from 0.05 to 0.09 across the four PDF sets.
* For the 515 GeV/$c$ E672/E706 data, the NLO calculation with SMRS and GRV gives better $\chi^2$/ndf values than xFitter and JAM; the $GG$ contribution dominates the cross section up to $x_F$ values of 0.5-0.7 depending on the PDF set.
* For the 300 GeV/$c$ E705 data, the best $\chi^2$/ndf is obtained with SMRS PDFs, while JAM PDFs give a significantly degraded $\chi^2$/ndf.
* For the 280 GeV/$c$ NA3 hydrogen data, $\chi^2$/ndf values for xFitter and JAM are 2-4 times larger than for SMRS and GRV.
* The $GG$ contribution starts to dominate the total cross section at $\sqrt{s}=13$ GeV for SMRS, $\sqrt{s}=11$ GeV for GRV, $\sqrt{s}=19$ GeV for xFitter, and $\sqrt{s}=21$ GeV for JAM.

## Context

This work extends the global analyses of pion PDFs by Owens (1984), Aurenche-Baier-Fontannaz-Kienzle-Focacci-Werlen (1989), Sutton-Martin-Roberts-Stirling (1991), and Gluck-Reya-Vogt (1991), which were based mostly on Drell-Yan and prompt-photon data, by showing that existing $J/\psi$ production data can constrain the poorly known gluon distribution at large $x$. The results favor the SMRS and GRV pion PDFs, which contain significant gluon content at large $x$, over the more recent xFitter and JAM PDFs that have much smaller gluon densities. This enables future global fits to include $J/\psi$ production data as a constraint on pion gluon distributions, and motivates new measurements such as those planned by the COMPASS and AMBER collaborations.
