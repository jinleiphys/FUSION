# Disoriented Chiral Condensate and Charge-Neutral Particle Fluctuations in Heavy Ion Collisions

> The posibility of large charge and isospin fluctuations in high-energy heavy ion-collisions is studied within the framework of the nonlinear $σ$-model with quark degrees of freedom. The multipion exchange potential between two quarks is derived. It is shown that the soft chiral pion bremsstralung leads to anomalously large fluctuations in the ratio of neutral to charged pions, predicted by the formation of a disoriented chiral condensate (DCC). The factorization property of the scattering amplitude in the impact parameter space of the leading two-nucleon system is used to study semiclassical production of pions in the central region. We show that the DCC-type fluctuations are suppressed if a large number of pions are produced in $ρ$-type clusters. Our conclusion is supported through the calculation of two pion correlation parameters as a function of the $ρ$-to-$π$ ratio.

# Disoriented Chiral Condensate and Charge-Neutral Particle Fluctuations in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

Within the nonlinear sigma model coupled to quarks, large isospin fluctuations of the DCC type are strongly suppressed when a significant number of pions are produced in rho-type clusters; the observation of DCC-type behavior depends critically on the rho-to-pion ratio being small in an event, and the characteristic 1/sqrt(f) distribution of neutral pion fraction is not a unique signature of DCC formation since soft chiral pion bremsstrahlung also produces anomalously large neutral-to-charged pion ratios.

## Method

The authors develop a unitary eikonal model with factorization in impact parameter space, incorporating global isospin conservation and a coherent-state description of pion production from classical sources. They derive a generating function for pion multiplicity distributions that includes both directly produced pions and rho-type clusters decaying into two short-range correlated pions, and calculate the probability distribution P(n0) and two-pion correlation parameters as functions of the average numbers of pions and rho clusters. Separately, they analyze the quantum nonlinear sigma model coupled to quarks, deriving the multipion exchange potential between two quarks and the chiral super propagator, and examine soft chiral pion bremsstrahlung as an alternative mechanism for large fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- The average number of rho mesons to pions at accelerator energies is estimated as n_rho = 0.10 n_pi (from reference [6] cited in the paper)
- Calculations are shown for total pion number n = 50 with various combinations of (n_pi, n_rho)
- The probability distribution for DCC domains is P_DCC(f) = 1/(2*sqrt(f)) for the neutral pion fraction f = n0/n
- The generic binomial distribution for normal events gives P_B(n0,n) = C(n,n0)*(1/3)^n0*(2/3)^(n-n0), approaching a delta function at f = 1/3 as n approaches infinity

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier eikonal model approach (Martinis and Mikuta-Martinis, 2002) and addresses the long-standing puzzle of Centauro events observed in cosmic rays, which show anomalously large charged-to-neutral pion ratios. The paper connects to the extensive DCC literature including the Anselm-class solutions (Anselm, 1988) and the standard DCC probability distribution derived by several groups (Bjorken, 1992; Kowalski and Taylor, 1992; Blaizot and Krzywicki, 1992; Gavin and Moschelli, 1992). The key finding that rho-cluster production suppresses DCC signals provides a practical constraint for experimental searches at RHIC and LHC, suggesting that event-by-event fluctuations of the rho-to-pion ratio must be considered when searching for DCC signatures. The paper also shows that the characteristic 1/sqrt(f) distribution can arise from soft chiral bremsstrahlung without DCC formation, complicating experimental identification.
