# Spin-Orbit-Like Terms in Semileptonic Weak Hamiltonian

> It is shown that new spin-orbit-like terms appear in the effective nonrelativistic weak Hamiltonian for nucleon provided that nuclear potential is taken into account. Arguments for their considerable enhancement, in particular, in relativistic nuclear model of Walecka are advanced.

# Spin-Orbit-Like Terms in Semileptonic Weak Hamiltonian

## Key claim
New spin-orbit-like terms appear in the effective nonrelativistic weak Hamiltonian for nucleons when the nuclear potential is taken into account, and these terms may be enhanced by a factor of approximately 20 in the Walecka relativistic nuclear model, potentially making them comparable in magnitude to the first-order terms usually retained.

## Method
The author performed a Foldy–Wouthuysen transformation on the one-nucleon relativistic Hamiltonian that includes both the weak interaction Hamiltonian and a central nucleon-nucleus potential, rather than treating the nucleon as free. The transformation was carried out to second order in 1/M, and the crossing of odd operators from the weak Hamiltonian with the nuclear potential in the commutator term [O,[O,E]] was evaluated to extract the new spin-orbit-like contributions. The analysis was then extended to the Walecka relativistic nuclear model, where the nucleon interacts with scalar and vector meson mean fields.

## Key numbers
- The energy release in beta decay is about 10^{-3}M, while in muon capture it is about 10^{-1}M, where M is the nucleon mass.
- For the ^{40}Ca nucleus in the Walecka model, the vector potential at the origin is V(0) ≈ 0.37M and the scalar potential at the origin is Φ(0) ≈ 0.45M.
- The nuclear spin-orbit coupling is enhanced by a factor of approximately 20 compared to the term entering the nonrelativistic Hamiltonian, and the same enhancement factor is expected for the new spin-orbit-like terms in the weak Hamiltonian.

## Context
This work extends the standard nonrelativistic reduction of the weak Hamiltonian, which was previously performed for free nucleons by Fujii (1959) for first-order terms and by Friar (1966) and Ohtsubo (1966) for second-order corrections. The new result shows that the nuclear potential, when included in the Foldy–Wouthuysen transformation, generates additional spin-orbit-like terms that have been neglected in prior treatments. The finding enables improved description of muon capture data, such as the anomalous low value of the induced pseudoscalar form factor g_P observed in ^{28}Si by Brudanin et al. (1995), and provides a test for relativistic nuclear models.
