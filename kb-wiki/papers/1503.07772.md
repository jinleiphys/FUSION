# $J/ψ$ production and suppression in high energy proton-nucleus collisions

> We apply a Color Glass Condensate+Non-Relativistic QCD (CGC+NRQCD) framework to compute $J/ψ$ production in deuteron-nucleus collisions at RHIC and proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC. Our results match smoothly at high $p_\perp$ to a next-to-leading order perturbative QCD + NRQCD computation. Excellent agreement is obtained for $p_\perp$ spectra at RHIC and LHC for central and forward rapidities, as well as for the normalized ratio $R_{pA}$ of these results to spectra in proton-proton collisions. In particular, we observe that the $R_{pA}$ data is strongly bounded by our computations of the same for each of the individual NRQCD channels; this result provides strong evidence that our description is robust against uncertainties in initial conditions and hadronization mechanisms.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Color Glass Condensate + Non-Relativistic QCD (CGC+NRQCD) framework provides a robust description of J/psi production in proton-nucleus collisions, with the key hedge being that the normalized ratio R_pA is strongly bounded by computations for individual NRQCD channels, making the result insensitive to uncertainties in initial conditions and hadronization mechanisms, provided the two free non-perturbative parameters are related by a self-consistency condition (Eq. RA) that ensures R_pA approaches 1 at high transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors computed J/psi production cross sections in deuteron-nucleus collisions at RHIC and proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC using a CGC+NRQCD framework, where short-distance coefficients for color-singlet and color-octet channels were calculated within the CGC effective field theory. The dipole forward scattering amplitudes were obtained by solving the running coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) equation with McLerran-Venugopalan initial conditions, and the results were matched smoothly at high p_perp to next-to-leading order perturbative QCD + NRQCD computations. The effective nuclear radius bar_R_A was fixed by requiring that R_pA approaches 1 at high p_perp, yielding bar_R_A = sqrt(A/2) * bar_R_p.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass set to m = 1.5 GeV, approximately one half the J/psi mass.
- Color-octet long distance matrix elements (LDMEs) extracted from Tevatron data: <O^{J/psi}(^3S_1^{[1]})> = 1.16/(2N_c) GeV^3, <O^{J/psi}(^1S_0^{[8]})> = 0.089 +/- 0.0098 GeV^3, <O^{J/psi}(^3S_1^{[8]})> = 0.0030 +/- 0.0012 GeV^3, <O^{J/psi}(^3P_J^{[8]})> = 0.0056 +/- 0.0021 GeV^3.
- Effective proton gluon radius bar_R_p = 0.48 fm.
- Initial saturation scale in nucleus Q_{s0,A}^2 = N * Q_{s0,p}^2 with N = 2 chosen for results presented.
- Effective nuclear radius bar_R_A = sqrt(A/2) * bar_R_p, giving 4.9 fm for Pb and 4.8 fm for Au.
- Color-singlet channel contribution is about 15-20% at small p_perp and decreases as p_perp becomes larger.
- A global 30% uncertainty was added to account for correlated errors of LDMEs, feed down, velocity corrections, and radiative corrections.
- A 5% systematic error was assumed for each channel in R_pA to account for the approximation in Eq. RA.

## Context

This work extends the CGC+NRQCD framework previously applied to proton-proton collisions by Ma, Venugopalan, and Zhang (2014) to proton-nucleus and deuteron-nucleus collisions, providing the first comprehensive description of J/psi production in both collision systems at collider energies. It contrasts with earlier CGC+Color Evaporation Model (CEM) computations by Fujii et al. (2013) that found R_pA suppression relative to data, and with the recent argument by Ducloue et al. (2015) that better agreement could be obtained with different treatment of nuclear effects. The results enable future global analyses within the CGC/NLO+NRQCD framework to constrain LDMEs more stringently and extend the approach to other quarkonium states such as Upsilon and psi(2S).
