# Novel method for producing very-neutron-rich hypernuclei via charge-exchange reactions with heavy ion projectiles

> We propose a novel method for producing very-neutron-rich hypernuclei and corresponding resonance states by employing charge-exchange reactions via pp($^{12}$C, $^{12}$N $K^+$)n$Λ$ with single-charge-exchange and ppp($^{9}$Be, $^{9}$C $K^+$)nn$Λ$ with double-charge-exchange, both of which produce $ΛK^+$ in a target nucleus. The feasibility of producing very-neutron-rich hypernuclei using the proposed method was analysed by applying an ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamics model to a $^6$Li+$^{12}$C reaction at 2 $A$ GeV. The yields of very-neutron-rich hypernuclei, signal-to-background ratios, and background contributions were investigated. The proposed method is a powerful tool for studying very-neutron-rich hypernuclei and resonance states with a hyperon for experiments employing the Super-FRS facility at FAIR and HFRS facility at HIAF.

# Novel method for producing very-neutron-rich hypernuclei via charge-exchange reactions with heavy ion projectiles

## Key claim
The authors propose that very-neutron-rich hypernuclei and hyper-resonance states can be produced via single- and double-charge-exchange reactions (pp(¹²C, ¹²N K⁺)nΛ and ppp(⁹Be, ⁹C K⁺)nnΛ) at beam energies of 2 A GeV or higher, but acknowledge that the cross sections are very small (0.05 nb and 0.03 nb for the test cases) and that the feasibility depends on achieving sufficient signal-to-background ratios with high-resolution spectrometers.

## Method
The authors employed the ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model to simulate ¹⁶Li+¹²C reactions at 2 A GeV, generating approximately 15 billion events. They performed kinematic clustering to form target and projectile fragments and hypernuclei in the target- and projectile-rapidity regions, then identified events corresponding to single- and double-charge-exchange reactions with ΛK⁺ production. The production cross sections for ¹²C(⁶Li, ⁶Be K⁺)¹²_ΛBe and ¹²C(⁶Li, ⁶B K⁺)¹²_ΛLi were estimated, and background contributions from π-meson production and target-residue breakup were evaluated using phase-space calculations with assumed momentum resolutions.

## Key numbers
- Production cross section for ¹²C(⁶Li, ⁶Be K⁺)¹²_ΛBe: 0.05 ± 0.035 nb
- Production cross section for ¹²C(⁶Li, ⁶B K⁺)¹²_ΛLi: 0.03 ± 0.03 nb
- Estimated production rate for ¹²_ΛBe: 1.7 × 10² per day and 1.2 × 10³ per week, with 2.4 × 10² observable per week (assuming 10⁸ particles/s beam intensity and 8 g/cm² target thickness)
- Estimated production rate for ¹²_ΛLi: 1.0 × 10² per day and 7.0 × 10² per week, with 1.5 × 10² observable per week
- Signal-to-background ratio for ¹²C(⁶Li, ⁶Be K⁺) reaction: 2/425 ≈ 4.7 × 10⁻³
- Signal-to-background ratio for ¹²C(⁶Li, ⁶B K⁺) reaction: 1/9 ≈ 1.1 × 10⁻¹
- Missing mass peak width for ¹²_ΛC: approximately 4.5 MeV in σ (with Δp/p = 10⁻⁴ for projectile residue and 10⁻² for K⁺)
- π-meson multiplicity ratio (multiplicity=0 to multiplicity=1) at A=6 for ¹²_ΛC production: 69/1
- π-meson multiplicity ratio (multiplicity=0 to multiplicity=1) at A=6 for ¹²_ΛB production: 46/1
- Cross section for ³_ΛH production in projectile-rapidity region: 3.6 μb (compared to measured 3.9 ± 1.4 μb from HypHI Phase 0)
- Cross section for Λ-hyperon production integrated over all rapidity: 1.98 mb (compared to measured 1.7 ± 0.8 mb from HypHI Phase 0)

## Context
This work extends the heavy-ion hypernuclear production approach demonstrated by the HypHI Phase 0 experiment at GSI (Rappold, 2013), which produced light hypernuclei via ⁶Li+¹²C reactions at 2 A GeV. The proposed method addresses the difficulty of producing very-neutron-rich hypernuclei through conventional fragmentation reactions, where the probability is very small. The charge-exchange reaction approach is inspired by the production of a tetra-neutron resonance state using double-charge-exchange reactions (⁴He(⁸He, ⁸Be)4n at 186 A MeV). The method enables study of neutral hypernuclei such as Λnn (³_Λn), whose possible bound state was reported by HypHI Phase 0 (Rappold, 2013) but contradicted by theoretical calculations (Gal, 2014; Schafer, 2020), and other neutron-rich species like ⁶_ΛH (observed by FINUDA collaboration but not confirmed by J-PARC E10) and ¹⁰_ΛLi (observed by KEK-PS-E521 with small statistics). The proposed reactions can be implemented at the Super-FRS facility at FAIR and HFRS facility at HIAF using detector systems similar to the WASA-FRS hypernuclear experiment.
