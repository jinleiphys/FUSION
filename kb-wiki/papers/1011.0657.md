# The study of the Three Nucleon Force in full QCD Lattice calculations

> We study the three nucleon force in the triton channel using dynamical clover fermion lattice QCD. The Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave function is utilized to obtain the potentials among three nucleons. Since the straightforward calculation is prohibitively expensive, two different frameworks are developed to meet the challenge. In the first method, we study the effective two nucleon potentials in the three nucleon system, where the differences between the effective two nucleon potentials and the genuine two nucleon potentials correspond to the three nucleon system effect, part of which is originated from the three nucleon force. The calculation is performed using Nf=2 clover fermion at m(π)= 1.13GeV generated by CP-PACS Collaboration, and Nf=2+1 clover fermion at m(π)= 0.70, 0.57GeV generated by PACS-CS Collaboration. In the second method, we study the three nucleon system with 3D-configuration of nucleons fixed. This enables us to extract the three nucleon force directly, if both of parity-even and parity-odd two nucleon potentials are provided. Since parity-odd two nucleon potentials are not available in lattice QCD at this moment, we propose a new general procedure to identify the three nucleon force using only parity-even two nucleon potentials. The calculation are performed with Nf=2 clover fermion at m(π)= 1.13GeV generated by CP-PACS Collaboration, employing the linear setup for the 3D-configuration. Preliminary results for the scalar/isoscalar three nucleon force are presented.

## Key claim
The paper presents first-principles lattice QCD calculations of the three nucleon force (TNF) in the triton channel, but the results are preliminary and obtained at unphysically heavy pion masses (mπ = 1.13, 0.70, 0.57 GeV), with the clearest indication of repulsive TNF appearing only at the heaviest mass and requiring further confirmation.

## Method
The authors use Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions from lattice QCD to extract nuclear potentials. Two complementary methods are developed: (1) effective two-nucleon (2N) potentials in the three-nucleon (3N) system, where differences from genuine 2N potentials reveal 3N effects; (2) fixed 3D-configuration calculations with a linear setup (three nucleons equally spaced), enabling direct TNF extraction using only parity-even 2N potentials via a newly proposed procedure that exploits the antisymmetric spin/isospin channel ψ_S.

## Key numbers
- mπ = 1.13 GeV, mN = 2.15 GeV for Nf=2 clover fermion configurations (CP-PACS Collaboration) with lattice spacing a⁻¹ = 1.269 GeV and volume (2.5 fm)³
- mπ = 0.70 GeV (mN = 1.58 GeV) and mπ = 0.57 GeV (mN = 1.41 GeV) for Nf=2+1 clover fermion configurations (PACS-CS Collaboration) with a⁻¹ = 2.18 GeV and volume 32³ × 64
- Statistical error of Veff(r) - V2N(r) in the tensor potential remains less than 5 MeV even at the lightest quark mass (mπ = 0.57 GeV)
- The linear setup calculation uses 598 configurations with 16 source time slice measurements at 7 physical points of the distance r

## Context
This work extends the lattice QCD methodology for nuclear forces pioneered by Ishii et al. (2007) from two-nucleon to three-nucleon systems, building on prior studies of hyperon-nucleon interactions by Nemura and Inoue. The results are compared with phenomenological TNF models such as the Urbana IX model (UIX) and the Fujita-Miyazawa two-pion exchange picture, with the goal of providing first-principles input for ab initio nuclear structure calculations and neutron star equations of state. The paper identifies the need for lighter quark masses and further systematic studies to confirm the observed repulsive short-range TNF.
