# Quark Droplets in the NJL Mean Field

> We study the conditions for the existence of stable quark matter in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio mean field at zero temperature and discuss its interpretation.

# Quark Droplets in the NJL Mean Field

## Key claim

Stable quark droplets (dense phase coexisting with vacuum) are possible in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio mean field at zero temperature, but only if the critical chemical potential for chiral symmetry restoration is less than the vacuum constituent quark mass; this condition is not a necessary or very probable consequence of restricting model parameters to realistic values, contrary to some earlier claims.

## Method

The authors study the thermodynamic potential of the NJL model with two and three quark flavors in the Hartree mean-field approximation at zero temperature, including scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector four-fermion interactions. They locate stationary points of the thermodynamic potential with respect to the constituent quark mass and the renormalized chemical potential, then identify the absolute minimum to determine stable phases. A three-dimensional sharp cutoff regularizes divergent integrals, and the pion decay constant is fixed to its empirical value to constrain parameters.

## Key numbers

- Critical chemical potential for chiral restoration: μ_crit = 376 MeV for parameters m_vac = 400 MeV, Λ = 587.9 MeV, G_S Λ² = 2.48, G_V = 0 (chiral limit).
- Baryon number density of the dense phase at coexistence: ρ_B = 0.47 fm⁻³ = 2.75 ρ_0 (where ρ_0 is nuclear saturation density).
- With vector coupling G_V = 0.5 G_S, the phase transition becomes case II with μ_crit = 410.3 MeV > m_vac, yielding no stable droplets.
- For m_vac < 343 MeV, no bound quark matter exists even without vector interaction.
- In the three-flavor model with fitted parameters (m_0^u = m_0^d = 5.5 MeV, m_0^s = 140.7 MeV, Λ = 602.3 MeV, G_S Λ² = 1.84, K Λ⁵ = 12.4), stable quark matter appears at ρ = 2.36 ρ_0 with binding energy per quark of 6.5 MeV.
- Without the determinant interaction (G_S Λ² = 2.31, K = 0), binding energy is 2.9 MeV at ρ = 2.43 ρ_0.

## Context

This work extends the earlier classification of NJL phase structures by Buballa (1996) and addresses the interpretation of quark droplets as schematic bag-model baryons, analogous to the MIT bag model. The results contradict Alford (1998), who found stable droplets to be most likely; the authors show that Alford's parameters likely correspond to a pion decay constant below 60 MeV, smaller than the empirical value of 92.4 MeV. The study enables systematic mapping of parameter regions where the NJL model can mimic confinement through droplet formation, but cautions that the droplets are unstable against evaporation at any finite temperature due to the absence of true confinement.
