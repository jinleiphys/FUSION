# The Gluon Condensate and Running Coupling of QCD

> An expression for the photon condensate in quantum electrodynamics is presented and generalized to deduce a simple relation between the gluon condensate and the running coupling constant of quantum chromodynamics (QCD). Ambiguities in defining the condensates are discussed. The values of the gluon condensate from some Ansätze for the running coupling in the literature are compared with the value determined from QCD sum rules.

## Key claim
The gluon condensate can be related to the running coupling constant of QCD through a simple integral relation, provided that power-divergent terms (artifacts of regularization) are consistently subtracted; the resulting relation provides a constraint on Ansätze for the running coupling in the low-momentum region, but the condensate is not necessarily linked to confinement.

## Method
The authors derive a relation between the gluon condensate and the running coupling by generalizing a QED expression for the photon condensate, using dimensional regularization and cutoff regularization with subtraction of power terms. They evaluate the integral for several phenomenological Ansätze for the running coupling (a modified one-loop form, a Gaussian form, and a form with a quadratic factor) and compare the resulting condensate values to the standard QCD sum-rule value.

## Key numbers
- For the Ansatz α₀(k²) with N_c = N_f = 3 and τ = 3, the gluon condensate is (0.568 Λ_QCD)⁴; for τ = 10, it is (1.44 Λ_QCD)⁴.
- For the Ansatz α_s(k²) = α₀(k²) + α₁(k²) with a = (387 MeV)⁻⁴, Δ = (510 MeV)², τ = 10, and Λ_QCD = 228 MeV, the gluon condensate is (788 MeV)⁴.
- For the Ansatz α_s(k²) = α₀(k²) + α₂(k²) with χ = 1.14 GeV, Δ = 0.002 GeV², τ = 3, and Λ_QCD = 190 MeV, the gluon condensate is (191 MeV)⁴.
- The phenomenological gluon condensate from QCD sum rules is approximately (350 MeV)⁴.

## Context
This work extends earlier discussions on the operator product expansion and the separation of power terms from genuine nonperturbative effects, particularly the debate between Novikov et al. (1985) and David (1986) on the two-dimensional O(N) σ model. It provides a constraint on running-coupling Ansätze used in Dyson-Schwinger equation approaches (e.g., Jain and collaborators, 1993; Praschifka and collaborators, 1989) and in quark potential models, enabling a consistency check against the gluon condensate value from QCD sum rules.
