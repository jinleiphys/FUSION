# Chiral Symmetry Breaking and Chiral Polarization: Tests for Finite Temperature and Many Flavors

> It was recently conjectured that, in SU(3) gauge theories with fundamental quarks, valence spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking is equivalent to condensation of local dynamical chirality and appearance of chiral polarization scale $Λ_{ch}$. Here we consider more general association involving the low-energy layer of chirally polarized modes which, in addition to its width ($Λ_{ch}$), is also characterized by volume density of participating modes ($Ω$) and the volume density of total chirality ($Ω_{ch}$). Few possible forms of the correspondence are discussed, paying particular attention to singular cases where $Ω$ emerges as the most versatile characteristic. The notion of finite-volume "order parameter", capturing the nature of these connections, is proposed. We study the effects of temperature (in N$_f$=0 QCD) and light quarks (in N$_f$=12), both in the regime of possible symmetry restoration, and find agreement with these ideas. In N$_f$=0 QCD, results from several volumes indicate that, at the lattice cutoff studied, the deconfinement temperature $T_c$ is strictly smaller than the overlap-valence chiral transition temperature $T_{ch}$ in real Polyakov line vacuum. Somewhat similar intermediate phase (in quark mass) is also seen in N$_f$=12. It is suggested that deconfinement in N$_f$=0 is related to indefinite convexity of absolute X-distributions.

## Key claim

The paper tests the conjecture that valence spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking (vSChSB) is equivalent to the existence of a chirally polarized layer of low-energy Dirac modes, characterized by a positive volume density of participating modes (\(\Omega > 0\)) in the infinite volume limit, and proposes that this equivalence holds for SU(3) gauge theories with arbitrary numbers of fundamental quark flavors at any temperature.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using the overlap Dirac operator as the valence probe, studying two systems: N\(_f\)=0 (quenched) QCD at finite temperature across several volumes, and N\(_f\)=12 QCD with light dynamical quarks. They computed spectral measures of dynamical chirality including the chiral polarization density \(\rho_{ch}(\lambda)\), the chiral polarization scale \(\Lambda_{ch}\), the volume density of participating modes \(\Omega\), and the volume density of total chirality \(\Omega_{ch}\), and compared these to the valence chiral condensate and the deconfinement temperature.

## Key numbers

* In N\(_f\)=0 QCD at the lattice cutoff studied, the deconfinement temperature \(T_c\) is strictly smaller than the overlap-valence chiral transition temperature \(T_{ch}\) in real Polyakov line vacuum, indicating an intermediate phase \(T_c < T < T_{ch}\).
* In N\(_f\)=0, the intermediate phase exhibits singular chiral polarization behavior with \(\Lambda_{ch}=0\), \(\Omega_{ch}>0\), and \(\Omega>0\).
* In N\(_f\)=12, an intermediate phase (in quark mass) with properties similar to the N\(_f\)=0 mixed phase is observed.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier conjecture (Alexandru and Horvath 2012) that valence spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking is equivalent to dynamical chirality condensation, by providing a more general framework involving the low-energy layer of chirally polarized modes characterized by \(\Lambda_{ch}\), \(\Omega\), and \(\Omega_{ch}\). The paper tests this framework against two routes to symmetry restoration: thermal effects in N\(_f\)=0 QCD and light dynamical quarks in N\(_f\)=12, finding agreement with the proposed equivalence. The results enable further study of the relationship between chiral symmetry breaking and deconfinement, and suggest that deconfinement in N\(_f\)=0 is related to indefinite convexity of absolute X-distributions.
