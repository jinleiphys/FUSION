# Deep learning for jet modification in the presence of the QGP background

> Jet interactions with the color-deconfined QCD medium in relativistic heavy-ion collisions are conventionally assessed by measuring the modification of the distributions of jet observables with respect to their baselines in proton-proton collisions. Deep learning methods enable per-jet evaluation of these modifications, enhancing the use of jets as precision probes of the nuclear medium. In this work, we predict the jet-by-jet fractional energy loss $χ$ for jets evolving through a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) medium using a Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model. To approximate realistic experimental conditions, we embed medium-modified jets in a thermal background and apply Constituent Subtraction for background removal. Two network architectures are studied: convolutional neural networks (CNNs) using jet images, and dynamic graph convolutional neural networks (DGCNNs) using particle clouds. We find that CNNs achieve accurate predictions for background-free jets but degrade in the presence of the QGP background and remain below the background-free baseline even after background subtraction. In contrast, DGCNNs applied to background-subtracted particle clouds maintain high accuracy across the entire $χ$ range, demonstrating the advantage of point-cloud-based graph neural networks that exploit full jet structure under realistic conditions.

## Key claim

Deep learning models can predict jet-by-jet fractional energy loss for jets evolving through a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) medium, but convolutional neural networks (CNNs) degrade significantly in the presence of a thermal QGP background and remain below the background-free baseline even after Constituent Subtraction background removal; dynamic graph convolutional neural networks (DGCNNs) applied to background-subtracted particle clouds maintain high accuracy across the entire energy loss ratio range.

## Method

The authors used PYTHIA 8 with the Monash 2013 tune to generate 140k proton-proton collision events at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV, and the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model to simulate jet interactions with a QGP medium provided by the (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model CLVisc for 0-10% centrality Pb+Pb collisions. A thermal toy model generated soft pion background particles with a total multiplicity of 1538 and average transverse momentum of 0.5696 GeV within |y|<1 to emulate experimental conditions, and Constituent Subtraction was applied for background removal. Two network architectures were trained to predict the fractional energy loss chi = p_T^f / p_T^i: a CNN processing 33x33 jet images of transverse momentum deposited per pixel, and a DGCNN (ParticleNet architecture) operating on particle-cloud representations with three EdgeConv modules and k=16 nearest neighbors.

## Key numbers

- 140k PYTHIA proton-proton collision events generated at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV, with hard collision triggers uniformly sampled within [300, 1000] GeV
- Final dataset of about 170k medium-modified jets after matching LBT jets to PYTHIA counterparts with Delta R < 0.4
- Thermal background model generated pions with total multiplicity 1538 and average transverse momentum 0.5696 GeV within |y|<1, corresponding to 0-10% Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV
- Jets reconstructed using anti-k_T algorithm with radius parameter R=0.4, selecting jets with p_T > 100 GeV and pseudorapidity within |eta| < 1-R
- CNN training time about 8 hours and DGCNN training time about 33 hours on a single NVIDIA T4 GPU; per-jet inference times about 0.05 ms for CNN and 0.5 ms for DGCNN
- CNN with background-subtracted jets shows prediction error improvement for chi less than about 0.90 but degradation for chi greater than 0.95 compared to jets within background

## Context

This work extends previous per-jet energy loss predictions using machine learning (Du 2020, du 2021) that were based on a hybrid strong/weak coupling model without QGP background effects, by incorporating the LBT model (Cao 2016, Luo 2023) and explicit thermal background particles to emulate realistic experimental conditions. The study demonstrates that point-cloud-based graph neural networks (DGCNN/ParticleNet, Wang 2019, Komiske 2019) outperform image-based CNNs for jet energy loss prediction in the presence of background, enabling future application of these methods to real experimental data from heavy-ion collisions to mitigate selection bias in jet quenching measurements.
