# The antikaon potential in nuclear matter at finite momentum

> We study the antikaon potential at finite momentum for proton and neutron nuclear matter. Our approach is based on the momentum dependence of the real part of the total $K^-N$ scattering amplitude, which is fixed in free space by experimental data, using the dispersion relation approach. We decompose the contributions to the scattering amplitude from different processes and consider the $Λ$(1405) and $Σ$(1385) to be dissolved in nuclear matter at density $ρ_0$. Within this approach the $K^-$ potential is found to be attractive up to high kaon momenta. Our result is in line with the data on the antikaon potential evaluated from the analysis of kaonic atoms (low momenta), heavy-ion (interdmediate momenta) and proton-nucleus (high momenta) collisions.

# The antikaon potential in nuclear matter at finite momentum

## Key claim
The antikaon potential in nuclear matter remains attractive up to high kaon momenta, provided that the Lambda(1405) and Sigma(1385) resonances are considered dissolved at normal nuclear density rho_0. The result is consistent with data from kaonic atoms, heavy-ion collisions, and proton-nucleus collisions, but the analysis depends on discarding the unphysical-region contribution from the resonances when extrapolating to finite momentum.

## Method
The authors use the low-density theorem to relate the real part of the antikaon potential to the real forward K- -nucleon scattering amplitude D, which is evaluated via a dispersion relation using experimental total cross sections for K- N and K+ N scattering. They decompose the scattering amplitude into contributions from a pole term, an unphysical domain (containing the Lambda(1405) and Sigma(1385) resonances), and a physical region, then discard the unphysical contribution to model the in-medium dissolution of these resonances at density rho_0. The potential is calculated at baryon density 0.16 fm^{-3} and averaged over the Fermi distribution with Fermi momentum approximately 260 MeV/c.

## Key numbers
- The antikaon potential from kaonic atom analysis is approximately -180 MeV at normal nuclear density rho_0.
- Heavy-ion collision studies suggest an attractive potential of approximately -100 to -120 MeV.
- The boundary value D(0) for the K- p forward scattering amplitude is -2.73 fm.
- The Lambda(1405) and Sigma(1385) resonance energies are E_Lambda = 64 MeV and E_Sigma = 155 MeV, with the unphysical domain threshold at E_{Lambda pi} = 239 MeV.
- The FHS Collaboration data for K- production from p+Be collisions at p_K = 1.28 GeV/c indicate an attractive K- potential of -74 to -198 MeV.
- The calculation is performed at baryon density rho_B = 0.16 fm^{-3} with Fermi momentum p_F approximately 260 GeV/c (likely a typo for MeV/c).

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier proposal (Sibirtsev, 1999) that the discrepancy between kaonic atom potentials (approximately -180 MeV at zero momentum) and heavy-ion collision potentials (approximately -100 to -120 MeV at intermediate momenta) can be explained by the momentum dependence of the antikaon potential. It contrasts with models relying exclusively on the Lambda(1405) properties in the medium, such as those by Brown (1990s) and dynamical calculations of the in-medium modification of the Lambda(1405). The results enable experimental tests at the COSY/ANKE facility, which can probe the crucial momentum range below 1.5 GeV/c to check whether the scattering amplitude changes sign.
