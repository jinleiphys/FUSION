# Generalized Pions in Dense QCD

> QCD superconductors in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase sustain light Goldstone modes (that will be referred to as generalized pions) that can be described as pairs of particle and/or hole excitations around a gapped Fermi surface. In weak coupling and to leading logarithm accuracy, their form factor, mass and decay constant can be evaluated exactly. These modes are found to satisfy an axial-Ward-identity, constraining the mass of the Goldstone modes in the CFL phase.

# Generalized Pions in Dense QCD

## Key claim

In the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase of dense QCD, the Goldstone modes (generalized pions) have a temporal decay constant \(F_T^2 \approx \mu^2/\pi^2\) and a spatial decay constant \(F_S^2 = F_T^2/3\), but only in weak coupling and to leading logarithm accuracy; the mass of these modes vanishes to order \(m_f^2\) in the CFL phase, contrary to what a naive Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation might suggest.

## Method

The authors solve the BCS gap equation for the CFL phase using a Nambu-Gorkov formalism with a screened gluon propagator (Debye and magnetic screening), obtaining the gap function \(G(p)\) to leading logarithm accuracy. They then solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the pion vertex at zero total momentum, extract the temporal and spatial decay constants from the axial-vector current matrix element, and derive an axial Ward identity that relates the pion mass to quark masses via the gap and decay constant.

## Key numbers

- Temporal pion decay constant: \(F_T^2 \approx \mu^2/\pi^2\) (Eq. t12), implying \(F_T^2/G_0^2 \gg 1\).
- Ratio of spatial to temporal decay constants: \(F_S^2/F_T^2 = 1/3\) (Eq. Fequx).
- Gap magnitude: \(G_0 \approx (4\Lambda_\perp^6 / \pi m_E^5) \exp(-\sqrt{3}\pi / 2h_*)\) with \(h_*^2 = (4/3)(g^2/8\pi^2)\) (Eqs. Gzero, HCFL).
- Transverse cutoff: \(\Lambda_\perp = 2\mu\).
- Debye mass: \(m_E^2 = (3/2)(g\mu/\pi)^2\) (used in final mass expression).
- Pion mass scales as \(m_f^2\) but the color-flavor trace in Eq. GOR13 vanishes, so the generalized pions remain massless to order \(m_f^2\) in the CFL phase.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic description of Goldstone modes in the CFL phase beyond the effective Lagrangian approaches of Rho et al. (1999) and Gatto et al. (1999), which could not determine the underlying parameters. The results confirm the axial Ward identity structure suggested by Pisarski (1999) and provide explicit weak-coupling expressions for the decay constants that are needed for quantitative predictions of thermodynamic and transport properties of QCD superconductors, including the mass of the superqualiton discussed by Rho et al. (1999). The small pion size (\(r_\pi \approx 1/F_T\)) implies that superqualitons are heavy, with \(M_s/G_0 \gg 1\).
