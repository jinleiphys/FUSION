# Neutron electric dipole moment using $N_f{=}2{+}1{+}1$ twisted mass fermions

> We evaluate the neutron electric dipole moment $\vert \vec{d}_N\vert$ using lattice QCD techniques. The gauge configurations analyzed are produced by the European Twisted Mass Collaboration using $N_f{=}2{+}1{+}1$ twisted mass fermions at one value of the lattice spacing of $a \simeq 0.082 \ {\rm fm}$ and a light quark mass corresponding to $m_π \simeq 373 \ {\rm MeV}$. Our approach to extract the neutron electric dipole moment is based on the calculation of the $CP$-odd electromagnetic form factor $F_3(Q^2)$ for small values of the vacuum angle $θ$ in the limit of zero Euclidean momentum transfer $Q^2$. The limit $Q^2 \to 0$ is realized either by adopting a parameterization of the momentum dependence of $F_3(Q^2)$ and performing a fit, or by employing new position space methods, which involve the elimination of the kinematical momentum factor in front of $F_3(Q^2)$. The computation in the presence of a $CP$-violating term requires the evaluation of the topological charge ${\cal Q}$. This is computed by applying the cooling technique and the gradient flow with three different actions, namely the Wilson, the Symanzik tree-level improved and the Iwasaki action. We demonstrate that cooling and gradient flow give equivalent results for the neutron electric dipole moment. Our analysis yields a value of $\vert \vec{d}_N\vert=0.045(6)(1)\ \barθ \ e \cdot {\rm fm}$ for the ensemble with $m_π=373$ MeV considered.

## Key claim

The neutron electric dipole moment is computed using lattice QCD at a single lattice spacing and a single pion mass, yielding a value proportional to the CP-violating parameter $\bar{\theta}$, with the result valid only to leading order in $\bar{\theta}$ and under the assumption that $\bar{\theta}$ is small enough to treat the Chern-Simons term perturbatively.

## Method

The authors use gauge configurations generated by the European Twisted Mass Collaboration with $N_f=2+1+1$ twisted mass fermions at a lattice spacing of $a \simeq 0.082$ fm and pion mass $m_\pi \simeq 373$ MeV. They compute the CP-odd electromagnetic form factor $F_3(Q^2)$ by expanding the path integral to leading order in the vacuum angle $\theta$, evaluating the topological charge $\mathcal{Q}$ using both cooling and gradient flow with Wilson, Symanzik tree-level improved, and Iwasaki actions. The zero-momentum-transfer limit $F_3(0)$ is extracted either by fitting the $Q^2$-dependence of $F_3(Q^2)$ with a dipole parameterization or by using new position-space methods that eliminate the kinematical momentum factor.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: $a \simeq 0.082$ fm
- Pion mass: $m_\pi \simeq 373$ MeV
- Neutron mass: $m_N = 1.220(5)$ GeV
- Neutron electric dipole moment: $\vert \vec{d}_N\vert = 0.045(6)(1) \ \bar{\theta} \ e \cdot {\rm fm}$ for the ensemble with $m_\pi=373$ MeV
- Number of gauge configurations analyzed: 4623
- Spatial lattice extent: $L = 2.6$ fm ($32^3 \times 64$ lattice)
- Product $L m_\pi = 4.97$

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of the neutron electric dipole moment, such as those by Shintani et al. (2014) using $N_f=2+1$ domain wall fermions and Shintani et al. (2008) using $N_f=2$ clover fermions with a background electric field method, as well as chiral perturbation theory calculations by Ottnad et al. (2009). The results are compared with these prior determinations and found to be compatible within uncertainties. The new position-space methods developed here enable extraction of $F_3(0)$ without model assumptions on the $Q^2$-dependence, and the demonstration that cooling and gradient flow give equivalent results for the topological charge provides a methodological benchmark for future lattice studies of CP-violating observables.
