# Exploring the $D^*ρ$ system within QCD sum rules

> We present a study of the $D^*ρ$ system made by using the method of QCD sum rules to determine the mass of possible resonances generated in the same system. Using isospin and spin projectors, we investigate the different configurations and obtain evidences for three $D^*$ mesons with isospin $I=1/2$, spin S=0, 1, 2 and with masses $2500\pm 67$ MeV, $2523\pm60$ MeV, and $2439\pm119$ MeV, respectively. The last state can be associated with $D^*_2(2460)$ (spin 2) listed by the Particle Data Group, while one of the first two might be related to $D^*(2640)$, with unknown spin-parity. In the case of $I=3/2$ we also find evidences of three states with spin 0, 1 and 2, respectively, with masses $2467\pm82$ MeV, $2420\pm128$ MeV, and $2550\pm56$ MeV. The results for the sector $I=1/2$ and S=0, 1, 2, are intriguingly similar to a previous study of the $D^*ρ$ system based on effective field theories, supporting in this way a molecular picture for the resonances $D^*(2640)$ and $D^*_2(2460)$, while the results for $I=3/2$ hint towards the existence of exotic mesons since a multiquark configuration is required to get the quantum numbers of the states found.

# Exploring the $D^*\rho$ system within QCD sum rules

## Key claim
The QCD sum rule analysis provides evidence for three $D^*$ molecular states with isospin $I=1/2$ and spins $S=0,1,2$, with masses that agree with a prior effective field theory study of the same system (Raquel et al., 2013), supporting a molecular interpretation for the $D^*_2(2460)$ and $D^*(2640)$ resonances. For $I=3/2$, three additional states are found whose quantum numbers require a multiquark configuration, hinting at exotic mesons, though the prior effective field theory study found repulsive interactions in this channel and disfavored resonance formation.

## Method
The authors constructed interpolating molecular currents for the $D^*\rho$ system with definite isospin ($I=1/2$ and $3/2$) and applied covariant spin projectors (a novelty in QCD sum rules) to project onto spin $S=0,1,2$ states. They computed the two-point correlation function using the operator product expansion up to dimension-7 condensates, then matched the OPE description to a phenomenological parametrization (single narrow resonance plus continuum) via Borel transformation. Masses were extracted from the resulting sum rule by averaging over a Borel window determined by pole dominance and OPE convergence criteria, with uncertainties estimated by varying the continuum threshold (3.00-3.15 GeV) and the input quark masses and condensates.

## Key numbers
- $I=1/2$, $S=0$: mass $2500 \pm 67$ MeV, coupling $(3.63 \pm 0.39) \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^5$
- $I=1/2$, $S=1$: mass $2523 \pm 60$ MeV, coupling $(6.51 \pm 0.61) \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^5$
- $I=1/2$, $S=2$: mass $2439 \pm 119$ MeV, coupling $(8.14 \pm 1.61) \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^5$
- $I=3/2$, $S=0$: mass $2467 \pm 82$ MeV, coupling $(3.48 \pm 0.48) \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^5$
- $I=3/2$, $S=1$: mass $2420 \pm 128$ MeV, coupling $(5.22 \pm 1.12) \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^5$
- $I=3/2$, $S=2$: mass $2550 \pm 56$ MeV, coupling $(6.60 \pm 0.62) \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^5$
- Input parameters: charm quark mass $m_c = 1.23 \pm 0.05$ GeV; light quark condensate $\langle \bar{q} q \rangle = -(0.23 \pm 0.03)^3$ GeV$^3$; gluon condensate $\langle g^2 G^2 \rangle = (0.88 \pm 0.25)$ GeV$^4$

## Context
This work extends the study of the $D^*\rho$ system from effective field theories (Raquel et al., 2013) to the QCD sum rule framework, finding remarkably consistent masses for the $I=1/2$ sector. The $S=2$ state is identified with the PDG-listed $D^*_2(2460)$, while one of the $S=0$ or $S=1$ states near 2500-2523 MeV may correspond to $D^*(2640)$ (whose spin-parity is unknown). The $I=3/2$ results contradict the repulsive interaction found in the effective field theory approach (Raquel et al., 2013) and suggest the existence of exotic mesons requiring multiquark configurations. The authors note that a width calculation via QCD sum rules would be needed to distinguish which of the two nearly degenerate $I=1/2$ states (spin 0 or 1) corresponds to the narrow $D^*(2640)$ ($\Gamma < 15$ MeV). The spin projection technique used here builds on earlier work for the $X(3872)$ state (Hidalgo et al., 2012).
