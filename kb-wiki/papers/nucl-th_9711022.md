# Non perturbative chiral approach to s-wave \bar{K}N interactions

> The s-wave meson-nucleon interaction in the $S = -1$ sector is studied by means of coupled-channel Lippmann Schwinger equations, using the lowest order chiral Lagrangian and a cut off to regularize the loop integrals. The method reproduces succesfully the $Λ(1405)$ resonance and the $K^- p \to K^- p, \bar{K}^0 n, π^0 Λ, π^0 Σ, π^+ Σ^-, π^- Σ^+$ cross sections at low energies. The inclusion of the $ηΛ, ηΣ^0$ channels in the coupled system is found very important and allows a solution in terms of only the lowest order Lagrangian.

# Non perturbative chiral approach to s-wave \bar{K}N interactions

## Key claim
The s-wave \bar{K}N interaction and the \Lambda(1405) resonance can be described using only the lowest order chiral Lagrangian with a single cutoff parameter, provided that the \eta\Lambda and \eta\Sigma^0 channels are included in the coupled-channel system. This contrasts with prior work by Kaiser, Siegel and Weise (1995) which required five parameters from higher order Lagrangians to reproduce the data, because omitting the \eta channels led to threshold ratios that were poorly reproduced.

## Method
Coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equations were solved for eight coupled channels (K^-p, \bar{K}^0n, \pi^0\Lambda, \pi^0\Sigma^0, \pi^+\Sigma^-, \pi^-\Sigma^+, \eta\Lambda, \eta\Sigma^0) using transition potentials derived from the lowest order chiral Lagrangian. The loop integrals were regularized with a momentum cutoff q_max, and the on-shell factorization reduced the integral equations to algebraic equations. The two free parameters were the cutoff q_max and the pion decay constant f, which was allowed to vary moderately from its pionic value.

## Key numbers
- Optimal parameters: f = 1.15 f_\pi (with f_\pi = 93 MeV), q_max = 630 MeV
- Threshold ratio \gamma = \Gamma(K^-p \rightarrow \pi^+\Sigma^-)/\Gamma(K^-p \rightarrow \pi^-\Sigma^+) = 2.36 \pm 0.04 (experimental); reproduced within 5% in the full calculation
- Threshold ratio R_c = 0.664 \pm 0.011 (experimental); reproduced within 5%
- Threshold ratio R_n = 0.189 \pm 0.015 (experimental); reproduced within 5%
- Ratio \gamma is reduced by a factor of 2.2 when \eta channels are omitted
- K^-p scattering length: (-0.78 \pm 0.15 \pm 0.03) + i(0.49 \pm 0.25 \pm 0.12) fm from kaonic hydrogen X-rays (Miw97); results are in agreement
- K^-n scattering length: (0.37 + i 0.60) fm from Adler (1981) with 15% estimated error; results are in qualitative agreement
- S = 1, T = 0 scattering length: 2.4 \times 10^{-7} fm (compatible with experimental 0.02 \pm 0.04 fm from Dover (1982))
- S = 1, T = 1 scattering length: -0.26 fm (compared to experimental -0.32 \pm 0.02 fm from Dover (1982))

## Context
This work extends the unitary coupled-channel method of Oller and Oset (1997), which successfully described meson-meson interactions using only the lowest order Lagrangian and a cutoff, to the meson-baryon sector with strangeness S = -1. It contradicts the conclusion of Kaiser, Siegel and Weise (1995) that higher order Lagrangians are necessary, by showing that inclusion of the \eta\Lambda and \eta\Sigma^0 channels (which were omitted in that earlier work) allows a solution with only the lowest order Lagrangian. The approach enables future studies of kaon propagation in nuclear matter, a topic of active interest following work by Brown, Lee, Rho and Thorsson (1994) and others.
