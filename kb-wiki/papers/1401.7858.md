# Dense baryonic matter in the hidden local symmetry approach

> The dense baryonic matter and hadron properties in medium were simulated by using a Skyrme model including the ground state vector mesons introduced from the hidden local symmetry approach up to the next to leading order. We found that both the $ρ$ and $ω$ mesons affect the baryonic matter and medium modified hadron properties dramatically. The most remarkable observation is that, the pion decay constant and the nucleon mass have the similar density dependence which agrees with the large $N_c$ argument. Explicitly, they drop with increasing density in the Skyrmion phase and stop decreasing at $n_{1/2}^{}$ at which the skyrmions in medium fractionize into half-skyrmions and remains nearly constants in the half-skyrmion phase. This density dependence in the half-skyrmion phase indicates that, although $\langle \bar{q}q\rangle \neq 0$ on average in this phase, chiral symmetry is not restored since hadrons are still massive and there exist pions. In addition, the nearly constant nucleon mass means that it could has a non-vanishing component up to the chiral transition, which might shed light on the origin of the nucleon mass.

## Key claim

The pion decay constant and the nucleon mass exhibit a similar density dependence, dropping in the Skyrmion phase and then remaining nearly constant in the half-skyrmion phase, which suggests that the nucleon mass may have a non-vanishing component up to the chiral transition. This behavior occurs even though the chiral condensate \(\langle \bar{q}q\rangle\) vanishes on average in the half-skyrmion phase, indicating that chiral symmetry is not restored there.

## Method

The baryonic matter was simulated by placing skyrmions from a hidden local symmetry (HLS) Lagrangian (including \(\pi\), \(\rho\), and \(\omega\) mesons up to next-to-leading order) onto a face-centered cubic (FCC) crystal lattice. The density was varied by changing the crystal size \(L\), and the low-energy constants were determined self-consistently from holographic QCD master formulas using only the empirical pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV and the rho meson mass \(m_\rho = 775.5\) MeV. Medium-modified hadron properties were then obtained by incorporating fluctuations of the meson fields around their classical crystal solutions.

## Key numbers

- The half-skyrmion transition density \(n_{1/2}\) in the full HLS(\(\pi,\rho,\omega\)) model is approximately equal to the normal nuclear matter density \(n_0\).
- In the HLS(\(\pi,\rho\)) model (without \(\omega\)), the half-skyrmion transition density is \(n_{1/2} \cong 6 n_0\).
- The input empirical values used are the pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV and the vector meson mass \(m_\rho = 775.5\) MeV.
- The medium-modified pion decay constant \(f_\pi^\ast\) is given by \(\frac{f_\pi^\ast}{f_\pi} = \sqrt{1 - \frac{2}{3} \left( 1- \left\langle \sigma^2_{(0)} \right\rangle \right) }\), which becomes a non-zero constant in the half-skyrmion phase.

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model approach to dense baryonic matter by including vector mesons (\(\rho\) and \(\omega\)) via the hidden local symmetry framework, building on earlier Skyrme model studies (e.g., PV09, LPMRV03). It contrasts with minimal HLS models (HLS\(_{\text{min}}\)) by showing that the \(\rho\) meson raises the half-skyrmion transition density while the \(\omega\) meson lowers it, and that the full model yields \(n_{1/2} \approx n_0\), closer to nature. The finding that the nucleon mass remains constant in the half-skyrmion phase supports the parity doublet picture of baryons (DK89) and suggests that a component of the nucleon mass survives up to chiral restoration, which could inform future studies of the origin of nucleon mass and the role of missing effects like the dilaton and three-body interactions.
