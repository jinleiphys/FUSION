# Properties of H particle-admixed compact star

> We explore the potential manifestation of a hexaquark, the H particle, as a constituent within neutron stars. The H particle, characterized by a quark composition of $uuddss$, is constructed using the framework of Chromomagnetic Interaction (CMI). Specifically, we contemplate the flavor-singlet state H with $J^P=0^+$. Our computations indicate that the three-flavor hexaquark state, the H particle, possesses a lower mass of $2212.7~\rm{MeV}$ in comparison to the $d^*(2380)$, implying greater stability than the two-flavor $d^*(2380)$. The analysis involving the H particle is carried out using the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model. We investigate the influence of H particle couplings, a key factor in determining the system stability, and focus on the potential existence of H particle within neutron stars. We find that H particle could potentially endure as a stable constituent within neutron stars, and lead to a reduction of the maximum mass.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the H particle (a hexaquark with quark composition \(uuddss\)) could exist as a stable constituent inside neutron stars, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific choice of the H particle coupling strengths to the scalar and vector meson fields, which are treated as free parameters due to the lack of experimental constraints on the H particle potential.

## Method

The mass of the H particle was calculated using the Chromomagnetic Interaction (CMI) model, constructing the total wave function from the direct product of space, flavor, color, and spin wave functions with full antisymmetrization for identical quarks. The equation of state for neutron star matter containing nucleons, leptons, and H particles was then computed using the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model, with two treatments of the isovector coupling: a constant coupling (RMF) and a density-dependent coupling (RMFL). The H particle couplings \(g_{\sigma H}\) and \(g_{\omega H}\) were treated as free parameters to account for the unknown H particle potential.

## Key numbers

- The H particle mass is calculated to be \(2212.7~\rm{MeV}\) for the \(J^P=0^+\) flavor-singlet state, which is lower than the \(d^*(2380)\) mass.
- The observed massive neutron star PSR J0740+6620 has a lower mass limit of \(2.14_{-0.09}^{+0.10}~M_\odot\) (68.3% credibility interval).
- The heaviest known neutron star PSR J0952-0607 has a mass of \(2.35\pm{0.17}~M_\odot\) (68.3% credibility interval).
- NICER measurements for PSR J0030+0451 give \(M=1.34_{-0.16}^{+0.15}~M_{\odot}\), \(R=12.71_{-1.19}^{+1.14}~\mathrm{km}\) (68% credible interval) from Riley (2019) and \(M=1.44_{-0.14}^{+0.15}~M_{\odot}\), \(R=13.02_{-1.06}^{+1.24}~\mathrm{km}\) from Miller (2019).
- NICER measurements for PSR J0740+6620 give \(M=2.072_{-0.066}^{+0.067}M_\odot\), \(R=12.39_{-0.98}^{+1.30}~\mathrm{km}\) (68.3% credibility interval) from Riley (2021) and \(M=2.08\pm0.07M_\odot\), \(R=13.7_{-1.5}^{+2.6}~\mathrm{km}\) from Miller (2021).
- The tidal deformability constraint from GW170817 gives \(\Lambda_{1.4}<800\) for a canonical \(1.4~M_\odot\) neutron star, and the inferred radius constraint gives \(R_{1.4}<13.6~\rm{km}\) from Annala (2018).
- The coupling parameters for the \(nnnnss\) states extracted from traditional hadron masses are: \(m_{nn}=181.2~\rm{MeV}\), \(m_{ns}=226.7~\rm{MeV}\), \(m_{ss}=262.3~\rm{MeV}\), \(v_{nn}=19.1~\rm{MeV}\), \(v_{ns}=13.3~\rm{MeV}\), \(v_{ss}=12.2~\rm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies on dibaryonic matter in neutron stars, particularly the hexaquark \(d^*(2380)\) studied by Vidana (2018), Bashkanov (2019), and Kim (2020), which has experimental support from Adlarson (2014). The H particle, originally proposed by Jaffe (1977) using a perturbative bag model, is expected to be more stable than \(d^*(2380)\) due to its three-flavor symmetry and lower mass. The results enable future comparisons with observational constraints from massive neutron stars like PSR J0740+6620 and PSR J0952-0607, as well as tidal deformability constraints from GW170817 and GW190814.
