# Formation of Kaonic Atoms and Kaonic Nuclei by In-flight ($K^-,p$) reactions

> We study the kaonic atom and kaonic nucleus formation by the in-flight ($K^-, p$) reactions for C, O, Si and Ca target cases theoretically. Deeply bound kaonic atoms were predicted to exist as quasi-stable states and were expected to be observed in some proper experimental methods. Kaonic nuclear states are also expected to exist with large decay widths. We evaluate the formation cross sections of the kaonic atoms and kaonic nuclei using an effective number approach. We show that the indications of the kaonic bound states can be observed in the outgoing proton energy spectra.

## Key claim

The authors claim that indications of kaonic bound states can be observed in the outgoing proton energy spectra of in-flight ($K^-,p$) reactions, but only for kaonic atomic states; for kaonic nuclear states, the predicted widths are so large that no peak-like structure appears in the calculated spectra, only a smooth slope, regardless of which optical potential model is used.

## Method

The authors solved the Klein-Gordon equation with a kaon-nucleus optical potential to obtain binding energies and widths of kaonic bound states for targets of C, O, Si and Ca, using two different potentials: one from a chiral unitary approach and one from a phenomenological fit by Batty (1997). They then calculated the double differential cross section for the ($K^-,p$) reaction using an effective number approach with eikonal distorted waves, summing over kaon-particle proton-hole configurations and including a phase-space factor for decay width suppression from Mares, Friedman and Gal (2004).

## Key numbers

- For the phenomenological optical potential, the kaonic nuclear 1s state in $^{39}$K has binding energy 206.7 MeV and width 253.8 MeV; for the chiral unitary potential, the same state has binding energy 29.3 MeV and width 100.3 MeV.
- For kaonic atomic states in $^{39}$K, the 1s state binding energy is 1408.0 keV with width 355.4 keV (phenomenological) and 1422.7 keV with width 337.3 keV (chiral unitary).
- The peak height of the atomic 1s state formation cross section in $^{40}$Ca($K^-,p$) coupled to the [$d_{3/2}^{-1}$] proton-hole state is approximately 7 $\mu$b/(sr MeV) at incident kaon energies $T_K$ = 20 MeV and 100 MeV.
- At $T_K$ = 400 MeV, the absolute cross section strength is approximately 1/3 to 1/4 of that at 100 MeV.
- The momentum transfer for atomic state formation can satisfy zero recoil at $T_K$ = 10-20 MeV, while for nuclear states the momentum transfer is always nonzero.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of kaonic atom formation via the ($K^-,\gamma$) reaction (Hirenzaki 2000) to the in-flight ($K^-,p$) reaction, which was proposed by Friedman (1999) and Kishimoto (1999) but for which realistic spectra had not been calculated. The theoretical framework follows the approach used for deeply bound pionic atom formation via ($d,^3$He) reactions (Hirenzaki 1991). The results enable experimental planning for kaonic atom spectroscopy, but show that kaonic nuclear states will likely not appear as distinct peaks in ($K^-,p$) spectra due to their large widths, in contrast to predictions of narrow nuclear states by Akaishi (2002).
