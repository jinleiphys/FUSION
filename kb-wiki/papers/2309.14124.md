# Emergence of hydrodynamics in expanding relativistic plasmas

> I consider a simple set of equations that govern the expansion of boost-invariant plasmas of massless particles. These equations describe the transition from a collisionless regime at early time to hydrodynamics at late time. Their mathematical structure encompasses all versions of second order hydrodynamics. We emphasize that the apparent success of Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics at early time has little to do with ``hydrodynamics'' proper, but rather with a particular feature of Israel-Stewart equations that allows them to effectively mimic the collisionless regime.

## Key claim

The apparent success of Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics at early times in boost-invariant plasmas does not reflect genuine hydrodynamic behavior, but rather arises because the Israel-Stewart equations share a mathematical structure with the kinetic moment equations, allowing them to approximately mimic the collisionless regime.

## Method

The author analyzes a kinetic equation with a relaxation-time collision term for massless particles undergoing Bjorken (boost-invariant) longitudinal expansion. By expanding the distribution function in Legendre moments, an infinite hierarchy of coupled ordinary differential equations for the moments is obtained; truncating this hierarchy yields effective equations for the energy density and the viscous pressure that encompass all versions of second-order hydrodynamics. The fixed-point structure of the resulting nonlinear differential equation for the exponent \(g(w) = \tau \partial_\tau \ln \varepsilon\) is studied as a function of \(w = \tau/\tau_R\), connecting the free-streaming regime at early times to the hydrodynamic regime at late times.

## Key numbers

- The stable free-streaming fixed point is \(g_+ = -1\) and the unstable free-streaming fixed point is \(g_- = -2\) (exact values when all higher moments are included).
- The hydrodynamic fixed point is \(g_* = -4/3\), corresponding to ideal hydrodynamics with \(\varepsilon \sim \tau^{-4/3}\).
- In the two-moment truncation without higher moments, the stable free-streaming fixed point is \(g_+ = -0.929\) and the unstable one is \(g_- = -2.213\).
- The renormalized parameter \(a_1' = 31/15\) reproduces the exact stable free-streaming fixed point \(g_+ = -1\).
- The coefficients in the moment equations include \(a_0 = 4/3\) and \(a_1 = 38/21\).

## Context

This work extends the kinetic-theory analysis of Bjorken flow initiated by Baym (1984) and the moment method developed by Blaizot (2017). It clarifies the nature of the "hydrodynamic attractor" concept introduced by Heller and Spalinski (2015), arguing that the attractor connects a collisionless fixed point to a hydrodynamic fixed point, and that the gradient expansion is divergent and requires trans-series information from early-time dynamics. The analysis shows that Israel-Stewart theory (Israel and Stewart, 1979) works at early times only because its equations structurally resemble the kinetic moment equations, not because hydrodynamics applies there. This enables a precise understanding of when genuine hydrodynamic behavior emerges: when the collision rate becomes comparable to the expansion rate (\(\tau \gtrsim \tau_R\)).
