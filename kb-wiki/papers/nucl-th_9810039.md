# Weak Form Factors of the Nucleon

> We limit ourselves basically to the SU(2) flavor sector of the CKM matrix, as probed in processes like nuclear $β$-decay, normal and radiative muon capture and neutrino reactions. Current interests in this primarily stem from our aspirations to interpret the weak form factors of the nucleon in the framework of QCD. Given a standard set of known form factors, we can look also in these processes for possible physics signals beyond the standard model.

# Weak Form Factors of the Nucleon

## Key claim
The weak form factors of the nucleon, particularly the axial-vector and pseudoscalar couplings, can be interpreted within QCD and tested via nuclear beta-decay, muon capture, and neutrino reactions; however, the radiative muon capture experiment at TRIUMF yields a pseudoscalar form factor value 1.46 times the expectation from chiral perturbation theory, posing a challenge to the standard application of PCAC/chiral perturbation theory in this reaction.

## Method
The author reviews the Lorentz structure of the nucleon charged weak current, defining the vector form factors (g_V, g_M, g_S) and axial-vector form factors (g_A, g_P, g_T) as functions of momentum transfer q². Experimental extractions of these form factors are compared across processes: neutron beta-decay for g_A/g_V at q²=0, ordinary muon capture in hydrogen for g_P, radiative muon capture in hydrogen for g_P via high-energy photon spectra, and muon capture on ³He for the statistical capture rate. The Goldberger-Treiman relation and its discrepancy function Δ_π are used to connect g_A, g_πNN, and F_π, with lattice QCD results from Liu et al. providing non-perturbative input.

## Key numbers
- (g_A/g_V)^e at q²=0 from neutron beta-decay (1998 PDG): 1.2601 ± 0.0025
- (g_A/g_V)^μ at q²=0 extracted from ordinary muon capture in hydrogen: 1.24 ± 0.04
- Axial dipole mass M_A from neutrino scattering: 0.89^{+0.09}_{-0.09} GeV
- Pseudoscalar form factor g_P(q²_0) from PCAC/chiral perturbation theory: 8.44 ± 0.23, where q²_0 = -0.88 m_μ²
- Pseudoscalar form factor g_P(q²_0) from ordinary muon capture in hydrogen: 8.7 ± 2.9
- Pseudoscalar form factor from TRIUMF radiative muon capture: g_P(-0.88 m_μ²) = (9.8 ± 0.7 ± 0.3) g_A(0), which is 1.46 times the chiral perturbation theory expectation
- Statistical muon capture rate on ³He: Λ_stat^exp = 1496 ± 3(stat) ± 3(sys) s⁻¹
- Theoretical statistical muon capture rate on ³He (Congleton and Truhlik): Λ_stat^th = 1497 ± 21 s⁻¹
- Phase φ_AV of g_A from neutron beta-decay T-invariance test (PDG-98): 180.07 ± 0.18 degrees
- g_A from QCD sum rule (Ioffe): 1.37 ± 0.10
- Second-class current limit from Minanisono et al. in A=12 system: 2M g_T/g_S = 0.22 ± 0.05 ± 0.15(syst) ± 0.05(th)
- QCD sum rule expectation for second-class current: 2M g_T/g_A = 0.0152 ± 0.0053

## Context
This invited talk at WEIN-98 reviews the status of weak nucleon form factors, extending the traditional approach of Mukhopadhyay (1977) and the reviews by Herczeg on physics beyond the standard model. The work connects to the Bjorken sum rule from deep inelastic scattering (EMC, SMC, SLAC experiments) and the Adler-Weissberger sum rule relating g_A to pion-nucleon cross-sections. The discrepancy between the TRIUMF radiative muon capture result and chiral perturbation theory calculations (Bernard et al., Ecker-Mojzis Lagrangian at O(p³)) motivates improved measurements of the ordinary muon capture rate in hydrogen (PSI proposal by Petitjean and Kammel aiming for 1% precision) and further lattice QCD calculations (Liu et al.) of the pion-nucleon and pseudoscalar form factors. The precise ³He muon capture result enables searches for physics beyond the standard model, as explored by Govaerts, Herczeg, and Mohapatra.
