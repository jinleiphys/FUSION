# Spinodal instabilities within BUU approach

> Using a recently developed method for the inclusion of fluctuation in the BUU dynamics, we study the self-consistent propagation of inherent thermal noise of unstable nuclear matter. The large time behaviour of the evolving system exhibits synergism between fluctuation and non-linearities in a universal manner which manifest in the appearance of macroscopic structure in the average description.

# Spinodal instabilities within BUU approach

## Key claim

The large-time behavior of unstable nuclear matter evolving under the Boltzmann-Langevin equation exhibits a universal synergism between fluctuation and non-linearity that leads to macroscopic structure in the average density description, but only when thermal fluctuations are included in the initial state and the system is prepared inside the spinodal zone at a temperature of 3 MeV.

## Method

The author implements a recently developed simulation scheme that treats the phase-space occupancy factor as discrete (0 or 1) in elementary cells, generating fluctuations through a stochastic transition probability for collisions. The Vlasov propagation is handled via a modified leap-frog algorithm on a discrete grid, with a smearing procedure to prevent double occupancy. The system is initialized as a uniform gas of fermions on a two-dimensional torus at half saturation density with a Fermi-Dirac momentum distribution at T=3 MeV, and the evolution of density fluctuations is analyzed via Fourier mode decomposition and compared to solutions of the Langevin and Fokker-Planck equations with non-linear generalizations.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.55 fm⁻²
- Skyrme interaction parameters: A = -100.3 MeV, B = 48 MeV
- Fermi momentum: P_f = 260 MeV/c
- In-medium cross section: 2.4 fm
- Temperature of initial state: T = 3 MeV
- Grid spacing: x_d = 1 fm in coordinate space, p_d = 40 MeV/c in momentum space
- Lattice size: 21×21 in coordinate space, 31×31 in momentum space
- Number of samples for parallel runs: N_s = 100
- Total energy per particle conserved up to 95% at time t = 100 fm/c
- Sample average density fluctuation from initial value: ~3% in linear regime, ~20% at t = 150 fm/c
- Saturation times t_k⁰ for unstable modes with k_x ranging from 0.3 to 0.6 (k_y=0) are shown in Fig. 1
- Linear regime persists for some modes even at t = 120 fm/c
- Spatial average of √⟨(δρ)²⟩/⟨ρ⟩ reaches value 1 at approximately t = 75 fm/c

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of spinodal decomposition in nuclear matter using the BUU equation by Burgio et al. (1992) and the Boltzmann-Langevin approach of Ayik and Gregoire (1988). The author applies Suzuki's model of dynamical phase transition to describe the non-linear regime, showing that the evolution follows a universal scaling behavior. The results are compared to a Vlasov-based study by Baldo et al. (1995) where fluctuations overshoot without saturation, and the present work demonstrates that including the collision term changes the nature of the solutions to a diffusive process. This enables future studies of fragment formation in nuclear multifragmentation using a consistent treatment of fluctuations beyond the mean-field approximation.
