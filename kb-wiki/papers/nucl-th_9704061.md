# Stability of bubble nuclei through Shell-Effects

> We investigate the shell structure of bubble nuclei in simple phenomenological shell models and study their binding energy as a function of the radii and of the number of neutron and protons using Strutinsky's method. Shell effects come about, on the one hand, by the high degeneracy of levels with large angular momentum and, on the other, by the big energy gaps between states with a different number of radial nodes. Shell energies down to -40 MeV are shown to occur for certain magic nuclei. Estimates demonstrate that the calculated shell effects for certain magic numbers of constituents are probably large enough to produce stability against fission, alpha-, and beta-decay. No bubble solutions are found for mass number A < 450.

## Key claim

Shell effects can produce energy gains large enough to stabilize bubble-shaped nuclei against fission, alpha-decay, and beta-decay, but only for mass numbers A ≥ 450; the authors qualify that the bubble solutions are unstable in the liquid-drop model alone and that stability depends on the shell correction energy being sufficiently large.

## Method

The authors used Strutinsky's shell correction method to calculate the total binding energy of spherical bubble nuclei as a sum of a liquid-drop energy and a shell correction energy. Single-particle energies were computed for two extreme potentials, an infinite square well and a harmonic oscillator, both with a spin-orbit term treated in perturbation theory, and the shell correction energy was evaluated as a function of the inner and outer radii (parameterized by the hole fraction f) and of neutron and proton numbers.

## Key numbers

- Shell energies down to -40 MeV occur for certain magic bubble nuclei.
- No bubble solutions are found for mass number A ≤ 450.
- For A=580, Z=280, the liquid-drop surface energy E_S = 1291 MeV, the fissility parameter X_0 = 2.62, the barrier height is ~20 MeV, and the bubble minimum energy is ~-140 MeV.
- The largest liquid-drop energy gains per particle occur for nucleon numbers 1200 ≤ A ≤ 2000 and proton numbers 325 ≤ Z ≤ 400.
- Shell correction energy gains up to -20 MeV are found for one sort of particles (neutrons or protons) at f=0.28.
- For A=700, Z=270, and R_1=11 fm, the Coulomb potential at the outer surface is 2Ze²₀/R₁ ≈ 70 MeV, and the estimated Q-value for alpha decay is Q_α ≈ 20 MeV.
- The fission barrier for a sample bubble nucleus is about 25 MeV (barrier top at -3.9 MeV relative to the spherical bubble liquid-drop energy, with a valley depth of about -30 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the earlier investigations of bubble nuclei by C.Y. Wong (several papers, 1970s), who used Strutinsky's method for known nuclei near beta-stability and found bubble solutions with very small inner radii for doubly magic nuclei like ²⁰⁰₈₀Hg₁₂₀ and ¹³⁸₅₈Ce₈₀. The present study explores a much broader range of neutron and proton numbers beyond known nuclei, showing that shell effects can stabilize bubbles against fission, alpha-decay, and beta-decay for sufficiently large mass numbers. The results suggest that stable or very long-lived bubble nuclei may exist, though their high masses and charges make production in earthly laboratories unlikely; the authors note that more careful work with full Hartree-Bogoliubov theory is needed, especially to determine lower mass and charge limits, and that such objects might play a role in neutron stars.
