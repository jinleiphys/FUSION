# 7-Li(p,n) Nuclear Data Library for Incident Proton Energies to 150 MeV

> We describe evaluation methods that make use of experimental data, and nuclear model calculations, to develop an ENDF-formatted data library for the reaction p + Li7 for incident protons with energies up to 150 MeV. The important 7-Li(p,n_0) and 7-Li(p,n_1) reactions are evaluated from the experimental data, with their angular distributions represented using Lengendre polynomial expansions. The decay of the remaining reaction flux is estimated from GNASH nuclear model calculations. The evaluated ENDF-data are described in detail, and illustrated in numerous figures. We also illustrate the use of these data in a representative application by a radiation transport simulation with the code MCNPX.

## Key claim

The authors present an evaluated nuclear data library for p+⁷Li reactions up to 150 MeV, but acknowledge that the GNASH statistical model calculations used for the non-direct reaction flux are unreliable for light nuclei like lithium, and that the evaluation is most accurate for the n₀ and n₁ neutron groups which are based directly on experimental measurements.

## Method

The evaluation uses experimental data for the direct ⁷Li(p,n₀) and ⁷Li(p,n₁) reactions, fitting measured angular distributions with Legendre polynomial expansions up to 150 MeV. For the remaining reaction flux (including lower-energy neutrons, charged particles, and gamma rays from preequilibrium and compound nucleus decay), the authors employ GNASH nuclear model calculations with a modified optical potential originally developed for ⁶Li by Chiba et al. (1998). The GNASH-calculated n₀ and n₁ contributions are then replaced with the experimentally-based evaluated cross sections in the final ENDF-6 formatted library.

## Key numbers

* Forward-angle laboratory cross section for ⁷Li(p,n) approaches 7.4 mb/sr at E_p = 15 MeV and 14.5 mb/sr at E_p = 20 MeV
* Above E_p = 7 MeV, n₁ neutrons constitute greater than 25% of the n₀ neutron yield, reaching a maximum of about 55% at E_p ∼ 9 MeV
* The ratio R = n₁/n₀ was fixed at 0.35 for energies above 26 MeV, based on measurements by Poppe et al. (1976) at E_p = 25.9 MeV
* The library covers incident proton energies up to 150 MeV
* Natural lithium consists of ⁷Li (92.58%) and ⁶Li (7.42%) abundances

## Context

This work extends the earlier proton evaluation library by Chadwick et al. (1999) for 42 isotopes to include ⁷Li, which was not covered in that previous effort. The library is designed for use with the MCNPX transport code to support the Low Energy Demonstration Accelerator (LEDA) at LANSCE for the Accelerator Production of Tritium (APT) Project. The evaluation enables radiation transport simulations of thin and thick lithium targets for quasimonoenergetic neutron production, with potential applications in fusion materials testing, accelerator transmutation of waste, and medical isotope production.
