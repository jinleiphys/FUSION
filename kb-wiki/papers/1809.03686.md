# Microscopic description of structural evolution in Pd, Xe, Ba, Nd, Sm, Gd and Dy isotopes

> Aiming to understand the role of triaxiality and the evolution of the ground state nuclear shapes, we have carried out a microscopic study for a series of chains of Pd, Xe, Ba, Nd, Sm, Gd, and Dy isotopes. This is done within the self-consistent Relativistic-Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) formalism, and supported by the Triaxial Projected Shell Model (TPSM) approach. Pairing interaction separable in the momentum space with DD-ME2 force parameter is used to generate the potential energy surfaces(PESs) under the axial and triaxial symmetry. Shape evolution manifest themself in very clear manner in almost all the isotopic chains. Properties of the global mimima have been found to be in good agreement with the available experimental data. Relatively flat PESs, and $γ$-soft nature, have been suggested $^{108,110}$Pd, $^{132,134}$Xe and $^{134}$Ba as good candidates for E(5) symmetry, while $^{102}$Pd is not found suitable for E(5) symmetry. The PESs with a bump, and rigidity against triaxial variable($γ$) suggested $^{150}$Nd, $^{152}$Sm and $^{154}$Gd to be good candidates while $^{150}$Sm and $^{156}$Dy are poor candidates of X(5) critical-point symmetry. The findings of the present RHB calculations supported by TPSM are qualitatively in good agreement with the experimental and other theoretical calculations.

## Key claim

The paper identifies candidate nuclei for E(5) and X(5) critical-point symmetries based on the flatness or bump-like shape of their potential energy surfaces (PESs) from Relativistic-Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations, but the authors explicitly note that some previously proposed candidates (e.g., $^{102}$Pd for E(5); $^{150}$Sm and $^{156}$Dy for X(5)) are "not found suitable" or are "poor candidates" based on their PES criteria.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Relativistic-Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations using the density-dependent DD-ME2 meson-exchange force with a pairing interaction separable in momentum space, generating axial and triaxial potential energy surfaces (PESs) for isotopic chains of Pd (Z=46), Xe (Z=54), Ba (Z=56), Nd (Z=60), Sm (Z=62), Gd (Z=64), and Dy (Z=66). They then supported the RHB results by applying the Triaxial Projected Shell Model (TPSM) approach to investigate high-spin behavior of nuclei near the critical points, using a pairing-plus-quadrupole-quadrupole Hamiltonian with monopole pairing strengths G1=20.12 and G2=13.13.

## Key numbers

- $^{108}$Pd and $^{110}$Pd show flat PESs with energy differences between prolate and oblate minima of about 0.5 MeV (minimum) for $^{108}$Pd and about 3 MeV (maximum) for $^{102}$Pd
- $^{132,134}$Xe and $^{138}$Xe have flat-bottom PESs with energy differences $\leq$0.75 MeV
- $^{134}$Ba has a flat PES covering $0.1 \leq |\beta_2| \leq 0.15$
- $^{150}$Nd has a flat prolate side from $\beta_2 = 0.2$ to 0.4, with an oblate minimum at 2.6 MeV excitation energy and a 6 MeV energy barrier
- $^{150}$Sm shows $\gamma$-softness of $35^\circ$ up to 1.86 MeV of energy; $^{152}$Sm shows $\gamma$-softness of $15^\circ$ up to 1.9 MeV
- $^{154}$Gd shows $\gamma$-softness of $15^\circ$ up to 1.55 MeV; $^{156}$Dy shows $\gamma$-softness of $30^\circ$ up to 1.9 MeV
- $^{106}$Pd has two minima at $(0.2, 15^\circ)$ and $(0.4, 5^\circ)$ with energy difference of 1.21 MeV
- $^{108}$Pd has two minima at $(0.25, 25^\circ)$ and $(0.35, 15^\circ)$ with energy difference of 1.10 MeV

## Context

This work extends the search for E(5) and X(5) critical-point symmetries, originally introduced by Iachello in the framework of the Bohr Hamiltonian, to multiple isotopic chains using a microscopic RHB approach. It builds on prior studies by Casten and Zamfir (2000) who proposed $^{134}$Ba as an E(5) candidate, and by Clark (2004) and others who identified N=90 isotones as X(5) candidates. The paper contradicts some earlier assignments: $^{102}$Pd, previously proposed as an E(5) candidate by Zamfir (2002) and Clark (2004), is found unsuitable, and $^{150}$Sm and $^{156}$Dy are deemed poor X(5) candidates despite earlier suggestions by Casten (2001) and others. The results enable future experimental verification of the proposed E(5) candidates ($^{108,110}$Pd, $^{132,134}$Xe, $^{134}$Ba) and X(5) candidates ($^{150}$Nd, $^{152}$Sm, $^{154}$Gd) through measurements of transition probabilities and energy spectra.
