# Spin and Pseudo-Spin Symmetries in Radial Dirac Equation and Exceptional Hermite Polynomials

> We have generalized the solutions of the radial Dirac equation with a tensor potential under spin and pseudospin symmetry limits to exceptional orthogonal Hermite polynomials family. We have obtained new general rational potential models which are the generalization of the nonlinear isotonic potential families and also energy dependent.

## Key claim
The authors claim that the radial Dirac equation with a tensor potential, under both spin and pseudospin symmetry limits, can be solved using exceptional Hermite polynomials, yielding new rational potential models that generalize the nonlinear isotonic potential families. The hedge is that these solutions and potentials are energy-dependent, meaning the potential functions themselves depend on the energy eigenvalue, which is a nontrivial extension of standard fixed-potential approaches.

## Method
The authors start from the radial Dirac equation with scalar, vector, and tensor potentials, imposing either spin symmetry (S(r)=V(r)) or pseudospin symmetry (V(r)=-S(r)). They apply a series of point transformations and a mapping to the differential equation satisfied by exceptional Hermite polynomials, specifically using the partition λ={1,1} and a linear coordinate transformation r(z)=αz. By comparing coefficients, they derive explicit forms for the scalar, vector, and tensor potentials, as well as the energy eigenvalues and wavefunctions in terms of exceptional Hermite polynomials.

## Key numbers
- Energy eigenvalues for both spin and pseudospin symmetric cases: E_n = ±√(M² + 2α²(n-m)), where α is a scaling parameter, n is the principal quantum number, and m is an integer.
- Vector potential for the spin symmetric case: V(r) = (-9 + 13r² + 4r⁶) / [2(E+M)(1+2r²)²].
- Vector potential for the pseudospin symmetric case: V(r) = (-9 + 13r² + 4r⁶) / [2(E-M)(1+2r²)].
- Tensor potential for the spin symmetric case: U(r) = k/r + V'(r)/(E-M-2V(r)).
- Tensor potential for the pseudospin symmetric case: U(r) = k/r + [r(49-26r²+12r⁴+8r⁶)] / [(1+2r²)(-9+13r²+4r⁶-(E²-M²)(1+2r²)²)].

## Context
This work extends the study of spin and pseudospin symmetries in the Dirac equation, which were first identified by Hecht and Arima (1970s) in nuclear shell-model orbitals, to include exceptional orthogonal polynomials. It builds on the recent development of exceptional Hermite polynomials by Kamran (2009) and their application in quantum mechanics by Sasaki and Quesne. The paper generalizes the nonlinear isotonic oscillator models (referenced as iso1-iso4) to the relativistic Dirac framework, enabling new energy-dependent potential models that could be applied to nuclear structure studies, particularly for understanding magic numbers in exotic nuclei and meson properties in quark-antiquark systems.
