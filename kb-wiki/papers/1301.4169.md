# Error estimates and specification parameters for functional renormalization

> We present a strategy for estimating the error of truncated functional flow equations. While the basic functional renormalization group equation is exact, approximated solutions by means of truncations do not only depend on the choice of the retained information, but also on the precise definition of the truncation. Therefore, results depend on specification parameters that can be used to quantify the error of a given truncation. We demonstrate this for the BCS-BEC crossover in ultracold atoms. Within a simple truncation the precise definition of the frequency dependence of the truncated propagator affects the results, indicating a shortcoming of the choice of a frequency independent cutoff function.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the dependence of truncated functional renormalization group results on "specification parameters" (free parameters in the projection prescriptions for running couplings) can be used to quantify the error of a given truncation, and demonstrates this for the BCS-BEC crossover in ultracold atoms, finding that results depend significantly on the specification parameter for frequency dependence but not for momentum dependence.

## Method

The authors introduce finite-difference projection prescriptions for the wave function renormalization \(Z_k\) and gradient coefficient \(A_k\) of the inverse boson propagator, controlled by specification parameters \(c_Z\) and \(c_A\) that set the momentum/frequency scale at which the finite differences are evaluated. They solve the resulting flow equations for the effective average action at zero temperature across the BCS-BEC crossover, using optimized regulator functions that depend only on spatial momenta, and compute the equation of state, Bertsch parameter, gap parameter, Tan contact, and dimer-dimer scattering length as functions of \(c_Z\) and \(c_A\).

## Key numbers

- Bertsch parameter \(\xi = \mu/\epsilon_F\) at unitary: \(\xi = 0.553\) for \(c_Z = c_A = 0\) (derivative projection); \(\xi \approx 0.46\) for \(c_Z = 1.4\) independent of \(c_A\)
- Bertsch parameter values for \(c_A = 0\): \(\xi = 0.530\) at \(c_Z = 1\), \(\xi = 0.462\) at \(c_Z = 1.4\), \(\xi = 0.252\) at \(c_Z = 2.0\)
- Gap parameter \(\Delta/\epsilon_F\) at unitary: 0.60 at \(c_Z = 0\), 0.59 at \(c_Z^2 = 1\), 0.55 at \(c_Z^2 = 2\)
- Tan contact \(C/k_F^4\) at unitary: 0.11 at \(c_Z = 0\), 0.11 at both \(c_Z^2 = 1\) and \(c_Z^2 = 2\)
- Dimer-dimer to atom-atom scattering length ratio \(a_D/a\): 0.73 at \(c_Z = 0\); matches exact value 0.6 at \(c_Z \approx 0.90\)

## Context

This work extends the functional renormalization group approach to the BCS-BEC crossover developed in prior studies by the same group (PhysRevA.76.021602, PhysRevA.76.053627, ANDP:ANDP201010458) and others (Scherer 2010, Boettcher 2012), which used derivative projections at vanishing momentum and frequency. The method of varying specification parameters provides a systematic error estimate for truncated flow equations, complementing earlier investigations of cutoff dependence (Litim 2000) and partial bosonization dependence (PhysRevD.68.025020). The strong dependence on \(c_Z\) indicates that the frequency-independent regulator functions used in this truncation are insufficient, motivating the development of regulators that depend on both spatial momenta and frequencies.
