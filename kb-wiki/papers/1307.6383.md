# Analytical transformed harmonic oscillator basis for three-body nuclei of astrophysical interest: Application to 6He

> Recently, a square-integrable discrete basis, obtained performing a simple analytical local scale transformation to the harmonic oscillator basis, has been proposed and successfully applied to study the properties of two-body systems. Here, the method is generalized to study three-body systems. To test the goodness of the formalism and establish its applicability and limitations, the capture reaction rate for the nucleosynthesis of the Borromean nucleus 6He (4He + n + n) is addressed. Results are compared with previous publications and with calculations based on actual three-body continuum wave functions, which can be generated for this simple case. The obtained results encourage the application to other Borromean nuclei of astrophysical interest such as 9Be and 12C, for which actual three-body continuum calculations are very involved.

## Key claim

The analytical transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) method, previously validated for two-body systems, is generalized to three-body systems and tested on the Borromean nucleus ⁶He; the authors show that the method reproduces results from actual three-body continuum calculations for this simple case, but the formalism's reliability for more complex Borromean nuclei (⁹Be, ¹²C, ¹⁷Ne) remains to be established since actual three-body continuum calculations for those systems are very involved.

## Method

The authors construct a square-integrable discrete basis by applying an analytical local scale transformation (LST) of the Karataglidis form to the harmonic oscillator (HO) hyperradial functions, then diagonalize the three-body Hamiltonian in this transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis using hyperspherical coordinates. The Hamiltonian includes two-body n-n and α-n potentials (fixed for all j^π) plus a simple central hyperradial three-body force whose parameters are adjusted to reproduce the experimental 0⁺ ground-state energy and 2⁺ resonance position of ⁶He. Transition probabilities B(Eλ) are computed from the resulting eigenstates, and the radiative capture reaction rate for ⁴He + n + n → ⁶He + γ is obtained via the inverse photodissociation process using a Poisson smoothing procedure to convert discrete B(E1) values into continuous distributions.

## Key numbers

- Experimental binding energy of ⁶He ground state: 0.975 MeV
- Experimental rms point nucleon matter radius of ⁶He: 2.5–2.6 fm
- Known 2⁺ resonance in ⁶He: 0.824 MeV above breakup threshold
- THO basis truncated at maximum hypermomentum K_max = 20
- THO basis parameters for 0⁺ states: b = 0.7 fm, γ = 1.4 fm^{1/2}
- LST parameter ξ fixed to 4 for all calculations
- Quadrupole electric contribution to the reaction rate is several orders of magnitude lower than the dipole contribution

## Context

This work extends the analytical THO method from two-body systems (previously developed by Moro et al. (2009) and Lay et al. (2010)) to three-body Borromean nuclei, building on earlier numerical THO applications to ⁶He by Rodríguez-Gallardo et al. (2005, 2008). The method provides an alternative to the binning procedure used in Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels (CDCC) formalism (Austern, 1987) and to the R-matrix method (Wigner, 1947; Thompson, 2000; Descouvemont, 2006), with the advantage that it does not require calculating continuum wave functions or matching asymptotic behavior. The successful benchmark on ⁶He enables future application to astrophysically important Borromean nuclei such as ⁹Be (α+α+n) and ¹²C (α+α+α), where the triple-α reaction rate for stellar nucleosynthesis remains uncertain, and to ¹⁷Ne (¹⁵O+p+p) for rp-process nucleosynthesis.
