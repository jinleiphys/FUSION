# Two-pion-exchange in the non-mesonic weak decay of $Λ$-hypernuclei

> The non-mesonic weak decay of $Λ$--hypernuclei is studied within a one-meson-exchange potential supplemented by a chirally motivated two-pion-exchange mechanism. The effects of final state interactions on the outgoing nucleons are also taken into account. In view of the severe discrepancies between theoretical expectations and experimental data, particular attention is payed to the asymmetry of the protons emitted by polarized hypernuclei. The one-meson-exchange model describes the non-mesonic rates and the neutron-to-proton ratio satisfactorily but predicts a too large and negative asymmetry parameter. The uncorrelated and correlated two-pion mechanisms change the rates moderately, thus maintaining the agreement with experiment. The modification in the strength and sign of some decay amplitudes becomes crucial and produces asymmetry parameters which lie well within the experimental observations.

## Key claim

The addition of uncorrelated and correlated two-pion-exchange mechanisms to a one-meson-exchange (OME) potential for the non-mesonic weak decay of Λ-hypernuclei changes the decay rates only moderately but produces intrinsic asymmetry parameters that lie well within recent experimental observations, resolving the long-standing discrepancy where OME models predicted too large and negative asymmetries.

## Method

The authors employed a finite-nucleus approach with a one-meson-exchange potential (π, ρ, K, K*, ω, η) from Ref. Pa97, supplemented by uncorrelated and correlated two-pion-exchange potentials from Ref. Os01, where the correlated two-pion-exchange in the scalar-isoscalar channel was treated via a chiral unitary approach that dynamically generates the σ-meson resonance. The weak transition amplitudes were computed in a coupled two-body spin-isospin basis using harmonic oscillator wave functions for the initial ΛN system, with nucleon final state interactions accounted for through a Monte Carlo intranuclear cascade model.

## Key numbers

- For ⁵_ΛHe with the full OME+2π+2π/σ model: Γ_NM = 0.388 (in units of free Λ decay width), Γ_n/Γ_p = 0.415, intrinsic asymmetry a_Λ = +0.041
- For ¹¹_ΛB with the full model: Γ_NM = 0.627, Γ_n/Γ_p = 0.474, intrinsic asymmetry a_Λ = -0.181
- For ¹²_ΛC with the full model: Γ_NM = 0.667, Γ_n/Γ_p = 0.357, intrinsic asymmetry a_Λ = -0.698
- Experimental intrinsic asymmetry for ⁵_ΛHe from KEK-E462: 0.11 ± 0.08 ± 0.04 (Ref. OutaVa, Ka06, Ma05) and 0.07 ± 0.08^{+0.08}_{-0.00} (Ref. Ma06)
- Experimental intrinsic asymmetry for ¹¹_ΛB from KEK-E508: -0.20 ± 0.26 ± 0.04 (Ref. OutaVa, Ma05) and -0.16 ± 0.28^{+0.18}_{-0.00} (Ref. Ma06)
- The OME-only model gave a_Λ = -0.590 for ⁵_ΛHe, -0.809 for ¹¹_ΛB, and -0.698 for ¹²_ΛC

## Context

This work extends previous one-meson-exchange models (Pa97, Sa00, Ba06) that successfully described non-mesonic decay rates and the Γ_n/Γ_p ratio but failed to reproduce the small asymmetry parameters observed in recent KEK experiments (OutaVa, Ma05, Ma06). It builds on the two-pion-exchange studies of Os01 and It02, which found sizable cancellation between uncorrelated and correlated 2π-exchange, and on the effective field theory approach of Pa04 that suggested the importance of scalar-isoscalar contact interactions. The results demonstrate that two-pion-exchange mechanisms, while moderately affecting decay rates, crucially modify the interference between parity-conserving and parity-violating amplitudes, enabling simultaneous agreement with both rate and asymmetry data for s- and p-shell hypernuclei.
