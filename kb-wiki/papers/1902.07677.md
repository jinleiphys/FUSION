# Calculation of an $A=3$ bound-state matrix element in pionless effective field theory

> In this paper, we establish a general framework for calculating pionless matrix elements between $A=3$ bound-states up to next-to-leading-order. This framework is useful for pionless calculations of electroweak observables, such as $^3$H,$^3$He magnetic moments and $^3$H $β$ decay. Starting from a Bethe-Salpeter equation, we prove that for a bound-state, the three-nucleon wave-function normalization can be expressed diagrammatically in a way that is equivalent to the unit operator between two identical three-nucleon bound-states. This diagrammatic form of the identity matrix element is the foundation for constructing an $A=3$ matrix element of a general operator. We show that this approach can be used to calculate the energy difference between $^3$H and $^3$He due to the Coulomb interaction, and to calculate the NLO corrections to the $^3$H and $^3$He scattering amplitudes due to effective range corrections.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a general diagrammatic framework for calculating matrix elements between A=3 bound states in pionless effective field theory up to next-to-leading order, proving that the three-nucleon wave-function normalization can be expressed diagrammatically in a way equivalent to the unit operator between two identical three-nucleon bound-states, with the caveat that the regular part of the scattering amplitude is neglected near the binding energy pole.

## Method

The authors start from a Bethe-Salpeter equation and use a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation on the pionless EFT Hamiltonian to obtain a Hamiltonian of single nucleons and dibaryon fields, whose interactions are tuned to reproduce physical scattering lengths and effective ranges. They then derive the homogeneous Faddeev equations for the A=3 bound-state amplitudes of tritium and helium-3, including Coulomb interactions perturbatively for the latter, and show that the normalization of the bound-state wave-function can be expressed as a sum over diagrammatic connections between two identical three-nucleon amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron residue Z_d = 1.690(3) from the full propagator, with LO value Z_d^LO = 1 and NLO value Z_d^NLO = 1.408
- Deuteron binding momentum gamma_t = 45.701 MeV
- Triplet effective range rho_t = 1.765 fm
- Singlet scattering length a_s = -23.714 fm
- Singlet effective range rho_s = 2.73 fm
- Proton-proton scattering length a_p = -7.8063 fm
- Proton-proton effective range rho_C = 2.794 fm
- Typical momentum scale for He-3 bound-state Q ~ 70 MeV, giving Coulomb parameter eta(Q) << 1

## Context

This work extends the pionless EFT calculations of A=3 systems by Konig et al. (2015-2017), who calculated the binding energy difference between H-3 and He-3 from Coulomb interactions, and Vanasse et al. (2014-2015), who calculated NLO corrections to scattering amplitudes and effective range corrections to three-nucleon binding energy. The framework enables future calculations of electroweak observables such as H-3 and He-3 magnetic moments and H-3 beta decay, building on the 2017 NPLQCD collaboration calculation of triton beta-decay that calibrated the weak low-energy constant L_{1,A}.
