# The hep reaction and the solar neutrino problem

> The results of a new calculation of the astrophysical S-factor for the proton weak capture on 3He are here reviewed. The methods used to obtain very accurate initial and final state wave functions and to construct the nuclear weak current operator are described. Finally the implications of these results for the Super-Kamiokande solar neutrino data are discussed.

## Key claim

The new calculation yields an astrophysical S-factor for the hep reaction that is about 4.5 times larger than the Standard Solar Model value, but this enhancement is still insufficient to fully resolve the observed excess of high-energy events in the Super-Kamiokande solar neutrino data.

## Method

The authors used the correlated-hyperspherical-harmonics (CHH) method to compute accurate four-body wave functions for the initial p-3He scattering state and the final 4He bound state, employing the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana-IX three-nucleon interactions (and older AV14/UVIII for comparison). The nuclear weak current operator was constructed including one-body terms with 1/m^2 relativistic corrections, two-body vector currents derived from the nucleon-nucleon interaction via the conserved-vector-current hypothesis, and two-body axial currents with Δ-isobar excitation treated non-perturbatively in the transition-correlation-operator scheme, with the NΔ axial coupling constant adjusted to reproduce tritium β-decay.

## Key numbers

- The best estimate for the astrophysical S-factor at 10 keV (near the Gamow-peak energy) is 10.1 × 10^{-20} keV b, compared to the Standard Solar Model value of 2.3 × 10^{-20} keV b.
- The S-factor at zero energy is 9.64 × 10^{-20} keV b (AV18/UIX model, full current), with the 3S1 channel contributing 6.38 × 10^{-20} keV b and P-wave channels contributing the remainder.
- The S-factor at 10 keV is only about 4% larger than at 0 keV, showing weak energy dependence.
- Inclusion of the three-nucleon interaction (AV18/UIX vs. AV18) reduces the total S-factor by about 20%, from 12.1 to 9.64 × 10^{-20} keV b at zero energy.
- The many-body axial currents reduce the 3S1 capture S-factor by more than a factor of four (from 26.4 to 6.38 × 10^{-20} keV b at zero energy).
- The 4He binding energy calculated with the CHH method is 27.89 MeV for AV18/UIX, compared to the experimental value of 28.3 MeV.
- The p-3He triplet scattering length is 9.13 fm for AV18/UIX, compared to experimental values of 8.1±0.5 fm and 10.2±1.5 fm.

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation by Schiavilla et al. (1992), which used variational Monte Carlo techniques and obtained the SSM value of 2.3 × 10^{-20} keV b, by including all P-wave contributions, using the more accurate CHH method with modern realistic interactions, and incorporating 1/m^2 relativistic corrections in the one-body axial current. The new S-factor value of 10.1 × 10^{-20} keV b implies a hep flux enhancement factor α of 4.4 (without neutrino oscillations) or 2.2 (with the observed 0.5 suppression), which is insufficient to fully explain the Super-Kamiokande high-energy excess but enables better discrimination among proposed neutrino oscillation scenarios.
