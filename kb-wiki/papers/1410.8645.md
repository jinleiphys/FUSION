# $D^*\bar{D}_1(2420)$ and $D\bar{D}'^*(2600)$ interactions and the charged charmonium-like state $Z(4430)$

> The $D^*\bar{D}_1(2420)$ and $D\bar{D}'^*(2600)$ interactions are studied in a one-boson-exchange model. Isovector bound state solutions with spin parity $J^P=1^{+}$ are found from the $D^*\bar{D}_1(2420)$ interaction, which may be related to the observed charged charmonium-like state $Z(4430)$. There is no bound state solution found from the $D\bar{D}'^*(2600)$ interaction.

## Key claim
Isovector bound state solutions with spin parity $J^P=1^+$ are found from the $D^*\bar{D}_1(2420)$ interaction in a one-boson-exchange model, which may be related to the observed charged charmonium-like state $Z(4430)$. However, the new observed mass of $Z(4430)$ is above the $D^*\bar{D}_1(2420)$ threshold, so the interpretation remains conditional on the large current uncertainties and broad width of the experimental state.

## Method
The $D^*\bar{D}_1(2420)$ and $D\bar{D}'^*(2600)$ interactions were studied by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation reduced to a three-dimensional integral equation via the covariant spectator theory, combined with a one-boson-exchange potential. The potential was derived from effective Lagrangians based on heavy quark effective theory and chiral symmetry, including $\pi$ and $\sigma$ exchange, with form factors introduced to compensate off-shell effects and self-energies of the unstable constituents. The integral equation was solved numerically after discretization by Gaussian quadrature using a recursion method, scanning cut-off parameters from 0.8 to 2 GeV for all quantum numbers $J \leq 2$.

## Key numbers
- The $Z(4430)$ mass measured by Belle: $M = 4433 \pm 4 \text{(stat)} \pm 2 \text{(syst)}$ MeV, width $\Gamma = 45^{+18}_{-13} \text{(stat)}^{+30}_{-13} \text{(syst)}$ MeV.
- The $Z(4430)$ mass from LHCb: $4475 \pm 7^{+15}_{-25}$ MeV, width $172 \pm 13^{+37}_{-34}$ MeV, with favored spin parity $J^P = 1^+$.
- The coupling constant for $D_1 \to D^*\pi$ extracted from old data: $h' = 0.55$ GeV$^{-1}$, corresponding to $\Gamma_{tot}(D_1(2420)) \approx 6$ MeV; the PDG value is $25 \pm 6$ MeV, giving a largest possible $h'$ of about 1 GeV$^{-1}$.
- The coupling constant for $D'^*$ decay: $g' = 0.086$ and $h'' = 0.42$ GeV$^{-1}$, extracted from quark model decay widths $\Gamma_{D'^* \to D\pi} = 10.84$ MeV and $\Gamma_{D'^* \to D_1\pi} = 0.28$ MeV.
- With $h' = 0.95$ GeV$^{-1}$, isovector bound states with $J^{PC} = 1^{++}$ and $J^{PC} = 1^{+-}$ are generated at cut-offs of about 1.3 GeV and 1.5 GeV, respectively.
- No bound state solution was found from the $D\bar{D}'^*(2600)$ interaction, even with $h''^2$ increased by a factor of 10.
- A bound solution with $J^P = 0^-$ exists from $D^*\bar{D}_1$ with cut-off about 1.8 GeV.

## Context
This work extends previous one-boson-exchange calculations by Close (2010) and Liu (2008) that studied $D^*\bar{D}_1(2420)$ as an $S$-wave molecular state with $J^P = 0^-$, but addresses the new Belle and LHCb results favoring $J^P = 1^+$. It also tests the suggestion by Ma (2014) that $Z(4430)$ may arise from $S$-wave $D\bar{D}'^*_1(2600)$ interaction. The calculation finds that the $D\bar{D}'^*(2600)$ interaction produces no bound state, and that the $D^*\bar{D}_1(2420)$ interaction with $J^P = 1^+$ is the viable molecular candidate. The work enables future studies of the $Z(4430)$ as a resonance from the $D^*\bar{D}_1(2420)$ interaction rather than a bound state, and calls for more comprehensive coupled-channel calculations with proper treatment of the non-zero widths of excited $D$ mesons.
