# The Transverse Energy-Energy Correlator in the Back-to-Back Limit

> We present an operator based factorization formula for the transverse energy-energy correlator (TEEC) hadron collider event shape in the back-to-back (dijet) limit. This factorization formula exhibits a remarkably symmetric form, being a projection onto a scattering plane of a more standard transverse momentum dependent factorization. Soft radiation is incorporated through a dijet soft function, which can be elegantly obtained to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) due to the symmetries of the problem. We present numerical results for the TEEC resummed to next-to-next-to-leading logarithm (NNLL) matched to fixed order at the LHC. Our results constitute the first NNLL resummation for a dijet event shape observable at a hadron collider, and the first analytic result for a hadron collider dijet soft function at NNLO. We anticipate that the theoretical simplicity of the TEEC observable will make it indispensable for precision studies of QCD at the LHC, and as a playground for theoretical studies of factorization and its violation.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) resummation for a dijet event shape observable at a hadron collider, but this result is restricted to the back-to-back (dijet) limit and to NNLL accuracy where the factorization formula is expected to hold; factorization-violating Glauber contributions are anticipated to appear at N4LO and are explicitly excluded from the present analysis.

## Method

The authors derive an operator-based factorization formula in Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) for the transverse energy-energy correlator (TEEC) in the limit where the azimuthal angle between hadrons approaches 180 degrees (the dijet limit). They compute the TEEC soft function analytically to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) by relating it to the color-singlet transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) soft function, and they perform resummation using renormalization group evolution in both virtuality and rapidity scales. The resummed results at NNLL are matched to fixed-order NLO calculations from Nlojet++ and compared with parton-level predictions at the LHC.

## Key numbers

- LHC center-of-mass energy: sqrt(s) = 13 TeV
- Jet transverse momentum threshold: average jet P_T >= 250 GeV
- Jet rapidity acceptance: |Y| < 2.5 for the two leading jets
- Particle rapidity acceptance: |y| < 2.5 for TEEC computation
- Anti-k_T jet algorithm with cone size R = 0.4
- Strong coupling constant: alpha_s(M_Z) = 0.118
- The factorization formula describes all contributions scaling as 1/tau modulo logarithms, where tau = sin^2((pi - phi)/2) and phi is the azimuthal angle between hadrons
- Factorization-violating terms are expected to appear first at N4LO

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of hadron collider event shapes beyond the zero-jet and one-jet cases (Stewart 2010, Becher 2015, Jouttenus 2013), where NNLL resummation had already been achieved, to the dijet case for the first time at NNLL. The TEEC observable was previously measured by ATLAS (2015, 2017) for jets, and the paper builds on recent progress in understanding the energy-energy correlator (EEC) in e+e- collisions (Moult 2018, Dixon 2018). The simplicity of the TEEC soft function, which can be expressed in terms of the color-singlet TMD soft function through NNLO, enables future studies of factorization violation at N3LL and provides a laboratory for precision QCD studies at the LHC.
