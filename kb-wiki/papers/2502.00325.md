# Transverse force distributions in the proton from lattice QCD

> Single-spin asymmetries observed in polarised deep-inelastic scattering are important probes of hadron structure. The Sivers asymmetry provides information about the transverse momentum of the struck quark and can be related to final-state interactions. Understanding these asymmetries at the quark level has been the subject of much interest in QCD phenomenology. In this contribution, we present a lattice QCD calculation of the transverse spatial distribution of a colour-Lorentz force acting on the struck quark in a proton. Our lattice calculations employ $N_f = 2 + 1$ flavours of dynamical fermions at the SU(3) symmetric point across three lattice spacings. We determine a central, spin-independent confining force, as well as spin-dependent force distributions with local forces larger than the QCD string tension. These distributions offer a new, complimentary picture that underlies the Sivers asymmetry in transversely polarised deep-inelastic scattering.

# Transverse force distributions in the proton from lattice QCD

## Key claim
The paper presents a lattice QCD calculation of the transverse spatial distribution of a colour-Lorentz force acting on the struck quark in a proton, claiming that these force distributions offer a complementary picture underlying the Sivers asymmetry in transversely polarised deep-inelastic scattering. The hedge is that the force magnitudes are estimated by assuming the density-weighted force factorises in impact parameter space and by dividing out the quark density dependence, with the results showing some model dependence.

## Method
The authors compute off-forward matrix elements of twist-three operators using three gauge ensembles with N_f = 2+1 flavours of dynamical fermions at the SU(3) symmetric point across three lattice spacings. They extract three new form factors (Phi_1, Phi_2, Phi_3) from these matrix elements using a two-state fit to account for excited state contamination, then perform 2D Fourier transforms to obtain the colour-Lorentz force distributions in transverse impact parameter space. The operators are renormalised non-perturbatively using the RI'-MOM scheme, with mixing between the operator of interest and a lower-dimensional operator accounted for.

## Key numbers
- The colour-Lorentz force acting on an unpolarised up quark in an unpolarised proton rises to a maximum of 3 GeV/fm at approximately 0.15 fm and remains approximately constant for larger separations.
- This local colour-Lorentz force is nearly three times as large as the phenomenological value for the QCD string tension.
- The force magnitude for an unpolarised up quark in a proton polarised in the x-direction decreases to approximately 3 GeV/fm at about 0.25 fm.
- The up quark Phi_1 form factor magnitude is roughly double in size relative to the down quark, consistent with quark counting expectations.
- The finest lattice spacing used is a = 0.052 fm.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of Burkardt (2013) and Burkardt (2019), who proposed interpreting twist-three matrix elements as colour-Lorentz forces and generalised the forward matrix element d_2 to off-forward kinematics for imaging in impact parameter space. The calculation provides a complementary perspective to the Sivers transverse momentum distribution approach, which has been studied experimentally by HERMES (2007, 2008) and COMPASS (2006). The results enable future studies of the transverse distribution of these forces and may help develop intuitive visual representations of the origin of single-spin asymmetries, with the authors noting that expanding the momentum range on which the form factors are calculated will allow for a more rigorous model dependence study.
