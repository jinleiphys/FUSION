# Hadronization and Quark Probes of Deconfinement at RHIC

> We discuss experimental features of identified particle production from nucleus-nucleus collisions. These features reflect hadronization from a deconfined partonic matter whose particle formation scheme is distinctly different from fragmentation phenomenology in elementary collisions. Multi-parton dynamics, such as quark coalescences or recombinations, appear to be essential to explain the experimental measurements at the intermediate transverse momentum of 2--5 GeV/c. Constituent quarks seem to be the dominant degrees of freedom at hadronization. Heavy quark production should help quantify deconfined matter properties.

# Hadronization and Quark Probes of Deconfinement at RHIC

## Key claim

Multi-parton dynamics such as quark coalescence or recombination, rather than traditional fragmentation, are essential to explain hadron production in the intermediate transverse momentum region of 2–5 GeV/c in nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC; constituent quarks appear to be the dominant degrees of freedom at hadronization, though the authors present this as an interpretation of experimental patterns rather than a proven conclusion.

## Method

The authors review experimental data from the STAR and PHENIX collaborations at RHIC for identified particle production (pions, kaons, protons, lambdas, xis, omegas) in AuAu collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=130 and 200 GeV, comparing yields, nuclear modification factors (R_CP and R_AA), and azimuthal anisotropy (v_2) across centrality classes. They also examine charm production via D meson reconstruction and semi-leptonic electron decays, comparing measured cross sections and transverse momentum distributions to pQCD calculations including FONLL and fragmentation function models.

## Key numbers

- Baryon-to-meson ratios (anti-lambda/K_S and anti-proton/pi) in central AuAu collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV show values approaching or exceeding 1.0 in the intermediate p_T region of 2–5 GeV/c, far above the ~20% maximum observed in e+e- collisions
- Net baryon density at mid-rapidity in the most central 5% of AuAu200 reactions: d(B-anti-B)/dy = 14 ± 2
- Strangeness yield at mid-rapidity: ds/dy = 135 ± 10
- Entropy yield at mid-rapidity: dS/dy = 4900 ± 400
- Strangeness per entropy ratio: s/S = (2.9 ± 0.3)×10^(-3) for central collisions, compared to (1.9 ± 0.3)×10^(-3) for peripheral collisions
- Baryochemical potential at hadronization: mu_B = 25 ± 1 MeV
- Hadronization temperature: T = 140–155 ± 8 MeV
- STAR measured total charm quark pair production cross section: sigma_cc-bar = 1300 ± 200 ± 400 microbarns
- PHENIX preliminary non-photonic electron measurement: sigma_cc-bar = 709 ± 85 (stat) +332/-281 (sys) microbarns
- Mean transverse momentum for D^0 from STAR: <p_T> = 1.32 ± 0.08 GeV/c
- Next-to-leading order pQCD calculation yields sigma_cc-bar = 300–450 microbarns, significantly below STAR measurement

## Context

This paper extends the line of work on strangeness enhancement as a signature of deconfinement, building on earlier observations from the WA97 and NA57 experiments at CERN-SPS and theoretical predictions by Rafelski (1982) and Koch, Mueller, and Rafelski (1986). The authors argue that the observed constituent quark number scaling of elliptic flow (v_2/n versus p_T/n) and the distinct meson/baryon grouping in nuclear modification factors cannot be explained by parton energy loss scenarios (Vitev, 2003) or hydrodynamic flow alone (Huovinen et al., 2001), and instead require a sudden hadronization mechanism from a deconfined partonic source. The charm production results raise questions about fragmentation function universality, as the D meson p_T distribution matches bare charm quark distributions from FONLL calculations (Cacciari et al., 2004) better than predictions using Peterson or Lund fragmentation functions.
