# Branching ratios for the beta decay of 21Na

> We have measured the beta-decay branching ratio for the transition from 21Na to the first excited state of 21Ne. A recently published test of the standard model, which was based on a measurement of the beta-nu correlation in the decay of 21Na, depended on this branching ratio. However, until now only relatively imprecise (and, in some cases, contradictory) values existed for it. Our new result, 4.74(4)%, reduces but does not remove the reported discrepancy with the standard model.

# Branching Ratios for the Beta Decay of 21Na

## Key claim

The new measurement of the beta-decay branching ratio from 21Na to the first excited state of 21Ne, 4.74(4)%, reduces but does not remove the reported discrepancy between the measured beta-neutrino angular correlation coefficient and the standard-model prediction. The improved branching ratio shifts the standard-model prediction downward by 0.9% relative to the value used in the original work, yet the experimental result of Scielzo et al. (2004) still disagrees with the prediction.

## Method

The authors produced a 21Na secondary beam via the 1H(22Ne,2n)21Na reaction using a 28A-MeV 22Ne beam from the Texas A&M K500 superconducting cyclotron, purified the beam to >99% purity using the MARS spectrometer, and implanted it on a tape transport system. They measured beta-gamma coincidences between a plastic scintillator beta detector and a HPGe gamma detector, determining the branching ratio from the ratio of 351-keV gamma rays observed in coincidence with positrons to the total number of beta singles, corrected for random coincidences, real-coincidence summing, dead time, and beta-detector energy response.

## Key numbers

- Branching ratio to the first excited state of 21Ne: R1 = 4.74(4)% (0.85% relative uncertainty)
- Ground-state branching ratio: R0 = 95.26(4)%
- Ratio of beta-coincident 351-keV gamma rays to beta singles: N_gamma-beta/N_beta = 2.378(13) x 10^-4
- HPGe detector absolute efficiency at 351 keV: epsilon_gamma = 5.12(3) x 10^-3
- Correction factor k = 1.0208(24), composed of k1 = 0.9884(10) (random coincidences), k2 = 1.0178(17) (real-coincidence summing), k3 = 1.0018(1) (dead time), k4 = 1.0129(13) (beta-detector response)
- Extracted ft value for ground-state transition: fV t = 4040(9) s
- Ratio of axial-vector to vector components: lambda = 0.7033(24)
- Standard-model prediction for beta-neutrino angular correlation coefficient: a_beta-nu = 0.553(2)
- Revised correction factor for Scielzo et al. data: +6.44(5)%, shifting their result from 0.524(9) to 0.523(9)

## Context

This work resolves a long-standing inconsistency among five previous measurements of the 21Na branching ratio spanning 1960-1980 (Talbert and Stewart 1960, Arnell and Wernbom 1963, Alburger 1974, Azuelos et al. 1977, Wilson et al. 1980), which ranged from 2.2(3)% to 5.1(2)% and were based on a method comparing 351-keV peak area to annihilation radiation. The new result enables a more precise standard-model test using the beta-nu correlation measurement of Scielzo et al. (2004), but the authors note that the remaining discrepancy may be due to 21Na2 dimers in the trapped sample rather than new physics. The paper also identifies that the half-life of 21Na (last measured in 1974 and 1977) and its Q-value (from a single 1969 measurement) could be improved by modern measurements to further refine the standard-model prediction.
