# Spectroscopic factors in dripline nuclei

> Single-nucleon knockout reaction studies of the proton-dripline nuclei $^9$C and $^{13}$O suggest an appreciable suppression of spectroscopic factors. In this work, we calculate the one-neutron and one-proton spectroscopic factors for the mirror pair $^9$C-$^9$Li and $^{13}$O using two variants of the continuum shell model: the complex-energy Gamow Shell Model and the real-energy Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum. Our results indicate that the continuum effects strongly suppress the spectroscopic factors of well-bound orbits in the dripline systems, but have less impact on the spectroscopic factors of weakly-bound states.

## Key claim

Continuum coupling effects strongly suppress spectroscopic factors for the removal of well-bound (minority species) nucleons from dripline nuclei, but have little impact on spectroscopic factors when the removed nucleon is weakly bound or unbound (majority species).

## Method

The authors performed calculations using two open-quantum-system frameworks: the complex-energy Gamow Shell Model (GSM) and the real-energy Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC). They computed one-neutron and one-proton spectroscopic factors for the mirror pair ⁹C-⁹Li and for ¹³O, comparing results with standard closed-quantum-system shell model calculations to quantify continuum effects. The GSM used a Woods-Saxon core potential with Furutani-Horiuchi-Tamagaki two-body interaction in ps- and psd-shell model spaces, while SMEC used the YSOX interaction in a 4ħω (psd)-model space with a Wigner-Bartlett contact interaction for continuum coupling.

## Key numbers

- For ⁹C → ⁸C (neutron knockout, S_n = 14.22 MeV): HO-SM spectroscopic factor = 0.86; GSM-psd with N_cont=4 gives 0.48
- For ⁹Li → ⁸He (proton knockout, S_p = 13.94 MeV): HO-SM spectroscopic factor = 0.85; GSM-psd with N_cont=4 gives 0.65
- For ⁹C → ⁸B (proton knockout, S_p = 1.30 MeV): HO-SM spectroscopic factor = 0.95; GSM-psd with N_cont=4 gives 0.89
- For ⁹Li → ⁸Li (neutron knockout, S_n = 4.06 MeV): HO-SM spectroscopic factor = 0.96; GSM-psd with N_cont=4 gives 0.88
- The ⁵/₂₁⁺ resonance in ¹³F has a width Γ = 1.01(27) MeV and lies 0.48(19) MeV above the proton decay threshold of ¹²O(2₂⁺)
- SMEC continuum-coupling strength V₀ = -100 MeV·fm³ reproduces the measured decay width of ¹³F

## Context

This work extends studies of spectroscopic factor suppression in dripline nuclei, addressing the unresolved systematic behavior of the ratio R_s = σ_expt/σ_theor versus ΔS = S_p - S_n reported by Gade (2004, 2008) and Tostevin (2021). It contradicts the conclusion of Atar (2018) and Holl (2019) that spectroscopic factors show no significant dependence on proton-neutron asymmetry, while supporting coupled-cluster findings by Jensen (2011) showing significant quenching. The results enable consistent treatment of reaction and structure models for nucleon removal cross sections, which had not been accomplished previously.
