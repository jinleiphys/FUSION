# Forward doubly-virtual Compton scattering off an unpolarised deuteron in pionless effective field theory

> We calculate the forward unpolarised doubly-virtual Compton scattering (VVCS) off the deuteron in the framework of pionless effective field theory, up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) for the longitudinal and next-to-leading order (NLO) for the transverse amplitude. The charge elastic form factor of the deuteron, obtained from the residue of the longitudinal VVCS amplitude, is used to extract the value of the single unknown two-nucleon one-photon contact coupling that enters the longitudinal amplitude at N3LO. We also study the lowest spin-independent generalised polarisabilities of the deuteron. The calculated unpolarised VVCS amplitude provides a high-precision model-independent input for a future calculation of the two-photon-exchange correction to the Lamb shift of muonic deuterium.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the forward unpolarised doubly-virtual Compton scattering (VVCS) off the deuteron using pionless effective field theory up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) for the longitudinal amplitude and next-to-leading order (NLO) for the transverse amplitude, but note that the predictive power of the theory for the two-photon-exchange correction to the muonic deuterium Lamb shift is exhausted at N3LO because nucleon polarisability contributions to the longitudinal amplitude appear at N4LO and require an unknown two-nucleon one-lepton contact term.

## Method

The calculation employs pionless effective field theory (piEFT) with a non-relativistic expansion, using the z-parametrisation scheme for the nucleon-nucleon T-matrix in the spin-triplet channel, which reproduces the residue of the scattering amplitude at the deuteron pole at NLO rather than fitting the effective range. The longitudinal VVCS amplitude is computed to N3LO (order P) and the transverse amplitude to NLO (order P), including all relevant contact interactions from the two-nucleon Lagrangian up to those orders, with the single unknown two-nucleon one-photon contact coupling in the longitudinal amplitude determined by fitting to the deuteron charge radius.

## Key numbers

- The piEFT expansion parameter is gamma/m_pi approximately 1/3, where gamma approximately 45 MeV is the deuteron binding momentum, giving an expected relative precision of order (1/3)^4 approximately 1% at N3LO
- The deuteron electric dipole polarisability alpha_E1 scales as O(P^{-4}) with leading term alpha M/(32 pi gamma^4)
- The deuteron magnetic dipole polarisability beta_M1 scales as O(P^{-2}) with leading term -alpha/(32 M gamma^2)[1 - (16/3)mu_1^2 + (32/3)mu_1^2 gamma/(gamma_s - gamma)]
- The longitudinal amplitude f_L starts at O(P^{-2}) while the transverse amplitude f_T starts two orders higher at O(P^0)
- The deuteron binding energy E_d = -2.224575(9) MeV and residue Z = 1.6893(30) are used as input parameters

## Context

This work extends previous pionless EFT calculations of deuteron structure functions, particularly the NNLO result by Emmons et al. (2020) which achieved approximately 5% precision for the inelastic part of the two-photon-exchange correction, and provides an alternative to nuclear Hamiltonian approaches by Friar (1977, 2013) and Pachucki (2011, 2015) as well as dispersion relation approaches by Carlson (2013) and Hernandez (2019). The calculated VVCS amplitude enables a future high-precision, model-independent calculation of the two-photon-exchange correction to the Lamb shift of muonic deuterium, with the detailed study to be presented in a subsequent publication.
