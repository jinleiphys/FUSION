# Density dependence of the s-wave repulsion in pionic atoms

> Several mechanisms of density dependence of the s-wave repulsion in pionic atoms, beyond the conventional model, are tested by parameter fits to a large (106 points) set of data from $^{16}$O to $^{238}$U, including `deeply bound' states in $^{205}$Pb. Special attention is paid to the proper choice of nuclear density distributions. A density-dependent isovector scattering amplitude suggested recently by Weise to result from a density dependence of the pion decay constant is introduced and found to account for most of the so-called anomalous repulsion. The presence of such an effect might indicate partial chiral symmetry restoration in dense matter. The anomalous repulsion is fully accounted for when an additional relativistic impulse approximation term is included in the potential.

# Density dependence of the s-wave repulsion in pionic atoms

## Key claim

The anomalous s-wave repulsion in pionic atoms is mostly accounted for by a density-dependent isovector scattering amplitude derived from a density dependence of the pion decay constant, as suggested by Weise (2001); the anomaly is fully removed when an additional relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) term is included, but the paper notes that the RIA term is not unique and that the fits cannot distinguish between the various models on the basis of quality alone.

## Method

The author performed chi-squared fits of several pion-nucleus potential models to a data set of 106 strong-interaction level shifts and widths from pionic atoms ranging from 16O to 238U, including deeply bound states in 205Pb. The conventional Ericson-Ericson potential was compared with potentials incorporating a density-dependent isovector parameter b1(rho) following Weise (2001), two RIA terms with different effective nucleon mass parameterizations, and combinations of both. Fits were repeated for two types of nuclear density distributions (macroscopic and single-particle) and for two sets of neutron rms radii, with the p-wave part of the potential constrained to respect the low-density limit.

## Key numbers

- Data set contains 106 data points from 16O to 238U, including deeply bound 1s and 2p states in 205Pb.
- For the conventional potential, chi-squared per degree of freedom is about 1.9.
- The density-dependent isovector parameter follows b1(rho) = b1(0)/(1 - 2.3 rho) for sigma_N = 50 MeV, with rho in fm^-3.
- The RIA term uses effective nucleon mass M(rho)/M(0) = 1/(1 + a rho) with a = 2.7 fm^3 (giving ratio 0.7 at normal density) or a = 1.56 fm^3 (giving ratio 0.8).
- For 205Pb, proton rms radius is 5.45 fm; neutron rms radii used are 5.63 or 5.71 fm.
- For the conventional potential, ReB0 is 3-4 times larger than ImB0, which is described as "quite unacceptable."
- The real s-wave potential at the nuclear surface (near 6.2 fm for 208Pb) is about 25 MeV; at the half-density point (6.9 fm) it is about 11.8 MeV; the interior potential is close to 30-35 MeV, consistent with the 28 +/- 3 MeV value of Friedman and Gal (1998).

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of Friedman (2002) by using a larger data set (106 points vs. 60), more flexible nuclear density distributions including relativistic mean field constraints, and more freedom in the RIA model. It tests the suggestion of Weise (2001) and Kaiser and Weise (2001) that density dependence of the pion decay constant, indicating partial chiral symmetry restoration, can explain the s-wave repulsion anomaly previously noted by Oset et al. (1984), Goudsmit et al. (1988), and Oset et al. (1995). The results enable further tests of chirally motivated potentials through low-energy elastic scattering of pions by nuclei, an experiment approved at PSI (Friedman et al., 2002).
