# Nuclear electric dipole moment of light nuclei in the gaussian expansion method

> The nuclear electric dipole moment is a very sensitive probe of CP violation beyond the standard model, and for light nuclei, it can be evaluated accurately using few-body calculational methods. In this talk, we present the deuteron, $^3$He, $^3$H, $^6$Li, and $^9$Be electric dipole moments calculated using the Gaussian expansion method with a realistic nuclear force, and assuming the one-meson exchange model for the P, CP-odd nuclear force. We then give future prospects for models beyond the standard model such as the supersymmetry.

# Nuclear electric dipole moment of light nuclei in the Gaussian expansion method

## Key claim
The nuclear electric dipole moments (EDMs) of deuteron, ³He, ³H, ⁶Li, and ⁹Be can be accurately calculated using the Gaussian expansion method with a realistic nuclear force, assuming the one-meson exchange model for the P, CP-odd nuclear force. The results are presented as linear coefficients of the CP-odd couplings, with the caveat that the three-body force has not been considered in the ab initio calculations.

## Method
The authors solved the Schrödinger equation for few-nucleon systems using the Gaussian expansion method, where the nuclear wave function is expressed as a superposition of Gaussian basis functions with geometric progression range parameters. For the deuteron, ³He, and ³H, ab initio calculations were performed using the Argonne v18 interaction and the CP-odd one-pion exchange potential. For ⁶Li and ⁹Be, the cluster approximation was used, treating these nuclei as α+n+p and α+α+n three-body systems respectively, with the orthogonality condition model to approximate antisymmetrization effects and the Argonne v8' interaction for the N-N interaction.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron: linear coefficient a_π^(1) = 1.45 × 10⁻² e fm for the isovector CP-odd coupling; spin expectation values ⟨σ_p⟩ = 0.914 and ⟨σ_n⟩ = 0.914
- ³He: linear coefficients a_π^(0) = 0.59, a_π^(1) = 1.08, a_π^(2) = 1.68 (all in 10⁻² e fm); ⟨σ_p⟩ = -0.04, ⟨σ_n⟩ = 0.89
- ³H: linear coefficients a_π^(0) = -0.59, a_π^(1) = 1.08, a_π^(2) = -1.70 (all in 10⁻² e fm); ⟨σ_p⟩ = 0.88, ⟨σ_n⟩ = -0.05
- ⁶Li: linear coefficient a_π^(1) = 2.8 × 10⁻² e fm for the isovector CP-odd coupling; ⟨σ_p⟩ = 0.88, ⟨σ_n⟩ = 0.88
- ⁹Be: linear coefficient a_π^(1) = 1.7 × 10⁻² e fm for the isovector CP-odd coupling; ⟨σ_n⟩ = 0.75
- The nucleon number density of the α cluster used in folding: ρ_α(r) = (4/(b³ π^(3/2))) e^(-r²/b²) with b = 1.358 fm
- Planned storage ring EDM experiments have a sensitivity of O(10⁻²⁹) e cm

## Context
This work extends previous EDM calculations for light nuclei using the Gaussian expansion method, with results for the deuteron agreeing with Liu (2004), Afnan (2005), and Song (2011), and results for ³He and ³H agreeing with chiral effective field theory calculations by Bsaisou (2012, 2013). The calculations enable probing supersymmetry breaking mass scales at the level of 10 TeV and new physics mass scales up to the PeV range through planned storage ring experiments. The ⁶Li nucleus shows enhanced sensitivity to the isovector CP-odd nuclear force due to constructive interference between the CP-odd α-N interaction and the deuteron subcluster EDM, making it twice as sensitive as the deuteron alone.
