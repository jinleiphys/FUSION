# Nuclear electric dipole moment of light nuclei in the gaussian expansion method

> The nuclear electric dipole moment is a very sensitive probe of CP violation beyond the standard model, and for light nuclei, it can be evaluated accurately using few-body calculational methods. In this talk, we present the deuteron, $^3$He, $^3$H, $^6$Li, and $^9$Be electric dipole moments calculated using the Gaussian expansion method with a realistic nuclear force, and assuming the one-meson exchange model for the P, CP-odd nuclear force. We then give future prospects for models beyond the standard model such as the supersymmetry.

## Context
This work extends previous EDM calculations for light nuclei using the Gaussian expansion method, with results for the deuteron agreeing with Liu (2004), Afnan (2005), and Song (2011), and results for آ³He and آ³H agreeing with chiral effective field theory calculations by Bsaisou (2012, 2013). The calculations enable probing supersymmetry breaking mass scales at the level of 10 TeV and new physics mass scales up to the PeV range through planned storage ring experiments. The âپ¶Li nucleus shows enhanced sensitivity to the isovector CP-odd nuclear force due to constructive interference between the CP-odd خ±-N interaction and the deuteron subcluster EDM, making it twice as sensitive as the deuteron alone.
