# Ground-State Properties of $^{4}$He and $^{16}$O Extrapolated from Lattice QCD with Pionless EFT

> We extend the prediction range of Pionless Effective Field Theory with an analysis of the ground state of $^{16}$O in leading order. To renormalize the theory, we use as input both experimental data and lattice QCD predictions of nuclear observables, which probe the sensitivity of nuclei to increased quark masses. The nuclear many-body Schrödinger equation is solved with the Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo method. For the first time in a nuclear quantum Monte Carlo calculation, a linear optimization procedure, which allows us to devise an accurate trial wave function with a large number of variational parameters, is adopted. The method yields a binding energy of $^{4}$He which is in good agreement with experiment at physical pion mass and with lattice calculations at larger pion masses. At leading order we do not find any evidence of a $^{16}$O state which is stable against breakup into four $^4$He, although higher-order terms could bind $^{16}$O.

## Key claim

At leading order in Pionless Effective Field Theory, the ground state of $^{16}$O is not bound against breakup into four $^4$He nuclei, although the authors note that higher-order terms in the EFT expansion could bind $^{16}$O. The method yields $^4$He binding energies in good agreement with experiment at physical pion mass and with lattice QCD calculations at larger pion masses, but the agreement for $^4$He at physical pion mass is described as "somewhat fortuitous" when truncation errors are considered.

## Method

The authors solved the nuclear many-body Schrödinger equation using the Auxiliary Field Diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) method with a leading-order Pionless Effective Field Theory Hamiltonian containing two-nucleon contact interactions and one three-nucleon contact interaction, regularized with a Gaussian cutoff. For the first time in a nuclear quantum Monte Carlo calculation, they adopted a linear optimization procedure to devise accurate trial wave functions with a large number of variational parameters (30 parameters for two- and three-body Jastrow correlations and 15 parameters for each radial orbital function in $^{16}$O). The low-energy constants were determined by fitting to experimental data (deuteron and triton binding energies, $^1S_0$ scattering length) at physical pion mass and to lattice QCD predictions at unphysical pion masses ($m_\pi \simeq 510$ MeV and $m_\pi \simeq 805$ MeV).

## Key numbers

* $^4$He binding energy at physical pion mass ($m_\pi=140$ MeV): extrapolated asymptotic value of $-30^{+0.3}_{-2}$ MeV (stat) $\pm 0.3$ MeV (sys), compared to experimental value of $-28.30$ MeV
* $^4$He binding energy at $m_\pi=510$ MeV: extrapolated asymptotic value of $-39^{+1}_{-2}$ MeV (stat) $\pm 1$ MeV (sys), compared to lattice QCD value of $-43.0 \pm 14.4$ MeV
* $^4$He binding energy at $m_\pi=805$ MeV: extrapolated asymptotic value of $-124^{+3}_{-1}$ MeV (stat) $\pm 3$ MeV (sys), compared to lattice QCD value of $-107.0 \pm 24.2$ MeV
* For $\Lambda=8$ fm$^{-1}$ at physical pion mass: $^4$He energy of $-26.04 \pm 0.05$ MeV
* For $\Lambda=8$ fm$^{-1}$ at $m_\pi=805$ MeV: $^4$He energy of $-101.72 \pm 0.03$ MeV
* Cutoff values used: 2, 4, 6, and 8 fm$^{-1}$, with extrapolation to $\Lambda \rightarrow \infty$

## Context

This work extends the Pionless Effective Field Theory (EFT($\slashed{\pi}$)) program to medium-mass nuclei ($A>6$) for the first time, building on prior calculations for $A=2,3$ systems by Chen (1999), Kong (1999), Vanasse (2013), and Konig (2015), and for $A=4$ by Platter (2004) and $A=6$ by Stetcu (2006). The authors use lattice QCD data from Beane (2012) at $m_\pi \simeq 805$ MeV and Yamazaki (2012) at $m_\pi \simeq 510$ MeV as input, following the approach of Barnea (2013) and Kirscher (2015). The results enable future higher-order calculations with more precise lattice QCD input, and the linear optimization method developed here opens the door for AFDMC calculations with more complex trial wave functions for heavier nuclei.
