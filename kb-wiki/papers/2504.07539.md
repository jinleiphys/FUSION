# $C$ and $CP$ violation in effective field theories and applications to $η$-meson decays

> The quest for sources of the simultaneous violation of $C$ and $CP$ symmetry was popular in the 1960s, but has since been neglected for a long time. We revisit the operators that break $C$ and $CP$ for flavor-conserving transitions in both the Standard Model effective field theory and the low-energy effective field theory, which subsequently can be matched to light-meson physics using chiral perturbation theory. As applications, we discuss in particular the $C$-odd Dalitz plot asymmetries in $η\to3π$, but also decays with dilepton pairs in the final state, such as long-distance contributions to the rare semileptonic decays $η\toπ^0\ell^+\ell^-$ as well as asymmetries in $η^{(\prime)} \to γ\ell^+\ell^-$ and $η^{(\prime)} \to π^+π^-\ell^+\ell^-$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that searches for simultaneous $C$ and $CP$ violation in $\eta$ and $\eta'$ meson decays are more promising than previously thought, because recent work shows that $C$-odd interactions can be generated from dimension-6 Standard Model Effective Field Theory (SMEFT) operators rather than requiring dimension-7 or -8 operators, making them comparable in scale to electric dipole moment operators. The key hedge is that the observables rely on interference between Standard Model and beyond-Standard-Model amplitudes, which requires non-vanishing strong final-state-interaction phases to be observable.

## Method

The author constructs a hierarchy of effective field theories connecting SMEFT to the low-energy effective field theory (LEFT) below the electroweak scale, then matches LEFT operators to chiral perturbation theory for light mesons using the external-source method with spurion fields. Dispersion-theoretical methods, including Khuri-Treiman equations and Omnès solutions, are employed to analyze final-state rescattering effects in $\eta\to3\pi$ decays and to relate these to transition form factors for semileptonic decays such as $\eta\to\pi^0\ell^+\ell^-$ and $\eta^{(\prime)}\to\gamma\ell^+\ell^-$.

## Key numbers

- $M_\eta = 547.86$ MeV, $\Gamma_\eta = 1.31$ keV; $M_{\eta'} = 957.78$ MeV, $\Gamma_{\eta'} = 196$ keV
- $C$-odd coupling constants from KLOE Dalitz plot data: $g_0 = -2.8(4.5)$ GeV$^{-6}$, $g_2 = -9.3(4.6) \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-2}$
- Standard Model branching ratio predictions: $\mathcal{B}(\eta\to\pi^0e^+e^-) = 1.36(15)\times 10^{-9}$, $\mathcal{B}(\eta\to\pi^0\mu^+\mu^-) = 0.67(7)\times 10^{-9}$
- Experimental upper limits: $\mathcal{B}(\eta\to\pi^0e^+e^-) < 7.5\times 10^{-6}$ (WASA-at-COSY 2018), $\mathcal{B}(\eta\to\pi^0\mu^+\mu^-) < 5\times 10^{-6}$ (Dzhelyadin 1980)
- Theoretical upper limits from $C$-odd long-distance contributions (isovector plus isoscalar): $\mathcal{B}(\eta\to\pi^0e^+e^-) < 29\times 10^{-6}$, $\mathcal{B}(\eta\to\pi^0\mu^+\mu^-) < 10\times 10^{-6}$
- $\mathcal{B}(\eta'\to\pi^0\pi^+\pi^-) \approx 3.6\times 10^{-3}$

## Context

This work extends the systematic effective-field-theory treatment of $C$-odd, $P$-even interactions for light mesons initiated by Akdag (2022), building on earlier operator classifications by Khriplovich (1990), Ramsey-Musolf (1999), and Kurylov (2000). A recent development by Shi (2024) shows that the leading $C$-odd four-quark operator can be generated from dimension-6 SMEFT operators via $W$-exchange, making these effects more accessible experimentally. The dispersive framework connects Dalitz plot asymmetries in $\eta\to3\pi$ to long-distance contributions in $\eta\to\pi^0\ell^+\ell^-$ decays, and enables systematic searches for $C$ violation in $\eta^{(\prime)}\to\gamma\ell^+\ell^-$ and $\eta^{(\prime)}\to\pi^+\pi^-\ell^+\ell^-$ decays at facilities like BESIII and future high-statistics $\eta$ factories.
