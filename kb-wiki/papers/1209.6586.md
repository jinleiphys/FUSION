# Recent Developments in Neutrino/Antineutrino - Nucleus Interactions

> Recent experimental results and developments in the theoretical treatment of neutrino-nucleus interactions in the energy range of 1-10 GeV are discussed. Difficulties in extracting neutrino-nucleon cross sections from neutrino-nucleus scattering data are explained and significance of understanding nuclear effects for neutrino oscillation experiments is stressed. Detailed discussions of the status of two-body current contribution in the kinematic region dominated by quasi-elastic scattering and specific features of partonic nuclear effects in weak DIS scattering are presented.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the long-standing discrepancy between the axial mass $M_A$ extracted from MiniBooNE CCQE data ($1.35\pm0.17$ GeV) and the world average from deuterium targets ($\approx 1.03$ GeV) can be resolved by including multinucleon (2p2h) mechanisms in the theoretical description, rather than requiring an anomalously large axial mass. The authors stress that the MiniBooNE "CCQE-like" signal definition includes events with no pions in the final state, which naturally incorporates two-body current contributions that mimic genuine quasielastic interactions.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical approaches to neutrino-nucleus interactions in the 1-10 GeV energy range, focusing on the hadronic tensor formalism for charged-current and neutral-current inclusive reactions. It compares relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) models, spectral function (SF) approaches, random phase approximation (RPA) corrections, and superscaling (SuSA) analyses against recent experimental data from MiniBooNE, K2K, MINOS, SciBooNE, NOMAD, and MINERvA, with particular emphasis on the role of multinucleon (2p2h) mechanisms in the CCQE-like kinematic region.

## Key numbers

- MiniBooNE CCQE axial mass extraction: $M_A = 1.35 \pm 0.17$ GeV (carbon, $Q^2 > 0$ GeV$^2$)
- World average from deuterium: $M_A \approx 1.03$ GeV
- K2K (oxygen, $Q^2 > 0.2$ GeV$^2$): $M_A = 1.2 \pm 0.12$ GeV
- K2K (carbon, $Q^2 > 0.2$ GeV$^2$): $M_A = 1.14 \pm 0.11$ GeV
- MINOS (iron, $Q^2 > 0$ GeV$^2$): $M_A = 1.19 \pm 0.17$ GeV; MINOS (iron, $Q^2 > 0.3$ GeV$^2$): $M_A = 1.26 \pm 0.17$ GeV
- NOMAD (carbon, $Q^2 > 0$ GeV$^2$): $M_A = 1.07 \pm 0.07$ GeV
- PCAC-based axial mass from weak pion production: $M_A = 1.077 \pm 0.039$ GeV
- MiniBooNE antineutrino CCQE DATA/MC cross-section ratio: $1.21 \pm 0.12$ (with NUANCE MC using $M_A = 1.35$ GeV)
- Spectral function calculations reduce total cross sections by approximately 15% relative to RFG
- MINERvA preliminary antineutrino CCQE results consistent with $M_A = 0.99$ GeV
- SciBooNE CCQE best fit factors in $E_\nu \in (0.6, 1.6)$ GeV: between 1.00 and 1.09, translating to $M_A \sim 1.25 - 1.3$ GeV

## Context

This review extends the discussion of neutrino-nucleus interactions from the lower-energy summary by Gallagher (2011) and addresses puzzles raised by recent measurements from MiniBooNE, K2K, MINOS, and SciBooNE that challenge the previously accepted axial mass value from deuterium experiments (Bernard et al., 2001; Lyubushkin et al., 2008). The work enables improved interpretation of neutrino oscillation experiments (T2K, NOvA, MINOS) by emphasizing that effective parameters like $M_A^{eff}$ depend on neutrino flux, target nucleus, and detector acceptance, and that reliable Monte Carlo event generators must incorporate multinucleon mechanisms alongside genuine quasielastic scattering, RPA correlations, and spectral function effects.
