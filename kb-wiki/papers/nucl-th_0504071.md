# Triaxiality, chirality and gamma-softness

> Current work explores the impact of gamma-softness on partner bands built on the h11/2h11/2 proton-particle-neutron-hole configurations in triaxial odd-odd nuclei. The results of calculations conducted using a core-particle-hole coupling are presented. The model Hamiltonian includes the collective core, the single-particle valence nucleons, and separable quadrupole-quadrupole interactions. The Kerman-Klein method was applied to find eigenstates, which provided a convenient way for exploring core effects. Calculations were made for triaxial cores with various gamma-softness using the General Collective Model keeping the expectation value for the triaxiality parameter fixed at <gamma>=30 deg. The degeneracy in the proton-neutron h11/2h11/2 bands results from the calculations for the gamma-rigid core but is lifted for the gamma-unstable core.

## Key claim

The energy separation between partner bands built on the πh11/2νh11/2 configuration in doubly-odd nuclei correlates with the γ-softness of the core: the degeneracy observed for a γ-rigid core is lifted for a γ-unstable core, but the calculations are presented as qualitative rather than quantitative because the model parameters were not optimized for any particular nucleus and the core basis was truncated at spin 10⁺.

## Method

Core-particle-hole coupling calculations were performed using the Kerman-Klein method to find eigenstates of a Hamiltonian including the collective core, single-particle valence nucleons, and separable quadrupole-quadrupole interactions. Triaxial cores of varying γ-softness were constructed using the General Collective Model, with the expectation value of the triaxiality parameter fixed at ⟨γ⟩ = 30° while keeping ⟨β⟩, ⟨β²⟩, and ⟨γ⟩ nearly identical across cores. The two lowest energy states at each spin were calculated for the πh11/2νh11/2 configuration coupled to these cores.

## Key numbers

- Energy separation between states of the same spin in the mid-spin range near ∼14ħ decreases from ∼400 keV for the γ-unstable core (core A) to ∼40 keV for the γ-rigid core (core F), a change of nearly an order of magnitude.
- In ¹⁰⁴Rh, the two 17⁻ states built on the πg⁻¹9/2νh11/2 configuration are 2 keV apart.
- In ¹³⁴Pr, the states at 15⁺ and 16⁺ built on the πh11/2νh⁻¹11/2 configuration are less than ∼50 keV apart.

## Context

This work extends the study of chiral partner bands in triaxial doubly-odd nuclei, following the proposal by Starosta (2004) that small energy spacing between states of the same spin in doublet bands is a signature of chiral geometry formation. The calculations show that γ-softness, previously difficult to distinguish in even-even and odd-mass nuclei as noted by Davydov (1958) and Wilets (1956), can be probed through particle-hole configurations in doubly-odd transitional nuclei. The results enable future improved calculations with more complete core bases and optimized parameters to examine whether stable triaxiality exists in nuclei such as ¹⁰⁴Rh and ¹³⁴Pr, and to test consistency with experimental electromagnetic transition rates.
