# Role of Vector Mesons in High-Q^2 Lepton-Nucleon Scattering

> The possible role played by vector mesons in inclusive deep inelastic lepton-nucleon scattering is investigated. In the context of the convolution model, we calculate self-consistently the scaling contribution to the nucleon structure function using the formalism of time-ordered perturbation theory in the infinite momentum frame. Our results indicate potentially significant effects only when the vector meson---nucleon form factor is very hard. Agreement with the experimental antiquark distributions, however, requires relatively soft form factors for the $πN$, $ρN$ and $ωN$ vertices.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether vector mesons (ρ and ω) contribute significantly to the nucleon structure function in deep inelastic scattering, finding that potentially significant effects occur only when the vector meson-nucleon form factor is very hard (Λ ≥ 1.8 GeV), but agreement with experimental antiquark distributions requires relatively soft form factors that make vector meson contributions marginal.

## Method

The authors use time-ordered perturbation theory in the infinite momentum frame to calculate self-consistently the meson and baryon distribution functions for πN, ρN, and ωN intermediate states, avoiding off-mass-shell ambiguities present in covariant formulations. They evaluate both the struck meson and struck baryon contributions to the nucleon structure function F₂N, using exponential form factors dependent on the center-of-mass energy squared of the meson-baryon system, and compare the resulting antiquark distributions against experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For Λ = 1.4 GeV, the ρ meson multiplicity ⟨n⟩_{ρN} is considerably smaller than ⟨n⟩_{πN}; only with Λ ≥ 1.8 GeV does the ρ multiplicity become comparable to that of the pion
- The πN N vertex with a dipole form factor cut-off Λ_{πN} ≈ 830 MeV saturates the SU(2) antiquark content of the nucleon in the intermediate-x region, corresponding to Λ ≈ 1.2 GeV in the exponential form factor
- For Λ ≤ 1.4 GeV, ⟨n⟩_{ρN} is much smaller than ⟨n⟩_{πN}; the πN multiplicity ⟨n⟩_{πN} ≈ 0.25 for the comparison shown
- The ρNN coupling constants used are g²_{ρNN}/4π = 0.55 and f_{ρNN}/g_{ρNN} = 6.1; the ωNN coupling constants are g²_{ωNN}/4π = 8.1 and f_{ωNN}/g_{ωNN} = 0

## Context

This work extends the convolution model previously applied to pion contributions in deep inelastic scattering, notably by Thomas (1983) and others who studied the pion cloud's role in generating antiquark asymmetries and the Gottfried sum rule violation observed by the NMC collaboration. The authors explicitly compare their time-ordered perturbation theory approach with the covariant formulations of earlier work, showing that the infinite momentum frame method automatically satisfies the relation f_{MB}(y) = f_{BM}(1-y) between meson and baryon distribution functions. The results constrain the allowed form factor cut-offs for vector mesons, showing that vector meson contributions are marginal for the range of cut-offs consistent with experimental antiquark data, and that including πΔ states would further reduce the maximum allowed Λ.
