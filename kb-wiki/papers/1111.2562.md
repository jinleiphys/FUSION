# Chiral symmetry breaking and the spin content of hadrons

> From the parton distributions in the infinite momentum frame one finds that only about 30% of the nucleon spin is carried by spins of the valence quarks, which gave rise to the term "spin crisis". Similar results hold for the lowest mesons, as it follows from the lattice simulations. We define the spin content of a meson in the rest frame and use a complete and orthogonal $\bar q q$ chiral basis and a unitary transformation from the chiral basis to the (2S+1)LJ basis. Then, given a mixture of different allowed chiral representations in the meson wave function at a given resolution scale, one can obtain its spin content at this scale. To obtain the mixture of the chiral representations in the meson we measure in dynamical lattice simulations a ratio of couplings of interpolarors with different chiral structure. For the rho meson we obtain practically the 3S1 state with no trace of the spin crisis. Then a natural question arises: which definition does reflect the spin content of a hadron?

# Chiral symmetry breaking and the spin content of hadrons

## Key claim

The paper argues that the "spin crisis" (only about 30% of nucleon spin carried by valence quark spins) may be an artifact of the infinite-momentum-frame definition; when the spin content of a meson is defined in the rest frame using a chiral basis and unitary transformation to the angular-momentum basis, the rho meson appears as practically a pure 3S1 state with no trace of the spin crisis. The authors explicitly raise the question of which definition reflects the true spin content of a hadron.

## Method

The authors define the spin content of a meson in the rest frame using a complete and orthogonal $\bar q q$ chiral basis and a unitary transformation from that basis to the (2S+1)LJ basis. They measure on dynamical lattice simulations (using Chirally Improved fermions and Luscher-Weisz gauge action on a 16^3 x 32 lattice with two mass-degenerate light quarks) the ratio of couplings of vector and pseudotensor interpolators to the rho and rho' mesons at different resolution scales, achieved by gauge-invariant Gaussian smearing of quark fields with radii between 0.33 fm and 0.85 fm.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of vector to pseudotensor couplings for the ground state rho meson yields an angular momentum content of approximately $0.99\,|^3S_1\rangle - 0.1\,|^3D_1\rangle$ in the rest frame.
- For the excited rho' meson, the lower bound on the angular momentum content is $0.88\,|^3S_1\rangle - 0.48\,|^3D_1\rangle$ and the upper bound is $0.97\,|^3S_1\rangle - 0.25\,|^3D_1\rangle$.
- The lattice spacing is approximately 0.15 fm, with a spatial volume of approximately 2.4^3 fm^3.
- Pion masses in the simulations range from 296(6) MeV (set B2) to 526(7) MeV (set A); rho masses range from 795(15) MeV (set C) to 911(11) MeV (set A).
- Smearing radii used: narrow 0.33-0.36 fm, wide 0.66-0.69 fm, ultrawide 0.81-0.85 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous method (Glozman et al., 2009-2011) for defining and measuring the spin content of mesons in the rest frame at different resolution scales, building on the chiral basis classification of quark-antiquark states by Cohen and Ji (1990s) and Glozman and Nefediev (2007). The results contradict the interpretation of the "spin crisis" derived from parton distributions in the infinite momentum frame, as seen in deep inelastic scattering experiments (European Muon Collaboration, 1988) and in lattice simulations reviewed by Hagler (2010). The method enables future study of how chiral symmetry breaking and confinement generate hadron spin and mass at infrared scales.
