# Clusterization aspects in the states of non-rotating and fast rotating nuclei

> The understanding of clustering aspects at the ground state of nuclei and in fast rotating ones within the framework of covariant density functional theory has been reviewed and reanalyzed. The appearance of many exotic nuclear shapes in nuclear chart can be inferred from combined analysis of nodal structure of the densities of the single-particle states and the evolution of such states in the Nilsson diagram with deformation and particle number. Such analysis which is supported by fully self-consistent calculations allows to predict the existence of nuclear configurations with specific shape or cluster properties at ground state and at high spin. For example, it indicates that in a given shell with principal quantum number $N$ only the lowest in energy two-fold degenerate deformed state can contribute to the formation of linear chain $α$ cluster structures.

# Clusterization aspects in the states of non-rotating and fast rotating nuclei

## Key claim

The appearance of many exotic nuclear shapes can be inferred from combined analysis of nodal structure of single-particle densities and the evolution of single-particle states in the Nilsson diagram with deformation and particle number, but only the lowest in energy two-fold degenerate deformed state in a given N-shell can contribute to the formation of linear chain alpha cluster structures; this analysis is supported by fully self-consistent cranked relativistic mean field calculations.

## Method

The author reviews and reanalyzes results obtained within the framework of cranked relativistic mean field theory, focusing on reflection-symmetric shapes with extreme deformation. The analysis examines the nodal structure of single-particle density distributions at spherical and extremely deformed shapes, and correlates these with the evolution of single-particle states in the Nilsson diagram as a function of quadrupole deformation and particle number. The study considers both non-rotating and fast rotating nuclei, with particular attention to how collective rotation affects clusterization features.

## Key numbers

- In a given N-shell, cluster forming single-particle states represent 100%, 33%, 15%, 10%, and 6.5% of all single-particle states in the N=0, 1, 2, 3, and 4 shells, respectively.
- The configuration confA(16O) in 16O is the lowest in energy at extreme deformation beta_2 = 1.2 ± 0.4.
- The ground state rotational band of 20Ne terminates at I=8+.
- Extremely deformed structures at spin zero are located at excitation energies of 15 to 40 MeV.
- The 14Ne rotational band becomes proton bound at high spin, with low density proton clusters at approximately rho_pi ≈ 0.01 fm^-3 centered at around z ≈ ±7 fm.

## Context

This work extends and reanalyzes results from cranked relativistic mean field theory studies by the author and collaborators (Afanasjev 2018, Raduta and Afanasjev 2016, and Afanasjev et al. 2024). It builds upon experimental observation of a rotational band in 14C built on a linear chain of three alpha particles plus two neutrons (C14-lin-chain 2023) and ab-initio investigations of alpha clusterization in 8Be and 12C (Otsuka 2022). The analysis enables prediction of specific nuclear configurations with cluster properties at ground state and high spin, and suggests that collective rotation can bring extremely deformed structures from high excitation energies at zero spin down to the yrast line at high spin, potentially making experimental observation feasible.
