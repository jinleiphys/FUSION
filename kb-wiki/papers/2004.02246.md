# Secondary $γ$-ray decays from the partial-wave $T$ matrix with an $R$-matrix application to ${}^{15}{\rm N}(p,α_1γ){}^{12}{\rm C}$

> The secondary $γ$ rays emitted following a nuclear reaction are often relatively straightforward to detect experimentally. Despite the large volume of such data, a practical formalism for describing these $γ$ rays in terms of partial-wave $T$-matrix elements has never been given. The partial-wave formalism is applicable when $R$-matrix methods are used to describe the reaction in question. This paper supplies the needed framework, and it is demonstrated by the application to the ${}^{15}{\rm N}(p,α_1γ){}^{12}{\rm C}$ reaction.

## Key claim

This paper provides the first practical partial-wave formalism for calculating secondary γ-ray angular distributions from nuclear reactions, enabling R-matrix codes to directly analyze such data; the formalism assumes the reaction is sequential (A(a,b)B followed by B→C+γ) and that photon emission from the alternative order of emission is negligible.

## Method

The authors derive the general particle-γ angular correlation function in terms of partial-wave T-matrix elements, starting from the polarization tensor formalism of Satchler (1983). They specialize the results to two common experimental scenarios: the differential cross section for γ-ray emission when outgoing particles are undetected, and the correlation function when γ-rays are detected at 0° relative to the beam direction. The γ-ray angular distribution calculation is implemented in the R-matrix code AZURE2 and applied to the ¹⁵N(p,α₁γ)¹²C reaction.

## Key numbers

- The γ-ray transition from the first excited state to ground state of ¹²C is a pure E2 transition (2⁺→0⁺), contributing only to k=0, 2, and 4 terms in the angular distribution expansion.
- The formalism is demonstrated for the ¹⁵N(p,α₁γ)¹²C reaction in the vicinity of 1-MeV proton energy, using data from Bray et al. (1977).
- The R-matrix parameters from deBoer et al. (2017) provide an excellent description of the b₂ and b₄ angular distribution coefficients, despite those coefficients not being included in the original fit.

## Context

This work extends the R-matrix formalism of Lane and Thomas (1958) and the angular correlation framework of Satchler (1983) to secondary γ-ray decays, which had not previously been given a practical partial-wave treatment. It enables direct analysis of secondary γ-ray data in R-matrix codes like AZURE2, complementing prior work by Bray et al. (1977) who performed R-matrix fits to ¹⁵N(p,α₁γ)¹²C data without publishing their formalism. The authors anticipate applications to additional reactions, with measurements already performed at the University of Notre Dame extending to higher energies.
