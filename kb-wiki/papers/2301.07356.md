# Core-corona approach to describe hyperon global polarization in semi-central relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We report on the core-corona model developed to describe the main features of hyperon global polarization in semicentral relativistic heavy-ion collisions as a function of the collision energy. We first neglect the contribution to polarization from hyperons produced in the corona. In this scenario, the global polarization turns out to be described by a delicate balance between the vorticity-to-spin transferring reactions in the core and the predominance of corona over core matter at low energies. We show how this last feature provides a key ingredient missing in our original model that helps to better describe the excitation function for $Λ$ and $\overlineΛ$ global polarization. To improve the description, we then introduce the contribution to the global polarization coming from the transverse polarization of $Λ$s produced in the corona, which is hereby assumed to be similar to the well-known polarization produced in p + p reactions. The results show a small positive contribution to the global polarization, however they are not yet conclusive due to the small size of the MC sample used in the analysis.

## Key claim

The core-corona model can describe the main features of hyperon global polarization as a function of collision energy, but only when including the previously neglected contribution from hyperons produced in the corona; the results for this corona contribution are not yet conclusive due to the small size of the Monte Carlo sample used.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD event generator with a hybrid fluid-dynamical evolution (SHASTA algorithm) to simulate Bi + Bi collisions at √sNN = 9.2 GeV, separating particles into a high-density core and a low-density corona. They assigned a transverse polarization of 40% to Λ hyperons from the corona, oriented perpendicular to the production plane, and projected this polarization onto the global angular momentum direction to estimate its contribution to global polarization. The MpdRoot framework was used to reconstruct Λ decay products and propagate the polarization assignment.

## Key numbers

- Transverse polarization assigned to Λs from the corona: 40%, based on measured values in p + p collisions at √s = 53 GeV (−0.34 ± 0.07) and √s = 62 GeV (−0.40 ± 0.20)
- Average global polarization contribution from corona Λs: approximately 0.25%, obtained from a Monte Carlo sample of 150,000 Bi + Bi events at √sNN = 9.2 GeV
- Decay asymmetry parameter α used: 0.732

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous core-corona model (Ayala 2020, Ayala 2021) that described Λ and anti-Λ global polarization but assumed corona-produced hyperons were unpolarized. The model had limitations for smaller systems like Ag + Ag at √sNN = 2.55 GeV and for Au + Au at √sNN = 3 GeV at centralities above 40% (Ayala 2022). The approach enables future measurements with the MPD experiment at NICA and connects to experimental results from STAR (2017, 2021) and HADES (2022) showing that global polarization increases with decreasing collision energy.
