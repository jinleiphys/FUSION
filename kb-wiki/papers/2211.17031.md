# Chiral perturbation theory

> Chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) is an effective field theory that describes the properties of strongly-interacting systems at energies far below typical hadron masses. The degrees of freedom are hadrons instead of the underlying quarks and gluons. ChPT is a systematic and model-independent approximation method based on an expansion of amplitudes in terms of light-quark masses and momenta. The following is a brief overview of ChPT that is largely based on Scherer, Schindler, Lect. Notes Phys. 830 (2012), which can be referred to for a more detailed introduction.

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) is a systematic and model-independent effective field theory that approximates low-energy QCD by expanding amplitudes in powers of light-quark masses and momenta, but its validity is restricted to energies far below the chiral symmetry breaking scale \(\Lambda_\chi = 4\pi F_0 \approx 1\) GeV, and the expansion is only meaningful when external momenta and Goldstone-boson masses are small compared to that scale.

## Method

The authors construct the most general effective Lagrangian for Goldstone bosons (pions, kaons, eta) consistent with the chiral symmetry of QCD, organized in a simultaneous derivative and quark-mass expansion (chiral dimension). The lowest-order Lagrangian \({\cal L}_2\) contains two parameters \(F_0\) and \(B_0\); higher-order Lagrangians (e.g., \({\cal L}_4\) with twelve low-energy constants \(L_1,\ldots,L_{10},H_1,H_2\)) are included to absorb ultraviolet divergences from loop diagrams. For baryons, nucleon fields are introduced as an SU(2) doublet with a nonlinear transformation law under chiral rotations, and the Lagrangian is constructed analogously with a covariant derivative containing the connection \(\Gamma_\mu\).

## Key numbers

*   The chiral symmetry breaking scale is \(\Lambda_\chi = 4\pi F_0 \approx 1\) GeV (Manohar, 1983).
*   Lowest-order (LO) predictions for the pion-decay constant in the chiral limit: \(F_0\) is defined via \(\langle 0|J^\mu_{Aa}(x)|\phi_b(p)\rangle = ip^\mu F_0 \exp(-ip\cdot x)\delta_{ab}\).
*   LO Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relations: \(M_{\pi,2}^2 = 2B_0\hat m\), \(M_{K,2}^2 = B_0(\hat m+m_s)\), \(M_{\eta,2}^2 = \frac{2}{3}B_0(\hat m+2m_s)\).
*   Using empirical masses \(M_\pi=135\) MeV, \(M_K=496\) MeV, \(M_\eta=548\) MeV gives quark-mass ratios \(m_s/\hat m = 25.9\) (from kaon) and \(m_s/\hat m = 24.3\) (from eta).
*   LO \(\pi\pi\)-scattering lengths: \(a_0^0 = 7 M_\pi^2/(32\pi F_\pi^2) = 0.160\), \(a_0^2 = -M_\pi^2/(16\pi F_\pi^2) = -0.0456\), using \(F_\pi=92.2\) MeV and \(M_\pi=M_{\pi^+}=139.57\) MeV.
*   The scalar singlet quark condensate is related to \(B_0\) by \(3F_0^2 B_0 = -\langle\bar q q\rangle_0\).
*   The Wess-Zumino-Witten action at leading order contains a factor \(N_c = 3\) and yields a parameter-free prediction for processes like \(K^+K^-\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0\).

## Context

This paper is a pedagogical overview of chiral perturbation theory, largely based on the textbook by Scherer and Schindler (2012). It extends the foundational work of Gasser and Leutwyler (1984, 1985) who constructed the \({\cal O}(q^4)\) mesonic Lagrangian and established the modern framework, and of Gasser, Sainio, and Švarc (1988) who extended ChPT to the baryon sector. The review enables practitioners to apply ChPT to low-energy hadronic processes, including meson-meson scattering, meson-baryon interactions, and decays involving the chiral anomaly, and it provides the basis for systematic inclusion of loop corrections and higher-order terms.
