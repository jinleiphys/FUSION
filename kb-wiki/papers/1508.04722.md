# Strangeness in nuclei and neutron stars: a challenging puzzle

> The prediction of neutron stars properties is strictly connected to the employed nuclear interactions. The appearance of hyperons in the inner core of the star is strongly dependent on the details of the underlying hypernuclear force. We summarize our recent quantum Monte Carlo results on the development of realistic two- and three-body hyperon-nucleon interactions based on the available experimental data for light- and medium-heavy hypernuclei.

## Key claim

The appearance of hyperons in neutron star interiors is strongly dependent on the details of the hypernuclear force, and parametrizations that predict relatively small differences in hyperon separation energies of hypernuclei give very different results for infinite matter, with the equation of state spanning the entire regime from substantial hyperon fraction at ~2ρ0 to the complete absence of Λ particles across all stellar densities.

## Method

The authors employed auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations to develop phenomenological two-body ΛN and three-body ΛNN interactions. The ΛN two-body component was fit to existing Λp scattering data, while the ΛNN three-body sector was re-fit to available Λ separation energies of single-Λ hypernuclei across a wide mass range. The resulting interactions were then used to compute hypernuclear ground state properties and extrapolated to infinite neutron matter with strangeness.

## Key numbers

- Central densities of neutron stars can reach several times nuclear saturation density ρ0 = 0.16 fm⁻³
- At densities larger than ~2ρ0, conversion of nucleons into hyperons might become energetically favorable
- Maximum neutron star mass constraints come from millisecond pulsars PSR J1614-2230 (~2 M⊙) and PSR J0348+0432 (~2 M⊙)
- For ³ΛH (1/2⁺): computed BΛ = 0.23(9) MeV, experimental BΛ = 0.13(5) MeV
- For ⁴ΛH (0⁺): computed BΛ = 1.95(9) MeV, experimental BΛ = 2.04(4) MeV
- For ⁴ΛHe (0⁺): computed BΛ = 2.37(9) MeV, experimental BΛ = 2.39(3) MeV
- Charge symmetry breaking energy splitting ΔBΛ for A=4 hypernuclei: computed 0.42(11) MeV, experimental 0.35(6) MeV
- For ⁵ΛHe with Minnesota NN + ΛN: AFDMC total energy E = -37.69(8) MeV, BΛ = 6.95(9) MeV; NSHH total energy E = -37.77(10) MeV, BΛ = 6.99(10) MeV
- For ⁵ΛHe with AV4′ NN + ΛN: AFDMC total energy E = -39.46(12) MeV, BΛ = 6.70(16) MeV; NSHH total energy E = -39.54(10) MeV, BΛ = 6.84(10) MeV
- ³Λn is predicted to be unbound

## Context

This work extends the hyperon puzzle problem, where theoretical predictions for neutron star maximum masses violate the ~2 M⊙ constraints from pulsar observations (Demorest 2010; Antoniadis 2013). The authors build on prior quantum Monte Carlo studies of hypernuclei (Lonardoni 2013, 2014, 2015; Pederiva 2015) and benchmark their AFDMC method against nonsymmetrized hyperspherical harmonics (HSHH) calculations (Deflorian 2013). The results enable future systematic experimental investigation of hypernuclear properties across a wide mass range to resolve ambiguities in the three-body hyperon-nucleon force and reconcile theoretical neutron star predictions with astrophysical observations.
