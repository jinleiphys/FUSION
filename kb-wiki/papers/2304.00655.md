# Semiclassical origin of nuclear ground-state octupole deformations

> Background: Ground-state octupole deformations are suggested in nuclei located in the north-east neighbor of the doubly magic nuclei on the nuclear chart (N,Z), such as those in Ba and Ra-Th regions. This systematics has been attributed to the parity mixing of the approximately degenerate Delta l=3 pair of single-particle levels near the Fermi surface. Purpose: Nuclear deformations are governed in most cases by the gross shell structures of the single-particle spectra. I will consider the systematics in octupole deformation from the view point of the gross shell structure, and investigate the mechanism of its manifestation using the semiclassical periodic-orbit theory (POT), which describes the quantum shell effect by means of the periodic orbits (POs) in the corresponding classical system. Methods: To focus on the role of deformation, simplified infinite-well (cavity) and radial power-law potential models are employed taking account of quadrupole and octupole shape degrees of freedom. Nuclear ground-state deformations are investigated over the nuclear chart, and the properties of the deformed shell structures are analyzed by means of the semiclassical POT. Results and conclusions: The systematics in nuclear ground-state octupole deformations are reproduced in simplified mean-field potential models either with or without parity mixing between Delta l=3 pair of levels. The strong octupole deformed shell effect at above the spherical shell closures are explained simply and clearly using the semiclassical POT. They are associated with the local restoration of dynamical symmetry, which enhance the contribution of classical POs to the gross shell effect.

# Semiclassical origin of nuclear ground-state octupole deformations

## Key claim

The systematic appearance of ground-state octupole deformations in nuclei just above spherical closed-shell configurations can be explained by the gross shell structure alone, using semiclassical periodic-orbit theory, without requiring the traditional mechanism of parity mixing between Delta l=3 pairs of single-particle levels. The strong octupole deformed shell effect is associated with the local restoration of dynamical symmetry, which enhances the contribution of classical periodic orbits to the gross shell effect.

## Method

The author employs simplified infinite-well (cavity) and radial power-law potential models with axially symmetric quadrupole and octupole shape degrees of freedom, using two different shape parametrizations: a stretched octupole parametrization based on spherical harmonic expansion with exponential factors, and a spheroid-paraboloid merging parametrization. Single-particle spectra are computed quantum mechanically for these potentials over the nuclear chart, and ground-state deformations are obtained by minimizing the total energy. The semiclassical periodic-orbit theory (POT) trace formula is then used to analyze the deformed shell structures by evaluating contributions of classical periodic orbits, particularly focusing on orbit bifurcations and local symmetry restoration at the equatorial plane.

## Key numbers

- Spherical magic numbers in the cavity model: 8, 20, 34, 58, 92, 138 (close to real nuclear magic numbers but slightly deviating due to absence of spin-orbit coupling)
- Doubly magic nuclei in the cavity model correspond to (Z,N) = (58,92) for Sn-132 and (92,138) for Pb-208
- Contour intervals for shell-deformation energy surfaces are drawn at 2 MeV intervals
- Fermi wave number is approximately independent of particle number: k_F = (9pi/2)^(1/3) r_0^(-1)
- The condition for shell energy minimization associated with a specific periodic orbit is cos(k_F L_PO - pi mu_PO/2) = -1

## Context

This work extends the author's previous analysis (Arita 2023) using the infinite-well cavity model with a sphere-paraboloid parametrization, and generalizes it to arbitrary combinations of axially symmetric quadrupole and octupole deformations. It builds on earlier studies of bifurcations of equatorial orbits in cavity and oscillator-type potential models (Arita 1995, Sugita 1997), which showed that such bifurcations at certain combinations of quadrupole and octupole deformations provide remarkable shell effects. The results contradict the traditional attribution of octupole deformations solely to parity mixing of Delta l=3 pairs near the Fermi surface (Butler 1996), by demonstrating that the systematics is reproduced even in oscillator-type potentials without such level pairs. The work enables a semiclassical understanding of why octupole deformations appear systematically in the Ba and Ra-Th regions, and may inform studies of fission-fragment mass distributions and cluster radioactivity where octupole shape degrees of freedom play a role (Scamps 2018, Scamps 2019, Warda 2012, Warda 2018).
