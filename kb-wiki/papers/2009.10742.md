# Evolution of Non-Gaussian Hydrodynamic Fluctuations

> In the context of the search for the QCD critical point using non-Gaussian fluctuations, we obtain the evolution equations for non-Gaussian cumulants to the leading order of the systematic expansion in the magnitude of thermal fluctuations. We develop a diagrammatic technique in which the leading order contributions are given by tree diagrams. We introduce a Wigner transform for multipoint correlators and derive the evolution equations for three- and four-point Wigner functions for the problem of nonlinear stochastic diffusion with multiplicative noise.

## Key claim

The authors derive evolution equations for non-Gaussian cumulants (three- and four-point Wigner functions) in stochastic nonlinear diffusion with multiplicative noise, to leading order in a systematic expansion controlled by the small parameter \((q\xi)^3\) (the ratio of fluctuation wavelength to correlation length), which is the regime where hydrodynamic fluctuation theory applies even near the QCD critical point.

## Method

The authors start from a general multivariable Langevin system with multiplicative noise, define the process via the Fokker-Planck equation using the equilibrium distribution \(S = \log P_{\rm eq}\) and the Onsager matrix \(M\), and develop a perturbative expansion in the magnitude of fluctuations parameterized by \(\varepsilon\). They apply this formalism to stochastic nonlinear diffusion of a conserved density, using a dictionary to map the discrete variables to continuous fields, and introduce a symmetric generalization of the Wigner transform for multipoint correlation functions. The leading-order evolution equations for the two-, three-, and four-point Wigner functions are obtained and represented diagrammatically as tree diagrams.

## Key numbers

* The expansion parameter controlling the hierarchy is \(\varepsilon \sim (q\xi)^3\), where \(q\) is the fluctuation wave number and \(\xi\) is the correlation length.
* The scaling of cumulants with the expansion parameter is \(G^c_n \sim \varepsilon^{n-1}\) (e.g., \(G^c_3 \sim \varepsilon^2\), \(G^c_4 \sim \varepsilon^3\)).
* The relaxation rate of Wigner functions is proportional to the square of the wave number, \(\gamma q^2\), where \(\gamma = \lambda \alpha'\) (conductivity times derivative of chemical potential with respect to density).
* The equilibrium two-point Wigner function is \(W_2^{\rm eq} = 1/\alpha'\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism for two-point correlation functions in stochastic hydrodynamics developed by Akamatsu et al. (2017, 2018) and An et al. (2019) to non-Gaussian (three- and four-point) cumulants, which are essential for the experimental search for the QCD critical point via event-by-event fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions. The equations generalize the approach of Mukherjee et al. (2015) by including terms from multiplicative noise (derivatives of conductivity \(\lambda\)), which are important near the liquid-gas-type critical point where \(\lambda\) diverges as noted by Hohenberg (1977). The results enable quantitative modeling of the evolution of non-Gaussian fluctuation measures in expanding heavy-ion collision systems, accounting for memory effects and wave-number-dependent relaxation.
