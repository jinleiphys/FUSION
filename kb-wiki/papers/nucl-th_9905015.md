# Scalar meson exchange and the baryon spectra

> We explore the role of a scalar meson exchange interaction between quarks in a semirelativistic constituent quark model where the quarks are also subject to a linear confinement. We search for a variational solution and study how the spectrum evolves when the strength of the scalar meson exchange increases. Our results have good implications on the description of the low-lying baryon masses, especially on the long-standing problem of the relative position of the first positive-parity (Roper resonance) and the first orbitally excited states.

## Key claim

In a semirelativistic constituent quark model with linear confinement, adding an explicit scalar (sigma) meson exchange interaction can reverse the ordering of the first radially excited state (Roper resonance) and the first orbitally excited state, but only if the coupling constant exceeds a threshold near g_sigma^2/(4pi) = 0.20; below this value the ordering remains incorrect.

## Method

The authors solved a semirelativistic three-quark Hamiltonian variationally, using a linear confinement potential with string tension sqrt(sigma) = 1 GeV/fm and a sigma-exchange potential regularized via the Pauli-Villars method with sigma mass mu_sigma = 600 MeV. They treated the coupling constant g_sigma^2/(4pi) and the regularization parameter Lambda as variable parameters, and computed the energies of the ground state (1s), first orbitally excited state (1p), and first radially excited state (2s) for a three-quark system.

## Key numbers

* For Lambda -> infinity, the energy difference E_2s - E_1p becomes negative (correct ordering) for g_sigma^2/(4pi) greater than or approximately equal to 0.20; at g_sigma^2/(4pi) = 0.20 the energies are E_1p = 1431 MeV and E_2s = 1446 MeV, giving R = 0.970.
* At zero coupling (g_sigma^2/(4pi) = 0.00), the energies are E_1p = 1287 MeV and E_2s = 1444 MeV, giving R = 0.688 (incorrect ordering).
* For a finite cutoff Lambda = 2 GeV, the coupling constant was varied up to 1.34, twice the pion-quark coupling constant value of 0.67.
* The string tension was fixed at sqrt(sigma) = 1 GeV/fm and the sigma meson mass at mu_sigma = 600 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Goldstone boson exchange (GBE) models of Glozman, Riska, Plessas and collaborators (1996-1998), which used only pseudoscalar meson exchange and achieved correct level ordering through flavour-spin operators. The authors argue that the chiral partner of the pion, the sigma meson, should be included explicitly rather than mocked up in parameters, and that a scalar attractive interaction cooperates with the flavour-spin operator. The results are consistent with the two-pion exchange analysis of Riska and Brown (1999), and the next step is to study a potential with both scalar and pseudoscalar terms.
