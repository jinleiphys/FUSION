# Probing Nucleons and Nuclei in High Energy Collisions

> This volume is a collection of contributions for the 7-week program "Probing Nucleons and Nuclei in High Energy Collisions" that was held at the Institute for Nuclear Theory in Seattle, WA, USA, from October 1 until November 16, 2018. The program was dedicated to the physics of the Electron Ion Collider (EIC), the world's first polarized electron-nucleon (ep) and electron-nucleus (eA) collider to be constructed in the USA. These proceedings are organized by chapters, corresponding to the weeks of the program: Week I, Generalized parton distributions; Week II, Transverse spin and TMDs; Week III, Longitudinal spin; Week IV, Symposium week; Weeks V & VI, eA collisions; Week VII, pA and AA collisions. We hope these proceedings will be useful to readers as a compilation of EIC-related science at the end of the second decade of the XXI century.

## Key claim

The proton mass can be understood via the QCD trace anomaly, and near-threshold exclusive photo-production of J/psi in lepton-proton scattering provides a promising experimental avenue to access the gluon condensate contribution to the proton mass, though the extrapolation to t=0 requires knowledge of gravitational form factors that cannot yet be derived from QCD factorization and must rely on nonperturbative models such as holographic AdS/CFT.

## Method

The authors relate the physical amplitude for gamma(*) P -> J/psi P' to the forward amplitude J/psi P -> J/psi P via vector dominance, then express the nonforward matrix element of the renormalized gluon condensate F^2_R in terms of gluon gravitational form factors (A_g, B_g, C_g, bar-C_g) using perturbative coefficients K_q and K_g calculated up to three loops in the MSbar scheme. A holographic model based on AdS/CFT correspondence is employed to connect the scattering amplitude to these gravitational form factors, with the graviton exchange dual to the energy-momentum tensor and the dilaton exchange dual to the F^2 operator.

## Key numbers

- The momentum transfer near threshold sqrt(-t) is approximately 1.5 GeV, on the order of the charm quark mass
- The threshold photon energy in the proton rest frame is E_gamma = 8.2 GeV
- The parameter b is defined as b = <P| m(1+gamma_m)(bar-psi psi)_R |P> / (2M^2), with 1-b = <P| (beta/(2g)) (F^2)_R |P> / (2M^2); the empirical value from the nucleon sigma term is b ~ 0.1
- The holographic model fit to GlueX data shows better agreement for b=0 (proton mass entirely from gluon condensate) than for b=1 (proton mass entirely from quark condensate)

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal by Kharzeev, Satz, Syamtomov and Zinovjev (1999) to use near-threshold J/psi photo-production to access the gluon condensate, and addresses the t-dependence issue raised by Frankfurt and Strikman (2002) regarding two-gluon form factors. The holographic approach builds on the AdS/CFT correspondence and provides a framework for interpreting GlueX collaboration data from Jefferson Lab. The results suggest that the EIC, as well as RHIC ultraperipheral collisions and the lower-energy EIcC in China, could further probe the proton mass decomposition, with future first-principle QCD factorization calculations still needed.
