# Dispersion relation for hadronic light-by-light scattering: pion pole

> The pion-pole contribution to hadronic light-by-light scattering in the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon $(g-2)_μ$ is fully determined by the doubly-virtual pion transition form factor. Although this crucial input quantity is, in principle, directly accessible in experiment, a complete measurement covering all kinematic regions relevant for $(g-2)_μ$ is not realistic in the foreseeable future. Here, we report in detail on a reconstruction from available data, both space- and time-like, using a dispersive representation that accounts for all the low-lying singularities, reproduces the correct high- and low-energy limits, and proves convenient for the evaluation of the $(g-2)_μ$ loop integral. We concentrate on the systematics of the fit to $e^+e^-\to 3π$ data, which are key in constraining the isoscalar dependence, as well as the matching to the asymptotic limits. In particular, we provide a detailed account of the pion transition form factor at low energies in the time- and space-like region, including the error estimates underlying our final result for the pion-pole contribution, $a_μ^{π^0\text{-pole}}=62.6^{+3.0}_{-2.5}\times 10^{-11}$, and demonstrate how forthcoming singly-virtual measurements will further reduce its uncertainty.

## Key claim
The pion-pole contribution to hadronic light-by-light scattering in the muon anomalous magnetic moment is fully determined by the doubly-virtual pion transition form factor, which the authors reconstruct from available space- and time-like data using a dispersive representation that accounts for low-lying singularities and reproduces correct high- and low-energy limits, but the reconstruction depends on fits to \(e^+e^-\to 3\pi\) data and matching to asymptotic limits, with the final result carrying an uncertainty of \(62.6^{+3.0}_{-2.5}\times 10^{-11}\).

## Method
The authors reconstruct the doubly-virtual pion transition form factor using a dispersive representation that separates the form factor into a dispersive part accounting for low-energy intermediate states, a small effective part from higher intermediate states, and an asymptotic term implementing perturbative QCD constraints at order \(1/Q^2\). The isoscalar dependence is constrained by fitting the \(e^+e^-\to 3\pi\) cross section data up to \(1.8\ \text{GeV}\) using a parameterization based on Khuri–Treiman equations for the \(\gamma^*_s\to 3\pi\) amplitude, with the subtraction function \(a(q^2)\) modeled by a once-subtracted dispersion integral over Breit–Wigner resonances (\(\omega\), \(\phi\), \(\omega'(1420)\), \(\omega''(1650)\)) plus a conformal polynomial to account for inelastic channels. The pion-pole contribution to \(a_\mu\) is then evaluated via a three-dimensional integral representation of the two-loop master formula using the reconstructed form factor.

## Key numbers
- \(a_\mu^{\pi^0\text{-pole}} = 62.6^{+3.0}_{-2.5} \times 10^{-11}\) (final result for the pion-pole contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment)
- \(F_{\pi^0\gamma^*\gamma^*}(0,0) = 1/(4\pi^2 F_\pi)\) with \(F_\pi = 92.28(9)\ \text{MeV}\) (chiral anomaly normalization of the pion transition form factor, tested to \(1.4\%\) in Primakoff measurement of \(\pi^0\to\gamma\gamma\) decay width)
- \(\alpha_A = F_{3\pi}/3 \times 1.066(10)\) (subtraction constant for the \(\gamma^*_s\to 3\pi\) amplitude, fixed by the chiral anomaly with quark-mass renormalization correction)
- Integration cutoff for the isoscalar part: \(s_\text{is} = (1.8\ \text{GeV})^2\) (energy up to which the \(e^+e^-\to 3\pi\) description based on Khuri–Treiman equations is applied)
- Inelastic threshold for the conformal polynomial: \(s_\text{inel} = 1\ \text{GeV}^2\) (motivated by the nearby \(K\bar K\) threshold)

## Context
This work extends the dispersive framework for hadronic light-by-light scattering developed by Colangelo et al. (2014, 2015) and Hoferichter et al. (2013, 2014, 2017), providing a detailed data-driven determination of the pion-pole contribution that improves upon earlier model-dependent estimates (e.g., Knecht et al. 2001, Melnikov and Vainshtein 2003, Nyffeler 2016) and lattice QCD calculations (Gerardin et al. 2016). The dispersive reconstruction of the pion transition form factor from \(e^+e^-\to 3\pi\) data and space-like measurements enables a model-independent evaluation of the dominant pion-pole contribution with controlled uncertainties, and the resulting form factor representation will also be valuable for improved predictions of \(\pi^0\to e^+e^-\) decay.
