# Asymmetry of the neutrino mean free path in hot neutron matter under strong magnetic fields

> The neutrino mean free path in neutron matter under a strong magnetic field is evaluated for the inelastic scattering reaction and studied as a function of the neutron matter density in the range $0.05 \leq ρ\leq 0.4$ fm$^{-3}$ for several temperatures up to 30 MeV and magnetic field strengths B=0 G, $10^{18}$ G and $2.5\times 10^{18}$ G. Polarized neutron matter is described within the non--relativistic Brueckner--Hartree--Fock (BHF) approach using the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential supplemented with the Urbana IX three-nucleon force. Explicit expressions of the cross section per unit volume for the scattering of a neutrino with a spin up or spin down neutron are derived from the Fermi Golden rule. Our results show that the mean free path depends strongly on the angle of the incoming neutrino, leading to an asymmetry in this quantity. This asymmetry depends on the magnetic field intensity and on the density, but it is rather independent of the temperature. For a density of $0.16$ fm$^{-3}$ at a temperature T$=30$MeV, the asymmetry in the mean free path is found to be of $\sim 15\%$ for B=$10^{18}$G and $\sim 38\%$ for B=$2.5 \times 10^{18}$G.

## Key claim

The neutrino mean free path in hot neutron matter under strong magnetic fields exhibits an asymmetry that depends on the angle of the incoming neutrino relative to the magnetic field direction; this asymmetry is strongly dependent on magnetic field intensity and density but is rather independent of temperature, contrary to what the abstract's focus on the existence of asymmetry alone might suggest.

## Method

The authors evaluated the neutrino mean free path for inelastic scattering in hot neutron matter using the non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach with the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential and the Urbana IX three-nucleon force. They derived explicit expressions for the neutrino cross section per unit volume for scattering with spin-up and spin-down neutrons from the Fermi Golden rule, incorporating spin projection operators to account for the polarization induced by the magnetic field. The structure function describing the response of neutron matter was computed using a quadratic approximation for the neutron single-particle energies.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino mean free path asymmetry of ~15% for B = 10^18 G and ~38% for B = 2.5 x 10^18 G at density ρ = 0.16 fm^-3 and temperature T = 30 MeV
- Density range studied: 0.05 ≤ ρ ≤ 0.4 fm^-3
- Temperatures studied: up to 30 MeV
- Magnetic field strengths: B = 0 G, 10^18 G, and 2.5 x 10^18 G
- Neutrino mean free path varies by up to four orders of magnitude with temperature (from T = 3 MeV to T = 30 MeV) for fixed neutrino momentum |p_ν| = 3T
- At T = 3 MeV and θ_ν = π/2, appreciable differences between B = 0 G and B = 2.5 x 10^18 G are noticed only at densities below ~0.15 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutrino mean free path in dense matter without magnetic fields (Tubbs, Sawyer, Iwamoto, Backman, Haensel, Reddy, and others) and earlier work on neutrino behavior under strong magnetic fields (Bezchastnov, Kusenko, Horowitz, and others) by focusing specifically on the asymmetry in neutrino emission induced by the magnetic field. The results enable more realistic modeling of neutrino transport in magnetars and could inform studies of the pulsar kick problem, where an asymmetry as small as ~1% in neutrino emission has been suggested as a possible mechanism (Sagert, 2008).
