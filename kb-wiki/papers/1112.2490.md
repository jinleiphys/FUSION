# SU(3) centre vortices underpin confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking

> The mass function of the nonperturbative quark propagator in SU(3) gauge theory shows only a weak dependence on the vortex content of the gauge configurations. Of particular note is the survival of dynamical mass generation on vortex-free configurations having a vanishing string tension. This admits the possibility that mass generation associated with dynamical chiral symmetry breaking persists without confinement. In this presentation, we examine the low-lying ground-state hadron spectrum of the pi, rho, N and Delta and discover that while dynamical mass generation persists in the vortex-free theory, it is not connected to dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. In this way, centre vortices in SU(3) gauge theory are intimately linked to both confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. We conclude that centre vortices are the essential underlying feature of the QCD vacuum.

## Key claim
Centre vortex removal in SU(3) gauge theory spoils both confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, but the dynamical mass generation that survives in vortex-free configurations is not connected to chiral symmetry; it instead reflects a theory of weakly interacting constituent quarks with no pseudo-Goldstone pion.

## Method
The authors generated 200 SU(3) gauge-field configurations using the Luscher-Weisz mean-field improved action on a 20^3 x 40 lattice with spacing 0.125 fm. They identified centre vortices by gauge-fixing to Maximal Centre Gauge and removed them by multiplying each link by the complex conjugate of its centre-projected phase. They then computed Euclidean two-point correlation functions for the pi, rho, nucleon, and Delta hadrons on both original and vortex-free configurations, extracting effective masses via jackknife analysis with covariance-matrix based chi-squared fitting.

## Key numbers
- Lattice volume: 20^3 x 40, spacing 0.125 fm, ensemble of 200 configurations
- Pion mass on original configurations at kappa=0.1310 (m_q=0.0293 GeV): 0.3564(34) GeV; on vortex-free configurations at same kappa: 0.5204(29) GeV
- Pion mass on vortex-free configurations at kappa=0.1325 (m_q=-0.0389 GeV): 0.3431(43) GeV
- Rho mass on original configurations at kappa=0.1280 (m_q=0.1705 GeV): 1.146(8) GeV; on vortex-free at same kappa: 0.8781(23) GeV
- Nucleon mass on original configurations at kappa=0.1280: 1.720(12) GeV; on vortex-free: 1.3309(43) GeV
- Delta mass on original configurations at kappa=0.1280: 1.896(16) GeV; on vortex-free: 1.3187(48) GeV
- At heaviest quark mass (0.17 GeV), meson mass in vortex-free theory is 0.87 GeV and baryon mass is 1.32 GeV, compared to free-theory expectations of 0.34 GeV and 0.51 GeV respectively
- Mass function of quark propagator on vortex-free configurations shows an upward vertical shift of approximately 60 MeV relative to original configurations

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of centre vortices in SU(2) gauge theory by Bowman (2008), where vortex removal eliminated both confinement and dynamical mass generation, and in SU(3) by Bowman (2010), which found that dynamical mass generation survived vortex removal while string tension vanished. The present hadron spectrum analysis resolves the ambiguity from those propagator studies by showing the surviving mass generation is not chiral. The results enable future investigations using staggered or overlap fermion actions to explore the vortex-free spectrum at vanishing quark mass, and establish centre vortices as the fundamental degrees of freedom underlying both confinement and chiral symmetry breaking in QCD.
