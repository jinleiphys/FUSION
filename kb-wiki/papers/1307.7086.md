# Inhomogeneous holographic thermalization

> The sudden injection of energy in a strongly coupled conformal field theory and its subsequent thermalization can be holographically modeled by a shell falling into anti-de Sitter space and forming a black brane. For a homogeneous shell, Bhattacharyya and Minwalla were able to study this process analytically using a weak field approximation. Motivated by event-by-event fluctuations in heavy ion collisions, we include inhomogeneities in this model, obtaining analytic results in a long wavelength expansion. In the early-time window in which our approximations can be trusted, the resulting evolution matches well with that of a simple free streaming model. Near the end of this time window, we find that the stress tensor approaches that of second-order viscous hydrodynamics. We comment on possible lessons for heavy ion phenomenology.

## Key claim

The paper extends the homogeneous holographic thermalization model of Bhattacharyya and Minwalla (2009) to include spatial inhomogeneities, finding that in a long wavelength expansion valid at early times (short compared to the inverse local temperature), the evolution of the stress tensor matches a free streaming model and later approaches second-order viscous hydrodynamics, with the caveat that the approximations are only reliable in this early-time window.

## Method

The authors consider a massless scalar field minimally coupled to gravity in four-dimensional anti-de Sitter (AdS) spacetime, with the scalar field serving as a boundary source that drives black brane formation. They solve the Einstein-scalar equations perturbatively in two expansions: a weak field expansion in the amplitude epsilon of the scalar source, and a gradient expansion in spatial derivatives along one inhomogeneous direction (the x-coordinate). The bulk solution is constructed up to second order in the source amplitude and up to fourth order in the gradient expansion, from which the boundary stress-energy tensor is extracted and compared to free streaming and hydrodynamic models.

## Key numbers

* The scalar source is turned on for a finite time interval delta t, which should be small compared to the inverse temperature of the eventual black brane.
* The scalar field solution at first order in epsilon and zeroth order in the gradient expansion is phi_{1,0}(v,r,x) = phi_0(v,x) + dot_phi_0(v,x)/r, matching the homogeneous result of Bhattacharyya and Minwalla (2009) when the x-dependence is removed.
* The metric component f at second order in epsilon and zeroth order in gradients is f_{2,0}(v,r,x) = -(dot_phi_0)^2/(8r), with the AdS radius set to one.
* The metric component h at second order in epsilon and zeroth order in gradients is h_{2,0}(v,r,x) = -3(dot_phi_0)^2/4 + C_{2,0}(v,x)/r, where C_{2,0} involves an integral of the source over time.
* The first non-vanishing contribution to the dvdx metric component appears at order mu (first order in gradients) and is k_{2,1}(v,r,x) = (1/2) dot_phi_0 phi_0' + K_{2,1}(v,x)/(6r).

## Context

This work extends the homogeneous holographic thermalization model of Bhattacharyya and Minwalla (2009), which used a weak field approximation to study black brane formation from a scalar source, by introducing spatial inhomogeneities motivated by event-by-event fluctuations in heavy ion collisions. The results enable analytic study of how local density fluctuations affect the approach to hydrodynamic behavior, complementing numerical studies of anisotropic plasmas by Chesler and Yaffe (2009) and Heller et al. (2011), and providing a tractable model for comparing holographic thermalization to free streaming and second-order viscous hydrodynamics. The paper suggests that the early-time evolution of the stress tensor in this inhomogeneous model matches well with a simple free streaming model, with implications for understanding the rapid onset of hydrodynamic behavior in heavy ion collisions.
