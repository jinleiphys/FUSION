# Correlated Dirac Eigenvalues and Axial Anomaly in Chiral Symmetric QCD

> We investigate the Dirac eigenvalue spectrum ($ρ(λ,m_l)$) to study the microscopic origin of axial anomaly in high temperature phase of QCD. We propose novel relations between the derivatives ($\partial^n ρ(λ,m_l)/\partial m_l^n$) of the Dirac eigenvalue spectrum with respect to the quark mass ($m_l$) and the $(n+1)$-point correlations among the eigenvalues ($λ$) of the massless Dirac operator. Based on these relations, we present lattice QCD results for $\partial^n ρ(λ,m_l)/\partial m_l^n$ ($n=1, 2, 3$) with $m_l$ corresponding to pion masses $m_π=160-55$ MeV, and at a temperature of about 1.6 times the chiral phase transition temperature. Calculations were carried out using (2+1)-flavors of highly improved staggered quarks and the tree-level Symanzik gauge action with the physical strange quark mass, three lattice spacings $a=0.12, 0.08, 0.06$ fm, and lattices having aspect ratios $4-9$. We find that $ρ(λ\to0,m_l)$ develops a peaked structure. This peaked structure, which arises due to non-Poisson correlations within the infrared part of the Dirac eigenvalue spectrum, becomes sharper as $a\to0$, and its amplitude is proportional to $m_l^2$. After continuum and chiral extrapolations, we find that the axial anomaly remains manifested in two-point correlation functions of scalar and pseudo-scalar mesons in the chiral limit. We demonstrate that the behavior of $ρ(λ\to0,m_l)$ is responsible for it.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in high-temperature QCD (T ≈ 1.6 Tc), the Dirac eigenvalue spectrum ρ(λ→0, m_l) develops a peaked structure whose amplitude scales as m_l², and after continuum and chiral extrapolations this peak causes the axial anomaly to remain manifested in two-point correlation functions of scalar and pseudo-scalar mesons even in the chiral limit, contradicting the possibility of effective U(1)_A restoration at this temperature.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using (2+1)-flavors of highly improved staggered quarks and the tree-level Symanzik gauge action with physical strange quark mass at T ≈ 205 MeV ≈ 1.6 Tc. They computed the Dirac eigenvalue spectrum ρ(λ, m_l) and its first three derivatives with respect to the light quark mass m_l using the Chebyshev filtering technique with stochastic estimation on about 2000-10000 configurations per parameter set, at three lattice spacings a = 0.12, 0.08, 0.06 fm and pion masses m_π = 160-55 MeV. They derived novel relations connecting ∂ⁿρ/∂m_lⁿ to (n+1)-point eigenvalue correlations C_{n+1}, and used these to reconstruct the U(1)_A-breaking susceptibilities χ_π - χ_δ and χ_disc.

## Key numbers

- Temperature T ≈ 205 MeV ≈ 1.6 Tc, where Tc = 132^{+3}_{-6} MeV from O(4) scaling analyses
- Three lattice spacings: a = 0.12, 0.08, 0.06 fm (corresponding to N_τ = 8, 12, 16)
- Pion masses m_π = 160, 140, 110, 80, 55 MeV (from m_l = m_s/20, m_s/27, m_s/40, m_s/80, m_s/160)
- Aspect ratios N_σ/N_τ = 4-9
- After continuum and chiral extrapolation: χ_disc(0,0) = 3.0 ± 1.1 (sequential fit) and 5.7 ± 2.3 (joint fit), 2-3 σ away from zero
- After continuum and chiral extrapolation: [χ_π - χ_δ](0,0) = 6.7 ± 1.1 (sequential fit) and 7.8 ± 2.2 (joint fit), 4-6 σ away from zero
- χ_disc and χ_π - χ_δ are nonvanishing at a confidence level above 95%

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of the axial anomaly in the high-temperature phase, where previous calculations had conflicting results: some observed infrared enhancement in the Dirac eigenvalue spectrum (Bazavov 2012, Buchoff 2013, Dick 2015) while others did not (Cossu 2013, Tomiya 2016, Suzuki 2020). The paper provides the first controlled continuum and chiral extrapolations showing that ρ(λ→0, m_l) ∝ m_l², consistent with a dilute instanton gas approximation picture (t Hooft 1976). The results suggest that for T ≈ 1.6 Tc, the chiral phase transition in (2+1)-flavor QCD belongs to the three-dimensional O(4) universality class, and the formalism developed for directly accessing ∂ⁿρ/∂m_lⁿ will be essential for future studies using chiral fermions.
