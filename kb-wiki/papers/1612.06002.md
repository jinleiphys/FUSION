# The analytic properties of the quark propagator from an effective infrared interaction model

> In this paper I investigate the analytic properties of the quark propagator Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) in the Landau gauge. In the quark self-energy, the combined gluon propagator and quark-gluon vertex is modeled by an effective interaction (the so-called Maris-Tandy interaction), where the ultraviolet term is neglected. This renders the loop integrand of the quark self-energy analytic on the cut-plane -Pi < arg(x) < Pi of the square of the external momentum. Exploiting the simplicity of the truncation, I study solutions of the quark propagator in the domain x in [-5.1,0] GeV^2 X i[0,10.2] GeV^2. Because of a complex conjugation symmetry, this region fully covers the parabolic integration domain for Bethe-Salpeter equations (BSEs) for bound state masses of up to 4.5 GeV. Employing a novel numerical technique that is based on highly parallel computation on graphics processing units (GPUs), I extract more than 6500 poles in this region, which arise as the bare quark mass is varied over a wide range of closely spaced values. The poles are grouped in 23 individual 'trajectories', that capture the movement of the poles in the complex region as the bare mass is varied. The raw data of the pole locations and -residues is provided as supplemental material, which can be used to parametrize solutions of the complex quark propagator for a wide range of bare mass values and for large bound state masses. This study is a first step towards an extension of previous work on the analytic continuation of perturbative one-loop integrals, with the long-term goal of establishing a framework that allows for the numerical extraction of the analytic properties of the quark propagator with a truncation that extends beyond the rainbow by making adequate adjustments in the contour of the radial integration of the quark self-energy.

## Key claim

The quark propagator, computed using only the infrared part of the Maris-Tandy interaction in Landau gauge, exhibits more than 6500 poles in the complex momentum plane for bound state masses up to 4.5 GeV, but this result is conditional on neglecting the ultraviolet term of the interaction, which is only justified for light meson masses.

## Method

The rainbow-truncated quark propagator Dyson-Schwinger equation was solved in Euclidean space using the infrared part of the Maris-Tandy interaction in the Alkofer-Watson-Weigel parametrization, where the angular integral was expressed analytically in terms of modified Bessel functions of the first kind. A novel numerical technique based on highly parallel computation on graphics processing units (GPUs) was employed to evaluate the quark propagator on an 850x850 lattice covering the region x in [-5.1,0] GeV^2 x i[0,10.2] GeV^2, and poles were extracted using the Cauchy argument principle combined with a masked global maximum search and residue theorem verification.

## Key numbers

* More than 6500 poles were extracted for 500 different bare mass values ranging from 5 MeV to 2500 MeV in increments of 5 MeV
* The complex region studied covers the parabolic integration domain for Bethe-Salpeter equations for bound state masses up to 4.5 GeV
* The lattice resolution was 6x10^{-3} GeV^2 for the real part and 12x10^{-3} GeV^2 for the imaginary part
* 23 individual pole trajectories were identified, labeled A through Q (with sub-labels for B)
* For bare mass values above m0 ~ 455 MeV, the segment of the real axis enclosed by the parabola for bound state masses of 4.5 GeV is free of real poles
* The pole search used a circular contour of radius r = 0.1 GeV^2 for residue computation with at least 1024 integration points per contour

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Dorkin (2013, 2014) on the analytic properties of the quark propagator with the infrared Maris-Tandy interaction, which had identified some pole locations for bound state masses up to 3.5 GeV. The present study identifies all poles within the parabolic region for bound state masses up to 4.5 GeV and provides the complete bare mass dependence of pole positions and residues. This is a first step toward extending the analytic continuation techniques developed by Windisch (2012, 2013) for perturbative one-loop integrals to non-perturbative truncations beyond rainbow level, with the long-term goal of studying positivity violation and confinement as investigated by Strauss (2012) for the Yang-Mills sector.
