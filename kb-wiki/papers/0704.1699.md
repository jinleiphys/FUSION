# Relativistic Hydrodynamics at RHIC and LHC

> Recent development of a hydrodynamic model is discussed by putting an emphasis on realistic treatment of the early and late stages in relativistic heavy ion collisions. The model, which incorporates a hydrodynamic description of the quark-gluon plasma with a kinetic approach of hadron cascades, is applied to analysis of elliptic flow data at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider energy. It is predicted that the elliptic flow parameter based on the hybrid model increases with the collision energy up to the Large Hadron Collider energy.

## Key claim

The hybrid model predicts that the elliptic flow parameter scaled by initial eccentricity increases monotonically with collision energy from RHIC to LHC, contrary to ideal fluid approaches which show saturation; however, this prediction depends on the assumption that CGC initial conditions are applicable at SPS energies and that hadronic dissipation is correctly accounted for.

## Method

The author formulates a hybrid model combining fully three-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics for the quark-gluon plasma phase with a hadronic cascade model (JAM) below a switching temperature of 169 MeV. Initial conditions are generated either from a Glauber model or from the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) picture using the KLN gluon production formalism. The model is applied to compute elliptic flow coefficients and particle spectra at RHIC and LHC energies, with comparisons to PHOBOS and STAR data.

## Key numbers

- Switching temperature between QGP fluid and hadronic cascade: T^sw = 169 MeV
- Transition temperature for QGP to hadronic matter: T_c = 170 MeV
- Chemical freezeout temperature: T^ch = 170 MeV
- Thermal freezeout temperature in pure hydrodynamics: T^th = 100 MeV (for radial flow reproduction) or T^th = 169 MeV (just below transition)
- Initial time for hydrodynamic evolution: tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c
- Eccentricity from CGC initial conditions is 20-30% larger than from Glauber model
- Hybrid model reproduces data for identified hadrons below p_T ~ 1.5 GeV/c
- Hadronic dissipation reduces v_2 by approximately 20% at RHIC energies
- Impact parameter for semi-central studies: b = 7.2 fm (20-30% centrality) and b = 6.3 fm for excitation function

## Context

This work extends the hybrid modeling approach of Bass and Dumitru (2000), Teaney (2003), and Nonaka and Bass (2007) by combining 3D ideal hydrodynamics with hadronic cascades. It contradicts the saturation prediction of Kolb (2000) for the v_2 excitation function at higher energies. The results enable discrimination between Glauber-type and CGC-based initial conditions through elliptic flow measurements, and motivate inclusion of shear viscosity in the QGP phase if CGC initial conditions are correct.
