# Speed of sound in dense strong-interaction matter

> We study the speed of sound in strong-interaction matter at zero temperature and in density regimes which are expected to be governed by the presence of a color-superconducting gap. At (very) high densities, our analysis indicates that the speed of sound approaches its asymptotic value associated with the non-interacting quark gas from below, in agreement with first-principles studies which do not take the presence of a color-superconducting gap into account. Towards lower densities, however, the presence of a gap induces an increase of the speed of sound above its asymptotic value. Importantly, even if gap-induced corrections to the pressure may appear small, we find that derivatives of the gap with respect to the chemical potential can still be sizeable and lead to a qualitative change of the density dependence of the speed of sound. Taking into account constraints on the density dependence of the speed of sound at low densities, our general considerations suggest the existence of a maximum in the speed of sound. Interestingly, we also observe that specific properties of the gap can be related to characteristic properties of the speed of sound which are indirectly constrained by observations.

## Key claim

The authors show that even when gap-induced corrections to the pressure appear small, derivatives of the color-superconducting gap with respect to the chemical potential can still be sizeable and qualitatively change the density dependence of the speed of sound, suggesting the existence of a maximum in the speed of sound when combined with low-density constraints.

## Method

The authors expand the pressure of dense strong-interaction matter as a power series in the gauge-invariant quantity |Δ/μ|², where Δ is the color-superconducting gap, using coefficients γᵢ extracted from correlation functions. They then compute the speed of sound from the ratio of first and second derivatives of the pressure with respect to chemical potential, employing two different functional forms for the gap: one from the weak-coupling limit and one from a recent functional renormalization group (fRG) study by Braun et al. (2021).

## Key numbers

- The speed of sound approaches its asymptotic value cₛ = 1/√3 from below for n → ∞, in agreement with perturbative QCD calculations.
- A local minimum in the speed of sound appears at a density n = n_min, dividing the regime where gap corrections are negligible (n > n_min) from the regime where they qualitatively change the density dependence (n < n_min).
- The crossing density n* (where the speed of sound exceeds its asymptotic value) depends on the gap size Δ* defined at n/n₀ = 10, with values Δ* = 0.02 GeV, 0.16 GeV, 0.21 GeV, and 0.30 GeV studied in the analysis.
- The weak-coupling gap formula is |Δ| = s μ g⁻⁵ exp(-3π²/(√2 g)), with s = 512π⁴ exp(-(4+π²)/8) s₀, where s₀ = 1 recovers the result from Pisarski et al. (1999) and Rischke (2003).
- The one-loop running coupling uses Λ_QCD ≈ 0.265 GeV, obtained from g₀²/(4π) ≈ 0.179 at Λ₀ = 10 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Leonhardt et al. (2019) and Braun et al. (2021, 2022) that found a maximum in the speed of sound induced by color-superconducting gaps, and is consistent with constraints from neutron-star observations by Bedaque et al. (2014), Tews et al. (2018), and Annala et al. (2019) that suggest a maximum exceeding the asymptotic value at densities n/n₀ ≲ 10. The analysis enables relating the size of the color-superconducting gap to the specific density at which the speed of sound exceeds its asymptotic value, and provides a framework for interpreting future observational constraints on the speed of sound in dense matter.
