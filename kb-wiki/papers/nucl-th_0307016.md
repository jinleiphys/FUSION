# Effect of Resonant Continuum on Pairing Correlations in the Relativistic Approach

> A proper treatment of the resonant continuum is to take account of not only the energy of the resonant state, but also its width. The effect of the resonant states on pairing correlations is presented based on the relativistic mean field theory plus Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer(BCS) approximation with a constant pairing strength. The study is performed in an effective Lagrangian with the parameter set NL3 for neutron rich even-even Ni isotopes. The results show that the contribution of the proper treatment of the resonant continuum to pairing correlations for those nuclei close to neutron drip line is important. The pairing gaps, Fermi energies, pairing correlation energies, and binding energies are considerably affected with a proper consideration of the width of resonant states. The problem of an unphysical particle gas, which may appear in the calculation of the traditional mean field plus BCS method for nuclei in the vicinity of drip line could be well overcome when the pairing correlation is performed by using the resonant states instead of the discretized states in the continuum.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a proper treatment of the resonant continuum in pairing correlations must include not only the energy of the resonant state but also its width, and that this width effect becomes important for nuclei close to the neutron drip line, where it reduces pairing gaps and pairing correlation energies compared to calculations that neglect the width.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations with the NL3 parameter set for neutron-rich even-even Ni isotopes, combined with a Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) approximation using a constant pairing strength G = C/A with C = 20.5 MeV. They compared three approaches: (a) standard RMF+BCS with a box boundary condition; (b) RMF+BCSR, where resonant state wave functions are obtained with a scattering boundary condition but widths are neglected; and (c) RMF+BCSRW, where the widths of resonant states are explicitly included by introducing a continuum level density g_nu = (1/pi) d(delta_nu)/d(epsilon_nu) into the gap equations.

## Key numbers

* For the neutron-rich nucleus 90Ni, the pairing gap in RMF+BCSR is Delta_n = 1.558 MeV and the Fermi energy is lambda_n = -1.278 MeV; in RMF+BCSRW these are reduced to Delta_n = 1.223 MeV and lambda_n = -1.256 MeV.
* The constant pairing strength uses C = 20.5 MeV in the relation G = C/A.
* The neutron drip line for Ni isotopes is predicted to lie between 98Ni and 100Ni in all three approaches.
* The empirical pairing gap is given by 11.2/sqrt(A) MeV for comparison.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic treatment of resonant continuum in pairing correlations by Sandulescu et al. (1997, 2000) and Kruppa et al. (2001) to the relativistic framework, and complements the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations of Meng et al. (1998). The authors show that the simple RMF+BCS method with proper treatment of resonant continuum can reproduce the pairing correlation energies predicted by continuum HFB approaches up to the drip line, while avoiding the unphysical particle gas that appears in traditional box-discretized BCS calculations for nuclei near the drip line.
