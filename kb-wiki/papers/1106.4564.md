# Sensitivity of Azimuthal Jet Tomography to Early Time Energy-Loss at RHIC and LHC

> We compute the jet path-length dependence of energy-loss for higher azimuthal harmonics of jet-fragments in a generalized model of energy-loss that can interpolate between pQCD and AdS/CFT limits and compare results with Glauber and CGC/KLN initial conditions. We find, however, that even the high-pT second moment is most sensitive to the poorly known early-time evolution during the first fm/c. Moreover, we demonstrate that quite generally the energy and density-dependence leads to an overquenching jet fragments relative to the first LHC $R_{AA}$-data, once the parameters of the energy-loss model are fixed from $R_{AA}$-data at RHIC.

## Key claim
The paper shows that even the high-pT second Fourier moment (v2) of jet fragments is most sensitive to the poorly known early-time evolution during the first fm/c, and that a generic energy-loss model fixed to RHIC RAA data systematically overquenches high-pT particles relative to first LHC RAA data.

## Method
The authors compute the path-length dependence of energy-loss for higher azimuthal harmonics of jet-fragments using a generalized energy-loss model that interpolates between pQCD (dE/dx ~ l) and AdS/CFT (dE/dx ~ l^2) limits. They compare results using both Glauber and CGC/KLN initial conditions, with initialization times τ0 = 1 fm and τ0 = 0.01 fm, fitting the coupling κ to reproduce the most central RAA value at RHIC energies.

## Key numbers
- At RHIC energies (pT = 10 GeV), with τ0 = 1 fm, CGC/KLN initial conditions get close to the data for both pQCD and AdS/CFT-like energy loss, while Glauber initial conditions underpredict the data.
- With τ0 = 0.01 fm, v2(Npart) is reduced for both pQCD and AdS/CFT-like energy loss, increasing the difference between the two models compared to the τ0 = 1 fm case.
- At LHC energies (pT just below 10 GeV), keeping a and κ fixed from RHIC leads to underprediction of RAA as a function of centrality for both pQCD and AdS/CFT-like energy loss and both τ0 values.

## Context
This work extends the first simple jet absorption model from Jia (2008) that simultaneously described RAA(Npart) and v2(Npart) at RHIC for high-pT particles. The authors show that the earlier conclusion, that only CGC/KLN initial conditions with AdS/CFT energy-loss can describe both observables, depends sensitively on the assumed initialization time τ0. The finding that density-dependent energy-loss prescriptions overquench at LHC when parameters are fixed from RHIC poses a puzzle common to all such models, as discussed in Horowitz (2011).
