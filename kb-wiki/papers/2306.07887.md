# Improving Euler-Heisenberg-Schwinger effective action with dressed photons

> We implement a longstanding proposal by Weisskopf to apply virtual polarization corrections to the in/out external fields in study of the Euler-Heisenberg-Schwinger effective action. Our approach requires distinguishing the electromagnetic and polarization fields based on mathematical tools developed by Białynicki-Birula, originally for the Born-Infeld action. Our solution is expressed as a differential equation where the one-loop effective action serves as input. As a first result of our approach, we recover the higher-order one-cut reducible loop diagrams discovered by Gies and Karbstein.

# Improving Euler-Heisenberg-Schwinger effective action with dressed photons

## Key claim

The authors implement Weisskopf's 1936 proposal to apply virtual polarization corrections to the external fields in the Euler-Heisenberg-Schwinger (EHS) effective action, showing that the one-cut reducible loop diagram summation discovered by Gies and Karbstein (2016) was foretold in Weisskopf's work; the approach recovers the two-loop result of Gies and Karbstein as a first step, under the condition that polarization corrections to the external field are small (|D - E|/|E| << 1).

## Method

The authors use a Legendre transform formalism developed by Białynicki-Birula (1984) to convert the EHS effective action from a function of non-superposable electromagnetic fields (E, B) into an auxiliary form using superposable fields (D, H). They then insert polarization corrections by replacing D with D - P = E and H with H + M = B in the nonlinear part of the auxiliary action, thereby dressing the external fields with vacuum polarization effects. Finally, they perform an inverse Legendre transform to return to an effective action expressed in terms of the original EM fields, yielding a differential equation where the one-loop EHS action serves as input.

## Key numbers

- The two-loop contribution to the effective action for a pure electric field is given by L_2(E) = -(∂L_1(E)/∂E)^2, matching Eq. (32) of Gies and Karbstein (2016)
- The three-loop contribution for a pure electric field is L_3(E) = (5/2)(∂²L_1(E)/∂E²)(∂L_1(E)/∂E)²
- The perturbative expansion parameter is the fine-structure constant α = 1/137, used as the coupling constant for the loop expansion

## Context

This work extends the 1936 proposal by Weisskopf to include "everlasting" vacuum polarization in the EHS effective action, connecting it to the modern discovery of nonvanishing two-loop reducible diagram corrections by Gies and Karbstein (2016) and subsequent higher-order loop summations by Karbstein (2017, 2019, 2021). The approach complements earlier work by Białynicki-Birula, Rudnicki and Wienczek (2011) on finite-time regularization of the essential singularity in the one-loop EHS result, and can be applied to extend any one-loop effective action, including for strongly-interacting vacuum structure.
