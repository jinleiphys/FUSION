# Non-radial oscillation modes in hybrid stars: consequences of a mixed phase

> We study the possibility of the existence of a deconfined quark matter in the core of neutron star (NS)s and its relation to non-radial oscillation modes in NSs and hybrid star (HS)s. We use relativistic mean field (RMF) models to describe the nuclear matter at low densities and zero temperature. The Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model is used to describe the quark matter at high densities and zero temperature. A Gibbs construct is used to describe the hadron-quark phase transition (HQPT) at large densities. Within the model, as the density increases, a mixed phase (MP) appears at density about $2.5$ times the nuclear matter saturation density $(ρ_0)$ and ends at density about $5 ρ_0$ beyond which the pure quark matter phase appears. It turns out that a stable HS of maximum mass, $M=2.27 M_{\odot}$ with radius $R=14$ km (for NL3 parameterisation of nuclear RMF model), can exist with the quark matter in the core in a MP only. HQPT in the core of maximum mass HS occurs at radial distance, $r_c=0.27R$ where the equilibrium speed of sound shows a discontinuity. Existence of quark matter in the core enhances the non-radial oscillation frequencies in HSs compared to NSs of the same mass. This enhancement is significantly large for the $g$ modes. Such an enhancement of the $g$ modes is also seen for a density dependent Bayesian (DDB) parmeterisation of the nucleonic EOS. The non-radial oscillation frequencies depend on the vector coupling in the NJL model. The values of $g$ and $f$ mode frequencies decrease with increase the vector coupling in quark matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the presence of a mixed phase of hadronic and quark matter in hybrid stars enhances non-radial oscillation frequencies, particularly the gravity (g) modes, compared to pure neutron stars of the same mass; this enhancement depends on the vector coupling in the NJL model and is conditional on using a Gibbs construct for the hadron-quark phase transition.

## Method

The authors use relativistic mean field (RMF) models (NL3 and density dependent Bayesian parameterizations) for nuclear matter at zero temperature and the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for quark matter, with a Gibbs construction to describe the mixed phase at the hadron-quark phase transition. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for stellar structure and then compute non-radial oscillation modes (f and g modes) by solving the general relativistic fluid perturbation equations, using the Brunt-Vaisala frequency derived from the equilibrium and adiabatic sound speeds.

## Key numbers

- The mixed phase appears at density about 2.5 times nuclear matter saturation density (rho_0) and ends at about 5 rho_0, beyond which pure quark matter appears.
- A stable hybrid star with maximum mass M = 2.27 solar masses and radius R = 14 km exists for the NL3 parameterization, with quark matter in the core only in a mixed phase.
- The hadron-quark phase transition in the maximum mass hybrid star occurs at radial distance r_c = 0.27 R, where the equilibrium speed of sound shows a discontinuity.
- For the NL3 parameterization with G_v = 0.2 G_s, quark matter starts appearing at rho_B = 2.75 rho_0 and hadronic matter melts completely at rho_B = 5.72 rho_0.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of non-radial oscillations in hybrid stars (Sotani 2010, Flores 2013, Brillante 2014, Wei 2018, Sandoval 2018, Lau 2020, Rodriguez 2020, Jaikumar 2021) by using RMF models for nuclear matter and the NJL model for quark matter rather than parameterized equations of state or bag models. The results enable distinguishing hybrid stars from pure neutron stars through gravitational wave asteroseismology, addressing the "masquerade" problem (Alford 2004) where different compositions produce similar mass-radius relations.
