# Thermal transport in a weakly magnetized hot QCD medium

> The thermal transport coefficients in a weakly magnetized quark-gluon plasma have been investigated within the ambit of a quasiparticle model to encode the effects of the realistic equation of state. The presence of a weak magnetic field leads to the Hall-type conductivity associated with thermal transport in the medium. An effective covariant kinetic theory has been employed to quantify the thermal dissipation while incorporating the mean field contributions in the medium. The interplay of thermal transport and electric charge transport in the weakly magnetized medium has been explored in terms of the Wiedemann-Franz law. Strong violation of the Wiedemann-Franz law has been observed in temperature regimes near to the transition temperature. The behaviour of thermal conductivity in the strong magnetic field limit has also been studied. It is observed that both the magnetic field and equation of state have a significant impact on the thermal dissipation in the medium.

## Key claim

The paper claims that thermal transport in a weakly magnetized quark-gluon plasma exhibits a strong violation of the Wiedemann-Franz law near the transition temperature, but this result is conditional on the magnetic field being a subdominant energy scale compared to temperature, allowing the magnetic field effects to enter only through the cyclotron frequency in the Lorentz force term of the Boltzmann equation.

## Method

The author employed an effective covariant kinetic theory within the relaxation time approximation, using the effective fugacity quasiparticle model (EQPM) to incorporate realistic equation-of-state effects from lattice QCD. The Boltzmann equation was solved iteratively via a Chapman-Enskog-like approach to obtain the non-equilibrium distribution function, with the weak magnetic field treated as a perturbation through the Lorentz force term. Thermal conductivity coefficients (longitudinal and Hall-type) were derived from the heat current expression, and the Wiedemann-Franz law was evaluated by comparing thermal and electrical conductivities in the same weakly magnetized framework.

## Key numbers

- Thermal conductivity \(\kappa_0\) and Hall-type thermal conductivity \(\kappa_1\) were computed at magnetic field strength \(|eB| = 0.01\) GeV\(^2\) and quark chemical potential \(\mu = 200\) MeV
- The cyclotron frequency \(\Omega_{c\,k} = q_{f_k} |\mathbf{B}| / \omega_k\) appears in the denominator factor \((1 + \tau_{R_k}^2 \Omega_{c\,k}^2)\) in both \(\kappa_0\) and \(\kappa_1\) expressions
- The effective coupling \(\alpha_{\text{eff}}\) is defined via PolyLog functions with \(N_c = 3\) and \(N_f\) flavors, and the running coupling \(\alpha_s(T, \mu)\) is taken from 2-loop QCD gauge coupling constants
- Thermal relaxation time scales as \(\tau^{-1}_{R\,{g,q,\bar{q}}} \sim T \alpha^2_{\text{eff}} \ln\{1/\alpha_{\text{eff}}\}\)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of thermal conductivity in QGP using kinetic theory (Mitra 2017, Kalikotay 2019) and Kubo formalism (Fernandez-Fraile 2009) to include weak magnetic field effects, and complements investigations of electrical and Hall conductivity in weakly magnetized media (Feng 2017, Das 2019). The analysis enables future studies of thermoelectric behavior (Seebeck coefficient) in magnetized QGP and provides a framework for comparing transport in weak versus strong magnetic field regimes, where the latter involves Landau level kinematics (Kurian 2018, Rath 2019).
