# Investigating the NCQ scaling of elliptic flow at LHC with a multiphase transport model

> The number of constituent quark (NCQ) scaling behavior of elliptic flow has been systematically studied at the LHC energy within the framework of a multiphase transport model (AMPT) in this work. We find that the parameters used to generate the initial states and the collision centrality are important for the existence of NCQ scaling even when hadronic rescattering contribution is off in Pb-Pb collisions of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV. By turning on the hadron rescattering process, the hadronic evolution impacts are also found to be significant. Extending the analysis to Pb-Pb collsions of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV, one would observe similar qualitative features.

## Key claim

The NCQ scaling of elliptic flow at LHC energies depends not only on the hadronization mechanism (quark coalescence) but also on the initial conditions of parton evolution and on hadronic interactions, with the latter causing a significant violation of the scaling even when the parton-level scaling holds.

## Method

The authors used the string-melting version of the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model to simulate Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV and 5.02 TeV. They varied the Lund string fragmentation parameters (sets A and B), the parton-parton scattering cross section (3 mb and 10 mb), and the hadronic rescattering time (\(t_{max}\)) to isolate the effects of initial conditions, parton cascade, and hadronic evolution on the NCQ scaling of elliptic flow for pions, kaons, and protons. Elliptic flow was calculated using the event plane method with particles in \(|\eta|<0.8\).

## Key numbers

*   The violation of NCQ scaling, quantified by \(\chi\) (defined in Eq. 4), is 0.06 for primordial particles (right after coalescence) with set A parameters and \(\sigma=3\) mb in 30-40% centrality Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV.
*   With set B parameters (smaller string tension) and the same \(\sigma=3\) mb, the violation \(\chi\) increases to 0.14 for the same centrality and energy.
*   Including resonance decay increases \(\chi\) from 0.06 to 0.16; further including hadron rescattering up to \(t_{max}=90\) fm/\(c\) increases \(\chi\) to 0.24 for 30-40% centrality at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV.
*   The ratio of integrated \(n_q\)-scaled \(v_2\) (proton over pion) in the range \(0.3<(m_T-m_0)/n_q<1\) GeV approaches unity for peripheral collisions (30-60% centrality) but deviates for central collisions (0-1% centrality) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV.

## Context

This work extends studies of NCQ scaling from RHIC (where it was observed with surprising accuracy by experiments such as PHENIX and STAR) to LHC energies, where ALICE reported a violation of the scaling at the level of \(\pm 20\%\). The paper systematically tests the role of initial conditions (via Lund fragmentation parameters as in Lin (2014)) and hadronic interactions (using the ART model within AMPT) in producing this violation, finding that both are important. It enables future studies to disentangle parton-level and hadron-level contributions to flow scaling and suggests testing coalescence models that preserve momentum collimation.
