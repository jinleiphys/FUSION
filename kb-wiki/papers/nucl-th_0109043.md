# Quadrupole Oscillations as Paradigm of the Chaotic Motion in Nuclei. Part 2

> The present communication is a continuation of the review, the first part of which was presented in nucl-th/0109033. The second part dealswith the manifistation of Chaotic dynamics at quantum level. The variations of statistical properties of energy spectrum in the process of R-C-R transition have been studied in detail. We proved that the type of the classical motion is correlated with the structure of the eigenfunctions of highly excited states in the R-C-R transition. Shell structure destruction induced by the increase of nonintegrable perturbation was analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the type of classical motion (regular or chaotic) in the quadrupole oscillation model is correlated with the structure of eigenfunctions of highly excited states, and that the statistical properties of the quantum energy spectrum (nearest-neighbor spacing distribution and spectral rigidity) evolve systematically through the regular-chaotic-regular (R-C-R) transition, with the semiclassical normal-form quantization reproducing exact quantum spectra well in regular regions but failing near the transition to chaos.

## Method

The authors diagonalized the quantum Hamiltonian for quadrupole surface oscillations (scaled to a two-dimensional form with a scaled Planck's constant) using a basis of two-dimensional harmonic oscillator eigenfunctions with C3v symmetry, which allowed separate diagonalization of A1, A2, and E-type submatrices. They computed energy spectra and eigenfunctions for one-well potentials (W < 16) across three energy intervals corresponding to the first regular region (R1), chaotic region (C), and second regular region (R2), using basis dimensions up to n = 408 for submatrices (total matrix dimensions up to 2556). Additionally, they constructed a semiclassical approximation by quantizing the classical normal form of the Hamiltonian through fourth order using Weyl's correspondence rule, yielding an analytical formula for the energy spectrum E(N,L).

## Key numbers

* Basis dimensions used: n = 408 for submatrices, corresponding to maximum oscillator numbers N0 = 67 (A1 type), N0 = 70 (A2 type), and N0 = 48 (E1,2 type), with total matrix dimensions m = 2346, 2556, and 1225 respectively.
* For the Hamiltonian parameters b = 0.04416, c = 0.00015, Ecr = 90, the semiclassical approximation reproduced exact quantum levels with relative error Delta E/E approximately 0.01% in the regular region (e.g., level 10: Eapprox = 5.9801 vs Eexact = 5.9807, error 0.01%).
* For the case W = 13, Ecr = 10, the difference between exact and semiclassical levels increased sharply at the transition to the chaotic region, as shown in Fig. 20(b).
* For the regular region R1 and R2, acceptable precision for levels with ordinal numbers 100-200 was reached at basis dimension n approximately 400, while the chaotic region C required n approximately 700 for the same precision.

## Context

This work extends the review presented in Part 1 (nucl-th/0109033) and builds on the hypothesis of universal fluctuations in energy spectra, which holds that classically chaotic systems exhibit level repulsion (Wigner distribution) while regular systems show level clustering (Poisson distribution), as established by Berry and Robnik (1984) and Bogomolny (1985). The paper applies this framework to the nuclear quadrupole oscillation model, demonstrating that the R-C-R transition is reflected in the statistical properties of the quantum spectrum, and that the semiclassical normal-form quantization (following the approach of Swimm and Delos (1979) for the Henon-Heiles Hamiltonian) works well in regular regions but fails near the onset of chaos. This enables future studies of shell structure destruction induced by nonintegrable perturbations in nuclear systems.
