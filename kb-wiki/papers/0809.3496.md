# Lowest Order Constrained Variational Calculation of the Polarized Nuclear Matter with the Modern $AV_{18}$ Potential

> The lowest order constrained variational method is applied to calculate the polarized symmetrical nuclear matter properties with the modern $AV_{18}$ potential performing microscopic calculations. Results based on the consideration of magnetic properties show no sign of phase transition to a ferromagnetic phase.

## Key claim

Using the lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method with the modern AV18 potential, the authors find no sign of a phase transition to a ferromagnetic state in polarized symmetrical nuclear matter, in agreement with Brueckner-Hartree-Fock results from Zuo et al. (2003) but contradicting predictions from Skyrme-like interactions that place the transition at densities between 1 and 4 times the nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The lowest order constrained variational (LOCV) method was applied to polarized symmetrical nuclear matter using the AV18 two-body potential. The energy functional was expanded up to the two-body cluster term, with the correlation function minimized subject to a normalization constraint that keeps higher-order terms small. The magnetic susceptibility, Landau parameter G0, and equation of state were computed as functions of density for various spin polarizations.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: ρ0 = 0.16 fm⁻³ (used as reference density throughout)
- The energy per particle of polarized symmetrical nuclear matter increases with polarization, with the minimum occurring at δ=0 for all densities, indicating a paramagnetic ground state
- The ratio of magnetic susceptibility to the free Fermi gas value (χ/χF) decreases continuously with increasing density, with no sign of divergence that would indicate a ferromagnetic transition
- The Landau parameter G0 remains positive and monotonically increasing up to the highest densities considered, never reaching the instability condition G0 < -1
- The quadratic spin polarization coefficient a_nucl(ρ) was extracted from the nearly linear dependence of energy on δ²

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous LOCV calculations on polarized neutron matter (Bordbar and Bigdeli, 2007) to symmetrical nuclear matter, and agrees with the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock results of Zuo et al. (2003) using the same AV18 potential, which also found no ferromagnetic transition. The results contradict predictions from Skyrme-like interactions (e.g., Vidaña and Bombaci, 2002) that suggest a ferromagnetic transition at densities of (1-4)ρ0, which would have implications for the magnetic fields of pulsars and neutron star interiors. The calculations enable further microscopic studies of spin correlations in dense matter, which affect neutrino cross sections and mean free paths in protoneutron stars.
