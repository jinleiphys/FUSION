# Superallowed Beta Decay of Nuclei with A >or= 62: The Limiting Effect of Weak Gamow-Teller Branches

> The most precise value of V_{ud}, which is obtained from superallowed nuclear beta decay, leads to a violation of CKM unitarity by 2.2 sigma. Experiments are underway on two continents to test and improve this result through decay studies of odd-odd N = Z nuclei with A >or= 62. We show, in a series of illustrative shell-model calculations, that numerous weak Gamow-Teller branches are expected to compete with the superallowed branch in each of these nuclei. Though the total Gamow-Teller strength is significant, many of the individual branches will be unobservably weak. Thus, new techniques must be developed if reliable ft-values are to be obtained with 0.1% precision for the superallowed branches.

# Superallowed Beta Decay of Nuclei with A ≥ 62: The Limiting Effect of Weak Gamow-Teller Branches

## Key claim

The authors show through illustrative shell-model calculations that superallowed beta decays of odd-odd N = Z nuclei with A ≥ 62 are complicated by numerous weak Gamow-Teller branches that collectively compete significantly with the superallowed branch, unlike the lighter A ≤ 54 cases where such branches are negligible. These weak branches, many of which will be unobservably weak with conventional detection techniques, must be accounted for if the superallowed ft-values are to be determined with the 0.1% precision required for testing charge-dependent corrections and CKM unitarity.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations for the superallowed decays of four nuclei with 62 ≤ A ≤ 74 (62Ga, 66As, 70Br, 74Rb) and three lighter comparison nuclei with 46 ≤ A ≤ 54 (46V, 50Mn, 54Co). For the lighter nuclei, a 40Ca core with (f7/2)n-r(p3/2,f5/2,p1/2)r model space truncated to r ≤ 3 was used with KB3 and FPMI3 effective interactions. For the heavier nuclei, a 56Ni core with (p3/2,f5/2,p1/2)n model space was used with MSDI interactions, and for 74Rb an extended space including g9/2, d5/2, and g7/2 orbitals was employed with a tuned MSDI′ interaction. For each 1+ state in the daughter nucleus within the QEC window, the Gamow-Teller transition probability B(GT) and partial width were computed, and branching ratios were summed to obtain total Gamow-Teller branching fractions.

## Key numbers

- For the lighter nuclei (46V, 50Mn, 54Co), the total calculated Gamow-Teller branching fraction is 0.025% or less of the total beta decay
- For 62Ga, the total calculated Gamow-Teller branching fraction is 0.28%
- For 66As, the total calculated Gamow-Teller branching fraction is 0.67%
- For 70Br, the total calculated Gamow-Teller branching fraction is 1.59%
- For 74Rb, the total calculated Gamow-Teller branching fraction is 0.72% with MSDI and 0.92% with MSDI′
- The largest single non-superallowed branch calculated is 0.1% to the sixth 1+ state in 62Zn, 0.2% to the third 1+ state in 66Ge, 1% to the third 1+ state in 70Se, and 0.3% to the fifth 1+ state in 74Kr
- For 74Rb decay, there are 20 transitions with individual branching ratios above 0.005%
- The fraction of summed Gamow-Teller strength decaying through the lowest 2+1 state ranges from 80% for 62Ga down to 47% for 74Rb (MSDI) and 56% for 74Rb (MSDI′)
- The expected δC corrections for A ≥ 62 nuclei are in the 1.0 to 1.8% range, three to five times larger than for the nine precisely measured cases with A ≤ 54

## Context

This work extends the study of superallowed beta decay to heavier odd-odd N = Z nuclei with A ≥ 62, where new radioactive-beam facilities have made production possible. Previous precise measurements on lighter nuclei (A ≤ 54) by Hardy and collaborators (1990) and the world data analysis by Towner and Hardy (1998) yielded a CKM unitarity violation of 2.2σ, but the charge-dependent corrections δC calculated by Ormand and Brown (1995) are predicted to be much larger for these heavier nuclei, offering a more stringent test. The authors demonstrate a new complication not present in lighter cases: the presence of numerous weak Gamow-Teller branches that collectively constitute 0.3% to 1.6% of the total decay strength, which must be measured to achieve 0.1% precision on superallowed ft-values. This work enables the development of new experimental techniques, particularly total absorption spectrometry, to address these weak branches, and it does not alter the existing non-unitarity result from lighter nuclei.
