# Muon production in low-energy electron-nucleon and electron-nucleus scattering

> Recently, muon production in electron-proton scattering has been suggested as a possible candidate reaction for the identification of lepton-flavor violation due to physics beyond the Standard Model. Here we point out that the Standard-Model processes $e^- p \to μ^- p \barν_μν_e$ and $e^- p \to e^- n μ^+ ν_μ$ can cloud potential beyond-the-Standard-Model signals in electron-proton collisions. We find that Standard-Model $e p \to μX$ cross sections exceed those from lepton-flavor-violating operators by several orders of magnitude. We also discuss the possibility of using a nuclear target to enhance the $e p \to μX$ signal.

## Key claim

Standard-Model processes \(e^- p \to \mu^- p \bar{\nu}_\mu \nu_e\) and \(e^- p \to e^- n \mu^+ \nu_\mu\) produce muon cross sections that exceed those from lepton-flavor-violating beyond-the-Standard-Model operators by several orders of magnitude, and the BSM contribution is constrained by the \(\mu \to e \gamma\) branching ratio bound to be below \(7.0 \times 10^{-15}\) fb, making it unobservable at current facilities.

## Method

The authors computed the cross section for the electroweak process \(e^- p \to \mu^- \bar{\nu}_\mu \nu_e p\) using a single-photon-exchange diagram with Fermi-theory weak vertices, evaluating the squared matrix element with nucleon form factors and performing the phase-space integrals numerically. They also computed the cross section for the strong-interaction process \(e p \to e n \mu^+ \nu_\mu\) using leading-order chiral perturbation theory (\(\chi\)PT) tree-level diagrams involving virtual pion exchange and decay. For the BSM process \(e p \to \mu p\), they used a dimension-five operator coupling to the electromagnetic field-strength tensor and constrained its cross section via the experimental bound on \(\mu \to e \gamma\).

## Key numbers

- Minimum electron beam energy for muon production via the electroweak process: \(E_e^{\rm min} = 111.6\) MeV.
- Total cross section for \(e^- p \to \mu^- p \nu_e \bar{\nu}_\mu\) rises from \(3 \times 10^{-16}\) fb at \(E_e = 120\) MeV to \(4 \times 10^{-13}\) fb at \(E_e = 140\) MeV.
- Upper bound on BSM \(e p \to \mu p\) cross section from \(\mu \to e \gamma\) constraint: \(\sigma < 7.0 \times 10^{-15}\) fb.
- Bound on the BSM scale from the same constraint: \(\Lambda \geq 1.5 \times 10^4\) TeV.
- Elastic \(ep\) scattering cross section at \(E_e = 140\) MeV is approximately \(10^7\) fb, about 20 orders of magnitude larger than the electroweak muon-production cross section.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Diener (2004), who proposed electron-to-muon conversion (\(e p \to \mu p\)) as a probe of lepton-flavor violation, by quantifying the Standard-Model backgrounds that would obscure such a signal. The authors show that the electroweak and pion-production processes produce muon cross sections far exceeding any BSM contribution, and that using a nuclear target could enhance the signal through coherent interactions with the total nuclear charge. The results imply that any experiment seeking lepton-flavor violation in electron-nucleon scattering must veto processes with additional particles or detect the muon charge to distinguish BSM signals from Standard-Model backgrounds.
