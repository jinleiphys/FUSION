# The magnetic moments and electromagnetic form factors of the decuplet baryons in chiral perturbation theory

> We have systematically investigated the magnetic moments and magnetic form factors of the decuplet baryons to the next-to-next-leading order in the framework of the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. Our calculation includes the contributions from both the intermediate decuplet and octet baryon states in the loops. We also calculate the charge and magnetic dipole form factors of the decuplet baryons. Our results may be useful to the chiral extrapolation of the lattice simulations of the decuplet electromagnetic properties.

## Key claim

The magnetic moments and electromagnetic form factors of the decuplet baryons are calculated to next-to-next-leading order (𝒪(p³)) in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, including both intermediate octet and decuplet baryon states in the loops, with the caveat that the short-distance low-energy constants are estimated using the vector meson dominance model rather than being fully determined from first principles.

## Method

The authors employed heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) to compute the charge (E0), magnetic dipole (M1), electric quadrupole (E2), and magnetic octupole (M3) form factors of the decuplet baryons up to one-loop order (𝒪(p³)). They included contributions from both intermediate octet and decuplet baryon states in the loop diagrams, using dimensional regularization and the modified minimal subtraction scheme to handle divergences. The short-distance low-energy constants were estimated using the vector meson dominance model, while the long-range parts were fixed by the loop corrections.

## Key numbers

- The Ω⁻ magnetic moment is measured experimentally as μ_Ω⁻ = (-2.02 ± 0.05) μ_N (from PDG).
- The Δ⁺⁺ magnetic moment was extracted experimentally as μ_Δ⁺⁺ = (5.6 ± 1.9) μ_N.
- The Δ⁺ magnetic moment was extracted experimentally as μ_Δ⁺ = (2.7 ± 3.5) μ_N.
- The octet-decuplet mass splitting used is δ = -0.2937 GeV in the isospin symmetry limit.
- The pseudoscalar meson masses used are m_π = 0.140 GeV, m_K = 0.494 GeV, and m_η = 0.550 GeV.
- The averaged octet baryon mass used is M_B = 1.158 GeV; the averaged decuplet baryon mass used is M_T = 1.452 GeV.
- The φ𝒩𝒯 coupling constant is 𝒞 = -1.2 ± 0.1; the φ𝒯𝒯 coupling constant is ℋ = -2.2 ± 0.6 (from Butler (1992)).

## Context

This work extends previous chiral perturbation theory calculations of decuplet baryon electromagnetic properties, which were performed only to next-to-leading order (𝒪(p²)) by Butler (1993) and others, by including the next-to-next-leading order (𝒪(p³)) corrections. The results are intended to provide chiral extrapolation formulas for lattice QCD simulations of decuplet baryon electromagnetic form factors, similar to how Shanahan (2014) improved lattice results for octet baryons. The calculations also complement experimental efforts at MAMI and Jefferson Laboratory to measure magnetic moments of short-lived baryon resonances.
