# Compatibility of Correlation Dynamics of SU(N) Gauge Theories and Gauss Law in Temporal Gauge

> A constraint correlation dynamics up to 4-point Green's functions is proposed for SU(N) gauge theories which reduces the N-body quantum field problem to the two-body level. The resulting set of nonlinear coupled equations fulfills all conservation laws including baryon number, linear and angular momenta as well as the total energy. Apart from the conservation laws in the space-time degrees of freedom the Gauss law is conserved identically for all times. The constraint dynamical equations are highly non-perturbative and thus applicable also in the strong coupling regime as e.g., low-energy QCD problems.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a constraint correlation dynamics for SU(N) gauge theories, truncated at the level of 4-point Green's functions, and shows that this truncation scheme is compatible with a "weak" form of the Gauss law (imposed as a quantum expectation value rather than an operator condition on physical states), provided the initial conditions obey the gauge constraints.

## Method

The authors start from the Hamiltonian formulation of SU(N) gauge theory in temporal gauge and derive equations of motion for equal-time Green's functions from the Heisenberg equations using canonical commutation relations. They truncate the infinite hierarchy of coupled equations via a cluster expansion, keeping only up to 4-point connected Green's functions. The Gauss law is imposed in a weak form as a vanishing expectation value of the Gauss operator on physical states, and the authors show algebraically that this condition is conserved in time and compatible with the truncation scheme, using the Lie algebra structure of the Gauss operators and their commutation with the Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

* The truncation scheme is performed at order n+1, meaning all (n+1)-point correlation Green's functions are set to zero; for the Gauss law operator g^a(x), which contains 1- and 2-gluon-field and 2 quark-field operators, this implies a correlation dynamics up to 4-point Green's functions obeys the weak Gauss law.
* The bare vertices in non-abelian gauge theories involve at most four fields (4-gluon interaction), making truncation after the 4-point functions natural.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic two-body correlation dynamics previously developed for nuclear physics (Wang, Cassing, and others, 1990s) to non-abelian gauge theories, specifically SU(N) QCD. It contrasts with the semi-classical, abelian approximation of the transport theory based on gauge covariant Wigner functions proposed by Elze et al. (1980s), which corresponds to lowest-order perturbation theory. The approach enables non-perturbative studies of low-energy QCD problems, such as confinement and the deconfinement transition, and provides a framework for variational calculations using trial wave functions like those based on the chromomagnetic field (Greensite and others, 1980s), though the authors note that numerical solution of the lengthy equations and renormalization of singularities remain open problems.
