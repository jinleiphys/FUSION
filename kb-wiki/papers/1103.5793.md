# Mixed-Spin Pairing Condensates in Heavy Nuclei

> We show that the Bogoliubov-de Gennes equations for nuclear ground-state wave functions support solutions in which the condensate has a mixture of spin-singlet and spin-triplet pairing. We find that such mixed-spin condensates do not occur when there are equal numbers of neutrons and protons, but only when there is an isospin imbalance. Using a phenomenological Hamiltonian, we predict that such nuclei may occur in the physical region within the proton dripline. We also solve the Bogoliubov-de Gennes equations with variable constraints on the spin-singlet and spin-triplet pairing amplitudes. For nuclei that exhibit this new pairing behavior, the resulting energy surface can be rather soft, suggesting that there may be low-lying excitations associated with the spin mixing.

# Mixed-Spin Pairing Condensates in Heavy Nuclei

## Key claim

The Bogoliubov-de Gennes equations for nuclear ground-state wave functions support solutions with a mixture of spin-singlet and spin-triplet pairing, but only when there is an isospin imbalance (N ≠ Z); such mixed-spin condensates do not occur when neutron and proton numbers are equal. The authors predict that these mixed-spin nuclei may occur in the physical region within the proton dripline, suggesting experimental relevance.

## Method

The authors solved the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equations using a gradient method with 8 simultaneous constraining fields: 2 for neutron and proton particle numbers, and 6 for the pairing amplitudes corresponding to the distinct spin-isospin channels. The Hamiltonian employed a contact two-body interaction with spin-triplet and spin-singlet strengths fitted to phenomenological shell-model Hamiltonians, within a spherical Wood-Saxon basis. For odd-A nuclei, the calculation imposed odd number parity on one block of the Hamiltonian, testing each block to find the global energy minimum.

## Key numbers

- Correlation energy threshold for "practically no pairing": E_corr < 0.5 MeV (blank squares in Fig. 1)
- Mass number A=132 examined in detail for three nuclei: 132Nd (Z=60), 132Gd (Z=64), 132Dy (Z=66)
- For 132Nd (spin-singlet): peaks near (x,y) ≈ (±20, 0) in the κ₁-κ₅ amplitude plane
- For 132Dy (spin-triplet, N=Z): peaks at (x,y) ≈ (0, ±22)
- For 132Gd (mixed-spin, N-Z=4): maxima at (x,y) ≈ (±11, ±20)
- Typical pairing gaps in normally paired nuclei: 0.7-0.9 MeV
- Pairing gaps drop to 0.25 MeV for some spin-triplet cases (threshold for "small" gaps: Δ_o^(3)(n) < 0.4 MeV)
- Neutron numbers mapped: 50 to 75, with Z ≤ N

## Context

This work extends prior studies of spin-triplet (T=0) neutron-proton pairing near the N=Z line, which was suggested long ago by Goodman (1972, 1999) and discussed experimentally by Macchiavelli et al. (2000a, 2000b). The authors build on their own earlier work (Bertsch and Luo, 2010) which found spin-triplet pairing in very large N=Z nuclei beyond the proton dripline. The present results contradict findings by Sanchez Solano et al. (1997) and Terasaki et al. (1998) that states of different pairing character do not mix strongly, and also differ from Sanchez Solano et al. (2001) who reported a sudden phase transition as a function of N-Z. The smooth crossover found here enables predictions for experimentally accessible nuclei and suggests low-lying excitations associated with spin mixing, though the authors note that mean-field theory must be extended to restore broken symmetries (particle number, angular momentum) before spectroscopic predictions can be made.
