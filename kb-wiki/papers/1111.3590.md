# Transverse emission of isospin ratios as a probe of high-density symmetry energy in isotopic nuclear reactions

> Transverse emission of preequilibrium nucleons, light clusters (complex particles) and charged pions from the isotopic $^{112,124}$Sn+$^{112,124}$Sn reactions at a beam energy of 400\emph{A} MeV, to extract the high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy, are investigated within an isospin and momentum dependent transport model. Specifically, the double ratios of neutron/proton, triton/helium-3 and $π^{-}/π^{+}$ in the squeeze-out domain are analyzed systematically, which have the advantage of reducing the influence of the Coulomb force and less systematic errors. It is found that the transverse momentum distribution of isospin ratios strongly depend on the stiffness of nuclear symmetry energy, which would be a nice observable to extract the high-density symmetry energy. The collision centrality and the mass splitting of neutron and proton in nuclear medium play a significant role on the distribution structure of the ratios, but does not change the influence of symmetry energy on the spectrum.

# Transverse emission of isospin ratios as a probe of high-density symmetry energy in isotopic nuclear reactions

## Key claim
The transverse momentum distributions of double ratios of neutron/proton, triton/helium-3, and π⁻/π⁺ in the squeeze-out domain of isotopic Sn+Sn reactions at 400A MeV are sensitive to the stiffness of the nuclear symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities, but the collision centrality and the mass splitting of neutron and proton in nuclear medium do not change the influence of symmetry energy on the spectrum.

## Method
The Lanzhou quantum molecular dynamics (LQMD) transport model, which includes isospin- and momentum-dependent mean-field potentials, Skyrme-type momentum-dependent interactions, and inelastic channels for baryon-baryon and meson-baryon collisions with resonance decays, was used to simulate ¹²⁴Sn+¹²⁴Sn and ¹¹²Sn+¹¹²Sn reactions at 400A MeV. The double ratios of neutron/proton, triton/helium-3, and π⁻/π⁺ were calculated in the mid-rapidity bin (|y/y_proj| < 0.3) for different collision centralities and compared for hard (γ_s=2), linear (γ_s=1), soft (γ_s=0.5), and supersoft symmetry energies, with two mass-splitting scenarios (m*_n > m*_p and m*_n < m*_p).

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: 400A MeV for ¹¹²,¹²⁴Sn+¹¹²,¹²⁴Sn reactions.
- The symmetry energy at saturation density is fixed at 31.5 MeV for all stiffness cases.
- The double ratio of free nucleons varies by about 5% to 10% depending on the symmetry energy stiffness, particularly at low transverse momentum in near-central collisions.
- The compression modulus of symmetric nuclear matter is set to K = 230 MeV.
- The effective mass at saturation density for symmetric nuclear matter is m*/m = 0.75.
- The bulk parameters used are α = -296.6 MeV, β = 197 MeV, and γ = 1.143.

## Context
This work extends prior transport model studies of symmetry energy at subnuclear densities from Fermi-energy collisions (e.g., Famiano et al. (2006) at 50 MeV/A at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory) and earlier IBUU04 calculations by Li et al. (2006) that investigated double neutron/proton ratios at higher energies. The LQMD model results are compared with stochastic mean-field (SMF) calculations by Gaitanos et al. (2010) and show consistency with IBUU04 except at high transverse momentum. The findings contradict the results of Yong et al. (2006) regarding the sign of the symmetry energy effect on double π⁻/π⁺ ratios. The work enables experimental constraints on the high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy, which has applications to neutron star structure, protoneutron star cooling, and supernova nucleosynthesis.
