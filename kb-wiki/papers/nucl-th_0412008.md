# Some electromagnetic properties of the nucleon from Relativistic Chiral Effective Field Theory

> Considering the magnetic moment and polarizabilities of the nucleon we emphasize the need for relativistic chiral EFT calculations. Our relativistic calculations are done via the forward-Compton-scattering sum rules, thus ensuring the correct analytic properties. The results obtained in this way are equivalent to the usual loop calculations, provided no heavy-baryon expansion or any other manipulations which lead to a different analytic structure (e.g., infrared regularization) are made. The Baldin sum rule can directly be applied to calculate the sum of nucleon polarizabilities. In contrast, the GDH sum rule is practically unsuitable for calculating the magnetic moments. The breakthrough is achieved by taking the derivatives of the sum rule with respect to the anomalous magnetic moment. As an example, we apply the derivative of the GDH sum rule to the calculation of the magnetic moment in QED and reproduce the famous Schwinger's correction from a tree-level cross-section calcualation. As far as the nucleon properties are concerned, we focus on two issues: 1) chiral behavior of the nucleon magnetic moment and 2) reconciliation of the chiral loop and $Œî$-resonance contributions to the nucleon magnetic polarizability.

# Some electromagnetic properties of the nucleon from Relativistic Chiral Effective Field Theory

## Key claim
Relativistic chiral effective field theory calculations of nucleon magnetic moments and polarizabilities, performed via forward-Compton-scattering sum rules, yield results equivalent to standard loop calculations only when no heavy-baryon expansion or infrared regularization is applied; the derivative of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule with respect to the anomalous magnetic moment provides a linear relation between the magnetic moment and photoabsorption cross-section, enabling calculation of Schwinger's correction from tree-level cross-sections alone.

## Method
The author computes forward Compton scattering amplitudes using dispersion relations and the optical theorem, relating the real parts of the scalar amplitudes to integrals over total photoabsorption cross-sections. For the nucleon magnetic moment, a "linearized" GDH sum rule is derived by introducing a trial Pauli term in the Lagrangian and taking derivatives with respect to the anomalous magnetic moment, then evaluating tree-level pion photoproduction cross-sections. For nucleon polarizabilities, the Baldin sum rule is applied to relativistic one-loop calculations of single-pion photoproduction cross-sections, with the Delta-resonance contribution added via an effective gamma N Delta Lagrangian.

## Context
This work extends the relativistic chiral EFT approach to nucleon electromagnetic properties, contrasting with the heavy-baryon ChPT calculations of Bernard et al. and the infrared-regularized covariant calculations of Kubis and Meissner (KuM). The author shows that the relativistic one-loop results for magnetic moments exhibit correct large-m_œÄ behavior (scaling as 1/m_quark), unlike the infrared-regularized results which diverge at m_œÄ = 2M, making them better suited for chiral extrapolation of lattice QCD data from Zanotti et al. (2004). For polarizabilities, the relativistic calculation resolves the "polarizability puzzle" where heavy-baryon ChPT without the Delta agrees with experiment but including the Delta worsens agreement; the relativistic result naturally accommodates the Delta contribution, bringing the sum of polarizabilities into better agreement with the Baldin sum rule values. This enables improved chiral extrapolations of lattice data and a consistent treatment of resonance contributions in chiral EFT.
