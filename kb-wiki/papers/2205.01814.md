# Enhanced moments of inertia for rotation in neutron-rich nuclei

> Ground-state moments of inertia (MoI) are investigated for about 1700 even-even nuclei from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line up to $Z=120$ and $N=184$. The cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equation is solved in the coordinate space. This model describes well the available experimental data of more than 300 nuclides possessing an appreciable deformation. I find that the MoI greatly increase near the drip line whereas the deformation is not as strong as estimated by the empirical relation. Systematic measurements of the excitation energy and the transition probability to the first $I^π=2^+$ state in neutron-rich nuclei not only reveal the evolution of deformation but can also constrain an effective pair interaction.

## Key claim

The moments of inertia (MoI) greatly increase near the neutron drip line, but this enhancement is not primarily due to stronger deformation; instead, it arises from a combination of deformed-shell effects and the isovector-density dependence of the pairing functional, which effectively weakens the pair interaction in highly asymmetric nuclei.

## Method

The cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) equation was solved in three-dimensional coordinate space with a Cartesian mesh (mesh size 1.0 fm, 12 points per direction) and box boundary condition, using the SkM* and SLy4 energy density functionals augmented by the Yamagami-Shimizu-Nakatsukasa (YSN) pairing-density functional. The MoI was evaluated in the zero-frequency limit as the ratio of the angular momentum expectation value to the rotational frequency, at ω_rot = 0.05 MeV. Calculations were performed for about 1700 even-even nuclei from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line up to Z=120 and N=184, with quasiparticle energy cutoff at 60 MeV.

## Key numbers

- MoI calculated for about 1700 even-even nuclei; comparison with experimental data for 362 nuclei (SkM*) and 375 nuclei (SLy4) with deformation β > 0.1
- For SkM* functional: average ratio R = J_th/J_exp = 1.02 (1.07 excluding weakly deformed nuclei with β < 0.1); dispersion σ = 0.09
- For SLy4 functional: average ratio R = J_th/J_exp = 1.16 (1.15 excluding weakly deformed nuclei); dispersion σ = 0.12
- For E(2+) comparison with 332 deformed nuclei (SkM*+YSN): average R_E = -0.021, standard deviation σ_E = 0.11
- For deformation β comparison with 219 nuclei (SkM*) with β > 0.1: average R_β = -0.08, dispersion σ = 0.07
- MoI in neutron-rich Dy isotopes with N ~ 150 is about twice as large as in the N ~ 100 region, despite similar proton deformation
- Mixed-type pairing gives almost 50% reduction of MoI near the drip line compared to YSN pairing for Dy isotopes
- Total computational cost: about 17000 node hours on SQUID computer facility (Intel Xeon Platinum 8368 processors)

## Context

This work extends systematic cranking-model calculations of moments of inertia to the neutron drip line, where previous studies were mostly restricted to axially symmetric nuclei (Teran 2003, Blazkiewicz 2005, Yoshida 2008, Oba 2008, Pei 2008, Kashiwaba 2021). The results contradict the empirical relation between E(2+) and B(E2) found by Raman (2001) for highly asymmetric nuclei (α > 0.3). The findings enable systematic measurements of E(2_1+) and B(E2) in neutron-rich nuclei to constrain the density dependence of the pairing effective interaction, and explain the significant lowering of E(2_1+) observed in the near-drip-line nucleus 40Mg (Crawford 2019).
