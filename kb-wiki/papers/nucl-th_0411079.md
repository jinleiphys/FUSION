# Anatomy of three-body decay II. Decay mechanism and resonance structure

> We use the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method to discuss the the two mechanisms of sequential and direct three-body decay. Both short-range and Coulomb interactions are included. Resonances are assumed initially populated by a process independent of the subsequent decay. The lowest adiabatic potentials describe the resonances rather accurately at distances smaller than the outer turning point of the confining barrier. We illustrate with realistic examples of nuclei from neutron ($^{6}$He) and proton ($^{17}$Ne) driplines as well as excited states of beta-stable nuclei ($^{12}$C).

## Key claim

The hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method can distinguish between sequential and direct three-body decay mechanisms by analyzing the structure of the wave function along the decay path, but the distinction depends on model interpretation and is not always unambiguous, for example, the decay of the 2+ state in 6He appears compatible with direct decay despite the presence of a two-body resonance in the 5He subsystem.

## Method

The authors solve the Faddeev equations using a hyperspherical adiabatic expansion, where for fixed hyperradius ρ they first solve the angular Faddeev equations to obtain adiabatic potentials, then expand the total wave function on these angular eigenfunctions. The coupled hyperradial equations are solved with complex rotation (ρ → ρ exp(iθ)) to obtain resonance energies and widths as complex S-matrix poles. The decay mechanism is inferred from the probability distributions and the evolution of angular-momentum components of the wave function as the hyperradius increases from small to large distances.

## Key numbers

- 6He(2+): computed resonance energy 0.82 MeV, width 0.12 MeV (experimental: 0.84 MeV, 0.09 MeV); WKB estimate from lowest adiabatic potential gives 0.19 MeV
- 12C(0+1): computed energy 0.33 MeV, width 2×10−5 MeV (experimental: 0.37 MeV, 8×10−5 MeV); WKB width 6×10−5 MeV
- 12C(0+2): computed energy 4.3 MeV, width 0.64 MeV (experimental: 3.0±0.3 MeV, 3±0.7 MeV); WKB width 0.4 MeV
- 17Ne(3/2−): resonance energy 0.34 MeV, WKB width 3.6×10−12 MeV (experimental upper limit <2.5×10−11 MeV)
- 17Ne(5/2−): resonance energy 0.82 MeV, WKB width 1.3×10−10 MeV (experimental lower limit >3×10−10 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the schematic model of Garrido (2005) by including realistic short-range and Coulomb interactions for three-body decays of nuclear resonances. It provides a framework to interpret experimental data that are typically analyzed assuming sequential decay via R-matrix theory (Barker 2003) or through two-body sequential emission (Brown 2003). The results enable future studies of two-proton radioactivity and the role of virtual sequential decay through energetically inaccessible intermediate states, as seen in 17Ne.
