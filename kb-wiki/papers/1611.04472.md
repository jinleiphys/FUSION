# Effects of sterile neutrino and extra-dimension on big bang nucleosynthesis

> By assuming the existence of extra-dimensional sterile neutrinos in big bang nucleosynthesis (BBN) epoch, we investigate the sterile neutrino ($ν_{\rm s}$) effects on the BBN and constrain some parameters associated with the $ν_{\rm s}$ properties. First, for cosmic expansion rate, we take into account effects of a five-dimensional bulk and intrinsic tension of the brane embedded in the bulk, and constrain a key parameter of the extra dimension by using the observational element abundances. Second, effects of the $ν_{\rm s}$ traveling on or off the brane are considered. In this model, the effective mixing angle between a $ν_{\rm s}$ and an active neutrino depends on energy, which may give rise to a resonance effect on the mixing angle. Consequently, reaction rate of the $ν_{\rm s}$ can be drastically changed during the cosmic evolution. We estimated abundances and temperature of the $ν_{\rm s}$ by solving the rate equation as a function of temperature until the sterile neutrino decoupling. We then find that the relic abundance of the $ν_{\rm s}$ is drastically enhanced by the extra-dimension and maximized for a characteristic resonance energy $E_{\rm res}\gtrsim 0.01$ GeV. Finally, some constraints related to the $ν_{\rm s}$, mixing angle and mass difference, are discussed in detail with the comparison of our BBN calculations corrected by the extra-dimensional $ν_{\rm s}$ to observational data on light element abundances.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether an extra-dimensional sterile neutrino can be produced abundantly enough during big bang nucleosynthesis to affect primordial light element abundances, finding that the relic abundance of the sterile neutrino is drastically enhanced by the extra dimension and maximized for a characteristic resonance energy \(E_{\rm res}\gtrsim 0.01\) GeV, but only under the condition that the sterile neutrino production rate is large enough relative to the cosmic expansion rate.

## Method

The authors adopt a five-dimensional brane-world model where a sterile neutrino can travel on or off the brane, with the effective mixing angle between sterile and active neutrinos modified by both the extra-dimensional shortcut parameter and the matter effect (MSW-like). They solve the rate equation for the sterile neutrino number density from 100 GeV down to 1 MeV, using a flavor-change probability derived from a wave-packet formalism, and then incorporate the resulting sterile neutrino energy density into the modified Friedmann equation to compute BBN light-element yields. The cosmic expansion rate itself is also modified by the extra-dimensional bulk term \(\mathcal{E}/a^4\), and both the expansion rate and the sterile neutrino abundance are constrained by comparing calculated D and \(^4\)He abundances to observational data.

## Key numbers

* The extra-dimensional parameter \(\mathcal{E}/a_{\rm i}^4\) is constrained to \(120 < \mathcal{E}/a_{\rm i}^4\ ({\rm s}^{-2}) < 149\) at the \(2\sigma\) level from combined D and \(^4\)He abundances.
* The resonance energy for maximal sterile neutrino production satisfies \(E_{\rm res} \gtrsim 0.01\) GeV.
* The equilibrium temperature where scattering and wave-packet overlap time scales equal is \(T_{\rm eq} = 44\ {\rm MeV}\ (\delta m^2 / 1\ {\rm eV}^2)^{1/6}\).
* For a sterile neutrino mass of \(m_{\nu_{\rm s}} = 1\) eV, the final abundance \(Y_{\nu_{\rm s}}\) is nearly proportional to \(\theta^2\) for small mixing angles, with equilibrium reached for \(\theta = 0.1\) but not for \(\theta = 0.01\) or \(0.001\) at \(E_{\rm res} = 0.1\) GeV.
* A discontinuity in the abundance contours occurs at a critical resonance energy \(E_{\rm res,cr} \approx 7\) MeV, corresponding to the condition \(T_{{\rm res},1} = T_{\rm eq} = 44\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the extra-dimensional sterile neutrino model of Pas et al. (2005) by performing a detailed numerical BBN calculation that includes both the modified cosmic expansion rate from the five-dimensional bulk (following Ichiki (2002) and Sasankan (2016)) and the energy density of the sterile neutrino itself. The authors find that the observational \(^4\)He abundance can be better explained by the sterile neutrino model than by standard BBN alone, and they constrain the extra-dimensional parameter \(\mathcal{E}\) and the sterile neutrino mixing parameters. This enables future comparisons with IceCube constraints on sterile neutrino parameter space and provides a cosmological test for brane-world scenarios.
