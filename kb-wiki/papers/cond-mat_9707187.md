# Quantum Versus Classical Decay Laws in Open Chaotic Systems

> We study analytically the time evolution in decaying chaotic systems and discuss in detail the hierarchy of characteristic time scales that appeared in the quasiclassical region. There exist two quantum time scales: the Heisenberg time t_H and the time t_q=t_H/\sqrt{κT} (with κ>> 1 and T being the degree of resonance overlapping and the transmission coefficient respectively) associated with the decay. If t_q < t_H the quantum deviation from the classical decay law starts at the time t_q and are due to the openness of the system. Under the opposite condition quantum effects in intrinsic evolution begin to influence the decay at the time t_H. In this case we establish the connection between quantities which describe the time evolution in an open system and their closed counterparts.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates analytically that in open chaotic quantum systems, a new quantum time scale \(t_q = t_H / \sqrt{\kappa T}\) (where \(\kappa \gg 1\) is the resonance overlapping parameter and \(T\) is the transmission coefficient) governs the onset of quantum deviations from the classical exponential decay law, but only when \(t_q < t_H\); under the opposite condition (\(\kappa T \ll 1\)), quantum effects in the intrinsic evolution begin at the Heisenberg time \(t_H\) instead.

## Method

The authors employ the random matrix approach and the formalism of the effective non-Hermitian Hamiltonian to compute the two-point correlator of the Green's operator, from which the leakage of norm \(P(t)\) inside an open chaotic system is derived. They perform both a diagonal approximation using the known distribution of resonance widths for the unitary ensemble (broken time-reversal symmetry) and an exact calculation using supersymmetry techniques, analyzing the resulting integrals in different regimes of the ratio \(t_q / t_H\).

## Key numbers

- The Heisenberg time is \(t_H = 2\pi\rho\), where \(\rho\) is the mean level density (with \(\hbar = 1\)).
- The quantum time scale associated with decay is \(t_q = \sqrt{\kappa/T}\, t_W = t_H / \sqrt{\kappa T}\), where \(t_W \equiv 1/\Gamma_W\) is the characteristic lifetime and \(\Gamma_W = MT / 2\pi\rho\) is the Weisskopf width (with \(M\) the number of open decay channels).
- The classical decay probability is \(P_{cl}(t) = \exp(-\Gamma_W t)\).
- The asymptotic power law for the decay is \(P^{(as)}_d(t) = \kappa^{-1} (\Gamma_W t / M)^{-M}\).
- The relation \(t_H = \kappa\, t_W\) holds, identifying \(\kappa\) as analogous to the dimensionless conductance in mesoscopic physics.
- For the case \(\kappa T \ll 1\), the relation \(P_{open}(t) = (1 + \Gamma_W t / M)^{-M} P_{closed}(t)\) remains valid until the time \(t_f = t_W / T = t_H / \kappa T\).

## Context

This work extends the numerical findings of Casati et al. (1997) on the kicked rotor model by showing that the new time scale \(t_q\) is a general feature of open quantum chaotic systems, not specific to that model. It also corrects the conjecture in that prior work by identifying an additional factor \(\sqrt{T}\) in the ratio \(t_W / t_q\). The results connect to the theory of resonance scattering via the effective non-Hermitian Hamiltonian formalism (VWZ, SZ89) and to the width distribution analysis of Fyodorov and Sommers (1996, 1997). The paper enables a unified understanding of quantum decay in chaotic systems, linking the overlapping parameter \(\kappa\) to the dimensionless conductance in mesoscopic physics and establishing a general relation between open and closed system time evolution.
