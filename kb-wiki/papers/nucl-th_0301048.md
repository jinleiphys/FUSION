# Kugo-Ojima confinement and QCD Green's functions in covariant gauges

> In Landau gauge QCD the Kugo-Ojima confinement criterion and its relation to the infrared behaviour of the gluon and ghost propagators are reviewed. It is demonstrated that the realization of this confinement criterion (which is closely related to the Gribov-Zwanziger horizon condition) results from quite general properties of the ghost Dyson-Schwinger equation. The numerical solutions for the gluon and ghost propagators obtained from a truncated set of Dyson--Schwinger equations provide an explicit example for the anticipated infrared behaviour. The results are in good agreement, also quantitatively, with corresponding lattice data obtained recently. The resulting running coupling approaches a fixed point in the infrared, $α(0) = 8.915/N_c$. Solutions for the coupled system of Dyson--Schwinger equations for the quark, gluon and ghost propagators are presented. Dynamical generation of quark masses and thus spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry takes place. In the quenched approximation the quark propagator functions agree well with those of corresponding lattice calculations. For a small number of light flavours the quark, gluon and ghost propagators deviate only slightly from the ones in quenched approximation. While the positivity violation of the gluon spectral function is manifest in the gluon propagator, there are no clear indications of analogous positivity violations for quarks so far.

## Key claim
The Kugo-Ojima confinement criterion and the Gribov-Zwanziger horizon condition are satisfied in Landau gauge QCD if the ghost propagator is more singular than a simple pole in the infrared, which follows from general properties of the ghost Dyson-Schwinger equation under the assumption that all Green's functions can be expanded in a power series.

## Method
The authors solved a truncated set of coupled Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) for the gluon, ghost, and quark propagators in Landau gauge, using bare three-point functions and neglecting four-gluon vertices. For the quark-gluon vertex, a factorized ansatz was employed consisting of an Abelian tensor structure multiplied by a non-Abelian factor containing the ghost renormalization function. The infrared behaviour was extracted analytically via power-law expansions, and numerical solutions were obtained for non-vanishing momenta.

## Key numbers
- Infrared exponent for ghost and gluon dressing functions: κ = (93 − √1201)/98 ≈ 0.595, giving Z(k²) ∼ (k²)^{2κ} and G(k²) ∼ (k²)^{−κ}
- Infrared fixed point of the running coupling: α(0) = 8.915/N_c, which for SU(3) gives α(0) ≈ 2.972; corrections from ghost-gluon vertex dressings give 2.5 < α(0) ≤ 2.97
- Scale parameter in the MOM scheme: Λ = 0.714 GeV for N_f = 0
- Fit parameters for running coupling: Fit A uses a₁=1.106, a₂=2.324, b₁=0.004, b₂=3.169; Fit B uses a=1.020, b=1.052
- Dynamically generated quark masses in the typical phenomenological range of 300–400 MeV

## Context
This work extends the DSE approach of von Smekal (1997) by using a simpler truncation scheme that still reproduces the correct one-loop anomalous dimensions and infrared exponents. The results agree quantitatively with lattice calculations by Langfeld (2001) for SU(2) Yang-Mills propagators and by Bonnet (2002) for quenched quark propagators. The paper enables future studies of deconfinement and chiral restoration at non-zero temperature and density, and suggests that for more than six light flavours the Kugo-Ojima confinement criterion may cease to be valid.
