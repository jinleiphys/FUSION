# Momentum Kick Model Description of the Ridge in (Delta-phi)-(Delta eta) Correlation in pp Collisions at 7 TeV

> The near-side ridge structure in the (Delta phi)-(Delta eta) correlation observed by the CMS Collaboration for pp collisions at 7 TeV at LHC can be explained by the momentum kick model in which the ridge particles are medium partons that suffer a collision with the jet and acquire a momentum kick along the jet direction. Similar to the early medium parton momentum distribution obtained in previous analysis for nucleus-nucleus collisions at 0.2 TeV, the early medium parton momentum distribution in pp collisions at 7 TeV exhibits a rapidity plateau as arising from particle production in a flux tube.

## Key claim

The near-side ridge structure in pp collisions at 7 TeV can be explained by the momentum kick model, where ridge particles are medium partons that suffer a collision with a jet and acquire a momentum kick along the jet direction; the model assumes that the early medium parton momentum distribution exhibits a rapidity plateau arising from particle production in a flux tube, similar to what was previously found for nucleus-nucleus collisions at 0.2 TeV.

## Method

The author applies the momentum kick model, previously developed for nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC, to pp collisions at 7 TeV. The model treats the correlated particles as arising from jet-(medium parton) collisions: a jet imparts a momentum kick q to a medium parton, changing its momentum from p_i to p = p_i + q, and the kicked medium partons subsequently materialize as observed ridge hadrons via parton-hadron duality. The normalized initial medium parton momentum distribution is extracted from previous STAR, PHENIX, and PHOBOS data and parametrized with a temperature parameter T_MP and a fall-off parameter a, and the total associated particle distribution is the sum of the ridge component (NJF-NKMP) and the jet fragment component (NJF-NJF).

## Key numbers

- The peak of the p_T distribution of medium partons kicked by the near-side jet lies at p_T ~ 1 GeV/c for a high-p_T trigger in AuAu collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 0.2 TeV.
- The jet fragment number N_JF = 0.15 + (0.10 / GeV/c) * p_t^trig for pp collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 0.2 TeV.
- The jet fragment temperature T_JF = 0.19 GeV + 0.06 * p_t^trig for pp collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 0.2 TeV.
- The width parameter sigma_phi of the jet fragment cone is parametrized as sigma_phi = sigma_phi0 * m_a / sqrt(m_a^2 + p_t^2) with m_a = 1.1 GeV.
- The ridge yield is greatest at 1 < p_T < 3 GeV/c in the CMS pp data at 7 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the momentum kick model, previously applied successfully to extensive sets of triggered associated particle data from the STAR, PHENIX, and PHOBOS Collaborations for AuAu collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 0.2 TeV (Wong 2007, 2008, 2009), to the new CMS observation of a ridge in pp collisions at 7 TeV. The model provides a unified description of ridges in pp and PbPb collisions at LHC and in AA collisions at RHIC, and it explains puzzling features in STAR minimum-bias pair correlation data (Adams 2006, Trainor 2008, Daugherity 2008) such as the persistence of (p_T1, p_T2) correlations centering at p_T ~ 1 GeV as a function of centrality. The analysis enables extraction of the early medium parton momentum distribution and the number of kicked medium partons per jet from ridge yield data.
