# An extended Vlasov equation with pairing correlations

> A simplified version of the Wigner--transformed time--dependent Hartree--Fock--Bogoliubov equations, leading to a solvable model for finite systems of fermions with pairing correlations, is introduced. In this model, pairing correlations result in a coupling of the Vlasov--type equation for the normal phase--space density with that for the imaginary part of the anomalous density. The effect of pairing correlations on the linear response of the system is studied for a finite one--dimensional system and an explicit expression for the correlated propagator is given.

# An extended Vlasov equation with pairing correlations

## Key claim

The paper introduces a simplified, solvable model for finite fermion systems with pairing correlations by deriving a coupled system of Vlasov-type equations for the normal phase-space density and the imaginary part of the anomalous density, but this approximation violates particle-number conservation, which can be remedied by a modified propagator that excludes the n=0 mode.

## Method

The authors start from the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) equations, take their Wigner transform, and introduce a phenomenological constant pairing gap Δ (replacing the self-consistent pairing field) to obtain coupled linearized equations for the fluctuations δρ and δκ_i. They solve these equations exactly for a one-dimensional finite system with fixed-surface boundary conditions, neglecting mean-field fluctuations (single-particle approximation), and derive an explicit expression for the correlated propagator.

## Key numbers

- The phenomenological pairing gap is taken as Δ ≈ 1 MeV, typical for heavy nuclei.
- The chemical potential μ is set to 33 MeV for the equilibrium density plots.
- For a system with ħω₀(μ) = 10 MeV, the first peak in the correlated response is shifted relative to the uncorrelated δ-function peak at ħω = 10 MeV.
- For a system with ħω₀(μ) = 1 MeV, the correlated n=1 mode appears at ħω̃_{n=1}(μ) ≈ 2.23 MeV.
- The energy-weighted sum rule for the correlated system is M̃₁ ≈ M₁[1 + 14.66(Δ/ħω₀)²], where M₁ = (2/3)ħ²AL²/m is the uncorrelated sum rule.
- The correlated response function displays a gap of approximately 2Δ at low excitation energy.
- The numerical smearing parameter ε = 0.1 MeV is used in the response calculations.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical Vlasov approach to superfluid systems, building on the static semiclassical HFB treatment of Bengtsson and Schuck and the earlier work on normal systems by Brink, Dellafiore, and Matera (2000). The one-dimensional model serves as a preliminary step toward three-dimensional spherical systems, and the authors note that more sophisticated moving-surface boundary conditions (as in Abrosimov et al., 2005) could be considered in future work. The model enables study of how pairing correlations modify the linear response of finite fermion systems, including the appearance of an energy gap and the shift of collective mode frequencies.
