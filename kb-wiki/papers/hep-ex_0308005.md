# Modeling Quasi-elastic Form Factors for Electron and Neutrino Scattering

> We calculate the total and differential quasielastic cross sections for neutrino and antineutrino scattering on nucleons using up to date fits to the nucleon elastic electromagnetic form factors $G_E^p$, $G_E^n$, $G_M^p$, $G_M^n$, and weak and pseudoscalar form factors. We find that using the updated non-zero value of $G_E^n$ has a significant effect on both the total and differential neutrino and antineutrino quasielastic cross sections. Previous extractions of the weak axial form factor from neutrino scattering data are sensitive to the assumptions that were used for the vector form factors. We perform a re-analysis of previous neutrino data using updated form factors and obtain updated value of the axial vector mass.

## Key claim

Using updated nucleon electromagnetic form factors (the "BBA-2003" parameterization) changes predicted neutrino and antineutrino quasielastic cross sections by up to 6% compared to the standard dipole approximation, and reduces the extracted axial-vector mass \(M_A\) from previous deuterium bubble chamber data by 0.025 GeV, bringing it into better agreement with the value predicted from pion electroproduction.

## Method

The authors fit electron scattering cross section data (and, for some fits, polarization transfer data from Jefferson Lab Hall A) to inverse-polynomial parameterizations of the proton and neutron electric and magnetic form factors \(G_E^p\), \(G_M^p\), \(G_M^n\), and used the Krutov et al. (2002) parameterization for \(G_E^n\). They then computed quasielastic neutrino and antineutrino cross sections on free nucleons using the conserved vector current (CVC) hypothesis to relate electromagnetic and weak vector form factors, and re-analyzed \(Q^2\) distributions from four previous deuterium bubble chamber experiments (Baker et al. 1981, Barish et al. 1977, Miller et al. 1982, Kitagaki et al. 1983) to extract updated values of the axial-vector mass \(M_A\).

## Key numbers

* The BBA-2003 form factors yield a neutrino quasielastic cross section that is 3% higher than the dipole approximation at high energy and up to 6% higher at 1 GeV neutrino energy.
* The shift in extracted \(M_A\) from deuterium experiments due to updated form factors and \(g_A\) is \(-0.025\) GeV (from the published average of 1.026 \(\pm\) 0.020 GeV to a corrected value of 1.001 \(\pm\) 0.020 GeV).
* The shift in \(M_A\) when going from the dipole approximation to BBA-2003 form factors, keeping \(g_A\) fixed, is \(-0.050\) GeV.
* The world average \(M_A\) from neutrino experiments is 1.026 \(\pm\) 0.020 GeV; the value from pion electroproduction, after a 0.055 GeV correction, is 1.014 \(\pm\) 0.016 GeV.
* Using BBA-2003 form factors with \(M_A = 1.00\) GeV versus dipole form factors with \(M_A = 1.11\) GeV (as in the initial K2K analysis) yields cross section differences as large as 12%.
* The pseudoscalar form factor \(F_P\) contributes at the 5% level for tau neutrino interactions, but is negligible for muon neutrinos except below 0.2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous extractions of the axial form factor from neutrino scattering (Baker et al. 1981, Barish et al. 1977, Miller et al. 1982, Kitagaki et al. 1983) by incorporating modern electron scattering measurements of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors, including the non-zero electric form factor of the neutron. It contradicts the common assumption that the dipole approximation with \(G_E^n = 0\) is sufficient for neutrino quasielastic calculations. The updated form factors enable more accurate predictions for neutrino oscillation experiments (e.g., K2K, Fermilab programs) and motivate further study of nuclear corrections, such as those from Fermi gas models (Smith and Moniz 1972) or bound-nucleon form factor modifications (Tsushima et al. 2003).
