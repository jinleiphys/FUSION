# Fully Microscopic Calculations for Closed-Shell Nuclei with Realistic Nucleon-Nucleon Potentials

> The ground-state energy of the doubly magic nuclei 4He and 16O has been calculated within the framework of the Goldstone expansion starting from modern nucleon-nucleon potentials. A low-momentum potential V-low-k has been derived from the bare potential by integrating out its high-momentum components beyond a cutoff Lambda. We have employed a simple criterion to relate this cutoff momentum to a boundary condition for the two-nucleon model space spanned by a harmonic-oscillator basis. Convergence of the results has been obtained with a limited number of oscillator quanta.

# Fully Microscopic Calculations for Closed-Shell Nuclei with Realistic Nucleon-Nucleon Potentials

## Key claim

The ground-state energy of doubly magic nuclei can be calculated reliably using a low-momentum potential derived from realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials within the Goldstone expansion, provided the cutoff momentum is consistently mapped to a boundary condition for the harmonic-oscillator model space. The approach yields converged results with a limited number of oscillator quanta, though the agreement with experiment for 16O depends on the choice of potential, with discrepancies of 11 MeV for CD-Bonn and smaller for Bonn A.

## Method

The authors start from realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (CD-Bonn, N3LO, and Bonn A) and derive a low-momentum potential V-low-k by integrating out high-momentum components beyond a cutoff momentum Lambda. They map this cutoff to a maximum number of harmonic-oscillator quanta N_max using the relation (N_max + 3/2) hbar omega = hbar^2 Lambda^2 / M, then construct a Hartree-Fock basis and sum the Goldstone expansion up to fourth order in the two-body interaction, using the Pade approximant [2|2] to estimate the converged value.

## Key numbers

- 4He ground-state energy with CD-Bonn potential: -25.92 MeV (for hbar omega = 36 MeV, N_max = 4 to 6)
- 4He ground-state energy with N3LO potential: (-25.02 +/- 0.05) MeV
- 4He ground-state energy with Bonn A potential: (-27.78 +/- 0.03) MeV
- Largest discrepancy between Goldstone expansion and Faddeev-Yakubovsky results for 4He: 0.39 MeV (for N3LO potential)
- 16O ground-state energy with CD-Bonn potential: (-117 +/- 1) MeV (for hbar omega = 27.25 MeV), discrepancy with experimental value of 11 MeV
- 16O ground-state energy with Bonn A potential: (-130.0 +/- 0.5) MeV
- Model spaces for 16O did not exceed N_max = 9

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations (Coraggio 2003, 2005) by introducing a systematic criterion to relate the cutoff momentum Lambda to the harmonic-oscillator model space size (Coraggio 2006). The 4He results are validated against exact Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations (Gloeckle 1993, Nogga 2005). The 16O results are consistent with those obtained by Fujii et al. using the unitary model-operator approach and by Vary et al. in the no-core shell model framework. The rapid convergence with model-space size suggests the approach can be extended to heavier systems.
