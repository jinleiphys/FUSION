# Side Ridge in Ar + KCl Collisions at 1.8 GeV/nucleon with Reaction-Plane Deblurring

> Reaction-plane deblurring is applied to the triple-differential distributions of protons, deuterons and negatively charged pions from central Ar + KCl collisions at 1.8 GeV/nucleon, measured with the LBL-GSI Streamer Chamber, to yield distributions relative to the true reaction plane of the collisions. Within the reaction plane and in the forward cm rapidity region a side ridge away from the beam axis is observed: the distributions peak away from the beam direction for protons and deuterons but not for pions. These findings are consistent with a source of particles moving at an in-plane transverse velocity of ~0.1 c. Transport pBUU calculations yield results in semi-quantitative agreement with those from the data.

## Key claim

Reaction-plane deblurring applied to just 495 events from central Ar + KCl collisions at 1.8 GeV/nucleon reveals a side ridge (peak away from the beam axis) in the triple-differential distributions of protons and deuterons but not pions, consistent with a source moving at an in-plane transverse velocity of ~0.1 c. The claim is hedged by noting that the pBUU transport calculations yield only semi-quantitative agreement with the data, and that the data marginally favor a stronger momentum dependence in the nucleonic mean field.

## Method

The authors applied reaction-plane deblurring using the iterative Richardson-Lucy method to triple-differential distributions of protons, deuterons, and negatively charged pions from 495 central (b ≲ 2.4 fm) Ar + KCl collisions at 1.8 GeV/nucleon measured with the LBL-GSI Streamer Chamber. The reaction plane was estimated for each particle using a Q-vector constructed from weighted transverse momenta of all other particles, excluding the particle under consideration to avoid self-correlation. The blurring function, derived from Gaussian fluctuations of the Q-vector around its average, was characterized by ⟨Q^x⟩/σ_Q ≈ 0.80, and deblurring was applied with a regularization parameter λ = 2.4% to restore the distributions relative to the true reaction plane.

## Key numbers

- 495 events of central Ar + KCl collisions at 1.8 GeV/nucleon with impact parameter b ≲ 2.4 fm
- ⟨Q^x⟩/σ_Q = 2.33 GeV/c / 2.93 GeV/c = 0.80 for the blurring function
- Side ridge peaks at p^x/A ∼ 100 MeV/c for baryons, corresponding to a transverse collective velocity of 0.1 c
- Forward rapidity region selected: 0.18 < y^*/y_B^* < 0.82
- 16 azimuthal bins covering 360° and 200 MeV/c transverse momentum bins used in the analysis
- pBUU calculations tested with two effective mass values: m^*/m = 0.87 (weaker momentum dependence) and m^*/m = 0.65 (stronger momentum dependence) at the Fermi momentum in normal matter

## Context

This work extends the reaction-plane deblurring technique proposed by Danielewicz and Kurata-Nishimura (2022) to demonstrate the existence of a side ridge in a light system at relatively high energy, where previous work by the Plastic Ball Collaboration (Gustafsson et al., 1984) had claimed a "bounce-off" effect in 400 MeV/nucleon Nb + Nb collisions but without correcting for self-correlation biases. The analysis revisits data originally published by Stroebele et al. (1983) and Danielewicz (1985) that first turned attention to azimuthal moments relative to the reaction plane. The results enable future searches for more detailed features in triple-differential yields, such as differences between participant and spectator shapes, and suggest that improved equation-of-state resolution may be achievable for heavier collision systems with higher statistics.
