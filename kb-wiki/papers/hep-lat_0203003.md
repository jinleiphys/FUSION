# Nucleons in Two-Flavor Partially-Quenched Chiral Perturbation Theory

> Properties of the proton and the neutron are explored in partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory with two non-degenerate light flavors. Masses, magnetic moments, matrix elements of isovector twist-2 operators and axial-vector currents are computed at the one-loop level in the chiral expansion.

## Key claim

The paper computes one-loop chiral corrections to nucleon properties in partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory with two non-degenerate light flavors, but the results are explicitly conditional on the sea-quark masses and valence-quark masses being varied independently to extract low-energy constants, and the expressions only reduce to standard QCD results in the limit where all quark masses become equal.

## Method

The authors construct the SU(4|2)_L ⊗ SU(4|2)_R partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory including nucleons (from the 70-dimensional representation) and Δ-resonances (from the 44-dimensional representation) as dynamical fields. They compute one-loop contributions to nucleon masses, magnetic moments, isovector twist-2 operator matrix elements, and axial-vector currents using the heavy baryon expansion, with the singlet meson propagator treated via the Haxton–Liu formalism to handle the η-η mixing from the large m_0 limit.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant is taken as f ∼ 132 MeV throughout the calculations
- The Δ-nucleon mass splitting Δ is treated as a parameter much less than Λ_χ ∼ 1 GeV
- The proton mass one-loop contribution includes terms proportional to m_{uu}^3, m_{ud}^3, m_{ju}^3, m_{lu}^3 with coefficients involving g_A^2/3, g_1^2/12, g_A g_1/3, and g_{ΔN}^2/(9π)
- The neutron mass one-loop contribution includes terms proportional to m_{dd}^3, m_{ud}^3, m_{jd}^3, m_{ld}^3 with the same coupling structure as the proton
- In the isospin-symmetric QCD limit, the nucleon mass reduces to M_N = M_0 − 2m̄(α_M + 2σ_M) − (1/(8πf^2))[(3/2)g_A^2 m_π^3 + (4g_{ΔN}^2/(3π))F_π]

## Context

This work extends the SU(6|3)_L ⊗ SU(6|3)_R partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory of Chen and Savage (2001) to the two-flavor case relevant for lattice simulations that use only two light dynamical quarks. The formalism enables extraction of low-energy constants (α_M^(PQ), β_M^(PQ), σ_M^(PQ), μ_α, μ_β, μ_γ) from lattice data by varying both valence and sea quark masses, and provides the chiral extrapolation formulas needed to connect lattice calculations at m_π ∼ 500 MeV to physical quark masses.
