# Polarized Dissociation and Spin Alignment of Moving Quarkonium in Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Recent experiments have found spin alignment of $J/ψ$ with respect to event plane in heavy ion collisions, suggesting a medium effect that is spin dependent. We propose a possible mechanism with polarized dissociation from the motion of $J/ψ$ with respect to the medium. We calculate polarized dissociation rate for quarkonium spin triplet state from spin chromomagnetic coupling in the potential non-relativistic QCD framework. This is done for the leading order gluo-dissociation process and next to leading order inelastic Coulomb scattering process. The polarized dissociation rate is expressed as a function of relative velocity between quarkonium and QGP and the quantization axis. Applying the polarized dissociation rate to quarkonium evolution with dissociation effect only in a Bjorken flow, we find the spin $0$ state to dissociate less than the other spin states, leading to positive $ρ_{00}-1/3$. Regeneration contribution is expected to give a contribution with the opposite sign.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the spin alignment of $J/\psi$ observed in heavy ion collisions can arise from a spin-dependent dissociation rate of moving quarkonium in the quark-gluon plasma, mediated by the spin chromomagnetic coupling; the effect is conditional on the quarkonium having a nonzero velocity relative to the medium, and the calculation is performed only for the dissociation component, with regeneration expected to give a contribution of the opposite sign.

## Method

The authors calculate the polarized dissociation rate for a quarkonium spin triplet state using the potential non-relativistic QCD (pNRQCD) framework, focusing on the spin chromomagnetic dipole interaction. They compute the dissociation rate for the leading order gluo-dissociation process and the next-to-leading order inelastic Coulomb scattering process, expressing the rate as a function of the relative velocity between the quarkonium and the QGP and the quantization axis. The calculation is performed in the real-time formalism, and the moving quarkonium case is treated by boosting the isotropic chromomagnetic field fluctuations from the QGP frame to the quarkonium rest frame, expanding to second order in the quarkonium velocity.

## Key numbers

* The spin-averaged leading order dissociation rate from chromomagnetic dipole interaction is proportional to $g_s^2/m_Q^2$, with the phase space constraint $p_{\text{rel}} = \sqrt{m_Q(q - \epsilon_B)}$, where $q$ is the gluon energy, $m_Q$ is the heavy quark mass, and $\epsilon_B$ is the binding energy.
* For a static quarkonium, the dissociation rate for the spin-0 state is exactly $1/3$ of the spin-summed dissociation rate, consistent with isotropic chromomagnetic field fluctuations.
* The next-to-leading order inelastic Coulomb scattering contribution contains logarithmically enhanced terms of the form $\ln(T/\epsilon_B)$ and $\ln(T/m_{\text{g}})$, where $T$ is the temperature and $m_{\text{g}} \sim g_s T$ is the gluon thermal mass.
* The spin alignment signal $\rho_{00} - 1/3$ is positive when only dissociation is considered, with the spin-0 state dissociating less than the other spin states in a Bjorken flow model.

## Context

This work extends the study of spin alignment of vector mesons in heavy ion collisions, following experimental observations by STAR (2022) for $\phi$ mesons and ALICE (2022) for $J/\psi$, which show different energy dependencies. The authors build on the pNRQCD framework of Brambilla (2004) and the spin-dependent interaction analysis of Chen (2017), who obtained spin-averaged dissociation rates. The polarized dissociation mechanism proposed here is distinct from coalescence-based explanations for $\phi$ meson spin alignment (Sheng 2022, 2023; Xu 2024) and provides a possible explanation for the $J/\psi$ signal at high collision energies where dissociation of initially produced quarkonium is significant. The work enables future studies that include both dissociation and regeneration contributions to fully describe the spin alignment of moving quarkonium in the quark-gluon plasma.
