# Hydrodynamics with spacetime-dependent scattering length

> Hydrodynamics provides a concise but powerful description of long-time and long-distance physics of correlated systems out of thermodynamic equilibrium. Here we construct hydrodynamic equations for nonrelativistic particles with a spacetime-dependent scattering length and show that it enters constitutive relations uniquely so as to represent the fluid expansion and contraction in both normal and superfluid phases. As a consequence, we find that a leading dissipative correction to the contact density due to the spacetime-dependent scattering length is proportional to the bulk viscosity ($ζ_2$ in the superfluid phase). Also, when the scattering length is slowly varied over time in a uniform system, the entropy density is found to be produced even without fluid flows in proportion to the bulk viscosity, which may be useful as a novel probe to measure the bulk viscosity in ultracold-atom experiments.

## Key claim
The paper constructs hydrodynamic equations for nonrelativistic particles with a spacetime-dependent scattering length and shows that the scattering length enters constitutive relations uniquely to represent fluid expansion and contraction, with the leading dissipative correction to the contact density proportional to the bulk viscosity (ζ₂ in the superfluid phase). The authors find that when the scattering length is slowly varied over time in a uniform system, entropy density is produced even without fluid flows in proportion to the bulk viscosity, which may serve as a novel probe to measure bulk viscosity in ultracold-atom experiments.

## Method
The authors start from a quantum field theory Hamiltonian for nonrelativistic bosons or fermions with N spin components in arbitrary spatial dimension d, where the bare coupling λ(x) is related to the scattering length a(x) via dimensional regularization. They derive operator identities for conserved charge and current densities, then construct hydrodynamic constitutive relations for both normal fluids and superfluids by imposing the second law of thermodynamics. The entropy production equation is derived, and the dissipative corrections are expressed in terms of transport coefficients including thermal conductivity, shear viscosity, and bulk viscosities, with the spacetime-dependent scattering length entering through a modified bulk strain rate.

## Key numbers
- The scattering length a(x) is related to the bare coupling via λ(x) = (d-2)Ω_{d-1}a^{d-2}(x), where Ω_{d-1} = 2, 2π, 4π for d = 1, 2, 3 respectively
- For a uniform system with time-dependent scattering length, the dissipative correction to the contact density is C(t) = C_eq(t) + mΩ_{d-1}d²·ζ(t)a^{d-3}(t)ȧ(t) + O(ȧ²)
- The entropy density production rate in a uniform system without fluid flows is T(t)Ṡ(t) = d²·ζ(t)/a²(t) · ȧ²(t) + O(ȧ³), where ζ(x) in the normal phase is replaced with ζ₂(x) in the superfluid phase
- The bulk viscosity must vanish as ζ(x) ~ a²(x) for a(x) → 0 and ζ(x) ~ 1/a²(x) for a(x) → ∞ in the normal phase
- In the superfluid phase, bulk viscosities must vanish as ζ₁(x) ~ a(x), ζ₂(x) ~ a²(x) for a(x) → 0 and ζ₁(x) ~ 1/a(x), ζ₂(x) ~ 1/a²(x) for a(x) → ∞

## Context
This work extends the hydrodynamic description of ultracold atomic gases to include spacetime-dependent scattering lengths, building on prior work on conformal invariance and bulk viscosity in unitary Fermi gases by Son (2007), Dusling (2013), and Elliott (2014). The results are consistent with the vanishing bulk viscosities of the unitary Fermi gas in both normal and superfluid phases, and the formalism enables a novel experimental probe of bulk viscosity in ultracold-atom experiments by measuring entropy production from time-dependent scattering length variations. The paper also provides microscopic confirmation of the hydrodynamic results using linear response theory and the Kubo formula for bulk viscosity.
