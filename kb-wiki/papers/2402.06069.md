# Exploring the competition between $α$-decay and proton radioactivity: A comparative study of proximity potential formalisms

> We have conducted a comprehensive and systematic study of the proton radioactivity and $α$-decay half-lives of neutron-deficient nuclei. This investigation involved the utilization of various Proximity potentials and also considered the incorporation of thermal effects. For the half-life calculations, we employed both temperature-independent and temperature-dependent interaction potentials. We observed that proton radioactivity serves as the dominant mode of decay for nuclides situated very close to the proton drip-line. We explored a universal curve that examines the correlation between the decimal logarithm of experimental half-lives and the negative decimal logarithm of the penetrability for both proton radioactivity and $α$-decay.

## Key claim
The paper systematically tests six proximity potential formalisms (Bass73, CW76, Guo2013, Denisov, gp77, and Prox00 DP) for calculating proton radioactivity and alpha-decay half-lives in neutron-deficient nuclei, finding that proton radioactivity dominates for nuclides very close to the proton drip-line. The authors qualify that the agreement between calculated and experimental half-lives depends strongly on the accuracy of experimental Q-values, and that temperature-dependent potentials do not systematically improve predictions over temperature-independent ones.

## Method
The authors computed half-lives for neutron-deficient nuclei with atomic numbers Z=53 to 81 using the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) tunneling method with six different proximity potentials for the nuclear interaction term. They calculated both temperature-independent and temperature-dependent versions of each potential, where temperature was derived from the excitation energy via E* = (1/9)AT^2 - T. The half-life was obtained from the decay constant lambda = nu * P, where nu is the assault frequency (derived from zero-point vibration energy) and P is the penetration probability through the Coulomb barrier.

## Key numbers
- Studied neutron-deficient nuclei in the range Z=53 to 81, covering both proton radioactivity and alpha-decay
- For the Guo2013 proximity potential, the surface coefficient gamma = 0.9517[1 - 1.7826((N-Z)/A)^2] and the universal function parameters are p1 = -17.72, p2 = 1.30, p3 = 0.854
- For the Bass73 potential, the surface term a_s = 17.0 MeV and the range parameter d = 1.35 fm
- For the CW76 potential, the universal function is phi(r - R1 - R2) = exp(-(r - R1 - R2)/0.63)
- The zero-point vibration energy for proton emission depends on parent nucleus parity: 0.1045*Q_p for even-Z even-N, 0.0962*Q_p for odd-Z even-N, 0.0907*Q_p for even-Z odd-N, and 0.0767*Q_p for odd-Z odd-N parent nuclei
- For alpha emission, the zero-point vibration energy is E_v = Q{0.056 + 0.039*exp[(4-A2)/2.5]} for A2 >= 4
- The temperature-dependent radius formula is R_i(T) = R_i(T=0)[1 + 0.0005*T^2] fm
- The temperature-dependent surface tension is gamma(T) = gamma(0)*(1 - 0.07T)^2

## Context
This work extends previous comparative studies of proximity potentials by Yao (2015), Ghodsi (2016), and Santhosh (2017) for alpha-decay and heavy particle radioactivity, now including proton radioactivity and thermal effects. The study uses the same theoretical framework (WKB tunneling with proximity potentials) that has been applied to heavy ion fusion by Santhosh (2009, 2014) and ternary fission by Santhosh (2014, 2015). The results enable future experimental work to predict which decay mode dominates for unmeasured neutron-deficient nuclei near the proton drip-line, and provide a benchmark for comparing different nuclear potential formalisms in this region.
