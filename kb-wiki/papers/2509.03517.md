# Color-superconducting quarkyonic matter

> We explore the role of color superconductivity in quarkyonic matter under the conditions of color and electric neutrality at $β$- and strong equilibrium, as relevant for neutron stars. By explicitly incorporating the color-superconducting pairing gap into the phenomenological model of a smooth transition from hadron to quark matter, we extend the known quarkyonic framework to include this essential aspect relevant at high densities. The momentum dependence of the pairing gap, motivated by the running of the QCD coupling and introduced similarly to chiral quark models with nonlocal interaction, is a novel element of the model that is crucial for enabling the simultaneous onset of all color-flavor quark states in the presence of color superconductivity. While asymptotically conformal behavior of the present model is ensured by construction, we demonstrate that reaching the conformal limit in agreement with the predictions of perturbative QCD is provided by the proper momentum dependence of the thickness of the hadron shell in momentum space. We employ the flexible meta-modeling approach to nuclear matter, analyzing the structure of the hadron shell in momentum space and focusing on the effects of color superconductivity in quarkyonic matter. Similar to the effects induced by the onset of the quarkyonic phase, color superconductivity leads to stiffening of the equation of state of the NS matter. This causes a significant impact on observable properties of neutron stars, which are analyzed and compared to recent astrophysical and theoretical constraints. We argue that the developed model of color-superconducting quarkyonic matter provides a new, consistent tool for studying the scenario of smooth quark-hadron transition in NSs.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a model of color-superconducting quarkyonic (CSQY) matter that incorporates a momentum-dependent pairing gap for two-flavor color superconductivity (2SC phase) into the quarkyonic framework, but the model relies on a parametrized momentum-space hadron shell with a power-law exponent κ ≥ 3 (rather than the previously used κ = 2) to ensure that nucleon densities vanish at high densities in agreement with asymptotic freedom.

## Method

The authors construct a phenomenological model of quarkyonic matter where nucleons occupy a momentum shell of width δ_a above the quark Fermi momentum, with the shell width parametrized as a piecewise power function of the nucleon maximum momentum k_a. Color superconductivity is incorporated by modifying the Nambu-Gorkov single-particle distribution functions for paired red and green quarks, while blue quarks remain unpaired, and the momentum-dependent pairing gap is introduced analogously to nonlocal Nambu–Jona–Lasinio (NJL) models. The nuclear component is treated using a meta-modeling approach with a Taylor expansion of the isoscalar potential around nuclear saturation density, truncated at the cubic term to fix coefficients from the binding energy, incompressibility, and skewness. The conditions of color neutrality, electric neutrality, β-equilibrium, and strong equilibrium are imposed to model neutron star matter.

## Key numbers

- Neutron star masses in the range 1.2 – 2 M⊙ and radii about 11 – 13 km are cited as typical observational values.
- The mass of PSR J0740+6620 is above 2 M⊙, while PSR J0437-4715, PSR J0030+0451, and PSR J0614-3329 have intermediate masses about 1.4 M⊙.
- The Black Widow Pulsar J0952-0607 has mass 2.35 ± 0.17 M⊙.
- The nuclear saturation density n0 is used as the reference density for the meta-model expansion.
- The power-law exponent for the hadron shell width is taken as κ ≥ 3, in contrast to the previously used κ = 2 in works by McLerran (2018) and Zhao (2020).

## Context

This work extends the quarkyonic matter framework originally proposed by McLerran (2007) and Hidaka (2008), which describes a smooth transition from hadron to quark matter, by incorporating color superconductivity for the first time in this context. The model builds on the meta-modeling approach of Margueron (2017) for nuclear matter and uses the momentum-dependent pairing gap similar to nonlocal NJL models by Blaschke (2022) and Contrera (2022). The results enable consistent modeling of neutron stars with color-superconducting quark cores, providing a tool to study the smooth quark-hadron transition scenario and compare with observational constraints from NICER and gravitational-wave data.
