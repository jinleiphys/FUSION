# Three-dimensional calculation of inhomogeneous structure in low-density nuclear matter

> In low-density nuclear matter which is relevant to the crust region of neutron stars and collapsing stage of supernovae, non-uniform structures called "nuclear pasta" are expected. So far, most works on nuclear pasta have used the Wigner-Seitz cell approximation with anzats about the geometrical structures like droplet, rod, slab and so on. We perform fully three-dimensional calculation of non-uniform nuclear matter for some cases with fixed proton ratios and in beta-equilibrium based on the relativistic mean-field model and the Thomas-Fermi approximation. In our calculation typical pasta structures are observed. However, there appears some difference in the density region of each pasta structure.

## Key claim

The authors perform fully three-dimensional calculations of nuclear pasta structures without assuming any predefined geometry, and find that while typical pasta shapes (droplet, rod, slab, tube, bubble) appear, the density region for each structure differs from previous Wigner-Seitz cell approximation results, and some structures (bubble for Yp=0.3; tube and bubble for Yp=0.1) do not appear at all.

## Method

The authors use the relativistic mean-field model with the Thomas-Fermi approximation at zero temperature, solving the equations of motion for sigma, omega, rho mesons and the Coulomb potential on a three-dimensional cubic grid with periodic boundary conditions. Starting from random density distributions, they iteratively adjust the local densities to achieve uniform chemical potentials across all grid points, and subtract the dipole moment of the electric field to minimize higher-order Coulomb interactions. They perform calculations for fixed proton mixing ratios Yp = 0.5, 0.3, and 0.1, and for beta-equilibrium matter.

## Key numbers

- For symmetric nuclear matter (Yp = 0.5), the maximum proton density in the structures reaches 0.08 fm^-3 (red in figures), with blue corresponding to 0 fm^-3.
- For Yp = 0.3, the density range for the color scale is 0 to 0.075 fm^-3.
- For Yp = 0.1, the density range for the color scale is 0 to 0.05 fm^-3.
- The dipole interaction contributes up to 5% of the Coulomb energy, motivating the dipole subtraction procedure.
- The parameter set uses meson masses: m_sigma = 400 MeV, m_omega = 783 MeV, m_rho = 769 MeV, with coupling constants g_sigmaN = 6.3935, g_omegaN = 8.7207, g_rhoN = 4.2696.

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear pasta structures beyond the Wigner-Seitz cell approximation used by Maruyama et al. (previous RMF calculations) and Ravenhall et al. (1983), who assumed specific geometries like droplet, rod, and slab. The fully three-dimensional approach allows for the possibility of intermediate or non-typical shapes (such as dumbbell-like pieces from QMD calculations or gyroid shapes from compressible liquid-drop models) that cannot be described by the WS approximation. The results enable future studies with larger cubic cells that can accommodate multiple periods of structures, and provide a more realistic equation of state for neutron star crust and supernova matter.
