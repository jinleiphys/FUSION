# Jet quenching: RHIC results and phenomenology

> I review the main experimental results on jet physics in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions as studied via inclusive leading hadron spectra and di-hadron correlations at high transverse momentum. In central Au+Au at RHIC (sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV), the observed large suppression of high-$p_T$ hadron spectra as well as the strongly modified azimuthal dijet correlations compared to baseline p+p results in free space, provide crucial information on the thermodynamical and transport properties of QCD matter.

## Key claim

The observed suppression of high-\(p_T\) hadrons and modified dijet correlations in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC provide quantitative access to the thermodynamical and transport properties of the produced QCD matter, but several aspects of the data (such as the path-length dependence of energy loss, the unsuppressed baryon spectra at intermediate \(p_T\), and the large heavy-quark quenching) are less well reproduced by the canonical non-Abelian energy loss paradigm.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental results from RHIC on inclusive leading hadron spectra (measured via the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\)) and di-hadron azimuthal (\(\Delta\phi\)) and pseudo-rapidity (\(\Delta\eta\)) correlations in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV. These measurements are compared to baseline p+p results and to theoretical predictions from parton energy loss models (GLV, BDMPS) that relate the observed suppression to medium properties such as gluon density, transport coefficient, and speed of sound.

## Key numbers

- \(R_{AA} \approx 0.2\) for high-\(p_T\) hadrons in central Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, indicating a factor of \(\sim\)5 suppression relative to p+p.
- Initial gluon rapidity density \(dN^g/dy \approx 1000\) derived from the suppression magnitude using the GLV model.
- Transport coefficient \(\langle\hat{q}\rangle \approx 14\) GeV\(^2\)/fm derived from the suppression magnitude using the BDMPS model.
- Total charged hadron multiplicity \(dN/d\eta \approx 3/2 \cdot dN_{ch}/d\eta \approx 1000\) in central Au+Au.
- For \(\pi^0\) at \(p_T \approx 4\) GeV/\(c\), the suppression factor increases with collision energy from \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 20\) to 200 GeV.
- The away-side azimuthal correlation shows a double-peak structure at \(\Delta\phi \approx \pi \pm 1.2\) in central Au+Au, interpreted as a Mach cone angle \(\theta_M \approx 1.2\) rad, yielding a speed of sound \(c_s \approx 0.36\).
- In the Cerenkov gluonstrahlung interpretation, a dielectric constant corresponding to index of refraction \(n \approx 2.75\) is needed to reproduce the peak location.

## Context

This review extends the experimental program of jet quenching studies at RHIC, following the theoretical proposals of Bjorken (1982) for jet absorption and the subsequent development of non-Abelian energy loss models by Gyulassy (1990), Baier-Dokshitzer-Mueller-Peigne-Schiff (BDMPS), and Wiedemann. The results enable quantitative extraction of medium properties such as gluon density, transport coefficient, and speed of sound, and motivate further theoretical work to resolve discrepancies in path-length dependence, baryon production via coalescence (recombination models), and heavy-quark energy loss.
