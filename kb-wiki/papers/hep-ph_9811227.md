# Quark Condensation, Induced Symmetry Breaking and Color Superconductivity at High Density

> The phase structure of hadronic matter at high density relevant to the physics of compact stars and relativistic heavy-ion collisions is studied in a low-energy effective quark theory. The relevant phases that figure are (1) chiral condensation, (2) diquark color condensation (color superconductivity) and (3) induced Lorentz-symmetry breaking (ISB). For reasonable strengths for four-Fermi interactions implied by one-gluon exchange quark interactions as well as instanton-mediated interactions, we find that the ISB phase sets in at a moderate density while the quark condensate vanishes, followed by color superconductivity associated with scalar diquark condensation appearing only at an asymptotic density. Lack of confinement in the model makes the result of this analysis only qualitative but the hierarchy of the transitions we find seems to be quite robust.

## Key claim

For reasonable strengths of four-Fermi interactions implied by one-gluon exchange and instanton-mediated interactions, the induced Lorentz-symmetry breaking (ISB) phase sets in at a moderate density where the quark condensate vanishes, while color superconductivity from scalar diquark condensation appears only at asymptotic density; the authors note that lack of confinement in the model makes the result only qualitative, but the hierarchy of transitions appears robust.

## Method

The authors study a low-energy effective quark theory with four-quark contact interactions, using a path-integral formulation in Euclidean space with bosonic auxiliary fields (sigma, pions, vector field V_mu, and diquark field Delta) to mediate the interactions. They compute the effective potential at mean-field level by integrating out quark fields in a Nambu-Gorkov formalism, then determine the ground state condensates (chiral condensate, V_0 condensate, and diquark gap) by finding the global minimum of the effective potential. The coupling strengths are estimated by Fierz-transforming the one-gluon-exchange current-current interaction, with the vector channel strength varied to account for additional attraction from collective modes such as N* N^{-1} excitations.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: rho_0 ≈ 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Coupling ratios from one-gluon exchange (N=3): g_chi : m_v^2 : g_Delta^{scalar} : g_Delta^{vector} = 1 : 6 : 6 : 12
- The scalar diquark coupling strength: g_Delta^{scalar} = 2N/G (with N=3)
- The vector diquark coupling strength: g_Delta^{vector} = 4N/G (with N=3)
- The vector meson mass-squared parameter from one-gluon exchange: m_v^2 = (N/G) * 1/(1 - N/4)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of induced Lorentz symmetry breaking at finite density (Langfeld 1997, 1998) and the revived discussion of color superconductivity (Bailin and Love 1984; Alford, Rajagopal, Wilczek 1997). The paper contrasts the ISB scenario with standard constituent quark model treatments of chiral restoration (Klevanski) and relates the vector channel attraction to the mechanism for "dropping" vector meson masses proposed by Brown and Rho (1991) and Klingl, Rapp, and Weise. The results suggest that color superconductivity is unlikely in heavy-ion collisions but may play a role in compact stars, and the hierarchy of transitions provides a framework for future lattice QCD studies at finite chemical potential.
