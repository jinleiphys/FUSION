# Chiral symmetry in nuclei

> The role of the quark condensate in low-energy QCD and its behaviour in nuclear matter are discussed. Partial restoration of chiral symmetry, as indicated by a reduction of the quark condensate in matter, could significantly alter the properties of nucleons and mesons. Various signals of these effects are discussed: enhancement of the axial charge of a nucleon, lepton pair production and $\overline K$-nucleus attraction. (Talk presented at the Nuclear Physics Study Weekend on Extreme States of Nuclei, Abingdon, October 1995)

# Chiral symmetry in nuclei

## Key claim

Partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear matter, as indicated by a roughly 30% reduction of the quark condensate at normal nuclear density, could significantly alter the properties of nucleons and mesons; however, the model-independent linear density dependence (3.2) holds only at low densities, and extrapolations beyond normal nuclear matter density show large and strongly model-dependent corrections.

## Method

The paper uses the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation to connect the quark condensate to pion observables, then applies the Feynman-Hellmann theorem to express the density dependence of the condensate in terms of the energy density of nuclear matter. The leading (linear) density dependence is obtained from the pion-nucleon sigma commutator, and corrections from binding energy and nucleon correlations are estimated using relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations and models of pion and meson exchange. Consequences for nucleon and meson properties are examined through the linear sigma model and soft-pion theorems.

## Key numbers

- Quark condensate in vacuum: approximately -3 fm^{-3} (with current quark mass mbar in the range 5–10 MeV at renormalisation scale 1 GeV)
- Pion decay constant: f_pi = 92.4 ± 0.3 MeV
- Pion-nucleon sigma commutator: sigma_piN = 45 ± 7 MeV (from Gasser et al.)
- At nuclear matter density rho_0 ≈ 0.17 fm^{-3}, the quark condensate is reduced by approximately 30% relative to vacuum
- Free nucleon axial charge coupling: g_A = 1.257 ± 0.003
- Effective axial charge in lead-region nuclei shows enhancements of approximately 80–100% (from Warburton’s studies of first-forbidden beta decays)
- One-pion exchange contribution to axial charge enhancement is limited to at most approximately 50% by a soft-pion theorem
- CERES collaboration data on e+e- pairs from S on Au collisions at 200 GeV per nucleon show enhancement around invariant mass 400 MeV; a transport model describes this with rho-meson mass reduced to 380 MeV in the initial fireball (rho ≈ 3.5 rho_0, T ≈ 185 MeV)

## Context

This talk extends the model-independent result for the quark condensate in matter derived by Drukarev and Levin (1990), Cohen, Furnstahl and Griegel (1992), and Lutz, Klimt and Weise (1992), showing that the linear density dependence holds up to normal nuclear density with small corrections. It builds on the scaling arguments of Brown and Rho (1993, 1995) relating hadron mass changes to condensate reduction, and on the Dirac phenomenology of Serot and Walecka (1985) and Wallace (1987). The work enables experimental searches for partial chiral symmetry restoration through enhanced axial charge in beta decays (Warburton, 1991), lepton pair production from vector meson decays (planned at CEBAF and GSI), and kaon-nucleus attraction relevant to kaon condensation in dense matter (Kaplan and Nelson, 1986; Brown et al., 1992).
