# Wounded Quarks and Multiplicity at Relativistic Ion Colliders

> In this paper, we propose a parameterization which is based on a phenomenological model involving the wounded quarks interactions for explaining the average charged particle multiplicity $\ < n_{ch}\ >$, the central pseudo-rapidity density $\ < (dn/dη)_{η=0}\ >$ and complete rapidity dependence of $dn/dη$ in relativistic heavy-ion collider experiments. The model also interrelates nucleus-nucleus (A-A) collisions with p-A and p-p interactions. Our parameterization rests on simple assumptions regarding mean number of participating quarks and their average number of collisions. The results for $\ < n_{ch}\ >$ and their variations with the mass number of colliding nuclei, center-of-mass energy ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}$) and collision centrality are well supported by the available experimental data. Finally we give the predictions from our model for A-A collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) and Compressed Baryonic Matter (CBM) experiments. Our results indicate the existence of a possible universal production mechanism for p-p, p-A and A-A collisions.

# Wounded Quarks and Multiplicity at Relativistic Ion Colliders

## Key claim
The paper proposes that a universal charged-particle production mechanism exists for p-p, p-A, and A-A collisions, describable by a phenomenological wounded-quark model with a cubic-logarithmic energy dependence. However, the model requires additional fitting parameters (β, γ, σ) to reproduce the full pseudo-rapidity distributions, and the claimed universality holds only for the specific parameterization where the same four constants (a', b', c', d') are used across all collision systems.

## Method
The authors construct a phenomenological model based on the wounded-quark picture, where the mean number of participating quarks (N_q) and their mean number of inelastic collisions (ν_q) are calculated using the Additive Quark Model and Glauber-type nuclear density profiles. The total charged-particle multiplicity is parameterized with a cubic logarithmic function of the available center-of-mass energy per interacting quark, using the same four fitted constants for p-p, p-A, and A-A collisions. For pseudo-rapidity distributions, a two-component model is employed where soft processes scale with N_q and hard processes scale with N_q·ν_q, combined with a functional form for the η-dependence that introduces three additional fitting parameters (β, γ, σ) per collision system and energy.

## Key numbers
- Total multiplicity in p-p collisions: parameterized as ⟨n_ch⟩_hp = (1.8 + 0.37·ln√s_a + 0.43·ln²√s_a + 0.04·ln³√s_a) − 0.85, where √s_a is the available center-of-mass energy and 0.85 accounts for the leading particle effect
- Central pseudo-rapidity density in p-p collisions: ⟨(dn_ch/dη)_pp(η=0)⟩ = (1.24 + 0.18·ln√s_a + 0.044·ln²√s_a) − α'_1, with α'_1 being small but non-negligible
- Total charged multiplicity in central Au-Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV: model gives 5277 for 0-6% centrality, compared to experimental 5095±255
- Total charged multiplicity in central Cu-Cu collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV: model gives 1532 for 0-6% centrality, compared to experimental 1474±69
- Total charged multiplicity in d-Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV: model gives 82 (minimum bias) and 162 (central), compared to PHOBOS experimental values of 87(+7/-6) and 167(+14/-11)
- The hard-component fraction x varies from 0.1 to 0.125 depending on √s_NN
- For Pb-Pb at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, the pseudo-rapidity distribution parameters are β=1.82, γ=0.80, σ=3.28

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological model of Singh et al. (earlier work) by incorporating a cubic logarithmic energy dependence for total multiplicity, motivated by PHOBOS collaboration observations and the work of Jeon and collaborators (2000s) showing that RHIC multiplicities require such a term. The model contrasts with standard Glauber model calculations and modified Glauber model approaches (Feofilov, 2000s), which the authors show give poorer fits to Au-Au data. The wounded-quark approach relates to the Additive Quark Model framework (Anisovich, Bialas, Kobrinsky, Nyiri, 1970s-1980s) and competes with other approaches such as HIJING, UrQMD, and saturation-based models (Albacete, Kharzeev, Levin, Armesto, 2000s) that show varying agreement with LHC data. The model enables predictions for LHC and CBM/FAIR experiments and suggests that final-state interactions have negligible effect on multiplicity, supporting the view of Eskola and Lin (2000s) that hadron production is driven by initial parton production.
