# Photon- and pion-nucleon interactions in a unitary and causal effective field theory based on the chiral Lagrangian

> We present and apply a novel scheme for studying photon- and pion-nucleon scattering beyond the threshold region. Partial-wave amplitudes for the $γ\, N$ and $πN$ states are obtained by an analytic extrapolation of subthreshold reaction amplitudes computed in chiral perturbation theory, where the constraints set by electromagnetic-gauge invariance, causality and unitarity are used to stabilize the extrapolation. Based on the chiral Lagrangian we recover the empirical s- and p-wave amplitudes up to energies $\sqrt{s}\simeq 1300$ MeV in terms of the parameters relevant at order $Q^3$.

## Key claim

The authors present a novel scheme that extends chiral perturbation theory beyond its near-threshold applicability by analytically extrapolating subthreshold amplitudes using constraints from electromagnetic-gauge invariance, causality, and unitarity, recovering empirical s- and p-wave amplitudes up to approximately 1300 MeV without introducing an explicit isobar field in the chiral Lagrangian.

## Method

The authors compute tree-level and one-loop amplitudes for pion-nucleon scattering, pion photoproduction, and Compton scattering from the chiral Lagrangian truncated at order Q^3. They then construct partial-wave amplitudes free of kinematic singularities and solve non-linear integral equations using N/D techniques, where the generalized potential is approximated via conformal mapping that exploits the analytic structure of the amplitudes. The parameters of the chiral Lagrangian are adjusted to reproduce empirical data for s- and p-wave partial-wave amplitudes, with inelastic channels containing two or more pions neglected.

## Key numbers

* The scheme recovers empirical s- and p-wave amplitudes up to center-of-mass energies √s ≃ 1300 MeV.
* The chiral Lagrangian is truncated at order Q^3, with parameters c₁, c₂, c₃, c₄, d₁+d₂, d₃, d₅, d₈, d₉, d₁₄-d₁₅, d₁₈, d₂₀, d₂₁, and d₂₂ contributing at that order.
* The lowest s-channel unitarity threshold is at √s = μ_thr = m_N (nucleon mass) for γN intermediate states.
* The expansion point μ_E for analytic continuation is set to m_N + m_π for πN → πN, m_N + ½m_π for γN → πN, and m_N for γN → γN.

## Context

This work extends chiral perturbation theory (χPT) studies of pion-nucleon scattering by Bernard (1995, 2007) and Fettes (1998, 2000), and pion photoproduction by Bernard (1992, 1994, 1996), which are limited to near-threshold energies. It contrasts with model computations by Scholten (1996), Krehl (1999), Penner (2002), and Drechsel (2007) that address higher energies but lack systematic effective field theory foundations. The scheme builds on the approach proposed by Lutz and Kolomeitsev (2010) and enables a unified, causal, and unitary description of photon- and pion-nucleon interactions from threshold through the isobar region using only chiral Lagrangian parameters.
