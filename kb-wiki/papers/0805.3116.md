# Neutron Structure Functions

> Neutron structure functions can be extracted from proton and deuteron data and a representation of the deuteron structure. This procedure does not require DIS approximations or quark structure assumptions. We find that the results depend critically on properly accounting for the Q^2 dependence of proton and deuteron data. We interpolate the data to fixed Q^2, and extract the ratio of neutron to proton structure functions. The extracted ratio decreases with increasing x, up to x \approx 0.9, while there are no data available to constrain the behavior at larger x.

## Key claim

Neutron structure functions can be extracted from proton and deuteron data using a Poincare covariant convolution relation that does not require the deep-inelastic scattering approximation, but the results depend critically on properly accounting for the Q^2 dependence of the proton and deuteron data, and the extracted ratio of neutron to proton structure functions decreases with increasing x up to x approximately 0.9, with no data available to constrain behavior at larger x.

## Method

The authors interpolate proton and deuteron structure function data to a fixed Q^2 of 12 GeV^2, using a fit to the proton structure function from a global analysis and direct measurements of the ratio of deuteron to proton structure functions from SLAC, BCDMS, and NMC experiments. They apply a Poincare covariant convolution relation with the CD-Bonn potential deuteron wave function to relate nucleon and deuteron structure functions, then parameterize the neutron-to-proton ratio R_np as a function of xi and use MINUIT to fit the parameters that best reproduce the measured deuteron-to-proton ratio.

## Key numbers

- The analysis uses data with W^2 greater than 3 GeV^2 and Q^2 between 6 and 40 GeV^2, yielding 450 R_dp measurements interpolated to Q_0^2 = 12 GeV^2
- The Q^2 dependence of the deuteron-to-proton ratio is dR_dp/d(ln(Q^2)) approximately -0.013, with little x dependence except for x less than about 0.1 and x greater than about 0.7
- The best fit to R_np yields chi^2 = 14.6 for 17 degrees of freedom (27 data points, 7 parameters in the x-dependence, and 3 normalization factors)
- At x = 0.901, the extracted R_np = 0.169 with statistical uncertainty 0.047 and systematic uncertainty 0.011, plus an extraction uncertainty of 0.034
- The overall normalization uncertainty on the global R_dp measurements is 1%, corresponding to an overall absolute shift in R_np of approximately 0.014
- The correlated systematic uncertainty that grows with x is parameterized as delta R_np = 0.11 exp(-12(1-x))

## Context

This work extends previous extractions of neutron structure functions by Whitlow et al. (1992), who used the deep-inelastic scattering convolution and the Paris potential deuteron wave function, and by Melnitchouk et al. (1996). The key advance is using a Poincare covariant convolution relation that does not require the deep-inelastic approximation, properly accounting for the Q^2 dependence of the data by interpolating to fixed Q^2, and using the CD-Bonn potential for the deuteron wave function. The extracted ratio enables improved constraints on parton distribution functions and tests of quark structure models for nucleons, particularly at large x where the neutron-to-proton ratio is small but poorly constrained above x = 0.9.
