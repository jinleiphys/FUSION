# Nuclear structure study using a hybrid approach of shell model and Gogny-type density functionals

> Nuclear density functional theory (DFT) is able to reproduce the saturation properties of nuclear matter, as well as properties of finite nuclei. Consequently, the DFT calculations are applicable to nuclei across a wide range of masses on nuclear chart. The Gogny-type density functional, which is equivalent to the mean-field calculations with finite-range density-dependent effective interactions, is a successful example. In contrast, the shell-model (configuration-interaction) calculation is a powerful tool to describe nuclear structure, especially spectroscopic properties. The shell model is able to take into account correlations beyond mean field in a truncated model space. In this work, we report investigation on $\textit{sd}$-shell nuclei and Ca isotopes using a hybrid approach of the shell model and Gogny-type DFT.

## Key claim

The hybrid approach combining Gogny-type density functionals with shell-model calculations can reproduce ground-state and excited-state properties of sd-shell nuclei and Ca isotopes with accuracy comparable to empirical interactions, but it systematically underestimates ground-state energies in neutron-rich regions and excitation energies near closed-shell configurations, likely due to neglected core excitations.

## Method

The authors constructed a shell-model Hamiltonian using the Gogny D1S density functional to generate both the single-particle energies and the two-body matrix elements (TBMEs) in a 0ℏω valence space (sd shell for O, Ne, Mg; pf shell for Ca). The nuclear density required for the density-dependent term of the Gogny interaction was determined iteratively: starting from a Woods-Saxon density, the shell model calculation was performed, and the resulting ground-state density was used to recompute the TBMEs until convergence. Shell model calculations were carried out using the KSHELL code, and results were compared with the empirical USDB interaction (sd shell) and SDPF-MU interaction (pf shell).

## Key numbers

- The Gogny-D1S TBMEs agree with the empirical USDB and SDPF-MU interactions roughly within 2 MeV.
- The two-body center-of-mass correction in HFB calculations is around 8 MeV for Ca isotopes.
- The shell model results successfully reproduce experimental ground-state energies of sd-shell nuclei up to N=16 and ⁴⁰–⁴⁸Ca.
- For neutron-rich ⁵⁰–⁶⁰Ca, the binding energy is underestimated and the deviation from experiment increases with neutron number.
- The large 2⁺ excitation energy of ²⁴O (indicating the N=16 sub-shell gap) is reproduced by both Gogny D1S and USDB.
- The Gogny-D1S B(E2;0₁⁺→2₁⁺) values for Mg isotopes are systematically smaller than USDB results with the same effective charges (eₚ, eₙ) = (1.36, 0.45)e; agreement improves with (eₚ, eₙ) = (1.5, 0.5)e.

## Context

This work extends the hybrid approach of PhysRevC.98.044320, which applied Gogny-type effective interactions as shell-model interactions in the mass region Z∼8, to heavier nuclei including sd-shell nuclei and Ca isotopes. The approach contrasts with empirical interactions like USDB (PhysRevC.74.034315) and SDPF-MU (PhysRevC.86.051301), which require fitting to experimental data in each valence space, whereas the present method constructs the interaction from a density functional designed to reproduce ground-state properties across the nuclear chart. The results enable future applications to heavy unknown nuclei and suggest the need to include cross-shell excitations and explore other energy density functionals.
