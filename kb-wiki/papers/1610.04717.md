# Study of Lambda polarization in relativistic nuclear collisions at sqrt{s}_NN = 7.7-200 GeV

> We present a calculation of the global polarization of Lambda hyperons in relativistic Au-Au collisions at RHIC Beam Energy Scan range sqrt{s}_NN = 7.7 - 200 GeV with a 3+1 dimensional cascade + viscous hydro + cascade model, UrQMD+vHLLE. Within this model, the mean polarization of Lambda in the out-of-plane direction is predicted to decrease rapidly with collision energy from a top value of about 2% at the lowest energy examined. We explore the connection between the polarization signal and thermal vorticity and estimate the feed-down contribution to Lambda polarization due to the decay of higher mass hyperons.

## Key claim
The global polarization of Λ hyperons in Au-Au collisions decreases rapidly with collision energy, from about 2% at √s_NN = 7.7 GeV to much lower values at 200 GeV, but this trend is robust against variations in model parameters such as initial state granularity, shear viscosity, and particlization energy density.

## Method
The authors used a 3+1D hybrid model (UrQMD + vHLLE) combining a cascade initial state, viscous hydrodynamic evolution with shear viscosity, and a particlization stage where Λ polarization was calculated from thermal vorticity via the Cooper-Frye formalism. They simulated semi-central Au-Au collisions at √s_NN = 7.7–200 GeV, computing the mean spin vector of primary Λ hyperons and estimating feed-down contributions from decays of higher-mass hyperons like Σ^0, Σ(1385), and others up to Σ(1670).

## Key numbers
- Mean out-of-plane Λ polarization at √s_NN = 7.7 GeV: about 2% (top value at lowest energy examined).
- At √s_NN = 200 GeV, the mean polarization is consistent with earlier results from Becattini et al. (2015) (about 0.2% for low-p_T Λ).
- Fractions of final Λ from different sources: 28% primary, 32% from Σ(1385) decays, 17% from Σ^0 decays (negligible energy dependence).
- Variation of R_⊥ by ±40% changes P_J by ±20% at √s_NN = 62.4 GeV; variation of R_η by ±40% changes P_J by ±25% at the same energy.

## Context
This work extends earlier hydrodynamic calculations of Λ polarization by Becattini et al. (2015) and Karpenko et al. (2015) to the full RHIC Beam Energy Scan range, using a model tuned to reproduce basic hadron observables. It contradicts the larger polarization values (few percent at 200 GeV) predicted by Csernai et al. (2013) with different initial conditions, and instead finds a strong energy dependence driven by baryon stopping at low energies versus baryon transparency at high energies. The feed-down analysis, including decays of Σ^0, Σ(1385), and other resonances, enables more accurate comparisons with STAR experimental data and future measurements at lower collision energies.
