# Marrying {\it ab initio} calculations and Halo-EFT: the case of ${}^7{\rm Li} + n \rightarrow {}^8{\rm Li} + γ$

> We report a leading-order calculation of radiative ${}^{7}\mathrm{Li}$ neutron captures to both the ground and first excited state of ${}^{8}\mathrm{Li}$ in the framework of a low-energy effective field theory (Halo-EFT). Each of the possible final states is treated as a shallow bound state composed of both $n+{}^{7}\mathrm{Li}$ and $n+{}^{7}\mathrm{Li}^{*}$ (core excitation) configurations. The {\it ab initio} variational Monte Carlo method is used to compute the asymptotic normalization coefficients of these bound states, which are then used to fix couplings in our EFT. We calculate the total and partial cross sections in the radiative capture process using this calibrated EFT. Fair agreement with measured total cross sections is achieved and excellent agreement with the measured branching ratio between the two final states is found. In contrast,a previous Halo-EFT calculation [G. Rupak and R. Higa, Phys. Rev. Lett {\bf 106}, 222501 (2011)] assumes that the $n$-${}^{7}\mathrm{Li}$ couplings in different spin channels are equal, fits the $P$-wave "effective-range" parameter to the threshold cross section for ${}^7{\rm Li} + n \rightarrow {}^8{\rm Li} + γ$, and assumes the core excitation is at high enough energy scale that it can be integrated out.

## Key claim

The authors report a leading-order Halo-EFT calculation of radiative neutron capture on ⁷Li to both the ground and first excited state of ⁸Li, where the asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) from *ab initio* variational Monte Carlo calculations are used to fix the EFT couplings, in contrast to previous Halo-EFT work (Rupak and Higa, 2011) which assumed equal couplings in different spin channels and fitted the P-wave effective-range parameter to the threshold cross section.

## Method

The authors constructed a Halo-EFT Lagrangian treating the ⁸Li ground and first excited states as shallow bound states composed of both n+⁷Li and n+⁷Li* (core excitation) configurations, with the ⁷Li* excitation energy of 0.478 MeV included as a dynamical degree of freedom. They used *ab initio* variational Monte Carlo (VMC) calculations with realistic two- and three-nucleon interactions to compute the asymptotic normalization coefficients of these bound states, which then fixed the EFT couplings. The dressed dimer propagators were computed including self-energy contributions from both n+⁷Li and n+⁷Li* intermediate states, and the T-matrix was analytically continued to negative energy to extract binding energies and ANCs.

## Key numbers

- Binding momentum of ⁸Li ground state relative to n+⁷Li threshold: γ = 57.8 MeV
- Binding momentum of ⁸Li first excited state relative to n+⁷Li threshold: γ̃ = 41.6 MeV
- ⁷Li* excitation energy: E* = 0.478 MeV, corresponding to momentum γ_Δ = 30.0 MeV
- S-wave scattering length in the ⁵S₂ channel: a_(⁵S₂) = -3.63(5) fm
- S-wave scattering length in the ³S₁ channel: a_(³S₁) = 0.87(7) fm
- P-wave effective range for J=2 channel: r = -1.43(2) fm⁻¹
- P-wave effective range for J=1 channel: r̃ = -1.86(6) fm⁻¹
- High-momentum scale from ⁷Li breakup energy (2.5 MeV): Λ ≈ 90 MeV

## Context

This work extends Halo-EFT treatments of the ⁷Li(n,γ)⁸Li system (Rupak and Higa, 2011; Fernando and collaborators, 2011) by incorporating *ab initio* VMC ANCs as input rather than fitting to cross section data, and by including the ⁷Li* core excitation as a dynamical degree of freedom rather than integrating it out. The approach enables systematic uncertainty estimates and provides a framework for connecting *ab initio* nuclear structure calculations to reaction observables, with potential application to the isospin mirror process ⁷Be(p,γ)⁸B relevant for solar neutrino physics.
