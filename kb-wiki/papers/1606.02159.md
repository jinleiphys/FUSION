# Avoiding common pitfalls and misconceptions in extractions of the proton radius

> In a series of recent publications, different authors produce a wide range of electron radii when reanalyzing electron proton scattering data. In the light of the proton radius puzzle, this is a most unfortunate situation. However, we find flaws in most analyses that result in radii around 0.84 fm. In this paper, we explain our reasoning and try to illustrate the most common pitfalls.

## Key claim

The authors argue that most reanalyses of electron-proton scattering data that yield proton radii around 0.84 fm contain methodological flaws, and that a polynomial fit to form factor data is not equivalent to a Taylor expansion, meaning the convergence of such fits is not limited by the pion-production threshold at \(Q^2 = 4m_\pi^2\).

## Method

The paper reviews common statistical and fitting errors in proton radius extractions, using the Mainz high-precision electron-proton scattering data set as a reference. The authors generate pseudo-data from known polynomial models to test the bias and consistency of low-order polynomial fits, and they replicate the conformal mapping approach of Lorenz et al. (2014) to demonstrate failures in finding the global \(\chi^2\) minimum and the lack of a stable plateau in extracted radii.

## Key numbers

- The muonic hydrogen Lamb shift experiment gives a proton radius of 0.8409(4) fm (Pohl 2010, Antognini 2013), while the CODATA value from electron-based experiments is 0.8751(61) fm (Mohr 2015).
- A 10th-order polynomial fit to the standard dipole (pole at \(Q^2 = -0.71\ (\mathrm{GeV}/c)^2\)) approximates the dipole to within 40 ppm over the range 0 to 1 \((\mathrm{GeV}/c)^2\), whereas the Taylor expansion diverges strongly above \(Q^2 \approx 0.15\ (\mathrm{GeV}/c)^2\).
- For first-order polynomial fits to pseudo-data with a true radius of 0.8855 fm, the average extracted radius at a \(Q^2\) cut-off of 0.02 \((\mathrm{GeV}/c)^2\) shows a bias of more than 0.04 fm, yielding a value near 0.84 fm.
- In the authors' replication of the conformal mapping fits, the charge radius extracted for \(k_\mathrm{max} = 6\) to 8 ranges from 0.866 to 0.876 fm, in good agreement with the Mainz result, not the muonic value.

## Context

This work directly critiques the analyses of Lorenz et al. (2014), Griffioen et al. (2015), Higinbotham et al. (2015), and Horbatsch et al. (2015), which claimed smaller proton radii from electron scattering data. It extends the discussion of the proton radius puzzle by clarifying that polynomial fits are not Taylor expansions and that low-order fits to low-\(Q^2\) data are strongly biased. The paper enables future extractions to avoid these pitfalls by using flexible models with proper statistical validation, as advocated in the original Mainz analysis by Bernauer et al. (2010, 2013).
