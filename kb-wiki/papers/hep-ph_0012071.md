# Equation of state of strongly coupled Hamiltonian lattice QCD at finite density

> We calculate the equation of state of strongly coupled Hamiltonian lattice QCD at finite density by constructing a solution to the equation of motion corresponding to an effective Hamiltonian using Wilson fermions. We find that up to and beyond the chiral symmetry restoration density the pressure of the quark Fermi sea can be negative indicating its mechanical instability. This result is in qualitative agreement with continuum models and should be verifiable by future numerical simulations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the pressure of the quark Fermi sea in strongly coupled Hamiltonian lattice QCD at finite density can be negative up to and beyond the chiral symmetry restoration density, indicating mechanical instability; this result is in qualitative agreement with continuum effective QCD models but is obtained using an analytic solution that exactly diagonalizes the effective Hamiltonian only to second order in field operators and to leading order in the 1/N_c expansion.

## Method

The author constructs an explicit solution to the equation of motion for Smit's effective Hamiltonian using Wilson fermions, extending a free-space solution (Umino 2000) to finite temperature and chemical potential via a generalized thermal Bogoliubov transformation. In the T → 0 limit, the ansatz exactly diagonalizes the effective Hamiltonian to second order in field operators for all densities, and the dynamical quark mass and total chemical potential are determined self-consistently by solving coupled gap equations to leading order in N_c. The equation of state is then obtained by numerically evaluating the thermodynamic potential density from the vacuum energy density.

## Key numbers

- For effective coupling constant K = 0.9, the chiral phase transition is second order with critical total chemical potential (μ_tot)_C ≈ 0.716 (in lattice units).
- For K = 1.0, the chiral phase transition is first order with critical total chemical potential (μ_tot)_C ≈ 0.871.
- Lattice saturation sets in around μ_tot ≈ 0.898 for K = 0.9, and immediately above (μ_tot)_C for K = 1.0.
- The energy required to excite one color electric flux link is E = 1/K, which is larger than the chemical potentials studied (e.g., E ≈ 1.11 for K = 0.9 and E = 1.0 for K = 1.0).
- Input parameters used: current quark mass M_0 = 0, Wilson parameter r = 0.25, number of colors N_c = 3.

## Context

This work extends earlier analytical studies of strongly coupled lattice QCD at finite chemical potential using the Hamiltonian formulation with Kogut-Susskind fermions (Patkos 1984, van den Doel 1984, Le Yaouanc 1988, Gregory 2000) and the Euclidean formulation (Damgaard 1985, Ilgenfritz 1985, Bilic 1992). The result of negative pressure is in qualitative agreement with continuum effective models such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Buballa 1996) and the effective instanton-induced 't Hooft interaction model (Alford 1998), and should be verifiable by future numerical simulations of finite density lattice QCD.
