# The nuclear liquid-gas transition in QCD

> We estimate the nuclear saturation density and the binding energy in a nuclear liquid from precision data on the coupling of the four-quark scattering vertex in the vector channel, computed within functional QCD. We show that this coupling is directly related to the density-density potential and the latter is used for the estimates. In a first qualitative computation we find a saturation density of 0.2 fm${}^{-3}$ and an upper bound for the binding energy of 21.5 MeV, in agreement with the empirical values of 0.16 fm${}^{-3}$ and 16 MeV, respectively. We also use the scattering vertex for constructing an emergent low-energy effective theory for the liquid gas transition from QCD correlation function, whose coupling parameters can be determined within QCD. As a first consistency check of this construction we estimate the in-medium reduction of the nucleon pole mass.

## Key claim

The authors estimate the nuclear saturation density and binding energy from first-principles QCD correlation functions, finding values compatible with empirical data. The key hedge is that this is a "first qualitative computation" with large error estimates (approximately 25%), and the saturation density is derived from a simple cubic lattice approximation rather than from the full density-density potential.

## Method

The authors compute the static density-density potential V_b(r) in nuclear matter by taking the Fourier transform of the RG-invariant four-quark scattering vertex in the vector channel, lambda_omega(p), which was previously computed using functional renormalisation group methods in QCD (Cyrol 2017). They identify the minimum of this potential at distance d_b with the typical inter-nucleon spacing in the nuclear liquid, and the depth of the minimum with an upper bound for the binding energy per nucleon. The saturation density is then estimated from a cubic lattice approximation n_b ~ 1/d_b^3.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density estimate: 0.21 ± 0.16 fm^{-3}, compared to the empirical value of 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Upper bound for binding energy: 21 ± 5 MeV, compared to the empirical value of 16 MeV
- Minimum of the density-density potential located at d_b = 8.5 ± 2.1 GeV^{-1} ≈ 1.67 ± 0.42 fm
- A rescaling factor gamma ≈ 0.92 is applied to account for the difference between two-flavor and (2+1)-flavor QCD, based on the ratio of light constituent quark masses

## Context

This work extends first-principles QCD approaches to the nuclear liquid-gas transition, which has previously been studied primarily through chiral effective field theory (Drischler 2017) and phenomenological models such as quantum hadrodynamics (Serot and Walecka). The authors use QCD correlation functions from functional renormalisation group computations (Cyrol 2017, Mitter 2014, Rennecke 2015) to directly estimate nuclear matter properties without fitting parameters. This enables a direct connection between QCD and the density-dependent phenomenological potentials used in nuclear physics, such as Skyrme and Gogny interactions.
