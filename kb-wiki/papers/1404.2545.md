# The quark-gluon vertex in Landau gauge bound-state studies

> We present a practical method for the solution of the quark-gluon vertex for use in Bethe--Salpeter and Dyson--Schwinger calculations. The efficient decomposition into the necessary covariants is detailed, with the numerical algorithm outlined for both real and complex Euclidean momenta. A truncation of the quark-gluon vertex, that neglects explicit back-coupling to enable the application to bound-state calculations, is given together with results for the quark propagator and quark-gluon vertex for different quark flavours. The relative impact of the various components of the quark-gluon vertex is highlighted with the flavour dependence of the effective quark-gluon interaction obtained, thus providing insight for the construction of phenomenological models within Rainbow-Ladder. Finally, we solve the corresponding Green's functions for complex Euclidean momenta as required in future bound-state calculations.

## Key claim

The paper presents a practical truncation of the quark-gluon vertex Dyson-Schwinger equation that neglects explicit back-coupling of the vertex to itself, enabling application to bound-state calculations while retaining flavour dependence; the truncation is validated only for the specific case where internal quark-gluon vertices are replaced by a scalar function of gluon momentum, and the Abelian diagram is omitted entirely.

## Method

The quark-gluon vertex DSE was truncated to include only the non-Abelian one-loop diagram with a dressed three-gluon vertex at tree-level, while internal quark-gluon vertices were replaced by an Ansatz scalar function L1(p^2) fitted iteratively to match the computed gamma-mu component. The quark propagator and vertex were solved simultaneously for real space-like Euclidean momenta using a transverse orthonormal basis decomposition, with the gluon propagator taken from quenched Yang-Mills decoupling solutions. For the analytic continuation required in bound-state studies, the shell-method was employed to solve the quark DSE and vertex DSE on expanding parabolic shells in the complex momentum plane.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter approach to bound-states beyond the Rainbow-Ladder truncation of Maris and Roberts (1997, 1999), which used a flavour-independent effective interaction. The method enables systematic inclusion of the quark-gluon vertex structure while maintaining consistency with the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity, following earlier work by Bender (1996), Bhagwat (2004), and Fischer (2005, 2009). The results provide a framework for future bound-state calculations of meson spectra beyond ground-states, particularly for heavy-light systems and excited states where the h_5 and h_6 components of the vertex (related to the anomalous chromomagnetic moment) may be significant.
