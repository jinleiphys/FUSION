# Evidence for strong isovector nuclear spin-orbit interaction

> The nucleon spin-orbit interaction is a cornerstone of nuclear structure theory, yet its isospin dependence remains elusive owing to the lack of clean experimental probes. Here we show that the charge-weak form factor difference in $^{48}$Ca, recently extracted in a model-independent manner by the CREX experiment, exhibits strong sensitivity to the isovector spin-orbit interaction. Using Skyrme-like energy density functionals, we demonstrate that a significantly enhanced isovector spin-orbit interaction, about four times stronger than conventional parametrizations, can resolve the PREX-CREX puzzle, which has challenged modern nuclear theories and our understanding of nuclear symmetry energy, while maintaining a good description of nuclear bulk properties and well-established shell structure of finite nuclei. This enhanced isovector spin-orbit interaction also provides a novel mechanism for the emergence of the $N = 14$, $16$, $32$ and $34$ magic numbers in neutron-rich nuclei on the mean-field level. These findings point to a strong isospin dependence of the nucleon spin-orbit interaction, which is expected to have important implications for nuclear structures, electroweak nuclear processes, and related problems in nuclear astrophysics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a significantly enhanced isovector spin-orbit interaction, about four times stronger than conventional parametrizations, can resolve the PREX-CREX puzzle, the failure of modern energy density functionals to simultaneously reproduce the charge-weak form factor differences in 208Pb and 48Ca, while maintaining a good description of nuclear bulk properties and shell structure.

## Method

The authors construct extended Skyrme-like energy density functionals where the isovector spin-orbit strength bIV is treated as a free parameter, unlike conventional functionals that fix bIV = bIS/3. They fit new functionals to ground-state observables of doubly-magic nuclei, the isoscalar giant monopole resonance in 208Pb, and constraints on nuclear matter from heavy-ion collisions and microscopic calculations. Two representative functionals are compared: eS53 with conventional weak IVSO (bIV = 53 MeV fm5) and eS250 with strong IVSO (bIV = 250 MeV fm5), with all other parameters identical.

## Key numbers

- The CREX experiment reports ΔFCW48(q = 0.8733 fm-1) = 0.0277 ± 0.0055 for 48Ca
- The PREX-II experiment reports ΔFCW208(q = 0.3977 fm-1) = 0.041 ± 0.013 for 208Pb
- The strong IVSO functional eS250 has bIV = 250 MeV fm5, about four times larger than the conventional value bIV = 53 MeV fm5 (which equals bIS/3)
- The combined analysis yields L ≈ 58 MeV and bIV ≈ 250 MeV fm5
- The symmetry-energy slope L = 58.7 ± 28.1 MeV is the current world average value

## Context

This work addresses the PREX-CREX puzzle identified by the CREX collaboration (2022), where modern energy density functionals fail to simultaneously reproduce the charge-weak form factor differences in 208Pb and 48Ca. The paper extends prior Skyrme EDF approaches (SLy4 by Chabanat 1997, UNEDF0 by Kortelainen 2010, UNEDF1 by Kortelainen 2011) by freeing the isovector spin-orbit strength. The strong IVSO interaction provides a mean-field mechanism for the emergence of N = 14, 16, 32, and 34 magic numbers in neutron-rich nuclei, complementary to tensor-force effects described by Otsuka (2018). Future high-precision PVES experiments such as the Mainz Radius Experiment (Schlimme 2024) with targets having distinct SO shell structures will be crucial for pinning down the IVSO strength.
