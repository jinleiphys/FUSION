# Numerical Study of Spectrum and HBT Radii for Three-Dimensionally Expanding, Cylindrically Symmetric, Finite Systems

> This study is devoted to a detailed numerical testing of the analytical results obtained recently for the $M_t$-scaling of the parameters of the Bose-Einstein correlation functions. Numerical testing of analytical results for the spectrum is also performed, since the geometrical sizes are folded into the momentum distribution in the scaling limiting case. The analytical results are shown to be valid in a wider rapidity-range than thought before.

## Key claim

The analytical results for the $M_t$-scaling of Bose-Einstein correlation function parameters and the invariant momentum distribution, derived via saddle-point approximation, are shown to be valid over a wider rapidity range than previously thought, with the condition that the saddle-point approximation requires $\Delta\eta_*^2 \ll 1$ and $\Delta\tau_*^2 / \tau_s^2 \ll 1$, which imposes a lower limit in transverse mass.

## Method

The authors numerically integrated the model emission function for a three-dimensionally expanding, cylindrically symmetric finite system with boost-invariant longitudinal expansion and non-relativistic transverse flow, using the Wigner-function formalism. They compared the numerically evaluated Gaussian HBT radius parameters (in the Longitudinally Co-Moving System) and the invariant momentum distribution to the analytical results obtained from saddle-point integration, testing various combinations of geometrical sizes, transverse flow, and temperature gradients.

## Key numbers

- For $\Delta\eta = 0.3$ with $a = b = d = 0$, the agreement between analytical and numerical HBT radii and IMD is "almost perfect" (Figure 2).
- For $\Delta\eta = 1.2$ with $a = b = d = 0$, the relative error on HBT radii is maximal around $m_t = 240$ MeV for pions, being about 10%.
- For $\Delta\eta = 1.5$ with $a, b, d \neq 0$, the relative error on HBT radii is maximal around $m_t = 240$ MeV for pions, being about 20%.
- The condition for validity of the saddle-point approximation requires $\Delta\eta_*^2 \ll 1$ and $\Delta\tau_*^2 / \tau_s^2 \ll 1$, which for finite systems at mid-rapidity gives $m_t \gg T_0 (2 - 1/\Delta\eta^2)$.

## Context

This work extends the analytical framework developed by the Budapest group (Csorgo, Levai, Lorstad, 1994-1995) for $M_t$-scaling of HBT radii in expanding systems, providing the first detailed numerical verification of those results. The model incorporates features from earlier work by Sinyukov (1993) on homogeneity lengths and by the Marburg and Regensburg groups on out-longitudinal cross-terms. The numerical tests demonstrate that the analytical formulas remain reliable even for systems with large longitudinal sizes ($\Delta\eta \approx 1.5$) typical of 200 AGeV heavy ion collisions at CERN, enabling future simultaneous fits of HBT radii and invariant momentum distributions to extract both geometrical sizes and temperature/flow profiles.
