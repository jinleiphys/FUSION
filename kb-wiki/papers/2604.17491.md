# A Note on Coadjoint Orbits for Multifermion Systems

> The coadjoint orbit action for a multifermion system, as an exact description of its dynamics, is considered. A parametrization of the variables involved is given which facilitates the approximation of this by another coadjoint orbit action suitable for expansions around the Fermi surface, recovering various actions which have been used in previous literature. The presentation of this in terms of functions on phase space with star-products as well as further truncations are briefly considered.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the exact coadjoint orbit action for a multifermion system can be systematically reduced through a specific parametrization to the Hartree-Fock approximation and further to phase-space descriptions with star-products, but this reduction involves neglecting intrinsic many-particle correlations (the parameters \(\phi\) for clusters of two or more particles) and is only justified when such correlations are dynamically negligible.

## Method

The author parametrizes the general \(K\)-fermion state on \(\mathbb{CP}^{\mathcal{N}-1}\) (where \(\mathcal{N} = N!/(K!(N-K)!)\)) by separating the unitary transformations on the single-particle Hilbert space \(U(N)\) from additional parameters \(\phi_{a_1\ldots a_l, j_1\ldots j_l}\) that encode intrinsic two-particle and multi-particle correlations. Setting \(\phi = 0\) restricts the dynamics to the coset \(SU(N)/[SU(K)\times SU(N-K)\times U(1)]\), yielding the Hartree-Fock approximation with an occupancy matrix \(\rho_0\). The resulting action is then converted, using holomorphic wave functions from geometric quantization of a Kähler manifold (e.g., \(S^2\)), into a description in terms of symbols and star-products on phase space.

## Key numbers

* The total number of possible states for a \(K\)-fermion system with single-particle Hilbert space dimension \(N\) is \(\mathcal{N} = N!/(K!(N-K)!)\).
* For the \(S^2\) example, the dimension of the single-particle Hilbert space is \(N = n+1\), where \(n\) is an integer proportional to the magnetic flux on \(S^2\).
* The background magnetic field on \(S^2\) is \(B = n/(2r^2)\), where \(r\) is the radius of \(S^2\).
* The star-product expansion on \(S^2\) includes a term \(-(1/n) R_-(A) R_+(B)\) at first order, with explicit coordinate form \((1/n)(1+\bar{z}z)^2 (\partial (A)/\partial \bar{z})(\partial (B)/\partial z)\).

## Context

This work extends the coadjoint orbit approach to multifermion systems developed by Rosensteel, Rowe and others (1980s) for the Hartree-Fock approximation, and connects it to the bosonization methods used for quantum Hall droplets by Wadia, Sakita, and Karabali, Kim, Nair (1990s-2000s). The parametrization clarifies the assumptions behind truncating the exact dynamics to single-particle unitary transformations, and enables systematic inclusion of many-body correlations beyond Hartree-Fock. The star-product formulation provides a bridge to deformation quantization approaches such as those of Son (recent work), with the expansion parameter being inverse powers of the symplectic form rather than \(\hbar\).
