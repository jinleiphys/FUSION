# Quadrupole Strength in Isobaric Triplets

> The dependence of the $E2$ matrix elements on isospin projection $T_z$ is linked to the conservation of the isospin symmetry. To study this conjecture, we calculated the ${B(E2: 2^+ \rightarrow 0^+)}$ rates for the even-even $T=1$ mirror nuclei with $42$ $\leq$ $A$ $\leq$ $98$ within nuclear density functional theory, employing the generalized Bohr Hamiltonian, and carrying out angular momentum projection. We demonstrated that collective effects are crucial for describing experimental data near the $N=Z$ line without invoking explicit beyond-Coulomb isospin symmetry-breaking corrections. We also determined the $B(E2\downarrow)$ values for odd-odd $T_z=0$ nuclei $^{70}Br$ and $^{78}Y$ in doubly-blocked configurations. We discussed the requirements for accurately describing isobaric analog states and emphasized how current theoretical results should be interpreted within the study of isospin symmetry across isospin triplets.

## Key claim

Collective effects (shape mixing and zero-point energy corrections) are crucial for describing experimental B(E2) data near the N=Z line without invoking explicit beyond-Coulomb isospin symmetry-breaking corrections, but the authors note that the extension of the generalized Bohr Hamiltonian model to odd-odd nuclei with decoupled proton-neutron pairs still needs to be developed.

## Method

The authors performed nuclear density functional theory calculations using the UNEDF1 energy density functional for even-even T=1 mirror nuclei with 42 ≤ A ≤ 98, employing both the generalized Bohr Hamiltonian (GBH) approach and exact angular momentum projection (AMP). For the odd-odd Tz=0 nuclei 70Br and 78Y, they used doubly-blocked HFB calculations with AMP, breaking time-reversal and signature symmetries to project onto good J=0 and J=2 angular momenta. The GBH calculations included vibrational and rotational zero-point energy corrections and accounted for shape mixing across the (β,γ) deformation plane.

## Key numbers

- B(E2: 2+ → 0+) values were calculated for even-even T=1 mirror nuclei across the mass range 42 ≤ A ≤ 98
- The B(E2) in 70Kr was found to be around 60% larger than that of 70Se in prior experimental work (Wimmer 2021)
- The B(E2) in 78Sr is around 50% larger than in the N=Z system 78Y according to prior experimental work (Llewellyn 2020)
- The rotational zero-point energy correction exceeds 4 MeV difference between spherical and deformed minima for A=70 and A=78 triplets
- The total zero-point energy lowers deformed configurations by 2-3 MeV compared to uncorrected calculations
- The false-vacuum equilibrium quadrupole moments are Q20min ≈ -3.2 b for A=70 and Q20min ≈ 7.5 b for A=78 isotriplets
- The difference ΔB(E2) ≡ B(E2, Tz=-1) - B(E2, Tz=+1) is positive in all cases

## Context

This work extends the study of isospin symmetry in isobaric triplets, following experimental studies by Wimmer (2021) on the A=70 triplet and Llewellyn (2020) on the A=78 triplet, which showed deviations from the linear trend predicted by the isospin formalism of Bernstein. The authors demonstrate that shape mixing within the GBH framework provides better agreement with experimental data than single-reference AMP calculations, which overestimate B(E2) values for deformed minima in the A=74-84 region. The results enable future development of GBH models for odd-odd nuclei and provide a framework for interpreting isospin symmetry-breaking effects without invoking explicit beyond-Coulomb corrections.
