# Solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation for bound states of scalar theories in Minkowski space

> We apply the perturbation theory integral representation (PTIR) to solve for the bound state Bethe-Salpeter (BS) vertex for an arbitrary scattering kernel, without the need for any Wick rotation. The results derived are applicable to any scalar field theory (without derivative coupling). It is shown that solving directly for the BS vertex, rather than the BS amplitude, has several major advantages, notably its relative simplicity and superior numerical accuracy. In order to illustrate the generality of the approach we obtain numerical solutions using this formalism for a number of scattering kernels, including cases where the Wick rotation is not possible.

## Key claim

The paper claims that solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation directly for the bound-state vertex (rather than the amplitude) using the perturbation theory integral representation (PTIR) yields a real integral equation in two variables that is numerically tractable for arbitrary scattering kernels, including cases where Wick rotation is impossible. The hedge is that the results are derived only for scalar field theories without derivative coupling, and the numerical demonstrations are limited to equal-mass constituents.

## Method

The authors reformulate the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the bound-state vertex using the PTIR spectral representation for the vertex, the constituent propagators, and the scattering kernel. By combining these representations via Feynman parametrization and applying the uniqueness theorem of PTIR, they derive a real integral equation for the vertex weight function ρ_n(α,z) in two variables (α and z). They solve this equation numerically by iteration on a grid of α and z values, extracting the coupling eigenvalue λ = g²/(4π)² for fixed bound-state mass.

## Key numbers

- For the pure ladder kernel with m_σ = m/2, eigenvalues λ = g²/(4π)² were obtained for bound-state masses between P² = 0 and P² = 4m², with agreement to better than 0.03% compared to the Wick-rotated results of Linden and Mitter.
- For the dressed ladder kernel with m_σ = m and P² = 3.24m², the s-wave eigenvalue was λ = 1.516 on an 80 × 41 grid and λ = 1.518 on a 150 × 91 grid, compared to the Linden and Mitter value λ_E = 1.518.
- For the generalised kernel (ladder plus two random non-ladder terms) with P² = 1.44m², the s-wave eigenvalue was λ = 1.3569, compared to the pure ladder value λ = 1.9402 for the same bound-state mass.
- Solutions were obtained for orbital excitations up to ℓ = 4 with no difficulties.
- Relative accuracy of 1 part in 10⁴ was routinely achieved on a workstation.

## Context

This work extends and improves earlier PTIR approaches applied to the BS amplitude (Kusaka, Simpson, Williams, 1995) by solving for the vertex instead, which eliminates singularity structures present in the amplitude kernel and reduces numerical noise. The results agree with Euclidean-space calculations by Linden and Mitter (1984) and Nieuwenhuis and Tjon (1995) for ladder and dressed ladder kernels. The formalism enables solution of the BS equation for kernels where Wick rotation fails, and opens the way to applications involving crossed ladder kernels, separable kernels, and ultimately extensions to fermions and derivative coupling for meson studies in QCD.
