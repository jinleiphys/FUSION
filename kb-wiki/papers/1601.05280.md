# Search for scaling onset in exclusive reactions with the lightest nuclei

> The dimensional scaling of the differential cross sections of binary reactions $dσ/d t\sim s^{-(n-2)}$, where $n$ is given by constituent quark counting rules, was predicted for asymptotically high energies $\sqrt{s}\gg m_i$ and transferred momenta $-t\gg m_i^2$ at $t/s=const$ (here $s$ and $t$ are the Mandelstam variables and $m_i$ denotes a hadron mass), but manifested itself at surprisingly moderate energies of few GeV at large fixed cms angles $θ_{cm}\sim 90^\circ$. This behaviour is observed not only in reactions with free hadrons, but with the deuteron and $^3He$ too both for electromagnetic and pure hadronic interactions. One may suppose that observed scaling points out to effective restoration of near-conformal and, probably, chiral symmetry in these processes. A systematical experimental study of the scaling behaviour of the reactions with the deuteron, $^3H$, $^3He$, and $^4He$ nuclei is still absent. We consider a possibility to carry out this study in $dd$ collisions at the JINR Nuclotron.

## Key claim

The dimensional scaling predicted by constituent counting rules (CCR) for binary reactions may manifest at surprisingly moderate energies (few GeV) in reactions with light nuclei, but a systematic experimental study of this scaling behavior for reactions with deuteron, \(^3H\), \(^3He\), and \(^4He\) is still absent; the paper proposes to carry out such a study in \(dd\) collisions at the JINR Nuclotron, noting that the observed scaling may point to effective restoration of near-conformal and possibly chiral symmetry rather than perturbative QCD.

## Method

The author reviews existing data on binary reactions with light nuclei (deuteron, \(^3He\)) that follow the CCR scaling law \(d\sigma/dt \sim s^{-(n-2)}\), where \(n\) is the total number of constituent quarks. For the reaction \(dd \to ^3He n\) (or \(dd \to ^3H p\)), the scaling behavior is analyzed using the one-nucleon exchange (ONE) mechanism to relate the scaling onset to internal nucleon momenta \(q_{pn}\) in the deuteron. The paper then presents a proposal to measure the differential cross section of \(dd \to ^3H p\) at the BM@N facility at JINR Nuclotron for beam energies 1–2.5 GeV and center-of-mass angles \(\theta_{cm} \leq 50^\circ\), with counting rate estimates based on extrapolation from SATURNE data using the \(s^{-22}\) scaling law.

## Key numbers

- The reaction \(dd \to ^3He n\) (or \(dd \to ^3H p\)) has \(n = 24\) constituent quarks, giving the scaling law \(d\sigma/dt \sim s^{-22}\).
- At SATURNE, data for \(dd \to ^3He n\) at \(\theta_{cm} = 50^\circ - 60^\circ\) and \(T_d = 0.5 - 1.25\) GeV show the CCR behavior with \(\chi^2_{n.d.f.} = 1.18\).
- The scaling onset in \(dd \to ^3He n\) appears at internal momenta \(q_{pn} > 0.5\) GeV/c, which is twice lower than in \(\gamma d \to pn\) where scaling starts at \(q_{pn} > 1\) GeV/c.
- For the proposed measurement at JINR Nuclotron, the differential cross section at \(T_d = 1.234\) GeV and \(\theta_{cm} = 50^\circ\) is \((d\sigma/dt)_0 = (0.3 \pm 0.02) \mu b/\)GeV\(^2\).
- At \(T_d = 2.2\) GeV and \(\theta_{lab} = 16^\circ - 17^\circ\) (corresponding to \(\theta_{cm} = 48^\circ - 50^\circ\)), the expected number of events in 24 hours is \(N = 108\) at luminosity \(L = 1.35 \times 10^{30} \text{cm}^{-2}\text{sec}^{-1}\).
- To measure \(d\sigma/d\Omega_{cm}\) at \(\theta_{cm} = 50^\circ\) for six energies (1.3, 1.5, 1.6, 1.8, 2.0, 2.2 GeV) with statistical uncertainty \(\leq 4\%\) (\(N \geq 600\) events), two weeks of beam time are needed.

## Context

This work extends the study of dimensional scaling from free hadron reactions (e.g., \(pp\) elastic scattering, \(\gamma p \to \pi^+ n\)) to reactions involving light nuclei, following observations of CCR behavior in \(\gamma d \to pn\) at SLAC (Schulte et al., Bochna et al.) and in \(^3He(\gamma,pp)n\) at JLab (Pomerantz et al., Ilieva et al.). The paper builds on an earlier analysis by Uzikov (2005) that found scaling in \(dd \to ^3He n\) and \(dp \to dp\) reactions, and contrasts the scaling onset in different reactions (e.g., \(\gamma d \to pn\) vs. \(pp \to d\pi^+\)) to argue for a mechanism involving effective restoration of chiral symmetry (Glozman, 2007). The proposed measurements at JINR Nuclotron would determine the boundaries of the scaling region for \(dd \to ^3H p\) and enable a broader experimental program including reactions such as \(dd \to dd\), \(pd \to ^3H \pi^+\), and \(pp \to d\rho^+\).
