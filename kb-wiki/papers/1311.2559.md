# Paramagnetic squeezing of QCD matter

> We determine the magnetization of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) for several temperatures around and above the transition between the hadronic and the quark-gluon phases of strongly interacting matter. We obtain a paramagnetic response that increases in strength with the temperature. We argue that due to this paramagnetism, chunks of quark-gluon plasma produced in non-central heavy ion collisions should become elongated along the direction of the magnetic field. This anisotropy will then contribute to the elliptic flow v_2 observed in such collisions, in addition to the pressure gradient that is usually taken into account. We present a simple estimate for the magnitude of this new effect and a rough comparison to the effect due to the initial collision geometry. We conclude that the paramagnetic effect might have a significant impact on the value of v_2.

## Key claim

The paramagnetic response of QCD matter, measured via lattice simulations, produces a force that elongates quark-gluon plasma along the magnetic field direction in non-central heavy-ion collisions; this "paramagnetic squeezing" may contribute significantly to elliptic flow \(v_2\), but the authors present only a first estimate based on equilibrium thermodynamics and note that a full dynamical treatment is needed for a quantitative conclusion.

## Method

The magnetization of QCD was computed using lattice simulations with tree-level Symanzik improved gluonic action and stout smeared staggered quarks for three light flavors at physical quark masses, on lattices of size \(N_s^3 \times N_t\) with \(N_t = 6, 8, 10\). The magnetization was extracted from the difference of lattice pressures parallel and perpendicular to the magnetic field, using the relation \(M \cdot eB \approx \sum_f A(\mathcal{F}_f)\), where the fermionic anisotropy \(A(\mathcal{F}_f)\) is the difference between the parallel and perpendicular components of the Dirac operator. The divergent \(\mathcal{O}((eB)^2)\) contribution at zero temperature was subtracted to obtain the renormalized magnetization \(M^r\).

## Key numbers

- The coefficient of the divergent \(\mathcal{O}((eB)^2)\) term in the magnetization at zero temperature is \(0.016(4)\), consistent with the leading-order perturbative QED \(\beta\)-function coefficient \(\beta_1^{\mathrm{QED}} \approx 0.0169\) for three quark flavors with \(N_c=3\) colors.
- The linear magnetic susceptibility \(\chi_1\) at \(T = 300\ \mathrm{MeV}\) is \(3.9(6) \times 10^{-2}\) (dimensionless in the units used; to obtain SI units multiply by \(e^2 \approx 4\pi/137\)).
- The estimated pressure gradient difference from paramagnetic squeezing is \(|\Delta \mathcal{P}| \approx 0.007\ \mathrm{GeV}/\mathrm{fm}^4\) for magnetic fields of order \(5\, m_\pi^2\) (typical for RHIC energies) and \(|\Delta \mathcal{P}| \approx 0.7\ \mathrm{GeV}/\mathrm{fm}^4\) for fields of \(50\, m_\pi^2\) (typical for LHC energies).
- The estimated geometric pressure gradient difference is \(|\Delta p^{\mathrm{g}}| \approx 0.1\ \mathrm{GeV}/\mathrm{fm}^4\) for RHIC and \(|\Delta p^{\mathrm{g}}| \approx 1\ \mathrm{GeV}/\mathrm{fm}^4\) for LHC energies.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature lattice measurements of Bali et al. (2013) to finite temperatures around and above the QCD transition, confirming the paramagnetic nature of the QCD vacuum predicted by the hadron resonance gas model of Endrodi (2013). The results provide a first estimate of how paramagnetic squeezing could modify elliptic flow \(v_2\) in heavy-ion collisions, suggesting that the effect may be a 10% correction at RHIC energies and comparable to geometric pressure gradients at LHC energies; the authors call for dynamical model calculations incorporating both the time-dependent magnetic field and the onset of hydrodynamization to refine these estimates.
