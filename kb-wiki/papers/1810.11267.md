# An exact solution of spherical mean-field plus orbit-dependent non-separable pairing model with two non-degenerate j-orbits

> An exact solution of nuclear spherical mean-field plus orbit-dependent non-separable pairing model with two non-degenerate j-orbits is presented. The extended one-variable Heine-Stieltjes polynomials associated to the Bethe ansatz equations of the solution are determined, of which the sets of the zeros give the solution of the model, and can be determined relatively easily. A comparison of the solution to that of the standard pairing interaction with constant interaction strength among pairs in any orbit is made. It is shown that the overlaps of eigenstates of the model with those of the standard pairing model are always large, especially for the ground and the first excited state. However, the quantum phase crossover in the non-separable pairing model cannot be accounted for by the standard pairing interaction.

# An exact solution of spherical mean-field plus orbit-dependent non-separable pairing model with two non-degenerate j-orbits

## Key claim

The paper presents an exact Bethe-ansatz solution for a nuclear spherical mean-field plus orbit-dependent non-separable pairing model with two non-degenerate j-orbits, showing that eigenstate overlaps with the standard pairing model are always large (above 94% in the example studied), but the quantum phase crossover in the non-separable model cannot be reproduced by the standard pairing interaction.

## Method

The authors construct eigenstates as a product of Gaudin-Richardson-type pairing operators acting on a seniority-based vacuum state, where the spectral parameters are determined by Bethe ansatz equations. They derive the commutators of the Hamiltonian with the collective pairing operator, expressing them in terms of the operators S+ and S+(x) using binomial expansions that are only possible for the two-orbit case. The resulting Bethe ansatz equations are mapped to an extended Heine-Stieltjes polynomial problem, whose zeros provide the exact solution.

## Key numbers

- Single-particle energies used in the example: epsilon_1 = 1 MeV, epsilon_2 = 2 MeV
- Angular momenta of the two orbits: j_1 = 19/2, j_2 = 21/2, each accommodating 5 pairs
- On-site pairing interaction parameters fixed at g_{1,1} = g_{2,2} = 1 MeV
- Ground-state energy for delta_g = -0.50 MeV: -48.95 MeV; for delta_g = 0.50 MeV: -90.78 MeV
- Overlap-square eta(zeta) between NSPM and SPM ground states ranges from 99.600% (delta_g = -0.50) to 99.934% (delta_g = 0.50)
- Overlap-square for the first excited state (0+_2) ranges from 98.989% (delta_g = -0.50) to 99.842% (delta_g = 0.50)
- Quantum phase crossover in occupation probability occurs around g ~ 0.05-0.1 MeV, with a crossing point near g ~ 0.21 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Gaudin-Richardson exact solution method, previously applied to the standard pairing model (Richardson, 1963; Dukelsky et al., 2004) and to separable pairing models (Pan et al., 2013; Balantekin et al., 2007), to the more general case of orbit-dependent non-separable pairing interactions. The solution is limited to two non-degenerate j-orbits because the binomial expansion of commutators used in the derivation is only possible for p=2 orbits; for three or more orbits, p(p+1)/2 independent terms would be needed. The results enable systematic comparison between non-separable and standard pairing models, showing that while standard pairing approximates the non-separable model well for strong inter-orbit pairing, it fails to capture quantum phase crossovers relevant to ds- and fp-shell nuclei.
