# A brief overview of hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory

> The poor convergence of quantum field theory at finite temperature has been one of the main obstacles in the practical applications of thermal QCD for decades. Here we briefly review the progress of hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) in reorganizing the perturbative expansion in order to improve the convergence. The quantum mechanical anharmonic oscillator is used as a simple example to show the breakdown of weak-coupling expansion, and variational perturbation theory is introduced as an effective resummation scheme for divergent weak-coupling expansions. We discuss HTLpt thermodynamic calculations for QED, pure-glue QCD, and QCD with N_f=3 up to three-loop order. The results suggest that HTLpt provides a systematic framework that can be used to calculate both static and dynamic quantities for temperatures relevant at LHC.

## Key claim

Hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) provides a systematic, gauge-invariant reorganization of finite-temperature perturbation theory that improves the convergence of thermodynamic calculations for gauge theories at intermediate coupling (g ~ 2), but the method requires a prescription for the Debye mass and thermal quark mass parameters, and at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) the variational mass prescription yields a complex Debye mass for QCD, necessitating the use of perturbative mass parameters instead.

## Method

The authors review HTLpt, which adds and subtracts a hard-thermal-loop (HTL) effective action to the QCD Lagrangian, treating the subtracted piece as a formal expansion parameter δ. The thermodynamic potential is calculated through three-loop (NNLO) order by expanding Feynman diagrams in powers of m_D/T and m_q/T, keeping terms through order g^5. The mass parameters m_D and m_q are then fixed either by variational gap equations or by perturbative expressions, and the renormalization scale μ is varied around 2πT to estimate theoretical uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- For QED with N_f=1 at e=2.4 and μ=2πT, the variational and perturbative mass prescriptions agree to within 0.6% for the scaled pressure at NNLO.
- The NNLO HTLpt pressure for QED with N_f=1 agrees with a three-loop Φ-derivable approximation at the subpercentage level even at large coupling.
- For pure-glue QCD, the NNLO HTLpt pressure (using the Braaten-Nieto Debye mass and m_q=0) is compared to lattice data from the Bielefeld collaboration (Boyd 1996) with the renormalization scale varied by a factor of 2 around μ=2πT.
- For QCD with N_f=3, the NNLO HTLpt pressure is compared to N_f=2+1 lattice data from the hotQCD (Bazavov 2009) and Wuppertal-Budapest (Borsanyi 2010) collaborations, with the same scale variation.
- The weak-coupling expansion for QCD pressure with N_f=3 through order g^5 shows oscillations and no convergence in the temperature range probed by RHIC and LHC, using three-loop running for α_s with Λ_MSbar(N_f=3)=344 MeV.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on screened perturbation theory (SPT) for scalar field theories (Karsch 1997, Chiku 1998, Andersen 2000) to gauge theories via HTLpt, which was introduced by Andersen, Braaten, and Strickland (1999) and developed through three-loop order for QED (Andersen 2009) and QCD (Andersen 2009, 2010, 2011). The results enable systematic calculations of both static thermodynamic quantities and dynamic quantities for temperatures relevant at LHC, and they contradict the poor convergence of naive weak-coupling expansions (Arnold 1994, Braaten 1995) by providing a resummation scheme that matches lattice data at intermediate coupling.
