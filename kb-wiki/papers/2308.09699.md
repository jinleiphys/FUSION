# Emergent interaction-driven elliptic flow of few fermionic atoms

> Hydrodynamics provides a successful framework to effectively describe the dynamics of complex many-body systems ranging from subnuclear to cosmological scales by introducing macroscopic quantities such as particle densities and fluid velocities. According to textbook knowledge, it requires coarse graining over microscopic constituents to define a macroscopic fluid cell, which is large compared to the interparticle spacing and the mean free path. In addition, the entire system must consist of many such fluid cells. In high energy heavy ion collisions, hydrodynamic behaviour is inferred from the observation of elliptic flow. Here, we demonstrate the emergence of elliptic flow in a system of few strongly interacting atoms. In our system a hydrodynamic description is a priori not applicable, as all relevant length scales, i.e. the system size, the inter-particle spacing, and the mean free path are comparable. The single particle resolution, deterministic control over particle number and interaction strength in our experiment allow us to explore the boundaries between a microscopic description and a hydrodynamic framework in unprecedented detail.

## Key claim

Elliptic flow, the characteristic inversion of the initial aspect ratio driven by anisotropic pressure-gradient forces, emerges in a system of as few as ten strongly interacting fermionic atoms, even though all relevant length scales (system size, interparticle spacing, and mean free path) are comparable and a hydrodynamic description is a priori not applicable.

## Method

The authors prepared mesoscopic systems of 1+1 to 5+5 spin-up and spin-down ultracold fermionic ⁶Li atoms in the ground state of an elliptically shaped, effectively two-dimensional harmonic trap. After switching off the horizontal confinement, they measured the positions or momenta of each atom at various interacting expansion times using single-atom-resolved fluorescence imaging, with interactions tuned via a magnetic Feshbach resonance. The experimental results were compared to ideal hydrodynamic simulations using the measured initial density and the equation of state of a two-dimensional macroscopic Fermi gas.

## Key numbers

- Trap frequencies: ω_x/(2π) = 1280(3) Hz, ω_y/(2π) = 3384(7) Hz, giving harmonic oscillator lengths l_HO^x ≈ 1.1 μm and l_HO^y ≈ 0.7 μm.
- Mean interparticle spacing estimated as 1/√n₀ ≈ 1.3 μm for the non-interacting system.
- For 5+5 atoms at interaction parameter ln(k⁰_F a_2D) = 1.15, the characteristic inversion of the initial aspect ratio in real space occurs after t_int ≳ 250 μs.
- The build-up of momentum anisotropy subsides at t_int ≈ 160 μs.
- After 9 ms expansion, the many-body limit prediction for the final aspect ratio δr_x/δr_y is 0.52.
- For 1+1 and 2+2 atoms, the interacting system behaves as the non-interacting case within error; significant deviations signalling interaction-driven inversion start at 3+3 atoms.

## Context

This work extends observations of elliptic flow from high-energy heavy-ion collisions (Ollitrault, 1992) and macroscopic ultracold quantum gases (Thomas, 2002; Fletcher, 2018) down to the few-body regime, linking to the observation of collectivity in small systems such as proton-proton collisions (Khachatryan, 2010) and integrable systems (Malvania, 2021). The experiment enables detailed exploration of the boundary between microscopic and hydrodynamic descriptions, and may relate to the chemical freeze-out of the quark-gluon plasma (Andronic, 2017). Future work will investigate connections to superfluidity, as seen in helium nanodroplets (Grebenev, 1998), and exotic strongly correlated quantum states (Palm, 2020).
