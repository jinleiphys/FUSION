# Quark properties from the Hadron Resonance Gas

> We show how the quark free energy can be determined from a string and the Hadron Resonance Gas model with one heavy quark below the de-confinement phase transition. We discuss the interesting problem of identification of degrees of freedom at increasing temperatures, as well as the relevance of string breaking and avoided crossings.

# Quark properties from the Hadron Resonance Gas

## Key claim

The heavy quark free energy below the deconfinement phase transition can be determined from a string model combined with the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) description, provided that the hadronic spectrum saturates the thermodynamic sum rules up to temperatures as high as 170–180 MeV, close to the critical temperature. The analysis relies on the assumption that avoided crossings between the heavy quarkonium and heavy-light meson channels are not resolved by finite temperature, making the crossings effectively diabatic.

## Method

The authors compute the color-averaged heavy quark free energy from the Polyakov loop correlator, using a two-channel model that includes the Cornell potential for the heavy quarkonium ground state and the spectrum of heavy-light mesons (charm and bottom) from the relativized quark model (RQM) of Godfrey and Isgur (1985) and Capstick and Isgur (1986). The free energy is obtained by summing over the diabatic energy levels without mixing, using Eq. (2) which separates the heavy quarkonium contribution from the heavy-light meson contribution. The cumulative hadron spectrum is also extracted from lattice trace anomaly data via a chi-squared fit to the HRG expression.

## Key numbers

- The string tension is taken as sigma = (0.42 GeV)^2 for the Cornell potential, and alternatively sigma = (0.4 GeV)^2 in the free energy calculation.
- The HRG description with PDG or RQM spectra saturates lattice trace anomaly data up to T less than or approximately 175 MeV, using N_dat = 10 lattice points from Borsanyi et al. (2013) and Bazavov et al. (2014).
- A fit to the cumulative number of states N(M) yields A = 1.5 and T_H = 300 MeV with chi^2/nu = 0.94 for a threshold mass M_th = 2 GeV and maximum temperature T_max = 185 MeV.
- The critical distance for string breaking is estimated as r_c approximately 1.2–1.4 fm, using the dynamical quark mass M_0.
- The deuteron binding energy is B_d = 2.2 MeV, and the deuteron does not contribute to the thermodynamic counting due to Levinson's theorem.
- The heavy-light meson spectrum is included up to Delta = 3.19 GeV for charm and bottom quarks, with a mixing strength of g = 50 MeV between fundamental and excited states.
- The Polyakov loop computed from the heavy-light meson spectrum agrees with lattice data for T < 0.8 T_c, with a renormalization constant C = -40 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the HRG approach to include heavy quarks, building on earlier lattice QCD calculations of the trace anomaly by Borsanyi et al. (2013) and Bazavov et al. (2014), and on the Polyakov loop free energy data of Kaczmarek et al. (2005). The analysis uses the relativized quark model of Godfrey and Isgur (1985) and Capstick and Isgur (1986) to provide a complete spectrum of heavy-light mesons, which the PDG lacks for heavy quarks. The results enable a determination of the hadronic spectrum directly from lattice thermodynamics and clarify the role of string breaking and avoided crossings in the heavy quark free energy, with implications for understanding the crossover to the quark-gluon plasma.
