# Statistical analysis of excitation energies in the actinide and rare-earth nuclei

> Statistical analysis of distributions of the collective states in the actinide and rare-earth nuclei is performed in terms of the nearest neighbor spacing distribution (NNSD). Several approximations, such as the linear approach to the level repulsion density and that suggested by Brody to the NNSDs were applied for the analysis. We found an intermediate character of the experimental spectra between the order and the chaos for a number of the rare-earth and actinide nuclei. They are more close to the Wigner distribution for energies limited by 3 MeV, and to the Poisson distribution for data including higher excitation energies and higher spins. The latter is in agreement with the theoretical calculations. These features are confirmed by the cumulative distributions, where the Wigner contribution dominates at smaller spacings while the Poisson one is more important at larger spacings.

## Key claim

The experimental spectra of collective states in actinide and rare-earth nuclei show an intermediate character between order and chaos, being closer to the Wigner distribution for excitation energies limited to 3 MeV and closer to the Poisson distribution when higher excitation energies and higher spins are included, with the latter behavior agreeing with theoretical calculations.

## Method

The authors performed a statistical analysis of nearest neighbor spacing distributions (NNSDs) for sequences of collective 0⁺, 2⁺, 4⁺, and 6⁺ states identified via (p,t) transfer reactions in actinide and rare-earth nuclei. They applied two fitting approaches: a linear Wigner-Dyson (LWD) approximation to the level repulsion density (a two-parameter model) and the Brody distribution (a one-parameter model), using least-square fitting to compare experimental NNSDs with theoretical Poisson and Wigner limits.

## Key numbers

- For rare-earth 0⁺ states up to 3 MeV: LWD fit gives 39% Poisson contribution (a = 0.43) and 61% Wigner contribution (b = 0.69); Brody parameter q = 0.48
- For ¹⁵⁸Gd and ¹⁶⁸Er 0⁺ states up to 4 MeV: LWD fit gives 76% Poisson (a = 0.83) and 24% Wigner (b = 0.26); Brody parameter q = 0.20
- For actinide 0⁺ states up to 3 MeV: LWD fit gives 21% Poisson (a = 0.27) and 79% Wigner (b = 1.01); Brody parameter q = 0.58
- For actinide 2⁺ states: LWD fit gives 41% Poisson (a = 0.52) and 59% Wigner (b = 0.75); Brody parameter q = 0.38
- For actinide 4⁺ states: LWD fit gives 62% Poisson (a = 0.67) and 38% Wigner (b = 0.41); Brody parameter q = 0.28
- For actinide 6⁺ states: LWD fit gives 50% Poisson (a = 0.52) and 50% Wigner (b = 0.52); Brody parameter q = 0.42
- Total level counts: 128 rare-earth 0⁺ states below 3 MeV; 58 states in ¹⁵⁸Gd and ¹⁶⁸Er below 4 MeV; 78-153 levels per actinide nucleus across all spins

## Context

This work extends earlier statistical analyses of nuclear level spacings by Shriner et al. (1990, 1991, 2004) and Gomez et al. (2011) to long sequences of collective states in deformed nuclei, using data from (p,t) transfer reactions that provide more complete level sequences than previously available. The results enable comparison with theoretical predictions from the quasiparticle-phonon model, showing agreement for the trend toward Poisson statistics at higher excitation energies, and suggest that the degree of chaoticity depends on both excitation energy and angular momentum in deformed nuclei.
