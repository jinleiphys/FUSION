# Combined Analysis of Near-Threshold Production of omega and phi Mesons in Nucleon-Nucleon Collisions within an Effective Meson-Nucleon Model

> Vector meson ($V = ω, ϕ$) production in near-threshold elementary nucleon-nucleon collisions $pp\to ppV$, $pn\to pnV$ and $pn\to dV$ is studied within an effective meson-nucleon theory. It is shown that a set of effective parameters can be established to describe fairly well the available experimental data of angular distributions and the energy dependence of the total cross sections without explicit implementation of the Okubo-Zweig-Iisuka rule violation. Isospin effects are considered in detail and compared with experimental data whenever available.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a consistent set of effective parameters within a meson-nucleon model can describe near-threshold omega and phi meson production in nucleon-nucleon collisions without explicitly invoking Okubo-Zweig-Iizuka (OZI) rule violation, but the apparent violation seen in cross-section ratios arises from dynamical effects (different bremsstrahlung and conversion diagram contributions, interference, and phase-space differences) rather than exotic nucleon components.

## Method

The authors compute covariant amplitudes for vector meson production in pp → ppV, pn → pnV, and pn → dV processes using an effective meson-nucleon theory with interaction Lagrangians for scalar (σ), pseudoscalar (π), and vector (ρ, ω, φ) mesons. They include two types of Feynman diagrams: bremsstrahlung-type (meson emission from nucleon lines via VNN vertices) and internal conversion-type (ρπV vertices). Parameters are fixed by fitting pp → ppω and pp → ppφ angular distribution data from COSY-TOF and DISTO experiments, with final-state interaction (FSI) effects treated via the Jost function formalism. For the pn → dV channel, the Bethe-Salpeter formalism is employed with the deuteron described by a BS amplitude.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant g_{ρπω} = 12.9 GeV^{-1} (fixed from radiative decays and vector meson dominance)
- Coupling constant g_{ρπφ} = -1.1 GeV^{-1} (from φ → ρπ decay width Γ = 0.69 MeV)
- Cutoff Λ_{ρπω} = 980 MeV (adjusted to COSY-TOF ω angular distribution data)
- Cutoff Λ_{ρπφ} = 1340 MeV (from combined analysis with DISTO data)
- g_{ωNN} = 9.0 (with κ_ω = 0.5)
- g_{φNN} = -0.8 (from g_{φNN} = -g_{ωNN} tan Δθ with Δθ ≈ 5°)
- Cutoff Λ_{ωNN} = Λ_{φNN} = 1500 MeV
- Nucleon cutoff Λ_N = 720 MeV
- Excess energy for ω angular distribution fit: Δs^{1/2} = 173 MeV
- Excess energy for φ angular distribution fit: Δs^{1/2} = 83 MeV
- FSI effects become negligible at excess energy Δs^{1/2} > 200 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of vector meson production in nucleon-nucleon collisions by Nakayama (2001) and others, going beyond pp reactions to include pn channels and the deuteron final state. It provides a combined analysis that enables reliable input for simulating heavy-ion and proton-nucleus collisions, particularly relevant for ongoing HADES experiments. The authors show that the OZI rule violation suggested by cross-section ratios at equal excess energies is an apparent effect due to different diagram contributions and phase-space differences, and that at equal beam energies the ratios become compatible with naive OZI predictions, resolving discrepancies noted in DISTO data (2000).
