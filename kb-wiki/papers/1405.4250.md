# RHIC and LHC jet suppression in non-central collisions

> Understanding properties of QCD matter created in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions is a major goal of RHIC and LHC experiments. An excellent tool to study these properties is jet suppression of light and heavy flavor observables. Utilizing this tool requires accurate suppression predictions for different experiments, probes and experimental conditions, and their unbiased comparison with experimental data. With this goal, we here extend our dynamical energy loss formalism towards generating predictions for non-central collisions; the formalism takes into account both radiative and collisional energy loss, dynamical (as opposed to static) scattering centers, finite magnetic mass, running coupling and uses no free parameters in comparison with experimental data. Specifically, we here generate predictions for all available centrality ranges, for both LHC and RHIC experiments, and for four different probes (charged hadrons, neutral pions, D mesons and non-prompt $J/ψ$). We obtain a very good agreement with all available non-central data, and also generate predictions for suppression measurements that will soon become available. Finally, we discuss implications of the obtained good agreement with experimental data with different medium models that are currently considered.

## Key claim

The authors extend their dynamical energy loss formalism to generate jet suppression predictions for non-central heavy-ion collisions, finding very good agreement with all available data across multiple probes and centrality ranges. The key hedge is that this agreement holds for momentum ranges larger than 10 GeV, and the formalism does not explicitly incorporate medium evolution, instead using effective/average medium parameters.

## Method

The authors extend their existing dynamical energy loss formalism, which includes both radiative and collisional energy loss, dynamical scattering centers, finite magnetic mass, and running coupling, to non-central collisions. Path-length distributions for different centrality ranges are obtained from Dainese (2004), and temperatures for each centrality region are determined from charged particle multiplicity measurements using the relation T^3 ~ (dN_g/dy)/V, with constants fixed by ALICE and PHENIX effective temperature measurements. The numerical procedure integrates these with parton production, fragmentation functions, and multi-gluon and path-length fluctuations to generate suppression predictions with no free parameters.

## Key numbers

- Effective temperature of 304 MeV used for 0-40% centrality Pb+Pb collisions at LHC (2.76 TeV), as extracted by ALICE
- Effective temperature of 221 MeV used for 0-20% centrality Au+Au collisions at RHIC (200 GeV), as extracted by PHENIX
- Number of effective light quark flavors n_f = 2.5 for RHIC and n_f = 3 for LHC
- Perturbative QCD scale Lambda_QCD = 0.2 GeV
- Charm mass M = 1.2 GeV, bottom mass M = 4.75 GeV
- Magnetic mass ratio range 0.4 < mu_M/mu_E < 0.6 used for all predictions
- Momentum ranges for centrality dependence plots: charged hadrons 6-12 GeV, D mesons 8-16 GeV, non-prompt J/psi 6.5-30 GeV at LHC; neutral pions above 7 GeV at RHIC

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous predictions for most central collisions (Djordjevic and Djordjevic, 2014) to all available centrality ranges at both RHIC and LHC, testing four different probes (charged hadrons, neutral pions, D mesons, and non-prompt J/psi). The robust agreement with data above 10 GeV suggests that for hard probes, medium expansion does not play a major role in explaining jet propagation, in contrast to approaches that simplify energy loss to more explicitly incorporate evolving medium (such as Bass et al., 2009; Betz and Gyulassy, 2014; Xu and Greiner, 2005; Gossiaux et al., 2013). The results enable future mapping of validity ranges for different medium models.
