# One- and two-neutron halos in effective field theory

> Published version that incorporates minor changes. To appear in "Few-body Systems" as part of the Proceedings of the 22nd European Conference on Few-body Problems in Physics, Krakow, Poland, September 9-13, 2013

## Key claim

Halo effective field theory (EFT) can extract neutron-core scattering parameters from Coulomb dissociation data for one-neutron halos and, for two-neutron halos, can constrain the two-neutron separation energy using measured matter radii, but the accuracy is limited by the expansion parameter (approximately 0.4 for 19C and approximately 0.5 for 8Li) and requires input from ab initio calculations or experimental data to fix low-energy constants.

## Method

For one-neutron halos, the author's group derived the dipole transition strength dB(E1)/dE for 19C in terms of the binding momentum γ0 and effective range r0 of the n-18C interaction, then fitted these parameters to experimental Coulomb dissociation angular and energy distributions from Nakamura et al. (1999, 2003). For the 7Li(n,γ)8Li capture, they used asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) from Variational Monte Carlo calculations with the Argonne v18 NN potential and UIX 3N force to fix leading-order EFT parameters, then predicted the capture cross section. For the two-neutron halo 22C, they applied universal EFT relations connecting the matter radius to the two-neutron separation energy B and the neutron-core virtual-state energy Enc, using the experimental matter radius from Tanaka et al. (2010) to constrain these parameters.

## Key numbers

- One-neutron separation energy of 19C: S1n(19C) = (575 ± 55 (stat.) ± 20 (EFT)) keV
- Effective range of n-18C interaction: r0 = (2.6 +0.6/-0.9 (stat.) ± 0.1 (EFT)) fm
- Root-mean-square matter radius of 22C: ⟨rm2⟩1/2 = 5.4 ± 0.9 fm (from Tanaka et al. 2010)
- Effective range for n-7Li scattering in the 8Li channel: r1 = -1.43 fm-1
- Binding energy of 8Li with respect to 7Li-n threshold: 2.03 MeV
- One-neutron separation energy of 18C: S1n(18C) = 4.2 MeV
- Breakup energy of 7Li → t + 4He: 2.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends Halo EFT, previously applied to p-wave bound states in 5He and 8Li by Rupak et al. and to two-neutron halos by Canham and Hammer, to new systems including 19C, 8Li, and 22C. For 19C, the EFT description affirms the dominance of the s-wave configuration of the valence neutron and agrees with the longitudinal momentum distribution data of Bazin et al. (1998). For 8Li, the approach improves on the earlier EFT calculation of Rupak et al. by using ab initio ANCs, and the predicted ratio of capture into ground and excited states agrees within 1% with experiment. For 22C, the analysis puts a model-independent upper limit of 100 keV on the two-neutron separation energy, and the recent finding by Mosby et al. (2013) of no low-energy resonance in 21C suggests the separation energy is less than 20 keV, motivating future Coulomb dissociation calculations.
