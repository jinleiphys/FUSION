# Effect of Chiral Symmetry Restoration on Pentaquark $Θ^+$ Mass and Width at Finite Temperature and Density

> We investigate the effect of chiral phase transition on the pentaquark $Θ^+$ mass and width at one-loop level of $NΘ^+K$ coupling at finite temperature and density. The behavior of the mass, especially the width in hadronic medium is dominated by the characteristics of chiral symmetry restoration at high temperature and high density. The mass and width shifts of positive-parity $Θ^+$ are much larger than that of negative-parity one, which may be helpful to determine the parity of $Θ^+$ in high energy nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The mass and width shifts of the pentaquark $\Theta^+$ in hot and dense nuclear matter are dominated by the characteristics of chiral symmetry restoration, and the shifts for positive-parity $\Theta^+$ are much larger than for negative-parity $\Theta^+$, which may help determine the parity of $\Theta^+$ in high energy nuclear collisions.

## Method

The authors calculate the $\Theta^+$ self-energy at one-loop level of $N\Theta^+ K$ coupling using both pseudovector and pseudoscalar effective Lagrangians, at finite temperature and density in the imaginary time formalism. The in-medium nucleon mass is obtained from the mean-field gap equation of the NJL model, which describes chiral symmetry breaking and restoration. The $\Theta^+$ mass and width shifts are then extracted from the pole of the in-medium propagator in the $\Theta^+$ rest frame.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum $\Theta^+$ mass set to $M_\Theta = 1540$ MeV and vacuum decay width set to $\Gamma_{\Theta^+} = 15$ MeV.
- Pseudovector coupling constants: $g_A^\star = 0.28$ for positive parity, $g_A^\star = 0.16$ for negative parity.
- Pseudoscalar coupling constants: $g = 3.8$ for positive parity, $g = 0.53$ for negative parity.
- Kaon mass fixed at $M_K = 494$ MeV.
- At $T = 200$ MeV and $\mu = 1000$ MeV, mass shifts are: $-115$ MeV ($PS^+$), $-40$ MeV ($PV^+$), $-10$ MeV ($PV^-$), $-2$ MeV ($PS^-$).
- Maximum width shift is 17 times the vacuum value for $PS^+$ and 7 times for $PV^+$.
- At SIS conditions ($T = 150$ MeV, $\mu = 700$ MeV), width shift for $PV^+$ is 57.6 MeV (380% of vacuum), for $PS^+$ is 115.1 MeV (760% of vacuum).
- At RHIC conditions ($T = 200$ MeV, $\mu = 50$ MeV), width shift for $PV^+$ is 1.1 MeV (7% of vacuum), for $PS^+$ is 3.5 MeV (23% of vacuum).

## Context

This work extends studies of the pentaquark $\Theta^+$ in medium, following earlier density-dependent calculations by Navarra (2004) using a phenomenological nucleon propagator, and production studies in the coalescence model by Chen (2004). The authors use the NJL model (Nambu and Jona-Lasinio, 1961) to incorporate chiral symmetry restoration, which is not captured by simple density-dependent models. The results enable predictions for $\Theta^+$ production and decay in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC, SPS, and SIS energies, and suggest that parity determination may be possible through medium-induced width measurements.
