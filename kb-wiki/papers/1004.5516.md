# The Status of Exotic-quantum-number Mesons

> The search for mesons with non-quark-antiquark (exotic) quantum numbers has gone on for nearly thirty years. There currently is experimental evidence of three isospin one states, the $π_{1}(1400)$, the $π_{1}(1600)$ and the $π_{1}(2015)$. For all of these states, there are questions about the identification of these state, and even if some of them exist. In this article, we will review both the theoretical work and the experimental evidence associated with these exotic quantum number states. We find that the $π_{1}(1600)$ could be the lightest exotic quantum number hybrid meson, but observations of other members of the nonet would be useful.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the π₁(1600) could be the lightest exotic-quantum-number hybrid meson, but cautions that observations of other members of the nonet would be useful to confirm this identification, and that questions remain about the existence and identification of all three candidate isospin-one states (π₁(1400), π₁(1600), and π₁(2015)).

## Method

The authors review theoretical predictions for exotic-quantum-number mesons from multiple models (bag model, QCD spectral sum rules, flux-tube model, Coulomb-gauge Hamiltonian, and lattice QCD) and compare these predictions to experimental evidence for the π₁(1400), π₁(1600), and π₁(2015) states. They also examine decay-mode predictions from the ³P₀ model and flux-tube model calculations to assess the hybrid nature of observed candidates.

## Key numbers

- Bag model predictions for the 1⁻⁺ exotic hybrid mass: 1.0 to 1.4 GeV (Barnes 1982, Chanowitz 1982)
- QSSR calculations favor a 1⁻⁺ hybrid mass in the range of 1.6 to 2.1 GeV (Narison 2000)
- Flux-tube model predicts all eight hybrid nonets degenerate in mass near 1.9 GeV (Isgur 1985)
- Coulomb-gauge Hamiltonian predicts the 1⁻⁺ exotic hybrid mass in the range of 2.1 to 2.3 GeV (Cotanch 2001)
- Lattice QCD predictions for the uū/dd̅ 1⁻⁺ hybrid mass range from 1.74 ± 0.25 GeV (Hedditch 2005) to 2.11 ± 0.10 ± (sys) GeV (MILC 1999)
- SESAM collaboration lattice results: π₁(1⁻⁺) at 1.9 ± 0.2 GeV/c², b₂(2⁺⁻) at 2.0 ± 1.1 GeV/c², b₀(0⁺⁻) at 2.3 ± 0.6 GeV/c² (Lacock 1998)
- Bernard (2004) calculated the splitting between 0⁺⁻ and 1⁻⁺ states as 0.270 ± 0.2 GeV
- Predicted decay widths for the π₁(1⁻⁺) exotic hybrid: 81-168 MeV (PSS model) and 117 MeV (IKP model) (Page 1999)
- Predicted decay widths for the b₂(2⁺⁻) exotic hybrid: 5-11 MeV (PSS model) and 248 MeV (IKP model) (Page 1999)

## Context

This review extends the long-standing search for mesons with non-quark-antiquark quantum numbers, building on early bag-model calculations by Barnes (1982) and Chanowitz (1982), flux-tube model work by Isgur (1985), and lattice QCD predictions from collaborations including UKQCD (Lacock 1997), MILC (Bernard 1997, 1999), SESAM (Lacock 1998), and Dudek (2009, 2010). The paper enables future experimental searches by providing a comprehensive comparison of theoretical mass predictions and decay patterns, and highlights that recent lattice calculations by Dudek (2010) now allow assessment of the gluonic content of calculated states, with work underway to extend results to the isoscalar sector and to extract flavor mixing angles.
