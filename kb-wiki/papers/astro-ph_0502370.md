# A case for nucleosynthesis in slowly evolving models

> We present a case for Cosmological Nucleosynthesis in an FRW universe in which the scale factor expands linearly with time: $a(t) \sim t$. It is demonstrated that adequate amount of $^4He$ requires a baryon density that saturates mass bounds from galactic clusters. There is a collataral metallicity production that is quite close to the lowest metallicity observed in metal poor Pop II stars and clouds. On the other hand, sites for incipient low metallicity (Pop II) starformation can support environments conducive to Deuterium production up to levels observed in the universe. A profile of a revised ``Standard Cosmology'' is outlined.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a linearly coasting FRW universe (scale factor \(a(t) \sim t\)) can produce adequate primordial \(^4He\) and metallicity close to the lowest observed values, but only if the baryon density is high enough to saturate mass bounds from galactic clusters; the main difficulty, low residual deuterium, is then circumvented by spallation in incipient low-metallicity (Pop II) star-forming environments, provided that the accelerated particle beam is deficient in \(^4He\) and the target has very low heavy-element content.

## Method

The authors adapted standard big-bang nucleosynthesis (SBBN) network codes to a linearly coasting FRW cosmology with curvature \(K = -1\), using the present age \(t_o = H_o^{-1} \approx 15 \times 10^9\) years and CMB temperature 2.73 K. They computed light-element abundances as a function of the baryon-entropy ratio \(\eta\) and compared the resulting \(^4He\) mass fraction and metallicity to observations. For deuterium, they revisited spallation reactions (e.g., \(p + ^4He \rightarrow D + ^3He\)) and argued that the "no-go" constraint of Epstein et al. (1976) can be avoided if the incident beam is depleted in alpha particles and the target cloud has very low metallicity, conditions expected in incipient Pop II star formation.

## Key numbers

- Baryon-entropy ratio required for adequate \(^4He\): \(\eta \approx 7.8 \times 10^{-9}\).
- Resulting \(^4He\) mass fraction: approximately 23.9% (by mass).
- Corresponding baryon density parameter: \(\Omega_b \approx 0.69\).
- Metallicity produced alongside \(^4He\): roughly \(10^{-5}\) times solar metallicity.
- Age of the universe in this model: \(15 \times 10^9\) years (from \(H_o = 65\ \mathrm{km\ s^{-1}\ Mpc^{-1}}\)).
- Last scattering surface redshift: \(z^* = 1037\) with thickness \(\Delta z \approx 68\) (standard cosmology: \(z = 1065\), \(\Delta z \approx 80\)).
- Hubble angle at last scattering: \(\theta_H \approx 15.5\) minutes.
- For density perturbations: modes with \(k \ge 3000\) can grow from \(D_g \sim 10^{-15}\) at last scattering to nonlinear regime by redshift \(\sim 50\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of linearly coasting cosmologies by Sethi, Lohiya, and collaborators (e.g., "meetu", "annu", "pranav") and directly challenges the standard big-bang nucleosynthesis (SBBN) paradigm, which relies on a single parameter \(\eta\) to fit light-element abundances over nine orders of magnitude. The authors cite Epstein et al. (1976) for the "no-go" argument against deuterium production via spallation, and they reference observational tensions such as low \(^4He\) values in objects like \(\mu\) Cassiopeiae A (5%) and SBS 0335-052 (\(Y_p = 0.21 \pm 0.01\)), as well as high deuterium in Lyman-alpha systems. The paper enables a revised cosmology that does not require non-baryonic cold dark matter or a generation of Pop III stars, instead relying on a high baryon density (\(\Omega_b \approx 0.69\)) and spallation in early Pop II environments to produce deuterium.
