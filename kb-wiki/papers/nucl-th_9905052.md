# Scalar-isoscalar mesons in pion production at threshold

> We present the contributions of the non-linear chiral scalar field S to cross sections of $pp\to dπ^{+}, pp\to pnπ^{+}$ and $pp\to ppπ^0$ reactions at threshold. We compare our results with the meson $σ$ contribution from the $σ$-linear model. We show that the chiral scalar field S is almost 5 times bigger than the other contributions, which indicates that the two-pion exchange dynamics embebbed in the S field is the most important term for the pion production at threshold.

# Scalar-isoscalar mesons in pion production at threshold

## Key claim
The non-linear chiral scalar field S, which simulates two-pion exchange dynamics, provides the dominant contribution to near-threshold pion production cross sections in pp collisions, being almost 5 times larger than other contributions. This resolves the long-standing factor-of-5 discrepancy between data and calculations based on impulse approximation plus pion rescattering, but the authors emphasize this is a demonstration of the mechanism's importance rather than a precise fit to data.

## Method
The authors computed cross sections for pp → dπ⁺, pp → pnπ⁺, and pp → ppπ⁰ at threshold using a non-linear chiral Lagrangian for the scalar-isoscalar field S, which includes an additional SπNN contact vertex absent in the σ-linear model. They extracted the irreducible amplitude in the center-of-mass frame, took the non-relativistic limit keeping terms up to order ω/m, and evaluated matrix elements between initial and final NN states using the Argonne v18 potential with a cutoff mass Λ_S = 2000 MeV for form factors.

## Key numbers
- The chiral scalar S contribution to σ/η for pp → dπ⁺ at η = 0.01 is 262 μb, compared to 0.18 μb for the σ-linear meson, 110 μb for the Weinberg-Tomosawa term, and 0.39 μb for the impulse approximation.
- The scalar meson mass was set to m_S = m_σ = 550 MeV and the coupling constant to g_S = g_σ = 8.7171 for comparison between models.
- The cutoff mass for form factors was Λ_S = 2000 MeV.
- The new term in the chiral S amplitude is enhanced by a factor 2m/ω_q ≈ 13 at threshold (where ω_q ≈ μ, the pion mass).

## Context
This work extends earlier attempts to explain near-threshold pion production, where impulse approximation and pion rescattering (Koltun and Reitan, 1966) underestimated cross sections by a factor of 5 (Miller and Sauer, 1991). Previous approaches using heavy meson exchange (Lee and Riska, 1993; Horowitz et al., 1994; Hanhart et al., 1995) and chiral perturbation theory (Cohen et al., 1996) also failed to reproduce data by the same factor. The chiral S field provides an effective representation of two-pion exchange loops, suggesting that second-order contributions in χPT are dominant at threshold. The authors note that a detailed comparison of the chiral S with long-range terms and a systematic extraction of the S mass from NN potentials are in progress.
