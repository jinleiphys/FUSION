# Enhanced antiproton production in Pb(160 AGeV)+Pb reactions: evidence for quark gluon matter?

> The centrality dependence of the antiproton per participant ratio is studied in Pb(160 AGeV)+Pb reactions. Antiproton production in collisions of heavy nuclei at the CERN/SPS seems considerably enhanced as compared to conventional hadronic physics, given by the antiproton production rates in $pp$ and antiproton annihilation in $\bar{p}p$ reactions. This enhancement is consistent with the observation of strong in-medium effects in other hadronic observables and may be an indication of partial restoration of chiral symmetry.

# Enhanced antiproton production in Pb(160 AGeV)+Pb reactions: evidence for quark gluon matter?

## Key claim

The observed factor-of-3 enhancement of the antiproton-per-participant ratio in Pb+Pb collisions at 160 AGeV, compared to expectations from hadronic transport models calibrated to pp data, is consistent with an increased string tension by a factor of 2.6 (enhancing diquark production) but the exact cause remains ambiguous; the enhancement may indicate partial restoration of chiral symmetry or quark-gluon plasma formation, yet the flat centrality dependence suggests such exotic mechanisms would need to operate even in peripheral collisions where conditions are less favorable.

## Method

The authors employed the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model, a microscopic transport model with hadronic and constituent (di)quark degrees of freedom, to simulate Pb(160 AGeV)+Pb collisions. They compared the standard UrQMD predictions for the antiproton-per-participant ratio as a function of centrality (number of participating nucleons) against NA49 collaboration data, and then performed two modified calculations: one with antiproton annihilation turned off entirely, and another with enhanced antiproton production achieved by increasing the string tension by a factor of 2.6 (raising the diquark suppression parameter gamma_qq from 0.095 to 0.4).

## Key numbers

- Standard UrQMD underestimates the antiproton-per-participant ratio in Pb+Pb at 160 AGeV by a factor of 3 compared to NA49 data, despite overestimating antiproton production in pp collisions by 50% at sqrt(s) = 20 GeV.
- The diquark suppression parameter gamma_qq in UrQMD is 0.095 for pp collisions (using light quark mass m_q = 0.223 GeV, diquark mass m_qq = 2m_q, string tension kappa = 1 GeV/fm = 0.2 GeV^2); to describe the AA data, gamma_qq must be enhanced to 0.4, corresponding to an effective string tension kappa' = 0.511 GeV^2 (a factor of 2.6 increase).
- With antiproton annihilation turned off, the antiproton-per-participant ratio increases towards central collisions, inconsistent with the flat centrality dependence observed in NA49 data.
- The enhanced production scenario (constant enhancement adjusted to most central collisions) yields a flat antiproton-per-participant ratio versus centrality, in line with data.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Bleicher et al. (1999) that centrality dependence of hadron ratios involving antibaryons can distinguish equilibrium from non-equilibrium scenarios. The results contradict purely hadronic transport predictions and are consistent with predictions by Koch et al. and Ellis et al. of a factor 2.5-10 enhancement of antibaryon production due to QGP formation, as well as with the observed (anti-)hyperon enhancement at SPS energies. The study enables future experimental measurement of antiproton anti-flow (v1) as a direct probe of the in-medium antiproton-proton annihilation cross section, which could distinguish between enhanced production and suppressed annihilation scenarios.
