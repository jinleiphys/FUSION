# $╬ś^+$ baryon, $N^* (1685)$ resonance, and $ŽĆN$ sigma term in the context of the LEPS and DIANA experiments

> We reexamine properties of the baryon antidecuplet $╬ś^+$ and $N^*$, and the $ŽĆN$ sigma term within the framework of a chiral soliton model, focusing on their dependence on the $╬ś^+$ mass. It turns out that the measured value of the $N^*$ mass, $M_{N^*}=1686$ MeV, is consistent with that of the $╬ś^+$ mass $M_{╬ś^+}=1524$ MeV by the LEPS collaboration. The $N^*\to N╬│$ magnetic transition moments are almost independent of the $╬ś^+$ mass. The ratio of the radiative decay width $╬ō_{nn^*}$ to $╬ō_{pp^*}$ turns out to be around 5. The decay width for $╬ś^+\to NK$ is studied in the context of the LEPS and DIANA experiments. When the LEPS value of the $╬ś^+$ mass is employed, we obtain $╬ō_{╬śNK} =(0.5\pm0.1)$ MeV. The $ŽĆN$ sigma term is found to be almost independent of the $╬ś^+$ mass. In addition, we derive a new expression for the $ŽĆN$ sigma term in terms of the isospin mass splittings of the hyperon octet as well as that of the antidecuplet $N^*$.

## Key claim

The measured mass of the $N^*(1685)$ resonance is consistent with the LEPS collaboration value of the $\Theta^+$ mass ($M_{\Theta^+}=1524$ MeV) within the chiral soliton model, but only when isospin symmetry breaking effects are included; the DIANA value of $M_{\Theta^+}$ yields a larger $N^*$ mass that deviates from the experimental data.

## Method

The authors used a chiral soliton model ($\chi$SM) with collective quantization, including SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking and isospin symmetry breaking, to compute masses, magnetic transition moments, decay widths, and the $\pi N$ sigma term for the baryon antidecuplet. Parameters were fixed using baryon octet masses, the $\Omega^-$ mass, and the $\Theta^+$ mass, with the $\Theta^+$ mass then treated as a free parameter to study the dependence of observables. The $N^*\to N\gamma$ transition magnetic moments and $\Theta^+\to NK$ decay width were computed from collective operators parameterized by six free parameters fitted to octet magnetic moments and semileptonic decays.

## Key numbers

- $M_{N^*} = 1686 \pm 7 \pm 5$ MeV (Kuznetsov and Polyakov, 2011) is the experimental value of the $N^*$ resonance mass.
- $M_{\Theta^+} = 1524 \pm 2 \pm 3$ MeV/$c^2$ (LEPS collaboration, 2008) is the $\Theta^+$ mass used as input.
- $M_{\Theta^+} = 1538 \pm 2$ MeV/$c^2$ (DIANA collaboration, 2009) is the alternative $\Theta^+$ mass.
- $\Gamma_{\Theta NK} = (0.5 \pm 0.1)$ MeV is the predicted decay width for $\Theta^+ \to NK$ when using the LEPS $\Theta^+$ mass.
- $\Gamma_{nn^*}/\Gamma_{pp^*} = 4.36 \pm 1.02$ is the ratio of radiative decay widths for $N^*\to N\gamma$.
- $\sigma_{\pi N} = (36.4 \pm 3.9)$ MeV is the predicted $\pi N$ sigma term from the present work.
- $M_{N^*} \simeq 1690$ MeV is the predicted $N^*$ mass when using the LEPS $\Theta^+$ mass of 1524 MeV.
- $M_{N^*} = 1708$ MeV is the predicted $N^*$ mass when using the DIANA $\Theta^+$ mass.

## Context

This work extends the chiral soliton model analysis of Yang (2010) by systematically studying the dependence of antidecuplet observables on the $\Theta^+$ mass, in the context of the LEPS and DIANA experiments. It contrasts with earlier analyses by Diakonov (1997) and Ellis (2004), which used different parameter sets and predicted $N^*$ masses of 1700 MeV and 1631 MeV respectively, and with Schweitzer (2003) who obtained $\sigma_{\pi N} = (74 \pm 12)$ MeV using different input masses. The results enable future tests of the antidecuplet interpretation of the $N^*(1685)$ resonance and provide a new expression for the $\pi N$ sigma term in terms of isospin mass splittings.
