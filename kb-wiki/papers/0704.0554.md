# In-medium effects on particle production in heavy ion collisions

> The effect of possible in-medium modifications of nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) cross sections on particle production is investigated in heavy ion collisions ($HIC$) at intermediate energies. In particular, using a fully covariant relativistic transport approach, we see that the density dependence of the {\it inelastic} cross sections appreciably affects the pion and kaon yields and their rapidity distributions. However, the $(π^{-}/π^{+})$- and $(K^{0}/K^{+})$-ratios depend only moderately on the in-medium behavior of the inelastic cross sections. This is particularly true for kaon yield ratios, since kaons are more uniformly produced in high density regions. Kaon potentials are also suitably evaluated in two schemes, a chiral perturbative approach and an effective meson-quark coupling method, with consistent results showing a similar repulsive contribution for $K^{+}$ and $K^{0}$. As a consequence we expect rather reduced effects on the yield ratios. We conclude that particle ratios appear to be robust observables for probing the nuclear equation of state ($EoS$) at high baryon density and, particularly, its isovector sector.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while the absolute yields of pions and kaons in heavy ion collisions are appreciably affected by the density dependence of in-medium inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross sections, the particle ratios (π⁻/π⁺ and K⁰/K⁺) depend only moderately on these in-medium modifications, making them robust observables for probing the nuclear equation of state, particularly its isovector sector.

## Method

The authors used a fully covariant relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) transport approach to simulate heavy ion collisions. In-medium inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross sections for the NN → NΔ channel were taken from Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations and extended via an exponential decay law with a density-dependent correction factor f(ρB) = 1 + a₀(ρB/ρ₀) + a₁(ρB/ρ₀)² + a₂(ρB/ρ₀)³. Kaon potentials were evaluated in two independent schemes: a chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) approach and an effective meson-quark coupling (OBE/RMF) method, with the latter being fully consistent with the covariant transport equations.

## Key numbers

- The inelastic NN → NΔ cross section threshold in free space is E_tr = √s = 2.014 GeV, corresponding to a laboratory energy of 0.32 GeV
- The kaon-nucleon sigma term Σ_KN was chosen as 450 MeV, with the pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV and free kaon mass m_K = 494 MeV
- At ρ_B = 2ρ₀ with isospin asymmetry α = 0.2, the K⁰ and K⁺ in-medium energies differ by approximately 5% in the ChPT approach (with E_K⁰ > E_K⁺)
- In the OBE/RMF approach, the isospin splitting of kaon potentials at ρ_B = 2ρ₀ and α = 0.2 is about 1.5% for the NLρ Lagrangian and about 3.0% for the NLρδ Lagrangian
- The NL2 parametrization used for symmetric nuclear matter has a compression modulus of 200 MeV and a Dirac effective mass of m* = 0.82 M at saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends previous investigations by Ferini et al. (2006) on the isovector equation of state by testing the robustness of particle ratios against in-medium modifications of cross sections and kaon potentials that were neglected in those earlier studies. The paper is motivated by new measurements from the FOPI collaboration (Lopez, 2006) and builds on the suggestion by Aichelin and Ko (1985) that strangeness ratios can probe the high-density nuclear equation of state, as well as on studies by Fuchs et al. (2006) and Oeschler et al. (2006) that used kaon yield ratios to favor a soft nuclear EoS. The results enable more reliable extraction of the symmetry energy from experimental particle ratio data by demonstrating that these ratios are robust against uncertainties in in-medium cross sections and kaon potentials.
