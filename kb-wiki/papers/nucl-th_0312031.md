# On the nuclear symmetry energy and the neutron skin in neutron-rich nuclei

> The symmetry energy for nuclear matter and its relation to the neutron skin in finite nuclei is discussed. The symmetry energy as a function of density obtained in a self-consistent Green function approach is presented and compared to the results of other recent theoretical approaches. A partial explanation of the linear relation between the symmetry energy and the neutron skin is proposed. The potential of several experimental methods to extract the neutron skin is examined.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the symmetry energy of nuclear matter and the neutron skin thickness in heavy nuclei are correlated, but the relationship is only approximate and depends on the many-body approximation used; the self-consistent Green function (SCGF) approach yields a lower symmetry energy at saturation (24.9 MeV) than the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) method (28.9 MeV), while the slope parameter remains nearly unchanged.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Green function (SCGF) calculations for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter using the Reid93 nucleon-nucleon interaction, extending earlier SCGF work on symmetric matter to pure neutron matter. They compared these results with continuous-choice Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (ccBHF) calculations using the same interaction, and with variational, Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock, relativistic mean-field, and effective field theory results from the literature. They also used a Landau-Migdal mean-field model to derive a linear relation between the Landau parameter f' and the neutron skin thickness in 208Pb, and reviewed several experimental methods for extracting the neutron skin.

## Key numbers

- SCGF symmetry energy at saturation density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}: a_4 = 24.9 MeV (compared to 28.9 MeV in ccBHF)
- SCGF slope parameter p_0 = 1.99 MeV fm^{-3} (compared to 2.11 MeV fm^{-3} in ccBHF)
- BHF symmetry energy at rho_0 using modern potentials: average a_4 = 29.83 MeV, spread about 1 MeV
- Variational calculations with Av18+UIX: a_4 = 29.23 MeV, p_0 = 3.24 MeV fm^{-3}; with Av18+delta v+UIX*: a_4 = 30.1 MeV, p_0 = 2.95 MeV fm^{-3}
- Relativistic mean-field models: a_4 ~ 30-36 MeV, p_0 ~ 4-6 MeV fm^{-3}
- Effective field theory (chiral perturbation): a_4 = 33 MeV
- Neutron skin in 208Pb from antiprotonic atoms: 0.15 +/- 0.02 fm
- Neutron skin in 208Pb from (p,p') at 0.5-1.04 GeV: 0.097 +/- 0.014 fm
- Neutron skin in 208Pb from nucleon scattering (40-200 MeV): 0.17 fm
- Neutron skin in 208Pb from giant dipole resonance excitation: 0.19 +/- 0.09 fm
- Neutron skin in 208Pb from antiprotonic atoms: 0.15 +/- 0.02 fm
- All recent results (except one) consistent with Delta R ~ 0.13 +/- 0.03 fm

## Context

This work extends the discussion of the correlation between symmetry energy and neutron skin first pointed out by Brown (2000) and Furnstahl (2002) in mean-field models, by testing it with more microscopic many-body methods (SCGF and BHF). The paper compares its SCGF results with variational calculations by Wiringa et al. (1988) and Akmal et al. (1998), Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations by Lee et al. (1998) and Toki et al. (1999), and effective field theory by Kaiser et al. (2002). The finding that the SCGF symmetry energy lies below the BHF result and close to variational results suggests that correlations beyond BHF reduce the symmetry energy, and that the small experimental neutron skin (0.13 fm) favors a small slope parameter p_0 ~ 2 MeV/fm^3 over the larger values predicted by relativistic mean-field models. The paper enables future experimental tests via parity-violating electron scattering (planned at the time) and improved measurements of isovector giant resonances.
