# Baryon number in proton-proton and proton-nucleus high energy collisions

> New analyses of baryon spectra in proton-proton and proton-carbon collisions at $\sqrt{s}_\mathrm{_{NN}}=17.3$ GeV, made in the framework of two phenomenological models are presented. The first model in question is the classic Dual Parton Model by Capella and Tran Thanh Van, the second is the Gluon Exchange Model very recently proposed by the authors. For both studies, the usage of modern experimental data from the CERN SPS eliminates several of the most important limitations inherent to earlier studies of this type. In both studies, the standard mechanism of baryon stopping with preservation of the diquark, proposed by Capella and Tran Thanh Van fails to describe the distribution of non-strange baryons in collisions of the projectile proton with {\em more than one} nucleon from the carbon target obtained from experimental data, and the upper limit for the contribution of this mechanism can be established. In both cases, the conclusion is that the projectile diquark must be very often disintegrated. This opens new diagrams not available in proton-proton collisions which lead to the transport of baryon number over long distances in rapidity. The present limitations, and possibility of improvement in both approaches are discussed. The implications of our findings for new measurements are addressed, in particular using antiproton beams.

## Key claim

The standard mechanism of baryon stopping with preservation of the diquark, proposed by Capella and Tran Thanh Van, fails to describe the distribution of non-strange baryons in collisions of the projectile proton with more than one nucleon from the carbon target; the authors conclude that the projectile diquark must be very often disintegrated, opening new diagrams not available in proton-proton collisions which lead to transport of baryon number over long distances in rapidity.

## Method

The authors analyzed net proton and net neutron spectra in proton-proton and proton-carbon collisions at a center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair of 17.3 GeV, using data from the NA49 experiment at the CERN SPS. They applied two phenomenological models: the Dual Parton Model (DPM) by Capella and Tran Thanh Van, and the Gluon Exchange Model (GEM) recently proposed by the authors. For the DPM analysis, they used a Glauber simulation to separate single and multiple proton-nucleon collisions in the pC data, then compared the model predictions for multiple collisions to the experimental data, introducing a diquark-disintegrating diagram as an alternative mechanism.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair: $\sqrt{s}_\mathrm{NN}=17.3$ GeV (beam energy 158 GeV at CERN SPS)
- The integration of NA49 proton and neutron spectra over the entire projectile hemisphere of pp collisions gives unity within an accuracy better than 5%
- Upper limit for the contribution of the standard diquark-preserving mechanism in multiple proton-nucleon collisions: about two thirds (model simulation scaled by 0.6)
- Probabilities of diquark preservation and disintegration in the best tuning: 0.66 and 0.34, respectively
- Sea quark transverse mass $\mu$ used in the quark distribution formula: 0.3 GeV/$c^2$
- The isospin correction $\Delta(dn/dy)$ from the difference between pp and pN collisions does not exceed 0.01 at rapidity $y\sim 0.5$

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of baryon stopping in the Dual Parton Model by Capella and Tran Thanh Van and by Jezabek (1985), using modern experimental data from the NA49 experiment that eliminate limitations of earlier studies, particularly the lack of full projectile hemisphere coverage and reliance on assumptions about isospin flip into neutrons. The analysis resolves an old controversy in favor of Busza regarding nuclear stopping power, and the findings enable future measurements using antiproton beams to further investigate baryon number transport. The authors note that the fragmentation function of the "Y object" (three quarks with no diquark) displays a characteristic symmetric, double-peaked shape completely different from a standard D-q chain, and the isospin flip function for this object exhibits a very strong isospin effect at the limit of conceivability.
