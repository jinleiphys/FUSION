# Spin-triplet proton-neutron pair in spin-dipole excitations

> Background: Spin-triplet ($S=1$) proton-neutron (pn) pairing in nuclei has been under debate. It is well known that the dynamical pairing affects the nuclear matrix element of the Gamow-Teller (GT) transition and the double beta decay. Purpose: We investigate the effect of the pn-pair interaction in the $T=0, S=1$ channel on the low-lying spin-dipole (SD) transition. We then aim at clarifying the distinction of the role in between the SD and GT transitions. Method: We perform a three-body model calculation for the transition ${}^{80}\mathrm{Ni}\to{}^{80}\mathrm{Cu}$, where ${}^{78}\mathrm{Ni}$ is taken as a core. The strength of the pair interaction is varied to see the effect on the SD transition-strength distribution. To fortify the finding obtained by the three-body model, we employ the nuclear energy-density functional method for the SD transitions in several nuclei, where one can expect a strong effect. Results: The effect of the $S=1$ pn-pair interaction depends on the spatial overlap of the pn pair and the angular momentum of the valence nucleons; the higher the angular momentum of the orbitals, the more significant the effect. Conclusions: The dynamical $S=1$ pairing is effective even for SD states although the spatial overlap of the pn pair can be smaller than GT states. The SD transition involving high-$\ell$ orbitals with the same principal quantum number is strongly affected by the dynamical $S=1$ pairing.

## Key claim

The effect of the spin-triplet (S=1) proton-neutron pair interaction on spin-dipole (SD) transitions depends on the spatial overlap of the pair and the angular momentum of the valence nucleons; the higher the angular momentum of the orbitals, the more significant the effect, even though the spatial overlap is generally smaller than in Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions.

## Method

The authors performed a three-body model calculation for the transition 80Ni → 80Cu, with 78Ni taken as a core, varying the strength of the S=1 pair interaction to observe its effect on the SD transition-strength distribution. To fortify these findings, they employed the nuclear energy-density functional (EDF) method with the SGII functional for SD transitions in several nuclei (132,134Zr, 120,122Sn, and 68,70Ni), using a self-consistent Kohn-Sham-Bogoliubov and proton-neutron quasiparticle-random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) calculation.

## Key numbers

* In the three-body model for 80Ni → 80Cu, the diagonal matrix element of the S=1 pair interaction for the ν2d5/2 ⊗ π2p3/2 configuration was -0.27 MeV, and for the ν2d5/2 ⊗ π1f5/2 configuration was -0.44 MeV, with fNN=1.7.
* The ground-state binding energy of the three-body system 78Ni + 2n was 4.0 MeV, comparable to the estimated value of 4.5 MeV.
* In the nuclear DFT calculation for 132Zr and 134Zr, the low-energy SD 0- state appeared at ET = -15.2 MeV and -16.0 MeV, respectively, with the neutron occupation probability of the 1h9/2 orbital being 0.04 and 0.06.
* In the SD 2- transition, the neutron occupation probability of the 1h11/2 orbital was 0.28 in 120Sn and 0.37 in 122Sn; the neutron occupation probability of the 1g9/2 orbital was 0.09 in 68Ni and 0.23 in 70Ni.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of S=1 pn-pairing effects on Gamow-Teller transitions, notably the three-body model work by Tanimura and collaborators (2014, 2016) and the nuclear EDF calculations by Bai and collaborators (2014), which found low-energy GT states with strong strength. The present paper investigates the less-studied spin-dipole transitions, where the principal quantum numbers of the single-particle orbitals differ by one unit (ΔN=1) rather than ΔN=0 as in GT transitions, and finds that the S=1 pairing is still effective, especially for high-ℓ orbitals. This enables more accurate predictions of first-forbidden β-decay rates in neutron-rich nuclei, which are sensitive to the S=1 pair interaction.
