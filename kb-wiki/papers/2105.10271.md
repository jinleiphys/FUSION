# Positive magnetoresistance induced by hydrodynamic fluctuations in chiral media

> We analyze the combined effects of hydrodynamic fluctuations and chiral magnetic effect (CME) for a chiral medium in the presence of a background magnetic field. Based on the recently developed non-equilibrium effective field theory, we show fluctuations give rise to a CME-related positive contribution to magnetoresistance, while the early studies without accounting for the fluctuations find a CME-related negative magnetoresistance. At zero axial relaxation rate, the fluctuations contribute to the transverse conductivity in addition to the longitudinal one.

## Key claim

The authors show that hydrodynamic fluctuations in a chiral medium produce a positive contribution to magnetoresistance related to the chiral magnetic effect (CME), whereas earlier studies that neglected fluctuations found only a CME-related negative magnetoresistance; this positive contribution arises from nonlinear couplings among density fluctuations and modifications to the dispersion relation of fluctuation modes.

## Method

The authors construct a non-equilibrium effective field theory (EFT) for hydrodynamic fluctuations of vector and axial charge densities in a chiral medium, incorporating the chiral anomaly, axial charge relaxation, and the CME. They expand the action around thermal equilibrium, derive Feynman rules for propagators and cubic vertices, and compute one-loop corrections to the conductivity tensor using the Kubo formula. The analysis is performed for both finite and zero axial relaxation rate \(r\).

## Key numbers

* The anomaly coefficient is \(C = 1/(2\pi^2)\).
* The tree-level longitudinal conductivity is \((\sigma_{\parallel})_{\text{tree}}(\omega) = (\sigma_{\text{V}})_0 + \frac{C^2 \mathbf{B}^2 r}{\chi_{\text{A}}(r^2 + \omega^2)}\), reproducing the known CME-induced negative magnetoresistance.
* The tree-level transverse conductivity is \((\sigma_{\perp})_{\text{tree}}(\omega) = (\sigma_{\text{V}})_0\).
* The velocity of the chiral magnetic wave is defined as \(\mathbf{v} \equiv \frac{C\mathbf{B}}{\sqrt{\chi_{\text{V}} \chi_{\text{A}}}}\).
* The axial relaxation rate is defined as \(r \equiv \gamma / \chi_{\text{A}}\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of magnetoresistance in chiral media beyond the tree-level results of Son and Surowka (2012) and Stephanov and Yin (2014), which found negative magnetoresistance from the CME without fluctuations. It uses the non-equilibrium EFT framework developed by Crossley, Glorioso, and Liu (2015, 2017) and Glorioso, Crossley, and Liu (2017) for including anomalies. The results may apply to physical systems such as the quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions, Weyl semimetals, and the electroweak plasma in the primordial Universe, and they complement diagrammatic calculations for QGP by Fukushima, Hidaka, and Pu (2017, 2019).
