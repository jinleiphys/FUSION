# Intruder bands and configuration mixing in the lead isotopes

> A three-configuration mixing calculation is performed in the context of the interacting boson model with the aim to describe recently observed collective bands built on low-lying $0^+$ states in neutron-deficient lead isotopes. The configurations that are included correspond to the regular, spherical states as well as two-particle two-hole and four-particle four-hole excitations across the Z=82 shell gap.

# Intruder bands and configuration mixing in the lead isotopes

## Key claim
A three-configuration mixing calculation within the interacting boson model (IBM) can describe the recently observed collective bands built on low-lying 0⁺ states in neutron-deficient lead isotopes, but the slope with which intruder states gain binding energy toward midshell is too steep, indicating that intruder-spin (I-spin) symmetry is not satisfied completely.

## Method
The authors performed an IBM configuration-mixing calculation including three configurations: regular 0p-0h spherical states (N bosons), 2p-2h intruder excitations (N+2 bosons), and 4p-4h intruder excitations (N+4 bosons) across the Z=82 shell gap. The Hamiltonian for each configuration contained a d-boson energy term and a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, with mixing between configurations induced by a two-boson transfer operator. Parameters for the intruder configurations were determined using I-spin symmetry arguments, taking experimental data from analogous bands in adjacent Pt nuclei (for 2p-2h) and W nuclei (for 4p-4h).

## Key numbers
- The regular configuration Hamiltonian uses ε_reg = 0.9 MeV, fixed to the 2⁺₁ state of heavier Pb isotopes (A=198 to 204)
- In ¹⁹²Pt, the experimental ratio R = B(E2:2⁺₂→0⁺₁)/B(E2:2⁺₁→0⁺₁) = 0.00732; the theoretical value is 0.00711
- In ¹⁹⁴Pt, the experimental ratio R = 0.00485; the theoretical value is 0.00500
- In ¹⁸²W, the experimental ratio R lies in the range 0.0252–0.0295; the calculated ratio is 0.0276
- The mixing matrix element ratio between 2p-2h and 4p-4h 0⁺ states is approximately twice as strong as that between the ground state and 2p-2h states, with α₂ ≈ 2α₁ in the large-boson-number limit
- For the finite boson numbers in Pb, the square root factor in the ratio equation equals about 0.8

## Context
This work extends earlier two-configuration IBM mixing calculations by Duval and Barrett (1982) and others in the Hg, Pt, and Po regions, as well as previous two-band mixing studies by Dracoulis (1998) and Allatt (1998). It goes beyond earlier three-configuration studies that were confined to 0⁺ band heads, such as those by Heyde (1991) and Cottle (2000), and the phenomenological band-mixing calculation in ¹⁸⁶Pb by Page (2002). The results enable future calculations that aim to describe energy spectra, band structures, electromagnetic properties (E2 and E0), isotopic and isomeric shifts, and nuclear ground-state binding energies across the neutron-deficient W, Os, Pt, Hg, Pb, Po, and Rn region.
