# Two-colour QCD phases and the topology at low temperature and high density

> We delineate equilibrium phase structure and topological charge distribution of dense two-colour QCD at low temperature by using a lattice simulation with two-flavour Wilson fermions that has a chemical potential $μ$ and a diquark source $j$ incorporated. We systematically measure the diquark condensate, the Polyakov loop, the quark number density and the chiral condensate with improved accuracy and $j\to0$ extrapolation over earlier publications; the known qualitative features of the low temperature phase diagram, which is composed of the hadronic, Bose-Einstein condensed (BEC) and BCS phases, are reproduced. In addition, we newly find that around the boundary between the hadronic and BEC phases, nonzero quark number density occurs even in the hadronic phase in contrast to the prediction of the chiral perturbation theory (ChPT), while the diquark condensate approaches zero in a manner that is consistent with the ChPT prediction. At the highest $μ$, which is of order the inverse of the lattice spacing, all the above observables change drastically, which implies a lattice artifact. Finally, at temperature of order $0.45T_c$, where $T_c$ is the chiral transition temperature at zero chemical potential, the topological susceptibility is calculated from a gradient-flow method and found to be almost constant for all the values of $μ$ ranging from the hadronic to BCS phase. This is a contrast to the case of $0.89T_c$ in which the topological susceptibility becomes small as the hadronic phase changes into the quark-gluon plasma phase.

## Key claim

The authors find that around the boundary between the hadronic and BEC phases, nonzero quark number density occurs even in the hadronic phase in contrast to the prediction of chiral perturbation theory (ChPT), while the diquark condensate approaches zero in a manner consistent with the ChPT prediction; this hadronic matter regime appears at temperatures where thermal excitation of diquarks becomes possible.

## Method

The authors performed lattice simulations of two-colour QCD with two-flavour Wilson fermions using the Iwasaki gauge action, incorporating a chemical potential μ and a diquark source j. They employed a reweighting method to extrapolate observables to the j→0 limit, measuring the diquark condensate, Polyakov loop, quark number density, and chiral condensate at two temperatures (T=0.45Tc and T=0.89Tc), and calculated the topological susceptibility using a gradient-flow method.

## Key numbers

- The pseudoscalar meson mass at zero chemical potential is amPS = 0.623(3) and mPS/mV = 0.823(9) at (β,κ) = (0.800, 0.159)
- The critical chemical potential for the hadronic-to-superfluid transition is μB/mPS ≃ 1/2
- The Polyakov loop susceptibility is marginally peaked at μD/mPS ∼ 1.44, corresponding to aμD = 0.90
- The quark number density in the hadronic matter regime ranges from approximately μ/mPS = 0.42 to 0.50
- The maximal value of a³⟨nq⟩ observed is 0.707(2), significantly smaller than the saturation value of 8
- The scaling law ⟨qq⟩ ∝ (μ − μB)^βm with βm = 0.50 (mean-field ChPT prediction) gives χ²/(d.o.f.) = 1.31 for 9 data points in the range 1/2 ≤ μ/mPS < 0.70

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice simulations of dense two-colour QCD by Hands et al. (2006), Cotter et al. (2012), and Braguta et al. (2016) with improved accuracy and a new reweighting method for the j→0 extrapolation. The finding of nonzero quark number density in the hadronic phase (hadronic matter) contradicts the ChPT prediction of Kogut et al. (1999, 2000) but is consistent with a recent Schwinger-Dyson analysis by Contant and collaborators (2019). The constant topological susceptibility at T=0.45Tc across all μ values contrasts with the behaviour at T=0.89Tc, where the susceptibility decreases as the hadronic phase changes into the quark-gluon plasma phase, and extends earlier work by Alles et al. (2006) and Hands et al. (2011) on Nf=4 and Nf=8 systems.
