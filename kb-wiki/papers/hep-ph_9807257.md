# Multipion symmetrization effects on the source distribution

> Any-order pion interferometry formulas for fixed pion multiplicity events and for mixed events are given. Multipion Bose-Einstein correlation effects on the two-pion interferometry and source distribution are studied. It is shown that generalized pion interferometry formula should depneds on pion multiplicity distribution. Pion condensate is also discussed in the paper.

## Key claim

The paper shows that generalized pion interferometry formulas depend not only on the correlator (as assumed previously) but also on the pion multiplicity distribution, which was neglected in earlier studies; multipion Bose-Einstein symmetrization effects on the source distribution become stronger as pion multiplicity increases, and in the limit of very large multiplicity all pions concentrate in a single phase space, leading to a pion condensate.

## Method

The author derives any-order pion interferometry formulas for fixed pion multiplicity events and for mixed events using a Wigner function \(g(x,k)\) representing the probability of finding a pion at point \(x\) with momentum \(p\). The source distribution is modeled as a Gaussian in both coordinate and momentum space with parameters \(R\) and \(\Delta\), and the multipion Bose-Einstein correlation effects on two-pion interferometry and on the effective source distribution in coordinate and momentum space are computed analytically. The dependence on the pion multiplicity distribution \(P_n\) is studied by varying the coefficients \(a_n\) in a coherent-state expansion of the pion state.

## Key numbers

- For the Gaussian source model, the effective half-widths satisfy \(\sqrt{\frac{\Delta}{2R}} \le \Delta_{\text{eff}} \le \Delta\) and \(\sqrt{\frac{R}{2\Delta}} \le R_{\text{eff}} \le R\).
- In the limit \(\frac{\langle n \rangle}{(R\Delta)^3} \rightarrow \infty\), the source size reaches the minimal Heisenberg relationship: \(R_{\text{eff}} = \sqrt{\frac{R}{2\Delta}}\) and \(\Delta_{\text{eff}} = \sqrt{\frac{\Delta}{2R}}\).
- For fixed pion multiplicity events, as \(n \rightarrow \infty\) the two-pion correlation function \(C_n^2\) approaches 1.

## Context

This work extends the earlier derivations of multipion Bose-Einstein correlation effects by Lam and Lo (who introduced the BASER concept), Zajc (who used Monte-Carlo methods for fixed multiplicity), Pratt (who first suggested condensate effects), and Chao, Gao, and Zhang (1995) who gave detailed derivations for single-particle spectra and two-pion interferometry. The paper also builds on the density-matrix approach of Zimanyi and Csorgo and on the author's own previous work (Zhang 1997) giving any-order formulas for a special class of density matrices. The results enable more accurate modeling of pion interferometry in high-energy heavy-ion collisions by incorporating the multiplicity distribution, and they suggest that previous studies neglecting this dependence may need revision; the author notes that experimental results by Egger, Lipa, and Buschbeck appear to support this conclusion.
