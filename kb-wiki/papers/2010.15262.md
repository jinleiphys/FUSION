# Coriolis contribution to excited states of odd-mass nuclei with different deformation-dependent mass coeffcients

> Within the collective Bohr Hamiltonian, the adoption of a mass tensor as a function of collective coordinates has demonstrated its importance for describing the structure of nuclei. On the other hand, for odd-mass nuclei, the Coriolis interaction between the rotational and single-particle motions affects significantly the structure of nuclear excited states. In the framework of a recently developed extended Bohr Hamiltonian, by considering the Deformation-Dependent Mass Formalism whith different mass parameters for the rotation and the two \betha and γvibrations and taking into account the Coriolis contribution, we investigate the bands structure of the 173Yb, 163Dy, 155Eu and 153Eu nuclei. Excited-state energies and B(E2) transition probabilities are calculated and compared with the available experimental data. Besides, we investigate the effect of DDMF and the Coriolis force on nuclear observables.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including the Coriolis interaction in the extended Bohr Hamiltonian with deformation-dependent mass coefficients (DDMF) and different mass parameters for rotation, beta, and gamma vibrations improves agreement with experimental energy spectra and B(E2) transition probabilities for odd-mass nuclei, but the DDMF effect is reduced when the Coriolis contribution is introduced, as the two effects have opposite correlations on moments of inertia.

## Method

The authors solve the Bohr Hamiltonian with three different mass parameters (for rotation, beta vibration, and gamma vibration) and a deformation-dependent mass function f = 1 + a*beta^2, using the Asymptotic Iteration Method (AIM). They include the Coriolis interaction term 2(L1*j1 + L2*j2) in the rotational Hamiltonian, which makes the quantum numbers K and Omega nonconserved, and diagonalize the resulting Hamiltonian matrix including nondiagonal elements. Calculations are performed for two cases (without DDMF where a=0, and with DDMF where a is nonzero) for the ground-state, beta, and gamma bands of 173Yb, 163Dy, 155Eu, and 153Eu, using the Davidson potential for beta and harmonic oscillator potential for gamma.

## Context

This work extends the previous study by Ait Ben Hammou and Oulne (2019) on odd-mass nuclei within the DDMF but without Coriolis interaction, and corrects the inaccurate energy formula used by Ermamatov et al. (2015, 2016) for 173Yb, 163Dy, 155Eu, and 153Eu. The paper shows that the DDMF reduces the rate of increase of the moment of inertia with angular momentum, while the Coriolis interaction increases it, and that the DDMF effect is reduced when Coriolis is included. This enables more accurate calculations of excited-state energies and B(E2) transition probabilities for odd-mass deformed nuclei, particularly for the ground-state and beta bands, while the gamma band remains insensitive to DDMF when different mass coefficients are used.
