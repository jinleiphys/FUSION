# Longitudinal spin polarization in a thermal model with dissipative corrections

> In this work, we address the problem of longitudinal spin polarization of the $Λ$ hyperons produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We combine a relativistic kinetic-theory framework that includes spin degrees of freedom treated in a classical way with the freeze-out parametrization used in previous investigations. The use of the kinetic theory allows us to incorporate dissipative corrections (due to the thermal shear and gradients of thermal vorticity) into the Pauli-Lubanski vector that determines spin polarization and can be directly compared with the experimental data. As in earlier similar studies, it turns out that a successful description of data can only be achieved with additional assumptions -- in our case, they involve the use of projected thermal vorticity and a suitably adjusted time for spin relaxation ($τ_s$). From our analysis, we find that $τ_s \sim 5$ fm/$c$, which is comparable with other estimates.

## Key claim

A successful description of the longitudinal spin polarization of Lambda hyperons in relativistic heavy-ion collisions requires both the use of projected thermal vorticity (with electric-like components set to zero) and the inclusion of dissipative corrections from kinetic theory, with a fitted spin relaxation time of approximately 5 fm/c.

## Method

The authors combined a relativistic kinetic-theory framework with classical spin degrees of freedom and a single-freeze-out thermal model parametrization for Au-Au collisions. Dissipative corrections to the Pauli-Lubanski vector were derived from the kinetic equation in the relaxation time approximation, incorporating contributions from thermal shear and gradients of thermal vorticity. The spin polarization tensor was taken as the projected thermal vorticity (with all electric-like components neglected), and the spin relaxation time was fitted to experimental data using a reduced chi-squared minimization procedure.

## Key numbers

- Spin relaxation time for Lambda hyperons: tau_s = 4.9 fm/c, with reduced chi-squared value chi^2_r = 1.5
- Spin relaxation time for anti-Lambda hyperons: tau_s = 7.5 fm/c, with reduced chi-squared value chi^2_r = 0.6
- Freeze-out temperature: T_f = 0.165 GeV
- Lambda hyperon mass: m = 1.116 GeV
- Speed of sound squared used: c_s^2 = 0.15
- Beam energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV for Au-Au collisions
- Thermal model parameters for 30-60% centrality: epsilon = 0.137, delta = 0.37, tau_f = 4.266 fm, r_max = 3.779 fm
- Thermal model parameters for 15-30% centrality: epsilon = 0.097, delta = 0.26, tau_f = 6.258 fm, r_max = 5.417 fm
- Thermal model parameters for 0-15% centrality: epsilon = 0.055, delta = 0.12, tau_f = 7.666 fm, r_max = 6.540 fm

## Context

This work extends earlier thermal model studies of longitudinal polarization by Florkowski et al. (2019, 2021) by incorporating dissipative corrections from kinetic theory. It addresses the sign problem in longitudinal polarization predictions, where earlier theoretical results disagreed with STAR experimental data. The use of projected thermal vorticity follows the approach of Voloshin (2018) and the authors' previous work, while the inclusion of thermal shear effects builds on the work of Fu et al. (2021) and Becattini et al. (2021). The estimated spin relaxation time is consistent with estimates from Kapusta (2019), Ayala (2020), Hidaka (2023), and Wagner (2024). This work enables more accurate modeling of spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions by demonstrating that dissipative corrections play an important role in explaining the observed longitudinal polarization.
