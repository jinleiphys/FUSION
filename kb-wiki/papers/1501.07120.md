# 2p-2h excitations in neutrino scattering: angular distribution and frozen approximation

> We study the phase-space dependence of 2p-2h excitations in neutrino scattering using the relativistic Fermi gas model. We follow a similar approach to other authors, but focusing in the phase-space properties, comparing with the non-relativistic model. A careful mathematical analysis of the angular distribution function for the outgoing nucleons is performed. Our goals are to optimize the CPU time of the 7D integral to compute the hadron tensor in neutrino scattering, and to conciliate the different relativistic and non relativistic models by describing general properties independently of the two-body current. For some emission angles the angular distribution becomes infinite in the Lab system, and we derive a method to integrate analytically around the divergence. Our results show that the frozen approximation, obtained by neglecting the momenta of the two initial nucleons inside the integral of the hadron tensor, reproduces fairly the exact response functions for constant current matrix elements.

## Key claim

The frozen approximation, which neglects the momenta of the two initial nucleons inside the integral of the hadron tensor, reproduces fairly the exact response functions for constant current matrix elements, but the approximation is best justified for high momentum transfer where \(q \gg k_F > h_i\).

## Method

The authors computed the 2p-2h phase-space function \(F(\omega,q)\) for neutrino scattering using the relativistic Fermi gas model, starting from a 7D integral over initial and final nucleon momenta. They performed a mathematical analysis of the angular distribution of the outgoing nucleons, identifying divergences in the Lab system for certain emission angles and deriving an analytic method to integrate around those poles. They then introduced the frozen nucleon approximation, which sets the initial hole momenta to zero, reducing the integral to 1D, and compared the resulting phase-space function to the full 7D integral for a range of momentum transfers.

## Context

This work extends the phase-space analysis of 2p-2h excitations in neutrino scattering previously studied by Martini (2009), Nieves (2011), and Amaro (2010, 2011) using the relativistic Fermi gas model, and by Van der Sluys (1981) for the non-relativistic model. The frozen approximation is intended to reduce the computational time of the 7D hadron tensor integral, making it suitable for Monte Carlo event generators, and the authors are working on implementing the method with a complete model of meson-exchange current (MEC) operators.
