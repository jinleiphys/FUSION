# Measurement and analysis of neutron capture reaction rates of light neutron-rich nuclei

> Several neutron capture cross sections of light neutron-rich nuclei were measured in the astrophysically relevant energy region of 5 to 200 keV. The experimental data are compared to calculations using the direct capture model. The results are used for the calculation of neutron capture cross sections of unstable isotopes. Furthermore, neutron sources with energies below E_n \approx 10 keV are discussed.

## Key claim

The direct capture (DC) model, using folding potentials and spectroscopic factors derived from thermal-energy comparisons, can reliably predict neutron capture cross sections for unstable neutron-rich nuclei in the mass region A=10-50, provided the potential depth is adjusted to the scattering length and binding energy of a stable analog (e.g., ⁴⁸Ca).

## Method

Neutron capture cross sections were measured for several light neutron-rich target nuclei (A=10-50) using the fast cyclic activation technique at the Karlsruhe 3.75 MV Van-de-Graaff accelerator. Neutrons were produced via the ⁷Li(p,n) reaction near threshold (giving a quasi-Maxwellian spectrum with kT=25 keV) and with thin Li targets at higher energies, plus additional experiments at thermal energies at the BR1 reactor in Mol, Belgium. For lower neutron energies (below ~20 keV), the authors analyzed (p,n) reactions on ⁶⁵Cu, ⁵¹V, and ⁴⁵Sc using a pulsed proton beam and a 91 cm flight path, confirming monoenergetic neutrons at 8.15 keV from ⁴⁵Sc and finding a new weak resonance at 4.20 keV in ⁵¹V. Theoretical analysis used the direct capture formalism with folding potentials, where the potential depth for ⁴⁸Ca was adjusted to the scattering length and binding energy, and spectroscopic factors for unstable isotopes like ⁵⁰Ca and ⁵¹Ca were obtained from Hartree-Fock densities and shell-model calculations (OXBASH code, FPD6 interaction).

## Key numbers

- Neutron capture cross sections were measured in the energy region of 5 to 200 keV.
- A quasi-Maxwellian neutron spectrum with kT = 25 keV was obtained from the ⁷Li(p,n) reaction near threshold.
- A quasi-Maxwellian spectrum at kT = 52 keV was obtained from the ³H(p,n) reaction.
- For ⁴⁵Sc, monoenergetic neutrons at E_n = 8.15 keV were confirmed at ϑ_lab = 0°.
- For ⁵¹V, a new weak resonance was found at E_n = 4.20 keV.
- For ⁵⁰Ca(n,γ)⁵¹Ca at 30 keV: total capture cross section σ(30 keV) = 0.61 mb; total reaction rate factor N_A⟨σv⟩ = 8.80 × 10⁴ cm³ s⁻¹ mol⁻¹.
- For ⁵¹Ca: ground state (3/2⁻) spectroscopic factor C²S = 0.47, excited state (1/2⁻ at E_x = 1.773 MeV) C²S = 0.82.

## Context

This work extends previous direct capture studies of neutron-rich nuclei in the mass region A=10-50 (see references in the paper) by exploring neutron energies below the ~20 keV limit of the standard ⁷Li(p,n) reaction, using alternative (p,n) sources on ⁴⁵Sc, ⁵¹V, and ⁶⁵Cu. The calculated cross section for ⁵⁰Ca(n,γ) is somewhat smaller than a prior calculation by Krausmann (year not given in text), but the conclusions of that earlier work remain unchanged. The results enable improved reaction rate predictions for unstable neutron-rich isotopes relevant to s-process nucleosynthesis in light nuclei, r-process scenarios, and α-rich freeze-out, and demonstrate that the direct capture model with folding potentials can be reliably extrapolated from stable to unstable nuclei.
