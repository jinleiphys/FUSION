# Recent lattice QCD results and phase diagram of strongly interacting matter

> I review recent lattice QCD results on a few selected topics which are relevant to the heavy ion physics community. Special emphasis is put on the QCD equation of state at vanishing and nonzero baryon chemical potential, the onset of deconfinement of open strange and charmed hadrons, the contribution from experimentally not yet observed hadrons to the thermodynamics of the hadronic gas and their influence on freeze-out conditions of strange and light-quark hadrons.

## Key claim

The QCD crossover temperature region (Tc ≈ 155 MeV) governs not only the deconfinement of light quarks but also the onset of deconfinement of open strange and charmed hadrons, and experimentally unobserved hadrons predicted by quark models contribute significantly to QCD thermodynamics and affect freeze-out temperature determinations by 5–8 MeV.

## Method

The author reviews lattice QCD results using staggered fermion actions (stout by Wuppertal-Budapest, HISQ by HotQCD) with continuum extrapolations from multiple lattice spacings (Nτ = 6, 8, 10, 12). Taylor expansion in baryon chemical potential μB up to fourth order is used to compute the equation of state at nonzero density. Fluctuations and correlations of conserved charges (baryon number B, strangeness S, charm C, electric charge Q) are calculated as derivatives of the pressure with respect to chemical potentials to probe deconfinement and hadron resonance gas (HRG) model comparisons.

## Key numbers

- Continuum-extrapolated trace anomaly, pressure, and entropy density from stout and HISQ actions agree within 7% at 400 MeV (entropy density shows the largest discrepancy)
- Chiral crossover temperature Tc ∈ [149, 163] MeV; critical energy density εc ∈ [0.18, 0.5] GeV/fm³
- Baryon number density nB can be reliably described down to √sNN ≈ 30 GeV using terms up to O(μB³); corrections from O(μB⁵) reach about 30% at √sNN ≈ 15 GeV
- Energy density at freeze-out stays roughly constant at ε ≈ 0.33 GeV/fm³ down to √sNN ≈ 50 GeV
- Upper bound on freeze-out temperature from net baryon number fluctuations: Tf ≤ 148 ± 4 MeV
- Freeze-out temperature for strange hadrons from QM-HRG is 5–8 MeV smaller than from PDG-HRG, becoming consistent with light-quark hadron freeze-out
- All charmonia states melt at T ≥ 1.5 Tc; S-wave bottomonia melt above 2 Tc, P-wave bottomonia melt immediately above Tc

## Context

This review extends the lattice QCD equation of state work by the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (stout action) and HotQCD collaboration (HISQ action), achieving consensus at zero baryon density. It builds on prior studies of deconfinement indicators (Matsui and Satz, 1986) and freeze-out determinations from lattice fluctuations (Borsanyi et al., 2013). The results enable improved modeling of heavy-ion collision evolution via hydrodynamics, particularly for the Beam Energy Scan program, and demonstrate that HRG models must include quark-model-predicted hadrons not yet in the PDG tables to correctly describe lattice data and freeze-out conditions.
