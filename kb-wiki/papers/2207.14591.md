# Intrinsic states of deformed N=Z nuclei in a quartet formalism

> The band structure of N=Z nuclei is constructed from intrinsic states defined in terms of quartets. The simplest of these states is a condensate of collective quartets with isospin T=0. The other intrinsic states are built by promoting one quartet of the condensate to an excited T=0 configuration. From these intrinsic states, by angular momentum projection, band structures are generated that approximate well the experimental ones. The projected states also reproduce to a very good extent the spectra resulting from configuration interaction calculations based on the same quartets forming the intrinsic states. These results show that the quartet-based intrinsic states provide the appropriate framework to understand in a simple and intuitive manner the emergence of band-like structures in N=Z nuclei.

# Intrinsic states of deformed N=Z nuclei in a quartet formalism

## Key claim

The band structure of deformed N=Z nuclei can be generated to a very good extent by projecting states of good angular momentum from intrinsic states defined as a condensate of T=0 quartets and states where one quartet is promoted to an excited T=0 configuration, provided a criterion based on the energy of the intrinsic states is used to select the appropriate set of states.

## Method

The authors construct intrinsic states for N=Z nuclei (with Nπ=Nν and Nπ+Nν=4n, n=2,3) using a condensate of collective T=0 quartets (the "ground" intrinsic state) and additional states formed by promoting one quartet to an excited T=0 configuration. The quartet amplitudes are determined by energy minimization. From these intrinsic states, which have undefined angular momentum, states of good angular momentum are obtained by angular momentum projection using standard tensor coupling rules, followed by orthonormalization and Hamiltonian diagonalization in the resulting basis (maximum basis size of 6). Calculations are performed in the sd shell using the USDB interaction and in the pf shell using the KB3G interaction, with 16O and 40Ca as cores respectively.

## Key numbers

- For 24Mg, 28Si, and 48Cr, the angular momentum values entering the quartet summations are restricted to J=0,2,4 for the ground intrinsic state and for the k=0 state, and to J=k,k+1,k+2 for states with k≠0; for 48Cr an extra J=6 quartet is added to the ground intrinsic state.
- In the case of 28Si, only three J=2 projected states are generated, while the Quartet Model and shell model calculations produce two and three J=2 states respectively at the second projected level.
- The maximum size of the orthonormal basis constructed from projected states in the calculations presented is 6.

## Context

This work extends the quartet-based approach developed by the same authors in Sambataro and Sandulescu (2022) where configuration-interaction calculations using dynamical quartets showed improved agreement with exact spectra compared to static quartets. The present study demonstrates that the trial states previously used can be interpreted as genuine intrinsic states by showing that angular momentum projection from them reproduces the band structures. The intrinsic states are analogous to those used by Maglione and collaborators to describe band structures of deformed nuclei, but with quartets replacing collective pairs. This formalism also generalizes the treatment of isovector-plus-isoscalar proton-neutron pairing interactions discussed in Sambataro and Sandulescu (2022), where condensates of T=0,J=0 quartets were used for spherical systems, to the case of deformed systems where the quartets do not carry well-defined angular momentum.
