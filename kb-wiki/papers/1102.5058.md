# Measuring the Gluon Density in e + A Collisions: KLN CGC, DGLAP Glauber, or Neither?

> We predict readily experimentally measurable differences in the diffractive cross section in the coherent exclusive photoproduction of J/psi mesons in e + A collisions at eRHIC and LHeC energies for nuclear gluon distributions assumed to 1) evolve in x with DGLAP dynamics and have a spatial distribution proportional to the Glauber nuclear thickness function and 2) evolve in x and b according to the KLN prescription of CGC dynamics. We find that CGC physics predicts that the nuclear gluon density widens significantly as a function of x yielding diffractive peaks and minima that evolve dramatically with x; on the other hand the DGLAP Glauber distribution yields peaks and minima constant in x. We also find that the dipole cross section at the level of two gluon exchange within the KLN parameterization of the CGC satisfies the black disk limit whereas this limit is violated when DGLAP evolution is used; the normalization of the diffractive cross section grows more slowly in x by several orders of magnitude when using the KLN parameterization as compared to the result when employing DGLAP evolution.

## Key claim

The diffractive cross section in coherent exclusive J/psi photoproduction in e+A collisions at eRHIC and LHeC energies can experimentally distinguish between two models of nuclear gluon distributions: one using DGLAP evolution with a Glauber spatial distribution, and the other using the KLN prescription of the Color Glass Condensate (CGC). The abstract glosses over the fact that the results are obtained at leading order in the two-gluon exchange without exponentiation, and that the KLN CGC calculation uses a specific parameterization with a free parameter kappa set to 1.

## Method

The author computed the coherent diffractive cross section dσ/dt for exclusive J/psi photoproduction using the leading-order two-gluon exchange formalism. For the DGLAP Glauber model, the dipole-nucleus cross section was taken proportional to the LO MSTW gluon PDF times the Glauber nuclear thickness function from a Woods-Saxon distribution for gold-197. For the KLN CGC model, the dipole cross section was computed using the KLN unintegrated gluon distribution with a saturation scale Q_s^2 defined via the MSTW gluon PDF and the nuclear thickness function, with the running coupling cutoff α_s^max varied to explore theoretical uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- At x = 10^{-5}, the KLN CGC dipole cross section at b=0 is nearly two orders of magnitude smaller than the DGLAP Glauber result for dipole size r = 1 GeV^{-1}.
- At x = 10^{-3}, the DGLAP Glauber coherent diffractive cross section at t=0 is an order of magnitude larger than the KLN CGC result; at x = 10^{-6}, it is two orders of magnitude larger.
- The DGLAP Glauber dipole cross section at b=0 violates the black disk limit by an order of magnitude at x = 10^{-5}, while the KLN CGC result satisfies it.
- The half-width radius b_{1/2} for the KLN CGC dipole cross section continues to rise sublinearly with log(s) down to x ~ 10^{-13}, satisfying the Froissart bound.
- The Woods-Saxon parameters used for gold-197 are R = 6.38 fm and a = 0.535 fm.
- The nucleon gluonic width parameter B_G is approximately 4.25 GeV^{-2} from HERA data.

## Context

This work extends the study of Caldwell (2010) on how modest changes in the Woods-Saxon distribution affect diffractive peaks in EVMP, by comparing two fundamentally different physical pictures of the nuclear gluon distribution. The two models investigated, DGLAP Glauber and KLN CGC, are the dominant models used in heavy ion physics to estimate the uncertainty in the viscosity-to-entropy ratio of the quark-gluon plasma from initial condition uncertainties (Hirano 2005, Luzum 2008). The results enable experimental discrimination between these models at eRHIC and LHeC, potentially resolving whether saturation effects are important in the initial nuclear profiles for heavy ion collisions.
