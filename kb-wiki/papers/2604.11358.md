# Emulator-Assisted Nuclear DFT Inference and Its Consequences for the Structure of Neutron Stars

> Nuclear density functional theory provides a unified description of finite nuclei and bulk nuclear matter, and is widely used to model the neutron star equation of state. However, extrapolations to supra-saturation densities require a quantified treatment of uncertainties arising from parameter estimation and functional choices. We present an updated Bayesian inference of a Skyrme energy density functional augmented by a flexible meta-model density dependence at high density. Nuclear observables are computed using a Gaussian emulator of the publicly available Milano HFBCS-QRPA code, enabling efficient exploration of a high-dimensional parameter space. Relative to previous analyses, we extend the calibration set with isospin-sensitive data, including masses and charge radii along selected Ca and Sn isotopic chains, and updated constraints from giant monopole resonances. The resulting posteriors are further constrained by \emph{ab initio} neutron-matter calculations and astrophysical observations, including recent NICER measurements, yielding consistent crust and core properties of catalyzed NS compatible with current constraints. Bulk nuclear-matter parameters are well approximated by a multivariate Gaussian with covariance matrix provided for direct reuse, while several finite-nucleus parameters exhibit pronounced non-Gaussianity.

## Key claim
The paper presents an updated Bayesian inference of a Skyrme energy density functional augmented with a flexible meta-model density dependence, finding that the inclusion of masses and charge radii along Ca and Sn isotopic chains shifts the symmetry energy parameters to relatively low values compared to other works in the literature, primarily because the model must simultaneously describe polarizabilities and binding energies within their respective uncertainties.

## Method
The authors performed a two-stage Bayesian inference using a Gaussian emulator of the Milano HFBCS-QRPA code to efficiently explore a 16-dimensional parameter space of a Skyrme functional extended with a meta-model density dependence at high density. The first stage calibrated the functional to nuclear observables including binding energies, charge radii, giant monopole resonance energies, and isovector-sensitive observables, while the second stage incorporated constraints from chiral effective field theory neutron-matter calculations, NICER measurements, and gravitational wave data from GW170817.

## Key numbers
- The excitation energy of the giant monopole resonance of 90Zr was updated to 18.65 ± 0.17 MeV, compared to the previous value of 17.66 ± 0.07 MeV
- The root-mean-square deviation for binding energies across 150 spherical nuclides is 1.23 MeV, with a mean distance from experimental values of 0.97 MeV
- The root-mean-square deviation for charge radii across 150 spherical nuclides is 0.02 fm, with a mean distance from experimental values of 0.02 fm
- 91% of binding energy predictions fall within 2 MeV of experimental values, and all charge radius predictions except two fall within 0.05 fm
- The maximum observed neutron star mass constraint used is 2.08 ± 0.07 solar masses from pulsar J0740+6620

## Context
This work extends previous Bayesian analyses by Klausner (2025) and Klausner (2025) by adding open-shell isotopes of Ca and Sn to the calibration set, removing N=Z nuclei that are sensitive to poorly known proton-neutron pairing channels, and updating the giant monopole resonance constraint for 90Zr. The resulting posterior distributions enable consistent calculation of both crust and core properties of catalyzed neutron stars, and the bulk nuclear-matter parameters are provided as a multivariate Gaussian approximation for reuse in future astrophysical simulations. The paper confirms the tension between PREX-II data and other isovector-sensitive observables noted in prior studies.
