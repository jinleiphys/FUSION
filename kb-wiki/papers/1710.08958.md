# Tubes, Mono Jets, Squeeze Out and CME

> Glasma Flux Tubes, Mono Jets with squeeze out flow around them plus the Chiral Magnetic Effect(CME) are physical phenomenon that generate two particle correlation with respect to the reaction plane in mid-central 20\% to 30\% Au-Au collision $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200.0 GeV measured at RHIC.

## Key claim

Glasma flux tubes, mono jets with squeeze-out flow, and the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) together generate two-particle correlations with respect to the reaction plane in mid-central 20% to 30% Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 200.0 GeV, but the CME signal can only be observed as a variance (not a direct P-odd observable) because its orientation flips event-by-event.

## Method

The author constructed a Monte Carlo simulation of 20% to 30% mid-central Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 200.0 GeV using 9 Glasma flux tubes (5 surface tubes following the PBME model plus 4 internal tubes), with 6 tubes lying in the reaction plane and 3 fluctuating above and below it. Mono jets were generated from hard scatterings in the less-dense regions above and below the reaction plane, with the away-side jet absorbed in the dense medium and the outgoing partner showering into a mono jet; squeeze-out was added by redirecting soft thermal particles into a cone at 45° relative to the mono jet axis. The CME was implemented by increasing the number of like-sign pairs moving out of the reaction plane on an event-by-event basis, and the two correlators \(C_{112} = \langle \cos(\phi_1 + \phi_2 - 2\Psi_{RP}) \rangle\) and \(C_{123} = \langle \cos(3\phi_1 - \phi_2 - 2\Psi_{RP}) \rangle\) were computed for like-sign and unlike-sign pairs.

## Key numbers

- One mono jet every 7 collisions on average was required to match the measured \(C_{112}\) and \(C_{123}\) correlations from data for 20% to 30% Au-Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 200.0 GeV.
- For 40% to 50% centrality, the simulation used flux tubes reduced by a factor of two and mono jets occurring once every 30 events.
- The squeeze-out cone was set at a fixed angle of 45° relative to the mono jet axis.
- The proposed isobar comparison between \(^{96}_{44}\mathrm{Ru}\) and \(^{96}_{40}\mathrm{Zr}\) would exploit a 9% difference in charge (and thus magnetic field) between the two colliding systems.

## Context

This work extends the Glasma flux tube model of Longacre (2015) and the PBME surface-tube model to include internal tubes, mono jets, and squeeze-out, building on prior studies of jet absorption and Mach-cone-like flow (shadowing, squeeze, ART). The paper directly compares its simulated correlations to the measured \(C_{112}\) and \(C_{123}\) data from the STAR collaboration (monojets paper) and to the in-plane/out-of-plane separation shown in the Csepvsplane analysis. The key next step proposed is a controlled isobar run (\(^{96}_{44}\mathrm{Ru}\) vs. \(^{96}_{40}\mathrm{Zr}\)) at RHIC to isolate the CME signal by exploiting the 9% difference in magnetic field while keeping the collision geometry nearly identical.
