# P odd fluctuations and Long Range Order in Heavy Ion Collisions. Deformed QCD as a Toy Model

> We study the local violation of P and CP invariance in heavy ion collisions as observed at RHIC and LHC using a simple "deformed QCD" model. This model is a weakly coupled gauge theory, which however has all the relevant crucial elements allowing us to study difficult and nontrivial questions which are known to be present in real strongly coupled QCD. Essentially, we want to understand the physics of long range order in form of coherent low dimensional vacuum configurations observed in Monte Carlo lattice simulations. Apparently precisely such kind of configurations are responsible for sufficiently strong intensity of asymmetries observed in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the local violation of P and CP invariance observed in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC originates from long-range coherent vacuum configurations (low-dimensional sheets of topological charge) rather than from conventional short-range QCD fluctuations, and that this long-range order is a necessary condition for the observed asymmetry to be sufficiently strong.

## Method

The author uses a weakly coupled "deformed QCD" model (center-stabilized Yang-Mills on ℝ³ × S¹) where semiclassical computations are controllable, and introduces a single massless quark field to study the chiral magnetic effect. The model is analyzed via its dual sine-Gordon description, where domain wall solutions interpolating between topologically equivalent vacuum states are constructed, and the topological charge density distribution and its coupling to the η' meson field are computed.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) originally proposed by Kharzeev (2004, 2007) and developed by Fukushima (2008), by providing a microscopic mechanism for the long-range order required for CME to operate. It directly connects to lattice simulations by Horvath (2003, 2005) and Ilgenfritz (2007, 2008) that observed laminar, low-dimensional sheets of topological charge in the QCD vacuum, and uses the deformed QCD model of Yaffe (2008) as a controlled theoretical laboratory. The analysis enables a deeper understanding of how external magnetic fields can serve as a probe of the QCD vacuum structure, and suggests that the observed P-odd asymmetries at RHIC and LHC are direct manifestations of this long-range order rather than conventional finite-size fluctuations.
