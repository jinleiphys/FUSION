# Light-quark baryon spectroscopy from ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channels analysis

> We review our recent efforts for determining resonance parameters associated with light-quark baryons ($N^*$, $Δ^*$, $Λ^*$, $Σ^*$) through comprehensive analyses of various meson production reactions off the nucleon within the ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channels approach.

# Light-quark baryon spectroscopy from ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channels analysis

## Key claim

The ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channels approach can extract resonance parameters (pole masses and residues) for light-quark baryons ($N^*$, $\Delta^*$, $\Lambda^*$, $\Sigma^*$) from comprehensive analyses of meson production reactions, but a visible analysis dependence still exists in the extracted resonance parameters, and the existing $K^-p$ reaction data are not sufficient to eliminate such dependence on the extracted mass spectrum.

## Method

The analysis solves coupled-channels integral equations for partial-wave amplitudes of meson-baryon reactions, including eight channels ($\gamma^{(*)}N$, $\pi N$, $\eta N$, $K\Lambda$, $K\Sigma$, $\pi\Delta$, $\rho N$, $\sigma N$) for the $N^*$ and $\Delta^*$ sector and channels including $\bar K N$, $\pi\Sigma$, $\pi\Lambda$, $\eta\Lambda$, $\pi\Sigma^*$, and $\bar K^* N$ for the $\Lambda^*$ and $\Sigma^*$ sector. The model includes both bare $N^*$ and $\Delta^*$ states that become resonances through coupling to reaction channels and dynamically generated resonance poles from iterative exchange potentials, with analytic continuation of amplitudes to the complex energy plane performed by changing the path of the momentum integral.

## Key numbers

- The $N^*$ and $\Delta^*$ model covers energy range up to $W = 2.3$ GeV for $\pi N$ scattering and up to $W = 2.1$ GeV for other reactions
- For the $\Lambda^*$ and $\Sigma^*$ sector, the analysis fits more than 17,000 data points from $K^-p$ reactions from threshold up to $W = 2.1$ GeV
- A narrow $J^P=1/2^-$ $\Lambda$ resonance is found near the $\eta\Lambda$ threshold at $W \sim 1.67$ GeV: $M_R = 1669^{+3}_{-8} -i (9^{+9}_{-1})$ MeV for Model A, $M_R = 1667^{+1}_{-2} -i (12^{+3}_{-1})$ MeV for Model B
- A narrow $J^P=3/2^+$ $\Lambda$ resonance is found only in Model B with $M_R = 1671^{+2}_{-8}-i(5^{+11}_{-2})$ MeV
- Electroproduction analyses have been performed up to $Q^2 = 1.5$ GeV$^2$ with a 6-channel model and up to $Q^2 = 3$ GeV$^2$ with an 8-channel model
- The J-PARC E45 experiment is expected to increase world data of $\pi N\to \pi\pi N$ reactions by a factor of 100 or more

## Context

This work extends the dynamical coupled-channels approach previously applied to $N^*$ and $\Delta^*$ spectroscopy (Kamano et al. 2013) to the $\Lambda^*$ and $\Sigma^*$ sector, where only Breit-Wigner masses and widths had been listed by PDG before 2012. The results are compared with analyses from the Julich group (2013), Bonn-Gatchina group (2012), and Kent State University group (Zhang et al. 2013). The framework enables future studies of neutrino-induced reactions for neutrino-oscillation experiments in the multi-GeV region and meson spectroscopy, while the remaining analysis dependence motivates new experimental data from facilities such as HADES and J-PARC.
