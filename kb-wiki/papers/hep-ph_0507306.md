# A Neutral Two Flavor Loff Color Superconductor

> In this paper we construct analytically a LOFF color superconducting state that is both color and charge neutral using the weak coupling approximation. We demonstrate that this state is free from chromomagnetic instabilities. Its relevance to the realistic quark matter at moderately high baryon density is discussed.

## Key claim

The authors construct analytically a two-flavor LOFF color superconducting state that satisfies both color and charge neutrality and is free from chromomagnetic instabilities, but the weak coupling approximation requires treating the sum of the electric charges of the u and d quarks as a free parameter epsilon much smaller than 1, with the expectation that qualitative conclusions can be extrapolated to the realistic case epsilon = 1/3.

## Method

The authors use an NJL effective Lagrangian in the mean field approximation at T=0, working in a grand canonical ensemble at fixed baryon chemical potential with the 8th-color chemical potential set to zero. They compute the pressure as a sum of normal phase and condensate contributions, then impose the charge neutrality condition to determine the displacement parameter delta for each phase. The analysis focuses on values of delta slightly below the upper threshold of the LOFF window, where the LOFF condensate pressure can be expressed analytically.

## Key numbers

- The LOFF window for the displacement parameter is delta_c' approximately 0.706 Delta_0 to delta_c approximately 0.754 Delta_0
- The ratio q/delta at the upper edge of the LOFF window is rho_c approximately 1.20, given by (1/rho_c) ln((rho_c+1)/(rho_c-1)) = 2
- For the charge neutral LOFF state, epsilon*mu_bar approximately 1.508 Delta_0 when delta_n approximately delta_c
- For realistic quark matter with charge sum 1/3, Delta_0/mu_bar approximately 0.22
- The Meissner mass tensor components for the 4-7th gluons are A = (g^2 mu_bar^2)/(96 pi^2) * 1/(rho_c^2-1)^2 * (Delta/delta)^4 >= 0 and B = (g^2 mu_bar^2)/(8 pi^2) * 1/(rho_c^2-1) * (Delta/delta)^2 >= 0

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous demonstration of chromomagnetic stability for a simple two-flavor LOFF state (Giannakis, Hou, Ren 2004) by implementing both color and charge neutrality conditions. It addresses the problem that the gapless CSC state (Shovkovy and Huang 2003) suffers from chromomagnetic instabilities when coupled to gluon-photon fields, while previous LOFF state analyses had not fully implemented charge neutrality. The result enables future numerical analysis of the LOFF state for realistic quark matter and suggests that the LOFF window may be modified when different phases have different displacement parameters.
