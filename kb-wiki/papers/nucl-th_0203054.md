# Fano theory for hadronic resonances: the rho meson and the pionic continuum

> We develop a model-independent analysis of hadronic scattering data in the resonance region, where the resonance shape follows from the matrix elements of a Hamiltonian. We investigate the rho meson in the tau decay. We demonstrate that the rho meson resonance in the two-pion decay of the tau lepton is described well through the coupling of a bare rho meson to the two-pion and the four-pion continuum. Furthermore, this four-pion continuum corresponds with the data of the four-pion decay channel of the tau lepton at energies up to 1.1 GeV.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the rho meson resonance in tau decay can be described by coupling a bare rho state to both the two-pion and four-pion continua, with the four-pion continuum naturally correcting the high-energy tail of the two-pion decay without requiring artificial barrier terms or form factors.

## Method

The author adapts Fano's Hamiltonian formalism for atomic resonances to hadronic systems, constructing a Hamiltonian that couples discrete resonance states to continua. The coupling functions are derived from chiral Lagrangians via the equivalence of the one-particle self-energy, and on-shell renormalization is used to handle divergences. The method is applied to the rho meson by coupling a bare rho state to two-pion and four-pion continua, with the matrix elements determined from chiral Lagrangian interactions.

## Key numbers

- The four-pion threshold is at 558 MeV
- The rho meson mass is 770 MeV
- The four-pion continuum corresponds with data up to 1.1 GeV
- The coupling constant to the four-pion state is g2 ≈ 0.2, fitted from the ππ decay of the rho meson
- The omega-pion system has a threshold at 921 MeV

## Context

This work extends Fano's atomic resonance theory (Fano 1961) to hadronic physics, providing a model-independent alternative to Breit-Wigner fits and chiral perturbation theory approaches. It contrasts with hidden local symmetry (HLS) models and vector meson dominance (VMD) approaches (Sakurai 1960) that require form factors and fail at higher energies. The method enables extraction of universal Hamiltonian matrix elements from scattering data and can be extended to coupled-channel problems with multiple resonances and continua.
