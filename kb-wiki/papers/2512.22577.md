# Exploring Interplays Between $^3\text{P}_2$ Neutron Superfluid Vortices and $^1\text{S}_0$ Proton Fluxtubes in the Outer Core of Neutron Stars

> In the outer core of neutron stars, $^3$P$_2$ superfluid neutrons and $^1$S$_0$ superconducting protons are deemed to exist, forming quantum vortices and magnetic fluxtubes, respectively. Those quantum vortices and fluxtubes play an important role in explaining observed sudden changes of rotational frequency, known as pulsar ``glitches.'' While the most of conventional glitch models rely on pinning/unpinning dynamics of neutron $^1\text{S}_0$ superfluid vortices in the inner crust, contributions of the outer core have not been ruled out. However, the latter possibility has been less explored so far and further thorough investigations are desired. In this study, we are thus developing a microscopic model based on spin-2 Gross-Pitaevskii equation (GPE) for neutron $^3$P$_2$ superfluid vortices coupled with Ginzburg-Landau equation (GLE) for fluxtubes associated with superconducting $^1\text{S}_0$ protons. In this contribution, we outline our theoretical framework and report tentative results showing how shape of quantum vortices could be affected by the presence of a proton fluxtube.

## Key claim

The authors report tentative results showing that the presence of a proton fluxtube can substantially alter the vortex configuration of a neutron \(^3\text{P}_2\) superfluid, specifically causing a transition from four half-integer quantum vortices to two integer quantum vortices in the \(m=0\) component, but these results are preliminary and neglect proton-neutron interactions (setting \(\xi=0\) and \(\eta=0\)).

## Method

The authors developed a coupled theoretical framework combining a spin-2 Gross-Pitaevskii equation (GPE) for neutron \(^3\text{P}_2\) superfluid vortices with a Ginzburg-Landau equation (GLE) for proton \(^1\text{S}_0\) superconducting fluxtubes. They solved these equations in real space using the imaginary-time evolution method on a \(100\times100\) grid with 0.1 fm spacing for 2D simulations and a \(50\times50\times50\) grid with 0.4 fm spacing for 3D simulations, imposing a \(4\pi\) phase rotation for neutrons and a \(2\pi\) phase rotation for protons, with a tube-shaped magnetic field mimicking a fluxtube introduced via a linear Zeeman term.

## Key numbers

* Magnetic flux quantum for a fluxtube: \(\Phi = h/(2e)\), where \(h\) is Planck's constant and \(e\) is the elementary charge.
* 2D simulations used a \(100\times100\) grid with mesh spacing of 0.1 fm; 3D simulations used \(50\times50\times50\) grids with mesh spacing of 0.4 fm.
* The magnetic field strength inside the fluxtube is given by \(g_n\mu_N B(r) = g_n\mu_N h / [2e (\pi r_{\mathrm{FT}}^2)]\) for \(r < r_{\mathrm{FT}}\), where \(r_{\mathrm{FT}}\) is the fluxtube radius.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of neutron vortex-proton fluxtube interactions, which previously focused on neutron \(^1\text{S}_0\) superfluid vortices in the inner crust (Cheng 1988) and in the outer core (Thong, Melatos, and Drummond 2023), to the \(^3\text{P}_2\) superfluid phase expected in the outer core. The results enable future self-consistent calculations of magnetic field configurations and time-dependent simulations of vortex-fluxtube dynamics, which could inform models of pulsar glitches such as the vortex avalanche model (Cheng 1988) and the vortex network model (Marmorinni and Nitta 2024).
