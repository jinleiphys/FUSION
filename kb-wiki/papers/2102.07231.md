# Thermal quarks and gluon propagators in two-color dense QCD

> We study Landau gauge gluon propagators in two-color QCD at finite quark chemical potential ($μ_q$) and temperature ($T$). We include medium polarization effects at one-loop by quarks into massive gluon propagators, and compared the analytic results with the available lattice data. We particularly focus on the high density phase of color-singlet diquark condensates whose critical temperature is $\sim 100$ MeV with weak dependence on $μ_q$. At zero temperature the color singlet condensates protect the IR limit of electric and magnetic gluon propagators from the medium screening effects. At finite temperature, this behavior remains true for the magnetic sector, but the electric screening mass should be generated by thermal, and hence gapless, particles which are unbound from the diquark condensates. Treating thermal excitations as quasi-quarks, we found that the electric screening develops too fast compared to the lattice results. Beyond the critical temperature for diquark condensates the analytic results are consistent with the lattice results.

## Key claim

The authors find that at finite temperature in the high-density phase of two-color QCD with color-singlet diquark condensates, treating thermal excitations as quasi-quarks leads to electric screening that develops too fast compared to lattice results, suggesting that thermal excitations may appear as hadrons rather than individual quarks.

## Method

The authors compute one-loop quark polarization effects in the Nambu-Gor'kov formalism assuming a momentum-independent diquark gap, and include these into massive gluon propagators in Landau gauge. They compare their analytic results with lattice data from unimproved Wilson gauge action with two flavors of unimproved Wilson quarks, using the BCS formulas for the temperature dependence of the diquark gap as a baseline.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature of color-singlet diquark condensates is approximately 100 MeV with weak dependence on quark chemical potential
- Zero-temperature diquark gap estimated as Delta_{T=0} approximately 158-211 MeV from the BCS relation Delta_{T=0} approximately T_SF/0.57
- At T approximately 44 MeV with Delta_{T=0}=200 MeV, the gap is Delta_T approximately 157 MeV, giving a Boltzmann factor of approximately 0.03 for thermal quarks
- Lattice pion mass is m_pi = 717(25) MeV, with onset chemical potential mu_c = m_pi/2 approximately 358 MeV
- Critical temperature of deconfinement from Polyakov loop is T_D = 217(23) MeV
- Inverse lattice spacing a^{-1} = 1.41 GeV for the beta=2.1 data set covering T from approximately 44 MeV to approximately 353 MeV
- Gluon masses used for fitting: m_E = 0.6 GeV and m_M = 0.5 GeV at T approximately 44 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous zero-temperature study of gluon propagators in two-color QCD (Suenaga and Kojo, 2019) to finite temperature, and compares with lattice data from Boz et al. (2018). The results are relevant to the quark-hadron continuity at finite temperature and the quarkyonic matter conjecture. The paper enables future studies of whether thermal excitations in dense QCD appear as quarks or hadrons, and tests the quasi-particle description for application to three-color QCD.
