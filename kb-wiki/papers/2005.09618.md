# Transport in neutron star mergers

> After an introduction to the QCD phase diagram, the nuclear equations of state, and neutron star mergers, I discuss three projects related to transport and nuclear matter in neutron star mergers. The first is the nature of beta equilibrium in the portion of a merger that is transparent to neutrinos. We calculate the weak interaction (Urca) rates and find that the beta equilibrium condition needs to be modified by adding an additional chemical potential, which changes slightly the particle content in neutrino-transparent beta equilibrium. Secondly, we calculate the bulk viscosity in neutrino-transparent nuclear matter in conditions encountered in neutron star mergers. Bulk viscosity arises from a phase lag between the pressure and density in the nuclear matter, which is due to the finite rate of beta equilibration. When bulk viscosity is sufficiently strong, which happens when the equilibration rate nearly matches the frequency of the density oscillation, it can noticeably dampen the oscillation. We find that in certain thermodynamic conditions likely encountered in mergers, oscillations in nuclear matter can be damped on timescales on the order of 10 milliseconds, so we conclude that bulk viscosity should be included in merger simulations. Finally, we study thermal transport due to axions in neutron star mergers. We conclude that axions are never trapped in mergers, but instead escape, carrying energy away from the merger. We calculate the cooling time due to the energy carried away by axions and find that within current constraints on the axion-nucleon coupling, axions could cool fluid elements in mergers on timescales which could affect the dynamics of the merger.

## Key claim

The paper argues that three transport processes, modified beta equilibrium in neutrino-transparent matter, bulk viscosity from finite-rate beta equilibration, and axion-mediated cooling, should be included in neutron star merger simulations because they can affect merger dynamics on observable timescales, though the magnitude of each effect depends on specific thermodynamic conditions and coupling constants.

## Method

The author calculates weak interaction (Urca) rates to determine the beta equilibrium condition in neutrino-transparent regions of neutron star mergers, finding that an additional chemical potential term is needed. Bulk viscosity is computed from the phase lag between pressure and density arising from finite beta equilibration rates, and its damping effect on density oscillations is evaluated. Thermal transport from axions is studied by calculating axion production and escape rates, determining cooling timescales based on current constraints on the axion-nucleon coupling.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of neutron star merger simulations that evolve Einstein's equations coupled to relativistic hydrodynamics and nuclear equations of state, which typically include neutrino transport and electrodynamics but not bulk viscosity or axion cooling. The author's calculations of bulk viscosity and axion transport build on the hadron resonance gas model and relativistic mean field theories of nuclear matter (Oertel 2016, Burgio 2018), and the axion analysis uses current constraints on axion-nucleon coupling. The results enable future merger simulations to incorporate these additional transport processes, potentially affecting predictions of gravitational wave signals and electromagnetic counterparts.
