# Suppression of thermal vorticity as an indicator of QCD critical point

> We study the impact of the QCD critical point (CP) on the spin polarization of $Λ$-hyperon generated by the thermal vorticity in viscous quark gluon plasma (QGP). The equations of the relativistic causal viscous hydrodynamics have been solved numerically in (3+1) dimensions to evaluate the thermal vorticity. The effects of the CP have been incorporated through the equation of state (EoS) and the scaling behavior of the transport coefficients. A significant reduction in the global polarization has been found as the CP is approached. A drastic change induced by the CP in the rapidity dependence of the spin polarization is observed which can be used as a signature of the CP.

## Key claim

The authors claim that as the QCD critical point (CP) is approached in heavy-ion collisions, the thermal vorticity and resulting spin polarization of Lambda hyperons are significantly suppressed, and that a drastic qualitative change in the rapidity dependence of the polarization can serve as a signature of the CP. The hedge is that this result depends on the CP being incorporated through a specific equation of state (EoS) and scaling behavior of transport coefficients, and that the effect is only observed for collision energies where the fireball trajectory passes through the critical domain (below about 19.6 GeV center-of-mass energy).

## Method

The authors solved (3+1)-dimensional relativistic causal viscous hydrodynamics numerically, using the algorithm of Karpenko (2014), for Au+Au collisions at impact parameter b=5.6 fm (15-25% centrality) at various center-of-mass energies. The effects of the CP were incorporated through the EoS of Parotto (2020), which maps the 3D Ising model to QCD with the CP located at (mu_c, T_c) = (350 MeV, 143.2 MeV), and through scaling behavior of shear and bulk viscosities and their relaxation times with the equilibrium correlation length. The thermal vorticity and Lambda polarization were evaluated on the freeze-out hypersurface defined by energy density epsilon = 0.3 GeV/fm^3 using the Cornelius code.

## Key numbers

- CP location: (mu_c, T_c) = (350 MeV, 143.2 MeV) from the Parotto (2020) EoS
- Initial times tau_0: 2.2 fm at sqrt(s_NN)=14.5 GeV, 1.8 fm at 19.6 GeV, 1.4 fm at 27 GeV, 1.3 fm at 39 GeV, 1.0 fm at 62.4 GeV and 200 GeV
- Impact parameter: b=5.6 fm for all collision energies
- Nuclear radius R_0=6.37 fm and surface thickness delta=0.535 for Au nucleus
- Critical correlation length threshold: xi_0 = 1.75 fm defines the extent of the critical domain
- Shear viscosity scaling: eta ~ xi^0.05; bulk viscosity scaling: zeta ~ xi^3
- Non-critical shear viscosity parameter: C_eta = 0.08
- Freeze-out energy density: epsilon = 0.3 GeV/fm^3
- Integration ranges for spin polarization: azimuthal angle 0 to 2pi, rapidity |y| < 1, transverse momentum 0 < p_T < 3 GeV

## Context

This work extends the study of Lambda hyperon spin polarization from thermal vorticity, previously measured by the STAR collaboration (2017), by incorporating the effects of the QCD critical point through the EoS of Parotto (2020) and scaling transport coefficients following Amonni (2019). It builds on the hydrodynamic framework of Karpenko (2014) and the EoS model that reproduces lattice QCD results at zero baryon chemical potential. The results enable experimental searches for the CP through measurements of Lambda polarization as a function of rapidity and collision energy, particularly in the Beam Energy Scan-II program at RHIC and upcoming experiments at FAIR and NICA, complementing other proposed signatures such as non-monotonic fluctuations in multiplicity (Xu, 2016) and changes in kurtosis of order parameter fluctuations (Stephanov, 2011).
