# Relative flow fluctuations as a probe of initial state fluctuations

> Elliptic flow, $v_2$, and triangular flow, $v_3$, are to a good approximation linearly proportional to the corresponding spatial anisotropies of the initial density profile, $\varepsilon_2$ and $\varepsilon_3$. Using event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations, we point out when deviations from this linear scaling are to be expected. When these deviations are negligible, relative $v_n$ fluctuations are equal to relative $\varepsilon_n$ fluctuations, and one can directly probe models of initial conditions using ratios of cumulants, for instance $v_n\{4\}/v_n\{2\}$. We argue that existing models of initial conditions tend to overestimate flow fluctuations in central Pb+Pb collisions, and to underestimate them in peripheral collisions. We make predictions for $v_3\{6\}$ in noncentral Pb+Pb collisions, and for $v_3\{4\}$ and $v_3\{6\}$ in high-multiplicity p+Pb collisions.

# Relative flow fluctuations as a probe of initial state fluctuations

## Key claim

The paper argues that ratios of cumulants of flow harmonics can directly probe initial-state anisotropies only when the linear scaling between $v_n$ and $\varepsilon_n$ holds, which is approximately true for $v_2$ up to 20-30% centrality in Pb+Pb collisions and for $v_3$ across a wider range, but deviations from linearity due to nonlinear hydrodynamic response become significant in more peripheral collisions.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event viscous relativistic hydrodynamic simulations using the V-USPHYDRO code with shear viscosity over entropy ratio $\eta/s=0.08$, starting from Glauber Monte Carlo initial conditions for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV. They computed flow coefficients for pions in the transverse momentum range $0.2 < p_t < 3$ GeV/$c$ and compared ratios of cumulants $v_n\{4\}/v_n\{2\}$ from hydrodynamics to the corresponding ratios $\varepsilon_n\{4\}/\varepsilon_n\{2\}$ from the initial density profiles. For testing initial condition models, they used the TRENTo parametric Monte Carlo model with varying parameter $p$ (controlling the combination of thickness functions) and the Monte Carlo rcBK model, simulating between 1000 and 5000 events per centrality window.

## Key numbers

- $v_3\{4\}/v_3\{2\}$ is smaller than $v_2\{4\}/v_2\{2\}$ in Pb+Pb collisions, indicating larger relative fluctuations for $v_3$
- For Pb+Pb collisions, the ratio $\varepsilon_2\{4\}/\varepsilon_2\{2\}$ from TRENTo with $p=1$ (wounded nucleon scaling) falls below experimental data, while $p=0$ and $p=-1$ (saturation-inspired models) show fair agreement up to 20% centrality
- In p+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV, the rms $\varepsilon_2$ increases by 8% when the nucleon width $\sigma$ is lowered from 0.4 fm to 0.3 fm, and the rms $\varepsilon_3$ increases by 12%
- The paper predicts $v_3\{6\}/v_3\{4\}$ between 0.75 and 0.85 in the 30-50% centrality range for Pb+Pb collisions

## Context

This work extends the study of flow fluctuations beyond the standard linear response assumption tested in earlier hydrodynamic simulations by Gardim (2011), Niemi (2012), and Gardim (2014). It directly tests the validity of equating cumulant ratios of $v_n$ and $\varepsilon_n$, which was previously assumed in analyses by Bhalerao (2011) and Renk (2014). The paper enables direct comparison of initial condition models (Glauber Monte Carlo, IP-Glasma, MC-KLN, EKRT, and the TRENTo parametrization) to experimental data from ATLAS, CMS, and ALICE without the confounding factor of the hydrodynamic response coefficient. It also makes testable predictions for higher-order cumulants $v_3\{6\}$ in both Pb+Pb and p+Pb collisions, which can discriminate between models of initial-state fluctuations.
