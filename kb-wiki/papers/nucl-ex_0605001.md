# The Coulomb Dissociation of 8B; A Triumph of Good Science

> The GSI1, GSI2 (as well as the RIKEN2 and the corrected GSI2) measurements of the Coulomb Dissociation (CD) of 8B are in good agreement with the most recent Direct Capture (DC) 7Be(p,g)8B reaction measurement performed at Weizmann and in agreement with the Seattle result. Yet it was claimed that the CD and DC results are sufficiently different and need to be reconciled. We show that these statements arise from a misunderstanding (as well as misrepresentation) of CD experiments. We recall a similar strong statement questioning the validity of the CD method due to an invoked large E2 component that was also shown to arise from a misunderstanding of the CD method. In spite of the good agreement between DC and CD data the slope of the astrophysical cross section factor (S17) can not be extracted with high accuracy due to a discrepancy between the recent DC data as well as a discrepancy of the three reports of the GSI CD data. The slope is directly related to the d-wave component that dominates at higher energies and must be subtracted from measured data to extrapolate to zero energy. Hence the uncertainty of the measured slope leads to an additional uncertainty of the extrapolated zero energy cross section factor, S17(0). This uncertainty must be alleviated by future experiments to allow a precise determination of S17(0), a goal that so far has not be achieved in spite of strong statement(s) that appeared in the literature.

## Key claim

Despite the abstract's claim of good agreement between Coulomb Dissociation (CD) and Direct Capture (DC) measurements of the ⁷Be(p,γ)⁸B reaction, the slope of the astrophysical S₁₇ factor cannot be extracted with high accuracy due to discrepancies among recent DC data themselves and among the three reports of GSI CD data, leading to additional uncertainty in the extrapolated zero-energy cross section factor S₁₇(0).

## Method

The author compares published CD data from GSI1, GSI2, RIKEN2, and corrected GSI2 with DC data from the Seattle, Weizmann, Bochum, and Orsay groups, focusing on the slope parameter S' = dS/dE extracted over the energy range 300–1500 keV. The analysis examines the d-wave contribution that dominates at higher energies and must be subtracted to extrapolate the s-wave to zero energy, and evaluates the consistency of S₁₇(0) values obtained using the extrapolation procedure of Descouvemont and Baye.

## Key numbers

- The weighted average of S₁₇(0) from four CD experiments is 20.0 ± 0.7 eV-b with χ² = 0.5
- The d-wave contribution at zero energy is approximately 6% of the total
- Individual DC data points from the Bochum and Seattle groups disagree by as much as five sigma
- The overall agreement between CD and DC slope data is at the 1.7 sigma level
- The theoretical error of the Descouvemont and Baye extrapolation increases to approximately 5% at 500 keV

## Context

This work extends the debate over the ⁷Be(p,γ)⁸B cross section, which is essential for calculating the ⁸B solar neutrino flux. The author argues against claims by Esbensen, Bertsch, and Snover (2005) that CD data require substantial higher-order and E2 corrections, and disputes the re-analysis by Junghans et al. (2003) that found CD-derived S₁₇(0) values 10% lower than modern direct measurements. The paper calls for future experiments to resolve systematic disagreements among DC data from the Orsay-Bochum and Weizmann-Seattle groups before a reliable "world average" can be established.
