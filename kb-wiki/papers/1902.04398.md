# Forbidden Nuclear Reactions

> Exothermal nuclear reactions which become forbidden due to Coulomb repulsion in the\ $\varepsilon \rightarrow 0$ limit ($\lim_{\varepsilon \rightarrow 0}σ\left( \varepsilon \right) =0$) are investigated. ($σ\left( \varepsilon \right) $ is the cross section and $\varepsilon $ is the center of mass energy.) It is found that any perturbation may mix states with small but finite amplitude to the initial state resulting finite cross section (and rate) of the originally forbidden nuclear reaction in the $% \varepsilon \rightarrow 0$ limit. The statement is illustrated by modification of nuclear reactions due to impurities in a gas mix of atomic state. The change of the wavefunction of reacting particles in nuclear range due to their Coulomb interaction with impurity is determined using standard time independent perturbation calculation of quantum mechanics. As an example, cross section, rate and power densities of impurity assisted nuclear $pd$ reaction are numerically calculated. With the aid of astrophysical factors cross section and power densities of the impurity assisted $d(d,n)_{2}^{3}He$, $d(d,p)t$, $d(t,n)_{2}^{4}He$, $% _{2}^{3}He(d,p)_{2}^{4}He$, $_{3}^{6}Li(p,α)_{2}^{3}He$, $% _{3}^{6}Li(d,α)_{2}^{4}He$, $_{3}^{7}Li(p,α)_{2}^{4}He$, $% _{4}^{9}Be(p,α)_{3}^{6}Li$, $_{4}^{9}Be(p,d)_{4}^{8}Be$, $% _{4}^{9}Be(α,n)_{6}^{12}C$, $_{5}^{10}B(p,α)_{4}^{7}Be$ and $% _{5}^{11}B(p,α)_{4}^{8}Be$ reactions are also given. The affect of gas mix-wall interaction on the process is considered too.

## Key claim

Any perturbation can mix states with small but finite amplitude into the initial state of an exothermal nuclear reaction that is forbidden in the zero-energy limit due to Coulomb repulsion, resulting in a finite cross section and rate at epsilon approaching zero. The claim is illustrated specifically for impurity-assisted reactions, but the authors state it applies to every nuclear process whose cross section has the form sigma(epsilon) = S(epsilon) exp[-2 pi eta(epsilon)]/epsilon and vanishes in the epsilon to 0 limit.

## Method

The authors use standard time-independent perturbation theory of quantum mechanics to calculate the first-order change in the three-particle wavefunction due to screened Coulomb interactions between an assisting impurity nucleus and the two reacting particles. They compute second-order transition probabilities for impurity-assisted nuclear reactions, using a square-well strong interaction potential for the pd reaction and astrophysical S-factors at zero energy for reactions with two final fragments. The cross section for the assisted process is expressed as v_{23} sigma_{23}^{(2)} = n_1 S_{reaction}, where n_1 is the number density of assisting particles.

## Key numbers

- For the impurity-assisted pd reaction (p + d to He-3 + 5.493 MeV), S_pd = 1.89 x 10^{-53} z_1^2 cm^6 s^{-1}, where z_1 is the charge number of the assisting nucleus
- With Xe (z_1 = 54) as the assisting impurity and 0.1 eV initial kinetic energy (T = 1160 K), sigma_{23}^{(2)} = 0.0066 nb at n_1 = 2.65 x 10^{19} cm^{-3}
- For the d(d,t)p reaction with Pd as host metal (z_1 = 46, A_1 = 106), S_{d(d,t)p}(Pd) = 7.9 x 10^{-49} cm^6 s^{-1} and n_1 S_{d(d,t)p}(Pd) = 1.08 x 10^{-25} cm^3 s^{-1}
- The second-order process dominates over the first-order process for the d(d,t)p reaction when epsilon < 0.001762 MeV
- For the d(d,n)He-3 reaction with Xe assistance, S_reaction = 1.01 x 10^{-48} cm^6 s^{-1}, Delta = 3.269 MeV, and power density p_reaction = 9.82 W cm^{-3}
- For the d(t,n)He-4 reaction with Xe assistance, S_reaction = 1.06 x 10^{-46} cm^6 s^{-1}, Delta = 17.59 MeV, and power density p_reaction = 5.57 x 10^3 W cm^{-3}

## Context

This work addresses anomalies in low-energy nuclear reactions, specifically the anomalous screening effect observed in deuterated metal targets (Huke, approximately 2009) and the broader low-energy nuclear reaction (LENR) field that emerged after the Fleischmann and Pons (1989) "cold fusion" publication. The authors provide a theoretical mechanism for how impurities or host metal atoms can enable nuclear reactions that are otherwise forbidden by Coulomb repulsion at very low energies. The results enable quantitative predictions for coincidence measurements of the assisting particle and reaction products, and suggest that the anomalous screening phenomenon may be connected to the second-order processes discussed here.
