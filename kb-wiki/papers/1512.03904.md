# Self-consistent Thomas-Fermi Approximation for Equation of State at Subnuclear Densities

> The self-consistent Thomas-Fermi approximation is an essential method for studying the non-uniform nuclear matter with relativistic mean-field theory. In this method, the nucleon distribution in the Wigner-Seitz cell is obtained self-consistently. We make a detailed comparison between the present results and previous calculations in the Thomas-Fermi approximation with a parameterized nucleon distribution that has been adopted in the widely used Shen equation of state.

# Self-consistent Thomas-Fermi Approximation for Equation of State at Subnuclear Densities

## Key claim
The self-consistent Thomas-Fermi (STF) approximation yields free energies per baryon that are slightly higher than those from the parameterized Thomas-Fermi (PTF) approximation with the standard surface parameter \(F_0 = 70\ \mathrm{MeV\,fm^5}\), but the differences are small enough that they do not affect the general behavior of the equation of state for supernova simulations. The bubble phase appears at subnuclear densities and delays the transition to uniform matter, a feature not captured by the PTF approximation with its assumed nucleon distribution form.

## Method
The authors solve the relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory with the TM1 parameter set for non-uniform nuclear matter at finite temperature, using the self-consistent Thomas-Fermi approximation in which nucleon density distributions in a Wigner-Seitz cell are obtained from the Fermi-Dirac occupation probabilities without assuming a functional form. They minimize the free energy with respect to the cell radius at fixed temperature, proton fraction, and baryon density, and compare the results for droplet, bubble, and homogeneous phases against the earlier parameterized Thomas-Fermi (PTF) approximation used in the Shen equation of state.

## Key numbers
- At \(Y_p = 0.3\), \(T = 1\ \mathrm{MeV}\), and \(\rho_B = 10^{13.0}\ \mathrm{g\,cm^{-3}}\), the STF free energy per baryon is \(-8.087\ \mathrm{MeV}\), compared to \(-8.304\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for PTF with \(F_0 = 70\ \mathrm{MeV\,fm^5}\) and \(-8.023\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for PTF with \(F_0 = 90\ \mathrm{MeV\,fm^5}\).
- At the same conditions, the STF cell radius is \(20.0\ \mathrm{fm}\), while PTF with \(F_0 = 70\) gives \(19.3\ \mathrm{fm}\) and PTF with \(F_0 = 90\) gives \(20.3\ \mathrm{fm}\).
- The bubble phase appears at approximately \(\rho_B \sim 10^{13.9}\ \mathrm{g\,cm^{-3}}\) for \(Y_p = 0.3\) at \(T = 1\ \mathrm{MeV}\), where the free energy shows a significant decrease in the STF calculation.

## Context
This work extends the Shen equation of state (Shen et al., 1998, 2011), which used the parameterized Thomas-Fermi approximation with an assumed nucleon distribution form, by implementing a fully self-consistent treatment. The authors show that the PTF approximation with the standard surface parameter \(F_0 = 70\ \mathrm{MeV\,fm^5}\) underestimates the surface energy compared to the STF result, but that adjusting \(F_0\) to \(90\ \mathrm{MeV\,fm^5}\) brings the two methods into close agreement. The inclusion of the bubble phase, which is naturally obtained in the STF approach, enables a more accurate description of the transition from non-uniform to uniform matter at subnuclear densities, relevant for supernova and neutron star simulations.
