# Accessing gluon parton distributions in large momentum effective theory

> Gluon parton distribution functions (PDFs) in the proton can be calculated directly on Euclidean lattices using large momentum effective theory (LaMET). To realize this goal, one has to find renormalized gluon quasi-PDFs in which power divergences and operator mixing are thoroughly understood. For the unpolarized distribution, we identify four independent quasi-PDF correlators that can be multiplicatively renormalized on the lattice. Similarly, the helicity distribution can be derived from three independent multiplicatively renormalizable quasi-PDFs. We provide a LaMET factorization formula for these renormalized quasi-PDFs from which one can extract the gluon PDFs.

# Accessing gluon parton distributions in large momentum effective theory

## Key claim
Gluon parton distribution functions (PDFs) can be calculated from lattice QCD using large momentum effective theory (LaMET), provided that the gluon quasi-PDF operators are properly renormalized to remove power divergences and operator mixing. The authors identify four independent unpolarized and three independent helicity gluon quasi-PDF correlators that are multiplicatively renormalizable, but caution that the specific operator combination used in a prior lattice simulation (Fan 2018) is *not* multiplicatively renormalizable.

## Method
The authors introduce an auxiliary adjoint "heavy quark" field into the QCD Lagrangian, replacing the Wilson line in the gluon quasi-PDF operator by a product of two local composite operators. They analyze renormalization in dimensional regularization expanded around d=3 to track linear divergences gauge-invariantly, and identify the mixing pattern among operators allowed by BRST symmetry. The renormalization factors Z11, Z22 and the residual mass term δm are to be determined non-perturbatively on the lattice, either separately or via the RI/MOM scheme.

## Key numbers
- The residual mass for the auxiliary adjoint "heavy quark" at O(αs) is δm = iπαsCA/(2a), where a is the lattice spacing.
- Power corrections to the LaMET factorization formula are of order O(M²/Pz², ΛQCD²/Pz²), where M is the hadron mass, Pz the hadron momentum, and ΛQCD the QCD scale.
- The gluon quasi-PDF operator O⁵ (the combination used in Fan 2018) is not multiplicatively renormalizable because O¹ and O²,⁴ have different renormalization factors Z11 and Z22 respectively.

## Context
This work extends the LaMET framework, previously applied predominantly to isovector quark PDFs (Chen 2018, Lin 2018, Alexandrou 2018, Liu 2018), to the gluon sector. It resolves the renormalization issues raised in earlier studies of gluon quasi-PDFs (Wang 2017) where operator mixing was discovered, and corrects the assumption of multiplicative renormalization made in the first exploratory lattice calculation of the gluon unpolarized PDF (Fan 2018). The results enable direct lattice QCD extraction of gluon PDFs, which are crucial for interpreting LHC and future Electron-Ion Collider data.
