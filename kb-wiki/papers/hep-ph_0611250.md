# Role of Glueballs in Non-Perturbative Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Discussed is how non-perturbative properties of quark gluon plasma, recently discovered in RHIC experiment, can be related to the change of properties of scalar and pseudoscalar glueballs. We set up a model with the Cornwall-Soni's glueball-gluon interaction, which shows that the pseudoscalar glueball becomes massless above the critical temperature of deconfinement phase transition. This change of properties gives rise to the change of sign of the gluon condensate at $T > T_c$. We discuss the other physical consequences resulting from the drastic change of the pseudoscalar glueball mass above the critical temperature.

## Key claim

The pseudoscalar glueball becomes massless above the critical temperature of the deconfinement phase transition, which gives rise to the change of sign of the gluon condensate at \(T > T_c\); this result depends on the specific Lorentz structure of the pseudoscalar glueball-gluon interaction and does not depend on particular values of the model parameters.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian using the Cornwall-Soni glueball-gluon interaction, where scalar and pseudoscalar glueball fields couple to gluon field strength and its dual. They calculate the glueball mass operator at finite temperature using standard methods of finite-temperature field theory, assuming a nonperturbative gluon propagator with an effective thermal mass and a Gaussian ultraviolet cut-off in Euclidean space. The contribution of glueballs to the gluon condensate is then computed in the lowest order of the effective coupling constant.

## Key numbers

*   The scalar glueball mass at zero temperature is taken as \(M_S(0) \approx 1.7\) GeV from quenched lattice results.
*   The pseudoscalar glueball mass at zero temperature is taken as \(M_P = 2.6\) GeV from quenched lattice results.
*   The residue \(f_S = 0.35\) GeV is fixed from the low-energy theorem using the gluon condensate value \(<g^2G^2> \approx 0.5\) GeV\(^4\) at zero temperature.
*   The deconfinement temperature for pure \(SU(3)_c\) is taken as \(T_c \approx 270\) MeV.
*   The effective thermal gluon mass is approximated as \(m_g(T) \approx 3T\) for \(T_c < T < 2T_c\).
*   At zero temperature, the calculated total gluon condensate is \(<g^2G^2(0)>_{total} = 0.47\) GeV\(^4\), with scalar contribution \(<g^2G^2(0)>_S = 1.31\) GeV\(^4\) and pseudoscalar contribution \(<g^2G^2(0)>_P = -0.84\) GeV\(^4\).

## Context

This work extends the investigation of glueball properties in quark-gluon plasma, previously discussed for the scalar glueball by Vento (2005), to the pseudoscalar glueball. It is motivated by lattice results for pure \(SU(3)_c\) theory showing that the gluon condensate changes sign and remains large at high temperature, contrasting with the vanishing quark condensate. The model suggests that a massless pseudoscalar glueball above \(T_c\) could dominate gluon-gluon interactions via glueball exchange over screening perturbative one-gluon exchange, and its role in QGP may be analogous to that of the massless pion in nuclear matter below the deconfinement temperature.
