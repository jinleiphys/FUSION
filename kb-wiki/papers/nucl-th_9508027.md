# Few-Body Descriptions of the piNN System in Three and Four Dimensions

> We summarise the recent theoretical progress in few-body descriptions of the piNN system. Previous descriptions, both three- and four-dimensional, are shown to possess serious theoretical inconsistencies. We illustrate how three-dimensional approaches suffer from renormalisation problems, and how four-dimensional descriptions contain both overcounting and undercounting of diagrams. We then show how such theoretical problems have been recently overcome, leading to new practical few-body equations for the piNN system.

## Key claim

The paper claims that previous three- and four-dimensional descriptions of the piNN system contain serious theoretical inconsistencies, specifically renormalization problems in three-dimensional approaches and overcounting/undercounting of diagrams in four-dimensional approaches, and that these problems have been overcome by new formulations using convolution integrals (three-dimensional) and a classification of Feynman diagrams by irreducibility (four-dimensional).

## Method

The authors derive new three-dimensional piNN equations using convolution integrals to sum over all relative time orderings of subamplitudes, thereby retaining full dressing of propagators and vertices without truncating the Hilbert space to a maximum number of pions. For the four-dimensional formulation, they classify Feynman diagrams by their irreducibility and introduce subtraction terms to eliminate overcounting, while retaining certain three-body forces that would otherwise cause undercounting of two-body contributions. Both formulations are presented in a matrix form closely resembling the earlier unitary NN-piNN model of Afnan and Blankleider (1980), allowing direct comparison and use of existing numerical codes.

## Key numbers

- The residue at the nucleon pole, Z, is typically between 0.6 and 0.8, leading to an effective piNN coupling constant in the unitary NN-piNN model that is a factor Z^(1/2) times the physical coupling constant.
- The dressed two-nucleon propagator G_NN is expressed as a convolution integral: G_NN(E) = -(1/(2πi)) ∫ dz g_1(E-z) g_2(z), with momenta set to zero for simplicity.
- The fully dressed piNN propagator G_0 is given by the double convolution G_0 = g_1 ⊗ g_2 ⊗ g_3.

## Context

This work extends the long history of few-body descriptions of the piNN system, which began with Faddeev-type approaches in the 1960s. It directly addresses inconsistencies in the unitary NN-piNN model of Avishai and collaborators (1980) and in four-dimensional formulations by Taylor (1960s) and later authors. The new convolution equations overcome the renormalization problems identified by Sauer and by the Adelaide group, while the four-dimensional equations resolve overcounting issues illustrated by Jennings (1975). The results enable practical few-body calculations for piNN reactions including pi-d scattering, NN -> pi-d, and NN -> NN, with full dressing of vertices and propagators.
