# Energy of a point-like neutron in an external electromagnetic field

> A point-like neutron in an external electromagnetic field experiences a shift in energy that mimicks the effect of an actual structural deformation of an extended neutron, i.e., a proper polarizability. In order to be able to differentiate between the former and the latter, a Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation is constructed which yields the energy shift of a point-like neutron quadratic in the external field in a derivative expansion, generalizing a long-known result for the dipole electric polarizability due to Foldy. The ten leading Foldy contributions to the energy are determined for a zero-momentum neutron. In addition, eliminating the momentum operator in favor of the velocity operator, analogous results are derived for a zero-velocity neutron. In this case, operator ordering ambiguities are encountered that permit only a determination of eight of the ten Foldy terms.

## Key claim

A point-like neutron with an anomalous magnetic moment in an external electromagnetic field experiences energy shifts quadratic in the field that mimic the effect of a proper polarizability due to structural deformation; the Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation yields the ten leading Foldy contributions for a zero-momentum neutron, but for a zero-velocity neutron, operator ordering ambiguities permit determination of only eight of the ten terms.

## Method

The authors constructed a Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation of the Dirac Hamiltonian for a neutral point particle with an anomalous magnetic moment, expanding in powers of the external electromagnetic field strengths and their derivatives up to second order in the fields and first derivatives. They evaluated the resulting Hamiltonian in terms of background fields and momenta, then extracted the energy shift for a zero-momentum neutron by setting the momentum operator to zero. For the zero-velocity case, they eliminated the momentum operator in favor of the velocity operator, encountering operator ordering ambiguities that limited the determination to eight of the ten Foldy terms.

## Key numbers

- The well-known Foldy dipole electric polarizability is given by \(\alpha_{E}^{Foldy} = -\mu^{2}/m\), where \(\mu\) is the anomalous magnetic moment and \(m\) is the neutron mass.
- For a zero-momentum neutron in vacuum, the Foldy energy shift includes the term \(\frac{\mu^{2}}{2m} E^{2}\) (dipole electric), \(-\mu \sigma \cdot B\) (magnetic dipole), and terms proportional to \(\frac{\mu^{2}}{8m^{2}}\) and \(\frac{\mu^{2}}{4m^{2}}\) for spin and mixed polarizabilities.
- The perturbative correction to the local limit from the electric field spatial dependence in a box of side \(L\) is \(W^{E,1} = (mL)^{2} \frac{\mu^{2}}{24m^{3}} \nabla_{j} E_{i} \nabla_{j} E_{i}\), showing dependence on the product \(mL\).

## Context

This work generalizes the long-known Foldy result for the dipole electric polarizability (Foldy, 1958) to all ten polarizabilities defined in the effective Hamiltonian of Babusci et al. (1998), including spin, dispersion, and quadrupole polarizabilities. It extends the treatment of Foldy-type effects to match the level of detail needed for modern phenomenological extractions of spin polarizabilities (Martel et al., 2011; Miskimen et al., 2012; Paudyal et al., 2019) and for lattice QCD calculations of neutron polarizabilities (Woloshyn, 1995; Lee et al., 2005; Shintani et al., 2007; Detmold et al., 2006; Alexandru et al., 2016). The results enable separation of the point-particle Foldy contributions from the proper polarizabilities due to neutron substructure in both zero-momentum and zero-velocity states, with the caveat that the local effective Hamiltonian form (Babusci, 1998) has limitations when applied to states with finite spatial extent.
