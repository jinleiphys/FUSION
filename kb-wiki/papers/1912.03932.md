# An overview of the scientific contribution of Andrea Vitturi to Nuclear Physics (being an account of the recent TNP19 meeting held in Padova)

> We give an account of the main achievements of the scientific career of Andrea Vitturi so far, that have recently been discussed during the workshop "Theoretical Nuclear Physics in Padova" on the occasion of his retirement from full professor at the University of Padova. He has oftentimes been the driving force behind numerous contributions to nuclear structure and nuclear reactions that are here reviewed: giant resonances, pairing correlations, collective modes, algebraic models, inelastic excitations, electromagnetic response, break-up and transfer reactions, coupled-channel formalism, clustering, subbarrier fusion processes, etc. Among these topics several inspirational works and ideas can be found that we would like to highlight.

## Key claim

This review paper summarizes the scientific career of Andrea Vitturi, highlighting his role as a driving force behind numerous contributions to nuclear structure and reactions, but the authors note that his humble attitude often prevented him from taking full credit for developments where he was the primary instigator.

## Method

The paper organizes Vitturi's extensive scientific production (more than 200 items) into topical sections covering quadrupole collective models, quantum shape phase transitions in the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) and Interacting Boson-Fermion Model (IBFM), giant resonances, giant pairing vibrations, two-neutron pairing correlations, transfer reactions, eikonal approximation, clustering, and subbarrier fusion processes. The review draws on work presented at the workshop "Theoretical Nuclear Physics in Padova" held in May 2019, supplemented by additional papers from Vitturi's career that the authors deemed important.

## Context

This review extends the tradition of summarizing nuclear physics contributions from prominent theorists, similar to previous review papers on Bohr Hamiltonian solutions (2005FO18, buganu, Casten, Cejnar) and critical point symmetries (Iachello 2000, 2001). The paper enables future work by documenting the Padova-Sevilla collaboration's long-standing contributions to connecting algebraic IBM/IBFM models with geometric collective models, and by highlighting open questions about the extraction of structure properties from reaction cross-sections, particularly regarding the separation of reaction and structure factors in transfer and inelastic scattering processes.
