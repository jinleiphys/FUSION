# Investigation of effects of pairing correlations on calculated $β$-decay half-lives of fp-shell nuclei

> Pairing of nucleons plays a key role in solving various nuclear physics problems. We investigate the probable effects of pairing correlations on the calculated Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions and the associated $β$-decay half-lives. Computations are performed for a total of 35 fp-shell nuclei using the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model. The nuclei were selected because of their importance in various astrophysical environments. Pairing gaps are one of the key parameters in the pn-QRPA model to compute GT transitions. We employed three different values of the pairing gaps obtained from three different empirical formulae in our calculation. The GT strength distributions changed significantly as the pairing gap values changed. This in turn resulted in contrasting centroid and total strength values of the calculated GT distributions and led to differences in calculated half-lives using the three schemes. The half-life values computed via the three-term pairing formula, based on separation energies of nucleons, were in best agreement with the measured data. We conclude that the traditional choice of pairing gap values, $Δ_p = Δ_n = 12/\sqrt{A}$, may not lead to half-life values in good agreement with measured data. The findings of this study are interesting but warrant further investigation.

# Investigation of effects of pairing correlations on calculated β-decay half-lives of fp-shell nuclei

## Key claim
The traditional choice of pairing gap values (Δp = Δn = 12/√A) may not yield β-decay half-lives in good agreement with measured data; instead, pairing gaps computed from a three-term formula based on nucleon separation energies produce half-lives that best match experimental values, though the authors caution that the findings are preliminary and warrant further investigation with a larger pool of nuclei.

## Method
The authors used the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model with a Nilsson deformed basis and BCS pairing to compute Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions and β-decay half-lives for 35 fp-shell nuclei of astrophysical importance. Three different empirical formulae for pairing gaps were employed: the traditional mass-dependent formula (TF, Δ = 12/√A), a three-term formula (3TF) based on proton and neutron separation energies, and a five-term formula (5TF) based on binding energies. The calculated half-lives were compared with experimental data from Kondev (2021).

## Key numbers
- 35 fp-shell nuclei were studied, of which 17 decay via electron emission and 18 via β+ decay
- The 3TF scheme produced the lowest standard deviation from measured half-lives: 102 s
- The 5TF scheme gave a standard deviation of 762 s
- The TF scheme gave the largest standard deviation: 6879 s
- The 3TF scheme reproduced 15 β-decay half-lives within a factor of 2 of experimental data (compared to 11 for TF and 10 for 5TF)
- The case of β+ decay of 45Ti was excluded from standard deviation calculations due to a missing entry in the 5TF scheme
- The ph and pp interaction strengths were chosen as χ = 5.2/A^0.7 and κ = 0.58/A^0.7, respectively
- The constant C in the half-life formula was taken as 6295 s, and gA/gV was taken as -1.254

## Context
This work extends previous pn-QRPA calculations of β-decay properties by Nabi and collaborators (2021) and addresses the sensitivity of GT strength distributions to pairing gap parameters, a key input in the pn-QRPA model as established by Staudt (1990) and Hirsch (1993). The study contrasts with the gross theory approach of Takahashi (1973) and shell model techniques of Martinez-Pinedo (1999), which may be limited to lighter nuclei as noted by Tan (2017). The findings enable improved predictions of β-decay half-lives for astrophysical simulations of supernova evolution and nucleosynthesis, particularly for the weak interaction rates that govern stellar core collapse.
