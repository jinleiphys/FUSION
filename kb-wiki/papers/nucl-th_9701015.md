# Meson-exchange contributions to the nuclear charge operator

> The role of the meson-exchange current correction to the nuclear charge operator is studied in electron scattering processes involving the excitation of medium and heavy nuclei to energies up to the quasi-elastic peak. The effect of these contributions in the quasi-free electron scattering process is a reduction of at most a 3% in the longitudinal response at the energy of the peak, a value which is below the experimental error and must not be taken into account in calculations in this energy region. On the other hand, the excitation of low-lying nuclear levels of neutronic character shows, with respect to the protonic ones, a considerable effect due to the inclusion of the two-body term in the charge operator. More realistic calculations, such as those performed in the random-phase approximation framework, give rise to a mixing of one particle-one hole configurations of both kinds which reduce these effects. However, it has been found that the excitation of some of these levels is sizeably affected by the meson-exchange contribution. More precise experimental data concerning some of these states, such as e.g. the high-spin states in 208Pb, could throw some light in the problem of a more feasible determination of these effects and, as a consequence, could provide an alternative procedure to obtain the charge neutron form factor.

## Key claim

The meson-exchange current (MEC) correction to the nuclear charge operator produces a reduction of at most 3% in the longitudinal response at the quasi-elastic peak energy, a value below experimental error and therefore negligible in that energy region; however, for low-lying nuclear levels of neutronic character, the two-body term in the charge operator can produce sizeable effects, though these are reduced when more realistic random-phase approximation (RPA) calculations mix proton and neutron 1p-1h configurations.

## Method

The author calculated the MEC contribution to the nuclear charge operator, focusing on the seagull-type term of pion exchange, and evaluated its effect on electron scattering observables for medium and heavy nuclei. For the quasi-elastic peak, a Fermi gas model with symmetric nuclear matter was used to compute the longitudinal response including the interference between the impulse approximation (IA) and MEC terms. For bound-state excitations, the longitudinal form factors were calculated for closed-shell nuclei (16O, 40Ca, 48Ca, 208Pb) first in the extreme shell model with Woods-Saxon single-particle states, then in the RPA framework using a Landau-Migdal residual interaction.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of MEC contributions to nuclear charge form factors in light nuclei (Kloet and Tjon 1974 for 3H and 3He; Radomski and Riska 1976 for 16O and 40Ca; Negele and Riska 1978 for closed-shell nuclei throughout the periodic table; Riska and Strüve 1983; Lodhi and Hamilton 1985 for 6Li) by systematically investigating MEC effects in electron scattering to both the quasi-elastic peak and low-lying bound states in medium and heavy nuclei. The results show that while MEC contributions are negligible for the quasi-elastic peak, they can be sizeable for neutronic transitions in closed-shell nuclei, particularly in 48Ca and 208Pb, suggesting that precise experimental data on high-spin states in 208Pb could provide an alternative method to determine the charge neutron form factor.
