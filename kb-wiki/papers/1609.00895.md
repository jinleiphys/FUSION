# Theoretical study of the Xi(1620) and Xi(1690) resonances in Xi_c -> pi^+ MB decays

> Nonleptonic weak decays of Xi_c into pi^+ and a meson (M)-baryon (B) final state, MB, are analyzed from the viewpoint of probing S=-2 baryon resonances, i.e. Xi(1620) and Xi(1690), of which spin-parity and other properties are not well known. We argue that the weak decay of Xi_c is dominated by a single quark-line diagram, preferred by the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa coefficient, color recombination factor, the diquark correlation, and the kinematical condition. The decay process has an advantage of being free from meson resonances in the pi^+M invariant mass distribution. The invariant mass distribution of the meson-baryon final state is calculated with three different chiral unitary approaches, assuming that the Xi(1620) and Xi(1690) resonances have J^P=1/2^-. It is found that a clear peak for the Xi(1690) is seen in the piXi and KbarLambda spectra. We also suggest that the ratios of the piXi, KbarLambda and KbarSigma final states are useful to distinguish whether the peak is originated from the Xi(1690) resonance or it is a KbarSigma threshold effect.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the $\Xi_c^+ \to \pi^+ MB$ decay provides a clean filter for $I=1/2$, $S=-2$ baryon resonances, specifically the $\Xi(1620)$ and $\Xi(1690)$, under the assumption that these resonances have spin-parity $J^P = 1/2^-$, and that the decay is dominated by a single quark-line diagram.

## Method

The authors analyzed the $\Xi_c^+ \to \pi^+ MB$ decay using a dominant quark-line diagram where the $c$ quark decays weakly into an $s$ quark and a high-momentum $\pi^+$, followed by $\bar{q}q$ pair creation and final-state interaction of the meson-baryon pair. The final-state interaction was calculated using three different chiral unitary approaches (denoted ROB, GLN, and Sekihara) to account for the coupled-channel scattering of the meson-baryon system in the $S=-2$ sector.

## Key numbers

* The $\Xi(1690)$ resonance has a PDG mass of $1690 \pm 10$ MeV and a width of $<30$ MeV.
* The $\Xi(1620)$ resonance is a one-star PDG state with unknown spin and parity.
* The $\Xi_c^+ \to \pi^+ (\bar{K}^0\Lambda)$ reaction is favored because the $\pi^+\bar{K}^0$ pair must be in isospin $I=3/2$, making it free from meson resonances like the $K^*(892)$.
* The coefficients for the intermediate meson-baryon state in the isospin basis are $h_{\bar{K}\Lambda} = 1/\sqrt{6}$, $h_{\bar{K}\Sigma} = -\sqrt{3/2}$, and $h_{\eta\Xi} = -1/\sqrt{3}$, with $h_{\pi\Xi} = 0$.

## Context

This work extends the methodology used in previous studies of $\Lambda_b$ and $\Lambda_c$ decays, such as those by Roca (2015) and Miyahara (2015), which showed that weak decays of heavy baryons can act as filters for specific isospin states of baryon resonances. The paper specifically addresses the poorly known $\Xi(1620)$ and $\Xi(1690)$ resonances, which have been studied in chiral unitary approaches by Ramos (2002), Garcia-Recio (2003), and Sekihara (2015), and suggests that the $\Xi_c$ decay offers a cleaner environment than $\Lambda_c$ decays for studying these states because it avoids overlap with meson resonances like the $a_0(980)$.
