# The ground state in a spin-one color superconductor

> Color superconductors in which quarks of the same flavor form Cooper pairs are investigated. These Cooper pairs carry total spin one. A systematic group-theoretical classification of possible phases in a spin-one color superconductor is presented, revealing parallels and differences to the theory of superfluid $^3$He. General expressions for the gap parameter, the critical temperature, and the pressure are derived and evaluated for several spin-one phases, with special emphasis on the angular structure of the gap equation. It is shown that the (transverse) color-spin-locked phase is expected to be the ground state.

## Key claim

The paper systematically classifies possible phases in a spin-one color superconductor (where same-flavor quarks form spin-one Cooper pairs) and argues that the (transverse) color-spin-locked (CSL) phase is expected to be the ground state, based on a group-theoretical analysis and evaluation of the condensation energy, though this conclusion is drawn within the weak-coupling approximation and for the specific case of a one-flavor system.

## Method

The author performs a group-theoretical classification of possible symmetry breaking patterns for a spin-one color superconductor, using the invariance condition of the order parameter under transformations of the residual symmetry group, analogous to the classification of phases in superfluid ³He. General expressions for the gap parameter, critical temperature, and pressure are derived from the effective action using the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) formalism, and these are evaluated for four specific inert phases (polar, planar, A, and CSL), each considered in three chirality-pairing cases (longitudinal, mixed, and transverse).

## Key numbers

- The ratio of condensation energies of the A phase and B phase in superfluid ³He is Δp_A/Δp_B ≃ 0.88 (from weak-coupling theory at zero temperature).
- For the 2SC phase, the gap parameter at zero temperature is φ₀^(2SC) = 2 b̃ b₀' μ exp(-π/(2ḡ)), where ḡ ≡ g/(3√2 π), b̃ ≡ 256π⁴ (2/(N_f g²))^(5/2), and b₀' ≡ exp(-(π²+4)/8).
- For the CFL phase, the gap parameter is φ₀^(CFL) = 2^(-1/3) φ₀^(2SC).
- The ratio of critical temperature to gap parameter for a two-gap structure is T_c/φ₀ = (e^γ/π) e^ζ ≃ 0.57 e^ζ, where γ ≃ 0.577 is the Euler-Mascheroni constant and ζ ≡ ln(λ₁^(a₁) λ₂^(a₂))^(1/2).

## Context

This work extends the classification of spin-one color superconducting phases previously discussed by Bailin and Love (1984), Rischke (2000), Schäfer (2000), and the author's own prior work, drawing explicit parallels to the theory of superfluid ³He as developed by Leggett (1975) and Vollhardt and Wölfle (1990). The results enable quantitative comparison of the condensation energies of different spin-one phases, which is relevant for determining the ground state of dense quark matter in the interior of neutron stars, where densities can reach an order of magnitude larger than nuclear ground state density and temperatures are of order keV.
