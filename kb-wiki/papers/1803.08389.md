# Holographic Charged Fluid with Chiral Electric Separation Effect

> Hydrodynamics with both vector and axial currents is under study within a holographic model, consisting of canonical $U(1)_V\times U(1)_A$ gauge fields in an asymptotically AdS$_5$ black brane. When gravitational back-reaction is taken into account, the chiral electric separation effect (CESE), namely the generation of an axial current as the response to an external electric field, is realized naturally. Via fluid/gravity correspondence, all the first order transport coefficients in the hydrodynamic constitutive relations are evaluated analytically: they are functions of vector chemical potential $μ$, axial chemical potential $μ_5$ and the fluid's temperature $T$. Apart from the proportionality factor $μμ_5$, the CESE conductivity is found to be dependent on the dimensionless quantities $μ/T$ and $μ_5/T$ nontrivially. As a complementary study, frequency-dependent transport phenomena are revealed through linear response analysis, demonstrating perfect agreement with the results obtained from fluid/gravity correspondence.

## Key claim

The chiral electric separation effect (CESE) arises naturally in a holographic model with canonical \(U(1)_V\times U(1)_A\) gauge fields when gravitational back-reaction is taken into account, even without explicit interaction between the gauge fields in the bulk action or Chern-Simons terms; the coupling between vector and axial current perturbations emerges from the back-reaction itself.

## Method

The authors consider a 5-dimensional Einstein-gravity action with a negative cosmological constant coupled to two Maxwell fields for the vector and axial gauge symmetries, without Chern-Simons terms. Using the fluid/gravity correspondence, they analytically compute all first-order dissipative transport coefficients in the hydrodynamic constitutive relations for the stress-energy tensor and the vector and axial currents. As a complementary study, frequency-dependent transport coefficients are obtained via linear response analysis, and the low-frequency limits are shown to agree with the fluid/gravity results.

## Key numbers

* Shear viscosity \(\eta = s/(4\pi)\) and bulk viscosity \(\zeta = 0\), where \(s\) is the entropy density of the dual fluid.
* In the high-temperature limit (\(\bar{\mu}, \bar{\mu}_5 \ll 1\)), the Ohmic conductivity \(\sigma_e \simeq \pi T\) and the CESE conductivity \(\sigma_{5e} \simeq -\pi T \bar{\mu} \bar{\mu}_5\).
* In the low-temperature limit (\(\bar{\mu}, \bar{\mu}_5 \gg 1\)), \(\sigma_e \simeq \pi T \bar{\mu}_5^2 / \sqrt{6(\bar{\mu}^2+\bar{\mu}_5^2)}\) and \(\sigma_{5e} \simeq -\pi T \bar{\mu} \bar{\mu}_5 / \sqrt{6(\bar{\mu}^2+\bar{\mu}_5^2)}\).
* The intrinsic conductivities satisfy \(\sigma_e \sigma_{5e} \ge \sigma_{5e} \tilde{\sigma}_{5e}\) as required by the second law of thermodynamics.

## Context

This work extends earlier holographic studies of CESE that employed the probe limit (e.g., Pu et al. (2014) using the Sakai-Sugimoto model) by including gravitational back-reaction, which generates the coupling between vector and axial perturbations. It contrasts with perturbative calculations in thermal QED (Huang et al. (2013)) and QCD (Jiang et al. (2014)) where the CESE conductivity was positive, whereas here it is found to be negative in the high-temperature regime. The results enable further investigation of CESE in strongly coupled plasmas and its potential phenomenological consequences in heavy-ion collisions and Dirac/Weyl semimetals.
