# Evidence for In-Medium Changes of Four-Quark Condensates

> Utilizing the QCD sum rule approach to the behavior of the omega meson in nuclear matter we derive evidence for in-medium changes of particular four-quark condensates from the recent CB-TAPS experiment for the reaction gamma + A -> A' + omega (-> pi0 gamma) with A = Nb and LH2.

## Key claim

The observed in-medium reduction of the omega meson mass in the CB-TAPS experiment implies a drastic (more than 50%) drop of specific four-quark condensates in nuclear matter, provided the QCD sum rule is truncated beyond mass dimension-6 and the linear density expansion is valid up to nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The authors use QCD sum rules for the omega meson in nuclear matter, performing an operator product expansion and Borel transformation of the twice-subtracted dispersion relation for the current-current correlator. They expand the four-quark condensates in density using correction factors kappa_Omega relative to the factorized ground-state saturation approximation, and linearize the resulting expressions. The experimental invariant mass distributions from the CB-TAPS reaction gamma + A -> A' + omega (-> pi0 gamma) for Nb and LH2 targets are used to extract the in-medium omega meson mass squared via a normalized moment of the spectral function.

## Key numbers

- m_omega^2 (LH2) = 0.599 GeV^2 and m_omega^2 (Nb) = 0.568 GeV^2, extracted from CB-TAPS count rates in the interval s = 0.41 to 0.77 GeV^2 for Borel mass M ~ O(1) GeV
- The in-medium shift parameter Delta is approximately (4 - kappa_N) * 0.03 / n_0 GeV^2, where n_0 = 0.15 fm^-3 is nuclear saturation density
- The combined four-quark condensates must drop by more than 50% at nuclear saturation density, requiring kappa_N >= 4 for the described parameter set
- The condition for a decreasing m_omega^2 in medium is kappa_N > 4^{+0.7}_{-0.7} - 2.8^{+0.2}_{-0.4} * 10^3 GeV^{-8} n_0 c_4^{(1)}, with c_4^{(1)} < 1.7^{+0.3}_{-0.6} * 10^{-3} n_0^{-1} GeV^8

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach to four-quark condensates in the vector channel, previously studied by Shuryak and Schafer, and builds on the formalism developed by Zschocke and Kampfer (2004). The analysis uses experimental data from the CB-TAPS collaboration, which observed additional low-energy omega decay strength for a Nb target compared to a LH2 target. The results contradict the simple factorization approximation (ground state saturation) and the large-N_c expectation that kappa_N = kappa_0, and instead require a strong density dependence of the combined pure flavor scalar dimension-6 condensates. This enables future studies of other channels to gain more information on these particular four-quark condensates as order parameters for chiral symmetry restoration.
