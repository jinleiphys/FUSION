# Vector-axialvector mixing in hot matter and its hadronic effective field theory description

> We discuss the importance of the axialvector meson in chiral phase transition at finite temperature. We show that there exists a significant contribution in the vector spectral function from the axialvector meson through the vector-axialvector mixing in hot matter.

## Key claim

The vector-axialvector mixing in hot matter, driven by thermal pions, produces a significant contribution to the vector spectral function from the axialvector meson; this effect is demonstrated within a hadronic effective field theory (generalized hidden local symmetry, GHLS) and is shown to persist even when the ρ and a₁ masses drop toward chiral restoration, though the mixing itself vanishes at the critical temperature.

## Method

The authors used the generalized hidden local symmetry (GHLS) framework, which incorporates pions, ρ mesons, and a₁ mesons consistently with chiral symmetry, to compute the vector current correlator and its spectral function at finite temperature. They compared full calculations including vector-axialvector mixing with calculations where the a₁ meson was eliminated, and studied the temperature evolution of the spectral function from T/T_c = 0.65 to 1.0, as well as the effect of dropping versus non-dropping ρ bare masses.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature set to T_c = 200 MeV for the calculations.
- Vector spectral function shown at T/T_c = 0.85, comparing full calculation (including a₁) with calculation eliminating the axialvector meson.
- Temperature dependence displayed from T/T_c = 0.65 to 1.0 in steps of 0.1.
- The a₁ meson mass is given by M_{a₁}² = M_ρ² + δM²(T), where δM²(T) vanishes at T_c.
- The vector spectral function at T/T_c = 0.85 is compared for dropping and non-dropping ρ bare mass scenarios.

## Context

This work extends the model-independent low-temperature theorem for vector-axialvector mixing (valid only for T < 2f_π) to higher temperatures near the chiral phase transition, using the GHLS framework developed by Harada and collaborators (2003) and the vector manifestation scenario (VM) of Harada and Yamawaki (2001). The results are relevant for interpreting dilepton production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where baryon density is low and many-body effects are reduced compared to SPS energies; the authors note that previous dilepton studies (e.g., Rapp and Wambach, 2000; Hatsuda and Lee, 1992) have not properly treated a₁-meson properties and V-A mixing near the critical point.
