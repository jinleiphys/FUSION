# A Microscopic Picture of Strangeness Production at the SPS

> The microscopic origin of the strangeness enhancements observed in PbPb collisions at the SPS is studied by testing the effects of three mechanisms. First, the junction processes found in the HIJING/BBbar event generator are reviewed showing that they can account for only part of the observed hyperon enhancements. Second, the efficient final state chemical changing processes (e.g. Lambda + K -> Xi + pi) are included by adding the General Cascade Program (GCP) to HIJING/BBbar. While these processes are shown to further enhance multistrange baryon production, HIJING/BBbar+GCP is still unable to fully describe either the large hyperon yields or the increasing K+/pi+ ratio. Finally, the addition of transient field fluctuations or ropes to HIJING/BBbar+GCP is shown to be needed to account for the large hyperon yields and for the behavior of the K+/pi+ ratio.

# A Microscopic Picture of Strangeness Production at the SPS

## Key claim

Three hadronic mechanisms, baryon junction processes, final state strangeness exchange interactions, and transient field fluctuations (ropes), are together required to reproduce the observed strangeness enhancements in PbPb collisions at the SPS, but no single mechanism nor the combination of the first two can fully account for both the hyperon yields and the rising K+/pi+ ratio without the addition of ropes.

## Method

The HIJING/BBbar event generator was used to simulate baryon junction stopping and junction loop pair production. The General Cascade Program (GCP), which solves the relativistic Boltzmann equation with two-body collisions, was coupled to HIJING/BBbar to include final state strangeness exchange, strangeness creation, and baryon-antibaryon annihilation processes. Finally, transient field fluctuations (ropes) were added by increasing the string tension as the square-root of the number of overlapping wounded nucleons within a radius of 0.25 fm, enhancing strange quark pair production via the Schwinger mechanism.

## Key numbers

- A factor of 20 enhancement is observed for the Omega in central PbPb collisions over that found in pPb and pBe collisions (WA97 Collaboration).
- The K+/pi+ ratio in central PbPb collisions is twice the value observed in pp collisions (NA49 Collaboration).
- In pp interactions, the string tension kappa is approximately 1 GeV/fm (0.2 GeV^2), giving a strange-to-up quark pair production probability P_{s/u} of about 0.3 using constituent masses m_s = 0.45 GeV and m_u = 0.325 GeV.
- When ropes are added, the string tension grows as kappa = sqrt(n) GeV/fm, where n is the number of overlapping wounded nucleons within a radius of 0.25 fm.
- HIJING/BBbar+GCP underestimates the K+/pi+ ratio in the most central collisions by a factor of 2 compared to preliminary NA49 data.

## Context

This work extends the HIJING/BBbar event generator approach (Vance, 1999) by adding final state interactions via the General Cascade Program (Pang, 1992) and rope mechanisms (Biro, 1984). It contrasts with models assuming an initial quark-gluon plasma state, such as statistical fireball models (Letessier, 2000; Braun-Munzinger, 1999), the ALCOR coalescence model, and a first-order phase transition model (Bass, 2000), which can describe the observed yields from a plasma initial condition. The paper demonstrates that purely hadronic mechanisms can reproduce the SPS strangeness data without assuming a plasma, enabling further microscopic studies of strangeness production in heavy-ion collisions.
