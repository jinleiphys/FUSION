# Neutron star equation of state from the quark level in the light of GW170817

> Matter state inside neutron stars is an exciting problem in astrophysics, nuclear physics and particle physics. The equation of state (EOS) of neutron stars plays a crucial role in the present multimessenger astronomy, especially after the event of GW170817. We propose a new neutron star EOS "QMF18" from the quark level, which describes well robust observational constraints from free-space nucleon, nuclear matter saturation, heavy pulsar measurements and the tidal deformability of the very recent GW170817 observation. For this purpose, we employ the quark-mean-field (QMF) model, allowing one to tune the density dependence of the symmetry energy and study effectively its correlations with the Love number and the tidal deformability. We provide tabulated data for the new EOS and compare it with other recent EOSs from various many-body frameworks.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new neutron star equation of state called "QMF18" built from the quark level that satisfies all current observational constraints, but they find no simple correlation between the symmetry energy slope and the tidal deformability, meaning that gravitational-wave measurements of tidal deformability do not necessarily translate into a measurement of neutron star radius.

## Method

The authors employ the quark-mean-field (QMF) model, where constituent quarks are confined by a harmonic oscillator potential with parameters fitted to reproduce the free-space nucleon mass (939 MeV) and radius (0.87 fm). They then construct nuclear matter in the mean-field approximation with meson coupling parameters fitted to empirical saturation properties (density 0.16 fm^-3, binding energy -16 MeV, incompressibility 240 MeV, symmetry energy 31 MeV) for four different values of the symmetry energy slope L (20, 40, 60, 80 MeV). The resulting equations of state are used to compute neutron star mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities, which are compared with observations of massive pulsars and the GW170817 event.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3, binding energy E/A = -16 MeV, incompressibility K = 240 MeV, symmetry energy E_sym = 31 MeV, effective mass ratio M_N*/M_N = 0.77
- Free nucleon properties: M_N = 939 MeV, r_N = 0.87 fm, fitted potential parameters V_0 = -62.257187 MeV and a = 0.534296 fm^-3
- For the QMF18 model (L = 40 MeV): maximum TOV mass M_TOV = 2.0805 M_sun, radius for 1.4 M_sun star R(1.4) = 11.77 km, tidal deformability Lambda(1.4) = 331
- For L = 40 MeV with different crust models, Lambda(1.4) ranges from 324 to 344, with crust influence limited to about 6 percent
- GW170817 constraint: Lambda(1.4) less than 800 for low-spin prior, upper limit 1400 for high-spin prior

## Context

This work extends the quark-mean-field (QMF) model approach previously applied to nuclear matter (Zhu et al. 2018) by refitting parameters to achieve two-solar-mass maximum mass for the first time within QMF, and by incorporating the GW170817 tidal deformability constraint from LIGO/Virgo (2017). The authors compare their QMF18 EOS with other recent EOSs from various many-body frameworks including relativistic mean-field models (NL3omegaRho, DDME2, DD2 from Fortin et al. 2016), density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF and DDRHFDelta from Zhu et al. 2016), Skyrme functional (Sly9 from Fortin et al. 2016), and the BCPM energy density functional (Sharma et al. 2015). The finding that tidal deformability does not correlate simply with the symmetry energy slope contradicts expectations from earlier work (Lattimer & Prakash 2001, 2004; Horowitz & Piekarewicz 2001) and enables caution in claims about constraining neutron star radius from gravitational-wave measurements.
