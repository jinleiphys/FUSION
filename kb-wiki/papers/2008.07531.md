# TMD factorization for dijet and heavy-meson pair in DIS

> We study a transverse momentum dependent (TMD) factorization framework for the processes of di-jet and heavy meson pair production in deep-inelastic-scattering in an electron-proton collider, considering the measurement of the transverse momentum imbalance of the two hard probes in the Breit frame. For the factorization theorem we employ soft-collinear and boosted-heavy-quark effective field theories. The factorized cross-section for both processes is sensitive to gluon unpolarized and linearly polarized TMD distributions and requires the introduction of a new soft function. We calculate the new soft function here at one loop, regulating rapidity divergences with the $δ$-regulator. In addition, using a factorization consistency relation and a universality argument regarding the heavy-quark jet function, we obtain the anomalous dimension of the new soft function at two loops.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a transverse momentum dependent (TMD) factorization framework for dijet and heavy-meson pair production in deep-inelastic scattering, but the factorization holds only under the condition that the transverse momentum imbalance of the two hard probes is much smaller than their average transverse momentum, specifically \(|\mathbf{r}_T| \ll p_T\), and requires that the partonic Mandelstam variables satisfy \(\hat{s} \sim |\hat{t}| \sim |\hat{u}|\) to avoid large logarithms in the hard factor.

## Method

The authors employed soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) and boosted-heavy-quark effective field theory to derive factorized cross-sections for the processes \(\ell + h \to \ell' + J_1 + J_2 + X\) and \(\ell + h \to \ell' + H + \bar{H} + X\) in the Breit frame. They calculated the new three-direction soft function at one-loop order using the \(\delta\)-regulator for rapidity divergences, and used a factorization consistency relation along with a universality argument for the heavy-quark jet function to obtain the anomalous dimension of the new soft function at two loops.

## Key numbers

- The dijet process is considered for jet transverse momenta \(p_T \in [4, 20]\) GeV and parton energy fraction \(\xi \in [10^{-2}, 1]\).
- The kinematic region for factorization requires \(p_T^{H/\bar{H}} \gg m_H\) for heavy-meson pair production, where \(m_H\) is the heavy meson mass.
- The soft function one-loop result contains color factors \(C_A = N_c\) and \(C_F = (N_c^2 - 1)/(2N_c)\), with \(\gamma_g = \beta_0/(2C_A)\) and \(\gamma_q = 3/2\).
- The parameter \(A_{\mathbf{b}} = (v_1 \cdot v_2) / [2 (v_1 \cdot \hat{b}) (v_2 \cdot \hat{b})]\) is bounded to values less than or equal to \(-1\).
- The EIC coverage considered includes center-of-mass energies \(\sqrt{s} = 140\), 63, and 28 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the TMD factorization framework for gluon distributions beyond conventional processes like Drell-Yan and semi-inclusive DIS, building on prior studies of dijet production at the EIC (Chu 2017, Dumitru 2018, Zheng 2018, Page 2019) and heavy-meson pair production (Zhu 2013, Zhang 2017, Boer 2010). The new three-direction soft function has similarities to that found in vector boson plus jet processes in hadronic colliders (Buffing 2018, Chien 2019), but with a different structure of rapidity divergences. The results enable future phenomenological studies at the EIC and raise questions about the universality of TMDs beyond conventional processes, requiring further theoretical work to quantify universality-breaking effects.
