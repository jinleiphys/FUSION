# Resonant tunneling of deuteron-triton fusion in strong high-frequency electromagnetic fields

> We investigate deuteron-triton (DT) fusion in the presence of linearly polarized strong electromagnetic fields in high-frequency limit, in which a complex spherical square-well potential is exploited to describe the nuclear potential. Within the framework of the Kramers-Henneberger (KH) transformation, we have calculated the total and angular differential fusion cross sections by investigating the asymptotical phase shifts of the Coulomb wavefunctions. With introducing a dimensionless quantity of $n_d$ representing the ratio of the particle quiver oscillation amplitude to the radius of nuclear potential, we find that, even though the tunneling probability of passing through the Coulomb repulsive potential keeps almost identical to that in the absence of electromagnetic fields, the peak of total fusion sections shows an apparent shift from the well known value of 110 keV to 78 keV for $n_d=0.01$. The angular differential cross sections also show some resonance peaks that shift from zero inclination angle to $π/2$ with increasing the parameter $n_d$. The corresponding astrophysical $S$-factors are found to be enhanced by several times in amplitudes. With the help of Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) approximate wavefunctions, the shape-resonance tunneling mechanism of the above findings are uncovered and some implications are discussed.

## Key claim
The peak of the total DT fusion cross section shifts from the well-known 110 keV to 78 keV for a dimensionless field parameter \(n_d = 0.01\), even though the tunneling probability through the Coulomb barrier remains almost unchanged; the astrophysical \(S\)-factor is enhanced several times in amplitude, indicating that strong electromagnetic fields can significantly alter the nuclear part of the fusion cross section via a shape-resonance tunneling mechanism.

## Method
The authors model the deuteron-triton (DT) nuclear interaction with a complex spherical square-well optical potential (real part \(V_r = -30.00\) MeV, imaginary part \(V_i = -49.64\) keV, radius parameter \(r_0 = 1.338\) fm). They apply the Kramers-Henneberger (KH) transformation to treat the linearly polarized high-frequency electromagnetic field, then time-average the resulting potential to obtain an effective static, angle-dependent potential. The angle-dependent differential fusion cross section is computed from the complex phase shift of the S-wave (\(l=0\)) Coulomb wavefunction, and the total cross section is obtained by integration over solid angle.

## Key numbers
- The peak of the total DT fusion cross section shifts from 110 keV (field-free) to 78 keV for \(n_d = 0.01\).
- The dimensionless field parameter is defined as \(n_d = r_e / r_n = 4.89 \times 10^{-6} \sqrt{I} / (\hbar \omega)^2\), where \(I\) is in W/cm\(^2\) and \(\hbar\omega\) in eV.
- The optical potential parameters used are \(V_r = -30.00\) MeV, \(V_i = -49.64\) keV, and \(r_0 = 1.338\) fm.
- The effective charge for the DT system is \(q_{\rm eff} = e/5\).

## Context
This work extends prior studies of DT fusion in strong laser fields that focused on enhancing the tunneling probability through the Coulomb barrier (Queisser 2019, Lv 2019, Wang 2020, Liu 2021, Kohlfu 2021, Lv 2021, Qi 2021) but assumed the astrophysical \(S\)-factor unchanged. By using a complex spherical square-well optical model (as in Li Xingzhong 2000, 2008; Singh 2019; Wu 2022), the authors show that the field modifies the nuclear potential parameters, leading to shape-resonance tunneling that shifts the cross-section peak and enhances the \(S\)-factor. This suggests that the Lawson criterion for controlled DT fusion may be relaxed in strong electromagnetic fields, and opens the door to future studies with more realistic nuclear potentials (e.g., Woods-Saxon) and higher-order Fourier terms in the KH expansion.
