# Investigating saturation effects in ultraperipheral collisions at the LHC with the color dipole model

> We investigate saturation effects in $ep$ scattering as well as in ultraperipheral $p$A and AA collisions at small $x$ with four variants of the impact parameter dependent color dipole model: with and without gluon saturation and with and without a novel mechanism that suppresses unphysical dipole radii above the confinement scale, a problem not addressed by most implementations. We show that $ep$ scattering at HERA can be very well described by any of the four variants. When going from $ep$ to $e$A scattering, saturation effects are expected to increase as $\sim$A$^{1/3}$. In lieu of an electron-ion collider, we confront the different versions of the dipole model with data recorded in ultraperipheral collisions at the LHC in order to estimate the sensitivity of the data to gluon saturation in the target nuclei. We find that ultraperipheral PbPb collisions indicate strong saturation effects while $p$Pb collisions turn out to not have any discriminating power to distinguish saturation from non-saturation scenarios.

## Key claim

The paper shows that ultraperipheral PbPb collisions at the LHC indicate strong saturation effects, while pPb collisions have no discriminating power to distinguish saturation from non-saturation scenarios, even after introducing a new damping mechanism that suppresses unphysical large dipole radii above the confinement scale.

## Method

The authors implemented four variants of the impact parameter dependent color dipole model (bSat and bNonSat, each with and without a novel damping mechanism for large dipole radii) in the Sartre event generator. They fit the model parameters to HERA I+II inclusive DIS data (409 data points for reduced cross section and 34 for charm) and then compared predictions to exclusive J/psi production data from ultraperipheral pPb and PbPb collisions at the LHC at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV and 2.76 TeV respectively.

## Key numbers

- The bNonSat model with damping achieves chi^2/Ndf = 1.108 for the HERA fit (409+34 data points), while bSat with damping achieves chi^2/Ndf = 1.270
- The damping parameter R_shrink is 0.9025 fm for bNonSat and 1.1889 fm for bSat
- The proton thickness parameter B_p is fixed to 4 GeV^{-2}
- The DGLAP starting scale mu_0^2 is 1.1 GeV^2
- The strong coupling satisfies alpha_s(M_Z^2) = 0.1183
- The fit includes data in the range x < 0.01 and 1.5 <= Q^2 <= 50 GeV^2
- For the pPb collisions at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV, the rapidity range |y| < 4 corresponds to 10^{-5} <= xpom <= 10^{-2}
- For PbPb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV, the rapidity range |y| < 3.5 corresponds to 3*10^{-5} <= xpom <= 3*10^{-2}
- The real part correction factor (1+beta^2) ranges from 1.17 to 1.53, and the skewedness correction R_g ranges from 1.5 to 2.0

## Context

This work extends the color dipole model approach of Kowalski (2006), Rezaeian (2012), and Mantysaari (2018) by adding a damping mechanism for large dipole radii inspired by Flensburg (2008). It addresses the problem that previous implementations allowed unphysical dipole radii beyond the confinement scale. The study uses ultraperipheral collisions at the LHC as a substitute for an electron-ion collider (EIC) to test saturation effects, finding that PbPb data show strong saturation signals while pPb data do not discriminate between models. The results enable future comparisons with EIC data and motivate including subnucleonic gluon fluctuations following Mantysaari and Schenke (2017) to improve descriptions of incoherent cross-sections.
