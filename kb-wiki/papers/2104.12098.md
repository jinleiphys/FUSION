# Symmetry energy extracted from the S$π$RIT pion data in Sn+Sn systems

> With the improved particular Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport model (impIBUU) including the nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations, the ratios and yields of $π^{-}$ and $π^{+}$ in Sn+Sn systems with different asymmetries at 270 MeV/nucleon are studied. It is found that the yields of $π^{-}$ and $π^{+}$ and their ratios in Sn+Sn systems characterized by different neutron to proton ratios obtained from the very recent S$π$RIT pion data, are quite well described by the model, especially when a soft symmetry energy with $L(ρ_{0})$ = 66.75$\pm$24.75 MeV is used. The calculations also clearly demonstrate that the high-momentum tail of the nucleon initialization in momentum space strongly affects the yields and ratios of pion production in Sn+Sn systems with different asymmetries near or below threshold. In addition, given many insoluble theoretical uncertainties in transport models, multi-system experimental measurements with various N/Z asymmetries are proposed to extract the symmetry energy less model-dependently by using heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

A soft symmetry energy with slope parameter \(L(\rho_{0}) = 66.75 \pm 24.75\) MeV is favored by the SπRIT pion data, but this extraction depends on including nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations (high-momentum tail) in the transport model; without the HMT, the extracted symmetry energy would differ.

## Method

The improved isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (impIBUU) transport model was used, which includes nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations in the initial momentum distributions (a high-momentum tail up to 1.75 times the local Fermi momentum), an isospin- and momentum-dependent single-nucleon potential (MDI) with variable symmetry energy stiffness parameter \(x\), and in-medium baryon-baryon cross sections scaled by the square of the effective-to-free reduced mass ratio. Pion production was modeled via the \(\Delta\) resonance mechanism, and the yields and ratios of \(\pi^{-}\) and \(\pi^{+}\) were calculated for three Sn+Sn systems (\(^{132}\mathrm{Sn}+^{124}\mathrm{Sn}\), \(^{112}\mathrm{Sn}+^{124}\mathrm{Sn}\), \(^{108}\mathrm{Sn}+^{112}\mathrm{Sn}\)) at 270 MeV/nucleon with impact parameter \(b = 3\) fm, then compared to the SπRIT experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Slope parameter of the symmetry energy: \(L(\rho_{0}) = 66.75 \pm 24.75\) MeV, extracted from fitting tri-system Sn+Sn pion data.
- Symmetry energy at saturation density: \(E_{\mathrm{sym}}(\rho_{0}) = 34.57\) MeV (model input).
- Saturation density: \(\rho_{0} = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\); binding energy: \(E_{0} = -16\) MeV; incompressibility: \(K_{0} = 230\) MeV (model inputs).
- High-momentum tail extends to \(\lambda k_{F_{n,p}}(r) = 1.75 \times k_{F_{n,p}}(r)\); about 20% of nucleons are in the HMT for symmetric nuclei, decreasing as \(20\%(1-\delta^{2})\) with asymmetry \(\delta\).
- Effects of the HMT on charged pion yields can be as high as 50%.
- Symmetry energy stiffness parameter \(x = 1\) corresponds to \(L(\rho_{0}) = 42\) MeV; \(x = 0\) corresponds to \(L(\rho_{0}) = 91.5\) MeV; \(x = -1\) corresponds to \(L(\rho_{0}) = 141.5\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous transport-model analyses of the SπRIT pion data (SπRIT collaboration, 2021) by incorporating nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations, which were absent in the models used in that experimental paper. The extracted soft symmetry energy with \(L(\rho_{0}) = 66.75 \pm 24.75\) MeV is consistent with recent constraints combining astrophysical data, PREX-II, and chiral effective field theory (Reed et al., 2021). The author proposes that multi-system measurements with varying N/Z asymmetries (e.g., N/Z = 1, 1.2, 1.4, 1.6) can resolve the conflicting conclusions from previous single-system studies such as the FOPI Au+Au data (FOPI collaboration, 2007), which different transport models interpreted differently.
