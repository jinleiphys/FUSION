# Microscopic derivation of transition-state theory for complex quantum systems

> The decay of quantum complex systems through a potential barrier is often described with transition-state theory, also known as RRKM theory in chemistry. Here we derive the basic formula for transition-state theory based on a generic Hamiltonian as might be constructed in a configuration-interaction basis. Two reservoirs of random Hamiltonians from Gaussian orthogonal ensembles are coupled to intermediate states representing the transition states at a barrier. Under the condition that the decay of the reservoirs to open channels is large, an analytic formula for reaction rates is derived. The transition states act as independent Breit-Wigner resonances which contribute additively to the total transition probability, as is well known for electronic conductance through resonant tunneling states. It is also found that the transition probability is independent of the decay properties of the states in the second reservoir over a wide range of decay widths.

# Microscopic derivation of transition-state theory for complex quantum systems

## Key claim

The authors derive transition-state (RRKM) theory from a microscopic Hamiltonian using random-matrix reservoirs, showing that the average transmission coefficient factorizes into a product of formation and decay probabilities only in the limit where the dimensionless decay widths of both reservoirs are large compared to the number of states (T_k/N_k >> 1). The derivation reveals that the transition states contribute as independent Breit-Wigner resonances and that the transmission probability is insensitive to the decay width of the second reservoir over a wide parameter range.

## Method

The authors construct a block-tridiagonal Hamiltonian coupling two Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) random-matrix reservoirs (H_1 and H_3) to a bridge Hamiltonian H_2 representing transition states at a barrier. They compute the ensemble-averaged transmission coefficient T_in,3 from an entrance channel in the first reservoir to all decay channels in the third reservoir using the Green's function formalism (Eq. 2), then evaluate the averages analytically in the limit T_k/N_k >> 1. Numerical Monte Carlo sampling with 10000 GOE realizations tests the accuracy of the analytic reduction for finite parameter values.

## Key numbers

- The average level density of each GOE reservoir at E=0 is rho_k = N_k^(1/2)/(pi v_k), where N_k is the matrix dimension and v_k the GOE width parameter.
- For N_2=1 transition state with parameters (v_k, v_k2, gamma_k, N_k) = (0.1, 0.1, 0.1, 100) and gamma_in = 0.01, the analytic reduction gives T = 0.00079 while the full trace formula gives 0.0010 (a ~20% discrepancy at T_k/N_k = 20).
- For N_2=2 transition states at the same energy, the analytic reduction gives T = 0.00148 and the full trace gives 0.0020, showing approximate scaling with the number of transition states.
- Varying gamma_3 from 0.05 to 0.4 changes the transmission probability only from 0.00074 to 0.00085, confirming insensitivity to the second reservoir decay width.
- The ratio R of analytic reduction to full trace ranges from 2.75 at T_k/N_k = 1 to 1.25 at T_k/N_k = 20, showing slow convergence as the fluctuation amplitude dies off only as N_k^(-1/2).

## Context

This work generalizes a model by Weidenmuller (2022) that assumed a single barrier configuration, extending it to multiple transition states in the bridge Hamiltonian. The derivation provides a microscopic foundation for transition-state theory in configuration-interaction frameworks, complementing earlier statistical approaches by Hanggi (1990) and Weiss (2012). The result that transition states act as independent Breit-Wigner resonances connects to the well-known formula for electronic conductance through resonant tunneling states (Alhassid, 2000; Bertsch, 2014), and the insensitivity property extends findings by Bertsch and Hagino (2021, 2023). The work clarifies necessary conditions for transition-state theory to hold in complex quantum systems near barrier tops.
