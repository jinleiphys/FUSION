# Transition electromagnetic form factor in the Minkowski space Bethe-Salpeter approach

> Using the solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space for bound and scattering states found in previous works, we calculate the transition electromagnetic form factor describing the electro-disintegration of a bound system.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the transition electromagnetic form factor for electro-disintegration of a bound system using Bethe-Salpeter solutions in Minkowski space, but only for the case of spinless particles and with the restriction that the plane wave contribution is not considered in this presentation.

## Method

The transition current is expressed as a 4D integral involving the initial bound state and final scattering state vertex functions obtained from previous Bethe-Salpeter equation solutions. The singularities in the integral are treated by decomposing each propagator into principal value integrals and delta-function contributions, then performing subtractions with an auxiliary function that has the same pole singularities. The calculation is carried out in a reference frame where the initial and final energies are equal, and the form factor is extracted from the zero component of the conserved current constructed from the non-conserved one-body current.

## Key numbers

- Constituent mass m = 1, exchange boson mass μ = 0.5, coupling constant α = 1.437
- Bound state mass M_i = 1.99
- For p_s = 0.1, final state mass M_f = 2.01
- For p_s = 0.5, final state mass M_f = 2.236

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous efforts to solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space for bound and scattering states (Carbonell and Karmanov, 2012; 2013) and to compute elastic electromagnetic form factors (Carbonell, Karmanov, and Mangin-Brinet, 2013). It contrasts with Euclidean space approaches, which cannot be used for transition form factors because the Wick rotation cannot be performed in the integral expressions. The method is currently the only one available for calculating this quantity in the absence of scattering state solutions in the Nakanishi representation form, which is still under development (Frederico, Salme, and Viviani, 2012).
