# Lithium isotopes within the ab intio no-core full configuration approach

> We perform no-core full configuration calculations for the Lithium isotopes, 6Li, 7Li, and 8Li with the realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction JISP16. We obtain a set of observables, such as spectra, radii, multipole moments, transition rates, etc., and compare with experiment where available. We also present one-body density distributions for selected states. Convergence properties of these density distributions shed light on the convergence properties of one-body observables. We obtain underbinding by 0.5 MeV, 0.7 MeV, and 1.0 MeV for 6Li, 7Li, 8Li respectively. Magnetic moments are well-converged and agree with experiment to within 20%.

## Key claim

The no-core full configuration (NCFC) calculations for 6Li, 7Li, and 8Li using the JISP16 nucleon-nucleon interaction produce underbinding by 0.5 MeV, 0.7 MeV, and 1.0 MeV respectively, and magnetic moments agree with experiment to within 20%, but these results are obtained without explicit three-body forces and without meson-exchange current corrections, which are known to cause significant deviations for observables such as magnetic moments.

## Method

The authors perform no-core full configuration (NCFC) calculations by diagonalizing the many-body Hamiltonian in a harmonic oscillator basis with truncation parameter Nmax, using the realistic JISP16 nucleon-nucleon interaction. They compute ground-state energies, excitation spectra, radii, multipole moments, transition probabilities, and one-body density distributions for 6Li, 7Li, and 8Li, with basis spaces up to about 1.2 billion dimensions. For binding energies they apply an exponential extrapolation to the infinite basis limit, while for long-range observables they assess convergence by examining independence from the basis parameters Nmax and harmonic oscillator energy.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state underbinding: 0.5 MeV for 6Li, 0.7 MeV for 7Li, and 1.0 MeV for 8Li
- Largest basis space dimensions: 805,583,856 for 6Li at Nmax=16; 1,244,131,981 for 7Li at Nmax=14; 1,222,330,036 for 8Li at Nmax=12
- 6Li ground-state quadrupole moment: -0.077(5) e fm2 compared to experimental -0.082(2) e fm2
- 6Li ground-state magnetic moment: 0.839(2) micro_N compared to experimental 0.822 micro_N
- 6Li ground-state point-proton RMS radius: 2.3 fm compared to experimental 2.45(5) fm
- 6Li excitation energies: 3+ state at 2.56(2) MeV (experiment 2.186(2) MeV), 0+ state at 3.68(6) MeV (experiment 3.56(1) MeV), 2+ state at 4.5(3) MeV (experiment 4.312(22) MeV)
- 6Li B(M1) transition from 0+ to ground state: 14.2(1) micro_N2 compared to experimental 15.43(32) micro_N2

## Context

This work extends the NCFC method previously applied to lighter nuclei (Maris 2008, Bogner 2007) to the lithium isotopes 6Li, 7Li, and 8Li, using the JISP16 interaction developed by Shirokov (2005) which was designed to describe light nuclei without explicit three-body forces. The results are compared with alternative realistic interactions such as Argonne V18 (Pieper 2004), CD-Bonn, and INOY, and with experimental data from Tilley (2002) and Nortershauser (2011). The paper introduces a technique for unfolding center-of-mass motion from one-body density distributions to obtain translationally-invariant densities, which enables better assessment of convergence for long-range observables and may help future studies of clustering phenomena in nuclei.
