# Two-loop HTL pressure at finite temperature and chemical potential

> We calculate the two-loop pressure of a plasma of quarks and gluons at finite temperature and chemical potential using the hard thermal loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) reorganization of finite temperature/density quantum chromodynamics. The computation utilizes a high temperature expansion through fourth order in the ratio of the chemical potential to temperature. This allows us to reliably access the region of high temperature and small chemical potential. We compare our final result for the two-loop HTLpt pressure at finite temperature and chemical potential with perturbative quantum chromodynamics (QCD) calculations and available lattice QCD results.

## Key claim

The two-loop hard thermal loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) pressure at finite temperature and chemical potential is computed using a high-temperature expansion through fourth order in the ratio of chemical potential to temperature, which restricts the reliable applicability to the region of high temperature and small chemical potential, rather than the full parameter space implied by the abstract.

## Method

The authors performed a two-loop calculation of the thermodynamic potential for a plasma of quarks and gluons using the HTLpt reorganization of finite-temperature and finite-density QCD, expanding all sum-integrals in powers of the Debye mass \(m_D\) and quark mass \(m_q\) (treated as order \(g\)) through nominally order \(g^5\). The computation involved separating contributions into hard-hard and hard-soft momentum regions, evaluating the necessary scalar sum-integrals analytically via dimensional regularization, and fixing the mass parameters \(m_D\) and \(m_q\) variationally by requiring the first derivative of the thermodynamic potential with respect to each mass to vanish.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order one-loop quark free energy contribution is \(-\frac{7\pi^2}{180}T^4\left(1+\frac{120}{7}\hat\mu^2+\frac{240}{7}\hat\mu^4\right)\), where \(\hat\mu = \mu/(2\pi T)\).
* The hard-hard two-loop contribution from fermionic diagrams is \(\frac{5\pi^2}{72}\frac{\alpha_s}{\pi}T^4\left[1 + \frac{72}{5}\hat\mu^2 + \frac{144}{5}\hat\mu^4\right]\).
* The hard-soft two-loop contribution is \(-\frac{1}{6}\alpha_s m_D T^3 (1+12\hat\mu^2) - \frac{\alpha_s}{2\pi^2} m_q^2 m_D T\) plus terms of order \(\alpha_s m_D^3 T\).
* The Debye mass squared in the high-temperature limit is \(m_D^2 = \frac{g^2}{3}\left[\left(N_c + \frac{N_f}{2}\right)T^2 + \frac{3N_f}{2\pi^2}\mu^2\right]\).
* The quark mass squared in the high-temperature limit is \(m_q^2 = \frac{g^2}{4}\frac{N_c^2-1}{4N_c}\left(T^2+\frac{\mu^2}{\pi^2}\right)\).

## Context

This work extends the HTLpt framework, previously applied at finite temperature and zero chemical potential by Andersen et al. (2002, 2003, 2004), to include finite chemical potential, building on leading-order HTLpt calculations at finite \(\mu\) by Munshi et al. (2011) and Su (2011). The result enables comparison with perturbative QCD calculations up to order \(g^6\ln(1/g)\) by Vuorinen (2003) and with lattice QCD results at small chemical potential, providing a systematically improvable resummation method for the equation of state relevant to heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC, LHC, and FAIR.
