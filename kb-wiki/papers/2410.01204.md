# p--$ϕ$ femtoscopic correlation analysis using a dynamical model

> We analyse the p--$ϕ$ correlation functions in high-multiplicity proton+proton collisions at the LHC using a dynamical model, DCCI2, and discuss the effects of collision dynamics on the p--$ϕ$ femtoscopic study. Collision dynamics, such as collective expansion and hadronic rescatterings, leads to the relative momentum-dependent non-Gaussian source functions. This results in deviations in the correlation functions compared to those using the Gaussian source function adopted in existing studies. Our analysis shows the importance of using the source functions that reflect more realistic collision dynamics for future precision analysis of hadron interactions via femtoscopy.

# p--φ femtoscopic correlation analysis using a dynamical model

## Key claim
Using a dynamical model (DCCI2) for the source function in p--φ femtoscopy produces correlation functions that deviate from those obtained with the simple Gaussian source function assumed in previous studies, and the analysis supports the existence of a p--φ bound state in the ²S₁/₂ channel with a binding energy around 10 to 70 MeV, contingent on the adjustable parameter β being in the range 7 to 8.

## Method
The authors used the DCCI2 dynamical core-corona initialisation model to generate p--φ source functions from 2.3×10⁶ minimum bias proton+proton collision events at √s = 13 TeV, selecting the top 0.17% of events by charged hadron multiplicity. They solved the Schrödinger equation with a lattice QCD potential for the ⁴S₃/₂ channel and a parameterised potential (with adjustable β) for the ²S₁/₂ channel, then computed correlation functions via the Koonin-Pratt formula and compared them to ALICE experimental data.

## Key numbers
- 2.3×10⁶ minimum bias DCCI2 simulation events for proton+proton collisions at √s = 13 TeV were generated, yielding 3,702 events after event selection (top 0.17% multiplicity and transverse sphericity cut 0.7 < S_T < 1.0)
- The Gaussian source function adopted in previous studies had source size r₀ = 1.08 fm
- The Pearson correlation coefficient between relative momentum q and source size r from DCCI2 was approximately ρ(q,r) ≈ 0.1
- The source size in the region q ≲ 20 MeV was significantly smaller than in other regions
- The adjustable parameter β in the ²S₁/₂ potential was constrained to β ≈ 7–8 to reproduce the ALICE data
- The resulting binding energy for the p--φ bound state in the ²S₁/₂ channel was around 10 to 70 MeV

## Context
This work extends femtoscopic studies of the p--φ interaction, following the ALICE measurement (2021) of the p--φ correlation function in high-multiplicity proton+proton collisions and the reanalysis by Chizzali et al. (2022) that disentangled spin components using lattice QCD potentials from Lyu et al. (2022). Previous studies assumed simple Gaussian source functions, whereas this work demonstrates that collision dynamics (collective expansion and hadronic rescatterings) produce non-Gaussian, relative-momentum-dependent source functions that affect the extracted interaction parameters. This enables more precise future determinations of hadron interactions via femtoscopy using realistic dynamical models.
