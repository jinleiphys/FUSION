# Global analysis for determining fragmentation functions and their uncertainties in light hadrons

> Fragmentation functions are determined for the pion, kaon, and proton by analyzing charged-hadron production data in electron-positron annihilation. It is important that uncertainties of the determined fragmentation functions are estimated in this analysis. Analysis results indicate that gluon and light-quark functions have large uncertainties especially at small Q^2. We find that next-to-leading-order (NLO) uncertainties are significantly reduced in comparison with leading-order (LO) ones in the pion and kaon. The fragmentation functions are very different in various analysis groups. However, all the recent functions are roughly within the estimated uncertainties, which indicates that they are consistent with each other. We provide a code for calculating the fragmentation functions and their uncertainties at a given kinematical point of z and Q^2 by a user.

## Key claim

The authors determine fragmentation functions for pions, kaons, and protons with quantified uncertainties for the first time, finding that while gluon and light-quark functions have large uncertainties especially at small Q², the next-to-leading-order (NLO) uncertainties are significantly reduced compared to leading-order (LO) ones for pions and kaons. The paper hedges that all recent fragmentation functions from different groups are roughly within the estimated uncertainty bands, indicating consistency rather than a uniquely correct set.

## Method

The authors perform a global χ² analysis of charged-hadron production data from electron-positron annihilation, including accurate SLD data from 2004 not used in prior analyses. Fragmentation functions are parametrized at a fixed scale Q₀² = 1 GeV² using the form D_i^h(z, Q₀²) = N_i^h z^{α_i^h} (1-z)^{β_i^h}, with parameters determined by minimizing χ². Uncertainties are estimated using the Hessian method with Δχ² chosen to correspond to the one-sigma range in multiparameter space, and the analysis is performed at both leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) in α_s.

## Key numbers

- The analysis uses data spanning four orders of magnitude in the fragmentation function F^h(z, Q²) for charged pions, as shown in the comparison plot.
- The initial scale for parametrization is Q₀² = 1 GeV², with additional starting scales at m_c² and m_b², and evolution up to Q² = M_Z² (approximately 91.2 GeV²).
- Comparisons with other fragmentation functions (KKP, AKK, Kretzer) are shown at Q² = 2, 10, and 100 GeV² for pions, kaons, and protons.
- NLO uncertainties are significantly smaller than LO uncertainties for pions and kaons, though no specific numerical reduction factor is given in the text.

## Context

This work extends the error-analysis techniques previously applied to unpolarized parton distribution functions (PDFs), polarized PDFs, and nuclear PDFs to the case of fragmentation functions. It improves upon prior fragmentation function determinations by Kniehl, Kramer, and Potter (KKP), Kretzer, and Albino, Kniehl, and Kramer (AKK) by including accurate SLD data from 2004 and by providing uncertainty estimates. The results enable more reliable use of fragmentation functions in hadron-production measurements at small p_T in lepton-proton, proton-proton, and heavy-ion experiments such as HERMES, RHIC-Spin, and RHIC heavy-ion experiments, where the large uncertainties at small Q² must be taken into account.
