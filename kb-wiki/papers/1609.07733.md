# Model-independent determination of the compositeness of near-threshold quasibound states

> We study the compositeness of near-threshold states to clarify the internal structure of exotic hadron candidates. Within the framework of effective field theory, we extend the Weinberg's weak-binding relation to include the nearby CDD (Castillejo-Dalitz-Dyson) pole contribution with the help of the Pade approximant. Finally, using the extended relation, we conclude that the CDD pole contribution to the Lambda(1405) baryon in the Kbar N amplitude is negligible.

# Model-independent determination of the compositeness of near-threshold quasibound states

## Key claim
The authors extend Weinberg's weak-binding relation to include nearby CDD pole contributions using a Padé approximant, and conclude that for the Λ(1405) baryon the CDD pole contribution to the \(\bar{K}N\) amplitude is negligible, meaning the effective range expansion converges well for this state.

## Method
The authors derive the weak-binding relation within nonrelativistic effective field theory with contact interactions, separating expansions in \(R_{\mathrm{typ}}/R\) from those in \(R_{\mathrm{eff}}/R\). They improve the relation by including the fourth-order term in the effective range expansion and by employing a Padé approximant for \(p\cot\delta\) to account for nearby CDD poles. The compositeness of the Λ(1405) is then calculated using three different versions of the weak-binding relation with input from the chiral SU(3) dynamics amplitude.

## Key numbers
- Scattering length for \(\bar{K}N\): \(a_0 = 1.39 - i0.85\ \mathrm{fm}\)
- Effective range for \(\bar{K}N\): \(r_e = 0.24 - i0.05\ \mathrm{fm}\)
- Eigenenergy of the higher pole state of Λ(1405): \(E_h = -10 - i26\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Compositeness \(X_{\bar{K}N}\) calculated with the original weak-binding relation: \(1.3 + i0.1\)
- Compositeness \(X_{\bar{K}N}\) calculated with the fourth-order ERE extension: \(1.3 + i0.2\)
- Compositeness \(X_{\bar{K}N}\) calculated with the Padé approximant extension: \(1.4 + i0.2\)

## Context
This work extends the weak-binding relation originally formulated by Weinberg (1965) for the deuteron, which relates the scattering length and effective range to the compositeness of shallow bound states. The authors build on their own previous generalization for unstable states (Kamiya 2015, Kamiya 2016) and address the limitation noted by Weinberg himself regarding CDD poles near threshold. The extended relations enable model-independent determination of compositeness for near-threshold states even when the effective range expansion convergence is limited by nearby CDD poles, as discussed by Baru (2010) and Guo (2016). The application to Λ(1405) confirms that the \(\bar{K}N\) composite component dominates this state, consistent with chiral SU(3) dynamics studies by Ikeda (2011, 2012).
