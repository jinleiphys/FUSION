# Are back-to-back particle--antiparticle correlations observable in high energy nuclear collisions?

> Analytical formulae are presented which provide quantitative estimates for the suppression of the anticipated back-to-back particle--antiparticle correlations in high energy nuclear collisions due to the finite duration of the transition dynamics. They show that it is unlikely to observ the effect.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the back-to-back particle–antiparticle correlation effect predicted by Asakawa and Csörgő (1996) is unlikely to be observable in high-energy nuclear collisions because a smooth, realistic transition from the in-medium to the vacuum state leads to an exponential suppression of the effect, rather than the moderate power-law suppression found in earlier work.

## Method

The author solves the time-dependent Klein-Gordon equation for a smoothly time-dependent dispersion relation, parameterized by a transition profile \(F(t)\) that falls from 1 to 0 over a characteristic duration \(\tau\). Two approximate analytical methods are used: the single-reflection approximation, which sums coherent partial reflections from a continuous change in \(\Omega(t)\), and the complex WKB (CWKB) method, which computes the reflected amplitude from the nearest complex turning point where \(\Omega^2(t)=0\). An exact closed-form solution is also given for the Fermi-function transition profile.

## Key numbers

- For charged pion pairs (\(\pi^{+}\pi^{-}\)) with \(\Omega_{\vec k} \approx 300\) MeV, the correlation \(C_2(\vec{k},-\vec{k})-1\) falls below the \(10^{-5}\) level for transition times \(\tau\) beyond 3 fm/\(c\) at a freeze-out temperature of \(T \approx 140\) MeV.
- The suppression factor for the antiparticle amplitude in the single-reflection approximation is \(|g(2\bar\omega)|^2 \propto e^{-4\alpha\bar\omega\tau}\) for large \(\bar\omega\tau\), with \(\alpha = 2/\pi, 1, \pi/2\) for the three analytic profile cases (a), (b), and (c) respectively.
- The exact Fermi-function result gives \({\cal A}^2_{\rm Fermi} = \left|\frac{\sinh(\pi(\Omega-\omega)\tau/2)}{\sinh(\pi(\Omega+\omega)\tau/2)}\right|^2\), which reduces to the sudden result when \(\tau \to 0\).
- The CWKB estimate for the Fermi-function case yields \(|{\cal A}^{\mbox{\tiny CWKB}}_{\rm Fermi}|^2 = \exp[-2\pi\; {\rm Min}(\Omega,\omega)\ \tau]\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier sudden-transition treatment of Asakawa and Csörgő (1996) and the follow-up by Asakawa, Csörgő, and Gyulassy (1998), which used a discontinuous transition profile that gave only a moderate \((2\omega\tau)^{-2}\) suppression. By considering smooth profiles such as the Fermi function, the author shows that the suppression is exponential, making the effect unmeasurable for realistic expansion timescales (full-width-half-maximum values beyond 4 fm/\(c\)). The result implies that the back-to-back correlation effect is an artifact of the sudden limit and that stochastic processes, which add incoherently, should be included in transport codes instead.
