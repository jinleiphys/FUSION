# Nucleon tensor charges and electric dipole moments

> A symmetry-preserving Dyson-Schwinger equation treatment of a vector-vector contact interaction is used to compute dressed-quark-core contributions to the nucleon $σ$-term and tensor charges. The latter enable one to directly determine the effect of dressed-quark electric dipole moments (EDMs) on neutron and proton EDMs. The presence of strong scalar and axial-vector diquark correlations within ground-state baryons is a prediction of this approach. These correlations are active participants in all scattering events and thereby modify the contribution of the singly-represented valence-quark relative to that of the doubly-represented quark. Regarding the proton $σ$-term and that part of the proton mass which owes to explicit chiral symmetry breaking, with a realistic $d$-$u$ mass splitting the singly-represented $d$-quark contributes 37% more than the doubly-represented $u$-quark; and in connection with the proton's tensor charges, $δ_T u$, $δ_T d$, the ratio $δ_T d/δ_T u$ is 18% larger than anticipated from simple quark models. Of particular note, the size of $δ_T u$ is a sensitive measure of the strength of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking; and $δ_T d$ measures the amount of axial-vector diquark correlation within the proton, vanishing if such correlations are absent.

## Key claim

A symmetry-preserving Dyson-Schwinger equation treatment of a vector-vector contact interaction predicts that the ratio of tensor charges δ_T d/δ_T u is 18% larger than anticipated from simple quark models, and that the d-quark contribution to the proton σ-term is 37% greater than that from the u-quark when a realistic d-u mass splitting is included, but these results are obtained within a rainbow-ladder truncation that may underestimate the physical σ_N by roughly a factor of two.

## Method

The authors solved the Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation for the nucleon using a vector-vector contact interaction, with dressed-quark propagators and diquark Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes obtained from the same interaction kernel. They computed the Q^2=0 values of the scalar and tensor currents for the nucleon's dressed-quark core, using a static approximation for the diquark exchange kernel, and evolved the tensor charges from the model scale ζ_H ≈ M to ζ_2 = 2 GeV using leading-order evolution.

## Key numbers

- σ_N = 21 MeV (computed value of the nucleon σ-term at the model scale)
- σ_N,d / σ_N,u = 0.65 (ratio of d-quark to u-quark contributions to the proton σ-term in the isospin symmetric limit)
- With m_u/m_d = 0.47 ± 0.04, the d-quark contributes 37% more than the u-quark to the part of the proton mass from explicit chiral symmetry breaking
- δ_T u = 0.55(8), δ_T d = -0.11(2) at ζ_2 = 2 GeV (tensor charges evolved to 2 GeV)
- g_T^(0) = 0.44(7), g_T^(1) = 0.66(10) at ζ_2 = 2 GeV (isoscalar and isovector tensor charges)
- δ_T d vanishes entirely if axial-vector diquark correlations are omitted, while δ_T u increases by 11% to 0.61(9) at ζ_2 = 2 GeV
- Reducing α_IR by 20% reduces the dressed-quark mass by 33% (M = 0.368 GeV → M_< = 0.246 GeV) and decreases δ_T u by 20% to 0.44 at ζ_2 = 2 GeV
- The proton EDM is given by d̃_p = 0.69 d̃_u - 0.14 d̃_d and the neutron EDM by d̃_n = -0.14 d̃_u + 0.69 d̃_d

## Context

This work extends Dyson-Schwinger equation studies of nucleon structure using a contact interaction, previously applied to meson and baryon spectra and form factors by Roberts et al. (2011) and others. The computed tensor charges are compared with phenomenological estimates from Anselmino et al. (2013) and lattice-QCD results from Alexandrou et al. (2014) and Gockeler et al. (2005), showing best agreement with the phenomenological values. The results enable direct determination of how dressed-quark electric dipole moments contribute to neutron and proton EDMs, which is relevant for beyond-the-Standard-Model searches in precision neutron experiments and storage-ring measurements of proton EDMs.
