# Theoretical Interpretations of J/Psi Suppression: A Summary

> The strong "anomalous" J/Psi suppression observed recently by the NA50 Collaboration at CERN SPS has attracted considerable attention. Is it the first signature of a long-awaited quark-gluon plasma, or just a peculiar combination of "conventional" effects acting together to produce the puzzling pattern observed experimentally? This talk is an attempt to summarize the theoretical explanations proposed during the last two years.

# Theoretical Interpretations of J/Psi Suppression: A Summary

## Key claim

The strong “anomalous” J/psi suppression observed by NA50 in Pb-Pb collisions at the CERN SPS may be the first signature of quark-gluon plasma formation, but it could also arise from a combination of conventional mechanisms; the paper argues that no existing conventional model can reproduce the sharp discontinuity in the J/psi/Drell-Yan ratio, and only a deconfinement scenario with additional assumptions (such as a critical bubble size in a first-order phase transition) can potentially explain it, though such models remain ad hoc.

## Method

The paper reviews and critiques theoretical explanations proposed between 1995 and 1997 for the J/psi suppression observed by the NA50 Collaboration in Pb-Pb collisions at 158 GeV per nucleon. It examines four classes of models: initial-state interactions (nucleon energy loss, parton-level interactions), final-state interactions with hadronic secondaries (comovers), interactions with partonic secondaries (gluo-effect and semi-hard partons), and deconfinement scenarios (Debye screening, string percolation, vacuum decay). Each model is evaluated against the experimental data, particularly the centrality dependence of the J/psi/Drell-Yan ratio and the apparent discontinuity near transverse energy ET ~ 50 GeV.

## Key numbers

* The pre-resonance absorption cross section extracted from previous J/psi production data is sigma_abs = 7.3 ± 0.6 mb (Glauber model fit).
* The anomalous suppression in Pb-Pb sets in around ET ~ 50 GeV, corresponding to an average impact parameter b ~ 8 ± 1 fm.
* The proper formation time of physical J/psi and psi’ states is estimated to be about 0.3 fm.
* The psi’/J/psi ratio in S-U collisions drops by a factor of two compared to its value in pp and pA reactions.
* The color Casimir factor for the pre-resonance dipole (octet-octet) relative to the usual triplet-antitriplet dipole is 9/4.
* At SPS energies (P = 200 GeV), the longitudinal distance over which final partons propagate inside the nucleus for central rapidity is less than 1 fm (for Q ~ 3 GeV, x ~ 0.15).
* The average density of hadronic secondaries that interact with J/psi increases by only about 10% from S-U to Pb-Pb systems.
* Chi states contribute approximately 40% to J/psi production.

## Context

This paper summarizes the theoretical debate following the NA50 Collaboration’s discovery of anomalous J/psi suppression in Pb-Pb collisions (Ramello, 1997), which extended earlier measurements by NA38 (1987) in pA and light-ion collisions. The pre-resonance absorption model (Kharzeev and Satz, 1995; Kharzeev, Lourenco, Nardi, and Satz, 1996) had successfully described all earlier data, but the Pb-Pb results showed a sharp discontinuity that conventional hadronic comover models (Gavin and Vogt, 1996; Capella and Kaidalov, 1996) could not reproduce. The paper contrasts these with deconfinement-based approaches (Matsui and Satz, 1986; Shuryak, 1997; Kharzeev, Nardi, and Satz, 1997) that invoke critical density thresholds or first-order phase transition dynamics. The work identifies the need for higher-statistics Drell-Yan data and energy/beam-system scans to distinguish between conventional and deconfinement explanations.
