# Solving the two-center nuclear shell-model problem with arbitrarily-orientated deformed potentials

> A general new technique to solve the two-center problem with arbitrarily-orientated deformed realistic potentials is demonstrated, which is based on the powerful potential separable expansion method. As an example, molecular single-particle spectra for $^{12}$C + $^{12}$C $\to$ $^{24}$Mg are calculated using deformed Woods-Saxon potentials. These clearly show that non-axial symmetric configurations play a crucial role in molecular resonances observed in reaction processes for this system at low energy.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates a general technique for solving the two-center nuclear shell-model problem with arbitrarily-orientated deformed potentials, showing that non-axial symmetric configurations play a crucial role in molecular resonances for the \(^{12}\)C + \(^{12}\)C system, but the claim is hedged by the fact that the calculations are illustrative and reaction dynamical calculations for quantitative understanding are still in progress.

## Method

The method uses the potential separable expansion method to solve the two-center problem with deformed Woods-Saxon potentials, including a spin-orbit term and, for protons, a Coulomb potential from a uniformly charged spheroid. The key new aspect is the inclusion of the operator of finite rotations to handle arbitrary orientations of the deformed nuclei's intrinsic symmetry axes relative to the internuclear axis. The formalism reduces the Schrödinger equation to a set of linear equations for amplitudes, with the solvability condition that the determinant vanishes, yielding adiabatic single-particle energies as a function of internuclear distance \(R\).

## Key numbers

- For the \(^{12}\)C + \(^{12}\)C reaction, convergence of single-particle energies required \(n_{max}=3\) and \(l_{max}=4\) basis states.
- The \(^{12}\)C nucleus is treated as oblate with quadrupole deformation \(\beta_2 = -0.5\).
- For non-axial symmetric configurations, neutron levels show a minimum at separations between 4–6 fm, indicating a possible "molecular pocket" in the collective potential energy surface.
- The critical radius for fusion (where the shell structure of \(^{24}\)Mg starts to build up) is \(\lesssim 2\) fm for non-axial symmetric configurations.

## Context

This work extends the two-center shell model (TCSM) beyond the rotationally symmetric potentials used by the Frankfurt school (Maruhn) and later improvements for asymmetric fission (Mirea, Radu). It directly addresses the need for a general TCSM for arbitrarily-orientated deformed nuclei, following only one prior attempt by Nuhn using ellipsoidally deformed Gaussian potentials for \(^{13}\)C + \(^{16}\)O. The method enables microscopic calculations of collective potentials and mass surfaces for molecular reaction dynamics, with applications to heavy and superheavy element formation, breakup effects in fusion, and astrophysical fusion reactions such as \(^{12}\)C + \(^{12}\)C.
