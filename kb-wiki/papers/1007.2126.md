# Singularity structure of the pi N scattering amplitude in a meson-exchange model up to energies W < 2.0 GeV

> Within the previously developed Dubna-Mainz-Taipei meson-exchange model, the singularity structure of the pi N scattering amplitudes has been investigated. For all partial waves up to F waves and c.m. energies up to W = 2 GeV, the T-matrix poles have been calculated by three different techniques: analytic continuation into the complex energy plane, speed-plot and the regularization method. For all 4-star resonances, we find a perfect agreement between the analytic continuation and the regularization method. We also find resonance poles for resonances that are not so well established, but in these cases the pole positions and residues obtained by analytic continuation can substantially differ from the results predicted by the speed-plot and regularization methods.

## Key claim

For all 4-star resonances, the pole positions and residues obtained by analytic continuation into the complex energy plane and the regularization method show perfect agreement, but for less well-established resonances the analytic continuation results can substantially differ from those predicted by the speed-plot and regularization methods.

## Method

The authors used the Dubna-Mainz-Taipei (DMT) meson-exchange model, a field-theoretical coupled-channels model for πN scattering, to calculate T-matrix poles for all partial waves up to F waves and center-of-mass energies up to W = 2.0 GeV. They extracted resonance pole parameters using three different techniques: analytic continuation into the complex energy plane (the most accurate method), the speed-plot technique (based on the derivative dT/dW), and the regularization method (which extends the speed-plot idea to higher derivatives). The DMT model includes πN, ππN, and ηN channels and treats resonances as bare states that acquire width through coupling to these channels.

## Key numbers

- The analysis covers partial waves up to F waves and center-of-mass energies up to W = 2.0 GeV.
- The most important particle thresholds in the energy region below 2 GeV are: ππN at 1178 MeV, πΔ at (1350 − 50i) MeV, ηN at 1486 MeV, and ρN at (1713 − 75i) MeV.
- For a single-channel Breit-Wigner resonance, the maximum time delay is Δt(Mp) = 4/Γp, and the speed at half-maximum satisfies SP(Mp ± ½Γp) = ½ SP(Mp).
- The regularization method uses the relation |rN| = |T(N)(W)|/N! × |μN − W|(N+1) to approximate pole parameters at order N, with the speed plot corresponding to N = 1.

## Context

This work extends the Dubna-Mainz-Taipei (DMT) meson-exchange model (Chen 2007) by systematically comparing three pole-extraction methods for all partial waves up to F waves. The DMT model itself builds on the Taipei-Argonne πN meson-exchange model (Lee 1991, Hung 2001) which described scattering up to 400 MeV pion laboratory energy, and was extended to 2.0 GeV by Chen (2002, 2007). The results enable more reliable extraction of resonance parameters from experimental data and provide a benchmark for comparing pole-extraction techniques, which is relevant for interpreting lattice QCD results (Luscher 1986, Bernard 2008) where the pion mass is still above the physical value and extrapolation using dynamical models is necessary.
