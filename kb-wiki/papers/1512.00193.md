# Expected impact from weak reactions with light nuclei in core-collapse supernova simulations

> We study the role of light nuclear clusters in simulations of core-collapse supernovae. Expressions for the reaction rates are developed for a large selection of charged current absorption and scattering processes with light clusters. Medium modifications are taken into account at the mean-field level. We explore the possible impact on the supernova dynamics and the neutrino signal during the mass accretion phase prior to the possible explosion onset as well as during the subsequent protoneutron star deleptnoization after the explosion onset has been launched.

## Key claim

The inclusion of weak reactions with light nuclear clusters (deuteron, triton, helium-3) in core-collapse supernova simulations has a small and even negligible impact on the supernova dynamics and neutrino signal during the mass accretion phase prior to explosion onset, and only a mild effect on the average antineutrino energy during the later protoneutron star deleptonization phase.

## Method

The authors extended the AGILE-BOLTZTRAN spherically symmetric general relativistic neutrino radiation hydrodynamics code with three-flavor Boltzmann neutrino transport to include a substantially expanded set of charged-current absorption and neutral-current scattering processes on light nuclear clusters (deuteron, triton, helium-3). Reaction rates were derived in the elastic approximation with medium modifications incorporated at the mean-field level via shifts in the vacuum cross sections, using the HS(DD2) nuclear equation of state. Simulations were performed for an 11.2 solar mass progenitor, comparing results with and without the new weak rates.

## Key numbers

- The Gamow-Teller transition strength for the triton to helium-3 ground state transition is B(GT) = 5.87, known from triton decay.
- The vacuum cross section normalization constant for charged-current absorption on triton/helium-3 is sigma_0 = 1.48 x 10^{-43} cm^2 MeV^{-2}.
- The vacuum Q-value for the triton/helium-3 mass difference is Q_0 = 0.529 MeV.
- The protoneutron star deleptonization phase lasts 10-30 seconds.
- The progenitor mass used in simulations is 11.2 solar masses.

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Furusawa (2013) that considered inelastic neutrino absorption on light nuclei using vacuum cross sections without detailed balance or final state Pauli blocking, and by Arcones (2008) that studied weak reactions with light nuclei in the context of neutrino-driven wind nucleosynthesis. The authors find that despite the substantial extension of the reaction set and inclusion of medium modifications, the overall impact remains small, consistent with the low abundances of light clusters relative to free neutrons and protons in the neutrino decoupling region. This work enables future systematic studies of additional processes, particularly with helium-3, and suggests that multidimensional phenomena are unlikely to change the overall picture.
