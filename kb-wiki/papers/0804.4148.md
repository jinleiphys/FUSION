# Equation of state and more from lattice regularized QCD

> We present results from a calculation of the QCD equation of state with two light (up, down) and one heavier (strange) quark mass performed on lattices with three different values of the lattice cut-off. We show that also on the finest lattice analyzed by us observables sensitive to deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration, respectively, vary most rapidly in the same temperature regime.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, in lattice QCD with nearly physical quark masses, observables sensitive to deconfinement (such as the strange quark number susceptibility) and chiral symmetry restoration (such as the subtracted chiral condensate) vary most rapidly in the same temperature interval, even though the transition is a crossover and not a genuine phase transition. The hedge is that this conclusion is drawn from calculations on lattices with three different cut-offs but still shows cut-off dependence, and the finest lattice results (Nτ=8) are preliminary.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations with (2+1)-flavor staggered fermions using two improved discretization schemes (asqtad and p4fat3 actions) on lattices with temporal extents Nτ = 4, 6, and 8, corresponding to different lattice cut-offs. They computed the trace anomaly (ε-3p)/T⁴, from which pressure and energy density were derived via integration, and also calculated the strange quark number susceptibility and the subtracted chiral condensate Δl,s(T). The quark masses were set to physical strange quark mass and light quark masses corresponding to a pion mass of about 220 MeV.

## Key numbers

* The peak of the trace anomaly (ε-3p)/T⁴ is located at T ≈ 200 MeV.
* The ratio of pressure to energy density, p/ε, deviates significantly from the conformal ideal gas value of 1/3 for energy densities ε ≲ 10 GeV/fm³.
* The velocity of sound squared, c_s², reaches a value of about 0.1 in the transition region at energy densities ε ≈ 1 GeV/fm³.
* The temperature interval where deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration observables vary most rapidly is 185 MeV ≤ T ≤ 195 MeV.
* The Sommer scale parameter used to set the temperature scale was fixed to r₀ = 0.469 fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice QCD equation-of-state calculations by the RBC-Bielefeld and MILC collaborations (Aoki et al., MILC Collaboration, RBC-Bielefeld Collaboration) from coarser lattices (Nτ=4 and 6) to finer lattices (Nτ=8) using both asqtad and p4fat3 actions. It addresses the question raised by Aoki et al. (2006) of whether deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration occur at different temperatures in the crossover regime. The results enable improved input for hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and point toward the need for continuum extrapolations with physical quark masses.
