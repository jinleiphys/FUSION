# Fragmentation with a Cut on Thrust: Predictions for B-factories

> When high-energy single-hadron production takes place inside an identified jet, there are important correlations between the fragmentation and phase-space cuts. For example, when one-hadron yields are measured in on-resonance B-factory data, a cut on the thrust event shape T is required to remove the large b-quark contribution. This leads to a dijet final state restriction for the light-quark fragmentation process. Here we complete our analysis of unpolarized fragmentation of (light) quarks and gluons to a light hadron h with energy fraction z in e+ e- -> dijet + h at the center-of-mass energy Q=10.58 GeV. In addition to the next-to-next-to-leading order resummation of logarithms of 1-T, we include the next-to-leading order (NLO) nonsingular O(1-T) contribution to the cross section, the resummation of threshold logarithms of 1-z, and the leading nonperturbative contribution to the soft function. Our results for the correlations between fragmentation and the thrust cut are presented in a way that can be directly tested against B-factory data. These correlations are also observed in Pythia, but are surprisingly smaller at NLO.

## Key claim

The paper provides a resummed perturbative QCD calculation of the correlations between a thrust cut and the energy fraction of a detected hadron in electron-positron annihilation at B-factory energies, showing that these correlations are sizable and important for data analysis, but the abstract's claim of consistency with Pythia is qualified by the observation that the effect is "surprisingly smaller at NLO" than in the resummed calculation.

## Method

The authors use soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) to derive a factorization theorem for the cross section of e+e- → dijet + hadron with a cut on thrust T, resumming logarithms of 1-T to next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) accuracy and threshold logarithms of 1-z. They include the NLO nonsingular O(1-T) contribution to the cross section, the leading nonperturbative correction to the soft function as a shift in the thrust distribution, and evaluate the cross section at center-of-mass energy Q=10.58 GeV using the HKNS fragmentation functions as input.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy: Q = 10.58 GeV
- A thrust cut of τ = 1-T < 0.2 removes 98% of the B data (from reference to Seidl (2008))
- Nonperturbative shift parameter: Ω₁ = 0.252 GeV (from Abbate (2010))
- Strong coupling: αs(mZ) = 0.125 for HKNS FF input, αs(mZ) = 0.118 for moment-space analysis
- Nonperturbative corrections to the soft function become up to about 15% for τcut = 0.15
- Moments are provided for N = 3,4,...,8 and τcut = 0.15,0.16,...,0.2

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous framework for fragmentation inside identified jets (Procura (2009), Jain (2011), Procura (2011)) by including NLO nonsingular contributions, threshold resummation, and nonperturbative soft function corrections. It enables quantitative tests against on-resonance Belle data, where a thrust cut is needed to remove the b-quark background, and provides the theoretical tools for extracting light-quark fragmentation functions from such measurements. The results are compared with the Pythia event generator (Sjostrand (2006,2007)) and with the HKNS fragmentation function parameterization (Hirai (2007)).
