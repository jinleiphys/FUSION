# Nuclear expansion with excitation

> The expansion of an isolated hot spherical nucleus with excitation energy and its caloric curve are studied in a thermodynamic model with the SkM* force as the nuclear effective two-body interaction. The calculated results are shown to compare well with the recent experimental data from energetic nuclear collisions. The fluctuations in temperature and density are also studied. They are seen to build up very rapidly beyond an excitation energy of 9 MeV/u. Volume-conserving quadrupole deformation in addition to expansion indicates, however, nuclear disassembly above an excitation energy of 4 MeV/u

## Key claim

The paper claims that the expansion of an isolated hot nucleus with excitation energy, calculated in a thermodynamic model with the SkM* force, yields break-up densities and caloric curves that compare well with experimental data, but only when a microcanonical (isolated) treatment is used rather than a canonical (heat bath) one; the canonical treatment terminates at a limiting temperature and cannot reproduce the observed densities at higher excitations.

## Method

The authors use a finite-temperature Thomas-Fermi framework with the SkM* Skyrme effective interaction to prepare a hot spherical nucleus (¹⁵⁰Sm) in a heat bath, then detach it and allow self-similar expansion at constant total excitation energy until the entropy is maximized. The equilibrium configuration at each excitation energy is found by scanning over temperature and the scaling parameter λ, with the entropy computed from the single-particle level density including both the k-mass and a phenomenological ω-mass. Volume-conserving quadrupole deformation is also explored as a proxy for fragmentation, with the excess surface and Coulomb energies calculated in a sharp-surface approximation.

## Key numbers

- The central density ρ_c/ρ_0 for the isolated expanded nucleus at equilibrium drops to approximately 0.3–0.4 ρ_0 at an excitation energy of ~8 MeV/u, consistent with experimental data from caloric curve analysis (Natowitz et al.).
- The canonical (heat bath) calculations terminate at an excitation energy of ~5.5 MeV/u, corresponding to a limiting temperature T ≃ 8.5 MeV for ¹⁵⁰Sm.
- Fluctuations in temperature and density build up very rapidly beyond an excitation energy of ~9 MeV/u, indicating instability against prompt multifragmentation.
- Volume-conserving quadrupole deformation becomes favorable (entropy gain over spherical shape) above an excitation energy of ~4 MeV/u, signaling a precursor to fragmentation.
- The highest temperature encountered in the microcanonical calculations is ~8 MeV, where the ω-mass factor m_ω/m is typically ~1.02 at the surface region.

## Context

This work extends the schematic calculation of Sobotka et al. (2004) by using a microscopic Skyrme interaction (SkM*) instead of ad hoc parameters, and it provides a theoretical framework for interpreting experimental break-up densities extracted from caloric curve data (Natowitz et al., 2002) and Coulomb barrier systematics (Braquet et al., Viola et al.). The results enable a more quantitative understanding of the nuclear equation of state at moderate excitations and the onset of multifragmentation, and they suggest that the plateau in the experimental caloric curve arises naturally from nuclear expansion rather than requiring a phase-coexistence interpretation.
