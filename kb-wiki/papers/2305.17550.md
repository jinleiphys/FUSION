# Signatures for shape coexistence and shape/phase transitions in even-even nuclei

> Systematics of B(E2) transition rates connecting the first excited 0+ state to the first excited 2+ state of the ground state band in even-even nuclei indicates that shape coexistence of the ground state band and the first excited K=0 band should be expected in nuclei lying within the stripes of nucleon numbers 7-8, 17-20, 34-40, 59-70, 96-112 predicted by the dual shell mechanism of the proxy-SU(3) model, avoiding their junctions, within which high deformation is expected. Systematics of the excitation energies of the first excited 0+ states in even-even nuclei show that shape coexistence due to proton-induced neutron particle-hole excitations is related to a first-order shape/phase transition from spherical to deformed shapes, while shape coexistence due to neutron-induced proton particle-hole excitations is observed along major proton shell closures.

## Key claim

Shape coexistence of the ground state band with a nearby excited K=0 band is expected only in nuclei that satisfy three conditions: they lie within specific stripes of nucleon numbers (7-8, 17-20, 34-40, 59-70, 96-112) predicted by the proxy-SU(3) dual shell mechanism, they fall outside the P≈5 contours that mark the junctions of these stripes, and they exhibit R4/2 < 3.05, R0/2 < 5.7, and B02 > 0.1. The abstract presents the stripes as a necessary condition but glosses over the additional sufficient conditions involving the P-factor contours and the specific numerical thresholds.

## Method

The authors compiled all even-even nuclei beyond Z=18, N=18 with experimentally known B(E2; 0₂⁺→2₁⁺) transition rates and K=0 bands from the ENSDF database, computing the ratios R4/2 = E(4₁⁺)/E(2₁⁺), R0/2 = E(0₂⁺)/E(2₁⁺), and B02 = B(E2; 0₂⁺→2₁⁺)/B(E2; 2₁⁺→0₁⁺). They plotted B02 versus R0/2 and compared the experimental systematics to parameter-independent predictions of the Bohr Hamiltonian (X(5), X(5)-β²ⁿ, and confined β-soft solutions) and to IBM calculations using the extended consistent Q formalism with large boson numbers. The nuclei were placed on the nuclear chart and compared to the proxy-SU(3) stripes and P≈5 contours.

## Key numbers

- The stripes for expected shape coexistence are nucleon numbers 7-8, 17-20, 34-40, 59-70, 96-112 (and 146-168 for protons or neutrons).
- Shape coexistence candidates should satisfy R4/2 < 3.05, R0/2 < 5.7, and B02 > 0.1.
- Deformed nuclei are characterized by R4/2 > 3.05; transitional nuclei have 2.4 < R4/2 < 3; vibrational nuclei have R4/2 < 2.4.
- The N=90 isotones ¹⁵⁰Nd, ¹⁵²Sm, and ¹⁵⁴Gd have experimental B02 values lower than the X(5) value by a factor of two.
- The N=60 isotones ⁹⁸Sr, ¹⁰⁰Zr, and ¹⁰²Mo have R0/2 values lower than the X(5) value by a factor of 3.
- Deformed nuclei are characterized by P > 5; nearly spherical and transitional nuclei have P < 5.
- For the IBM calculation with χ = -√7/2 and N_B = 250, the critical point is ζ_crit = 0.4721.

## Context

This work extends the dual shell mechanism of the proxy-SU(3) model (Bonatsos 2021) and the covariant density functional theory studies of particle-hole excitations (CDFT, 2021-2022) that identified islands of shape coexistence around Z=82, Z=50, N=90, and N=60. The authors connect shape coexistence to first-order shape/phase transitions from spherical to deformed shapes, as previously studied in the N=90 region (X(5) critical point symmetry of Iachello 2001) and the N=60 region (Ramos 2018, 2020, 2021). The results enable targeted experimental searches for new shape coexistence candidates by providing quantitative selection criteria, particularly for the Hg, Pb, and Po isotopes where B(E2; 0₂⁺→2₁⁺) measurements are still needed.
