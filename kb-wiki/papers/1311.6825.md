# Dynamics of the chiral phase transition

> The intention of this study is the search for signatures of the chiral phase transition in heavy-ion collisions. To investigate the impact of fluctuations, e.g., of the baryon number, at the transition or at a critical point, the linear sigma model is treated in a dynamical (3+1)-dimensional numerical simulation. Chiral fields are approximated as classical mean fields, and quarks are described as quasi particles in a Vlasov equation. Additional dynamics is implemented by quark-quark and quark-sigma-field interactions. For a consistent description of field-particle interactions, a new Monte-Carlo-Langevin-like formalism has been developed and is discussed.

## Key claim
The paper presents a dynamical (3+1)-dimensional simulation of the linear sigma model to search for signatures of the chiral phase transition in heavy-ion collisions, but the authors acknowledge that without chemical processes the system cannot undergo a phase transition and remains trapped in the chiral-symmetry restored phase.

## Method
The chiral fields (sigma and pions) are treated as classical mean fields on a 3D grid using a leap-frog algorithm, while quarks are described as test particles in a Vlasov equation with a Monte-Carlo phase-space distribution. Binary elastic collisions between quarks are implemented using a collision term similar to the BAMPS model, and a virtual heat bath is used to cool or heat the system. A new Monte-Carlo-Langevin-like formalism is developed to allow discrete, stochastic energy-momentum transfer between particles and classical fields, tested on a damped harmonic oscillator.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass set to \(m_\pi = 138\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and vacuum expectation value of the order parameter \(\langle \sigma \rangle_0 = f_\pi = 93\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Interaction coupling \(g = 3\)–\(6\) gives constituent-quark mass \(m_q \approx 330\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Quarks cooled from \(T = 150\ \mathrm{MeV}\) to \(T = 80\ \mathrm{MeV}\) via interactions with a virtual heat bath
- For the damped harmonic oscillator test, the damping factor is \(\gamma = 0.35\) and the statistical method uses a scaling of \(N = 50\)

## Context
This work extends the linear sigma model simulations of Nahrgang (2011) and Herold (2013) by addressing the lack of thermal and chemical equilibration between quarks and meson-mean fields. Previous Langevin approaches treated quarks as a large thermalized reservoir and violated energy conservation, whereas the new method enables microcanonical field-particle coupling. The next step is to apply the method to the full (3+1)-dimensional linear sigma model to treat chemical reactions between quarks and chiral fields consistently within Vlasov-Boltzmann dynamics.
