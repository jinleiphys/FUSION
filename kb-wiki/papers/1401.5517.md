# Gamov-Teller transitions from 14N ground to 14C ground and excited states

> Gamov-Teller transitions from the $^{14}$N ground state to the $^{14}$C ground and excited states were investigated, based on the model of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. The calculated strengths for the allowed transitions to the $0^+$, $1^+$, and $2^+$ states of $^{14}$C were compared with the experimental data measured by high-resolution charge-exchange reactions. The calculated GT transition to the $2^+_1$ state is strong while those to the $0^+_{2,3}$ and $2^+_{2,3}$ states having dominant $2\hbarω$ excited configurations are relatively weak. The present calculation can not describe the anonymously long life time of $^{14}$C, though the strength of the $^{14}$C ground state is somewhat suppressed because of the cluster (many-body) correlation in the ground states of $^{14}$C and $^{14}$N.

## Key claim

The antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculation cannot describe the anomalously long lifetime of 14C (i.e., the vanishing GT strength to the ground state), although the ground-state cluster correlation does somewhat suppress the GT strength to the 14C ground state compared with simple shell-model predictions.

## Method

The authors applied the AMD method with variation after spin-parity projection (AMD+VAP) to calculate the 1+ ground state of 14N and the 0+, 1+, and 2+ states of 14C, using a phenomenological effective nuclear interaction (MV1 central force plus G3RS spin-orbit force). They also performed a beta-gamma constrained AMD plus generator coordinate method (GCM) calculation to investigate the role of cluster correlations. The GT transition strengths were computed from the 14N ground state to each 14C state and compared with experimental data from high-resolution charge-exchange reactions and with large-scale no-core shell-model (NCSM) calculations.

## Key numbers

- B(GT) for 14C(0+1) ground state: calculated values range from 0.07 to 0.16 depending on interaction parameters, compared with the experimental value of 1.90E-06
- B(GT) for 14C(2+1) state: calculated values range from 2.1 to 2.5, compared with the experimental value of 0.45
- B(GT) for 14C(2+2) state: calculated values range from 0.17 to 0.75, compared with the experimental value of 0.37
- B(GT) for 14C(1+1) state: calculated values range from 0.21 to 0.32, compared with the experimental value of 0.072
- Excitation energy of 14C(0+2): calculated 9.0-10.9 MeV depending on interaction, compared with experimental 6.589 MeV
- Excitation energy of 14C(2+1): calculated 7.2-10.1 MeV, compared with experimental 7.012 MeV
- B(E2; 2+1 to 0+1) for 14C: calculated 4.1-7.4 e2fm4, compared with experimental 3.74 e2fm4
- Magnetic moment of 14N ground state: calculated +0.34 mu_N, compared with experimental +0.40376100(6) mu_N
- Electric quadrupole moment of 14N ground state: calculated +0.92 e.fm2, compared with experimental +1.93(8) e.fm2
- The 14C(0+1) ground state contains 25% 2-hbar-omega and 5% 4-hbar-omega excited configurations in the AMD+VAP calculation

## Context

This work extends previous AMD studies of cluster structures in 14C by Suhara and Kanada-En'yo (2010) and addresses the need to include cluster correlations in GT transition calculations, as suggested by Negret et al. (2006) based on charge-exchange reaction data. The results are compared with the large-scale NCSM calculation by Aroua et al. (2002) using the AV8' interaction, which also failed to reproduce the detailed GT strengths for excited 0+ and 2+ states. The present AMD approach better describes the 0+2 and 2+2 states than the NCSM calculation, but neither approach can reproduce the vanishing GT strength to the 14C ground state, which requires fine-tuning of interactions as shown by Jancovici and Talmi (1954), Fayache et al. (1999), and Holt et al. (2007, 2009). The work enables future studies of how cluster correlations affect GT transitions in other light nuclei.
