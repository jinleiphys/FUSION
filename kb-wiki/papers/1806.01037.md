# Proton-Nucleus Elastic Scattering: Comparison between Phenomenological and Microscopic Optical Potentials

> Elastic scattering is a very important process to understand nuclear interactions in finite nuclei. Despite decades of efforts, the goal of reaching a coherent description of this physical process in terms of microscopic forces is still far from being completed. In previous papers (Phys. Rev. C93, 034619 (2016), Phys. Rev. C96, 044001 (2017)) we derived a nonrelativistic theoretical optical potential from nucleon-nucleon chiral potentials at fourth (N3LO) and fifth order (N4LO). We checked convergence patterns and established theoretical error bands. With this work we study the performances of our optical potential in comparison with those of a successful nonrelativistic phenomenological optical potential in the description of elastic proton scattering data on several isotopic chains at energies around and above 200 MeV. We use the same framework and the same approximations of our previous papers, where the nonrelativistic optical potential is derived at the first-order term within the spectator expansion of the multiple scattering theory and adopting the impulse approximation and the optimum factorization approximation. The cross sections and analyzing powers for elastic proton scattering off calcium, nickel, tin, and lead isotopes are presented for several incident proton energies, exploring the range $156 \le E \le 333$ MeV, where experimental data are available. In addition, we provide theoretical predictions for Ni56 at 400 MeV, which is of interest for the future experiments at EXL. Our results indicate that microscopic optical potentials derived from nucleon-nucleon chiral potentials at N4LO can provide reliable predictions for the cross section and the analyzing power both of stable and exotic nuclei, even at energies where the reliability of the chiral expansion starts to be questionable.

## Key claim

Microscopic optical potentials derived from nucleon-nucleon chiral potentials at fifth order (N4LO) can provide reliable predictions for elastic proton scattering cross sections and analyzing powers for both stable and exotic nuclei, even at energies up to approximately 300 MeV where the chiral expansion's reliability starts to become questionable, though the upper limit depends on the regularization scheme used for the NN interaction.

## Method

The authors constructed a nonrelativistic microscopic optical potential at first order within the spectator expansion of multiple scattering theory, adopting the impulse approximation and optimum factorization approximation, using two versions of N4LO chiral potentials (EKM and EMN) with different regularization parameters. They compared these microscopic predictions against the phenomenological global optical potential of Koning and Delaroche (KD) for elastic proton scattering differential cross sections and analyzing powers across calcium, nickel, tin, and lead isotopes at incident proton energies between 156 and 333 MeV, using experimental data from the literature.

## Key numbers

- Proton energies explored: 156 to 333 MeV for available experimental data, plus a prediction for 56Ni at 400 MeV
- For 58Ni at 295 MeV: microscopic potentials gave better description than KD up to approximately 40 degrees scattering angle
- For 58Ni at 333 MeV: both EKM and EMN gave "much better and very good description of data" compared to KD
- For 120Sn at 200 MeV: KD gave better description than microscopic potentials (120Sn included in KD database up to 160 MeV)
- For 208Pb at 295 MeV: EMN results gave better agreement with data than EKM or KD
- For 16O at 200 MeV: KD described data well for angles less than or equal to 20 degrees, while EKM and EMN gave reasonable agreement up to approximately 50 degrees
- For calcium isotopes at 200 MeV: KD gave "excellent agreement" with 40Ca data (included in KD database) and good agreement for other isotopes
- For 60Ni at 178 MeV: microscopic potentials gave "better and reasonable agreement" with data than KD
- For 62Ni at 156 MeV: KD gave "very good agreement" with data

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Vorabbi 2016, Vorabbi 2017) where they derived microscopic optical potentials from N3LO and N4LO chiral potentials and established convergence patterns and theoretical error bands. The present paper systematically compares these microscopic predictions against the phenomenological global optical potential of Koning and Delaroche (2003, 2014) across multiple isotopic chains, finding that the microscopic approach has comparable or better predictive power above 200 MeV. The results enable future applications to exotic nuclei, particularly providing predictions for 56Ni at 400 MeV relevant for the EXL experiment at FAIR, and suggest that EKM chiral potentials may remain reliable at higher energies than EMN potentials.
