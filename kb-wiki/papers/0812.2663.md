# One-particle properties of deformed N $\approx$ 28 odd-N nuclei with weakly-bound or resonant neutrons

> Possible deformation of odd-N nuclei with N $\approx$ 28 towards the neutron drip line is investigated using the Nilsson diagram based on deformed Woods-Saxon potentials. Both weakly-bound and resonant one-particle levels are properly obtained by directly solving the Schrödinger equation in mesh of space coordinate with the correct boundary condition. If we use the same diffuseness of the potential as that of $β$-stable nuclei, the energy difference between the neutron 2p$_{3/2}$ and 1f$_{7/2}$ levels becomes very small or the N=28 energy gap almost disappears, as the binding energies of those levels approach zero. This suggests that the ground states of those neutron drip line nuclei are likely to be deformed. In particular, the spin-parity and the magnetic moment of the ground state of odd-N nuclei, $^{43}_{16}$S$_{27}$ and $^{45}_{16}$S$_{29}$, are examined. Moreover, it is suggested that in $^{39}_{12}$Mg$_{27}$ lying outside the drip line the lowest resonant state may have 5/2$^{-}$, if the N=28 energy gap almost vanishes.

# One-particle properties of deformed N ≈ 28 odd-N nuclei with weakly-bound or resonant neutrons

## Key claim
If the same diffuseness of the Woods-Saxon potential as that of β-stable nuclei is used, the energy difference between the neutron 2p₃/₂ and 1f₇/₂ levels becomes very small or the N=28 energy gap almost disappears as the binding energies of those levels approach zero, suggesting that the ground states of neutron drip line nuclei with N≈28 are likely to be deformed rather than spherical.

## Method
The author solves the coupled Schrödinger equations in coordinate space with correct asymptotic boundary conditions for large r, using deformed Woods-Saxon potentials with standard parameters from stable nuclei except for the depth. One-particle resonant states are identified when a calculated eigenphase increases through π/2 as energy increases, extending the phase-shift definition from spherical potentials to deformed potentials. The Nilsson diagrams are constructed for A=45 (representing ⁴⁵S) and A=39 (representing ³⁹Mg) to examine bound and resonant one-particle levels as functions of axially-symmetric quadrupole deformation.

## Key numbers
- At β=0 for A=45 with V_WS = -41 MeV, the 2p₃/₂ level lies 2.27 MeV higher than the 1f₇/₂ level, much smaller than the N=28 gap in stable nuclei; for comparison, the energy difference between 1f₇/₂ and 1d₃/₂ (N=20 magic gap) is 4.92 MeV.
- For A=39 with V_WS = -37 MeV, the distance between the 2p₃/₂ and 1f₇/₂ resonant levels (N=28 energy gap) is only 180 keV.
- At β=0 for A=39, the 1f₇/₂ resonance at ε = 0.26 MeV has a calculated width of 0.8 keV, while the 2p₃/₂ resonance at ε = 0.44 MeV has a width of 890 keV.
- For ⁴⁴S, the measured B(E2; 0⁺_g.s. → 2⁺₁) = 314(88) e² fm⁴ with E_x(2⁺₁) = 1297(18) keV from Coulomb excitation.
- For ⁴³S, a level at 940 keV with B(E2) = 175(69) e² fm⁴ is reported from Coulomb excitation.
- The measured magnetic moment of the ground state (I^π=7/2⁻) of ⁴⁷Ca₂₇ is -1.38(3) μ_N; the preliminary value for the ground state (I^π=3/2⁻) of ⁴⁹Ca₂₉ is -1.38(6) μ_N.
- The measured magnetic moment of the ground state (I^π=3/2⁻) of ³³Mg₂₁ is -0.7456(5) μ_N.
- For reference, in stable ⁵²Cr₂₈ the energy distance between 2p₃/₂ and 1f₇/₂ at β=0 is 4.03 MeV.
- With increased diffuseness a=0.75 fm for A=39, the 2p₃/₂ and 1f₇/₂ resonant energies become 0.14 and 0.37 MeV respectively, with the 2p₃/₂ lying 230 keV lower than the 1f₇/₂.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous study (Hamamoto 2007) of weakly-bound and resonant one-particle levels to N≈28 neutron drip line nuclei, motivated by the observation that the two-neutron separation energy S₂n of S isotopes shows a pronounced change of slope around N=26 compared to Ca isotopes (Friedman and Schatz 2000). The paper provides Nilsson diagrams for interpreting future experimental data on neutron-rich S and Mg isotopes, complementing self-consistent mean-field calculations that do not properly treat weakly-bound and resonant one-particle levels. The results suggest that measurement of spin-parity and magnetic moments of ⁴³S and ⁴⁵S could reveal whether N=28 ceases to be a magic number near the neutron drip line, and that the lowest resonant state in the unbound nucleus ³⁹Mg may have 5/2⁻ if the N=28 gap collapses.
