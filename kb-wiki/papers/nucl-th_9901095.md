# Study of Omega, Eta, Eta-prime and D-minus mesic nuclei

> Using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, we investigate whether $ω, η, η'$ and $D^-$ mesons form meson-nucleus bound states. Our results suggest that one should expect to find $η$- and $ω$-nucleus bound states in all the nuclei considered. Furthermore, it is shown that the $D^-$ meson will form quite narrow bound states with $^{208}$Pb.

## Key claim

Using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, the study predicts that η and ω mesons should form bound states with all nuclei considered, and that the D⁻ meson will form quite narrow bound states with ²⁰⁸Pb, but these predictions depend on phenomenological parameters for in-medium meson widths (γ_η = 0.5, γ_ω = 0.2) and on whether the vector coupling strength is increased by a factor of 1.4² for the D⁻ case.

## Method

The author calculated meson effective masses in symmetric nuclear matter and in finite nuclei using the QMC model, where Dirac equations for quarks and antiquarks inside meson bags are solved with mean-field potentials (σ, ω, ρ) that are determined self-consistently. Bound state energies for ω, η, η', and D⁻ mesons were then obtained by solving a Klein-Gordon equation with complex effective masses that include phenomenological width parameters. For the D⁻ meson, two choices for the ω vector potential were considered: the standard QMC coupling and an enhanced coupling (1.4² times larger) previously needed to reproduce K⁺-nucleus scattering data.

## Key numbers

- η 1s bound state energy in ²⁰⁸Pb: −56.3 MeV with width Γ_η = 33.2 MeV (γ_η = 0.5)
- ω 1s bound state energy in ²⁰⁸Pb: −118 MeV with width Γ_ω = 33.1 MeV (γ_ω = 0.2)
- η' 1s bound state energy in ²⁰⁸Pb: −57.5 MeV (widths set to zero)
- D⁻ 1s bound state energy in ²⁰⁸Pb with standard vector coupling (V^q_ω): −35.2 MeV (widths set to zero)
- D⁻ 1s bound state energy in ²⁰⁸Pb with enhanced vector coupling (1.4² V^q_ω): −10.6 MeV (widths set to zero)
- D⁻ 1s bound state energy in ²⁰⁸Pb with standard vector coupling and no Coulomb potential: −11.2 MeV
- Current quark masses used: m_q = 5 MeV, m_s = 250 MeV, m_c = 1300 MeV; nucleon bag radius R_N = 0.8 fm
- Normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Hayano et al. (1998) to produce η and ω mesons with nearly zero recoil in nuclei, and applies the QMC model previously developed by the author and collaborators (QMC model references, finite nuclei studies) to predict meson-nucleus bound states. The results are relevant to experiments at CERES and HELIOS at CERN/SPS, and planned experiments at TJNAF and GSI, as well as to understanding chiral symmetry restoration in nuclear matter. The study enables experimental searches for η-, ω-, and D⁻-mesic nuclei, which would provide information on the Lorentz scalar σ field and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking.
