# Testing the chiral magnetic and chiral vortical effects in heavy ion collisions

> We devise a test of the Chiral Magnetic and Chiral Vortical effects (CME and CVE) in relativistic heavy ion collisions that relies only on the general properties of triangle anomalies. We show that the ratio $R_{EB}=J_E/J_B$ of charge $J_E$ and baryon $J_B$ currents for CME is $R^{\rm CME}_{EB} \to \infty$ for three light flavors of quarks ($N_f =3$), and $R^{\rm CME}_{EB} = 5$ for $N_f =2$, whereas for CVE it is $R^{\rm CVE}_{EB} =0$ for $N_f =3$ and $R^{\rm CME}_{EB} = 1/2$ for $N_f =2$. The physical world with light $u,d$ quarks and a heavier $s$ quark is in between the $N_f =2$ and $N_f =3$ cases; therefore, the ratios $R_{EB}$ for CME and CVE should differ by over an order of magnitude. Since the ratio of electric charge and baryon asymmetries is proportional to $R_{EB}$, the measurement of baryon and electric charge asymmetry fluctuations should allow to separate clearly the CME and CVE contributions. In both cases, there has to be a positive correlation between the charge and baryon number asymmetries that can be tested on the event-by-event basis. At a lower collision energy, as the baryon number density increases and the CVE potentially plays a role, we expect the emergence of the baryon number asymmetry.

## Key claim

The ratio of electric charge to baryon currents provides a clean test to separate the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) from the Chiral Vortical Effect (CVE) in heavy ion collisions, relying only on the general properties of triangle anomalies rather than detailed dynamical assumptions.

## Method

The authors compute the ratio \(R_{EB} = J_E/J_B\) of electric charge current to baryon current for both CME and CVE using the anomalous hydrodynamic formalism of Son and Son (2009), evaluating the flavor traces for the cases of three light quark flavors (\(N_f=3\)) and two light flavors (\(N_f=2\)). They then argue that the physical world with light \(u,d\) quarks and a heavier \(s\) quark lies between these two limits, so the ratios for CME and CVE should differ by over an order of magnitude, allowing experimental discrimination via event-by-event correlation measurements of charge and baryon asymmetries.

## Key numbers

- For CME with \(N_f=3\): \(R^{\rm CME}_{EB} \to \infty\) (baryon current vanishes exactly)
- For CME with \(N_f=2\): \(R^{\rm CME}_{EB} = 5\)
- For CVE with \(N_f=3\): \(R^{\rm CVE}_{EB} = 0\) (charge current vanishes exactly)
- For CVE with \(N_f=2\): \(R^{\rm CVE}_{EB} = 1/2\)
- For CME with \(N_f=3\), the charge current prefactor is \(2/3\) and the baryon current prefactor is 0 (in units of \(N_c \mu_5 \vec B/(2\pi^2)\))
- For CME with \(N_f=2\), the charge current prefactor is \(5/9\) and the baryon current prefactor is \(1/9\) (same units)
- For CVE with \(N_f=3\), the charge current prefactor is 0 and the baryon current prefactor is 1 (in units of \(N_c \mu_5 \mu \vec \omega/\pi^2\))
- For CVE with \(N_f=2\), the charge current prefactor is \(1/3\) and the baryon current prefactor is \(2/3\) (same units)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of anomalous hydrodynamics developed by Son and Son (2009) and the earlier proposal of the Chiral Magnetic Effect by Kharzeev (2004, 2007) to provide a specific experimental test that can discriminate between CME and CVE contributions. The proposal builds on the experimental observation of charge asymmetry fluctuations by the STAR Collaboration (2009) and PHENIX Collaboration at RHIC, and the recognition that the matter created at RHIC behaves as an almost perfect liquid describable by hydrodynamics. The test enables future experimental studies at RHIC, LHC, FAIR, and NICA to determine whether the observed charge asymmetry fluctuations indeed originate from topological effects in QCD, and to study the role of baryon chemical potential by varying collision energy.
