# Neutron-antineutron oscillations on the lattice

> One possible low energy process due to beyond the Standard Model (BSM) physics is the neutron-antineutron transition, where baryon number changes by two units. In addition to providing a source of baryon number violation in the early universe, interactions of this kind are natural in grand unified theories (GUTs) with Majorana neutrinos that violate lepton number. Bounds on these oscillations can greatly restrict a variety of GUTs, while a non-zero signal would be a "smoking gun" for new physics; however, to make a reliable prediction, the six-quark nucleon-antinucleon matrix elements must first be calculated non-perturbatively via lattice QCD. We review the current understanding of this quantity, describe the lattice formalism, and present preliminary results from $32^3\times256$ clover-Wilson lattices with a pion mass of 390 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper presents preliminary lattice QCD results for the six-quark matrix elements that govern neutron-antineutron oscillations, but these results are bare (unrenormalized) and computed at an unphysically large pion mass of 390 MeV, so they cannot yet be directly compared to experimental bounds or used to constrain beyond-the-Standard-Model theories.

## Method

The authors compute neutron-antineutron three-point correlation functions on \(32^3 \times 256\) anisotropic clover-Wilson lattices with a pion mass of 390 MeV, using 159 configurations and 7268 Gaussian-smeared propagators. The matrix elements \(\langle \overline{n} | \mathcal{P}_i | n \rangle\) for six independent \(\Delta B = 2\) operators are extracted from the long Euclidean time behavior of a ratio of three-point to two-point correlation functions, with a two-dimensional correlated fit over source-operator separation \(t_1\) and operator-sink separation \(t_2\) to control excited state contamination.

## Key numbers

* Lattice parameters: \(32^3 \times 256\) volume, temporal lattice spacing \(\sim 0.035\) fm, spatial lattice spacing \(\sim 0.123\) fm, spatial extent \(\sim 4\) fm, pion mass 390 MeV, \(m_\pi L \sim 7.8\).
* Bare matrix element for \(\mathcal{P}_1\): \(1.57 \pm 0.85^{+0.25}_{-0.30} \times 10^{-5}\) GeV.
* Bare matrix element for \(\mathcal{P}_2\): \(-0.20 \pm 0.14^{+0.14}_{-0.12} \times 10^{-5}\) GeV.
* Bare matrix element for \(\mathcal{P}_3\): \(-0.24 \pm 0.26^{+0.10}_{-0.07} \times 10^{-5}\) GeV.
* Bare matrix element for \(\mathcal{P}_4\): \(-0.02 \pm 0.39^{+0.07}_{-0.18} \times 10^{-5}\) GeV.
* Bare matrix element for \(\mathcal{P}_5\): \(0.34 \pm 0.82^{+0.27}_{-0.57} \times 10^{-5}\) GeV.
* Bare matrix element for \(\mathcal{P}_6\): \(-2.07 \pm 1.10^{+1.28}_{-0.77} \times 10^{-5}\) GeV.
* Current experimental bound from Super-K (2011): \(\tau_{n \overline{n}} > 3.5 \times 10^8\) seconds (nucleon-bound).
* Current experimental bound from ILL (1993): \(\tau_{n \overline{n}} > 0.86 \times 10^8\) seconds (free neutron).

## Context

This work extends earlier MIT bag model calculations of the six-quark matrix elements by Rao and Shrock (1982) to a first-principles lattice QCD approach, and provides the first lattice calculation of these quantities. The results are needed to interpret experimental bounds from Super-K and the ILL experiment, and to constrain grand unified theories such as \(SO(10)\) seesaw models (Babu, 2012) and \(SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R \times SU(4)_c\) models (Babu, 2008). The authors plan to extend this work to lighter pion masses (240 MeV), smaller volumes (2.5 fm), and eventually physical pion masses with domain-wall fermions, enabling rigorous comparison with future experiments that aim for \(\mathcal{O}(1000)\) improvements in sensitivity.
