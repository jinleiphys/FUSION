# Dynamic effects of nuclear surface in isoscalar dipole modes

> Dynamic surface effects in the isoscalar dipole modes of heavy nuclei are studied within a semiclassical model based on the solution of the Vlasov kinetic equation for finite Fermi systems with a moving surface. In order to clarify the role of dynamic surface effects we have considered an approximate solution, which takes into account only the part of the variation of the phase-space distribution function caused by the dynamic surface (the dynamic-surface approximation). It is shown that the dynamic surface effects have an essential influence on the features of the isoscalar dipole modes. The isoscalar dipole strength function has a two-resonance structure already in the dynamic-surface approximation, and the centroid energies of both the low-energy resonance and the high-energy resonance are close to corresponding centroid energies of exact strength function. Calculations of the velocity field in the dynamic-surface approximation show the vortex character of the low-energy isoscalar dipole resonance and the compression character of the high-energy one.

## Key claim

The paper shows that dynamic surface effects (oscillations of the nuclear surface) are essential for forming both the low-energy vortex and high-energy compression isoscalar dipole resonances in heavy nuclei, but this conclusion is conditional on the "dynamic-surface approximation" which neglects the static-surface contribution to the phase-space distribution function.

## Method

The authors solve the linearized Vlasov kinetic equation for a finite Fermi system with a moving spherical surface, using a separable isoscalar dipole residual interaction and Thomas-Fermi equilibrium distribution. They introduce the "dynamic-surface approximation" which retains only the part of the distribution function variation caused by the moving surface, discarding the static-surface contribution. The internal isoscalar dipole strength function and velocity fields are then computed for a system of A=208 nucleons and compared with exact solutions and static-surface approximations.

## Key numbers

- Centroid energy of the low-energy isoscalar dipole resonance: 11.5 MeV (for A=208 system, exact calculation)
- Centroid energy of the high-energy isoscalar dipole resonance: 24.3 MeV (for A=208 system, exact calculation)
- Isoscalar dipole residual interaction strength parameter: kappa_1 = -7.5 x 10^-3 MeV/fm^2 (chosen to reproduce giant monopole resonance energy in Pb-208)
- Incompressibility modulus: K_A = 160 MeV
- Nuclear radius parameter: r_0 = 1.25 fm
- Fermi energy: epsilon_F = 30.94 MeV
- Nucleon effective mass parameter: m = 1.04 MeV (10^-22 sec)^2/fm^2

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous semiclassical kinetic model of finite Fermi systems with moving surfaces (Abrosimov 2005) to isoscalar dipole modes, complementing quantum RPA studies of isoscalar dipole excitations (Giai 1981, Colo 2000, Vretenar 2000, Kvasil 2013). The results show that dynamic surface effects are crucial for isoscalar dipole resonances, in contrast to the isovector giant dipole resonance where surface effects only shift the energy (Abrosimov 2001). The findings enable further study of surface dynamics in nuclear collective excitations and provide a semiclassical framework for interpreting experimental data on isoscalar dipole resonances in heavy spherical nuclei.
