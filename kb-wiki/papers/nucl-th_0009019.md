# Jet Quenching and Azimuthal Anisotropy of Large $p_T$ Spectra in Non-central High-energy Heavy-ion Collisions

> Parton energy loss inside a dense medium leads to the suppression of large $p_T$ hadrons and can also cause azimuthal anisotropy of hadron spectra at large transverse momentum in non-central high-energy heavy-ion collisions. Such azimuthal anisotropy is studied qualitatively in a parton model for heavy-ion collisions at the RHIC energies. The coefficient $v_2(p_T)$ of the elliptic anisotropy at large $p_T$ is found to be very sensitive to parton energy loss. It decreases slowly with $p_T$ contrary to its low $p_T$ behavior where $v_2$ increases very rapidly with $p_T$. The turning point signals the onset of contributions of hard processes and the magnitude of parton energy loss. The centrality dependence of $v_2(p_T)$ is shown to be sensitive to both size and density dependence of the parton energy loss and the later can also be studied via variation of the colliding energy. The anisotropy coefficient $v_2/ε$ normalized by the spatial ellipticity $ε$ is found to decrease significantly toward semi-peripheral collisions, differing from the hydrodynamic results for low $p_T$ hadrons. Constrained by the existing WA98 experimental data at the SPS energy on parton energy loss, both hadron spectra suppression and azimuthal anisotropy at high $p_T$ are predicted to vanish for $b>$7-8 fm in $Au+Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=$130-200 GeV when the hadron rapidity density per unit area of the initial overlapped region is less than what is achieved in the central $Pb+Pb$ collisions at the SPS energy.

## Key claim

Parton energy loss inside a dense medium causes both suppression of large $p_T$ hadrons and azimuthal anisotropy of hadron spectra at large transverse momentum in non-central high-energy heavy-ion collisions; the study is explicitly qualitative, using a parton model with simplified hard-sphere geometry and neglecting longitudinal and transverse expansion, to demonstrate the effect for a given averaged $dE/dx$.

## Method

The author uses a parton model extending collinear factorized parton scattering to include intrinsic transverse momentum and its nuclear broadening, with parton energy loss incorporated via modified fragmentation functions that depend on the distance $L(\phi)$ a parton travels through the medium as a function of azimuthal angle. The model employs a hard-sphere nuclear distribution to estimate the propagation distance inside the overlapped region, and calculates the single inclusive hadron spectra and the elliptic anisotropy coefficient $v_2$ for charged hadrons in $Au+Au$ collisions at RHIC energies. The centrality dependence is studied using a Glauber model with Woods-Saxon nuclear distributions, and the parton energy loss $dE/dx$ is parameterized as proportional to the particle density in the overlapped region.

## Key numbers

- $v_2$ decreases slowly with $p_T$ for $p_T > 3$ GeV/$c$ in semi-peripheral $Au+Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=130$ GeV, in contrast to hydrodynamic predictions where $v_2$ increases with $p_T$
- For $dE/dx=0.5$ GeV/fm and $\lambda=1$ fm in central $Au+Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=130$ and 200 GeV, the suppression of large $p_T$ spectra is demonstrated; the soft component with $dn_{ch}/d\eta=520$ (130 GeV) and 700 (200 GeV) is negligible at $p_T>3$ GeV/$c$
- $\pi^0$ spectra at high $p_T$ are about 40-30% lower than $(h^-+h^+)/2$ in central $Au+Au$ collisions
- For $dE/dx=0.1 (L/{\rm fm})$ GeV/fm and $\lambda=1$ fm, azimuthal distributions of charged hadrons at $p_T=5$, 7, and 9 GeV/$c$ in semi-peripheral ($b=$ 7-8 fm) $Au+Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=130$ GeV show strong azimuthal dependence
- $v_2$ at $p_T=5$ GeV/$c$ increases with impact parameter for both constant $dE/dx=0.5$ GeV/fm and distance-dependent $dE/dx=0.1 (L/{\rm fm})$ GeV/fm in $Au+Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=130$ GeV
- $v_2/\varepsilon$ decreases significantly toward semi-peripheral collisions, differing from hydrodynamic results; for the density-dependent scenario, $v_2$ vanishes for $b>7$ fm at $\sqrt{s}=130$ GeV and $b>8$ fm at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV
- The ratio $R_{AA}(p_T,b)$ at $p_T=5$ GeV/$c$ in $Au+Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=130$ GeV approaches 1 for very peripheral collisions; for the scenario constrained by WA98 data, the ratio becomes larger than 1 for $b>7$ fm due to the Cronin effect

## Context

This work extends studies of parton energy loss (Gyulassy-Wang 1994, Baier-Dokshitzer-Mueller-Peigne-Schiff 1997, Zakharov 1996) and jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions (Wang-Gyulassy 1992, Wang 1998) to the azimuthal anisotropy of large $p_T$ hadrons. It contrasts with hydrodynamic models (Ollitrault 1992, Heinz 1999, Shuryak 1999, Hirano 2000) that predict $v_2$ increasing with $p_T$ and constant $v_2/\varepsilon$ over a wide centrality range. The study is constrained by WA98 experimental data at SPS energy showing no indication of parton energy loss in central $Pb+Pb$ collisions, and enables future experimental tests at RHIC to extract the size and density dependence of parton energy loss by combining measurements of hadron suppression and azimuthal anisotropy at different colliding energies and centralities.
