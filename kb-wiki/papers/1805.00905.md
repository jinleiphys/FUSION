# Nucleon axial form factor from a Bayesian neural-network analysis of neutrino-scattering data

> The Bayesian approach for feed-forward neural networks has been applied to the extraction of the nucleon axial form factor from the neutrino-deuteron scattering data measured by the Argonne National Laboratory (ANL) bubble chamber experiment. This framework allows to perform a model-independent determination of the axial form factor from data.. When the low $0.05 < Q^2 < 0.10$ GeV$^2$ data is included in the analysis, the resulting axial radius disagrees with available determinations. Furthermore, a large sensitivity to the corrections from the deuteron structure is obtained. In turn, when the low-$Q^2$ region is not taken into account, with or without deuteron corrections, no significant deviations from the dipole ansatz have been observed. A more accurate determination of the nucleon axial form factor requires new precise measurements of neutrino-induced quasielastic scattering on hydrogen and deuterium.

## Key claim

Using a Bayesian neural-network approach to extract the nucleon axial form factor from ANL neutrino-deuteron scattering data, the authors find that when low-$Q^2$ data ($0.05 < Q^2 < 0.10$ GeV$^2$) are included, the resulting axial radius disagrees with available determinations and shows large sensitivity to deuteron structure corrections; however, when this low-$Q^2$ region is excluded, no significant deviations from the dipole ansatz are observed, regardless of whether deuteron corrections are applied.

## Method

The authors applied a Bayesian framework for feed-forward neural networks (multilayer perceptrons with one hidden layer and 1-4 hidden units) to perform a semiparametric, model-independent extraction of the nucleon axial form factor $F_A(Q^2)$ from the $Q^2$ distribution of charged-current quasielastic $\nu_\mu d \to \mu^- p p$ events measured by the ANL bubble chamber experiment. The neural network output was normalized to the dipole parametrization, and the analysis was repeated with and without deuteron structure corrections from Singh (1986), and with three variants of the data set that either included or excluded the lowest $Q^2$ bins.

## Key numbers

* Axial coupling constant fixed to $g_A = 1.2723 \pm 0.0023$ from neutron beta-decay (PDG value), used as a constraint at $Q^2=0$.
* Reference dipole axial mass $M_A = 1.016 \pm 0.026$ GeV from Bodek et al. (2007), used for comparison.
* Axial radius from $z$-expansion analysis of neutrino-scattering data: $r_A^2 = 0.46 \pm 0.22$ fm$^2$ (Meyer et al., 2016).
* Axial radius from $z$-expansion analysis of muon capture: $r_A^2 = 0.43 \pm 0.24$ fm$^2$ (Hill et al., 2017).
* Axial radius from lattice QCD global analysis: $r_A^2 = 0.263 \pm 0.038$ fm$^2$ (Yao et al., 2017).
* Total number of ANL events used: $N_\mathrm{ANL} = 1792$, distributed over 25 $Q^2$ bins (bins 1-23 with width $0.05$ GeV$^2$, bins 24-25 with width $0.65$ GeV$^2$).
* Flux-normalization systematic uncertainty assumed: $\Delta p = 0.20$ (20%).

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian neural-network methodology previously applied to nucleon electromagnetic form factors by Graczyk et al. (2010) to the axial form factor, providing a model-independent alternative to the dipole ansatz and to the $z$-expansion analysis of Meyer et al. (2016). The results highlight the sensitivity of the extracted axial radius to low-$Q^2$ data and deuteron corrections, and they motivate new precise measurements of neutrino-induced quasielastic scattering on hydrogen and deuterium to resolve the current tensions between empirical determinations and lattice QCD results from Yao et al. (2017).
