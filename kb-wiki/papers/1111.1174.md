# Study of Energy Spectra and Electromagnetic moments of Double Beta Decay Nuclei in Deformed Hartree-Fock Model

> Spectroscopy of nuclei in the mass range A=76 to A=130, participating in double beta decay processes are studied in the framework of the self-consistent deformed Hartree-Fock (HF) and angular momentum (J) projection model. Spectra of ground bands have been studied and compared with available experimental results for even-even parent and daughter as well as for intermediate odd-odd nuclei. To test the reliability of the wave functions we have also calculated the reduced E2 transition matrix elements, electric quadrupole moments and magnetic dipole moments for these nuclei. The calculated results are compared with the experimental findings and substantial agreement is achieved.

## Key claim

The self-consistent deformed Hartree-Fock (DHF) model with angular momentum projection, using only a few low-lying intrinsic configurations, can reasonably reproduce the yrast spectra and electromagnetic properties of double-beta-decay nuclei in the mass range A=76 to A=130, though the agreement is qualified by the fact that pairing is not included explicitly and that for some nuclei (e.g., 116Cd, 116Sn, 124Te) the calculated B(E2) values deviate significantly from experiment.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent deformed Hartree-Fock calculations using a surface delta residual interaction (strength ~0.36 for p-p, p-n, and n-n interactions) within a model space spanning orbits from 1p3/2 to 0h11/2 above a 56Ni core. They then applied angular momentum projection to obtain states of good angular momentum from the deformed intrinsic states, mixing a few low-lying configurations (including prolate and oblate shapes where degenerate) to produce energy spectra, and calculated reduced E2 transition matrix elements, electric quadrupole moments, and magnetic dipole moments using effective charges e_p = 1 + e_ff and e_n = e_ff with e_ff values of 0.4, 0.5, and 0.6.

## Key numbers

- For 76Ge, the calculated B(E2; 0+ -> 2+) ranges from 0.195 e^2b^2 (e_ff=0.4) to 0.295 e^2b^2 (e_ff=0.6), compared to experimental values of 0.268 +/- 0.008 e^2b^2 (average) and 0.230 +/- 0.035 e^2b^2.
- For 76Se, the calculated B(E2; 0+ -> 2+) ranges from 0.311 e^2b^2 (e_ff=0.4) to 0.501 e^2b^2 (e_ff=0.6), compared to experimental values of 0.420 +/- 0.010 e^2b^2 (average) and 0.39 +/- 0.26 e^2b^2.
- For 76Ge 2+ state, the calculated quadrupole moment is -0.166 eb (e_ff=0.4) to -0.168 eb (e_ff=0.6), compared to experimental -0.19 +/- 0.06 eb; the calculated magnetic moment is 0.582 nm, compared to experimental 0.84 +/- 0.05 nm.
- For 76As 2- state, the calculated magnetic moment is -0.775 nm, compared to experimental -0.906 +/- 0.005 nm.
- For 82Kr, the calculated magnetic moment for the 2+ state is 0.202 nm, compared to experimental 0.80 +/- 0.03 nm (a discrepancy of about 0.6 nm).
- For 106Pd, the calculated magnetic moment differs from experimental data by 0.8 nm (exact values not given in the truncated text).
- The HF ground state energy for 76Ge (prolate) is -45.772 MeV, with intrinsic quadrupole moment Q20 = 3.964 b^2 for protons and 4.659 b^2 for neutrons.
- The HF ground state energy for 130Xe (prolate) is -291.346 MeV, with Q20 = 5.105 b^2 for protons and 7.570 b^2 for neutrons.

## Context

This work extends the application of the angular-momentum-projected deformed Hartree-Fock model, previously developed by Praharaj (1982) and others, to a systematic study of double-beta-decay nuclei across a wide mass range (A=76 to A=130). The approach contrasts with full shell-model calculations (which are not feasible for most of these nuclei) and with BCS+RPA treatments (which suffer from instabilities), as noted by the authors. The results enable future calculations of nuclear transition matrix elements for both two-neutrino and neutrinoless double beta decay, using the wave functions validated here against experimental energy spectra and electromagnetic moments.
