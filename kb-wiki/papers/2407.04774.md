# Gluon to $q\bar q$ antenna in anisotropic QCD matter: spin-polarized and azimuthal jet observables

> We study the production of a quark-antiquark antenna in the presence of a dense and anisotropic QCD medium. We assume the antenna to originate from an unpolarized gluon state, and consider both massless and massive final states. The medium anisotropy is captured by allowing the jet quenching coefficient to take different magnitudes in orthogonal directions with respect to the jet axis. We find that the final particle distribution is sensitive to the medium anisotropy, and more importantly, that this effect couples directly to the helicity/spin of the final states. We propose to look into these effects by performing a Fourier decomposition of the particle distribution inside the jet. In our medium model, we find that the spin independent terms contribute to the even harmonics of the cosine series. The helicity/spin dependence enters only through the sine Fourier series. We further explore the spin dependence by examining the degree of polarization of the final states in different directions. Our results indicate that the anisotropies present in the QCD matter produced in heavy ion collisions can be probed by studying azimuthal and spin observables inside jets.

## Key claim

The final particle distribution from a gluon splitting to a quark-antiquark pair inside an anisotropic QCD medium is sensitive to the medium anisotropy, and this effect couples directly to the helicity or spin of the final states; the spin-independent terms contribute only to even cosine Fourier harmonics, while the helicity or spin dependence enters exclusively through the sine Fourier series.

## Method

The authors computed the leading-order cross-section for a gluon branching to a quark-antiquark antenna inside a dense QCD medium, using the BDMPS-Z path integral formalism to resum multiple scatterings with the medium. The medium anisotropy was modeled by allowing the jet quenching parameter \(\hat q\) to take different values in two orthogonal directions transverse to the jet axis (\(\hat q_x \neq \hat q_y\)), following the approach of Hauksson (2023). The calculation was performed in the large \(N_c\) limit, assuming a static medium and using the harmonic approximation for the dipole cross-section, for both massless and massive final states.

## Key numbers

- The azimuthal distribution of final particles is expressed as \(\frac{2\pi}{ dN^h/dz} \frac{dN^h}{dzd\phi} = 1+ v_2 \cos\left(2 \phi \right) + w_2^{(h)} \sin\left(2 \phi \right)\), where \(v_2\) and \(w_2\) vanish in isotropic matter and all helicity or spin dependence lies in \(w_2^{(h)}\).
- The vacuum splitting function used is \(P_{qg}^{vac}(z)= T_R (z^2+ (1-z)^2)\).
- The harmonic oscillator frequencies are \(\Omega_i = \left(\frac{1-i}{\sqrt{2}}\right)\omega_i\) with \(\omega_i \approx \sqrt{\frac{\hat{q}_i P_{qg}^{vac}(z)}{\tilde{q}_0^+}}\), where \(\tilde{q}_0^+=z(1-z)q_0^+\).
- The opening angle between the quark and antiquark is related to the relative transverse momentum by \(|\boldsymbol{P}^{rel}| \approx \frac{\theta_{q\bar q}}{\sqrt{2}} \tilde{q}_0^+\).

## Context

This work extends the study of anisotropic medium effects on jet observables from pure gluonic systems, as done by Hauksson (2023), to quark-antiquark antennas produced from unpolarized gluons. It proposes using Fourier decomposition of the azimuthal particle distribution inside jets, analogous to the soft-sector harmonic analysis in heavy ion collisions, to probe the anisotropic structure of the quark-gluon plasma. The results enable future studies correlating jet measurements with soft-sector physics to extract the transport properties of the medium produced in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions.
