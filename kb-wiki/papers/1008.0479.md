# Meson Production at COSY-TOF and COSY-ANKE

> The roles of the COSY-TOF and COSY-ANKE spectrometers in the measurement of strange meson production are briefly reviewed, mainly in connection with new results on the pp -> K^+ p Lambda, pp -> K^+ p Sigma^0 and pp -> K^+ n Sigma^+ reactions.

# Meson Production at COSY-TOF and COSY-ANKE

## Key claim

The exclusive COSY-TOF data on pp -> K+ p Lambda show a cusp enhancement at the Sigma N threshold that accounts for a large fraction of what had been interpreted in inclusive HIRES measurements as direct Sigma production, meaning the HIRES collaboration's inferred Sigma production cross sections are overestimated. For the pp -> K+ n Sigma+ reaction, the ANKE measurements yield a cross section ratio sigma(Sigma+)/sigma(Sigma0) approximately 0.7 +/- 0.1, which is two orders of magnitude below the earlier COSY-11 result and far below the HIRES assumption.

## Method

The COSY-TOF spectrometer measured exclusive pp -> K+ p Lambda events at beam momenta of 2.95, 3.20, and 3.30 GeV/c, using its large-acceptance time-of-flight barrel to reconstruct delayed Lambda -> p pi- decay vertices and perform kinematic fits. The COSY-ANKE magnetic spectrometer measured pp -> K+ n Sigma+ cross sections at excess energies of 13, 47, 60, and 82 MeV using three independent techniques: inclusive K+ production, K+ p coincidences, and K+ pi+ coincidences, with the latter providing gold-plated events since any K+ pi+ coincidences in pp collisions at low energies must come from Sigma+ production.

## Key numbers

- Forward inclusive cross section for pp -> K+ X at the Sigma N threshold shows a cusp enhancement at all three beam momenta (2.95, 3.20, and 3.30 GeV/c), with the 3.30 GeV/c data scaled by a factor of 0.6 for presentation.
- The ratio sigma(Sigma+)/sigma(Sigma0) is approximately 0.7 +/- 0.1 from ANKE K+ pi+ coincidence data at 1.958 GeV, with a 95% confidence level upper limit of sigma(Sigma+)/sigma(Sigma0) less than or equal to 2 from inclusive ratio measurements 13 MeV above versus 5 MeV below the Sigma+ n threshold.
- COSY-11 had reported sigma(Sigma+) cross sections about two orders of magnitude higher than those for Sigma0 production at excess energies of Q = 13 and 60 MeV.
- ANKE measured about 500 Sigma- events from pd -> p_sp K+ p X- at both 2.12 and 2.22 GeV via p_sp K+ p triple coincidences.
- The N*(1535) model prediction for pp -> p K+ K- p at Q = 108 MeV overestimates the data by a factor of 2.6, while the prediction for pp -> p K+ pi0 Sigma0 at Q = 212 MeV agrees reasonably well.

## Context

This work reviews and compares results from the COSY-TOF and COSY-ANKE spectrometers, extending earlier studies of strange meson production at COSY. The TOF results on pp -> K+ p Lambda (Abd El-Samad et al., 2010) provide evidence for both the Lambda p final state interaction and the N*(1650) isobar, while the ANKE results on pp -> K+ n Sigma+ (Valdau et al., 2010) contradict the anomalously large cross sections reported by COSY-11 (Rozek et al., 2006) and the assumptions made by the HIRES collaboration (Budzanowski et al., 2010). The data enable improved theoretical modeling of hyperon-nucleon final state interactions and isospin relations linking pp and pn channels, with future work planned on quasi-free hyperon production using deuterium targets at both facilities.
