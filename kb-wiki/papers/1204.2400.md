# Power Counting and Perturbative One Pion Exchange in Heavy Meson Molecules

> We discuss the possible power counting schemes that can be applied in the effective field theory description of heavy meson molecules, such as the X(3872) or the recently discovered Zb(10610) and Zb(10650) states. We argue that the effect of coupled channels is suppressed by at least two orders in the effective field theory expansion, meaning that they can be safely ignored at lowest order. The role of the one pion exchange potential between the heavy mesons, and in particular the tensor force, is also analyzed. By using techniques developed in atomic physics for handling power-law singular potentials, which have been also successfully employed in nuclear physics, we determine the range of center-of-mass momenta for which the tensor piece of the one pion exchange potential is perturbative. In this momentum range, the one pion exchange potential can be considered a subleading order correction, leaving at lowest order a very simple effective field theory consisting only on contact-range interactions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that for heavy meson molecules with sufficiently small binding energies, the one-pion exchange potential (OPE) can be treated as a perturbative correction rather than a non-perturbative interaction, leaving a simple contact-range effective field theory at leading order; the key condition is that the center-of-mass momentum must lie below a calculable breakdown scale that depends on the reduced mass and tensor force strength.

## Method

The author applies techniques from atomic physics for handling power-law singular potentials, originally developed by Cavagnero (1994) and Gao (1999) and extended to nuclear physics by Birse (2005), to analyze the tensor piece of the one-pion exchange potential in heavy meson-antimeson systems. By considering the OPE potential in the chiral limit where it reduces to a pure \(1/r^3\) potential, the breakdown scale for perturbative pions is extracted from the long-range solutions of the Schrödinger equation. The analysis is performed for the \(\mathrm{P^*\bar{P}}\) and \(\mathrm{P^*\bar{P}^*}\) systems in the charm and bottom sectors, using the heavy quark limit and the known values of the pion axial coupling, pion decay constant, and reduced masses.

## Key numbers

- For the \(\mathrm{D^*\bar{D}}\) system (charm sector), the wave number of the \(X(3872)\) bound state is \(Q = \sqrt{2\mu_R B} \sim \text{tens of MeV}\), with the binding energy \(B\) such that \(\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle} \sim 10\,\mathrm{fm}\).
- For the charged \(\mathrm{D^{*+}D^-}\) component of the \(X(3872)\), the soft scale is \(Q \sim 125\,\mathrm{MeV}\), of the order of the pion mass \(m_\pi = 138\,\mathrm{MeV}\).
- In the bottom sector, a binding energy of \(4\,\mathrm{MeV}\) corresponds to \(Q \sim m_\pi\) due to the large reduced mass.
- The coupled-channel momentum scale \(\Lambda_C\) is estimated as \(\Lambda_C \simeq 520\,\mathrm{MeV}\) for \(\mathrm{DD-DD^*}\) and \(\mathrm{DD^*-D^*D^*}\) pairs in the charm sector, and \(\Lambda_C \simeq 740\,\mathrm{MeV}\) for \(\mathrm{DD-D^*D^*}\); in the bottom sector, \(\Lambda_C \simeq 490\,\mathrm{MeV}\) and \(\Lambda_C \simeq 700\,\mathrm{MeV}\) respectively.
- The pion decay constant is \(f_\pi = 132\,\mathrm{MeV}\) and the pion mass is \(m_\pi = 138\,\mathrm{MeV}\).
- For the deuteron in the two-nucleon system, the wave number is \(\gamma = 45\,\mathrm{MeV}\) and the breakdown scale of the KSW counting is at most \(\Lambda_0 \sim 100\,\mathrm{MeV}\); the theory would not converge for binding energies of about \(4\,\mathrm{MeV}\) or more.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Birse (2005) on perturbative pions in the two-nucleon system to the case of heavy meson molecules, including the \(X(3872)\), \(Z_b(10610)\), and \(Z_b(10650)\) states. It builds on the KSW counting of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) and the X-EFT approach of Fleming, Stewart, and Mehen (2007), which treated pions perturbatively in the \(X(3872)\). The paper also references the Ericson and Karl (1993) argument about the relative strength of OPE in heavy meson molecules and the coupled-channel analysis of Nieves and Valderrama (2011). The results enable a systematic power counting for heavy meson molecules where pions are subleading, simplifying the EFT to contact-range interactions at leading order.
