# Momentum-kick model application to high multiplicity pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}=13\,\mathrm{TeV}$ at the LHC

> In this study, the momentum-kick model is used to understand the ridge behaviours in dihadron $Δη$--$Δφ$ correlations recently reported by the LHC in high-multiplicity proton-proton (pp) collisions. The kick stand model is based on a momentum kick by leading jets to partons in the medium close to the leading jets. The medium where partons move freely is assumed in the model regardless of collision systems. This helps us apply the method to small systems like pp collisions in a simple way. Also, the momentum transfer is purely kinematic and this provides us a strong way to approach the ridge behaviour analytically. There are already several results with this approach in high-energy heavy-ion collisions from the STAR and PHENIX at RHIC and from the CMS at LHC. The momentum-kick model is extended to the recent ridge results in high-multiplicity pp collisions with the ATLAS and CMS at LHC. The medium property in high-multiplicity pp collisions is diagnosed with the result of the model.

## Key claim

The momentum-kick model, previously validated for heavy-ion collisions, can explain the ridge behavior in high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at √s = 13 TeV, but only if the momentum kick parameter q is reduced from 2 GeV/c (used for minimum-bias pp at 7 TeV) to 1.2 GeV/c, indicating a denser medium at higher collision energies.

## Method

The authors apply the momentum-kick model, which assumes leading jets transfer a fractional momentum kick q to nearby partons in a medium created by Multiple Parton Interactions (MPIs), to dihadron Δη–Δφ correlation data from ATLAS and CMS. The model uses a thermal initial parton distribution characterized by a medium temperature T, and the final ridge yield is computed from the kinematic transformation of the kicked parton distribution plus a jet fragmentation component. The free parameters q and f_R⟨N_k⟩ (the product of survival factor and average number of kicked partons per jet) are fitted to the experimental ridge yields for trigger and associated particle transverse momenta in the ranges 0.5–5 GeV/c (ATLAS) and 1–4 GeV/c (CMS) with N_ch^offline > 120 (ATLAS) or > 135 (CMS).

## Key numbers

- Medium temperature T = 1.54 GeV for high-multiplicity pp at √s = 13 TeV, increased by about 300% relative to the RHIC Au–Au case at √s_NN = 200 GeV (T/T_PHENIX = 3.08)
- Best-fit momentum kick q = 1.2 GeV/c for high-multiplicity pp at √s = 13 TeV, compared to q = 2 GeV/c used for minimum-bias pp at √s = 7 TeV
- For CMS data with q = 1.2 GeV/c, f_R⟨N_k⟩ = 0.93 for 1 < p_T^trig < 2 GeV and increases to 1.37 for 2 < p_T^trig < 3 GeV
- For ATLAS data with q = 1.2 GeV/c, f_R⟨N_k⟩ = 1.0; with q = 2 GeV/c, f_R⟨N_k⟩ = 0.7
- For CMS data with q = 1.2 GeV/c, f_R⟨N_k⟩ = 0.74; with q = 2 GeV/c, f_R⟨N_k⟩ = 0.54

## Context

This work extends the momentum-kick model previously applied to Au–Au collisions at RHIC (Wong, 2009) and Pb–Pb collisions at LHC (Youn, 2017) to the recent ridge observations in high-multiplicity pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV reported by ATLAS (Aad, 2015) and CMS (Khachatryan, 2015). The model provides an alternative to hydrodynamic approaches (Werner, 2010; Bozek, 2012) and color reconnection models (Christiansen, 2015; Ortiz, 2013) for explaining long-range correlations in small collision systems. The results enable diagnosis of medium properties in high-multiplicity pp collisions and suggest that the average momentum kick decreases in denser media, consistent with trends observed in heavy-ion collisions.
