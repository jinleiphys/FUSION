# Coherent $γ^*$-nucleus scattering and coherent nuclear states

> In the context of a McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model for a large nucleus, coherent scattering of a virtual photon on that nucleus is evaluated in the $A_-=0$ gauge, the gauge appropriate for the target nucleus. The evaluation of the scattering in $A_-=0$ gauge is very intricate compared to the usual $A_+=0$ gauge evaluation natural for the scattering process, but has the advantage of directly giving the scattering in terms of a partonic description of the nucleus. In the limit where a tagged forward jet puts the dipole-nucleus scattering in the saturation regime the coherent reactions are equal to the inelastic reactions. In terms of the nuclear wave function the coherent reactions come from color singlet and zero total transverse momentum quark-antiquark pairs in the wave function and in the saturation regime the nuclear wave function is a coherent state for these pairs. In the saturation region half of all quarks (or antiquarks) come from zero momentum and color charge pairs.

## Key claim

Coherent elastic gamma*-nucleus scattering in the saturation regime corresponds to the nuclear wave function being a coherent state of color-singlet, zero-total-transverse-momentum quark-antiquark pairs, and in this regime the coherent and inelastic cross sections become equal, a duality that is made manifest by performing the calculation in A_- = 0 gauge rather than the usual A_+ = 0 gauge.

## Method

The author evaluates virtual photon-nucleus scattering in the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model using A_- = 0 gauge, which is unconventional for high-energy scattering but directly yields a partonic description of the target nucleus. The calculation is first performed in momentum space for single coherent scattering, then converted to coordinate space to handle multiple scattering, and the results are compared with the standard A_+ = 0 gauge calculations. The scattering graphs in A_- = 0 gauge are shown to be identical to the partonic graphs for the quark distribution of the target, allowing the author to extract saturated quark and antiquark distributions for both elastic and inelastic reactions.

## Key numbers

- Quark saturation momentum: Q_S^2 = (4 pi^2 alpha C_F)/(N_C^2 - 1) rho L xG, with xG evaluated at x_perp^2 = 4/Q_S^2.
- Elastic dipole-nucleus scattering amplitude: T(b, x_perp) = 1 - exp(-Q_S^2 x_perp^2/4).
- In the saturation regime where Q_S^2 x_perp^2/4 >> 1, sigma_in = sigma_el (coherent equals inelastic).
- In the saturation region, one-half of all quarks (and antiquarks) come from zero momentum and color charge pairs.

## Context

This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan model (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1994) for a large nucleus by providing a partonic interpretation of coherent scattering that is not accessible in the usual A_+ = 0 gauge. The calculation makes manifest the duality between reaching the unitarity limit in dipole-nucleus scattering and having the nuclear wave function become a coherent state of quark-antiquark pairs. This enables predictions for processes such as muon-pair production in nucleus-nucleus collisions, where the rate in the saturation region is four times larger than that predicted from quark distributions extracted solely from inelastic electron-nucleus scattering.
