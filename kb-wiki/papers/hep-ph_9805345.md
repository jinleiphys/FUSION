# Baryons in a chiral constituent quark model

> In the low-energy regime light and strange baryons should be considered as systems of constituent quarks with confining interaction and a chiral interaction that is mediated by Goldstone bosons as well as by vector and scalar mesons. The flavor-spin structure and sign of the short-range part of the spin-spin force reduces the $SU(6)_{FS}$ symmetry down to $SU(3)_F \times SU(2)_S$, induces hyperfine splittings and provides correct ordering of the lowest states with positive and negative parity. There is a cancellation of the tensor force from pseudoscalar- and vector-exchanges in baryons. The spin-orbit interactions from $ρ$-like and $ω$-like exchanges also cancel each other in baryons while they produce a big spin-orbit force in NN system. A unified description of light and strange baryon spectra calculated in a semirelativistic framework is presented. It is demonstrated that the same short-range part of spin-spin interaction between the constituent quarks induces a strong short-range repulsion in $NN$ system when the latter is treated as $6Q$ system. Thus one can achieve a simultaneous understanding of a baryon structure and baryon-baryon interaction in the low-energy regime.

## Key claim

Light and strange baryons in the low-energy regime should be understood as systems of constituent quarks whose residual interaction is mediated by Goldstone bosons, vector mesons, and scalar mesons, rather than by one-gluon exchange; the short-range part of the resulting flavor-spin interaction is the dominant force that explains both baryon spectroscopy and the short-range repulsion in the nucleon-nucleon system.

## Method

The authors construct a semirelativistic chiral constituent quark model with a linear confining interaction (string tension fixed to 1 GeV/fm from Regge slopes) and a chiral interaction mediated by pseudoscalar, scalar, and vector meson exchanges. The coupling constants are fixed from empirical pi-N, rho-N, and omega-N coupling constants, and the kinetic energy operator is taken in relativistic form. The three-quark Hamiltonian is solved variationally in momentum space using a stochastic method, with four free parameters (including cut-off parameters in monopole form factors) fitted to 35 confirmed low-lying baryon states. For the NN interaction, a nonrelativistic parametrization of the chiral interaction is used with an adiabatic Born-Oppenheimer approximation for the internucleon potential at zero separation.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses: M_u,d = 340 MeV, M_s = 500 MeV (not fitted, taken as typical values)
- Quark condensate order parameter: <|bar q q|> approx -(240-250 MeV)^3
- N-Delta splitting used to determine constant C_chi = 29.3 MeV
- Harmonic oscillator parameter hbar omega approx 250 MeV, determined from mass differences between first excited 1/2+ states and ground states (e.g., N(1440) - N)
- Predicted mass difference for N, Lambda, Sigma sectors: m(1/2+) - m(1/2- - 3/2-) = -102 MeV
- Predicted mass difference for Lambda system (Lambda(1600) vs Lambda(1405)-Lambda(1520)): m(1/2+) - m(1/2- - 3/2-) = 74 MeV
- Short-range NN repulsion at zero separation: V_NN(R=0) is highly repulsive in both ^3S_1 and ^1S_0 partial waves, with the core being of order 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model approach previously developed by Glozman and collaborators (GLO2, GPVW, GPVWVECTOR) and contrasts with the one-gluon-exchange (OGE) model, which the authors argue has no room in light baryon spectroscopy and is contradicted by lattice QCD calculations (Liu). The model solves the long-standing problem of the ordering of lowest positive- and negative-parity states (e.g., N(1440) below N(1535)), which could not be achieved with color-magnetic or instanton-induced hyperfine interactions. It enables a unified description of baryon structure and baryon-baryon interaction, predicting that the same short-range chiral interaction that produces hyperfine splittings also induces strong short-range repulsion in the NN system, with implications for YN and YY systems where the repulsion is expected to be weaker due to explicit chiral symmetry breaking.
