# Kurtosis of elliptic flow fluctuations

> Elliptic flow ($v_2$) in ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions fluctuates event to event, both in magnitude and in orientation with respect to the reaction plane. Even though the reaction plane is not known event to event in experiment, we show that the statistical properties of $v_2$ fluctuations in the reaction plane can be precisely extracted from experimental data. Previous studies have shown how to measure the mean, variance and skewness using the first three cumulants $v_2\{2\}$, $v_2\{4\}$ and $v_2\{6\}$. We complement these studies by providing a formula for the kurtosis, which requires an accurate determination of the next cumulant $v_2\{8\}$. Using existing data, we show that the kurtosis is positive for most centralities, in contrast with the kurtosis of triangular flow fluctuations, which is negative. We argue that these features are robust predictions of fluid-dynamical models.

## Key claim
The kurtosis of elliptic flow fluctuations along the reaction plane is positive for most centralities in Pb+Pb collisions, in contrast to the negative kurtosis of triangular flow fluctuations; this sign difference is argued to be a robust prediction of fluid-dynamical models, but the extraction requires high-precision data and the result is only statistically significant above 30% centrality.

## Method
The authors derive a formula expressing the standardized kurtosis of elliptic flow fluctuations in the reaction plane as a combination of the measured cumulants \(v_2\{4\}\), \(v_2\{6\}\), and \(v_2\{8\}\), valid for large systems (nucleus-nucleus collisions) to leading order in a \(1/N\) expansion. They test this formula using the TRENTo model of initial conditions and the Elliptic Power distribution, then apply it to existing CMS and ALICE data on Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02\) TeV.

## Key numbers
- The standardized kurtosis \(\gamma_2\) of initial eccentricity fluctuations in the TRENTo model (p=0) reaches unity at 60% centrality (Fig. 1).
- The skewness \(\gamma_1\) of elliptic flow fluctuations reaches \(-0.4\) at 60% centrality, confirming prior hydrodynamic predictions.
- For the Elliptic Power distribution, the asymptotic kurtosis is \(\gamma_2 = 6(12\varepsilon_0^2 - 1)/N + \mathcal{O}(1/N^2)\), changing sign when the mean eccentricity \(\varepsilon_0\) crosses approximately 0.28–0.29.
- The kurtosis extracted from CMS data is positive above 30% centrality (Fig. 3), with error bars estimated assuming independent errors on \(v_2\{8\}/v_2\{6\}\) and \(v_2\{6\}/v_2\{4\}\).
- The absolute difference between the exact kurtosis \(\gamma_2\) and the experimental estimate \(\gamma_2^{\rm expt}\) in the TRENTo model is of order 0.01 for all centralities.

## Context
This work extends prior studies that showed how to measure the mean, variance, and skewness of elliptic flow fluctuations using cumulants \(v_2\{2\}\), \(v_2\{4\}\), and \(v_2\{6\}\) (Giacalone 2016). It provides the first formula for extracting the kurtosis from \(v_2\{8\}\), complementing the analysis of Abbasi (2017) who found the kurtosis of elliptic flow in hydrodynamic calculations to be compatible with zero. The positive kurtosis observed in non-central collisions is interpreted as a consequence of large eccentricity in the reaction plane, and the reduction of non-Gaussianities by hydrodynamic evolution is highlighted as a probe of nonlinear response. The results enable future high-statistics LHC data to discriminate between models of initial conditions, particularly those inspired by high-energy QCD versus Glauber-type models.
