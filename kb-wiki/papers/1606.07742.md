# A kinetic regime of hydrodynamic fluctuations and long time tails for a Bjorken expansion

> We develop a set of kinetic equations for hydrodynamic fluctuations which are equivalent to nonlinear hydrodynamics with noise. The hydro-kinetic equations can be coupled to existing second order hydrodynamic codes to incorporate the physics of these fluctuations. We first show that the kinetic response precisely reproduces the renormalization of the shear viscosity and the fractional power ($\propto ω^{3/2}$) which characterizes equilibrium correlators of energy and momentum for a static fluid. Then we use the hydro-kinetic equations to analyze thermal fluctuations for a Bjorken expansion, evaluating the contribution of thermal noise from the earliest moments and at late times. In the Bjorken case, the solution to the kinetic equations determines the coefficient of the first fractional power of the gradient expansion ($\propto 1/(τT)^{3/2}$) for the expanding system. Numerically, we find that the contribution to the longitudinal pressure from hydrodynamic fluctuations is larger than second order hydrodynamics for typical medium parameters used to simulate heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper develops a kinetic description of hydrodynamic fluctuations that is equivalent to nonlinear fluctuating hydrodynamics, and shows that for a Bjorken expansion the contribution to longitudinal pressure from these fluctuations is larger than second-order viscous hydrodynamics for typical medium parameters used in heavy-ion collision simulations, with the qualification that this result applies specifically to the regime where the dissipative scale is well separated from both the inverse system size and the microscopic mean free path.

## Method

The authors linearize the equations of stochastic hydrodynamics around a static homogeneous background and a Bjorken-expanding background, then derive relaxation equations for the equal-time two-point correlation functions of energy and momentum density fluctuations in the eigenbasis of the ideal acoustic matrix. They solve these hydro-kinetic equations perturbatively for a static fluid with an external gravitational perturbation and non-perturbatively for the Bjorken expansion, using the solutions to evaluate the contribution of short-wavelength hydrodynamic modes to the stress tensor.

## Key numbers

- The fractional power in the retarded Green function for a static fluid is proportional to $\omega^{3/2}$ with coefficient $(1+i)\frac{(\frac{3}{2})^{3/2}+7}{240\pi} T \gamma_{\eta}^{-3/2}$, where $\gamma_{\eta} \equiv \eta/(e+p)$ is the momentum diffusion coefficient
- For Bjorken flow, the longitudinal pressure correction from hydrodynamic fluctuations is $\frac{1.08318}{s\, (4\pi \gamma_{\eta} \tau)^{3/2}}$ in units of $e+p$, where $s$ is the entropy density and $\tau$ is the proper time
- The dissipative scale for Bjorken flow is $k_{*} \sim 1/(\gamma_{\eta} \tau)^{1/2}$, where the viscous damping rate balances the expansion rate $1/\tau$
- The scale separation condition requires $\frac{1}{c_s \tau} \ll k_{*} \ll \frac{1}{\ell_{\rm mfp}}$, which holds when the hydrodynamic expansion parameter $\epsilon \equiv \omega \eta/((e+p)c_s^2) \ll 1$

## Context

This work extends the diagrammatic one-loop calculations of Kovtun, Moore, and Romatschke (2011) for equilibrium hydrodynamic fluctuations to the non-equilibrium setting of a Bjorken expansion, which is the standard model for the longitudinal expansion in heavy-ion collisions (Bjorken, 1983). The hydro-kinetic equations can be coupled to existing second-order hydrodynamic codes (Baier et al., 2008) to incorporate thermal fluctuation effects, and the results enable systematic inclusion of long-time tail corrections in realistic simulations of quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and the LHC, where only about 20000 particles are produced and thermal fluctuations may become significant.
