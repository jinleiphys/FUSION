# Nuclear thermodynamics from chiral low-momentum interactions

> We investigate the thermodynamic equation of state of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter with microscopic nuclear forces derived within the framework of chiral effective field theory. Two- and three-body nuclear interactions constructed at low resolution scales form the basis for a perturbative calculation of the finite-temperature equation of state. The nuclear force models and many-body methods are benchmarked against bulk properties of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature, which are found to be well reproduced when chiral nuclear interactions constructed at the lowest resolution scales are employed. The calculations are then extended to finite temperatures, where we focus on the liquid-gas phase transition and the associated critical point. The Maxwell construction is applied to construct the physical equation of state, and the value of the critical temperature is determined to be T_c =17.2-19.1 MeV, in good agreement with the value extracted from multifragmentation reactions of heavy ions.

## Key claim

The critical temperature of the liquid-gas phase transition in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter is determined to be \(T_c = 17.2-19.1\) MeV from chiral low-momentum interactions, but this range depends on the specific choice of resolution scale and low-energy constants in the two- and three-body potentials, and the result is obtained via the Kohn-Luttinger-Ward formalism rather than a direct grand canonical calculation.

## Method

The authors performed a perturbative calculation of the finite-temperature equation of state for isospin-symmetric nuclear matter using chiral effective field theory two- and three-body interactions constructed at low resolution scales (414, 450, and 500 MeV, plus two \(V_{\text{low-}k}\) potentials at 2.1 and 2.3 fm\(^{-1}\)). They employed many-body perturbation theory within the Kohn-Luttinger-Ward formalism to ensure thermodynamic consistency between the zero-temperature Brueckner-Goldstone expansion and the finite-temperature grand canonical potential, including first-order two- and three-body contributions and second-order normal and anomalous contributions, with the Maxwell construction applied to obtain the physical equation of state across the liquid-gas phase transition.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature range: \(T_c = 17.2-19.1\) MeV for isospin-symmetric nuclear matter, determined from five different sets of chiral low-momentum interactions.
- Saturation density of nuclear matter: \(\rho_0 \simeq 0.17\) fm\(^{-3}\) (used as reference density).
- Resolution scales for chiral two-nucleon potentials: \(\Lambda = 414, 450, 500\) MeV (smooth regulator) and \(\Lambda = 2.1, 2.3\) fm\(^{-1}\) (sharp cutoff for \(V_{\text{low-}k}\)).
- Low-energy constants for the n3lo500 potential: \(c_E = -0.205\), \(c_D = -0.20\), \(c_1 = -0.81\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_3 = -3.2\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_4 = 5.4\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
- Low-energy constants for the VLK21 potential: \(c_E = -0.625\), \(c_D = -2.062\), \(c_1 = -0.76\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_3 = -4.78\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c_4 = 3.96\) GeV\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends previous zero-temperature studies of nuclear matter from chiral effective field theory (e.g., Hebeler 2010, Tews 2012, Holt 2013) to finite temperatures, focusing on the liquid-gas phase transition and critical point. It provides a microscopic foundation for the nuclear equation of state needed in astrophysical simulations of core-collapse supernovae and neutron star mergers, complementing earlier finite-temperature studies using phenomenological Skyrme forces (Mosel) and in-medium chiral perturbation theory (Fiorilla 2011). The results enable future investigations of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperatures relevant for astrophysical applications.
