# Equations of state and compact stars in gauge/gravity duality

> We propose a new doorway to study the interplay between equations of state of dense matter and compact stars in gauge/gravity correspondence. For this we construct a bulk geometry near the boundary of five-dimensional spacetime. By solving a constraint equation derived from the bulk equation of motion together with the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation, we determine the equations of state for compact stars. The input parameters in this study are the energy density and pressure at the center of the compact objects. We also study how the equation of state depends on the parameters.

## Key claim

The authors propose that by solving a constraint equation derived from the five-dimensional bulk Einstein equations near the boundary, together with the four-dimensional Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations, one can determine the equation of state (EOS) of dense matter in compact stars rather than using the EOS as an input. The EOS becomes an output of the holographic construction, but only under the assumption that the energy density at the stellar surface is zero, which reduces the input parameter space to the central energy density and central pressure.

## Method

The authors construct a five-dimensional bulk metric in Fefferman-Graham coordinates near the AdS boundary, with a static spherically symmetric boundary metric describing a perfect-fluid star. They solve the five-dimensional vacuum Einstein equations with a negative cosmological constant (pure gravity case) and then generalize to include a bulk scalar field with mass m_phi^2 = -3/L^2. By expanding the bulk equations order by order in the holographic coordinate z, they derive a constraint equation at O(z^2) that, when combined with the TOV equations (mass function, hydrostatic equilibrium, and the f'(r) relation), yields a differential equation for the energy density rho'(r). For the scalar field case, they also solve the scalar field equation of motion to obtain coupled equations for rho(r), P(r), and the scalar source phi_1(r).

## Key numbers

* For a non-uniform density star with central density rho_c = 2.5713 x 10^9 MeV^4, central pressure P_c = 3.2141 x 10^8 MeV^4, and phi_1(0) = 0.2703, the resulting star has radius 11.45 km and mass 1.26 solar masses, with surface energy density 3.26 x 10^8 MeV^4.
* For the same central density and pressure but with the surface density set to zero, the star has radius 11.66 km and mass 1.26 solar masses.
* For the ratio P_c = (1/3) rho_c, the maximum mass in the table is 2.43 solar masses (at central density 3 tilde-rho, where tilde-rho = 8.5704 x 10^8 MeV^4) with radius 13.33 km; the minimum mass is 1.33 solar masses (at central density 10 tilde-rho) with radius 7.30 km.
* For P_c = (1/8) rho_c, the maximum mass is 1.26 solar masses (at central density 3 tilde-rho) with radius 11.66 km; the minimum mass is 0.69 solar masses (at central density 10 tilde-rho) with radius 6.39 km.

## Context

This work extends the application of gauge/gravity duality (AdS/CFT) to gravitationally bound compact stars, building on prior holographic studies of stellar objects by de Boer (2009), Arsiwalla (2010), Parente (2010), and Kim (2011). The authors introduce a finite UV-cutoff to couple the boundary conformal field theory to four-dimensional gravity, following the generalized AdS/CFT framework of Gubser (1999) and Hawking (2000). This approach enables the determination of the EOS from the holographic constraint rather than from flat-space nuclear physics, and it opens the possibility of computing the full bulk metric throughout the five-dimensional spacetime in future work.
