# Airy structure in $^{16}$O+$^{14}$C nuclear rainbow scattering

> The Airy structure in $^{16}$O+$^{14}$C rainbow scattering is studied with an extended double folding (EDF) model that describes all the diagonal and off-diagonal coupling potentials derived from the microscopic realistic wave functions for $^{16}$O using a density-dependent nucleon-nucleon force. The experimental angular distributions at $E_L$=132, 281 and 382.2 MeV are well reproduced by the calculations. By studying the energy evolution of the Airy structure, the Airy minimum at around $θ$=76$^\circ$ in the angular distribution at $E_L$=132 MeV is assigned as the second order Airy minimum $A2$ in contrast to the recent literature which assigns it as the third order $A3$. The Airy minima in the 90$^\circ$ excitation function is investigated in comparison with well-known $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O and $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C systems. Evolution of the Airy structure into the molecular resonances with the $^{16}$O+$^{14}$C cluster structure in the low energy region around $E_{c.m.}$=30 MeV is discussed. It is predicted theoretically for the first time for a non-$4N$ $^{16}$O+$^{14}$C system that Airy elephants in the 90$^\circ$ excitation function are present.

## Key claim

The Airy minimum at around θ=76° in the angular distribution of 16O+14C scattering at EL=132 MeV is assigned as the second order Airy minimum A2, contradicting the recent literature which assigned it as the third order A3. This reassignment is based on studying the energy evolution of the Airy structure over a wide range of incident energies, not merely on the shape similarity of angular distributions between 16O+14C and 16O+12C systems.

## Method

The authors used an extended double folding (EDF) model that describes all diagonal and off-diagonal coupling potentials derived from microscopic realistic wave functions for 16O using a density-dependent nucleon-nucleon force (DDM3Y-FR). They performed both single channel double folding calculations and coupled channel calculations including transition densities for the 3- (6.13 MeV) and 2+ (6.92 MeV) excited states of 16O, with a phenomenological Woods-Saxon imaginary potential. The order of Airy minima was determined by calculating angular distributions with the imaginary potential switched off and tracking the energy evolution of the Airy structure between EL=116 and 281 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The experimental angular distributions at EL=132, 281, and 382.2 MeV are well reproduced by the calculations.
- At EL=382.2 MeV, the first order Airy minimum A1 is observed at θ=30°; the darkside of the rainbow starts beyond θ=40°.
- At EL=281 MeV, the A1 minimum appears at θ=45° and the A2 minimum at θ=30°.
- At EL=132 MeV, the Airy minimum at θ=76° is assigned as A2, with A3 observed at θ=50° and a remnant of A1 at around θ=120°.
- The volume integral per nucleon pair JV for the real potential is 285 MeV fm3 at 132 MeV, 273 MeV fm3 at 281 MeV, and 254 MeV fm3 at 382.2 MeV (single channel calculations).
- For the 90° excitation function, the A1 minimum is predicted to appear at EL=158 MeV and the A2 minimum at EL=116 MeV for the 16O+14C system.
- The energy spacing between A1 and A2 minima is about 20 MeV for 16O+14C, similar to the 16O+16O system.
- Molecular resonances with the 16O+14C cluster structure have been reported at Ec.m.=23.4, 27.4, and 31.05 MeV (Freeman 1992) and at Ec.m.=18.2, 19.1, 22.9, and 23.8 MeV (Abbondanno 1990).

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear rainbow scattering from the well-characterized 16O+16O system (Ohkubo 2002) and the 16O+12C system (Ohkubo 2014) to the asymmetric non-4N system 16O+14C. It corrects the previous Airy minimum assignment by Glukhov (2007), who had assigned the θ=76° minimum as A3 based on similarity to the 16O+12C system. The paper predicts for the first time that Airy elephants (gross structures in the 90° excitation function) exist for a non-4N system, and suggests that the highest order Airy minima will evolve into molecular resonances with 16O+14C cluster structure around Ec.m.=30 MeV, similar to the behavior observed in 16O+16O (Ohkubo 2002). Experimental study of 16O+14C elastic scattering below EL=132 MeV and above 65 MeV is needed to connect the Airy structures to molecular resonances.
