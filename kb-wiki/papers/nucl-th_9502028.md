# Constituent Quark Model Calculation for a possible J^P=0^-,T=0 Dibaryon

> There exists experimental evidence that a dibaryon resonance d' with quantum numbers J^P=0^-,T=0 and mass 2065 MeV could be the origin of the narrow peak in the (π^+ ,π^- ) double charge exchange cross--sections on nuclei. We investigate the six--quark system with these quantum--numbers within the constituent quark model, with linear confinement, effective one--gluon exchange at short range and chiral interactions between quarks (πand σexchange). We classify all possible six quark states with J^P=0^-,T=0, and with N=1 and N=3 harmonic oscillator excitations, using different reduction chains. The six--quark Hamiltonian is diagonalized in the basis including the unique N=1 state and the 10 most important states from the N=3 shell. We find, that with most of the possible sets of parameters, the mass of such a "dibaryon" lies above the N(939)+N^\ast(1535) threshold. The only possibility to describe the supposed d'(2065) in the present context is to reduce the confinement strength to very small values, however at the expense of describing the negative parity resonances N^\ast. We also analyze the J^P=0^-,T=2,N=1 six--quark state.

## Key claim

The constituent quark model calculation finds that a J^P=0^-, T=0 six-quark state with mass near the experimentally suggested d'(2065) can only be obtained by reducing the confinement strength to very small values, but this comes at the expense of failing to describe the negative parity N* resonances; with parameter sets that reproduce the baryon spectrum, the calculated dibaryon mass lies 100-200 MeV above the N(939)+N*(1535) threshold.

## Method

The authors diagonalize a six-quark Hamiltonian containing linear confinement, effective one-gluon exchange, and chiral interactions (π and σ exchange) in a translationally invariant harmonic oscillator shell model basis. They classify all six-quark states with J^P=0^-, T=0 for N=1 and N=3 harmonic oscillator excitations, then diagonalize in a basis consisting of the unique N=1 state plus the 10 most important N=3 states selected for their symmetric color-spin Young patterns. Parameters are fitted to the nucleon, Δ(1232), and N*(1535) masses using the nucleon stability condition.

## Key numbers

- The experimentally suggested d' dibaryon mass is 2065 MeV, with a width of approximately 5 MeV
- With parameter sets that describe the baryon spectrum, the calculated J^P=0^-, T=0 six-quark mass lies 100-200 MeV above the N(939)+N*(1535) threshold
- Reducing the confinement strength to approximately 25 MeV/fm (set V) yields a dibaryon mass close to 2065 MeV with an oscillator length b_6 ≈ 1.25 fm
- The pion kinetic energy of the narrow peak in double charge exchange reactions is T_π = 50 MeV
- The chiral symmetry breaking cutoff Λ is taken in the range 0.7-1.5 GeV
- The nucleon quark core radius b_N is taken in the range 0.45-0.6 fm
- The constituent quark mass m_q is taken in the range 200-400 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier constituent quark model studies of the six-quark system (Wagner et al., 1994) that used only the lowest s^5p shell-model state, by including configuration mixing with 10 excited N=3 states. It contrasts with string-like bag model calculations (Mulders et al., 1980; KMS, 1980) that lack antisymmetrization between quarks in different clusters, which the authors argue is essential at the expected 1 fm scale. The results suggest that confirmation of the d' resonance would provide constraints on the nature of confinement in multi-quark systems, as the extension of baryon confinement to six-quark systems is not straightforward.
