# Constraints on Skyrme Equations of State from Doubly Magic Nuclei, Ab-Initio Calculations of Low-Density Neutron Matter, and Neutron Stars

> We use properties of doubly-magic nuclei, ab-initio calculations of low-density neutron matter, and of neutron stars to constrain the parameters of the Skyrme energy-density functional. We find all of these properties can be reproduced within a constrained family of Skyrme parameters. The maximum mass of a neutron star is found to be sensitive to the neutron effective mass. A value of [$ m^{*}_{\rm n}/m](ρ_0) = 0.60-0.65 $ is required to obtain a maximum neutron star mass of 2.1 solar masses. Using the constrained Skyrme functional with the aforementioned effective mass, the predicted radius for a neutron star of 1.4 solar masses is 12.4(1) km and $Λ$ = 423(40).

## Key claim

The neutron effective mass at saturation density must be reduced from 0.85 to 0.60-0.65 to reconcile Skyrme energy-density functionals that already fit nuclear data and low-density neutron matter with the observed maximum neutron star mass of about 2.1 solar masses; this change has little effect on the quality of fits to nuclear data or the low-density neutron equation of state.

## Method

The authors started with 12 Skyrme energy-density functionals previously refit to binding energies, charge radii, and single-particle energies of doubly magic nuclei, as well as to ab-initio calculations of low-density neutron matter up to 0.04 neutrons/fm³. They then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for neutron star structure and tidal deformability, and refit the Skyrme parameters to additionally require that the maximum neutron star mass reach about 2.1 solar masses, which forced a reduction in the neutron effective mass at saturation density.

## Key numbers

- Neutron effective mass at saturation density: [m*_n/m](ρ₀) = 0.60-0.65 required to obtain a maximum neutron star mass of 2.1 solar masses
- Predicted radius for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: 12.4(1) km
- Predicted dimensionless tidal deformability Λ for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: 423(40)
- Density derivative of symmetry energy at ρ₀ = 0.16 nucleons/fm³: L = 65(7) MeV
- Neutron skin of ²⁰⁸Pb: R_skin = 0.194(7) fm
- Neutron skin of ⁴⁸Ca: R_skin = 0.178(3) fm
- Symmetric nuclear matter incompressibility range for the 12 functionals: K₀ = 212-242 MeV
- rms deviation for binding energies of seven doubly magic nuclei: 0.6 to 0.9 MeV
- rms deviation for charge radii of four nuclei: 0.015 to 0.024 fm

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of Brown (2013) which constrained Skyrme parameters using doubly magic nuclei and ab-initio low-density neutron matter, but left the neutron effective mass as the largest remaining uncertainty. By adding the constraint from the 2.01(4) solar mass neutron star observed by Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis (2013), and from the gravitational wave event GW170817 reported by the LIGO Scientific Collaboration (2017), the authors narrow the neutron effective mass range and produce tighter predictions for neutron star radii and tidal deformability. The results enable more precise predictions for neutron star thermal properties such as heat capacity and neutrino luminosity, which are important for understanding neutron star cooling mechanisms.
