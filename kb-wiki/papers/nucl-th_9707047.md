# Probing proton halos through pion photoproduction

> Charged pion photoproduction is proposed as a new method to study halo nuclei. In particular, it is demonstrated that the reaction 17O(gamma,pi^-)17F(1/2+, 0.495 MeV) is well-suited to investigate the well-known proton halo in 17F which is not amenable to radioactive beam experiments. The cross sections are shown to be very sensitive to the halo structure of the loosely bound proton.

## Key claim

Charged pion photoproduction on a stable nucleus can probe the proton halo in the first excited state of 17F, a halo that cannot be studied via radioactive beam experiments; the cross sections are shown to be very sensitive to the halo structure, but the authors stress this investigation is exploratory and meant to stimulate theoretical discussion and experimental efforts.

## Method

The authors calculated differential cross sections for the reaction 17O(gamma,pi-)17F using both harmonic oscillator (HO) and Woods-Saxon (WS) single-particle wave functions for the valence proton, with the WS potential tuned to reproduce the 105 keV separation energy of the 1s1/2 proton in the excited state. The Blomqvist-Laget elementary pion photoproduction amplitude was used, and the outgoing pion distortion was treated with the Stricker-McManus-Carr optical potential in a distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA). The nuclear structure was described by pure single-particle transitions (0d5/2 to 0d5/2 for the ground state, 0d5/2 to 1s1/2 for the excited state) outside a closed 16O core.

## Key numbers

- The separation energy of the valence proton in the first excited 1/2+ state of 17F is 105 keV.
- The r.m.s. radius of the Woods-Saxon wave function for the 1s1/2 proton in 17F* is 5.1 fm.
- The ground state cross section at forward angles is around 3 microbarn/sr, decreasing to about 1 microbarn/sr at backward angles for E_gamma = 200 MeV.
- Using the halo WS wave function instead of the standard HO density reduces the excited state cross section at large angles by a factor of two.
- The pion final state interaction affects the cross section by at most 10 percent.
- The M3 multipole provides about 70 percent of the cross section for the excited state transition.
- The binding energy for the WS wave function of the ground state proton was set to 600 keV.

## Context

This work extends the study of halo nuclei beyond neutron-rich systems like 11Be and 11Li, which have been studied via heavy-ion collisions, to proton halos that are difficult to access with radioactive beams due to the Coulomb barrier. The authors propose charged pion photoproduction as an alternative to strong-interaction charge-exchange reactions [(p,n), (n,p), (pi+-,pi0)] for probing halo states, noting that the electromagnetic probe can largely preserve the momenta of valence nucleons. The paper builds on prior evidence for the 17F proton halo from the large Thomas-Ehrman Coulomb shift (Nolen and Schiffer, 1969; Sherr and Bertsch, 1985), the large B(E2) value (Brown, Arima, and McGrory, 1977), and the mirror asymmetry in first-forbidden beta decay (Borge et al., 1993), while noting that Millener (1997) argued these differences could be explained by charge-dependent effects. The reaction 17O(gamma,pi-)17F is presented as a cleaner case than comparable processes leading to neutron-halo nuclei such as 11B(gamma,pi+)11Be or 15N(gamma,pi+)15C.
