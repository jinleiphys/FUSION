# nNNPDF2.0: Quark Flavor Separation in Nuclei from LHC Data

> We present a model-independent determination of the nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs) using machine learning methods and Monte Carlo techniques based on the NNPDF framework. The neutral-current deep-inelastic nuclear structure functions used in our previous analysis, nNNPDF1.0, are complemented by inclusive and charm-tagged cross-sections from charged-current scattering. Furthermore, we include all available measurements of W and Z leptonic rapidity distributions in proton-lead collisions from ATLAS and CMS at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV and 8.16 TeV. The resulting nPDF determination, nNNPDF2.0, achieves a good description of all datasets. In addition to quantifying the nuclear modifications affecting individual quarks and antiquarks, we examine the implications for strangeness, assess the role that the momentum and valence sum rules play in nPDF extractions, and present predictions for representative phenomenological applications. Our results, made available via the LHAPDF library, highlight the potential of high-energy collider measurements to probe nuclear dynamics in a robust manner.

## Key claim

The nNNPDF2.0 determination achieves a model-independent extraction of nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs) that separates individual quark and antiquark flavors in nuclei, enabled by the inclusion of charged-current DIS data and LHC electroweak boson production measurements, but the separation relies on the assumption that the free-proton boundary condition from NNPDF3.1 is valid and that non-isoscalar corrections for targets like lead are properly accounted for.

## Method

The analysis uses the NNPDF machine learning framework with a single neural network parameterizing six evolution-basis nPDF combinations (Σ, T₃, T₈, V, V₃, g) as functions of x and atomic mass number A, with preprocessing exponents fitted simultaneously. The fit includes neutral-current nuclear structure function ratios from fixed-target experiments, inclusive and charm-tagged charged-current neutrino DIS cross-sections from CHORUS and NuTeV, and W/Z leptonic rapidity distributions from ATLAS and CMS in proton-lead collisions at √s = 5.02 TeV and 8.16 TeV, all evaluated at NLO in QCD using APFEL and MCFM with FONLL heavy quark schemes.

## Key numbers

- Total dataset contains n_dat = 1467 data points after kinematic cuts of Q² = 3.5 GeV² and W² = 12.5 GeV²
- CHORUS provides 846 data points for inclusive neutrino and anti-neutrino double-differential cross-sections on lead
- NuTeV provides 39 neutrino and 37 anti-neutrino di-muon cross-section data points from neutrino-iron scattering
- CMS Run II W⁺/W⁻ data at √s = 8.16 TeV contributes 48 data points based on integrated luminosity ℒ = 173 nb⁻¹ (compared to ℒ = 34.6 nb⁻¹ for Run I)
- ATLAS Z rapidity distributions at √s = 5.02 TeV contribute 14 data points; CMS W⁺/W⁻ at 5.02 TeV contribute 20 data points; CMS Z at 5.02 TeV contribute 12 data points
- Neural network has 256 free parameters with one hidden layer of 25 neurons
- Parameterization scale Q₀ = 1 GeV; strong coupling α_S(m_Z) = 0.118; charm mass m_c = 1.51 GeV; bottom mass m_b = 4.92 GeV

## Context

This work extends the nNNPDF1.0 analysis (Khalek et al., 2019) by adding charged-current DIS data from CHORUS and NuTeV, and LHC electroweak boson production data from ATLAS and CMS, which together enable quark flavor separation that was impossible with only NC DIS data. The approach contrasts with the EPPS16 analysis, which used forward-to-backward ratios of LHC data rather than absolute rapidity distributions. The resulting nNNPDF2.0 set, available via LHAPDF, enables future studies of nuclear modifications to individual quark flavors and provides predictions for LHC observables sensitive to nuclear dynamics.
