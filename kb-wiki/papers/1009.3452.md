# Microscopically-based energy density functionals for nuclei using the density matrix expansion: Implementation and pre-optimization

> In a recent series of papers, Gebremariam, Bogner, and Duguet derived a microscopically based nuclear energy density functional by applying the Density Matrix Expansion (DME) to the Hartree-Fock energy obtained from chiral effective field theory (EFT) two- and three-nucleon interactions. Due to the structure of the chiral interactions, each coupling in the DME functional is given as the sum of a coupling constant arising from zero-range contact interactions and a coupling function of the density arising from the finite-range pion exchanges. Since the contact contributions have essentially the same structure as those entering empirical Skyrme functionals, a microscopically guided Skyrme phenomenology has been suggested in which the contact terms in the DME functional are released for optimization to finite-density observables to capture short-range correlation energy contributions from beyond Hartree-Fock. The present paper is the first attempt to assess the ability of the newly suggested DME functional, which has a much richer set of density dependencies than traditional Skyrme functionals, to generate sensible and stable results for nuclear applications. The results of the first proof-of-principle calculations are given, and numerous practical issues related to the implementation of the new functional in existing Skyrme codes are discussed. Using a restricted singular value decomposition (SVD) optimization procedure, it is found that the new DME functional gives numerically stable results and exhibits a small but systematic reduction of our test $χ^2$ function compared to standard Skyrme functionals, thus justifying its suitability for future global optimizations and large-scale calculations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a semi-phenomenological nuclear energy density functional derived from the Density Matrix Expansion (DME) applied to chiral effective field theory interactions can be implemented in existing Skyrme codes and yields numerically stable results, with a small but systematic reduction of the test chi-squared function compared to standard Skyrme functionals, but the authors stress that a detailed comparison against experimental data is premature before applying a more rigorous global optimization.

## Method

The authors constructed a DME-based functional where the coupling constants from zero-range contact interactions in chiral EFT are released for optimization to finite-density observables, while the density-dependent coupling functions arising from finite-range pion exchanges are kept fixed. They constrained the zero-range volume parameters to reasonable values for equilibrium characteristics of infinite nuclear matter, and obtained the zero-range surface parameters through a restricted singular value decomposition (SVD) optimization procedure using binding energies of 72 even-even nuclei and 8 odd-even mass differences (4 neutron and 4 proton). The functional was implemented in a FORTRAN 90 module designed to be portable to existing Skyrme EDF solvers.

## Key numbers

- The optimization used binding energies of 72 even-even nuclei and 8 odd-even mass differences (4 neutron and 4 proton) as the fitting dataset.
- The parameter gamma in the contact part of the functional typically takes values between 1/6 and 1/3 in standard Skyrme parameterizations.
- The local Fermi momentum is defined as k_F(R) = (3*pi^2/2 * rho_0(R))^(1/3), relating the DME expansion scale to the isoscalar density.
- The contact part of the functional contains 13 parameters: {C_t0^(rho^2), C_tD^(rho^2), C_t^(rho*Delta*rho), C_t^(rho*tau), C_t^(J^2), C_t^(rho*grad*J), gamma}.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework developed by Gebremariam, Bogner, and Duguet (2009, 2010), who derived a microscopically based nuclear energy density functional by applying the DME to chiral EFT two- and three-nucleon interactions through N^2LO. The paper provides the first proof-of-principle implementation and pre-optimization of this functional, enabling future global optimizations and large-scale EDF calculations across the nuclear mass table. The approach is part of the UNEDF SciDAC-2 collaboration effort to develop nuclear energy functionals with reduced global errors and improved predictive power for applications to radioactive isotope beam facilities and astrophysical systems.
