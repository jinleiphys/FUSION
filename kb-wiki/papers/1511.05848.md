# Simultaneous occurrence of distinct symmetries in nuclei

> We show that distinct emergent symmetries, such as partial dynamical symmetry and quasi dynamical symmetry, can occur simultaneously in the same or different eigenstates of the Hamiltonian. Implications for nuclear spectroscopy in the rare-earth region and for first-order quantum phase transitions between spherical and deformed shapes, are considered.

# Simultaneous occurrence of distinct symmetries in nuclei

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that partial dynamical symmetry (PDS) and quasi dynamical symmetry (QDS), previously considered unrelated, can occur simultaneously in the same eigenstates or in different eigenstates of the same Hamiltonian, specifically within the interacting boson model (IBM) framework for nuclei that are not at any exact dynamical symmetry limit.

## Method

The study uses the extended consistent-Q formalism (ECQF) of the interacting boson model, with Hamiltonian parameters ξ and χ that interpolate between the U(5), O(6), and SU(3) dynamical symmetry limits. For ground-band states, the degree of O(6) symmetry is quantified by fluctuations Δσ_L in the O(6) quantum number σ, computed from wave-function expansions in the O(6) basis. For shape-coexistence at a first-order quantum phase transition, Peres lattices and Shannon entropies are used to classify eigenstates as regular or chaotic, and to identify U(5) or SU(3) purity versus coherent mixing.

## Key numbers

- At the SU(3) dynamical symmetry limit (ξ=1, χ=-√7/2), ground-state O(6) fluctuations are Δσ₀ ≈ 1.25 (for N=14).
- At the U(5) dynamical symmetry limit (ξ=0), ground-state O(6) fluctuations saturate at Δσ₀ ≈ 2.47 (for N=14).
- In the valley of low Δσ₀, fluctuations are two orders of magnitude lower than at saturation (order 10⁻²).
- For ¹⁶⁰Gd (N=14, ξ=0.84, χ=-0.53), the fraction of O(6) basis states with σ=N in the ground state is 99.1%, with Δσ₀=0.19.
- For ¹⁶²Dy (N=15, ξ=0.92, χ=-0.31), the σ=N fraction reaches 99.9% with Δσ₀=0.07.
- For the critical-point Hamiltonian Ĥ_cri, the 2⁺₂ state has a 96% n_d=1 component; the L=0⁺₂ state is a pure n_d=0 state with U(5) Shannon entropy S_U5=0.

## Context

This work extends the concepts of partial dynamical symmetry (PDS), introduced by Leviatan (2011), and quasi dynamical symmetry (QDS), discussed by Bahri and Rowe (2000) and Rowe (2004), by showing they can coexist. It builds on extensive ECQF fits to rare-earth nuclei by McCutchan and Zamfir (2004) and on prior studies of O(6)-PDS in rare-earth nuclei by Kremer et al. (2014). The results enable identification of candidate nuclei with approximate O(6)-PDS in the ground band while simultaneously exhibiting SU(3)-QDS, and demonstrate that emergent symmetries can characterize regular patterns amidst chaotic environments in quantum shape-phase transitions.
