# Glueball Decay in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto Model and Finite Quark Masses

> We discuss recent results on the calculation of glueball decay rates in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, which favor the $f_0(1710)$ meson as a glueball candidate. The flavor asymmetric decay of $f_0(1710)$ is frequently attributed to a putative chiral suppression in glueball decays, which is however questionable in view of the large constituent quark masses induced by chiral symmetry breaking. We find that this can be explained by what we call nonchiral enhancement when finite quark masses are included in the holographic model, with good quantitative agreement with experimental data for $f_0(1710)$. Assuming the latter to indeed be a nearly pure glueball, the model makes essentially parameter-free and thus falsifiable predictions for its decay rates involving vector mesons and an upper limit on the $ηη'$ decay rate.

# Glueball Decay in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto Model and Finite Quark Masses

## Key claim

The Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model with finite quark masses can explain the flavor-asymmetric decay pattern of the $f_0(1710)$ meson, specifically its preferential decay into kaons and $\eta$ mesons over pions, through a mechanism the authors call "nonchiral enhancement," without requiring the glueball to have significant $q\bar q$ admixture. This agreement is contingent on assuming the $f_0(1710)$ is a nearly pure glueball and on a particular symmetric coupling between the glueball and the mass terms of pseudoscalar fields.

## Method

The authors use the top-down holographic Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, a string-theoretic realization of gauge/gravity duality, to compute scalar glueball decay rates into pairs of pseudoscalar mesons. They extend the flavor-symmetric model to include finite quark masses via a nonlocal mass term that couples the glueball mode to the pseudoscalar fields, and they diagonalize the resulting mass terms for the full nonet of pseudoscalar bosons to obtain physical $\eta$ and $\eta'$ masses and mixing angles.

## Key numbers

- Glueball mass in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model (dilatonic mode): 1487 MeV, compared to the $f_0(1710)$ mass which is 16% higher.
- Holographic result for $\Gamma(G\to\pi\pi)/M$ with vanishing quark masses: 0.009 to 0.012.
- Predicted ratio $\frac{4}{3} \cdot \Gamma(\pi\pi)/\Gamma(K\bar K)$ for $f_0(1710)$: 0.463, compared to experimental value $0.55^{+0.15}_{-0.23}$.
- Predicted ratio $4 \cdot \Gamma(\eta\eta)/\Gamma(K\bar K)$: 1.12, compared to experimental value $1.92 \pm 0.60$.
- Pseudoscalar mixing angle $\theta_P$ from the model: $-14.4^\circ$ to $-24.2^\circ$, with central value approximately $-19^\circ$ consistent with the PDG determination $(-18 \pm 2)^\circ$ from $\Gamma(\eta'\to2\gamma)/\Gamma(\eta\to2\gamma)$.
- Witten-Veneziano mass $m_0$ for $N_f=N_c=3$, $\MKK=949$ MeV, and $\lambda$ from 16.63 to 12.55: 967 to 730 MeV.
- Predicted ratio $\Gamma(G\to4\pi)/\Gamma(G\to2\pi) \approx 2.5$ for a pure glueball of mass 1.7 GeV.
- Predicted ratio $\Gamma(G\to2\omega)/\Gamma(G\to2\pi) \approx 1.1$ for a pure glueball of mass 1.7 GeV.
- Upper limit prediction for $\Gamma(\eta\eta')/\Gamma(\pi\pi) \lesssim 0.04$, assuming $f_0(1710)$ is a pure glueball and current $\Gamma(\pi\pi)/\Gamma(K\bar K)$ data hold.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of glueball decay in the massless Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model (Brunner, Parganlija, Rebhan 2015) by incorporating finite quark masses, which break SU(3)$_f$ flavor symmetry. It addresses the long-standing puzzle of why the $f_0(1710)$ decays preferentially into kaons and $\eta$ mesons rather than pions, contrary to the expectation of flavor-blind glueball decays. The results contradict the "chiral suppression" mechanism proposed by Carlson (1980), Sexton (1995), and Chanowitz (2005), which would require decay amplitudes proportional to quark masses; instead, the authors find that finite quark masses produce an enhancement of decays into heavier pseudoscalars. The model makes falsifiable predictions for decays into vector mesons and the $\eta\eta'$ channel that can be tested by experiments such as BESIII and CMS/TOTEM.
