# Hyperon interaction with dense nuclear matter and link to neutron stars

> The theoretical status of the hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions is reviewed, paying a special attention to chiral effective field theories. Results on hyperons in dense matter are presented and the consequences for the properties of neutron stars are analyzed.

## Key claim
This review argues that chiral effective field theory (χEFT) provides a systematic framework for describing hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions, but the resulting in-medium hyperon single-particle potentials depend sensitively on how SU(3) symmetry is implemented in the contact terms, as shown by the differences between the NLO13 and NLO19 interactions.

## Method
The author surveys theoretical approaches to hyperon-nucleon (YN) and hyperon-hyperon (YY) interactions, focusing on χEFT where the potential is expanded in powers of the pion momentum. The YN interaction at leading order (LO) includes single pseudoscalar-meson exchanges and non-derivative contact terms, while next-to-leading order (NLO) adds two-pion exchanges and contact terms with two derivatives. The single-particle potentials of Λ, Σ, and Ξ hyperons in symmetric nuclear matter are computed within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock framework by solving a regularized Lippmann-Schwinger equation with the χEFT potentials as kernel.

## Key numbers
- The Λ single-particle potential from the NLO19 interaction is much more attractive than from the NLO13 interaction, while the NLO19 Σ potential is slightly more repulsive than NLO13.
- The Ξ single-particle potential in nuclear matter ranges between -3 to -5 MeV, similar to other Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations.
- The Λ single-particle potential becomes repulsive starting at two-to-three times saturation density.
- Neutron star mass measurements have reached approximately 2 solar masses (2M⊙), with precise values determined for several pulsars.

## Context
This review extends the line of work by the Jülich-Bonn-Munich group on χEFT for baryon-baryon interactions, following their earlier NN interaction (Polinder 2006) and subsequent YN studies (Haidenbauer 2013, 2014, 2016, 2019). It contrasts with meson-exchange models such as the Jülich (Holzenkamp 1989, Haidenbauer 2005) and Nijmegen potentials (Rijken 2010), as well as lattice QCD approaches by the HALQCD and NPLQCD collaborations. The results enable more precise predictions for the equation of state of neutron star matter containing hyperons, directly addressing the "hyperon puzzle" where hyperon softening must be reconciled with 2M⊙ neutron star observations.
