# Characterizing the initial and final state effects in isobaric collisions at energies available at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider

> The Multi-Phase Transport model (AMPT) is employed to predict Symmetric Correlations (SC), Asymmetric Correlations (ASC), Normalized Symmetric Correlations (NSC), and Normalized Asymmetric Correlations (NASC) in $^{96}Ru+^{96}Ru$ and $^{96}Zr+^{96}Zr$ collisions at 200~GeV. Our study offers insights into the behavior of SC, ASC, NSC, and NASC, considering various nuclear structure scenarios to account for differences between the two isobars. Additionally, we emphasize the importance of detailed experimental measurements as they will serve as a critical constraint for refining the predictions of theoretical models.

## Key claim

The AMPT model predicts that symmetric correlations (SC), asymmetric correlations (ASC), normalized symmetric correlations (NSC), and normalized asymmetric correlations (NASC) in isobaric collisions at 200 GeV can distinguish between different nuclear structure scenarios for Ru-96 and Zr-96, but the paper emphasizes that detailed experimental measurements are needed to serve as a critical constraint for refining theoretical model predictions, rather than claiming definitive discrimination.

## Method

The study uses the AMPT model with the string-melting option to simulate Ru-96+Ru-96 and Zr-96+Zr-96 collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, employing a deformed Woods-Saxon distribution for nuclear density with different parameter sets for the two isobars. Multi-particle correlation techniques with two-subevent cumulant methods (using pseudorapidity gap Delta-eta > 0.7) and traditional cumulant methods are used to calculate two-, three-, four-, five-, and six-particle symmetric and asymmetric correlations. The parton-scattering cross-section is fixed at sigma_pp = 2.8 mb, and the ZPC Parton cascade model governs the space-time evolution of partons.

## Key numbers

* Woods-Saxon parameters for Ru-96 (Case-1): R0 = 5.09 fm, a0 = 0.46 fm, beta2 = 0.162, beta3 = 0.00
* Woods-Saxon parameters for Zr-96 (Case-2): R0 = 5.02 fm, a0 = 0.52 fm, beta2 = 0.06, beta3 = 0.20
* Woods-Saxon parameters for Zr-96 (Case-3): R0 = 5.09 fm, a0 = 0.46 fm, beta2 = 0.06, beta3 = 0.20
* Woods-Saxon parameters for Zr-96 (Case-4): R0 = 5.09 fm, a0 = 0.46 fm, beta2 = 0.06, beta3 = 0.00
* Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
* Two-subevent pseudorapidity gap: Delta-eta > 0.7 (eta_a > 0.35 and eta_b < -0.35)
* Parton-scattering cross-section: sigma_pp = 2.8 mb

## Context

This work extends prior studies of symmetric and asymmetric correlations by Mordasini (2019), Moravcova (2020), Li (2021), Bilandzic (2021), Nijs (2021), and Zhang (2021) to isobaric Ru-96+Ru-96 and Zr-96+Zr-96 collisions at RHIC top energy. It builds on the author's previous investigation (Magdy, 2022) showing that SC, ASC, NSC, and NASC can simultaneously constrain initial-state eccentricities and final-state viscous attenuation (eta/s). The results provide AMPT model predictions that can be compared with future experimental data from the STAR collaboration at RHIC, which will help constrain nuclear deformation differences between the two isobars and refine theoretical models of heavy-ion collisions.
