# Present status of the long range component of the nuclear force

> In order to settle the fundamental question whether the nuclear forces involve the long range components, the S-wave amplitude of the proton-proton scattering is analysed in search for the extra singularity at $ν=0$, which corresponds to the long range force. To facilitate the search, a function, which is free from the singularities in the neighborhood of $ν=0$ when all the interactions are short range, is constructed. The calculation of such a function from the phase shift data reveals a sharp cusp at $ν=0$ in contradiction to the meson theory of the nuclear force. The type of the extra singularity at $ν=0$ is close to what is expected in the case of the strong van der Waals interaction. Physical meanings of the long range force in the nuclear force are discussed. Low energy p-p experiments to confirm directly the strong long range interaction are also proposed, in which the characteristic interference patten of the Coulonb and the Van der Waals forces is predicted.

## Key claim

The S-wave proton-proton scattering amplitude contains a sharp cusp at \(\nu=0\) that contradicts the meson-theory expectation of purely short-range nuclear forces; this cusp is consistent with a strong van der Waals interaction, though the claim is hedged by the dependence on the precision of the phase-shift data and the specific choice of the \(\pi\)-\(N\) coupling constant.

## Method

The author constructs a "Kantor amplitude" \(K_0(\nu)\) for the S-wave proton-proton scattering, which is designed to be free of singularities near \(\nu=0\) if all interactions are short range, by subtracting the unitarity cut and the one-pion-exchange (OPE) contribution. Using the Nijmegen multi-energy phase shifts (Nijmegen-88 and Nijmegen-90) in the range \(0.5\ \mathrm{MeV} \leq T_{\mathrm{lab}} \leq 100\ \mathrm{MeV}\), the once-subtracted non-OPE Kantor amplitude \(\tilde{K}_0^{\mathrm{once}}(\nu)\) is evaluated numerically via a meromorphic fit to the effective range function \(X_0(\nu)\), and its behavior near \(\nu=0\) is examined.

## Key numbers

* The scattering length parameters are fixed as \(c_0 = 0.18698\) (inverse scattering length) and \(c_1 = 0.9541\) (half the effective range), with accuracies of 0.05% and 0.6% respectively.
* The pole location of the effective range function is \(\nu_P = 6.39\) (in units of the neutral pion Compton wavelength), with an uncertainty of about 3%.
* The chi-square minimum for the power \(\gamma\) of the threshold spectral function occurs at \(\gamma = 1.48 \sim 1.62\), close to the value \(\gamma = 1.5\) expected for a London-type van der Waals interaction.
* The lower bound of the underlying Coulombic force strength \(^{\ast}e^2\) is estimated as 3.3 or 14, depending on the assumed radius of the composite particle (from nucleon form factor or the distance where the asymptotic van der Waals form breaks down).

## Context

This work extends the search for long-range components of the nuclear force beyond the Yukawa model, where the one-pion-exchange (OPE) pole at \(t=\mu^2\) and the two-pion-exchange cut starting at \(t=4\mu^2\) are the only expected singularities. It contradicts the meson-theory assumption that all non-OPE nuclear forces are shorter than \(1/(2\mu)\), and instead supports predictions from the dyon model of hadrons (Sawada, earlier work) and the QCD-inspired view of nucleons as composite particles. The results enable future low-energy proton-proton scattering experiments to directly confirm the strong van der Waals interaction by observing the predicted destructive interference pattern between Coulomb and van der Waals forces.
