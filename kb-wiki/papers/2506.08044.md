# Estimation of Electron Screening Potential in the 6Li(d,α)4He Reaction Using Multi-Layer Perceptron Neural Network

> Reactions between light charged nuclei at sub-Coulomb energies are crucial in astrophysical environments, but accurate cross-section measurements are hindered by electron screening. Traditional methods, including polynomial extrapolation and the Trojan Horse Method, often yield screening potentials exceeding adiabatic predictions. Building on the success of an MLP-based Artificial Neural Network (ANN) for the 6Li(p, α)3He reaction [1], this work applies the same approach to the 6Li(d, α)4He reaction. Experimental astrophysical S-factor data from literature are reanalyzed using the ANN to model the energy-dependent S-factor. The bare S-factor is extracted from data above 70 keV, where screening effects are minimal, and the screening potential is obtained by comparing with the low-energy region. The resulting screening potential is 147.95 eV, demonstrating the robustness of ANN-based methods for evaluating electron screening in low-energy nuclear reactions involving light nuclei.

# Estimation of Electron Screening Potential in the 6Li(d,α)4He Reaction Using Multi-Layer Perceptron Neural Network

## Key claim

The electron screening potential for the 6Li(d,α)4He reaction is determined to be 147.95 eV using a Multi-Layer Perceptron neural network trained on experimental S-factor data, with the bare S-factor extracted from data above 70 keV where screening effects are minimal. This value agrees with the R-matrix result of 149.5 eV from Grineviciute (2015) but is significantly lower than the adiabatic prediction of 175 eV and earlier polynomial-fit results.

## Method

Experimental astrophysical S-factor data for the 6Li(d,α)4He reaction from the EXFOR database (Engstler 1992, Spitaleri 2001) were used to train a feedforward Multi-Layer Perceptron neural network with two hidden layers (32 and 16 neurons) using the LBFGS solver and tanh activation function. The model was optimized via randomized hyperparameter search with five-fold cross-validation; data above 70 keV were used to train the network to predict the bare S-factor, and the screening potential was extracted by comparing the ratio of the total (screened) S-factor to the predicted bare S-factor in the low-energy region.

## Key numbers

- Electron screening potential: Ue = 147.95 eV for the 6Li(d,α)4He reaction
- Bare S-factor at zero energy: Sb(0) = 20.86 MeV-barn, extrapolated from the ANN model
- Coefficient of determination: R² = 0.982 on training set and 0.971 on testing set
- Normalized Mean Squared Error: 4.3% for training and 5% for testing datasets
- Comparison values from literature: Engstler (1992) reported 380 ± 250 eV (atomic target) and 330 ± 120 eV (molecular target); Spitaleri (2001) reported 340 ± 51 eV via Trojan Horse Method; Barker (2002) reported 248 eV; Ruprecht (2004) reported 190 ± 50 eV; Grineviciute (2015) reported 149.5 eV via R-matrix analysis

## Context

This work extends the ANN-based methodology previously applied by Chattopadhyay (2024) to the 6Li(p,α)3He reaction, now applied to the 6Li(d,α)4He system which is important for lithium nucleosynthesis in astrophysical environments. The result (147.95 eV) is consistent with the R-matrix analysis of Grineviciute (2015) at 149.5 eV, and lies below the adiabatic limit of 175 eV, contrasting with earlier polynomial extrapolation and Trojan Horse Method results that yielded higher values (330-380 eV). This demonstrates that ANN-based methods can serve as robust alternatives to traditional approaches for extracting electron screening potentials in low-energy nuclear reactions involving light nuclei.
