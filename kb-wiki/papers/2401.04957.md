# Lee-Yang edge singularities in QCD via the Dyson-Schwinger Equations

> We take the Dyson-Schwinger Equation approach of QCD for the quark propagator at complex chemical potential to study the QCD phase transition. The phase transition line of the $(2+1)$-flavor QCD matter in the imaginary chemical potential region is computed via a simplified truncation scheme, whose curvature is found to be consistent with the one at real chemical potential. Moreover, the computation in the complex chemical potential plane allows us to determine the location of the Lee-Yang edge singularities. We show explicitly that the critical end point coincides with the Lee-Yang edge singularities on the real $μ_{B}^{}$ axis. We also investigate the scaling behavior of the singularities and discuss the possibility of extrapolating the CEP from a certain range of chemical potential.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the Lee-Yang edge singularities in the complex chemical potential plane coincide with the critical end point on the real baryon chemical potential axis, and that the trajectory of these singularities can be used to extrapolate the CEP location, though the extrapolation requires data within a range corresponding to approximately 20% in temperature and 15% in chemical potential to achieve 10% error control.

## Method
The authors solve the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator at finite temperature and complex chemical potential using a simplified truncation scheme for the quark-gluon vertex, following the Slavnov-Taylor identity with a parametrized non-Abelian part. The gluon propagator is taken from a combined functional-lattice input with thermal effects incorporated via the hard thermal loop approximation. The chiral susceptibility is scanned over the temperature and complex chemical potential plane to locate the Lee-Yang edge singularities, and the scaling behavior is analyzed using both O(4) and Z(2) universality class mappings.

## Key numbers
- Pseudo-critical temperature at zero chemical potential: T_c = 155.7 MeV, consistent with lattice QCD results
- Curvature of the phase transition line at real chemical potential: kappa_2^B = 0.026 (extracted from mu_B in (0,360) MeV range)
- Curvature at pure imaginary chemical potential: kappa_2^B = 0.024 (extracted from mu_B^2 in (-210^2, 0) MeV^2 range)
- Critical end point location from direct calculation: (T_CEP, mu_B,CEP) = (83 MeV, 723 MeV)
- Regularized quark condensate in vacuum: (333.6 MeV)^3; reduced condensate: (335.8 MeV)^3
- Extracted critical exponent product from Z(2) scaling fit near CEP: beta*delta = 1.38
- Extracted slope parameter c_2 = 6.38 GeV^(1-beta*delta) and dimensionless parameter bar{c}_2 = 0.28
- Estimated CEP location using Z(2) scaling with curvature kappa=0.016 and T_c(0)=156 MeV: (T_CEP, mu_B,CEP) = (118 MeV, 606 MeV)

## Context
This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to study Lee-Yang edge singularities in QCD for the first time, building on prior functional QCD calculations of the phase diagram by Gao (2020), Gunkel (2021), and Fu (2019). The results connect to lattice QCD studies of imaginary chemical potential by Bernhardt (2023) and to the scaling analysis framework developed by Mukherjee (2019) and Basar (2021). The extracted scaling parameters enable comparison with Ising model universality class predictions and provide a method for estimating the critical end point location that could be combined with lattice QCD results from Goswami (2024) and Bollweg (2022).
