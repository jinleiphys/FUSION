# Alpha-like correlations in $^{20}$Ne, comparison of quartetting wave function and THSR approaches

> $^{20}$Ne can be considered as a double-magic $^{16}$O core nucleus surrounded by four nucleons, the constituents of an $α$-like quartet. Similar to other nuclei ($^{212}$Po, $^{104}$Ti, etc.) with a quartet on top of a double-magic core nucleus, significant $α$-like correlations are expected. Correlations in the ground state of $^{20}$Ne are investigated using different approaches. The quartetting wave function approach (QWFA) predicts a large $α$-like cluster contribution near the surface of the nuclei. The Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke (THSR) approach describes $α$-like clustering in nuclear systems. The results of the QWFA in the Thomas-Fermi and shell-model approximation are compared with THSR calculations for the container model. Results for the $α$ formation probability and the rms radii are shown.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for $^{20}$Ne, the quartetting wave function approach (QWFA) predicts a large $\alpha$-like cluster contribution near the nuclear surface, but the Thomas-Fermi rule ($E_\alpha = \mu_4$) is not strictly valid; instead, the energy eigenvalue $E_\alpha$ lies below the chemical potential $\mu_4$, indicating that correlated quartets are energetically more favorable than uncorrelated single-nucleon shell-model states.

## Method

The authors applied the quartetting wave function approach (QWFA) to $^{20}$Ne, treating the nucleus as a $^{16}$O double-magic core plus four nucleons forming an $\alpha$-like quartet, and solved the center-of-mass wave equation for the quartet using both a Thomas-Fermi local density approximation and a shell-model approach with Woods-Saxon single-particle states. They compared these QWFA results with Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke (THSR) container-model calculations for the same nucleus, evaluating the $\alpha$ formation probability and root-mean-square (rms) radii.

## Key numbers

- The critical density for $\alpha$ cluster dissolution (Mott density) is $n_{\rm crit} = 0.02917$ fm$^{-3}$, corresponding to a critical radius $r_{\rm crit} = 3.302$ fm in the $^{16}$O core density distribution.
- The energy eigenvalue for the quartet center-of-mass motion in the Thomas-Fermi model is $E_\alpha = -33.0$ MeV, derived from the binding energy difference $B_{^{16}{\rm O}} - B_{^{20}{\rm Ne}} = -33.0$ MeV.
- The calculated rms point radius for $^{20}$Ne using the Thomas-Fermi model is 2.864 fm, in good agreement with the experimental rms point radius of 2.87 fm.
- The preformation factor $P_\alpha$ from the shell-model-based QWFA calculation is 0.72 (with $c=10623$, $d=3415.56$) or 0.74 (with $c=11032$, $d=3513.46$), and the chemical potential $\mu_4$ is $-32.388$ MeV or $-32.12$ MeV respectively.
- The shell-model calculation using Woods-Saxon $2s$ states gives the quartet energy eigenvalue $E_{2s^4} = -36.65$ MeV.
- The free $\alpha$ particle binding energy is $B_\alpha = 28.3$ MeV.
- The parameters for the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction are $c = 4650$ MeV and $d = 1900$ MeV for the Thomas-Fermi model.

## Context

This work extends the QWFA previously applied to $\alpha$-decaying heavy nuclei such as $^{212}$Po (Röpke et al., 2014; Xu et al., 2016, 2017) and $^{104}$Te (Yang et al., 2020) to the lighter stable nucleus $^{20}$Ne, where both QWFA and THSR approaches are computationally feasible. The THSR approach, developed by Tohsaki, Horiuchi, Schuck, and Röpke, has successfully described $\alpha$ clustering in light 4$n$ nuclei including $^8$Be, the Hoyle state of $^{12}$C, and excited states of $^{16}$O, and was recently applied to $^{20}$Ne by Bo et al. (2023) using a two-parameter container model. The comparison between QWFA and THSR for $^{20}$Ne enables a better understanding of how $\alpha$-like correlations are described in nuclear systems, and the QWFA results for the preformation factor and rms radii can inform future studies of $\alpha$ clustering in medium-mass nuclei.
