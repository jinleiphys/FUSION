# N-quantum approach to quantum field theory at finite T and $μ$: the NJL model

> We extend the N-quantum approach to quantum field theory to finite temperature ($T$) and chemical potential ($μ$) and apply it to the NJL model. In this approach the Heisenberg fields are expressed using the Haag expansion while temperature and chemical potential are introduced simultaneously through a generalized Bogoliubov transformation. Known mean field results are recovered using only the first term in the Haag expansion. In addition, we find that at finite T and in the broken symmetry phase of the model the mean field approximation can not diagonalize the Hamiltonian. Inclusion of scalar and axial vector diquark channels in the SU(2)$_{rm f}$ $otimes$ SU(3)$_{\rm c}$ version of the model can lead to a lowering of the vacuum energy density. We discuss how to go beyond the mean field approximation by including higher order terms in the Haag expansion.

## Key claim

The N-quantum approach (NQA) to quantum field theory can be extended to finite temperature and chemical potential by applying a generalized thermal Bogoliubov transformation to the asymptotic fields in the Haag expansion, but the mean field approximation (using only the first term in the Haag expansion) cannot diagonalize the Hamiltonian at finite temperature in the broken symmetry phase of the NJL model.

## Method

The authors express Heisenberg fields using the Haag expansion, then apply a thermal Bogoliubov transformation to the creation and annihilation operators of the asymptotic fields, accompanied by a thermal doubling of the Hilbert space. Temperature and chemical potential are introduced as parameters through the coefficients of this transformation, which are the Fermi distribution functions for particles and antiparticles. The method is applied to both the U(1) and SU(2)_f ⊗ SU(3)_c versions of the NJL model, using only the first term in the Haag expansion to recover mean field results.

## Key numbers

- For the SU(2)_f ⊗ SU(3)_c NJL model, input parameters are Λ = 0.05 GeV, g_1 = 5.01 GeV^{-2}, and g_2 = -12 g_3 = 3.11 GeV^{-2}
- The critical chemical potential at T = 0.1 GeV is μ_c = 0.253 GeV
- The phase transition line begins at (T, μ) = (0, 0.307) GeV and ends at (T, μ) = (0.185, 0) GeV
- The gap equation for the U(1) NJL model at zero temperature is m = 4g_0 ∫ d^3p (m/ω_p) with ω_p = sqrt(|p|^2 + m^2)
- Dynamical symmetry breaking occurs only for coupling constants satisfying π^2/Λ^2 < g_0

## Context

This work extends the N-quantum approach developed by Greenberg (1965, 1994) to finite temperature and chemical potential, building on previous applications to the NJL model by Greenberg and Mohapatra (1986) and Greenberg and Orr (1987). The method recovers standard mean field results from the NJL model literature reviewed by Klevansky (1992) and Hatsuda and Kunihiro (1994), while also finding that the Hamiltonian cannot be diagonalized at finite T in the broken symmetry phase. The formalism enables future studies of color superconductivity beyond the mean field approximation, as investigated by Rapp, Schaefer, Shuryak and Velkovsky (1998) and Alford, Rajagopal and Wilczek (1998) using instanton-induced interactions.
