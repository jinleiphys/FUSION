# A brief review of the theory of charmonium suppression in heavy ion collisions

> A brief overview of the theory of charmonium suppression in heavy ion collisions is presented. In particular I will concentrate on the effects caused by the hot, deconfined medium and on the effort to treat them using field-theoretical, QCD-based techniques, such as lattice QCD and Effective Field Theories.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while charmonium suppression in heavy ion collisions has been observed experimentally, a rigorous QCD-based theoretical description requires moving beyond simple potential models to field-theoretical approaches such as Effective Field Theories (EFTs) and lattice QCD, which reveal that the in-medium potential is complex-valued with a sizeable imaginary part that encodes thermal width effects.

## Method

The review surveys three main QCD-based approaches to charmonium suppression: (1) extraction of the spectral function from lattice Euclidean correlators via the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM); (2) potential models using the singlet free energy or internal energy from lattice calculations; and (3) Effective Field Theories (NRQCD and pNRQCD) extended to finite temperatures, which systematically integrate out scales from the heavy quark mass m down to the binding energy mv², yielding a rigorously defined complex potential. The EFT framework is applied in both weak-coupling scenarios (where thermal scales are hierarchical) and non-perturbative settings (where scales are comparable).

## Key numbers

- Lattice QCD predicts a crossover transition to the Quark-Gluon Plasma for temperatures T ranging from 150 to 200 MeV.
- Recent MEM studies find no charmonium bound states above 1.5 T_c (Ding 2012).
- Lattice NRQCD results show survival of S-wave bottomonium ground states up to approximately 2 T_c, with rapid disappearance of P states after the transition.
- The Debye mass is given by m_D² = g² T² (1 + n_f/6), where n_f is the number of quark flavors.
- The hot medium expansion and cooling time is estimated to be at most approximately 10 fm/c.
- Thermalization is believed to happen in less than 1 fm/c.

## Context

This review extends the original 1986 hypothesis of Matsui and Satz that colour screening in a deconfined medium would dissociate the J/ψ, and it builds upon subsequent lattice calculations (Petreczky 2012) and experimental results from SPS, RHIC, and LHC. The EFT approach, initiated by Brambilla (2008) and Escobedo (2008), provides a rigorous QCD derivation of the complex potential first identified by Laine (2006) and Beraudo (2007), enabling future phenomenological models that couple the complex potential to hydrodynamic evolution of the plasma, as demonstrated for LHC Υ data by Strickland.
