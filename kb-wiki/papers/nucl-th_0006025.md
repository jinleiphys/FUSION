# Are there universal freeze-out parameters for central 158AGeV Pb+Pb collisions?

> Earlier attempts to extract parameters of kinetic freeze-out in Pb+Pb collisions at 158$A$GeV are critically reviewed. Many of these analyses have used approximations which have significant impact on the extracted parameters. Simple estimates are obtained which attempt to avoid the most critical approximations. It is pointed out that constraints based on pion interferometry are less reliable than those from momentum spectra. A universal set of freeze-out parameters from transverse mass spectra would require T > 135 MeV and <beta> < 0.35.

# Wiki Page: Are there universal freeze-out parameters for central 158AGeV Pb+Pb collisions?

## Key claim
A universal set of kinetic freeze-out parameters from transverse mass spectra of multiple hadron species in central 158AGeV Pb+Pb collisions would require temperature T ≥ 135 MeV and average transverse flow velocity ⟨β_T⟩ ≤ 0.35, but this conclusion depends on excluding pion interferometry constraints which are argued to be less reliable than those from momentum spectra.

## Method
The author re-analyzes published inverse slope parameters of transverse mass spectra for pions, kaons, protons, antiprotons, and deuterons from the NA44 and NA49 collaborations using a relativistic formula (Eq. 3) that relates effective slope to temperature and flow velocity, avoiding the non-relativistic approximations used in earlier work. A full hydrodynamical parameterization of neutral pion spectra from the WA98 collaboration, including resonance decay effects and using both Gaussian and Woods-Saxon density profiles, is compared with slope-based estimates from heavier particles. The author also critically examines the assumptions underlying the extraction of freeze-out parameters from pion interferometry radii, particularly the Boltzmann approximation and the assumption of a momentum-independent geometric source size.

## Key numbers
- Temperature T ≈ 139 MeV and ⟨β_T⟩ = 0.33 from comparing positive kaons and protons; T ≈ 152 MeV and ⟨β_T⟩ = 0.28 from negative kaons and antiprotons
- Temperature T ≈ 100 MeV and ⟨β_T⟩ ≈ 0.41 from pions and (anti)protons; T ≈ 84 MeV and ⟨β_T⟩ ≈ 0.51 from pions and kaons
- Universal freeze-out parameters from all momentum spectra require T ≥ 135 MeV and ⟨β_T⟩ ≤ 0.35
- Proton-proton correlation radii from NA49 and NA44: R_pp = 3.5–4.0 fm, compared to pion interferometry radii R_ππ = 6–7 fm
- Example two-source scenario: source 1 with R_1 = 10 fm and T_1 = 200 MeV, source 2 with R_2 = 2 fm and T_2 = 400 MeV
- NA49 interferometry analysis used T = 120 MeV, ⟨β_T⟩ = 0.55, and R_G = 6.5 fm; corrected Bose-Einstein analysis would require T = 85 MeV, ⟨β_T⟩ = 0.55, and R_G = 7.1 fm

## Context
This paper critically reviews earlier freeze-out parameter extractions from Pb+Pb collisions at 158AGeV by NA44 (published 1997) and NA49 (published 1999), pointing out that non-relativistic approximations in those analyses significantly affect the extracted temperature and flow values. The work extends the hydrodynamical analysis of neutral pion spectra from WA98 (1999) and compares with slope parameters from NA44 and NA49. The author argues that constraints from pion interferometry, as used in the NA49 hydrodynamical analysis, are less reliable than those from momentum spectra due to the Boltzmann approximation and neglected correlations between momentum and coordinate space. The paper enables a more cautious approach to extracting freeze-out parameters and suggests that either pion HBT does not measure kinetic freeze-out or that freeze-out is not universal across particle species.
