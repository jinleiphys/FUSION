# An estimate of QGP viscosity from STAR data on $ϕ$ mesons

> In the Israel-Stewart's theory of dissipative hydrodynamics, with a lattice based equation of state, where the confinement-deconfinement transition is a cross-over at $T_{co}$=196 MeV, we have analysed the STAR data on $ϕ$ meson production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}$=200 GeV. From a simultaneous fit to $ϕ$ mesons multiplicity, mean $p_T$ and integrated $v_2$, we obtain a phenomenological estimate of QGP viscosity, $η/s =0.15 \pm 0.05 \pm 0.03$, the first error is due to the experimental uncertainty in STAR measurements, the second reflects the uncertainties in initial and final conditions of the fluid. A host of STAR data, e.g. $ϕ$ multiplicity, integrated $v_2$, mean $p_T$, $p_T$ spectra ($p_T <$3 GeV), in central Au+Au collisions, are consistent with the estimate of viscosity.

# An estimate of QGP viscosity from STAR data on φ mesons

## Key claim
The shear viscosity to entropy ratio of the quark-gluon plasma is estimated as η/s = 0.15 ± 0.05 ± 0.03, but this value depends on the assumed initial time, initial velocity profile, freeze-out temperature, and numerical accuracy of the hydrodynamic code; the quoted systematic error of ±0.03 reflects these uncertainties rather than a rigorous bound.

## Method
The paper uses Israel-Stewart's second-order dissipative hydrodynamics (code AZHYDRO-KOLKATA) with a lattice-based equation of state where the confinement-deconfinement transition is a cross-over at T_co = 196 MeV. The initial energy density is parameterized as a linear combination of participant and collision number distributions, and the fluid is initialized with zero transverse velocity at τ_i = 0.2 fm. The shear stress tensor is initialized to its boost-invariant value, and the relaxation time uses the Boltzmann approximation τ_π = 3η/2p. The invariant yield of φ mesons is computed via the Cooper-Frye prescription at freeze-out temperature T_F = 140 MeV.

## Key numbers
- η/s = 0.15 ± 0.05 (experimental uncertainty) ± 0.03 (systematic uncertainty from initial/final conditions)
- Cross-over temperature T_co = 196(3) MeV from lattice simulations
- Initial central energy density for ideal fluid (η/s = 0): ε_i = 32.9 ± 3.7 GeV/fm³ in b=0 Au+Au collisions
- Initial central temperature for ideal fluid: T_i = 369.3 ± 10.2 MeV
- For η/s = 0.16: ε_i = 11.6 ± 1.4 GeV/fm³, T_i = 284.2 ± 8.9 MeV
- For η/s = 0.25: ε_i = 6.15 ± 0.85 GeV/fm³, T_i = 241.6 ± 8.1 MeV
- χ²/N for φ multiplicity at η/s = 0.16: 0.19; for mean p_T: 0.49; for integrated v₂: 4.8
- Mean p_T decreases by ~10% if initial time increases from 0.2 fm to 1.0 fm (for η/s = 0.16)
- Systematic uncertainty from freeze-out temperature range 130-150 MeV: ~8% in η/s
- Numerical accuracy of AZHYDRO-KOLKATA: ~5%, leading to ~9% uncertainty in η/s

## Context
This work extends the program of extracting QGP transport coefficients from RHIC data, following earlier ideal hydrodynamic analyses of Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV (QGP3, 2003) and dissipative hydrodynamic implementations by Teaney (2003), Romatschke (2007), and Song and Heinz (2008). The result η/s ≈ 0.15 lies above the ADS/CFT lower bound of 1/4π ≈ 0.08 (Policastro, 2001) and below perturbative QCD estimates of η/s ∼ 1 (Arnold, 2000), consistent with the lattice-based estimate of η/s = 0.134(33) at T = 1.165 T_c by Meyer (2007). The analysis enables more reliable extraction of initial conditions for hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions and provides a benchmark for future measurements of other strange hadrons and higher p_T ranges.
