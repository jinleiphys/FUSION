# Magnetic moments of antidecuplet pentaquarks

> We analyze the magnetic moment of the exotic pentaquarks of the flavor antidecuplet in the constituent quark model for the case in which the ground state is in an orbital L(p)=0(+) or a L(p)=1(-) state. We derive sum rules for the magnetic moments. The magnetic moment of the Theta(1540) is found to be 0.38, 0.09 and 1.05 mu_N for J(p)=1/2(-), 1/2(+) and 3/2(+), respectively, which is compared with the results obtained in other approaches.

## Key claim
The magnetic moment of the Θ⁺(1540) pentaquark is calculated in the constituent quark model to be 0.38, 0.09, and 1.05 μ_N for J^p = 1/2⁻, 1/2⁺, and 3/2⁺, respectively, but these values depend on the assumed parity and angular momentum of the ground state, which remain experimentally undetermined.

## Method
The authors construct pentaquark wave functions from five constituent quarks using Jacobi coordinates and group-theoretical classification under the tetrahedral group T_d (isomorphic to S_4). They compute magnetic moments as the sum of spin and orbital contributions from the constituent quarks, using quark magnetic moments μ_u = 1.852 μ_N, μ_d = −0.972 μ_N, and μ_s = −0.613 μ_N extracted from the proton, neutron, and Λ magnetic moments. The analysis covers both negative parity (L^p = 0⁺, J^p = 1/2⁻) and positive parity (L^p = 1⁻, J^p = 1/2⁺ and 3/2⁺) antidecuplet states.

## Key numbers
- Θ⁺(1540) magnetic moment: 0.38 μ_N for J^p = 1/2⁻
- Θ⁺(1540) magnetic moment: 0.09 μ_N for J^p = 1/2⁺
- Θ⁺(1540) magnetic moment: 1.05 μ_N for J^p = 3/2⁺
- Ξ_{3/2}^{−−} magnetic moment: −0.44 μ_N for J^p = 1/2⁻
- Ξ_{3/2}^{+} magnetic moment: 0.50 μ_N for J^p = 1/2⁻
- Ξ_{3/2}^{−−} magnetic moment: −2.64 μ_N for J^p = 3/2⁺
- Ξ_{3/2}^{+} magnetic moment: 1.59 μ_N for J^p = 3/2⁺
- For J^p = 1/2⁺, all three exotic pentaquarks (Θ⁺, Ξ_{3/2}^{+}, Ξ_{3/2}^{−−}) have equal magnetic moment: 0.09 μ_N

## Context
This work extends constituent quark model calculations to the magnetic moments of exotic antidecuplet pentaquarks, complementing prior studies using the chiral soliton model (Kim and Praszalowicz, 2004), correlated quark models (Jaffe and Wilczek, 2003; Shuryak and Zahed, 2004; Karliner and Lipkin, 2003), the MIT bag model (DeGrand and Jaffe, 1976), and QCD sum rules (Huang et al., 2004). The derived sum rules connect antidecuplet magnetic moments to those of decuplet and octet baryons, and the numerical results can serve as input for calculations of photo- and electroproduction cross sections that may help determine the quantum numbers of the Θ⁺.
