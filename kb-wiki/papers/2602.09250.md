# Mean-field proton-neutron pairing correlations with the Gogny D1S energy density functional

> We study proton-neutron pairing correlations within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) framework using Gogny-type energy density functionals. By allowing for proton-neutron mixing in the quasi-particle transformation, both isovector ($T=1$) and isoscalar ($T=0$) pairing channels are explicitly included at the mean-field level. The \texttt{TAURUS} code has been extended to treat density-dependent Gogny interactions in this generalized HFB scheme. We examine the numerical behavior of the widely used Gogny D1S functional and compare it with calculations performed using the Hamiltonian-based Brink-Boecker B1 interaction supplemented by a zero-range spin-orbit term. When proton-neutron mixing is included and large single-particle spaces are employed, instabilities are observed for Gogny D1S due to the zero-range density-dependent term contribution to the proton-neutron pairing field, whereas stable solutions are obtained with the B1 interaction. Constrained HFB calculations performed in reduced configuration spaces allow us to explore total energy curves as functions of proton-neutron pairing collective coordinates in selected $sd$-shell nuclei. In all cases studied, the self-consistent minima correspond to vanishing proton-neutron pairing, with energy increasing rapidly as proton-neutron pairing correlations are introduced. These results provide insight into the behavior of Gogny functionals under generalized HFB conditions and offer useful guidance for future developments.

## Key claim

When proton-neutron mixing is allowed in Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with the Gogny D1S functional, instabilities arise from the zero-range density-dependent term's contribution to the proton-neutron pairing field, whereas stable solutions are obtained with the Hamiltonian-based Brink-Boecker B1 interaction; in all sd-shell nuclei studied, the self-consistent minima correspond to vanishing proton-neutron pairing.

## Method

The authors extended the TAURUS code to treat density-dependent Gogny interactions within a generalized Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov scheme that allows proton-neutron mixing in the quasiparticle transformation, enabling both isovector (T=1) and isoscalar (T=0) pairing channels at the mean-field level. Constrained HFB calculations were performed using a spherical harmonic oscillator basis with up to 10 major shells, applying constraints on axial quadrupole deformation and pairing operators constructed from J-coupled pair creation and annihilation operators. The numerical behavior of the Gogny D1S functional was compared with calculations using the Brink-Boecker B1 interaction supplemented by a zero-range spin-orbit term.

## Key numbers

- For the nucleus 20Mg, calculations with Gogny D1S including proton-neutron mixing become unstable beyond N_sho = 5 oscillator shells, with no converged solution found for larger bases
- The oscillator length parameter was set to b = 1.005 A^{-1/6} fm, a standard value
- For 24Mg, the absolute HFB minimum corresponds to a prolate deformed configuration with beta_2 = 0.5
- Calculations were performed using up to N_sho = 10 oscillator shells as the limiting case for evaluating convergence
- Energies for Gogny D1S with proton-neutron mixing in 20Mg showed erratic behavior at N_sho = 8, with energy increasing by approximately 3 MeV relative to the stable solution

## Context

This work extends the study of proton-neutron pairing correlations within the energy density functional approach, which has been widely developed using Skyrme, Gogny, Fayans, and relativistic functionals (Bender 2003, Robledo 2018). The authors specifically examine the Gogny D1S functional (Berger 1984) and compare with the Brink-Boecker B1 interaction (Blaizot 1976, Brink 1967). The results demonstrate that Gogny functionals optimized within restricted HFB frameworks without proton-neutron mixing may not be suitable for generalized mean-field or beyond-mean-field calculations that include proton-neutron pairing correlations, and they suggest future work should focus on functionals such as the finite-range D2 (Chappert 2015) and DG (Zietek 2026) Gogny interactions.
