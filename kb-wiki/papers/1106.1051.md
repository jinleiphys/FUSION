# Current Topics in Heavy Quarkonium Physics

> I review some recent progress, open puzzles and future opportunities in heavy quarkonium physics in the framework of effective field theories

## Key claim

This review argues that effective field theories (EFTs) such as NRQCD and pNRQCD provide a systematic, model-independent framework for heavy quarkonium physics, but the attribution of specific quarkonium states to either the perturbative or nonperturbative soft regime remains ambiguous due to the lack of direct information on quarkonium radii.

## Method

The paper surveys the construction of a hierarchy of EFTs (NRQCD, pNRQCD) by systematically integrating out high-energy scales from QCD, with matching coefficients encoding high-energy contributions and low-energy degrees of freedom remaining dynamical. It reviews applications to quarkonium spectra, decays, production, and finite-temperature behavior, including perturbative calculations at high orders, resummation of large logarithms, and nonperturbative matching to lattice QCD and Wilson-loop operators.

## Key numbers

- Heavy-quark velocity squared: $v^2 \sim 0.1$ for $b\bar{b}$, $v^2 \sim 0.3$ for $c\bar{c}$, $v^2 \sim 0.01$ for $t\bar{t}$
- Determination of $\alpha_s$ at the $\Upsilon$ mass: $\alpha_s(M_\Upsilon(1S)) = 0.184^{+0.015}_{-0.014}$, giving $\alpha_s(M_z) = 0.119^{+0.006}_{-0.005}$
- Zero-flavor $r_0\Lambda_{\overline{MS}} = 0.622^{+0.019}_{-0.015}$ from NNNLL static energy comparison with lattice data
- Predicted $B_c$ hyperfine separation: $\Delta = 50 \pm 17\ (\text{th})\ ^{+15}_{-12}\ (\delta\alpha_s)$ MeV
- Predicted $\Upsilon(1S)$-$\eta_b$ hyperfine separation: $41 \pm 11\ (\text{th})\ ^{+9}_{-8}\ (\delta\alpha_s)$ MeV
- $\Gamma(J/\psi \to \gamma\,\eta_c) = (1.5 \pm 1.0)$ keV
- $\Gamma(\Upsilon(1S) \to \gamma\,\eta_b) = (k_\gamma/71\ \text{MeV})^3\,(15.1 \pm 1.5)$ eV

## Context

This review extends the work of the Quarkonium Working Group (Brambilla et al., 2010, 2004) and summarizes progress in EFT-based quarkonium physics since those comprehensive reviews. It enables precision determinations of Standard Model parameters (heavy quark masses, $\alpha_s$) and provides a rigorous framework for studying quarkonium in hot QCD media, where recent EFT developments (Brambilla et al., 2008; Laine et al., 2006) have shown that thermal dissociation arises from an imaginary part of the potential rather than color screening alone. Open questions include the nature of newly discovered exotic states near threshold and the need for improved lattice calculations of nonperturbative gluon correlators.
