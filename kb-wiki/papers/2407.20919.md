# System size dependence of energy loss and correlations of heavy mesons at LHC energies

> We study the system size dependence of heavy quark (HQ) observables at a center of mass energy of $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV to explore whether it can provide further constraints on the physical processes which are involved: energy loss of HQs in the quark gluon plasma (QGP), hadronization and hadronic rescattering. We use the EPOS4HQ approach to study p-p and 0-10\% central O-O, Ar-Ar, Kr-Kr and Pb-Pb reactions and investigate in detail the momentum change of heavy quarks from creation until their detection as part of a hadron as well as the enhancement of heavy baryon production at low $p_T$. We investigate furthermore the origin and the system size dependence of the azimuthal correlations between the heavy quark $Q$ and the heavy antiquark $\bar Q$ and how one can bypass the problem that correlations are washed out due to the combinatorial background. We conclude that a systematic study of the system size dependence of the momentum loss allows to separate the momentum loss due to the passage through the QGP from the momentum change due to hadronization and the $Q\bar Q$ correlations allow to gain inside into the different pQCD processes in which the heavy quarks are created.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a systematic study of the system size dependence of heavy quark momentum loss allows one to separate the momentum loss due to passage through the quark gluon plasma (QGP) from the momentum change due to hadronization, but this separation relies on the assumption that hadronization is a local process independent of system size, which is tested by comparing integrated pT spectra across different collision systems.

## Method

The authors used the EPOS4HQ approach, which embeds heavy quark physics in the EPOS4 event generator, to simulate p-p and 0-10% central O-O, Ar-Ar, Kr-Kr, and Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV. Heavy quarks interact with QGP partons via elastic and inelastic pQCD cross sections, then hadronize either by coalescence or fragmentation depending on the heavy quark transverse momentum, with hadronic rescattering described by UrQMD. The momentum change of heavy quarks was tracked from creation through QGP passage, hadronization, and hadronic rescattering, and azimuthal correlations between Q and Qbar pairs were analyzed for the three different pQCD production processes: flavor excitation, flavor creation (Born), and gluon splitting.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for QGP hadronization is T_FO=167 MeV, with critical energy density epsilon_FO=0.57 GeV/fm^3
- For Pb-Pb collisions at pT>5 GeV, the charm quark spectrum before hadronization is suppressed by more than a factor of 5 compared to the spectrum at creation
- For O-O collisions at pT>5 GeV, the suppression before hadronization is close to a factor of 2
- In Pb-Pb collisions, 90% of heavy quarks with initial pT>7 GeV/c are shifted to momenta below 7 GeV; in p-p collisions this is only 20%
- The momentum shift due to hadronization is roughly a factor of two between pT=10 GeV and pT=20 GeV
- The maximum of the Lambda_c/D^0 enhancement ratio shifts from pT approximately 3 GeV for p-p to pT approximately 4 GeV for Pb-Pb, with the enhancement at maximum increasing by 40% from p-p to Pb-Pb
- The initial time tau_0 for hydrodynamic evolution is 0.4 fm/c for p-p, 1.024 fm/c for O-O, 1.078 fm/c for Ar-Ar, 1.167 fm/c for Kr-Kr, and 1.457 fm/c for Pb-Pb collisions

## Context

This work extends the EPOS4HQ approach previously applied to p-p collisions (Zhao 2023) and Pb-Pb collisions to systematically study intermediate system sizes (O-O, Ar-Ar, Kr-Kr) to disentangle QGP energy loss from hadronization effects. The approach contrasts with other models such as PHSD (Song 2015), the Catania model (Plumari 2017), LBT (Cao 2016), TAMU (He 2019), Duke (Cao 2015), and Torino (Beraudo 2022) which use different treatments of the medium and heavy quark dynamics. The results enable future experiments to calibrate transport models by comparing the system-size-dependent momentum loss in the QGP against the system-size-independent hadronization contribution, and the azimuthal correlation analysis provides a way to distinguish the three pQCD production mechanisms of heavy quark pairs.
