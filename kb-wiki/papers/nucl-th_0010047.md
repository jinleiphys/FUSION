# Semiclassical Calculation of Shell Effects in Deformed Nuclei

> We summarize recent work in which the shell effect, which causes the onset of the mass asymmetry in nuclear fission, could be explained semiclassically in the framework of the periodic orbit theory. We also present new results for the inclusion of a spin-orbit interaction in the semiclassical calculation of the level density.

# Semiclassical Calculation of Shell Effects in Deformed Nuclei

## Key claim
The onset of mass asymmetry in nuclear fission can be explained semiclassically using periodic orbit theory, with the shell-correction energy dominated by only the shortest periodic orbits (polygons with p=2 and p=3 reflections). However, the authors explicitly state this semiclassical picture is only qualitative and is not meant to replace Strutinsky or Hartree-Fock type calculations.

## Method
The authors approximated the nuclear mean field by a cavity with constant volume and reflecting walls, neglecting spin-orbit, Coulomb, and pairing interactions and considering only one kind of particle. They used the (c,h,alpha) parameterization to define the cavity boundary and applied the Gutzwiller trace formula to compute the oscillating part of the level density and shell-correction energy from periodic orbits. For the spin-orbit interaction, they used a three-dimensional deformed harmonic oscillator potential with a generalized spin-orbit term proportional to the gradient of the potential crossed with momentum, and determined periodic orbits by Newton-Raphson iteration.

## Key numbers
- In 240Pu, the maximum of the symmetric outer barrier is found near c=1.53, h=alpha=0.
- The semiclassical model yields a realistic value of approximately 0.13 for the asymmetry parameter alpha at the asymmetric saddle point.
- For the spin-orbit calculation, energy eigenvalues up to approximately 150 hbar*omega_x were included in the exact diagonalization.
- The Gaussian averaging range for the Fourier transform was gamma=0.2 hbar*omega_x.
- For the spin-orbit coupling parameter kappa=0.1, the six planar orbit frequencies closely agree with peak positions found in the Fourier spectrum.
- The Gaussian averaging range for the level density comparison was 0.5 hbar*omega_x.

## Context
This work extends the periodic orbit theory approach of Gutzwiller (1971) and the generalization by Strutinsky, Magner, and others to explain nuclear ground-state deformations. It directly builds on the authors' own recent work (Brack, Meier, Reimann, and Sieber, 1998-2000) on fission asymmetry, where the diabatic states causing the asymmetry effect quantum-mechanically were shown to have probability maxima on the planes containing the leading periodic orbits. The inclusion of spin-orbit interaction addresses a known limitation of previous semiclassical calculations, following the theoretical framework of Littlejohn and Flynn (1991) and the heuristic prescription of Frisk and Guhr (1993), which has been justified through the relativistic trace formula of Bolte and Keppeler (1999). This enables future semiclassical calculations of shell structure in deformed nuclei that include spin-orbit effects, which are known to crucially affect shell structure.
