# Lattice Calculation of Baryon Masses using the Clover Fermion Action

> We present a calculation of the lowest-lying baryon masses in the quenched approximation to QCD. The calculations are performed using a non-perturbatively improved clover fermion action, and a splitting is found between the masses of the nucleon and its parity partner. An analysis of the mass of the first radial excitation of the nucleon finds a value considerably larger than that of the parity partner of the nucleon, and thus little evidence for the Roper resonance as a simple three-quark state

## Key claim
Using a non-perturbatively improved clover fermion action in the quenched approximation, the authors find a mass splitting between the nucleon and its parity partner, but the first radial excitation of the nucleon is considerably heavier than that parity partner, providing little evidence for the Roper resonance as a simple three-quark state.

## Method
The calculation employed the quenched approximation to QCD with the standard Wilson gluon action and a non-perturbatively improved clover fermion action. Two interpolating operators (N₁ and N₂) were used to extract the low-lying J=1/2 nucleon spectrum from two-point correlators on multiple lattice volumes and spacings, with chiral extrapolations performed using a linear ansatz in (am_π)².

## Key numbers
- Nucleon and parity-partner masses were computed at multiple lattice spacings and extrapolated to the continuum using r₀ ~ 0.5 fm as the scale; results showed good consistency with physical values despite systematic uncertainties.
- At a quark mass around that of the strange quark, the positive-parity state constructed from N₂ (the radial excitation) was considerably heavier than the negative-parity state from N₁, and therefore much heavier than the Roper resonance at 1440 MeV.
- The mass splitting between the radial excitation and the nucleon parity partner was comparable to that between the parity partner and the nucleon, in accord with other lattice calculations (Lee 1998, Lee 2000, Sasaki 1999).

## Context
This work extends earlier lattice studies of the excited nucleon spectrum (Lee 1998, Lee 2000, Sasaki 1999, Adelaide 2002) by using a non-perturbatively improved clover action to reduce discretization errors. The results contradict a simple quark-model interpretation of the Roper resonance as a three-quark state, and the authors note that further precise calculations will require anisotropic lattices (Lee 2000, LHPC 2002) to access higher excitations with reduced statistical noise.
