# Ternary quasifission in collisions of actinide nuclei

> The microscopic framework of time-dependent covariant density functional theory is applied to a systematic study of ternary quasifission in collisions of pairs of $^{238}$U nuclei. It is shown that the inclusion of octupole degree of freedom in the case of head-to-head collisions, extends the energy window in which ternary quasifission occurs, and greatly enhances the number of nucleons contained in a middle fragment. Dynamical pairing correlations, included here in the time-dependent BCS approximation, prevent the occurrence of ternary quasifission in head-to-head collisions, and have an effect on the location of the energy window in which a middle fragment is formed in tail-to-tail collisions. In the latter case, as well as for tail-to-side collisions, the formation of very heavy neutron-rich systems in certain energy intervals is predicted.

## Key claim

The inclusion of octupole deformation in head-to-head collisions of $^{238}$U nuclei extends the energy window for ternary quasifission and greatly enhances the number of nucleons in the middle fragment, but dynamical pairing correlations in the time-dependent BCS approximation prevent ternary quasifission in head-to-head collisions entirely, while shifting the energy window for middle fragment formation in tail-to-tail collisions.

## Method

The authors applied time-dependent covariant density functional theory (TDCDFT) with the PC-PK1 density functional to simulate collisions of $^{238}$U + $^{238}$U and $^{226}$Ra + $^{226}$Ra on a 3D lattice. Initial states were prepared using self-consistent CDFT calculations, and time evolution was performed with a predictor-corrector method and fourth-order Taylor expansion of the time-evolution operator. Pairing correlations were included dynamically using a monopole interaction in the time-dependent BCS approximation.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium minimum of $^{238}$U without pairing has deformation parameters $\beta_{20} = 0.29$ and $\beta_{30} = 0.15$; the quadrupole-only minimum lies 0.99 MeV higher in energy.
- In head-to-head collisions without octupole deformation, the middle fragment corresponds to $^{13}$B; with octupole deformation, the middle fragment corresponds to $^{55}$Ca at $E_{\text{c.m.}} = 900$ MeV.
- Without octupole deformation, the energy window for ternary quasifission in head-to-head collisions spans 800 MeV to 920 MeV; with octupole deformation, it extends from 780 MeV to 960 MeV.
- At $E_{\text{c.m.}} = 780$ MeV with octupole deformation, the average proton number in fragments L and R drops from $Z = 92$ to $Z = 80$, and neutron number from $N = 146$ to $N = 126$, while the middle fragment has $Z = 24$ and $N = 40$.
- For head-to-head collisions with octupole deformation, ternary quasifission occurs for impact parameters up to $b = 3.5$ fm; without octupole deformation, it ceases at $b \geq 2$ fm.
- For $^{226}$Ra + $^{226}$Ra head-to-head collisions without pairing, ternary quasifission occurs in the energy interval 880 MeV to 940 MeV, with middle fragment characterized by $8 \leq Z \leq 13$ and $14 \leq N \leq 23$.
- In tail-to-tail collisions without pairing, the middle fragment has $95 \leq Z \leq 105$ and $159 \leq N \leq 174$; with pairing, the numbers change to $86 \leq Z \leq 92$ and $144 \leq N \leq 153$, and the energy window shifts upward by more than 100 MeV.
- For tail-to-side collisions with pairing at $E_{\text{c.m.}} = 1300$ MeV, the heavy fragment has average $Z = 113$ and $N = 182$; at energies above 1100 MeV, the heavy fragment has $111 \leq Z \leq 114$ and $180 \leq N \leq 183$.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of $^{238}$U + $^{238}$U collisions using the Quantum Molecular Dynamics model (Tian 2008, Zhao 2009, 2013, 2015, 2016) and time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory (Golabek 2009), which first identified ternary quasifission but left the roles of deformation, pairing, and orientation unclear. The results demonstrate that shell closures at $Z = 82$ and $N = 126$ strongly influence nucleon transfer, consistent with suggestions from TDHF studies (Wakhle 2014, Simenel 2021, Hinde 2022, McGlynn 2023). The predicted formation of very heavy neutron-rich systems in tail-to-tail and tail-to-side collisions provides a possible pathway for producing neutron-rich actinide and transactinide isotopes.
