# On the small-x evolution of the color quadrupole and the Weizsäcker-Williams gluon distribution

> Color quadrupoles have been found to be important in the proper description of observables sensitive to the small-x regime in nuclei as well as in the operator definition of the Weizsäcker-Williams gluon distribution. In this paper, we derive the small-x evolution equation of the quadrupole and the Weizsäcker-Williams gluon distribution without taking large N_c limit and study the properties of the equation in both dilute and saturation regime. We find that the quadrupole evolution follows the BFKL evolution in the dilute regime and then saturates in the dense region due to nonlinear terms. This leads us to conclude that the Weizsäcker-Williams gluon distribution should obey the same geometrical behavior as the dipole gluon distribution as found in the inclusive DIS measurement.

## Key claim

The color quadrupole evolution follows BFKL dynamics in the dilute regime and saturates in the dense region due to nonlinear terms, leading the authors to conclude that the Weizsäcker-Williams gluon distribution should exhibit the same geometrical scaling behavior as the dipole gluon distribution found in inclusive DIS, although the quadrupole evolution equation is much more complicated and does not close on its own.

## Method

The authors derived the small-x evolution equation for the color quadrupole amplitude and the Weizsäcker-Williams gluon distribution using the JIMWLK Hamiltonian formalism without taking the large N_c limit. They then studied the linear (BFKL) regime by expanding the quadrupole amplitude to second order in the background gauge field, and analyzed the fixed points of the evolution equations in both the dilute and saturation regimes.

## Context

This work extends the study of color quadrupoles in small-x physics, which were shown to be essential for two-particle production processes in DIS and pA collisions by Dominguez (2011) and Jalilian-Marian (2004). The paper derives the full evolution equation without the large N_c approximation used in earlier work by Jalilian-Marian (2004), and shows that the Weizsäcker-Williams gluon distribution requires quadrupole evolution rather than having a closed equation of its own. This enables systematic description of dijet processes in both DIS and pA collisions, though numerical studies of quadrupole evolution will be needed to understand details in the transition region between scaling and saturation regimes.
