# Sigma photoproduction in the resonance region

> A study of p(gamma,K)Sigma processes in an isobar model at tree level is reported. By comparing model calculations to the published SAPHIR data, we explore the possible role of different isospin I=1/2 (N*) and I=3/2 (Delta*) resonances in the reaction dynamics. In our analysis, the inclusion of the ``missing'' D_{13}(1895) resonance does only slightly improve the global description of the Sigma photoproduction data. More convincing signals for the presence of such a ``missing'' resonance emerged in the analysis of the isospin related p(gamma,K+)Lambda reaction. Various implementations of the nonresonant part of the Sigma photoproduction amplitude are presented. The sensitivity of the computed observables and extracted resonance parameters to the uncertainties inherent to the treatment of the nonresonant (background) diagrams are discussed.

# Sigma photoproduction in the resonance region

## Key claim

The inclusion of the "missing" D13(1895) resonance provides only a minor improvement in describing Sigma photoproduction data (chi-squared from 3.20 to 2.88 with five additional free parameters), in contrast to the more convincing signals for this resonance found in the isospin-related p(gamma,K+)Lambda reaction. The relative roles of different N* and Delta* resonances are rather independent of the treatment of non-resonant background diagrams, but the extracted quantitative resonance parameters show substantial model dependence.

## Method

The authors constructed a tree-level isobar model using effective Lagrangians for the p(gamma,K)Sigma reactions, including Born terms, K* t-channel exchange, and s-channel N* and Delta* resonances. They performed chi-squared minimization fits to the combined SAPHIR data sets for p(gamma,K+)Sigma0 and p(gamma,K0)Sigma+ reactions, comparing four different models (A, B, C, D) for implementing the non-resonant background diagrams, with varying treatments of hadronic form factors, hyperon resonances in the u-channel, and SU(3) flavor symmetry constraints on coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- The core set of three N* resonances (S11(1650), P11(1710), P13(1720)) alone yields chi-squared = 6.52 for the combined Sigma photoproduction data
- Adding the two Delta* resonances S31(1900) and P31(1910) improves the fit to chi-squared = 3.20 with 15 free parameters
- Including the D13(1895) resonance together with the two Delta* states gives chi-squared = 2.88 with 20 free parameters, only a minor improvement over chi-squared = 3.20 without it
- Model A achieves chi-squared = 2.03 with a cutoff mass of 0.44 GeV, near the imposed lower limit of 0.4 GeV
- Model B achieves chi-squared = 1.95 with a "hard" cutoff mass of 1.6 GeV, using Lambda*(1810) and Sigma*(1880) hyperon resonances in the u-channel
- Model C achieves chi-squared = 1.96 with g_{K+ Lambda p}/sqrt(4 pi) = -0.23 and g_{K+ Sigma0 p}/sqrt(4 pi) = 0.28, dramatically smaller than the SU(3) predictions of -3.75 and 1.09 respectively
- The SAPHIR data set contains 21 total and 70 differential cross section points for Sigma0, plus 5 total and 18 differential cross section points for Sigma+, over an energy range from threshold up to 2.0 GeV (Sigma0) and 1.55 GeV (Sigma+)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous tree-level analysis of Lambda photoproduction (Janssen et al., 2001) to the Sigma channel, building on isobar models by Feuster and Mosel (1998) and Mart and Bennhold (2000). The study addresses the long-standing issue that "bare" Born terms overpredict the measured cross sections by a factor of three or four, and explores whether the "missing" D13(1895) resonance, suggested by Mart (2000) to explain structure in Lambda photoproduction, also appears in Sigma production. The results enable future coupled-channel analyses, which Chiang et al. (2002) report can affect cross sections by about 20%, to benefit from an improved understanding of tree-level contributions.
