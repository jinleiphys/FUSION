# Pair creation in boost-invariantly expanding electric fields and two-particle correlations

> Pair creation of scalar particles in a boost-invariant electric field which is confined in the forward light cone is studied. We present the proper-time evolution of momentum distributions of created particles, which preserve the boost invariance of the background field. The two-particle correlation of the created particles is also calculated. We find that long-range rapidity correlations may arise from the Schwinger mechanism in the boost-invariant electric field.

## Key claim

Long-range rapidity correlations in particle production may arise from the Schwinger mechanism in a boost-invariant electric field confined to the forward light cone, but this result depends on the fact that particles are created as coherent superpositions of longitudinal momentum eigenstates, which the author derives as an operator relation in a field-theoretical sense rather than a classical coordinate transformation.

## Method

The author studies scalar QED pair creation in a constant, boost-invariant electric field that exists only inside the forward light cone, neglecting back-reaction. Canonical quantization is performed on equal-proper-time surfaces using curvilinear coordinates, and the physical vacuum is defined by requiring it to be the same as the Minkowski-space vacuum in Cartesian coordinates, which selects Hankel function mode solutions. The author then computes the proper-time evolution of momentum distributions and two-particle correlation functions using the Bogoliubov transformation between the Cartesian and curvilinear particle bases.

## Key numbers

- The mode functions for the boost-invariant quantization are given by Hankel functions: \(\chi^+_{\mathbf{p}_\perp,\lambda}(\tau) = \frac{\sqrt{\pi}}{2i} e^{\frac{\pi}{2}\lambda} H_{i\lambda}^{(2)}(m_\perp \tau)\) and \(\chi^-_{\mathbf{p}_\perp,\lambda}(\tau) = -\frac{\sqrt{\pi}}{2i} e^{\frac{\pi}{2}\lambda} H_{-i\lambda}^{(1)}(m_\perp \tau)\), where \(m_\perp^2 = m^2 + p_\perp^2\).
- The relation between Cartesian and curvilinear creation operators is \(a_\mathbf{p} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi \omega_p}} \int_{-\infty}^\infty d\lambda \, e^{i\lambda y_p} \mathfrak{a}_{\mathbf{p}_\perp,\lambda}\), where \(y_p = \tanh^{-1}(p_z/\omega_p)\) and \(\omega_p = \sqrt{\mathbf{p}^2 + m^2}\).
- The classical momentum conjugate to space-time rapidity is \(\lambda = p_z t - \omega_p z\), and under a boost to the particle's rest frame, \(\lambda = p_z' \tau\), where \(p_z'\) is the longitudinal momentum in that frame.

## Context

This work extends the study of the Schwinger mechanism in heavy-ion collisions by treating the electric field as confined to the forward light cone, following the flux-tube model of Low and Nussinov and the glasma framework of Lappi and McLerran. It contrasts with earlier work by Martin and Vautherin (1990), who calculated pair creation rates using Green's function expansions, and by Cooper and collaborators (1993), who defined the initial vacuum by requiring a vanishing regularized current. The author's approach enables the study of two-particle correlations, connecting to the near-side ridge phenomena observed at RHIC by STAR (2005), PHENIX (2008), and PHOBOS (2010), and more recently at the LHC by CMS, and to the multiparticle correlation work of Fukushima, Gelis, and Lappi (2009) for homogeneous fields.
