# Transport Properties of QCD at Large $N_c$ and the Gauge/String Duality

> Below the deconfinement phase transition large $N_c$ QCD is expected to be a very viscous hadronic fluid because both the shear and bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio, $η/s,ζ/s \sim N_c^2$. In this letter I show that $η/s \sim N_c^2$ in the confined phase of holographic models of QCD at large $N_c$ defined in the supergravity approximation. Our results show that the gauge/string duality can be used to describe not only nearly perfect fluids but also extremely viscous systems such as a cold gas of glueballs.

## Key claim
Below the deconfinement phase transition, large N_c QCD is expected to be a very viscous hadronic fluid with shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s ∼ N_c^2; the paper shows that η/s ∼ N_c^2 in the confined phase of holographic models of QCD at large N_c defined in the supergravity approximation, indicating that gauge/string duality can describe both nearly perfect fluids and extremely viscous systems such as a cold gas of glueballs.

## Method
The author computes the shear viscosity η in the confined phase of holographic QCD models using Kubo's formula and the gauge/string duality, evaluating the graviton absorption cross section in a horizonless thermal AdS geometry that describes the confined phase. The calculation uses the supergravity approximation with a 5d gravity action coupling a metric and dilaton field, where the confined phase background has no horizon and the entropy density vanishes at leading order in N_c. The shear viscosity is evaluated at the confining scale r_σ, using the string tension σ to obtain η ∼ N_c^2.

## Key numbers
- η/s ∼ N_c^2 in the confined phase of holographic models of QCD at large N_c, with η = (1/(16π G_5)) (σ/T_s)^(3/2) (1/λ_σ^2) where σ is the string tension, T_s = 1/(2π ℓ_s^2) is the fundamental string tension, and λ_σ = e^{Φ_0(r_σ)}.
- In the deconfined phase, η/s = 1/(4π) for any gauge theory dual to supergravity, as established by Kovtun (2004).
- The entropy density in the confined phase at low T (T/M_0 ≪ 1, with M_0 the lightest glueball mass) scales as s_0(T) ∼ (M_0/T) (M_0 T/(2π))^(3/2) e^{-M_0/T}, giving the smallest s_0 and largest η/s in the confined phase.
- The deconfinement phase transition is first-order, with a specific heat of order N_c^2.

## Context
This work extends the gauge/string duality framework, previously used to describe nearly perfect fluids in the deconfined phase (Kovtun, Son, and Starinets 2004), to the confined phase where kinetic theory predicts η/s ∼ N_c^2 (Csernai 2006). It builds on holographic QCD models by Gursoy and collaborators (2008) that reproduce lattice data for Yang-Mills theories at large N_c, and shows that these models can describe extremely viscous systems like a cold gas of glueballs. The results enable future studies of transport coefficients in the confined phase of holographic theories, including bulk viscosity and second-order coefficients like τ_π, and may provide a framework for investigating hot hadronic matter in heavy ion collisions.
