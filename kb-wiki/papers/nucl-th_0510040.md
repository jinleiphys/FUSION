# Nuclear shape dependence of Gamow-Teller distributions in neutron-deficient Pb isotopes

> We study Gamow-Teller strength distributions in the neutron-deficient even isotopes (184-194)Pb in a search for signatures of deformation. The microscopic formalism used is based on a deformed quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) approach, which involves a self-consistent quasiparticle deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock (HF) basis and residual spin-isospin forces in both the particle-hole and particle-particle channels. By analyzing the sensitivity of the Gamow-Teller strength distributions to the various ingredients in the formalism, we conclude that the beta-decay of these isotopes could be a useful tool to look for fingerprints of nuclear deformation.

## Key claim

The Gamow-Teller strength distributions in neutron-deficient even Pb isotopes (184-194) show distinct, deformation-specific patterns that persist even when the Skyrme force or pairing treatment is changed, making beta-decay a potential tool to identify nuclear shape (spherical, oblate, or prolate) despite the fact that the mean-field calculations themselves do not always reproduce the experimentally known spherical ground states.

## Method

The authors performed deformed Hartree-Fock calculations with Skyrme forces (Sk3, SG2, SLy4) and BCS pairing correlations, using either fixed gap parameters from odd-even mass differences or constant pairing strengths. They then applied a deformed quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) with separable spin-isospin residual interactions in both the particle-hole and particle-particle channels to compute Gamow-Teller strength distributions for the even-even isotopes 184-194Pb at spherical, oblate, and prolate equilibrium deformations.

## Key numbers

- The GT resonance in 208Pb from (p,n) reactions is at 19.2 MeV; the ph coupling strength is set to reproduce this: χ^{ph}_{GT} = 0.09 MeV (SG2) or 0.07 MeV (Sk3).
- The pp coupling strength used is κ^{pp}_{GT} = 0.02 MeV, compatible with the parametrization κ^{pp}_{GT} = 0.58/A^{0.7} MeV.
- For 190Pb, the experimental half-life is 71 s; the oblate calculation gives T_{1/2} = 83 s (Sk3), while the prolate gives 27 s (Sk3).
- For 192Pb, the experimental half-life is 210 s; the oblate shape (Sk3) reproduces this well, while the prolate shape gives a factor of 8 shorter half-life.
- The maximum Q_{EC} energy among the isotopes is 5.84 MeV (184Pb).
- For 194Pb, the experimental half-life is 720 s; the SG2 force with oblate shape gives a half-life in agreement with experiment, while the spherical shape gives a half-life too large.
- The oblate deformation at the minima is stable at β = -0.2; the prolate deformation reaches β = 0.32 in 184Pb.

## Context

This work extends the deformed HF+BCS+QRPA approach previously applied by Sarriguren et al. to A≈70 nuclei (Kr and Sr isotopes), where GT strength distributions were used to determine nuclear shapes via comparison with ISOLDE measurements. It addresses the shape coexistence phenomenon in neutron-deficient Pb isotopes, where experimental data (Julin, Heese, Andreyev) show low-lying 0+ states and rotational bands interpreted as oblate and prolate deformations, while mean-field calculations (Niksic 2002, Tajima) are highly sensitive to pairing and force details. The results suggest that beta-decay measurements of 188,190,192Pb, which have dominant β+/EC decay branches and half-lives of 25-210 s, could distinguish spherical, oblate, and prolate shapes by the pattern of low-energy GT strength.
