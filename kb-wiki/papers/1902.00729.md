# Extracting jet transport parameter $\hat{q}$ from a multiphase transport model

> Within a multi-phase transport model with string melting scenario, jet transport parameter $\hat{q}$ is calculated in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN} } $= 200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN} } $= 2.76 TeV. The $\hat{q}$ increases with the increasing of jet energy for both partonic phase and hadronic phase. The energy and path length dependences of $\hat{q}$ in full heavy-ion evolution are consistent with the expectations of jet quenching. The correlation between jet transport parameter $\hat{q}$ and dijet transverse momentum asymmetry $A_{J}$ is mainly investigated, which discloses that a larger $\hat{q}$ corresponds to a larger $A_{J}$. It supports a consistent jet energy loss picture from the two viewpoints of single jet and dijet. It is proposed to measure dijet asymmetry distributions with different jet transport parameter ranges as a new potential method to study jet quenching physics in high energy heavy-ion collisions.

# Extracting jet transport parameter $\hat{q}$ from a multiphase transport model

## Key claim
Within the AMPT model with string melting, the jet transport parameter $\hat{q}$ increases with jet energy in both partonic and hadronic phases, and a larger $\hat{q}$ corresponds to a larger dijet transverse momentum asymmetry $A_J$, supporting a consistent jet energy loss picture from single-jet and dijet viewpoints. The authors propose that measuring dijet asymmetry distributions for different $\hat{q}$ ranges could serve as a new method to study jet quenching, but this remains a proposal based on model calculations rather than an experimentally validated technique.

## Method
The authors used the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model with string melting scenario, incorporating a jet triggering technique in the HIJING initial condition to generate hard dijets. Jet transport parameter $\hat{q}$ was extracted by reconstructing jets before and after parton cascade (using the anti-$k_t$ algorithm with cone radius R=0.5), pairing identical jets via pseudorapidity and azimuthal angle matching criteria ($|\Delta\eta| < 0.2$, $|\Delta\phi| < 0.2$), and computing $\hat{q} = \langle p_\perp^2 \rangle / L$ where $p_\perp$ is the transverse momentum broadening and $L$ is the jet path length in coordinate space. The analysis was performed separately for partonic phase, hadronic phase, and full evolution in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV.

## Key numbers
- Jet transverse momentum threshold for leading jet: $p_T > 40$ GeV/c (or $p_T > 120$ GeV/c for $A_J$ calculations); for subleading jet: $p_T > 20$ GeV/c (or $p_T > 50$ GeV/c for $A_J$ calculations)
- Jet cone radius R = 0.5; pseudorapidity range $|\eta| < 2$
- $\hat{q}$ increases with jet energy in the partonic phase for both collision systems; $\hat{q}$ from $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV Pb+Pb is slightly larger than from $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV Au+Au at low jet energy, but close at high jet energy
- $\hat{q}$ in the hadronic phase is smaller than in the partonic phase; hadronic $\hat{q}$ from Au+Au at 200 GeV is larger than from Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV
- $\hat{q}$ decreases with increasing travel path length in the full evolution
- $\hat{q}$ increases with increasing $A_J$ for both leading and subleading jets; $\hat{q}$ for leading jet is always larger than for subleading jet

## Context
This work extends previous studies of jet quenching within the AMPT model, which had successfully described reconstructed jet observables such as $\gamma$-jet imbalance (Ma, 2013), dijet asymmetry (Ma, 2013), and jet fragmentation functions (Ma, 2013), but had not systematically extracted the jet transport parameter $\hat{q}$. The results are consistent with the expectation from Casalderrey-Solana (2007) that higher energy jets have larger transverse momentum transfer to the medium. The correlation between $\hat{q}$ and $A_J$ connects single-jet and dijet perspectives of jet quenching, and the proposed method of constraining $\hat{q}$ from dijet asymmetry distributions could complement existing approaches such as those using di-hadron correlations (Tannenbaum, 2017; Chen, 2016; Mueller, 2016) and the JET Collaboration's phenomenological extractions (Burke, 2013).
