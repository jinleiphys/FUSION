# Polarization in relativistic fluids: a quantum field theoretical derivation

> We review the calculation of polarization in a relativistic fluid within the framework of statistical quantum field theory. We derive the expressions of the spin density matrix and the mean spin vector both for a single quantum relativistic particle and for a quantum free field. After introducing the formalism of the covariant Wigner function for the scalar and the Dirac field, the relation between spin density matrix and the covariant Wigner function is obtained. The formula is applied to the fluid produced in relativistic nuclear collisions by using the local thermodynamic equilibrium density operator and recovering previously known formulae. The dependence of these results on the spin tensor and pseudo-gauge transformations of the stress-energy tensor is addressed.

## Key claim
The paper derives the exact relation between the spin density matrix and the covariant Wigner function for spin-1/2 particles, conclusively demonstrating that the expression of polarization is independent of the spin tensor and pseudo-gauge choice, contrary to earlier derivations that relied on the canonical spin tensor.

## Method
The author first derives the spin density matrix for a single relativistic quantum particle with spin S in global equilibrium using group theory techniques and a factorization of the density operator involving thermal vorticity. The covariant Wigner function formalism is then introduced for both scalar and Dirac fields, and the relation between the spin density matrix and the covariant Wigner function is obtained. The formula is applied to the fluid produced in relativistic nuclear collisions using the local thermodynamic equilibrium density operator.

## Key numbers
- The mean spin vector for a particle with spin S at global equilibrium is given by S^mu(p) = -(1/(2m))*(S(S+1)/3)*epsilon^{mu alpha beta nu}*p_nu*varpi_{alpha beta}, where varpi is the thermal vorticity tensor
- For local thermodynamic equilibrium, the formula becomes S^mu(p) = -(1/(2m))*(S(S+1)/3)*epsilon^{mu alpha beta nu}*p_nu*(integral over Sigma dSigma_lambda p^lambda f(x,p) varpi_{alpha beta}(x))/(integral over Sigma dSigma_lambda p^lambda f(x,p))
- The spin density matrix to first order in thermal vorticity is Theta(p)_{rs} approx delta_{rs}/(2S+1) + (1/(2(2S+1)))*varpi_*(p)^{alpha beta}*epsilon_{alpha beta rho nu}*D^S(J^rho)^r_{r'}*hat t^nu

## Context
This work extends the original derivation by Becattini (2013) of polarization of quasi-free particles in a relativistic fluid at local thermodynamic equilibrium, filling conceptual gaps in that earlier work. The paper addresses the dependence of results on the spin tensor and pseudo-gauge transformations of the stress-energy tensor, a topic previously discussed by Becattini (2018). The formalism enables future calculations of polarization for particles with any spin in relativistic heavy ion collisions, building on the discovery of global polarization of Lambda hyperons by the STAR collaboration (2017).
