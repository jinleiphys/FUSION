# Short-range correlations in quark matter

> We investigate the role of short-range correlations in quark matter within the framework of the SU(2) NJL model. Employing a next-to-leading order expansion in 1/N_c for the quark self energy we construct a fully self-consistent model that is based on the relations between spectral functions and self energies. In contrast to the usual quasiparticle approximations we take the collisional broadening of the quark spectral function consequently into account. Mesons are dynamically generated in the fashion of a random phase approximation, using full in-medium propagators in the quark loops. The results are self-consistently fed back into the quark self energy. Calculations have been performed for finite chemical potentials at zero temperature. The short-range correlations do not only generate finite widths in the spectral functions but also have influence on the chiral phase transition.

## Key claim

Short-range correlations in quark matter, modeled via a self-consistent next-to-leading order expansion in 1/N_c within the SU(2) NJL model, generate finite widths in quark spectral functions and influence the chiral phase transition; however, the self-consistent calculation yields a conservative estimate of the short-range correlations, and the RPA pions become massive in the chirally broken phase rather than remaining massless Goldstone modes.

## Method

The authors constructed a fully self-consistent model based on the relations between spectral functions and self energies, employing a next-to-leading order expansion in 1/N_c for the quark self energy within the SU(2) NJL model. Mesons were dynamically generated in a random phase approximation using full in-medium propagators in the quark loops, with results self-consistently fed back into the quark self energy. Calculations were performed for finite chemical potentials at zero temperature, using a three-momentum cutoff regularization and the real-time formalism for in-medium propagators.

## Key numbers

* The NJL coupling strength parameter G*Lambda^2 is approximately 2.14 (Set 0, chiral limit) and 2.19 (Set I, with m_0 = 5.5 MeV), where Lambda is the three-momentum cutoff.
* The three-momentum cutoff Lambda is 653 MeV (Set 0) and 631 MeV (Set I).
* The effective quark mass in vacuum is 313 MeV (Set 0) and 336 MeV (Set I).
* The quark condensate in vacuum is -250 MeV (Set 0) and -247 MeV (Set I) when raised to the 1/3 power.
* At zero temperature, the fermionic distribution function is a step function: n_F(k_0) = Theta(mu - k_0).
* At zero temperature, the bosonic distribution function is n_B(k_0) = -Theta(-k_0).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier approach (Froemel and Leupold, 2001) which used Born diagrams in a loop-expansion and found quark widths more than one order of magnitude smaller than in nuclear matter. It also builds on 1/N_c expansions in the NJL model by Quack (1993) and Dmitrasinovic (1995), and on self-consistent calculations of short-range correlations in nuclear matter by Lehr (2000, 2001). The approach enables future studies of flavor asymmetric systems and could serve as input for additional formalisms yielding realistic pion properties, though color superconductivity and diquark condensates are not considered.
