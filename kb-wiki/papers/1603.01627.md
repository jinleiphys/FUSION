# Low-energy $^{6}$He scattering in a microscopic model

> A microscopic version of the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) method is used to investigate $^{6}$He scattering on $^{27}$Al, $^{58}$Ni, $^{120}$Sn, and $^{208}$Pb at energies around the Coulomb barrier. The $^{6}$He nucleus is described by an antisymmetric 6-nucleon wave function, defined in the Resonating Group Method. The $^{6}$He continuum is simulated by square-integrable positive-energy states. The model is based only on well known nucleon-target potentials, and is therefore does not depend on any adjustable parameter. I show that experimental elastic cross sections are fairly well reproduced. The calculation suggests that breakup effects increase for high target masses. For a light system such as $^{6}$He+$^{27}$Al, breakup effects are small, and a single-channel approximation provides fair results. This property is explained by a very simple model, based on the sharp-cut-off approximation for the scattering matrix. I also investigate the $^{6}$He-target optical potentials, which confirm that breakup channels are more and more important when the mass increases. At large distances, polarization effects increase the Coulomb barrier, and provide a long-tail absorption component in the imaginary part of the nucleus-nucleus interaction.

# Low-energy $^{6}$He scattering in a microscopic model

## Key claim
Experimental elastic cross sections for $^{6}$He scattering on targets from $^{27}$Al to $^{208}$Pb at energies around the Coulomb barrier are fairly well reproduced by a microscopic CDCC model with no adjustable parameters, but the agreement is not perfect at all angles and convergence is slow for heavy targets. The calculation suggests that breakup effects increase for high target masses, while for light systems such as $^{6}$He+$^{27}$Al, breakup effects are small and a single-channel approximation provides fair results.

## Method
A microscopic version of the Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (MCDCC) method was applied, where the $^{6}$He projectile is described by an antisymmetric 6-nucleon wave function using the Resonating Group Method (RGM) with an $\alpha$+n+n cluster approximation. The $^{6}$He continuum was simulated by square-integrable positive-energy pseudostates obtained from the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM), and the nucleon-target optical potentials were taken from the Koning and Delaroche (2003) compilation. Elastic cross sections were computed for $^{6}$He scattering on $^{27}$Al, $^{58}$Ni, $^{120}$Sn, and $^{208}$Pb, and equivalent polarization potentials were extracted from the coupled-channel calculations.

## Key numbers
- $^{6}$He two-neutron separation energy: $S_{2n} = -0.973$ MeV
- $^{6}$He matter radius from the model: $\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle_m} = 2.35$ fm (experiment: $2.33 \pm 0.04$ fm)
- $^{6}$He charge radius from the model: $\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle_p} = 1.80$ fm (experiment: $2.054 \pm 0.014$ fm)
- Coulomb barrier for $^{6}$He+$^{208}$Pb: $V_B = 18.4$ MeV (c.m. energy at 22 MeV lab: $E_{c.m.} = 21.4$ MeV)
- Coulomb barrier for $^{6}$He+$^{27}$Al: $V_B \approx 3.9$ MeV (c.m. energies studied: 7.8, 9.0, 9.8, 11.0 MeV)
- For $^{6}$He+$^{208}$Pb at $E_{lab} = 22$ MeV, convergence required $j_{max} = 3$ and $E_{max} = 15$ MeV
- For $^{6}$He+$^{208}$Pb, inclusion of non-natural-parity states ($j=0^-,1^+,2^-$) changed cross sections by less than 0.5%
- For $^{6}$He+$^{208}$Pb, the imaginary part of the polarization potential is negative beyond $R = 11$ fm

## Context
This work extends the microscopic CDCC (MCDCC) approach previously applied to $^{7}$Li by Descouvemont and collaborators (2013, 2015) to the three-cluster projectile $^{6}$He, which is a halo nucleus with an $\alpha$ core and two neutrons. The results are compared with non-microscopic CDCC calculations by Rodriguez-Gallardo et al. (2008) for $^{6}$He+$^{208}$Pb and with experimental data from various groups (e.g., Sanchez-Benitez et al. 2008, Acosta et al. 2011 for $^{208}$Pb; Benjamim et al. 2007 for $^{27}$Al; Mohr et al. 2014 for $^{58}$Ni; Di Pietro et al. 2010 for $^{120}$Sn). The study enables systematic investigation of breakup effects across a wide target mass range using identical theoretical conditions, and provides equivalent $^{6}$He-target polarization potentials that clarify the role of breakup in elastic scattering. The weak halo effect relative to breakup effects is explained through the volume integral property of folding potentials.
