# A Detailed Study and Synthesis of Flow Observables in the IP-Glasma+MUSIC+UrQMD Framework

> In this work we use the IP-Glasma+MUSIC+UrQMD framework to systematically study a wide range of hadronic flow observables at 2.76 TeV. In addition to the single particle spectra and anisotropic flow coefficients $v_n$ previously studied in \cite{1609.02958}, we consider event-plane correlations, non-linear response coefficients $χ_{npq}$, and event shape engineering. Taken together, these observables provide a wealth of insight into the collective behavior of the QGP and initial state fluctuations. They shed light on flow fluctuations, flow at fixed system size but different initial geometries, as well as the non-linear hydrodynamic response to the initial state spatial eccentricities. By synthesizing this information we can gain further insight into the transport properties of the QGP as well as the fluctuation spectrum of the initial state.

## Key claim

The IP-Glasma+MUSIC+UrQMD framework qualitatively reproduces the spread of values and correlations between flow harmonics seen in experimental data, but the overestimation of some observables is largely due to the fact that the transport coefficients (shear viscosity to entropy density ratio and temperature-dependent bulk viscosity) were previously extracted from particle spectra and integrated flow harmonics at the expense of not describing the differential flow harmonics for all transverse momenta.

## Method

The authors used the IP-Glasma model to generate event-by-event initial conditions with sub-nucleonic fluctuations, then evolved the system with the second-order relativistic viscous hydrodynamics code MUSIC using a constant shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of 0.095 and a temperature-dependent bulk viscosity. After hydrodynamic evolution, the fluid hadronized at an isothermal hypersurface of 145 MeV, from which the UrQMD model handled resonance decays and hadronic re-scatterings. They computed event-plane correlations, non-linear response coefficients, and event shape engineering observables for Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV and compared them to ATLAS and ALICE data.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: η/s = 0.095 (constant)
- Hadronization temperature: T_sw = 145 MeV (isothermal hypersurface)
- Collision energy: 2.76 TeV (Pb-Pb collisions)
- Transverse momentum range for event shape engineering analysis: 0.5 GeV ≤ p_T ≤ 2.0 GeV (compared to ATLAS data)
- Transverse momentum range for transport coefficient extraction: 0.2 GeV ≤ p_T ≤ 5.0 GeV (using ALICE data)
- The non-linear response coefficient χ_6222 is defined as ⟨V_6(V_2^*)^3⟩/⟨|V_2|^6⟩ and χ_633 as ⟨V_6(V_3^*)^2⟩/⟨|V_3|^4⟩
- The v_6{Ψ_2} contribution overtakes v_6{Ψ_3} at approximately 20% centrality

## Context

This work extends the study of single particle spectra and anisotropic flow coefficients v_n from the same framework reported in McDonald et al. (2016) to include event-plane correlations, non-linear response coefficients, and event shape engineering. The anti-correlation between v_2 and v_3 in peripheral collisions, previously observed by ALICE (2016) using non-symmetric cumulants, is confirmed in both experiment and simulation. The results enable further constraints on the shear and bulk viscosities of the quark-gluon plasma through direct comparison of these more differential observables between theory and experiment, and the event shape engineering technique provides many data points by which to constrain model parameters, building on the work of Qian et al. (2016) showing sensitivity of the "boomerang" relationship to shear viscosity.
