# A Model for the Parton Distribution in Nuclei

## Key claim

The authors extend a simple Gaussian-based model of parton distributions in free nucleons to nuclei, claiming that by modifying only the width of the primordial partonic Gaussian (reduced by about 10%) and shifting the pionic energy distribution to zero effective mass, they can describe the nuclear structure function ratio \(R(x) = F_2^A(x)/F_2^D(x)\) over most of the Bjorken \(x\) range, except for very small \(x \leq 0.06\) where the convolution model is not justifiable.

## Method

The authors start from a model where partons in a nucleon at rest have a spherically symmetric Gaussian momentum distribution with width \(\sigma_q = 0.18\) GeV for valence quarks and \(\sigma_\pi = 0.052\) GeV for pions (the source of sea quarks). They extend this to nuclei by incorporating Fermi motion of nucleons via a relativistic Fermi gas distribution \(\rho^A(y_A)\) and by modifying the bound nucleon's parton distributions: the Gaussian width for quarks is reduced to \(\sigma_q = 0.165\) GeV and the pionic energy distribution is centered at \(m_\pi = 0\) instead of 0.14 GeV. The nuclear structure function is computed via a convolution of the nucleon distribution function with the momentum-dependent bound-nucleon structure function, using a Monte Carlo procedure that accepts events satisfying kinematic constraints on the invariant mass.

## Key numbers

- Width of primordial Gaussian for valence quarks in free nucleon: \(\sigma_q = 0.18\) GeV; reduced to \(\sigma_q = 0.165\) GeV for bound nucleons in nuclei (about 10% decrease).
- Width of pionic distribution in nucleon: \(\sigma_\pi = 0.052\) GeV (unchanged in nuclear medium).
- Pion mass shifted from \(m_\pi = 0.14\) GeV to \(m_\pi = 0\) for the energy distribution of nucleonic pions in nuclei.
- Average nuclear density used for \(^{56}\)Fe: \(\rho = 0.12\) fm\(^{-3}\), with Fermi momentum \(p_F = 240\) MeV.
- Nucleon chemical potential: \(\mu = 8\) MeV (essentially constant except for very light nuclei).
- Momentum sum rule violated by about 2% in the successful fit, implying that fraction of nucleonic momentum is taken by gluons.
- Sea quarks carry 7.7% of momentum in free nucleons (unchanged from the original model).
- The model fails for \(x \leq 0.06\), where the convolution approach is not justifiable.

## Context

This work extends the parton distribution model of Edin and Ingelman (1999) from free nucleons to nuclei, and builds on the authors' prior work (Rozynek, Szwed, and Wilk) on nuclear parton distributions. The approach contrasts with the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) theory, which the authors show fails to describe nuclear structure function data when using unmodified nucleon structure functions. The model enables a simple parametrization of nuclear effects (nucleon swelling and effective pion mass shift) that reproduces the characteristic pattern of shadowing, antishadowing, the EMC effect dip near \(x \sim 0.7\), and the cumulative region, though it does not address the very small-\(x\) shadowing region where more sophisticated treatments are needed.
