# Equation of state for hot QCD and compact stars from a mean field approach

> The thermodynamic properties of high temperature and high density QCD-matter are explored within the Chiral SU(3)-flavor parity-doublet Polyakov-loop quark-hadron mean-field model, CMF. The quark sector of the CMF model is tuned to describe the $μ_B=0$ thermodynamics data of lattice QCD. The resulting lines of constant physical variables as well as the baryon number susceptibilities are studied in some detail in the temperature/chemical potential plane. The CMF model predicts three consecutive transitions, the nuclear first-order liquid-vapor phase transition, chiral symmetry restoration, and the cross-over transition to a quark-dominated phase. All three phenomena are cross-over, for most of the $T-μ_B$-plane. The deviations from the free ideal hadron gas baseline at $μ_B=0$ and $T\approx 100-200$ MeV can be attributed to remnants of the liquid-vapor first order phase transition in nuclear matter. The chiral crossing transition determines the baryon fluctuations at much higher $μ_B\approx1.5$ GeV, and at even higher baryon densities $μ_B\approx2.4$ GeV, the behavior of fluctuations is controlled by the deconfinement cross-over. The CMF model also describe well the static properties of high $μ_B$ neutron stars as well as the new neutron star merger observations. The effective EoS presented here describes simultaneously lattice QCD results at $μ_B=0$, as well as observed physical phenomena (nuclear matter and neutron star matter) at $T\cong0$ and high densities, $μ_B>1$ GeV.

## Key claim

The Chiral SU(3)-flavor parity-doublet Polyakov-loop quark-hadron mean-field (CMF) model simultaneously describes lattice QCD thermodynamics at zero baryon chemical potential and nuclear matter/neutron star properties at zero temperature and high densities, but the chiral condensate drops more slowly with temperature at $\mu_B=0$ than lattice QCD indicates because baryonic resonances are not coupled to the chiral fields in the current implementation.

## Method

The CMF model combines a parity-doublet baryon octet with mesonic mean fields, excluded-volume corrections for hadrons, and a Polyakov-loop potential for quarks, all within a mean-field approach. The quark sector parameters (Polyakov-loop potential coefficients and quark-chiral field couplings) were tuned by scanning an $8\times6\times7\times6\times6$ grid to minimize the root-mean-square deviation of the model's trace anomaly $I/T^4$ from lattice QCD results at $\mu_B=0$. The model then predicts baryon number susceptibilities, lines of constant physics, and the phase diagram in the temperature-chemical potential plane, and is applied to heavy-ion collisions via the Taub adiabat and to neutron star properties.

## Key numbers

- Trace anomaly $I/T^4$ at $\mu_B=0$ fitted to lattice QCD data from Borsanyi (2013) and Bazavov (2014) over $T\approx 100-200$ MeV.
- Best-fit Polyakov-loop potential parameters: $T_0 = 180.0$ MeV, $a_1 = -11.67$, $a_2 = 9.33$, $b_3 = -0.53$, and $g_{q\sigma}=g_{s\zeta} = -1.0$.
- Baryon mass in the restored phase: $m_0 = 759$ MeV; strange quark mass: $m_s = 130$ MeV.
- Quark explicit mass terms: $\delta m_q = 5$ MeV, $\delta m_s = 150$ MeV, $m_{0q} = 253$ MeV.
- Excluded volumes: $v_B = 1$ fm$^3$ for (anti-)baryons, $v_M = 1/8$ fm$^3$ for mesons, $v_q = 0$ for quarks.
- Chiral critical point temperature: $T_{\rm chiral}^{\rm CP} \approx 17$ MeV.
- Transitions at $\mu_B \approx 1.5$ GeV (chiral symmetry restoration) and $\mu_B \approx 2.4$ GeV (deconfinement cross-over).
- Speed of sound reaches $c_s^2 \approx 0.7$ in the high-density nuclear matter region before deconfinement.
- For $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.2$ GeV ($E_{\rm lab}=1.6$ GeV): $S/A=2.9$, $T=63.3$ MeV, $n_B/n_0=2.6$, quark fraction $0.0$.
- For $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 9.1$ GeV ($E_{\rm lab}=43.2$ GeV): $S/A=15.0$, $T=278.9$ MeV, $n_B/n_0=21.3$, quark fraction $1.0$.

## Context

This work extends the CMF model previously developed by Steinheimer (2010, 2011), Dexheimer (2012), and Mukherjee (2016) by tuning the quark sector to lattice QCD data from the Wuppertal-Budapest and HotQCD collaborations (Borsanyi 2013, Bazavov 2014, 2017). It simultaneously describes nuclear matter saturation properties, neutron star observations including the GW170817 merger event, and lattice QCD thermodynamics at $\mu_B=0$, enabling unified studies of heavy-ion collision trajectories and neutron star mergers within a single equation of state. The model predicts three consecutive transitions (liquid-vapor, chiral restoration, deconfinement) that are cross-overs for most of the phase diagram, with first-order transitions only below $T<50$ MeV.
