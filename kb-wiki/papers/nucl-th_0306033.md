# Rho meson photoproduction at low energies

> The $σ$-exchange and $f_2$-exchange mechanisms for $ρ$ meson photoproduction are re-examined. Then the commonly employed $σ$-exchange amplitude is revised by using the recent information from the analyses on the $ρ\to π^0π^0γ$ decay and the $σNN$ coupling constant from Bonn potential. Instead of relying on the Pomeron-$f$ proportionality assumption, the $f_2$ meson exchange amplitude is established from an effective Lagrangian which is constructed from the tensor structure of the $f_2$ meson. Phenomenological information together with tensor meson dominance and vector meson dominance assumptions are used to estimate the $f_2$ coupling constants. As a first step to improve the current theoretical models, we have also explored the effects due to the un-correlated $2π$ exchange amplitude with $πN$ intermediate state. This leading-order $2π$ exchange amplitude can be calculated using the coupling constants determined from the study of pion photoproduction and the empirical width of $ρ\to ππ$ decay. In comparing with the existing differential cross section data, we find that a model with the constructed $2π$, $σ$, and $f_2$ exchanges is comparable to the commonly used $σ$ exchange model in which the $σ$ coupling parameters are simply adjusted to fit the experimental data. We suggest that experimental verifications of the predicted single and double spin asymmetries in the small $|t|$ ($ < 2$ GeV$^2$) region will be useful for distinguishing the two models and improving our understanding of the non-resonant amplitude of $ρ$ photoproduction. Possible further improvements of the model are discussed.

## Key claim

The commonly used σ-exchange model for ρ meson photoproduction is inconsistent with the empirical decay width of ρ → π⁰π⁰γ, and a model combining uncorrelated 2π exchange, a revised σ exchange with coupling fixed by that decay, and an f₂ exchange constructed from the tensor structure of the f₂ meson can reproduce the differential cross section data comparably well, but the two models can only be distinguished by measuring single and double spin asymmetries in the small |t| (< 2 GeV²) region.

## Method

The authors constructed two models for γp → ρp. Model (A) includes Pomeron, σ, π, η exchanges and s- and u-channel nucleon terms, with the σ-exchange parameters freely adjusted to fit data. Model (B) replaces the σ exchange with a newly constructed f₂ exchange amplitude derived from an effective Lagrangian respecting the tensor structure of the f₂ meson, plus an uncorrelated 2π exchange amplitude with a πN intermediate state calculated using coupling constants from pion photoproduction and the empirical ρ → ππ width; a revised σ exchange with g_{ρσγ} fixed by the ρ → π⁰π⁰γ decay width and g_{σNN} from the Bonn potential is also included. The f₂ coupling constants were estimated using tensor meson dominance and vector meson dominance assumptions.

## Key numbers

- g_{ρσγ} ≈ 0.25 from the SND experiment branching ratio BR(ρ → σγ) = (1.9 +0.9 −0.8 ± 0.4) × 10⁻⁵, giving Γ(ρ → σγ) ≈ 2.83 keV
- g_{ρππ} = 6.04 determined from Γ(ρ⁰ → π⁺π⁻) = 150.7 MeV
- g_{ρσγ} = 3.0 in the conventional σ-exchange model (model A), an order of magnitude larger than the value from ρ → π⁰π⁰γ decay
- g_{σNN}²/4π = 8.0 from Bonn potential
- f₂ coupling constants: G_{fNN}²/4π = 2.2, F_{fNN} = 0, G_{fVV} = 5.76
- Cutoff parameters for f₂ exchange: Λ_{fNN} = Λ_{fVγ} = 1.4 GeV
- σ meson mass M_σ = 0.5 GeV
- f₂(1270) mass M_f = 1275.4 ± 1.2 MeV, width Γ(f₂) = 185.1 +3.4 −2.6 MeV
- g_{ρNN} = 6.2, κ_ρ = 1.0 from analyses of pion photoproduction and πN scattering
- g_{ργπ} = 0.756 from Γ(ρ⁰ → π⁰γ) = 121 ± 31 keV
- g_{ργη} = 1.476 from Γ(ρ⁰ → ηγ) = 62 ± 17 keV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of nonresonant mechanisms in vector meson photoproduction (Oh and Lee, 2002; Oh, Titov, and Lee, 2001) and addresses inconsistencies in the σ-exchange model of Friman and Soyeur (1996). It contradicts the f₂ exchange approach of Laget (2000) and Kondratyuk et al. (2001-2002), which relied on the Pomeron-f proportionality assumption and treated the f₂ as a vector particle. The model enables future experimental tests of spin asymmetries at Jefferson Lab (CLAS), GRAAL, and LEPS to distinguish between competing nonresonant mechanisms, which is essential for resolving the "missing resonance" problem in baryon spectroscopy.
