# Beyond the Standard Model with Precision Nucleon Matrix Elements on the Lattice

> Precision measurements of nucleons provide constraints on the Standard Model and can discern the signatures predicted for particles beyond the Standard Model (BSM). Knowing the Standard Model inputs to nucleon matrix elements will be necessary to constrain the couplings of dark matter candidates such as the neutralino, to relate the neutron electric dipole moment to the CP-violating theta parameter, or to search for new TeV-scale particles though non-$V-A$ interactions in neutron beta decay. However, these matrix elements derive from the properties of quantum chromodynamics (QCD) at low energies, where perturbative treatments fail. Using lattice gauge theory, we can nonperturbatively calculate the QCD path integral on a supercomputer. In this proceeding, I will discuss a few representative areas in which lattice QCD (LQCD) can contribute to the search for BSM physics, emphasizing suppressed operators in neutron decay, and outline prospects for future development.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD can provide nonperturbative Standard Model inputs for nucleon matrix elements needed to interpret low-energy precision experiments searching for beyond-the-Standard-Model physics, but the precision of these lattice calculations must be improved so that they do not become the limiting factor in constraining new physics.

## Method

The author reviews three representative lattice QCD calculations: the strange-quark contribution to the proton scalar density (sigma term) via direct calculation and the Feynman-Hellman theorem; the neutron electric dipole moment via electromagnetic form factors, external electric field methods, and anomalous magnetic moment measurements; and the scalar and tensor charges \(g_S\) and \(g_T\) relevant for non-\(V-A\) interactions in neutron beta decay. Global fits are performed to existing dynamical lattice data from multiple collaborations, with chiral extrapolations to the physical pion mass.

## Key numbers

- Strange sigma term from global fit to dynamical lattice data: \(\sigma_s^{\rm LQCD} = 47(8)\) MeV
- Neutron electric dipole moment from combined lattice data extrapolated to physical pion mass: \(d_n^{\rm LQCD} = -0.015(5)\ \theta e\cdot{\rm fm}\)
- Tensor charge from global analysis of \(N_f=2+1\) lattice calculations: \(g_T^{\rm LQCD} = 0.95(5)\)
- Scalar charge from global analysis of \(N_f=2+1\) lattice calculations: \(g_S^{\rm LQCD} = 0.69(9)\)
- Lower bounds on new physics scales from combining lattice results with expected 2013 experimental precision: \(\Lambda_S \geq 2.9\) TeV and \(\Lambda_T \geq 11.2\) TeV
- Typical neutralino-nucleon cross-section in Constrained Minimal Supersymmetry Standard Model: about 1 zeptobarn (\(10^{-49}\ {\rm m}^2\))

## Context

This proceeding extends the lattice QCD calculations of nucleon matrix elements by groups such as JLQCD, QCDSF, and the PNDME collaboration, and provides global fits that improve upon earlier estimates by Giedt (2009). The results enable constraints on dark matter candidates like the neutralino, relate the neutron electric dipole moment to the CP-violating theta parameter, and set lower bounds on the TeV-scale of new particles in scalar and tensor interaction channels. Future work requires physical pion masses, nonperturbative renormalization, improved statistics, and continuum extrapolation to match the precision of upcoming experiments such as the ultra-cold neutron beta decay study at LANL.
