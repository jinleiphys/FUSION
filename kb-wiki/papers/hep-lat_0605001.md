# Glauber dynamics of phase transitions: SU(3) lattice gauge theory

> Motivated by questions about the QCD deconfining phase transition, we studied in two previous papers Model A (Glauber) dynamics of 2D and 3D Potts models, focusing on structure factor evolution under heating (heating in the gauge theory notation, i.e., cooling of the spin systems). In the present paper we set for 3D Potts models (Ising and 3-state) the scale of the dynamical effects by comparing to equilibrium results at first and second order phase transition temperatures, obtained by re-weighting from a multicanonical ensemble. Our finding is that the dynamics entirely overwhelms the critical and non-critical equilibrium effects. In the second half of the paper we extend our results by investigating the Glauber dynamics of pure SU(3) lattice gauge on $N_τ N_σ^3$ lattices directly under heating quenches from the confined into the deconfined regime. The exponential growth factors of the initial response are calculated, which give Debye screening mass estimates. The quench leads to competing vacuum domains of distinct $Z_3$ triality, which delay equilibration of pure gauge theory forever, while their role in full QCD remains a subtle question. As in spin systems we find for pure SU(3) gauge theory a dynamical growth of structure factors, reaching maxima which scale approximately with the volume of the system, before settling down to equilibrium. Their influence on various observables is studied and different lattice sizes are simulated to illustrate an approach to a finite volume continuum limit. Strong correlations are found during the dynamical process, but not in the deconfined phase at equilibrium.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the dynamical growth of structure factors after a heating quench in pure SU(3) lattice gauge theory entirely overwhelms critical and non-critical equilibrium effects, and that competing Z3 vacuum domains delay equilibration forever in pure gauge theory, though their role in full QCD remains a subtle question.

## Method

The authors performed Glauber (Model A) dynamics simulations using heatbath algorithms for 3D Potts models (Ising and 3-state) and pure SU(3) lattice gauge theory on Nτ Nσ³ lattices, applying heating quenches from the confined into the deconfined regime. For Potts models, equilibrium properties were established via multicanonical simulations covering a temperature range from β_min=0 to β_max values above the transition temperature, while for SU(3) gauge theory the quenches started from 6/g²=5.5 to final coupling constants corresponding to T_f/T_c = 1.25 and 1.568. The exponential growth factors of the initial response were calculated to estimate Debye screening masses, and structure factor evolution was tracked through the spinodal decomposition period.

## Key numbers

- For the 3D Ising model on an 80³ lattice, the maximum of the first structure factor F₁^max = 257.4 (2.4) at β_m = 0.221462, with the infinite volume critical temperature estimated as β_c = 0.22157 (3)
- A two-parameter fit to the finite size scaling form for the 3D Ising model gave exponent b = 1.959 (12) with goodness of fit Q = 0.82, compatible with η = 0.0364 (5)
- For the 3D 3-state Potts model on a 50³ lattice, F₁^max = 80.46 (55) at β_m = 0.275181, with the transition temperature β_c = 0.2752720 (49)
- For SU(3) gauge theory on 4 × Nσ³ lattices quenched to 6/g²_f = 5.92, the structure factor maxima scale approximately as V = L³ and the time positions t^max(Nσ) ∼ Nσ²
- The time rescaling factors λ_t(Nτ,1.25) were 1:2.655:5.457 for Nτ = 4:6:8, and λ_t(Nτ,1.568) were 1:2.768:6.362 for the same Nτ values
- For T_f/T_c = 1.568, the Nτ = 6 and 8 fits for the critical momentum p_c were within statistical errors identical

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same authors (Bazavov, Berg, Velytsky, 2004-2005) on Glauber dynamics of 2D and 3D Potts models, which focused on structure factor evolution under heating quenches. The SU(3) gauge theory results are compared to the Potts model analogue, where spins mimic Polyakov loops as suggested by Svetitsky and Yaffe (1982). The exponential growth factors are interpreted using a phenomenological model by Miao and Ogilvie (2002) to estimate Debye screening masses, and the finite volume continuum limit is approached by increasing Nτ from 4 to 6 to 8 while keeping the physical volume constant. The results enable future studies of non-equilibrium effects in heavy ion experiments and raise questions about the role of Z3 vacuum domains in full QCD.
