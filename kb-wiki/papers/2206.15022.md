# Radiative neutron capture reaction rates for r-process nucleosynthesis

> About half of the elements beyond iron are synthesized in stars by rapid-neutron capture process (r-process). The stellar environment provides very high neutron flux in a short time ($\sim$ seconds) which is conducive for the creation of progressively neutron-rich nuclei till the waiting point is reached after which no further neutron capture reactions proceed. At this point such extremely neutron-rich nuclei become stable via $β^-$ decay. A detailed understanding of the r-process remains illusive. In the present work, we explore the radiative neutron-capture (n,$γ$) cross sections and reaction rates around the r-process peak near mass number eighty. The inherent uncertainties remain large in some cases, particularly in case of neutron-rich nuclei. When the low-energy enhancement exists, it results in significant increase in the reaction rate for neutron-capture.

# Radiative neutron capture reaction rates for r-process nucleosynthesis

## Key claim

The Hauser-Feshbach statistical model calculations of (n,γ) cross sections and reaction rates for Fe, Co, Ni, Cu, Zn, Ga, Ge, As, and Se isotopes around the r-process peak near mass number 80 show that inherent uncertainties remain large for neutron-rich nuclei, and that when a low-energy enhancement in the gamma-strength function exists, it results in a significant increase in the neutron-capture reaction rate.

## Method

The authors performed Hauser-Feshbach statistical model calculations using the TALYS computer code, employing temperature-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov level densities based on the Gogny force and a Brink-Axel Lorentzian for the gamma-ray strength function. Radiative neutron-capture cross sections were computed as functions of incident neutron energy, and Maxwellian-averaged thermonuclear reaction rates were obtained by folding these cross sections with the Maxwell-Boltzmann energy distribution at stellar temperatures. The calculated reaction rates were compared with the JINA REACLIB database (including the ka, kd, and ks compilations).

## Key numbers

- The r-process requires of the order of 100 neutrons per seed nucleus to produce the heaviest r-process elements.
- Neutron-rich weakly bound nuclei are formed with neutron separation energies as low as 2 MeV during r-process nucleosynthesis.
- The r-process abundance peaks occur at mass numbers approximately 80, 130, and 195, linked with neutron magic numbers at 50, 82, and 126.
- The r-process environment provides extremely high neutron densities of approximately 10^24 free neutrons per cm^3.
- The Avogadro number used in reaction rate calculations is 6.023 × 10^23.
- Experimental results for (n,γ) cross sections are uncertain by a few orders of magnitude even for nuclei in the vicinity of the valley of stability.

## Context

This work extends prior Hauser-Feshbach statistical model calculations of neutron-capture reaction rates (Rauscher 1997, Rauscher 2000, Rauscher 2001, Rauscher 2010) to isotopes around the A ~ 80 r-process peak, using updated TALYS capabilities including parity-dependent level densities and width fluctuation corrections. The calculations are compared with the JINA REACLIB database (Bao and Kappeler, Dillman et al., and KADoNiS compilations). The authors identify an acute need for more experimental data to constrain the (n,γ) reaction rates near mass region 80, and suggest that new techniques such as the surrogate method for neutron-rich nuclei (Kozub 2012) and the beta-Oslo method (Spyrou 2014) may provide crucial information.
