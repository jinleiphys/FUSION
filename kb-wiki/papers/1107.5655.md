# Covariant description of shape evolution and shape coexistence in neutron-rich nuclei at N\approx60

> The shape evolution and shape coexistence phenomena in neutron-rich nuclei at $N\approx60$, including Kr, Sr, Zr, and Mo isotopes, are studied in the covariant density functional theory (DFT) with the new parameter set PC-PK1. Pairing correlations are treated using the BCS approximation with a separable pairing force. Sharp rising in the charge radii of Sr and Zr isotopes at N=60 is observed and shown to be related to the rapid changing in nuclear shapes. The shape evolution is moderate in neighboring Kr and Mo isotopes. Similar as the results of previous Hartree-Fock-Bogogliubov (HFB) calculations with the Gogny force, triaxiality is observed in Mo isotopes and shown to be essential to reproduce quantitatively the corresponding charge radii. In addition, the coexistence of prolate and oblate shapes is found in both $^{98}$Sr and $^{100}$Zr. The observed oblate and prolate minima are related to the low single-particle energy level density around the Fermi surfaces of neutron and proton respectively. Furthermore, the 5-dimensional (5D) collective Hamiltonian determined by the calculations of the PC-PK1 energy functional is solved for $^{98}$Sr and $^{100}$Zr. The resultant excitation energy of $0^+_2$ state and E0 transition strength $ρ^2(E0;0^+_2\rightarrow0^+_1)$ are in rather good agreement with the data. It is found that the lower barrier height separating the two competing minima along the $γ$ deformation in $^{100}$Zr gives rise to the larger $ρ^2(E0;0^+_2\rightarrow0^+_1)$ than that in $^{98}$Sr.

## Key claim

The covariant density functional theory with the PC-PK1 parameter set and a separable pairing force reproduces the sharp rise in charge radii of Sr and Zr isotopes at N=60 and predicts shape coexistence in 98Sr and 100Zr, but the calculated E0 transition strengths overestimate the experimental values, indicating that the quantitative description of shape mixing remains imperfect.

## Method

The authors performed constrained relativistic mean-field (RMF) plus BCS calculations using the point-coupling interaction PC-PK1 in the particle-hole channel and a separable pairing force in the particle-particle channel. Potential energy surfaces (PES) in the beta-gamma plane were computed for even-even Kr, Sr, Zr, and Mo isotopes near N=60, and a five-dimensional (5D) collective Hamiltonian was constructed from the PES and solved for 98Sr and 100Zr to obtain excitation energies and E0 transition strengths.

## Key numbers

- Charge radius of prolate minimum in Sr isotopes is about 0.12 fm larger than that of the spherical minimum, originating from prolate deformation.
- In 98Sr, the triaxial barrier separating prolate and oblate minima has a height of approximately 2.22 MeV.
- In 98Sr, the spherical barrier height is about 3.4 MeV, lowered to about 2.2 MeV after considering the gamma degree of freedom.
- In 100Zr, the barrier height along gamma deformation is about 0.5 MeV.
- Calculated excitation energy of the 0+2 state in 98Sr is 0.216 MeV (experimental: 0.215 MeV).
- Calculated excitation energy of the 0+2 state in 100Zr is 0.468 MeV (experimental: 0.331 MeV).
- Calculated E0 transition strength rho^2(E0; 0+2 -> 0+1) in 98Sr is 116.841 x 10^-3 (experimental: 51(5) x 10^-3).
- Calculated E0 transition strength rho^2(E0; 0+2 -> 0+1) in 100Zr is 150.321 x 10^-3 (experimental: 108(19) x 10^-3).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of shape evolution in the A~100 region using covariant density functional theory, complementing nonrelativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with the Gogny force by Delaroche et al. (2010) and Rodriguez-Guzman et al. (2010). The results enable further investigations of shape coexistence using the 5D collective Hamiltonian approach, and the overestimation of E0 strengths highlights the need for improved treatment of shape mixing or beyond-mean-field effects.
