# Importance of Non-Linear Couplings in Fusion Barrier Distributions and Mean Angular Momenta

> The effects of higher order coupling of surface vibrations to the relative motion on heavy-ion fusion reactions at near-barrier energies are investigated. The coupled channels equations are solved to all orders, and also in the linear and the quadratic coupling approximations. It is shown that the shape of fusion barrier distributions and the energy dependence of the average angular momentum of the compound nucleus can significantly change when the higher order couplings are included. The role of octupole vibrational excitation of ^{16}O in the ^{16}O + ^{144}Sm fusion reaction is also discussed using the all order coupled-channels equations.

## Key claim

Higher-order (non-linear) couplings of surface vibrations to relative motion significantly alter the shape of fusion barrier distributions and the energy dependence of average angular momenta, even in very asymmetric systems where coupling is weak; the linear coupling approximation is not valid even in such systems, and its use can lead to spurious conclusions when comparing with high-precision data.

## Method

The authors solved coupled-channels equations for heavy-ion fusion reactions at near-barrier energies, treating the coupling between relative motion and nuclear surface vibrations to all orders (full coupling) by evaluating nuclear coupling matrix elements without expanding the deformed nuclear potential. They compared these all-order results with calculations using the linear coupling approximation (keeping only the first-order term in the deformation parameter) and the quadratic coupling approximation (keeping up to second order). For the \(^{16}\text{O} + ^{144}\text{Sm}\) system, they specifically examined the role of the octupole vibrational excitation of \(^{16}\text{O}\) by performing calculations both with and without projectile excitation, using both linear and all-order coupling treatments.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{64}\text{Ni} + ^{96}\text{Zr}\): the linear coupling approximation considerably underestimates fusion cross sections at sub-barrier energies; the all-order coupling calculation agrees very well with experimental data, while the quadratic approximation still leaves considerable discrepancies.
- For \(^{16}\text{O} + ^{144}\text{Sm}\): the linear coupling approximation underestimates the coupling strength at the barrier position around \(r = 10.8\) fm and overestimates it in the inner region around \(r = 8.5\) fm.
- For \(^{16}\text{O} + ^{144}\text{Sm}\) including the octupole vibration of \(^{16}\text{O}\) at 6.1 MeV: the all-order coupling calculation re-establishes the double-peaked barrier distribution structure seen in experiment, which was missing in the linear coupling calculation; the all-order result is shifted by 2 MeV relative to the calculation ignoring projectile excitation.

## Context

This work extends the standard coupled-channels approach that had previously used the linear coupling approximation, which was successful for very asymmetric systems but failed for heavier, nearly symmetric systems such as \(^{64}\text{Ni} + ^{96}\text{Zr}\) studied by Stefanini et al. (1992). The paper resolves contradictory conclusions about the role of projectile excitation in \(^{16}\text{O} + \text{Sm}\) reactions: calculations by Gross et al. (1994) had indicated its importance, while experiments by Leigh et al. (1995) found no specific features associated with it. The all-order treatment shows that the octupole excitation of \(^{16}\text{O}\) mainly renormalizes the static potential barrier without changing the barrier distribution shape, explaining the earlier discrepancy. This work enables more reliable analysis of high-precision fusion data and warns against using simplified first-order approximations.
