# Aspects of meson properties in dense nuclear matter

> We investigate the modification of meson spectral densities in dense nuclear matter at zero temperature. These effects are studied in a fully relativistic mean field model which goes beyond the linear density approximation and also includes baryon resonances. In particular, the role of N*(1520) and N*(1720) on the rho meson spectral density is highlighted. Even though the nucleon-nucleon loop and the nucleon-resonance loop contribute with the opposite sign, an overall reduction of rho meson mass is still observed at high density. Importantly, it is shown that the resonances cause substantial broadening of the rho meson spectral density in matter and also induces non-trivial momentum dependence. The spectral density of the a0 meson is also shown. We study the dispersion relations and collective oscillations induced by the rho meson propagation in nuclear matter together with the influence of the mixing of rho with the a0 meson. The relevant expression for the plasma frequency is also recovered analytically in the appropriate limit.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in dense nuclear matter at zero temperature, the inclusion of baryonic resonances N*(1520) and N*(1720) causes substantial broadening of the rho meson spectral density and induces non-trivial momentum dependence, even though the nucleon-nucleon loop and nucleon-resonance loop contribute with opposite sign; an overall reduction of rho meson mass is still observed at high density.

## Method

The authors use a fully relativistic mean field model (Walecka-type quantum hadrodynamics) that goes beyond the linear density approximation by resumming one-loop polarization diagrams in the random phase approximation. They calculate the rho meson self-energy from nucleon-nucleon loops, nucleon-resonance loops (for N*(1520) and N*(1720)), and pion-pion loops, then solve the Dyson equation to obtain the dressed propagator and dielectric functions. The mixing between rho and a0 mesons via nucleon-nucleon loops is also incorporated, and the longitudinal and transverse spectral densities are extracted from the imaginary parts of the propagators.

## Key numbers

- The free-space decay width of the rho meson is dominantly determined by the two-pion channel; N*(1520) and N*(1720) have total widths of approximately 120 MeV and 150 MeV respectively, with branching ratios into the rho-N channel of approximately 24 MeV and 115 MeV.
- The linear density approximation is justified only up to normal nuclear matter density (rho_0); the ratio of resummed to linear-approximation self-energies deviates significantly above that density.
- At baryonic density rho = 1.5 rho_0, the rho meson physical mass (at q_z = 0) drops from its free-space value; the dispersion curves show the effect of resonances partially offsetting the mass drop.
- At density rho = 2.5 rho_0, the longitudinal spectral density becomes even broader than at 1.5 rho_0, and at high momenta all peaks merge into one broad peak.
- The plasma frequency is recovered analytically in the long-wavelength limit as Omega^2 = g_v^2 / M^* * rho_B, where M^* is the effective nucleon mass.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of rho meson in-medium properties by going beyond the linear density approximation used in Post (2001) and by incorporating baryonic resonances in a fully relativistic framework, building on non-relativistic calculations by Urban (1998) and Friman that showed double-peak structures. The results enable more realistic modeling of dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions and complement QCD sum-rule approaches (Dutt-Mazumder, 2001) and chiral model studies (Shiomi, 1994). The mixing of rho with a0 meson, previously discussed by Teodorescu (2000, 2001), is included to show the first calculation of the a0 spectral density in dense matter.
