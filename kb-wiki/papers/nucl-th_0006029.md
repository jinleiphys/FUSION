# The rho meson in a nuclear medium

> In this work, propagation properties of the rho meson in symmetric nuclear matter are studied. We make use of a coupled channel unitary approach to meson-meson scattering, calculated from the lowest order Chiral Perturbation Theory lagrangian including explicit resonance fields. Low energy chiral constraints are considered by matching our expressions to those of one loop Ch.P.T. To account for the medium corrections, the rho couples to pion-pion and kaon-antikaon pairs which are properly renormalized in the nuclear medium.

# The rho meson in a nuclear medium

## Key claim

The rho meson broadens significantly in symmetric nuclear matter, with its width reaching around 350 MeV at normal nuclear density, but the kaon-antikaon channel has almost no influence on the pion-pion channel even at normal nuclear density, contrary to what might be expected from coupled-channel effects.

## Method

The authors use a coupled-channel unitary approach to meson-meson scattering in the isospin-1 channel, starting from tree-level amplitudes derived from lowest-order Chiral Perturbation Theory including explicit resonance fields. The T-matrix is obtained via the N/D method, and medium corrections are incorporated by modifying the meson propagators in the loops: the pion selfenergy includes nucleon-hole and delta-hole excitations with short-range correlations (Landau-Migdal parameter g' = 0.7), while the kaon and antikaon selfenergies include S-wave and P-wave contributions from coupled-channel calculations and hyperon-hole excitations.

## Key numbers

- The rho meson width at normal nuclear density (ρ = ρ₀) is around 350 MeV.
- The zero of the real part of the ππ → ππ amplitude shifts downward by 100–150 MeV at normal nuclear density.
- The Landau-Migdal parameter g' is set to 0.7 for short-range correlations in the pion selfenergy.
- The kaon selfenergy is approximated as Π_K(ρ) ≈ 0.13 m_K² (ρ/ρ₀) in MeV².

## Context

This work extends the vacuum chiral unitary approach of Oller, Oset, and Pelaez (1998) for meson-meson scattering to the nuclear medium, using the same framework that successfully described ππ P-wave phase shifts and electromagnetic vector form factors up to √s ≲ 1.2 GeV. The medium modifications build on prior treatments of pion selfenergies with Lindhard functions (Chiang, 1978) and antikaon selfenergies from self-consistent coupled-channel calculations (Ramos, 1999). The results enable future studies of rho meson propagation in nuclear matter and its effects on dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions.
