# Phase structure of the QCD vacuum in a magnetic field at low temperature

> We study the QCD phase structure in magnetic field $H$ at low temperature $T$. The hadronic phase free energy in a constant homogeneous magnetic field is calculated in one-loop approximation of the chiral perturbation theory. The dependence of the quark and gluon condensates upon the temperature and field strength is found. It is shown that the chiral phase transition order parameter $<\bar q q>$ remains constant provided field strength and temperature are related via $H=const \cdot T^2$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the chiral phase transition order parameter (the quark condensate \(\langle \bar q q\rangle\)) remains constant when the magnetic field strength \(H\) and temperature \(T\) are increased together according to the relation \(H = \text{const} \cdot T^2\), specifically \(H = 0.013 \cdot T^2\), a phenomenon the author calls "freezing" of the order parameter by the magnetic field.

## Method

The author calculates the hadronic-phase free energy in a constant homogeneous magnetic field at low temperature using the one-loop approximation of chiral perturbation theory (ChPT). The partition function is expressed in terms of an effective chiral Lagrangian, treating charged and neutral pions as an ideal Bose gas in a magnetic field, and the free energy is then used to derive the quark and gluon condensates via derivatives with respect to quark mass and the gauge coupling, respectively. Low-energy theorems at finite temperature and magnetic field are also established to relate the condensates to the free energy.

## Key numbers

- The quark condensate shift in the strong-field limit (\(\sqrt{H} \gg T\)) is given by \(\frac{\langle \bar q q\rangle (T,H)}{\langle \bar q q\rangle} = 1 - \frac{1}{3} \cdot \frac{T^2}{8 F_\pi^2} + \frac{H}{(4\pi F_\pi)^2} \ln 2 - \frac{H^{3/4} T^{1/2}}{(2\pi)^{3/2} F_\pi^2} e^{-\sqrt{H}/T}\), where \(F_\pi = 93\) MeV.
- In the weak-field limit (\(\sqrt{H} \ll T\)), the quark condensate shift is \(\frac{\langle \bar q q\rangle (T,H)}{\langle \bar q q\rangle} = 1 - \frac{T^2}{8 F_\pi^2} + \frac{H}{(4\pi F_\pi)^2} A - \frac{7 \sqrt{H} T}{48 \pi F_\pi^2} - \frac{H}{(4\pi F_\pi)^2} \ln \frac{H}{T^2}\), with \(A \simeq 4.93\).
- The "freezing" condition is given by the numerical solution \(\lambda_* = 0.111\), where \(\lambda = \sqrt{H}/T\), corresponding to \(H = 0.013 \cdot T^2\).
- At the chiral phase transition temperature \(T_c \simeq 150\) MeV, the magnetic field satisfies \(H(T_c) / (4\pi F_\pi)^2 \simeq 2 \cdot 10^{-4} \ll 1\).
- The gluon condensate shift is \(\langle G^2\rangle (T,H) = \langle G^2\rangle - \frac{2}{3 b_0} H^2\), where \(b_0 = (11 N_c - 2 N_f)/3\) is the one-loop beta-function coefficient.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the quark condensate in a magnetic field using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Ref. [0.8]) and one-loop QCD calculations (Ref. [8]), which found that the condensate grows with increasing field, contradicting a naive analogy with superconductivity. It also builds on prior ChPT calculations of the temperature dependence of the quark condensate (Gasser and Leutwyler, 1987; Ref. [5]) and the gluon condensate (Ref. [7]), as well as low-energy theorems for gluodynamics at finite temperature (Ref. [14]). The results enable further study of the QCD phase diagram in the presence of magnetic fields, particularly the interplay between thermal and magnetic effects on the chiral condensate, and suggest that the Le Chatelier-Braun principle governs the "freezing" phenomenon.
