# Hidden charm pentaquarks: mass spectrum, magnetic moments, and photocouplings

> We develop an extension of the usual three flavor quark model to four flavors (u, d, s and c), and discuss the classification of pentaquark states with hidden charm. We fit our model to the known baryon spectrum and we predict the double and triple charm baryons, finding good agreement with the most recent lattice QCD calculations. We compute the the ground state of hidden charm pentaquarks and their associated magnetic moments and electromagnetic couplings, of interest to pentaquark photoproduction experiments.

## Key claim

The authors extend the three-flavor quark model to four flavors (u, d, s, c) to classify hidden-charm pentaquark states (qqqc\bar{c}), but the SU(4) flavor symmetry is explicitly broken by the large mass difference between the charm and light quarks, and the mass formula parameters are determined with the condition that z_2 = 0 because double and triple charm states have only one-star PDG status and cannot independently constrain both z_1 and z_2.

## Method

The authors develop a mass formula based on a generalization of the Gursey-Radicati form to SU(4) flavor, including quadratic Casimir operators for SU_sf(8), SU_f(4), and SU_f(3), plus spin, isospin, hypercharge Y and Z terms, and an orbital term linear in L. They fit 10 free parameters (plus fixed m_u = m_d = 312.972 MeV and m_c sampled uniformly between 1400 and 1500 MeV) to 51 three- and four-star baryon resonances from the PDG using a bootstrap technique with 10^4 resamplings, then apply the same mass formula to predict ground-state masses of hidden-charm pentaquarks belonging to octet and decuplet configurations.

## Context

This work extends the collective stringlike model of Bijker (1994, 2000) and the hypercentral constituent quark model of Giannini (2015) from three flavors to four flavors, providing a classification scheme for the hidden-charm pentaquark candidates observed by LHCb in Lambda_b0 -> J/psi p K- decay (Aaij 2015, 2016). The calculated masses, magnetic moments, and photocouplings are intended to guide near-threshold J/psi photoproduction experiments proposed by Kubarovsky (2015), Karliner (2015), and Fernandez-Ramirez (2017) to confirm the pentaquark interpretation.
