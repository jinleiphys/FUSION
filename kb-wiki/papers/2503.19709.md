# Exploring Efimov states in $D^*D^*D^*$ and $DD^*D^*$ three-body systems

> The Efimov effect is an intriguing three-body quantum phenomenon. Searching for Efimov states within the realms of nuclear and hadronic physics presents a challenge due to the inherent inability of natural physical systems to exhibit adjustable two-body scattering lengths. In this study, we examine the potential existence of Efimov states in the $D^*D^*D^*$ and $DD^*D^*$ three-hadron systems. Utilizing a pionless effective field theory framework, we determine that the presence of Efimov states in the spectrum of the $D^*D^*D^*$ system is contingent upon the existence of an $(I,J)=(1,2)$ $D^*D^*$ two-body bound system. If only the $T_{cc}^+$ and its heavy quark spin partner $T_{cc}^{*+}$ exist while there is no near-threshold pole in all other $S$-wave $D^{(*)}D^*$ scattering amplitudes, no Efimov effect is expected in the $D^{(*)}D^*D^*$ systems.

## Key claim

The existence of Efimov states in the $D^*D^*D^*$ system is contingent upon the existence of an $(I,J)=(1,2)$ $D^*D^*$ two-body bound system; if only the $T_{cc}^+$ and its heavy quark spin partner $T_{cc}^{*+}$ exist while there is no near-threshold pole in all other $S$-wave $D^{(*)}D^*$ scattering amplitudes, no Efimov effect is expected in the $D^{(*)}D^*D^*$ systems.

## Method

The authors constructed a pionless effective field theory (EFT) with dimer fields representing the $T_{cc}$ and $T_{cc}^*$ near-threshold bound states, and derived integral equations for $S$-wave $T_{cc}^*D^*$ scattering and coupled-channel $T_{cc}^*D$-$T_{cc}D^*$ scattering. They projected the scattering equations onto partial waves with definite isospin $I$ and total angular momentum $J$, and searched for three-body bound states by examining the cutoff dependence of the determinant of the discretized integral equations and the corresponding scattering phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- The binding energy of the $T_{cc}^*$ dimer was set to $E_B^* = -0.50$ MeV, and the binding energy of the $T_{cc}$ dimer was set to $E_B^c = -0.36$ MeV, taken from Du (2021).
- The cutoff $\Lambda$ was varied over a range from $1.5$ to $2.5$ GeV to test for cutoff dependence in the $T_{cc}^*D^*$ scattering equations.
- For the $T_{cc}^*D^*$ system, the spin-isospin coefficient $C_0^J C_0^I$ was found to be $-2$ for the $(I,J)=(1/2,0)$ channel, and $+1$ for both the $(1/2,1)$ and $(1/2,2)$ channels.
- For the $T_{cc}^*D$-$T_{cc}D^*$ coupled-channel system with $(I,J)=(1/2,1)$, the coefficients were $C_1^I C_1^J = \sqrt{2}$, $C_2^I C_2^J = \sqrt{2}$, and $C_3^I C_3^J = -1$.

## Context

This work extends the search for Efimov states in three-charm hadron systems, previously explored by Ortega (2024) under the assumption of a $D^*D^*$ molecule with $(I)J^P=(0)1^+$, and by Canham (2009) and Hammer (2010). The authors find that the Efimov effect does not occur in the $D^*D^*D^*$ or $DD^*D^*$ systems when only the $T_{cc}$ and $T_{cc}^*$ exist as near-threshold poles, contradicting the claim of a bound state in the $(I,J)=(1/2,0)$ channel made by Ortega (2024) due to a different partial-wave projection coefficient. The results enable future studies to focus on the necessary condition of an $(I,J)=(1,2)$ $D^*D^*$ bound state for Efimov physics in these systems.
