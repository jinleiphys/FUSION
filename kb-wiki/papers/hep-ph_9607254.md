# Status of the Proton Spin Problem

> The proton spin problem triggered by the EMC experiment and its present status are closely examined. Recent experimental and theoretical progresses and their implications are reviewed. It is pointed out that the sign of the sea-quark polarization generated perturbatively by hard gluons via the anomaly mechanism is predictable: It is negative if the gluon spin component is positive. We stress that the polarized nucleon structure function $g_1(x)$ is independent of the $k_\perp$-factorization scheme chosen in defining the quark spin density and the hard photon-gluon scattering cross section. Consequently, the anomalous gluon and sea-quark interpretations for $Γ_1$, the first moment of $g_1(x)$, are equivalent. It is the axial anomaly that accounts for the observed suppression of $Γ_1^p$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the apparent suppression of the first moment of the polarized proton structure function, \(\Gamma_1^p\), can be explained by the axial anomaly, and that the two competing interpretations, anomalous gluon contribution versus sea-quark polarization, are equivalent and depend only on the choice of \(k_\perp\)-factorization scheme used to define the quark spin density and the hard photon-gluon scattering cross section.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental data from polarized deep inelastic scattering (DIS) experiments (EMC, SMC, SLAC-E142, SLAC-E143) and theoretical developments, including perturbative QCD calculations of the photon-gluon scattering box diagram. It analyzes the first moment of the polarized structure function \(g_1(x)\) using operator product expansion (OPE) and parton model approaches, comparing results from different factorization schemes (chiral-invariant vs. gauge-invariant) to demonstrate their equivalence. The sign of sea-quark polarization generated perturbatively by hard gluons via the anomaly mechanism is predicted to be negative if the gluon spin component \(\Delta G\) is positive.

## Key numbers

- EMC experiment (1987) measured \(\Gamma_1^p = 0.126 \pm 0.010 \pm 0.015\) at \(\langle Q^2 \rangle = 10.7\) GeV\(^2\), substantially lower than the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule prediction of \(0.171 \pm 0.006\) at \(Q^2 = 10\) GeV\(^2\).
- SMC (1994) measured \(\Gamma_1^p = 0.136 \pm 0.011 \pm 0.011\) at \(\langle Q^2 \rangle = 10\) GeV\(^2\).
- SLAC-E143 (1994) measured \(\Gamma_1^p = 0.127 \pm 0.004 \pm 0.010\) at \(\langle Q^2 \rangle = 3\) GeV\(^2\).
- Global fit at \(Q^2 = 10\) GeV\(^2\) yields \(\Delta u = 0.83 \pm 0.03\), \(\Delta d = -0.43 \pm 0.03\), \(\Delta s = -0.10 \pm 0.03\), and total quark spin \(\Delta\Sigma = 0.31 \pm 0.07\).
- Bjorken sum rule test: SMC gives \(\Gamma_1^p - \Gamma_1^n = 0.199 \pm 0.038\) at \(Q^2 = 10\) GeV\(^2\), compared to theoretical prediction \(0.187 \pm 0.003\); E143 gives \(0.163 \pm 0.010 \pm 0.016\) at \(Q^2 = 3\) GeV\(^2\), compared to \(0.171 \pm 0.008\).
- The probability that the deuteron is in a \(D\) state is \(\omega_D = 0.058\).

## Context

This paper reviews the "proton spin problem" triggered by the EMC experiment (1987), which contradicted the Ellis-Jaffe sum rule (Ellis and Jaffe, 1974) and the naive quark-model expectation that quarks carry most of the proton spin. It extends earlier theoretical work by Efremov and Teryaev (1988), Altarelli and Ross (1988), and Carlitz, Collins, and Mueller (1988) on anomalous gluon contributions, and resolves the dispute between the anomalous gluon and sea-quark interpretations by showing their equivalence, following the clarification by Bodwin and Qiu (1990) and Manohar (1990). The review enables future next-to-leading order analyses of spin-dependent parton distributions and motivates further experimental probes of gluon and sea-quark polarization, as well as orbital angular momentum contributions to the proton spin.
