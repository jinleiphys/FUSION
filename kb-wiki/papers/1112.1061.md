# One-loop Factorization for Inclusive Hadron Production in $pA$ Collisions in the Saturation Formalism

> We demonstrate the QCD factorization for inclusive hadron production in $pA$ collisions in the saturation formalism at one-loop order, with explicit calculation of both real and virtual gluon radiation diagrams. The collinear divergences associated with the incoming parton distribution of the nucleon and the outgoing fragmentation function of the final state hadron, as well as the rapidity divergence with small-$x$ dipole gluon distribution of the nucleus are factorized into the splittings of the associated parton distribution and fragmentation functions and the energy evolution of the dipole gluon distribution function. The hard coefficient function is evaluated at one-loop order, and contains no divergence.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that inclusive hadron production in proton-nucleus collisions can be factorized at one-loop order in the saturation formalism, with collinear divergences absorbed into parton distribution and fragmentation functions and rapidity divergences absorbed into the BK evolution of the dipole gluon distribution, provided the calculation is performed in coordinate space where a factorization form exists despite the absence of simple k-perp factorization beyond leading order.

## Method

The authors performed a complete next-to-leading order calculation of the scattering amplitude squared for the process p + A → h + X, including both real and virtual gluon radiation diagrams for the quark channel qA → q + X. They used dimensional regularization in D = 4 - 2ε with MS-bar subtraction to isolate collinear divergences, and identified rapidity divergences when the longitudinal momentum of the radiated gluon goes to zero. The divergences were factorized into the DGLAP evolution of the collinear parton distribution and fragmentation functions and the Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution of the dipole gluon distribution, yielding finite hard coefficients at one-loop order.

## Key numbers

- The leading order hard coefficient for the quark channel is H_{2qq}^{(0)} = e^{-ik_perp·r_perp} δ(1-ξ)
- The one-loop hard coefficient for the two-point function in the quark channel at large N_c limit is H_{2qq}^{(1)} = -(3/2)N_c δ(1-ξ) e^{-ik_perp·r_perp} ln(c_0²/(r_perp² k²_perp)) where c_0 = 2e^{-γ_E} with γ_E the Euler constant
- The one-loop hard coefficient for the gluon channel two-point function at large N_c is H_{2gg}^{(1)} = (22/9) H_{2qq}^{(1)}
- The splitting kernel for the quark channel is P_{qq}(ξ) = ((1+ξ²)/(1-ξ))_+

## Context

This work extends the leading-order saturation formalism calculations used to interpret RHIC data on forward hadron suppression in dAu collisions (Albacete 2010) and addresses the need for next-to-leading order corrections identified in earlier studies (Gelis 2008, Balitsky 2010). It improves upon partial one-loop calculations by Dumitru (2005) and Altinoluk (2011) by including virtual diagrams not previously calculated and properly identifying the rapidity divergence associated with BK evolution. The results enable complete NLO phenomenological applications to RHIC and LHC experiments and provide a framework for establishing QCD factorization for other hard processes in small-x physics.
