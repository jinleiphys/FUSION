# (Anti-) Deuteron Coalescence in Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
Deuterons observed in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions are created by coalescence of a neutron-proton pair at freeze-out, not by surviving inside the fireball; the coalescence probability depends on flow gradients in the fireball expansion, and the model is parameter-free except for fireball size and flow parameters determined from other spectra.

## Method
The authors derive a coalescence model using the Wigner function formalism, starting from a complete quantum mechanical description in terms of density matrices. They reduce the deuteron formation rate to an integral over a product of nucleon single particle Wigner functions (approximated by hydrodynamical, local equilibrium distribution functions) and a quantum mechanical transition matrix element. The deuteron spectrum is obtained by integrating over the freeze-out hypersurface, with the coalescence probability sensitive to flow gradients in the same way as two-particle interferometry correlation functions.

## Key numbers
- Experimental invariant coalescence factor $B_2$ for heavy ion collisions at the BEVALAC: $(1-2)\times10^{-2}$ GeV$^2$.
- $B_2$ from CERN Newmass/NA52 experiment for $^{208}$Pb+Pb collisions at $158$ GeV/$A$: $(1.3\pm0.2)\times10^{-3}$ GeV$^2$ for deuterons.
- $B_2$ from CERN Newmass/NA52 experiment for $^{208}$Pb+Pb collisions at $158$ GeV/$A$: $(1.1\pm0.3)\times10^{-3}$ GeV$^2$ for antideuterons.
- First estimates for the NA52 data give values for the quantum mechanical factor ${\cal A}$ of approximately $0.6-0.75$ at the space point with maximal thermal emission.

## Context
This work extends the coalescence approach of Sato and the Wigner function method of Danielewicz (1990s) to a hydrodynamical picture of the reaction zone. It recovers the earlier model by Hagedorn for pp collisions in the limit of small fireball volumes. The model enables extraction of fireball size and flow parameters from deuteron spectra, with ongoing work in the authors' group to determine these from single particle and pion correlation spectra.
