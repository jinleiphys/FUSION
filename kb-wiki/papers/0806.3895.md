# Neutron capture on 130Sn during r-process freeze-out

> We examine the role of neutron capture on 130Sn during r-process freeze-out in the neutrino-driven wind environment of the core-collapse supernova. We find that the global r-process abundance pattern is sensitive to the magnitude of the neutron capture cross section of 130Sn. The changes to the abundance pattern include not only a relative decrease in the abundance of 130Sn and an increase in the abundance of 131Sn, but also a shift in the distribution of material in the rare earth and third peak regions.

## Key claim

The global r-process abundance pattern is sensitive to the magnitude of the neutron capture cross section of 130Sn during freeze-out in the neutrino-driven wind environment, but this sensitivity is conditional on the specific astrophysical conditions and the underlying nuclear data and thermodynamic model choices.

## Method

The authors employed a 1D thermodynamic model of the neutrino-driven wind from a core-collapse supernova, tracking a mass element from the protoneutron star surface. They used two coupled reaction networks: an intermediate network at temperatures above T9 ≈ 2.5, transitioning to an r-process network that includes beta-decay, beta-delayed neutron emission, neutron capture, and photo-disintegration. The neutron capture cross section of 130Sn was artificially increased by scaling factors of 10 and 100 (χ = 10, 100) to probe the influence of cross section uncertainties on the final abundance pattern.

## Key numbers

- The beta-decay lifetime of 130Sn is τβ = 162 s, which is long enough that only small amounts of material exit 130Sn by beta-decay
- For an increase in the neutron capture cross section of 130Sn by a factor of 100, the abundance of A=130 nuclei decreases by ~93% and the abundance of A=131 nuclei increases by ~191%
- For the same factor-of-100 increase, changes to individual nuclear species above the second r-process peak region reach as much as ~34%, with an average percent change in abundance of ~12%
- The baseline calculation used an entropy per baryon of s/k = 100, an outflow timescale of τ = 0.3 s, and an initial electron fraction of Ye = 0.26 at T9 = 10
- The neutron separation energy of 133Sn is Sn ≈ 2.6 MeV, while that of 131Sn is Sn ≈ 5.1 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Surman and Engel (2001) who showed that individual neutron capture cross sections near A ~ 195 can influence the r-process during freeze-out, and by Rauscher (2005) who examined how simultaneously changing all r-process neutron capture cross sections affects freeze-out timing. The paper demonstrates that a single nucleus, 130Sn, can produce global changes to the abundance pattern through a mechanism involving its long beta-decay lifetime, prominent position on the r-process path, and large Q-value for neutron capture. This enables experimental investigations at the Holifield Radioactive Ion Beam Facility to reduce cross section uncertainties for 130Sn and identifies that other nuclei with similar characteristics may have comparable influence in different r-process scenarios.
