# The Triaxial Rotation Vibration Model in the Xe-Ba Region

> The axial Rotation Vibration Model is here extended to describe also triaxial equilibrium shapes with beta and gamma vibrations allowing for the interaction between vibrations and rotations. This Triaxial Rotation Vibration Model (TRVM) is applied to Xe and Ba isotopes with mass numbers between 120 and 130. This area has recently been pointed out to be the O(6) limit of the Interacting Boson Approximation (IBA). The present work shows that the TRVM can equally well describe these nuclei concerning their excitation energies and E2 branching ratios.

# The Triaxial Rotation Vibration Model in the Xe-Ba Region

## Key claim

The Triaxial Rotation Vibration Model (TRVM), an extension of the axial Rotation Vibration Model to allow triaxial equilibrium shapes, can describe excitation energies and E2 branching ratios in Xe and Ba isotopes (A=120–130) equally well as the O(6) limit of the Interacting Boson Approximation (IBA), despite the earlier conclusion that IBA agreed better with data due to the axial RVM's restriction to axial symmetry.

## Method

The authors extend the axial Rotation Vibration Model (RVM) of Faessler and Greiner by allowing a triaxial equilibrium deformation parameterized by β₀ and a₂, with β and γ vibrations treated as harmonic oscillations around this shape. They diagonalize the Hamiltonian in a truncated basis of up to 31 states with K ≤ 6 and up to two phonons (n₂ + n₀ ≤ 2), applying a Lipas ansatz for the moment of inertia. The model has four independent parameters: the β and γ vibration energies Eβ and Eγ, the inverse moment of inertia ε, and the triaxial equilibrium deformation a₂.

## Key numbers

- For ¹³⁰Ba: the 0⁺ bandhead of the γ one-phonon band is fitted at 1179 keV through the vibration energy Eγ
- For ¹²⁶Xe: the parameters a₂/β₀ and Eβ must be increased relative to those in ¹³⁰Ba to obtain the best fit
- The Lipas parameter αL is approximately 10⁻⁴ keV⁻¹
- For the transition from the initial 4⁺₃ state in ¹³⁰Ba, the TRVM result is about two orders of magnitude larger than the experimental ratio B(E2,4⁺₃→2⁺₂)/B(E2,4⁺₃→4⁺₂), while the IBA result is two orders of magnitude lower than experiment
- For ¹²⁶Xe, the agreement between TRVM, IBA, and experiment is much better for the B(E2) ratios

## Context

This work extends the axial Rotation Vibration Model developed by Faessler and Greiner (1960s) to allow triaxial equilibrium shapes, directly competing with the O(6) limit of the Interacting Boson Approximation (IBA) developed by Iachello and collaborators. The Cologne group (Sei, Lie, Kir) had previously concluded that the O(6) IBA with an additional parameter χ in the E2 operator agreed better with data than the axial RVM, but the authors show this was due to the axial symmetry restriction. The TRVM enables a consistent description of the Xe-Ba region without requiring the γ-instability assumption of the O(6) limit, though the authors note their predictions for the β bandhead are not reliable because the results are insensitive to the Eβ parameter.
