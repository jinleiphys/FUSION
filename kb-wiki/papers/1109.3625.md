# Benchmark calculation of n-3H and p-3He scattering

> The n-3H and p-3He elastic phase-shifts below the trinucleon disintegration thresholds are calculated by solving the 4-nucleon problem with three different realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions (the I-N3LO model by Entem and Machleidt, the Argonne v18 potential model, and a low-k model derived from the CD-Bonn potential). Three different methods -- Alt, Grassberger and Sandhas, Hyperspherical Harmonics, and Faddeev-Yakubovsky -- have been used and their respective results are compared. For both n-3H and p-3He we observe a rather good agreement between the three different theoretical methods. We also compare the theoretical predictions with the available experimental data, confirming the large underprediction of the p-3He analyzing power.

## Key claim

The three theoretical methods (AGS, HH, and FY) produce phase-shifts for n-³H and p-³He elastic scattering that agree within about 1% or better when using the same realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction, confirming that the previously observed discrepancies between theoretical predictions and experimental data are not due to inaccuracies in solving the four-nucleon scattering problem but rather to deficiencies in the interaction models themselves.

## Method

The four-nucleon scattering problem was solved using three independent methods: the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations in momentum space with screening and renormalization for Coulomb, the Hyperspherical Harmonics (HH) expansion with the Kohn variational principle, and the Faddeev-Yakubovsky (FY) equations in configuration space. Calculations were performed for n-³H at laboratory energies E_n = 1, 2, 3.5, and 6 MeV and for p-³He at E_p = 2.25, 4.05, and 5.54 MeV using three different realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials: the chiral I-N3LO model (cutoff Λ=500 MeV), the Argonne v18 (AV18) potential, and a low-k potential derived from CD-Bonn (cutoff Λ=2.5 fm⁻¹).

## Key numbers

- For n-³H with the I-N3LO potential, the maximal deviation between the three methods is less than 1%, with AGS and HH results differing by more than 0.5% in only a few cases
- The strongest deviation for n-³H with I-N3LO is about 0.4 degrees observed with FY results at the largest studied energy (6 MeV)
- For n-³H with AV18, the total cross sections from the three methods agree within 0.05 b at all considered energies
- At E_n = 1 MeV, the experimental total cross section is about 1.6 b, while I-N3LO and AV18 give about 1.8 b; the low-k potential gives a value close to the experimental one
- At E_n = 3.5 MeV (the peak region), the experimental total cross section is about 2.45 b; AV18 and low-k underpredict this value sizeably while I-N3LO is quite close
- For p-³He with AV18, the HH results are estimated to have uncertainty of about 0.5% in extrapolated phase-shifts due to slow convergence with the grand angular quantum number K_max
- For p-³He with I-N3LO and low-k potentials, the HH uncertainty is estimated at most 0.3%
- The FY method estimates its accuracy at 1% level for the partial wave truncation scheme used (j_x ≤ 4, j_y ≤ 4, j_z ≤ 3)
- For p-³He S-waves at E_p = 2.25 MeV, the AGS method estimates accuracy around 1% due to slower convergence of the screening radius R (ranging from 10 to 15 fm with n=4)

## Context

This work extends previous benchmark studies of the four-nucleon bound state (Kea01) to the scattering regime, resolving discrepancies found in an earlier benchmark (Lea05) where different groups obtained results at variance with each other. The calculations confirm the large underprediction of the p-³He analyzing power A_y0 previously reported by Lea05, Fisher06, and others, and provide a foundation for testing three-nucleon force models and their T=3/2 components against the new TUNL phase-shift analysis (Dan10) at E_p = 1.60, 2.25, 4.05, and 5.54 MeV.
