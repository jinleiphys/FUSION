# On $^{146}$Nd, $^{144}$Sm and other unexplored 2$β$ decay isotopes

> $^{146}$Nd is one of only four (over a total of 35 existing) 2$β^-$ decay isotope candidates whose half-lives currently lack experimental limits. The $α$ activity of the $^{146}$Sm daughter nuclide allows placement of limits on the 2$β$ decay using the $^{146}$Nd/$^{142}$Nd abundance ratio ($T^{ββ}_{1/2} \gsim 3 \times 10^9$ yr) or direct search for $^{146}$Sm with accelerator mass spectrometers ($T^{ββ}_{1/2} \gsim 4.5 \times 10^{19}$ yr). With a similar approach, a modest ($\sim$ Gyr) first limit on half-lives for the other unexplored $2β$ unstable isotopes and competitive limits (few $10^{13}$yr) for $^{98}$Mo and $^{122}$Sn are also given. Finally, it is shown how the limit $T^{0νεβ^+}_{1/2} \gsim 10^{15}$ yr for the unexplored $^{144}$Sm $0νεβ^+$ decay may be obtained from the data of a GSO crystal scintillator.

## Key claim

The paper sets first experimental half-life limits for ten previously unexplored double-beta decay isotopes using geochemical abundance ratios and reanalysis of existing detector data, but these limits are generally modest (∼Gyr) and rely on the conservative assumption that daughter nuclei have not been overproduced by double-beta decay during the Solar System lifetime.

## Method

The author derives lower limits on double-beta decay half-lives from the daughter-to-parent abundance ratio in the Earth's crust, integrating the growth equation from Solar System formation (4.5 Gyr ago) to the present. For 146Nd, limits are also obtained by searching for the 146Sm daughter via its alpha decay, using existing accelerator mass spectrometer data and Sm-doped scintillator measurements. For 144Sm, a limit on 0νεβ+ decay is inferred from the background spectrum of a GSO crystal scintillator containing trace samarium.

## Key numbers

- 146Nd 2β− half-life limit from 146Nd/142Nd abundance ratio: Tββ1/2 ≳ 3 × 10⁹ yr
- 146Nd 2β− half-life limit from AMS search for 146Sm: Tββ1/2 ≳ 4.5 × 10¹⁹ yr
- 146Nd 2β− half-life limit from Sm-doped ZnWO₄ detector: Tββ1/2 ≳ 3.5 × 10¹⁵ yr
- 98Mo 2β− half-life limit from crustal abundance ratio: Tββ1/2 > 5 × 10¹³ yr
- 122Sn 2β− half-life limit from crustal abundance ratio: Tββ1/2 > 1.3 × 10¹³ yr
- 144Sm 0νεβ+ half-life limit from GSO scintillator data: T0νεβ+1/2 ≳ 10¹⁵ yr
- 80Se 2β− half-life limit: Tββ1/2 > 3.5 × 10¹³ yr (noted as potentially non-conservative due to Kr gas loss)
- 86Kr 2β− half-life limit: Tββ1/2 > 2 × 10⁸ yr
- 204Hg 2β− half-life limit: Tββ1/2 > 8.8 × 10⁸ yr
- 152Gd 2ε half-life limit: Tββ1/2 > 6 × 10⁸ yr

## Context

This work extends the double-beta decay tables compiled in DBDtables (1995) by providing first experimental limits for ten previously unconstrained isotopes, completing the survey of all 35 2β− candidates. The geochemical approach using crustal abundance ratios follows the methodology of earlier work by Fireman (1952) and DBDtables (1995), while the 146Nd limits improve upon the AMS-based search by Tau (2017). The results enable future experimental searches to focus on the most promising unexplored isotopes, particularly 146Nd where the 0ν2β decay may dominate over the 2ν process due to the very low Q-value of 70.2 keV.
