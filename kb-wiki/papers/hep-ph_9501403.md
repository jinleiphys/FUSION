# CHIRAL SYMMETRY RESTORATION AS THE GEORGI VECTOR LIMIT

> I discuss recent work done with Gerry Brown on chiral phase transition at high temperature and/or density described in terms of Georgi's vector limit. The notion of ``mended symmetry" is suggested to play an important role in understanding the properties of hadrons in dense and/or hot matter before reaching the phase transition. It is shown that while the QCD vacuum in baryon-free space is resistant to ``melting" up to the critical temperature, baryon-rich medium can induce the vacuum to become softer in temperature: the hadron masses drop faster in temperature when baryon matter is present.

## Key claim

The paper argues that as temperature and/or density approach the chiral phase transition, the hidden gauge symmetry of the vector mesons flows to the "Georgi vector limit" where the gauge coupling vanishes and the pion and scalar isovector become degenerate, without requiring the large Nc limit that Weinberg and Georgi originally invoked.

## Method

The author uses the hidden gauge symmetric Lagrangian of Bando et al. (1985) to describe the approach to chiral restoration, analyzing the renormalization group flow of the gauge coupling g and the parameter κ (which controls the mixing between left and right currents) using one-loop beta functions from Harada and Yamawaki. The resulting vector limit (κ=0, g→0) is then applied to calculate the quark-number susceptibility near the critical temperature and to explain the appearance of cool kaon components in heavy-ion collisions, using the dimensionally reduced model of Koch et al. (1992) and the constituent quark model of Kunihiro (1991).

## Key numbers

- The chiral phase transition temperature is given as T_c ≈ 140 MeV.
- At nuclear matter density ρ₀, the in-medium pion decay constant is f_π^*/f_π ≈ 0.8.
- The scalar meson mass in the Beane-van Kolck model at nuclear matter density is m_σ ∼ 400 MeV.
- The quark-number susceptibility ratio just below T_χSR is calculated as χ(T_χSR)/χ₀(T_χSR) ≈ 0.47, using f_π = 93 MeV and κ=0.
- The quark-number susceptibility ratio just above T_χSR is calculated as χ(T_χSR⁺)/χ₀(T_χSR⁺) ≈ 0.68, using α_s ≈ 0.19.
- The expansion velocity of the fireball in 15 GeV/N Si on Pb collisions is v/c ∼ 0.1, with expansion time ∼ 25-30 fm/c.
- The repulsive K⁺N potential from vector exchange is V_{K⁺N} ≈ 90 MeV (ρ/ρ₀).
- The attractive K⁺N potential from the sigma term is S_{K⁺N} ≈ -45 MeV (ρ_s/ρ₀).
- Cool kaon components from the E-814 experiment show effective temperatures of 12 MeV for K⁺ and 10 MeV for K⁻.

## Context

This work extends the earlier Brown-Rho scaling proposal (Brown and Rho, 1991) for hadron masses in medium and builds on the "mended symmetry" concept of Weinberg (1990) and the vector limit of Georgi (1990). The Beane-van Kolck model (1994) is used to bring the scalar dilaton into the low-energy sector. The analysis of the quark-number susceptibility connects to lattice gauge calculations by Gottlieb et al. (1987) and to the NJL model work of Kunihiro (1991). The cool kaon scenario provides a possible explanation for the E-814 data from Stachel (1995) and is supported by the calculations of Koch (1995). The paper enables further lattice gauge tests of the temperature dependence of the Brown-Rho scaling and suggests that the Georgi vector limit may be realized in heavy-ion collisions and compact stars.
