# Renormalization of NN-Scattering with One Pion Exchange and Boundary Conditions

> A non perturbative renormalization scheme for Nucleon-Nucleon interaction based on boundary conditions at short distances is presented and applied to the One Pion Exchange Potential. It is free of off-shell ambiguities and ultraviolet divergences, provides finite results at any step of the calculation and allows to remove the short distance cut-off in a suitable way. Low energy constants and their non-perturbative evolution can directly be obtained from experimental threshold parameters in a completely unique and model independent way when the long range explicit pion effects are eliminated. This allows to compute scattering phase shifts which are, by construction consistent with the effective range expansion to a given order in the C.M. momentum $p$. In the singlet $^1S_0$ and triplet $^3S_1- ^3D_1$ channels ultraviolet fixed points and limit cycles are obtained respectively for the threshold parameters. Data are described satisfactorily up to CM momenta of about $p \sim m_π$.

## Key claim

The paper presents a non-perturbative renormalization scheme for Nucleon-Nucleon scattering using boundary conditions at short distances, applied to the One Pion Exchange Potential, which is free of off-shell ambiguities and ultraviolet divergences; the scheme works only up to center-of-mass momenta of about \(p \sim m_\pi\), where two-pion exchange effects are expected to become important.

## Method

The authors formulate the scattering problem in coordinate space using the Schrödinger equation with mixed boundary conditions at a variable boundary radius \(R\), encoding short-distance physics in a boundary condition matrix \(\mathbf{L}_k(R)\). They derive a first-order nonlinear differential equation for the variable S-matrix as a function of \(R\), and for the low-energy limit they obtain evolution equations for the running scattering length matrix, effective range, and curvature parameters. Starting from experimental threshold parameters at infinity, they integrate these equations downward to short distances to determine the short-distance S-matrix, then integrate back upward to predict phase shifts consistent with the effective range expansion.

## Key numbers

- The OPE potential parameters used: nucleon mass \(M_N = 938.92\,\text{MeV}\), pion mass \(m_\pi = 138\,\text{MeV}\), pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 93\,\text{MeV}\), axial coupling \(g_A = 1.25\).
- The tensor potential diverges as \(U_T \to -\frac{3 M_N g_A^2}{16 \pi f_\pi^2 r^3}\) as \(r \to 0\), with the coefficient independent of \(m_\pi\).
- Data are described satisfactorily up to CM momenta of about \(p \sim m_\pi = 138\,\text{MeV}\).
- In the singlet \(^1S_0\) channel, a trivial ultraviolet fixed point for the scattering length is found non-perturbatively, coinciding with the perturbative result of Kaplan (1996).
- In the triplet \(^3S_1-^3D_1\) channel, ultraviolet limit cycles are obtained for the threshold parameters.
- The minimal boundary radius from earlier phenomenological studies is \(R_S = 1.4-1.8\,\text{fm}\), corresponding to a momentum cutoff \(\Lambda = 2\pi / R_S \sim 600\,\text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the boundary condition approach to NN scattering, previously used with phenomenological potentials (e.g., by the Nijmegen group, Stoks 1993) and in early EFT studies (Phillips 1996, Cohen 1998, van Kolck 1998), by showing that the boundary radius can be removed entirely for OPE. It contrasts with the cut-off regularization of Epelbaum (1998) and the dimensional regularization approach of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998), which encountered convergence issues at NNLO (Fleming 1999). The method enables model-independent extraction of low-energy constants from threshold parameters and provides a framework for testing whether OPE can be seen in low partial waves at intermediate energies \(m_\pi/2 \le k \le m_\pi\).
