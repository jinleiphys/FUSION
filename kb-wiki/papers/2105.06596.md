# Linking continuum and lattice quark mass functions via an effective charge

> The quark mass function is computed both by solving the quark propagator Dyson-Schwinger equation and from lattice simulations implementing overlap and Domain-Wall fermion actions for valence and sea quarks, respectively. The results are confronted and seen to produce a very congruent picture, showing a remarkable agreement for the explored range of current-quark masses. The effective running-interaction is based on a process-independent charge rooted on a particular truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations in the gauge sector, establishing thus a link from there to the quark sector and inspiring a correlation between the emergence of gluon and hadron masses.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the quark mass function computed from the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) using a process-independent effective charge can be brought into remarkable agreement with lattice QCD results, provided the quark-gluon vertex includes an Anomalous Chromomagnetic Moment (ACM) term with two tuned parameters. The agreement holds across a range of current-quark masses from approximately 2 to 60 MeV, but the effective running-interaction is redefined from a phenomenological charge rather than derived directly from the gauge sector.

## Method
The authors solve the quark propagator Dyson-Schwinger equation (gap equation) in Landau gauge using a running-interaction defined by a phenomenological effective charge, combined with a Ball-Chiu vertex plus an ACM term containing two free parameters (m0 and eta). On the lattice side, they compute the quark mass function from five RBC/UKQCD 2+1 flavor Domain-Wall fermion ensembles using overlap fermions for valence quarks, employing a vertex-definition renormalization scheme (Z2^Ver) to control discretization errors. For the heavier ensembles, they perform interpolations to reach the unitary point where valence and sea quark masses match, producing mass functions for four current-quark masses ranging from 2.1 to 57.8 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Current-quark masses used for comparison: 2.1, 19.3, 38.5, and 57.8 MeV
- ACM parameters: m0 = 2 GeV, eta in [1.27, 1.32]
- Chiral limit pion decay constant: f_pi^(0) = 89.5(1.8) MeV
- Chiral quark condensate at zeta = 19 GeV: -<qbar q>_zeta^(0) = (0.283(4) MeV)^3
- Resulting current-quark mass from Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner formula: m_ud^zeta ≈ 6.8 MeV (to yield m_pi = 0.140 GeV)
- Lattice ensembles: 64I (a=0.0837 fm, m_pi=139 MeV), 48I (a=0.1141 fm, m_pi=139 MeV), 24I (a=0.1105 fm, m_pi=340 MeV), 24Ih (a=0.1105 fm, m_pi=432 MeV), 24Ih2 (a=0.1105 fm, m_pi=576 MeV)

## Context
This work extends the program of Binosi et al. (2014, 2016) and Cui et al. (2019) that connects the QCD gauge sector to the matter sector via a process-independent effective charge derived from PT-BFM truncation of Dyson-Schwinger equations. The phenomenological effective charge used here was previously employed by Cui et al. (2020) for all-orders DGLAP evolution of pion parton distribution functions, where it showed agreement with experimental data. The paper enables future symmetry-preserving solutions of the meson Bethe-Salpeter equation using this running-interaction, and the successful reproduction of the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation supports further phenomenological applications.
