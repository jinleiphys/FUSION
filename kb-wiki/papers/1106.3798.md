# $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ structures produced by the initial single pion emission in the $Υ(5S)$ decays

> We propose an unique mechanism called {\it Initial Single Pion Emission} existing in the $Υ(5S)$ decays, and further study the line shapes of $dΓ(Υ(5S\to Υ(nS)π^+π^-))/dm_{Υ(nS)π^+}$ ($n=1,2,3$) and $dΓ(Υ(5S\to h_b(mP)π^+π^-))/dm_{h_b(mP)π^+}$ ($m=1,2$). We find sharp structures around 10610 MeV and 10650 MeV in the obtained theoretical line shapes of $dΓ(Υ(5S\to Υ(nS)π^+π^-))/dm_{Υ(nS)π^+}$ and $dΓ(Υ(5S\to h_b(mP)π^+π^-))/dm_{h_b(mP)π^+}$ distributions, which could naturally correspond to the $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ structures newly observed by Belle.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the sharp structures observed by Belle at 10610 MeV and 10650 MeV in the invariant mass spectra of $\Upsilon(nS)\pi^\pm$ and $h_b(mP)\pi^\pm$ from $\Upsilon(5S)$ decays can be reproduced by a conventional hadronic mechanism called Initial Single Pion Emission (ISPE), without invoking any exotic state assignments such as molecular or tetraquark states. The claim is conditional: the ISPE mechanism produces these peaks only when intermediate $B\bar{B}^*$ and $B^*\bar{B}^*$ states are considered, while the $B\bar{B}$ contribution yields only smooth phase-space-like shapes.

## Method
The authors calculated the differential decay widths $d\Gamma(\Upsilon(5S)\to \Upsilon(nS)\pi^+\pi^-)/dm_{\Upsilon(nS)\pi^+}$ ($n=1,2,3$) and $d\Gamma(\Upsilon(5S)\to h_b(mP)\pi^+\pi^-)/dm_{h_b(mP)\pi^+}$ ($m=1,2$) using the ISPE mechanism, where $\Upsilon(5S)$ first emits a single pion and decays into a $B^{(*)}\bar{B}^{(*)}$ pair, which then rescatters via $B^{(*)}$ exchange to produce the final $\Upsilon(nS)\pi$ or $h_b(mP)\pi$ system. The amplitudes were evaluated using effective Lagrangians in the heavy quark limit, with monopole form factors to account for off-shell effects, and the coupling constants were set to unity for the $\Upsilon(5S)B^{(*)}B^{(*)}\pi$ vertices to focus on line shapes rather than absolute rates.

## Key numbers
- The $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ structures observed by Belle have masses $M_{Z_b(10610)} = 10608.4 \pm 2.0$ MeV/c$^2$ and $M_{Z_b(10650)} = 10653.2 \pm 1.5$ MeV/c$^2$, with widths $\Gamma_{Z_b(10610)} = 15.6 \pm 2.5$ MeV/c$^2$ and $\Gamma_{Z_b(10650)} = 14.4 \pm 3.2$ MeV/c$^2$.
- The ISPE mechanism produces sharp peaks in the $m_{\Upsilon(nS)\pi^+}$ distributions at approximately 10610 MeV and 10650 MeV, corresponding to the $B\bar{B}^*$ and $B^*\bar{B}^*$ thresholds, respectively.
- The calculation uses $\Lambda_{QCD} = 220$ MeV and a monopole form factor with $\Lambda = m_{B^{(*)}} + \beta \Lambda_{QCD}$, where $\beta = 1$ is taken; the line shapes are reported to be weakly dependent on the $\beta$ value.
- The coupling constant $g = 0.59$ and pion decay constant $f_\pi = 132$ MeV are used in the heavy quark limit relations.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical discussion of the $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ structures first reported by the Belle Collaboration (2011) in the hidden-bottom decays of $\Upsilon(5S)$. It contrasts with prior interpretations that treat these states as $B\bar{B}^*$ and $B^*\bar{B}^*$ molecular states (Liu 2008, Bondar 2011), tetraquark states (Guo 2011), or cusp effects from thresholds (Bugg 2011). The ISPE mechanism provides an alternative explanation without exotic state assignments, and the authors suggest it can be extended to study dipion decays of higher bottomonia like $\Upsilon(11020)$ and higher charmonia such as $\psi(4040)$, $\psi(4160)$, and $\psi(4415)$.
