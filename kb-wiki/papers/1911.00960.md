# Spectrum of fully-heavy tetraquarks from a diquark+antidiquark perspective

> Using a relativized diquark model Hamiltonian, we calculate the masses of $J^{PC}=0^{++}$ ground-state tetraquarks in the following systems: $b s \bar b \bar s$, $bb \bar n \bar n$ ($n=u, d$), $bb \bar s \bar s$, $cc\bar c \bar c$, $b b \bar b \bar b$, $b c\bar b \bar c$ and $b b \bar c \bar c$. We also compute extensive spectra for the fully-heavy quark flavour combinations. Finally, as a test of the diquark model approach, we compute the masses of fully-heavy baryons in the diquark model. Our results may be compared soon to the forthcoming experimental data for fully-heavy three-quark systems.

## Key claim
Using a relativized diquark model Hamiltonian with linear confinement and one-gluon exchange, the authors predict masses for fully-heavy tetraquarks and baryons, with the caveat that the results are parameter-free predictions contingent on diquark masses determined from a separate quark-quark binding calculation and that the theoretical uncertainty is on the order of 100 MeV.

## Method
The authors model tetraquarks as a color-antitriplet diquark bound to a color-triplet antidiquark via a single relative coordinate Hamiltonian. The interaction potential includes a linear confining term and a one-gluon exchange (OGE) potential with spin-spin, tensor, and spin-orbit components, using parameters from prior work on relativized quark models. Diquark masses are computed by binding a quark-quark pair with the same OGE potential, and the resulting Hamiltonian is solved to obtain ground-state and excited-state masses for several heavy-quark combinations.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state mass of the fully-charm tetraquark $cc\bar c \bar c$: $5.88\,(17)$ GeV (av-av configuration).
- Ground-state mass of the fully-bottom tetraquark $bb\bar b \bar b$: $18.75(07)$ GeV (av-av configuration).
- Ground-state mass of the $bc\bar b \bar c$ tetraquark: $12.37\,(09)$ GeV (av-av configuration) and $12.52\,(08)$ GeV (sc-sc configuration).
- Ground-state mass of the $bb \bar c \bar c$ tetraquark: $12.45\,(11)$ GeV (av-av configuration).
- Ground-state mass of the $bb\bar n \bar n$ tetraquark ($n=u,d$): $10.31\,(17)$ GeV.
- Ground-state mass of the $bb\bar s \bar s$ tetraquark: $10.53\,(16)$ GeV.
- Ground-state mass of the $bn\bar b \bar n$ tetraquark (av-av configuration): $10.12\,(11)$ GeV.
- Ground-state mass of the $bs\bar b \bar s$ tetraquark (av-av configuration): $10.35\,(10)$ GeV.
- Calculated mass of the $\eta_c$ meson: $2.91\,(06)$ GeV.
- Calculated mass of the $\eta_b$ meson: $9.33\,(07)$ GeV.
- Calculated mass of the $B_c$ meson: $6.18\,(08)$ GeV.

## Context
This work extends the diquark+antidiquark model previously applied to hidden-charm and hidden-bottom tetraquarks by Anwar et al. (2017, 2018) to fully-heavy systems ($cc\bar c \bar c$, $bb\bar b \bar b$, $bc\bar b \bar c$, $bb \bar c \bar c$) and heavy-light systems ($bq\bar b \bar q$, $bb \bar q \bar q$). The predictions are intended to be compared with forthcoming experimental data from LHCb, particularly the $X(6900)$ structure observed in the $J/\psi$-pair invariant mass spectrum, and to guide searches for orbitally-excited and $J\neq 0$ tetraquark states. The authors also test the diquark model by computing fully-heavy baryon masses, noting that the same Hamiltonian can describe baryons in a quark-diquark approximation via a dynamical baryon-meson supersymmetry.
