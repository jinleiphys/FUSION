# Nuclear parton density functions from jet production in DIS at the EIC

> We investigate the potential of inclusive-jet production in deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) at a future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) to improve our current knowledge of nuclear parton density functions (PDFs). We demonstrate that the kinematic reach is extended similarly to inclusive DIS, but that the uncertainty of the nuclear PDFs, in particular of the gluon density at low Bjorken-x, is considerably reduced, by up to an order of magnitude compared to the present situation. Using an approximate next-to-next-to-leading order (aNNLO) calculation implemented in the program JetViP, we also make predictions for three different EIC designs and for four different light and heavy nuclei.

## Key claim

Inclusive-jet production in deep-inelastic scattering at a future Electron-Ion Collider can reduce the uncertainty of nuclear parton density functions, particularly the gluon density at low Bjorken-x, by up to an order of magnitude compared to the present situation, but this improvement depends on the specific EIC design and the nuclear species studied.

## Method

The authors used the program JetViP to calculate inclusive-jet production cross sections in deep-inelastic scattering at next-to-leading order and approximate next-to-next-to-leading order in QCD, employing the nCTEQ15 nuclear PDF set with 32 error PDFs determined via the Hessian method. Jets were reconstructed in the Breit frame using the anti-kT algorithm with distance parameter R=1, and predictions were made for three different EIC designs (eRHIC with 16 or 21 GeV electrons on 100 GeV/nucleon ions, and MEIC with 12 GeV electrons on 40 GeV/nucleon ions) and four nuclei (He-4, C-12, Fe-56, Pb-208). The impact of nuclear PDF uncertainties was estimated by comparing the envelope of error PDF predictions to expected experimental precision.

## Key numbers

- The gluon contributes up to 70% of the inclusive-jet cross section at small Bjorken-x and forward rapidities, where nuclear PDF uncertainty reaches ±25%
- Nuclear shadowing reduces cross sections by 22% for Pb-208, 12% for Fe-56, 6% for C-12, and 3% for He-4 at x ≈ 2×10⁻⁴
- EMC suppression reduces cross sections by up to 35% for Pb-208 and Fe-56, 25% for C-12, and 20% for He-4 at large x
- Anti-shadowing enhancements of up to 10% are observed at intermediate x ≈ 10⁻²
- The eRHIC design extends jet pT reach to 35 GeV, while MEIC reaches 15-20 GeV
- Annual integrated luminosities are approximately 10 fb⁻¹ for eRHIC and 100 fb⁻¹ for MEIC
- The kinematic range extends from x ≥ 4×10⁻³ in νA DIS and x ≥ 10⁻² in eA/μA DIS to x ≥ 10⁻⁴ and below
- Q² range extends from 10² GeV² to 10³ GeV² and beyond
- NLO K-factors reach 2-2.5 at low pT ≥ 4 GeV and Q² ≥ 1 GeV²
- The EPPS16 analysis, which includes LHC dijet data, shows about half the suppression and half the uncertainty of nCTEQ15 at high pT

## Context

This work extends previous studies of jet production in photoproduction (Klasen, 1995) and DIS (Klasen, 1997) at NLO, which were improved to aNNLO (Klasen, 2013; Biekotter, 2015). It contrasts with current nuclear PDF analyses like DSSZ (de Florian, 2011) and nCTEQ15 (Kovarik, 2015) that rely on inclusive pion data from RHIC, which depend on pion fragmentation functions that may be modified by medium effects. The EPPS16 update (Eskola, 2009; Armesto, 2015) uses LHC p-Pb dijet data, but the EIC would provide cleaner cold nuclear effects without the collective effects debated in pA collisions. The results enable future studies of dijet production, jet shapes, and transverse-momentum dependent distribution functions in polarized and unpolarized ep and eA collisions.
