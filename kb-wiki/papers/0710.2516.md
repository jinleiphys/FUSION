# Are there compact heavy four-quark bound states?

> We present an exact method to study four-quark systems based on the hyperspherical harmonics formalism. We apply it to several physical systems of interest containing two heavy and two light quarks using different quark-quark potentials. Our conclusions mark the boundaries for the possible existence of compact, non-molecular, four-quark bound states. While $QQ\bar n \bar n$ states may be stable in nature, the stability of $Q\bar Qn \bar n$ states would imply the existence of quark correlations not taken into account by simple quark dynamical models

## Key claim

Compact, non-molecular four-quark bound states may exist for $QQ\bar n \bar n$ systems (two heavy quarks and two light antiquarks), but the stability of $Q\bar Q n \bar n$ systems (heavy quark-antiquark plus light quark-antiquark) would require quark correlations not captured by simple quark dynamical models. The authors hedge that their calculation cannot definitively exclude large molecular objects with binding energies smaller than 1-2 MeV induced by long-range interactions.

## Method

The authors solve the non-relativistic four-body Schrödinger equation using a hyperspherical harmonics (HH) expansion of the spatial wave function, with color, isospin, and spin states coupled to satisfy the Pauli principle. They apply this exact method to $cc\bar n \bar n$ and $c\bar c n \bar n$ systems using two standard quark-quark potentials: a Bhaduri-like potential (BCN) and a constituent quark model with boson exchange (CQC). The HH expansion is truncated at a maximum grand angular momentum $K$ value, with convergence tested up to $K=24$ or $K=26$ depending on the system.

## Key numbers

- For the $cc\bar n \bar n$ $(S,I)=(1,0)$ state with the BCN model at $K=24$: energy of 3899.7 MeV, compared to 3904.7 MeV from Jan04 and 3931.0 MeV from Sil93
- For $cc\bar n \bar n$ states with the CQC model: the difference between the $\ell_i=0$ approximation and the exact HH result at $K=24$ can be as large as 200 MeV (e.g., $(S,I)=(0,1)$ state: 4154 MeV vs 3911 MeV)
- For the $c\bar c n \bar n$ $J^{PC}=1^{++}$ state with the CQC model: energy converges to 3786 MeV at $K=20$, above the $J/\psi\,\omega$ threshold at 3745 MeV
- For the $cc\bar n \bar n$ $J^{P}=1^{+}$ state with the CQC model: energy stabilizes at 3861 MeV at $K=22$, below the $D\,D^*$ threshold at 3937 MeV
- For the $cc\bar n \bar n$ $J^{P}=1^{+}$ state: the four-quark radius is 0.37 fm, compared to the sum of two-meson radii of 0.44 fm
- For the $c\bar c n \bar n$ $J^{PC}=1^{++}$ state with the BCN model: the $J/\psi\,\omega$ threshold is 3874 MeV, while the $\chi_{cJ}\,\eta$ threshold is 3655 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier variational calculations of four-quark systems by Vij04 and Sil93, demonstrating that those approximate methods can miss up to 200 MeV of binding energy. The authors compare their exact HH results to the stochastic variational approach of Jan04, finding close agreement for the $cc\bar n \bar n$ system. The paper establishes that for $c\bar c n \bar n$ systems, the system reorders into the lightest two-meson state, making compact bound states unlikely unless diquark correlations (as discussed by Jaf05 and Mai04) or three-body color forces (Dmi05) are present. The findings are strengthened by a complementary string model analysis in Vij07b. This work enables future lattice QCD calculations (Ale07) to test whether QCD dynamics favors diquark formation sufficient to bind tetraquarks.
