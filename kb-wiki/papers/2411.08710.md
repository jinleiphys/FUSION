# Damping of density oscillations from bulk viscosity in quark matter

> In this contribution, we extend the discussion about the calculation of the bulk viscosity of quark matter in the normal phase due to electroweak processes and its effect on the damping of baryon density oscillations that might occur in the coalescence of two compact stars. Employing the EoSs from the MIT bag model and perturbative quantum chromodynamics (pQCD) up to $\mathcal{O}(α_s)$, we analyze our results varying densities in the range of temperatures from 0 to 10 MeV for frequencies around 1 kHz. Our estimates show that bulk viscous effects might play a relevant role during the postmerger stage if the system reaches a deconfined quark matter phase.

## Key claim

Bulk viscous effects from electroweak processes in deconfined three-flavor quark matter might damp baryon density oscillations on timescales of a few to hundreds of milliseconds at temperatures between 0.01 and 0.1 MeV, making bulk viscosity potentially relevant during the postmerger stage of neutron star coalescence if the system reaches a deconfined quark matter phase.

## Method

The authors compute the bulk viscosity of unpaired three-flavor quark matter in the neutrino-transparent regime using linear-response transport theory, incorporating both nonleptonic (u+d ↔ u+s) and semileptonic electroweak processes expanded to first order in chemical imbalances. They evaluate the resulting analytic expression for bulk viscosity and the associated damping time of baryon density oscillations using two equations of state: the MIT bag model with a fixed strange quark mass of 150 MeV, and perturbative QCD up to O(α_s) with running strange quark mass and coupling. The calculations are performed for baryon densities expressed as multiples of nuclear saturation density n_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}, temperatures from 0 to 10 MeV, and oscillation frequencies around 1 kHz.

## Key numbers

- Maximum bulk viscosity occurs at temperatures in the range 0.01 to 0.1 MeV for oscillation frequency ω/2π = 1 kHz
- Minimal damping times of density oscillations at those temperatures lie in the range of a few to hundreds of milliseconds
- Nuclear saturation density used as reference: n_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}
- Strange quark mass fixed at m_s = 150 MeV for MIT bag model calculations
- Temperature range studied: 0 to 10 MeV
- Oscillation frequency studied: approximately 1 kHz

## Context

This work extends the calculation of bulk viscosity in normal quark matter from Hernandez et al. (2024) by analyzing its effect on damping of baryon density oscillations in the postmerger context. The results suggest that bulk viscous dissipation may be physically important on timescales similar to those found in numerical simulations of neutron star mergers, analogous to conclusions obtained for dense nuclear matter at higher temperatures by Harris (2024). The findings open the possibility that bulk viscosity should be included in numerical simulations of merger dynamics if deconfined quark matter is present.
