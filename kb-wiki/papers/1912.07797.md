# Unified model of nucleon elastic form factors and implications for neutrino-oscillation experiments

> Precise knowledge of the nucleon's axial-current form factors is crucial for modeling GeV-scale neutrino-nucleus interactions. Unfortunately, the axial form factor remains insufficiently constrained to meet the precision requirements of upcoming long-baseline neutrino-oscillation experiments. This work studies the nucleon's axial and vector form factors using the light-front approach to build a quark-diquark model of the nucleon with an explicit pion cloud. The light-front wave functions in both the quark and pion-baryon Fock spaces are first calibrated to existing experimental information on the nucleon's electromagnetic form factors, and then used to predict the axial form factor. The resulting squared charge radius of the axial pseudo-vector form factor is predicted to be $r_A^2\! =\! 0.29\! \pm\! 0.03\, \mathrm{fm}^2$, where the small error accounts for the model's parametric uncertainty. We use our form factor results to explore the (quasi-)elastic scattering of neutrinos by (nuclei)nucleons, with the result that the the widely-implemented dipole ansatz is an inadequate approximation of the full form factor for modeling both processes. The approximation leads to a $5\!-\!10\%$ over-estimation of the total cross section, depending on the (anti)neutrino energy. We project over-estimations of similar size in the flux-averaged cross sections for the upcoming DUNE long-baseline neutrino-oscillation experiment.

## Key claim

The widely-used dipole approximation for the nucleon's axial form factor overestimates (anti)neutrino-nucleon and (anti)neutrino-nucleus quasi-elastic cross sections by 5-10% for neutrino energies above 0.5 GeV, with similar overestimations projected for the DUNE experiment, based on a light-front quark-diquark model with an explicit pion cloud calibrated to electromagnetic form factors.

## Method

The authors constructed a light-front quark-diquark model of the nucleon that includes both a quark-diquark core and explicit pion-baryon Fock components (nucleon and Delta resonances). They first calibrated the model's light-front wave functions to existing experimental data on nucleon electromagnetic form factors, then used the calibrated model to predict the axial-vector form factor. The form factor results were implemented in the GiBUU event generator to simulate neutrino-40Ar quasi-elastic scattering and compute flux-averaged cross sections using the projected DUNE neutrino flux.

## Key numbers

- Squared axial charge radius: \(r_A^2 = 0.29 \pm 0.03 \, \mathrm{fm}^2\)
- Dipole mass parameter derived from the predicted radius: \(M_A = 1.28 \pm 0.07\) GeV
- The dipole approximation overestimates total (anti)neutrino-nucleon cross sections by 5-10% for \(E_\nu \gtrsim 0.5\) GeV
- For DUNE flux-averaged cross sections, the dipole approximation overestimates by 5% at \(Q^2 < 0.2 \, \mathrm{GeV}^2\) and by 10-15% at larger \(Q^2\)
- Comparison value from combined analysis of neutrino-nucleon scattering and muonic hydrogen capture: \(r_A^2 = 0.46 \pm 0.16 \, \mathrm{fm}^2\) (Hill 2017)

## Context

This work extends the light-front quark model approach of Miller (2002), Pasquini (2007), and Cloet (2012) by simultaneously describing electromagnetic and axial form factors in a unified framework, in contrast to phenomenological fits (Kelly 2004), z-expansion methods (Bhattacharya 2011, Meyer 2016, Hill 2017), or lattice QCD calculations that are restricted to lower momentum transfer. The results enable improved modeling of neutrino-nucleus interactions for next-generation long-baseline oscillation experiments like DUNE, and demonstrate that the dipole approximation commonly used in event generators introduces systematic errors that must be corrected.
