# Exploring hadron-quark phase transition in heavy-ion collisions using particle emission ratios in heavy and light reaction systems

> Based on the AMPT model, which incorporates both hadronic and quark degrees of freedom, we studied the productions of lambda, kaon, proton, and pion in reaction systems $^{40}$Ca+$^{40}$Ca, $^{48}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca, and $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au. It is found that the ratios of identical particle emissions from heavy and light reaction systems, especially the emission ratios of strange particles $Λ^{0}$ or K$^{+}$ in heavy and light reaction systems, are highly sensitive to the hadron-quark phase transition in heavy-ion collisions. Detailed explanations and validations of these results are given.

# Exploring hadron-quark phase transition in heavy-ion collisions using particle emission ratios in heavy and light reaction systems

## Key claim
The ratio of identical particle yields (especially Λ⁰ and K⁺) between heavy and light reaction systems at √sNN = 4.2 GeV is sensitive to whether a hadron-quark phase transition occurs, but only if the measured ratios align with the AMPT-SM (string melting) scenario rather than the AMPT-HC (pure hadronic cascade) scenario. The authors hedge that this observable works because partonic multiple scatterings do not increase parton number, while hadronic multiple scatterings do increase hadron yields in heavier systems.

## Method
The authors used the AMPT model in two versions: the string melting (AMPT-SM) mode, which includes partonic degrees of freedom via initial parton production, ZPC elastic parton scatterings, quark coalescence, and hadronic cascade; and the pure hadronic cascade (AMPT-HC) mode, which uses a Woods-Saxon nucleon density distribution with local Thomas-Fermi approximation and direct hadron cascade via the ART model. They simulated central collisions of ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁸Ca+⁴⁸Ca, and ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au at √sNN = 4.2 GeV, computing rapidity distributions of Λ⁰, K⁺, π⁺, and protons, then took ratios of yields between heavy and light systems. The PACIAE 3.0 model was used to separately verify the effects of partonic inelastic scatterings and hadronic rescatterings on particle yields.

## Key numbers
- The nucleon number ratio between ⁴⁸Ca and ⁴⁰Ca is 1.2; the nucleon number ratio between ¹⁹⁷Au and ⁴⁰Ca is 4.925.
- At mid-rapidity (y = 0), π⁺ yields from the HC mode constitute 49%, 52%, and 74% of those from the SM mode as the system evolves from ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca to ⁴⁸Ca+⁴⁸Ca to ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au.
- For the ⁴⁸Ca/⁴⁰Ca ratio, Λ⁰ and K⁺ ratios in the HC mode are significantly higher than 1.2; for the ¹⁹⁷Au/⁴⁰Ca ratio, all four particle types in the SM mode are around 4.925, while in the HC mode all ratios are consistently greater than 4.925.
- The sensitivity of the proposed observable is approximately 20% for pion ratios, 40% for proton ratios, and about 100% for Lambda and kaon ratios.

## Context
This work extends the use of the AMPT model (Lin, 2005) and its pure hadronic variant (Yong, 2021; Yong, 2023) to propose a new observable for detecting the hadron-quark phase transition at beam energy scan energies. It builds on prior probes such as anisotropic flow (Csernai, 1999; Shen, 2021), jet quenching (Baier, 2002), particle fluctuations (Stephanov, 1999; Asakawa, 2015), and strange particle production (Yong, 2023), but differs by using ratios between two reaction systems rather than observables within a single system. The approach aims to bypass uncertainties in the high-density nuclear matter Equation of State, theoretical uncertainties in particle production channels, and model framework differences. Experimental validation would require measurements from the E866/E917/E895 Collaborations for Au+Au and Ca+Ca systems at √sNN ≈ 4.2 GeV.
