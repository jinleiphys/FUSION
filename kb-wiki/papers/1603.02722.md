# Baryons and the Borromeo

> The kernels in the tangible matter of our everyday experience are composed of light quarks. At least, they are light classically; but they don't remain light. Dynamical effects within the Standard Model of Particle Physics change them in remarkable ways, so that in some configurations they appear nearly massless, but in others possess masses on the scale of light nuclei. Modern experiment and theory are exposing the mechanisms responsible for these remarkable transformations. The rewards are great if we can combine the emerging sketches into an accurate picture of confinement, which is such a singular feature of the Standard Model; and looming larger amongst the emerging ideas is a perspective that leads to a Borromean picture of the proton and its excited states.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the proton and its excited states can be understood as Borromean bound-states, where three valence quarks are bound such that no two can form an independent asymptotic bound-state, with binding arising from dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) and diquark correlations rather than from static flux tubes or linear potentials.

## Method
The authors employ a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation framework based on Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) to describe baryons as quark+diquark systems, using dressed-quark propagators that incorporate a running mass from DCSB and soft diquark correlations generated by the same interaction kernel. For the Roper resonance, they compute transition form factors from the dressed-quark core and then estimate meson-baryon final-state interaction (MB-FSI) contributions via a renormalisation procedure with a 20% maximum amplitude modification.

## Key numbers
- The QCD mass-scale Λ_QCD ∼ 200 MeV sets the natural scale of the theory.
- At Q² ≈ 4 GeV², the running coupling α_s(ζ₂²) ≳ 0.3.
- The mass-gap in pure-gauge QCD is Δ ≳ 1.5 GeV from lattice simulations.
- The length-scale for flux tube dissolution in QCD with light quarks is r_σ̸ ≃ (1/3) fm.
- The length-scale characterising changes in analytic structure of propagators is ς ≈ 0.5 fm.
- DCSB is responsible for more than 98% of the visible mass in the Universe.
- Scalar diquark mass-scale: m_{[ud]_{0⁺}} ≈ 0.7–0.8 GeV.
- Pseudovector diquark mass-scale: m_{{uu}_{1⁺}} ≈ 0.9–1.1 GeV.
- Scalar diquark size: r_{[ud]_{0⁺}} ≳ r_π; pseudovector diquark size: r_{{uu}_{1⁺}} ≳ r_ρ.
- The meson-baryon FSI renormalisation uses Λ_MB = 1 GeV as the transition midpoint.
- The Roper resonance's Breit-Wigner mass is reduced by approximately 20% due to the meson cloud.
- The dressed-quark core of the Roper is revealed to probes with Q² ≳ 3 m_N².
- CLAS12 will probe electrocouplings at Q² ∈ [6, 12] GeV², corresponding to momenta k ≲ 1.2 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the continuum-QCD approach to baryons developed by the authors and collaborators (Segovia 2015, Roberts 2015) and unifies top-down gauge-sector analyses (Binosi 2014) with bottom-up hadron phenomenology. It contradicts the static flux-tube picture of confinement (Bali 2005, Prkacin 2005) and argues that linear potentials and Regge trajectories are irrelevant for light-quark hadrons. The framework enables parameter-free predictions of baryon properties and provides a foundation for interpreting CLAS12 measurements of nucleon resonance electroproduction at Jefferson Lab.
