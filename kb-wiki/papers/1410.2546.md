# Static quark-antiquark potential in the quark-gluon plasma from lattice QCD

> We present a state-of-the-art determination of the complex valued static quark-antiquark potential at phenomenologically relevant temperatures around the deconfinement phase transition. Its values are obtained from non-perturbative lattice QCD simulations using spectral functions extracted via a novel Bayesian inference prescription. We find that the real part, both in a gluonic medium as well as in realistic QCD with light $u$, $d$ and $s$ quarks, lies close to the color singlet free energies in Coulomb gauge and shows Debye screening above the (pseudo) critical temperature $T_c$. The imaginary part is estimated in the gluonic medium, where we find that it is of the same order of magnitude as in hard-thermal loop resummed perturbation theory in the deconfined phase.

## Key claim

The complex-valued static quark-antiquark potential is extracted from non-perturbative lattice QCD simulations at temperatures around the deconfinement phase transition, with the real part found to lie close to the color singlet free energies in Coulomb gauge and the imaginary part estimated only in the gluonic medium, where it is of the same order of magnitude as hard-thermal loop resummed perturbation theory in the deconfined phase.

## Method

The authors used Euclidean lattice QCD simulations to compute Wilson line correlators in Coulomb gauge, then applied a novel Bayesian inference prescription (improved over the Maximum Entropy Method) to reconstruct the spectral function ρ(ω,r) from the Euclidean data. The real and imaginary parts of the static potential were extracted by fitting the lowest-lying spectral peak to a skewed Lorentzian form, which encodes the potential contributions through its position and width. Quenched QCD configurations were generated on anisotropic lattices with a_s=0.039 fm and temperatures from 210 MeV to 839 MeV, while full QCD configurations with light u, d and s quarks were provided by the HotQCD collaboration on isotropic 48^3×12 lattices.

## Key numbers

- Quenched QCD temperatures: 210 MeV (0.78 T_c) ≤ T ≤ 839 MeV (3.11 T_c), with T_c ≈ 271 MeV
- Full QCD temperatures: 148 MeV ≤ T ≤ 286 MeV, with pseudo-critical temperature T_c ≈ 174 MeV
- Quenched lattice spacing: a_s = 0.039 fm on 32^3 × N_t lattices with anisotropy a_s/a_τ = 4
- Full QCD lattice: 48^3 × 12 isotropic lattices with lattice spacings from 0.111 fm (β=6.8) to 0.057 fm (β=7.48)
- For the imaginary part extraction, datasets with N_τ ~ O(100) datapoints at precision ΔD/D < 10^{-4} are required for ~25% accuracy
- Spectral reconstruction used N_ω = 4000 frequency points for quenched and N_ω = 4600 for full QCD

## Context

This work extends the strategy proposed by Rothkopf, Hatsuda and Sasaki (2012) for evaluating the real-time definition of the static potential using Euclidean lattice QCD, and builds on the improved Bayesian approach developed by Burnier and Rothkopf (2013). The results are consistent with findings from a recent modeling approach based on HTL spectral functions by Bazavov, Burnier and Petreczky (2014). The extracted potential enables future heavy quarkonium spectral function studies and phenomenological investigations of quarkonium melting and regeneration observed at RHIC and LHC experiments.
