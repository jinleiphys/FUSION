# Spin-Vorticity Coupling for Massive Vector Mesons

> Recent experiments at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and Large Hadron Collider (LHC) have indicated that hadrons containing strange quarks produced in non-central heavy ion collisions can be polarized. We investigate in detail the coupling of spin and vorticity for electrically neutral, massive vector bosons using the Proca equation, and provide the nonrelativistic reduction of the field equations via a single Foldy--Wouthuysen transformation. We find that the resulting Hamiltonian is not-Hermitian, but ${\cal PT}$ invariant, and involves a spin dependent term $\textstyle{\frac{1}{2}} s_z \hbar ω$ to leading order in vorticity. We also calculate further relativistic and quantum corrections to the Hamiltonian.

# Spin-Vorticity Coupling for Massive Vector Mesons

## Key claim

The authors find that the nonrelativistic Hamiltonian for massive vector mesons in a rotating frame contains a spin-dependent term of $\frac{1}{2} s_z \hbar \omega$ to leading order in vorticity, not the $\pm \omega$ splitting one might naively expect; the resulting Hamiltonian is non-Hermitian but ${\cal PT}$ invariant, with the non-Hermitian term being a relativistic and quantum correction of order $\hbar/c^2$ that does not appear in classical physics.

## Method

The authors start from the Proca equation for electrically neutral, massive vector bosons and transform the field equations to a rotating frame of reference using tetrads. They derive a Schrodinger-like Hamiltonian formulation using a specific linear combination of the fields and their conjugate momenta, then perform a Foldy-Wouthuysen nonrelativistic reduction to obtain the Hamiltonian for positive energy states including leading relativistic corrections. The non-Hermitian but ${\cal PT}$ symmetric term is analyzed by solving truncated versions of the $2 \times 2$ Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- The inferred vorticity from STAR measurements is $\omega = (9 \pm 1) \times 10^{21}$ s$^{-1}$, translating to an energy of $\omega = 6$ MeV (using units where $\hbar = c = k_B = 1$).
- The leading order spin-dependent term in the nonrelativistic Hamiltonian is $\frac{1}{2} \hbar \omega S_3$, giving energy splittings of $0, \pm \frac{1}{2} \omega$.
- The relativistic correction to the kinetic energy is $-({\bf p}^2)^2 / (8m^3 c^2)$.
- The vorticity-dependent quantum correction term is $(\hbar \omega)^2 / (8m c^2) S_3^2$.
- For plane wave solutions near the origin, positive energies are $E = \sqrt{E_p^2 + \frac{1}{4} \omega^2} \pm \frac{1}{2} \omega$, where $E_p^2 = p^2 + m^2$.

## Context

This work is motivated by experimental observations of hyperon polarization by the STAR Collaboration at RHIC and the ALICE Collaboration at LHC, and by the puzzling spin alignment of vector mesons ($K^{*0}(892)$ and $\phi(1020)$) measured by ALICE at LHC energies. The authors extend their previous work on strange quark equilibration with vorticity (Kapusta, Rrapaj, Rudaz, 2018-2020) to massive vector mesons, using the Proca equation rather than the Dirac equation used for spin-1/2 particles. The results are relevant to understanding polarization phenomena in heavy ion collisions, rapidly rotating neutron stars, and rotating cold atomic gases. The nonrelativistic reduction follows earlier work by Young (1976) and Silenko (2018) on vector mesons in electromagnetic fields.
