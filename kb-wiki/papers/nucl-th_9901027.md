# Neutrino nucleus scattering

> The status of the theoretical description of neutrino-nuclear interaction for low and intermediate energies is reviewed and its result compared with the existing data. Particular emphasis is on $^{12}$C, the ingredient of liquid scintillator, and on $^{16}$ the main component of the water Čerenkov detectors. First, I show that the data on the exclusive process populating the ground state of $^{12}$N are well reproduced by the theory. This is also the case for the excitation of the continuum with low energy neutrinos from the muon decay at rest and for the muon capture. However, for not yet understood reasons, the theory overestimates the cross section for higher energy neutrinos from the pion decay in flight, by up to 50%. I also show that the Continuum Random Phase Approximation and the Relativistic Fermi Gas model give very similar full and differential cross sections for the neutrino energies of several hundred MeV, thus checking one method against the other.

# Neutrino Nucleus Scattering

## Key claim

The theoretical description of neutrino-nuclear interactions at low and intermediate energies reproduces data well for exclusive processes and low-energy inclusive reactions on ¹²C and ¹⁶O, but for not yet understood reasons the theory overestimates the cross section for higher energy neutrinos from pion decay in flight by up to 50%.

## Method

The paper reviews calculations using three theoretical approaches: the nuclear shell model for low energies, the Continuum Random Phase Approximation (CRPA) for intermediate energies, and the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) model for higher energies. These methods are compared against experimental data from the KARMEN and LSND collaborations for charged-current and neutral-current reactions on ¹²C and ¹⁶O, including exclusive transitions to bound states and inclusive reactions populating the continuum.

## Key numbers

- Exclusive ¹²C(ν_e,e⁻)¹²N_gs cross section (decay-at-rest ν_e): experiment 9.4±0.5±0.8 ×10⁻⁴² cm² (KARMEN), shell model 9.1 ×10⁻⁴² cm², CRPA 8.9 ×10⁻⁴² cm²
- Exclusive ¹²C(ν_μ,μ⁻)¹²N_gs cross section (decay-in-flight ν_μ): experiment 66±10±10 ×10⁻⁴² cm² (LSND), shell model 63.5 ×10⁻⁴² cm², CRPA 63.0 ×10⁻⁴² cm²
- Inclusive ¹²C(ν_e,e⁻)¹²N* cross section (decay-at-rest): CRPA 6.3 ×10⁻⁴² cm² (Bonn potential) and 5.9 ×10⁻⁴² cm² (Migdal force), compared to LSND measurement 5.7±0.6±0.6 ×10⁻⁴² cm²
- Inclusive ¹²C(ν_μ,μ⁻)¹²N* cross section (decay-in-flight): CRPA 19.2 ×10⁻⁴⁰ cm², compared to LSND measurement 11.3±0.3±1.8 ×10⁻⁴⁰ cm² (discrepancy of about a factor 1.5)
- Muon capture inclusive rates: CRPA gives 0.342, 0.969, and 26.2 ×10⁵ s⁻¹ for ¹²C, ¹⁶O, and ⁴⁰Ca; measured rates are 0.320, 0.924, and 25.6 ×10⁵ s⁻¹ for the same nuclei
- Typical momentum transfers: ⟨|q|⟩ ≈ 50 MeV for decay-at-rest ν_e on ¹²C, ≈ 90 MeV for muon capture, ≈ 200 MeV for decay-in-flight ν_μ on ¹²C
- Threshold for ¹²C(ν_μ,μ⁻) reaction: 123 MeV; threshold for ¹²C(ν_e,e⁻) reaction: 17 MeV

## Context

This review extends the author's series of calculations with Kolbe, Langanke, and Engel (1994-1995) comparing CRPA and shell model predictions to KARMEN and LSND data. The work contrasts with calculations by Oset (1998), Auerbach (who claims pairing effects reduce cross sections), and Mintz (who uses elementary-particle treatment to obtain values closer to experiment). The discrepancy at LAMPF energies is particularly important because the LSND collaboration's evidence for ν_μ → ν_e oscillations relies on the same neutrino beam and requires knowledge of the inclusive cross section on ¹²C. The comparison of CRPA and RFG at several hundred MeV validates the method used by Super-Kamiokande (1998) to analyze the atmospheric neutrino zenith angle dependence.
