# Non-local Potentials and Rotational Bands of Resonances in Ion Collisions

> Sequences of rotational resonances (rotational bands) and corresponding antiresonances are observed in ion collisions. In this paper we propose a description which combines collective and single-particle features of cluster collisions. It is shown how rotational bands emerge in many-body dynamics, when the degeneracies proper of the harmonic oscillator spectra are removed by adding interactions depending on the angular momentum. These interactions can be properly introduced in connection with the exchange-forces and the antisymmetrization, and give rise to a class of non-local potentials whose spectral properties are analyzed in detail. In particular, we give a classification of the singularities of the resolvent, which are associated with bound states and resonances. The latter are then studied using an appropriate type of collective coordinates, and a hydrodynamical model of the trapping, responsible for the resonances, is then proposed. Accordingly, we derive, from the uncertainty principle, a spin-width of the unstable states which can be related to their angular lifetime.

## Key claim

Rotational bands of resonances in ion collisions emerge from many-body dynamics when harmonic oscillator degeneracies are removed by adding angular-momentum-dependent interactions, which can be introduced through exchange forces and antisymmetrization; the paper proposes a description combining collective and single-particle features, but the analysis is limited to the one-channel elastic scattering case and omits Coulomb effects.

## Method

The authors first formulate the many-body problem using Jacobi coordinates and hyperspherical coordinates, showing that harmonic oscillator potentials produce cluster ground-state wavefunctions as products of gaussians. They then derive an integro-differential equation for the relative motion of two clusters, incorporating non-local potentials that arise from antisymmetrization and exchange forces, and analyze the spectral properties of the resolvent using the Fredholm alternative. Finally, they introduce collective Z-coordinates and a hydrodynamical model of the trapping mechanism to study resonances, deriving a spin-width from the uncertainty principle.

## Key numbers

- Energy spectrum of rotational bands fits the form \(E_\ell = A + B \, \ell(\ell+1)\), where \(\ell\) is the orbital angular momentum and \(A, B\) are constants almost independent of \(\ell\).
- For three identical particles of mass \(m\) with harmonic oscillator potential \(V(\rho) = 3G\rho^2\) and \(K = 6G\), the energy eigenvalues are \(E_j = (j+3)\omega\) with \(\omega = (K/m)^{1/2}\) and \(j = j_1 + j_2\).
- The dimension formula for SU(3) representations gives \(\text{dim}(j_1,j_2) = (j_1+1)(j_2+1)(j_1+j_2+2)/2\), which for \((2n,0)\) yields \(\ell = 0,2,4,\ldots,2n\) (even parity band) and for \((2n+1,0)\) yields \(\ell = 1,3,5,\ldots,2n+1\) (odd parity band).
- The ground state radial wavefunction for \(N\) identical particles is \(R(\rho) = \exp(-m\omega\rho^2/2)\) with \(\rho^2 = \sum_{i=1}^{N-1} \xi_i^2\).

## Context

This work extends the algebraic-geometric approach to rotational bands (Elliott rule, known from nuclear shell and rotational models) by deriving it within the Jacobi coordinate framework for the many-body problem, which the authors note has not been done previously. The paper builds on the resonating group method (Tang), complex generator coordinate technique (LeMere), and cluster coordinate method (Wildermuth) for deriving non-local potentials from many-body dynamics, but focuses on spectral analysis rather than computational methods. The results enable a unified description of bound states, resonances, and antiresonances in cluster collisions through non-local potentials, and introduce the concept of spin-width for rotational resonances that can be related to angular lifetime.
