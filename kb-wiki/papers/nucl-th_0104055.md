# Two-Frequency Shell-Model Calculations for p-Shell Nuclei

> We have studied p-shell nuclei using a two-frequency shell-model approach with an effective interaction derived from the Bonn-A nucleon-nucleon potential by means of a G-matrix folded-diagram method. Shell-model wave functions of two different oscillator constant are employed, one for the inner 0s core orbit and the outer valence orbits, respectively. The binding energies, energy spectra, and electromagnetic properties are calculated and compared with experiment. A quite satisfactory agreement with the experimental data is obtained, which is in some cases even better than that produced by large-basis shell-model calculations.

## Key claim

The two-frequency shell-model (TFSM) approach, using different oscillator constants for core and valence orbits, can reproduce binding energies, spectra, and electromagnetic properties of p-shell nuclei with an agreement that is in some cases even better than that produced by large-basis shell-model calculations, but only when the outer length parameter \(b_{\rm out}\) is chosen separately for different groups of nuclei (He isotopes, Li isotopes, and Be/B isotopes) based on their binding energy per valence nucleon.

## Method

The authors derived an effective interaction from the Bonn-A nucleon-nucleon potential using a G-matrix folded-diagram method, but with the G-matrix calculated in a space of harmonic oscillator wave functions having two different length parameters: \(b_{\rm in}=1.45\) fm for the inner \(0s\) core orbit and a variable \(b_{\rm out}\) (1.45 to 2.50 fm) for the outer valence orbits. They then performed shell-model calculations for p-shell nuclei with \(A \leq 9\) using \(^4\)He as a closed core and valence nucleons occupying the \(0p_{3/2}\) and \(0p_{1/2}\) orbits, with the single-particle spacing taken from the experimental spectrum of \(^5\)He.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of \(^6\)Li: experimental 31.99 MeV; TFSM with \(b_{\rm out}=2.00\) fm gives 32.25 MeV (discrepancy 0.26 MeV)
- Binding energy of \(^6\)He: experimental 29.27 MeV; TFSM with \(b_{\rm out}=2.25\) fm gives 29.76 MeV (discrepancy 0.49 MeV)
- Binding energy of \(^8\)Be: experimental 56.50 MeV; TFSM with \(b_{\rm out}=1.75\) fm gives 57.76 MeV, with \(b_{\rm out}=2.00\) fm gives 52.20 MeV (optimum \(b_{\rm out}=1.9\) fm)
- Binding energy of \(^8\)He: experimental 31.41 MeV; TFSM with \(b_{\rm out}=2.25\) fm gives 31.79 MeV
- Binding energy of \(^8\)Li: experimental 41.28 MeV; TFSM with \(b_{\rm out}=2.00\) fm gives 40.96 MeV
- Single-particle spacing \(\epsilon_{1/2} - \epsilon_{3/2} = 4.0\) MeV taken from \(^5\)He experimental spectrum
- Single-particle energy \(\epsilon_{3/2} = 0.886\) MeV (experimental one-neutron separation energy for \(^5\)He)
- Core length parameter fixed at \(b_{\rm in}=1.45\) fm
- Pauli operator boundary parameter \(n_3 = 21\) used for G-matrix calculations
- Expansion of mixed-frequency matrix elements required \(N \leq 10\) terms for accuracy

## Context

This work extends the two-frequency shell-model approach proposed by Kuo, Muther, and Amir Azimi-Nili (1996) and Kuo, Krmpotic, and Tzeng (1997) to a systematic study of p-shell nuclei. It contrasts with the classic Cohen and Kurath (1965) phenomenological shell-model study and with large-basis no-core shell-model calculations by Navratil and Barrett (1998) using the Reid 93 potential, finding comparable or better agreement despite using a smaller model space. The approach enables future studies of halo nuclei such as \(^6\)He and \(^{11}\)Li, where the spatial extension of valence neutrons relative to a tight core is a key structural feature.
