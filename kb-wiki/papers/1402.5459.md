# Energy-dependent existence of soliton in the synthesis of chemical elements

> Light chemical elements are, for instance, produced through ion collisions taking place in the core of stars, where fusion is particularly important to the synthesis of chemical elements. Meanwhile soliton provides non-interacting transparency leading to the hindrance of fusion cross section. In order to explain high fusion cross section actually observed in low incident energies, it is necessary to discover the suppression mechanism of soliton propagation. In this paper, based on a systematic three-dimensional time-dependent density functional calculation, the existence of soliton is examined for ion collisions with some incident energies, impact parameters, and nuclear force parameter sets. As a result solitons are suggested to exist highly depending on the energy. The suppression of soliton is consequently due to the spin-orbit force and the momentum-dependent components of the nuclear force.

## Key claim

Solitons are suggested to exist in heavy-ion collisions only at specific incident energies, with the suppression of soliton propagation being caused by the spin-orbit force and the momentum-dependent components of the nuclear force, particularly the highly momentum-dependent term (t2-term). The paper claims that the energy-dependent existence of solitons explains why high fusion cross sections are observed at low incident energies.

## Method

The authors performed systematic three-dimensional time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) calculations for collisions between ⁴He and ⁸He nuclei. They examined 32 different nuclear interaction parameter sets based on the SV-bas Skyrme interaction, with binary labels (e.g., SVb11111) indicating which terms (t0, t1, t2, t3, and spin-orbit W0) were turned on or off. Collisions were simulated at reduced incident energies of 1.0, 10.0, and 100.0 MeV for both central (b=0.0 fm) and peripheral (b=4.0 fm) impact parameters, and mass-transparency, momentum-transparency, and charge equilibration rates were calculated to identify soliton behavior.

## Key numbers

- Reduced incident energies examined: 1.0 MeV, 10.0 MeV, and 100.0 MeV
- Relative velocities for central collisions: 0.09c (1.0 MeV), 0.28c (10.0 MeV), and 0.81c (100.0 MeV)
- Duration times: 2.7×10⁻²² s (1.0 MeV), 0.7×10⁻²² s (10.0 MeV), and 0.2×10⁻²² s (100.0 MeV)
- Highest incident energy for fusion appearance: 3.0 MeV for SVb11011 and SVb11110 interactions, 4.0 MeV for SVb11111 interaction
- Momentum-transparency rates for SVb11111 at 10.0 MeV: Tτ = 0.85 (central collision)
- Mass-transparency rates for SVb11111 at 10.0 MeV: Tρ = 0.95 (central collision)
- Charge equilibration rate for SVb11111 at 1.0 MeV: Ece = 1.00 (central collision)
- Charge equilibration rate for SVb11111 at 10.0 MeV: Ece = 0.27 (central collision)
- Spatial grid: 1.0 fm increments; vacuum boxes 24×24×24 fm³ (stationary) and 32×32×32 fm³ (non-stationary)
- Time step: one-third of 10⁻²³ s

## Context

This work extends the earlier soliton studies in heavy-ion reactions by Raha and Weiner (1983), who examined momentum transparency but left the suppression mechanism of solitons as an open problem, particularly for three-dimensional realistic conditions. The paper also connects to charge equilibration studies by Iwata et al. (2010), which found an upper-limit incident energy for fast charge equilibration and a constant propagation speed of approximately 0.25c. The results enable future investigations into how the spin-orbit force and momentum-dependent nuclear force components control soliton existence and fusion cross sections in nucleosynthesis-relevant energy regimes.
