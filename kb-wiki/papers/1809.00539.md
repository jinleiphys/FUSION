# Effect of Confinement of Gluons and One Pion Exchange in Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction

> The Nucleon-Nucleon interaction in the singlet ($^1 S_0$) and triplet ($^3 S_1$) channels have been studied in the framework of Relativistic Harmonic Model using the Resonating Group Method in Born-Oppenheimer approximation. The full Hamiltonian consists of the kinetic energy, two body confinement potential, confined one gluon exchange potential and one pion exchange potential. Contribution of Confined One Gluon Exchange Potential and One Pion Exchange Potential to the adiabatic nucleon-nucleon interaction potential is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the confined one gluon exchange potential (COGEP) alone can generate intermediate-range attraction in the nucleon-nucleon (NN) adiabatic potential, effectively playing the role usually attributed to sigma meson exchange, but only when the one pion exchange potential (OPEP) is also included as a state-independent repulsive contribution.

## Method

The authors used the Relativistic Harmonic Model (RHM) to describe quarks confined within nucleons, with the full Hamiltonian including kinetic energy, a two-body confinement potential, the confined one gluon exchange potential (COGEP) derived from the current confinement model (CCM), and the one pion exchange potential (OPEP). The Resonating Group Method (RGM) with a totally antisymmetric six-quark wave function was employed in the Born-Oppenheimer approximation to compute the adiabatic NN interaction potential for the singlet (1S0) and triplet (3S1) channels as a function of the relative distance between nucleons.

## Key numbers

- Oscillator size parameter b = 0.6 fm, chosen to be consistent with experimental charge distribution of nucleons and axial charge distribution.
- Quark-gluon coupling constant αs = 6.5, fixed by the N-Δ mass splitting from the color magnetic term of COGEP.
- Pion mass mπ = 140.0 MeV used in the OPEP calculation.
- Quark mass M = 160.6 MeV and confinement strength α2 = 200.01 MeV fm2.
- Two values of the quark-pion coupling constant were tested: fq2 = 12.6 and fq2 = 20.6, showing changes in the adiabatic potential.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by the same group (Nilakanthan et al., prior work) that computed NN scattering potentials and phase shifts without OPEP, to now include the one pion exchange contribution. The results are consistent with established findings from other quark model approaches (e.g., the work cited as [17,30,31]) regarding short-range repulsion in both 1S0 and 3S1 states. The paper demonstrates that confinement of gluons can generate intermediate-range attraction without invoking sigma mesons, and the inclusion of OPEP provides the necessary chiral symmetry constraints. The approach is complementary to lattice QCD calculations of nuclear potentials (e.g., the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave function method) and is relevant to ongoing experimental programs at J-PARC, PANDA, NICA, and HIAF.
