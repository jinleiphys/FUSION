# Molecular $Ω_{cc}$ , $Ω_{bb}$ and $Ω_{bc}$ states

> We study the interaction of meson-baryon coupled channels carrying quantum numbers of $Ω_{cc}$ , $Ω_{bb}$ and $Ω_{bc}$ presently under investigation by the LHCb collaboration. The interaction is obtained from an extension of the local hidden gauge approach to the heavy quark sector that has proved to provide accurate results compared to experiment in the case of $Ω_{c}$ , $Ξ_{c}$ states and pentaquarks, $P_c$ and $P_{cs}$. We obtain many bound states, with small decay widths within the space of the chosen coupled channels. The spin-parity of the states are $J^P={\frac{1}{2}}^-$ for coupled channels of pseudoscalar-baryon (${\frac{1}{2}}^+$), $J^P={\frac{3}{2}}^-$ for the case of pseudoscalar-baryon (${\frac{3}{2}}^+$), $J^P={\frac{1}{2}}^-,{\frac{3}{2}}^-$ for the case of vector-baryon (${\frac{1}{2}}^+$) and $J^P={\frac{1}{2}}^-,{\frac{3}{2}}^-,{\frac{5}{2}}^-$ for the vector-baryon (${\frac{3}{2}}^+$) channels. We look for poles of the states and evaluate the couplings to the different channels. The couplings obtained for the open channels can serve as a guide to see in which reaction the obtained states are more likely to be observed.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the existence of many molecular bound states of mesons and baryons with the quantum numbers of Omega_cc, Omega_bb, and Omega_bc, but these predictions are conditional on the chosen coupled channels and the neglect of pseudoscalar exchange terms, which are expected to break spin degeneracy only by a small amount.

## Method

The interaction between meson-baryon coupled channels is obtained from an extension of the local hidden gauge approach to the heavy quark sector, using vector meson exchange as the driving mechanism. The authors classify the coupled channels into four spin-parity blocks (pseudoscalar-baryon with 1/2+ baryons, pseudoscalar-baryon with 3/2+ baryons, vector-baryon with 1/2+ baryons, and vector-baryon with 3/2+ baryons) and solve for poles of the scattering T-matrix to identify bound states and evaluate their couplings to the different channels.

## Key numbers

* For Omega_cc: the Xi_cc Kbar channel has a threshold mass of 4115 MeV, the Omega_cc eta channel has 4263 MeV, the Xi_c D channel has 4338 MeV, and the Xi'_c D channel has 4448 MeV (PB(1/2+) block, J^P = 1/2-).
* For Omega_bb: the Xi_bb Kbar channel has a threshold mass of 10833 MeV, the Omega_bb eta channel has 10778 MeV, the Xi_b Bbar channel has 11076 MeV, and the Xi'_b Bbar channel has 11214 MeV (PB(1/2+) block, J^P = 1/2-).
* For Omega_bc: the Xi_bc Kbar channel has a threshold mass of 7415 MeV, the Omega_bc eta channel has 7559 MeV, the Xi_b D channel has 7667 MeV, and the Xi_c Bbar channel has 7747 MeV (PB(1/2+) block, J^P = 1/2-).
* The coupling constant g is taken as m_V/(2 f_pi) with m_V = 800 MeV and f_pi = 93 MeV.
* A suppression factor lambda = 0.25 is used for the exchange of a D* meson relative to light vector meson exchange.

## Context

This work extends the molecular approach previously applied successfully to Omega_c states (Debastiani 2017), Xi_cc states (Dias 2018), Xi_bb and Omega_bbb states (Dias 2019), Xi_c and Xi_b states (Yu 2018), Xi_bc states (Yu 2019), and Omega_b states (Liang 2017). The predictions are motivated by ongoing LHCb investigations of Omega_cc, Omega_bb, and Omega_bc states, and the calculated couplings to open channels can guide experimental searches for these predicted molecular states.
