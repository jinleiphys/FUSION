# The quark star in the Quark Mean Field approach

> revtex4, 25 pages, 15 colour eps figures;Presented at the workshop: Physics of Neutron Star Interiors, in the European Center for Theoretical Studies in Nuclear Physics and related Areas ECT*, Trento, Italy, 2000

# The quark star in the Quark Mean Field approach

## Key claim

The quark mean field (QMF) model extended with strange meson fields predicts that strange quark matter (SQM) is not absolutely stable in flat spacetime, but a quark star can become gravitationally bound and stable when the gravitational binding energy is taken into account, with a possible transition from a hyperon star to a quark star releasing approximately 8.9 × 10⁵² ergs.

## Method

The authors extended the quark mean field (QMF) model, originally proposed by Guichon, by incorporating additional strange meson fields (scalar f₀ and vector φ) that couple only to the s quark, using a Lagrangian density derived from the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (OTV) equations for static spherical stars using the equation of state obtained from the mean field approximation, with two parameter sets: one with SU(3)-symmetric quark masses (310.6 MeV for all flavors) from Buballa and Oertel, and one with non-symmetric masses (367.7 MeV for u,d; 549.5 MeV for s) from Schertler et al. The quark matter was treated in β-equilibrium with charge neutrality, and the results were compared to a hyperon star described by the relativistic mean field (RMF) model with TM1 parameter set including Λ hyperons.

## Key numbers

- Quark masses for SU(3)-symmetric case: m*_u = m*_d = m*_s = 310.6 MeV (from Buballa and Oertel parameter set)
- Quark masses for non-symmetric case: m*_u = m*_d = 367.7 MeV, m*_s = 549.5 MeV (from Schertler et al. parameter set)
- Bag pressure B₀ = 57.3 MeV/fm³, originating from the NJL model
- NJL cutoff parameter Λ = 602.3 MeV, with GΛ² = 1.835
- Critical density for strange quark core appearance: ρ_cs = 8.92 × 10¹⁴ g/cm³
- Central density for example star: ρ_c = 2 × 10¹⁵ g/cm³
- Normal nuclear matter density: n₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³
- Baryon number of the Sun: N_⊙ = 1.187 × 10⁵⁷
- Gravitational binding energy difference between hyperon and quark star: ∼ 8.9 × 10⁵² ergs
- Example conversion: hyperon star with M_B = M_⊙, M ∼ 0.92 M_⊙, R ∼ 13 km, ρ_c ∼ 4 g/cm³ transforms to quark star with M ∼ 0.85 M_⊙, R ∼ 8.5 km, ρ_c ∼ 8.9 g/cm³
- Meson masses used: M_ω = 783 MeV, M_ρ = 770 MeV, m_s = 511.198 MeV, m_s* = 975 MeV, M_φ = 1020 MeV

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling model of Guichon (known as QMF or QMC) by incorporating strange meson fields to study quark stars, building on the three-flavor NJL model calculations of Buballa and Oertel and Schertler et al. The authors find that while flat-space calculations suggest SQM is not absolutely stable (contradicting the original conjecture of Bodmer and Witten), gravitational binding can stabilize quark stars, and the energy released during a neutron-to-quark star conversion may explain gamma-ray bursts as suggested by Ignatiev. The results enable future studies of mixed hyperon-quark stars and the role of kaon condensation in softening the equation of state.
