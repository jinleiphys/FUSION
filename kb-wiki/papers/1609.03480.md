# Breaking the sound barrier in AdS/CFT

> It has been conjectured that the speed of sound in holographic models with UV fixed points has an upper bound set by the value of the quantity in conformal field theory. If true, this would set stringent constraints for the presence of strongly coupled quark matter in the cores of physical neutron stars, as the existence of two-solar-mass stars appears to demand a very stiff Equation of State. In this article, we present a family of counterexamples to the speed of sound conjecture, consisting of strongly coupled theories at finite density. The theories we consider include ${\cal N}=4$ super Yang-Mills at finite R-charge density and non-zero gaugino masses, while the holographic duals are Einstein-Maxwell theories with a minimally coupled scalar in a charged black hole geometry. We show that for a small breaking of conformal invariance, the speed of sound approaches the conformal value from above at large chemical potentials.

## Key claim
The paper presents a family of counterexamples to the conjecture that the speed of sound in holographic models with UV fixed points has an upper bound set by the conformal value \(v_s^2 = 1/3\). The violation occurs specifically for strongly coupled theories at finite density with a small breaking of conformal invariance, where the speed of sound approaches the conformal value from above at large chemical potentials.

## Method
The authors construct holographic duals using Einstein-Maxwell gravity with a minimally coupled charged scalar field in a charged black hole geometry, corresponding to \(\mathcal{N}=4\) super Yang-Mills theory at finite R-charge density with non-zero gaugino masses. They solve the linearized scalar field equations in the AdS Reissner-Nordstrom background, first analytically in the near-extremal limit and then numerically for general nonextremal solutions, extracting the speed of sound from the thermodynamic quantities via the renormalized on-shell action.

## Key numbers
- Speed of sound squared approaches \(v_s^2 = 1/3\) from above at large chemical potentials, with correction \(v_s^2 \simeq \frac{1}{3}(1 + 4 C_\nu \mu_r^{2\nu-4})\), where \(C_\nu\) is positive for certain parameter ranges (e.g., \(q=0\) gives \(C_\nu > 0\) for all \(\nu\) in \(1 \leq \nu \leq 2\)).
- For the scalar field, the conformal dimension must satisfy \(\Delta > 2 + \sqrt{1 + q^2/2} \geq 3\) to have real \(\lambda = \sqrt{\nu^2 - q^2/2 - 1}\) and avoid oscillatory behavior.
- The critical charge for the extremal AdS Reissner-Nordstrom solution is \(Q_c = \sqrt{2} r_H^3\) (where \(T=0\)).
- Nuclear saturation density is given as \(n_s \approx 0.16/\text{fm}^3\) for context.

## Context
This work directly contradicts the speed of sound conjecture proposed by Hohler and Stephanov (2009) and Cherman, Cohen, and Nellore (2009), which claimed that asymptotically AdS geometries predict \(v_s^2 \leq 1/3\). It extends the authors' previous study of hybrid neutron stars (Hoyos et al., 2016), where conformal quark matter led to instability. The results enable construction of holographic equations of state for deconfined quark matter that can support two-solar-mass neutron stars, addressing the tension noted by Bedaque and Steiner (2014) between the speed of sound bound and the existence of massive neutron stars observed by Demorest et al. (2010) and Antoniadis et al. (2013).
