# Symmetry energy: from nuclear matter to finite nuclei

> We suggest a particular procedure of derivation of the beta-stability line and isotopic symmetry energy. The behavior of the symmetry energy coefficient $b(A,N-Z)$ is analyzed. We redefine the surface tension coefficient and the surface symmetry energy for an asymmetric nuclear Fermi-liquid drop with a finite diffuse layer. Following Gibbs-Tolman concept, we introduce the equimolar radius at which the surface tension is applied. The relation of the nuclear macroscopic characteristics like surface and symmetry energies, Tolman length, etc. to the bulk properties of nuclear matter is considered. The surface-to-volume symmetry energy ratio for several Skyrme-force parametrizations is obtained.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the beta-stability line and symmetry energy coefficient can be extracted from experimental binding energies using the isospin shift of neutron-proton chemical potentials, but note that the strict condition \(\lambda_n - \lambda_p = 0\) for beta-stability is "too strong" because beta-decay is forbidden only when \(|\lambda_n - \lambda_p| < m_e c^2\), leading to a more smooth behavior than the formal condition predicts.

## Method

The authors derive the beta-stability line \(X^*(A)\) and symmetry energy coefficient \(b(A)\) from experimental binding energies using the relation \(\Delta\lambda(A,X)/4 = [b(A) + e_C(A)]X - e_C(A)\), where \(\Delta\lambda\) is the neutron-proton chemical potential shift evaluated from differences in binding energies of neighboring nuclei. They then apply the Gibbs-Tolman concept of dividing surfaces to a nuclear Fermi-liquid drop with finite diffuse layer, using the extended Thomas-Fermi approximation (ETFA) with Skyrme forces to compute surface tension, equimolar radius, and Tolman length as functions of asymmetry parameter \(X\).

## Key numbers

- Volume symmetry energy coefficient: \(b_{\mathrm{sym,vol}} = 27\) MeV, surface symmetry energy coefficient: \(b_{\mathrm{sym,surf}} = -23\) MeV, giving surface-to-volume ratio \(r_{S/V} \approx 0.85\) from fitting the beta-stability line.
- Coulomb energy parameter fit: \(e_C(A) = C_1 A^{2/3} + C_2 A^{1/3}\) with \(C_1 = 0.207\), \(C_2 = -0.174\) from data with neutron excess \(A_- = 0\).
- Surface-to-volume symmetry energy ratio for Skyrme force parametrizations: \(r_{S/V} = |b_S/b_V| = 1.17\) to \(1.47\) from the Gibbs-Tolman analysis.
- The difference in equimolar radius between calculations with and without Coulomb interaction does not exceed 0.5% for mass number \(A\) of about 200.

## Context

This work extends the Gibbs-Tolman approach (Gibbs, 1878; Tolman, 1949) for curved interfaces to asymmetric nuclear droplets, building on prior ETFA studies with Skyrme interactions (Brueckner et al., 1985; Centelles et al., 1998; Roca-Maza et al., 2011). The authors' extraction of the symmetry energy from chemical potential shifts follows the method of Kolomietz and Sanzhur (2010) and contrasts with standard mass formula fits (Jänecke and Dooley, 2003) that cannot separate volume and surface contributions directly. The results enable improved modeling of neutron-rich nuclei, multifragmentation yields, and the role of curvature corrections in small fragments, where the exponential dependence of yield on surface tension (Laliena and Latora, 1998) makes the Tolman length significant.
