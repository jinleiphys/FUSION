# Nonperturbative renormalization of staple-shaped Wilson line operators in lattice QCD

> Quark bilinear operators with staple-shaped Wilson lines are used to study transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions (TMDPDFs) from lattice quantum chromodynamics (QCD). Here, the renormalization factors for the isovector operators, including all mixings between operators with different Dirac structures, are computed nonperturbatively in the regularization-independent momentum subtraction scheme for the first time. This study is undertaken in quenched QCD with three different lattice spacings. With Wilson flow applied to the gauge fields in the calculations, the operator mixing pattern due to chiral symmetry breaking with the lattice regularization is found to be significantly different from that predicted by one-loop lattice perturbation theory calculations. These results constitute a critical step towards the systematic extraction of TMDPDFs from lattice QCD.

## Key claim

The nonperturbative renormalization factors for isovector quark bilinear operators with staple-shaped Wilson lines, including all 16×16 operator mixing between different Dirac structures, are computed for the first time in the regularization-independent momentum subtraction (RI/MOM) scheme; however, with Wilson flow applied to the gauge fields, the observed operator mixing pattern due to chiral symmetry breaking differs significantly from that predicted by one-loop lattice perturbation theory.

## Method

The RI/MOM renormalization matrix Z^RI_O(p_R,a) was computed from amputated Green's functions of staple-shaped Wilson line operators in off-shell quark states in Landau gauge, using quenched QCD ensembles at three lattice spacings (0.04, 0.06, and 0.08 fm) with a fixed physical volume L∼2 fm. Valence quark propagators were computed with tree-level O(a) improved Wilson clover fermions at a pion mass of 1.2 GeV (and additionally 340 MeV on the coarsest ensemble), and the gauge fields were subjected to 100 steps of Wilson flow to flow-time t=1.0. The resulting RI/MOM factors were matched to the MS scheme using one-loop continuum perturbation theory matching coefficients, with a redefinition that absorbs the quasi soft factor to cancel large perturbative corrections.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacings studied: 0.04 fm (E48 ensemble), 0.06 fm (E32 ensemble), and 0.08 fm (E24 ensemble), all with L∼2 fm
- Staple extents η ranged from 0.6–0.8 fm (7,9,11 lattice units on E24; 10,12,14 on E32; 15,18,21 on E48)
- Ten different quark momenta considered, with p_R^2 ranging from 5.7 to 28 GeV^2 and p_R^z from 1.3 to 2.6 GeV
- Pion masses: m_π = 1.20(5) GeV on all ensembles, and additionally m_π = 340(20) MeV on the E24 ensemble
- Number of configurations analyzed: 30 on E24, 30 on E32, 10 on E48

## Context

This work extends previous perturbative studies of renormalization for straight Wilson line operators (Constantinou 2017, Green 2017, Chen 2017) and one-loop matching calculations for staple-shaped operators (Constantinou 2019, Ebert 2019) by providing the first nonperturbative determination of the full 16×16 mixing matrix. The results enable systematic extraction of TMDPDFs from lattice QCD via the LaMET approach (Ji 2014, 2018; Ebert 2018, 2019), particularly for ratios of quasi TMDPDFs that determine the Collins-Soper kernel, though the unexpectedly large operator mixing observed with Wilson flow implies that future calculations must account for a broader set of Dirac structures than perturbative studies suggest.
