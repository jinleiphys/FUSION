# Low energy scattering phase shifts for meson-baryon systems

> In this work, we calculate meson-baryon scattering phase shifts in four channels using lattice QCD methods. From a set of calculations at four volumes, corresponding to spatial sizes of 2, 2.5, 3, and 4 fm, and a pion mass of m_pi ~ 390 MeV, we determine the scattering lengths and effective ranges for these systems at the corresponding quark masses. We also perform the calculation at a lighter quark mass, m_pi ~ 230 MeV, on the largest volume. Using these determinations, along with those in previous work, we perform a chiral extrapolation of the scattering lengths to the physical point after correcting for the effective range contributions using the multi-volume calculations performed at m_pi ~ 390 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that effective range contributions to meson-baryon scattering phase shifts on smaller volumes are significantly larger than naively expected (where naive expectation is \(r_0 \sim 1/m_\pi\)), particularly for the \(K^{+}n\) and \(\pi^{+}\Xi^{0}\) channels, and that these range corrections must be accounted for when extrapolating scattering lengths to the physical point.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations of meson-baryon scattering phase shifts in four channels (\(\pi^{+}\Sigma^{+}\), \(K^{+}p\), \(\pi^{+}\Xi^{0}\), \(K^{+}n\)) using an \(n_f=2+1\)-flavor anisotropic clover fermion action. They used four volumes (spatial sizes 2, 2.5, 3, and 4 fm) at a pion mass of \(m_\pi \sim 390\) MeV, and one volume (4 fm) at \(m_\pi \sim 230\) MeV, extracting ground-state energy shifts via thermal-plus-excited-state fits and the matrix-Prony method. They applied Luscher's method to obtain scattering phase shifts and fit them to an effective range expansion to extract scattering lengths and effective ranges, then performed a chiral extrapolation to the physical point using Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory.

## Key numbers

*   Pion masses used: \(m_\pi \sim 390\) MeV and \(m_\pi \sim 230\) MeV.
*   Lattice volumes: spatial sizes of 2, 2.5, 3, and 4 fm (corresponding to \(L=16,20,24,32\) in lattice units).
*   Effective range for \(\pi^{+}\Xi^{0}\): \(\frac{m_\pi^2}{\mu}r_0 = -220(100)\).
*   Effective range for \(K^{+}n\): \(\frac{m_\pi^2}{\mu}r_0 = -296(88)\).
*   Scattering length parameter for \(\pi^{+}\Sigma^{+}\): \(\frac{1}{a\mu} = -1.39(31)\).
*   Scattering length parameter for \(K^{+}p\): \(\frac{1}{a\mu} = -0.73(25)\).
*   Scattering length parameter for \(\pi^{+}\Xi^{0}\): \(\frac{1}{a\mu} = -2.8(13)\).
*   Scattering length parameter for \(K^{+}n\): \(\frac{1}{a\mu} = 0.7(12)\).
*   Correlation coefficients between scattering length and effective range range from \(-0.83\) to \(-0.93\) across the four channels.

## Context

This work extends the earlier single-volume lattice QCD calculation by Torok et al. (2009) for the same meson-baryon systems, which used a \((2.5\ \mathrm{fm})^3\) volume and may have contained sizeable effective range corrections. It also builds on a larger-volume calculation by Detmold et al. (2013) that found scattering lengths differing significantly from the earlier results. The multi-volume approach at \(m_\pi \sim 390\) MeV allows the authors to determine effective ranges and correct for them in the chiral extrapolation, enabling a more reliable determination of the physical scattering lengths.
