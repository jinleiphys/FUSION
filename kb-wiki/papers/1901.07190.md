# Recent Progress on the QCD Phase Diagram

> Recent progress and the latest results on the bulk thermodynamic properties of QCD matter from lattice are reviewed. In particular, I will stress upon the fact that lattice techniques are now entering into precision era where they can provide us with new insights on even the microscopic degrees of freedom in different phases of QCD. I will discuss some instances, from the recent studies of topological fluctuations and screening masses. The progress towards understanding the effects of anomalous $U_A(1)$ symmetry on the chiral crossover transition and transport properties of QCD matter will also be discussed.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD techniques are now entering a precision era that can provide new insights into microscopic degrees of freedom in different phases of QCD, but many results remain preliminary and depend on extrapolations to the continuum limit, chiral limit, or infinite volume limit.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD calculations of bulk thermodynamic properties, topological fluctuations, screening masses, and the effects of anomalous U_A(1) symmetry on the chiral crossover transition. It summarizes results from multiple collaborations using different fermion discretizations (HISQ, stout-smeared staggered, Möbius domain wall, overlap, twisted mass) at various lattice spacings and volumes, with continuum extrapolations where available.

## Key numbers

- Pseudo-critical temperature T_c = 156.5 ± 1.5 MeV from an unweighted average of inflection points of subtracted chiral condensate and peaks of chiral susceptibility, representing an 80% reduction in systematic errors compared to the earlier value T_c = 154(9) MeV (Bazavov 2011)
- The equation of state for 2+1 flavor QCD has been measured up to T = 2 GeV using HISQ fermions with continuum extrapolation, consistent with 3-loop HTL perturbation theory
- Topological susceptibility χ_t for SU(3) gauge theory at T ∼ 4 T_d has been calculated with continuum extrapolation using reweighting techniques
- For N_f = 3 QCD with HISQ fermions on N_τ = 6 lattices, Z(2) scaling studies constrain the Z(2) line to exist for pion masses m_π < 50 MeV
- Studies with stout-smeared staggered fermions at lattice spacings N_τ = 4-10 (finest a = 0.1 fm) find no first-order Roberge-Weiss transition for M_π ≥ 50 MeV
- The tricritical point for N_f = 2 QCD on coarse lattices was found at μ_q² = 0.85(5) T², suggesting a first-order chiral transition at μ_q = 0 on those lattices

## Context

This review extends the lattice QCD phase diagram program that established the crossover nature of the transition at vanishing baryon density (Aoki 2006, Bazavov 2011, Bhattacharya 2014) and produced continuum results for the equation of state at zero baryon density (Borsanyi 2013). It discusses ongoing efforts to extend calculations to finite baryon density using imaginary chemical potential techniques (deForcrand 2002, D'Elia 2002) and Taylor expansion in μ_B (Allton 2002), aiming to constrain the possible location of a critical end-point. The work enables future studies of transport properties, jet quenching parameters, and the microscopic origin of confinement through dyon dynamics.
