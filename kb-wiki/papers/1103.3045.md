# Critical point of $N_f = 3$ QCD from lattice simulations in the canonical ensemble

> A canonical ensemble algorithm is employed to study the phase diagram of $N_f = 3$ QCD using lattice simulations. We lock in the desired quark number sector using an exact Fourier transform of the fermion determinant. We scan the phase space below $T_c$ and look for an S-shape structure in the chemical potential, which signals the coexistence phase of a first order phase transition in finite volume. Applying Maxwell construction, we determine the boundaries of the coexistence phase at three temperatures and extrapolate them to locate the critical point. Using an improved gauge action and improved Wilson fermions on lattices with a spatial extent of $1.8 \fm$ and quark masses close to that of the strange, we find the critical point at $T_E = 0.925(5) T_c$ and baryon chemical potential $μ_B^E = 2.60(8) T_c$.

## Key claim

Using a canonical ensemble algorithm with exact Fourier projection of the fermion determinant, the authors locate a critical endpoint for three-flavor QCD at \(T_E = 0.925(5) T_c\) and \(\mu_B^E = 2.60(8) T_c\), but note that this result is obtained on a small volume (spatial extent 1.8 fm) with three degenerate quark masses near the strange quark mass, and that the existence of a critical point for physical quark masses remains an open question.

## Method

The authors simulate \(N_f = 3\) QCD on \(6^3 \times 4\) lattices using an improved gauge action and clover-improved Wilson fermions with quark masses corresponding to pion masses of 750–775 MeV. They compute the canonical partition function via an exact Fourier transform of the fermion determinant, enabling simulations at fixed net baryon number. For temperatures below \(T_c\) (0.85, 0.87, 0.90, and 0.93 \(T_c\)), they measure the baryon chemical potential as a function of baryon number and identify an S-shaped wiggle indicating a first-order phase transition; they then apply the Maxwell construction to determine the coexistence phase boundaries at three temperatures and extrapolate them to locate the critical point.

## Key numbers

* Critical temperature: \(T_E = 0.925(5) T_c\) (from the abstract) or \(T_E/T_c = 0.927(5)\) (from the full text); converted to \(T_E \approx 157\) MeV using lattice spacing \(a \approx 0.3\) fm.
* Critical baryon chemical potential: \(\mu_B^E = 2.60(8) T_c\); converted to \(\mu_B^E \approx 441\) MeV.
* Critical baryon number from extrapolation: \(n_B^E = 5.7(3)\).
* Pion masses: 750 MeV for the lowest temperature to 775 MeV for the highest temperature.
* Lattice spatial extent: 1.8 fm.
* The average sign in the canonical ensemble was more than \(3\sigma\) above zero for all baryon numbers studied, indicating no sign problem.

## Context

This work extends the canonical ensemble method previously applied to \(N_f = 4\) QCD by Li et al. (2010) to the more realistic \(N_f = 3\) case. It provides a cross-check on results from grand canonical approaches such as multi-parameter reweighting by Fodor and Katz (2004), Taylor expansion by Allton et al. (2005) and Gavai and Gupta (2008), and the curvature of the critical surface by de Forcrand and Philipsen (2008), which have not settled the existence or location of the critical point. The results enable future studies of quark mass dependence and checks on larger volumes and higher cutoffs to determine whether a critical point exists for physical (2+1 flavor) QCD.
