# Exploring $^3P_0$ Superfluid in Dilute Spin-Polarized Neutron Matter

> We explore the theoretical possibility of $^3P_0$ neutron superfluid in dilute spin-polarized neutron matter, which may be relevant to the crust region of a magnetized neutron star. In such a dilute regime where the neutron Fermi energy is less than 1 MeV, the $^1S_0$ neutron superfluid can be suppressed by a strong magnetic field of the compact star. In the low-energy limit relevant for dilute neutron matter, the $^3P_0$ interaction is stronger than the $^3P_2$ one which is believed to induce the triplet superfluid in the core. We present the ground-state phase diagram of dilute neutron matter with respect to the magnetic field and numerically estimate the critical temperature of the $^3P_0$ neutron superfluid, which is found to exceed $10^7$ K.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a $^3P_0$ neutron superfluid, previously overlooked, can exist in dilute spin-polarized neutron matter under strong magnetic fields, specifically in the crust region of magnetized neutron stars where the neutron Fermi energy is less than 1 MeV and the $^1S_0$ superfluid is suppressed by the magnetic field. The claim is conditional on the system being in the fully spin-polarized regime where the Zeeman shift exceeds the saturation value, and the density is low enough that the $^3P_0$ interaction becomes stronger than the $^3P_2$ one.

## Method

The authors constructed a mean-field framework for $^3P_0$ neutron superfluidity using a separable interaction to reproduce the $^3P_0$ scattering amplitude, with low-energy constants (scattering volume $v=-2.638$ fm$^3$ and effective range $r=3.182$ fm$^{-1}$) from the Nijmegen partial wave analysis. They solved the gap equation and number density equation self-consistently to obtain the critical temperature as a function of spin-polarized neutron density, and used results from strongly interacting Fermi gases near the unitary limit to characterize the $^1S_0$ superfluid properties and construct the ground-state phase diagram.

## Key numbers

- Neutron Fermi energy at density $\rho=10^{-3}\rho_0$ (near neutron drip): $E_{\rm F}\simeq 0.6$ MeV
- $^1S_0$ scattering length: $a=-18.5$ fm
- Energy scale for $^1S_0$ superfluid in dilute system: $1/(Ma^2)=0.12$ MeV
- Saturation Zeeman shift expressed as $h_{\rm s}=(E_{\rm F}+|E_{\rm P}|)/2$, with polaron energy $E_{\rm P}\simeq -0.67E_{\rm F}$ at unitarity
- Critical temperature of $^3P_0$ superfluid exceeds $10^7$ K around $\rho_{+1/2}\simeq 0.01$ fm$^{-3}$
- Scattering volume: $v=-2.638$ fm$^3$; effective range: $r=3.182$ fm$^{-1}$
- Cutoff momentum from separable interaction fit: $\Lambda=0.63058$ fm$^{-1}$; coupling constant: $Mg=-74.745$ fm$^3$

## Context

This work extends studies of neutron superfluidity in neutron stars, where $^1S_0$ pairing dominates in the crust (reviewed by Sedrakian and Clark, 2019) and $^3P_2$ pairing is expected in the core (Takatsuka and Tamagaki, 1993). The authors note that previous work by Stein et al. (2016) calculated the unpaired magnetic field for $^1S_0$ superfluid using mean-field theory with separable interactions. The paper enables future searches for $^3P_0$ superfluid in magnetar crusts by combining magneto-hydrodynamic simulations of magnetic field evolution with the calculated phase diagram and critical temperature, and suggests that the topological nature of the $^3P_0$ gap (with Weyl nodes) could lead to characteristic spin transport and edge currents.
