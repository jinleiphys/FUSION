# B_c Production at RHIC as a Signal for Deconfinement

> The B_C meson is the bound state of b-cbar whose recent detection is the first step toward completion of the spectroscopy of heavy quark mesonic states. The b-c states have properties that conveniently fill the gap between the J/Psi and Upsilon states. Thus it is probable that at RHIC the B_c mesons will serve as a probe of deconfined matter. We find that significant differences arise for B_c formation in deconfined and confined matter. Our initial calculations suggest that: (a) The rates of normal hadronic production mechanisms at RHIC energies are not sufficient to produce a detectable number of B_c mesons, (b) If a region of deconfined quarks and gluons is formed, the production (and survival) rate can be enhanced by several orders of magnitude, and (c) The observation of B_c mesons at RHIC would signal a source of deconfined charmed quarks, and the rate of B_c production will be a measure of the initial density and temperature of that source.

## Key claim

The production of \(B_c\) mesons at RHIC would be enhanced by several orders of magnitude if a deconfined quark-gluon plasma is formed, but the paper explicitly hedges that these are "initial calculations" and "preliminary results" that will see "considerable refinement"; the enhancement depends on the initial temperature and density of the deconfined source, and the observation of any \(B_c\) at RHIC would serve as a "smoking gun" signal for deconfinement.

## Method

The authors estimated \(B_c\) production rates in two scenarios: (1) normal hadronic production from nucleon-nucleon collisions using pQCD calculations at order \(\alpha_s^4\), and (2) production in a deconfined phase where \(b\)-quarks from one nucleon-nucleon collision can combine with \(c\)-quarks from independent collisions in the same event. They calculated thermal dissociation rates of \(B_c\) states due to gluon collisions using a quarkonium break-up cross section based on the operator product expansion, and formation rates via detailed balance, then used these to compute equilibrium bound state fractions at a transition temperature of 160 MeV, both with full chemical equilibrium charm densities and with only the initially-produced charm quarks under isentropic expansion.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC, pQCD estimates give about 10 \(c\bar{c}\) pairs and 0.05 \(b\bar{b}\) pairs per central collision.
- The bound state fraction \(R_b \equiv (B_c + B_c^*)/b\bar{b}\) from normal hadronic production is \(3-10 \times 10^{-5}\) at RHIC energies.
- Under the "first year" luminosity scenario (20 inverse microbarns, no trigger), the expected number of detectable \(B_c\) events without a deconfined phase is 0.05–0.18; under "design luminosity" (65 Hz event rate, 10% centrality trigger, \(10^7\) sec/year), it is 1.5–4.9.
- With a deconfined phase and full chemical equilibrium charm, the expected \(B_c\) events rise to 18 (first year) and 490 (design luminosity).
- Using only initially-produced charm quarks at initial temperature \(T_o = 500\) MeV, the expected \(B_c\) events are 130 (first year) and 3530 (design luminosity); at \(T_o = 400\) MeV, 235 and 6420; at \(T_o = 300\) MeV, 475 and 12900.
- The binding energy of \(B_c\) is 840 MeV.
- The breakup rates for \(B_c\) in deconfined matter at expected RHIC temperatures lead to time scales of order \(1-10\) fm.
- The transition temperature used for evaluating final bound state populations is \(T_f = 160\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of heavy quarkonium as a probe of deconfined matter, building on prior potential model analyses of \(b\bar{b}\) and \(c\bar{c}\) sectors (Thews, 1999) and the recent CDF detection of \(B_c\) mesons (CDF Collaboration, 1999). The authors note that the Hard Probes Collaboration (1999) provided estimates for heavy quark production at RHIC, and they use color singlet fits from that collaboration for \(J/\psi\) and \(\Upsilon\) production. The paper enables future refinement of kinetic equations with time-dependent formation and breakup rates, and positions \(B_c\) observation as both a signal for deconfinement and a quantitative probe of initial temperature and charm density in the quark-gluon plasma.
