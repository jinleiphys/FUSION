# Electric dipole excitations near the neutron separation energies in $^{96}$Mo

> Electric dipole strength near the neutron separation energy significantly impacts nuclear structure properties and astrophysical scenarios. These excitations are complex in nature and may involve the so-called pygmy dipole resonance (PDR). Transition densities play a crucial role in understanding the nature of nuclear excited states, including collective excitations, as well as in constructing transition potentials in DWBA or coupled-channels equations. In this work, we focus on electric dipole excitations in spherical molybdenum isotopes, particularly $^{96}$Mo, employing fully consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) and Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) methods. We analyze the dipole strength near the neutron separation energy, which represents the threshold for neutron capture processes, and examine the isospin characteristics of PDR states through transition density calculations. Examination of proton and neutron transition densities reveals distinctive features of each dipole state, indicating their isoscalar and isovector nature. We observe that the primary component in the enhanced low-energy region exhibits isovector character. The PDR displays a mixture of isoscalar and isovector nature, distinguishing it from the isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR). These findings lay the groundwork for future investigations into the role of transition densities in reaction models and for their application to inelastic scattering calculations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) in $^{96}$Mo displays a mixture of isoscalar and isovector character, but the primary component in the enhanced low-energy region near the neutron separation energy exhibits isovector character, not pure pygmy mode behavior.

## Method

The authors performed fully consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) and Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) calculations using the Gogny D1M finite-range effective interaction to compute electric dipole excitations in $^{96}$Mo. They analyzed the dipole strength near the neutron separation energy and examined the isospin characteristics of PDR states through proton and neutron transition density calculations, using both the electromagnetic E1 operator and the isoscalar dipole operator with center-of-mass corrections.

## Key numbers

- The ground state root-mean-square radius for $^{96}$Mo is approximately 4.3 fm, where neutron excess indicates formation of a neutron skin
- The largest peak in the potential PDR region appears at 13.5 MeV in $^{96}$Mo, exhibiting isovector nature similar to the IVGDR
- The discrete spectrum was folded with Lorentzian functions using a width of 1 MeV to produce continuous dipole strength curves

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the PDR in neutron-rich nuclei using self-consistent mean-field models (Paar 2007, Lanza 2023) and builds on the understanding that PDR states show in-phase oscillations inside the nucleus with neutron-dominated oscillations at the surface (Vretenar 2001, Tsoneva 2008, Colo 2009). The findings lay groundwork for future inelastic scattering calculations using microscopically calculated transition densities as inputs for folding calculations, which will enable more accurate nuclear data evaluations for neutron-induced reactions and surrogate experiments aimed at constraining neutron capture cross sections.
