# Reworking the Zubarev's approach to non-equilibrium quantum statistical mechanics

> In this work the non-equilibrium density operator approach introduced by Zubarev more than 50 years ago to describe quantum systems at local thermodynamic equilibrium is revisited. This method - which was used to obtain the first ''Kubo" formula of shear viscosity, is especially suitable to describe quantum effects in fluids. This feature makes it a viable tool to describe the physics of the Quark Gluon Plasma in relativistic nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The paper revisits and reformulates Zubarev's non-equilibrium density operator approach, arguing that the original method's crucial feature of stationarity was not properly emphasized and that the extra exponential factor introduced by Zubarev is unnecessary for deriving Kubo formulas; the reformulation uses Gauss' theorem to obtain a stationary non-equilibrium density operator directly from the local equilibrium density operator at an initial time.

## Method

The authors start from the local equilibrium density operator (LEDO) defined on a spacelike hypersurface and enforce stationarity by fixing the density operator to its value at an initial time hypersurface. They then use Gauss' theorem to rewrite this stationary operator in terms of operators on a later hypersurface plus a volume integral involving gradients of the thermodynamic fields. The resulting non-equilibrium density operator is expanded perturbatively in the volume term (assumed small in the hydrodynamic limit) to obtain linear response expressions, which are then manipulated to derive covariant Kubo formulas for transport coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The paper uses natural units with ℏ = c = K = 1
- The Minkowskian metric tensor is diag(1, -1, -1, -1) with Levi-Civita convention ε^0123 = 1
- The local equilibrium density operator is defined with Lagrange multiplier functions β (four-temperature vector) and ζ (ratio of chemical potential to temperature)
- The non-equilibrium density operator is expressed as exp[−∫_Σ(τ₀) dΣ_μ (T^μν β_ν − j^μ ζ)] / Z
- The linear response expansion yields eq. (lrt): ρ ≃ ρ_LE + ∫₀¹ dz exp[zA] B exp[−zA] ρ_LE − ⟨B⟩_LE ρ_LE
- The Kubo formula for deviations of stress-energy tensor: δT^μν(x) ≃ (T^μν, T^ρσ) ∂_ρ β_σ(x) − (T^μν, j^ρ) ∂_ρ ζ(x)
- The Kubo formula for deviations of current: δj^μ(x) = (j^μ, T^ρσ) ∂_ρ β_σ(x) − (j^μ, j^ρ) ∂_ρ ζ(x)

## Context

This work extends the original non-equilibrium density operator approach by Zubarev (1960s) and its relativistic formulation by Zubarev and collaborators (1979), which was used by Hosoya et al. to derive the first "Kubo" formula for shear viscosity in quantum field theory. The reformulation clarifies the stationarity requirement and eliminates the need for the ad hoc exponential factor ε in Zubarev's evolution equation, making the method more transparent for applications to relativistic hydrodynamics. The approach is particularly suited for studying quantum effects in the Quark Gluon Plasma produced in relativistic nuclear collisions, including the recently observed non-vanishing polarization (STAR collaboration).
