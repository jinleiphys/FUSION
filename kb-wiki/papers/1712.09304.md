# Pairing dynamics and time dependent density functional theory

> We discuss issues related to pairing dynamics in nuclear large amplitude collective motion. The examples of effects which are not properly described within BCS theory are presented. In the second part we review properties of TDDFT and in particular we discuss the time-dependent local density approximation (TDSLDA) starting from the stationary action principle.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the common treatment of pairing correlations in nuclear dynamics, which assumes the pairing field adjusts adiabatically to density variations (as in BCS or HF+BCS), is fundamentally insufficient for large-amplitude collective motion; instead, the pairing field's own dynamical degrees of freedom, such as Bogoliubov phonons and Andreev reflection, must be treated on the same footing as the normal density, requiring the time-dependent superfluid local density approximation (TDSLDA) framework.

## Method

The authors contrast the TDHF+BCS approach with the TDSLDA (time-dependent superfluid local density approximation) derived from the stationary action principle. They show that TDHF+BCS equations violate the continuity equation and cannot describe processes involving quasiparticle scattering on a nonuniform pairing field, such as Andreev states or quantum vortices. The TDSLDA equations are obtained by requiring the Bogoliubov transformation to satisfy the completeness relation, leading to a set of coupled equations for the quasiparticle amplitudes \(U_\mu(\mathbf{r},t)\) and \(V_\mu(\mathbf{r},t)\) that evolve both the normal and anomalous densities self-consistently.

## Key numbers

- The pairing gap in nuclear systems does not exceed 3% of the Fermi energy, yet pairing correlations are essential for dynamics of medium or heavy nuclei.
- The Landau-Zener formula predicts that the probability of single-particle excitation decreases exponentially with the square of the pairing gap magnitude.
- Mass parameters in the vicinity of level crossings behave as \(1/\Delta\), where \(\Delta\) is the pairing gap.
- In the induced fission of \(^{240}\)Pu, the dynamics of the pairing field causes much longer fission times than expected from simplified pairing treatments (no specific numerical times given in the text).
- In collisions of two superfluid nuclei, pairing field dynamics leads to soliton-like excitations that modify the kinetic energy of fragments and the capture cross section (no specific numerical cross sections given).

## Context

This work extends the discussion of pairing dynamics beyond the standard BCS and HF+BCS frameworks, which are shown to be inadequate for large-amplitude collective motion such as nuclear fission and heavy-ion collisions. The authors build on prior developments in density functional theory for superfluid systems, including the SLDA (Superfluid Local Density Approximation) developed in a series of papers by Bulgac and collaborators (2002-2013). The paper specifically references the induced fission of \(^{240}\)Pu by Bulgac (2016) and collisions of superfluid nuclei by Magierski (2017) as examples where pairing dynamics plays a crucial role. The TDSLDA framework enables future studies of processes like quantum vortex dynamics in neutron star crusts and the pulsar glitch phenomenon, which cannot be addressed within HF+BCS theory.
