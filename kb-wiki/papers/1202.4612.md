# Nuclear symmetry energy and neutron skin thickness

> The relation between the slope of the nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density and the neutron skin thickness is investigated. Constraints on the slope of the symmetry energy are deduced from the neutron skin data obtained in experiments with antiprotonic atoms. Two types of neutron skin are distinguished: the "surface" and the "bulk". A combination of both types forms neutron skin in most of nuclei. A prescription to calculate neutron skin thickness and the slope of symmetry energy parameter $L$ from the parity violating asymmetry measured in the PREX experiment is proposed.

# Nuclear symmetry energy and neutron skin thickness

## Key claim
The slope of the nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density, L, can be constrained to 55 ± 25 MeV from antiprotonic atom data, but this value carries a large uncertainty due to experimental error bars; the paper also proposes that the PREX parity-violating asymmetry measurement can provide an independent determination of both the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb and the parameter L, though the first PREX run achieved only 10% accuracy, insufficient to discriminate among models.

## Method
The authors used the droplet model of nuclei to derive analytical relations between neutron skin thickness and the symmetry energy slope L, distinguishing "bulk" and "surface" contributions to the neutron skin. They fitted the droplet model formula (including a diffuseness term for neutron and proton surface widths) to experimental neutron skin data from antiprotonic atoms to extract L. For the PREX experiment, they performed distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations of parity-violating asymmetry Apv for 208Pb using a large set of nuclear mean-field models, then established linear correlations between Apv, neutron skin thickness, and L.

## Key numbers
- L = 55 ± 25 MeV from the fit to antiprotonic atom data, indicating a "soft" symmetry energy.
- The diffuseness term in the droplet model contributes 0.035 fm to 0.055 fm to the total neutron skin thickness of 208Pb across various models, amounting to 20% to 40% of the total.
- The linear relation between parity-violating asymmetry and neutron skin thickness in 208Pb: Apv (ppm) = 0.788 − 0.375 Δrnp (fm).
- The linear relation between Apv and L: Apv (ppm) = 0.750 − 0.000557 L (MeV).
- Preliminary PREX result: Apvexp = 0.6571 ± 0.0604 (stat) ± 0.0130 (syst) at 1.06 GeV beam energy and scattering angle ~5° (momentum transfer qlab ≈ 0.47 fm−1).
- The D1S parametrization gives L = 22 MeV ("soft" symmetry energy) while NL3 gives L = 118 MeV ("stiff" symmetry energy).

## Context
This work extends the droplet model analysis of neutron skins by Centelles et al. (2009) and Warda et al. (2009), explicitly separating "bulk" and "surface" contributions to the neutron skin. It provides a direct prescription linking the PREX parity-violating asymmetry to both the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb and the symmetry energy slope L, enabling future higher-precision measurements (e.g., PREX-II) to constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy. The antiprotonic atom data from Trzcinska et al. (2001) and Jastrzebski et al. (2004) are used as the experimental input for the L extraction.
