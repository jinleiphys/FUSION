# Hyperon-nucleon force from lattice QCD

> We calculate potentials between a proton and a $Ξ^0$ (hyperon with strangeness -2) through the equal-time Bethe-Salpeter wave function, employing quenched lattice QCD simulations with the plaquette gauge action and the Wilson quark action on (4.5 fm)^4 lattice at the lattice spacing $a \simeq 0.14$ fm. The ud quark mass in our study corresponds to $m_π\simeq 0.37$ and 0.51 GeV, while the s quark mass corresponds to the physical value of $m_K$. The central $p Ξ^0$ potential has a strong (weak) repulsive core in the $^1S_0$ ($^3S_1$) channel for $r \lsim 0.6$ fm, while the potential has attractive well at the medium and long distances (0.6 fm $\lsim r \lsim 1.2$ fm) in both channels. The sign of the $p Ξ^0$ scattering length and its quark mass dependence indicate a net attraction in both channels at low energies.

# Hyperon-nucleon force from lattice QCD

## Key claim

The paper claims that the central $p\Xi^0$ potential has a strong repulsive core in the $^1S_0$ channel and a weak repulsive core in the $^3S_1$ channel for $r \lesssim 0.6$ fm, with an attractive well at medium and long distances ($0.6$ fm $\lesssim r \lesssim 1.2$ fm) in both channels, but this result is obtained under quenched lattice QCD with quark masses corresponding to $m_\pi \simeq 0.37$ and $0.51$ GeV, not at the physical pion mass.

## Method

The authors computed the equal-time Bethe-Salpeter wave function for the $p\Xi^0$ system from four-point correlators in quenched lattice QCD with the plaquette gauge action and Wilson quark action on a $(4.5\ \text{fm})^4$ lattice at lattice spacing $a \simeq 0.14$ fm. An effective central potential $V_C(r)$ was extracted from the wave function via the leading-order term of a derivative expansion of the non-local potential, using the non-relativistic energy $E = k^2/(2\mu)$ obtained by fitting the asymptotic wave function to the lattice Green's function. The $ud$ quark mass was varied to correspond to $m_\pi \simeq 0.37$ and $0.51$ GeV, while the $s$ quark mass was fixed to the physical $m_K$ value.

## Key numbers

- Lattice volume: $(4.5\ \text{fm})^4$ with lattice spacing $a = 0.1416(9)\ \text{fm}$ ($1/a = 1.393(9)\ \text{GeV}$)
- Pion masses studied: $m_\pi \simeq 368\ \text{MeV}$ and $511\ \text{MeV}$
- Non-relativistic energy at $m_\pi \simeq 368\ \text{MeV}$: $E = -0.4(2)\ \text{MeV}$ ($^1S_0$) and $E = -0.8(2)\ \text{MeV}$ ($^3S_1$)
- Non-relativistic energy at $m_\pi \simeq 511\ \text{MeV}$: $E = -0.19(4)\ \text{MeV}$ ($^1S_0$) and $E = -0.34(4)\ \text{MeV}$ ($^3S_1$)
- Scattering lengths at $m_\pi \simeq 511\ \text{MeV}$: $a_{0s} = 0.05(1)\ \text{fm}$, $a_{0t} = 0.09(1)\ \text{fm}$
- Scattering lengths at $m_\pi \simeq 368\ \text{MeV}$: $a_{0s} = 0.10(6)\ \text{fm}$, $a_{0t} = 0.21(6)\ \text{fm}$
- Repulsive core region: $r \lesssim 0.6\ \text{fm}$; attractive well region: $0.6\ \text{fm} \lesssim r \lesssim 1.2\ \text{fm}$

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD approach to defining baryon-baryon potentials, previously applied to the nucleon-nucleon system by Ishii, Aoki, and Hatsuda (2006, 2007, 2008), to the hyperon-nucleon sector, specifically the $p\Xi^0$ system in the isovector ($I=1$) channel. The results are compared with phenomenological models such as the boson exchange model SC97f (ESC04), the quark cluster model fss2 (Fujiwara, 2006), chiral effective field theory (Haidenbauer, 2007), and QCD sum rules (Kondo), which give conflicting signs and magnitudes for the scattering lengths. The study enables future full (2+1)-flavor QCD simulations at lighter quark masses to make quantitative predictions for hypernuclear structure and neutron star interiors.
