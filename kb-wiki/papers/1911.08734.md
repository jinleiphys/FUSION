# Cluster correlation and nuclear vorticity in low-lying $1^-$ states of $^{24}$Mg

> We investigated cluster correlation and nuclear voricity in low-lying $1^-$ states of $^{24}$Mg within antisymmetrized molecular dynamics framework. We found that the toroidal and compressional dipole modes separately appear as the $K=1$ and $K=0$ states. The $1^-$ ($K=1$) state is the toroidal dipole state with the strong nuclear vorticity but no prominent cluster structure, and the $1^-$ ($K=0$) state is the compressional dipole state having enhanced cluster structure but has the weaker vorticity.

## Key claim

The low-lying $1^-$ states of $^{24}$Mg separate into two distinct modes: the $1^-_1$ ($K=1$) state is a toroidal dipole mode with strong nuclear vorticity but no prominent cluster structure, while the $1^-_2$ ($K=0$) state is a compressional dipole mode with enhanced cluster structure but weaker vorticity, contradicting the earlier suggestion by Nesterenko et al. (2017) that the toroidal state should be associated with cluster structure.

## Method

The authors applied the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) framework combined with the generator coordinate method (GCM), using both quadrupole deformation ($\beta\gamma$) constraints and inter-cluster distance ($d$) constraints for $^{20}$Ne+$\alpha$, $^{16}$O+$^{8}$Be, and $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C quasi-cluster configurations. They performed energy variation for negative-parity wave functions, projected to angular momentum eigenstates, and superposed basis states to solve the Hill-Wheeler equation. Transition strengths were calculated for the compressional dipole (CD) and toroidal dipole (TD) operators, and intrinsic transition current densities were analyzed for vorticity.

## Key numbers

- The $1^-_1$ ($K=1$) state appears at excitation energy $E_x = 9.5$ MeV with $B(\text{TD}) = 1.20 \times 10^{-3}$ fm$^4$ and negligible $B(\text{CD})$.
- The $1^-_2$ ($K=0$) state appears at $E_x = 11.2$ MeV with $B(\text{CD}) = 2.38 \times 10^{-3}$ fm$^4$ and $B(\text{TD}) = 0.41 \times 10^{-3}$ fm$^4$.
- Inclusion of cluster configurations lowers the $1^-_{K=1}$ state by 0.3 MeV and the $1^-_{K=0}$ state by 1.0 MeV, and enhances $B(\text{CD})$ of the $K=0$ state by 50% (from 1.61 to 2.38 $\times 10^{-3}$ fm$^4$).
- The $1^-_{K=1}$ state has 89% overlap with the $^{20}$Ne+$\alpha$ configuration at $d_{20+4}=2.5$ fm; the $1^-_{K=0}$ state has 88% overlap with the $^{16}$O+$^{8}$Be configuration at $d_{16+8}=2.5$ fm and 23% overlap at $d_{16+8}=4.9$ fm.
- The ground state energy minimum occurs at $(\beta,\gamma)=(0.49,13^\circ)$ for $J^\pi=0^+$; the $1^-$ energy minimum occurs at $(\beta,\gamma)=(0.5,25^\circ)$.

## Context

This work extends previous AMD studies of positive-parity states in $^{24}$Mg by Kanada-En'yo and collaborators (1995-2012) and negative-parity bands by Kimura et al. (2012), and directly addresses the prediction by Nesterenko et al. (2017) using Skyrme QRPA that a toroidal dipole state in $^{24}$Mg should be associated with cluster structure. The results enable future investigation of how deformation and cluster correlations interplay in low-energy dipole modes across the nuclear chart, particularly in light nuclei where both mean-field and cluster degrees of freedom are relevant.
