# The odd-even differences in stability peninsula for $106 \leqslant Z \leqslant 112$ region with the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum

> The predictive power of the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) with density functional PC-PK1 is demonstrated for superheavy region ($101 \leqslant Z \leqslant 120$) by comparing with available experimental and evaluated data in the AME2020. The DRHBc theory predicts 93 bound nuclei beyond the drip line $N = 258$ in the region of $106 \leqslant Z \leqslant 112$, which form a stability peninsula. The odd-even differences between odd-$N$ and even-$N$ nuclei are remarkable in the stability peninsula; the number of bound odd-$N$ nuclei is less than that of bound even-$N$ nuclei, and the one-neutron separation energy of an odd-$N$ nucleus is smaller than those of its neighboring even-$N$ nuclei due to the blocking effect. The deformation effect is indispensable for the reentrant stability beyond the drip line by significantly affecting the structure of single-particle levels around the Fermi energy. The interplay between deformation and pairing effects affects the position where the odd-$N$ nucleus becomes bound in the stability peninsula. By examining the deformation effect at different orders, it is found that quadrupole deformation makes leading contribution to the appearance of stability peninsula and the effects of hexadecapole and hexacontatetrapole deformations are nonnegligible.

## Key claim

The deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) predicts 93 bound nuclei beyond the neutron drip line N = 258 in the region 106 ≤ Z ≤ 112, forming a stability peninsula, but the abstract's claim of "reentrant stability" is qualified by the finding that odd-N nuclei are less stable than even-N nuclei, with the reentrant stability for odd-N nuclei appearing later and ending earlier along an isotopic chain.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equations in a Dirac Woods-Saxon basis with the density functional PC-PK1, incorporating deformation via Legendre expansion up to λmax = 10 and pairing correlations with a zero-range density-dependent pairing force. For odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei, the blocking effect of unpaired nucleons was included using the equal filling approximation, with automatic blocking followed by orbital-fixed blocking when necessary. The calculations were performed for superheavy nuclei in the region 101 ≤ Z ≤ 120, with binding energies compared against the AME2020 database.

## Key numbers

- DRHBc rms deviation from 36 experimental mass data in AME2020: 0.763 MeV; from 240 total (experimental plus evaluated) data: 0.833 MeV, compared to WS4 (0.339 and 1.302 MeV) and FRDM(2012) (0.692 and 2.809 MeV)
- 93 bound nuclei predicted beyond the neutron drip line N = 258 in the region 106 ≤ Z ≤ 112
- For Ds (Z = 110) isotopes in the stability peninsula: 5 bound odd-N nuclei (N = 275 to 283) versus 11 bound even-N nuclei
- One-neutron separation energy for 368Ds (N = 258) is positive, while 369Ds is unstable against one-neutron emission
- The spherical nucleus 368Ds shows a gap of approximately 3 MeV between the highest occupied and lowest unoccupied neutron orbitals
- Deformation parameter β2 for Ds isotopes increases from 0.05 at N = 264 to 0.165 at N = 383, 0.177 at N = 384, and 0.190 at N = 385

## Context

This work extends previous DRHBc studies of the stability peninsula for even-even nuclei (Zhang 2021, Pan 2021, He 2021) to include odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei, incorporating the blocking effect. The reentrant stability phenomenon was first predicted by Stoitsov (2003) using Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations, and later studied with relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations by Afanasjev (2013). The present results enable future half-life estimations for neutron emitters in the peninsula using the DRHBc + WKB approach, which has already been applied to alpha-decay and one-proton emission half-lives.
