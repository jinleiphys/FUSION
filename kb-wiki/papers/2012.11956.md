# Comprehensive test of the Brink-Axel hypothesis in the energy region of the pygmy dipole resonance

> The validity of the Brink-Axel hypothesis, which is especially important for numerous astrophysical calculations, is addressed for 116,120,124Sn below the neutron separation energy by means of three independent experimental methods. The $γ$-ray strength functions (GSFs) extracted from primary $γ$-decay spectra following charged-particle reactions with the Oslo method and with the Shape method demonstrate excellent agreement with those deduced from forward-angle inelastic proton scattering at relativistic beam energies. In addition, the GSFs are shown to be independent of excitation energies and spins of the initial and final states. The results provide a critical test of the generalized Brink-Axel hypothesis in heavy nuclei, demonstrating its applicability in the energy region of the pygmy dipole resonance.

## Key claim

The generalized Brink-Axel hypothesis, which states that the gamma-ray strength function (GSF) depends only on gamma energy and not on the energies, spins, or parities of initial and final states, is shown to hold for the heavy semimagic nuclei 116,120,124Sn in the energy region of the pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) below the neutron separation energy, based on agreement between three independent experimental methods. The authors note that this conclusion may hold generally for heavy nuclei with sufficiently high level densities, but the limits of applicability remain an open question.

## Method

The GSFs for 116,120,124Sn were extracted using three independent methods: (1) the Oslo method, which extracts the GSF and level density from primary gamma-decay spectra following charged-particle reactions (3He-induced for 116Sn and proton-induced for 120,124Sn) using an iterative subtraction technique and chi-squared minimization; (2) the Shape method, which provides a largely model-independent extraction of the GSF energy dependence from selective decay to specific final states (ground state and first excited 2+ state) using the OSCAR LaBr3(Ce) detector array; and (3) forward-angle inelastic proton scattering at 295 MeV using relativistic Coulomb excitation to extract E1 and M1 strength distributions. The GSFs from the Oslo method were normalized using discrete low-lying levels, s-wave neutron resonance spacings, and average total radiative widths.

## Key numbers

- The GSFs from the Oslo method and from inelastic proton scattering agree within experimental uncertainties for all three isotopes (116,120,124Sn) in the energy region between 6 MeV and the neutron separation energy (8.489-9.563 MeV depending on isotope)
- Peak-like structures at E_gamma approximately 6.5 MeV with a width of about 200 keV (FWHM) are observed in the (p,p') data, with strength increasing from 1 x 10^-7 MeV^-3 to 2.2 x 10^-7 MeV^-3 from 116Sn to 124Sn
- For 120Sn, the GSF extracted for three narrow initial excitation energy bins (256 keV width) shows overall good agreement with the Oslo-method result from the full excitation range (4.5-9.1 MeV)
- The OSCAR LaBr3(Ce) detectors have an energy resolution of approximately 2.7% at E_gamma = 662 keV
- Approximately 5.3 x 10^7 and 1.3 x 10^7 proton-gamma coincidences were measured for 120Sn and 124Sn, respectively

## Context

This work provides a critical test of the generalized Brink-Axel hypothesis in heavy nuclei, extending previous studies that had yielded controversial results either confirming (Guttormsen 2016, Martin 2017, Campo 2018, Scholz 2020) or violating (Angell 2012, Isaak 2013, Netterdon 2015, Isaak 2019) the hypothesis. The results verify the assumptions made in calculations of (n,gamma) reactions relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis, which rely on GSFs extracted from ground-state photoabsorption. The study enables future investigations into the limits of the Brink-Axel hypothesis as a function of gamma energy, level density, and mass number, particularly for doubly magic nuclei and for exploring whether the conclusions hold for deformed heavy nuclei with higher level densities.
