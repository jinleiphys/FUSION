# Novel shape evolution in exotic Ni isotopes and configuration-dependent shell structure

> The shapes of neutron-rich exotic Ni isotopes are studied. Large-scale shell model calculations are performed by advanced Monte Carlo Shell Model (MCSM) for the $pf$-$g_{9/2}$-$d_{5/2}$ model space. Experimental energy levels are reproduced well by a single fixed Hamiltonian. Intrinsic shapes are analyzed for MCSM eigenstates. Intriguing interplays among spherical, oblate, prolate and gamma-unstable shapes are seen including shape fluctuations, $E$(5)-like situation, the magicity of doubly-magic $^{56,68,78}$Ni, and the coexistence of spherical and strongly deformed shapes. Regarding the last point, strong deformation and change of shell structure can take place simultaneously, being driven by the combination of the tensor force and changes of major configurations within the same nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper introduces "Type II shell evolution," where the shell structure (e.g., spin-orbit splitting) changes significantly between spherical and deformed states within the same nucleus due to particle-hole excitations and the tensor force, as distinct from conventional "Type I shell evolution" driven by changes in proton or neutron number.

## Method

Large-scale shell model calculations using the Monte Carlo Shell Model (MCSM) were performed for Ni isotopes with even neutron numbers N=28-50, using the full pf shell plus 0g9/2 and 1d5/2 orbits for both protons and neutrons with no truncation. Intrinsic shapes of the MCSM eigenstates were analyzed by projecting each Slater determinant onto the potential energy surface (PES) obtained from constrained Hartree-Fock calculations, with circle size proportional to the overlap probability with the eigenstate.

## Key numbers

- The proton f7/2-f5/2 gap in 68Ni is reduced by about 3 MeV in the 0+3 state compared to the 0+1 state, narrowing from its spherical value to approximately 3 MeV at large deformation (|Q0| ~ 200 fm2)
- The prolate deformed band in 68Ni has a deformation of Q0 ~ 200 fm2, corresponding to beta2 ~ 0.4
- The 0+3 and 2+2 states of 68Ni form a strongly prolate band with beta2 ~ 0.4
- The overlap probability with the closed shell is 60% for 56Ni, 53% for 68Ni, and 75% for 78Ni
- The 2+2 level of 70Ni is observed at about 2 MeV, reproduced well by the calculation
- B(E2;0+1 -> 2+1) values were calculated with neutron and proton effective charges of 0.5 and 1.5, respectively

## Context

This work extends shell model studies of Ni isotopes by Lenzi (2010) and Kaneko (2011) using a larger model space and the MCSM method, and provides a mechanism for shape coexistence previously observed in nuclei such as 186Pb (Andreyev 2000). The concept of Type II shell evolution enables predictions for heavier exotic nuclei where unique-parity orbits and the tensor force (Otsuka 2005) can produce similar effects, and suggests that simple shape classifications from stable nuclei may not apply to exotic nuclei.
