# Thermodynamic Susceptibilities for a Unitary Fermi Gas

> The unitary Fermi gas provides a unique window into both cold atom experiments and neutron star properties. There are major challenges in determining the physical properties within a neutron star, both experimentally and theoretically. However, there is a region within the crust of a neutron star that resembles a gas of fermions that is recreated in a cold atom laboratory. This is the so-called unitary Fermi gas characterized by a large negative scattering length and small effective range. We set out to calculate from first principles certain transport coefficients that appear at second order in the hydrodynamic expansion. These particular transport coefficients are obtained from two point correlation functions in flat space that do not require analytic continuation from Euclidean to Minkowski space. We are motivated by the potential to learn about neutron star properties from cold atom experiments.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the thermodynamic susceptibility \(f_3\) for a unitary Fermi gas from first principles, but the result is obtained within the R0 approximation (equivalent to leading large \(N\) expansion or mean-field theory), which yields a Bertsch parameter \(\xi_{R0} \approx 0.59\) that differs from the measured value \(\xi \approx 0.38\).

## Method

The author starts from the non-relativistic Hamiltonian for fermions with a contact interaction and decouples the four-fermi term via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation. The path integral is then evaluated in the R0 approximation, where the auxiliary field is restricted to its global zero mode \(\Delta\), and the saddle point condition yields the gap equation. The thermodynamic susceptibility \(f_3\) is obtained from the zero-frequency, small-momentum limit of a combination of Euclidean stress-tensor two-point correlators, \(C_E(k) = -\langle T_E^{01}T_E^{01}\rangle(k) + \langle T_E^{12}T_E^{12}\rangle(k)\), evaluated at zero temperature in the unitary limit \(a_s \to -\infty\).

## Key numbers

* Bertsch parameter from the R0 approximation: \(\xi_{R0} \approx 0.59\), compared to the measured value \(\xi \approx 0.38\) (Ku 2012, Endres 2013).
* Gap-to-chemical-potential ratio at unitarity: \(\Delta \approx 1.162\mu\) from the numerical solution of the gap equation, and \(\Delta \approx 1.083\mu\) from the analytic approximation \(\Delta = \mu e^{-2+3\ln 2}\).
* Pressure at unitarity relative to free Fermi gas: \(p \approx 2.2\, p_{\text{free}}\).
* Thermodynamic susceptibility \(f_3\) at unitarity: \(f_3 = \frac{n(\mu)}{120\mu} \left(\frac{\mu^2}{\Delta^2}\right)\), where \(n(\mu)\) is the number density.
* In the free limit (\(\Delta \to 0\)), the result reduces to \(f_3 = -\frac{(2m\mu)^{3/2}}{192\pi^2\mu}\), matching the non-relativistic free fermion result of Shukla (2018).

## Context

This work extends the calculation of thermodynamic transport coefficients for the unitary Fermi gas, following the framework of Kovtun and Shukla (2018) who showed that certain second-order transport coefficients can be obtained from equilibrium susceptibilities without analytic continuation. It builds on the calculation of the transport coefficient \(\kappa\) for the UFG by Lawrence and Romatschke (2022). The result for \(f_3\) is the second of seven thermodynamic susceptibilities to be computed for this system; the remaining five, including \(f_2\) which requires diagonal stress-tensor correlators, are left for future work. The calculation is motivated by the unitary gas conjecture of Tews (2017), which uses the UFG energy as a lower bound for pure neutron matter, and by the possibility of measuring second-order transport in cold atom experiments to infer neutron star properties.
