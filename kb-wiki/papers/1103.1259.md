# Does parton saturation at high density explain hadron multiplicities at LHC?

> An addendum to our previous papers in Phys. Lett. B539 (2002) 46 and Phys. Lett. B502 (2001) 51, contributed to the CERN meeting "First data from the LHC heavy ion run", March 4, 2011

# Does parton saturation at high density explain hadron multiplicities at LHC?

## Key claim

The charged-particle multiplicities measured in central heavy ion collisions at LHC energies may not be directly determined by initial conditions from saturation models, but instead receive an additional enhancement from entropy production during the weakly-coupled "bottom-up" thermalization process, parametrically estimated as a factor of approximately 1/α_s^{2/5}.

## Method

The authors compare the ALICE multiplicity measurement at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV with predictions from saturation models using the running coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) equation, and interpret the difference as evidence for gluon number non-conservation during thermalization. They apply the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario with two undetermined constants c and c_eq, which they estimate by fitting to RHIC data at √s_0 = 130 GeV and then extrapolating to LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- ALICE measured dN_ch/dη = 1584 ± 4 (stat) ± 76 (sys) in central Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV
- Saturation model (rcBK) prediction at the same energy: dN_ch/dη = 1175 ± 75
- Ratio of soft to hard gluons at LHC energy: R(2.76 TeV) ≈ 1.35 (± 0.15)
- Equilibration time at LHC: τ_eq ≈ 2.2 fm
- Equilibration temperature at LHC: T_eq ≈ 490 MeV
- Predicted multiplicity at √s = 5.5 TeV: dN_ch/dη ≈ 1910 (± 50), compared to saturation model value of 1390 ± 95
- 2/N_part dN_ch/dη ≈ 10.0 at LHC, using N_part = 381

## Context

This addendum extends the authors' earlier work Baier et al. (2001, 2002) on the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario, and directly addresses the discrepancy between saturation model predictions from Albacete (2010) and the ALICE measurements at LHC. The work enables a framework where final state multiplicities are not simply proportional to initial parton densities, but include entropy production from gluon thermalization, with the caveat that two O(1) parameters remain undetermined by the theory.
