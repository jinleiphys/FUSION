# Quantum Simulations of Hadron Dynamics in the Schwinger Model using 112 Qubits

> Hadron wavepackets are prepared and time evolved in the Schwinger model using 112 qubits of IBM's 133-qubit Heron quantum computer ibm_torino. The initialization of the hadron wavepacket is performed in two steps. First, the vacuum is prepared across the whole lattice using the recently developed SC-ADAPT-VQE algorithm and workflow. SC-ADAPT-VQE is then extended to the preparation of localized states, and used to establish a hadron wavepacket on top of the vacuum. This is done by adaptively constructing low-depth circuits that maximize the overlap with an adiabatically prepared hadron wavepacket. Due to the localized nature of the wavepacket, these circuits can be determined on a sequence of small lattices using classical computers, and then robustly scaled to prepare wavepackets on large lattices for simulations using quantum computers. Time evolution is implemented with a second-order Trotterization. To reduce both the required qubit connectivity and circuit depth, an approximate quasi-local interaction is introduced. This approximation is made possible by the emergence of confinement at long distances, and converges exponentially with increasing distance of the interactions. Using multiple error-mitigation strategies, up to 14 Trotter steps of time evolution are performed, employing 13,858 two-qubit gates (with a CNOT depth of 370). The propagation of hadrons is clearly identified, with results that compare favorably with Matrix Product State simulations. Prospects for a near-term quantum advantage in simulations of hadron scattering are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that hadron wavepackets can be prepared and time-evolved on a 112-qubit superconducting quantum processor with results that compare favorably to classical Matrix Product State simulations, but they hedge that this serves as a proof-of-concept rather than demonstrating quantum advantage, and the truncation of electric interactions is justified only because confinement in the Schwinger model causes exponential decay of correlations at long distances.

## Method

The authors first prepare the interacting vacuum across a 56-site (112-qubit) lattice using the SC-ADAPT-VQE algorithm, then extend this algorithm to prepare a localized hadron wavepacket by adaptively constructing low-depth circuits that maximize overlap with an adiabatically prepared wavepacket. Time evolution is implemented using second-order Trotterization with a quasi-local Hamiltonian where electric interactions between charges separated by more than one spatial site are truncated, exploiting the exponential screening of charges in the confining Schwinger model. The quantum circuits are executed on IBM's 133-qubit Heron quantum computer ibm_torino using 112 qubits, with error mitigation applied across up to 14 Trotter steps.

## Key numbers

- 112 qubits of IBM's 133-qubit Heron quantum computer ibm_torino were used for the simulations
- 13,858 two-qubit gates (CNOTs) were employed, with a CNOT depth of 370
- Up to 14 Trotter steps of time evolution were performed
- The Schwinger model parameters used were m=0.5, g=0.3, giving a hadron mass of m_hadron ≈ 1.1
- The interaction truncation range was set to λ̄=1 spatial sites, corresponding to a correlation length of ξ̄ ~ 0.5 spatial sites
- For vacuum preparation, 2-step SC-ADAPT-VQE used 7.8 CNOTs per qubit, and 3-step used 21 CNOTs per qubit

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous SC-ADAPT-VQE algorithm (Farrell 2023) from vacuum preparation on 100 qubits to both vacuum and hadron wavepacket preparation on 112 qubits, building on earlier quantum simulations of the Schwinger model that began with four-qubit trapped-ion experiments (Martinez 2016). The results enable future simulations of inelastic hadron scattering, fragmentation, and hadronization, with the authors suggesting these processes are strong candidates for near-term quantum advantage over classical methods.
