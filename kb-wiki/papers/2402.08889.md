# Indispensability of cross-shell contributions in neutron resonance spacing

> Spin and parity dependent nuclear level densities (NLDs) are obtained for configuration interaction shell model using a numerically efficient spectral distribution method. The calculations are performed for $^{24}$Na, $^{25,26,27}$Mg nuclei using full $sd$-$pf$ model space that incorporates the cross-shell excitations from $sd$ to $pf$-shell. The NLDs so obtained are then employed to determine the s-wave neutron resonance spacing (D$_0$) which is one of the crucial inputs for the predictions of astrophysical reaction rates. Though the considered nuclei are not neutron-rich, the contributions from cross-shell excitations to $pf$-shell are indispensable to explain the experimental data for D$_0$ which otherwise are significantly overestimated.

## Key claim
Cross-shell excitations from the sd-shell to the pf-shell are indispensable for reproducing experimental s-wave neutron resonance spacing (D0) in 24Na and 25,26,27Mg, even though these nuclei lie near the line of stability and the average pf-shell occupancy is only about 5% of valence nucleons; without these excitations, D0 values are significantly overestimated.

## Method
The authors used the spectral distribution method (SDM) within a configuration interaction shell model framework to compute spin- and parity-dependent nuclear level densities (NLDs). They employed the SDPF-MU effective interaction in a full sd-pf model space (14 single-particle orbitals) and obtained ground-state energies via diagonalization with the KSHELL code, allowing up to (0,4)ħω excitations to the pf-shell. The NLDs were then used to calculate s-wave neutron resonance spacing D0 from the level density at the neutron separation energy for the relevant target spin and parity.

## Key numbers
- D0 for 24Na: 91.1 keV (sd-pf model) vs. experimental 100±20 keV; sd-only model gave 149.7 keV
- D0 for 25Mg: 490.7 keV (sd-pf model) vs. experimental 480±70 keV; sd-only model gave 696.4 keV
- D0 for 26Mg: 54.2 keV (sd-pf model) vs. experimental 50±10 keV; sd-only model gave 78.9 keV
- D0 for 27Mg: 226.0 keV (sd-pf model) vs. experimental 210±80 keV; sd-only model gave 578.4 keV (over 100% off)
- Ground-state pf-shell occupancy sum for protons and neutrons in 24Na: approximately 0.5 (protons 0.130, neutrons 0.182 in f-orbits)
- Ground-state energy lowering from cross-shell excitations: 5-8 MeV across the studied nuclei

## Context
This work extends previous shell-model NLD studies performed only in the sd-shell by Karampagia (2018), which significantly overestimated D0 values. The authors demonstrate that including the pf-shell via cross-shell excitations, previously shown to be important for neutron-rich nuclei by Utsuno (2012) and for 28Mg by Williams (2019), is also essential for nuclei near stability. The results enable more reliable predictions of astrophysical neutron-capture reaction rates, and the authors provide predicted D0 values for 25Na (46.8 keV) and 26Na (45.6 keV) using the sd-pf model space.
