# Fermi Operator Expansion for the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov Theory

> A variety of phases in the inner crust of neutron stars are crucial for understanding the pulsar phenomena. However, the three-dimensional coordinate-space calculation of the phases is computationally demanding. We aim to generalize the Fermi operator expansion (FOE) method that is effective for finite-temperature coordinate-space simulation, from the Hartree-Fock theory to Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory including the pairing effects. Furthermore, the periodic structure with free neutrons in the inner crust requires us to treat the system with the band theory. We give a concise proof that the generalized density matrix in the HFB theory can be obtained with the FOE. The Chebyshev polynomial expansion is used for calculations of the HFB band theory. Using a model for a slab phase of the inner crust, the FOE method produces results in good agreement with those based on the diagonalization of the HFB Hamiltonian. The FOE method for the HFB band theory is a powerful tool for studying the nontrivial exotic structures in neutron stars. The FOE method is suitable for parallelization and further acceleration is possible with nearsightedness.

## Key claim

The authors prove that the generalized density matrix in Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory can be obtained directly from the Fermi-Dirac distribution function of the HFB Hamiltonian, enabling the Fermi operator expansion (FOE) method to be extended from Hartree-Fock to HFB theory including pairing effects, with the caveat that the method is demonstrated only for a one-dimensional slab phase model rather than full three-dimensional calculations.

## Method

The authors derive an identity showing that the generalized density matrix equals the Fermi-Dirac function of the HFB Hamiltonian, then apply Chebyshev polynomial expansion to compute this function without diagonalization. For the band theory extension, they introduce Bloch vectors and projectors onto periodic functions, then use a recursion relation for Chebyshev polynomials to compute the generalized density matrix elements independently for each lattice site and Bloch momentum. The method is tested on a one-dimensional slab phase model with a Woods-Saxon single-particle potential and zero-range pairing interaction, using self-consistent iteration to update the pair potential.

## Context

This work extends the FOE method previously applied to Hartree-Fock theory by Nakatsukasa (2022) to include pairing effects via HFB theory, which is necessary for describing Cooper pairs that influence the entrainment effect in neutron star inner crusts as shown by Watanabe (2017) and Minami (2022). The method enables three-dimensional coordinate-space simulations of pasta phases without the O(N³) cost of diagonalization, and is suitable for parallelization with potential O(N) complexity through nearsightedness, building on earlier work by Kashiwaba (2019, 2020) and Sekizawa (2022) on band theory calculations for neutron star crusts.
