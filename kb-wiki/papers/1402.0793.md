# Astrophysical weak-interaction rates for selected $A=20$ and $A=24$ nuclei

> We have evaluated the electron capture rates on $^{20}$Ne, $^{20}$F, $^{24}$Mg, $^{24}$Na and the $β$ decay rates for $^{20}$F and $^{24}$Na at temperature and density conditions relevant for the late-evolution stages of stars with $M=8$-12 M$_\odot$. The rates are based on recent experimental data and large-scale shell model calculations. We show that the electron capture rates on $^{20}$Ne, $^{24}$Mg and the $^{20}$F, $^{24}$Na $β$-decay rates are based on data in this astrophysical range, except for the capture rate on $^{20}$Ne, which we predict to have a dominating contribution from the second-forbidden transition between the $^{20}$Ne and $^{20}$F ground states in the density range $\log ρY_e (\mathrm{g~cm}^{-3}) = 9.3$-9.6. The dominance of a few individual transitions allows us to present the various rates by analytical expressions at the relevant astrophysical conditions. We also derive the screening corrections to the rates.

## Key claim

The electron capture rates on \(^{20}\)Ne, \(^{24}\)Mg and the \(\beta\)-decay rates for \(^{20}\)F and \(^{24}\)Na are based on experimental data across the astrophysically relevant temperature-density range, except for the capture rate on \(^{20}\)Ne, which is predicted to have a dominating contribution from the second-forbidden ground-state-to-ground-state transition in the density range \(\log \rho Y_e (\mathrm{g~cm}^{-3}) = 9.3\)–9.6, for which only an upper limit is known experimentally.

## Method

The authors evaluated electron capture and \(\beta\)-decay rates using the formalism of Fuller, Fowler, and Newman, combining recent experimental Gamow-Teller strength distributions from charge-exchange reactions with large-scale shell-model calculations. For the forbidden ground-state-to-ground-state transition in \(^{20}\)Ne, they used an upper limit from experiment. The rates were computed for temperatures \(T = 10^8\)–\(10^{10}\) K and densities \(\rho = 10^8\)–\(10^{10}\) g cm\(^{-3}\), and screening corrections were derived. Because the rates are dominated by a few individual transitions, the authors presented them as analytical expressions using relativistic Fermi integrals, avoiding the need for interpolation on a sparse grid.

## Key numbers

- Electron capture rates on \(^{20}\)Ne, \(^{20}\)F, \(^{24}\)Mg, \(^{24}\)Na and \(\beta\)-decay rates for \(^{20}\)F and \(^{24}\)Na were evaluated for stars of mass \(M = 8\)–12 M\(_\odot\).
- The second-forbidden ground-state-to-ground-state transition in \(^{20}\)Ne dominates the capture rate in the density range \(\log \rho Y_e (\mathrm{g~cm}^{-3}) = 9.3\)–9.6.
- The constant \(K\) for superallowed Fermi transitions was taken as \(K = 6144 \pm 2\) s.
- An effective axial coupling constant \(g_A^{\text{eff}} = 0.74 g_A\) was used for theoretical Gamow-Teller matrix elements, with \(g_A = -1.26\).
- For conditions \(\mu_e \ll -Q_{if}\), the average neutrino energy from electron capture is \(\langle E_\nu\rangle^{\text{ec}} \approx 3 kT\).
- For conditions \(\mu_e \gg -Q_{if}\), the average neutrino energy from electron capture is \(\langle E_\nu\rangle^{\text{ec}} \approx \frac{5}{6} \mu_e\).

## Context

This work extends the pioneering rate tabulations of Fuller, Fowler, and Newman (1980–1985) and the later shell-model calculations of Oda et al. (1994) and Takahara et al. (1989) for sd-shell nuclei, by incorporating recent experimental Gamow-Teller strength data for \(^{20}\)Ne and \(^{24}\)Mg from charge-exchange reactions. The improved rates are crucial for simulations of the late-stage evolution of 8–12 M\(_\odot\) stars and electron-capture supernovae, where electron captures on \(^{20}\)Ne and \(^{24}\)Mg trigger core collapse as described by Nomoto (1984, 1987) and Hillebrandt, Nomoto, and Wolff (1984). The analytical expressions provided enable more accurate interpolation than the sparse grid tabulations previously available, and the screening corrections derived here are a new addition for these specific nuclei.
