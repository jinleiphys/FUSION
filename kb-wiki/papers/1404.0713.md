# Timelike Compton scattering with a linearly polarized photon beam

> We study the timelike virtual Compton scattering process with a linearly polarized photon beam, in the generalized Bjorken scaling region and in the medium energy range which will be studied intensely at JLab12 experiments. We define new observables and show how they should help to access the polarized quark and gluon generalized parton distributions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that using a linearly polarized photon beam in timelike Compton scattering (TCS) allows access to the polarized quark and gluon generalized parton distributions (GPDs) \(\tilde H\) and \(\tilde E\) through newly defined observables, but this access is conditional on isolating the interference term between the TCS and Bethe-Heitler (BH) mechanisms, which dominates the cross section and requires specific angular projections of the lepton pair.

## Method

The authors calculated the differential cross section for \(\gamma p \to l^+ l^- p\) at leading twist and next-to-leading order in \(\alpha_S\), including contributions from TCS, BH, and their interference, for a linearly polarized photon beam. They defined two angular observables, \(\tilde{R}\) and \(\tilde{R}_3\), which project out the unpolarized and linearly polarized parts of the interference cross section, respectively, and combined them into a ratio \(C\) sensitive to the polarized GPD \(\tilde{\mathcal{H}}\). Numerical estimates were performed using the Goloskokov-Kroll GPD model, with the gluonic contribution \(\tilde{H}_g\) varied by factors of \(\{-1,0,1,2,3\}\) relative to the model.

## Key numbers

* The observable \(C\) shows significant sensitivity to \(\tilde{H}_g\) at \(Q^2 = 4\ \text{GeV}^2\) and \(t = t_0(\eta) = -4\eta^2 M^2/(1-\eta^2)\), with effects of the order of \(30\%\) visible when varying the gluonic contribution.
* For \(Q^2 = 4\ \text{GeV}^2\) and \(\eta = 0.1\), the ratio \(C\) as a function of \(t\) also shows variations of approximately \(30\%\) depending on the magnitude of \(\tilde{H}_g\).
* The BH contribution is parametrically enhanced by a factor of \(Q^2/(-t)\) compared to the TCS part of the differential cross section.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for TCS established by Berger, Diehl, and Pire (2001) to include linearly polarized photon beams, which are now experimentally available at JLab12 through forward tagging at CLAS12 and coherent bremsstrahlung at GlueX (Somov, 2011). The proposed observables provide a new tool to access polarized GPDs \(\tilde H\) and \(\tilde E\), which have proven difficult to extract from pseudoscalar meson deep leptoproduction. The results enable future experimental tests at JLab12 to constrain the gluon spin contribution to the nucleon, a longstanding open question in hadronic physics.
