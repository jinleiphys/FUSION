# Quantum fluctuations of baryon number density

> Quantum fluctuation expression of the baryon number for a subsystem consisting of hot relativistic spin-$\frac{1}{2}$ particles are derived. These fluctuations seems to diverge in the limit where system size goes to zero. For a broad range of thermodynamic parameters numerical solutions are obtained which might be helpful to interpret the heavy-ion experimental data.

## Key claim

Quantum fluctuations of the baryon number density for a subsystem of hot relativistic spin-1/2 particles are derived, and these fluctuations appear to diverge in the limit where the subsystem size goes to zero, though they agree with thermodynamic-limit results for sufficiently large subsystem sizes.

## Method

The author derives an expression for the variance of the baryon number density operator using a Gaussian smoothing profile of length scale \(a\) placed at the origin, with thermal expectation values evaluated via the Grand Canonical Ensemble characterized by temperature \(T\) and baryon chemical potential \(\mu\). The calculation uses the Dirac field operator for spin-1/2 particles, normal ordering to remove vacuum contributions, and the canonical anticommutation relations for creation and annihilation operators. Numerical integration is performed over a broad range of thermodynamic parameters to obtain results for any subsystem size.

## Key numbers

- Normalized fluctuation \(\sigma_n\) is computed for subsystem sizes \(a\) in the range shown in figures, with parameters: \(0.1\ \text{GeV} \leq T \leq 0.25\ \text{GeV}\), \(0.5\ \text{GeV} \leq \mu \leq 1.0\ \text{GeV}\), and \(0.0 \leq m \leq 1.0\ \text{GeV}\), with internal degeneracy factor \(g = 10\).
- The ratio \(V_a \sigma^2 / (T^3 \chi_2^{(B)})\) approaches 1 in the thermodynamic limit \(a \to \infty\), where \(V_a = a^3 (2\pi)^{3/2}\) is the effective volume of the Gaussian subsystem.
- The second-order baryon number susceptibility \(\chi_2^{(B)}\) is given by \(\chi_2^{(B)} = (2g/T^3) \int dK [f(\omega_k)(1-f(\omega_k)) + \bar{f}(\omega_k)(1-\bar{f}(\omega_k))]\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of quantum fluctuations of energy density in hot gases of bosons and fermions (Das 2020, Das 2021) to the case of baryon number density fluctuations for spin-1/2 particles. The results may be relevant for interpreting heavy-ion collision experimental data and for studying the QCD phase diagram and the search for the critical point, building on prior theoretical work by Berges (1998), Stephanov (1998, 1999, 2008), and others. The formalism connects to standard statistical physics results for susceptibilities in thermo-chemical equilibrium (Nahrgang 2014) and reproduces the thermodynamic limit for large subsystem sizes.
