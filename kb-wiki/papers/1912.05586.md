# Multi-gluon correlations and evidence of saturation from dijet measurements at an Electron Ion Collider

> We study inclusive and diffractive dijet production in electron-proton and electron-nucleus collisions within the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory. We compute dijet cross sections differentially in both mean dijet transverse momentum $\mathbf{P}$ and recoil momentum $\mathbfΔ$, as well as the anisotropy in the relative angle between $\mathbf{P}$ and $\mathbfΔ$. We use the nonlinear Gaussian approximation to compute multiparticle correlators for general small $x$ kinematics, employing running coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution to determine the dipole amplitude at small $x$. Our results cover a much larger kinematic range than accessible in previous computations performed in the correlation limit approximation, where it is assumed that $|\mathbf{P}| \gg |\mathbfΔ|$. We validate this approximation in its range of applicability and quantify its failure for $|\mathbf{P}| \lesssim |\mathbfΔ|$. We also predict significant target-dependent deviations from the correlation limit approximation for $|\mathbf{P}| > |\mathbfΔ|$ and $|\mathbf{P}| \lesssim Q_s$, which offers a straightforward test of gluon saturation and access to multi-gluon distributions at a future electron ion collider.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that inclusive and diffractive dijet production at a future Electron Ion Collider can directly probe gluon saturation and multi-gluon correlations, but only when measurements go beyond the standard correlation limit approximation (where mean dijet momentum \(|\mathbf{P}| \gg |\mathbf{\Delta}|\)) into the regime where \(|\mathbf{P}| \lesssim Q_s\) or \(|\mathbf{P}| \lesssim |\mathbf{\Delta}|\), where significant target-dependent deviations from the correlation limit appear.

## Method

The authors compute inclusive and incoherent diffractive dijet cross sections in electron-proton and electron-nucleus collisions within the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory at leading order in \(\alpha_s\), resumming all terms \(\sim \alpha_s \ln 1/x\). They employ the nonlinear Gaussian approximation to express all multiparticle correlators (quadrupole and dipole-dipole) as functions of the dipole correlator, which is obtained from running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution fitted to HERA data, with nuclear targets treated via the Optical Glauber model using a Woods-Saxon nuclear density distribution.

## Key numbers

- Photon virtuality \(Q^2 = 10\) GeV\(^2\) and center-of-mass energy of the photon-nucleon system \(W = 90\) GeV used for all presented results.
- For gold targets, nuclear radius \(R_A = 6.37\) fm and nuclear thickness \(a = 0.535\) fm; effective area \(S^{\mathrm{Au}}_\perp = \pi R_A^2\).
- For inclusive dijets on a gold target, significant deviations from the correlation limit appear for \(|\mathbf{P}| < 1.5\) GeV even when \(|\mathbf{\Delta}| < |\mathbf{P}|\), with much milder deviations for the proton target.
- Minimum elliptic anisotropy \(v_{2T} \sim -30\%\) for proton targets at \(|\mathbf{P}| \sim |\mathbf{\Delta}| \sim 1.8\) GeV, and \(v_{2T} \sim -20\%\) for gold at \(|\mathbf{P}| \sim |\mathbf{\Delta}| \sim 2.2\) GeV.
- For diffractive dijets, the fraction of diffractive events increases from proton to gold by a factor of 1.9 (transverse polarization) and 2.3 (longitudinal polarization) at \(|\mathbf{P}| \approx 1\) GeV and \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\), smaller than the naive expectation of 2.6 from the small dipole expansion.

## Context

This work extends previous computations of dijet production that were restricted to the correlation limit approximation (Dominguez 2011, Mueller 2013) by covering a much larger kinematic range including \(|\mathbf{P}| \lesssim |\mathbf{\Delta}|\) and \(|\mathbf{P}| \lesssim Q_s\). It provides the first calculation of incoherent diffractive dijet cross sections and their elliptic anisotropies within the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory, building on earlier work on coherent diffractive dijets (Mantysaari 2019, Salazar 2019). The results enable experimental tests of gluon saturation at a future Electron Ion Collider through differential measurements in \(\mathbf{P}\) and \(\mathbf{\Delta}\) that probe genuine multi-gluon correlations beyond the improved transverse momentum dependent framework (Altinoluk 2019).
