# The effective cross section for double parton scattering within a holographic AdS/QCD approach

> A first attempt to apply the AdS/QCD framework for a bottom-up approach to the evaluation of the effective cross section for double parton scattering in proton-proton collisions is presented. The main goal is the analytic evaluation of the dependence of the effective cross section on the longitudinal momenta of the involved partons, obtained within the holographic Soft-Wall model. If measured in high-energy processes at hadron colliders, this momentum dependence could open a new window on 2-parton correlations in a proton.

## Key claim

The paper presents a first application of the AdS/QCD holographic Soft-Wall model to compute the effective cross section for double parton scattering (DPS), predicting an analytic dependence on the longitudinal momentum fractions \(x_1, x_2\) of the two partons. The authors hedge that this \(x_i\)-dependence is a "largely model independent feature" that could be observed experimentally if measured with sufficient precision, but they note that current experimental data do not show it clearly due to large error bars.

## Method

The authors use the holographic Soft-Wall model of AdS/QCD, which maps string modes in a five-dimensional anti-de Sitter space to light-front wave functions in four-dimensional QCD. They extract generalized parton distributions (GPDs) at zero skewness from this framework, then approximate double parton distribution functions (dPDFs) as a product of two GPDs depending on the transverse momentum \(k_\perp\). The effective cross section \(\sigma_{eff}\) is evaluated analytically by integrating the squared dPDF over \(k_\perp\), restricting the analysis to valence \(u\) quarks and the zero-rapidity region where \(x_i = x'_i\).

## Key numbers

* The parameter \(\alpha = 0.41\) GeV is fixed from the Soft-Wall model dilaton definition.
* For \(x_1 = x_2 = 0.4\), the effective cross section is \(\sigma_{eff} \simeq 26.6\) mbarn, obtained from the analytic formula \(\sigma_{eff} \simeq \frac{2\pi}{\alpha^2} [\ln(1/x_1) + \ln(1/x_2)]\).
* The normalized effective cross section shows a suppression of 20–30% at \(x_1 = 0.01\) (depending on the fixed \(x_2\) value) and around 50% at \(x_1 = 0.1\), relative to the reference point \(x_1 = x_2 = 10^{-3}\).
* The correction term involving the anomalous magnetic moment \(\kappa_u \approx 1.673\) produces a very small modification to the leading result, shown for \(x_2 = 0.2\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous light-front (LF) calculation of \(\sigma_{eff}\) (Traini et al., 2016) by using a completely different non-perturbative approach, AdS/QCD holography, to confirm the predicted \(x_i\)-dependence of the effective cross section. The result is consistent with the earlier LF analysis and with experimental extractions (e.g., from LHC collaborations) in the valence region, but the paper emphasizes that the \(x_i\)-dependence is not yet evident in data due to large error bars. This dependence, if measured, would provide a new window on two-parton correlations in the proton and on the three-dimensional structure of the nucleon, building on the factorization framework proposed by Burkardt (2004) and others.
