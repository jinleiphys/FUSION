# The hadronization time of heavy quark in nuclear matter

> We study the hadronization time of heavy quark in nuclear matter by using the coalescence model and the spatial diffusion constant of heavy quark from lattice Quantum Chromodynamic calculations, assuming that the main interaction of heavy quark at the critical temperature is hadronization. It is found that the hadronization time of heavy quark is about 3 fm/c for $2πT_c D_s=6$, if a heavy quark is combined with the nearest light antiquark in coordinate space without any correlation between momentum of heavy quark and that of light antiquark which form a heavy meson. However, the hadronization time reduces to 0.6-1.2 fm/c for charm and 0.4-0.9 fm/c for bottom, depending on heavy meson radius, in the presence of momentum correlation. Considering the interspace between quarks and antiquarks at the critical temperature, it seems that the hadronization of heavy quark does not happen instantaneously but gradually for a considerable time, if started from the thermal distribution of quarks and antiquarks.

# The hadronization time of heavy quark in nuclear matter

## Key claim

The hadronization time of heavy quarks in nuclear matter is not instantaneous but gradual, lasting 0.6–1.2 fm/c for charm and 0.4–0.9 fm/c for bottom when momentum correlation between the heavy quark and its coalescence partner is present, assuming a heavy meson radius of 0.5–1.0 fm. However, without any momentum correlation (vanishing meson radius), the hadronization time increases to about 3 fm/c for the diffusion constant value $2\pi T_c D_s = 6$.

## Method

The authors combine the spatial diffusion constant of heavy quarks from lattice QCD calculations (Banerjee 2011) and the Dynamical Quasi-Particle Model (Berrehrah 2014) with a coalescence model using a simple harmonic oscillator wavefunction to compute the momentum transfer to a heavy quark when it absorbs a light antiquark. The hadronization time is then obtained from the relation $t = D_s \langle (\Delta k)^2 \rangle / (6 T_c^2)$, where $\langle (\Delta k)^2 \rangle$ is the average squared momentum transfer computed from the coalescence probability weighted by Fermi-Dirac distributions at the critical temperature $T_c = 160$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- Hadronization time for charm quark: 0.6–1.2 fm/c, assuming heavy meson radius 0.5–1.0 fm and $2\pi T_c D_s = 6$
- Hadronization time for bottom quark: 0.4–0.9 fm/c, assuming heavy meson radius 0.5–1.0 fm and $2\pi T_c D_s = 6$
- Hadronization time of about 3 fm/c for vanishing heavy meson radius (no momentum correlation) with $2\pi T_c D_s = 6$
- Heavy quark masses used: 1.5 GeV for charm, 4.5 GeV for bottom
- Light antiquark mass: 0.3 GeV; critical temperature $T_c = 160$ MeV
- Number density of quarks and antiquarks at critical temperature: about 1 fm$^{-3}$, with interspace of about 1 fm

## Context

This work extends the study of heavy quark interactions in nuclear matter by connecting the spatial diffusion constant from lattice QCD (Banerjee 2011) and the Dynamical Quasi-Particle Model (Berrehrah 2014) with the coalescence model (Greco 2003) to determine the hadronization time scale. The results support the interpretation that the minimum of the spatial diffusion constant near the critical temperature arises from hadronization processes. The approach enables future studies of heavy flavor hadronization dynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, with the authors noting that their estimate is a lower limit because elastic scattering contributions near the critical temperature are neglected.
