# Alternative Interpretation of Sharply Rising E0 Strengths in Transitional Regions

> It is shown that strong 0+2 -> 0+1 E0 transitions provide a clear signature of phase transitional behavior in finite nuclei. Calculations using the IBA show that these transition strengths exhibit a dramatic and robust increase in spherical-deformed shape transition regions, that this rise matches well the existing data, that the predictions of these E0 transitions remain large in deformed nuclei, and that these properties are intrinsic to the way that collectivity and deformation develop through the phase transitional region in the model, arising from the specific d-boson coherence in the wave functions, and that they do not necessarily require the explicit mixing of normal and intruder configurations from different IBA spaces.

## Key claim

Strong 0⁺₂ → 0⁺₁ E0 transitions in transitional nuclei do not necessarily require explicit mixing of normal and intruder configurations from different IBA spaces, but can arise naturally from the specific d-boson coherence in wave functions within a single IBA-1 space as deformation develops through a phase transition.

## Method

The authors used the IBA-1 model with a simple two-term Hamiltonian spanning the full symmetry triangle, varying the control parameter ζ from 0 (U(5) spherical limit) to 1 (SU(3) deformed limit) for boson numbers N = 4, 10, and 16. They calculated ρ²(E0;0⁺₂ → 0⁺₁) values across the entire parameter space and decomposed the E0 strengths into contributions from individual n_d components in the wave functions to identify the role of d-boson coherence.

## Key numbers

- The spherical-deformed first order phase transition occurs at ζ = 0.5 for large N and at ζ ∼ 0.54 for typical boson numbers (N ∼ 10).
- The E0 operator parameter β' was fixed at 6 × 10⁻³/eR₀² for the calculations compared to data.
- In deformed rare earth nuclei, known ρ² values are very small (∼2 × 10⁻³) in ¹⁶⁶Er and ¹⁷²Yb, while ¹⁷⁰Yb shows a strong E0 transition from the 0⁺₃ state with ρ² = 27(5) × 10⁻³.
- In the Duval-Barrett calculations for Mo isotopes, mixing between Nπ=1 and Nπ=3 spaces was less than 10% for N = 54 and less than 5% for N = 60, 62, with substantial mixing only for N = 56, 58.

## Context

This work extends earlier IBA studies of E0 transitions by Scholten et al. (early work on Sm isotopes) and Estep et al. (calculations for Mo isotopes using shape coexistence formalism), but provides a more general, parameter-free analysis spanning the full IBA symmetry triangle. The paper contradicts the common interpretation that large E0 strengths necessarily indicate mixing of coexisting spherical and intruder configurations, showing instead that the single-space IBA-1 model reproduces the sharp rise in ρ²(E0) values in transitional regions. This enables future experimental tests in well-deformed nuclei where predicted large E0 strengths have not yet been systematically measured.
