# N-jettiness beam functions at N3LO

> We present the first complete calculation for the quark and gluon $N$-jettiness ($\Tau_N$) beam functions at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO) in perturbative QCD. Our calculation is based on an expansion of the differential Higgs boson and Drell-Yan production cross sections about their collinear limit. This method allows us to employ cutting edge techniques for the computation of cross sections to extract the universal building blocks in question. The class of functions appearing in the matching coefficents for all channels includes iterated integrals with non-rational kernels, thus going beyond the one of harmonic polylogarithms. Our results are a key step in extending the $\Tau_N$ subtraction methods to N$^3$LO, and to resum $\Tau_N$ distributions at N$^3$LL$^\prime$ accuracy both for quark as well as for gluon initiated processes.

## Key claim

The authors present the first complete calculation of the quark and gluon N-jettiness beam function matching kernels at N3LO in perturbative QCD, but the results are limited to the strict collinear limit and require further renormalization and infrared subtraction to obtain the physical beam functions.

## Method

The calculation expands the differential Drell-Yan and Higgs boson production cross sections about their collinear limit, using reverse unitarity, integration-by-part identities, and the method of differential equations to compute the required master integrals. The authors extract the universal beam function matching kernels from the strict n-collinear limit of these cross sections, then perform UV renormalization and infrared subtraction in Fourier (y) space to obtain the renormalized matching kernels.

## Key numbers

- The N3LO beam function corrections are approximately 1-2% of the NNLO result at canonical scale μ = √t_cut = 30 GeV, with sizable z-dependence
- The cumulative beam functions are evaluated at t_cut = (10 GeV)^2 and μ = 100 GeV for u-quark and gluon channels
- The power series expansions around z=0 and z=1 are provided up to 50 terms for numerical evaluation
- The matching kernels are expressed in terms of iterated integrals with an alphabet containing 6 letters, including a square root letter 1/(√(4-z)√z)

## Context

This work extends the NNLO beam function calculations of Stewart (2010), Berger (2010), Gaunt (2014), and Gaunt (2014) to N3LO, and builds on partial N3LO results for the quark case by Melnikov (2018), Melnikov (2019), and Behring (2019). The results provide a critical ingredient for extending the N-jettiness subtraction method to N3LO as proposed by Cieri (2018) and Billis (2019), and enable N3LL' resummation of N-jettiness distributions for both quark and gluon initiated processes.
