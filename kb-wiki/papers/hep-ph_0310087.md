# Vector Mesons and Dence Skyrmion Matter

> In our continuing effort to understand hadronic matter at high density, we have developed a unified field theoretic formalism for dense skyrmion matter using a single Lagrangian to describe simultaneously both matter and meson fluctuations and studied in-medium properties of hadrons. Dropping the quartic Skyrme term, we incorporate into our previous Lagrangian the vector mesons rho and omega in a form which is consistent with the symmetries of QCD. The results that we have obtained, reported here, expose a hitherto unsuspected puzzle associated with the role the omega meson plays at short distance. Since the omega meson couples to baryon density, it leads to a pseudo-gap scenario for the chiral symmetry phase transition, which is at variance with standard scenario of QCD at the phase transition. We find that in the presence of the omega mesons, the scale-anomaly dilaton field is prevented from developing a vanishing vacuum expectation value at the chiral restoration, as a consequence of which the in-medium pion decay constant does not vanish. This seems to indicate that the omega degree of freedom obstructs the vector manifestation which is considered to be a generic feature of effective field theories matched to QCD.

## Key claim

The authors find that incorporating the omega meson into their dense skyrmion matter model prevents the dilaton field from developing a vanishing vacuum expectation value at chiral restoration, leading to a non-vanishing in-medium pion decay constant; this produces a pseudo-gap scenario for the chiral phase transition that is at variance with the standard vector manifestation scenario expected from effective field theories matched to QCD.

## Method

The authors construct a Lagrangian for dense skyrmion matter that includes pion, rho, omega, and dilaton fields, dropping the quartic Skyrme term and incorporating vector mesons via hidden local gauge symmetry consistent with QCD symmetries. They study the ground state of infinite matter as an FCC skyrmion crystal, using a Fourier series expansion method generalized from Kugler and Shtrikman (1989) to treat all fields, and solve the omega meson equation of motion self-consistently rather than including its expansion coefficients in the variational minimization.

## Key numbers

- Single skyrmion mass in the full pi-rho-omega-chi model: 1408.3 MeV, with a mean square radius of 0.51 fm^2
- In the pi-rho-chi model (without omega), single skyrmion mass: 906.5 MeV, with mean square radius 0.19 fm^2
- The omega meson increases the single skyrmion mass by more than 415 MeV and the mean square radius by more than 0.28 fm^2 when comparing pi-rho and pi-rho-omega models
- Normal nuclear matter density rho_0 = 0.17/fm^3 corresponds to FCC lattice parameter L ~ 1.43
- In models with the omega, at high density the interaction reduces E/B to 85% of the B=1 skyrmion mass; in the pi-rho-chi model E/B goes down to 74% of the B=1 skyrmion mass

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of dense skyrmion matter (Park et al., 2000-2003) by incorporating vector mesons rho and omega into a Lagrangian that previously included only pions and the dilaton field. The results contradict the vector manifestation scenario of Harada and Yamawaki (2001) and the BR scaling predictions of Brown and Rho (1991), which require the pion decay constant to vanish at chiral restoration; instead, the omega meson forces a pseudo-gap scenario. The findings enable further investigation of whether the omega degree of freedom must be modified or integrated out to restore consistency with QCD at the chiral phase transition.
