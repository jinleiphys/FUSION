# Description of He isotopes using the particle-particle random-phase approximation model

> The two-neutron RPA model has been used to describe helium isotopes with N (the neutron number) = 4, 6, 7, 8 in their ground and excited states. The properties of all these isotopes are given by a single system of equations so that their properties are interdependent. Since the ground states of $^{9}$He and $^{10}$He are still not well established, we have looked how the properties of $^{9}$He induce the properties of the other isotopes, energies and wave functions. Our results suggest an inversion of $2s$-$1p_{1/2}$ shells in $^{9}$He. The corresponding ground states of $^{9}$He and $^{10}$He are slightly unbound and respectively $1/2^+$ and $0^+$ states while the $1/2^-$ and $0^+$ seen in experiments appear to be excited states. With this assumption on $^{9}$He, we get not only a nice picture of $^{10}$He but also a very good two-neutron separation energy in $^{8}$He.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that the ground states of $^{9}$He and $^{10}$He are slightly unbound and have spins $1/2^+$ and $0^+$ respectively, implying an inversion of the $2s$ and $1p_{1/2}$ shells in $^{9}$He, while the $1/2^-$ and $0^+$ states observed in experiments are actually excited states.

## Method

The authors used a two-neutron particle-particle random-phase approximation (RPA) model with an inert $^{8}$He core, solving a single system of equations to simultaneously describe $^{6}$He, $^{8}$He, $^{9}$He, and $^{10}$He. Single neutron energies in the field of the $^{8}$He core were obtained from a Saxon-Woods potential with a surface term simulating neutron-phonon couplings, using the Gogny DIS effective interaction for the neutron-neutron force, with unbound states discretized in a 16 fm box.

## Key numbers

* Two-neutron separation energy $S_{2n}(^{8}\text{He})$ = 2.29 MeV for $\epsilon(1p_{1/2})=0.92$ MeV and $\epsilon(2s)=0.29$ MeV, compared to the experimental value of 2.14 MeV (Audi 2003).
* Two-neutron separation energy $S_{2n}(^{10}\text{He})$ = -0.07 MeV for $\epsilon(1p_{1/2})=0.92$ MeV and $\epsilon(2s)=0.29$ MeV, indicating an unbound ground state.
* Energy of the first excited $0^+$ state in $^{10}\text{He}$, $E(0^+_2)$ = 1.4 MeV for $\epsilon(1p_{1/2})=0.92$ MeV and $\epsilon(2s)=0.29$ MeV, close to the experimental value of 1.2 MeV.
* The $1/2^+$ ground state of $^{9}\text{He}$ is predicted at an energy of 0.2-0.3 MeV above the $n+^{8}\text{He}$ threshold.

## Context

This work extends the two-neutron RPA approach previously applied to $^{11}$Li and $^{14}$Be (Vinh Mau 1996, Labiche 1999, Blanchon 2010) to the helium isotope chain, where the ground states of $^{9}$He and $^{10}$He remain disputed. The results support the suggestion by Chen (2001) of a $1/2^+$ ground state in $^{9}$He and the proposal by Aoyama (2002, 2003) that the $0^+$ resonance in $^{10}$He is an excited state rather than the ground state. The predicted $2s$-$1p_{1/2}$ shell inversion in $^{9}$He parallels similar inversions known in $^{11}$Be, $^{10}$Li, and $^{13}$Be.
