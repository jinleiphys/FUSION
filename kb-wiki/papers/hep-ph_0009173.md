# Bethe-Salpeter equation for diquarks in color-flavor locked phase of cold dense QCD

> The Bethe-Salpeter equations for diquarks with the quantum numbers of the Nambu-Goldstone bosons are analyzed in the color-flavor locked phase of cold dense QCD with three quark flavors. The decay constants and the velocities of the Nambu-Goldstone bosons are calculated in the Pagels-Stokar approximation. It is also shown that, in contrast to the case of dense QCD with two flavors, there are no massive radial excitations with quantum numbers of the Nambu-Goldstone bosons in the color-flavor locked phase. The role of the Meissner effect in the pairing dynamics of diquarks is explained.

## Key claim

The Bethe-Salpeter equations for diquarks in the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase of cold dense QCD with three flavors are solved in the hard dense loop improved ladder approximation, showing that the Nambu-Goldstone bosons appear as massless bound states and that, unlike the two-flavor case, there are no massive radial excitations with the quantum numbers of the Nambu-Goldstone bosons in the CFL phase.

## Method

The authors formulate the Bethe-Salpeter equation in matrix form using Majorana spinors that are singlets and octets under the color-flavor locked symmetry group, working in the hard dense loop improved ladder (rainbow) approximation. They derive the Ward identities for the quark-gluon vertex functions corresponding to broken generators of global and gauge symmetries, and use these identities to determine the structure of the Bethe-Salpeter wave functions for the Nambu-Goldstone bosons in the limit of zero momentum transfer. The decay constants and velocities of the Nambu-Goldstone bosons are then calculated in the Pagels-Stokar approximation.

- The gap in the singlet channel is approximately twice the gap in the octet channel: Δ₁⁻(p₄) ≈ 2Δ₂⁻(p₄) ≈ 2Δ_(3̄,3̄)⁻(p₄)
- The asymptotic gap parameter is Δ₀ ≈ [16(2π)^(3/2) μ] / [(3α_s)^(5/2)] × exp(−3π^(3/2) / [2^(3/2) √α_s])
- The gap function for momenta p₄ ≥ Δ₀ behaves as Δ_(3̄,3̄)⁻(p₄) ≈ Δ₀ sin(√(2α_s/(9π)) ln(Λ/p₄)) with Λ ≡ 16(2π)^(3/2) μ / (3α_s)^(5/2)

## Context

This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter analysis previously applied to two-flavor dense QCD by Miransky, Shovkovy, and Wijewardhana (2000) to the three-flavor color-flavor locked phase. The results confirm the low-energy effective theory predictions for the CFL phase formulated by Casalbuoni and Gatto (1999), Son and Stephanov (2000), and others, while providing a microscopic derivation of the Nambu-Goldstone boson properties. The finding that no massive radial excitations exist in the Nambu-Goldstone boson channel for three flavors, in contrast to the two-flavor case, constrains the possible spectrum of diquark bound states in dense quark matter relevant for neutron star interiors.
