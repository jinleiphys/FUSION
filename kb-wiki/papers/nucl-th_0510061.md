# Effects of mode-mode and isospin-isospin correlations on domain formation of disoriented chiral condensates

> The effects of mode-mode and isospin-isospin correlations on nonequilibrium chiral dynamics are investigated by using the method of the time dependent variational approach with squeezed states as trial states. Our numerical simulations show that large domains of the disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) are formed due to the combined effect of the mode-mode and isospin-isospin correlations. Moreover, it is found that, when the mode-mode correlation is included, the DCC domain formation is accompanied by the amplification of the quantum fluctuation, which implies the squeezing of the state. However, neither the DCC domain formation nor the amplification of the quantum fluctuation is observed if only the isospin-isospin correlation is included. This suggests that the mode-mode coupling plays a key role in the DCC domain formation.

## Key claim

Large domains of disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) are formed due to the combined effect of mode-mode and isospin-isospin correlations, but the mode-mode coupling plays the key role: neither DCC domain formation nor amplification of quantum fluctuation is observed if only the isospin-isospin correlation is included.

## Method

The authors applied the time-dependent variational approach (TDVA) with squeezed states as trial states to the O(4) linear sigma model, solving the coupled equations of motion for the mean fields and the two-point correlation functions in momentum space. They performed four numerical simulations on a 1+1 dimensional lattice (with translational invariance in the x and y directions) to separately isolate the effects of mode-mode and isospin-isospin correlations, starting from a quench initial condition with random mean fields and coherent-state quantum fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- Lattice parameters: total length in z direction L = 64 fm, lattice spacing d = 1.0 fm, corresponding to three-dimensional momentum cutoff Λ = 1071 MeV.
- Model parameters: pion mass Mπ = 138 MeV, sigma meson mass Mσ = 500 MeV, pion decay constant fπ = 93 MeV, with λ = 3.44, v = 110 MeV, and h = (103 MeV)^3.
- Initial Gaussian width for mean fields: δ = 0.19 v.
- Initial degenerate mass for coherent states: m0 = 200 MeV.
- Total energy conserved to an accuracy of less than 1 percent throughout the time evolution.
- In case i(a) (both correlations included), the off-diagonal components of the two-point function in isospin space evolve up to about one fourth of the diagonal components by t ∼ 25 fm.
- The sigma field oscillation almost disappears by t = 25 fm in case i(a), while the correlation length of the pion field continues to grow until about t = 40 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous 1+1 dimensional quantum calculation (Ikezi, Asakawa, Tsue, 2004) that first showed DCC domain formation with mode-mode correlations included, and contrasts with classical simulations by Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993) that found amplification of low momentum modes lasting beyond the rolling-down time scale. The results show that mode-mode coupling is a distinct mechanism for DCC formation beyond the parametric resonance scenario (which is essentially a one-mode problem within mean field theory), and that the isospin-isospin correlation provides an additional but secondary enhancement of domain size.
