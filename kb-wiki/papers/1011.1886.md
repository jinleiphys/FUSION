# Non-perturbative predictions for cold atom Bose gases with tunable interactions

> We derive a theoretical description for dilute Bose gases as a loop expansion in terms of composite-field propagators by rewriting the Lagrangian in terms of auxiliary fields related to the normal and anomalous densities. We demonstrate that already in leading order this non-perturbative approach describes a large interval of coupling-constant values, satisfies Goldstone's theorem, yields a Bose-Einstein transition that is second-order, and is consistent with the critical temperature predicted in the weak-coupling limit by the next-to-leading order large-N expansion.

## Key claim

The authors derive a non-perturbative theoretical framework for dilute Bose gases that, already at leading order in a composite-field loop expansion, satisfies Goldstone's theorem, yields a second-order Bose-Einstein condensation transition, and reproduces the critical temperature shift predicted by the next-to-leading order large-N expansion in the weak-coupling limit, but the method is designed to also describe medium-to-strongly interacting regimes where the dimensionless parameter ρ^{1/3}a_0 is not small.

## Method

The authors rewrite the Lagrangian for a dilute Bose gas using a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce auxiliary fields χ(x) and A(x) related to the normal and anomalous densities, with a mixing parameter θ chosen so that the leading-order dispersion reduces to the Bogoliubov spectrum. They then perform a stationary-phase (loop) expansion in the auxiliary-field propagators, keeping only the leading-order term (LOAF), and derive the effective potential and gap equations in the imaginary-time formalism, renormalizing using standard effective-field theory techniques.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant is λ = 4π ħ² a₀ / m, where a₀ is the s-wave scattering length.
- At zero temperature and weak coupling, the chemical potential is μ = 8π ρ a₀ [1 + (32/3) √(ρ a₀³ / π)].
- For ρ^{1/3}a₀ ≫ 1, LOAF predicts that the relative critical temperature shift ΔT_c/T₀ approaches 0.396 in the unitarity limit.
- The non-interacting critical temperature is T₀ = (2π ħ² / m) [ρ / ζ(3/2)]^{2/3}, where ζ(x) is the Riemann zeta function.

## Context

This work extends the large-N expansion approach of Baym et al. (2000) and the functional renormalization techniques of Floerchinger et al. (2008) by treating normal and anomalous densities on equal footing, unlike the large-N expansion which sets the mixing parameter θ = 0 and yields a non-interacting dispersion. The LOAF framework improves upon the Popov bosonic approximation (Popov, 1987), which produces an artificial first-order phase transition at T_c, by predicting a second-order transition and a finite temperature range T_c < T < T* where the anomalous density remains nonzero despite a vanishing condensate fraction. The method enables consistent calculation of all experimentally relevant quantities including T_c, collective mode frequencies, and compressibility, and can be systematically improved via the two-particle irreducible (2-PI) formalism and bare-vertex approximation.
