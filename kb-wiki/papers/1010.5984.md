# Dileptons and Photons at RHIC

> Electromagnetic emission in the form of photons or dileptons provide important information on the onset and evolution of a heavy ion collision at ultrarelativistic energies. We briefly summarize the theoretical assessments of the thermalized electromagnetic emissivities from a hot partonic and hadronic medium, and compare them to current experiments at collider energies.

## Key claim

Theoretical calculations of thermal photon and dilepton emissivities from a hot partonic and hadronic medium are broadly consistent with current experimental data at RHIC and SPS energies, except for the dilepton spectrum in the intermediate mass region reported by PHENIX, where experiment exceeds the best calculations by almost a factor of 1.5.

## Method

The author computes hadronic photon rates using chiral reduction formulae that express the thermal electromagnetic current-current correlation function in terms of vacuum correlators constrained by electroproduction data, e+ e- annihilation, and tau-decay data. These hadronic rates are compared to leading-order quark-gluon plasma (QGP) rates, then both are convoluted with a hydrodynamical evolution of the fireball from initial time (0.5-1 fm/c) to thermal freezeout (10-15 fm/c) for SPS, RHIC, and LHC collision parameters. The evolved rates are folded with detector acceptance and compared to experimental data from WA98 (SPS) and PHENIX (RHIC).

## Key numbers

- Hadronic photon rates are consistently higher than leading-order QGP rates at the highest hadronic temperature T = 190 MeV and coupling alpha_s = 3/4 across a broad range of photon energies
- Initial hydrodynamical temperatures at RHIC: T(r_perp=0, tau_0) = 336 MeV (RHIC1) and 398 MeV (RHIC2) at tau_0 = 1 fm/c and 0.5 fm/c respectively
- Initial hydrodynamical temperature at SPS: T(r_perp=0, tau_0) = 245 MeV at tau_0 = 1 fm/c
- Initial hydrodynamical temperature at LHC: T(r_perp=0, tau_0) = 501 MeV at tau_0 = 0.5 fm/c
- Freezeout temperatures: 120 MeV (SPS), 140-160 MeV (RHIC), 140 MeV (LHC)
- Number of participants: 340 (SPS, b=3 fm), 269 (RHIC, b=4.5 fm), 293 (LHC, b=4.8 fm)
- The dilepton spectrum discrepancy at PHENIX in the intermediate mass range: experiment exceeds best calculations by almost a factor of 1.5
- Primordial QGP temperatures inferred: approximately 350 MeV at RHIC and perhaps 500 MeV at LHC

## Context

This work extends the chiral reduction approach of Steele (1996) for hadronic photon rates and compares with leading-order QGP rates from AMMY. The analysis uses hydrodynamical evolution parameters similar to Dusling and Zahed (2009) and addresses the persistent discrepancy between PHENIX dilepton data and theoretical calculations in the intermediate mass region, while noting that PHENIX's extrapolated photon measurements are consistent with current calculations. The results enable future comparisons with LHC data, where QGP-dominated photon emissivities are expected between 1-2.5 GeV.
