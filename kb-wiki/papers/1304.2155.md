# Stellar electron-capture rates in pf-shell nuclei from quasiparticle random-phase approximation calculations

> Electron-capture rates at different density and temperature conditions are evaluated for a set of pf-shell nuclei representative of the constituents in presupernova formations. The nuclear structure part of the problem is described within a quasiparticle random-phase approximation based on a deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock selfconsistent mean field with pairing correlations and residual interactions in particle-hole and particle-particle channels. The energy distributions of the Gamow-Teller strength are evaluated and compared to benchmark shell-model calculations and experimental data extracted from charge-exchange reactions. The model dependence of the weak rates are discussed and the various sensitivities to both density and temperature are analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that QRPA calculations based on a self-consistent deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock mean field with pairing and residual interactions can reproduce experimental Gamow-Teller strength distributions and electron-capture rates in pf-shell nuclei with quality comparable to large-scale shell-model calculations, contrary to earlier claims that QRPA systematically underperforms shell-model approaches.

## Method

The authors performed deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations with SLy4 and SG2 interactions, including BCS pairing correlations, to generate single-particle wave functions and occupation probabilities for pf-shell nuclei. They then applied a proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation with separable residual interactions in both particle-hole and particle-particle channels to compute Gamow-Teller strength distributions, using coupling strengths χ_GT^ph = 0.10 MeV and κ_GT^pp = 0.05 MeV for all nuclei. Electron-capture rates were calculated as functions of density and temperature by combining these nuclear structure inputs with phase space factors that account for continuum electron capture from a degenerate Fermi-Dirac electron plasma.

## Key numbers

- Effective axial-vector coupling ratio used: (g_A/g_V)_eff = 0.7 (g_A/g_V)_bare, applied as a quenching factor
- Temperature range considered: T = 1-10 GK (T_9 = 1-10, with k_BT = 0.862 MeV at T_9 = 10)
- Density conditions studied: ρY_e = 10^6, 10^7, 10^8, 10^9, 10^10 mol/cm^3
- Lowest excited state in even-even nuclei: 2^+ state in ^56Fe at E = 0.847 MeV, used to justify neglecting thermal population at these temperatures
- Coupling strengths fixed for all nuclei: χ_GT^ph = 0.10 MeV, κ_GT^pp = 0.05 MeV
- D = 6146 s in the decay rate formula λ_i = (ln 2 / D) Σ_f B_if Φ_if(ρ,T)

## Context

This work extends the systematic comparison of theoretical models for Gamow-Teller strength distributions initiated by Cole et al. (2012), which found that QRPA calculations using the formalism of Krumlinde and Moeller (1984) with finite-range droplet model deformations produced systematically larger deviations from experimental data than shell-model calculations. The present results show that alternative QRPA implementations based on self-consistent Skyrme mean fields can achieve comparable quality to shell-model calculations using KB3G (Poves, 2001) and GXPF1a (Honma, 2002) interactions, and that the earlier negative assessment of QRPA does not apply to all QRPA-type calculations. The method enables extension to heavier nuclei beyond the reach of full shell-model diagonalizations, which is relevant for presupernova and supernova modeling.
