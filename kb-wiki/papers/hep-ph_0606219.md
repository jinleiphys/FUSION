# Meson loop effect on high density chiral phase transition

> We test the stability of the mean-field solution in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. For stable solutions with respect to both the σand πdirections, we investigate effects of the mesonic loop corrections of 1/N_c, which correspond to the next-to-leading order in the 1/N_c expansion, on the high density chiral phase transition. The corrections weaken the first order phase transition and shift the critical chemical potential to a lower value. At N_c=3, however, instability of the mean field effective potential prevents us from determining the minimum of the corrected one.

## Key claim

The mesonic loop corrections (next-to-leading order in the 1/N_c expansion) weaken the first-order chiral phase transition and shift the critical chemical potential to a lower value, but at N_c=3 the instability of the mean-field effective potential prevents determining the minimum of the corrected potential, so the analysis is performed semi-quantitatively at N_c=20.

## Method

The authors use the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with two quark flavors and employ the auxiliary field method to perform a 1/N_c expansion of the effective potential. They compute the mean-field (leading order) effective potential and the next-to-leading order correction from meson loop diagrams (sigma and pion contributions) in the zero-temperature limit, then evaluate the stability of the mean-field solution with respect to both the sigma and pion directions before including the corrections.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass at the mean-field minimum for mu = 330 MeV is approximately M = 340 MeV (chirally broken phase)
- First-order phase transition point in mean-field approximation occurs at mu = 348 MeV, where two minima degenerate
- Parameter set used: m_0 = 5.5 MeV, G = 32.976 GeV^{-2}, cutoff Lambda = 631 MeV, reproducing F_pi = 93 MeV and m_pi = 138 MeV at T = mu = 0
- At mu = 500 MeV, the constituent quark mass at the minimum is approximately 50 MeV, indicating incomplete chiral restoration (crossover behavior)

## Context

This work extends the mean-field NJL model studies of the chiral phase transition on the temperature-chemical potential plane by AY, BR, Sca, and Fuj (all cited in the text) to include meson loop corrections beyond the mean-field approximation. It builds on the 1/N_c expansion formalism developed by Nikolov et al. (cited in the text) for T = mu = 0 and by Hufner et al. (cited in the text) for finite temperature, applying it to the finite-density case. The results enable future studies of the phase diagram at finite temperature and chemical potential to locate the critical end point, as well as extensions to three-flavor models and color superconductivity.
