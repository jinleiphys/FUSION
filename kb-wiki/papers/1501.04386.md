# Scaling of nuclear modification factors for hadrons and light nuclei

> The number of constituent quarks (NCQ-) scaling of hadrons and the number of constituent nucleons (NCN-) scaling of light nuclei are proposed for nuclear modification factors ($R_{cp}$) of hadrons and light nuclei, respectively, according to the experimental investigations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Based on coalescence mechanism the scalings are performed for pions and protons in quark level, and light nuclei $d (\bar d)$ and $^3$He for nucleonic level, respectively, formed in Au + Au and Pb + Pb collisions and nice scaling behaviour emerges. NCQ or NCN scaling law of $R_{cp}$ can be respectively taken as a probe for quark or nucleon coalescence mechanism for the formation of hadron or light nuclei in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the nuclear modification factor $R_{cp}$ for hadrons and light nuclei can be scaled by the number of constituent quarks (NCQ) and number of constituent nucleons (NCN), respectively, based on the coalescence mechanism; the scaling holds with a constant factor difference between particle species that shows a centrality dependence, rather than a perfect collapse onto a single universal curve.

## Method

The authors apply the coalescence model to derive scaling formulas for $R_{cp}$: for hadrons, they scale $R_{cp}(n_q \cdot p_T)^{1/n_q}$ by the ratio of binary collision numbers raised to $(1/n_q - 1)$, and for light nuclei they replace $n_q$ with the mass number $A$. They test the NCQ scaling using published $R_{cp}$ data for pions and protons from Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV (STAR) and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2760$ GeV (ALICE). They test the NCN scaling using $R_{cp}$ data for protons, deuterons, and $^3$He from Pb+Pb at 17.2 GeV (NA49), Au+Au at 200 GeV (PHENIX), and Pb+Pb at 2760 GeV (ALICE).

- For Au+Au at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV, the scaled $R_{cp}$ of protons is multiplied by a constant factor (listed in figure 2) to collapse with pions; the factor shows centrality dependence.
- For Pb+Pb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2760$ GeV, the scaled factor for protons increases with increasing centrality of the numerator of $R_{cp}$, showing similar centrality dependence as at RHIC energy.
- The NCN scaling is demonstrated for three collision energies: $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 17.2$ GeV (Pb+Pb), 200 GeV (Au+Au), and 2760 GeV (Pb+Pb), covering deuterons and $^3$He in addition to protons.

## Context

This work extends the NCQ scaling previously established for elliptic flow (STAR 2004; Tian et al.) to the nuclear modification factor $R_{cp}$, and proposes for the first time an analogous NCN scaling for light nuclei. The NCN scaling of $R_{cp}$ complements the earlier prediction of nucleon-number scaling of elliptic flow by Ma's group (LowEv2Scaling; Ma_scaling), which was later supported by STAR data (PRC2016). The results enable using $R_{cp}$ scaling as a probe to distinguish quark coalescence (for hadrons) from nucleon coalescence (for light nuclei) in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and support the interpretation that energy loss originates from the partonic stage for hadrons and from the hadronic stage for light nuclei.
