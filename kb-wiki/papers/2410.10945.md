# From Supernovae to Neutron Stars: A Systematic Approach to Axion Production at Finite Density

> We present a systematic study of QCD axion production in environments with finite baryon density and temperature, implying significant changes to axion phenomenology. Within heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, we derive the effective Lagrangian describing axion interactions with nucleons and mesons up to next-to-leading-order in the chiral expansion. We focus on corrections to the axion-nucleon couplings from higher orders and finite density. These couplings are modified by up to an order of magnitude near nuclear saturation density, significantly impacting axion production in supernovae and neutron stars. Density-dependent corrections enhance the axion luminosity in supernovae by an order of magnitude, strengthening current best bounds by a factor of three. We stress the importance of including all axion production channels up to a given chiral order for a consistent luminosity calculation and classify the missing contributions up to the third chiral order. The modified axion-nucleon couplings also affect neutron star cooling rates via axion emission. A re-evaluation of existing neutron star cooling bounds, constrained to regions where perturbative control is reliable, weakens these bounds by a factor of four. Lastly, our results have implications for terrestrial axion searches that rely on precise knowledge of axion-nucleon couplings.

## Key claim

The paper systematically calculates QCD axion production in dense baryonic environments using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, finding that axion-nucleon couplings are modified by up to an order of magnitude near nuclear saturation density, which strengthens supernova bounds on the axion decay constant by a factor of three but weakens neutron star cooling bounds by a factor of four when restricting to regions where perturbative control is reliable.

## Method

The authors derive the effective Lagrangian for QCD axion interactions with nucleons and pions up to next-to-leading order in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, including finite density and temperature effects. They compute the density-dependent corrections to axion-nucleon couplings by evaluating loop diagrams and contact interactions in the presence of a nucleon Fermi sea, then apply these modified couplings to calculate axion production rates via the nucleon bremsstrahlung process \(NN\to NNa\) in supernova and neutron star environments.

## Key numbers

- Axion-nucleon couplings are modified by up to \(\mathcal{O}(10)\) for the KSVZ axion-neutron coupling and \(\mathcal{O}(1)\) for the KSVZ axion-proton coupling at nuclear saturation density \(n_0\simeq 0.16~\text{fm}^{-3}\)
- Supernova bound on the axion decay constant: \(f_a \gtrsim 1.0^{+0.5}_{-0.2} \times 10^9~\text{GeV}\), corresponding to axion mass \(m_a \lesssim 5.9^{+1.8}_{-2.0}~\text{meV}\) for the KSVZ axion
- Neutron star cooling bounds are weakened by a factor of four when restricting to densities below nuclear saturation density where perturbative control is reliable
- The isovector axial coupling \(G_A = 1.2754(13)\) and isoscalar axial coupling \(G_0 = 0.440(60)\) are used as input parameters

## Context

This work extends previous phenomenological treatments of axion production in supernovae and neutron stars by Chang (2018) and Carenza (2019) by providing the first systematic calculation within chiral perturbation theory with controlled error estimates. It builds on the initial estimate of finite-density effects on axion-nucleon couplings by Balkin (2020) and the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory framework developed by Bernard (1995) and Jenkins (1991). The results enable more reliable astrophysical bounds on the QCD axion and have implications for terrestrial axion searches such as spin precession experiments and nuclear clock searches involving the \({}^{229}\)Th nucleus.
