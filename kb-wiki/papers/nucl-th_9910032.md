# Configuration mixing of mean-field wave-functions projected on angular momentum and particle number; application to 24Mg

> We present in this paper the general framework of a method which permits to restore the rotational and particle number symmetries of wave functions obtained in Skyrme HF+BCS calculations. This restoration is nothing but a projection of mean-field intrinsic wave functions onto good particle number and good angular momentum. The method allows also to mix projected wave functions. Such a configuration mixing is discussed for sets of HF+BCS intrinsic states generated in constrained calculations with suitable collective variables. This procedure gives collective states which are eigenstates of the particle number and the angular momentum operators and between which transition probabilities are calculated. An application to 24Mg is presented, with mean-field wave functions generated by axial quadrupole constraints. Theoretical spectra and transition probabilities are compared to the experiment.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general framework for simultaneously restoring rotational and particle number symmetries of Skyrme HF+BCS wave functions and mixing the resulting projected configurations, but the application to 24Mg is presented as a test case with the caveat that the method is limited to BCS transformations (rather than full HFB) and to axial quadrupole constraints, and that the density dependence of the interaction requires a specific generalization for non-diagonal matrix elements.

## Method

The authors generate a set of intrinsic wave functions via constrained Skyrme HF+BCS calculations with axial quadrupole constraints, then project these wave functions onto good angular momentum (J) and good proton and neutron numbers using projection operators integrated over Euler angles and gauge angles. They then perform a configuration mixing of these projected states for each angular momentum by solving the discretized Hill-Wheeler equation, obtaining collective states that are eigenstates of particle number and angular momentum. The method is implemented on a 3D Cartesian mesh, requiring special treatment of the incomplete single-particle bases and the loss of symmetry under rotation.

## Key numbers

* The spherical configuration of 24Mg has a N=Z=12 component weight of 0.18 after particle number projection
* The energy gained by projection on particle number for the spherical configuration is approximately 1 MeV for most Skyrme parametrizations, except with SIII where it is significantly larger
* Projection on angular momentum increases the energy difference between the spherical configuration and the minimum of the J=0+ curve by 3 to 4 MeV
* The well deformed prolate minimum of the mean-field energy corresponds to a mass quadrupole moment of approximately 1 b
* The pairing strength used for most results is G = 1000 MeV fm^3, with tests ranging from G = 900 MeV fm^3 to G = 1250 MeV fm^3

## Context

This work extends previous methods that performed either angular momentum projection alone (Cauvin 1977, Baye 1984, Rodriguez 1999) or particle number projection with configuration mixing via the generator coordinate method (Heenen 1993) by including both symmetries simultaneously. The approach enables calculation of transition probabilities between collective states that are eigenstates of angular momentum and particle number, going beyond the cranking method which is limited to rigid nuclei and cannot properly describe transitions in nuclei soft with respect to collective variables. The method is tested on 24Mg using three Skyrme parametrizations (SIII, SkM*, SLy4) and compared to experimental spectra and transition probabilities.
