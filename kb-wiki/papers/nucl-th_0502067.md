# Constrained Molecular Dynamics II: a N-body approach to nuclear systems

> In this work we illustrate the basic development of the constrained molecular dynamics applied to the N-body problem in nuclear physics. The heavy computational taskes related to quantum effects, to the presence of the "hard core" repulsive interaction have been worked out by defining a set of transformations based on the concept of impulsive forces. In particular in the implemented version II of the Constrained Molecular Dynamics model the problem related to the non conservation of the total angular momentum has been solved. This problem can affect others semiclassical microscopic approaches as due to the "hard core" repulsive interaction or to the use of stochastic forces. The effect of the restored conservation law on the fusion cross section for 40Ca+40Ca system is also briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents the Constrained Molecular Dynamics (CoMD) model version II, which solves the problem of total angular momentum non-conservation that affects other semiclassical microscopic approaches due to hard-core repulsive interactions or stochastic forces; the authors show that restoring this conservation law reduces the fusion cross section for the 40Ca+40Ca system by about 30% at higher energies, but note that work is still in progress to reproduce the experimental point at E_cm=150 MeV.

## Method

The authors implement a constrained molecular dynamics approach where nucleons are represented by Gaussian wave packets and the equations of motion include impulsive forces (P_i) that enforce the Pauli principle (average occupation numbers f_i ≤ 1) through multiple two-body scatterings in phase space. For CoMD II, they add a collective transformation R that restores total angular momentum conservation by applying a collective angular velocity to the ensemble of nucleons that underwent collisions in a given time step, followed by a radial momentum scaling to conserve energy. The algorithm identifies clusters via a coalescence model at each time step and applies the angular momentum correction separately to each cluster to avoid unphysical long-range interactions.

## Key numbers

- Ground state configurations are obtained for nuclei with mass number ranging from N=30 to 197, using Gaussian parameters σ_r=1.13 fm and σ_p=0.43 ħ to achieve average binding energy of 8 MeV/nucleon and nuclear radii R=1.2·N^(1/3) (within 10%) with average compressibility of 200 MeV.
- For 40Ca+40Ca collisions at 35 MeV/nucleon, calculations with the constraint show satisfactory agreement with experimental charge distributions, while calculations without the constraint show a binary "U" shape due to a factor of 3 suppression of the nucleon-nucleon cross section from Pauli blocking.
- For 40Ca+40Ca at E_lab=220 MeV and impact parameter b=2 fm, the time dependence of total angular momentum is shown comparing CoMD I (dotted line) and CoMD II (continuous line).
- The fusion cross section for 40Ca+40Ca shows about 30% reduction at higher energy when L conservation is taken into account, substantially due to a 40-50% increase of the binary process for the L conserved case.

## Context

This work extends the Constrained Molecular Dynamics approach introduced in Papa et al. (CoMD I) by solving the angular momentum conservation problem that also affects other semiclassical approaches such as QMD (Quantum Molecular Dynamics) and approaches using the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) and Boltzmann-Nordheim-Vlasov (BNV) equations. The paper contrasts its N^2 dimensionality approach with more complex antisymmetrized methods like FMD (Fermionic Molecular Dynamics) and AMD (Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics), which require N^4 dimensionality and approximations such as the "physical coordinate approximation." The results enable more reliable predictions for fusion cross sections and binary decay processes in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for the 40Ca+40Ca system where experimental data exist at E_cm=150 MeV.
