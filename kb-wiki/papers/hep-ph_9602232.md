# Evolution of Inhomogeneous Condensates after Phase Transitions

> Using the O(4) linear $σ$ model, we address the topic of non-equilibrium relaxation of an inhomogeneous initial configuration due to quantum and thermal fluctuations. The space-time evolution of an inhomogeneous fluctuation of the condensate in the isoscalar channel decaying via the emission of pions in the medium is studied within the context of disoriented chiral condensates. We use out of equilibrium closed time path methods in field theory combined with the amplitude expansion. We give explicit expressions for the asymptotic space-time evolution of an initial inhomogeneous configuration including the contribution of thresholds at zero and non-zero temperature. At non-zero temperature we find new relaxational processes due to thermal cuts that have no counterpart in the homogeneous case. Within the one-loop approximation, we find that the space time evolution of such inhomogeneous configuration out of equilibrium is effectively described in terms of a rapidity dependent temperature $T(\vartheta)=T/\cosh[\vartheta]$ as well as a rapidity dependent decay rate $Γ(\vartheta, T(\vartheta))$. This rate is to be interpreted as the production minus absorption rate of pions in the medium and approaches the zero temperature value at large rapidities. An initial configuration localized on a bounded region spreads and decays in spherical waves with slower relaxational dynamics at large rapidity.

## Key claim

Using the O(4) linear sigma model at one-loop order, the authors show that an inhomogeneous chiral condensate decays via spherical waves with a rapidity-dependent effective temperature \(T(\vartheta)=T/\cosh[\vartheta]\) and decay rate \(\Gamma(\vartheta, T(\vartheta))\), where the rate approaches the zero-temperature value at large rapidities; this description applies only for small initial field amplitudes where the evolution equations linearize.

## Method

The authors study the linear relaxation dynamics of an inhomogeneous scalar order parameter in the O(4) linear sigma model using out-of-equilibrium closed time path (CTP) methods combined with the amplitude expansion, keeping only the linear term in the amplitude. They compute the retarded self-energy at one-loop order from diagrams with pions and sigma mesons in the internal loops, then solve the linearized equation of motion for the spatial Fourier transform of the condensate via Laplace transform, analyzing the analytic structure of the propagator in the complex s-plane. The time evolution is obtained by inverse Laplace transform, separating contributions from single-particle poles and multiparticle cuts, with explicit expressions for the imaginary parts of the self-energy at zero and non-zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- The sigma meson mass is \(M_{\sigma} \approx 600\ \mathrm{Mev}\), the pion mass is \(m_{\pi} \approx 130\ \mathrm{Mev}\), and the pion decay constant is \(f_{\pi} \approx 90\ \mathrm{Mev}\).
- The explicit symmetry breaking parameter \(h \approx (120\ \mathrm{Mev})^3\).
- At zero temperature, the one-loop correction to the sigma mass on shell is \(0.045 M_{\sigma}^2\), while the imaginary part on shell is \(1.195 M_{\sigma}^2\).
- The asymptotic pole contribution decays as \(\tau^{-3/2}\) with amplitude proportional to \(M_0^{3/2} \cosh[\vartheta_r] / \tau^{3/2}\), while the cut contribution decays as \(\tau^{-3}\) with terms proportional to \(M_{\sigma}^2 \sin(2M_{\sigma}\tau)\) and \(M_{\pi}^2 \sin(2M_{\pi}\tau)\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of disoriented chiral condensates (DCC) that focused on homogeneous mean fields and fluctuations (Wilczek and Rajagopal, 1993; Pisarski, 1993; Cooper, 1994; Gavin, 1994; Mueller, 1994; Boyd, 1995) by addressing the relaxational dynamics of inhomogeneous field configurations including quantum and thermal effects. The results apply to the chiral phase transition in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, as well as to cosmological scenarios involving textures or cosmic strings (Vilenkin, 1985; Kibble, 1980). The analysis enables future study of the spectrum of fluctuations in the cosmic microwave background radiation and provides a framework for understanding relaxation of inhomogeneous scalar order parameters in a medium.
