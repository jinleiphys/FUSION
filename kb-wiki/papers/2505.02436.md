# Jet shape modification in a transport calculation

> A precise quantification of the medium-modification of the high transverse momenta jets in relativistic heavy ion collisions rely on consistent modelling of elastic and inelastic energy loss suffered by the jet and the concurrent underlying medium evolution. We have developed a unified framework for jet and bulk medium evolution within a multiphase transport approach where the jets and medium share energy and momentum via multiple elastic scatterings and medium-induced gluon radiation during the parton transport. The formulation enables realistic predictions of jet based observables extended to a large radius of the jet cone in central Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV. The model provides reasonable quantitative agreement with the experimental data from inclusive jet suppression and the full jet shape function up to large radial distances induced by both the collisional and radiative jet energy loss and migration of the lost $p_T$ in the medium. We find that gradual degradation of jet energy through gluon emissions alters the energy-momentum evolution in the jet, essential to describe the entire range of jet shape ratio relative to proton-proton collisions. Pure collisional energy loss injects appreciable $p_T$ broadening and migration of the medium partons, resulting in an enhanced population at large angular distances in the jet-shape ratio.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a unified kinetic framework combining elastic and inelastic parton energy loss within the AMPT transport model can provide reasonable quantitative agreement with experimental data on inclusive jet suppression and full jet shape functions up to large radial distances in central Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, but the abstract glosses over that the model shows under-quenching for low transverse momentum jets below 150 GeV and an opposite cone-size dependence compared to the nuclear modification factor data, suggesting the need for further improvements such as including the MATTER framework.

## Method

The authors extended the string-melting AMPT (A MultiPhase Transport) model by implementing medium-induced gluon radiation via the higher-twist energy loss formalism alongside existing elastic scattering processes, using PYTHIA 6.4 to generate initial jet showers with formation times calculated from the splitting kinematics. Jets and bulk medium partons were evolved together through Boltzmann transport with explicit energy-momentum conservation, where radiated gluons participated in further elastic and inelastic interactions only after their formation time and above a transverse momentum cutoff of 2 GeV. Final-state hadronization was performed via independent fragmentation for hard partons using PYTHIA and via coalescence for soft medium partons as in the original AMPT, with jets reconstructed using the anti-\(k_T\) algorithm and the same analysis procedures as experimental collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Jet nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}^{\rm jet}\) for inclusive jets with cone radii \(R = 0.2\) and 0.4 in (0-10)% central Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02\) TeV, compared to ATLAS and ALICE data
- For a gluon jet with initial energy \(E^{\rm init}_{\rm g-jet} = 200\) GeV, medium-induced radiation contributes about 67% of the total energy loss at late times
- Elastic scattering cross section for gluon-gluon scatterings \(\sigma^{\rm el}_{gg} = 1.5\) mb, corresponding to strong coupling constant \(\alpha_s = 0.333\) and Debye screening mass \(\mu = 3.226\) fm\(^{-1}\)
- Jet shape \(\rho(\Delta r)\) for inclusive jets with \(p_T^{\rm jet} > 120\) GeV at \(|\eta_{\rm jet}| < 1.6\) constructed with cone \(R = 0.4\) and hadron \(p_T^{\rm trk} > 0.7\) GeV, compared to CMS data up to radial distance \(\Delta r = 1\)
- Additional simulation with smaller Debye mass \(\mu = 1.767\) fm\(^{-1}\) corresponding to \(\sigma^{\rm el}_{gg} = 5\) mb showed stronger medium modification of the jet shape function

## Context

This work extends prior jet quenching studies within the AMPT model that previously included only elastic scatterings (Luo 2021) and addresses the need for a unified kinetic framework that treats both collisional and radiative energy loss concurrently with medium evolution, unlike hybrid models that couple jets to hydrodynamic backgrounds with instantaneous thermalization assumptions (Tachibana 2017, 2020; Yang 2022, 2025). The approach enables realistic predictions of jet observables extended to large cone radii and can potentially delineate the underlying QGP medium density and evolution, though the authors note that improvements by including the MATTER framework (Majumder 2013, Cao 2017) for high-virtuality parton evolution may provide better agreement with quenching data.
