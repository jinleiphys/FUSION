# The 5-dimensional Langevin approach to fission of atomic nuclei

> We have generalized the four-dimensional Langevin approach used in our previous works for the description of fission process to the five-dimensional by considering the neck parameter $\eps$ in the two-center shell model shape parametrization as an independent dynamical variable. The calculated results for the mass distribution of fission fragments are in better agreement with the available experimental data. In particular, the transition from the mass-symmetric to mass-asymmetric fission via the triple-humped distribution in fission of Thorium isotopes is well reproduced.

## Key claim

The authors claim that generalizing their previous four-dimensional Langevin approach to five dimensions by treating the neck parameter $\epsilon$ as an independent dynamical variable yields fission fragment mass distributions in better agreement with experimental data, particularly for the transition from mass-symmetric to mass-asymmetric fission via triple-humped distributions in thorium isotopes. However, the five-dimensional computations are extremely time-consuming and the grid in $\epsilon$ is limited to only 5 points due to memory constraints.

## Method

The authors solve five-dimensional Langevin equations for the time evolution of collective variables describing nuclear shape using the two-center shell model parametrization with five deformation parameters: elongation $z_0/R_0$, deformations of left and right parts $\delta_1$ and $\delta_2$, mass asymmetry $\alpha$, and neck parameter $\epsilon$. The free energy is calculated within the macroscopic-microscopic model using a folded Yukawa macroscopic energy and temperature-dependent shell corrections from a deformed Woods-Saxon potential fitted to the TCSM shapes, with collective inertia from the Werner-Wheeler approximation and friction from the wall-and-window formula with reduction factor $k_s=0.27$.

## Key numbers

- The critical neck radius for scission is set to $r_{neck}^{(crit)} = 1$ fm
- The frequency of local collective vibration is taken as $\hbar\varpi = 2$ MeV, with test calculations also performed at $\hbar\varpi = 0$ and $\hbar\varpi = 4$ MeV
- The damping parameter $\eta_{z_0z_0}$ for $^{222}$Th varies from 2.6 to 0.35, while $\eta_{\epsilon\epsilon}$ varies from 1.9 to 1.3
- The grid in the five-dimensional space uses $41 \times 31 \times 31 \times 40$ points for $z_0, \delta_1, \delta_2, \alpha$ with only 5 points in $\epsilon$
- The initial deformation parameters for all thorium isotopes are $\{q_{\mu}\} = \{0.2, 0.2, 0.2, 0.0, 0.35\}$ with zero collective momenta

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous four-dimensional Langevin approach (Ivanyuk et al., 2021) which treated $\delta_1$ and $\delta_2$ as independent variables but kept $\epsilon$ fixed at 0.35. The four-dimensional approach successfully reproduced the rapid change of mass distributions from asymmetric to symmetric fission in fermium isotopes and the saw-tooth structure of neutron multiplicity, but failed to reproduce the triple-humped mass distributions observed for thorium isotopes around $^{227}$Th in experiments at GSI Darmstadt (Schmidt et al., 2000) and later with the R3B/SOFIA setup (Chatillon et al., 2021). The five-dimensional generalization enables description of super-long and super-short fission modes and shows that the fission process is neither under-damped nor over-damped, questioning the applicability of Kramers' high-viscosity limit formula to nuclear fission widths.
