# Ground State Correlations and Final State Interactions in Two-Nucleon Emission Processes off Few-Nucleon Systems

> The two nucleon emission process from ^3He, ^3He(e,e'N_2N_3)N_1, has been theoretically analyzed using realistic three-nucleon wave functions and taking the final state interaction into account. Various kinematical conditions have been considered in order to clarify the question whether the effects of the final state interaction could be minimized by a proper choice of the kinematics of the process.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that in two-nucleon emission from ^3He, the distorting effects of final state interactions (FSI) can be minimized by a proper choice of kinematics, specifically by selecting the "two-nucleon correlation region" where the excitation energy E* satisfies E* ≈ k_1^2/(4M). This condition allows the investigation of ground state correlations without significant FSI contamination.

## Method
The authors calculated the cross section for ^3He(e,e'N_2N_3)N_1 using realistic three-nucleon wave functions (from the Pisa group and the AV14 interaction) and included FSI through two-nucleon rescattering in the continuum. They computed the nucleon spectral function and the differential cross section M(p_m,t) for various kinematic conditions, comparing the "No FSI" approximation (plane wave) with results including NN rescattering and, in preliminary calculations, three-body rescattering via a Glauber-type approach.

## Key numbers
- The two-nucleon relative energy was fixed at E* = 50 MeV for the angular dependence calculations.
- Three values of the missing momentum k_1 were studied: 0.5 fm^{-1}, 2.2 fm^{-1} (the correlation region), and 3 fm^{-1}.
- The relative momentum |t| = 245 MeV/c was used in the three-body rescattering calculation.
- The correlation region condition is E* = t^2/M ≈ k_1^2/(4M).

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of ground state correlations in few-nucleon systems by Ciofi degli Atti and collaborators (CPS, 1990s) and uses realistic wave functions from the Pisa group (Rosati, 1990s) and the AV14 interaction (Wiringa, 1980s). The results enable experimental investigation of two-nucleon correlations in ^3He at facilities such as those described by Larry and Eddy (2000s), by showing that FSI effects can be controlled through kinematic selection, complementing the Gloeckle group's (1990s) approach to few-body FSI calculations.
