# Additional resonant contribution to the potential model for the 12C(alpha,gamma)16O reaction

> The additional resonant contribution to the potential model is examined in $α$+$^{12}$C elastic scattering and the low-energy $^{12}$C($α$,$γ$)$^{16}$O reaction. The excitation function of elastic scattering below $E_{c.m.}= 5$ MeV seems to be reproduced by the potential model satisfactorily, and it is not profoundly disturbed by the additional resonances. The weak coupling is good enough to describe the $^{16}$O structure in the vicinity of the $α$-particle threshold, especially below $E_{c.m.}= 8$ MeV, corresponding to the excitation energy $E_x \approx 15$ MeV. The additional resonances give the complement of the astrophysical $S$-factors from the simple potential model. The $S$-factor of $^{12}$C($α$,$γ$)$^{16}$O at $E_{c.m.}=300$ keV is dominated by the $E$2 transition, which is enhanced by the subthreshold 2$^+_1$ state at $E_x= 6.92$ MeV. The contribution from the subthreshold 1$^-_1$ state at $E_x= 7.12$ MeV is predicted to be small. The additional resonances do not give the large contribution to the thermonuclear reaction rates of $^{12}$C($α$,$γ$)$^{16}$O at helium burning temperatures.

## Key claim

The additional narrow resonances, included as Breit-Wigner terms, complement the astrophysical S-factors from the simple potential model but do not produce a large contribution to the thermonuclear reaction rates of 12C(alpha,gamma)16O at helium burning temperatures (below T9 = 3), with the increase in reaction rates remaining below 5 percent. The weak coupling to other reaction channels is sufficient to describe the structure of 16O below excitation energy Ex approximately 15 MeV, and the subthreshold 1-1 state at Ex = 7.12 MeV does not enhance the E1 S-factor at low energies.

## Method

The author uses a parity-dependent real nuclear potential with Woods-Saxon form factors to describe the relative motion between alpha-particle and 12C, solving the Schrodinger equation for the continuum and bound states. Known resonances are appended to the direct-capture component via a Breit-Wigner term in the transition amplitude, with energy-dependent alpha-particle and gamma widths. The excitation function of alpha+12C elastic scattering is calculated using a dispersion formula for the S-matrix, and the astrophysical S-factors and Maxwellian-averaged reaction rates are computed from the resulting radiative capture cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The S-factor at Ec.m. = 300 keV is S_E1 approximately 3 keV b and S_E2 = 152 keV b, approximately the same as previous values from Katsuma (2008).
- The additional resonances increase the reaction rates below T9 = 3 by less than 5 percent relative to the previous recommended rates (KA12) from Katsuma (2012).
- The 2+2 resonance appears at Ec.m. = 2.683 MeV (Ex = 11.52 MeV) and the 2+3 resonance at Ec.m. = 4.357 MeV.
- The Gamov peak energy at T9 = 3 is E0 = 1.92 MeV with width Delta E0 = 1.63 MeV.
- The potential parameters for the alpha+12C continuum state are V+ = -199.7 MeV, R+ = 2.18 fm, a+ = 0.743 fm for even parity and V- = -168.1 MeV, R- = 2.76 fm, a- = 0.567 fm for odd parity.
- The effective charges are e_tilde_E1 = 9.96 x 10^-3 e and e_tilde_E2 = 1.69 e for the direct-capture component, and e_tilde_E1 = 0.68 e for the resonance at En = 5.928 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the simple potential model calculations of Katsuma (2008, 2012) by explicitly including narrow resonances that were previously omitted, examining whether they significantly alter the low-energy cross sections and reaction rates. The study contrasts with R-matrix analyses (e.g., Lane and Thomas, 1958) by emphasizing the transparent feature of the alpha+12C system and the weak coupling description of 16O structure. The results enable improved reaction rate estimates for stellar helium burning, confirming that the simple potential model captures the dominant physics below T9 = 3, while the remaining uncertainties at higher temperatures (T9 > 1) arise primarily from cascade transitions through excited states of 16O, to be addressed in future work.
