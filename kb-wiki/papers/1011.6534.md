# Nuclear PDFs at NLO - status report and review of the EPS09 results

> We review the current status of the global DGLAP analysis of nuclear parton distribution functions, nPDFs, focusing on the recent EPS09 analysis, whose output, EPS09NLO, is the best-constrained NLO nPDF set on the market. Collinear factorization is found to work very well in the kinematical region studied. With the error sets released in the EPS09 package one can compute how the nPDF-related uncertainties propagate into factorizable nuclear hard-process cross sections. A comparison with the other existing NLO nPDF sets is shown, and the BRAHMS forward-$η$ hadron data from d+Au collisions are discussed in the light of the EPS09 nPDFs and their error sets.

## Key claim

The EPS09 global DGLAP analysis demonstrates that collinear factorization works very well in the kinematical region studied, but the strong suppression seen in the BRAHMS forward-rapidity hadron data from d+Au collisions at eta=3.2 and pT>2 GeV is attributed to an excess in the p+p data rather than to suppression in the d+Au data, which is why these BRAHMS data were excluded from the EPS09 analysis.

## Method

The authors performed a global DGLAP analysis of nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs) at NLO, defining bound proton PDFs relative to free proton CTEQ6.1M PDFs via nuclear modification ratios R_i^A(x,Q^2) for gluons, valence quarks, and sea quarks at an initial scale Q^2_0=1.69 GeV^2. They minimized a generalized chi^2 with respect to 15 fit parameters using 929 data points from 32 data sets including deep inelastic lepton-nucleus scattering, Drell-Yan dimuon production in p+A collisions from FNAL, and neutral-pion production at mid-rapidity in d+Au collisions from RHIC, then performed error analysis using the Hessian method with a Delta chi^2 of 50 to produce 30 error sets.

## Key numbers

* The global chi^2 minimum is chi^2_0=731.3 for 929 data points, giving chi^2/N=0.79
* The initial scale for the analysis is Q^2_0=1.69 GeV^2
* The Delta chi^2 used for error sets is approximately 50
* The EPS09 analysis uses 929 data points from 32 data sets, compared to 1241 for HKN07 and 420 for nDS
* The kinematical range covered is 0.005 less than or approximately equal to x less than or equal to 1, 1.69 less than or equal to Q^2 less than or equal to approximately 150 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the earlier EKS98 LO analysis and the first NLO nPDF set nDS (de Florian and Sassot, 2004) by including RHIC data and providing, for the first time, 30 concrete nPDF error sets for each A, reaching the same technical rigor as free proton PDF analyses like CT10 and MSTW. The EPS09 results enable precision studies of how nPDF uncertainties propagate into factorizable nuclear hard-process cross sections, and the authors note that further constraints for nuclear gluons at lowest x would come from planned future colliders eRHIC and LHeC.
