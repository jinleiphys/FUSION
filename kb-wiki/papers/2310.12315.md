# Gravitational form factors of light nuclei: Impulse approximation

> The gravitational form factors of light nuclei are evaluated up to momenta of the order of the nucleon mass, using the impulse approximation. The nucleon gravitational form factors are reduced non-relativistically, and used to derive the gravitational form factors of light nuclei. The deuteron gravitational form factors are analysed using the Reid soft core potential. The helium-4 gravitational form factors are assessed using the K-harmonics method, and compared to those following from a mean-field approximation with a Woods-Saxon potential. The importance of removing the center of mass motion for the ensuing form factors is emphasized. The mass radii of these light nuclei are extracted and compared to their charge radii counterparts. The details of their pressure and shear distributions are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper evaluates the gravitational form factors (GFFs) of light nuclei (deuteron and helium-4) using the impulse approximation, assuming nucleons are quasi-free particles. The key hedge is that the non-relativistic reduction of the nucleon GFFs is assumed to hold for momentum transfers up to the nucleon mass scale, with the justification that a similar assumption works for electromagnetic probes at that scale.

## Method

The authors perform a non-relativistic reduction of the nucleon energy-momentum tensor (EMT) form factors (A, B, C) and use them in the impulse approximation to derive the deuteron EMT, employing the Reid soft core potential to obtain the deuteron S- and D-wave functions. For helium-4, they use both the K-harmonics method and a mean-field approximation with a Woods-Saxon potential, emphasizing the removal of spurious center-of-mass motion. The nucleon GFFs are parametrized using lattice results for quarks and a holographic model for gluons.

## Key numbers

*   Deuteron binding energy: 2.2246 MeV
*   Deuteron charge radius: 2.13 fm
*   Deuteron quadrupole moment: approximately 0.31 fm²
*   D-state admixture in deuteron: 6.53%
*   Spin-averaged deuteron D-term at zero momentum transfer: D₀(0) = -10.43 (impulse approximation), compared to -13.126 (Skyrme model) and -24.33 (light cone convolution model)
*   Gluon mass radius parameter: m_{TT,g} = 1.612 GeV; quark mass radius parameter: m_{TT,q} = 1.477(44) GeV
*   Gluon D-term at zero: D_g(0) = -1.275; quark D-term at zero: D_q(0) = -1.30(49)
*   Gluon A(0): 0.430; quark A(0): 0.510(25) (set to 0.57 to satisfy sum rule A_q(0)+A_g(0)=1)

## Context

This work extends the study of gravitational form factors from the nucleon to light nuclei, building on prior analyses of nucleon GFFs from lattice QCD (Hackett 2023) and holographic models (Mamo 2022), as well as recent experimental extractions from charmonium photoproduction at JLab (GlueX 2019, Duran 2022). The deuteron GFFs were previously discussed using a liquid drop model (Polyakov 2002), relativistic nuclear potentials (Guzey 2005), nuclear structure approaches (Kim 2012, Hudson 2017, Dupre 2015), and a generalized Skyrme model (GarciaMartin-Caro 2023). The results enable future comparisons with threshold charmonium photoproduction off light nuclei, which could reveal nuclear effects on the energy-momentum tensor.
