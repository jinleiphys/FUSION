# Transport coefficients of O(N) scalar field theories close to the critical point

> We investigate the critical dynamics of O(N)-symmetric scalar field theories to determine the critical exponents of transport coefficients as a second-order phase transition is approached from the symmetric phase. A set of stochastic equations of motion for the slow modes is formulated, and the long wavelength dynamics is examined for an arbitrary number of field components, $N$, in the framework of the dynamical renormalization group within the $ε$ expansion. We find that for a single component scalar field theory, N=1, the system reduces to the model C of critical dynamics, whereas for $N>1$ the model G is effectively restored owing to dominance of O(N)-symmetric charge fluctuations. In both cases, the shear viscosity remains finite in the critical region. On the other hand, we find that the bulk viscosity diverges as the correlation length squared, for N=1, while it remains finite for $N>1$.

## Key claim

The paper determines the dynamical universality class of O(N) scalar field theories near the critical point, finding that for N=1 the system reduces to model C, while for N>1 it effectively restores model G; in both cases the shear viscosity remains finite, but the bulk viscosity diverges as the correlation length squared only for N=1 and remains finite for N>1.

## Method

The authors formulate a stochastic equation of motion for the slow modes (order parameter fluctuations, energy density, momentum density, and O(N) charge density) based on the effective Hamiltonian and the projection method in the Markovian approximation. They then apply the dynamical renormalization group within the epsilon expansion (ε = 4 − d) to examine the long-wavelength dynamics for an arbitrary number of field components N, deriving recursion relations for the transport coefficients and identifying fixed points that determine the dynamical critical exponents and scaling behavior.

## Key numbers

- For N=1, the bulk viscosity diverges as the correlation length squared (ξ²) near the critical point.
- For N>1, the bulk viscosity remains finite at the critical point.
- The shear viscosity remains finite in the critical region for both N=1 and N>1.
- The dynamical critical exponent for model G is z_G = 3/2.
- The dynamical critical exponent for model C with N=1 is z_C = 2 + α/ν, where α and ν are static critical exponents.
- The fixed point value of the dimensionless three-point coupling v* = α̃ + O(ε²), which vanishes for negative α.
- The scaling dimension of the relevant parameter r is Δ_r = 2 − (N+2)/(N+8)ε + O(ε²).
- The fixed point coupling u* = ε / [4Ω₄(N+8)] + O(ε²).

## Context

This work extends the dynamical renormalization group classification of Hohenberg and Halperin (1977) to O(N) scalar field theories, showing that the dynamical universality class depends on the number of field components N. The results contradict earlier kinetic theory approaches that neglect higher-order correlations important near the critical point, as discussed by Chen (2007, 2010) and Schafer (2009). The findings enable future studies of critical transport in QCD effective theories, particularly for N=4 which serves as a low-energy chiral effective theory for two-flavor QCD as noted by Pisarski (1983).
