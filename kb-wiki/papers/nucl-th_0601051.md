# Phase-space methods in nuclear reactions around the Fermi energy

> Some prescriptions for in-medium complex particle production in nuclear reactions are proposed. They have been implemented in two models to simulate nucleon-nucleus (nIPSE) and nucleus-nucleus (HIPSE) reactions around the Fermi energy \cite{Lac04,Lac05}. Our work emphasizes the effect of randomness in cluster formation, the importance of the nucleonic Fermi motion as well as the role of conservation laws. The key role of the phase-space exploration before and after secondary decay is underlined. This is illustrated in the case of two debated issues: the memory loss of the entrance channel in central collisions and the $(N,Z)$ partitions after the pre-equilibrium stage.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that simple, minimal prescriptions for cluster formation based on random nucleon sampling from a Thomas-Fermi distribution, combined with energy and barrier constraints, can reproduce the characteristics of nuclear fragmentation around the Fermi energy, but the authors hedge that quantitative comparisons require incorporating impact parameter effects, in-medium nucleon-nucleon collisions, and secondary decay.

## Method

The authors define a three-step scenario for nucleon-induced reactions: absorption of the incident nucleon, in-medium cluster formation by randomly picking nucleons from a Thomas-Fermi distribution, and cluster emission subject to two constraints (overcoming the interaction barrier and satisfying the energy balance). These rules were implemented in two Monte-Carlo models: nIPSE for nucleon-nucleus reactions and HIPSE for nucleus-nucleus reactions, with few parameters adjusted only by beam energy and kept fixed when changing target or projectile.

## Key numbers

- For a proton-induced reaction at \(E_B = 39\) MeV, an alpha particle can only be emitted in a small interval of kinetic energy (the "escape window"), leading to significant restriction in phase-space.
- In Xe+Sn reactions, the models reproduce charge distributions, average kinetic energies, fluctuations, angular distributions, parallel velocities, event-by-event internal correlations, and isospin diffusion effects for beam energies from 25 to 80 MeV/A.
- For the reaction Xe+Sn at 50 MeV/A, the isotropic ratio \(\left< R_{iso} \right>\) saturates around a value of 0.75 when plotted as a function of multiplicity, but reaches approximately 1 for the smallest impact parameters.
- The reaction \(^{48}\)Ca+\(^{40}\)Ca at beam energy \(E_B=25\) MeV/A shows partial isospin equilibration between quasi-projectile and quasi-target, while mid-rapidity clusters and compound nucleus are equilibrated in average.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of the nIPSE and HIPSE models (Lacroix 2004, 2005) and addresses the debated issue of memory loss of the entrance channel in central collisions, following the approach of Eschenauer (2005) using the isotropic ratio. The models enable future application to radioactive beam reactions and provide access to phase-space configurations before secondary decay, which is of interest for linking to statistical physics.
