# Variational Approach to the Dynamics of Bose-Einstein Condensates.I --Variational Ansatz and Quantum Dynamics--

> Variational method is applied to describe Bose-Einstein condensates (BEC) interacting via a pseudo-potential, taking into account quantum fluctuations around the mean field by the Gaussian approximation. Contributions from the pair-wise scattering by the singular potential should be removed carefully to avoid double countings in the perturbation series in terms of original bare interaction. We show that this procedure removes all dangerous terms which contain divergences.

## Key claim

The authors show that quantum fluctuations around the mean field in Bose-Einstein condensates can be treated variationally using a Gaussian wave functional, but only after systematically removing contributions that double-count the pair-wise scattering already accounted for by the pseudo-potential; this removal eliminates all ultraviolet divergences that otherwise arise from the singular contact interaction.

## Method

The authors construct a time-dependent Gaussian variational wave functional in the functional Schrödinger picture, with the mean field and the two-point correlation functions as variational parameters. They derive coupled equations of motion: a generalized Gross-Pitaevskii equation for the condensate wavefunction that includes fluctuation terms, and a Liouville equation for the reduced density matrix of fluctuations (the time-dependent Hartree-Bogoliubov equations). For a uniform condensate, they solve these equations in momentum space and identify divergent terms in the gap equation, which they remove by subtracting diagrams that correspond to repeated use of the effective interaction in the two-particle scattering channel.

## Key numbers

- The effective pseudo-potential strength is \( g = 4\pi \hbar^2 a / m \), where \( a \) is the S-wave scattering length and \( m \) is the particle mass.
- The quasiparticle energy spectrum is \( E_{\mathbf{k}} = \sqrt{W_{\mathbf{k}}^2 - |\Delta|^2} \), with \( W_{\mathbf{k}} = \mathbf{k}^2/(2m) - \mu + 2g(|\Phi_0|^2 + \langle \hat{\psi}^\dagger \hat{\psi} \rangle) \) and \( \Delta = g(\Phi_0^2 + \langle \hat{\psi} \hat{\psi} \rangle) \).
- The gap equation obtained after divergence removal is \( \Delta/g = \Phi_0^2 - \Delta \int d^3\mathbf{k}/(2\pi)^3 \, (n_{\mathbf{k}} + 1/2)/E_{\mathbf{k}} \), which the authors state is a new result not previously written explicitly in the literature.

## Context

This work extends the Gross-Pitaevskii mean-field theory (Gross 1961, Pitaevskii 1961) by including quantum fluctuations via a Gaussian variational method previously developed by the authors for quantum field dynamics (Tsue, Vautherin, Matsui 1999, 2000). The resulting equations are identical to the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations for interacting Bose systems derived by Blaizot and Ripka (1986) and by Griffin (1996). The divergence-removal procedure follows the physical interpretation of Lee, Huang, and Yang (1957) that the pseudo-potential already accounts for all two-body scattering, so repeated use in many-body diagrams must be subtracted. The method enables future calculation of the density response function of the system, to be presented in a forthcoming paper.
