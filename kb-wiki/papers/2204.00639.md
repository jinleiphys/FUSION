# Freezing Out Fluctuations in Hydro+ Near the QCD Critical Point

> We introduce a freeze-out procedure to convert the critical fluctuations in a droplet of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) that has, as it expanded and cooled, passed close to a posited critical point on the phase diagram into cumulants of hadron multiplicities that can subsequently be measured. The procedure connects the out-of-equilibrium critical fluctuations described in concert with the hydrodynamic evolution of the droplet of QGP by extended hydrodynamics, known as Hydro+, with the subsequent kinetic description in terms of observable hadrons. We introduce a critical scalar isoscalar field sigma whose fluctuations cause correlations between observed hadrons due to the couplings of the sigma field to the hadrons via their masses. We match the QGP fluctuations obtained by solving the Hydro+ equations describing the evolution of critical fluctuations before freeze-out to the correlations of the sigma field. In turn, these are imprinted onto correlations and fluctuations in the multiplicity of hadrons, most importantly protons, after freeze-out via the generalization of the familiar half-century-old Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription which we introduce. The proposed framework allows us to study the effects of critical slowing down and the consequent deviation of the observable predictions from equilibrium expectations quantitatively. We also quantify the suppression of cumulants due to conservation of baryon number. We demonstrate the procedure in practice by freezing out a Hydro+ simulation in an azimuthally symmetric and boost invariant background that includes radial flow discussed in arXiv:1908.08539.

## Key claim
The paper introduces a freeze-out procedure to convert out-of-equilibrium critical fluctuations in quark-gluon plasma (QGP), described by Hydro+ extended hydrodynamics, into cumulants of hadron multiplicities that can be measured experimentally. The authors demonstrate that critical slowing down and conservation laws (Model H dynamics) significantly suppress the magnitude of fluctuations relative to equilibrium expectations, with the suppression depending on the diffusion parameter and the proximity of freeze-out to the critical point.

## Method
The authors match QGP fluctuations obtained from Hydro+ equations (which evolve the fluctuation measure φ_Q according to Model H relaxation dynamics with conservation laws) to correlations of a critical scalar isoscalar sigma field. They then generalize the Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription to imprint these sigma field correlations onto hadron multiplicity fluctuations, focusing on protons. The procedure is applied to two scenarios: an idealized Bjorken flow (boost-invariant longitudinal expansion with no transverse flow) and a semi-realistic simulation with radial flow using the VH1+1 hydrodynamic code with a Glauber initial profile for Au-Au collisions at √s=200 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Initial hydrodynamic simulation time τ_i = 1 fm, with initial central temperature T_i = 330 MeV
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s = 0.08 throughout
- Spatial lattice with 1024 points spaced by 0.0123 fm and time step of 0.005 fm
- Freeze-out temperature T_f = 140 MeV used for the Bjorken scenario results
- Diffusion parameter values D_0 = 0.25 fm and D_0 = 1 fm compared in the semi-realistic simulation
- Maximum equilibrium correlation length values ξ_max = 1 fm and ξ_max = 3 fm compared
- In the Bjorken scenario, temperatures T = 235, 160, and 140 MeV correspond to times τ = 1, 4.6, and 8.8 fm respectively
- The relaxation rate parameter Γ_0 = 1 fm^{-1} used in the Bjorken scenario

## Context
This work extends the Hydro+ framework introduced by Rajagopal et al. (2019) by adding a freeze-out procedure that connects out-of-equilibrium critical fluctuations to observable hadron multiplicity cumulants. It contrasts with earlier work by Rajagopal et al. (2019) that used Model A dynamics (without conservation laws) and neglected freeze-out. The paper enables quantitative predictions for experimental searches for the QCD critical point in heavy-ion collisions, particularly at RHIC Beam Energy Scan, by showing how critical slowing down and baryon number conservation suppress fluctuation signals relative to equilibrium expectations. The results also connect to studies of thermal smearing ("blurring") effects by Ling et al. (2015) and Ohnishi et al. (2016) that translate spatial rapidity into kinematic particle rapidity.
