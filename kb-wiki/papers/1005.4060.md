# The Beam Thrust Cross Section for Drell-Yan at NNLL Order

> At the LHC and Tevatron strong initial-state radiation (ISR) plays an important role. It can significantly affect the partonic luminosity available to the hard interaction or contaminate a signal with additional jets and soft radiation. An ideal process to study ISR is isolated Drell-Yan production, pp -> X l+l- without central jets, where the jet veto is provided by the hadronic event shape beam thrust tau_B. Most hadron collider event shapes are designed to study central jets. In contrast, requiring tau_B << 1 provides an inclusive veto of central jets and measures the spectrum of ISR. For tau_B << 1 we carry out a resummation of alpha_s^n ln^m tau_B corrections at next-to-next-to-leading-logarithmic order. This is the first resummation at this order for a hadron-hadron collider event shape. Measurements of tau_B at the Tevatron and LHC can provide crucial tests of our understanding of ISR and of tau_B's utility as a central jet veto.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first complete resummation of large logarithms at next-to-next-to-leading-logarithmic (NNLL) order for a hadron-hadron collider event shape, specifically the beam thrust cross section for Drell-Yan production in the limit tau_B << 1, where the resummation is necessary to obtain reliable perturbative predictions and uncertainties.

## Method

The authors use the factorization theorem for beam thrust in Drell-Yan production, which separates the cross section into hard, beam, and soft functions evaluated at their natural scales (mu_H = Q, mu_B = sqrt(tau_B) Q, mu_S = tau_B Q) and evolved to a common scale via evolution kernels that sum the large logarithms. The NNLL resummation requires NLO expressions for the hard function, quark and gluon beam function Wilson coefficients, and soft function, together with NNLL evolution kernels. Perturbative uncertainties are estimated by varying the three scales mu_H, mu_B, and mu_S around their central values using three different variation schemes.

## Key numbers

- The cut tau_B <= 0.1 reduces the Drell-Yan cross section by a factor of about 1.3 above the Z peak at the LHC with E_cm = 7 TeV
- The cut tau_B <= 0.02 reduces the cross section by a factor of 5 to 1.5 above the Z peak
- Results are not plotted below Q tau_B = mu_S <= 1 GeV, where the soft function becomes nonperturbative and large corrections of order Lambda_QCD/mu_S are expected
- The MSTW2008 NLO PDFs are used with alpha_s(m_Z) = 0.1208
- Scale variations use r = {1/2, 2} in three different variation schemes (a), (b), and (c)

## Context

This work extends the state of the art for hadron collider event shape resummation, which was previously at next-to-leading logarithm (NLL) plus next-to-leading order (NLO) in the analysis by Banfi (2004). The beam thrust event shape was introduced by Stewart (2009), who also established the all-orders factorization theorem for small tau_B. The results enable experimental measurements of beam thrust at the Tevatron and LHC to test understanding of initial-state radiation, and provide a theoretically well-controlled jet veto with applications to processes such as Higgs production as studied by Berger (2010).
