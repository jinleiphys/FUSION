# Nucleons or diquarks? Competition between clustering and color superconductivity in quark matter

> We study the instabilities of quark matter in the framework of a generalized Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model, in order to explore possible competition between three-quark clustering to form nucleons and diquark formation leading to color superconductivity. Nucleon and $Δ$ solutions are obtained for the relativistic Faddeev equation at finite density and their binding energies are compared with those for the scalar and axial-vector diquarks found from the Bethe-Salpeter equation. In a model with interactions in both scalar and axial diquark channels, bound nucleons exist up to nuclear matter density. However, except at densities below about a quarter of that of nuclear matter, we find that scalar diquark formation is energetically favored. This raises the question of whether a realistic phase diagram of baryonic matter can be obtained from any model which does not incorporate color confinement.

## Key claim

The paper finds that in a generalized Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, bound nucleons exist up to nuclear matter density, but scalar diquark formation is energetically favored except at densities below about a quarter of nuclear matter density, raising the question of whether a realistic phase diagram of baryonic matter can be obtained from any model that does not incorporate color confinement.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for scalar and axial-vector diquarks and the relativistic Faddeev equation for nucleons and Delta baryons at finite density within an NJL model with separable interactions. The Faddeev equation was reduced to an effective two-body Bethe-Salpeter equation describing quark-diquark scattering, and the binding energies of three-quark clusters were compared with those of diquarks as a function of Fermi momentum. The calculations used a sharp 3-momentum cut-off regulator and two sets of parameters corresponding to constituent quark masses of 450 MeV and 500 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For the parameter set with M=450 MeV, nucleons are more stable than diquarks only for Fermi momenta smaller than about 130 MeV, which corresponds to 1/8 of nuclear matter density.
- For the parameter set with M=500 MeV, nucleons remain more stable than diquarks up to k_F=170 MeV, or 1/4 of nuclear matter density.
- Nuclear matter density corresponds to a quark Fermi momentum of k_F=270 MeV.
- Chiral symmetry restoration occurs at k_F=359 MeV for M=450 MeV and k_F=372 MeV for M=500 MeV.
- For M=400 MeV, the minimum value of g_S/g_pi for diquark binding is 0.4, and for g_S/g_pi=0.6 and g_S/g_pi=0.8 the scalar diquark masses are 699 MeV and 507 MeV respectively.
- The minimum coupling strength to bind the axial-vector diquark is g_A/g_pi=0.46.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of color superconductivity by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998) and Rapp, Schafer, Shuryak, and Velkovsky (1998), which focused on diquark condensation in quark matter, by also considering three-quark clustering into nucleons. The authors follow the formalism developed by Ishii, Bentz, and Yazaki (1995) for the Faddeev approach to baryons in the NJL model, generalizing it to finite density. The results suggest that any model without color confinement may be unable to produce a realistic phase diagram of baryonic matter, motivating future work that incorporates confinement mechanisms.
