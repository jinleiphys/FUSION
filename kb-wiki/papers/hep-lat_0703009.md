# BB Potentials in Quenched Lattice QCD

> The potentials between two B-mesons are computed in the heavy-quark limit using quenched lattice QCD at $m_π\sim 400~{\rm MeV}$. Non-zero central potentials are clearly evident in all four spin-isospin channels, (I,s_l) = (0,0) , (0,1) , (1,0) , (1,1), where s_l is the total spin of the light degrees of freedom. At short distance, we find repulsion in the $I\ne s_l$ channels and attraction in the I=s_l channels. Linear combinations of these potentials that have well-defined spin and isospin in the t-channel are found, in three of the four cases, to have substantially smaller uncertainties than the potentials defined with the s-channel (I,s_l), and allow quenching artifacts from single hairpin exchange to be isolated. The BB*πcoupling extracted from the long-distance behavior of the finite-volume t-channel potential is found to be consistent with quenched calculations of the matrix element of the isovector axial-current. The tensor potentials in both of the s_l = 1 channels are found to be consistent with zero within calculational uncertainties.

## Key claim

The paper computes potentials between two B-mesons in the heavy-quark limit using quenched lattice QCD at a pion mass of approximately 400 MeV, finding that the tensor potentials in both s_l = 1 channels are consistent with zero within calculational uncertainties, and that the long-distance behavior of the t-channel potentials allows extraction of the BB*pi coupling consistent with quenched calculations of axial matrix elements.

## Method

The authors used 284 quenched gauge configurations on a 16^3 x 32 lattice with the DBW2 action at beta = 1.04, corresponding to a lattice spacing of 0.0997 fm. Wilson light-quark propagators were generated from smeared sources, and heavy-quark propagators were constructed as Wilson lines with positive-energy projectors in the static limit. The BB potentials were extracted from two-point correlation functions of two static B-mesons separated by lattice vectors r = b(n,0,0) and r = b(0,0,n) for n = 0 through 8, plus additional off-axis separations, by computing the ground state energy difference between the two-meson system and two isolated B-mesons.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_pi = 402.5 ± 6.7 MeV

- Lambda_b - B mass difference: 474 ± 15 (stat) ± 14 (syst) MeV
- Sigma_b - B mass difference: 601 ± 16 (stat) ± 24 (syst) MeV
- Sigma_b - Lambda_b mass difference: 126 ± 19 (stat) ± 19 (syst) MeV

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of BB potentials by Richards (1990), Mihaly (1996), Stewart (1998), Michael (1999), Pennanen (1999), Green (1999), Fiebig (2001), Cook (2002), Takahashi (2006), and Doi (2006), which had large statistical uncertainties. The authors use a relatively small lattice volume to explore intermediate and short-distance components of the potential, and define t-channel potentials to isolate quenching artifacts from single hairpin exchange, as discussed by Beane (2002). The results enable future investigations of possible shallow molecular tetra-quark bound states analogous to the deuteron in the nucleon-nucleon sector.
