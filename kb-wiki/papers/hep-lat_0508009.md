# Extrapolation Formulas for Neutron EDM Calculations in Lattice QCD

> Lattice QCD is rapidly progressing toward being able to reliably compute the electric dipole moment of the neutron as a function of the strong CP-violating parameter theta. Present day calculations are performed at unphysical values of the light quark masses, in volumes that are not exceptionally large and at lattice spacings that are not exceptionally small. We use chiral perturbation theory to determine the leading contributions to the neutron electric dipole moment at finite volume, and in partially-quenched calculations.

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory provides extrapolation formulas that connect lattice QCD calculations of the neutron electric dipole moment (EDM) performed at unphysical quark masses and finite volumes to the physical infinite-volume value, but the leading-order one-loop contribution is large and the finite-volume corrections are correspondingly significant, unlike for nucleon mass or magnetic moment where loop contributions are subleading.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop diagrams contributing to the neutron EDM in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, both in standard QCD and in partially-quenched QCD (PQQCD), using the p-regime power counting for volumes where the spatial dimension L satisfies m_pi L >> 1 and the epsilon-prime regime for smaller volumes where m_pi L is of order unity or less. They derive analytic expressions for the finite-volume corrections as sums over modified Bessel functions K_0(m_pi L |n|) in the p-regime and as power-law 1/L^3 terms in the epsilon-prime regime, and they obtain the quark-mass dependence of the partially-quenched result including the logarithmic chiral logarithms and local counterterms.

## Key numbers

- One-loop contribution to the neutron EDM in QCD: d_n ~ -1.2 x 10^{-16} theta-bar e cm, using alpha = (M_p - M_n)/(m_u - m_d) ~ 0.26 with m_u = 5 MeV and m_d = 10 MeV
- Experimental upper limit: |d_n| < 6.3 x 10^{-26} e cm, implying |theta-bar| < 5 x 10^{-10}
- Pion decay constant: f ~ 132 MeV
- Axial coupling constant: g_A ~ 1.26
- Nucleon sigma term: sigma_N ranges between 45 +/- 8 MeV and 64 +/- 7 MeV
- In the epsilon-prime regime (m_pi L less than or similar to 1), the finite-volume neutron EDM scales as d_n^{(L)} proportional to 1/(m_pi^3 L^3)

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume and partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory framework previously applied to nucleon mass, magnetic moments, and axial current matrix elements (Beane 2004, Beane 2004) to the case of the neutron EDM from the QCD theta-term. The authors provide the explicit formulas needed to extrapolate lattice QCD calculations (such as those by Aoki 1989, Guadagnoli 2002, Berruto 2004, Shintani 2005) to physical quark masses and infinite volume, enabling a rigorous comparison with experimental limits from neutron EDM searches (Brad, Harris 1999). The results also reveal a pathology of partially-quenched theory: the one-loop contribution diverges as m_valence goes to zero for fixed sea quark mass, unlike the QCD case where it vanishes as m_pi^2 log(m_pi^2).
