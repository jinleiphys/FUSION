# Reaction cross sections of proton scattering from carbon isotopes (A=8-22) by means of the relativistic impulse approximation

> Reaction cross sections of carbon isotopes for proton scattering are calculated in large energy region. Density distributions of carbon isotopes are obtained from relativistic mean field results. Calculations are based on two procedures: the Glauber theory and relativistic impulse approximation, and results are compared with each other as well as with experimental data. A strong relationship between reaction cross section and root-mean-square radius is clearly shown for 12C using a model distribution.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates a strong, nearly unique relationship between the reaction cross section and the root-mean-square radius for carbon isotopes, but this relationship holds specifically when the root-mean-square radius is fixed and the diffuseness and half-density parameters of a Woods-Saxon distribution are varied; the relationship is less straightforward for proton-rich isotopes because the proton-proton scattering cross section is smaller than the proton-neutron cross section at low energies.

## Method

The author calculated reaction cross sections for proton scattering from carbon isotopes (A=8-22) using the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) with the IA2 parametrization of the NN amplitude, where nuclear densities were obtained from relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations. For comparison, Glauber model calculations were also performed using the same RMF vector densities. The RIA optical potential was constructed in momentum space using the t-rho form, then transformed to coordinate space to solve the Dirac equation, yielding Schrodinger-equivalent potentials for computing reaction cross sections and elastic scattering observables.

## Key numbers

- Reaction cross sections for 8-22C at 71 MeV range from 24.18 fm^2 (8C) to 63.87 fm^2 (22C); at 800 MeV they range from 24.52 fm^2 (8C) to 43.87 fm^2 (22C)
- Root-mean-square matter radius for 8C is 3.324 fm; for 22C it is 3.289 fm; for 12C it is 2.267 fm
- Root-mean-square charge radius for 12C from RMF is 2.417 fm, about 2% smaller than the reference value of 2.4702 fm from Angeli (2013)
- For 22C at 40 MeV, the Glauber calculation with RMF densities gives 810.4 mb, while the experimental value is 1338(274) mb
- For 19C and 20C at 40 MeV, Glauber calculations give 732.6 mb and 761.1 mb, respectively, compared to experimental values of 754(22) mb and 791(34) mb

## Context

This work extends previous studies of reaction cross sections for unstable nuclei using the Glauber model by Horii (2007), Ibraheem (2008), and Sharma (2013), and applies the relativistic impulse approximation approach previously used by the author for calcium isotopes (Kaki 1999, 2009), nickel isotopes (Kaki 2015), and Pb-208 (Kaki 2004). The results enable future determination of density distributions for carbon isotopes from reaction cross section measurements, and suggest that the RMF densities for 12C may be too compact and for 9C may give too large a matter radius compared to experimental data from Yamaguchi (2013).
