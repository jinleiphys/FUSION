# Meson production in high-energy electron-nucleus scattering

> Pseudoscalar mesons can be produced and studied in high-energy electron-nucleus scattering. We review and extend our previous analysis of meson production in the nuclear Coulomb field. The $P\rightarrow γγ$ decay rates are most directly determined for mesons produced in the double-Coulomb region where both photons are nearly real, and provided the background-hadronic contribution remains small. The larger the mass of the meson the higher the electron energy needed to assure such a condition.

## Key claim

The paper claims that pseudoscalar meson decay rates (P → γγ) can be most directly determined from mesons produced in the double-Coulomb region of high-energy electron-nucleus scattering, but only if the background hadronic contribution remains small; the required electron energy increases with meson mass to maintain this condition.

## Method

The author develops a Glauber-model description of pseudoscalar meson (π⁰, η, η′) production in electron-nucleus coherent scattering via two-photon fusion, extending previous Born-approximation work by Hadjimichael and Fallieros. The amplitude is decomposed into Coulomb (two-photon exchange) and hadronic (omega-meson exchange) contributions, with the latter Reggeized following the approach of Guidal et al. The cross-section distributions are derived including electron Coulomb distortion, pion-nucleus rescattering, and shadowing effects from intermediate rho-meson production.

## Key numbers

- For pion production at 11 GeV/c: double-Coulomb region has Q⊥ ≈ |Q∥| = 0.85 MeV/c and q⊥ ≈ me = 0.52 MeV/c
- For eta production at 11 GeV/c: double-Coulomb region has Q⊥ ≈ |Q∥| = 8.5 MeV/c and q⊥ ≈ me = 0.52 MeV/c
- For eta-prime production at 11 GeV/c: double-Coulomb region has Q⊥ ≈ |Q∥| = 43 MeV/c and q⊥ ≈ me = 0.52 MeV/c
- Ratio of Coulomb to hadronic amplitude for pions on lead: Rπ = 110 with omega exchange, Rπ = 910 with Regge exchange
- Ratio of Coulomb to hadronic amplitude for etas on lead: Rη = 1.8 with omega exchange, Rη = 15 with Regge exchange
- The inverse electron mass 1/me ≈ 390 fm sets the cutoff for the electron impact parameter integration
- For eta production, the inverse longitudinal momentum transfer 2K∥/mη² ≈ 14 fm sets the cutoff for the meson impact parameter integration

## Context

This work extends the author's previous Glauber-model analysis of meson photoproduction (Fäldt, 2011) to electroproduction, and builds on the Born-approximation treatment by Hadjimichael and Fallieros. The theoretical framework is motivated by the PrimEx Collaboration experiment at 11 GeV/c, which aims to measure electromagnetic properties of pseudoscalar mesons. The analysis enables determination of P→γγ decay constants (gπγγ, gηγγ, gη′γγ) from electroproduction data, provided the hadronic background can be controlled, and shows that factorization of the electroproduction amplitude into photoproduction and electron-scattering factors holds for eta and eta-prime but not for pions at 11 GeV/c.
