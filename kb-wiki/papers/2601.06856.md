# Probing the microscopic origin of prompt and non-prompt $D^{0}$ production through event-shape engineering in proton-proton collisions at the LHC

> Heavy-flavour hadrons are produced in the early stages of ultra-relativistic collisions at the LHC via hard partonic interactions and experience the whole system evolution. The study of prompt and non-prompt $D^{0}$ mesons provides an independent avenue to test the theories of quantum chromodynamics and to investigate beauty hadron production. Moreover, the production of both prompt and non-prompt $D^{0}$ is influenced by microscopic processes such as multi-partonic interactions (MPI) and hadronisation through fragmentation. In this study, an attempt is made to understand the production of prompt and non-prompt $D^{0}$ mesons in proton-proton collisions at $\sqrt{s}=13.6$ TeV using the PYTHIA8 event generator, which offers a qualitative description of charm production. The role of the transverse momentum transfer in the hardest partonic scattering ($\hat{p}_{\rm T}$), MPI, and color reconnection is systematically explored. In addition, the charged particle production in different topological regions with respect to the leading $D^{0}$ meson is studied to assess the influence of the $D^{0}$ meson on the event topology and to examine the selection biases arising from the use of charged particle multiplicity as an event classifier.

## Key claim

The study uses PYTHIA8 simulations to show that the production of prompt and non-prompt \(D^{0}\) mesons in proton-proton collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=13.6\) TeV is sensitive to microscopic processes such as multi-partonic interactions (MPI) and color reconnection (CR), but the abstract's claim of "qualitative description of charm production" hedges that these are Monte Carlo results, not experimental measurements.

## Method

The authors generated \(5.2 \times 10^{9}\) minimum-bias proton-proton collision events at \(\sqrt{s}=13.6\) TeV using the PYTHIA8 event generator (version 8.315) with the 4C tune, enabling hard QCD processes, charmonium and bottomonium production, and using the gluon-move color reconnection model. Events were classified by charged particle multiplicity using the FT0 detector acceptance and by transverse spherocity (\(S_{0}\)) to separate jetty (lowest 20% of \(S_{0}\)) from isotropic (highest 20% of \(S_{0}\)) events, and the production of prompt and non-prompt \(D^{0}\) mesons was studied as a function of \(\hat{p}_{\rm T}\) (momentum transfer in the hardest partonic scattering), \(N_{\rm mpi}\) (number of multi-partonic interactions), and \(p_{\rm T}^{D^{0}-{\rm lead}}\) (transverse momentum of the leading \(D^{0}\) meson), with comparisons between CR-on and CR-off configurations.

## Key numbers

* Self-normalised yield of non-prompt \(D^{0}\) increases more steeply with \(\hat{p}_{\rm T}\) than prompt \(D^{0}\), which in turn increases more steeply than charged hadrons, reflecting a mass ordering in pp collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=13.6\) TeV.
* \(\langle\hat{p}_{\rm T}\rangle\) for prompt \(D^{0}\) in the CR-off case is less than 5% higher than in the CR-on case, while no significant difference is observed for non-prompt \(D^{0}\).
* \(\langle N_{\rm mpi}\rangle\) saturates for \(p_{\rm T}^{D^{0}-{\rm lead}} \gtrsim 10\) GeV/\(c\) due to finite energy available in pp collisions.
* For prompt \(D^{0}\), \(\langle S_{0}\rangle\) saturates for \(p_{\rm T}^{D^{0}-{\rm lead}} \geq 8\) GeV/\(c\), while for non-prompt \(D^{0}\), \(\langle S_{0}\rangle\) remains fairly constant across all \(p_{\rm T}^{D^{0}-{\rm lead}}\).
* The self-normalised charged particle yield in the transverse region saturates beyond \(p_{\rm T}^{D^{0}-{\rm lead}} > 10\) GeV/\(c\) for the MPI-on case.

## Context

This work extends Monte Carlo studies of event-shape observables in small systems, particularly transverse spherocity as developed by Ortiz (2015) and applied in ALICE (2012) and Prasad (2025), into the heavy-flavour sector where such classifiers are "barely explored." The study builds on experimental observations from ALICE (2022, 2023) and CMS (2020, 2022) that prompt and non-prompt \(D^{0}\) mesons exhibit different flow coefficients and multiplicity-dependent yields, and it enables future experimental searches for QGP-like signatures in proton-proton collisions using event-shape engineering with heavy-flavour hadrons.
