# State dependence of tunneling processes and thermonuclear fusion

> We discuss the sensitivity of tunneling processes to the initial preparation of the quantum state. We compare the case of Gaussian wave packets of different positional variances using a generalised Woods-Saxon potential for which analytical expressions of the tunneling coefficients are available. Using realistic parameters for barrier potentials we find that the usual plane wave approximation underestimates fusion reactivities by an order of magnitude in a range of temperatures of practical relevance for controlled energy production.

# State dependence of tunneling processes and thermonuclear fusion

## Key claim
The usual plane wave approximation underestimates fusion reactivities by up to an order of magnitude for Gaussian wave packets with realistic positional variances, but only in a specific temperature range (10–100 keV) relevant to controlled thermonuclear fusion; at higher temperatures the enhancement disappears and the plane wave approximation becomes adequate.

## Method
The authors compute transmission coefficients for Gaussian wave packets of varying positional width ξ impinging on a one-dimensional Generalized Woods-Saxon (GWS) potential that admits exact analytical solutions for the tunneling coefficient T(E). They then calculate fusion reactivities by convolving the quantum-mechanical wave-vector distribution of the Gaussian packet with a classical Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution of the packet's mean energy, yielding an effective positional spreading ξ_eff that depends on temperature.

## Key numbers
- Barrier height of the GWS potential: 0.540 MeV (with parameters a=0.6 fm⁻¹, L=5 fm, V₀=45 MeV, W₀=56 MeV)
- Well depth: 40.336 MeV; well width: 7.35 fm
- At the lowest reported energies, the plane wave transmission coefficient underestimates the Gaussian wave packet transmission coefficient by five orders of magnitude (for ξ smaller than the well size)
- For a temperature-dependent positional spreading ξ = λ(β)/√2 (thermal wavelength), the fusion reactivity ratio (wave packet / plane wave) reaches about 1.5 in the 10–100 keV range, with a mild increase to almost 2 at higher temperatures before dropping below unity
- Reactivity enhancement depends on reduced mass: comparison shown for reduced masses of 1 a.m.u. and 6 a.m.u.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Dodonov and collaborators (Dodonov 1996, Andreata 2004, Dodonov 2014a, Dodonov 2014b) who showed that tunneling rates from Gaussian wave packets can differ by orders of magnitude from WKB predictions, but under specific conditions not covering the full parameter space. The present paper provides exact analytical results for arbitrary positional and momentum spreading using the GWS potential introduced in GWS (1995). The findings suggest that optimizing wave packet states could enable deuterium-deuterium fusion at more moderate plasma densities, as previously discussed by Onofrio (2023), and have implications for primordial nucleosynthesis models (Bonetti).
