# Valence quark and meson cloud contributions for the gamma* Lambda -> Lambda* and gamma* Sigma0 -> Lambda* reactions

> We estimate the valence quark contributions for the gamma* Y -> Lambda* (Y=Lambda, Sigma0) electromagnetic transition form factors. We focus particularly on the case Lambda*=Lambda(1670) as an analog reaction with gamma* N -> N(1535). The results are compared with those obtained from chiral unitary model, where the Lambda* resonance is dynamically generated and thus the electromagnetic structure comes directly from the meson cloud excitation of the baryon ground states. The form factors for the case Y=Sigma0 in particular, depend crucially on the two real phase (sign) combination, a phase between the Lambda and Lambda* states, and the other, the phase between the Lambda and Sigma0 radial wave functions. Depending on the combination of these two phases, the form factors for the gamma* Sigma0 -> Lambda* reaction can be enhanced or suppressed. Therefore, there is a possibility to determine the phase combination by experiments.

## Key claim
The valence quark model can account for the Dirac-type transition form factor \(F_1^\ast\) in the \(\gamma^\ast \Lambda \to \Lambda(1670)\) reaction, but the Pauli-type form factor \(F_2^\ast\) likely requires meson cloud contributions; for the \(\gamma^\ast \Sigma^0 \to \Lambda(1670)\) reaction, the sign of the form factors depends on the unknown combination of two relative phases, one between the \(\Lambda\) and \(\Lambda^\ast\) wave functions (\(\eta_{\Lambda^\ast}\)) and one between the \(\Lambda\) and \(\Sigma^0\) radial wave functions (\(\eta_{\Lambda\Sigma^0}\)), which could be determined by future experiments.

## Method
The authors used the covariant spectator quark model, representing baryons as a quark-diquark system with a vector meson dominance parametrization for the constituent quark electromagnetic form factors, to compute the valence quark contributions to the \(\gamma^\ast Y \to \Lambda^\ast\) (\(Y = \Lambda, \Sigma^0\)) transition form factors and helicity amplitudes. They compared these results with those from a chiral unitary model, where the \(\Lambda(1670)\) resonance is dynamically generated from meson-baryon coupled channels (\(\bar{K}N\), \(\pi\Sigma\), \(\eta\Lambda\), \(K\Xi\), \(\pi\Lambda\), \(\eta\Sigma\)) and the electromagnetic structure arises from meson cloud excitations.

## Key numbers
- The \(\Lambda(1670)\) mass is taken as \(M_{\Lambda^\ast} \simeq 1.670\) GeV; the model is applicable for \(Q^2 \gg 0.21\) GeV\(^2\) for the \(\gamma^\ast \Lambda \to \Lambda^\ast\) reaction and for \(Q^2 \gg 0.17\) GeV\(^2\) for the \(\gamma^\ast \Sigma^0 \to \Lambda^\ast\) reaction.
- The overlap integral \({\cal I}_Y(Q^2)\) is proportional to the photon three-momentum \(|{\bf q}|_Y\) for small \(|{\bf q}|_Y\); at \(Q^2=0\), \(|{\bf q}|_{0Y} = (M_{\Lambda^\ast}^2 - M_Y^2)/(2 M_{\Lambda^\ast})\) equals 0.21 GeV\(^2\) for \(Y=\Lambda\) and 0.17 GeV\(^2\) for \(Y=\Sigma^0\).
- The quark anomalous magnetic moments used are \(\kappa_u = 1.6690\), \(\kappa_d = 1.9287\), and \(\kappa_s = 1.4620\); the VMD parameters include \(c_+ = 4.160\), \(c_- = 1.160\), \(d_+ = d_- = -0.686\), \(c_0 = 4.427\), \(d_0 = -1.860\), and \(\lambda_q = 1.21\).

## Context
This work extends the valence quark model approach previously applied to the \(\gamma^\ast N \to N^\ast(1535)\) reaction (Ramalho et al., 2010) to the strange sector, focusing on the \(\Lambda(1670)\) as an analog of the \(N^\ast(1535)\). The results are compared with the chiral unitary model calculations of Doring et al. (2010) and Oset et al. (2001), where the \(\Lambda(1670)\) is treated as a dynamically generated meson-baryon molecular state. The study enables future experimental determination of the relative phase between the \(\Lambda\) and \(\Sigma^0\) wave functions, which is currently unknown and linked to the sign of the \(\gamma^\ast \Lambda \to \Sigma^0\) transition magnetic moment.
