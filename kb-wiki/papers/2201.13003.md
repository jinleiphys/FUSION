# An overview of recent STAR jet measurements

> These proceedings discuss recent jet measurements by the STAR experiment at RHIC to study jet substructure in p+p and jet quenching in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$ = 200 GeV. Furthermore, STAR's future plans for precision jet measurements with the upcoming data-taking periods in 2023-2025 are presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents recent STAR jet measurements at RHIC, claiming that jet substructure observables in p+p collisions show no pTjet dependence above 30 GeV/c and are more asymmetric than leading-order DGLAP splitting functions, while in Au+Au collisions strong jet suppression is observed with no jet radius dependence for inclusive jets but a potential radius dependence for gamma+jet recoil jets.

## Method

Jets were reconstructed using the anti-kT algorithm with resolution parameters R between 0.2 and 0.6, clustering charged tracks from the time projection chamber and neutral particles from the barrel electromagnetic calorimeter. SoftDrop jet grooming with zcut=0.1 and beta=0 was applied to study jet substructure observables (zg and Rg) and jet mass in p+p collisions. For heavy-ion collisions, inclusive jet spectra were measured with a high-pT leading hadron requirement (pT,leadmin=5 GeV/c) to suppress combinatorial background, while semi-inclusive hadron+jet and gamma+jet measurements used a mixed-event technique for background subtraction.

## Key numbers

- SoftDrop observables zg and Rg measured in p+p collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV for inclusive jets with R=0.2, 0.4, and 0.6, and 15 < pTjet < 60 GeV/c
- Jet mass distributions reported for p+p collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV with jets in range 30 < pTjet < 40 GeV/c
- Inclusive jet RAApythia measured in 0-10% central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(sNN)=200 GeV for R=0.2, 0.3, and 0.4 within 15 < pTjetch < 30 GeV/c, showing strong suppression with no jet radius dependence
- RCP for inclusive jets shows strong and similar suppression for R=0.2 and 0.3 jets at sqrt(sNN)=200 GeV
- Gamma+jet IAApythia for R=0.2 within 15 < ETtrig < 20 GeV shows stronger suppression than for R=0.5, hinting at potential radius dependence of recoil jet suppression at RHIC
- Trigger energy ranges: gamma and pi0 triggers with 9 < ETtrig < 20 GeV and 9 < ETtrig < 15 GeV respectively

## Context

These measurements extend STAR's earlier work on high-pT hadron suppression (STAR 2002) and dihadron correlations (STAR 2002, 2006) to fully reconstructed jets, providing more detailed information on jet quenching mechanisms. The p+p jet substructure results provide the first fully corrected SoftDrop observables at RHIC energy and serve as baseline for future heavy-ion measurements, while the inclusive jet suppression measurements show comparable suppression to charged hadrons at RHIC and similar magnitude to LHC Pb+Pb collisions. The results enable quantitative comparisons of jet energy loss between RHIC and LHC via the pTjet shift observable, and future high-statistics data-taking in 2023-2025 will enable precision jet measurements with extended kinematic coverage.
