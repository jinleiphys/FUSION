# On the Staggering Effect of Dynamical Moments of Inertia in Superdeformed Bands

> The possibility of the appearance of the C$_{4}$ symmetry in the rotational bands is studied within the particle-rotor model. Allowing for a slight triaxiality of the rotor it is shown that the lowest states of the system for a sufficiently high spin possess the symmetry which allows to distinguish states differing by two units of angular momentum.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the lowest states of a particle-rotor system at sufficiently high spin can possess an approximate C₄ symmetry that distinguishes states differing by two units of angular momentum, but only when a slight triaxiality of the rotor is allowed; for an axial rotor the symmetry is completely broken.

## Method

The author uses a particle-rotor model in the strong coupling limit, where a single particle occupying a j=11/2 shell is coupled to a triaxial core. The Hamiltonian includes rotational kinetic terms, mean-field quadrupole parameters, and a C₄-type deformation term. The Hamiltonian is diagonalized in a basis of rotor and single-particle states, and the measure of C₄ symmetry is defined as the absolute difference between the squared amplitudes of wave function components belonging to different representations of the C₄ group.

## Key numbers

- The staggering amplitude observed experimentally in superdeformed bands is of the order of 50 eV.
- Calculations were performed with parameters A₃ = 10 keV, A₁ = 21 keV, A₂ = 240 keV for three different sets of parameters.
- The quantity ||a|²−|b|²| is plotted versus total angular momentum and versus the ratio 𝒥₁/𝒥₃ for I = 101/2.

## Context

This work extends the study of ΔI=4 staggering observed in superdeformed bands of ¹⁴⁹Gd (Flibotte 1993), ¹⁵³Dy (Cederwall), ¹⁹⁴Hg (Cederwall 1994), and ¹³¹,¹³²Ce (Semple 1995). It contrasts with the approach of Hamamoto and Mottelson (1994, 1995), who used a quartic rotational Hamiltonian with C₄ perturbation along the symmetry axis, and with the model of Pavlichenkov and Flibotte (1995), where C₄ perturbation appears along the rotation axis. The paper enables further study of how triaxiality and the alignment of angular momentum generate the staggering effect without requiring a static hexadecapole deformation.
