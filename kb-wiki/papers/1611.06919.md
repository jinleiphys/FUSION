# In-medium rho-meson properties in a light-front approach

> Properties of \r{ho}-meson in symmetric nuclear matter are investigated within a light-front constituent quark model (LFCQM), using the in-medium input calculated by the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. The LFCQM used here was previously applied in vacuum to calculate the \r{ho}-meson electromagnetic properties, namely, charge G 0 , magnetic G 1 , and quadrupole G 2 form factors, as well as the electromagnetic radius and decay constant. We predict the in-medium modifications of the \r{ho}-meson electromagnetic form factors in symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the in-medium modifications of the rho-meson electromagnetic form factors in symmetric nuclear matter are appreciable, but the magnetic moment at Q^2 approximately zero shows only a 4.5% decrease relative to vacuum at the maximum nuclear density considered, indicating that the medium effect on the magnetic moment is not strong at that kinematic point.

## Method

The authors combine a light-front constituent quark model (LFCQM) previously applied to the rho-meson in vacuum with in-medium input from the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. The QMC model provides the effective constituent quark mass and effective rho-meson mass in symmetric nuclear matter at various densities, which are then used as inputs to the light-front calculation of the electromagnetic form factors G0, G1, and G2. The model parameters are fixed by reproducing the experimental rho-meson decay constant in vacuum (156 +/- 8 MeV) using quark mass m_q = 430 MeV and regulator mass m_R = 3.0 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum rho-meson decay constant: 156 +/- 8 MeV (used to fix model parameters)
- Quark mass used: m_q = 430 MeV; regulator mass: m_R = 3.0 GeV
- Vacuum rho-meson magnetic moment: approximately 2.20 [e/2m_rho]
- At maximum nuclear density considered, magnetic moment: approximately 2.10 [e/2m_rho], a decrease of 4.5% relative to vacuum
- Zero of the charge form factor in vacuum: q^2_zero approximately 3.0 GeV^2
- Normal nuclear matter density: rho_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}
- Effective nucleon mass at normal density for m_q = 430 MeV: m_N* = 565.3 MeV; nuclear incompressibility K = 361.4 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous LFCQM calculations for the rho-meson in vacuum (de Melo 1997, de Melo 2012) to symmetric nuclear matter, using the QMC model originally introduced by Guichon (1988) and Frederico et al. (1989). It is analogous to their earlier study of the pion in medium (de Melo 2014). The results enable future comparisons with experimental data on in-medium hadron properties and provide predictions for how the rho-meson electromagnetic structure is modified by the nuclear environment.
