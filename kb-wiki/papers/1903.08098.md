# Triangle singularities in $\bar{B}^0\to Ï‡_{c1}K^-Ï€^+$ relevant to $Z_1(4050)$ and $Z_2(4250)$

> $Z_1(4050)$ and $Z_2(4250)$ observed in $\bar{B}^0\toÏ‡_{c1}K^-Ï€^+$ by the Belle Collaboration are candidates of charged charmonium-like states that minimally includes two quarks and two antiquarks. While $Z_1(4050)$ and $Z_2(4250)$ have been interpreted as tetraquark states previously, we propose a completely different scenario based on a kinematical effect called the triangle singularity. We demonstrate that the triangle singularities cause in the $Ï‡_{c1}Ï€^+$ invariant mass distribution resonance-like bumps that fit very well the Belle data. If these bumps are simulated by the $Z_1(4050)$ and $Z_2(4250)$ resonance excitations, the spin-parity of them are predicted to be $1^-$ for $Z_1(4050)$ and $1^+$ or $1^-$ for $Z_2(4250)$. The bump corresponding to $Z_1(4050)$ has a highly asymmetric shape, which the Belle data exactly indicate. We show that the asymmetric shape originates from an interplay between the triangle singularity and the opening of the $X(3872)Ï€^+$ channel near the triangle-singularity energy. This characteristic lineshape could be used to discriminate different interpretations of $Z_1(4050)$. An interesting prediction from interpretting $Z_1(4050)$ and $Z_2(4250)$ as the triangle singularities is that similar bumps caused by the same mechanisms possibly appear also in $\bar{B}^0\to J/ÏˆK^-Ï€^+$ data; the already observed $Z_c(4200)$ corresponds to $Z_2(4250)$ of $J^P=1^+$.

## Key claim

The resonance-like structures previously interpreted as tetraquark states $Z_1(4050)$ and $Z_2(4250)$ in $\bar{B}^0\to\chi_{c1}K^-\pi^+$ can instead be explained as kinematical effects called triangle singularities, provided the triangle diagrams have experimentally detectable strengths; the spin-parity predictions are $1^-$ for $Z_1(4050)$ and $1^+$ or $1^-$ for $Z_2(4250)$ depending on the interaction used.

## Method

The author calculated the $\bar{B}^0\to\chi_{c1}K^-\pi^+$ decay amplitudes from triangle diagrams A and B, where diagram A involves $X(3872)$ and $K^*(892)$ in the loop and diagram B involves $\psi(3770)$ and $K^*_2(1430)$ in the loop. The amplitudes were computed using a general formula for triangle diagrams with form factors parametrized by a cutoff $\Lambda=1$ GeV, and the resulting $\chi_{c1}\pi^+$ invariant mass distributions were fitted with Breit-Wigner resonance forms to extract effective masses and widths. The $X(3872)\pi^+$ threshold effect was studied by artificially lowering the threshold energy and by turning off the on-shell $X(3872)\pi^+$ contribution.

## Key numbers

* Triangle diagram A produces a peak at $m_{\chi_{c1}\pi}\sim 4.02$ GeV; the fitted Breit-Wigner mass is $4041\pm 1$ MeV and width $115\pm 17$ MeV, compared to the Belle values of $4051\pm 14^{+20}_{-41}$ MeV and $82^{+21}_{-17}{}^{+47}_{-22}$ MeV for $Z_1(4050)$.
* Triangle diagram B produces a peak at $m_{\chi_{c1}\pi}\sim 4.22$ GeV; for $J^P=1^+$ the fitted mass is $4247\pm 53$ MeV and width $345\pm 67$ MeV, and for $J^P=1^-$ the fitted mass is $4309\pm 116$ MeV and width $468\pm 90$ MeV, compared to the Belle values of $4248^{+44}_{-29}{}^{+180}_{-35}$ MeV and $177^{+54}_{-39}{}^{+316}_{-61}$ MeV for $Z_2(4250)$.
* The $X(3872)\pi^+$ channel opens at $m_{\chi_{c1}\pi}\sim 4.01$ GeV, and the triangle singularity energy for diagram A is $\sim 4.025$ GeV; lowering the $X(3872)\pi^+$ threshold by 50, 100, and 150 MeV progressively moderates the asymmetric rise of the bump.
* The cutoff $\Lambda$ was varied over 1.0, 1.5, and 2.0 GeV, and the peak positions from diagram A remained almost the same while the width broadened somewhat.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous application of triangle singularities to $Z_c(4430)$ and $Z_c(4200)$ in $\bar{B}^0\to\psi(2S)K^-\pi^+$ and $J/\psi K^-\pi^+$ (Nakamura, 2019), and contradicts the tetraquark interpretations of $Z_1(4050)$ and $Z_2(4250)$ proposed by earlier diquark-antidiquark and QCD sum rule studies (various authors, 2008-2015). The results enable experimental discrimination between kinematical and genuine resonance interpretations by searching for the characteristic asymmetric lineshape in higher-statistics Belle data and by predicting similar bumps in $\bar{B}^0\to J/\psi K^-\pi^+$, where $Z_c(4200)$ would correspond to $Z_2(4250)$ with $J^P=1^+$.
