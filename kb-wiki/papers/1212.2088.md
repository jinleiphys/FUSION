# On the toroidal nature of the low-energy E1 mode

> The nature of E1 low-energy strength (LES), often denoted as a "pygmy dipole resonance", is analyzed within the random-phase-approximation (RPA) in $^{208}$Pb using Skyrme forces in fully self-consistent manner. A first overview is given by the strength functions for the dipole, compressional and toroidal operators. More detailed insight is gained by averaged transition densities and currents where the latter provide very illustrative flow pattern. The analysis reveals clear isoscalar toroidal flow in the low-energy bin 6.0-8.8 MeV of the LES and a mixed isoscalar/isovector toroidal/compression flow in the higher bin 8.8-10.5 MeV. Thus the modes covered by LES embrace both vortical and irrotational motion. The simple collective picture of the LES as a "pygmy" mode (oscillations of the neutron excess against the nuclear core) is not confirmed.

## Key claim

The low-energy E1 strength (LES) in \(^{208}\)Pb, often called the "pygmy dipole resonance," is not a simple oscillation of the neutron excess against the nuclear core; instead, the analysis of transition currents reveals that the LES at 6.0-8.8 MeV is dominated by isoscalar toroidal (vortical) flow, while the higher bin 8.8-10.5 MeV shows a mixed isoscalar/isovector toroidal/compression flow.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent random-phase-approximation (RPA) calculations for \(^{208}\)Pb using the Skyrme functional SLy6, with a 1D spherical coordinate-space grid (mesh 0.3 fm, box 21 fm) and a large configuration space including 1ph states up to \(\sim\)35 MeV plus fluid dynamical basis modes up to \(\sim\)200 MeV. They computed strength functions for the dipole (E1), toroidal (TR), and compressional (CR) operators, and then constructed averaged transition densities and current transition densities (velocity fields) summed over RPA states in two energy bins (6.0-8.8 MeV and 8.8-10.5 MeV), weighted by isovector dipole (\(D_1\)) and isoscalar compressional (\(D_0\)) probe operators.

## Key numbers

- The low-energy bin is defined as 6.0-8.8 MeV and the higher bin as 8.8-10.5 MeV.
- The E1(T=1) strength function shows two peaks in the LES region at 7.5 MeV and 10.3 MeV.
- The toroidal T=0 strength is strictly peaked at 7-8 MeV, dominating over the compressional T=0 strength in that region.
- The experimental giant dipole resonance (GDR) centroid and width are used as a benchmark; the computed GDR agrees with experiment (no explicit numerical value given for the experimental centroid, but the line and arrow in Fig. 2a indicate it).
- The experimental low- and high-energy branches of the isoscalar giant dipole resonance (ISGDR) from Uchida (2004) are at 12-14 MeV and higher energy, respectively; the computed TR(T=0) peak at 7-8 MeV lies below this range.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the low-energy E1 strength in \(^{208}\)Pb, such as those by Vretenar (2001) and Paar (2007), which interpreted the LES as a pygmy dipole resonance (PDR). It directly contradicts the simple PDR picture by showing that the flow pattern is predominantly toroidal, consistent with earlier suggestions of toroidal and compressional contributions by Kvasil (2011, 2012) and by Ryczayeva (2002). The results enable future experimental investigation of LES flows using \((e,e')\) reactions, which can probe both the nuclear surface and interior, rather than peripheral reactions like \((\alpha, \alpha')\) that are sensitive mainly to the surface.
