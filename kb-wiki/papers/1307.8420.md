# Role of vector and pseudoscalar mesons in understanding $1/2^-$ $N^*$ and $Δ$ resonances

> A study of nonstrange meson-baryon systems has been made with the idea of understanding the properties of the low-lying $1/2^-$ $N^*$ and $Δ$ resonances. The coupled channels are built by considering the pseudoscalar and vector mesons together with the octet baryons. The formalism is based on obtaining the interactions from the lowest order chiral Lagrangian when dealing with pseudoscalar mesons and relying on the hidden local symmetry in case of the vector mesons. The transition between the two systems is obtained by replacing the photon by a vector meson in the Kroll-Ruderman theorem for the photoproduction of pseudoscalar mesons. The subtraction constants, required to calculate the loop-function in the scattering equations, are constrained by fitting the available experimental data on some of the reactions with pseudoscalar meson-baryon final states. As a consequence, we find resonances which can be related to $N^*(1535)$, $N^*(1650)$ (with a double pole structure), $N^*(1895)$ and $Δ(1620)$. We conclude that these resonances can be, at least partly, interpreted as dynamically generated resonances and that the vector mesons play an important role in determining the dynamical origin of the low-lying $N^*$ and $Δ$ states.

## Key claim

The authors find that the low-lying $1/2^-$ $N^*$ and $\Delta$ resonances can be at least partly interpreted as dynamically generated from meson-baryon interactions, but only when vector mesons are coupled to pseudoscalar meson-baryon channels; the subtraction constants required to fit experimental data differ from the "natural" scheme, indicating that these resonances cannot be interpreted as purely dynamically generated states and that some information is missing in the formalism.

## Method

The authors solve coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equations for nine channels (πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ, ρN, ωN, φN, K*Λ, K*Σ) in isospin 1/2 and 3/2 configurations. Pseudoscalar-baryon interactions are obtained from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian, vector meson-baryon interactions from hidden local symmetry including s-, t-, u-channel and contact terms, and PB-VB transition amplitudes from a Kroll-Ruderman-type Lagrangian with the vector meson as a gauge boson. The subtraction constants for loop regularization are either set to "natural" values following Hyodo-Hosaka (2010) or fitted to experimental data on πN amplitudes and π⁻p → ηn, π⁻p → K⁰Λ cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Pole found at 1504 - i55 MeV, associated with N*(1535); branching ratios calculated as 43% to πN and 55% to ηN
- Twin pole structure for N*(1650) found at 1668 - i28 MeV and 1673 - i67 MeV, similar to poles found by Arndt (2006) at 1673 - i41 and 1689 - i96 MeV
- Additional poles found at 1801 - i96 MeV and 1912 - i54 MeV, associated with N*(1895)
- One isospin 3/2 pole found at 1689 - i56 MeV, associated with Δ(1620)
- The Kroll-Ruderman coupling constant g_KR = m_ρ/(√2 f_π) ≈ 6, using f_π = 93 MeV and m_ρ = 770 MeV
- Natural subtraction constants range from -0.3976 (πN) to -1.91566 (φN) for PB channels and from -1.5843 (ρN) to -1.91566 (φN) for VB channels
- Fitted subtraction constants range from -4.476 (KΛ) to -0.184 (K*Λ); all are negative numbers

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of meson-baryon dynamics (Khemchandani et al., 2011, 2012, 2013) from strange to nonstrange systems, and addresses the finding by Hyodo and Hosaka (2010) that N*(1535) does not fit well as a dynamically generated resonance in pseudoscalar-baryon systems alone. The results contradict earlier work by Inoue (2002) which found that PB systems based on the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian could not reproduce data beyond 1550 MeV, and show that including vector mesons improves agreement with experimental data up to about 2 GeV. The work enables future studies incorporating the ππN channel, which has been found to dynamically generate N*(1710) in other approaches.
