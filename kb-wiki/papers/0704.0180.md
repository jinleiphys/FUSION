# Neutron Skin and Giant Resonances in Nuclei

> Some aspects, both experimental and theoretical, of extracting the neutron skin $ΔR$ from properties of isovector giant resonances are discussed. Existing proposals are critically reviewed. The method relying on the energy difference between the GTR and IAS is shown to lack sensitivity to $ΔR$. A simple explanation of the linear relation between the symmetry energy and the neutron skin is also given.

# Neutron Skin and Giant Resonances in Nuclei

## Key claim

The method relying on the energy difference between the Gamow-Teller resonance (GTR) and the isobaric analogue state (IAS) is shown to lack sensitivity to the neutron skin ΔR, contrary to claims in the literature; the energy splitting is dominated by spin-orbit and residual interaction terms that do not depend on ΔR, and even under optimistic assumptions the theoretical uncertainties are comparable to the experimental ones.

## Method

The author employs a Landau-Migdal mean-field model with a Hamiltonian consisting of a single-particle mean field and a residual particle-hole interaction parameterized by Landau-Migdal parameters F' and G'. The neutron skin ΔR is calculated as a function of the dimensionless parameter f' = F'/(300 MeV fm^3), and the linear correlation between ΔR and the symmetry energy is derived via the Migdal relation a_4 = (ε_F/3)(1+2f'). The sensitivity of the GTR-IAS energy spacing to ΔR is evaluated analytically using sum-rule estimates and a simple model where the neutron density is varied while keeping the difference G' - F' fixed.

## Key numbers

- The symmetry energy at saturation density is a_4 ≈ 30 MeV, with saturation density ρ_0 ≈ 0.17 fm^{-3}
- In ^208Pb, the isovector monopole resonance (IMR) excitation energy is about 24 MeV
- For ^208Pb with R_p = 5.5 fm and ΔR = 0.2 fm, the ΔR-dependent term in the spin-dipole sum rule is only 25% of the leading term; 5% accuracy in S^{(-)} is needed to determine ΔR with 20% accuracy
- The IAS energy can be determined experimentally with better than 0.1% accuracy
- The spin-orbit contribution to the GTR-IAS splitting is about 5 MeV; the particle-hole contribution is about 1-2 MeV in absolute value
- In Sn isotopes with R_p ≈ 4.6 fm, a variation of ΔR of about 0.1 fm causes a relative change δΔE_ph/ΔE_ph of 0.3 for ^112Sn and 0.15 for ^132Sn, corresponding to an absolute change of about 0.3 MeV in ΔE
- The parity non-conserving amplitude correction in ^133Cs is δE/E ≈ -(0.1-0.4)% depending on the ΔR estimate

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Dieperink (2003) on the relationship between the symmetry energy and neutron skin, and critically reviews proposals by Krasznahorkay (1999) using spin-dipole resonances and by Ring (2003) using the GTR-IAS energy spacing. The author shows that the linear correlation between ΔR and the symmetry energy observed in mean-field calculations by Brown (2000) and Furnstahl (2002) can be understood within the Landau-Migdal approach. The paper enables a more cautious assessment of indirect methods for determining neutron skin, highlighting that parity-violating electron scattering remains the least model-dependent approach, though with claimed accuracy of 1% in neutron radii translating to 20-30% uncertainty in the skin.
