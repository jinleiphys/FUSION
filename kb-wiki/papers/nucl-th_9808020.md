# Role of the $Δ$ isobar in the reaction $NN \to NNπ$ near threshold

> A model calculation for pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions is presented. Direct production, pion rescattering and contributions from pair diagrams are taken into account. The amplitudes for the elementary processes are based on well established microscopic models of nucleon-nucleon and pion-nucleon scattering. The $Δ(1232)$ is included explicitly and is found to play an important role even at energies close to production threshold. A good overall agreement with existing data from the pion production threshold up to the Delta resonance region is achieved.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Δ(1232) isobar plays an important role in pion production near threshold, but this claim is qualified: the Δ contributions are moderate compared to some earlier reports (e.g., Niskanen), and for the reaction pp → ppπ⁰ the Δ actually reduces the cross section near threshold by about 20%, contrary to what one might expect.

## Method

The authors perform a distorted wave Born approximation calculation in momentum space for the reactions pp → ppπ⁰, pp → pnπ⁺, pp → dπ⁺, and pn → ppπ⁻. They include direct production, pion rescattering, pair diagrams, and explicit Δ(1232) excitation, using the CCF coupled-channel NN potential (derived from the full Bonn model) for initial and final state distortions and a meson-exchange πN T-matrix from the Jülich group. All NN and πN partial waves up to orbital angular momentum L=2 and all states with relative orbital angular momentum l≤2 between the NN system and the pion are considered in the final state.

## Key numbers

- For the πNN coupling constant: f²_NNπ/4π = 0.0778
- For the πNΔ coupling constant: f²_NΔπ/4π = 0.26
- Monopole form factor cutoff mass Λ_π = 900 MeV at the πNN and πNΔ vertices
- For the ω exchange HME contribution: g²_ωNN/4π = 10 and Λ_ωNN = 1.5 GeV (monopole form factor)
- The Δ isobar reduces the pp → ppπ⁰ cross section near threshold by about 20%
- The Δ increases the pp → dπ⁺ cross section near threshold by about 30%
- The basic model for pp → ppπ⁰ (direct production plus rescattering) yields only about 60% of the measured cross section
- The Δ width influences observables only for energies T_lab ≥ 420 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier s-wave pion production studies by the same group (Hanhart et al., 1997) to higher partial waves, and addresses the controversy between the heavy meson exchange (HME) mechanism proposed by Lee and Riska (1993) and Horowitz et al. (1993) and the off-shell πN amplitude approach of Hernandez and Oset (1995). The model enables predictions for differential cross sections and spin-dependent observables that can discriminate between production mechanisms, and the authors note that their results for the Δ contribution differ significantly from those of Niskanen (1996), who found a factor of 3 increase in the dπ⁺ cross section near threshold.
