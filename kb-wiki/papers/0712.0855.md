# Ab initio no-core shell model and microscopic reactions: recent achievements

> We report on recent microscopic calculations of reaction properties based upon the nuclear structure of the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM).

## Key claim

The paper reports that *ab initio* calculations of nuclear reaction properties can be performed by combining the no-core shell model (NCSM) with either the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method or the resonating-group method (RGM), with results that show overall good agreement with experiment, though the near-threshold experimental situation for the \(^4\)He photo-absorption cross section remains controversial.

## Method

The authors applied the NCSM/LIT approach to compute the \(^4\)He total photo-absorption cross section in the unretarded dipole approximation, using chiral effective field theory two-nucleon (NN) forces at N\(^3\)LO and three-nucleon (NNN) forces at N\(^2\)LO, with three-body effective interactions for convergence. Separately, they developed the NCSM/RGM approach for reactions involving two nuclear fragments, using *ab initio* NCSM wave functions for each cluster and effective interactions derived from realistic forces, and applied it to low-energy neutron-\(^4\)He scattering.

## Key numbers

- The \(^4\)He photo-absorption cross section peaks at an excitation energy of \(\omega = 27.8\) MeV, with the peak height mildly sensitive to the NNN force.
- The \(^2S_{1/2}\) phase shift for the \(n+^4\)He system was computed using the \(V_{lowk}\) potential at \(\hbar\Omega = 18\) MeV in a \(16\hbar\Omega\) model space, and the \(\chi\)EFT NN interaction at \(\hbar\Omega = 19\) MeV in a \(14\hbar\Omega\) model space, showing overall good agreement with experiment.

## Context

This work extends the *ab initio* NCSM approach to reaction calculations, building on prior NCSM structure calculations (NCSM, 2000) and the LIT method (LIT, 1994) and RGM (RGM, 1970). The NCSM/LIT calculation of the \(^4\)He photo-absorption cross section uses chiral EFT interactions from Entem (2003) and NNN forces from the N\(^2\)LO order, and the results are compared to experimental data from Nilsson et al. and Nakayama et al. The NCSM/RGM approach enables future *ab initio* studies of low-energy scattering and reactions involving light nuclei.
