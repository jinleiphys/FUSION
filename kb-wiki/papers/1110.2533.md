# Physical Degrees of Freedom for Gauge Fields and the Issue of Spin

> The conflict between the physical degrees of freedom of gauge bosons and the Lorentz group irreps naturally used to describe their couplings to matter fields are illustrated and discussed, and applied to issues of linear and angular momentum.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the usual gauge-invariant decomposition of angular momentum in QED and QCD fails because the individual components do not obey the canonical commutation relations for angular momentum; this conflict can be resolved by separating the gauge field into a physical (transverse) part and a pure-gauge (longitudinal) part, using the existence of a spin-one irreducible representation of the Lorentz group that carries only the physical degrees of freedom of a vector boson.

## Method

The author first illustrates the spin "loss" of a Dirac fermion when viewed from a boosted frame, using explicit spinor transformations. He then constructs a parity-conserving spin-one representation of the Lorentz group that in the massless limit yields only two transverse polarizations, matching the physical degrees of freedom of a photon. Using this representation, he defines a decomposition of the gauge field into a physical part (transverse, curl-free) and a pure-gauge part (longitudinal, divergence-free), and applies this decomposition to construct gauge-invariant angular momentum and linear momentum operators that satisfy the canonical commutation relations. The same approach is extended to QCD using covariant derivatives that include only the pure-gauge part of the gluon field.

## Key numbers

- For a hydrogenic atom with nuclear charge Z, the matrix element of the spin operator Σ₃ or the rotation generator σ₁₂ after angular integration is 1/(1+[(1-γ)/(Zα)]²) < 1, where γ = √(1−Z²α²).
- The boost of a spin-up Dirac fermion at rest to a frame with momentum p and energy E produces a spinor with lower components proportional to p/(E+m), showing that the matrix element of the rotation generator or the axial current is not preserved depending on boost direction.
- The massless limit of the spin-one (1,0)⊕(0,1) representation yields two transverse polarization states: [1,0,0,0,0,1]ᵀ and [0,0,1,1,0,0]ᵀ.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier decomposition (Goldman, 2011) of gauge fields into physical and pure-gauge parts, and addresses the long-standing problem that the gauge-invariant angular momentum operators proposed by Ji (1997) do not obey angular momentum commutation relations. The paper contrasts with the light-cone approach of Jaffe and Manohar (1990), the classic gauge-invariant operators of Leader (2011), and the decomposition of Wakamatsu (2010) and Cho et al. (2011), all of which the author argues either violate the commutation algebra or conflict with Lorentz invariance. The proposed decomposition enables a consistent physical interpretation of spin and orbital angular momentum contributions in QED and QCD, and also resolves the issue of the Pauli-Dirac Hamiltonian dichotomy by identifying a physical momentum operator.
