# Limits of Time-Reversal Violating Interaction from Compound Nuclear Experiments

> Mean square matrix elements of the time reversal invariance violating (TRIV) interaction between the compound nuclear states are calculated within the statistical model, using the explicit form of the TRIV interaction via the $ρ$-meson exchange. From the comparison of the calculated values with the data known for $p+ ^{27}Al \Leftrightarrow ^{24}Mg + α$ reaction, and for $γ$-correlation measurements in $^{113}Cd(n,γ)^{114}Cd$ process, the bounds on the TRIV constant are obtained ${\bar g}_ρ \alt 1.8 \times 10^{-2}$ and ${\bar g}_ρ \alt 1.1 \times 10^{-2}$. The sensitivity of the recently proposed detailed balance test experiments on isolated resonances in $^{32}S$ to the value of ${\bar g}_ρ$ is shown to be as high as to reach values ${\bar g}_ρ \sim 10^{-4}$.

## Key claim

The paper calculates mean square matrix elements of the time-reversal invariance violating (TRIV) interaction between compound nuclear states using a statistical model with the explicit form of the TRIV interaction via rho-meson exchange, and from comparison with experimental data obtains bounds on the TRIV constant that are close to those from atomic dipole moment data, while also showing that proposed detailed balance experiments on isolated resonances in sulfur-32 could reach sensitivity down to values of the constant around 10^{-4}.

## Method

The author computed the root mean square matrix element of the TRIV interaction between compound nuclear states using a statistical method based on the equivalence of canonical and microcanonical ensembles, with the TRIV interaction given by a rho-meson exchange Hamiltonian characterized by a single dimensionless constant g-bar-rho. The calculation employed a Woods-Saxon single-particle basis with spin-orbit interaction, included short-range correlations via the Jastrow method, and used the Landau-Migdal particle-hole interaction to compute the root mean square of the strong interaction matrix elements needed for comparison with experimental asymmetry parameters. The results were compared with data from the p + Al-27 to Mg-24 + alpha detailed balance test and from gamma-correlation measurements in Cd-113(n,gamma)Cd-114, and the sensitivity was estimated for proposed detailed balance tests on isolated resonances in sulfur-32.

## Key numbers

* For the p + Al-27 to Mg-24 + alpha reaction in the compound nucleus Si-28: alpha = 1.44 x 10^{-2} g-bar-rho, giving the bound g-bar-rho less than 1.8 x 10^{-2}
* For gamma-correlation measurements in Cd-113(n,gamma)Cd-114: v_TRIV = g-bar-rho x 0.11 x 10^3 eV, giving the bound g-bar-rho less than 1.1 x 10^{-2}
* For the proposed detailed balance test on isolated resonances in S-32: the sensitivity could reach values of g-bar-rho around 10^{-4}, with the expected v_TRIV ranging from 6.7 eV down to 0.67 eV for a measurable asymmetry Delta of about 10^{-2}
* The experimental limit on the asymmetry parameter alpha from the improved detailed balance experiment was alpha less than 2.6 x 10^{-4} at 80 percent confidence level

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Haxton, Horing and Musolf (1994) who obtained bounds on g-bar-rho from atomic dipole moment data (g-bar-rho less than 9.3 x 10^{-3}), and the calculations of Engel, Gould and Hnizdo (1994) who used the rho-meson exchange TRIV interaction to obtain an effective optical potential. The paper shows that bounds from compound nuclear experiments can compete with atomic constraints, and that the proposed detailed balance experiments on S-32 by Drake, Mitchell and collaborators could probe values of g-bar-rho down to 10^{-4}, approaching the theoretical expectations from effective Lagrangian considerations by Engel, Friar and Schiavilla (1995) which suggested no TRIV effects above g-bar-rho around 10^{-6}.
