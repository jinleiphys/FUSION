# Confronting fluctuations of conserved charges in central nuclear collisions at the LHC with predictions from Lattice QCD

> We construct net baryon number and strangeness susceptibilities as well as correlations between electric charge and strangeness from experimental data of the ALICE Collaboration at the CERN LHC. The data were taken in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.76 TeV. The resulting fluctuations and correlations are consistent with Lattice QCD results at the chiral crossover pseudocritical temperature $T_c\simeq 155$ MeV. This agreement lends strong support to the assumption that the fireball created in these collisions is of thermal origin and exhibits characteristic properties expected in QCD at the transition from the quark gluon plasma to the hadronic phase. The volume of the fireball for one unit of rapidity at $T_c$ is found to exceed 4000 fm$^3$. A detailed discussion on uncertainties in the temperature and volume of the fireball is presented. The results are linked to pion interferometry measurements and predictions from percolation theory.

## Key claim

The second-order fluctuations and correlations of conserved charges (baryon number, strangeness, electric charge) extracted from ALICE Pb-Pb collision data at 2.76 TeV are consistent with Lattice QCD predictions at the chiral crossover pseudocritical temperature T_c approximately 155 MeV, but only within a temperature window of 0.15 to 0.163 GeV where hadronic degrees of freedom remain valid.

## Method

The authors constructed net baryon number and strangeness susceptibilities, as well as electric charge-strangeness correlations, from inclusive particle yields measured by the ALICE Collaboration at midrapidity in central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV. They assumed a Skellam probability distribution for net charge fluctuations, which allows second-order cumulants to be expressed directly in terms of mean particle and antiparticle yields per unit rapidity. The resulting susceptibility ratios were compared to continuum-extrapolated Lattice QCD results at various temperatures, and the fireball volume was extracted by matching experimental susceptibilities to LQCD values.

## Key numbers

- Ratio chi_B/chi_S = 0.404 +/- 0.028 from ALICE data, compared to LQCD at T=155 MeV
- Ratio chi_B/chi_{QS} = 1.141 +/- 0.1266 from ALICE data, compared to LQCD at T=155 MeV
- Lower limit on (-chi_{BS}/chi_S) > 0.193 +/- 0.0127 from ALICE data
- Fireball volume for one unit of rapidity at T_c exceeds 3000 fm^3 (abstract) and is found in the range 5000 > V >= 3000 fm^3 (full text)
- Total particle number per unit rapidity <N_t> = 2486 +/- 146 in central Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV
- Critical percolation density n_c^{per} = 0.53 +/- 0.024 fm^{-3} for hadrons with proton strong interaction radius sqrt(<r_p^2>) = 0.67 +/- 0.02 fm
- HBT volume from ALICE two-pion correlations: V_{HBT} = 4800 +/- 580 fm^3 for 0-5% centrality

## Context

This work extends the approach of using conserved charge fluctuations as probes of QCD phase structure, previously applied at RHIC energies by the STAR Collaboration (Aggarwal 2010, Adamczyk 2014), to LHC energies. The method connects experimental data directly to Lattice QCD calculations from the HotQCD collaboration (Bazavov 2012, Bazavov 2014) without requiring event-by-event fluctuation measurements or acceptance corrections. The results enable determination of the chemical freezeout temperature and volume at LHC energies, and are linked to pion interferometry measurements and percolation theory predictions for deconfinement (Castorina). The temperature window 0.15 < T < 0.163 GeV identified here is consistent with thermal model analyses of particle yields (Stachel 2013).
