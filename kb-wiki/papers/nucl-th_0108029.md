# Saturation properties of nuclear matter and correlated nucleons

> A brief overview is given of the properties of spectral functions in finite nuclei as obtained from (e,e'p) experiments. Based on recent experimental data from this reaction it is argued that the empirical value of the saturation density of nuclear matter is dominated by short-range correlations. This observation and the observed fragmentation and depletion of the single-particle strength in nuclei provide the motivation for attempting a self-consistent description of the nucleon spectral functions with full inclusion of short-range and tensor correlations in nuclear matter. Results for these ``second generation'' spectral functions will be discussed with emphasis on the consequences for the saturation properties of nuclear matter. Arguments are presented to clarify the obscuring role of pionic long-range correlations in this long-standing problem.

## Key claim
The empirical saturation density of nuclear matter (0.16 nucleons/fm³, corresponding to k_F = 1.33 fm⁻¹) is dominated by short-range correlations (SRC), and a self-consistent treatment of nucleon spectral functions that includes only SRC, while discarding long-range pionic correlations, can yield reasonable saturation properties.

## Method
The authors performed self-consistent calculations of nucleon spectral functions in nuclear matter by solving the ladder equation (Eq. 1) with fully dressed particle and hole propagators, using the Reid interaction. The self-energy was parametrized with two Gaussians above and two below the Fermi energy, including contributions from relative S-waves and the tensor-coupled ³D₁ channel. Self-consistency was achieved iteratively (about ten steps) at each density, with higher partial waves (L=2,3) included after convergence of the dominant S-waves.

## Key numbers
- The empirical central density of heavy nuclei is 0.16 nucleons/fm³, corresponding to k_F = 1.33 fm⁻¹.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, the spectroscopic factor for the last 3s₁/₂ proton is 0.65, and the occupation number for that orbit is about 0.75.
- All deeply-bound proton orbitals in ²⁰⁸Pb are depleted by about 15% due to short-range correlations.
- In ¹⁶O, quasihole states contribute only 37% of the total binding energy, with 63% coming from continuum terms (high-momentum components).
- Self-consistent results were obtained at two densities: k_F = 1.36 fm⁻¹ and k_F = 1.45 fm⁻¹.
- Three hole-line calculations (Baldo et al.) yield saturation at k_F = 1.565 fm⁻¹ with binding energy -16.18 MeV, requiring additional physics (three-body forces or relativistic effects) to match empirical saturation.

## Context
This work extends earlier approximate implementations of nuclear-matter spectral functions, such as those using mean-field propagators (Angels et al., 1990) or parametrized spectral functions (Dickhoff, 1999). It directly contradicts the conventional hole-line expansion (Baldo et al., 2001) and variational calculations (Day, 1981) that include long-range pionic correlations, which overestimate saturation density. The results enable a new approach to the nuclear saturation problem that focuses solely on short-range correlations, with the expectation that three-body forces in finite nuclei remain slightly attractive beyond A=10 (Wiringa et al., 1995).
