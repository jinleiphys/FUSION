# A complete set of in-medium splitting functions to any order in opacity

> In this Letter we report the first calculation of all ${\cal O}(α_s)$ medium-induced branching processes to any order in opacity. Our splitting functions results are presented as iterative solutions to matrix equations with initial conditions set by the leading order branchings in the vacuum. The flavor and quark mass dependence of the in-medium $q \rightarrow qg$, $g\rightarrow gg$, $q \rightarrow g q$, $g \rightarrow q\bar{q}$ processes is fully captured by the light-front wavefunction formalism and the color representation of the parent and daughter partons. We include the explicit solutions to second order in opacity as supplementary material and present numerical results in a realistic strongly-interacting medium produced in high center-of-mass energy heavy ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider. Our numerical simulations show that the second order in opacity corrections can change the energy dependence of the in-medium shower intensity. We further find corrections to the longitudinal and angular distributions of the in-medium splitting kernels that may have important implications for jet substructure phenomenology.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first calculation of all O(α_s) medium-induced branching processes to any order in opacity, extending beyond the soft-gluon approximation that previously limited such calculations. The authors find that second-order-in-opacity corrections can change the energy dependence of the in-medium shower intensity, with corrections that can be positive or negative depending on the kinematic region.

## Method

The authors construct a reaction operator formalism using light-front wavefunctions and recursion relations that encode all four partonic splitting channels (q→qg, g→gg, q→gq, g→qq̅) to any order in opacity, with initial conditions set by vacuum leading-order branchings. They evaluate these recursion relations numerically at second order in opacity using the VEGAS Monte-Carlo algorithm, employing a realistic 2+1D event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic model (iEBE-VISHNU) of the expanding quark-gluon plasma produced in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, with Monte-Carlo-Glauber initial conditions at τ₀ = 0.6 fm.

## Key numbers

- For light quark jets and gluon jets with energies around ~100 GeV, the corrections from second order in opacity are modest, of order ~O(10%)
- For light partons in the small x and large x regions, second-order corrections are negative and sizeable, of order 30%–70% depending on the channel
- The integral I^{x_max}_{x_min} is evaluated with cutoffs x_min = 0.05, x_max = 0.95 for 0–10% centrality Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV
- Statistical errors from Monte-Carlo integration are kept at ≤ 1%
- Heavy bottom quarks are treated with mass m = 4.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the formalism constructed in Sievert (2018) to allow calculation of all partonic splitting channels beyond the soft-gluon approximation to any order in opacity, and evaluates them numerically at second order for the first time. Unlike earlier works by Gyulassy (2000) and Wicks (2008) that used medium models chosen for theoretical convenience, this calculation employs realistic state-of-the-art hydrodynamic models of the expanding quark-gluon plasma. The results enable precision jet substructure phenomenology and can directly enter fixed-order and resummed calculations of jet observables in heavy ion collisions.
