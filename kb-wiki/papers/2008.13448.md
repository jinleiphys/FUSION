# Evidence of shadowing in inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section

> The Glauber modeling plays a key role in centrality-dependent measurements of heavy-ion collisions. A central input parameter in Glauber models is the inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section $σ_{\text{nn}}^{\text{inel}}$ which is nearly always taken from proton-proton measurements. At the LHC energies $σ_{\text{nn}}^{\text{inel}}$ depends on the QCD dynamics at small $x$ and low interaction scales where the shadowing/saturation phenomena are expected to become relatively more important for larger nuclei than for the proton. Thus, $σ_{\text{nn}}^{\text{inel}}$ e.g. in Pb+Pb collisions may well be lower than what is seen in proton-proton collisions. In this talk, we demonstrate how to use the recent $W^\pm$ and $Z$ measurements as a "standard candle" to extract $σ_{\text{nn}}^{\text{inel}}$ in Pb+Pb collisions. Our analysis -- built on the ATLAS data, state-of-the-art NNLO QCD calculations and nuclear PDFs -- indicate that at the LHC energies $σ_{\text{nn}}^{\text{inel}}$ in Pb+Pb collisions is suppressed relative to the proton-proton measurements by tens of percents. We demonstrate that this is in line with expectations from nuclear PDFs.

# Evidence of shadowing in inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section

## Key claim

The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies is suppressed by tens of percent relative to the value measured in proton-proton collisions, with a best-fit value of 41.5 mb compared to the nominal 70 mb from proton-proton data, based on a data-driven extraction using W and Z boson measurements as a standard candle.

## Method

The authors compared the measured nuclear modification factor R_PbPb for W and Z boson production from ATLAS Run II data at sqrt(s_nn) = 5.02 TeV with next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) perturbative QCD calculations using NNPDF3.1 proton PDFs and EPPS16 nuclear PDFs. They varied the inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section sigma_nn_inel in the Glauber model normalization of the experimental data to minimize a chi-squared function that matches the re-normalized experimental R_PbPb to the theoretical prediction, accounting for nPDF uncertainties. An eikonal minijet model with nuclear shadowing from EPPS16 and nCTEQ15 was used to provide theoretical support for the extracted suppression.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit value of sigma_nn_inel in Pb+Pb collisions: 41.5 +16.2 -12.0 mb at sqrt(s_nn) = 5.02 TeV
- Nominal proton-proton value: 70 +/- 5 mb at sqrt(s_nn) = 5.02 TeV
- The suppression is several tens of percent relative to the proton-proton measurement

## Context

This work extends the use of electroweak boson production as a standard candle for calibrating Glauber model inputs, as previously proposed by the same authors (Eskola et al., 2020). The extracted suppression is consistent with expectations from nuclear PDF shadowing in an eikonal minijet model using both EPPS16 and nCTEQ15 nuclear modifications. The result implies that all experimental analyses relying on Glauber models for centrality-dependent normalization in heavy-ion collisions may need to account for a nuclear-suppressed inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section.
