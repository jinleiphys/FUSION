# On the electromagnetic hadron current derived from the gauged Wess-Zumino-Witten action

> In a recent paper, based on the Skyrme model with the Wess-Zumino-Witten term including electromagnetism, Eto et al. pointed out an intriguing possibility that the Gell-Mann-Nishijima relation is corrected under the presence of back-ground electromagnetic fields, thereby being led to the conclusion that even a neutron acquires non-zero net charge in external magnetic fields. We point out that this remarkable conclusion is inseparably connected with an unwelcome feature of the gauged Wess-Zumino-Witten action, i.e. the non-conservation of source current of Maxwell equation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the recent claim by Eto et al. that the Gell-Mann-Nishijima relation is corrected under background electromagnetic fields, leading to a neutron acquiring non-zero net charge in external magnetic fields, is inseparably connected with an unwelcome feature of the gauged Wess-Zumino-Witten action: the non-conservation of the source current of the Maxwell equation.

## Method

The author compares two methods for defining the electromagnetic hadron current from the gauged Wess-Zumino-Witten action: the Gell-Mann-Levy method for obtaining the Noether current, and the method of using the equations of motion to define the source current of the Maxwell equation. The analysis is first applied to scalar electrodynamics as a consistent reference case, then applied to the two-flavor Skyrme model with the gauged Wess-Zumino-Witten term including electromagnetism.

## Key numbers

* The baryon current is given by \(j^\mu_B = -\frac{1}{24\pi^2}\epsilon^{\mu\nu\alpha\beta}\text{tr}(L_\nu L_\alpha L_\beta)\), with the baryon number \(N_B = \int d^3x\,j^0_B\).
* The anomalous current is given by \(j^\mu_{anm} = \frac{i e N_c}{96\pi^2}\epsilon^{\mu\nu\alpha\beta}F_{\nu\alpha}\text{tr}\,\tau_3(L_\beta+R_\beta)\), with \(N_c=3\) for QCD.
* The modified Gell-Mann-Nishijima relation is \(Q = I_3 + N_B/2 + Q_{anm}/2\), where \(Q_{anm} = \int d^3x\,j^0_{anm}\).
* The source current derived from the gauged Wess-Zumino-Witten action is not conserved: \(\partial_\mu j^\mu = \partial_\mu X^\mu \neq 0\), with \(X^\mu = \frac{3 i e}{48\pi^2}\epsilon^{\mu\nu\alpha\beta}A_\nu\partial_\alpha\text{tr}\,Q(L_\beta+R_\beta)\).

## Context

This work directly responds to the claim by Eto et al. (2011) that the gauged Wess-Zumino-Witten action predicts anomalous charge induction for nucleons in magnetic fields. The author identifies a fundamental inconsistency: unlike scalar electrodynamics where the Noether current and source current coincide and are both gauge-invariant and conserved, the gauged Wess-Zumino-Witten action yields currents that fail to satisfy both properties simultaneously. The paper notes that Kaymakcalan, Rajeev and Schechter (1984) had previously argued that such inconsistencies might be tolerable in an effective action used as a mnemonic for vertices. A related work by Kharzeev, Yee, and Zahed (2011), using a simpler effective lagrangian, found that the anomaly induces a quadrupole moment for a neutron but does not induce net electric charge, consistent with the intuitive picture presented here.
