# Possible polarized neutron-nucleus scattering search for a new spin-dependent nucleon-nucleon coupling in a Fm range

> An experimental opportunity is presented for the future to measure possible P- and T-non-invariant axion-like interaction between nucleons in a Fm range. This interaction may be searched for in the measurement spin-dependent asymmetry of scattering of polarized neutrons in a keV-MeV energy range by heavy nuclei.

# Possible polarized neutron-nucleus scattering search for a new spin-dependent nucleon-nucleon coupling in a Fm range

## Key claim
A future polarized neutron-nucleus scattering experiment in the keV-MeV energy range could search for a P- and T-non-invariant axion-like interaction between nucleons at force ranges of 10⁻¹³ to 10⁻⁹ cm, but the achievable sensitivity depends on exploiting narrow energy intervals where the imaginary part of the neutron-nucleus scattering amplitude is large and the total cross section is small.

## Method
The author calculates the spin-dependent scattering asymmetry that would arise from a monopole-dipole interaction potential between polarized neutrons and unpolarized heavy nuclei, using the formalism of s-wave and p-wave neutron-nucleus scattering amplitudes with resonance contributions. The sensitivity factor B(Eₙ) is computed as a function of neutron energy by summing over known s- and p-resonances for a target nucleus such as ²⁰⁷Pb, using the imaginary part of the nuclear scattering amplitude and the total cross section. The proposed measurement would detect the interference of the imaginary axion-like scattering amplitude with the imaginary part of the nuclear scattering amplitude in either left-right asymmetry of transversely polarized neutrons or forward-backward asymmetry of longitudinally polarized neutrons.

## Key numbers
- The neutron wave vector in the center-of-mass system is given by k (F⁻¹) = 2.1968 × 10⁻⁴ × [A/(A+1)] × Eₙ¹/², with Eₙ in eV in the lab system.
- Neutron energies in the range 10 < Eₙ < 10⁶ eV correspond to force ranges 3 < λ < 10³ Fm and axion masses 0.2 < mₐ < 70 MeV.
- The axion-like scattering amplitude is maximal at q = 1/λ, with magnitude |fₐ(λ)| = gₛgₚNλ/(8π).
- With a sensitivity factor B = 10 Fm⁻¹ and a measured asymmetry A = 10⁻⁴, the limit on gₛgₚ may be obtained at the level 10⁻⁶ at λ = 10 Fm.
- The order-of-magnitude estimate gives gₛgₚ = A/λ (Fm) when σₜₒₜ = 10 b, Im f = 5 Fm, and N = 200.
- The most stringent current limits from the neutron crystal-diffraction method are gₛgₚ ≤ 10⁻¹² for λ ≥ 10⁻⁸ cm and gₛgₚ ≤ 3 × 10⁻²⁹/λ² for λ ≤ 10⁻⁹ cm.

## Context
This work extends the search for axion-like interactions to the Fm range using low-energy neutron-nucleus scattering, complementing beam-dump experiments and astrophysical constraints that are more model-dependent. The proposed approach is contrasted with the neutron crystal-diffraction method of Voronin et al., which has produced the most stringent current limits on gₛgₚ. The paper provides a framework for future time-of-flight measurements that could exploit the strong fluctuations of the sensitivity factor B(Eₙ) near neutron resonances in heavy nuclei like ²⁰⁷Pb, without requiring assumptions about particle decay properties or couplings to leptons and photons.
