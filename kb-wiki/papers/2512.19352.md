# Measurement of Fifth- and Sixth-Order Fluctuations of (Net-)proton Number in Au+Au Collisions from Phase II of the Beam Energy Scan Program at RHIC

> We report high-statistics measurements of fifth- and sixth-order factorial cumulants and cumulant ratios of (net-)proton multiplicity distributions in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7$--27 GeV, using data from the STAR experiment collected during the Beam Energy Scan Phase~II at RHIC. Protons and antiprotons are identified at midrapidity ($|y| < 0.5$) with transverse momentum $0.4 < p_T < 2.0$ GeV/$c$. The proton factorial cumulants $κ_4$, $κ_5$, and $κ_6$ increase with order but exhibit no sign alternation within current uncertainties, offering no evidence for a two-component structure in the proton multiplicity distribution, as might be expected near a first-order phase transition. The cumulant ratios $C_{5}/C_{1}$ and $C_{6}/C_{2}$ fluctuate around zero in collisions at 0--40\% centrality. The results are consistent with both the negative predictions from lattice QCD (LQCD) and the positive trends obtained from the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model. At $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \gtrsim 27$ GeV, the $C_4/C_2$ and $C_5/C_1$ results are compatible with predictions from lattice QCD, functional renormalization group (FRG), and hadron resonance gas (HRG) models, while UrQMD describes the data better at lower energies. These measurements place constraints on baryon number fluctuations and offer valuable insights into the QCD phase structure.

## Key claim

The fifth- and sixth-order factorial cumulants of proton multiplicity distributions increase with order but exhibit no sign alternation within current uncertainties, offering no evidence for a two-component structure in the proton multiplicity distribution that might be expected near a first-order phase transition; the cumulant ratios C5/C1 and C6/C2 fluctuate around zero in 0-40% centrality collisions, consistent with both negative predictions from lattice QCD and positive trends from the UrQMD model.

## Method

The STAR collaboration analyzed high-statistics Au+Au collision data at center-of-mass energies per nucleon pair from 7.7 to 27 GeV, collected during Phase II of the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC. Protons and antiprotons were identified at midrapidity (|y| < 0.5) with transverse momentum 0.4 < pT < 2.0 GeV/c using ionization energy loss in the Time Projection Chamber and timing from the Time-of-Flight detector. Fifth- and sixth-order factorial cumulants and cumulant ratios were extracted from event-by-event multiplicity distributions and corrected for finite detection efficiency using the binomial response method, with statistical uncertainties estimated via bootstrap resampling and systematic uncertainties evaluated by varying key analysis parameters.

## Key numbers

- Proton factorial cumulants κ4, κ5, and κ6 increase with order but show no sign alternation within uncertainties in 0-40% centrality Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 7.7-27 GeV
- At √sNN = 19.6 GeV in 0-40% centrality, the total uncertainty on C6/C2 is reduced by a factor of 6 compared to BES-I
- The number of good-quality events ranges from 45 million at √sNN = 7.7 GeV to 270 million at 19.6 GeV
- At √sNN = 19.6 GeV, an 18-fold increase in statistics was achieved compared to BES-I
- For net-proton C6/C2 in 0-40% centrality at √sNN = 19.6 GeV, DCA and PID contributions account for 93% and 32% of the total systematic uncertainty, respectively
- The probability of cumulant ratios up to fifth order following the predicted ordering ranges from 63.8% to 99.5% over √sNN = 9.2-27 GeV and decreases to 8.5% at 7.7 GeV
- The current measurements of C6/C2 yield a significance of about 0.2σ for observing negative values at more than half of the collision energies in the range √sNN = 7.7-200 GeV, compared to 1.7σ obtained in BES-I

## Context

This work extends the STAR collaboration's earlier measurements of net-proton cumulants up to fourth order from BES-I (STAR, 2013; STAR, 2020) and earlier fifth- and sixth-order cumulants with limited statistics (STAR, 2021; STAR, 2022). The results are compared with predictions from lattice QCD (Bazavov, 2017; Bazavov, 2020), functional renormalization group calculations (Fu, 2021), hadron resonance gas models (Braun-Munzinger, 2020), and the UrQMD transport model (Bass, 1998; Bleicher, 1999). At √sNN ≳ 27 GeV, the C4/C2 and C5/C1 results are compatible with lattice QCD, FRG, and HRG models, while UrQMD describes the data better at lower energies (≲ 11.5 GeV). These measurements place constraints on baryon number fluctuations and offer insights into the QCD phase structure, with the improved precision from BES-II enabling more stringent tests of theoretical predictions.
