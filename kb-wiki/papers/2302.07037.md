# Directed and elliptic flows of protons and deuterons in HADES Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.4$ GeV

> Within a transport model coupled with a microscopic coalescence model, the directed and elliptic flows of protons and deuterons as well as their scalling properties are studied in the centrality of 20-30% Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.4$ GeV. It is found that the flows as well as their scaling properties simulated with the isospin- and momentum-dependent nuclear mean field with an incompressibility $K_{0}=230$ MeV fit fairly the HADES data, while those simulated with the commonly used momentum-independent nuclear mean field with an incompressibility $K_{0}=380$ MeV can only fit partially the HADES data. Moreover, by checking the rapidity distributions of both protons and deuterons in the centrality of 0-10% Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.4$ GeV, we find that the rapidity distributions of deuterons are underestimated while those of protons are overestimated by the simulations with the momentum-independent nuclear mean field. In contrast, the rapidity distributions of both protons and deuterons simulated with the isospin- and momentum-dependent nuclear mean field are in good agreement with the HADES data. Our findings imply that the momentum dependence of nuclear mean field is an unavoidable feature for a fundamental understanding of nuclear matter properties and for the successful interpretation of the HADES data.

# Directed and elliptic flows of protons and deuterons in HADES Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.4 GeV

## Key claim

The momentum-dependent nuclear mean field with incompressibility K0=230 MeV reproduces the HADES flow data for protons and deuterons, while the momentum-independent mean field with K0=380 MeV can only fit the data partially, implying that momentum dependence of the nuclear mean field is essential for interpreting the HADES data. The symmetry energy effect on the flows is found to be negligible at this beam energy.

## Method

The authors used an isospin- and momentum-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model coupled with a microscopic coalescence model based on the Hulthén wave function for deuteron formation. Two nuclear mean field scenarios were compared: a momentum-independent (MID) field with K0=380 MeV and a momentum-dependent (MDI) field with K0=230 MeV, each with soft and stiff symmetry energy parametrizations. The directed flow v1 and elliptic flow v2 of protons and deuterons were computed as functions of transverse momentum and rapidity for 20-30% centrality Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.4 GeV, and rapidity distributions were also examined for 0-10% centrality.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: sqrt(s_NN)=2.4 GeV for Au+Au collisions
- Centrality studied: 20-30% for flow analysis; 0-10% for rapidity distributions
- Incompressibility values: K0=230 MeV (MDI scenario) and K0=380 MeV (MID scenario)
- Normal density: rho0=0.16 fm^{-3}
- Symmetry energy at normal density: E_sym(rho0)=32.5 MeV
- Symmetry energy slope parameters: L=48.75 MeV (gamma=0.5, soft) and L=195.0 MeV (gamma=2.0, stiff) for MID; L=33.06 MeV (x=0.6) and L=92.66 MeV (x=-0.2) for MDI
- Deuteron root-mean-square radius: 1.96 fm
- Spin-isospin statistical factor for deuteron: G=3/8
- Isoscalar effective mass: m*_s=0.7m
- Isoscalar potential at infinite nucleon momentum: U^infty_0(rho0)=75 MeV
- Isovector potential at infinite nucleon momentum: U^infty_sym(rho0)=-100 MeV
- Symmetry energy at 2rho0/3: E_sym(2rho0/3)=25.5 +/- 1 MeV

## Context

This work addresses a tension between two recent studies: Hillmann et al. (2020) claimed that a momentum-independent mean field with K0=380 MeV could fit HADES flow data using the UrQMD model, while Fang et al. (2022) found that a momentum-dependent mean field reproduced the same data within the IQMD model. The present study uses a BUU-type transport model (IBUU) to perform a comparative test of both scenarios, finding that only the momentum-dependent field with K0=230 MeV consistently fits the HADES data for both protons and deuterons, including their rapidity distributions and the nucleon-number scaling of elliptic flows. The results enable further constraints on the nuclear equation of state at densities around 2.5 times saturation density and demonstrate that the coalescence mechanism for deuteron production is validated only when momentum dependence is included in the mean field.
