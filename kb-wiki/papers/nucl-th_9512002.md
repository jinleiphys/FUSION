# Charm Correlation as a Diagnostic Probe of Quark Matter

> The use of correlation between two open-charm mesons is suggested to give information about the nature of the medium created in heavy-ion collisions. Insensitivity to the charm production rate is achieved by measuring normalized cumulant. The acollinearity of the D momenta in the transverse plane is a measure of the medium effect. Its dependence on nuclear size or E_T provides a signature for the formation of quark matter.

# Charm Correlation as a Diagnostic Probe of Quark Matter

## Key claim
The acollinearity of D meson momenta in the transverse plane, measured via a normalized cumulant that is insensitive to the charm production rate, can serve as a signature for quark-gluon plasma formation: the mean acollinearity should be smaller if the medium is deconfined quark matter than if it is hadron matter, and should become essentially independent of nuclear size or transverse energy in the quark matter case.

## Method
The author proposes measuring the normalized cumulant C(p,φ) of two open-charm D mesons produced nearly back-to-back in the transverse plane at midrapidity, with momenta in the 2–5 GeV/c range and nearly equal magnitudes. The width λ of the angular distribution in φ (or equivalently the distribution in ξ = p_t/p, where p_t is the transverse deviation from exact back-to-back alignment) is analyzed as the root-mean-square sum of three contributions: the acollinearity of the initial c and anti-c partons (λ₁₂), and the angular deviations from parton to hadron for each charm quark (λ₁ and λ₂). The medium effect enters through the hadronization functions H(k_i → p_i), which describe how a c quark traversing the medium recombines with a light antiquark to form a D meson. For quark matter, the c quark remains deconfined until exiting the medium and then recombines, yielding small and path-length-independent transverse momentum broadening; for hadron matter, D mesons form inside the medium and undergo multiple hadronic collisions, producing larger broadening that increases with path length.

## Key numbers
- The proposed momentum range for D mesons is 2–5 GeV/c, with nearly equal magnitudes |p₁| ≈ |p₂| ≡ p.
- The average intrinsic transverse momentum of the c anti-c pair is estimated at 0.3 GeV/c, giving λ₁₂ ≤ 0.15 for k > 2 GeV/c.
- The color screening mass μ is estimated as μ ≈ 0.4 GeV for α_s = 0.3 and T = 200 MeV, setting the scale for k_t in the quark matter scenario.
- For quark matter, the width λ is approximately 0.5/p (with p in GeV/c), so for p > 2 GeV/c the width is small.
- The mean path length L for a c quark crossing a disc of radius R is L = 4R/3.
- Data from hadron-nucleus collisions show that σ(p_T=0.5)/σ(p_T=0.3) stays essentially unchanged at 0.5 and 0.48 for A=Cu and Pb respectively (for p_π+ = 100 GeV/c and p_K+ = 80 GeV/c), supporting the claim that k_t does not increase with path length at low p_T.

## Context
This work extends the concept of acoplanarity of jets suggested by Appel (1995) to the specific case of open-charm meson pairs, addressing ambiguities that have plagued conventional probes such as dileptons and J/ψ suppression. The proposal builds on earlier work showing that open charm production in hadronic collisions is well described by recombination (Hwa 1995) rather than by perturbative QCD or fragmentation models (Gavai 1995), and that for p_T < 6 GeV/c and large nuclei, hadronization through recombination dominates over fragmentation by at least an order of magnitude (Hwa 1995). The analysis of parton transverse momentum broadening draws on studies of the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect (Wang 1995) and on experimental data from hadron-nucleus collisions (Skubic 1978, Barton 1983) showing that p_T distributions in the beam fragmentation region are essentially independent of nuclear size at low p_T. The proposal enables experimental searches for quark matter that are independent of uncertainties in the charm production rate, initial temperature, and the validity of perturbative QCD.
