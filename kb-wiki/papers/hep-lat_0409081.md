# Pion form factor using domain wall valence and asqtad sea quarks

> We compute the pion electromagnetic form factor in a hybrid calculation with domain wall valence quarks and improved staggered (asqtad) sea quarks. This method can easily be extended to rho-to-gamma-pi transition form factors.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that hybrid lattice QCD calculations using domain wall valence quarks on asqtad sea quark configurations can reproduce the pion electromagnetic form factor consistent with vector meson dominance (VMD), but only when using a lattice dispersion relation rather than the continuum dispersion relation at high momenta.

## Method
The authors performed a hybrid lattice calculation using MILC 20^3×64 configurations with N_f=2+1 and N_f=3 asqtad sea quarks, combined with domain wall valence quarks (height m_0=1.7, extent L_s=16). They computed the pion form factor using a ratio method with pseudoscalar sink operators at fixed momentum p_f=(1,0,0), imposing a free lattice boson dispersion relation to determine energies at high momenta from low-momentum correlators.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing a^{-1} ≈ 1.6 GeV for 20^3×64 dynamical MILC asqtad lattices
- Three valence quark masses studied: am_val = 0.01, 0.05, and 0.081, producing pion masses of 318(3) MeV, 602(5) MeV, and 758(5) MeV respectively
- Rho meson masses: 956(22) MeV, 955(19) MeV, and 1060(14) MeV for the three valence masses
- Domain wall height m_0 = 1.7 with extent L_s = 16 of the extra dimension
- Dirichlet boundary conditions imposed 32 timeslices apart

## Context
This work extends earlier lattice calculations validating vector meson dominance at low Q^2 (Martinelli 1988, Draper 1989) and more recent lattice results extending to Q^2 up to 2 GeV^2 (van der Heide 2003, Nemoto 2003, Abdel-Rehim 2004). The hybrid approach combining domain wall valence quarks with asqtad sea quarks enables future extension to rho-to-gamma-pi transition form factors, though the authors note that axial vector sink operators would be needed for statistically significant results at higher momenta.
