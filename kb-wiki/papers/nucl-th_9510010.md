# Two step mechanism of $η, η', ω, ϕ$ meson prodction in $pD \to ^3He $ reaction$

> The differential cross sections of $pD\to ^3He X$ reactions, where $X=η, \ η'\ ,ω, \ ϕ$, are calculated on the basis of a two-step mechanism involving the subprocesses $pp\to dπ^+$ and $π^+n\to Xp $. It is shown that this model describes well the form of energy dependence of available experimental cross sections at the final c.m.s. momentum $p^*$=0.4-1$ GeV/c$ for the $η$ and at $p^*=0-0.5$ GeV/c for the $ω$ meson as well as the ratios $R(η'/η) $ and $R(ϕ/ω)$. The absolute value of the cross section is underestimated by an overall normalization factor of about 3 for $η, \ η'$ and by nearly one order of magnitude for $ω$ and $ϕ$. The spin-spin correlations are predicted for the reaction ${\vec D}{\vec p}\to ^3He X$ in the forward-backward approximations for the elementary amplitudes.

## Key claim

The two-step mechanism (subprocesses $pp\to d\pi^+$ and $\pi^+n\to Xp$) describes the shape of the energy dependence of available experimental cross sections for $\eta$ and $\omega$ meson production and the ratios $R(\eta'/\eta)$ and $R(\phi/\omega)$, but the absolute cross section is underestimated by a factor of about 3 for $\eta$ and $\eta'$ and by nearly an order of magnitude for $\omega$ and $\phi$, with the normalization factor for $\omega$ reaching $N=9.6$ in some kinematic regions.

## Method

The authors calculated differential cross sections for $pD\to ^3He X$ ($X=\eta, \eta', \omega, \phi$) using a two-step model where the reaction proceeds via $pp\to d\pi^+$ followed by $\pi^+n\to Xp$, with the intermediate pion propagator and nuclear wave functions (RSC deuteron wave function and a parametrized $^3He$ wave function in the $d+p$ channel) treated exactly in the form factor integral. The amplitudes of the elementary subprocesses were factored outside the integral at zero Fermi momenta, and the spin-averaged cross sections were computed assuming $R_0=R_1=1/3$ for the spin factor, corresponding to the condition $|a_1|\gg |b_1|$ in the forward-backward amplitudes. Experimental data for the $pp\to d\pi^+$ differential cross section were taken from Ritchie (parametrization), and total cross sections for $\pi^+n\to Xp$ were taken from CERN (1983) with an assumed isotropic angular distribution.

## Key numbers

- For $\eta$ meson production, the model describes the energy dependence at final c.m.s. momentum $p^*=0.4-1.0$ GeV/c with a normalization factor $N=3$ (close to $N=2.4$ found by Faldt and Wilkin at threshold).
- For $\eta'$ meson production near threshold ($p^*=22$ MeV/c) and at $T_p=3$ GeV, the same normalization factor $N=3$ as for $\eta$ gives agreement with experimental data.
- For $\omega$ meson, the shape of the squared amplitude $|f|^2$ as a function of $p^*=0-500$ MeV/c agrees with experiment, but the normalization factor is $N=5.9$ at threshold and $N=9.6$ in the range $100\leq p^*\leq 400$ MeV/c.
- For $\phi$ meson near threshold ($p^*\sim 20$ MeV/c), the normalization factor is $N=6.6$.
- The predicted ratio $R(\phi/\omega)=0.052$ near thresholds agrees with the experimental value $R^{exp}=0.07\pm 0.02$.
- The spin-spin asymmetry $\Sigma_1$ for $\phi$ meson near threshold is $\Sigma_1(\phi)=-0.95$, going rapidly to $-1$ above threshold; for $\omega$ meson $\Sigma_1(\omega)=-0.92$.

## Context

This work extends the two-step model previously applied to $\eta$ meson production near threshold by Kondratyuk and Uzikov (earlier work) and by Faldt and Wilkin (1995) to the production of $\eta'$, $\omega$, and $\phi$ mesons above threshold, where previously only Laget and Lecolley had investigated $\eta$ production in a microscopic model. The model contradicts the earlier three-body mechanism of Lagel (which also highlighted the role of intermediate pions) by showing that the two-step mechanism with velocity matching at $\theta_{c.m.}\sim 90^\circ$ remains important beyond the matching conditions. The results enable predictions for spin-spin correlations in the proposed Dubna experiment with polarized deuteron and proton beams, and raise open questions about the missing absolute normalization, possibly due to contributions from non-deuteron states in the $pp\to \pi NN$ subprocess or the full spin structure of elementary amplitudes beyond the forward-backward approximation.
