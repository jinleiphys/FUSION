# Exotic Structure of Carbon Isotopes

> We studied firstly the ground state properties of C-isotopes using a deformed Hartree-Fock (HF)+ BCS model with Skyrme interactions. Shallow deformation minima are found in several neutron$-$rich C-isotopes. It is shown also that the deformation minima appear in both the oblate and the prolate sides in $^{17}$C and $^{19}$C having almost the same binding energies. Secondly, we carried out shell model calculations to study electromagnetic moments and electric dipole transitions of the C-isotopes. We point out the clear configuration dependence of the quadrupole and magnetic moments in the odd C-isotopes, which will be useful to find out the deformations and the spin-parities of the ground states of these nuclei. We studied electric dipole states of C-isotopes focusing on the interplay between low energy Pigmy strength and giant dipole resonances. Reasonable agreement is obtained with available experimental data for the photoreaction cross sections both in the low energy region below $\hbar ω$=14 MeV and in the high energy giant resonance region (14 MeV $<\hbar ω\leq $30 MeV). The calculated transition strength below dipole giant resonance ($\hbar ω\leq $14 MeV) in heavier C-isotopes than $^{15}$C is found to exhaust about $12\sim16%$ of the classical Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule value and $50\sim80%$ of the cluster sum rule value.

# Exotic Structure of Carbon Isotopes

## Key claim

The paper suggests that neutron-rich carbon isotopes exhibit shallow deformation minima with competing prolate and oblate shapes, and that the ground state spin-parities of odd isotopes like 17C and 19C can be determined through measurements of magnetic and quadrupole moments, which show strong configuration dependence. The authors hedge that the deformation assignments are model-dependent and that experimental confirmation is "desperately desired."

## Method

The authors performed deformed Hartree-Fock (HF) plus BCS calculations with Skyrme interactions (SkI4, SIII, SkM*) to study ground state properties and energy surfaces of carbon isotopes as a function of the quadrupole deformation parameter β2. They also carried out large-scale shell model calculations using the WBP10 interaction in the (0p-0d1s) configuration space, with model spaces up to (1+3)ħω excitations for 12C, 13C, and 14C, and 1ħω excitations for heavier isotopes, to study electromagnetic moments and electric dipole transitions.

## Key numbers

- The calculated transition strength below the dipole giant resonance (ħω ≤ 14 MeV) in heavier C-isotopes than 15C exhausts about 12-16% of the classical Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) sum rule value and 50-80% of the cluster sum rule value.
- In 17C, the calculated g-factor for the 3/2+ and 5/2+ states are -0.514 μN and -0.505 μN, respectively, while the 1/2+ state gives -2.82 μN; the experimental value |g(exp)| = 0.5054 ± 0.0025 excludes the 1/2+ state.
- The neutron and proton contributions to the quadrupole moment in 17C are 17.1 mb and 6.4 mb for the 3/2+ state, and -5.5 mb and -3.8 mb for the 5/2+ state.
- In 19C, the neutron and proton contributions to the quadrupole moment are -18.4 mb and -14.7 mb in the 3/2+ state, and -0.6 mb for neutrons and 1.6 mb for protons in the 5/2+ state.
- The photoreaction cross section below ħω = 14 MeV exhausts 7.8% of the TRK sum rule for 15C, 16.3% for 16C, 13.1% for 17C, 11.6% for 18C, and 12.6% (14.1%) for 19C with 1/2+ (3/2+) ground state.
- The GDR peak in 12C appears at Ex ~ 21 MeV, in 13C at 25-26 MeV (experimental 24 MeV), and in 14C at 28 MeV (experimental 25.6 MeV).
- The integrated cross section for 13C calculated up to Ex = 30 MeV amounts to 98% of the TRK sum rule, while the observed value is 71%.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of exotic nuclear structure in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes, particularly the new shell structure at N=16 found by Ozawa (2000) and the dipole excitations studied by H. Sagawa et al. (1998), Emi et al. (1998), and Suzuki and Sagawa (1999). The deformed HF results are compared with antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations by Enyo et al., which showed similar neutron number dependence of deformation. The shell model calculations use polarization charges from a microscopic particle-vibration model by Sagawa and Asahi (2001), which were previously applied to explain quadrupole moments of boron isotopes. The paper enables future experimental measurements of magnetic and quadrupole moments to determine ground state deformations and spin-parities, particularly for 19C which is identified as a keystone for establishing the new shell structure at N=16 in carbon isotopes.
