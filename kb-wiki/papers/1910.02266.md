# Effects of nuclear symmetry energy and equation of state on neutron star properties

> We study the effects of nuclear symmetry energy on the mass-radius relation and tidal deformability of neutron stars, considering the self-consistency of the equation of state (EOS). We first construct a set of unified EOSs based on relativistic mean-field models with different density dependence of the symmetry energy. For the description of pasta phases appearing in the inner crust of neutron stars, we perform a self-consistent Thomas-Fermi calculation using the same nuclear interaction as that for the uniform matter in the core. To examine possible effects from the self-consistency of the EOS on neutron-star properties, we separately investigate the impacts of crust and core segments. By matching the same core EOS to different crust EOSs, it is found that neutron-star radii are significantly affected by the crust segment. On the other hand, the neutron-star radii are also strongly dependent on the core EOS. However, the correlation between the radius and the symmetry energy slope of the core EOS is opposite to that of the crust EOS. It is likely that the nuclear model with a small slope parameter is favored by recent astrophysical observations.

# Effects of nuclear symmetry energy and equation of state on neutron star properties

## Key claim
The paper shows that neutron-star radii and tidal deformability depend on the symmetry energy slope parameter L, but the correlation between radius and L from the core EOS is opposite to that from the crust EOS. The authors find that nuclear models with a small slope parameter (L = 40 MeV) are favored by the GW170817 tidal deformability constraint and by neutron-star cooling observations, though they acknowledge that the slope parameter cannot be precisely constrained by radius observations due to model dependency.

## Method
The authors constructed a set of unified equations of state based on relativistic mean-field (RMF) models using the TM1 parametrization, with different density dependence of the symmetry energy controlled by adjusting the rho-meson coupling constant g_rho and the omega-rho coupling term Lambda_v. For the inner crust, they performed self-consistent Thomas-Fermi calculations for pasta phases (droplet, rod, slab, tube, bubble) using the same nuclear interaction as the core, and determined the crust-core transition by comparing energy densities between pasta phases and homogeneous matter. They then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities, and separately examined crust and core effects by matching different crust EOSs to a fixed core EOS.

## Key numbers
- Maximum neutron-star masses for the unified EOSs lie in the range 2.12-2.18 solar masses, compatible with the observational constraints of PSR J1614-2230 (M = 1.928 +/- 0.017 solar masses), PSR J0348+0432 (M = 2.01 +/- 0.04 solar masses), and PSR J0740+6620 (M = 2.14^{+0.10}_{-0.09} solar masses).
- Radius of a canonical 1.4 solar mass neutron star (R_1.4) is approximately 14.21 km for the TM1(L=111) model and reduces to approximately 13.12 km for the TM1(L=40) model.
- Tidal deformability for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star is Lambda = 652 for the TM1(L=40) model and Lambda = 1047 for the TM1(L=111) model.
- The crust-core transition density decreases with increasing L: from 0.099 fm^{-3} for L=40 MeV to 0.062 fm^{-3} for L=111 MeV.
- The threshold density for the direct Urca process is 0.67 fm^{-3} for the L=40 MeV model and 0.21 fm^{-3} for the L=111 MeV model.
- The symmetry energy at saturation density varies from 31.38 MeV (L=40) to 36.89 MeV (L=111), while all models have fixed E_sym = 28.05 MeV at a density of 0.11 fm^{-3}.

## Context
This work extends previous studies on unified EOSs using the compressible liquid drop model by Haensel (2001), the parameterized Thomas-Fermi method by Shen (2002) and Miyatsu (2013), and the self-consistent Thomas-Fermi approximation by Chamel (2013), Sharma (2015), and Fortin (2016). The authors specifically examine the separate influences of crust and core segments on neutron-star properties, finding that matching different crust EOSs to the same core EOS significantly affects radii. The results enable more quantitative constraints on the symmetry energy slope from the GW170817 tidal deformability measurements and from neutron-star cooling observations, with the TM1(L=40) model being compatible with the 90% credible constraint from the latest LIGO/Virgo analysis.
