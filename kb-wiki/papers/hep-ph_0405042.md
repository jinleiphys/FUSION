# On the chiral phase transition in hadronic matter

> A qualitative analysis of the chiral phase transition in QCD with two massless quarks and non--zero baryon density is performed. It is assumed that at zero baryonic density, ρ=0, the temperature phase transition is of the second order. Due to a specific power dependence of baryon masses on the chiral condensate the phase transition becomes of the first order at the temperature T=T_{ph}(ρ) for ρ>0. At temperatures T_{cont}(ρ) > T > T_{ph}(ρ) there is a mixed phase consisting of the quark phase (stable) and the hadron phase (unstable). At the temperature T = T_{cont}(ρ) the system experiences a continuous transition to the pure chirally symmetric phase.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in QCD with two massless quarks, the chiral phase transition at zero baryon density is assumed to be second order, but at any non-zero baryon density it becomes first order due to a specific power-law dependence of baryon masses on the chiral condensate (m ∝ η^(1/3)). The analysis is limited to the region |T − T_c^(0)|/T_c^(0) ≪ 1 and low baryon densities, and ignores possible confinement effects that could introduce an endpoint.

## Method

The authors construct a Landau-type thermodynamic potential Φ(η) expanded in powers of the chiral condensate η up to η^4, then add a baryon-density term C η^(2/3) ρ derived from the relation m ∝ η^(1/3) for nucleon masses. By minimizing Φ(η, ρ) and analyzing its second derivative, they determine the temperatures T_ph(ρ) (first-order transition) and T_cont(ρ) (continuous transition to pure chirally symmetric phase) as functions of baryon density.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature at zero baryon density is T_c^(0) ≈ 180 MeV, obtained from extrapolation of the chiral condensate η(T) in chiral effective theory.
- The first-order phase transition temperature at non-zero density is given by T_ph(ρ) = T_c^(0) − (5/a) [(2Cρ/3)]^(3/5) (B/4)^(2/5).
- The continuous transition temperature is T_cont(ρ) = T_c^(0) − (5/a) [(2Cρ/9)]^(3/5) (B/2)^(2/5).
- The critical density has a universal temperature dependence ρ_ph(T) ∝ [T_c^(0) − T]^(5/3), independent of the potential parameters a and B.
- The specific heat discontinuity at the approximate second-order transition is ΔC_p = a^2 T_c/B.

## Context

This work extends the Landau theory of second-order phase transitions (Landau, 1937) to finite baryon density in QCD with two massless quarks, building on lattice results indicating a second-order transition at zero chemical potential (Karsch, 2002; lattice collaborations). It contradicts lattice simulations that find an endpoint in the QCD phase diagram with finite chemical potential (Fodor and Katz, 2002; de Forcrand and Philipsen, 2003), which the authors attribute to neglecting confinement effects at high density. The results enable future studies of how strange quark masses (m_s ≈ 150 MeV) might modify the phase diagram near T_c^(0) ∼ 200 MeV.
