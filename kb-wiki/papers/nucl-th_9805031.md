# Derivation of the Effective Pion-Nucleon Lagrangian within Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We develop a method for constructing the Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory Lagrangian (L_{HBChPT}), to a given chiral order, within HBChPT. We work within SU(2) theory, with only the pion field interacting with the nucleon. The main difficulties, which are solved, are to develop techniques for implementing charge conjugation invariance, and for taking the nucleon on shell, both within the nonrelativistic formalism. We obtain complete lists of independent terms in L_{HBChPT} through O(q^3) for off- shell nucleons. Then, eliminating equation-of-motion (eom) terms at the relativistic and nonrelativistic level (both within HBChPT), we obtain L_{HBCHPT} for on-shell nucleons, through O(q^3). The extension of the method (to obtain on-shell L_{HBChPT} within HBChPT) to higher orders is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper develops a method for constructing the Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) Lagrangian directly within the nonrelativistic framework, without first constructing the relativistic Baryon ChPT Lagrangian and then performing a 1/m-reduction. The method works for SU(2) theory with only pions and nucleons, and the authors claim to obtain complete lists of independent terms through O(q^3) for both off-shell and on-shell nucleons, with the caveat that the on-shell lists match those previously obtained by Ecker and Mojzis (1995) via the longer 1/m-reduction procedure.

## Method

The authors construct the HBChPT Lagrangian by first building all possible Lorentz scalar, isoscalar, parity-even, and hermitian terms from the nonrelativistic building blocks (v_mu, S_mu, D_mu, u_mu, chi_+, chi_-) using a nonlinear realization of chiral symmetry. They develop a new phase rule, (-1)^{q+P+j}=1, to enforce charge conjugation invariance directly within the nonrelativistic formalism by relating it to the counting of i chi_- fields, commutators, and Levi-Civita tensors. After eliminating trace-dependent terms using isospin identities and reducing the number of independent terms via algebraic identities such as the curvature relation [D_mu, D_nu] = (1/4)[u_mu, u_nu] and Jacobi identities, they obtain complete off-shell lists for O(q^2) and O(q^3). For on-shell reduction, they eliminate equation-of-motion terms by rewriting certain BChPT terms proportional to (i slash-D - m) psi and then taking the nonrelativistic limit, working entirely within HBChPT.

## Key numbers

* The chiral order of building blocks: u_mu and D_mu are O(q); chi_+ and chi_- are O(q^2).
* The phase rule for charge conjugation invariance: (-1)^{q+P+j}=1, where q is the number of i chi_- fields, P is the number of commutators, and j is 0 or 1 for Levi-Civita tensors.
* The Lorentz invariance condition: (-1)^{m+n+l+r}=1, where m, n, l, r count D_mu, u_mu, v_mu, and S_mu respectively.
* The parity condition: (-1)^{n+q+r+j}=1, where n counts u_mu, q counts chi_-, r counts S_mu, and j counts Levi-Civita tensors.
* The curvature relation: [D_mu, D_nu] = (1/4)[u_mu, u_nu].
* The paper lists 10 terms of type A (proportional to the nonrelativistic equation of motion iv·D H) and 7 terms of type B (of the form bar-H O_mu D^mu H + h.c.) that are eliminated in the on-shell reduction at O(q^3).

## Context

This work extends the method of constructing HBChPT Lagrangians pioneered by Manohar and Jenkins (1990) and Bernard et al. (1992), who obtained the nonrelativistic Lagrangian via 1/m-reduction of the relativistic Baryon ChPT Lagrangian of Gasser, Sainio, and Svarc (1988). The authors provide a direct construction within HBChPT, solving the difficulty of implementing charge conjugation invariance without anti-nucleons. The off-shell Lagrangian can be applied to nuclear HBChPT processes such as pion double charge exchange scattering off nuclear targets involving bound nucleons. The on-shell lists are shown to be identical to those of Ecker and Mojzis (1995), who obtained them by the longer 1/m-reduction formalism, and the method is stated to be extendable to higher chiral orders.
