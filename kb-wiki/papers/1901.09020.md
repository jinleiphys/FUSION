# Critical view of nonlocal nuclear potentials in alpha decay

> Different models for the nonlocal description of the nuclear interaction are compared through a study of their effects on the half-lives of radioactive nuclei decaying by the emission of alpha particles. The half-lives are evaluated by considering a pre-formed alpha particle ($^4$He nucleus) which tunnels through the Coulomb barrier generated by its interaction with the daughter nucleus. An effective potential obtained from a density dependent double folding strong potential between the alpha and the daughter nucleus within the nonlocal framework is found to decrease the half-lives as compared to those in the absence of nonlocalities. Whereas the percentage decrease within the older Perey-Buck and São Paulo models ranges between 20 to 40\% for medium to heavy nuclei, a recently proposed effective potential leads to a decrease of only 2 - 4 \%. In view of these results, we provide a closer examination of the approximations used in deriving the local equivalent potentials and propose that apart from the scattering data, the alpha decay half-lives could be used as a complementary tool for constraining the nonlocality models.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while all nonlocal nuclear potential models reduce alpha-decay half-lives compared to local potentials, the magnitude of the reduction depends strongly on the model: the older Perey-Buck and São Paulo models give a 20–40% decrease for medium to heavy nuclei, whereas a newer "Mumbai" effective potential gives only a 2–4% decrease, suggesting that alpha-decay half-lives could serve as a complementary constraint for nonlocality models beyond scattering data.

## Method

The authors start from a density-dependent double-folding (DF) alpha-nucleus potential and construct local equivalent potentials in three nonlocal models: the Perey-Buck (PB) model, the São Paulo (SP) model, and the Mumbai model. They then compute alpha-decay half-lives for several spherical nuclei using a semiclassical JWKB tunneling approach with s-wave emission, setting the preformation probability P_alpha = 1 to isolate the effect of nonlocality. The resulting half-lives from each model are compared to those obtained with the local double-folding potential alone.

## Key numbers

* For medium to heavy nuclei, the Perey-Buck and São Paulo models produce a 20–40% decrease in half-lives relative to the local potential.
* The Mumbai effective potential produces only a 2–4% decrease in half-lives relative to the local potential.
* The nonlocality parameter beta is taken as 0.22 fm, following the prescription in Jackson.
* Experimental half-lives used for comparison include: 254Fm (1.2 x 10^4 s), 212Po (2.99 x 10^-7 s), 210Po (1.2 x 10^7 s), 180W (5.7 x 10^25 s), 168Pt (2 x 10^-3 s), 144Nd (7.1 x 10^22 s), and 106Te (7 x 10^-5 s).

## Context

This work extends the density-dependent double-folding model for alpha decay (Weber et al., various years; Ni and Ren, review) by introducing nonlocal nuclear potentials, which had previously been studied mainly in the context of elastic scattering (Perey and Buck, 1962; Chamon et al., 2002). The paper contrasts the older Perey-Buck and São Paulo local equivalent potentials with the newer energy-independent Mumbai potential (Kelkar et al., 2017, 2018), finding that the Mumbai potential behaves differently at small distances (r -> 0) and produces much smaller nonlocality effects. The results suggest that alpha-decay half-lives could be used alongside scattering data to constrain nonlocality models, particularly because nonlocality is expected to affect reaction processes more than elastic scattering.
