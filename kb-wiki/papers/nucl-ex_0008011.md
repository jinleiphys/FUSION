# Study of Residual Product Nuclide Yields in 1.0 GeV Proton-Irradiated 208-Pb and 2.6 GeV Proton-Irradiated nat-W Thin Targets

> 113 residual product nuclide yields in a 1.0 GeV proton-irradiated thin monoisotopic 208-Pb sample and 107 residual product nuclide yields in a 2.6 GeV proton-irradiated nat-W sample have been measured and simulated by 8 different codes. The irradiations were made using proton beams extracted from the ITEP synchrotron. The nuclide yields were gamma-spectrometered directly using a high-resolution Ge-detector. The gamma-spectra were processed by the GENIE-2000 code. The ITEP-developed SIGMA code was used together with the PCNUDAT nuclear decay database to identify the gamma-lines and to determine the cross-sections. The 27-Al(p,x)Na-22 reaction was used to monitor the proton flux. The measured yields are compared with calculation the LAHET (with ISABEL and Bertini options), CEM95, CEM2k, CASCADE, CASCADE/INPE, INUCL, and YIELDX codes. Estimates of the mean deviation factor are used to demonstrate the predictive power of the codes. The results obtained may be of interest in studying the parameters of the Pb and W target modules of the hybrid Accelerator-Driven System (ADS) facilities.

## Key claim

The paper provides measured yields of 113 residual product nuclides from 1.0 GeV proton-irradiated thin monoisotopic 208-Pb and 107 yields from 2.6 GeV proton-irradiated nat-W, but the abstract's claim of "comparison with 8 different codes" glosses over the fact that not all codes could simulate all measured products, and the predictive power varies dramatically by mass region and target, with mean deviation factors ranging from 1.62 to 3.78 depending on the code.

## Method

Thin foil targets of monoisotopic 208-Pb (139.4 mg/cm2, 97.2% enrichment) and nat-W (38.1 mg/cm2) were irradiated with extracted proton beams from the ITEP U-10 synchrotron at average flux densities of 1.4e10 p/cm2 and 2.8e10 p/cm2, respectively. Residual product nuclide yields were measured by direct gamma-spectrometry using a high-resolution Ge-detector, with spectra processed by the GENIE-2000 code and gamma-line identification and cross-section determination performed by the ITEP-developed SIGMA code using the PCNUDAT nuclear decay database. The 27-Al(p,x)22-Na reaction was used to monitor the proton flux, and the measured yields were compared with simulations from eight codes: LAHET (with ISABEL and Bertini options), CEM95, CEM2k, CASCADE, CASCADE/INPE, INUCL, and YIELDX.

## Key numbers

- 113 residual product nuclide yields measured from 1.0 GeV proton-irradiated 208-Pb, comprising 6 independent yields, 17 metastable state yields, 15 yields of metastable and ground states, 64 cumulative yields, and 11 supra cumulative yields
- 107 residual product nuclide yields measured from 2.6 GeV proton-irradiated nat-W, comprising 6 independent yields, 9 metastable state yields, 5 yields of metastable and ground states, 86 cumulative yields, and 1 supra cumulative yield
- Experimental errors ranging within approximately 6-35 percent
- For 1.0 GeV Pb data, the best mean deviation factor was 1.62 (CEM2k, with 38 of 66 simulated yields within 30 percent of experiment), while the worst was 2.87 (INUCL and YIELDX)
- For 2.6 GeV W data, the best mean deviation factor was 2.04 (YIELDX, with 25 of 93 simulated yields within 30 percent), while the worst was 3.78 (INUCL)
- For 1.0 GeV Pb, LAHET achieved 41 of 90 simulated yields within 30 percent of experiment, with mean deviation factor 2.06
- For 2.6 GeV W, CASCADE achieved 48 of 91 simulated yields within 30 percent, with mean deviation factor 2.24
- The 208-Pb sample had isotopic composition: 97.2 percent 208-Pb, 1.93 percent 207-Pb, 0.87 percent 206-Pb, less than 0.01 percent 204-Pb
- Average proton flux density during Pb irradiation was 1.4e10 p/cm2; during W irradiation was 2.8e10 p/cm2

## Context

This work extends earlier measurements of residual nuclide yields from high-energy proton irradiation of lead and tungsten, materials considered as promising target candidates for Accelerator-Driven System (ADS) facilities, and provides benchmark data for code validation. The results are compared with data from ZSR Hannover and GSI Darmstadt (referenced as [6] in the paper), showing generally consistent values for products such as 200-Tl (ITEP: 22.7 plus or minus 1.5 mb; ZSR: 22.3 plus or minus 6.1 mb; GSI: 17.0 plus or minus 0.4 mb). The comprehensive dataset enables systematic evaluation of eight simulation codes, revealing that while all codes adequately describe weak spallation reactions (within a factor of 2), their predictive power varies substantially in deep spallation and fission product regions, with CEM2k showing the best agreement for 1 GeV Pb spallation data but overestimating fission cross sections at 2.6 GeV by a factor of 6 compared to the expected value of about 41 mb from Prokofiev (2000).
