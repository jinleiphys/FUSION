# Tackling the infamous $g^6$ term of the QCD pressure

> We report on ongoing efforts to tackle an important open problem in QCD thermodynamics, namely an evaluation of the pressure to order $g^6$ in a weak-coupling expansion, corresponding to four loops. In particular, we identify a class of contributing Feynman sum-integrals with lower-loop factors, describe the formalism to tensor decompose those, and manage to map them onto scalar master sum-integrals that have already been evaluated in the literature.

## Key claim

The paper reports ongoing progress toward evaluating the previously uncomputed order-\(g^6\) (four-loop) contribution to the QCD pressure in a weak-coupling expansion, specifically for pure Yang-Mills theory. The authors identify a class of factorizable four-loop Feynman sum-integrals and demonstrate that, after tensor decomposition, these can be mapped onto scalar master sum-integrals already known from lower-loop calculations, but they note that the remaining genuine four-loop sum-integrals still require evaluation.

## Method

The authors generate all four-loop vacuum diagrams contributing to the pressure via a skeleton expansion of the Schwinger-Dyson equations, separating diagrams into 2-particle irreducible (2PI) and 2-particle reducible (2PR) types. They define a single four-loop family of massless inverse propagators in momentum space and develop a tensor decomposition method for thermal sum-integrals that accounts for the reduced (3d rotational) symmetry by introducing an extra four-vector \(U=(1,\vec 0)\). The method is applied to decouple factorizable four-loop integrals into products of lower-loop (one-loop, two-loop, three-loop) scalar sum-integrals, using known results for the latter.

## Key numbers

* The four-loop pressure contribution is of order \(g^6\) in the weak-coupling expansion, corresponding to four loops in the loop expansion.
* The calculation uses dimensional regularization with \(d=3-2\varepsilon\) for spatial dimensions and \(D=4-2\varepsilon\) for spacetime dimensions.
* The one-loop scalar tadpole result is given as \(I_s^a = \frac{2T\,\zeta(2s-a-d)}{(2\pi T)^{2s-a-d}}\,\frac{\Gamma(s-d/2)}{(4\pi)^{d/2}\,\Gamma(s)}\) for \(d=3-2\varepsilon\).
* The tensor decomposition yields coefficients involving products such as \(\prod_{j=0}^{k-1} \frac1{D+1-2s+2j}\) for the 1-loop tensor tadpole case.
* Four specific sum-integrals from sector 1008 (\(I_{10}, I_{11}, I_{12}, I_{13}\)) are reduced to known lower-loop sum-integrals, including three-loop structures \(J_1, J_{11}, J_{12}, V_1, V_2, V_4\) that had been evaluated in prior work (Gynther 2007, Moller 2010, Andersen 2008, Schroder 2012, Ghisoiu 2012).

## Context

This work extends the program outlined by Braaten (1995) and Kajantie (2002) to compute the QCD pressure to order \(g^6\) using a hierarchy of effective theories, which reduces the problem to evaluating hard-scale four-loop contributions. The only previous results at this order were for scalar theory (Gynther 2007) and the large-\(N_f\) limit of QCD (Gynther 2009). The tensor decomposition method developed here enables evaluation of factorizable four-loop sum-integrals, and the remaining challenge is to evaluate the genuine (non-factorizable) four-loop sum-integrals, which may require entirely new methods for sectors such as 511, 1022, and 1011.
