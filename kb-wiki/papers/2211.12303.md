# Momentum transport of chiral modes in a thermal QCD medium

> We have investigated the effect of a weak magnetic field on momentum transport in a thermal QCD medium at finite quark chemical potential using a semiclassical kinetic theory. In the presence of a magnetic field, the momentum transport coefficients acquire a tensor structure, reflected by the five shear $\left(η_0, η_1, η_2, η_3\right.$ and $\left.η_4\right)$ and two bulk viscous components $\left(ζ_0\right.$ and $\left. ζ_1\right)$. The weak magnetic field removes the degeneracy in the effective mass of flavours, leading to different masses for the left-handed (L) and right-handed (R) chiral modes of quarks. The coefficients, $η_0, η_1$ and $η_3$ decrease with magnetic field in L mode and increase in $\mathrm{R}$ mode, whereas, $η_2$ and $η_4$ increase in both L and $\mathrm{R}$ mode. The bulk viscous coefficients, $ζ_0$ and $ζ_1$ increase with magnetic field for both L and R mode. The shear and bulk viscous coefficients positively amplify with baryon asymmetry.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how a weak magnetic field (satisfying \(T^2 > q_f B > m_{f0}^2\)) and finite quark chemical potential affect shear and bulk viscosity in a thermal QCD medium, finding that the magnetic field lifts the degeneracy between left-handed and right-handed chiral modes of quarks by giving them different effective masses, which in turn produces opposite trends in some shear viscosity components between the two chiral modes.

## Method

The authors employ a semiclassical kinetic theory with the relativistic Boltzmann transport equation under relaxation time approximation, using a quasiparticle description where interactions are encoded as medium-generated masses. The quark self-energy is computed to first order in the magnetic field via the Schwinger-Dyson equation, yielding distinct effective masses for left-handed and right-handed chiral modes. The viscous coefficients are extracted by decomposing the dissipative tensor using fourth-rank projection tensors in terms of hydrodynamic and magnetic degrees of freedom, with the magnetic field treated as a weak perturbation to the thermal medium.

## Key numbers

- The weak magnetic field condition is defined as \(T^2 > q_f B > m_{f0}^2\), where \(m_{f0}\) is the current quark mass.
- The thermal mass squared for quarks at finite chemical potential is \(m_{th}^2 = \frac{1}{8}g^2 C_F (T^2 + \mu^2/\pi^2)\) with \(C_F = 4/3\) for \(N_C = 3\).
- The left-handed mode mass squared is \(m_L^2 = m_{th}^2 + 4g^2 C_F M^2\) and the right-handed mode mass squared is \(m_R^2 = m_{th}^2 - 4g^2 C_F M^2\), where \(M^2 = \frac{|q_f B|}{16\pi^2} \left( \frac{\pi T}{2 m_{f0}} - \ln 2 + \frac{7 \mu^2 \zeta(3)}{8\pi^2 T^2} \right)\).
- The one-loop running coupling constant is \(\alpha_s(\Lambda^2) = 1 / [b_1 \ln(\Lambda^2 / \Lambda_{\overline{MS}}^2)]\) with \(b_1 = (11 N_c - 2 N_f)/(12\pi)\) and \(\Lambda_{\overline{MS}} = 0.176\) GeV.
- The magnetic-field-dependent running coupling is \(\alpha_s(\Lambda^2, |eB|) = \alpha_s(\Lambda^2) / [1 + b_1 \alpha_s(\Lambda^2) \ln(\Lambda^2/(\Lambda^2 + |eB|))]\).
- The shear viscosity coefficients \(\eta_0, \eta_1, \eta_3\) decrease with magnetic field in the left-handed mode and increase in the right-handed mode; \(\eta_2\) and \(\eta_4\) increase in both modes.
- The bulk viscosity coefficients \(\zeta_0\) and \(\zeta_1\) increase with magnetic field for both left-handed and right-handed modes.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of viscous coefficients in magnetized QCD matter, such as those using Kubo formalism by Hattori et al. and kinetic theory approaches by Denicol et al. (2018) and others, but specifically addresses the weak magnetic field regime where transverse and Hall components contribute, unlike the strong field case where only longitudinal components survive. The inclusion of distinct chiral mode masses contrasts with the approach of Shubha et al., who used pure thermal mass without magnetic field correction. The results provide input parameters for relativistic magnetohydrodynamic simulations of quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
