# The Shear Viscosity to Entropy Density Ratio of Hagedorn States

> The fireball concept of Rolf Hagedorn, developed in the 1960's, is an alternative description of hadronic matter. Using a recently derived mass spectrum, we use the transport model GiBUU to calculate the shear viscosity of a gas of such Hagedorn states, applying the Green-Kubo method to Monte-Carlo calculations. Since the entropy density is rising ad infinitum near $T_H$, this leads to a very low shear viscosity to entropy density ratio near $T_H$. Further, by comparing our results with analytic expressions, we find a nice extrapolation behavior, indicating that a gas of Hagedorn states comes close or even below the boundary $1/4π$ from AdS-CFT.

## Key claim

Using the Green-Kubo method with Monte-Carlo simulations of the transport model GiBUU, the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of a gas of Hagedorn states approaches or falls below the AdS-CFT bound of \(1/4\pi\) near the Hagedorn temperature \(T_H\), but only when analytic extrapolations to infinite Hagedorn state masses are included; the direct Monte-Carlo results, limited to masses below 10 GeV, remain above this bound.

## Method

The authors used the GiBUU transport model to simulate a gas of Hagedorn states in a fixed box volume of \((10\ \mathrm{fm})^3\) with non-reflecting boundaries, where all interactions are replaced by Hagedorn state creation and decay processes (\(2\to1\) and \(1\to2\)). The shear viscosity was extracted via the Green-Kubo formalism by computing the time-correlation function of the shear stress tensor component \(\pi^{xy}\) from the Monte-Carlo trajectories, fitting an exponential decay to obtain the relaxation time \(\tau\), and combining it with the variance \(C^{xy}(0)\). The Hagedorn mass spectrum used was a recently derived numerical solution of the bootstrap equation with baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge conservation, tabulated up to masses of 10 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The Hagedorn temperature of the spectrum is \(T_H = 165\ \mathrm{MeV}\) with exponent \(a = 2.98\) (for a Hagedorn state radius \(R = 1.0\ \mathrm{fm}\)).
- The constant cross section used for the Hagedorn gas is \(\sigma = \pi R^2 = 31.4\ \mathrm{mb}\); for the pion gas a constant cross section \(\sigma = 30\ \mathrm{mb}\) was used.
- For the pion gas, the time step size was \(\Delta t = 0.1\ \mathrm{fm}\) with \(N_t = 30000\) timesteps; for the Hagedorn gas, \(\Delta t = 0.01\ \mathrm{fm}\) with \(N_t = 25000\) timesteps.
- The analytic extrapolation of the entropy density using the fitted Hagedorn spectrum shows a divergence as \(T\) approaches \(T_H\); the Monte-Carlo results for \(\eta/s\) at temperatures below about 160 MeV are consistent with the analytic estimates, while the extrapolation to infinite masses yields \(\eta/s\) below \(1/4\pi\) at \(T_H\).

## Context

This work extends the Hagedorn fireball concept (Hagedorn, 1960s) and the bootstrap equation reformulation by Frautschi (1971) by computing transport coefficients for a gas of Hagedorn states, using a recently derived mass spectrum with explicit conservation of baryon number, strangeness, and charge (Beitel et al., 2014; Gallmeister et al., 2017). It contrasts with prior Green-Kubo studies of hadronic matter using models such as UrQMD (Muronga, 2003; Demir, 2008), SMASH (Rose, 2017), and pHSD (Ozvenchuk, 2012), and with the S-matrix based Hadron Resonance Model via the Chapman-Enskog method (Dash, 2019). The results suggest that the singular behavior of \(\eta/s\) near \(T_H\) may be cured by a phase transition to a phase with increasing \(\eta/s\), since the Hagedorn temperature is a limiting temperature.
