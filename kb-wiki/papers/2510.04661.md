# Overview of the latest developments in understanding the initial state and thermalization

> A proper description of the non-equilibrium matter preceding the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in heavy-ion collisions and its observable consequences remain a major theoretical challenge, while at the same time offering new opportunities for experimental exploration. In these proceedings, I provide an overview of studies presented in talks and posters at Quark Matter 2025 on this topic. We will focus on the latest developments regarding the features and the numerical description of the non-equilibrium pre-QGP matter, as well as the potential to use hard probes as a means to study the hydrodynamization dynamics of the QCD plasma.

# Overview of the latest developments in understanding the initial state and thermalization

## Key claim
The weak-coupling picture of hydrodynamization in heavy-ion collisions, combining initial-state modeling, classical-statistical simulations, and QCD kinetic theory, is being refined by extending studies to fully three-dimensional dynamics and embedding them in phenomenological frameworks, but this picture relies on weak-coupling assumptions and the more realistic coupling lambda ~ 10 is neither weak nor strong, meaning the strong-coupling holographic approach remains a viable alternative.

## Method
The paper summarizes theoretical developments presented at Quark Matter 2025, focusing on numerical descriptions of the non-equilibrium pre-QGP matter and the use of hard probes (electromagnetic probes, jets, heavy flavors, and quarkonia) to study hydrodynamization dynamics. Specific approaches reviewed include classical-statistical simulations of the Glasma, QCD kinetic theory in the diffusion approximation, adiabatic hydrodynamization frameworks with eigenvalue analysis, and hybrid frameworks such as KøMPøST generalized to multiple spatial dimensions.

## Key numbers
- The saturation scale Q_s defines transverse domain sizes ~ 1/Q_s in the Glasma, with timescale tau less than or about 1/Q_s ~ 0.1–0.2 fm/c
- Typical local thermalization timescales are tau less than or about 1 fm/c
- Heavy-quark diffusion coefficient kappa_i and jet quenching parameter hat q_i show anisotropic ordering kappa_z > kappa_T and hat q_z > hat q_y in both Glasma and kinetic phases
- The total amount of heavy-quark momentum broadening from the pre-equilibrium regime is of the same parametric order as in the hydrodynamic phase: <Delta p^2>_neq ~ <Delta p^2>_eq
- In the Glasma phase, transport coefficients show an initial increase kappa_i ~ tau due to a coherence effect, with negative kappa_z < 0 observed in two-dimensional Glasma-like systems

## Context
This overview extends the standard weak-coupling picture of hydrodynamization established by Berges (2020), Kurkela (2015), and Baier (2000), and contrasts it with the strong-coupling holographic approach. The work enables multi-stage phenomenological frameworks that incorporate pre-equilibrium dynamics into jet quenching and heavy-flavor suppression analyses, with implications for the high-luminosity LHC Runs 4 and 5, small collision systems, and CBM-FAIR experiments.
