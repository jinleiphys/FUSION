# Spherical Hartree-Fock calculations with linear momentum projection before the variation.Part II: Spectral functions and spectroscopic factors

> The hole--spectral functions and from these the spectroscopic factors have been calculated in an Galilei--invariant way for the ground state wave functions resulting from spherical Hartree--Fock calculations with projection onto zero total linear momentum before the variation for the nuclei 4He, 12C, 16O, 28Si, 32S and 40Ca. The results are compared to those of the conventional approach which uses the ground states resulting from usual spherical Hartree--Fock calculations subtracting the kinetic energy of the center of mass motion before the variation and to the results obtained analytically with oscillator occupations.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that Galilei-invariant (projected) spectroscopic factors for uncorrelated systems differ considerably from the conventional value of one, meaning that deviations from unity in experimental spectroscopic factors cannot be automatically attributed to nucleon-nucleon correlations; instead, the projected results must be taken as the reference baseline for an uncorrelated system.

## Method
The authors computed hole-spectral functions and spectroscopic factors in a Galilei-invariant way for ground states obtained from spherical Hartree-Fock calculations with projection onto zero total linear momentum before the variation (CMPSHF). They used the Brink-Boeker force B1 complemented with a short-range two-body spin-orbit term derived from the D1S parametrization of the Gogny force, with a single-particle basis of up to 19 oscillator major shells, for the nuclei 4He, 12C, 16O, 28Si, 32S, and 40Ca. Results were compared to conventional spherical Hartree-Fock calculations (subtracting center-of-mass kinetic energy before variation) and to analytical results from pure oscillator occupations.

## Key numbers
* For 16O, the projected spectroscopic factor for the 0s-hole is 4/5 (0.80), representing a 20% depletion relative to the conventional value of 1.
* For 16O, the projected spectroscopic factor for the 0p-hole is 16/15 (approximately 1.067), showing an enhancement above 1.
* For 40Ca, the projected spectroscopic factor for the 0s̃-hole (Gram-Schmidt orthonormalized) is 1410/1521 (approximately 0.927), and for the 0p-hole it is 1400/1521 (approximately 0.920).
* For 40Ca, the projected spectroscopic factor for the 1s-hole is 1600/1521 (approximately 1.052), and for the 0d-hole it is 1600/1521 (approximately 1.052).
* The violation of the sum rule for projected Hartree-Fock results (epsilon/A) varies only between 0.12% and 0.35% across the studied nuclei.
* The oscillator length used for 4He was b = 1.41 Fm, for 12C b = 1.72 Fm, for 16O b = 1.79 Fm, for 28Si b = 1.85 Fm, for 32S b = 1.91 Fm, and for 40Ca b = 1.97 Fm.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Rodriguez-Guzman and Schmid, 2005, Part I) on spherical Hartree-Fock with linear momentum projection before the variation, now analyzing spectral functions and spectroscopic factors. It builds on analytical model investigations by the same group using oscillator determinants (Rodriguez-Guzman and Schmid, 2004) and confirms that the depletion/enhancement pattern predicted there persists in more realistic Hartree-Fock wave functions. The results contradict the conventional assumption that uncorrelated systems yield spectroscopic factors of exactly one for all occupied orbits, and provide the necessary reference baseline for interpreting experimental data from quasi-elastic electron scattering and one-nucleon transfer reactions in terms of genuine nucleon-nucleon correlations.
