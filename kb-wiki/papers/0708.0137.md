# Viscosity in the excluded volume hadron gas model

> The shear viscosity $η$ in the van der Waals excluded volume hadron-resonance gas model is considered. For the shear viscosity the result of the non-relativistic gas of hard-core particles is extended to the mixture of particles with different masses, but equal values of hard-core radius r. The relativistic corrections to hadron average momenta in thermal equilibrium are also taken into account. The ratio of the viscosity $η$ to the entropy density s is studied. It monotonously decreases along the chemical freeze-out line in nucleus-nucleus collisions with increasing collision energy. As a function of hard-core radius r, a broad minimum of the ratio $η/s\approx 0.3$ near $r \approx 0.5$ fm is found at high collision energies. For the charge-neutral system at $T=T_c=180$ MeV, a minimum of the ratio $η/s\cong 0.24$ is reached for $r\cong 0.53$ fm. To justify a hydrodynamic approach to nucleus-nucleus collisions within the hadron phase the restriction from below, $r~ \ge ~0.2$ fm, on the hard-core hadron radius should be fulfilled in the excluded volume hadron-resonance gas.

# Viscosity in the excluded volume hadron gas model

## Key claim
The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s in the excluded volume hadron-resonance gas model shows a broad minimum near η/s ≈ 0.3 for hard-core radius r ≈ 0.5 fm at high collision energies, but this result depends on the assumption that all hadron species share the same hard-core radius and that the Boltzmann approximation is used. For a charge-neutral system at T = Tc = 180 MeV, the minimum reaches η/s ≈ 0.24 at r ≈ 0.53 fm, which is above the conjectured lower bound of 1/4π.

## Method
The authors extended the non-relativistic shear viscosity formula for a gas of hard-core spheres to a multi-component mixture of hadron resonances with different masses but equal hard-core radius r, using the van der Waals excluded volume procedure. Relativistic corrections to average thermal momenta were included via modified Bessel function ratios K5/2(m/T)/K2(m/T). The thermodynamic functions (pressure, particle densities, entropy density) were calculated using the THERMUS package with Boltzmann statistics, and the viscosity was evaluated along the chemical freeze-out line for central nucleus-nucleus collisions from SIS to LHC energies.

## Key numbers
- Minimum η/s ≈ 0.3 for r ≈ 0.5 fm at high collision energies along the chemical freeze-out line.
- For charge-neutral system at T = Tc = 180 MeV, minimum η/s ≈ 0.24 for r ≈ 0.53 fm.
- At the highest collision energy (√sNN = 5500 GeV, LHC), η/s = 0.29 for r = 0.5 fm and η/s = 0.46 for r = 0.3 fm.
- At the lowest collision energy (√sNN = 2.32 GeV, SIS), η/s = 0.82 for r = 0.5 fm and η/s = 1.94 for r = 0.3 fm.
- The ideal gas limit (r → 0) is singular: η/s diverges as r⁻².
- To justify hydrodynamic approach, the hard-core radius must satisfy r ≥ 0.2 fm, corresponding to η/s ≤ 5.

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations of η/s in the hadron phase, which were limited to pion gas and pion-kaon mixtures (Refs. eta4, etameson1, etapion1, etapion2) or performed within microscopic transport models such as UrQMD (Ref. transport1) and URASiMA (Ref. transport2). The excluded volume hadron gas model had previously been used to study particle number fluctuations (vdw-fluc, 2006), and the present paper applies it to viscosity. The results provide a baseline for η/s in the hadron phase near the transition to the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP), enabling comparison with lattice QCD estimates (visc-lQCD2, finding η/s = 0.134 ± 0.033 at T = 1.65 Tc) and quasiparticle model results (visc-sQGP, finding η/s ≈ 0.34 at T ≈ 1.5 Tc). The paper also addresses the conjectured lower bound η/s ≥ 1/4π from Ref. eta2 (2005).
