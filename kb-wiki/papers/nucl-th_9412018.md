# Induced Parity Nonconserving Interaction and Enhancement of Two-Nucleon Parity Nonconserving Forces

> Two-nucleon parity nonconserving (PNC) interaction induced by the single-particle PNC weak potential and the two-nucleon residual strong interaction is considered. An approximate analytical formula for this Induced PNC Interaction (IPNCI) between proton and neutron is derived ($Q({\bf r} {\bf σ}_{p} \times {\bf σ}_{n}) δ({\bf r}_{p}-{\bf r}_{n})$), and the interaction constant is estimated. As a result of coherent contributions from the nucleons to the PNC potential, IPNCI is an order of magnitude stronger ($\sim A^{1/3}$) than the residual weak two-nucleon interaction and has a different coordinate and isotopic structure (e.g., the strongest part of IPNCI does not contribute to the PNC mean field). IPNCI plays an important role in the formation of PNC effects, e.g., in neutron-nucleus reactions. In that case, it is a technical way to take into account the contribution of the distant (small) components of a compound state which dominates the result. The absence of such enhancement ($\sim A^{1/3}$) in the case of T- and P-odd interaction completes the picture.

# Induced Parity Nonconserving Interaction and Enhancement of Two-Nucleon Parity Nonconserving Forces

## Key claim
The induced parity nonconserving interaction (IPNCI) between a proton and neutron, arising from the combination of the single-particle weak potential and the residual strong interaction, is approximately an order of magnitude stronger than the residual two-nucleon weak interaction, but only under the condition that the coherent contributions from all nucleons to the weak potential are taken into account; the enhancement factor scales as \(A^{1/3}\) and does not appear in the case of T- and P-odd interactions.

## Method
The authors derive an approximate analytical formula for the IPNCI operator, \(Q \, \mathbf{r} \, (\boldsymbol{\sigma}_p \times \boldsymbol{\sigma}_n) \, \delta(\mathbf{r}_p - \mathbf{r}_n)\), using a unitary transformation approach that compensates the single-particle weak potential in the nuclear Hamiltonian. They employ the Landau-Migdal parametrization of the residual strong interaction with contact, spin- and isospin-dependent terms, and evaluate the commutator \([ \hat{A}, V_S ]\) to obtain the effective two-body PNC interaction. The analysis includes both velocity-independent and velocity-dependent components of the strong interaction, with the latter requiring renormalization of the weak coupling constants via a compensation equation.

## Key numbers
- The IPNCI enhancement factor relative to the residual two-nucleon weak interaction is \(\sim A^{1/3}\); for heavy nuclei with \(A \simeq 114\) to \(240\), this yields an order-of-magnitude enhancement.
- The Landau-Migdal interaction constant is \(C = 300 \, \text{MeV} \cdot \text{fm}^3\).
- The weak potential constant is \(\varepsilon = 1.0 \times 10^{-8} g^W\), where \(g^W\) is the dimensionless weak coupling constant.
- The Fermi constant is \(G = 10^{-5} \, m^{-2}\) (with \(m\) the nucleon mass).
- The Fermi momentum \(p_F\) and internucleon distance \(r_0 = 1.15 \, \text{fm}\) satisfy \(p_F r_0 \sim 1\).
- The velocity-dependent strong interaction constants are \(h_1 = -0.5\) and \(h'_1 = -0.26\), leading to an additional renormalization factor \(\tilde{\xi} \sim 1.5 \xi\).
- Numerical calculations show the IPNCI contribution to root-mean-square matrix elements between compound states is 7 to 12 times larger than that of the initial weak interaction \(W\).
- For the regular PNC effect in neutron capture, the estimated asymmetry is \(P \sim 1.3 \times 10^{-3} \tilde{g}^W_n / \sqrt{E}\) (with \(E\) in eV), which is too small compared to the observed value \(P \simeq 0.3 / \sqrt{E}\) for \(^{232}\text{Th}\).

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of parity nonconservation in nuclei by addressing a limitation of the single-particle weak potential: it cannot mix opposite-parity valence orbitals within the principal components of compound states, as noted by Zaretsky (1970) and Kadmensky (1976). The IPNCI provides a mechanism to incorporate contributions from distant small components of compound states without explicit perturbation theory in the residual strong interaction. The result contrasts with the case of T- and P-odd interactions, where no \(A^{1/3}\) enhancement occurs, completing the picture for parity-violating effects in neutron-nucleus reactions. The approach builds on the Landau-Migdal theory of finite Fermi systems (Migdal, 1967; Brown, 1971) and enables more accurate calculations of PNC matrix elements in heavy nuclei such as those in the thorium-uranium region.
