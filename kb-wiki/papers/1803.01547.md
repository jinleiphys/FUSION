# Energy dependence of light (anti)nuclei and (anti)hypertriton production in the Au-Au collision from $\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} =5.0$ to $5020$ GeV

> The energy dependence of light (anti)nuclei and (anti)hypertriton production are investigated in central Au-Au collisions from AGS up to LHC energies at midrapidity, using the parton and hadron cascade model (PACIAE) together with the dynamically constrained phase-space coalescence model(DCPC). We find that the yields, yield ratios of the antiparticles to their corresponding particles, the coalescence parameters $B_A$ and the strangeness population factor $s_3$ of light (anti)nuclei and (anti)hypertriton strongly depend on the energy. Furthermore, we analyze and discuss the strangeness population factor $s_3$ and the coalescence parameters $B_A$, and find a transition point near by 20 GeV. These results thus suggest the potential usefulness of the $s_3$ and $B_A$ of light nuclei production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions as a direct probe of the transition point associated with the QCD critical phenomena. The results from PACIAE+DCPC model are well consistent with experimental data.

## Key claim
The PACIAE+DCPC model predicts a transition point near 20 GeV in the energy dependence of the coalescence parameters \(B_A\) and the strangeness population factor \(s_3\) for light (anti)nuclei and (anti)hypertriton production in central Au-Au collisions, suggesting this may be a direct probe of the QCD critical phenomena. The abstract presents this as a finding, but the full text clarifies that this is a model-based observation that "needs to be further studied."

## Method
The authors used the parton and hadron cascade model (PACIAE) to simulate the final-state hadrons from 0-10% centrality Au-Au collisions at midrapidity (\(|y|<0.5\)) for 16 collision energies from \(\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 5.0\) to 5020 GeV. They then applied the dynamically constrained phase-space coalescence model (DCPC) to these hadronic final states to form light (anti)nuclei (deuteron, helium-3) and (anti)hypertriton, using constraints on invariant mass and spatial distance between constituent nucleons and hyperons. The model parameters were tuned by fitting STAR data for \(p\), \(\overline{p}\), \(\Lambda\), and \(\overline{\Lambda}\) yields at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 7.7-200\) GeV.

## Key numbers
- The transition point in the energy dependence of \(B_A\) and \(s_3\) is observed near \(\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 20\) GeV.
- The strangeness population factor \(s_3\) saturates at approximately 0.8 in the high energy region (above 20 GeV).
- The study covers collision energies from \(\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 5.0\) GeV to 5020 GeV (5.02 TeV), with 16 specific energies: 5, 6, 7, 7.7, 9.9, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, 130, 200, 800, 2760, and 5020 GeV.
- The coalescence parameter \(\sqrt[A-1]{B_A}\) shows a minimum value around \(\sqrt{s_{\rm{NN}}} = 20\) GeV for light (anti)nuclei at \(p_T/A = 0.65\) GeV/c.

## Context
This work extends the PACIAE+DCPC approach previously applied to Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV and Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV (Dong et al., 2016; Chen et al., 2017) to a broad energy range from AGS to LHC energies. The results are consistent with STAR data for deuteron and anti-deuteron yields (STAR Collaboration, 2015) and with the statistical hadronization model (SHM) for Pb-Pb collisions (Andronic et al., 2011). The observed transition near 20 GeV in \(B_A\) and \(s_3\) suggests these observables could probe the QCD critical endpoint, connecting to prior work on the energy dependence of viscous coefficients and pion HBT results (Heinz and Kolb, 2002).
