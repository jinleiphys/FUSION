# A new approach for open quantum systems based on a phonon number representation of a harmonic oscillator bath

> To investigate a system coupled to a harmonic oscillator bath, we propose a new approach based on a phonon number representation of the bath. Compared to the method of the hierarchical equations of motion, the new approach is computationally much less expensive in a sense that a reduced density matrix is obtained by calculating the time evolution of vectors, instead of matrices, which enables one to deal with large dimensional systems. As a benchmark test, we consider a quantum damped harmonic oscillator, and show that the exact results can be well reproduced. In addition to the reduced density matrix, our approach also provides a link to the total wave function by introducing new boson operators.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new method for open quantum systems coupled to a harmonic oscillator bath that is computationally less expensive than the hierarchical equations of motion (HEOM) approach, but the authors explicitly state that their method includes a certain approximation compared to the vector-based HEOM approach of Ref. [NT18], and that the Taylor expansion of the influence functional corresponds to truncating the phonon number expansion of the bath.

## Method

The authors expand the Feynman-Vernon influence functional in a Taylor series with respect to the bath correlation function, then express the bath correlation function using a sum of products of time-dependent basis functions (chosen as Bessel functions) that are closed under differentiation. This expansion allows the reduced density matrix to be expressed as a sum over products of auxiliary wavefunctions (vectors) that satisfy a set of hierarchical equations of motion (HEOM), where the hierarchy index corresponds to the total phonon number in the bath. The method is benchmarked by applying it to a quantum damped harmonic oscillator (the Caldeira-Leggett model) and comparing the results to exact solutions.

## Key numbers

- The largest system previously analyzed with the conventional HEOM method was a quantum spin glass with 12 spins on triangular lattices, corresponding to a dimension of 4096.
- The method requires solving for \(\sum_{n=0}^{N_{\text{max}}} (n+K-1)!/n!(K-1)!\) expansion functions, where \(N_{\text{max}}\) is the truncation order and \(K\) is the number of basis functions used to expand the bath correlation.
- The bath correlation function \(L(t)\) is expanded as \(\frac{1}{\hbar} L(t_1-t_2) = \sum_{k=1}^{K} \lambda_k v_k(t_1) v_k^*(t_2)\), where \(\lambda_k\) are real and positive eigenvalues of the matrix \(D\) and \(v_k(t)\) are transformed basis functions.

## Context

This work extends the vector-based HEOM approach introduced by Ref. [NT18] by providing an alternative derivation based on a phonon number representation of the bath, which offers a clear physical interpretation and a link to the total wave function via newly introduced boson operators. The method is related to the coupled-channels (close-coupling) method used in nuclear physics (Ref. [HT12]) and can be seen as a way to handle an infinite number of harmonic oscillators in that framework. The approach enables the study of larger-dimensional systems than previously possible with conventional HEOM, which was limited to systems like the 12-spin quantum spin glass studied by Ref. [TT15].
