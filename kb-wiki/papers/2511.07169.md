# Isotone Chain Study of $\bar{p}$-atom spectroscopy and Strong Spin-orbit splittings

> Antiprotonic atoms have served as a pivotal tool for investigating the properties of baryon-baryon interactions, including their spin dependence. Examining the spin-orbit splittings induced by their strong interactions also could help clarify the nature of the $\bar{p}$-nucleus interactions and their fraction mediated by scalar and vector mesons. Although the strong spin-orbit splittings for a certain nucleus have been observed experimentally, thorough theoretical investigations have not yet been conducted. In this study, theoretical calculations based on the Dirac equation are systematically performed for nuclei along several isotone ``chains''. As a result, it is found that the magnitude of the strong spin-orbit splittings exhibits a significant dependence not only on the corresponding level shifts and widths almost linearly, but also on whether the optical potential enters as a vector or scalar potential. A simple perturbative analysis indicates that the relativistic corrections have a dominant effect the magnitude of the splittings. These results are expected to provide deeper insights into $\bar{p}$-nucleus interactions, and by extension baryon-baryon interactions, as well as into the properties of the mesons that mediate them.

## Key claim
The magnitude of strong spin-orbit splittings in antiprotonic atoms depends almost linearly on the corresponding level shifts and widths, and also depends significantly on whether the optical potential enters as a vector or scalar potential in the Dirac equation, with relativistic corrections having a dominant effect on the splitting magnitude.

## Method
The authors performed systematic theoretical calculations using the Dirac equation for antiprotonic atoms along isotone chains (fixed neutron number, varying proton number), specifically the N=28 chain (48Ca, 50Ti, 52Cr, 54Fe) and N=50 chain (86Kr, 88Sr, 90Zr, 92Mo), with additional sub-chains. They employed the optical model with Friedman's best fit parameter b0 = 1.3+1.9i fm and Wycech's best fit parameters b0 = 1.8+0.95i fm with c0 = 1.05i fm, and examined two cases where the optical potential enters entirely as a vector potential or entirely as a scalar potential.

## Key numbers
- Friedman's best fit optical potential parameter: b0 = 1.3+1.9i fm
- Wycech's best fit parameters: b0 = 1.8+0.95i fm, c0 = 1.05i fm
- Anomalous magnetic moment of antiproton: kappa = 1.79284734
- Averaged nucleon mass: M_N = 938.918 MeV
- Spin-orbit splitting for n=11 to 10 states of antiprotonic 208Pb calculated as 1199.88 eV, consistent with experimental data of 1199(5) eV
- Neutron skin thickness parameterization: Delta r_np = -0.09 + 1.46*delta, where delta = (N_n - N_p)/(N_n + N_p)
- Nuclear density diffuseness parameter a_q fixed to 2.3 fm/(4*ln(3))
- Superconducting micro-calorimeter detector resolution: 50-70 eV in the energy range of interest

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical studies of antiprotonic atoms that relied primarily on the spin-independent Klein-Gordon equation, such as the global fitting efforts by Batty (1995) and Friedman (2005). It builds on the authors' prior investigation of calcium isotopes (Yoshimura, 2024) which suggested the importance of isovector terms. The study addresses unresolved questions from experimental observations of strong spin-orbit splitting in 174Yb reported by Kreissl (1988) and the lack of such splitting in 172Yb and 176Yb reported by Klos (2004). The results are expected to guide future experiments using superconducting micro-calorimeter detectors for high-precision antiprotonic atom spectroscopy.
