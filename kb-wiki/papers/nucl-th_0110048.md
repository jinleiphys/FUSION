# Final state interaction contribution to the response of confined relativistic particles

> We report studies of the response of a massless particle confined by a potential. At large momentum transfer q it exhibits \tilde{y} or equivalently Nachtmann ξscaling, and acquires a constant width independent of q. This width has a contribution from the final state interactions of the struck particle, which persists in the q->\infty limit. The width of the response predicted using plane wave impulse approximation is smaller because of the neglect of final state interactions in that approximation. However, the exact response may be obtained by folding the approximate response with a function representing final state interaction effects. We also study the response obtained from the momentum distribution assuming that the particle is on the energy shell both before and after being struck. Quantitative results are presented for the special case of a linear confining potential. In this case the response predicted with the on-shell approximation has correct values for the total strength, mean energy and width, however its shape is wrong.

## Key claim

The width of the response of a confined massless particle at large momentum transfer has a contribution from final state interactions (FSI) that persists in the \(|\mathbf{q}| \to \infty\) limit, and this FSI contribution is neglected in the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA); however, the exact response can be obtained by folding the PWIA response with a function representing FSI effects.

## Method

The response \(R(\mathbf{q},\nu)\) of a massless particle confined by a linear (flux-tube) potential \(V(r) = \sqrt{\sigma} r\) was calculated exactly by summing over all energy eigenstates \(|I\rangle\) of the Hamiltonian \(H = \sqrt{|\mathbf{p}|^2} + \sqrt{\sigma} r\). Analytic expressions for the first and second moments (mean excitation energy \(\overline{\nu}\) and width \(\Delta\)) were derived in the scaling limit \(|\mathbf{q}| \to \infty\) for a general potential, and compared with the moments from the PWIA and the on-shell approximation (OSA). Numerical results for the response at \(|\mathbf{q}| = 10\) GeV were obtained by smoothing the discrete exact spectrum with a Lorentzian of width \(\Gamma_0\).

## Key numbers

* The exact width \(\Delta\) of the response for \(\Gamma_0 \to 0\) is 409 MeV, while the PWIA width \(\Delta_{PWIA} = 326\) MeV, for a linear confining potential with \(\sqrt{\sigma} = 1\) GeV/fm.
* The full-width at half-maximum (FWHM) of a Gaussian response is approximately \(5\Delta/3\); the exact and PWIA responses have larger FWHM because they are not exactly Gaussian.
* The exact response was calculated for \(|\mathbf{q}| \leq 10\) GeV, with the scaling limit obtained at \(|\mathbf{q}| = 10\) GeV.
* The folding function \(F\) obtained by deconvolution peaks at \(\nu \sim 0.2\) GeV, while the approximate Gaussian folding function \(F_A\) peaks at \(\nu = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Paris, 2001) of the response of a massless particle confined by a linear potential, which demonstrated \(\tilde{y}\) (Nachtmann \(\xi\)) scaling. It contrasts the PWIA, commonly used in nuclear physics for electron scattering from nuclei (Frois, 1991; Benhar, 1993), and the OSA, used in hadron physics for deep inelastic scattering (DIS) of leptons by hadrons (Brodsky). The results show that FSI effects, which have been studied in nuclear targets (BFFMPS, 1991; Benhar, 1993) and liquid helium (Sokol, 1989), also persist in the scaling limit for confined constituents, and must be removed via a folding function before extracting momentum distributions from measured responses. The Gaussian folding function approximation provides a reasonable but imperfect representation of FSI effects.
