# Freeze-out conditions from net-proton and net-charge fluctuations at RHIC

> We calculate ratios of higher-order susceptibilities quantifying fluctuations in the number of net protons and in the net-electric charge using the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model. We take into account the effect of resonance decays, the kinematic acceptance cuts in rapidity, pseudo-rapidity and transverse momentum used in the experimental analysis, as well as a randomization of the isospin of nucleons in the hadronic phase. By comparing these results to the latest experimental data from the STAR collaboration, we determine the freeze-out conditions from net-electric charge and net-proton distributions and discuss their consistency.

# Freeze-out conditions from net-proton and net-charge fluctuations at RHIC

## Key claim
By simultaneously analyzing the lowest-order cumulant ratio σ²/M for net-electric charge and net-proton distributions within the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model, the authors extract freeze-out temperatures and baryo-chemical potentials for RHIC collision energies from 11.5 to 200 GeV. However, the extracted freeze-out parameters cannot simultaneously reproduce the higher-order cumulants (Sσ and κσ²) for net protons at lower collision energies, suggesting either limitations in the HRG model treatment of isospin randomization or the possible onset of chiral critical fluctuations at larger baryo-chemical potential.

## Method
The authors calculate ratios of higher-order susceptibilities for net-proton and net-electric charge fluctuations using the HRG model with hadronic states up to 2 GeV mass, incorporating resonance decays, kinematic acceptance cuts (rapidity |y|<0.5 and transverse momentum 0.4-0.8 GeV/c for net protons; pseudo-rapidity |η|<0.5 and pT 0.2-2 GeV/c for net charge), and isospin randomization of nucleons via Kitazawa-Asakawa (KA) corrections for Δ(1232) resonance regeneration. They compare these calculations to efficiency-corrected experimental data from the STAR collaboration for most central collisions (0-5%) and extract freeze-out conditions by simultaneously fitting two experimentally measured susceptibility ratios for each collision energy.

## Key numbers
- Freeze-out temperature Tch = 146.8 ± 1.2 MeV and baryo-chemical potential μB,ch = 24.3 ± 0.6 MeV at √s = 200 GeV
- Freeze-out temperature Tch = 135.5 ± 8.3 MeV and μB,ch = 326.7 ± 25.9 MeV at √s = 11.5 GeV
- Freeze-out temperature Tch = 148.4 ± 1.6 MeV and μB,ch = 192.5 ± 3.9 MeV at √s = 19.6 GeV
- Freeze-out temperature Tch = 148.5 ± 0.7 MeV and μB,ch = 140.4 ± 1.4 MeV at √s = 27 GeV
- Freeze-out temperature Tch = 151.2 ± 0.8 MeV and μB,ch = 99.9 ± 1.4 MeV at √s = 39 GeV
- Freeze-out temperature Tch = 149.9 ± 0.5 MeV and μB,ch = 66.4 ± 0.6 MeV at √s = 62.4 GeV
- The extracted freeze-out temperatures are constrained to better than 5 MeV for √s > 11.5 GeV
- The smallest collision energy considered is √s = 11.5 GeV; below this energy isospin randomization is not expected to be realized

## Context
This work extends previous HRG model studies of fluctuations (Karsch 2010, Fu, Garg 2013, Nahrgang 2014) by simultaneously analyzing net-electric charge and net-proton fluctuations to extract freeze-out conditions. The extracted freeze-out temperatures are lower than those from standard statistical hadronization model fits to particle yields (Cleymans 2005), and lie close to the lower bound of the lattice QCD transition temperature Tc. The results enable future combined analyses of fluctuation observables across different conserved charges, and the discrepancy between lower-order and higher-order cumulant descriptions at larger μB may indicate the onset of critical fluctuations near a possible QCD critical point, to be investigated by the RHIC beam energy scan and the CBM experiment at FAIR.
