# Decay widths at the scission point in nuclear fission

> An outstanding problem in the theory of nuclear fission is understanding the Hamiltonian dynamics at the scission point. Here we apply the Generator Coordinate Method to calculate decay widths for pre-scission configurations into the two-fragment continuum. Transitions that are allowed under diabatic dynamics can have widths up to several MeV. For non-diabatic decays through the pairing interaction, typical widths to a specific final state channel are 2-3 orders of magnitude smaller. The nucleus U-236 is taken as a representative example in the calculations.

# Decay widths at the scission point in nuclear fission

## Key claim

The Generator Coordinate Method can calculate decay widths for pre-scission configurations into the two-fragment continuum, but the widths vary over many orders of magnitude depending on whether the dynamics are diabatic or mediated by the pairing interaction; for the nucleus U-236, diabatic transitions can have widths up to several MeV, while non-diabatic decays through pairing are typically 2-3 orders of magnitude smaller.

## Method

The authors apply the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) with Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov wave functions constrained by the relative distance between nascent fragments and the neck-size operator, using the Gogny D1S energy functional. They construct continuum channel wave functions from chains of GCM configurations with a given K-partition, augmenting the basis with simulated configurations at larger separations to approximate a flat-bottomed potential. Decay widths are extracted from strength functions in the discretized continuum, using both the full width at half maximum of smoothed strength distributions and the Fermi Golden Rule applied after orthogonalization via Lanczos tridiagonalization.

## Key numbers

- Diabatic decay width for the Glider configuration at z_rel = 16.71 fm in U-236: Gamma_FWHM approximately 3 MeV from the smoothed strength function, and Gamma_FGR = 2.2 MeV from the Fermi Golden Rule estimate
- Pairing-assisted decay width from intermediate configuration A to the Glider channel: 5 keV
- Pairing-assisted decay width from intermediate configuration B to the Glider channel: 60 keV
- Overlap across the scission point for Glider configurations: 0.38, compared to typical neighboring overlaps of 0.85-0.95
- Gaussian width parameter s for the relative coordinate wave function: 0.3 fm
- Kinetic energy from GCM diagonalization: T_5 = 7.9 MeV, compared to free-particle kinetic energy T_free = 6.8 MeV at the same momentum k = 4.36 fm^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the GCM-based approach to fission dynamics developed in Bertsch (2019a) and Bertsch (2019b), applying it specifically to calculate decay widths at the scission point for U-236. The results are consistent with time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations by Bulgac (2015), which found a delay time at scission of order 10000 fm/c corresponding to a decay width of approximately 20 keV, falling between the calculated pairing-assisted widths. The method enables future estimates of branching ratios into different exit channels, which could address observables such as odd-even staggering in fragment charge distributions and total kinetic energy distributions, as discussed in Bertsch (2018).
