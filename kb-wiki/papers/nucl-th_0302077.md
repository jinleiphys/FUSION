# Jet Quenching and Radiative Energy Loss in Dense Nuclear Matter

> We review recent finite opacity approaches (GLV, WW, WOGZ) to the computation of the induced gluon radiative energy loss and their application to the tomographic studies of the density evolution in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

This review argues that finite opacity approaches (GLV, WW, WOGZ) to computing induced gluon radiative energy loss are the most suitable theoretical tools for interpreting jet quenching data at RHIC, but the authors explicitly restrict their discussion to the energy range of minijets accessible experimentally (3-15 GeV) and note that fluctuations in energy loss tend to reduce the magnitude of attenuation by about a factor of two at RHIC, which must be included when inverting the attenuation pattern to determine initial plasma density.

## Method

The authors review two complementary formalisms for computing radiative energy loss: the GLV approach based on an algebraic reaction operator formalism that expands in opacity χ = L/λ, and the WW/WOGZ twist expansion approach. They apply these formalisms to modify vacuum fragmentation functions by replacing them with effective quenched fragmentation functions that account for medium-induced gluon radiation, including both average energy loss and multigluon fluctuations via an energy loss distribution P(ε,E). The modified hadron production in A+A collisions is then computed using a pQCD parton model with nuclear shadowing functions (EKS'98) and Glauber binary collision geometry.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC, charged hadrons beyond 4 GeV are suppressed by a factor of 2.5 to 5 (suppression factor 0.2-0.4) relative to naive binary scaling in central Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 AGeV (STAR preliminary data)
- The elastic energy loss of partons in a QCD plasma of temperature T ∼ 300 MeV is too small (dE_el/dx < 500 MeV/fm) for jet extinction
- The HIJING model assumed gluon energy loss dE/dx = 2 GeV/fm due to induced gluon radiative energy loss, predicting up to an order of magnitude suppression of charged hadrons at p_T ∼ 5 GeV
- Back-to-back jet correlations in central Au+Au collisions at 200 AGeV are attenuated by an order of magnitude relative to p+p (STAR data)
- The pQCD model with intrinsic ⟨k_T²⟩ = 1.8 GeV² and GRV 98 PDFs describes p+p data at √s = 540 and 1800 GeV with a ±25% error band

## Context

This review extends the earlier work of Bjorken (1982) on elastic energy loss and the early studies by Gyulassy, Wang, and collaborators (1990-1992) that suggested induced radiative energy loss could be much larger than elastic loss, leading to the HIJING predictions. It contrasts the strong suppression seen at RHIC with the Cronin enhancement observed at SPS energies (17 AGeV) in Pb+Pb collisions by Aggarwal (1998). The review enables jet tomography of the quark-gluon plasma by providing the theoretical framework to invert the attenuation pattern and determine initial plasma density, with the GLV formalism providing results to all orders in opacity (or equivalently all twist parton-parton correlations).
