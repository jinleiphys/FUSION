# Dragging $D$ mesons by hot hadrons

> We evaluate the drag and diffusion coefficients of a hot hadronic medium consisting of pions, nucleons, kaons and eta using open charm mesons as a probe. The interaction of the probe with the hadronic matter has been treated in the framework of effective field theory. It is observed that the magnitude of both the transport coefficients are significant, indicating substantial amount of interaction of the heavy mesons with the thermal hadronic system. The results may have noticeable impact on the experimental observable like the suppression of single electron spectra originating form the decays of heavy mesons in nuclear collisions at relativistic energies.

## Key claim

The drag and diffusion coefficients of D mesons in a hot hadronic medium are significant, indicating substantial interaction, but the results carry an uncertainty of about 11% from the experimental error in the D* decay width used to fix the coupling constant, and the lower bound of this uncertainty yields a drag coefficient that agrees reasonably well at high temperatures with earlier empirical work by He et al.

## Method

The authors computed the drag coefficient γ and diffusion coefficient B0 for D mesons scattering elastically with thermal pions, nucleons, kaons, and eta mesons in a temperature range of 100–180 MeV. The scattering amplitudes were obtained from effective field theory: for D–light meson interactions, a covariant chiral perturbation theory with D* meson exchanges and contact terms was used; for D–nucleon interactions, amplitudes proceeded via Λc and Σc exchanges using Lagrangians from Liu et al. (2009). Form factors with a cutoff Λ = 1 GeV were included at each vertex, and the thermal averages were performed over the phase space of the bath particles.

## Key numbers

- Drag coefficient γ increases with temperature; the relaxation time τ ∼ γ⁻¹ is about 4–5 fm/c.
- At T = 180 MeV, the diffusion coefficient from the transport calculation differs from the Einstein relation B0 = MD γ T by about 10%.
- The spatial diffusion coefficient Dx = T/(MD γ) at T = 180 MeV is ∼ 2/(2π T), i.e., about 2 times the thermal wavelength λ = 1/(2π T).
- The D*Dπ coupling constant g = 1177 ± 137 MeV, obtained from the experimental D* → Dπ decay width of ∼ 65 ± 15 keV.
- Physical masses used: mD = 1867 MeV, mD* = 2008 MeV, mDs = 1969 MeV.
- Coupling constants for D–nucleon interactions: fDNΛc/mD = 7.18 GeV⁻¹ and fDNΣc/mD = 2.01 GeV⁻¹, obtained using SU(4) symmetry.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of heavy quark diffusion in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) by addressing the hadronic phase, which had been largely ignored in prior theoretical attempts to explain the nuclear suppression factor RAA and elliptic flow v2 of single electrons from heavy meson decays. It builds on the evaluation of D meson diffusion using heavy meson chiral perturbation theory by Laine (2011) and using empirical elastic scattering amplitudes by He et al. (2011). The results enable more reliable extraction of QGP transport coefficients by allowing subtraction of hadronic contributions from experimental observables in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies.
