# Comparing the Tsallis distribution with and without thermodynamical description in p+p collisions

> We compare two types of Tsallis distribution, i.e., with and without thermodynamical description, using the experimental data from the STAR, PHENIX, ALICE and CMS Collaborations on the rapidity and energy dependence of the transverse momentum spectra in p+p collisions. Both of them can give us the similar fitting power to the particle spectra. We show that the Tsallis distribution with thermodynamical description gives lower temperatures than the ones without it. The extra factor $m_T$ (transverse mass) in the Tsallis distribution with thermodynamical description plays an important role in the discrepancies between the two types of Tsallis distribution. But for the heavy particles, the choice to use the $m_T$ or $E_T$ (transverse energy) in the Tsallis distribution becomes more crucial.

## Key claim

The Tsallis distribution with thermodynamical description (Type-B) and the one without it (Type-A) both fit experimental transverse momentum spectra equally well, but Type-B systematically yields lower temperatures than Type-A; the extra factor of transverse mass \(m_T\) in Type-B is the primary cause of the discrepancy for light particles, while for heavier particles the choice between \(m_T\) and transverse energy \(E_T\) becomes more important.

## Method

The authors fitted five distribution functions, Type-A (Eq. 5, without thermodynamical description), Type-B (Eq. 8, with thermodynamical description), and three transient forms (Eqs. 9, 10, 11) that bridge the two, to published transverse momentum spectra of pions, kaons, protons, and charged particles from p+p collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=62.4\), 200, 900, 2760, and 7000 GeV, using data from the STAR, PHENIX, ALICE, and CMS Collaborations. The fitting parameters (temperature \(T\), normalization \(A\), and power-law index \(n\) or entropic factor \(q\)) were extracted via \(\chi^2\) minimization, and the resulting temperatures from the five distributions were compared as functions of collision energy and particle species.

## Key numbers

- For \(\pi^+\) at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV (STAR data): Type-B temperature \(T_1 = 0.0895\) GeV, Type-A temperature \(T_5 = 0.128\) GeV; both fits gave \(\chi^2/ndf \approx 5\text{–}6.5\).
- For \(\pi^+\) at \(\sqrt{s}=900\) GeV (ALICE data): \(T_1 = 0.0716\) GeV, \(T_5 = 0.125\) GeV; \(\chi^2/ndf \approx 13\text{–}25\).
- For \(K^++K^-\) at \(\sqrt{s}=2760\) GeV (ALICE data): \(T_1 = 0.0953\) GeV, \(T_5 = 0.190\) GeV; \(\chi^2/ndf \approx 10\text{–}14.5\).
- For \(p+\bar{p}\) at \(\sqrt{s}=7000\) GeV (ALICE data): \(T_1 = 0.112\) GeV, \(T_5 = 0.247\) GeV; \(\chi^2/ndf \approx 12.7\text{–}14.9\).
- For charged particles at \(\sqrt{s}=7000\) GeV (CMS data): \(T_1 = 0.101\) GeV, \(T_5 = 0.147\) GeV; \(\chi^2/ndf \approx 64\text{–}80\).
- For pions, the transient distribution without the extra \(m_T\) term (Eq. 10) gave \(T_3\) values similar to \(T_5\) (e.g., \(\pi^+\) at 200 GeV: \(T_3 = 0.126\) GeV vs. \(T_5 = 0.128\) GeV), indicating cancellation between the effects of the power \(q\) and the \(m_T\) factor.
- For protons at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV (STAR data): \(T_3 = 0.104\) GeV, \(T_5 = 0.180\) GeV, showing that for heavier particles the \(m_T\) vs. \(E_T\) choice dominates.

## Context

This work extends the authors’ previous systematic analysis of Tsallis fits to p+p, p+A, and A+A collisions at RHIC and LHC (Zheng and Zhu, 2015) and directly compares the two most popular Tsallis formulations: Type-A, used by the STAR, PHENIX, ALICE, and CMS Collaborations, and Type-B, used by Cleymans and collaborators (Cleymans, 2013; Azmi and Cleymans, 2014). By introducing three transient distributions, the paper isolates the roles of the extra \(m_T\) factor and the power \(q\) in causing the temperature discrepancy. The results clarify that the choice of distribution form significantly affects the extracted temperature, especially for heavy particles, and provide a basis for interpreting future p+p data at 8 and 13 TeV.
