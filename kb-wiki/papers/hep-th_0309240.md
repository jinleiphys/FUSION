# Recovering Lorentz Invariance of DLCQ

## Key claim

The authors propose that Lorentz invariance of the perturbative S matrix in Discrete Light-Cone Quantization (DLCQ) can be recovered by adding extra operators to the DLCQ action, but only if those operators are constructed from the zero-mode loop contributions that are absent in the naive discretization; the trivial vacuum remains intact under this modification.

## Context

This work extends the original DLCQ formulation of Maskawa and Yamawaki (1976), who first identified the Zero-Mode Constraint and proved that DLCQ yields a trivial vacuum but violates Lorentz invariance at the operator level due to surface terms at x‚ĀĽ = ¬ĪL. The authors had previously shown in Taniguchi et al. (2003) that this violation persists at the S matrix level because zero-mode loops are absent. The proposed modification parallels the lattice field theory approach where the discretized action must be chosen to yield the correct continuum limit. The method enables future non-perturbative DLCQ calculations that preserve both the trivial vacuum and Lorentz invariance, with extension to four-dimensional theories and higher loop orders under investigation.
