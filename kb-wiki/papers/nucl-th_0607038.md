# Hadronic Parity Violation: an Analytic Approach

> Using a recent reformulation of the analysis of nuclear parity-violation (PV) within the framework of effective field theory (EFT), we show how predictions for parity-violating observables in low-energy light hadronic systems can be understood in an analytic fashion. It is hoped that such an analytic approach may encourage additional experimental work as well as add to the understanding of such parity-violation phenomena, which is all too often obscured by its description in terms of numerical results obtained from complex two-body potential codes.

## Key claim

The paper argues that parity-violating observables in low-energy light hadronic systems can be understood using a simple analytic effective-field-theory approach rather than complex two-body potential codes, but this analytic treatment is intended as a didactic and motivational introduction rather than a replacement for precision calculations using state-of-the-art wavefunctions such as the Argonne V18 or Bonn potentials.

## Method

The author reformulates the analysis of nuclear parity violation (PV) within an effective field theory (EFT) framework, where the low-energy PV nucleon-nucleon interaction is entirely short-ranged and described at leading order by five independent amplitudes parameterized by low-energy constants (LECs). The analytic treatment uses a simple effective interaction approach with short-range potentials, justified because the scattering lengths (a_s ~ -20 fm, a_t ~ 5 fm) are much larger than the ~1 fm range of the strong interaction. The method is applied to low-energy (E_cm ≤ 50 MeV) two-nucleon PV observables, focusing on the deuteron as the simplest nuclear system.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-proton spin-singlet scattering length: a_0^s = -23.715 ± 0.015 fm, effective range r_0^s = 2.73 ± 0.03 fm
- Neutron-proton spin-triplet scattering length: a_0^t = 5.423 ± 0.005 fm, effective range r_0^t = 1.73 ± 0.02 fm
- Deuteron binding momentum: κ = 45.7 MeV, corresponding to binding energy E_B = -2.23 MeV
- Deuteron binding momentum from asymptotic form: γ = 45.3 MeV
- Deuteron binding energy: 2.223 MeV
- Deuteron magnetic dipole moment: μ_d = 0.856 μ_N
- Deuteron electric quadrupole moment: Q_d = 0.286 e fm^2
- Deuteron charge radius: sqrt(r_d^2) ~ 2 fm
- The DDH weak coupling values (in units of g_π = 3.8 × 10^{-8}) include: f_π best value +12 (range 0 to 30), h_ρ^0 best value -30 (range 30 to -81), h_ρ^1 best value -0.5 (range -1 to 0), h_ρ^2 best value -25 (range -20 to -29), h_ω^0 best value -5 (range 15 to -27), h_ω^1 best value -3 (range -5 to -2)

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory reformulation of nuclear parity violation developed by Zhu et al. (2005), which provides a model-independent alternative to the traditional Desplanques-Donoghue-Holstein (DDH) meson-exchange framework from 1980. The DDH framework has shown inconsistencies, most notably the vastly different value for h_π extracted from PV gamma-decays of 18F and 19F versus the combination of the p-p asymmetry and the cesium anapole moment. The analytic approach aims to make the subject more accessible and encourage additional experimental work, while the five low-energy constants determined from few-body measurements would provide benchmark numbers for particle theorists and enable reanalysis of PV effects in many-body systems.
