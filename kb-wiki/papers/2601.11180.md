# Fine-tunings in radiative $α$-particle capture on $^{12}$C at astrophysical energies

> We investigate the fine-tuning of radiative alpha-particle capture on carbon, $α(^{12}{\rm C},^{16}{\rm O})γ$, at astrophysical energies. Utilizing results from cluster effective field theory for this reaction, we find that the low-energy data of the astrophysical S-factor allow for only very small variations in the electromagnetic fine-structure constant $α$, namely $|δα/α| \leq 0.2\,$ per mille, in both the $E1$ and the $E2$ radiative capture.

## Key claim

The low-energy astrophysical S-factor data for radiative alpha-particle capture on carbon allow only very small variations in the electromagnetic fine-structure constant alpha, namely |delta alpha / alpha| ≤ 0.2 per mille, for both E1 and E2 radiative capture. This bound is much stronger than those found for other element generation processes in stars or in Big Bang nucleosynthesis.

## Method

The authors used cluster effective field theory (EFT) results for the radiative capture reaction 4He + 12C → 16O + gamma, calculating the cross sections for E1 and E2 transitions from amplitudes that include initial state radiation, counter terms, loop diagrams, and Coulomb interactions. The astrophysical S-factor was computed as a function of center-of-mass energy, and the fine-structure constant alpha was varied by fractional changes delta (alpha = alpha_0 (1 + delta)) to determine how much variation the experimental data could tolerate. The effective range parameters for the alpha-12C scattering channels were taken from prior work by Ando et al. (2022, 2025), and the normalization y^(0) = 0.0798 MeV^(-1/2) was used as a compromise between describing the S_E1 and S_E2 factors.

## Key numbers

- The bound on the fine-structure constant variation is |delta alpha / alpha| ≤ 0.2 per mille (0.0002) for both E1 and E2 radiative capture.
- For E1 capture, a variation of delta = ±0.0002 shifts the 1- resonance peak position by Delta(E_R) = 0.06 MeV, and the peak value changes by almost a factor of 2.
- The nominal fine-structure constant used is alpha_0 = 7.2973525693(11) × 10^(-3) = 1/137.035999084(21) from the Particle Data Group (2024).
- The effective range parameters for E1 capture are r_1 = 0.415314 fm^(-1), P_1 = -0.57427 fm, Q_1 = 0.02032 fm^3; for E2 capture they are r_2 = 0.157453 fm^(-3), P_2 = -1.04781 fm^(-1), Q_2 = 0.1403 fm.
- The renormalized couplings used are h_r^(1) = 272125 MeV^3 for E1 and h_r^(2) = 4.591 × 10^12 MeV^4 for E2, with a cut-off r_c = 0.01 fm.
- For E1 capture, the inverse propagator D_1 vanishes at E = 2.438 MeV, corresponding to the 1- resonance at E_x = 9.585(11) MeV with width Gamma = 0.42(2) MeV.
- For E2 capture, the sum of amplitudes |U^(2) + V^(2) + W^(2)| varies by approximately 55% and |A^(2)(U^(2)+V^(2)+W^(2))| by approximately 60% for the delta range considered.

## Context

This work extends the study of fine-tuning in nuclear astrophysics beyond the well-known Hoyle state in 12C, which was previously investigated using nuclear lattice effective field theory by Epelbaum et al. (2012, 2013) and Lahde et al. (2019). The bound found here (|delta alpha/alpha| ≤ 0.2 per mille) is much stronger than those from Big Bang nucleosynthesis reported by Meissner et al. (2023) or from the Hoyle state itself. The results enable future confirmation using nuclear lattice effective field theory along the lines of the alpha-alpha scattering work by Elhatisari et al. (2021), though such calculations require substantial computational resources.
