# Equation of state of a quark-Polyakov loop-meson mixture in the PNJL model at finite temperature

> Recent consensus on the $N_f=2+1$ equation of state at vanishing chemical potential from different lattice-QCD groups has spoiled the previous agreement with the outcome from the mean-field Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. In this letter we review the thermodynamics of the PNJL model introducing two important aspects needed to describe the pressure computed in the lattice QCD. First, we consider the thermodynamics of the model beyond the mean-field approach to include pseudoscalar and scalar mesonic-like fluctuations into the grand-canonical potential. This accounts for the hadronic pressure of the system below the critical temperature. On the other hand we also implement the back reaction of quarks into the Polyakov-loop effective potential bringing a reduction of the pressure above $T_c$ from the Stefan-Boltzmann limit. We get a good agreement with lattice-QCD data at low and moderate temperatures, opening the door to a straightforward extension to finite chemical potential.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the PNJL model can still reproduce the lattice-QCD equation of state at zero chemical potential once two corrections are added: mesonic fluctuations below the critical temperature and the backreaction of quarks onto the Polyakov-loop effective potential above it, but the agreement is only "quite satisfactory" and the underestimation around T_c could be cured by adding more hadronic states.

## Method

The authors compute the grand-canonical potential of the N_f=2+1 PNJL model at O(1/N_c), combining the mean-field quark contribution (O(N_c)) with mesonic fluctuations (O(N_c^0)) from scalar and pseudoscalar channels (pi, K, anti-K, eta, eta', f_0) via the ring-diagram summation and coupling-constant integration technique. They replace the pure Yang-Mills Polyakov-loop potential with a "glue" potential that incorporates quark backreaction, using a temperature rescaling relation T^* derived from functional renormalization group results (Haas 2013). The total pressure is obtained by evaluating the grand-canonical potential at the self-consistent mean-field solutions for the quark condensates and Polyakov-loop expectation values.

## Key numbers

- The chiral transition temperature (inflection point of the light quark condensate) in the PNJL model is T_c = 209 MeV, compared to the lattice-QCD value of T_c = 157 ± 6 MeV (Borsanyi 2010).
- The glue potential rescaling relation is (T^* - T^{*,cr})/T^{*,cr} = 0.57 × (T - T^{cr})/T^{cr}, with T^{*,cr} = 270 MeV for the Yang-Mills case and T^{cr} = 190 MeV for the glue potential.
- The total pressure is compared to two lattice-QCD data sets: Set 1 from Borsanyi (2013) and Set 2 from Bazavov (2014), covering temperatures up to 400 MeV.
- The mesonic fluctuations include all scalar and pseudoscalar states with vacuum mass below 1 GeV, with a total spin-isospin degeneracy sum of g_M = 18 for all J^π = 0^± states.

## Context

This work addresses the recent consensus between the Bielefeld/HotQCD and Wuppertal-Budapest lattice-QCD groups (Borsanyi 2013, Bazavov 2014) that disfavored the previous mean-field PNJL model results (Ratti 2005, Marty 2013). It extends the earlier PNJL thermodynamics by incorporating mesonic fluctuations following the approach of Quack (1993) and Hufner (1994), and by implementing the quark backreaction on the Polyakov-loop potential using the functional renormalization group results of Haas (2013). The improved model enables a straightforward extension to finite chemical potential, where lattice-QCD methods are computationally expensive.
