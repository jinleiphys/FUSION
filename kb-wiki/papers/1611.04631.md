# Euclidean relativistic quantum mechanics - scattering asymptotic conditions

> We discuss the formulation of the scattering asymptotic condition in a relativistic quantum theory formulated in terms of reflection positive Euclidean Green functions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that scattering asymptotic conditions can be formulated directly in the Euclidean representation of a relativistic quantum theory, without requiring explicit analytic continuation of Euclidean times, provided the Euclidean Green functions satisfy cluster properties and a polynomial approximation to a mass-filtering function can be constructed that preserves the Euclidean time-support condition.

## Method

The authors start from the Osterwalder-Schrader reconstruction theorem, which yields a physical Hilbert space and Poincaré generators from reflection-positive Euclidean Green functions. They formulate the scattering asymptotic condition (the Cook condition) in this Euclidean representation, expressing the norm of the relevant integrand in terms of Euclidean Green functions. To isolate asymptotic particle states, they construct mass eigenstates by applying a polynomial approximation to a function of the Euclidean Laplacian, \(h(\nabla^2)\), to wave functions with positive Euclidean time support, using the Carleman condition to ensure the polynomial approximation converges and preserves the support condition. They then show that the disconnected terms in the Euclidean Green functions cancel when the mass-filtering polynomial is applied, leaving only the connected part which decays at large times.

## Key numbers

- The connected part of the Euclidean 4-point Green function is expected to fall off like \(1/t^3\) for large time, analogous to the Minkowski case.
- The Carleman condition involves the sum \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty \gamma_n^{-1/(2n)} > \infty\), where \(\gamma_n\) are moments of the Lehmann weight; the authors show this sum diverges for moments of the form \(\gamma_n' = \int_0^\infty e^{-\sqrt{m^2 + \mathbf{p}^2} \tau} / (2\sqrt{m^2 + \mathbf{p}^2}) \, m^{2n+k} dm\), establishing that \(\sum_{n=1}^\infty \gamma_n^{\prime -1/(2n)} > \sum_{n=0}^\infty c/(2n+k-2) > \infty\).

## Context

This work extends the Euclidean formulation of quantum field theory pioneered by Schwinger (1950s) and the reconstruction theorem of Osterwalder and Schrader (1973, 1975) to include scattering theory, which had been problematic due to the imaginary-time formulation. It builds on the authors' prior work (Aiello and Polyzou, 2015; Polyzou, 2015) arguing that scattering can be formulated given a Hilbert space representation with cluster properties. The method enables computation of S-matrix elements using Euclidean Green functions, with practical approaches using polynomials in \(e^{-\beta H}\) that have been tested in a solvable model (Kopp, 2011).
