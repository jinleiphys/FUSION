# Medium modifications of the bound nucleon GPDs and the quark contribution to the spin sum rule

> We estimate the nuclear medium modifications of the quark contribution to the bound nucleon spin sum rule, J^{q*}, as well as the separate helicity, ΔΣ*, and the angular momentum, L^{q*}, contributions to J^{q*}. For the calculation of the bound nucleon generalized parton distributions (GPDs), we use as input the bound nucleon elastic form factors predicted in the quark-meson coupling model. Our model for the bound nucleon GPDs is relevant for incoherent deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) with nuclear targets. We find that the m5Aedium modifications increase J^{q*} and L^{q*} and decrease ΔΣ* compared to the free nucleon case. The effect is large and increases with increasing nuclear density ρ. For instance, at ρ=ρ_0=0.15 fm^{-3}, J^{q*} increases by 7%, L^{q*} increases by 20%, and ΔΣ* decreases by 17%. These in-medium modifications of the bound nucleon spin properties may be understood qualitatively in terms of the enhancement of the lower component of the quark Dirac spinor in the nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The nuclear medium modifies the quark contribution to the bound nucleon spin sum rule, increasing the total quark angular momentum contribution and decreasing the quark helicity contribution, with the magnitude of the effect growing with nuclear density; the authors model these modifications by assuming bound nucleon generalized parton distributions (GPDs) scale in proportion to the corresponding bound nucleon elastic form factors from the quark-meson coupling model, while neglecting EMC, Fermi motion, nuclear shadowing, and antishadowing effects.

## Method

The authors constructed a model for bound nucleon GPDs by scaling the free nucleon GPDs \(H^{q/N}\) and \(E^{q/N}\) by the ratios of the bound to free nucleon elastic Dirac and Pauli form factors, respectively, using form factors predicted by the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. They then computed the quark contribution to the bound nucleon spin sum rule \(J^{q*}\) by integrating the forward limit of the GPDs (with \(t,\xi \to 0\)) using NNLO MRST2002 parton distributions at \(Q^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\) and the model of Guidal et al. (2004) for the forward limit of \(E^q\). The quark helicity contribution \(\Delta\Sigma^*\) was estimated by scaling the free nucleon helicity distributions (GRSV2000 at \(Q^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\)) by the ratio of the in-medium to free axial coupling constant \(g_A^*/g_A\) from the QMC model, and the orbital angular momentum contribution \(L^{q*}\) was obtained as \(J^{q*} - \Delta\Sigma^*\).

## Key numbers

- At nuclear density \(\rho = \rho_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\), the quark contribution to the bound nucleon spin sum rule \(J^{q*}\) increases by 7% relative to the free nucleon value.
- At \(\rho = \rho_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\), the quark orbital angular momentum contribution \(L^{q*}\) increases by 20% relative to the free nucleon value.
- At \(\rho = \rho_0 = 0.15\) fm\(^{-3}\), the quark helicity contribution \(\Delta\Sigma^*\) decreases by 17% relative to the free nucleon value.
- For the free nucleon at \(\rho/\rho_0 = 0\), the quark contribution to the spin sum rule is \(2J^q = 0.610\) at \(Q^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\).
- The bound nucleon value at \(\rho = \rho_0\) is \(2J^{q*} = 1.070 \times 2J^q\) (i.e., a ratio of 1.070).
- The proton and neutron anomalous magnetic moments used are \(k^p = 1.793\) and \(k^n = -1.913\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier model for bound nucleon GPDs in \(^4\)He (Guzey et al., 2008) to arbitrary nuclear density and studies the spin sum rule, complementing prior work on medium modifications of parton distributions (EMC effect) and elastic form factors (QMC model by Lu et al., 1997). The results are relevant for interpreting incoherent deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) on nuclear targets, and the predicted suppression of \(\Delta\Sigma^*\) is consistent with the known suppression of the axial coupling constant \(g_A\) in nuclei from Gamow-Teller beta decay measurements (Buck, 1975; Goodman, 1985) and QMC model calculations (QMCgA, QMCneuA).
