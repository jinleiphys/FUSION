# Colour deconfinement in hot and dense matter

> We first introduce the conceptual basis of critical behaviour in strongly interacting matter, with colour deconfinement as QCD analog of the insulator-conductor transition and chiral symmetry restoration as special case of the associated shift in the mass of the constituents. Next we summarize quark-gluon plasma formation in finite temperature lattice QCD. We consider the underlying symmetries and their spontaneous breaking/restoration in the transition, as well as the resulting changes in thermodynamic behaviour. Finally, we turn to the experimental study of strongly interacting matter by high energy nuclear collisions, using charmonium production to probe the confinement status of the produced primordial medium. Recent results from Pb-Pb collisions at CERN may provide first evidence for colour deconfinement.

## Key claim

Recent results from Pb-Pb collisions at CERN may provide first evidence for colour deconfinement, but only if the sudden onset of anomalous J/psi suppression and the transparency of confined matter to J/psi can be confirmed by further experimental and theoretical studies.

## Method

The paper reviews the conceptual basis of critical behaviour in strongly interacting matter, summarises finite temperature lattice QCD results for the deconfinement transition and chiral symmetry restoration, and then analyses experimental data on J/psi production in nuclear collisions as a probe of the confinement status of the produced medium. The author compares J/psi suppression in p-A, O-Cu, O-U, S-U and Pb-Pb collisions at CERN-SPS energies, using the survival probability of pre-resonance charmonium states in normal nuclear matter as a baseline to identify anomalous suppression.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature from lattice QCD: T_c approximately 0.15 GeV when gauged by the rho-mass, or up to 0.20 GeV when using the string tension
- Energy density needed for deconfinement: 1 - 2 GeV/fm^3
- Bag model estimate of T_c: approximately 140 MeV using B^{1/4} approximately 0.2 GeV from charmonium spectroscopy
- J/psi mass: approximately 3.1 GeV; binding energy approximately 0.64 GeV; radius approximately 0.3 fm
- Pre-resonance charmonium dissociation cross section in nuclear matter: 6.3 +/- 1.2 mb from p-A data
- Average energy density in S-U collisions: 1 - 3 GeV/fm^3
- Central Pb-Pb collisions show J/psi suppression more than twice that due to pre-resonance absorption in normal nuclear matter

## Context

This review extends the line of work on QCD phase transitions and quark-gluon plasma formation, building on the lattice QCD formulation introduced by Wilson (1974) and the J/psi suppression proposal by Matsui and Satz (1986). The paper synthesises lattice results from Karsch and Laermann (1994) and Boyd et al. (1995) with experimental data from the NA38 and NA50 collaborations, and discusses the anomalous J/psi suppression observed in Pb-Pb collisions as a potential signal of deconfinement, following the model calculations of Gupta and Satz (1992) and Blaizot and Ollitrault (1996). The results enable further experimental searches for deconfinement in heavy-ion collisions and motivate the development of lattice methods for non-zero baryon chemical potential.
