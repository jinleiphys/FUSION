# Effective Theory for Trapped Few-Fermion Systems

> We apply the general principles of effective field theories to the construction of effective interactions suitable for few- and many-body calculations in a no-core shell model framework. We calculate the spectrum of systems with three and four two-component fermions in a harmonic trap. In the unitary limit, we find that three-particle results are within 10% of known semi-analytical values even in small model spaces. The method is very general, and can be readily extended to other regimes, more particles, different species (e.g., protons and neutrons in nuclear physics), or more-component fermions (as well as bosons). As an illustration, we present calculations of the lowest-energy three-fermion states away from the unitary limit and find a possible inversion of parity in the ground state in the limit of trap size large compared to the scattering length. Furthermore, we investigate the lowest positive-parity states for four fermions, although we are limited by the dimensions we can currently handle in this case.

# Effective Theory for Trapped Few-Fermion Systems

## Key claim

The authors combine effective field theory (EFT) with the no-core shell model (NCSM) to compute spectra of three and four two-component fermions in a harmonic trap, finding that three-particle results in the unitary limit agree within 10% of known semi-analytical values even in small model spaces, but the method's accuracy for four-fermion systems is limited by current computational dimensions.

## Method

The authors construct a leading-order EFT Hamiltonian with a single non-derivative two-body contact interaction between opposite-spin fermions, renormalized by fixing the coupling constant in each truncated harmonic-oscillator model space to reproduce the two-body ground-state energy from the transcendental equation of Busch et al. (1998). They then solve the three- and four-body Schrödinger equation in a NCSM basis truncated by maximum shell number N_max, using both relative-coordinate codes and a Slater determinant code (REDSTICK), and extrapolate energies to the N_max → ∞ limit using a power-law fit.

## Key numbers

* Three-fermion ground state (j^π = 1/2^-) in unitary limit: extrapolated E_∞/ω = 2.76, compared to semi-analytical value 2.77 from Werner and Castin (2006).
* First excited 1/2^- state in unitary limit: extrapolated E_∞/ω = 4.71, compared to semi-analytical value 4.77.
* Lowest 1/2^+ state in unitary limit: extrapolated E_∞/ω = 3.17, compared to semi-analytical value 3.17.
* Lowest 3/2^+ state in unitary limit: extrapolated E_∞/ω = 4.11, compared to semi-analytical value 4.10.
* For b/a_2 = 1 (attractive), three-fermion ground state shifts downward relative to unitary limit; for b/a_2 = -1 (repulsive), it shifts upward.
* Four-fermion ground state (0^+) in unitary limit: estimated E_∞/ω between 3.58 (α=1/2 fit) and 3.85 (α=1 fit).
* Short-range scaling exponent γ for three particles: approximately -0.24; for four particles: between -0.65 and -0.92.

## Context

This work extends the NCSM+EFT approach previously introduced by Stetcu et al. (2007) for nuclear systems to trapped atomic fermions, providing an alternative to the pseudopotential solutions of Busch et al. (1998) for two particles and Werner and Castin (2006) for three particles. The method enables systematic calculations for four or more fermions in a trap, with potential extensions to multi-component fermions, bosons, and nuclear systems where a harmonic-oscillator center-of-mass term is added to the intrinsic Hamiltonian as in Lipkin (1958).
