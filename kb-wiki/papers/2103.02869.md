# Measurement methods of radial flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Radial flow can be directly extracted from the azimuthal distribution of mean transverse rapidity. We apply the event-plane method and the two-particle correlation method to estimate the anisotropic Fourier coefficient of the azimuthal distribution of mean transverse rapidity. Using the event sample generated by a multiphase transport model with string melting, we show that both methods are effective. For the two-particle correlation method to be reliable, the mean number of particles in an azimuthal bin must be above a certain threshold. Using these two methods, anisotropic radial flow can be estimated in a model-independent way in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Anisotropic radial flow can be estimated in a model-independent way using the event-plane method (EPM) and the two-particle correlation method (TPCM), but the TPCM is only reliable when the mean number of particles in an azimuthal bin is above a threshold of ten.

## Method

The authors applied the event-plane method and the two-particle correlation method to estimate the second Fourier coefficient of the azimuthal distribution of mean transverse rapidity (MTR), which defines anisotropic radial flow. They tested both methods using 4 million events of Au + Au collisions at 200 GeV generated by the AMPT model with string melting, comparing the estimated values to true values obtained directly from the known reaction plane angle in the simulation.

## Key numbers

- 4 million events of Au + Au collisions at 200 GeV were generated using the AMPT model with string melting
- The analysis used the kinematic ranges |eta| <= 2.5 and pT in [0.15, 2] GeV/c
- Nine centrality bins were defined by Npart corresponding to nine multiplicity ranges
- For the event-plane method, two subevents for resolution corrections were constructed in longitudinal windows |eta| in [3.3, 4.5]
- The TPCM fails when the mean number of particles in an azimuthal bin (langle N_m rangle) is less than 10
- Nbin = 25 was chosen for the analysis, where the two-particle correlation remained independent of Nbin for Nbin >= 20

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to measure radial flow in a model-independent way, replacing the conventional blast-wave parametrization fitting approach (used by the STAR Collaboration at RHIC and others) which is inherently model dependent. The authors had previously introduced mean-transverse rapidity (MTR) and its azimuthal distribution (Yang et al., 2015; Li et al., 2015) as a direct observable for radial flow. The paper adapts the event-plane method (developed by Poskanzer and Voloshin, 1998) and the two-particle correlation method (used for elliptic flow estimation) to the measurement of anisotropic radial flow, enabling future hydrodynamic calculations of shear viscosity without relying on blast-wave model assumptions.
