# A solvable quantum field theory with asymptotic freedom in 3+1 dimensions

> Recently, Ai, Bender and Sarkar gave a prescription on how to obtain $\mathcal{PT}$-symmetric field theory results from an analytic continuation of Hermitian field theories. I perform this analytic continuation for the massless (critical) O(N) model with quartic interaction in 3+1 dimensions. In the large N limit, this theory is exactly solvable, and has negative $β$-function in the ultraviolet, and a stable bound state in the infrared. The coupling diverges at a scale $Λ_c$, but can be continued into the far infrared. At finite temperature, the theory exhibits two phases separated by a second-order phase transition near $T_c\simeq Λ_c/\sqrt{e}$.

# A solvable quantum field theory with asymptotic freedom in 3+1 dimensions

## Key claim
The PT-symmetric massless O(N) model in 3+1 dimensions, obtained by analytic continuation of the standard Hermitian O(N) model to negative coupling, is non-perturbatively renormalizable and asymptotically free in the large N limit, with the Landau pole of the Hermitian theory reinterpreted as a benign scale separating two distinct phases rather than a fundamental obstruction.

## Method
The author starts from the Euclidean path integral for the massless Hermitian O(N) model with quartic interaction, performs a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce an auxiliary field, and evaluates the partition function to leading order in the large N limit using the method of steepest descent. The PT-symmetric theory is obtained by analytically continuing the coupling constant λ → -g + i0⁺ following the prescription of Ai, Bender and Sarkar (2022), and the resulting theory is renormalized non-perturbatively in dimensional regularization. The pressure, running coupling, and bound state spectrum are computed from the saddle point equations and the auxiliary field propagator.

## Key numbers
- The running coupling in the PT-symmetric theory is g_R(μ̄) = 4π² / ln(μ̄²/Λ_c²), where Λ_c is the scale where the coupling diverges.
- The β-function is β(g_R) = -g_R²/(2π²), which is negative for all real g_R.
- At zero temperature, the two saddle point solutions are m₀ = 0 and m₁ = √e Λ_c ≈ 1.6487 Λ_c.
- The zero-temperature pressure per component is p_PT(T=0) = Λ_c⁴ e² / (128π²).
- The critical temperature for the second-order phase transition is T_c ≈ 0.616 Λ_c, which is consistent with T_c ≃ Λ_c/√e ≈ 0.6065 Λ_c.
- The bound state mass in the low-temperature phase is m_ζ ≈ 1.84 m₁ ≈ 3 Λ_c.

## Context
This work extends the prescription of Ai, Bender and Sarkar (2022) for obtaining PT-symmetric field theory results via analytic continuation of Hermitian theories, applying it to the massless O(N) model in 3+1 dimensions. It contradicts the prevailing interpretation that the Hermitian O(N) model cannot be continued beyond its Landau pole (Aizenman 2019), and instead shows that the PT-symmetric continuation yields physically sensible results including asymptotic freedom and a second-order phase transition. The large N solvability technique follows the standard approach reviewed by Moshe and Zinn-Justin (2003), while the finite-temperature methods use thermal quantum field theory techniques from Laine and Vuorinen (2016). The results enable future studies of PT-symmetric field theories beyond perturbation theory, including investigations of bound states and phase structure.
