# Magnetic g Factors With a Surface Delta Interaction

> We examine the consequences of using a surface delta interaction to obtain the nuclear magnetic dipole g factors for 86Kr in a 2 proton hole model space. We find that the g factors of lowest the 2+ and 4+ states are equal to one, or more properly gl. The inclusion of a spin-orbit interaction is also discussed. Comparisons are made with large space calculations.

## Key claim

Using an attractive surface delta interaction (SDI) with degenerate single-particle energies for two proton holes in the \(f_{5/2}\) and \(p_{3/2}\) shells, the magnetic \(g\)-factors of the lowest \(2^{+}\) and \(4^{+}\) states in \({}^{86}\)Kr are both exactly equal to 1 (the orbital \(g\)-factor \(g_l\)), with the spin contribution cancelling out completely; this result holds only in the restricted model space and is modified when realistic single-particle splittings are introduced.

## Method

The authors diagonalized the surface delta interaction (SDI) of Green and Moszkowski in a model space of two proton holes occupying the \(f_{5/2}\) and \(p_{3/2}\) orbitals, taking the single-particle energies as degenerate. They computed the wavefunction coefficients for the \(J=4^{+}\) and \(J=2^{+}\) states and evaluated the magnetic dipole \(g\)-factors using bare proton \(g\)-factors (\(g_l=1\), \(g_s=5.586\)). They then compared these results with large-space shell model calculations using the CDBonn, JUN45, and jj4b effective interactions in a space including the \(f_{5/2}\), \(p_{3/2}\), \(p_{1/2}\), and \(g_{9/2}\) orbitals, with a quenched \(g_s\) factor of 0.75.

## Key numbers

- For the SDI with degenerate single-particle energies: \(g(2^{+}) = g(4^{+}) = 1\) (exactly equal to \(g_l\)).
- Wavefunction coefficients from SDI diagonalization: \(J=4^{+}\) state: \(a = -0.447189\), \(b = 0.894444\); \(J=2^{+}\) state: \(c = 0.692819\), \(d = -0.48992\), \(e = 0.5291337\).
- With a single-particle splitting of 0.4 MeV (from \({}^{87}\)Rb spectrum): \(g(2^{+}) = 1.927\) and \(g(4^{+}) = 1.091\); excitation energies \(E(2^{+}) = 1.684\) MeV and \(E(4^{+}) = 1.824\) MeV.
- Experimental \(g\)-factors from Mertzimekis et al.: \(g(2^{+}) = 1.12(14)\); from Kumbartzki et al.: \(g(2^{+}) = 1.10(5)\) and \(g(4^{+}) = 1.07(15)\).
- Large-space shell model results (CDBonn interaction): \(g(2^{+}) = 1.03\) (orbital 0.99, spin 0.04), \(g(4^{+}) = 0.99\) (orbital 1.00, spin -0.01); energies: \(E(2^{+}) = 1.613\) MeV, \(E(4^{+}) = 2.265\) MeV.
- Large-space shell model results (JUN45 interaction): \(g(2^{+}) = 1.22\) (orbital 0.93, spin 0.29), \(g(4^{+}) = 1.03\) (orbital 0.99, spin 0.04).
- Large-space shell model results (jj4b interaction): \(g(2^{+}) = 1.09\) (orbital 0.97, spin 0.12), \(g(4^{+}) = 1.03\) (orbital 0.99, spin 0.04).

## Context

This work extends the schematic surface delta interaction approach of Green and Moszkowski (1960s) and Arvieu and Moszkowski, as formalized in Talmi's book, to the magnetic properties of \({}^{86}\)Kr. It contrasts with the large-space shell model calculations of Kumbartzki et al. using the CDBonn interaction (Sieja et al.) and also examines the JUN45 (Honma et al.) and jj4b interactions. The finding that the \(g\)-factors are dominated by the orbital contribution, even without pure LS coupling, connects to the pseudo-spin symmetry concepts of Hecht et al. and Arima et al., and the quasi-spin formulation of Kerman and Talmi's generalized seniority scheme. The results enable further study of orbital dominance in nuclear magnetic moments and suggest that the simple SDI model provides a useful starting point for understanding the near-unity \(g\)-factors observed experimentally.
