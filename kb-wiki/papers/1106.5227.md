# Turbulent fluctuations around Bjorken flow

> We study the evolution of local event-by-event deviations from smooth average fluid dynamic fields, as they can arise in heavy ion collisions from the propagation of fluctuating initial conditions. Local fluctuations around Bjorken flow are found to be governed by non-linear equations whose solutions can be characterized qualitatively in terms of Reynolds numbers. Perturbations at different rapidities decouple quickly, and satisfy (after suitable coordinate transformations) an effectively two-dimensional Navier-Stokes equation of non-relativistic form. We discuss the conditions under which non-linearities in these equations cannot be neglected and turbulent behavior is expected to set in.

## Key claim

Local event-by-event fluctuations around Bjorken flow in heavy ion collisions are governed by non-linear equations that can become effectively two-dimensional and non-relativistic at late times, but the Reynolds numbers typically realized in heavy ion collisions (Re ≈ s/η = O(10)) are too small for fully developed turbulence, though they may be sufficient for the onset of turbulent phenomena.

## Method

The authors derived evolution equations for velocity fluctuations δu^j and energy density fluctuations δϵ from relativistic viscous fluid dynamics, expanding around the Bjorken scaling solution. They decomposed the velocity field into irrotational (divergence ϑ) and solenoidal (vorticity ω) parts, and performed a coordinate transformation to a rescaled time t = 3τ^(4/3)/(4τ_0^(1/3)), temperature field d, and velocity v_j that yields an effectively two-dimensional Navier-Stokes equation of non-relativistic form with a solenoidal constraint.

## Key numbers

- The energy density of the Bjorken background scales as ε̄ = ε_Bj(τ_0) (τ_0/τ)^(4/3)
- Transverse vorticity components ω_1 and ω_2 fall off like 1/τ^(2/3) for small wave-vectors or small kinematic viscosity ν_0
- Vorticity mode ω_3 grows algebraically (h_3 = 1) for small wave-vectors or small kinematic viscosity ν_0
- Reynolds numbers in heavy ion collisions are estimated to be Re ≈ s/η = O(10)
- The kinematic viscosity ν_0 = η/(ε+p) = η/(sT) is evaluated at time τ_0

## Context

This work extends the study of event-by-event fluctuations in heavy ion collisions, motivated by measurements of higher order flow coefficients v_3, v_4, v_5, and v_6 at RHIC and LHC that complement elliptic flow v_2 measurements (Alver 2010, Holopainen 2010). The authors derive non-linear evolution equations for fluctuations around Bjorken flow, showing they reduce to an effectively two-dimensional Navier-Stokes equation, which enables discussion of turbulent phenomena and potential inverse cascades (Kraichnan, Batchelor) in heavy ion collisions, in contrast to the linearized treatment of primordial fluctuations in cosmology.
