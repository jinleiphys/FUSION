# Classical interactions of the instanton-dyons with antidyons

> Instanton-dyons, also known as instanton-monopoles or instanton-quarks, are topological constituents of the instantons at nonzero temperature and nonzero expectation value of $A_4$. While the interaction between instanton-dyons has been calculated to one-loop order by a number of authors, that for dyon-antidyon pairs remains unknown even at the classical level. In this work we are filling this gap, by solving the gradient flow equation on a 3d lattice. We start with two well separated objects. We find that, after initial rapid relaxation, the configurations follow "streamline" set of configurations, which is basically independent on the initial configurations used. In striking difference to instanton-antiinstanton streamlines, in this case it ends at a quasi-stationary configuration, with an abrupt drop to perturbative fields. We parameterize the action of the streamline configurations, which is to be used in future many-body calculations.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first classical-level calculation of the interaction between instanton-dyon and antidyon pairs (specifically \(M\bar{M}\) and \(L\bar{L}\) pairs) by solving the gradient flow equation on a 3d lattice, finding that the streamline ends at a quasi-stationary metastable configuration rather than smoothly annihilating, in contrast to the instanton-antiinstanton case.

## Method

The authors construct a "sum ansatz" for a dyon-antidyon pair by combing the gauge fields, performing a time-dependent gauge transformation to remove the asymptotic \(A_4\) expectation value, superimposing the two dyons, and then reintroducing the expectation value. They place this configuration on a 3d lattice with link variables and evolve it via the gradient flow equation \(\frac{dA_\mu^a}{d\tau} = -\frac{\delta S}{\delta A_\mu^a}\), using a custom update rule based on the current derived from plaquettes. The flow is run until the configuration collapses to perturbative fields, and the action and dyon positions are tracked throughout.

## Key numbers

- The action of a single dyon on the \(64^3\) lattice is \(5\%\) lower than the analytic value of \(4\pi v\), attributed to fields outside the box.
- The action of two well-separated dyons is 23.88 (in units where \(v=1\)) for the \(64^3\) lattice.
- For initial separations smaller than \(4.2/v\), the dyons move away from each other to the same metastable configuration.
- The lattice size is \(40/v\) in each dimension with spacing \(a = 0.625/v\), using a \(64^3\) cubic lattice for most calculations.

## Context

This work fills a gap left by prior calculations of dyon-dyon interactions at one-loop order (Diakonov et al., 2004; Diakonov, 2009), which did not address dyon-antidyon pairs even at the classical level. It extends the streamline method previously applied to instanton-antiinstanton pairs (Shuryak, 1987; Balitsky and Yung, 1986; Verbaarschot, 1991) to the dyon-antidyon case, which lacks the conformal symmetry that simplified those earlier studies. The parameterized action will be used in future many-body simulations of dyon ensembles, which have been studied by Shuryak (2012) and Faccioli (2013) for understanding confinement and chiral symmetry breaking.
