# From Chiral Lagrangians to Landau Fermi Liquid Theory of Nuclear Matter

> A simple relation between the effective parameters of chiral Lagrangians in medium as predicted by BR scaling and Landau Fermi liquid parameters is derived. This provides a link between an effective theory of QCD at mean-field level and many-body theory of nuclear matter. It connects in particular the scaling vector-meson mass probed by dileptons produced in heavy-ion collisions (e.g., CERES of CERN-SPS) to the scaling nucleon-mass relevant for low-energy spectroscopic properties, e.g., the nuclear gyromagnetic ratios $δg_l$ and the effective axial-vector constant $g_A^\star$.

# From Chiral Lagrangians to Landau Fermi Liquid Theory of Nuclear Matter

## Key claim

The paper derives a direct relation between the BR scaling parameters of chiral Lagrangians in medium and the Landau Fermi liquid parameters of nuclear matter, showing that the universal scaling factor Phi(rho) that governs vector-meson mass dropping can be connected to the Landau effective mass and to low-energy nuclear observables such as the gyromagnetic ratio delta_g_l and the effective axial-vector constant g_A^star, provided one distinguishes between the Walecka-type scalar effective mass and the Landau effective mass.

## Method

The authors start from an effective chiral Lagrangian that incorporates the QCD trace anomaly via a chiral-singlet scalar field chi, and derive BR scaling relations for meson and baryon masses at mean-field level. They then map this Lagrangian onto a baryon chiral Lagrangian with four-Fermi contact interactions, identify the coupling strengths with meson masses, and use the Landau-Migdal Fermi liquid theory to relate the quasiparticle interaction parameters F_1 and F_1' to the scaling factor Phi(rho). The nucleon gyromagnetic ratio and the in-medium axial-vector coupling constant are computed by summing contributions from single-particle currents, omega-meson exchange, rho-meson exchange, and pion-exchange (Miyazawa) terms, reproducing Migdal's formula.

## Key numbers

- The universal scaling factor at nuclear matter density extracted from QCD sum rules: Phi(rho_0) = 0.78, from m_rho^star/m_rho = 0.78 +/- 0.08 (Jin et al.)
- QCD sum-rule result for the nucleon mass scaling: m_N^star/m_N = 0.67 +/- 0.05 at rho = rho_0 (Jin et al.)
- The pion Fock contribution to the Landau parameter: (1/3) tilde_F_1^pi = -0.153 at nuclear matter density (p_F approximately 2 m_pi)
- Predicted Landau effective mass: m_N^star/m_N = 0.69(7) at rho = rho_0
- Predicted in-medium axial-vector coupling constant: g_A^star = 1.0(0) at rho = rho_0
- Predicted isovector gyromagnetic ratio correction: delta_g_l = 0.22(7) tau_3 at rho = rho_0
- Experimental delta_g_l for protons from the dipole sum rule in Bi-209: delta_g_l^proton = 0.23 +/- 0.03 (extracted via Fujita-Hirata relation)
- Experimental delta_g_l from Yamazaki's analysis in the Pb-208 region: delta_g_l^proton approximately 0.33, delta_g_l^neutron approximately -0.22
- Experimental delta_g_l from Arima et al.: delta_g_l approximately 0.25 tau_3

## Context

This work extends the BR scaling proposal of Brown and Rho (1991) by providing a formal connection to Landau-Migdal Fermi liquid theory, which had previously been applied to nuclear matter phenomenology. It builds on the demonstration by Li, Ko, and Brown (1995) that dilepton production data from CERES and HELIOS-3 at CERN-SPS could be explained by dropping vector-meson masses, and on the Walecka model of nuclear matter where the scalar nucleon mass drops with density. The paper enables a unified description linking high-energy heavy-ion observables (dilepton spectra) to low-energy nuclear spectroscopy (gyromagnetic ratios, axial-vector coupling), and suggests that the in-medium axial coupling constant cannot be described by low-order chiral perturbation theory, supporting earlier arguments by Rho (1993).
