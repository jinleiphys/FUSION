# Structure of protoneutron stars within a static approach

> To investigate the stability of the protoneutron stars in their early evolution, the minimum gravitational mass plays a fundamental role. This quantity depends upon the temperature profile assumed. We study within a static approach the stability of a protoneutron star. In particular we focus on a suitable temperature profile suggested by dynamical calculations. We consider a protoneutron star as composed of an isothermal core and an isentropic outer part. To describe physical properties of the interior we employ a microscopically derived equation of state for nuclear matter. For the outer part we employ the Lattimer-Swesty equation of state. The global structure is studied. The assumed temperature profile turns out to give a range of stability which supports temperature values in line with those coming from dynamical calculations. The maximum mass instead depends only upon the equation of state employed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a hybrid temperature profile (isothermal core plus isentropic envelope) yields stable protoneutron star configurations with minimum masses that are consistent with dynamical calculations, but only if the core temperature does not exceed about 40 MeV in the neutrino-trapped case.

## Method

The author constructs static protoneutron star models by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations, using a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock equation of state for the isothermal core and the Lattimer-Swesty equation of state (with incompressibility K=220 MeV) for the isentropic envelope. The matching between core and envelope is performed by fine-tuning the entropy per baryon in the envelope so that all thermodynamic quantities (energy density, free energy density, and temperature) are continuous at the matching density near 0.01 fm⁻³.

## Key numbers

- For a neutrino-free core at T_core = 30 MeV with s_env = 6.13, the minimum gravitational mass is 1.320 M_sun and the maximum is 1.863 M_sun.
- For a neutrino-trapped core (Y_e = 0.4) at T_core = 30 MeV with s_env = 6.23, the minimum gravitational mass is 1.532 M_sun and the maximum is 1.805 M_sun.
- For a neutrino-trapped core at T_core = 40 MeV with s_env = 7.98, no stable configuration exists (minimum and maximum masses are not reported).
- The maximum mass for all considered configurations lies in the narrow range 1.77 to 1.87 M_sun.
- The baryonic mass window for a PNS with T_core = 30 MeV and trapped neutrinos ranges from 1.58 M_sun to 1.98 M_sun.
- The difference in gravitational mass between hot PNS and cold NS is on average about 0.1 M_sun.

## Context

This work extends the static approach of Nicotra (2006) by refining the temperature profile used in earlier BHF-based protoneutron star models (Nicotra, 2006). It addresses the problem that purely isentropic or isothermal profiles produce unrealistic minimum masses, as noted in prior work by Bombaci (1996) and Hean (2005). The results enable future studies of protoneutron stars with hyperonic degrees of freedom, once finite-temperature BHF equations of state with hyperons become available.
