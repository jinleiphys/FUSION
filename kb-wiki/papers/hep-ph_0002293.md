# SNO: Predictions for Ten Measurable Quantities

> We calculate the range of predicted values for 10 quantities that will be measured by the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory (SNO). We use neutrino oscillation solutions (vacuum and MSW; active and sterile neutrinos) that are globally consistent with all available neutrino data and estimate realistic theoretical and experimental uncertainties. The neutral current to charged current double ratio is predicted to be more than 9 sigma from the no-oscillation solution for all of the currently favored neutrino oscillation solutions. The best-fit oscillation solutions predict a CC day-night rate difference between -0.1% and +12.5% and a NC day-night difference < 0.01%. We present also the predicted range for the first and the second moments of the charged current electron recoil energy spectrum, the charged current, the neutral current, and the neutrino-electron scattering rates, the seasonal dependence of the charged current rate, and the double ratio of neutrino-electron scattering rate to charged current rate.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that for all six currently favored neutrino oscillation solutions (vacuum and MSW; active and sterile neutrinos) that are globally consistent at 99% C.L. with existing solar neutrino data, the neutral current to charged current double ratio will be more than 9 sigma from the no-oscillation solution, though the exact predicted values depend on which oscillation scenario is correct.

## Method

The authors calculated the range of predicted values for ten measurable quantities at the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory (SNO) using a representative sample of neutrino parameters from each of the six globally allowed two-flavor oscillation solutions (LMA, SMA, LOW, VAC_S, VAC_L, and Sterile). For each quantity, they expressed predictions as a reduced ratio of the observed value to the standard model value, making the results independent of the absolute solar neutrino flux. They estimated theoretical uncertainties from the B8 neutrino spectrum shape, neutrino interaction cross sections on deuterium, and the hep neutrino flux, and combined these with detector-related uncertainties including energy resolution, energy scale, and statistical errors.

## Key numbers

- The neutral current to charged current double ratio is predicted to be more than 9 sigma from the no-oscillation solution for all currently favored oscillation solutions
- Best-fit oscillation solutions predict a CC day-night rate difference between -0.1% and +12.5%
- Best-fit oscillation solutions predict a NC day-night difference less than 0.01%
- Without oscillations, expected event rates per year in SNO above a 5 MeV threshold: approximately 4.4 x 10^3 CC events, 1.1 x 10^3 NC events, and 415 ES events (assuming 50% NC detection efficiency and a B8 neutrino flux of 0.475 times the standard model flux)
- For an 8 MeV threshold, the estimated CC rate is about 45% of the rate for a 5 MeV threshold and the ES rate is about 28% of the 5 MeV threshold rate
- The best-fit oscillation parameters for the six scenarios: LMA (Delta m^2 = 2.7 x 10^-5 eV^2, sin^2(2theta) = 7.9 x 10^-1); SMA (5.0 x 10^-6 eV^2, 7.2 x 10^-3); LOW (1.0 x 10^-7 eV^2, 9.1 x 10^-1); VAC_S (6.5 x 10^-11 eV^2, 7.2 x 10^-1); VAC_L (4.4 x 10^-10 eV^2, 9.0 x 10^-1); Sterile (4.0 x 10^-6 eV^2, 6.6 x 10^-3)
- Total fractional uncertainties for key quantities: first moment of CC spectrum 1.3%, second moment 2.4%, reduced CC rate 6.7%, reduced NC rate 7.4%, reduced ES rate 5.2%, NC/CC double ratio 3.6%, ES/CC double ratio 7.6%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous series of papers on SNO's potential for determining neutrino oscillation properties (Bahcall, Krastev, and Smirnov, 1998-2000) by providing more specific 99% C.L. predictions for a larger number of measurable quantities than earlier analyses. The paper builds on the foundational work on vacuum oscillations by Gribov and Pontecorvo and on matter oscillations (MSW effect) by Mikheyev, Smirnov, and Wolfenstein, and it considers both active and sterile neutrino scenarios. The predictions enable SNO to discriminate between different oscillation solutions through simultaneous analysis of multiple observables, particularly the powerful diagnostic of the NC/CC double ratio.
