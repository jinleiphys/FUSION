# Possibility of shears mechanism due to attractive interaction in the island of $A$ $\sim$ 140 region

> The present work identify an island in the values of dimensionless parameter $χ$ as +0.10 $<$ $χ$ $<$ +0.55 outside which no magnetic rotational (MR) bands due to the repulsive interaction would be observed. The semi-classical calculations within the hybrid shears mechanism with the principal axis cranking model (HSPAC) reveal the possibility of the shears band due to the attractive interaction for nuclei in A $\sim$ 140 region. Though the limited information exists in literature, the HSPAC calculation exhibits the dipole bands above the 4389-keV and 4160-keV excited states in $^{145}$Sm and $^{146}$Eu nuclei, respectively, demand their candidature of the attractive shears bands. Other interesting consequence of the HSPAC calculations is the increasing nature of the $B(M1)$ and $B(E2)$ transition strengths with spin unlike the decreasing nature of the transition strengths ($B(M1)$ and $B(E2)$ values) of the MR bands. Thus measurement of the transition probability has been required on urgent basis to validate the strong candidature of such an extreme excitation mechanism in $^{145}$Sm and $^{146}$Eu nuclei.

# Possibility of shears mechanism due to attractive interaction in the island of A ∼ 140 region

## Key claim

The paper identifies an island in the dimensionless parameter χ as +0.10 < χ < +0.55 outside which no magnetic rotational (MR) bands due to repulsive interaction would be observed, and further claims that semi-classical HSPAC model calculations reveal the possibility of shears bands due to attractive interaction (negative χ) for nuclei in the A ∼ 140 region, specifically for dipole bands above the 4389-keV and 4160-keV excited states in 145Sm and 146Eu nuclei, respectively.

## Method

The author developed a hybrid shears mechanism with principal axis cranking (HSPAC) model that introduces a single dimensionless parameter χ representing the ratio of core rotational contribution to shears mechanism contribution, replacing the two independent parameters (core moment of inertia and particle-hole interaction potential) used in the standard SPAC model. The model was applied to existing experimental data for MR bands in 139,141-143Sm and 141,143Eu nuclei, and then extended to predict attractive shears bands in 145Sm and 146Eu by treating χ as negative. The calculations minimized the total energy with respect to the shears angle θ1 (or θ1 and θ2 for the symmetry-axis alignment case) to obtain rotational frequency, B(M1), and B(E2) transition strengths as functions of spin.

## Key numbers

- The island for MR bands due to repulsive interaction is identified as +0.10 < χ < +0.55; outside this range no MR bands due to repulsive interaction would be observed.
- For Sm isotopes, χ values obtained from HSPAC fits are: +0.21 for N=81 (143Sm), +0.34 for N=80 (142Sm), +0.31 for N=79 (141Sm), and +0.54 for N=77 (139Sm).
- For Eu isotopes, χ = +0.41 for 141Eu (N=78) and χ = +0.13 for 143Eu (N=80).
- For the candidate attractive shears band in 145Sm, HSPAC calculations used χ = -0.18 with j1 = 6.5ħ and a = 2.62 for the rotation-axis anti-parallel case, and χ = -0.10 with j1 = 17ħ and a = 0.38 for the symmetry-axis anti-parallel case.
- For the candidate attractive shears band in 146Eu, HSPAC calculations used χ = -0.19 with j1 = 6.5ħ and a = 3.15 for the rotation-axis anti-parallel case, and χ = -0.10 with j1 = 19.5ħ and a = 0.33 for the symmetry-axis anti-parallel case.
- The dipole band in 145Sm is built on the 4389-keV excited state with M1 γ-ray energies of 715, 477, 397, and 313 keV.
- The dipole band in 146Eu is built on the 4160-keV excited state with γ-ray energies of 896, 772, 520, and 482 keV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier concept of a dimensionless parameter χ introduced by Clark and Macchiavelli for symmetric shears, and applies it within the SPAC framework (previously used by Pasternak, Podsvirova, and Rajbanshi in multiple studies of MR bands in the A ∼ 140 region) to create the HSPAC model. The paper identifies candidate attractive shears bands in 145Sm (data from Odahara, 2005) and 146Eu (data from de Guzman, 2007), which would represent a new excitation mechanism distinct from conventional MR bands (repulsive interaction) and antimagnetic rotational bands. The key prediction is that B(M1) and B(E2) transition strengths increase with spin for attractive shears, opposite to the decreasing trend in MR bands, and the author calls urgently for experimental measurement of these transition probabilities to validate the claim.
