# Casimir Interaction among Objects Immersed in a Fermionic Environment

> Using ensembles of two, three and four spheres immersed in a fermionic background we evaluate the (integrated) density of states and the Casimir energy. We thus infer that for sufficiently smooth objects, whose various geometric characteristic lengths are larger then the Fermi wave length one can use the simplest semiclassical approximation (the contribution due shortest periodic orbits only) to evaluate the Casimir energy. We also show that the Casimir energy for several objects can be represented fairly accurately as a sum of pairwise Casimir interactions between pairs of objects.

# Casimir Interaction among Objects Immersed in a Fermionic Environment

## Key claim

The Casimir energy for multiple objects immersed in a fermionic environment can be represented fairly accurately as a sum of pairwise Casimir interactions, even though the Casimir energy is known not to be pairwise additive in general; this approximation holds when the objects are sufficiently smooth with geometric characteristic lengths larger than the Fermi wavelength, and the simplest semiclassical approximation using only the shortest periodic orbits is adequate.

## Method

The authors used the Krein formula to relate the change in the density of states to the determinant of the N-body scattering matrix, expressed via the Koringa-Kohn-Rostoker (KKR) multiple scattering matrix. They computed numerically the integrated density of states and Casimir energy for ensembles of two, three, and four hard spheres immersed in a fermionic background, and compared these exact results to semiclassical approximations using the Gutzwiller trace formula with contributions from primitive periodic orbits only.

## Key numbers

- For two large spheres with $k_Fa > 1$, the Casimir energy is approximately $E_C \approx -\nu \mu \frac{a^2}{2\pi r(r-2a)} j_1[2k_F(r-2a)]$, where $\nu$ is the spin degeneracy factor, $\mu$ is the chemical potential, $a$ is the sphere radius, and $r$ is the center-to-center distance.
- For a large sphere at distance $r$ from an infinite plane, $E_C \approx -\nu \mu \frac{a}{2\pi (r-a)} j_1[2k_F(r-a)]$.
- At smallest separations where $r-2a \ll a$, the semiclassical estimate is only about 30% lower than the exact result.
- For three identical spheres at the vertices of an equilateral triangle, the Casimir energy satisfies $E_3 \approx 3E_2$ with high accuracy when $k_Fa \gg 1$; for $k_Fa \le 1$ corrections can reach 10%.
- For four identical spheres at the vertices of a tetrahedron, $E_4 \approx 6E_2 \approx 2E_3$; for $k_Fa \le 1$ corrections can reach 25%.
- The 3-bounce orbit gives its largest contribution, approximately 10% correction, at $r \approx 2.5a$.

## Context

This work extends the study of Casimir interactions from the traditional electromagnetic case (Casimir 1948) to fermionic environments relevant to neutron star physics (Bulgac and Magierski 2001) and quark-gluon plasma (Neergaard and Madsen 2000). The authors show that contrary to the known non-additivity of Casimir interactions for extended objects (Mostepanenko and Trunov 1997; Kardar and Golestanian 1999), pairwise additivity holds well for fermionic Casimir energies in three dimensions. This enables simplified calculations for complex geometries such as nuclei embedded in a neutron gas or buckyballs in an electron gas, and suggests that genuine many-body Casimir interactions are relatively short-ranged.
