# Universality and Leading Corrections in Few-Body Systems

> A large two-body scattering length leads to universal behavior in few-body systems. In particular, the three-body system displays interesting features such as exact discrete scale invariance in the bound state spectrum in the limit of infinite scattering length. Here, I will discuss how an effective field theory (EFT) can be used to study these features and how the finite range of the underlying interaction impacts the bound state spectrum at first order in the EFT expansion.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the discrete scaling symmetry of the three-body Efimov spectrum in the unitary limit (infinite two-body scattering length) is so constraining that the entire spectrum remains unchanged at next-to-leading order in the effective field theory expansion, despite the inclusion of finite-range corrections.

## Method

The author uses a short-range effective field theory (EFT) to describe few-body systems with a large two-body scattering length. At next-to-leading order, a perturbing hyperspherical potential derived from a Bethe-Peierls boundary condition is used to calculate the shift in binding energies of Efimov trimers in the unitary limit, with a three-body counterterm included for renormalization.

## Key numbers

- The discrete scaling factor for the three-body bound state spectrum is \(e^{-2\pi/s_0}\) with \(s_0 \approx 1.00624\).
- In nuclear physics, the ratio of effective range over scattering length is roughly \(\sim 1/3\).
- The shift in binding energy at next-to-leading order, \(\Delta B^{(1)}_n\), is zero for all states \(n\) in the unitary limit.

## Context

This work extends the universal few-body physics pioneered by Efimov (1971, 1979), who showed that a large two-body scattering length leads to a geometric spectrum of three-body bound states. The short-range EFT framework developed by Braaten (2004) and others reproduces these Efimov results at leading order. The paper reports the surprising analytical result, also found numerically by Thøgersen et al. (2008), that the unitary-limit Efimov spectrum is unperturbed by first-order range corrections, which constrains future calculations of electroweak reactions in nuclear astrophysics and applications to halo nuclei and alpha-clusters.
