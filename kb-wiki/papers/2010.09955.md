# Charge-dependent transverse momentum and its impact on the search for the chiral magnetic wave

> The chiral magnetic wave (CMW) is sought using the charge asymmetry ($A_{\rm ch}$) dependence of anisotropic flow in heavy-ion collisions. The charge dependent transverse momentum ($p_{\rm T}$), however, could play a role as a background. With the string fragmentation models, including PYTHIA, we demonstrate the origin of the $A_{\rm ch}-p_{\rm T}$ correlation and its connection with the local charge conservation (LCC). The impact of $A_{\rm ch}-p_{\rm T}$ and its behavior in varied kinematic windows are also discussed. This study provides more insights for the search for the CMW and comprehending the collective motion of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The charge asymmetry dependence of transverse momentum, observed in both Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions, originates from local charge conservation in string fragmentation and resonance decay, and this correlation serves as a universal background that cannot be fully eliminated in the search for the chiral magnetic wave.

## Method

The authors used the Lund string fragmentation models PYTHIA, DPMJET, and HIJING to simulate Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}\) = 5.02 TeV, and compared results with the AMPT model. They dissected PYTHIA events into six production cases (three from string fragmentation and three from resonance decay) to trace the event-wise \(A_{\rm ch}-\Delta\langle p_{\rm T}\rangle\) correlation down to the local level, analyzing how unpaired particles at detector edges carry lower transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- Normalized slope of \(\Delta\langle p_{\rm T}\rangle\) versus \(A_{\rm ch}\) is approximately 0.1 in PYTHIA, DPMJET, and HIJING, consistent with CMS data in Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}\) = 5.02 TeV.
- For \(\rho^0\rightarrow\pi^+\pi^-\) decays, paired pions (case a) have mean \(p_{\rm T}\) = 0.64 GeV/\(c\) while unpaired pions (cases b, c) have mean \(p_{\rm T}\) = 0.59 GeV/\(c\).
- For string fragmentation, paired primordial hadrons (case A) have mean \(p_{\rm T}\) = 0.74 GeV/\(c\) while unpaired primordial hadrons (cases B, C) have mean \(p_{\rm T}\) = 0.68 GeV/\(c\).
- The normalized slope of \(\Delta\langle v_2\rangle\) versus \(A_{\rm ch}\) is approximately 0.2–0.3 in CMS data, larger than the \(\Delta\langle p_{\rm T}\rangle\) slope of 0.1.
- When narrowing the \(p_{\rm T}\) range for \(A_{\rm ch}\) to 1 GeV/\(c\) while keeping \(\langle p_{\rm T}\rangle\) at \(p_{\rm T} > 0.2\) GeV/\(c\) and \(|\eta| < 0.8\), the slope of \(A_{\rm ch}-\Delta\langle p_{\rm T}\rangle\) increases by a factor of 6.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of local charge conservation (LCC) as a background to the chiral magnetic wave (CMW) search, building on earlier hydrodynamic studies by Bzdak (2013) and blast-wave model demonstrations by Voloshin (2014). The authors show that the \(A_{\rm ch}-p_{\rm T}\) correlation observed by CMS (2019) arises naturally from string fragmentation and resonance decay, and they propose that measuring the slope of \(A_{\rm ch}-\Delta p_{\rm T}\) and \(A_{\rm ch}-\Delta|\eta|\) in varied kinematic windows and with identified hadrons can help disentangle LCC-induced from CMW-induced \(A_{\rm ch}-v_2\) dependence.
