# The properties of the neutron star PSR J0348+0432

> The properties of the massive neutron star PSR J0348+0432 is calculated in the framework of the relativistic mean field theory by choosing the suitable hyperon coupling constants. It is found that the central energy density $Œµ_{c}$ and the central pressure $p_{c}$ of the massive neutron star PSR J0348+0432 respectively are 1.5 times larger and 3.6 times larger than those of the canonical mass neutron star. It is also found that in the neutron star PSR J0348+0432 there are five kinds of baryons appearing: n, p, $Œõ$, $Œû^{-}$ and $Œû^{0}$ but in the canonical mass neutron star there are only three kinds of particles appearing: n, p and $Œõ$. In our models, the positive well depth $U_Œ£^{(N)}$ will restrict the production of the hyperons $Œ£^{-}$, $Œ£^{0}$ and $Œ£^{+}$ and therefore either in the neutron star PSR J0348+0432 or in the canonical mass neutron star the hyperons $Œ£^{-}$, $Œ£^{0}$ and $Œ£^{+}$ all do not appear. In addition, our results also show that the radius $R$ of the massive neutron star PSR J0348+0432 is less than that of the canonical mass neutron star while the gravitational redshift of the former is larger than that of the latter.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of massive neutron stars (PSR J1614-2230 at 1.97 M‚òâ, Demorest 2010) by applying RMF theory with hyperons to the more massive PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis et al. 2013). The predicted gravitational redshift of 0.4026 exceeds the earlier range of 0.25‚Äì0.35 quoted by Liang (1986), which the author attributes to the higher mass of this star. The results are consistent with recent work by Colucci (2013), van Dalen (2014), Oertel (2014), and Raduta (2014) suggesting that massive neutron stars may contain hyperons. The author notes that the specific parameter set is not unique, but the results illustrate the properties of this massive neutron star.
