# Neutral weak current two-body contributions in inclusive scattering from $^{12}$C

> An ab initio calculation of the sum rules of the neutral weak response functions in $^{12}$C is reported, based on a realistic Hamiltonian, including two- and three-nucleon potentials, and on realistic currents, consisting of one- and two-body terms. We find that the sum rules of the response functions associated with the longitudinal and transverse components of the (space-like) neutral current are largest and that a significant portion ($\simeq 30$ %) of the calculated strength is due to two-body terms. This fact may have implications for the MiniBooNE and other neutrino quasi-elastic scattering data on nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors find that two-body terms in the neutral weak current contribute approximately 30% of the calculated strength in the sum rules of the neutral weak response functions for $^{12}$C, which may have implications for interpreting MiniBooNE and other neutrino quasi-elastic scattering data on nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed an ab initio calculation of the sum rules of the neutral weak response functions in $^{12}$C using Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) solutions of the Schrödinger equation with the Argonne $v_{18}$ (AV18) two-nucleon and Illinois-7 (IL7) three-nucleon potentials. The neutral weak current included both one- and two-body terms, with two-body axial currents derived from pion-exchange, rho-meson exchange, axial rho-pi transition, and Delta excitation mechanisms, using two different sets of cutoff masses (Set I and Set II) for regularization.

## Key numbers

- Two-body terms contribute approximately 30% of the calculated strength in the neutral weak response sum rules for $^{12}$C
- At momentum transfer $q \simeq 1$ fm$^{-1}$, the enhancement from two-nucleon currents is about 50% relative to the one-body values
- For $S_{xx}$ and $S_{xy}$ sum rules, enhancement from $T=1$ $np$ pairs becomes appreciable for $q\gtrsim 1$ fm$^{-1}$, remaining below about 15% of that due to $T=0$ pairs
- For $S_{zz}$ at $q \simeq 1$ fm$^{-1}$, contributions from $T=1$ $np$ pairs are about 30% of those due to $T=0$ pairs
- The axial-vector part of the $S_{xx}$ sum rule has two-body contributions of the order of 30% relative to the one-body contributions
- The Weinberg angle used is $\sin^2\theta_W = 0.2312$ and the Fermi constant is $G_F = 1.1803 \times 10^{-5}$ GeV$^{-2}$
- The axial coupling constant $g_A$ is 1.2694

## Context

This work extends the standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA) previously applied to electromagnetic response functions in $^{12}$C by Lovato (2013) to neutral weak current responses. The results contrast with earlier calculations of neutrino-deuteron scattering by Shen (2012), where two-body current contributions amounted to only a few percent at the quasielastic peak, because correlations in $np$ pairs in nuclei with mass number $A \geq 3$ are stronger than in the deuteron. The findings can serve as constraints for more phenomenological approaches such as the relativistic Fermi gas models of Nieves (2004) and the random-phase approximation treatments of Martini (2009, 2010), and may help guide improvements to experimental analyses of quasi-elastic scattering in neutrino experiments.
