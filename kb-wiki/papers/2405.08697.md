# Color Chiral Cherenkov radiation and energy loss in quark-gluon plasma

> We introduce and investigate the Color Chiral Cherenkov effect which consists in radiation of the circularly polarized gluons by a fast color charge moving with constant velocity in the presence of the Chiral Magnetic current. We derive the transition rates for all gluon polarizations. We compute the contribution of the Color Chiral Cherenkov effect to the parton energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper introduces the Color Chiral Cherenkov effect, radiation of circularly polarized gluons by a fast color charge moving at constant velocity in the presence of a Chiral Magnetic current, and computes its contribution to parton energy loss in quark-gluon plasma, but only for the anomaly-induced radiation, which is a fraction of the total gluon radiation.

## Method

The authors derive the gluon dispersion relation modified by the chiral magnetic current, including screening effects via a plasma frequency term. They compute transition rates for the quasi-Abelian channel \(q\to qg\) and the novel non-Abelian channel \(g\to gg\) using high-energy approximations, where momenta along the jet axis are much larger than transverse momenta and effective masses. The calculation incorporates the anomalous triple-gluon vertex arising from the \(\theta\)-term in the Lagrangian and integrates over transverse momentum to obtain gluon emission spectra and energy loss rates.

## Key numbers

* The color factor for \(q\to qg\) is \(C_{q\to qg}=4/3\); for \(g\to gg\) it is \(C_{g\to gg}=3\).
* The infrared threshold energy for the incident quark is \(E_1 = (2m\omega_p + \omega_p^2)/b_0\).
* The infrared threshold energy for the incident gluon is \(E_2 = 3\omega_p^2/b_0\).
* In the high-energy limit \(b_0E \gg m^2,\omega_p^2\), the energy loss for \(q\to qg\) is \(-\frac{dE_{q\to qg}}{dz} = \frac{4\alpha_s g^2 b_0 E}{9}\).
* In the high-energy limit, the energy loss for \(g_R\to gg\) is \(-\frac{dE_{g_R\to gg}}{dz} = \frac{3\alpha_s g^2 b_0 E}{4}\left(\ln\frac{b_0 E}{\omega_p^2} - 1\right)\).
* In the high-energy limit, the energy loss for \(g_L\to gg\) is \(-\frac{dE_{g_L\to gg}}{dz} = \frac{3\alpha_s g^2 b_0 E}{4}\left(\ln\frac{b_0 E}{\omega_p^2} - \frac{17}{6}\right)\).
* The plasma frequency is \(\omega_p^2 = \frac{g^2 T^2}{18}(2N_c + N_f)\) and the quark thermal mass is \(m^2 = \frac{g^2 T^2}{16N_c}(N_c^2 - 1)\); example calculations use \(g=2\), \(T=300\) MeV, \(b_0=50\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Chiral Cherenkov effect from QED, previously studied by Tuchin (2018) and Hansen (2020), to QCD in the quark-gluon plasma. It introduces the novel \(g\to gg\) channel where all three gluons are excitations of the chiral medium, which was absent in the QED case. The results enable incorporation of anomaly-induced energy loss into parton evolution equations and jet quenching phenomenology, complementing conventional energy loss mechanisms reviewed by Cao (2021).
