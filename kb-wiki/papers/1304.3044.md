# Collective dynamics of the high-energy proton-nucleus collisions

> We analyze the proton-lead collisions at the LHC energy of 5.02TeV in the three-stage approach, previously used to successfully describe the relativistic A-A collisions. The approach consists of the early phase, modeled with the Glauber model, the event-by-event viscous 3+1 dimensional (3+1 D) relativistic hydrodynamics, and the statistical hadronization at freeze-out. We show that features typical of collective dynamics, such as the harmonic flow and the ridge structures in the two-particle correlations in relative azimuth and pseudorapidity, may be naturally explained in our framework. In the proton-nucleus system the harmonic flow is generated from an initially event-by-event deformed system and is entirely due to these initial fluctuations. Notably, fluctuations of strength of the initial Glauber sources which yield the observed distribution of hadron multiplicities and, at the same time, lead to correct values of the elliptic flow coefficients both from the two- and four-particle cumulant method, as measured by the ATLAS collaboration. The azimuthally asymmetric flow is not modified significantly when changing the viscosity coefficient, the initial time for the collective expansion, or the initial size of the fireball. The results present an estimate of the collective component in the two-particle correlations measured experimentally. We demonstrate that the harmonic flow coefficients can be experimentally measured with methods based on large rapidity gaps which reduce some of the other sources of correlations.

## Key claim

The paper shows that collective dynamics, specifically harmonic flow and ridge structures in two-particle correlations, can naturally explain proton-lead collisions at LHC energies using a three-stage hydrodynamic framework, but only for the most central collisions (highest multiplicity events) and for soft particles with transverse momenta below 2 GeV.

## Method

The authors used an event-by-event three-stage approach: initial conditions generated with the Glauber Monte Carlo model (GLISSANDO), followed by 3+1 dimensional viscous relativistic hydrodynamics with shear and bulk viscosities, and finally statistical hadronization at freeze-out using THERMINATOR. They compared two variants of the initial source placement (standard and compact) and included additional fluctuations via a Gamma distribution overlaid on participant nucleons (Glauber+NB case) to reproduce the observed multiplicity distributions.

- Lattice spacing for hydrodynamics: 0.15 fm
- Beam rapidity: y_b = 8.58
- Longitudinal profile parameters: eta_0 = 2.5, sigma_eta = 1.4

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous hydrodynamic analyses of proton-nucleus and deuteron-nucleus systems (Bozek 2011) and event-by-event studies of correlation functions (Bozek 2012) to the LHC p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV. The results support the collective flow interpretation of ridge structures observed by the ATLAS and CMS collaborations, providing an alternative to explanations based on saturation and color-glass-condensate theory (Dusling 2012, Kovchegov 2012). The framework enables quantitative estimates of the collective component in two-particle correlations and demonstrates that harmonic flow coefficients can be measured using methods with large rapidity gaps to reduce non-flow correlations.
