# Gluon dissociation of J/psi beyond the dipole approximation

> Using a nonrelativistic potential model, we calculate the cross section for the leading-order gluon dissociation of J/psi by including the full gluon wave function. We find that the resulting cross section as a function of gluon energy is reduced by about a factor of three at its maximum value compared to that calculated in the dipole approximation that is usually adopted in theoretical studies. The effect of the reduced cross section on the J/psi dissociation width at finite temperature is also discussed.

## Key claim

The cross section for leading-order gluon dissociation of J/psi is reduced by about a factor of three at its maximum value when the full gluon wave function is included beyond the dipole approximation, but the effect on the J/psi dissociation width at finite temperature is small.

## Method

The authors use a nonrelativistic potential model treating the gluon as an external field, with the J/psi described by a Coulomb-like wave function from the one-gluon exchange potential. They calculate the transition matrix element from two contributions: the direct coupling of the gluon to the heavy quarks (H_I) and the three-gluon interaction term (V_1) where the gluon couples to the exchanged gluon between the charm and anticharm quarks. The cross section is obtained by integrating over the relative momentum of the final-state c-cbar pair, and the finite-temperature width is computed using thermal gluon masses and temperature-dependent binding energies from lattice QCD potentials.

## Key numbers

- The J/psi binding energy in vacuum is epsilon = 0.64 GeV, with J/psi mass m_J/psi = 3.1 GeV and charm quark mass m_c = 1.87 GeV.
- The ratio of the beyond-dipole cross section to the dipole cross section is about 0.3 at gluon energy omega ~ 1 GeV, where the cross section peaks.
- The threshold for gluon dissociation is 0.07 GeV higher beyond the dipole approximation compared to the dipole approximation.
- For the free energy potential case, the J/psi width ratio (beyond dipole / dipole) is larger than 0.8 at all temperatures.
- For the internal energy potential case at T = 1.2 T_c, the binding energy is 0.3 GeV, the gluon mass is about 0.34 GeV, and the J/psi mass is 3.3 GeV, giving alpha_eff ~ 0.9.
- The ratio (p/p_dipole)^3 has a maximum value of 0.53 at gluon energy omega = 1.28 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the original Bhanot and Peskin (1979) calculation of J/psi gluon dissociation using the operator product expansion, as well as later leading-order pQCD calculations by Oh et al. (2001) and next-to-leading-order work by Park et al. (2007). The dipole approximation (neglecting the gluon wave function phase) has been standard in all these studies, but the authors show it is not accurate because kr ~ pi/2 for J/psi. The results enable more precise calculations of J/psi suppression in quark-gluon plasma, particularly for determining whether the dipole approximation is valid at finite temperature when using either the free energy or internal energy from lattice QCD calculations as the heavy quark potential.
