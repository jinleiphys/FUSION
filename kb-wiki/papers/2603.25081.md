# Extracting Resonance Width from Lattice Quantum Monte Carlo Simulations Using Analytical Continuation Method

> Nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) provides an efficient ab initio framework for computing low-lying states via imaginary-time projection. However, the extraction of unstable resonances, especially those with broad widths, remains a significant challenge. Traditional techniques such as the complex scaling method are often limited by sign problems or inherent statistical uncertainties. In this work, we present the first direct extraction of a nuclear resonance width within NLEFT by combining a high-precision, sign-problem-free nuclear interaction with the analytical continuation in the coupling constant (ACCC) approach. To address numerical instabilities in the ACCC framework, we implement a robust Pade solver based on singular value decomposition (SVD), incorporating ridge regularization and pole-safety criteria to ensure reliable extrapolation to the resonance pole. We detail the methodology and apply it to the unbound ground state of $^5$He ($J^π=3/2^-$). Our calculation yields a resonance energy $E=0.80(10)$ MeV and a width $Γ=1.05(9)$ MeV, in agreement with recent experimental results ($E_{\rm exp}=0.798$ MeV, $Γ_{\rm exp}=0.648$ MeV). This work establishes a practical and precise strategy for studying resonances within the ab initio lattice framework, paving the way for investigations of many-body resonances in exotic nuclei near the drip lines.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first direct extraction of a nuclear resonance width within nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) by combining a sign-problem-free lattice interaction with the analytic continuation in the coupling constant (ACCC) method, but the extracted width of 1.05(9) MeV for the 5He 3/2- resonance shows a 0.4 MeV discrepancy with the experimental value of 0.648 MeV, indicating that the method is not yet quantitatively precise for broad resonances.

## Method
The authors used the LAT-OPT1 sign-problem-free lattice interaction on an 11^3 cubic lattice to compute bound-state energies of 4He and 5He via auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (AFQMC) for a range of coupling constant values above the branch point. They then applied the ACCC method using a Pade approximant of the second kind (PAII) with singular value decomposition (SVD)-based ridge regularization and pole-safety criteria to analytically continue the complex momentum from the bound-state region to the physical coupling, extracting the resonance pole position. Jackknife analysis was used to estimate uncertainties from the discrete sampling of the bound-state region.

## Key numbers
- Resonance energy E = 0.80(10) MeV and width Gamma = 1.05(9) MeV for the 5He J^pi = 3/2- resonance, extracted from the ACCC analysis
- Experimental reference values: E_exp = 0.798 MeV, Gamma_exp = 0.648 MeV
- Branch point lambda_0 = 1.1331, determined by solving p(lambda_0)=0 with the bisection method
- Bound-state data were computed in the interval z = sqrt(lambda - lambda_0) in [0.1, 0.6] on a lattice of size L = 11
- Pade orders (3,3) through (6,6) yielded E values ranging from 0.80(18) to 0.80(21) MeV and Gamma values from 0.98(22) to 1.07(17) MeV
- LOOCV values ranged from 0.0163 to 0.0252 and RMS values from 0.0121 to 0.0122 across Pade orders

## Context
This work extends the ACCC method, previously applied in potential models by Kukulin (1989) and others, to ab initio lattice Monte Carlo calculations for the first time. It addresses the long-standing challenge of extracting resonance widths in NLEFT, which previously could only treat very narrow resonances like the Hoyle state in 12C as bound states. The framework enables future studies of many-body resonances in exotic nuclei near the drip lines, building on recent progress in NLEFT for ground states and scattering by Lu (2025) and others.
