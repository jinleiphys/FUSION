# Next-to-eikonal corrections to dijet production in Deep Inelastic Scattering in the dilute limit of the Color Glass Condensate

> We analyze the effects of next-to-eikonal corrections on dijet production in Deep Inelastic Scattering off nuclear targets in the framework of the Color Glass Condensate. They require the knowledge of correlators of fields in the target beyond those computed in the standard McLerran-Venugopalan model, specifically those between transverse and boost-enhanced components, and of the recoil of the fields. We neglect the latter, while for the former we develop a linear model valid for large nuclei. We considered the unpolarized cross sections for dijet production in the approximation of a homogenous dilute nucleus, obtaining simple analytic expressions for the cross sections at nex-to-eikonal accuracy, valid in the limit of total dijet momentum and dijet momentum imbalance larger than the saturation scale of the nucleus. We perform a numerical study of the results at energies of the Electron Ion Collider, finding $\mathcal{O}(10\%)$ effects in the cross sections at large total momentum. We also analyze the azimuthal asymmetries between total momentum and imbalance, finding that non-eikonal corrections induce odd azimuthal harmonics for the situation of jets with equal momentum fractions from the virtual photon, where they are absent in the eikonal approximation. Finally, in the eikonal approximation we have compared the results of our analytic expansion valid in the dilute limit of the target, and the full Color Glass Condensate results in the McLerran-Venugopalan model and their correlation limit. Our analytic expressions match the correlation limit ones in the region where both should be simultaneously valid and reproduce very well the full Color Glass Condensate results in its validity region.

## Key claim

Next-to-eikonal corrections to dijet production in Deep Inelastic Scattering off nuclear targets produce effects of order 10% in the cross sections at large total momentum at Electron Ion Collider energies, and induce odd azimuthal harmonics that are absent in the eikonal approximation, but the analysis neglects the recoil of the target fields and assumes a linear model for correlators between transverse and boost-enhanced field components valid only for large nuclei.

## Method

The authors start from the expressions for dijet production in DIS with next-to-eikonal corrections computed in Altinoluk (2022), neglect the dependence of target fields on the longitudinal coordinate \(x^-\), and develop an extension of the McLerran-Venugopalan model to provide target averages of correlators involving perpendicular field components. They then perform the lowest-order expansion of the correlators of Wilson lines (two-gluon exchange approximation) valid for total dijet momentum and momentum imbalance larger than the saturation scale \(Q_s\), obtaining fully analytic expressions for the unpolarized cross sections at next-to-eikonal accuracy, and compare these with the full eikonal Color Glass Condensate results and the correlation limit.

## Key numbers

- Non-eikonal corrections are \(\mathcal{O}(10\%)\) in the cross sections at large total momentum for \(e^-{\rm Au}\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 90\) GeV.
- The analytic expressions are valid in the limit where total dijet momentum \(|{\bf P}|\) and dijet momentum imbalance \(|{\bf \Delta}|\) are larger than the saturation scale \(Q_s\).
- The study considers center-of-mass energies in the range \(20-140\) GeV/nucleon, corresponding to the Electron Ion Collider design parameters.
- Non-eikonal effects are significant for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\lesssim 100\) GeV, below top RHIC energies.

## Context

This work extends the study of non-eikonal corrections in the Color Glass Condensate framework, which began with the relaxation of the shockwave approximation by Altinoluk (2014, 2015) and continued with corrections to propagators and quark exchanges by Altinoluk (2020, 2021) and Agostini (2023). It builds directly on the cross section computation including non-eikonal corrections by Altinoluk (2022), which required knowledge of target field averages beyond the standard McLerran-Venugopalan model. The results enable numerical implementation of next-to-eikonal effects in dijet production at the Electron Ion Collider, where these corrections are expected to be sizeable, and provide a link between Color Glass Condensate calculations and transverse momentum distributions in the correlation limit studied by Dominguez (2011), Kotko (2015), and Dumitru (2015, 2018).
