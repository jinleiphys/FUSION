# Impacts of dark matter on the $f$-mode oscillation of hyperon star

> We investigate the $f$-mode oscillation of the dark matter admixed hyperon star within the relativistic Cowling approximation. The macroscopic properties are calculated with the relativistic mean-field equation of states by assuming that the dark matter particles are inside the star. The $f$-mode oscillation frequencies (only for $l=2$) are calculated with four different neutron star equation of states. We also check the effects of hyperons/dark matter and hyperons with dark matter equation of states on the $f$-mode oscillations varying with different astrophysical quantities such as mass ($M$), radius ($R$), compactness ($M/R$), surface red-shift ($Z_s$), average density ($\barρ$), dimensionless tidal deformability ($Λ$) of the neutron star. Significant changes have been seen in the $f$-mode frequencies with and without hyperons/dark matter or hyperons+dark matter. Substantial correlations are observed between canonical frequencies and $Λ$ ($f_{1.4}-Λ_{1.4}$) and maximum frequencies and canonical $Λ$ ( $f_{max}-Λ_{1.4}$).

## Key claim
The paper investigates how dark matter (DM) and hyperons individually and jointly affect the fundamental-mode (f-mode) oscillation frequencies of neutron stars, finding that the presence of DM and/or hyperons softens the equation of state and systematically lowers the f-mode frequencies, but only within the relativistic Cowling approximation (which neglects metric perturbations) and only for the quadrupole (l=2) mode.

## Method
The authors use the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with four different parameter sets (NL3, IOPB-I, FSUGarnet, G3) to compute equations of state for neutron star matter including baryons (nucleons and hyperons Λ, Σ, Ξ) and dark matter particles (neutralinos of mass 200 GeV interacting via Higgs exchange). The f-mode oscillation frequencies are calculated by solving the perturbed fluid equations within the relativistic Cowling approximation for the l=2 quadrupole mode, using the TOV equations to obtain mass-radius relations and then computing the frequencies as a function of stellar mass, radius, compactness, surface redshift, average density, and dimensionless tidal deformability.

## Key numbers
- Dark matter particle mass: 200 GeV (neutralino candidate)
- Dark matter Fermi momentum varied from 0 to 0.06 GeV; primary calculation uses k_f^DM = 0.03 GeV (labeled DM3)
- DM-Higgs coupling y = 0.07; Higgs vacuum expectation value v = 246 GeV
- Baryon-Higgs form factor f_B = 0.35 for all baryons
- Higgs mass M_h = 125 GeV
- Hyperon potential depths: U_Λ = -28 MeV, U_Σ = +30 MeV, U_Ξ = -18 MeV
- Maximum masses: IOPB-I (no DM/hyperons) = 2.15 M_⊙; IOPB-I+DM3 = 2.05 M_⊙; IOPB-I+Y = 1.70 M_⊙; IOPB-I+Y+DM3 = 1.61 M_⊙
- Corresponding radii at maximum mass: IOPB-I = 11.75 km; IOPB-I+DM3 = 11.02 km; IOPB-I+Y = 11.39 km; IOPB-I+Y+DM3 = 10.40 km
- Spin-independent scattering cross-section for nucleon-DM: 9.70 × 10^{-46} cm^2 (for M_χ = 200 GeV)
- Spin-independent cross-sections for hyperon-DM: 1.37 × 10^{-45} cm^2 (Λ), 1.56 × 10^{-45} cm^2 (Σ), 1.91 × 10^{-45} cm^2 (Ξ)

## Context
This work extends previous studies of neutron star f-mode oscillations (Anderson 1996, Anderson 1998; Sotani 2011) by including both hyperons and dark matter simultaneously for the first time. It builds on the authors' prior calculations of dark matter admixed neutron star properties (Das 2020, Das 2021, DasMNRAS 2021, DasPRD 2021) and hyperon star studies (Biswal 2019; Lopes 2014, 2018). The results enable future gravitational wave observations to potentially distinguish between neutron stars with different compositions (pure nucleonic, hyperonic, dark matter admixed, or combined) by comparing observed f-mode frequencies with the predicted correlations between frequency and tidal deformability (f_1.4-Λ_1.4 and f_max-Λ_1.4).
