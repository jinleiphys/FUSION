# Pion Valence Structure at Intermediate x in the Residual Field Approach

> We calculate the valence parton distribution function~(PDF) of pion, within the theoretical approach based on spectral function representation of valence quarks in the pion. In this approach we assume that the soft partonic structure of pion is defined by the valence $q\bar q$ cluster, consisting of current quarks, embedded in the residual field of the pion. The valence PDF is calculated using phenomenological Light-Front wave functions for the $q\bar q$ cluster and the residual field. Our result indicates that the peak position of the x weighted valence PDF~(xPDF) depends on parameters characterizing the virtuality of the cluster and the mass of the residual system. Magnitudes of these parameters are obtained by fitting to the height and the peak position of empirical pion xPDF evaluated at starting $Q_0$. They indicate that unlike the nucleon case, very little residual mass is needed to describe existing pion PDFs at intermediate x. They also indicate that the $q\bar q$-cluster is highly virtual and on average the interacting quark carries almost all of the momentum of the $q\bar q$ cluster. This picture is consistent with the dominance of the Feynman mechanism leaving little room for the hard component in the PDF, and practically describing $\sim (1-x)$ behavior observed recently at $x\to 1$ limit. Our non-trivial observation is that the height and the peak position of xPDF define the analytic behavior of valence PDF at $x\to 1$ limit.

## Key claim
The valence parton distribution function (PDF) of the pion at intermediate x can be described by a model where a valence q\bar{q} cluster of current quarks is embedded in a residual field, and the height and peak position of the x-weighted valence PDF (xPDF) determine the analytic behavior of the valence PDF in the x→1 limit; the model finds that very little residual mass is needed to describe existing pion PDFs, unlike the nucleon case.

## Method
The authors model the pion as a transition into a valence q\bar{q} cluster and a residual system (R), using phenomenological Light-Front wave functions for both the q\bar{q} cluster and the valence-residual (VR) system. The valence PDF is derived from the hadronic tensor in deep-inelastic scattering, factorizing the response functions of the q\bar{q} cluster and the residual system. The model parameters (cluster virtuality m_V, residual mass m_R, and slope parameter B_R) are obtained by fitting the theoretical xPDF to the empirical JAM collaboration pion PDF at the starting scale Q_0.

## Key numbers
- The fit to the empirical pion xPDF is performed in the range 0.1 ≤ x ≤ 0.85.
- The q\bar{q} cluster response function peaks at β = 1/2, consistent with a relativistic wave function of massless q\bar{q} system.
- The normalization of the q\bar{q} wave function is N_{q\bar{q}} = 1 - h, where h accounts for hard components and Regge dynamics at x < 0.1.
- The residual mass m_R is found to be very small compared to the nucleon case, with the q\bar{q} cluster being highly virtual such that the interacting quark carries almost all of the cluster's momentum.
- The model describes a (1-x)^β behavior with β ≈ 1-1.2 at large x, consistent with recent observations.

## Context
This work extends the residual field approach previously applied to the nucleon by Leon et al. (2020), where the valence PDF peaking property was described using a residual system with mass comparable to the pion mass, indirectly favoring a pion cloud structure in the nucleon. For the pion, the model finds that very little residual mass is needed, contrasting with the nucleon case and suggesting dominance of the Feynman mechanism with little room for hard components. The approach enables further investigation of pion form factors, generalized partonic distributions, transverse momentum distributions, and processes involving target fragmentation in collider kinematics, as noted by Abir et al. (2023).
