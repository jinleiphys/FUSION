# Fermionic Squeezed State for Simple Algebraic Models in Many-Fermion Systems

> It is shown that a variational approach with fermionic squeezed states to many-fermion systems such as pairing model is one of useful methods beyond the usual Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximation. A pairing-type quasi-spin squeezed state is constructed and adopted as a trial state in the variational method. By using this state, quantum fluctuations are taken into account. In pairing model, dynamical and static approaches are discussed in the context of comparing with the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximation about ground state energy. As a possible extension to the O(4) model, prospective squeezed states are investigated and the usefulness is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a variational approach using fermionic squeezed states (specifically a pairing-type quasi-spin squeezed state) can reproduce the exact ground state energy of the pairing model more accurately than the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (coherent state) approximation, but only under a large-\(N\) and large-\(\Omega\) limit or through numerical minimization with a chemical potential; the analytical reproduction of the exact energy requires a consistency condition derived from the time-dependent variational principle.

## Method

The authors construct a quasi-spin squeezed state on top of an \(su(2)\)-coherent (BCS) state by applying an exponential of a squared boson-like operator \(\hat{B}^{\dagger 2}\) built from Bogoliubov-transformed fermion operators. They then apply a time-dependent variational principle to derive equations of motion for canonical variables \((X, X^*)\) and \((Y, Y^*)\), where \(|Y|^2\) represents quantum fluctuations. For the static treatment, they minimize \(\langle \hat{H} - \mu \hat{N} \rangle\) with respect to the variational parameters \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\) to enforce particle number conservation.

## Key numbers

- Exact ground state energy for the pairing model: \(E_0 = \epsilon N - \frac{1}{4} G N \Omega \left(2 - \frac{N}{\Omega} + \frac{2}{\Omega}\right)\) (Eq. 2-11).
- Coherent state (BCS) ground state energy: \(E = \epsilon N - \frac{1}{4} G N \Omega \left(2 - \frac{N}{\Omega} + \frac{N}{\Omega^2}\right)\) (Eq. 2-18).
- Quasi-spin squeezed state ground state energy (large \(N\), \(\Omega\) limit): \(H_{\text{sq}} = \epsilon N - \frac{1}{4} G N \Omega \left(2 - \frac{N}{\Omega} + \frac{2}{\Omega} + \text{O}(1/N\Omega, 1/\Omega^2, 1/N^2)\right)\) (Eq. 3-31).
- Numerical results are shown for \(N = \Omega = 8\) (Fig. 1) and for \(\epsilon = 1.0\), \(G = 1.0\) with varying \(N\) (Fig. 2), where the squeezed state energy nearly matches the exact eigenvalue.

## Context

This work extends the quasi-spin squeezed state approach previously applied to the Lipkin model (Tsue et al., 1994; Tsue et al., 1996) to the pairing model with a single energy level, and further proposes extensions to the O(4) model with pairing plus quadrupole interactions. The method goes beyond the standard Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (BCS) approximation and the random phase approximation (RPA) by incorporating quantum fluctuations dynamically. The O(4) extension is motivated by recent analyses of shape coexistence in nuclei (Nakatsukasa and Walet, 1998; Kaneko et al., 2003).
