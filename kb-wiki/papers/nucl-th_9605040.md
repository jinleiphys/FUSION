# On the $α-$decay of deformed actinide nuclei

> $α-$decay through a deformed potential barrier produces significant mixing of angular momenta when mapped from the nuclear interior to the outside. Using experimental branching ratios and either semi-classical or coupled-channels transmission matrices, we have found that there is a set of internal amplitudes which are essentially constant for all even--even actinide nuclei. These same amplitudes also give good results for the known anisotropic $α-$particle emission of the favored decays of odd nuclei in the same mass region. PACS numbers: 23.60.+e, 24.10.Eq, 27.90.+b

# On the α-decay of deformed actinide nuclei

## Key claim

The internal α-particle amplitudes near the nuclear surface are essentially constant across all even-even actinide nuclei (daughter mass 220 ≤ A_d ≤ 248, atomic number 88 ≤ Z_d ≤ 96), and these same amplitudes, when combined with a modified transmission formula that decouples the daughter spin, can reproduce the measured anisotropies in favored decays of odd-even nuclei, but only for one specific choice of relative phases of the external amplitudes ({+--}).

## Method

The authors extracted internal amplitudes a_L (L=0,2,4) from experimental branching ratios by inverting the transmission matrix that connects the wave function inside the nucleus to the outgoing α-particle waves outside the deformed Coulomb barrier. They used both a semi-classical (WKB) method and exact coupled-channels calculations, with deformation parameters (β_2 and β_4) taken from Möller et al. (1995), and considered four possible sign combinations for the external coefficients c_L. For odd-even nuclei, they applied a modified formula where the transmission matrix is weighted by Clebsch-Gordan coefficients that couple the parent and daughter spins, effectively decoupling the daughter excited states.

## Context

This work extends the semi-classical barrier-penetration approach of Fröman (1957) and the coupled-channels formalism used by Delion et al. (1992, 1994) for α-decay of deformed nuclei, but avoids any specific model of α-particle formation or preformation. The key finding, that internal amplitudes are nearly nucleus-independent, suggests that the best physical model is one where α-particle amplitudes are projected from pair-correlated neutron and proton Nilsson-model states, since pairing through two-body angular momentum J=0 would naturally give |a_0| > |a_2| > |a_4| as observed. The results enable future work to test whether microscopic BCS-based calculations can reproduce these constant amplitudes, and whether the same decoupling mechanism applies to other odd-even actinide decays where anisotropy measurements become available.
