# Sub Coulomb barrier d+$^{208}$Pb scattering in the time-dependent basis function approach

> We employ the non-perturbative time-dependent basis function (tBF) approach to study the scattering of the deuteron on $^{208}$Pb below the Coulomb barrier. We obtain the bound and discretized scattering states of the projectile, which form the basis representation of the tBF approach, by diagonalizing a realistic Hamiltonian in a large harmonic oscillator basis. We find that the higher-order inelastic scattering effects are noticeable for sub barrier scatterings with the tBF method. We have successfully reproduced experimental sub Coulomb barrier elastic cross section ratios with the tBF approach by considering only the electric dipole ($\mathit{E1}$) component of the Coulomb interaction between the projectile and the target during scatterings. We find that the correction of the polarization potential to the Rutherford trajectory is dominant in reproducing the data at very low bombarding energies, whereas the role of internal transitions of the deuteron projectile induced by the $\mathit{E1}$ interaction during the scattering becomes increasingly significant at higher bombarding energies.

## Key claim
The time-dependent basis function (tBF) approach reproduces experimental sub-Coulomb-barrier elastic cross section ratios for deuteron scattering on \(^{208}\)Pb using only the electric dipole (E1) component of the Coulomb interaction, without any optical potential, but only when the polarization potential correction to the Rutherford trajectory is included at very low bombarding energies.

## Method
The authors solved the deuteron bound and discretized scattering states by diagonalizing a realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction (LENPIC-N\(^4\)LO) in a large three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis. They then evolved the projectile state non-perturbatively in time along a classical trajectory determined by either the Coulomb potential alone or the Coulomb potential plus a polarization potential, using only the E1 component of the Coulomb interaction between the deuteron and the \(^{208}\)Pb target. The elastic cross section ratio \(R(E_d)\) was computed from the elastic scattering probability and the classical differential cross section.

## Key numbers
- The E1 polarizability of the deuteron predicted by the LENPIC-N\(^4\)LO interaction converges to \(\alpha = 0.635\) fm\(^3\), consistent with experimental values of \(0.7 \pm 0.05\) fm\(^3\) (Rodning 1982) and \(0.61 \pm 0.04\) fm\(^3\) (Friar 1983).
- At \(E_d = 7\) MeV and \(\theta = 150^\circ\), the total probability for populating breakup states is approximately 0.022 when higher-order transitions are included, compared to about 0.00015 when only first-order E1-allowed transitions are retained.
- The tBF results reproduce experimental \(R(E_d)\) data for bombarding energies \(E_d = 3-6\) MeV but fall below experiment at \(E_d = 7\) MeV.
- The most significant change in \(R(E_d)\) when increasing \(N_{\rm max}\) by 20% (to 240) and \(E_{\rm cut}\) by 20% (to 16.8 MeV) is on the order of \(10^{-4}\) at \(E_d = 7\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the time-dependent basis function (tBF) approach previously developed by Du (2018) to treat scattering of a deuteron on a heavy target, using a realistic NN interaction from chiral effective field theory (LENPIC collaboration). It contrasts with earlier optical model analyses by Rodning (1982) and Moro (1999), which required phenomenological optical potentials with uncertain parameters, and with the continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) method by Aoki (2000), which included an imaginary optical potential. The results demonstrate that the tBF method can describe sub-barrier scattering without an optical potential, enabling future applications to other light projectiles using ab initio nuclear structure methods.
