# Constraining the $^{22}$Ne($α$,$γ$)$^{26}$Mg and $^{22}$Ne($α$,n)$^{25}$Mg reaction rates using sub-Coulomb $α$-transfer reactions

> The $^{22}$Ne($α$,$γ$)$^{26}$Mg and $^{22}$Ne($α$,n)$^{25}$Mg reactions play an important role in astrophysics because they have significant influence on the neutron flux during the weak branch of the s-process. We constrain the astrophysical rates for these reactions by measuring partial $α$-widths of resonances in $^{26}$Mg located in the Gamow window for the $^{22}$Ne+$α$ capture. These resonances were populated using $^{22}$Ne($^6$Li,d)$^{26}$Mg and $^{22}$Ne($^7$Li,t)$^{26}$Mg reactions at energies near the Coulomb barrier. At these low energies $α$-transfer reactions favor population of low spin states and the extracted partial $α$-widths for the observed resonances exhibit only minor dependence on the model parameters. The astrophysical rates for both the $^{22}$Ne($α$,$γ$)$^{26}$Mg and the $^{22}$Ne($α$,n)$^{25}$Mg reactions are shown to be significantly different than the previously suggested values.

## Key claim

The astrophysical rates for the $^{22}$Ne($\alpha$,$\gamma$)$^{26}$Mg and $^{22}$Ne($\alpha$,n)$^{25}$Mg reactions are shown to be significantly different than previously suggested values, but the new rates depend on assumed spin-parity assignments for the observed resonances, and the 11.17 MeV resonance that was previously considered important is found to have a very stringent upper limit on its partial $\alpha$-width.

## Method

The authors used the $^{22}$Ne($^6$Li,d)$^{26}$Mg and $^{22}$Ne($^7$Li,t)$^{26}$Mg $\alpha$-transfer reactions at center-of-mass energies of 4.7 MeV and 5.3 MeV, respectively, which are below the Coulomb barrier of approximately 6 MeV. Deuterons and tritons were detected at 5 degrees in the lab frame using the MDM spectrometer at the Texas A&M Cyclotron Institute, and the excitation energies of states in $^{26}$Mg were reconstructed via missing mass. Partial $\alpha$-widths for four observed resonances in the Gamow window (10.83, 10.95, 11.08, and 11.32 MeV excitation) were extracted using Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) calculations with the code FRESCO, with the widths calculated for assumed spin-parity assignments of 0$^+$, 1$^-$, and 2$^+$.

## Key numbers

*   Partial $\alpha$-width for the 11.32 MeV state in $^{26}$Mg: 6.1 $\pm$ 0.4 $\pm$ 1.0 $\times$ 10$^{-5}$ eV (for 0$^+$ assignment), 1.3 $\pm$ 0.1 $\pm$ 0.2 $\times$ 10$^{-5}$ eV (for 1$^-$), and 3.0 $\pm$ 0.2 $\pm$ 0.5 $\times$ 10$^{-6}$ eV (for 2$^+$).
*   Upper limit for the partial $\alpha$-width of the 11.17 MeV state: less than 3 $\times$ 10$^{-9}$ eV (for 0$^+$ assignment) and less than 13 $\times$ 10$^{-12}$ eV (for 2$^+$ assignment).
*   Partial $\alpha$-width for the 10.95 MeV state: 3.0 $\pm$ 0.3 $\substack{+0.75 \\ -0.6}$ $\times$ 10$^{-14}$ eV (for 1$^-$ assignment).
*   Partial $\alpha$-width for the 10.83 MeV state: 2.1 $\pm$ 0.3 $\pm$ 0.4 $\times$ 10$^{-22}$ eV (for 2$^+$ assignment).
*   Neutron decay strength $\omega\gamma_{(\alpha,n)}$ for the 11.32 MeV state: 42 $\pm$ 11 $\mu$eV, derived from direct measurements and branching ratio, or 32 $\pm$ 7 $\mu$eV using the $\Gamma_\alpha$ from this work.

## Context

This work extends previous indirect studies of the $^{22}$Ne($\alpha$,n)$^{25}$Mg and $^{22}$Ne($\alpha$,$\gamma$)$^{26}$Mg reactions, particularly the $^{22}$Ne($^6$Li,d) studies by Giesen (1993) and Talwar (2016), by performing the $\alpha$-transfer reactions at sub-Coulomb energies to reduce model dependence. The results contradict the direct $^{22}$Ne($\alpha$,n)$^{25}$Mg measurements by Jaeger (2001) and Giesen (1993) for the 11.32 MeV resonance strength, but agree with direct $^{22}$Ne($\alpha$,$\gamma$)$^{26}$Mg measurements by Wolke (1989) and Hunt (2019) and the branching ratio study by Ota (2019). The stringent upper limit on the 11.17 MeV resonance resolves a controversy from Talwar (2016) and Massimi (2017), and the new partial $\alpha$-widths enable tighter constraints on the $^{22}$Ne($\alpha$,$\gamma$)$^{26}$Mg reaction rate, though firm spin-parity assignments for the observed states are still needed.
