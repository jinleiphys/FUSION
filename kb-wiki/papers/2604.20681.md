# Cutoff-independent predictions from nuclear lattice effective field theory

> Cutoff independence is an essential requirement for the predictive power of nuclear \textit{ab initio} calculations based on effective field theory (EFT). While it is conventionally assumed that such invariance necessitates high-order interactions and complex many-body forces, we present a minimal chiral nuclear force that exhibits remarkable cutoff independence across a broad range from light to medium-mass nuclei and sub-saturated nuclear matter. Our framework comprises only contact terms up to next-to-leading order, a single three-nucleon contact force, and a leading-order one-pion-exchange potential, all constrained strictly in the $A \leq 3$ sector. Despite its simplicity, this interaction accurately reproduces experimental binding energies up to $^{40}\text{Ca}$ with unexpectedly small residual cutoff dependencies of only a few MeV. We demonstrate that the use of a lattice-inspired \emph{absolute}-momentum regulator efficiently suppresses high-momentum modes, resolving the overbinding problem for soft chiral forces without invoking complex many-body forces. These results establish a robust and economic foundation for EFT-based \textit{ab initio} calculations in both continuum and lattice frameworks.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a minimal chiral nuclear force, comprising only contact terms up to next-to-leading order, a single three-nucleon contact force, and a leading-order one-pion-exchange potential constrained strictly in the A ≤ 3 sector, exhibits remarkable cutoff independence across light to medium-mass nuclei and sub-saturated nuclear matter, contrary to the conventional assumption that such invariance necessitates high-order interactions and complex many-body forces.

## Method

The authors constructed a minimal chiral nuclear force using contact terms up to next-to-leading order (NLO), a single three-nucleon contact force, and a leading-order one-pion-exchange potential, with all low-energy constants calibrated strictly using neutron-proton scattering data and the triton binding energy (A ≤ 3 sector). They employed a lattice-inspired absolute-momentum regulator that imposes a cutoff on single-nucleon momenta, and performed many-body calculations using nuclear lattice effective field theory for finite nuclei and the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock method for symmetric nuclear matter, repeating the calculations for different cutoff values to test cutoff independence.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies up to ⁴⁰Ca are reproduced with residual cutoff dependencies of only a few MeV.
- The ⁴He binding energy is reproduced with cutoff independence within 100 keV.
- Cutoff values were varied between 250 MeV and 400 MeV for the systematic RG study.

## Context

This work extends the study of renormalization group invariance in chiral effective field theory from few-body systems (A ≤ 4), where a single three-body force was shown sufficient for renormalization by Bedaque (1999), Wang (2012), and others, to substantially larger systems including ⁴⁰Ca and nuclear matter. It contradicts the widely held view articulated by Machleidt (2023) that robust RG invariance requires higher-order interactions or complex many-body forces, and it resolves the overbinding problem for soft chiral forces that has persisted in previous studies using conventional relative-momentum regulators. The absolute-momentum regulator was originally introduced in nuclear lattice effective field theory calculations to remove lattice artifacts, and this work demonstrates its decisive role in simultaneously enhancing RG invariance and improving agreement with experiment across a broad range of nuclear systems.
