# Charge Symmetry Breaking and Parity Violating Electron-Proton Scattering

> Charge symmetry breaking contributions to the proton's neutral weak form factors must be understood in order for future measurements of parity violating electron-proton scattering to be definitively interpreted as evidence of proton strangeness. We calculate these charge symmetry breaking form factor contributions using chiral perturbation theory with resonance saturation estimates for unknown low-energy constants. The uncertainty of the leading-order resonance saturation estimates is reduced by incorporating nuclear physics constraints. Higher-order contributions are investigated through phenomenological vertex form factors. We predict that charge symmetry breaking form factor contributions are an order of magnitude larger than expected from naive dimensional analysis but are still an order of magnitude smaller than current experimental bounds on proton strangeness. This is consistent with previous calculations using chiral perturbation theory with resonance saturation.

## Key claim

Charge symmetry breaking (CSB) contributions to the proton's neutral weak form factors are an order of magnitude larger than expected from naive dimensional analysis but remain an order of magnitude smaller than current experimental bounds on proton strangeness, provided that the vector-meson nucleon coupling constants used in resonance saturation estimates are constrained by nuclear physics data rather than the larger values used in prior work.

## Method

The authors calculated CSB form factor contributions using relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory (RBchiPT) at leading order, incorporating pion loop diagrams and a CSB nucleon-photon contact interaction. The unknown low-energy constant for the contact interaction was estimated via resonance saturation from rho-omega mixing, using vector-meson nucleon coupling constants constrained by the He-3 - H-3 binding energy difference and nucleon-nucleon scattering data. Higher-order corrections were investigated by including phenomenological vertex form factors for the pion-photon and pion-nucleon vertices.

## Key numbers

- The CSB magnetic moment contribution from rho-omega mixing using the authors' preferred coupling constants is 0.006, compared to the Kubis and Lewis (2006) value of 0.025 +/- 0.02
- The omega-nucleon coupling constant g_omega = 10.1 is used instead of the value g_omega ~ 42 used by Kubis and Lewis (2006), based on the compilation of Dumbrajs (1983) and Ericson (1988)
- The rho-omega mixing amplitude is Theta_{rho omega} = (-3.75 +/- 0.36) x 10^{-3} GeV^2
- The pion decay constant f_pi = 92.21 +/- 0.14 MeV
- The nucleon mass splitting Delta m_N = m_n - m_p = 1.2933322 +/- 0.0000004 MeV
- The axial dipole mass M_A = 1.00 +/- 0.02 GeV
- The pion form factor cutoff Lambda_pi = 677 +/- 16 MeV
- The He-3 - H-3 binding energy difference from CSB strong interactions is 73 +/- 22 keV
- The nn S-0-1 scattering length is more attractive than the pp one by 1.5 +/- 0.5 fm

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory calculation of Kubis and Lewis (2006) for CSB contributions to proton neutral weak form factors, which had been cited by experimental groups as a reason to halt searches for proton strangeness. The authors argue that the large omega-nucleon coupling constant used by Kubis and Lewis (2006) is incompatible with constraints from the He-3 - H-3 binding energy difference and nucleon-nucleon scattering, as established by Coon and Barrett (1987) and reviewed by Miller (1990, 1994, 2006). Using coupling constants consistent with these nuclear physics constraints reduces the predicted CSB magnetic moment contribution by approximately a factor of four, suggesting that CSB uncertainties need not preclude future strangeness measurements.
