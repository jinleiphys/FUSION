# Isospin-symmetry breaking in superallowed Fermi beta-decay due to isospin-nonconserving forces

> We investigate isospin-symmetry breaking effects in the sd-shell region with large-scale shell-model calculations, aiming to understand the recent anomalies observed in superallowed Fermi beta-decay. We begin with calculations of Coulomb displacement energies (CDE's) and triplet displacement energies (TDE's) by adding the T=1,J=0 isospin nonconserving (INC) interaction into the usual isospin-invariant Hamiltonian. It is found that CDE's and TDE's can be systematically described with high accuracy. A total number of 122 one- and two-proton separation energies are predicted accordingly, and locations of the proton drip-line and candidates for proton-emitters are thereby suggested. However, attempt to explain the anomalies in the superallowed Fermi beta-decay fails because these well-fitted T=1,J=0 INC interactions are found no effects on the nuclear matrix elements. It is demonstrated that the observed large isospin-breaking correction in the 32Cl beta-decay, the large isospin-mixing in the 31Cl beta-decay, and the small isospin-mixing in the 23Al beta-decay can be consistently understood by introducing additional T=1,J=2 INC interactions related to the s1/2 orbit.

## Key claim

The authors show that the standard isospin-nonconserving (INC) interactions with T=1,J=0, which successfully describe Coulomb displacement energies and triplet displacement energies across the sd-shell, have no effect on superallowed Fermi beta-decay matrix elements; only by introducing additional T=1,J=2 INC interactions related to the s1/2 orbit can the anomalous isospin-breaking corrections observed in 32Cl, 31Cl, and 23Al decays be consistently explained.

## Method

Large-scale shell-model calculations were performed in the sd model space using the charge-independent USDA interaction (H0) plus additional isovector and isotensor T=1,J=0 INC interactions fitted to experimental Coulomb displacement energies (CDEs) and triplet displacement energies (TDEs) for nuclei with A=18-39. After reproducing CDEs and TDEs with rms deviations of 66 keV and 34 keV respectively, the authors introduced additional T=1,J=2 INC interactions related to the s1/2 orbit to calculate Fermi matrix elements |MF|2 and isospin-breaking corrections δC1 for the superallowed beta-decays of 32Cl, 31Cl, and 23Al.

## Key numbers

- CDE rms deviation between calculation and experiment: 66 keV
- TDE rms deviation between calculation and experiment: 34 keV
- One-proton separation energy rms deviation: 94 keV; two-proton separation energy rms deviation: 148 keV (for 72 nuclei with data)
- Without T=1,J=0 INC forces, rms deviations for Sp and S2p increase by 18.5 keV and 30.9 keV respectively
- For 32Cl decay: calculated δC1 = 4.39% at Vs = 100 keV; adding δC2 = 0.865% gives total δC = 5.26%, matching the experimental δC = 5.3(9)%
- For 31Cl decay at Vs = 100 keV: calculated |MF|2 = 2.49 for T=3/2 and |MF|2 = 0.47 for T=1/2
- For 23Al decay: calculated |MF|2 = 2.97, corresponding to logft = 3.32
- Mixing matrix element v = 37.6 keV for 32Cl decay (experimental v = 36.8 keV); v = 30 keV for 31Cl decay (experimental v = 41 keV)
- Fitted T=1,J=0 INC strengths (in keV): V(1)J=0(d5/2)=100, V(2)J=0(d5/2)=150; V(1)J=0(s1/2)=-200, V(2)J=0(s1/2)=-190; V(1)J=0(d3/2)=-100, V(2)J=0(d3/2)=170

## Context

This work extends previous studies of isospin-symmetry breaking using INC interactions in the f7/2 shell by Brown (2002) and Kaneko (2013), and addresses anomalies in superallowed Fermi beta-decay reported by Melconian (2011) for 32Cl and Bennett (2016) for 31Cl. The finding that T=1,J=0 INC forces cannot explain the superallowed decay data, but that additional T=1,J=2 interactions related to the s1/2 orbit can, builds on the suggestion by Bentley (2015) that J-dependent INC interactions are needed for mirror energy differences. The results enable improved calculations of isospin-breaking corrections for superallowed Fermi decays, which are crucial inputs for testing the CKM matrix element Vud, and provide predictions for 122 one- and two-proton separation energies that locate the proton drip-line and suggest candidates for proton emitters in the sd-shell.
