# Reliability of the Optimized Perturbation Theory for scalar fields at finite temperature

> The thermodynamics of a massless scalar field with a quartic interaction is studied up to third order in the Optimized Perturbation Theory (OPT) method. A comparison with other nonperturbative approaches is performed such that the reliability of OPT is accessed.

## Key claim

The Optimized Perturbation Theory (OPT) applied to a massless scalar field with quartic interaction yields a faster convergence for the normalized pressure than the Screened Perturbation Theory (SPT) when both are truncated at comparable orders, but this conclusion is conditional on the use of the Principle of Minimal Sensitivity (PMS) as the variational criterion rather than the tadpole gap equation used in SPT.

## Method

The authors studied the thermodynamics of a massless scalar field with a \(g\phi^4\) interaction by computing the free energy up to third order (\(\mathcal{O}(\delta^3)\)) in the OPT formalism, using a linear interpolation that introduces a variational mass parameter \(\eta\). The optimum value of \(\eta\) was determined by applying the Principle of Minimal Sensitivity (PMS) to the free energy, and the resulting pressure was compared with results from the SPT approach up to four loops, where the mass parameter was fixed via the tadpole gap equation.

## Key numbers

- The normalized pressure \(P/P_{\text{ideal}}\) was computed for OPT at orders \(\mathcal{O}(\delta^2)\) and \(\mathcal{O}(\delta^3)\) and compared with SPT results; the percentage difference between the two OPT orders is shown in Fig. 1 (right panel) and indicates faster convergence for OPT than for SPT.
- The ideal gas pressure used for normalization is \(P_{\text{ideal}} = \pi^2 T^4 / 90\).
- The SPT calculation was truncated at order \(g^7\) and required evaluation of a three-loop diagram using techniques from Arnold (1997).

## Context

This work extends the nonperturbative treatment of finite-temperature scalar field theory by applying OPT up to third order, building on prior OPT studies of similar models (Farias, Ramos, and collaborators, 2011) and on the SPT calculations up to four loops by Andersen and Kyllingstad (2009). The results suggest that OPT with the PMS criterion provides a more reliable and faster-converging alternative to SPT for computing thermodynamic quantities, enabling future applications to more realistic models such as the linear sigma model or QCD effective theories.
