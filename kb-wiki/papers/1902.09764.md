# Astrophysical $S$-factor of the direct $α(d,γ)^6$Li capture reaction in a three-body model

> At the long-wavelength approximation, electric dipole transitions are forbidden between isospin-zero states. In an $α+n+p$ model with $T = 1$ contributions, the $α(d,γ)^6$Li astrophysical $S$-factor is in agreement with the experimental data of the LUNA collaboration, without adjustable parameter. The exact-masses prescription used to avoid the disappearance of $E1$ transitions in potential models is not founded at the microscopic level.

# Astrophysical \(S\)-factor of the direct \(\alpha(d,\gamma)^6\)Li capture reaction in a three-body model

## Key claim

The \(\alpha(d,\gamma)^6\)Li astrophysical \(S\)-factor can be reproduced in agreement with LUNA experimental data using an \(\alpha+n+p\) three-body model that includes small \(T=1\) components in the final bound state, without any adjustable parameter. The commonly used "exact-masses" prescription for generating non-zero \(E1\) transitions in potential models is not microscopically founded and should not be trusted for reactions between \(N=Z\) nuclei.

## Method

The authors construct a three-body \(\alpha+n+p\) model where the final \(J_f=1^+\) bound state of \(^6\)Li is described in hyperspherical coordinates and the initial scattering states in Jacobi coordinates, with the deuteron treated as a frozen \(l_i=0\), \(S_i=1\) cluster. The isovector part of the effective three-body \(E1\) operator at the long-wavelength approximation is proportional to the Jacobi coordinate between the neutron and proton, and the \(E2\) matrix elements are corrected by matching overlap integrals at 7.75 fm to exact Whittaker asymptotic functions with realistic asymptotic normalization coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The final \(J_f=1^+\) bound state of \(^6\)Li contains a small \(T_f=1\) component of about 0.5%.
- The \(E2\) overlap integrals are corrected by matching at 7.75 fm to the exact Whittaker asymptotic form.
- The isovector \(E1\) contribution dominates the total \(S\)-factor below about 0.1 MeV.
- The isoscalar \(E1\) contribution beyond the long-wavelength approximation is negligible for this reaction.

## Context

This work extends the \(\alpha+n+p\) three-body model of Tursunov et al. (2016) by including proper treatment of isospin-forbidden \(E1\) transitions through small \(T=1\) admixtures, and directly addresses the recent LUNA experimental data (Anders et al., 2014; Trezzi et al., 2017). The authors argue that the "exact-masses" prescription used in potential models (e.g., Tursunov et al., 2016) is unfounded because it would incorrectly predict vanishing \(E1\) transitions for the \(d(d,\gamma)^4\)He reaction and introduces unphysical sensitivity to the long \(T=0\) tail of the \(^6\)Li wave function. The work enables future development of three-body models with \(T=1\) admixtures in both initial and final states, and points toward the need for microscopic six-body and _ab initio_ calculations for deeper understanding.
