# Production and evolution path of dileptons at HADES energies

> Dilepton production in intermediate energy nucleus-nucleus collisions as well as in elementary proton-proton reactions is analysed within the UrQMD transport model. For C+C collisions at 1 AGeV and 2 AGeV the resulting invariant mass spectra are compared to recent HADES data. We find that the experimental spectrum for C+C at 2 AGeV is slightly overestimated by the theoretical calculations in the region around the vector meson peak, but fairly described in the low mass region, where the data is satisfactorily saturated by the Dalitz decay of the $η$ meson and the $Δ$ resonance. At 1 AGeV an underestimation of the experimental data is found, pointing that at lower energies the low mass region is not fully saturated by standardly parametrized $Δ$ Dalitz decays alone. Furthermore, predictions for dilepton spectra for $pp$ reactions at 1.25 GeV, 2.2 GeV and 3.5 GeV and Ar+KCl reactions at 1.75 AGeV are presented. The study is complemented by a detailed investigation of the role of absorption of the parent particles on the corresponding dilepton yields in the regime which has so far been probed by HADES.

## Key claim

The UrQMD transport model slightly overestimates the HADES data for C+C collisions at 2 AGeV in the vector meson peak region, while at 1 AGeV the model underestimates the data, indicating that standardly parametrized Delta Dalitz decays alone do not fully saturate the low mass region at lower energies.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD (Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics) non-equilibrium transport model to simulate dilepton production from mesonic Dalitz decays (pi0, eta, eta', omega), direct decays of vector mesons (rho, omega, phi), and Delta resonance Dalitz decays. For C+C collisions at 1 AGeV and 2 AGeV, the calculated invariant mass spectra were compared to HADES data using the HADES detector filter function. The "shining" method was employed for short-lived vector mesons and Delta resonances, integrating the dilepton emission rate over the full lifetime of each parent particle.

## Key numbers

* C+C collisions at 2 AGeV: the theoretical calculation slightly overestimates the experimental spectrum around the vector meson peak, but fairly describes the low mass region where data is saturated by eta meson and Delta resonance Dalitz decays.
* C+C collisions at 1 AGeV: the model underestimates the experimental data, showing that standardly parametrized Delta Dalitz decays alone do not fully saturate the low mass region.
* The eta production cross section in pn reactions is about a factor five higher than in pp reactions, based on data from Calen (1998).
* The eta multiplicity from UrQMD for C+C reactions is consistent with the value measured by the TAPS Collaboration (Averbeck, 1997).
* Predictions are presented for pp reactions at 1.25 GeV, 2.2 GeV, and 3.5 GeV, and for Ar+KCl reactions at 1.75 AGeV.
* For pp reactions at 1.04-2.09 GeV compared to DLS data (Wilson, 1998), agreement is reasonable for M less than or equal to 0.45 GeV, with systematic overestimation at higher masses.
* At 4.88 GeV pp reactions, the model underestimates data in the low invariant mass region but describes the vector meson region well.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical analysis of dilepton production at SIS energies using the UrQMD model, following earlier studies by the DLS Collaboration (Porter, 1997) which observed an enhancement at lower invariant masses in nucleus-nucleus collisions at 1 AGeV that could not be explained by in-medium scenarios that had successfully described ultra-relativistic data. The paper provides predictions for the HADES experimental program at GSI, building on prior transport calculations by Bratkovskaya and Cassing (2008) and Cozma et al. (2006), and enables future comparisons between the broadening versus dropping mass scenarios for vector mesons at intermediate energies.
