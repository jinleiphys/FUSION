# The impact of energy conservation in transport models on the $π^-/π^+$ multiplicity ratio in heavy-ion collisions and the symmetry energy

> The charged pion multiplicity ratio in intermediate energy central heavy-ion collisions has been proposed as a suitable observable to constrain the high density dependence of the isovector part of the equation of state, with contradicting results. Using an upgraded version of the Tübingen QMD transport model, which allows the conservation of energy at a local or global level by accounting for the potential energy of hadrons in two-body collisions and leading thus to particle production threshold shifts, we demonstrate that compatible constraints for the symmetry energy stiffness can be extracted from pion multiplicity and elliptic flow observables. Nevertheless, pion multiplicities are proven to be highly sensitive to the yet unknown isovector part of the in-medium $Δ$(1232) potential which hinders presently the extraction of meaningful information on the high density dependence of the symmetry energy. A solution to this problem together with the inclusion of contributions presently neglected, such as in-medium pion potentials and retardation effects, are needed for a final verdict on this topic.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that when energy conservation is properly enforced in transport models (via global energy conservation, GEC), compatible constraints for the symmetry energy stiffness can be extracted from both pion multiplicity ratio and elliptic flow observables, contradicting previous contradictory results. However, the charged pion multiplicity ratio remains highly sensitive to the unknown isovector part of the in-medium Delta(1232) potential, which currently prevents extraction of meaningful information on the high-density dependence of the symmetry energy.

## Method

The author upgraded the Tubingen QMD transport model to enforce energy conservation at either a local (LEC) or global (GEC) level by accounting for the potential energy of hadrons in two-body collisions, leading to particle production threshold shifts. Simulations of central Au+Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon were performed using a Gogny-inspired momentum-dependent parametrization of the isovector equation of state, with the stiffness parameter x varied to adjust the symmetry energy density dependence. The model incorporated medium-modified elastic and inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross-sections via effective mass scaling, and compared results across three energy conservation scenarios: vacuum energy conservation (VEC), local energy conservation (LEC), and global energy conservation (GEC).

## Key numbers

- The energy conservation violation in VEC and LEC scenarios amounts to about 3 GeV excess kinetic energy per event, corresponding to about 7.5 MeV of extra kinetic energy per nucleon.
- For the standard soft compressibility modulus K=210 MeV, experimental pion multiplicities are overestimated by about 20% for pi- and 40% for pi+ in Au+Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon.
- The best description of FOPI data is achieved with compressibility modulus K=245 MeV coupled with medium-modified cross-sections.
- The ratio PMR(x=-2)/PMR(x=2) increases by a factor of 2 between T_lab=400 MeV/nucleon and T_lab=200 MeV/nucleon.
- Threshold shifts for the GEC scenario average about 30 MeV for the optical potential contribution, compared to about 15 MeV for the LEC scenario.
- The slope parameter L values for the Gogny parametrization range from L=152 MeV (x=-2, stiff) to L=-31 MeV (x=2, soft), with S_0 fixed at 30.6 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies that used the charged pion multiplicity ratio to constrain the symmetry energy, which had produced contradictory results: Xiao et al. (2009) using IBUU favored a soft asy-EoS, Feng and Jin (2009) using IQMD favored a stiff asy-EoS, and Xie et al. (2013) using a Boltzmann-Langevin approach supported a super-soft scenario. The paper also builds on earlier elliptic flow analyses by Russotto et al. (2011) and Wang et al. (2014) using UrQMD, and Cozma (2013) using TuQMD, which extracted L values of 83+/-52 MeV, 89+/-45 MeV, and 123+33-52 MeV respectively. The results enable future experimental measurements by the ASYEOS and SAMURAI TPC collaborations to be interpreted with a consistent theoretical framework, though the unknown isovector Delta(1232) potential and neglected effects such as in-medium pion potentials and retardation effects must be resolved before a final verdict on the symmetry energy at suprasaturation densities can be reached.
