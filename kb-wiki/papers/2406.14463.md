# Hydrodynamic-like behaviour of glasma

> At the earliest stage of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions the produced matter is a highly populated system of gluons called glasma which can be approximately described in terms of classical chromodynamic fields. Although the system's dynamics is governed by Yang-Mills equations, glasma evolution is shown to strongly resemble hydrodynamic behaviour.

# Hydrodynamic-like behaviour of glasma

## Key claim

Glasma evolution, despite being governed by classical Yang-Mills equations and far from equilibrium, strongly resembles hydrodynamic behaviour at very early times after a heavy-ion collision, provided the system is boost-invariant and the energy-momentum tensor is initially diagonal and traceless. The resemblance is exact order-by-order in a proper-time expansion at mid-rapidity, not merely approximate.

## Method

The authors use a "near field expansion" (proper-time expansion) of chromodynamic potentials in powers of the proper time τ, solving the sourceless Yang-Mills equations iteratively for the post-collision glasma fields. The pre-collision potentials are expressed through colour charge distributions of the incoming nuclei, averaged over a Gaussian distribution using the Glasma Graph approximation. The energy-momentum tensor is computed for Pb-Pb collisions at eighth order in the proper-time expansion, with SU(3) gauge group, coupling g=1, saturation scale Q_s=2 GeV, and infrared cutoff m=0.2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The measure Δ = (|L|-|R|)/(|L|+|R|) comparing the left and right sides of the universal flow equation T^{tx} = -(1/2) t ∂T^{xx}/∂x is 0.184 at t=0.06 fm, η=0, and impact parameter b=2 fm.
- At b=0, η=0, t=0.06 fm: the average measure ⟨σ^{μν}⟩ over all tensor components is 1.51 for ideal hydrodynamics and 0.304 for anisotropic hydrodynamics.
- At b=2 fm, η=0, t=0.06 fm: ⟨σ^{μν}⟩ = 1.86 for ideal hydrodynamics and 0.404 for anisotropic hydrodynamics.
- At b=2 fm, η=0.5, t=0.06 fm: ⟨σ^{μν}⟩ = 0.519 for ideal hydrodynamics and 0.473 for anisotropic hydrodynamics.
- Using the alternative measure ⟨σ̃^{μν}⟩ (weighted by T^{tt}), anisotropic hydrodynamics gives 0.0042 versus ideal hydrodynamics giving 0.0436 at b=0, η=0, t=0.06 fm, an improvement of about a factor of 10.
- The saturation scale used is Q_s = 2 GeV and the infrared cutoff is m = 0.2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the findings of Vredevoogd (2008) who derived an equation for transverse flow under similar assumptions, and builds on the authors' own prior studies (Carrington 2021, 2023, 2024) where approximate eccentricity scaling of glasma elliptic flow was found. The paper clarifies that the universal flow equation T^{tx} ≈ -(1/2) t ∂T^{tt}/∂x is satisfied exactly order-by-order at mid-rapidity, not just approximately. This enables a quantitative assessment of how well glasma can be modelled by ideal versus anisotropic hydrodynamics, showing that anisotropic hydrodynamics (Florkowski 2010, Martinez 2010) provides a significantly better description, especially at early times and mid-rapidity. The work also demonstrates that glasma becomes more fluid-like as time advances, consistent with its evolution toward thermodynamic equilibrium.
