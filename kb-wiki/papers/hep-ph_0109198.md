# The role of jet quenching in the p-bar >= pi- anomaly at RHIC

> Preliminary PHENIX data on $Au+Au$ reactions at $\sqrt{s}= 130$ AGeV suggest that $\bar{p}$ yields may exceed $π^-$ at high $p_{\rm T} > 2$ GeV/c. We propose that jet quenching in central collisions suppresses the hard PQCD component of the spectra in central $A+A$ reactions, thereby exposing a novel non-perturbative component of baryon dynamics. We suggest that baryon junctions provide a possible explanation of the anomalous component. We predict that the $\bar{p} \geq π^-$ anomaly is limited to a finite $p_{\rm T}$ window and decreases with increasing impact parameter.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the observed excess of antiprotons over pions at high transverse momentum (p_T > 2 GeV/c) in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC arises from jet quenching suppressing the perturbative QCD component of pion spectra, thereby exposing a novel non-perturbative baryon component; the anomaly is predicted to be limited to a finite p_T window of 2-5 GeV and to decrease with increasing impact parameter.

## Method

The authors extend a two-component hybrid model that combines a perturbative QCD (PQCD) parton fragmentation calculation with a soft non-perturbative component parameterized by mean inverse slopes T_0 for hadrons. Jet quenching is computed using the non-abelian energy loss formalism of Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV), which modifies the fragmentation functions via medium-induced gluon radiation. The baryon junction picture is invoked to motivate a relation between the mean inverse slopes of pions and protons, T_p ≈ √3 T_π, and the soft component parameters are fixed using existing pp and AA data on particle ratios and baryon transport.

## Key numbers

- The high p_T spectra of π^0 in the 10% most central Au+Au collisions at √s = 130 AGeV are suppressed by a factor of 3-4 relative to PQCD predictions scaled by nuclear geometry.
- The midrapidity antiproton-to-proton ratio in central collisions is approximately 0.65, and the net baryon number dN^B/dy ≈ 15.
- The mean inverse slope of pions is taken as T_0^π ≈ 210-220 MeV, with the proton slope related by T_0^p ≈ √3 T_0^π.
- The initial gluon rapidity density in central collisions is estimated as dN^g/dy ≈ 500-1000 from comparison of quenching factors to PHENIX data.
- The anomalous baryon/meson ratio exceeds unity in the p_T = 2-5 GeV window for central collisions, with the maximum baryon excess R_B max subject to 30-50% uncertainties from the mean inverse slopes and degree of quenching.
- Boosted thermal source calculations with transverse velocity v_T = tanh η_r ≈ 0.6 and freeze-out temperature T_f ≈ 160 MeV produce a monotonic R_B that saturates at 2 from spin counting.

## Context

This work extends the hybrid model of Gyulassy, Vitew, and Wang (2001) by incorporating baryon junction dynamics from the non-perturbative baryon transport mechanism of Kharzeev (1996) to explain the anomalous baryon/meson ratio observed in preliminary PHENIX data at RHIC. The paper predicts that the anomaly disappears at high p_T > 5-6 GeV where the ratio returns to PQCD-dominated values, and that the effect vanishes in peripheral collisions where jet quenching is absent, with preliminary PHENIX data supporting the suggested centrality dependence.
