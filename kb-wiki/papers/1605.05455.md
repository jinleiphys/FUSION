# Comment on "Solution of the $α$-Potential Mystery in the $γ$ Process and Its Impact on the Nd/Sm Ratio in Meteorites"

> A competition of the low-energy Coulomb excitation (CE) with the compound nucleus (CN) formation in $α$-induced reactions below the Coulomb barrier has been assumed by Rauscher [Phys. Rev. Lett. 111, 061104 (2013)] in order to use the same optical model (OM) potential for description of the latter and the alpha-particle emission as well. In this Comment we show that the corresponding partial waves and integration radii provide evidence for the distinct account of the CE cross section and OM total-reaction cross section.

# Comment on "Solution of the α-Potential Mystery in the γ Process and Its Impact on the Nd/Sm Ratio in Meteorites"

## Key claim
The authors argue that the Coulomb excitation (CE) cross section and the optical model (OM) total-reaction cross section must be treated distinctly, contrary to the assumption by Rauscher (2013) that CE competes with compound nucleus (CN) formation and can be used to justify a single OM potential for both α-induced reactions and α-particle emission. The partial waves and integration radii show that CE involves larger partial waves than those contributing to the OM total-reaction cross section, so only the CE+NE (nuclear excitation) cross sections within a short-range integration radius of ~15 fm should be subtracted from the OM total-reaction cross section.

## Method
The authors performed distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) calculations using the code DWUCK4 for collective inelastic scattering of α-particles on the first 2⁺ excited states of ⁶⁴Zn and ¹⁴⁴Sm, using the McFadden and Satchler optical model potential and deformation parameters β of 0.242 and 0.088, respectively. They compared cross sections for pure Coulomb excitation, pure nuclear excitation, and their coherent interference (NE+CE) using integration radii of up to 15 fm (typical for short-range nuclear interactions) and up to 30-40 fm (for the long-range Coulomb field), and also compared these with semiclassical CE results from the code GOSIA and with OM total-reaction cross sections.

## Key numbers
- For ⁶⁴Zn and ¹⁴⁴Sm, only 18 and 21 partial waves contribute to the pure NE cross sections at α-particle energies of 15 and 16 MeV, respectively.
- At α-particle energies below ~0.7B (where B is the Coulomb barrier), the CE and NE+CE cross sections at the upper Rmax values are higher than the OM total-reaction cross section.
- The larger Rmax values correspond to an increase from ~20 to ~60 partial waves contributing to both CE and NE+CE cross sections at the upper α-particle energies.
- The CE cross sections for the first 2⁺ excited state are several orders of magnitude larger than the σR values given by the OM analysis of elastic scattering data.

## Context
This Comment directly addresses the work of Rauscher (2013), who proposed that CE competes with CN formation in α-induced reactions below the Coulomb barrier to resolve the "α-potential mystery." The authors extend earlier work by Stelson and McGowan (1964) and use updated CE results from the semiclassical code GOSIA and updated B(E2) values from the Evaluated Nuclear Structure Data File (ENSDF). They note that the same CE account was found necessary for ¹⁴⁴Sm(α,γ)¹⁴⁸Gd and reactions on ¹⁴¹Pr and ¹⁶⁹Tm, but not for ¹³⁰,¹³²Ba and ¹⁶⁸Yb, and that subsequent work by the authors (2015) showed CE cross sections for those latter targets were not small compared to CN formation cross sections. The question of the α-potential mystery remains open for further consideration in both nuclear astrophysics and nuclear technology.
