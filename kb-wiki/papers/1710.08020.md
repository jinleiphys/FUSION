# Enhancements of high order cumulants across the 1st order phase transition boundary

> In this proceeding, we investigate the dynamical evolution of the $σ$ field with a trajectory across the 1st order phase transition boundary, using Langevin dynamics from the linear sigma model. We find the high order cumulants of the $σ$ field are largely enhanced during the dynamical evolution, compared with the equilibrium values, due to the supercooling effect of the first order phase transition.

## Key claim

High-order cumulants (C₃ and C₄) of the σ field are largely enhanced during dynamical evolution across a first-order phase transition boundary compared to equilibrium values, specifically due to the supercooling effect that causes the field to distribute in both minima of the effective potential.

## Method

The authors solved the Langevin equation for the σ field within the linear sigma model with constituent quarks, using event-by-event simulations with initial conditions sampled from the equilibrium probability distribution. The system was evolved along a trajectory at fixed chemical potential μ = 240 MeV with a Hubble-like temperature decrease T(t)/T₀ = (t/t₀)^(-0.45), and cumulants were computed from the spatial configurations of the σ field at each time step.

## Key numbers

- Critical point located at (μ_c, T_c) = (205, 100.2) MeV in the linear sigma model phase diagram
- Vacuum expectation value of σ field: ⟨σ⟩ = f_π = 93 MeV
- Pion mass: m_π = 138 MeV; sigma mass: m_σ ≈ 600 MeV (with λ² = 20)
- Current quark mass: m₀ = 5.5 MeV; quark-sigma coupling: g = 3.3
- Trajectory studied at fixed chemical potential μ = 240 MeV, crossing the first-order phase transition boundary
- Temperature evolution follows T(t)/T₀ = (t/t₀)^(-0.45)

## Context

This work extends dynamical critical fluctuation studies based on the Fokker-Planck equation and Langevin dynamics by Mukherjee (2015) and Jiang (2017), which showed that critical slowing-down effects can cause signs of high-order dynamical cumulants to differ from equilibrium values. The present study focuses specifically on the first-order phase transition scenario (rather than the critical point) and demonstrates that supercooling produces enhanced fluctuations beyond equilibrium predictions, which is relevant for interpreting net-proton cumulant measurements from the STAR Beam Energy Scan program at collision energies below 40 GeV.
