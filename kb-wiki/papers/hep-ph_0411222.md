# N K Pi molecular state with I=1 and J(Pi)=3/2-

> The structure of the molecule-like state of $NKπ$ with spin-parity $J^π={3/2}^-$ and isospin I=1 is studied within the chiral SU(3) quark model. First we calculate the $NK$, $Nπ$, and $Kπ$ phase shifts in the framework of the resonating group method (RGM), and a qualitative agreement with the experimental data is obtained. Then we perform a rough estimation for the energy of $(NKπ)_{J^π={3/2}^-,I=1}$, and the effect of the mixing to the configuration $(ΔK)_{J^π={3/2}^-,I=1}$ is also considered. The calculated energy is very close to the threshold of the $NKπ$ system. A detailed investigation is worth doing in the further study.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that a three-body molecule-like state of $NK\pi$ with $J^{\pi}=3/2^{-}$ and $I=1$ could exist as a bound state very near its threshold, but the authors emphasize that their calculation is only a rough estimate and does not yet produce a bound state; further refinements in the interaction and method could lead to such a state.

## Method

The authors used the chiral SU(3) quark model to calculate the $NK$, $N\pi$, and $K\pi$ interactions by fitting their low-energy phase shifts via the resonating group method (RGM). They then constructed a harmonic oscillator wave function for the $(NK\pi)_{J^{\pi}=3/2^{-},I=1}$ system and estimated its energy by summing the kinetic energy ($2\omega$) and the pairwise interaction energies weighted by the number of pairs in each channel. Finally, they considered configuration mixing with the $(\Delta K)_{J^{\pi}=3/2^{-},I=1}$ state by computing the $\Delta N\pi$ vertex matrix element.

## Key numbers

- The calculated energy of $(NK\pi)_{J^{\pi}=3/2^{-},I=1}$ is about 70–80 MeV higher than the $NK\pi$ threshold (1572 MeV) when using a harmonic oscillator frequency $\omega$ of several tens MeV.
- After mixing with the $(\Delta K)_{J^{\pi}=3/2^{-},I=1}$ configuration, the energy is reduced by 10–20 MeV, bringing it closer to the $NK\pi$ threshold.
- The $\Delta N\pi$ coupling constant used is $f^{2}_{\Delta N\pi} = 0.29$, and the size parameter $b$ is chosen around 0.8–1.0 fm, yielding a mixing matrix element of approximately 30–50 MeV.
- In the $(NK\pi)_{J^{\pi}=3/2^{-},I=1}$ system, there are: 1 pair of $(N\pi)_{l=1,j=3/2,t=3/2}$ (attractive), 0.28 pairs of $(K\pi)_{l=0,j=0,t=1/2}$ (attractive), 0.60 pairs of $(K\pi)_{l=1,j=1,t=1/2}$ (attractive), 0.64 pairs of $(NK)_{l=0,j=1/2,t=0}$ (repulsive), and 0.32 pairs of $(NK)_{l=0,j=1/2,t=1}$ (repulsive).

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) quark model, previously applied to baryon-baryon and baryon-meson scattering (Huang 2004, Huang 2005), to a three-body $NK\pi$ system. It contrasts with the pentaquark interpretation of the $\Theta^{+}$ resonance (Diakonov 1997, Stancu 2003) and with the $NK\pi$ state with $J^{\pi}=1/2^{+}$ and $I=0$ studied by Bicudo (2004). The authors suggest that if the $\Theta^{++}$ observed by the RHIC-STAR group exists, it could be explained as this $NK\pi$ molecular state, and they call for more accurate dynamical calculations to confirm whether a bound state actually forms.
