# Nuclear $β$-decay half-lives in the relativistic point-coupling model

> The self-consistent proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation approach is employed to calculate $β$-decay half-lives of neutron-rich even-even nuclei with $8\leqslant Z \leqslant 30$. A newly proposed nonlinear point-coupling effective interaction PC-PK1 is used in the calculations. It is found that the isoscalar proton-neutron pairing interaction can significantly reduce $β$-decay half-lives. With an isospin-dependent isoscalar proton-neutron pairing strength, our results well reproduce the experimental $β$-decay half-lives, although the pairing strength is not adjusted using the half-lives calculated in this study.

## Key claim

The self-consistent proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) with the nonlinear point-coupling effective interaction PC-PK1 reproduces experimental beta-decay half-lives of neutron-rich even-even nuclei with \(8 \leq Z \leq 30\) when an isospin-dependent isoscalar (\(T=0\)) proton-neutron pairing strength is used, even though that pairing strength was not adjusted using the half-lives calculated in this study.

## Method

The authors employed the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model combined with the proton-neutron QRPA in the canonical single-nucleon basis, using the nonlinear point-coupling effective interaction PC-PK1. The particle-hole residual interaction included isovector-vector, direct one-pion, and zero-range counter terms, while the particle-particle channel included isovector (\(T=1\)) pairing from the Gogny D1S force and an isospin-dependent isoscalar (\(T=0\)) pairing interaction with a Gaussian form. Beta-decay half-lives were calculated in the allowed Gamow-Teller approximation using the QRPA transition strengths and integrated phase volumes.

## Key numbers

- The isospin-dependent \(T=0\) pairing strength parameter \(V_0\) was set to 175.0 MeV (replacing the earlier value of 134.0 MeV from Niu (2013)), with other parameters \(V_D = 121.1\) MeV, \(a = 8.5\), and \(b = -0.4\) taken directly from Niu (2013) without further adjustment.
- For \(^{72}\)Fe, the dominant transition energy shifted from \(E = -4.95\) MeV (without \(T=0\) pairing) to \(E = -7.93\) MeV (with \(T=0\) pairing), and the transition strength increased, reducing the calculated half-life.
- The constant \(D = 6163.4 \pm 3.8\) s was used in the half-life formula, with \(g_A = 1\).
- The calculation covered neutron-rich even-even nuclei with atomic numbers \(8 \leq Z \leq 30\).

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent relativistic QRPA approach for beta-decay half-lives from the meson-exchange model (Niksic 2005, Marketin 2007) and from earlier point-coupling calculations for neutron-deficient nuclei (Niu 2013) to neutron-rich nuclei using the PC-PK1 interaction. It contrasts with the FRDM+QRPA approach (Moller 1997), which systematically overestimates half-lives in this region, and with the D\(^3\)C* interaction (Marketin 2007), which required isotope-specific fitting of the \(T=0\) pairing strength. The results enable systematic predictions for r-process nucleosynthesis and highlight the need for including deformation (for S isotopes) and beyond-mean-field effects such as tensor forces or particle-vibration coupling (for Ni and Zn isotopes) to resolve remaining discrepancies.
