# Study on initial geometry fluctuations via participant plane correlations in heavy ion collisions: part II

> Further investigation of the participant plane correlations within a Glauber model framework is presented, focusing on correlations between three or four participant planes of different order. A strong correlation is observed for $\cos(2Φ_{2}^*+3Φ_{3}^*-5Φ_{5}^*)$ which is a reflection of the elliptic shape of the overlap region. The correlation between the corresponding experimental reaction plane angles can be easily measured. Strong correlations of similar geometric origin are also observed for $\cos(2Φ_{2}^*+4Φ_{4}^*-6Φ_{6}^*)$, $\cos(2Φ_2^*-3Φ_3^*-4Φ_4^*+5Φ_5^*)$, $\cos(6Φ_2^*+3Φ_3^*-4Φ_4^*-5Φ_5^*)$, $\cos(Φ_1^*-2Φ_2^*-3Φ_3^*+4Φ_4^*)$, $\cos(Φ_1^*+6Φ_2^*-3Φ_3^*-4Φ_4^*)$, and $\cos(Φ_1^*+2Φ_2^*+3Φ_3^*-6Φ_6^*)$, which are also measurable. Experimental measurements of the corresponding reaction plane correlators in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC may improve our understanding of the physics underlying the measured higher order flow harmonics.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that strong correlations between three or four participant planes of different order exist in the initial geometry of heavy ion collisions, and these correlations are expected to survive in the final-state momentum-space reaction plane measurements, particularly in central collisions where non-linear hydrodynamic mixing is small.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo Glauber simulations of Au+Au collisions with a nucleon-nucleon cross-section of 42 mb, defining participant plane angles through the distribution of participants and binary collisions weighted by a factor of 0.14 for binary collisions and 0.43 for participants. They also compared with Color Glass Condensate simulations using both r^2 and r^n weighting schemes. The correlations were analyzed via Fourier expansion of the event distribution in two-dimensional angular difference space, and the centrality dependence of various three-plane and four-plane correlators was computed.

## Key numbers

- The resolution parameter χ_n for event planes measured in 3<|η|<5 in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC decreases from about 2 for n=2 to about 0.08 for n=6, and is negligible for n≥7
- The weighting factors used in the Glauber model are δ=0.14 for binary collisions and (1-δ)/2=0.43 for participants
- The correlator ⟨cos(2Φ₂*+3Φ₃*-5Φ₅*)⟩ and ⟨cos(2Φ₂*+4Φ₄*-6Φ₆*)⟩ show signals even larger than ⟨cos(Φ₁*+2Φ₂*-3Φ₃*)⟩ and comparable to ⟨cos4(Φ₂*-Φ₄*)⟩
- The values of ⟨cos(2Φ₂*+3Φ₃*-5Φ₅*)⟩ and ⟨cos(2Φ₂*+4Φ₄*-6Φ₆*)⟩ reach a constant at 10% for Npart>300 in the Glauber model

## Context

This work extends the participant plane correlation method proposed in Jia (2012) to three-plane and four-plane correlators not covered in the previous literature, building on analytical work by Bhalerao (2011) and numerical studies by Teaney (2010), Qin (2011), and Staig (2010). The correlations are related to the elliptic shape of the overlap region and can be understood via an independent cluster model. Experimental measurements at RHIC and the LHC of the corresponding reaction plane correlators may help disentangle the relative role of linear and non-linear hydrodynamic response, as discussed by Gardim (2011) and Qiu (2011).
