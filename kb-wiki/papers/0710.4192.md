# Nuclear Matter and Neutron stars in a Parity Doublet Model

> We investigate the properties of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter and neutron stars in a chiral model approach adopting the SU(2) parity doublet formulation. This ansatz explicitly incorporates chiral symmetry restoration with the limit of degenerate masses of the nucleons and their parity partners. Instead of searching for an optimized parameter set we explore the general parameter dependence of nuclear matter and star properties in the model. We are able to get a good description of ground state nuclear matter as well as large values of mass for neutron stars in agreement with observation.

# Nuclear Matter and Neutron stars in a Parity Doublet Model

## Key claim

The parity doublet model can simultaneously reproduce ground-state nuclear matter saturation properties and neutron star maximum masses around 1.9 solar masses, but only when the bare mass parameter m0 is restricted to a narrow range between approximately 870 and 900 MeV, and when the chiral partner mass is kept high (1535 MeV) with no vector meson self-interactions.

## Method

The authors apply the SU(2) parity doublet model in mean-field approximation to isospin-symmetric nuclear matter and to charge-neutral neutron star matter including leptons. They perform a parameter scan over the bare mass m0, the nucleon-vector coupling gNω, and the sigma meson mass mσ, while fixing the chiral partner mass MN− to 1535 MeV and setting the quartic vector self-coupling g4 to zero. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations are solved to obtain neutron star masses and radii.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: ρ0 = 0.15 ± 0.015 fm−3
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: E/A − MN = −16.0 ± 0.5 MeV
- Nuclear matter compressibility range considered acceptable: 100 MeV < K < 400 MeV
- Bare mass parameter m0 restricted to values between approximately 870 and 900 MeV when combining compressibility and neutron star mass constraints
- Maximum neutron star mass for MN− = 1535 MeV: Mmax = 1.87 M⊙
- Maximum neutron star mass for MN− = 1379 MeV: Mmax = 1.77 M⊙
- Asymmetry energy at saturation fixed to: Esym = 32.5 MeV
- Pion mass: mπ = 138 MeV; pion decay constant: fπ = 93 MeV; omega meson vacuum mass: mω = 783 MeV; rho meson vacuum mass: mρ = 761 MeV

## Context

This work extends the parity doublet model study of ZParity (2006) by allowing a broader range of saturation properties rather than fine-tuning to a single set, and by including neutron star constraints. The authors find a larger allowed parameter space for nuclear matter than ZParity (2006), but show that requiring both reasonable compressibility and neutron star masses above 1.4 M⊙ narrows m0 to a small window. The approach contrasts with standard linear sigma models where nucleon mass is almost entirely generated by dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, whereas here most mass comes from the parity-mixing term m0. This enables future extension to SU(3) flavor, which the authors note is in progress.
