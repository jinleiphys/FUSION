# Compton scattering from the proton in an effective field theory with explicit Delta degrees of freedom

> We analyse the proton Compton-scattering differential cross section for photon energies up to 325 MeV using Chiral Effective Field Theory and extract new values for the electric and magnetic polarisabilities of the proton. Our EFT treatment builds in the key physics in two different regimes: photon energies around the pion mass ("low energy") and the higher energies where the Delta(1232) resonance plays a key role. The Compton amplitude is complete at N4L0, O(e^2 delta^4), in the low-energy region, and at NLO, O(e^2 delta^0), in the resonance region. Throughout, the Delta-pole graphs are dressed with pi-N loops and gamma-N-Delta vertex corrections. A statistically consistent database of proton Compton experiments is used to constrain the free parameters in our amplitude: the M1 gamma-N-Delta transition strength b_1 (which is fixed in the resonance region) and the polarisabilities alpha and beta (which are fixed from data below 170 MeV). In order to obtain a reasonable fit we find it necessary to add the spin polarisability gammaM1 as a free parameter, even though it is, strictly speaking, predicted in chiral EFT at the order to which we work. We show that the fit is consistent with the Baldin sum rule, and then use that sum rule to constrain alpha+beta. In this way we obtain alpha=[10.65+/-0.35(stat})+/-0.2(Baldin)+/-0.3(theory)]10^{-4} fm^3, and beta =[3.15-/+0.35(stat)-/+0.2(Baldin)-/+0.3(theory)]10^{-4} fm^3, with chi^2 = 113.2 for 135 degrees of freedom. A detailed rationale for the theoretical uncertainties assigned to this result is provided.

## Key claim

The paper extracts the electric and magnetic polarisabilities of the proton from Compton scattering data up to 325 MeV using Chiral Effective Field Theory with explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom, but the fit requires adding the spin polarisability gamma_M1 as a free parameter even though it is predicted at the working order, and the results are constrained by the Baldin sum rule.

## Method

The authors compute the Compton scattering amplitude in two kinematic regimes: low energy (photon energies around the pion mass, up to about 140 MeV) where the amplitude is complete at N4LO (order e^2 delta^4), and the resonance region (photon energies around the Delta-nucleon mass splitting, up to 325 MeV) where the amplitude is complete at NLO (order e^2 delta^0). Delta-pole graphs are dressed with pi-N loops and gamma-N-Delta vertex corrections. A statistically consistent database of proton Compton experiments is used to constrain the M1 gamma-N-Delta transition strength b_1 from resonance-region data and the polarisabilities alpha and beta from data below 170 MeV, with the Baldin sum rule then used to constrain alpha+beta.

## Key numbers

* Electric polarisability of the proton: alpha = [10.65 +/- 0.35(stat) +/- 0.2(Baldin) +/- 0.3(theory)] x 10^{-4} fm^3
* Magnetic polarisability of the proton: beta = [3.15 -/+ 0.35(stat) -/+ 0.2(Baldin) -/+ 0.3(theory)] x 10^{-4} fm^3
* Chi-squared value: chi^2 = 113.2 for 135 degrees of freedom
* Photon energy range of the analysis: up to 325 MeV
* Energy cutoff for polarisability extraction: data below 170 MeV

## Context

This work merges three refinements in Chiral Effective Field Theory: higher orders in the chiral sector, explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom following Pascalutsa and Phillips (2003), and partially-covariant formulations. It extends earlier extractions by Bernard et al. (1991, 1992) who calculated polarisabilities at leading one-loop order without explicit Delta degrees of freedom, and by Beane et al. (2002, 2004) who used higher-order pion-nucleon-loop amplitudes. The results enable comparisons with lattice QCD calculations that are becoming available, and provide input for extracting neutron polarisabilities from Compton scattering on deuterium, as well as for the two-photon-exchange contribution to the Lamb shift in muonic hydrogen.
