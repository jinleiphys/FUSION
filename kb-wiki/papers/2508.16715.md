# Hubble's Law in Heavy Ion Collisions

> The evolution of the "microscopic" Hubble parameter related to the expansion of matter born in heavy-ion collisions was obtained for nucleons and pions. The calculations were carried out within the parton-hadron-string dynamics (PHSD) transport model. Au+Au collisions with $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.8$ GeV at $b = 2.5,\ 5.0,\ 7.5$, and $10.0$ fm were considered. A new method for determining the "microscopic" Hubble parameter from simulated data was used. The ballistic motion was obtained for the longitudinal direction after the separation of the nuclei. In earlier times, the evolution of the "microscopic" Hubble parameter in this direction was more complicated. For transverse directions, an exponential low-time asymptotics of the Hubble parameter was observed. The obtained values of the "microscopic" Hubble parameter are about 40 orders of magnitude higher than the cosmological Hubble constant.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the "microscopic" Hubble parameter in heavy-ion collisions can be extracted from simulated data using a new statistical method based on distributions of velocity-field derivatives, and that its evolution is qualitatively different for longitudinal versus transverse expansion, with transverse expansion exhibiting an exponential "little inflation" phase before the nuclei separate.

## Method

The authors used the parton-hadron-string dynamics (PHSD) transport model to simulate Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.8\) GeV for impact parameters \(b = 2.5, 5.0, 7.5, 10.0\) fm. They applied a "fluidization" procedure that discretizes the fireball into lattice cells and defines a velocity field using the Eckart definition with quadratic spline smearing, averaging over approximately \(10^6\) configurations to suppress statistical fluctuations. The "microscopic" Hubble parameter was then determined by analyzing the statistical distributions of the directional derivatives \(\partial_i v_i\) of the velocity field components, identifying the peak corresponding to Hubble-like motion via Gaussian fitting after excluding cells from nonlinear edge regions.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.8\) GeV with impact parameters \(b = 2.5, 5.0, 7.5, 10.0\) fm
- For longitudinal ballistic motion after nuclei separation: \(H_z = C/(t - t_0)\) with \(C = 1.00(1)\) and \(t_0 = 4.07(4)\) fm/\(c\)
- Before nuclei separation, longitudinal Hubble parameter values in the interval \(0.4 - 1.2\) (fm/\(c\))\(^{-1}\)
- For transverse exponential growth: common power coefficient \(k = 0.824(7)\) (fm/\(c\))\(^{-1}\) in \(H_i(b, t) = C(b) e^{k t}\)
- After nuclei separation, transverse Hubble parameter values in the interval \(0.02 - 0.2\) (fm/\(c\))\(^{-1}\)
- Cosmological Hubble constant \(H_{\text{cosm}} = 70\) (km/s)/Mpc \(\sim 10^{-41}\) (fm/\(c\))\(^{-1}\), about 40 orders of magnitude smaller than the typical values obtained in heavy-ion collisions
- Estimated time moments for Au+Au at this energy: first touch \(t_{\text{FT}} \approx 1.80\) fm/\(c\), maximum overlap \(t_{\text{X}} \approx 3.52\) fm/\(c\), last touch \(t_{\text{LT}} \approx 5.35\) fm/\(c\)

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of Hubble-like expansion in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the studies by Inghirami (2021) and Tsegelnik (2022) which observed deviations from ballistic motion, and the Cracow model by Broniowski (2001) and Florkowski (2004) which by construction contains Hubble-like ballistic motion. The authors compare their results for nucleons with the nonrelativistic hydrodynamic solution for isentropic expansion of a nucleonic sphere from Bondorf (1978). The new method based on statistical distributions of velocity derivatives, introduced by Voronyuk (2024), allows separate determination of the Hubble parameter for different particle species (pions and nucleons) and different spatial directions, enabling future connections to anisotropic flows, polarization phenomena, and possible experimental extraction using blast-wave fits combined with femtoscopy data.
