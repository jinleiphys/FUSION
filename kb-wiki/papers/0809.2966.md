# Nuclear matter and neutron matter for improved quark mass density- dependent model with $ρ$ mesons

> A new improved quark mass density-dependent model including u, d quarks, $σ$ mesons, $ω$ mesons and $ρ$ mesons is presented. Employing this model, the properties of nuclear matter, neutron matter and neutron star are studied. We find that it can describe above properties successfully. The results given by the new improved quark mass density- dependent model and by the quark meson coupling model are compared.

# Nuclear matter and neutron matter for improved quark mass density-dependent model with ρ mesons

## Key claim

The improved quark mass density-dependent (IQMDD) model extended with ρ mesons can describe symmetric nuclear matter, neutron matter, and neutron star properties successfully, but the authors hedge that the neutron star treatment is "too rough" and the maximum mass value "is not important", the main purpose is to show qualitative agreement with the quark meson coupling (QMC) model, suggesting the IQMDD model "perhaps" could replace the QMC model.

## Method

The authors extend their earlier IQMDD model by adding isovector ρ mesons to account for isospin asymmetry, working within the mean field approximation (MFA). They solve the coupled equations for the σ, ω, and ρ mean fields self-consistently with the density-dependent quark masses from the QMDD ansatz, using the Friedberg-Lee soliton bag to describe nucleons. For neutron star calculations, they impose charge neutrality and β-equilibrium conditions and integrate the Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³ with binding energy E/A = -15 MeV at zero temperature
- Compression constant K(ρ₀) = 210 MeV
- Symmetry energy coefficient a_sym = 33.2 MeV
- Coupling constants: g_σ^q = 4.67, g_ω^q = 2.44, g_ρ = 9.07
- Bag constant B = 174 MeV fm⁻³
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation M_N^*(ρ₀) = 775 MeV
- Bag radius R = 0.85 fm
- Maximum neutron star mass M_max = 1.73 M_⊙ (solar masses)
- For comparison, QMC model gives M_max = 2.2 M_⊙

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier IQMDD model (Wu and Su, 2008; Wu, Qian and Su, 2005) by adding ρ mesons to handle asymmetric nuclear matter, building on the original QMDD model of Fowler, Raha and Weiner (1981). The results are compared directly with the QMC model of Guichon (1988) and Saito and Thomas (1994), and with the QHD-II model. The authors find that IQMDD results lie between those of QHD-II and QMC, and suggest the model can overcome two shortcomings of the QMC model: it can describe quark deconfinement phase transitions and allows many-body calculations beyond MFA. The neutron star treatment follows the same simplified approximation as Menezes, Panda and Providencia (2005).
