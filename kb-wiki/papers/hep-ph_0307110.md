# Pseudoscalar qqbar mesons and effective QCD coupling enhanced by <A^2> condensate

> Recent developments provided evidence that the dimension 2 gluon condensate <A^2> is important for the nonperturbative regime of Yang-Mills theories (quantized in the Landau gauge). We show that it may be relevant for the Dyson-Schwinger approach to QCD. In order that this approach leads to a successful hadronic phenomenology, an enhancement of the effective quark-gluon interaction seems to be needed at intermediate (p^2 \sim 0.5 GeV^2) momenta. It is shown that the gluon condensate <A^2> provides such an enhancement. It is also shown that the resulting effective strong running coupling leads to the sufficiently strong dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and successful phenomenology at least in the light sector of pseudoscalar mesons.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the dimension-2 gluon condensate ⟨A²⟩, which is not gauge-invariant but has a gauge-invariant meaning in Landau gauge, can provide the enhancement of the effective quark-gluon coupling at intermediate momenta (p² ∼ 0.5 GeV²) needed for successful Dyson-Schwinger phenomenology of light pseudoscalar mesons, though the authors acknowledge that the proposed form of the running coupling is oversimplified and its behavior at very low p² is an unrealistic artifact.

## Method

The authors incorporate operator product expansion (OPE) results for the gluon and ghost propagators, including contributions from the ⟨A²⟩ condensate and dimension-4 condensates parametrized as C_A and C_G, into the Dyson-Schwinger (DS) and Bethe-Salpeter (BS) equations in the rainbow-ladder approximation (RLA). They solve the gap equation for quark propagators and the BS equation for pseudoscalar meson bound-state vertices, using the proposed effective running coupling α_eff(p²) in the RLA interaction kernel, following the same calculational procedures as in earlier work by Jain and Munczek (1993) and the authors' own previous studies.

## Key numbers

* The lattice value of g²⟨A²⟩ is 2.76 GeV², giving m_A = 0.845 GeV via the relation m_A² = (3/32) g²⟨A²⟩.
* The best-fit parameter set uses C_A = (0.6060 GeV)^4 = C_G and m_A = 0.8402 GeV, only slight modifications from the initial estimates.
* The enhancement of α_eff(p²) is centered around p² ≈ m_A²/2, i.e., approximately 0.35 GeV².
* The perturbative QCD scale is Λ_QCD = 0.228 GeV, with infrared regulator x₀ = 10.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger approach to QCD by proposing that the ⟨A²⟩ condensate, which has been studied in operator product expansion by Lavelle (1991), Kondo (2001), and others, provides the missing enhancement mechanism for the effective quark-gluon interaction. It contrasts with ab initio DS studies by Alkofer (2000) and Fischer and Alkofer (2003), which found insufficient dynamical chiral symmetry breaking from the running coupling alone, and with phenomenological models by Jain and Munczek (1993) and Maris, Roberts, and Tandy (1997, 1999) that required ad hoc bumps in the effective coupling. The results enable further DS studies to incorporate condensate effects systematically, though the authors note that lattice results from Bloch (2002) and Boucaud (2003) disagree on the infrared behavior of the running coupling.
