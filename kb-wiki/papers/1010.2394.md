# Correlation between conserved charges in PNJL Model with multi-quark interactions

> We present a study of correlations among conserved charges like baryon number, electric charge and strangeness in the framework of 2+1 flavor Polyakov loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model at vanishing chemical potentials, up to fourth order. Correlations up to second order have been measured in Lattice QCD which compares well with our estimates given the inherent difference in the pion masses in the two systems. Possible physical implications of these correlations and their importance in understanding the matter obtained in heavy-ion collisions are discussed. We also present comparison of the results with the commonly used unbound effective potential in the quark sector of this model.

## Key claim

The PNJL model with multi-quark interactions reproduces the qualitative features of Lattice QCD correlations among conserved charges at vanishing chemical potential, but quantitative differences persist due to the inherent difference in the pion masses between the two systems; the unbound effective potential (UEP) appears closer to Lattice results than the bound effective potential (BEP).

## Method

The authors compute the thermodynamic potential of the 2+1 flavor Polyakov loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model in the mean-field approximation, including eight-quark interaction terms to produce a bound effective potential. They perform a Taylor expansion of the pressure in powers of baryon, electric charge, and strangeness chemical potentials around zero, extracting the off-diagonal susceptibility coefficients up to fourth order by fitting the pressure as a polynomial. The results are compared with available Lattice QCD data for the leading-order correlations and with the Stefan-Boltzmann limits at high temperature.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass fixed at 138 MeV, kaon mass at 494 MeV, eta mass at 480 MeV, eta-prime mass at 957 MeV, pion decay constant at 93 MeV, kaon decay constant at 117 MeV, and up quark mass at 5.5 MeV.
- For the bound effective potential (BEP): strange quark mass 183.468 MeV, cutoff Lambda = 637.720 MeV, four-quark coupling g_S * Lambda^2 = 2.914, six-quark coupling g_D * Lambda^5 = 75.968, eight-quark couplings g_1 = 2.193e-21 MeV^{-8} and g_2 = -5.890e-22 MeV^{-8}, Polyakov loop parameter kappa = 0.06, crossover temperature T_C = 169 MeV.
- For the unbound effective potential (UEP): strange quark mass 134.758 MeV, cutoff Lambda = 631.357 MeV, four-quark coupling g_S * Lambda^2 = 3.664, six-quark coupling g_D * Lambda^5 = 74.636, eight-quark couplings zero, kappa = 0.13, T_C = 181 MeV.
- The Stefan-Boltzmann limit for the ratio c_{11}^{BS} / c_2^S is -2/3.
- The Polyakov loop potential parameters: a_0 = 6.75, a_1 = -1.95, a_2 = 2.625, a_3 = -7.44, b_3 = 0.75, b_4 = 7.5, T_0 = 190 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of fluctuations and correlations in the 2+1 flavor PNJL model (Fuku 2009, Wu 2009, Deb 2010) by computing off-diagonal susceptibilities up to fourth order, including both bound and unbound effective potentials. The results are compared with Lattice QCD data from Cheng (2008) and others, and with the diagnostic tools proposed by Koch (2005) for identifying quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions. The higher-order correlations with strangeness show two cusps corresponding to chiral crossovers in the light and strange quark sectors, which could serve as indicators of the crossover region in experiments at CERN and Brookhaven.
