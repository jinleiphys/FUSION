# Inverse-scattering separable NN potential constrained to phase-shift data up to 2.5 GeV. I.- Uncoupled states

> We introduce a new method to construct, within inverse-scattering theory, an energy-independent separable potential capable of reproducing exactly both phase shift and absorption over a predefined energy range. The approach relies on the construction of non-overlapping multi-rank separable potentials, whose form factors are obtained by solving linear equations on intervals where the $K$ matrix does have zeros. Applications are made to nucleon-nucleon $NN$ interactions constrained to the SAID-SP07 phase-shift analysis up to 2.5 GeV lab energy. The inversion potentials are channel dependent with rank dictated by the number of zeros of the $K$ matrix, reproducing the data up to a selected upper momentum. The account for absorption yields complex separable form factors, resulting in a non-Hermitian potential. Applications are restricted to $NN$ spin-uncoupled states considering a Schrödinger-like wave equation with minimal relativity. Its extension to spin-coupled states and relativistic kernels are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors introduce a new inverse-scattering method to construct energy-independent separable nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials that exactly reproduce both phase shift and absorption data up to 2.5 GeV lab energy, but only for spin-uncoupled states and using a Schrödinger-like wave equation with minimal relativity, with extension to spin-coupled states and relativistic kernels deferred to future work.

## Method

The method constructs non-overlapping multi-rank separable potentials by solving linear equations for the squared form factors on intervals where the K matrix does not vanish, with the rank determined by the number of zeros of the K matrix. The form factors are obtained by discretizing the principal-value integral equation and performing direct matrix inversion, using a smooth ultraviolet cutoff regulator to suppress high-momentum components. For channels with phase-shift zeros, piecewise solutions are constructed over separate momentum intervals using Heaviside step functions to ensure real form factors when absorption is absent.

## Key numbers

- Phase-shift data constrained to SAID-SP07 analysis up to 2.5 GeV lab energy
- Ultraviolet cutoff values used: k_c = 2.5, 3.5, 4.5, and 5.5 fm^{-1}
- Regulator diffuseness parameter d = 0.05 fm^{-1}
- Pion production threshold at E_Lab ≈ 290 MeV (k ≈ 1.8 fm^{-1})
- Zero of phase-shift in ^1S_0 channel near 290 MeV
- Zero of phase-shift in ^1D_2 channel near 890 and 1900 MeV
- Zero of phase-shift in ^1P_1 channel near 1660 and 2240 MeV
- Zero of phase-shift in ^3D_2 channel near 1115 MeV
- Zero of phase-shift in ^3P_0 channel near 200 MeV
- Zero of phase-shift in ^3F_3 channel near 640 MeV

## Context

This work extends inverse-scattering separable potential constructions beyond earlier efforts such as Kwong (1997) which went up to 1.6 GeV neglecting inelasticities, and Khokhlov (2021) which addressed only the ^1S_0 channel up to 3 GeV. The method enables exact reproduction of NN scattering data including absorption above pion production threshold, providing energy-independent separable form factors suitable for use in few- and many-body calculations where computational efficiency is critical. The approach is complementary to realistic potential models like those of Wiringa (1995), Lacombe (1980), and chiral effective field theory interactions that are limited to energies below about 300-500 MeV.
