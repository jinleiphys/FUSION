# Polarization degrees of freedom in photoinduced two-nucleon knockout from finite nuclei

> The polarization degrees of freedom in photoinduced two-nucleon knockout from finite nuclei are studied. It is pointed out that they open good perspectives to study the dynamics of dinucleons in the medium in detail. The ($γ,pp$) and ($γ,pn$) angular cross sections, photon asymmetries and outgoing nucleon polarizations are calculated for the target nuclei $^{16}$O and $^{12}$C and photonenergies ranging from 100 up to 500 MeV. It is investigated to which degree the two-nucleon emission reaction is dominated by photoabsorption on $^3S_1(T=0)$ proton-neutron and $^1S_0(T=1)$ proton-proton pairs in the nuclear medium. The calculations demonstrate that dominance of $S$ wave photoabsorption in the ($γ,pn$) channel does not necessarily imply that the reaction mechanism is similar to what is observed in deuteron photodisintegration.

## Key claim

The paper argues that polarization observables (photon asymmetries and outgoing nucleon polarizations) in photoinduced two-nucleon knockout provide a sensitive probe of dinucleon dynamics in the nuclear medium, but cautions that dominance of S-wave photoabsorption in the (γ,pn) channel does not necessarily imply the reaction mechanism is identical to deuteron photodisintegration.

## Method

The authors calculate (γ,pp) and (γ,pn) angular cross sections, photon asymmetries Σ, and outgoing nucleon polarizations P_y using both a factorized model (assuming relative S-wave absorption and zero-range approximation) and an unfactorized model that includes distorted waves for outgoing nucleons, meson-exchange currents, isobaric currents with dynamic Δ propagators, and Jastrow-type ground-state correlations. The calculations are performed for ¹⁶O and ¹²C targets at photon energies from 100 to 500 MeV, using mean-field wave functions from Hartree-Fock calculations with the Skyrme SkE2 force.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy range: 100 up to 500 MeV for calculations on ¹⁶O and ¹²C targets
- πNN pseudovector coupling constant: f_πNN²/4π = 0.079
- πNN vertex cutoff mass: Λ_πNN = 1200 MeV
- Δ resonance mass: M_Δ = 1232 MeV
- Δ selfenergy imaginary part in light nuclei: 40 MeV at full nuclear density
- Multipole convergence achieved including all electric and magnetic multipoles up to J = 5
- In the factorized model for (γ,pp) with only isobar current contributions and no ground-state correlations, the photon asymmetry Σ equals exactly -1 for planar kinematics

## Context

This work extends the Gottfried (1950s) approach relating two-nucleon knockout cross sections to pair functions, and builds on models from the Valencia group (Carrasco et al., 1990s) which use a diagrammatic approach with LDA, and the Pavia and Gent groups which emphasize nuclear structure aspects. The calculations are motivated by recent PIP-TOF collaboration data from Mainz (100-800 MeV), polarized beam experiments on ³He at LEGS (220-305 MeV) by Tedeschi et al., and ¹⁶O measurements by the LEGS collaboration. The results enable future experimental studies using polarized photon beams and recoil nucleon polarimetry to distinguish between different photoabsorption mechanisms and test for medium modifications of the Δ resonance.
