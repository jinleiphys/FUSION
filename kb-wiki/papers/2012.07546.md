# Role of baryon resonances in the $π^-p \to ne^+e^-$ reaction within an effective-Lagrangian model

> We present a study of the reaction $π^-p \to ne^+e^-$ for $\sqrt{s}=1.49\,\textrm{GeV}$, including non-resonant Born terms and contributions of the $N(1440)$, $N(1520)$, $N(1535)$ resonances ($R$), using an effective-Lagrangian model, which we extended by a phenomenological phase factor at the $RNρ$ vertex function. We give predictions for both the differential cross section $dσ/dm$ and the spin density matrix elements of the virtual photon that decays into the lepton pair. In the studied energy range, the cross section is dominated by the Born and $N(1520)$ contributions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model calculation of the reaction π⁻p → ne⁺e⁻ at a fixed center-of-mass energy of √s = 1.49 GeV, including both non-resonant Born terms and contributions from the N(1440), N(1520), and N(1535) resonances, but the authors introduce a phenomenological phase factor at the resonance-nucleon-rho vertex as a free parameter, meaning the predictions for the differential cross section and spin density matrix elements depend on this unconstrained phase.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective-Lagrangian model based on vector-meson dominance (VMD), where hadrons couple to the electromagnetic field both directly and via an intermediate rho meson. They included s-channel, u-channel, t-channel, and contact-term Born diagrams, plus s- and u-channel diagrams for the three baryon resonances, with form factors applied to the Born contributions and the pion-nucleon-resonance vertices. Coupling constants for the resonances were fixed by fitting partial decay widths to experimental branching ratios from the Particle Data Group and HADES results on pion-pair production, and a phenomenological phase factor was introduced at the rho-nucleon-resonance vertices to explore interference effects between direct-photon and rho-meson contributions.

## Key numbers

- The calculation is performed at a fixed center-of-mass energy of √s = 1.49 GeV.
- For the N(1520) resonance: mass = 1.520 GeV, width = 0.110 GeV, branching ratio to nucleon-rho = 12.2 ± 1.9%, with a coupling constant g_ρNR = 13.9.
- For the N(1440) resonance: mass = 1.440 GeV, width = 0.350 GeV, branching ratio to nucleon-rho < 0.2%, with a coupling constant g_ρNR = 3.37.
- For the N(1535) resonance: mass = 1.530 GeV, width = 0.150 GeV, branching ratio to nucleon-rho = 3.2 ± 0.7%, with a coupling constant g_ρNR = 1.97.
- The integrated cross section for invariant mass above 0.3 GeV, with no extra phase factor, is σ(invM > 0.3 GeV) = 0.73 μb, comprising 0.28 μb from Born terms, 0.17 μb from N(1520) terms, and 0.28 μb from Born-N(1520) interference.
- The rho-meson-photon coupling constant is g_ρ = 4.96.

## Context

This work extends the models of MiklosWolf (no year given) and Speranza (2017) by combining their approaches: it includes both Born terms (from MiklosWolf) and a consistent treatment of higher-spin baryon resonances (from Speranza 2017), while adding a phenomenological phase factor at the rho-nucleon-resonance vertex. The calculation is motivated by recent HADES experiments at the SIS18 synchrotron at GSI, and the results provide predictions for the dilepton invariant-mass spectrum and spin density matrix elements that can be compared with future HADES data, potentially enabling extraction of electromagnetic transition form factors of baryon resonances in the timelike region.
