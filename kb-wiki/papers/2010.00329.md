# D-wave effects in heavy quarkonium production in ultraperipheral nuclear collisions

> The $D$-wave admixture in quarkonium wave functions is acquired from the photonlike structure of $V\to Q\bar Q$ transition in the light-front frame widely used in the literature. Such a $D$-wave ballast is not justified by any nonrelativistic model for $Q-\bar Q$ interaction potential and leads to falsified predictions for the cross sections in heavy quarkonium production in ultra-peripheral nuclear collisions. We analyze this negative role of $D$-wave contribution by comparing with our previous studies based on a simple non-photon-like "$S$-wave-only" $V\to Q\bar Q$ transition in the $Q\bar Q$ rest frame.

# D-wave effects in heavy quarkonium production in ultraperipheral nuclear collisions

## Key claim

The photonlike structure of the $V\to Q\bar Q$ transition in the light-front frame, widely used in the literature, introduces an unjustified D-wave admixture into quarkonium wave functions that is not supported by any nonrelativistic $Q-\bar Q$ interaction potential; this spurious D-wave component leads to falsified predictions for production cross sections in ultraperipheral nuclear collisions, with the magnitude of the effect depending strongly on the quarkonium state and being particularly severe for radially excited states.

## Method

The authors compared two scenarios for the $V\to Q\bar Q$ transition vertex within the light-front color dipole formalism: Scenario I uses the standard photonlike vertex structure in the light-front frame (which introduces a D-wave admixture), while Scenario II employs a simple "S-wave-only" vertex in the $Q\bar Q$ rest frame with subsequent Melosh spin rotation to the light-front frame. Nuclear cross sections for coherent and incoherent photoproduction were calculated including finite-coherence-length corrections via a Green function formalism and gluon shadowing corrections, using the Buchmuller-Tye and power-like potentials for quarkonium wave functions and the GBW, KST, and IPsat models for the dipole-nucleon cross section.

## Key numbers

- For $J/\psi(1S)$ production in UPC, the D-wave admixture causes a $7\%-10\%$ undesirable enhancement of $d\sigma/dy$ at midrapidity, depending on collision energy
- For $\psi'(2S)$ production, the D-wave enhancement of $d\sigma/dy$ is much larger, representing $\sim 30\%-35\%$ in the LHC energy range
- For $\psi''(3S)$ production, the D-wave modification reaches almost $50\%$
- The $\psi'(2S)$-to-$J/\psi(1S)$ ratio at midrapidity for coherent production at $\sqrt{s_N}=5.02$ TeV is $0.207-0.245$ for Scenario I (with D-wave) versus $0.164-0.201$ for Scenario II (S-wave only), depending on the dipole model and potential
- For $\Upsilon(1S)$ production at $\sqrt{s_N}=5.02$ TeV, the D-wave component causes only $\sim 5\%$ modification of $d\sigma/dy$
- For $\Upsilon'(2S)$ production, the D-wave enhancement is $\sim 10\%-12\%$ of $d\sigma/dy$
- For $\Upsilon''(3S)$ production, the D-wave enhancement reaches $\sim 15\%$
- Coherent $J/\psi(1S)$ cross section at midrapidity at $\sqrt{s_N}=5.02$ TeV: Scenario I gives $5.316$ mb (GBW, BT potential) to $7.221$ mb (GBW, POW potential); Scenario II gives $4.910$ mb to $6.660$ mb
- Coherent $\psi'(2S)$ cross section at midrapidity at $\sqrt{s_N}=5.02$ TeV: Scenario I gives $1.300$ mb (GBW, BT) to $1.530$ mb (GBW, POW); Scenario II gives $0.989$ mb to $1.134$ mb

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of D-wave effects in diffractive electroproduction of heavy quarkonia off proton targets (Krelina 2019) to nuclear targets in ultraperipheral collisions, analyzing for the first time the relative contribution of the D-wave admixture to nuclear cross sections as a function of rapidity and collision energy. The results are consistent with the conclusion of Lappi (2020) about the lack of significant D-wave contribution in 1S charmonium electroproduction, but provide quantitative comparisons that the earlier work did not. The study enables future experimental identification of the D-wave component through measurements of the $\psi'(2S)$-to-$J/\psi(1S)$ ratio in UPC, which can discriminate between the photonlike and S-wave-only vertex structures and help eliminate unjustified theoretical assumptions in quarkonium production models.
