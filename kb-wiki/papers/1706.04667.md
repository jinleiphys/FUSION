# Isospin breaking from diquark clustering

> Although SU(2) isospin symmetry is generally assumed in the basic theory of the strong interaction, a number of significant violations have been observed in scattering and bound states of nucleons. Many of these violations can be attributed to the electromagnetic interaction but the question of how much of the violation is due to it remains open. A schematic model based on clustering of quarks in the interior of the confinement region of the two-nucleon system is introduced and evaluated. In this model the Coulomb interaction is the source of all isospin breaking. It draws on a picture of the quark density based on the diquark-quark model of hadron structure which has been investigated by a number of groups. The model produces three isospin breaking potentials connecting This illustrative model suggests that the breaking seen in the low-energy NN interaction may be understood in terms of the Coulomb force between members of diquark clusters. It allows the prediction of the charge symmetry breaking interaction and the $nn$ scattering length from the well measured $np$ singlet scattering length. Values of the $nn$ scattering length around $-18$ fm are favored. Since the model is based on the quark picture, it can be easily extended, in the SU(3) limit, to calculate isospin breaking in the strange sector in the corresponding channels. A natural consequence of isospin breaking from diquark clustering is that the breaking in the strange sector, as measured by the separation energy difference between $_Λ^4$H and $_Λ^4$He, is several times larger than that seen in the comparison of three-nucleon mirror nuclei as observed experimentally.

## Key claim

The paper presents a schematic model in which all isospin breaking in the low-energy nucleon-nucleon interaction arises solely from the Coulomb force between members of diquark clusters inside the overlapping confinement volumes of two nucleons, with the strong interaction itself treated as SU(2) symmetric. The model is illustrative rather than definitive, and the authors note that it does not produce a full NN potential but instead infers quark density parameters by comparing the Coulombic effect of clustering with experimentally observed isospin breaking.

## Method

The authors construct a schematic model based on a diquark-quark picture of nucleon structure, where each nucleon contains a diquark (one u and one d quark) and an unpaired quark. When two nucleons overlap, the six quarks can rearrange, and the unpaired quarks from different nucleons can form a new correlation. The Coulomb energy between all quark pairs is calculated as a function of the nucleon center-of-mass separation, using a single quark density that is the same for all singlet NN pairs (pp, nn, np). The resulting three isospin-breaking potentials are then used to connect an unbroken SU(2)-invariant scattering amplitude to the pp, nn, and np singlet channels. The unbroken strong interaction is represented by a phenomenological Reid soft-core potential fitted to pp scattering data below 30 MeV.

## Key numbers

* The pp scattering length is given as -17.3 ± 0.4 fm, obtained from Coulomb-corrected pp scattering.
* The np singlet scattering length from ortho- and para-hydrogen scattering is -23.748 ± 0.009 fm.
* The nn scattering length from the π⁻d → nnγ reaction is -18.5 ± 0.3 fm, while values from nd breakup are around -16.1 to -16.3 fm.
* The model favors nn scattering lengths around -18 fm.
* In the three-nucleon system, the conventional Coulomb potential supplies about 648 keV of the 764 keV binding energy difference between ³He and ³H, leaving about 116 keV; of this, approximately 46 keV can be attributed to small effects (magnetic interactions, kinetic energy from np mass difference), leaving about 70 keV to be explained by further charge-symmetry-breaking interactions.
* For a single diquark, the Coulomb energy scale is given by E_C = 0.277 n / ⟨r²⟩^(1/2) MeV, where ⟨r²⟩^(1/2) is the r.m.s. radius of the correlation density in fm (ranging from 0.25 to 1 fm in different models) and n = +4, +1, -2 for uu, dd, and ud pairs respectively.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing effort to understand isospin breaking in the NN interaction, particularly the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly and the differences among the pp, nn, and np singlet scattering lengths. It offers an alternative to the meson-exchange theory of charge symmetry breaking, which relied on ρ-ω mixing but was challenged by calculations from Goldman, Henderson and Thomas (1990s), Piekarowicz and Williams, and Hatsuda et al. showing that form factors or QCD sum rules reduce the ρ-ω contribution significantly. The model also contrasts with two-pion-exchange treatments of charge independence breaking by Coon and Niskanen and by Li and Machleidt, which the authors note are sensitive to the pion form factor cutoff and give widely varying results. The approach enables prediction of the nn scattering length from the well-measured np singlet scattering length and can be extended to the strange sector (Λ-N) to calculate isospin breaking in hypernuclei such as ⁴_ΛH and ⁴_ΛHe.
