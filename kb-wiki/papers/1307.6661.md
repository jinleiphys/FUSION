# Eccentricity is not the only source of elliptic flow fluctuations

> Sources of event-by-event elliptic flow fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions are investigated in a multiphase transport model. Besides the well-known initial eccentricity fluctuations, several other sources of dynamical fluctuations are identified. One is fluctuations in initial parton configurations at a given eccentricity. Second is quantum fluctuations in parton interactions during system evolution. Third is fluctuations caused by hadronization and final-state hadronic scatterings. The magnitudes of these fluctuations are investigated relative to eccentricity fluctuations and average flow magnitude. The fluctuations from the latter two sources are found to be negative. The results may have important implications to the interpretation of elliptic flow data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that event-by-event elliptic flow ($v_2$) fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions are not solely determined by initial-state eccentricity ($\varepsilon_2$) fluctuations; additional dynamical fluctuations from parton interactions, hadronization, and final-state hadronic scatterings contribute significantly, with the latter two sources producing *negative* fluctuations that reduce the total $v_2$ spread.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model with string melting to simulate Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV at three fixed impact parameters ($b = 0$, 4, and 8 fm). They compared default runs (with all fluctuations) to runs with identical initial parton configurations (same positions and momenta but different random seeds for evolution), allowing them to isolate fluctuations from initial configuration, parton interactions, and hadronization/hadronic scatterings. Statistical fluctuations from finite multiplicity were subtracted using an analytic formula and a particle-discarding method.

## Key numbers

- At $b = 8$ fm, the conversion slope $k$ from $\langle \varepsilon_2 \rangle$ to $\langle v_2 \rangle$ is smaller than at $b = 0$ and 4 fm; the same $k$ is observed for parton and hadron $v_2$ at a given $b$.
- Dynamical fluctuations from parton interactions ($\sigma_{v_2}\{\rm par\}$) and from hadronization plus hadronic scatterings ($\sigma_{v_2}\{\rm had\}$) are both negative; the latter is larger in magnitude than the former.
- The total $v_2$ fluctuations in AMPT are larger than the contribution from eccentricity fluctuations alone ($\sigma_{v_2}\{\varepsilon_2\} = k \times \sigma_{\varepsilon_2}$), and the ratio of total fluctuations to $\sigma_{v_2}\{\varepsilon_2\}$ does not show a simple scaling.
- The negative dynamical fluctuations relative to $\langle v_2 \rangle$ decrease in magnitude (smaller absolute value) with increasing impact parameter.

## Context

This work extends the common assumption, used in many hydrodynamical studies (e.g., Voloshin (2008) and PHOBOS (2007)), that $v_2$ is strictly proportional to $\varepsilon_2$ and that $v_2$ fluctuations are dominated by eccentricity fluctuations. The authors identify additional fluctuation sources in the AMPT transport model that are absent in deterministic hydrodynamics. The results suggest that hydrodynamical calculations, which lack these negative dynamical fluctuations, may overpredict $v_2$ data, and they call for further investigation of the physics mechanisms of anisotropic flow fluctuations, including studies of $v_3$ and multiplicity dependence.
