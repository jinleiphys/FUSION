# Thermal correlators in the hadron resonance gas: a dual Hagedorn distance

> Fluctuations and correlations of conserved quantities in the confined phase of QCD are a viable way to characterize the existence of exotic and missing states with given quantum numbers in the hadronic spectrum. We study a realization of the Hadron Resonance Gas model in the light quark (uds) flavor sector of QCD to study the fluctuations and static correlators of electric charge, baryon number and strangeness. It is also conjectured an interesting duality between the correlators at zero temperature, and the fluctuations of integrated quantities at low temperatures, leading to the appearance of a dual Hagedorn distance for the former.

# Thermal correlators in the hadron resonance gas: a dual Hagedorn distance

## Key claim
The authors conjecture a duality between zero-temperature static correlators of conserved charges and finite-temperature susceptibilities in the Hadron Resonance Gas model, which implies the existence of a "dual Hagedorn distance" (r_H ~ 0.21 fm) below which the hadronic representation of correlators diverges, analogous to the Hagedorn temperature divergence of the partition function.

## Method
The authors compute static correlators of electric charge, baryon number, and strangeness within the Hadron Resonance Gas model using the light quark (uds) flavor sector, summing over meson and baryon states from the Relativized Quark Model (RQM) of Godfrey and Isgur (1985, 1989) with spins up to J ≤ 7/2. They compare the small-distance behavior of zero-temperature correlators with the low-temperature behavior of thermal susceptibilities, identifying a formal analogy under the replacement r ↔ 1/T.

## Key numbers
- Hagedorn temperature T_H ≈ 150 MeV, at which the HRG partition function diverges as Z_HRG ~ A/(T_H - T)
- Dual Hagedorn distance r_H ≈ 1/(2π T_H) ≈ 0.21 fm, below which the hadronic representation of static correlators diverges
- In the large temperature limit for uds quarks, susceptibilities approach: χ_QQ → 2/3, χ_BB → 1/N_c, χ_SS → 1
- The zero-temperature static correlator for spin J > 0 particles behaves as C^{ab J}_{00}(r) ~ (m^2/r^4) × 1/(mr)^{4J} at small distances
- The HRG susceptibilities diverge as χ_ab|_HRG ~ 1/(T_H - T) near the Hagedorn temperature

## Context
This work extends the Hadron Resonance Gas model approach previously applied to the equation of state by Megias et al. (2012, 2014), where PDG and RQM spectra were compared with lattice QCD data for T ≲ 0.8 T_c. The authors had earlier found that conventional hadrons from PDG or RQM are insufficient to saturate sum rules for the Polyakov loop and entropy shift, suggesting missing conventional states and hybrid states. The present study proposes fluctuations and correlators of conserved charges as a more discriminating tool than the equation of state for identifying missing and exotic states in the QCD spectrum, and conjectures a duality that could be tested against future lattice QCD results.
