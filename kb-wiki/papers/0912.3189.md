# The Coulomb phase shift revisited

> We investigate the Coulomb phase shift, and derive and analyze new and more precise analytical formulae. We consider next to leading order terms to the Stirling approximation, and show that they are important at small values of the angular momentum $l$ and other regimes. We employ the uniform approximation. The use of our expressions in low energy scattering of charged particles is discussed and some comparisons are made with other approximation methods.

## Key claim

The paper derives new, more precise analytical formulae for the Coulomb phase shift by including next-to-leading-order terms from the Stirling series for the gamma function, showing that these corrections are important at small values of the angular momentum \(l\) and other regimes where the standard Stirling approximation is insufficient.

## Method

The authors start from the exact expression for the Coulomb phase shift in terms of gamma functions, \(e^{2i\sigma_l} = \Gamma(1+l+i\eta)/\Gamma(1+l-i\eta)\), and derive an exact representation using the Gudermann series for the gamma function. They then apply the Stirling series (an asymptotic expansion) to obtain a first-order corrected approximation for the phase shift, and compare this with the standard WKB and eikonal approximations for various regimes of angular momentum and Sommerfeld parameter \(\eta\).

## Key numbers

- The first-order corrected s-wave phase shift approximation is \(\sigma_0^{(1)} = \frac{1}{2}\tan^{-1}(\eta) + \eta(\ln\sqrt{1+\eta^2} - 1) - \eta/[12(1+\eta^2)]\), with relative errors below 1% for \(\eta\) between 0 and 5 (except near \(\eta \simeq 1.81\) where \(\sigma_0\) vanishes).
- The exact \(\sigma_0\) vanishes at \(\eta \simeq 1.810\), which is close to \(\sqrt{2}\).
- For \(\eta=0.1\) and \(l=0\), the exact phase shift is \(\sigma_0^{\text{exact}}/\pi = -0.01825\), while \(\sigma_0^{(0)}/\pi = -0.01581\) and \(\sigma_0^{(1)}/\pi = -0.01844\).
- For \(\eta=1.0\) and \(l=0\), the exact phase shift is \(\sigma_0^{\text{exact}}/\pi = -0.09602\), while \(\sigma_0^{(0)}/\pi = -0.08299\) and \(\sigma_0^{(1)}/\pi = -0.09625\).
- The bound on the correction term \(M_{l,\eta}\) is \(|M_{l,\eta}| \leq 1/[6(l+1+\sqrt{(l+1)^2+\eta^2})]\).

## Context

This work extends the standard treatment of Coulomb phase shifts found in textbooks such as Brink (1985), providing improved analytical expressions that go beyond the usual large-\(l\) or large-\(\eta\) approximations. The authors compare their results with the WKB approximation (which uses the Langer correction \(l+1/2\)) and the eikonal approximation of Glauber (1959), showing that the first-order Stirling correction yields accurate results even for small angular momenta and small Sommerfeld parameters. The improved formulae are relevant for low-energy scattering of charged particles and for applications such as the Mott cross section for identical particles, where the critical value \(\eta = \sqrt{2}\) was previously identified by Canto et al. (2001).
