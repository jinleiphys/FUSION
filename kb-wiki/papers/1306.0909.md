# Inclusive b-jet production in heavy ion collisions at the LHC

> Theoretical and experimental advances in understanding light jet production and modification in Pb+Pb reactions at a center-of-mass energy 2.76 TeV have been a highlight of the LHC heavy ion program. At the same time, the detailed mechanisms of heavy quark energy loss in dense QCD matter are not yet fully understood. With this motivation, we present theoretical predictions for the nuclear-induced attenuation of the differential cross sections for inclusive b-jet production in heavy ion collisions at the LHC for comparison to upcoming data. We find that for transverse momenta pT > 75 GeV both hadronization and mass effects are negligible and this attenuation is comparable to the one observed for light jets. We discuss how the detailed b-jet quenching patterns can be used to gain new insight into the in-medium heavy flavor dynamics.

## Key claim

The authors predict that for b-jets with transverse momentum above 75 GeV in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, the nuclear suppression (R_AA) becomes comparable to that of light jets, because both hadronization and heavy quark mass effects become negligible in the fully coherent energy loss regime.

## Method

The authors combine Pythia 8 simulations (with CTEQ6L1 PDFs, leading-order matrix elements, parton shower, and Lund string hadronization) for p+p baseline b-jet production with cold nuclear matter effects and a first-order-in-opacity calculation of medium-induced gluon radiation in the quark-gluon plasma. They treat the b-jet flavor composition by separating contributions from heavy quark pair creation, gluon splitting, and other channels, and apply a convolution of the p+p cross section with an energy-loss probability distribution that includes both radiative and collisional dissipation, accounting for the jet cone radius R and the geometry of the collision via an optical Glauber model.

## Key numbers

* For b-jets with transverse momentum p_T > 75 GeV, the nuclear suppression R_AA is comparable to that of light jets; the heavy quark mass effect (m_b = 4.5 GeV to 2m_b = 9 GeV) disappears in this regime.
* The gluon splitting contribution dominates the b-jet cross section at all studied p_T; the heavy quark pair creation contribution is only 10-20% depending on p_T, rising to about 30% at p_T ~ 300 GeV when requiring the b-quark to be the leading particle in the jet.
* For central Pb+Pb collisions (N_part = 360) at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, the predicted R_AA for b-jets with R = 0.2 and coupling g_med = 2.0 shows a modest p_T dependence up to 300 GeV, with the suppression largest for the smallest jet radius.
* Cold nuclear matter effects produce a constant ~15-20% suppression of b-jet production in minimum bias Pb+Pb collisions, independent of the jet radius.
* The splitting time for g -> b bbar is tau_split < 1 fm for jets of transverse momentum 200 GeV at midrapidity, much smaller than the typical medium size R_Pb.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of light jet quenching in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC (He 2011, Neufeld 2010, Qin 2010) to heavy flavor jets, addressing the open question of heavy quark energy loss mechanisms that have not been sufficient to explain observed heavy meson suppression at RHIC and LHC (Adler 2005, ALICE 2012). The predictions enable direct comparison to upcoming CMS and ATLAS b-jet data in heavy ion collisions, and the authors note that the degeneracy between coupling strength and cold nuclear matter effects in Pb+Pb alone can only be resolved by p+Pb measurements at high transverse momentum.
