# Formulation of effective interaction in terms of renormalized vertices and propagators

> One of the useful and practical methods for solving quantum-mechanical many-body systems is to recast the full problem into a form of the effective interaction acting within a model space of tractable size. Many of the effective-interaction theories in nuclear physics have been formulated by use of the so called $\hatQ$ box introduced by Kuo et.al. It has been one of the central problems how to calculate the $\hatQ$ box accurately and efficiently. We first show that, introducing new basis states, the Hamiltonian is transformed to a block-tridiagonal form in terms of submatrices with small dimension. With this transformed Hamiltonian, we next prove that the $\hatQ$ box can be expressed in two ways: One is a form of continued fraction and the other is a simple series expansion up to second order with respect to renormalized vertices and propagators. This procedure ensures to derive an exact $\hatQ$ box, if the calculation converges as the dimension of the Hilbert space tends to infinity. The $\hatQ$ box given in this study corresponds to a non-perturbative solution for the energy-dependent effective interaction which is often referred to as the Bloch-Horowitz or the Feshbach form. By applying the $\hatZ$-box approach based on the $\hatQ$ box proposed previously, we introduce a graphical method for solving the eigenvalue problem of the Hamiltonian. The present approach has a possibility of resolving many of the difficulties encountered in the effective-interaction theory.

## Key claim

The authors prove that the $\widehat Q$ box can be expressed exactly either as a continued fraction or as a simple series expansion up to second order with respect to renormalized vertices and propagators, provided the calculation converges as the dimension of the Hilbert space tends to infinity.

## Method

The Hamiltonian is first transformed into a block-tridiagonal form by introducing new basis states that generate Krylov subspaces, where each block submatrix has dimension at most equal to that of the model space. Using this transformed Hamiltonian, a set of coupled equations for the operator $\chi(E) = (E - QHQ)^{-1} QHP$ is derived and solved by two recursion methods: one yielding a continued-fraction representation for the $\widehat Q$ box, and the other giving a second-order expansion in terms of renormalized vertices and propagators. The $\widehat Z$-box approach is then applied to introduce a graphical method for solving the eigenvalue problem.

## Key numbers

- The $\widehat Q$ box is expressed as $\widehat{Q}(E) = PHP + PHQ_1 \frac{1}{\widetilde{e}_1(E)} Q_1 HP$, where $\widetilde{e}_1(E)$ is a renormalized inverse propagator obtained from the continued fraction.
- The recurrence relation for the continued fraction is $\widetilde{e}_{n-1}(E) = e_{n-1}(E) - H_{n-1,n} \frac{1}{\widetilde{e}_n(E)} H_{n,n-1}$, starting from $\widetilde{e}_N(E) = Q_N (E-H) Q_N$.
- The second solution expresses the $\widehat Q$ box as a sum of terms up to second order with respect to renormalized vertices and propagators.

## Context

This work extends the $\widehat Q$-box formalism introduced by Kuo and collaborators (Kuo, Lee, Ratcliff, 1971) and addresses the long-standing problem of calculating the $\widehat Q$ box accurately and efficiently. The approach provides a non-perturbative alternative to the perturbative methods of Krenciglowa-Kuo (1974) and Lee-Suzuki (1980), which have known convergence limitations and pole problems. The method enables rigorous calculation of the effective interaction without matrix inversion of the huge-dimensional $QHQ$ operator, and the graphical $\widehat Z$-box method offers a way to resolve difficulties encountered in standard effective-interaction theory.
