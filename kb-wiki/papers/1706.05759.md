# Central depression in nucleonic densities: Trend analysis in nuclear density-functional-theory approach

> Central depression of nucleonic density, i.e., a reduction of density in the nuclear interior, has been attributed to many factors. E.g., bubble structures in superheavy (SH) nuclei are believed to be due to the electrostatic repulsion. In light nuclei, the mechanism has been discussed in terms of shell effects associated with occupations of s-orbits. The objective of this work is to reveal mechanisms behind the formation of central depression in nucleonic densities in light and heavy nuclei. We introduce several measures of the internal nucleonic density. Through the statistical analysis, we study the information content of these measures with respect to nuclear matter properties. We apply nuclear density functional theory with Skyrme functionals. Using the statistical tools of linear least square regression, we inspect correlations between various measures of central depression and model parameters, including nuclear matter properties. We study bivariate correlations with selected quantities as well as multiple correlations with groups of parameters. Detailed correlation analysis is carried out for $^{34}$Si for which a bubble structure has been reported recently, $^{48}$Ca, and N=82, 126, and 184 isotonic chains. We show that the central depression in medium-mass nuclei is very sensitive to shell effects, whereas for SH systems it is firmly driven by the electrostatic repulsion. An appreciable semi-bubble structure in proton density is predicted for $^{294}$Og, which is currently the heaviest nucleus known experimentally. Our correlation analysis reveals that the central density indicators in nuclei below $^{208}$Pb carry little information on parameters of nuclear matter; they are predominantly driven by shell structure. On the other hand, in the SH nuclei there exists a clear relationship between the central nucleonic density and symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The paper shows that central depression in nucleonic densities is driven by different mechanisms depending on nuclear mass: in medium-mass nuclei below lead-208 it is governed by shell effects (particularly s-orbital occupations), while in superheavy nuclei it is firmly driven by electrostatic repulsion; the authors hedge that for silicon-34 the prediction of bubble structure varies significantly with the nuclear Hamiltonian used.

## Method

The authors applied nuclear density functional theory with three Skyrme energy density functionals (SV-min, SLy6, and UNEDF1) to compute nucleonic densities for spherical nuclei including silicon-34, calcium-48, and isotonic chains at N=82, 126, and 184. They introduced two measures of central depression: a normalized density difference using the diffraction radius, and a dimensionless parameter derived from the charge form factor within the modified Helm model. Using linear least square regression, they computed bivariate correlation coefficients and multiple correlation coefficients between central density indicators and nuclear matter properties as well as Skyrme model parameters.

## Key numbers

- For silicon-34, the proton 1s shell occupation is sensitive to the functional: SLy6 predicts a 1s-shell vacancy and large central depression, while other models predict non-vanishing proton pairing and weaker depression; the value of the central depression parameter w_p in silicon-34 increases dramatically when pairing is turned off
- For the N=184 isotonic chain, the coefficient of determination between Coulomb energy and central proton density rho_c[p] is high, while for lighter nuclei this correlation is marginal
- In oganesson-294 (the heaviest nucleus known experimentally), an appreciable semi-bubble structure in proton density is predicted even with triaxial deformation
- For the superheavy nucleus SHE-472-164, all central density indicators correlate strongly (greater than 0.8) with model parameters
- For calcium-48, coefficients of determination between central density indicators and model parameters are mostly less than 0.5
- The multiple correlation coefficient between the liquid drop model parameter group and the normalized central proton density in superheavy nuclei becomes close to 100%

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of bubble nuclei dating back to Wilson (1946) and quantitative calculations by Davies (1972) and Campi (1972), and addresses the conflicting predictions for silicon-34 noted by Duguet (2017). The correlation analysis reveals that central density indicators in nuclei below lead-208 carry little information on nuclear matter parameters, while in superheavy nuclei there exists a clear relationship between central nucleonic density and symmetry energy, consistent with the finite-range droplet model of Myers (1969) and Moller (1992). The results enable using central density measurements in superheavy nuclei to constrain the symmetry energy, and confirm that the region of deformed semi-bubble nuclei has been reached experimentally with oganesson-294.
