# Twist Mode in Spherical Alkali Metal Clusters

> A remarkable orbital quadrupole magnetic resonance, so-called twist mode, is predicted in alkali metal clusters where it is represented by $I^π=2^-$ low-energy excitations of valence electrons with strong M2 transitions to the ground state. We treat the twist by both macroscopic and microscopic ways. In the latter case, the shell structure of clusters is fully exploited, which is crucial for the considered size region ($8\le N_e\le 1314$). The energy-weighted sum rule is derived for the pseudo-Hamiltonian. In medium and heavy spherical clusters the twist dominates over its spin-dipole counterpart and becomes the most strong multipole magnetic mode.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that a specific orbital quadrupole magnetic resonance, the twist mode (Iπ=2−), exists in spherical alkali metal clusters and becomes the strongest multipole magnetic mode in medium and heavy clusters, but this dominance is conditional on the cluster size (starting from Ne=40) and the mode is masked by low-energy E1 excitations in photoabsorption spectra, requiring alternative detection methods like resonant Raman scattering.

## Method

The authors treat the twist mode using both macroscopic elastodynamical models and microscopic calculations within the spherical jellium model, employing the Kohn-Sham single-particle scheme for Na and K clusters and a pseudo-Hamiltonian for Li clusters that accounts for local and non-local ionic effects. They analyze the particle-hole (1p-1h) M2 response for spherical clusters with valence electron numbers from 8 to 1314, deriving energy-weighted sum rules for the orbital and spin components of the M2 transition operator.

## Key numbers

- Twist energy follows ω ∝ Ne−1/3, with values shown for K (rs=5 a.u.), Na (rs=3.96 a.u.), and Li (rs=3.25 a.u.) clusters across the size range 8 ≤ Ne ≤ 1314
- B(M2) strength grows as Ne2, reaching 2·105 μb2 Å2 eV for the orbital energy-weighted sum rule Sl in Na clusters with Ne=440
- The ratio R = Sl/Ss (orbital to spin energy-weighted sum rule) reaches 1.40 for Ne=40, 3.21 for Ne=92, and 9.72 for Ne=440
- For Na9+, the dominant twist peak exhausts 100% of the total M2 strength; for Na93+, 80%; for Na1315+, 60%
- The elastodynamical model overestimates twist energy by up to 80% and strength by up to 30% compared to microscopic results
- For Li clusters, the effective mass correction m*/me ∼ 1.2 decreases Sl by a factor of ∼4/5

## Context

This work extends the concept of the twist mode, previously studied in atomic nuclei (Holzward and Ekardt, 1978; Schwesinger, 1984; von Neumann-Cosel et al., 1999), to alkali metal clusters, complementing earlier predictions of the M1 scissor mode in deformed clusters (Lipparini and Stringari, 1989; Nesterenko et al., 1999). The microscopic analysis, including novel energy-weighted sum rules, enables future experimental searches via resonant Raman scattering, particularly for clusters with about 104 atoms where the twist strength is largest and concentrated in a narrow low-energy interval well separated from ionic vibrations (∼50 meV).
