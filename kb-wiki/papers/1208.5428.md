# Disentangling effects of collision geometry and symmetry energy in U+U collisions

> Effects of the collision geometry on experimental observables that are known to be sensitive to the high-density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy are examined in U+U collisions at 0.52 GeV/nucleon using an isospin- and momentum-dependent interaction within the framework of IBUU transport model. It is found that the neutron-proton differential flow in tip-tip collisions and the difference of neutron and proton elliptic flow in body-body collisions are more sensitive to the symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities compared with collisions of spherical nuclei of the same masses. In addition, the n/p ratio of pre-equilibrium nucleons is found to be slightly more sensitive to the symmetry energy in tip-tip collisions, and the collision geometry affects the π^-/π^+ ratio significantly.

## Key claim

In U+U collisions at 0.52 GeV/nucleon, the neutron-proton differential transverse flow in tip-tip collisions and the difference of neutron and proton elliptic flow in body-body collisions are more sensitive to the symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities compared with collisions of spherical nuclei of the same masses, but the collision geometry effects are comparable to or sometimes even larger than the symmetry energy effects.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model with an isospin- and momentum-dependent interaction to simulate U+U collisions at 0.52 GeV/nucleon. They compared tip-tip, body-body, and sphere-sphere (spherical nuclei) collision geometries using two parameterizations of the symmetry energy (x=0 and x=-1, representing softer and stiffer symmetry energy, respectively). For each geometry, they generated approximately 200,000 events at both central (b=0 fm) and non-central (b=b_max/2) impact parameters, with b_max values of 12.4 fm (tip-tip), 13.6 fm (sphere-sphere), and 16.0 fm (body-body).

## Key numbers

- Neutron-proton differential transverse flow slope parameter in non-central tip-tip collisions: -9.92 ± 0.49 MeV/c (x=0) and -14.10 ± 0.48 MeV/c (x=-1)
- Neutron-proton differential transverse flow slope parameter in non-central body-body collisions: -1.75 ± 0.46 MeV/c (x=0) and -2.64 ± 0.46 MeV/c (x=-1)
- Neutron-proton differential transverse flow slope parameter in non-central sphere-sphere collisions: -5.15 ± 0.48 MeV/c (x=0) and -7.68 ± 0.48 MeV/c (x=-1)
- Mid-rapidity high-pT (pT > 0.5 GeV/c) neutron and proton elliptic flow difference in central body-body collisions: -0.02 ± 0.06% (x=0) and -0.26 ± 0.06% (x=-1)
- Mid-rapidity high-pT (pT > 0.5 GeV/c) neutron and proton elliptic flow difference in non-central body-body collisions: 1.46 ± 0.09% (x=0) and 1.03 ± 0.09% (x=-1)
- Mid-rapidity high-pT (pT > 0.5 GeV/c) neutron/proton ratio in central tip-tip collisions: 1.241 ± 0.001 (x=0) and 1.286 ± 0.001 (x=-1)
- Final pi-/pi+ ratio in central tip-tip collisions: 2.130 ± 0.002 (x=0) and 2.095 ± 0.002 (x=-1)
- Final pi-/pi+ ratio in central body-body collisions: 2.119 ± 0.002 (x=0) and 2.085 ± 0.002 (x=-1)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of collision geometry effects in U+U collisions at intermediate energies (Li 2000, Luo 2007, Cao 2010) by systematically comparing the sensitivity of multiple isospin observables to the symmetry energy across different orientations. The results are directly relevant to planned U+U experiments at 0.52 GeV/nucleon at the cooling storage ring in Lanzhou, China, and provide guidance for selecting optimal collision geometries to probe the high-density symmetry energy. The study also highlights that assuming spherical nuclei in analyses of deformed nuclei can introduce large errors in isospin-sensitive observables, motivating the development of orientation triggers as proposed by Nepali (2007) and Wu (2008).
