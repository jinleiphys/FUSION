# Electromagnetic structure and weak decay of meson K in a light-front QCD-inspired

> The kaon electromagnetic (e.m.) form factor is reviewed considering a light-front constituent quark model. In this approach, it is discussed the relevance of the quark-antiquark pair terms for the full covariance of the e.m. current. It is also verified, by considering a QCD dynamical model, that a good agreement with experimental data can be obtained for the kaon weak decay constant once a probability of about 80% of the valence component is taken into account.

# Electromagnetic structure and weak decay of meson K in a light-front QCD-inspired model

## Key claim
Good agreement with experimental data for the kaon weak decay constant can be obtained in a light-front QCD-inspired dynamical model, but only when the valence component of the wave function has a probability of about 80%. The kaon electromagnetic form factor is fully covariant only after including non-valence (pair term) contributions to the minus component of the electromagnetic current.

## Method
The authors compute the kaon electromagnetic form factor using light-front constituent quark model wave functions, evaluating both the plus (J+) and minus (J-) components of the electromagnetic current in the Breit frame (q+=0). For the weak decay constants, they solve an eigenvalue equation for the effective square mass operator of the pseudoscalar meson, using two different parameterizations of the model form factor (g^(a) and g^(b)), with parameters adjusted to reproduce the experimental pion mass and electromagnetic radius.

## Key numbers
- Constituent quark masses: m_u = m_d = 220 MeV, m_s = 419 MeV (for electromagnetic form factor calculation)
- Regulator mass m_R = 946 MeV (adjusted to fit kaon electromagnetic radius)
- Calculated kaon mean-square radius: ⟨r²_K+⟩ = 0.354 fm² (experimental value: 0.340 fm²)
- For the QCD-inspired dynamical model: m_u = 384 MeV, m_s = 508 MeV, α = 0.5
- Kaon weak decay constant from model (a): f_K = 126 MeV; from model (b): f_K = 121 MeV (experimental: 113.0 ± 1.0 ± 0.31 MeV)
- Pion weak decay constant from both models: f_π = 110 MeV (experimental: 92.4 ± 0.07 ± 0.25 MeV)
- Calculated kaon mass from both models: 490 MeV (experimental: 494 MeV)

## Context
This work extends the light-front approach of Cardarelli (1996), Pacheco (1999), and Bakker (2001) for studying quark-antiquark bound states, and builds on the authors' previous work (Pereira 2005) on kaon electromagnetic form factors. The inclusion of pair terms in the J- component is shown to be essential for covariance, resolving a known issue in light-front calculations. The results enable further studies of kaon structure and decay using light-front QCD-inspired models, with the valence probability of 80% providing a constraint for future dynamical calculations.
