# On growth of spinodal instabilities in nuclear matter

> Early growth of density fluctuations of nuclear matter in spinodal region is investigated employing the stochastic mean-field approach. In contrast to the earlier treatments in which only collective modes were included in the calculations, in the present work non-collective modes are also included, thus providing a complete treatment of the density correlation functions. Calculations are carried out for symmetric matter in non-relativistic framework using a semi-classical approximation.

## Key claim

The authors show that including non-collective modes (the "cut contribution") in the calculation of density correlation functions removes the unphysical divergent behavior of the spectral intensity at the maximum wave number, which had previously required an ad-hoc cut-off; the pole and cut contributions diverge with opposite signs and cancel exactly, producing a regular spectral intensity.

## Method

The authors employ the stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach in a non-relativistic, semi-classical framework for symmetric nuclear matter. They linearize the equation of motion around a uniform initial state inside the spinodal zone and use the method of one-sided Fourier transform to obtain a nearly analytical expression for the equal-time density correlation function. The contour integral in the complex frequency plane is evaluated by including both the collective pole contributions (imaginary solutions of the dispersion relation) and the cut contribution arising from non-collective poles on the real axis, using a Skyrme-type effective potential.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature of the effective Skyrme potential: \(T_c = 15.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\) at density \(\rho \approx 0.3\rho_0\).
- Saturation density: \(\rho_0 = 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\); binding energy: \(\varepsilon_0 = 15.7\ \mathrm{MeV/nucleon}\); compressibility modulus: \(K = 201.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\); surface energy coefficient: \(a_{\mathrm{surf}} = 18.6\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Correlation lengths: \(x_C = 2.0\ \mathrm{fm}\) for initial density \(\rho = 0.2\rho_0\) and \(x_C = 3.0\ \mathrm{fm}\) for \(\rho = 0.4\rho_0\), nearly independent of temperature and time.
- At \(\rho = 0.4\rho_0\) and \(T = 5.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\) at \(t = 50\ \mathrm{fm}/c\), the dispersion of density fluctuations is \(\sqrt{\sigma(x_C, t)} = 0.04\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), giving nucleon number fluctuations in the correlation volume in the range \(3 \le \Delta A \le 12\).
- Spectral intensity calculations are shown at times \(t = 30\ \mathrm{fm}/c\) and \(t = 50\ \mathrm{fm}/c\) for temperatures \(T = 1\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(T = 5\ \mathrm{MeV}\) at initial densities \(\rho = 0.2\rho_0\) and \(\rho = 0.4\rho_0\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigations using the SMF approach (Yilmaz et al., 2014; Ayik et al., 2014) which included only collective modes and required an ad-hoc wave-number cut-off to handle divergences. It directly addresses a point raised by Bozek (2005) that non-collective modes are necessary for a complete treatment. The results enable future studies of charge-asymmetric nuclear matter and nonlinear evolution of density fluctuations beyond the linear response regime, where the exponential growth saturates and fragment size distributions are modified by recombination and breakup.
