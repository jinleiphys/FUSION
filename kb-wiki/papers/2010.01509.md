# Was GW190814 a black hole -- strange quark star system?

> We investigate the possibility that the low mass companion of the black hole in the source of GW190814 was a strange quark star. This possibility is viable within the so-called two-families scenario in which neutron stars and strange quark stars coexist. Strange quark stars can reach the mass range indicated by GW190814, $M\sim (2.5-2.67) M_\odot$ due to a large value of the adiabatic index, without the need for a velocity of sound close to the causal limit. Neutron stars (actually hyperonic stars in the two-families scenario) can instead fulfill the presently available astrophysical and nuclear physics constraints which require a softer equation of state. In this scheme it is possible to satisfy both the request of very large stellar masses and of small radii while using totally realistic and physically motivated equations of state. Moreover it is possible to get a radius for a 1.4 $M_\odot$ star of the order or less than 11 km, which is impossible if only one family of compact stars exists.

# Was GW190814 a black hole – strange quark star system?

## Key claim
The low-mass companion of the black hole in GW190814 (mass ~2.5–2.67 M⊙) could be a strange quark star rather than a neutron star or black hole, but only within the "two-families scenario" where neutron stars and strange quark stars coexist. This explanation avoids requiring a speed of sound near the causal limit, provided a sizable color-flavor locking superconducting gap exists in the quark phase.

## Method
The authors computed mass-radius relations for two families of compact stars using separate equations of state: hadronic matter (via Brueckner-Hartree-Fock and relativistic mean-field approaches including hyperons and Δ-resonances) and strange quark matter (via a grand canonical potential with bag constant, perturbative QCD corrections, and CFL superconducting gap). They compared these relations against astrophysical constraints from thermonuclear bursts, quiescent low-mass X-ray binaries, NICER results for PSR J0030+0451, and GW170817 tidal deformability limits. They also analyzed the conversion of metastable hadronic stars into quark stars via deconfinement nucleation.

## Key numbers
- GW190814 companion mass range: M ~ (2.5–2.67) M⊙
- Maximum mass of quark stars in the model: M_max^Q = 2.58 M⊙ (for parameter set B^{1/4} = 135 MeV, a_4 = 0.7, Δ = 80 MeV, m_s = 100 MeV)
- Maximum mass of hadronic stars (BHF EOS): M^H_max ~ 2 M⊙
- Maximum mass of hadronic stars (RMF EOS with Δ-resonances): M^H_max ~ 1.6 M⊙
- Tidal deformability for 1.4 M⊙ hadronic star (BHF EOS): Λ_{1.4} = 388
- Tidal deformability for 1.4 M⊙ hadronic star (RMF EOS): Λ_{1.4} = 150
- Radius of 1.4 M⊙ star (RMF EOS): R_{1.4} < 11 km
- Threshold mass for prompt collapse to black hole (BHF EOS): M_threshold = 2.79 M⊙
- Threshold mass for prompt collapse (RMF EOS): M_threshold ~ 2.5 M⊙
- Critical baryonic masses for conversion to quark star: 1.67 M⊙ (BHF) and 1.68 M⊙ (RMF)
- Energy released in hadronic-to-quark star conversion: ~10^53 erg
- Saturation density: n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}

## Context
This work extends the two-families scenario previously developed by Bombaci, Drago, and collaborators (Bombaci & Datta 2000; Berezhiani 2002; Bombaci 2004; Drago 2013, 2015, 2020) to explain the GW190814 event reported by Abbott (2020). It contrasts with single-family interpretations requiring very stiff equations of state (Fattoyev 2020; Tews 2020) or near-Keplerian rotation (Most 2020; Zhang 2020), which face tension with heavy-ion collision constraints (Danielewicz 2002) and GW170817 tidal deformability limits. The paper enables future investigations of GW170817 as a hadronic star–quark star merger and of short gamma-ray burst production via protomagnetar models (Thompson 2004; Metzger 2007, 2010), where quark star surfaces reduce baryon loading timescales to tenths of a second (Drago 2015).
