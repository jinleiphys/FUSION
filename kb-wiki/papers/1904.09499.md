# Total number of $J$ levels for identical particles in a single-$j$ shell using coefficients of fractional parentage

> Analytical expressions of the total number of $J$ levels for three and four fermions in a nuclear $j^n$ shell are provided. The formulas were derived using a combination of sum rules for coefficients of fractional parentage, and "unusual" identities, i.e. which do not contain the weighting factor $(2J + 1)$ involving $6j$ and $9j$ symbols.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first exact analytical expressions for the total number of spin-J levels (summed over all J) for three and four identical fermions in a single-j nuclear shell, using sum rules for coefficients of fractional parentage combined with "unusual" sum rules for 6j and 9j symbols that lack the usual weighting factor (2J+1).

## Method

The author derived the total number of levels N_tot(j,n) for n=3 and n=4 identical fermions in a single-j shell by summing the known expressions for N(J,j,n) over all possible total angular momenta J. For three particles, the derivation used an unusual sum rule for 6j symbols combined with the normalization properties of coefficients of fractional parentage. For four particles, the summation over J was inverted to sum over intermediate angular momenta J1 and J2 first, then an unusual sum rule for 9j symbols (due to Elbaz, using results from Dunlap and Judd) was applied to evaluate the resulting expression.

## Key numbers

* For three fermions in a single-j shell: N_tot(j,3) = (4j^2 - 1)/8; this yields 6 levels for j=7/2, 10 for j=9/2, 15 for j=11/2, 21 for j=13/2, and 28 for j=15/2.
* For four fermions in a single-j shell: N_tot(j,4) = (2j+1)/72 * [2j(4j+1) + 9] minus a term involving sums over 6j and 9j symbols; numerical values are 8 for j=7/2, 21 for j=9/2, 30 for j=11/2, 55 for j=13/2, and 86 for j=15/2.
* The difference between even-J and odd-J state counts for four fermions is given by N_even(j,4) - N_odd(j,4) = binom(2j+1, 2) = (2j-1)(2j+1)/2.

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to count spin-J states in single-j shells, notably the empirical formulas of Zhao and Arima (2003), the recursion relations of Talmi (2005), and the generating-function approach of Pain (2011). The author's earlier work on odd-even staggering (Pain, 2018) is used to separate even and odd J counts for four particles. The results enable determination of Hamiltonian matrix sizes in nuclear-structure calculations and can in principle be generalized to more particles, though the required unusual sum rules for higher-order 3nj symbols are not yet known.
