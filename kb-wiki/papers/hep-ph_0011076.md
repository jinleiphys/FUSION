# Instantons and color symmetry breaking in the vacuum

> The instanton interaction in QCD generates an effective potential for scalar quark-antiquark condensates in the color singlet and octet channels. For three light quark flavors the cubic term in this potential induces an octet condensate and ``spontaneous breaking'' of color in the vacuum. Realistic masses of the rho- and eta'-mesons are compatible with renormalization-group-improved instanton perturbation theory.

# Instantons and color symmetry breaking in the vacuum

## Key claim

The instanton-induced effective six-quark interaction in QCD generates a cubic term in the effective potential for scalar quark-antiquark condensates that drives spontaneous color symmetry breaking via an octet condensate, provided the gluon mass induced by that condensate acts as an infrared cutoff on the instanton size integral. The quantitative results depend on an unknown proportionality constant $c_\rho$ of order one that parametrizes how the gluon mass cuts off the instanton contribution.

## Method

The author starts from the 't Hooft instanton vertex for three massless flavors and performs partial bosonization to replace quark-antiquark bilinears by singlet and octet bosonic fields. The effective instanton potential is then evaluated along the direction of a color octet condensate $\bar\xi$, with the coefficient $\zeta(\bar\xi)$ computed from an integral over instanton sizes that is cut off at $\rho_{\rm max}(\bar\xi) = c_\rho/\mu_\rho$, where $\mu_\rho$ is the effective gauge boson mass proportional to $\bar\xi$ and the running coupling. The running coupling $\alpha(\mu)$ is taken in the $\overline{\rm MS}$-scheme at three-loop order with $\Lambda = 330$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- The maximum of the instanton size distribution $f(\rho)$ occurs at $\rho^{-1} = 613$ MeV.
- For $c_\rho = 1.0$, the vector-meson mass $\bar\mu_\rho = 0.65$ GeV; for $c_\rho = 1.2$, $\bar\mu_\rho = 0.74$ GeV; for $c_\rho = 1.4$, $\bar\mu_\rho = 0.84$ GeV.
- The resulting vector-meson mass range is $600$ MeV $\lesssim \bar\mu_\rho \lesssim 900$ MeV.
- The singlet chiral condensate $|<\bar\psi\psi>|^{1/3}$ is $0.28$ GeV for $c_\rho = 1.0$, $0.27$ GeV for $c_\rho = 1.2$, and $0.27$ GeV for $c_\rho = 1.4$, evaluated at $\bar\mu = 2$ GeV.
- The $\eta'$-meson mass is set to $M_{\eta'} = 960$ MeV for $r_0 = 0.5$ and $\kappa = 1$, using $f_\theta = 154$ MeV and $\tilde m_g = 340$ MeV.
- The two-photon decay width of the $\eta'$ is $\Gamma(\eta'\to 2\gamma) = 2.7$ keV.

## Context

This work extends the Higgs picture of confinement proposed by the author and collaborators (Wetterich, Mean), where color is spontaneously broken by an octet quark-antiquark condensate and gluons acquire masses identified with the $\rho$, $K^*$, and $\omega$ mesons. It provides a dynamical mechanism for generating that octet condensate via the 't Hooft instanton interaction (Hooft, Shifman), addressing two longstanding problems: the infrared cutoff for large instantons (now provided by the gluon mass) and the unboundedness of the naive instanton potential. The results enable a unified treatment of confinement and instanton physics, and the quantitative agreement with the $\rho$ and $\eta'$ masses motivates further detailed computation of the cutoff parameter $c_\rho$.
