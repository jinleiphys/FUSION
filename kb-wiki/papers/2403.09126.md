# Femtoscopic study of the $Λα$ interaction

> We examine the $Λ$-${}^4\mathrm{He}$ ($α$) momentum correlation in high-energy collisions to elucidate the interaction between Lambdas ($Λ$) and nucleons ($N$). We compare phenomenological $Λα$ potentials with different strengths at short range. In addition to the conventional Gaussian-type potentials, we construct the $Λα$ potentials by substituting the nucleon density distribution in $α$ into the Skyrme-type $Λ$ potentials. We find that the dependence on the employed potential models is visible in the correlation functions from a small-size source. This indicates that the $Λα$ momentum correlation could constrain the property of the $ΛN$ interaction at high densities, which is expected to play an essential role in dense nuclear matter. Also, we verify that the Lednicky-Lyuboshits formula can yield erroneous results for a small-size source with a potential which has a large interaction range, like the $Λα$ system.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the $\Lambda\alpha$ momentum correlation function from a small-size source (R = 1 fm) can distinguish between different $\Lambda\alpha$ potential models that have different short-range behavior, thereby constraining the $\Lambda N$ interaction at high densities; however, the authors note that for larger source sizes (R = 3 and 5 fm) the differences among models become almost negligible.

## Method

The authors calculated $\Lambda\alpha$ momentum correlation functions using the Koonin-Pratt formula with a spherical Gaussian source function, solving the relative Schrodinger equation for five different $\Lambda\alpha$ potential models. Two phenomenological Gaussian-type potentials (Isle and SG) were taken from prior work by Kumagai-Fuse (1994), while three new potentials (Chi3, LY-IV, and Chi3 w/o mom) were constructed by substituting the alpha-particle density distribution into Skyrme-type $\Lambda$ potentials from nuclear matter calculations, with all models fitted to reproduce the experimental $\Lambda$ binding energy of ${}^5_\Lambda \mathrm{He}$ (3.12 MeV). The correlation functions were compared with the Lednicky-Lyuboshits formula to test its validity for this system.

## Key numbers

- $\Lambda$ binding energy of ${}^5_\Lambda \mathrm{He}$: 3.12 MeV (experimental value used to fit all five potential models)
- Scattering lengths: Isle = 4.24 fm, SG = 3.79 fm, Chi3 = 4.01 fm, LY-IV = 3.89 fm, Chi3 w/o mom = 3.95 fm
- Effective ranges: Isle = 2.07 fm, SG = 1.56 fm, Chi3 = 1.84 fm, LY-IV = 1.70 fm, Chi3 w/o mom = 1.77 fm
- Alpha particle charge radius: 1.67824(83) fm (used to determine the width parameter $\nu = 0.20$ fm$^{-2}$)
- Source sizes studied: R = 1, 3, and 5 fm
- The LL formula severely underestimates the KP result for R = 1 fm in the momentum region q $\le$ 100 MeV/$c$

## Context

This work extends femtoscopy techniques, previously applied to baryon-baryon systems such as $p\Lambda$ (ALICE, 2018) and $\Lambda\Lambda$ (ALICE, 2019), to systems including light nuclei. It builds on phenomenological $\Lambda\alpha$ potentials from Kumagai-Fuse (1994) and Skyrme-type $\Lambda$ potentials from Lanskoy (1997) and Jinno (2023), connecting the $\Lambda\alpha$ interaction to the hyperon puzzle in neutron stars. The results enable future experimental measurements of $\Lambda\alpha$ correlations in high-energy collisions to discriminate between $\Lambda N$ interaction models that predict different high-density behavior, such as Chi3 (which avoids hyperon appearance in neutron stars) versus LY-IV (which allows hyperon appearance).
