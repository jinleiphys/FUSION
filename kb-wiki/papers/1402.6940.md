# Complete set of observables for photoproduction of two pseudoscalars on a nucleon

> The problem of determining completely the spin amplitudes of photoproduction of two pseudoscalar mesons on a nucleon from observables is studied. The procedure of reconstruction of the scattering matrix elements from a complete set of observables is based on the expressions of all observables as quadratic hermitean forms in the reaction matrix elements which are derived explicitly. Their inversion allows one to find explicit solutions for the reaction matrix elements in terms of observables. Two methods for finding a complete set of observables are presented. In particular, one set was found, which does not contain a triple polarization observable.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a complete set of 15 observables is sufficient to determine all eight complex spin amplitudes for photoproduction of two pseudoscalar mesons on a nucleon, up to an overall phase. Crucially, the authors show that one such complete set can be constructed without any triple polarization observable, contradicting earlier claims that at least one triple polarization observable would be required.

## Method
The authors derived explicit expressions for all 64 observables (differential cross section and recoil polarization components) as quadratic hermitean forms in the eight independent t-matrix elements. They then applied two distinct mathematical methods to invert these relations: the first method uses a Jacobian-based criterion to select a minimal set of observables, while the second method exploits graphical representations of bilinear t-matrix products to construct complete chains of interference terms. The inversion procedure yields explicit algebraic solutions for all t-matrix elements in terms of the chosen observables.

## Key numbers
- 8 independent complex t-matrix elements (with one arbitrary overall phase) constitute 15 independent real parameters to be determined
- 64 total observables exist (16 from differential cross section, 48 from recoil polarization components)
- A minimal complete set requires at least 15 observables
- One example complete set contains: 1 unpolarized observable, 4 single polarization, 8 double polarization, and 2 triple polarization observables
- The alternative complete set without triple polarization observables uses 8 observables from groups "A1" and "B" plus additional observables, with two quadratic relations (equations 4.13 and 4.15) allowing elimination of triple polarization terms

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework for model-independent amplitude reconstruction from photoproduction of two pseudoscalar mesons, building on the general expressions for polarization observables developed by Roberts and Oed (2005). The authors previously applied a truncated partial wave analysis to this problem (Fix and Arenhoevel, 2013). The methods are adapted from earlier work on deuteron electro- and photodisintegration by Arenhoevel, Leidemann, and Tomusiak (1998, 2000). The results enable complete experimental determination of reaction amplitudes at facilities such as MAMI, ELSA, and CEBAF, where precise polarization data are becoming available.
