# Neutrino-Oxygen interactions: role of nuclear physics in the atmospheric neutrino anomaly

> The apparent anomaly in the ratio of muon to electron atmospheric neutrinos first observed by Kamiokande and IMB has been confirmed by Super-Kamiokande and Soudan-2. The experimental analysis, including the asymmetry in the zenithal distributions of the $ μ-\mathrm{type} $ events in Super-Kamiokande gives a strong support to the neutrino oscillation hypothesis to solve the anomaly. In this work we are interested by the role of nuclear physics in the neutrino-oxygen reactions used to detect the atmospheric neutrinos. We point out that multi-nucleon excitations of np-nh type and that nuclear correlations could modify an experimental analysis à la Super-Kamiokande because they lead to a substantial enhancement of the number of 1 Čerenkov ring retained events.

## Key claim

Multi-nucleon excitations of np-nh type and nuclear correlations could modify an experimental analysis à la Super-Kamiokande because they lead to a substantial enhancement of the number of 1 Čerenkov ring retained events, potentially explaining the under-estimation of experimental ν_e + ν̄_e data by Monte-Carlo simulations.

## Method

The authors computed the inclusive charged-current cross section for neutrino-oxygen reactions at energies relevant for atmospheric neutrinos (∼1 GeV) using a semi-classical model based on local Fermi momentum from experimental nuclear density. They evaluated nuclear response functions in the isospin and spin-isospin channels, including "bare" responses and full RPA responses solved in the ring approximation with Landau-Migdal parameters, and incorporated multi-nucleon excitations (2p-2h and 3p-3h) and a modified Delta width in the nuclear medium. The differential cross sections were then folded with the Bartol atmospheric neutrino fluxes to obtain event yields, classifying events by number of Čerenkov rings under simplifying assumptions (neglecting pion re-interactions, nucleons above Čerenkov threshold, and particle misidentification).

## Key numbers

- The double ratio r = (R_μ/e)_exp / (R_μ/e)_MC is found to be of the order of ∼0.6.
- The maximal reduction factor of 1 Čerenkov ring events yields due to RPA correlations is of the order of 10%.
- At the maximum value of the yields, the enhancement of the total 1 Čerenkov ring yield with respect to the "bare" quasi-elastic one is around 20%.
- The threshold kinetic energy for a nucleon to produce a Čerenkov ring in water is about 0.5 GeV.
- The threshold for a pion to produce a Čerenkov ring in water is ∼70 MeV.
- The resonant Delta channel arises at high energy transfer (ω ∼ 450 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the study of the atmospheric neutrino anomaly, first observed by Kamiokande and IMB and confirmed by Super-Kamiokande and Soudan-2, by examining nuclear physics effects in neutrino-oxygen reactions that are typically neglected in simulations. The authors build on the semi-classical model developed by Delorme and Guichon (1990s) and the parameterization of the Delta width in the nuclear medium by Oset and Salcedo (1980s), as well as calculations of two-body pion absorption by Shimizu and Faessler (1980s). The results suggest that multi-nucleon excitations could modify analyses based on absolute event rates in Super-Kamiokande, and further investigation of pion absorption effects is needed to determine the full enhancement.
