# Neutron Transfer Dynamics and Doorway to Fusion in Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock Theory

> We analyze the details of mass exchange in the vicinity of the Coulomb barrier for heavy-ion collisions involving neutron-rich nuclei using the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory. We discuss the time-dependence of transfer and show that the potential barriers seen by individual single-particle states can be considerably different than the effective barrier for the two interacting nuclei having a single center-of-mass. For this reason we observe a substantial transfer probability even at energies below the effective barrier.

## Key claim
The paper shows that in TDHF collisions involving neutron-rich nuclei, individual single-particle states experience potential barriers that differ from the effective ion-ion barrier, leading to substantial neutron transfer even at energies below the effective Coulomb barrier.

## Method
The authors performed three-dimensional unrestricted TDHF calculations using the Skyrme SLy4 effective interaction, including all time-odd terms, with a mesh spacing of 1 fm. They analyzed head-on collisions for the 16O+24O system at center-of-mass energies from 6 to 9.5 MeV and for the 40Ca+96Zr system at 91 and 97 MeV, using density-constrained TDHF (DC-TDHF) to extract the effective potential barriers and examining partially integrated neutron densities and single-particle probability densities along the collision axis.

## Key numbers
- For the 16O+24O system, the DC-TDHF potential barrier peaks at about 8.4 MeV at a nuclear separation of 9.9 fm.
- Mass transfer values at sub-barrier energies for 16O+24O: 0.0022 at 6 MeV, 0.024 at 7 MeV, 0.1464 at 8 MeV, and 0.6768 at 9 MeV.
- For 16O+24O, the distance of closest approach at 7, 8, and 9 MeV is 13.2 fm, 11.6 fm, and 10.1 fm, respectively.
- For 16O+24O, kinetic energy dissipation ranges from 0.05 MeV at 6 MeV to 0.40 MeV at 9 MeV.
- For the 40Ca+96Zr system, the DC-TDHF barrier peak is about 95 MeV at a separation of about 11.5 fm.
- For 40Ca+96Zr at 97 MeV, the integrated neutron transfer at an ion-ion separation of about 12 fm is approximately 0.5.
- For 40Ca+96Zr, the 2d5/2 state has single-particle quadrupole moments of 11.8, 3.7, and -15.6 fm2 for its three substates.

## Context
This work extends the findings of ZSW07, who studied neutron transfer in the 40Ca+96Zr system using a semi-empirical time-dependent three-body Schrodinger equation, by confirming those results with the fully microscopic TDHF approach. The paper also builds on earlier TDHF studies of dynamical charge equilibration by SC01, SC07, and US85, and on the DC-TDHF method developed in UO06b. The results enable improved fusion barrier calculations that incorporate neutron transfer effects and single-particle state properties, which are necessary for understanding fusion of neutron-rich nuclei and superheavy element synthesis.
