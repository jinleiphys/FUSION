# Diquark Bose-Einstein Condensation and Nuclear Matter

> We study a possible transition between symmetric nuclear matter and the diquark Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) matter at zero temperature. We find that chiral restoration transition is first order and coincides with deconfinement. We investigate various possible coexistence patterns which may emerge from the first order deconfinement phase transition by assuming different values for the critical deconfinement chemical potential. If deconfinement takes place at higher chemical potential, there exists a mixed phase of nuclear and chirally restored diquark BEC matter. This coexistence region extends over a large density region for a bigger diquark BEC or a weaker diquark-diquark interaction. For model parameters with heavy diquark in vaccum, phase transition to diquark matter becomes of second-order. We also show that in the case of precocious deconfinement, droplets of nucleons and droplets of chirally restored Bose-Einstein condensed diquarks coexist surrounded by non-trivial vacuum. We show that a early deconfinement and a weak repulsive diquark-diquark interaction soften the equation of state. We propose a scenario in which nuclear matter saturates due to the formation of the diquark BEC and deconfinement phenomena. In this picture, instead of repulsive vector-meson exchange the compressibility of the equation of state is related to a repulsive diquark-diquark interaction. In general, we emphasize the importance of a diquark BEC phase at rather low density before quark BCS-pairing transition.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that nuclear matter saturation may be driven by the formation of a diquark Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) and deconfinement, rather than by repulsive vector-meson exchange; however, this scenario depends on the specific choice of the critical deconfinement chemical potential and the diquark-diquark interaction strength, and the authors emphasize that the diquark BEC phase may appear at rather low density before the quark BCS-pairing transition.

## Method

The authors model symmetric nuclear matter using a chiral sigma-omega model with exact SU(2)×SU(2) symmetry, including nucleons, pions, sigma, and omega fields, and treat the Dirac sea with a nucleonic ultraviolet cutoff of 324 MeV. For the deconfined phase, they describe diquarks as complex scalar fields in the color anti-triplet representation, with a repulsive diquark-diquark interaction and a dynamically generated mass via coupling to the sigma field, and compute the one-loop effective potential with a sharp momentum cutoff of 900 MeV. The phase transition between hadronic and diquark BEC matter is determined by imposing Gibbs conditions (equal pressure and chemical potential) at a chosen critical baryonic chemical potential, with the diquark chemical potential related to the baryonic one by μ = (2/3)μ_b.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation is reproduced at a binding energy per nucleon of E_B/A = -15.75 MeV at a Fermi momentum k_F = 256 MeV (density ρ_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}).
- The compression modulus at saturation for the hadronic phase is K = 455 MeV.
- The critical deconfinement chemical potential is taken as μ_b^⋆ = 1214.5 MeV, corresponding to a hadronic density ρ_b^h = 3.0 ρ_0.
- At this transition point, the hadronic pressure is P_h = 89.8 MeV/fm^3.
- For parameter set A1, the diquark mass in vacuum is m_d(ρ=0) = 637.1 MeV, with diquark-diquark coupling g = 14.1 and diquark-sigma coupling g_d = 6.85.
- For parameter set A4, the diquark mass in vacuum is m_d(ρ=0) = 558.0 MeV, with g = 11.23 and g_d = 6.0, and the diquark density at the transition is ρ_b^d = 8.6 ρ_0.
- The speed of sound in the diquark BEC phase at mean-field level is v_s^2 = sqrt((μ^2 - m_d^2)/(3μ^2 - m_d^2)), approaching the relativistic value v_s = 1/√3 at very high density (μ ≫ m_d).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of diquark dynamics in nuclear matter, such as the covariant diquark-quark picture for baryons (n-d) and the description of nuclear matter properties using nucleons as relativistic bound states of diquarks and quarks (rp, bw). It also connects to the broader investigation of color superconductivity (arw, br) and the BEC-BCS crossover in QCD. The paper proposes a non-conventional saturation mechanism that could reduce the unphysically high compression modulus found in the sigma-omega model of Boguta (bog) and the Walecka model, and it enables future studies of the interplay between deconfinement, chiral restoration, and diquark condensation at moderate densities relevant for neutron stars and heavy-ion collisions.
