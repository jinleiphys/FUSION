# Scaling of hadron masses and widths in thermal models for ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> By means of a simple rescaling, modifications of hadron masses and widths are incorporated into the thermal analysis of particle ratios in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We find that moderate, up to 20%, changes of hadron masses do not spoil the quality of the fits, which remain as good as those obtained without modifications. The fits with the modified masses yield, however, modified values of the optimal temperature and baryon chemical potential. In particular, with decreasing masses of all hadrons (except for pseudo-Goldstone bosons) the fitted values of the temperature and the baryon chemical potential are lowered, with the change approximately proportional to the scaling of masses. In addition, we find that the broadening of the hadron widths by less than a factor of two practically does not affect the fits.

# Scaling of hadron masses and widths in thermal models for ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

Moderate (up to 20%) rescaling of hadron masses (except pseudo-Goldstone bosons) does not spoil the quality of thermal-model fits to particle ratios, but the fitted temperature and baryon chemical potential change approximately proportionally to the mass scaling factor. The broadening of hadron widths by less than a factor of two has practically no effect on the fits.

## Method

The authors used the ideal-gas expression for particle densities at chemical freeze-out, with in-medium hadron masses parameterized by universal scaling factors x_M for mesons and x_B for baryons (excluding pions, kaons, and eta mesons). They performed chi-squared minimization fits to 19 measured particle ratios from Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies, allowing temperature and baryon chemical potential to vary, while strangeness and isospin chemical potentials were fixed by conservation laws. For width effects, they introduced a scaling parameter y for all hadron widths and used a relativistic Breit-Wigner spectral function in the density integrals.

## Key numbers

- For Pb+Pb at SPS with no mass scaling (x_M = x_B = 1): T_chem = 169 MeV, mu_B = 250 MeV, mu_S = 65 MeV, mu_I = -9 MeV
- These values agree with prior results from BM3 (2000): T_chem = 168 MeV, mu_B = 266 MeV, mu_S = 71 MeV, mu_I = -5 MeV
- Mass scaling of x_M = x_B = 0.9 (10% decrease) yields a slightly better chi-squared value than the unscaled fit
- Width scaling by a factor y = 2 (doubling of vacuum widths) has little effect on chi-squared, temperature, or baryon density

## Context

This work extends the thermal-model analyses of particle ratios in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, previously performed by BM3 (2000) and others, by incorporating in-medium modifications of hadron properties predicted by model calculations (Rapp and Wambach, 1999; Hatsuda and Lee, 1992; Klingl and Weise, 1997). It generalizes earlier studies by Florkowski and Broniowski (2000) that examined mass modifications without refitting thermodynamic parameters. The finding that lower masses yield lower fitted temperatures is desirable because the standard T_chem ~ 170 MeV is close to the expected deconfinement temperature and may correspond to quark-gluon plasma rather than hadron gas. The results enable future work to incorporate more realistic, density-dependent in-medium hadron properties into thermal fits.
