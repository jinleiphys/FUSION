# Bottomonium suppression in an open quantum system using the quantum trajectories method

> We solve the Lindblad equation describing the Brownian motion of a Coulombic heavy quark-antiquark pair in a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma using the highly efficient Monte Carlo wave-function method. The Lindblad equation has been derived in the framework of pNRQCD and fully accounts for the quantum and non-Abelian nature of the system. The hydrodynamics of the plasma is realistically implemented through a 3+1D dissipative hydrodynamics code. We compute the bottomonium nuclear modification factor and compare with the most recent LHC data. The computation does not rely on any free parameter, as it depends on two transport coefficients that have been evaluated independently in lattice QCD. Our final results, which include late-time feed down of excited states, agree well with the available data from LHC 5.02 TeV PbPb collisions.

## Key claim

The authors solve the Lindblad equation for bottomonium suppression using the Monte Carlo wave-function method, achieving agreement with LHC 5.02 TeV PbPb collision data without any free parameters, but only under the specific hierarchy condition that the inverse Bohr radius is much larger than the temperature and Debye mass, which in turn are much larger than the binding energy.

## Method

The authors implement the Monte Carlo wave-function (quantum trajectories) method to solve the Lindblad equation derived from potential Non-Relativistic QCD (pNRQCD) for a Coulombic heavy quark-antiquark pair in a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma. Between quantum jumps, the wave-function is evolved using a split-step pseudospectral method with Fourier sine transforms, while the medium evolution is modeled using a 3+1D dissipative hydrodynamics code. The calculation uses two transport coefficients (the heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient κ and its dispersive counterpart γ) that have been evaluated independently in lattice QCD, and includes late-time feed-down from excited bottomonium states.

## Key numbers

- The lattice spacing used is 0.1 times the Bohr radius of the Υ(1S) (a₀), with a lattice size of 40 times a₀ in previous work; the new method allows increasing volume without the same computational cost scaling
- The computation uses LHC data from 5.02 TeV PbPb collisions for comparison
- The probability for an octet state to jump to a singlet state is 2/(N_c² - 2), where N_c is the number of colors
- The split-step DST evolution scales as O(N log N) where N is the number of lattice points, compared to O(N²) for a Crank-Nicolson scheme with the same derivative stencil

## Context

This work extends the open quantum system framework developed by Brambilla et al. (2016, 2017) for quarkonium evolution in the quark-gluon plasma, which was previously limited by computational cost to S-wave and P-wave states with simplified temperature profiles. The new method enables realistic 3+1D dissipative hydrodynamics and inclusion of all orbital angular momenta, building on the pNRQCD approach that originated from the work of Matsui and Satz (1986) on quarkonium suppression as a QGP signal. The results enable parameter-free comparisons with LHC data and can be applied to other heavy quarkonium systems.
