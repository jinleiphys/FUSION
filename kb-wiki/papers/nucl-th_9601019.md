# Modeling Cluster Production at the AGS

> Deuteron coalescence, during relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions, is carried out in a model incorporating a minimal quantal treatment of the formation of the cluster from its individual nucleons by evaluating the overlap of intial cascading nucleon wave packets with the final deuteron wave function. In one approach the nucleon and deuteron center of mass wave packet sizes are estimated dynamically for each coalescing pair using its past light-cone history in the underlying cascade, a procedure which yields a parameter free determination of the cluster yield. A modified version employing a global estimate of the deuteron formation probability, is identical to a general implementation of the Wigner function formalism but can differ from the most frequent realisation of the latter. Comparison is made both with the extensive existing E802 data for Si+Au at 14.6 GeV/c and with the Wigner formalism. A globally consistent picture of the Si+Au measurements is achieved. In light of the deuteron's evident fragility, information obtained from this analysis may be useful in establishing freeze-out volumes and help in heralding the presence of high-density phenomena in a baryon-rich environment.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a parameter-free, dynamically determined wave-packet size for each coalescing nucleon pair (ARC Dynamic) can reproduce absolute deuteron yields and transverse-mass spectra in Si+Au collisions at 14.6 GeV/c, but the abstract's claim of a "globally consistent picture" hedges on the fact that differences between theory and experiment are typically ~10% and occasionally as large as 30-50%.

## Method

The authors embed a quantum-mechanical coalescence model into the relativistic cascade ARC, evaluating the squared overlap between Gaussian wave packets for the neutron and proton and a Gaussian approximation to the deuteron wave function. In the "Dynamic" approach, the wave-packet size for each nucleon pair is estimated from the rms radius of particles in the backward light-cone of the coalescence point; in the "Static" approach, a global wave-packet size is used. The coalescence probability is then applied via Monte Carlo to np pairs whose last interactions have ceased, and the nucleons are replaced by a deuteron if coalescence occurs.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron internal wave-function size parameter α = 1.76 fm, yielding a deuteron charge radius r_d = 2.15 fm; the point-nucleon radius after correcting for proton charge radius (r_p ~ 0.8 fm) is r_d = 1.91 fm with α = 1.56 fm.
- Theory-experiment differences for deuteron yields are generally ~10%, with rare discrepancies as large as 30-50%.
- The reaction studied is Si+Au at 14.6 GeV/c, comparing to E802 data for both central and peripheral collisions.

## Context

This work extends earlier coalescence models (Butler and Pearson 1961, Schwarzschild and Zupancic 1963, Remler 1981, Bond 1980, Mekjian 1978, Kapusta 1980, Gyulassy et al. 1983, Dover et al. 1991) by incorporating a quantum-mechanical overlap integral with dynamically determined wave-packet sizes, improving on the authors' previous cutoff-model treatment of the H-dibaryon (Kahana and Dover 1989). The approach enables extraction of freeze-out volumes from absolute deuteron yields, complementary to HBT measurements, and could signal unusual high-density phenomena if discrepancies with cascade predictions arise.
