# Heavy Quark Dynamics in Heavy Ion Reactions

> Collisions between heavy nuclei at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider liberate from the nuclear wavefunction of order 10,000 gluons, quarks and antiquarks. The system is dominated by gluons and up and down (anti) quarks. Heavy quarks, though having little effect on the overall equation of state, are critical as probes of the surrounding medium. We compare predictions from a scenario where the charm quarks escape the medium unaffected and fragment into hadrons in vacuum, and one where the charm quarks are swallowed up by the medium and reflect the overall system hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

Heavy quarks in heavy ion collisions at RHIC may either escape the medium unaffected and fragment in vacuum, or be absorbed by the medium and follow the system's hydrodynamics; current data cannot distinguish between these two scenarios due to large systematic uncertainties and the possibility that heavy quarks that lose energy are then re-accelerated by the collective flow.

## Method

The author compares two models for charm quark behavior in Au-Au collisions at RHIC: a PYTHIA-based pQCD calculation where charm quarks fragment in vacuum, and a hydrodynamic parameterization where charm quarks lose energy but then receive a transverse velocity boost from the expanding medium. The hydrodynamic model is fit to PHENIX measured pion, kaon, and proton spectra to extract flow parameters, then applied to D and B mesons with no additional parameters, normalizing the total yield to PYTHIA predictions. The resulting single electron spectra from semi-leptonic decays are compared to PHENIX data.

## Key numbers

- Charm cross section of 330 μb at √s = 130 GeV and 650 μb at √s = 200 GeV (with ±50% uncertainty) from PYTHIA
- Energy loss values considered: dE/dx = -0.5, -1.0, -2.0 GeV/fm in Au-Au reactions at RHIC
- dE/dx = -2.0 GeV/fm leads to an order of magnitude suppression in intermediate pT charm quarks
- Hydrodynamic fit parameters: temperature T = 128 MeV, βmax = 0.65 for 0-5% central Au-Au, decreasing to βmax = 0.25 for 60-92% central
- Number of binary collisions: Nbinary = 975 for 10% most central events, Nbinary = 71 for 40-70% peripheral events
- Charm quark with pT ≈ 3.0 GeV has velocity v ≈ 0.92c

## Context

This work extends the discussion of parton energy loss in heavy ion collisions, following observations of high-pT hadron suppression at RHIC by PHENIX (2002). It contrasts with the calculation by Lin et al. (2002) that assumed stopped charm quarks receive a simple thermal distribution, instead proposing that re-scattering in the hydrodynamic flow can boost heavy quarks to higher pT. The "dead-cone" effect proposed by Dokshitzer and Kharzeev (2001) and the Ter-Mikayelian effect are discussed as possible explanations for the lack of observed heavy quark energy loss. The paper identifies anisotropic flow (v2) measurements of charm mesons or J/ψ as a definitive test to discriminate between transparent and opaque medium scenarios.
