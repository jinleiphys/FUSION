# Screening of heavy quark free energies at finite temperature and non-zero baryon chemical potential

> We analyze the dependence of heavy quark free energies on the baryon chemical potential (mu_b) in 2-flavour QCD using improved (p4) staggered fermions with a bare quark mass of m/T = 0.4. By performing a 6th order Taylor expansion in the chemical potential which circumvents the sign problem. The Taylor expansion coefficients of colour singlet and colour averaged free energies are calculated and from this the expansion coefficients for the corresponding screening masses are determined. We find that for small mu_b the free energies of a static quark anti-quark pair decrease in a medium with a net excess of quarks and that screening is well described by a screening mass which increases with increasing mu_b. The mu_b-dependent corrections to the screening masses are well described by perturbation theory for T > 2 T_c. In particular, we find for all temperatures above T_c that the expansion coefficients for singlet and colour averaged screening masses differ by a factor 2.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for small baryon chemical potential (μ_b), the free energies of a static quark-antiquark pair decrease in a medium with a net excess of quarks, and that screening is well described by a screening mass which increases with increasing μ_b, with the μ_b-dependent corrections to the screening masses being well described by perturbation theory for temperatures above 2 T_c.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with 2-flavour improved (p4) staggered fermions on a 16^3 × 4 lattice with bare quark mass m/T = 0.4, using a 6th order Taylor expansion in the quark chemical potential to circumvent the sign problem. They calculated the Taylor expansion coefficients for colour singlet and colour averaged Polyakov loop correlation functions, then extracted the corresponding expansion coefficients for screening masses from the large-distance behaviour of the free energies. The analysis used 1000 to 4000 gauge field configurations at various temperatures, with additional configurations generated at T = 3 T_c and T = 4 T_c.

## Key numbers

- The bare quark mass is m/T = 0.4, corresponding to a pion mass of about 770 MeV.
- The lattice size is 16^3 × 4, with temporal extent N_τ = 4.
- The analysis used 1000 up to 4000 gauge field configurations at several bare gauge couplings, plus 1000 configurations at T = 3 T_c and 1600 at T = 4 T_c.
- The fit for the leading order screening mass yields A = 1.397(18) for the parameter in m^1_0(T) = A · (2/√3) g(T) T.
- The ratio m^1_2(T)/m^1_0(T) reaches the perturbative value of 3/(8π^2) for T/T_c ≥ 2.
- The free energies were calculated for μ/T values from 0.0 up to 0.8.
- The smallest distance at which matching to the T=0 potential could be performed is r_0 = 1/(4T).

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of heavy quark free energies at zero baryon density (fthq2, 2004) to non-zero baryon chemical potential using Taylor expansion methods previously applied to the equation of state (eos, 2004). The results enable non-perturbative determination of screening properties in a baryon-rich quark-gluon plasma and show that the leading order perturbative prediction for the Debye mass dependence on chemical potential (debye-mu) works well for T ≥ 2 T_c, while also confirming that the colour averaged screening mass corrections are twice those of the singlet channel, consistent with two-gluon exchange dominance.
