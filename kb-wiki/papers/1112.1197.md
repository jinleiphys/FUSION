# Sum rules for hadronic contributions to low-energy light-by-light scattering

> We present a set of sum rules relating the low-energy light-by-light scattering to integrals of γγ-fusion cross sections and use them to study the hadronic contributions.

## Key claim

The paper derives sum rules that relate low-energy light-by-light scattering constants to integrals of \(\gamma\gamma\)-fusion cross sections, and shows that these sum rules can be used to study hadronic contributions, but the saturation of the superconvergence sum rule by resonance contributions is only approximate (e.g., the \(a_2(1320)\) tensor meson compensates only about 70% of the \(\pi^0\) contribution).

## Method

The authors use analyticity, crossing symmetry, and the optical theorem to derive dispersion relations for forward \(\gamma\gamma\to\gamma\gamma\) helicity amplitudes. Expanding these dispersion relations in powers of the Mandelstam variable \(s\) and matching to the Euler-Heisenberg Lagrangian yields sum rules for the low-energy constants \(c_1\) and \(c_2\) and a superconvergence sum rule for the helicity-difference cross section. The sum rules are verified in perturbative QED for scalar, spinor, and spin-1 (massive vector boson) loop calculations, and then applied to hadronic contributions using Breit-Wigner parametrizations of meson production cross sections.

## Key numbers

* For scalar QED: \(c_1 = 7\alpha^2/(1440 m^4)\), \(c_2 = \alpha^2/(1440 m^4)\); for spinor QED: \(c_1 = \alpha^2/(90 m^4)\), \(c_2 = 7\alpha^2/(360 m^4)\); these agree with one-loop calculations.
* For spin-1 QED (Standard Model parameters \(l_1=1\), \(l_2=0\)): \(c_1 = 29\alpha^2/(160 M^4)\), \(c_2 = 27\alpha^2/(160 M^4)\).
* Pseudoscalar meson contributions to the superconvergence sum rule: \(\pi^0\) gives \(-195.0 \pm 15.0\) nb; \(\eta\) gives \(-190.7 \pm 11.2\) nb; \(\eta^\prime\) gives \(-301.0 \pm 10.5\) nb; sum of \(\eta,\eta^\prime\) gives \(-492 \pm 22\) nb.
* Tensor meson contributions to the superconvergence sum rule (Breit-Wigner): \(a_2(1320)\) gives \(137 \pm 8\) nb; \(f_2(1270)\) gives \(479 \pm 56\) nb; \(f_2^\prime(1525)\) gives \(7 \pm 1\) nb; sum of \(f_2,f_2^\prime\) gives \(486 \pm 57\) nb.
* Pseudoscalar meson contributions to low-energy constants: \(\pi^0\) gives \(c_2 = 10.8 \times 10^{-4}\) GeV\(^{-4}\); \(\eta\) gives \(c_2 = 0.7 \times 10^{-4}\) GeV\(^{-4}\); \(\eta^\prime\) gives \(c_2 = 0.4 \times 10^{-4}\) GeV\(^{-4}\); all give \(c_1 = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the classic sum-rule approach of Baldin (1960), Gerasimov (1965), and Drell and Hearn (1966) from nucleon Compton scattering to the \(\gamma\gamma\) system, and builds on the systematic derivation by Pascalutsa (2010) for light-by-light scattering. The sum rules enable a data-driven evaluation of hadronic contributions to low-energy photon-photon scattering, which is relevant for the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment \((g-2)_\mu\), a problem where the main effect comes from hadronic scales (Melnikov, 2006). The paper also shows that the sum rules can constrain transition form factors of mesons, such as the relation \(F_{a_2\gamma^*\gamma}^{\Lambda=2}(Q^2) \approx \frac{1}{2} F_{\pi^0\gamma^*\gamma}(Q^2)\) at low \(Q^2\).
