# Unitarity and the color confinement

> We discuss how confinement property of QCD results in the rational unitarization scheme and how unitarity saturation leads to appearance of a hadron liquid phase at very high temperatures.

# Unitarity and the color confinement

## Key claim

The authors argue that the confinement property of QCD can lead to a rational (U-matrix) unitarization scheme for the scattering matrix, and that saturation of unitarity at very high energies would restore confinement rather than produce a deconfined quark-gluon plasma, resulting instead in a hadron liquid phase.

## Method

The authors extend an approach by Bogolyubov (1960s) by decomposing the total state vector into physical hadron states (Hilbert space H_phi) and confined quark/gluon states (Hilbert space H_omega), imposing the confinement condition that in- and out-vectors for confined objects are mirror reflections (|omega,in> + |omega,out> = 0). From this non-unitary scattering operator in the total space, they derive a unitary scattering operator S = (1-U)(1+U)^{-1} acting in the physical Hilbert space, where U is the generalized reaction matrix associated with confined constituent dynamics. They then analyze the impact-parameter representation of elastic scattering and the resulting reflective scattering mode when U(s,b) exceeds unity.

## Key numbers

* The reflective scattering mode appears at the energy s_R determined by U(s_R, b=0) = 1.
* At very high energies (s → infinity), the hadron liquid density n_R(T,mu) ~ 1/kappa(s) ~ M^3 / ln^3 s, where M is a mass scale related to the pion mass.
* The reflective scattering radius R(s) grows logarithmically: R(s) ~ (1/M) ln s.
* The confinement scale Lambda_QCD is given as 100-300 MeV, while the chiral symmetry breaking scale Lambda_chi is approximately 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the rational unitarization scheme of Heitler (1940s) and Bogolyubov (1960s) by providing a physical interpretation connecting the U-matrix to confined quark and gluon degrees of freedom. It contradicts the standard expectation that high temperatures lead to a quark-gluon plasma (deconfinement), instead proposing a return to a confined hadron liquid phase via unitarity saturation. The authors build on percolation-based deconfinement models by Satz (2000s) and the van der Waals approach used by Cleymans (1980s) and Limdens (2000s), but argue that percolation alone is insufficient and that reflective scattering provides a mechanism for confinement restoration at very high energies. The paper enables future experimental searches at LHC heavy-ion collisions for new phases of hadronic matter beyond the quark-gluon plasma.
