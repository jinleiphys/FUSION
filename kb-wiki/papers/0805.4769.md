# High-density QCD with CMS at the LHC

> The capabilities of the CMS experiment to explore the rich heavy-ion physics programme offered by the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC) are summarised. Various representative measurements in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.5 TeV are covered. These include "bulk" observables (charged hadron multiplicity, low-pT inclusive hadron spectra and elliptic flow) which provide information on the collective properties of the system; as well as perturbative processes (high-pT hadrons, jets, gamma-jet and quarkonium production) which yield "tomographic" information of the densest phases of the reaction.

## Key claim

The CMS experiment can carry out a comprehensive heavy-ion physics program at the LHC, covering both soft bulk observables and hard perturbative probes, but the actual reach for many measurements depends on the High Level Trigger system's ability to process every Pb-Pb event with offline-quality algorithms, yielding a factor of 20 to 300 larger statistical reach for hard probes compared to minimum-bias triggering.

## Method

The paper presents full detector simulation and reconstruction studies for Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV using the CMS apparatus, which features a 4 T solenoid, silicon pixel and microstrip tracking, electromagnetic and hadronic calorimetry, and muon detectors. The authors evaluate the expected performance for soft observables (charged hadron multiplicity via hit counting in the innermost pixel layer, identified hadron spectra via dE/dx in silicon pixels, and elliptic flow via reaction plane determination from multiple detector subsystems) and for hard probes (high-pT hadrons, full jet reconstruction with iterative cone algorithm, gamma-jet fragmentation functions, quarkonium dimuon decays, and exclusive Upsilon photoproduction in ultraperipheral collisions).

## Key numbers

- Charged hadron multiplicity expected at LHC: dN/dη|η=0 ≈ 1200–2000 for central Pb-Pb collisions
- Bjorken energy density estimate: ε_Bjorken ≈ 10 GeV/fm³ at initial time τ0 = 1 fm/c
- Hard probe cross sections at LHC are 10 to 10⁴ times larger than at RHIC
- CMS tracking efficiency: 60% above pT > 1 GeV/c with fake track rate below 5% for central Pb-Pb
- Transverse momentum resolution better than 2% up to highest pT values within |η| < 1
- Leading hadron suppression measurable up to 300 GeV/c
- Jet reconstruction: jets distinguishable above background at ET ∼ 30 GeV, fully reconstructed above 75 GeV with energy resolution better than 15%
- Jet ET reach up to 0.5 TeV in central Pb-Pb with 0.5 nb⁻¹ integrated luminosity
- Expected gamma-jet events: 4300 per Pb-Pb year at nominal luminosity for ETγ > 70 GeV
- CMS dimuon mass resolution: 54 MeV/c² at the Upsilon mass in central barrel (|η| < 0.8)
- Expected quarkonium yields in one month (0.5 nb⁻¹): 1.8×10⁵ J/psi and 2.5×10⁴ Upsilon states
- Exclusive Upsilon photoproduction: expected ∼400 Upsilon/year in dielectron and dimuon channels
- Low-x values probed in UPC quarkonium photoproduction: x ∼ 10⁻⁴

## Context

This work extends the heavy-ion physics program established at RHIC (where strong collective flow and jet quenching were discovered) to the much higher energy densities and hard probe rates available at the LHC. The paper builds on prior CMS simulation studies (D'Enterria 2007) and more recent developments (Loizides 2008), and compares expected LHC results to theoretical models such as GLV (Vitev 2002) for parton energy loss with gluon densities dNg/dy = 2000–4000, and PQM (Loizides 2006) with transport coefficient qhat ≈ 30–80 GeV²/fm. The measurements will enable detailed tomographic studies of quark-gluon matter properties including viscosity, transport coefficient, and critical energy density, as well as constraints on low-x nuclear parton distribution functions through exclusive vector meson photoproduction.
