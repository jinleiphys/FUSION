# Neutron matter with a model interaction

> An infinite system of neutrons interacting by a model pair potential is considered. We investigate a case when this potential is sufficiently strong attractive, so that its scattering length tends to infinity. It appeared, that if the structure of the potential is simple enough, including no finite parameters, reliable evidences can be presented that such a system is completely unstable at any finite density. The incompressibility as a function of the density is negative, reaching zero value when the density tends to zero. If the potential contains a sufficiently strong repulsive core then the system possesses an equilibrium density. The main features of a theory describing such systems are considered.

## Key claim

For an infinite system of neutrons interacting via a purely attractive model potential whose scattering length tends to infinity (a → −∞), the system is completely unstable at any finite density, with negative incompressibility that reaches zero only as density tends to zero; however, if the potential contains a sufficiently strong repulsive core, the system can possess an equilibrium density.

## Method

The authors model the ground state energy density E(ρ) of a homogeneous infinite neutron system using a Skyrme-like functional (kinetic term plus t₀ρ² and t₃ρ⁷/³ terms). They analyze the incompressibility K(ρ) = ρ² d²E/dρ² as a function of density, identifying the critical densities ρc1 and ρc2 where K=0, which separate stable, unstable, and restabilized regimes. The analysis is extended to the case where the scattering length a is the only dominant parameter, using a dimensionless expansion of the ground state energy in powers of z = pF a.

## Key numbers

- The scattering length of neutron-neutron interaction is about −20 fm, greatly surpassing the interaction radius r₀.
- For a system with a pure attractive potential and a → −∞, the critical density ρc1 scales as ρc1 ∼ |a|⁻³.
- In the low-density expansion of the ground state energy, the leading correction term is (10/9π)z, where z = pF a.
- For liquid ³He, the dimer binding energy does not exceed 10⁻⁴ meV while the ground state energy per atom is about 2 × 10⁻¹ meV.

## Context

This work extends the analytical treatment of strongly interacting fermion systems beyond the dilute gas approximation (Lifshitz and Pitaevskii) and the Random Phase Approximation for electron gas (Gell-Mann and Brueckner). The authors argue that their qualitative analysis applies to neutron matter (where the neutron-neutron scattering length is large) and to trapped Fermi atomic gases with tunable interactions, similar to experiments on Bose gases. The paper identifies the need for self-consistent calculations that satisfy the relation between the linear response function and incompressibility, pointing to prior work by Shaginyan and collaborators on functional equations for the effective interaction.
