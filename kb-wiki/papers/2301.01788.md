# Nucleon Energy Correlators for the Color Glass Condensate

> We demonstrate the recently proposed nucleon energy-energy correlator (nucleon EEC) $f_{\rm EEC}(x,θ)$ can unveil the gluon saturation in the small-$x$ regime in $eA$ collisions. The novelty of this probe is that it is fully inclusive just like the deep-inelastic scattering (DIS), with no requirements of jets or hadrons, but still provides an evident portal to the small-$x$ dynamics through the shape of the $θ$-distribution. We find that the saturation prediction is significantly different from the expectation of the collinear factorization.

## Key claim

The nucleon energy-energy correlator (NEEC) $f_{\rm EEC}(x,\theta)$ can reveal gluon saturation in the small-$x$ regime of $eA$ collisions through the shape of the $\theta$-distribution, with the key condition that the measurement is fully inclusive like deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) and requires no jet or hadron identification, yet the saturation prediction differs significantly from collinear factorization expectations.

## Method

The authors compute the weighted cross section $\Sigma(Q^2,x_B,\theta)$ for unpolarized DIS $l + A \to l' + X$ in the Breit frame, measuring Bjorken $x_B$, photon virtuality $Q^2$, and the energy deposited at angle $\theta$ relative to the beam. They derive the NEEC $f_{\rm EEC}(x,\theta)$ in the small-$x$ regime using the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory, evaluating the dipole distribution with both the MV model with rcBK running and the GBW model. The collinear factorization prediction is obtained from fixed-order $\alpha_s$ calculations using CT18A and EPPS21 PDF sets, with validation from Pythia8 simulations.

## Key numbers

- For proton at $x_B = 0.003$, $Q^2 = 25\ {\rm GeV}^2$, $\sqrt{s} = 105\ {\rm GeV}$: the turning point in the $\theta^2\Sigma$ distribution is roughly at $\theta \sim 0.15 - 0.2$, giving $Q_s \sim \theta Q \sim 0.75 - 1.0\ {\rm GeV}$
- For Au at $x_B = 0.003$, $Q^2 = 25\ {\rm GeV}^2$, $\sqrt{s} = 105\ {\rm GeV}$: the turning point is around $\theta \sim 0.4 - 0.5$, giving $Q_s \sim \theta Q \sim 2 - 2.5\ {\rm GeV}$
- For Au at $x_B = 0.003$, $Q^2 = 100\ {\rm GeV}^2$, $\sqrt{s} = 318\ {\rm GeV}$: the turning point is around $\theta \sim 0.2 - 0.3$, giving $Q_s \sim 2 - 3\ {\rm GeV}$
- The MV model initial condition uses $Q_{s0}^2 = A^{1/3} \times 0.168\ {\rm GeV}^2$ with $\Lambda = 0.241\ {\rm GeV}$, $\gamma = 1.119$, $x_0 = 0.01$
- The GBW model uses $x_0 = 2.24 \times 10^{-4}$, $\lambda = 0.27$, with $A_N = 1$ for proton and $A_N = 5$ for Au

## Context

This work extends the nucleon energy-energy correlator (NEEC) concept proposed by Liu et al. (2022) to probe gluon saturation in electron-nucleus collisions, building on the CGC framework established by McLerran et al. (1993-1994) and the BK-JIMWLK evolution equations. The approach differs from previous saturation probes such as di-jet/di-hadron azimuthal correlations studied by Dominguez et al. (2010-2011) and Mueller et al. (2013), and from TMD-based measurements, because the NEEC is fully inclusive like DIS structure functions studied by Albacete et al. (2010) and Lappi et al. (2013), yet provides angular sensitivity through the $\theta$ distribution. The results enable future EIC experiments to extract the saturation scale $Q_s$ from the turning point in the $\theta^2\Sigma$ distribution, with the nuclear modification factor $R_{pA}$ providing a systematic-reduced observable.
