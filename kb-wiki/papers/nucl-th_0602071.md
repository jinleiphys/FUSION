# On the stability of Quantum Hadro-Dynamics

> We explore the possible occurrence of $σ$-$ω$ condensation in the Quantum Hadro-Dynamics (QHD), namely the Serot and Walecka model, finding that at the mean field level it corresponds to a critical value of the coupling constant $g_σ=8.828$ and density $k_F=207.2$ MeV/c, significantly below the standard value of QHD.

## Key claim

The paper shows that at the mean-field level of the boson loop expansion (BLE), the Serot-Walecka model of Quantum Hadro-Dynamics (QHD) predicts a sigma-omega condensate instability for coupling constants and densities below the standard QHD parameter values, but this does not invalidate the model because a higher-order calculation and reparametrization could restore stability.

## Method

The authors compute the coupled Dyson equations for the sigma and omega meson propagators in nuclear matter within the Random Phase Approximation (RPA), which constitutes the mean field of the boson loop expansion. They evaluate the longitudinal denominator of the coupled propagator matrix at zero energy transfer (q0=0) as a function of Fermi momentum kF and the sigma coupling constant gσ, using both bare and self-consistently determined effective nucleon masses. The critical condition for sigma-omega condensation is the vanishing of this denominator at some momentum transfer q.

## Key numbers

- Critical sigma coupling constant for condensation: gσ = 8.828, compared to the standard QHD value of gσ = 9.573
- Critical Fermi momentum for condensation: kF = 207.8 MeV/c (approximately 207.2 MeV/c in the abstract), significantly below the normal nuclear density value of kF = 1.36 fm⁻¹ (approximately 268 MeV/c)
- For the simplified sigma-only model at normal nuclear density, the critical coupling constant is gσ = 2.47, well below the QHD value of gσ = 9.573
- The limiting value from the low-density slope condition gives gσ = 8.197
- Standard QHD parameters used: mσ = 550 MeV, mω = 783 MeV, gω = 11.67

## Context

This work extends the analysis of sigma-omega mixing in nuclear matter initiated by Du (2003) and builds on the formal framework of the boson loop expansion developed by Alzetta, Cenni, Molinari, and Saracco (1987) and Cenni, Colò, and Saracco (1997). The results show that the standard mean-field QHD of Serot and Walecka (1986) is unstable against sigma-omega condensation at densities below normal nuclear matter, requiring a reparametrization of the model at the two-boson-loop level. The prediction of vanishing nucleon effective mass at high density follows the earlier work of Lee and Wick (1974).
