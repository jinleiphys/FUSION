# Ultra-relativistic heavy-ion physics with AFTER@LHC

> We outline the opportunities for ultra-relativistic heavy-ion physics which are offered by a next generation and multi-purpose fixed-target experiment exploiting the proton and ion LHC beams extracted by a bent crystal.

## Key claim

AFTER@LHC would enable ultra-relativistic heavy-ion fixed-target physics at center-of-mass energies of √sNN ≈ 72 GeV for PbA collisions and √sNN ≈ 115 GeV for pA collisions, using a bent crystal to extract a small fraction of the LHC beam that would otherwise be lost in collimators, thereby achieving luminosities that surpass those of RHIC and the LHC in collider mode for certain observables.

## Method

The paper presents a design study for a fixed-target experiment (AFTER) that uses a bent crystal to extract proton and lead-ion beams from the LHC halo and direct them onto a variety of fixed targets (liquid hydrogen, liquid deuterium, Be, Cu, W, Pb). The authors compute expected instantaneous and yearly luminosities based on an extracted beam intensity of 2 × 10⁵ Pb/s, and estimate inclusive yields for J/ψ and Υ production using standard cross-section calculations, comparing these to RHIC and LHC collider-mode projections.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy for PbA collisions: √sNN ≈ 72 GeV (from 2.76 TeV per nucleon Pb beam on fixed target)
- Center-of-mass energy for pA collisions: √sNN ≈ 115 GeV (from 7 TeV proton beam on fixed target)
- Extracted beam intensity: 2 × 10⁵ Pb/s, extracting about 15% of the lead ions in the beam (4.1 × 10¹⁰) over a 10-hour fill
- Instantaneous luminosity for PbPb with 1 cm Pb target: 7 mb⁻¹ s⁻¹; for PbBe with 1 cm Be target: 25 mb⁻¹ s⁻¹
- Yearly luminosity for PbPb: 7 nb⁻¹, which is twice that expected at RHIC in AuAu at 200 GeV and 60 times that at 62 GeV
- Yearly J/ψ yield in PbPb at √sNN = 72 GeV: 5.7 × 10⁶ yr⁻¹, comparable to 7.3 × 10⁶ yr⁻¹ expected in LHC PbPb at 5.5 TeV and 4.4 × 10⁶ yr⁻¹ at RHIC AuAu at 200 GeV
- Yearly Υ yield in PbPb at √sNN = 72 GeV: 1.1 × 10⁴ yr⁻¹, comparable to 3.6 × 10⁴ yr⁻¹ expected in LHC PbPb at 5.5 TeV and 1.1 × 10⁴ yr⁻¹ at RHIC AuAu at 200 GeV
- Rapidity shift between c.m.s. and laboratory frame: tanh⁻¹ β ≈ 4.3, with accessible c.m.s. rapidity region −4.8 ≤ y_c.m.s. ≤ 1

## Context

This work extends the fixed-target heavy-ion program from earlier SPS-era experiments and proposes a new facility that competes with RHIC (PHENIX, 200 GeV AuAu) and LHC collider-mode heavy-ion runs despite lower center-of-mass energy. The bent-crystal extraction technique builds on prior tests at the SPS (Arduini 1997) and recent small-angle deflection tests (Scandale 2011), as well as laser-ablation diamond crystal bending (Balling 2009). The paper enables future detailed studies of quarkonium suppression, jet quenching, and direct photon production in a fixed-target configuration, with particular advantages for accessing the backward c.m.s. hemisphere and for studying cold nuclear matter effects via target-species variation.
