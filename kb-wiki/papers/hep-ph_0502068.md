# The interaction of slow J/psi and psi' with nucleons

> The interaction of the charmonium resonances J/psi and psi' with nucleons at low energies is considered using the multipole expansion and low-energy theorems in QCD. A lower bound is established for the relevant gluonic operator average over the nucleon. As a result we find the discussed interaction to be significantly stronger than previously estimated in the literature. In particular we conclude that the cross section of the J/psi - nucleon elastic scattering at the threshold is very likely to exceed 17 millibarn and that existence of bound states of the J/psi in light nuclei is possible. For the psi' resonance we estimate even larger elastic scattering cross section and also a very large cross section of the process psi'+N -> psi+N giving rise to the decay width of tens of MeV for the psi' resonance in heavy nuclei.

# Wiki Page: The interaction of slow J/ψ and ψ' with nucleons

## Key claim

The J/ψ-nucleon elastic scattering cross section at threshold is very likely to exceed 17 millibarn, and the interaction is significantly stronger than previously estimated in the literature. This conclusion depends on establishing a lower bound for the gluonic operator average over the nucleon, and the authors argue that the actual value should be close to this bound.

## Method

The authors use the multipole expansion in QCD to describe the interaction of compact charmonium states with soft gluon fields, with the leading term arising from double E1 (chromo-electric dipole) interaction. The coupling of soft gluonic fields to nucleons is determined using low-energy theorems based on the trace anomaly of the QCD energy-momentum tensor. The chromo-polarizability of charmonium states is related to the matrix element of the squared chromo-electric field over the nucleon, and a lower bound for this matrix element is derived from the non-negativity of the chromo-magnetic average over the nucleon.

## Key numbers

- J/ψ-nucleon elastic scattering cross section at threshold: σ_J/ψN ≈ 17 mb × [(1+C)/2 × α_J/ψ/(2 GeV⁻³)]², where C ≥ 1 and α_J/ψ is the diagonal chromo-polarizability
- J/ψ-nucleon scattering length: a_J/ψ ≈ 0.37 fm × [(1+C)/2 × α_J/ψ/(2 GeV⁻³)]
- J/ψ potential in nuclear matter at density ρ_N = 0.16 fm⁻³: V_J/ψ ≈ -21 MeV × [(1+C)/2 × α_J/ψ/(2 GeV⁻³)]
- Transition chromo-polarizability for ψ' → J/ψ: |α_ψ'J/ψ| ≈ 2 GeV⁻³, determined from the known ψ' → J/ψ ππ decay rate
- Cross section for ψ' + N → J/ψ + N near threshold: σ ≈ 16 mb × [1 GeV/p_i] × [(1+C)/2]² × |F(q²)|², where p_i is the initial c.m. momentum and F(q²) is a form factor
- Decay width of ψ' in heavy nuclei from the process ψ' + N → J/ψ + N: Γ_ψ'J/ψ ≈ 70 MeV × [(1+C)/2]² × [α_ψ'/(2 GeV⁻³)]² × |F(q²)|² at nuclear density ρ_N = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Momentum transfer in ψ' + N → J/ψ + N: -q² ≈ 0.82 GeV²
- Ratio of imaginary to real part of elastic ψ'-nucleon scattering amplitude: Im T_ψ' / Re T_ψ' ≈ 1.6 × [(1+C)/2] × [2 GeV⁻³/α_ψ'] × |F(q²)|²

## Context

This work extends the approach of Voloshin and collaborators (Voloshin, 1979; Novikov and Shifman, 1981) that successfully described hadronic transitions in charmonium (ψ' → J/ψ ππ) and bottomonium (Υ' → Υ ππ) using multipole expansion and low-energy theorems. The authors establish a significantly stronger lower bound for the J/ψ-nucleon interaction than the estimate of Kaidalov and Volkovitsky (Kaidalov, 1992), who used a Coulomb-like model for charmonium wave functions and found α_J/ψ ≈ 0.6 GeV⁻³. The results enable predictions for bound states of J/ψ in light nuclei and for substantial modification of charmonium properties in nuclear matter, which could be tested at the Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research (FAIR) at GSI. The authors note that their threshold estimates cannot be directly interpolated to higher energies where other theoretical approaches apply, and that the only available experimental value for J/ψ-nucleon total cross section (Anderson, 2003) of 3.8 ± 0.8 ± ~0.5 mb at √s ≈ 5.7 GeV suggests a noticeable rise toward threshold.
