# Nonperturbative QCD Effects in High Energy Collisions

> High energy hadron-hadron collisions are discussed. It is argued that soft collisions should involve in an essential way nonperturbative QCD. A way is outlined how to calculate properties of high energy elastic hadron-hadron scattering using field theoretic methods. The functional integrals occuring there are evaluated using the ``stochastic vacuum model''. A satisfactory comparison between theory and experiment is achieved. Then the question of possible nonperturbative QCD effects in high energy hard hadron-hadron collisions is raised. It is shown that some spin effects in the Drell-Yan process may give a hint that such effects exist indeed in nature.

# Nonperturbative QCD Effects in High Energy Collisions

## Key claim

Soft high-energy hadron-hadron collisions should involve nonperturbative QCD in an essential way, and the stochastic vacuum model can be used to evaluate the relevant functional integrals; the author argues that spin effects in the Drell-Yan process may provide a hint that nonperturbative effects exist in hard reactions as well, potentially challenging the factorization hypothesis.

## Method

The author develops a field-theoretic framework for high-energy elastic hadron-hadron scattering by expressing the quark-quark scattering amplitude as an average over external gluon potentials, with the high-energy limit governed by nonabelian phase factors. The resulting functional integrals are evaluated using the Minkowski-space extension of the stochastic vacuum model, which provides an ansatz for gluon field strength correlators and a factorization scheme for higher-point correlators. The model parameters (gluon condensate and correlation length) are then determined by fitting to experimental data on proton-proton elastic scattering at a reference energy, and the predicted relationship between total cross section and slope parameter is compared with data across a wide energy range.

## Key numbers

- Gluon condensate from sum rules: \( G_2 = (2.4 \pm 1.1) \times 10^{-2} \text{ GeV}^4 = (335-430 \text{ MeV})^4 \)
- Vacuum correlation length from low-energy phenomenology: \( a \simeq 0.35 \text{ fm} \)
- Proton electromagnetic radius: \( R_p = 0.86 \text{ fm} \)
- Total cross section formula for pp scattering: \( \sigma_{tot}(pp) = 0.00881 (R_p/a)^{3.277} \cdot (3\pi^2 G_2)^2 \cdot a^{10} \)
- Slope parameter formula: \( b_{pp} = 1.558 a^2 + 0.454 R_p^2 \)
- Fitted correlation length from pp data at \( \sqrt{s} = 20 \text{ GeV} \): \( a = 0.31 \text{ fm} \)
- Fitted gluon condensate from pp data at \( \sqrt{s} = 20 \text{ GeV} \): \( G_2 = 6.61 \times 10^{-2} \text{ GeV}^4 \)
- Input total cross section (Pomeron part) at \( \sqrt{s} = 20 \text{ GeV} \): 35 mb
- Input slope parameter at \( \sqrt{s} = 20 \text{ GeV} \): \( b_{pp} = 12.5 \text{ GeV}^{-2} \)
- Estimated energy range for synchrotron photons: \( \omega < 300-500 \text{ MeV} \)

## Context

This work extends the stochastic vacuum model of Dosch and Simonov (1990s), originally formulated for Euclidean space, into Minkowski space via the analytic continuation approach of Dosch and Kramer (1990s). It builds on earlier work by Nachtmann and Landshoff (1990s) connecting QCD vacuum structure to soft high-energy scattering, and addresses the long-standing question of whether nonperturbative effects can violate the factorization hypothesis for hard reactions, which was established in perturbative QCD by Collins, Soper, and Sterman (1980s). The results enable a unified description of soft elastic scattering across energies from 20 GeV to 1.8 TeV, and suggest experimental tests for nonperturbative spin correlations in Drell-Yan processes that could challenge the standard QCD-improved parton model.
