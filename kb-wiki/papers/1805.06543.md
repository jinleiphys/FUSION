# Temperature Dependence of the Neutron Lifespan

> Current precision big bang nucleosynthesis (BBN) studies motivate us to revisit the neutron lifespan in the plasma medium of the early universe. The mechanism we explore is the Fermi-blocking of decay electrons and neutrinos by plasma. As result, neutrons live longer and we find a significant 6.4\% modification of neutron abundance in the BBN era arising from in plasma modification of the neutron lifetime. This effect can influence the final abundances of the light elements in BBN.

## Key claim

The paper claims that Fermi-blocking of decay electrons and neutrinos by the cosmic plasma lengthens the neutron lifetime in the early universe, leading to a 6.4% increase in neutron abundance at the time of Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) compared to the standard vacuum calculation; this effect is conditional on the temperature range between neutron freeze-out (T_f ≈ 0.8 MeV) and BBN (T_BBN ≈ 0.07 MeV).

## Method

The authors compute the neutron decay rate in a heat bath by incorporating Fermi-Dirac suppression factors (1 - f_i) for electrons, protons, and antineutrinos into the phase-space integral of the standard neutron decay matrix element. They then integrate the modified decay rate over the cosmic expansion history between neutron freeze-out and BBN, using entropy conservation to relate the photon temperature T and the neutrino temperature T_ν after neutrino decoupling and electron-positron annihilation. The resulting neutron abundance is compared to the standard vacuum decay case.

## Key numbers

- Neutron lifetime in vacuum (experimental, Particle Data Group): τ_n^0 = 880.2 ± 1.0 sec
- Latest neutron lifetime measurement (magnetogravitational trap): 877.7 ± 0.7 sec (Pattie 2018)
- Neutron lifetime in vacuum after applying Coulomb and recoil corrections: τ_n^Vacuum = 879.481 sec
- At high temperature (T = 100 MeV), the neutron lifetime in plasma is 3495 sec, which is 3.974 times larger than the vacuum lifetime
- Neutron abundance ratio at BBN (T_BBN ≈ 0.07 MeV) for freeze-out temperature T_f = 0.08 MeV: X_n^Medium / X_n^Vacuum = 1.064
- Resulting neutron abundance in plasma medium: X_n^Medium = 0.138, a 6.4% increase over the standard value of 0.13

## Context

This work extends the method of Kuznetsova et al. (2010) for medium-dependent particle decay to the specific case of neutron decay in the early universe, a process that had not been previously considered in BBN calculations. The results motivate a re-evaluation of precision BBN predictions for light element abundances (hydrogen, helium-4, and others) as computed by Pitrou et al. (2018), and the authors note that the same medium-modification approach should be applied to other weak interaction rates in BBN.
