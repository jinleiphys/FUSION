# The nuclear quadrupole moment of 57Fe from microscopic nuclear and atomic calculations

> The nuclear quadrupole moment of the I=3/2- excited nuclear state of 57Fe at 14.41 keV, important in Mössbauer spectroscopy, is determined from the large-scale nuclear shell-model calculations for 57Fe and also from the electronic ab initio and density functional theory calculations including solid state and electron correlation effects for the molecules Fe(CO)_5 and Fe(C_5 H_5)_2. Both independent methods yield very similar results. The recommended value is 0.16(1) eb. The NQM of the isomeric 10+ in 54Fe has also been calculated. The new value (0.5 eb), consistent with the perturbed angular distribution data, is by a factor of two larger than the currently recommended value.

# The nuclear quadrupole moment of 57Fe from microscopic nuclear and atomic calculations

## Key claim
The nuclear quadrupole moment (NQM) of the 14.41 keV I=3/2- excited state in 57Fe is determined to be 0.16(1) eb by combining large-scale nuclear shell-model calculations with electronic ab initio and density functional theory calculations, but the shell-model result required a two-level mixing correction constrained by the experimental magnetic moment because the nearly degenerate 3/2- doublet makes pure shell-model predictions highly sensitive to the choice of effective interaction.

## Method
Large-scale nuclear shell-model calculations were performed for 57Fe using the code NATHAN in the pf shell, with a full diagonalization of 25,743,302 3/2- states (one of the largest shell-model diagonalizations to date) using three effective interactions (KB3F, KB3G, FDP6). Because the two lowest 3/2- states are nearly degenerate and strongly mixed, a two-level mixing procedure was applied using the precisely measured magnetic moment of the first 3/2- state to determine the mixing amplitudes. Independently, electronic structure calculations for the molecules Fe(CO)5 and Fe(C5H5)2 were performed using density functional theory with a wide range of functionals and coupled-cluster singles doubles including non-iterative triple excitations [CCSD(T)], with solid-state effects checked via periodic calculations using CRYSTAL98.

## Key numbers
- Recommended NQM for 57Fe 3/2-1 state: 0.16(1) eb
- Shell-model result after two-level mixing: Q(3/2-1) ≈ 0.17 eb (from KB3F and KB3G interactions)
- Experimental magnetic moment used for constraint: μ(3/2-1) = -0.1549(2) μN
- NQM for isomeric 10+ state in 54Fe: 0.5 eb (calculated values: 0.51 eb for KB3F, 0.50 eb for KB3G, 0.56 eb for FDP6)
- Ratio Q[54Fe(10+)]/Q[57Fe(3/2-1)]: 2.9 (KB3F), 3.1 (KB3G), compared to experimental value 3.62 ± 0.22
- From CCSD(T) EFGs and Mossbauer data: 0.177 eb (Fe(CO)5) and 0.159 eb (Fe(C5H5)2)
- Best atomic estimate: 0.15(2) eb
- Energy splitting between the two 3/2- states: experimental 353 keV; theoretical 526 keV (KB3F), 209 keV (KB3G), 465 keV (FPD6)
- Magnetic moment of 54Fe 10+ state: 6.4 μN (KB3F), 6.5 μN (KB3G), 7.1 μN (FDP6); experimental 7.28 ± 0.01 μN

## Context
This work resolves a long-standing controversy in Mossbauer spectroscopy where reported NQM values for 57Fe ranged from -0.19 to +0.44 eb, with previous shell-model calculations by the Utrecht Group (Vennink et al.) restricted to only three f7/2 holes and earlier atomic calculations by Duff (1974) giving 0.082 eb. The result agrees with the density functional theory value of 0.16 eb reported by Dufek et al. (1995) and the Sternheimer-corrected value of 0.12(3) eb from Su and Coppens. The new NQM for the 54Fe 10+ isomer (0.5 eb) is a factor of two larger than the previously recommended value from Hass et al., enabling improved interpretation of perturbed angular distribution data and more reliable structural determinations from Mossbauer spectroscopy of iron-containing compounds.
