# Femtosecond formation of collective modes due to meanfield fluctuations

> Starting from a quantum kinetic equation including the mean field and a conserving relaxation-time approximation we derive an analytic formula which describes the time dependence of the dielectric function in a plasma created by a short intense laser pulse. This formula reproduces universal features of the formation of collective modes seen in recent experimental data of femtosecond spectroscopy. The presented formula offers a tremendous simplification for the description of the formation of quasiparticle features in interacting systems. Numerical demanding treatments can now be focused on effects beyond these gross features found here to be describable analytically.

## Key claim

The paper derives an analytic formula for the time-dependent dielectric function in a plasma created by a short intense laser pulse, but this formula only captures the "gross features" of collective mode formation due to mean-field fluctuations; deviations observed in experimental data (e.g., for InP) are attributed to higher-order correlations beyond the mean-field treatment.

## Method

Starting from a quantum kinetic equation that includes the mean field and a conserving relaxation-time approximation (Mermin's approach), the authors derive an integral equation for the density response function. By taking the long-wavelength limit and approximating the carrier density as a step function in time, they reduce the problem to a second-order differential equation for the inverse dielectric function, which is solved analytically.

## Key numbers

- Plasma frequency for GaAs: ω_p = 14.4 THz; relaxation time τ = 85 fs; pump pulse at t₀ = -40 fs; probe pulse full-width at half-maximum = 27 fs.
- Plasma frequency for InP: ω_p = 14.4 THz; relaxation time τ = 77 fs; t₀ = -70 fs.
- GaAs longitudinal optical phonon frequency: ω_LO/2π = 8.8 THz; transverse optical phonon frequency: ω_TO/2π = 8.1 THz; lattice damping γ = 0.2 ps⁻¹; non-resonant background polarizability ε_∞ = 11.0.
- InP longitudinal optical phonon frequency: ω_LO/2π = 10.3 THz; transverse optical phonon frequency: ω_TO/2π = 9 THz; lattice damping γ = 0.2 ps⁻¹; ε_∞ = 9.6.

## Context

This work extends the Mermin (1970) conserving relaxation-time approximation for the dielectric function to the time domain, providing an analytic description of collective mode formation observed in femtosecond spectroscopy experiments on GaAs (Huber et al., 2001; Huber et al., 2002) and InP (Huber et al., 2005). The formula enables experimentalists to subtract the mean-field contribution from data, isolating effects due to higher-order correlations that require numerically demanding quantum kinetic treatments (Banyai et al., 1998; Gartner et al., 1999; Vu and Haug, 2000; Kwong and Binder, 2000).
