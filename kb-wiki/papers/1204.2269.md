# Neutrino-Long-Baseline Experiments and Nuclear Physics

> Neutrino long-baseline experiments nowadays all use nuclear targets. The extraction of neutrino oscillation parameters from such experiments requires a good understanding of the interaction of neutrinos with nuclei. In this talk we discuss results on quasielastic scattering and pion production which are the relevant processes in the neutrino energy regime around 1 GeV. We also discuss implications of the reaction mechanisms for the reconstruction of the neutrino energy which is not known a priori.

## Key claim

The extraction of neutrino oscillation parameters from long-baseline experiments using nuclear targets requires a good understanding of neutrino-nucleus interactions, but the standard quasielastic energy reconstruction method is contaminated by pion-production events that mimic QE signatures after final-state interactions, leading to systematic uncertainties in the reconstructed neutrino energy that are often glossed over in experimental analyses.

## Method

The authors use the GiBUU model, a Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck transport-theory framework, to simulate neutrino-nucleus reactions on carbon and oxygen targets. The model factorizes the reaction into a primary neutrino-nucleon interaction (covering quasielastic scattering, resonance excitation, and deep inelastic scattering) and a detailed treatment of final-state interactions via an intranuclear cascade with off-shell transport of collision-broadened hadrons. The calculations are compared to MiniBooNE and K2K data for charged-current quasielastic-like events and charged-current single-pion production.

## Key numbers

- The Cherenkov detector identification method for QE-like events (muon, 0 pions) yields a cross section about 20% higher than the true CCQE cross section on carbon, due to pion-production events that become pionless after FSI.
- The tracking detector method (muon, 1 proton, 0 pions) yields a QE-like cross section about 20% lower than the true value, because multiple nucleon knock-out and undetectable neutrons remove events from the sample.
- For a fixed incoming neutrino energy of 1 GeV, the reconstructed energy distribution from QE-like events has an rms width of about 22% when including the lower-energy bump from pion production (versus about 16% from Fermi motion alone).
- The MiniBooNE CCQE data require an axial mass of M_A = 1.23 GeV (or even 1.35 GeV) to fit within a Fermi-gas model, compared to the world average of M_A = 1.026 GeV.
- For charged-current 1π+ production on CH2 at 1 GeV, the GiBUU calculations with FSI lie about a factor of 1.5 below the MiniBooNE data; at 2 GeV the discrepancy reaches a factor of about 2.
- The total cross section for misidentified neutral pions (counted as electron neutrino appearance events) in the T2K experiment is 0.09 × 10⁻³⁸ cm², which is 26% of the true pion events.

## Context

This work extends the GiBUU transport model, previously tested against photonuclear and electronuclear data, to neutrino-nucleus reactions and directly addresses the "QE puzzle" (MiniBooNE cross section excess over Fermi-gas predictions) and the "pion puzzle" (MiniBooNE pion production cross sections exceeding conventional calculations). The results contradict simpler approaches that treat FSI only as absorption (e.g., optical models or Glauber approximation) and highlight the need for coupled-channel treatments. The paper enables improved event generators for experiments such as T2K, MiniBooNE, and NOvA by quantifying how pion-production contamination and 2p-2h many-body effects (as discussed by Martini (2009) and Nieves (2011)) degrade neutrino energy reconstruction, which is critical for oscillation parameter extraction.
