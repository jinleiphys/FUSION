# Triplet-odd pairing in finite nuclear systems: Even-even singly closed nuclei

> Background: The appearance of the pairing condensate is an essential feature of many-fermion systems. There are two possible types of pairing: spin-singlet and spin-triplet. However, an open question remains as to whether the spin-triplet pairing condensate emerges in finite nuclei. Purpose: The aim of this work is to examine the coexistence of the spin-singlet and spin-triplet like-particle pairing condensates in nuclei. We also discuss the dependence on the type of pairing functional. Method: The Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with a Skyrme $+$ local-pair energy-density functional (EDF) are performed to investigate the pairing condensate in the spherical ground states of Ca and Sn isotopes. Results: The spin-singlet pair EDF induces not only the spin-singlet but also the spin-triplet pairing condensates due to a strong spin-orbit splitting. By discarding the spin-orbit EDF, only the spin-singlet pairing condensate appears. The spin-triplet pair EDF, however, induces the spin-orbit splitting and accordingly the spin-singlet pairing condensate. Conclusions: The spin-orbit splitting plays an essential role in the coexistence of the spin-singlet and spin-triplet pairing condensates in nuclei.

# Triplet-odd pairing in finite nuclear systems: Even-even singly closed nuclei

## Key claim
The spin-singlet and spin-triplet like-particle pairing condensates can coexist in finite nuclei even when only one type of pairing energy-density functional is present, provided that spin-orbit splitting is active; the spin-orbit EDF is essential for this coexistence, and without it only the spin-singlet condensate appears from a spin-singlet pair EDF.

## Method
Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with a Skyrme plus local-pair energy-density functional (EDF) were performed for the spherical ground states of Ca and Sn isotopes. The spin-singlet and spin-triplet pair condensations were evaluated using the local pair density and the spin-current (tensor) pair density, respectively, with the spin-orbit coupling constant varied to isolate its role. The spin-triplet pair EDF coupling constant was adjusted to reproduce the pairing energy of 44Ca obtained with the spin-singlet pair EDF.

## Key numbers
- Spin-singlet coupling constant from the G3RS-1E-1 potential: \(\tilde{C}^\rho_t = -697.087\) MeV fm\(^3\) and \(\tilde{C}^{\Delta\rho}_t = 1363.253\) MeV fm\(^5\)
- Spin-triplet coupling constant from the G3RS-3O-1 potential: \(\tilde{C}^J_t = 6794.724\) MeV fm\(^5\) (repulsive in the \(^3P_1\) channel)
- Spin-singlet volume-type contact pair EDF strength used in calculations: \(\tilde{C}_n^{\rho} = -46.625\) MeV fm\(^3\) (standard notation \(V_0 = -186.5\) MeV fm\(^3\)), adjusted to reproduce the neutron pairing gap of 1.245 MeV in \(^{120}\)Sn
- Spin-triplet pair EDF coupling constant adjusted to reproduce the pairing energy of \(^{44}\)Ca: \(\tilde{C}_n^{J1} = -46.125\) MeV fm\(^5\); pairing energy was \(-5.08\) MeV (singlet-pair EDF) and \(-5.25\) MeV (triplet-pair EDF)
- Pairing rotational moment of inertia at \(N=30\) for the triplet-pair EDF was \(-17.86\) MeV\(^{-1}\) (negative, not shown in figure), while the singlet-pair EDF reproduced the experimental value
- Cutoff parameter of 60 MeV used in the HFB calculations

## Context
This work extends the study of unconventional pairing in nuclear systems, specifically the like-particle spin-triplet (triplet-odd) pairing that has been less actively investigated compared to spin-singlet pairing. The connection between spin-orbit splitting and triplet-odd pairing was suggested in earlier work by Oishi (2021) on magnetic-dipole excitation, and the formalism builds on the density matrix expansion approach of Bender (2003) and the Skyrme-HFB framework of Bennaceur (2017). The results enable future inclusion of both spin-singlet and spin-triplet pair EDFs into nuclear EDFs for more detailed descriptions of pairing condensation, including deformed nuclei where pseudoscalar and pseudotensor components must be considered.
