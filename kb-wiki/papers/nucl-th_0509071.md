# Relation between widths of proton resonances and neutron asymptotic normalization coefficients in mirror states of light nuclei in a microscopic cluster model

> It has been suggested recently ({\it Phys. Rev. Lett.} 91, 232501 (2003)) that the widths of narrow proton resonances are related to neutron Asymptotic Normalization Coefficients (ANCs) of their bound mirror analogs because of charge symmetry of nucleon-nucleon interactions. This relation is approximated by a simple analytical formula which involves proton resonance energies, neutron separation energies, charges of residual nuclei and the range of their strong interaction with the last nucleon. In the present paper, we perform microscopic-cluster model calculations for the ratio of proton widths to neutron ANCs squared in mirror states for several light nuclei. We compare them to predictions of the analytical formula and to estimates made within a single-particle potential model. A knowledge of this ratio can be used to predict unknown proton widths for very narrow low-lying resonances in the neutron-deficient region of the $sd$- and $pf$-shells, which is important for understanding the nucleosynthesis in the $rp$-process.

## Key claim

The ratio of a narrow proton resonance width to the squared neutron asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) of its bound mirror analog is approximately model-independent, but the simple analytical formula from Timofeyuk (2003) deviates from microscopic cluster model calculations by up to 30% for broad resonances and by smaller amounts for narrow resonances, with the actual deviation depending on the orbital angular momentum, the nuclear potential, and the inclusion of core excitations.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic cluster model (MCM) calculations for several light nuclei (8B, 12N, 13N, 23Al, 27P) using the Volkov V2 and Minnesota effective nucleon-nucleon interactions, with and without core excitations. They computed the ratio of the proton resonance width (both the R-matrix pole residue width Γ⁰ and the observed width Γ) to the squared neutron ANC of the mirror bound state, and compared these ratios to the predictions of the analytical formula from Timofeyuk (2003) and to single-particle potential model estimates.

## Key numbers

- For 8B(1+), 12N(2+), and 23Al(1/2+), the ratio Γp/Cn² changed by less than 1% when varying the two-body Woods-Saxon potential well, while Γp itself changed by 30-120%
- For 27P(3/2+), the ratio Γp/Cn² changed by 8.5% compared to 121% changes in Γp when varying the potential well
- For 12N(0+) and 13N(3/2-), the ratio Γp/Cn² showed 15% and 10% dependence on the potential well, respectively, because these states have very small spectroscopic factors
- The analytical formula R0^res reproduced the two-body model ratio Γp/Cn² to within 30% for all cases, with the smallest deviation (less than 3%) for d-wave resonances and the largest (about 30%) for the broad 12N(0+) resonance
- For 0p-shell nuclei, the effect of different NN potential choices on the MCM ratio was normally less than 7%, with p-wave resonances showing less than 2% sensitivity
- For 23Al and 27P with core excitations, the sensitivity to the NN potential increased to 10% and 30%, respectively
- For 0p-shell nuclei, including or excluding core excitations changed the MCM ratio by no more than 7%

## Context

This work extends the analytical formula proposed by Timofeyuk (2003) relating proton resonance widths to neutron ANCs in mirror states, which was based on charge symmetry of nucleon-nucleon interactions. The authors had previously studied mirror symmetry in ANCs for bound states (Timofeyuk and Descouvemont, 2005) using the same microscopic cluster model. The results enable prediction of unknown proton widths for very narrow low-lying resonances in neutron-deficient sd- and pf-shell nuclei (such as 25Si, 27P, 33Ar, 36K, and 43,46V) important for rp-process nucleosynthesis, with potential accuracy of 10-30% compared to the typical 50% uncertainty from distorted-wave analysis of stripping to continuum.
