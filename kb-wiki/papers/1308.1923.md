# On the fluid behavior of a baryon rich hadron resonance gas

> We investigate the effects of finite baryon chemical potential on the transport properties of a hadron resonance gas. We find that a hadron resonance gas with large baryon number density is closer to the ideal fluid limit than the corresponding gas with zero baryon number. This suggests that the system created at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) at lower collision energies may behave as a fluid, with an effective fluidity close to the one found at RHIC's highest energy near phase transition. This might explain why the differential elliptic flow coefficient measured at lower collisional energies at RHIC is similar to the one observed at high energies.

## Key claim

A hadron resonance gas with large baryon number density is closer to the ideal fluid limit than the corresponding gas with zero baryon number, as measured by the ratio ηT/(ε+p) rather than η/s. This suggests that systems created at RHIC at lower collision energies may display fluid-like behavior with effective fluidity close to that found at RHIC's highest energy near the phase transition, potentially explaining why differential elliptic flow coefficients remain similar across collision energies.

## Method

The authors computed the shear viscosity of a hadron resonance gas at nonzero temperature and baryon chemical potential using Chapman-Enskog theory within a simple hadronic model, considering all hadrons and resonances with masses up to 2 GeV. They used two sets of constant cross sections: case (i) with all hadrons having a 1 fm radius (σ_T = π fm²), and case (ii) with meson-meson, meson-baryon, and baryon-baryon cross sections scaling as 4:6:9 respectively, with baryon-baryon cross section set to π fm². The retarded Green's function for the shear-stress tensor was computed at vanishing wavenumber using a single-basis truncation equivalent to the 14-moment approximation.

## Key numbers

- At the chemical freeze-out transition, η/s is reduced by a factor of approximately 3 at 100 MeV when baryon chemical potential is present
- For μ_B = 500 MeV, the entropy density and enthalpy-to-temperature ratio both increase compared to μ_B = 0, with the increase in h/T being more pronounced
- The parametrization T(μ_B) ≈ a - bμ_B² - cμ_B⁴ was used, where a ≈ 0.166 GeV, b ≈ 0.139 GeV⁻¹, and c ≈ 0.053 GeV⁻³, extracted from thermal fits to heavy ion collisions
- The dimensionless combination λT/η, associated with second-order gradient terms, is also reduced when baryon chemical potential is present

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of η/s in hadron gases at zero baryon chemical potential (Prakash, Noronha, Wiranata) and at finite chemical potential using Chapman-Enskog formalism for a pion-nucleon gas (Japonese). It contradicts the finding of Hauer, who estimated that η/s increases with baryon chemical potential using an excluded volume hadron gas model. The results suggest that the baryon-rich hadron gas formed at intermediate RHIC energies and future facilities such as FAIR can display strong collective flow, consistent with the flow harmonic data already obtained at RHIC.
