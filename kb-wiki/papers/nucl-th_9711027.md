# Shell-model calculations for the three-nucleon system

> We use Faddeev's decomposition to solve the shell-model problem for three nucleons. The dependence on harmonic-oscillator excitations allowed in the model space, up to $32 \hbarΩ$ in the present calculations, and on the harmonic-oscillator frequency is studied. Effective interactions derived from Nijmegen II and Reid93 potentials are used in the calculations. The binding energies obtained are close to those calculated by other methods. The structure of the Faddeev equations is discussed and a simple formula for matrix elements of the permutation operators in a harmonic-oscillator basis is given. The Pauli principle is properly treated in the calculations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that combining Faddeev's decomposition with the shell-model approach allows three-nucleon calculations in model spaces up to 32 ℏΩ, yielding binding energies that converge to those of traditional 34-channel Faddeev calculations (7.62-7.63 MeV) but only for harmonic-oscillator frequencies ℏΩ = 22-24 MeV; convergence is not reached for lower frequencies within the studied model spaces.

## Method

The authors solve the three-nucleon bound-state problem by applying Faddeev's decomposition to the shell-model Hamiltonian with a bound center-of-mass term, using a harmonic-oscillator basis that explicitly separates center-of-mass and relative-coordinate wave functions. Effective two-body interactions are derived from the Nijmegen II and Reid93 nucleon-nucleon potentials using the Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation method, solving the two-nucleon relative-coordinate Hamiltonian in a basis truncated at n ≤ 152. The three-body calculation is performed by diagonalizing either the non-Hermitian Faddeev Hamiltonian or its Hermitized version in a model space truncated by total oscillator quanta N ≤ Nmax, with Nmax ranging from 8 to 32.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy for Nijmegen II potential converges toward 7.62 MeV (the 34-channel Faddeev result) for ℏΩ = 22-24 MeV at Nmax = 32
- Binding energy for Reid93 potential converges toward 7.63 MeV (the 34-channel Faddeev result) for ℏΩ = 22-24 MeV at Nmax = 32
- D-state probability approaches 8.4% for Reid93 potential at larger model spaces
- S'-state probability reaches 1.2% for Reid93 potential at larger model spaces
- P-state probability calculated as 0.06%
- S''-state probability approximately 10⁻⁵%
- Binding-energy difference between ³H and ³He approaches 0.66 MeV (experimental value 0.764 MeV)
- Contribution of j = 5,6 partial waves to binding energy is about 5 keV
- Ground-state energy affected by less than 0.3 keV when including partial waves up to j = 9 compared to j ≤ 6
- Isospin-breaking effects at Nmax = 8 with ℏΩ = 19 MeV change binding energy by 11 keV and nucleon radius by less than 0.001 fm
- Low-lying eigenvalues of the two-nucleon Hamiltonian differ by at most ≈ 10⁻³ MeV between basis truncation at n ≤ 152 and exact differential equation solution

## Context

This work extends the no-core shell-model approach previously applied to light nuclei by Zheng, Vary, and Barrett (1994) and Navratil and Barrett (1996), combining it with the Faddeev method to access much larger model spaces than traditional shell-model calculations. The results confirm the convergence of 34-channel Faddeev calculations reported by Friar, Payne, Stoks, and de Swart (1993) to within 0.01 MeV. The method enables testing of effective interactions for shell-model applications and provides a framework that can be generalized to the A=4 system and to constructing three-body effective interactions for standard shell-model calculations of light nuclei.
