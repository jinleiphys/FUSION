# Effective interactions between nuclear clusters

> The effective interactions between two nuclear clusters, $d+d$, $t+t$, and $α+α$, are investigated within a cluster model using local nucleon-nucleon~($NN$) forces. It is shown that the interaction in the spin-aligned $d+d$ system is repulsive for all inter-cluster distances, whereas the $α+α$ and spin-aligned $t+t$ systems are attractive at intermediate distances. The Pauli blocking between identical-nucleon pairs is responsible for the cluster-cluster repulsion and becomes dominant in the shallow binding limit. We demonstrate that two $d$-clusters could be bound if the $NN$ force has nonzero range and is strong enough to form a deeply bound $d$-cluster, or if the $NN$ force has both even-parity and odd-parity attraction. Effective dimer-dimer interactions for general quantum systems of two-component fermions are also discussed in heavy-light mass limit, where one component is much heavier than the other, and their relation to inter-cluster interactions in nuclear systems are discussed. Our findings provide a conceptual foundation for conclusions obtained numerically in the literature, that increasing the range or strength of the local part of the attractive nucleon-nucleon interaction results in a more attractive cluster-cluster interaction.

## Key claim

The effective interaction between two nuclear clusters can be either repulsive or attractive depending on the balance between Pauli blocking between identical-nucleon pairs (which produces repulsion) and the range and strength of the local nucleon-nucleon force (which produces attraction), with the spin-aligned deuteron-deuteron system being repulsive at all distances while the alpha-alpha and spin-aligned triton-triton systems show attraction at intermediate distances.

## Method

The authors used a microscopic cluster model with Brink-Bloch two-cluster wave functions and local nucleon-nucleon forces to calculate effective interactions between d+d, t+t, and alpha+alpha systems. For the dimer-dimer systems, they employed a heavy-light ansatz (Born-Oppenheimer approximation) in the limit where one fermion component is much heavier than the other, solving the two-delta potential problem both exactly and with a frozen dimer approximation. Energies of the lowest states of two-cluster systems were calculated using the generator coordinate method (GCM).

## Key numbers

- For the zero-range delta potential in 1D, the two-dimer energy measured from threshold is ΔE(R) = |ε⁽⁰⁾| × [4κ₀R e^{-2κ₀R} / (1 - (1+κ₀R)² e^{-2κ₀R})] > 0, showing repulsion at all distances
- For the zero-range delta potential in 3D, the two-dimer energy measured from threshold is ΔE(R) = |ε⁽⁰⁾| × [4 e^{-2κ₀R} / (κ₀R (1 - e^{-2κ₀R}))] > 0, showing repulsion at all distances
- The asymptotic expansion for the 1D exact solution gives leading term ΔE(R) = |ε⁽⁰⁾| × 4κ₀R e^{-2κ₀R} for large κ₀R
- The asymptotic expansion for the 3D exact solution gives leading term ΔE(R) = |ε⁽⁰⁾| × [4 e^{-2κ₀R} / (κ₀R)] for large κ₀R
- The frozen dimer ansatz is a good approximation for κ₀R ≳ 2 in 1D and for κ₀R ≳ 1.5 in 3D
- The number of identical-nucleon pairs n_id is 2 for [d+d]_{S=2}, 3 for [t+t]_{S=1}, 4 for [alpha+alpha]_{S=0}, 0 for [d+d]_{S=0}, and 2 for [t+t]_{S=0}

## Context

This work extends the analysis of cluster-cluster interactions beyond the universal repulsion found for dimer-dimer systems at large scattering length by Petrov (2005) and the numerical lattice calculations by Rokash (2016) and Elhatisari (2016). It provides a conceptual foundation for the numerical findings of Elhatisari (2016) that increasing the range or strength of the local part of the attractive nucleon-nucleon interaction results in a more attractive cluster-cluster interaction. The results enable a deeper understanding of how alpha-alpha interactions determine whether nuclear matter forms a nuclear liquid or a Bose-Einstein condensate of alpha particles, and connect to the general theory of fermionic quartet condensation discussed by Ropke (1998) and Lee (2007).
