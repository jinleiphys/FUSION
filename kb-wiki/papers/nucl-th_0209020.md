# Randomness in nuclei and in the quark-gluon plasma

> The issue of averaging randomness is addressed, mostly in nuclear physics, but shortly also in QCD. The Feshbach approach, so successful in dealing with the continuum spectrum of the atomic nuclei ("optical model"), is extended to encompass bound states as well ("shell model"). Its relationship with the random-matrix theory is discussed and the bearing of the latter on QCD, especially in connection with the spectrum of the Dirac operator, is briefly touched upon. Finally the question of whether Feshbach's theory can cope with the averaging required by QCD is considered.

## Key claim

The paper extends Feshbach's unified theory of nuclear reactions, originally developed for continuum states (the optical model), to bound states (the shell model) by introducing an energy-averaging procedure that separates the Hilbert space into a deterministic P-space and a chaotic Q-space, with the caveat that the averaging distribution only works properly when the parameter epsilon is neither too large nor too small.

## Method

The authors partition the many-body Hilbert space into P and Q sectors, where P contains the deterministic (mean-field) dynamics and Q contains the chaotic (fluctuating) components. They introduce an energy-averaging procedure using a Lorentzian-like distribution to average over the rapidly varying Q-space wave functions, deriving a mean-field Hamiltonian whose eigenvalues in the P-space remain stable while Q-space eigenvalues are shifted away. The formalism is applied to nuclear matter using Hartree-Fock states as building blocks for the P-space, with the Q-space organized in blocks of increasing complexity (2p-2h, 4p-4h excitations), and the complexity expansion is carried to first order.

## Context

This work extends the Feshbach approach (Feshbach 1992, 2000) from nuclear reactions to bound states, building on earlier developments by the same group (Car98, DeP99) that applied the formalism to nuclear matter. The paper connects random-matrix theory (Wigner's GOE/GUE/GSE ensembles) as reviewed by Weidenmueller et al. (Guh98) with Feshbach's dynamical correlation approach, and briefly touches on applications to QCD via the Dirac operator spectrum. The formalism enables a systematic treatment of fluctuations around mean-field energies in nuclear matter, with the complexity expansion providing a controlled approximation scheme.
