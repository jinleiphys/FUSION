# Universal decay law in charged-particle emission and exotic cluster radioactivity

> A linear universal decay formula is presented starting from the microscopic mechanism of the charged-particle emission. It relates the half-lives of monopole radioactive decays with the $Q$-values of the outgoing particles as well as the masses and charges of the nuclei involved in the decay. This relation is found to be a generalization of the Geiger-Nuttall law in $α$ radioactivity and explains well all known cluster decays. Predictions on the most likely emissions of various clusters are presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents a linear universal decay law (UDL) that relates the half-lives of monopole radioactive decays to the Q-values of outgoing particles and the masses and charges of the nuclei involved, which is claimed to be a generalization of the Geiger-Nuttall law valid for all cluster radioactivities including alpha decay, but the authors acknowledge that the coefficients must be determined by fitting to experimental data and that the formation amplitude still varies by eight orders of magnitude across different clusters.

## Method

The authors start from the R-matrix description of cluster decay and approximate the Coulomb-Hankel function for the l=0 channel, expanding it in a power series to derive a linear relation between log half-life and two variables: chi' = Z_c Z_d sqrt(A/Q_c) and rho' = sqrt(A Z_c Z_d (A_d^{1/3} + A_c^{1/3})). They then fit the three free parameters (a, b, c) in the equation log T_{1/2} = a chi' + b rho' + c to experimental data for 139 alpha decay events from even-even nuclei with Z=78-108 and 11 measured cluster decay events from C-14 to Si-34.

## Key numbers

* For alpha decay fitting (set I): a = 0.4065, b = -0.4311, c = -20.7889; the UDL reproduces experimental half-lives within a factor of about 2.2.
* For cluster decay fitting (set II): a = 0.3671, b = -0.3296, c = -26.2681; the UDL reproduces experimental half-lives within a factor of about 4.1.
* For combined alpha and cluster decay fitting (set III): a = 0.3949, b = -0.3693, c = -23.7615.
* The formation amplitude for alpha decays is in the range log |R F_c(R)| = -1.5 to -0.75 fm^{-1/2}.
* For the nucleus Rf-254, the UDL predicts T_{1/2} = 42 ms, while the experimental lower limit is T_{1/2} > 1.5 ms.

## Context

This work extends the Geiger-Nuttall law (Geiger and Nuttall, 1911) for alpha decay, which had limited predictive power because its coefficients change for each isotopic series and when magic numbers are crossed (Buck, 1990). The UDL generalizes previous efforts to create a universal description of alpha decay (Brown, 1992; Moller, 1997; Royer, 2000; Poenaru, 2002, 2006; Delion, 2004) to include all cluster radioactivities. The formula enables model-independent predictions of half-lives for all cluster emitters throughout the nuclear chart using only binding energies, and may guide searches for new cluster decay modes from superheavy nuclei.
