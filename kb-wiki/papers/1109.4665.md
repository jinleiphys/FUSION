# Long-distance properties of baryons in the Sakai-Sugimoto model

> We reconsider the realization of baryons in the Sakai-Sugimoto model. In this theory, which is the gravity dual of a QCD-like theory, baryons appear as soliton solutions. These solitons were approximated as flat-space instantons in previous studies. However, with this approximation, it has been shown that one does not reproduce some model-independent predictions for the behavior of baryon electromagnetic form factors which are connected with long-range pion physics. This made it appear that the long-range pion physics of baryons may be hidden in (intractable) α' corrections in the gravity dual. In this paper, we study the long-range properties of baryons in the Sakai-Sugimoto model without relying on the flat-space instanton approximation. The solution we obtain gives the correct result for the model-independent ratio of form factors, implying that the model captures the expected infrared properties of baryons without the need to go beyond the leading order in the α' expansion.

## Key claim

The Sakai-Sugimoto model, when solved without the flat-space instanton approximation, reproduces the model-independent chiral perturbation theory prediction for the long-distance ratio of baryon electromagnetic form factors, showing that the correct infrared pion physics is captured at leading order in the α' expansion rather than being hidden in intractable stringy corrections.

## Method

The authors construct an SO(3)-symmetric ansatz for the U(2) gauge fields in the five-dimensional Yang-Mills-Chern-Simons theory that describes baryons as solitons in the Sakai-Sugimoto model, without imposing the flat-space instanton approximation used in prior work. They solve the resulting coupled nonlinear partial differential equations analytically in a 1/r expansion to obtain the long-distance behavior of the soliton fields, then use collective coordinate quantization and the holographic dictionary to compute the position-space electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon.

## Key numbers

- The model-independent chiral perturbation theory prediction for the ratio of position-space form factors is lim_{r→∞} r² (G̃_E^{I=0} G̃_E^{I=1})/(G̃_M^{I=0} G̃_M^{I=1}) = 18, which the Sakai-Sugimoto model satisfies.
- The Chern-Simons coupling parameter γ = N_c/(16π² κ) = 27π/(2λ), where λ is the 't Hooft coupling.
- The five-dimensional Yang-Mills coupling is κ = λ N_c/(216π³).
- The Kaluza-Klein scale M_KK = 3 U_KK^{1/2}/(2R^{3/2}) sets the only dimensionful scale in the model (set to 1 in the calculation).

## Context

This work resolves a puzzle raised by Cherman (2009), who found that the flat-space instanton approximation to baryons in the Sakai-Sugimoto model failed to reproduce the model-independent chiral perturbation theory prediction for long-distance form factors, suggesting the infrared pion physics was hidden in α' corrections. The authors show that by solving the full equations without this approximation, the model correctly captures the long-range pion physics at leading order in α', contrary to the pessimistic conclusion of that earlier analysis. This enables reliable calculation of baryon properties in holographic QCD without requiring intractable stringy corrections, and extends the successful application of the Sakai-Sugimoto model from the meson sector to baryon observables constrained by chiral perturbation theory.
