# Scaling law for the electromagnetic form factors of the proton

> The violation of the scaling law for the electric and magnetic form factors of the proton are examined within the cloudy bag model. The suppression of the ratio of the electric and magnetic form factors is natural in the bag model. The pion cloud plays a moderate role in understanding the recent data from TJNAF.

## Key claim

The violation of the scaling law (where the electric and magnetic form factors of the proton are equal up to the magnetic moment) is a natural consequence of the MIT bag model, and the cloudy bag model can semi-quantitatively reproduce the recent TJNAF data showing a suppression of the ratio μ_p G_Ep(Q^2)/G_Mp(Q^2) as Q^2 increases, with the pion cloud playing a moderate role.

## Method

The authors calculated the proton electric and magnetic Sachs form factors within the cloudy bag model, starting from the MIT bag model with massless quarks in the static cavity approximation. They incorporated center-of-mass motion corrections using the Peierls-Thouless projection method and relativistic effects using the Licht-Pagnementa prescription, then added pion cloud contributions in the one-loop approximation with intermediate N(939) and Δ(1232) baryon states. The ratio μ_p G_Ep(Q^2)/G_Mp(Q^2) was computed for bag radii of 0.7 fm and 0.8 fm, with quark masses of 0 MeV and 10 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of charge to magnetic root-mean-squared radii in the MIT bag model is ⟨r²⟩_Ep/⟨r²⟩_Mp = 1.36, using ω_S = 2.04, independent of bag radius R.
- The πNN coupling constant used is f²_πNN = 0.0791.
- The bag radius R = 0.8 fm was used for the primary calculations, with R = 0.7 fm as the smallest radius allowing perturbative treatment of the pion cloud.
- The addition of the pion cloud for R = 0.8 fm increases the ratio μ_p G_Ep/G_Mp by roughly 20% toward the recent TJNAF data.
- The decline of the ratio slows down as Q² > 2 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous cloudy bag model calculations (Lu, Thomas, and Williams, 1998) by focusing specifically on the ratio of form factors and the violation of the scaling law established from early Rosenbluth separation experiments. The results are compared with recent direct polarization measurements from TJNAF (Jones et al., 1999), and the behavior at large Q² is noted to be consistent with perturbative QCD predictions (F_1p ~ 1/Q⁴, F_2p ~ 1/Q⁶), unlike other nucleon models such as constituent quark models, vector meson dominance models, and Skyrme models. The work enables further refinement of quark models to better reproduce the experimental form factor ratio at intermediate momentum transfers.
