# Quantum fluctuation in an inhomogeneous background and its influence on the phase transition in a finite volume system

> We have studied the grand potential and phase transitions of an inhomogeneous finite volume spherical quark system. First the finite volume effects are considered by applying the multiple reflection expansion method which is an approximation for the density of states of the momentum in the grand potential of the finite size system. Then, the density of states of momentum is further modified by the thermal fluctuations in the thermal system with the inhomogeneous field background. The modification of the density of states is calculated by the scattering phase shift from the Dirac equation of quarks in the inhomogeneous field background. By the numerical calculation we show how the phase transition is changed by varying the configuration of the inhomogeneous field background and find that the strong first order phase transition could be weakened or even changed to a crossover as a result.

## Key claim

The authors show that quantum fluctuations from an inhomogeneous scalar field background, calculated via scattering phase shifts, can weaken or even change a strong first-order deconfinement phase transition into a crossover in a finite-volume spherical quark system, but only when the spatial extent of the inhomogeneity exceeds a threshold (e.g., Woods-Saxon width parameter \(r_0 \gtrsim 1.9\ \mathrm{fm}\) for a fixed system radius \(R=3\ \mathrm{fm}\)).

## Method

The grand potential of a finite spherical quark system is computed using the Friedberg-Lee (FL) model. Finite-volume effects are included via the multiple reflection expansion (MRE) method, which adds surface and curvature corrections to the density of states. The inhomogeneous background is modeled as a Woods-Saxon deviation \(\tilde{\sigma}(r)\) from the vacuum sigma field, and the resulting modification to the density of states is obtained from the scattering phase shift of the Dirac equation for quarks in that background, summed over s-wave (\(l=0\)) and p-wave (\(l=1\)) partial waves. The order parameter \(\sigma_v\) (the global minimum of the grand potential) is then tracked as a function of temperature to determine the nature of the phase transition.

## Key numbers

*   For a homogeneous system in the mean-field approximation (MFA), the critical temperature is \(T_c = 120.1\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
*   For a finite spherical system of radius \(R=3\ \mathrm{fm}\) with only MRE corrections (no inhomogeneous background), the critical temperature rises to \(T_c = 136.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
*   For the inhomogeneous system at \(R=3\ \mathrm{fm}\) with fixed \(\beta = 8\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) and \(\alpha \approx 0.26\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\), the first-order transition is weakened when \(r_0\) reaches \(1.5\ \mathrm{fm}\) and changes to a crossover for \(r_0 \gtrsim 1.9\ \mathrm{fm}\).
*   A bound state in the p-wave channel emerges when \(r_0 \gtrsim 1.0\ \mathrm{fm}\) (with \(\beta = 8\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\)), causing a sudden increase in the critical temperature and a marked weakening of the first-order transition.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the FL model that found a first-order deconfinement phase transition at finite temperature in the MFA (Gao 1992, Mao 2008). It also builds on the scattering phase shift method for quantum fluctuations in inhomogeneous backgrounds developed by Schwinger (1954) and Jaffe (1998, 2002). The results are consistent with chiral effective model studies (Skokov 2010, Herbst 2013, Brandes 2021) showing that quantum fluctuations weaken first-order transitions, but here the fluctuations are treated in an inhomogeneous rather than homogeneous background. The work enables future studies of how the spatial profile of a fireball in heavy-ion collisions can affect the order of the QCD phase transition.
