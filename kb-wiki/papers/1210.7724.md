# The statistical model in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC

> We briefly review the predictions of the thermal model for hadron production in comparison to latest data from RHIC and extrapolate the calculations to LHC energy. Our main emphasis is to confront the model predictions with the recently released data from ALICE at the LHC. This comparison reveals an apparent anomaly for protons and anti-protons which we discuss briefly. We also demonstrate that our statistical hadronization predictions for J/$ψ$ production agree very well with the most recent LHC data, lending support to the picture in which there is complete charmonium melting in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) followed by statistical generation of J/$ψ$ mesons at the phase boundary.

## Key claim

The statistical hadronization model successfully describes J/ψ production at LHC energies, supporting the picture of complete charmonium melting in the quark-gluon plasma followed by statistical generation at the phase boundary; however, an apparent anomaly exists for protons and anti-protons, whose yields are about a factor of 1.4 below the calculated values when all other hadron yields are well described.

## Method

The authors performed thermal model fits to ALICE data on hadron production in central Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, using chemical freeze-out temperature T, baryo-chemical potential μb, and fireball volume V as parameters. They compared the model predictions to ALICE data on J/ψ nuclear modification factor RAA as a function of centrality and rapidity, and also compared to RHIC data from PHENIX at √sNN = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Temperature from fit to all ALICE hadron data: T = 152 MeV, with reduced χ² approaching 4
- Temperature from fit excluding protons and anti-protons: T = 164 MeV, with excellent χ²
- Nucleon yields about a factor of 1.4 below calculated values when protons and anti-protons are excluded from the fit
- At LHC energy, much less J/ψ suppression is observed compared to RHIC results at both forward and mid-rapidity
- Expected further increase of RAAJ/ψ of up to 40% at mid-rapidity for central collisions at full LHC energy

## Context

This work extends the statistical model description of hadron production from AGS through RHIC energies (reviewed by Andronic et al. 2006) to LHC energy, confronting predictions with ALICE data. The proton anomaly observed here is similar to the situation at RHIC where PHENIX and BRAHMS proton yields were overpredicted. The successful J/ψ description supports the statistical hadronization model over transport models (Rapp and van Hees 2010; Peshier and Ferreiro 2007) that invoke continuous formation and destruction of charmonia in the QGP. Future measurements of ψ' and χc states with the ALICE upgrade, plus pPb collisions to clarify shadowing contributions, will differentiate between models.
