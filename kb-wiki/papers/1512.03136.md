# Understanding the Nature of $Λ(1405)$ through Regge Physics

> It appears that there are two resonances with $J^P= 1/2^-$ quantum numbers in the energy region near the $Λ(1405)$ hyperon. The nature of these states is a topic of current debate. To provide further insight we use Regge phenomenology to access how these two resonances fit the established hyperon spectrum. We find that only one of these resonances is compatible with a three-quark state.

## Key claim
Using Regge phenomenology, the authors find that only one of the two poles in the Λ(1405) region (the one at 1429−i12 MeV) is compatible with an ordinary three-quark state, while the other pole (at 1325−i90 MeV) does not fit the pattern of a linear Regge trajectory for known three-quark hyperons and is therefore likely a nonordinary state.

## Method
The authors constructed Chew-Frautschi plots (J vs. Re(s_p)) and J vs. -Im(s_p) plots for the leading Λ and Σ Regge trajectories using known hyperon pole positions from the PDG and from a recent coupled-channel K-matrix analysis. They then performed quantitative fits using three different parametrizations of the Regge trajectory (a simple square-root model, a threshold-normalized model, and a dispersive phase-space model) to test whether each of the two Λ(1405) poles could belong to the 0^- leading trajectory.

## Key numbers
- Λ(1405)_a pole mass: 1429(8) MeV, width: 24(6) MeV (from Mai2015)
- Λ(1405)_b pole mass: 1325(15) MeV, width: 180(36) MeV (from Mai2015)
- Λ(1116) ground state mass: 1116 MeV (PDG2014)
- Σ(1192) ground state mass: 1192 MeV (PDG2014)
- Σ(1385) mass: 1385(2) MeV, width: 37(5) MeV (PDG2014)
- Canonical Regge intercept for 0^+ trajectory: α₀ ≈ -0.6, slope α' ≈ 0.9 GeV⁻² (from PDBCollins)
- Canonical Regge intercept for 1^+ trajectory: α₀ ≈ -0.8, slope α' ≈ 0.9 GeV⁻² (from PDBCollins)
- Fitted intercept for 0^+ trajectory (linear fit): α₀ ≈ -0.74, α' ≈ 0.98 GeV⁻²
- Fitted intercept for 1^+ trajectory (linear fit): α₀ ≈ -0.89, α' ≈ 0.92 GeV⁻²

## Context
This work addresses the ongoing debate about whether the Λ(1405) is a three-quark state or a K̅N molecule, following lattice QCD calculations that have given conflicting results (Engel 2013 finding a three-quark state, Hall 2015 finding a molecular state) and phenomenological analyses that find two poles in the region (Mai 2015, Roca 2015). The Regge analysis supports the interpretation that only one of the two poles is a conventional three-quark baryon, consistent with quark-diquark model expectations (Santopinto 2015, Faustov 2015) and large-Nc calculations (LargeNc). The result enables further studies to determine whether the nonordinary Λ(1405)_b is a pentaquark or a molecular state, with the latter favored by existing literature.
