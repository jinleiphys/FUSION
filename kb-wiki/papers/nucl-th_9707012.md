# Dissipative Field Theory with Caldeira-Leggett Method and its Application to Disoriented Chiral Condensation

> The effective field theory including the dissipative effect is developed based on the Caldeira-Leggett theory at the classical level. After the integration of the small field fluctuations considered as the field radiation, the integro-differential field equation is given and shown to include the dissipative effects. In that derivation, special cares should be taken for the boundary condition of the integration. Application to the linear sigma model is given, and the decay process of the chiral condensate is calculated with it, both analytically in the linear approximation and numerically. With these results, we discuss the stability of chiral condensates within the quenched approximation.

## Key claim

The paper develops a dissipative field theory by extending the Caldeira-Leggett method from particle dynamics to field dynamics, and shows that the resulting integro-differential equation for the order parameter contains a dissipative term arising from the imaginary part of the Green function for the background field, provided the advanced Green function is selected to enforce the correct boundary condition.

## Method

The authors start with a system-plus-reservoir Lagrangian coupling an order-parameter field Phi(x) to a background field f(x) with mass m. They integrate out the background field using Green functions, then isolate the imaginary part of the Green function as the dissipative term while absorbing the real part into a redefinition of the effective potential. They apply this formalism to the linear sigma model of Gell-Mann and Levy (1960), decomposing the sigma and pion fields into condensates (order parameters) and fluctuations, then deriving an integro-differential equation for the chiral angle phi that describes the dynamics of the disoriented chiral condensate.

## Key numbers

* Pion decay constant: f_pi = 92.5 MeV
* Nucleon mass: M = 940 MeV
* Pion mass: m_pi = 135 MeV
* Sigma meson mass: m_sigma = 600 MeV
* Coupling constant lambda = 20.0 and nu = 87.4 MeV, derived from the above masses and f_pi
* Dissipative coefficient at zero momentum: gamma = 2 p(0) = 0.7 m_pi
* Life-time of the neutral DCC state: tau_L = 1/p(0) ~ 3 m_pi^{-1}
* Formation time from the quenching scenario (Rajagopal and Walczek, 1993): tau_R ~ sqrt(2) m_sigma^{-1} ~ 0.3 m_pi^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the Caldeira-Leggett method (1981, 1983) from particle dynamics to field theory, enabling the study of dissipative effects in nuclear and hadronic systems with multi-particle emission. The application to the linear sigma model addresses the decay of disoriented chiral condensates (DCC), a topic previously studied by Rajagopal and Walczek (1993) and others, where the authors find that the neutral DCC life-time is about ten times longer than the formation time, suggesting the state is sufficiently metastable to be observable. The formalism enables future extensions beyond the chiral circle approximation and to charged DCC states with photon radiation.
