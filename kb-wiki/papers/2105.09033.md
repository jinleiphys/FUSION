# Recombination in the universal four-fermion system

> In the systems of spin $\frac12$ fermions with resonant $S$-wave interactions supporting only weakly bound dimers the antisymmetry forbids recombination of three (or more) fermions at zero energy. However, the fermion-fermion-dimer recombination is only partially suppressed. It is studied in the framework of momentum-space integral equations for the four-particle transition operators. In the vicinity of the unitary limit the fermion-fermion-dimer recombination rate, rescaled to build dimensionless quantity, is found to be linear in the effective range parameter, enabling a simple and accurate parametrization as well as evaluation of finite-range effects for any potential model. This feature makes the present results very useful in benchmarking different methods for three-cluster breakup and recombination calculations in four-particle systems. The interplay of the three-fermion and fermion-fermion-dimer recombination processes and their consequences for ultracold mixtures of fermions and dimers is discussed.

# Recombination in the universal four-fermion system

## Key claim

The fermion-fermion-dimer recombination rate at zero energy is only partially suppressed by antisymmetry (unlike three-fermion recombination, which is fully forbidden), and in the vicinity of the unitary limit the dimensionless recombination rate is linear in the effective range parameter, enabling a simple parametrization. However, this linearity holds to better than 1% only below r_e/a = 0.1, and a quadratic term provides a more accurate description over the full range studied.

## Method

The author solved the symmetrized Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) integral equations for four-particle transition operators in momentum-space partial-wave representation, using the complex-energy method with special integration weights to handle kernel singularities from one- and two-dimer poles. The two-fermion interaction was modeled by enhancing the CD Bonn potential to produce bound dimers with scattering lengths ranging from 9.1 to 176.1 fm, while setting all higher partial waves (l_x > 0) to zero to focus on S-wave universality. The zero-energy recombination rate was extracted from the three-cluster breakup amplitude at the threshold where the three-cluster kinetic energy vanishes.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless zero-energy fermion-fermion-dimer recombination rate K_4^0 m / (hbar a^4) is parametrized as 10.20 - 18.27 (r_e/a) + 3.47 (r_e/a)^2, where a is the two-fermion scattering length and r_e is the effective range.
- The linear approximation in r_e/a holds to better than 1% for r_e/a below 0.1.
- The two-fermion scattering length a was varied from 9.1 to 176.1 fm, with the effective range r_e staying between 2.14 and 2.56 fm.
- Partial waves with l_y, l_z = 3 contribute about 0.2% to K_4^0, while l_y, l_z > 0 waves contribute about 25% overall.
- At the threshold, the 3+1 and 2+2 configuration contributions to the recombination amplitude cancel by up to 90%.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study of dimer-dimer elastic scattering (Deltuva, 2017) to the recombination channel, using the same Faddeev-Yakubovsky AGS framework. It contrasts with the three-fermion recombination case studied by Esry et al. (2001), where the rate vanishes at zero energy and scales with energy due to antisymmetry constraints. The universal result for the zero-energy recombination rate provides a benchmark for three-cluster breakup and recombination calculations in four-particle systems using different methods, and has implications for understanding dimer production in ultracold mixtures of spin-1/2 fermions and dimers.
