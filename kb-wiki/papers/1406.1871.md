# Universal QGP Hadronization Conditions at RHIC and LHC

> We address the principles governing QGP hadronization and particle production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We argue that chemical non-equilibrium is required and show that once this condition is assumed a very good description of hadron production in collider RHIC and at LHC heavy ion experiments follows. We present results of our analysis as a function of centrality. Comparing most extreme experimental conditions we show that only the reaction volume and degree of strangeness phase space saturation change. We determine the universal QGP fireball hadronization conditions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a chemical non-equilibrium statistical hadronization model (SHM) is required to describe hadron production at RHIC and LHC, and that under this assumption the QGP fireball hadronization conditions are universal across both colliders, with only the reaction volume and strangeness phase space saturation changing between the two.

## Method

The authors fit a statistical hadronization model (SHM) with chemical non-equilibrium parameters to experimental hadron yield data from STAR at RHIC (sqrt(s_NN)=62 GeV) and ALICE at LHC (sqrt(s_NN)=2760 GeV) as a function of collision centrality, characterized by the number of participating nucleons N_part. The model uses quark fugacities composed of a phase space occupancy factor gamma_f and a chemical potential factor lambda_f for light (u,d) and strange (s) quarks, with the net strangeness fixed to zero and the net charge per baryon fixed by lambda_3. The fit yields temperature T, volume V, light quark occupancy gamma_q, and strangeness occupancy gamma_s for each centrality bin.

## Key numbers

- At LHC, the chemical freeze-out temperature for most peripheral collisions is T = 143 +/- 2 MeV, decreasing to below about 140 MeV for most central collisions.
- The light quark phase space occupancy gamma_q saturates near the maximum value gamma_q = 1.63 for all centralities at both RHIC and LHC.
- The strangeness phase space occupancy gamma_s at LHC saturates at gamma_s < 2.1 for centralities with N_part > 150.
- The universal QGP fireball hadronization conditions yield: energy density epsilon = 0.50 +/- 0.05 GeV/fm^3, entropy density sigma = 3.35 +/- 0.30 fm^{-3}, and pressure P = 82 +/- 8 MeV/fm^3.
- The reaction volume at LHC is 2 to 4 times larger than at RHIC.

## Context

This work extends the chemical non-equilibrium SHM approach previously applied to SPS data by Letessier (1998) and to SPS and RHIC data by Rafelski (2009). It demonstrates that the same universal hadronization conditions (energy density, entropy density, pressure) apply at both RHIC and LHC, supporting the picture of sudden hadronization of a supercooled QGP fireball. The results enable further study of the QGP breakup mechanism and the role of strangeness saturation as a function of collision energy and centrality.
