# Pairing gaps in Hartree-Fock Bogoliubov theory with the Gogny D1S interaction

> As part of a program to study odd-A nuclei in the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory, we have developed a new calculational tool to find the HFB minima of odd-A nuclei based on the gradient method and using interactions of Gogny's form. The HFB minimization includes both time-even and time-odd fields in the energy functional, avoiding the commonly used "filling approximation". Here we apply the method to calculate neutron pairing gaps in some representative isotope chains of spherical and deformed nuclei, namely the Z=8,50 and 82 spherical chains and the Z=62 and 92 deformed chains. We find that the gradient method is quite robust, permitting us to carry out systematic surveys involving many nuclei. We find that the time-odd field does not have large effect on the pairing gaps calculated with the Gogny D1S interaction. Typically, adding the T-odd field as a perturbation increases the pairing gap by ~100 keV, but the re-minimization brings the gap back down. This outcome is very similar to results reported for the Skyrme family of nuclear energy density functionals. Comparing the calculated gaps with the experimental ones, we find that the theoretical errors have both signs implying that the D1S interaction has a reasonable overall strength. However, we find some systematic deficiencies comparing spherical and deformed chains and comparing the lighter chains with the heavier ones. The gaps for heavy spherical nuclei are too high, while those for deformed nuclei tend to be too low. The calculated gaps of spherical nuclei show hardly any A-dependence, contrary to the data. Inclusion of the T-odd component of the interaction does not change these qualitative findings.

# Pairing gaps in Hartree-Fock Bogoliubov theory with the Gogny D1S interaction

## Key claim
The time-odd field has only a small effect on neutron pairing gaps calculated with the Gogny D1S interaction: adding the T-odd field as a perturbation increases the pairing gap by roughly 100 keV, but full re-minimization brings the gap back down to values very close to those obtained with the simpler "filling approximation." The calculated gaps show systematic deficiencies compared to experiment, heavy spherical nuclei are overpredicted, deformed nuclei are underpredicted, and spherical gaps show hardly any A-dependence, that are not corrected by inclusion of the T-odd component.

## Method
The authors implemented a gradient method to find Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) minima for odd-A nuclei using the Gogny D1S interaction, including both time-even and time-odd fields in the energy functional and avoiding the commonly used filling approximation. They calculated neutron pairing gaps, defined as Δ^(3)(Z,N) = ½[B(Z,N-1) + B(Z,N+1) - 2B(Z,N)], for five representative isotope chains: the spherical Z=8 (Oxygen), Z=50 (Tin), and Z=82 (Lead) chains, and the deformed Z=62 (Samarium) and Z=92 (Uranium) chains. Calculations used an oscillator basis with N_sh = 10 to 14 shells depending on mass region and a fixed oscillator length b = 2.1 fm.

## Key numbers
- Adding the T-odd field as a perturbation increases the pairing gap by roughly 100 keV, but full re-minimization restores the gap to near the filling approximation value.
- For the Z=8 chain (N=9-15), the experimental average pairing gap is 1.51 MeV; the filling approximation error is -0.41 MeV, the T-odd perturbative error is -0.07 MeV, and the full T-odd error is -0.36 MeV.
- For the Z=50 chain (N=53-83), the experimental average is 1.18 MeV; filling approximation error is +0.06 MeV, T-odd perturbative error is +0.22 MeV, and full T-odd error is +0.06 MeV.
- For the Z=62 chain (N=75-99), the experimental average is 0.99 MeV; filling approximation error is -0.29 MeV, T-odd perturbative error is -0.21 MeV, and full T-odd error is -0.30 MeV.
- For the Z=82 chain (N=97-131), the experimental average is 0.98 MeV; filling approximation error is +0.27 MeV, T-odd perturbative error is +0.37 MeV, and full T-odd error is +0.27 MeV.
- For the Z=92 chain (N=135-147), the experimental average is 0.64 MeV; filling approximation error is -0.19 MeV, T-odd perturbative error is -0.13 MeV, and full T-odd error is -0.21 MeV.
- The Oxygen chain shows a strong gap quenching at N=15, corresponding to an open s_{1/2} shell.
- For strongly deformed nuclei (β₂ > 0.2), the j_z and parity of the theoretical ground state agrees with experiment in only 5 out of 11 cases.

## Context
This work extends the gradient method for odd-A HFB minima developed by Robledo and Bertsch (2011) to systematic surveys across multiple isotope chains, using the Gogny D1S interaction. The results are compared to earlier HFB surveys using the filling approximation with Skyrme functionals by Bertsch et al. (2000, 2009) and to recent studies of time-odd fields in Skyrme functionals by Potel et al. (2010) and Schunck et al. (2010). The finding that time-odd fields have small effect on pairing gaps is consistent with those Skyrme-based studies. The systematic deficiencies identified, particularly the incorrect A-dependence and deformation dependence, point to missing physics beyond the HFB framework, including angular momentum projection, particle number projection (shown by Anguiano et al. (2002) and Bertsch et al. (2009) to affect Skyrme results), induced pairing interactions (Broglia et al.), and coupling to 4-quasiparticle excitations. The authors plan to extend the method to two-quasiparticle wave functions for odd-odd nuclei and to implement angular momentum restoration.
