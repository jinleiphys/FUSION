# From Trapped Atoms to Liberated Quarks

> We discuss some aspects of cold atomic gases in the unitarity limit that are of interest in connection with the physics of dense hadronic matter. We consider, in particular, the equation of state at zero temperature, the magnitude of the pairing gap, and the phase diagram at non-zero polarization.

## Key claim

Cold atomic gases in the unitarity limit provide a simpler, experimentally controllable system that can address many of the same questions as strongly interacting phases of QCD, including the equation of state, pairing gaps, and phase diagrams, with the caveat that the connection is qualitative for the quark-gluon plasma and quantitatively relevant only for dilute neutron matter.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical approaches to cold fermionic gases at the unitarity limit, combining effective field theory with lattice Monte Carlo simulations using a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation of a four-fermion interaction lagrangian. For the equation of state, the author presents results from lattice simulations, Green Function Monte Carlo calculations, and analytical methods including ladder diagram summation and expansions in the number of spatial dimensions. For pairing and stressed pairing, the analysis uses BCS theory, effective lagrangians for Goldstone bosons and baryon fields, and energy functionals to study supercurrent and LOFF-type instabilities.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-neutron scattering length: \(a_{nn} = -18\) fm, effective range \(r_{nn} = 2.8\) fm
- Lattice result for the universal parameter \(\xi\) (energy per particle in units of free Fermi gas): \(\xi = 0.09-0.42\) from Lee and Schafer (2005); canonical simulations at \(T=0\) give \(\xi = 0.25\) (Lee, 2005)
- Green Function Monte Carlo result: \(\xi = 0.44\) (Carlson, 2003)
- Analytical ladder diagram result: \(\xi \simeq 0.32\)
- Expansion around \(d=4\) at next-to-leading order: \(\xi = 0.475\) (Nishida and Son, 2006)
- Critical temperature for the superfluid transition in the unitarity limit: \(T_c = 0.152(7)E_F\) (Burovski et al., 2006)
- Pairing gap at zero temperature from Green Function Monte Carlo: \(\Delta = 0.54E_F\) (Chang, 2004)
- Maximum critical temperature in weak-coupling color superconductivity: \(T_c = 0.025E_F\) at \(g=4.2\); phenomenological extrapolations give \(T_c = 0.15E_F\)
- LOFF window boundaries: \(\delta\mu_1 = \Delta_0/\sqrt{2} \simeq 0.71\Delta_0\) and \(\delta\mu_2 \simeq 0.754\Delta_0\); gap at \(\delta\mu_1\) is \(\Delta \simeq 0.25\Delta_0\)
- Critical strange quark mass for onset of supercurrent in CFL phase: \(\mu_s = (4/3 + ah_{crit}/3)\Delta\)

## Context

This review connects the physics of cold atomic gases in the unitarity limit to dense hadronic matter, specifically dilute neutron matter (where the large neutron-neutron scattering length makes the system strongly correlated even at low density, as noted by Friedman (1981)) and color superconducting quark matter. The work extends earlier studies of the BEC-BCS crossover and the LOFF phase (Larkin and Ovchinnikov, 1964; Fulde and Ferrell, 1964) to the unitarity limit, and draws analogies between the parameter \(\xi\) in atomic gases and the 3/4 ratio of entropy density in strongly coupled \({\cal N}=4\) SUSY Yang-Mills theory from the AdS/CFT correspondence (Maldacena, 1997; Gubser, 1998). The analysis enables future experimental and theoretical work on polarized fermionic gases, transport properties, and the phase structure of QCD at high baryon density.
