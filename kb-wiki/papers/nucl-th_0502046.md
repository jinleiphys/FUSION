# Baryons on the lattice

> I comment on progress of lattice QCD techniques and calculations. Recent results on pentaquark masses as well as of the spectrum of excited baryons are summarizned and interpreted. The present state of calculations of quantities related to the nucleon structure and of electromagnetic transition form factors is surveyed.

# Baryons on the lattice

## Key claim
Lattice QCD has become a precision predictive tool for hadron spectroscopy and structure, but most calculations remain limited to the quenched approximation or to unphysically heavy pion masses above 400 MeV, and pentaquark lattice results remain ambiguous as to whether they represent resonant states or scattering states.

## Method
The paper reviews lattice QCD simulations of baryons, including calculations of hadron masses from the Dirac operator on a discretized space-time lattice with spacing a⁻¹ = 1–4 GeV. Simulations incorporate either the quenched approximation (valence quarks only) or dynamical sea quarks (n_f = 2), with quark propagators computed from fixed source points or, for disconnected diagrams, using stochastic estimator techniques. Results are extrapolated to the physical pion mass using chiral perturbation theory (χPT) fits, including non-analytic terms such as the m_π³ contribution to the nucleon mass.

## Key numbers
- Quenched light hadron masses agree with experiment within 10%.
- String breaking distance r_c ≈ 1.16 fm (extrapolated to physical light quark masses) with energy gap ΔE ≈ 50 MeV resolved at 10 standard deviations.
- Nucleon mass chiral extrapolation uses data with m_π > 550 MeV; the leading non-analytic coefficient a_3 = −3g_A²/(32πF_π²).
- Electromagnetic γ*N → Δ transition form factors studied in quenched QCD over Q² range 0.1–1.4 GeV²; R_EM = G_E2/G_M1 ≈ −0.02(1) in the chiral limit; R_CM = G_C2/G_M1 decreases from −0.01(1) at 0.1 GeV² to −0.09(3) at Q² > 1 GeV².
- Pion masses as low as 180 MeV achieved in quenched simulations using chiral overlap fermions.
- The Θ⁺(1530) pentaquark candidate has mass about 100 MeV above the KN threshold with width Γ ≪ 10 MeV.

## Context
This review surveys lattice QCD progress in the mid-2000s, extending earlier quenched calculations by groups such as CP-PACS, JLQCD, UKQCD, and QCDSF (AliKhan et al., 2001; Allton et al., 2001). It interprets pentaquark studies by the Kentucky-Washington group (Mathur et al., 2004) and others, and compares excited baryon results from the BGR Collaboration (Burch et al., 2004) and the Kentucky group (Dong et al., 2003) with experiment. The work enables future precision simulations combining light sea quarks, all-to-all propagators, and chiral fermion actions, which the author estimates may take up to a decade to achieve simultaneously.
