# Light Quarkonium - Glueball Mixing from a Holographic QCD

> We study the mixing structure of isospin-singlet scalars, the light quarkonium $(\bar{q}q)$ and glueball $(gg)$ in two-flavor QCD, based on a holographic model of bottom-up hard-wall type. In the model the pure quarkonium and glueball states are unambiguously defined in terms of the different $U(1)_A$ charges in the restoration limit of the chiral $U(2)_L \times U(2)_R$ symmetry, in which the quarkonium gets massless as the chiral partner of the pion. Hence the $\bar{q}q$-$gg$ mixing arises in the presence of the nonzero chiral condensate or pion decay constant. At the realistic point where the pion decay constant and other hadron masses reach the observed amount, we predict the tiny mixing between the lightest quarkonia and glueball: The smallness of the mixing is understood by the slightly small ratio of the chiral and gluon condensate scales. The low-lying two scalar masses are calculated to be $\simeq 1.25$ GeV and $\simeq 1.77$ GeV, which are compared with masses of $f_0(1370)$ and $f_0(1710)$. Our result implies that $f_0(1710)$ predominantly consists of glueball.

## Key claim

The paper predicts a tiny mixing between the lightest quarkonium and glueball states in two-flavor QCD, with the smallness explained by the somewhat small ratio of the chiral condensate scale to the gluon condensate scale at the realistic point where the pion decay constant and other hadron masses reach observed values.

## Method

The authors employ a bottom-up hard-wall holographic QCD model with five-dimensional bulk fields dual to the gluon condensate operator \(G_{\mu\nu}^2\) and the chiral condensate operator \(\bar{q}q\). Pure quarkonium and glueball states are defined unambiguously in the chiral restoration limit (\(U(2)_L \times U(2)_R\) symmetry limit with \(\langle \bar{q}q \rangle\) and \(f_\pi \to 0\)), and the mixing is evaluated by computing off-diagonal mass-squared elements between these pure states as the chiral condensate develops from zero to the realistic value.

## Key numbers

- The optimal parameter values reproducing experimental observables are: \(\xi \simeq 3.1\), \(G \simeq 0.25\), and \(z_m^{-1} \simeq 347\) MeV, yielding \(f_\pi \simeq 92\) MeV, \(m_\rho \simeq 775\) MeV, and \(m_{a_1} \simeq 1264\) MeV.
- The chiral condensate is predicted as \(\langle -\bar{q}q \rangle \simeq (277\ \text{MeV})^3\) and the gluon condensate as \(\langle \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} G_{\mu\nu}^2 \rangle^{1/4} \simeq (331\ \text{MeV})^4\).
- The low-lying two scalar masses after mixing are calculated to be \(m_1 \simeq 1.25\) GeV and \(m_2 \simeq 1.77\) GeV, compared with masses of \(f_0(1370)\) and \(f_0(1710)\).
- The mass-squared matrix (in GeV\(^2\)) for the three lowest states is: diagonal elements \((1.25)^2\), \((1.77)^2\), and \((2.26)^2\) with off-diagonal mixing elements \((0.14)^2\) and \(-(0.12)^2\).
- The ratio of condensate scales is \((\Lambda_{\bar{q}q}/\Lambda_{gg})^3 \simeq (0.84)^3 \simeq 0.6\) at the realistic point.

## Context

This work extends holographic QCD studies of scalar meson mixing, specifically building on the model of Haba (2010) which simultaneously incorporates bulk fields dual to both gluon and chiral condensate operators. The results are consistent with the pure glueball mass estimate from lattice simulations by Morningstar (1999) and with a phenomenological model analysis by Janowski (2014) indicating that \(f_0(1710)\) is predominantly a glueball. The approach provides a parameter-free prediction of the mixing strength without using phenomenological inputs from observed scalar decay properties, in contrast to effective hadron model approaches.
