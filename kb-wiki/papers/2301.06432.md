# Neutron Stars in Modified Teleparallel Gravity

> We investigate compact objects in modified teleparallel gravity with realistic equations of state. We propose a modification of Teleparallel Equivalent to General Relativity, then an appropriate tetrad is applied to the field equations. A specific set of relations showing an equivalency between our gravitational model and the New General Relativity is found. The conservation equation implies that our Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are presented with an effective gravitational coupling constant. Numerical analysis using realistic equations of state is made, and the behavior of mass, radius, and the relation mass-radius as functions of a free parameter of our model is also investigated.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a modified teleparallel gravity model, which introduces free parameters into the torsion scalar Lagrangian, produces Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations structurally identical to those of Teleparallel Equivalent to General Relativity (TEGR) but with an effective gravitational coupling constant \(\bar{\chi} = \chi / \beta_3\); the conservation equation is preserved only under the constraint \(2\beta_3 - \beta_2 - \beta_1 = 0\), which is derived for static spherically symmetric systems and may not hold in general.

## Method

The authors modify the TEGR action by replacing the tensor \(S^{\rho\mu\nu}\) with a general linear combination \(\Sigma^{\rho\mu\nu}\) parameterized by \(\beta_1, \beta_2, \beta_3\). They apply a non-diagonal "good tetrad" for a static spherically symmetric metric to the field equations, impose the conservation equation to obtain the constraint \(2\beta_3 - \beta_2 - \beta_1 = 0\), and derive TOV-like equations with an effective gravitational constant \(\bar{\chi} = \chi / \beta_3\). These equations are solved numerically using two relativistic mean-field equations of state (FSUGold2 and Z271v6) with charge neutrality and \(\beta\)-equilibrium conditions, and the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland crust EoS.

## Key numbers

- For the Z271v6 EoS with \(\beta_3 = 0.8\), the maximum mass is approximately \(1.43 M_\odot\); with \(\beta_3 = 1.2\), the maximum mass is approximately \(1.75 M_\odot\).
- For the FSUGold2 EoS with \(\beta_3 = 0.8\), the maximum mass is approximately \(1.85 M_\odot\); with \(\beta_3 = 1.2\), the maximum mass is approximately \(2.27 M_\odot\).
- For \(\beta_3 = 1\) (TEGR/GR limit), the maximum mass is \(1.60 M_\odot\) for Z271v6 and \(2.08 M_\odot\) for FSUGold2.
- Varying \(\beta_3\) from 0.86 to 2.76 yields radii from 9.76 km to 17.49 km for Z271v6, and from 11.23 km to 20.11 km for FSUGold2.
- For FSUGold2, the allowed range of \(\beta_3\) from observational constraints is \(0.430 \leq \beta_3 \leq 2.170\); for Z271v6, the range is \(0.580 \leq \beta_3 \leq 2.790\).
- The overlapping interval accommodating both EoS parameterizations is \(0.58 \leq \beta_3 \leq 2.17\).

## Context

This work extends the study of compact objects in modified teleparallel gravity, building on the New General Relativity framework of Hayashi (1979) and the TEGR formalism of Aldrovandi (1995) and Andrade (2000). It applies realistic nuclear equations of state (FSUGold2 from the FSUGold2 paper, Z271v6 from the Z271v6 paper) to neutron star structure, contrasting with earlier studies that used simpler EoS. The results enable future comparison with cosmological constraints on \(\beta_3\) to distinguish between EoS parameterizations, and the model is tested against observational data from NICER (PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620), LIGO-VIRGO events (GW170817, GW190425, GW190814), and pulsar mass measurements (PSR J0952-0607).
