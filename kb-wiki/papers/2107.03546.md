# Parton evolution time dependent transverse shape asymmetry in peripheral Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV

> In this paper, we use a multi-phase transport model to simulate the parton evolution time-dependent transverse asymmetry in peripheral Pb-Pb (b=14-15 fm) and p-Pb (b=0-1 fm) collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$= 5.02 TeV, respectively. The simulated results showed that the initial spatial asymmetry depends on the collision system, while both the initial momentum asymmetry and final momentum asymmetry are independent on the collision system. It is also shown that the final momentum asymmetry is similar to the initial momentum asymmetry. Furthermore, the averaged transverse shape asymmetry, which includes the initial spatial asymmetry, the initial momentum asymmetry, and the final momentum asymmetry, is significantly dependent on the transverse momentum and particle species identity (PID). The PID scales ordering transverse asymmetry indicated that it provides a possible observation for studying the fluctuating droplet properties of quark-gluon plasma, which may produce in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Using a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model, the simulated results show that initial spatial asymmetry depends on the collision system (Pb-Pb vs. p-Pb), while both initial and final momentum asymmetry are independent of the collision system; the final momentum asymmetry is similar to the initial momentum asymmetry, and the averaged transverse shape asymmetry depends significantly on transverse momentum and particle species identity (PID).

## Method

The study used the AMPT model with specific shear viscosity η/s = 0.273 and Lund string fragmentation parameters (a=0.5, b=0.9 GeV⁻², αs=0.33, μ=3.2 fm⁻¹) to simulate peripheral Pb-Pb collisions at impact parameter b=14-15 fm and p-Pb collisions at b=0-1 fm, both at √sNN = 5.02 TeV. For each impact parameter, 5×10⁵ events were simulated, and the transverse shape asymmetry A(x) was computed event-by-event for spatial and momentum coordinates at different parton evolution times. All charged particles were considered in the range 0.3 < pT < 3.0 GeV and |η| < 2.40, with events selected to have final charged hadron multiplicity Mch ∈ [60, 100].

## Key numbers

- Impact parameters: Pb-Pb collisions at b=14-15 fm; p-Pb collisions at b=0-1 fm
- Center-of-mass energy: √sNN = 5.02 TeV
- Specific shear viscosity: η/s = 0.273
- Lund string parameters: a=0.5, b=0.9 GeV⁻², αs=0.33, μ=3.2 fm⁻¹
- Number of simulated events: 5×10⁵ per impact parameter
- Final averaged transverse momentum asymmetry: ⟨A(pT^final)⟩ (Pb-Pb) ≈ 0.67; ⟨A(pT^final)⟩ (p-Pb) ≈ 0.69
- Pearson correlation coefficients between final and initial transverse momentum asymmetry: C[A(pT^initial), A(pT^final)] (Pb-Pb) ≈ 0.21; C[A(pT^initial), A(pT^final)] (p-Pb) ≈ 0.18
- PID scales ordering of final averaged transverse momentum asymmetry: ⟨A(pT^final)⟩(charged pions) ≥ ⟨A(pT^final)⟩(charged kaons) ≥ ⟨A(pT^final)⟩(protons)

## Context

This work extends studies of geometric fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the role of subnucleon-scale fluctuations in small collision systems as discussed by Noronha-Hostler (2016) and Mantysaari (2020). It builds on the recognition that hot spot fluctuations in subnucleons are crucial for understanding initial-stage geometry, as shown by Schlichting (2014) and Mantysaari (2016), and that geometric fluctuations of hot spots in the projectile are the main source of eccentricity (Demirci, 2021). The study enables future investigation of the fluctuating droplet properties of quark-gluon plasma that may be produced in heavy-ion collisions, with the PID-dependent transverse asymmetry providing a possible experimental observable.
