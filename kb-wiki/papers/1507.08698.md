# Frustrated fragmentation and re-aggregation in nuclei: a non-equilibrium description in spallation

> Heavy nuclei bombarded with protons and deuterons in the 1 GeV range have a large probability of undergoing a process of evaporation and fission; less frequently, the prompt emission of few intermediate-mass fragments can also be observed. We employ a recently developed microscopic approach, based on the Boltzmann-Langevin transport equation, to investigate the role of mean-field dynamics and phase-space fluctuations in these reactions. We find that the formation of few IMF's can be confused with asymmetric fission when relying on yield observables, but it can not be assimilated to the statistical decay of a compound nucleus when analysing the dynamics and kinematic observables: it can be described as a fragmentation process initiated by phase-space fluctuations, and successively frustrated by the mean-field resilience. As an extreme situation, which corresponds to non-negligible probability, the number of fragments in the exit channel reduces to two, so that fission-like events are obtained by re-aggregation processes. This interpretation, inspired by nuclear-spallation experiments, can be generalised to heavy-ion collisions from Fermi to relativistic energies, for situations when the system is closely approaching the fragmentation threshold.

## Key claim

The formation of few intermediate-mass fragments (IMFs) in spallation reactions can be confused with asymmetric fission when relying only on yield observables, but it cannot be assimilated to the statistical decay of a compound nucleus when analyzing dynamics and kinematic observables; instead, it is a fragmentation process initiated by phase-space fluctuations and successively frustrated by mean-field resilience, with the extreme case of re-aggregation producing fission-like events.

## Method

The authors employed the Boltzmann-Langevin One Body (BLOB) transport approach, which solves the Boltzmann-Langevin transport equation in three dimensions to describe the time evolution of the semiclassical one-body distribution function in its own self-consistent mean field. The heated target system was initialized by processing a simplified cascade induced by the incoming light projectile, calculating the energy deposited and its phase-space distribution, then the dynamics of the heavy nucleus was followed without any a priori assumption on the degree of equilibration. The model uses a Skyrme-like (SKM*) effective interaction with a soft isoscalar equation of state (compressibility K = 200 MeV) and treats fluctuations through binary collisions involving extended phase-space agglomerates of test particles.

## Key numbers

* The compressibility of the isoscalar equation of state was set to K = 200 MeV, corresponding to a soft equation of state.
* The potential energy per nucleon parameters were A = -356 MeV, B = 303 MeV, and sigma = 7/6.
* The symmetry energy was taken as constant C_sym = 32 MeV (linear density dependence).
* The nucleon-nucleon cross section was taken equal to the free cross section with a cutoff at 100 mb.
* The potential depth used for calculating transmission probability at the nuclear boundary was 40 MeV.

## Context

This work extends dynamical descriptions of spallation reactions beyond earlier statistical approaches (Serber 1947, Thomas 1968, Sanders 1999) and prior stochastic one-body approaches (Colonna 1997) that treated fluctuations incompletely. It builds on experimental observations from inverse-kinematics experiments at GSI (Napolitani 2007, Napolitani 2011) and the Spalladin campaign (Le Gentil 2008, Gorbinet 2011, Gorbinet 2012) that revealed two kinematic contributions to IMF production. The interpretation can be generalized to heavy-ion collisions from Fermi to relativistic energies for systems approaching the fragmentation threshold, and it enables a fully dynamical description of the transition between multifragmentation and compound-nucleus decay without assuming equilibration.
