# Tests of the Quark-Gluon Plasma Coupling Strength at Early Times with Heavy Quarks

> The redistribution in momentum space of heavy quarks via their interactions in the quark-gluon plasma is an excellent probe of the heavy quark coupling strength to the medium. We utilize a Monte Carlo Langevin calculation for tracking heavy quark - antiquark pairs embedded in a viscous hydrodynamic space-time evolution. We find that the nuclear modification factor ($R_{AA}$) for charm quarks is relatively insensitive to the coupling to the quark-gluon plasma at early times where the highest temperatures are acheived. In contrast the azimuthal angular correlation of charm and anticharm quarks is extremely sensitive to the early time evolution. For beauty quarks the situation is reversed in terms of sensitivity. Future measurements of heavy quarks have the potential to map out the temperature dependence of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s).

## Key claim

The nuclear modification factor ($R_{AA}$) for charm quarks is relatively insensitive to the coupling strength to the quark-gluon plasma at early times where the highest temperatures are achieved, while the azimuthal angular correlation of charm and anticharm quarks is extremely sensitive to the early time evolution; for beauty quarks the sensitivity pattern is reversed.

## Method

The authors implemented a Monte Carlo Langevin calculation to trace the diffusion and drag of individual heavy quark-antiquark pairs embedded in a viscous hydrodynamic space-time evolution generated by the Luzum and Romatschke code. Initial charm and beauty quark transverse momentum distributions were selected from a power-law parameterization with $\alpha = 3.9$ (charm) and $\alpha = 4.9$ (beauty), with initial positions sampled from a Monte Carlo Glauber distribution for central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV. The Langevin equation was evolved in time steps of 0.025 fm/c through the hydrodynamic background, with the diffusion parameter $D$ controlling both viscous drag and stochastic scattering via the fluctuation-dissipation theorem.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark $R_{AA}$ at low $p_T$ (below 2.0 GeV/c) shows only $\pm$ 10% variation despite an eight-fold variation of the diffusion parameter $D$ (from $D = 0.5 \times 3/(2\pi T)$ to $D = 4 \times 3/(2\pi T)$)
- The initial heavy quark transverse momentum distribution uses $\alpha = 3.9$ and $\Lambda = 2.1$ for charm quarks, and $\alpha = 4.9$ and $\Lambda = 7.5$ for beauty quarks
- An initial transverse momentum $k_T$ sampled from a normal distribution with $\mu = 0$ and $\sigma^2 = 1.0$ GeV/c is added to each heavy quark pair
- The Langevin time step is 0.025 fm/c, tested to be stable when increased by a factor of 10
- The diffusion parameter is related to $\eta/s$ via $D(T) = (\eta/s) \times 6/T$, based on the relation from Adare (2006)

## Context

This work extends the Langevin approach of Moore and Teaney (2004) by coupling heavy quark diffusion to a full viscous hydrodynamic space-time evolution, rather than using a static or blast-wave medium model. The authors find that the blast-wave model prediction from Batsouli (2002) for $D$ meson $R_{AA}$ in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV cannot be reproduced by partonic-stage interactions alone, suggesting that hadronic mechanisms such as coalescence (as in PhysRevLett.110.112301) may be relevant. The results enable future experimental measurements to map out the temperature dependence of $\eta/s$ by combining $R_{AA}$ and azimuthal correlation observables for charm and beauty quarks.
