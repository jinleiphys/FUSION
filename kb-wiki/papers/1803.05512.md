# $ΛΛ$ pairing in multi-strange hypernuclei

> Multi-strange Ca, Sn and Pb hypernuclei with $ΛΛ$ pairing interaction are investigated within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach. The unknown $ΛΛ$ pairing strength is calibrated to match with the maximal value for the prediction of the $Λ$ pairing gap in uniform matter for densities and isospin asymmetries equivalent to those existing in multi-$Λ$ hypernuclei. In this way, we provide an upper bound for the prediction of the $Λ$ pairing gap and its effects in hypernuclei. The condensation energy is predicted to be about 3~MeV as a maximum value, yielding small corrections on density distributions and shell structure. In addition, conditions on both Fermi energies and orbital angular momenta are expected to quench the nucleon-$Λ$ pairing for most of hypernuclei.

## Key claim

The paper provides an upper bound for the $\Lambda$ pairing gap in multi-strange hypernuclei by calibrating the unknown $\Lambda\Lambda$ pairing strength to the maximal predicted value from uniform matter calculations, showing that even under this optimistic scenario the pairing effects remain weak.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations for multi-strange Ca, Sn, and Pb hypernuclei with closed proton and neutron shells, using the SLy5 Skyrme interaction for the nucleon channel and three different density functionals (DF-NSC89, DF-NSC97a, DF-NSC97f) for the N$\Lambda$ channel with the EmpC prescription for the $\Lambda\Lambda$ channel. The $\Lambda\Lambda$ pairing strength was calibrated by iterating from -50 to -300 MeV fm$^3$ until the average mean pairing gap over each isotopic chain matched the $\Lambda$ pairing gap predictions from uniform hypernuclear matter calculations at corresponding densities and Fermi momenta.

## Key numbers

- The condensation energy from $\Lambda\Lambda$ pairing is predicted to be about 3 MeV as a maximum value
- The $\Lambda$ pairing gap in uniform matter at saturation density and $k_{F_\Lambda} \approx 0.8$ fm$^{-1}$ reaches a maximum of about 0.5-0.8 MeV across different predictions
- The average mean pairing gaps in hypernuclei are typically 0.5 MeV, with specific values: 0.59 MeV (DF-NSC89+EmpC for Ca chain), 0.50 MeV (DF-NSC97a+EmpC for Ca chain), 0.49 MeV (DF-NSC97f+EmpC for Ca chain)
- For Sn isotopes, average mean gaps are 0.46 MeV (DF-NSC89+EmpC), 0.45 MeV (DF-NSC97a+EmpC), and 0.46 MeV (DF-NSC97f+EmpC)
- For Pb isotopes, average mean gaps are 0.44 MeV (DF-NSC89+EmpC), 0.46 MeV (DF-NSC97a+EmpC), and 0.44 MeV (DF-NSC97f+EmpC)
- The average single-particle gap between neighboring $\Lambda$ orbitals is generally larger than 4 MeV, except for 2s-1d and 3s-2d gaps which are between 1 and 3 MeV
- The $\Lambda$ pairing strengths calibrated range from -139 to -220 MeV fm$^3$ depending on the force set and hypernucleus

## Context

This work extends previous studies of multi-strange hypernuclei using density functional approaches (DF-NSC89, DF-NSC97a-f, DF-ESC08) by Khan and Margueron (2015) and others, which had difficulty reproducing experimental bond energies of double-$\Lambda$ hypernuclei like $^6_{\Lambda\Lambda}$He and $^{10}_{\Lambda\Lambda}$Be. The paper provides the first investigation of $\Lambda$ pairing in finite hypernuclei, complementing BCS calculations of pairing gaps in hypernuclear matter by Raduta and others (2017). The results enable future studies to incorporate $\Lambda$ pairing effects in multi-strange hypernuclei and suggest that nucleon-$\Lambda$ pairing is quenched for most hypernuclei due to mismatches in Fermi energies and orbital angular momenta, except possibly for about 10% of heavy Pb hypernuclei.
