# Nucleon Form Factors in a Covariant Diquark-Quark Model

> In a model where constituent quarks and diquarks interact through quark exchange the Bethe-Salpeter equation in ladder approximation for the nucleon is solved. Quark and diquark confinement is effectively parametrized by choosing appropriately modified propagators. The coupling to external currents is implemented via nontrivial vertex functions for quarks and diquarks to ensure gauge invariance at the constituent level. Nucleon matrix elements are evaluated in a generalised impulse approximation, and electromagnetic, pionic and axial form factors are calculated.

## Key claim

The paper presents a covariant diquark-quark model of the nucleon where confinement is effectively parametrized by modifying propagators to remove poles, but the authors note that this introduces an essential singularity at timelike infinite momentum and prohibits the use of dispersion relations, meaning the model deliberately violates unitarity by construction.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in ladder approximation for the nucleon as a bound state of a constituent quark and a scalar diquark interacting through quark exchange, using a Yukawa coupling. Quark and diquark confinement is mimicked by multiplying free propagators by a factor (1 - exp[-d(p²+m²)/m²]) that removes poles at finite timelike momenta. The Bethe-Salpeter vertex functions are expanded in hyperspherical (Gegenbauer) harmonics, and the integral equations are solved numerically as eigenvalue problems for the coupling constant g_s at a given bound state mass M.

## Key numbers

- For type I (non-confining) propagators with M = m_q, the eigenvalue g_s² converges to 16.0812 with m_max = 3; for M = 1.9 m_q, g_s² = 9.8485; for M = 1.99 m_q, g_s² = 8.1494.
- For type II (confining) propagators with d=1 and M = m_q, the eigenvalue g_s² converges to 18.2507 with m_max = 3; for M = 1.9 m_q, g_s² = 14.0487; for M = 1.99 m_q, g_s² = 13.5918.
- The momentum cutoff parameter Λ is set to 2 m_q, where m_q is the constituent quark mass (equal to the scalar diquark mass m_s in this calculation).
- For k_max ≥ 30 grid points in momentum space, eigenvalues and eigenvectors become independent of mesh size.

## Context

This work extends the diquark-quark model approach previously studied by Buck et al. (1992) and Kuss et al. (1997), who solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation with a static approximation for quark exchange. The present paper goes beyond that by retaining the full momentum dependence of the vertex functions, which is necessary for form factor calculations. The confining propagator parametrization is motivated by studies of the Munczek-Nemirovsky model (1983) and recent work by Bender et al. (1996) showing that diquarks become confined when a non-trivial quark-gluon vertex is included. The next step is to include axialvector diquarks and calculate electromagnetic, pionic, and axial form factors using Mandelstam's formalism.
