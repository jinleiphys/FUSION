# Recent progress on in-medium properties of heavy mesons from finite-temperature EFTs

> Mesons with heavy flavor content are an exceptional probe of the hot QCD medium produced in heavy-ion collisions. In the past few years, significant progress has been made toward describing the modification of the properties of heavy mesons in the hadronic phase at finite temperature. Ground-state and excited-state thermal spectral properties can be computed within a self-consistent many-body approach that employs appropriate hadron-hadron effective interactions, providing a unique opportunity to confront hadronic Effective Field Theory predictions with recent and forthcoming lattice QCD simulations and experimental data. In this article, we revisit the application of the imaginary-time formalism to extend the calculation of unitarized scattering amplitudes from the vacuum to finite temperature. These methods allow us to obtain the ground-state thermal spectral functions. The thermal properties of the excited states that are dynamically generated within the molecular picture are also directly accessible. We present here the results of this approach for the open-charm and open-bottom sectors. We also analyze how the heavy-flavor transport properties, which are strongly correlated to experimental observables in heavy-ion collisions, are modified in hot matter. In particular, transport coefficients can be computed using an off-shell kinetic theory that is fully consistent with the effective theory describing the scattering processes. The results of this procedure for both charm and bottom transport coefficients are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that thermal effective field theory (EFT) methods, combining unitarized heavy-meson chiral perturbation theory with the imaginary-time formalism, can compute the in-medium modifications of both ground-state and excited-state spectral properties for open-charm and open-bottom mesons in hot hadronic matter, with the caveat that the results rely on the molecular interpretation of the excited states and on self-consistent iterative solutions of coupled equations.

## Method

The authors employ heavy-meson chiral perturbation theory (HMChPT) at next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion and leading order in the heavy-quark mass expansion to construct hadron-hadron interaction potentials. They then unitarize these potentials via the Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels, using the imaginary-time formalism to incorporate thermal corrections into the two-meson propagator and heavy-meson self-energy. The resulting set of coupled equations for the scattering amplitude, self-energy, and spectral function is solved iteratively until self-consistency is achieved. Transport coefficients are subsequently computed using an off-shell kinetic theory derived from Kadanoff-Baym equations, which is fully consistent with the underlying effective theory.

## Key numbers

- The chiral phase transition temperature is taken as \(T_c = 156\) MeV, the temperature at which the hadronic medium forms in heavy-ion collisions.
- The hard cutoff used to regularize the vacuum two-meson propagator is \(\Lambda = 800\) MeV, consistent with the regularization scheme of Guo (2018).
- The mass difference between the \(D\) and \(D^*\) ground states is approximately 140 MeV, which mirrors the mass difference between the \(D_{s0}^*(2317)\) and \(D_{s1}(2460)\) excited states.
- The reported mass of the \(D_0^*(2300)\) ranges from approximately 2300 MeV (from \(B\)-meson decays) to approximately 2400 MeV (from \(\gamma A\) reactions), depending on the production mechanism.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous self-consistent thermal EFT calculations for charmed mesons (Montana 2020, Montana 2021) to the bottom sector, providing predictions for \(\bar B\) meson thermal properties and transport coefficients. The approach builds on the molecular interpretation of states such as the \(D_{s0}^*(2317)\) and \(D_0^*(2300)\), which were first identified as hadronic molecules by Kolomeitsev (2003) and Guo (2006), and has been validated against lattice QCD results at the level of Euclidean correlators (Montana 2020). The results enable direct comparison with forthcoming lattice QCD simulations and experimental data from heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC, including femtoscopy measurements of \(D\) meson scattering parameters by the ALICE and STAR collaborations.
