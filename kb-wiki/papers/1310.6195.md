# Studying the high x frontier with A Fixed-Target ExpeRiment at the LHC

> The opportunities which are offered by a next generation and multi-purpose fixed-target experiment exploiting the proton and lead LHC beams extracted by a bent crystal are outlined. In particular, such an experiment can greatly complement facilities with lepton beams by unraveling the partonic structure of polarised and unpolarised nucleons and of nuclei, especially at large momentum fractions.

# Studying the high x frontier with A Fixed-Target ExpeRiment at the LHC

## Key claim
A fixed-target experiment using bent-crystal-extracted LHC proton and lead beams can access the largely uncharted region of parton momentum fraction up to x → 1 in the target, complementing lepton-beam facilities, provided that the detector is designed as a multi-purpose apparatus capable of measuring Drell-Yan pairs, quarkonium, isolated photons, and heavy-flavour production.

## Method
The authors propose extracting a fraction of the LHC beam loss using a bent crystal, directing 3.5 TeV protons or 1.38 TeV per nucleon lead ions onto fixed targets (solid or liquid hydrogen, liquid deuterium, or lead). The resulting center-of-mass energies are √s_NN = 115 GeV for proton-nucleus collisions and √s_NN = 72 GeV for lead-nucleus collisions, with a laboratory-to-center-of-momentum boost of γ ≈ 60 for the 7 TeV proton beam. The experiment would measure Drell-Yan pair production, heavy quarkonium (J/ψ, Υ, χ_c), open heavy flavour, isolated photons, and associated production channels to probe parton distributions at large x in the target rapidity region.

## Key numbers
- Center-of-mass energy in proton-nucleus collisions: √s_{NN} = 115 GeV
- Center-of-mass energy in lead-nucleus collisions: √s_{NN} = 72 GeV
- Extracted proton beam intensity: 5 × 10^8 p^+ s^{-1}
- Extracted lead beam intensity: 2 × 10^5 Pb/s
- Integrated luminosity for proton on liquid hydrogen target: 20000 pb^{-1} per year (10^7 s)
- Integrated luminosity for proton on liquid deuterium target: 24000 pb^{-1} per year
- Integrated luminosity for proton on lead target: 160 pb^{-1} per year
- Expected Drell-Yan single-transverse-spin asymmetries in the backward region up to 10% in the mass region 4 < M < 9 GeV
- Laboratory-to-center-of-momentum boost factor: γ ≈ 60

## Context
This work extends the concept of the AFTER proposal (Brodsky 2012) by detailing the physics reach for parton distribution measurements at high x, a region where uncertainties in nuclear PDFs are larger than at low x and do not decrease with scale. The proposed experiment would complement the low-x reach of the LHeC (Abelleira Fernandez 2012) and the intermediate-x coverage of COMPASS, RHIC, and their upgrades (which do not go beyond x ~ 0.3), as well as the E906 Drell-Yan experiment (Reimer 2011). The bent-crystal extraction technique has been successfully tested at SPS (Arduini 1997), Fermilab (Asseev 1997), and Protvino (Afonin 2012), and will be tested at the LHC by the LUA9 collaboration. The measurements would enable precision studies of the gluon Sivers effect (following the PHENIX attempt by Adare 2010), the Boer-Mulders effect for gluons, intrinsic charm and bottom components in the nucleon (following Pumplin 2007 and Brodsky 2012), and nuclear matter effects on quarkonium production at RHIC energies.
