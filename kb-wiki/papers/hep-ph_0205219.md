# Crystalline Color Superconductivity: Effective Lagrangian and Phonon Dispersion Law

> The recent study by Bowers and Rajagopal on the possible structures for the QCD crystalline color superconducting phase favors a cubic structure and allows for a first discussion of the QCD dynamics in the crystalline phase. The cubic structure leads to complete breaking of traslation invariance and to three phonon modes. We discuss the expected form of the effective Lagrangian and the phonon dispersion laws assuming the proposed crystal structure. Besides the interest for QCD and the related possible relevance to compact stars these considerations may become useful to atomic systems exhibiting crystalline superfluidity.

## Key claim

Assuming the face-centered cubic crystal structure favored by Bowers and Rajagopal (2002) for the QCD crystalline color superconducting phase, the effective low-energy Lagrangian for the three resulting phonon modes and their dispersion laws can be derived, with the dispersion depending on three arbitrary parameters (a, b, c) that reflect the cubic symmetry of the crystal.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian for three scalar fields representing phonon degrees of freedom, imposing invariance under translations, rotations, and the cubic group O_h. They expand the Lagrangian to second order in spatial derivatives of the phonon fields around the vacuum expectation value, obtaining a general form with three independent parameters. The phonon dispersion laws are then derived by diagonalizing the resulting 3x3 matrix, and the results are specialized for propagation along the symmetry axes of the cube (C_4, C_3, and C_2 axes).

## Key numbers

- The condensate is built from eight wave vectors of equal length q = π/a, where a is the lattice spacing.
- For propagation along the 4-fold axes (e.g., n = (1,0,0)): v_1² = a + b, v_2² = v_3² = a.
- For propagation along the 3-fold axes (e.g., n = (1,1,1)/√3): v_1² = a + (b+2c)/3, v_2² = v_3² = a + (b-c)/3.
- For propagation along the 2-fold axes (e.g., n = (1,1,0)/√2): v_1² = a + (b+c)/2, v_2² = a + (b-c)/2, v_3² = a.
- Stability constraints require: a > 0, a + b > 0, b + 2a > |c|, and a + (b+2c)/3 > 0.

## Context

This work extends the single-plane-wave LOFF phase analysis of Casalbuoni et al. (2001), which produced only one phonon mode, to the face-centered cubic crystal structure identified by Bowers and Rajagopal (2002) as the favored condensate pattern in a Ginzburg-Landau expansion. The cubic structure leads to complete breaking of translation invariance and three phonon modes. The results enable future calculation of the parameters a, b, c from microscopic QCD dynamics, and may also apply to atomic systems exhibiting crystalline superfluidity, such as fermionic atom gases below their Fermi temperature.
