# Order and Chaos in Roto--Vibrational States of Atomic Nuclei

> Using a classical analytical criterion (that of curvature) and numerical results (Poincarè sections and spectral statistics), a transition order--chaos--order in the roto--vibrational model of atomic nuclei has been shown. Numerical calculations were performed for some deformed nuclei.

## Key claim
Using the curvature criterion and numerical simulations, the authors demonstrate an order–chaos–order transition in the roto-vibrational model of atomic nuclei as a function of energy and rotational frequency, but note that the curvature criterion guarantees only local instability and must be combined with Poincaré sections for global characterization.

## Method
The classical Hamiltonian of the Bohr–Mottelson roto-vibrational model was expressed in terms of deformation parameters \(a_0\) and \(a_2\) (related to \(\beta\) and \(\gamma\)). The curvature criterion was applied to the effective potential \(W(a_0,a_2)\) to identify regions of negative curvature where chaotic motion may appear. Classical trajectories were integrated using a fourth-order Runge–Kutta method, and Poincaré sections were constructed for several deformed nuclei. Spectral statistics (nearest-neighbor spacing distribution \(P(s)\) and \(\Delta_3\) statistic) were computed from quantum energy levels obtained via Euclidean path integral methods.

## Key numbers
- For \(^{160}\text{Gd}\), Poincaré sections were computed at energy \(5.5\) MeV for different rotational frequencies, showing a chaos–order transition as \(\omega\) increases.
- For \(^{166}\text{Er}\), spectral statistics show predominantly chaotic behaviour for energies above the saddle energy (about \(4\) MeV) and mixed behaviour with predominance of regular trajectories at higher energies (about \(15\) MeV).
- The critical angular momentum \(I_c\) and the energy limits \(E^-_{\text{chaos}}\) and \(E^+_{\text{chaos}}\) for \(\omega = 0\) are given in Table 3 for several nuclei (numerical values not reproduced in the truncated text).
- The discriminant for the curvature criterion is \(\Delta = \frac{72}{35} C_2 C_4 (\chi - \frac{14}{3} - 14\frac{B\omega^2}{C_2})\), where \(\chi = C_3^2/(C_2 C_4)\).

## Context
This work extends the study of quantum chaos in nuclear physics by applying the curvature criterion (previously used by other authors, e.g., the work cited as [12]) to the Bohr–Mottelson roto-vibrational model. It goes beyond prior studies by calculating explicit energy ranges of chaotic regions and demonstrating a chaos–order transition as a function of rotational frequency \(\omega\), not just energy. The results enable further investigation of how collective rotation suppresses chaos in deformed nuclei, and the spectral statistics confirm the connection between classical chaos and GOE predictions established by Bohigas, Giannoni, and Schmit (1984) and Berry (1985).
