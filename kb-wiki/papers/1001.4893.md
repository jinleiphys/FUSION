# Mean field study of structural changes in Pt isotopes with the Gogny interaction

> The evolution of the nuclear shapes along the triaxial landscape is studied in the Pt isotopic chain using the selfconsistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximation based on the Gogny interaction. In addition to the parametrization D1S, the new incarnations D1N and D1M of this force are also included in our analysis to asses to which extent the predictions are independent of details of the effective interaction. The considered range of neutron numbers 88<N<26 includes prolate, triaxial, oblate and spherical ground state shapes and serves for a detailed comparison of the predictions obtained with the new sets D1N and D1M against the ones provided by the standard parametrization Gogny-D1S in a region of the nuclear landscape for which experimental and theoretical fingerprints of shape transitions have been found. Structural evolution along the Pt chain is discussed in terms of the deformation dependence of single particle energies.

# Mean field study of structural changes in Pt isotopes with the Gogny interaction

## Key claim

The evolution of nuclear shapes along the Pt isotopic chain from prolate through triaxial to oblate and spherical ground states is robustly predicted by the Gogny interaction, with the new parametrizations D1N and D1M yielding results extremely similar to the standard D1S force; however, the spherical barrier heights and pairing energies show some sensitivity to the specific parametrization, with D1M giving the largest pairing energies and D1S the smallest.

## Method

The authors performed selfconsistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny interaction in three parametrizations (D1S, D1N, D1M) for the Pt isotopes with neutron numbers N=88-126. They first computed potential energy curves (PECs) under axial symmetry constraints on the quadrupole moment Q20, then constructed full potential energy surfaces (PESs) in the (Q,γ) plane by constraining on both β and γ deformations. Single-particle energies (SPEs) were obtained from the eigenvalues of the constrained Routhian, and Thouless-Valatin moments of inertia were computed using the selfconsistent cranking approximation.

## Key numbers

- The axial quadrupole moment Q20 of the absolute minima increases until A≈180 (N≈102) where it reaches Q20 ≈ 10 b
- The energy difference |ΔE_p-o| between prolate and oblate minima shows a first maximum at A=178 with values 2.19 MeV (D1S), 1.97 MeV (D1N), and 2.11 MeV (D1M)
- A second maximum in |ΔE_p-o| occurs at A=198 with values 1.16 MeV (D1S), 0.95 MeV (D1N), and 0.99 MeV (D1M)
- The nucleus 188Pt has almost degenerate prolate and oblate minima with |ΔE_p-o| values of 45 keV (D1S), 111 keV (D1N), and 71 keV (D1M)
- Ground state triaxial coordinates evolve from (Q ≈ 5 b, γ ≈ 50°-58°) in 196Pt to (Q ≈ 9 b, γ ≈ 10°) in 184Pt
- For 188Pt, the triaxial-to-saddle energy difference is |ΔE|_triaxial-saddle ≤ 0.9 MeV
- Neutron pairing collapses for 204Pt (N=126 shell closure)
- The isotopes 202-198Pt exhibit oblate ground states with Q ≈ 3-4.5 b
- The isotopes 170-166Pt display prolate ground states with Q ≈ 3-4.5 b

## Context

This work extends previous mean field studies of shape evolution in the Z=82 region, including HFB calculations with Gogny-D1S for Pb isotopes (Rayner, 2003) and systematic studies of Yb, Hf, W, Os, and Pt isotopes using both Skyrme and Gogny interactions (Rodriguez-Guzman, 2010). The results agree well with axial calculations using the Skyrme parametrization SLy4 (Robledo, 2008) and with macroscopic-microscopic calculations (Moller, 1995; Moller, 2008). The study provides the first systematic mean field comparison of D1N and D1M parametrizations for Pt nuclei, demonstrating that these newer forces, despite being fitted primarily to nuclear masses, reproduce the fine details of shape evolution predicted by the well-tested D1S force. The work enables future dynamical treatments incorporating symmetry restoration and shape fluctuations via the Generator Coordinate Method or Bohr Hamiltonian approach.
