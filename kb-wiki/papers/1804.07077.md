# $J/ψ\to γη'π^+π^-$ and the structure observed around the $\bar pp$ threshold

> We analyze the origin of the structure observed in the reaction $J/ψ\to γη'π^+π^-$ for $η'π^+π^-$ invariant masses close to the antiproton-proton ($\bar pp$) threshold, commonly associated with the $X(1835)$ resonance. Specifically, we explore the effect of a possible contribution from the two-step process $J/ψ\to γ\bar NN \to γη'π^+π^-$. The calculation is performed in distorted-wave Born approximation which allows an appropriate inclusion of the $\bar NN$ interaction in the transition amplitude. The $\bar NN$ amplitude itself is generated from a corresponding potential recently derived within chiral effective field theory. We are able to reproduce the measured spectra for the reactions $J/ψ\to γ\bar pp$ and $J/ψ\to γη'π^+π^-$ for invariant masses around the $\bar pp$ threshold. The structure seen in the $η'π^+π^-$ spectrum emerges as a threshold effect due to the opening of the $\bar pp$ channel.

# J/ψ → γη'π⁺π⁻ and the structure observed around the pp̅ threshold

## Key claim

The structure observed in the η'π⁺π⁻ invariant mass spectrum near the pp̅ threshold, commonly attributed to the X(1835) resonance, can be explained as a threshold effect from the opening of the pp̅ channel via the two-step process J/ψ → γpp̅ → γη'π⁺π⁻, without requiring a genuine resonance. The authors reproduce the measured spectra for both J/ψ → γpp̅ and J/ψ → γη'π⁺π⁻ using chiral effective field theory NN̅ potentials, but note that the description depends on the specific NN̅ interaction employed and that bound states in the I=1 channel are not necessarily required for the I=0 channel relevant to the η'π⁺π⁻ final state.

## Method

The calculation is performed in distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA), solving coupled equations for the NN̅ scattering amplitude, the NN̅ → η'π⁺π⁻ transition amplitude, and the J/ψ → γpp̅ and J/ψ → γη'π⁺π⁻ amplitudes. The NN̅ interaction in the ¹S₀ partial wave is taken from chiral effective field theory potentials up to N³LO, with the I=1 amplitude refitted to reproduce the J/ψ → γpp̅ invariant mass spectrum. The elementary annihilation potential for NN̅ → η'π⁺π⁻ and the primary J/ψ → γpp̅ amplitude are parameterized by contact terms, with constants fixed to experimental branching ratios and event rates.

## Key numbers

- The branching ratio BR(pp̅ → η'π⁺π⁻) = 0.626% (from Amsler 2004), corresponding to a cross section of 2.23 mb at p_lab = 106 MeV/c
- The pp̅ elastic cross section in the ¹S₀ partial wave is approximately 20 mb from the NN̅ potential and from partial-wave analysis
- For the N³LO NN̅ interaction, the bound state in the I=1 ¹S₀ partial wave is located at E_B = (-50.8 − i 40.9) MeV relative to the NN̅ threshold
- For the N²LO NN̅ interaction, the bound state in the I=1 ¹S₀ partial wave is at E_B = (-2.1 − i 94.0) MeV
- The Paris NN̅ potential gives a bound state in I=0 ¹S₀ at E_B = (-4.8 − i 26) MeV (from Dedonder 2009, 2018)
- The NN̅ interaction from Milstein (2017) gives a pole at E_B = (22 − i 33) MeV (above threshold)
- Data are fitted in the range 1800 MeV ≤ E ≤ 1950 MeV for the η'π⁺π⁻ invariant mass

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of threshold effects in e⁺e⁻ annihilation to multipion states (Haidenbauer 2015), where structures near the NN̅ threshold in channels such as e⁺e⁻ → 3(π⁺π⁻) and e⁺e⁻ → 2(π⁺π⁻π⁰) were explained as threshold effects rather than resonances. It also builds on earlier analyses of J/ψ → γpp̅ using the Migdal-Watson approximation (Sibirtsev 2004, Haidenbauer 2006) and DWBA with chiral NN̅ potentials (Kang 2015). The present work contradicts interpretations requiring the X(1835) as a genuine resonance or baryonium state, and contrasts with studies using the Paris NN̅ potential (Dedonder 2009, 2018) or the interaction of Milstein (2017) that find bound states in the I=0 channel. The results enable future investigations of whether similar threshold effects appear in other J/ψ radiative decays such as J/ψ → γωρ⁰.
