# Microscopic calculations of spin polarized neutron matter at finite temperature

> The properties of spin polarized neutron matter are studied both at zero and finite temperature within the framework of the Brueckner--Hartree--Fock formalism, using the Argonne v18 nucleon-nucleon interaction. The free energy, energy and entropy per particle are calculated for several values of the spin polarization, densities and temperatures together with the magnetic susceptibility of the system. The results show no indication of a ferromagnetic transition at any density and temperature.

## Key claim

Using the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock formalism with the Argonne v18 interaction, the authors find no indication of a ferromagnetic transition in neutron matter at any density or temperature studied, contrary to predictions from Skyrme-like interactions which had suggested such a transition at densities around (1-4) times nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The authors extended the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone (BBG) expansion to finite temperature and arbitrary spin polarization, solving the Bethe-Goldstone equation for the neutron-neutron G-matrix with the Argonne v18 potential. Single-particle potentials and effective masses were computed self-consistently for neutrons with spin up and down, using the continuous prescription for the single-particle potential and replacing zero-temperature occupation numbers with Fermi-Dirac distributions at finite temperature. The free energy, energy, and entropy per particle were then calculated for various densities, temperatures, and spin polarizations, from which the magnetic susceptibility was derived.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Effective mass ratio at rho = 0.16 fm^{-3} and T = 40 MeV: m*_up(Delta=1)/m*_{up,down}(Delta=0) = 1.09, which is smaller than the limit of 2^{2/3} required for entropy of polarized phase to be lower than unpolarized phase
- Free energy per particle differences between fully polarized and non-polarized phases are positive for all densities and temperatures studied, indicating the non-polarized phase is energetically preferred
- The ratio chi_F/chi (magnetic susceptibility of free Fermi gas over interacting neutron matter) increases with density at all temperatures, with no signal of approaching zero

## Context

This work extends previous zero-temperature Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations by Vidana, Polls, and Ramos (2002) and Vidana and Bombaci (2002) to finite temperature, and directly contradicts the finite-temperature Skyrme interaction results of Rios, Polls, and Vidana (2005), which had predicted a ferromagnetic transition with the critical density decreasing as temperature increases. The results are consistent with Monte Carlo calculations by Fantoni, Sarsa, and Schmidt (2001) that excluded ferromagnetic transitions up to five times nuclear saturation density. The findings have implications for protoneutron star evolution, particularly for neutrino cross sections and mean free paths, which depend on spin correlations in the medium.
