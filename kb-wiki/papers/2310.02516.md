# Can negative bare couplings make sense? The $\vecϕ^4$ theory at large $N$

> Scalar $λϕ^4$ theory in 3+1D, for a positive coupling constant $λ>0$, is known to have no interacting continuum limit, which is referred to as quantum triviality. However, it has been recently argued that the theory in 3+1D with an $N$-component scalar $\vecϕ$ and a $(\vecϕ\cdot\vecϕ)^{\,2}=\vecϕ^{\,4}$ interaction term does have an interacting continuum limit at large $N$. It has been suggested that this continuum limit has a negative (bare) coupling constant and exhibits asymptotic freedom, similar to the $\mathcal{P}\mathcal{T}$-symmetric $-gϕ^4$ field theory. In this paper I study the $\vecϕ^{\,4}$ theory in 3+1D at large $N$ with a negative coupling constant $-g<0$, and with the scalar field taking values in a $\mathcal{P}\mathcal{T}$-symmetric complex domain. The theory is non-trivial, has asymptotic freedom, and has a Landau pole in the IR, and I demonstrate that the thermal partition function matches that of the positive-coupling $λ>0$ theory when the Landau poles of the two theories (in the $λ>0$ case a pole in the UV) are identified with one another. The spirit of renormalization is that observables do not depend on the renormalization scale. Here we see even if the coupling is taken negative above the scale of the Landau pole, thermodynamic observables are unaffected. Thus the $\vecϕ^{\,4}$ theory at large $N$ appears to have a negative bare coupling constant; the coupling only becomes positive in the IR, which in the context of other $\mathcal{P}\mathcal{T}$-symmetric and large-$N$ quantum field theories I argue is perfectly acceptable.

## Key claim

The $\vec{\phi}^4$ theory at large $N$ in 3+1D can have a negative bare coupling constant and still produce a physically acceptable continuum limit, provided the path integral is defined over a $\mathcal{PT}$-symmetric complex domain; the negative-coupling theory's thermal partition function matches that of the positive-coupling theory when the Landau poles of both theories are identified, meaning the coupling only becomes positive in the infrared.

## Method

The author computes the thermal partition function for the $-g\vec{\phi}^4/N$ theory at large $N$ using a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation and dimensional regularization in (3−2ϵ)+1D, with the scalar field defined on a $\mathcal{PT}$-symmetric complex domain that breaks O(N) symmetry to O(N−1). The pressure per component is obtained from the saddle-point (gap) equation, and the renormalized running coupling is fixed by requiring scale independence of the pressure. A Lefschetz thimble analysis determines which saddle points contribute to the partition function, and the result is compared to the positive-coupling theory of Romatschke (2022).

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature separating real from complex-conjugate saddle solutions is $T_c \approx 0.616 \, \Lambda_{\text{LP}}$, where $\Lambda_{\text{LP}}$ is the Landau pole scale.
- At zero temperature, the dominant saddle gives a mass gap $m^2 = e \, \Lambda_{\text{LP}}^2$ (with $e$ the base of natural logarithms).
- The renormalized running coupling for the negative-coupling theory is $g_R(\bar{\mu}) = 4\pi^2 / \ln(\bar{\mu}^2 / \Lambda_{\text{LP}}^2)$, identical in form to the positive-coupling running coupling $\lambda_R(\bar{\mu})$ up to a sign.
- The pressure per component at high temperatures asymptotes to the Stefan-Boltzmann limit for a single free boson.

## Context

This work extends the large-N analysis of scalar $\phi^4$ theory by Romatschke (2022, 2023), who found an interacting continuum limit for the positive-coupling theory despite the known quantum triviality problem for $N=1,2$ (Frohlich, 1982; Aizenman, 2019). The author addresses the apparent contradiction that the positive-coupling theory has a Landau pole preventing a continuum limit, by showing that a negative bare coupling defined on a $\mathcal{PT}$-symmetric domain yields identical thermodynamics. This enables a consistent interpretation of the $\vec{\phi}^4$ theory at large $N$ as asymptotically free, and it resolves the issue of triviality by allowing the bare coupling to be negative above the Landau pole scale.
