# Color-superconductivity in the strong-coupling regime of Landau gauge QCD

> The chirally unbroken and the superconducting 2SC and CFL phases are investigated in the chiral limit within a Dyson-Schwinger approach for the quark propagator in QCD. The hierarchy of Green's functions is truncated such that at vanishing density known results for the vacuum and at asymptotically high densities the corresponding weak-coupling expressions are recovered. The anomalous dimensions of the gap functions are analytically calculated. Based on the quark propagator the phase structure is studied, and results for the gap functions, occupation numbers, coherence lengths and pressure differences are given and compared with the corresponding expressions in the weak-coupling regime. At moderate chemical potentials the quasiparticle pairing gaps are several times larger than the extrapolated weak-coupling results.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that at moderate chemical potentials (relevant for neutron star interiors), the quasiparticle pairing gaps in color-superconducting phases are several times larger than what would be expected from extrapolating weak-coupling results, using a Dyson-Schwinger approach that recovers both vacuum results at zero density and weak-coupling expressions at asymptotically high densities.

## Method

The authors solve a truncated Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator in Landau gauge QCD at finite chemical potential, incorporating medium effects through a polarization tensor in the random-phase approximation similar to the Hard-Dense-Loop approximation. The quark-gluon vertex is approximated by its vector-type coupling only, and two different running couplings are employed: one from coupled gluon, ghost and quark DSEs (denoted α_I) and one from quenched lattice data with an abelian vertex approximation (denoted α_II). The gap equations are solved self-consistently for the 2SC and CFL phases in the chiral limit with N_c=3 and N_f=3.

## Key numbers

- The running coupling α_I has α_I(0)=2.972 with Λ_I=0.714 GeV, parameterized by a1=1.106, a2=2.324, b1=0.004, b2=3.169
- The Debye screening mass squared is given by m²(p²)=N_f α_s(p²) μ²/π
- The anomalous dimension of the gap function in the attractive color-antitriplet channel is γ_φ,3̄ = 6/(33-2N_f)
- The anomalous dimension of the gap function in the repulsive color-sextet channel is γ_φ,6 = -3/(33-2N_f)
- The asymptotic behavior of the gap function φ⁺_{C,i}(p) scales as 1/p² times (ln(p²/Λ²))^(γ_φ,i - 1)

## Context

This work extends Dyson-Schwinger methods previously applied to vacuum QCD (Fischer 2003) to finite density, providing a QCD-based alternative to Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model studies (Alford and Rapp) that are common for color-superconductivity at moderate densities. The approach enables quantitative estimates of when weak-coupling results (Schäfer and Hong) become reliable as a function of chemical potential, and provides a framework for studying the transition from strong to weak coupling in dense quark matter.
