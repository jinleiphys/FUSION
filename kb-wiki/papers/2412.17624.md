# Axion effects on quark matter and quark-matter cores in massive hybrid stars

> Using a three-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model to describe the charge-parity violating effects through axion field, we investigate the axion effects on quark matter and quark-matter cores in massive hybrid stars. The properties of quark matter vary with the scaled axion field $a/f_a$ in a periodic manner, with a period of $2π$. Within the range from 0 to $π$, axion field decrease the baryon chemical potential of the first-order phase transition, leading to an increase in normalized pressure and stiffening of the quark matter equation of state. The effect of axions on hybrid star matter that includes the hadron-quark phase transition is contrary to expectations. The axion field shifts the onset of the hadron-quark mixed phase to lower densities but slightly softens the equation of state of the mixed phase matter, which also results in a slight decrease in the maximum mass and corresponding radius of the hybrid stars. However, we also find that the lowering of the onset of the mixed phase significantly increases the radius and mass of the quark-matter core in the hybrid star. Therefore, our results indicate with axion effects, a sizable quark-matter core can appear in $2M_{\odot}$ massive neutron stars.

## Key claim

The axion field, while stiffening pure quark matter, actually slightly softens the equation of state of the mixed hadron-quark phase in hybrid stars, leading to a slight decrease in maximum mass and radius; however, it significantly increases the size of the quark-matter core, enabling a sizable core in stars near two solar masses.

## Method

The authors use a three-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a Kobayashi-Maskawa-t' Hooft determinant term that incorporates the axion field to describe CP-violating effects in quark matter. The hadronic phase is described by an improved isospin- and momentum-dependent interaction (ImMDI) model, and the hadron-quark mixed phase is treated via Gibbs construction with beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are then solved to obtain the mass-radius relation of hybrid stars.

## Key numbers

* For the case \(a/f_a = \pi\), the quark-matter core in a hybrid star with nearly \(2M_{\odot}\) reaches a mass of up to \(0.7M_{\odot}\) and a core radius \(R_{QC}=6.2\) km, exceeding half of the star's total radius.
* The maximum mass of hybrid stars slightly decreases with increasing \(a/f_a\), with the baseline ImMDI model (without axions) allowing a \(2.08M_{\odot}\) neutron star at saturation density \(\rho_0=0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\).
* The NJL model parameters used are: \(m_u = m_d = 3.6\) MeV, \(m_s = 87\) MeV, \(G_S\Lambda^2 = 3.6\), \(K\Lambda^5 = 8.9\), and cutoff \(\Lambda = 750\) MeV.
* The vector coupling is fixed at \(G_V = 0.5 G_S\) to allow the maximum hybrid star mass to approach \(2M_{\odot}\).

## Context

This work extends studies of axion effects on quark matter within the NJL framework (Boo09, Cha12, Abh21) and applies them to hybrid star structure, complementing prior work on quark-matter cores in massive neutron stars (Ann20) and signatures of hadron-quark phase transitions in binary neutron star mergers (Bau19, Hua22). The results enable further investigation of axion effects on the QCD phase diagram and critical point, and complement research on beyond-Standard-Model particles in neutron star structure and binary mergers.
