# Effective Field Theory Analysis of Three-Boson Systems at Next-To-Next-To-Leading Order

> We use an effective field theory for short-range forces (SREFT) to analyze systems of three identical bosons interacting via a two-body potential that generates a scattering length, $a$, which is large compared to the range of the interaction, $\ell$. The amplitude for the scattering of one boson off a bound state of the other two is computed to next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO) in the $\ell/a$ expansion. At this order, two pieces of three-body data are required as input in order to renormalize the amplitude (for fixed $a$). We apply our results to a model system of three Helium-4 atoms, which are assumed to interact via the TTY potential. We generate N$^2$LO predictions for atom-dimer scattering below the dimer breakup threshold using the bound-state energy of the shallow Helium-4 trimer and the atom-dimer scattering length as our two pieces of three-body input. Based on the convergence pattern of the SREFT expansion, as well as differences in the predictions of two renormalization schemes, we conclude that our N$^2$LO phase- shift predictions will receive higher-order corrections of $< 0.2$%. In contrast, the prediction of SREFT for the binding energy of the "deep" trimer of Helium-4 atoms displays poor convergence.

## Key claim

The authors compute the three-boson atom-dimer scattering amplitude to next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO) in the short-range effective field theory (SREFT) expansion in $\ell/a$, finding that an additional energy-dependent three-body counterterm is required for consistent renormalization at this order, contrary to the claim of Platter and Phillips (2006) but supporting the conclusion of Bedaque et al. (2002). The convergence of the SREFT expansion is excellent for shallow-trimer observables (higher-order corrections below 0.2%) but poor for the deep-trimer binding energy.

## Method

The authors perform a perturbative expansion of the three-body scattering t-matrix in powers of the effective range $r_0$ up to $r_0^2$, using the leading-order (LO) modified Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian integral equation as the starting point. They compute N$^2$LO contributions from five diagram classes (A through E) involving insertions of the N$^2$LO dimer propagator, N$^2$LO three-body contact interactions, and double insertions of NLO terms, then relate the resulting t-matrix to a real K-matrix to obtain phase shifts and bound-state energies. The formalism is applied to a model system of three Helium-4 atoms interacting via the TTY potential, using the shallow trimer binding energy and atom-dimer scattering length as the two required three-body inputs.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of two-body scattering length to interaction range for Helium-4 atoms is $a/\ell \sim 10$ (from experimental data by Grisenti et al., 2000).
- The geometric ratio between consecutive trimer binding energies in the unitary limit is $515$ (Efimov, 1970).
- The N$^2$LO phase-shift predictions are estimated to receive higher-order corrections of $< 0.2\%$, based on convergence pattern and differences between two renormalization schemes.
- The parameter $s_0$ appearing in the analytic form of the LO three-body force is $s_0 = 1.00624$.
- The relation between the atom-dimer scattering length $a_{ad}$ and the LO renormalization parameter $\bar{\Lambda}$ is given by $a_{ad} = [1.46 + 2.15 \cot(s_0 \ln(a\bar{\Lambda}) + 0.09)] a$.

## Context

This work extends the NLO perturbative calculation of Ji et al. (2011) to N$^2$LO, resolving a controversy between Bedaque et al. (2002), who found an energy-dependent three-body counterterm needed at N$^2$LO, and Platter and Phillips (2006), who argued no such term was required in the limit $\Lambda \rightarrow \infty$. The authors show that the Platter and Phillips result applies only when $\ell \ll r_0$, not to the generic case $r_0 \sim \ell$, and that an energy-dependent three-body counterterm is indeed necessary for consistent renormalization. The results enable N$^2$LO predictions for three-nucleon systems (such as neutron-deuteron scattering) where $r_0/a \sim 1/3$ and NLO accuracy of $\sim 10\%$ is insufficient for comparison with experiments.
