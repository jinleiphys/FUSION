# Thermodynamics of Delta resonances

> The thermodynamic potential of a system of pions and nucleons is computed including the piN interactions in the P33 channel. A consistent treatment of the width of the resonance in this channel, the Delta(1232) resonance, is explored in detail. In the low-density limit we recover the leading term of the virial expansion for the thermodynamic potential. An instructive diagrammatic interpretation of the contributions to the total baryon number is presented. Furthermore, we examine within a fireball model the consequences for the pion spectra in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies, including the effect of collective flow. A consistent treatment of the Delta width leads to a substantial enhancement of the pion yield at low momenta.

## Key claim

A consistent treatment of the Delta(1232) resonance width in the thermodynamic potential of a pion-nucleon system requires including not only the spectral function of the Delta but also a correction term for correlated pion-nucleon pairs; the frequently used prescription of employing only the free Delta spectral function is incorrect and neglects an important contribution to the low-momentum pion spectrum.

## Method

The authors compute the thermodynamic potential of a pion-nucleon-Delta gas using a Green-function approach based on the Baym-Kadanoff formalism, starting from a two-loop Phi-derivable approximation. They relax full self-consistency by employing a low-density expansion where pion and nucleon Green functions are taken at the pole level with physical masses, while the Delta vacuum self-energy is treated explicitly. The resulting weight function for the Delta contribution is expressed in terms of the energy derivative of the piN scattering phase shift in the P33 channel, and the formalism is applied to a fireball model for heavy-ion collisions at 1 GeV per nucleon.

## Key numbers

- The Delta coupling constant is fitted as f_piNDelta = 3.28, with a cutoff Lambda = 290 MeV and bare Delta mass m_Delta^0 = 1316 MeV.
- At freeze-out in Au+Au at E_lab/A = 1 GeV, the temperature is T = 57 MeV, baryon chemical potential mu_B = 830 MeV, charge chemical potential mu_Q = -18 MeV, and mean flow velocity bar{v} = 0.36.
- The freeze-out baryon density is n_B = 0.4 rho_0, where rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} is normal nuclear matter density.
- Using the spectral function A, the fraction of Delta resonances at freeze-out is n_Delta/n_B = 0.035, while the combined fraction of Deltas and piN pairs is tilde{n}_Delta/n_B = 0.054.
- For a stable Delta (Gamma_Delta = 0) at the same T and mu_B, the fraction is n_Delta/n_B = 0.037.

## Context

This work extends the S-matrix formulation of statistical mechanics by Dashen, Ma, and Bernstein (1969) and the virial expansion of Beth and Uhlenbeck (1937) to consistently treat broad resonances like the Delta(1232) in a Green-function framework. It corrects earlier calculations by Weldon (1990), Gorringe et al. (1995), and Danielewicz (1995) that used an ad hoc prescription with the spectral function A instead of the full weight function B. The results enable a proper description of low-momentum pion spectra in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies and show that the invariant mass distribution of piN pairs from a fireball should not be directly identified with a thermally weighted Delta resonance.
