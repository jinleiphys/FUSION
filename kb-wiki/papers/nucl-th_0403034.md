# Strangeness Production at RHIC in the Perturbative Regim

> We investigate strange quark production in Au-Au collisions at RHIC in the framework of the Parton Cascade Model(PCM). The yields of (anti-) strange quarks for three production scenarios -- primary-primary scattering, full scattering, and full production -- are compared to a proton-proton baseline. Enhancement of strange quark yields in central Au-Au collisions compared to scaled p-p collisions increases with the number of secondary interactions. The centrality dependence of strangeness production for the three production scenarios is studied as well. For all production mechanisms, the strangeness yield increases with $(N_{\rm part})^{4/3}$. The perturbative QCD regime described by the PCM is able to account for up to 50% of the observed strangeness at RHIC.

# Strangeness Production at RHIC in the Perturbative Regime

## Key claim

The Parton Cascade Model (PCM), which describes only the perturbative QCD-driven pre-equilibrium phase of heavy-ion collisions, can account for up to 50% (or 55% when compared to STAR data at mid-rapidity) of the observed strangeness yield at RHIC, with the remaining yield attributed to non-perturbative processes in the later thermalized QGP phase that the PCM cannot capture.

## Method

The authors used the Parton Cascade Model (VNI/BMS implementation) based on the relativistic Boltzmann equation with pQCD matrix elements for parton-parton scattering, using GRV-HO parton distribution functions with an initialization scale of Q0^2 = 0.5 GeV^2 and a pT^min cutoff of 0.5 GeV. They compared three production scenarios for central Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: primary-primary scattering only (single elastic collisions), full scattering (multiple elastic rescatterings), and full production (elastic plus inelastic collisions including gg -> s sbar, q qbar -> s sbar, and time-like branchings like g -> s sbar), with all results compared to a p-p baseline scaled by N_part = 197.

## Key numbers

- The PCM accounts for 55% of STAR's measured strangeness yield at mid-rapidity for central Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.
- Enhancement factors for integrated strangeness yield in Au-Au relative to scaled p-p are 4.5 (primary-primary scattering), 6.0 (full scattering), and 10.5 (full production).
- Approximately 13% of initial strangeness is released by primary-primary interactions, with another 10% released through secondary rescatterings; about 40% of total strangeness yield is released from the initial state and nearly 60% is newly produced.
- The Wroblewski factor lambda_s for full production is 0.52 for Au-Au and 0.36 for p-p; for primary-primary scattering it is 0.26 (Au-Au) and 0.25 (p-p); for full scattering it is 0.31 (Au-Au) and 0.28 (p-p).
- For all three production scenarios, the total integrated strangeness multiplicity increases with (N_part)^(4/3).

## Context

This work extends earlier pQCD rate-equation calculations by Biro et al. (1993) and a very early PCM implementation by Geiger (1993), confirming the trend that pQCD processes alone undersaturate strangeness production at RHIC. The study addresses a gap in understanding the dynamics of initial strangeness production in the deconfined phase at RHIC, which had not received much attention despite strong evidence for QGP from collective flow, high-pT suppression, and parton recombination. The results enable future investigations of strangeness balance functions, strangeness equilibration in infinite partonic matter, and estimation of soft nonperturbative contributions to strangeness production, potentially through introducing a screening mass instead of a hard momentum cut-off.
