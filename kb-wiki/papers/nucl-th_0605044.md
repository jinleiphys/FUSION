# Universal scaling of the elliptic flow at RHIC

> Recent PHOBOS measurements of the excitation function for the pseudo-rapidity dependence of elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, have posed a significant theoretical challenge. We show here that these data are described by the Buda-Lund model. A universal scaling curve, predicted by the Buda-Lund hydro model, describes not only PHOBOS data, but also recent, detailed PHENIX and STAR elliptic flow data. This is a consequence of perfect fluid hydrodynamics as implemented in the Buda-Lund hydro model.

# Universal scaling of the elliptic flow at RHIC

## Key claim
The Buda-Lund hydro model, based on perfect fluid hydrodynamics, describes the pseudorapidity dependence of elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions at RHIC across multiple beam energies, and predicts a universal scaling curve that collapses PHOBOS, PHENIX, and STAR data onto a single function of the form $v_2 = I_1(w)/I_0(w)$. The claim is conditional on the data satisfying certain conditions detailed in Csanad (2003), and the fits exclude points with large rapidity ($\eta>4$ for 19.6 GeV, $\eta>4.5$ for 62.4 GeV) and large transverse momentum ($p_t>1.3$ GeV).

## Method
The authors used the Buda-Lund hydro model, an ellipsoidally symmetric generalization of exact analytic hydrodynamical solutions, to compute elliptic flow $v_2$ as a function of pseudorapidity $\eta$ and transverse momentum $p_t$. For $v_2(\eta)$ data from PHOBOS, they performed a saddle-point integration in transverse momentum and changed variables to pseudorapidity; for $v_2(p_t)$ data from PHENIX and STAR, they compared directly to the model result. Fits to PHOBOS data used two free parameters (momentum-space eccentricity $\epsilon$ and longitudinal elongation $\Delta\eta$), while fits to PHENIX and STAR data used $\epsilon$ and average transverse flow $u_t$, with other model parameters fixed ($T_0=200$ MeV, $a=1$, and for $v_2(\eta)$ fits $u_t=1.5$).

## Key numbers
- For PHOBOS Au+Au $v_2(\eta)$ fits: $\epsilon = 0.30 \pm 0.03$ (19.6 GeV), $0.37 \pm 0.01$ (62.4 GeV), $0.40 \pm 0.01$ (130 GeV), $0.42 \pm 0.01$ (200 GeV); $\Delta\eta = 2.0 \pm 0.3$ (19.6 GeV), $2.28 \pm 0.05$ (62.4 GeV), $2.60 \pm 0.04$ (130 GeV), $2.70 \pm 0.04$ (200 GeV).
- For PHENIX 200 GeV Au+Au $v_2(p_t)$ fits: $\epsilon = 0.31 \pm 0.003$ (pions), $0.32 \pm 0.04$ (kaons), $0.3 \pm 0.1$ (protons); $u_t = 1.2 \pm 0.1$ (pions), $0.7 \pm 0.12$ (kaons), $0.6 \pm 0.3$ (protons).
- For STAR 200 GeV Au+Au $v_2(p_t)$ fits (pions, kaons, protons combined): $\epsilon = 0.38 \pm 0.01$, $u_t = 1.0 \pm 0.1$.
- The universal scaling variable is $w = (E_K / 2 T_*) \varepsilon$, where $E_K = p_t^2 / (2 \overline{m}_t)$, $T_*$ is the azimuthally averaged effective slope parameter, and $\varepsilon$ is the eccentricity of the single-particle spectra.

## Context
This work extends the Buda-Lund hydro model, previously successful in describing single-particle spectra and HBT radii at RHIC (Csorgo 1995, Csanad 2003), to the elliptic flow excitation function. It directly addresses the failure of earlier hydrodynamical models to describe the PHOBOS $v_2(\eta)$ data, as noted by Hirano (2005) who attributed discrepancies to possible late hadronic viscosity. The universal scaling prediction, made analytically in Csanad (2003), is here confirmed against PHOBOS, PHENIX, and STAR data, providing quantitative evidence for perfect fluid behavior extending far from mid-rapidity. This enables future simultaneous fits of $v_2(\eta)$ and pseudorapidity distributions with the same $\Delta\eta$ parameter, and motivates searches for similar scaling in other flow harmonics.
