# Alternative methods to measure global polarization of $Λ$ hyperons

> We propose alternative methods to measure the global polarization of $Λ$ hyperons. These methods involve event averages of proton's and $Λ$'s momenta in the lab frame. We carry out simulations using these methods and show that all of them work equivalently well in obtaining the global polarization of $Λ$ hyperons.

## Key claim

The paper proposes alternative methods to measure the global polarization of Λ hyperons that use event averages of proton and Λ momenta in the lab frame, rather than in the Λ rest frame; the methods are shown via simulation to work equivalently well to the standard STAR method, but the method using Eq. (9) is only valid in the central rapidity range.

## Method

The authors use the UrQMD model to generate an ensemble of Λ four-momenta for Au+Au collisions at various energies with impact parameter 6 fm. Each Λ is then decayed into a proton and pion according to the weak decay angular distribution with a known input polarization (𝒫Λ = 1/3), and the proton momenta are boosted back to the lab frame. From the ensemble of lab-frame proton and Λ momenta, the global polarization is extracted using several alternative formulas derived from Lorentz transformations, and the results are compared to the standard STAR method.

## Key numbers

- Input polarization set to 𝒫Λ = 1/3 (complete polarization) for all simulations.
- At 200 GeV, method 1 (STAR method) gives 𝒫Λ = 0.33581, method 3 (Eq. 8) gives 0.3324, method 4 (Eq. 11) gives 0.33014, and method 5 (Eq. 9, central rapidity) gives 0.308495, from 1,304,795 Λs in full rapidity.
- At 7.7 GeV, method 1 gives 0.341257, method 3 gives 0.364069, method 4 gives 0.34862, and method 5 gives 0.302301, from 434,697 Λs.
- Number of events collected: 4×10⁴ at 200 GeV and 2.5×10⁴ at other energies.

## Context

This work extends the method used by the STAR collaboration (STAR, 2017; Abelev, 2007) to measure global Λ polarization via the event average of sin(φ_p* − ψ_RP) in the Λ rest frame, which requires a reaction plane resolution correction. The proposed alternatives avoid the need for the Λ rest frame and the reaction plane resolution factor by using lab-frame momenta only. The methods enable simpler experimental measurements of global polarization and can be applied to existing and future heavy-ion collision data to study the vorticity of the quark-gluon plasma, which has been estimated as ω = (9±1)×10²¹ s⁻¹ from previous data.
