# Calculation of the pion electromagnetic form factor from lattice QCD

> We present a lattice calculation of the vector form factor of the pion for two flavours of non-perturbatively O(a) improved Wilson fermions. For the measurements we utilise the CLS ensembles which include various lattice spacings and pion masses down to about 250 MeV. To obtain a fine momentum resolution near zero momentum transfer (q^2) partially twisted boundary conditions are employed using several twist angles. Due to the fine resolution around q^2=0 we are able to determine the slope of the form factor and, in turn, extract the charge radius of the pion without any model dependence. The results for the form factor and the charge radius are then compared to chiral perturbation theory and phenomenological models which are used to extrapolate the results to the physical point.

## Key claim

The authors claim to extract the pion charge radius from lattice QCD without any model dependence by using partially twisted boundary conditions to achieve a fine momentum resolution near zero momentum transfer, but they note that their results may show residual cut-off dependence and that the chiral extrapolation to the physical point requires next-to-next-to-leading order chiral perturbation theory, as next-to-leading order is insufficient to describe their data.

## Method

The calculation uses two flavours of non-perturbatively O(a) improved Wilson fermions on CLS ensembles with three lattice spacings (0.08 fm, 0.07 fm, 0.05 fm) and pion masses down to about 250 MeV. Partially twisted boundary conditions with five twist angles generate up to 30 values of momentum transfer below the lowest accessible Fourier momentum, and the form factor is extracted from a combination of three ratios of two- and three-point functions using stochastic Z(2) x Z(2) wall sources. The charge radius is obtained from a linear fit to the form factor in the region (r0 q)^2 <= -0.15, with results expressed in units of the Sommer scale r0 = 0.471 fm.

## Key numbers

* Pion masses down to about 250 MeV are used across ensembles with lattice spacings of 0.08 fm, 0.07 fm, and 0.05 fm.
* The form factor data reach down to (r0 q)^2 less than about -10^-4 with small statistical uncertainties.
* The linear fit region for extracting the charge radius is defined as (r0 q)^2 <= -0.15.
* The Sommer scale is taken as r0 = 0.471 fm, from a 2011 lattice result.
* Statistical errors are estimated with the bootstrap method using 1000 samples.

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD calculations of the pion electromagnetic form factor by using partially twisted boundary conditions, following the approach of Boyle (2007), to access very small momentum transfers that were previously inaccessible. The results are compared to those from PACS-CS (Nguyen, 2011), ETMC (Frezzotti, 2008), and UKQCD (Boyle, 2008), as well as experimental data from Amendolia (1986). The authors find that next-to-leading order chiral perturbation theory (Gasser, 1983) cannot describe their data consistently, motivating a future analysis at next-to-next-to-leading order (Bijnens, 1998) and studies of finite volume effects and lattice artefacts.
