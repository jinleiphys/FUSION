# Shortcuts to nuclear structure: lessons in Hartree-Fock, RPA, and the no-core shell model

> While the no-core shell model is a state-of-the-art microscopic approach to low-energy nuclear structure, its intense computational requirements lead us to consider time-honored approximations such as the Hartree-Fock (HF) approximation and the random phase approximation (RPA). We review RPA and point out some common misunderstandings, then apply HF+RPA to the no-core shell model. Here the main issue is appropriate treatment of contamination by spurious center-of-mass motion.

## Key claim

While the no-core shell model is a state-of-the-art microscopic approach to low-energy nuclear structure, its intense computational requirements lead the authors to consider time-honored approximations such as Hartree-Fock (HF) and the random phase approximation (RPA); the main issue in applying HF+RPA to the no-core shell model is appropriate treatment of contamination by spurious center-of-mass motion, and the authors' initial results were not very successful, likely due to inadequate treatment of center-of-mass and matrix elements in incongruent model spaces.

## Method

The authors implemented Hartree-Fock and RPA in occupation space using a code called SHERPA (SHell-model RPA), solving the RPA matrix equations with the only restriction that the Hartree-Fock wavefunction must be real, allowing for arbitrary deformation and mixing as allowed by the model space. They applied deformed HF+RPA to the no-core shell model for several 0s- and 0p-shell nuclei (4He, 8Be, 10B, 12,14C, 14N, and 16O) in a 2-hbar-Omega space using an effective interaction derived from the bare Argonne V8' interaction, and compared results to exact interacting shell-model calculations in the same model space. To address spurious center-of-mass contamination, they attempted constrained HF by adding beta * H_cm to the variational Hamiltonian and extrapolating the ground state energy as a function of <H_cm> - 3/2 hbar-Omega.

## Key numbers

* For 12C in a 2-hbar-Omega space, the HF values were lower than the exact shell-model (SM) values, which can happen because the HF calculation can mix in spurious motion.
* One signal of mixing of higher-order excitations is that HF and RPA states have spurious center-of-mass contamination, signaled by <H_cm> > 3/2 hbar-Omega.
* The HF+RPA value of <H_cm> took on unphysical values less than 3/2 hbar-Omega.
* For 16O in a 0p1/2-0d5/2 space, as the 0p1/2-0d5/2 splitting increases, the HF state transitions (second order) from mixed parity to pure parity, and both <Q^2> and ground state correlation energy show unphysical contribution from, or "collapse" of, RPA.
* For 28Si in the sd-shell with the Wildenthal interaction, as the 0d5/2 single-particle energy is lowered, the HF state transitions (first order) from deformed to spherical, and there is no collapse in either <Q^2> or ground state correlation energy.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering no-core shell model calculations of Hasan, Vary, and Navratil (2004), who applied spherical Hartree-Fock with second-order corrections to 4He and 16O, by allowing for deformations and parity mixing. The authors present two new results: comparing first- and second-order phase transitions in a non-trivial framework (the interacting shell model) to illuminate the so-called "collapse" of RPA, and applying HF+RPA to the no-core shell model with arbitrary deformation and mixing of parity. The failure of the latter approach is likely due to inadequate treatment of center-of-mass motion and matrix elements in incongruent model spaces, and the authors plan to redo the calculations using nonzero out-of-space matrix elements.
