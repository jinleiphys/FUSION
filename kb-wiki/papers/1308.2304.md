# From the crust to the core of Neutron Stars on a microscopic basis

> Within a microscopic approach the structure of Neutron Stars is usually studied by modelling the homogeneous nuclear matter of the core by a suitable Equation of State, based on a many-body theory, and the crust by a functional based on a more phenomenological approach. We present the first calculation of Neutron Star overall structure by adopting for the core an Equation of State derived from the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory and for the crust, including the pasta phase, an Energy Density Functional based on the same Equation of State, and which is able to describe accurately the binding energy of nuclei throughout the mass table. Comparison with other approaches is discussed. The relevance of the crust Equation of state for the Neutron Star radius is particularly emphasised.

## Key claim

The authors present the first calculation of the overall structure of a neutron star that uses a consistent microscopic framework for both the core and the crust, with the caveat that the treatment is limited to nucleon degrees of freedom only, excluding exotic components like hyperons and quarks due to excessive uncertainty.

## Method

The core equation of state was derived from the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) many-body theory with three-body forces (Urbana model), while the crust was described using the BCPM (Barcelona-Catania-Paris-Madrid) energy density functional whose bulk part is the same BHF equation of state. The crust structure, including the pasta phase (spheres, rods, and slabs), was calculated using a fully self-consistent Thomas-Fermi variational approach within the Wigner-Seitz approximation, with the total energy minimized under constraints of fixed average baryon density and charge neutrality. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were then integrated to obtain the mass-radius relationship.

## Key numbers

- The BCPM functional fit to 579 even-even nuclei gave an average quadratic deviation of 1.58 MeV in binding energy.
- The charge radii of 313 even-even nuclei (not included in the fit) showed a deviation of 0.027 fm.
- The incompressibility is 213 MeV for the BCPM equation of state, 264 MeV for the LS (Lattimer-Swesty) Ska version, and 274 MeV for the SH (Shen) equation of state.
- The saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter was tuned to E/A = -0.16 MeV at a density of 0.16 fm^{-3}.
- The largest observed neutron star mass used for comparison is 2.01 solar masses with an uncertainty of 0.04 solar masses.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Baldo et al. (2008, 2010) and the BCPM functional development to the full neutron star structure, contrasting with more phenomenological models such as the compressible liquid drop model of Lattimer and Swesty (LS) and the relativistic mean field approach of Shen et al. (SH), which also cover the whole star but use different theoretical frameworks for core and crust. The results enable future comparisons with the Brussels-Montreal Skyrme force models (BSk19, BSk20, BSk21) and highlight that using a crust equation of state from a different model than the core can introduce sizeable uncertainties in the neutron star radius, even for standard masses up to 1.2-1.3 solar masses.
