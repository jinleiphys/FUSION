# The LPM effect in sequential bremsstrahlung: dimensional regularization

> The splitting processes of bremsstrahlung and pair production in a medium are coherent over large distances in the very high energy limit, which leads to a suppression known as the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect. Of recent interest is the case when the coherence lengths of two consecutive splitting processes overlap (which is important for understanding corrections to standard treatments of the LPM effect in QCD). In previous papers, we have developed methods for computing such corrections without making soft-gluon approximations. However, our methods require consistent treatment of canceling ultraviolet (UV) divergences associated with coincident emission times, even for processes with tree-level amplitudes. In this paper, we show how to use dimensional regularization to properly handle the UV contributions. We also present a simple diagnostic test that any consistent UV regularization method for this problem needs to pass.

## Key claim

The paper shows that dimensional regularization provides a correct and consistent method for handling ultraviolet divergences in calculations of overlapping double splitting (sequential bremsstrahlung) in the LPM effect, correcting an earlier incomplete \(i\epsilon\) prescription that missed certain contributions from the pole at \(\Delta t = 0\).

## Method

The authors generalize the effective 2-dimensional non-Hermitian quantum mechanics description of the splitting process to an arbitrary number \(d\) of transverse spatial dimensions, using the harmonic oscillator propagator in \(d\) dimensions. They then compute the UV-divergent integrals analytically in terms of Euler beta functions, take the \(d \to 2\) limit, and compare the results against a diagnostic test based on QED double bremsstrahlung in the independent emission approximation for small momentum fractions \(x, y \ll 1\).

## Key numbers

- The single splitting rate formula \(\frac{d\Gamma}{dx} = \frac{\alpha P(x)}{\pi} \, \Re(i \Omega)\) is reproduced from dimensional regularization, where \(\Omega = \sqrt{ -i \hat q_{\rm A} / (2E) \left( -1 + \frac{1}{1-x} + \frac{1}{x} \right) }\) for QCD \(g \to gg\).
- The discrepancy between the correct \(i\epsilon\) prescription and the naive prescription of Ref. [2brem] in the \(y \ll x \ll 1\) limit of QED double bremsstrahlung is \(\frac{d\Gamma}{dx\,dy} - \left[ \frac{d\Gamma}{dx\,dy} \right]^{\rm naive} \simeq \frac{4\alpha_{\rm EM}^2}{\pi^2 x y} \, 2 \Re(-i\Omega_x)\), where \(\Omega_x^{\rm QED} \equiv \sqrt{-i x \hat q / 2E}\).
- The dimensional regularization result for the single splitting integral in \(d\) transverse dimensions is \(- \frac{i d M}{2} \left( \frac{M\Omega}{2\pi} \right)^{d/2} \Beta(\tfrac12{+}\tfrac{d}{4},-\tfrac{d}{4})\), which in the \(d \to 2\) limit gives \(\frac{i M^2\Omega}{\pi}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous methods for computing corrections to standard LPM effect treatments in QCD without soft-gluon approximations (Arnold and Iqbal 2015; Arnold, Chang and Iqbal 2016). It corrects the incomplete \(i\epsilon\) prescription proposed in those earlier papers, which missed so-called "\(1/\pi^2\)" pole contributions. The dimensional regularization approach provides a clean, generalizable method that passes a diagnostic test based on QED independent emission, enabling correct calculations of overlapping double splitting for both QED and QCD at arbitrary momentum fractions.
