# Directed flow of transported and non-transported protons in Au+Au collisions from UrQMD model

> The directed flow of inclusive, transported and non-transported (including produced) protons, as well as antiprotons, has been studied in the framework of Ultra-Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics approach (UrQMD model) for Au+Au collisions at\surdsNN =7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4 and 200 GeV. The rapidity, centrality and energy dependence of directed flow for various proton groups are presented. It is found that the integrated directed flow decreases monotonically as a function of collision energy for\surdsNN =11.5 GeV and beyond. However, the sign-change of directed flow of inclusive protons, seen in experimental data as a function of centrality and collision energy, can be explained by the competing effect of directed flow between transported and non-transported protons. Similarly the difference in directed flow between protons and antiprotons can be explained. Our study offers a conventional explanation on the cause of the v1 sign-change other than the antiflow component of protons alone which is argued to be linked to a phase transition.

## Key claim
The sign-change of directed flow (v1) for inclusive protons as a function of centrality and collision energy can be explained by the competing effect between transported protons (which carry initial quarks from the original nuclei) and non-transported protons (which contain at least one produced quark), rather than requiring a phase transition signal. The study offers a conventional explanation for the v1 sign-change that does not invoke the antiflow component of protons alone.

## Method
The authors used the Ultra-Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model to simulate Au+Au collisions at seven beam energies. They classified protons by tracing the number of initial quarks (from the original projectile/target nuclei) remaining in each final-state proton: transported protons have three initial quarks, non-transported protons have one or two initial quarks, and produced protons have zero initial quarks. The directed flow v1(y) was calculated for each proton group as a function of rapidity, centrality, and collision energy.

## Key numbers
- Transported protons account for 12% of inclusive protons at sqrt(s_NN)=11.5 GeV, and 0.91% at 200 GeV
- Produced protons account for 2.1% of inclusive protons at 11.5 GeV, and 50% at 200 GeV
- The v1(y') slope for inclusive protons is positive at 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, and 39 GeV, then becomes negative at 62.4 GeV
- The integrated directed flow decreases monotonically as a function of collision energy for sqrt(s_NN)=11.5 GeV and beyond
- The v1(y') slope of transported protons reaches a maximum at approximately 20% centrality at 11.5 GeV

## Context
This work extends the experimental studies of directed flow by the STAR Collaboration at RHIC, particularly the measurements of proton and antiproton v1 at 200 GeV (STAR, 2008) and at lower energies (STAR, 2011). The authors provide an alternative explanation to the phase transition interpretation previously proposed for the v1 sign-change. The study enables future work to disentangle transport and production contributions in experimental data, and suggests that the apparent "collapse" of v1 may be understood through conventional hadronic dynamics rather than requiring new physics.
