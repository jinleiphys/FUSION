# First clear evidence of quantum chaos in the bound states of an atomic nucleus

> We study the spectral fluctuations of the $^{208}$Pb nucleus using the complete experimental spectrum of 151 states up to excitation energies of $6.20$ MeV recently identified at the Maier-Leibnitz-Laboratorium at Garching, Germany. For natural parity states the results are very close to the predictions of Random Matrix Theory (RMT) for the nearest-neighbor spacing distribution. A quantitative estimate of the agreement is given by the Brody parameter $ω$, which takes the value $ω=0$ for regular systems and $ω\simeq 1$ for chaotic systems. We obtain $ω=0.85 \pm 0.02$ which is, to our knowledge, the closest value to chaos ever observed in experimental bound states of nuclei. By contrast, the results for unnatural parity states are far from RMT behavior. We interpret these results as a consequence of the strength of the residual interaction in $^{208}$Pb, which, according to experimental data, is much stronger for natural than for unnatural parity states. In addition our results show that chaotic and non-chaotic nuclear states coexist in the same energy region of the spectrum.

# First clear evidence of quantum chaos in the bound states of an atomic nucleus

## Key claim

For natural parity states in 208Pb, the Brody parameter ω = 0.85 ± 0.02 is the closest value to chaos ever observed in experimental bound states of nuclei, while unnatural parity states in the same energy region (ω = 0.43 ± 0.03) are far from Random Matrix Theory behavior, demonstrating that chaotic and non-chaotic nuclear states coexist in the same spectrum.

## Method

The authors analyzed the complete experimental spectrum of 151 states in 208Pb up to 6.20 MeV excitation energy, identified via (p, p'), (d, p) and (d, d') reactions using the Q3D magnetic spectrograph at the Maier-Leibnitz-Laboratorium. They performed separate unfolding of all Jπ sequences with at least 5 consecutive states using the constant temperature formula, then fitted the cumulative nearest-neighbor spacing distribution to the Brody distribution to extract the Brody parameter ω for natural parity states, unnatural parity states, and the full set.

## Key numbers

- Brody parameter for natural parity states: ω = 0.85 ± 0.02 (71 spacings)
- Brody parameter for unnatural parity states: ω = 0.43 ± 0.03 (44 spacings)
- Brody parameter for all states combined: ω = 0.68 ± 0.02 (115 spacings)
- Total of 151 states identified in 208Pb at excitation energies below 6.20 MeV
- Typical experimental resolution of 3 keV; median uncertainty of 70 eV for (p, p') excitation energies
- Neutron separation energy S(n) = 7.368 MeV in 208Pb

## Context

This work extends the systematic studies of spectral fluctuations in nuclear bound states by Shriner et al. (1991), who found ω = 0.43 ± 0.05 across 988 spacings from 60 nuclei and ω = 0.72 ± 0.16 for light nuclei with A ≤ 50. The results confirm shell-model predictions by Zelevinsky et al. (1996) and Molina et al. (2000) that chaos arises when the residual interaction is strong enough to destroy mean-field order, and that the residual pn interaction is much stronger than the nn interaction. The complete, pure level sequences in 208Pb enable future studies of how chaos develops with excitation energy and how parity-dependent residual interactions govern the transition from regular to chaotic dynamics.
