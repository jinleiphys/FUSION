# Spindown of Isolated Neutron Stars: Gravitational Waves or Magnetic Braking?

> We study the spindown of isolated neutron stars from initially rapid rotation rates, driven by two factors: (i) gravitational wave emission due to r-modes and (ii) magnetic braking. In the context of isolated neutron stars, we present the first study including self-consistently the magnetic damping of r-modes in the spin evolution. We track the spin evolution employing the RNS code, which accounts for the rotating structure of neutron stars for various equations of state. We find that, despite the strong damping due to the magnetic field, r-modes alter the braking rate from pure magnetic braking for B<10^{13}G. For realistic values of the saturation amplitude, the r-mode can also decrease the time to reach the threshold central density for quark deconfinement. Within a phenomenological model, we assess the gravitational waveform that would result from r-mode driven spindown of a magnetized neutron star. To contrast with the persistent signal during the spindown phase, we also present a preliminary estimate of the transient gravitational wave signal from an explosive quark-hadron phase transition, which can be a signal for the deconfinement of quarks inside neutron stars.

# Spindown of Isolated Neutron Stars: Gravitational Waves or Magnetic Braking?

## Key claim

The paper claims that r-mode gravitational wave emission alters the magnetic braking rate of isolated neutron stars for magnetic fields below 10^13 G, but contrary to prior work by Lai (2001) which found r-mode effects up to 10^14 G, the inclusion of self-consistent magnetic damping of r-modes restricts r-mode-driven spindown effects to fields below approximately 10^12 G.

## Method

The authors solve coupled evolution equations for the neutron star's angular frequency Ω and r-mode amplitude α, including gravitational radiation damping, viscous damping (bulk and shear), magnetic braking from a rotating dipole field, and a magnetic damping term F_m from toroidal field generation by the r-mode. They use the RNS code to construct sequences of rotating neutron star models for three equations of state (EoS A, BBB2, APR) with constant baryonic mass, tracking the time to reach a central density of 5ρ_0 (the assumed threshold for quark deconfinement). The spin evolution is computed numerically from the Kepler frequency at birth down to the critical configuration where the non-rotating star has central density 5ρ_0.

## Key numbers

- For magnetic fields B ≤ 10^12 G, r-mode spindown takes over from magnetic braking at a few hundred seconds after birth.
- The gravitational damping rate ratio F_g^APR / F_g^EoS A ≈ 6, making r-mode spindown more pronounced for stiffer equations of state.
- For B = 10^13 G with α_sat = 0.01, the gravitational wave signal from the saturated phase is at least an order of magnitude above the anticipated sensitivity of Advanced LIGO at 20 Mpc, with a typical signal-to-noise ratio of approximately 20.
- At B = 10^14 G for the softest EoS, the signal-to-noise ratio in Advanced LIGO drops to approximately 2.
- The characteristic cooling time from the modified URCA process is τ_c ≈ 1 year, with the star cooling to T ≈ 10^9 K within a few seconds from birth temperatures of 10^11 K.
- The critical configuration (just reaching deconfinement density) shows no change in time to deconfinement for any magnetic field value, but heavier masses at birth would reach deconfinement faster.

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Staff et al. (2010) on magnetic braking alone to deconfinement, and by Lai (2001) on r-modes in magnetized neutron stars, by including self-consistent magnetic damping of r-modes that was omitted in those works. The results contradict the conclusion of Lai (2001) that r-modes alter spindown for fields below 10^14 G, finding instead that magnetic damping restricts this to B ≤ 10^12 G. The paper also updates the gravitational wave signal calculations of Owen et al. (1998) for the case of magnetized neutron stars, and provides preliminary estimates for gravitational waves from an explosive quark-hadron phase transition (Quark-Nova) as studied by Niebergal et al. (2010). The work enables predictions for detectability of r-mode signals by Advanced LIGO and the Einstein Telescope, and suggests that gravitational waves could probe the neutron star equation of state.
