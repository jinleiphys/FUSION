# Systematic study on production of hidden-bottom pentaquark via $γp$ and $π^{-}p$ scatterings

> The production of hidden-bottom pentaquark $P_{b}$ states via $γp$ and $π^{-}p$ scatterings is studied within an effective Lagrangian approach and the vector-meson-dominance mechnism. For the $P_{b}$ production in the process $γp\rightarrow Υp$, the dipole Pomeron model is employed to calculate the background contribution, and the experimental data can be well described. For the process $π^{-}p\rightarrow Υn$, the Reggeized $t$-channel with $π$ exchange is considered as the main background for the $P_{b}$ production. Near the threshold, two-peak structure from the states $P_{b (11080)$ and $P_{b}(11125)$ can be observed if energy bin width is chosen as 0.01 GeV, and the same result is obtained in the $π^{-}p$ scattering. Moreover, by taking the branching ratio of Br$[{P_{b}\rightarrow πN}]\simeq 0.05\%$, the numerical result shows that the average value of cross section from the $P_{b}(11080)$ state produced in the $γp$ or $π^{-}p$ scattering reaches at least 0.1 nb with a bin of 0.1 GeV. Even if we reduce the branching ratio of the $P_{b}$ state into $πN$ channel by one order, the theoretical average of the cross section from $P_{b}(11080)$ production in $π^{-}p$ scattering can reach the order of 0.01 nb with a bin of 0.1 GeV, which means that the signal can still be clearly distinguished from the background. The experimental measurements and studies on the hidden-bottom pentaquark $P_{b}$ state production in the $γp $ or $π^{-}p$ scattering near-threshold energy region around $W\simeq 11$ GeV are strongly suggested, which are accessible at COMPASS and JPARC. Particularly, the result of the photoproduction suggests that it is very promising to observe the hidden-bottom pentaquark at proposed EicC facility in China.

# Systematic study on production of hidden-bottom pentaquark via γp and π⁻p scatterings

## Key claim
The hidden-bottom pentaquark states P_b(11080) and P_b(11125) can be produced via γp → Υp and π⁻p → Υn scatterings near threshold energy W ≈ 11 GeV, with predicted cross sections that should be experimentally observable at COMPASS, J-PARC, and the proposed EicC facility, provided the branching ratio Br[P_b → πN] is at least 0.05% and the energy bin width is sufficiently small (0.01 GeV) to resolve the two-peak structure.

## Method
The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach combined with the vector-meson-dominance (VMD) mechanism to model the production of P_b states through s-channel exchange. For the γp → Υp reaction, the dipole Pomeron model was used to calculate the background contribution, with parameters tuned to reproduce existing experimental data. For the π⁻p → Υn reaction, the background was described by Reggeized t-channel pion exchange, using the Regge propagator with trajectory α_π(t) = 0.7(t − m_π²). The coupling constants for the P_b states were derived from predicted decay widths from Gutsche (2019) and the assumed branching ratio Br[P_b → πN] ≈ 0.05%.

## Key numbers
- Masses of the two predicted P_b states: P_b(11080) and P_b(11125), with decay widths Γ_{P_b(11080)→ΥN} = 0.38 MeV and Γ_{P_b(11125)→ΥN} = 3.27 MeV
- Coupling constants: g_{P_b(11080)ΥN} = 0.074, g_{P_b(11125)ΥN} = 0.213; electromagnetic couplings eh = 0.00016 and 0.00045 respectively
- With Br[P_b → πN] ≈ 0.05% and bin width 0.1 GeV, the average cross section from P_b(11080) production reaches at least 0.1 nb in both γp and π⁻p scatterings
- With Br[P_b → πN] reduced to 0.0005%, the average cross section from P_b(11080) in π⁻p scattering reaches the order of 0.01 nb with a 0.1 GeV bin
- The Υ → e⁺e⁻ decay width is Γ_{Υ→e⁺e⁻} ≈ 1.34 keV, giving e/f_Υ ≈ 0.0076
- The Υ → ππ decay width is 0.027 keV, giving g_{Υππ} ≈ 0.0007
- The coupling constant g_{πNN}²/4π = 12.96
- Estimated event rate at EicC: 1×10⁵ events per 0.01 GeV near the P_b mass for one-day run with 0.1 fb⁻¹ luminosity, assuming 1 nb/0.01 GeV cross section

## Context
This work extends the study of hidden-charm pentaquark production (P_c states observed by LHCb in 2019) to the bottom sector, using the heavy quark symmetry that relates Σ_b(*)B interactions to Σ_c(*)D interactions. The authors build on the molecular state predictions of Gutsche (2019) for hidden-bottom pentaquark masses and decay widths, and on their own previous work on P_c photoproduction (Wang 2019) and pion-induced production (Wang 2019). The study enables experimental searches at existing facilities (COMPASS, J-PARC) and the proposed EicC in China, providing specific cross section predictions and optimal energy windows (W ≈ 11 GeV) for observing these states.
