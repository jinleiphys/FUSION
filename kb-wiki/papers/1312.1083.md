# On the conversion of neutron stars into quark stars

> The possible existence of two families of compact stars, neutron stars and quark stars, naturally leads to a scenario in which a conversion process between the two stellar objects occurs with a consequent release of energy of the order of $10^{53}$ erg. We discuss recent hydrodynamical simulations of the burning process and neutrino diffusion simulations of cooling of a newly formed strange star. We also briefly discuss this scenario in connection with recent measurements of masses and radii of compact stars.

## Key claim

The paper argues that if two families of compact stars (neutron stars and quark stars) exist, the conversion of a neutron star into a quark star could release energy of order 10^53 erg, but this scenario is presented as a speculative possibility whose phenomenological relevance depends on the specific equation of state parameters (Set1 vs. Set2) and the assumption that the conversion can be triggered.

## Method

The author reviews 3+1D hydrodynamical simulations of the conversion process modeled as a deflagration wave, using the Lattimer-Swesty equation of state for the neutron star (compressibility K=240 MeV) and the MIT bag model for quark stars with two parameter sets (Set1 and Set2). Neutrino diffusion simulations are then performed to model the cooling of the newly formed strange star, using the inverse mean free path for neutrino scattering off degenerate quarks to estimate diffusion timescales.

## Key numbers

- Energy release from conversion: of order 10^53 erg
- Nuclear saturation density: n_0 = 0.16 fm^-3
- Critical temperature for hadron-to-quark-gluon plasma crossover: T_c ~ 170 MeV
- Sound velocity of hadron resonance gas: ~ sqrt(1/6); of quark gluon plasma: ~ sqrt(1/3)
- Neutron star masses up to 2 solar masses (citing Demorest 2010)
- Indications of stars with radius smaller than about 10 km (citing Guillot 2013, Lattimer 2013)
- MIT bag model parameters: Set1: B^{1/4}_eff = 142 MeV, a_4 = 0.9; Set2: B^{1/4}_eff = 141 MeV, a_4 = 0.65
- Strange quark mass: m_s = 100 MeV
- Energy per baryon of ground state strange quark matter: Set1 E/A = 860 MeV, Set2 E/A = 930 MeV
- Central temperature of newly formed strange star: up to 40 MeV
- Neutrino diffusion timescale estimate: ~ 1 s (using T ~ 50 MeV, mu_i ~ 500 MeV, R ~ 10 km)
- Cooling time to below 1 MeV: 10-15 s
- Initial neutrino luminosity: 10^52 erg/s

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron star to quark star conversion (Drago 2005, Niebergal 2010) by incorporating full 3+1D hydrodynamical simulations of the burning process (Herzog 2011, Pagliara 2013) and neutrino diffusion simulations. The scenario is motivated by the difficulty of explaining both 2 solar mass neutron stars and very compact stars (radius < 10 km) with a single family of compact objects (Lattimer 2006). The paper enables future searches for neutrino signals from galactic conversion events and speculates on connections to gamma-ray-burst light curves with quiescent times, such as GRB 110709B (Zhang 2011).
