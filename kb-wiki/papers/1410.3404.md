# Photon Emission from a Momentum Anisotropic Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We compute the photon emission rate from a quark-gluon plasma with an anisotropic particle momentum distribution induced by a non-vanishing local shear pressure tensor. Our calculation includes photon production through Compton scattering and quark-antiquark annihilation at leading order in $α_s$, with all off-equilibrium corrections to leading order in the momentum anisotropy. For fermions we prove that the Kubo-Martin-Schwinger (KMS) relation holds in the hard loop regime for any particle momentum distribution function that is reflection-symmetric. This supports the equivalence, for 2 to 2 scattering processes, of the diagrammatic and kinetic approaches to calculating the photon emission rate. We compare the viscous rates from these two approaches at weak and realistic coupling strengths and provide parameterizations of the equilibrium and viscous photon emission rates for phenomenological studies in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper computes the photon emission rate from a quark-gluon plasma with a momentum anisotropy induced by shear viscosity, including viscous corrections to both the particle distribution functions and the scattering matrix elements, but only to leading order in the shear stress tensor and only for 2-to-2 processes at leading order in α_s, leaving the inclusion of soft 2-to-n diagrams for future work.

## Method

The authors compute the photon emission rate using both a diagrammatic approach (starting from the photon self-energy in the closed time path formalism) and a kinetic theory approach (using the collision integral for Compton scattering and quark-antiquark annihilation). For the diagrammatic approach, the momentum loop integral is split into a soft part (q < q_cut) requiring hard thermal loop resummed quark propagators and a hard part (q > q_cut) using bare propagators, with the cutoff dependence checked for consistency. The viscous correction is linearized in the shear stress tensor π^{μν}/(e+p), and the KMS relation is proven to hold for the fermion self-energy in the hard loop regime for any reflection-symmetric momentum distribution.

- The asymptotic thermal quark mass is m_∞² = C_F g_s² T² / 4, where C_F is the Casimir invariant for the fundamental representation of SU(3).
- The exponent λ in the energy dependence of the off-equilibrium correction χ(k/T) = (k/T)^λ is constrained to 1 ≤ λ ≤ 2.
- The pseudocritical temperature for the quark-hadron phase transition is T_c ∼ 155–165 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts by Dusling (2008, 2009) and Dion (2011) that included viscous corrections to distribution functions but ignored medium modifications of the collision matrix element itself, and builds on the approach of Baier (1997) and Schenke (2006) for simple parameterizations of momentum anisotropy. The results enable phenomenological studies of thermal photon emission in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC by providing parameterizations of the equilibrium and viscous photon emission rates that can be used with hydrodynamic simulations.
