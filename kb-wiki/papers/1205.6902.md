# Response functions of cold neutron matter: density, spin and current fluctuations

> We study the response of a single-component pair-correlated baryonic Fermi-liquid to density, spin, and their current perturbations. A complete set of response functions is derived in the low-temperature regime both within an effective theory based on a small momentum transfer expansion and within a numerical scheme valid for arbitrary momentum transfers. A comparison of these two approaches validates the perturbative approximation within the domain of its convergence. We derive the spectral functions of collective excitations associated with the density, density-current, spin, and spin-current perturbations. The dispersion relations of density and spin fluctuations are derived and it is shown that the density fluctuations lead to exciton-like undamped bound states, whereas the spin excitations correspond to diffusive modes above the pair-breaking threshold. The contribution of the collective pair-breaking modes to the specific heat of neutron matter at subnuclear densities is computed and is shown to be comparable to that of the degenerate electron gas at not too low temperatures.

## Key claim

The paper derives a complete set of response functions for density, spin, and current perturbations in cold neutron matter, showing that density fluctuations produce undamped exciton-like bound states while spin fluctuations yield diffusive modes above the pair-breaking threshold; these results are valid specifically in the low-temperature regime (T/T_c ≤ 0.5) and at subnuclear densities where S-wave pairing dominates.

## Method

The authors used finite-temperature Green's function methods for superfluid Fermi systems, resumming an infinite series of particle-hole ladder diagrams in neutron matter with contact interactions parametrized by leading-order Landau parameters. They derived analytic expressions for the four coupled vertex functions (normal and anomalous) and the corresponding polarization tensors, then computed the response functions both via a low-momentum transfer perturbative expansion (small q/k_F or q v_F/ω) and via direct numerical evaluation valid for arbitrary momentum transfers. The comparison between these two approaches validated the perturbative approximation within its domain of convergence.

## Key numbers

- The pairing gap in the inner crust of a neutron star is Δ ≤ 1 MeV for S-wave neutron pairing
- The temperature scale characterizing dynamical processes in neutron stars is T ≤ 1 MeV
- Fermi energies of fermions in compact stars are in the range ε_F ∼ 10–100 MeV
- The density of states on the Fermi surface is ν = m* k_F / (2π²)
- The specific heat contribution from collective pair-breaking modes is comparable to that of the degenerate electron gas at not too low temperatures (no explicit numerical value given for the specific heat)

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study of density response in superfluid neutron matter (Sedrakian 2010) to include spin, density-current, and spin-current perturbations, and revises some perturbative results from that prior work. It builds on the Abrikosov-Gor'kov theory of superconductors and the Larkin-Migdal-Leggett generalization of Landau Fermi-liquid theory to superfluids, using Landau parameters for bulk neutron matter computed in Sedrakian (2006). The results enable calculation of neutrino emission rates from compact stars via weak interactions, where the density and spin response functions map directly onto the vector and axial-vector weak current vertices in the non-relativistic limit.
