# Optimized Two-Baryon Operators in Lattice QCD

> A set of optimized interpolating operators which are dominantly coupled to each eigenstate of two baryons on the lattice is constructed by the HAL QCD method. To test its validity, we consider heavy dibaryons $Ω_{3Q}Ω_{3Q}$ ($Q=s,c$) calculated by (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations with nearly physical pion mass. The optimized two-baryon operators are shown to provide effective energies of the ground and excited states separately stable as a function of the Euclidean time. Also they agree to the eigenenergies in a finite lattice box obtained from the leading-order HAL QCD potential $V({\boldsymbol{r}})$ within statistical errors. The overlapping factors between the optimized sink operators and the state created by the wall-type source operator indicate that $V( \boldsymbol{r})$ can be reliably extracted, no matter whether the spacetime correlation of two baryons is dominated by the ground state or the excited state. It is suggested that the optimized set of operators is useful for variational studies of hadron-hadron interactions.

## Key claim

The optimized two-baryon sink operators constructed from the leading-order HAL QCD potential provide effective energies for ground and excited states that agree with the eigenenergies obtained from solving the Schrödinger equation on the lattice, *provided* the derivative expansion of the non-local potential is a good approximation within statistical errors.

## Method

The authors used (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations with nearly physical pion mass (146 MeV) on an (8.1 fm)^3 lattice to compute the spacetime correlation function R(r,t) for two heavy dibaryons Ω_{3Q}Ω_{3Q} (Q=s,c) in the ^1S_0 channel. From R(r,t), they extracted the leading-order (LO) HAL QCD potential V(r) via the time-dependent HAL QCD method, then diagonalized the LO Hamiltonian H_LO = H_0 + V on the finite lattice box to obtain eigenfunctions ψ_n(r) and eigenenergies ΔE_n. These eigenfunctions were used to construct projected sink operators S_n(t) = Σ_r ψ_n^†(r) [Σ_x Ω(x+r,t)Ω(x,t)], and the resulting projected temporal correlators R_n(t) were analyzed to extract effective energies ΔE^eff_n(t).

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_π ≃ 146 MeV; lattice spacing a ≃ 0.0846 fm (a^{-1} ≃ 2.333 GeV); lattice size L=96, giving La ≃ 8.1 fm
- Ω_{3s} mass: m_{Ω_{3s}} ≃ 1712 MeV; Ω_{3c} mass: m_{Ω_{3c}} ≃ 4796 MeV
- For 2Ω_{3s}: total measurements = 307,200; LO potential extracted at t/a = 17
- For 2Ω_{3c}: total measurements = 896; LO potential extracted at t/a = 26
- Overlap ratio for 2Ω_{3s}: |a_1/a_0| ≃ 0.4; for 2Ω_{3c}: |a_1/a_0| ≃ 3 (wall source couples primarily to n=1 state)
- To achieve 10% systematic error in unprojected effective energy, required t/a > 580 for 2Ω_{3s} and t/a > 1500 for 2Ω_{3c}

## Context

This work extends the optimized operator approach proposed by Iritani et al. (2019) from heavy pion mass (510 MeV) and smaller volumes (3.6-5.8 fm)^3 to near-physical pion mass (146 MeV) with a large volume (8.1 fm)^3. It demonstrates that the HAL QCD method can reliably extract baryon-baryon interactions regardless of whether the ground or excited state dominates the correlation function, while the naive Lüscher finite-volume method without variational analysis can produce "fake plateaux" as shown by Iritani et al. (2016, 2019). The results enable future variational studies using the optimized operator basis for hadron-hadron interactions.
