# Meson-Baryon s-wave Resonances with Strangeness -3

> Starting from a consistent SU(6) extension of the Weinberg-Tomozawa (WT) meson-baryon chiral Lagrangian (Phys. Rev. D74 (2006) 034025), we study the s-wave meson-baryon resonances in the strangeness S=-3 and negative parity sector. Those resonances are generated by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with the WT interaction used as kernel. The considered mesons are those of the 35-SU(6)-plet, which includes the pseudoscalar (PS) octet of pions and the vector (V) nonet of the rho meson. For baryons we consider the 56-SU(6)-plet, made of the 1/2+ octet of the nucleon and the 3/2+ decuplet of the Delta. Quantum numbers I(J^P)=0(3/2^-) are suggested for the experimental resonances Omega*(2250)- and Omega*(2380)-. Among other, resonances with I=1 are found, with minimal quark content sss\bar{l}l', being s the strange quark and l, l' any of the the light up or down quarks. A clear signal for such a pentaquark would be a baryonic resonance with strangeness -3 and electric charge of -2 or 0, in proton charge units. We suggest looking for K- Xi- resonances with masses around 2100 and 2240 MeV in the sector 1(1/2^-), and for pi Omega- and K- Xi*- resonances with masses around 2260 MeV in the sector 1(3/2^-).

## Key claim

The authors suggest that the experimentally observed Omega*(2250)- and Omega*(2380)- resonances have quantum numbers I(J^P)=0(3/2-), based on a spin-flavor SU(6) extension of the Weinberg-Tomozawa chiral Lagrangian, but they note that this identification is tentative and based on relative closeness of masses and widths and observed decay channels.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation using a spin-flavor SU(6) extended Weinberg-Tomozawa meson-baryon interaction as the kernel, considering mesons from the 35-SU(6)-plet (pseudoscalar octet and vector nonet) and baryons from the 56-SU(6)-plet (1/2+ octet and 3/2+ decuplet). They search for poles of the T-matrix in the second Riemann sheet to extract masses, widths, and coupling constants of dynamically generated resonances in the strangeness S=-3, negative parity sector. The calculations neglect the widths of decuplet baryons and vector mesons.

## Key numbers

- Theoretical Omega*(2265)- with mass 2265 MeV and width 82 MeV in the I=0, J^P=3/2- sector, tentatively identified with the experimental Omega*(2250)- (mass 2252 +/- 9 MeV, width 55 +/- 18 MeV)
- Theoretical Omega*(2343)- with mass 2343 MeV and width 36 MeV in the I=0, J^P=3/2- sector, tentatively identified with the experimental Omega*(2380)- (mass 2384 +/- 13 MeV, width 26 +/- 23 MeV)
- Two I=1, J^P=1/2- resonances predicted: one at mass 2100 MeV with width 118 MeV coupling to K- Xi-, and one at mass 2241 MeV with width 63 MeV coupling to K- Xi-
- An I=1, J^P=3/2- resonance at mass 2258 MeV with width 101 MeV coupling to pi Omega- and K- Xi*-
- An I=1, J^P=3/2- resonance at mass 2288 MeV with width 32 MeV coupling to pi Omega- and K- Xi*-
- Experimental Omega*(2470)- has mass 2474 +/- 12 MeV and width 72 +/- 33 MeV, decaying to Omega- pi+ pi-

## Context

This work extends previous SU(3) chiral unitary approaches that successfully described lower-lying baryon resonances such as Lambda(1405), N*(1535), and Xi(1620) using only pseudoscalar mesons and the baryon octet (previous work by the same community). It also extends studies of J^P=3/2- resonances like Lambda(1520) and Xi(1820) from the pseudoscalar meson octet coupled to the baryon decuplet. The inclusion of vector mesons and the full 56-baryon multiplet is motivated by the observation that Omega*(2380)- decays to both K- Xi*0 and anti-K*0 Xi- with similar strengths. The predicted I=1 resonances with minimal quark content sss anti-l l' would constitute pentaquark signals, and the authors suggest experimental searches for K- Xi- resonances around 2100 and 2240 MeV and for pi Omega- and K- Xi*- resonances around 2260 MeV.
