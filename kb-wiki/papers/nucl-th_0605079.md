# Baryonic Strangeness and Related Susceptibilities in QCD

> The ratios of off-diagonal to diagonal conserved charge susceptibilities e.g., chi_{BS}/chi_{S}, chi_{QS}/chi_{S}, related to the quark flavor susceptibilities, have proven to be discerning probes of the flavor carrying degrees of freedom in hot strongly interacting matter. Various constraining relations between the different susceptibilities are derived based on the Gell-Mann-Nishijima formula and the assumption of isospin symmetry. Using generic models of deconfined matter and results form lattice QCD, it is demonstrated that the flavor carrying degrees of freedom at a temperature above 1.5T_c are quark-like quasiparticles. A new observable related by isospin symmetry to C_{BS} = -3chi_{BS}/chi_{S} and equal to it in the baryon free regime is identified. This new observable, which is blind to neutral and non-strange particles, carries the potential of being measured in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the ratio \(C_{BS} = -3\chi_{BS}/\chi_{S}\) and related observables can distinguish between different degrees of freedom in hot QCD matter, and that lattice QCD results for temperatures above \(1.5T_c\) are consistent with quark-like quasiparticles rather than composite bound states, under the assumption of isospin symmetry and vanishing chemical potentials.

## Method

The authors derive constraining relations between diagonal and off-diagonal conserved charge susceptibilities (baryon number \(B\), electric charge \(Q\), strangeness \(S\)) using the Gell-Mann-Nishijima formula and isospin symmetry. They compare lattice QCD results from Gavai and Gupta (2005) for the ratios \(C_{BS}\) and \(C_{QS}\) with predictions from a hadronic resonance gas model (truncated at the \(\Omega^-\) mass) and from a non-interacting quark quasiparticle gas, evaluating the temperature dependence from below \(T_c\) up to approximately \(3T_c\).

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature is \(T_c \sim 170\) MeV, with an uncertainty range of \(T_c = 170 \pm 10\) MeV used in the hadron gas comparison.
- The lattice results for \(C_{BS}\) and \(C_{QS}\) are compared with the hadron resonance gas model up to \(T_c\); above \(1.5T_c\) the data approach \(C_{BS} \approx 1\) and \(C_{QS} \approx 1\), consistent with a quark quasiparticle picture.
- The relation \(C_{QS} \simeq (3 - C_{BS})/2\) is derived from isospin symmetry and verified against lattice data from Gavai and Gupta (2005), where the hazed cyan points (calculated from \(C_{BS}\)) exactly coincide with the solid blue points for \(C_{QS}\).
- In the two-flavor case (no strangeness), the relation \(C_{QB} = 1/2\) is derived as a test for any model.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Koch et al. (2005), who proposed conserved charge susceptibilities as diagnostics of flavor-carrying degrees of freedom, and builds on lattice calculations by Gavai and Gupta (2005). The paper shows that a hadronic resonance gas model (as originally introduced by Hagedorn) describes the susceptibility ratios well below \(T_c\) but fails above it, while a quark quasiparticle model matches lattice results above \(1.5T_c\). The derived isospin relations enable a new observable \(C_{BS}\) that is blind to neutral and non-strange particles, potentially measurable in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC.
