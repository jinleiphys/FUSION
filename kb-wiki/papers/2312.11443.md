# Enhanced contribution of the pairing gap to the QCD equation of state at large isospin chemical potential

> I study QCD at large isospin density, which is known to be in the superfluid state with Cooper pairs carrying the same quantum number as pions. I solve the gap equation derived from the perturbation theory up to the next-to-leading order corrections. The pairing gap at large isospin chemical potential is found to be enhanced compared to the color-superconducting gap at large baryon chemical potential due to the $\sqrt{2}$ difference in the exponent arising from the stronger attraction in one-gluon exchange in the singlet channel. Then, using the gap function, I evaluate the contribution of the condensation energy of the superfluid state to the QCD equation of state. At isospin chemical potential of a few GeV, where the lattice QCD and the perturbative QCD can be both applied, the effect of the condensation energy becomes dominant even compared to the next-to-leading order corrections to the pressure in the perturbation theory. It resolves the discrepancy between the recent lattice QCD results and the perturbative QCD result.

## Key claim

The pairing gap in QCD at large isospin chemical potential is exponentially enhanced compared to the color-superconducting gap at large baryon chemical potential due to a factor of \(\sqrt{2}\) difference in the exponent arising from stronger one-gluon exchange attraction in the color-singlet \(q\bar{q}\) channel; this enhancement makes the condensation energy contribution to the equation of state dominant over next-to-leading order perturbative corrections at isospin chemical potentials of a few GeV, resolving the discrepancy between lattice QCD and perturbative QCD results.

## Method

The author solves the gap equation derived from perturbation theory up to next-to-leading order corrections in the isospin basis (using \(u\) and \(d\) quarks with opposite chemical potentials), following the formalism developed for color superconductivity but adapted for the \(q\bar{q}\) pairing channel. The gap equation is converted to a differential equation and solved using Airy functions, with the wave-function renormalization factor \(b_0'\) computed for the first time at nonzero isospin chemical potential. The condensation energy contribution to the pressure is then evaluated using the obtained gap function.

## Key numbers

- The coefficient \(b_{-1}\) in the weak-coupling expansion of the gap is \(b_{-1} = 3\pi^2/2\) for isospin QCD, which is \(1/\sqrt{2}\) times the value \(3\pi^2/\sqrt{2}\) for baryon QCD.
- The wave-function renormalization factor is \(b_0' = (\pi^2 + 4)/16\) for isospin QCD, giving a numerical factor \(e^{-b_0'} \simeq 0.420\); for baryon QCD, \(b_0' = (\pi^2 + 4)/8\) with \(e^{-b_0'} \simeq 0.177\).
- The color factor for the one-gluon exchange in the singlet channel is \(C_F = 4/3\) for isospin QCD, compared to \(2/3\) for the 2SC phase at nonzero baryon chemical potential.
- The lattice QCD calculation by Abbott et al. (2023) provided the QCD equation of state at \(T \approx 0\) up to \(\mu_I \simeq 3\) GeV, with states of up to 6144 pions.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD calculations of Graf et al. (2015) by incorporating the nonperturbative BCS condensation energy, which was previously identified as potentially significant by Son (2000) and Chiba et al. (2023) but without a reliable evaluation. The results resolve the discrepancy between the lattice QCD results of Abbott et al. (2023) and perturbative QCD at \(\mu_I \gtrsim 2\) GeV, and the formalism parallels the color-superconductivity calculations of Brown et al. (1999) and Wang et al. (2001) adapted to the isospin channel. This enables more accurate equation of state predictions for the phase-quenched theory, which Moore and Gorda (2023) showed can bound the pressure of the original QCD theory through rigorous inequalities.
