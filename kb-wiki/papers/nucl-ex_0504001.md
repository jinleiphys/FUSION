# High pT leading hadron suppression in nuclear collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 20 -- 200 GeV: data versus parton energy loss models

> Experimental results on high transverse momentum (leading) hadron spectra in nucleus-nucleus collisions in the range sqrt(s_NN) = 20 -- 200 GeV are reviewed with an emphasis on the observed suppression compared to free space production in proton-proton collisions at the corresponding center-of-mass energies. The transverse-momentum and collision-energy (but seemingly not the in-medium path length) dependence of the experimental suppression factors measured in central collisions is consistent with the expectations of final-state non-Abelian parton energy loss in a dense QCD medium.

## Key claim

The observed suppression of high transverse momentum hadrons in central nucleus-nucleus collisions is consistent with final-state non-Abelian parton energy loss in a dense QCD medium, but the dependence of the suppression on the in-medium path length (as tested via reaction-plane anisotropy) is significantly stronger than predicted by current jet quenching models, pointing to an additional source of azimuthal anisotropy such as collective elliptic flow.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental data on inclusive high-\(p_T\) leading hadron spectra from CERN-SPS (Pb+Pb, Pb+Au, S+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\approx 20\) GeV) and RHIC (Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 62.4\), 130, and 200 GeV), comparing the measured nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}(p_T)\) to theoretical predictions from parton energy loss models (GLV formalism and Salgado-Wiedemann quenching weights). The author compiles \(R_{AA}\) values for neutral pions and charged hadrons as a function of transverse momentum, collision energy, and azimuthal angle with respect to the reaction plane, and confronts them with calculations using initial gluon densities \(dN^g/dy\) and transport coefficients \(\langle\hat{q}\rangle\) extracted via "jet tomography."

## Key numbers

- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV in central Au+Au, hadron suppression reaches a factor of \(\sim 5\) (i.e., \(R_{AA} \approx 0.2\)) for pions, eta mesons, and inclusive charged hadrons above \(p_T \approx 5\) GeV/\(c\).
- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 62.4\) GeV in central Au+Au, above \(p_T \approx 5\) GeV/\(c\) there are \(\sim 3\) times fewer pions and inclusive hadrons than expected from binary scaling of p+p cross-sections.
- At SPS energies (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 17.3-19.4\) GeV), high-\(p_T\) hadroproduction in central A+A is consistent with \(R_{AA} \approx 1\) (within errors), in contrast to a factor of \(\sim 2\) enhancement observed in peripheral collisions (Cronin effect).
- The extracted initial gluon densities from parton energy loss calculations are: \(dN^g/dy = 400\) at SPS (17.3 GeV), 650 at RHIC (62.4 GeV), \(\sim 900\) at RHIC (130 GeV), and 1100 at RHIC (200 GeV).
- The corresponding time-averaged transport coefficients are: \(\langle\hat{q}\rangle = 3.5\) GeV\(^2\)/fm at SPS, 7 GeV\(^2\)/fm at 62.4 GeV, \(\sim 11\) GeV\(^2\)/fm at 130 GeV, and 14-15 GeV\(^2\)/fm at 200 GeV.
- The measured charged particle multiplicity densities at mid-rapidity are: \(dN_{ch}^{exp}/d\eta = 312 \pm 21\) at SPS, \(475 \pm 33\) at 62.4 GeV, \(602 \pm 28\) at 130 GeV, and \(687 \pm 37\) at 200 GeV.
- The nuclear modification factor for \(\pi^0\) at fixed \(p_T = 4\) GeV/\(c\) as a function of \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) increases monotonically from \(R_{AA} \approx 1\) at SPS to \(R_{AA} \approx 0.2\) at 200 GeV, with model predictions saturating at \(R_{AA} \approx 0.02\) for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \gtrsim 3\) TeV.
- The azimuthal anisotropy of suppression shows a factor of \(\sim 2\) more suppression out-of-plane (\(\phi = \pi/2\)) than in-plane (\(\phi = 0\)) for all centralities at 200 GeV, significantly larger than model predictions using \(\langle\hat{q}\rangle \approx 14\) GeV\(^2\)/fm.

## Context

This review extends the experimental program of jet quenching measurements from CERN-SPS (WA98, CERES/NA45, WA80) through RHIC (PHENIX, STAR, PHOBOS) and confronts the data with theoretical frameworks developed by Gyulassy (1990), Baier-Dokshitzer-Mueller-Peigne-Schiff (BDMPS), and the GLV formalism (Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev). The work builds on earlier studies of the Cronin effect (Cronin, 1975) and Drell-Yan scaling in nuclear collisions (NA50), and enables quantitative extraction of medium properties (gluon density and transport coefficient) via "jet tomography." The observed discrepancy between the measured reaction-plane dependence and model predictions (Dainese, 2004) challenges the assumed \(\propto L\) path-length dependence of energy loss in an expanding medium and motivates further theoretical work on the interplay of parton energy loss and collective flow.
