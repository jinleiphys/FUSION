# Elliptic flow of $ϕ$ meson and strange quark collectivity at RHIC

> Based on A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model, we have studied the elliptic flow $v_{2}$ of $ϕ$ mesons from reconstructed $K^{+}K^{-}$ decay channel at the top Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider energy at Brookhaven National Laboratory. The dependences of $v_{2}$ on transverse momentum $p_T$ and collision centrality are presented and the rescattering effect of $ϕ$ mesons in the hadronic phase is also investigated. The results show that experimental measurement of $v_{2}$ for $ϕ$ mesons can retain the early collision information before $ϕ$ decays and that the $ϕ$ $v_2$ value obeys the constituent quark number scaling which has been observed for other mesons and baryons. Our study indicates that the $ϕ$ $v_2$ mostly reflects partonic level collectivity developed during the early stage of the nucleus-nucleus collision and the strange and light up/down quarks have developed similar angular anistropy properties at the hadronization.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the elliptic flow \(v_2\) of \(\phi\) mesons, reconstructed from \(K^{+}K^{-}\) pairs in the AMPT model, obeys constituent quark number scaling and mostly reflects partonic-level collectivity developed during the early stage of the collision, with the hedge that this conclusion is based on a model calculation using a parton scattering cross section of 10 mb, which produces \(v_2\) values about 25% larger than experimental data for charged kaons and protons at low \(p_T\).

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model with the string melting scenario to simulate minimum-bias \(^{197}\)Au + \(^{197}\)Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV. \(\phi\) mesons were reconstructed from their \(K^{+}K^{-}\) decay channel by fitting the invariant mass distribution with a Breit-Wigner function plus a linear background, and the elliptic flow \(v_2\) was extracted using the event-plane method with a resolution correction. The hadronic rescattering effect was studied by comparing results with and without the ART hadronic transport process.

## Key numbers

- The parton scattering cross section used in the AMPT model was 10 mb.
- The event plane resolution obtained was 0.91, which is 20% larger than the experimental resolution reported by STAR.
- The AMPT calculation results of \(v_2\) for charged kaons and protons are about 25% larger than the experimental data in the \(p_T < 1.5\) GeV/\(c\) region.
- The NCQ scaling was observed for \(\phi\) mesons in the intermediate \(p_T\) region of \(p_T/n_q > 0.6\) GeV/\(c\).
- The \(\phi\) meson \(v_2\) was studied in centrality intervals of 0-20%, 20-40%, 40-80%, and 0-80%.
- The hadronic rescattering effect on \(\phi\) meson \(v_2\) was found to be negligible within errors for \(p_T > 0.4\) GeV/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends the study of elliptic flow and constituent quark number scaling, previously observed for pions, kaons, and protons by the STAR and PHENIX collaborations (STAR 2003, PHENIX 2003), to the \(\phi\) meson, which contains strange quarks. The results suggest that strange quarks develop similar angular anisotropy to light up/down quarks at hadronization, and that \(\phi\) meson \(v_2\) can retain early collision information due to weak hadronic rescattering. This enables future experimental measurements at RHIC to test whether the partonic collectivity inferred from the model is realized in data, and to further probe the properties of the quark-gluon plasma.
