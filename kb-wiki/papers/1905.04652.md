# A diquark model for the d*(2380) dibaryon resonance?

> Diquark models have been applied with varying degree of success to tetraquark and pentaquark states involving both light and heavy quark degrees of freedom. We discuss the applicability of such models to light quark dibaryons, viewed as three-diquark objects. Highlighting the case of the d*(2380) dibaryon resonance, we demonstrate the inapplicability of diquark models in the light quark sector.

## Key claim
Diquark models are inapplicable to light-quark dibaryons such as the d*(2380) resonance, because the isospin-color recoupling factor of 1/9 required to transform a three-diquark (3D) state into a Delta-Delta doorway state would suppress the decay width to less than 10 MeV, far below the observed width of approximately 70 MeV.

## Method
The authors evaluate the mass and decay width of the d*(2380) dibaryon under the assumption that it consists of three vector diquarks in a color-antitriplet configuration, using the diquark mass and interaction parameters from Shi et al. (2019). They compute the SU(2) isospin and SU(3) color recoupling coefficients needed to transform the 3D state into a Delta-Delta hadronic doorway state, and apply these to estimate the resulting width suppression. They also calculate the masses of the I=0 J^P=1^+ deuteron-like and I=1 J^P=0^+ virtual-like NN states predicted by the same diquark model.

## Key numbers
- The d*(2380) resonance has a total width Gamma_d* ≈ 70 MeV, peaked about 80 MeV below the Delta-Delta threshold.
- The isospin recoupling coefficient from 3D to Delta-Delta is -sqrt(1/3), and the color recoupling coefficient is sqrt(1/3), giving a combined product of -1/3 and a quadratic width suppression factor of 1/9.
- The 3D model predicts the deuteron-like state at 2120 MeV (263 MeV below the d*(2380) mass of 2383 MeV, and about 245 MeV above the physical deuteron), and the virtual-like state at 2067 MeV (53 MeV further down).
- The effective diquark mass used is 913.2 MeV, with three diquarks totaling 2740 MeV, implying a binding energy of about 360 MeV for the d*(2380) and a tiny radius of approximately 0.4 fm.

## Context
This work directly contradicts the diquark model proposed by Shi et al. (2019) for the d*(2380) dibaryon, which had overlooked the color-flavor rearrangement suppression. It extends earlier hadronic coupled-channel calculations by Gal and Garcilazo (2013, 2014) and six-quark resonating-group-method calculations by Dong et al. (2016), which both provided more successful descriptions of the d*(2380) width. The finding that the 3D model predicts deuteron-like and virtual-like NN states at masses where no phase-shift anomalies have been observed (up to E_cm = 2.4 GeV) further undermines the diquark approach and suggests that hadronic sizes of 1-2 fm, rather than sub-fermi 3D structures, are relevant for light-quark dibaryons.
