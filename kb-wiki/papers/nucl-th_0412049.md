# Large basis ab initio shell model investigation of 9-Be and 11-Be

> We are presenting the first ab initio structure investigation of the loosely bound 11-Be nucleus, together with a study of the lighter isotope 9-Be. The nuclear structure of these isotopes is particularly interesting due to the appearance of a parity-inverted ground state in 11-Be. Our study is performed in the framework of the ab initio no-core shell model. Results obtained using four different, high-precision two-nucleon interactions, in model spaces up to 9\hbarΩ, are shown. For both nuclei, and all potentials, we reach convergence in the level ordering of positive- and negative-parity spectra separately. Concerning their relative position, the positive-parity states are always too high in excitation energy, but a fast drop with respect to the negative-parity spectrum is observed when the model space is increased. This behavior is most dramatic for 11-Be. In the largest model space we were able to reach, the 1/2+ level has dropped down to become either the first or the second excited state, depending on which interaction we use. We also observe a contrasting behavior in the convergence patterns for different two-nucleon potentials, and argue that a three-nucleon interaction is needed to explain the parity inversion. Furthermore, large-basis calculations of 13-C and 11-B are performed. This allows us to study the systematics of the position of the first unnatural-parity state in the N=7 isotone and the A=11 isobar. The 11-B run in the 9\hbarΩmodel space involves a matrix with dimension exceeding 1.1 x 10^9, and is our largest calculation so far. We present results on binding energies, excitation spectra, level configurations, radii, electromagnetic observables, and 10-Be+n overlap functions.

# Large basis *ab initio* shell model investigation of ⁹Be and ¹¹Be

## Key claim

The first *ab initio* structure investigation of the loosely bound ¹¹Be nucleus shows that, using only two-nucleon interactions in model spaces up to 9ħΩ, the positive-parity states are always too high in excitation energy relative to negative-parity states, but a fast drop is observed with increasing model space; the authors argue that a three-nucleon interaction is needed to explain the parity inversion of the ¹¹Be ground state, as the 1/2⁺ level only drops to become either the first or second excited state depending on which interaction is used, not the ground state.

## Method

The study employs the *ab initio* no-core shell model (NCSM), solving the A-body Schrödinger equation in a Slater determinant harmonic oscillator basis with up to 9ħΩ excitations. Four high-precision two-nucleon interactions are used: Argonne V8′ (AV8′), CD-Bonn 2000 (CDB2k), N³LO, and the Inside Nonlocal Outside Yukawa (INOY) potential. Effective interactions are derived at the two-body cluster level using the Lee-Suzuki unitary transformation, and the many-body eigenvalue problem is solved using the Lanczos algorithm with the specialized shell model code ANTOINE.

## Key numbers

- ⁹Be ground state binding energy with INOY interaction: -56.05 MeV (experimental: -58.16 MeV); with AV8′: -50.20 MeV; with CDB2k: -51.16 MeV; with N³LO: -50.47 MeV (all in 8ħΩ/9ħΩ model spaces)
- ¹¹Be 1/2⁺ level in the largest model space (9ħΩ) drops to become either the first or second excited state, depending on the interaction used
- ¹¹B calculation in 9ħΩ model space involves a matrix dimension exceeding 1.1 × 10⁹
- ¹¹Be 9ħΩ model space dimension: 7.1 × 10⁸; ⁹Be 9ħΩ model space dimension: 2.0 × 10⁸
- ¹³C largest model space: 8ħΩ with dimension 8.2 × 10⁸
- Optimal HO frequencies: ⁹Be: 16 MeV (INOY), 12 MeV (CDB2k and AV8′), 11 MeV (N³LO); ¹¹Be: 17 MeV (INOY), 13 MeV (CDB2k), 12 MeV (N³LO and AV8′)
- ⁹Be ground state quadrupole moment with AV8′: 4.01 e·fm² (experimental: 5.288(38) e·fm²); magnetic moment with AV8′: -1.22 μ_N (experimental: -1.1778(9) μ_N)
- ⁹Be B(E2; 5/2₁⁻ → 3/2₁⁻) with AV8′: 15.0 e²·fm⁴ (experimental: 27.1(2) e²·fm⁴)
- ⁹Be B(M1; 5/2₁⁻ → 3/2₁⁻) with AV8′: 0.37 μ_N² (experimental: 0.54(6) μ_N²)

## Context

This work extends the *ab initio* NCSM approach, previously applied to p-shell nuclei like ⁶Li and ¹⁰B by Navratil et al. (2004), to the neutron-rich isotopes ⁹Be and ¹¹Be for the first time. It contrasts with earlier phenomenological studies such as the variational shell model by Otsuka et al. (1993) using Skyrme interactions, the coupled-channels treatment by Esbensen et al. (1995) and Nunes et al. (1996) using a ¹⁰Be + n picture with Woods-Saxon potentials, and the AMD+HF model by Doté et al. (2000). The results enable future inclusion of three-nucleon forces in large model spaces to resolve the parity inversion problem, and the cluster overlap functions calculated for ¹¹Be → ¹⁰Be + n provide a starting point for *ab initio* descriptions of low-energy nuclear reactions.
