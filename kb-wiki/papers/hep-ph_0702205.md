# Thermal Dilepton Production from Dropping rho in the Vector Manifestation

> We study the pion electromagnetic form factor and the dilepton production rate in hot matter based on the vector manifestation (VM) of chiral symmetry in which the massless vector meson becomes the chiral partner of the pion, giving a theoretical framework of the dropping $ρ$ \^^ a la Brown-Rho scaling. The VM predicts a strong violation of the vector dominance (VD) near the phase transition point associated with the dropping $ρ$. We present the effect of the VD violation to the dilepton production rate and make a comparison to the one predicted by assuming the VD together with the dropping $ρ$.

## Key claim

The vector manifestation (VM) predicts that vector dominance (VD) of the pion electromagnetic form factor is strongly violated near the chiral symmetry restoration point, with the direct γππ coupling approaching 1/2 rather than zero; this violation reduces the dilepton production rate compared to predictions that assume VD together with a dropping ρ mass.

## Method

The authors used the hidden local symmetry (HLS) theory with parameters determined by Wilsonian matching to QCD at a matching scale Λ ~ 1 GeV, incorporating both hadronic thermal corrections (for T < T_f, the flash temperature) and intrinsic temperature effects (for T > T_f) that cause the ρ mass to scale with the quark condensate. They computed the pion electromagnetic form factor and the dilepton production rate from the two-pion process via the imaginary part of the photon self-energy, comparing results with and without the violation of vector dominance.

## Key numbers

- The direct γππ coupling approaches 1/2 with increasing T toward the critical point, implying VD is violated by up to 50% near the critical point.
- At T = 0.8 T_c, the dilepton production rate predicted by the VM is enhanced by a factor of about two compared with the rate from the vacuum ρ parameters.
- The matching scale for the HLS Lagrangian is Λ ~ 1 GeV.
- The ρ meson mass scales as m_ρ(T) ∝ ⟨q̄q⟩_T near T_c.

## Context

This work extends the vector manifestation framework of Harada and Yamawaki (2003) and earlier studies of the VM in hot matter by Harada, Kim, and Rho (2000) and others, which predicted that the ρ meson becomes massless at chiral restoration. It directly addresses the interpretation of dielectron mass spectra observed at CERN SPS (CERES collaboration) and later at NA60, where an enhancement below the ρ/ω resonance was seen. The results enable a field-theoretically controlled comparison to dilepton data that can distinguish between dropping-mass scenarios and collisional broadening models (Rapp and Wambach, 2000), and can be extended to finite density for conditions at CERN/SPS and future GSI/FAIR.
