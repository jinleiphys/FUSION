# Observation of the Upsilon(2 3D1) and indication of the Upsilon(1 3D1)

> 3 pages, 6 figures; v2: fig 1a replaced, some text changed accordingly; v3: 5 pages, 7 figures; substantial changes; in particular, we studied the significance of the discovered signals and obtained results as expected from v1 and v2. As a consequence, the Upsilon(2 3D1) seems to be observed by us in BABAR data, whereas we obtain a strong indication for the existence of the Upsilon(1 3D1)

## Key claim

The authors claim to have observed the Upsilon(2 3D1) and found a strong indication of the Upsilon(1 3D1) in previously published BABAR data, but the Upsilon(1 3D1) signal is only at 3.0 sigma significance, which they describe as a "strong indication" rather than a definitive observation.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed BABAR data from e+e- -> pi+ pi- Upsilon(1S) -> pi+ pi- e+e- events, applying much narrower invariant-mass windows (plus or minus 20-30 MeV) around the Upsilon(1S) mass than the original analysis. They studied the growth of event counts in 10-MeV-wide bins as a function of the window size, fitting quadratic curves to determine background levels and signal significances for candidate bins in the invariant-mass distributions.

## Key numbers

- Upsilon(1 3D1) candidate mass: 10098 plus or minus 5 MeV, with a signal significance of 3.0 standard deviations (12.0 plus or minus 3.98 signal events over a background of 18.0 plus or minus 2.53 events in the relevant bin)
- Upsilon(2 3D1) candidate mass: 10495 plus or minus 5 MeV, with a signal significance of 10.7 standard deviations (49 plus or minus 4.58 signal events over a background of 81 plus or minus 0.81 events in the relevant bin)
- The Upsilon(1 3D1) signal bin (bin no. 7) contained 30 plus or minus 3.07 total events for a window of Delta = 30 MeV
- The Upsilon(2 3D1) signal bin contained 130 plus or minus 4.51 total events for a window of Delta = 22 MeV

## Context

This work extends the search for D-wave bottomonium states beyond the S-wave states already established in the b-bbar spectrum, following the BABAR Collaboration's analysis of hadronic transitions (BABAR, 2008). The results support the harmonic-oscillator approximation to the resonance-spectrum expansion (HORSE) model previously developed by the authors (van Beveren and Rupp, 2009), and the observed spacing of about 380 MeV between the D states makes a 3 3D1 assignment for the Upsilon(10860) more plausible than 5 3S1. The CLEO Collaboration (2004) had previously discovered a 2-- b-bbar resonance at 10161.1 MeV, which BABAR later confirmed (2010) at 10164.5 MeV, but the present work finds the 1-- Upsilon(1 3D1) about 63 MeV lower, making CLEO's quantum number assignment plausible.
