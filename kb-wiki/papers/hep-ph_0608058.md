# Quark-hadron duality in neutrino scattering

> We present a phenomenological model of the quark-hadron transition in neutrino-nucleon scattering. Using recently extracted weak nucleon transition form factors, we investigate the extent to which local and global quark-hadron duality is applicable in the neutrino F_1, F_2 and F_3 structure functions, and contrast this with duality in electron scattering. Our findings suggest that duality works relatively well for neutrino-nucleon scattering for the F_2 and F_3 structure functions, but not as well for F_1. We also calculate the quasielastic, resonance and deep inelastic contributions to the Adler sum rule, and find it to be satisfied to within 10% for 0.5 < Q^2 < 2 GeV^2.

## Key claim

Quark-hadron duality works relatively well for neutrino-nucleon scattering for the \(F_2\) and \(F_3\) structure functions, but not as well for \(F_1\); the Adler sum rule is satisfied to within 10% for \(0.5 < Q^2 < 2\) GeV\(^2\), though the model only includes the first four resonances and the degree of duality depends on the axial form factor fall-off scenario.

## Method

The authors computed neutrino-nucleon structure functions in the resonance region (\(1.1 \leq W \leq 1.6\) GeV) using recently extracted weak nucleon transition form factors for the \(P_{33}(1232)\), \(P_{11}(1440)\), \(D_{13}(1520)\), and \(S_{11}(1535)\) resonances, with two scenarios ("fast fall-off" and "slow fall-off") for the axial form factors. They compared the averaged resonance structure functions to leading twist (twist-two) structure functions from GRV, CTEQ, and MRST parton distribution functions at \(Q^2 = 10\) GeV\(^2\), quantifying duality via the ratio of integrals over the Nachtmann variable \(\xi\). They also calculated the quasielastic, resonance, and deep inelastic contributions to the Adler sum rule.

## Key numbers

- For electron scattering, the ratio \(I_2^{eN}\) of integrated resonance to leading twist \(F_2\) is within \(\sim 20\%\) of unity for \(Q^2 \gtrsim 1\) GeV\(^2\) when using the total DIS structure function, and for \(Q^2 \gtrsim 0.5\) GeV\(^2\) when using the valence-only structure function.
- For electron scattering, the ratio \(I_1^{eN}\) for \(2xF_1\) is \(30-50\%\) above unity for most of the range \(Q^2 \gtrsim 0.5\) GeV\(^2\).
- The Adler sum rule is satisfied to within 10% for \(0.5 < Q^2 < 2\) GeV\(^2\).
- The vector form factor for the \(P_{33}(1232)\) resonance uses a dipole function \(D_V = 1/(1+Q^2/M_V^2)^2\) with vector mass parameter \(M_V = 0.84\) GeV; the axial form factor uses \(D_A = 1/(1+Q^2/M_A^2)^2\) with axial mass \(M_A = 1.05\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of Bloom-Gilman quark-hadron duality, previously confirmed for electron scattering at Jefferson Lab (Niculescu et al., 2000; Christy et al., 2002; Armstrong et al., 2003; Liang et al., 2004), to neutrino scattering, for which almost nothing was known empirically. The authors use transition form factors from their earlier work (Lalakulich et al., 2005, 2006) and compare with leading twist parton distributions from GRV, CTEQ, and MRST. The results enable future tests of duality with high-intensity neutrino beams at Fermilab and provide constraints on axial form factors and nonresonant backgrounds needed for neutrino-oscillation experiments.
