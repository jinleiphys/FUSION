# Deconfinement in the presence of a strong magnetic background: an exercise within the MIT bag model

> We study the effect of a very strong homogeneous magnetic field B on the thermal deconfinement transition within the simplest phenomenological approach: the MIT bag pressure for the quark- gluon plasma and a gas of pions for the hadronic sector. Even though the model is known to be crude in numerical precision and misses the correct nature of the (crossover) transition, it provides a simple setup for the discussion of some subtleties of vacuum and thermal contributions in each phase, and should provide a reasonable qualitative description of the critical temperature in the presence of B. We find that the critical temperature decreases, saturating for very large fields.

## Key claim
The critical temperature for deconfinement decreases with increasing magnetic field strength and saturates at very large fields, contrary to earlier model predictions that it would vanish. This qualitative behavior is consistent with the most recent lattice QCD results, though the MIT bag model necessarily predicts a first-order transition rather than the correct crossover.

## Method
The authors compute the renormalized pressure for the quark-gluon plasma phase using the MIT bag model with quarks in a strong magnetic field, treating the full Landau level sum and taking the leading order of a large-field expansion. For the hadronic phase, they model a free pion gas with charged pions coupling to the magnetic field and neutral pions unaffected. The critical temperature is extracted from the equality of pressures between the two phases, with careful subtraction of purely magnetic contributions to ensure consistency with magnetic catalysis.

## Key numbers
- For two flavors with quark masses m_u = m_d = 5 MeV and bag constant B^{1/4} = 200 MeV, the QGP pressure increases with magnetic field for values eB = 20 m_pi^2, 40 m_pi^2, and 60 m_pi^2 (where m_pi = 138 MeV is the vacuum pion mass).
- The critical temperature for deconfinement decreases and saturates for large B, with no value of magnetic field that allows a vanishing critical temperature.
- The maximum magnetic field expected in non-central collisions at the LHC with deconfined matter is conservatively estimated at eB ~ 210 m_pi^2, corresponding to the early universe value from Vachaspati (1991).
- In the large B limit, the charged pion pressure reduces to P = -(eB)^2/(4 pi^2) * (-ln(2)/2) * x_pi, where x_pi = m_pi^2/(2eB).

## Context
This work extends the MIT bag model treatment of deconfinement under magnetic fields, differing qualitatively from the earlier approach of Agasian (2008) which predicted the critical temperature vanishing at eB ~ 25 m_pi^2. The authors' subtraction procedure yields results consistent with magnetic catalysis and with the lattice QCD findings of Bali (2011) showing a saturating critical temperature, in contrast to earlier effective model calculations and lattice simulations that predicted increasing or flat behavior. The approach enables a simple qualitative description of the T-eB phase diagram and suggests that confinement may be the central driver of the critical temperature's functional behavior.
