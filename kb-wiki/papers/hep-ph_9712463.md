# Neutrino ground state in a dense star

> It has recently been argued that long range forces due to the exchange of massless neutrinos give rise to a very large self-energy in a dense, finite-ranged, weakly-charged medium. Such an effect, if real, would destabilize a neutron star. To address this issue we have studied the related problem of a massless neutrino field in the presence of an external, static electroweak potential of finite range. To be precise, we have computed to one loop the exact vacuum energy for the case of a spherical square well potential of depth alpha and radius R. For small wells, the vacuum energy is reliably determined by a perturbative expansion in the external potential. For large wells, however, the perturbative expansion breaks down. A manifestation of this breakdown is that the vacuum carries a non-zero neutrino charge. The energy and neutrino charge of the ground state are, to a good approximation for large wells, those of a neutrino condensate with chemical potential mu=alpha. Our results demonstrate explicitly that long-range forces due to the exchange of massless neutrinos do not threaten the stability of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that long-range forces due to massless neutrino exchange do not destabilize a neutron star, contrary to earlier claims by Fischbach (1997). The key hedge is that their calculation is performed for a spherical square well potential of modest strength and range compared to a realistic neutron star, but they argue the result is sufficient to show the ground state energy is well-behaved.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop exact vacuum energy for a massless left-handed neutrino field in the presence of a static, spherical square well electroweak potential of depth α and radius R. They solve the Weyl equation for scattering solutions, derive the phase shifts, and compute the renormalized vacuum energy and neutrino charge by subtracting the first and second Born terms from the phase shift integrals. The calculation is non-perturbative in the external potential and covers the range 0 ≤ αR ≤ 100.

## Key numbers

- For αR = 20 (the point where Fischbach (1997) predicted energy on the order of a neutron star mass), the renormalized vacuum energy is W ≃ −2.3 keV.
- The crossover to a non-zero neutrino charge in the ground state occurs at αR = π.
- For large wells, the ground state energy approaches W_cond = −α⁴R³/(18π), corresponding to a neutrino condensate with chemical potential μ = α.
- The neutrino charge of the condensate for large wells approaches q_cond = 2(αR)³/(9π).
- In a typical neutron star, α ∼ 20 eV and R ∼ 10 km, giving αR ∼ 10¹².

## Context

This work extends the critique by Abada et al. (1996), who argued Fischbach's (1997) calculation of neutrino exchange energy in neutron stars was incorrect but did not account for finite-size effects. The authors complete the proof by showing that the neutrino ground state energy in a finite-range potential is well-behaved and matches the condensate picture anticipated by Abada et al. (1996). The result enables future studies of neutrino condensates in dense astrophysical objects and resolves the question of whether massless neutrinos threaten neutron star stability.
