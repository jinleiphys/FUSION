# Nucleon electric and magnetic polarizabilities in Holographic QCD

> We analyze the resonance contributions to the generalized Baldin sum rule, namely the sum of the generalized electric and magnetic nucleon polarizabilities $α_E(Q^2)$ and $β_M(Q^2)$, within the Holographic QCD model by Witten, Sakai, and Sugimoto (WSS). In particular, we account for the contributions from the first low-lying nucleon resonances with spin 1/2 and 3/2 and both parities. After having extrapolated the WSS model parameters to fit experimental data on baryonic observables, we compare our findings with alternative predictions in literature, finding a qualitative agreement with all of them. Moreover, at least for the proton case, where data are available, our results are in qualitative accordance with resonance contributions extracted from experimental nucleon-resonance helicity amplitudes.

## Key claim
The Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto holographic QCD model, after extrapolating parameters to fit experimental baryon masses, yields resonance contributions to the generalized Baldin sum rule that are in qualitative agreement with alternative theoretical predictions and, for the proton, with resonance contributions extracted from experimental helicity amplitude data, though the model produces a smaller magnitude and an unphysical decrease to zero at very low momentum transfer.

## Method
The author computed the helicity amplitudes for transitions between nucleons and low-lying nucleon resonances (spin 1/2 and 3/2, both parities) using the WSS holographic model's baryon wavefunctions and electromagnetic current operators. These helicity amplitudes were inserted into the generalized Baldin sum rule expression relating the sum of electric and magnetic polarizabilities to an integral over the structure function F1(x,Q2). The WSS model parameters were fixed by setting Nc=3, matching the nucleon-Delta(1232) mass difference to 293 MeV (giving MKK ≈ 488 MeV), and setting the nucleon mass to 939 MeV (giving λ ≈ 54.4).

## Key numbers
- Delta(1232) resonance gives the dominant contribution to the generalized Baldin sum rule at low Q2, and is isospin-independent (identical for proton and neutron)
- The WSS model parameters were fixed using m_Delta(1232) - m ≈ 293 MeV, yielding M_KK ≈ 488 MeV
- The nucleon mass was set to m ≈ 939 MeV, giving λ ≈ 54.4
- The sum α_E(Q2) + β_M(Q2) displays a smooth fall-off with increasing momentum squared for Q2 ≳ 0.05 GeV2
- At very small Q2 a peak is observed, which the author suggests may be an artifact of the model

## Context
This work extends the holographic QCD approach previously applied to nucleon spin polarizabilities by Bigazzi (2023) to the electric and magnetic generalized polarizabilities and the Baldin sum rule. The results are compared with chiral perturbation theory predictions by Alarcon (2020) and Nevado (2007), the MAID model by Drechsel (2000, 1998), empirical parametrizations of the F1 structure function by Sibirtsev (2013) and Hall (2014), and experimental helicity amplitude data from CLAS (2009) and Hiller Blin (2022). The approach enables a complementary theoretical account of resonance effects that are only partially described by chiral effective field theories, and may help interpret the puzzling low-Q2 behavior of the proton electric polarizability observed in Jefferson Lab and MAMI experiments.
