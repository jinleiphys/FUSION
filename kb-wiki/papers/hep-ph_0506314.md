# Nucleon and hadron structure changes in the nuclear medium and impact on observables

> We review the effect of hadron structure changes in a nuclear medium using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which is based on a mean field description of non-overlapping nucleon (or baryon) bags bound by the self-consistent exchange of scalar and vector mesons. This approach leads to simple scaling relations for the changes of hadron masses in a nuclear medium. It can also be extended to describe finite nuclei, as well as the properties of hypernuclei and meson-nucleus deeply bound states. It is of great interest that the model predicts a variation of the nucleon form factors in nuclear matter. We also study the empirically observed, Bloom-Gilman (quark-hadron) duality. Other applications of the model include subthreshold kaon production in heavy ion collisions, D and D-bar meson production in antiproton-nucleus collisions, and J/Psi suppression. In particular, the modification of the D and D-bar meson properties in nuclear medium can lead to a large J/Psi absorption cross section, which explains the observed J/Psi suppression in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model predicts that hadron structure changes in the nuclear medium, leading to a new saturation mechanism for nuclear matter and scaling relations for hadron masses, but the model's validity depends on the Born-Oppenheimer approximation that quarks have enough time to adjust to local mean fields, which is justified because the typical adjustment time (approximately 0.5 fm) is much shorter than the time for the nucleon to move a significant fraction of its own radius (approximately 9 fm).

## Method

The authors use the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which describes non-overlapping nucleon bags interacting self-consistently with isoscalar-scalar (sigma) and isoscalar-vector (omega) mesons in the mean-field approximation, where mesons couple directly to confined quarks rather than to point-like nucleons. For finite nuclei, they apply the Born-Oppenheimer approximation to derive the effective equation of motion for the nucleon and self-consistent equations for the meson mean fields. The model is extended to include the rho meson for correct symmetry energy, and variants incorporate meson structure effects (QMC-II) or treat the sigma meson through appropriate parametrization.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density is denoted as rho_0, used as the reference density throughout the paper
- The in-medium omega meson mass is approximately 722 MeV at 0.6 rho_0, as measured by the CBELSA/TAPS collaboration for momenta less than 500 MeV/c
- The maximum acceleration of a nucleon at the nuclear surface is given by alpha_max = V_0/(4a M_N), where V_0 is approximately -50 MeV, a is approximately 0.5 fm, and M_N is the free nucleon mass
- The typical time for adjustment in quark motion is approximately 0.5 fm, corresponding to the Delta excitation energy
- The nucleon position in the instantaneous rest frame changes by less than approximately 9 fm before the quark motion adjusts, based on the ratio |R'(t)-R_0'|/R_N approximately t^2/75 with t in fm

## Context

This review extends the QMC model originally developed by Guichon (1988) and refined by Saito and Thomas in the 1990s, which itself built on earlier work by Celenza et al. using nontopological soliton models and by Wong using the MIT bag model. The paper contrasts the QMC approach with Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) and Skyrme effective forces, showing that QMC provides natural coupling constants through naive dimensional analysis and can explain phenomena such as the nuclear EMC effect, the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly, and J/Psi suppression without requiring a quark-gluon plasma phase. The work enables systematic predictions for hypernuclei, meson-nucleus bound states, and in-medium hadron property modifications that can be tested in experiments at JLab, MAMI, ELSA, and KEK.
