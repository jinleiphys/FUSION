# Neutrino-nucleus cross section in the impulse approximation regime

> In the impulse approximation regime the nuclear response to a weakly interacting probe can be written in terms of the measured nucleon structure fuctions and the target spectral function, yielding the energy and momentum distribution of the constituent nucleons. We discuss a calculation of charged current neutrino-oxygen interactions in the quasielastic channel, carried out within nuclear many body theory. The proposed approach, extensively and successfully employed in the analysys of electron-nucleus scattering data, allows for a parameter free prediction of the neutrino-nucleus cross section, whose quantitative understanding will be critical to the analysis of the next genaration of high precision neutrino oscillation experiments.

## Key claim

The paper presents a parameter-free calculation of charged-current neutrino-oxygen quasielastic cross sections using nuclear many-body theory, but the accuracy of the impulse approximation (IA) scheme depends on the assumption that at high momentum transfer the scattering involves only one nucleon, with the hadron produced at the weak vertex being totally decoupled from the recoiling spectator system.

## Method

The authors computed the neutrino-nucleus cross section in the impulse approximation regime using the nuclear spectral function obtained within the Local Density Approximation (LDA) and dipole parametrizations for the nucleon form factors. Final state interactions (FSI) were included via a folding function derived from measured nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes and Monte Carlo configurations sampled from the realistic oxygen wave function of Pieper (1992). The effects of Pauli blocking were incorporated by modifying the spectral function with a step function depending on the average nuclear Fermi momentum.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino energy used in calculations: E = 1 GeV
- Electron scattering angle for differential cross section: theta_e = 30 degrees
- Fermi gas model parameters used for comparison: Fermi momentum k_F = 221 MeV, average separation energy epsilon = 35 MeV
- Average nuclear Fermi momentum from Local Density Approximation: k_F = 209 MeV
- Momentum transfer squared at the quasielastic peak for the 30-degree setup: Q^2 ~ 2.4 GeV^2
- Pauli blocking effects become very large at Q^2 < 0.2 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the nuclear many-body theory approach previously and successfully applied to electron-nucleus scattering data (Benhar 1991, Benhar 1993) to the case of neutrino-nucleus interactions. The calculations enable parameter-free predictions for neutrino oscillation experiments, where the deficit of measured cross sections at low Q^2 relative to Monte Carlo simulations (Q2 reference) may be explained by the combined effects of Pauli blocking and final state interactions. The approach can be extended to exclusive and inelastic channels following the work done for electron-nucleus scattering.
