# Light nuclei photoproduction in relativistic heavy ion ultraperipheral collisions

> We have investigated light nuclei pair photoproduction in relativistic heavy ion ultraperipheral collisions. As a first attempt, we employ our previously developed quantum electrodynamics model, which incorporates a wave-packet description of initial nuclei, to compute the cross section for proton-antiproton pair photoproduction. The effective vertex for the photon and proton interaction is chosen based on studies of two-photon exchange effects in hadron physics. We present the transverse momentum, invariant mass, and azimuthal angle distributions of proton-antiproton pairs at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV in Au+Au ultraperipheral collisions. We observe a $\cos(2ϕ)$ modulation and an almost negligible $\cos(4ϕ)$ modulation in the azimuthal angle distribution. Our studies helps us better understand the matter generated by light.

# Light nuclei photoproduction in relativistic heavy ion ultraperipheral collisions

## Key claim
As a first attempt using a quantum electrodynamics model with a wave-packet description of initial nuclei, the authors compute the Born-level cross section for proton-antiproton pair photoproduction in ultraperipheral collisions, finding that the azimuthal angle distribution exhibits a clear cos(2φ) modulation but only an almost negligible cos(4φ) modulation. The results depend on the choice of the neutron emission probability function P(b_T), with total cross sections varying by a factor of about 3-4 depending on which parameterization is used.

## Method
The authors employ a QED model incorporating a wave-packet description of the initial nuclei to compute the differential cross section for γ+γ → p+ p̅ at Born level in Au+Au ultraperipheral collisions. The effective photon-proton vertex Γ^μ is chosen based on studies of two-photon exchange effects in hadron physics, using the fermionic propagator with the Sachs form factors G_E and G_M parameterized with Λ = 0.84 GeV. The high-dimensional integration is performed using the ZMCintegral package, and three different parameterizations of the neutron emission probability P(b_T) are compared: the Giant Dipole Resonance model form 1-exp(-w), its linear approximation w, and the XnXn model using equivalent photon approximation.

## Key numbers
- Total cross section for p p̅ pair photoproduction at √s_NN = 200 GeV in Au+Au UPCs: 4.5 ± 0.1 μb (P(b_T)=1-e^{-w}), 6.3 ± 0.1 μb (P(b_T)=w), 15.7 ± 0.3 μb (XnXn model)
- Total cross section at √s_NN = 54 GeV: 51 ± 3 nb (P(b_T)=1-e^{-w}), 78 ± 4 nb (P(b_T)=w), 170 ± 1 nb (XnXn model)
- Differential cross section dσ/dP_T^{p p̅} ranges from 0.1 to 0.3 mb/GeV, with peaks around 0.035-0.040 GeV
- Azimuthal modulation coefficients at √s_NN = 200 GeV: A_0 = 0.73 μb, A_2 = -0.08 μb, A_4 ~ 10^{-3} μb (P(b_T)=1-e^{-w}); A_0 = 1.01 μb, A_2 = -0.11 μb, A_4 ~ 10^{-3} μb (P(b_T)=w); A_0 = 2.52 μb, A_2 = -0.28 μb, A_4 = 0.01 μb (XnXn)
- The invariant mass distribution grows smoothly for M^{p p̅} < 2.4 GeV and decreases slowly above 2.4 GeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous QED model with wave-packet description of nuclei (Wang 2021, Wang 2022, Lin 2022), which was developed for lepton pair photoproduction, to the case of light nuclei (proton-antiproton) pair production. It is closely linked to studies of two-photon exchange effects in hadron physics (Guttmann 2011, Zhou 2009, Blunden 2003) and complements recent related work on γ+γ → p+ p̅ through different schemes (Zhang 2024) and γ+γ → π+π- in e+e- collisions (Jia 2024). The results suggest that further experimental measurements of p p̅ pair photoproduction in UPCs can provide additional constraints on the effective photon-proton vertex and two-photon exchange effects, and the framework can be extended to deuterium or helium nuclei photoproduction.
