# Elliptic flow of heavy flavors

> The propagation of charm and bottom quarks through an ellipsoidal domain of quark gluon plasma has been studied within the ambit of non-equilibrium statistical mechanics. Energy dissipation of heavy quarks by both radiative and collisional processes are taken in to account. The experimental data on the elliptic flow of the non-photonic electrons resulting from the semi-leptonic decays of hadrons containing heavy flavours has been reproduced with the same formalism that has been used earlier to reproduce the nuclear suppression factors. The elliptic flow of the non-photonic electron from heavy meson decays produced in nuclear collisions at LHC and low energy RHIC run have also been predicted.

## Key claim
The elliptic flow of non-photonic electrons from heavy-flavor decays at RHIC can be reproduced using the same Fokker-Planck formalism and input parameters that previously reproduced the nuclear suppression factor, but only when both radiative and collisional energy loss are included and the background matter expands hydrodynamically with a specific sound velocity.

## Method
The authors solved the Fokker-Planck equation for charm and bottom quarks propagating through an ellipsoidal quark-gluon plasma domain, using drag and diffusion coefficients that include both collisional and radiative processes (with dead cone and Landau-Pomerenchuk-Migdal effects). The background medium evolution was modeled with (2+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics with boost invariance, and the heavy quark initial momentum distributions were taken from NLO MNR calculations. The final non-photonic electron spectra were obtained by convoluting the heavy quark distributions with Peterson fragmentation functions and semi-leptonic decay kinematics.

## Key numbers
- At RHIC energy (√sNN = 200 GeV) for minimum bias Au+Au collisions, v2HF reaches a maximum of about 8% then saturates for pT > 2 GeV, using cs = 1/√4.
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio extracted from energy dissipation is η/s ∼ 0.1–0.2, slightly above the AdS/CFT bound.
- For low energy RHIC runs at √sNN = 39 GeV, the baryonic chemical potential at freeze-out is given by μB(sNN) = a(1 + √sNN/b)−1 with a = 0.967 ± 0.032 GeV and b = 6.138 ± 0.399 GeV.
- Predictions are made for v2HF at LHC (0–10% centrality) and low energy RHIC (√sNN = 39 GeV, 0–10% centrality).

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier Fokker-Planck studies (Das et al., 2009–2010) that reproduced nuclear suppression factors RAA at RHIC, now applying the same formalism to elliptic flow. It builds on prior heavy quark flow calculations by Rapp (2009), Gossiaux (2009), and Hirano (2009), and uses the hydrodynamic framework of Teaney (2003) and the (2+1)-dimensional hydrodynamical model with boost invariance. The results enable predictions for LHC and low-energy RHIC runs, and the extracted η/s values provide constraints on the QGP transport properties.
