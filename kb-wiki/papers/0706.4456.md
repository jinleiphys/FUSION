# Heavy-Quark Kinetics in the QGP at LHC

> We present predictions for the nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow of D and B mesons, as well as of their decay electrons, in semicentral Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC. Heavy quarks are propagated in a Quark-Gluon Plasma using a relativistic Langevin simulation with drag and diffusion coefficients from elastic interactions with light anti-/quarks and gluons, including non-perturbative resonance scattering. Hadronization at T_c is performed within a combined coalescence-fragmentation scheme.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that at LHC energies, the nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow of D and B mesons and their decay electrons will be quantitatively similar to earlier RHIC results, because harder initial heavy-quark spectra are offset by a moderate increase in interaction strength in the early phases where non-perturbative resonance scattering is inoperative.

## Method

Heavy quarks (charm and bottom) were propagated through a Quark-Gluon Plasma using a relativistic Langevin simulation with drag and diffusion coefficients derived from elastic scattering with light quarks, antiquarks, and gluons, including non-perturbative resonance scattering via D- and B-meson states. The medium was modeled as a spatially homogeneous elliptic thermal fireball expanding isentropically, with temperature inferred from an ideal gas QGP equation of state with Nf=2.5 massless quark flavors. Hadronization at the critical temperature Tc was performed using a combined coalescence-fragmentation scheme, with semileptonic electron decays computed using 3-body kinematics.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature T0 ≈ 520 MeV for semicentral Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.5 TeV with impact parameter b ≈ 7 fm
- Total charged hadron multiplicity extrapolated to dNch/dy ≈ 1400 for central Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.5 TeV
- Fireball lifetime τfb ≈ 6 fm/c at the end of the hadron-gas QGP mixed phase
- Inclusive light-quark elliptic flow ⟨v2⟩ = 7.5%
- D- and B-meson resonance dissociation temperature Tdiss = 2 Tc = 360 MeV, with a melting factor of (1 + exp[(T - Tdiss)/Δ])⁻¹ where Δ = 50 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier Fokker-Planck and Langevin studies of heavy-quark diffusion at RHIC (van Hees 2004, van Hees 2005, Rapp 2006) to LHC energies, using the same non-perturbative resonance scattering approach motivated by lattice QCD computations suggesting survival of mesonic states above Tc. The predictions enable experimental tests at LHC of whether the strong coupling of charm and bottom quarks observed at RHIC persists at higher energies, and whether the correlation between large elliptic flow and small nuclear modification factor is partially reversed by coalescence hadronization.
