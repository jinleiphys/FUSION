# Efimov Effect Revisited with Inclusion of Distortions

> An elementary proof of the 3-body Efimov effect is provided in the case of a separable 2-body potential which binds at zero energy a light particle to a heavy one. The proof proceeds by two steps, namely {\it i)} a projection of the Hamiltonian in a subspace and the observation that the projected Hamiltonian generates an arbitrarily large number of bound states, and {\it ii)} a use of the Hylleraas-Undheim theorem to recover the unprojected Hamiltonian. The definition of the projectors we use can include mean field distortions.

## Key claim

The paper provides an elementary proof of the Efimov effect for a three-body system with a separable two-body potential that binds a light particle to a heavy one at zero energy, but the proof is conditional: an arbitrarily large number of three-body bound states is obtained only if the mass ratio of the light to heavy particles is sufficiently small.

## Method

The authors use a Born-Oppenheimer (BO) approach, treating the light particle as the fast degree of freedom and the two heavy particles as the slow degree of freedom, with the heavy-heavy interaction assumed too weak to bind. They first solve the fast Hamiltonian for the light particle bound to two fixed heavy centers, obtaining an effective potential proportional to -1/x^2 for large heavy-particle separation x. They then reinterpret the BO ansatz as a generator coordinate method (GCM), which projects the full Hamiltonian into a trial subspace, and use the Hylleraas-Undheim theorem to extend the result to the full Hilbert space. A Hartree self-consistent treatment with harmonic oscillator constraints is introduced to include mean-field distortions of the pair wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The critical strength parameter for the separable potential is lambda = gamma^3, where 1/gamma defines the short range of the form factor f(p)=1/[pi(p^2+gamma^2)].
- The asymptotic binding energy of the light particle in the BO approximation is eta(x) = -c^2/x^2 + O(1/x^3), with c approximately 0.5671 and d approximately 0.3079/gamma.
- The number of bound states N_B is estimated to diverge as N_B proportional to Lambda Log(Lambda), where Lambda is of order 1/epsilon and epsilon = m/M is the mass ratio of the light to heavy particles.
- The repulsive correction to the effective potential from the gradient of the fast wave function is of order epsilon/x, which for sufficiently small epsilon still allows an arbitrarily large number of bound states.

## Context

This work extends the formal proof of the Efimov effect by Fonseca, Redish, and Shanley (1979) using a simpler Born-Oppenheimer approach rather than Faddeev equations, and provides a rigorous connection to the generator coordinate method. The paper enables future studies of Efimov states with more flexible wave function descriptions including mean-field distortions, and suggests extension to coupled-channel treatments of the three possible pair partitions. The authors note that the four-body Efimov effect for three heavy particles and one light particle has been studied by Kruger and Perne (1980) and Adhikari and Fonseca (1981).
