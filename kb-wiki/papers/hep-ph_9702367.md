## Key claim

The Gottfried sum rule is violated, indicating a flavor asymmetry between light antiquark distributions in the nucleon, but the sum rule is not an exact consequence of QCD and its violation does not threaten the fundamental theory; the observed deficit is attributed to a nonperturbative excess of \(\bar d\) over \(\bar u\) in the proton.

## Method

The paper reviews the derivation of the Gottfried sum rule in the naive parton model and summarizes experimental measurements of the integral \(I_G = \int dx (F_2^p - F_2^n)/x\) from deep inelastic lepton scattering at SLAC, EMC, BCDMS, and NMC, as well as Drell-Yan data from E288, E772, and NA51. It then surveys theoretical explanations for the flavor asymmetry, including meson-cloud models, chiral models, the Pauli exclusion principle, diquark models, lattice QCD studies, and isospin-symmetry violation, and discusses experimental probes such as Drell-Yan, W/Z production, quarkonium production, charged-hadron production, and neutrino scattering.

## Key numbers

* NMC (1991) Gottfried integral: \(I_G(0,1) = 0.240 \pm 0.016\) at \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\), approximately 28% smaller than the Gottfried sum 1/3.
* NMC (1994) reanalysis: \(I_G(0,1) = 0.235 \pm 0.026\).
* SLAC (1975) integral in the range \(0.02 \le x \le 0.82\): \(I_G = 0.200 \pm 0.040\).
* EMC (1987) integral in the whole \(x\) range: \(I_G(0,1) = 0.235^{+0.110}_{-0.099}\).
* BCDMS (1990) integral in the range \(0.06 \le x \le 0.8\) at \(Q^2 = 20\) GeV\(^2\): \(I_G = 0.197 \pm 0.006 \text{(stat.)} \pm 0.036 \text{(syst.)}\).
* NA51 Drell-Yan data: \(\bar u/\bar d = 0.51 \pm 0.04 \pm 0.05\) at \(x = 0.18\).
* Flavor asymmetry from NMC deficit: \(\int_0^1 dx (\bar u - \bar d) = -0.140 \pm 0.024\).
* Perturbative QCD correction to the Gottfried sum is about 0.3% at \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This review extends the earlier experimental indications of Gottfried sum-rule violation from SLAC (1975), EMC (1983, 1987), and BCDMS (1990), and was triggered by the definitive NMC result (1991, 1994). It surveys theoretical models proposed to explain the \(\bar u-\bar d\) asymmetry, including meson-cloud models (e.g., the Adelaide model, the Julich group), chiral soliton models, Pauli blocking, and diquark models, and discusses how the Fermilab-E866 Drell-Yan experiment and other processes can distinguish among them. The work enables future experimental tests of the flavor asymmetry and its relation to nucleon spin and nuclear effects.
