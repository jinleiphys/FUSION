# Nucleon Form Factors in a Light-Cone Quark Model with Two-Photon Exchange

> We estimate the two-photon exchange corrections to both proton and neutron electromagnetic physical observables in a relativistic light cone quark model. At a fixed momentum transfer the corrections are found to be small in magnitudes, but with a strong angular dependence. Our results are comparable to those from simple hadronic model in a moderate momentum transfer region.

## Key claim

The two-photon exchange (TPE) corrections to nucleon electromagnetic observables are small in magnitude but have a strong angular dependence; however, the authors cannot fully separate all TPE contributions to the form factors, so they adopt the assumption that the TPE correction to the electric form factor is zero, making their results a rough estimate rather than a complete extraction.

## Method

The authors compute TPE corrections in a relativistic light-cone constituent quark model, first evaluating the TPE amplitude at the quark level with massive quarks (mass 0.22 GeV) and then embedding it into the nucleon via a three-quark light-cone wave function with a Gaussian form. The soft (infrared-divergent) part of the TPE amplitude is treated separately using a simple hadronic model with a nucleon intermediate state, while the hard part is evaluated from the quark-level matrix elements. The TPE corrections to the unpolarized differential cross section and to the recoil polarization observables \(P_x\) and \(P_z\) are then calculated under the assumption \(\Delta G_E = 0\).

## Key numbers

- At \(Q^2 = 1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\), the TPE correction to the proton unpolarized differential cross section is about \(1.3\%\); at \(Q^2 = 3\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) it is nearly \(2\%\).
- For the neutron, the TPE correction to the unpolarized differential cross section is about \(0.3\%\) at \(Q^2 = 1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) and about \(0.2\%\) at \(Q^2 = 3\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).
- For the proton polarized observable \(P_x\) at \(Q^2 = 1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\), the TPE correction is about \(0.5\%\) of the one-photon-exchange value; for the neutron, the correction is about \(5\%\).
- For the proton polarized observable \(P_z\) at \(Q^2 = 1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\), the TPE correction is about \(1\%\); for the neutron, it is about \(0.5\%\).
- The quark mass is set to \(m_q = 0.22\ \mathrm{GeV}\) and the Gaussian wave-function parameter to \(\beta = 0.55\ \mathrm{GeV}\), taken from prior work (PRD-2201, PLB-86).

## Context

This work extends the study of TPE corrections from the simple hadronic model (e.g., Mo and Tsai (1969), and later calculations including nucleon and \(\Delta(1232)\) intermediate states by Blunden et al. (2003, 2005)) and from the parton model (e.g., Chen et al. (2004)) into the medium-\(Q^2\) region using a light-cone constituent quark model for the first time. The results are comparable to the hadronic model for the proton but yield smaller corrections for the neutron; the authors note that their inability to fully separate all TPE form factors and the omission of nucleon resonances in the hadronic model leave uncertainties. This work enables future studies using a di-quark model within the light-cone formalism to better separate the TPE contributions.
