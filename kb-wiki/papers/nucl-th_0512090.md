# Evidence for two Lambda(1405) resonance states

> The $K^- p \to 蟺^0 蟺^0 危^0$ reaction is studied within a chiral unitary model. The distribution of $蟺^0 危^0$ states forming the $螞(1405)$ shows, in agreement with a recent experiment, a peak at 1420 MeV and a relatively narrow width of $螕= 38$ MeV. We use these data in combination with those of the $蟺^- p \to K^0 蟺危$ reaction and elements of chiral unitary theory to prove that there are two $螞(1405)$ states instead of one as so far assumed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance actually consists of two distinct resonance states, not one, and that the $K^- p \to \pi^0 \pi^0 \Sigma^0$ reaction provides the first clear evidence for this two-pole nature by preferentially populating the second, narrower state.

## Method

The authors model the $K^- p \to \pi^0 \pi^0 \Sigma^0$ reaction using a chiral unitary approach, considering a nucleon pole term where one pion is emitted before the $K^- p \to \pi^0 \Sigma^0$ amplitude forms the $\Lambda(1405)$. They calculate the $\pi^0 \Sigma^0$ invariant mass distribution for kaon laboratory momenta between 514 and 750 MeV/c, implementing symmetrization for the two indistinguishable pions, and compare their results to experimental data from Prakhov (2005).

## Key numbers

- The $\pi^0 \Sigma^0$ invariant mass distribution shows a peak at 1420 MeV, with a width of $\Gamma = 38$ MeV for the pure resonant part after background removal.
- The two predicted poles have masses around 1390 MeV (width ~130 MeV, coupling mostly to $\pi \Sigma$) and around 1425 MeV (width ~30 MeV, coupling mostly to $\bar{K} N$).
- The peak appears at $M_I^2 = 2.02$ GeV$^2$, corresponding to $M_I = 1420$ MeV, larger than the nominal $\Lambda(1405)$ mass.
- The apparent experimental width is about 40-45 MeV, while the nominal $\Lambda(1405)$ width from the Particle Data Group is $50 \pm 2$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary predictions of two $\Lambda(1405)$ poles from earlier theoretical studies by Oller (2000), Jido (2002), and Garcia-Recio (2002), which had not been experimentally confirmed. The authors combine their analysis of the $K^- p \to \pi^0 \pi^0 \Sigma^0$ reaction with the $\pi^- p \to K^0 \pi \Sigma$ reaction studied by Hyodo (2003), showing that the different shapes observed in these two reactions can only be explained by the existence of two distinct resonance states. This enables future experiments to distinguish the two poles by selecting reactions that preferentially populate either the $\bar{K} N$ or $\pi \Sigma$ coupled channels.
