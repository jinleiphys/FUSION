# The Nijmegen Potentials

> A review is given of the various Nijmegen potentials. Special attention is given to some of the newest developments, such as the extended soft-core model, the high-quality potentials, and the Nijmegen optical potentials for NN.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the Nijmegen group's program of constructing nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials, emphasizing that the "high-quality" (HQ) potentials achieve a \(\chi^2/N_d < 1.05\) when compared directly with experimental data, but that even these potentials require an imaginary (optical) component to describe inelasticities above pion-production thresholds, and that the resulting optical potentials still yield \(\chi^2/\text{# data} \simeq 2.4\) to 2.45 for np data below 500 MeV, significantly above the minimum from partial-wave analysis.

## Method

The Nijmegen group constructed several classes of NN potentials: hard-core models (Nijm D, Nijm F), soft-core models (Nijm78, Nijm93), extended soft-core models (ESC) incorporating two-meson exchange and pair diagrams, and high-quality potentials (Nijm I, Nijm II, Reid93) fitted to partial-wave analyses of pp and np scattering data. Optical potentials were then formed by adding an imaginary part to the real HQ potentials, using the same imaginary part as in the group's partial-wave analysis of np data below 500 MeV. The potentials were tested against the Nijmegen NN database and compared with deuteron parameters extracted from scattering data.

## Key numbers

- The high-quality potentials Nijm I, Nijm II, and Reid93 each achieve \(\chi^2/N_d = 1.03\) when compared directly with experimental data.
- The best experimental deuteron d/s ratio is \(\eta = 0.0252(1)\).
- The effective range at the deuteron pole is \(\rho(-\varepsilon,-\varepsilon) = 1.764(3)\) fm.
- The deuteron d-state probability is \(p_d = 5.7(1)\%\).
- The uncorrected electric quadrupole moment is \(Q_0 = 0.271(1)\) fm\(^2\), compared to the experimental value \(Q = 0.2859(3)\) fm\(^2\).
- For the Nijm D hard-core potential (circa 1975), the \(\pi\)NN coupling constant was \(f^2/4\pi = 0.074\), compared to the present best value \(f^2/4\pi = 0.0748\).
- The Nijm D deuteron d/s ratio was \(\eta = 0.0251\), and the effective range at the deuteron pole was \(\rho(-\varepsilon,-\varepsilon) = 1.776\) fm.
- The Nijm D d-state probability was \(p_d = 5.9\%\), and the uncorrected electric quadrupole moment was \(Q_0 = 0.272\) fm\(^2\).
- For the extended soft-core model (ESC), a preliminary fit achieved \(\chi^{2}_{p.d.p.} = 1.08\).
- In the partial-wave analysis of np data, using a purely real potential works well up to \(T_L = 400\) MeV, works well up to \(T_L = 450\) MeV, and works reasonably up to \(T_L = 500\) MeV; the increase in \(\chi^2\) when omitting the imaginary part is 880 for the full 0–500 MeV interval (3988 data points).
- The optical Nijm I and Nijm II potentials give \(\chi^2/\text{# data} \simeq 2.4\) to 2.45 for np data below 500 MeV.

## Context

This review summarizes the Nijmegen group's decades-long program of constructing baryon-baryon potentials, extending earlier hard-core models (Nijm D, Nijm F) and soft-core models (Nijm78) to high-quality potentials that fit NN scattering data with \(\chi^2/N_d\) near unity. The work builds on the group's own partial-wave analyses and enables future construction of optical potentials valid up to 1 GeV, as well as extensions to hyperon-nucleon (YN) and antibaryon-baryon channels using SU(3) symmetry. The paper contrasts the Nijmegen potentials with the older Reid potential (updated as Reid93) and notes that the low \(\pi\)NN coupling constant found in Nijm D (1975) was not taken seriously at the time due to prevailing beliefs from the \(\pi\)N community.
