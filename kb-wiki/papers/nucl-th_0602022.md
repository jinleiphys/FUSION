# Magic Numbers from New Systematics

> A new systematics from the separation energy of deuteron is used to examine the magicity of stable as well as nuclei towards dripline.

# Magic Numbers from New Systematics

## Key claim
The deuteron separation energy (S_d) plotted against neutron or proton numbers can serve as a new systematics for identifying magic numbers, including both well-established and newly proposed magicities near the dripline. The method relies on breaks in the systematic trend of S_d, with a specific prescription: if S_d at an odd neutron number N falls below the line joining the N-2 and N-4 nuclei, then the isotope with N-1 is magic or extra stable; if S_d is above the line, magicity is quenched.

## Method
The author calculated deuteron separation energies using experimental binding energies from the mass table of Audi and Wapstra (2003), defined as S_d(N,Z) = B(N,Z) - B(N-1,Z-1) - B_d, where B_d is the deuteron binding energy. These S_d values were plotted as a function of neutron number at fixed Z values and as a function of proton number at fixed N values, and breaks in the systematic trend were identified as indicators of magicity. For cases where the breaks were not striking, the author applied the prescription of comparing S_d at odd N to the line connecting the N-2 and N-4 nuclei.

## Key numbers
- N=2 (Z=4), N=8 (Z=8), N=20, 28 (Z=20), N=50, 82 (Z=50), and N=126 (Z=82) are clearly indicated by the new systematics as well-known neutron magic numbers.
- Z=8 (N=8, 16), Z=20 (N=24), Z=50 (N=65), Z=82 (N=102, 126) are clearly visible as well-known proton magic numbers.
- N=20 magicity vanishes at Z=10-12 only and is present at Z=13; this disagrees with prior work that found it vanishes at Z=11-14, 18, 22-24.
- N=28 magicity is absent at Z=16, 17, 18, and 26, but present at Z=25.
- Z=8 proton magicity is quenched at N=11, disagreeing with the Q_beta- systematics that observed it at N=11, 13.
- N=16 magicity is observed for Z=9, 10, consistent with the region T_z >= 3 (Z <= 10) predicted by Ozawa et al. (2000).
- N=30 magicity is observed at Z=26 only.
- Z=14 magicity is observed at N=16 and N=18, in the range N=13-19 predicted by one prior work.
- Z=16 magicity is observed at N=22-27 but absent at N=21.
- N=26 extra stability is observed for Z=13-17 (Al to Cl isotopes), consistent with a pseudo shell closure reported from mass measurements at GANIL and Dubna.

## Context
This work extends the use of separation energy systematics for identifying magic numbers, which has previously been done using one- and two-neutron or proton separation energies, Q-values for beta decay, excitation energies of the first excited state, and alpha particle energies. The author compares results with prior studies: Ozawa et al. (2000) who identified N=16 as a magic number from 1-neutron separation energy breaks; another work that found N=16 magicity only at Z=7,8 from 1-neutron separation energy plots; and Q_beta- systematics that identified various new magic numbers. The new systematics using deuteron separation energy shows both agreements and disagreements with these prior approaches, and the author suggests that the present systematics should be considered alongside other systematics for a complete picture. The observation of N=26 extra stability for Al to Cl isotopes enables further investigation of this pseudo shell closure.
