# Dihadron fragmentation framework for near-side energy-energy correlators

> We establish an approach to analyze the free hadron and transition (nonperturbative) regions of near-side energy-energy correlators (EECs) based on dihadron fragmentation functions (DiFFs). We introduce a (nonperturbative) function we call the "EEC-DiFF" and explicitly show that expanding it for large relative transverse momentum between the two hadrons gives the $O(α_s)$ expression for the "EEC jet" function used in the quark/gluon (perturbative) region. This connection indicates that a formal theoretical matching will be able to bridge the free hadron, transition, and quark/gluon regions and allow all of them to be analyzed simultaneously. We further derive a result valid for near-side EECs in the free hadron and transition regions of $e^+e^-$ annihilation in terms of the EEC-DiFF. Using a simple model for the function, we perform the first fit within the dihadron framework to experimental data in this regime. We find reasonable agreement with the measurements and reproduce the salient features of near-side EECs in the free hadron and transition regions.

## Key claim
The paper establishes a dihadron fragmentation function (DiFF) framework for analyzing the free hadron and transition regions of near-side energy-energy correlators (EECs), and shows that expanding the newly introduced "EEC-DiFF" for large relative transverse momentum reproduces the perturbative EEC jet function at O(α_s), suggesting a formal matching can bridge all three regions (free hadron, transition, and quark/gluon) simultaneously.

## Method
The authors define a nonperturbative "EEC-DiFF" function D^i(z_χ, Q^2) by integrating the dihadron fragmentation function D_1^{h1h2/i}(ξ_1, ξ_2, R_T) over momentum fractions and relative transverse momentum with a delta function constraint. They derive a theoretical expression for EEC(χ) in e+e- annihilation in terms of the EEC-DiFF and hard factors, then perform a leading-order fit using a simple Gaussian-based model with three free parameters to experimental data from TASSO, MAC, MARKII, TOPAZ, and OPAL collaborations, using Bayesian Monte Carlo with data resampling and scale variation.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical understanding of near-side EECs beyond the perturbative quark/gluon region studied by Dixon et al. (2019) and others, into the nonperturbative free hadron and transition regimes that have recently received increased attention (Schindler et al. 2023, Lee et al. 2024, Chen et al. 2024, Kang et al. 2024, Barata et al. 2024, Liu et al. 2024). The DiFF framework provides a natural explanation for the Q-dependence and scaling behaviors of near-side EECs observed by CMS (2024) and ALICE (2024), and enables future matching between all three angular regions. The approach also opens avenues for studying azimuthal/spin-dependent near-side EEC observables in e+e-, lepton-nucleon, and proton-proton collisions, building on the transverse spin physics work of Radici et al. (2015, 2016) and Cocuzza et al. (2023).
