# The Non-perturbative term of the Pseudovector Interaction in the Pion-Nucleon System and the Pion Form Factor

> The correction of the higher-order process for the photon-pion-pion vertex is calculated by the field theoretical method of the pion-nucleon system. The non-perturbative term of the pseudovector coupling interaction is included to examine the effect of the self-energy.

# Wiki Page: Non-perturbative Pseudovector Interaction in Pion-Nucleon System and Pion Form Factor

## Key claim
The non-perturbative term of the pseudovector coupling interaction modifies the pion form factor through a self-energy parameter \(c\), but the resulting charge radius of 1.06 fm is larger than the experimental value of 0.672 ± 0.008 fm, indicating that additional interaction effects are needed to correctly construct the \(q^2\) dependence.

## Method
The photon-pion-pion vertex correction was calculated using field-theoretical methods with a loop diagram containing three nucleon propagators and an anomalous interaction term. The non-perturbative term from the pseudovector coupling was incorporated by modifying the pion-nucleon-nucleon vertex with a self-energy expansion \(\sigma(a,b)\) approximated to lowest order, and the loop integral was regularized using a cut-off parameter \(\Lambda\) rather than dimensional regularization.

## Key numbers
- Charge radius of pion: \(\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle} = 1.06\) fm (calculated), compared to experimental value \(0.672 \pm 0.008\) fm from PDG.
- Self-energy parameter \(c\) has two expected regions: \(c \sim 2\) (for electromagnetic properties of nucleon, beta decay, intermediate-energy pion-nucleon scattering) and \(c \sim -0.4\) (for low-energy pion-nucleon elastic scattering and photoproduction below resonance).
- With \(c = -0.39\) and the divergent term turned off, the charge radius decreases by about 10% but remains larger than experiment.
- The cut-off parameter \(\Lambda\) is roughly of order the nucleon mass \(M\).
- The ratio \(m^2/M^2 \sim 0.02\) justifies setting the pion mass to zero in certain terms.

## Context
This work extends the author's prior calculation of the pion-nucleon-nucleon vertex using matrix inversion under phase-shift parameters for pion-nucleon elastic scattering (Kinpara). It applies the same non-perturbative framework to the photon-pion-pion vertex and the pion form factor, which is needed for electron-pion elastic scattering. The results show that the current approximation using free nucleon propagators and a lowest-order non-perturbative term requires improvement, possibly through exact nucleon propagators or modified anomalous interactions, to achieve cut-off-independent results consistent with experiment.
