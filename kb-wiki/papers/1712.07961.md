# Mass sensitivity of the three-flavor chiral phase transition

> The mass sensitivity of the chiral phase transition of QCD with and without axial $U_A(1)$-symmetry breaking at vanishing and finite quark chemical potential is investigated. To focus on the low-energy sector of QCD, a quark-meson model with three dynamical quark flavors is employed. Non-perturbative quantum fluctuations are taken into account with the functional renormalization group. The inherent ambiguities in fixing the low-energy model parameters away from the physical mass point and their consequences for spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking are discussed in detail and a heuristic parameter fixing scheme motivated by chiral perturbation theory is proposed. The influence of vacuum and thermal fluctuations of quarks and mesons on the order of the chiral phase transition is additionally assessed with a mean-field analysis.

## Key claim
The order of the chiral phase transition in the three-flavor quark-meson model depends sensitively on the parameter-fixing scheme used away from the physical mass point; the fixed-UV scheme and the fixed-\(f_\pi\) scheme produce qualitatively different Columbia plots, and the inclusion of the axial \(U_A(1)\) anomaly can change the transition order only when combined with a specific parameter choice.

## Method
The authors employed a three-flavor quark-meson model with a functional renormalization group (FRG) in the local potential approximation (LPA) to compute the scale-dependent effective potential on a two-dimensional grid in the chiral invariants \(\rho_1\) and \(\rho_2\). They also performed an extended mean-field approximation (eMFA) by switching off mesonic fluctuations in the FRG flow, thereby including only quark vacuum and thermal fluctuations. The initial UV parameters were fixed at \(\Lambda = 700\) MeV to reproduce physical pion and kaon decay constants and masses at the physical point, and then varied away from the physical point using two distinct schemes: fixed-UV (only the symmetry-breaking sources \(j_{l,s}\) change) and fixed-\(f_\pi\) (the initial action is adjusted to keep \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV at every mass point).

## Key numbers
- The UV cutoff scale is \(\Lambda = 700\) MeV, with the scale of chiral symmetry breaking around \(k_{\chi\text{SB}} \approx 500\) MeV.
- At the physical point, the FRG calculation yields \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV, \(f_K = 113\) MeV, \(m_\pi = 138\) MeV, \(m_K = 496\) MeV, and \(m_l = 300\) MeV.
- With the axial anomaly (\(c_A = 4807.84\) MeV), the FRG gives \(m_\sigma = 530\) MeV and \(\sqrt{m_{\eta'}^2 + m_\eta^2} = 1103.6\) MeV; without the anomaly (\(c_A = 0\)), the corresponding values are \(m_\sigma = 500\) MeV and \(\sqrt{m_{\eta'}^2 + m_\eta^2} = 649.6\) MeV.
- The explicit symmetry-breaking sources at the physical point are \(j_l^{(\text{phys})} = 120.73^3\) MeV\(^3\) and \(j_s^{(\text{phys})} = 336.41^3\) MeV\(^3\).

## Context
This work extends previous FRG studies of the three-flavor quark-meson model by Mitter and Schaefer (2013) and Rennecke (2016) by computing the full Columbia plot at vanishing and finite chemical potential for the first time with non-perturbative FRG methods. It addresses longstanding lattice QCD controversies about the size of the first-order region near the three-flavor chiral limit, where results from groups such as Karsch (2000, 2001, 2003) and de Forcrand (2007) show large discrepancies. The study also contrasts with earlier mean-field NJL model results by Fukushima (2008) and with CJT formalism studies by Lenaghan (2000), showing that the parameter-fixing ambiguity away from the physical point is a critical source of uncertainty that must be resolved before reliable predictions for the QCD phase diagram can be made.
