# Scheming in Dimensional Regularization

> We consider the most general loop integral that appears in non-relativistic effective field theories with no light particles. The divergences of this integral are in correspondence with simple poles in the space of complex space-time dimensions. Integrals related to the original integral by subtraction of one or more poles in dimensions other than D=4 lead to nonminimal subtraction schemes. Subtraction of all poles in correspondence with ultraviolet divergences of the loop integral leads naturally to a regularization scheme which is precisely equivalent to cutoff regularization. We therefore recover cutoff regularization from dimensional regularization with a nonminimal subtraction scheme. We then discuss the power-counting for non-relativistic effective field theories which arises in these alternative schemes.

## Key claim

The paper shows that dimensional regularization with a nonminimal subtraction scheme that removes all poles corresponding to ultraviolet divergences is precisely equivalent to cutoff regularization, but that the simpler Power-Law Divergence Subtraction (PDS) scheme, which only subtracts the pole at D=3, is the simplest definition that yields good power-counting for coefficients in non-relativistic effective field theories with an unnaturally large scattering length.

## Method

The authors analyze the most general divergent loop integral \(I_n(\mu)\) that appears in non-relativistic effective field theories of two identical heavy particles, regularized using dimensional regularization. They identify the poles of this integral in the complex plane of space-time dimensions \(D\), and define alternative subtraction schemes by canceling different sets of these poles. They then solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the on-shell T-matrix in each scheme and match to the effective range expansion to deduce the scaling behavior of the low-energy coefficients \(C_{2n}\) in the Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

* The most general divergent loop integral in \(D=4\) with a sharp cutoff \(\beta\) yields a real part with power-law divergences up to \(\beta^{2n+1}/(2n+1)\), as shown in Eq. (7).
* In dimensional regularization, the integral \(\tilde{I}_n(\mu)\) in minimal subtraction at \(D=4\) gives \(\tilde{I}_n(\mu) = -p^{2n} (i M p)/(4\pi)\), with no real part (Eq. 18).
* In the PDS scheme, the integral becomes \(\tilde{I}_n^{\rm{PDS}} = -p^{2n} (M/(4\pi))(ip + \mu/\pi)\) (Eq. 30).
* The PDS scaling for coefficients in an unnatural theory (large scattering length) is \(C_0 \sim 1/(M\mu)\) and \(C_{2n} \sim 1/(M \mu^{n+1} \Lambda_0^n)\) (Eq. 33), where \(\Lambda_0\) is the natural scale.
* In a natural theory (\(1/a \sim \Lambda_0\)), the PDS scheme yields \(C_{2n} \sim 1/(M \Lambda_0^{2n+1})\) (Eq. 34).

## Context

This work extends the PDS regularization scheme introduced by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) for non-relativistic effective field theories with large scattering lengths, and clarifies its relationship to cutoff regularization and minimal subtraction. The authors show that while many subtraction schemes exist, only PDS provides a consistent power-counting for the bare Lagrangian coefficients in the presence of an unnaturally large scattering length, whereas the "uvPDS" scheme (equivalent to cutoff regularization) introduces spurious energy dependence that spoils the power-counting. This analysis enables practitioners to understand why PDS is the preferred scheme for systems such as nucleon-nucleon scattering, and connects the discussion to renormalization group treatments by Birse (1998) and van Kolck (1998).
