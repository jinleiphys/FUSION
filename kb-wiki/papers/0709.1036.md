# Longitudinal and Transverse Form Factors from $^{12}$C

> Electron scattering form factors from $^{12}$C have been studied in the framework of the particle-hole shell model. Higher configurations are taken into account by allowing particle-hole excitations from the 1$s$ and 1$p$ shells core orbits up to the 1$f-2p$ shell. The inclusion of the higher configurations modifies the form factors markedly and describes the experimental data very well in all momentum transfer regions.

## Key claim

The inclusion of higher-energy configurations (particle-hole excitations from the 1s and 1p shells up to the 1f-2p shell) in a Tamm-Dancoff approximation particle-hole shell model markedly improves the description of electron scattering form factors for T=1 states in 12C, but the calculated amplitudes for several transitions must be scaled by ad hoc factors (ranging from 0.2 to 1.2) to match low-momentum-transfer data, indicating that even this extended space does not fully capture the nuclear structure.

## Method

The author performed particle-hole shell model calculations in the Tamm-Dancoff approximation (TDA) for 12C, using a closed 1s1/2 and 1p3/2 ground state. The residual interaction was taken as the modified surface delta interaction (MSDI) with parameters A0=0.8 MeV, A1=1.0 MeV, B=0.7 MeV, and C=-0.3 MeV. Higher configurations were included by mixing ground state configurations from the 1s1/2 and 1p3/2 shells with those from the 2s1/2 and 2p3/2 shells, and by extending the particle-hole basis to include excitations up to the 1f-2p shell. Form factors were computed using harmonic oscillator wavefunctions with size parameter b=1.64 fm (adjusted for some states) and compared with experimental data for the 1- (18.12 MeV), 21- (19.50 MeV), 22- (22.70 MeV), 3- (18.60 MeV), and 3+ (20.60 MeV) states.

## Key numbers

- Oscillator parameter b = 1.64 fm used for most calculations, chosen to reproduce the elastic form factor and root mean square charge radius.
- For the M2 transition to the 22- state (23.50 MeV), the b value was reduced by 14% (to approximately 1.50 fm).
- Calculated energies for T=1 states: 1- at 18.44 MeV (experimental: 18.12 MeV), 21- at 19.88 MeV (experimental: 19.50 MeV), 22- at 23.50 MeV (experimental: 22.70 MeV), 3- at 18.87 MeV (experimental: 18.60 MeV), 3+ at 27.10 MeV (experimental: 20.60 MeV).
- Amplitude scaling factors applied: 1.3 reduction for the 1- state, 1.2 enhancement for the 21- state, 1.82 reduction for the 22- state, and factor of 5 reduction for the 3+ state.
- Ground state correlation parameters: gamma = 0.95 for 21-, 0.97 for 22-, 1.0 for 3-, and 0.7 for 3+.
- The calculations describe experimental data well for momentum transfer q less than or equal to 3.0 fm-1 for the 21-, 3-, and 3+ states, and for q less than or equal to 1.0 fm-1 for the 1- state.

## Context

This work extends earlier particle-hole model calculations for 12C by Donnelly (1970) and Bennhold et al. (1985), and is consistent with the core-polarization studies of Radhi et al. (2001) and the configuration mixing calculations of Booten et al. (1994). The author's previous work (Majeed, 2006) studied isovector states of 12C using configuration mixing shell model; the present paper extends that approach by including higher-energy configurations up to the 1f-2p shell. The results enable improved predictions for electron scattering form factors at higher momentum transfer, though the need for large amplitude scaling factors (especially factor 5 for the 3+ state) suggests that 2p-2h excitations or other higher-order effects remain important for a complete description.
