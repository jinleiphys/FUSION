# Secondary Neutron Production from Thick Target Deuteron Breakup

> Thick target deuteron breakup is a variable-energy accelerator-based source of high-energy neutrons, with applications in fundamental and applied nuclear science and engineering. However, the breakup mechanism remains poorly understood, and data on neutron yields from thick target breakup remains relatively scarce. In this work, the double-differential neutron yields from deuteron breakup have been measured on a thick beryllium target at $ε_d=33$ and 40 MeV, using both time-of-flight and activation techniques. We have also introduced a simple hybrid model for the double-differential deuteron breakup cross section, applicable in the $ε_d=10$--$100$ MeV energy range on light ($Z\leq 6$) targets. This model features four empirical parameters that have been fit to reproduce experimental breakup measurements on beryllium targets, using the method of least-squares. It was shown that these parameters extrapolate well to higher energies, and to other low-Z target materials. We also include optimization of the parameters that modify the Kalbach systematics for compound and pre-equilibrium reactions, in order to better reproduce the experimental data for beryllium targets at large angles.

## Key claim

The paper presents a simple hybrid model for double-differential deuteron breakup cross sections on light targets (Z ≤ 6) in the 10–100 MeV range, but the model's four empirical parameters were fit specifically to beryllium target data and are shown to extrapolate well to higher energies and other low-Z materials, not universally to all targets.

## Method

The authors measured double-differential neutron yields from deuteron breakup on a thick beryllium target at 33 and 40 MeV using time-of-flight and activation techniques. They developed a parameterized hybrid model combining a modified Serber stripping theory for the breakup component with semi-empirical Kalbach systematics for compound and pre-equilibrium reactions, fitting six scaling parameters to literature data from Meulders (1975), Saltmarsh (1977), and Harrig (2018) using least-squares optimization.

## Key numbers

- Approximately 9% of a 40 MeV deuteron beam on beryllium breaks up and is converted into a forward-focused neutron beam
- The model is applicable in the deuteron energy range ε_d = 10–100 MeV on light targets with Z ≤ 6
- The deuteron binding energy E_B = 2.225 MeV is used in the width parameter w_d = 0.37·E_B for the breakup energy distribution
- At 50 MeV incident deuteron energy, approximately 20% of the deuteron beam is attenuated in a thick beryllium target
- The compound nuclear temperature parameter is kT_CM = 0.1 + 0.27·√(ε_d + Q) MeV, where Q = 4.36 MeV for ⁹Be(d,n)
- The pre-equilibrium temperature parameter is kT_PE = 0.75 + 0.63·√(ε_d + Q) MeV

## Context

This work extends the limited experimental data on thick target deuteron breakup neutron yields, which were mostly measured in the 1970s for cancer therapy applications (Meulders 1975, Saltmarsh 1977) and more recently by Harrig (2018) at 16 MeV. The hybrid model addresses the poor agreement of existing semi-empirical models (Kalbach 2017, Avrigeanu 2017) with light-target neutron yield data, which were primarily fit to (d,p) reaction data on medium-mass nuclides. The results enable improved neutron source characterization for isotope production, nuclear physics measurements using time-of-flight techniques, and fusion reactor material damage studies.
