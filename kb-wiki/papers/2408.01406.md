# Hyperons during proto-neutron star deleptonization and the emission of dark flavoured particles

> Complementary to high-energy experimental efforts, indirect astrophysical searches of particles beyond the standard model have long been pursued. The present article follows the latter approach and considers, for the first time, the self-consistent treatment of the energy losses from dark flavoured particles produced in the decay of hyperons during a core-collapse supernova (CCSN). To this end, general relativistic supernova simulations in spherical symmetry are performed, featuring six-species Boltzmann neutrino transport, and covering the long-term evolution of the nascent remnant proto-neutron star (PNS) deleptonization for several tens of seconds. A well-calibrated hyperon equation of state (EOS) is therefore implemented into the supernova simulations and tested against the corresponding nucleonic model. It is found that supernova observables, such as the neutrino signal, are robustly insensitive to the appearance of hyperons for the simulation times considered in the present study. The presence of hyperons enables an additional channel for the appearance of dark sector particles, which is considered at the level of the $Λ$ hyperon decay. Assuming massless particles that escape the PNS after being produced, these channels expedite the deleptonizing PNS and the cooling behaviour. This, in turn, shortens the neutrino emission timescale. The present study confirms the previously estimated upper limits on the corresponding branching ratios for low and high mass PNS, by effectively reducing the neutrino emission timescale by a factor of two. This is consistent with the classical argument deduced from the neutrino detection associated with SN1987A.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that while hyperons themselves have negligible impact on supernova observables such as the neutrino signal during the first tens of seconds of proto-neutron star evolution, their presence enables a new channel for dark flavored particle production via Lambda hyperon decay that can shorten the neutrino emission timescale by a factor of two, consistent with upper limits previously estimated from SN1987A.

## Method

The authors performed general relativistic spherically symmetric core-collapse supernova simulations using the AGILE-BOLTZTRAN code with six-species Boltzmann neutrino transport, implementing the FSU2H* relativistic mean-field equation of state both with and without hyperons (Lambda, Sigma-, Xi-). They evolved two progenitor models of 18 and 25 solar masses through the proto-neutron star deleptonization phase for several tens of seconds, and introduced dark flavored particles as massless particles produced in Lambda hyperon decay that escape the proto-neutron star without interaction.

## Key numbers

- Central densities in hyperon simulations reach significantly higher values than nucleon-only simulations, with Lambda abundances of Y_Lambda approximately 0.001-0.025 for the 18 solar mass model and 0.01-0.07 for the 25 solar mass model
- Proto-neutron star gravitational masses at about 10 seconds post bounce: 1.435 solar masses (nucleons) vs 1.450 solar masses (hyperons) for the 18 solar mass progenitor; 1.792 solar masses (nucleons) vs 1.791 solar masses (hyperons) for the 25 solar mass progenitor
- Proto-neutron star radii at about 10 seconds post bounce: 14.18 km (nucleons) vs 14.09 km (hyperons) for the 18 solar mass model; 14.84 km (nucleons) vs 14.45 km (hyperons) for the 25 solar mass model
- The dark flavored particle emission shortens the neutrino emission timescale by a factor of two, confirming previously estimated upper limits on branching ratios for both low and high mass proto-neutron stars

## Context

This work extends previous studies of exotic cooling in core-collapse supernovae that considered axions from nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung (Turner 1987, Mayle 1987, Burrows 1988) and pions (Carenza 2020, Fischer 2021), as well as earlier hyperon studies by Martin Camalich (2020) and Alonso-Alvarez (2021) that used spherically symmetric simulations without exotic cooling and conventional equations of state without hyperons. The self-consistent implementation of hyperons in supernova simulations overcomes previous limitations by studying the feedback of dark boson production on supernova dynamics and neutrino emission, enabling more robust constraints on dark sector models from the SN1987A neutrino signal.
