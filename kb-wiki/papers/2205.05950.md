# Towards the discovery of novel $B_c$ states: radiative and hadronic transitions

> The properties of the $B_c$-meson family ($c\bar b$) are still not well determined experimentally because the specific mechanisms of formation and decay remain poorly understood. Unlike heavy quarkonia, i.e. the hidden heavy quark-antiquark sectors of charmonium ($c\bar c$) and bottomonium ($b\bar b$), the $B_c$-mesons cannot annihilate into gluons and they are, consequently, more stable. The excited $B_c$ states, lying below the lowest strong-decay $BD$-threshold, can only undergo through radiative decays and hadronic transitions to the $B_c$ ground state, which then decays weakly. As a result of this, a rich spectrum of narrow excited states below the $BD$-threshold appear, whose total widths are two orders of magnitude smaller than those of the excited levels of charmonium and bottomonium. In a different article, we determined bottom-charmed meson masses using a non-relativistic constituent quark model which has been applied to a wide range of hadron physical observables, and thus the model parameters are completely constrained. Herein, continuing to our study of the $B_c$ sector, we calculate the relevant radiative decay widths and hadronic transition rates between $c\bar b$ states which are below $BD$-threshold. This shall provide the most promising signals for discovering excited $B_c$ states that are below the lowest strong-decay $BD$-threshold. Finally, our results are compared with other models to measure the reliability of the predictions and point out differences.

## Key claim
The paper calculates radiative decay widths and hadronic transition rates for excited $B_c$ meson states lying below the lowest strong-decay BD-threshold, using a non-relativistic constituent quark model whose parameters were previously fixed by global fits to hadron observables. The authors claim these predictions provide the most promising signals for discovering novel excited $B_c$ states, but note that the hadronic transition model loses predictive power for spin-flip transitions unless a duality argument between physical light hadrons and two-gluon final states is invoked.

## Method
The authors used a non-relativistic constituent quark model with one-gluon exchange and screened confinement potentials, solving the Schrodinger equation via the Gaussian Expansion Method. Radiative decay widths were computed using standard E1 and M1 multipole formulae adapted to the quark model wave functions. Hadronic transitions were calculated using the QCD multipole expansion (QCDME) approach, where intermediate color-octet hybrid meson states were modeled via an extension of the Buchmuller-Tye quark-confining string model, with the gluon-to-light-hadron conversion treated either through phenomenological constants (C1, C2, C3) fixed from bottomonium studies or via a duality argument between physical light hadrons and two-gluon final states.

## Key numbers
- The ATLAS Collaboration observed a peak at 6842 ± 4 ± 5 MeV/c², interpreted as either the Bc*(2³S₁) excited state or an unresolved pair of peaks from Bc(2¹S₀) → Bc(1¹S₀)π⁺π⁻ and Bc*(2³S₁) → Bc*(1³S₁)π⁺π⁻ followed by Bc(1³S₁) → Bc(1¹S₀)γ.
- Quark model parameters include: charm quark mass m_c = 1763 MeV, bottom quark mass m_b = 5110 MeV, OGE coupling α₀ = 2.118, confinement strength a_c = 507.4 MeV, and screening parameter μ_c = 0.576 fm⁻¹.
- Hybrid meson masses in the c-bbar sector range from 7328 MeV (K=1, L=0) up to 8588 MeV (K=8, L=1), with a threshold at 8595 MeV; the variation of parameter α_n between 1 and √2 modifies energies by up to 30 MeV, with α_n = √1.5 used.
- The total widths of excited Bc states below BD-threshold are two orders of magnitude smaller than those of excited charmonium and bottomonium levels.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of the Bc spectrum (Ortega et al., 2020) which examined the influence of two-meson thresholds on Bc states, finding dynamically generated states near the DB* and D*B* thresholds. The calculations use the same non-relativistic constituent quark model previously applied to charmonium and bottomonium sectors (Segovia et al., 2008-2016), and the hadronic transition formalism follows the QCD multipole expansion approach reviewed by Kuang (2006). The results are compared with other theoretical frameworks including relativistic quark models (Godfrey, 1985; Ebert, 2002), QCD sum rules (Wang, 2012), effective field theories (Brambilla, 2000; Peset, 2018), and lattice QCD (Allison, 2004; Dowdall, 2012). The predictions enable experimental searches for excited Bc states at LHCb, CMS, and future facilities, particularly through radiative decays and ππ hadronic transitions to the Bc ground state.
