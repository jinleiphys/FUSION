# Solution of the Hyperon Puzzle within a Relativistic Mean-Field Model

> The equation of state of cold baryonic matter is studied within a relativistic mean-field model with hadron masses and coupling constants depending on the scalar field. All hadron masses undergo a universal scaling, whereas the coupling constants are scaled differently. The appearance of hyperons in dense neutron star interiors is accounted for, however the equation of state remains sufficiently stiff if a reduction of the $ϕ$ meson mass is included. Our equation of state matches well the constraints known from analyses of the astrophysical data and the particle production in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the "hyperon puzzle" (the softening of the neutron star equation of state and reduction of maximum mass when hyperons are included) can be resolved within a relativistic mean-field model if a reduction of the phi meson mass is included, and additionally if the sigma-hyperon coupling constants are allowed to scale with density. The hedge is that the solution depends on specific choices of scaling functions and that the model with only phi-meson mass scaling (MKVORHphi) does not satisfy the "strong" direct Urca constraint (M_DU > 1.5 M_sun), though the authors argue this constraint may be softened for hyperon reactions.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic mean-field model where hadron masses and meson-baryon coupling constants depend on the scalar mean field through scaling functions. They include nucleons (p,n) and hyperons (Lambda, Sigma, Xi) interacting with sigma, omega, rho, and phi meson mean fields, with hyperon-vector-meson couplings determined by SU(6) symmetry relations and scalar couplings constrained by hyperon binding energies in isospin-symmetric matter at saturation density. The equation of state for cold baryonic matter in beta-equilibrium is computed, and neutron star configurations are obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation, matched to the BPS crust equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass for KVORH model (with hyperons, no phi scaling): M_max = 1.66 M_sun
- Maximum neutron star mass for MKVORHphi model (with hyperons and phi-meson mass scaling): M_max = 2.22 M_sun
- Maximum neutron star mass for MKVORHphi sigma model (with hyperons, phi scaling, and sigma-hyperon coupling scaling): M_max = 2.29 M_sun
- Critical density for first hyperon appearance in KVORH model: n_DU^Lambda = 2.82 n_0, corresponding to star mass M_DU^Lambda = 1.38 M_sun
- Critical density for first hyperon appearance in MKVORHphi model: n_DU^Lambda = 2.63 n_0, corresponding to star mass M_DU^Lambda = 1.43 M_sun
- Saturation density for both KVOR and MKVOR models: n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation: m_N^*(n_0) = 0.805 m_N (KVOR), 0.73 m_N (MKVOR)
- Symmetry energy at saturation: J_tilde = 32 MeV (KVOR), 30 MeV (MKVOR)
- Symmetry energy slope parameter L = 71 MeV (KVOR), 41 MeV (MKVOR)
- Compressibility modulus K = 275 MeV (KVOR), 240 MeV (MKVOR)
- Maximum NS mass without hyperons: M_max = 2.01 M_sun (KVOR), 2.33 M_sun (MKVOR)
- Neutron star radii for MKVORHphi and MKVORHphi sigma models: 11.7 +/- 0.5 km for star masses M > 0.5 M_sun
- Strangeness concentration in heaviest NS: f_S = 0.034 (KVORH), 0.023 (MKVORHphi), 6.2 x 10^{-3} (MKVORHphi sigma)

## Context

This work extends the KVOR model of Kolomeitsev and Voskresensky (2004) by including hyperons and a phi meson mean field, addressing the "hyperon puzzle" where previous relativistic mean-field models (e.g., Glendenning, Djapo 2008) could not simultaneously produce massive neutron stars above 2 M_sun and include hyperons without violating constraints from heavy-ion collisions (Danielewicz 2002). The approach contrasts with standard RMF models using SU(6) symmetry relations (Weissenborn 2011) and with microscopic calculations like the APR equation of state (Akmal, Pandharipande, Ravenhall 1998) and auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (Gandolfi 2009). The results enable future studies of neutron star cooling with hyperons and provide equations of state that satisfy constraints from the double pulsar J0737-3039 (Podsiadlowski 2005, Kituara 2006) and the massive pulsars PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest 2010) and PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis 2013).
