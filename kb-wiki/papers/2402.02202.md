# Three-body scattering area for particles with infinite or zero scattering length in two dimensions

> We derive the asymptotic expansions of the wave function of three particles having equal mass with finite-range interactions and infinite or zero two-dimensional scattering length colliding at zero energy and zero orbital angular momentum, from which a three-body parameter $D$ is defined. The dimension of $D$ is length squared, and we call $D$ three-body scattering area. We find that the ground state energy per particle of a zero-temperature dilute Bose gas with these interactions is approximately $\frac{\hbar^2 D }{6m}ρ^2$, where $ρ$ is the number density of the bosons, $m$ is the mass of each boson, and $\hbar$ is Planck's constant over $2π$. Such a Bose gas is stable at $D\geq 0$ in the thermodynamic limit, and metastable at $D<0$ in the harmonic trap if the number of bosons is less than $N_{cr}\approx 3.6413 \sqrt{\frac{\hbar}{mω|D|}}$, where $ω$ is the angular frequency of the harmonic trap. If the two-body interaction supports bound states, $D$ typically acquires a negative imaginary part, and we find the relation between this imaginary part and the amplitudes of the pair-boson production processes. We derive a formula for the three-body recombination rate constant of the many-boson system in terms of the imaginary part of $D$.

## Key claim

The paper defines a three-body scattering area \(D\) (dimension length squared) for three identical bosons in two dimensions when the two-body scattering length \(a\) is either infinite or zero, and shows that the ground state energy per particle of a dilute Bose gas with these interactions is approximately \(\frac{\hbar^2 D}{6m}\rho^2\), but only under the condition that the two-body scattering length is exactly zero or infinite, which simplifies the three-body wave function structure.

## Method

The authors derive asymptotic expansions of the zero-energy, zero-orbital-angular-momentum three-body wave function for equal-mass particles with finite-range interactions in two dimensions, using Jacobi coordinates and two-body special functions (s-wave and d-wave) defined via a sequence of equations involving the two-body Hamiltonian. They extract the three-body parameter \(D\) from the leading and subleading terms of these expansions when all three particles are far apart or when one pair is far from the third particle, then apply the result to compute the energy shift of a many-boson system using the Gross-Pitaevskii equation.

## Key numbers

* The three-body parameter \(D\) has dimension length squared, in contrast to the three-dimensional three-body scattering hypervolume whose dimension is length to the fourth power.
* The ground state energy per particle of a zero-temperature dilute Bose gas is approximately \(\frac{\hbar^2 D}{6m}\rho^2\), where \(\rho\) is the number density.
* For a metastable Bose gas with \(D<0\) in a harmonic trap, the critical boson number is \(N_{\mathrm{cr}}\approx 3.6413 \sqrt{\frac{\hbar}{m\omega |D|}}\), where \(\omega\) is the angular frequency of the trap.
* The s-wave scattering phase shift for the case \(a=\infty\) or \(0\) is given by \(\cot\delta_s = \frac{2}{\pi}\left[-\frac{1}{P(k_E)} + \ln\frac{k_E}{k_*}\right]\), with \(P(k_E)=r_{s*}k_E^2 + r'_{s*}k_E^4 + O(k_E^6)\).
* The d-wave scattering phase shift satisfies \(k_E^4 \cot\delta_d = -\frac{1}{a_d} + O(k_E^2)\), where \(a_d\) is the d-wave scattering "length" with dimension length to the fourth power.

## Context

This work extends the concept of the three-body scattering hypervolume, previously defined for three identical bosons in three dimensions by Tan (2008) and Zhu and Tan (2017), to two dimensions under the special conditions of infinite or zero two-body scattering length. It generalizes a two-channel model study by PhysRevA.100.042707 to more general short-range interactions and derives new asymptotic expansions for the wave function when one pair is far from the third particle. The results enable calculation of the three-body recombination rate constant for many-boson systems and provide stability criteria for trapped Bose gases with attractive three-body interactions, analogous to predictions for Bose gases with attractive two-body interactions by Ruprecht (1995) and Adhikari (2000).
