# Holographic Charged Transport with Higher Derivatives

> We compute the first-order hydrodynamic transport coefficients (shear viscosity $η$, bulk viscosity $ζ$, and charge conductivity $σ$) for a broad class of strongly coupled, four-dimensional charged relativistic gauge theory plasma with holographic gravitational duals containing higher-derivative corrections. The landscape of our holographic models captures non-conformal gauge theories with an arbitrary number of relevant coupling constants and a general scalar potential in the gravitational dual, allowing for a systematic exploration of charged transport along generic holographic RG flows. The leading-order higher-derivative corrections probe gauge theories with non-equal central charges $c\ne a$ at the ultraviolet fixed point, and enable the engineering of diverse temperature and charge density profiles for the viscosities and the conductivity. Our results establish the membrane paradigm in higher-derivative holographic models: all the transport coefficients are extracted from the black brane horizon values of the gravitational scalars, and various functions defining the gravitational holographic dual.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that all first-order hydrodynamic transport coefficients (shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and charge conductivity) for strongly coupled charged plasmas with holographic duals containing higher-derivative corrections can be extracted entirely from black brane horizon data, provided the higher-derivative couplings are treated perturbatively (to leading order in the deformation parameter) and the background geometry is homogeneous and isotropic.

## Method

The authors extend the membrane paradigm framework of Buchel (2023) to include a bulk U(1) gauge field, allowing computation of transport coefficients for charged equilibrium states. They consider two classes of five-dimensional holographic models: Model I with perturbative four-derivative curvature and gauge field corrections involving nine arbitrary scalar-dependent couplings, and Model II with finite Gauss-Bonnet corrections plus perturbative higher-derivative terms coupling Gauss-Bonnet to the field strength. The transport coefficients are obtained by solving master equations for gauge-invariant scalar and gauge field fluctuations at zero frequency, then evaluating these fluctuations at the black brane horizon, with no need to solve quasinormal mode equations.

## Context

This work extends the membrane paradigm framework of Buchel (2023) from neutral to charged plasmas, enabling computation of transport coefficients for holographic models with baryonic chemical potential relevant to heavy-ion collisions. It generalizes earlier two-derivative membrane paradigm results from Iqbal and Liu (2008) and the Eling-Oz formula for bulk viscosity, which was found to be incorrect for higher-derivative theories by Buchel (2023, 2024). The results enable systematic exploration of temperature and chemical potential dependence of transport coefficients in improved holographic QCD models, potentially allowing experimental data on \(\{\eta,\zeta,\sigma\}\) to be used alongside lattice equation of state data for phenomenological model engineering.
