# Decoding Two-Particle States in QCD with Spatial Wavefunctions

> A systematic way to constructing optimized interpolating operators strongly coupled to QCD two-particle states is developed, which is achieved by incorporating inter-hadron spatial wavefunctions. To efficiently implement these operators in lattice QCD, a novel quark smearing technique utilizing noise vectors is proposed. Applied to the $Ω_{ccc}Ω_{ccc}$ system, the optimized operators resolve distinct eigenstates separated by only $\sim 5$ MeV near the threshold $2m_{Ω_{ccc}} \simeq 9700$ MeV. This exceptional resolving power opens new possibilities for studies of a wide range of hadronic systems in QCD.

## Key claim
The paper claims that optimized two-hadron operators constructed from inter-hadron spatial wavefunctions can resolve distinct eigenstates separated by only ~5 MeV near the Omega_ccc Omega_ccc threshold, but this resolving power is demonstrated specifically for the heavy Omega_ccc system and the iterative procedure requires convergence, which was achieved in one iteration for this test case.

## Method
The authors first compute the leading-order local potential using the time-dependent HAL QCD method from unoptimized (wall source) correlation functions, then solve the Schrodinger eigenvalue problem on a finite box to obtain approximate wavefunctions and their dual basis. They implement the optimized operators at the source using a novel quark smearing technique that combines a local smearing function with Z_3 noise vectors weighted by the cubic root of the inter-hadron spatial wavefunction, avoiding all-to-all propagator computations. The iterative procedure is applied to the Omega_ccc Omega_ccc system in the ^1S_0 channel on a 96^3 lattice with 8.1 fm box size at physical pion mass (m_pi ~ 137 MeV) using HAL-conf-2023 gauge configurations.

## Key numbers
- Energy gap between ground state and first excited state: ~5 MeV near threshold 2m_Omega_ccc ~ 9700 MeV
- Omega_ccc mass: m_Omega_ccc ~ 4847 MeV
- Lattice size: L = 96, La ~ 8.1 fm
- Pion mass: m_pi ~ 137 MeV
- Local smearing parameter: B = 0.475 a^{-1} for exponential smearing function f(r) = e^{-B|r|}
- Residue factor for unoptimized wall source at t/a = 20: 24.1(0.3)%; at t/a = 28: 3.6(0.1)%
- Residue factor for optimized ground state at t/a = 20: 3.7(1.4)%; at t/a = 28: 0.5(0.5)%
- Residue factor for optimized first excited state at t/a = 20: 1.6(2.5)%; at t/a = 28: 0.4(0.4)%

## Context
This work extends the HAL QCD method (Ishii 2007, Ishii 2012) and previous studies on two-baryon systems (Iritani 2019, Lyu 2022) by introducing a systematic iterative procedure to construct optimized operators from spatial wavefunctions. The approach addresses the challenge of dense two-hadron spectra where energy gaps scale as 4pi^2/(m_H L^2), which previously led to misidentification of two-nucleon states at heavy pion masses (BaSc 2025). The method enables direct derivation of nuclear forces from QCD and provides an operator basis for the conventional variational method (Luscher 1990, Blossier 2009).
