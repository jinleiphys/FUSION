# Physics case for quarkonium studies at the Electron Ion Collider

> The physics case for quarkonium-production studies accessible at the US Electron Ion Collider is described.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Electron Ion Collider (EIC) will provide unique opportunities for quarkonium studies, but the precision and scope of these measurements depend critically on detector design choices, particularly whether a dedicated muon detector is included, as the current baseline ePIC detector lacks muon-identification instrumentation and has only demonstrated feasibility for exclusive J/ψ reconstruction via the e⁺e⁻ channel.

## Method

The paper reviews the planned EIC accelerator parameters, detector designs (ATHENA, CORE, ECCE, and the selected ePIC baseline), and the kinematic variables relevant to quarkonium production in electron-nucleon and electron-nucleus collisions. It discusses QED radiative corrections and their impact on semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) measurements, presenting a hybrid QCD+QED factorisation formalism using lepton distribution functions (LDFs) and lepton fragmentation functions (LFFs) to resum logarithmically-enhanced QED contributions. The paper also reviews intrinsic charm models and their potential probes via associated J/ψ + charm production at the EIC.

## Key numbers

- Centre-of-mass energy range: 28 GeV to 141 GeV for lepton-proton collisions; up to 89 GeV/nucleon for lepton-ion collisions
- Expected instantaneous luminosity: 10³³ to 10³⁴ cm⁻²s⁻¹ for electron-proton collisions, with maximum at √s = 105 GeV; ~10³⁴ cm⁻²s⁻¹ for electron-ion reactions
- Integrated luminosities: 10 to 100 fb⁻¹ per year
- Designed average beam polarisation: ~70% for electron, proton, and ³He beams
- Pseudorapidity coverage of ePIC detector: -4 < η < 4
- Magnetic field: 1.7 T for ePIC (compared to 3.0 T for ATHENA and 1.4 T for ECCE)
- Particle identification momentum range: up to 50 GeV
- Intrinsic charm probability extracted from earlier analyses: 0.1% to 1%
- LHCb measurement consistent with intrinsic charm component as large as 1% at large Z rapidity

## Context

This paper extends the physics case outlined in the EIC White Paper (Accardi 2012), the NAS report (NAP25171), and the EIC Yellow Report (AbdulKhalek 2021) by focusing specifically on quarkonium production capabilities. It builds on prior work on QED radiative corrections in SIDIS (Liu 2021) and intrinsic charm models (Brodsky 1980, 1981; Sufian 2020), and discusses how the EIC could test intrinsic charm via associated J/ψ + charm production using a variable-flavour-number scheme (Shao 2020; Flore 2020). The paper enables future detector optimisation studies and provides a framework for designing quarkonium measurement strategies at the EIC, particularly motivating the inclusion of muon-identification capabilities in a potential second interaction point detector.
