# A review of the open charm and open bottom systems

> Since the discovery of the first charmed meson in 1976, many open-charm and open-bottom hadrons were observed. In 2003 two narrow charm-strange states $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ and $D_{s1}(2460)$ were discovered by the BaBar and CLEO Collaborations, respectively. After that, more excited heavy hadrons were reported. In this work, we review the experimental and theoretical progress in this field.

## Key claim

This paper is a comprehensive review of experimental and theoretical progress on open-charm and open-bottom hadrons, covering both conventional quark-model states and possible exotic candidates, without presenting new original results or a single unifying claim.

## Method

The authors systematically survey the known experimental observations of open-charm mesons (D, D_s), open-bottom mesons (B, B_s), charmed baryons, and bottom baryons, and compare them with predictions from various theoretical approaches including the relativized quark model of Godfrey and Isgur (1985), the relativistic quark model of Ebert, Faustov, and Galkin (1998, 2010), lattice QCD calculations such as those by Lang et al. (2014), and QCD sum rules. They also discuss the possible interpretation of several states as hadronic molecules or multiquark configurations, and review the status of the X, Y, Z exotic states in the open-flavor sector.

## Key numbers

* The D_s0*(2317) and D_s1(2460) charm-strange states were discovered in 2003 by the BaBar and CLEO Collaborations, respectively, with masses near 2317 MeV and 2460 MeV.
* The D0 Collaboration reported evidence for a B_s0 pi+- state (2016) with a significance that prompted a search by LHCb (2016) in the same B_s0 pi+- invariant mass spectrum.
* The Particle Data Group review (Olive et al., 2016) provides the standard reference for established hadron masses and quantum numbers.

## Context

This review extends earlier surveys of heavy hadron spectroscopy such as those by Colangelo, De Fazio, and Ferrandes (2004), Swanson (2006), and the review of hidden-charm pentaquark and tetraquark states by Chen, Chen, Liu, and Zhu (2016). It covers the rapid experimental progress from B-factories (BaBar, Belle), CLEO, CDF, D0, LHCb, and CMS through 2016, and provides a unified theoretical framework for interpreting the many newly observed excited heavy hadrons. The work enables future studies to systematically compare candidate exotic states with conventional quark-model expectations and to guide searches for missing predicted states.
