# Deciphering yield modification of hadron-triggered semi-inclusive recoil jets in heavy-ion collisions

> In relativistic heavy-ion collisions, a hot and dense state of matter, called the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP), is produced. Semi-inclusive jets recoiling from trigger hadrons of high transverse momenta ($p_{\mathrm{T}}$) can serve as an effective probe of the QGP properties, as they are expected to experience jet quenching when traversing the QGP. Recent experimental results on the ratio of recoil jet yields normalized by the trigger counts in heavy-ion collisions to that in $p$+$p$ collisions ($I_{\mathrm{AA}}$) pose an unexpected challenge in its interpretation. It is observed that $I_{\mathrm{AA}}$ rises with the jet $p_{\mathrm{T}}$ and possibly exceeds unity at high $p_{\mathrm{T}}$, while traditionally it is expected that jet quenching would lead to $I_{\mathrm{AA}} < 1$. To address this challenge, we utilize the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model to simulate jet transport in the QGP, and study the effect of jet quenching for high-$p_{\mathrm{T}}$ triggers and recoil jets separately on $I_{\mathrm{AA}}$. We find that the quenching of the colored triggers alone is responsible for the rising trend and larger-than-unity value observed experimentally.

## Key claim

The observed rise of the recoil-jet yield ratio \(I_{\mathrm{AA}}\) above unity at high jet transverse momentum does not contradict jet quenching; instead, it arises because the energy loss of the colored trigger hadrons themselves shifts the baseline for unquenched recoil jets away from unity, so that \(I_{\mathrm{AA}} > 1\) can still signal jet quenching.

## Method

The authors used the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model to simulate the evolution of parton showers from PYTHIA8 through a hydrodynamic QGP background, including both elastic and inelastic scatterings. They compared the yield of parton-level recoil jets per trigger in heavy-ion collisions to that in proton-proton collisions, defining \(I_{\mathrm{AA}}\) as the ratio of the per-trigger recoil-jet spectra. To isolate the effect of trigger energy loss, they constructed a hybrid sample in which the trigger partons were taken from the LBT simulation (with energy loss) but the recoil jets were taken from the corresponding PYTHIA event (without energy loss), defining this as the “true” baseline \(I_{\mathrm{AA}}^{\mathrm{baseline}}\).

## Key numbers

* In central Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 5.02\) TeV, only about 37% of triggers with \(20 < p_{\mathrm{T,trig}} < 50\) GeV/\(c\) lose less than 1 GeV/\(c\) of energy; the rest lose more.
* In central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 200\) GeV, about 70% of triggers with \(9 < p_{\mathrm{T,trig}} < 11\) GeV/\(c\) lose less than 1 GeV/\(c\).
* The baseline \(I_{\mathrm{AA}}^{\mathrm{baseline}}\) for \(R = 0.2\) jets in central Au+Au collisions rises to about 4 at high jet \(p_{\mathrm{T}}\) for the lowest trigger \(p_{\mathrm{T}}\) range examined.
* The LBT calculation with \(\alpha_s = 0.3\) qualitatively reproduces the rising trend of \(I_{\mathrm{AA}}\) with jet \(p_{\mathrm{T}}\) seen in ALICE data for 0-10% central Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 5.02\) TeV.

## Context

This work addresses the unexpected observation by ALICE (2023) that \(I_{\mathrm{AA}}\) for hadron-triggered recoil jets in Pb+Pb collisions rises above unity at high jet \(p_{\mathrm{T}}\), contrary to the traditional expectation that jet quenching should give \(I_{\mathrm{AA}} < 1\). The authors extend prior studies of surface bias (Muller, 2002; Renk, 2006) and di-jet momentum imbalance (Milhano, 2015) by showing that trigger energy loss, not the recoil jet suppression alone, controls the \(I_{\mathrm{AA}}\) baseline. The finding enables future work to use triggers that do not experience jet quenching (e.g., photons) to maintain a baseline of unity, or to use the coherent description of both trigger and recoil jet energy loss as a stronger constraint on model calculations.
