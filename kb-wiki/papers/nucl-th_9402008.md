# The Nucleon-Nucleon Potential in the Chromo-Dielectric Soliton Model: Statics

> We study the N-N interaction in the framework of the chromo-dielectric soliton model. Here, the long-range parts of the nonabelian gluon self-interactions are assumed to give rise to a color-dielectric function which is parameterized in terms of an effective scalar background field. The six-quark system is confined in a deformed mean field through an effective non-linear interaction between the quarks and the scalar field. The CDM is covariant, respects chiral invariance, leads to absolute color confinement and is free of the spurious long range Van der Waals forces which trouble non-relativistic investigations employing a confining potential. Six-quark molecular-type configurations are generated as a function of deformation and their energies are evaluated in a coupled channel analysis. By using molecular states instead of cluster model wave functions, all important six-quark configurations are properly taken into account. The corresponding Hamiltonian includes the effective interaction between the quarks and the scalar background field and quark-quark interactions generated through one gluon exchange treated in Coulomb gauge. When evaluating the gluonic propagators, the inhomogeneity and deformation of the dielectric medium are taken into account. Results for the adiabatic nucleon-nucleon potential are presented, and the various contributions are discussed. Finally, an outlook is given on how, in the next stage of our investigation, the dynamical effects will be incorporated by employing the generator coordinate method.

## Key claim

The paper presents an adiabatic (static) nucleon-nucleon potential calculated in the chromo-dielectric soliton model, but the authors explicitly note that a significant part of the short-range repulsion is due to dynamics and that the absence of a repulsive core in some early calculations is an artifact of the adiabatic approximation, meaning the static results shown here are incomplete without the planned dynamical treatment via the generator coordinate method.

## Method

The authors construct six-quark molecular-type configurations as a function of a deformation parameter α, using the two lowest single-particle orbitals (positive parity |σ⟩ and negative parity |π⟩) from a constrained mean-field calculation with a deformed scalar σ-field. The Hamiltonian includes an effective quark-scalar field interaction and one-gluon exchange treated in Coulomb gauge, with gluonic propagators evaluated accounting for the inhomogeneity and deformation of the dielectric medium. The six-quark basis includes seven states (three "physical" NN, ΔΔ, CC states plus four additional configurations of type |r⁴ℓ²+r²ℓ⁴⟩ and |r⁵ℓ+rℓ⁵⟩) and the Hamiltonian is diagonalized in this truncated Hilbert space for each deformation.

## Key numbers

- The scalar field parameters are fitted to the nucleon mass, Δ mass, and proton rms charge radius of 0.83 fm
- The glueball mass m_GB lies between 1.5 GeV and 2.0 GeV for the parameter sets considered (c=10000)
- The experimental string tension is approximately 913 MeV, used as a reference for parameter adjustment
- The axial vector coupling g_A is inherently too small by about 10 percent in the model
- For the parameter sets studied, f = b²/ac takes values of 3, 3.2, and ∞, with c fixed at 10000

## Context

This work extends the chromo-dielectric soliton model (F88, K88) to the six-quark N-N system, building on earlier studies of the Friedberg-Lee non-topological soliton model (F77) and the MIT bag model (DT77). It contrasts with non-relativistic constituent quark models (L77) and cluster model approaches (O81, H81) by using molecular orbitals instead of cluster model wave functions, which the authors show (St88, St89) substantially lowers ground state energies at zero separation. The paper is the first in a series; subsequent papers will incorporate dynamics via the generator coordinate method and attach the derived interaction to a phenomenological OBE potential at larger distances.
