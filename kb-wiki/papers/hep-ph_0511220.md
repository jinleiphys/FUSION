# Nuclear effects on open charm production in p+A reactions

> We calculate and resum the nuclear enhanced power corrections from the final state partonic scattering in nuclear matter to open charm production and correlations. In p+A reactions, we find that single and double inclusive D mesons can be suppressed as much as the neutral pions from the dynamical high twist shadowing. Effects of initial state energy loss in p+A collisions are also investigated and may lead to significantly weaker transverse momentum dependence of the nuclear suppression.

## Key claim

The paper finds that nuclear suppression of single and double inclusive D mesons in p+A reactions can be as large as that of neutral pions from dynamical high twist shadowing, but initial state energy loss may produce a significantly weaker transverse momentum dependence of the suppression.

## Method

The authors calculate open charm production cross sections to lowest order in perturbative QCD using CTEQ6 parton distribution functions and fragmentation functions from heavy quark effective field theory. They resum all high twist nuclear-enhanced power corrections from final state partonic scattering in nuclear matter, and separately incorporate initial state energy loss phenomenologically using incoherent Bertsch-Gunion type radiation that scales with path length through the nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass set to \(m_c \sim 1.5\) GeV, a relatively hard scale
- Binding energy scale of open charm meson is \(\sim M_D - m_c \sim\) hundreds of MeV
- Critical value for onset of coherence in nuclear matter is \(x_N = 1 / (2 r_0 m_N) \sim 0.1\)
- For minimum bias collisions, initial state energy loss is modeled by a rapidity shift of \(\Delta y = 0.25\)
- Calculations performed for d+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV at rapidities \(y = 1.25\) and \(y = 2.5\)

## Context

This work extends the high twist shadowing formalism previously applied to light hadrons by Qiu (2004) to open charm production and correlations, providing the first calculation of coherent resummed QCD power corrections for heavy quarks in p+A reactions. The results establish a baseline for heavy quark tomography of the quark-gluon plasma at forward rapidities, as studied by Djordjevic (2004), and are intended to guide experimental measurements at RHIC such as those by PHENIX.
