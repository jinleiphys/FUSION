# Perspective on rainbow-ladder truncation

> Prima facie the systematic implementation of corrections to the rainbow-ladder truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations will uniformly reduce in magnitude those calculated mass-dimensioned results for pseudoscalar and vector meson properties that are not tightly constrained by symmetries. The aim and interpretation of studies employing rainbow-ladder truncation are reconsidered in this light.

## Key claim

The rainbow-ladder truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations systematically overestimates mass-dimensioned results for pseudoscalar and vector meson properties that are not tightly constrained by symmetries, by approximately 35% in units of mass; this uniform overestimate allows corrections to be absorbed through simple parameter modifications, but features such as decay widths cannot be reproduced in this way.

## Method

The authors employ a renormalisation-group-improved rainbow-ladder Dyson-Schwinger equation model with a Bethe-Salpeter kernel that combines a long-range interaction term (a finite-width representation) with a perturbative short-range term. They determine the interaction strength parameter C(ω, m̂) by requiring that the rainbow-ladder truncation reproduce a specified trajectory for the ρ-meson mass as a function of pion mass squared, Eq. (masstrajectory), which encodes the expected attraction from corrections. The dressed-quark propagator and meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes are then solved from the gap equation and Bethe-Salpeter equation, and physical quantities (condensate, decay constants, charge radius) are computed for varying current-quark masses.

## Key numbers

- The ρ-meson mass in rainbow-ladder truncation at the chiral limit is mρ⁰ = 0.99 GeV, with corrections from nonresonant diagrams producing ~100 MeV of attraction and pseudoscalar meson loops providing ~120 MeV.
- At the physical pion mass (mπ = 0.138 GeV), calculated results overestimate experiment by 34 ± 4% in units of mass: the condensate [−⟨q̄q⟩₁⁰]^(1/3) = 0.319(2) GeV (phenomenological 0.236 GeV); fπ⁺ = 0.178 GeV (experimental 0.131 GeV); fρ⁺ = 0.283(9) GeV (phenomenological 0.216 GeV); mρ = 0.99 GeV (experimental 0.77 GeV); rπ = 0.485(3) fm (experimental 0.672 fm).
- The product rπ fπ = 0.44(1) is constant independent of current-quark mass, compared to the experimental value 0.445 ± 0.007.
- The off-shell ρππ coupling constant gρππ(0)e^(−ϱ) ≈ 2.75(40) on the relevant (ω, mπ) domain.

## Context

This work extends the rainbow-ladder DSE model of Maris and Tandy (1997, 1999) by systematically quantifying the expected overestimate from the truncation, rather than treating it as a free parameter. It contradicts the assumption that rainbow-ladder predictions can be directly compared to experiment without correction, and instead provides a framework where the uniform 35% overestimate allows corrections from nonresonant diagrams (Bhagwat 2004, Matevosyan 2006) and meson loops (Pichowsky 1999) to be incorporated. The study enables future work to implement systematic corrections and obtain reliable predictions for light-quark observables, while noting that decay widths and nonanalyticities from hadron loops cannot be captured in this truncation.
