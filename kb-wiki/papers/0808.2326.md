# Electromagnetic Transition Form Factors of Nucleon Resonances

> Recent measurements of nucleon resonance transition form factors with CLAS at Jefferson Lab are discussed. The new data resolve a long-standing puzzle of the nature of the Roper resonance, and confirm the assertion of the symmetric constituent quark model of the Roper as the first radial excitation of the nucleon. The data on $nπ^+$ production at high $Q^2$ confirm the slow fall off of the $S_{11}(1535)$ transition form factor with $Q^2$, and better constrain the branching ratios $β_{Nπ}=0.50$ and $β_{Nη}=0.45$. Also, new results on the transition amplitudes for the $D_{13}(1520)$ resonance are presented showing a rapid change from helicity 3/2 dominance seen at the real photon point to helicty 1/2 dominance at higher $Q^2$.

## Key claim

The new CLAS data resolve the long-standing puzzle of the Roper resonance's nature, confirming the symmetric constituent quark model's assertion that it is the first radial excitation of the nucleon, but this conclusion is hedged by the observation that the low-\(Q^2\) behavior is not well described by light-front quark models, indicating missing meson-baryon interactions at large distances.

## Method

The CLAS detector at Jefferson Lab measured exclusive single-pion and eta electroproduction reactions (\(p(e,e'p)\pi^0\), \(ep\rightarrow e\pi^+ n\), and \(p\eta\)) over a large range in photon virtuality \(Q^2\) and invariant mass \(W\). Differential cross sections and polarized beam asymmetries were analyzed using fixed-t dispersion relations and a unitary isobar model (UIM) to extract transition form factors and helicity amplitudes for the \(\Delta(1232)\), Roper \(P_{11}(1440)\), \(S_{11}(1535)\), and \(D_{13}(1520)\) resonances.

## Key numbers

- For the \(N\Delta(1232)\) transition, \(R_{EM}\) remains negative, small, and nearly constant in the range \(0 < Q^2 < 6\) GeV\(^2\), with \(R_{EM} \approx -0.03\) at high \(Q^2\).
- The Roper \(P_{11}(1440)\) transverse amplitude \(A_{1/2}\) changes sign near \(Q^2 = 0.5\) GeV\(^2\), reaching about the same magnitude but opposite sign at \(Q^2 = 2\) GeV\(^2\) as at \(Q^2 = 0\).
- For the \(S_{11}(1535)\), branching ratios were constrained to \(\beta_{N\pi} = 0.50\) and \(\beta_{N\eta} = 0.45\), bringing the \(n\pi^+\) and \(p\eta\) data sets into excellent agreement at higher \(Q^2\).
- The \(D_{13}(1520)\) shows a rapid helicity switch: \(A_{3/2}\) decreases rapidly with \(Q^2\) while \(A_{1/2}\) increases rapidly in magnitude, with \(A_{1/2}\) completely dominating at \(Q^2 > 2\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the program of nucleon resonance electroexcitation measurements using CLAS at Jefferson Lab, resolving a decade-long debate about the Roper resonance that had prompted alternative models such as a gluonic excitation (Li and Burkert, 1999) or a hadronic molecule (Krewald, 2005). The new data confirm and refine earlier results from CLAS and Hall C on the \(S_{11}(1535)\) hard form factor (Thompson, 2001; Armstrong, 1999), and provide the first extraction of the longitudinal amplitude for that state. The results enable future comparisons with lattice QCD calculations, which have recently produced unquenched results for the Roper (Lattice QCD Collaboration, 2008) and the \(N\Delta\) transition (Alexandrou, 2006), and challenge model builders to explain the lack of asymptotic behavior in the \(\Delta(1232)\) multipole ratios.
