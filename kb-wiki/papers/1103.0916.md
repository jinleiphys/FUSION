# Transport Model Studies of the Baryon-Rich Quark-Gluon Plasma formed in Heavy Ion Collisions

> Heavy ion collisions in the low energy run at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and future Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research (FAIR) in Germany are expected to produce a quark-gluon plasma that has a finite baryon chemical potential, allowing thus the possibility to study the location of the critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram. In this talk, using a multiphase transport model, that includes interactions in both initial partonic and final hadronic matters and the transition between these two phases of matter, we discuss the effects of partonic interactions on observables such as the elliptic flow that have played essential roles in studying the properties of the net baryon free quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy ion collisions at higher energies at RHIC. Also, we study the effect of density fluctuations due to a first-order transition between the quark-gluon plasma and hadronic matter on fluctuations of hadron mean transverse momentum and produced deuteron number as well as on two-pion correlations. We further discuss the possibility of studying the mechanism of charm energy loss in the baryon-rich quark-gluon plasma and the properties of phi mesons in hot-dense matter produced in these collisions.

# Transport Model Studies of the Baryon-Rich Quark-Gluon Plasma formed in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
Using a multiphase transport (AMPT) model with string melting, the authors predict that in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=7 GeV (available at RHIC low-energy runs and FAIR), the baryon-rich quark-gluon plasma will produce distinct signatures including enhanced elliptic flow from partonic interactions, a larger energy loss for anti-charm quarks than charm quarks due to resonance scattering in a baryon-rich medium, and a possible lower-mass phi meson peak in dilepton spectra if a first-order phase transition occurs.

## Method
The study uses the AMPT model, a hybrid transport model that combines minijet partons from hard processes and strings from soft processes (via HIJING) as initial conditions, with parton evolution described by Zhang's Parton Cascade (ZPC) model using elastic scatterings with a perturbative QCD cross section (strong coupling alpha_s=0.47). The string melting version converts hadrons from HIJING into valence quarks/antiquarks, hadronizes via coordinate-space quark coalescence, and models final-state hadronic scatterings with a relativistic transport (ART) model. For studying first-order phase transition effects, the authors artificially introduce large density fluctuations by redistributing hadrons into a volume four times larger with five clusters following a Poisson distribution.

## Key numbers
- Parton scattering cross section used: 6-10 mb to reproduce RHIC elliptic flow data
- For Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=7 GeV: total multiplicity density at midrapidity is about 450, about a factor of three lower than at high-energy RHIC runs
- Initial energy density of central cell at sqrt(s_NN)=7 GeV: about 10 GeV/fm^3, about a factor of three lower than at high-energy RHIC runs
- Net baryon rapidity density at midrapidity at sqrt(s_NN)=7 GeV: about 135, almost half of that for pions
- Duration of partonic stage: about 4.2 fm/c at RHIC, less than 3 fm/c at lower energies and FAIR
- For Au+Au at E_beam=40 AGeV: initial temperature T_0=225 MeV and baryon chemical potential mu_0=300 MeV from AMPT model
- Charm quark mass: m_c=1.5 GeV; light quark mass: m_q=300 MeV (for elliptic flow calculations) or 5-10 MeV (for resonance scattering)
- Charmed meson mass for resonance scattering: m_D=2 GeV, width Gamma_D=0.3-0.5 GeV, peak cross section about 6 mb
- Drag coefficient from resonance scattering: gamma ~ 0.16 c/fm
- Phi meson width: ~4 MeV

## Context
This work extends the AMPT model studies previously applied to high-energy RHIC collisions (Zhang 2000, Lin 2001, Lin 2004) to the baryon-rich regime accessible at RHIC low-energy runs and FAIR. It builds on the observation that the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC is strongly interacting (STAR collaboration) and on PNJL model predictions that the crossover transition at zero baryon chemical potential becomes first-order at large baryon chemical potential (Stephanov 2006). The predictions for charm energy loss asymmetry between charm and anti-charm quarks test the resonance scattering mechanism proposed by Hees (2008) for explaining large charm elliptic flow at RHIC. The phi meson double-peak signature in dilepton spectra follows the hydrodynamic model calculations of Asakawa (1994) for first-order phase transitions. These results enable experimental searches for the QCD critical endpoint at FAIR and RHIC low-energy runs.
