# Multiple scattering of heavy-quarks in dense matter and the parametric prominence of drag

> The case of heavy quark propagation in dense extended matter is studied in the multiple scattering formalism of the higher twist energy loss scheme. We consider the case of deep inelastic scattering off a large nucleus. The hard lepton scatters off a heavy quark fluctuation within one of the nucleons. This heavy quark then propagates through the dense medium, multiply scattering off the gluon field of the remaining nucleons in its path. We consider the fictitious process where a heavy quark propagates through the nucleus without radiation. Invoking Soft-Collinear Effective Theory power counting arguments, we consider the case of a "semi-hard" heavy-quark where the mass is of the order of the out-going momentum and larger than the transverse momentum imparted per unit length due to scattering. In this limit, it is found that longitudinal momentum exchanges (quantified by the transport coefficient $\hat{e}$) have a comparable effect on the off-shellness of the propagating quark, as the transverse momentum exchanges (quantified by $\hat{q}$) which constitute the leading cause of off-shellness for propagating light quarks or gluons. Consequences of this new hierarchy for the propagation of the heavy quark in dense matter are discussed.

## Key claim
For "semi-hard" heavy quarks where the mass is of order the outgoing momentum and larger than the transverse momentum imparted per unit length, longitudinal momentum exchanges (quantified by the transport coefficient $\hat{e}$) have a comparable effect on the off-shellness of the propagating quark as transverse momentum exchanges (quantified by $\hat{q}$), which constitute the leading cause of off-shellness for light quarks or gluons. This represents a departure from the light-quark case where longitudinal drag is subleading.

## Method
The authors study heavy quark propagation in dense extended matter using the multiple scattering formalism of the higher twist energy loss scheme, applied to deep inelastic scattering off a large nucleus. They consider the fictitious process where a heavy quark propagates through the nucleus without radiation, invoking Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) power counting arguments with a small parameter $\lambda$ to classify momentum scales. The hadronic tensor is expanded in a power series in $\lambda$, and the leading terms yield both a transverse diffusion equation and a longitudinal drag equation, unlike the light-quark case which yields only a diffusion equation.

## Key numbers
- The heavy quark mass $M$ is taken to scale as $M \sim \sqrt{\lambda} Q$, where $Q$ is the hardest scale (the off-shellness of the hard virtual photon), and the outgoing quark momentum $p \sim \sqrt{\lambda} Q$.
- The exchanged momenta with the medium scale as $k \equiv [k^{+}, k^{-}, \vec{k}_{\perp}] \sim [\lambda^{3/2}, \lambda^{2}, \lambda] Q$.
- The Bjorken variable $x_B = Q^2 / (2 p^{+} q^{-})$ is of order $\mathcal{O}(x_B) \sim 1$, i.e., a large momentum ratio.
- For the heavy quark, $p_i^{+} \sim \lambda^{3/2} Q$ (controlled by both $p_i^{-}$ and ${p_i}_{\perp}$), whereas for a light quark $p_i^{+} \sim \lambda^{2} Q$ (dominantly controlled by ${p_i}_{\perp}^{2}/(2q^{-})$).

## Context
This work extends the next-to-leading twist formalism of Guo and Wang (2000) and Wang (2001) to heavy flavors, building on prior efforts by Majumder (2007, 2008, 2009), Idilbi (2008), and Qin (2012) that systematically extended the formalism to a scattering-resummed approach for light partons. The paper lays groundwork for calculating gluon radiation from heavy quarks undergoing multiple scattering, which will be addressed in future work, and is motivated by the need for more sophisticated approaches to heavy-quark suppression measurements at the LHC, where the medium is larger and denser than at RHIC.
