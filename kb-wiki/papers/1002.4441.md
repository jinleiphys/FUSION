# Heavy quark(onium) at LHC: the statistical hadronization case

> We discuss the production of charmonium in nuclear collisions within the framework of the statistical hadronization model. We demonstrate that the model reproduces very well the availble data at RHIC. We provide predictions for the LHC energy where, dependently on the charm production cross section, a dramatically different behaviour of charmonium production as a function of centrality might be expected. We discuss also the case in elementary collisions, where clearly the statistical model does not reproduce the measurements.

## Key claim

The statistical hadronization model reproduces charmonium data in nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC and SPS, but fails dramatically in elementary collisions (e+e-, pp, pA), establishing that the thermal behavior is a unique signature of deconfined matter formed in heavy-ion collisions; the LHC predictions depend critically on the charm production cross section, which can reverse the centrality trend of J/ψ production.

## Method

The authors apply the statistical hadronization model, where all charm quarks are produced in initial hard collisions but charmed hadrons and charmonia form exclusively at the phase boundary with thermal weights. For nucleus-nucleus collisions, the model uses chemical freeze-out parameters (temperature, baryochemical potential, volume) from fits of non-charmed hadrons, the charm production cross section from NLO pQCD or experiment, and a charm balance equation with canonical suppression factors to determine the charm fugacity. For elementary collisions, they employ a canonical statistical model with either a 5-flavor jet scheme or a purely thermal ansatz, using temperature and volume from fits to light-hadron multiplicities.

## Key numbers

- In e+e- collisions at √s=91 GeV, the thermal model with the 5-flavor scheme yields χ²/dof = 21.7/16 (excluding Υ and prompt J/ψ) and 34/18 including all species.
- The prompt J/ψ yield in Z0 decays is measured at about 3×10⁻⁴; the thermal model predicts 4.1×10⁻⁶ (underprediction by almost 2 orders of magnitude).
- The Υ meson yield in e+e- is underpredicted by the thermal model by 17 orders of magnitude.
- In pp and pA collisions, the ψ'/J/ψ ratio is 0.137±0.009 (average of measurements), a factor of about 4 above the statistical model prediction (10 experimental standard deviations).
- The temperature needed to explain the ψ'/J/ψ ratio in elementary collisions would be 300 MeV, well above the Hagedorn limiting temperature of about 200 MeV.
- At RHIC (√sNN=200 GeV), the model reproduces the centrality dependence of RAAJ/ψ, with the rapidity density of initially produced c-cbar pairs dNc-cbar/dy = 1.6 for central collisions using the FONLL charm cross section.
- At LHC (√sNN=5.5 TeV), the charm production cross section is expected to be about an order of magnitude larger than at RHIC, leading to RAAJ/ψ exceeding unity for central collisions; a factor of 2 larger charm cross section yields about a factor of 2 enhancement.

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model originally proposed by Braun-Munzinger and Stachel (2000) and previously applied to SPS and RHIC data by Andronic et al. (2007-2008). It directly contradicts the original Debye-screening suppression scenario of Matsui and Satz (1986) by showing that J/ψ production in nucleus-nucleus collisions can be explained by statistical recombination at the phase boundary rather than sequential melting. The paper provides predictions for the LHC that enable a decisive test: if RAAJ/ψ increases with centrality, it would confirm the statistical hadronization mechanism and establish charmonium as a probe of the QCD phase boundary. The failure of the model in elementary collisions (e+e- at LEP, pp at Tevatron and RHIC, pA) is contrasted with the success in heavy-ion collisions, emphasizing the uniqueness of the thermal behavior in deconfined matter.
