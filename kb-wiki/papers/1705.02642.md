# Weak quadrupole moments

> Collective effects in deformed atomic nuclei present possible avenues of study on the non-spherical distribution of neutrons and the violation of the local Lorentz invariance. We introduce the weak quadrupole moment of nuclei, related to the quadrupole distribution of the weak charge in the nucleus. The weak quadrupole moment produces tensor weak interaction between the nucleus and electrons and can be observed in atomic and molecular experiments measuring parity nonconservation. The dominating contribution to the weak quadrupole is given by the quadrupole moment of the neutron distribution, therefore, corresponding experiments should allow one to measure the neutron quadrupoles. Using the deformed oscillator model and the Schmidt model we calculate the quadrupole distributions of neutrons, $Q_{n}$, the weak quadrupole moments ,$Q_{W}^{(2)}$, and the Lorentz Innvariance violating energy shifts in $^{9}$Be, $^{21}$Ne , $^{27}$Al, $^{131}$Xe, $^{133}$Cs, $^{151}$Eu, $^{153}$Eu, $^{163}$Dy, $^{167}$Er, $^{173}$Yb, $^{177}$Hf, $^{179}$Hf, $^{181}$Ta, $^{201}$Hg and $^{229}$Th.

# Weak quadrupole moments

## Key claim

The paper introduces the weak quadrupole moment of nuclei, which is dominated by the quadrupole distribution of neutrons rather than protons, and argues that this tensor property is collectively enhanced in deformed nuclei, making it observable in atomic and molecular parity nonconservation experiments. The authors qualify that these properties have yet to be experimentally detected, though constraints exist for Lorentz invariance violation.

## Method

The authors use the Nilsson deformed oscillator model to calculate single-particle wavefunctions and occupation numbers for deformed nuclei, filling Nilsson energy level plots according to experimental nuclear spin values. For near-spherical nuclei where the Nilsson model is inapplicable, they combine the Schmidt model valence contribution with a deformation contribution estimated from the measured proton quadrupole moment scaled by the neutron-to-proton ratio. The weak quadrupole moment is then constructed as \( Q_W^{(2)} = q_{w,p} Q_p + q_{w,n} Q_n \) using the weak charges of protons and neutrons.

## Context

This work extends the concept of the weak quadrupole moment introduced in Flambaum (2016) and provides a semi-empirical method applicable to many deformed nuclei, complementing the \( s-d \) shell model calculations of Brown (2016) for \(^{21}\)Ne, \(^{131}\)Xe, and \(^{201}\)Hg. The results enable experimental searches for parity nonconservation in molecules such as HfF\(^+\) (used in the electron electric dipole moment measurement by Cairncross et al., 2017) and in atoms such as \(^{173}\)Yb (Antypas, 2017) and \(^{163}\)Dy (Leefer, 2017), as well as improved constraints on Lorentz invariance violation in the neutron sector.
