# Variational Master Field for Large-N Interacting Matrix Models - Free Random Variables on Trial

> Matrices are said to behave as free non-commuting random variables if the action which governs their dynamics constrains only their eigenvalues, i.e. depends on traces of powers of individual matrices. The authors use recently developed mathematical techniques in combination with a standard variational principle to formulate a new variational approach for matrix models. Approximate variational solutions of interacting large-N matrix models are found using the free random matrices as the variational space. Several classes of classical and quantum mechanical matrix models with different types of interactions are considered and the variational solutions compared with exact Monte Carlo and analytical results. Impressive agreement is found in a majority of cases.

## Key claim

The authors propose a variational method for large-N interacting matrix models that uses free random matrices as the variational space, claiming that the optimal free approximation to any action is given by its "free partner", but the accuracy of this approximation cannot be estimated a priori and must be tested case by case against exact solutions.

## Method

The method applies the standard Gibbs-Bogoliubov variational inequality to matrix model partition functions, using as trial actions only those that make the matrix variables free with respect to one another. For a given action S, the optimal free trial action S₀ is shown to be the "free partner" S_f of S, obtained by replacing all correlation functions with their expressions under the free random variable axioms (a generalized Wick's theorem for the Cuntz algebra). The variational freedom is extended by allowing linear transformations of the original matrix variables M_i = Σ_j c_{ij} B_j, then constructing the free partner of the transformed action and minimizing the resulting free energy with respect to the coefficients c_{ij}.

## Key numbers

- For the two-matrix model with action S = (1/N) Tr(M₁⁴ + M₂⁴ + g M₁² M₂²), the optimal variational choice switches from original variables M₁, M₂ to B₁ = (M₁+M₂)/2, B₂ = (M₁-M₂)/2 at a critical coupling g_cr = 2.
- For the free matrix model with potential V = (1/N) Tr M₁² + (1/N) Tr M₂² + (1/N²) Tr M₁² Tr M₂², the self-consistency conditions yield x₁ = x₂ = (√3 − 1)/2 for the second moments ⟨M₁²⟩ = ⟨M₂²⟩ = x₁.
- For the two-matrix model with action S = (1/N) Tr(M₁² + M₂² + g M₁ M₂ M₁ M₂), exact analytical solutions exist (Che, year not given in text).

## Context

This work extends the large-N master field concept initiated by 't Hooft (1974) and the exact solution of single-matrix models by Brezin et al. (1978), applying Voiculescu's (1991) free random variable theory to construct variational approximations for interacting multi-matrix models. The method enables approximate analytical treatment of matrix models that are not exactly solvable, including both classical and quantum mechanical models, and is motivated by the long-range physics of Quantum Chromodynamics in the large-N_c limit. The approach is analogous to Hartree or Hartree-Fock mean field theory, with the free random variable axioms playing the role of Wick's theorem.
