# Kibble-Zurek Scaling in a Holographic p-wave Superconductor

> We study the Kibble-Zurek mechanism in a 2d holographic p-wave superconductor model with a homogeneous source quench on the critical point. We derive, on general grounds, the scaling of the Kibble-Zurek time, which marks breaking-down of adiabaticity. It is expressed in terms of four critical exponents, including three static and one dynamical exponents. Via explicit calculations within a holographic model, we confirm the scaling of the Kibble-Zurek time and obtain the scaling functions in the quench process. We find the results are formally similar to a homogeneous quench in a higher dimensional holographic s-wave superconductor. The similarity is due to the special type of quench we take. We expect differences in the quench dynamics if the condition of homogeneous source and dominance of critical mode are relaxed.

## Key claim

The Kibble-Zurek scaling in a 2d holographic p-wave superconductor with a homogeneous source quench is formally similar to that in a higher-dimensional holographic s-wave superconductor, but this similarity is due to the specific choice of homogeneous source and dominance of the critical mode; differences are expected if these conditions are relaxed.

## Method

The authors study the Kibble-Zurek mechanism in a 2d holographic p-wave superconductor model derived from Einstein-Yang-Mills theory in an asymptotic AdS3 background. They derive the scaling of the Kibble-Zurek time on general grounds using four critical exponents (three static and one dynamical), then confirm this scaling through explicit numerical calculations of quasi-normal modes and analysis of the bulk equations of motion in the probe limit.

## Key numbers

- Static critical exponents: (α, β, γ, δ, η, ν) = (0, 1/2, 1, 3, 1, 1)
- Dynamical critical exponent: z = 1
- The Kibble-Zurek time scales as t_KZ ∼ v^{-2/5}, where v is the quench rate
- The relaxation time scales with source J as τ ∼ J^{-2/3}, confirmed by numerical QNM analysis giving a slope of -0.65 for log(-Im ω) versus -log J
- The critical charge density is ρ_c = 21.7 T_H (in probe limit units) or ρ_c = 3.45 (in rescaled units)
- The lowest QNM imaginary part near criticality: Im(ω_QNM) = 0.0009175 + 0.89666 ε_ρ - 1.08382 ε_ρ^2, with linear coefficient indicating τ ∼ ε_ρ

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Kibble-Zurek scaling in holographic s-wave superconductors (Das 2014) to a p-wave model with SU(2) symmetry breaking, finding formal similarity due to the homogeneous source quench. The holographic p-wave model corresponds to "model A" in the Hohenberg-Halperin (1977) classification of dynamical universality classes. The results enable future studies of quench dynamics when the conditions of homogeneous source and dominance of the critical mode are relaxed, where differences from s-wave models are expected.
