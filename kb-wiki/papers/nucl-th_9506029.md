# Photo- and Electroproduction of Eta Mesons

> Eta photo- and electroproduction off the nucleon is investigated in an effective lagrangian approach that contains Born terms and both vector meson and nucleon resonance contributions. In particular, we review and develop the formalism for coincidence experiments with polarization degrees of freedom. The different response functions appearing in single and double polarization experiments have been studied. We will present calculations for structure functions and kinematical conditions that are most sensitive to details of the lagrangian, in particular with regard to contributions of nucleon resonances beyond the dominant $S_{11}$(1535) resonance.

## Key claim

The paper develops a comprehensive formalism for eta photo- and electroproduction including polarization degrees of freedom, but the authors hedge that while the dominant $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance is well understood, extracting couplings of other resonances requires polarization observables because even precise angular distributions are insufficient to constrain small resonance contributions.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian model containing Born terms, vector meson exchange in the $t$-channel, and nucleon resonance contributions parametrized via Breit-Wigner helicity amplitudes. They derive the full multipole decomposition of the CGLN amplitudes and relate them to electromagnetic helicity couplings $A_{1/2}^N$, $A_{3/2}^N$, and $C_{1/2}^N$, using non-relativistic quark model wavefunctions with configuration mixing for the $S_{11}(1535)$ and $D_{13}(1520)$ states. The formalism is developed for coincidence experiments with beam, target, and recoil polarization, yielding 16 polarization observables for photoproduction and 36 for electroproduction.

## Key numbers

- The $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance has a 45-55% branching ratio for eta decay, while the $S_{11}(1650)$ has only about 1% branching ratio for eta decay
- The $D_{13}(1520)$ resonance has an eta branching ratio of the order of $10^{-3}$
- Constituent quark mass taken as $m_q = 350$ MeV and oscillator spring constant as $300$ MeV, following Isgur and Karl (1978)
- The effective interaction radius $R$ for Blatt-Weisskopf functions is fixed at $1.0$ fm
- The $g^2_{\eta NN}/(4\pi)$ coupling constant from SU(3) flavour symmetry ranges between 0.8 and 1.9, but previous analysis by Tiator et al. (1994) found values of order 0.4 are necessary to fit eta photoproduction data
- Electroproduction data exist at $Q^2 = 0.056$ GeV$^2$ from the Elan collaboration at ELSA (Wilhelm et al., 1993)

## Context

This work extends the model of Bennhold and Tanabe (1991) for eta photoproduction in the region of the $S_{11}(1535)$ to higher energies and includes higher resonances, while also developing the full polarization formalism. It contrasts with earlier coupled-channel calculations by Batinić et al. (1991), Sauer, and Feuster that treat $\pi N \rightarrow \pi N$, $\pi N \rightarrow \pi\pi N$, and $\pi N \rightarrow \eta N$ unitarily but leave considerable model dependence. The paper enables future complete experiments at facilities like LEGS (Brookhaven), GRAAL (Grenoble), and CEBAF to determine the transition current $J^\mu$ and identify higher multipole contributions in the presence of the strong $S$-wave transition.
