# Flavor analysis of nucleon electromagnetic form factors

> We have performed an evaluation of the individual up- and down-flavor contributions to the nucleon electromagnetic form factors within a relativistic constituent-quark model. It is found that the theoretical parameter-free predictions agree surprisingly well with recent experimental data from a flavor decomposition of the proton and neutron electromagnetic form factors for momentum transfers up to $Q^2\sim\;$3.5 GeV$^2$. It means that in this regime three-quark valence degrees of freedom dominate and other contributions, such as explicit mesonic effects, can at most play a minor role.

## Key claim

The Goldstone-boson-exchange relativistic constituent-quark model (GBE RCQM) predicts flavor-separated nucleon electromagnetic form factors that agree with phenomenological data up to \(Q^2 \sim 3.5\) GeV\(^2\), but the agreement is qualified by a departure for the \(d\)-flavor ratio \(F_2^d/F_1^d\) starting at \(Q^2 \sim 1.5\) GeV\(^2\), where the model's prediction for the \(d\)-flavor ratio begins to deviate from the data.

## Method

The authors computed the \(u\)- and \(d\)-flavor contributions to the Dirac (\(F_1\)) and Pauli (\(F_2\)) form factors of the proton and neutron using the GBE RCQM, which employs a hyperfine interaction derived from Goldstone-boson exchange (spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry) and a linear confinement potential. The calculations were performed in the framework of Poincare-invariant quantum mechanics using the point form, with the electromagnetic current constructed according to a spectator model that guarantees current conservation. The nucleon wave functions include non-zero orbital angular momenta and mixed-symmetric spatial components, and the matrix elements are strictly frame-independent.

## Key numbers

- The model predictions agree with phenomenological flavor-decomposed data up to \(Q^2 \sim 3.5\) GeV\(^2\).
- The \(d\)-flavor ratio \(F_2^d/F_1^d\) starts to depart from phenomenological data at \(Q^2 \sim 1.5\) GeV\(^2\).
- The functions \(S^q(Q^2) = Q^2 F_2^q / F_1^q\) for \(q = u, d\) show an almost linear rise with increasing \(Q^2\) in the data; the GBE RCQM reproduces this for the \(u\)-flavor but not for the \(d\)-flavor beyond \(Q^2 \sim 1.5\) GeV\(^2\).
- The ratios \(F_i^d / F_i^u\) for \(i = 1, 2\) reasonably follow the phenomenological data, with the model's predictions for \(F_2^d / F_2^u\) falling between two incompatible data sets from Cates (2011) and Diehl (2013).

## Context

This work extends the GBE RCQM predictions for nucleon electromagnetic form factors (Wagenbrunn 2000, Boffi 2001, Berger 2004) to the flavor-separated level, directly comparing with the phenomenological flavor decomposition of Cates (2011), Qattan (2012), and Diehl (2013). The results challenge the interpretation that the faster fall-off of \(d\)-flavor form factors at high \(Q^2\) indicates diquark clustering, since the GBE RCQM (a genuine three-quark model with no diquark configurations) reproduces the same behavior. The work enables further tests of whether three-quark valence degrees of freedom dominate up to \(Q^2 \sim 3.5\) GeV\(^2\), with explicit mesonic effects playing at most a minor role.
