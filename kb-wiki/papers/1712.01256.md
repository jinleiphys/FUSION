# IR properties of chiral effects in pionic matter

> Chiral effects exhibit peculiar universality in idealized theoretical limits. However, they are known to be infrared sensitive and get modified in more realistic settings. In this work, we study how the corresponding conductivities vary with the constituent mass. We concentrate on a pionic realization of chiral effects which provides a better control over infrared properties of the theory. The pionic medium is considered at finite vector and axial isospin chemical potentials in the presence of an external magnetic field. This system supports electric and axial isospin currents along the magnetic field which correspond to chiral magnetic and chiral separation effects. We show that these currents are sensitive to the finite mass of the constituents, but the conductivities follow a simple scaling with the corresponding charge densities as one would expect for polarization effects. It is argued that this relation can capture the dependence of chiral effects on other infrared parameters. Finally, we briefly comment on the realization of the 't Hooft matching condition in pionic media at finite densities.

# IR properties of chiral effects in pionic matter

## Key claim

The chiral magnetic effect (CME) and chiral separation effect (CSE) conductivities in a pionic medium depend on the pion mass only through the corresponding charge densities, following a simple scaling relation analogous to that seen in free fermion systems; this relation holds even in the condensed phase of isospin superfluidity, and the authors argue it can capture the dependence of chiral effects on other infrared parameters.

## Method

The authors use the chiral Lagrangian with a Wess-Zumino-Witten (WZW) term to describe pionic matter at finite vector and axial isospin chemical potentials in an external magnetic field. They compute the anomalous contributions to the electric and axial isospin currents by varying the WZW action with respect to the appropriate gauge fields, then evaluate these currents in the ground state determined by minimizing the effective potential for the pion field, including the effect of a finite pion mass. The mass dependence is studied both in the normal phase and in the condensed (superfluid) phase where the isospin chemical potential exceeds the pion mass.

## Key numbers

- CSE current in the strong field limit for free fermions: \( J_5^i = \frac{\sqrt{\mu^2 - m^2}}{2\pi^2} B^i \), which reduces to \( J_5 = \frac{\mu}{2\pi^2} B \) in the massless limit.
- Isospin density in the condensed phase: \( \rho_{I,V} = f_\pi^2 \mu_{I,V} \left(1 - \frac{m_\pi^4}{\mu_{I,V}^4}\right) \), which goes to zero as \( |\mu_{I,V}| \to m_\pi \).
- CSE axial current in the superfluid phase: \( J_{A,3}^i = \frac{N_c \tr Q}{4\pi^2} \mu_{I,V} \left(1 - \frac{m_\pi^4}{\mu_{I,V}^4}\right) B^i \), vanishing as \( (|\mu_{I,V}| - m_\pi) \to 0 \).
- High-temperature limit of the CSE current (for \( T \gg m_\pi \)): \( J_{A,3}^i = \frac{N_c \mu_{I,V}}{24\pi^2} \frac{T^2}{f_\pi^2} B^i \), using the thermal expectation \( \langle \pi^2 \rangle = T^2/12 \).
- CME current in the massless limit with trivial vacuum: \( J_V^i = \frac{N_c \tr Q}{4\pi^2} \mu_{I,A} B^i \).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of anomalous transport in pionic media by Lublinsky and Zahed (2009), Fukushima and Mameda (2012), and Kalaydzhyan (2014) beyond the exact chiral limit, incorporating a finite pion mass. The authors compare the pionic realization of chiral effects to the well-studied case of a deconfined fermionic plasma, where the CSE current depends on fermion mass only through the fermion number density as shown by Metlitski and Zhitnitsky (2005). The results enable a more realistic treatment of anomalous transport in the confined phase of QCD, relevant for heavy-ion collision phenomenology, and the authors comment on the realization of the 't Hooft matching condition in chiral media, relating it to constraints from ideal hydrodynamics.
