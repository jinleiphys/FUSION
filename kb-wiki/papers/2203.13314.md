# Investigating saturation effects and the virtual pion in leading neutron events at HERA with the dipole model

> We investigate events with very forward neutrons in $ep$ collisions at HERA using impact parameter dependent colour dipole models with and without saturation. This is the first study of the leading neutron process deploying these models. The model predictions are compared with the available HERA measurements for $6<Q^2<100$~GeV$^2$, $70<W<245$~GeV. Our analysis shows that the models exhibit Feynman scaling, independent of $Q^2$. Our results demonstrate that the $W$ and $Q^2$ dependence of the cross section is independent of the presence of a forward neutron as predicted by the limiting fragmentation hypothesis, which is a consequence of Feynman scaling itself. We infer that the HERA leading neutron production inclusive data is insensitive to saturation physics and these cross sections may not be able to distinguish gluon saturation effects in future $ep$ colliders. We provide a good description of the leading neutron structure function $F_2^{LN}$ at small $x$ using an assumption that the small-$x$ structure of protons and pions is universal up to a normalisation. We also show that the observables in the exclusive diffractive measurements with a vector meson in the final state are more sensitive to saturation physics at small $x$ than inclusive measurements. At last, we provide a prediction for the $\hat t$ spectrum in exclusive vector meson production in the dipole model using Yukawa theory to model the virtual pion's spatial wave function.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the Feynman scaling observed in leading neutron production at HERA is not a signature of gluon saturation, contrary to earlier claims by Carvalho, Goncalves, Spiering, and Navarra (2015), but instead arises from the identical asymptotic behavior of the pion and proton structure functions at small x.

## Method

The authors used impact-parameter dependent colour dipole models, specifically the saturated bSat (IP-Sat) model and its linearised version bNonSat without saturation, to calculate the leading neutron structure function F2^LN and the differential cross section for ep to e X n. The dipole-pion cross section was assumed to be proportional to the dipole-proton cross section via a scaling factor Rq, and the pion flux was described using the one-pion exchange approximation with a covariant form factor corrected by a Regge factor. For exclusive J/psi production with a leading neutron, the authors modelled the virtual pion's spatial wave function using Yukawa theory to predict the hat-t spectrum.

## Key numbers

* The analysis covers photon virtualities 6 < Q^2 < 100 GeV^2 and photon-proton centre-of-mass energies 70 < W < 245 GeV, matching the H1 (2014) measurements.
* The pion-proton coupling constant used is g^2_{p pi p}/(4 pi) = 14.4, with a form factor parameter R = 0.6 GeV^{-1} determined from HERA data.
* The scaling factor Rq relating dipole-pion to dipole-proton cross sections was varied between 0.4 and 0.6, with the central value Rq = 0.5 providing a good description of the H1 data.
* The proton transverse profile parameter Bp = 4 +/- 0.4 GeV^{-2} was constrained from exclusive J/psi production at HERA.
* The inclusive DIS cross section normalisation used the parametrisation c = 0.18, d = 0.0481, and Lambda_0 = 0.292 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the impact-parameter dependent dipole model framework, previously applied to inclusive and exclusive processes at HERA by Kowalski (2006), Rezaeian (2012), and Mantysaari (2018), to leading neutron production for the first time. The authors contradict the earlier conclusion by Carvalho, Goncalves, Spiering, and Navarra (2015) that Feynman scaling in leading neutron spectra is associated with gluon saturation, showing instead that both saturated and non-saturated models exhibit the scaling. The results enable future studies of exclusive vector meson production with leading neutrons as a more sensitive probe of saturation effects, and provide predictions for the hat-t spectrum that could be tested at future electron-ion colliders such as the LHeC or FCC.
