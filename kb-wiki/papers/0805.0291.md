# Manifestation of three-body forces in f7/2-shell nuclei

> The traditional nuclear shell model approach is extended to include many-body forces. The empirical Hamiltonian with a three-body force is constructed for the identical nucleons on the 0f7/2 shell. Manifestations of the three-body force in spectra, binding energies, seniority mixing, particle-hole symmetry, electromagnetic and particle transition rates are investigated. It is shown that in addition to the usual expansion of the valence space within the tranditional two-body shell model, the three-body component in the Hamiltonian can be an important part improving the quality of the theoretical approach.

# Manifestation of three-body forces in f7/2-shell nuclei

## Key claim
Three-body forces are shown to be a necessary component for accurately describing nuclear structure in the 0f7/2 shell, beyond what can be achieved by simply expanding the valence space in traditional two-body shell model calculations. The paper demonstrates that a three-body effective interaction can improve agreement with experimental data without requiring the extensive configuration mixing that characterizes large-scale two-body shell model approaches.

## Method
The author constructs an empirical Hamiltonian including one-body, two-body, and three-body terms for identical nucleons in the 0f7/2 shell, using a rotationally-invariant n-body formalism with normalized creation operators coupled to total angular momentum. A full least-square fit is performed to experimental binding energies and excitation energies of states in N=28 isotones (proton valence) and Z=20 isotopes (neutron valence), with the fit being nonlinear and requiring iterations due to seniority mixing introduced by the three-body terms. The resulting parameters are compared with traditional two-body-only fits and with large-scale two-body shell model calculations in expanded model spaces (f7/2p and full fp-shell).

## Key numbers
- RMS deviation for N=28 isotones drops from 120 keV (2Bf7/2) to 80 keV (3Bf7/2) when three-body forces are included
- RMS deviation for Z=20 isotopes drops from 220 keV (2Bf7/2) to 170 keV (3Bf7/2) with three-body forces
- The three-body parameter V7/2^(3) is determined as 55(28) keV for N=28 and 53(70) keV for Z=20
- The energy difference between 4_1^+ and 4_2^+ states in 52Cr is 400 keV experimentally; 2Bf7/2 gives 84 keV, while 3Bf7/2 reproduces the observed splitting
- B(E2) for 4_2→2_1 transition in 52Cr: 0 e^2 fm^4 (2Bf7/2), 56.8 e^2 fm^4 (3Bf7/2), 69±18 e^2 fm^4 (experiment)
- Quadrupole moment Q(2_1^+) in 52Cr: 0 e fm^2 (2Bf7/2), -2.4 e fm^2 (3Bf7/2), -8.2±1.6 e fm^2 (experiment)
- Spectroscopic factor for 4_1^+ state in 51V(3He,d)52Cr: 0.00 (2Bf7/2), 0.63 (3Bf7/2), 0.51 (experiment)

## Context
This work extends the empirical shell model approach dating back to Bacher (1934) and Talmi (1957), and builds on prior studies of three-body forces in nuclei by Eisenstein (1973), Poves (1981), and Hees (1989, 1990). The paper addresses the long-standing problem of seniority mixing in f7/2-shell nuclei, which had previously been attributed to configuration mixing beyond the single-j shell by authors such as Engeland (1966), Auerbach (1967), and Poves (2001). The results suggest that three-body effective interactions can explain seniority mixing and particle-hole symmetry violations without requiring the extensive configuration mixing that creates inconsistencies with g-factor measurements (Speidel, 2000). This enables future phenomenological investigations in other mass regions and motivates further work on renormalization techniques linking fundamental and phenomenological forces.
