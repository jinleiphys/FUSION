# Matter Neutrino Resonance Transitions above a Neutron Star Merger Remnant

> The Matter-Neutrino Resonance (MNR) phenomenon has the potential to significantly alter the flavor content of neutrinos emitted from compact object mergers. We present the first calculations of MNR transitions using neutrino self interaction potentials and matter potentials generated selfconsistently from a dynamical model of a three-dimensional neutron star merger. In the context of the single angle approximation, we find that Symmetric and Standard MNR transitions occur in both normal and inverted hierarchy scenarios. We examine the spatial regions above the merger remnant where propagating neutrinos will encounter the matter neutrino resonance and find that a significant fraction of the neutrinos are likely to undergo MNR transitions.

## Key claim

Using self-consistent neutrino and matter potentials from a 3D dynamical neutron star merger simulation, the authors find that both Symmetric and Standard Matter-Neutrino Resonance (MNR) transitions occur in the single-angle approximation, but the occurrence and type of transition depend sensitively on the specific neutrino trajectory and the spatial variation of the matter potential above the merger remnant.

## Method

The authors took a snapshot at ~40 ms from a 3D Newtonian simulation of a binary neutron star merger remnant (Perego 2014) and computed neutrino flavor evolution along test trajectories using the single-angle approximation. They solved the Schrodinger-like evolution equations for neutrino and antineutrino scattering matrices, incorporating vacuum mixing, matter potentials from the simulation's density and electron fraction profiles, and neutrino self-interaction potentials computed from ray-traced neutrino emission including absorption effects.

## Key numbers

- Merger remnant at t0 ≈ 40 ms has a massive neutron star of ~2.6 solar masses and an accretion disk of ~0.15 solar masses
- Total neutrino luminosity exceeds 10^53 erg s^-1
- Mean energies at t0: ⟨E_νe⟩ ≈ 10.6 MeV, ⟨E_ν̄e⟩ ≈ 15.3 MeV, ⟨E_νx⟩ ≈ 17.3 MeV
- At t0, integrated ν̄e luminosity is ~4 × 10^52 erg s^-1, νe luminosity is ~3 × 10^52 erg s^-1, each heavy flavor νx has L_νx ≈ 9 × 10^51 erg s^-1
- For trajectory one (25° to vertical), Standard MNR transition begins at ~24 km and ends at ~118 km, with final survival probabilities P_νe ≈ 0 and P_ν̄e ≈ 1
- For trajectory two (18° to vertical), Symmetric MNR occurs between ~24 km and ~40 km, with both neutrino and antineutrino survival probabilities going from 1 to 0
- Electron neutrino luminosity can be reduced up to ~35% along escaping paths due to absorption

## Context

This work extends previous studies of MNR transitions (Malkus 2012, Malkus 2014, Malkus 2015) that used phenomenological flat neutrino emission surfaces, by instead using self-consistent density profiles and neutrino emission geometry from a dynamical merger simulation (Perego 2014). The results demonstrate that MNR transitions occur in realistic merger geometries and can significantly alter neutrino flavor content, which has implications for nucleosynthesis in neutrino-driven winds and for interpreting potential neutrino signals from mergers.
