# On the Fractional Quark-Antiquark Confinement and Symplectic Quantum Mechanics

> Using the formalism of generalized fractional derivatives, a two-dimensional non-relativistic meson system is studied. The mesons are interacting by a Cornell potential. The system is formulated in the domain of the symplectic quantum mechanics by means of the generalized fractional Nikiforov-Uvarov method. The corresponding Wigner function and the energy eigenvalues are then derived. The effect of fractional parameters $α$ and $β$ with the ground state solution is analyzed through the Wigner function for the charm-anticharm, bottom-antibottom and $b\overline{c}$ mesons. ## Key claim
Using generalized fractional derivatives within symplectic quantum mechanics, the authors derive energy eigenvalues and Wigner functions for heavy mesons interacting via a Cornell potential, claiming improved agreement with experimental mass spectra compared to prior theoretical models, but only for the specific fractional parameter values α = 0.155 and β = 0.5, which are chosen to satisfy a reality condition on the energy eigenvalues.

## Method
The authors formulate a two-dimensional non-relativistic meson system in phase space using symplectic quantum mechanics, where the Cornell potential is expanded to second order around a point q₀. They apply the generalized fractional Nikiforov-Uvarov method to solve the resulting fractional symplectic Schrödinger equation, deriving energy eigenvalues and the Wigner function for the ground state. Mass spectra are then computed from the energy eigenvalues using the relation M = m_q + m_anti-q + E', with quark masses taken from the literature.

## Key numbers
- For the 1S-state c anti-c meson: computed mass 3.0966 GeV, compared to experimental value 3.097 GeV (difference 0.0004 GeV)
- For the 1S-state b anti-b meson: computed mass 9.4564 GeV, compared to experimental value 9.4603 GeV (difference 0.0039 GeV)
- For the 1S-state b anti-c meson: computed mass 6.2730 GeV, compared to experimental value 6.277 GeV (difference 0.004 GeV)
- Total relative error across all three mesons: 0.03%
- Fractional parameters used: α = 0.155, β = 0.5
- Reduced masses: m_c anti-c = 0.73 GeV, m_b anti-b = 2.34 GeV, m_b anti-c = 1.11 GeV
- Confinement parameters for c anti-c: λ = 0.3093 GeV², σ = 0.1130 GeV³
- Confinement parameters for b anti-b: λ = 0.2370 GeV², σ = 0.1185 GeV³
- Confinement parameters for b anti-c: λ = 0.1721 GeV², σ = 0.0488 GeV³
- Maximum relative distances for quark-antiquark interaction: 4.077 GeV⁻¹ (c anti-c), 2.58 GeV⁻¹ (b anti-b), 2.15 GeV⁻¹ (b anti-c)

## Context
This work extends prior studies of heavy quarkonium using the Cornell potential and symplectic quantum mechanics, specifically improving upon the results of Abu-Shady (2022) which used similar methods but with different fractional parameters (α = 0.84, β = 1) and obtained masses of 3.074 GeV for c anti-c and 9.465 GeV for b anti-b. The present results also improve upon the predictions of Kumar (2020), mabu1 (2018), and rluz (2022) for the 1S-state masses. The approach enables future studies of excited states and thermodynamic properties of heavy mesons within the fractional symplectic framework, and may be extended to other quarkonium systems or to include temperature effects.
