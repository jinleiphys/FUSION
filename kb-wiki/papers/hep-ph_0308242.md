# Magnetic moments of exotic pentaquarks in the chiral quark-soliton model

> We investigate the magnetic moments of the baryon antidecuplet within the framework of the chiral quark-soliton model in the chiral limit in a ``\emph{model-independent}'' approach. Sum rules for the magnetic moments are derived. The magnetic moment of $Θ^{+}$ is found to be about $0.2\sim 0.3 μ_{N}$.

# Magnetic moments of exotic pentaquarks in the chiral quark-soliton model

## Key claim
The magnetic moment of the exotic pentaquark Θ⁺ is predicted to be about 0.2–0.3 μ_N, which is unexpectedly small, almost an order of magnitude smaller than magnetic moments of charge Q=1 baryons in the octet and decuplet, but this prediction depends on an unknown parameter v₂ that must be estimated from model calculations rather than fixed by experimental data alone.

## Method
The authors extend a "model-independent" approach within the chiral quark-soliton model (χQSM) in the chiral limit, where the collective magnetic moment operator is parametrized by three dynamical variables v₁, v₂, v₃. Two linear combinations of these variables are fitted to experimental octet magnetic moments, while the third (v₂) is taken from numerical model calculations of the soliton inertia parameters. The collective matrix elements for the antidecuplet are computed using SU(3) Clebsch-Gordan coefficients and the known antidecuplet wave functions.

## Key numbers
- Magnetic moment of Θ⁺: 0.2–0.3 μ_N (range from fits I and II, with v₂ ≈ 5 from model calculations)
- Fit I parameters: v = −0.331, w = 0.037 (from proton and neutron magnetic moments as input)
- Fit II parameters: v = −0.268, w = 0.060 (from average of octet magnetic moments, independent of linear m_s corrections)
- Model calculation parameters: v = −0.264, w = 0.029, v₂ ∼ 5
- For fit I, μ_Θ⁺ = 0.92 − v₂/8; for fit II, μ_Θ⁺ = 0.82 − v₂/8; for χQSM, μ_Θ⁺ = 0.75 − v₂/8
- Even with 50% error on v₂, largest possible μ_Θ⁺ from fit I with v₂ = 2.5 is 0.61 μ_N
- Proton magnetic moment from fit I: 2.79 μ_N (input); from fit II: 2.39 μ_N
- Neutron magnetic moment from fit I: −1.91 μ_N (input); from fit II: −1.49 μ_N
- Δ⁺⁺ magnetic moment: 4.52 μ_N (experiment), 5.52 μ_N (fit I), 4.92 μ_N (fit II), 4.47 μ_N (χQSM)
- Ω⁻ magnetic moment: −2.02 μ_N (experiment), −2.76 μ_N (fit I), −2.46 μ_N (fit II), −2.23 μ_N (χQSM)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous "model-independent" analysis of octet and decuplet magnetic moments (Kim 1997, Kim 1998) to the baryon antidecuplet, which contains the exotic pentaquark Θ⁺ discovered by the LEPS collaboration (Nakano 2003) and the Ξ₃/₂ reported by NA49. The predicted small magnetic moments are consistent with the recent finding of Zhu (2003) using QCD sum rules. The results enable future studies of Θ⁺ production via photo-reactions, where the magnetic moment is a crucial input, and provide sum rules analogous to the Coleman-Glashow relations (Coleman 1961) that can be tested experimentally.
