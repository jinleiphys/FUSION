# Parity-violating asymmetry of $W$ bosons produced in $p$-$p$ collisions

> The parity-violating asymmetry is an ideal tool to study the quark helicity distribution in the proton. We study the parity-violating asymmetry of $W^{\pm}$ bosons produced by longitudinally polarized $p$-$p$ collision in RHIC, based on predictions of quark distributions of the proton in the SU(6) quark-spectator-diquark model and a perturbative QCD based counting rule analysis. We find that the two models give nearly equal asymmetry for $W^+$ but that for $W^-$ quite different. Therefore future experiments on such quantity can help to clarify different predictions of the value $Δd(x)/d(x)$ at $x \to 1$ in the proton.

## Key claim
The parity-violating asymmetry of \(W^{-}\) bosons produced in longitudinally polarized \(p\)-\(p\) collisions at RHIC can distinguish between two conflicting predictions for the ratio \(\Delta d(x)/d(x)\) at \(x \to 1\): the pQCD counting rule analysis predicts \(\Delta d(x)/d(x) \to 1\) while the SU(6) quark-spectator-diquark model predicts \(\Delta d(x)/d(x) \to -1/3\), and the two models give nearly equal asymmetry for \(W^{+}\) but quite different asymmetry for \(W^{-}\).

## Method
The authors calculated the parity-violating asymmetry \(A_L^{W^{\pm}}\) as a function of rapidity \(y_W\) and momentum fraction \(x_1\) using two sets of quark helicity distributions: one from the SU(6) quark-spectator-diquark model and one from a pQCD based counting rule analysis. They used CTEQ LO parametrization (CTEQ5 set 3) for unpolarized quark distributions at \(Q = M_W = 80.419\) GeV, and computed the asymmetries at center-of-mass energies \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) GeV and \(\sqrt{s} = 500\) GeV, corresponding to RHIC conditions.

## Key numbers
- \(M_W = 80.419\) GeV: the mass of the \(W\) boson used as the scale for quark distribution parametrization.
- \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) GeV and \(\sqrt{s} = 500\) GeV: the two center-of-mass energies at which \(A_L^{W^{\pm}}(y_W)\) and \(A_L^{W^{\pm}}(x_1)\) were calculated.
- The pQCD based analysis predicts \(\Delta d(x)/d(x) \to 1\) at \(x \to 1\); the SU(6) quark-spectator-diquark model predicts \(\Delta d(x)/d(x) \to -1/3\) at \(x \to 1\).
- At large \(y_W\), \(A_L^{W^+} \approx \Delta u(x)/u(x)\) and \(A_L^{W^-} \approx \Delta d(x)/d(x)\).

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of parity-violating asymmetry in \(W\) production at RHIC (Soffer and collaborators, 1994; Bourrely and Soffer, 1995) by comparing two specific models for quark helicity distributions: the SU(6) quark-spectator-diquark model (Ma, 1991; Ma and collaborators, 2000) and a pQCD based counting rule analysis (Farrar and Jackson, 1975; Brodsky and collaborators, 1995). The authors note that inclusive DIS experiments (E155, HERMES) cannot separate quark and antiquark helicities, and semi-inclusive DIS (JLab Hall A) depends on fragmentation models; \(W\) production at RHIC avoids these limitations. The results enable future RHIC experiments (PHENIX, STAR) to test the large-\(x\) behavior of \(\Delta d(x)/d(x)\) and thereby discriminate between the two theoretical predictions.
