# Denominator Regularization in Quantum Field Theory

> We propose a novel regularization scheme in quantum field theory, denominator regularization (den reg). As simple to apply as dimensional regularization, and similarly compatible with a minimal subtraction renormalization scheme, den reg manifestly 1) maintains Lorentz invariance, 2) maintains gauge invariance, 3) maintains supersymmetry, 4) correctly predicts the axial anomaly, and 5) yields Green functions that satisfy the Callan-Symanzik equation. Den reg also naturally enables regularization in asymmetric spacetimes, finite spacetimes, curved spacetimes, and in thermal field theory.

# Denominator Regularization in Quantum Field Theory

## Key claim

Denominator regularization (den reg) is proposed as a novel regularization scheme that is as simple to apply as dimensional regularization and manifestly maintains Lorentz invariance, gauge invariance, supersymmetry, correctly predicts the axial anomaly, and yields Green functions satisfying the Callan-Symanzik equation, while also naturally enabling regularization in asymmetric, finite, curved, and thermal spacetimes. The scheme relies on analytically continuing the power of the denominator after combining with Feynman parameters, rather than the number of spacetime dimensions, and introduces uniquely specified coefficient functions \(f_{(n,p)}(\epsilon)\) that are fixed by requiring the Laurent expansion of the amplitude has only a simple pole at \(\epsilon=0\).

## Method

Denominators are first combined using Feynman parameters; then the overall power of the single denominator is analytically continued from \(n\) to \(n+\epsilon\), with \(\epsilon\) taken sufficiently large to ensure UV convergence. A fictitious scale \(\mu\) is introduced to preserve dimensions, and an overall coefficient function \(f_{(n,p)}(\epsilon)\) is included that depends on the original denominator power \(n\) and the superficial degree of divergence \(p\), uniquely specified by requiring the Laurent expansion in \(\epsilon\) has only a simple pole at \(\epsilon=0\) including in the massless limit. The method is applied to scalar \(\phi^4\) theory at NLO, QED and Yang-Mills gauge boson self-energies at one loop, and the axial anomaly triangle diagrams, with all manipulations performed in fixed four spacetime dimensions.

## Key numbers

- In \(\phi^4\) theory at NLO, the regulated integral yields \(\int k_E^3 dk_E \frac{\mu^{2\epsilon}}{(k_E^2+\Delta^2)^{2+\epsilon}} = \frac12 \frac{1}{\epsilon(1+\epsilon)} \left(\frac{\mu^2}{\Delta^2}\right)^\epsilon\) for the logarithmically divergent case.
- The coefficient function for logarithmically divergent integrals is fixed as \(f_{(2,0)}(\epsilon) \equiv \Gamma^{-2}(1-\epsilon)\).
- For the quadratically divergent QED photon self-energy, the integral \(\int \ell_E^3 d\ell_E \frac{\mu^{2\epsilon}\ell_E^2}{(\ell_E^2+\Delta^2)^{2+\epsilon}} = -\frac{\Delta^2}{(1-\epsilon)\epsilon(1+\epsilon)}\left(\frac{\mu^2}{\Delta^2}\right)^\epsilon\) captures the quadratic divergence via the \(1/(1-\epsilon)\) pole.
- The coefficient function for quadratically divergent integrals is fixed as \(f_{(2,2)}(\epsilon) \equiv (1-\epsilon)\Gamma^{-2}(1-\epsilon)\).
- For the axial anomaly, the linearly divergent integral yields \(\tilde I_1 \equiv f_{(3,1)}(\epsilon)\left\{ \frac{x}{2} - \frac{x}{(1+\epsilon)(2+\epsilon)}\left[ \frac{3}{\epsilon} + \frac{2xy p\cdot k}{\Delta^2} \right]\left( \frac{\mu^2}{\Delta^2}\right)^\epsilon \right\}\), with \(f_{(3,1)} \equiv \Gamma^{-1}(1-\epsilon)\Gamma^{-1}(2-\epsilon)\).
- The axial anomaly result is \(i q_\mu i\mathcal M^{\mu\nu\lambda} = \frac{e^2}{4\pi^2}\epsilon^{\alpha\lambda\beta\nu}k_\alpha p_\beta + \mathcal O(\epsilon)\) for \(m=0\), matching the standard result.
- The shift integral \(S^\mu(a)\) in den reg gives \(\frac{2\pi^2}{(2\pi)^4}\frac14 a^\mu + \mathcal O(\epsilon)\), while dimensional regularization gives identically zero for any \(d<4\).

## Context

This work extends the precursor application of den reg to finite system size corrections in \(\phi^4\) theory by Horowitz (2022) and addresses shortcomings of existing regularization schemes: momentum cutoff breaks Lorentz invariance and violates Ward identities (Peskin and Schroeder, 1995); Pauli-Villars is cumbersome for gauge invariance (Bjorken and Drell, 1965; Slavnov, 1971); dimensional regularization breaks supersymmetry (Siegel, 1979) and unitarity (Hogervorst et al., 2015), has fundamental issues with \(\gamma^5\) and the Levi-Civita symbol (t Hooft and Veltman, 1972; Breitenlohner and Maison, 1977), and cannot be generalized to asymmetric or curved spacetimes; dimensional reduction fails to give the correct axial anomaly or breaks gauge invariance (Stockinger, 2005); zeta and operator regularization violate BRS symmetry (Rebhan, 1988); and analytic regularization violates gauge invariance (Rebhan, 1988). Den reg enables future applications to Chern-Simons theory, curved spacetime quantities (Hawking, 1976), finite size corrections to running couplings (Horowitz, 2022), and critical exponents in asymmetric systems, with potential relevance to detecting the QCD critical endpoint in heavy-ion collisions (Stephanov, 1998; Luo and Xu, 2017).
