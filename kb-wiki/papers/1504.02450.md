# Transition electromagnetic form factor and current conservation in the Bethe-Salpeter approach

> The transition form factor for electrodisintegration of a two-body bound system is calculated in the Bethe-Salpeter framework. For the initial (bound) and the final (scattering) states, we use our solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space which were first obtained recently. The gauge invariance, which manifests itself in the conservation of the transition electromagnetic current Jq = 0, is studied numerically. It results from a cancellation between the plane wave and the final state interaction contributions. This cancellation takes place only if the initial bound state BS amplitude, the final scattering state and the operator of electromagnetic current are strictly consistent with each other, that is if they are found in the same dynamical framework. A reliable result for the transition form factor can be obtained in this case only.

## Key claim

The transition electromagnetic current for electrodisintegration of a two-body bound system is conserved only if the initial bound state Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, the final scattering state amplitude, and the electromagnetic current operator are all strictly consistent with each other, i.e., found in the same dynamical framework; otherwise, the extracted form factor is invalid even if extracted from the conserved part of the current.

## Method

The authors computed the transition form factor for electrodisintegration of a two-body bound system of spinless particles using the Bethe-Salpeter (BS) framework in Minkowski space, with both bound and scattering state solutions obtained from the BS equation with a one-boson exchange ladder kernel. They separated the transition current into a final state interaction (FSI) contribution and a plane wave (PW) contribution, calculated each numerically by treating the singular propagator poles via principal value subtraction and delta-function integration, and then summed them to obtain the full form factor and the non-conserved form factor F'(Q^2).

## Key numbers

* Binding energy B = 0.01 m (initial state mass M = 1.99 m) and final state relative momentum p_s = 0.1 m (final state mass M' ≈ 2.00998 m) were used for the main numerical example.
* For Q^2 = 0.01 with p_s = 0.1 m, the total non-conserved form factor F'_tot = (5.490e-04 + i 2.304e-04), compared to the conserved form factor F = (2.948e-03 - i 1.571e-03).
* For Q^2 = 0.5 with p_s = 0.1 m, F'_tot = (1.047e-02 - i 7.653e-03) while F = (1.047e-02 - i 7.653e-03) (values appear identical in text).
* For Q^2 = 1.5 with p_s = 0.1 m, F'_tot was one order of magnitude smaller than F and two orders of magnitude smaller than the individual FSI and PW contributions.
* For Q^2 = 5 with p_s = 0.1 m, the cancellation worsened but F'_tot remained a few times smaller than the individual FSI and PW contributions.
* For the larger final relative momentum p_s = 0.5 m, final state mass M' = 2.236 m was used.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior development of Minkowski-space BS solutions for bound and scattering states (Carbonell and Karmanov, 2013; 2014) and their earlier calculation of the elastic EM form factor (Carbonell, Karmanov, and Mangin-Brinet, 2013). It numerically confirms the general expectation discussed by Adam, Van Orden, and Gross (1990s) that internal consistency between states, currents, and the dynamical equation is required for gauge invariance. The results enable reliable calculation of inelastic transition form factors within the BS framework and provide a strong numerical test for the consistency of BS solutions.
