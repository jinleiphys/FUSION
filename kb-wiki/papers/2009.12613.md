# Effect of Inner Crust EoS on Neutron star properties

> The neutron star maximum mass and the radius are investigated within the framework of the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model. The variation in the radius at the canonical mass, $R_{1.4}$, using different inner crust equation of state (EoS) with different symmetry energy slope parameter is studied. It is found that although the NS maximum mass and the corresponding radius do not vary much with different inner crust EoSs, the radius and the tidal deformability at 1.4$M_{\odot}$ vary with the different choice of crust EoS and variation of about 1-2 km is seen in the radius at the canonical mass. For non-unified EoSs, the crust with a low symmetry energy slope parameter produces a low NS radius at the canonical mass. The properties of maximally rotating neutron stars are also studied. The variation in the radius of rotating star at the canonical mass 1.4$M_{\odot}$ is also seen with the slope parameter. Similar to the static neutron star, the radius at 1.4$M_{\odot}$ of rotating neutron star is affected by slope parameter of the inner crust. Other important quantities like moment of inertia, frequency, rotational kinetic energy to gravitational energy ratio are also calculated. The variation in these quantities with the crust slope parameter is found to be more proportional to the mass and the radius of NS.

## Key claim

The neutron star maximum mass and corresponding radius do not vary much with different inner crust equations of state, but the radius and tidal deformability at the canonical mass of 1.4 solar masses vary by about 1-2 km depending on the choice of inner crust EoS, with the symmetry energy slope parameter of the inner crust being the key factor.

## Method

The authors constructed unified and non-unified equations of state by combining the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland outer crust EoS with seven different inner crust EoSs (NL3, TM1, NL3ωρ, FSU, IU-FSU, DD-ME2, DD-MEδ) and five different core EoSs (NL3, TM1, IU-FSU, IOPB-I, G3) within the relativistic mean-field and effective relativistic mean-field frameworks. The inner crust was described using the Thomas-Fermi approximation within the same RMF model, and the crust-core transition boundary was determined using the thermodynamic stability method. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved for static neutron stars, and the RNS code was used for maximally rotating neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- For NL3 core with different inner crust EoSs, the maximum mass varies from 2.764 to 2.787 solar masses, with corresponding radius from 13.027 to 13.378 km
- For NL3 core, the radius at 1.4 solar masses varies from 13.853 to 14.496 km with inner crust, compared to 14.987 km without inner crust
- For TM1 core, maximum mass ranges from 2.141 to 2.177 solar masses, radius at 1.4 solar masses from 13.572 km (unified TM1 crust) to 15.549 km (no inner crust)
- For IU-FSU core, maximum mass ranges from 1.931 to 1.940 solar masses, radius at 1.4 solar masses from 12.295 to 12.778 km
- For IOPB-I core, maximum mass varies from 2.141 to 2.156 solar masses, radius at 1.4 solar masses from 13.118 to 13.508 km
- For G3 core, radius at 1.4 solar masses varies from 12.436 to 14.447 km
- For maximally rotating stars with NL3 core, radius at 1.4 solar masses ranges from 19.5 to 21.4 km
- The symmetry energy slope parameter L_sym for the inner crust EoSs ranges from 47.2 MeV (IU-FSU) to 118.3 MeV (NL3)

## Context

This work extends studies by PhysRevC.94.035804 and PhysRevC.94.015808 which showed that inner crust EoS choice and matching to core EoS is critical, with variations larger than 0.5 km obtained for a 1.4 solar mass star. The results are consistent with constraints from gravitational wave events GW170817 and GW190814, and with NICER measurements of pulsar PSR J0030+0451 by Riley (2019) and Miller (2019). The finding that unified EoSs (where inner crust and core have matching symmetry energy slope parameters) produce smaller radii at canonical mass enables more precise modeling of neutron star structure and helps constrain the nuclear symmetry energy through combined mass-radius-tidal deformability measurements.
