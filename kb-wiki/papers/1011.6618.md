# $q{\bar q}$ Potential at Finite T and Weak Coupling in ${\cal N}=4$

> We compute the potential between a $q{\bar q}$ singlet for ${\cal N}=4$ $SUSY$ with gauge group $SU(N)$ at finite temperature $T$, large distances $rT \gg 1$ and weak coupling $g$. As a first step, we only consider at the electric modes and we compute the Debye mass $m_D$ where we find that each of the $8N$ bosonic degrees of freedom contributes to $m_D^2$ with $\frac{1}{6}g^2 T^2$ while each of the $8N$ fermionic degrees of freedom contributes with $\frac{1}{12}g^2 T^2$ yielding to $m_D^2=2Ng^2T^2$. Then, motivated by results obtained in the literature from both, a weak coupling approach for $QCD$ and a large coupling investigation of ${\cal N}=4$ $SUSY$ through $AdS/CFT$, we attempt to include magnetic mode corrections.

## Key claim

The paper computes the heavy-quark singlet potential in \({\cal N}=4\) supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory at finite temperature and weak coupling, finding that at sufficiently large distances the potential falls off as \(1/r^4\) and is repulsive, but the author notes that this repulsive sign disagrees with the attractive result obtained from AdS/CFT at strong coupling, and the inclusion of scalar contributions did not alter the sign.

## Method

The author computes the Debye mass \(m_D\) from the static limit of the gluon self-energy \(\Pi^{00}\) at one-loop order, summing contributions from gauge bosons, four Weyl fermions (one gaugino and three adjoint fermions), and six real scalars in the \({\cal N}=4\) theory. Using the temporal axial gauge and the linear response formalism, the singlet potential is obtained by Fourier transforming the static propagator; magnetic mode corrections are then included by expanding \(\Pi^{00}\) to linear order in \(|\mathbf{q}|\) and applying the technique of Gale (1987) originally developed for QCD.

## Key numbers

* Debye mass squared: \(m_D^2 = 2 N g^2 T^2\), where each of the \(8N\) bosonic degrees of freedom contributes \(\frac{1}{6} g^2 T^2\) and each of the \(8N\) fermionic degrees of freedom contributes \(\frac{1}{12} g^2 T^2\).
* Yukawa potential at leading order: \(V(r) = -\frac{g^2 N}{4\pi} \frac{1}{r} e^{-m_D r}\).
* Large-distance potential including magnetic corrections: \(V(r) = \frac{1}{(4\pi)^2} \frac{1}{T^3 r^4}\), which is independent of \(N\) and \(g\).

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD calculation of the heavy-quark potential at finite temperature by Gale (1987) to the \({\cal N}=4\) supersymmetric theory, finding the same power-law fall-off \(1/r^4\) at large distances. The result is compared with the strong-coupling AdS/CFT calculation of Albacete (2008, 2009), which found the same power-law fall-off but an attractive rather than repulsive potential; the author notes that the sign discrepancy remains unresolved. The calculation enables further comparison between weak-coupling and strong-coupling results for \({\cal N}=4\) SYM observables relevant to the quark-gluon plasma.
