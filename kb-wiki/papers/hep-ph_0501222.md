# Production of Forward Rapidity Photons in High Energy Heavy Ion Collisions

> We consider production of prompt photons in high energy gold-gold and deuteron-gold collisions in the forward rapidity region of RHIC ($y \sim 3.8$). In this kinematics, the projectile partons typically have large $x_{bj}$ while the target partons are mostly at very small $x_{bj}$ so that the primary partonic collisions involve valence quarks from the projectile and gluons from the target. We take the target nucleus to be a Color Glass Condensate while the projectile deuteron or nucleus is treated as a dilute system of partons. We show that the photon production cross section can be written as a convolution of a quark-nucleus scattering cross section, involving a quark anti-quark dipole, with the Leading Order quark-photon fragmentation function. We consider different models of the quark anti-quark dipole and show that measurement of photons in the forward rapidity region at RHIC can distinguish between different parameterizations of the dipole cross section as well as help clarify the role of parton coalescence models in hadron production at RHIC.

## Key claim

Measurement of forward-rapidity photons in deuteron-gold and gold-gold collisions at RHIC can distinguish between different parameterizations of the quark-antiquark dipole cross section (IIM and KKT), but the paper's conclusions are conditional on the assumption that the Color Glass Condensate formalism is the dominant physics in this kinematic region, and that parton coalescence models are not required.

## Method

The author calculates the prompt photon production cross section in the forward rapidity region (y ~ 3.8) of RHIC by treating the target nucleus as a Color Glass Condensate and the projectile (deuteron or gold) as a dilute system of partons. The cross section is expressed as a convolution of a quark-nucleus scattering cross section (involving a quark-antiquark dipole) with the Leading Order quark-photon fragmentation function, after isolating and absorbing a collinear divergence. The calculation uses two phenomenological parameterizations of the dipole cross section (IIM and KKT) and includes nuclear shadowing effects for the gold projectile via the EKS98 parameterization, while limiting the analysis to central collisions (0-5%).

## Key numbers

- Forward rapidity region considered: y ~ 3.8
- Dipole profile evaluated at x_g = 1.6 x 10^{-4} (corresponding to k_t = 1.5 GeV at y_photon = 3.8)
- In forward proton-proton collisions at RHIC, direct photons contribute about 10-15% at k_t = 1 GeV, and their contribution equals that of fragmentation photons at k_t = 4 GeV
- Anti-shadowing of quarks and anti-quarks in the projectile gold nucleus can be as big as 10% in the large x region

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate formalism for hadron production in forward rapidity (previously applied by Dima et al. and others to describe RHIC data) to photon production, which has the advantage that photons do not interact strongly after production. The paper contrasts the IIM dipole parameterization (fit to HERA DIS data on proton targets) with the KKT parameterization (fit to RHIC particle production data), noting that the Golec-Biernat-Wusthoff parameterization lacks the correct anomalous dimension in the low density region. The results enable future experimental tests at RHIC to determine whether gluon saturation or parton coalescence models (such as those by Rudi et al.) dominate forward rapidity particle production.
