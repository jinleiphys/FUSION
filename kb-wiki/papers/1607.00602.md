# Single inclusive hadron production in pA collisions at NLO

> We study single inclusive forward hadron production in high energy proton-nucleus collisions at next-to-leading order in the Color Glass Condensate framework. Recent studies have shown that the next-to-leading order corrections to this process are large and negative at large transverse momentum, leading to negative cross sections. We propose to overcome this difficulty by introducing an explicit rapidity factorization scale when subtracting the rapidity divergence into the evolution of the target.

# Single inclusive hadron production in pA collisions at NLO

## Key claim

The authors propose that introducing an explicit rapidity factorization scale when subtracting the rapidity divergence in the Color Glass Condensate NLO calculation can cure the problem of negative cross sections at large transverse momentum, but the results remain highly sensitive to the choice of this scale within its natural range.

## Method

The authors compute the NLO multiplicity for single inclusive forward hadron production in proton-nucleus collisions, considering only the q→q channel for simplicity. They introduce a rapidity factorization scale ξf as a lower cutoff in the ξ-integral when subtracting the rapidity divergence into the Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution of the target dipole cross section, analogous to how collinear divergences are absorbed in DGLAP evolution. They test this procedure using the Golec-Biernat and Wüsthoff (GBW) model for the dipole cross section and propose a ξf(k⊥) function based on light cone energy ordering to interpolate between small and large transverse momentum regimes.

## Key numbers

- At √s = 200 GeV, αs = 0.2, μ² = 10 GeV², and yh = 3.2, with ξf = 0 the NLO multiplicity becomes negative for p⊥ values larger than about 2 GeV
- The GBW model parameters used: Qs0 = 1 GeV, x0 = 3.04 × 10⁻⁴, λ = 0.288, c = 0.56
- With the choice xg/xf = 0.5, the range of positivity of the multiplicity is extended significantly compared to xg/xf = 1 or 2

## Context

This work extends the NLO calculations of single inclusive hadron production in pA collisions by Chirilli, Xiao, and Yuan (2011, 2012), where the first numerical implementation by Stasto, Xiao, and Zaslavsky (2013) showed that NLO corrections become large and negative at high transverse momentum. The authors propose an alternative solution to this problem compared to earlier proposals by Kang et al. (2014), Altinoluk et al. (2014), and Watanabe et al. (2015). The approach enables further study of how to properly factorize rapidity divergences in NLO calculations within the Color Glass Condensate framework, though the authors note that using a dipole cross section obtained from solving the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation rather than the GBW model should reduce the sensitivity to NLO corrections.
