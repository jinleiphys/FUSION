# Covariance of Antiproton Yield and Source Size in Nuclear Collisions

> We confront for the first time the widely-held belief that combined event-by-event information from quark gluon plasma signals can reduce the ambiguity of the individual signals. We illustrate specifically how the measured antiproton yield combined with the information from pion-pion HBT correlations can be used to identify novel event classes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that measuring the event-by-event covariance of antiproton yield and pion HBT transverse radius can reveal the presence of a new event class (e.g., quark-gluon plasma formation) even when the event-averaged values of each observable individually appear smooth and ambiguous. The authors stress that this utility depends on the existence of event classes with genuinely different signal values, and that correlating non-signals will not help.

## Method

The authors construct a thermal model of hadron production in the Bjorken hydrodynamic framework, assuming local thermal equilibrium near midrapidity. They derive analytic expressions for the variances and covariance of antiproton yield and HBT transverse radius, accounting for fluctuations in volume, temperature, and net baryon number. They then implement a Monte Carlo event generator that simulates two-event-class scenarios (hadronic and plasma) for Au+Au collisions at RHIC, incorporating geometric centrality dependence, thermodynamic fluctuations, and experimental resolution effects.

## Key numbers

- Antiproton rapidity density for hadronic class: \(\overline{n}_h = 40\), consistent with HIJING, HIJING/\(B\overline{B}\), and RQMD predictions (which give 60, 20, and 20 respectively)
- Antiproton rapidity density for plasma class in central collisions: \(\overline{n}_q \approx 26\) (a conservative 30% enhancement over the hadronic value)
- HBT transverse radius for hadronic class: \(r_h = 6\) fm, based on geometric radius of the overlap area
- HBT transverse radius for plasma class: \(r_q \approx 9\) fm (a 50% increase over the hadronic value)
- Total charged particle multiplicity at central collisions: \(N_{\rm tot}(0) = 2100\) from HIJING simulation in STAR acceptance
- Experimental fluctuation in \(R_T\): \(\Delta R/R \sim 10\%\), in accord with STAR feasibility studies
- For scenario I (Pangloss), the plasma fraction transitions from zero to one within \(\sim 1\) fm of impact parameter \(b_0 = 6\) fm
- For scenario II, plasma events appear in up to 25% of the most central collisions for \(b < 3\) fm

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of event-by-event fluctuations in heavy ion collisions, building on the thermal fluctuation formalism of Landau and Lifshitz (Statistical Physics). It directly addresses the conjecture underlying the PHENIX and STAR experiments at RHIC that correlating multiple signals can reduce ambiguity. The authors specifically reference the work of Stephanov, Rajagopal, and Shuryak (1998) on critical fluctuations, and the HBT radius predictions of Pratt and Bertsch (1984, 1986, 1988). The paper enables future experimental searches for quark-gluon plasma formation at RHIC by providing a concrete observable (the covariance) that can be measured with high statistics, and suggests that an antiproton trigger could be used to gather a statistically significant sample of candidate plasma events for further study.
