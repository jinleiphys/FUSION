# Deconfinement transition in the revolving bag model

> Based on the bag model, we revisit the deconfinement phase transition under rotation. On top of the usual rotational energy for noninteracting particles, we perturbatively analyze the revolution effect of the hadron bag, i.e., of the potential confining quarks. The revolution effect can be phenomenologically translated into the rotational correction to the QCD vacuum energy or the gluon condensate. We demonstrate that if the revolution effect is (is not) taken into account, the transition temperature increases (decreases) as the angular velocity increased. The `revolving bag model' provides a feasible explanation of the recent lattice simulations, contrary to effective models, showing that rotation favors the confined phase.

## Key claim
The paper claims that when the "revolution effect" of the hadron bag (the rotational correction to the confining potential) is included in the bag model, the deconfinement transition temperature increases with angular velocity, consistent with recent lattice QCD simulations; without this effect, the transition temperature decreases with rotation, as predicted by standard effective models.

## Method
The authors construct a "revolving bag model" by embedding a spherical MIT bag into a rotating cylindrical system with rigid angular velocity. They compute the pressures of noninteracting quark, gluon, and pion gases in the finite-size cylinder using discretized transverse momenta from Dirichlet boundary conditions. For the confined quarks inside the revolving bag, they treat the rotational Hamiltonian as a perturbation (with conditions ωR_b ≪ 1 and ωρ ≪ 1), computing first-order and second-order energy corrections via degenerate perturbation theory. The second-order correction to the ground-state quark energy modifies the bag constant, which is then used in the pressure balance equation to determine the critical temperature.

## Key numbers
- The second-order energy correction for the two unperturbed ground states is E_g^(2) = -α/R_b (ωρ)^2 with α ≃ 1.07.
- The bag constant for ω=0 is B_0^(1/4) ≃ 200 MeV, obtained with parameters R = 10 fm, M_p = 1 GeV, and C = 2.51.
- The critical temperature at ω=0 is T_c(ω=0) ≃ 146 MeV (slightly above the Stefan-Boltzmann limit value of ≃ 144 MeV due to finite-size effects).
- The system radius is set to R = 10 fm, and the proton mass is M_p = 1 GeV.

## Context
This work addresses a discrepancy between effective model predictions (which find that rotation decreases the deconfinement temperature) and lattice QCD simulations by Braguta et al. (2020-2022) and Yang et al. (2023), which indicate that rotation favors confinement. The revolving bag model extends the standard MIT bag model of Chodos et al. (1974) by incorporating the rotational response of the confining potential itself, unlike other effective models where interaction parameters are fixed. The result suggests that the gluon condensate (the QCD vacuum energy) carries a large positive moment of inertia, which future nonperturbative analyses and field-theoretical perturbation theory under rotation (Mameda, in preparation) should investigate further.
