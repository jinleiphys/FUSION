# Kaon Condensation in Neutron Stars with Skyrme-Hartree-Fock Models

> We investigate nuclear matter equations of state in neutron star with kaon condensation. It is generally known that the existence of kaons in neutron star makes the equation of state soft so that the maximum mass of neutron star is not likely to be greater than 2.0 $M_{\odot}$, the maximum mass constrained by current observations. With existing Skyrme force model parameters, we calculate nuclear equations of state and check the possibility of kaon condensation in the core of neutron stars. The results show that even with the kaon condensation, the nuclear equation of state satisfies both the maximum mass and the allowed ranges of mass and radius.

## Key claim
Even with kaon condensation softening the equation of state, the four selected Skyrme-Hartree-Fock models can still produce neutron stars with maximum masses above 2.0 solar masses, satisfying the observational constraints from PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432, provided the kaon-nucleon interaction parameter \(a_3 m_s\) is not too negative (i.e., not too strong an attraction).

## Method
The authors employed four Skyrme-Hartree-Fock models (SLy4, SkI4, SGI, SV) to generate nuclear matter equations of state. Kaon interactions were incorporated via an SU(3) non-linear chiral effective model, with the kaon condensate amplitude determined by minimizing the total energy density under beta-equilibrium and local charge neutrality conditions. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were then solved to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations, with Maxwell construction applied to treat thermodynamically unstable regions in the equation of state.

- Maximum masses without kaon condensation: SLy4 2.07 \(M_\odot\), SkI4 2.19 \(M_\odot\), SGI 2.25 \(M_\odot\), SV 2.44 \(M_\odot\) (Table 1).
- Critical densities for kaon condensation in SLy4 range from 0.8580 fm\(^{-3}\) (5.36 \(\rho_0\)) for \(a_3 m_s = -134\) MeV to 0.4183 fm\(^{-3}\) (2.61 \(\rho_0\)) for \(a_3 m_s = -310\) MeV (Table 2).
- Critical densities for SV range from 0.5944 fm\(^{-3}\) (3.83 \(\rho_0\)) for \(a_3 m_s = -134\) MeV to 0.3608 fm\(^{-3}\) (2.33 \(\rho_0\)) for \(a_3 m_s = -310\) MeV (Table 2).
- Observed neutron star masses used for comparison: \((1.97 \pm 0.04) M_\odot\) for PSR J1614-2230 and \((2.01 \pm 0.04) M_\odot\) for PSR J0348+0432.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of strangeness in neutron stars, such as those by Mornas (2005) and Guleria (2012) on hyperons within Skyrme models, and by Thorsson et al. (1994) on kaon condensation using simpler nuclear force parameterizations. The results show that realistic Skyrme models can accommodate kaon condensation while still reaching the 2 \(M_\odot\) threshold, in contrast to earlier expectations that kaons would soften the equation of state too much. This enables future constraints on the kaon-nucleon interaction strength \(a_3 m_s\) and the symmetry energy slope \(L\) from combined mass and radius observations.
