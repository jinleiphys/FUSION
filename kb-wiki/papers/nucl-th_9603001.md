# Quasi-elastic and inelastic inclusive electron scattering from an oxygen jet target

> The results of an experiment on inclusive electron scattering from an oxygen jet target, performed in a wide range of energy and momentum transfer covering both quasi-elastic and $Δ$(1232) resonance regions, are reported. In the former region the theoretical predictions, obtained including effects of nucleon-nucleon correlations in both initial and final states, give a good description of the experimental data. In the inelastic region a broadening as well as a damping of the resonant part of the cross section with respect to the free nucleon case is observed. The need of more detailed calculations including nuclear structure effects on the electroproduction cross section of nucleon resonances is highlighted.

## Key claim
The paper reports that in the quasi-elastic region, theoretical predictions including nucleon-nucleon correlations in both initial and final states give a good description of experimental data, but only after final-state interactions are included; in the inelastic region, a broadening and damping of the Δ(1232) resonance is observed relative to the free nucleon case, with the damping becoming stronger as Q² increases.

## Method
Inclusive electron scattering cross sections were measured from a pure ¹⁶O jet target at the ADONE storage ring using a BGO calorimeter at scattering angles of 32°, 37.1°, and 83° with beam energies from 0.5 to 1.5 GeV. Data were radiatively corrected and compared to theoretical calculations using spectral functions that include nucleon-nucleon correlations and final-state interactions for the quasi-elastic region, and to Fermi-motion-folded free-nucleon parametrizations for the Δ(1232) and N(1520) resonance regions.

## Key numbers
- Three-momentum transfer q up to ~800 MeV/c and squared four-momentum transfer Q² in the range 0.1–0.6 (GeV/c)² for quasi-elastic data.
- Systematic error of 4.5% in the quasi-elastic peak, obtained from quadratic sum of radiative corrections (3%), calibration (3%), beam energy (1%), and solid angle uncertainty (1%).
- Systematic error lower than 6% in the D₁₃(1520) resonance region.
- An overall damping of 25% observed at Q² ≈ 0.5 (GeV/c)² in the Δ(1232) region for both ¹⁶O and ¹²C data.
- Extra width σ applied to resonances was found to be quite independent of kinematics; suppression factors for Δ(1232) approach unity at low Q² but increase with Q².
- For N(1520) resonance, a damping is always observed, providing continuity with the real photon case.

## Context
This work extends previous inclusive electron scattering experiments on nuclei [1–4] to a wider range of energy and momentum transfer using a pure oxygen jet target, and provides data at lower Q² (0.1–0.6 (GeV/c)²) than the SLAC data [28] used to test the theoretical approach of ref. [24]. The results enable further development of realistic calculations including medium-dependent modifications of nucleon resonances and the difference between proton and neutron absorption, as highlighted by the need for more detailed calculations beyond the Δ-hole approach [31] and the work of refs. [29,30].
