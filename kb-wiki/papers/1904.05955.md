# On relation between bulk, surface and curvature parts of nuclear binding energy within the model of hexagonal clusters

> Using the model of hexagonal clusters we express the surface, curvature and Gauss curvature coefficients of the nuclear binding energy in terms of its bulk coefficient. Using the derived values of these coefficients and a single fitting parameter we are able to reasonably well describe the experimental binding energies of nuclei with more than 100 nucleons. To improve the description of lighter nuclei we introduce the same correction for all the coefficients. In this way we determine the apparent values of the surface, curvature and Gauss curvature coefficients which may be used for infinite nuclear matter equation of state. This simple model allows us to fix the temperature dependence of all these coefficients, if the temperature dependence for the bulk term is known. The found estimates for critical temperature are well consistent both with experimental and with theoretical findings.

## Key claim

Using a geometric model of hexagonal clusters with nearest-neighbor interactions, the surface, curvature, and Gauss curvature coefficients of the nuclear binding energy can be expressed analytically in terms of the bulk coefficient, but the resulting curvature term is too large to fit experimental binding energies for nuclei with fewer than 100 nucleons unless additional leptodermous corrections are introduced.

## Method

The authors model a nucleus as a central spherical nucleon surrounded by hexagonal layers of spherical nucleons, with each nearest-neighbor pair contributing a fixed binding energy epsilon. They derive exact expressions for the number of nucleons in each layer and, using the leading-order approximation for large layer number, express the surface and curvature coefficients as fixed ratios of the bulk coefficient. They then introduce three different ansatze for a leptodermous correction to the pair binding energy (as a power series in A^{-1/3}) and fit the resulting binding energy formula to the empirical parameterizations of Mor (2012) for nuclei with A >= 10.

## Key numbers

* The hexagonal model predicts a_S / a_V = 4 / (90^(2/3)) ~ 1/1.1204, so for a_V = 16 MeV the surface coefficient is a_S = 17.9248 MeV, which is 0.4% less than the typical SMM value of 18 MeV.
* The model predicts a_S / a_C = (0.3)^(1/3) ~ 0.6694, giving a_C / a_S ~ 1.4939.
* For the SkM Skyrme interaction, the Gibbs-Thomson approach of Kolom (2011) yields a_S ~ 16.65 MeV and a_C ~ 10 MeV, giving a_C / a_S ~ 0.6, which is 40% of the hexagonal model ratio.
* The best fit to set I (a_V = 15.6 MeV, a_S = 17.32 MeV, a_C = 0) using ansatz II with a_G = 0 gives a_V = 15.70 MeV, q_2 = 1.43, q_3 = 20.5, and a mean deviation Delta_10 = 0.067 MeV.
* The best fit to set IV (a_V = 15.26 MeV, a_S = 15.26 MeV, a_C = 3.6 MeV) using ansatz III with a_G = 0 gives a_V = 15.35 MeV, q_2 = 2.3, q_3 = -3.86, and a mean deviation Delta_10 = 0.009 MeV.
* The Tolman length from the hexagonal model is xi_hex = -0.9 fm, while the set IV fit gives xi_setIV = -0.14 fm and the Gibbs-Thomson result of Kolom (2011) gives xi_GT = -0.36 fm.

## Context

This work extends the cubic nucleus model of Mor (2012) to a more realistic hexagonal geometry, and it addresses the longstanding discrepancy between theoretical predictions for the curvature coefficient a_C (from the Gibbs-Thomson approach of Kolom (2011) and the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock review of Brack (1985)) and the empirical fits that yield a much smaller or zero curvature term. The derived temperature-dependent coefficients can be used in the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) of Bondorf (1985) to provide a thermodynamically consistent treatment of surface and curvature terms, and the critical temperature estimates are consistent with experimental and theoretical findings.
