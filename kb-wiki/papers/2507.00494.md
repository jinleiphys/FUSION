# Susceptibilities of rotating quark matter in Fourier-Bessel basis

> We analyze various two-point correlation functions of fermionic bilinears in a rotating finite-size cylinder at finite temperatures, with a focus on susceptibility functions. Due to the noninvariance of radial translation, the susceptibility functions are constructed using the Dirac propagator in the Fourier-Bessel basis instead of the plane-wave basis. As a specific model to demonstrate the susceptibility functions in an interacting theory, we employ the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. We show that the incompatibility between the mean-field analysis and the Fourier-Bessel basis is evaded under the local density approximation, and derive the resummation formulas of susceptibilities with the help of a Ward-Takahashi identity. The resulting formulation reveals the rotational effects on meson, baryon number, and topological susceptibilities, as well as the moment of inertia. Our results may serve a useful benchmark for future lattice QCD simulations in rotating frames.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that, under the local density approximation and guided by a Ward-Takahashi identity, meson susceptibilities in rotating quark matter can be expressed in the same resummed bubble-ring form as in translationally invariant systems, despite the radial coordinate dependence introduced by rotation and the finite cylindrical geometry.

## Method

The authors construct the Dirac propagator in a rotating finite-size cylinder using the Fourier-Bessel basis, with Bessel functions replacing plane waves due to the violation of radial translational invariance. They then employ the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model under the mean-field approximation, imposing the local density approximation to handle the radially dependent dynamical quark mass. The susceptibility functions for meson, baryon number, and topological channels are derived via resummation of bubble diagrams, with the Ward-Takahashi identity for chiral symmetry enforcing the vanishing of redundant radial terms.

## Key numbers

* The radial momentum is discretized as \(p_\perp \to p_{l,k} = \xi_{l,k}/R\) for \(l=0,1,\dots\) and \(p_{l,k} = \xi_{-l-1,k}/R\) for \(l=-1,-2,\dots\), where \(\xi_{l,k}\) is the \(k\)th positive zero of the Bessel function \(J_l(x)\), and the cylinder radius \(R\) satisfies the causality constraint \(\Omega R \leq 1\).
* The phase space integral is modified to \(\int \frac{dp_z}{2\pi} \sum_{k=1}^\infty \sum_{l=-\infty}^\infty \frac{1}{\pi R^2 N_{l,k}^2}\) with normalization factor \(N_{l,k} = |J_{l+1}(\xi_{l,k})|\) for \(l \geq 0\) and \(N_{l,k} = |J_{-l}(\xi_{-l-1,k})|\) for \(l < 0\).
* The ultraviolet regulator function is \(f(p_z;\Lambda) = \Lambda^{10} / [\Lambda^{10} + (\sqrt{p_{l,k}^2 + p_z^2})^{10}]\) with cutoff \(\Lambda\).
* The Fermi distribution function under rotation is \(n_F(E) = [e^{\beta\{E - \Omega(l+1/2)\}} + 1]^{-1}\) with \(E = \sqrt{p_{l,k}^2 + p_z^2 + M(r)^2}\).
* The one-loop pion susceptibility is given by \(\chi_\pi^{({\rm 1-loop})}(r;M(r)) = N_c N_f \int\frac{dp_z}{2\pi} \sum_{l,k} \frac{J_l(p_{l,k} r)^2 + J_{l+1}(p_{l,k} r)^2}{\pi R^2 N_{l,k}^2} \frac{f(p_z;\Lambda) - 2 n_F(E)}{E}\) with \(N_c=3\) and \(N_f=2\).

## Context

This work extends the study of rotating QCD matter beyond the mean-field analysis of the chiral condensate (Jiang 2016, Ebihara 2016) to two-point correlation functions and susceptibilities, which involve multi-point propagators and thus more radial coordinates. The Fourier-Bessel basis formulation follows Ambrus (2015), and the use of Ward-Takahashi identities to constrain susceptibilities builds on prior work for nonrotating systems by Gomez Nicola (2016, 2017) and Kawaguchi (2020, 2023). The results provide a benchmark for future lattice QCD simulations in rotating frames, which currently suffer from a sign problem (Yamamoto 2013), and enable systematic study of how rotation modifies meson property fluctuations and symmetry restoration patterns.
