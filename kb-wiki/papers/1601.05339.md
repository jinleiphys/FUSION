# The Hyperon Puzzle in Neutron Stars

> The so called "hyperon puzzle", i.e. the difficulty to reconcile the measured masses of neutron stars (NSs) with the presence of hyperons in their interiors, is one of the hot topics in astrophysics which is stimulating copious experimental and theoretical research in hypernuclear physics. After illustrating the origin of the hyperon puzzle, I discuss some of its possible solutions, and particularly those related to the role of hyperonic two- and three-body interactions on the equation of state of dense matter. Afterward, I discuss a possibility to circumvent the hyperon puzzle allowing for the presence of strangeness in NSs in the form of deconfined strange quark matter, and thus considering the so called quark stars, i.e. hybrid stars or strange stars. Finally I discuss the astrophysical consequences of the possible conversion process of an hadronic star to a quark star.

## Key claim

The hyperon puzzle arises because including hyperons in neutron star interiors softens the equation of state so much that the maximum stellar mass drops below the observed ~2 solar masses, yet the presence of hyperons at high densities seems unavoidable based on simple chemical potential arguments. The paper reviews possible solutions but notes that fixing interaction parameters to obtain hyperon stars with masses above 2 solar masses is an ad hoc condition rather than a genuine solution.

## Method

The author performed Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations of the equation of state for β-stable hyperonic matter using the Argonne v18 nucleon-nucleon interaction, a three-nucleon interaction from Bombaci et al. (1997) to reproduce nuclear saturation, and the Nijmegen ESC08b potential for hyperon-nucleon interactions. No hyperon-hyperon interactions or three-body interactions involving hyperons were included in this baseline calculation. The resulting equation of state was then used to solve the relativistic stellar structure equations to obtain mass-radius relations for both nucleon stars and hyperon stars.

## Key numbers

- Onset density for Λ hyperons: n = 0.35 fm⁻³; for Σ⁻ hyperons: n = 0.64 fm⁻³
- At n = 5 n₀ (where n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³), hyperons constitute about 43% of the total baryon number
- Maximum mass for nucleon stars (Av18+TNI): M_max = 2.28 M_sun
- Maximum mass for hyperon stars (Av18+TNI+ESC08b): M_max = 1.38 M_sun
- Central density at maximum mass for the nucleon star configuration: n_c(M_max) = 1.02 fm⁻³
- Relative increase of maximum mass due to three-body hyperonic interactions in the most favorable case: ~16%, giving M_max ≈ 1.6 M_sun (from Vidana et al. 2011)
- Energy released in hadronic star to quark star conversion: 0.5–4.0 × 10⁵³ erg

## Context

This paper reviews the hyperon puzzle first made acute by the mass measurements of PSR J1614-2230 at 1.97 ± 0.04 M_sun (Demorest et al. 2010) and PSR J0348+0432 at 2.01 ± 0.04 M_sun (Antoniadis et al. 2013), which ruled out many earlier hyperonic equations of state. The work extends earlier microscopic calculations by Baldo et al. (2000), Vidana et al. (2000), and Schulze and Rijken (2011) that also found maximum masses below 2 M_sun when hyperons were included. The paper discusses possible resolutions including repulsive hyperon-hyperon interactions via φ meson exchange in relativistic mean field models (Weissenborn et al. 2012), universal three-baryon repulsion via multi-pomeron exchange (Yamamoto et al. 2013, 2014), and the alternative scenario where neutron stars are actually hybrid stars or strange stars containing deconfined quark matter, which would circumvent the puzzle entirely.
