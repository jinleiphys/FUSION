# Phenomenological Analysis of $pp$ and $\bar{p}p$ Elastic Scattering Based on Theoretical Bounds in High-Energy Physics

> Considering the Froissart-Martin bound, Jin-Martin-Cornille bound and the optical theorem, we propose a novel parametrization for the total cross-section of proton-proton and antiproton-proton elastic scattering data. Using derivative dispersion relations we obtain the real part of the elastic scattering amplitude and thus the $ρ$ parameter. Simultaneous fits to $σ_{tot}$ and $ρ$ are performed allowing very good statistical descriptions of the available data. Furthermore, predictions to $σ_{tot}$ and $ρ$ at energies not used in the fit procedures are presented. For $σ_{tot}$ we obtain predictions at RHIC, LHC and future HC energies.

## Key claim
The authors propose a novel four-parameter phenomenological parametrization for the total cross-section of proton-proton and antiproton-proton elastic scattering, derived from the Froissart-Martin upper bound and the Jin-Martin-Cornille lower bound from axiomatic quantum field theory, but note that at present-day energies the data cannot unambiguously discriminate between asymptotic fits of \(\ln s\) and \(\ln^2 s\).

## Method
The total cross-section is parametrized as a sum of a low-energy term proportional to \(s^{-(6+\gamma)}\) (from the Jin-Martin-Cornille bound) and a high-energy term proportional to \(s^{-\alpha}\ln^2 s\) (from the Froissart-Martin bound). The real part of the forward elastic scattering amplitude is obtained via first-order derivative dispersion relations, and the \(\rho\) parameter (ratio of real to imaginary part) is derived. Simultaneous fits to \(\sigma_{\text{tot}}\) and \(\rho\) data for both \(pp\) and \(\bar{p}p\) are performed using four independent parameters per reaction, with the low-energy cutoff \(s_{\text{min}}\) varied to find the widest energy range with statistically acceptable fit quality.

## Key numbers
- For \(pp\) total cross-section at \(\sqrt{s}=14\) TeV (LHC): \(\sigma_{\text{tot}} \simeq 100.44\) mb.
- For \(pp\) total cross-section at \(\sqrt{s}=10\) TeV: \(\sigma_{\text{tot}} \simeq 95.99\) mb.
- For \(pp\) total cross-section at \(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV: \(\sigma_{\text{tot}} \simeq 91.35\) mb.
- For \(\bar{p}p\) total cross-section at \(\sqrt{s}=1.8\) TeV (Tevatron): \(\sigma_{\text{tot}}^{\bar{p}p} = (73.22 \pm 8.30)\) mb (using \(\sqrt{s_{\text{min}}}=5\) GeV).
- For \(\bar{p}p\) total cross-section at \(\sqrt{s}=546\) GeV: \(\sigma_{\text{tot}}^{\bar{p}p} = (61.93 \pm 6.17)\) mb (using \(\sqrt{s_{\text{min}}}=5\) GeV).
- For \(pp\) at \(\sqrt{s}=30\) TeV (cosmic-ray energy): \(\sigma_{\text{tot}} = (110.69 \pm 19.32)\) mb (using \(\sqrt{s_{\text{min}}}=3\) GeV).
- For \(pp\) at \(\sqrt{s}=40\) TeV (cosmic-ray energy): \(\sigma_{\text{tot}} = (114.60 \pm 20.55)\) mb.

## Context
This work extends phenomenological model-independent approaches to high-energy elastic scattering based on axiomatic quantum field theory bounds, following earlier parametrizations by Block (2006), the COMPETE collaboration (2002), and others. The parametrization is designed to respect the Froissart-Martin bound and the Jin-Martin-Cornille bound, and the use of derivative dispersion relations follows the formalism of Avila and Menon (1991) and Bronzan, Kane, and Sukhatme (1974). The results enable predictions for total cross-sections at RHIC, LHC, and future hadron collider energies, and the authors note that the same functional form can be extended to describe the full scattering amplitude beyond the forward direction in future work.
