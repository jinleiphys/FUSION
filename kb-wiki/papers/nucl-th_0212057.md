# Statistics of 2+ Levels in Even-Even Nuclei

> Using all the available empirical information, we analyze the spacing distributions of low-lying 2-plus levels of even-even nuclei. To obtain statistically relevant samples, the nuclei are grouped into classes defined by the ratio R4/2 of the excitation energies of the first 4-plus and 2-plus levels. This ratio serves as a measure of collectivity in nuclei. With the help of Bayesian inference, we determine the chaoticity parameter for each class. This parameter is found to vary strongly with R4/2 and takes particularly small values in nuclei that have one of the dynamical symmetries of the interacting Boson model.

## Key claim

The chaoticity parameter \( \overline{f} \) of low-lying \( 2^+ \) levels in even-even nuclei varies strongly with the ratio \( R_{4/2} = E(4_1^+)/E(2_1^+) \), taking particularly small values at \( R_{4/2} = 2.0, 2.5, \) and \( 3.3 \), which correspond to the dynamical symmetries of the interacting Boson model (IBM); however, the analysis of many short sequences tends to overestimate \( \overline{f} \), so the authors focus on the relative variation of \( \overline{f} \) with \( R_{4/2} \) rather than its absolute value.

## Method

The authors compiled 1306 \( 2^+ \) levels from 169 even-even nuclei using published nuclear data, requiring at least five consecutive \( 2^+ \) levels with unambiguous spin-parity assignments per nucleus. They grouped nuclei into classes defined by intervals of the ratio \( R_{4/2} = E(4_1^+)/E(2_1^+) \), with interval widths of 0.1 when the class contained about 100 or more spacings. Each sequence was unfolded using the constant-temperature formula, and the nearest-neighbor spacing (NNS) distribution for each class was analyzed via Bayesian inference to determine the chaoticity parameter \( f \), assuming the NNS distribution follows a parameterized form that interpolates between the Wigner (GOE) and Poisson limits.

## Key numbers

- 1306 levels of spin-parity \( 2^+ \) belonging to 169 nuclei were analyzed.
- The effective temperature from unfolding follows \( T = (15 \pm 4) A^{-(0.62 \pm 0.05)} \) MeV.
- Deep minima of \( \overline{f} \) occur at \( R_{4/2} = 2.0, 2.5, \) and \( 3.3 \), corresponding to the IBM dynamical symmetries \( U(5) \), \( O(6) \), and \( SU(3) \), respectively.
- A further minimum of statistical significance occurs for \( 2.25 \leq R_{4/2} \leq 2.35 \), associated with the critical \( E(5) \) symmetry at \( R_{4/2} = 2.2 \).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Abul-Magd and collaborators (1990s) that suggested NNS distributions of low-lying nuclear levels lie between the Wigner and Poisson limits, using a much larger data set and Bayesian inference for more definitive statements. The results confirm and quantify the connection between collectivity (measured by \( R_{4/2} \)) and spectral regularity, supporting the IBM predictions of Alhassid et al. (1990s) that chaoticity is reduced near dynamical symmetries. The analysis enables future studies of shape transitions and critical-point symmetries in nuclei, such as the \( E(5) \) symmetry proposed by Iachello (2001) and observed by Casten and Zamfir (2001).
