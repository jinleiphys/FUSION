# Cluster Properties and Particle Production in Relativistic Quantum Mechanics

> I formulate a class of relativistic quantum mechanical models that satisfy the cluster property and allow particle production. The models have a finite number of bare-particle degrees of freedom. The class of models include relativistic isobar models and the relativistic Lee model. I discuss elements of the construction that may be relevant to treat the general case of an infinite number of degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

The paper formulates a class of relativistic quantum mechanical models that simultaneously satisfy the cluster property and allow particle production, but only under the restriction of artificial conservation laws that keep the number of bare-particle degrees of freedom finite. The construction is limited to models where the net change of any particle is positive, which is an unphysical restriction that the author acknowledges must eventually be removed.

## Method

The author constructs dynamical unitary representations of the Poincaré group on a model Hilbert space that is the orthogonal direct sum of tensor products of bare-particle Hilbert spaces, using Clebsch-Gordan coefficients of the Poincaré group to decompose tensor products of irreducible representations. Dynamics is included by adding invariant mass interactions that commute with spin and the operators that label vectors in each irreducible subspace, in what the author calls "Wigner's form of dynamics." To satisfy the cluster property for more than two degrees of freedom, the author uses a generalization of the Mayer cluster expansion based on a partial ordering of subsystems and a C* algebra of operators whose unitary elements preserve binding energies and S-matrix elements, enabling a recursive construction of a unitary representation that clusters into tensor products of subsystem representations.

## Key numbers

- The cluster property provides justification for few-body programs at modern accelerators such as TJNAF, where accurate measurement and precise calculations of few-body observables are used to understand dynamics of systems with a manageable number of degrees of freedom.
- For physics on distance scales of less than one Fermi, a relativistic formulation of quantum theory is essential and the formulation of the cluster property is non-trivial.
- The paper contains a detailed treatment of a theory having bare nucleon, pion, delta, and rho meson degrees of freedom (no specific numerical results given for these degrees of freedom).

## Context

This work extends prior formulations of relativistic quantum mechanical models satisfying the cluster property for N-particle systems, as developed by Sokolov (year not given), Coester and Polyzou (year not given), and others. The paper specifically addresses the additional difficulties that arise when particle production is allowed, which previous N-particle formulations did not handle because they assumed a fixed number of particles. The construction enables future work on removing the "positive charge" restriction by replacing the partial ordering on charge with a partial ordering on center-of-momentum energy, which the author identifies as a necessary step toward treating the general case of an infinite number of degrees of freedom.
