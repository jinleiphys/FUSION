# Forced canonical thermalization in a hadronic transport approach at high density

> Hadronic transport approaches based on an effective solution of the relativistic Boltzmann equation are widely applied for the dynamical description of heavy ion reactions at low beam energies. At high densities, the assumption of binary interactions often used in hadronic transport approaches may not be applicable anymore. Therefore, we effectively simulate the high-density regime using the local forced canonical thermalization. This framework provides the opportunity to interpolate in a dynamical way between two different limits of kinetic theory: the dilute gas approximation and the ideal fluid case. This approach will be important for studies of the dynamical evolution of heavy ion collisions at low and intermediate energies as experimentally investigated at the beam energy scan program at RHIC, and in the future at FAIR and NICA. On the other hand, this new way of modelling hot and dense strongly-interacting matter might be relevant for small systems at high energies (LHC and RHIC) as well.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that forced canonical thermalization in high-density regions of a hadronic transport approach can dynamically interpolate between the dilute gas approximation and the ideal fluid limit, but this interpolation is conditional on the choice of an energy density threshold \(\epsilon_c\) above which thermalization is applied, and the approach assumes that multi-particle collisions are so intense that thermalization happens rapidly, replacing the local distribution function by a thermal one.

## Method

The authors implement forced canonical thermalization on top of the SMASH hadronic transport approach in cascade mode, neglecting nuclear potentials, Fermi motion, and Pauli blocking. In regions where the local rest frame energy density exceeds a predefined threshold \(\epsilon_c\), particles are removed and new ones are sampled according to a thermal distribution that conserves total energy, momentum, baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge, using an equation of state of an ideal Boltzmann hadron gas. The thermalization is performed every \(\Delta t_{th} = 1\) fm/c starting from \(t_{start} = 0\) fm/c, and the system evolves via conventional SMASH cascade between thermalization steps.

## Key numbers

* The critical temperature for the QCD cross-over at zero baryo-chemical potential is around 154 MeV, as known from lattice QCD calculations (Aoki 2006, Soltz 2015).
* For the thermal box test, temperature \(T = 0.15\) GeV and zero chemical potential \(\mu_B = 0\) are used, with a box volume \(V = (5 \text{ fm})^3\).
* The smearing parameter \(\sigma = 1\) fm is used in all simulations except the sphere setup, where \(\sigma = 0.3\) fm.
* For Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 3\) GeV, typical temperatures are around 110 MeV and typical baryon chemical potentials are of order 700 MeV.
* The correction for quantum statistics at these conditions is approximately 7% for pions and 4% for protons.
* The unbiased Becattini-Ferroni sampling algorithm is approximately 4 times slower than the biased version or mode sampling.

## Context

This work extends hybrid approaches that combine hydrodynamics and transport (such as the UrQMD hybrid approach by Petersen 2008) by providing a method where the boundary between the thermalized and transport regions is found dynamically rather than a posteriori, avoiding negative Cooper-Frye contributions that arise in standard hybrid models (Bugaev 1996, Oliinychenko 2014). The approach is conceptually similar to core-corona separation (Steinheimer 2011) but with dynamical coupling between thermalized and transport domains, and it enables studies of the QCD phase transition at low and intermediate beam energies relevant for the RHIC beam energy scan program, FAIR, and NICA.
