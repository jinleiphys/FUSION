# Directed Flow of Baryons in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The collective motion of nucleons from high-energy heavy-ion collisions is analyzed within a relativistic two-fluid model for different equations of state (EoS). As function of beam energy the theoretical slope parameter F_y of the differential directed flow is in good agreement with experimental data, when calculated for the QCD-consistent EoS described by the statistical mixed-phase model. Within this model, which takes the deconfinement phase transition into account, the excitation function of the directed flow <P_x> turns out to be a smooth function in the whole range from SIS till SPS energies. This function is close to that for pure hadronic EoS and exhibits no minimum predicted earlier for a two-phase bag-model EoS. Attention is also called to a possible formation of nucleon antiflow (F_y < 0) at energies of the order of 100 A GeV.

## Key claim

The excitation function of the directed flow ⟨P_x⟩ for baryons, calculated within a relativistic two-fluid model using the QCD-consistent statistical mixed-phase (MP) equation of state, is a smooth function from SIS to SPS energies and exhibits no minimum, contrary to predictions from a two-phase bag-model equation of state. The authors also note a possible formation of nucleon antiflow (F_y < 0) at beam energies above approximately 100 A·GeV, though this prediction is not confirmed by existing experimental data at 158 A·GeV.

## Method

The authors performed three-dimensional two-fluid hydrodynamic simulations of non-central Au+Au collisions, using a friction term between the projectile and target fluids that accounts for finite stopping power and direct pion emission. The equation of state was taken from the statistical mixed-phase model, which describes a continuous crossover deconfinement transition consistent with lattice QCD data, and results were compared to those from a pure hadronic gas model and a two-phase bag model. The slope parameter F_y of the differential directed flow and the average directed flow ⟨P_x⟩ were computed as functions of beam energy and compared to experimental data from SIS to SPS energies.

## Key numbers

- The softest point of the MP model equation of state at zero baryon density is located at energy density ε_SP ≈ 0.45 GeV/fm³, compared to ε_SP ≈ 1.5 GeV/fm³ for the bag-model EoS.
- The deconfinement temperature in the MP model is T_dec = 153 MeV for two light quark flavors at zero baryon density.
- The effective deceleration length used in the friction term is parameterized as λ_eff = a exp(-b√s) with a = 6.6 fm and b = 0.106 GeV⁻¹, adjusted to rapidity distributions at AGS and SPS energies.
- The local freeze-out density used in the hydrodynamic calculations is n_f = 0.8 n₀, where n₀ is normal nuclear matter density.
- The softest point for baryonic matter in the MP model vanishes for baryon densities n_B ≳ 0.4 n₀.
- The calculated slope parameter F_y becomes negative (antiflow) for beam energies ≳ 100 A·GeV, while experimental data at 158 A·GeV give a small positive value.

## Context

This work extends earlier one-fluid hydrodynamic studies by Rischke et al. (1996) and Hung and Shuryak (1995), which predicted a deep minimum in the directed flow excitation function near E_lab = 6 A·GeV using a bag-model equation of state with a first-order phase transition. The present results contradict those predictions and are consistent with preliminary experimental data from the E895 Collaboration that do not confirm such a minimum. The study also compares with transport model calculations (ARC, ART, RQMD, RBUU) and with three-fluid hydrodynamic calculations by other groups, showing that the directed flow is sensitive to the equation of state but that this sensitivity is masked by non-equilibrium dynamics. The work enables future investigation of elliptic flow within the same two-fluid framework with the mixed-phase EoS.
