# Improved Actions for Nuclear Effective Field Theories

> Effective field theories have been successful in describing nuclei up to the alpha particle but face significant challenges for larger nuclei due to leading-order instabilities. These issues can be addressed with the introduction of a fake interaction range at leading order, whose effects are compensated for in perturbation theory at higher orders. The calculation of two-body phase shifts and ground-state energies for up to five $^4$He atoms in a theory with only contact interactions shows that, as long as it remains smaller or comparable to the experimental effective range, the fake range does not alter the convergence of the EFT expansion but is often beneficial at the lowest orders. I discuss the implications of this improved-action approach to the ground-state energies of nuclei such as $^6$Li, $^{12}$C, and $^{16}$O.

## Key claim

The leading-order instability of nuclear effective field theories for systems with more than four nucleons can be overcome by introducing a "fake" interaction range at leading order, as long as this fake range remains smaller than or comparable to the experimental effective range; the fake range does not alter the convergence of the EFT expansion but is often beneficial at the lowest orders.

## Method

The author uses Pionless Effective Field Theory (Short-Range EFT) with only contact interactions, where a fake interaction range is introduced at leading order through two- and three-body Gaussians with a range parameter. The effects of this fake range are then compensated for perturbatively at next-to-leading order (NLO) and higher orders. The method is tested by calculating two-body phase shifts and ground-state energies for up to five ⁴He atoms, and then applied to estimate ground-state energies of ⁶Li, ¹²C, and ¹⁶O.

## Key numbers

- For ³He at NLO: B⁽¹⁾(³He) = (7.62 ± 0.17) MeV, within about 1.5% of the observed value.
- For ⁴He at LO with resummed corrections: B⁽⁰⁾(⁴He) = (31.8 ± 6.6) MeV.
- For ⁶Li at NLO with the improved action (x=1): B⁽¹⁾(⁶Li) = (31.6 ± 0.3) MeV.
- For ¹²C at NLO with the improved action (x=1): B⁽¹⁾(¹²C) = (97 ± 5) MeV.
- For ¹⁶O at NLO with the improved action (x=1): B⁽¹⁾(¹⁶O) = (156 ± 20) MeV.
- The improvement allows variation only in a narrow window 0.9 ≤ x ≤ 1, where x measures the departure from fake ranges that produce the correct two-nucleon effective ranges.

## Context

This work extends the Pionless EFT approach that has successfully described nuclei up to the alpha particle (Contessi 2017, Bansal 2017, Yang 2020) but faced leading-order instabilities for larger nuclei. The improved-action method generalizes the concept of improved actions from lattice quantum field theory (Symanzik 1983) and builds on earlier work showing that fake-range effects can be compensated at NLO for ⁴He clusters (Contessi 2023). The approach enables systematic EFT calculations for heavier nuclei such as ⁶Li, ¹²C, and ¹⁶O, though the author notes that the relative discrepancy with experiment increases with mass number, suggesting more nuclei and higher orders are needed to confirm convergence.
