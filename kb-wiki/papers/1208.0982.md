# eta' meson under partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear medium

> We shed light upon the eta' mass in nuclear matter in the context of partial restoration of chiral symmetry, pointing out that the U_{A}(1) anomaly effects causes the eta'-eta mass difference necessarily through the chiral symmetry breaking. As a consequence, it is expected that the eta' mass is reduced by order of 100 MeV in nuclear matter where partial restoration of chiral symmetry takes place. The discussion given here is based on Ref. [1].

# η′ meson under partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear medium

## Key claim
The η′ meson mass is expected to be reduced by order of 100 MeV in nuclear matter where partial restoration of chiral symmetry takes place, because the U_A(1) anomaly effect causes the η′-η mass difference necessarily through chiral symmetry breaking. This strong mass reduction corresponds to a strong attractive force between the η′ meson and a finite nucleus, potentially leading to η′-nucleus bound states.

## Method
The authors analyze the chiral multiplet structure of pseudoscalar mesons in the (3̄,3) ⊕ (3,3̄) representation of chiral SU_L(3)×SU_R(3), showing that when chiral symmetry is dynamically broken, the octet pseudoscalar mesons become massless Nambu-Goldstone bosons while the flavor-singlet η₀ (η′) remains massive due to the U_A(1) anomaly. They assume the η-η′ mass difference is proportional to the quark condensate, and use a simple optical potential model V_η′(r) = V₀ ρ(r)/ρ₀ with Woods-Saxon nuclear density to calculate η′-nucleus bound state spectra in ¹¹C.

## Key numbers
- The η-η′ mass difference is suppressed at saturation density ρ₀ by 150 MeV, assuming about 35% reduction of the quark condensate in magnitude at ρ₀
- The η′ meson is expected to get a strong attraction of order 150 MeV at saturation density
- The optical potential strength V₀ is taken from 100 to 200 MeV for the real part
- The imaginary part of the optical potential is taken as 20 MeV, corresponding to 40 MeV absorption width in nuclear matter (larger than the experimental value of 15-25 MeV extracted from γA → η′X reactions)
- The η′N scattering length extracted from three-body ppη′ final state is suggested to be as small as a few 0.1 fm with unknown sign

## Context
This work extends the discussion of partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclei, which has been phenomenologically confirmed in pionic atom observation and low-energy pion-nucleus scattering (Jido 2008). The authors build on their earlier work (Jido 2011) showing that chiral symmetry breaking induces the η-η′ mass splitting through the U_A(1) anomaly. The predicted strong η′ attraction in nuclear matter enables the possibility of η′-nucleus bound systems, which can be studied in formation experiments (Nagahiro 2004, 2006, 2011; Itahashi 2012). The approach can be checked by chiral effective theories such as the NJL model (Nagahiro 2006) or linear sigma model (Sakai). The small η′N scattering length from three-body data (Moskal 2000) would indicate weak η′N interaction (Oset 2010), which contrasts with the strong in-medium attraction predicted here and suggests the need for further experimental data.
