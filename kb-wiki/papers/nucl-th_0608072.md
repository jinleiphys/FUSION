# K^- ^3He and K^+K^- interactions in the pd -> ^3He K^+K^- reaction

> We investigate the K^- ^3He and K^+ K^- interactions in the reaction pd -> ^3He K^+ K^- near threshold and compare our model calculations with data from the MOMO experiment at COSY-Juelich. A large attractive effective K^- p amplitude would give a significant K^- ^3He final-state interaction effect which is not supported by the experimental data. We also estimate upper limits for the a_0(980) and f_0(980) contributions to the produced K^+ K^- pairs.

# Wiki Page: K⁻³He and K⁺K⁻ interactions in the pd → ³He K⁺K⁻ reaction

## Key claim

The authors find that a strongly attractive effective K⁻p amplitude, as proposed by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002) to produce deeply bound kaonic nuclear states, would cause a significant deformation of the K⁻³He relative-energy spectrum that is not supported by the MOMO experimental data. The data are best described by a pure phase-space distribution without K⁻³He final-state interactions, though solutions using vacuum K⁻N amplitudes from Sets 1–3 cannot be ruled out due to uncertainties in the multiple-scattering approach and experimental errors.

## Method

The authors calculate K⁻³He and K⁻α scattering lengths using a multiple-scattering approach in the fixed center approximation with a Gaussian single-nucleon density model. They compare these predictions with data from the MOMO experiment at COSY-Jülich for the pd → ³He K⁺K⁻ reaction at an excess energy of 41 MeV, fitting the K⁺K⁻ and K³He relative-energy distributions. They also estimate upper limits for a₀(980) and f₀(980) resonance contributions by fitting the K⁺K⁻ invariant-mass distribution with Flatté parameterizations from four literature sets.

## Key numbers

- The total pd → ³He K⁺K⁻ cross section is 9.6 ± 1.0 nb, with the φ(1020) meson contribution found to be about 16% of this total.
- The K⁻α scattering length from the multiple-scattering approach using vacuum K⁻N amplitudes (Set 1) is A^MS(K⁻α) = (−1.80 + i 0.90) fm; for K⁻³He it is A^MS(K⁻³He) = (−1.50 + i 0.83) fm.
- Using the strongly attractive in-medium K⁻N amplitudes of Akaishi and Yamazaki (Set 5), the calculated K⁻³He scattering length is A^MS(K⁻³He) = (−3.93 + i 4.03) fm, with an alternative value of [−3.91 + i 3.62] fm using different nuclear density parameters.
- The a₀(980) contribution to the total cross section may reach 20–25% within a χ²_min+3 limit, while the f₀(980) contribution does not exceed approximately 10% at an excess energy of 41 MeV.
- The χ²_min for the fit without a₀ or f₀ resonances is 11.5.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of the K⁻α scattering length from the dd → α K⁺K⁻ reaction (Grishina, 2005) to the K⁻³He system using the pd → ³He K⁺K⁻ reaction. It tests the strongly attractive K⁻N interaction model of Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002), which predicted deeply bound kaonic nuclear states, against MOMO experimental data and finds it inconsistent. The results also provide upper limits on a₀(980) and f₀(980) production in this reaction, enabling future searches for these scalar mesons in hadronic production. The analysis uses elementary K⁻N scattering lengths from Barrett (Set 1), Oller (2001, Set 2), and Conboy (Set 3), as well as in-medium effective amplitudes from Ramos and Oset (2000, Set 4).
