# Phenomenological quark model for baryon magnetic moments and beta decay ratios ($G_A/G_V$)

> Baryon magnetic moments and beta decay ratios ($G_A/G_V$) are calculated in a phenomenological quark model. Non-static effects of pion exchange and orbital excitation are included. Good agreement with experiment is found for a combined fit to all measured baryon magnetic moments and beta decay ratios. The model predicts an antiquark content for the proton that is consistent with the Gottfried sum rule.

# Phenomenological quark model for baryon magnetic moments and beta decay ratios ($G_A/G_V$)

## Key claim

The paper claims that a phenomenological quark model including non-static effects from pion exchange and orbital excitation can achieve good simultaneous agreement with experimental baryon magnetic moments and beta decay ratios, but the fit requires a theoretical error of 0.05 nm (magnetic moments) and 0.05 (beta decay ratios) added in quadrature with experimental errors to avoid domination by the most accurate measurements, and the actual level of accuracy of the model is closer to 0.08.

## Method

The author constructs physical baryon states as superpositions of static quark model wave functions with pion components (including both direct and exchange currents), using a pion emission operator with separate coefficients for octet-octet and octet-decuplet transitions. An orbital excitation state with Dalitz angular momenta l=1, L=1 and total L+l=0 (the S' state) is added, along with Lambda-Sigma0 mixing. A chi-squared fit to ten magnetic moments and five beta decay ratios is performed using six parameters: quark masses, pion admixture probability, effective pion magnetic moment, ratio of Delta-pi to N-pi admixture, and orbital excitation probability.

## Key numbers

- Static quark model prediction for neutron G_A/G_V is 1.67, compared to experimental value 1.27; the full model gives 1.32
- Best fit parameters: m_u = 297 +/- 20 MeV, m_s = 453 +/- 20 MeV, P_pi = 33 +/- 7%, M(pi) = 4.7 +/- 1.0 nm, R_Delta = 8 +/- 5%, eta(orbital) = 8 +/- 2%
- Chi-squared minus degrees of freedom for full model: 23-9; leaving out orbital excitation increases chi-squared to 51; leaving out pion exchange increases chi-squared to 104
- Lambda-Sigma0 mixing angle = -0.014 +/- 0.004 radians
- Predicted antiquark content of proton: anti-u = 0.07, anti-d = 0.26, anti-d minus anti-u = 0.19 +/- 0.03
- Gottfried sum rule prediction from model: S_G = 0.21 +/- 0.02, compared to experimental result 0.24 +/- 0.03
- Rest frame quark spin projections: Delta u = 0.98 +/- 0.05, Delta d = -0.35 +/- 0.01, Sigma = 0.63 +/- 0.06
- Pion orbital angular momentum L_z = 0.19 +/- 0.06

## Context

This work extends the author's previous treatment of pion contributions to baryon magnetic moments (Franklin, 1999, referred to as "I") by adding orbital excitation and simultaneously fitting beta decay ratios, which had been difficult to reconcile with magnetic moments in the static quark model. The static quark model predictions (Beg, Lee, and Pais, 1964; Sakita, 1964) gave qualitative agreement with early measurements but failed for precise data, particularly the magnetic moment sum rules that violate SU(3) symmetry by up to 0.5 nm. The model enables calculation of rest-frame quark spin distributions and antiquark content of the proton, connecting to polarized deep inelastic scattering and the Gottfried sum rule measured by the New Muon Collaboration (1991). The author notes that the experimental G_A/G_V values for hyperon decays are actually theoretical numbers derived assuming SU(3) symmetry (g_2=0), which is at variance with the model, and cites the Hsueh et al. (1985) measurement of Sigma- to n decay that allowed g_2 to vary freely.
