# A Solution to the Quenched ${g_A}$ Problem in Nuclei and Dense Baryonic Matter

> When scale symmetry is combined with chiral symmetry in a scale-chiral Lagrangian, it can be shown in Fermi-liquid fixed point theory that $g_A^{\rm eff}\approx 1$ in finite nuclei {\it as well as} in dense baryonic matter. This is suggested as a signal for emergence of hidden symmetries of QCD in baryonic matter from low to very high density. This calculation throws doubt on the "first principles" explanation of the quenching of $g_A$ in nuclei with two-body meson-exchange currents. It also has relevance to Gamow-Teller matrix elements in neutrinoless double $β$ decay.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the effective axial coupling constant \(g_A^{\rm eff}\approx 1\) in finite nuclei and dense baryonic matter arises from a combination of large \(N_c\) QCD and Fermi-liquid fixed point theory, rather than from two-body meson-exchange currents as claimed in "first-principles" calculations. The author questions whether the result from Gysbers et al. (2019) can truly be considered a first-principles solution, suggesting it may be a coincidental outcome of nuclear renormalizations rather than a fundamental QCD effect.

## Method

The author constructs a scale-invariant hidden local symmetric (bsHLS) Lagrangian that includes nucleons, pions, vector mesons (\(\rho, \omega\)) as hidden gauge fields, and a dilaton \(\chi\) (identified with \(f_0(500)\)) as a pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone boson of scale symmetry. This Lagrangian is treated in the mean-field approximation and shown to be equivalent to Landau Fermi-liquid fixed point theory, with parameters endowed with intrinsic density dependence (IDD) from QCD condensates at the matching scale. The effective axial coupling \(g_A^{\rm eff}\) is then computed in the limit of large \(N_c\) and large \(\bar{N}\) (where \(\bar{N}=k_F/(\Lambda_{\rm FL}-k_F)\)), using the Landau-Migdal interaction parameters and the in-medium pion decay constant.

## Key numbers

- The experimental quenching factor for \(^{100}\)Sn is \(q_{exp}=0.75(2)\), corresponding to \(g_A^{\rm eff}=0.95-1.08\) from the calculation of Gysbers et al. (2019)
- The Fermi-liquid fixed point calculation yields \(q^L\simeq 0.79\) at nuclear matter density \(n_0\simeq 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- The anomalous orbital gyromagnetic ratio for proton predicted by the model is \(\delta g_l^p (n_0)\simeq 0.21\), compared to the experimental value \(\delta g_l^{\rm proton}=0.23\pm 0.03\) measured in the Pb region
- The axial-charge matrix element prediction for nuclear matter gives \(\epsilon_{theory}=2.0\pm 0.2\), compared to \(\epsilon_{exp}=2.01\pm 0.05\) for transitions in the Pb region (\(A=205-212\))

## Context

This work extends the long-standing investigation of the "quenched \(g_A\) problem" first noted by Wilkinson (1970s), where Gamow-Teller transitions in shell-model nuclei require a universal quenching factor \(\sim 0.75\). The author directly challenges the "first-principles" resolution claimed by Gysbers et al. (2019) for the \(^{100}\)Sn super-allowed Gamow-Teller decay, which combined no-core shell model techniques with chiral effective field theory. The paper instead supports the "chiral filter hypothesis" from Kubodera, Delorme, and Rho (1970s), arguing that soft-pion two-body currents dominate the axial-charge operator but are suppressed for the Gamow-Teller operator. The result enables a unified understanding of \(g_A^{\rm eff}\approx 1\) from low-density nuclei to high-density compact-star matter, with implications for neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements.
