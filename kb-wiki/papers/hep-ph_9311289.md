# 1) Spin and flavor sum rules based on a parton model, 2) SU(2) flavor breaking in antiquark distributions

> 1) We discuss a sum rule of the tensor structure function $b_1(x)$ for spin-one hadrons along with the Gottfried sum rule. Both sum rules are similar in the sense that they are phenomenological ones based on a naive parton model. As the Gottfried sum rule became useful for studying SU(2)$_{flavor}$ breaking in antiquark distributions, the $b_1$ sum rule could provide important information on sea-quark tensor polarization. 2) Pionic contributions to the antiquark distribution $\bar u-\bar d$ and violation of the Gottfried sum rule are discussed. We find that the contributions account for about half of the discrepancy found by the New Muon Collaboration between their result and the sum rule. In order to distinguish various theoretical explanations, we need independent experimental tests, for example, by using Drell-Yan processes. As an application of mesonic calculations, the difference between strange and antistrange quark distributions in the nucleon is briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a phenomenological sum rule for the tensor structure function \(b_1(x)\) of spin-one hadrons, analogous to the Gottfried sum rule, which holds only under the assumption that sea-quark tensor polarization vanishes; any measured deviation from zero would indicate non-zero tensor polarization in the sea.

## Method

The author derives the sum rule for the integral of \(b_1(x)\) over Bjorken \(x\) by expressing it in terms of valence-quark tensor polarization and sea-quark tensor polarization in the parton model. The valence contribution is related to the elastic quadrupole form factor \(F_Q(t)\) of the deuteron in the limit \(t \rightarrow 0\), while the sea term \(\delta Q_{sea}\) is assumed zero in the naive model. The same formalism is applied to the Gottfried sum rule, where pionic contributions to \(\bar u - \bar d\) are calculated to estimate the violation observed by the New Muon Collaboration.

## Key numbers

- The Gottfried sum rule violation from pionic contributions accounts for about half of the discrepancy found by the New Muon Collaboration between their experimental result and the sum rule prediction.
- The derived sum rule for \(b_1(x)\) is \(\int dx\, b_1(x) = \lim_{t\rightarrow 0} -\frac{5}{3} \frac{t}{4M^2} F_Q(t) + \frac{1}{9} \delta Q_{sea}\), which reduces to zero when \(\delta Q_{sea}=0\).
- The Gottfried sum rule including flavor breaking is given as \(\int \frac{dx}{x} [F_2^p(x)-F_2^n(x)] = \frac{1}{3} + \frac{2}{3} \int dx [\bar u(x)-\bar d(x)]\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of the Gottfried sum rule, originally derived by Gottfried (1967), to the tensor structure function \(b_1\) of spin-one hadrons, following earlier theoretical work on spin-one structure functions by the MIT group. The paper discusses pionic contributions to the \(\bar u - \bar d\) asymmetry as a mechanism for the Gottfried sum rule violation observed by the New Muon Collaboration, and suggests that future measurements at HERA or the proposed 15 GeV European Electron Facility could test the \(b_1\) sum rule and distinguish between theoretical explanations. The author also briefly applies the mesonic calculation to the difference between strange and antistrange quark distributions in the nucleon.
