# Proton-Size Resolution of the Hyperfine Puzzle in Hydrogen

> Baym and Farrar (arXiv:2601.02300v1) have recently pointed out a puzzle in understanding the role of the hyperfine interaction in the ground state of a hydrogen atom. If one uses a variational wave function in which the Bohr radius, $a_0$ is replaced by a variational radius parameter, $R$, first-order perturbation theory can give a contribution to the energy proportional to $-1/R^3$. This raises the question of why the hyperfine interaction does not lead to collapse of hydrogen. I show that including the effects of the non-zero size of the proton leads to a resolution of the puzzle such that the variational procedure yields a value of $R$ that is indistinguishable from $a_0$.

## Key claim

The hyperfine interaction in the spin-singlet state of hydrogen does not cause collapse of the variational wave function when the non-zero size of the proton is included; the variational procedure yields a value of the radius parameter \(R\) that is indistinguishable from the Bohr radius \(a_0\), with a shift of only \(6 \times 10^{-6} a_0\).

## Method

The author uses a variational wave function \(\phi_R(r) = e^{-r/R}/\sqrt{\pi R^3}\) and computes the expectation value of the hyperfine interaction Hamiltonian, incorporating the proton's finite size via the dipole form of the Sachs magnetic form factor \(G_D(q^2) = 1/(1+q^2/\Lambda^2)^2\) with \(\Lambda = 0.843\) GeV. The total energy \(E_1(R) = E_0(R) + E_{\text{hf}}(R)\) is then minimized, where \(E_0(R)\) is the sum of kinetic and Coulomb energies, and the minimum is found both analytically by expansion about \(R = a_0\) and numerically using dimensionless variables.

## Key numbers

- The proton magnetic form factor dipole parameter is \(\Lambda = 0.843\) GeV.
- The shift in the variational radius at the energy minimum is \(R - a_0 \approx 6 \times 10^{-6} a_0\) for the spin-singlet state.
- The hyperfine energy contribution is proportional to \(\Lambda^3 R^3 / (2 + \Lambda R)^3\), which remains finite at \(R = 0\).

## Context

This work resolves a puzzle raised by Baym and Farrar (2026), who noted that a variational treatment of the hyperfine interaction in hydrogen, using a point-like proton, leads to an energy divergence as the variational radius \(R\) approaches zero. The author shows that including the finite proton size, parameterized by the dipole magnetic form factor, removes the divergence and leaves the Bohr radius as the stable minimum. This enables future studies of hyperfine structure in hydrogen to safely use point-like approximations for the proton when the finite-size correction is negligible, and it clarifies the role of the proton's internal structure in atomic bound-state calculations.
