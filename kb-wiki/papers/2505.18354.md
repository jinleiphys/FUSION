# High-precision Penning trap mass measurements of neutron-rich chlorine isotopes at the N=28 shell closure

> Although it is known that the $N=28$ spherical shell closure erodes, the strength of the closure with decreasing proton number $Z<20$ is an open question in nuclear structure. In this region of interest, direct high-precision mass measurements of neutron-rich $^{43-45}$Cl isotopes were performed at the Low Energy Beam and Ion Trap (LEBIT) when coupled to the National Superconducting Cyclotron Lab. The resulting mass excesses (MEs) are ME($^{43}$Cl) = -24114.4(1.7) keV, ME($^{44}$Cl) = -20450.8(10.6) keV, and ME($^{45}$Cl) = -18240.1(3.7) keV, and improve the uncertainty of these masses by up to a factor of ~40 compared to the previous values reported in the 2020 Atomic Mass Evaluation. Comparison to $\textit{ab initio}$ calculations using the Valence-Space In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group (VS-IMSRG) shows good agreement up to and including the closure.

## Key claim
The N=28 spherical shell closure strength for Z<20 remains an open question; this work provides high-precision mass measurements of neutron-rich chlorine isotopes that improve uncertainties by up to a factor of ~40 compared to the 2020 Atomic Mass Evaluation, but the large uncertainty from unmeasured masses beyond N=28 prevents definitive determination of the shell closure strength in the chlorine chain.

## Method
The masses of 43-45Cl were measured using the Time-of-Flight Ion Cyclotron Resonance (TOF-ICR) technique at the Low Energy Beam and Ion Trap (LEBIT) facility coupled to the National Superconducting Cyclotron Lab. The isotopes were produced by impinging a 140 MeV/u 48Ca beam on a 9Be target, separated using the A1900 fragment separator, stopped in gas cells, and delivered as singly charged ions to a 9.4 T Penning trap mass spectrometer. Cyclotron frequency ratios were measured relative to reference ions (39K+, [12C14N1H216O]+, and [28Si16O1H]+) with excitation times of 50-100 ms.

## Key numbers
- Mass excess of 43Cl: -24114.4(1.7) keV, improving precision by a factor of 34 over the AME2020 value of -24160(60) keV
- Mass excess of 44Cl: -20450.8(10.6) keV, improving precision by a factor of 8 over the AME2020 value of -20480(90) keV
- Mass excess of 45Cl: -18240.1(3.7) keV, improving precision by a factor of 37 over the AME2020 value of -18260(140) keV
- One-neutron shell gap at N=28 for chlorine: Δ1n(28,17) = 2.294 ± 0.098 MeV, compared to potassium Δ1n(28,19) = 3.726 ± 0.002 MeV and phosphorus Δ1n(28,15) = 2.122 ± 0.591 MeV

## Context
This work extends the high-precision mass measurements in the N=28 region beyond previous LEBIT measurements of neutron-rich sulfur isotopes (2009) and ISOLTRAP measurements of argon isotopes (2020). The results enable comparison to ab initio Valence-Space In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group (VS-IMSRG) calculations using the 1.8/2.0(EM) NN+3N Hamiltonian, showing reasonable agreement in the three-point estimator of the pairing gap. The measurements motivate future high-precision mass measurements of 46,47Cl and isotopes in the sulfur and phosphorus chains beyond N=28, now accessible with LEBIT coupled to the Facility for Rare Isotope Beams (FRIB).
