# Chiral Dynamics of scalar mesons: radiative $ϕ$ decay and $σ$ in the medium through $π^0 π^0$ nuclear photoproduction

> In order to assess the relevance of chiral dynamics in the scalar sector we address two recent problems: radiative decay of the $ϕ$, for which there are quite recent data from Frascati, and the modification of the $σ$ properties in the nuclear medium seen through the $π^0 π^0$ photoproduction in nuclei.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the scalar mesons $f_0(980)$ and $\sigma$ can be understood as dynamically generated resonances from multiple meson scattering using unitarized chiral perturbation theory, without introducing them as explicit degrees of freedom. The authors show that their parameter-free approach for $\phi$ radiative decays agrees with new Frascati data within theoretical uncertainties, despite the dynamically generated $f_0(980)$ having a very small width (around 30 MeV) that appears much larger experimentally due to gauge-invariance-required kinematic factors.

## Method
The authors calculate the $\phi \to \pi^0\pi^0\gamma$ and $\phi \to \pi^0\eta\gamma$ decay amplitudes using chiral Lagrangians with vector meson resonances, including kaon loop diagrams from $\phi \to K^+K^-$ decay, sequential vector meson exchange mechanisms (VMD), and sequential axial-vector meson exchange mechanisms. The meson-meson scattering amplitudes are obtained from the Bethe-Salpeter equation using unitarized chiral perturbation theory, which generates the $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ resonances dynamically. For the nuclear medium study, the $(\gamma,\pi^0\pi^0)$ reaction on nuclei is modeled using tree-level diagrams with final state interaction of pions implemented through the Bethe-Salpeter equation, with the pion propagators and scattering amplitudes modified in nuclear matter.

## Key numbers
- Branching ratio $BR(\phi\to\pi^0\pi^0\gamma) = (1.2 \pm 0.3) \times 10^{-4}$, compared to experimental values $(1.22 \pm 0.10 \pm 0.06) \times 10^{-4}$ (Novosibirsk), $(0.92 \pm 0.08 \pm 0.06) \times 10^{-4}$ (Novosibirsk), and $(1.09 \pm 0.03 \pm 0.05) \times 10^{-4}$ (Frascati)
- Branching ratio $BR(\phi\to\pi^0\eta\gamma) = (0.59 \pm 0.19) \times 10^{-4}$, compared to experimental values $(0.88 \pm 0.14 \pm 0.09) \times 10^{-4}$ (Novosibirsk), $(0.90 \pm 0.24 \pm 0.10) \times 10^{-4}$ (Novosibirsk), and $(0.85 \pm 0.05 \pm 0.06) \times 10^{-4}$ (Frascati)
- The dynamically generated $f_0(980)$ has a width of approximately 30 MeV in the model
- Parameters used: $F_V = 156 \pm 5$ MeV, $G_V = 55 \pm 5$ MeV, $f_\pi = 92.4$ MeV, $\tilde{\epsilon} = 0.059 \pm 0.004$, $\tilde{D} = -1000 \pm 120$ MeV, $\tilde{F} = 1550 \pm 150$ MeV, cutoff $\Lambda = 1000 \pm 50$ MeV
- The $\phi$-$\omega$ mixing parameter $\Theta_{\phi\omega}$ is in the range $20000-29000$ MeV$^2$

## Context
This work extends the unitarized chiral perturbation theory approach of Oller, Oset, and collaborators (1998-2000) for meson-meson scattering to radiative $\phi$ decays, providing a parameter-free prediction that generates the $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ resonances dynamically. The results are compared with new high-statistics data from the Frascati experiments (2002-2003) and earlier Novosibirsk experiments (1999-2001). The nuclear medium study extends the work of Chiang, Oset, and collaborators (1998) on $\pi\pi$ scattering in nuclear matter to the $(\gamma,\pi^0\pi^0)$ reaction, showing a clear signature of the $\sigma$ meson modification in the medium that agrees with preliminary experimental data from Messchendorp (2002). This enables future experimental tests of the dynamically generated nature of scalar mesons through nuclear photoproduction experiments.
