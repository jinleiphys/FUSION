# Analysis of parity nonconservation in the Ra+-ion

> We report on a theoretical analysis of the suitability of the 7s 2S1/2 <-> 6d 2D3/2 transition in singly ionized radium to measure parity nonconservation, in the light of an experiment planned at the KVI of the University of Groningen. Relativistic coupled-cluster theory has been employed to perform an ab initio calculation of the parity nonconserving electric dipole amplitude of this transition, including single, double, and leading triple excitations. We discuss the prospects for a sub-1% precision test of the electroweak theory of particle physics.

## Key claim

The $7s\,^2S_{1/1} \leftrightarrow 6d\,^2D_{3/2}$ transition in singly ionized radium (Ra⁺) is a promising candidate for a sub-1% precision test of the electroweak Standard Model via atomic parity nonconservation (APNC), provided that the atomic theory can be pushed to sub-1% accuracy and that missing experimental data on relevant atomic properties become available.

## Method

The authors employed relativistic coupled-cluster (RCC) theory with single, double, and leading triple excitations (CCSD(T)) to perform an *ab initio* calculation of the parity-nonconserving electric dipole amplitude ($E1_{\text{PNC}}$) for the $7s\,^2S_{1/2} \leftrightarrow 6d\,^2D_{3/2}$ transition in $^{226}$Ra⁺. The first-order perturbed wave functions due to the nuclear-spin-independent weak interaction were solved directly rather than using a sum-over-intermediate-states approach, and core correlation effects modified by the weak interaction were evaluated to all orders. Numerical Dirac-Fock V$^{N-1}$ orbitals and Gaussian-type orbitals were used to represent bound and continuum states, with a Fermi charge distribution for the finite nuclear size.

## Key numbers

- Best calculated $E1_{\text{PNC}}$ amplitude for $^{226}$Ra⁺: $46.4 \times 10^{-11} i e a_0 (-Q_W/N)$ at the CCSD(T) level.
- The APNC effect in Ra⁺ is larger than in Cs by a factor close to 50 (Cs $E1_{\text{PNC}}$ is about $0.9 \times 10^{-11} i e a_0 (-Q_W/N)$).
- The dominant contribution to $E1_{\text{PNC}}$ comes from the $7p\,^2P_{1/2}$ intermediate state, contributing 111% of the total.
- Calculated excitation energies for Ra⁺: $7s\,^2S_{1/2} \rightarrow 7p\,^2P_{1/2}$ at 21509 cm⁻¹ (experiment: 21351 cm⁻¹); $7s\,^2S_{1/2} \rightarrow 7p\,^2P_{3/2}$ at 26440 cm⁻¹ (experiment: 26209 cm⁻¹); $6d\,^2D_{3/2} \rightarrow 7p\,^2P_{1/2}$ at 9734 cm⁻¹ (experiment: 9267 cm⁻¹).
- Calculated hyperfine constant ratio $[A_I/g_I(7S_{1/2}) / A_I/g_I(7P_{1/2})]^{1/2}$ differs by 3% from experiment, giving an estimated uncertainty of $E1_{\text{PNC}} = 46.4(1.4) \times 10^{-11} i e a_0 (-Q_W/N)$.
- Calculated lifetimes of metastable $D$-states: 0.627(4) s for $6d\,^2D_{3/2}$ and 0.297(4) s for $6d\,^2D_{5/2}$.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework previously applied to Ba⁺ (Bijaya et al., 2006) to the heavier alkali-like ion Ra⁺, which is being studied at the TRI$\mu$P facility at KVI, Groningen. The calculation improves upon earlier work by Dzuba et al. (2001), who used sum-over-states and mixed-states approaches, by including core correlation effects to all orders and normalization of states. The results enable a planned single-ion APNC experiment on Ra⁺, following proof-of-principle experiments on Ba⁺ by the Fortson group (Koerber et al., 2003; Koerber et al., 2002; Sherman et al., 2005), and could provide a test of the electroweak theory complementary to the Cs measurement by the Boulder group (Wood et al., 1997; Bennett et al., 1999).
