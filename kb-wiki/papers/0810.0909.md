# Ultrarelativistic Electron-Positron Plasma

> Ultrarelativistic electron-positron plasmas can be produced in high-intensity laser fields and play a role in various astrophysical situations. Their properties can be calculated using QED at finite temperature. Here we will use perturbative QED at finite temperature for calculating various important properties, such as the equation of state, dispersion relations of collective plasma modes of photons and electrons, Debye screening, damping rates, mean free paths, collision times, transport coefficients, and particle production rates, of ultrarelativistic electron-positron plasmas. In particular, we will focus on electron-positron plasmas produced with ultra-strong lasers.

## Key claim

Perturbative QED at finite temperature can be used to calculate the properties of ultrarelativistic electron-positron plasmas, but the results assume thermal and chemical equilibrium, an ideal gas, and an infinitely extended homogeneous system; deviations from these assumptions (such as the 12-order-of-magnitude density deficit expected in laser-produced plasmas) require extensions to non-equilibrium QED.

## Method

The author applies perturbative QED at finite temperature using the imaginary or real time formalism, computing the polarization tensor and electron self-energy at one-loop order in the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation. From these quantities, the dielectric tensor, dispersion relations for collective plasma modes (photons and electrons), Debye screening length, damping rates, transport rates, mean free paths, collision times, shear viscosity, and collisional energy loss are derived, with many results carried over from analogous calculations in quark-gluon plasma physics.

## Key numbers

- Equilibrium electron-positron number density: \(\rho_e^{eq} = 0.37 T^3\); at \(T=10\) MeV, \(\rho_e^{eq} = 4.9 \times 10^{40}\) m\(^{-3}\)
- Total energy density: \(\epsilon^{eq} = 1.81 T^4\); at \(T=10\) MeV, \(\epsilon^{eq} = 3.8 \times 10^{29}\) J m\(^{-3}\)
- Coulomb coupling parameter: \(\Gamma_C = 5.3 \times 10^{-3}\) at \(T=10\) MeV, confirming weak coupling
- Effective photon mass: \(m_{ph} = eT/3 = 1\) MeV at \(T=10\) MeV
- Plasma frequency: \(\omega_{pl} = m_{ph} = 1.5 \times 10^{21}\) s\(^{-1}\) at \(T=10\) MeV
- Debye screening length: \(\lambda_D = 1/(\sqrt{3}m_{ph}) = 1.1 \times 10^{-13}\) m at \(T=10\) MeV
- Effective electron mass: \(m_F = eT/\sqrt{8} = 1.1\) MeV at \(T=10\) MeV
- Electron damping rate: \(\gamma_e = 86\) keV at \(T=10\) MeV
- Electron transport rate: \(\Gamma_e = 0.54\) keV for thermal particles at \(T=10\) MeV
- Electron mean free path: \(\lambda_e^{mfp} = 0.37\) nm at \(T=10\) MeV
- Photon mean free path: \(\lambda_{ph}^{mfp} = 0.28\) nm at \(T=10\) MeV
- Total shear viscosity: \(\eta = (1.1 - 1.6) \times 10^{11}\) Pa s at \(T=10\) MeV
- Collisional energy loss of a 100 MeV electron: \(dE/dx = 200\) MeV/nm at \(T=10\) MeV
- For non-equilibrium laser-produced plasma at \(T=10\) MeV with fugacity \(\lambda = 10^{-12}\): effective photon mass \(m_{ph}^{noneq} = 1\) eV, plasma frequency \(\omega_{pl}^{noneq} = 1.5 \times 10^{15}\) Hz, Debye screening length \(\lambda_D = 0.1\) \(\mu\)m

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature QED formalism previously applied to the quark-gluon plasma (Thoma, 2000) to the electron-positron plasma case, providing a comprehensive reference for properties including collective modes, transport coefficients, and particle production. The paper enables predictions for laser-produced electron-positron plasmas (Shen and Meyer-ter-Vehn, 2001) and astrophysical settings such as supernovae and magnetar environments, while also identifying open questions such as the observability of the plasmino branch via van Hove singularities and the need for calculations of radiative energy loss.
