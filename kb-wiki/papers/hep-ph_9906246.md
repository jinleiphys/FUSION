# Off Mass Shell Effects in Hadron Electric Dipole Moments

> We note that off the quark mass shell the operators $(p_i+p_f)_μγ_5$ and $iσ_{μν}(p_i -p_f)^νγ_5$, both of which reduce to $-\vecσ\cdot\vec{E}$ in the non-relativistic limit, are no longer identical. In this paper we explore the effects of this difference in the contribution of these quark electric moments to hadronic electric moments.

# Off Mass Shell Effects in Hadron Electric Dipole Moments

## Key claim

Off the quark mass shell, the two operators $(p_i+p_f)_\mu\gamma_5$ and $i\sigma_{\mu\nu}(p_i-p_f)^\nu\gamma_5$, which are equivalent on mass shell via the Gordon identity, produce different contributions to hadronic electric dipole moments; for the rho meson, the $(p_i+p_f)_\mu\gamma_5$ term contributes roughly 67-83% of the standard $i\sigma_{\mu\nu}q^\nu\gamma_5$ term, depending on the non-perturbative model used.

## Method

The authors calculated the electric dipole moment of the rho meson generated by a $P$ and $T$ violating quark-photon vertex of the form $\tilde{\Gamma}^{\mu_q} = [D_{1,q}(p_i+p_f)^\mu\gamma_5 + i D_{2,q}\sigma^{\mu\nu}(p_i-p_f)_\nu\gamma_5] Q_q$, using the impulse approximation with dressed quark propagators from Dyson-Schwinger equation studies and three different phenomenological Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes for the rho meson. The dipole term was projected out of the $\rho\rho\gamma$ vertex integral using a projection operator and evaluated numerically with Gaussian quadrature, with results compared to perturbative, bag model, and non-relativistic limits.

## Key numbers

- For the Higgs model with Higgs mass 100 GeV and top mass 170 GeV, the ratio $d_{1,d}/d_{2,d} = 2.34$ for the down quark.
- The $(p_i+p_f)_\mu\gamma_5$ contribution was approximately 67% of the $i\sigma_{\mu\nu}q^\nu\gamma_5$ result for the Mitchell-Tandy amplitude, 83% for the Chappell amplitude, and 81% for the Pichowsky-Lee amplitude.
- The model quark propagator parameters include mass scale $D = 0.160$ GeV$^2$ and mean light quark mass $5.0$ MeV.
- The rho Bethe-Salpeter amplitude parameters for Chappell: $a_1 = 0.38845$ GeV, $a_2 = 0.01478$, $\alpha = 2$ GeV; for Pichowsky-Lee: $a_V = 0.400$ GeV, $b_V = 0.008$ GeV, $c_V = 125.0$; for Mitchell-Tandy: $a = 0.194$ GeV.
- In the perturbative free-quark limit, the $(p_i+p_f)_\mu\gamma_5$ term gives $d_{\rho;{\rm pert}} = 0$.
- In the non-relativistic limit, $d_\rho^{(NR)} = (d_{1,u} + d_{2,u}) + (d_{1,d} + d_{2,d})$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of quark contributions to rho meson electromagnetic properties (Hecht and McKellar, 1998) by examining the off-shell ambiguity in the $P$ and $T$ violating quark-photon vertex. The study uses the same Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter framework as Roberts and Williams (1994) and the same quark propagator models as Burden, Roberts, and Thomson (1996) and Mitchell and Tandy (1997). The results indicate that for confined quarks, the two forms of the electric dipole operator are not equivalent, contrary to the on-shell assumption used in prior calculations; this has implications for future precision calculations of the neutron electric dipole moment, which is reviewed in He, McKellar, and Pakvasa (1989).
