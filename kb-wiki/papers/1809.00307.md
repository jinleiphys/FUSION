# Relating hadronic CP-violation to higher-twist distributions

> The nucleon sigma term of the isoscalar and isovector quark chromo-magnetic dipole moments are essential inputs for the determination of the CP-odd pion-nucleon couplings induced by quark chromo-electric dipole moments. We demonstrate that the former can be mapped to the third moment of the nucleon twist-three chiral-odd distribution functions $e^q(x)$ which are in principle measurable in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering processes. We perform a survey on existing model calculations as well as experimental data on $e^u(x)+e^d(x)$ and derive a predicted range for the isoscalar chromo-magnetic dipole moment sigma term.

## Key claim

The nucleon sigma terms of the isoscalar and isovector quark chromo-magnetic dipole moments (cMDM), which are essential for determining CP-odd pion-nucleon couplings, can be mapped to the third Mellin moment of the nucleon twist-three chiral-odd distribution functions \(e^q(x)\), measurable in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS). This mapping relies on two key assumptions: that the third moment of \(e^q(x)\) is dominated by the pure twist-three contribution (justifiable) and that the unknown constants \(B^q\) vanish in the non-relativistic limit (introducing a systematic error of order \(\mathcal{O}(1/N_c)\)).

## Method

The author relates the nucleon matrix elements of the quark chromo-magnetic dipole moment operator, \(\langle N|\bar{q}\sigma\cdot G q|N\rangle\), to the third moment of the twist-three distribution function \(e^q(x)\) by parameterizing the spin-averaged \(\bar{q}\sigma\cdot G q\) matrix element in terms of two dimensionless invariants, \(A^q\) and \(B^q\). The cMDM sigma terms \(\sigma_C^{0,3}\) are expressed in terms of \(e_2^q\) and \(B^q\); neglecting \(B^q\) (assumed subdominant in the large-\(N_c\) limit) yields a direct relation. The author then surveys existing model calculations (bag model, spectator model, chiral quark-soliton model, light-front constituent quark model) and fits experimental data from the CLAS Collaboration on \(e(x)=e^u(x)+(1/4)e^{\bar{d}}(x)\) to extract a range for the third moment of \(e^u(x)+e^d(x)\) and thus for \(\sigma_C^0\).

## Key numbers

* Model predictions for the third moment of \(e^u(x)+e^d(x)\) at \(\mu=1\) GeV range from 0.032 (bag model) to 0.11 (light-front constituent quark model), implying \(\sigma_C^0\) values from 0.085 GeV\(^2\) to 0.29 GeV\(^2\).
* A fit to CLAS data at \(Q^2=1.5\) GeV\(^2\) yields \(0.03 < e_2^u+e_2^d < 0.13\), implying \(\sigma_C^0 = (0.08-0.34)\) GeV\(^2\) at the same scale.
* The ratio of the direct to vacuum alignment contribution to \(\bar{g}_1\) is estimated as \(-0.63(e_2^u+e_2^d) = -(0.02-0.07)\), using lattice values \(\bar{m}\approx 3.6\) MeV, \(\sigma^0\approx 37\) MeV, and the sum-rule estimate \(r\approx 0.4\) GeV\(^2\).
* The vacuum alignment ratio \(\bar{g}_0/\bar{g}_1|_{\mathrm{vac}} \approx 0.12 (\tilde{d}_0/\tilde{d}_3)\) is derived from lattice inputs \(\varepsilon\approx 0.33\), \(\sigma^0\approx 37\) MeV, and \(\sigma^3\approx 2.9\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the classic relation between nucleon tensor charges and the transversity distribution \(h_1^q(x)\) (Jaffe and Ji, 1991) to the CP-odd sector, linking cMDM sigma terms to the twist-three function \(e^q(x)\). It provides an alternative to direct lattice QCD calculations of these matrix elements, which are not yet available (LATT2018). The results enable future SIDIS experiments to constrain the CP-odd pion-nucleon couplings \(\bar{g}_0\) and \(\bar{g}_1\) that are central to interpreting electric dipole moment searches in multi-nucleon systems (Engel et al., 2013; Yamanaka et al., 2017; Chupp et al., 2017).
