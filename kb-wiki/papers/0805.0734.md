# Mass dispersions from giant dipole resonances using the Balian-Veneroni variational approach

> The Balian-Veneroni variational approach has been implemented using a 3-dimensional time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) code with realistic Skyrme interactions and used to investigate the mass dispersions from giant dipole resonances in S-32 and Sn-132 decaying through particle emission. The fluctuations obtained are shown to be quantitatively larger than the standard TDHF results.

# Mass dispersions from giant dipole resonances using the Balian-Veneroni variational approach

## Key claim
The Balian-Veneroni (BV) variational approach, implemented with a fully 3-dimensional time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) code using realistic Skyrme interactions, yields quantitatively larger mass fluctuations from giant dipole resonances decaying through particle emission than standard TDHF calculations, but the method requires extrapolation to the epsilon-to-zero limit and is computationally intensive due to the need for multiple time-reversed TDHF runs.

## Method
The authors performed static Hartree-Fock calculations to obtain ground states for S-32 and Sn-132, then excited the nuclei with a dipole boost to create giant dipole resonances. They ran TDHF forward in time to t1 = 250 fm/c, computed the standard TDHF mass fluctuation, then applied the BV boundary condition by multiplying the final-state wavefunctions with a phase factor exp(i*epsilon*theta(Rc - r)) for a range of epsilon values (0.05 to 0.95 in steps of 0.05), ran TDHF backwards in time to t0, and extracted the BV fluctuation by fitting a straight line to the linear portion of the Delta N^2 versus epsilon curve and extrapolating to epsilon = 0.

## Key numbers
- For S-32: standard TDHF mass fluctuation Delta N^2_TDHF = 4.08 with mean particle number <N> = 26.65 (emission of approximately 5 nucleons) using cutoff radius Rc = 8 fm; BV result Delta N^2_BV = 5.92, representing a 20% increase in Delta N relative to standard TDHF.
- For S-32: dipole oscillation periods of approximately 71 fm/c for Qx (excitation energy Ex approximately 17.5 MeV) and approximately 68 fm/c for Qy and Qz (excitation energies Ey approximately Ez approximately 18.3 MeV), consistent with a prolate deformed ground state (beta2 = 0.11).
- For Sn-132: standard TDHF mass fluctuation Delta N^2_TDHF = 8.46 with <N> = 121.02 (emission of 11 nucleons); BV result Delta N^2_BV = 13.30, representing a 25% increase in Delta N relative to standard TDHF.
- For Sn-132: dipole oscillation period of approximately 88 fm/c corresponding to resonance energy approximately 14.1 MeV, compared with the experimentally measured value of 16.1(7) MeV from Adrich (2005).
- For S-32: HF ground state binding energy 260.36 MeV (experimental value 271.78 MeV from Audi (2003)); for Sn-132: HF binding energy 1099.71 MeV (accepted value 1102.85 MeV from Audi (2003)).

## Context
This work extends the Balian-Veneroni variational theory (Balian and Veneroni, 1970s) from simplified interactions with spherical or axial symmetry (Troudet, 1980s; Marston, 1980s; Bonche, 1980s) to fully 3-dimensional TDHF with realistic Skyrme interactions (SLy6 parametrization). The standard TDHF approach is known to underestimate fluctuations in single-particle observables due to its neglect of two-body correlations (Reinhard, 1980s). The method enables improved calculation of mass dispersions in nuclear decays and is being applied to heavy ion collisions.
