# The $B=5$ Skyrmion as a two-cluster system

> The classical $B=5$ Skyrmion can be approximated by a two-cluster system where a $B=1$ Skyrmion is attached to a core $B=4$ Skyrmion. We quantise this system, allowing the $B=1$ to freely orbit the core. The configuration space is 11-dimensional but simplifies significantly after factoring out the overall spin and isospin degrees of freedom. We exactly solve the free quantum problem and then include an interaction potential between the Skyrmions numerically. The resulting energy spectrum is compared to the corresponding nuclei -- the Helium-5/Lithium-5 isodoublet. We find approximate parity doubling not seen in the experimental data. In addition, we fail to obtain the correct ground state spin. The framework laid out for this two-cluster system can readily be modified for other clusters and in particular for other $B=4n+1$ nuclei, of which the $B=5$ is the simplest example.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $B=5$ Skyrmion, quantized as a two-cluster system with a $B=1$ Skyrmion orbiting a $B=4$ core, produces an energy spectrum that can be compared to the Helium-5/Lithium-5 isodoublet, but the model yields approximate parity doubling not seen in experimental data and fails to obtain the correct ground state spin.

## Method

The authors quantize the $B=5$ Skyrmion as a two-cluster system where a $B=1$ Skyrmion orbits a fixed $B=4$ cubic core at a fixed radius, with both clusters free to rotate and isorotate. They factor out overall spin and isospin degrees of freedom from the 11-dimensional configuration space, solve the free quantum problem exactly using Wigner-D functions and Finkelstein-Rubinstein constraints, and then include an interaction potential between the Skyrmions numerically by diagonalizing a truncated set of free states.

## Key numbers

- The $B=5$ Skyrmion separates into two clusters as the loosely bound potential parameter $m_2$ is increased from 0 to 3.2, with $m_1=1$ (pion mass) held fixed.
- The symmetry group $\mathcal{G}_{JI}$ has 48 elements, with irreducible representations including dimensions 1, 2, 3, and 4; the character table shows the $\boldsymbol{4}$ irrep has character -4 under a $2\pi$ rotation.
- For $(J,I,S,T) = (1/2,1/2,1/2,1/2)$ there is one allowed state; for $(J,I,S,T) = (3/2,1/2,5/2,1/2)$ there are four allowed states.
- The ground states of Helium-5 and Lithium-5 have spin $3/2$ experimentally, but the model produces the $3/2^-$ and $1/2^-$ states in the wrong order.

## Context

This work extends previous Skyrme model calculations that reproduced low-energy spectra for light nuclei with $B=1-8$ except the $^5$Li/$^5$He isodoublet, as noted in work by Manton, Murugan, and Wainwright (2015) and Halcrow (2017). The two-cluster approach mirrors the shell model description of an inert $\alpha$-particle core plus one orbiting nucleon, and the framework is designed to be applicable to other $B=4n+1$ nuclei and general two-cluster systems such as Lithium-7 or $\alpha$-particle scattering.
