# Confining density functional approach for color superconducting quark matter and mesonic correlations

> We present a novel relativistic density-functional approach to modeling quark matter with a mechanism to mimic confinement. The quasiparticle treatment of quarks provides their suppression due to large quark selfenergy already at the mean-field level. We demonstrate that our approach is equivalent to a chiral quark model with medium-dependent couplings. The dynamical restoration of the chiral symmetry is ensured by construction of the density functional. Beyond the mean field, quark correlations in the pseudoscalar channel are described within the Gaussian approximation. This explicitly introduces pionic states into the model. Their contribution to the thermodynamics potential is analyzed within the Beth-Uhlenbeck framework. The modification of the meson mass spectrum in the vicinity of thee (de)confinement transition is interpreted as the Mott transition. Supplemented with the vector repulsion and diquark pairing the model is applied to construct a hybrid quark-hadron EoS of cold compact-star matter. We study the connection of such a hybrid EoS with the stellar mass-radius relation and tidal deformability. The model results are compared to various observational constraints including the NICER radius measurement of PSR J0740+6620 and the tidal deformability constraint from GW170817. The model is shown to be consistent with the constraints, still allowing for further improvement by adjusting the vector repulsion and diquark pairing couplings.

## Key claim
The paper presents a relativistic density-functional approach to quark matter that mimics confinement via a divergent quark self-energy at low densities, and demonstrates that this approach is equivalent to a chiral quark model with medium-dependent couplings. The model is shown to be consistent with observational constraints from NICER and GW170817, but the authors note that further improvement is possible by adjusting the vector repulsion and diquark pairing couplings.

## Method
The authors construct a density functional for two-flavor quark matter that depends on the chiral invariant \((\overline{q}q)^2 + (\overline{q}i\gamma_5\vec\tau q)^2\), expanded to second order around mean-field expectation values. At zero chemical potential, pseudoscalar correlations are treated within the Gaussian approximation and the pion contribution to the thermodynamic potential is analyzed via the Beth-Uhlenbeck formalism. At zero temperature, the model is extended to include vector repulsion and diquark pairing in the 2SC channel, and a hybrid quark-hadron equation of state is constructed using the Maxwell construction with the hadronic KVORcut03 model.

## Context
This work extends the string-flip model (SFM) approach of Kaltenborn (2017) by formulating it in a chirally symmetric way and introducing diquark pairing channels not previously studied within the density functional framework. The model is applied to construct hybrid quark-hadron equations of state for neutron stars, and the results are compared to observational constraints from the NICER radius measurement of PSR J0740+6620 (Riley 2021, Miller 2021) and the tidal deformability constraint from GW170817 (Abbott 2018). The approach enables further studies of the interplay between confinement, chiral symmetry restoration, and color superconductivity in compact-star matter.
