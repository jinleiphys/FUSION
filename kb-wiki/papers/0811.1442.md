# A stabilized pairing functional

> We propose a modified pairing functional for nuclear structure calculations which avoids the abrupt phase transition between pairing and non-pairing states. The intended application is the description of nuclear collective motion where the smoothing of the transition is compulsory to remove singularities. The stabilized pairing functional allows a thoroughly variational formulation, unlike the Lipkin-Nogami (LN) scheme which is often used for the purpose of smoothing. First applications to nuclear ground states and collective excitations prove the reliability and efficiency of the proposed stabilized pairing.

# A stabilized pairing functional

## Key claim

The authors propose a modified pairing functional that avoids the abrupt phase transition between pairing and non-pairing states by introducing a counteracting term proportional to the inverse of the pairing energy, which diverges as the pairing gap approaches zero. The stabilized functional provides a thoroughly variational formulation, unlike the Lipkin-Nogami (LN) scheme, and is intended specifically for nuclear collective motion calculations where smoothing the transition is compulsory to remove singularities.

## Method

The stabilized pairing functional is constructed by modifying any given pairing energy functional E_pair as E_pair^(stab) = E_pair - E_cutp^2 / E_pair, where E_cutp is a new cutoff parameter. The total energy is then varied with respect to single-particle wavefunctions and occupation amplitudes to yield coupled mean-field and gap equations, with the stabilized gap taking the form Δ_k^(stab) = Δ_k (1 + E_cutp^2 / E_pair^2). The method is tested on a schematic two-level model with N=20 particles and constant pairing force, then applied to realistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations using the SkI3 parameterization for ground states of Sn isotopes and for collective deformation paths of 132Sn.

## Key numbers

- In the two-level model with N=20, BCS produces a phase transition at relative coupling strength G/E_ul ≈ 0.05; LN and stabilized pairing both remove this discontinuity.
- For Sn isotopes, the average size of binding energy modification from stabilized pairing is 0.2–0.5 MeV, compared to typical Skyrme force uncertainties of 0.7–1 MeV.
- The effect on charge r.m.s. radii is extremely small, below typical uncertainties of 0.02–0.04 fm.
- A good choice for the cutoff parameter is E_cutp = 0.3 MeV, which produces small side-effects and comes close to LN results.
- Stabilized pairing provides a factor three faster convergence than LN in computing collective deformation paths.

## Context

This work extends the standard BCS treatment of pairing in nuclear mean-field models, which suffers from an unphysical sudden phase transition in finite systems as noted by Gro (1997). The widely used Lipkin-Nogami (LN) scheme (Lipkin 1960, Nogami 1964) avoids this transition but introduces complicated terms, is not strictly variational, and slows numerical convergence. The stabilized pairing functional offers a simpler, fully variational alternative that is particularly suited for large-amplitude collective motion calculations (e.g., Ben 2003, Fle 2004) and future time-dependent mean-field applications where phase transitions would be disastrous.
