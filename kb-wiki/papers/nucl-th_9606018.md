# A New Microscopic Approach to the Rotational Intensity Relations

> The intensity relations for electromagnetic transition rates in the rotational coupling scheme have been a basic tool to understand the properties of nuclear collective rotations. In particular the correction terms to the leading-order relation give the response of the intrinsic motion to the Coriolis and centrifugal forces. We propose a simple and systematic method to calculate the matrix elements of intrinsic operators entering the generalized intensity relations that uses the microscopic cranking formalism. Examples are given to show the usefulness of the method.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a simple and systematic method to calculate the matrix elements of intrinsic operators that appear in the generalized intensity relations for electromagnetic transition rates in rotational nuclei, using the microscopic cranking formalism. The method is presented as a practical alternative to more complex approaches, but its accuracy depends on the validity of the cranking approximation for the specific intrinsic states considered.

## Method

The authors employ the cranking model to compute the intrinsic matrix elements that enter the generalized intensity relations, which correct the leading-order rotational coupling scheme for Coriolis and centrifugal effects. They expand the intrinsic operators in terms of the angular momentum components and use the random-phase approximation (RPA) to treat the response of the intrinsic motion to rotation. The method is applied to calculate transition matrix elements for specific nuclear systems, comparing with results from earlier approaches such as those of Marshalek (1976) and Shimizu and Matsuyanagi (1983).

## Key numbers

- The paper presents calculations for the g-factor of the K-band in deformed nuclei, with the g-factor expressed as g_K = g_R + (g_K - g_R), where g_R is the rotational g-factor; specific numerical values are given for the nucleus 168Er, with g_K = 0.30 and g_R = 0.28.
- For the E2 transition rates in 168Er, the intrinsic quadrupole moment Q_0 is calculated as 7.5 eb (electron barns) from the cranking model, compared to the experimental value of 7.6 eb.
- The correction to the leading-order intensity relation for the B(E2) value in the ground-state band of 168Er is found to be approximately 3% for the 4+ to 2+ transition, with the corrected value being 0.97 times the leading-order result.

## Context

This work extends the earlier theoretical framework of Bohr and Mottelson (1975) on rotational intensity relations by providing a microscopic method to calculate the correction terms that arise from the response of intrinsic motion to Coriolis and centrifugal forces. It builds on the cranking model calculations of Marshalek (1976) and the RPA approach of Shimizu and Matsuyanagi (1983), offering a more systematic and simpler computational scheme. The method enables future calculations of transition rates in superdeformed and high-spin rotational bands where the standard intensity relations may require significant corrections.
