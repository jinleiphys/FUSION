# Isoscalar-isovector proton-neutron pairing and quartet condensation in N=Z nuclei

> We show that the correlations generated in the ground state of $N=Z$ nuclei by the isovector and isoscalar pairing forces can be treated with high precision as a condensate of alpha-like quartets. To treat these correlations, the quartet condensation model (QCM) is extended to the treatment of spherically symmetric isovector $(T=1,J=0)$ and isoscalar $(T=0,J=1)$ pairing forces . Within QCM we discuss the competition between $T=1$ and $T=0$ pairing correlations in the case of a two-level model and for $N=Z$ nuclei with nucleons moving in the open shells above $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca and $^{100}$Sn. We show that in $N=Z$ systems isovector and isoscalar proton-neutron pairing correlations always coexist.

# Isoscalar-isovector proton-neutron pairing and quartet condensation in N=Z nuclei

## Key claim

The ground state correlations generated by both isovector (T=1, J=0) and isoscalar (T=0, J=1) pairing forces in N=Z nuclei can be treated with high precision as a condensate of identical alpha-like quartets, provided the spin-orbit interaction is not so strong that the isoscalar ground state shifts to J=2. The authors show that isovector and isoscalar proton-neutron pairing correlations always coexist in N=Z systems, contrary to some earlier HFB calculations that predicted coexistence only for particular forces or nuclei.

## Method

The quartet condensation model (QCM) is extended to treat spherically symmetric isovector and isoscalar pairing forces simultaneously, representing the ground state as a product of identical quartets formed by coupling two isovector pairs or two isoscalar pairs to total J=0 and T=0. The mixing amplitudes defining the collective quartets are determined variationally by minimizing the expectation value of the pairing Hamiltonian, and the accuracy is tested against exact diagonalization for a two-level model (f7/2 and f5/2 orbits) and for realistic N=Z nuclei in the sd, pf, and 100Sn valence shells.

## Key numbers

- For 6 proton-neutron pairs in f7/2 and f5/2 orbits with spin-orbit splitting of 7 MeV, the QCM error for isovector pairing correlation energy is 0.5% and for isoscalar pairing is 16.7% (the latter because the exact ground state becomes J=2 rather than J=0)
- For the physical mixing parameter x=0.2 (corresponding to g0/g1 ratio of about 1.5), the QCM error for correlation energy is approximately 0.1%
- For 24Mg with Hamiltonian (1), QCM gives correlation energy 5.914 MeV with 0.7% error; isovector pairing energy -4.545 MeV; isoscalar pairing energy -2.660 MeV
- For 112Ba with Hamiltonian (1), QCM gives correlation energy 14.377 MeV with 0.5% error; isovector pairing energy -14.376 MeV; isoscalar pairing energy -4.994 MeV
- For 112Ba with the general Hamiltonian (17), QCM gives correlation energy 7.017 MeV with 0.59% error, compared to exact 7.059 MeV

## Context

This work extends the quartet condensation model previously developed for isovector pairing only (Sambataro and Sandulescu, 2013) to include both isovector and isoscalar pairing channels. It builds on the exactly solvable SO(8) and SU(4) models (Dobes, 1980; Lerma and collaborators, 2011) which show that degenerate-level N=Z systems are exactly described by quartet condensates rather than pair condensates. The results contradict earlier HFB calculations (Goodman, 1979; Gerzelis and Bertsch, 1980) that found coexistence of isovector and isoscalar pairing only for specific forces or nuclei; the QCM approach shows coexistence for all admixtures. The work enables future studies of alpha-particle transfer reactions, predicting collective enhancement with quartet number rather than enhancement of proton-neutron pair transfer.
