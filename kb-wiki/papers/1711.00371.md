# Transport coefficients in neutron star cores in BHF approach. Comparison of different nucleon potentials

> Thermal conductivity and shear viscosity of npe$μ$ matter in non-superfluid neutron star cores are considered in the framework of Brueckner-Hartree-Fock many-body theory. We extend our previous work (Shternin, Baldo and Haensel, 2013) by analysing different nucleon-nucleon potentials and different three-body forces. We find that the use of different potentials leads up to one order of magnitude variations in the values of the nucleon contribution to transport coefficients. The nucleon contribution dominates the thermal conductivity, but for all considered models the shear viscosity is dominated by leptons.

## Key claim

The use of different nucleon-nucleon potentials and three-body forces in Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations leads to up to one order of magnitude variation in the nucleon contribution to thermal conductivity and shear viscosity in non-superfluid neutron star cores; however, the shear viscosity is dominated by leptons for all considered models, while the nucleon contribution dominates the thermal conductivity.

## Method

The authors calculated thermal conductivity and shear viscosity of npeμ matter in non-superfluid neutron star cores using the non-relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) many-body approach. They employed several nucleon-nucleon potentials: Argonne v18 (Av18) and CD-Bonn, each supplemented with the Urbana IX (UIX) three-body force, as well as Av18 with a meson-exchange three-body force (Av18+TBF). The transport coefficients were obtained by solving the linearized system of transport equations for a multicomponent Fermi liquid, using the effective masses and quasiparticle scattering amplitudes derived from the BHF approximation.

## Key numbers

- At baryon density n_B = 0.3 fm^{-3} and T = 10^8 K for the Av18+TBF model: thermal conductivity κ = 1.2 × 10^{23} erg cm^{-1} s^{-1} K^{-1}
- At the same conditions: shear viscosity η = 2.8 × 10^{18} g cm^{-1} s^{-1}
- At the same conditions: neutron effective relaxation time τ_n = 1.2 × 10^{-16} s, proton effective relaxation time τ_p = 0.79 × 10^{-16} s
- At the same conditions: neutron effective mass m_n^* = 0.81 (in units of nucleon mass), proton effective mass m_p^* = 0.67
- At the same conditions: proton fraction x_p = 0.1
- The lepton contribution to shear viscosity has non-Fermi-liquid temperature dependence η_{eμ} ∝ T^{-5/3}
- The lepton contribution to thermal conductivity has non-Fermi-liquid temperature dependence κ_{eμ} = const(T)

## Context

This work extends the previous study by Shternin, Baldo and Haensel (2013) which calculated transport coefficients using only the Av18+UIX potential. The authors compare results from different nucleon-nucleon potentials (Av18 and CD-Bonn) and three-body forces (UIX and TBF), following the same microscopic potentials used in Baldo (2014). The findings show that in-medium effects are crucial, as two-body level calculations give much higher values than free-space interaction results, while inclusion of the UIX three-body force partially counteracts this effect; the meson-exchange TBF model has a much smaller effect. The authors note that the precise value of thermal conductivity is usually not important because it is so large that the neutron star core is nearly isothermal, and they identify the study of superfluidity effects as a future project.
