# Viscosity and the Soft Ridge at RHIC

> Correlation studies exhibit a ridge-like feature in rapidity and azimuthal angle, with and without a jet trigger. We ask whether the feature in untriggered correlations can be a consequence of transverse flow and viscous diffusion.

# Viscosity and the Soft Ridge at RHIC

## Key claim
The soft ridge observed in untriggered momentum correlations at RHIC can be explained by the combined effects of transverse flow and viscous diffusion, but the viscosity-to-entropy ratio extracted from these data remains uncertain within a factor of four (0.08 to 0.32) because the measured observable differs from the ideal covariance that would directly constrain the viscosity.

## Method
The authors extend a previously developed hydrodynamic diffusion equation for momentum density fluctuations to include transverse radial flow, assuming Bjorken longitudinal flow coupled to a radial flow field that depends on transverse position and proper time. They solve this diffusion equation to obtain the spatial correlation function of momentum current fluctuations, then project it onto momentum space using a Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription with a blast-wave parameterization of the mean flow, fitting the flow parameters to measured average transverse momentum and elliptic flow data.

## Key numbers
- Viscosity-to-entropy ratio range: 0.08 < eta/s < 0.11 is consistent with the azimuthal and rapidity width data when other parameters are held fixed, but the full uncertainty range from the earlier work remains 0.08 < eta/s < 0.32 due to unknown number density contributions.
- The rapidity width difference between central and peripheral collisions is given by sigma_eta_c^2 - sigma_eta_p^2 = 4 nu (tau_f_p^{-1} - tau_f_c^{-1}), where nu is the kinematic viscosity and tau_f are freeze-out times.
- Centrality is quantified using 2N_bin/N_part, where N_bin and N_part are numbers of binary collisions and participants respectively.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier study (Gavin 2006) that attributed rapidity broadening of untriggered momentum correlations to viscous diffusion and extracted a first estimate of eta/s. It directly addresses the centrality dependence of both the azimuthal and rapidity widths of the soft ridge measured by STAR (Daugherity 2006, STAR momentum correlation results). The results are complementary to an alternative explanation by Lappi and McLerran (2008) that attributes the soft ridge amplitude to glasma correlations plus radial flow but does not explain the centrality dependence of the widths; the authors suggest glasma may set the initial conditions for the hydrodynamic evolution they describe. Direct measurements of the covariance C rather than the related quantity Delta_sigma_pt:n^2 are needed to reduce the dominant uncertainty in the viscosity estimate.
