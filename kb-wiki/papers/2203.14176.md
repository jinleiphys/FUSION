# Ab initio calculation of the $β$-decay from $^{11}$Be to a p${+}^{10}$Be resonance

> The exotic $β$-delayed proton emission is calculated in $^{11}$Be from first principles using chiral two- and three-nucleon forces. To investigate the unexpectedly-large branching ratio measured in [PRL 123, 082501 (2019)] we calculate the proposed $(1/2^+,1/2)$ proton resonance in $^{11}$B using the no-core shell model with continuum. This calculation helps to address whether this enhancement is caused by unknown dark decay modes or an unobserved proton resonance. We report a branching ratio of $b_p = (1.3\pm0.5)\times10^{-6}$, suggesting that its unexpectedly-large value is caused by an unobserved proton resonance in $^{11}$B.

## Key claim

The calculated branching ratio of \(b_p = (1.3\pm0.5)\times10^{-6}\) for the \(\beta\)-delayed proton emission from \(^{11}\)Be is lower than the experimentally observed value of \(1.3(3)\times10^{-5}\), but the authors argue this is consistent because the non-resonant decay branching ratio from prior work is two orders of magnitude smaller, and the discrepancy supports the existence of an unobserved \((1/2^+,1/2)\) proton resonance in \(^{11}\)B rather than exotic dark decay modes.

## Method

The authors performed no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) calculations using chiral two- and three-nucleon forces (NN-N\(^4\)LO(500)+3N\(_{\rm lnl}\)) softened by similarity renormalization group evolution to \(\lambda_{\rm SRG}=1.8\) fm\(^{-1}\) with a harmonic oscillator basis of \(\hbar\Omega = 18\) MeV and basis sizes up to \(N_{\rm max}=7\). They carried out \(p+^{10}\)Be scattering calculations to identify the proposed \((1/2^+,1/2)\) resonance in \(^{11}\)B, then developed the framework to compute \(\beta\)-decay Gamow-Teller matrix elements within the NCSMC approach and evaluated the branching ratio by integrating the energy-dependent \(B_{\rm GT}(E)\) over the resonance region folded with the phase-space factor.

## Key numbers

- Branching ratio for \(\beta\)-delayed proton emission: \(b_p = (1.3\pm0.5)\times10^{-6}\) (NCSMC\(_{\rm pheno}\) at \(N_{\rm max}=7\))
- Experimental branching ratio from Ayyad et al. (2019): \(b_p = 1.3(3)\times10^{-5}\)
- Indirect experimental branching ratio from Riisager et al. (2014): \(b_p = 8.3(9)\times10^{-6}\)
- Prior theoretical branching ratio from Baye (2011): \(b_p = 3.0\times10^{-8}\)
- \(B({\rm GT})\) for the transition to the \((1/2^+,1/2)\) resonance: \(0.565\) (NCSMC\(_{\rm pheno}\) at \(N_{\rm max}=7\))
- Experimental \(B({\rm GT})\) from Ayyad et al. (2019): \(5.5^{+8.3}_{-3.3}\)
- \(Q\)-value at the \(p+^{10}\)Be threshold: \(Q_0 = 0.281\) MeV
- Half-life of \(^{11}\)Be: \(T^{^{11}{\rm Be}}_{1/2} = 13.8\) s
- Neutron separation energy of \(^{11}\)Be: 0.5016 MeV
- Experimentally predicted resonance energy: 197 keV
- Phenomenologically adjusted \((3/2^+,1/2)\) resonance appears at 573 keV above threshold

## Context

This work extends prior NCSMC calculations of nuclear structure and scattering to the first application of the NCSMC approach for \(\beta\)-decay transitions. It directly addresses the discrepancy between the experimental branching ratios measured by Riisager (2014) and Ayyad (2019) and the much smaller cluster-expansion calculation by Baye (2011). The results contradict shell-model analyses by Volya (2020) and shell model embedded in the continuum calculations by Marek (2021) that claimed the observed branching ratio could not be explained by a proton resonance, while supporting the halo effective field theory analysis by Elkamhawy (2021). The work enables future inclusion of the \(^7\)Li+\(\alpha\) and \(^{10}\)B+n mass partitions to further improve the description.
