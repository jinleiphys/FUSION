# Time-reversal-invariance-violating nucleon-nucleon potential in the 1/N_c expansion

> We apply the large-$N_c$ expansion to the time-reversal-invariance-violating (TV) nucleon-nucleon potential. The operator structures contributing to next-to-next-to-leading order in the large-$N_c$ counting are constructed. For the TV and parity-violating case we find a single operator structure at leading order. The TV but parity-conserving potential contains two leading-order terms, which however are suppressed by 1/$N_c$ compared to the parity-violating potential. Comparison with phenomenological potentials, including the chiral EFT potential in the TV parity-violating case, leads to large-$N_c$ scaling relations for TV meson-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon couplings.

# Time-reversal-invariance-violating nucleon-nucleon potential in the 1/N_c expansion

## Key claim

The large-N_c expansion of QCD predicts a hierarchy among time-reversal-violating (TV) nucleon-nucleon potentials: the TV and parity-violating (TVPV) potential is enhanced by a factor of N_c relative to the TV but parity-conserving (TVPC) potential, with the dominant TVPV operator being isovector and arising from pion and omega exchanges, though this hierarchy must be superimposed on any suppression from the underlying beyond-standard-model physics.

## Method

The authors construct the TV nucleon-nucleon potential up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in the 1/N_c expansion using the Hartree expansion of the nuclear Hamiltonian, where spin, isospin, and mixed operators are assigned N_c-scaling based on their matrix elements between single-nucleon states. The resulting operator structures are compared with phenomenological parameterizations including the general Herczeg (1966) form, one-meson-exchange potentials (pi, eta, rho, omega mesons), and chiral effective field theory potentials, allowing extraction of large-N_c scaling relations for the TV meson-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon couplings.

## Key numbers

- The TVPV potential has a leading-order (LO) contribution at order N_c, with a single operator structure: N_c * U^1(p_-^2) * p_- * (sigma_1 tau_1^z - sigma_2 tau_2^z)
- The TVPC potential has LO contributions at order N_c^0 (i.e., suppressed by 1/N_c relative to TVPV), with two operator structures involving p_-^i p_+^j [sigma_1 sigma_2]^{ij}_2 multiplied by either tau_1*tau_2 or [tau_1 tau_2]^{zz}_2
- Corrections to the LO terms in both potentials are suppressed by single powers of 1/N_c, but for a given isospin sector the first correction is suppressed by 1/N_c^2
- The isovector TVPV pion-nucleon coupling bar_g_pi^(1) scales as N_c^{1/2}, while bar_g_pi^(0) and bar_g_pi^(2) scale as N_c^{-1/2} (suppressed by 1/N_c relative to bar_g_pi^(1))
- The TVPC rho-meson coupling tilde_g_rho scales as N_c^{1/2}, while the h_1 coupling tilde_g_h scales as N_c^{-1/2}
- The strong couplings scale as: g_pi ~ N_c^{3/2}, g_eta ~ N_c^{1/2}, g_omega ~ N_c^{1/2}, g_rho ~ N_c^{-1/2}, g_h ~ N_c^{-1/2}

## Context

This work extends the large-N_c analysis previously applied to the time-reversal-conserving parity-conserving (TCPC) NN potential by Kaplan and Manohar (1995, 1996), to three-nucleon forces by Phillips and Schat (2013), and to the time-reversal-conserving parity-violating (TCPV) potential by Phillips, Samart, and Schindler (2014) and Schindler, Phillips, and Samart (2015). The derived hierarchy of TV couplings provides theoretical constraints that can simplify analyses of TV observables such as neutron and nuclear electric dipole moments, and suggests that the isovector TV pion exchange may enhance sensitivity in neutron scattering experiments on nuclear targets as discussed by Bowman (2014). The results also predict that the LO chiral EFT TVPV potential contains the dominant large-N_c operator, unlike the TCPV case where the LO chiral pion exchange is subleading in the 1/N_c expansion.
