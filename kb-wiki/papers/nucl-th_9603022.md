# Bethe-Salpeter Amplitudes and Static Properties of the Deuteron

> Extended calculations of the deuteron's static properties, based on the numerical solution of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, are presented. A formalism is developed, which provides a comparative analysis of the covariant amplitudes in various representations and nonrelativistic wave functions. The magnetic and quadrupole moments of the deuteron are calculated in the Bethe-Salpeter formalism and the role of relativistic corrections is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a fully covariant calculation of the deuteron's magnetic and quadrupole moments using the exact numerical solution of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, without additional approximations such as separable interactions or putting one nucleon on mass-shell. The authors claim that the Bethe-Salpeter formalism consistently accounts for meson exchange effects due to pair creation currents, but the results depend on the specific meson-exchange parameters fitted to NN scattering data.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation in ladder approximation for the deuteron bound state, including exchanges of six mesons (π, σ, ω, ρ, δ, η) with parameters taken from the fit of Zuilhof and Tjon (1982) to NN phase shifts and the deuteron binding energy. They computed the electromagnetic current matrix elements in the Breit frame using the numerically obtained Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, then extracted the magnetic and quadrupole moments from the form factors at zero momentum transfer. The relativistic amplitudes were also transformed to a spectroscopic basis (³S₁⁺⁺, ³D₁⁺⁺, etc.) to enable comparison with nonrelativistic wave functions from the Bonn and Paris potentials.

## Key numbers

- The D-wave probability extracted from the relativistic calculation is approximately P_D ≈ 5%, compared to 4.3% for the Bonn potential and 5.9% for the Paris potential.
- The charge density and spin density from the Bethe-Salpeter solution agree with nonrelativistic results for momenta |p| ≤ 0.5 GeV/c, with deviations at higher momenta attributed to model differences rather than purely relativistic effects.
- The normalization condition for the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude yields a diagonal form with weighting factors ω_α that include terms (M_D - 2E_p) and (2E_p + M_D) for the different partial wave components.

## Context

This work extends the covariant treatment of the deuteron by Zuilhof and Tjon (1982) and Gross (1982) by using the exact numerical solution of the Bethe-Salpeter equation rather than approximations such as separable interactions or the one-nucleon-on-mass-shell approach. The authors provide explicit transformation matrices between different representations of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, enabling comparison with nonrelativistic wave functions from the Bonn and Paris potentials. The results enable future calculations of deuteron electromagnetic form factors at finite momentum transfer and provide a foundation for extracting neutron structure functions from deuteron data at facilities such as CEBAF.
