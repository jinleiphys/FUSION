# Higher forbidden unique $β^-$ decay transitions and shell-model interpretation

> In the present work, we have predicted the half-lives for the $β^{-}$ decay for higher forbidden unique transitions in the mass range of nuclei from A = 40-138. For these transitions, the experimental data for half-lives are not available except for a few cases. The calculations for half-lives are performed within the framework of the nuclear shell model (SM). We have used the effective interactions sdpf-mu, gxpf1a, gwbxg, G-matrix, snet, sn100pn, and jj56pnb to perform the SM calculations in different mass regions. A comprehensive discussion has been made between the SM-predicted half-lives and the scaled half-lives from proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA). The results of the present study will be useful to plan new experiments to measure half-lives for these higher forbidden unique $β^{-}$ transitions.

## Key claim

The authors predict half-lives for higher forbidden unique β⁻ decay transitions in the mass range A = 40-138 using nuclear shell-model calculations, but note that experimental half-life data are not available for most of these transitions except for a few cases, making the predictions testable only by future experiments.

## Method

The authors performed nuclear shell-model calculations using seven different effective interactions (sdpf-mu, gxpf1a, gwbxg, G-matrix, snet, sn100pn, and jj56pnb) across different mass regions to compute one-body transition densities (OBTDs) between initial and final nuclear states. The partial half-lives were calculated from the integrated shape function using harmonic oscillator wave functions for single-particle matrix elements, with next-to-leading-order corrections included, and the results were compared with scaled half-lives from the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) approach by Kostensalo and Suhonen (2017).

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁰K(4⁻) → ⁴⁰Ca(0⁺) 3rd forbidden unique transition: shell-model half-life is 9.2 × 10⁸ yr (with g_A^eff = 1.0, g_V = 1.0), compared to experimental value of 1.398(3) × 10⁹ yr
- For ⁴⁸Ca(0⁺) → ⁴⁸Sc(5⁺) 4th forbidden unique transition: shell-model half-life is 5.1 × 10²⁰ yr, with experimental lower limit > 2.5 × 10²⁰ yr
- For ⁶⁰Fe(0⁺) → ⁶⁰Co(5⁺) 4th forbidden unique transition: shell-model half-life is 2.2 × 10¹⁹ yr (Q value = 237(3) keV)
- For ⁵²Sc(3⁺) → ⁵²Ti(0⁺) 2nd forbidden unique transition: shell-model half-life is 54.3 h (Q value = 8954(4) keV)
- For ⁹²Nb(7⁺) → ⁹²Mo(0⁺) 6th forbidden unique transition: shell-model half-life is 5.8 × 10²⁸ yr (Q value = 355.3(18) keV)
- For ⁹⁶Tc(7⁺) → ⁹⁶Ru(0⁺) 6th forbidden unique transition: shell-model half-life is 4.3 × 10³⁰ yr (Q value = 259(5) keV)
- For ¹¹⁸Cd(0⁺) → ¹¹⁸In*(8⁻) 7th forbidden unique transition: shell-model half-life is 8.6 × 10³³ yr (Q value = 527(21) keV)
- For ¹³⁸La(5⁺) → ¹³⁸Ce(0⁺) 4th forbidden unique transition: shell-model half-life is 6.2 × 10¹⁵ yr (Q value = 1052.42(41) keV)

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of higher forbidden unique β⁻ decay transitions previously performed using the pnQRPA approach by Kostensalo and Suhonen (2017), providing shell-model predictions as an alternative nuclear-structure framework. The results are intended to guide future experiments measuring half-lives for these rare transitions, which are relevant for understanding nuclear structure in exotic nuclei and for applications such as geochronology (e.g., ⁴⁰K decay). The paper also compares with experimental data for ⁴⁰K, ⁴⁸Ca, ⁹⁶Zr, and ¹³⁸La, where available, and discusses the role of the axial-vector coupling constant g_A quenching in these transitions.
