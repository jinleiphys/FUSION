# Two generalizations of the Boltzmann equation

> We connect two different generalizations of Boltzmann's kinetic theory by requiring the same stationary solution. Non-extensive statistics can be produced by either using corresponding collision rates nonlinear in the one-particle densities or equivalently by using nontrivial energy composition rules in the energy conservation constraint. Direct transformation formulas between key functions of the two approaches are given.

## Key claim

Two distinct generalizations of the Boltzmann equation, one using nonlinear collision rates in the one-particle densities and the other using nontrivial energy composition rules, are equivalent in that they can produce the same stationary non-extensive thermodynamic distribution, provided the energy composition rule is associative.

## Method

The authors connect the nonlinear Boltzmann equation (NLBE), where the collision rate depends on production factors \(a(f)\) and blocking factors \(b(f)\) with ratio \(\kappa(f)=a(f)/b(f)\), to the non-extensive Boltzmann equation (NEBE), where the collision rate remains multilinear but energy conservation is replaced by a generalized composition rule \(h(E_1,E_2)=h(E_3,E_4)\) derived from a monotonic mapping function \(X(E)\). They derive transformation formulas between \(\kappa(f)\) and \(X_s(E/T)\) (the scaled mapping function) by requiring the same stationary distribution \(f_{eq}\). The correspondence is demonstrated for several known distributions, including Maxwell-Boltzmann, Tsallis, Lévy, pure power-law, and quon distributions.

## Key numbers

- Tsallis distribution: \(f(E) = (1+aE)^{-1/aT}\) with Tsallis index \(q=1-aT\); the energy composition rule is \(h(x,y)=x+y+axy\).
- Lévy-type distribution: \(f(E) = \exp(-(E/T)^v)\) with fractional power \(v\); mapping function \(X(x)=x^v\).
- Pure power-law distribution: \(f(E) = (bE/T)^{1/(q-1)}\); mapping function \(X(x)=\frac{1}{1-q}\ln(bx)\).
- Quon distribution: \(f(E) = 1/(e^{E/T}+q)\); production-to-blocking ratio \(\kappa(f)=f/(1-qf)\).
- Numerical simulations for the Tsallis case used \(N=10^5-10^6\) test particles with energy mapping \(X(E)=\frac{1}{a}\ln(1+aE)\).
- The NEBE approach allows computer simulations with resource effort not worse than \(\mathcal{O}(N^2)\).

## Context

This work extends prior kinetic theory generalizations by Biro (NLBE, 2005) and Kaniadakis (NEBE, 2005), showing their formal equivalence for stationary states. It connects to Tsallis non-extensive thermodynamics (Tsallis, 1988) and the associated entropy, as well as to Rényi entropy. The results enable the use of either nonlinear collision rates or non-extensive energy composition rules in parton cascade simulations, with the NEBE approach offering computational advantages by avoiding phase-space blocking factors.
