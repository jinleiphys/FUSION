# Neutrinoless double beta decay in chiral effective field theory: lepton number violation at dimension seven

> We analyze neutrinoless double beta decay ($0νββ$) within the framework of the Standard Model Effective Field Theory. Apart from the dimension-five Weinberg operator, the first contributions appear at dimension seven. We classify the operators and evolve them to the electroweak scale, where we match them to effective dimension-six, -seven, and -nine operators. In the next step, after renormalization group evolution to the QCD scale, we construct the chiral Lagrangian arising from these operators. We develop a power-counting scheme and derive the two-nucleon $0νββ$ currents up to leading order in the power counting for each lepton-number-violating operator. We argue that the leading-order contribution to the decay rate depends on a relatively small number of nuclear matrix elements. We test our power counting by comparing nuclear matrix elements obtained by various methods and by different groups. We find that the power counting works well for nuclear matrix elements calculated from a specific method, while, as in the case of light Majorana neutrino exchange, the overall magnitude of the matrix elements can differ by factors of two to three between methods. We calculate the constraints that can be set on dimension-seven lepton-number-violating operators from $0νββ$ experiments and study the interplay between dimension-five and -seven operators, discussing how dimension-seven contributions affect the interpretation of $0νββ$ in terms of the effective Majorana mass $m_{ββ}$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, beyond the dimension-five Weinberg operator, the first contributions to neutrinoless double beta decay from Standard Model Effective Field Theory appear at dimension seven, and that the leading-order decay rate depends on a relatively small number of nuclear matrix elements, though the overall magnitude of these matrix elements can differ by factors of two to three between different nuclear-structure methods.

## Method

The authors classify all dimension-seven lepton-number-violating operators in the Standard Model Effective Field Theory, evolve them via renormalization group equations to the electroweak scale, and match them onto effective dimension-six, -seven, and -nine operators. They then perform renormalization group evolution to the QCD scale and construct the chiral Lagrangian arising from these operators, developing a power-counting scheme to derive the two-nucleon neutrinoless double beta decay currents up to leading order for each lepton-number-violating operator.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory treatment of neutrinoless double beta decay beyond the standard light Majorana neutrino exchange mechanism, which is commonly interpreted via the dimension-five Weinberg operator (Weinberg, 1979). The paper builds upon prior studies of dimension-six and dimension-nine operators by Doi (1985), Pas (1999), Deppisch (2012), and Horoi (2017), and on the dimension-seven operator classification by Lehman (2014). The results enable a systematic interpretation of future neutrinoless double beta decay experiments in terms of multiple possible lepton-number-violating mechanisms, and allow for studies of the interplay between dimension-five and dimension-seven contributions when extracting the effective Majorana mass $m_{\beta\beta}$.
