# Semiclassical Interpretation of the Mass Asymmetry in Nuclear Fission

> We give a semiclassical interpretation of the mass asymmetry in the fission of heavy nuclei. Using only a few classical periodic orbits and a cavity model for the nuclear mean field, we reproduce the onset of left-right asymmetric shapes at the fission isomer minimum and the correct topology of the deformation energy surface in the region of the outer fission barrier. We point at the correspondence of the single-particle quantum states responsible for the asymmetry with the leading classical orbits, both lying in similar equatorial planes perpendicular to the symmetry axis of the system.

# Semiclassical Interpretation of the Mass Asymmetry in Nuclear Fission

## Key claim
Using only a few classical periodic orbits and a cavity model for the nuclear mean field, the authors reproduce the onset of left-right asymmetric shapes at the fission isomer minimum and the correct topology of the deformation energy surface in the region of the outer fission barrier, but only for a specific range of deformations (c ≥ 1.4) where the shortest periodic orbits lie in equatorial planes perpendicular to the symmetry axis.

## Method
The authors replace the nuclear mean field by a cavity with reflecting walls and use the periodic orbit theory (POT) trace formula of Gutzwiller and Balian-Bloch to compute the oscillating part of the level density and the shell-correction energy. They employ a uniform approximation in terms of Pearcey's integral to handle bifurcations where three equatorial planes of periodic orbits nearly coincide. The shell-correction energy is calculated using only periodic orbits with winding number t=1 and up to p=10 reflections, with the diameter (p=2) and triangle (p=3) orbits being sufficient to reproduce the essential features.

## Key numbers
- The fission isomer minimum occurs at deformation c=1.42, h=0, alpha=0 for the chosen Fermi wave number k_F.
- The semiclassical shell-correction energy results are the same within a few percent when only orbits with p=2 and 3 (diameter and triangle orbits) are included, compared to using up to p_max=10 reflections.
- The energy unit used is E_0 = hbar^2/(2mR_0^2), where R_0 is the radius of the spherical box.
- The onset of mass asymmetry occurs for deformations c ≥ 1.42, corresponding to the fission isomer region.

## Context
This work extends the shell-correction method of Strutinsky (1967) by providing a semiclassical interpretation using periodic orbit theory, building on earlier work by Strutinsky et al. (1982) who generalized Gutzwiller's theory to systems with continuous symmetries and explained nuclear ground-state deformations. The authors reproduce the quantum-mechanical neutron shell-correction energy calculations for 240Pu from the work of the Lund group (1974), showing that the valley leading from the isomer minimum over the outer fission barrier follows the path of constant action of the leading classical orbits. This enables a deeper understanding of how specific single-particle quantum states with large angular momenta along the symmetry axis, identified by Gustafsson, Moller, and Nilsson (1971), correspond to classical periodic orbits in equatorial planes.
