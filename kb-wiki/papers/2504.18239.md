# Continuum quasiparticle random-phase approximation for $(n,γ)$ reactions on neutron-rich nuclei: collectivity and resonances in low-energy cross section

> We formulate a microscopic theory to calculate cross section of the radiative neutron capture on neutron-rich nuclei using the continuum quasiparticle random-phase approximation. This formulation is designed to be applied to neutron-rich nuclei around the $r$-process path, for which the compound nuclear model may not be appropriate. It takes into account effects of various excitation modes such as the soft dipole excitation, the giant resonances, and the quasiparticle resonance in addition to the surface vibrations such as quadrupole and octupole modes. We perform numerical calculations to demonstrate new features of the present theory, employing reactions $^{89}{\rm Ge}(n,γ)^{90}{\rm Ge}$ and $^{91}{\rm Zn}(n,γ)^{92}{\rm Zn}$ with $E1$ transitions populating the ground state, collective $2^{+}_{1}$ and $3^{-}_{1}$ states in $ ^{90}{\rm Ge}$ and $^{92}{\rm Zn}$. With these examples, we discuss enhanced resonance contributions in the $(n,γ)$ reaction at low energy, which originate from the quasiparticle resonance and the pygmy quadrupole resonance located just above the one neutron separation energy, and from combination with the low-lying octupole vibrational state.

## Key claim
The paper formulates a microscopic theory for radiative neutron capture on neutron-rich nuclei using the continuum quasiparticle random-phase approximation (cQRPA), claiming that this approach can describe enhanced resonance contributions at low energy from quasiparticle resonances and pygmy quadrupole resonances, but only for nuclei where the compound nuclear model may not be appropriate due to low level density at small excitation energies.

## Method
The authors formulate the (n,γ) reaction as the inverse of the (γ,n) process using the reciprocity theorem, employing the cQRPA approach within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) framework to describe the photoabsorption reaction leading to excited states including unbound single-particle configurations. They apply the Zangwill-Soven decomposition to evaluate the T matrix of the (γ,n) process, and perform numerical calculations using the Skyrme energy density functional (SkM* for 92Zn and SLy4 for 90Ge) with a density-dependent delta interaction for pairing, solving the radial HFB equation in a spherical box with Rmax=20 fm and mesh Δr=0.2 fm.

## Key numbers
- Neutron separation energy for the studied nuclei is S1n ~ 2-3 MeV, compared to S1n ~ 8 MeV for stable nuclei where the compound nuclear model works well
- The cutoff energy of quasiparticle states is Ecut=60 MeV with cutoff orbital angular momentum lcut=8
- The pairing interaction parameters are v0=-458.4 MeV and ρc=0.08 fm-3, with dimensionless parameters α=0.59, η=0.76 for 92Zn and α=0.59, η=0.69 for 90Ge
- The lowest-lying 2+ state is observed at 0.599 MeV in neighbor nucleus 84Zn and at 0.527 MeV in 86Ge
- The neutron Fermi energy for 90Ge is λn=-2.14 MeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous prototype model (Matsuo 2015) which described only γ decay feeding to the ground state, and a subsequent method (Saito 2023) that described γ transitions to excited states but without pair correlation, limiting it to closed-shell nuclei. The new formulation enables application to open-shell neutron-rich nuclei around the r-process path, particularly near the N=50 waiting point relevant to the weak r process (Surman 2014). The approach enables future calculations of (n,γ) cross sections incorporating collective excitations and resonances that have been neglected in direct capture models used in r-process studies (Mathews 1983, Rauscher 1998, Xu 2012).
