# Study of the phase diagram of dense two-color QCD within lattice simulation

> In this paper we carry out a low-temperature scan of the phase diagram of dense two-color QCD with $N_f=2$ quarks. The study is conducted using lattice simulation with rooted staggered quarks. At small chemical potential we observe the hadronic phase, where the theory is in a confining state, chiral symmetry is broken, the baryon density is zero and there is no diquark condensate. At the critical point $μ= m_π/2$ we observe the expected second order transition to Bose-Einstein condensation of scalar diquarks. In this phase the system is still in confinement in conjunction with non-zero baryon density, but the chiral symmetry is restored in the chiral limit. We have also found that in the first two phases the system is well described by chiral perturbation theory. For larger values of the chemical potential the system turns into another phase, where the relevant degrees of freedom are fermions residing inside the Fermi sphere, and the diquark condensation takes place on the Fermi surface. In this phase the system is still in confinement, chiral symmetry is restored and the system is very similar to the quarkyonic state predicted by SU($N_c$) theory at large $N_c$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the low-temperature phase diagram of dense two-color QCD with two flavors exhibits three distinct phases, hadronic, Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC), and Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS)/quarkyonic, but the transition between BEC and BCS is smooth rather than sharp, and the identification of the BCS phase relies on the diquark condensate scaling as \(\mu^2\) over a limited range of chemical potential.

## Method

The authors performed lattice simulations of SU(2) gauge theory (two-color QCD) with \(N_f=2\) rooted staggered fermions on a \(16^3 \times 32\) lattice at fixed inverse coupling \(\beta=2.15\) and bare quark mass \(ma=0.005\), corresponding to temperature \(T \approx 55\) MeV. They scanned the chemical potential \(\mu\) from 0 to 1759 MeV, measuring the Polyakov loop, chiral condensate, baryon density, and diquark condensate; for \(\mu \leq 1055\) MeV they simulated at three values of the diquark source \(\lambda\) and extrapolated linearly to \(\lambda \to 0\), while for larger \(\mu\) only \(\lambda=0.0005\) was used.

## Key numbers

* Critical chemical potential for the hadron-to-BEC transition: \(\mu^c = 215(10)\) MeV from a chiral perturbation theory fit, or \(\mu^c = 193(10)\) MeV from a modified fit, both consistent with \(\mu^c = m_\pi/2\) where \(m_\pi = 378(4)\) MeV.
* Lattice spacing: \(a = 0.112(1)\) fm at \(\beta=2.15\) and \(ma=0.005\), determined from the Sommer scale \(r_0 = 0.468(4)\) fm.
* Diquark condensate at \(\mu=211\) MeV extrapolated to \(\lambda \to 0\): \(\langle qq \rangle|_{\lambda \to 0} = -0.0021(12)/T^3\), compatible with zero; at \(\mu=246\) MeV: \(\langle qq \rangle|_{\lambda \to 0} = 0.0058(14)/T^3\), indicating the BEC phase.
* In the region \(\mu \in [528, 1055]\) MeV, the diquark condensate scales as \(\langle qq \rangle \sim \mu^2\), characteristic of BCS condensation on the Fermi surface.

## Context

This work extends prior lattice studies of two-color QCD by Kogut et al. (2001, 2002) using staggered fermions with \(N_f=4\) and by Hands et al. (2006, 2010) using Wilson fermions with \(N_f=2\), but with a lighter pion mass (\(m_\pi=378\) MeV versus heavier pions in earlier staggered studies) and a rooting procedure to achieve two fundamental flavors. The results confirm the qualitative phase structure predicted by chiral perturbation theory (Kogut 2000, Splittorff 2001) and the large-\(N_c\) quarkyonic matter concept (McLerran 2007), and enable future comparisons with functional renormalization group and NJL model calculations for dense two-color matter.
