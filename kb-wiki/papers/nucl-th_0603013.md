# Relativistic eikonal description of A(p,pN) reactions

> The authors present a relativistic and cross-section factorized framework for computing quasielastic A(p,pN) observables at intermediate and high energies. The model is based on the eikonal approximation and can accomodate both optical potentials and the Glauber method for dealing with the initial- and final-state interactions (IFSI). At lower nucleon energies, the optical-potential philosophy is preferred, whereas at higher energies the Glauber method is more natural. This versatility in dealing with the IFSI allows one to describe A(p,pN) reactions in a wide energy range. Most results presented here use optical potentials as this approach is argued to be the optimum choice for the kinematics of the experiments considered in the present paper. The properties of the IFSI factor, a function wherein the entire effect of the IFSI is contained, are studied in detail. The predictions of the presented framework are compared with two kinematically different experiments. First, differential cross sections for quasielastic proton scattering at 1 GeV off 12C, 16O, and 40Ca target nuclei are computed and compared to data from PNPI. Second, the formalism is applied to the analysis of a 4He(p,2p) experiment at 250 MeV. The optical-potential calculations are found to be in good agreement with the data from both experiments, showing the reliability of the adopted model in a wide energy range.

## Key claim

The authors present a relativistic, cross-section factorized framework for computing quasielastic A(p,pN) observables that can accommodate both optical potentials and the Glauber method for initial- and final-state interactions (IFSI), but most results use optical potentials because this approach is argued to be the optimum choice for the kinematics of the experiments considered in the paper.

## Method

The framework uses the eikonal approximation to describe the propagation of the incoming and outgoing protons, with the IFSI incorporated either through relativistic optical potentials (ROMEA) or through the relativistic multiple-scattering Glauber approximation (RMSGA). The cross section is factorized into a free proton-proton scattering cross section (obtained from the SAID code) multiplied by a distorted momentum distribution that accounts for the IFSI effects via an IFSI factor. The bound nucleon wave functions are obtained from a relativistic mean-field description, and the formalism is applied to compute differential cross sections for quasielastic proton scattering at 1 GeV off 12C, 16O, and 40Ca targets, and for 4He(p,2p) at 250 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross sections for quasielastic proton scattering at 1 GeV incident proton energy off 12C, 16O, and 40Ca target nuclei were computed and compared to data from PNPI.
- The formalism was applied to a 4He(p,2p) experiment at 250 MeV incident proton energy, with data from van Oers et al. (1982) at TRIUMF.
- The optical-potential calculations were found to be in good agreement with the data from both experiments.

## Context

This work extends the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) framework, which has been successful for A(p,pN) cross sections at energies of 76-600 MeV (Kullander 1971, Bhowmik 1974, Kitching 1980, van Oers 1982, Samanta 1986, Cowley 1991, 1995, 1998, Ikebata 1995, Miller 1998, Mano 1998, Carman 1999, Neveling 2002, Maxwell and Cooper), to the GeV energy regime where partial-wave expansions become cumbersome. The eikonal approach provides a computationally efficient alternative to full partial-wave DWIA calculations at higher energies, and the framework enables systematic studies of A(p,pN) reactions across a wide energy range from a few hundred MeV to GeV.
