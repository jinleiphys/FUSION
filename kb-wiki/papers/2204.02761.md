# Lepton pair photoproduction in peripheral relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We study the lepton pair photoproduction in peripheral heavy-ion collisions based on the formalism in our previous work [Phys. Rev. D 104, 056011 (2021)]. We present the numerical results for the distributions of the transverse momentum, azimuthal angle and invariant mass for $e^{+}e^{-}$ and $μ^{+}μ^{-}$ pairs as functions of the impact parameter and other kinematic variables in Au+Au collisions. Our calculation incorporates the information on the transverse momentum and polarization of photons which is essential to describe the experimental data. We observe a broadening effect in the transverse momentum for lepton pairs with and without smear effects. We also observe a significant enhancement in the distribution of $\cos(2φ)$ for $μ^{+}μ^{-}$ pairs. Our results provide a baseline for future studies of other higher order corrections beyond Born approximation and medium effects in the lepton pair production.

## Key claim

The authors extend their previous wave-packet formalism for lepton pair photoproduction from ultra-peripheral collisions (UPC) to peripheral heavy-ion collisions, demonstrating that incorporating the transverse momentum and polarization of photons is essential to reproduce experimental data, while noting that the observed broadening effect in transverse momentum is not fully explained by their Born-level calculation and may require higher-order corrections or medium effects.

## Method

The authors employ a narrow wave-packet description of colliding nuclei and the Born-level Wigner function for photons to compute differential cross sections for lepton pair photoproduction via the Breit-Wheeler process. They perform high-dimensional numerical integrations using the ZMCintegral algorithm to calculate distributions of transverse momentum, azimuthal angle, and invariant mass for electron-positron and muon-antimuon pairs in Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV across different centrality bins, incorporating a smearing effect extracted from J/ψ → l¯l measurements by STAR.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV with centrality bins: 60-80% (impact parameters 11.4-13.2 fm), 40-60% (9.4-11.6 fm), 10-40% (4.8-9.4 fm), and UPC (>2R_A where R_A = 1.2A^(1/3) fm)
- Momentum cutoffs: k_(1T)^(e-), k_(2T)^(e+) ≥ 200 MeV; P_T^(ee) ≤ 100 MeV in UPC and ≤ 150 MeV in peripheral collisions; k_(1)^(μ-), k_(2)^(μ+) ∈ [180, 300] MeV; P_T^(μμ) ≤ 100 MeV
- The √⟨(P_T^(ee))^2⟩ increases with decreasing impact parameter and increasing invariant mass M_ee, with values shown for M_ee ranges including [0.40, 0.76] GeV
- The average ⟨cos(4φ)⟩_e reaches maximum at P_T^(ee) ∼ 0.02-0.03 GeV in 60-80% and 80-100% centralities
- ⟨cos(2φ)⟩_μ is proportional to (m_μ/P_T^(μμ))^2 and is sizable, with minimal values in the range P_T^(μμ) ∼ 0.005-0.015 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous wave-packet formalism from Wang (2021) for UPC to peripheral collisions, and is compared with the generalized EPA (gEPA) approach of Zha (2018) and the QED model of Brandenburg (2021). The calculation provides a baseline for future studies of higher-order corrections beyond the Born approximation and medium effects in lepton pair production, and the authors note that the smear corrections extracted from STAR (2005) are insufficient to fully match experimental data, suggesting the need for Sudakov factors or Lorentz force effects as discussed by Klein (2018, 2020) and Li (2019).
