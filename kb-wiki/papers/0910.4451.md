# Astrophysics of dense quark matter in compact stars

> Massive neutron stars may harbor deconfined quark matter in their cores. I review some recent work on the microphysics and the phenomenology of compact stars with cores made of quark matter. This includes the equilibrium and stability of non-rotating and rapidly rotating stars, gravitational radiation from deformations in their quark cores, neutrino radiation and dichotomy of fast and slow cooling, and pulsar radio-timing anomalies.

## Key claim

Massive neutron stars may harbor deconfined quark matter in their cores, but the existence of stable hybrid configurations depends critically on the nuclear equation of state being hard enough to enable thermodynamical equilibrium with the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model quark matter, and the quark core mass ranges from about one third to half of the total stellar mass.

## Method

The paper reviews the microphysics and phenomenology of compact stars with quark matter cores, covering equilibrium and stability of non-rotating and rapidly rotating stars via the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations, gravitational radiation from deformations in crystalline color superconducting (CCS) quark cores, neutrino emission and cooling via Urca processes in the 2SC phase, and pulsar radio-timing anomalies interpreted within the hydrodynamic theory of superfluids. The quark phase is modeled using the NJL model, and the nuclear and quark equations of state are matched via the Maxwell construction at an interface.

## Key numbers

- Maximum masses for stable hybrid configurations (in solar masses): model A 2.052 (non-rotating) to 2.462 (Keplerian rotation); model A1 2.017 to 2.428/2.4174; model B 1.981 to 2.35.
- Quark core masses cover the range 0 ≤ M_core/M_⊙ ≤ 0.75–0.88 for central densities 1.3 × 10^15 ≤ ρ_c ≤ 2 × 10^15 g cm^{-3}.
- The strain of gravitational wave emission for the Crab pulsar (ν = 29.6 Hz, distance 2 kpc) is close to the current experimental upper limit h_0 < 3.4 × 10^{-25} for a gap Δ = 50 MeV and breaking strain σ_max = 10^{-4}.
- The Crab pulsar's current limit implies σ_max Δ^2 ∼ 0.25 MeV^2, assuming maximally strained matter.
- Glitches show abrupt increases in rotation rate ΔΩ/Ω ∼ 10^{-6}–10^{-8} and spin-down rate ΔΩ̇/Ω ∼ 10^{-3}.

## Context

This review extends earlier work on color superconductivity (Bailin 1983) and the LOFF phase (Bowers 2002) to astrophysical observables, and builds on calculations of shear modulus in CCS matter (Mannarelli 2008) and gravitational wave estimates for incompressible quark stars (Lin 2007). The results enable future gravitational wave searches to constrain QCD parameters of dense matter, and suggest that the dichotomy of fast and slow cooling in neutron stars may be explained by a density-dependent pairing parameter z = Δ/δμ in the 2SC phase.
