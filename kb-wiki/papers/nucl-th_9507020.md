# A Comment on General Formulae for Polarization Observables in Deuteron Electrodisintegration and Linear Relations

> We establish a simple, explicit relation between the formalisms employed in the treatments of polarization observables in deuteron two-body electrodisintegration published by Arenhövel, Leidemann, and Tomusiak in Few-Body Systems {\bf 15}, 109 (1993) and the results of the present authors published in Phys.~Rev.~C {\bf 40}, 2479 (1989). We comment on the overlap between the two sets of results.

## Key claim

The authors establish a simple, explicit relation between the ALT (Arenhövel, Leidemann, Tomusiak, 1993) and DG (Dmitrašinović and Gross, 1989) formalisms for polarization observables in deuteron two-body electrodisintegration, showing that the ALT amplitudes are linear combinations of the helicity amplitudes used in DG. The paper argues that the ALT conclusion that one "cannot totally avoid" measuring two observables with both outgoing nucleons polarized contradicts the DG result that no such measurements are required for a complete separation of amplitudes.

## Method

The authors compare the two formalisms by identifying the ALT reduced amplitudes \(t_{sm_s\lambda m}\) with the Jacob-Wick helicity amplitudes \(\langle \lambda_p \lambda_n | J_{\lambda_\gamma} | \lambda_D \rangle\) through the relations \(\lambda = \lambda_\gamma\), \(m = -\lambda_D\), and \(m_s = \lambda_p - \lambda_n\). They then derive explicit Clebsch-Gordan relations connecting the ALT amplitudes for total spin \(s=1\) and \(s=0\) to the helicity amplitudes, and show that the parity transformation properties of the ALT amplitudes follow directly from the helicity parity relation. The authors further discuss how the hybrid transversity basis developed in DG simplifies the counting of independent observables.

## Key numbers

* The total number of spin variables in deuteron electrodisintegration is \(3 \times 3 \times 2 \times 2 = 36\), with 18 independent complex amplitudes after parity constraints.
* There are 324 real bilinear products that can be formed from the 18 complex amplitudes, depending on only 35 independent real functions.
* For reactions where only one outgoing nucleon polarization is measured (\(N_1 = p\)), 162 bilinear products are accessible; adding neutron measurements (\(N_1 = n\)) gives 80 new products, with 82 products requiring \(d(e,e' \vec{p} \vec{n})\) measurements.
* The ALT formalism generates 648 bilinear products, requiring 324 linear relations from parity constraints to reduce to the physical 324.

## Context

This comment extends the earlier work of Dmitrašinović and Gross (1989, Phys. Rev. C 40, 2479) on polarization observables in deuteron electrodisintegration, and clarifies its relationship to the formalism of Arenhövel, Leidemann, and Tomusiak (1993, Few-Body Systems 15, 109). The paper traces both approaches to earlier foundations: the ALT work descends from the nonrelativistic spin polarization formalism of Ashkin and Wolfenstein (1952) and De Swart (1959), while the DG approach descends from the relativistic helicity formalism of Jacob and Wick (1959) as first applied to deuteron disintegration by LeBellac, Renard, and Tran Thanh Van (1964-1965). The explicit connection established here enables future work to use the simpler hybrid transversity basis of DG for complete separation strategies in deuteron electrodisintegration.
