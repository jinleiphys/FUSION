# Nonlinear Phenomena of Ultracold Atomic Gases in Optical Lattices: Emergence of Novel Features in Extended States

> The system of a cold atomic gas in an optical lattice is governed by two factors: nonlinearity originating from the interparticle interaction, and the periodicity of the system set by the lattice. The high level of controllability associated with such an arrangement allows for the study of the competition and interplay between these two, and gives rise to a whole range of interesting and rich nonlinear effects. This review covers the basic idea and overview of such nonlinear phenomena, especially those corresponding to extended states. This includes "swallowtail" loop structures of the energy band, Bloch states with multiple periodicity, and those in "nonlinear lattices", i.e., systems with the nonlinear interaction term itself being a periodic function in space.

## Key claim

This review argues that when the nonlinearity from interparticle interactions in a Bose-Einstein condensate becomes comparable to the periodic potential of an optical lattice, novel extended-state phenomena emerge, including swallowtail loop structures in the energy band, that are absent in the linear (non-interacting) regime and have not been well explored in conventional solid-state physics.

## Method

The authors review mean-field descriptions of superfluid atomic gases in one-dimensional sinusoidal optical lattices, using the Gross-Pitaevskii equation for bosons and the Bogoliubov-de Gennes equations for fermions. They examine stationary Bloch solutions via plane-wave expansions and variational ansatze, and analyze energetic and dynamical stability through linear stability analysis of the eigenvalues of the stability matrix M(q) and the non-Hermitian matrix σ_z M(q).

## Key numbers

- The lattice potential is given by V(x) = s E_R sin²(q_B x), where E_R = ħ²q_B²/2m is the recoil energy, q_B = π/d is the Bragg wave number, and d is the lattice constant.
- The dimensionless parameter s characterizes the lattice height in units of E_R.
- For the nonlinear Landau-Zener model, when the nonlinearity parameter c exceeds the coupling strength v (c > v), loop structures appear in the adiabatic energy levels.
- The linear Landau-Zener transition probability is r₀ = exp(-πv²/2α); for strong nonlinearity (c > v) there is a finite transition probability even in the adiabatic limit α → 0.

## Context

This review complements the existing review by Malomed (2005) on nonlinear phenomena in lattices, which focused mainly on localized states rather than extended states. The work extends the nonlinear two-level model introduced by Niu, Wu, and collaborators (2000) and the swallowtail band structure analysis by Diakonov et al. (2002) and Machholm et al. (2003). The phenomena discussed are relevant beyond cold atoms, including superfluid neutrons in neutron star crusts and superconducting electrons in solids.
