# Flavor Twisted Boundary Conditions in the Breit Frame

> We use a generalization of chiral perturbation theory to account for the effects of flavor twisted boundary conditions in the Breit frame. The relevant framework for two light flavors is an SU(6|4) partially quenched theory, where the extra valence quarks differ only by their boundary conditions. Focusing on the pion electromagnetic form factor, finite volume corrections are calculated at next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion and are estimated to be small on current lattices.

## Key claim

The authors use a partially quenched chiral perturbation theory with an enlarged SU(6|4) flavor group to compute finite volume corrections to the pion electromagnetic form factor when flavor twisted boundary conditions are implemented in the Breit frame; these corrections are estimated to be small on current lattices, but the abstract does not emphasize that the calculation is restricted to the p-regime and that the result maintains isospin symmetry and discrete rotational symmetry, unlike the rest frame kinematics result which had both isospin breaking and cubic symmetry breaking terms.

## Method

The authors formulate a partially quenched chiral perturbation theory (PQχPT) with an SU(6|4) graded symmetry group, where ten quark fields (four valence, two sea, four ghost) differ only by their boundary conditions. They remove the twists by a field redefinition in favor of periodic quark fields, with the twist effects shuffled into a uniform U(1) gauge potential Bμ in the covariant derivative. They then calculate the finite volume modification to the time-component of the current matrix element for the pion electromagnetic form factor at next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion, working in the p-regime with time treated as infinite.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant f is taken as f ∼ 130 MeV.
- For a fixed lattice size L = 2.5 fm, the finite volume corrections to the pion form factor are at the percent level, becoming non-negligible only for light pions with small twists.
- The momentum transfer squared is given by Q² = 4θ²/L², with an example value of Q² = θ² × 0.025 GeV² shown in the figure.

## Context

This work extends the Breit frame implementation of flavor twisted boundary conditions described by Boyle (2007) to compute volume corrections to observables, specifically the pion electromagnetic form factor. The result contrasts with the rest frame kinematics calculation by Jiang (2006) which possessed both isospin breaking and cubic symmetry breaking terms from finite volume effects; the Breit frame result maintains both isospin symmetry and discrete rotational symmetry. This enables future investigation of nucleon isovector quantities in the Breit frame, where previous work by Tiburzi (2006) on the nucleon isovector magnetic moment suggested larger than expected volume corrections from cubic symmetry breaking terms.
