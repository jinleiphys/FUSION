# Nuclear-bound quarkonia and heavy-flavor hadrons

> In our quest to win a deeper understanding of how QCD actually works, the study of the binding of heavy quarkonia and heavy-flavor hadrons to atomic nuclei offers enormous promise. Modern experimental facilities such as FAIR, Jefferson Lab at 12 GeV and J-PARC offer exciting new experimental opportunities to study such systems. These experimental advances are complemented by new theoretical approaches and predictions, which will both guide these experimental efforts and be informed and improved by them. This review will outline the main theoretical approaches, beginning with QCD itself, summarize recent theoretical predictions and relate them both to past experiments and those from which we may expect results in the near future.

## Key claim

This review argues that studying the binding of heavy quarkonia and heavy-flavor hadrons to atomic nuclei offers a promising avenue for understanding nonperturbative QCD, particularly because the Zweig rule prevents binding through light meson exchange, so any observed binding must arise from gluonic mechanisms such as gluon exchange.

## Method

The authors review theoretical approaches beginning with QCD itself, including non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) and potential NRQCD (pNRQCD), which combine effective field theory with a Hamiltonian describing gluon-mediated interactions of quarkonia with colorless systems. They also outline the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which calculates non-gluonic mechanisms for quarkonium binding by self-consistently solving field equations for hadrons immersed in nuclear matter. The review then surveys experimental and theoretical results for specific systems: J/Psi with nuclei, phi mesons with nuclei, eta/eta'/omega mesons, and heavy-flavored hadrons including D, anti-D mesons, Lambda_c+ and Lambda_b baryons.

## Key numbers

* Lambda_QCD values in the MS-bar scheme: 332 MeV for N_f=3, 292 MeV for N_f=4, 210 MeV for N_f=5, 89 MeV for N_f=6
* Running coupling alpha_s varies from approximately 0.08 at mu=1000 GeV to approximately 0.4 at mu=2 GeV
* Quark masses (MS-bar): u=0.0022 GeV, d=0.0047 GeV, s=0.096 GeV, c=1.27 GeV, b=4.18 GeV, t=160 GeV
* Bottom quark pole mass: m_b=4.93 GeV, obtained from the MS-bar mass using the relation m_b=1.18*m_b(m_b) evaluated up to alpha_s^3

## Context

This review is complementary to the recent review by Hosaka et al. (2016), which focused on properties of heavy hadrons in nuclear matter and few-body systems, while the present work focuses on nuclear binding phenomena of quarkonia and heavy-flavor hadrons relevant for experiments at FAIR, Jefferson Lab at 12 GeV, and J-PARC. The review extends earlier work on exotic hadrons including dibaryons (Jaffe 1976, Mulders 1982, Clement 2016), pentaquarks (DeVita 2006, Aaij 2015), and exotic mesons (Maiani 2017), and connects to lattice QCD results for ground state hadrons (Durr 2008). The trace anomaly relation connecting hadron masses to gluon field matrix elements (Kharzeev 1995) provides a theoretical foundation for using near-threshold quarkonium-hadron scattering to probe mass distributions inside hadrons.
