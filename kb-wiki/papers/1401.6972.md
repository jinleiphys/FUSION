# Holographic Chiral Electric Separation Effect

> We investigate the chiral electric separation effect, where an axial current is induced by an electric field in the presence of both vector and axial chemical potentials, in a strongly coupled plasma via the Sakai-Sugimoto model with an $U(1)_R\times U(1)_L$ symmetry. By introducing different chemical potentials in $U(1)_R$ and $U(1)_L$ sectors, we compute the axial direct current (DC) conductivity stemming from the chiral current and the normal DC conductivity. We find that the axial conductivity is approximately proportional to the product of the axial and vector chemical potentials for arbitrary magnitudes of the chemical potentials. We also evaluate the axial alternating current (AC) conductivity induced by a frequency-dependent electric field, where the oscillatory behavior with respect to the frequency is observed.

## Key claim

The authors find that the axial conductivity induced by an electric field in a strongly coupled plasma is approximately proportional to the product of the axial and vector chemical potentials for arbitrary magnitudes of the chemical potentials, not only in the small-chemical-potential limit assumed in prior weak-coupling work.

## Method

The authors use the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic QCD model with U(1)_R × U(1)_L symmetry in the deconfined, chirally restored phase. They introduce different chemical potentials in the left- and right-handed sectors, solve the Dirac-Born-Infeld action for the background gauge fields, then perturb the system with a frequency-dependent electric field along one spatial direction to compute the induced currents. The DC and AC conductivities in the left/right bases are extracted via holographic renormalization and then converted to vector and axial conductivities.

## Key numbers

- The critical chemical potential-to-temperature ratio for the validity of the background-field expansion is μ_c/T ≈ 4.51, obtained at y_c = 1.5 with T = 200 MeV, λ = 17, M_KK = 0.94 GeV.
- The temperature is fixed at T = 200 MeV (average RHIC temperature), with U_T = 1.02 GeV⁻¹.
- The dimensionless conductivity is defined as σ̂_i = 81 σ_i / (8 N_c λ T) for i = R, L, V, 5.
- For μ_V = T and varying μ_A, both normal and axial DC conductivities show mild enhancement with increasing axial chemical potential.
- For μ_A = 0.5 T and varying μ_V, both conductivities also increase with vector chemical potential.
- The axial conductivity is approximately linear in μ_A for small chemical potentials, consistent with σ_5 ∝ μ_V μ_A.

## Context

This work extends the chiral electric separation effect (CESE), originally proposed in weakly coupled systems by Huang and Liao (2013), to a strongly coupled plasma using holography. Unlike the chiral magnetic effect (CME) and chiral separation effect (CSE), which arise from the axial anomaly, CESE originates from interactions of chiral fermions and is free of the Chern-Simons term complications that plagued holographic studies of CME in the Sakai-Sugimoto model (Rebhan, 2009; Gynther, 2010). The results enable future studies of CESE in heavy-ion collisions, particularly in asymmetric collisions such as Cu+Au where strong electric fields may generate observable charge quadrupole signals.
