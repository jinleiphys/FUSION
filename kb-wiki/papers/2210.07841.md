# Scales in light-nuclei production near the QCD critical point

> Based on the coalescence model, we analyse the light-nuclei production near the critical point by expanding the phase-space distribution function $f(\mathbf{r},\mathbf{p})$ in terms of the phase-space cumulants $\sim \langle r^m p^m\rangle_c$. We show that the dominant contribution of the phase-space distribution to the yield of light nuclei is determined by the second-order phase-space cumulants. Here, we identify the fireball size, the homogeneity length, and the effective temperature, which are encoded in the second-order phase-space cumulants, as the relevant scales in explaining the yield of light nuclei. These scales are typically much larger than the correlation length of the critical fluctuations created in the rapid expansion of the heavy-ion systems, so we need to eliminate this dominant contribution of the relevant scales in order to isolate the critical contribution from the yield of light nuclei. We find that the second-order phase-space cumulants appeared in the yields of light-nuclei with different mass numbers share a similar structure. This property allows us to construct ratios of light-nuclei yields in appropriate combinations so that the effect of the relevant scales of the light-nuclei yield cancels, which isolates the critical effects.

# Scales in light-nuclei production near the QCD critical point

## Key claim
The dominant contribution to light-nuclei yields from the coalescence model comes from second-order phase-space cumulants (fireball size, homogeneity length, and effective temperature), which are typically much larger than the critical correlation length in rapidly expanding heavy-ion systems; therefore, these background scales must be eliminated via appropriate yield ratios to isolate the critical contribution from the QCD critical point.

## Method
The authors expand the nucleon phase-space distribution function in terms of phase-space cumulants and compute light-nuclei yields using the coalescence model with a Gaussian Wigner function for the harmonic oscillator. They then introduce critical fluctuations by coupling nucleons to a chiral sigma field, using static critical correlators with a Yukawa-type propagator characterized by the correlation length, and evaluate the event-averaged yields to identify the critical contribution.

## Key numbers
- The critical correlation length in heavy-ion collisions is limited to the order of 2–3 fm due to rapid expansion of the system (Berdnikov 1999).
- The Wigner function for light-nuclei production is given by \(W_A = 8^{A-1} \exp[-\frac{1}{2}\sum^{A-1}_{i=1}\bm{Z}_i^2]\).
- The statistical factor for light-nuclei yield is \(g_A = (2s+1)/2^A\), where \(s\) is the spin of the light nucleus.

## Context
This work extends the coalescence model approach of Fries (2003) and Zhu (2015) by incorporating phase-space cumulant expansions from Wu (2022) to systematically separate background scales from critical effects. It builds on the theoretical framework of Stephanov (2011) for critical fluctuations and the static critical correlators of Jiang (2015), and addresses the challenge raised by STAR (2020, 2022) measurements of non-monotonic behavior in net-proton fluctuations and light-nuclei yields as potential signatures of the QCD critical point. The proposed yield-ratio combinations enable future experimental searches for critical effects at RHIC Beam Energy Scan.
