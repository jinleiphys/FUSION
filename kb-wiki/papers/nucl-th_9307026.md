# Analysis of the instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for \(q\bar{q}\)-bound-states

> We investigate the structure of the instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for \(q\bar{q}\)-bound states in the general case of unequal quark masses and develop a numerical scheme for the calculation of mass spectra and Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes. In order to appreciate the merits of the various competing models beyond the reproduction of the mass spectra we present explicit formulas to calculate electroweak decays. The results for an explicit quark model will be compared to experimental data in a subsequent paper

## Key claim

The paper develops a general numerical scheme for solving the instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter (Salpeter) equation for quark-antiquark bound states with unequal quark masses, but the authors explicitly note that the use of free (bare) quark propagators with effective masses is an approximation that "might be incompatible with a confining kernel" and that it remains "an open question whether free propagators can also be applied to light quarks."

## Method

The authors start from the Bethe-Salpeter equation with an instantaneous interaction kernel in the bound-state rest frame and approximate the full quark propagators by bare propagators with effective masses, reducing the equation to the full Salpeter equation. They reformulate this as an eigenvalue problem for the bound-state mass \(M\) and derive a matrix representation analogous to the random phase approximation (RPA) equations from nuclear theory, expanding the Salpeter amplitude in a complete set of basis functions with Laguerre polynomials for the radial dependence. The normalization condition and scalar product are derived from the Mandelstam formalism, and the structure of solutions under charge conjugation is analyzed to show that eigenvalues come in pairs \((M, -M^*)\) with opposite norm.

## Key numbers

* The basis expansion uses about ten basis states (\(i \le i_{max} \approx 10\)) to solve the Salpeter equation "with rather high accuracy."
* The variational parameter \(\beta\) has dimension \(MeV^{-1}\) and sets the absolute scale for momentum dependence via \(E_i^{\beta}(\vec{p}) = \beta^{3/2}\,E_i^{\beta=1}(\vec{p}\beta)\).
* The normalization condition for Salpeter amplitudes in the rest frame is \(\left\langle \psi \right.\left| \psi \right\rangle = (2\pi)^2\,2M\).
* In the nonrelativistic limit, the lower component \(\Phi^{--}\) goes to zero as \(\vec{p}^2/m^2 \cdot \Phi^{++}\), while for deeply bound states the two components become equal when \(\Phi^{++} = \pm \Phi^{--}\) at \(M=0\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the Salpeter equation for equal-mass quarks by Lagaë (1992) to the general case of unequal quark masses, and builds on earlier treatments by Llewellyn Smith (1969), Le Yaouanc and coworkers (1970s), and the variational approach of Lagaë. The formalism enables calculation of mass spectra and electroweak decay observables (including leptonic decays and the \(\pi^0 \rightarrow 2\gamma\) decay) within the Mandelstam formalism, with the results for an explicit quark model to be compared to experimental data in a subsequent paper by Münz and collaborators (1994).
