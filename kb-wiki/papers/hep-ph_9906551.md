# Qualitons At High Density

> In the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase of the QCD superconductor we show that baryons behave as qualitons (called "superqualitons") with quantum numbers $B=(1 {\rm mod} 2)/3$, S=1/2 and Y=B. An intriguing possibility implied by this identification is that light baryonic modes in the form of superqualitons could be excited below the (color) superconducting gap in the CFL phase, a novel phenomenon foreign to normal superconductors.

## Key claim
In the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase of QCD, baryons emerge as "superqualitons" with baryon number \(B = (1 \mod 2)/3\), spin \(S = 1/2\), and hypercharge \(Y = B\); these modes may be excited below the superconducting gap, a phenomenon absent in normal superconductors, though the authors note that their numerical mass estimates are on the higher side of the gap while simple scaling arguments suggest the opposite.

## Method
The authors construct a low-energy effective Lagrangian for the CFL phase using a nonlinear sigma model for the Nambu-Goldstone bosons, including a Wess-Zumino-Witten (WZW) term whose coefficient is fixed by the triangle anomaly of the modified \(U(1)_{\tilde Q}\) gauge symmetry. They then seek static soliton solutions by embedding an \(SU(2)\) hedgehog profile in \(SU(3)\) color-flavor space, solving the equation of motion numerically for the profile function \(\theta(r)\) with screened color-electric interactions, and quantizing the zero modes via rigid \(SU(3)\) rotations.

## Key numbers
- Superqualiton mass \(M_S = 1.53 \times 4\pi F\) for \(\alpha_s = 1.0\) and \(m_E/F = 2\), where \(F \sim \Delta\) is the decay constant and \(\Delta\) is the superconducting gap.
- Superqualiton radius \(R_S = 0.29 \, F^{-1}\) for \(\alpha_s = 1.0\) and \(m_E/F = 2\).
- For \(\alpha_s = 1.0\) and \(m_E/F = 5\), \(M_S = 1.06 \times 4\pi F\) and \(R_S = 0.20 \, F^{-1}\).
- For \(\alpha_s = 1.0\) and \(m_E/F = 10\), \(M_S = 0.75 \times 4\pi F\) and \(R_S = 0.15 \, F^{-1}\).
- For fixed \(m_E/F = 2\) and \(\alpha_s = 0.5\), \(M_S = 1.21 \times 4\pi F\) and \(R_S = 0.24 \, F^{-1}\).
- For fixed \(m_E/F = 2\) and \(\alpha_s = 0.2\), \(M_S = 0.87 \times 4\pi F\) and \(R_S = 0.17 \, F^{-1}\).
- A simple scaling estimate gives \(M_S \sim \Delta/4\), which is less than \(\Delta/2\).

## Context
This work extends the qualiton scenario proposed by Kaplan (1991) to the high-density CFL phase, building on the Skyrme model for baryons at zero density (Skyrme 1961; Adkins, Nappi, Witten 1983) and on the Cheshire-Cat principle in hadronic structure (Rho 1994). It is motivated by the suggestion of Schafer and Wilczek (1999) that the CFL phase spectrum matches that of zero-density QCD, and by effective theory calculations of the CFL phase (Hong 1999). The results enable further study of hadron-quark continuity and the possibility of light baryonic modes inside the superconducting gap, with implications for neutron star cooling curves and the bulk properties of dense matter.
