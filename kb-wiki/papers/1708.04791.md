# Charge symmetry breaking in light $Λ$ hypernuclei

> Charge symmetry breaking (CSB) is particularly strong in the A=4 mirror hypernuclei $_Λ^4$H--$_Λ^4$He. Recent four-body no-core shell model calculations that confront this CSB by introducing $Λ$-$Σ^0$ mixing to leading-order chiral effective field theory hyperon-nucleon potentials are reviewed, and a shell-model approach to CSB in p-shell $Λ$ hypernuclei is outlined.

## Key claim

Charge symmetry breaking (CSB) in the A=4 mirror hypernuclei ⁴_ΛH–⁴_ΛHe is roughly an order of magnitude stronger than in ordinary nuclei, and can be reproduced by introducing Λ–Σ⁰ mixing into leading-order chiral effective field theory hyperon-nucleon potentials, but the calculated CSB splittings show strong cutoff dependence and the NLO χEFT version disagrees strongly with experiment.

## Method

The authors performed ab-initio no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations of the A=4 hypernuclei using a leading-order (LO) chiral effective field theory (χEFT) hyperon-nucleon (YN) charge-symmetric interaction model, with CSB generated by implementing a Λ–Σ⁰ mass-mixing vertex that converts a charge-symmetric ΛN↔ΣN coupling matrix element into a direct ΛN CSB matrix element via a 3% scale factor. For p-shell hypernuclei, an effective ΛΣ coupling shell-model approach was applied, using single-nucleon CSB operators summed over p-shell nucleons with matrix elements derived from reproducing hypernuclear γ-ray transition energies.

## Key numbers

- The CSB splitting of the A=4 mirror ground-state levels is ΔB_Λ^(J=0) = 233 ± 92 keV (experimental, from MAMI measurements)
- The excitation energy difference between mirror hypernuclei is E_x(⁴_ΛHe) – E_x(⁴_ΛH) = 320 ± 20 keV (experimental, from J-PARC E13)
- The LO χEFT NCSM calculation yields ΔB_Λ^(J=0) = 177^(+119)_(-147) keV and ΔB_Λ^(J=1) = -215^(+43)_(-41) keV, with strong cutoff dependence
- The OPE (Dalitz–Von Hippel) contribution alone gives ΔB_Λ^(J=0) ≈ 175 ± 40 keV and ΔB_Λ^(J=1) ≈ -50 ± 10 keV
- The calculated CS average B_Λ^(J=0) for A=4 is 2.37^(+0.20)_(-0.13) MeV (LO χEFT), compared to the experimental 2.27 ± 0.09 MeV
- The calculated CS average excitation energy E_x(0⁺_g.s.→1⁺_exc) is 1.29 ± 0.38 MeV (LO χEFT), compared to the experimental 1.25 ± 0.02 MeV
- For p-shell hypernuclei, the ⟨V_CSB⟩ contribution ranges from 17 keV (¹⁰_ΛB–¹⁰_ΛBe) to 119 keV (⁸_ΛBe–⁸_ΛLi) and 232 keV (⁴_ΛHe–⁴_ΛH)

## Context

This work extends earlier four-body calculations by Gazda et al. (2014) and the Dalitz–Von Hippel (1964) treatment of Λ–Σ⁰ mixing, and confronts recent experimental results from J-PARC E13 (measuring the ⁴_ΛHe 1⁺_exc→0⁺_g.s. γ-ray transition) and MAMI (determining B_Λ(⁴_ΛH) to better than 100 keV). The NCSM calculations use the Bonn–Jülich LO χEFT YN potential (2006) and N3LO NN interactions from Entem and Machleidt (2003), while the p-shell shell-model approach follows Millener (2012) and Gal (2015). The results enable future work to refit NLO χEFT contact terms to reproduce CS-averaged A=4 binding energies before applying CSB, and motivate experimental exploration of the (π⁻,K⁰) reaction to study hypernuclear isomultiplets simultaneously.
