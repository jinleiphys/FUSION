# Molecular States of Heavy Quark Mesons

> We explore molecular states of two open heavy-quark mesons (Q qbar)-(qbar Q) in a quark-based model in terms of a four-body non-relativistic Hamiltonian with pairwise effective interactions. Molecular states are found in the combinations of {D, D*, B, B*} with {Dbar, Dbar*, Bbar, Bbar*}, including a weakly-bound D-Dbar* state near the threshold which may be qualitatively identified as the 3872 state observed recently by the Belle Collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper claims that weakly-bound molecular states of two open heavy-quark mesons can be described within a completely quark-based model using effective charges and a screened color van der Waals interaction, and that a weakly-bound \(D^+ D^{*-}\) state with a binding energy of 7.4 MeV may be qualitatively identified as the 3872 state observed by Belle, but only under the condition that the average meson-meson separation \(R\) is much greater than the average meson radius \(a\) (\(R \gg a\)), which suppresses single-gluon exchange contributions.

## Method

The authors use a four-body non-relativistic Hamiltonian with pairwise effective interactions between quarks and antiquarks, where the interquark potential includes a screened color-Coulomb term, a screened linear confining term, and a hyperfine spin-spin term. The Hamiltonian is partitioned into an unperturbed two-meson part and a residue interaction; the meson-meson potential is then obtained as the sum of a direct potential and an attractive polarization potential, evaluated analytically using Gaussian wave functions for the mesons. The eigenvalue equation for the relative motion of the two mesons is solved as a Schrodinger equation to find bound states.

## Key numbers

*   Binding energy of the candidate \(D^+ D^{*-}\) molecular state: 7.4 MeV, obtained as \(3.872\ \mathrm{GeV} - M(D^+) - M(D^{*-})\).
*   Estimated average separation \(R\) between \(D^+\) and \(D^{*-}\) for a binding energy of 7.4 MeV: about 1.6 fm.
*   Root-mean-square radius of \(D^+\) and \(D^{*-}\) mesons: about 0.3 fm (half of the calculated root-mean-square quark-antiquark separation from Wong (2002)).
*   Screening mass \(\mu\) used in the effective interaction: 0.28 GeV, corresponding to a screening length of about 0.7 fm.
*   String tension coefficient \(b\): 0.335 GeV\(^2\).
*   Quark masses used: \(m_u = m_d = 0.334\ \mathrm{GeV}\), \(m_c = 1.87\ \mathrm{GeV}\), \(m_b = 5.18\ \mathrm{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of heavy-quark molecular states, particularly the pion-exchange model of Tornqvist (1992) and the quark-based one-gluon exchange model of Swanson (2003), which found insufficient attraction to bind a \(D \bar D^*\) state. The paper introduces a screened color van der Waals interaction to address the lack of experimental evidence for inverse-power color forces, as previously criticized by Lipkin and Greenberg (1982). The results enable future comparison of different model predictions (pion-exchange, one-gluon exchange, and the present effective-charge model) with experimental data to determine the dominant binding mechanisms for heavy-quark molecular states.
