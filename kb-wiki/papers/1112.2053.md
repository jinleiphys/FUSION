# Short distance repulsion in 3 nucleon forces from perturbative QCD

> We investigate the short distance behavior of 3 nucleon forces (3NF) defined through Nambu--Bethe--Salpeter wave functions, using the operator product expansion(OPE) and calculating anomalous dimensions of 9--quark operators in perturbative QCD. As is the case of NN forces previously considered, we show that 3NF have repulsions at short distance at 1--loop, which becomes exact in the short distance limit thanks to the asymptotic freedom of QCD. Moreover these behaviors are universal in the sense that they do not depend on the energy of the NBS wave function for 3 nucleons.

## Key claim

The authors show that three-nucleon forces (3NF) defined through Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave functions have a repulsive core at short distance at 1-loop order in perturbative QCD, and this repulsion becomes exact in the short distance limit due to asymptotic freedom. Unlike the two-nucleon force case where the sign of the short-distance potential required additional nonperturbative input, the 3NF repulsion is determined purely from perturbative QCD and is universal in the sense that it does not depend on the energy of the three-nucleon state used to define the wave function.

## Method

The authors extend their previous operator product expansion (OPE) analysis of two-nucleon forces to three-nucleon forces, computing the anomalous dimensions of all gauge-invariant local 9-quark operators at 1-loop order in perturbative QCD. They use renormalization group equations to determine the short-distance behavior of the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave function for three nucleons, from which the three-nucleon potential is extracted. The calculation employs dimensional regularization in the MS scheme and uses Mathematica and Maple programs to diagonalize the mixing matrix of 9-quark operators, imposing gauge identities that reduce the number of independent operators.

## Key numbers

- The largest eigenvalue of the 1-loop anomalous dimension matrix for 9-quark operators is \(16d\) where \(d = 1/(96\pi^2)\), occurring already at tree level
- The tree-level coefficient \(\beta_A^{\text{tree}} = -14/(33-2N_f)\) where \(N_f\) is the number of dynamical quark flavors
- The three-nucleon central potential at short distance behaves as \(V_{3NF}(\vec r,\vec\rho) \simeq \frac{1}{m_N} \frac{-4\beta_A^{\text{tree}}}{s^2(-\log(\Lambda s))}\) as \(s = \sqrt{r^2 + \rho^2} \rightarrow 0\)
- The nucleon operator 1-loop anomalous dimension coefficient is \(\gamma_N^{(1)} = 24d = 24/(96\pi^2)\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous OPE analysis of two-nucleon forces (Aoki, Balog, Weisz 2009, 2010) to the three-nucleon case, where the 2NF analysis required additional nonperturbative input to determine the sign of the short-distance potential. The 3NF defined through NBS wave functions had previously been extracted from lattice QCD simulations by Doi et al. (2010, 2011), following the method developed for nucleon-nucleon potentials by Ishii, Aoki, and Hatsuda (2006). The results provide a first-principles QCD foundation for the phenomenological repulsive core of three-nucleon forces that had been introduced empirically in nuclear structure calculations (Pieper 2001), and may inform studies of neutron star structure and the nuclear equation of state at high density.
