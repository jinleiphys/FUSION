# Parameter-Free Calculation of the Solar Proton Fusion Rate in Effective Field Theory

> Spurred by the recent complete determination of the weak currents in two-nucleon systems up to ${\cal O}(Q^3)$ in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, we carry out a parameter-free calculation of the solar proton fusion rate in an effective field theory that combines the merits of the standard nuclear physics method and systematic chiral expansion. Using the tritium beta-decay rate as an input to fix the only unknown parameter in the effective Lagrangian, we can evaluate with drastically improved precision the ratio of the two-body contribution to the well established one-body contribution; the ratio is determined to be (0.86\pm 0.05) %. This result is essentially independent of the cutoff parameter for a wide range of its variation (500 MeV \le Λ\le 800 MeV), a feature that substantiates the consistency of the calculation.

# Parameter-Free Calculation of the Solar Proton Fusion Rate in Effective Field Theory

## Key claim

The two-body meson-exchange current contribution to the solar proton fusion rate is determined to be (0.86 ± 0.05)% of the one-body contribution, a result that is essentially independent of the cutoff parameter Λ over the range 500 MeV ≤ Λ ≤ 800 MeV, provided the unknown contact-term strength d̂^R is fixed by reproducing the tritium β-decay rate. This represents a drastic improvement over the previous estimate of (4.0 ± 0.5)% from Park et al. (1998), which had assigned roughly 100% uncertainty to the contact term contribution that was dropped by fiat.

## Method

The authors combine standard nuclear physics wave functions (Argonne v18 potential with Urbana-IX three-nucleon force for A=3 systems, and the same potential for the deuteron and pp scattering wave) with chiral transition operators up to O(Q³) in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory. The single unknown parameter d̂^R, which enters the two-body Gamow-Teller matrix element, is fixed by reproducing the experimentally measured tritium β-decay rate; the same combination of counterterms appears in both processes. Gaussian regularization with cutoff Λ is applied consistently to both the A=2 and A=3 systems, and the resulting two-body matrix element is evaluated for several Λ values to verify cutoff independence.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of two-body to one-body contribution: δ₂B = (0.86 ± 0.05)%
- Two-body Gamow-Teller matrix element M₂B = (0.039 – 0.044) fm, stable across Λ = 500, 600, and 800 MeV
- Astrophysical S-factor at zero energy: S_pp(0) = 3.94 × 10⁻²⁵ MeV-barn, with uncertainties of 0.15% from one-body input parameters, 0.10% from two-body part, and ≈0.1% estimated from higher-order chiral contributions
- The one-body matrix element squared Λ_pp² = 6.91 (compared to 6.93 in Park et al. 1998)
- The 1/m_N² relativistic correction to the one-body current decreases the one-body matrix element by 0.13%

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach of Park et al. (1998, PKMR98), which had calculated the pp fusion rate at N³LO but could not fix the contact term d̂^R and therefore assigned large uncertainties. By using tritium β-decay data to determine d̂^R, the authors achieve a parameter-free prediction with dramatically improved precision. The result agrees with the standard nuclear physics approach calculations of Schiavilla et al. (1998), which found δ₂B = 0.5–0.8% by constraining short-range axial currents using the same tritium β-decay input. The methodology can be applied to related processes including the hep reaction, μ-capture on deuteron, and ν–d scattering.
