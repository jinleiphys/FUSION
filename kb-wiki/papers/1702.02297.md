# Heavy Flavours in Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Recent progresses in lattice studies of heavy quark and quarkonium at non-zero temperature are discussed. Formulating a tail of spectral functions as a transport coefficient allows lattice determination of momentum diffusion coefficient ($κ$) for charm quark in the heavy quark mass limit and lattice determination of heavy quark/heavy anti-quark chemical equilibration rate in NRQCD. Quenched lattice study on a large volume gives $κ/T^3 = 1.8 \cdots 3.4$ in the continuum limit. A recent study with $N_f = 2+1$ configurations estimates the charmonium chemical equilibration rate $Γ_{\rm chem}$. At $T = 400$ MeV with $M \sim 1.5$ GeV, $Γ_{\rm chem}^{-1} \sim 150$ fm/c. Earlier results from the two studies (with different lattice setups and with different Bayesian priors) which calculate bottomonium correlators using NRQCD and employ Bayesian method to calculate spectral functions are summarized: $Υ(1S)$ survives upto $T \sim 1.9 T_c$ and excited states of $Υ$ are sequentially suppressed. The spectral functions of $χ_{b1}$ channel shows a Bayesian prior dependence of its thermal behavior: the $χ_{b1}$ spectral function with MEM prior shows melting above $T_c$ but that with a new Bayesian prior hints survival of $χ_{b1}$ upto $\sim 1.6 T_c$. Preliminary results from the efforts to understand the difference in the behavior of $χ_{b1}$ spectral function is given.

# Heavy Flavours in Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim
Lattice studies of heavy quark transport and quarkonium spectral functions at non-zero temperature show that the momentum diffusion coefficient for charm quark in the heavy quark mass limit can be extracted from the power-law tail of the spectral function rather than the narrow transport peak, and that the chemical equilibration rate for charm quarks is too slow to reach equilibrium within the QGP lifetime of current heavy ion collisions. The survival of the χb1 state above Tc depends on the choice of Bayesian prior used in spectral function reconstruction, with MEM prior showing melting above Tc while a new Bayesian prior hints survival up to approximately 1.6 Tc.

## Method
The momentum diffusion coefficient κ was determined from quenched lattice QCD on large volumes (64³ × 16 to 192³ × 48) at fixed temperature T = 1.5 Tc using a Heavy Quark Effective Theory formulation that relates the transport coefficient to the power-law tail of the spectral function, with continuum extrapolation and multiple fitting strategies. The chemical equilibration rate Γchem for charm quarks was computed using NRQCD on Nf = 2+1 dynamical FASTSUM configurations (24³ × Nτ, as/aτ = 3.5) by calculating thermal averaged Sommerfeld factors from density-density correlators. Quarkonium spectral functions for bottomonium states were reconstructed from NRQCD correlators using both Maximum Entropy Method (MEM) and a new Bayesian prior, with comparisons between FASTSUM configurations and HotQCD configurations at multiple temperatures.

## Key numbers
- Momentum diffusion coefficient: κ/T³ = 1.8 to 3.4 in the continuum limit from quenched lattice study at T = 1.5 Tc (Francis et al., 2015)
- Charm quark kinetic equilibration time: τkin = (1.8 to 3.4) × (Tc/T)² × (M/1.5 GeV) fm/c, giving approximately 1 fm/c near Tc
- Charmonium chemical equilibration time: Γchem⁻¹ ≈ 150 fm/c at T = 400 MeV with M ∼ 1.5 GeV from Nf = 2+1 lattice study (Kim, 2016)
- Υ(1S) survives up to T ∼ 1.9 Tc from FASTSUM 2nd generation configurations with MEM
- χb1 spectral function with new Bayesian prior hints survival up to ∼ 1.6 Tc (Kim, 2014), while MEM prior shows melting above Tc
- Lattice singlet Sommerfeld factor S̄₁ ranges ∼ O(100-1000), compared to perturbative estimate ∼ O(10) without bound state contribution
- Lattice octet Sommerfeld factor S̄₈ stays ∼ O(1), consistent with perturbative estimate

## Context
This work extends the Heavy Quark Effective Theory formulation of Caron-Huot (2009) and Bodeker (2012) for non-perturbative lattice determination of heavy quark transport coefficients, and provides the first lattice determination of the chemical equilibration rate for heavy quarks. The quarkonium results build on earlier FASTSUM collaboration studies (Aarts, 2010-2014) using NRQCD correlators with MEM, and the work of Kim, Petreczky, and Rothkopf (2014) comparing Bayesian priors. The finding that the χb1 spectral function shows prior dependence motivates further studies toward the Bayesian continuum limit, with preliminary results from higher-statistics HotQCD configurations at higher temperatures (up to 2.75 Tc) showing eventual melting of χb1. The quenched determination of κ provides a benchmark for future dynamical calculations, while the chemical equilibration result confirms that charm quarks do not chemically equilibrate within the ∼10 fm/c QGP lifetime of current heavy ion collisions.
