# A quantum Monte Carlo based approach to intranuclear cascades

> We propose a novel approach to intranuclear cascades which takes as input quantum MonteCarlo nuclear configurations and uses a semi-classical, impact-parameter based algorithm to modelthe propagation of protons and neutrons in the nuclear medium. We successfully compare oursimulations to available proton-carbon scattering data and nuclear-transparency measurements. Byanalyzing the dependence of the simulated observables upon the ingredients entering our intranuclearcascade algorithm, we provide a quantitative understanding of their impact. Particular emphasisis devoted to the role played by nuclear correlations, the Pauli exclusion principle, and interactionprobability distributions.

## Key claim

The authors propose a novel intranuclear cascade model that uses quantum Monte Carlo nuclear configurations (retaining all correlation effects) and a semi-classical, impact-parameter-based algorithm, and they show that this approach reproduces proton-carbon scattering data and nuclear-transparency measurements, though the agreement depends on the choice of interaction probability function and nucleon-nucleon cross-section parametrization.

## Method

The model generates nuclear configurations from Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations using the Argonne v18 plus Illinois-7 Hamiltonian, sampling 36,000 configurations for ¹²C. A semi-classical cascade algorithm propagates nucleons in discrete time steps, checking for interactions via an accept-reject test based on impact-parameter-dependent probability distributions (either a cylinder step function or a Gaussian). When an interaction occurs, final-state phase space is generated using fully differential nucleon-nucleon cross sections, Pauli blocking is applied by comparing final-state momenta to the local Fermi momentum, and a formation zone is set for outgoing particles before they can interact again.

## Key numbers

- Mean free path validation: for a 500 MeV test proton in a 0.16 nucleons/fm³ background density with a 50 mb fixed cross section, the fitted mean free path is 1.23 fm, compared to the expected value of 1.25 fm.
- Proton-carbon reaction cross section: at low proton kinetic energies (below 200 MeV), the Gaussian and cylinder interaction probability methods yield predictions differing by approximately 15%; the mean free path method yields cross sections approximately 50% smaller than the Gaussian and cylinder methods.
- The Gaussian and cylinder algorithms reproduce the correct qualitative behavior of the proton-carbon cross section data, though the position of the peak is not exactly reproduced.

## Context

This work extends the semi-classical intranuclear cascade approach pioneered by Serber (1947) and Metropolis (1958) by incorporating realistic nuclear configurations from quantum Monte Carlo calculations, which include nucleon-nucleon correlations absent in mean-field models. The authors compare their results to the mean free path method used in event generators like GENIE (Andreopoulos, 2009) and NuWro (Golan, 2012), and find that the impact-parameter-based algorithms (cylinder and Gaussian) significantly outperform the mean free path approach for proton-carbon scattering. The model enables future inclusion of pion degrees of freedom and more sophisticated treatments of Pauli blocking, as discussed in Gonzalez-Jimenez (2019).
