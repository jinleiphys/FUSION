# Photoabsorption on nuclei

> We calculate the total photoabsorption cross section on nuclei in the energy range from 300 MeV to 1 GeV within the framework of a semi-classical phase space model. Besides medium modifications like Fermi motion and Pauli blocking we focus on the collision broadening of the involved resonances. The resonance contributions to the elementary cross section are fixed by fits to partial wave amplitudes of pion photoproduction. The cross sections for $N \, R \to N \, N$, needed for the calculation of collision broadening, are obtained by detailed balance from a fit to $N \, N \to N \, N \, π$ cross sections. We show that a reasonable collision broadening is not able to explain the experimentally observed disappearance of the $D_{13}(1520)$-resonance in the photoabsorption cross section on nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a reasonable collision broadening of nucleon resonances, calculated from a consistent microscopic model, is not able to explain the experimentally observed disappearance of the $D_{13}(1520)$-resonance in the total photoabsorption cross section on nuclei; the collision widths at the resonance poles are only of the order 20–40 MeV, far smaller than the 300 MeV needed to wash out the structure.

## Method

The authors calculate total photoabsorption cross sections on nuclei from 300 MeV to 1 GeV using a semi-classical phase space model with a local Thomas-Fermi density distribution. Resonance contributions to the elementary photon-nucleon cross section are fixed by fits to partial-wave amplitudes of pion photoproduction, and cross sections for resonance-nucleon absorption processes ($N R \to N N$) are obtained by detailed balance from fits to $N N \to N N \pi$ data. Medium modifications include Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, and collision broadening of the $P_{33}(1232)$, $D_{13}(1520)$, $F_{15}(1680)$, and $S_{11}(1535)$ resonances, with in-medium widths calculated in nuclear matter and applied to finite nuclei via local density approximation.

## Key numbers

- At nuclear saturation density $\rho_0 = 0.168/\text{fm}^3$, the collision width from $N \Delta \to N N$ at the $\Delta(1232)$ pole (mass 1.232 GeV) is about 25 MeV.
- The matrix element for $N N \to N R$ (higher resonances) is fitted to $|{\cal M}|^2 / (16\pi) = 18\ \text{mb GeV}^2$.
- The matrix element for $p p \to p N^+(1535)$ is $|{\cal M}|^2 / (16\pi) = 8\ \text{mb GeV}^2$; for $p n \to n N^+(1535)$ it is $20\ \text{mb GeV}^2$.
- The collision widths at the poles of the $D_{13}(1520)$, $S_{11}(1535)$, and $F_{15}(1680)$ resonances are only of order 20–40 MeV.
- At a photon energy of 700 MeV, the calculation overestimates the experimental cross section by about 25%.
- The $\Delta$-peak is reduced by about 100 $\mu$b per nucleon from Fermi motion alone, and by another 100 $\mu$b from Pauli blocking; applying in-medium widths shifts the peak and increases it by about 70 $\mu$b per nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the semi-classical transport approach (BUU model) previously applied to heavy-ion collisions (Teis 1993) and pion-nucleus reactions (Engel 1993) to photonuclear reactions in the resonance region. It directly contradicts the phenomenological fits of Alberico (1994) and Kondratyuk (1994), who inferred that the $D_{13}$-resonance width must increase by a factor of about 4 in nuclei; the present calculation shows such large collision widths are not microscopically justified. The results enable future work on alternative in-medium effects, such as a downward mass shift of the $\rho$-meson (Chanfray 1995) or medium modifications of the two-pion background, and motivate exclusive measurements of two-pion photoproduction on nuclei to clarify the disappearance of the $D_{13}$-resonance.
