# Constraints on two-neutron separation energy in the Borromean $^{22}$C nucleus

> The recently extracted matter radius of carbon isotope $^{22}$C allows us to estimate the mean-square distance of a halo neutron with respect to the center-of-mass of this nucleus. By considering this information, we suggest an energy region for an experimental investigation of the unbound $^{21}$C virtual state. Our analysis, in a renormalized zero-ranged three-body model, also indicates that the two-neutron separation energy in $^{22}$C is expected to be found below $\sim$0.4~MeV, where the $^{22}$C is approximated by a Borromean configuration with a pointlike $^{20}$C and two $s$-wave halo neutrons. A virtual-state energy of $^{21}$C close to zero, would make the $^{22}$C, within Borromean nuclei configurations, the most promising candidate to present an excited bound Efimov state or a continuum three-body resonance.

## Key claim

The two-neutron separation energy in the Borromean nucleus ²²C is expected to be found below approximately 0.4 MeV, based on a renormalized zero-range three-body model that uses the recently extracted matter radius of ²²C as a constraint; this result depends on the assumption that the ²¹C virtual-state energy is close to zero.

## Method

The authors used a renormalized zero-range (Dirac-delta) three-body Faddeev model with a pointlike ²⁰C core and two s-wave halo neutrons, solving subtracted integral equations for the spectator functions. They calculated the root-mean-square distance of a halo neutron to the three-body center of mass as a function of the two-neutron separation energy S₂ₙ, for assumed ²¹C virtual-state energies ranging from 0 to 100 keV. The calculated neutron distances were then compared with the experimental value of 15 ± 4 fm derived from the measured matter radii of ²²C and ²⁰C.

## Key numbers

- Matter radius of ²²C: 5.4 ± 0.9 fm, extracted via finite-range Glauber analysis of reaction cross section on a liquid hydrogen target at about 40 MeV/nucleon (Tanaka 2010).
- Matter radius of ²⁰C: 2.98 ± 0.05 fm (Ozawa).
- Estimated root-mean-square distance of a halo neutron to the center of mass of ²²C: rₙ ≈ 15 ± 4 fm.
- Experimental two-neutron separation energy of ²²C: S₂ₙ⁽ᵉˣᵖ⁾ = 0.42 ± 0.94 MeV (Tanaka 2010).
- Singlet n-n virtual-state energy: Eₙₙ = −143 keV.
- For a ²¹C virtual-state energy at threshold (0 keV), the model gives S₂ₙ below approximately 0.4 MeV; increasing the virtual-state energy to 20 keV drops the upper limit of S₂ₙ to approximately 0.3 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the three-body halo model previously applied to describe halo radii (Yamashita 2004) and two-neutron correlation functions (Yamashita 2005) to the heaviest known Borromean halo nucleus ²²C, following the breakthrough measurement of its matter radius by Tanaka (2010). The analysis suggests that ²²C is a more promising candidate than ²⁰C (Amorim 1997) to exhibit an excited bound Efimov state or a continuum three-body resonance, provided the ²¹C virtual-state energy is close to zero. The results provide guidance for future experimental searches for the ²²C two-neutron separation energy and the ²¹C virtual-state energy.
