# Scenario for quark confinement from infrared safe Yang-Mills dynamics

> We revisit the non-Abelian dipole problem in the context of a simple semiclassical approach that incorporates some essential features of the infrared sector of Yang-Mills theories in the Landau gauge, in particular, the fact that both the running coupling and the gluon propagator remain finite at infrared scales and that the latter shows positivity violations that reflects the presence of massless modes. We obtain a simple flux tube solution in a controlled approximation scheme, which we compare to the results of lattice simulations.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the existence of a flux tube configuration, and thus quark confinement, follows generically from the infrared finiteness of the Yang-Mills coupling and the positivity violations in the gluon propagator, rather than from any specific model. The claim is hedged by the fact that the present semiclassical calculation is performed at leading order in a controlled expansion and that the precise value of the mass scale \(M\) depends on the details of the infrared-safe approach used (here, the Curci-Ferrari model).

## Method

The authors work in the Landau-DeWitt gauge and expand the background field effective action to quadratic order in an inhomogeneous background field component \(A(r)\), treating the homogeneous background \(\kappa\) exactly. They solve the resulting linearized equation of motion for the flux tube profile, using the one-loop expression for the background field two-point function from the Curci-Ferrari model, which provides a second root \(q^2 = M^2 > 0\) in the Euclidean domain. The homogeneous background \(\kappa\) is then determined by minimizing the effective action including the next-order (quartic) terms in a local potential approximation.

## Key numbers

*   For SU(2), using the Curci-Ferrari best-fit parameters \(g=7.5\) and \(m=0.68\ \text{GeV}\) at \(\mu=1\ \text{GeV}\), the predicted string tension is \(\sqrt{\sigma}=463\ \text{MeV}\), compared to the lattice value \(\sqrt{\sigma_{\text{lat}}}=440\ \text{MeV}\).
*   For SU(3), using the best-fit parameters \(g=4.9\) and \(m=0.54\ \text{GeV}\), the predicted string tension is \(\sqrt{\sigma}=334\ \text{MeV}\), compared to \(\sqrt{\sigma_{\text{lat}}}=440\ \text{MeV}\).
*   A fit of the SU(2) lattice data of Koma et al. (2003) for the longitudinal chromoelectric field yields \(\phi=2.38\) and \(\kappa=1.33\ \text{GeV}\), giving \(\sqrt{\sigma}=446\ \text{MeV}\), remarkably close to the lattice value \(\sqrt{\sigma_{\text{lat}}}=440\ \text{MeV}\).
*   A fit of the SU(3) lattice data of DIK (2003) yields \(\phi=2.8\) and \(\kappa=1.08\ \text{GeV}\), giving \(\sqrt{\sigma}=425\ \text{MeV}\), compared to the lattice value \(\sqrt{\sigma_{\text{lat}}}=464\ \text{MeV}\).
*   The leading-order Curci-Ferrari prediction for the ratio of the critical temperature to the string tension is \(T_c/\sqrt{\sigma}=0.68\) for SU(2) and \(0.59\) for SU(3), in agreement with the lattice results \(0.7\) and \(0.64\), respectively.

## Context

This work extends the observation that infrared-safe Yang-Mills dynamics (finite coupling, nonzero gluon screening mass, positivity violations) explains the vanishing Polyakov loop at low temperatures (Braun, 2007) to the problem of the flux tube between static quarks. It provides a semiclassical flux tube solution from first-principles equations of motion, in contrast to the dual superconductor model (Nielsen, 1973; Mandelstam, 1974; t Hooft, 1974; Kogut, 1974; Polyakov, 1975) which appeals to effective scalar degrees of freedom, and to earlier attempts (Savvidy, 1977; Adler, 1981) that relied on standard perturbation theory with its spurious Landau pole. The results enable systematic computation of corrections, extension to higher representations, finite-distance effects, and inclusion of dynamical quarks and nonzero temperature.
