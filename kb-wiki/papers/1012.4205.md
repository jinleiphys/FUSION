# Leading infrared logarithms for sigma-model with fields on arbitrary Riemann manifold

> We derive non-linear recursion equation for the leading infrared logarithms (LL) in four dimensional sigma-model with fields on an arbitrary Riemann manifold. The derived equation allows one to compute leading infrared logarithms to essentially unlimited loop order in terms of geometric characteristics of the Riemann manifold. We reduce the solution of the SU(oo) principal chiral field in arbitrary number of dimensions in the LL approximation to the solution of very simple recursive equation. This result paves a way to the solution of the model in arbitrary number of dimensions at N-->oo

## Key claim

The paper derives a non-linear recursion equation for the leading infrared logarithms (LL) in a four-dimensional sigma-model with fields on an arbitrary Riemann manifold, allowing computation to essentially unlimited loop order in terms of geometric characteristics; however, the authors did not find an analytic solution to the recursion equation for the general case, and the solution for the SU(∞) principal chiral field is reduced to a simple recursive equation that also has not yet been solved analytically.

## Method

The authors use general principles of quantum field theory, analyticity, unitarity, and crossing, to derive a non-linear recursion equation (Eq. 5) that expresses higher-loop leading logarithm coefficients in terms of lower-loop coefficients, starting from tree-level expressions (Eq. 4) that involve the Riemann curvature tensor at the origin of normal coordinates. The method is applied to the general sigma-model action with fields on an arbitrary Riemann manifold, and then specialized to the cases of the N-dimensional sphere S^N and the SU(N) principal chiral field (target space G×G/G). For the SU(∞) case, the recursion is further reduced using isometries and projectors onto irreducible representations of SU(N), yielding a simpler recursion equation (Eq. 10) for the coefficients ρ_{nl}.

## Key numbers

- Tree-level LL coefficients: (ω_{10})_{abcd} = -½( R̊_{acbd} + R̊_{adbc} ), (ω_{11})_{abcd} = ½ R̊_{abcd} (Eq. 4).
- 1-loop LL coefficients for general sigma-model are given in Eq. 6; for S^N, explicit expressions involve F (radius of sphere) and N, e.g., (ω_{20})_{abcd} = (1/(18 F^4))[(3N-1)(δ_{ad}δ_{bc}+δ_{ac}δ_{bd}) + (9N-17)δ_{ab}δ_{cd}].
- 2-loop LL coefficients for general sigma-model are given in Eq. 7; for S^N, coefficients a_{30} = -59 + 27N - 18N^2, b_{30} = 86 - 158N + 72N^2, etc.
- Large N solution for S^N: (ω_{nl})_{abcd}^{Large N} = (1/F^2)(-N/(2F^2))^{n-1} [ ((2l+1)n n!/(n+l+1)!)(δ_{ad}δ_{bc}+(-1)^l δ_{ac}δ_{bd}) + (-1)^{n-1} δ_{l0} δ_{ab}δ_{cd} ] (Eq. 8).
- For SU(N) principal chiral field at large N: (ω_{nl})_{abcd}^{Large N} = (ρ_{nl}/(16 F^2)) (N/(2F^2))^{n-1} tr([t^a,t^b][t^c,t^d]) for odd l, with ρ_{10}=ρ_{11}=1 (Eq. 9, 10).

## Context

This work extends the method of non-linear recursion equations for leading logarithms developed in Refs. MKV_LLog, MKV_FF, KPV (2008-2010) from simpler models to the general sigma-model on an arbitrary Riemann manifold, providing a geometric interpretation of LL coefficients. It contrasts with earlier approaches such as the renormalization group method of BC_2003, which required calculation of non-trivial one-loop diagrams at each order and was applied by Bijnens (2005) up to five loops for the O(N+1)/O(N) sigma model, and with dispersive methods used by Biss2 (2007) for three-loop LLs in chiral perturbation theory. The results enable computation of LLs to arbitrarily high loop order for any sigma-model, and the reduction for SU(∞) principal chiral field to a simple recursive equation (Eq. 10) opens the possibility of solving this model in arbitrary dimensions at N→∞, which has not been achieved despite many hopes (Ref. AMP).
