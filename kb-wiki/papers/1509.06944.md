# Baryon stopping signal for mixed phase formation in HIC

> It is argued that an irregularity in the baryon stopping is a natural consequence of onset of deconfinement occurring in the compression stage of a nuclear collision. It is an effect of the softest point inherent in an equation of state (EoS) with a deconfinement transition. In order to illustrate this effect, calculations within the three-fluid model were performed with three different EoS's: a purely hadronic EoS, an EoS with a first-order phase transition and that with a smooth crossover transition.

# Baryon stopping signal for mixed phase formation in HIC

## Key claim
An irregularity in the incident-energy dependence of baryon stopping, specifically a "peak-dip-peak-dip" pattern in the shape of net-proton rapidity distributions, is a natural consequence of deconfinement onset during the compression stage of a nuclear collision, arising from the softest point in an equation of state with a phase transition. The claim is hedged by the fact that the experimental evidence is only a "hint" based on preliminary NA49 data, and the first-order-transition scenario qualitatively matches the wiggle but quantitatively disagrees with data in the energy region 8A GeV ≤ E_lab ≤ 40A GeV.

## Method
The author performed calculations within the three-fluid dynamics (3FD) model for central Au+Au (AGS) and Pb+Pb (SPS) collisions, using three different equations of state: a purely hadronic EoS, an EoS with a first-order phase transition (2-phase EoS), and an EoS with a smooth crossover transition. The net-proton rapidity distributions were fitted with a sum of two thermal sources shifted from midrapidity, and the reduced curvature C_y at midrapidity was extracted as a quantitative measure of the distribution shape across collision energies.

## Key numbers
- The first-order-transition scenario produces a "peak-dip-peak-dip" irregularity in the energy dependence of the reduced curvature C_y, while the hadronic scenario produces a purely monotonous behavior and the crossover EoS produces only a weak wiggle.
- The 2-phase-EoS results quantitatively disagree with data in the energy region 8A GeV ≤ E_lab ≤ 40A GeV.
- The curvature C_y is defined as (y_beam^3 d^3N/dy^3)_(y=0) / (y_beam dN/dy)_(y=0), with fit ranges of |y| < 0.7 y_beam and |y| < 0.5 y_beam used to produce shaded areas in the results.
- The fit function used is dN/dy = a [exp{-(1/w_s) cosh(y-y_s)} + exp{-(1/w_s) cosh(y+y_s)}], where a, y_s, and w_s are fit parameters.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of the softest point in equations of state with deconfinement transitions, previously studied by Hung and Shuryak (1994) and Nikonov et al. (1998), and applies it to baryon stopping as a signal from the hot and dense compression stage of nuclear collisions. The results contradict purely hadronic scenarios (gasEOS) and smooth crossover transitions (Toneev et al., 2006), while qualitatively matching preliminary NA49 data. The irregularity was previously demonstrated by Ivanov (2010) to survive even under limited acceptance conditions, and new experimental data from FAIR and NICA are needed to confirm the trend.
