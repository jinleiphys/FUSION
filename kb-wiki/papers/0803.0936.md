# Dynamical coupled channel approach to omega meson production

> The dynamical coupled channel approach is applied to study the $ω$--meson production induced by pions and photons scattering from the proton. The parameters of the model are fixed in a two-channel (\on,\pn) calculation for the non-resonant and resonant contributions to the $T$ matrix by fitting the available unpolarized differential cross section data. The polarized photon beam asymmetry is predicted and compared to existing data.

## Key claim

The dynamical coupled channel approach can describe unpolarized differential cross section data for omega meson production from both pion and photon beams on the proton, but the predicted polarized photon beam asymmetry, while having the correct sign and magnitude, is not quantitatively consistent with existing data; the author notes that inclusion of additional channels (eta N, pi Delta, rho N, sigma N) and possibly more resonances may improve the agreement.

## Method

The authors apply the Matsuyama-Sato-Lee (MSL) dynamical coupled channel formalism in a two-channel (pi N, omega N) calculation, separating the T matrix into non-resonant and resonant contributions. The non-resonant kernel is given by Born amplitudes depending on coupling constants and cutoff parameters (g_{rho NN}, kappa_rho, g_{rho pi pi}, g_{omega NN}, kappa_omega, g_{omega pi rho}, and five cutoff parameters), while the resonant contribution includes bare masses, vertex amplitudes, and cutoffs for strong and electromagnetic vertices. Parameters are fixed by fitting unpolarized differential cross section data for both pi- p -> omega n and gamma p -> omega p simultaneously.

## Key numbers

* The omega meson width is neglected in the calculation; its physical value is given as Gamma_omega = 8.5(1) MeV.
* The center-of-mass energy range considered is 1.4 GeV to 2.0 GeV (the second and third resonance regions).
* The photon beam asymmetry prediction is shown at E = 1.743 GeV.
* The differential cross section for gamma p -> omega p is plotted in units of microbarns per steradian for several center-of-mass energies (values shown in figure panels, e.g., 1.743 GeV).

## Context

This work extends the dynamical coupled channel formalism of Matsuyama, Sato, and Lee (MSL) to omega meson production, which had previously been applied to other meson production channels. The approach is similar to that of Penner (2001) who described threshold behavior in coupled channel calculations. The study is a preliminary step toward a full calculation incorporating stable eta N and unstable pi Delta, rho N, and sigma N channels, which the author states is being pursued. The model's ability to predict polarized observables from fits to unpolarized data is tested, with the goal of resolving the nucleon resonance spectrum in the second and third resonance regions.
