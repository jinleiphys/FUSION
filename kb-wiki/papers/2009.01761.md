# Coulomb sum rule for $^4$He and $^{16}$O from coupled-cluster theory

> We demonstrate the capability of coupled-cluster theory to compute the Coulomb sum rule for the $^4$He and $^{16}$O nuclei using interactions from chiral effective field theory. We perform several checks, including a few-body benchmark for $^4$He. We provide an analysis of the center-of-mass contaminations, which we are able to safely remove. We then compare with other theoretical results and experimental data available in the literature, obtaining a fair agreement. This is a first and necessary step towards initiating a program for computing neutrino-nucleus interactions from first principles and supporting the experimental long-baseline neutrino program with a state-of-the-art theory that can reach medium-mass nuclei.

## Key claim

Coupled-cluster theory can compute the Coulomb sum rule for \(^4\)He and \(^{16}\)O using chiral effective field theory interactions, but only after removing center-of-mass contaminations that arise from translationally non-invariant operators; the agreement with experiment is described as "fair" rather than exact.

## Method

The authors used coupled-cluster theory with singles-and-doubles (CCSD) approximation to compute the Coulomb sum rule via two approaches: method A computes the sum rule as a ground-state expectation value of the squared charge operator, while method B performs a multipole decomposition of the charge operator and sums over multipoles. For method B, spurious center-of-mass excitations were identified by their unphysically low excitation energies (below 15 MeV for \(^4\)He and at 0.15 MeV for \(^{16}\)O) and removed by projecting them out of the Lanczos iterations. The calculations used the N\(^3\)LO-EM nucleon-nucleon potential for benchmarks and the NNLO\(_{\rm sat}\) interaction (including three-nucleon forces) for comparison with experiment.

## Key numbers

- For \(^4\)He at \(q=100\) MeV, removing spurious states improved agreement between method A and method B from 50% to 0.5%; at \(q=200\) MeV, from 35% to 3%.
- For \(^{16}\)O at \(q=100\) MeV, removing spurious states improved agreement from 38% to 3.5%; at \(q=200\) MeV, from 20% to 8%.
- For \(q\) below 200 MeV, only four multipoles were needed for convergence; for \(q=500\) MeV, nine multipoles were needed for \(^4\)He and eleven for \(^{16}\)O.
- The spurious \(1^-\) state in \(^4\)He appeared at approximately \(5\times10^{-2}\) MeV above ground state; a \(2^+\) state appeared at 10 MeV above ground state.
- The spurious \(1^-\) state in \(^{16}\)O appeared at 0.15 MeV above ground state.
- The center-of-mass oscillator frequency was \(\hbar\tilde{\omega}=20\) MeV for \(^4\)He and 16 MeV for \(^{16}\)O.
- Calculations used 15 major oscillator shells with \(\hbar\omega=20\) MeV for the harmonic oscillator basis.

## Context

This work extends the Lorentz integral transform coupled-cluster (LIT-CC) method previously applied to giant dipole resonances (Bacca et al., 2013, 2014) toward electroweak response calculations for neutrino-nucleus scattering. It provides a benchmark against hyperspherical harmonics calculations for \(^4\)He and compares with Green's function Monte Carlo results (Lovato et al., 2013) using the AV18+IL7 potential and with earlier coupled-cluster calculations by Mihaila et al. (2000) using the AV18+UIX potential. The ability to compute the Coulomb sum rule from first principles in medium-mass nuclei is a necessary step toward supporting the experimental long-baseline neutrino program (DUNE, T2HK) with ab initio calculations of neutrino-nucleus interactions.
