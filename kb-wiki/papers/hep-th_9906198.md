# Magnetic Confinement in QCD

> We present a strong evidence for the magnetic confinement in QCD by demonstrating that the one loop effective action of SU(2) QCD induces a dynamical symmetry breaking thorugh the monopole condensation, which could induce the dual Meissner effect and guarantee the confinement of color in the non-Abelian gauge theory. The result is obtained by separating the topological degrees which describes the non-Abelian monopoles from the dynamical degrees of the potential, and integrating out all the dynamical degrees of QCD.

## Key claim

The one-loop effective action of SU(2) QCD induces monopole condensation, which could produce the dual Meissner effect and guarantee color confinement, but this result depends on the separation of topological degrees (the non-Abelian monopole potential) from dynamical degrees and on the exclusion of unphysical tachyonic modes from the functional determinant.

## Method

The authors decompose the SU(2) gauge potential into an Abelian projection (the restricted potential that leaves a unit isotriplet vector field invariant) and a gauge-covariant charged vector field (valence gluons). They then integrate out all dynamical degrees (the electric potential, the valence gluons, and ghost fields) in one-loop approximation, treating the magnetic monopole potential as a background, and compute the resulting effective action using heat kernel methods and zeta-function regularization.

## Key numbers

- The effective Lagrangian is \( \mathcal{L}_{eff} = -\frac{Z}{4} \vec{H}^2_{\mu\nu} \) with \( Z = 1 + \frac{22}{3} \frac{g^2}{(4\pi)^2} \left( \ln \frac{gH}{\mu^2} - c \right) \), where \( c = 1.2921409\ldots \).
- The vacuum expectation value of the magnetic field is \( \langle H \rangle = \frac{\mu^2}{g} \exp\left( -\frac{24\pi^2}{11g^2} + c - \frac12 \right) \).
- The effective mass generated for the monopole potential is \( m^2 = \frac{11g^4}{96\pi^2} \left\langle \frac{(\vec{C}_\mu \times \vec{H}_{\mu\nu})^2}{H^2} \right\rangle \).
- The beta function obtained is \( \beta(\bar\mu) = -\frac{11}{24\pi^2} \bar{g}^3 \), matching the standard perturbative QCD result for SU(2).
- The anomalous dimension for the monopole potential is \( \gamma = -\frac{11}{24\pi^2} \bar{g}^2 \), compared to \( \gamma(\vec{A}_\mu) = \frac{5}{24\pi^2} \bar{g}^2 \) for the gluon field in perturbative SU(2) QCD.

## Context

This work extends the magnetic confinement hypothesis proposed by Nambu and by Cho (early 1980s) and the Abelian projection formalism developed by Cho and by 't Hooft, providing a first-principles demonstration of monopole condensation in one-loop QCD. It contrasts with the instanton-based confinement mechanism and with the earlier Savvidy vacuum (Savvidy, 1977), which was unstable; the authors argue their vacuum is stable after excluding tachyonic modes. The result enables a derivation of the Skyrme-Faddeev Lagrangian from QCD and connects the effective theory to topological knot solitons, while also reproducing the standard perturbative beta function.
