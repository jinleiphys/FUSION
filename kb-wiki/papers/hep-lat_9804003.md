# An interquark potential model for multi-quark systems

> A potential model for four interacting quarks is constructed in SU(2) from six basis states -- the three partitions into quark pairs, where the gluon field is either in its ground state or first excited state. With four independent parameters to describe the interactions connecting these basis states, it is possible to fit 100 pieces of data -- the ground and first excited states of configurations from six different four-quark geometries calculated on a 16^3*32 lattice.

# An interquark potential model for multi-quark systems

## Key claim

A six-basis-state potential model for four interacting static SU(2) quarks, including both ground-state and first-excited gluon field configurations, can fit 100 pieces of lattice data across six geometries with only four independent parameters, but the model is constructed in the quenched approximation and uses SU(2) rather than full QCD, so its applicability to real QCD systems remains conditional on the assumption that SU(2) suffices for this type of discussion.

## Method

The authors construct a potential model for four static quarks using six basis states: the three possible partitions into quark pairs (A, B, C) where each pair forms a color singlet, plus three additional states (A*, B*, C*) where the gluon field between each pair is excited to the lowest E_u representation of the lattice symmetry group. The model incorporates overlap factors f, f^a, and f^c that reduce the off-diagonal matrix elements from their weak-coupling one-gluon-exchange values as the quarks separate, with the two-quark potentials v(ij) and excited-state potentials v*(ij) fitted separately to lattice data. The resulting 6x6 generalized eigenvalue problem is solved and fitted using Minuit to 100 data points from six four-quark geometries (tetrahedra, squares, rectangles, quadrilaterals, non-planar, and linear) calculated on a 16^3 x 32 lattice at beta=2.4.

## Key numbers

- 100 pieces of data fitted: ground and first excited states from six different four-quark geometries
- 4 independent parameters in the final model (k_f, f^a_2, k_a, and either a_0 or c_0)
- chi^2/d.o.f. of 1.10 for the six-basis-state model (Model III)
- chi^2/d.o.f. of 3.16 for the two-basis-state model (Model Ia) with k_f = 0.571(12)
- For regular tetrahedra, the lowest state binding energy increases from -0.0202(8) to -0.028(3) as the cube side d increases from 2 to 4 lattice units
- For squares, the lowest state binding energy decreases from -0.0572(4) to -0.047(3) as d increases from 2 to 5
- Two-quark potential fit: v(r_ij) = 0.562 + 0.0696 r_ij - 0.255/r_ij - 0.045/r^2_ij (in lattice units at beta=2.4)
- Excited-state potential difference: Delta v = pi/r_ij - 4.24/r^2_ij + 3.983/r^4_ij
- Lattice spacing at beta=2.4 is approximately 0.12 fm
- Minimum error assumed: 0.005 or 10% of the eigenvalue, whichever is larger (corresponding to about 8 MeV)

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies by the Helsinki group (Green, Pennanen, and collaborators, 1996) of four static SU(2) quarks in various geometries, and constructs a phenomenological model that can be extended to more complex multi-quark systems. The model goes beyond simpler two-basis-state approaches (Furui, 1995; Sakai, 1997) that used only the standard two-quark potential, which the authors show fails badly for larger geometries (e.g., a 6x6 square gives E_0 = -0.124, E_1 = 0.3721 versus lattice values of -0.028(5) and 0.065(7)). The six-basis-state model enables future applications such as Resonating Group calculations of meson-meson scattering (Sakai, 1997) and can be extended to systems with two light and two heavy quarks using full QCD gauge configurations from the UKQCD collaboration.
