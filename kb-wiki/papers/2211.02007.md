# A Bayesian inference of relativistic mean-field model for neutron star matter from observation of NICER and GW170817/AT2017gfo

> The observations of optical and near-infrared counterparts of binary neutron star mergers not only enrich our knowledge about the abundance of heavy elements in the Universe, or help reveal the remnant object just after the merger as generally known, but also can effectively constrain dense nuclear matter properties and the equation of state (EOS) in the interior of the merging stars. Following the relativistic mean-field description of nuclear matter, we perform the Bayesian inference of the EOS and the nuclear matter properties using the first multi-messenger event GW170817/AT2017gfo, together with the NICER mass-radius measurements of pulsars. The kilonova is described by a radiation-transfer model with the dynamical ejecta, and light curves connect with the EOS through the quasi-universal relations between the ejecta properties (the ejected mass, velocity, opacity or electron fraction) and binary parameters (the mass ratio and reduced tidal deformability). It is found that the posterior distributions of the reduced tidal deformability from the AT2017gfo analysis display a bimodal structure, with the first peak enhanced by the GW170817 data, leading to slightly softened posterior EOSs, while the second peak cannot be achieved by a nuclear EOS with saturation properties in their empirical ranges. The inclusion of NICER data in our analyses results in stiffened EOS posterior because of the massive pulsar PSR J0740+6620. We give results at nuclear saturation density for the nuclear incompressibility, the symmetry energy and its slope, as well as the nucleon effective mass, from our analysis of those observational data.

## Key claim

The kilonova AT2017gfo data alone produce a bimodal posterior distribution for the reduced tidal deformability, where only the first peak is consistent with nuclear equations of state that have saturation properties within empirical ranges; the second peak cannot be achieved by such nuclear EOSs. Including NICER data stiffens the EOS posterior, primarily because of the massive pulsar PSR J0740+6620.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian inference of the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model for neutron star matter, using the six saturation properties of nuclear matter (saturation density, energy per baryon, symmetry energy, incompressibility, symmetry energy slope, and nucleon effective mass) as free parameters. They connected the EOS to kilonova light curves through quasi-universal relations linking ejecta properties (ejected mass, velocity, electron fraction) to binary parameters (mass ratio and reduced tidal deformability), and computed the likelihood from the AT2017gfo light curve data, the GW170817 gravitational wave data via the `toast` package, and the NICER mass-radius measurements of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620 using kernel density estimation.

## Key numbers

- The chirp mass of the binary was fixed with a uniform prior between 1.18 and 1.21 solar masses.
- The mass ratio q was sampled with a uniform prior from 1 to 2.
- The ejected mass Mej had a uniform prior from 0.001 to 0.01 solar masses.
- The low opacity kappa_low had a constrained uniform prior from 0.1 to 30 cm^2/g, with best-fit value 0.7844 cm^2/g.
- The high opacity kappa_high had a constrained uniform prior from 0.1 to 30 cm^2/g, with best-fit value 6.9509 cm^2/g.
- The minimal velocity v_min had a uniform prior from 0.01c to 0.15c, with best-fit value 0.1061c.
- The maximal velocity v_max had a uniform prior from 0.18c to 0.65c, with best-fit value 0.5066c.
- The symmetry energy J0 was sampled uniformly from 30 to 35 MeV.
- The incompressibility K0 was sampled uniformly from 220 to 280 MeV.
- The symmetry energy slope L0 was sampled uniformly from 20 to 85 MeV.
- The effective mass ratio M_N*/M_N was sampled uniformly from 0.65 to 0.80.
- The three deviation parameters (alpha_m, alpha_v, alpha_e) for the quasi-universal relations each had a Gaussian prior with mean 0 and standard deviation 0.2.

## Context

This work extends the approach of connecting nuclear matter microscopic parameters to kilonova data, following studies such as Margalit (2017), Radice (2017b), Coughlin (2019), Breschi (2021), and Holmbeck (2022) that used piecewise polytropes or spectral parametrizations. By using the RMF model, the authors directly infer physical nuclear matter properties (incompressibility, symmetry energy, effective mass) that can be compared with laboratory nuclear structure and reaction studies. The analysis enables future incorporation of strangeness phase transitions, as explored by Miao (2020), Li (2021), Li (2021b), Sun (2022), and Miao (2022).
