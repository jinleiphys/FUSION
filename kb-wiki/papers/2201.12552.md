# Relativistic description of dense matter equation of state and compatibility with neutron star observables: a Bayesian approach

> The general behavior of the nuclear equation of state (EOS), relevant for the description of neutron stars (NS), is studied within a Bayesian approach applied to a set of models based on a density dependent relativistic mean field description of nuclear matter. The EOS is subjected to a minimal number of constraints based on nuclear saturation properties and the low density pure neutron matter EOS obtained from a precise next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^{3}$LO) calculation in chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT). The posterior distributions of the model parameters obtained under these minimal constraints are employed to construct the distributions of various nuclear matter properties and NS properties such as radii, tidal deformabilites, central energy densities and speeds of sound etc. We found that 90% confidence interval (CI) for allowed NS mass - radius relationship and tidal deformabilites are compatible with GW170817 and recent NICER observations, without invoking the exotic degrees of freedom. A central speed-of-sound of the order of $\sqrt{2/3}$ $c$ is obtained. The maximum neutron star mass allowed by the model is 2.5 $M_\odot$.

## Key claim
Using a Bayesian approach with minimal constraints from nuclear saturation properties and chiral effective field theory, the authors find that a purely nucleonic (no exotic degrees of freedom) density-dependent relativistic mean field description of neutron star matter is compatible with the 90% confidence intervals of GW170817 and NICER observations, yielding a maximum neutron star mass of 2.5 solar masses.

## Method
The authors performed a Bayesian parameter estimation for a density-dependent relativistic mean field (DDH) model of nuclear matter, where the coupling constants of the sigma, omega, and rho mesons to nucleons are density-dependent. The model was constrained using nuclear saturation properties and the low-density pure neutron matter equation of state from a next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) chiral effective field theory calculation, without imposing a speed-of-sound causality condition since the relativistic framework is inherently causal. Posterior distributions of model parameters were then used to construct distributions of nuclear matter properties and neutron star observables by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations.

## Key numbers
- Maximum neutron star mass allowed by the model: 2.5 solar masses
- Central speed of sound obtained: of the order of sqrt(2/3) c (approximately 0.816 c)
- 90% confidence interval for neutron star mass-radius relationship and tidal deformabilities found compatible with GW170817 and NICER observations

## Context
This work extends the density-dependent relativistic mean field approach of Typel (1999) and Typel (2009) by applying Bayesian inference to systematically explore the parameter space under minimal constraints. It complements recent meta-model studies such as Thi (2021) which concluded that the "nucleonic hypothesis" is sufficient to explain current neutron star observations. The results enable future extensions to include exotic degrees of freedom (hyperons or deconfinement transitions) to test whether such additions are required by observational data.
