# Exploring GPDs through the photoproduction of a $γρ$ pair

> We describe the process $γN \to γ\,ρN'$ in the generalized Bjorken regime where the $γ\,ρ$ pair has a large invariant mass. In the collinear QCD factorization framework, the amplitude gives access to both chiral-even and chiral-odd quark generalized parton distributions (GPDs), and is insensitive to gluon GPDs. The separation of longitudinally and transversely polarized $ρ$ meson production allows to distinguish chiral-even and chiral-odd contributions. Production rates are estimated in the kinematics of the near-future JLab~12-GeV experiments.

# Exploring GPDs through the photoproduction of a γρ pair

## Key claim
The process γN → γρN' in the generalized Bjorken regime, where the γρ pair has a large invariant mass, gives access to both chiral-even and chiral-odd quark generalized parton distributions (GPDs) while being insensitive to gluon GPDs, with production rates estimated to be large enough for measurement at JLab 12-GeV experiments, though the cross-section estimates rely on specific models of the GPDs and leading-order calculations in αs.

## Method
The authors applied collinear QCD factorization at leading order in the strong coupling constant αs, treating the short-distance part of the amplitude perturbatively and encapsulating long-distance physics in leading-twist hadronic matrix elements: the ρ meson distribution amplitude (DA) and nucleon generalized parton distributions (GPDs). The scattering amplitude was computed via a tensorial decomposition, with the integral over the meson DA variable z performed analytically using a Gegenbauer polynomial expansion, and the integral over the GPD variable x reduced to numerical evaluation of building-block integrals. The differential cross section dσ/dM²γρ was obtained by integrating over the momentum transfer t and u' variables, with a form factor F²H(t) included.

## Key numbers
- The ρ meson decay constants used: fρ⁰ = 216 MeV for longitudinal polarization and f⊥ρ = 160 MeV for transverse polarization.
- The differential cross sections dσ/dM²γρ for both longitudinal and transverse ρ production show a maximum around M²γρ ≈ 3 GeV² for most energy values in the JLab range.
- Cross sections were computed for SγN values in the set 8, 10, 12, 14, 16, 18, 20 GeV², covering the JLab energy range.
- For longitudinal ρ production (chiral-even GPDs), the differential cross section dσ/dM²γρ is shown in units of nb·GeV⁻²; for transverse ρ production (chiral-odd GPDs), it is shown in units of pb·GeV⁻², indicating that longitudinal production dominates over transverse production by roughly three orders of magnitude.

## Context
This work extends the factorization framework previously applied to wide-angle Compton scattering on a meson (Lepage, 1980) and exclusive meson electroproduction near the forward region (Collins, 1996) to the photoproduction of a γρ pair. The process is analogous to timelike Compton scattering (TCS) but with a more complex momentum flow that allows both timelike (M²γρ) and spacelike (u') large scales. The results enable future experimental access to transversity GPDs at JLab 12-GeV, though the authors note that next-to-leading-order corrections and careful renormalization/factorization scale fixing (e.g., using the BLM method) should be evaluated to confirm the order-of-magnitude estimates.
