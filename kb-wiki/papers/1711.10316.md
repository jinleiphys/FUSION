# Hyperasymptotics and quark-hadron duality violations in QCD

> We investigate the origin of the quark-hadron duality-violating terms in the expansion of the QCD two-point vector correlation function at large energies in the complex $q^2$ plane. Starting from the dispersive representation for the associated polarization, the analytic continuation of the operator product expansion from the Euclidean to the Minkowski region is performed by means of a generalized Borel-Laplace transform, borrowing techniques from hyperasymptotics. We establish a connection between singularities in the Borel plane and quark-hadron duality violating contributions. Starting with the assumption that for QCD at $N_c=\infty$ the spectrum approaches a Regge trajectory at large energy, we obtain an expression for quark-hadron duality violations at large, but finite $N_c$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that quark-hadron duality violations in QCD arise from singularities in the Borel plane that are located away from the origin, and that these singularities are directly connected to the form of the hadronic resonance spectrum, under the assumption that at large \(N_c\) the spectrum follows a Regge trajectory at large energy.

## Method

The authors start from a dispersive representation of the Adler function and rewrite it as a generalized Borel-Laplace transform of the spectral function. By rotating the integration contour in the complex Borel (\(\sigma\)) plane, they analytically continue the Adler function from the Euclidean region to the Minkowski region, and show that singularities crossed during this rotation generate duality-violating contributions. They first analyze a simple Regge model with a linear trajectory and constant residues in the large-\(N_c\) limit, then generalize to a spectrum with subleading logarithmic and power-law corrections, and finally extend the analysis to finite \(N_c\) where resonances acquire widths.

## Key numbers

* In the simple Regge model with \(M^2(n) = \Lambda^2 n\) and \(F(n)=F^2\) (with \(\Lambda=1\), \(F=1\)), the Borel transform \(\sigma \hat{\rho}\) has simple poles at \(\sigma = \pm 2k\pi i\) for \(k=1,2,3,\dots\), with residues \(\pm 2k\pi i\).
* The OPE expansion coefficients in this model are the Bernoulli numbers \(B(n)\), with \(B(0)=1\), \(B(1)=-1/2\), and \(B(n>1) = (-1)^{n+1} 2 (2n)! / (2\pi)^{2n} \, \zeta(2n)\).
* The finite radius of convergence of the Borel transform in this model is \(2\pi\), the distance of the singularity closest to the origin in the Borel plane.
* The duality-violating contribution to \(\Pi(q^2)\) for \(q^2>0\) in the simple Regge model is \(-\pi (\cot \pi q^2 + i)\), leading to a spectral function \(\frac{1}{\pi} \mathrm{Im}\, \Pi(q^2+i\epsilon) = \sum_{n=1}^\infty \delta(q^2 - n)\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of duality violations in QCD, such as those by Blok, Cata, and collaborators, by providing a more formal derivation based on hyperasymptotics and Borel-plane singularities. It connects the form of duality violations to the Regge behavior of the resonance spectrum, which is known from two-dimensional QCD (t Hooft, Callan, Coote, Gross) and from phenomenology (Masjuan). The results enable a systematic parametrization of duality violations for use in analyses of hadronic \(\tau\)-decay data, such as those by Boito et al. (2015), and provide a theoretical foundation for the exponential suppression of duality violations observed at finite \(N_c\).
