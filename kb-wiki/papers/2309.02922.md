# Effect of Backing on Neutron Spectra for Low Energy Quasi-Mono-energetic p+$^7$Li Reaction

> $\underline{\textbf{MO}}$nte-carlo $\underline{\textbf{N}}$ucleon transport $\underline{\textbf{C}}$ode (MONC) for nucleon transport is extended for below 20MeV proton transport using ENDF and EXFOR data base. It is used to simulate p+$^7$Li reaction upto 20MeV proton energies and produced neutron spectra are reported here. The simulated results are compared with calculated values from other available codes like PINO, EPEN, SimLiT and experimental data. The spectra reported here can be used to get the neutron cross-section for the quasi-mono-energetic neutron reaction and will help to subtract the low energy contribution. The neutron spectra also useful as this reaction is used for accelerator based Boron Neutron Capture Therapy. The backing materials are used to fully stop the proton beam hence contribution from the neutrons from backing materials is estimated. It is found that Tantalum is good backing material below $\sim$8 MeV and Carbon is better at higher energies.

## Key claim

The Monte Carlo code MONC is extended for proton transport below 20 MeV and used to simulate neutron spectra from the p+$^7$Li reaction, but the agreement with experimental data and other codes is good only for the first neutron group (ground-state transition), while the second group (excited-state transition) shows disagreement and is underestimated by the PINO code.

## Method

MONC simulations were performed for a 4 mg/cm² thick $^7$Li target (92.41% abundance in natural lithium) at proton energies of 6, 10, 15, and 21 MeV, using cross-section data from ENDF VIII.0 and EXFOR. The code incorporates intranuclear cascade, pre-equilibrium, and evaporation models, with low-energy neutron transport using ACE-format cross-sections; the three-body breakup channel $^7$Li(p, n+$^3$He)$\alpha$ was included using data from Scott (1971). Tantalum and natural carbon backing materials were placed behind the lithium target to stop the proton beam, and their neutron contributions were simulated separately.

## Key numbers

- The threshold for the $^7$Li(p, n)$^7$Be$_g$ reaction is approximately 1.88 MeV.
- The second neutron group from $^7$Li(p, n)$^7$Be$^*$ appears above 2.37 MeV proton energy (excited state of $^7$Be at 429 keV).
- The three-body breakup channel $^7$Li(p, n+$^3$He)$\alpha$ has a threshold of 3.7 MeV.
- The lithium target thickness of 4 mg/cm² corresponds to a typical thickness used at experimental facilities in Mumbai, India.
- The ratio of the two neutron groups (ground-state to excited-state) around 0°–10° emission angles is 10–15%, as measured by Phys. Rev. 121.871.
- Neutron production from natural carbon backing is less than that from the lithium target by a factor of two orders of magnitude.
- The thresholds for $^{181}$Ta(p, n), $^{181}$Ta(p, 2n), and $^{181}$Ta(p, 3n) reactions are 0.99 MeV, 7.70 MeV, and 16.16 MeV, respectively.
- The $^{13}$C(p, n) reaction has a threshold of 3.24 MeV and a Coulomb barrier of approximately 1.15 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the MONC code, previously benchmarked for spallation reactions in an IAEA benchmark (Kumawat 2010), to low-energy proton transport for the $^7$Li(p, n)X reaction used in neutron cross-section measurements and Boron Neutron Capture Therapy. The simulated spectra are compared with results from the PINO code (which is valid only below 7.0 MeV and includes only the two-body channels) and with EPEN/SimLiT calculations from the literature. The findings enable experimentalists to properly subtract low-energy neutron contributions and select appropriate backing materials (tantalum below approximately 8 MeV, carbon at higher energies) for quasi-mono-energetic neutron sources.
