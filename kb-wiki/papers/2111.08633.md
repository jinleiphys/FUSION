# Evolution of low-lying M1 modes in germanium isotopes

> Magnetic dipole strength functions are determined for the series of germanium isotopes from $N = Z = 32$ to $N$ = 48 on the basis of a large number of transition strengths calculated within the shell model. The evolution of the strength with increasing neutron number in the $1g_{9/2}$ orbital is analyzed. A bimodal structure comprising an enhancement toward low transition enery and a resonance in the region of the scissors mode is identified. The low-energy enhancement is strongest near closed shells, in particular at the almost completely filled $1g_{9/2}$ orbital, while the scissorslike resonance is most pronounced in the middle of the open shell, which correlates with the magnitude of the also deduced electric quadrupole transition strengths. The results are consistent with previous findings for the shorter series of iron isotopes and proves the occurrence and correlation of the two low-lying magnetic dipole modes as a global structural feature.

# Evolution of low-lying M1 modes in germanium isotopes

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the bimodal structure of low-lying magnetic dipole strength, comprising a low-energy enhancement (LEMAR) near zero transition energy and a scissors resonance (SR) around 3.5 MeV, appears systematically across the germanium isotopic chain from N=32 to N=48, with the LEMAR strongest near shell closures and the SR most pronounced in the middle of the open shell. The authors qualify that this correlation is consistent with previous findings for iron isotopes and proves the two modes as a global structural feature, but note that the integrated M1 strength up to 5 MeV is about 1 μ_N^2 for Ge isotopes, an order of magnitude smaller than the ~10 μ_N^2 found for Fe isotopes, reflecting different valence proton configurations.

## Method
Shell-model calculations were performed in the jj44pn model space with the jj44bpn Hamiltonian using the code NuShellX@MSU, covering the neutron orbitals (1f5/2, 2p3/2, 2p1/2, 1g9/2) for germanium isotopes from 64Ge to 80Ge. The lowest 40 states of each spin from J=0 to 10 and each parity were calculated, yielding more than 24000 M1 transitions per parity, which were sorted into 0.1 MeV bins of transition energy to construct average B(M1) values and M1 strength functions f_M1(E_γ). Effective charges e_π = 1.5 e and e_ν = 0.5 e were used for E2 transitions, and effective g factors g_s^eff = 0.7 g_s^free were applied for M1 transitions.

## Key numbers
- Integrated B(M1) strength in the LEMAR region (E_γ < 2 MeV) ranges from 0.30 μ_N^2 (64Ge) to 0.84 μ_N^2 (80Ge); in the SR region (2 MeV ≤ E_γ < 5 MeV) from 0.28 μ_N^2 (80Ge) to 0.62 μ_N^2 (70Ge); total sum up to 5 MeV ranges from 0.70 μ_N^2 (66Ge) to 1.16 μ_N^2 (70Ge).
- Experimental B(E2, 2^+_1 → 0^+_1) values: 190(36) e^2fm^4 for 66Ge, 356(7) e^2fm^4 for 70Ge, 609(7) e^2fm^4 for 74Ge, 455(79) e^2fm^4 for 78Ge, 279(55) e^2fm^4 for 80Ge.
- Calculated B(E2, 2^+_1 → 0^+_1) values: 296 e^2fm^4 (64Ge), 300 e^2fm^4 (66Ge), 336 e^2fm^4 (70Ge), 369 e^2fm^4 (74Ge), 320 e^2fm^4 (78Ge), 233 e^2fm^4 (80Ge).
- Summed B(M1, 1^+ → 0^+_1) strengths from discrete transitions: experimental 0.04(1) μ_N^2 for 70Ge and 0.30(3) μ_N^2 for 74Ge; calculated values range from 0.001 μ_N^2 (64Ge) to 0.57 μ_N^2 (74Ge).
- Energy ratios E(4^+_1)/E(2^+_1) calculated as 2.44, 2.59, 2.59, 2.38, 2.32, 2.22 for 64,66,70,74,78,80Ge, compared with experimental ratios of 2.28, 2.27, 2.07, 2.46, 2.54, 2.64.
- Integrated B(E2) strength up to 5 MeV ranges from 123 e^2fm^4 (80Ge) to 241 e^2fm^4 (74Ge).

## Context
This work extends the systematic shell-model study of low-lying M1 strength previously performed for iron isotopes by Schwengner et al. (2017) and for molybdenum isotopes by Schwengner et al. (2013), confirming that the correlated appearance of the LEMAR spike and scissors resonance is a global structural feature across different mass regions. The results are consistent with experimental observations of the upbend in gamma-ray strength functions found in iron by Voinov et al. (2004), in molybdenum by Guttormsen et al. (2005), in cadmium by Larsen et al. (2013), and in samarium by Simon et al. (2016, 2019). The study enables improved modeling of photonuclear reactions and radiative-capture processes in stellar nucleosynthesis by providing systematic M1 strength functions for an isotopic chain, and opens the question of how isospin conservation suppresses the LEMAR spike in N=Z nuclei like 64Ge.
