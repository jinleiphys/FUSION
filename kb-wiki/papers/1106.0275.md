# Probing the high momentum component of the deuteron at high Q^2

> The d(e,e'p) cross section at a momentum transfer of 3.5 (GeV/c)^2 was measured over a kinematical range that made it possible to study this reaction for a set of fixed missing momenta as a function of the neutron recoil angle theta_nq and to extract missing momentum distributions for fixed values of theta_nq up to 0.55 GeV/c. In the region of 35 (deg) <= theta_nq <= 45 (deg) recent calculations, which predict that final state interactions are small, agree reasonably well with the experimental data. Therefore these experimental reduced cross sections provide direct access to the high momentum component of the deuteron momentum distribution in exclusive deuteron electro-disintegration.

## Key claim

The measured reduced cross sections for the d(e,e'p) reaction at Q^2 = 3.5 (GeV/c)^2 provide direct access to the high momentum component of the deuteron momentum distribution, but only in the specific kinematic region of neutron recoil angles between 35 degrees and 45 degrees where final state interaction contributions are predicted to be small (10-20%).

## Method

The experiment used the two high-resolution spectrometers in Hall A at Jefferson Lab with a 5.008 GeV/c electron beam incident on a 15 cm liquid deuterium target. The d(e,e'p) cross section was measured at fixed Q^2 = 3.5 (GeV/c)^2 for missing momenta of 0.1, 0.2, 0.4, and 0.5 GeV/c while varying the neutron recoil angle theta_nq, with the energy transfer, electron scattering angle, proton final momentum, and proton direction adjusted accordingly for each theta_nq value. The experimental cross sections were divided by a Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) cross section to produce ratios R(theta_nq), and reduced cross sections were extracted for fixed theta_nq bins of 35, 45, and 75 degrees as a function of missing momentum.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer Q^2 = 3.5 (GeV/c)^2
- Missing momenta studied: 0.1, 0.2, 0.4, and 0.5 GeV/c
- Missing momentum distributions extracted up to 0.55 GeV/c
- Bjorken scaling variable x_B ranged from 0.78 to 1.52 across the kinematic settings
- Beam momentum: 5.008 GeV/c
- Target thickness reduction due to boiling: factor of 0.94 +/- 0.02 at 100 microamps average current
- Coincidence efficiency: 96.4 +/- 2 percent, determined from ^1H(e,e'p) elastic cross section
- Overall systematic error: 4.5 percent
- For missing momentum 0.4 GeV/c, the ratio R = sigma_exp/sigma_pwia showed a maximum of approximately 1.6 at theta_nq around 75 degrees
- For missing momentum 0.5 GeV/c, the ratio R showed a maximum of approximately 2.5 at theta_nq around 75 degrees
- Final state interaction contributions predicted by the MS model: between -18% and -5% for missing momenta 0.2 to 0.5 GeV/c in the 35-45 degree theta_nq range

## Context

This work extends previous d(e,e'p) experiments at Q^2 < 1 (GeV/c)^2 (edsacley, bl98, boe08, Ulm02, Rva05) where final state interactions, meson exchange currents, and isobar configurations dominated and prevented direct access to the high momentum components of the deuteron wave function. The results overlap with a CLAS experiment (Kim3) that found FSI and IC dominating except for missing momenta below 0.1 GeV/c or theta_nq above 110 degrees, but the present data provide finer kinematic binning and fixed theta_nq distributions for the first time. The measurements enable direct tests of theoretical models including those by Sargsian (MS), Laget (JML), and Jeschonnek and Van Orden (JVO), and support the generalized eikonal approximation for describing final state interactions at high Q^2.
