# Effective field theories for van der Waals interactions

> Van der Waals interactions between two neutral but polarizable systems at a separation $R$ much larger than the typical size of the systems are at the core of a broad sweep of contemporary problems in settings ranging from atomic, molecular and condensed matter physics to strong interactions and gravity. We reexamine the dispersive van der Waals interactions between two hydrogen atoms. The novelty of the analysis resides in the usage of nonrelativistic EFTs of QED. In this framework, the van der Waals potential acquires the meaning of a matching coefficient in an EFT suited to describe the low energy dynamics of an atom pair. It may be computed systematically as a series in $R$ times some typical atomic scale and in the fine structure constant $α$. The van der Waals potential gets short range contributions and radiative corrections, which we compute in dimensional regularization and renormalize here for the first time. Results are given in $d$ spacetime dimensions. One can distinguish among different regimes depending on the relative size between $1/R$ and the typical atomic bound state energy $mα^2$. Each regime is characterized by a specific hierarchy of scales and a corresponding tower of EFTs. The short distance regime is characterized by $1/R \gg mα^2$ and the LO van der Waals potential is the London potential. We compute also NNNLO corrections. In the long distance regime we have $1/R\ll mα^2$. In this regime, the van der Waals potential contains contact terms, which are parametrically larger than the Casimir-Polder potential that describes the potential at large distances. In the EFT the Casimir-Polder potential counts as a NNNLO effect. In the intermediate distance regime, $1/R\sim mα^2$, a significantly more complex potential is obtained which we compare with the two previous limiting cases. We conclude commenting on the hadronic van der Waals case.

## Key claim

The paper reexamines dispersive van der Waals interactions between two hydrogen atoms using nonrelativistic effective field theories (EFTs) of QED, showing that the van der Waals potential can be systematically computed as a matching coefficient in a low-energy EFT (WEFT) and renormalized for the first time in dimensional regularization, with results given in \(d\) spacetime dimensions and distinguished across three regimes depending on the relative size of \(1/R\) and the typical atomic binding energy \(m\alpha^2\).

## Method

The authors construct a tower of EFTs: starting from QED, they integrate out the hard scale to obtain NRQED, then the soft scale to obtain pNRQED, and finally integrate out photons at scale \(1/R\) and virtual atomic states to obtain the van der Waals EFT (WEFT). The van der Waals potential is computed by matching pNRQED to pNRQED' (integrating out photons of momentum \(\sim 1/R\)) and then matching pNRQED' to WEFT at one-loop order, using dimensional regularization and renormalization in the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme. The matching is performed separately for the short-distance regime (\(1/R \gg m\alpha^2\)), the long-distance regime (\(1/R \ll m\alpha^2\)), and the intermediate-distance regime (\(1/R \sim m\alpha^2\)).

## Key numbers

* The London potential at leading order in the short-distance regime is \(W_{\rm Lon} = -C_6/R^6\), where \(C_6\) is a positive coefficient depending on the dipole moments and energies of the hydrogen atom.
* The Casimir-Polder potential in the long-distance regime is \(W_{\rm CP} = -C_7/R^7\), where \(C_7\) is a positive coefficient.
* The fine-structure constant is \(\alpha = e^2/(4\pi) \approx 1/137\).
* The typical atomic binding energy is of order \(m\alpha^2\), the inverse Bohr radius is \(1/a_0 \sim m\alpha\), and the electron mass is \(m\).
* In the short-distance regime, the LO van der Waals potential is of order \(m\alpha^3\sqrt{\alpha}\) in coordinate space, NLO is of order \(m\alpha^4\sqrt{\alpha}\), and NNNLO corrections are computed.
* The matching coefficient \(c_F\) for the electron magnetic moment is \(c_F = 1 + \alpha/(2\pi)\) at order \(\alpha\).
* The four-electron matching coefficients in the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme are \(d_s = \alpha^2[\log(m^2/\nu^2) - 2/3]\) and \(d_v = \alpha^2\).
* The tree-level magnetic dipole exchange potential \(\widetilde{V}^{\text{tree}}_{LO,\,B}\) is of order \(\alpha/m^2\) in momentum space (order \(m\alpha^5\sqrt{\alpha}\) in coordinate space).

## Context

This work extends the classic treatments of London (1930) and Casimir and Polder (1948) by providing a systematic EFT framework that allows for renormalization and higher-order corrections, building on prior EFT approaches by Holstein (2008) and the pNRQED formalism of Pineda and Soto (1997, 1998). The results enable rigorous computation of van der Waals interactions in QED and provide a template for analogous studies in QCD, where gluonic van der Waals interactions are relevant for heavy quarkonium-nucleon scattering and the possible formation of molecular bound states, as discussed in the context of the LHCb pentaquark observation (Aaij et al., 2015).
