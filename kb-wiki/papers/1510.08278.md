# Resonance Extraction from the SAID Analysis

> Resonances are extracted from a number of energy-dependent and single-energy fits to scattering data. The influence of recent, precise EPECUR data is investigated. Results for the single-energy fits are derived using the L+P method of analysis and are compared to those obtained using contour integration applied to the global energy-dependent fits.

# Resonance Extraction from the SAID Analysis

## Key claim
Resonance pole positions extracted from single-energy fits using the Laurent+Pietarinen method can reveal states missing from global energy-dependent fits, but the extracted pole positions are sensitive to the choice of energy-bin centers and widths, and the inclusion of precise EPECUR data reduces single-energy fit errors by up to approximately 30 percent.

## Method
Energy-dependent and single-energy fits to πN elastic scattering data from the SAID analysis were re-examined. Single-energy amplitudes were fitted using the Laurent+Pietarinen (L+P) parameterization, which extrapolates the T-matrix into the complex energy plane via a sum of pole terms plus a non-pole background constructed from a conformal mapping. The influence of new, precise EPECUR cross section data was tested by including them in revised energy-dependent fits, which then served as starting points for new single-energy solutions, and the bin centers and widths were randomly shifted to assess systematic uncertainties.

## Key numbers
- Including EPECUR data reduced single-energy fit errors by up to approximately 30 percent, depending on the partial wave.
- For the P11 partial wave, a 2-pole L+P fit with the second pole fixed at (1659 - i262) MeV gave a chi-squared of 1.1 per datum.
- Allowing the second pole to vary in the P11 wave gave a pole at (1725 - i100) MeV, in good agreement with the PDG estimate of (1720 - i115) MeV.
- With randomly shifted energy bins, the second P11 pole (N(1710)) was found at (1653 - i84) MeV, significantly different from the previous determination.
- For the D13 partial wave, the L+P fit found a pole at (1752 - i286) MeV.
- The L+P(WI08) fit gave the N(1710) pole at (1711 ± 10 ± 0.6) MeV real and (-84 ± 20 ± 2) MeV imaginary.
- The L+P(EPECUR) fit gave the N(1710) pole at (1725 ± 18) MeV real and (-200 ± 37) MeV imaginary.
- The L+P(Bin Shift) fit gave the N(1710) pole at (1653 ± 22) MeV real and (-168 ± 41) MeV imaginary.

## Context
This work extends the SAID analysis program by addressing the long-standing difficulty of extracting the N(1710)1/2- and N(1700)3/2- resonances, which are 3-star PDG states that the global energy-dependent SAID fits have failed to reliably determine. The L+P method applied to single-energy fits builds on prior attempts to add resonances via soft amplitude constraints from the Karlsruhe-Helsinki (KH80) and Carnegie-Mellon-Berkeley (CMB) analyses, as well as explicit Chew-Mandelstam K-matrix pole insertion (Workman 2008). The results enable improved pole extraction methodology that accounts for binning uncertainties, and the updated amplitudes can be transferred to the SAID photoproduction analysis, which shares the same Chew-Mandelstam formalism.
