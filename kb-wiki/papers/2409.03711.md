# Relaxation times for disoriented isospin condensates in high energy heavy ion collisions

> Fluctuations between charged and neutral kaons measured by the ALICE Collaboration in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC exceed conventional explanations. Previously it was shown that if the scalar condensate is accompanied by an electrically neutral isospin--1 field then the combination can produce large equilibrium fluctuations where $\langle \bar{u}u\rangle \ne \langle \bar{d}d\rangle$. Hadronizing strange and anti-strange quarks might then strongly fluctuate between charged ($u\bar{s}$ or $s\bar{u}$) and neutral ($d\bar{s}$ or $s\bar{d}$) kaons. Here we estimate the times for the condensates to achieve their equilibrium probability distributions within causal volumes in high energy heavy ion collisions. This is achieved by modeling the temperature dependence of the condensates, mesonic collective excitations, decay rates of the associated fields, and employing the Langevin and Fokker-Planck equations. Within this model, we find that the equilibration times are short compared with the expansion time, making disoriented isospin condensates a viable explanation for the anomalous fluctuations observed at the LHC.

## Key claim

The authors estimate that the equilibration times for disoriented isospin condensates (DICs) are short compared with the expansion time of the heavy ion collision fireball, making DICs a viable explanation for the anomalous kaon fluctuations observed by ALICE, but only within the specific model assumptions of temperature-dependent condensates, mesonic collective excitations, and the strong damping limit of the Langevin equation.

## Method

The authors model the isospin fluctuation amplitude φ as a spatially uniform field and evolve it using the Langevin and Fokker-Planck equations, with dissipative terms added to the equations of motion for the scalar meson fields σ_u and σ_d. They parameterize the temperature dependence of the light and strange quark condensates by fitting lattice QCD results to a mean-field Ising model form, and model the temperature-dependent masses and decay rates of the σ meson (f₀(500)) and the ζ field (f₀(980)) as collective excitations of the medium. The relaxation time for the condensate to reach equilibrium is computed from the resulting Fokker-Planck equation in the strong damping limit, and the analysis is extended to include the second-order time derivative to check whether the system is underdamped.

## Key numbers

* The zero-temperature values of the condensates are f_π = 92.1 MeV and f_K = 110.1 MeV, giving σ̄(0) = f_π and ζ̄(0) = (2f_K − f_π)/√2.
* Best-fit parameters for the light quark condensate: T_cl = 166.762 MeV, h_l = 0.921635 MeV; for the strange quark condensate: T_cs = 204.316 MeV, h_s = 2.6074 MeV.
* The σ meson vacuum mass is taken as either 450 MeV or 500 MeV, with T_π = 160 MeV; the corresponding λ values are 10.814 and 13.614, giving vacuum decay widths Γ_{σππ} of 207.82 MeV and 312.950 MeV respectively.
* The Yukawa coupling g is estimated as 3.2, chosen so that M_l ≈ 300 MeV and M_s ≈ 500 MeV at T = 0 and so that T_q is close to but less than T_π.
* The long-wavelength relaxation time is τ₀ = σ̄² Γ_σ / (2a(T)), and the system is underdamped when τ₀ < 4/Γ_σ, which holds for all temperatures under consideration.

## Context

This work extends the DIC proposal of Kapusta (earlier work) to explain the anomalously large ν_dyn[K_s⁰, K^±] correlations measured by the ALICE Collaboration in Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, which could not be explained by conventional mechanisms like resonance decays, charge conservation, or Bose symmetrization (Kapusta, 2022). The authors address the causal volume limitation described by Castorina and Satz as the counterpart of the cosmological horizon problem, showing that DICs can form on timescales short enough to be relevant. If confirmed, this would provide experimental evidence for approximate chiral symmetry restoration in QCD at high temperatures.
