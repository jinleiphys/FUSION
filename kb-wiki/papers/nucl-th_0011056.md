# Microscopic structure of fundamental excitations in N=Z nuclei

> Excitation energies of the $T$=1 states in even-even as well as $T$=0 and $T$=1 states in odd-odd $N$=$Z$ nuclei are calculated within the mean-field approach. It is shown that the underlying structure of these states can be determined in a consistent manner only when both isoscalar and isovector pairing collectivity as well as isospin projection, treated within the iso-cranking approximation, are taken into account. In particular, in odd-odd $N$=$Z$ nuclei, the interplay between quasiparticle excitations (relevant for the case of $T$=0 states) and iso-rotations (relevant for the case of $T$=1 states) explains the near-degeneracy of these fundamental excitations.

## Key claim

The near-degeneracy of T=0 and T=1 states in odd-odd N=Z nuclei can be explained microscopically only when both isoscalar and isovector pairing collectivity and isospin projection (via iso-cranking) are included; the authors argue that previous claims that this degeneracy proves a lack of T=0 pairing correlations were based on a poor understanding of the underlying excitation structure.

## Method

The authors use a deformed Woods-Saxon single-particle potential with seniority-type isovector and isoscalar pairing interactions, solved via the Lipkin-Nogami method with a general Bogolyubov transformation. For T=1 states in even-even nuclei, they block the two lowest quasiparticle states and apply iso-cranking to reach T_x=1, while for odd-odd nuclei they treat T=0 states as self-consistent 2qp excitations and T=1 states as iso-cranked configurations of the odd-odd "false vacuum" with T_x=√2.

## Key numbers

- For the single-particle model, level crossings occur at frequencies ℏω_c^(n) = (2n-1)δe for even-T bands and ℏω_c^(n) = 2nδe for odd-T bands, where n=1,2,3...
- The odd-T iso-rotational band excitation energy follows ΔE = (1/2)δe + (1/2)δe T_x^2
- For ⁴⁴Ti, the nucleus becomes trapped at ℏω ≈ 1.5 MeV and remains trapped until ℏω ≈ 5.8 MeV
- The spatial cranking frequency used to remove degeneracies in the quasiparticle spectrum is ℏω_s ∼ 0.01 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous mean-field treatment of T=2 states in even-even N=Z nuclei (Satula and Wyss, 2000) to T=1 states in even-even nuclei and both T=0 and T=1 states in odd-odd nuclei. It contradicts the conclusions of Macchiavelli et al. (2000), who claimed the near-degeneracy of T=0 and T=1 states in odd-odd nuclei indicated a lack of T=0 pairing correlations. The model simultaneously accounts for the Wigner energy, T=1 and T=2 excitation energies in even-even nuclei, and the near-degeneracy and inversion of T=0 and T=1 states in odd-odd nuclei, enabling consistent microscopic studies of isobaric excitations along the N=Z line.
