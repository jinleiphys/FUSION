# Dynamical Correlation Length near the Chiral Critical Point

> The dynamical evolution of small systems undergoing a chiral symmetry breaking transition in the course of rapid expansion is discussed. The time evolution of the dynamical correlation length for trajectories passing through a second-order critical point is extracted. It is shown that while the maximum value of the correlation length is bound from above by dynamical effects, the time interval during which it is near its maximum grows steadily with the system size and with decreasing expansion rate.

# Dynamical Correlation Length near the Chiral Critical Point

## Key claim
The dynamical correlation length in finite, rapidly expanding systems passing through a second-order chiral critical point is always finite and bounded from above by dynamical effects, even though the equilibrium correlation length would diverge at the critical point. The maximum value of the correlation length grows only very slowly with system size, and the time interval during which it remains near its maximum grows steadily with increasing system size and decreasing expansion rate.

## Method
The author uses Chiral Hydrodynamics, coupling a perfect relativistic fluid of quarks to classical chiral fields described by the SU(2)×SU(2) linear sigma model. The system is initialized as a spherical fireball with a linear velocity profile and Gaussian random field fluctuations correlated over an initial length of 1.2 fm. The dynamical correlation length is extracted by fitting a polynomial to the logarithm of the field probability distribution (the effective action) obtained from histograms of the field values within a 1 fm sphere at the center of the system.

## Key numbers
- Initial temperature: T_i ≈ 160 MeV, well above the transition temperature T_c ≈ 138 MeV
- Pion decay constant: f_π = 93 MeV, used as the vacuum expectation value of the sigma field
- Critical coupling: g_c ≈ 3.7, where the first-order phase transition line ends in a second-order critical point
- Initial correlation length: ξ_0 ≈ 1.2 fm, used for the Gaussian random field fluctuations
- Surface thickness parameter: a = 0.3 fm for the Woods-Saxon initial field profile
- For a system of initial radius R = 1 fm, the correlation length remains approximately constant at ξ(t) ≈ ξ_0 throughout the expansion
- For larger systems (R ≥ 3 fm), the maximum correlation length reaches roughly twice ξ_0 (about 2.4 fm)

## Context
This work extends the finite-time scaling analysis of Berdnikov (1999), who studied infinitely large systems and estimated that the correlation length cannot exceed 2–3 times the initial correlation length. The paper provides real-time dynamical simulations of finite expanding systems relevant to heavy-ion collisions, where the critical point in the temperature vs. baryon-chemical potential plane was located on the lattice by Fodor (2001). The results imply that experimental observables will not exhibit fully developed critical behavior even for trajectories passing directly through the critical point, but the growing time interval near maximum correlation length for larger systems may be relevant for observables that integrate over the entire collision history.
