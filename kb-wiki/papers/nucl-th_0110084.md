# The Solar $hep$ Processes in Effective Field Theory

> By combining effective field theory with the standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA) we obtain a high-precision estimate of the $S$ factor for the solar $hep$ process. The accurate wave functions available in SNPA are used to evaluate the nuclear matrix elements for the transition operators that result from chiral perturbation theory (ChPT). All the contributions up to \nlo3 in ChPT are included. The resulting parameter-free, error-controlled prediction is: $S(hep)=(8.6 \pm 1.3)\times 10^{-20}$ keV-b.

# The Solar $hep$ Processes in Effective Field Theory

## Key claim
By combining effective field theory with the standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA), the authors obtain a parameter-free, error-controlled prediction of the $S$ factor for the solar $hep$ process, but the result carries a residual uncertainty from the cutoff dependence that spans the range $\Lambda = 500$–800 MeV.

## Method
The authors use heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) to derive the transition operators for the $hep$ process, including one-body and two-body currents. They evaluate the nuclear matrix elements using correlated-hyperspherical-harmonics (CHH) wave functions from the Argonne $v_{18}$ potential (plus Urbana-IX three-nucleon force for $A \ge 3$ nuclei). The single unknown low-energy constant $\hat d^R$ in the contact-term part of the two-body current is fixed by fitting the experimental tritium beta-decay rate, making the prediction parameter-free.

## Key numbers
- $S(hep) = (8.6 \pm 1.3) \times 10^{-20}$ keV-b, where the error spans the cutoff dependence for $\Lambda = 500$–800 MeV.
- The two-body contribution ratio $\delta_{\rm 2B}^{\rm OPE}$ (OPE only) is $(-1.1,\ -1.5,\ -2.0)$ for $\Lambda = 500$, 600, 800 MeV; after including the contact term, $\delta_{\rm 2B}^{\rm N3LO} = (-0.60,\ -0.64,\ -0.73)$ for the same cutoffs.
- The fitted constant $\hat d^R = (1.00 \pm 0.07,\ 1.78 \pm 0.08,\ 3.90 \pm 0.10)$ for $\Lambda = 500$, 600, 800 MeV.
- The previous SNPA estimate from Marcucci et al. (2000) gave $S = 9.64 \times 10^{-20}$ keV-b.
- The maximum neutrino energy from the $hep$ process is $E_\nu^{\rm max}(hep) \simeq 20$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA) of Marcucci et al. (2000) by incorporating chiral perturbation theory operators up to N3LO, yielding a more controlled theoretical uncertainty. The result supports the earlier SNPA estimate while providing a systematic error budget. The same EFT framework can be applied to the $hen$ process ($^3$He + $n \rightarrow ^4$He + $\gamma$), for which accurate experimental cross sections exist but no quantitative theoretical explanation has been achieved, offering a future test of the formalism.
