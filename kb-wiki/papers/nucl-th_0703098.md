# Overview of Antikaon-Nuclear Theory and Phenomenology

> Experimental evidence for antikaon-nuclear quasibound states is briefly reviewed. Theoretical and phenomenological arguments for and against deep antikaon-nucleus potentials which might allow for narrow quasibound states are reviewed, with recent calculations suggesting widths larger than 100 MeV for binding energy smaller than 100 MeV. Results of RMF calculations that provide a lower limit of 50+/-10 MeV for the width of deeply bound states are discussed.

# Overview of Antikaon-Nuclear Theory and Phenomenology

## Key claim

Deep antikaon-nucleus potentials (150–200 MeV depth at nuclear-matter density) are supported by fits to K⁻-atom data, but self-consistent chiral-model calculations yield shallower potentials (40–60 MeV); the resulting quasibound nuclear states likely have widths larger than 100 MeV for binding energies below 100 MeV, with a lower limit of 50 ± 10 MeV for deeply bound states.

## Method

The paper reviews global fits of density-dependent optical potentials to 65 K⁻-atom data points (level shifts, widths, yields) across nuclei from ⁷Li to ²³⁸U, and compares these to chirally motivated in-medium coupled-channel calculations. Relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations are performed self-consistently for K⁻ 1s nuclear states across the periodic table, incorporating scalar and vector couplings, an imaginary absorptive term fitted to atomic data, and an energy-dependent phase-space suppression factor for mesonic and nonmesonic decay channels.

## Key numbers

- Global fits to K⁻-atom data yield deep density-dependent potentials with nuclear-matter depth Re V_K(ρ₀) ∼ −(150–200) MeV
- Self-consistent chiral-model calculations give shallow potentials with Re V_K(ρ₀) ∼ −(40–60) MeV
- Recent calculations suggest Γ_K ≥ 100 MeV for B_K ≤ 100 MeV
- RMF calculations provide a lower limit Γ_K ≥ 50 ± 10 MeV for binding energies B_K ∼ 100–200 MeV
- For K⁻ 1s state in ¹²C, static calculation gave B = 132 MeV while dynamical calculation gave B = 172 MeV
- K⁻-atom deeply bound atomic states have calculated width Γ_atom ≤ 2 MeV
- K⁻pp quasibound state: Faddeev calculations give B ∼ 55–75 MeV, Γ ∼ 100 MeV for mesonic width, with an additional estimated 20 MeV from nonmesonic channels
- FINUDA collaboration reported K⁻pp state with B ∼ 115 MeV; OBELIX collaboration reported B ∼ 160 MeV, Γ ∼ 30 MeV
- Preliminary FINUDA evidence for K⁻NNN I=0 state with B = 67 ± 5 MeV, Γ = 37 ± 10 MeV

## Context

This review extends the debate between deep potentials from K⁻-atom fits (Friedman, Gal, Mareš, 1999–2007) and shallow potentials from self-consistent chiral models (Schaffner-Bielich, Koch, Weise, 2000; Ramos, Oset, 2000; Cieplý, Friedman, Gal, 2001). The work was triggered by Kishimoto (1999) and Akaishi and Yamazaki (1999, 2002) who suggested searching for deeply bound K⁻ nuclear states, and by Nogami (1963) who first predicted K⁻pp binding. The RMF dynamical calculations (Mareš, Friedman, Gal, 2006) show that nuclear core polarization increases density but not enough to produce the narrow widths claimed by some experiments; the results contradict claims of very narrow states (Γ ∼ 20–30 MeV) and set a conservative lower width limit that challenges experimental identification. Ongoing experiments at J-PARC and FINUDA aim to resolve the controversy through exclusive formation reactions such as K⁻ + ³He → n + {K⁻[NN]_{I=1}} (Nagae, 2006).
