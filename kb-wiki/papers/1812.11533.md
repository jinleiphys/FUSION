# Jet fragmentation in a QCD medium: Universal quark/gluon ration and Wave turbulence

> We calculate the evolution of a jet shower due to medium induced splittings in the deep LPM regime. Due to the characteristic energy dependence of the formation time $t_{\rm form}(ω)= \sqrt{ω/\hat{\bar{q}}}$, the radiative break-up process exhibits turbulent characteristics, allowing for analytic predictions of various inclusive properties of the medium induced cascade in an inertial range of energies $T \ll ω\ll E$, where $E$ is the energy of the jet and $T$ is the temperature of the medium.

## Key claim
The medium-induced jet fragmentation cascade in the deep LPM regime exhibits turbulent (Kolmogorov-Zakharov) characteristics, leading to a universal quark/gluon ratio of approximately 0.07 for small-x fragments that is independent of the nature (quark or gluon) of the leading jet parton and of the evolution time inside the medium.

## Method
The authors derive and solve coupled kinetic equations for the in-medium fragmentation functions of gluons and flavor-singlet quarks, treating successive medium-induced splittings as quasi-instantaneous and independent. The splitting rates incorporate the characteristic energy dependence of the formation time \(t_{\rm form}(\omega)=\sqrt{\omega/\hat{\bar{q}}}\) from the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect. Numerical solutions are obtained for a gluon jet initial condition and analyzed for the stationary turbulent solutions of the kinetic equations.

## Key numbers
- Universal quark/gluon ratio for small-x fragments: \(Q/G \approx 0.07\), derived from the balance of \(\glu\to\qua \bar{\qua}\) and \(\qua\to\glu\qua\) processes.
- The turbulent cascade operates in an inertial range of energies \(T \ll \omega \ll E\), where \(E\) is the jet energy and \(T\) is the medium temperature.
- For short evolution times (\(\tau \ll 1\)), the energy loss rate exhibits Casimir scaling: \(\left.dE/dt \right.|_{\rm q-jet} = C_F/C_A ~\left.dE/dt \right.|_{\rm g-jet}\).
- The energy loss rate for short times is given by \(\frac{dE}{dt}=\frac{\alpha^2}{\pi^2}\hat{\bar{q}}~\gamma_{\glu} C_{A}^{1/2} C_{R}~t\).
- Deviations from single-splitting behavior become clear for momentum fractions \(x \lesssim 0.1\) already after very short evolution times \(\tau = 0.002 - 0.12\) inside the medium.

## Context
This work extends the turbulent cascade approach to jet fragmentation in QCD media previously studied by Baier (2000) and Blaizot (2013), providing analytic predictions for inclusive properties of the medium-induced cascade. The universal quark/gluon ratio and the turbulent energy flux enable predictions for jet substructure observables, including modified strangeness and heavy-flavor production in heavy-ion jets. The results can be tested against Monte-Carlo event generators such as MARTINI or JEWEL, which are based on the same underlying microscopic processes.
