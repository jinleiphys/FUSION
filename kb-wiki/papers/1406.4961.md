# Massive Neutron Stars with Antikaon Condensates in a Density Dependent Hadron Field Theory

> The measurement of $1.97 \pm 0.04 M_{solar}$ for PSR J1614-2230 and $2.01 \pm 0.04M_{solar}$ for PSR J0348+0432 puts a strong constraint on the neutron star equation of state and its exotic composition at higher densities. In this paper, we investigate the possibility of exotic equation of state within the observational mass constraint of $2M_{solar}$ in the framework of relativistic mean field model with density-dependent couplings. We particularly study the effect of antikaon condensates in the presence of hyperons on the mass-radius relationship of the neutron star.

# Massive Neutron Stars with Antikaon Condensates in a Density Dependent Hadron Field Theory

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that within the density-dependent DD2 relativistic mean field model, neutron stars containing both hyperons and antikaon condensates can still satisfy the observational mass constraint of approximately 2 solar masses, but only for sufficiently deep antikaon optical potentials (|U_K| >= 120 MeV) and only when the specific combination of exotic particles is considered; for shallower potentials, the maximum mass remains above 2 solar masses but the exotic phases do not appear at relevant central densities.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic mean field model with density-dependent meson-baryon couplings (the DD2 parametrization of Typel et al.) to compute the equation of state of neutron star matter containing the full baryon octet (n, p, Lambda, Sigma, Xi) and antikaon condensates (K-, anti-K0). They solve the mean-field meson equations self-consistently with chemical equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions, using SU(6) symmetry relations for hyperon-meson couplings and fitting the scalar kaon coupling to the antikaon optical potential at saturation density for values ranging from -60 to -140 MeV. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are then solved to obtain mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of nucleon-only star: 2.417 solar masses, with central density 5.71 n0 and radius 11.87 km
- Maximum mass with Lambda hyperons: 2.10 solar masses, central density 6.40 n0, radius 11.57 km
- Maximum mass with Lambda and Xi hyperons: 2.032 solar masses, central density 6.66 n0, radius 11.42 km
- For nucleons plus antikaons with U_K = -140 MeV: maximum mass 2.164 solar masses, central density 5.91 n0, radius 12.01 km
- For nucleons, Lambda hyperons, and antikaons with U_K = -140 MeV: maximum mass 2.02 solar masses, central density 6.63 n0, radius 11.48 km
- For nucleons, Lambda, Xi, and antikaons with U_K = -140 MeV: maximum mass 2.016 solar masses, central density 6.67 n0, radius 11.4 km
- Lambda hyperon threshold density: 2.22 n0
- Xi- hyperon threshold density: 2.44 n0
- K- condensation threshold in nucleon-only matter: 4.11 n0 for U_K = -60 MeV, decreasing to 2.79 n0 for U_K = -140 MeV
- K- condensation threshold in matter with Lambda hyperons: 6.54 n0 for U_K = -60 MeV, decreasing to 3.07 n0 for U_K = -140 MeV
- Saturation density of the DD2 model: 0.149065 fm^-3, binding energy -16.02 MeV, incompressibility 242.7 MeV
- Symmetry energy parameters for DD2: S_v = 31.67 MeV, L = 55.04 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of antikaon condensation in neutron stars (Kaplan and Nelson, 1986; Pal et al.; Glendenning and Schaffner-Bielich) by using the density-dependent DD2 model of Typel et al. (2005, 2009), which satisfies experimental constraints on symmetry energy (29-32.7 MeV) and its slope parameter (40.5-61.9 MeV) that older parametrizations like GM1, TM1, and NL3 do not. The results show that the 2 solar mass constraint from PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest et al., 2010) and PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis et al., 2013) does not rule out antikaon condensates combined with hyperons, contrary to earlier expectations that such soft exotic equations of state would be excluded. This enables future work to explore protoneutron star evolution and cooling with strangeness-bearing matter within observationally consistent models.
