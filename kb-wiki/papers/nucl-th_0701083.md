# Terminating states as a unique laboratory for testing nuclear energy density functional

> Systematic calculations of favored signature maximum-spin I_max and unfavored signature I_max - 1 terminating states for [f 7/2 ^ n] and [d 3/2 ^ (-1) f 7/2 ^ (n+1)] configurations (n denotes number of valence particles) in A ~ 44 mass region are presented. Following the result of Zdunczuk et al., Phys. Rev. C71 (2005) 024305 the calculations are performed using Skyrme energy density functional with empirical Landau parameters and slightly reduced spin-orbit strength. The aim is to identify and phenomenologically restore rotational symmetry broken by the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock solutions. In particular, it is shown that correlation energy due to symmetry restoration is absolutely crucial in order to reproduce energy splitting E(I_max) - E(I_max -1) in [f 7/2 ^ n] configurations but is relatively less important for [d 3/2 ^ (-1) f 7/2 ^ (n+1)] configurations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that terminating states in the A~44 mass region provide a stringent test for Skyrme energy density functionals, but the abstract's claim that correlation energy from symmetry restoration is "absolutely crucial" for reproducing energy splittings applies specifically to the [f7/2^n] configurations, while for [d3/2^-1 f7/2^(n+1)] configurations the mean-field itself dominates and symmetry restoration is "relatively less important."

## Method

Systematic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations were performed for favored signature maximum-spin I_max and unfavored signature I_max-1 terminating states in [f7/2^n] and [d3/2^-1 f7/2^(n+1)] configurations across A~44 nuclei, using Skyrme energy density functionals with empirical Landau parameters and slightly reduced spin-orbit strength following Zdunczuk et al. (2005). For the unfavored signature states, which break rotational symmetry at nearly spherical shape, the authors developed analytical 2x2 and 3x3 configuration mixing schemes (methods A and B) to restore rotational symmetry by projecting out the spurious |I_max, I_max-1> component from the mean-field solutions.

## Key numbers

- Mean deviation between calculated and experimental values for the energy difference ΔE = E[d3/2^-1 f7/2^(n+1)]_Imax - E[f7/2^n]_Imax was approximately 500 keV (about 10%) after constraining time-odd spin-field coupling constants to empirical Landau parameters
- The effective-mass-scaled isoscalar spin-orbit strength W0* for forces giving similar ΔE values (SLy4, SLy5, SIII, SkO, SkXc) was approximately 135 ± 10 MeV·fm^5
- For the [d3/2^-1 f7/2^(n+1)] configuration with the SLy4 force, symmetry restoration left a constant offset of about 455 keV (method A) or 385 keV (method B) between theoretical results and experimental data
- For the SkO force in the [d3/2^-1 f7/2^(n+1)] configuration, the spurious state energy λ1 did not exceed ±0.1 MeV except for 45Ti

## Context

This work extends the comparative study by Zdunczuk et al. (2005) which showed that SHF calculations with modified spin-fields and spin-orbit strength could reproduce shell-model results for terminating states in A~44 nuclei. The paper demonstrates that the same correlation between effective-mass-scaled spin-orbit strength and energy differences holds for analogous terminating configurations in A~16 and A~80 mass regions, enabling systematic testing of Skyrme parameterizations across multiple mass regions. The analytical symmetry restoration schemes developed here for quasi-spherical terminating states provide a method to correct for spontaneous rotational symmetry breaking in SHF solutions, which is distinct from the constructive symmetry breaking that produces rotational bands in deformed nuclei.
