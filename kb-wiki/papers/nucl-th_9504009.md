# Relativistic Two-Body Processes in Axial-Charge Transitions

> We study the contribution of two-body meson-exchange processes to axial charge transitions for nuclei in the lead, tin and oxygen regions. We conduct calculations in the Dirac-Hartree (the Walecka model) and the relativistic Hartree (where the full one-nucleon-loop effects are included) approximations. We present results indicating that one- and two-body processes enhance the matrix elements of the axial-charge operator by some (100+-20)% in all three regions studied. This agrees well with the fit of eighteen first-forbidden beta-decay transitions conducted by Warburton in the lead region. We also discuss some sensitivities present in the calculation.

# Relativistic Two-Body Processes in Axial-Charge Transitions

## Key claim

The authors claim that one- and two-body processes together enhance the matrix elements of the axial-charge operator by approximately (100±20)% in the lead, tin, and oxygen regions, but this result depends on using pseudovector πN coupling rather than pseudoscalar coupling (which gives anomalously large predictions), and the ratio of one-body to two-body contributions shifts from the former to the latter as nuclear mass increases.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic calculations within the quantum hadrodynamical model II (QHD-II), using both the Dirac-Hartree (Walecka model) and relativistic Hartree (including one-nucleon-loop vacuum corrections) approximations. They derived the two-body axial-vector current from π-ρ exchange diagrams, enforced partial conservation of the axial current (PCAC) to fix the π-ρ-ε vertex, and computed multipole-decomposed matrix elements for first-forbidden beta-decay transitions in nuclei across three mass regions (oxygen, tin, and lead).

## Key numbers

- The total enhancement δ_A (the fractional increase of the effective axial coupling constant over the free value) is approximately 1.2 for the ν:2g_{9/2} → π:1h_{9/2} transition in 209Pb → 209Bi, with 40% from one-body and 80% from two-body processes.
- For the π:(3s_{1/2})⁻¹ → ν:(3p_{1/2})⁻¹ transition in 207Tl → 207Pb, the one-body enhancement is 30% and the two-body enhancement is 55%.
- For the 133Sn → 133Sb decay (ν:2f_{7/2} → π:1g_{7/2}), the one-body enhancement is 42% and the two-body enhancement is 33%.
- For the 16N(0⁻) → 16O(0⁺) transition, the largest configuration gives 48% one-body and 42% two-body enhancement; the smallest configuration gives 18% one-body and 10% two-body enhancement.
- The "best fit" value reported by Warburton for nuclei in the A=206-212 mass region was δ_A = 1.05 ± 0.05.
- The pion decay constant used is f_π = 0.93 GeV, and the axial mass cutoff is M_A = 1.2 GeV.
- Meson masses used: M = 939 MeV (nucleon), m_ω = 783 MeV, m_ρ = 770 MeV, m_π = 139 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic calculations (Izquierdo, 1994; Barenboim, 1994) on axial transitions in heavy nuclei to include tin and oxygen regions, studying mass dependence and the influence of the scalar field. It addresses the long-standing puzzle of enhanced axial-charge transitions in first-forbidden beta decay, where Warburton found experimental enhancements exceeding non-relativistic theoretical estimates by a factor of two, and where earlier work by Delorme and Towner (1987) had concluded that relativistic mean-field theory was inconsistent with the data. The results support the current consensus that scalar heavy-meson exchanges can account for the discrepancies, and they enable systematic comparisons between relativistic and non-relativistic approaches to meson-exchange currents in nuclear beta decay.
