# The impact of isospin dependence of pairing on fission barriers in the fission cycling regions

> A systematic analysis of the ground state and fission properties of actinides and superheavy nuclei important for the $r$ process modeling has been performed within the framework of covariant density functional theory for the first time in Ref. [1]. A brief review of the results related to the heights of primary fission barriers and systematic uncertainties in their prediction is presented. In addition, new results on the potential impact of the isospin dependence of pairing on fission barriers in fission cycling regions is provided for the first time.

# The impact of isospin dependence of pairing on fission barriers in the fission cycling regions

## Key claim
The inclusion of isospin-dependent pairing interactions increases the heights of primary fission barriers in very neutron-rich nuclei located in fission cycling regions, but the magnitude of the increase depends on both the chosen covariant energy density functional and the specific nucleus, ranging from 0.25 MeV to 1.57 MeV for the cases studied.

## Method
The authors performed axial relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations using four covariant energy density functionals (DD-PC1, DD-ME2, NL3*, and PC-PK1) to compute primary fission barrier heights for actinides and superheavy nuclei. They then compared three versions of separable pairing interaction: a constant-strength version (v1) and two isospin-dependent versions (v2 and v3), applying them to the very neutron-rich nuclei 316Fm and 324Rf to quantify the impact on fission barrier heights.

## Key numbers
- Fission barriers obtained with DD-PC1 are on average higher by approximately 2 MeV compared with those calculated with NL3*
- For 316Fm with NL3*: E^B_v1 = 5.71 MeV, E^B_v2 = 6.49 MeV, E^B_v3 = 6.38 MeV; increases of 0.78 MeV (v2) and 0.756 MeV (v3)
- For 316Fm with DD-PC1: E^B_v1 = 8.83 MeV, E^B_v2 = 9.11 MeV, E^B_v3 = 9.45 MeV; increases of 0.28 MeV (v2) and 0.62 MeV (v3)
- For 316Fm with DD-ME2: E^B_v1 = 9.77 MeV, E^B_v2 = 10.36 MeV, E^B_v3 = 10.53 MeV; increases of 0.59 MeV (v2) and 0.76 MeV (v3)
- For 324Rf with NL3*: E^B_v1 = 6.39 MeV, E^B_v2 = 7.06 MeV, E^B_v3 = 7.23 MeV; increases of 0.67 MeV (v2) and 0.84 MeV (v3)
- For 324Rf with DD-PC1: E^B_v1 = 9.08 MeV, E^B_v2 = 10.44 MeV, E^B_v3 = 10.58 MeV; increases of 1.36 MeV (v2) and 1.50 MeV (v3)
- For 324Rf with DD-ME2: E^B_v1 = 10.44 MeV, E^B_v2 = 10.69 MeV, E^B_v3 = 12.01 MeV; increases of 0.25 MeV (v2) and 1.57 MeV (v3)
- A band of nuclei around N approximately 240 has extremely low fission barriers with heights of around 2 MeV in the NL3* functional

## Context
This work extends the first systematic analysis of ground state and fission properties of actinides and superheavy nuclei within covariant density functional theory performed by Afanasjev and Taninah (2020). It addresses the need for improved nuclear input for r-process modeling, where fission cycling determines the termination of the hot r-process and the final element abundance pattern. The results show that isospin dependence of pairing, previously established in systematic analyses by Afanasjev and Taninah (2021) and supported by non-relativistic Skyrme DFT investigations (Bertsch et al. 2012; Yamagami et al. 2012), is an additional factor affecting fission barriers that must be considered in future r-process simulations.
