# Separable approximation to two-body matrix elements

> Two-body matrix elements of arbitrary local interactions are written as the sum of separable terms in a way that is well suited for the exchange and pairing channels present in mean-field calculations. The expansion relies on the transformation to center of mass and relative coordinate (in the spirit of Talmi's method) and therefore it is only useful (finite number of expansion terms) for harmonic oscillator single particle states. The converge of the expansion with the number of terms retained is studied for a Gaussian two body interaction. The limit of a contact (delta) force is also considered. Ways to handle the general case are also discussed.

# Separable approximation to two-body matrix elements

## Key claim

The paper shows that two-body matrix elements of arbitrary local interactions can be written as an exact sum of separable terms using the Talmi-Brody-Moshinsky transformation to center-of-mass and relative coordinates, but this expansion is only useful (yields a finite number of terms) for harmonic oscillator single-particle states. The approximation becomes accurate with few terms only for short-range interactions, as the expansion is essentially a short-range expansion in the interaction range relative to the oscillator length.

## Method

The author transforms products of harmonic oscillator wave functions into center-of-mass and relative coordinate wave functions using Talmi-Brody-Moshinsky coefficients, then expresses the resulting interaction matrix in relative coordinates via its spectral decomposition. The separable expansion is obtained by truncating the sum over eigenvalues of the relative-coordinate interaction matrix, keeping only the largest eigenvalues. The method is tested on one-dimensional Gaussian interactions of varying range and on the zero-range delta interaction limit.

## Key numbers

- For a Gaussian interaction with range μ = 0.7 fm and oscillator length b = 2.0 fm, the approximation with N_C = 10 and L_C = 1 (eleven separable terms) already gives reasonable accuracy for the most relevant matrix elements (those with quantum numbers n_1, n_2, n_3, n_4 smaller than 7).
- For a Gaussian with twice the range (μ = 1.4 fm), the approximation worsens; convergence with N_C is much faster than with L_C, and L_C = 3 gives much better results than L_C = 1.
- The matrix element ⟨n,0|exp(-(x_1-x_2)^2/μ^2)|n,0⟩ for μ = 1.4 fm and b = 2.0 fm is a decreasing function of n, quenching promotion to high-n states and preventing ultraviolet divergence; the N_C = 10, L_C = 3 approximation is reasonable up to n = 15.
- For the zero-range delta interaction, the rank-one approximation is exact, and the number of terms in the N sum is reduced to M+1 (where M+1 is the number of basis states) due to parity selection rules.

## Context

This work extends the separable interaction approach of Duguet (2004, 2008) and Lesinski (2009), who introduced separable interactions in momentum representation fitted to nucleon-nucleon phase shifts or pairing gaps, and of Tian (2009a, 2009b, 2009c), who proposed a separable form for Gaussian two-body forces. The present method provides a general framework for any local interaction using the Talmi-Brody-Moshinsky transformation (Talmi 1959, Moshinsky 1959, Brody 1965), and can be extended to non-local interactions of the form given in the paper. The approach enables finite-range pairing calculations without the need for an arbitrary "active window" cutoff required by zero-range interactions, and can be applied to Yukawa and Coulomb potentials via their Gauss transform representation.
