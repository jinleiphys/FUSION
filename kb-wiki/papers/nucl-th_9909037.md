# Isoscalar Giant Quadrupole Resonance State in the Relativistic Approach with the Momentum-Dependent Self-Energies

> We study the excited energy of the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance with the scaling method in the relativistic many-body framework. In this calculation we introduce the momentum-dependent parts of the Dirac self-energies arising from the one-pion exchange on the assumption of the pseudo-vector coupling with nucleon field. It is shown that this momentum-dependence enhances the Landau mass significantly and thus suppresses the quadrupole resonance energy even giving the small Dirac effective mass which causes a problem in the momentum-independent mean-field theory.

## Key claim

The excitation energy of the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (ISGQR) is determined by the Landau mass, not the Dirac effective mass; introducing the momentum-dependent self-energies from one-pion exchange (with pseudo-vector coupling) enhances the Landau mass enough to bring the predicted resonance energy into agreement with experiment, even while the Dirac effective mass remains small.

## Method

The authors use the scaling method in infinite nuclear matter to derive the restoring force and mass parameter for the ISGQR, obtaining an expression for the resonance frequency identical to the non-relativistic macroscopic formula. They construct a model combining the relativistic Hartree (RH) approximation for sigma- and omega-meson exchanges (the local part) with the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) contribution from one-pion exchange with pseudo-vector coupling (the momentum-dependent part), subtracting the infinite-momentum limit to regularize the Fock terms. The parameters for the sigma- and omega-meson exchanges are fitted to reproduce saturation properties (binding energy 16 MeV, incompressibility 200 MeV, effective mass M*/M = 0.7 at saturation density rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}), and the Landau mass is computed from the single-particle energy derivative at the Fermi surface.

## Key numbers

- Landau mass from the momentum-dependent model (parameter set PF1) at saturation density: M*_L / M = 0.85, consistent with the value extracted from the experimental ISGQR energy (omega_Q ≈ 63 A^{-1/3} MeV, <T_K^{nr}> ≈ 25 MeV, r_0 ≈ 1.125 fm).
- Landau mass from the momentum-independent RH calculation (parameter set PM1) at saturation density: M*_L / M = 0.74.
- Dirac effective mass in both parameter sets: M*/M = 0.7 at saturation density rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}.
- Variation of the momentum-dependent self-energies below the Fermi level: at most 2.5 percent.
- The Landau mass in PF1 agrees with the bare mass at rho_B ≈ 0.5 rho_0 and becomes larger with decreasing density.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic treatment of giant resonances beyond the momentum-independent mean-field theory (RH approximation), which predicted ISGQR energies that were too high due to the small Dirac effective mass (M*/M = 0.55-0.7 giving M*_L/M = 0.6-0.75). The result contradicts the earlier finding of Bentz et al. (Ref. Benz2) that one-pion exchange reduces the Landau mass, which the authors attribute to the use of pseudo-scalar coupling in that work versus the pseudo-vector coupling used here. The approach enables future studies of finite nuclei and other observables sensitive to the Fermi velocity, such as the isovector magnetic moment, and highlights that the non-locality of the Dirac fields cannot be absorbed by density-dependent parameters in the RH approximation as done by Hirata (1991) and Cheng (1995).
