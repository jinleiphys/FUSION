# Onset of $η$ nuclear binding

> Recent studies of $η$ nuclear quasibound states by the Jerusalem-Prague Collaboration are reviewed, focusing on stochastic variational method self consistent calculations of $η$ few-nucleon systems. These calculations suggest that a minimum value Re$\,a_{ηN} \approx 1$ fm (0.7 fm) is needed to bind $η\,^3$He ($η\,^4$He).

# Onset of η nuclear binding

## Key claim

The paper concludes that η³He becomes bound for Re a_ηN ~ 1 fm (as in the GW model), while η⁴He binding requires a lower value of Re a_ηN ~ 0.7 fm (almost reached in the CS model); these onset values are larger than those estimated when using threshold ηN input, and if η⁴He is unbound then Re a_ηN < 0.7 fm.

## Method

The Jerusalem-Prague Collaboration performed stochastic variational method (SVM) self-consistent calculations of η few-nucleon systems (ηNN, ηNNN, ηNNNN) using several semi-realistic NN interaction models (pionless EFT, AV4', Minnesota soft core) together with two ηN interaction models (GW and CS) derived from meson-baryon coupled-channel calculations. Energy-dependent ηN potentials were constructed and self-consistency was imposed between the input subthreshold energy argument and the expectation value of the ηN cm energy generated in the few-body solution.

## Key numbers

- Minimum Re a_ηN ≈ 1 fm needed to bind η³He, and ≈ 0.7 fm needed to bind η⁴He (abstract and Section 6)
- GW model ηN scattering length: a_ηN^GW = (0.96 + i 0.26) fm (Section 2)
- CS model ηN scattering length: a_ηN^CS = (0.67 + i 0.20) fm (Section 2)
- Self-consistent η⁴He binding energy using AV4' NN and GW ηN potential with Λ = 4 fm⁻¹: B_η^SC = 3.5 MeV, compared to B_η^th = 13 MeV obtained using threshold input (Section 4)
- Self-consistent downward energy shift for ηN in Ca at nuclear saturation density: −55 ± 10 MeV (Section 4)
- Pionless EFT calculation yields B_Λ→∞(⁴He) = 27.8 ± 0.2 MeV, comparing well with B_exp(⁴He) = 28.3 MeV (Section 2)
- η³He scattering length deduced from experiment: a_η³He = [−(2.23 ± 1.29) + i(4.89 ± 0.57)] fm (Section 1)
- Using AV4' NN interaction near Λ = 4 fm⁻¹, η³He is barely bound (fraction of MeV) while η⁴He is bound by a few MeV (Section 5)
- Using the weaker CS ηN potential, η³He is unbound and η⁴He is barely bound only with the Minnesota NN interaction (Section 5)

## Context

This work extends earlier optical model calculations of η nuclear quasibound states across the periodic table (Friedman, Gal, Mares, Cieply, 2013-2014) to few-body systems using the stochastic variational method. It addresses the ambiguous experimental situation noted by Krusche and Wilkin (2015) regarding whether η³He or η⁴He are bound, and contradicts a Faddeev-Yakubovsky evaluation by Fix and Kolesnikov (2017) which found that the denser ⁴He nucleus leads to stronger reduction of the subthreshold ηN amplitude. The results enable future SVM calculations for heavier p-shell nuclei such as ⁶Li, where preliminary results suggest comfortable binding even at low scale parameters.
