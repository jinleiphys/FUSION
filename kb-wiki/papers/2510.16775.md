# Probing the shape evolution and shell structures in neutron-rich N=50 nuclei

> The structure of low-lying states of $N=50$ nuclei is investigated by the advanced Monte Carlo shell model (MCSM) in the $π{(fp)}$-$ν{(sdg)}$ model space. We have employed the shell-model Hamiltonian based on the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group, with minimal phenomenological adjustments to the single-particle energies. The MCSM results with the modified Hamiltonian nicely predict the shape coexistence of $^{78}$Ni, consistent with recent experimental data. The evolution of intrinsic shapes from the spherical shape to prolate shapes in the ground state of $N=50$ nuclei is discussed using the "T-plot" and effective single-particle energies, which visualize the intrinsic quadrupole deformation of the MCSM wave function. The present result shows that the monopole part of the tensor force does not enhance the shape coexistence of $^{78}$Ni, unlike the case of $^{68}$Ni.

## Key claim
The Monte Carlo shell model (MCSM) with a VS-IMSRG(3f2)-based Hamiltonian, after minimal phenomenological adjustment of only the single-particle energies, reproduces shape coexistence in 78Ni within about 250 keV of the experimental 2+2 state, but the monopole part of the tensor force does not enhance this shape coexistence, unlike the case of 68Ni.

## Method
The authors constructed a shell-model Hamiltonian in the π(fp)-ν(sdg) model space above a 60Ca core, starting from the VS-IMSRG(3f2) approximation with 1.8/2.0 (EM) nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions. They applied mass-dependent two-body matrix elements with exponent p = -0.3 and made phenomenological corrections only to the single-particle energies, optimized to reproduce low-lying states of 79Cu and 79Zn. Large-scale MCSM calculations with 100 Slater-determinant basis states were performed for even-Z N=50 nuclei from Z=20 to Z=30.

## Key numbers
- The root-mean-square deviation between theoretical and experimental energies for 79Cu and 79Zn was reduced from approximately 500 keV (original IMSRG SPEs) to about 110 keV after correction of the single-particle energies.
- The MCSM result for the second 2+ state of 78Ni reproduces the experimental value at 2.91 MeV within about 250 keV.
- The ratio R4/2 ≡ E(4+)/E(2+) for the rotational band in 78Ni is 2.95, comparable to the rigid rotor limit of 3.33.
- The B(E2; 2+2 → 0+2) value for 78Ni is 641 e2fm4, computed with effective charges (eπ, eν) = (1.31, 0.46)e.
- The MCSM predicts the 0+2 state in 80Zn at an excitation energy of 2.59 MeV.
- The T-plot analysis shows the 0+2 state of 78Ni has a prolate minimum at Q0 ∼ 200 fm2.

## Context
This work extends the VS-IMSRG approach to the N=50 region, building on the VS-IMSRG(3f2) method of BChe (2024) and the empirical shell-model Hamiltonian development of Purcell (2025). It addresses the discrepancy noted by Taniuchi (2019) where VS-IMSRG(2) calculations predicted the second 2+ state of 78Ni at 5.08 MeV versus the experimental 2.91 MeV. The results confirm the N=50 island of inversion predicted by Nowacki (2016) and Hu (2024), showing prolate ground states in 76Fe and 74Cr. The work enables further study of shape coexistence mechanisms in neutron-rich nuclei near the N=50 shell closure, particularly the different role of tensor forces compared to the N=40 region studied by Tsunoda (2014).
