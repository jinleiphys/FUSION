# Octet to decuplet electromagnetic transition in a relativistic quark model

> We study the octet to decuplet baryon electromagnetic transitions using the covariant spectator quark model, and predict the transition magnetic dipole form factors for those involving the strange baryons. Utilizing SU(3) symmetry, the valence quark contributions are supplemented by the pion cloud dressing based on the one estimated in the $γ^\ast N \to Δ$ reaction. Although the valence quark contributions are dominant in general, the pion cloud effects turn out to be very important to describe the experimental data. We also show that, other mesons besides the pion in particular the kaon, may be relevant for some reactions such as $γ^\ast Σ^+ \to Σ^{*+}$, based on our analysis for the radiative decay widths of the strange decuplet baryons.

## Key claim

The covariant spectator quark model predicts the magnetic dipole transition form factors for octet-to-decuplet baryon transitions involving strange baryons, but the valence quark contributions alone are insufficient to describe experimental data; explicit pion cloud effects, estimated via SU(3) symmetry from the γ*N → Δ reaction, are essential to reproduce the observed values, and kaon cloud contributions may be relevant for specific reactions such as γ*Σ+ → Σ*+.

## Method

The authors used the covariant spectator quark model with an S-state approximation for both octet and decuplet baryon wave functions, where only the magnetic dipole form factor G_M* survives (electric and Coulomb quadrupole form factors vanish). Valence quark contributions were calculated from the overlap of radial wave functions and flavor coefficients derived from SU(3) symmetry, while pion cloud contributions were estimated by scaling the parametrization established for the γ*N → Δ reaction using SU(3) meson-baryon coupling factors f_{BB'}.

## Key numbers

- For γ*p → Δ+, the upper limit of the valence quark contribution at Q²=0 is G_M^b(0) = 2.05, compared to the experimental value 3.02 ± 0.03, indicating valence quarks explain only about 70% of the result.
- For γ*Λ → Σ*0, the upper limit of the valence quark contribution at Q²=0 is G_M^b(0) = 2.02.
- For γ*Σ+ → Σ*+, the upper limit of the valence quark contribution at Q²=0 is G_M^b(0) = 2.30.
- For γ*Σ0 → Σ*0, the upper limit of the valence quark contribution at Q²=0 is G_M^b(0) = 1.07.
- For γ*Σ- → Σ*-, the upper limit of the valence quark contribution at Q²=0 is G_M^b(0) = -0.17.
- For γ*Ξ0 → Ξ*0, the upper limit of the valence quark contribution at Q²=0 is G_M^b(0) = 2.47.
- For γ*Ξ- → Ξ*-, the upper limit of the valence quark contribution at Q²=0 is G_M^b(0) = -0.19.
- The decuplet baryon masses used are M_Δ = 1.232 GeV, M_Σ* = 1.385 GeV, M_Ξ* = 1.533 GeV, and M_Ω = 1.672 GeV, reproduced with precision better than 0.1%.
- The quark anomalous magnetic moments used are κ_u = 1.711, κ_d = 1.987, and κ_s = 1.462, expressed in nuclear magneton units.

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator quark model, previously applied successfully to the γ*N → Δ transition (Ramalho and collaborators, 2010-2012) and to octet and decuplet baryon electromagnetic structure (Ramalho and Tsushima, 2011-2012), to all octet-to-decuplet transitions involving strange baryons. The study addresses the nearly nonexistent experimental data on these transitions by providing theoretical predictions, and it enables future experimental searches at facilities such as Jefferson Lab by identifying which reactions (e.g., γ*Σ+ → Σ*+) may require kaon cloud contributions beyond the dominant pion cloud effects.
