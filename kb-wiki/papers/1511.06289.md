# Hydrodynamic predictions for 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions

> We make predictions for momentum-integrated elliptic and triangular flow as well as mean transverse momentum for 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions, as planned at the Large Hadron Collider. We use hydrodynamic calculations to predict the change of these observables as the center-of-mass collision energy evolves from 2.76 TeV to 5.02 TeV per nucleon pair. By using previously measured values as a baseline, we are able to make a robust prediction without relying on a particular model for initial conditions and without precise knowledge of medium properties such as viscosity. Thus, though the predicted changes are small, they can provide a significant test of the current hydrodynamic picture of heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the change in momentum-integrated elliptic flow, triangular flow, and mean transverse momentum when Pb-Pb collision energy increases from 2.76 TeV to 5.02 TeV per nucleon pair will be small (several percent), but these predictions are robust because they use previously measured values as a baseline rather than relying on a specific model for initial conditions or precise knowledge of medium properties such as viscosity.

## Method

The authors calculated the change in initial spatial eccentricities (ε₂ and ε₃) from 2.76 TeV to 5.02 TeV using four different models of initial conditions: MC-Glauber, Trento, MC-KLN, and MCrcBK. They separately calculated the change in the hydrodynamic response (vₙ/εₙ) and mean transverse momentum using the 2+1 relativistic viscous hydrodynamic code v-USPhydro with varying parameters for shear viscosity, freeze-out temperature, and initial time, then combined both contributions to produce final predictions.

## Key numbers

- The charged hadron multiplicity scaling with collision energy is parameterized as dN/dη ∼ s^0.155, using the exponent from a recent measurement at 5.02 TeV
- The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section used is 64 mb at 2.76 TeV and 70 mb at 5.02 TeV
- Over all centralities and all models, the change in rms ε₂ from 2.76 TeV to 5.02 TeV is between -2% and +2%, and for ε₃ between -3% and +1%
- The predicted increase in mean transverse momentum ⟨pₜ⟩ is approximately 3% across all centralities, with a range of 2.5%-3.5%
- A 20% increase in multiplicity corresponds to a 3% increase in ⟨pₜ⟩, consistent with the relation d⟨pₜ⟩/⟨pₜ⟩ = (P/ε)(dNch/Nch) where P ≃ ε/6 in the relevant temperature range of 200-250 MeV

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic predictions from the 2.76 TeV LHC Run 1 measurements (ALICE, CMS, ATLAS) to the upcoming 5.02 TeV Run 2, using a method that reduces both theoretical and experimental systematic uncertainties by focusing on the change in observables rather than absolute values. The predictions are consistent with those from the EKRT model of initial conditions (Niemi 2015) for parameterizations that work across various energies, and if confirmed by experimental data, would provide a direct experimental verification of the QCD equation of state.
