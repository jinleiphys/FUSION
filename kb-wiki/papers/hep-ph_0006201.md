# Event-by-event fluctuations of the charged particle ratio from non-equilibrium transport theory

> The event by event fluctuations of the ratio of positively to negatively charged hadrons are predicted within the UrQMD model. Corrections for finite acceptance and finite net charge are derived. These corrections are relevant to compare experimental data and transport model results to previous predictions. The calculated fluctuations at RHIC and SPS energies are shown to be compatible with a hadron gas. Thus, deviating by a factor of 3 from the predictions for a thermalized quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The event-by-event fluctuations of the charged hadron ratio predicted by the UrQMD transport model at SPS and RHIC energies are compatible with a hadron gas, deviating by a factor of 3 from the predictions for a thermalized quark-gluon plasma, but only after applying corrections for finite acceptance and finite net charge that the abstract mentions without specifying their necessity for comparison.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model, a microscopic transport approach with covariant propagation of constituent quarks and diquarks, mesonic and baryonic degrees of freedom, string fragmentation, and resonance decays, to simulate heavy ion collisions at SPS and RHIC energies. They derived correction factors \(C_\mu = \tilde{R}^2_{\Delta y}\) for finite net charge and \(C_y = 1 - P\) for finite rapidity acceptance, where \(P = \langle N_{\rm ch}\rangle_{\Delta y} / \langle N_{\rm ch}\rangle_{\rm total}\), and applied them to the raw ratio fluctuations \(\langle N_{\rm ch}\rangle_{\Delta y} \langle \delta R^2\rangle_{\Delta y}\) to obtain the corrected observable \(\tilde{D}\). The corrected \(\tilde{D}\) values from UrQMD were then compared to analytic predictions for a quark-gluon gas (\(\tilde{D}=1\)), a resonance gas (\(\tilde{D}=2.8\)), and an uncorrelated pion gas (\(\tilde{D}=4\)).

## Key numbers

- The corrected observable \(\tilde{D}\) for a quark-gluon gas is 1, for a resonance gas is 2.8, and for an uncorrelated pion gas is 4 (Eq. \ref{eq:correct}).
- UrQMD predictions for \(\tilde{D}\) at SPS and RHIC energies are a factor of 3 larger than the fluctuations expected if a QGP was formed.
- At SPS, the full phase space ratio fluctuation \(\langle \delta R^2\rangle_{\rm total}\) is about 1%; at RHIC, it is about \(10^{-5}\).
- The limiting temperature of the UrQMD model in equilibrium is 135 MeV, similar to a Hagedorn gas.
- The simulations used Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt s = 200\) AGeV with impact parameter \(b \leq 2\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamic predictions of Jeon and Koch (2000) for charge ratio fluctuations as a QGP signature by providing the first non-equilibrium transport model calculation using UrQMD, which had previously been shown to reproduce event-by-event fluctuation data in Pb+Pb collisions at the SPS. The paper derives the necessary corrections for finite acceptance and finite net charge that were absent in the original grand-canonical treatment, enabling direct comparison with experimental data from NA49 at CERN-SPS and STAR at RHIC. The results contradict the possibility that string fragmentation or rescattering could mimic QGP fluctuations, and establish \(\tilde{D} \simeq 1\) as a clean indicator of QGP formation for future measurements.
