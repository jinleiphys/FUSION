# QCD Plasma Instabilities and Bottom-Up Thermalization

> We study the role of QCD plasma instabilities in non-equilibrium quark-gluon plasmas. First, we argue that such instabilities must drastically modify the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario for heavy-ion collisions. Second, we discuss conditions for the existence of instabilities in a more general context than previously treated in the QCD literature. We also give a thorough qualitative review of the origin of instabilities. We discuss some mechanisms whereby the growth of plasma instabilities saturates. Finally, we solve explicitly for instabilities and their growth rates for two extreme cases of anisotropic non-equilibrium plasmas that can be treated relatively simply and analytically: $f(\p) = F(p_\perp) δ(p_z)$ and $f(\p) = F(p_z) δ^{(2)}(p_\perp)$, where $f(\p)$ is the distribution of particles in momentum space.

## Key claim

Plasma instabilities must drastically modify the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario for heavy-ion collisions, even during its very first moments, because the growth time of instabilities is parametrically small compared to the expansion time for any proper time \(\tau \gg Q_s^{-1}\).

## Method

The authors use collisionless kinetic theory (the Vlasov equations) coupled to soft classical gauge fields, also known as the Hard Thermal Loop approximation, to study plasma instabilities in anisotropic non-equilibrium quark-gluon plasmas. They analyze the retarded self-energy \(\Pi^{\mu\nu}(\omega,\mathbf{q})\) for soft gauge fields in the presence of a homogeneous but non-equilibrium distribution of hard particles, and derive sufficient conditions for the existence of instabilities based on the eigenvalues of the zero-frequency self-energy. They solve explicitly for instabilities and their growth rates for two analytically tractable extreme cases: a planar distribution \(f(\mathbf{p}) = F(p_\perp) \delta(p_z)\) and a linear distribution \(f(\mathbf{p}) = F(p_z) \delta^{(2)}(\mathbf{p}_\perp)\).

## Key numbers

* The growth time for unstable soft modes is \(t_{\text{growth}} \sim 1/|m_{\text{soft}}| \sim \sqrt{\tau / Q_s}\), which is parametrically small compared to the expansion time \(\tau\) for \(Q_s \tau \gg 1\).
* The hard gluon spatial number density during the first stage scales as \(N_{\text{h}} \sim Q_s^3 / [\alpha (Q_s \tau)]\).
* The hard gluon occupation number per mode decreases as \(f_{\text{h}} \sim 1/[\alpha (Q_s \tau)^{2/3}]\) during the first stage.
* The mean free time between hard gluon collisions is \(t_{\text{col}} \sim (Q_s \tau)^{2/3} / Q_s\), which is parametrically long compared to \(t_{\text{growth}}\) for \(Q_s \tau \gg 1\).
* The soft scale \(m_{\text{soft}}^2 \sim \alpha N_{\text{h}} / Q_s \sim Q_s / \tau\).
* The hard gluon momentum spread in the \(z\) direction during the first stage is \(p_z \sim Q_s / (Q_s \tau)^{1/3}\).

## Context

This work extends the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario of Baier, Mueller, Schiff, and Son (2001) by showing that plasma instabilities, which were not considered in that scenario, must drastically modify its first stage. The authors build on the earlier advocacy of Mrówczyński (1993-2000) that plasma instabilities may play an important role in quark-gluon plasma thermalization from anisotropic initial conditions. The paper enables future research to determine detailed parametric estimates of the equilibration time scale, which the authors expect to be more rapid than the \(\alpha^{-13/5} Q_s^{-1}\) time scale of Baier et al.
