# Scaling of anisotropy flows in intermediate energy heavy ion collisions

> Anisotropic flows ($v_1$, $v_2$ and $v_4$) of light nuclear clusters are studied by a nucleonic transport model in intermediate energy heavy ion collisions. The number-of-nucleon scalings of the directed flow ($v_1$) and elliptic flow ($v_2$) are demonstrated for light nuclear clusters. Moreover, the ratios of $v_4/v_2^2$ of nuclear clusters show a constant value of 1/2 regardless of the transverse momentum. The above phenomena can be understood by the coalescence mechanism in nucleonic level and are worthy to be explored in experiments.

## Key claim

The number-of-nucleon scaling of directed flow ($v_1$) and elliptic flow ($v_2$) for light nuclear clusters, and a constant ratio $v_4/v_2^2 = 1/2$ independent of transverse momentum, are demonstrated in a nucleonic transport model at intermediate energy; these results are predictions that the authors state "are worthy to be explored in experiments" and are understood via the coalescence mechanism at the nucleonic level.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IDQMD) model to simulate reactions of $^{40}$Ca + $^{40}$Ca, $^{86}$Kr + $^{58}$Ni, and $^{86}$Kr + $^{124}$Sn at 25 MeV/nucleon for peripheral collisions (impact parameter ranges 4-8 fm, 6-10 fm, and 7-11 fm, respectively). Nuclear clusters were identified at freeze-out (200 fm/c) using a coalescence criterion: nucleons within $\Delta r_{min} \leq 3.5$ fm in coordinate space and $\Delta p_{min} \leq 300$ MeV/c in momentum space. Three simulation conditions were compared: hard equation of state (EOS) without isospin terms, soft EOS with isospin terms, and soft EOS without isospin terms.

## Key numbers

- Directed flow $v_1$ shows a negative slope versus rapidity for all three systems at 25 MeV/nucleon, indicating attractive mean field dominance; $v_1/A$ curves for different cluster masses collapse onto a single curve.
- Elliptic flow $v_2$ is positive and increases with transverse momentum $p_t$; the number-of-nucleon scaling $v_2/A$ versus $p_t/A$ holds for $p_t/A < 0.25$ GeV/$c$.
- The ratio $v_4/v_2^2$ for light nuclear clusters (neutrons, protons, A=2, A=3, A=4 fragments) shows a constant value of 1/2 regardless of transverse momentum, independent of the EOS and isospin terms.
- The coalescence model parameters used: $\Delta r_{min} \leq 3.5$ fm, $\Delta p_{min} \leq 300$ MeV/c; Gaussian wave packet width $L = 2.16$ fm$^2$.
- Two EOS parameter sets: hard EOS with incompressibility $K = 380$ MeV ($\alpha = -124$ MeV, $\beta = 70.5$ MeV, $\gamma = 2.0$) and soft EOS with $K = 200$ MeV ($\alpha = -356$ MeV, $\beta = 303$ MeV, $\gamma = 7/6$); symmetry energy strength $C_{sym} = 32$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the concept of number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling observed at RHIC for hadrons (Adams, 2005) to the intermediate energy domain, where the scaling is instead in number of nucleons for light nuclear clusters. The coalescence mechanism, previously used to explain light fragment formation (Awes, 1981; Mekjian, 1978; Sato, 1981) and mass dependence of directed flow (Huang, 1996; Kunde, 1997), is applied here to explain the observed scalings. The constant $v_4/v_2^2 = 1/2$ matches a prediction from ideal fluid dynamics (Borghini, 2006) and also arises from a nucleonic coalescence model, suggesting a robust feature worth testing experimentally. The results enable experimental searches for these scaling laws in intermediate energy heavy ion collisions to probe nucleonic collectivity.
