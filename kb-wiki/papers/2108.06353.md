# Chiral Symmetry Breaking and Confinement from an Interacting Ensemble of Instanton-dyons in Two-flavor Massless QCD

> In this work we present the results from numerical simulations of an interacting ensemble of instanton-dyons in the $SU(3)$ gauge group with $N_f=2$ flavors of massless quarks. Dynamical quarks are included via the effective interactions induced by the fermionic determinant evaluated in the subspace of topological zero modes. The eigenvalue spectrum of the Dirac operator is studied at different volumes to extract the chiral condensate and eigenvalue gap, with both observables providing consistent values of the chiral transition temperature $T_c$. We find that a sufficient density of dyons is responsible for generating the confining potential and breaking the chiral symmetry, both of which are compatible with second-order transitions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that an interacting ensemble of instanton-dyons in SU(3) gauge theory with two massless quark flavors can simultaneously generate both confinement and chiral symmetry breaking, with both transitions being second-order, but the authors note that the deconfinement transition is actually a smooth crossover (not a true phase transition) because the exact Z3 symmetry is broken by dynamical quarks, and the chiral transition temperature determination carries larger uncertainties than those given by any individual fit.

## Method

The authors performed Monte-Carlo simulations of an interacting ensemble of instanton-dyons (M1, M2, and L types plus antidyons) in SU(3) with Nf=2 massless dynamical quarks, using the Metropolis algorithm to compute the free energy density as a function of temperature, holonomy parameter, and dyon densities. Dynamical quarks were included via the fermionic determinant evaluated in the subspace of topological zero modes, approximated by a hopping matrix Tij describing quark transitions between L and anti-L dyons. The physical parameters of the ensemble were determined by finding the free energy minimum in the space of input parameters for each temperature, and the Dirac eigenvalue spectrum was studied at three different volumes (120, 240, and 360 dyons) to extract the chiral condensate and eigenvalue gap via infinite-volume extrapolation.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature of the deconfinement transition is found at S0(T_deconf) = 10.44 ± 0.29, where S0 is the instanton action related to temperature by S0 = (11/3 Nc - 2/3 Nf) ln(T/Λ) with Λ = 4.8
- The Polyakov loop data was fitted to the O(4) universality class form with χ² = 0.0257, using critical exponents β = 0.380, δ = 4.824, and α = -0.2131
- The O(2) universality class fit gave χ² = 0.0178 with α = -0.0172, showing slightly better agreement
- Simulations used N_D = 120 dyons (60 dyons and 60 antidyons) for the main runs, with additional runs at 2N_D and 3N_D for finite-volume studies
- The hopping matrix element parameterization uses T_ij = ν̄ c' exp(-√(11.2 + (π ν̄ r T)²)) with ln(c') = 4.45
- The dyon densities range from n_M ≈ 0.12 to 0.45 and n_L ≈ n_M/1.643 at the fitted ratio
- The instanton action S0 was varied from 8 to 14.5 in steps of 0.5
- The holonomy parameter ν was varied from 0.1933 to 0.3533 in steps of 0.01

## Context

This work extends previous numerical studies of instanton-dyon ensembles from SU(2) gauge theory (Larsen 2015, Lopez-Ruiz 2019) and pure SU(3) gauge theory (DeMartini 2021) to include Nf=2 massless dynamical quarks, moving closer to physical QCD. The results are compatible with lattice QCD studies by the Bielefeld group (Ding 2018, HotQCD 2019, Clarke 2019, Kaczmarek 2020) on the phase transition in the chiral limit, and the approach enables future studies of the interplay between confinement and chiral symmetry breaking using the instanton-dyon framework, with the dyon densities and correlation functions from lattice studies (Larsen 2019) serving as constraints on the model parameters.
