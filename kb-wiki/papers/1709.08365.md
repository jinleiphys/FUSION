# General structure of fermion two-point function and its spectral representation in a hot magnetised medium

> We have systematically constructed the general structure of the fermion self-energy and the effective quark propagator in presence of a nontrivial background like hot magnetised medium. This is applicable to both QED and QCD. The hard thermal loop approximation has been used for the heat bath. We have also examined transformation properties of the effective fermion propagator under some of the discrete symmetries of the system. Using the effective fermion propagator we have analysed the fermion dispersion spectra in a hot magnetised medium along with the spinor for each fermion mode obtained by solving the modified Dirac equation. The fermion spectra is found to reflect the discrete symmetries of the two-point functions. We note that for a chirally symmetric theory the degenerate left and right handed chiral modes in vacuum or in a heat bath get separated and become asymmetric in presence of magnetic field without disturbing the chiral invariance. The obtained general structure of the two-point functions is verified by computing the three-point function, which agrees with the existing results in one-loop order. Finally, we have computed explicitly the spectral representation of the two-point functions which would be very important to study the spectral properties of the hot magnetised medium corresponding to QED and QCD with background magnetic field.

## Key claim

The paper systematically constructs the general structure of the fermion self-energy and effective quark propagator in a hot magnetised medium, applicable to both QED and QCD, using the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation for the heat bath and a weak-field expansion for the magnetic field; the authors note that for a chirally symmetric theory, the degenerate left- and right-handed chiral modes in vacuum or in a heat bath become separated and asymmetric in the presence of a magnetic field without breaking chiral invariance.

## Method

The authors used the Schwinger proper-time formalism to write the fermion propagator in a constant background magnetic field, then constructed the most general Lorentz-tensor structure of the fermion self-energy in a hot magnetised medium using the four-velocity of the heat bath \(u^\mu\) and the direction of the magnetic field \(n^\mu\). They computed the one-loop self-energy in the HTL approximation with a weak magnetic field, obtained the effective propagator via the Dyson-Schwinger equation, and analysed the dispersion relations and discrete symmetry properties (chirality, reflection) of the two-point functions. The general structure was verified by computing the three-point function at one-loop order, and the spectral representation of the two-point functions was explicitly derived.

## Key numbers

* The magnetic field strength at LHC is estimated as \(eB \sim 15 m^2_\pi\) (several times the pion mass squared), based on VSkokov (2009).
* Magnetars exhibit a magnetic field of \(10^{18}-10^{20}\) Gauss at the inner core and \(10^{12}-10^{13}\) Gauss at the surface.
* The weak-field domain of applicability is given as \(m_{th}^2 (\sim g^2 T^2) < q_f B < T^2\).
* The structure functions for the one-loop self-energy in HTL and weak-field approximations are given by:
  * \(a(p_0,|\vec p|) = -\frac{m^{2}_{th}}{|\vec{p}|^{2}} Q_{1}\left(\frac{p_{0}}{|\vec{p}|}\right)\)
  * \(b(p_0,|\vec p|) = \frac{m^{2}_{th}}{|\vec{p}|}\left[\frac{p_{0}}{|\vec{p}|}Q_{1}\left(\frac{p_{0}}{|\vec{p}|}\right)-Q_{0}\left(\frac{p_{0}}{|\vec{p}|}\right)\right]\)
  * \(b^{\prime}(p_0,|\vec p|) = 4C_{F}g^{2}M^{2}(T,m_f,q_{f}B)\frac{p_{z}}{|\vec{p}|^{2}}Q_{1}\left(\frac{p_{0}}{|\vec{p}|}\right)\)
  * \(c^{\prime}(p_0,|\vec p|) = 4C_{F}g^{2}M^{2}(T,m_f,q_{f}B)\frac{1}{|\vec{p}|}Q_{0}\left(\frac{p_{0}}{|\vec{p}|}\right)\)

## Context

This work extends the HTL resummation programme (Brateen et al., Nucl. Phys. B 337, 1990) to include a background magnetic field, building on prior calculations of the thermomagnetic correction to the quark self-energy and quark-gluon three-point function by Ayala et al. and the weak-field expansion of the fermion propagator by Chyi et al. The general structure and spectral representation derived here enable systematic studies of spectral properties (e.g., collective excitations, Landau damping) of hot QED and QCD plasmas in a background magnetic field, relevant for heavy-ion collision phenomenology at LHC and RHIC and for magnetar physics.
