# Heavy quark potential and LQCD based quark condensate at finite magnetic field

> In the present work, we have studied heavy quarkonia potential in hot and magnetized quark gluon plasma. Inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) effect is incorporated within the system through the magnetic field modified Debye mass by modifying the effective quark masses. We have obtained the real and imaginary part of the heavy quark potential in this new scenario. After the evaluation of the binding energy and the decay width we comment about the dissociation temperatures of the heavy quarkonia in presence of magnetic field.

## Key claim

The authors incorporate the inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) effect into the heavy quark potential by using lattice QCD-based quark condensates to define effective constituent quark masses, which then modify the Debye screening mass; the IMC effect produces a mild suppression of the Debye mass near the transition temperature compared to the massless quark case, but this suppression is not strongly visible in the final dissociation temperatures.

## Method

The authors start from the Cornell potential (Coulomb plus string terms) and obtain its in-medium modification by dividing its Fourier transform by the dielectric permittivity, which is derived from the static limit of the gluon propagator. The Debye screening mass is computed via semi-classical transport theory using Landau-quantized quark distribution functions summed over all Landau levels, with the quark dispersion relation modified by a temperature- and magnetic-field-dependent constituent quark mass obtained from lattice QCD quark condensate data (Bali et al., 2014). The real and imaginary parts of the heavy quark potential are then expressed analytically in terms of the Debye mass, and the binding energy and thermal width are computed from the ground-state Coulombic wave function.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strengths considered: eB = 0.2, 0.4, and 0.6 GeV²
- Temperatures used for Debye mass plots: T = 100 MeV and T = 200 MeV
- Charm quark mass: m_c = 1.5 GeV; bottom quark mass: m_b = 4.5 GeV
- String tension: σ = 0.184 GeV²
- Lambda parameter for running coupling: Λ_MSbar = 0.176 GeV
- Debye mass shows a mild suppression when using IMC-based constituent quark mass compared to the massless quark case in the temperature range T = 0.080–0.160 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy quark potential in a magnetized medium (Singh et al., 2017; Hasan et al., 2017) by moving beyond the lowest Landau level approximation to include all Landau levels, and by incorporating the inverse magnetic catalysis effect discovered in lattice QCD calculations (Bali et al., 2014). The approach enables future studies of heavy quarkonium dissociation in a more realistic magnetized QGP that includes non-perturbative QCD effects near the transition temperature, complementing earlier perturbative calculations of Debye mass in magnetic fields (Bandyopadhyay et al., 2016; Bonati et al., 2017).
