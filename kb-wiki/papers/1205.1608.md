# Volume Dependence of the Axial Charge of the Nucleon

> It is shown that the strong volume-dependence of the axial charge of the nucleon seen in lattice QCD calculations can be understood quantitatively in terms of the pion-induced interactions between neighbouring nucleons. The associated wave function renormalization leads to an increased suppression of the axial charge as the strength of the interaction increases, either because of a decrease in lattice size or in pion mass.

## Key claim

The strong volume-dependence of the nucleon axial charge observed in lattice QCD can be quantitatively explained by pion-exchange interactions between neighboring nucleons on the periodic lattice, where the associated wave function renormalization dominates over vertex renormalization because the $N \rightarrow \Delta \pi$ transition is suppressed by an extra factor of $\exp[-\delta L]$ with $\delta = m_\Delta - m_N$.

## Method

The authors computed the wave function renormalization contribution from pion exchange between a nucleon and its periodic images on the lattice, using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory with modified Bessel functions $K_n(m_\pi L)$. They evaluated contributions from nearest, next-to-nearest, and next-to-next-to-nearest neighbors (distances $L$, $\sqrt{2}L$, $\sqrt{3}L$), including the vertex renormalization factors (1/5, 1/6, and 1/9 respectively) that partially cancel the wave function renormalization. The total correction $\delta g_A$ was then applied to lattice data from QCDSF and RBC collaborations to test the model.

## Key numbers

- The total nearest-neighbor wave function renormalization is $\delta Z_2^{\rm nn} = 15 \frac{m_\pi^2}{4 \pi^2} \left( \frac{g_A}{2 f_{\pi}} \right)^2 (K_0(m_\pi L) + K_2(m_\pi L))$
- The nearest-neighbor correction to $g_A$ is $\delta g_A^{\rm n1} = \frac{4}{5} \delta Z_2^{\rm nn}$
- For a 2 fm lattice at $m_\pi = 0.3$ GeV, the correction to $g_A$ is almost 20%
- For a 2.5 fm lattice at $m_\pi = 0.3$ GeV, the correction is 6%
- For a 4 fm lattice at physical pion mass, the correction is as large as 10%
- The next-to-nearest neighbor correction: $\delta g_A^{\rm n2} = \frac{5}{6} \cdot 24 {\cal F}(m_\pi^2, \sqrt{2} m_\pi L)$
- The next-to-next-to-nearest neighbor correction: $\delta g_A^{\rm n3} = \frac{8}{9} \cdot 12 {\cal F}(m_\pi^2, \sqrt{3} m_\pi L)$

## Context

This work resolves a long-standing puzzle in lattice QCD where the axial charge $g_A$ showed unexplained strong volume dependence, previously attributed to surface terms (hep-ph/0108015) or standard chiral loop discretization (hep-lat/0603028). The authors show that earlier attempts using SU(6) couplings for $\Delta$ excitations (Beane 2004) produced insignificant effects compared to the pion-exchange mechanism identified here. This enables reliable extraction of $g_A$ from finite-volume lattice calculations and suggests similar finite-volume corrections may affect calculations of quark spin fractions, orbital angular momentum, hadron magnetic moments, and the E2/M1 ratio for the $\Delta$ baryon.
