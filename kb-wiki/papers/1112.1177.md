# Analyzing flow anisotropies with excursion sets in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We show that flow anisotropies in relativistic heavy-ion collisions can be analyzed using a certain technique of shape analysis of excursion sets recently proposed by us for CMBR fluctuations to investigate anisotropic expansion history of the universe. The technique analyzes shapes (sizes) of patches above (below) certain threshold value for transverse energy/particle number (the excursion sets) as a function of the azimuthal angle and rapidity. Modeling flow by imparting extra anisotropic momentum to the momentum distribution of particles from HIJING, we compare the resulting distributions for excursion sets at two different azimuthal angles. Angles with maximum difference in the two distributions identify the event plane, and the magnitude of difference in the two distributions relates to the magnitude of momentum anisotropy, i.e. elliptic flow.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a shape analysis of excursion sets (patches of transverse energy or particle number fluctuations above a threshold) can identify the event plane and quantify elliptic flow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, but the authors emphasize that this is a proof-of-principle demonstration using an ad hoc momentum modification factor \(f_{p_x}\) in HIJING, and that a quantitative correspondence requires future hydrodynamic simulations.

## Method

The authors model anisotropic flow by multiplying the \(P_x\) momentum of every final-state particle from HIJING by a factor \(f_{p_x}\) (ranging from 1.0 to 1.4). They bin transverse energy fluctuations in 100 azimuthal angle (\(\phi\)) bins and 100 rapidity (\(y\)) bins, subtract the average, and select bins where fluctuations exceed 0.2 times the maximum fluctuation to define excursion sets. They then slice the \(\phi\)-\(y\) plane into thin rapidity slices (width 0.18), measure the \(\phi\)-widths of the intersections of patches with these slices, and compare the frequency distributions of widths within 45° arcs centered at \(\phi = 0\) and \(\phi = \pi/2\).

## Key numbers

- For non-central Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2\) TeV with impact parameter \(b = 3-4\) fm, the ratio of the heights of the two peaks (at \(\phi = 0\) vs. \(\phi = \pi/2\)) is 1.3 for \(f_{p_x} = 1.1\), 1.6 for \(f_{p_x} = 1.2\), and 2.0 for \(f_{p_x} = 1.3\).
- The threshold for excursion sets is set at 0.2 \(\times\) maximum fluctuation.
- The rapidity slice width used for width distributions is 0.18.
- The histogram bin width for \(\phi\)-widths is 0.05 radians.
- A lower cutoff of \(P_T \simeq 150\) MeV is applied to exclude very large rapidity values.
- The peak near 0.1 radians in the width distributions corresponds to fluctuations of size order 1 fm at a distance of about 10 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior technique for analyzing anisotropic expansion in CMBR fluctuations (Mohapatra, Saumia, and Srivastava, 2011) to relativistic heavy-ion collisions, building on their earlier proposal (Mohapatra, Saumia, and Srivastava, 2010) that flow coefficients \(v_n^{rms}\) in heavy-ion collisions exhibit features analogous to CMBR acoustic peaks. It also relates to studies of magnetic field effects on flow (Mohapatra, Saumia, and Srivastava, 2011; Tuchin, 2011) which predicted enhancement of elliptic flow \(v_2\) by almost 30%. The method is presented as a novel, independent cross-check for conventional event-plane determination techniques, and the authors suggest future application of Minkowski functionals and genus statistics (used in CMBR and large-scale structure) to heavy-ion collision analysis.
