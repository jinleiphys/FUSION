# Production of the bottomonium-like states $Z_b$ states at $e$-$h$ and ultraperipheral $h$-$h$ collisions

> The photoproduction of bottomonium-like states $Z_{b}(10610)$ and $Z_{b}(10650)$ via $γp$ scattering is studied within an effectiv Lagrangian approach and the vector-meson-dominance model. The Regge model is employed to calculate the photoproduction of $Z_{b}$ states via $t$-channel with $π$ exchange.The numerical results show that the values of the total cross-sections of $Z_{b}(10610)$ and $Z_{b}(10650)$ can reach 0.09 nb and 0.02 nb, respectively, near the center of mass energy of 22 GeV. The experimental measurements and studies on the photoproduction of $Z_{b}$ states near energy region around $W\simeq 22$ GeV is suggested. Moreover, with the help of eSTARlight and STARlight programs, one obtains the cross-sections and event numbers of $Z_{b}(10610)$ production in electron-ion collision (EIC) and Ultraperipheral collisions (UPCs). The results show that a considerable number of events from $Z_{b}(10610)$ can be produced on the relevant experiments of EICs and UPCs. Also, one calculates the rates and kinematic distributions for $γp\rightarrow Z_{b}n$ in $ep$ and $pA$ collisions via EICs and UPCs, and the relevant results will provide an important reference for the RHIC, LHC, EIC-US, LHeC, and FCC experiments to search for the bottomonium-like $Z_{b}$ states.

# Production of the bottomonium-like states Z_b states at e-h and ultraperipheral h-h collisions

## Key claim
The photoproduction cross-sections of Z_b(10610) and Z_b(10650) via γp scattering reach maxima of 0.09 nb and 0.02 nb respectively near W ≃ 22 GeV, but these values depend on the cutoff parameter Λ_t: varying it from 0.5 to 0.9 GeV changes the Z_b(10610) cross-section at that energy to between approximately 0.06 and 0.12 nb.

## Method
The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach combined with the vector-meson-dominance (VMD) model to compute the photoproduction of Z_b states via the t-channel with pion exchange in γp → Z_b n reactions. The pion propagator was Reggeized by replacing the Feynman propagator with a Regge propagator using the trajectory α_π(t) = 0.7(t − m_π^2). The resulting γp cross-sections were then fed into the eSTARlight and STARlight Monte-Carlo packages to simulate Z_b(10610) production in electron-ion collisions (EICs) and ultraperipheral hadron-hadron collisions (UPCs), yielding cross-sections, event numbers, rapidity distributions, and transverse momentum distributions for various accelerator configurations.

## Key numbers
- Total cross-section of γp → Z_b(10610)n reaches a maximum of 0.09 nb near center-of-mass energy W ≃ 22 GeV.
- Total cross-section of γp → Z_b(10650)n reaches a maximum of 0.02 nb near W ≃ 22 GeV.
- With cutoff Λ_t varied from 0.5 to 0.9 GeV, the Z_b(10610) cross-section at W ≃ 22 GeV lies between approximately 0.06 and 0.12 nb.
- Z_b(10610) cross-section in e-p EIC-US (18 GeV e vs. 275 GeV p): 6.2 pb; in e-p LHeC (60 GeV e vs. 7 TeV p): 8.5 pb; in e-p FCC (60 GeV e vs. 50 TeV p): 9.8 pb.
- Z_b(10610) cross-section in p-Au UPCs at RHIC (100 GeV p vs. 100 GeV Au): 2.0 nb; in p-Pb UPCs at LHC (7 TeV p vs. 2.778 TeV Pb): 30 nb.
- Expected Z_b(10610) event statistics (10^6 events): EIC-US 0.062, LHeC 0.085, FCC 1.5, RHIC p-Au 0.0090, LHC p-Pb 0.060.
- For EicC with 5 GeV e vs. 20 GeV p: Z_b(10610) cross-section 0.52 pb, expected 5.2 × 10^3 events (10 fb^-1 integrated luminosity).
- Coupling constants g_{Z_bΥπ} for Z_b(10610): 0.487 (Υ(1S)), 3.299 (Υ(2S)), 9.292 (Υ(3S)); for Z_b(10650): 0.206 (Υ(1S)), 1.468 (Υ(2S)), 4.916 (Υ(3S)).
- e/f_Υ values: 0.008 (Υ(1S)), 0.005 (Υ(2S)), 0.004 (Υ(3S)).

## Context
This work extends the study of exotic hadron photoproduction, previously applied to charmonium-like states by Liu (2008), He (2009), and Wang (2015), to the bottomonium-like Z_b(10610) and Z_b(10650) states first observed by the Belle Collaboration in 2011. The calculations provide the first predictions for Z_b photoproduction cross-sections and kinematic distributions, enabling experimental searches at RHIC, LHC, EIC-US, LHeC, and FCC. The results also offer a test of the Regge exchange model and may help clarify whether these states are genuine tetraquarks or hadronic molecules, as debated by Guo (2019) and others.
