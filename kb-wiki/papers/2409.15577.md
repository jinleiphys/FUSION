# Partial-wave projection of relativistic three-body amplitudes

> We derive the integral equations for partial-wave projected three-body scattering amplitudes, starting from the integral equations for three-body amplitudes developed for lattice QCD analyses. The results, which hold for generic three-body systems of spinless particles, build upon the recently derived partial-wave projected one-particle exchange, a primary component of the relativistic framework proven to satisfy S matrix unitarity. We derive simplified expressions for factorizable short-distance interactions, $\mathcal{K}_{3}$, in two equivalent formalisms - one symmetric under particle interchange and one asymmetric. For the asymmetric case, we offer parameterizations useful for amplitude analysis. Finally, we examine toy models for $3π$ systems at unphysically heavy pion masses with total isospins 0,1, and 2.

## Key claim

The paper derives integral equations for partial-wave projected three-body scattering amplitudes that hold for generic three-body systems of spinless particles, building on the recently derived partial-wave projected one-particle exchange. The authors note that the results apply to systems where the K matrix is either symmetric or asymmetric under particle interchange, and they examine toy models for 3π systems at unphysically heavy pion masses.

## Method

The authors start from relativistic integral equations for three-body amplitudes developed for lattice QCD analyses and perform partial-wave projection to definite total angular momentum J and parity P using the spin-orbit basis of coupled angular momenta. They derive simplified expressions for factorizable short-distance interactions (K₃) in two equivalent formalisms: one symmetric under particle interchange and one asymmetric, providing parameterizations useful for amplitude analysis for the asymmetric case. The numerical solutions are obtained by solving coupled integral equations for toy models of 3π systems with total isospins 0, 1, and 2.

## Context

This work extends the partial-wave projection of the one-particle exchange presented in Jackura (2023) to full three-body scattering amplitudes, building on the relativistic integral equations developed by Hansen and collaborators (2015, 2020) and Jackura (2022). The results enable lattice QCD analyses of three-hadron systems with definite angular momentum and parity, including systems with two-body bound states via the Lehmann-Symanzik-Zimmermann formalism, and provide a framework for analyzing resonances such as the T_cc, X(2370), and spin-exotic π_1.
