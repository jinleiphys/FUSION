# Determination of HQET parameter λ_1 from Inclusive Semileptonic B Meson Decay Spectrum

> We estimate the heavy quark effective theory parameter λ_1 from inclusive semileptonic B-meson decay spectrum. By using recent CLEO double lepton tagging data of B -> X e nu, which shows the lepton momentum as low as 0.6 GeV, we extracted λ_1 \sim -0.58 GeV^2. We also derived \barΛ\sim 0.46 GeV and |V_{cb}| = 0.041 \pm 0.002.

# Determination of HQET parameter λ₁ from Inclusive Semileptonic B Meson Decay Spectrum

## Key claim

The heavy quark effective theory (HQET) parameter λ₁ is extracted as approximately -0.58 GeV² from CLEO double lepton tagging data, but this value depends strongly on the assumed b-quark mass m_b; for m_b = 4.7 GeV the result is λ₁ = -(0.45 ± 0.19) GeV², while for m_b = 4.9 GeV it becomes λ₁ = -(0.70 ± 0.27) GeV², with the central value of -0.58 GeV² corresponding to m_b = 4.8 GeV.

## Method

The authors performed a minimum χ² fit of the theoretical lepton energy spectrum in inclusive B → X e ν decay, including Born, non-perturbative (HQET 1/m_b² corrections), and perturbative α_s corrections, to CLEO double lepton tagging data with lepton momenta from 0.6 GeV up to a cutoff E_QCD = 2.0 GeV. The fit used the b-quark mass m_b = 4.8 ± 0.1 GeV from QCD sum rules, fixed the mass difference m_b - m_c = 3.41 GeV, and normalized the theoretical distribution to the measured branching ratio of 10.49%. The parameter λ₂ was fixed to 0.12 GeV² from the B-B* mass splitting.

## Key numbers

- λ₁ = -(0.58 ± 0.23) GeV² for m_b = 4.8 GeV, with m_b - m_c = 3.41 GeV
- λ₁ = -(0.45 ± 0.19) GeV² for m_b = 4.7 GeV, with m_b - m_c = 3.41 GeV
- λ₁ = -(0.70 ± 0.27) GeV² for m_b = 4.9 GeV, with m_b - m_c = 3.41 GeV
- \barΛ = 0.46 ± 0.022 GeV for m_b = 4.8 GeV
- |V_cb| = 0.041 ± 0.002, extracted from the fitted parameters and the CLEO branching ratio
- Non-perturbative correction is about -4.5% and perturbative correction is about -12% relative to the leading Born approximation

## Context

This work extends earlier determinations of λ₁ from single lepton tagging data by Gremm et al. (1996), which gave λ₁ = -0.19 ± 0.10 GeV², by using CLEO double lepton tagging data that allows model-independent access to lower lepton energies (down to 0.6 GeV). The results are consistent with other theoretical estimates in the range -0.4 to -0.7 GeV² from Ball et al., Hwang et al., and Di Fazio, and also agree with a preliminary CLEO analysis of lepton energy moments giving λ₁ ∼ -0.75 ± 0.20 GeV². The extracted |V_cb| = 0.041 ± 0.002 is consistent with the CLEO ISGW model result of 0.040 ± 0.001 ± 0.002 and the Particle Data Group value of 0.0395 ± 0.0017. The strong dependence of λ₁ on the assumed b-quark mass and the mass ratio m_c/m_b highlights the need for more precise determinations of these inputs to reduce the uncertainty on the kinetic energy parameter.
