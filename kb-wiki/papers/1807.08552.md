# Matrix Models of Strength Distributions

> In this work we use matrix models to study the problem of strength distributions. This is motivated by noticing near exponential fall offs of strengths in calculated magnetic dipole excitations. We emphasize that the quality of the exponential fall offs depend on the parameters in our matrices, especially the relative size of the couplings to the unperturbed level separations. We also find a matrix for which all transitions vanish.

# Matrix Models of Strength Distributions

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that exponential fall-offs of strength distributions, previously observed in realistic calculations of magnetic dipole excitations, can be reproduced in simple matrix models with nearest-neighbor couplings, but the quality of the exponential behavior depends on the ratio of the coupling strength v to the unperturbed level spacing E.

## Method
A Hamiltonian matrix is constructed with equally spaced diagonal energies E_n = n*E (with E = 1 MeV) and a constant nearest-neighbor coupling v. The transition operator T has constant matrix elements of 1 between states differing by one index. The Hamiltonian is diagonalized for 11 basis states, and the strength O^2 from the ground state to each excited state is computed and plotted as ln(O^2) versus excitation energy.

## Key numbers
- For v = 0.5 (weak coupling), the linear fit to ln(O^2) vs. excitation energy is: 7.38 - 5.72 E*
- For v = 1, the linear fit is: 5.44 - 3.84 E*
- For v = 2, the linear fit is: 3.60 - 2.20 E*
- For v = 3 (strong coupling), the linear fit is: 1.42 - 1.34 E*
- Standard deviations from linear fits: 0.2001 for v = 0.5, 0.2089 for v = 1, 0.5379 for v = 2, and 0.5683 for v = 3
- Transition strength from ground to first excited state: 1 (v = 0), 0.6838 (v = 0.5), 0.3833 (v = 1), 0.2195 (v = 2), and 0.0983 (v = 3)
- The fifth excited state has energy exactly 5 MeV regardless of coupling strength

## Context
This work extends earlier realistic shell-model calculations of magnetic dipole strength distributions by Harper and Zamick (2017) and others, where binning revealed approximately exponential decreases of B(M1) strength with excitation energy. The matrix model approach is contrasted with the Breit-Wigner resonance shape derived by Bohr and Mottelson using a different matrix model with constant coupling to many states. The authors note that when all diagonal energies are set to zero (E = 0), all transition matrix elements vanish identically. Future work will explore other matrix models and strength distributions for different operators.
