# Bound, virtual and resonance $S$-matrix poles from the Schrödinger equation

> A general method, which we call the potential $S$-matrix pole method, is developed for obtaining the $S$-matrix pole parameters for bound, virtual and resonant states based on numerical solutions of the Schrödinger equation. This method is well-known for bound states. In this work we generalize it for resonant and virtual states, although the corresponding solutions increase exponentially when $r\to\infty$. Concrete calculations are performed for the $1^+$ ground and the $0^+$ first excited states of $^{14}\rm{N}$, the resonance $^{15}\rm{F}$ states ($1/2^+$, $5/2^+$), low-lying states of $^{11}\rm{Be}$ and $^{11}\rm{N}$, and the subthreshold resonances in the proton-proton system. We also demonstrate that in the case the broad resonances their energy and width can be found from the fitting of the experimental phase shifts using the analytical expression for the elastic scattering $S$-matrix. We compare the $S$-matrix pole and the $R$-matrix for broad $s_{1/2}$ resonance in ${}^{15}{\rm F}$

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the well-known potential $S$-matrix pole method for bound states to resonant and virtual states, despite the fact that the corresponding wave functions increase exponentially at large distances. The method is claimed to be universal for bound, virtual, and resonance states (both narrow and broad) because it uses the relationship between the $S$-matrix residue and the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC), avoiding the need to normalize the exponentially growing wave function directly.

## Method

The authors solve the radial Schrödinger equation numerically with a Woods-Saxon nuclear potential and a Coulomb potential, imposing the boundary condition that the coefficient of the incoming wave vanishes at the matching radius. For bound, virtual, and resonance states, they search for the complex energy (or momentum) at which the Jost function $C_l^{(-)}(k)=0$, which corresponds to a pure outgoing wave asymptotically. The residue of the $S$-matrix at the pole is then extracted by fitting $C_l^{(-)}(k)$ near the pole to a quadratic polynomial, and the single-particle ANC is obtained from the residue via the relation $A_l(k_0)=(-1)^{l+1} i\, b_l^2$.

## Key numbers

- For the $1^+$ ground state of $^{14}\mathrm{N}$ (as $^{13}\mathrm{C}+p$): single-particle ANC $b_{1(gr)} = 4.250\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1/2}$, $S$-matrix residue $A_{gr}=18.061\,i\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$.
- For the $0^+$ excited state of $^{14}\mathrm{N}$: single-particle ANC $b_{1(exc)} = 2.457\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1/2}$, $S$-matrix residue $A_{exc}=6.039\,i\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$.
- For the virtual (antibound) state in the $n\,p$ system ($l=0$, Yukawa potential): energy $\epsilon_{np} = -0.067$ MeV, $S$-matrix residue $A_{np} = -0.072\,i\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$, single-particle ANC $b_0 = 0.268\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1/2}$.
- For the $1/2^+$ resonance in $^{15}\mathrm{F}$ (potential $S$-matrix pole method): $E_0 = 1.198$ MeV, $\Gamma = 0.530$ MeV; using the analytical $S$-matrix fit: $E_0 = 1.194$ MeV, $\Gamma = 0.531$ MeV.
- For the $5/2^+$ resonance in $^{15}\mathrm{F}$ (potential $S$-matrix pole method): $E_0 = 2.780$ MeV, $\Gamma = 0.293$ MeV.
- Residues for $^{15}\mathrm{F}$: $(-0.038 + i\,0.008)\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$ ($1/2^+$) and $(0.015 - i\,0.009)\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$ ($5/2^+$); corresponding single-particle ANCs: $(-0.123 + i\,0.153)\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1/2}$ and $(0.115 + i\,0.067)\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1/2}$.

## Context

This work extends the standard bound-state $S$-matrix pole method to virtual and resonance states, addressing limitations of prior approaches such as the complex scaling method (Aguilar, Balslev, Combes, 1971) and the Fox-Goodwin method of Vertse, Pal, and Balogh (1982), which fails for broad resonances, subthreshold resonances, and virtual states. The method also contrasts with the $R$-matrix approach, which gives model-dependent resonance parameters for broad states; the authors show that their $S$-matrix pole results for the $1/2^+$ resonance in $^{15}\mathrm{F}$ differ significantly from $R$-matrix values (e.g., $E_0=1.400$ MeV, $\Gamma=0.700$ MeV from scattering phase shift). The technique enables model-independent extraction of resonance parameters from experimental phase shifts using the analytical $S$-matrix representation of Baz, and provides ANCs that are crucial for determining astrophysical $S$-factors in peripheral reactions.
