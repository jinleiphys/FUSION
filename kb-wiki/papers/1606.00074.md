# Systematic study of infrared energy corrections in truncated oscillator spaces

> We study the convergence properties of nuclear binding energies and two-neutron separation energies obtained with self-consistent mean-field calculations based on the Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) method with Gogny-type effective interactions. Owing to lack of convergence in a truncated working basis, we employ and benchmark one of the recently proposed infrared energy correction techniques to extrapolate our results to the limit of an infinite model space. We also discuss its applicability to global calculations of nuclear masses.

## Key claim
The paper tests the reliability of a theoretically derived infrared (IR) energy correction technique for extrapolating Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) ground-state energies to an infinite harmonic oscillator basis, finding that the method works well for well-converged systems like 16O but that the underlying assumption of smooth convergence fails for heavier neutron-rich nuclei such as cadmium isotopes, where the extrapolation's accuracy is limited by the lack of an intrinsic momentum cutoff in the Gogny interaction.

## Method
The authors performed self-consistent HFB calculations with the Gogny D1S effective interaction using spherical harmonic oscillator (SHO) single-particle bases with varying numbers of major oscillator shells (N_OS from 11 to 21) and oscillator lengths b. They applied the first-order IR correction formula E_L(L_IR) = A_infinity exp(-2k_infinity L_IR) + E_infinity, where L_IR is the effective hard-wall radius derived from the smallest eigenvalue of the momentum-squared operator in the truncated SHO basis, and selected only UV-converged calculations by imposing a threshold on the UV cutoff Lambda_UV > Lambda_thr. The extrapolation was benchmarked on 16O and then tested on 120Cd and the full cadmium isotopic chain.

## Key numbers
- For 16O, the difference between the converged HFB energy E_min(21) and the IR-extrapolated values was approximately 60 keV across different basis combinations.
- For 16O, using only N_OS=13 (an underconverged basis) with various Lambda_thr values (850, 950, 1050 MeV/c) yielded extrapolated E_infinity values falling very close to E_min(21), with a narrow spread.
- For 16O, the estimated threshold for significant UV-contamination was around Lambda_thr approximately 750 MeV/c.
- For the oxygen isotopic chain, the energy gain from increasing N_OS from 11 to 21 was about 0.06 MeV for 16O and about 0.42 MeV for the drip-line nucleus 28O.
- For the cadmium isotopic chain, the underconvergence of the N_OS=11 basis relative to N_OS=21 was 1.70 MeV for 90Cd and 6.94 MeV for 152Cd.

## Context
This work extends the IR correction technique developed by Furnstahl, Hagen, and Papenbrock (2012) and refined in subsequent papers (2013, 2014, 2015), which had previously been tested on a few simple systems and individual nuclei like 16O, 22O, and 24O. The paper directly tests the validity of an earlier phenomenological extrapolation prescription used in large-scale HFB mass models by Goriely et al. (2009, 2013) and others, finding that the assumption of a smooth halving of energy gains between successive oscillator shells is not supported by the present calculations, particularly near magic nuclei like 130Cd. The results enable systematic error quantification for global nuclear mass calculations with Gogny EDFs and identify the need for an explicit UV regulator when applying IR corrections to interactions without an intrinsic momentum cutoff.
