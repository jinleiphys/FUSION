# Temperature effects on the magnetization of quasi-one-dimensional Peierls distorted materials

> It is shown that temperature acts to disrupt the magnetization of Peierls distorted quasi-one-dimensional materials (Q1DM). The mean-field finite temperature phase diagram for the field theory model employed is obtained by considering both homogeneous and inhomogeneous condensates. The tricritical points of the second order transition lines of the gap parameter and magnetization are explicitly calculated. It is also shown that in the absence of an external static magnetic field the magnetization is always zero, at any temperature. As expected, temperature does not induce any magnetization effect on Peierls distorted Q1DM.

# Temperature Effects on the Magnetization of Quasi-One-Dimensional Peierls Distorted Materials

## Key claim

Temperature acts to disrupt the magnetization of Peierls distorted quasi-one-dimensional materials (Q1DM), and in the absence of an external static magnetic field the magnetization is always zero at any temperature. The tricritical point of the second-order transition line of the gap parameter remains at the same location when inhomogeneous condensates are considered, even under an external Zeeman magnetic field.

## Method

The author employs the Gross-Neveu (GN) model as an effective field theory for the Takayama-Lin-Liu-Maki (TLM) continuum version of the Su-Schrieffer-Heeger (SSH) Hamiltonian describing trans-polyacetylene. The mean-field finite temperature phase diagram is obtained by calculating the renormalized effective potential at high temperature, using a Ginzburg-Landau expansion, and considering both homogeneous and inhomogeneous (kink-like) condensates. The tricritical points of the second-order transition lines for the gap parameter and magnetization are calculated analytically from the coefficients of the effective potential expansion.

## Key numbers

- Critical magnetic field for full polarization in trans-polyacetylene: $B_{0,c} \approx 8.6$ kT (using $\mu_c = \Delta_0/\sqrt{2}$, $\Delta_0 \approx 0.7$ eV, $g \approx 2$)
- Tricritical point for the gap parameter: $\eta_{tc} = 1/(2e) \approx 0.184$, $t_{tc} = 1/\sqrt{e}$ (where $t = T/T_c(0)$)
- Tricritical point for densities and magnetization: $\eta_{tc}^* = 1/(2e^2)$, $t_{tc}^* = 1/e$
- Zero-field critical temperature for gap vanishing: $T_c(0) = (e^{\gamma_E}/\pi)(\Delta_0/k_B)$ where $\gamma_E \approx 0.577$
- Zero-field critical temperature for density vanishing: $T_c^*(0) = T_c(0)/\sqrt{e}$
- Fermi velocity in trans-polyacetylene: $v_F \approx 10^6$ m/s
- Lattice spacing for trans-polyacetylene: $a \cong 1.22$ Å
- Critical doping concentration: $y_c \cong 6\%$

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature analysis of asymmetrically doped polyacetylene under an external magnetic field from Caldas (NPB1) to finite temperature, and builds upon the Gross-Neveu model studies at finite temperature and density by Wolff, Treml, and Klimenko. The results confirm that the tricritical point location is unaffected by inhomogeneous condensates, consistent with findings for symmetric systems by the gnpolymers and Basar groups. The work enables future investigation of transport properties in asymmetrically doped Peierls distorted Q1DM at both zero and finite temperature.
