# Relativistic quantum mechanics: A Dirac's point-form inspired approach

> This paper describes a tentative relativistic quantum mechanics approach inspired by Dirac's point-form, which is based on the physics description on a hyperboloid surface. It is mainly characterized by a non-standard relation of the constituent momenta of some system to its total momentum. Contrary to instant- and front-form approaches, where it takes the form of a 3-dimensional delta function, the relation is given here by a Lorentz-scalar constraint. Thus, in the c.m. frame, the sum of the constituent momenta, which differs from zero off-energy shell, has no fixed direction, in accordance with the absence of preferred direction on a hyperboloid surface. To some extent, this gives rise to an extra degree of freedom entering the description of the system of interest. The development of a consistent formalism within this picture is described. Comparison with other approaches is made.

# Relativistic quantum mechanics: A Dirac's point-form inspired approach

## Key claim

The paper develops a tentative relativistic quantum mechanics approach inspired by Dirac's point-form, where the relation of constituent momenta to total momentum takes the form of a Lorentz-scalar constraint rather than a 3-dimensional delta function; this introduces an extra degree of freedom (the undetermined orientation of the sum of constituent momenta) that must be integrated over, and the formalism is shown to be consistent only if the interaction is constructed to be independent of the total momentum following the Bakamjian-Thomas procedure.

## Method

The authors start from the expression of the 4-momentum operator in terms of free-particle momenta and a Lagrangian interaction density integrated over a hyperboloid surface, then derive a mass equation for a two-body system of scalar constituents exchanging a spinless meson. They show that the wave function must contain a factor δ((p−P)²) and that the orientation vector u = (p−P)/(e−E_P) is conserved by the interaction, allowing a change of variables that separates internal variables k from the total momentum P and the orientation u, leading to a mass operator M² = 4e_k² + 4mṼ that depends only on internal variables.

## Key numbers

- The Lorentz-scalar constraint takes the form (p₁+p₂−P)² − (e₁+e₂−E_P)² = 0, which is equivalent to (p₁+p₂−P)² = 0
- The interaction kernel for one-boson exchange in momentum space is given by Ṽ(k,k') = −√(m/e_k) × g²/(μ²+(k−k')²) × √(m/e_k')
- The normalization integral over the orientation u yields ∫ (dû/4π) × M²/(û·P)² = 1, which is Lorentz invariant
- The mass operator equation is M² = 4e_k² + 4mṼ, where e_k = √(k²+m²)

## Context

This work extends Dirac's (1949) classification of forms of relativistic quantum mechanics by attempting a direct implementation of the point-form on a hyperboloid surface, as opposed to the implementation by Sokolov (1985) and Klink (1998) which actually describes physics on a hyperplane perpendicular to the system's velocity. The approach corrects for the systematic failure of earlier point-form implementations to provide the expected asymptotic power law behavior of form factors. It enables the calculation of form factors in hadronic systems using a formalism where the orientation of constituent momenta relative to total momentum is not fixed but integrated over, following the Bakamjian-Thomas (1953) construction for removing total momentum dependence from the mass operator.
