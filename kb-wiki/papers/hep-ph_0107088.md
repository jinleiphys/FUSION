# Axial structure of the nucleon

> We review the current status of experimental and theoretical understanding of the axial nucleon structure at low and moderate energies. Topics considered include (quasi)elastic (anti)neutrino-nucleon scattering, charged pion electroproduction off nucleons and ordinary as well as radiative muon capture on the proton.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the current experimental and theoretical understanding of the axial nucleon structure at low and moderate energies, but the abstract glosses over a significant discrepancy: the axial mass determined from neutrino scattering (1.026 ± 0.021 GeV) differs from that determined from charged pion electroproduction (1.069 ± 0.016 GeV), leading to an axial radius difference of about 5%.

## Method

The authors review experimental data from (quasi)elastic (anti)neutrino-nucleon scattering and charged pion electroproduction off protons to extract the nucleon axial form factor G_A(t), parameterized by a dipole form with axial mass M_A. They also examine ordinary muon capture (OMC) and radiative muon capture (RMC) on the proton to determine the induced pseudoscalar coupling constant g_P and the induced pseudoscalar form factor G_P(t). Theoretical analyses employ current algebra, PCAC relations, and chiral perturbation theory to connect measured cross sections to the axial form factors.

## Key numbers

- Axial-vector coupling constant: g_A = 1.2673 ± 0.0035 from polarized neutron beta-decay
- Axial mass from neutrino scattering: M_A = (1.026 ± 0.021) GeV (world average)
- Axial mass from pion electroproduction: M_A = (1.069 ± 0.016) GeV (world average)
- Axial mass discrepancy: ΔM_A = (0.043 ± 0.026) GeV
- Axial radius from neutrino scattering: ⟨r_A²⟩^{1/2} = (0.666 ± 0.014) fm
- Axial radius from electroproduction: ⟨r_A²⟩^{1/2} = (0.639 ± 0.010) fm
- Induced pseudoscalar coupling from OMC: g_P = 8.79 ± 1.92 (weighted world average)
- Induced pseudoscalar coupling from RMC: g_P^{RMC} = 12.35 ± 0.88 ± 0.38 (about 40% above OMC value)
- Ortho-para transition rate in liquid hydrogen: λ_OP = (4.1 ± 1.4) × 10⁴ s⁻¹ (experimental), λ_OP^{thy} = (7.1 ± 1.2) × 10⁴ s⁻¹ (theoretical)

## Context

This review extends the work of earlier compilations and analyses of nucleon axial structure, including the Particle Data Group (2000) compilation and the theoretical framework of chiral perturbation theory. The paper identifies an axial mass discrepancy between neutrino scattering and electroproduction determinations that requires resolution, and highlights the need for a new PSI experiment using a hydrogen gas target to measure g_P to one percent accuracy, independent of molecular complications that plagued earlier OMC experiments. The induced pseudoscalar form factor is identified as the least well known of all six electroweak nucleon form factors, motivating proposed experiments at MAMI-B to measure G_P(t) via charged pion electroproduction at low momentum transfer.
