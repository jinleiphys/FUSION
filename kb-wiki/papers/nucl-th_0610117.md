# Nucleon-nucleon wave function with short-range nodes and high-energy deuteron photodisintegration

> We review a concept of the Moscow potential (MP) of the $NN$ interaction. On the basis of this concept we derive by quantum inversion optical partial potentials from the modern partial-wave analysis (PWA) data and deuteron properties. Point-form (PF) relativistic quantum mechanics (RQM) is applied to the two-body deuteron photodisintegration. Calculations of the cross-section angular distributions cover photon energies between 1.1 and 2.5 GeV. Good agreement between our theory and recent experimental data confirms the concept of deep attractive Moscow potential with forbidden $S$- and $P$-states.

## Key claim

The paper argues that recent deuteron photodisintegration data at photon energies between 1.5 and 2.5 GeV confirm the Moscow potential model of the NN interaction, which features deep attractive partial potentials with forbidden S- and P-states, provided that the phase shift data for these partial waves are raised by 180 degrees according to the generalized Levinson theorem.

## Method

The authors reconstructed optical partial NN potentials by quantum inversion from modern partial-wave analysis data up to 1200 MeV for isoscalar states and up to 3 GeV for isovector states, combined with deuteron properties. They then applied point-form relativistic quantum mechanics in the spectator approximation to calculate the deuteron photodisintegration cross-section angular distributions, using the reconstructed Moscow-type potentials with forbidden states to generate the deuteron and final-state np wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy range for cross-section calculations: 1.1 to 2.5 GeV
- Phase shift data used for reconstruction: up to 1200 MeV for isoscalar states and up to 3 GeV for isovector states
- Node positions in S- and P-wave relative motion wave functions: approximately 0.5 fm
- Energies of forbidden states: in the range 300-750 MeV
- Node radii for continuum S- and P-wave functions: 0.5-0.9 fm at the considered energies

## Context

This work extends the Moscow potential concept originally proposed by the authors in 1975 (Neudatchin et al., 1975) for NN scattering, which was analogous to earlier deep attractive alpha-alpha potentials with forbidden states developed for the shell model. The paper contrasts with approaches using mesonic NN potentials (Khar'kov group, 1990s) that required revision of nucleon electromagnetic form factors, and with the hard rescattering model (Radyushkin et al., 1990s) that used empirical extrapolation of np-scattering data. The results enable further testing of the Moscow potential versus repulsive core potentials in electromagnetic reactions with two nucleons, particularly at higher energies.
