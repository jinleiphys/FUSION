# Renormalization Group Flows for Track Function Moments

> Track functions describe the collective effect of the fragmentation of quarks and gluons into charged hadrons, making them a key ingredient for jet substructure measurements at hadron colliders, where track-based measurements offer superior angular resolution. The first moment of the track function, describing the average energy deposited in charged particles, is a simple and well-studied object. However, measurements of higher-point correlations of energy flow necessitate a characterization of fluctuations in the hadronization process, described theoretically by higher moments of the track function. In this paper we derive the structure of the renormalization group (RG) evolution equations for track function moments. We show that energy conservation gives rise to a shift symmetry that allows the evolution equations to be written in terms of cumulants, $κ(N)$, and the difference between the first moment of quark and gluon track functions, $Δ$. The uniqueness of the first three cumulants then fixes their all-order evolution to be DGLAP, up to corrections involving powers of $Δ$, that are numerically suppressed by an effective order in the perturbative expansion for phenomenological track functions. However, at the fourth cumulant and beyond there is non-trivial RG mixing into products of cumulants such as $κ(4)$ into $κ(2)^2$. We analytically compute the evolution equations up to the sixth moment at $\mathcal{O}(α_s^2)$, and study the associated RG flows. These results allow for the study of up to six-point correlations in energy flow using tracks, paving the way for precision jet substructure at the LHC.

## Key claim

The renormalization group evolution equations for track function moments can be written in terms of cumulants, with the first three cumulants having their all-order evolution fixed to DGLAP form up to corrections involving powers of Δ (the difference between the first moment of quark and gluon track functions), which are numerically suppressed by an effective order in the perturbative expansion for phenomenological track functions; however, at the fourth cumulant and beyond there is non-trivial RG mixing into products of cumulants.

## Method

The authors derived the general structure of the renormalization group evolution equations for track function moments using shift symmetry constraints from energy conservation, which allows the evolution to be expressed in terms of cumulants. They analytically computed the evolution equations up to the sixth moment at O(α_s^2) in QCD, using the known timelike twist-2 anomalous dimensions (DGLAP splitting functions) to constrain the diagonal anomalous dimensions, and calculating the remaining mixing anomalous dimensions from the explicit splitting function expressions.

## Key numbers

- The first three cumulants have their all-order evolution fixed to DGLAP form, up to corrections involving powers of Δ, where Δ = T_q(1) - T_g(1) is numerically suppressed by an effective order in the perturbative expansion
- The evolution equations were computed up to the sixth moment at O(α_s^2)
- Results enable the study of up to six-point correlations in energy flow using tracks
- For pure Yang-Mills theory, the shift symmetry constrains the anomalous dimensions such that γ_11 = -γ_2, and similar relations hold for higher moments (e.g., γ_22 = 6γ_2 - 8γ_3 + 3γ_4 at O(α_s^2))

## Context

This work extends the study of track functions introduced by Chang (2013) and the energy flow on subsets of particles framework developed by Chen (2020) and Li (2021). The paper generalizes the standard DGLAP evolution of fragmentation functions to the more complicated case of track function moments, which incorporate correlations between final-state hadrons. The results enable precision jet substructure measurements at the LHC using track-based observables that are sensitive to up to six-point correlations in energy flow, going beyond the previously studied two-point energy correlators.
