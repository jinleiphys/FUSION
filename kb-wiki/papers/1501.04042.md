# Constraining the Eq. of State of Super-Hadronic Matter from Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The equation of state of QCD matter for temperatures near and above the quark-hadron transition (165 MeV) is inferred within a Bayesian framework through the comparison of data from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and from the Large Hadron Collider to theoretical models. State-of- the-art statistical techniques are applied to simultaneously analyze multiple classes of observables while varying 14 independent model parameters. The resulting posterior distribution over possible equations of state is consistent with results from lattice gauge theory.

## Key claim

The Bayesian analysis of heavy-ion collision data from RHIC and LHC constrains the QCD equation of state to be consistent with lattice gauge theory results, but the extracted speed of sound shows a preference for being slightly softer than lattice predictions, with the two parameterizations overlapping significantly.

## Method

The authors performed 1200 full-model runs combining relativistic viscous hydrodynamics for temperatures above 165 MeV with a microscopic hadronic simulation at lower temperatures, varying 14 independent parameters including two that parameterize the speed of sound as a function of energy density. They used a Gaussian-process emulator to interpolate between model runs and a Markov-chain Monte Carlo to sample the posterior distribution weighted by the likelihood of reproducing 30 observables (spectra, elliptic flow, and femtoscopic source sizes) from Au+Au collisions at RHIC (100A GeV + 100A GeV) and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC (1.38A TeV + 1.38A TeV) at two centralities (0-5% and 20-30%).

## Key numbers

* Transition temperature for quark-hadron transition: approximately 165 MeV
* Speed of sound in hadron gas at transition: approximately 0.15
* Model uncertainty assigned to each observable: 6% (changing to 9% only modestly affected results)
* Number of full-model runs: 1200 (1000 semi-random plus 200 from posterior distribution)
* Number of principal components used: 14, capturing over 99.9% of variance
* Elliptic flow correction factor for smoothed initial conditions: 1.10
* Parameter ranges for equation of state: X' from 0.5 to 5, R from -0.9 to 2
* In the limit R approaches -1, the speed of sound has a minimum of zero

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian framework developed by Novak et al. (2013) to simultaneously analyze RHIC and LHC data, building on decades of attempts to extract the equation of state from heavy-ion collisions that were limited by intertwined model parameters. Prior work by Danielewicz (2002) constrained the compressibility of dense hadronic matter at lower temperatures, while lattice calculations by Bazavov (2014) and Ratti (2011) predicted the behavior near the transition. The results enable quantitative validation that matter created in heavy-ion collisions behaves like equilibrated quark-gluon plasma, and provide experimental constraints that can be compared with ongoing lattice calculations.
