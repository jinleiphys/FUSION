# Parton Energy Loss in Two-Stream Plasma System

> The energy loss of a fast parton scattering elastically in a weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma is formulated as an initial value problem. The approach is designed to study an unstable plasma, but it also reproduces the well known result of energy loss in an equilibrium plasma. A two-stream system, which is unstable due to longitudinal chromoelectric modes, is discussed here some detail. In particular, a strong time and directional dependence of the energy loss is demonstrated.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the collisional energy loss of a fast parton in a two-stream quark-gluon plasma is not constant but exhibits strong time and directional dependence, specifically because the plasma is unstable due to longitudinal chromoelectric modes. The abstract glosses over the condition that this result applies only after a sufficiently long time when the chromodynamic field is dominated by the longitudinal chromoelectric component.

## Method

The authors formulate the energy loss of a fast parton scattering elastically in a weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma as an initial value problem using the Wong equations and one-sided Fourier transformations. They compute the chromoelectric field from the linearized Yang-Mills equations, then derive an expression for the energy loss that includes contributions from unstable modes with poles in the upper half-plane of complex frequency. For the two-stream system, they assume the field is dominated by longitudinal chromoelectric modes, invert the dielectric tensor analytically, and perform the remaining integral over momentum numerically using cylindrical coordinates with a cutoff at \(k_{\rm max} = 20\mu\).

## Key numbers

- The parton energy loss per unit length is computed for a system with parameters \(g = |{\bf v}| = 1\), \(|{\bf u}| = 0.9\), and \(C_R = 3\) (gluon test parton).
- The momentum integral is taken over a domain with \(-k_{\rm max} \le k_L \le k_{\rm max}\) and \(0 \le k_T \le k_{\rm max}\) where \(k_{\rm max} = 20\mu\).
- The parameter \(\mu^2 \equiv g^2 n / 2 E_{\bf q}\) is analogous to the Debye mass squared.
- The dispersion relation has four roots \(\omega_{\pm}^2({\bf k})\); the minus mode becomes unstable (imaginary \(\omega_-({\bf k})\)) when \({\bf k}^2 ({\bf k} \cdot {\bf u})^2 < 2 \mu^2 ({\bf k}^2 - ({\bf k} \cdot {\bf u})^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of collisional energy loss in equilibrium quark-gluon plasma (reviewed by Peigne (2008) and in the handbook by Le Bellac) to the case of an unstable, anisotropic plasma. It builds on prior calculations of collisional energy loss in anisotropic plasmas by Romatschke (2004) but incorporates the time evolution of the plasma due to chromomagnetic unstable modes (reviewed by Mrowczynski (2005)). The approach enables future studies of parton energy loss in more realistic, time-evolving plasmas relevant to the early stages of relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
