# Neutron star crust in Voigt approximation II: general formula for electron screening correction for effective shear modulus

> The main contribution to the effective shear modulus of neutron star crust can be calculated within Coulomb solid model and can be approximated by simple analytical expression for arbitrary (even multicomponent) composition. Here I consider correction associated with electron screening within Thomas-Fermi approximation. In particular, I demonstrate that for relativistic electrons (density $ПЃ>10^6$ g\,cm$^{-3}$) this correction can be estimated as $ОґОј_\mathrm{eff}^\mathrm{V}= -9.4\times 10^{-4}\sum_Z n_Z Z^{7/3} e^2/a_\mathrm{e}$, where summation is taken over ion species, $n_Z$ is number density of ions with charge $Ze$, $k_\mathrm{TF}$ is Thomas-Fermi screening wave number. Finally, $a_\mathrm{e}=(4 ПЂn_\mathrm{e}/3)^{-1/3}$ is electron sphere radius. Quasineutrality condition $n_\mathrm{e}=\sum_Z Z n_Z$ is assumed. For example, the screening correction reduces shear modulus by $\sim 9$\% for $Z\sim40$, which is typical for inner layers of neutron star crust.

## Context

This work extends Paper I (Chugunov 2021), which derived the Voigt-averaged effective shear modulus for pure Coulomb interactions in arbitrary (multicomponent or amorphous) matter, by adding the electron screening correction within the Thomas-Fermi approximation. The result generalizes numerical calculations by Kozhberov (2022) for bcc and fcc lattices to arbitrary composition and structure, and is related to work by Khrapak (2019) on transverse sound velocities of Yukawa systems. The formula can be directly applied to model torsional oscillations of neutron star crusts (e.g., for interpreting quasi-periodic oscillations after magnetar flares) and to estimate crustal mountains for gravitational wave emission, though the author notes that the Voigt average provides an upper bound and actual shear moduli may be lower.
