# Improved nuclear matter calculations from chiral low-momentum interactions

> We present new nuclear matter calculations based on low-momentum interactions derived from chiral effective field theory potentials. The current calculations use an improved treatment of the three-nucleon force contribution that includes a corrected combinatorial factor beyond Hartree-Fock that was omitted in previous nuclear matter calculations. We find realistic saturation properties using parameters fit only to few-body data, but with larger uncertainty estimates from cutoff dependence and the 3NF parametrization than in previous calculations.

## Key claim
Nuclear matter saturation properties can be reproduced within theoretical uncertainty bounds using chiral low-momentum interactions fit only to few-body (A ≤ 4) data, but the uncertainty estimates from cutoff dependence and the three-nucleon force parametrization are larger than in previous calculations due to a corrected combinatorial factor beyond Hartree-Fock that was omitted earlier.

## Method
The authors start from the N³LO nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential (EM 500 MeV) and evolve it via renormalization group to low-momentum interactions V_lowk with a smooth regulator. They construct a density-dependent two-body interaction from the three-nucleon force (3NF) by summing one particle over occupied Fermi-sea states, which allows inclusion of all 3NF double-exchange terms beyond Hartree-Fock with corrected combinatorial factors (changed from 1/6 to 1/2). Nuclear matter energy per particle is calculated at Hartree-Fock, second-order, and third-order particle-particle/hole-hole levels using full momentum-dependent single-particle Hartree-Fock propagators.

## Key numbers
- The 3NF couplings c_D and c_E are fit to the ³H binding energy of -8.482 MeV and the ⁴He point charge radius of 1.464 fm.
- For V_lowk interactions with NN cutoff Λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹ and 3N cutoff Λ_3NF = 2.0 fm⁻¹ (EM c_i's), c_D = +1.705 and c_E = -0.109.
- The compressibility K ranges from 175 to 210 MeV across all cases in the third-order results, within the empirical range.
- The cutoff dependence around saturation density is about 3 MeV, small compared to the kinetic energy (≈ 23 MeV) and potential energy (≈ -38 MeV) at that density.
- For NN-only low-momentum interactions from different chiral N³LO potentials, the model dependence around saturation density is approximately 2 MeV for V_lowk and SRG-evolved interactions, compared to about 13 MeV for unevolved N³LO potentials.
- The ⁴He binding energies predicted from the 3NF fits range from -28.22 to -28.45 MeV for V_lowk interactions and -28.53 to -28.71 MeV for SRG interactions.

## Context
This work extends previous nuclear matter calculations (Hebeler et al., previous_NM; nucmatt) by correcting the combinatorial factor for 3NF contributions beyond Hartree-Fock, which was incorrectly set to 1/6 instead of 1/2. The results enable progress toward ab-initio density functional theory for nuclei based on expanding about nuclear matter, analogous to the uniform electron gas approach in quantum chemistry. The authors note that while the theoretical errors are too large to be quantitatively competitive with phenomenological Skyrme energy functionals (Bender), the approach provides a microscopic foundation for developing next-generation functionals guided by EFT/RG.
