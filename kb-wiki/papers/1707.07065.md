# Modeling alignment enhancement for solid polarized targets

> A model of dynamic orientation using optimized radiofrequency (RF) irradiation produced perpendicular to the holding field is developed for the spin-1 system required for tensor-polarized fixed-target experiments. The derivation applies to RF produced close to the Larmor frequency of the nucleus and requires the electron spin-resonance linewidth to be much smaller than the nuclear magnetic resonance frequency. The rate equations are solved numerically to study a semi-saturated steady-state resulting from the two sources of irradiation: microwave from the DNP process and the additional RF used to manipulate the tensor polarization. The steady-state condition and continuous-wave NMR lineshape are found that optimize the spin-1 alignment in the polycrystalline materials used as solid polarized targets in charged-beam nuclear and particle physics experiments.

## Key claim

The paper develops a model showing that tensor polarization in spin-1 solid polarized targets (specifically ND₃) can be significantly increased by applying additional radiofrequency (RF) irradiation during dynamic nuclear polarization (DNP), but this enhancement is conditional on the electron spin-resonance linewidth being much smaller than the nuclear magnetic resonance frequency, and the model applies only to RF produced close to the Larmor frequency of the nucleus.

## Method

The author derives solid-effect rate equations for a coupled electron–deuteron spin system under simultaneous microwave DNP pumping and additional coil-generated RF irradiation, then solves them numerically to find a semi-saturated steady-state condition. The simulation uses a Pake-doublet lineshape augmented with Voigt functions to represent the NMR response, parameterized by empirical data from ND₃ samples polarized at 1 K and 5 T, with Monte Carlo sampling of 500 bins representing CW-NMR scan steps. The model incorporates spin-diffusion effects through a measured line-recovery rate and treats the RF-induced population changes at a single line position to simulate the full NMR lineshape.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron vector polarizations of 80–90% (tensor polarizations 55–75%) have been demonstrated in deuterated butanol and propanediol at temperatures below 300 mK using ³He/⁴He dilution refrigerators.
- Luminosities up to 10³⁶ cm⁻² s⁻¹ can be achieved with ND₃ or ⁶LiD samples polarized at 1 K, but deuteron vector polarization is typically less than 50% (tensor polarization less than 20%).
- In an example fit to ND₃ NMR data at 5 T and 1 K, the measured vector polarization was 42.3% and tensor polarization was 13.1%.
- The Lorentzian width Γ used in the lineshape function is approximately 0.05, and the φ-averaged value of η cos 2φ evaluates to about 0.04.
- The simulation uses 500 bins to represent the 500 RF scan steps of the CW-NMR.

## Context

This work extends earlier discussions of tensor polarization manipulation using saturating RF by Schilcher (rough estimates for ND₃) and de Boer (measurements on 1,2-propanediol at 2.5 T and 0.3 K), whose results were inconsistent with experimental T₂₀ measurements in πd elastic scattering. The model enables a testable RF manipulation and measurement technique for ND₃ polarized at 1 K and 5 T, relevant to proposed Jefferson Lab experiments measuring tensor-polarized structure functions in deep inelastic scattering and deuteron alignment effects in short-range correlations.
