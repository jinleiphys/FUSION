# Coexistence of order and chaos at critical points of first-order quantum phase transitions in nuclei

> We study the interplay between ordered and chaotic dynamics at the critical point of a generic first-order quantum phase transition in the interacting boson model of nuclei. Classical and quantum analyses reveal a distinct behavior of the coexisting phases. While the dynamics in the deformed phase is robustly regular, the spherical phase shows strongly chaotic behavior in the same energy intervals. The effect of collective rotations on the dynamics is investigated.

## Key claim

At the critical point of a first-order quantum phase transition in the interacting boson model (IBM) of nuclei, the dynamics in the deformed phase remains robustly regular even at energies far above the barrier, while the spherical phase becomes strongly chaotic at energies as low as about one-third of the barrier height. This separation of ordered and chaotic dynamics persists up to the barrier top and is more pronounced for higher potential barriers.

## Method

The authors constructed a critical IBM Hamiltonian with separate intrinsic and collective parts, designed so that the spherical and deformed condensates are degenerate at zero energy. They performed classical analysis using Glauber coherent states in the large-N limit, computing Poincaré sections for L=0 dynamics at three barrier heights (low, medium, high) and three energy values relative to the barrier. Quantum analysis employed Peres lattices of eigenstates for N=50 and N=80 bosons with angular momenta L=0,2,3,4, examining the effect of adding O(3), O(5), and O(6) rotational terms to the intrinsic Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- Barrier heights studied: V_b/h_2 = 0.04, 0.10, 0.16 for beta_0 = 1.0, 1.3, 1.5 respectively (compared to V_b/h_2 = 0.0009 in prior work by Whelan and Alhassid (1993))
- Poincaré sections analyzed at energies E_1 = V_b/4, E_2 = V_b, and E_3 = 4 V_b
- Spherical-phase chaos onset occurs at approximately E ≈ V_b/3
- Quantum calculations used N=50 and N=80 bosons with angular momenta L=0,2,3,4
- The potential barrier height formula: V_b = h_2 [1 - (1 + beta_0^2)^{1/2}]^2 / 4

## Context

This work extends studies of quantum chaos in the IBM by Whelan and Alhassid (1993), who used a simplified Hamiltonian with an extremely low barrier (V_b/h_2 = 0.0009), to generic first-order QPTs with realistic high barriers. The results demonstrate that the coexistence of order and chaos is a robust feature of first-order critical points, with the deformed phase supporting well-defined rotational bands (K=0 and K=2) that are preserved by O(3) and O(5) collective terms but suppressed by the O(6) term. This enables future studies of how rotational degrees of freedom affect the ordered and chaotic dynamics at nuclear shape phase transitions.
