# Spinodal Instabilities of Baryon-Rich Quark-gluon Plasma in the PNJL Model

> Using the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinia (PNJL) model, we study the spinodal instability of a baryon-rich quark-gluon plasma in the linear response theory. We find that the spinodal unstable region in the temperature and density plane shrinks with increasing wave number of the unstable mode and is also reduced if the effect of Polyakov loop is not included. In the small wave number or long wavelength limit, the spinodal boundaries in both cases of with and without the Polyakov loop coincide with those determined from the isothermal spinodal instability in the thermodynamic approach. Also, the vector interactions among quarks is found to suppress unstable modes of all wave numbers. Moreover, the growth rate of unstable modes initially increases with the wave number but is reduced when the wave number becomes large. Including the collisional effect from quark scattering via the linearized Boltzmann equation, we further find that it decreases the growth rate of unstable modes of all wave numbers. Relevance of these results to relativistic heavy ion collisions is discussed.

## Key claim

Using the PNJL model with linear response theory, the spinodal unstable region in the temperature-density plane shrinks with increasing wave number and is reduced when the Polyakov loop effect is omitted; the growth rate of unstable modes initially increases with wave number but decreases at large wave numbers, and including collisional effects from quark scattering further reduces the growth rate for all wave numbers.

## Method

The authors employed the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model in the mean-field approximation to study spinodal instabilities of baryon-rich quark-gluon plasma via linear response theory. They computed quark correlators (scalar-scalar, scalar-current, and current-current) in the imaginary time formalism under the quasi-particle approximation, then solved the determinant condition for non-zero solutions of the linearized equations to obtain spinodal boundaries and dispersion relations for unstable modes. The effect of collisions was further studied using the linearized Boltzmann equation.

## Key numbers

- The highest temperature of the spinodal instability region is about 68 MeV in the NJL model and about 120 MeV in the PNJL model (both with G_V=0).
- For G_V=0.2 G_S in the NJL model, unstable modes with k >= 0.2 fm^{-1} disappear.
- For a quark matter of net quark density n_q=0.7 fm^{-3} and temperature T=70 MeV with G_V=0, the growth rate peaks at k=0.15 fm^{-1}, corresponding to a clump size of about 40 fm.
- The typical growth rate for G_V=0 is 0.01 fm^{-1}, implying a growth time of about 100 fm.
- The momentum cutoff Lambda used in the PNJL model is 0.6023 GeV, with scalar coupling G_S*Lambda^2=3.67 and KMT interaction K*Lambda^5=12.36.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of spinodal instabilities in quark matter using the NJL model by incorporating the Polyakov loop to account for confinement-deconfinement effects, following the PNJL model framework of Fukushima (2003) and Roessner (2006). The results connect to earlier hydrodynamic approaches to spinodal instabilities by Herold (2014) and provide a microscopic basis for understanding density fluctuations in baryon-rich QGP produced in heavy-ion collisions, such as those studied by the STAR Collaboration's beam energy scan program. The findings suggest that spinodal instabilities may be difficult to observe directly in heavy-ion collisions due to their long growth times relative to typical collision lifetimes.
