# Neutrinoless double beta decay studied with configuration mixing methods

> We study neutrinoless double beta decay of several isotopes with state-of-the-art beyond self-consistent mean field methods to compute the nuclear matrix elements (NME). Generating coordinate method with particle number and angular momentum projection (GCM+PNAMP) is used for finding mother and granddaughter states and evaluating transition operators between different nuclei. We analyze explicitly the role of the deformation, pairing and configuration mixing in the evaluation of the NME.

## Key claim

The paper claims that beyond-mean-field methods (GCM+PNAMP) using the Gogny D1S energy density functional can reliably compute neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear matrix elements, but the final NME values depend critically on configuration mixing and are systematically smaller than those obtained assuming spherical symmetry, except for the A=96 decay.

## Method

The authors use the Generating Coordinate Method with particle number and angular momentum projection (GCM+PNAMP) based on the Gogny D1S energy density functional. They compute ground states of mother and granddaughter nuclei by restoring rotational and particle number symmetries and mixing axial quadrupole deformations. The 0νββ nuclear matrix elements are evaluated in the closure approximation, neglecting the tensor term, and the dependence on deformation is studied by computing the GT and Fermi matrix element kernels as functions of the quadrupole deformation of initial and final nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Relative errors for masses and charge radii are less than 1.5% for all isotopes studied.
- Theoretical total Gamow-Teller strengths S_{-} for mother nuclei (quenched by factor 0.74^2): 13.55 for 48Ca, 20.97 for 76Ge, 50.32 for 150Nd.
- Experimental total Gamow-Teller strengths S_{-}: 14.4±2.2 for 48Ca, 19.89 for 76Ge (no uncertainty given), no experimental value listed for 150Nd.
- Maximum GT NME values from the deformation-dependent kernel: 3.3 for A=48, 5.7 for A=76, 5.8 for A=150.
- Constant potentials used for the simplified GT operator: V_{GT,0} = -1.5 for A=48, -2.3 for A=76, -2.2 for A=150.

## Context

This work extends beyond-mean-field methods to 0νββ decay, complementing calculations by the Interacting Shell Model (Caurier, Menendez, Poves 2008; Menendez, Poves 2009) and proton-neutron QRPA (Simkovic, Pantis 1999; Simkovic, Faessler 2008; Kortelainen, Suhonen 2007; Suhonen, Civitarese 2010). It also relates to PHFB calculations with schematic interactions (Chaturvedi, Chandra 2008; Chandra 2009) and IBM calculations (Barea, Iachello 2009). The method enables systematic studies of deformation and pairing effects on NMEs for isotopes relevant to experiments such as CANDLES (48Ca), GERDA (76Ge), and SNO+ (150Nd), and highlights the importance of configuration mixing for reliable NME predictions.
