# Regularization and renormalization in effective field theories of the nucleon-nucleon interaction

> Some form of nonperturbative regularization is necessary if effective field theory treatments of the NN interaction are to yield finite answers. We discuss various regularization schemes used in the literature. Two of these methods involve formally iterating the divergent interaction and then regularizing and renormalizing the resultant amplitude. Either a (sharp or smooth) cutoff can be introduced, or dimensional regularization can be applied. We show that these two methods yield different results after renormalization. Furthermore, if a cutoff is used, the NN phase shift data cannot be reproduced if the cutoff is taken to infinity. We also argue that the assumptions which allow the use of dimensional regularization in perturbative EFT calculations are violated in this problem. Another possibility is to introduce a regulator into the potential before iteration and then keep the cutoff parameter finite. We argue that this does not lead to a systematically-improvable NN interaction.

# Regularization and renormalization in effective field theories of the nucleon-nucleon interaction

## Key claim
Cutoff regularization and dimensional regularization yield inequivalent renormalized amplitudes when applied nonperturbatively to the nucleon-nucleon interaction, and neither scheme provides a systematically improvable effective field theory for the NN potential. The bound state at unnaturally low energies in the S-wave channels violates the assumptions that allow dimensional regularization to work in perturbative EFT calculations.

## Method
The authors analyze a simple effective field theory for NN scattering in the ¹S₀ channel where the potential is truncated to contact terms V(p',p) = C + C₂(p² + p'²). They iterate this potential via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation and compare two regularization schemes: introducing a sharp or smooth cutoff, and applying dimensional regularization. The renormalized amplitudes are fixed using the experimental scattering length a and effective range rₑ. They also examine the consequences of introducing a regulator into the potential before iteration and keeping the cutoff finite.

## Key numbers
- The dimensional regularization result yields 1/T_DR(k) = -(M/4π)(1/(-a - ½ a² rₑ k²) - ik) for all a and rₑ.
- The cutoff regularization result yields 1/T_cutoff(k) = -(M/4π)(-1/a + ½ rₑ k² - ik) but only if rₑ ≤ 0; if rₑ > 0 the renormalization cannot be performed with real coefficients C and C₂ when the cutoff is taken to infinity.
- A theorem due to Wigner gives the bound rₑ ≤ 2(R - R²/a + R³/(3a²)) for a potential that vanishes beyond range R, implying a minimal range below which the effective range cannot be reproduced.

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory approach to the NN interaction originally suggested by Weinberg (1990) and developed by Ordonez et al. (1996), Kaplan et al. (1996), and others. It shows that the nonperturbative iteration required to generate the deuteron bound state and the ¹S₀ quasi-bound state creates regularization ambiguities absent in perturbative chiral perturbation theory. The paper identifies a fundamental obstacle to constructing a systematically improvable EFT for the NN interaction, motivating later developments such as the power-divergence subtraction scheme of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) and the pionful EFT approaches that followed.
