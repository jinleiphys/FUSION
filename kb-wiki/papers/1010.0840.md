# Cluster Expansion of Cold Alpha Matter Energy

> In the cluster expansion framework of Bose liquids we calculate analytical expressions of the two-body, three-body and four-body diagrams contributing to the g.s. energy of an infinite system of neutral alpha-particles at zero-temperature, interacting via the strong nuclear forces exclusively. This is analytically tractable by assuming a density dependent two-body correlation function of Gaussian type. For the alpha-alpha potential we adopt the phenomenological Ali-Bodmer interaction and semi-microscopic potentials obtained from the Gogny force parametrizations. We show that under such assumptions we achieve a rapid convergence in the cluster expansion, the four-body contributions to the energy being smaller than the two-body and three-body contributions by at least an order of magnitude.

## Key claim

The cluster expansion of the ground-state energy of cold alpha matter converges rapidly when a density-dependent Gaussian two-body correlation function is assumed, with four-body contributions smaller than two- and three-body contributions by at least an order of magnitude, but this result depends on the specific choice of correlation function and the neglect of Pauli blocking effects.

## Method

The authors applied the cluster expansion method for Bose liquids to an infinite, zero-temperature system of neutral alpha particles interacting via strong nuclear forces only. They adopted a Jastrow-type two-body correlation function of Gaussian form, with its width parameter determined by a normalization condition that makes it density-dependent, and used the Ali-Bodmer (AB) potential as well as two Gogny-force-derived (D1 and D1N) alpha-alpha potentials. Analytical expressions were derived for the two-body, three-body, and four-body (ring, diagonal, opened, and connected) diagram contributions to the energy per particle, using a folding technique with delta-function kernels to reduce the multi-dimensional integrals.

## Key numbers

* The two-body energy component \(E_2\) has a shallow minimum at a density approximately two times the saturation density of normal nuclear matter (for the Ali-Bodmer potential).
* The three-body energy \(E_3\) is weakly attractive at low density and becomes strongly repulsive with increasing density.
* The four-body contributions to the total energy are smaller than the two-body and three-body contributions by at least an order of magnitude.
* For the Ali-Bodmer potential, the equilibrium point lies at normal nuclear matter density; for the Gogny D1 interaction, the equilibrium occurs at almost one tenth of that density.
* The Ali-Bodmer potential parameters are: \(V_A = 130\) MeV, \(V_R = 475\) MeV, \(\mu_A = 0.475\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(\mu_R = 0.7\) fm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the cluster expansion approach of Bose liquids (Aviles 1958) to alpha matter, using a Gaussian correlation function that avoids the unphysical collapse seen in earlier work by Misicu and Carstoiu (2010) when using Pandharipande-Bethe equations. The results contrast with the expectation from Ropke and collaborators that Pauli blocking should dissolve alpha clusters above a Mott density between one-fifth and one-third of nuclear matter saturation density. The analytical expressions enable future studies of the equation of state of alpha matter for supernova simulations, and the folding method is noted as previously applied to heavy-ion potential calculations by Carstoiu (1992).
