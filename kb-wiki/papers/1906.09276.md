# Aspects of heavy flavor jet physics in heavy ion collisions

> n these proceedings I discuss several recent developments in the physics of heavy flavor jets in heavy ion collisions. i) The dijet mass modification in nucleus-nucleus reactions has been proposed as a new observable with enhanced sensitivity to parton energy loss in nuclear matter. It also enables more precise studies of heavy quark mass effects on parton shower formation. ii) Computational techniques from soft-collinear effective theory have allowed us to bridge the gap between high energy and heavy ion QCD phenomenology. I show the first application of the semi-inclusive jet function formalism to heavy flavor jet production in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at the LHC. iii) Last but not least, central to the theoretical calculations of heavy flavor jets is the accurate theoretical description of in-medium parton showers. A formalism to compute branching processes in nuclear matter to any desired order in opacity has been developed and illustrative numerical results are presented.

# Aspects of heavy flavor jet physics in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The dijet invariant mass modification in nucleus-nucleus collisions is proposed as a new observable with enhanced sensitivity to parton energy loss, where suppression can reach a factor of 10 (compared to a factor of 2 for inclusive jets), and heavy quark mass effects are more pronounced than in traditional dijet imbalance measurements. The formalism for computing in-medium branching processes to any desired order in opacity has been developed, with second-order corrections to splitting functions reaching 30-70% in certain kinematic regions.

## Method
The author applies semi-inclusive jet function (SiJF) formalism from soft-collinear effective theory to compute heavy flavor jet cross sections in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at the LHC, incorporating both initial-state cold nuclear matter energy loss and medium-induced splitting kernels. For in-medium parton showers, lightcone wavefunction techniques combined with recurrence relations are used to compute all four lowest-order branching processes (q→qg, g→gg, q→gq, g→qq̅) to arbitrary order in opacity, with a Mathematica code solving the resulting triangular matrix equation.

## Key numbers
- Dijet invariant mass suppression can be as large as a factor of 10 at both RHIC and the LHC, compared to typical inclusive jet suppression of a factor of 2
- A 10% variation in the coupling g between jet and medium can lead to more than a factor of 2 difference in the nuclear modification factor R_AA for dijet mass
- For a 100 GeV jet, second-order in opacity corrections to light parton splitting functions are negative and of order 30-70% in the small x and large x regions, and positive near x~0.5
- Heavy quark second-order in opacity corrections are noticeably smaller than light quark corrections in most of the available phase space
- The calculation of second-order corrections uses 10 times larger number of random samples than the leading order calculation in the VEGAS algorithm

## Context
This work extends the traditional heavy meson and light jet energy loss approach (Djordjevic 2003) by applying semi-inclusive jet functions derived for heavy flavor jets in vacuum (Dai 2018) and in-medium splitting kernels to first order in opacity (Kang 2016). The dijet mass observable (Kang 2018) provides an alternative to groomed soft-dropped momentum sharing distributions (Li 2017) for studying heavy quark mass effects. The higher-order opacity formalism (Sievert 2018) addresses the need for more precise in-medium splitting functions beyond the soft gluon approximation, enabling improved Monte-Carlo event generators for heavy ion physics and precision diagnostics of nuclear matter properties.
