# Heavy Quarkonia in the Light-Front Quark Model

> We present recent progress in constructing heavy quarkonia light-front wave functions from the light-front quark model, a relativistic framework well-suited for describing partonic structure and hadronic form factors. In this proceeding, we discuss the recent application of the model to the $M1$ radiative transition such as $J/ψ\to η_c γ$ among other transitions. Additionally, we compare our results on the transition form factor with available lattice QCD data. These show our efforts to deepen our understanding of the structure of heavy quarkonia from light-front model perspective.

# Heavy quarkonia in the light-front quark model

## Key claim
The light-front wave functions constructed from a variational approach combined with Melosh rotation, constrained by mass spectra and decay constants, yield transition form factors for M1 radiative decays that are consistent across different polarization vectors only when the physical meson mass M is replaced by the invariant mass M0 in the matrix element integrand.

## Method
The authors solved a QCD-motivated effective Hamiltonian with confinement, color Coulomb, and hyperfine interactions in the rest frame to obtain radial wave functions, then boosted them to the light-front frame using Melosh rotations to construct light-front wave functions for heavy quarkonia. The transition form factors for M1 radiative decays such as J/ψ → ηcγ were computed using the plus and transverse components of the electromagnetic current in the q+=0 frame, with the mass replacement M → M0 applied in the integrand to ensure consistency between different polarization choices.

## Key numbers
- Coupling constant g_{VPγ} for J/ψ → ηcγ: 0.745(15) GeV^{-1}, compared to experimental value 0.684(85) GeV^{-1} (ParticleDataGroup 2024)
- Coupling constant g_{VPγ} for ψ′ → ηcγ: -0.0605(37) GeV^{-1}, compared to experimental value -0.040(3) GeV^{-1}
- Coupling constant g_{VPγ} for Υ → ηbγ: -0.1279(9) GeV^{-1}
- Coupling constant g_{VPγ} for Υ′ → ηbγ: 0.0049(1) GeV^{-1}, compared to experimental value 0.0057(6) GeV^{-1}

## Context
This work extends the light-front quark model approach of Arifi (2022) and Arifi (2024) to heavy quarkonia and Bc mesons, building on the Bakamjian-Thomas construction (Bakamjian 1953) for treating meson states as noninteracting quark-antiquark systems. The results are compared with lattice QCD calculations (Dudek 2006), the Basis Light-Front Quantization approach (Li 2018), and the Godfrey-Isgur quark model (Godfrey 1985). The validated wave functions enable future computation of hadronic form factors at high momentum transfer and provide guidance for lattice QCD simulations and experiments.
