# $K^{+}$ and $K^{-}$ potentials in hadronic matter can be observed

> We aim to show that K+ and K- spectra at low transverse momentum measured in light symmetric systems at around 2AGeV depend strongly on the K potential. The ratio of the spectra can allow therefore for a direct determination of the strength of the K+ as well as that of the K- potential in a hadronic environment.

# K⁺ and K⁻ potentials in hadronic matter can be observed

## Key claim

The ratio of K⁺ to K⁻ transverse momentum spectra at low transverse momentum in light symmetric systems at around 2 AGeV depends strongly on the kaon potential and is nearly independent of other poorly known input quantities such as the lifetime of the Δ resonance or in-medium modifications of cross sections. This ratio can therefore serve as a direct observable for determining the strength of both the K⁺ and K⁻ potentials in hadronic matter, provided the K⁺ potential can be independently constrained.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate K⁺ and K⁻ production in light symmetric systems at around 2 AGeV. They separated K⁻ mesons into two classes: those produced directly from reactions such as BB → BB K⁺ K⁻, and those produced from secondary collisions involving πY or BY → K⁻. The kaon-nucleon potential strength was varied by multiplying it by a constant factor x, and the logarithmic ratio of the K⁺ to K⁻ transverse momentum spectra was examined as a function of transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

- The logarithmic ratio of K⁺ to K⁻ pT spectra is nearly constant without a kaon-nucleon potential (x = 0), but the slope changes sharply in the low momentum region when the potential is switched on.
- When the K⁺N potential is fixed at its theoretical prediction (x = 1) and the K⁻N potential is varied, the slope of the ratio still varies by a factor of two, making it a measurable quantity.
- Kaons observed in the detector are produced at densities around normal nuclear matter density, independent of the potential strength.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of sub-threshold kaon production (Aichelin, 1986) by addressing the problem that most observables depend simultaneously on the K⁻ potential and on several poorly known input quantities. The authors propose the K⁺/K⁻ spectral ratio as a "smoking gun" signal that isolates the potential dependence. This enables future experimental determination of the K⁻N potential strength in dense hadronic matter, which has been difficult to extract from other observables.
