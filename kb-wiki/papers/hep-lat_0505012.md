# Observation of String Breaking in QCD

> We numerically investigate the transition of the static quark-antiquark string into a static-light meson-antimeson system. Improving noise reduction techniques, we are able to resolve the signature of string breaking dynamics for n_f=2 lattice QCD at zero temperature. This result can be related to properties of quarkonium systems. We also study short-distance interactions between two static-light mesons.

## Key claim

The authors numerically demonstrate string breaking in QCD with two dynamical sea quark flavors at zero temperature, resolving both explicit mixing between the static quark-antiquark string and the static-light meson-antimeson system, and implicit mixing within the meson-meson correlator. The result is exploratory, restricted to one sea quark mass (slightly below the physical strange quark mass) and one lattice spacing.

## Method

The study uses lattice QCD with $n_f=2$ Wilson fermions on a $24^3\times 40$ lattice at $\beta=5.6$ and $\kappa=0.1575$, corresponding to $a\approx 0.083$ fm. A $2\times 2$ correlation matrix is constructed from Wilson loops ($C_{QQ}$), transition elements ($C_{QB}$), and static-light meson correlators ($C_{BB}$), with all-to-all light quark propagators computed via truncated eigenmode approximation (TEA), stochastic estimation (SET), and hopping parameter acceleration (HPA). APE and Wuppertal smearing are applied to enhance ground state overlaps, and an improved fat link static action reduces Wilson loop errors by factors of about five.

## Key numbers

- String breaking distance: $r_c \approx 15\,a \approx 2.5\,r_0 \approx 1.25$ fm, with $r_0 \approx 0.5$ fm
- Lattice spacing: $a^{-1} \approx 2.37$ GeV, $a \approx 0.083$ fm from $r_0 = 6.009(53)a$
- Pion mass: $m_\pi a = 0.276(5)$, with $m_\pi/m_V = 0.704(5)$
- Energy gap at string breaking: $\Delta E_c r_c = 0.33(5)$
- Wilson loop error reduction factor: about five from improved static action
- Disconnected contribution error reduction factor: about three from HPA

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of string breaking in QCD (Bali 2000, Aoki 1998, Duncan 2000, Bernard 2001) that lacked compelling evidence due to poor signal-to-noise ratios and insufficient overlap with the $B\overline{B}$ ground state. It also builds on toy model demonstrations of string breaking in $SU(2)$ gauge theory with scalar fields (Philipsen 1998, Knechtli 1998) and adjoint sources (deForcrand 1999). The results enable quantitative studies of quarkonium properties and open the way for future investigations at lighter sea quark masses and finer lattice spacings.
