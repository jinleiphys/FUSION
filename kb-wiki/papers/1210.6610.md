# Parton shower evolution in medium and nuclear modification of photon-tagged jets in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC

> We study the medium modification of jets correlated with large transverse momentum photons at the LHC via a transport and perturbative QCD hybrid model which incorporates the contributions from both elastic collisions and radiative energy loss experienced by the parton showers. Calculations are performed for the modification of the photon-tagged jet yield, the photon-jet energy imbalance, and the azimuthal distribution of away-side jets. The modifications of photon-tagged jets with different $x_T = p_{T, J}/p_{T, γ}$ values are studied and they exhibit different centrality and jet cone size dependence due to traversing different medium lengths and density profiles. We further investigate the influence of transverse and longitudinal jet transport coefficients on the nuclear modification of photon-tagged jet production and jet shape observables.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a transport and perturbative QCD hybrid model can describe the medium modification of photon-tagged jets in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, but the results depend on the assumed relative sizes of the longitudinal and transverse jet transport coefficients, which are not uniquely determined by the data.

## Method

The authors developed a Monte-Carlo transport and perturbative QCD hybrid model that simulates parton shower evolution in a dense QCD medium. Elastic collisions are treated via a multiple soft scattering limit with Gaussian momentum exchange controlled by transport coefficients, while radiative energy loss is simulated using the single gluon emission spectrum from the higher-twist approach. The initial photon-jet pairs are generated from PYTHIA8.1 for p+p collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, and the space-time evolution of the bulk QGP medium is provided by a (2+1)D viscous hydrodynamics model (VISH2+1) with Glauber initial conditions.

## Key numbers

- The gluon transport coefficient is fitted to be \(\hat{q}_A = 6.5\) GeV²/fm at the hydrodynamics initial time \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/c at the LHC (corresponding to \(\hat{q}_A = 2.9\) GeV²/fm at the same initial time at RHIC).
- The nuclear modification factor \(I_{AA} \approx 0.7\) in most central (0-10%) Pb+Pb collisions is used as the fitting target.
- The average fractional momentum loss \(\langle \Delta x_T \rangle\) increases from peripheral to central collisions, with values shown as a function of participant number \(N_{\rm part}\).
- Jets with smaller \(x_T\) values (\(x_T = [0.5, 0.6]\)) are found to have traversed about a few fm longer distance than larger \(x_T\) jets (\(x_T = [0.9, 1.0]\)).
- The energy loss of a full jet (cone size \(R=0.3\)) is increased by almost a factor of 2 when the contribution from elastic collisions is taken into account compared to radiative energy loss alone.

## Context

This work extends the study of jet quenching from single hadron and dihadron observables to full jet observables tagged by photons, following earlier calculations by Wang (1996) that proposed photon-jet correlations as a "golden" channel for studying jet quenching. The model builds on the higher-twist approach for radiative energy loss developed by Wang (2001) and Majumder (2009), and uses the same transport coefficients framework as Qin (2012). The results enable future quantitative extraction of jet transport coefficients from LHC data and motivate inclusion of medium response to jet propagation, as noted by Casalderrey-Solana (2012) and others.
