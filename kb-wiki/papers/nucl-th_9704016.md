# EMC effect from the QMC and the QHD descriptions

> The density dependence of the bag parameters is studied in a framework which links the Quark-Meson Coupling model and the field theory of hadrons for nuclear matter description. The EMC effect is treated in the dynamical rescaling hypothesis by using the density dependent bag radius and the Local Density Approximation. Our results are in good agreement with the expected values of the rescaling parameter.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a coherent description linking the Quark-Meson Coupling (QMC) model and Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) can generate density-dependent MIT bag parameters (radius R, bag constant B, and zero-point energy parameter z0) that reproduce the expected rescaling parameter for the EMC effect, but only when using the Zimanyi-Moszkowski hadronic model (M4) and a specific parameter set (set II with mu > 0); other hadronic models (M1-M3) fail to reproduce the qualitative behavior.

## Method

The authors impose two equilibrium conditions linking QHD and QMC descriptions: equality of the effective nucleon masses from both models and equality of the internal bag pressure with the external hadronic pressure at each baryon density. They treat the derivatives lambda = dB/d(sigma_bar) and mu = dz0/d(sigma_bar) as constant parameters, solve the coupled equations for four different hadronic models (M1: Walecka QHD-I; M2, M3 from Feldmeier and Lindner; M4: Zimanyi-Moszkowski), and then apply the Local Density Approximation with Woods-Saxon density distributions to compute average bag radii for finite nuclei and the corresponding dynamical rescaling parameter for the EMC effect.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: rho = 0.15 fm^{-3}; binding energy per nucleon: E_b = 16 MeV
- Free nucleon mass: M_N = 939 MeV; free quark mass: m_q = 10 MeV; free bag radius: R_0 = 0.6 fm
- Isothermal compressibility at saturation ranges between 100-300 MeV (from Blaizot et al.)
- For the EMC analysis: Lambda_QCD = 0.25 GeV, mu^2 = 0.66 GeV^2, Q^2 = 20 GeV^2 (following Jin and Jennings)
- Best fit for the rescaling parameter: 1% discrepancy for ^{197}Au, approximately 4% discrepancy for ^{118}Sn and ^{208}Pb
- For model M4 with set II, the bag constant B as a function of sigma_bar is fitted by a quadratic polynomial with coefficients A_0 = 184,723 MeV, A_1 = -186.42 MeV, A_2 = 112.37 MeV

## Context

This work extends the approach of Saito and Thomas (1994) who established a relationship between QMC and the Walecka model, and addresses the question raised by Jin and Jennings (1996) about fixing the MIT bag constant at its free-space value. The authors compare their results with the dynamical rescaling hypothesis of Close, Roberts, and Ross (1983) and with the Direct Coupling Model and Scaling Model of Jin and Jennings (1996). The work enables a self-consistent calculation of the EMC effect where the rescaling parameter is derived from the meson background properties rather than introduced phenomenologically, and suggests that including N-Z asymmetry and rho-meson contributions could improve results for light nuclei.
