# Dark Matter Influence on Quarkyonic Stars: A Relativistic Mean Field Analysis

> The formulation of quarkyonic matter consists of treating both quarks and nucleons as quasi-particles, where a cross-over transition occurs between the two phases. This work is based upon some of the early ideas of quark matter. It can satisfy the different observational constraints on the neutron star (NS), such as its maximum mass and the canonical radius. In addition, we put an extra component inside the NS known as Dark Matter (DM) because it is trapped due to its immense gravitational potential. In this work, we explore the impact of fermionic DM on the structure of the NS. The equation of state (EOS) is derived for the NS with the quarkyonic matter by assuming that nucleons and quarks are in equilibrium, followed by the relativistic mean-field (RMF) formalism. The recently modeled two parameterizations, such as G3 and IOPB-I, are taken to calculate the various macroscopic properties of the NS. The three unknown parameters such as the transition density ($n_t$), the QCD confinement scale ($Λ_{\rm sc}$), and the DM Fermi momentum ($k_f^{\rm DM}$) are varied to obtain the NS properties. The quarkyonic matter stiffens the EOS while DM softens it. The mutual combination provides us with good theoretical predictions for the magnitude of macroscopic properties consistent with the different observational results. Also, one can estimate the parameters of the DM admixed quarkyonic star with different statistical analyses, which can be further used to explore the other properties of the quarkyonic star.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including fermionic dark matter (DM) in quarkyonic neutron stars, modeled via a Higgs portal interaction with nucleons and quarks, can produce macroscopic properties consistent with observational constraints, but only when the softening effect of DM is balanced against the stiffening effect of the quarkyonic phase; the specific DM Fermi momentum and quarkyonic transition parameters must be tuned to match observations.

## Method

The authors construct the equation of state (EOS) for neutron star matter by combining a relativistic mean-field (RMF) description of baryonic matter (using the G3 and IOPB-I parameter sets) with a non-interacting quark gas for the quarkyonic phase and a fermionic DM component interacting via Higgs exchange. They impose chemical equilibrium among nucleons, quarks, and leptons, charge neutrality, and baryon number conservation, then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain mass, radius, tidal deformability, and moment of inertia for varying transition density (n_t), QCD confinement scale (Λ_cs), and DM Fermi momentum (k_f^DM).

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass for the G3 model with n_t = 0.3 fm^{-3}, Λ_cs = 800 MeV, and k_f^DM = 0.03 GeV is 2.54 M_⊙; for the FSU model under the same conditions it is 1.93 M_⊙.
- The canonical radius R_{1.4} for G3 under those parameters is 13.24 km; for IOPB-I it is not explicitly given in the excerpt but the G3 value is listed.
- The dimensionless tidal deformability Λ_{1.4} for G3 is 838.3; for FSU it is 450.96.
- The DM Fermi momentum is varied from 0.00 to 0.04 GeV, with a fiducial value of 0.03 GeV derived from the assumption that the nucleon density is 10^3 times the average DM density.
- The transition density n_t is taken as 0.3, 0.4, and 0.5 fm^{-3}, corresponding to approximately 2, 2.5, and 3 times nuclear saturation density n_0 ≈ 0.16 fm^{-3}.
- The QCD confinement scale Λ_cs is taken as 800 MeV and 1400 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the quarkyonic matter model of McLerran and Reddy (2019) and the subsequent beta-equilibrium formulation by Jhao and Lattimer (2020) by adding fermionic DM as an additional degree of freedom, following earlier studies of DM in neutron stars (e.g., DM1, DM2, DM3, DM5). It enables future statistical analyses to constrain DM parameters from neutron star observations, and it shows that the G3 and IOPB-I RMF parameter sets, when combined with quarkyonic and DM contributions, can satisfy constraints from NICER, GW170817, and pulsar mass measurements.
