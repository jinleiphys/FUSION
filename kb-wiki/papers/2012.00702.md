# Curing the unphysical behaviour of NLO quarkonium production at the LHC and its relevance to constrain the gluon PDF at low scales

> We address the unphysical energy dependence of quarkonium-hadroproduction cross sections at Next-to-Leading Order (NLO) in alpha_S which we attribute to an over-subtraction in the factorisation of the collinear singularities inside the PDFs in the MSbar scheme. Such over- or under-subtractions have a limited phenomenological relevance in most of the scattering processes in particle physics. On the contrary, it is particularly harmful for P_T-integrated charmonium hadroproduction which renders a wide class of NLO results essentially unusable. Indeed, in such processes, alphaS is not so small, the PDFs are not evolved much and can be rather flat for the corresponding momentum fractions and, finally, some process-dependent NLO pieces are either too small or too large. We propose a scale-fixing criterion which avoids such an over-subtraction. We demonstrate its efficiency for eta(c,b) but also for a fictitious light elementary scalar boson. Having provided stable NLO predictions for eta(c,b) P_T-integrated cross sections, sigma^NLO(eta(Q)), and discussed the options to study eta(b) hadroproduction, we argue that their measurement at the LHC can help better determine the gluon PDF at low scales and tell whether the local minimum in conventional NLO gluon PDFs around x=0.001 at scales below 2 GeV is physical or not.

## Key claim

The unphysical negative cross sections for NLO quarkonium hadroproduction at LHC energies arise from an over-subtraction in the collinear factorisation of the Altarelli-Parisi counterterm in the MSbar scheme, which is particularly harmful for charmonium because the process-dependent negative high-energy limit of the partonic cross section cannot be compensated by the universal DGLAP evolution when PDFs are flat at low scales.

## Method

The authors computed NLO (alpha_S^3) partonic cross sections for pseudoscalar quarkonium (eta_c, eta_b) hadroproduction in the collinear factorisation framework combined with NRQCD factorisation at leading order in v, using the colour-singlet ^1S_0[1] state. They derived analytical expressions for the rapidity-differential cross sections and analysed the high-energy (z -> 0) limit of the partonic cross section to identify the origin of negative contributions, then proposed a scale-fixing criterion that sets the factorisation scale mu_F such that the partonic cross section vanishes at large hat_s.

## Key numbers

- The proposed scale for eta_Q production is hat_mu_F = M_Q / sqrt(e) = 0.607 M_Q, yielding approximately 1.82 GeV for eta_c (M_Q ~ 3 GeV)
- For eta_c, |R(0)|^2 = 1 GeV^3 was used; for eta_b, |R(0)|^2 = 7.5 GeV^3
- The process-dependent constant A_a for eta_Q is -1 (identical for colour-singlet and colour-octet states)
- For comparison, A_a for a fictitious scalar boson with 2m_Q/m_H = 1 is -0.147, yielding hat_mu_F = 0.93 M_H
- The local minimum in conventional NLO gluon PDFs appears around x = 0.001 at scales below 2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier observations by Schuler (1994) and Mangano and Petrelli (1996) who reported negative NLO cross sections for eta_c production and attributed them to the partonic gg cross section approaching a negative constant at high hat_s. The authors provide a systematic explanation in terms of over-subtraction of collinear singularities in the MSbar scheme and propose a practical scale-fixing solution. The results enable using NLO eta_c and eta_b hadroproduction measurements at the LHC to constrain the gluon PDF at low scales, complementing the approach of Flett et al. (2020) who extracted gluon PDF constraints from J/psi exclusive photoproduction.
