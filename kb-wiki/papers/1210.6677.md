# Highlights from STAR

> In these proceedings, I highlight some selected results from the STAR experiment that were presented in the Quark Matter 2012 conference.

## Key claim

The paper presents selected STAR results from the Quark Matter 2012 conference, highlighting that the observed features in forward dihadron correlations, identified particle elliptic flow, and net-proton directed flow are consistent with a smooth transition from dilute parton gas to the Color Glass Condensate regime and with partonic collectivity dominating at top RHIC energy, but that these signatures begin to break down at lower collision energies in the Beam Energy Scan program.

## Method

The STAR detector at RHIC recorded heavy-ion collisions across multiple energies (200 GeV Au+Au, 193 GeV U+U, and BES energies from 7.7 to 39 GeV) using its large-acceptance mid-rapidity subsystems and forward calorimeters (FMS, EEMC). The analysis measured dihadron azimuthal correlations between forward pi0 triggers and associated particles at various pseudorapidities, identified particle elliptic flow (v2) as a function of transverse kinetic energy, dielectron mass spectra, directed flow (v1) slopes, and higher moments of net-proton multiplicity distributions. Comparisons were made to hadronic cocktail models, transport model calculations, and Poisson expectations.

## Key numbers

- Forward dihadron correlation width difference between d+Au and p+p collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV increases with increasing associated particle eta (decreasing gluon x in Au nucleus), consistent with transition to CGC regime
- NCQ scaling holds within 10% for all particles at (m_T-m_0)/n_q > 0.6 GeV/c^2 in 0-30% centrality Au+Au 200 GeV collisions
- In 30-80% centrality Au+Au 200 GeV, v2/n_q of multi-strange hadrons (phi, Xi) deviates from that of K_S^0 beyond 10-15%
- Charged hadron v2 in U+U 193 GeV collisions is always higher than in Au+Au 200 GeV at the same uncorrected dN_ch/deta for central and mid-central collisions
- Charge separation signal (gamma_OS - gamma_SS) in 0-1% central U+U collisions is consistent with zero while v2 is about 2.5%
- Charm production cross section per nucleon-nucleon collision follows binary scaling from p+p to central Au+Au 200 GeV
- D0 meson R_AA shows strong suppression at p_T > 3 GeV/c, larger in central collisions, with a modified hump structure at 1-3 GeV/c
- Dielectron excess in mass region 0.3-0.7 GeV/c^2 persists at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6, 62.4, and 200 GeV
- Proton v1 slope changes sign from positive to negative between 7.7 GeV and 11.5 GeV in Au+Au 10-40% centrality
- Net-proton v1 slope shows a minimum between 11.5-19.6 GeV and changes sign twice across the measured energy range
- In 0-5% central Au+Au collisions, moment products Ssigma and kappa*sigma^2 of net-proton multiplicity deviate from Poisson expectation at sqrt(s_NN) > 7.7 GeV

## Context

This conference proceedings summarizes STAR results presented at Quark Matter 2012, extending previous measurements of forward pi0-pi0 correlations in d+Au collisions (STAR, prior publication) and identified particle elliptic flow (partonic flow evidence from earlier RHIC results). The forward dihadron correlations with intermediate eta regions probe the transition between dilute parton gas and CGC regimes. The Beam Energy Scan results (7.7-39 GeV) systematically test the turn-off of sQGP signatures observed at top RHIC energy, with net-proton directed flow showing a non-monotonic behavior that transport models (UrQMD and others) cannot reproduce. The results motivate the BES phase-II program for precision measurements below sqrt(s_NN) ~ 20 GeV to locate the QCD phase boundary and critical point.
