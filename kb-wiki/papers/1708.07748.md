# Power series expansion method in tensor-optimized antisymmetrized molecular dynamics beyond the Jastrow correlation method

> We developed a new variational method for tensor-optimized antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (TOAMD) for nuclei. In TOAMD, the correlation functions for the tensor force and the short-range repulsion are introduced and used in the power series form of the wave function, which is different from the Jastrow method. Here, nucleon pairs are correlated in multi-steps with different forms, while they are correlated only once including all pairs in the Jastrow correlation method. Each correlation function in every term is independently optimized in the variation of total energy in TOAMD. For $s$-shell nuclei using the nucleon-nucleon interaction, the energies in TOAMD are better than those in the variational Monte Carlo method with the Jastrow correlation function. This means that the power series expansion using the correlation functions in TOAMD describes the nuclei better than the Jastrow correlation method.

## Key claim

The power-series expansion of correlation functions in tensor-optimized antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (TOAMD) yields lower total energies for s-shell nuclei than the variational Monte Carlo (VMC) method using the Jastrow correlation function, but only when each correlation function in every term of the expansion is independently optimized; if common correlation functions are used across all terms (mimicking the Jastrow approach), the TOAMD energies become close to, but not below, the VMC values.

## Method

The authors construct a TOAMD wave function as a power series of tensor (F_D) and short-range central (F_S) correlation functions multiplied onto an antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) Slater-determinant basis. They include terms up to double products (F_S F_S, F_S F_D, F_D F_S, F_D F_D) and optimize each correlation function independently using a Gaussian expansion with 7 Gaussians per function. The correlated Hamiltonian and norm operators are expanded via cluster expansion without truncation, and matrix elements are computed using Fourier transformation of the Gaussian functions to momentum space, followed by integration over single-particle plane-wave matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- For ^3H with the Malfliet-Tjon V (MT-V) central potential: TOAMD energy = -8.24 MeV, compared to VMC = -8.22(2) MeV and few-body = -8.25 MeV.
- For ^4He with MT-V: TOAMD energy = -31.28 MeV, compared to VMC = -31.19(5) MeV and few-body = -31.36 MeV.
- For ^3H with the Argonne V6 (AV6) bare NN interaction: TOAMD energy = -7.10 MeV, compared to VMC = -6.33(5) MeV and few-body = -7.15 MeV; kinetic energy in TOAMD = 44.56 MeV, radius = 1.76 fm.
- For ^4He with AV6: TOAMD energy = -24.31 MeV, compared to VMC = -22.75(10) MeV and few-body = -25.40 MeV; kinetic energy in TOAMD = 95.17 MeV, radius = 1.50 fm.
- In the constrained "Fixed F" calculation for ^3H (common correlation functions across all terms): energy = -6.04 MeV, an energy loss of 1.06 MeV relative to the full TOAMD result.
- In the constrained "Fixed F" calculation for ^4He: energy = -22.29 MeV, an energy loss of 2.02 MeV relative to the full TOAMD result.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous TOAMD development (Myo et al., 2015, 2017a, 2017b, 2017c) by directly comparing TOAMD with the Jastrow correlation method used in VMC calculations (Carlson, 1987) for the same bare NN interactions (MT-V and AV6). The results show that TOAMD achieves better variational accuracy than VMC for s-shell nuclei, and the independent optimization of correlation functions in each power-series term is essential for this improvement. The method enables future application to p-shell nuclei and cluster states such as ^8Be, and can incorporate three-nucleon interactions like the Fujita-Miyazawa type.
