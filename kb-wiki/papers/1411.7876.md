# The muon g-2: Dyson-Schwinger status on hadronic light-by-light scattering

> We give a status report on the hadronic light-by-light scattering contribution to the muon's anomalous magnetic moment from the Dyson-Schwinger approach. We discuss novel, model-independent properties of the light-by-light amplitude: we give its covariant decomposition in view of electromagnetic gauge invariance and Bose symmetry, and we identify the relevant kinematic regions that are probed under the integral. The decomposition of the amplitude at the quark level and the importance of its various diagrams are discussed and related to model approaches.

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) calculation of the dressed quark loop contribution to hadronic light-by-light scattering yields a value for the muon anomalous magnetic moment that is five times larger than the extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (ENJL) model result, potentially reducing the discrepancy between experiment and the Standard Model to approximately 2 sigma; however, this result neglects parts of the Ball-Chiu vertex and therefore is not gauge invariant, although gauge artifacts appear to be negligible.

## Method

The authors compute the dressed quark loop contribution to hadronic light-by-light scattering using Dyson-Schwinger equations, where the quark propagator dressing functions M(p^2) and A(p^2) are solved from the quark DSE and the quark-photon vertex dressing functions are obtained from its inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation. They perform a covariant decomposition of the light-by-light amplitude into 136 tensor structures organized by permutation group multiplets of S_4, which allows them to identify that the dressing functions depend primarily on the symmetric singlet variable S_0 with negligible angular dependence.

## Key numbers

- The DSE result for the dressed quark loop contribution to a_mu is 10.7(2) x 10^(-10) with four flavors (Goecke 2012)
- The Standard Model prediction for a_mu is 11,659,183.9 x 10^(-10) with uncertainty 5.8 x 10^(-10)
- The experimental value for a_mu is 11,659,208.9 x 10^(-10) with uncertainty 6.3 x 10^(-10)
- The difference between experiment and Standard Model is 25.0 x 10^(-10) with uncertainty 8.6 x 10^(-10)
- The hadronic light-by-light contribution from models is 11.6 x 10^(-10) with uncertainty 3.9 x 10^(-10)
- The pseudoscalar exchange contribution from models is in the range 8 to 11 x 10^(-10)
- The scalar exchange contribution from models is approximately -1 x 10^(-10)
- The axialvector exchange contribution from models is approximately 2 x 10^(-10)
- The pseudoscalar loops contribution from models is approximately -2 x 10^(-10)
- The constituent-quark loop with bare vertex and M=200 MeV yields approximately 10 x 10^(-10) for two light flavors
- Including the DSE result for A(p^2) suppresses the quark loop to approximately 5 x 10^(-10)
- Adding the Sigma_A dressing of the gamma^mu component restores the value to approximately 10 x 10^(-10)
- Including the leading transverse vertex with f_1 = -1/(Q^2+m_V^2) suppresses the value to approximately 4 x 10^(-10)
- Including the full Bethe-Salpeter result for f_1 restores the value to approximately 10 x 10^(-10)
- The DSE result for the pseudoscalar exchange diagram is (8.1 +/- 1.2) x 10^(-10) (Goecke 2010)

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger approach to hadronic light-by-light scattering, which has been previously applied to the quark loop in a series of papers (Fischer 2010, Goecke 2010, Goecke 2012). The DSE result for the quark loop (10.7 x 10^(-10)) is significantly larger than the ENJL model result (approximately 2 x 10^(-10)) from Bijnens (1995), and the authors attribute this to the additional quark momentum dependence of the vertex that suppresses the suppression found in the ENJL model. The paper provides a systematic decomposition of the light-by-light amplitude using permutation group methods, which enables future complete gauge-invariant calculations that include the quark scattering matrix contributions and meson exchange diagrams, with the goal of reducing the uncertainty in the Standard Model prediction for the muon g-2.
