# Holographic Odderon at TOTEM ?

> We consider the contribution of the Odderon to diffractive $pp$ and $p\bar p$ elastic scattering at large center of mass energy. We identify the Odderon and Pomeron with the Reggeized $1^{\pm-}$ and $2^{++}$ glueballs in the bulk, respectively. We use for the gravity dual description the repulsive wall model, to account for the proper Gribov diffusion for off-forward scattering. The eikonalized and unitarized amplitudes exhibit a vanishingly small rho-parameter, and a slope parameter fixed by twice the closed string slope. The results for the differential and total cross sections are compared to the empirical results reported recently by the TOTEM collaboration.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the Odderon contribution to diffractive $pp$ and $p\bar p$ elastic scattering can be described using a holographic dual gravity model with a repulsive wall, and that the resulting eikonalized amplitudes produce a vanishingly small rho-parameter and a slope parameter fixed by twice the closed string slope, but the comparison to TOTEM data is presented as a test of the model rather than a definitive confirmation of the Odderon.

## Method

The authors identify the Pomeron and Odderon with Reggeized $2^{++}$ graviton and $1^{\pm-}$ Kalb-Ramond fields in the bulk, respectively, using a bottom-up holographic approach with a repulsive wall metric to account for Gribov diffusion in off-forward scattering. They construct the bulk-to-bulk propagators for even and odd spin-j exchanges via Sommerfeld-Watson transforms, perform saddle-point approximations to obtain the Reggeized amplitudes, and then eikonalize and unitarize the resulting holographic amplitudes for comparison with TOTEM data.

## Key numbers

- The Pomeron intercept in the conformal limit is $j_{\mathbb P}=2-4/(2\sqrt{\lambda})$, and after linearizing the dilaton in the repulsive wall model it becomes $j_{\mathbb P}(t)=j_+-3/(2\sqrt{\lambda})+(\alpha'/2)t$ with $j_+=2$.
- The Odderon intercept is $j_{\mathbb O}(t)=j_--(m_k^2-1)/(2\sqrt{\lambda})+(\alpha'/2)t$ with $j_-=1$, where the leading branch corresponds to $k=1$ giving an intercept precisely at one.
- The even-signature amplitude factor is $f^+(\lambda)=i+4\sqrt{\lambda}/(3\pi)-\pi/(4\sqrt{\lambda})$, and the odd-signature factor is $f^-(\lambda)=i+4\sqrt{\lambda}/((m_k^2-1)\pi)-(m_k^2-1)\pi/(12\sqrt{\lambda})$.

## Context

This work extends the holographic approach to the Odderon initially discussed by Brower et al. (2008) using AdS/CFT, and builds on the repulsive wall model developed by Mamo and Zahed (2022) for the Pomeron. The authors compare their results to the empirical findings recently reported by the TOTEM collaboration (2020), which claimed evidence for the Odderon, and also relate their Odderon trajectory to one found in baryon-baryon scattering in AdS with D5 branes by Avsar et al. (2009). The approach enables a unified holographic description of both Pomeron and Odderon exchanges with proper Gribov diffusion, which the soft wall model fails to capture.
