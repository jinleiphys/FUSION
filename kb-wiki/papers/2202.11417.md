# Impact of hadronic interactions and conservation laws on cumulants of conserved charges in a dynamical model

> Understanding the phase diagram of QCD by measuring fluctuations of conserved charges in heavy-ion collision is one of the main goals of the beam energy scan program at RHIC. Within this work, we calculate the role of hadronic interactions and momentum cuts on cumulants of conserved charges up to fourth order in a system in equilibrium within a hadronic transport approach (SMASH). In our model the net baryon, net charge and net strangeness is perfectly conserved on an event-by-event basis and the cumulants are calculated as a function of subvolume sizes and compared to analytic expectations. We find a modification of the kurtosis due to charge annihilation processes in systems with simplified degrees of freedom. Furthermore the result of the full SMASH hadron gas for the net baryon and net proton number fluctuations is presented for systems with zero and finite values of baryon chemical potential. Additionally we find that due to dynamical correlations the cumulants of the net baryon number cannot easily be recovered from the net protons. Finally the influence of deuteron cluster formation on the net proton and net baryon fluctuations in a simplified system is shown. This analysis is important to better understand the relation between measurements of fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions and theoretical calculation which are often performed in a grand canonical ensemble.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that dynamical hadronic interactions, particularly charge annihilation processes and resonance formation, modify the kurtosis of conserved charge fluctuations compared to analytic expectations based solely on conservation laws, even when the net charge is perfectly conserved on an event-by-event basis.

## Method

The authors used the SMASH (Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-interacting Hadrons) hadronic transport approach with periodic boundary conditions to simulate infinite equilibrated hadronic matter. They initialized boxes with fixed particle numbers, allowed the system to reach thermal and chemical equilibrium, then calculated cumulants up to fourth order of net baryon, net charge, and net strangeness as functions of subvolume size. The calculations were performed for simplified systems with specific interactions (pions with rho mesons, nucleons with and without annihilation) and for the full SMASH hadron gas at zero and finite baryon chemical potential, both with full phase space and with a transverse momentum cut of 0.4 < pT < 2.0 GeV.

## Key numbers

- For the pion-rho system with V = (10 fm)^3 and N_init = 200 pi+ + 200 pi-, the charge density was rho ~ 0.25 fm^{-3} with approximately 80 interactions per fm, while for V = (20 fm)^3 the charge density was rho ~ 0.05 fm^{-3} with approximately 21 interactions per fm
- The kurtosis of net charge in the pion-rho system shifted toward kappa sigma^2 ~ 0 around x = 0.5 for the denser system, compared to the analytic expectation of (1 - 6x(1-x))
- For the full SMASH hadron gas at mu_B ~ 0, the proton scaled variance fit gave omega(x) = 0.995 - 0.361x in full phase space and omega(x) = 0.998 - 0.219x with the pT cut
- For the full SMASH hadron gas at mu_B ~ 250 MeV, the proton scaled variance fit gave omega(x) = 1.001 - 0.412x in full phase space and omega(x) = 1.001 - 0.240x with the pT cut
- The timestep size was required to be t_step < 0.05 fm to avoid numerical artifacts from wall interactions

## Context

This work extends the calculations of Petersen (2015) from net charge to net baryon number and more realistic interacting hadron gases using the SMASH approach. It tests the analytic expressions for cumulants with perfect charge conservation derived by Bzdak (2012) and the mapping between net proton and net baryon fluctuations proposed by Kitazawa (2012). The results are important for interpreting beam energy scan measurements at RHIC and future CBM experiments at FAIR, as they show that dynamical correlations prevent simple recovery of net baryon cumulants from net proton measurements, and that deuteron cluster formation can further modify the fluctuations.
