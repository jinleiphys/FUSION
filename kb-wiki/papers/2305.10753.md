# Analysis of $Ξ(1620)$ resonance and $\bar{K}Λ$ scattering length with chiral unitary approach

> We study the $Ξ(1620)$ resonance near the $\bar{K}Λ$ threshold in the light of the recent experimental constraints. The Belle collaboration have found a resonance peak of $Ξ(1620)$ slightly below the $\bar{K}^{0}Λ$ threshold in the $π^{+}Ξ^{-}$ invariant mass spectrum, and the ALICE collaboration have determined the $K^{-}Λ$ scattering length from the measurement of the momentum correlation functions in the heavy ion collisions. Using the effective range expansion, we classify the nature of the pole of the near-threshold eigenstate in terms of the scattering length, in the presence of the decay channel. It is shown that the quasibound state below the threshold can be described by only the scattering length, while the description of the resonance above the threshold requires the contribution from the effective range. Based on the chiral unitary approach, we construct a theoretical model which generates the pole of $Ξ(1620)$ below the $\bar{K}Λ$ threshold with relatively narrow width, as reported by the Belle collaboration. It is quantitatively demonstrated that the spectrum of the $Ξ(1620)$ quasibound state is distorted by the effect of the nearby $\bar{K}Λ$ threshold. We then construct another model which reproduces the $K^{-}Λ$ scattering length by the ALICE collaboration. In this case, the eigenstate pole does not appear in the physically relevant Riemann sheets, and the spectrum shows a cusp structure at the $\bar{K}Λ$ threshold. We finally examine the compatibility of the value of the $\bar{K}Λ$ scattering length and the subthreshold pole of $Ξ(1620)$ including the experimental uncertainties.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the $\Xi(1620)$ resonance can be described as either a quasibound state below the $\bar{K}\Lambda$ threshold (consistent with Belle data) or as a cusp structure at threshold (consistent with ALICE scattering length data), but these two interpretations are mutually incompatible within the same theoretical framework when experimental uncertainties are considered.

## Method

The authors use the chiral unitary approach with the Weinberg-Tomozawa leading-order interaction kernel to construct coupled-channel scattering amplitudes for meson-baryon systems with strangeness $-2$. They classify near-threshold eigenstates using effective range expansion, relating the complex scattering length to pole positions on different Riemann sheets. Two models are constructed: Model 1 reproduces the Belle $\Xi(1620)$ pole at $1610-30i$ MeV below the $\bar{K}\Lambda$ threshold, while Model 2 reproduces the ALICE $K^{-}\Lambda$ scattering length.

## Key numbers

* Belle $\Xi(1620)$ mass: $M_R = 1610.4 \pm 6.0 (\text{stat.})^{+6.1}_{-4.2} (\text{syst.})$ MeV
* Belle $\Xi(1620)$ width: $\Gamma_R = 59.9 \pm 4.8 (\text{stat.})^{+2.8}_{-7.1} (\text{syst.})$ MeV
* $\bar{K}^0\Lambda$ threshold energy: $1613.3$ MeV
* Central pole position from Belle: $z_{\text{ex}} = 1610 - 30i$ MeV
* Set 1 pole position (starting point): $1607 - 140i$ MeV
* Meson decay constants used: $f_\pi = f_K = f_\eta = 104.439$ MeV

## Context

This work extends prior chiral unitary studies of excited $\Xi$ baryons, particularly Ramos (2002) which generated $\Xi(1620)$ at around 1600 MeV but with a broad width, and Garcia-Recio (2003) which generated both $\Xi(1620)$ and $\Xi(1690)$ simultaneously. The paper addresses the tension between the Belle collaboration's narrow-width $\Xi(1620)$ peak and the ALICE collaboration's $K^{-}\Lambda$ scattering length measurement, showing that a quasibound state pole below threshold (Model 1) and a cusp structure at threshold (Model 2) represent incompatible interpretations. This enables future experimental work to distinguish between these scenarios through more precise measurements of the $\bar{K}\Lambda$ scattering length and the $\Xi(1620)$ line shape near threshold.
