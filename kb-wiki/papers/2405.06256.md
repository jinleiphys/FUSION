# Reconstructing generalised parton distributions from the lattice off-forward Compton amplitude

> We present a determination of the structure functions of the off-forward Compton amplitude $\mathcal{H}_1$ and $\mathcal{E}_1$ from the Feynman-Hellmann method in lattice QCD. At leading twist, these structure functions give access to the generalised parton distributions (GPDs) $H$ and $E$, respectively. This calculation is performed for an unphysical pion mass of $m_π=412\;\text{MeV}$ and four values of the soft momentum transfer, $t\approx 0, -0.3, -0.6, -1.1\;\text{GeV}^2$, all at a hard momentum scale of $\bar{Q}^2\approx 5\;\text{GeV}^2$. Using these results, we test various methods to determine properties of the real-time scattering amplitudes and GPDs: (1) we fit their Mellin moments, and (2) we use a simple GPD ansatz to reconstruct the entire distribution. Our final results show promising agreement with phenomenology and other lattice results, and highlight specific systematics in need of control.

# Reconstructing generalised parton distributions from the lattice off-forward Compton amplitude

## Key claim
The off-forward Compton amplitude structure functions H1 and E1 can be determined from lattice QCD using the Feynman-Hellmann method, and their Mellin moments and the underlying generalised parton distributions can be reconstructed, but only at an unphysical pion mass of 412 MeV and for a single hard scale of Qbar^2 ≈ 5 GeV^2, meaning the results include higher-twist and power corrections that have not yet been separated from the leading-twist contributions.

## Method
The Feynman-Hellmann method was applied to lattice QCD to compute the off-forward Compton amplitude by perturbing quark propagators with two background fields carrying momenta q1 and q2, then constructing perturbed nucleon correlators. By choosing the current direction collinear with the momentum transfer Delta, the structure functions H1 and E1 were isolated from the spin-parity projected correlator ratios using two different projectors (unpolarised and polarised). The resulting Euclidean Compton amplitude at kinematics |omega| < 1 was related to the real-time structure functions via a dispersion relation, and two reconstruction strategies were employed: (1) a model-independent fit of the Mellin moments from a power series expansion in omega, and (2) a model-dependent fit using a Regge-inspired GPD ansatz H(x,t) proportional to x^{-alpha_0 - alpha' t} (1-x)^beta.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass: m_pi = 412 MeV (unphysical, at the SU(3) flavour symmetric point)
- Hard momentum scale: Qbar^2 ≈ 5 GeV^2 for all kinematics
- Soft momentum transfer values: t ≈ 0, -0.29, -0.57, -1.14 GeV^2
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.068 fm on a 48^3 × 96 lattice with 2+1 quark flavours
- Number of gauge configurations: N_cfg = 537
- Dipole fit parameters for uu-dd quarks: A_{2,0}(0) = 0.226(59) with m_dip = 1.8(1.1) GeV; B_{2,0}(0) = 0.50(26) with m_dip = 1.8(1.3) GeV
- Angular momentum from Ji sum rule (uu-dd, neglecting higher-twist): <J^3_{u-d}> = 0.36(16)

## Context
This work extends the Feynman-Hellmann method from the forward Compton amplitude (previously studied by the same collaboration in Hannaford-Gunn et al. (2021) and earlier forward-scattering papers) to the off-forward case, building on an exploratory calculation by Hannaford-Gunn et al. (2021) that could only access linear combinations of structure functions. The results show promising agreement with generalised form factors A_{2,0} and B_{2,0} computed from local twist-two operators on the same gauge configurations using standard three-point function methods (following the approach of GPD lattice studies such as those by the QCDSF collaboration). The method enables future studies of the Qbar^2 scaling behaviour of the off-forward Compton amplitude and the off-forward subtraction function, which are inputs for experimental determinations of the proton pressure distribution, and complements quasi-distribution and pseudo-distribution approaches to GPDs that have been developed since 2015.
