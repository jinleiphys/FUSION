# A model independent analysis of gluonic pole matrix elements and universality of TMD fragmentation functions

> Gluonic pole matrix elements explain the appearance of single spin asymmetries (SSA) in high-energy scattering processes. They involve a combination of operators which are odd under time reversal (T-odd). Such matrix elements appear in principle both for parton distribution functions and parton fragmentation functions. We show that for parton fragmentation functions these gluonic pole matrix elements vanish as a consequence of the analytic structure of scattering amplitudes in Quantum Chromodynamics. This result is important in the study of the universality of transverse momentum dependent (TMD) fragmentation functions.

## Key claim

The authors show that gluonic pole matrix elements (T-odd operator combinations) vanish for parton fragmentation functions, based on the analytic structure of QCD scattering amplitudes under the assumptions of unitarity and analyticity. This result is conditional on the limit where the momenta of the gluons become zero, and it does not depend on partonic or hadronic masses, nor on integrations over transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors analyze the support properties of quark-quark-gluon correlators by relating them to forward parton-hadron scattering amplitudes via the LSZ formalism. They study the analytic structure of these amplitudes, specifically the singularities from cuts in the parton virtualities and Mandelstam invariants, and examine the integration contours for the \(k^-\) and \(k_1^-\) variables. By wrapping the integration contours around the relevant cuts for the case of fragmentation functions (where the momentum fraction \(x > 1\)), they show that the gluonic pole matrix element \(\Delta_G(x,x)\) vanishes in the limit \(x_1 \rightarrow 0\). The argument is then extended inductively to multi-gluonic pole matrix elements.

## Key numbers

* For fragmentation functions, the gluonic pole matrix element \(\Delta_G(x,x) = 0\) in the limit \(x_1 \rightarrow 0\), where \(x_1\) is the momentum fraction of the additional gluon.
* The support for distribution functions is \(\Phi_G(x,x) = \theta(x)\,\theta(1-x)\,\mbox{Disc}_{[s,s_1]}\mathcal{A} + \theta(-x)\,\theta(1+x)\,\mbox{Disc}_{[u,u_1]}\mathcal{A}\), showing non-vanishing behavior for \(0 < x < 1\).
* The analysis applies for momentum fractions \(|x| > 1\) (fragmentation region) versus \(0 < |x| < 1\) (distribution region).

## Context

This work extends earlier spectral studies of single-gluon pole matrix elements for fragmentation (Gamberg 2008, Meissner 2008) to a general, model-independent proof that applies to multi-gluonic pole matrix elements. It contradicts the possibility that T-odd fragmentation functions could arise from T-odd operator combinations in the matrix elements themselves; instead, T-odd effects in fragmentation come solely from final-state interactions (Collins 1992, Boer 1997). The result establishes the universality of transverse momentum dependent (TMD) fragmentation functions, including the Collins function \(H_1^\perp\) and the polarization fragmentation function \(D^\perp_{1T}\), meaning they are convoluted with standard partonic cross sections rather than with non-standard gluonic pole cross sections.
