# The structure of the neutron deficient Sn isotopes

> We calculate the spectra for the neutron deficient Sn isotopes with mass numbers from A=102 to A=108, using standard many-body techniques. The G-matrix used in the perturbative expansion was calculated using the Bonn potentials. A good agreement with the available data is obtained.

# The structure of the neutron deficient Sn isotopes

## Key claim

The authors calculate low-lying spectra for neutron-deficient Sn isotopes from A=102 to A=108 using a realistic shell model with an effective interaction derived from the Bonn A potential, achieving good agreement with available data, but only when the single-particle energies are taken as nearly degenerate for the $g_{7/2}$ and $d_{5/2}$ orbitals, and when the $h_{11/2}$ intruder orbital is included in the model space.

## Method

The authors derived a two-body neutron-neutron effective interaction from the Bonn A nucleon-nucleon potential using conventional perturbative many-body techniques, including all diagrams through third order in the G-matrix via the Lee-Suzuki iterative expansion. The model space consisted of the $N=4$ oscillator shell orbitals ($1d_{5/2}$, $0g_{7/2}$, $1d_{3/2}$, $2s_{1/2}$) plus the intruder $0h_{11/2}$ orbital from the $N=5$ shell, with single-particle energies taken from Grawe (1992). Shell model diagonalizations were performed using the Lanczos algorithm in an m-scheme Slater determinant basis, with matrix dimensions ranging from 36 for $^{102}$Sn to 323,682 for $^{108}$Sn.

## Key numbers

- Single-particle energy differences used: $\varepsilon_{g_{7/2}} - \varepsilon_{d_{5/2}} = 0.20$ MeV, $\varepsilon_{d_{3/2}} - \varepsilon_{d_{5/2}} = 2.89$ MeV, $\varepsilon_{s_{1/2}} - \varepsilon_{d_{5/2}} = 2.80$ MeV, $\varepsilon_{h_{11/2}} - \varepsilon_{d_{5/2}} = 5.0$ MeV.
- Excitation energy of the lowest $J=2$ states in even Sn isotopes: spacing of about 1.2 MeV, in good agreement with experiment.
- Measured E2 transition strength in $^{104}$Sn $6^{+} \rightarrow 4^{+}$: 4.0 Wu; in $^{106}$Sn $6^{+} \rightarrow 4^{+}$: 2.5 Wu.
- Calculated E2 transition strengths (with effective neutron charge $e_n = 1.0e$): 2.1 Wu and 3.0 Wu for two $6^{+} \rightarrow 4^{+}$ transitions, comparable to the observations in $^{104}$Sn and $^{106}$Sn respectively.
- In $^{104}$Sn, the calculated $4^{+}_{2}$ state at 1.942 MeV and $6^{+}_{2}$ state at 2.257 MeV correspond to observed transitions; in $^{106}$Sn, the $4^{+}_{3}$ state at 2.017 MeV and $6^{+}_{2}$ state at 2.321 MeV correspond.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier realistic shell model calculation for even Sn isotopes (Holt et al., 1993) to include odd isotopes $^{103}$Sn, $^{105}$Sn, and $^{107}$Sn, using the doubly magic $^{100}$Sn as a core. It addresses deficiencies in prior shell model calculations by Schubart et al., who had to scale their two-body interaction by a factor of 1.4 and could not reproduce measured E2 transition rates. The results enable further testing of the seniority and generalized seniority schemes proposed by Talmi (1977), and allow investigation of possible effective three-body interactions when the number of valence neutrons becomes significant.
