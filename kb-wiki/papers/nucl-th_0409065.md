# Improved Exponential model with pairing attenuation and the backbending phenomenon

> A modified version of the exponential model with paring attenuation is proposed, and used to describe successfully the backbending of the moment of inertia, in even-even nuclei, not only in well-deformed nuclei but also in slightly deformed nuclei. The model remarkably fits good the experimental observation with a few parameters.

## Key claim

The authors propose a modified exponential model for pairing attenuation by allowing the exponent parameter \(\nu\) to be a free variable, rather than fixing it at \(\nu=2\) as in the prior Sood and Jain (1979) formulation. This three-parameter model successfully describes backbending in the moment of inertia for both well-deformed and slightly deformed even-even nuclei, including the forward and down-bending regions that the earlier model could not reproduce.

## Method

The authors fit the rotational energy levels of ground-state bands using the expression \(E(I) = (\hbar^2 / 2\varphi_0) I(I+1) e^{\Delta_0 (1 - I/I_c)^{1/\nu}}\), where \(\varphi_0\), \(\Delta_0\), and \(\nu\) are free parameters adjusted via least-squares fitting to experimental data up to spin \(I=30\). The critical angular momentum \(I_c\) is scanned to minimize the root-mean-square deviation \(\sigma = \sqrt{(1/N) \sum (1 - E_{\text{cal}}/E_{\text{exp}})^2}\). The model is applied to 24 even-even nuclei across the mass range \(A=100\) to \(A=242\), and the resulting moment of inertia versus squared rotational frequency plots are compared with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{100}\text{Zr}\), the calculated energy at \(2^+\) is 0.18971 MeV (experimental: 0.21253 MeV); at \(14^+\) the calculated energy is 4.2147 MeV (experimental: 4.2085 MeV).
- For \(^{232}\text{Th}\), the calculated energy at \(2^+\) is 0.051554 MeV (experimental: 0.049369 MeV); at \(30^+\) the calculated energy is 5.163 MeV (experimental: 5.162 MeV).
- The fitted parameter \(\nu\) ranges from 0.114596 (for \(^{100}\text{Zr}\)) to 15.3588 (for \(^{242}\text{Pu}\)).
- The root-mean-square deviation \(\sigma\) ranges from 0.0031577 (for \(^{112}\text{Cd}\)) to 0.0411068 (for \(^{100}\text{Zr}\)).
- The ratio \(E_{4^+}/E_{2^+}\) ranges from 1.88 (for \(^{140}\text{Ba}\)) to 3.31 (for \(^{240}\text{Pu}\) and \(^{242}\text{Pu}\)).

## Context

This work extends the exponential model with pairing attenuation originally developed by Sood and Jain (1979), which was limited to well-deformed nuclei with \(150 < A < 190\) and could not describe forward or down-bending regions. By making the exponent \(\nu\) a free parameter, the present model successfully describes backbending in slightly deformed nuclei around \(A \sim 100\) and actinide nuclei near \(A \sim 240\), where the earlier model failed. This enables systematic phenomenological studies of ground-state band energies across a wider range of nuclear deformations without the restriction that the energy ratio \(E(4^+)/E(2^+)\) exceed 3.0.
