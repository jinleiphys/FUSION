# Neutrino scattering rates in the presence of hyperons from a Skyrme model in the RPA approximation

> The contribution of Lambda hyperons to neutrino scattering rates is calculated in the random phase approximation in a model where the interaction is described by a Skyrme potential. Finite temperature and neutrino trapping are taken into account in view of applications to the deleptonization stage of protoneutron star cooling. The hyperons can remove the problem of ferromagnetic instability common to (nearly) all Skyrme parametrizations of the nucleon-nucleon interaction. As a consequence, there is not any longer a pole at the transition in the neutrino-baryon cross section. However there still remains an enhancement in this region. In the absence of ferromagnetism the mean free path in npLambda matter is reduced compared to its value in np matter as consequence of the presence of this additional degree of freedom. At high density the results are very sensitive to the choice of the Lambda-Lambda interaction.

## Key claim

The inclusion of Λ hyperons in the Skyrme-RPA calculation removes the ferromagnetic instability pole that appears in the neutrino-baryon cross section for pure nucleonic matter, but a residual enhancement of the cross section remains in that density region; the mean free path in npΛ matter is reduced compared to np matter due to the additional hyperonic degree of freedom, though results at high density are very sensitive to the choice of the Λ-Λ interaction.

## Method

The neutrino-baryon scattering cross section and mean free path were calculated in the random phase approximation (RPA) using a nonrelativistic Skyrme interaction for nucleons (SkI3, SLy10, or SV parametrizations) extended to include Λ hyperons via the YBZ6 or LY-I NΛ potentials and either the SLL2 or no ΛΛ interaction. The Dyson equation for the polarization propagator was solved as a 3×3 matrix in the basis of neutrons, protons, and Λ hyperons, and the Landau parameters for the spin-0 (vector) and spin-1 (axial) channels were extracted from the Skyrme energy density functional. Finite temperature and neutrino trapping were included, and the hyperon fraction during deleptonization was estimated using the temperature and lepton-number evolution from Pons et al. (1999).

## Key numbers

- Λ hyperon threshold density in cold neutrino-free matter: 2.08 n_sat for SkI3+YBZ6+SLL2; in protoneutron star matter with lepton fraction Y_L=0.4, formation delayed to 2.85 n_sat.
- Maximum neutron star mass with hyperons: 1.64 M_⊙ for SkI3+YBZ6+SLL2 (reduced from 2.26 M_⊙ for npe matter); 1.425 M_⊙ for SLy10+LY-I+SLL2.
- Ferromagnetic transition density in npΛe matter: 9.194 n_sat for SkI3+YBZ6+SLL2, 6.183 n_sat for SLy10+LY-I+SLL2, 9.093 n_sat for SV+YBZ6+SLL2.
- Hyperon fraction at the star center becomes sizable as early as 10 seconds after collapse during deleptonization.
- Axial structure function for neutrons at n_B=4 n_sat, T=10 MeV, k=30 MeV shows strong RPA enhancement near ω=0 for SkI3 and SLy10 parametrizations, but no pole for the SV parametrization which is free of ferromagnetism.

## Context

This work extends previous nonrelativistic RPA calculations of neutrino-nucleon scattering in neutron matter (Navarro et al., 1999; Mornas, 2001) and npe matter by adding Λ hyperons, motivated by the need to assess hyperonic effects on neutrino transport during protoneutron star deleptonization. It uses the Skyrme parametrizations of Lanskoy et al. (1997, 1998) fitted to hypernuclear data, and compares with the hyperonic equation-of-state model of Balberg and Gal (1997) which cannot provide the spin-1 Landau parameters needed for the axial response. The results enable estimates of how hyperons modify the neutrino mean free path in the deleptonization stage (20–50 seconds after core collapse), complementing the dynamical cooling calculations of Pons et al. (1999).
