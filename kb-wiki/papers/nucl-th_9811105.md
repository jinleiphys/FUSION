# Chiral symmetry and properties of hadron correlators in matter

> The constraints imposed by chiral symmetry on hadron correlation functions in nuclear medium are discussed. It is shown that these constraints implies the certain structure of the in-medium hadron correlators and lead to the cancellation of the order $ρm_π$ term in the in-medium nucleon correlator. We also consider the effect of mixing of the chiral partners correlaton functions arising from the interaction of nuclear pions with corresponding interpolating currents. It reflects the phenomena of partial restoration of chiral symmetry. The different scenarios of such restoration are briefly discussed.

# Chiral symmetry and properties of hadron correlators in matter

## Key claim
Chiral symmetry imposes constraints that cancel the order ρ m_π term in the in-medium nucleon correlator, but this cancellation does not imply a direct one-to-one correspondence between changes in the quark condensate and changes in nucleon mass in matter. The paper shows that the leading chiral corrections to hadron correlators arise from soft pions and lead to mixing of chiral partner correlators, reflecting partial restoration of chiral symmetry, though the exact scenario of restoration remains model-dependent.

## Method
The author derives chiral corrections to in-medium hadron correlators by expanding the nuclear wave function in a Fock representation including zero-, one-, and two-pion states, then applying soft-pion theorems to reduce matrix elements involving pions to double commutators with axial charges. The resulting expressions are applied to the nucleon correlator (using the Ioffe choice of interpolating current) and to the vector-axial vector and scalar-pseudoscalar correlator pairs, yielding mixing relations parameterized by the leading nonanalytic part of the pion-nucleon sigma term.

## Key numbers
- The leading nonanalytic part of the pion-nucleon sigma term, $\overline{\sigma}_{\pi N}$, is approximately -20 MeV, compared to the full sigma term $\sigma_{\pi N} = 45$ MeV.
- The mixing parameter for the vector-axial vector system is $\xi = 4\rho\overline{\sigma}_{\pi N} / (3 f_\pi^2 m_\pi^2)$, with complete mixing occurring at densities $\rho \simeq 2.5 \rho_0$.
- The mixing parameter for the scalar-pseudoscalar ($\sigma$-$\pi$) system is $\xi = 2\rho\overline{\sigma}_{\pi N} / (3 f_\pi^2 m_\pi^2)$, giving a less pronounced effect at normal nuclear density than the $\rho$-$A_1$ case.
- Pionic corrections reduce the overall correction to the vacuum two-quark condensate by 12-15%, depending on the value accepted for $\overline{\sigma}_{\pi N}$.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of chiral symmetry constraints on hadron correlators from finite temperature (De, year not given) to finite nuclear density, building on earlier short communications by Krippa (year not given) and Birse (year not given). The paper addresses the problem raised by Cohen et al. (year not given) regarding the inconsistency of terms linear in quark mass in the nucleon mass expression, and shows how similar cancellations occur in matter. The mixing relations provide a model-independent framework that contrasts with earlier QCD sum rule calculations of the ρ-meson mass in matter (Ha, year not given), which predicted a 20% mass drop at normal density but used the factorization approximation that the present work shows violates chiral symmetry. The results enable future studies of dilepton spectra in heavy-ion collisions and two-pion production experiments, where the additional singularities from chiral mixing may produce observable enhancements.
