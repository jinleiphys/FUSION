# A schematic model for QCD. III : Hadronic states

> The hadronic spectrum obtained in the framework of a QCD-inspired schematic model, is presented. The model is the extension of a previous version, whose basic degrees of freedom are constituent quarks and antiquarks, and gluons. The interaction between quarks and gluons is a phenomenological interaction and its parameters are fixed from data. The classification of the states, in terms of quark and antiquark and gluon configurations is based on symmetry considerations, and it is independent of the chosen interaction. Following this procedure, nucleon and $Δ$ resonances are identified, as well as various penta- and hepta-quarks states. The lowest pentaquarks state is predicted at 1.5 GeV and it has negative parity, while the lowest hepta-quarks state has positive parity and its energy is of the order of 2.5 GeV.

## Key claim

The model predicts the lowest pentaquark state at 1.5 GeV with negative parity and the lowest heptaquark state at approximately 2.5 GeV with positive parity, but these results depend on the specific phenomenological interaction parameters fixed from meson data and adjusted to nucleon and Delta resonances.

## Method

The authors extend a previously developed QCD-inspired schematic model, where constituent quarks, antiquarks, and gluons are the basic degrees of freedom, with quarks occupying two 18-fold degenerate levels (positive and negative energy) and gluon pairs treated as a boson level. The model Hamiltonian includes fermion-boson coupling terms, with parameters V_{lambda S} fixed from the meson spectrum and parameters D_k and E_k adjusted to nucleon and Delta resonances respectively. States are classified using group theory (U(4Omega) and its subgroups) based on symmetry considerations independent of the interaction, and the hadronic spectrum is computed by coupling three valence quarks to meson-like configurations in a boson representation.

## Key numbers

- Quark level energy omega_f fixed at 0.33 GeV; gluon pair energy omega_b fixed at 1.6 GeV
- Meson interaction parameters: V_{00}=0.0337 GeV, V_{01}=0.0422 GeV, V_{10}=0.1573 GeV, V_{11}=0.0177 GeV
- Nucleon resonance parameters: D_1=-1.442 GeV, D_2=-0.439 GeV
- Delta resonance parameters: E_1=-1.187 GeV, E_2=-0.362 GeV
- Lowest pentaquark state predicted at 1.5 GeV with negative parity
- Lowest heptaquark state predicted at approximately 2.5 GeV with positive parity
- Theoretical nucleon mass: 0.950 GeV; theoretical Roper resonance: 1.49 GeV (experimental Roper at 1.44 GeV)
- Theoretical N(1650) at 1.51 GeV; theoretical N(1535) at 1.79 GeV
- Theta+(1540) pentaquark state predicted at 1.51 GeV
- Heptaquark H_1 at 2.45 GeV; heptaquark H_2 at 2.53 GeV
- Nucleon contains approximately 0.5 q-qbar pairs and 1.4 gluon pairs (59% quark content, 41% gluon content)
- Roper resonance contains approximately 2 q-qbar pairs and 1.9 gluon pairs (65% quark content, 35% gluon content)

## Context

This work extends the schematic model previously applied to mesons (Paper I) and to phase transitions and condensates (Paper II), now describing baryons including nucleon and Delta resonances as well as exotic pentaquark and heptaquark states. The model belongs to the class of algebraic models solvable by group theory techniques, similar to approaches by Lipkin, Schutte, Geyer, and Civitarese-Reboiro, and builds on the gluon classification work of the same group (gluons99). The results enable further study of hybrid baryons and molecular states, with the (2,2)0+ state at 0.797 GeV interpreted as a possible K+K- molecular state, and the negative parity states at 1.79 GeV identified as candidates for hybrid baryons containing one q-qbar pair and two gluons.
