# On the structure of the correlation coefficients S(E_e) and U(E_e) of the neutron beta decay

> In the standard effective V - A theory of low-energy weak interactions (i.e. in the Standard Model (SM)) we analyze the structure of the correlation coefficients S(E_e) and U(E_e), where E_e is the electron energy. These correlation coefficients were introduced to the electron-energy and angular distribution of the neutron beta decay by Ebel and Feldman ( Nucl. Phys. 4, 213 (1957)) in addition to the set of correlation coefficients proposed by Jackson et al. (Phys. Rev. 106, 517 (1957)). The correlation coefficients $S(E_e)$ and $U(E_e)$ are induced by simultaneous correlations of the neutron and electron spins and electron and antineutrino 3-momenta. These correlation structures do no violate discrete P, C and T symmetries. We analyze the contributions of the radiative corrections of order O(alpha/pi), taken to leading order in the large nucleon mass m_N expansion, and corrections of order O(E_e/m_N), caused by weak magnetism and proton recoil. In addition to the obtained SM corrections we calculate the contributions of interactions beyond the SM (BSM contributions) in terms of the phenomenological coupling constants of BSM interactions by Jackson et al. (Phys. Rev. 106, 517 (1957)) and the second class currents by Weinberg (Phys. Rev. 112, 1375 (1958)).

## Key claim

The correlation coefficients S(E_e) and U(E_e) in neutron beta decay, which were introduced by Ebel and Feldman (1957) in addition to the Jackson et al. (1957) set, do not survive at leading order in the large nucleon mass expansion within the Standard Model; they receive contributions only from weak magnetism and proton recoil at order O(E_e/m_N), with no contributions from radiative corrections of order O(alpha/pi).

## Method

The authors calculated the correlation coefficients S(E_e) and U(E_e) within the standard effective V-A theory of low-energy weak interactions, using the amplitude of neutron beta decay from Ivanov (2013) that includes one-virtual-photon exchange corrections, weak magnetism, and proton recoil. They evaluated the electron-energy and angular distribution for polarized neutrons, polarized electrons, and unpolarized protons, computing the traces over nucleon and lepton degrees of freedom to extract the coefficients. Beyond the Standard Model contributions were included using the phenomenological coupling constants of BSM interactions from Jackson et al. (1957) and the second class currents from Weinberg (1958).

## Key numbers

- S(E_e)^(SM) = -2.83 x 10^(-4) (SM contribution from weak magnetism and proton recoil, using g_A = 1.2764, m_e = 0.5110 MeV, m_N = 938.9188 MeV)
- U(E_e)^(SM) = 0 (no SM contribution from weak magnetism and proton recoil)
- The isovector anomalous magnetic moment of the nucleon kappa = kappa_p - kappa_n = 3.7059, with kappa_p = 1.7929 and kappa_n = -1.9130
- The end-point energy of the electron-energy spectrum E_0 = 1.2926 MeV

## Context

This work extends the theoretical analysis of neutron beta decay correlation coefficients beyond the well-studied set from Jackson et al. (1957), specifically addressing the S(E_e) and U(E_e) coefficients introduced by Ebel and Feldman (1957) that had been shown not to survive at leading order in Ivanov (2021). The authors provide the full SM background at the level of a few parts of 10^(-4), which enables experimental searches for BSM interactions using transversally polarized decay electrons as proposed by Bodek (2016), with the tensor BSM contributions proportional to Re(C_T - C_bar_T) and second class current contributions from Re(g_2(0)) and Re(f_3(0)).
