# Modification of surface energy in nuclear multifragmentation

> Within the statistical multifragmentation model we study modifications of the surface and symmetry energy of primary fragments in the freeze-out volume. The ALADIN experimental data on multifragmentation obtained in reactions induced by high-energy projectiles with different neutron richness are analyzed. We have extracted the isospin dependence of the surface energy coefficient at different degrees of fragmentation. We conclude that the surface energy of hot fragments produced in multifragmentation reactions differs from the values extracted for isolated nuclei at low excitation. At high fragment multiplicity, it becomes nearly independent of the neutron content of the fragments.

## Key claim

The surface energy coefficient of hot fragments produced in multifragmentation reactions differs from the standard value for isolated cold nuclei (B0 ≈ 18 MeV), and at high fragment multiplicity it becomes nearly independent of the neutron content of the fragments, contrary to the isospin dependence observed at lower excitation energies.

## Method

The authors used the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) to simulate the disassembly of equilibrated thermal sources representing projectile spectators from 600 MeV/nucleon collisions of 129Xe, 197Au, and 238U projectiles with various targets. They extracted the power-law parameter τ from fits to charge distributions of fragments with Z = 5–15 in different bins of reduced bound charge Zbound/Z0, and compared these to experimental ALADIN data to determine the surface energy coefficient B0 that best reproduces the observed τ values for each projectile and excitation energy range.

## Key numbers

- Standard surface coefficient for isolated cold nuclei: B0 ≈ 18 MeV, adopted from the Weizsaecker mass formula
- Critical temperature for nuclear liquid-gas phase transition: Tc ≈ 18 MeV, used as model parameter for temperature dependence of surface tension
- For the bin Zbound/Z0 = 0.85, required B0 values: ≈ 20.0 MeV for Xe, 18.1 MeV for Au, and 17.1 MeV for U sources
- At Zbound/Z0 = 0.65 and 0.55, B0 values converge to ≈ 18–19 MeV for all three sources without essential variation
- To reproduce Xe data at large Zbound via Tc variation alone, Tc would need to increase to ≈ 24 MeV; for U sources, Tc would need to decrease to ≈ 14 MeV
- Theoretical calculations predict Tc variation within only 2–3% over the N/Z range from 1.39 to 1.59

## Context

This work extends previous ALADIN analyses (Botvina 1995, Xi) that established the SMM framework for describing multifragmentation and the scaling behavior of fragment multiplicities. It builds on earlier extraction of symmetry energy reduction in hot nuclei (LeFevre, Shetty 2005) and on studies of Tc variations in multifragmentation (Ogul, Karna, Nihal). The results enable future investigations of in-medium modifications of nuclear properties relevant for astrophysical applications such as supernova II explosions and neutron star formation, and suggest that isotope distributions and neutron measurements (Sfienti, Trautmann) will be needed to further constrain source ensembles at high excitation energies.
