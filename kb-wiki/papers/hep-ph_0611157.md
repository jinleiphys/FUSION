# Lectures on multi-particle production in the Glasma

> In the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory, colliding sheets of Colored Glass form a strongly interacting, non-equilibrium state called the Glasma. How Colored Glass shatters to form the Glasma, the properties of the Glasma, and how the Glasma thermalizes into a Quark Gluon Plasma(QGP) are questions of central interest in understanding the properties of the strongly interacting matter produced in heavy ion collisions. We argue that these questions can be addressed in the framework of field theories with strong time dependent external sources. Albeit such field theories are non-perturbative for arbitrarily weak coupling, moments of the multiplicity distribution can be computed systematically in powers of the coupling constant. We demonstrate that the average multiplicity can be (straightforwardly) computed to leading order in the coupling and (remarkably) to next-to-leading order as well. We relate our formalism to results from previous 2+1 and 3+1 dimensional numerical simulations of the Glasma fields. The expanding Glasma is unstable; small fluctuations in the initial conditions grow exponentially with the square root of the proper time. Whether this explosive growth leads to early thermalization in heavy ion collisions requires at present a better understanding of these fluctuations on the light cone. In the final lecture, motivated by recent work of Bialas and Jezabek, we discuss the widely observed phenomenon of limiting fragmentation in the CGC framework.

## Key claim

The average multiplicity in the Glasma can be computed systematically to leading order and, remarkably, to next-to-leading order in the coupling constant using a field-theoretic framework with strong time-dependent external sources, even though the theory is non-perturbative for arbitrarily weak coupling.

## Method

The authors develop a formalism for computing multi-particle production in a field theory coupled to strong time-dependent external sources, using a scalar \(\phi^3\) toy model as a simpler analogue of QCD. They derive a general combinatoric expression for the probability \(P_n\) to produce \(n\) particles in terms of cut connected vacuum-vacuum diagrams, and show that moments of the multiplicity distribution can be computed systematically in powers of the coupling constant. The average multiplicity is obtained from the generating function \(F(z)\) via \(\langle n \rangle = (\ln F)'(z=1)\), and the next-to-leading order result requires solving small fluctuation equations of motion with retarded boundary conditions.

## Key numbers

- The source densities scale as \(\rho_1, \rho_2 \sim 1/g\) for small \(x\) and/or large nuclei, where \(g\) is the coupling constant.
- At tree level (\(n_L=0\)), vacuum-vacuum graphs are all of order \(1/g^2\).
- The probability \(P_1\) for producing one particle is given by \(P_1 = e^{-\frac{1}{g^2}\sum_r b_r} \; \frac{b_1}{g^2}\), where \(b_r\) are the sums of \(r\)-particle cuts through connected vacuum-vacuum diagrams.
- The average number of cut sub-diagrams is \(\langle n_{\rm cut} \rangle = \frac{1}{g^2} \sum_r b_r\).
- Small fluctuations in initial conditions of the expanding Glasma grow exponentially with the square root of the proper time \(\tau\), i.e., \(\sim \exp(\sqrt{\tau})\).

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory framework for multi-particle production in heavy ion collisions, building on the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model and the JIMWLK renormalization group evolution. The formalism relates to previous 2+1 and 3+1 dimensional numerical simulations of Glasma fields by Krasnitz, Venugopalan, Lappi, and others, and to the AGK cutting rules of Abramovsky, Gribov, and Kancheli (1973) in reggeon field theory. The results enable systematic computation of multiplicity moments beyond leading order, and the instability of the expanding Glasma may provide insight into early thermalization of the Quark Gluon Plasma observed at RHIC.
