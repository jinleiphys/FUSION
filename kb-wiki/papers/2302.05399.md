# Correlations Between Neutrinoless Double-Beta, Double Gamow-Teller and Double-Magnetic Decays in the pnQRPA Framework

> We explore the relation between the nuclear matrix elements of neutrinoless double-beta ($0νββ$) decay and two other processes: double Gamow-Teller (DGT) and double-magnetic dipole (M1M1) transitions, with focus on medium-mass to heavy nuclei studied with the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) framework. We explore a wide span of isoscalar proton-neutron pairing strengths covering the typical range of values that describe well $β$- and two-neutrino $ββ$-decay data. Our results indicate good linear correlations between $0νββ$ and both DGT and M1M1 matrix elements. Together with future measurements of DGT and M1M1 transitions, these correlations could help constrain the values of the $0νββ$-decay nuclear matrix elements.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that within the pnQRPA framework, there are good linear correlations between neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements and both double Gamow-Teller and double-magnetic dipole matrix elements, but only when a wide range of isoscalar proton-neutron pairing strengths is considered; the correlation is weaker and shifted compared to other many-body methods, and the pnQRPA results are more spread than in other approaches.

## Method
The authors performed systematic spherical pnQRPA calculations for nine double-beta decay triplets (76Ge, 82Se, 96Zr, 100Mo, 116Cd, 124Sn, 128Te, 130Te, and 136Xe) using large no-core single-particle bases with 18-26 orbitals. They varied the isoscalar proton-neutron pairing strength gppT=0 over a conservative range of 0.6-0.8 and also adjusted it to reproduce measured 2νββ-decay half-lives, while fixing the isovector part gppT=1 to restore isospin symmetry by forcing the Fermi 2νββ-decay NME to vanish. The 0νββ-decay NMEs included two-body weak currents and short-range contributions.

## Key numbers
- Nine ββ-decay triplets were studied: 76Ge, 82Se, 96Zr, 100Mo, 116Cd, 124Sn, 128Te, 130Te, and 136Xe
- The correlation coefficient between 0νββ-decay and DGT NMEs in pnQRPA is r=0.85
- The best linear fit for the correlation is: M_DGT = -1.295 + 0.645 A^{-1/6} M^{0ν}_L(1b)
- g_pp^{T=1} values range from 0.82 (116Cd) to 0.96 (76Ge, 82Se) for M_F^{2ν}=0
- g_pp^{T=0} values adjusted to 2νββ half-lives range from 0.67-0.69 (136Xe) to 0.87-0.89 (100Mo) with 1.0 ≤ g_A^eff ≤ 1.27
- The conservative g_pp^{T=0} range used is 0.6-0.8 (except for 124Sn)
- Bare axial coupling g_A=1.27 was used

## Context
This work extends previous studies of correlations between 0νββ-decay and DGT transitions that were observed in nuclear shell-model (Shimizu 2018), energy-density functional theory (Rodriguez 2013), and other approaches, but where no correlation had been found in the pnQRPA framework (Simkovic 2011, Lv 2023). The paper also extends the study of double-magnetic dipole transitions from the nuclear shell-model work of Romeo (2021) to the pnQRPA framework. The correlations found could enable future measurements of DGT and M1M1 transitions to help constrain 0νββ-decay nuclear matrix elements, though the pnQRPA correlation differs from other many-body methods, partly due to the different role of 1+ multipoles in the 0νββ-decay NME.
