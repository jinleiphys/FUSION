# Bayesian inference of quark star equation of state using the NICER PSR J0030+0451 data

> We constrain the equation of state of quark stars within the Bayesian statistical approach using the mass and radius measurements of PSR J0030+0451 from NICER. Three types of bag models, with and without non-zero finite quark mass and/or superfluidity, are employed for quark stars made up with self-bound strange quark matter. We find the $90\%$ posterior credible boundary around the most probable values of the quark star maximum mass is $M_{\rm TOV}=2.38_{-0.23}^{+0.26}\,M_{\odot}$, within the model flexibility of the finite quark mass, the quark pairing gap, and the perturbative contribution from the one-gluon exchange. The radius of a canonical $1.4\,M_{\odot}$ quark star is $R_{\rm 1.4}\sim12.3\,{\rm km}$, smaller than the results based on neutron star models.

# Bayesian inference of quark star equation of state using the NICER PSR J0030+0451 data

## Key claim
The NICER mass and radius measurements of PSR J0030+0451, when interpreted under the assumption that the star is a quark star rather than a neutron star, support quark star equations of state with enhanced stiffness above that required to support the 2.14 solar mass pulsar MSP J0740+6620, yielding a maximum mass of 2.38 solar masses with 90% credible intervals, but only within the model flexibility of finite quark mass, quark pairing gap, and perturbative one-gluon exchange contributions.

## Method
The authors performed a Bayesian analysis using three types of bag model parametrizations for self-bound strange quark matter: a two-parameter normal quark matter model (effective bag constant and perturbative QCD parameter), a three-parameter Color-Flavor Locked (CFL) superfluid model adding the pairing gap, and a four-parameter CFL model also varying the strange quark mass. They incorporated three sequential constraints: stability conditions requiring non-strange quark matter to be unstable and strange quark matter to be absolutely stable, the mass measurement of MSP J0740+6620 as a lower limit on maximum mass, and a Gaussian kernel density estimate of the NICER mass-radius posterior for PSR J0030+0451 from the ST+PST model of Riley et al. (2019).

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass of quark stars (CFLm model, joint analysis): $M_{\rm TOV} = 2.38_{-0.23}^{+0.26}\,M_{\odot}$ (90% credible interval)
- Radius of a canonical $1.4\,M_{\odot}$ quark star (CFLm model, joint analysis): $R_{1.4} \sim 12.3$ km (central value), with 90% interval $12.39_{-0.94}^{+0.93}$ km
- Surface density of quark stars (CFLm model, joint analysis): $n_{\rm surf} = 0.19_{-0.03}^{+0.05}$ fm$^{-3}$ (90% credible interval)
- Effective bag constant (CFLm model, joint analysis): $B_{\rm eff}^{1/4} = 134.4_{-7.7}^{+11.1}$ MeV (90% credible interval)
- Perturbative QCD parameter (CFLm model, joint analysis): $a_4 = 0.53_{-0.11}^{+0.18}$ (90% credible interval)
- CFL pairing gap (CFLm model, joint analysis): $\Delta = 33.9_{-30.6}^{+44.8}$ MeV (90% credible interval)
- Strange quark mass (CFLm model, joint analysis): $m_s = 51_{-46}^{+66}$ MeV (90% credible interval)
- Relative difference in radius between quark stars with and without a crust at $1.4\,M_{\odot}$: less than 1.9%

## Context
This work provides the first Bayesian analysis of NICER data under the Bodmer-Witten hypothesis that all compact stars are quark stars rather than neutron stars, extending prior bag-model studies by Li et al. (2018) and others. It contrasts with the neutron star model analyses of the same NICER data by Riley et al. (2019) and Miller et al. (2019), finding quark stars to be more compact (approximately 12.3 km versus approximately 13 km for a 1.4 solar mass star). The results enable future comparisons with gravitational wave constraints from GW170817 and GW190425, and the correlation between the pairing gap and tidal deformability may inform studies of color superconductivity in binary merger events.
