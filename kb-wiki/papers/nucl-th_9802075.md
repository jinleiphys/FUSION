# Two-Nucleon Systems from Effective Field Theory

> We elaborate on a new technique for computing properties of nucleon-nucleon interactions in terms of an effective field theory derived from low energy NN scattering data. Details of how the expansion is carried out to higher orders are presented. Analytic formulae are given for the amplitude to subleading order in both the 1S0 and 3S1-3D1 channels.

## Key claim

The paper presents a consistent power-counting scheme for nucleon-nucleon interactions in effective field theory that cures the inconsistency in Weinberg's original proposal (1991), where graphs at a given order required counterterms from operators treated as higher order. The expansion works for momenta \(p \lesssim m_\pi\) and improves as the pion mass gets smaller, though the chiral expansion parameter \(\eta_\pi = m_\pi / \Lambda_{NN} = 0.46\) suggests the expansion will not converge rapidly.

## Method

The authors develop an effective field theory for nonrelativistic nucleons interacting via contact interactions and one-pion exchange, using dimensional regularization with the power divergence subtraction (PDS) scheme that subtracts both \(1/(D-4)\) poles (log divergences) and \(1/(D-3)\) poles (power divergences). They compute the scattering amplitude by summing bubble diagrams with contact vertices dressed to all orders, then expand in powers of momentum while retaining \(ap\) to all orders for the large scattering length case. The renormalization group equations for the contact couplings \(C_{2n}(\mu)\) are derived and solved, with boundary conditions set by the scattering length \(a\) and effective range parameters.

## Key numbers

- The new scale \(\Lambda_{NN} = 8\pi f^2 / (g_A^2 M) = 300\) MeV, where \(f = 132\) MeV is the pion decay constant
- The chiral expansion parameter \(\eta_\pi = m_\pi / \Lambda_{NN} = 0.46\), indicating the expansion will not converge rapidly
- The leading-order coupling in the PDS scheme: \(C_0(\mu) = (4\pi/M)(1/(-\mu + 1/a))\)
- The subleading coupling: \(C_2(\mu) = (4\pi/M)(1/(-\mu + 1/a))^2 (r_0/2)\), where \(r_0\) is the effective range
- The critical coupling for bound state formation in the Yukawa toy model is \(\eta_1 = 1.7\), while the physical value \(\eta_\pi = 0.46\) gives OPE phase shifts never exceeding \(\sim 0.25\) radians

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous proposal (Kaplan, Savage, Wise 1996) for a consistent power-counting scheme in NN effective field theory, correcting the inconsistency identified in Weinberg's original approach (1991). The PDS scheme is contrasted with minimal subtraction (MS) and momentum cutoff regularization, and the renormalization group analysis shows that for large scattering length the four-fermion interaction becomes a relevant operator near a UV fixed point, explaining why it has large effects at low velocity. The approach enables systematic computation of NN phase shifts with error estimates and provides a framework extendable to three-nucleon forces, hypernuclei (Savage and Wise 1996), and kaon condensation (Kaplan and Nelson 1986).
