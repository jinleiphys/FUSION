# Photons from Pb + Pb and S + Au Collisions at CERN SPS Energies

> The effects of the variation of vector meson masses and decay widths on photon production from hot strongly interacting matter formed after Pb + Pb and S + Au collisions at CERN SPS energies are considered. It has been shown that the present photon spectra measured by WA80 and WA98 Collaborations can not distinguish between the formation of quark matter and hadronic matter in the initial state.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the measured photon spectra from Pb+Pb and S+Au collisions at CERN SPS energies cannot distinguish between a scenario where the system initially forms quark-gluon plasma and one where it forms hadronic matter, nor can they clearly differentiate between in-medium dropping masses and free masses of vector mesons given current experimental uncertainties.

## Method

The authors computed thermal photon transverse momentum distributions by convolving static photon emission rates with a (1+1)-dimensional Bjorken-like hydrodynamical expansion model. For the hadronic matter phase, they included photon-producing reactions and decays involving π, ρ, ω, and η mesons, incorporating temperature-dependent masses and decay widths parametrized from finite-temperature field theory. For the quark-gluon plasma phase, they used the two-loop QCD Compton and annihilation rates with a bag model equation of state, and compared both scenarios against WA80 and WA98 experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For Pb+Pb collisions at 158 GeV per nucleon with dNπ/dy = 600 and τi = 1 fm/c: initial temperature Ti = 180 MeV for the QGP scenario (gk = 37), Ti = 230 MeV for the hadronic scenario with in-medium effects, and Ti = 270 MeV for the hadronic scenario without medium effects
- Critical temperature Tc = 160 MeV and freeze-out temperature Tf = 130 MeV used throughout
- For the QGP scenario in Pb+Pb, the mixed phase ends at τH = 8.4 fm/c
- For S+Au collisions at 200 GeV per nucleon with dNπ/dy = 225 and τi = 1.2 fm/c: Ti = 190 MeV for the QGP scenario, Ti = 230 MeV for the hadronic scenario with in-medium effects, and Ti = 270 MeV for the hadronic scenario without medium effects
- Cooling law parametrizations for Pb+Pb hadronic scenario: T = 0.230/τ^0.169 (with medium effects) and T = 0.266/τ^0.2157 (without medium effects)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of thermal photon production from S+Au collisions by Arbex et al. (1997), Dumitru et al. (1993), and others that did not include medium effects on hadronic properties. The authors apply their previously developed finite-temperature field theory calculations of in-medium vector meson masses and widths (Sarkar et al., 1997) to the photon spectra measured by the WA80 and WA98 Collaborations. The results indicate that improved experimental precision is needed to distinguish between competing scenarios, and the analysis suggests that the scenario without medium effects for S+Au collisions likely exceeds the experimental upper bound, consistent with earlier analysis by Dumitru et al. (1993).
