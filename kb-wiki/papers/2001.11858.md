# Studying QGP with flow: A theory overview

> I review recent developments in the phenomenological study of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) transport properties based on a personal selection of results that were presented at Quark Matter 2019. The constraints on the temperature dependence of QGP shear and bulk viscosity are summarized. I discuss new theory advancements towards more realistic 3D dynamical simulations of heavy-ion collisions at finite baryon density. The challenges and opportunities of applying hydrodynamics to small collision systems are highlighted.

## Key claim
The review argues that phenomenological studies of flow observables have become a precision tool for extracting QGP transport properties, but emphasizes that the extracted values depend strongly on modeling choices for the pre-equilibrium phase and that a realistic EKT pre-hydrodynamic phase leads to a 35% larger extracted QGP bulk viscosity compared to simulations without it.

## Method
The paper reviews recent developments in relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (MUSIC) coupled with IP-Glasma initial conditions, an effective QCD kinetic theory pre-equilibrium phase (KøMPøST), and hadronic transport (UrQMD) to perform multi-stage dynamical simulations of heavy-ion collisions. Bayesian statistical analyses are used to systematically constrain the temperature dependence of QGP shear and bulk viscosities by calibrating against combined flow measurements from RHIC and the LHC. For low-energy collisions at the RHIC Beam Energy Scan, full 3D dynamical simulations with dynamic initialization schemes are employed to model the finite baryon density regime.

## Key numbers
- Effective shear viscosity \((\eta/s)_\mathrm{eff} = 0.12\) used in the IP-Glasma + EKT + MUSIC + UrQMD framework to reproduce flow measurements from 200 GeV to 5020 GeV
- The additional EKT phase leads to a 35% larger extracted QGP bulk viscosity compared to simulations without it
- The saturation-based IP-Glasma and EKRT initial conditions prefer an effective shear viscosity between 0.12 and 0.20 in the hydrodynamic phase
- The peak temperature of bulk viscosity was adjusted from \(T_\mathrm{peak} = 180\) MeV to 160 MeV in the IP-Glasma hybrid framework
- The universal hydrodynamic response to collision geometry dominates flow production in collisions with \(dN^\mathrm{ch}/d\eta > 10\) at mid-rapidity
- Causality conditions in a typical p+A collision are approximately 20% closer to the bound than those in an A+A collision

## Context
This review extends the line of work on QGP transport property extraction using relativistic hydrodynamics that began with the IP-Glasma hybrid framework (Gale 2012, Ryu 2015) and the Duke-OSU Bayesian inference (Bernhard 2016, Bernhard 2019). It contrasts the IP-Glasma and EKRT initial condition approaches (Niemi 2015) and highlights the JETSCAPE Collaboration's first simultaneous Bayesian calibration using flow measurements from both RHIC and the LHC (Paquet 2019). The paper enables future work on constraining the baryon chemical potential dependence of QGP viscosity through the RHIC Beam Energy Scan II data and on understanding the limits of hydrodynamics in small collision systems.
