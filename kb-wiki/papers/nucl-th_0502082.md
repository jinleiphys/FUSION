# The role of the pion pair term in the theory of the weak axial meson exchange currents

> The structure of the weak axial pion exchange current is discussed in various models. It is shown how the interplay of the chiral invariance and the double counting problem restricts uniquely the form of the pion potential term, in the case when the nuclear dynamics is described by the Schroedinger equation with the static nucleon-nucleon potential.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the weak axial pion exchange current (WAEC) must include a specific "pion pair term" (potential current) to satisfy the partially conserved axial current (PCAC) relation when nuclear dynamics is described by the Schrödinger equation with a static nucleon-nucleon potential; without this term, the current suffers from double counting or violates chiral invariance.

## Method
The authors construct the weak axial pion exchange current from two chiral Lagrangian models: the chiral perturbation theory Lagrangian and the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) Lagrangian. They isolate the positive-frequency (reducible) part of the nucleon Born term to avoid double counting with the static one-pion exchange potential, and show that the remaining contact term (the potential current) is identical in both models. They then verify that this potential current satisfies the PCAC continuity equation when combined with the commutator of the static potential and the one-nucleon axial charge density.

## Key numbers
- The space component of the weak axial exchange current is of order O(1/M³), where M is the nucleon mass.
- The potential current derived in Eq. (DvJbBf) contains a factor g_A * g²_πNN / (2M)³, where g_A is the axial coupling constant and g_πNN is the pion-nucleon coupling constant.
- The Δ(1232) isobar contribution in the standard current (Eq. LRO) includes the factor (f_πNΔ / f_πNN)² * 2M / [9(M_Δ - M)], with M_Δ the Δ isobar mass.
- The contact term Δj^a_5(pv) in Eq. (DvJbBf) is of the same leading order in 1/M as other pion exchange currents.

## Context
This work extends the chiral-invariant construction of weak axial exchange currents from earlier hard-pion models (Iachello-Truhlik, 1970s; Ivanov-Truhlik, 1980s) and the extended S-matrix method (Adam-Truhlik-Adam, 1980s). It directly addresses the double-counting problem overlooked in currents derived by Schiavilla (1990s), Towner-Khanna (1990s), and the HBChPT approach of Park-Min-Rho (1990s). The result enables consistent use of these currents with Schrödinger-equation-based nuclear wave functions, for example in calculations of deuteron weak disintegration by low-energy neutrinos.
