# BEC-BCS Crossover in Neutron Matter with Renormalization Group based Effective Interactions

> We study pure neutron matter in the BEC-BCS crossover regime using renormalization group based low-momentum interactions within the Nozières-Schmitt-Rink framework. This is an attempt to go beyond the mean field description for low-density matter. We work in the basis of so-called Weinberg eigenvectors where the operator $G_0V$ is diagonal, which proves to be an excellent choice that allows one to use non-local interactions in a very convenient way. We study the importance of correlations as a function of density. We notice that there is a significant reduction of the BCS critical temperature at low-densities as the neutron matter approaches the unitary limit.

## Key claim

The authors find that including pair correlations above the critical temperature via the Nozières-Schmitt-Rink (NSR) framework significantly reduces the BCS critical temperature at low densities as neutron matter approaches the unitary limit, but this reduction is much smaller than predicted by a contact interaction except at extremely low densities (below about 0.002 of nuclear saturation density).

## Method

The authors use renormalization group based low-momentum interactions (Vlowk) derived from the AV18 potential as the two-body input, working in the basis of Weinberg eigenvectors where the operator G0V is diagonal. They compute the critical temperature via the Thouless criterion (pole of the in-medium T matrix) and calculate the correlated density within the NSR ladder approximation, subtracting the Hartree-Fock-like contribution to isolate genuine correlation effects.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-neutron scattering length: approximately -18.7 fm
- BCS and NSR results agree above kF ~ 0.8 fm^-1, corresponding to density ~0.017 fm^-3 ~ 0.1 ρ0 (where ρ0 = 0.17 fm^-3 is nuclear saturation density)
- The point where neutron matter is closest to the unitary Fermi gas occurs at 1/(kFa) ~ -0.2, corresponding to density ~0.0007 fm^-3 ~ 0.004 ρ0
- For a contact interaction in the unitary limit, the NSR scheme predicts Tc/EF = 0.23
- Conversion factor: ℏ²/m = 41.44 MeV fm²; ℏc = 197.3 MeV fm

## Context

This work extends the study of pairing in neutron matter beyond the BCS mean-field description using the NSR framework, which was originally developed for symmetric matter (Rostock group, 1990s) and for contact interactions (SadeMelo, 1993). The use of Weinberg eigenvalues to diagnose pairing instabilities was previously applied at zero temperature by Ramanan (2007). The results enable more realistic calculations of neutron star crust properties (glitches and cooling rates) and connect low-density neutron matter to the BEC-BCS crossover studied in ultracold atom experiments (Regal, 2004; Zwierlein, 2004). The authors note that corrections beyond NSR, such as screening of the interaction (Shen, 2002) and effective mass effects (Hebeler and Schwenk, 2010), may further suppress the critical temperature.
