# Light Clusters and Pasta Phases in Warm and Dense Nuclear Matter

> The pasta phases are calculated for warm stellar matter in a framework of relativistic mean-field models, including the possibility of light cluster formation. Results from three different semiclassical approaches are compared with a quantum statistical calculation. Light clusters are considered as point-like particles, and their abundances are determined from the minimization of the free energy. The couplings of the light-clusters to mesons are determined from experimental chemical equilibrium constants and many-body quantum statistical calculations. The effect of these light clusters on the chemical potentials is also discussed. It is shown that including heavy clusters, light clusters are present until larger nucleonic densities, although with smaller mass fractions.

## Key claim

The inclusion of heavy clusters (pasta phases) in warm stellar matter calculations modifies the abundances of light clusters, keeping them present up to larger nucleonic densities but with smaller mass fractions, and the cluster-vector meson coupling parameter η must be reduced to approximately 0.65–0.70 (rather than η=1) to reproduce experimental chemical equilibrium constants at temperatures of 5–10 MeV.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach with the FSU parametrization to describe warm nuclear matter at subsaturation densities (n_B ≤ 0.15 fm⁻³) and temperatures T ≤ 20 MeV, including protons, neutrons, and light clusters (deuterons, tritons, helions, α-particles) as point-like particles with medium-modified masses. The pasta phases (droplet, rod, slab, tube, bubble configurations) were calculated using a finite-temperature Thomas-Fermi density functional formalism within Wigner-Seitz cells, with cluster-meson couplings fixed by fitting to experimental chemical equilibrium constants from heavy ion collision data (Qin et al., 2012) and to Mott densities from quantum statistical calculations. The free energy was minimized to determine cluster abundances, and results from three semiclassical approaches were compared with a quantum statistical calculation.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum binding energies used for light clusters are: B₀d = 2.224 MeV (deuteron), B₀t = 8.482 MeV (triton), B₀h = 7.718 MeV (helion), and B₀α = 28.296 MeV (α-particle).
- The cluster-vector meson coupling ratios for the FSU model are x_vd = 3.516, x_vt = 4.382, x_vh = 4.624, and x_vα = 5.675 (with η=1), but the experimental equilibrium constants require η ≈ 0.65–0.70.
- The calculations were performed for a global proton fraction Y_p = 0.41, at temperatures T = 5 MeV and T = 10 MeV, and baryon densities up to n_B ≈ 0.15 fm⁻³.
- The α-particle equilibrium constant K_c[α] shows a strong dependence on η, with η=1 giving values too small compared to experiment, while η ≈ 0.65–0.70 reproduces the experimental data of Qin et al. (2012).

## Context

This work extends previous RMF calculations of pasta phases (Pais et al., 2015) by including light clusters as additional degrees of freedom with more realistic cluster-meson couplings, and compares with quantum statistical calculations (Röpke, 2015) and experimental chemical equilibrium constants from heavy ion collisions (Qin et al., 2012; Hagel et al., 2012). The results enable improved equations of state for core-collapse supernova simulations, where light clusters affect neutrino energies and shock revival (Arcones et al., 2008; Furusawa et al., 2013, 2016), and provide a bridge between microscopic quantum statistical approaches and semiempirical density functional methods for warm dense matter.
