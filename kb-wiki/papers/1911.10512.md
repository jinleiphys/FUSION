# Influence of strong magnetic field on the structure properties of strange quark stars

> We investigate the thermodynamic properties of strange quark matter under the strong magnetic field in the framework of the MIT bag model in two cases of bag constants. We consider two cases of the magnetic field, the uniform magnetic field and the density-dependent magnetic field to calculate the equation of state of strange quark matter. For the case of density-dependent magnetic field, we use a Gaussian equation with two free parameters $β$ and $θ$ and use two different sets of the parameters for the magnetic field changes (a slow and a fast decrease of the magnetic field from the center to the surface). Our results show that the energy conditions based on the limitation of the energy-momentum tensor, are satisfied in the corresponding conditions. We also show that the equation of state of strange quark matter becomes stiffer by increasing the magnetic field. In this paper, we also calculate the structure parameters of a pure strange quark star using the equation of state. We investigate the compactification factor ($2M/R$) and the surface redshift of star in different conditions. The results show that the strange quark star is denser than the neutron star and it is more compact in the presence of the stronger magnetic field. As another result, the compactification factor increases when we use a slow increase of the magnetic field from the surface to the center. Eventually, we compare our results with the observational results for some strange star candidates, and we find that the structure of the strange star candidates is comparable to that of the star in our model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that strange quark stars become more compact (higher compactification factor and surface redshift) with increasing magnetic field strength, but this effect is conditional on the specific choice of bag constant (fixed vs. density-dependent) and on the spatial profile of the magnetic field (uniform vs. density-dependent with fast or slow decrease from center to surface). The abstract's statement that "the strange quark star is denser than the neutron star and it is more compact in the presence of the stronger magnetic field" glosses over the fact that the maximum gravitational mass actually decreases with increasing magnetic field, while compactness increases.

## Method

The authors use the MIT bag model with Landau quantization to compute the equation of state of strange quark matter (up, down, and strange quarks) under strong magnetic fields, considering both a fixed bag constant (90 MeV/fm³) and a density-dependent bag constant parameterized by a Gaussian function fitted to CERN experimental data. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations to obtain the mass, radius, compactification factor (2M/R), and surface redshift of pure strange quark stars, comparing uniform magnetic fields (0, 5×10¹⁷ G, 5×10¹⁸ G) with density-dependent magnetic fields using two parameter sets: Set A (β=0.05, θ=2) for fast decrease and Set B (β=0.1, θ=1) for slow decrease of the field from center to surface.

## Key numbers

- Fixed bag constant (90 MeV/fm³), zero magnetic field: maximum mass = 1.22 M_sun, radius = 7.10 km, compactification factor = 0.68, surface redshift = 0.42
- Fixed bag constant, uniform field 5×10¹⁸ G: maximum mass = 0.97 M_sun, radius = 5.32 km, compactification factor = 0.73, surface redshift = 0.47
- Fixed bag constant, density-dependent field Set B (slow decrease): maximum mass = 1.37 M_sun, radius = 6.99 km, compactification factor = 0.78, surface redshift = 0.54
- Density-dependent bag constant, zero magnetic field: maximum mass = 1.80 M_sun, radius = 8.65 km, compactification factor = 0.82, surface redshift = 0.61
- Density-dependent bag constant, uniform field 5×10¹⁸ G: maximum mass = 1.42 M_sun, radius = 6.37 km, compactification factor = 0.88, surface redshift = 0.71
- Density-dependent bag constant, density-dependent field Set B: maximum mass = 1.44 M_sun, radius = 6.45 km, compactification factor = 0.89, surface redshift = 0.71 (maximum value in the paper)
- Mass-radius relation exponent α: for fixed bag constant with density-dependent field, α = 3.65 (Set A) and α = 3.41 (Set B); for density-dependent bag constant, α = 3.21 (Set A) and α = 3.20 (Set B)
- Surface magnetic field assumed: B_surf = 5×10¹³ G
- Central magnetic field reaches approximately 4.9×10¹⁸ G (Set A) and 3.01×10¹⁸ G (Set B)

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Bordbar et al. on polarized and magnetized strange quark stars (Bordbar et al., various years) and by Mukhopadhyay on Landau quantization in white dwarfs, by systematically comparing uniform versus density-dependent magnetic field profiles and fixed versus density-dependent bag constants. The results are compared with observational data for six strange star candidates (RX J185635-3754, Her X-1, 4U 1608-52, 4U 1820-30, and two models for SAX J1808.4-3659), finding that the density-dependent bag constant model with density-dependent magnetic fields best matches the compactness of SAX J1808.4-3659. The paper enables future work on more realistic magnetic field profiles and their effects on the observational signatures of strange quark stars.
