# Gamow shell model description of neutron-rich He hyper-isotopes

> The Gamow shell model (GSM) framework has been extended to the study of weakly bound hypernuclei. As a first application, the neutron-rich He hyper-isotope chains, from 6ΛHe to 9ΛHe have been investigated to accurately account for the loosely bound or neutron-unbound character of hypernuclear many-body states. The energy spectra calculated with a phenomenological Hamiltonian show good agreement with experimental data. In particular, neutron-emitting resonant states are predicted for the neutron-rich nuclei 5-7He and the hypernucleus 6ΛHe. Furthermore, one-neutron densities exhibit the long-range character of weakly bound and resonant states. This study demonstrates that GSM is a practical tool for describing the complex structure of hypernuclei, especially for those close to drip lines.

## Key claim
The Gamow shell model (GSM) is extended to hypernuclei for the first time, enabling the description of weakly bound and resonant states in neutron-rich He hyper-isotopes. The authors note that only four experimental binding energies for the considered hypernuclei are available, and these can bear large experimental errors, making the fit analysis and theoretical error assessment difficult.

## Method
The GSM framework was applied within the cluster orbital shell model (COSM) using a Berggren basis containing bound, resonance, and scattering states. The Hamiltonian included a Woods-Saxon core-valence potential for an inert ⁴He core, a Furutani-Horiuchi-Tamagaki (FHT) interaction for nucleon-nucleon pairs, and a YN interaction derived from the SU(3)ₓ approximation applied to the FHT interaction, with ΛN-ΣN conversion channels included. The two SU(3)ₓ coupling constants C⁸ᵃ and C⁸ˢ were adjusted to reproduce the Λ binding energy of ⁷ΛHe.

## Key numbers
- ⁵He 3/2⁻ ground state: energy 0.759 MeV, width 0.669 MeV (experimental: 0.798 MeV, 0.648 MeV)
- ⁶ΛHe 1⁻ ground state: energy −3.64 MeV, Λ binding energy 4.40 MeV (experimental BΛ = 4.18 ± 0.1 MeV)
- ⁶ΛHe 0⁻ state: width 0.83 MeV (predicted broad resonance)
- ⁷ΛHe 1/2⁺ ground state: energy −6.22 MeV, Λ binding energy 5.23 MeV (experimental BΛ = 5.55 ± 0.10 ± 0.11 MeV)
- ⁷ΛHe 3/2⁺ state: Λ binding energy 3.49 MeV (experimental peak at BΛ = 3.65 ± 0.20 ± 0.11 MeV)
- ⁸ΛHe 1⁻ ground state: energy −7.24 MeV, Λ binding energy 6.75 MeV (experimental BΛ = 7.17 ± 0.7 MeV)
- ⁹ΛHe 1/2⁺ ground state: energy −10.60 MeV, Λ binding energy 8.28 MeV
- ⁶He 0⁺ ground state: energy −0.994 MeV (experimental: −0.975 MeV)
- ⁸He 0⁺ ground state: energy −2.32 MeV (experimental: −3.11 MeV)

## Context
This work extends the Gamow shell model, previously applied to halo and resonance nuclei (Michel 2021, Li 2021), to hypernuclei for the first time. It builds on cluster orbital shell model calculations by Myo and Hiyama (2023) for ⁶ΛHe–⁹ΛHe, which did not include ΛN-ΣN conversions, and on α+Λ+n calculations by Hiyama (1996, 2005, 2015). The results enable predictions for neutron-rich hypernuclei near drip lines, particularly ⁹ΛHe which is a target for future experiments at J-PARC.
