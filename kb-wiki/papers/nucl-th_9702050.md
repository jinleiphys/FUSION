# Deuteron Electroweak Disintegration

> We study the deuteron electrodisintegration with inclusion of the neutral currents focusing on the helicity asymmetry of the exclusive cross section in coplanar geometry. We stress that a measurement of this asymmetry in the quasi elastic region is of interest for an experimental determination of the weak form factors of the nucleon, allowing one to obtain the parity violating electron neutron asymmetry. Numerically, we consider the reaction at low momentum transfer and discuss the sensitivity of the helicity asymmetry to the strangeness radius and magnetic moment. The problems coming from the finite angular acceptance of the spectrometers are also considered.

## Key claim

The helicity asymmetry of the exclusive cross section for deuteron electrodisintegration in coplanar geometry, which vanishes in parity-conserving theory, is sensitive to the strangeness radius and magnetic moment of the nucleon at low momentum transfer, provided the finite angular acceptance of spectrometers is accounted for.

## Method

The authors calculate the parity-violating helicity asymmetry for the exclusive deuteron electrodisintegration reaction in coplanar kinematics, including both one-photon and one-Z⁰ boson exchange amplitudes in the Standard Model. They evaluate the γ-Z⁰ interference contribution to the cross section using the extreme relativistic limit for the electron and neglecting parity-violating nuclear interactions, meson exchange currents, and isobar excitation currents in the quasi-elastic region. The nuclear current matrix elements are computed in the center-of-mass frame of the outgoing nucleons using non-relativistic wave functions and the Sachs form of the electromagnetic and weak vector currents.

## Key numbers

- The calculation is performed at momentum transfer Q² = 0.1 (GeV)⁻² (i.e., Q² = 0.1 GeV²/c²)
- The weak mixing angle is taken as sin²ϑ_W ≃ 0.092, giving the electron vector coupling g_V^e = -1 + 4 sin²ϑ_W ≃ -0.092
- The axial-vector coupling of the electron is g_A^e = 1
- The nucleon electromagnetic form factors use the dipole form G_D^V(Q²) = (1 + Q²/M_V²)⁻² with M_V² = 0.71 GeV²
- The axial form factor uses a dipole with cut-off mass M_A = 1.032 GeV
- The neutron β-decay axial coupling is g_A = 1.2601 ± 0.0025
- The SU(3) octet axial form factor at Q²=0 is G_A^(8)(0) = 0.334 ± 0.014

## Context

This work extends previous studies of parity-violating electron scattering that focused on elastic scattering and inclusive inelastic reactions (Musolf et al., 1994; Donnelly, 1992; Horowitz, 1993) to the exclusive coincidence channel using the deuteron as a quasi-free neutron target. The approach is motivated by the need to disentangle strange-quark contributions to nucleon form factors from radiative corrections, which complicate interpretations from proton-only targets (Musolf, 1990). The results enable experimental determination of the parity-violating electron-neutron asymmetry through measurements of (e, e′p) and (e, e′n) reactions, complementing ongoing experiments at Bates, CEBAF, and MAMI.
