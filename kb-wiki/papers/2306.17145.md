# Nucleosynthesis of light nuclei and hypernuclei in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=3 GeV

> We analyze the experimental data on nuclei and hypernuclei yields recently obtained by the STAR collaboration. The hybrid dynamical and statistical approaches which have been developed previously are able to describe the experimental data reasonably. We discuss the intriguing difference between the yields of normal nuclei and hypernuclei which may be related to the properties of hypermatter at subnuclear densities. Most importantly new (hyper-)nuclei could be detected via particle correlations, and such measurements are relevant to pin down the production mechanism.

## Key claim

The hybrid dynamical-statistical approach combining UrQMD transport with clusterization and statistical decay can describe STAR data on light nuclei and hypernuclei yields, but the observed ratio of $^3_{\Lambda}$H to $^4_{\Lambda}$H (less than a factor 2) versus $^3$He to $^4$He (more than a factor 5) cannot be explained within existing models and may carry information on hyperon interaction during hypernuclei formation.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD transport model with a hard Skyrme equation of state to simulate central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=3 GeV (impact parameters $b \leq 3$ fm). After 20-40 fm/c, they applied a clusterization of baryons (CB) procedure that groups nucleons and hyperons into excited clusters based on velocity proximity (critical velocity $v_c$ = 0.14c or 0.22c) and coordinate proximity (distance less than $2\cdot A^{1/3}$ fm). These clusters, with subnuclear densities around $\sim$0.1$\rho_0$, were then decayed using the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) generalized for hypernuclei, including a Fermi-break-up model for light fragments.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 3 GeV for central Au+Au collisions
- Impact parameter selection: $b \leq 3$ fm for central events
- Clusterization time window: 20-40 fm/c after collision start
- Critical velocity parameters: $v_c$ = 0.14c and 0.22c, previously extracted from FOPI experimental data
- Subnuclear density of clusters: $\rho_c \approx \frac{1}{6} \rho_0$, where $\rho_0 \approx 0.15$ fm$^{-3}$
- Excitation energy of primary clusters: around 10 MeV per nucleon
- Experimental yield ratio of $^3_{\Lambda}$H to $^4_{\Lambda}$H: less than a factor 2
- Experimental yield ratio of $^3$He to $^4$He: more than a factor 5

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous hybrid approach (Botvina 2021, 2022) that successfully described normal light nuclei production in FOPI experiments, including the cross-over behavior of $^3$He and $^4$He yields with beam energy. The paper analyzes recent STAR data on hypernuclei production and identifies a puzzle in the relative yields of $^4_{\Lambda}$H and $^3_{\Lambda}$H compared to normal helium isotopes. This enables future investigations of hypermatter properties at subnuclear densities, relevant for astrophysical models of supernova explosions and binary neutron star mergers, and motivates new experiments measuring multiple hypernuclei species in the same reactions.
