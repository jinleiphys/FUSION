# Anisotropic escape mechanism and elliptic flow of bottomonia

> We study the role of anisotropic escape in generating the elliptic flow of bottomonia produced in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. We implement temperature-dependent decay widths for the various bottomonium states, to calculate their survival probability when traversing through the anisotropic hot medium formed in non-central collisions. We employ the recently developed 3+1d quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamic simulation to model the space-time evolution of the quark-gluon plasma. We provide a quantitative prediction for transverse momentum dependence of bottomonium elliptic flow and nuclear modification factor for Pb$\,+\,$Pb collisions in $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76$ TeV at the Large Hadron Collider.

## Key claim

The elliptic flow of bottomonia in non-central heavy-ion collisions arises primarily from the anisotropic escape mechanism, the path-length-dependent survival probability of bound states moving through an expanding, spatially anisotropic quark-gluon plasma, rather than from hydrodynamic flow of the bottomonia themselves, and the predicted $v_2$ for $\Upsilon(1S)$ is at the level of a few percent, with the exact value depending on the centrality class and transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors used the 3+1d quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics (QaHydro) framework to simulate the space-time evolution of the quark-gluon plasma created in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76$ TeV. They computed temperature-dependent thermal decay widths for five bottomonium states ($\Upsilon(1S)$, $\Upsilon(2S)$, $\Upsilon(3S)$, $\chi_b(1P)$, $\chi_b(2P)$) by solving the 3-D Schrödinger equation with a complex heavy-quark potential, then calculated each state’s survival probability along straight-line trajectories through the evolving medium. The inclusive $\Upsilon(1S)$ yield was obtained by summing directly produced and feed-down contributions from excited states using fixed fractions from $p+p$ data.

## Key numbers

* For Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76$ TeV in the 40–50% centrality class (average impact parameter 10.41 fm), the elliptic flow $v_2$ of $\Upsilon(1S)$ (including feed-down) is at the level of a few percent and increases with $p_T$ before saturating.
* The intrinsic formation times in the bottomonium rest frame are $\tau^0_{\rm form} = 0.2$ fm/$c$ for $\Upsilon(1S)$, 0.4 fm/$c$ for $\Upsilon(2S)$ and $\chi_b(1P)$, and 0.6 fm/$c$ for $\Upsilon(3S)$ and $\chi_b(2P)$.
* The initial central temperature of the hydrodynamic evolution is $T_0 = 600$ MeV, the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is $\eta/s = 0.2$, and the evolution begins at $\tau = 0.2$ fm/$c$.
* The freeze-out temperature is $T_f = 130$ MeV, and the critical temperature is $T_c = 160$ MeV.
* Feed-down fractions used for inclusive $\Upsilon(1S)$: $f_{2S\rightarrow 1S}=8.6\%$, $f_{3S\rightarrow 1S}=1\%$, $f_{1P\rightarrow 1S}=17\%$, $f_{2P \rightarrow 1S}=5.1\%$, $f_{3P \rightarrow 1S}=1.5\%$.
* Maximum $v_2$ for inclusive $\Upsilon(1S)$ is obtained for the 50–60% centrality class.

## Context

This work extends the anisotropic escape mechanism previously applied to $J/\psi$ (Wang, 2002) and qualitatively discussed for bottomonia (Das, 2018) by providing quantitative predictions using a realistic 3+1d hydrodynamic medium evolution (QaHydro framework of Alqahtani, 2015, 2017). The results are consistent with the finding of Du (2017) that excited bottomonium states have larger $v_2$ than the ground state due to weaker binding. The paper enables future comparisons with the first ALICE measurement of $\Upsilon(1S)$ $v_2$ at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV (Acharya, 2019) and motivates inclusion of medium-induced bound-state transitions predicted by open quantum system approaches (Brambilla, 2016; Blaizot, 2017).
