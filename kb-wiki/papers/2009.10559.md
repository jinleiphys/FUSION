# Quarkonium in Quark-Gluon Plasma: Open Quantum System Approaches Re-examined

> Dissociation of quarkonium in quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is a long standing topic in relativistic heavy-ion collisions because it has been believed to signal one of the fundamental natures of the QGP -- Debye screening due to the liberation of color degrees of freedom. Among recent new theoretical developments is the application of open quantum system framework to quarkonium in the QGP. Open system approach enables us to describe how dynamical as well as static properties of QGP influences the time evolution of quarkonium in a coherent way. Currently, there are several master equations for quarkonium corresponding to various scale assumptions, each derived in different theoretical frameworks. In this review, all of the existing master equations are systematically rederived as Lindblad equations in a unified framework. Also, as one of the most relevant descriptions in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, quantum Brownian motion of heavy quark pair in the QGP is studied in detail. The quantum Brownian motion is parametrized by a few fundamental quantities of QGP such as real and imaginary parts of heavy quark potential (complex potential), heavy quark momentum diffusion constant, and thermal dipole self-energy constant, which constitute in-medium self-energy of a static quarkonium. This indicates that the yields of quarkonia such as $J/ψ$ and $Υ$ in the relativistic heavy-ion collisions have the potential to determine these fundamental quantities.

## Key claim

This review systematically rederives all existing master equations for quarkonium in quark-gluon plasma as Lindblad equations within a unified framework, showing that the quantum Brownian motion description, parametrized by the complex heavy quark potential, heavy quark momentum diffusion constant, and thermal dipole self-energy constant, is the most relevant for relativistic heavy-ion collisions, though the applicability of each equation depends on specific scale assumptions.

## Method

The author re-examines all microscopic derivations of open quantum system dynamics for quarkonia in QGP, previously obtained via influence functional in path integral formalism, dynamical evolution of density matrix in operator formalism, and Dyson-Schwinger equations on the closed-time path, and reformulates them as Lindblad equations using a unified quantum mechanical picture of a quarkonium coupled to QGP particles. The review then studies in detail the quantum Brownian motion regime for a heavy quark pair, deriving the Lindblad equation from the microscopic Hamiltonian under weak-coupling assumptions and identifying the relevant QGP parameters that constitute the in-medium self-energy of a static quarkonium.

## Key numbers

- The spectral function analysis from lattice QCD shows that J/ψ and ηc peak structures survive up to T ≃ 1.6 Tc and disappear below 1.9 Tc (Asakawa and Hatsuda, 2004).
- The review covers derivations from at least eight prior works: Akamatsu (2013, 2014), Blaizot et al. (2015, 2017, 2018), Brambilla et al. (2016, 2017), and Yao et al. (2018).

## Context

This review extends the line of work initiated by Matsui and Satz (1986) on J/ψ suppression as a QGP signal, and builds on subsequent developments in quarkonium spectral functions from lattice QCD (Asakawa and Hatsuda, 2004) and the complex heavy quark potential (Laine et al., 2006). By unifying the various Lindblad equations derived by Akamatsu (2013, 2014), Blaizot et al. (2015, 2017, 2018), Brambilla et al. (2016, 2017), and Yao et al. (2018) into a single framework, the work enables future phenomenological studies to extract fundamental QGP quantities, such as the complex potential and momentum diffusion constant, from experimental yields of J/ψ and Υ in heavy-ion collisions.
