# Faddeev calculation for breakup neutron-deuteron scattering at 14.1 MeV lab energy

> A new computational method for solving the nucleon-deuteron breakup scattering problem has been applied to study the inelastic neutron-deuteron scattering on the basis of the configuration-space Faddeev equations. This method is based on the spline-decomposition in the angular variable and on a generalization of the Numerov method for the hyperradius. The Merkuriev-Gignoux-Laverne approach has been generalized for arbitrary nucleon-nucleon potentials and with an arbitrary number of partial waves. Neutron-deuteron observables at the incident nucleon energy 14.1 MeV have been calculated using the charge-independent AV14 nucleon-nucleon potential. Results have been compared with those of other authors and with experimental neutron-deuteron scattering data.

## Key claim

The authors present a new computational method for solving the configuration-space Faddeev equations for neutron-deuteron breakup scattering, generalizing the Merkuriev-Gignoux-Laverne approach to arbitrary nucleon-nucleon potentials with an arbitrary number of partial waves. The method yields results for elastic observables at 14.1 MeV that are in fair agreement with prior calculations, but the breakup observables only compare qualitatively with experimental data and show a deep minimum in the nucleon analyzing power that disagrees with predictions from Witala et al.

## Method

The method solves the Faddeev equations in configuration space using Jacobi coordinates, with the wave function expanded in a basis intermediate between LS and Jj coupling schemes. The numerical solution employs a generalization of the Numerov method for the hyperradius combined with spline-decomposition (quintic Hermitian splines) in the angular variable, and the resulting algebraic system is solved using a least-squares method (LSM) for the elastic and breakup amplitudes. Calculations were performed using the charge-independent AV14 nucleon-nucleon potential, with total two-nucleon angular momentum up to 3 and total three-nucleon angular momentum up to 13/2 for both parities (62 partial waves total).

## Key numbers

- Incident neutron energy: 14.1 MeV lab energy
- The value of hbar-squared/m used in the calculation: 41.47 MeV·fm²
- Total two-nucleon angular momentum j taken up to 3; total three-nucleon angular momentum M up to 13/2 with both parity values (+/-1), corresponding to up to 62 partial waves
- Theoretical predictions for elastic scattering at tens of MeV are 25-30% lower than experimental data from How (1977) and McK (1977)
- For the nucleon analyzing power A_y in breakup, a deep minimum occurs at arc length S equal to 7 in the authors' results, disagreeing with predictions from Witala et al.

## Context

This work extends the Merkuriev-Gignoux-Laverne (MGL) approach (Merkuriev, Gignoux, Laverne, 1976), which was previously limited to S-waves and simple nucleon-nucleon potentials, to handle arbitrary high-precision realistic potentials for neutron-deuteron inelastic processes. The results are compared with calculations from the Bochum group (Witala, 1995) using the AV18 potential and the Grenoble group (Gign, 1976) using de Tourreil and Sprung potentials, as well as experimental data from How (1977) and McK (1977). The method enables future studies of three-nucleon breakup with arbitrary nucleon-nucleon potentials, potentially addressing discrepancies such as the A_y puzzle and the star configuration in nucleon-deuteron breakup reactions.
