# Color Superconducting Gap in Schwinger-Dyson Equation and Nonlocal Gauge Fixing

> We solve Schwinger-Dyson (SD) equation for the color superconducting (CS) gap in nonlocal gauge which justifies the ladder approximation in subleading order calculation. The gap prefactor increases about 1.6 times to the leading order gap determined in Landau gauge.

## Key claim

The color superconducting gap prefactor increases by a factor of about 1.6 when the Schwinger-Dyson equation is solved in a nonlocal gauge that justifies the ladder approximation to subleading order, compared to the leading-order gap determined in Landau gauge. The actual increase depends on the nonlocal gauge parameter ξ, which is approximately 1/3 near the on-shell gap energy.

## Method

The authors solve the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the color superconducting gap in the color-flavor-locked phase of high-density QCD, using a nonlocal gauge fixing condition that makes the wavefunction renormalization constants equal to unity (a(p)=b(p)=1). This nonlocal gauge is chosen to preserve the Ward-Takahashi identity when using the bare vertex approximation (ladder approximation) to subleading order, and the gauge parameter ξ(k) is assumed to have only temporal dependence. The gap equation is then reduced to a differential equation and solved by matching the infrared and ultraviolet solutions at the gap scale.

## Key numbers

- The gap prefactor increases by a factor of about 1.6 (approximately e^(1/2)) compared to the leading-order Landau gauge result.
- The nonlocal gauge parameter near on-shell is ξ ≈ 1/3, determined from the condition that wavefunction renormalization corrections vanish.
- The screening mass M is given as M = g_s μ √(N_f) / (2π) for N_f light quarks in the hard-dense-loop approximation.
- The Higgs-like gluon mass M_0 is approximately g_s μ / (2π).
- The gap in the weak coupling limit is Δ_0 ≈ (2^7 π^4 / g^5) μ e^(1 + 3ξ/2) e^(-3π^2 / (√2 g)).

## Context

This work extends the leading-order analysis of the color superconducting gap by Son (1999) and the subleading-order calculations by Hong et al. (2000-2001), which found that the gap prefactor was gauge-dependent in the simple ladder approximation. The nonlocal gauge approach, originally developed by Georgi (1990) for QED, is applied here to QCD at high density to make the ladder approximation consistent with the Slavnov-Taylor identity to subleading order. This enables more reliable predictions for the gap magnitude in dense quark matter, relevant for neutron star phenomenology.
