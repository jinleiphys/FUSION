# Centrifugal effects in N-Delta states

> Recently it has been pointed out that in the two-baryon $NΔ$ or $ΔΔ$ system the width of the state is greatly diminished due to the relative kinetic energy of the two baryons, since the internal energy of the particles, available for pionic decay, is smaller. For nonzero orbital angular momenta this effect becomes state dependent. Also the real part of the $NΔ$ energy can get contribution from the centrifugal barrier leading to rotational series of diffuse states. Obviously, these states may have an interpretation as isospin one dibaryons. Direct and explicit calculations for this are presented with some details of the coupled state wave functions displayed. With finite expectation values of these repulsive effects it may be possible to define state dependent effective thresholds for $NΔ$ states and these, in turn, can show resonant like behavior.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the centrifugal barrier in the $N\Delta$ system produces a state-dependent effective threshold that can generate rotational series of diffuse dibaryon states, but this effect is conditional on the specific orbital angular momenta of both the $NN$ and $N\Delta$ channels, and the resulting effective thresholds require additional attraction (approximately 20-40 MeV) to match experimentally suggested dibaryon masses.

## Method

The author solves coupled radial Schrödinger equations for $NN$ and $N\Delta$ channels using a one-pion-exchange plus $\rho$-exchange transition potential $V_2(r)$, with the $NN$ diagonal potential taken as the modified Reid potential and the $N\Delta$ diagonal potential neglected. The expectation value $\langle 1/r^2 \rangle$ of the $N\Delta$ wave function is computed from the coupled-channel solutions at three laboratory energies (400, 578, and 800 MeV) for multiple partial waves, and this is converted to centrifugal energy using $\hbar^2 L'(L'+1)/(2m'r^2)$. The radial kinetic energy is also computed from the momentum-space representation of the wave function.

## Key numbers

- For the $^5S_2(N\Delta)$ state coupled to $^1D_2(NN)$ at 578 MeV: centrifugal energy = 0 MeV (by definition for $L'=0$), radial kinetic energy = 45 MeV
- For the $^5D_2(N\Delta)$ state coupled to $^1D_2(NN)$ at 578 MeV: centrifugal energy = 147 MeV, radial kinetic energy = 209 MeV
- For the $^5G_2(N\Delta)$ state coupled to $^1D_2(NN)$ at 578 MeV: centrifugal energy = 484 MeV, radial kinetic energy = 578 MeV
- For the $^5P_3(N\Delta)$ state coupled to $^3F_3(NN)$ at 578 MeV: centrifugal energy = 36 MeV, radial kinetic energy = 68 MeV
- For the $^5D_4(N\Delta)$ state coupled to $^1G_4(NN)$ at 578 MeV: centrifugal energy = 65 MeV, radial kinetic energy = 94 MeV
- The expectation value $\langle 1/r^2 \rangle$ for $^5D_2(N\Delta)$ at 578 MeV is 0.67 fm$^{-2}$, and for $^5G_2(N\Delta)$ it is 0.68 fm$^{-2}$, nearly identical
- The reduced mass factor $\hbar^2/(2m') = 36.54$ MeV fm$^2$
- The width of the $^5S_2(N\Delta)$ state at 578 MeV is 78 MeV, while the $^5D_2(N\Delta)$ width is 11.5 MeV
- The dominant $S$-wave maximum in $^1D_2(NN)$ remains at about 1.1-1.2 fm across energies
- The $^5P_3(N\Delta)$ maximum position is approximately 1.6 fm
- The $^5D_4(N\Delta)$ maximum is at about 2 fm

## Context

This work extends the author's previous calculations (Niskanen, 2019) on kinematic suppression of the $N\Delta$ width to study the real part of the $N\Delta$ energy, specifically the centrifugal barrier contribution. It builds on the coupled-channel $N\Delta$ formalism reviewed by Green and Arenhövel, and the transition potential tested in $pp \rightarrow d\pi^+$ calculations (Niskanen, 1980s). The results are compared to the dibaryon masses reported by Yokosawa (1980) and Hoshizaki's diproton resonances, showing that the $^5S_2(N\Delta)$ and $^5P_3(N\Delta)$ effective thresholds align reasonably with the $^1D_2$ (2.17 GeV) and $^3F_3$ (2.22 GeV) dibaryon candidates, while other states require additional attraction. The work enables future studies of state-dependent effective thresholds for $N\Delta$ systems and their interpretation as isospin-one dibaryons.
