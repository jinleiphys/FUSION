# Oscillations of recoil particles against mixed states

> Some consequences of the oscillations of neutral kaons and neutrinos are discussed, in particular, the possibility of oscillations of particles recoiling against kaons or neutrinos from the production process. We show that there are no stationary oscillations of these recoil particles in any order, and that the apparent long-wavelength oscillations, which might appear to result when an earlier treatment of ours was taken to higher order, are spurious. We show that the recoil particles may show a travelling interference pattern. It may be possible to observe this pattern for $Λ$s produced in a reaction, but there seems to be little hope of observing this for the case of neutrinos from muon decay.

## Key claim

The authors prove that there are no stationary oscillations of recoil particles (such as Λs or muons) in any order of the mass-difference expansion, contradicting an earlier claim by Srivastava, Widom and Sassaroli (1995) that such oscillations should appear. The apparent long-wavelength oscillations that emerge when the authors' own earlier treatment (Lowe et al., 1996) is taken to higher order are shown to be spurious artifacts of choosing the wrong reference frame for observation.

## Method

The authors analyze the reaction π⁻p → ΛK⁰ using plane-wave quantum mechanics, treating the K⁰ as a mixture of K_L and K_S mass eigenstates with different masses and momenta, and the recoiling Λ as having two corresponding momentum components Λ_L and Λ_S. They compute the joint probability distribution for detecting a Λ and a K⁰ with definite strangeness, then examine the phase difference between the two amplitude components to all orders in the mass difference δm = m_L − m_S. They identify a specific reference frame S* (defined by the velocity β* = (p_L + p_S)/(E_L + E_S) for each particle) in which the coefficient of the recoil particle's position vanishes exactly, proving the absence of stationary oscillations.

## Key numbers

- The mass difference for neutral kaons is δm = 3.5 × 10⁻¹⁵ GeV.
- For the π⁻p → ΛK⁰ reaction at a center-of-mass energy 0.2 GeV above threshold, the predicted wavelength of Λ oscillations in the S* frame is 40 cm.
- For muons from π → μν decay, assuming m_νμ ∼ 10 eV and m_νe ∼ 0 eV, the predicted neutrino oscillation wavelength is about 50 cm.
- The pion lifetime is 2.6 × 10⁻⁸ sec, which would determine the wave packet size for the π → μν decay if no measurements are made on the decay products.
- A typical detector time resolution of about 10⁻⁹ sec would give a spatial wave packet of about 30 cm for the incident pion in the ΛK⁰ reaction.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier calculation (Lowe et al., 1996) which corrected errors in the frame transformations of Srivastava, Widom and Sassaroli (1995). The results are consistent with subsequent treatments by Dolgov, Morozov, Okun and Schepkin (1997), by Kayser (1995), and by Grimus and Stockinger (1996), who first discussed wave-packet effects in this context. The paper resolves confusion in the literature between two distinct sources of error: the factor-of-2 error from assuming equal momentum for mass eigenstates (Lipkin, 1995) and the larger error from incorrect frame transformations (Srivastava et al., 1995). The work enables future experimental searches for the travelling interference pattern in the S* frame, though the authors conclude that for neutrino oscillations the experimental requirements are so demanding that direct neutrino detection would be necessary anyway.
