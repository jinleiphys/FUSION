# Kinetic theories: from curved space to flat space

> We generalize the equivalence between off-equilibrium state and gravitational perturbation of equilibrium state from dynamics of macroscopic quantities to that of microscopic particles. We also generalize the equivalence to incorporate off-equilibrium state with vorticity by torsional perturbation to equilibrium state. The equivalence is achieved by mapping kinetic theories of spinless and spinning particles in torsional curved space to flat space through suitable choice of inertial frame that eliminates geodesic forces on particles. The equivalence has been shown for hydrodynamic and elastic regimes. In the latter case, we predict spin polarization induced by time-variation of shear strain in elastic materials. We also provide an order-of-magnitude estimate for the polarization in Dirac semi-metal.

## Key claim

The paper establishes an equivalence between off-equilibrium states in flat space and gravitational (or torsional) perturbations of equilibrium states in curved space, extending this mapping from macroscopic quantities to the dynamics of individual microscopic particles (both spinless and spinning). The equivalence holds specifically under the conditions of a suitable choice of local inertial frame that eliminates geodesic forces and requires vanishing spin connection, and is demonstrated for both hydrodynamic and elastic regimes.

## Method

The author maps kinetic theories (Boltzmann equation for spinless particles and quantum kinetic equations for spin-1/2 particles) formulated in curved spacetime with torsion to their flat-space counterparts by choosing a local inertial frame (vielbein) that cancels geodesic forces against frame forces. For the hydrodynamic regime, the equilibrium fluid velocity and temperature in curved space are set to simulate off-equilibrium temperature variations and fluid velocities in flat space; for the elastic regime, the displacement field of a deformed lattice defines the vielbein. The mapping is verified by showing that with vanishing spin connection and the constraint T_mu = 0 (which follows from ideal hydrodynamic equations), the curved-space kinetic equations reduce exactly to flat-space forms, including the collision terms.

## Key numbers

- Spin polarization induced by shear strain in Dirac semi-metal is estimated to be of order 10^{-6}, using a shear tensor estimated as Sigma ~ 2 pi nu sigma_max with nu = 8 THz (high-frequency optical phonon frequency), sigma_max = 0.1 (maximum breaking strain), temperature T = 300 K, and Fermi velocity v_F = 10^6 m/s.
- The ratio p^2/p_0^2 appearing in the polarization estimate is approximately v_F^2, with v_F ~ 10^6 m/s.

## Context

This work extends the well-known Luttinger (1964) equivalence between temperature gradients and gravitoelectric potentials, and subsequent generalizations by Nomura (2011), Bradlyn (2014), Banerjee (2012), and Jensen (2012) that used metric perturbations to simulate fluid motion and derive hydrodynamics. The paper generalizes these ideas from macroscopic quantities to microscopic particle dynamics, incorporating both spinless and spinning particles, and extends the framework to include vorticity via torsional perturbations. The results enable predictions of spin polarization in elastic materials (e.g., Dirac semi-metals) induced by time-varying shear strain, and provide a unified geometric approach that avoids ad-hoc subtractions needed in prior work by Liu (2020, 2021).
