# The Pion Cloud of the Nucleon: Facts and popular Fantasies

> 9 pp, 3 figs, invited talk, to appear in the proceedings of the workshop ``Shape of Hadrons'' (Athens, Greece, 2006)

## Key claim

The concept of a "pion cloud" surrounding the nucleon provides a useful intuitive picture, but it cannot be defined in a unique, model-independent way because chiral loop contributions and short-distance operator contributions are scale-dependent and can be shuffled between each other.

## Method

The author analyzes the pion cloud contribution to nucleon structure using chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) and dispersion relations. The isovector spectral functions for the nucleon electromagnetic form factors are evaluated from two-pion exchange using unitarity constraints and experimental pion form factor data from CMD-2, KLOE, and SND. The isoscalar spectral functions are computed from two-loop CHPT diagrams in the heavy nucleon mass limit. A model-dependent separation of the uncorrelated two-pion contribution from the rho-meson contribution is performed using three different methods to estimate theoretical uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- The isovector Dirac radius at third order in the chiral expansion is given by ⟨r²⟩₁^V = (0.61 - (0.47 GeV²) × d̃(λ) + 0.47 log(λ/1 GeV)) fm², where d̃(λ) is a pion-nucleon low-energy constant
- The anomalous threshold for the isovector spectral functions is located at t_c = 3.98 M_π², very close to the physical threshold at 4 M_π²
- The anomalous threshold for the isoscalar spectral functions is at t_c = 8.90 M_π², near the normal threshold at 9 M_π²
- The two-pion contribution to the isovector electric charge G_E^V(0) is 0.1 to 0.2, and to the isovector magnetic moment G_M^V(0) is 0.4 to 1.0
- The pion cloud contribution to the isovector radii: ⟨r²⟩₁^V = 0.1 to 0.2 fm², ⟨r²⟩₂^V = 0.2 to 0.3 fm², ⟨r²⟩_E^V = 0.2 to 0.3 fm², ⟨r²⟩_M^V = 0.2 to 0.3 fm²
- The weighted charge densities from the pion cloud peak at r ≈ 0.3 fm
- The isoscalar spectral functions are two orders of magnitude smaller than the isovector ones

## Context

This work extends the dispersion-theoretical analysis of nucleon form factors by the Bonn-Mainz group (MMD, HMD, HM, BHMff) and the CHPT calculations of Bernard, Kaiser, and Meißner (GSS, BKMrev, BKMspec). It contradicts the analysis of Friedrich and Walcher (FW) which claimed larger pion cloud effects at distances beyond 1 fm and proposed bump-dip structures requiring spectral functions with additional light mass poles. The results enable more rigorous dispersive analyses of nucleon form factors that respect unitarity and chiral symmetry constraints, and clarify that the isoscalar spectral functions can be safely described by vector meson poles without additional near-threshold enhancements.
