# Energy averages and fluctuations in the decay out of superdeformed bands

> We derive analytic formulae for the energy average (including the energy average of the fluctuation contribution) and variance of the intraband decay intensity of a superdeformed band. Our results may be expressed in terms of three dimensionless variables: $Γ^{\downarrow}/Γ_S$, $Γ_N/d$, and $Γ_N/(Γ_S+Γ^{\downarrow})$. Here $Γ^{\downarrow}$ is the spreading width for the mixing of a superdeformed (SD) state $|0>$ with the normally deformed (ND) states $|Q>$ whose spin is the same as $|0>$'s. The $|Q>$ have mean level spacing $d$ and mean electromagnetic decay width $Γ_N$ whilst $|0>$ has electromagnetic decay width $Γ_S$. The average decay intensity may be expressed solely in terms of the variables $Γ^{\downarrow}/Γ_S$ and $Γ_N/d$ or, analogously to statistical nuclear reaction theory, in terms of the transmission coefficients $T_0(E)$ and $T_N$ describing transmission from the $|Q>$ to the SD band via $|0\angle$ and to lower ND states. The variance of the decay intensity, in analogy with Ericson's theory of cross section fluctuations depends on an additional variable, the correlation length $Γ_N/(Γ_S+Γ^{\downarrow})=\frac{d}{2π}T_N/(Γ_S+Γ^{\d ownarrow})$. This suggests that analysis of an experimentally obtained variance could yield the mean level spacing $d$ as does analysis of the cross section autocorrelation function in compound nuclear reactions. We compare our results with those of Gu and Weidenmüller.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytic formulae for the energy average and variance of the intraband decay intensity of a superdeformed band, showing that the variance depends on three dimensionless variables (Gamma_down/Gamma_S, Gamma_N/d, and Gamma_N/(Gamma_S+Gamma_down)) rather than the two variables needed for the average intensity alone, with the third variable analogous to the correlation width in Ericson's theory of cross section fluctuations.

## Method

The authors used the Kawai-Kerman-McVoy optical background representation from statistical nuclear reaction theory to decompose the intraband decay amplitude into a Lorentzian energy average plus a fluctuating part. They then applied statistical assumptions about the normally deformed states (random phases, overlapping resonances with Gamma_N/d much greater than 1) to evaluate the average decay intensity and variance analytically. The results were expressed in terms of transmission coefficients T_0(E) and T_N, following the approach of Gu and Weidenmuller (1999).

## Key numbers

- Average background contribution to intraband decay intensity: I_ina^av = 1/(1 + Gamma_down/Gamma_S) [Eq. (2Iav)]
- Average fluctuation contribution: ov(I_ina^fl) = 2(pi*Gamma_N/d)^{-1} (Gamma_down/Gamma_S)^2 / (1 + Gamma_down/Gamma_S)^3 [Eq. (2ovIfl)]
- Variance functions: f_1(xi) = 1/(1+xi) + xi/(1+xi)^2 + xi^2/[2(1+xi)^3] and f_2(xi) = 1/[2(1+xi)] where xi = (Gamma_S+Gamma_down)/Gamma_N [Eqs. (2f1) and (2f2)]
- Transmission coefficient T_N = 2*pi*Gamma_N/d [Eq. (TN)]
- Maximum of transmission coefficient T_0(E_0) = 4*I_ina^av*(1-I_ina^av) [from Eq. (1T0)]

## Context

This work extends the model of Gu and Weidenmuller (1999) for the decay-out of superdeformed bands by providing analytic formulae for the variance of the intraband decay intensity, which Gu and Weidenmuller did not fully derive. The results connect to Ericson's theory (1963) of cross section fluctuations in compound nuclear reactions, suggesting that experimental measurement of the variance could yield the mean level spacing d of normally deformed states, similar to how autocorrelation function analysis yields level spacings in compound nucleus reactions. The approach enables extraction of the spin dependence of the barrier separating superdeformed and normally deformed wells, which is contained in the spreading width Gamma_down as discussed by Vigezzi (1990) and Shimizu (1992, 1993, 2000).
