# Structure of the nucleon from electromagnetic space-like and time-like form factors

> Recent experimental data on space-like and time-like form factors of the nucleon are reviewed in light of a model of the nucleon with an intrinsic (quark-like) structure and a meson cloud. The analysis points to the astonishing result that the proton electric space-like form factor vanishes at Q^2~8 (GeV/c)^2 and becomes negative beyond that point. The intrinsic structure is estimated to have a r.m.s. radius of ~0.34 fm, much smaller than the proton r.m.s. radius ~0.82 fm. The calculations are in perfect agreement with space-like proton data, but deviate drastically from space-like neutron data at Q^2>1 (GeV/c)^2. Analysis of the time-like data appears to indicate excellent agreement with both proton and neutron data in the entire range of measured q^2=-Q^2 values.

# Structure of the nucleon from electromagnetic space-like and time-like form factors

## Key claim
The proton electric space-like form factor is predicted to vanish at Q² ~ 8 (GeV/c)² and become negative beyond that point, based on a two-component model of the nucleon with an intrinsic quark-like structure and a meson cloud. The model shows perfect agreement with space-like proton data but deviates drastically from space-like neutron data at Q² > 1 (GeV/c)², while time-like data agree excellently with both proton and neutron data across the entire measured range.

## Method
The author applies a 1973 model (Iachello, Jackson, and Lande) in which the nucleon electromagnetic form factors are parametrized using an intrinsic form factor g(Q²) = (1 + γ Q²)⁻² combined with vector meson dominance contributions from ρ, ω, and φ mesons. The Dirac and Pauli form factors are constructed separately for isoscalar and isovector channels, with the ρ meson width treated via a ππ cut correction. For the time-like region, the intrinsic form factor is analytically continued with a phase parameter θ, and the vector meson propagators are modified to account for the complex p̄p interaction.

## Key numbers
- The proton electric form factor G_Ep crosses zero at Q² ~ 8 (GeV/c)²
- The intrinsic structure r.m.s. radius is ~ 0.34 fm, compared to the proton r.m.s. radius ~ 0.82 fm
- The ratio μ_p G_Ep / G_Mp drops from 1 at Q² = 0 to ~ 0.27 at Q² = 5.6 (GeV/c)²
- The intrinsic form factor parameter γ = 0.25 (GeV/c)⁻²
- The ρ meson mass m_ρ = 0.765 GeV, ω mass m_ω = 0.784 GeV, φ mass m_φ = 1.019 GeV, ρ width Γ_ρ = 0.112 GeV
- The ρ meson coupling β_ρ = 0.672, β_ω = 1.102, β_φ = 0.112, α_φ = -0.052
- The dipole form factor G_D(Q²) = 1/(1 + Q²/0.71)²
- The intrinsic form factor g(Q²) is 80% scaled at Q² ≥ 34 (GeV/c)²
- The asymptotic predictions from the model: G_Ep → -4.08/Q⁴, G_Mp → 0.9120/Q⁴, G_En → -10.86/Q⁴, G_Mn → -4.33/Q⁴
- The ratio G_Mp/G_Mn approaches -0.21 at large Q²
- The time-like region threshold is at q² = 4M_N² = 3.52 (GeV/c)²
- The phase parameter θ ~ 53° for the time-like intrinsic form factor

## Context
This work reviews and extends the two-component model of Iachello, Jackson, and Lande (1973), which combines an intrinsic quark structure with vector meson dominance. The analysis is motivated by recent TJNAF experiments (Jones 2000, Gayou 2002) that showed the ratio μ_p G_Ep / G_Mp decreases dramatically with Q², contradicting earlier SLAC results (Andivahis) that appeared consistent with scaling. The model is compared with soliton models (Holzwarth) and relativistic constituent quark models (Miller, Simula). The time-like analysis extends earlier measurements from Frascati (Castellano 1972), Fermilab E835 (Ambrogiani), and the first neutron time-like measurement (Antonelli 1998). The work identifies a critical discrepancy for neutron form factors at Q² > 1 (GeV/c)² that may indicate isospin breaking or additional neutron-specific components, and calls for new measurements of the proton ratio beyond 10 (GeV/c)² and neutron form factors at large Q².
