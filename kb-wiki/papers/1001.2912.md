# Dependence of elliptic flow on number of parton degrees of freedom

> We calculate the elliptic flow parameter $v_2$ for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV employing the parton cascade BAMPS (Boltzmann Approach of Multiparton Scatterings). Besides gluon interactions including the bremsstrahlung process, interactions with quarks are considered in an effective, but approximate way to investigate the dependence of the collective flow on the number of parton degrees of freedom. We find that $v_2$ as a function of the transverse momentum $p_T$ is sensitive to the number of parton degrees of freedom, whereas the $p_T$ averaged $v_2$ does not. When including quarks, $v_2(p_T)$ shifts to lower $p_T$, the parton transverse momentum spectra become softer and the mean parton transverse momenta decrease.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the differential elliptic flow \(v_2(p_T)\) is sensitive to the number of parton degrees of freedom, shifting to lower \(p_T\) when quarks are included, whereas the \(p_T\)-averaged \(v_2\) is almost unchanged. This result is obtained under the simplifying assumption that quarks and gluons are treated as identical particles by enlarging the gluon degeneracy factor, which overestimates quark interaction rates and does not capture real quark-gluon chemical equilibration.

## Method

The authors employed the parton cascade BAMPS (Boltzmann Approach of Multiparton Scatterings) to simulate Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, using an initial ensemble of gluon minijets with transverse momenta above \(p_0=1.4\) GeV. To include effective quark degrees of freedom without explicit quark dynamics, the gluon degeneracy factor \(d_G\) was enlarged from 16 (pure gluon system) to 40 (quark-gluon system with two flavors), assuming quarks and gluons are identical particles with the same interaction cross sections. The elliptic flow \(v_2\) was calculated as the average of \((p_x^2-p_y^2)/(p_x^2+p_y^2)\) over particles within a momentum rapidity window \(|y|<1\), and the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/s\) was extracted using a formula based on transport collision rates.

## Key numbers

- The degeneracy factor was enlarged from \(d_G=16\) for a pure gluon system to \(d_G=40\) for a quark-gluon system with two flavors.
- The coupling constant was set to a constant \(\alpha_s=0.6\).
- The critical energy density for freeze-out was set to \(e_c=1\) GeV fm\(^{-3}\), corresponding to critical temperatures \(T_c=200\) MeV for pure gluon plasma and \(T_c=160\) MeV for quark-gluon plasma with two flavors.
- The impact parameter for the main analysis was \(b=8.6\) fm.
- The \(p_T\)-averaged \(v_2\) showed a difference of about \(5\%\) at later times between the pure gluon and quark-included calculations.
- Including quarks reduced the mean parton transverse momentum by an average factor of \(1.2\), less than the maximum possible reduction factor of \(1.26\) from thermal equilibrium.
- The differential elliptic flow \(v_2(p_T)\) shifted by about \(20\%\) toward lower \(p_T\) when quarks were added.
- At high \(p_T\), \(v_2(p_T)\) values decreased by \(20\%\) and were a factor of two smaller than experimental data.

## Context

This work extends previous BAMPS calculations by Xu and Greiner (2007, 2008) that considered only gluons and found harder \(p_T\) spectra and lower \(v_2(p_T)\) than experimental data. The study addresses the question of whether chemical equilibration of gluons and quarks affects elliptic flow buildup, in contrast to hydrodynamic calculations that assume chemical equilibrium. The results enable future work to implement explicit quark bremsstrahlung processes in BAMPS and to study the separate elliptic flow of quarks and gluons at hadronization, as well as to refine \(\eta/s\) extraction from transport models.
