# Pion double charge exchange on 4He

> The doubly differential cross sections for the $^4$He$(π^+,π^-) 4p$ reaction were calculated using both a two-nucleon sequential single charge exchange model and an intranuclear cascade code. Final state interactions between the two final protons which were the initial neutrons were included in both methods. At incident pion energies of 240 and 270 MeV the low-energy peak observed experimentally in the energy spectrum of the final pions can be understood only if the contribution of pion production is included. The calculated cross sections are compared with data.

# Pion double charge exchange on 4He

## Key claim

The low-energy peak observed in the energy spectrum of final pions from the 4He(π+,π-)4p reaction at incident energies of 240 and 270 MeV can only be understood if pion production contributions are included; the two-nucleon sequential single charge exchange model alone, even with final state interactions, underestimates this peak by about a factor of two at forward angles.

## Method

The authors calculated doubly differential cross sections using two complementary approaches: a two-nucleon sequential single charge exchange model with a Gaussian wave function for 4He and plane wave final states, and an intranuclear cascade (INC) code that follows the classical motion of pions through the nucleus. Final state interactions between the two final protons were included in both methods by replacing the plane wave for the active pair with an s-wave scattering wave function using the Malfliet-Tjon potential. Pion production was modeled by converting the pion-nucleon system into a two-pion plus nucleon system with an isotropic distribution and a Q(Q_max - Q) invariant mass distribution for the two-pion subsystem.

## Key numbers

- Final state interactions enhance the high-energy peak at small angles by approximately 30% at 25 degrees
- The total pion production cross section calculated at 240 MeV is 116 μb, compared to about 600 μb for a single free nucleon
- Pion production enhances the DCX cross section by 15% at 240 MeV and 30% at 270 MeV in 16O(π+,π-)X (from Vicente et al.)
- Final state interactions modify the cross section by up to a factor of 3
- The threshold for the reaction producing 3He plus a proton is about 167 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Gibbs et al. (1995) and Kulkarni (1999) on inclusive DCX reactions, and addresses the two-peak structure observed experimentally by Kinney (1993) and Yuly (1997) at the Clinton P. Anderson Meson Physics facility. The sequential mechanism approach follows Becker and Schmit (1984), while the forward-backward scattering explanation proposed by Thies and Van Loon (1995) is shown to be insufficient because the low-energy peak grows with increasing beam energy contrary to expectations from that model. The results enable improved understanding of pion production contributions to DCX reactions and suggest that future work should address the discrepancy at 180 MeV, possibly through production leading to 3He plus a proton.
