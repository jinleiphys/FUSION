# Deeply bound $Ξ$ tribaryon

> We have used realistic local interactions based on the recent update of the strangeness $-2$ Nijmegen ESC08c potential to calculate the bound state problem of the $ΞNN$ system in the $(I)J^P=(\frac{1}{2})\frac{3}{2}^+$ state. We found that this system presents a deeply bound state lying $13.5$ MeV below the $Ξd$ threshold. Since in lowest order, pure S$-$wave configuration, this system can not decay into the open $ΛΛN$ channel, its decay width is expected to be very small. We have also recalculated the $(I)J^P=(\frac{3}{2})\frac{1}{2}^+$ state and we have compared with results of quark-model based potentials.

## Key claim

Using the updated ESC08c Nijmegen potential, the $\Xi NN$ system in the $(I)J^P=(\frac{1}{2})\frac{3}{2}^+$ state exhibits a deeply bound state 13.5 MeV below the $\Xi d$ threshold; because this state cannot decay into the open $\Lambda\Lambda N$ channel in a pure S-wave configuration, its decay width is expected to be very small, making it experimentally identifiable as a sharp resonance.

## Method

The authors solved the bound-state Faddeev equations for the $\Xi NN$ system assuming all three particles are in S-wave states, using realistic local two-body potentials constructed from the ESC08c Nijmegen model for the $\Xi N$ channels and Malfliet-Tjon potentials for the $NN$ channels. The two-body amplitudes were obtained by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for each $(i,j)$ channel, with the $\Xi N$ potentials parameterized as a sum of attractive and repulsive Yukawa terms fitted to the low-energy data of Nag15. The coupled integral equations were reduced by exploiting the identity of the two nucleons, and the resulting one-dimensional integral equation was solved for the binding energies.

## Key numbers

- The $(I)J^P=(\frac{1}{2})\frac{3}{2}^+$ $\Xi NN$ state has a binding energy of **13.54 MeV** (ESC08c Nijmegen model) measured from the $\Xi d$ threshold.
- The $(I)J^P=(\frac{3}{2})\frac{1}{2}^+$ $\Xi NN$ state has a binding energy of **1.33 MeV** (ESC08c Nijmegen model) measured from the $N D^*$ threshold (where $D^*$ is the $\Xi N$ $(1,1)$ bound state at 1.56 MeV below $\Xi NN$ mass).
- The $(I)J^P=(\frac{1}{2})\frac{3}{2}^+$ state has a binding energy of **1.15 MeV** (chiral constituent quark model, CCQM).
- The $(I)J^P=(\frac{3}{2})\frac{1}{2}^+$ state has a binding energy of **0.43 MeV** (CCQM).
- The $\Xi N$ $(i,j)=(1,1)$ channel has a scattering length of **4.911 fm** and effective range of **0.527 fm** (ESC08c Nijmegen model).
- The $\Xi N$ $(i,j)=(0,1)$ channel has a scattering length of **-5.357 fm** and effective range of **1.434 fm** (ESC08c Nijmegen model).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Garcilazo and Valcarce, 2015) of $\Xi NN$ bound states in isospin $3/2$ channels, now focusing on the $(I)J^P=(\frac{1}{2})\frac{3}{2}^+$ state using the updated ESC08c Nijmegen potential that incorporates the KISO event (Nakazawa et al., 2015) and the Nagara event (Takahashi et al., 2001). The results contrast with quark-model based potentials (the chiral constituent quark model of Car12) which give much smaller binding energies, and with earlier Nijmegen models (Stoks et al., 1999) that do not predict $\Xi N$ bound states. The predicted deeply bound tribaryon, if confirmed by the upcoming J-PARC E07 experiment, would provide a clean test of the $\Xi N$ interaction strength and help resolve the uncertainties in the strangeness $-2$ baryon-baryon sector.
