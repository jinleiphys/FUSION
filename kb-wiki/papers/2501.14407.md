# Level shifts of exotic deuterium atoms from effective range parameters

> We discuss various methods for determining the level shifts induced by short-range interactions in exotic atoms which are formed by a hadron and a nucleus of opposite charge. The energy shifts can be related to the effective range parameters through Deser or Trueman formulas. In order to assess the accuracy and the reliability of this indirect approach, its predictions are compared with direct bound-state calculations. Notably, the importance of including the Coulomb potential in scattering calculations is highlighted. Finally, the impact of multipole terms in the asymptotic electromagnetic interaction is examined. It is demonstrated that an indirect approach remains relevant by accounting for these effects perturbatively.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Trueman relation, when extended to coupled P-waves by treating quadrupole effects perturbatively, provides level shifts for exotic deuterium atoms that agree with direct bound-state calculations to within about 1 eV, whereas the Deser formulas show deviations of several percent or more for kaonic deuterium.

## Method

The authors solve the Faddeev-Merkuriev equations in configuration space for three-body systems representing kaonic and antiprotonic deuterium, including coupled particle channels (p,n,K-) and (n,n,K0) or (p,n,antiproton) and (n,n,antineutron). They compute level shifts both directly via bound-state calculations using a two-domain Lagrange mesh and indirectly by extracting effective range parameters from low-energy scattering solutions and applying Deser or Trueman formulas. For coupled P-waves in antiprotonic deuterium, the quadrupole interaction from the deuteron's non-spherical charge distribution is treated at first-order perturbation theory, allowing the Trueman relation to be applied after removing the quadrupole potential from the scattering Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- For kaonic deuterium 1s state (J^Π=1^+) with the AV18 NN potential: direct bound-state shift ΔE_{1s} = (787 - 550 i) eV; second-order Trueman gives (787 - 552 i) eV; resummed Deser gives (794 - 508 i) eV
- For kaonic deuterium 2s state (J^Π=1^+) with AV18: direct bound-state shift ΔE_{2s} = (99.9 - 73.2 i) eV; third-order Trueman gives (100 - 73.0 i) eV
- Kaonic deuterium Coulomb-corrected scattering length a_0 with AV18: (1.34 - 1.044 i) fm; scattering length without Coulomb A_0: (1.45 - 1.361 i) fm
- Deuteron quadrupole moment Q_d ≈ 0.286 fm^2
- For the two-body test system with parameters C=2420 and ν=30, the Trueman relation with higher-order terms yields relative differences below 1% compared to exact values, except when a_0 exceeds the Bohr radius B=2 (λ > 0.85)

## Context

This work extends the indirect approach for computing level shifts in exotic atoms, previously applied to antiprotonic deuterium S-waves and spin-uncoupled P-states by Clement (2021) and Duerinck (2023), to coupled P-waves by treating the deuteron quadrupole moment perturbatively. The results are compared with direct three-body calculations using the Kyoto potential for kaonic deuterium (previously studied by Hoshino et al. 2017 using correlated Gaussian functions) and the Kohno-Weise potential for antiprotonic deuterium. The method enables reliable calculation of level shifts for excited states and coupled partial waves without the numerical expense of direct bound-state calculations for spatially extended Rydberg states, supporting upcoming experiments such as SIDDHARTA for kaonic deuterium and PUMA for antiprotonic systems.
