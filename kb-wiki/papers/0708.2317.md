# Effects of nuclear molecular configurations on the astrophysical S-factor for $^{16}$O + $^{16}$O

> The impact of nuclear molecular configurations on the astrophysical S-factor for $^{16}$O + $^{16}$O is investigated within the realistic two-center shell model based on Woods-Saxon potentials. These molecular effects refer to the formation of a neck between the interacting nuclei and the radial dependent collective mass parameter. It is demonstrated that the former is crucial to explain the current experimental data with high accuracy and without any free parameter, whilst in addition the latter predicts a pronounced maximum in the S-factor. In contrast to very recent results by Jiang et al., the S-factor does not decline towards extremely low values as energy decreases.

## Key claim

The formation of a neck between the interacting nuclei is crucial to explain existing experimental S-factor data for 16O + 16O with high accuracy and without any free parameter, while the radial dependent collective mass parameter predicts a pronounced maximum in the S-factor around 4.5 MeV. In contrast to the results reported by Jiang et al., the S-factor does not decline towards extremely low values as energy decreases; below 4 MeV it is suppressed only by a factor of five relative to calculations with constant reduced mass.

## Method

The authors used the realistic two-center shell model based on spherical Woods-Saxon potentials to calculate the adiabatic collective potential energy surface via Strutinsky's macroscopic-microscopic method and the radial dependent collective mass parameter via the cranking mass formula. They solved the radial Schrodinger equation with the modified Numerov method and incoming wave boundary condition inside the capture barrier, including only even partial waves due to the identity of the interacting nuclei. The fusion cross section was computed and converted to the astrophysical S-factor for comparison with experimental data from five different datasets.

## Key numbers

- The geometrical contact separation for 16O + 16O is Rc = 5.85 fm
- The s-wave capture barrier radius is Rb = 8.4 fm
- The experimental Q-value of the reaction is 16.54 MeV
- The cranking mass produces a local maximum in the S-factor around 4.5 MeV center-of-mass energy
- Below 4 MeV, the S-factor is suppressed by a factor of five compared to the calculation with constant reduced mass
- The S-factor reduction from the cranking mass begins around 7-8 MeV energy region

## Context

This work extends the molecular picture of heavy-ion fusion previously developed by the Frankfurt group using harmonic-oscillator two-center shell models, and applies it to the astrophysically important 16O + 16O reaction relevant for oxygen burning in massive stars. The results contradict the empirical analysis by Jiang et al. which suggested the S-factor declines to very small values at low energies, and also differ from calculations by Neff et al. using the Fermionic Molecular Dynamics approach. The method enables future application to other light systems of astrophysical interest such as 12C + 12C and 12C + 16O, particularly for deformed nuclei, using a generalized two-center shell model currently in development.
