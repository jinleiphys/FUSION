# Variational study for the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperatures

> An equation of state (EOS) for uniform asymmetric nuclear matter (ANM) is constructed at zero and finite temperatures by the variational method starting from the nuclear Hamiltonian that is composed of the Argonne v18 and Urbana IX potentials. At zero temperature, the two-body energy is calculated with the Jastrow wave function in the two-body cluster approximation which is supplemented by Mayer's condition and the healing-distance condition so as to reproduce the result by Akmal, Pandharipande and Ravenhall. The energy caused by the three-body force is treated somewhat phenomenologically so that the total energy reproduces the empirical saturation conditions. The masses and radii of neutron stars obtained with the EOS are consistent with recent observational data. At finite temperatures, thermodynamic quantities such as free energy, internal energy, entropy, pressure and chemical potentials are calculated with an extension of the method by Schmidt and Pandharipande. The validity of the frozen-correlation approximation employed in this work is confirmed as compared with the result of the fully minimized calculation. The quadratic proton-fraction-dependence of the energy of ANM is confirmed at zero temperature, whereas the free energy of ANM deviates from the quadratic proton-fraction-dependence markedly at finite temperatures. The obtained EOS of ANM will be an important ingredient of a new nuclear EOS for supernova numerical simulations.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an equation of state (EOS) for uniform asymmetric nuclear matter at zero and finite temperatures using a variational method with the Argonne v18 and Urbana IX potentials, but the treatment of the three-body force is phenomenological and the two-body energy calculation uses a two-body cluster approximation with constraints rather than a full many-body calculation, meaning the results are approximate and calibrated to reproduce empirical saturation conditions and the APR result rather than being derived from first principles alone.

## Method

The authors start from the nuclear Hamiltonian with the Argonne v18 two-body potential and Urbana IX three-body potential, using a Jastrow wave function with correlation functions that depend on the third component of isospin to handle asymmetry. The two-body energy is calculated in the two-body cluster approximation supplemented by Mayer's condition and a healing-distance condition (with healing distance parameter a_h = 1.76) to reproduce the FHNC results of Akmal, Pandharipande and Ravenhall (APR). The three-body energy is treated phenomenologically with parameters alpha = 0.43, beta = -0.34, gamma = -1804 MeV fm^6, and delta = 14.62 fm^3, fitted to reproduce empirical saturation density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}, saturation energy E_0/N = -16.09 MeV, incompressibility K = 245 MeV, and symmetry energy E_sym = 30.0 MeV. At finite temperatures, the method of Schmidt and Pandharipande is extended to asymmetric nuclear matter by introducing separate effective masses for protons and neutrons, with the frozen-correlation approximation used for the two-body correlation functions.

## Context

This work extends the variational method previously applied to symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter in papers I and II (Togashi and Takano, 2013) to the full range of proton fractions 0 < x < 1/2, enabling construction of a complete nuclear EOS for supernova simulations. The approach contrasts with phenomenological models such as the Skyrme-type interaction used by Lattimer and Swesty (1991) and the relativistic mean field theory used by Shen et al. (1998), as it starts from a realistic nuclear Hamiltonian with bare nuclear forces (Argonne v18 and Urbana IX). The results enable future work on supernova numerical simulations by providing an EOS table for uniform asymmetric nuclear matter that can be combined with Thomas-Fermi calculations for non-uniform matter.
