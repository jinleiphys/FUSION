# Exclusive production of pentaquarks in the scaling regime

> We investigate two exclusive reactions with a Theta+ pentaquark in the final state: electroproduction of a K meson on the nucleon, and K+ scattering on a neutron target producing a lepton pair. These reactions offer unique opportunities to investigate the structure of pentaquark baryons at parton level. We discuss the generalized parton distributions for the N --> Theta+ transition and give the leading order amplitude for these processes in the Bjorken regime.

## Key claim

The authors propose that exclusive electroproduction of a kaon and a Theta+ pentaquark, as well as kaon scattering on a neutron producing a lepton pair, can probe the partonic structure of pentaquarks via generalized parton distributions (GPDs), but they note that realistic cross section estimates are not possible due to ignorance of the N to Theta+ transition GPDs.

## Method

The authors apply the factorization theorem for hard exclusive meson production in the Bjorken limit to two reactions: ep to e anti-K0 Theta+ (and en to e K- Theta+) and K+ n to lepton pair Theta+. They define the leading-twist transition GPDs for the N to Theta+ transition, assuming the Theta+ has spin 1/2, isospin 0, and either positive or negative intrinsic parity, and derive the leading-order scattering amplitudes in alpha_s for longitudinal photon polarization.

## Key numbers

* The skewness variable xi is related to the Bjorken variable x_B by xi approx = x_B/(2 - x_B) for electroproduction, and to tau by xi approx = tau/(2 - tau) for the timelike process.
* The scattering amplitude for longitudinal photon polarization scales as 1/Q at fixed xi and t, up to logarithmic corrections from the running of alpha_s and scale evolution.
* The cross section for ep or en scattering is proportional to (1-y)/y times x_B^2/Q^6 times the squared amplitude.
* The cross section for K+ n to lepton pair Theta+ is proportional to sin^2(theta) times tau^2/Q^6 times the squared amplitude, reflecting purely longitudinal gamma* polarization.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous introduction of transition GPDs from the nucleon to the Theta+ pentaquark (Diehl, Pire, Szymanowski, 2004) and applies the factorization theorem for meson production (Collins, 1997) to pentaquark production. The proposed reactions could be studied at existing experiments at DESY and Jefferson Lab, as well as at intense kaon beam facilities under discussion (Nagamiya, 2004). The approach enables future extraction of pentaquark partonic structure once experimental data become available.
