# The Strong Decay Patterns of the $1^{-+}$ Exotic Hybrid Mesons

> We calculate the coupling constants of the decay modes $1^{-+}\rightarrowρπ, f_1π, b_1π, ηπ, η'π, a_1π, f_1η$ within the framework of the light-cone QCD sum rule. Then we calculate the partial width of these decay channels, which differ greatly from the existing calculations using phenomenological models. For the isovector $1^{-+}$ state, the dominant decay modes are $ρπ, f_1π$. For its isoscalar partner, its dominant decay mode is $a_1π$. We also discuss the possible search of the $1^{-+}$ state at BESIII, for example through the decay chains $J/ψ(ψ')\to π_1 +γ$ or $J/ψ(ψ')\to π_1 +ρ$ where $π_1$ can be reconstructed through the decay modes $π_1\to ρπ\to π^+π^-π^0$ or $π_1\to f_1(1285)π^0$. Hopefully the present work will be helpful to the experimental establishment of the $1^{-+}$ hybrid meson.

## Key claim

The light-cone QCD sum rule calculation yields partial widths for the $1^{-+}$ hybrid meson decay channels that differ greatly from existing phenomenological model calculations (e.g., the flux tube model of Page, Swanson, and Szczepaniak (PSS) and the IKP model), with the isovector state dominated by $\rho\pi$ and $f_1\pi$ modes and the isoscalar state dominated by $a_1\pi$, but the results depend on the assumed hybrid mass and the sum rule stability for some channels is poor.

## Method

The authors employed light-cone QCD sum rules (LCQSR) to calculate the coupling constants for the decay modes $1^{-+}\rightarrow\rho\pi$, $f_1\pi$, $b_1\pi$, $\eta\pi$, $\eta'\pi$, $a_1\pi$, and $f_1\eta$. They used an interpolating current for the $1^{-+}$ hybrid meson involving the gluon field strength tensor, performed the operator product expansion near the light cone with pion distribution amplitudes as input, and applied double Borel transformations with continuum subtraction at an asymmetric Borel parameter point to extract the coupling constants. The partial widths were then computed from the extracted couplings using standard decay formulas.

## Key numbers

- For the isovector $1^{-+}$ state with mass $m_{\pi_1}=1.6$ GeV, the $\rho\pi$ coupling constant $g_\rho$ is in the range $2.5\sim3.2$ GeV$^{-1}$, yielding a partial width of $73\sim120$ MeV.
- For $m_{\pi_1}=1.8$ GeV, the $\rho\pi$ partial width is $138\sim222$ MeV; for $m_{\pi_1}=2.0$ GeV, it is $216\sim370$ MeV.
- For $m_{\pi_1}=1.6$ GeV, the $f_1\pi$ partial width is $69\sim122$ MeV; for $m_{\pi_1}=1.8$ GeV, $96\sim175$ MeV; for $m_{\pi_1}=2.0$ GeV, $109\sim195$ MeV.
- The $b_1\pi$ partial width is very small: $0.14$ MeV for $m_{\pi_1}=1.6$ GeV and $1.2$ MeV for $m_{\pi_1}=1.8$ GeV.
- The $\eta\pi$ coupling constant $g_\eta$ is approximately $0.45$ for $m_{\pi_1}=1.6$ and $1.8$ GeV, and $0.42$ for $m_{\pi_1}=2.0$ GeV.
- The $\eta'\pi$ coupling constant $g_{\eta'}$ is approximately $-0.16$ for $m_{\pi_1}=1.6$ GeV, $-0.15$ for $1.8$ and $2.0$ GeV.
- The $f_1$ meson decay constant is taken as $f_{f_1}=0.17$ GeV; the $b_1$ meson decay constant is $f_{b_1}=0.18$ GeV$^3$.
- The hybrid meson overlapping amplitude is taken as $\tilde{f}_{\pi_1}=0.15$ GeV$^4$.

## Context

This work extends the earlier light-cone QCD sum rule study by Zhu (2000) for the $\rho\pi$ and $f_1\pi$ channels to a broader set of decay modes including $b_1\pi$, $\eta\pi$, $\eta'\pi$, $a_1\pi$, and $f_1\eta$, and provides a systematic comparison with the flux tube model results of Page, Swanson, and Szczepaniak (PSS) and the IKP model. The predicted partial widths differ significantly from these phenomenological models, particularly for the $\rho\pi$ channel where the present results are much larger (e.g., $73\sim120$ MeV versus 8-9 MeV at $m_{\pi_1}=1.6$ GeV). The results enable experimental searches at BESIII through production chains such as $J/\psi(\psi')\to \pi_1 +\gamma$ or $J/\psi(\psi')\to \pi_1 +\rho$, with $\pi_1$ reconstructed via $\rho\pi\to \pi^+\pi^-\pi^0$ or $f_1(1285)\pi^0$.
