# Proton-neutron random phase approximation studied by the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model in the SU(2) $\times$ SU(2)

> We study the proton-neutron RPA with an extended Lipikin-Meshkov-Glick model. We pay attention to the effect of correlated ground state and the case in which neutron and proton numbers are different. The effect of the correlated ground state are tested on the basis of quasi-boson approximation. We obtain the result that RPA excitation energies and transition strengths are in a good agreement with the exact solution up to a certain strength of the particle-particle interaction. However, the transition strength becomes worse if we consider the case in which neutron and proton numbers are different even at a weak particle-particle interaction.

## Key claim

For charge-exchange reactions in nuclei with unequal neutron and proton numbers (N ≠ Z), the standard proton-neutron RPA transition strengths deviate from the exact solution even at weak particle-particle interaction, and including the correlated ground state via the quasi-boson approximation only partially remedies this discrepancy.

## Method

The author extends the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model to an SU(2)×SU(2) basis representing two-level systems for protons and neutrons separately. The Hamiltonian includes a single-particle energy splitting ε, a particle-particle interaction Vpn, and a particle-hole interaction Wpn. The RPA equations are solved exactly in this model space, and the correlated RPA ground state is constructed up to first order using the phonon operator formalism of Rowe (1968). Excitation energies and β−/β+ transition strengths are compared with the exact diagonalization of the Hamiltonian for systems with equal and different neutron and proton numbers.

## Key numbers

- For Nn = Np = 5 and Nn = Np = 20 with NWpn = −0.2, RPA and RPA(corr.) excitation energies agree with the exact solution up to approximately NVpn ≈ 1.5, above which RPA collapses due to phase transition; RPA(corr.) has a larger critical point than RPA.
- For Nn = Np = 5 and Nn = Np = 20, RPA and RPA(corr.) transition strengths agree with the exact solution from NVpn = 0 to approximately NVpn = 1.5.
- For Np = 20 with Nn varying from 24 to 32, the difference between RPA transition strengths and the exact solution increases as Nn increases, with deviations already visible at small NVpn.
- The non-energy-weighted sum rule T− − T+ is exactly satisfied by RPA up to the critical point (equal to Nn − Np), but RPA(corr.) does not satisfy it perfectly.

## Context

This work extends the earlier LMG model studies of charge-exchange RPA by Stoica (1997, 2001) and Stoica (1998), who considered the correlated ground state up to first order but did not investigate the N ≠ Z case. The present results show that for N ≠ Z, 2p2h configurations (specifically [π²(πν)⁻¹] configurations) become important even at weak interaction, which cannot be captured by the correlated ground state used here. The author suggests that second RPA, which includes such 2p2h configurations, may improve the description, and notes that similar work has been performed in the SO(5) group by Civitarese (2005) and Hirsch (1997).
