# Nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering analysis to 2.5 GeV

> A partial-wave analysis of NN elastic scattering data has been completed. This analysis covers an expanded energy range, from threshold to a laboratory kinetic energy of 2.5 GeV, in order to include recent elastic pp scattering data from the EDDA collaboration. The results of both single-energy and energy-dependent analyses are described.

## Key claim

The partial-wave analysis of nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering has been extended to a laboratory kinetic energy of 2.5 GeV, but the high-energy region was constrained mainly by cross section data, so the present solution should be considered as a guide to the expected amplitudes rather than a definitive result.

## Method

A partial-wave analysis of both pp and np elastic scattering data was performed using an energy-dependent K-matrix parameterization, with a modified basis function that replaced the previous expansion to handle the extended energy range up to 2.5 GeV. Single-energy analyses were also carried out over energy bins, and fits with and without the new EDDA data were compared to show the influence of these measurements. The analysis used a total of 144 parameters in the energy-dependent fit.

## Key numbers

- The analysis covers from threshold to a laboratory kinetic energy of 2.5 GeV, extending the previous analysis (Arndt et al., 1994) which went to 1.6 GeV for pp and 1.3 GeV for np.
- The total database is about 20% larger than the previous analysis, which was based on 12838 pp and 10918 np data points.
- New pp differential cross sections from the EDDA collaboration range from 540 MeV to 2520 MeV in proton kinetic energy and from 35 degrees to 90 degrees in the center-of-mass scattering angle.
- The COSY data contribute a chi-squared per datum of 1.07 when included in the fit, which jumps to 5.6 when attempting a prediction based on the remaining data.
- Additional data added include about 60 polarized data at 200 MeV from the Indiana cooler, 35 differential cross sections between 490 and 790 MeV from LAMPF, and 450 points from ANL and 893 points from Saclay for the extension above 1.6 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the previous partial-wave analysis by Arndt et al. (1994) which covered up to 1.6 GeV, and includes recent elastic pp scattering data from the EDDA collaboration at COSY. The analysis finds no evidence for a dibaryon resonance near 2.7 GeV center-of-mass energy that had been suggested by earlier work (Hoshizaki, 1990s; Ball et al., 1990s), and the partial-wave amplitudes above 1.6 GeV are smooth and structureless. The results enable future analyses when planned polarization measurements from COSY, SATURNE II, PSI, and other facilities become available, and may help bridge the gap between low-energy and high-energy regimes, with the differential cross section appearing consistent with the dimensional counting rule prediction of s^{-10} at fixed center-of-mass angle.
