# Parity-Violating Excitation of the Δ(1232): Hadron Structure and New Physics

> We consider prospects for studying the parity-violating (PV) electroweak excitation of the Δ(1232) resonance with polarized electron scattering. Given present knowledge of Standard Model parameters, such PV experiments could allow a determination of the N -> Δelectroweak helicity amplitudes. We discuss the experimental feasibility and theoretical interpretability of such a determination as well as the prospective implications for hadron structure theory. We also analyze the extent to which a PV N -> Δmeasurement could constrain various extensions of the Standard Model.

## Key claim

Parity-violating (PV) electron scattering can determine the N → Δ electroweak helicity amplitudes, but a 25–30% determination of the axial vector response appears feasible with realistic run times, while a sub-one-percent precision search for new physics via this process is not currently possible because non-resonant background contributions are insufficiently well-known.

## Method

The authors analyze the parity-violating (PV) left-right asymmetry A_LR for inclusive pion production from a proton target via neutral-current excitation of the Δ(1232) resonance, using the formalism of electroweak response functions and multipole expansions. They decompose the asymmetry into three contributions: a term proportional to the isovector vector current coupling ξ_V^{T=1} (sensitive to new physics), a non-resonant background term, and an axial-vector term proportional to the ratio C_5^A/C_3^V (sensitive to hadron structure). They evaluate the kinematic dependence of the figure of merit, estimate non-resonant background contributions, and compare prospective constraints on new physics with those from other low-energy observables.

## Key numbers

- A determination of the axial vector response at the 25–30% level appears feasible with realistic run times.
- The vector helicity amplitudes from photoproduction are known with better than 2% precision (LEGS and Mainz experiments, Kha95 and Bec97).
- The disagreement between quark model predictions and experimental data for the magnetic transition form factor is at the 30% level; for the transition charge radius, the disagreement is at the 20% level.
- A one percent determination of ξ_V^{T=1} would require approximately 1000 hours of beam time at energies ≳ 3 GeV at forward angles, with non-resonant backgrounds understood at roughly ±25% or better and axial contributions understood at roughly ±30% or better.
- The axial contribution Δ^π_(3) is suppressed relative to Δ^π_(1) because g_V^e ≈ -0.1, while the product of leptonic and hadronic NC couplings in Δ^π_(1) is of order unity.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the neutral-current N → Δ transition (Refs. 5 and 10, and Section 4.7 of Ref. 4) and provides a resource for the G0 collaboration at Jefferson Lab, whose proposal to measure the PV N → Δ transition has been approved (Wel97, Wel97b). The analysis complements existing electromagnetic data from LEGS (Kha95) and Mainz (Bec97) that determine vector helicity amplitudes with better than 2% precision, and addresses the need for improved knowledge of axial vector form factors beyond the highly model-dependent determinations from charged-current reactions (Ref. 18). A measurement of C_5^A/C_3^V would test the off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation and its chiral corrections (HHM), and could help resolve discrepancies between quark model predictions and lattice calculations (Lei92) for vector current transition form factors.
