# Self-consistency in the phonon space of the particle-phonon coupling model

> In the paper the non-linear generalization of the time blocking approximation (TBA) is presented. The TBA is one of the versions of the extended random-phase approximation (RPA) developed within the Green-function method and the particle-phonon coupling model. In the generalized version of the TBA the self-consistency principle is extended onto the phonon space of the model. The numerical examples show that this non-linear version of the TBA leads to the convergence of the results with respect to enlarging the phonon space of the model.

## Key claim

The paper presents a non-linear generalization of the time blocking approximation (TBA) called the configuration blocking approximation (CBA), which extends the self-consistency principle onto the phonon space of the particle-phonon coupling model; the key hedge is that this approach achieves convergence of results with respect to enlarging the phonon space only when a blocking condition (requiring the 1p1h component of the norm to exceed 1/2) is imposed to exclude states dominated by complex configurations.

## Method

The authors formulate the CBA by replacing the RPA phonons in the induced interaction of the TBA with the TBA solutions themselves, creating a non-linear eigenvalue problem in the 1p1h space. They impose a blocking condition that discards TBA states where the 1p1h norm component is less than the complex-configuration norm component (threshold at 1/2), and they solve the equations in a diagonal approximation using an iterative procedure. The phonon space is preselected using a new collectivity measure based on the average square of the residual interaction, and the method is tested on the Skyrme parametrization SV-m64k6 for several nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The blocking threshold for the 1p1h norm component is set at \(\zeta^2_{\mathrm{min}} = 1/2\) (Eq. 38).
- The maximum energy of single-particle states in the 1p1h basis was 100 MeV; for \(^{208}\)Pb, the phonon basis was restricted by a maximum phonon energy of 100 MeV.
- The collectivity measure \(\kappa_n = \gamma^2_n/(1+\gamma^2_n)\) uses \(\gamma^2_n = \langle V^2\rangle_n / \omega^2_n\); archetype collective modes (e.g., \(3^-_1\) in \(^{16}\)O and \(^{40}\)Ca, \(2^+_1\) in \(^{48}\)Ca, \(2^+_1\) in \(^{208}\)Pb) have \(\gamma^2_n \gtrsim 1\) and \(\kappa_n \gtrsim 0.5\).
- For the \(3^-_1\) states in \(^{16}\)O, \(^{40}\)Ca, and \(^{208}\)Pb, the optimum cutoff \(\kappa_{\mathrm{min}}\) for standard TBA lies in the interval 0.05–0.1.
- The value \(\kappa_{\mathrm{min}} = 1\) corresponds to the RPA limit.

## Context

This work extends the time blocking approximation (TBA) developed by the authors and collaborators (Tselyaev 1989, Kamerdzhiev et al. 1997, Kamerdzhiev et al. 2004) by introducing self-consistency in the phonon space, building on earlier efforts to select relevant phonons (Lyutorovich 2017). The CBA improves upon standard TBA by eliminating the dependence on an ad hoc cutoff parameter for phonon selection, converging naturally to results consistent with previous optimized calculations (optim2017). The approach enables systematic calculations of nuclear response properties (such as giant resonance spreading widths) without the double counting and Pauli principle violations that plague phonon-coupling models with uncontrolled phonon spaces.
