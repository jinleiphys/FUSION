# The role of the N*(1535) resonance and the pi^- p --> KY amplitudes in the OZI forbidden pi N --> phi N reaction

> We study the pi N --> phi N reaction close to the phi N threshold within the chiral unitary approach, by combining the pi^- p --> K^+ Sigma^-, pi^- p --> K^0 Sigma^0 and pi^- p --> K^0 Lambda amplitudes with the coupling of the phi to the K components of the final states of these reactions via quantum loops. We obtain a good agreement with experiment when the dominant pi^- p --> K^0 Lambda amplitude is constrained with its experimental cross section. We also evaluate the coupling of the N*(1535) to phi N and find a moderate coupling as a consequence of partial cancellation of the large KY components of the N*(1535). We also show that the N*(1535) pole approximation is too small to reproduce the measured cross section for the pi N --> phi N reaction.

# The role of the N*(1535) resonance and the pi^- p --> KY amplitudes in the OZI forbidden pi N --> phi N reaction

## Key claim

The N*(1535) resonance alone, via a simple pole approximation, is insufficient to reproduce the measured pi N --> phi N cross section; instead, the full energy-dependent chiral unitary amplitude, which includes significant non-resonant background contributions, is required and yields agreement with experiment within a factor of about 2.5 without fitting any extra parameters beyond those fixed to pi N scattering data.

## Method

The authors combine chiral unitary coupled-channel amplitudes for pi^- p --> K+ Sigma-, pi^- p --> K0 Sigma0, and pi^- p --> K0 Lambda with the coupling of the phi meson to the kaon components of these final states via quantum loops, using the vector meson dominance hypothesis. The phi is coupled to the meson-baryon amplitude through its dominant decay channel phi --> Kbar K, and the full Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved for the T-matrix in s-wave, with the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction as the driving term and subtraction constants fixed to pi N scattering data up to about 1.6 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The N*(1535) pole position is found at M* = 1537 - 37i MeV for the neutral charge and M* = 1532 - 37i MeV for the positive charge.
- The effective coupling of the N*(1535) to phi n is g_{phi n} = 0.04 - 0.17 i, and to phi p is g_{phi p} = 0.03 - 0.15 i.
- The coupling to rho0 p is g_{rho0 p} = 0.63 - 0.04 i, consistent with the PDG branching ratio of 0.02 and width Gamma_{N*} = 150 MeV giving g_{rho N} = 0.89, or Gamma_{N*} = 90 MeV giving g_{rho N} = 0.69.
- The pi N --> phi N cross section predicted by the full amplitude overestimates the data by a factor of about 2.5, while the pole approximation underestimates it severely.
- The N*(1535) resonance lies about 400 MeV below the phi N threshold.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to phi --> pi0 pi0 gamma (Marco 1999, Markushin 2000, Palomar 2003) and to the electromagnetic form factors of the N*(1535) (Jido 2007). It contradicts the earlier suggestion by Xie (2007) that the N*(1535) resonance alone, via a Breit-Wigner parametrization, dominates the pi N --> phi N reaction; the present study shows that the full amplitude with background contributions is essential. The results enable improved modeling of related OZI-forbidden processes such as pp --> pp phi, and demonstrate that sub-threshold resonance pole approximations can severely underestimate cross sections at higher energies.
