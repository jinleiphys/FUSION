# Direct reaction contribution in the very low energy $^{19}$F(p,$α_0$) reaction

> The direct reaction component of the $^{19}$F(p,$α_{0}$) reaction at E$_{cm}$=180-600 keV is studied for the data that has become very recently available. This component has been found to be significant in the present work using the direct pickup model in framework of the DWBA formalism and indicate the strong cluster structure of $^{19}$F.

## Key claim
The direct reaction (pickup) component of the \(^{19}\)F(p,\(\alpha_0\)) reaction is significant at center-of-mass energies 180–600 keV, contrary to previous analyses that assumed only compound-nucleus (R-matrix) resonances; however, the direct pickup model explains the data satisfactorily only up to 500 keV, and at 616 keV the exchange process and resonance contributions must also be included.

## Method
The authors performed finite-range and zero-range distorted-wave Born approximation (FR-DWBA and ZR-DWBA) calculations using the code FRESCO version 3.1, modeling the reaction as a proton picking up a triton cluster from \(^{19}\)F to form an alpha particle and \(^{16}\)O. They fitted the calculated angular distributions to experimental data from Lombardo et al. (2020) at 327, 387, 486, and 616 keV and from Lorenz-Wirzba et al. (1978) at 250, 350, and 450 keV, adjusting optical potential parameters and spectroscopic amplitudes. Additionally, they performed R-matrix fits to the same angular distributions using the code Azure 2, varying the widths of four background states of \(^{20}\)Ne.

## Key numbers
- The reaction Q-value is +8.114 MeV.
- The zero-range coupling constant used was \(D_0^2 = 4.53 \times 10^5\) MeV\(^2\) fm\(^3\).
- In FR-DWBA, the best-fit spectroscopic amplitude for the \(t+^{16}\)O bound state in \(^{19}\)F lies between 0.5 and 0.7.
- In ZR-DWBA, the best-fit spectroscopic amplitude for the same target bound state lies between 0.1 and 0.2.
- For the exchange process at 616 keV, the bound state radius parameter \(r_0\) for \(^{15}\)N+p was adjusted to 1.4 fm.
- The asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) of the \(^{16}\)O ground state was fixed at 37460.25 fm\(^{-0.5}\) in the R-matrix fits.
- R-matrix fitted alpha widths (\(\Gamma_\alpha\)) for the four background states at 20 MeV excitation energy range from 0.159 MeV (2\(^+\) state at 250 keV) to 1452.490 MeV (1\(^-\) state at 486 keV), with \(\chi^2/N\) values between 0.08 and 1.46.

## Context
This work extends the direct-reaction analysis of Herndl et al. (1997), who used zero-range DWBA on older angular distribution data from Lorenz-Wirzba et al. (1978), and addresses the conflicting finite-range DWBA results of Yamashita et al. (2005) which suggested an exchange process was necessary. The authors use the new, more precise data from Lombardo et al. (2020), which were previously analyzed only with R-matrix theory, to show that the direct pickup component is significant and cannot be neglected in extracting the astrophysical S-factor. This enables improved extrapolation of the reaction rate to the Gamow window (27–94 keV) for stellar nucleosynthesis models of fluorine in AGB stars.
