# Geometric chaoticity leads to ordered spectra for randomly interacting fermions

> A rotationally invariant random interaction ensemble was realized in a single-j fermion model. The dominance of ground states with zero and maximum spin was confirmed and explained with a statistical approach based on the random coupling of individual angular momenta. The interpretation is supported by the structure of the ground state wave functions.

# Geometric chaoticity leads to ordered spectra for randomly interacting fermions

## Key claim

The dominance of ground states with spin zero and maximum spin in a rotationally invariant random interaction ensemble arises from statistical correlations of fermions and geometric chaoticity (random coupling of individual spins), not from coherent pairing forces; the effect is explained by a statistical cranking model that shows the ground state spin distribution is driven to the minimum or maximum value in approximately 50% of realizations.

## Method

A single-j shell model with N identical fermions was used, with a rotationally invariant two-body Hamiltonian expressed in terms of pair operators with even pair spin L. The coupling constants V_L were taken as random, uncorrelated, and uniformly distributed between -1 and 1, and the ground state spin J_0 was computed for each realization. A statistical cranking approach was developed using Fermi-Dirac distributions with Lagrange multipliers for particle number and total projection M, expanding occupation numbers in powers of the cranking frequency gamma to derive the expectation value of energy as a function of J^2 and J^4.

## Key numbers

- For N=4 and N=6 fermions at various j values, the fraction f_0 of ensemble realizations with ground state spin J_0=0 was much higher than the statistical fraction f^s_0 of J=0 states in the shell-model space (shown in Fig. 1).
- In the bosonic approximation, the predicted fraction f_0^b is approximately 1/k where k is the number of allowed L values, with other J values appearing with probabilities ~1/k^2.
- For a Gaussian distribution of V_L with zero mean, the statistical theory gives f_0 = 1/4 + (1/2pi) arctan[(D + A/J_max^2)/sqrt(AB - D^2)], which is close to 1/2.
- For N=6 particles with j=11/2, the dimension of the J=0 space is n=3, giving a Porter-Thomas distribution P(x) proportional to 1/sqrt(x) for overlaps with the degenerate pairing model ground state.

## Context

This work extends the findings of Johnson et al. (1998) and Bijker et al. (1999), who first observed that random but rotationally invariant Hamiltonians produce an excess of J=0 ground states, and of Baps et al. (1999) and Stuchbery (1999), who confirmed this and discovered an excess of maximum spin ground states. The paper contradicts the expectation that random interactions should produce chaotic spectra without regular features, showing instead that geometric chaoticity (previously used by Ericson (1960) for level density calculations) actually explains the ordered ground state spin pattern. The results enable further study of the quantitative relations between geometric chaoticity and pure dynamic effects in finite many-body systems, and suggest that the dominance of 0+ ground states in even-even nuclei may partly come from incoherent interactions rather than solely from coherent pairing.
