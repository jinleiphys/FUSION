# Dineutron correlations and BCS-BEC crossover in nuclear matter with the Gogny pairing force

> The dineutron correlations and the crossover from superfluidity of neutron Cooper pairs in the $^1S_0$ pairing channel to Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) of dineutron pairs in both symmetric and neutron matter are studied within the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory, with the effective interaction PK1 of the relativistic mean-field approach in the particle-hole channel and the finite-range Gogny force in the particle-particle channel. The influence of the pairing strength on the behaviors of dineutron correlations is investigated. It is found that the neutron pairing gaps at the Fermi surface from three adopted Gogny interactions are smaller at low densities than the one from the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction Bonn-B potential. From the normal (anomalous) density distribution functions and the density correlation function, it is confirmed that a true dineutron BEC state does not appear in nuclear matter. In the cases of the Gogny interactions, the most BEC-like state may appear when the neutron Fermi momentum $k_{Fn}\thicksim0.3 \rm{fm^{-1}}$. Moreover, based on the newly developed criterion for several characteristic quantities within the relativistic framework, the BCS-BEC crossover is supposed to realize in a revised density region with $k_{Fn}\in[0.15,0.63] \rm{fm^{-1}}$ in nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that a true dineutron BEC state does not appear in nuclear matter at any density when using Gogny pairing interactions, but the most BEC-like state may appear at a neutron Fermi momentum of approximately 0.3 fm^{-1}, which is larger than the value of 0.2 fm^{-1 found previously with the Bonn-B potential.

## Method

The authors employed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theory with the PK1 effective interaction in the particle-hole channel and three finite-range Gogny forces (D1, D1S, D1N) in the particle-particle channel for the 1S0 pairing channel. They solved the BCS gap equation self-consistently for both symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter, and analyzed the normal and anomalous density distribution functions, the density correlation function at zero-momentum transfer, the probability for pair partners within the average inter-neutron distance, and the ratio of the pairing gap to the Fermi kinetic energy.

## Key numbers

- The neutron pairing gap at the Fermi surface from the Gogny D1 interaction reaches a maximum of 3.13 MeV in symmetric nuclear matter and 3.40 MeV in pure neutron matter at k_Fn = 0.8 fm^{-1}.
- The most BEC-like state appears around k_Fn = 0.3 fm^{-1} for the Gogny interactions, compared to k_Fn = 0.2 fm^{-1} for the Bonn-B potential.
- The BCS-BEC crossover region is determined to be k_Fn in [0.15, 0.63] fm^{-1} in nuclear matter based on the newly developed criteria.
- For the Gogny D1N interaction, the BCS-BEC crossover region is k_Fn in [0.14, 0.63] fm^{-1} for symmetric nuclear matter and [0.15, 0.63] fm^{-1} for pure neutron matter.
- The probability P(d_n) criterion gives upper limits of k_Fn < 0.75 fm^{-1} (D1), 0.70 fm^{-1} (D1S), and 0.65 fm^{-1} (D1N) for symmetric nuclear matter.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dineutron correlations and BCS-BEC crossover in nuclear matter using the Bonn-B potential (Sun 2010, Sun 2012) by replacing the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction with effective Gogny forces in the pairing channel. The results show that the Gogny interactions produce weaker pairing gaps at low densities than the Bonn-B potential, leading to a narrower and more conservative density range for the BCS-BEC crossover (k_Fn in [0.15, 0.63] fm^{-1}) compared to the earlier estimate of k_Fn in [0.05, 0.70] fm^{-1}. The findings enable more reliable predictions for dineutron correlations in neutron-rich nuclei and provide benchmarks for future quantum Monte Carlo or virial expansion studies of low-density nuclear matter.
