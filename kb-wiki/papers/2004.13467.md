# Status of the search for $η$-mesic nuclei with particular focus on $η$-Helium bound states

> In this paper the search for eta-mesic nuclei with particular focus on light $η$-He bound states is reviewed. A brief description of recent theoretical studies and experimental results is presented.

## Key claim

This review paper summarizes the status of experimental searches for $\eta$-mesic nuclei, focusing on light $\eta$-Helium bound states, and reports that despite many dedicated experiments, no clear signature of their existence has been found; only upper limits on the total cross section for bound state formation have been determined, and a slight hint of a possible bound state signal in the $^{3}\text{He}$-$\eta$ system remains within systematic uncertainty.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental searches for $\eta$-mesic helium nuclei performed at the COSY facility using the WASA-at-COSY detection setup, with deuteron and proton beams on deuteron pellet targets. The experiments employed a ramped beam technique to continuously vary the beam momentum around the $\eta$ production threshold, and studied two decay mechanisms: $\eta$ meson absorption on a nucleon inside helium leading to N$^{*}(1535)$ resonance decay into a N-$\pi$ pair, and direct $\eta$ meson decay into $2\gamma$ or $3\pi^{0}$ while bound. Excitation functions for reactions such as $dd\rightarrow ^{3}\text{He} p \pi^{-}$, $dd\rightarrow ^{3}\text{He} n \pi^{0}$, $pd\rightarrow ^{3}\text{He} 2\gamma$, $pd\rightarrow ^{3}\text{He} 6\gamma$, and $pd\rightarrow d p \pi^{0}$ were measured and fitted with Breit-Wigner functions plus polynomial backgrounds to extract upper limits.

## Key numbers

- Upper limit for $dd\rightarrow(^{4}\text{He}-\eta)_{bound}\rightarrow ^{3}\text{He} n \pi^{0}$ total cross section varies from 2.5 to 3.5 nb at 90% confidence level (binding energy fixed to 30 MeV).
- Upper limit for $dd\rightarrow(^{4}\text{He}-\eta)_{bound}\rightarrow ^{3}\text{He} p \pi^{-}$ total cross section is about 6 nb, about four times better than the previous result.
- Upper limit for $pd\rightarrow(^{3}\text{He}-\eta)_{bound}\rightarrow ^{3}\text{He}(\eta\text{ decays})$ total cross section varies between 2 nb and 15 nb depending on bound state parameters (binding energy 0-15 MeV, width >20 MeV).
- Upper limit for $pd\rightarrow(^{3}\text{He}-\eta)_{bound}\rightarrow d p \pi^{0}$ total cross section varies from 13 to 24 nb for bound state parameters $B_{s}\in(0,40)$ MeV and $\Gamma\in(0,50)$ MeV.
- The $\eta$N scattering length values considered range from $a_{\eta N}=(0.18,0.16i)$ fm to $a_{\eta N}=(1.03,0.49i)$ fm.
- In-medium branching ratios for $\eta\rightarrow 2\gamma$ estimated from Klein-Gordon equation calculations vary from about $2\times10^{-5}$ to $7\times10^{-4}$ depending on optical potential parameters.

## Context

This review extends the experimental search for $\eta$-mesic nuclei, which began with heavier nuclei using pion, photon, and hadron beams (Chrien 1988, Johnson 1993, Sokol 2001, Budzanowski 2009), and now focuses on light helium systems. The work builds on earlier observations of a steep rise in total cross sections for $dp\rightarrow ^{3}\text{He}\eta$ (Berger 1988, Mayer 1996, Smyrski 2007, Mersmann 2007) and $dd\rightarrow ^{4}\text{He}\eta$ (Frascaria 1994, Willis 1997, Wronska 2005) reactions, which suggested strong attractive final state interactions. The results enable constraints on $\eta$-$^{4}\text{He}$ optical potential parameters (Ikeno 2017) and provide input for future combined analyses of multiple decay channels to search for $\eta$-mesic $^{3}\text{He}$ bound states.
