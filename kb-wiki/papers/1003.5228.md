# A preliminary analysis of eta' -> eta pi pi in chiral theories

> Preliminary results for the Dalitz plot distribution of $η^\prime\toηππ$ decays in the frameworks of Large-$N_c$ Chiral Perturbation Theory and Resonance Chiral Theory are given. We hope our results to be of some relevance for the present and forthcoming analysis of these decays at GAMS, CLEO, VES, KLOE-2, Crystal Ball, Crystal Barrel, WASA, and BES-III.

## Key claim

Preliminary results for the Dalitz plot parameters of η′ → ηππ decays are computed in Large-Nc Chiral Perturbation Theory and Resonance Chiral Theory, but the predictions do not yet fully reproduce all experimental parameters; in particular, the b parameter remains far from its measured value even after including subleading effects such as σ–f0 mass splitting and final-state interactions.

## Method

The author computed the Dalitz plot distribution for η′ → ηππ decays using two chiral frameworks: Large-Nc Chiral Perturbation Theory at next-to-leading order, and Resonance Chiral Theory with scalar meson exchange (σ, f0, a0). In the RChT analysis, the coupling constants cd and cm were fixed from the experimental branching ratio and the short-distance constraint 4cd cm = fπ², and subleading 1/Nc effects were incorporated by including a σ–f0 mass difference and a self-energy in the σ propagator.

## Key numbers

- Branching ratio B(η′ → ηπ⁺π⁻) = (44.6 ± 1.4) × 10⁻² (experimental value from Amsler (2008))
- At lowest order in Large-Nc ChPT, the predicted branching ratio is ∼ 10⁻², far below the experimental value
- Dalitz parameters from RChT with subleading effects: a = -0.121, d = -0.075, b = -0.015 (for the isospin-symmetric case)
- Dalitz parameters from Large-Nc ChPT at next-to-leading order: a = -0.282, d = -0.083, b ∼ 10⁻³
- Experimental Dalitz parameters from VES for η′ → ηπ⁺π⁻: a = -0.127 ± 0.016 ± 0.008, b = -0.106 ± 0.028 ± 0.014, d = -0.082 ± 0.017 ± 0.008
- Experimental Dalitz parameters from GAMS-4π for η′ → ηπ⁰π⁰: a = -0.066 ± 0.016 ± 0.003, b = -0.063 ± 0.028 ± 0.004, d = +0.018 ± 0.078 ± 0.006
- Coupling constants used: cd = 28.9 ± 0.2 MeV (fixed from branching ratio), cm from 4cd cm = fπ²; scalar multiplet mass MS = 980 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of η′ → ηππ decays using an effective chiral Lagrangian with scalar mesons (Fariborz (1999)) and a U(3) chiral effective field theory with coupled channels (Borasoy (2005)). The present analysis provides preliminary predictions from Large-Nc ChPT and RChT that can be compared with upcoming high-statistics measurements at KLOE-2, Crystal Ball, Crystal Barrel, WASA, and BES-III. The results show that while RChT with subleading effects reproduces the a and d parameters from VES, the b parameter remains problematic, in contrast to the coupled-channels approach of Borasoy (2005) which reproduces a and b but not d.
