# Implications of Standard-Model flavor violation for new physics searches

> I discuss far-flung ramifications of light-quark flavor physics for searches for physics beyond the Standard Model and consider, particularly, its role in the interpretation of CP-violating observables in meson decays.

## Key claim

The Standard Model's CKM mechanism of CP violation works very well at current precision, but resolving discrete ambiguities in the determination of the unitarity triangle angles (such as the sign of sin α) could be an efficient means of identifying new physics beyond the Standard Model.

## Method

The author reviews how light-quark flavor physics, including isospin-breaking effects such as ρ⁰-ω mixing in hadronic B-meson decays, impacts the interpretation of CP-violating observables. The analysis considers how flavor-based assumptions can eliminate some sensitivity to Standard Model physics while potentially inducing other uncertainties, and examines specific decay modes including B → J/ψ K_S, B → φ K_S, B → f₀(980) K_S, and B → ππ.

## Key numbers

- The measured direct CP violation rate asymmetry in B → K⁺ π⁻ is A_{Kπ} = -0.133 ± 0.030 (stat) + 0.009 (sys)
- The CKM matrix element |V_us| ≡ λ ∼ 0.2, which suppresses intergenerational mixing
- The CKM mechanism of CP violation can be described by a single parameter to an accuracy of some 20%
- The height of the unitarity triangle apex is of O(1), allowing large CP-violating effects in the Standard Model

## Context

This paper extends the discussion of flavor physics presented at a workshop honoring Tony Thomas' 60th birthday, building on prior work by Gardner and O'Connell (1997) on ρ-ω mixing and direct CP violation in hadronic B decays. The analysis connects to earlier studies by Londergan, Cloët et al., and Young on how flavor-breaking effects impact determinations of weak couplings from experiments like NuTeV and parity-violating electron scattering, and it enables sharper tests of whether new physics sources of CP violation exist beyond the CKM mechanism.
