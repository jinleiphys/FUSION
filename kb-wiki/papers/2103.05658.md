# Soft interactions in cold quark matter

> Accurate knowledge of the thermodynamic properties of zero-temperature, high-density quark matter plays an integral role in attempts to constrain the behavior of the dense QCD matter found inside neutron-star cores, irrespective of the phase realized inside the stars. In this Letter, we consider the weak-coupling expansion of the dense QCD equation of state and compute the next-to-next-to-next-to-leading-order contribution arising from the non-Abelian interactions among long-wavelength, dynamically screened gluonic fields. Accounting for these interactions requires an all-loop resummation, which can be performed using hard-thermal-loop (HTL) kinematic approximations. Concretely, we perform a full two-loop computation using the HTL effective theory, valid for the long-wavelength, or soft, modes. We find that the soft sector is well-behaved within cold quark matter, contrary to the case encountered at high temperatures, and find that the new contribution decreases the renormalization-scale dependence of the equation of state at high density.

## Key claim

The soft (long-wavelength) sector of cold quark matter is well-behaved and converges within the weak-coupling expansion, contrary to the high-temperature case where soft modes drive the breakdown of perturbation theory; the new next-to-next-to-next-to-leading-order (N3LO) contribution from soft gluon self-interactions decreases the renormalization-scale dependence of the equation of state at high density.

## Method

The authors performed a full two-loop computation using the hard-thermal-loop (HTL) effective theory, which is valid for long-wavelength (soft) gluonic modes with momentum \(k \lesssim m_E\) where \(m_E \sim \alpha_s^{1/2} \mu_q\) is the Debye mass scale. They analytically separated divergent contributions from finite parts of the three HTL diagrams shown in Figure 2, using dimensional regularization in \(d = 3 - 2\epsilon\) dimensions, and evaluated the finite parts with multidimensional numerics. The calculation accounts for the non-Abelian self-interactions of dynamically screened gluonic fields, requiring an all-loop resummation performed via the HTL kinematic approximations.

## Key numbers

- The Debye screening mass at zero temperature is \(m_E^2 = (2\alpha_s / \pi) \sum_f \mu_f^2\) for multiple massless quark flavors with chemical potentials \(\mu_f\).
- The N2LO soft pressure coefficient \(p^{\rm NNLO}_{-1} = 1\) and \(p^{\rm NNLO}_0 = 1.17201\).
- The N3LO soft pressure coefficients: \(p_{-2} = 11/(6\pi)\), \(p_{-1} = 1.50731(19)\), and \(p_0 = 2.2125(9)\).
- The principle of minimal sensitivity (PMS) factorization scale is \(\Lambda_h^{\rm PMS} \approx 0.275\, m_E\).
- For moderate couplings \(\alpha_s \lesssim 0.4\), the dominant correction to the pressure is the NLO term \(\alpha_s p_1^h\) from hard modes; the soft interaction correction \(\alpha_s^3 p_3^s\) is the first positive correction in the perturbative series.

## Context

This work extends the N2LO calculation of the cold quark matter equation of state by Freedman and McLerran (1970s) and later Kurkela et al. (2009) and Gorda et al. (2018) to include the N3LO soft contribution from gluon self-interactions. It contrasts with the high-temperature quark-gluon plasma case studied by Braaten (1995), Kajantie (2002, 2003), and others, where soft modes exhibit poor convergence and require extensive resummation schemes such as HTL (Andersen 1999) and electrostatic-QCD (Laine 2006). The result enables future determination of the remaining N3LO hard and mixed contributions, and provides a tighter constraint on the neutron-star matter equation of state used in astrophysical analyses such as those by Annala (2017, 2019).
