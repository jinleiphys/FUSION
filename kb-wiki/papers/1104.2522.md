# Strong Coupling Continuum QCD

> The Schwinger-Dyson, Bethe-Salpeter system of equations are the link between coloured quarks and gluons, and colourless hadrons and their properties. This talk reviews some aspects of these studies from the infrared behaviour of ghosts to the prediction of electromagnetic form-factors.

## Key claim

The Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter equations can link colored quarks and gluons to colorless hadrons, but the infinite set of nested integral equations can only be solved in particular truncations; the key physics that builds hadrons is one of strong coupling, and the properties of multiplicative renormalizability and gauge invariance serve as guides for useful truncations.

## Method

The author reviews the Schwinger-Dyson equation (SDE) approach for the fermion and boson propagators in QED and QCD, focusing on the construction of the fermion-boson vertex that ensures both multiplicative renormalizability and gauge invariance. For QCD, the coupled gluon and ghost propagator equations are examined in the Landau gauge, comparing the "scaling" solution (where ghosts are singular and gluons vanish in the infrared) with the "massive" gluon solution (where the ghost dressing function is finite at zero momentum). The quark propagator is then studied by modeling the product of the dressed gluon propagator and the quark-gluon vertex, and these quark functions are input into Bethe-Salpeter equations to compute hadronic properties.

## Key numbers

* The scaling solution for the gluon and ghost propagators gives the ghost behaving like p^{-2κ-2} and the gluon propagator like p^{4κ-2} as momentum p → 0, with κ ≈ 0.6.
* For up and down quarks with current masses of a few MeV, a mass of 300-400 MeV in the low momentum region is naturally generated.
* In the massless case, the operator product expansion applied to the large momentum behavior of the quark mass function yields a ⟨q̄q⟩ condensate of -(250 MeV)³.
* The ρ-meson mass varies with the square of the pion mass, with results following lattice data for unphysically heavy pions and bridging to the real pion mass m_π² = 0.02 GeV².
* The coupling used for the massive gluon solution is equivalent to α_s(M_Z) = 0.118 in the MS̄ scheme.

## Context

This review extends the work of von Smekal and Alkofer (early 2000s) on the scaling solution for ghosts and gluons, and contrasts it with the massive gluon solution long advocated by Papavassiliou and collaborators and by Cornwall and Tiktopoulos. The SDE-BSE approach connects to lattice results from Bowman (2005) and Cucchieri and collaborators, and enables predictions for electromagnetic form-factors of pions and nucleons that are planned for experimental test at the 12 GeV upgrade at JLab.
