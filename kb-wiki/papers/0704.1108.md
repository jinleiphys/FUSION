# Evidence for Symplectic Symmetry in Ab Initio No-Core Shell Model Results for Light Nuclei

> Clear evidence for symplectic symmetry in low-lying states of $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O is reported. Eigenstates of $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O, determined within the framework of the no-core shell model using the JISP16 $NN$ realistic interaction, typically project at the 85-90% level onto a few of the most deformed symplectic basis states that span only a small fraction of the full model space. The results are nearly independent of whether the bare or renormalized effective interactions are used in the analysis. The outcome confirms Elliott's \SU{3} model which underpins the symplectic scheme, and above all, points to the relevance of a symplectic no-core shell model that can reproduce experimental B(E2) values without effective charges as well as deformed spatial modes associated with clustering phenomena in nuclei.

## Key claim
The paper reports that low-lying eigenstates of 12C and 16O, calculated from the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) with the JISP16 realistic NN interaction, project at the 85-90% level onto a few of the most deformed symplectic (Sp(3,R)) basis states, even though those states span only a tiny fraction of the full model space. This symplectic structure is nearly independent of whether the bare or renormalized effective interaction is used, and it confirms Elliott's SU(3) model as a good first-order approximation.

## Method
The authors performed NCSM calculations for the lowest 0+, 2+, and 4+ states in 12C and the ground state in 16O using the JISP16 realistic NN interaction, with model spaces up to Nmax=6 (6ℏΩ). They constructed all 0p-0h and 2ℏΩ 2p-2h symplectic bandheads and generated their Sp(3,R) irreps up to Nmax=6, then computed overlaps of these symplectic basis states with the converged NCSM eigenstates to quantify the projection onto the symplectic subspace.

## Key numbers
- For 12C, the NCSM eigenstates project at approximately 85% onto a few dominant 0p-0h and 2ℏΩ 2p-2h Sp(3,R) irreps, with the leading irrep (0,4) S=0 carrying close to 70% of the wavefunction (Table I, ℏΩ=15 MeV).
- For 16O, the NCSM ground state projects at approximately 85% onto the Sp(3,R) subspace, with the leading irrep (0,0) S=0 carrying 75.02% of the wavefunction (Table II, ℏΩ=15 MeV).
- The typical dimension of a symplectic irrep in the Nmax=6 space is on the order of 10^2, compared to 10^7 for the full NCSM m-scheme basis space.
- For 12C, the B(E2;2+1→0+gs) transition rates computed from the three dominant Sp(3,R) irreps reproduce the NCSM results at 101% to 107% of the NCSM numbers with increasing ℏΩ.

## Context
This work extends prior NCSM studies of light nuclei (Navratil et al., 2000; Navratil and Ormand, 2003) by demonstrating that the resulting wavefunctions are dominated by symplectic symmetry, which had previously been used only with phenomenological interactions (Escher and Levai, 2002; Arickx et al., 1982) or simplistic semi-microscopic interactions (Avancini and Pato, 1993). The results enable a symplectic no-core shell model (Sp-NCSM) that can potentially reach heavier nuclei and reproduce experimental B(E2) values without effective charges, as well as describe α-cluster modes such as the second 0+ state in 12C and 16O (Funaki et al., 2003).
