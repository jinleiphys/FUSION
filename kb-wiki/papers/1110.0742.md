# Towards a Microscopic Reaction Description Based on Energy-Density-Functional Structure Models

> A microscopic calculation of reaction cross sections for nucleon-nucleus scattering has been performed by explicitly coupling the elastic channel to all particle-hole excitations in the target and one-nucleon pickup channels. The particle-hole states may be regarded as doorway states through which the flux flows to more complicated configurations, and subsequently to long-lived compound nucleus resonances. Target excitations for $^{40,48}$Ca, $^{58}$Ni, $^{90}$Zr and $^{144}$Sm were described in a random-phase framework using a Skyrme functional. Reaction cross sections obtained agree very well with experimental data and predictions of a state-of-the-art fitted optical potential. Couplings between inelastic states were found to be negligible, while the pickup channels contribute significantly. The effect of resonances from higher-order channels was assessed. Elastic angular distributions were also calculated within the same method, achieving good agreement with experimental data. For the first time observed absorptions are completely accounted for by explicit channel coupling, for incident energies between 10 and 70 MeV, with consistent angular distribution results.

## Key claim

For the first time, observed absorption in nucleon-nucleus scattering is completely accounted for by explicit channel coupling, without an imaginary optical potential, for incident energies between 10 and 70 MeV, but only when both inelastic couplings to particle-hole excitations and one-nucleon pickup (transfer) channels are included; couplings between inelastic states alone are insufficient.

## Method

The authors performed coupled-reaction-channels (CRC) calculations for neutron and proton scattering on ⁴⁰,⁴⁸Ca, ⁵⁸Ni, ⁹⁰Zr, and ¹⁴⁴Sm, coupling the elastic channel to all particle-hole (p-h) excitations described by random-phase approximation (RPA) using the Skyrme SLy4 energy-density functional, and to one-nucleon pickup (transfer) channels forming a deuteron. Transition densities and potentials were obtained by folding a Gaussian effective nucleon-nucleon interaction (matched to the M3Y interaction at 40 MeV) with the RPA transition densities, using only real potentials throughout.

## Key numbers

- Reaction cross sections were calculated for incident energies between 10 and 70 MeV, achieving complete agreement with experimental data and predictions of a state-of-the-art fitted optical potential.
- For ⁴⁰Ca at 30 MeV, the calculated reaction cross section for neutrons was approximately 1200 mb, matching experimental data within uncertainties.
- For ⁴⁸Ca at 20 MeV, the calculated reaction cross section for neutrons was approximately 1400 mb, in good agreement with experiment.
- For ⁹⁰Zr at 20 MeV, the calculated reaction cross section for neutrons was approximately 1500 mb, consistent with experimental values.
- For ¹⁴⁴Sm at 20 MeV, the calculated reaction cross section for neutrons was approximately 1700 mb, matching experimental data.
- Elastic angular distributions were calculated and achieved good agreement with experimental data across all targets and energies studied.
- The effective nucleon-nucleon interaction used had isoscalar strength V₀⁰ = -24.1921 MeV with range μ₀ = 0.7180 fm⁻¹, and isovector strength V₀¹ = 11.3221 MeV with range μ₁ = 0.7036 fm⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends earlier microscopic RPA-based approaches (Bernard 1979, Osterfeld 1981) that could account for only about 44% and 71% of observed absorption, respectively, by adding pickup channels and using modern Skyrme functionals. It enables fully microscopic reaction calculations for unstable nuclei where phenomenological optical potentials are unreliable, and provides a foundation for extending the method to heavier systems and to include QRPA for open-shell nuclei.
