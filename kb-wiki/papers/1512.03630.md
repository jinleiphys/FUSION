# Linking neutrino oscillations to the nucleosynthesis of elements

> Neutrino interactions with matter play an important role in determining the nucleosynthesis outcome in explosive astrophysical environments such as core-collapse supernovae or mergers of compact objects. In this article, we first discuss our recent work on the importance of studying the time evolution of collective neutrino oscillations among active flavors in determining their effects on nucleosynthesis. We then consider the possible active-sterile neutrino mixing and demonstrate the need of a consistent approach to evolve neutrino flavor oscillations, matter composition, and the hydrodynamics when flavor oscillations can happen very deep inside the supernovae.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the time evolution of collective neutrino oscillations among active flavors must be included to determine their effects on nucleosynthesis, and that when active-sterile mixing occurs deep inside supernovae, a fully consistent approach coupling flavor oscillations, matter composition, and hydrodynamics is necessary, a condition the abstract presents as a demonstration rather than a definitive result.

## Method

The authors performed numerical calculations of neutrino flavor conversion probabilities between active flavors for supernova models from Fischer (2009), using a spherically-symmetric, stationary approximation with a sharp neutrinosphere at r=R. They then coupled a single-angle flavor evolution equation with steady-state hydrodynamic equations for the neutrino-driven wind, including charged-current reactions, to study active-sterile mixing with a mass-squared difference of approximately 1.75 eV² and sin²2θ ≈ 0.1, comparing cases with no feedback, feedback on electron fraction only, and full coupling.

## Key numbers

- Mass-squared difference for active-sterile mixing: δm² ≈ 1.75 eV², with vacuum mixing angle sin²2θ ≈ 0.1, as indicated by neutrino anomalies
- Neutrino luminosities used: (L_νe, L_νe, L_νμ,τ) = (1.67, 2.01, 2.58) × 10⁵¹ erg/s
- Neutrino temperatures: (T_νe, T_νe, T_νμ,τ) = (2.68, 3.78, 3.71) MeV
- Proto-neutron star mass: M = 1.282 M⊙, radius: R = 18.07 km
- Outer boundary temperature: T_b = 0.12 MeV at r = 10³ km
- For the fully coupled active-sterile case, only approximately 15% of νe survive conversion to sterile neutrinos

## Context

This work extends studies of collective neutrino oscillations in supernovae (reviewed by Duan 2010, Duan 2015) by incorporating time-dependent flavor conversion probabilities for supernova models from Fischer (2009). It also builds on earlier active-sterile mixing studies by McLaughlin (1999), Tamborra (2011), and Wu (2013) that did not include feedback on hydrodynamics, showing that such feedback dramatically alters both flavor conversion and nucleosynthesis outcomes. The results enable future self-consistent simulations that include symmetry-breaking effects (Raffelt 2013, Chakraborty 2015, Abbar 2015, Dasgupta 2015) and full quantum kinetic equations.
