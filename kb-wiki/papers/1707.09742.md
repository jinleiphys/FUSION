# Non-critical fluctuations of (net) charges and (net) protons from iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model

> In this paper, we investigate the non-critical fluctuations of (net) charges and (net) protons in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7, 39 and 200 GeV, using iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model with Poisson fluctuations added in the particle event generator between hydrodynamics and UrQMD. Various effects, such as volume fluctuations hadronic evolution and scatterings, resonance decays, as well as realistic centrality cuts and acceptance cuts have been embedded in our model calculations. With properly tuned parameters, iEBE-VISHNU roughly describe the centrality dependent moments and cumulants of (net) charges and (net) protons measured in experiment. Further comparison simulations show that the volume fluctuation is the dominant factor to influence the multiplicity fluctuations, which makes the higher moments of (net) charges largely deviate from the Poison baselines. We also find that the effects from hadronic evolutions and resonance decays are pretty small or even negligible for the multiplicity fluctuations of both (net) charges and (net) protons.

## Key claim

The iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model, with Poisson fluctuations added at the switching surface between hydrodynamics and UrQMD, can roughly describe the centrality-dependent moments and cumulants of (net) charges and (net) protons measured in Au+Au collisions, but the model fails to describe the cumulant ratios of net protons at lower collision energies, suggesting that other effects such as baryon conservation laws or critical fluctuations may be needed.

## Method

The authors used the iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model, which combines (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics (VISH2+1) for the QGP expansion with the UrQMD hadron cascade for the hadronic phase, for Au+Au collisions at 7.7, 39, and 200 GeV. Poisson fluctuations were added in the Monte-Carlo event generator that samples particles on the switching hyper-surface between hydrodynamics and UrQMD, using the Cooper-Frye formula with tuned chemical freeze-out parameters (chemical potentials and strangeness saturation factor) to fit mean multiplicities. Event-by-event simulations used MC-Glauber initial conditions with smoothed entropy density profiles, and the centrality cuts and acceptance cuts followed the STAR experimental definitions.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 39, and 200 GeV were simulated.
- The chemical freeze-out temperature T_c was set to 143.2 MeV (7.7 GeV), 155.5 MeV (39 GeV), and 149.9 MeV (200 GeV).
- The baryon chemical potential mu_B for 0-5% centrality was 312.0 MeV (7.7 GeV), 86.5 MeV (39 GeV), and 21.6 MeV (200 GeV).
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio was fixed at eta/s = 0.08.
- The initial time tau_0 was set to 0.6 fm/c.
- In the most central collisions, the standard deviation sigma from iEBE-VISHNU was about 10% higher than the experimental data for positive and negative charges.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of non-critical fluctuations using the UrQMD model by Luo and collaborators (2016) and the HRG model, which assumed static systems in global equilibrium, by incorporating a dynamically evolving QGP phase via viscous hydrodynamics. The results enable future searches for the QCD critical point by providing non-critical fluctuation baselines from a hybrid model, and the finding that hadronic evolution effects are negligible for multiplicity fluctuations of (net) charges and (net) protons can increase numerical efficiency for massive data simulations. The paper also builds on earlier work by Skokov (2012) and Xu (2016) on volume fluctuation corrections.
