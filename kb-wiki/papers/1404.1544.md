# Pole positions and residues from pion photoproduction using the Laurent+Pietarinen expansion method

> We have applied a new approach to determine the pole positions and residues from pion photoproduction multipoles. The method is based on a Laurent expansion of the partial wave T-matrices, with a Pietarinen series representing the regular part of energy-dependent and single-energy photoproduction solutions. The method has been applied to multipole fits generated by the MAID and GWU/SAID groups. We show that the number and properties of poles extracted from photoproduction data correspond very well to results from $π$N elastic data and values cited by Particle Data Group (PDG). The photoproduction residues provide new information for the electromagnetic current at the pole position, which are independent of background parameterizations, as opposed to the Breit-Wigner representation. Finally, we present the photo-decay amplitudes from the current MAID and SAID solutions at the pole, for all four-star nucleon resonances below W=2 GeV.

## Key claim

The Laurent+Pietarinen (L+P) expansion method can extract pole positions and residues from pion photoproduction multipoles that correspond well to results from πN elastic data and PDG values, but the method's single-channel character prevents distinguishing between a fit with an extra resonance and real branch points versus a fit with one fewer resonance and a complex branch point.

## Method

The authors applied a Laurent expansion of partial-wave T-matrices, with the regular part represented by a convergent series of Pietarinen functions (one per cut), to energy-dependent and single-energy multipole fits from the MAID and GWU/SAID groups for eight dominant multipoles. They used three Pietarinen series (one for unphysical left-hand cuts, two for physical inelastic channels) combined with the minimal number of poles, fitting on the real axis and minimizing using MINUIT. Systematic errors were quantified by varying the third physical branch point between fixed thresholds of dominant inelastic channels and a free parameter.

## Context

This work extends the L+P method previously applied to πN elastic scattering (Svarc 2013) to single-pion photoproduction multipoles from the MAID (MAID2007) and GWU/SAID (CM12) groups. The method provides photoproduction residues that are independent of background parameterizations used in the Breit-Wigner approach, unlike earlier extractions. The results enable comparison of pole parameters across different analyses and provide photo-decay amplitudes at the pole for all four-star nucleon resonances below W=2 GeV, with the N(1710)1/2+ state identified as a candidate for resolution by future double-polarization experiments.
