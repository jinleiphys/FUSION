# Dirac sea effects on Heavy Quarkonia decay widths in magnetized matter -- a field theoretic model of composite hadrons

> We study the partial decay widths of charmonium (bottomonium) states to ${\rm D\bar D \; (B\bar B)}$ mesons in magnetized (nuclear) matter using a field theoretical model of composite hadrons with quark (and antiquark) constituents. These are computed from the mass modifications of the decaying and produced mesons within a chiral effective model, including the nucleon Dirac sea effects. The mass modifications of the open charm (bottom) mesons are calculated from their interactions with the nucleons and the scalar mesons, whereas the mass shift of the heavy quarkonium state is obtained from the medium change of a scalar dilaton field, $χ$, which mimics the gluon condensates of QCD. The Dirac sea contributions are observed to lead to a rise (drop) in the quark condensates as the magnetic field is increased, an effect called the (inverse) magnetic catalysis. These effects are observed to be significant and the anomalous magnetic moments (AMMs) of the nucleons are observed to play an important role. For $ρ_B$=0, there is observed to be magnetic catalysis (MC) without and with AMMs, whereas, for $ρ_B=ρ_0$, the inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) is observed when the AMMs are taken into account, contrary to MC, when the AMMs are ignored. In the presence of a magnetic field, there are also mixings of spin 0 (pseudoscalar) and spin 1 (vector) states (PV mixing) which modify the masses of these mesons. The magnetic field effects on the heavy quarkonium decay widths should have observable consequences on the production the heavy flavour mesons, which are created in the early stage of ultra-relativistic peripheral heavy ion collisions, at RHIC and LHC, when the produced magnetic fields can still be extremely large.

## Key claim

The partial decay widths of charmonium (bottomonium) states to open heavy flavor mesons in magnetized nuclear matter are significantly modified by nucleon Dirac sea effects, with the anomalous magnetic moments (AMMs) of nucleons determining whether magnetic catalysis (MC) or inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) occurs at nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The authors compute partial decay widths of ψ(3770) → D̄D and Υ(4S) → B̄B using a field theoretical model of composite hadrons with explicit quark and antiquark constituents, where the matrix element of the light quark-antiquark pair creation term from the free Dirac Hamiltonian is evaluated between initial and final meson states. The mass modifications of the decaying and produced mesons are calculated within a chiral SU(3) effective model that incorporates nucleon Dirac sea effects through summation of scalar (σ, ζ, δ) and vector (ω, ρ) tadpole diagrams, along with pseudoscalar-vector (PV) mixing effects and Landau level contributions for charged hadrons in the presence of an external magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- For ρ_B = 0, magnetic catalysis (MC) is observed both without and with nucleon AMMs, meaning the quark condensate rises with increasing magnetic field
- For ρ_B = ρ_0 (nuclear matter saturation density), inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC) is observed when nucleon AMMs are taken into account, contrary to MC when AMMs are ignored
- The study focuses on ψ(3770) and Υ(4S), which are the lowest quarkonium states that decay to D̄D and B̄B respectively in vacuum
- The PV mixing effects are observed to be much more dominant for open heavy flavor mesons (D(̄D)-D*(̄D*) and B(̄B)-B*(̄B*)) compared to ψ(3770)-η_c' and Υ(4S)-η_b(4S) mixings for the quarkonium states

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy quarkonium decay widths in magnetized matter (Mishra et al., various years) by incorporating nucleon Dirac sea effects that were not included in earlier calculations using the same chiral effective model and field theoretical composite hadron approach. The Dirac sea contributions, previously studied in the Walecka model by Haber et al. and by Arghya et al. (who found IMC at finite density with AMMs), are here applied to heavy flavor meson masses and decay widths, enabling predictions for heavy flavor meson production in ultra-relativistic peripheral heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC where magnetic fields can be extremely large.
