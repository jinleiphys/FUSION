# Hadronic transport approach to neutrino nucleus scattering: the Giessen BUU model and its validation

> We present the GiBUU model for neutrino nucleus scattering: assuming impulse approximation, this reaction is treated as a two step process. In the initial state step, the neutrinos interact with bound nucleons. In the final state step, the outgoing particles of the initial reaction are propagated through the nucleus and undergo final state interactions. In this contribution, we focus on the validation of the initial and final state interaction treatment in GiBUU using experimental data for pion-nucleus, photon-nucleus and electron-nucleus scattering.

## Key claim

The GiBUU model provides a consistent, coupled-channel treatment of both the initial neutrino-nucleon interaction and final-state interactions (FSI) within a single framework, and its validation against pion-, photon-, and electron-nucleus scattering data serves as a benchmark for the reliability of its neutrino-nucleus predictions, without any tuning of specific input parameters to describe particular reaction channels.

## Method

The GiBUU model treats neutrino-nucleus scattering as a two-step process under the impulse approximation: first, neutrinos interact with bound nucleons in a local Fermi gas with mean-field potentials and medium-modified cross sections; second, the outgoing particles are propagated through the nucleus using the coupled-channel semi-classical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport equations, which include mean-field potentials, collisional broadening, resonance propagation with medium-modified spectral functions, and explicit treatment of baryon-meson and baryon-baryon collisions. The model was validated by comparing its predictions to experimental data for pion absorption on nuclei, photon-induced pion production, and electron-induced proton knockout (transparency ratios) across multiple target nuclei and beam energies.

## Key numbers

- Pion absorption cross sections on nuclei show that including the Coulomb potential reduces the cross section for π⁺ and increases it for π⁻, with the hadronic potential adding to a strongly repulsive potential for π⁺; for π⁻ at very low energies the two potentials can compensate each other, particularly for light nuclei.
- For photon-induced π⁰ production on Ca, Nb, and Pb, the momentum differential cross sections show a reduction in the region around p_π = 0.3–0.5 GeV due to pion absorption via the Δ resonance (π N → Δ followed by Δ N → NN) and elastic scattering (π N → π N) that reshuffles pions to lower momenta.
- The transparency ratio for A(e,e' p) reactions on C, Fe, and Pb as a function of four-momentum transfer Q² shows perfect agreement with data from JLab and SLAC over the full Q² range.
- For neutrino-induced reactions, the MiniBooNE flux peaks at 0.7 GeV neutrino energy and the K2K flux peaks at 1.2 GeV neutrino energy; FSI have a major impact on both NC π⁰ production and proton knockout spectra.

## Context

This work extends the GiBUU framework, previously applied to heavy-ion collisions, pion-induced reactions (Buss, 2007), and electron-induced processes (Lehr, 2001), to neutrino-nucleus scattering. It differs from event generators like NUANCE and NEUT by not tuning specific inputs (e.g., pion absorption cross sections) to describe particular channels, instead aiming to explain a wide range of reactions simultaneously. The validation against pion, photon, and electron data provides a benchmark for neutrino predictions, and the results enable more reliable interpretation of neutrino oscillation experiments such as MiniBooNE and K2K, which use heavy nuclear targets (carbon, oxygen, iron).
