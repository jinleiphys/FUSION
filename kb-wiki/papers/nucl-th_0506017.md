# Isospin and symmetry energy effects on nuclear fragment production in liquid-gas type phase transition region

> We have demonstrated that the isospin of nuclei influences the fragment production during the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition. Calculations for Au197, Sn124, La124 and Kr78 at various excitation energies were carried out on the basis of the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM). We analyzed the behavior of the critical exponent tau with the excitation energy and its dependence on the critical temperature. Relative yields of fragments were classified with respect to the mass number of the fragments in the transition region. In this way, we have demonstrated that nuclear multifragmentation exhibits a 'bimodality' behavior. We have also shown that the symmetry energy has a small influence on fragment mass distribution, however, its effect is more pronounced in the isotope distributions of produced fragments.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the isospin (neutron-to-proton ratio) of the nuclear source influences fragment production during the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition, and that the symmetry energy has a small influence on fragment mass distributions but a more pronounced effect on isotope distributions; however, these conclusions are based on calculations using the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) with specific parameter choices, and the authors note that the average characteristics of produced hot fragments are not very sensitive to the symmetry energy.

## Method

The authors performed statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) calculations for four nuclear sources (Au197, Sn124, La124, and Kr78) at excitation energies from 2 to 20 MeV/nucleon, using a freeze-out volume of V=3V0 (density approximately ρ0/3). They analyzed the behavior of the critical exponent τ from power-law fits to fragment mass and charge yields (Y(A) ∝ A^(-τ) and Y(Z) ∝ Z^(-τ_Z)) as a function of excitation energy, and examined the influence of the symmetry energy coefficient γ (varying it between 8 and 25 MeV) on fragment distributions, including the effects of secondary deexcitation via evaporation.

## Key numbers

- The four nuclear sources have neutron-to-proton ratios (N/Z) of 1.49 (Au197), 1.48 (Sn124), 1.18 (La124), and 1.17 (Kr78).
- The minimum of the critical exponent τ occurs at excitation energies of approximately 5.3, 5.2, 4, and 4.2 MeV/nucleon for Au197, Sn124, La124, and Kr78, respectively.
- The multifragmentation onset (disappearance of U-shaped mass distributions) is about 5 MeV/nucleon for Au197 and Sn124, and about 4 MeV/nucleon for La124 and Kr78.
- The standard SMM parameters include: binding energy of infinite nuclear matter W0 = 16 MeV, surface coefficient B0 = 18 MeV, critical temperature Tc = 18 MeV, symmetry energy parameter γ = 25 MeV, and Coulomb parameter with r0 = 1.17 fm.
- The maximum variance of Amax/A0 occurs at an excitation energy of 4-5 MeV/nucleon for all sources.
- The temperature plateau (caloric curve) appears in the excitation energy range 3-7 MeV/nucleon, with the variance of temperature exhibiting a maximum at about 5-6 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous SMM analyses (Botvina, Bondorf) and experimental studies (Agostino, Hauger) of nuclear multifragmentation as a liquid-gas phase transition, specifically addressing isospin effects that are important for astrophysical applications such as supernova explosions and neutron star formation (Bethe, Botvina). The results enable future extraction of the symmetry energy from experimental isotope distributions via isoscaling analysis (Lozhkin) and provide a framework for interpreting recent experimental evidence of decreasing symmetry energy with temperature in multifragmentation (LeFevre, Shetty).
