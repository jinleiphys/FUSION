# The Nuclear Equation of State at high densities

> Ab inito calculations for the nuclear many-body problem make predictions for the density and isospin dependence of the nuclear equation-of-state (EOS) far away from the saturation point of nuclear matter. I compare predictions from microscopic and phenomenological approaches. Constraints on the EOS derived from heavy ion reactions, in particular from subthreshold kaon production, as well as constraints from neutron stars are discussed.

## Key claim

Ab initio calculations predict a soft nuclear equation of state (EOS) at densities up to about three times saturation density, with no way to obtain an EOS as stiff as the hard Skyrme force or NL3, but the high-density behavior of the symmetry energy remains dramatically unconstrained and requires heavy-ion reaction data.

## Method

The paper compares predictions for the nuclear EOS from three classes of models: phenomenological density functionals (Gogny, Skyrme, relativistic mean-field), effective field theory, and ab initio approaches (variational calculations, Brueckner-Hartree-Fock, relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock, and Green's function Monte-Carlo). It then discusses constraints from subthreshold kaon production in heavy-ion reactions and from neutron star observations, using transport model calculations (RQMD and IQMD) to compare with KaoS Collaboration data.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter: \(\rho_0 \simeq 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\)
- Nuclear densities between \(2\div 3 \rho_0\) are accessible at \(E_{\mathrm{lab}}\sim 0.1\div 1\) AGeV; highest baryon densities (\(\sim 8 \rho_0\)) will be reached at \(20\div 30\) AGeV
- DBHF Bonn A compressibility: K = 230 MeV
- Soft Skyrme EOS: K = 200 MeV; hard Skyrme EOS: K = 380 MeV
- Symmetry energy at \(\rho \simeq 0.6 \rho_0\): \(E_{\mathrm{sym}} \simeq 24\) MeV
- Linear asymmetry parameter \(a_4\) constrained to \(32\div 36\) MeV for relativistic functionals from charge radii and skin thickness in heavy nuclei
- Nonrelativistic effective mass and Dirac mass shown at Fermi momenta \(k_F = 1.07,\ 1.35,\ 1.7\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\)

## Context

This work extends comparisons between microscopic ab initio calculations (Akmal et al. 1998, Zuo et al. 2002, van Dalen et al. 2004) and phenomenological density functionals (NL3 from Lalazissis et al. 1997, DD-TW from Typel and Wolter 1999). It builds on the finding by Fuchs et al. (2001) that subthreshold \(K^+\) production provides a reliable tool for constraining the EOS, confirmed by Hartnack et al. (2005). The paper enables future constraints from radioactive beam facilities (FAIR/GSI, SPIRAL2/GANIL, RIA) and from neutron star observations, while highlighting that the isospin dependence of the nuclear forces remains poorly constrained at supra-normal densities.
