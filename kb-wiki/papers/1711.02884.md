# Chiral phase transition of three flavor QCD with nonzero magnetic field using standard staggered fermions

> Lattice simulations for (2+1)-flavor QCD with external magnetic field demonstrated that the quark mass is one of the important parameters responsible for the (inverse) magnetic catalysis. We discuss the dependences of chiral condensates and susceptibilities, the Polyakov loop on the magnetic field and quark mass in three degenerate flavor QCD. The lattice simulations are performed using standard staggered fermions and the plaquette action with spatial sizes Ns = 16 and 24 and a fixed temporal size Nt = 4. The value of the quark masses are chosen such that the system undergoes a first order chiral phase transition and crossover with zero magnetic field. We find that in light mass regime, the quark chiral condensate undergoes magnetic catalysis in the whole temperature region and the phase transition tend to become stronger as the magnetic field increases. In crossover regime, deconfinement transition temperature is shifted by the magnetic field when quark mass ma is less than 0.4. The lattice cutoff effects are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper finds that in three-flavor QCD with standard staggered fermions on a coarse lattice (Nt=4), the critical temperature for the chiral phase transition increases with magnetic field (magnetic catalysis) across a range of quark masses, contradicting the inverse magnetic catalysis observed in simulations using stout-smeared fermions at physical pion mass. The authors explicitly note that this increase might come from cutoff effects (lattice artifacts) due to the relatively heavy pion mass (around 290 MeV) in their setup.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with three degenerate flavors of standard staggered fermions and the plaquette gauge action on lattices of size 16^3 x 4 and 24^3 x 4. They implemented a uniform external magnetic field in the z-direction via U(1) link variables, with the magnetic flux quantized as Nb = 0 to 56. They measured the chiral condensate, its susceptibility, the Polyakov loop, and the Binder cumulant B4 for the chiral condensate across five quark mass values (ma = 0.024, 0.028, 0.2, 0.4, 0.8) to map the phase diagram as a function of magnetic field and quark mass.

## Key numbers

* For the light mass regime (ma = 0.024 on 24^3 x 4 lattice), the critical beta increases monotonically with magnetic flux Nb from 0 to 56, indicating magnetic catalysis; the phase transition remains first order and becomes stronger with increasing magnetic field.
* For ma = 0.028 on 16^3 x 4 lattice (crossover at B=0), the Binder cumulant B4 is consistent with crossover behavior at Nb=0, and the critical beta also increases with Nb.
* For the heavy mass regime, the deconfinement transition temperature (determined by Polyakov loop susceptibility) shifts with magnetic field when quark mass ma is less than 0.4, but for ma = 0.8 the effects of the magnetic field are tiny.
* The threshold mass for magnetic field domination is defined as a*m_th(Nb) = sqrt(2*pi*Nb/(Nx*Ny*|q|)), giving values of 0.19, 0.38, and 0.79 for Nb = 1, 4, and 17 respectively on the 16^3 lattice with q=2/3.

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of QCD with magnetic fields that found conflicting results: d2010qcd (2010) using standard staggered fermions with Nf=2 found Tc increases with B, while Bali et al. (2012) using stout-smeared fermions at physical pion mass found inverse catalysis and decreasing Tc with B. The authors systematically vary the quark mass in three-flavor QCD to probe the transition between these regimes, using parameters from Smith (2011) for the first-order transition region. The results enable future studies with improved lattice actions to separate continuum physics from lattice artifacts, particularly regarding the role of the lowest Landau level in magnetic catalysis.
