# Suppression of the LHC $p/π$ ratio due to the QCD mass spectrum

> Recent measurements of the proton to pion ratio at $\sqrt{s}_{NN}=2.7$ TeV by the ALICE collaboration at the LHC have found it to be lower than predictions from thermal fits. In this paper we investigate the role that the extended mass spectrum via Hagedorn states- massive resonances that follow an exponential mass spectrum and possess large decay widths- play in the determination of particle ratios at LHC through a scenario of multi-particle reactions and dynamical chemical equilibrium within the hadron gas phase. We show that it is possible to describe the lower $p/π$ ratio at LHC while still obtaining the experimental ratio of $K/π$ and $Λ/π^+$ in the Hagedorn state scenario if the protons are underpopulated at the switching temperature from hydrodynamics to the hadron gas phase.

# Suppression of the LHC $p/\pi$ ratio due to the QCD mass spectrum

## Key claim
The lower proton-to-pion ratio measured by ALICE at LHC can be explained by dynamical chemical equilibration in the hadron gas phase if protons are initially underpopulated at the switching temperature from hydrodynamics, using Hagedorn states (massive resonances following an exponential mass spectrum) that enable multi-particle reactions. The model simultaneously reproduces the $K/\pi$ and $\Lambda/\pi^+$ ratios only when protons start below their chemical equilibrium values.

## Method
The authors model an expanding, cooling fireball using a Bjorken expansion with accelerating radial flow, starting from a switching temperature $T_{sw}$ where the hadron gas phase begins. They solve rate equations for Hagedorn states, pions, and baryon-antibaryon pairs ($p\bar{p}$, $K\bar{K}$, $\Lambda\bar{\Lambda}$) using master equations that include both forward and backward reactions (detailed balance). Three different Hagedorn mass spectra ($\rho_1$, $\rho_2$, $\rho_3$) are tested, each with different prefactor dependencies, and the system evolves until a final temperature $T_{end}$ tuned to match experimental data.

## Key numbers
- ALICE measured $p/\pi = 0.046 \pm 0.003$, $K/\pi = 0.149 \pm 0.01$, and $\Lambda/\pi^+ = 0.0194 \pm 0.0025$ at $\sqrt{s}_{NN}=2.7$ TeV.
- Switching temperature $T_{sw}=155$ MeV (also tested at $T_{sw}=165$ MeV); final temperatures $T_{end}=133$ MeV for $\rho_1$, $T_{end}=136$ MeV for $\rho_2$, $T_{end}=128$ MeV for $\rho_3$ under standard assumptions.
- Hagedorn state masses range from minimum $M_0=1.7$ GeV to maximum $MM=3$ GeV (tested up to $MM=5$ GeV).
- Initial time $\tau_0=0.6$ fm (tested at $\tau_0=1.0$ fm); acceleration $a_0=0.03$ fm$^{-1}$ (tested at $a_0=0.05$ fm$^{-1}$).
- Decay width parameterization: $\Gamma_i [\text{GeV}] = 0.15 m_i - 0.027$.
- Total pion number at LHC: $N_{\pi^{all}} = 2197.5$ for $0-5\%$ central collisions.
- For RHIC at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV: $p/\pi=0.106$, $\bar{p}/\pi=0.082$, $K/\pi=0.156$, $\Lambda/\pi=0.054$ from STAR; $p/\pi=0.100$, $\bar{p}/\pi=0.075$, $K/\pi=0.174$ from PHENIX.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of Hagedorn states in heavy-ion collisions at SPS and RHIC (Noronha-Hostler 2007, 2009; Greiner 2004) to LHC energies, where the ALICE collaboration (Abelev 2012) found a lower $p/\pi$ ratio than predicted by thermal models. It contrasts with the out-of-equilibrium final state reactions approach using UrQMD (Steinheimer 2012) and the proposal of different hadronization temperatures for light and strange quarks (Ratti 2011). The Hagedorn state description enables matching lattice QCD thermodynamics up to $T\approx 155$ MeV (Noronha-Hostler 2012), beyond the standard hadron resonance gas limit of $T\approx 130$ MeV (Borsanyi 2010). The results enable further investigation of chemical freeze-out temperatures and the role of the extended mass spectrum in elliptic flow (Noronha-Hostler 2013) and thermal fits (Noronha-Hostler 2009).
