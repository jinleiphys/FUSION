# Ab-initio Gorkov-Green's function calculations of open-shell nuclei

> We present results from a new ab-initio method that uses the self-consistent Gorkov Green's function theory to address truly open-shell systems. The formalism has been recently worked out up to second order and is implemented here in nuclei for the first time on the basis of realistic nuclear forces. We find good convergence of the results with respect to the basis size in Ca44 and Ni74 and discuss quantities of experimental interest including ground-state energies, pairing gaps and particle addition/removal spectroscopy. These results demonstrate that the Gorkov method is a valid alternative to multireference approaches for tackling degenerate or near degenerate quantum systems. In particular, it increases the number of mid-mass nuclei accessible in an ab-initio fashion from a few tens to a few hundreds.

## Key claim

The Gorkov self-consistent Green's function method, truncated at second order and using only two-nucleon (2N) interactions, can produce converged ab-initio results for open-shell nuclei like Ca-44 and Ni-74, but the omission of three-nucleon (3N) forces leads to too much binding energy and too small radii, making the current results "mostly illustrative."

## Method

The authors implement the Gorkov-Green's function formalism up to second order in the diagrammatic expansion of the normal and anomalous self-energies, using a next-to-next-to-next-to-leading-order (N3LO) chiral two-nucleon interaction softened via free-space similarity renormalization group (SRG) techniques to a momentum scale of lambda = 2.0 fm^-1. Self-consistency is limited to the static (energy-independent) part of the self-energy (the "sc0" approximation), and the resulting eigenvalue problem is solved iteratively with a Lanczos algorithm to handle the increasing number of poles. The total binding energy is computed via the Koltun-Galitskii sum rule, and separation energies and spectroscopic factors are extracted from the poles and residues of the Gorkov propagators.

## Key numbers

*   Ground-state binding energy of Ca-44: -669.6(1) MeV (extrapolated to infinite oscillator basis, using 2N forces only).
*   Ground-state binding energy of Ni-74: -1269.7(2) MeV (extrapolated to infinite oscillator basis, using 2N forces only).
*   Neutron pairing gap for Ca-44: 1.16 MeV (from the three-point mass difference formula).
*   Neutron pairing gap for Ni-74: 1.17(1) MeV (from the three-point mass difference formula).
*   Matter root-mean-square radius for Ca-44: 2.48 fm.
*   Matter root-mean-square radius for Ni-74: 2.75 fm.
*   Convergence in Ca-44: going from N_max=11 to N_max=13 lowers the binding energy minima by just a few keV; the binding energy at N_max=13 varies by less than 200 keV over a wide range of harmonic oscillator spacings.
*   Convergence in Ni-74: going from N_max=11 to N_max=13 yields an additional 600 keV; scanning a large range of oscillator frequencies changes the binding energy by about 1 MeV.

## Context

This work extends ab-initio methods, previously limited to doubly closed (sub-)shell nuclei and their immediate neighbors by approaches such as coupled-cluster (CC) (Hagen, 2010), in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) (Tsukiyama, 2010), and Dyson self-consistent Green's function (Barbieri, 2009), to truly open-shell systems. By using a single-reference method based on a Bogoliubov vacuum that incorporates static pairing correlations, the Gorkov approach can access hundreds of mid-mass nuclei instead of a few tens. The next steps are to incorporate three-nucleon forces and to generalize the Faddeev random-phase approximation (FRPA) truncation scheme (Barbieri, 2000; Barbieri, 2010) to the Gorkov context, which would allow a quantitative treatment of nuclear superfluidity.
