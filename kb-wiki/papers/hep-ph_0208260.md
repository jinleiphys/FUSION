# Nucleon Compton Scattering with Two Space--Like Photons

> We calculated two--photon exchange effects for elastic electron--proton scattering at high momentum transfers. The corresponding nucleon Compton amplitude is defined by two space--like virtual photons that appear to have significant virtualities. We make predictions for a) a single--spin beam asymmetry, and b) a single--spin target asymmetry or recoil proton polarization caused by an unpolarized electron beam.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first theoretical evaluation of parity-conserving single-spin asymmetries in elastic electron-proton scattering that takes nucleon structure into account, but the results depend on a model for the non-forward Compton amplitude that assumes a form factor-like suppression with respect to the overall transferred momentum, which was not considered in earlier calculations.

## Method
The authors calculated the absorptive part of the two-photon exchange amplitude for elastic electron-proton scattering, which interferes with the one-photon exchange amplitude to produce single-spin asymmetries. They separated the calculation into elastic intermediate states (model-independent, using unitarity and known proton form factors) and inelastic intermediate states (model-dependent, using deep-inelastic structure functions and a non-forward Compton form factor related to generalized parton distributions). The integration over loop momenta was performed for both elastic and inelastic contributions, with the inelastic case requiring an additional integration over the invariant mass squared of the intermediate hadronic state.

## Key numbers
- Target asymmetry (= recoil proton polarization) is evaluated at the per cent level.
- The beam asymmetry measured by the SAMPLE Collaboration at beam energy 0.2 GeV has magnitude about 10^{-5}.
- The non-forward Compton form factor is modeled using the dipole formula F(Q^2) = (1 + Q^2/0.71 GeV^2)^{-2}.
- The integration region for exchanged photon virtualities is shown for representative electron beam energy E_b = 5 GeV and external Q^2 = 4 GeV^2.
- The inelastic contribution exceeds the elastic contribution at higher energies above the pion threshold.

## Context
This work extends earlier theoretical calculations by De Rujula et al. (1971, 1973) that considered deep-inelastic intermediate states but dramatically overestimated two-photon exchange effects by not including form factor-like suppression with respect to the overall transferred momentum. The calculations are compared with recent SAMPLE Collaboration data on beam asymmetry. The results enable experimental extraction of the absorptive part of the virtual Compton amplitude with two space-like photons at large Mandelstam t, and provide a framework for interpreting measurements of parity-conserving single-spin observables in elastic electron-proton scattering.
