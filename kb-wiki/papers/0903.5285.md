# Neutrino Induced Coherent Pion Production off Nuclei and PCAC

> We review the Rein--Sehgal model and criticize its use for low energy neutrino induced coherent pion production. We have studied the validity of the main approximations implicit in that model, trying to compare with physical observables when that is possible and with microscopical calculations. Next, we have tried to elaborate a new improved model by removing the more problematic approximations, while keeping the model still reasonably simple. Last, we have discussed the limitations intrinsic to any approach based on the partial conservation of the axial current hypothesis. In particular, we have shown the inability of such models to determine the angular distribution of the outgoing pion with respect to the direction of the incoming neutrino, except for the $q^2= 0$ kinematical point.

## Key claim

The Rein-Sehgal (RS) model, widely used by experimental collaborations for neutrino-induced coherent pion production, contains approximations that become invalid at low neutrino energies (below 2 GeV), and any approach based solely on the Partial Conservation of the Axial Current (PCAC) hypothesis cannot determine the angular distribution of the outgoing pion with respect to the incoming neutrino direction except at the single kinematical point \(q^2 = 0\).

## Method

The authors first review the RS model and identify its problematic approximations: neglecting the \(t\)-dependence of the pion-nucleon cross section, using a \(t\)-independent absorption factor, and assuming the off-shell pion-nucleus cross section can be approximated by the on-shell one. They then construct an improved model that removes these approximations by incorporating the full \(t\)-dependence of the non spin-flip pion-nucleon cross section (evaluated from SAID phase shifts) and using an eikonal distortion factor \(\Gamma(b,z)\) that depends on impact parameter and the inelastic pion-nucleon cross section. The improved model is tested against elastic pion-nucleus scattering data for carbon, oxygen, and calcium at various pion kinetic energies.

## Key numbers

- The RS model's absorption factor is \(F_{\rm abs} = e^{- \langle x \rangle / \lambda}\) with average path length \(\langle x \rangle = 3R/4\) for a hard sphere nucleus of radius \(R = R_0 A^{1/3}\) and \(R_0 = 1\) fm; for pion-nucleus scattering the authors use \(\langle x \rangle = 4R/3\) giving \(F_{\rm abs}^\pi = e^{-(A^{1/3} / \pi R_0^2) \sigma_{inel}}\).
- The pion decay constant used is \(f_\pi \approx 92.4\) MeV.
- The RS model attaches a propagator term \(1/(1 - q^2 / m_A^2)^2\) with \(m_A \approx 1\) GeV to extend the Adler PCAC formula to non-forward lepton directions.
- The nuclear form factor in the RS model is \(F_{\cal A}(t) = A e^{b t / 6}\) with \(b = R_0^2 A^{2/3}\) and \(R_0 = 1\) fm.
- For carbon at \(E_\pi = 250\) MeV (approximately the peak of the \(\pi^0\) yield in MiniBooNE), the corrected \(t\)-dependent pion-nucleon cross section produces a much narrower angular distribution and significantly smaller area compared to the RS \(t=0\) approximation.

## Context

This work extends and critiques the Rein-Sehgal model (Rein 1982), which was originally developed for neutrino energies above 2 GeV on aluminum but has been applied by experimental collaborations such as K2K (Hasegawa 2005) and MiniBooNE (Aguilar-Arevalo 2008) to much lower energies. The paper contrasts with microscopical \(\Delta(1232)\)-dominance models (Kim 1996, Kelkar 1996, Singh 2006, Alvarez-Ruso 2007, Amaro 2008) that are reliable only below 1 GeV, and with more recent PCAC-based corrections (Berger 2008, Paschos 2009) that still neglect the volume-surface problem first pointed out by Bell (1964). The improved model enables more reliable predictions for low-energy neutrino oscillation experiments where coherent pion production is a significant background, particularly for neutral-current \(\pi^0\) events that can mimic electron neutrino appearance signals.
