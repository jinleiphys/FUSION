# Electromagnetic Transition Strengths for Light Nuclei in the Skyrme model

> We calculate reduced $B(E2)$ electromagnetic transition strengths for light nuclei of mass numbers $B=8,12,16,20,24$ and $32$ within the Skyrme model. We find that the predicted transition strengths are of the correct order of magnitude and the computed intrinsic quadrupole moments match the experimentally observed effective nuclear shapes. For the Hoyle state we predict a large $B(E2)\!\uparrow$ value of $0.0521\, \rm{e}^2\rm{b}^2$. For Oxygen-16, we can obtain a quantitative understanding of the ground state rotational band and the rotational excitations of the second spin-0 state, $0_2^+$.

## Key claim

The Skyrme model, calibrated to the Carbon-12 ground state (the Lau-Manton calibration), predicts reduced $B(E2)$ transition strengths for light nuclei of mass numbers $B=8,12,16,20,24,32$ that are of the correct order of magnitude, with the computed intrinsic quadrupole moments matching experimentally observed effective nuclear shapes, though the calibration uses a pion mass substantially larger and a pion decay constant substantially lower than their experimental values.

## Method

The authors recalculated classical Skyrmion solutions for baryon numbers $B=8,12,16,20,24,32$ using nonlinear conjugate gradient and damped full field evolution numerical relaxation techniques on grids with $(201)^3$ points and spatial grid spacing $\Delta x=0.1$. They computed the electric quadrupole tensor from the charge density (half the baryon density for zero-isospin nuclei) and extracted intrinsic quadrupole moments $Q_0$, then converted these to reduced $B(E2)$ transition strengths using the rotational model formula $B(E2:0^+\rightarrow 2^+)=5Q_0^2/16\pi$. The Skyrme model was calibrated by matching the experimental nuclear mass (11178 MeV) and matter radius (2.43 fm) of the Carbon-12 ground state.

## Key numbers

- Carbon-12 ($B=12$, $D_{3h}$ Skyrmion): $B(E2:0_1^+\rightarrow 2_1^+)=0.00409\ \text{e}^2\text{b}^2$, deviating by 3% from the experimental value of $0.00397\ \text{e}^2\text{b}^2$; intrinsic quadrupole moment $Q_0=-0.203\ \text{e}\text{b}$ versus experimental $-0.200\ \text{e}\text{b}$
- Hoyle state ($B=12$, $D_{4h}$ Skyrmion): predicted $B(E2:0_2^+\rightarrow 2_2^+)=0.0521\ \text{e}^2\text{b}^2$, corresponding to 63.9 Weisskopf units, with intrinsic quadrupole moment $Q_0=0.724\ \text{e}\text{b}$
- Beryllium-8 ($B=8$, twisted $D_{4h}$ Skyrmion): $B(E2)=0.00366\ \text{e}^2\text{b}^2$ (7.7 W); untwisted $D_{4h}$ Skyrmion gives $0.00563\ \text{e}^2\text{b}^2$ (11.8 W)
- Calibration parameters: energy conversion factor $F_\pi/4e_{\text{Sky}}=6.154\ \text{MeV}$, length conversion factor $2/e_{\text{Sky}}F_\pi=1.061\ \text{fm}$, with $e_{\text{Sky}}=3.889$, $F_\pi=95.6\ \text{MeV}$, and $m_\pi=185.9\ \text{MeV}$

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model's application from predicting excitation energies of rotational bands in Carbon-12 (Lau 2014) to calculating electromagnetic transition strengths, providing a non-trivial test of the model. The Skyrmion configurations for $B=8,12,16,20,24,32$ correspond to arrangements of $B=4$ cubic sub-units, resembling those in the alpha-particle model of Brink (1970) and Blatt and Weisskopf. The results enable future comparison with experimental $B(E2)$ data for the Hoyle state, which is experimentally challenging to measure (Freer 2014), and provide a framework for understanding rotational bands in Oxygen-16, where tetrahedral and square Skyrmion configurations arise.
