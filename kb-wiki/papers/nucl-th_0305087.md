# Spurious modes in extended RPA theories

> Necessary conditions that the spurious state associated with the translational motion and its double-phonon state have zero excitation energy in extended RPA (ERPA) theories which include both one-body and two-body amplitudes are investigated using the small amplitude limit of the time-dependent density-matrix theory (STDDM). STDDM provides us with a quite general form of ERPA as compared with other similar theories in the sense that all components of one-body and two-body amplitudes are taken into account. Two conditions are found necessary to guarantee the above property of the single and double spurious states: The first is that no truncation in the single-particle space should be made. This condition is necessary for the closure relation to be used and is common for the single and double spurious states. The second depends on the mode. For the single spurious state all components of the one-body amplitudes must be included, and for the double spurious state all components of one-body and two-body amplitudes have to be included. It is also shown that the Kohn theorem and the continuity equations for transition densities and currents hold under the same conditions as the spurious states. ERPA theories formulated using the Hartree-Fock ground state have a non-hermiticity problem. A method for formulating ERPA with hermiticity is also proposed using the time-dependent density-matrix formalism.

## Key claim

The spurious state associated with translational motion and its double-phonon state have zero excitation energy in extended RPA (ERPA) theories only under two specific conditions: no truncation of the single-particle space (necessary for the closure relation), and inclusion of all components of the one-body amplitudes (for the single spurious state) or all components of both one-body and two-body amplitudes (for the double spurious state). The paper shows that any ERPA with restricted one-body amplitudes cannot give zero excitation energy to the translational spurious state, regardless of approximations made for the two-body amplitudes.

## Method

The authors used the small amplitude limit of the time-dependent density-matrix theory (STDDM), which includes all components of one-body and two-body amplitudes, to derive the equations of motion for the Fourier components of the one-body amplitude \(x_{\alpha\alpha'}\) and two-body amplitude \(X_{\alpha\beta\alpha'\beta'}\). They then evaluated the commutators \(\omega\langle\Phi_0|\vec{P}|\Phi_1\rangle\) and \(\omega\langle\Phi_0|\vec{P}\cdot\vec{P}|\Phi_2\rangle\) using these equations, the HF single-particle hamiltonian, and the closure relation, showing that the terms vanish only when the summation over single-particle states is unrestricted and all amplitude components are retained. The same approach was applied to demonstrate the Kohn theorem and continuity equations for transition densities and currents.

## Key numbers

- About 10% of eigenstates in exploratory numerical calculations for \(^{22,24}\)O using neutron \(2s\) and \(1d\) states with a pairing-type residual interaction have complex energies, with imaginary parts less than 0.1 MeV.
- The single Kohn mode eigenvalue is \(\omega = \pm\hbar\omega_0\) for a system confined to a harmonic potential \(U = \frac{1}{2}m\omega_0^2r^2\).
- The double Kohn mode eigenvalue is \(\omega = \pm 2\hbar\omega_0\) under the same harmonic potential.

## Context

This work extends the standard RPA treatment of spurious states (which guarantees zero excitation energy for the translational spurious state using only 1p-1h and 1h-1p amplitudes) to ERPA theories that include two-body amplitudes, such as Second RPA (SRPA) introduced by Sawicki (1970) and the approach for low-lying two-phonon states by Sakata (1990). The paper shows that SRPA, which keeps only 2p-2h and 2h-2p components of the two-body amplitudes, cannot give zero excitation energy to the double spurious state. The results enable the correct formulation of ERPA for studying double-phonon states of giant resonances, such as those investigated experimentally and theoretically by Chomaz (1990) and Bertulani (1990), and provide a method for constructing ERPA with hermiticity using the time-dependent density-matrix formalism with a correlated ground state.
