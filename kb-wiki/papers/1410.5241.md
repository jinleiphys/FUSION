# The Spinless Relativistic Yukawa Problem

> Noticing renewed or increasing interest in the possibility to describe semirelativistic bound states (of either spin-zero constituents or, upon confining oneself to spin-averaged features, constituents with nonzero spin) by means of the spinless Salpeter equation generalizing the Schrödinger equation towards incorporation of effects caused by relativistic kinematics, we revisit this problem for interactions between bound-state constituents of Yukawa shape, by recalling and applying several well-known tools enabling to constrain the resulting spectra.

## Key claim
The paper revisits the spinless Salpeter equation with a Yukawa potential to derive rigorous constraints on its eigensolutions, but the abstract glosses over the fact that the analysis is limited to establishing upper and lower bounds rather than exact solutions, and that the variational upper bounds are only as tight as the chosen trial space dimension (here d=25).

## Method
The authors apply the Rayleigh-Ritz variational method using a basis of generalized-Laguerre orthogonal polynomials in configuration space, with known analytic Fourier transforms to momentum space, to compute upper bounds on binding energies for the spinless Salpeter equation with Yukawa potential. They also derive lower bounds by comparing the Yukawa potential to the Coulomb potential, using known rigorous spectral bounds for the relativistic Coulomb problem from Herbst (1977) and Martin (1989). A constraint on the Yukawa coupling constant to avoid collapse is obtained by examining the large-mu limit of the simplest trial state expectation value.

## Key numbers
- Critical coupling for the relativistic Coulomb problem: kappa_c = 4/pi = 1.273239..., above which the Hamiltonian is not bounded from below (Herbst bound).
- Variational constraint on Yukawa coupling to avoid collapse: y <= 16/(3 pi) = 1.69765..., independent of the Yukawa range b.
- For m=5 fm^{-1}, y=1, b=0.01 fm^{-1}: lower bound on ground-state energy from Herbst bound gives E_0 >= 6.19 fm^{-1} (binding energy B_0 >= -3.81 fm^{-1}); improved Martin bound gives E_0 >= 7.07 fm^{-1} (B_0 >= -2.93 fm^{-1}).
- Variational upper bound on ground-state binding energy (n_r=0, l=0): overline{B} = -1.62087 fm^{-1} from the spinless Salpeter equation, compared to the Schrodinger upper bound overline{B}_{NR} = -1.24002 fm^{-1}.
- Bargmann bound on number of nonrelativistic Yukawa bound states: N_NR < 125250 for m=5 fm^{-1}, y=1, b=0.01 fm^{-1}.

## Context
This work extends the authors' prior program of applying envelope theory and variational methods to semirelativistic bound-state equations (Lucha 2000-2014), and provides rigorous benchmarks for approximate solutions to the spinless Salpeter equation with Yukawa potential. The paper directly tests the approximate "pseudo spinless Salpeter equation" approach of Hamzavi (2014) and Hassanabadi (2014), finding that their approximate solutions violate the derived bounds and exhibit unphysical behavior in the Coulomb limit b->0 and large-b limit. The results enable future workers to validate any proposed approximate solution against these rigorous upper and lower bounds.
