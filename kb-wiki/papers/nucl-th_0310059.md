# Two Proton Emission with Electromagnetic Probes

> A model to study two-proton emission from nuclei induced by electromagnetic probes is developed. The process is due to one-body electromagnetic operators, acting together with short-range correlations, and two-body $Δ$ currents. The model includes all the diagrams containing a single correlation function. The sensitivity of the cross section to the details of the correlation function is studied by using realistic and schematic correlations. Results for the $^{16}$O nucleus are presented.

## Key claim

The model shows that two-proton emission induced by electromagnetic probes can isolate short-range correlation (SRC) effects because two-body meson-exchange currents involving charged mesons do not contribute, but the quantitative extraction of SRC information requires a careful comparison between theoretical predictions and experimental data due to uncertainties in the theoretical input.

## Method

The authors develop a model based on Correlated Basis Function theory, where nuclear states are constructed by acting with a scalar correlation operator \(F\) on uncorrelated Slater determinants. They perform a cluster expansion truncated to include only diagrams containing a single correlation function \(h_{ij}=f^2_{ij}-1\), and calculate the (e,e'2p) cross section for \(^{16}\)O using one-body charge and magnetization current operators together with two-body \(\Delta\)-isobar currents. Hole wave functions are obtained from a Woods-Saxon potential, while particle wave functions use the optical potential of Schwandt et al. (1982).

## Key numbers

- Cross sections for \(^{16}\)O(e,e'2p) are calculated using three correlation functions: G (Gaussian), S3 (from FHNC with semi-realistic interaction), and V8 (scalar part of state-dependent correlation from FHNC with V8' Argonne plus three-body Urbana IX interaction); the V8 correlation has a deeper minimum than G and S3, and both V8 and S3 overshoot the asymptotic value of 1 between r=1 and 2 fm.
- The Gaussian correlation produces larger cross sections than the other correlations; lowering the minimum of a schematic box correlation does not produce a large effect, while inserting a part that overshoots the asymptotic value reduces the cross section, and reducing the size of the box also reduces the cross section.
- For the \(1^+\) state, the \(\Delta\)-isobar current dominates the cross section; for the \(0^+_1\) state, the terms associated with SRC (particularly 2-point diagrams) produce the larger contributions; for the \(2^+_1\) state, 3-point diagrams are also relevant.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous model for describing electromagnetic responses with short-range correlations (Mok00, Co01, Mok01, Ang02) to the case of two-nucleon knockout. Earlier studies found that SRC effects were small for inclusive (e,e') excitation of discrete states (Mok00), smaller than final state interactions in quasi-elastic inclusive scattering (Co01) and (e,e'p) reactions (Mok01), and dominated by meson exchange currents in (\(\gamma\),p) reactions (Ang02). The present work enables a consistent theoretical framework for analyzing elastic, inclusive, one-nucleon emission, and two-nucleon emission experiments together to extract information about short-range correlations.
