# Charged Multiplicities at SPS and RHIC and consequences for $J/ψ$ suppression

> Hadron multiplicities in nucleus--nucleus interactions are calculated in the Dual Parton Model and its dependence on the number of collisions and the number of participants is analyzed. Shadowing corrections are calculated as a function of impact parameter and the multiplicity per participant as a funtion of centrality is found to be in agreement with experiment at SPS and RHIC energies. The obtained results are used to compute the $J/ψ$ suppression in a comover approach.

## Key claim

The Dual Parton Model, including shadowing corrections, can reproduce charged multiplicities per participant at both SPS and RHIC energies, and the resulting multiplicities can be used in a comover approach to describe J/ψ suppression up to the knee of the ET distribution, but the model fails to reproduce the sharper decrease seen in the data beyond the knee.

## Method

The authors compute charged multiplicities in nucleus-nucleus collisions using the Dual Parton Model (DPM), where each inelastic collision produces two strings and the total number of strings is 2kn, with the multiplicity given by a linear combination of the number of participants nA and the number of binary collisions n. Shadowing corrections, based on Gribov theory and controlled by triple Pomeron diagrams, are applied and are negligible at SPS energies but reduce the multiplicity by 40-50% at RHIC. These multiplicities are then used in a comover approach to compute J/ψ suppression, where the survival probability is the product of a nuclear absorption factor (with absorptive cross-section σabs) and a comover interaction factor (with cross-section σco) that depends on the charged multiplicity in the dimuon trigger region.

## Key numbers

- At SPS energy √s = 17.3 GeV, the non-diffractive cross-section σND = 26 mb, with k = 1.4 and dNppND/dy = 1.56
- At RHIC energy √s = 130 GeV, the non-diffractive cross-section σND = 33 mb, with k = 2.0 and dNppND/dy = 2.72
- Shadowing corrections at RHIC energies reduce the multiplicity by 40 to 50%
- At √s = 130 GeV, the ratio of dN/dη to dN/dy at mid-rapidities can be as large as 1.3
- The parameters used for J/ψ suppression fits are: σabs = 4.5 mb, σco = 1 mb, q = 0.62, α = 0.076, and Nf = 1.15 fm-2
- The beam energy Ein = 158 GeV for the NA50 data

## Context

This work extends the Dual Parton Model calculations of Capella and collaborators to describe charged multiplicities at both SPS and RHIC energies, and applies them to J/ψ suppression in the comover approach previously developed by the same group (Capella et al., 1997; Capella et al., 2000). The results are compared to WA98 data at SPS and PHENIX data at RHIC for multiplicities, and to NA50 data for J/ψ suppression. The model successfully describes the suppression up to the knee of the ET distribution but fails beyond it, which the authors attribute to effects of the J/ψ trigger on the ET distribution in the tail region, as discussed in earlier work (Capella et al., 2000). The analysis enables further investigation of the anomalous J/ψ suppression observed in central Pb-Pb collisions.
