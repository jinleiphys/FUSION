# Traces of Theta^+ pentaquark in K^+ nucleus dynamics

> Long-standing anomalies in K^+ nucleus integral cross sections could be resolved by extending the impulse-approximation t*rho optical-potential framework to incorporate K^+ absorption on pairs of nucleons. Substantially improved fits to the data at p(lab)=500-700 MeV/c are obtained. An upper bound of 3.5 mb for the absorption cross section of K^+ per nucleon is derived. We conjecture that the underlying microscopic absorption process is K^+ n N --> Theta^+ N, where Theta^+(1540) is the newly discovered exotic Y=2, I=0, Z=1 pentaquark baryon, and estimate that the cross section for K^+ d --> Theta^+ p is a fraction of millibarn. Comments are made on Theta^+ production reactions on nuclei.

## Key claim

The long-standing anomalies in K+ nucleus integral cross sections can be resolved by adding a density-dependent term to the impulse-approximation t*rho optical potential, which the authors conjecture represents K+ absorption on two nucleons via the exotic Theta+(1540) pentaquark production channel K+ n N -> Theta+ N. The authors hedge that the absorption cross section per nucleon of 3.5 mb is an upper bound, and that the cross section for K+ d -> Theta+ p is estimated at a fraction of a millibarn.

## Method

The authors solved the Klein-Gordon equation with a K+ nucleus optical potential, starting from the standard t*rho impulse-approximation form and then adding either a rho^2(r) term or a bar-rho * rho(r) term to represent two-nucleon absorption. They fitted the resulting potentials to 32 data points for reaction and total cross sections on Li-6, C-12, Si-28, and Ca-40 at four laboratory momenta between 488 and 714 MeV/c, keeping the real part of the isospin-averaged KN scattering amplitude fixed at its free-space value.

## Key numbers

- Upper bound on K+ absorption cross section per nucleon: sigma_abs^(K+)/A ~ 3.5 mb, derived for dense targets (C, Si, Ca) at p_lab = 488 MeV/c.
- Estimated cross section for K+ d -> Theta+ p: 0.1 - 0.5 mb at p_lab = 488 MeV/c.
- Best-fit two-nucleon absorption coefficient ImB = 0.67(6) fm^4 using the bar-rho * rho form at p_lab = 488 MeV/c, with chi^2/N = 0.69.
- Best-fit imaginary part of the isospin-averaged KN amplitude Imb_0 = 0.126(4) fm at p_lab = 488 MeV/c using the bar-rho * rho form.
- Estimated one-step production cross section for K+ n -> Theta+ at the resonance energy (p_lab = 440 MeV/c): ~0.03 mb, assuming J^pi(Theta+) = (1/2)^+ and Gamma(Theta+ -> KN) ~ 1 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological analyses of Friedman et al. (1997) which established that K+ nucleus integral cross sections exceed predictions from the standard t*rho impulse-approximation optical potential. The authors propose that the missing reactivity arises from two-nucleon absorption into the Theta+(1540) pentaquark channel, rather than from nucleon swelling (Siegel et al., 1985) or reduced vector-meson masses (Brown et al., 1988) as previously suggested. The results enable future experimental searches for the K+ d -> Theta+ p reaction and for (K+,p) experiments on nuclear targets to study Theta+ production and possible bound states in hyponuclei.
