# QED corrections to the parity-violating asymmetry in high-energy electron-nucleus collisions

> The parity-violating asymmetry, including leading-order QED corrections to the Coulomb potential, is calculated non-perturbatively by solving the Dirac equation. At GeV collision energies and forward scattering angles, QED effects enhance the asymmetry by approximately 5% for the recently measured nuclei 27Al, 48Ca, and 208Pb. The corrections result in a shift of the estimated neutron radius, leading to an increase in the inferred neutron skin thicknesses of these nuclei and, thus, to the pressure neutrons feel around nuclear saturation density.

## Key claim

The leading-order QED corrections (vacuum polarization and vertex plus self-energy) to the Coulomb potential enhance the parity-violating asymmetry by approximately 5% for 27Al, 48Ca, and 208Pb at GeV collision energies and forward scattering angles, but this correction improves agreement with experiment for 27Al and 48Ca while worsening it for 208Pb, suggesting current nuclear structure theory may have difficulties accommodating a large neutron skin in 208Pb.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation non-perturbatively using the Fortran code RADIAL, incorporating the Coulomb target potential, the Uehling potential for vacuum polarization, and a potential for the vertex plus self-energy correction derived from the first-order Born transition amplitude. The parity-violating asymmetry was calculated from the differential cross sections for positive and negative helicity electrons via phase-shift analysis, using experimental ground-state charge distributions and weak densities obtained from a range of nuclear Energy Density Functionals.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb at 953 MeV and 4.7 degrees scattering angle, the QED-induced shift Delta A_pv is 0.022(1) ppm, with A_pv ranging from 0.556 to 0.591 ppm across models
- For 208Pb at 1060 MeV and 5 degrees, Delta A_pv is 0.029(1) ppm, with A_pv ranging from 0.671 to 0.729 ppm
- For 48Ca at 2180 MeV and 4.51 degrees, Delta A_pv is 0.120(10) ppm, with A_pv ranging from 2.06 to 2.40 ppm
- For 27Al at 1157 MeV and 7.61 degrees, Delta A_pv is 0.103(2) ppm, with A_pv ranging from 1.95 to 2.05 ppm
- The QED correction dA_pv(QED) is about 5% for all three nuclei, increasing to 7% for 48Ca at 9 degrees
- The inferred neutron skin thickness for 48Ca shifts from 0.121(35) fm to 0.161(35) fm after QED corrections
- The inferred neutron skin thickness for 208Pb shifts by 0.09 fm to 0.38(7) fm after QED corrections
- For 27Al, the neutron skin thickness shifts from -0.04(12) fm to 0.02(12) fm

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of parity-violating asymmetry measurements in 27Al (Aniol 2022), 48Ca (Adhikari 2022), and 208Pb (Abrahamyan 2012, Adhikari 2021) by including QED corrections to the Coulomb potential that had not been applied before. The gamma-Z box contributions had been previously accounted for via modifications to the weak charge (Gorchtein 2011, Erler 2013, Gorchtein and Horowitz 2009), but the electromagnetic sector corrections were missing. The results enable more accurate extraction of neutron skin thicknesses from parity-violating electron scattering experiments, with implications for the pressure of neutron matter near saturation density and neutron star physics, and upcoming experiments like MREX at Mainz will provide crucial tests of these corrections.
