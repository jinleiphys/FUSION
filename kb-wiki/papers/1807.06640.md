# Nuclear and neutron matter equations of state from high-quality potentials up to fifth order of the chiral expansion

> We present predictions for the equation of state of symmetric nuclear and pure neutron matter based on recent high-quality nucleon-nucleon potentials from leading order to fifth order in the chiral expansion. We include as well the next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) chiral three-nucleon force whose low-energy constants cD and cE are fitted to the binding energies of 3H and 3He as well as the \b{eta}-decay lifetime of 3H. The ground state energy per particle is computed in the particle- particle ladder approximation up to a few times saturation density. Due to the soft character of the interactions, uncertainties due to the convergence in many-body perturbation theory are small. We find that nuclear matter saturation is reproduced quantitatively at N3LO and N4LO, and therefore we encourage the application of these interactions in finite nuclei, where the description of ground- state energies and charge radii of medium-mass nuclei may be improved.

## Key claim

The paper claims that nuclear matter saturation is reproduced quantitatively at N3LO and N4LO using high-quality chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials combined with three-nucleon forces, but this result is conditional on the specific choice of momentum-space cutoff (450 or 500 MeV) and the inclusion of the two-pion-exchange three-body force contributions at higher orders, with the convergence pattern becoming less satisfactory at densities above approximately 0.20 fm^-3.

## Method

The authors computed the ground state energy per particle of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter using the particle-particle ladder approximation (nonperturbative summation of ladder diagrams) with chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials from leading order (LO) to fifth order (N4LO) from Ref. EMN (2018). They included the N2LO chiral three-nucleon force with low-energy constants c_D and c_E fitted to the binding energies of 3H and 3He and the beta-decay lifetime of 3H, and also incorporated the two-pion-exchange three-nucleon force contributions at N3LO and N4LO through effective low-energy constants. The calculations were performed for momentum-space cutoffs of 450 and 500 MeV, and the perturbative behavior was assessed by comparing results from third-order many-body perturbation theory, the [2|1] Pade approximant, and the nonperturbative ladder summation.

## Key numbers

- Saturation energy in symmetric nuclear matter varies in the range -14 MeV to -18 MeV per particle across chiral orders N2LO to N4LO with cutoff 450 MeV
- Saturation density in symmetric nuclear matter varies between 0.155 fm^-3 and 0.195 fm^-3 across the same orders
- The N4LO nucleon-nucleon potential reproduces NN data below pion production threshold with chi^2/datum = 1.15
- The low-energy constant c_3 changes by Delta c_3 = 0.89 GeV^-1 at N3LO and Delta c_3 = 1.94 GeV^-1 at N4LO when including higher-order two-pion-exchange three-body forces
- For the N4LO potential with cutoff 450 MeV, the fitted c_D value is 1.245 (plus or minus 0.225) and c_E is 0.28 (plus or minus 0.05) when using only the N2LO three-nucleon force parametrization

## Context

This work extends the high-quality chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials from Ref. EMN (2018) by adding the consistent N2LO three-nucleon force, building on earlier studies of nuclear matter saturation using chiral effective field theory. The results are relevant to ongoing efforts to improve ab initio calculations of medium-mass nuclei, where previous studies by Simonis et al. (2017) and Morris et al. (2017) showed that soft interactions with refitted three-nucleon forces could reproduce ground-state energies but produced charge radii that were too small. The paper suggests that these new potentials may produce similarly favorable results for finite nuclei without requiring similarity renormalization group evolution, and encourages their application to studies of medium-mass and heavy nuclei as well as neutron star structure.
