# Extracting the low-energy constant $L_0^r$ at three flavors from pion-kaon scattering

> Based on our analysis of the contributions from the connected and disconnected contraction diagrams to the pion-kaon scattering amplitude, we provide the first determination of the only free low-energy constant at $\mathcal{O}(p^4)$, known as $L_0^r$, in SU$(4|1)$ Partially-Quenched Chiral Perturbation Theory using the data from the Extended Twisted Mass Collaboration, $L_0^r(μ=M_ρ) = 0.77(20)(25)(7)(7)(2)\cdot 10^{-3}$. The theory uncertainties originate from the unphysical scattering length, the physical low-energy constants, the higher-oder chiral corrections, the (lattice) meson masses and the pion decay constant, respectively

## Key claim

The paper provides the first determination of the low-energy constant \(L_0^r\) in SU(4|1) Partially-Quenched Chiral Perturbation Theory (PQChPT) at \(\mathcal{O}(p^4)\), using lattice data from the Extended Twisted Mass (ETM) Collaboration for pion-kaon scattering; the result is \(L_0^r(\mu=M_\rho) = 0.77(33)\cdot 10^{-3}\), where the uncertainty aggregates five distinct sources including the unphysical scattering length, physical low-energy constants, higher-order chiral corrections, lattice meson masses, and the pion decay constant.

## Method

The authors analyzed the connected contraction diagrams \(T_a\) and \(T_b\) in \(K\pi\) scattering with total isospin \(I=3/2\), defining an unphysical single-channel amplitude \(T_\beta = T_a - T_b\) whose S-wave scattering length \(a_0^\beta\) depends on \(L_0^r\) at \(\mathcal{O}(p^4)\). They obtained \(a_0^\beta\) from the ETM collaboration's lattice ensembles A30.32 and A40.24 via the standard Luscher analysis of discrete energy levels extracted from the unphysical correlation function \(C_\beta(\tau)=C_a(\tau)-C_b(\tau)\). Using the known values of the physical low-energy constants \(L_{1-8}^r\) at \(\mu=M_\rho=770\) MeV from Bijnens (2014), they solved the chiral perturbation theory expression for \(a_0^\beta\) to extract \(L_0^r\).

## Key numbers

* \(L_0^r(\mu=M_\rho) = 0.77(33)\cdot 10^{-3}\) from ensemble A30.32 with method E2 (dividing out thermal pollution), the quoted final result.
* Individual determinations: A30.32 method E1 gave \(10^3 L_0^r = 0.78(21)(25)(7)(7)(2)\); A30.32 method E2 gave \(0.77(20)(25)(7)(7)(2)\); A40.24 method E1 gave \(0.86(25)(25)(7)(4)(2)\); A40.24 method E2 gave \(0.87(22)(25)(7)(4)(2)\), with uncertainties from \(a_0^\beta\), physical LECs, higher-order ChPT corrections, meson masses, and \(F_\pi\) respectively.
* Unphysical scattering lengths: \(\mu_{\pi K} a_0^\beta = -0.0956(91)\) (E1) and \(-0.0961(84)\) (E2) for A30.32; \(-0.1152(144)\) (E1) and \(-0.1142(131)\) (E2) for A40.24.
* Lattice spacing \(a = 0.0885(36)\) fm for both ensembles; pion masses \(aM_\pi = 0.1239(3)\) (A30.32) and \(0.1452(5)\) (A40.24); kaon masses \(aM_K = 0.236(7)\) (A30.32) and \(0.241(7)\) (A40.24).

## Context

This work extends the method of extracting \(L_0^r\) from contraction diagrams, previously applied to pion-pion scattering in SU(4|2) PQChPT by Acharya (2017, 2019), to the three-flavor case using SU(4|1) PQChPT. The result is more precise than the NNLO determination in SU(4|2) by Boyle (2015) (\(L_0^r(\mu=1\ \mathrm{GeV}) = 1.0(1.1)\cdot 10^{-3}\)) and the NLO determination in SU(4|2) by Acharya (2019) (\(L_0^r(\mu=1\ \mathrm{GeV}) = 5.7(1.9)\cdot 10^{-3}\)), and it completes the SU(4|1) PQChPT Lagrangian at \(\mathcal{O}(p^4)\). This enables future lattice studies of hadronic processes involving strange quarks, such as \(K\pi\to K\pi\), \(\pi\pi\to K\bar{K}\), and \(K\eta\to K\eta\) scatterings, particularly in channels with disconnected diagrams; the authors note that matching to the two-flavor case would require a larger graded algebra such as SU(5|2) in follow-up work.
