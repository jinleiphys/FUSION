# Impact of magnetic field on shear viscosity of quark matter in Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

> We have investigated shear viscosity of quark matter in presence of a strong uniform magnetic field background where Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model has been considered to describe the magneto-thermodynamical properties of the medium. In presence of magnetic field, shear viscosity coefficient gets split into different components because of anisotropy in tangential stress of the fluid. Four different components can be merged to two components in limit of strong field, where collisional width of quark becomes much lower than its synchrotron frequency. A simplified contact diagram of quark-quark interaction can estimate a small collisional width, where strong field limit expressions are exactly applicable. Although, for RHIC or LHC matter, one can expect a large thermal width, for which generalized four components viscosities are necessary. We have explored these all different possible cases in the thermodynamical framework of Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity of quark matter in a strong magnetic field splits into multiple components due to anisotropy, and in the strong field limit where the collisional width is much smaller than the synchrotron frequency, four components merge into two; however, for matter produced at RHIC or LHC, a large thermal width is expected, making the generalized four-component description necessary.

## Method

The authors used the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a determinant interaction to describe the magneto-thermodynamical properties of quark matter in a constant uniform magnetic field along the z-axis. They derived the shear viscosity coefficients using the relaxation time approximation (RTA) of the relativistic Boltzmann equation, first in the strong field limit where the collisional relaxation time is much larger than the magnetic-field-induced relaxation time, and then for the general case with four independent viscosity components. The collisional width was estimated from a simplified contact diagram of quark-quark scattering, and the constituent quark masses and entropy density were obtained from the NJL model gap equation and thermodynamic potential.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass in vacuum: 400 MeV, with current quark mass m = 6 MeV, three-momentum cutoff Λ = 590 MeV, and dimensionless coupling G₀Λ² = 2.435, yielding pion mass 140.2 MeV, pion decay constant 92.6 MeV, and quark condensate ⟨ūu⟩ = ⟨d̄d⟩ = (-241.5 MeV)³.
- At eB = 10 mπ², the magnetic-field-induced relaxation time τ_B remains within the range 0.8-3 fm, allowing the strong field limit condition τ_c >> τ_B to be satisfied.
- The collisional relaxation time τ_c from the contact diagram at eB = 10 mπ² is large enough that the strong field limit expressions for shear viscosity are applicable, with η₂/s becoming an increasing function of temperature (η₂ ∝ 1/τ_c) while η₄/s (Hall-type) is independent of τ_c and increases with temperature due to phase space.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of shear viscosity in magnetized quark matter by Li et al., Nam et al., Sedarkian et al., and Tawfik et al., which did not explore the component decomposition of shear viscosity, and follows the explicit component analysis of Tuchin (2011) and Landau (1987). The strong field limit expressions, where four components merge to two, are consistent with results from gauge-gravity duality by Finazzo et al. and Jain. The paper enables future magnetohydrodynamic simulations (Roy 2015, Pu 2016) and transport simulations (Das 2016) that require temperature- and magnetic-field-dependent transport coefficients, while noting that the contact diagram estimate yields a large τ_c not expected for RHIC or LHC matter, where meson exchange diagrams (as in work by Ghosh et al.) would provide smaller relaxation times.
