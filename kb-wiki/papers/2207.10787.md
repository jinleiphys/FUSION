# Spatial densities of momentum and forces in spin-one hadrons

> Densities associated with the energy-momentum tensor are calculated for spin-one targets. These calculations are done in a light front formalism, which accounts for relativistic effects due to boosts and allows for arbitrary spatial localization of the target. These densities include the distribution of momentum, angular momentum, and pressures over a two-dimensional plane transverse to the light front. Results are obtained for both longitudinally and transversely polarized targets, and the formalism is tailored to allow the possibility of massless targets. The momentum density and pressure distributions are calculated for a deuteron target in a light cone convolution model, with which the properties of this model (such as helicity dependence of the densities) is illustrated.

## Key claim

The paper derives light front densities (momentum, angular momentum, pressure) for spin-one hadrons from the energy-momentum tensor, showing that these densities depend on the target's helicity or transverse polarization state, and that the formalism works for both massive and massless systems. The abstract glosses over the fact that the decomposition into helicity amplitudes is not defined when the light-front momentum \(P^+ = 0\), which can occur for massless particles traveling in the \(-z\) direction.

## Method

The authors decompose the matrix elements of the symmetric energy-momentum tensor for spin-one targets into six helicity amplitudes (effective form factors) in a light front formalism with \(\Delta^+ = 0\). They relate these helicity amplitudes to the traditional gravitational form factors \(\mathcal{G}_1\) through \(\mathcal{G}_6\) for massive systems, and derive expressions for longitudinally polarized, transversely polarized, and generally polarized states using a spin-1 density matrix. The densities are then obtained via Fourier transforms of the helicity amplitudes to transverse position space, and are illustrated with a light cone convolution model of the deuteron.

## Key numbers

- The \(P^+\) density sum rule requires \(\mathcal{A}_{\lambda\lambda}(0) = 1\) for each helicity \(\lambda\), which is compatible with \(\mathcal{G}_1(0) = 1\) from momentum conservation.
- Angular momentum conservation enforces \(\mathcal{G}_5(0) = 2\).
- The light front \(P^+\) quadrupole moment for transversely polarized states is \(\mathcal{Q}_{\mathrm{LF}}(\mathbf{s}_\perp,\pm1) = \frac{P^+}{2M^2} \mathcal{G}_2(0)\) and \(\mathcal{Q}_{\mathrm{LF}}(\mathbf{s}_\perp,0) = -\frac{P^+}{M^2} \mathcal{G}_2(0)\), with \(\mathcal{G}_2(0)\) unconstrained by conservation laws.
- The \(P^+\) radius for helicity \(\pm1\) states is \(\langle b_\perp^2 \rangle_{P^+}(\lambda=\pm1) = 4 \frac{\mathrm{d}\mathcal{G}_1(t)}{\mathrm{d}t}\big|_{t=0} - \frac{\mathcal{G}_2(0)}{M^2}\).
- The \(P^+\) radius for helicity 0 states is \(\langle b_\perp^2 \rangle_{P^+}(\lambda=0) = 4 \frac{\mathrm{d}\mathcal{G}_1(t)}{\mathrm{d}t}\big|_{t=0} + \frac{1}{M^2} \big( 2 \mathcal{G}_1(0) - 2 \mathcal{G}_5(0) - \mathcal{G}_6(0) \big)\).
- In a model calculation for the rho meson (Freese 2019), \(\mathcal{G}^{\mathrm{NJL}}_2(0) \approx 0.158 > 0\) and \(\mathcal{G}^{\mathrm{NJL}}_6(0) \approx -1\), giving a positive light front \(P^+\) quadrupole moment but a negative Breit frame mass quadrupole moment.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of energy-momentum tensor densities for spin-zero and spin-half hadrons (Lorce 2018, Freese 2021) to spin-one targets, addressing the lack of light front density investigations for spin-one systems. It contrasts with Breit frame studies of spin-one gravitational form factors (Cosyn 2019, Polyakov 2019), which have been subject to controversy regarding physical interpretation. The formalism enables future studies of partonic contributions to spin-one densities and can be applied to the deuteron as a testing ground for how the inter-nucleon force arises from quantum chromodynamics.
