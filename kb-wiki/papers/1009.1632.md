# Critical Phenomena in DIS

> Saturation in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) and deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) is associated with a phase transition between the partonic gas, typical of moderate $x$ and $Q^2$, and partonic fluid appearing at increasing $Q^2$ and decreasing Bjorken $x$. In the statistical interpretation of DIS, the large-$x,(1-x)^n$ factor in the SF is associated with a statistical distribution (perfect gas), while the low-$x$, Regge behaved factor $x^{b(Q^2)}$ produces deviations from the perfect gas and ultimately leads to a gas-liquid phase transition. In this paper we do not intend to propose another parametrization of the structure function; instead we suggest a new insight into the internal structure of the nucleon, as seen in DIS, and its connection with that revealed in high-energy nucleons and heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that saturation in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) and deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) corresponds to a gas-liquid phase transition of partonic matter inside the nucleon, rather than presenting a new parametrization of structure functions. The authors explicitly state they do not intend to propose another parametrization, but instead suggest a new insight into the internal structure of the nucleon.

## Method

The authors map the kinematic regions of DIS structure functions (SF) onto the van der Waals equation of state (EoS), identifying the Bjorken scaling region with a dilute perfect partonic gas and the saturation region with a partonic fluid. They use the singlet (gluon) component of the SF, dominated at low Bjorken x, and compare its behavior to the van der Waals EoS for corresponding states, including the spinodal (mixed-phase) region. The mapping is performed by identifying the particle number density with the SF F2(x,Q2) and relating the van der Waals parameters a (long-range attraction) and b (short-range repulsion) to properties of the partonic system, such as constituent size ~1/Q.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature in the van der Waals EoS is given by Tc = 8a/(27b), with example values a = 5 GeV^{-2} and b = 0.2 GeV^{-3} used for illustrative calculations.
- The saturation momentum is given phenomenologically as Q2_s(x) ~ x^{-0.3}, from Golec (no year given).
- The geometrical scaling radius is R0^2(x) = (x/x0)^λ / Q2_0, with Q2_0 = 1 GeV^2, λ = 0.29, and x0 = 3×10^{-4}, from Golec (no year given).
- The effective Pomeron intercept Δ0 ≈ 0.1, with b ≈ 0.4 in the model of Eq. (1) for the singlet structure function.
- The change of regime in the SF behavior is visible around x ~ 10^{-2}, as shown in Fig. 1 from Yoshida (no year given).

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamic approach to DIS initiated by Fermi (1950) and subsequently applied by Bhalerao (no year given), Cleymans (no year given), Soffer (no year given), and others. It builds on the idea from Jenkovszky, Turoci, and Troshin (no year given) that saturation corresponds to condensation of partonic gas to a fluid, and contrasts with percolation-type models by Pajares (no year given) and Satz (no year given) that include (de)confinement transitions. The paper enables future experimental verification through observed particle spectra in DIS or DVCS, particularly the transverse momentum distributions below and beyond the saturation border line, and suggests that the nature of strongly interacting matter under extreme conditions should be universal across hadron-hadron, heavy-ion, and lepton-hadron collisions.
