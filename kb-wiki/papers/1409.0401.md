# The low lying scalar resonances in the $D^0$ decays into $K^0_s$ and $f_0(500)$, $f_0(980)$, $a_0(980)$

> The $D^0$ decay into $K^0_s$ and a scalar resonance, $f_0(500)$, $f_0(980)$, $a_0(980)$, is studied obtaining the scalar resonances from final state interaction of a pair of mesons produced in a first step in the $D^0$ decay into $K^0_s$ and the pair of pseudoscalar mesons. This weak decay is very appropriate for this kind of study because it allows to produce the three resonances in the same decay in a process that is Cabibbo allowed, hence the rates obtained are large compared to those of $\bar{B}^0$ decays into $J/ψ$ and a scalar meson that have at least one Cabibbo suppressed vertex. Concretely the $a_0(980)$ production is Cabibbo allowed here, while it cannot be seen in the $\bar{B}^0_s$ decay into $J/ψa_0(980)$ and is doubly Cabibbo suppressed in the $\bar{B}^0$ decay into $J/ψa_0(980)$ and has not been identified there. The fact that the three resonances can be seen in the same reaction, because there is no isospin conservation in the weak decays, offers a unique opportunity to test the ideas of the chiral unitary approach where these resonances are produced from the interaction of pairs of pseudoscalar mesons.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the relative production rates of the scalar resonances \(f_0(500)\), \(f_0(980)\), and \(a_0(980)\) in \(D^0 \to K^0_s\) decays using the chiral unitary approach with no free parameters, finding that the ratio of \(a_0(980)\) to \(f_0(980)\) production is large and agrees with experiment, but this result depends on neglecting the \(W\)-exchange mechanism and assuming the dominance of internal emission diagrams.

## Method

The authors start from the weak decay \(D^0 \to \bar{K}^0 u\bar{u}\) at the quark level, then hadronize the \(u\bar{u}\) pair by adding a \(\bar{q}q\) pair with vacuum quantum numbers, producing a pair of pseudoscalar mesons (\(\pi^+\pi^-\), \(\pi^0\pi^0\), \(\eta\eta\), \(K^+K^-\), etc.). The final state interaction of these meson pairs is then treated using the chiral unitary approach (Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels) to generate the scalar resonances \(f_0(500)\), \(f_0(980)\), and \(a_0(980)\) from the meson-meson scattering amplitudes. The invariant mass distributions for \(\pi^+\pi^-\) and \(\pi^0\eta\) are computed, and the ratio of branching fractions for \(a_0(980)\) to \(f_0(980)\) production is extracted by subtracting a smooth background.

## Key numbers

* Ratio of branching fractions \(\Gamma(D^0 \to \bar{K}^0 a_0(980), a_0(980) \to \pi^0 \eta) / \Gamma(D^0 \to \bar{K}^0 f_0(980), f_0(980) \to \pi^+\pi^-) = 6.7 \pm 1.3\) (theoretical prediction, with 20% error from background subtraction).
* Experimental ratio from PDG and CLEO data: \(R = 5.33^{+2.4}_{-1.9}\).
* Experimental branching fraction for \(D^0 \to \bar{K}^0 a_0(980), a_0(980) \to \pi^0 \eta\): \((6.5 \pm 2.0) \times 10^{-3}\).
* Experimental branching fraction for \(D^0 \to \bar{K}^0 f_0(980), f_0(980) \to \pi^+\pi^-\): \((1.22^{+0.40}_{-0.24}) \times 10^{-3}\).
* The apparent width of the \(a_0(980)\) resonance after background subtraction is 80 MeV, within the PDG range of 50-100 MeV.
* The cutoff used to regularize the meson-meson loop function is \(q_{\rm max} = 600\) MeV, following the work of Wei-Hong (2014) on \(\bar{B}^0\) decays.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to \(\bar{B}^0\) and \(\bar{B}^0_s\) decays into \(J/\psi\) plus scalar mesons (Wei-Hong, 2014) to the \(D^0\) decay system, where all three low-lying scalar resonances can be produced in a single Cabibbo-allowed decay. The \(a_0(980)\) production is Cabibbo allowed here, unlike in \(\bar{B}^0_s \to J/\psi a_0(980)\) (forbidden) or \(\bar{B}^0 \to J/\psi a_0(980)\) (doubly Cabibbo suppressed), enabling a simultaneous test of the dynamically generated nature of these resonances. The results support the molecular interpretation of the scalar mesons and enable future partial wave analyses of \(D^0 \to K^0_s \pi^+\pi^-\) data, similar to the LHCb analysis of \(\bar{B}^0 \to J/\psi \pi^+\pi^-\) (Aaij, 2014).
