# Semiclassical origin of anomalous shell effect for tetrahedral deformation in radial power-law potential model

> Shell structures in single-particle energy spectra are investigated against regular tetrahedral type deformation using radial power-law potential model. Employing a natural way of shape parametrization which interpolates sphere and regular tetrahedron, we find prominent shell effects at rather large tetrahedral deformations, which bring about shell energies much larger than the cases of spherical and quadrupole type shapes. We discuss the semiclassical origin of these anomalous shell structures using periodic orbit theory.

## Key claim

The authors find that for potentials with sharper surfaces (radial power-law exponent α ≳ 4), tetrahedral deformation produces shell effects much stronger than those for spherical or quadrupole shapes, with the enhancement occurring at rather large tetrahedral deformations (β_td ≈ 0.7) rather than at small deformations where spherical shell effects dominate.

## Method

The authors use a radial power-law potential model V ∝ r^α with a shape parametrization that smoothly interpolates between a sphere (β_td = 0) and a regular tetrahedron (β_td = 1) via a single deformation parameter, ensuring convex surfaces at all deformations. Quantum single-particle spectra are obtained by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in a harmonic oscillator basis decomposed into irreducible representations of the tetrahedral group T_d. Classical periodic orbits are computed using the monodromy method, and semiclassical shell correction energies are evaluated via the Gutzwiller trace formula, with Fourier analysis of the quantum level density used to identify periodic-orbit contributions.

## Key numbers

- For α = 6.0, strong level bunchings and large energy gaps appear around β_td ≈ 0.7, with deformed magic numbers N = 2, 8, 20, 40, … (same as spherical harmonic oscillator).
- At β_td = 0.7, the equivalent Y_32 expansion parameter is t_3 = 0.27, close to the value where tetrahedral shell effects are enhanced in prior work by Dudek et al.
- The orbit PB undergoes bifurcation at β_td = 0.283, producing orbit TC; TC undergoes touch-and-go bifurcation with orbit TA at β_td = 0.369 and submerges into orbit TB at β_td = 0.600.
- Orbit DA bifurcates at β_td = 0.562, producing orbit TD, which submerges into orbit TB at β_td = 0.607.
- Fourier peaks of the quantum level density show maximum amplitude around β_td ≈ 0.7, where periods of many periodic orbits converge to approximately the same value (τ ≈ 6.1).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of tetrahedral shell effects by Hamamoto et al. (using the modified oscillator model) and Dudek et al. (using realistic nuclear mean-field models with spin-orbit coupling), as well as Reimann et al.'s analysis of electronic shell structures in sodium clusters. The authors use periodic orbit theory to explain the anomalous shell enhancement as arising from multiple bridge orbit bifurcations and near-degeneracy of periodic orbit periods, analogous to the restoration of dynamical symmetry seen in rational harmonic oscillator systems. The results enable future studies incorporating spin-orbit coupling to test whether the strong shell effect survives in realistic nuclear systems.
