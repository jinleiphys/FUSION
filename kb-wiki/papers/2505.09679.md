# Neutrino-less double beta decay in the $ν$ Standard Model

> We perform a comprehensive study of the $3+3$ Type-I seesaw model for a broad range of right-handed mass scales (from keV to 10 TeV). We take into account and, in some cases, update the constraints from a large number of high- and low-energy experiments and study the implications on neutrino-less double beta ($0νββ$) decay experiments. We illustrate our findings through profile likelihood plots for the half-life $T_{1/2}^{0ν}$ and two-dimensional plots correlating $T_{1/2}^{0ν}$ to neutrino masses. We find that in this simple class of models for Majorana neutrino masses, current and next-generation $0νββ$ decay experiments have a broad discovery potential in both the normal and inverted orderings of the spectrum of light active neutrinos.

## Key claim

In the 3+3 Type-I seesaw model, current and next-generation neutrinoless double beta decay experiments have a broad discovery potential for both normal and inverted neutrino mass orderings, but the actual reach depends on whether Big Bang Nucleosynthesis constraints are imposed and on the sterile neutrino mass scale, with the 95% C.L. regions for the effective Majorana mass extending well beyond those of the light-neutrino-exchange mechanism.

## Method

The authors performed a comprehensive parameter scan of the 18-dimensional parameter space of the 3+3 Type-I seesaw model using the GAMBIT software package with the Diver differential evolution sampler, covering right-handed neutrino masses from 1 keV to 10 TeV. They incorporated constraints from neutrino oscillation data, electroweak precision observables, beta decay limits on active-sterile mixing, CKM matrix elements, DESI 2024 cosmological bounds on the sum of neutrino masses, LHCb results on lepton flavor universality, and CMS measurements of W boson branching fractions, with and without Big Bang Nucleosynthesis constraints. The neutrinoless double beta decay half-life was computed using an effective field theory approach that accounts for contributions from both active and sterile neutrinos across different mass regimes.

## Key numbers

- Cosmological upper bound on sum of active neutrino masses: 0.113 eV (NH) and 0.145 eV (IH) at 95% C.L. from DESI 2024
- Phase-space factors for 0νββ decay: G_01 = 1.5 × 10^{-14} yr^{-1} for 136Xe and 0.22 × 10^{-14} yr^{-1} for 76Ge
- Axial-vector coupling: g_A = 1.2754 ± 0.0013
- Active neutrino nuclear matrix elements: M_nu^(3) = 2.7 (136Xe) and 3.4 (76Ge)
- Lightest neutrino mass lower limit from EWPO preference: approximately 2 meV (NH) and 20 meV (IH) when BBN constraints are not imposed
- Sterile neutrino mass scan range: M_1 from 10^{-6} GeV to 10^4 GeV (1 keV to 10 TeV)
- Current experimental half-life limits: approximately 10^26 yr; future ton-scale experiments: approximately 10^28 yr
- Momentum scale for sterile-active transition: k_F approximately 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends the GAMBIT implementation of the νSM from Gambit (2021) by updating constraints with new experimental results (DESI 2024 cosmology, LHCb 2022 lepton flavor universality, CMS 2022 W boson branching fractions) and extending the sterile neutrino mass range down to 1 keV. It provides the first comprehensive profile likelihood analysis of 0νββ decay in the full 3+3 Type-I seesaw model, going beyond previous studies that considered only N=2 sterile neutrinos (de Vries 2024) or more restricted parameter scans (Abada 2018). The results enable quantitative projections for next-generation 0νββ experiments such as LEGEND, nEXO, and KamLAND2-Zen, and can be combined with future constraints from DUNE and SHiP.
