# Neutron density distributions for atomic parity nonconservation experiments

> The neutron distributions of Cs, Ba, Yb and Pb isotopes are described in the framework of relativistic mean-field theory. The self-consistent ground state proton and neutron density distributions are calculated with the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model. The binding energies, the proton and neutron radii, and the quadrupole deformations are compared with available experimental data, as well as with recent theoretical studies of the nuclear structure corrections to the weak charge in atomic parity nonconservation experiments.

# Neutron density distributions for atomic parity nonconservation experiments

## Key claim

The relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model with the NL3+D1S effective interaction predicts neutron-to-proton radius ratios for Cs, Ba, Yb, and Pb isotopes that are considerably larger than those obtained in non-relativistic Hartree-Fock Skyrme calculations, implying that nuclear structure corrections to the weak charge in atomic parity nonconservation experiments could be more than a factor of 2 larger than previous estimates. However, the changes in neutron distribution parameters along isotope chains should not limit the accuracy of Standard model tests using isotope ratios of PNC observables.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model with the NL3 effective interaction for the mean-field Lagrangian and the D1S Gogny force for the pairing channel to calculate self-consistent ground-state proton and neutron density distributions for isotopes of Cs, Ba, Yb, and Pb. The RHB equations were solved by expanding nucleon spinors and meson fields in eigenfunctions of a deformed axially symmetric oscillator potential, using 12 oscillator shells for fermion fields and 20 shells for boson fields, with a simple blocking procedure for odd-proton and odd-neutron systems.

## Key numbers

- For $^{133}$Cs, the RHB NL3 model calculates a neutron-to-proton radius ratio $r_n/r_p = 1.046$, compared to Hartree-Fock values of 1.022 for SkM$^*$ and 1.016 for SkIII
- The difference between neutron and proton rms radii for Yb isotopes increases by less than 0.05 fm between N=100 and N=106
- For $^{174}$Yb, the neutron quadrupole moment is $Q_n = 11.02$ b and the neutron hexadecupole moment is $H_n = 0.067$ b$^2$
- The calculated isovector dipole resonance peak energy in $^{208}$Pb is $E_p = 12.9$ MeV, compared to the experimental value of $13.3 \pm 0.1$ MeV
- The calculated isovector monopole resonance centroid in $^{208}$Pb is in agreement with the experimental value of $26 \pm 3$ MeV
- The differences between neutron and proton rms radii for the studied isotopes are of the order of 0.2 to 0.3 fm

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of nuclear structure corrections to atomic PNC experiments by Pollock, Fortson, and Wilets (1992) for Pb isotopes and by Chen and Vogel (1993) for Cs isotopes, who used non-relativistic Hartree-Fock Skyrme models. The authors note that relativistic mean-field models typically generate significantly larger neutron radii than non-relativistic models, as pointed out by Pollock and Welliver (1999) and Pollock, Fortson, and Wilets (1992). The results enable improved estimates of the nuclear structure uncertainty in atomic PNC experiments for the isotope chains of Cs, Ba, Yb, and Pb, and suggest that measurements of isotope ratios of PNC observables could provide tests of the Standard model without being limited by uncertainties in neutron distribution changes along the chains.
