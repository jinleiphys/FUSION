# A coupled-channel analysis of $KΛ$ production in the nucleon resonance region

> A unitary coupled-channel effective Lagrangian model is applied to the combined analysis of the $(π,γ) N \to KΛ$ reactions in the energy region up to 2 GeV. To constrain the resonance couplings to the $KΛ$ final state the recent photoproduction data obtained by the SAPHIR, SPring-8, and CLAS groups are included into the calculations. The main resonance contributions to the process stem from the $S_{11}(1650)$, $P_{13}(1720)$, and $P_{13}(1900)$ states. The second bump at 1.9 GeV seen in the photoproduction cross section data is described as a coherent sum of the resonance and background contributions. The prediction for the beam polarization observable is presented.

# A coupled-channel analysis of $K\Lambda$ production in the nucleon resonance region

## Key claim

The second bump at 1.9 GeV seen in the $\gamma N \to K\Lambda$ photoproduction cross section can be described as a coherent sum of resonance and background contributions without requiring any "missing" resonance, provided that the unitary coupled-channel framework is used. The extracted resonance parameters are fairly insensitive to the discrepancy between the SAPHIR and CLAS data sets because that discrepancy stems mainly from different non-resonant background contributions.

## Method

A unitary coupled-channel effective Lagrangian model (the Giessen model) was applied to simultaneously describe $\pi N \to K\Lambda$ and $\gamma N \to K\Lambda$ reactions up to center-of-mass energy 2 GeV. The Bethe-Salpeter equation was solved in the $K$-matrix approximation, with the interaction potential constructed from $s$-, $u$-, and $t$-channel tree-level Feynman diagrams. Two independent parameter sets (set $S$ and set $C$) were obtained by fitting separately to the SAPHIR and CLAS photoproduction data, while keeping couplings to non-strange channels fixed from prior work.

## Key numbers

- The $S_{11}(1650)$ resonance has a branching decay ratio to $K\Lambda$ of $3.2\%$ (set $C$) or $4.6\%$ (set $S$), with a summary value of $4(1)\%$.
- The $P_{13}(1720)$ resonance has a branching decay ratio to $K\Lambda$ of $4.6\%$ (set $C$) or $4.0\%$ (set $S$), with a summary value of $4.3(0.4)\%$.
- The $P_{13}(1900)$ resonance has a branching decay ratio to $K\Lambda$ of $2.4\%$ (set $C$) or $2.3\%$ (set $S$), with a summary value of $2.4(0.3)\%$.
- The cutoff at the $NK\Lambda$ vertex was fixed to $\Lambda_{NK\Lambda} = \Lambda_N = 0.95$ GeV; the $t$-channel cutoff was $\Lambda_t = 0.75$ GeV.
- The $\chi^2_{K\Lambda}$ for the photoproduction process was 2.0 (set $S$) and 2.2 (set $C$).
- The $P_{11}(1710)$ resonance branching ratio to $K\Lambda$ was $6.8\%$ (set $C$) or $3.1\%$ (set $S$), with a summary value of $5(3)\%$.
- The $D_{13}(1950)$ resonance branching ratio to $K\Lambda$ was $0.1\%$ in both sets, with a summary value of $0.1(0.1)\%$.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel analyses of Penner and Mosel (2002) and Shklyar, Lenske, and Mosel (2004) by incorporating new photoproduction data from SAPHIR (Glander, 2003), CLAS (McNabb, 2003), and SPring-8 (Zegers, 2003). It contrasts with single-channel Breit-Wigner models such as those of Mart and Bennhold (1999) and Ireland, Janssen, and Ryckebusch (2004), which identified the 1.9 GeV structure with a "missing" $D_{13}(1960)$ or $P_{11}(1900)$ resonance, respectively. The results support the conclusions of Usov and Scholten (2005) that background contributions strongly influence $K\Lambda$ photoproduction above 1.7 GeV. The work enables future tests of the predicted beam asymmetry at JLAB and LEPS facilities, and emphasizes that the inconsistency between SAPHIR and CLAS data must be resolved before further refinement of the reaction mechanism is meaningful.
