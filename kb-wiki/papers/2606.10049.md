# Momentum Dependence of Heavy Quark Diffusion in a Thermal Gluonic Plasma on the Lattice

> We study the dynamics of a heavy quark in a thermal plasma consisting of non-perturbatively interacting soft momentum gluons at high temperatures, described in terms of an effective theory of QCD discretized on a three-dimensional lattice. We propose a numerical strategy that allows us to simulate the dynamics of a heavy quark for different values of initial momenta in this thermalized plasma. This allows, for the first time, to extract the momentum dependence of the heavy quark drag and diffusion coefficients in a non-perturbatively interacting thermal, non-Abelian plasma.

# Momentum Dependence of Heavy Quark Diffusion in a Thermal Gluonic Plasma on the Lattice

## Key claim
For the first time, the momentum dependence of heavy quark drag and diffusion coefficients is extracted non-perturbatively from a thermal, non-Abelian plasma using lattice techniques, but only within an effective theory of soft gluons (momenta below the magnetic scale g²T/π) and for heavy quarks with mass M ≫ T. The Einstein relation is found to be significantly violated even at very small heavy quark velocities v ∼ 0.02, contrary to expectations from leading-order perturbative treatments.

## Method
The authors simulate the dynamics of a heavy quark (treated as a Dirac fermion with mass a_sM = 10.15) in a thermal background of non-perturbatively interacting soft gluons, described by the effective theory of Bodeker (1998) discretized on a 3D lattice with N = 32 sites per direction. The gauge fields are evolved via a Langevin equation with color conductivity σ set to its perturbative O(g²) value, while the heavy quark wavefunctions are evolved using the Dirac equation with an NLO-improved Wilson-Dirac operator. To prevent rapid saturation of momentum broadening, the quark-gluon coupling is scaled by a factor Λ = 0.005, and physical observables are recovered by rescaling moments of the momentum mode occupancy distribution (MMOD) by appropriate powers of Λ.

## Key numbers
- At T ≈ 1.6 T_c (≈ 480 MeV) for SU(3) with zero initial momentum, κ/T³ = 1.14(6) extracted from the linear growth of momentum variance in the time range Tt = 80–160.
- The ratio 2M/T × (η_D/T)/(κ_L/T³) deviates from unity (the Einstein relation value) even at the smallest initial momentum |p|/T ≈ 0.135, corresponding to velocity v ∼ 0.02.
- Initial momenta studied range from |p|/T = 0 to 0.923, with discrete values: 0, 0.135, 0.271, 0.405, 0.535, 0.658, 0.768, 0.858, 0.923.
- Temperature range covered: T = 1.6–12 T_c for SU(2) and T = 1.6–8 T_c for SU(3), where T_c ∼ 300 MeV.
- Lattice spacing satisfies a_s T ∼ 1.4–1.5; Langevin time step a_t/a_s = 0.025.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous formalism for zero-momentum heavy quark diffusion in a non-thermal SU(2) plasma (Pandey 2023) to finite initial momenta in a thermal plasma. It provides the first non-perturbative lattice calculation of κ(|p|) beyond the static limit, complementing prior lattice QCD studies of κ at zero momentum in quenched QCD (Banerjee 2011, Francis 2011, Brambilla 2020, Altenkort 2021) and in 2+1 flavor QCD (Altenkort 2023, HotQCD 2025). The results show significant deviation from the Einstein relation, consistent with expectations from strongly coupled N = 4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory (Gubser 2006) and recent discussions of non-Gaussian fluctuations (Rajagopal 2025). The momentum-dependent diffusion coefficients can reduce systematic uncertainties in modeling heavy quark elliptic flow and R_AA in heavy-ion collisions, and the framework can be generalized to study flavor dependence of heavy quark dynamics.
