# Dilute neutron matter on the lattice at next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory

> We discuss lattice simulations of the ground state of dilute neutron matter at next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory. In a previous paper the coefficients of the next-to-leading-order lattice action were determined by matching nucleon-nucleon scattering data for momenta up to the pion mass. Here the same lattice action is used to simulate the ground state of up to 12 neutrons in a periodic cube using Monte Carlo. We explore the density range from 2% to 8% of normal nuclear density and analyze the ground state energy as an expansion about the unitarity limit with corrections due to finite scattering length, effective range, and P-wave interactions.

## Key claim

The authors simulate the ground state of dilute neutron matter at next-to-leading order (NLO) in chiral effective field theory on a lattice, finding that the ground state energy can be analyzed as an expansion about the unitarity limit with corrections from finite scattering length, effective range, and P-wave interactions; however, the results show good agreement with other calculations near Fermi momentum \(k_F = 120\) MeV but disagree on whether the slope of the energy ratio versus \(k_F\) is positive or negative.

## Method

The authors used a lattice transfer matrix projection method with auxiliary-field hybrid Monte Carlo to simulate up to 12 neutrons in a periodic cube, using Gaussian-smeared lattice actions (LO\(_2\) and NLO\(_2\)) whose coefficients were previously determined by matching nucleon-nucleon scattering data for momenta up to the pion mass. The leading-order interactions were iterated nonperturbatively, while NLO corrections were computed via first-order perturbation theory. The ground state energy was extracted from asymptotic fits of transient energies as a function of Euclidean time, and the results were compared with published calculations from other groups.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order \(I=1\) contact coefficient \(C^{I=1}\) was set to \(-3.414 \times 10^{-5}\) MeV\(^{-2}\).
* The five NLO \(I=1\) operator coefficients (determined in a prior paper) are: \(\Delta C^{I=1} = -7.7 \times 10^{-7}\) MeV\(^{-2}\), \(C_{q^2}^{I=1} = -1.42 \times 10^{-9}\) MeV\(^{-4}\), \(C_{S^2,q^2}^{I=1} = -4.53 \times 10^{-10}\) MeV\(^{-4}\), \(C_{(q\cdot S)^2}^{I=1} = -1.80 \times 10^{-10}\) MeV\(^{-4}\), and \(C_{(iq\times S)\cdot k}^{I=1} = 9.81 \times 10^{-11}\) MeV\(^{-4}\).
* The spatial lattice spacing was \(a = (100\ \text{MeV})^{-1}\) and the temporal lattice spacing was \(a_t = (70\ \text{MeV})^{-1}\).
* For two-neutron precision tests, the LO ground state energy for the spin-singlet state was \(-2.9242\) MeV (exact) and \(-2.93(2)\) MeV (Monte Carlo); for the spin-triplet state it was \(25.030\) MeV (exact) and \(24.99(10)\) MeV (Monte Carlo).
* The NLO energy correction for the two-neutron spin-singlet state was \(-0.025025\) MeV (exact) and \(-0.0252(3)\) MeV (Monte Carlo); for the spin-triplet state it was \(3.3490\) MeV (exact) and \(3.349(7)\) MeV (Monte Carlo).
* Simulations were performed for \(N=8\) and \(N=12\) neutrons on lattices with spatial length \(L=5,6,7\) lattice units, covering a density range from 2% to 8% of normal nuclear density.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous lattice study (Borasoy et al., 2007) where the NLO lattice action coefficients were determined from nucleon-nucleon scattering data, by applying that action to many-body simulations of dilute neutron matter. The results are compared with several prior calculations: Friedman and Pandharipande (1981), Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall (1998), Carlson et al. (2003) using \(v6\) and \(v8'\) potentials, Schwenk and Pethick (2005), and Gezerlis and Carlson (2007). The study enables further lattice simulations of neutron matter at higher orders in chiral effective field theory and tests of model independence using different lattice actions and lattice spacings.
