# Decay Constants of Heavy Pseudoscalar Mesons: Reconciling QCD Sum Rules and Lattice QCD

> Exploiting recently proposed novel improvements of the techniques used for extracting from QCD sum rules pivotal hadron characteristics, including their systematic errors, we succeed to achieve a conspicuous agreement of the predictions of QCD sum rules for the decay constants $f_B$ and $f_{B_s}$ of the heavy pseudoscalar mesons $B$ and $B_s,$ which before tended to be slightly too large, with corresponding results from lattice QCD. Adopting for the crucial $b$-quark $\bar{\rm MS}$ mass the value $\bar{m}_b(\bar{m}_b)=(4.247\pm0.034)$ GeV, we get $f_B=(192.0\pm14.6)$ MeV and $f_{B_s}=(228.0\pm19.8)$ MeV; clearly, this may be also viewed as a determination of the $b$-quark mass from sufficiently precise decay constants.

## Key claim
By adopting a specific value of the \(b\)-quark \(\overline{\rm MS}\) mass and allowing the effective continuum threshold to depend on the Borel variable, QCD sum rules can reproduce the decay constants \(f_B\) and \(f_{B_s}\) in agreement with lattice QCD results, whereas earlier sum-rule predictions tended to be slightly too large.

## Method
The authors apply QCD sum rules with Borel transformations and a recently developed technique where the effective continuum threshold \(s_{\rm eff}\) is allowed to depend on the Borel variable. This dependence is determined by minimizing the deviation of the predicted meson mass squared from the true value over a range of Borel variables, using polynomial ansätze for \(s_{\rm eff}\) from linear to cubic. The systematic error is taken as the spread of results from these different ansätze. The sum rules are expressed in terms of the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) running mass of the heavy quark rather than its pole mass to improve perturbative convergence.

## Key numbers
- \(b\)-quark \(\overline{\rm MS}\) mass: \(\overline{m}_b(\overline{m}_b) = (4.247 \pm 0.034)\ \text{GeV}\).
- \(B\) meson decay constant: \(f_B = (192.0 \pm 14.6)\ \text{MeV}\) (combined QCD and systematic errors: \(192.0 \pm 14.3_{\rm QCD} \pm 3.0_{\rm syst}\ \text{MeV}\)).
- \(B_s\) meson decay constant: \(f_{B_s} = (228.0 \pm 19.8)\ \text{MeV}\) (combined QCD and systematic errors: \(228.0 \pm 19.4_{\rm QCD} \pm 4_{\rm syst}\ \text{MeV}\)).
- Lattice QCD averages used for comparison: \(f_B = (191.5 \pm 7.3)\ \text{MeV}\) and \(f_{B_s} = (228.8 \pm 6.9)\ \text{MeV}\), taken from several recent lattice evaluations.

## Context
This work extends the authors’ earlier improvements to the QCD sum-rule method (Lucha, Melikhov, Simula, 2013) and applies them to the bottom-meson system, following similar studies for charmed mesons (Lucha, Melikhov, Simula, 2013). The results reconcile previously discrepant QCD sum-rule predictions with lattice QCD computations for \(N_f\) dynamical sea-quark flavours, and also provide a determination of the \(b\)-quark mass from decay constants. This enables more reliable comparisons between sum-rule and lattice approaches for heavy-meson properties.
