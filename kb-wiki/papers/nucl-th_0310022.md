# A model for $J/ψ$ - kaon cross section

> We calculate the cross section for the dissociation of $J/ψ$ by kaons within the framework of a meson exchange model. We find that, depending on the values of the coupling constants used, the cross section can vary from 5 mb to 30 mb at $\sqrt{s}\sim5$ GeV.

## Key claim

The cross section for J/ψ dissociation by kaons, calculated using a meson exchange model, can vary from 5 mb to 30 mb at √s ∼ 5 GeV depending on the values of the coupling constants used in the hadronic vertices, which are not experimentally known.

## Method

The authors used an SU(4) effective Lagrangian with pseudo-scalar-vector-vector and four-point couplings to compute the amplitudes for the processes K J/ψ → D_s D̅* and K J/ψ → D_s* D̅ (and their charge conjugates). The coupling constants were determined either from vector meson dominance (VDM) or from the experimental ρππ coupling constant, and the cross sections were calculated by averaging over initial spins and summing over final spins with isospin factors included.

## Key numbers

- Total J/ψ-kaon dissociation cross section ranges from 5 mb to 30 mb at √s ∼ 5 GeV, depending on the coupling constant values used.
- Using VDM, the coupling constants are: g_{ψDD} = g_{ψD_sD_s} = g_{ψD*D*} = g_{ψD_s*D_s*} = 7.64, g_{K D_sD*} = g_{K DD_s*} = 6.6, and g_{K ψ D_sD*} = g_{K ψ DD_s*} = 50.55.
- Using the experimental ρππ coupling constant (g_{ρππ} = 6.06), the couplings become: g_{ψDD} = g_{ψD_sD_s} = g_{ψD*D*} = g_{ψD_s*D_s*} = 4.95, and g_{K D_sD*} = g_{K DD_s*} = 4.3.
- The J/ψ-kaon cross section is larger than the J/ψ-π → D̅ D* + D̅* D cross section shown for comparison.

## Context

This work extends the meson exchange approach previously applied to J/ψ-π and J/ψ-ρ cross sections (Haglin 2000, Lin and Ko 2000) to the J/ψ-kaon channel, correcting inconsistencies in the Lagrangians used by Haglin (2000). The results highlight the strong sensitivity of the cross section to the unknown coupling constants, motivating future work that will include form factors at hadronic vertices and anomalous parity interactions, as well as coupling estimates from QCD sum rules (Nielsen et al. 2001-2003).
