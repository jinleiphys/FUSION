# Probing Quark Matter In Neutron Stars

> The presence of quark matter in neutron star interiors may have distinctive signatures in basic observables such as (i) masses and radii [1], (ii) surface temperatures versus age [2], (iii) spin-down rates of milli-second pulsars [3], and (iv) neutrino luminosities from future galactic core collapse supernovae [4]. I highlight recent developments in some of these areas with a view towards assessing how theory may be confirmed by $ν-$signals from future galactic supernovae in detectors like SuperK, SNO and others under consideration, including UNO [5], and by multi-wavelength photon observations with new generation satellites such as the HST, Chandra, and XMM.

## Key claim

The presence of quark matter in neutron star interiors may produce distinctive signatures in neutrino signals from future galactic supernovae and in multi-wavelength photon observations, but discriminating among different exotic compositions (quarks, hyperons, kaon condensates) may require more than one observation of metastability.

## Method

The author presents benchmark calculations of proto-neutron star evolution by solving general relativistic neutrino transport and hydrostatic equations with equations of state and neutrino opacities for matter containing nucleons and quarks (npQ stars). Metastability timescales are computed for various baryon masses and compared with cases involving hyperons (npH) and kaon condensates (npK). The analysis also assesses how future neutrino detectors (SuperK, SNO, UNO) and photon observatories (HST, Chandra, XMM) could detect signatures of quark matter.

## Key numbers

- Maximum gravitational mass of cold catalyzed stars used in the benchmark quark EOS is 1.444 solar masses, matching observational constraints
- For an alternative quark EOS with maximum mass 1.85 solar masses, metastability timescales lie in a narrow range of 40-45 seconds
- Metastability times less than 10-15 seconds would indicate hyperonic (npH) matter composition
- Detection limits for a galactic supernova (8.5 kpc distance) are set to a count rate of 0.2 Hz in SNO, SuperK, and UNO detectors
- Neutron stars older than 10^6 years and hotter than approximately 10^7 K would signal quarks with large gaps

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of proto-neutron star evolution by Prakash (1997) and Pons (2001) to specifically address quark matter signatures, building on the suggestion by Collins and Perry (1975) that superdense matter in neutron star cores might consist of weakly interacting quarks. The results enable future observational tests using neutrino signals from galactic supernovae and photon observations of neutron star cooling, while highlighting the need for breakthroughs in lattice QCD simulations at finite baryon density to unambiguously determine the equation of state.
