# $Ξ^-$ emission probabilities at $(K^-,K^+)$ reaction points and the $ΞN$ interaction

> $Ξ^-$ escaping and scattering probabilities in the $(K^-,K^+)$ reaction with nuclear targets is investigated on the basis of the eikonal approximation. Calculations are performed using the $S=-2$ sector of the Nijmegen model-D interaction, whose hard core radii are treated phenomenologically. %The $Ξ^- N$, $Ξ^- p$ and $Ξ^- n$ elastic cross sections are The $Ξ^- N$ elastic cross sections are derived from the $^9$Be$(K^-,K^+)$ data of the BNL-E906 experiment, which is useful to determine the hard-core radii in $P$-states. The $Ξ$ single-particle potential energy is investigated with the G-matrix calculation in nuclear matter, which is sensitive to the hard-core radii in $S$-states. The important constraint for the $ΞN$ interaction is obtained by combining the two analyses.

## Key claim

The BNL-E906 experiment's measured $\Xi^-$ escaping and scattering probabilities can be used to separately determine the hard-core radii of the Nijmegen model-D interaction in $P$-states and $S$-states, respectively, but only when combined with $\Xi$ single-particle potential data from the E885 experiment; the two-step process contributions to $(K^-,K^+)$ events, estimated at up to 10%, introduce an ambiguity smaller than the experimental error bars.

## Method

The authors used the eikonal approximation to calculate $\Xi^-$ emission probabilities from $(K^-,K^+)$ reactions on a $^9$Be target, employing the $S=-2$ sector of the Nijmegen model-D (ND) interaction with hard-core radii treated as free parameters. They performed G-matrix calculations in symmetric nuclear matter to derive $\Xi$ single-particle potential energies, and constructed $\Xi$-nucleus potentials via local density approximation for comparison with Wood-Saxon potentials from the E885 experiment.

## Key numbers

- Escaping probability $P_{\Xi^-}^{\rm esc} = 63.6 \pm 8.2\%$ and scattering probability $P_{\Xi^-}^{\rm scat} = 14.5 \pm 2.6\%$ from the BNL-E906 experiment at $\Xi^-$ momentum 550 MeV/c
- In-medium $\Xi^-N$ elastic scattering cross section $20.9 \pm 4.5({\rm stat})^{+2.5}_{-2.4}({\rm syst})$ mb derived from the scattering probability using eikonal approximation
- $\Xi^- p$ scattering probability $5.8 \pm 3.5\%$ and $\Xi^- n$ scattering probability $8.7 \pm 4.4\%$ from the E906 experiment
- For $\alpha_{\rm two}=0$: $\sigma_{\rm abs}=21.1$ mb, $\tilde \sigma_{\rm scat}=21.6$ mb, $r_c(P)=0.480$ fm; for $\alpha_{\rm two}=0.10$: $\sigma_{\rm abs}=11.7$ mb, $\tilde \sigma_{\rm scat}=19.1$ mb, $r_c(P)=0.502$ fm
- $r_c(P) = 0.480^{+0.045}_{-0.033}$ fm from the experimental error of $P_{\Xi^-}^{\rm scat}$ at $\alpha_{\rm two}=0$
- $\Xi$ single-particle potential $U_\Xi = -14.3$ MeV at normal density for ND with $r_c(S)=r_c(P)=r_c(D)=0.5$ fm; $U_\Xi = -15.1^{-1.3}_{+1.8}$ MeV from the E906 data constraint
- Volume integral $J_V/A = 126$ MeV fm$^3$, root mean square radius $\langle R_V^2 \rangle^{1/2} = 2.9$ fm, and $B_\Xi(1S) = 2.2$ MeV for the $\Xi$-$^{11}$B potential from ND with $r_c=0.5$ fm
- Calculated $B_\Xi(2P) = 0.40$ MeV for $\Xi^-$-$^{12}$C (experimental value $0.57 \pm 0.19$ MeV from KEK-E176)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the BNL-E906 experiment (Tama, 2001) and complements the BNL-E885 experiment (E885, 1999) which measured missing mass spectra for $^{12}$C$(K^-,K^+)$X reactions and found a $\Xi$-nucleus Wood-Saxon well depth of about 14 MeV. The authors use the Nijmegen model-D interaction (NDF, 1990) and compare with a phenomenological one-range Gaussian (ORG) interaction. The results enable separate determination of $S$-state and $P$-state hard-core radii in the $\Xi N$ interaction, which is relevant for studying $\Xi^-$ mixing in neutron star matter (Nishi, 2000) and interpreting twin $\Lambda$ hypernuclei events from KEK-E176 (Aoki, 1993).
