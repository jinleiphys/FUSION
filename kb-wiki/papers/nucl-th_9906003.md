# Anisotropic flow from AGS to LHC energies

> Within hydrodynamics we study the effects of the initial spatial anisotropy in non-central heavy-ion collisions on the momentum distributions of the emitted hadrons. We show that the elliptic flow measured at midrapidity in 158 A GeV/c Pb+Pb collisions can be quantitatively reproduced by hydrodynamic expansion, indicating early thermalization in the collision. We predict the excitation functions of the 2nd and 4th harmonic flow coefficients from AGS to LHC energies and discuss their sensitivity to the quark-hadron phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper shows that elliptic flow measured at midrapidity in 158 A GeV/c Pb+Pb collisions can be quantitatively reproduced by hydrodynamic expansion, indicating early thermalization; however, the sensitivity of elliptic flow to the quark-hadron phase transition at a fixed beam energy is much weaker than naively expected, with the phase transition signature appearing as a bump (not a plateau and second rise) in the excitation function of v2.

## Method

The authors used a 2+1 dimensional ideal relativistic hydrodynamic model with Bjorken scaling for longitudinal expansion, solving the conservation equations for energy-momentum and baryon number. They investigated three equations of state: an ideal gas of massless particles (EOS I), a hadron resonance gas with a repulsive mean field (EOS H), and an equation of state with a first-order phase transition at Tc=164 MeV constructed by matching EOS H and EOS I using a bag constant B1/4=230 MeV (EOS Q). Model parameters were adjusted by fitting central 158 A GeV Pb+Pb collision data for negative hadron and proton spectra, then applied to non-central collisions using a Glauber-inspired initial condition.

## Key numbers

- For EOS Q, the freeze-out temperature Tdec = 120 MeV, initial central energy density e0 = 9.0 GeV/fm3, initial central baryon density n0 = 0.95 fm-3, and equilibration time tau0 = 0.8 fm/c
- The mixed phase for EOS Q ranges from eH = 0.45 GeV/fm3 to eQ = 1.6 GeV/fm3, with a latent heat of 1.15 GeV/fm3 at n=0
- For Pb+Pb collisions at b=7 fm with the same pt-cut as data, calculated v2 at midrapidity is 2.9% for pions and 11.7% for protons
- At high energies, v2 for pions drops from 6% (EOS H) to 5% (EOS Q); v4 is reduced by about 40% between EOS H and EOS Q
- The initial central temperature for the mushroom-like freeze-out surface calculation was T0 = 870 MeV at tau0 = 0.2 fm/c, yielding a pion midrapidity density of approximately 530 for b=8 fm

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic approach of Ollitrault (1992) and Teaney and Shuryak (1999) to systematically study anisotropic flow excitation functions from AGS to LHC energies. The paper contradicts earlier expectations from Heiselberg and Levy (1999) and Sorge (1999) that the elliptic flow response would show a plateau-like structure around the softest point, instead finding a bump in the v2 excitation function. The results enable predictions for future experiments at RHIC and LHC, and suggest that varying impact parameter at fixed beam energy (particularly at 40 A GeV at SPS) could provide an alternative way to study the phase transition signature.
