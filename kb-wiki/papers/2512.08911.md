# A Bayesian Approach Study of Hybrid Neutron Stars

> In this work, we explore how astronomical observations (specifically measurements of masses, radii, and tidal deformabilities) can constrain the presence of quark matter inside neutron stars, namely the phase transition from nuclear matter to deconfined quark matter. Our approach employs Bayesian analysis to study this phenomenon. Hadronic matter is modeled using the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approximation, for which we have selected two parameter sets: \(NL3^{*}ωρ\), representing hadronic matter with nucleons only, and $EL3ωρ$ with nucleons only and $EL3ωρY$, which includes hyperons. On the other hand deconfined quark matter is modeled using the vector-MIT bag model. For our purpose, the phase transition is implemented using the Maxwell construction. Bayesian inference is performed by tuning three parameters: the bag constant (i.e. $B^{1/4}$), the vector coupling constant \(\left(G_{v}\right)\), and the Dirac sea contribution ($b_{4}$). We found that a phase transition could exist at densities below \(2.0\,n_{0}\) for both the $EL3ωρ- EL3ωρY $ and $NL3^{*}ωρ$ parametrizations. As a consequence, our results also indicate that a hybrid neutron star could have a large quark core that comprises more than \(80\%\) of its size.

## Key claim
The paper finds that a phase transition from hadronic matter to deconfined quark matter could exist at densities below 2.0 times nuclear saturation density (n0), and that a hybrid neutron star could have a quark core comprising more than 80% of its size, but these results are conditional on the specific choice of hadronic model parameter sets (NL3*ωρ, EL3ωρ, and EL3ωρY) and the vector-MIT bag model parameters constrained by Bayesian analysis.

## Method
The authors model hadronic matter using the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approximation with three parameter sets (NL3*ωρ, EL3ωρ, and EL3ωρY) and deconfined quark matter using the vector-MIT bag model. They implement the hadron-quark phase transition via the Maxwell construction and perform Bayesian inference by tuning three parameters: the bag constant (B^{1/4}), the vector coupling constant (G_v), and the Dirac sea contribution (b_4), using observational data on masses, radii, and tidal deformabilities from PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0030+0451, and GW170817.

## Key numbers
- Phase transition could exist at densities below 2.0 n0 (nuclear saturation density) for both the EL3ωρ-EL3ωρY and NL3*ωρ parametrizations.
- A hybrid neutron star could have a quark core comprising more than 80% of its size.
- The inferred median values for the EL3ωρ set with two vMIT parameters are H2(G_v=0.25 fm^2, B^{1/4}=153.51 MeV); with three parameters, H3(G_v=0.26 fm^2, B^{1/4}=153.47 MeV, b_4=0.98).
- The uniform prior ranges used for Bayesian inference are: B^{1/4} in [150, 180] MeV, G_v in [0.2, 0.3] fm^2, and b_4 in [0, 2].
- Observational constraints include: PSR J0740+6620 mass 2.072^{+0.067}_{-0.066} M_sun and radius 12.39^{+1.30}_{-0.98} km; PSR J0030+0451 mass 1.34^{+0.15}_{-0.16} M_sun and radius 12.71^{+1.14}_{-1.19} km; GW170817 primary mass 1.46^{+0.12}_{-0.10} M_sun, secondary mass 1.27^{+0.09}_{-0.09} M_sun, and Lambda(1.4) = 190^{+390}_{-120}.

## Context
This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of neutron star equations of state (e.g., Imam 2021, Malik 2022, Coughlin 2019) by specifically constraining the presence of quark matter in hybrid stars using the vector-MIT bag model. It builds on hadronic models NL3*ωρ (Lopes 2021) and EL3ωρ (Lopes 2021) and applies the Maxwell construction for the phase transition, following the approach of Voskresensky (2002) and Maruyama (2007) regarding surface tension criteria. The results enable future multimessenger astronomy studies to distinguish between hadronic stars, pure quark stars, and hybrid stars using mass-radius and tidal deformability observations from NICER and LIGO/Virgo.
