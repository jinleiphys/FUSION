# Fluctuation, dissipation, and thermalization in non-equilibrium AdS_5 black hole geometries

> We give a simple recipe for computing dissipation and fluctuations (commutator and anti-commutator correlation functions) for non-equilibrium black hole geometries. The recipe formulates Hawking radiation as an initial value problem, and is suitable for numerical work. We show how to package the fluctuation and dissipation near the event horizon into correlators on the stretched horizon. These horizon correlators determine the bulk and boundary field theory correlation functions. In addition, the horizon correlators are the components of a horizon effective action which provides a quantum generalization of the membrane paradigm. In equilibrium, the analysis reproduces previous results on the Brownian motion of a heavy quark. Out of equilibrium, Wigner transforms of commutator and anti-commutator correlation functions obey a fluctuation-dissipation relation at high frequency.

## Key claim

The paper provides a recipe for computing dissipation and fluctuation correlation functions in non-equilibrium black hole geometries by formulating Hawking radiation as an initial value problem, but the fluctuation-dissipation relation is shown to hold exactly only at high frequency for out-of-equilibrium geometries, while in equilibrium it reproduces the standard relation for all frequencies.

## Method

The authors solve the linearized Nambu-Goto equations of motion for string fluctuations in an AdS₅ black hole background, using Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates to describe the near-horizon dynamics. They propagate initial data from the distant past through a narrow strip between the event horizon and a stretched horizon at r = 1+ε, packaging the effects of Hawking radiation into effective horizon correlators that determine the bulk and boundary correlation functions via a composition law for retarded propagators.

## Key numbers

- The drag coefficient for a heavy quark is η = (√λ/2π)(πT)², where λ is the 't Hooft coupling and T is the Hawking temperature
- Generic outgoing null geodesics reach the boundary in a time Δt ∼ 1/T
- The stretched horizon is placed at r_h = 1+ε, with ε a small positive number
- The coincident point singularity in the symmetrized correlator near the horizon has the form g_rr(v₁r₁|v₂r₂) = -(1/(4πη)) log|μ(v₁-v₂)(r₁-r₂)| plus exponentially small terms

## Context

This work extends the analysis of heavy quark Brownian motion in AdS/CFT by deBoer (2008) and Son (2009), who derived the fluctuation-dissipation relation for equilibrium black hole geometries using analytic continuation methods. The present paper provides a more physical derivation based on propagating near-horizon quantum fluctuations as an initial value problem, which enables application to non-equilibrium geometries. This enables future numerical studies of thermalization processes in time-dependent black hole backgrounds and generalizes the membrane paradigm to include quantum fluctuations.
