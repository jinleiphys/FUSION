# Deep Inelastic Scattering on a Nucleus using Holography

> We consider deep inelastic scattering (DIS) on a nucleus described using a density expansion. In leading order, the scattering is dominated by the incoherent scattering on individual nucleons distributed using the Thomas-Fermi approximation. We use the holographic structure functions for DIS scattering on single nucleons to make a non-perturbative estimate of the nuclear structure function in leading order in the density. Our results are compared to the data in the large-x regime.

## Key claim

The paper presents a non-perturbative estimate of nuclear structure functions in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) using holography, but only in leading order of a density expansion and restricted to the large-x regime, where the scattering is dominated by incoherent scattering on individual nucleons.

## Method

The authors organize the DIS scattering amplitude on a nucleus using a density expansion in terms of stable nucleon states, with nucleons distributed via the Thomas-Fermi approximation for a finite nucleus. They use the holographic nucleon structure function from Polchinski and Strassler (2003) for DIS on single nucleons, which at strong coupling and large number of colors gives a hard, nucleonic scattering instead of partonic. The leading-order contribution to the nuclear structure function and the R-ratio is then computed by averaging the single-nucleon forward Compton amplitude over the nuclear volume, including both bulk and surface contributions, with binding and Fermi motion effects incorporated.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear matter density: ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³
* Fermi momentum for uniform nuclear matter: p_F = 268 MeV
* Typical kinetic energy per nucleon: K = 23 MeV
* Binding energy per nucleon: B = 8.5 MeV
* Well depth in Thomas-Fermi approximation: V₀ = -40 MeV
* Nuclear radius parameter: R₀ = 1.12 fm, with surface thickness δ = 2.4 fm
* The R-ratio is evaluated at -q²/m_N² = 25 and twist τ = 3 for A = 12 and A = 42, compared to the HPC parametrization from the literature

## Context

This work extends the holographic approach to DIS on single nucleons, initiated by Polchinski and Strassler (2003), to nuclear targets using a density expansion. It provides a non-perturbative framework complementary to perturbative QCD analyses of the EMC effect, which was first observed by the EMC collaboration and later confirmed by experiments at CERN, SLAC, and FNAL. The leading-order results show depletion at intermediate-x and enhancement at large-x characteristic of the EMC effect, and the framework can be extended to include two-nucleon coherent scattering for the low-x region, as well as improved using soft-wall holographic models or lattice QCD quasi-distributions from Ji (2013).
