# Production of $ω$ and $ϕ$ Mesons in Near-Threshold $πN$ Reactions: Baryon Resonances and Validity of the OZI Rule

> Results of a combined analysis are presented for the production of $ω$ and $ϕ$ mesons in $πN$ reactions in the near-threshold region using throughoutly a conventional ''non-strange'' dynamics based on such processes which are allowed by the non-ideal $ω-ϕ$ mixing. We show that strong interferences of the $t$ (meson exchange) and $s$ and $u$ (nucleon and nucleon resonance) channels differ significantly in $ω$ and $ϕ$ production amplitudes. This leads to a decrease of the relative yields in comparison with expectations based on one-channel models with standard $ω- ϕ$ mixing. We find a strong and non-trivial difference between observables in $ω$ and $ϕ$ production reactions caused by the different role of the nucleon and nucleon resonance amplitudes. A series of predictions for the experimental study of this effect is presented.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed suppression of the ratio of ω to φ production cross sections in near-threshold πN reactions, which is often interpreted as evidence for hidden strangeness in the nucleon, can be explained entirely by conventional non-strange dynamics through strong interference between meson-exchange, nucleon, and nucleon resonance amplitudes, without invoking any violation of the OZI rule beyond the standard ω-φ mixing angle of Δθ_V ≈ 3.7°.

## Method

The authors performed a tree-level calculation of the reactions π⁻p → ωn and π⁻p → φn, summing coherently the t-channel ρ-meson exchange amplitude with s- and u-channel nucleon and nucleon resonance (N*) amplitudes for eight low-lying resonances up to 1720 MeV. The effective Lagrangians for the πNN*, ωNN*, and φNN* vertices were taken from a quark model by Riska and Brown (2000), with coupling constants for φ production related to those for ω production via the standard ω-φ mixing angle. The free parameters (cut-off parameters Λ^ρ_V = 1.24 GeV and Λ_B = 0.66 GeV) were fixed by fitting the total cross section data for both ω and φ production simultaneously.

## Key numbers

- The standard OZI rule violation from ideal ω-φ mixing gives a ratio of amplitudes R^{OZI}_{ω/φ} = ctg Δθ_V = 15.43, corresponding to a cross section ratio R^2_{ω/φ} ≈ 2.4×10².
- The pure meson-exchange amplitude ratio at threshold is R^M_{ω/φ} ≈ 9.9 × f(m_ω)/f(m_φ), which is already smaller than the OZI prediction.
- The pure resonance amplitude ratio is R^{N*}_{ω/φ} ∼ 500 at θ = π and ∼ 250 at θ = 0, much larger than the OZI value.
- The best-fit cut-off parameters are Λ^ρ_V = 1.24 GeV for the Vρπ vertex and Λ_B = 0.66 GeV for the baryon form factors.
- The φ → ρπ decay width of 0.69 MeV gives |g_{φρπ}| = 1.1 GeV⁻¹; the ωρπ coupling constant from vector dominance is g_{ωρπ} = 12.9 GeV⁻¹.
- The ωNN coupling constant used is g_{ωNN} = 10.35 with κ_{ωNN} = 0, taken from a fit to nucleon-nucleon scattering data by Rijken, Stoks, and Yamamoto (1999).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of φ production alone (Titov, Kämpfer, and Reznik, 2000) to include ω production with full resonance contributions, and directly addresses the OZI rule controversy raised by Ellis (1998, 1999) and the data compilation by Sibirtsev and Cassing (2000), who found a ratio of averaged amplitudes R_{ω/φ} = 8.7 ± 1.8 from existing data. The paper builds on the quark-model coupling relations of Riska and Brown (2000) and the Bonn potential parameters for the ρNN vertex. It enables future experimental tests of the predicted angular distributions and spin-density matrix elements in πN → VN reactions, which could distinguish this conventional interference mechanism from exotic strangeness interpretations.
