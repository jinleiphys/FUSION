# Anisotropic flow: A case for Bottomonia

> Studies of $J/$$ψ$ $v_2$ at RHIC and LHC energies have provided important elements towards the understanding on the production mechanisms and on the thermalization of charm quarks. Bottomonia has an advantage since it is a cleaner probe. A brief discussion has been provided for $Υ(1S)$ $v_2$, which can become the new probe for QGP, including the necessity of studies for small systems.

## Key claim

The paper argues that bottomonia (specifically $\Upsilon(1S)$) are a cleaner probe than charmonia for studying the anisotropic flow of quark-gluon plasma, because regeneration effects are negligible for bottomonia, but the authors hedge that this advantage depends on the reshuffling of bottomonium states in a time-dependent potential, which has not yet been incorporated into flow calculations.

## Method

The authors review existing experimental results on $J/\psi$ elliptic flow ($v_2$) from RHIC and LHC, then contrast the production and suppression mechanisms for charmonia versus bottomonia. They discuss the escape mechanism for bottomonia from a spatially asymmetric medium, and introduce a non-adiabatic evolution framework where the time-dependent heavy-quark potential causes transitions between bottomonium states, potentially affecting the anisotropic flow pattern of $\Upsilon(1S)$ and its excited states.

## Key numbers

- $J/\psi$ $v_2$ at RHIC in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200$ GeV is consistent with zero within large uncertainties (STAR Collaboration).
- Positive $J/\psi$ $v_2$ observed by ALICE in semi-central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76$ TeV with $2.7\sigma$ significance for inclusive $J/\psi$ with $2 < p_{\rm T} < 6$ GeV/$c$ at forward rapidity.
- Positive $J/\psi$ $v_2$ observed by ALICE in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02$ TeV in the transverse momentum range $2 < p_{\rm T} < 8$ GeV/$c$ in three centrality classes.
- Feed-down from higher mass bottomonia is between $40\%$ and $50\%$ for $\Upsilon(1S)$.
- Medium constituents at temperatures $T\sim 4T_c$ have transverse momenta $p_{\rm T} \sim 2$ GeV, a factor of 5 smaller than $M_\Upsilon$.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of anisotropic flow from charmonia, where regeneration complicates interpretation (as seen in ALICE and CMS results at LHC), to bottomonia, which are less affected by regeneration due to the lower production cross section of $b\bar{b}$ pairs. The paper builds on the escape mechanism originally proposed for $J/\psi$ by Wang (2002) and on the non-adiabatic evolution framework developed by Borghini (2011) and Dutta (2012). It enables future experimental studies of $\Upsilon(1S)$ $v_2$ at RHIC and LHC, and suggests that measuring flow in small systems (p-Pb, p-p) could test whether initial-state correlations or hydrodynamical explanations dominate.
