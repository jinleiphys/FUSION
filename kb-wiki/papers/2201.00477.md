# Quark-nuclear hybrid equation of state for neutron stars under modern observational constraints

> We study a family of equations of state for hybrid neutron star matter. The hybrid EOS are obtained by a Maxwell construction of the first-order phase transition between a hadronic phase described by the relativistic density-functional EOS of the "DD2" class with excluded volume effects and a deconfined quark matter phase modeled by an instantaneous nonlocal version of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in SU(2)$_f$ with vector interactions and color superconductivity. The form factor in the nonlocal quark matter model is fitted to lattice QCD results in the Coulomb gauge. Owing to strong coupling in the vector meson and diquark channels, a coexistence phase of color superconductivity and chiral symmetry breaking occurs. Our results show an approximately constant behavior for the squared speed of sound with values of 0.4 - 0.6 in the density region relevant for neutron star interiors. To simultaneously fulfill the constraints from the Neutron Star Interior Composition Explorer radius measurement for PSR J0740+6620 and tidal deformability from GW170817 it is necessary to consider a $μ$-dependent bag pressure that mimics confinement.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a hybrid neutron star equation of state (EOS) combining a hadronic phase (DD2 class with excluded volume) and a quark matter phase (nonlocal Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with vector interactions and color superconductivity) can simultaneously satisfy the NICER radius constraint for PSR J0740+6620 and the tidal deformability constraint from GW170817, but only if a chemical-potential-dependent bag pressure mimicking confinement is introduced.

## Method

The authors constructed hybrid EOS using a Maxwell construction for a first-order phase transition between hadronic matter described by the DD2 relativistic density functional with excluded volume effects and quark matter described by an instantaneous nonlocal SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with vector interactions and 2SC color superconductivity. The form factor in the nonlocal quark model was fitted to lattice QCD results in the Coulomb gauge, and the model parameters were fixed by reproducing the pion mass (138 MeV) and pion decay constant (92.4 MeV). The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities for comparison with observational constraints.

## Key numbers

* Squared speed of sound in the quark matter phase is approximately constant in the range 0.4 - 0.6 for energy densities 400 - 2000 MeV/fm³ relevant for neutron star interiors
* The form factor parameter Λ₀ = 885.47 MeV was obtained from fitting lattice QCD data for the normalized quark effective mass up to 3 GeV
* Current quark mass m_c = 2.29 MeV and scalar coupling G_S = 9.92 GeV⁻² were determined from fitting pion mass (138 MeV) and pion decay constant (92.4 MeV)
* For η_D = 1.1 and η_V = 0.5, the critical baryon chemical potential for the chiral crossover is μ_B = 931 MeV, while the diquark condensate vanishes at μ_B = 888 MeV
* Maximum masses up to about 2.6 M_⊙ at radius ≈ 13.75 km were obtained for η_V = 0.9 with constant bag pressure B = 10 MeV/fm³
* The 2SC pairing gap Δ ≈ 150 MeV at μ_B = 1 GeV yields c_s² = 0.46 via the analytic formula

## Context

This work extends previous hybrid EOS studies using local NJL models (Ippolito 2007, Baldo 2002, Buballa 2003) and nonlocal versions (Grigorian 2003, Klahn 2006) by including both vector interactions and diquark pairing simultaneously. The constant speed of sound (CSS) model used in phenomenological studies (Drischler 2020, Cierniak 2021, Somasundaram 2021) is given a microphysical justification through the nonlocal chiral quark model. The results enable systematic exploration of how the diquark coupling and vector coupling parameters affect the onset of deconfinement and maximum neutron star masses, while the need for a μ-dependent bag pressure highlights the importance of confinement effects missing in the NJL approach.
