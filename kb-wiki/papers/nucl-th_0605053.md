# Relativistic Coulomb excitation at small impact parameters

> The semiclassical model of relativistic Coulomb excitation is studied in situations in which the impact parameter is small enough so that projectile and target charge distributions overlap. The electromagnetic effects of this overlap are shown to be small. Realistic nucleon-nucleon reaction cross-sections, and realistic nuclear radial charge and matter distributions are used to determine a formula for the lower impact parameter limit to be used in the calculation of the Coulomb excitation cross-section. A wide selection of projectile-target pairs is explored, in the bombarding energy range of 1 GeV to 5 GeV per nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper shows that when projectile and target charge distributions overlap at small impact parameters, the correction to the standard Lienard-Wiechert potential treatment of relativistic Coulomb excitation is very small and can be safely ignored, allowing the use of a simpler formula with a sharp lower impact parameter cutoff determined by nuclear absorption.

## Method

The authors used the optical limit of the Glauber model to calculate the probability X(b) that no nuclear interaction occurs between projectile and target, folding realistic nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes with Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov nuclear density distributions from the IAEA compilation. They then computed the electromagnetic transition matrix element for the giant dipole resonance using the Goldhaber-Teller model, separating the contribution into a Lienard-Wiechert term and an overlap correction term, and evaluated the cross-section integral with the absorption factor X(b) for 200 projectile-target-energy combinations.

## Key numbers

- The overlap correction sigma_overlap is always very small compared to sigma_LW; for example, with a 208Pb projectile on 202Hg target at 2 GeV per nucleon, the overlap correction is negligible relative to the Lienard-Wiechert cross-section.
- The best-fit formula for the lower impact parameter limit is b_min = 1.3115 fm * (A_P^(1/3) + A_T^(1/3)) - 1.0509 fm * (A_P^(-1/3) + A_T^(-1/3)), reproducing 200 numerically calculated values with an rms error of 0.0248 fm.
- The alternative three-parameter formula b_min = 1.3338 fm * (A_P^(1/3) + A_T^(1/3)) + 0.1652 fm * (A_P^(1/3) * A_T^(1/3))/(A_P^(1/3) + A_T^(1/3)) - 1.0667 fm gives an rms error of 0.0382 fm per point.
- For mu=2 transitions, the decrease in b_min compared to mu=1 values is about 0.05 fm for 208Pb projectiles and about 0.1 fm for 16O projectiles, corresponding to cross-section increases of 0.7% to 1.5% and 1.5% to 7% respectively.
- The bombarding energy range explored is 1 GeV to 5 GeV per nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical model of relativistic Coulomb excitation by providing a reliable formula for the lower impact parameter cutoff, improving upon earlier treatments by Bertulani et al. (1988) who assumed zero extension for the nucleon-nucleon profile function and did not distinguish between identical and non-identical nucleon interactions. The approach is similar to that developed by Benesh, Cook and Vary (1989) and by Kox et al. (1987) for total reaction cross-sections. The results enable experimentalists to calculate Coulomb excitation cross-sections more accurately for a wide range of projectile-target pairs at relativistic energies without needing to perform the full numerical integration with the absorption factor.
