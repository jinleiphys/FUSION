# Effective nucleon-nucleon interactions and nuclear matter equation of state

> Nuclear matter equations of state based on Skyrme, Myers-Swiatecki and Tondeur interactions are written as polynomials of the cubic root of density, with coefficients that are functions of the relative neutron excess $δ$. In the extrapolation toward states far away from the standard one, it is shown that the asymmetry dependence of the critical point ($ρ_c, δ_c$) depends on the model used. However, when the equations of state are fitted to the same standard state, the value of $δ_c$ is almost the same in Skyrme and in Myers-Swiatecki interactions, while is much lower in Tondeur interaction. Furthermore, $δ_c$ does not depend sensitively on the choice of the parameter $γ$ in Skyrme interaction.

# Effective nucleon-nucleon interactions and nuclear matter equation of state

## Key claim

When nuclear matter equations of state (EOS) based on Skyrme, Myers-Swiatecki, and Tondeur interactions are fitted to the same standard state (same saturation density, volume energy, incompressibility, and symmetry energy), the critical relative neutron excess δ_c is almost the same for Skyrme and Myers-Swiatecki interactions, but is much lower for the Tondeur interaction; furthermore, δ_c does not depend sensitively on the choice of the parameter γ in the Skyrme interaction.

## Method

The authors expressed the nuclear matter EOS for Skyrme, Myers-Swiatecki, and Tondeur effective interactions as polynomials in the cubic root of density, with coefficients that are functions of the relative neutron excess δ. They first calculated standard nuclear matter properties (a₁, K₀, J, L, K_s) using the original published interaction parameters for each model, then readjusted all interaction parameters so that each EOS reproduces the same chosen standard state (ρ₀, a₁, K₀, J, L, K_s). Using both original and readjusted parameters, they computed the equilibrium density ρ_m(δ), equilibrium energy e_m(δ), and incompressibility K_m(δ) along the line of minima, and determined the critical point (ρ_c, δ_c) where the incompressibility vanishes. They compared the predictions for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter against microscopic calculations from Friedman and Pandharipande (1981), Wiringa (1988), and Akmal et al. (1998).

## Context

This work extends the comparison of Skyrme-type (Brack et al., 1985), Myers-Swiatecki (1996), and Tondeur (1984) effective interactions for nuclear matter EOS, focusing on extrapolation far from the standard saturation state. It builds on earlier studies of nuclear matter properties by showing that when all models are forced to share the same standard-state parameters, the critical point behavior differs significantly between Tondeur and the other two interactions. The results enable more informed choices among effective interactions for applications in neutron star structure, supernova explosions, and heavy-ion collisions, and highlight the need for experimental data on nuclear matter with large asymmetry δ and low density ρ to discriminate between models. The authors compare their predictions with microscopic calculations by Friedman and Pandharipande (1981) using the Urbana v₁₄ potential, Wiringa (1988) using Argonne v₁₄ plus Urbana VII, and Akmal et al. (1998) using Argonne v₁₈ plus Urbana IX.
