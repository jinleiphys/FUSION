# Probing the three-body force in hadronic systems with specific charge parity

> Three-body forces, a type of non-perturbative strong interaction, are widely studied in nuclear physics. However, whether their inclusion is necessary in nuclear systems remains a topic of intense debate. In this letter, we propose that the existence of three-body forces in certain three-body hadronic systems with definite $C$-parity is certain. Such systems consist of two components whose interactions are mediated by three-body forces--a mechanism not easily realized in conventional three-nucleon systems. We investigate two specific three-body hadronic systems, $\bar{D}_sDK$ and $\bar{D}^*Dη$, using contact-range potentials. The two-body hadron-hadron interactions are constrained by reproducing their scattering lengths, while the three-body couplings are constrained by charge symmetry. Our results indicate that three-body forces play a minor role in binding the $I(J^{PC})=0(0^{--})$ $\bar{D}_sDK$ system, but a crucial one in binding the $I(J^{PC})=0(1^{-+})$ $\bar{D}^*Dη$ system. In fact, three-body forces determine whether $\bar{D} ^*Dη$ forms a bound state, making this system a promising candidate for exploring three-body forces in hadronic physics.

# Probing the three-body force in hadronic systems with specific charge parity

## Key claim
The authors propose that three-body forces are certain to exist in three-body hadronic systems with definite C-parity, but their importance is system-dependent: they play a minor role in binding the $\bar{D}_sDK$ system while being crucial for binding the $\bar{D}^*D\eta$ system, where they determine whether a bound state forms at all.

## Method
The authors solve the Schrödinger equation using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) for two three-body hadronic systems, $\bar{D}_sDK$ and $\bar{D}^*D\eta$, with contact-range potentials in a pionless effective field theory framework. Two-body hadron-hadron interactions are constrained by reproducing scattering lengths and effective ranges from lattice QCD simulations or symmetry arguments, while the three-body coupling strength $C_3$ is treated as a free parameter with its sign determined by charge parity. The criteria for identifying a genuine three-body bound state are negative eigenenergy and finite, stable root-mean-square radii under basis variation.

## Key numbers
- For $DK$ scattering: scattering length $a_0 = -1.49 \pm 0.13$ fm and effective range $r_0 = 0.20 \pm 0.09$ fm at $m_\pi = 150$ MeV from lattice QCD (Bali 2017).
- Scattering lengths used: $a_{\bar{D}_sD} = 0.525^{+0.07}_{-0.06}$ fm (Ji 2022), $a_{\bar{D}^*D} = -1.7 \pm 0.4$ fm (Prelovsek 2013), $a_{D\eta} = 0.29^{+0.15}_{-0.22}$ fm (Guo 2018).
- For the $\bar{D}^*D\eta$ system with $b_2 = 0.5$ fm, a bound state forms when the three-body strength reaches approximately $C_3 \simeq -195$ MeV; with $C_3 = -240$ MeV, the binding energy is about $14$ MeV with rms radius $1 \sim 3$ fm.
- For the $\bar{D}^*D\eta$ system with $b_2 = 1.0$ fm, a bound state emerges when $C_3 \simeq -450$ MeV.
- The three-body potential contributes at least $18\%$ of the total potential energy for the $\bar{D}^*D\eta$ bound state, compared to at most $4\%$ for the $\bar{D}_sDK$ system.
- Predicted branching fractions for $X(4412)$ production in $B$ decays: $\mathcal{B}[B \to (X(4412) \to D^{*\pm}D^{\mp}) K] \approx 0.1 \times 10^{-6}$, $\mathcal{B}[B \to (X(4412) \to D^+D^-) K] \approx 0.5 \times 10^{-6}$, $\mathcal{B}[B \to (X(4412) \to D^{*+}D^{*-}) K] \approx 0.4 \times 10^{-6}$.
- Expected event numbers at LHCb with $9~\text{fb}^{-1}$: $0.4$ events in $B \to D^{*\pm}D^{\mp} K$ and $2$ events in $B \to D^+D^- K$.

## Context
This work extends the study of three-body forces from conventional three-nucleon systems, where the necessity of three-nucleon interactions was established by the failure of NN-only potentials (e.g., Argonne $v_{18}$, Bonn, chiral EFT potentials) to reproduce binding energies of $^3$H and $^4$He (Pudliner 1995, Navratil 2000). The authors build on the observation by Wu (2025) that three-body forces naturally arise in hadronic systems with definite charge parity, and they apply pionless EFT methods similar to those used in chiral EFT for nuclear forces (Epelbaum 2008, Hebeler 2020). The work enables experimental searches for the predicted $I(J^{PC})=0(1^{-+})$ $\bar{D}^*D\eta$ molecule (denoted $X(4412)$) in $B$ meson decays at LHCb, and suggests that momentum correlation functions and invariant-mass distributions could provide further insights into three-body forces.
