# Heavy quark diffusion in an overoccupied gluon plasma

> We extract the heavy-quark diffusion coefficient κand the resulting momentum broadening <p^2> in a far-from-equilibrium non-Abelian plasma. We find several features in the time dependence of the momentum broadening: a short initial rapid growth of <p^2>, followed by linear growth with time due to Langevin-type dynamics and damped oscillations around this growth at the plasmon frequency. We show that these novel oscillations are not easily explained using perturbative techniques but result from an excess of gluons at low momenta. These oscillation are therefore a gauge invariant confirmation of the infrared enhancement we had previously observed in gauge-fixed correlation functions. We argue that the kinetic theory description of such systems becomes less reliable in the presence of this IR enhancement.

# Heavy quark diffusion in an overoccupied gluon plasma

## Key claim

The heavy-quark diffusion coefficient and momentum broadening in a far-from-equilibrium non-Abelian plasma exhibit damped oscillations at the plasmon frequency, which are not easily explained by perturbative techniques and instead result from an excess of gluons at low momenta; the authors argue that kinetic theory descriptions become less reliable in the presence of this infrared enhancement.

## Method

The authors performed classical-statistical lattice simulations of SU(2) Yang-Mills theory in a fixed three-dimensional box, starting from a highly occupied initial condition characterized by a single-particle occupation number distribution f(t=0, p) = (0.2/g^2) (Q/p) exp(-p^2/(2Q^2)). They extracted the unequal-time chromoelectric field correlator at the same spatial location, from which they computed the momentum broadening ⟨p^2⟩ and the heavy-quark diffusion coefficient κ using gauge-invariant definitions appropriate for out-of-equilibrium dynamics, working in the infinite quark mass limit where the quark is stationary.

## Key numbers

- The hard scale Λ at Q t = 1500 is 2.1 Q
- The Debye mass m_D at Q t = 1500 is 0.21 Q
- The effective temperature g^2 T_* at Q t = 1500 is 0.03 Q
- The plasmon damping rate γ_plas at Q t = 1500 is 0.003 Q
- The scaling exponent β for the self-similar regime in d=3 spatial dimensions is -1/7
- The plasmon frequency ω_plas and Debye mass scale with time as ω_plas ∼ m_D ∼ Q (Q t)^β
- The damping rate γ_plas scales with time as γ_plas ∼ Q (Q t)^{3β}

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy quark diffusion in thermal equilibrium using perturbative methods (Arnold, 2000; Moore, 2004) and lattice approaches (Petreczky, 2005; Francis, 2015), as well as classical lattice studies of the heavy quark diffusion coefficient in equilibrium (Laine, 2009). The authors had previously observed an infrared enhancement in gauge-fixed correlation functions (Boguslavski, 2018), and the present work provides a gauge-invariant confirmation of that enhancement through the observed plasmon-frequency oscillations in momentum broadening. The results enable improved modeling of heavy quark dynamics and quarkonium evolution during the pre-equilibrium stages of heavy-ion collisions, where the glasma phase can contribute sizable momentum broadening (Ipp, 2020).
