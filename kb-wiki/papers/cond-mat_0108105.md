# Maximum occupation number for composite boson states

> One of the major differences between fermions and bosons is that fermionic states have a maximum occupation number of one, whereas the occupation number for bosonic states is in principle unlimited. For bosons that are made up of fermions, one could ask the question to what extent the Pauli principle for the constituent fermions would limit the boson occupation number. Intuitively one can expect the maximum occupation number to be proportional to the available volume for the bosons divided by the volume occupied by the fermions inside one boson, though a rigorous derivation of this result has not been given before. In this letter we show how the maximum occupation number can be calculated from the ground-state energy of a fermionic generalized pairing problem. A very accurate analytical estimate of this eigenvalue is derived. From that a general expression is obtained for the maximum occupation number of a composite boson state, based solely on the intrinsic fermionic structure of the bosons. The consequences for Bose-Einstein condensates of excitons in semiconductors and ultra cold trapped atoms are discussed.

## Key claim

The Pauli principle for the constituent fermions puts an upper limit on the occupation number of a composite boson state, irrespective of boson-boson interactions; this maximum occupation number (MON) can be calculated from the ground-state energy of a fermionic generalized pairing problem, and the authors derive a very accurate analytical estimate that yields MON approximately equal to \(S^2/4 + 1/4\) to \(S^2/4 + 1\), where \(S\) is the sum of the absolute values of the Bloch-Messiah coefficients.

## Method

The authors map the boson number operator for a composite boson state (composed of two fermions) onto a fermionic generalized pairing operator via the Bloch-Messiah theorem, which decomposes the boson creation operator into canonical fermion pair states. They then derive upper and lower bounds for the maximum eigenvalue of this operator using the Gershgorin circle theorem for the upper bound and a variational trial state (a fully paired state) for the lower bound, obtaining a tight analytical estimate for the MON. This estimate is applied to specific cases by calculating the quantity \(S = \sum_{\alpha} |c_{\alpha}|\) (the sum of the absolute Bloch-Messiah coefficients) for excitons in semiconductors (using hydrogenic wave functions) and for Gaussian wave functions (using the reduced density matrix and its square root trace).

## Key numbers

- For a three-dimensional cubic volume \(V\) with excitons having a 1s hydrogenic intrinsic wave function (Bohr radius \(a_x\)): \(\text{MON} = V / (4 \pi a_x^3)\), corresponding to a scaling factor \(\lambda = 4\pi\).
- For a two-dimensional box with surface \(V\) and the same exciton wave function: \(\text{MON} = V / ((\pi/2) a_x^2)\), with \(\lambda = \pi/2\).
- For Gaussian wave functions with one-boson width \(W\) and fermion distribution width \(v\): \(\text{MON} \simeq 2 (W/v)^3\).
- For hydrogen atoms in a harmonic trap of width \(W = 1\ \mu\text{m}\), using the Gaussian approximation with \(v \simeq (\sqrt{3}/2) a_0\) (where \(a_0\) is the atomic Bohr radius): \(\text{MON} = 16 W^3 / (3 \sqrt{3} a_0^3)\), yielding an order of magnitude of \(10^{13}\) for all alkali atoms.
- Present-day experiments (Ande95, Davi95, Brad95, Frie98) find occupation numbers of order \(10^6\) to \(10^9\), much lower than the MON estimate.

## Context

This work extends earlier intuitive arguments (Hana77) that the maximum occupation number should be of order \(V/r^D\) (with \(V\) the volume, \(D\) the dimension, and \(r\) the range of the intrinsic fermionic wave function) by providing a rigorous derivation and accurate analytical expression. It directly addresses recent studies (Comb01, Tang02) that derived criteria for the bosonic nature of excitons from mean-field states, finding a scaling factor \(\lambda \simeq 100\) to \(400\), whereas the present work obtains \(\lambda = 4\pi\) from the MON bound, which is independent of the specific many-exciton state. The results enable quantitative predictions for the maximum density of Bose-Einstein condensates of excitons and trapped atoms, and suggest that current atomic BEC experiments (with occupation numbers \(\sim 10^6\)–\(10^9\)) are far from the Pauli-limited regime, while nuclear physics shell-model calculations (Alha96, Honm01) may show bosonic pairs with occupation numbers near their maximum.
