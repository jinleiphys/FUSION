# Pion observables with the Minkowski Space Pion Model

> The pion structure in Minkowski space is described in terms of an analytic model of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude combined with Euclidean Lattice QCD results for the running quark mass. In the present work, a pion model previously proposed, which allows for a Nakanishi integral representation, is studied in order to verify the sensitivity of the pion electromagnetic form factor to small variations of the quark self-energy. In addition, we extend the previous work, providing the Nakanishi integral representation for the invariants associated with a decomposition of the pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Minkowski-space pion model, built from an analytic Bethe-Salpeter amplitude combined with Euclidean Lattice QCD results for the running quark mass, yields pion electromagnetic form factors consistent with experimental data when the model parameters are varied by up to 20 percent; the authors explicitly hedge that the quark self-energy parametrization violates positivity constraints of the Kallen-Lehman representation.

## Method

The authors construct a dressed quark propagator with a running mass function \(M(k^2)=m_0 - m^3/(k^2-\lambda^2+i\epsilon)\) fitted to Lattice QCD results in the Landau gauge, setting the wave function renormalization \(Z(k^2)=1\). They then compute the pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude using a Nakanishi integral representation, decompose it into Dirac invariants, and derive the corresponding Nakanishi weight functions analytically. The pion electromagnetic form factor is calculated using a quark current operator that satisfies the Ward-Takahashi identity, and the sensitivity of the form factor to parameter variations is tested by varying the input parameters by \(\pm 20\%\).

## Key numbers

- Running quark mass parameters (initial parameter set): \(m_0 = 0.014\ \text{GeV}\), \(m = 0.574\ \text{GeV}\), \(\lambda = 0.846\ \text{GeV}\).
- Real poles of the quark propagator: \(m_1 = 0.371\ \text{GeV}\), \(m_2 = 0.644\ \text{GeV}\), \(m_3 = 0.954\ \text{GeV}\).
- Spectral decomposition coefficients for \(A(k^2)\): \(D_1 = 1.4992\), \(D_2 = -0.594098\), \(D_3 = -0.0949811\).
- Spectral decomposition coefficients for \(B(k^2)\): \(E_1 = 0.42401285\), \(E_2 = -0.331377\), \(E_3 = -0.07863548\).
- Experimental pion electromagnetic radius: \(r^{\text{Exp.}}_{\pi} = 0.659 \pm 0.004\ \text{fm}\).
- Experimental weak pion decay constant: \(f^{\text{Exp.}}_{\pi} = 90.276 \pm 0.0707\ \text{MeV}\) (PDG 2014).

## Context

This work extends the earlier model of Clayton (2015) by providing the Nakanishi integral representation for all invariants of the pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude and by testing the sensitivity of the pion electromagnetic form factor to variations in the quark self-energy parameters. The results are compared with experimental data from Volmer (2000), Horn (2006), Tadevosyan (2007), and Huber (2008), as well as with Lattice QCD results from Parappilly (2005) and Rojas (2013). The model enables predictions for the pion form factor at higher momentum transfers, relevant for the 12 GeV upgrade at TJLAB (Dudek 2012), and provides a framework for incorporating more refined quark self-energy approaches.
