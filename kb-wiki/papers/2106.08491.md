# Langevin dynamics of heavy quarks in a soft-hard factorized approach

> By utilizing a soft-hard factorized model, which combines a thermal perturbative description of soft scatterings and a perturbative QCD-based calculation for hard collisions, we study the energy and temperature dependence of the heavy quark diffusion coefficients in Langevin dynamics. The adjustable parameters are fixed from the comprehensive model-data comparison. We find that a small value of the spatial diffusion coefficient at transition temperature is preferred by data $2πTD_{s}(T_{c}) \simeq 6$. With the parameter-optimized model, we are able to describe simultaneously the prompt $D^{0}$ $R_{\rm AA}$ and $v_{2}$ data at $p_{\rm T}\le8$ GeV in Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76$ and $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV. We also make predictions for non-prompt $D^{0}$ meson for future experimental tests down to the low momentum region.

## Key claim

The authors find that a small value of the spatial diffusion coefficient at the transition temperature, specifically \(2\pi TD_{s}(T_{c}) \simeq 6\), is preferred by experimental data, but this result is conditional on the specific choice of the intermediate cutoff \(t^{\ast}=1.5 m_{D}^{2}\) and the running coupling scale \(\mu=\pi T\) obtained from a global chi-squared optimization.

## Method

The authors employ a soft-hard factorized model that combines a thermal perturbative description (hard-thermal-loop resummation) for soft scatterings with a perturbative QCD Born approximation for hard collisions to compute the transverse and longitudinal momentum diffusion coefficients \(\kappa_{T}\) and \(\kappa_{L}\) for heavy quarks. These coefficients are then used in a Langevin transport equation coupled to a 3+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamic background to simulate heavy quark propagation through the quark-gluon plasma, with hadronization handled via a fragmentation-coalescence approach. The two free parameters of the model, the intermediate cutoff \(t^{\ast}\) and the running coupling scale \(\mu\), are fixed by a comprehensive chi-squared comparison to LHC data on \(D^{0}\), \(D^{+}\), \(D^{\ast+}\), and \(D_{s}^{+}\) meson nuclear modification factor \(R_{\rm AA}\) and elliptic flow \(v_{2}\) at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76\) and \(5.02\) TeV.

## Key numbers

* The optimized model yields a total \(\chi^{2}/d.o.f = 1.3\) with parameters \(|t^{\ast}|=1.5 m_{D}^{2}\) and \(\mu=\pi T\).
* The spatial diffusion coefficient at the critical temperature is found to be \(2\pi TD_{s}(T_{c}) \simeq 6\), with a range \(2\pi TD_{s}(T_{c})\simeq 3-6\) estimated from testing models.
* The model simultaneously describes prompt \(D^{0}\) \(R_{\rm AA}\) and \(v_{2}\) data for transverse momentum \(p_{\rm T}\le 8\) GeV in Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76\) TeV and \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02\) TeV.
* The critical temperature used in the hydrodynamic background is \(T_{c}=165\) MeV, with initial thermalization time \(\tau_{0}=0.6\) fm/\(c\) and shear viscosity \(\eta/s=1/(4\pi)\).

## Context

This work extends previous phenomenological extractions of heavy quark transport coefficients, such as the LGR model (CTGUHybrid4, 2020) and the LIDO approach (2018), by employing a soft-hard factorized framework that explicitly separates soft and hard scattering contributions. The results enable future experimental tests of non-prompt \(D^{0}\) meson production down to low momentum, and the extracted temperature dependence of \(\hat{q}/T^{3}\) and \(2\pi TD_{s}\) provides a data-driven benchmark for comparison with lattice QCD calculations (Brambilla, 2020; Altenkort, 2020) and other theoretical approaches like CUJET3 (2016-2018) and the Catania quasi-particle model.
