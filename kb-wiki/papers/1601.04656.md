# Stability of superfluid vortices in dense quark matter

> Superfluid vortices in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase of dense quark matter are known to be energetically disfavored relative to well-separated triplets of "semi-superfluid" color flux tubes. However, the short-range interaction (metastable versus unstable) has not been established. In this paper we perform numerical calculations using the effective theory of the condensate field, mapping the regions in the parameter space of coupling constants where the vortices are metastable versus unstable. For the case of zero gauge coupling we analytically identify a candidate for the unstable mode, and show that it agrees well with the results of the numerical calculations. We find that in the region of the parameter space that seems likely to correspond to real-world CFL quark matter the vortices are unstable, indicating that if such matter exists in neutron star cores it is very likely to contain semi-superfluid color flux tubes rather than superfluid vortices.

## Key claim

Superfluid vortices in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase of dense quark matter are unstable rather than metastable in the region of parameter space that seems likely to correspond to real-world CFL quark matter, indicating that if such matter exists in neutron star cores it is very likely to contain semi-superfluid color flux tubes rather than superfluid vortices. The authors qualify this by noting that the mean-field weak-coupling calculations used to relate the Ginzburg-Landau couplings to QCD parameters are not valid in the density range of interest for neutron stars.

## Method

The authors solved the classical field equations for the CFL condensate on a two-dimensional lattice using a non-Abelian Ginzburg-Landau effective theory. They generated an exact superfluid vortex solution by transferring the continuum vortex to the lattice and relaxing it via Langevin evolution with damping, then added small perturbations and evolved forward in time to detect exponentially growing unstable modes. They scanned the parameter space of the three couplings (gauge coupling g, scalar couplings λ and λ₂) to map regions where vortices are unstable versus metastable.

## Key numbers

- For zero gauge coupling, the analytic prediction gives δE₈ = ε²(λ₂ − λ) × (π m⁴/λ²) × ∫₀^∞ r dr β'² β², showing instability when λ > λ₂ (i.e., λ₁ > 0)
- The parameter space scan covered g ∈ [0.01, 1], λ ∈ [0.1, 6], λ₂ ∈ [0.01, 0.5]
- The metastable region vanishes around g ≈ 0.6
- No metastability was found above λ ≈ 0.6
- Using mean-field weak-coupling expressions, λ₁ = λ₂ = λ/4 = (36/7)(π⁴/ζ(3))(T_c/μ)², corresponding to μ ranging from 400 MeV to 500 MeV and T_c ranging from 10 MeV to 15 MeV
- The lattice calculations used m² = 0.25 and lattice spacing a = 1

## Context

This work extends earlier studies showing that superfluid vortices in the CFL phase are energetically disfavored relative to well-separated triplets of semi-superfluid color flux tubes (Balachandran 2005, Nakano 2007), but where the short-range interaction had not been calculated (Eto 2013). The authors use methods analogous to previous calculations for SU(2) Yang-Mills-Higgs theory (Gleiser 2008, Heinz 1996). The results enable future work on measuring vortex lifetimes in metastable regions, studying the effects of entrainment interactions, and applying similar methods to vortices in the color-spin-locked phase and two-flavor color superconducting quark matter.
