# Angle-dependent Gap state in Asymmetric Nuclear Matter

> We propose an axisymmetric angle-dependent gap (ADG) state with the broken rotational symmetry in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter. In this state, the deformed Fermi spheres of neutrons and protons increase the pairing probabilities along the axis of symmetry breaking near the average Fermi surface. We find that the state possesses lower free energy and larger gap value than the angle-averaged gap state at large isospin asymmetries. These properties are mainly caused by the coupling of different m_{j} components of the pairing gap. Furthermore, we find the transition from the ADG state to the normal state is of second order and the ADG state vanishes at the critical isospin asymmetry m_{j} where the angle-averaged gap vanishes.

## Key claim

The authors propose an axisymmetric angle-dependent gap (ADG) state in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter that possesses lower free energy and a larger gap value than the conventional angle-averaged gap (AAG) state, but only at large isospin asymmetries and low temperatures; at high temperatures or small asymmetries the angle-averaging procedure remains a satisfactory approximation.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled Gorkov gap equations for the isospin-singlet \(^{3}SD_{1}\) pairing channel in asymmetric nuclear matter at fixed density \(\rho = \rho_0 = 0.17\ \text{fm}^{-3}\), using the Argonne \(V_{18}\) potential as the bare pairing interaction and a free single-particle spectrum. They compare two approaches: the standard angle-averaged gap (AAG) state, where the angular dependence of the quasiparticle energy denominator is neglected, and the axisymmetric angle-dependent gap (ADG) state, where only the \(m_j = 0\) components of the gap are retained, yielding an angle-dependent \(D^2(k,\theta)\) and an angle matrix that couples the \(^{3}S_1\) and \(^{3}D_1\) gap components.

## Key numbers

- At temperature \(\beta^{-1} = 0.5\ \text{MeV}\) and isospin asymmetry \(\alpha \geq 0.07\), the value of \(\Delta_0(k_F)\) in the ADG state becomes larger than in the AAG state; the difference reaches 22 percent at \(\alpha = 0.23\).
- The critical isospin asymmetries \(\alpha_c\) where the gaps vanish are identical for both states: 0.267, 0.275, 0.30, and 0.315 at temperatures \(\beta^{-1} = 0.5\), 1.0, 2.0, and 3.0 MeV, respectively.
- At \(\beta^{-1} = 0.5\ \text{MeV}\) and \(\alpha > 0.17\), the free-energy difference \(\delta F\) in the ADG state is about 35 percent lower than in the AAG state.
- The critical temperature for the isospin-symmetric case is about \(\beta^{-1}_c = 7.5\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron-proton pairing in asymmetric nuclear matter, where the FFLO state (Fulde and Ferrell 1964; Larkin and Ovchinnikov 1965) and the deformed Fermi surfaces (DFS) state were investigated. The authors show that the commonly used angle-averaging procedure, which was a good approximation in symmetric nuclear matter (Alm et al. 1996), becomes insufficient at large isospin asymmetries and low temperatures. The ADG state provides a more realistic description of pairing in neutron star matter and supernova environments where isospin asymmetry is significant, and the results enable future studies incorporating more realistic single-particle spectra and screening effects.
