# Recent Developments in Lattice QCD

> Recent trends in lattice QCD calculations and latest results are reviewed, with particular emphasis on multiquark-states and the nucleon structure.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD has made significant progress in handling light quark masses, dynamical sea quarks, and all-to-all propagators, but simultaneous treatment of all three challenges remains years away, and chiral extrapolations of nucleon structure observables (e.g., \(\langle x\rangle_{u-d}\)) still show no sign of bending toward the chiral limit even at \(m_\pi \approx 300\) MeV.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD simulations that regularise the theory on a discrete spacetime lattice with spacing \(a\) and match the QCD coupling and quark masses to reproduce \(n_f+1\) hadron masses. It summarises results from multiple collaborations using both Wilson-type and chiral overlap fermions, with dynamical (sea) quarks (\(n_f=2\)) and in the quenched approximation, for studies of multiquark spectroscopy (pentaquarks, tetraquarks) and nucleon structure (moments of parton distributions, electromagnetic transition form factors).

## Key numbers

- String breaking distance in the static \(Q\bar{Q}\) system with \(n_f=2\) and \(m_q \approx m_s\): \(r_c \approx 1.16\) fm after extrapolation to physical light quark masses.
- Energy gap between the two lowest states in the string breaking region: \(\Delta E \approx 50\) MeV, resolved with a statistical significance of 10 standard deviations.
- Pion mass dependence of the algorithmic cost for dynamical simulations: \(\propto 1/(m_\pi a)^{\simeq 3}\) for fixed volume, and \(\propto 1/(m_\pi a)^{\simeq 7}\) when keeping \(m_\pi L\) fixed.
- The moment \(\langle x\rangle_{u-d}\) of the proton quark distribution is overestimated by almost 60% if linearly extrapolated in \(m_\pi^2\).
- For the \(\gamma^* N \rightarrow \Delta\) transition, the ratio \(R_{EM} = G_{E2}/G_{M1}\) is fairly constant at \(-0.02(1)\) after chiral extrapolation; \(R_{CM} = G_{C2}/G_{M1}\) decreases from \(-0.01(1)\) at \(Q^2 = 0.1\) GeV\(^2\) to \(-0.09(3)\) at \(Q^2 > 1\) GeV\(^2\).
- The \(\Theta^+(1530)\) pentaquark candidate has a mass about 100 MeV above the \(KN\) threshold and a width \(\Gamma \ll 10\) MeV.

## Context

This review extends the line of lattice QCD calculations that began with the demonstration of confinement and chiral symmetry breaking in the 1990s, and it updates the status of calculations of nucleon structure (e.g., moments of parton distributions from the QCDSF, SESAM, and LHP Collaborations) and exotic hadron spectroscopy (pentaquark studies by Csikor et al., Sasaki et al., Chiu et al., Mathur et al., and Ishii et al.). It enables future work on simultaneous light-quark, dynamical-fermion, and all-to-all propagator simulations, and it highlights the need for controlled chiral extrapolations and volume studies to distinguish resonant states from scattering states in multiquark systems.
