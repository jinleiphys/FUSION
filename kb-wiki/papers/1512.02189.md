# Hydrodynamic simulations of relativistic heavy-ion collisions with different lattice QCD calculations of the equation of state

> Hydrodynamic calculations of ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions are performed using the iEBE-VISHNU 2+1D code with fluctuating initial conditions and three different parameterizations of the Lattice QCD equations of state: continuum extrapolations for stout and HISQ/tree actions, as well as the s95p-v1 parameterization based upon calculations using the p4 action. All parameterizations are matched to a hadron resonance gas equation of state at T = 155 MeV, at which point the calculations are continued using the UrQMD hadronic cascade. Calculations for $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV Au+Au collisions in three centrality classes are compared to experimental data for final state particle spectra and anisotropic flow coefficients $v_2$ and $v_3$ as well as for pion HBT radii. Experimental observables for the stout and HISQ/tree equations of state are observed to differ by less than a few percent for all observables, while the s95p-v1 equation of state generates spectra and flow coefficients which differ by ~10-20%. Calculations in which the HISQ/tree equation of state is sampled from the published error distribution are also observed to differ by less than a few percent.

## Key claim

The stout and HISQ/tree lattice QCD equations of state produce hydrodynamic observables that differ by less than a few percent for all studied quantities, while the older s95p-v1 parameterization generates spectra and flow coefficients that differ by approximately 10-20% from the modern continuum-extrapolated results, with the caveat that all parameterizations are matched to a hadron resonance gas equation of state at T = 155 MeV before comparison.

## Method

The authors used the iEBE-VISHNU 2+1D boost-invariant viscous hydrodynamic code with fluctuating two-component Glauber initial conditions, coupled to the UrQMD hadronic cascade at a switching temperature of 155 MeV. They embedded three different lattice QCD equations of state (continuum-extrapolated stout from Wuppertal-Budapest, continuum-extrapolated HISQ/tree from HotQCD, and the older s95p-v1 parameterization from p4 action calculations) into the same hydrodynamic framework and compared the resulting particle spectra, anisotropic flow coefficients v2 and v3, and pion HBT radii for sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions across multiple centrality classes.

## Key numbers

- The stout and HISQ/tree equations of state produce observables that differ by less than a few percent for all studied quantities
- The s95p-v1 equation of state generates spectra and flow coefficients that differ by approximately 10-20% from the modern continuum-extrapolated results
- The S95 equation of state produces approximately 5% more particles at p_T = 0.5 GeV and approximately 20% fewer particles at p_T = 2.5 GeV compared to the HQ result across all three centralities
- The S95 parameterization generates approximately 5% less elliptic flow than the HQ equation of state across all centralities
- The S95 equation of state produces up to approximately 15% less triangular flow than the HQ equation of state in peripheral collisions
- The value of v3{2} calculated using the S95 equation of state is 12.6% smaller than the same calculation performed with the HQ equation of state
- Errors in the HotQCD continuum extrapolation account for order 1% differences in mean p_T, v2 and v3
- The binary collision fraction used in initial conditions is alpha = 0.14
- The specific shear viscosity is fixed at eta/s = 0.08 with vanishing bulk viscosity zeta/s = 0
- The hydro-to-micro switching temperature is T_sw = 155 MeV
- The matching temperature for the HRG is T_1 = 155 MeV with blending region extending to T_2 = 180 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier sensitivity studies on the influence of the equation of state on hydrodynamic simulations, which were limited to studies of the order of the phase transition by Huovinen (2005), different parameterization schemes by Huovinen (2009), and data-driven Bayesian techniques by Pratt (2015) and Sangaline (2015). The present study is the first to quantify the effect of inherent lattice QCD errors on hydrodynamic simulations, made possible by the recent availability of continuum extrapolations for the lattice QCD equation of state at zero baryon density from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Borsanyi, 2013) and the HotQCD collaboration (Bazavov, 2014). The results enable future Bayesian analyses to incorporate realistic lattice QCD error estimates when constraining the QCD equation of state from experimental data.
