# Search for dibaryon resonances in the reactions $dd\to ddππ$ and $pd \to pdππ$

> The isoscalar dibaryon resonance $D_{03}(2380)$, discovered at WASA@COSY in the total cross section of the reaction $pn\to dπ^0π^0$ and in the elastic pn scattering in the energy range corresponding to the invariant mass of the pn system of 2380 MeV, can reveal itself in the $pd\to pdππ$ and $dd\to ddππ$ reactions at higher energies due to deuteron excitation in the $t$ channel. In this paper, the cross sections of these reactions are estimated based on the model of the reaction $pn\to dπ^0π^0$, proposed earlier by Platonova and Kukulin.

# Search for dibaryon resonances in the reactions $dd\to dd\pi\pi$ and $pd \to pd\pi\pi$

## Key claim

The isoscalar dibaryon resonance $D_{03}(2380)$ can be observed in $pd\to pd\pi\pi$ and $dd\to dd\pi\pi$ reactions at SPD NICA energies via t-channel excitation of the deuteron through $\sigma$-meson exchange, even though s-channel production is impossible at these higher collision energies; however, the cross section for $dd\to dd\pi\pi$ is suppressed by one to two orders of magnitude compared to $pd\to pd\pi\pi$ due to the deuteron elastic form factor.

## Method

The authors calculate differential cross sections using a model where a $\sigma$-meson is exchanged between the beam particle (proton or deuteron) and the target deuteron, exciting the deuteron into the $D_{03}$ resonance. The $D_{03}$ decay includes two summed mechanisms: (i) $D_{03}\to \sigma + d$ followed by $\sigma \to \pi\pi$, and (ii) sequential decays $D_{03}\to \pi_1 + D_{12}$ and $D_{12}\to \pi_2 + d$, following the formalism of Platonova and Kukulin (2012). For the $dd\to dd\pi\pi$ reaction, the amplitude is obtained by multiplying the $pd\to pd\pi\pi$ amplitude by the deuteron elastic form factor $S_d(\Delta/2)$.

## Key numbers

- $D_{03}$ resonance mass: 2.380 GeV, width: 70 MeV
- $D_{12}$ resonance mass: 2.15 GeV, width: 0.11 GeV
- $\sigma$-meson mass: 0.55 GeV, width: 0.5 GeV, coupling $f_\sigma = 2.20$, cutoff $\lambda_\sigma = 1.3$ GeV
- Partial widths used: $\Gamma^{(2)}_{D_{03}\to d\sigma} = 20$ MeV, $\Gamma^{(1)}_{D_{03}\to D_{12}\pi} = 90$ MeV, $\Gamma^{(1)}_{D_{12}\to d\pi} = 10$ MeV
- Vertex form factor parameters: $\lambda_{d\sigma} = 0.18$ GeV, $\lambda_{d\pi} = 0.25$ GeV
- Isospin factor $C_T = 2.8$ for charged and neutral pion pairs
- At $T_p = 1.1$ GeV, the theoretical curve reproduces the position and absolute magnitude of the resonance peak in $pd\to pd\pi\pi$; at $T_p = 1.4$ GeV, the calculation overestimates data by a factor of approximately 1.7
- Calculations performed for $pd$ at proton beam energies 2, 4, 7, and 10 GeV; for $dd$ at $\sqrt{s_{dd}} = 5.4$, 6.64, 8.2, and 9.44 GeV (corresponding to deuteron beam energies 4, 8, 14, and 20 GeV)
- Deuteron scattering angle intervals for $dd\to dd\pi\pi$: $8^\circ-13^\circ$ (forward) and $172^\circ-167^\circ$ (backward)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the $D_{03}(2380)$ dibaryon resonance discovered by WASA@COSY in $pn\to d\pi^0\pi^0$ and elastic pn scattering. The model builds on the reaction mechanism for $pn\to d\pi^0\pi^0$ developed by Platonova and Kukulin (2012, 2021). Earlier attempts to explain ANKE@COSY data on $pd\to pd\pi\pi$ at 1-2 GeV were made by Uzikov (2019) and Tursunbayev (2020). The present work extends these calculations to SPD NICA energies ($\sqrt{s_{dd}} \leq 8$ GeV) and proposes that $dd\to dd\pi\pi$ provides a clean test for isoscalar dibaryons since the final $\pi\pi$ system must be in the isoscalar state due to isospin conservation. The authors note that heavier isoscalar resonances at 2470 MeV and 2630 MeV reported by ELPH (Ishikawa, 2018) and BGOOD (Jude, 2022) are not estimated here due to unknown structure and decay modes.
