# Deep sub-barrier fusion reactions of the light nuclei $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O

> Fusion reactions relevant for the carbon and oxygen burning cycles in highly evolved stars are investigated in a standard approach to fusion. We employ the double folding method to evaluate the ion-ion interactions with Gogny-D1 and M3Y-Paris $n-n$ effective forces. The cross-section evaluation do not indicate a possible hindrance even at the lowest energies under the barrier. Reaction rates at temperatures relevant for the stellar processes are estimated and compared to the traditional and modern extrapolation formulas.

# Deep sub-barrier fusion reactions of the light nuclei \(^{12}\)C and \(^{16}\)O

## Key claim

The authors find that for the light systems \(^{12}\)C+\(^{12}\)C, \(^{12}\)C+\(^{16}\)O, and \(^{16}\)O+\(^{16}\)O, the fusion cross-sections do not exhibit the hindrance phenomenon (a severe reduction of cross sections at deep sub-barrier energies) that has been observed in medium-heavy and heavy systems, even at the lowest energies under the barrier.

## Method

The authors employed the double folding method to compute ion-ion potentials using Gogny-D1 and M3Y-Paris finite-range effective nucleon-nucleon forces, with nuclear densities derived from spherical Hartree-Fock calculations using the Beiner-Lombard density functional. Fusion cross-sections were calculated using the incoming-wave boundary condition, with the \(^{12}\)C+\(^{12}\)C and \(^{12}\)C+\(^{16}\)O reactions treated in the no-coupling approximation and the \(^{16}\)O+\(^{16}\)O reaction requiring the coupled-channel method.

## Key numbers

- The experimental charge r.m.s. radii from the Angeli (2004) compilation are reproduced to better than 0.5% by the Hartree-Fock densities.
- Both the Gogny-D1 and M3Y-Paris forces produce the same barrier height for each reaction, with only small differences in barrier thickness at very low energies.
- The predicted reaction rates are close to the Fowler (1975) extrapolation and are only weakly dependent on the type of finite-range force employed.
- The \(^{12}\)C+\(^{12}\)C cross-section exhibits resonant structures under the barrier, while no maximum in the S-factor is found for any of the three reactions.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous systematic investigations of fusion hindrance (Misicu 2006a, 2006b; Jiang 2006, 2008; Esbensen 2007) from medium-heavy and medium-light systems to the light nuclei relevant for stellar carbon and oxygen burning. The results contradict the conjecture by Jiang (2007) that hindrance could affect stellar reaction rates for these light systems, and instead support the traditional Fowler (1975) extrapolation formulas, implying a speed-up of carbon and oxygen burning in the early phases of hydrostatic burning compared to hindrance-based extrapolations.
