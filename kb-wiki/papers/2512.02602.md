# Thermoelectric coefficients of two-flavor quark matter from the Kubo formalism

> The hot quark matter created in heavy-ion collision experiments can exhibit strong temperature and chemical-potential gradients, which in turn can generate electric fields through thermoelectric effects. In this work, we investigate two relevant thermoelectric coefficients -- the thermopower (Seebeck coefficient) and the Thomson coefficient -- of two-flavor quark matter using the Kubo formalism and the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model as an effective description of dense, finite-temperature QCD. The required two-point equilibrium correlation functions are evaluated using the Matsubara formalism of thermal field theory, applying a 1/Nc expansion to the relevant multi-loop Feynman diagrams. We employ previously derived quark spectral functions obtained from one--meson-exchange diagrams above the Mott transition temperature. Our numerical results show that both thermoelectric coefficients increase approximately linearly with temperature and decrease with increasing chemical potential. We also estimate the magnitude of the electric fields that can be generated in heavy-ion collisions by thermal gradients via the Seebeck effect.

## Method

The authors derive Kubo formulas for thermoelectric transport coefficients from the quantum Liouville equation, then evaluate the required two-point retarded correlation functions of electric and heat currents using the Matsubara (imaginary-time) formalism of thermal field theory. They apply a 1/Nc expansion to select the leading-order (single-loop) Feynman diagrams with fully dressed quark propagators, employing previously derived quark spectral functions obtained from one-meson-exchange diagrams above the Mott transition temperature. The NJL model with scalar-isoscalar and pseudoscalar-isovector four-fermion interactions is used as an effective description of dense, finite-temperature QCD, with parameters Λ=0.65 GeV, m0=5.5 MeV, and G=10.1 GeV−2.

## Key numbers

- The NJL model parameters used: current quark mass m0 = 5.5 MeV, four-fermion coupling G = 10.1 GeV−2, 3-momentum cutoff Λ = 0.65 GeV.
- The elementary charge is taken as e = √(4πα) with α = 1/137.
- Both thermoelectric coefficients (thermopower and Thomson coefficient) increase approximately linearly with temperature and decrease with increasing chemical potential (no explicit numerical values for the coefficients themselves are provided in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends previous NJL-based studies of thermoelectric coefficients in quark matter (Abhishek 2020, Zhang 2022) by employing the Kubo formalism rather than kinetic theory with relaxation-time approximations, providing a fully quantum-statistical treatment applicable to strongly interacting systems. The approach follows the methodology of Harutyunyan (2017) for computing correlation functions in the NJL model but now includes the thermopower and Thomson coefficient alongside thermal conductivity. The results enable estimates of electric fields generated in heavy-ion collisions by thermal gradients via the Seebeck effect, complementing holographic studies (Kim 2015) and kinetic-theory calculations in hadronic matter (Bhatt 2019, Das 2020).
