# Overview of experimental critical point search

> The existence and location of the QCD critical point is an object of vivid experimental and theoretical studies. Rich and beautiful data recorded by experiments at SPS and RHIC allow for a systematic search for the critical point - the search for a non-monotonic dependence of various correlation and fluctuation observables on collision energy and size of colliding nuclei.

## Key claim

The experimental search for the QCD critical point has produced four distinct hints of anomalous fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, but none individually constitutes a definitive discovery; the interpretation of these anomalies as critical-point signals remains model-dependent and requires further confirmation from upcoming data.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental results from the SPS (NA49, NA61/SHINE) and RHIC (STAR, PHENIX) collaborations that search for non-monotonic dependencies of correlation and fluctuation observables on collision energy and system size. The observables examined include multiplicity fluctuations, net-proton cumulants, net-kaon and net-charge fluctuations, femtoscopic HBT radii, Lévy source parameters, factorial moments as a function of momentum bin size, and light-nuclei production ratios.

## Key numbers

- Net-proton fourth-order fluctuation \(\kappa\sigma^{2}\) in 5% most central Au+Au collisions shows a non-monotonic dependence that may indicate a critical point around \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 7\) GeV (STAR, Adamczyk 2013)
- Gaussian emission source radii (\(R^{2}_{out} - R^{2}_{side}\)) from Au+Au (STAR, \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\)–200 GeV) and Pb+Pb (ALICE, \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV) show a maximum at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 47.5\) GeV (Lacey 2014)
- Finite-Size Scaling analysis suggests critical point position at \(T = 165\) MeV and \(\mu = 95\) MeV (Lacey 2014)
- Lévy exponent \(\alpha \approx 1.2\) measured in Be+Be at 17 GeV (NA61/SHINE, Porfy 2018) and Au+Au at 200 GeV (PHENIX, Adare 2017), significantly above the critical-point prediction of \(\alpha = 0.5\)
- Nucleon density fluctuation \(\Delta n\) from central Pb+Pb (NA49, Anticic 2016) and central Au+Au (STAR, Adamczyk 2017; Yu 2017) shows a peak at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 20\) GeV

## Context

This overview extends the critical-point search program initiated by theoretical work on QCD phase transitions (Asakawa 1989, Barducci 1989) and fluctuation signatures (Stephanov 2007). It summarizes results from the NA49, NA61/SHINE, STAR, PHENIX, and ALICE collaborations, comparing experimental findings to predictions from Stephanov (2011) and Lacey (2014). The four anomalous energy regions (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 7, 17, 20, 47\) GeV) provide candidate locations for the critical point that will be tested by upcoming data from NA61/SHINE at SPS and STAR Beam Energy Scan II at RHIC.
