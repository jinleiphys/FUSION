# Kilonova Emission from Neutron Star Mergers with Different Equations of State

> Kilonova is an optical-infrared transient powered by the radioactive decay of heavy nuclei from binary neutron star mergers. Its observational characteristics depend on the mass and the nuclide composition of meger ejecta, which are sensitive to the equation of state (EoS) of neutron star. We use astrophysical conditions derived from different EoSs as nucleosynthesis inputs to explore the impact of various EoS on the $r$-process nucleosynthesis and the kilonova emission. Our results show that both the abundance patterns of merger ejecta and kilonova light curves are strongly dependent on the neutron star EoSs. Given the mass of two neutron stars, the merger with a softer EoS tends to generate a larger amount of ejected material, and may lead to a brighter kilonova peak luminosity. The relationship between the neutron star EoS and the peak luminosity provides a probe for constraining the properties of EoS in multi-messenger observations of neutron star mergers.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the neutron star equation of state (EoS) strongly influences both the r-process abundance patterns and kilonova light curves from binary neutron star mergers, with softer EoSs producing brighter kilonova peak luminosities. The authors qualify that this relationship provides a probe for constraining EoS properties only when the masses of the two neutron stars are specified, typically requiring gravitational wave observations.

## Method
The authors used the nuclear reaction network SkyNet to perform r-process nucleosynthesis simulations, taking astrophysical inputs from numerical relativity simulations for four EoSs (BHBlp, DD2, LS220, SFHo) provided by Radice et al. (2018). They calculated kilonova light curves by dividing the ejected material into multiple layers with a density profile, evolving the thermal energy with radioactive heating from the ENDF/B-VIII.0 decay database and a thermalization efficiency formula from Barnes et al. (2016). The electron fraction was determined using an analytical formula fitted by Nedora et al. (2022) as a function of mass ratio and reduced tidal deformability.

## Key numbers
- The characteristic radius R_{1.35} for a 1.35 solar mass neutron star: SFHo = 11.92 km, LS220 = 12.64 km, BHBlp = 13.21 km, DD2 = 13.21 km
- In the F200W band, peak luminosity with soft EoS (SFHo) is higher than with stiff EoS (DD2) by a factor of approximately 2.4
- In the F444W band, peak luminosity with SFHo exceeds DD2 by a factor of approximately 3.7
- Across 40 distinct EoSs, the kilonova flux calculated with the softest EoS exceeds that with the stiffest EoS by a factor of approximately 3.13
- The distance to the source for the light curve calculations was set to 200 Mpc

## Context
This work extends the semi-analytical approach of Zhu et al. (2023) by incorporating detailed r-process nucleosynthesis simulations rather than using simplified models, thereby including the impact of EoS on heavy element composition, nuclear heating rates, and radiative transfer. The results are consistent with numerical relativity simulations by Bauswein et al. (2013), Hotokezaka et al. (2013), and Radice et al. (2018) showing that softer EoSs produce more ejecta. The findings enable future multi-messenger analyses combining gravitational wave observations from LIGO/Virgo with kilonova observations from JWST to constrain the neutron star EoS, with the authors noting that the observed kilonova AT2017gfo appears brighter than their calculated results, supporting a soft EoS consistent with neutron star radii less than approximately 13 km from GW170817 analyses.
