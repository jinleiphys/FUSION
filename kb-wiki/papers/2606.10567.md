# Simultaneous $αβ$ Decay: A New Mode of Nuclear Instability

> We propose simultaneous $αβ$ decay as a novel mode of nuclear instability that involves the strong and weak interactions in a single quantum transition. We develop a theoretical framework to predict its branching ratios and $α$-energy spectra, establishing exclusive and inclusive criteria based on whether the individual $α$ and $β$ channels are closed or open. A global survey of the nuclear chart identifies five exclusive $αβ^-$ candidates, all predicted to be experimentally inaccessible, and ranks the leading inclusive candidates for both the $αβ^-$ and $αβ^+$ modes. Remarkably, six of the top $αβ^+$ candidates coincide with known $β$-delayed-$α$ precursors. The observed $α$ spectra are naturally accounted for by simultaneous $αβ^+$ emission, suggesting direct decay as the dominant underlying mechanism. Our findings establish simultaneous $αβ$ decay as a distinct radioactive process and a sensitive probe of the interplay between the strong and weak interactions.

## Key claim
Simultaneous αβ decay is proposed as a distinct nuclear decay mode involving strong and weak interactions in a single quantum transition, but the paper hedges that for the exclusive case (where individual α and β channels are closed) all five identified candidates are predicted to be experimentally inaccessible, with half-lives far beyond detection capabilities. For inclusive candidates, the paper suggests that phenomena previously interpreted as sequential β-delayed-α emission may instead be dominated by simultaneous αβ⁺ decay, though it states no conclusive evidence can yet distinguish between the two interpretations.

## Method
The authors develop a second-order perturbation theory framework where the transition amplitude involves virtual intermediate nuclear states connecting the α-emission operator and the weak-interaction operator. They calculate the differential decay width as a function of α-particle energy by integrating over the four-body final-state phase space, using the saddle-point approximation to find the peak energy where the product of α-barrier penetrability and leptonic phase-space factor is maximized. A global survey of the nuclear chart is performed using available atomic mass data to identify candidates satisfying exclusive criteria (Qαβ > 0, Qα < 0, Qβ < 0) and inclusive criteria (Qαβ > 0 only), with relative branching ratios estimated under the assumption of equal effective reduced widths across candidates.

## Context
This work extends the study of exotic multiparticle decay modes beyond identical-particle emissions such as 2ν2β and 0ν2β decay (Gomez-Cadenas, 2023), 2n decay (PhysRevLett.110.152501, 2013), 2p decay (Blank, 2008; Pfutzner, 2023), and 2α decay (PhysRevLett.127.012501, 2021) to simultaneous transitions involving two distinct decay modes. The paper directly challenges the conventional interpretation of β-delayed-α emission data from the 1970s and 1980s (Tidemand-Petersson, 1982; 1985), where statistical-model calculations required three phenomenological modifications to match observed Gaussian-like α spectra. The simultaneous-emission framework reproduces these spectra without free parameters, suggesting that future high-precision decay spectroscopy and microscopic many-body calculations are needed to distinguish between sequential and direct mechanisms.
