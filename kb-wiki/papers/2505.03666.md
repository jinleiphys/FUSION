# A Centrality-independent Framework for Revealing Genuine Higher-Order Cumulants in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We propose a novel centrality definition-independent method for analyzing higher-order cumulants, specifically addressing the challenge of volume fluctuations that dominate in low-energy heavy-ion collisions. This method reconstructs particle number distributions using the Edgeworth expansion, with parameters optimized via a combination of differential evolution algorithm and Bayesian inference. Its effectiveness is validated using UrQMD model simulations and benchmarked against traditional approaches, including centrality definitions based on particle multiplicity. Our results show that the proposed framework yields cumulant patterns consistent with those obtained using number of participant nucleon ($N_{\text{part}}$) based centrality observables, while eliminating the conventional reliance on centrality determination. This consistency confirms the method's ability to extract genuine physical signals, thereby paving the way for probing the intrinsic thermodynamic properties of the produced medium through event-by-event fluctuations.

# A Centrality-independent Framework for Revealing Genuine Higher-Order Cumulants in Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The proposed centrality-definition-independent method, which reconstructs particle number distributions via Edgeworth expansion with parameters optimized by differential evolution and Bayesian inference, yields cumulant patterns consistent with those obtained using $N_{\text{part}}$-based centrality observables in UrQMD simulations of Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.5$ GeV, but only when physics-constrained optimization (including polynomial relationships among cumulants and slope constraints) is applied; without such constraints, the method may produce multiple solutions due to the multi-modal nature of the unconstrained model.

## Method

The authors used UrQMD model simulations of Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.5$ GeV to test their framework. Proton number distributions were reconstructed using the Edgeworth expansion, with cumulants parameterized as polynomial functions of the first cumulant $C_1$ (e.g., $C_2$ as a quadratic function of $C_1$, $C_3/C_2$ and $C_4/C_2$ as quadratic functions of $C_1$). Parameter optimization proceeded in two stages: first, a differential evolution algorithm provided initial parameter values; second, Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) sampling within a Bayesian inference framework refined the estimates, incorporating physics-informed priors including slope constraints between cumulant ratios and a 5% constraint on $C_3$ variation for the most central collisions. The method was validated against four different centrality resolution configurations (RefMult3 with and without scaling, RefSpect3 with and without scaling).

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.5$ GeV from UrQMD model; proton cumulants measured within rapidity $-0.5 < y < 0$, pseudorapidity $0 < \eta < 2.4$, and transverse momentum $0.4 < p_T < 2.0$ GeV/$c$.
- Centrality range of 0-50% with 2% centrality intervals (25 bins total).
- $C_1$ parameterized as a fourth-order polynomial of centrality bin index; $C_2$ as a quadratic polynomial of $C_1$ with coefficients $a_{c_2}$ in $[-0.2, 0]$, $b_{c_2}$ in $[1.15, 1.25]$, $c_{c_2}$ in $[-0.01, 0]$.
- $C_3/C_2$ as a quadratic polynomial of $C_1$ with $a_{c_3/c_2}$ in $[1, 1.2]$, $b_{c_3/c_2}$ in $[-0.01, 0]$, $c_{c_3/c_2}$ in $[-0.0001, 0.0001]$.
- $C_4/C_2$ as a quadratic polynomial of $C_1$ with $a_{c_4/c_2}$ in $[1, 2]$, $b_{c_4/c_2}$ in $[-0.1, 0]$, $c_{c_4/c_2}$ in $[-0.0001, 0.0001]$.
- $C_1$ parameterization coefficients: $a_{c_1}$ in $[0.00004, 0.001]$, $b_{c_1}$ in $[-0.01, 0]$, $c_{c_1}$ in $[0.01, 0.1]$, $d_{c_1}$ in $[0.1, 1]$, $e_{c_1}$ in $[4, 4.6]$.
- Constraint applied: variation of $C_3$ for the most central collision limited to within 5%.
- Scaling factors of 0.7 and 0.8 applied to charged particle multiplicities for RefMult3 and RefSpect3 respectively to test different centrality resolutions.
- Data range for reconstruction: proton number greater than 18 (to avoid Edgeworth expansion discrepancies in the left tail of peripheral distributions).
- Uncertainty estimation: $\delta_{total} = \sqrt{\delta_{sys.}^2 + \delta_{stat.}^2}$, with statistical uncertainty from Bootstrap resampling and systematic uncertainty from variations in constraint tightness and distribution boundaries.

## Context

This work extends the Centrality Bin Width Correction (CBWC) method introduced by Luo (2013) and Volume Fluctuation Correction (VFC) approaches that have been widely applied in STAR data analyses (STAR 2021, 2022, 2025). The proposed framework addresses the problem that initial volume fluctuations (IVF) are especially prominent in low-energy heavy-ion collisions and can significantly distort higher-order cumulant measurements, potentially obscuring critical behavior and phase transition signatures. The method enables future extraction of cumulants unaffected by initial volume fluctuations, with subsequent efficiency corrections, for probing the intrinsic thermodynamic properties of the hot dense QCD medium through event-by-event fluctuations, and can be extended to study net-proton number fluctuations, baryon-strangeness ($B-S$) correlations, and baryon-charge ($B-Q$) correlations.
