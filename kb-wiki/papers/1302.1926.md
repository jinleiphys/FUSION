# Time-dependent density-functional studies on strength functions in neutron-rich nuclei

> The electric dipole (E1) strength functions have been systematically calculated based on the time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT), using the finite amplitude method and the real-time approach to the TDDFT with pairing correlations. The low-energy E1 strengths in neutron-rich isotopes show peculiar behaviors, such as sudden enhancement and reduction, as functions of the neutron numbers.They seem to be due to the interplay between the neutron shell effect and the deformation effect.

## Key claim

The low-energy E1 strength in neutron-rich nuclei shows sudden enhancement and reduction as a function of neutron number, which the authors attribute to the interplay between neutron shell effects and ground-state deformation effects, rather than to neutron-skin modes.

## Method

The authors performed systematic calculations of electric dipole (E1) strength functions using two complementary approaches based on time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT): the finite amplitude method (FAM) for lighter nuclei without pairing correlations, and the real-time canonical-basis time-dependent Bogoliubov-de-Gennes-Kohn-Sham (TDBdGKS) method for heavier nuclei where pairing is expected to play a role. The calculations used the SkM* energy density functional and were applied to even-even Ca and Sr isotopes across a range of neutron numbers.

## Key numbers

- For Ca isotopes with N=20-28, negligible E1 strengths appear below E=10 MeV; for N=30-40, sizable E1 strengths appear below 10 MeV, indicating a sudden onset beyond the magic number N=28.
- The ratio of integrated energy-weighted E1 strength up to 10 MeV to the total sum-rule value is less than 1% for Sr isotopes with N ≤ 50, then jumps up beyond N=50.
- Sudden drops in the low-energy E1 ratio occur at N=58→60 and N=72→74 in Sr isotopes, coinciding with ground-state shape transitions.
- The shape transition at N=58→60 is from spherical (β=0) to prolate (β=0.37), and at N=72→74 from prolate (β=0.34, γ=0) to oblate (β=0.14, γ=60°).

## Context

This work extends systematic TDDFT calculations of E1 strength functions to neutron-rich isotopes, building on prior studies of low-energy dipole modes in O isotopes (Leistenschneider, Tryggestad), 26Ne (Gibelin), and Sn isotopes (Govaert, Adrich, Ozel, Klimkiewicz, Endres 2010). The results identify specific magic numbers (N=14→16, N=28→30, N=50→52) where low-energy E1 strength suddenly appears, and demonstrate that deformation changes can suppress this strength even in very neutron-rich nuclei. The finding that K=0 dominance in prolate nuclei cannot be attributed to anisotropic neutron-skin thickness contradicts the interpretation of relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation calculations for Sn isotopes (Piekarewicz et al. 2009), suggesting these low-energy strengths are not skin modes.
