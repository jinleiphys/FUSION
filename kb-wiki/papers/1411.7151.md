# Shadow poles in a coupled-channel problem calculated with Berggren basis

> In coupled-channel models the poles of the scattering S-matrix are located on different Riemann sheets. Physical observables are affected mainly by poles closest to the physical region but sometimes shadow poles have considerable effect, too. The purpose of this paper is to show that in coupled-channel problem all poles of the S-matrix can be calculated with properly constructed complex-energy basis. The Berggren basis is used for expanding the coupled-channel solutions. The location of the poles of the S-matrix were calculated and compared with an exactly solvable coupled-channel problem: the one with the Cox potential. We show that with appropriately chosen Berggren basis poles of the S-matrix including the shadow ones can be determined.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that by properly constructing Berggren bases with complex-energy contours tailored to specific Riemann sheets, all poles of the S-matrix in a coupled-channel problem, including shadow poles on non-adjacent sheets, can be determined numerically, with the caveat that the contour shape must be chosen according to which Riemann sheet's poles are sought.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled-channel Schrödinger equation by expanding the wave functions in Berggren bases constructed separately for each channel from the diagonal potentials. The Berggren basis for each channel includes resonant states (bound, virtual, and resonance solutions) whose wave numbers lie within a chosen complex contour, plus discretized scattering states along that contour. The Hamiltonian is diagonalized in the resulting bi-orthonormal basis to obtain complex eigenvalues, which are identified as the poles of the S-matrix on the Riemann sheet corresponding to the chosen contour shapes in the complex k and p planes.

## Key numbers

* For the Cox potential with parameters Δ=1 and β=0.1, the exact resonance energy E₁=0.4−i0.01 (with wave numbers k₁=0.632550−i0.007905, p₁=−0.006455+i0.774624) was reproduced numerically as E=0.400047−i0.0100011 using Berggren bases.
* The exact anti-bound state on the second Riemann sheet at E₃=−0.5604738 was reproduced as −0.561467−i0.494×10⁻⁷ (with the unperturbed anti-bound state included in the basis) and as −0.561008−i0.413×10⁻³ (without including the unperturbed anti-bound state).
* The shadow pole on the third Riemann sheet at exact energy E₄=−0.5995714 was reproduced as −0.600357−i0.149×10⁻⁴.
* For the single-channel test with β=0 and κ=1, the numerical wave numbers for bound and anti-bound states agreed with exact values to within 0.0005 or better for α₁ values from −0.4 to 0.7; the largest deviation was Im(k)=−0.699528 versus exact −0.7 for α₁=0.7.

## Context

This work extends the use of Berggren bases from single-channel problems (Berggren, 1968) and previous coupled-channel applications (Michel et al., 2008; Id Betan, 2008) to the systematic location of all S-matrix poles including shadow poles, which were previously studied in nuclear physics by Hale et al. (1987) for the ⁵He system and by Csótó et al. (1993) using complex scaling. The method is validated against the exactly solvable Cox potential (Cox, 1964; Pupasov et al., 2008) and enables the calculation of poles on any Riemann sheet in coupled-channel models, which is relevant for understanding the effect of shadow poles on scattering observables in nuclear, atomic, and particle physics.
