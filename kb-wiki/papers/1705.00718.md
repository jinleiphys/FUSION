# Polyakov loop modeling for hot QCD

> We review theoretical aspects of quantum chromodynamics (QCD) at finite temperature. The most important physical variable to characterize hot QCD is the Polyakov loop, which is an approximate order parameter for quark deconfinement in a hot gluonic medium. Additionally to its role as an order parameter, the Polyakov loop has rich physical contents in both perturbative and non-perturbative sectors. This review covers a wide range of subjects associated with the Polyakov loop from topological defects in hot QCD to model building with coupling to the Polyakov loop.

## Key claim

The Polyakov loop serves as an approximate order parameter for quark deconfinement in hot QCD, but the review emphasizes that it is not a true order parameter for gluon confinement because gluons in the adjoint representation are insensitive to the center symmetry of the gauge group.

## Method

The authors review the quantization of QCD at finite temperature using the imaginary-time formalism, deriving the Polyakov loop from the free energy of a static test quark. They then analyze center symmetry breaking as the mechanism for deconfinement, develop strong coupling expansions on the lattice to derive effective Polyakov loop potentials, and discuss both perturbative and non-perturbative approaches including caloron configurations and chiral model extensions.

## Key numbers

- The critical coupling for the SU(2) second-order phase transition occurs at J_c = 1/6 in the strong coupling effective model.
- The critical coupling for the SU(3) first-order phase transition occurs at J_c approximately 0.773 in the strong coupling effective model.
- The string tension in the strong coupling limit is sigma = a^{-2} ln(g^2 N_c) with a being the lattice spacing.
- The nearest neighbor interaction strength in the effective Polyakov loop model is J = N_c^2 exp(-beta sigma a).

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on center symmetry and phase transitions by Svetitsky (1985) and the classic finite-temperature QCD formulation by Gross, Pisarski, and Yaffe (1981). It provides a comprehensive semi-analytical treatment of Polyakov loop physics that complements lattice QCD reviews by Ding (2015) and Ratti (2016), enabling further development of Polyakov loop augmented chiral effective models such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and the quark-meson model for studying hot QCD matter and the sign problem at finite density.
