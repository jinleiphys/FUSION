# Self-consistent statistical error analysis of $ππ$ scattering

> We analyze the conditions under which a statistical error analysis can be carried out in the case of $ππ$ scattering, namely the normality of residuals in the conventional $χ^2$-fit method. Here we check that the current and benchmarking analyses only present very small violations of the normality requirements. In particular, we show how it is possible to amend slightly the selection of the experimental data, and improve the normality of residuals. As an example, we discuss the $0^{++}$ channel and the implications for the $f_0(500)$ and $f_0(980)$ resonances, obtaining that the new selection of data provides very similar and compatible results. In addition, the effect on the $f_0(500)$ and $f_0(980)$ resonance pole parameters is almost negligible, which reinforces the central results and the uncertainty analysis performed in these benchmarking determinations.

## Key claim

The paper checks whether the residuals from the standard \(\chi^2\)-fit method in \(\pi\pi\) scattering satisfy the normality condition required for valid statistical error propagation, finding that current benchmarking analyses show only very small violations; by slightly amending the selection of experimental data, normality can be improved without significantly altering the central results or uncertainty estimates for the \(f_0(500)\) and \(f_0(980)\) resonance pole parameters.

## Method

The authors applied normality tests (Pearson, Kolmogorov-Smirnov, Tail-Sensitive, and moment analysis) to the residuals of the Unconstrained Fit to Data (UFD) for the S0 wave of \(\pi\pi\) scattering, using the Madrid-Krakow group's parametrizations for the low-energy conformal fit, intermediate-energy polynomial fit, and inelasticity fit. They then performed a \(3\sigma\) self-consistent fit by iteratively discarding data points with residuals beyond \(3\sigma\) and re-evaluating the normality of the remaining residuals.

## Key numbers

* Low-energy conformal fit: \(N = 30\) data points, \(P = 4\) parameters, expected \(\chi^2/\nu = 1 \pm 0.28\) at \(1\sigma\).
* Intermediate-energy polynomial fit: \(N = 61\) data points, \(P = 5\) parameters, expected \(\chi^2/\nu = 1 \pm 0.19\) at \(1\sigma\).
* Inelasticity fit: \(N = 28\) data points, \(P = 4\) parameters, expected \(\chi^2/\nu = 1 \pm 0.28\) at \(1\sigma\).
* Combined expected \(\chi^2/\nu = 1 \pm 0.14\) for the total fit.
* For the Kolmogorov-Smirnov test at large \(N\), critical values are given as \(T_c = a/\sqrt{N}\) with \(a = 1.63\) at \(\alpha = 0.01\), \(a = 1.36\) at \(\alpha = 0.05\), and \(a = 1.22\) at \(\alpha = 0.10\).
* For the Tail-Sensitive test for \(N > 50\), critical values are parametrized as \(T_c = a/\sqrt{N} + b\) for significance levels \(\alpha = 0.2, 0.1, 0.05, 0.02, 0.01\).

## Context

This work extends the Madrid-Krakow \(\pi\pi\) scattering analyses (Pelaez 2004, Kaminski 2006, GarciaMartin 2011) by adding a statistical self-consistency check that had been overlooked in previous studies, despite the painstaking error reduction elsewhere. The results support the robustness of the resonance pole determinations from GarciaMartin (2011) and GarciaMartin (2011jx), and the methodology enables future comprehensive analyses based on solving Roy and Roy-like equations with dispersion relations and sum rules.
