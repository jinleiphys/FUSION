# Radiative corrections to $τ\toππν_τ$

> Hadronic $τ$ decays present an opportunity to determine the isovector part of the hadronic-vacuum-polarization contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon in a way complementary to $e^+e^-\to\text{hadrons}$ cross sections. However, the required isospin rotation is only exact in the isospin limit, and corrections need to be under control to draw robust conclusions, most notably for $τ\toππν_τ$ decays to determine the two-pion contribution, $a_μ^\text{HVP, LO}[ππ,τ]$. In this work, we present a novel analysis of the required radiative corrections using dispersion relations, thereby extending in a model-independent way the previous analysis in chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) beyond the threshold region. In particular, we include the dominant structure-dependent virtual corrections from pion-pole diagrams, leading to sizable changes in the vicinity of the $ρ(770)$ resonance. Moreover, we work out the matching to ChPT and devise a strategy for a stable numerical evaluation of real-emission contributions near the two-pion threshold, which proves important to capture isospin-breaking corrections enhanced by the threshold singularity. For the numerical analysis, we use a dispersive representation of the pion form factor including the $ρ'$, $ρ''$ resonances, perform fits to the available data sets for the $τ\toππν_τ$ spectral function, and calculate the corresponding radiative correction factor $G_\text{EM}(s)$ in a self-consistent manner. Based on these results, we evaluate the $τ$-specific isospin-breaking corrections to $a_μ^\text{HVP, LO}[ππ,τ]$.

## Key claim
The paper presents a novel dispersive analysis of radiative corrections to τ→ππν_τ decays, extending previous chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) calculations beyond the threshold region in a model-independent way, with the claim that structure-dependent virtual corrections from pion-pole diagrams lead to sizable changes near the ρ(770) resonance.

## Method
The authors calculate the radiative correction factor G_EM(s) using dispersion relations, dressing the triangle/box diagram vertices with the pion vector form factor (VFF) via unsubtracted dispersion relations. They match the dispersive calculation to ChPT at low energies to establish a basis for short-distance contributions and lattice QCD, and devise a strategy for stable numerical evaluation of real-emission contributions near the two-pion threshold. For the numerical analysis, they use a dispersive representation of the pion form factor including ρ' and ρ'' resonances, perform fits to τ→ππν_τ spectral function data from Belle, ALEPH, CLEO, and OPAL, and calculate G_EM(s) self-consistently.

## Key numbers
- The experimental value of the muon anomalous magnetic moment: a_μ^exp = 11,659,207.15(1.45) × 10^{-10} (124 parts per billion precision)
- The Standard Model prediction: a_μ^SM = 11,659,203.3(6.2) × 10^{-10} (uncertainty about four times larger than experimental precision)
- The short-distance electroweak correction factor: S_EW^{ππ} = 1.0233(3)(24)
- The two-pion channel accounts for more than 70% of the total leading-order hadronic vacuum polarization contribution to a_μ
- The threshold for the conformal mapping is set at s_in = (M_ω + M_π)^2, and the conformal map origin at s_c = -1 GeV²
- The phase shift continuation point is at √s_c^δ = 1.3 GeV, with phase shift values at s_0 = (0.8 GeV)² and s_1 = (1.15 GeV)²

## Context
This work extends the earlier ChPT analysis of radiative corrections to τ→ππν_τ decays by Cirigliano (2001, 2002) beyond the threshold region using dispersion relations, motivated by previous findings in e⁺e⁻→π⁺π⁻ by Ignatov (2022) and Colangelo (2022) that structure-dependent virtual corrections can be sizable. The results enable a more robust τ-based determination of the two-pion contribution to the hadronic vacuum polarization for the muon g-2, complementing e⁺e⁻-based evaluations and lattice QCD calculations, and addressing tensions among experimental data sets from CMD-3, BaBar, KLOE-2, and BESIII.
