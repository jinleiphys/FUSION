# The $α$ condensate states of atomic nuclei ${}^{12}C$, ${}^{16}O$ and ${}^{20}Ne$ in an analytical solvable model

> The $α$ condensation in the ${}^{12}C$, ${}^{16}O$ and ${}^{20}Ne$ nuclei is investigated within an analytical solvable model. It is found that the calculated ratio of the ground state energies of the Hoyle state of ${}^{12}C$ and the Hoyle-like state of ${}^{16}O$ is consistent with that of the experimental values. Along this clue, the ground state energy of ${}^{20}Ne$ is obtained to be 1MeV approximately, which is far less than the experimental value of 3MeV. Additionally, the root-mean-square radii of these nuclei are also calculated, and all of them lies around 9fm, which is different from the result calculated with the Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Ropke(THSR) wave function. Since the root-mean-square radius is relevant to the ground state energy of the $α$ condensate nucleus, the root-mean-square radii of ${}^{16}O$ and ${}^{20}Ne$ are also calculated with the ground state energies used in the THRS wave function. As a result, the root-mean-square radii of ${}^{16}O$ and ${}^{20}Ne$ reduced to 5fm, and it is similar to the result obtained with the THRS wave function. The calculation result manifests that the root-mean-square radius of $α$ condensate nuclei decreases with the energy increasing.

## Key claim

The ratio of the ground state energies of the Hoyle state of 12C and the Hoyle-like state of 16O calculated in an analytical solvable model with a harmonic oscillator potential matches the experimental ratio, but the model predicts a ground state energy for the 5α condensate state of 20Ne of about 1 MeV above the 5α threshold, which is far less than the experimental value of 3 MeV.

## Method

The three-body, four-body, and five-body problems are solved analytically by assuming a harmonic oscillator potential between all pairs of α particles and applying an orthogonal transformation to Jacobi coordinates. The ground state energies and root-mean-square radii of the α condensate states of 12C, 16O, and 20Ne are then calculated using the experimental ground state energies of 12C (0.38 MeV above the 3α threshold) and 16O (0.66 MeV above the 4α threshold) as inputs to determine the oscillator strength.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of ground state energies: E(3)(12C) : E(4)(16O) = 0.38 : 0.66 ≈ 1.727 : 3, consistent with the model ratio 3√3 : 9 ≈ 1.732 : 3.
- Calculated ground state energy of 20Ne: 0.98 MeV above the 5α threshold (model prediction), compared to the experimental value of 3 MeV.
- Root-mean-square radius of 12C (Hoyle state): 9.08 fm, using E(3)=0.38 MeV.
- Root-mean-square radius of 16O (sixth 0+ state): 8.95 fm, using E(4)=0.66 MeV.
- Root-mean-square radius of 16O with E(4)=2 MeV: 5.14 fm, consistent with THSR wave function results.
- Root-mean-square radius of 20Ne with E(5)=3 MeV: 5.01 fm, consistent with THSR wave function results.

## Context

This work extends the analytical solution of the three-body problem with harmonic oscillator potentials, summarized in a quantum mechanics problem set (Book), to four-body and five-body systems to study α condensation in light 4n nuclei. The results are compared to the Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Ropke (THSR) wave function calculations for the Hoyle state of 12C (Schuck 2001), the 4α condensate state of 16O (Schuck 2008), and the 5α condensate state of 20Ne (ZhouBo). The model reproduces the energy ratio between 12C and 16O but fails for 20Ne, and it shows that the root-mean-square radius of α condensate nuclei decreases as the ground state energy increases.
