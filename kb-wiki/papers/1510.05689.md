# $χ$EFT studies of few-nucleon systems: a status report

> A status report on $χ$EFT studies of few-nucleon electroweak structure and dynamics is provided, including electromagnetic form factors of few-nucleon systems, the $pp$ weak fusion and muon weak captures on deuteron and $^3$He, and a number of parity-violating processes induced by hadronic weak interactions.

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory (χEFT) calculations of few-nucleon electroweak observables now achieve quantitative agreement with experiment at low energies and momentum transfers, with some predictions reaching 1% accuracy, but the approach is limited to momentum transfers below approximately 5 fm⁻¹ and requires regularization with cutoff parameters in the range 500–600 MeV whose variation produces visible bands in the predictions.

## Method

The authors construct nuclear potentials and electroweak currents from chiral Lagrangians using time-ordered perturbation theory, matching the field-theory amplitude to the iteration of a potential in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation order by order in powers of Q/Λχ. The electromagnetic current operators are derived up to N3LO (order Q) and contain five unknown low-energy constants (LECs), which are fixed by reproducing the deuteron and trinucleon magnetic moments, assuming Δ-resonance saturation for two isovector LECs, and using either the np radiative capture cross section or the isovector trinucleon magnetic moment for the remaining isovector LEC. The axial current operators are derived up to N3LO with a single LEC fixed by the Gamow-Teller matrix element in tritium β-decay.

- Deuteron charge radius: 2.126(4) fm (experiment: 2.130(10) fm)
- Deuteron quadrupole moment: 0.2836(16) fm² (experiment: 0.2859(6) fm²)
- ³He charge radius: 1.962(4) fm (experiment: 1.973(14) fm)
- ⁴He charge radius: 1.663(11) fm (experiment: 1.681(4) fm)
- Muon capture rate on deuteron: Γ(²H) = (399 ± 3) sec⁻¹
- Muon capture rate on ³He: Γ(³He) = (1494 ± 21) sec⁻¹ (experiment: 1496 ± 4 sec⁻¹)
- Induced pseudoscalar form factor from ³He muon capture: GPS(q₀² = -0.95 mμ²) = 8.2 ± 0.7 (direct measurement: 8.06 ± 0.55; χPT prediction: 7.99 ± 0.20)
- pp weak fusion S-factor at zero energy: S(0) = (4.030 ± 0.006) × 10⁻²³ MeV-fm²
- LEC values for electromagnetic current at Λ = 500 MeV: cS = 4.1, dS × 10 = 2.2, cV(σnp) = -13, cV(μV) = -8.0 (units 1/Λ² for dS, 1/Λ⁴ for cS and cV)
- LEC values for electromagnetic current at Λ = 600 MeV: cS = 11, dS × 10 = 3.2, cV(σnp) = -22, cV(μV) = -12

## Context

This status report reviews work extending the χEFT program for few-nucleon systems pioneered by Weinberg (1990) and developed by Park et al. (1993, 1996), Ordonez et al. (1995), and Epelbaum et al. (1998). The calculations use chiral two-nucleon potentials at N3LO by Entem and Machleidt (2003) and leading-order three-nucleon potentials by Epelbaum et al. (2002), with LECs cD and cE constrained following Gardestig and Phillips (2006) by fitting tritium β-decay and trinucleon binding energies. The results enable precision tests of the Standard Model in weak processes such as muon capture and pp fusion, and provide a foundation for extending χEFT to heavier nuclei using quantum Monte Carlo methods with minimally non-local chiral potentials recently developed by Piarulli et al. (2015).
