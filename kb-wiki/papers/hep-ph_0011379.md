# Asymptotic deconfinement in high-density QCD

> We discuss QCD with two light flavors at large baryon chemical potential mu. Color superconductivity leads to partial breaking of the color SU(3) group. We show that the infrared physics is governed by the gluodynamics of the remaining SU(2) group with an exponentially soft confinement scale Lambda_QCD' Delta*exp[-a*mu/(g*Delta)], where Delta<<mu is the superconducting gap, g is the strong coupling, and a=0.81... We estimate that at moderate baryon densities Lambda_QCD' is O(10 MeV) or smaller. The confinement radius increases exponentially with density, leading to "asymptotic deconfinement." The velocity of the SU(2) gluons is small due to the large dielectric constant of the medium.

# Asymptotic deconfinement in high-density QCD

## Key claim

In two-flavor QCD at high baryon density, color superconductivity breaks SU(3) color to an SU(2) subgroup, and the infrared physics is governed by pure SU(2) gluodynamics with an exponentially soft confinement scale $\Lambda_{\rm QCD}' \sim \Delta \exp(-a\mu/(g\Delta))$; this means quarks are always confined in principle, but the confinement radius grows exponentially with density, leading to "asymptotic deconfinement" at any fixed distance scale.

## Method

The authors integrate out gapped quark degrees of freedom below the superconducting gap $\Delta$ to derive an effective local Lagrangian for the unbroken SU(2) gluons, characterized by a dielectric constant $\epsilon$ and magnetic permeability $\lambda$. They compute the one-loop polarization tensor $\Pi^{\mu\nu}_{ab}$ for the SU(2) gluons from the superconducting quark loop, expand it to quadratic order in momentum, and extract $\epsilon$ and $\lambda$. Using the rescaled effective action, they then apply the one-loop beta function of pure SU(2) Yang-Mills theory to estimate the confinement scale $\Lambda_{\rm QCD}'$.

## Key numbers

- Dielectric constant: $\epsilon = 1 + g^2\mu^2/(18\pi^2\Delta^2)$, which at high density becomes $\epsilon \approx g^2\mu^2/(18\pi^2\Delta^2) \gg 1$
- Magnetic permeability: $\lambda = 1$ (no spatial transverse contribution to $\Pi^{ij}$ at quadratic order)
- Speed of SU(2) gluons: $v = 1/\sqrt{\epsilon}$
- Effective coupling in the rescaled theory: $\alpha_s' = g^2/(4\pi\sqrt{\epsilon}) = (3/(2\sqrt{2}))(g\Delta/\mu)$
- Confinement scale estimate: $\Lambda_{\rm QCD}' \sim \Delta \exp(-(2\sqrt{2}\pi/11)(\mu/(g\Delta)))$
- Using one gap estimate ($b=512\pi^4$) with $\Lambda_{\rm QCD}=200$ MeV at $\mu=600$ MeV: $\Lambda_{\rm QCD}' \sim 10$ MeV
- Using another gap estimate ($b=512\pi^4\exp(-(\pi^2+4)/8)$) at the same chemical potential: $\Lambda_{\rm QCD}' \sim 0.3$ keV

## Context

This work extends the understanding of dense QCD beyond the original picture of Collins and Perry (1975), where quarks were thought to be simply liberated at high density. It builds on the discovery of color superconductivity by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998) and Rapp, Schafer, Shuryak, and Velkovsky (1998), and on the explicit calculation of Debye screening and Meissner effect in two-flavor color superconductors by Rischke (2000). The result contrasts with the three-flavor color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase studied by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999), where all gluons are screened and no pure Yang-Mills sector remains. The paper opens the question of accurately determining the superconducting gap $\Delta$, which controls the exponential sensitivity of the confinement scale, and suggests that at moderate densities the confinement scale is much smaller than $\Lambda_{\rm QCD}$.
