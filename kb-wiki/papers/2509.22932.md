# Theory of Quarkonia as Probes for Deconfinement

> This is a plenary talk given at Quark Matter 2025, summarizing recent theoretical developments for the understanding of quarkonium production in relativistic heavy ion collisions and how quarkonium uniquely probes the deconfined phase of QCD matter.

## Key claim

Quarkonium states may not completely melt inside the quark-gluon plasma; instead, they can persist as well-defined bound states up to temperatures of at least 260–350 MeV, with dissociation occurring via dynamical processes that transfer energy from the medium rather than via static screening.

## Method

The talk surveys multiple theoretical approaches: the Statistical Hadronization Model and Instantaneous Coalescence Model (which assume complete melting and regeneration at freezeout), lattice QCD calculations of the static potential and NRQCD at finite bottom quark mass, transport models (TAMU, Santiago Comover, Tsinghua) using rate equations or Boltzmann equations with dissociation and recombination, and open quantum system frameworks combined with effective field theory that separate quarkonium properties from QGP properties. The chromoelectric correlator is identified as a nonperturbative, gauge-invariant quantity that encodes the QGP property probed by quarkonium.

## Key numbers

- Ground state size of $B_c$ meson: ~0.34 fm; $J/\psi$: ~0.4 fm; $\Upsilon(1S)$: ~0.2 fm
- For $\Upsilon(1S)$ at $T=200$ MeV, $rT \sim 0.2$
- Lattice QCD in Coulomb gauge showed no screening in the real part of the potential up to $T=350$ MeV (static limit)
- Lattice NRQCD at finite bottom quark mass showed no mass change in bottomonium up to $T=260$ MeV
- $B_c$ meson production in 5 TeV Pb-Pb collisions is enhanced by a factor of ~5 at $p_T < 5$ GeV
- SHM shows good agreement with $J/\psi$ $R_{AA}$ data in 5 TeV Pb-Pb collisions at $p_T < 5$ GeV, but underestimates $\psi(2S)$ data (experimental $p_T$ cut 12 GeV for $\psi(2S)$ vs 8 GeV for $J/\psi$)

## Context

This plenary talk at Quark Matter 2025 extends the original proposal by Matsui and Satz (1986) that quarkonium suppression signals deconfinement. It contrasts the complete-melting picture (Statistical Hadronization Model of Andronic et al., Instantaneous Coalescence Model of Greco et al.) with lattice evidence from Bazavov et al. (2023) and Ding et al. (2025) showing bound states persist to high temperature. The open quantum system framework, first applied by Akamatsu and Rothkopf (2011), combined with effective field theory (Brambilla et al. 2016, Yao et al. 2018), enables systematic separation of quarkonium and QGP properties. The chromoelectric correlator identified here provides a QGP property uniquely probed by low-$p_T$ quarkonium production, and the framework is being extended to pp collisions where event activity dependence may challenge standard factorization.
