# Kinetic theory of spin-polarized systems in electric and magnetic fields with spin-orbit coupling: I. Kinetic equation and anomalous Hall and spin-Hall effects

> The coupled kinetic equation for density and spin Wigner functions are derived including spin-orbit coupling, electric and magnetic field as well as selfconsistent Hartree meanfields suited for SU(2) transport. The interactions are assumed to be with scalar and magnetic impurities as well as scalar and spin-flip potentials among the particles. The spin-orbit interaction is used in a form suitable to solid state physics with Rashba or Dresselhaus coupling, graphene, extrinsic spin-orbit coupling as well as effective nuclear matter coupling. The deficiencies of the two-fluid model are worked out consisting in the appearance of an effective in-medium spin-precession. The stationary solution of all these systems shows a band splitting controlled by an effective medium-dependent Zeeman field. The selfconsistent precession direction is discussed and a cancellation of linear spin-orbit coupling at zero temperature is reported. The precession of spin around this effective direction caused by spin-orbit coupling leads to anomalous charge and spin currents in an electric field. Anomalous Hall conductivity is shown to consists of the known results obtained from Kubo formula or Berry phases and a new symmetric part interpreted as inverse Hall effect. Analogously the spin-Hall and inverse spin-Hall effect of spin currents are discussed which are present even without magnetic fields showing a spin accumulation triggered by currents. The analytical dynamical expressions for zero temperature are derived and discussed in dependence on the magnetic field and effective magnetizations. The anomalous Hall and spin-Hall effect changes sign at higher than a critical frequency dependent on the relaxation time.

## Key claim

The paper derives a unified quantum kinetic equation for spin-polarized systems that includes spin-orbit coupling, electric and magnetic fields, and self-consistent Hartree mean fields, showing that the widely used two-fluid model is incomplete because it lacks an equation for the spin direction, which must be treated as a dynamical variable.

## Method

The author starts from the Kadanoff-Baym equations for nonequilibrium Green's functions in the Langreth-Wilkins notation, expands the convolutions to first-order gradients (quasi-classical limit), and derives coupled kinetic equations for the scalar Wigner function (density) and vector Wigner function (spin polarization). The interactions include scalar and magnetic impurities, scalar and spin-flip two-particle potentials, and spin-orbit coupling in a general form that covers Rashba, Dresselhaus, graphene, extrinsic, and nuclear-matter couplings. The collision integral is treated in a relaxation-time approximation, and the resulting equations are linearized to obtain stationary solutions and response functions.

## Key numbers

* The extrinsic spin-orbit coupling constant for electrons in vacuum is λ² ≈ 3.7 × 10⁻⁶ Å², derived from the Dirac equation expansion.
* For n-type GaAs, the intrinsic spin-orbit coupling constant is λ = 5.3 Å², which is six orders of magnitude stronger than in vacuum and has opposite sign.
* The Rashba spin-orbit coupling Hamiltonian is H_R = (β_R/ℏ) σ · (p × n) with winding number +1, while the linear Dresselhaus coupling has winding number -1.
* The anomalous Hall and spin-Hall effects change sign at a critical frequency that depends on the relaxation time (no explicit numerical value given for the critical frequency).

## Context

This work extends prior kinetic theories of spin-polarized transport by combining four problems usually treated separately: spin-polarized electrons (LhLa82a, LhLa82b), spin-orbit coupling (Raimondi 2006), transport in magnetic fields (Be58), and impurity scattering (SCC89). It corrects the two-fluid model of spin transport (FC68, Bala14) by adding the missing dynamical equation for the spin direction, and it provides a foundation for deriving random-phase-approximation response functions in a companion paper. The results for anomalous Hall conductivity agree with Kubo-formula and Berry-phase approaches (Nagaosa 2010) while adding a new symmetric part interpreted as the inverse Hall effect.
