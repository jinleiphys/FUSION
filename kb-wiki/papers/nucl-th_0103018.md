# $γ$-Radiation of Excited Nuclear Discrete Levels in Peripheral

> A new process of a nuclear excitation to discrete states in peripheral heavy ion collisions is studied. High energy photons are emitted by the exited nuclei with energies up to a few tens GeV at angles of a few hundred microradians with respect to the beam direction. We show that a two stage process where an electron - positron pair is produced by virtual photons emitted by nuclei and the n the electron or positron excites the nucleus, has large cross section. It is equal to about 5 b f or CaCa collisions. On one hand it produces a significant $γ$-rays background in the nuclear f ragmentation region but on the other hand it could be used to monitoring the nuclear beam intensity at LHC. These secondary nuclear photons could be a good signal for triggering peripheral nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a two-stage process, where an electron-positron pair is produced by virtual photons from the nuclei, and then the electron or positron excites one nucleus to a discrete level, has a large cross section (about 5 barns for CaCa collisions), but this result is conditional on the choice of a minimum impact parameter cutoff (R_min = 1/m_e = 386 fm) and applies specifically to peripheral collisions where the nuclei remain intact.

## Method

The authors calculate the cross section for nuclear excitation by an electron or positron in peripheral heavy ion collisions using a convolution of the equivalent photon approximation for e+e- pair production (from Ref. [8]) with the probability of nuclear excitation via quasi-elastic electron scattering, using the Glauber model and closure to sum over final states. They compute the angular and energy distributions of secondary photons from the de-excitation of the excited nucleus (specifically 40Ca) in the laboratory frame, assuming isotropic emission in the nuclear rest frame and a Lorentz factor of gamma_A = 3500 corresponding to LHC energies. They also estimate the cross sections for two competing processes: nuclear excitation by strong peripheral interactions and photon production from pi0 decay via virtual photon fusion.

## Key numbers

- Cross section for the two-stage process (nuclear excitation by electron/positron) in CaCa collisions: sigma_1 = 4.8 barns (with R_min = 1/m_e = 386 fm).
- Cross section for nuclear excitation by strong peripheral interactions (b > 2R_A): sigma_2 = 0.1 mb.
- Cross section for pi0 production via virtual photon fusion: sigma_3 = 0.19 mb.
- In the TOTEM angular region (20 to 120 microradians), the cross section is 16% of sigma_1; in the region up to the beam crossing angle of 300 microradians, it is 56% of sigma_1.
- Secondary photon energies from the 3^- level (E_gamma^0 = 3.74 MeV) of 40Ca are 22-26 GeV in the TOTEM angular range.
- The maximum in the angular distribution of secondary photons from the two-stage process occurs at theta_gamma = 150 microradians.
- The e+e- pair production cross section for CaCa at LHC energies is 1.5 Kb.
- The bremsstrahlung cross section from electrons/positrons for CaCa is about 10 b (from Ref. [15]), but photon energies are <= 3 MeV.

## Context

This work extends studies of electromagnetic processes in peripheral heavy ion collisions, specifically following the authors' previous work (Korotkikh and Chikin, 2001) on pi0 production via virtual photon fusion, and builds on the formalism for e+e- pair production from Ref. [8] and nuclear excitation by electron scattering from Ref. [21]. The paper proposes that secondary nuclear photons could serve as a beam intensity monitor for nuclear beams at LHC (where no such monitor yet exists) and as a trigger for peripheral collisions, complementing suggestions by Baur et al. (2000) to measure intact nuclei. The results also indicate that these photons will be a significant background in the nuclear fragmentation region, potentially affecting detectors like TOTEM.
