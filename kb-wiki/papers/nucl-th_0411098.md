# Compatibility of QCD sum-rules and Hadron field theory in a dense medium

> The compatibility of the QCD sum rules and effective hadronic models predictions are examined. For this purpose we have considered the results for the nucleon self-energy in a dense hadronic environment provided by two independent QCD sum-rules calculations. They are immersed in a theory of hadronic fields giving rise to non-linear interactions, whose vertices are parameterized in different ways. Although all of them reproduce the self-energy used as input, very different descriptions of nuclear observables are obtained. Only under very definite circumstances we have found an acceptable agreement with the nuclear matter properties. To achieve this, phenomenological parameters are not required at all.

## Key claim
The compatibility of QCD sum-rules predictions with hadronic field theory is feasible, but only under very specific conditions: the vertices must be parameterized in terms of mesonic degrees of freedom (case c), and the QCD sum-rules input must come from Drukarev's calculation rather than Cohen's. Even then, the model reproduces only the basic saturation properties of nuclear matter without requiring any phenomenological parameters, though quantitative agreement with collective phenomena remains limited.

## Method
The author takes nucleon self-energies from two independent QCD sum-rules calculations (Cohen and Drukarev) and inserts them into a hadronic field theory with density-dependent meson-nucleon vertices. Three different parameterizations of the vertices are tested: (a) both vertices depend on baryonic density, (b) scalar vertex depends on scalar density and vector vertex on baryonic density, and (c) scalar vertex depends on the sigma field and vector vertex on the omega field. The vertices are determined by solving differential equations that enforce reproduction of the input self-energies, including rearrangement terms for thermodynamic consistency, and the resulting nuclear matter properties are evaluated in the mean-field approximation.

## Key numbers
- The binding energy minimum for case cD (Drukarev input, mesonic parameterization) is \(E_B = -4\) MeV at density \(n = 0.8\, n_s\), where the empirical saturation density is \(n_s = 0.15\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) and the empirical binding energy is \(E_B \simeq -16\) MeV.
- With scaling factors \(X_s \simeq 1.19\) and \(X_v \simeq 1.12\) applied to the Drukarev self-energies, case cD reproduces the empirical saturation density and binding energy simultaneously.
- The nuclear compressibility for the non-scaled case cD is \(K = 245\) MeV, within the experimental range 230–270 MeV; the scaled case gives \(K = 660\) MeV.
- The giant monopole mode energy for non-scaled case cD is \(\omega_0 = 108\) MeV (experimental: \(\omega_0^{exp} = 80\) MeV); the scaled case gives \(\omega_0 = 180\) MeV.
- The quadrupole excitation energy for non-scaled case cD is \(\omega_2 = 78\) MeV (experimental: \(\omega_2^{exp} = 63\) MeV); the scaled case gives \(\omega_2 = 100\) MeV.
- The sound velocity at saturation for non-scaled case cD is \(v_s/c = 0.17\); for the scaled case it is \(v_s/c = 0.28\), becoming acausal before \(n \approx 2 n_s\).
- The fitted vertices for case cD are \(G_s = a/(1 + b\, s_3)\) with \(a = 7\), \(b = -0.59\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), and \(G_v = A + B\, s_4\) with \(A = 7.83\), \(B = 8.39\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).

## Context
This work extends the Density Dependent Hadron Field Theory (DDHFT) approach of Lenske and Typel, which previously used Dirac-Brueckner self-energies from one-boson-exchange potentials, by instead using QCD sum-rules self-energies as input. It contrasts with the earlier attempt by Weise, who used Cohen's QCD sum-rules results with four-fermion couplings and the simpler DDHFT prescription that omits rearrangement terms. The findings enable future tests of improved QCD sum-rules calculations (especially at high density) within hadronic models, and suggest that mesonic vertex parameterizations are the most promising path toward a consistent description of nuclear matter properties.
