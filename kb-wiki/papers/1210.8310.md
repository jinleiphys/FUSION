# Sensitivity of the electric dipole polarizability to the neutron skin thickness in ${}^{208}$Pb

> The static dipole polarizability, $α_{\rm D}$, in ${}^{208}$Pb has been recently measured with high-resolution via proton inelastic scattering at the Research Center for Nuclear Physics (RCNP). This observable is thought to be intimately connected with the neutron skin thickness, $r_{\rm skin}$, of the same nucleus and, more fundamentally, it is believed to be associated with the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy. The impact of $r_{\rm skin}$ on $α_{\rm D}$ in ${}^{208}$Pb is investigated and discussed on the basis of a large and representative set of relativistic and non-relativistic nuclear energy density functionals (EDF).

## Key claim

The paper investigates the correlation between the electric dipole polarizability (α_D) and the neutron skin thickness (r_skin) in 208Pb using a large set of nuclear energy density functionals (EDFs), finding that while a strong linear correlation exists within families of systematically varied interactions, the correlation weakens when different families of EDFs are combined, indicating that other (isoscalar) properties also affect α_D.

## Method

The authors computed the electric dipole polarizability α_D and neutron skin thickness r_skin for 208Pb using 48 different relativistic and non-relativistic nuclear energy density functionals (EDFs), all calibrated to global properties of finite nuclei and infinite nuclear matter. The dipole response R_E1(ω) was calculated using the consistent random-phase approximation (RPA) without any further adjustment of the models. The predictions were then compared to experimental constraints from the PREX experiment (r_skin) and the RCNP measurement (α_D).

## Key numbers

- The experimental value of α_D in 208Pb from the RCNP measurement is 20.1 ± 0.6 fm³.
- The PREX measurement gives r_skin = 0.33^{+0.16}_{-0.18} fm for 208Pb.
- From hadronic probes, r_skin = 0.16 ± (0.02)_stat ± (0.04)_syst fm (Klos et al., 2007) and r_skin = 0.211^{+0.054}_{-0.063} fm (Zentoi et al., 2010).
- The predicted linear correlation coefficient between α_D and r_skin is around 0.8 when all 48 models are considered, and close to one within specific families of interactions.
- From models compatible with the RCNP measurement, the authors obtain an average r_skin = 0.168 ± 0.022 fm.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the correlation between α_D and r_skin in 208Pb, previously suggested by macroscopic arguments (Lipparini and Stringari, 1982; Satula et al., 2006) and microscopic calculations (Reinhard and Nazarewicz, 2010; Piekarewicz, 2011), by using a large and representative set of EDFs rather than a single model. The study was motivated by the high-resolution measurement of the E1 strength distribution at RCNP (Tamii et al., 2011), which allowed a model-dependent extraction of r_skin from α_D using one single EDF (Klupfel et al., 2009) without systematic error estimation. The results enable a more robust determination of the neutron skin thickness and the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy, with implications for nuclear structure and neutron-star properties.
