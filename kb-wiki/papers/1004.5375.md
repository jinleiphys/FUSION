# Classical model of confinement

> The confinement mechanism proposed earlier and then applied successfully to meson spectroscopy by one of the authors is interpreted in classical terms. For this aim the unique solution of the Maxwell equations, an analog of the corresponding unique solution of the SU(3)-Yang-Mills equations describing linear confinement in quantum chromodynamics, is used. Motion of a charged particle is studied in the field representing magnetic part of the mentioned solution and it is shown that one deals with the full classical confinement of the charged particle in such a field: under any initial conditions the particle motion is accomplished within a finite region of space so that the particle trajectory is near magnetic field lines while the latter are compact manifolds (circles). An asymptotical expansion for the trajectory form in the strong field limit is adduced. The possible application of the obtained results in thermonuclear plasma physics is also shortly outlined.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a specific magnetic field configuration, derived as the U(1) analog of a unique SU(3)-Yang-Mills confining solution, produces full classical confinement of a charged particle: under any initial conditions, the particle motion remains within a finite region of space, with the trajectory oscillating near magnetic field lines that are circles. The hedge is that this holds "in case the field is strong enough" (i.e., the dimensionless parameter μ = E/(qb) must be small), and the confinement is not absolute if the particle reaches the z-axis, which is a singular line where motion is considered finished.

## Method

The authors study the classical equations of motion for a charged particle in the magnetic part of a unique spherically symmetric solution of the Maxwell equations, given by the vector potential A_φ = br + B (linear in the radial coordinate r). They derive the equations of motion in both spherical and Cartesian coordinates from the relativistic Lagrangian, and analyze the system qualitatively using first integrals and quantitatively by constructing asymptotic expansions in the small parameter μ = E/(qb) for the strong-field limit (b → ∞).

## Key numbers

* For a deuteron in a thermonuclear plasma at T ≈ 0.8625 × 10⁻² MeV (10⁸ K), the condition for confinement requires |b| >> 23.0 MeV; taking |b| = 1 GeV yields a magnetic field strength H ≈ 0.351 T near the particle trajectory at r₀ = 1 m and ϑ₀ = π/2.
* For a u-quark in a π⁺-meson, using m_u ≈ 2.25 MeV, v₀ ≈ 0.99, and α_s ≈ 0.485, the classical estimate gives b₀ ≈ 22.68 MeV, corresponding to H ≈ 0.119 × 10¹⁴ T at r₀ ≈ 0.672 fm and ϑ = π/2; the quantum values from the SU(3) solution give b ≈ 0.157 GeV and H ∼ (10¹⁵–10¹⁶) T.
* The asymptotic expansion for the radial coordinate in the strong-field limit is r ≈ r₀ + μ r₀² φ̇₀ sin²ϑ₀ + μ² r₀³ ϑ̇₀² sin²(ϑ̇₀ t + ϑ₀) + O(μ³), with μ = E/(qb).

## Context

This work extends the confinement mechanism previously developed by Goncharov (2001, 2005) for quantum chromodynamics, where a unique family of nonperturbative SU(3)-Yang-Mills solutions was used to describe meson spectroscopy, to a classical electromagnetic analog. The paper contrasts the behavior with motion in a homogeneous magnetic field (Landau and Lifshitz), which produces helical trajectories that can extend to infinity, whereas the field studied here gives full confinement. The results suggest possible applications in thermonuclear plasma physics, where the problem of long-duration plasma confinement remains unsolved, and the authors outline a physical realization using cone ferromagnetic pole pieces.
