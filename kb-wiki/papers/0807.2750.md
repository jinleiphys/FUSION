# Comments on "Rates of processes with coherent production of different particles and the GSI time anomaly"by C. Giunti, Phys. Lett. B 665, 92 (2008), 0805.0431

> We give comments on the recent paper by Giunti (Phys. Lett. B 665, 92 (2008), arXiv: 0805.0431 [hep-ph]) with a critique of our explanation of the experimentally observed periodic time-dependence of the interference term in the rate of the K-shell electron capture decay of the H-like ions 140Pr58+ and 142Pm60+ as a two-neutrino-flavour mixing. We show also that this phenomenon cannot be explained by a coherent mixing of two states of a mother ion as proposed by Giunti.

## Key claim

The authors argue that Giunti's (2008) critique of their two-neutrino-flavor mixing explanation for the GSI time anomaly is based on a technical error, the omission of delta functions enforcing energy-momentum conservation, and that Giunti's own alternative explanation, involving coherent mixing of two mother-ion mass states, fails because the two orthogonal coherent states would be produced with equal statistical probability, canceling any interference term.

## Method

The authors recalculate the electron-capture (EC) decay probability using standard time-dependent perturbation theory and quantum field theory, explicitly including the squared delta functions and the product of delta functions for different neutrino mass states. They show that the cross-term between different mass states vanishes due to energy-momentum conservation, reducing the decay probability to an incoherent sum over mass states. They then analyze Giunti's proposed coherent mother-ion state by computing the EC decay rate from both the coherent state |m⟩ and its orthogonal partner |m̃⟩, and averaging over the statistical ensemble of mother ions in the storage ring.

## Key numbers

- The experimentally observed oscillation period is \(T_d \simeq 7\ \text{sec}\) with amplitude \(a_{EC} \simeq 0.20\) for the EC decay of H-like ions \(^{140}\text{Pr}^{58+}\) and \(^{142}\text{Pm}^{60+}\).
- The Lorentz factor of the stored ions is \(\gamma = 1.43\).
- In Giunti's approach, the mass difference required to produce the observed period is \(\Delta M_m = 8.45 \times 10^{-16}\ \text{eV}/c^2\).
- The mixing angle needed to match the observed amplitude in Giunti's scheme is \(\theta \simeq 2.87^\circ\).

## Context

This comment paper responds to Giunti (2008, Phys. Lett. B 665, 92), who criticized the authors' earlier explanation (Ivanov et al., 2008) of the GSI time anomaly, the oscillatory EC decay rates observed by Litvinov et al. (2008), as arising from two-neutrino-flavor mixing. The authors extend their earlier critique (Ivanov et al., 2008) of Giunti's neutrino wave function and show that Giunti's alternative proposal of coherent mother-ion mixing cannot produce the observed oscillations because the orthogonal coherent state, produced with equal probability in the statistical ensemble, cancels the interference term. This analysis rules out both the neutrino-mixing and mother-ion-coherence explanations for the GSI anomaly, leaving the phenomenon unexplained within these frameworks.
