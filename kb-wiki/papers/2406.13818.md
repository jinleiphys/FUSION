# Effective theory tower for $ÎĽ\rightarrow e$ conversion

> We present theoretical predictions for $ÎĽ\rightarrow e$ conversion rates using a tower of effective field theories connecting the UV to nuclear physics scales. The interactions in nuclei are described using a recently developed nonrelativistic effective theory (NRET) that organizes contributions according to bound nucleon and muon velocities, $\vec{v}_N$ and $\vec{v}_ÎĽ$, with $|\vec{v}_N| > |\vec{v}_ÎĽ|$. To facilitate the top-down matching, we enlarge the set of Lorentz covariant nucleon-level interactions mapped onto the NRET operators to include those mediated by tensor interactions, in addition to the scalar and vector interactions already considered previously, and then match NRET nonperturbatively onto the Weak Effective Theory (WET). At the scale $ÎĽ\approx 2$ GeV WET is formulated in terms of $u$, $d$, $s$ quarks, gluons and photons as the light degrees of freedom, along with the flavor-violating leptonic current. We retain contributions from WET operators up to dimension 7, which requires the full set of 26 NRET operators. The results are encoded in the open-source Python- and Mathematica-based software suite MuonBridge, which we make available to the theoretical and experimental communities interested in $ÎĽ\rightarrow e$ conversion.

## Key claim

The paper provides a complete theoretical framework for predicting $\mu\rightarrow e$ conversion rates using a tower of effective field theories, extending previous work by including tensor-mediated interactions and muon-velocity-suppressed contributions, with the results encoded in the open-source MuonBridge software suite.

## Method

The authors construct a tower of effective field theories connecting UV-scale physics to nuclear scales, starting from the Weak Effective Theory (WET) at $\mu=2$ GeV with three light quark flavors ($u$, $d$, $s$), gluons, and photons, then matching nonperturbatively onto a nonrelativistic effective theory (NRET) that organizes contributions according to bound nucleon and muon velocities. The NRET basis of 26 operators is expanded to linear order in $\vec{v}_N$ and $\vec{v}_\mu$, and the matching from WET to NRET is performed including scalar, vector, and tensor interactions. The results are implemented in the MuonBridge software suite, which integrates the MuonConverter, Mu2e_NRET, and Elastic packages.

## Key numbers

* The three-momentum transfer in $\mu\rightarrow e$ conversion is approximately $q \approx m_\mu$, with the muon binding energy in $^{27}$Al being $E_\mu^\mathrm{bind}\approx 0.463$ MeV
* The difference between $q$ and $q_{\rm eff}$ is approximately 5% in $^{27}$Al
* The NRET basis contains 16 independent operators at first order in nucleon velocity $\vec{v}_N$ when neglecting muon velocity $\vec{v}_\mu$, and 26 operators when including $\vec{v}_\mu$ contributions
* The WET basis includes dimension-5 operators (2 dipole operators), dimension-6 operators (10 per quark flavor for $u$, $d$, $s$), and dimension-7 operators (8 gauge boson operators plus 8 derivative four-fermion operators per quark flavor)
* Current experimental limits: $B(\mu^-+$Ti$\rightarrow e^-+$Ti$)<6.1 \times 10^{-13}$ and $B(\mu^-+$Au$\rightarrow e^-+$Au$)<7 \times 10^{-13}$ at 90% C.L. from SINDRUM II
* Upcoming experiments Mu2e and COMET are expected to reach branching ratio sensitivities of about $10^{-17}$, probing new physics scales in excess of $10^4$ TeV

## Context

This work extends the NRET formalism previously developed for dark matter direct detection by Fan (2010) and Fitzpatrick (2012) to the $\mu\rightarrow e$ conversion process, building on the nuclear-level NRET developed by Rule (2021) and Haxton (2022). The framework enables systematic interpretation of experimental bounds from multiple nuclear targets, allowing characterization of the CLFV source if $\mu\rightarrow e$ conversion is discovered, and provides a general analysis framework for relating experimental bounds to high-scale sources of charged lepton flavor violation.
