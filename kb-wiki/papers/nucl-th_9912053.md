# Chiral SU(3) Dynamics with Coupled Channels: Inclusion of P-Wave Multipoles

> We extend our recent non-perturbative chiral SU(3) coupled channel approach to pion- and photon-induced $η$- and $K$-meson production off protons by including all strong and electromagnetic p-wave multipoles. We identify the p-wave amplitudes of the next-to-leading order SU(3) chiral meson-baryon Lagrangian with a coupled channel potential which is iterated to infinite orders in a separable Lippmann-Schwinger equation. Our approach to $η$- and $K$-photoproduction introduces no additional free parameters. By adjusting a few finite range parameters and the unknown parameters in the Lagrangian, we are able to simultaneously describe a very large amount of low-energy data. These include the total and differential cross sections of the $π$-induced reactions $π^- p \to ηn, K^0 Λ, K^0 Σ^0, K^+ Σ^-$ and $π^+ p \to K^+ Σ^+$ as well as those of photoproduction $γp \to ηp, K^+Λ, K^+ Σ^0, K^0 Σ^+$. The polarization observables measured in $η$- and $K$-photoproduction are particularly sensitive to interference terms between the s- and p-wave multipoles. The total cross section data are remarkably well reproduced in all channels. There remain, however, some open questions concerning details of angular distributions and polarization observables.

## Key claim

The paper extends a non-perturbative chiral SU(3) coupled channel approach to include all strong and electromagnetic p-wave multipoles for pion- and photon-induced eta- and K-meson production off protons, without introducing additional free parameters for photoproduction. However, the approach does not aim for accurate reproduction of elastic pi N phase shifts, and there remain open questions concerning details of angular distributions and polarization observables.

## Method

The authors identify the p-wave amplitudes of the next-to-leading order SU(3) chiral meson-baryon Lagrangian with a coupled channel potential matrix, which is then iterated to infinite orders in a separable Lippmann-Schwinger equation in momentum space. The strong interaction p-wave amplitudes (p_{1/2} and p_{3/2}) and photoproduction p-wave multipoles (E_{1+}, M_{1+}, M_{1-}) are calculated from the chiral effective Lagrangian, with the seven unknown parameters (g_D, g_F, g_0, g_1, h_D, h_F, h_1) fixed by fitting to a large set of scattering data. The photoproduction p-wave multipoles depend only on known baryon magnetic moments and the SU(3) axial vector coupling constants D and F, making the electromagnetic part parameter-free.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: f = 92.4 MeV
- Axial vector coupling constants: D = 0.782, F = 0.485
- Octet baryon mass in chiral limit: M_0 = 869 MeV
- Range parameters: alpha_{pi N} = 480 MeV, alpha_{eta N} = 663 MeV, alpha_{K Lambda} = 1362 MeV, alpha_{K Sigma} = 785 MeV
- Complex eta N scattering length: a_{eta N} = (0.32 + 0.25 i) fm
- s-wave multipole at threshold for gamma p -> eta p: E_{0+,th}^{(eta p)} = (9.8 + 12.8 i) x 10^{-3} m_pi^{-1}
- Pion-nucleon sigma-term from the fit: sigma_{pi N} = 31 MeV
- Scalar strangeness content in nucleon: y = 0.1

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous non-perturbative chiral SU(3) coupled channel approach (Caro Ramon et al., 1999; Siegel and Weise, 1988) by including p-wave multipoles, which were absent in the earlier s-wave-only treatment. Unlike most other approaches (Adelseck et al., 1985; Mart and Bennhold, 1996; Sauermann et al., 1995) that introduce explicit baryonic or mesonic resonances, this model generates resonance-like behavior (such as the Lambda(1405) and S_{11}(1535)) dynamically through coupled channel dynamics. The inclusion of p-waves enables description of angular distributions and polarization observables that vanish in pure s-wave approximation, and the approach can be extended to include explicit pi N resonances and coupling to the inelastic pi pi N continuum in future work.
