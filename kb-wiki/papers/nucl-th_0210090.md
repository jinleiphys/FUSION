# Relativistic Mean Field Model with Generalized Derivative Nucleon-Meson Couplings

> The quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) model with minimal nucleon-meson couplings is generalized by introducing couplings of mesons to derivatives of the nucleon field in the Lagrangian density. This approach allows an effective description of a state-dependent in-medium interaction in the mean-field approximation. Various parametrizations for the generalized couplings are developed and applied to infinite nuclear matter. In this approach, scalar and vector self-energies depend on both density and momentum similarly as in the Dirac-Brueckner theory. The Schrödiger-equivalent optical potential is much less repulsive at high nucleon energies as compared to standard relativistic mean field models and thus agrees better with experimental findings. The derivative couplings in the extended model have significant effects on properties of symmetric nuclear matter and neutron matter.

# Relativistic Mean Field Model with Generalized Derivative Nucleon-Meson Couplings

## Key claim

The authors propose a generalized relativistic mean field (RMF) model in which mesons couple to derivatives of the nucleon field, producing scalar and vector self-energies that depend on both density and momentum already at the mean-field level. This yields a Schrödinger-equivalent optical potential that is much less repulsive at high nucleon energies than standard RMF models, but the model is limited to not-too-high energies because the self-energies decrease linearly with energy, and the density range of applicability is constrained by the increase of the Fermi momentum.

## Method

The authors extend the quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) Lagrangian by introducing couplings of mesons to the covariant derivative of the nucleon field, parametrized by seven new coupling constants in addition to the four standard minimal couplings. They apply the model to infinite symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter in the mean-field approximation (no-sea approximation), derive the generalized Dirac equation with energy-dependent self-energies, and solve for the nucleon spinors, densities, and equation of state using plane-wave solutions. Three parametrizations of the new couplings are developed and compared to standard RMF models and to Dirac-Brueckner theory.

## Key numbers

- The new coupling constants are seven in number: Γσ^(1), Γσ^(2), Γω^(1), Γω^(2a), Γω^(2b), Γδ^(1), and Γρ^(1), all defined as pure numbers with mass factors introduced for convenience.
- The conserved baryon density in the DC model is given by ρ = ⟨J₀⟩ = (1/3π²)(pF_τ)³, where pF_τ is the Fermi momentum for protons or neutrons.
- The energy dependence of the self-energies is linear: Σ± = S± − T₀±E and Σ₀± = V₀± − (W± + X₀₀±)E, with the coefficients T₀±, W±, and X₀₀± increasing with density.
- Reasonable solutions of the Dirac equation exist only for −1 < Bτ < 1 and 1+Wτ+X₀₀τ ≠ 0, where Bτ = T₀τ/(1+Wτ+X₀₀τ).
- The optical potential from standard RMF models increases linearly at high energies and is much too large, whereas the DC model gives a less repulsive potential that levels off at about 1 GeV, in better agreement with experimental findings.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological RMF approach beyond models with nonlinear σ self-couplings (Boguta and Bodmer, 1977) and density-dependent couplings (Typel, 1999; Lenske and Fuchs, 1995) by introducing momentum-dependent self-energies already at the mean-field level, similar to the behavior seen in Dirac-Brueckner theory (de Jong and Lenske, 1991; Brockmann and Machleidt, 1990). The model aims to provide a common framework for describing both nuclear structure and nuclear reactions, enabling future application to finite nuclei and improving the description of proton-nucleus scattering and heavy-ion collisions where momentum dependence is important.
