# Decay Rate of Triaxially-Deformed Proton Emitters

> The decay rate of a triaxially-deformed proton emitter is calculated in a particle-rotor model, which is based on a deformed Woods-Saxon potential and includes a deformed spin-orbit interaction. The wave function of the $I=7/2^{-}$ ground state of the deformed proton emitter $^{141}$Ho is obtained in the adiabatic limit, and a Green's function technique is used to calculate the decay rate and branching ratio to the first excited 2$^{+}$ state of the daughter nucleus. Only for values of the triaxial angle $γ$ $<5^{\circ}$ is good agreement obtained for both the total decay rate and the 2$^{+}$ branching ratio.

## Key claim
Only for values of the triaxial angle γ less than 5° does the particle-rotor model, calculated in the adiabatic limit, achieve good agreement with experimental data for both the total decay rate and the 2⁺ branching ratio of the deformed proton emitter ¹⁴¹Ho.

## Method
The authors generalized a particle-rotor model, previously applied to axially-symmetric deformed cores, to include static triaxial deformation. They solved coupled radial equations in the adiabatic limit (rotational energies of the core set to zero) using a deformed Woods-Saxon potential with a deformed spin-orbit interaction, expanding the ground-state wave function (I=7/2⁻) of ¹⁴¹Ho in spherical components up to j=15/2⁻ and multipoles up to λ=14. A Green's function technique was used to calculate the total decay width and the branching ratio for proton emission to the first excited 2⁺ state of the daughter nucleus ¹⁴⁰Dy.

## Key numbers
- The product of calculated total decay width Γ_calc and spectroscopic factor S_calc = 0.6 for the ¹⁴¹Ho ground state was plotted as a function of γ; agreement with experiment is good only for small γ.
- The experimental 2⁺ branching ratio is 0.0070(15) (Rykaczewski et al.); the calculation agrees with this value only for γ ≤ 5°.
- The 2⁺ excitation energy in the daughter ¹⁴⁰Dy used in the calculation is 202 keV (recently measured by Cullen and Krolas), replacing an earlier value of 166 keV.
- The calculations used deformation parameters β₂ = 0.244 and β₄ = -0.046, with a proton pairing gap Δ_p = 0.9 MeV from a BCS calculation.

## Context
This work extends the particle-rotor model of Esbensen and Davids (2000) from axially-symmetric to triaxially-deformed proton emitters. It was motivated by in-beam γ-ray spectroscopy of ¹⁴¹Ho by Seweryniak et al., which suggested that a small static triaxial deformation could improve agreement with the observed rotational band energies. The results rule out a static triaxial angle greater than 5° for the ground state of ¹⁴¹Ho, but note that triaxiality may still influence the structure at higher spins and excitation energies. The formalism enables future calculations of proton decay rates for other triaxially-deformed nuclei beyond the proton drip line.
