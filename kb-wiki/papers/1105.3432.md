# Rotation of quantum liquid without singular vortex lines

> The operator equations for quantum hydrodynamics are discussed and solved in a simple cylindrical geometry. We find a solution with the velocity curl "frozen" into a density of the liquid in the absence of singular vortex lines. The spectrum of small oscillations around this solution is found as a generalization of the standard phonon spectrum, and the stability of the solution is demonstrated.

# Rotation of quantum liquid without singular vortex lines

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a quantum liquid can sustain a rotational state with a velocity curl "frozen" into the density distribution, without requiring singular vortex lines or a vortex lattice, provided the curl of the velocity is proportional to the local density via a constant c-number vector. This solution is stable against small oscillations and yields a spectrum that generalizes the standard phonon spectrum with a small energy gap determined by the magnitude of the velocity curl.

## Method

The authors solve the operator equations of quantum hydrodynamics in the Landau formulation for a Bose liquid confined in a cylindrical geometry, assuming planar motion with axial symmetry. They introduce an axial vector operator relating the curl of the velocity to the density, and find classical background solutions for the velocity and density fields that satisfy the equilibrium condition. Small oscillations around these background states are then analyzed by linearizing the equations of motion, expanding the fields in angular momentum modes, and solving the resulting differential equations with boundary conditions at the cylinder wall.

## Key numbers

- The angular momentum of the liquid in the vortex-free case (K=0) is given by L = Nℏs, where s = ΓR²/4, with Γ the constant c-number vector magnitude and R the cylinder radius.
- The critical angular velocity for the frozen rotation state is Ωc(Γ) ≈ Γ/4; for the minimum value Γmin = 4/R², the critical angular velocity is Ωc_min ≈ R⁻².
- The critical velocity for vortex-free rotation is Ω₀ = ℏ/mR², which is approximately ten times smaller than the vortex critical velocity Ωs = (ℏ/mR²)ln(R/a) for a vessel of radius R ≈ 1 mm.
- For a cylinder of radius R ≈ 1 mm, the critical angular velocities are about 0.01 rad/s.
- The energy spectrum of small oscillations contains a gap Γ, with dispersion relation ℰ² = Γ² ± q² + q⁴/4, where q is the wave number and ℰ is the excitation energy in the rotating frame.

## Context

This work extends the Landau theory of quantum hydrodynamics (Landau 1941) beyond irrotational motion, which Landau had considered for phonons and rotons in superfluid helium-4. The authors construct a class of solutions where both classical and quantum parts of the velocity curl are present, generalizing earlier work by Geilikman (1954) who linearized the equations for weak excitations above a uniform background. The paper addresses the long-standing question of whether a quantum liquid can rotate without forming individual vortices or a vortex lattice, as debated in experiments by Kojima (1971) and Walmsley (1958), and proposes that the "frozen" rotation state could explain observations previously attributed to the Landau state or Lin's hydrodynamics (Lin 1959, 1987). The results enable further theoretical and experimental investigation of rotational states in superfluid helium and ultracold atomic systems at angular velocities below the threshold for vortex nucleation.
