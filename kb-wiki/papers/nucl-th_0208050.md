# Complete next-to-leading order calculation for pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions at threshold

> Based on a counting scheme that explicitly takes into account the large momentum sqrt(M m_pi) characteristic for pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions we calculate all diagrams for the reaction NN --> NN pi at threshold up to next-to-leading order. At this order there are no free parameters and the size of the next-to-leading order contributions is in line with the expectation from power counting. The sum of loop corrections at that order vanishes for the process pp --> pp pi^0 at threshold. The total contribution at next-to-leading order from loop diagrams that include the delta degree of freedom vanishes at threshold in both reaction channels pp --> pp pi^0, pn pi^+.

# Complete next-to-leading order calculation for pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions at threshold

## Key claim

The chiral expansion for s-wave pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions appears to converge when using a counting scheme that accounts for the large momentum scale sqrt(M m_pi), but the authors hedge that the total next-to-leading order loop corrections either vanish or are in line with power-counting expectations, and that a complete next-to-next-to-leading order calculation has not yet been performed.

## Method

The authors evaluated all one-loop diagrams for the reaction NN -> NN pi at threshold up to next-to-leading order using a chiral counting scheme where the external momentum p ~ sqrt(M m_pi) ~ 362 MeV sets the scale, and the delta-nucleon mass difference Delta ~ 293 MeV is treated as comparable to p. They included explicit delta-isobar degrees of freedom at both tree level and one-loop order, and extracted only the genuine next-to-leading order pieces of the loop integrals by systematically dropping terms of order m_pi compared to loop energies.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon center-of-mass momentum at threshold is |p| = sqrt(m_pi (M + m_pi/4)) = 362 MeV, using M = 939 MeV and m_pi = 139.6 MeV.
- The delta-nucleon mass difference is Delta = 293 MeV.
- The leading-order Weinberg-Tomozawa contribution gives B^(WT) = -g_A / (2 M f_pi^3) with g_A ~ 1.3 and f_pi = 92.4 MeV.
- The leading-order one-pion exchange gives A^(1pi) = g_A^3 / (8 M f_pi^3) and B^(1pi) = 3 g_A^3 / (8 M f_pi^3).
- The tree-level delta-isobar contribution gives A^(Delta) = g_A^3 m_pi / (4 M f_pi^3 Delta) and B^(Delta) = 0.
- The numerical next-to-leading order nucleon-only loop correction gives B^(N-loop) = g_A^3 sqrt(M m_pi) / (256 f_pi^5) ~ 0.70 fm^4, which is about 50% of the leading-order contributions |B^(WT)| ~ 1.33 fm^4 and B^(1pi) ~ 1.69 fm^4.
- The suppression factor from power counting is p/M = sqrt(m_pi/M) ~ 0.4.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory calculations for pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions by employing a counting scheme from Bira (2001) and Hanhart (2001) that accounts for the large momentum sqrt(M m_pi), in contrast to the traditional Weinberg counting used in earlier loop calculations by Cohen (2001), Park (2001), and Ando (2001) which showed poor convergence. The authors find that the sum of loop corrections vanishes for pp -> pp pi^0 at threshold, and that loop diagrams with delta-isobars also cancel completely for both pp -> pp pi^0 and pp -> pn pi^+ channels. This enables a path toward a complete next-to-next-to-leading order calculation, which the authors state is within reach and should be performed, along with calculations of loop corrections to higher partial wave amplitudes.
