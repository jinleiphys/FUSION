# Quantifying Baryon Stopping in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> We propose a numerical definition for baryon stopping in relativistic heavy ion collisions that is obtainable from final hadron rapidity distributions as well as from bremsstrahlung measurements. Thus a new channel of communication is opened between the two methods.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a numerical definition for baryon stopping (or its inverse, baryon transparency) that can be obtained from either final hadron rapidity distributions or from bremsstrahlung measurements, thereby bridging the two methods. The definition is specifically formulated in rapidity space (not velocity space), and the key angle parameter \(\qh\) must be determined for each collision energy by requiring that a flat rapidity distribution yields \(\cs = \ct = 1/2\).

## Method

The author defines a stopping coefficient \(\cs\) and transparency coefficient \(\ct = 1-\cs\) via integrals over the normalized net baryon rapidity distribution \(\r(y)\), weighted by velocity-dependent factors and a parameter \(\qh\). The angle \(\qh\) is fixed by solving an equation that enforces \(\cs = \ct = 1/2\) for a flat rapidity distribution at a given beam energy. The stopping coefficient is then related to the soft-photon bremsstrahlung intensity measured at angle \(\qh\) in the limit of vanishing photon energy, allowing extraction from photon data alone.

## Key numbers

- At SPS I (Pb+Pb, \(\sqrt{s}/\)nucleon = 17.3 GeV, \(y_0 = 2.91\)): \(\qh = 28.83^\circ\)
- At SPS II (S+S, \(\sqrt{s}/\)nucleon = 19.4 GeV, \(y_0 = 3.03\)): \(\qh = 26.97^\circ\)
- At RHIC (\(\sqrt{s}/\)nucleon = 200.0 GeV, \(y_0 = 5.36\)): \(\qh = 7.93^\circ\)
- At LHC (\(\sqrt{s}/\)nucleon = 1500.0 GeV, \(y_0 = 7.38\)): \(\qh = 2.87^\circ\)
- For a flat rapidity distribution at RHIC, the test cases with \(\l\) from 0 to 1/2 yield \(\cs\) values between 0.50000 and 0.49689, all close to 0.5.
- For a flat distribution in velocity space at RHIC, the resulting stopping coefficient is \(\cs \simeq 0.97\) (i.e., \(\ct \simeq 0.03\)).
- The angle of maximum bremsstrahlung intensity for full stopping is \(\q_{\rm{max}} = 0.538^\circ\) at RHIC and \(\q_{\rm{max}} = 0.072^\circ\) at LHC.

## Context

This work extends the traditional qualitative discussion of baryon stopping (Landau vs. Bjorken pictures) by providing a quantitative definition, and it connects the hadron rapidity measurement approach used by NA49 at CERN SPS with the bremsstrahlung method proposed by Kapusta and collaborators. The definition enables direct comparison between the two experimental techniques and can be applied at RHIC and LHC to determine stopping from soft photon measurements, which are simpler to perform than full rapidity coverage of hadrons.
