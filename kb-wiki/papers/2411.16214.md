# Robust correlation between binding energies and charge radii of mirror nuclei

> Using the charge density from the two-parameter Fermi model, a robust and nontrival correlation between binding energis and charge radii of mirror nuclei is newly proposed. This correlation enables simple yet reliable predictions of the nuclear mass and charge radius of proton-rich nuclei. The validity of these predictions is demonstrated by comparing the predicted binding energies and charge radii with experimental data and predictions from other models. All 197 predicted binding energies and 199 charge radii involved in the comparisons are tabulated in the Supplemental Materials of this paper. The noticeable discrepancies are attributed to the large asymmetry in charge densities of mirror nuclei, suggesting that the proposed correlation could be a sensitive probe for local structural anomaly, such as shell closure and proton halo. The difference in mass dependence of charge radii near the proton dripline compared to those along the $β$-stability line supports the validity of our prediction method.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a new, robust correlation between binding energies and charge radii of mirror nuclei, governed by an observable η that is largely insensitive to the details of the assumed two-parameter Fermi charge density. The correlation enables predictions of nuclear masses and charge radii for proton-rich nuclei, with the caveat that the modeling uncertainty is larger for lighter nuclei (A ≤ 24) and decreases for heavier systems.

## Method
The authors derive an analytical expression for the observable η = Δε × R_c, where Δε is the reduced Coulomb displacement energy of mirror nuclei and R_c is the RMS charge radius, using the two-parameter Fermi model for the nuclear charge density. They calculate contributions from the direct Coulomb term, exchange term, and spin-orbit coupling term, and fit the resulting η(A) function to experimental data from the AME20, Rc13, and Rc21 databases, treating the nuclear geometry radius parameter r₀, the surface thickness ratio (a/R)₀, and the isospin asymmetry coefficient θ as free parameters. The fitted parameters are then used to predict binding energies and charge radii for proton-rich mirror nuclei, with uncertainties quantified separately in four mass regions using a maximum-likelihood method.

## Key numbers
- The linear fit of the nuclear geometry radius R to A^(1/3) gives R = 1.076(2) A^(1/3) fm for A < 100.
- The a/R ratio follows a/R = 0.531(4) A^(-1/3) for A < 100.
- The fit of Eq. (η-all) to experimental η values yields r₀ = 1.088 fm, (a/R)₀ = 0.584, and θ = 1.047 fm.
- The RMS deviation between experimental and theoretical η is approximately 0.05 η₀.
- The modeling uncertainty σ_η decreases from 0.024 (for 10 < A ≤ 24) to 0.009 (for A > 41), with systematic deviations μ ranging from -0.009 to 0.009 across the four mass regions.
- The paper tabulates 197 predicted binding energies and 199 predicted charge radii in the Supplemental Materials.

## Context
This work extends the long tradition of using isospin symmetry for mass predictions, including the isobaric multiplet mass equation (Wigner 1959, Garvey 1969, Benenson 1979, Brown 1998), the Garvey-Kelson relations (Garvey and Kelson 1966), and recent mirror-nuclei mass relations (Zong 2020, Fu 2024, Bao 2024). It also connects to charge-radius systematics (Angeli 2013, 2021) and the use of mirror-pair charge-radius differences as probes of the symmetry energy slope (Brown 2006, Yang 2011). The proposed correlation enables simultaneous predictions of masses and charge radii for proton-rich nuclei near the drip line, where global models like the DRHBc Mass Table (DRHBc22, DRHBc24) are less accurate, and where short-range correlations like Audi-Wapstra systematics fail due to distance limitations.
