# Parity violating elastic electron scattering and neutron density distributions in the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model

> Parity violating elastic electron scattering on neutron-rich nuclei is described in the framework of relativistic mean-field theory. Self-consistent ground state density distributions of Ne, Na, Ni and Sn isotopes are calculated with the relativistic Hartree- Bogoliubov model, and the resulting neutron radii are compared with available experimental data. For the elastic scattering of 850 MeV electrons on these nuclei, the parity-violating asymmetry parameters are calculated using a relativistic optical model with inclusion of Coulomb distortion effects. The asymmetry parameters for chains of isotopes are compared, and their relation to the Fourier transforms of neutron densities is studied. It is shown that parity-violating asymmetries are sensitive not only to the formation of the neutron skin, but also to the shell effects of the neutron density distribution.

# Parity violating elastic electron scattering and neutron density distributions in the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model

## Key claim

Parity-violating asymmetry parameters for elastic electron scattering at 850 MeV are sensitive to the formation of the neutron skin and to shell effects in neutron density distributions along isotope chains, but the authors find that these asymmetries would not be sensitive to the formation of a neutron halo, as illustrated by the example of neutron-rich Ne nuclei.

## Method

Self-consistent ground state neutron and proton density distributions for chains of Ne, Na, Ni, and Sn isotopes were calculated using the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model with the NL3 effective interaction for the mean-field and the Gogny D1S force for the pairing channel. The charge and weak densities were obtained by folding the point nucleon densities, and the parity-violating asymmetry parameter was computed by solving the partial wave Dirac equation for longitudinally polarized electrons at 850 MeV, including Coulomb distortion effects. The resulting asymmetries were compared to the Fourier transforms of the neutron densities.

## Key numbers

- For Ni isotopes, the difference between neutron and proton rms radii (r_n - r_p) increases from approximately 0.03 fm for 58Ni to 0.39 fm for 76Ni.
- For Sn isotopes, r_n - r_p = 0.27 fm for 124Sn.
- Parity-violating asymmetry parameters for elastic scattering at 850 MeV on Ni isotopes are of the order of 10^-5, with differences between neighboring isotopes of approximately 10^-6.
- For Ne isotopes, differences in asymmetry parameters become of order 10^-5 only at momentum transfer q approximately 2.5 fm^-1.
- The central neutron density for Na isotopes increases from approximately 0.065 fm^-3 for 26Na to more than 0.09 fm^-3 for 27Na.

## Context

This work extends the calculations of parity-violating asymmetries by Horowitz (1998), who used three-parameter Fermi densities and relativistic mean-field densities for doubly closed-shell nuclei, to neutron-rich isotopes described by the RHB model. The RHB model had previously been shown to reproduce experimental neutron radii for Na isotopes (Suzuki et al., 1995) and Sn isotopes (Krasznahorkay et al., 1999). The results suggest that parity-violating electron scattering could provide detailed information on neutron density distributions and neutron skin thickness, which would constrain the isovector channel of effective nuclear interactions, but the method appears insensitive to neutron halo formation.
