# Scale-chiral symmetry, $ω$ meson and dense baryonic matter

> It is shown that explicitly broken scale symmetry is essential for dense skyrmion matter in hidden local symmetry theory. Consistency with the vector manifestation fixed point for the hidden local symmetry of the lowest-lying vector mesons and the dilaton limit fixed point for scale symmetry in dense matter is found to require that the anomalous dimension ($γ_{G^2}$) of the gluon field strength tensor squared ($G^2$) that represents the quantum trace anomaly should be $0 <γ_{G^2} <3$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the divergence problem in dense skyrmion matter caused by the homogeneous Wess-Zumino (hWZ) term involving the omega meson can be resolved by explicitly breaking scale symmetry, requiring the anomalous dimension of the gluon field strength tensor squared to lie in the range 0 < gamma_{G^2} < 3.

## Method

The authors construct a scale-chiral effective Lagrangian with hidden local symmetry (HLS) that incorporates the dilaton field and the omega meson via the homogeneous Wess-Zumino term. They generalize the Crewther-Tunstall approach to HLS, introducing a parameter beta-prime (equal to the anomalous dimension gamma_{G^2}) that controls explicit scale symmetry breaking through the slope of the beta function. They then simulate dense skyrmion matter on a lattice and vary the parameters beta-prime and c_h (a constant in the hWZ term) to determine which values avoid divergences and yield the vector manifestation (VM) fixed point and dilaton-limit fixed point at high density.

## Key numbers

- The anomalous dimension gamma_{G^2} must satisfy 0 < gamma_{G^2} < 3 to be consistent with VM and dilaton-limit fixed points.
- The constant c_h in the hWZ term must lie in the range 0 <= c_h <= 0.3 for consistency.
- The dilaton mass is taken as m_sigma = 550 MeV and the dilaton decay constant as f_sigma = 100 MeV.
- The omega meson mass in vacuum is approximately 780 MeV.
- The half-skyrmion phase sets in at density n ~ 2 n_0, where n_0 is nuclear matter saturation density.

## Context

This work extends the hidden local symmetry approach to dense baryonic matter developed by Harada and Yamawaki (2000) and the scale-chiral effective theory of Crewther and Tunstall (2015). It resolves a divergence problem first identified in prior work by Park, Rho, and Vento (PRV1) where the hWZ term caused the energy and hadron properties to diverge at high density. The result enables a consistent description of dense matter that approaches both the vector manifestation fixed point and the dilaton-limit fixed point, and suggests that dense baryonic matter can provide information on the anomalous dimension of the gluon field strength tensor, a fundamental QCD quantity.
