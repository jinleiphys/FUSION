# Dibaryon model for nuclear force and the properties of the $3N$ system

> The dibaryon model for $NN$ interaction, which implies the formation of an intermediate six-quark bag dressed by a $σ$-field, is applied to the $3N$ system, where it results in a new three-body force of scalar nature between the six-quark bag and a third nucleon. A new multicomponent formalism is developed to describe three-body systems with nonstatic pairwise interactions and non-nucleonic degrees of freedom. Precise variational calculations of $3N$ bound states are carried out in the dressed-bag model including the new scalar three-body force. The unified coupling constants and form factors for $2N$ and $3N$ force operators are used in the present approach, in a sharp contrast to conventional meson-exchange models. It is shown that this three-body force gives at least half the $3N$ total binding energy, while the weight of non-nucleonic components in the $^3$H and $^3$He wavefunctions can exceed 10%. The new force model provides a very good description of $3N$ bound states with a reasonable magnitude of the $σNN$ coupling constant. A new Coulomb $3N$ force between the third nucleon and dibaryon is found to be very important for a correct description of the Coulomb energy and r.m.s. charge radius in $^3$He. In view of the new results for Coulomb displacement energy obtained here for A=3 nuclei, an explanation for the long-term Nolen--Schiffer paradox in nuclear physics is suggested. The role of the charge-symmetry-breaking effects in the nuclear force is discussed.

## Key claim

The dibaryon model, which replaces the conventional Yukawa meson-exchange mechanism at intermediate and short ranges with an s-channel formation of a dressed six-quark bag, is applied to the 3N system and yields a new scalar three-body force that provides at least half of the total 3N binding energy, while the weight of non-nucleonic components in the 3H and 3He wavefunctions can exceed 10%.

## Method

The authors developed a multicomponent formalism based on the Merkuriev group approach (1980s) to describe three-body systems with nonstatic pairwise interactions and non-nucleonic degrees of freedom, where the internal (dressed six-quark bag) channels are eliminated to produce an effective energy-dependent three-body force. Precise variational calculations of the 3H and 3He ground states were carried out using the dressed-bag model (DBM) for the NN interaction, which includes conventional t-channel pion and two-pion exchanges at long distances and s-channel dressed-bag contributions at short distances, with unified coupling constants and form factors for both two- and three-body force operators.

## Key numbers

- The new scalar three-body force gives at least half of the total 3N binding energy.
- The weight of non-nucleonic components in the 3H and 3He wavefunctions can exceed 10%.
- The DBM provides a good description of 3N bound states with a reasonable magnitude of the sigma NN coupling constant.
- A new Coulomb three-body force between the third nucleon and the dibaryon is found to be very important for a correct description of the Coulomb energy and r.m.s. charge radius in 3He.

## Context

This work extends the dressed-bag model previously developed by the Moscow-Tubingen group (Kukulin et al., 2004) for NN interactions up to 1 GeV, which contrasts with conventional meson-exchange models such as the Bonn, Nijmegen, and Argonne potentials that rely on t-channel sigma exchange for intermediate-range attraction. The new three-body force differs fundamentally from existing models like the Urbana-Illinois or Tucson-Melbourne three-body forces, which are based on two-pion exchange with intermediate Delta-isobar production and do not include sigma exchange; here the sigma field dominates both NN and 3N forces. The results suggest an explanation for the long-standing Nolen-Schiffer paradox regarding Coulomb displacement energy in A=3 nuclei, and the formalism enables future studies of charge-symmetry-breaking effects and applications to heavier nuclear systems.
