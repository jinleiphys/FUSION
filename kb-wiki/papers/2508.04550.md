# Experimental Study of Bremsstrahlung Gamma Ray Emission and Short-Range Correlations in $^{124}$Sn+$^{124}$Sn Collisions at 25 MeV/u

> Short-range correlation (SRC) in nuclei refers to nucleons forming temporally correlated pairs in close proximity, giving rise to the high momentum of the nucleons beyond the Fermi surface. It has been reported that bremsstrahlung $γ$ production from neutron-proton process in heavy-ion reactions provides a potential probe to the SRC abundance in nuclei. In this paper, we present in detail the precision measurement of bremsstrahlung $γ$-rays in $\rm ^{124}Sn$+$\rm ^{124}Sn$ reactions at 25 MeV/u using the Compact Spectrometer for Heavy IoN Experiment (CSHINE). A comprehensive experimental and analysis framework is established to ensure the reliability and robustness of the extracted results. Background contributions are evaluated and subtracted using independent methods, and the consistency of the analysis is systematically validated. By comparing the experimental $γ$ spectrum with the Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck simulations, the high momentum tail (HMT) fraction of $R_{\rm HMT}=(20 \pm 3)\%$ is derived in $^{124}$Sn nuclei. This work provides a detailed and validated experimental framework for extracting SRC information from bremsstrahlung $γ$-ray emission and demonstrates the feasibility of studying nucleon SRCs with high precision in low-energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim
The paper reports a high-momentum tail (HMT) fraction of \(R_{\rm HMT} = (20 \pm 3)\%\) in \(^{124}\)Sn nuclei, extracted by comparing measured bremsstrahlung \(\gamma\)-ray spectra from \(^{124}\)Sn+\(^{124}\)Sn collisions at 25 MeV/u with IBUU transport model simulations. The abstract's claim of "precision measurement" is hedged by the fact that this result is derived from a specific reaction system and model-dependent comparison, with systematic uncertainties evaluated from multiple sources including calibration variations and detector response corrections.

## Method
Bremsstrahlung \(\gamma\)-rays were measured using the CsI(Tl) hodoscope (CSHINE-Gamma) of the Compact Spectrometer for Heavy Ion Experiment (CSHINE) at the Radioactive Ion Beam Line in Lanzhou (RIBLL-1), with a \(^{124}\)Sn beam at 25 MeV/u incident on a 1 mg/cm\(^2\) \(^{124}\)Sn target. The experimental \(\gamma\)-ray spectrum was compared with predictions from the Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model using momentum-dependent interactions (MDI), where the initial nucleon momentum distribution included a high-momentum tail parameterized by the fraction \(R_{\rm HMT}\). Background contributions were evaluated and subtracted using independent methods, and the consistency of the analysis was validated through different coincidence selections and an independent unfolding approach.

## Key numbers
- HMT fraction in \(^{124}\)Sn: \(R_{\rm HMT} = (20 \pm 3)\%\), extracted from comparison of experimental \(\gamma\) spectrum with IBUU-MDI simulations.
- Beam energy: 25 MeV/u for \(^{124}\)Sn projectiles.
- Target thickness: 1 mg/cm\(^2\) of \(^{124}\)Sn.
- Energy resolution of CsI(Tl) units: about 3.6% for 1 MeV \(\gamma\)-rays and about 2% for \(\gamma\)-ray energy beyond 10 MeV.
- CsI(Tl) hodoscope: 15 units in a \(4 \times 4\) configuration (one corner vacant), each unit \(7 \times 7 \times 25\) cm\(^3\).
- Radiation length of CsI(Tl): \(X_0 = 8.39\) g/cm\(^2\); Moliere radius: \(r_{\rm M} = 3.531\) cm.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of short-range correlations (SRCs) using bremsstrahlung \(\gamma\)-rays in heavy-ion collisions, following an earlier CSHINE experiment with 25 MeV/u \(^{86}\)Kr + \(^{124}\)Sn reactions that provided only an indication of non-zero HMT fraction at about 90% confidence level (Qin 2023, Xu 2024). The present result marks the first statistically high-precision extraction of the SRC fraction in low-energy heavy-ion collisions using bremsstrahlung \(\gamma\)-rays as a clean probe, enabled by significant upgrades to CSHINE-Gamma including extended energy range and enhanced detection efficiency. The methodology enables future studies of SRC abundances in specific nuclei through symmetric reaction systems and provides a validated experimental framework for extracting SRC information from bremsstrahlung \(\gamma\)-ray emission.
