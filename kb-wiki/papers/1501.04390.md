# Search for η'(958)-nucleus bound states by (p,d) reaction at GSI and FAIR

> The mass of the η' meson is theoretically expected to be reduced at finite density, which indicates the existence of η'-nucleus bound states. To investigate these states, we perform missing-mass spectroscopy for the (p, d) reaction near the η' production threshold. The overview of the experimental situation is given and the current status is discussed.

## Key claim

The mass of the eta' meson is theoretically expected to decrease at finite nuclear density due to partial restoration of chiral symmetry, which could allow eta'-nucleus bound states to form; however, the existence of such states depends on whether the attractive interaction is strong enough and whether the real part of the optical potential is deeper than the imaginary part, as suggested by chiral unitary model calculations.

## Method

The collaboration performed inclusive missing-mass spectroscopy of the (p,d) reaction on a carbon-12 target using a 2.5 GeV proton beam at GSI's Fragment Separator (FRS), which served as a high-resolution spectrometer. Ejectile deuterons were momentum-analyzed using multi-wire drift chambers near the dispersive focal plane S4, with particle identification achieved via time-of-flight measurements between plastic scintillators at S2 and S4, supplemented by Cherenkov detectors with silica aerogel radiators. The excitation energy range from -90 MeV to 40 MeV relative to the eta' emission threshold was investigated by scaling the magnetic field of the FRS.

## Key numbers

- The eta' meson mass is 958 MeV/c^2.
- The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model predicts a mass reduction of -150 MeV at normal nuclear density, while the linear sigma model predicts -80 MeV, and the quark-meson coupling model predicts -37 MeV for an eta-eta' mixing angle of -20 degrees.
- The CBELSA/TAPS experiment determined the absorption width for an eta' meson with average momentum 1050 MeV/c to be 15-25 MeV at normal nuclear density.
- The CBELSA/TAPS experiment derived the eta'-nucleus potential depth as -(37 +/- 10 (stat) +/- 10 (syst)) MeV.
- The expected overall resolution of the GSI experiment is approximately 1.6 MeV in sigma.
- The simulated signal-to-noise ratio for the inclusive measurement is of the order of 1/100 at most.
- The deuteron-to-proton ratio in the raw data was approximately 1/200, improved to approximately unity with a time-of-flight trigger.
- The time-of-flight difference between protons and deuterons was about 20 ns.
- Protons from the two-nucleon absorption decay mode are expected to have kinetic energies around 300-600 MeV.

## Context

This work extends theoretical predictions of eta'-nucleus bound states from models such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Nagahiro 2006), the linear sigma model (Sakai 2013), and the quark-meson coupling model (Bass 2006), as well as the chiral unitary model (Nagahiro 2012). It builds on experimental constraints from the CBELSA/TAPS collaboration (Nanova 2012, 2013), which measured the eta' absorption width and potential depth via photoproduction off nuclei, and from the pp -> pp eta' reaction (Czerwinski 2014). The experiment enables the first direct search for eta'-mesic nuclei via missing-mass spectroscopy, with a planned upgrade to a semi-exclusive measurement at FAIR that will detect decay protons from two-nucleon absorption to improve the signal-to-noise ratio.
