# Number density interpretation of dihadron fragmentation functions

> We present a new quantum field-theoretic definition of fully unintegrated dihadron fragmentation functions (DiFFs) as well as a generalized version for $n$-hadron fragmentation functions. We demonstrate that this definition allows certain sum rules to be satisfied, making it consistent with a number density interpretation. Moreover, we show how our corresponding so-called extended DiFFs that enter existing phenomenological studies are number densities and also derive their evolution equations. Within this new framework, DiFFs extracted from experimental measurements will have a clear physical meaning.

## Key claim

The paper provides a new quantum field-theoretic definition of fully unintegrated dihadron fragmentation functions (uDiFFs) that, unlike the prior definition by Bianconi, Boffi, Jakob, and Radici (1999), allows these functions to retain a number density interpretation in a parton model framework, contingent on using specific prefactors of 1/(64π³z₁z₂) for quarks rather than 1/(4z).

## Method

The authors redefine the correlator for fragmentation of a parton into two hadrons by introducing specific prefactors (1/(64π³z₁z₂) for quarks and analogous for gluons) in the parameterization of the Dirac trace of the correlator. They prove number and momentum sum rules in a parton model framework to justify the number density interpretation, and derive the operator definition for extended DiFFs (extDiFFs) by changing variables from (z₁,z₂,P₁⊥,P₂⊥) to (z,ζ,k_T,R_T) and integrating over k_T. The evolution equations for extDiFFs are derived at O(α_s) by analyzing perturbative corrections to the dihadron fragmentation correlator.

## Key numbers

- The quark uDiFF prefactor is 1/(64π³z₁z₂) in Eq. (1) of the parameterization, compared to the prior 1/(4z) used by Bianconi, Boffi, Jakob, and Radici (1999)
- The Jacobian for changing variables from (z₁,z₂,P₁⊥,P₂⊥) to (z,ζ,k_T,R_T) is J = z³/2
- The Jacobian for changing variables to (z,ζ,k_T,M_h,φ_RT) is J = z³(1-ζ²)/8
- The leading-order cross section for e⁺e⁻ → (h₁h₂)X takes the form dσ/(dz dM_h) = σ̂ⁱ₀ D₁^{h₁h₂/i}(z,M_h), where σ̂⁰_q = 4πα_em²N_c e_q²/(3s) for quarks
- The number sum rule integrates over phase space to give ⟨N(N-1)⟩, the expectation value for the total number of hadron pairs produced in fragmentation

## Context

This work extends the pioneering correlator definition of dihadron fragmentation functions by Bianconi, Boffi, Jakob, and Radici (1999), which did not allow a number density interpretation. The new definition enables rigorous QCD global analyses of existing experimental data including Belle (2017) dihadron cross sections, Belle (2011) transverse single-spin asymmetries, HERMES (2008) and COMPASS (2023) semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering measurements, and STAR (2015, 2017) proton-proton collision data. The framework connects to double PDFs through analogous sum rules derived by Gaunt (2009) and Diehl (2018), and enables future extractions of transversity PDFs through the H₁^⦜ function that couples to dihadron observables.
