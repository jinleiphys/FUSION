# Dilepton production in p+p, Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions at 200 AGeV

> We study dilepton production in proton-proton, Cu+Cu as well as in Au+Au collisions at the center-of-mass energy 200 GeV per participating nucleon pair within an extended statistical hadronization model. In extension to earlier studies we incorporate transport calculations for an estimate of uncorrelated e+e- -pairs from semileptonic D meson decays. While the invariant mass spectrum of dielectrons is well understood in the p+p collisions, severe discrepancies among different model scenarios based on hadronic degrees of freedom and recent data from the PHENIX Collaboration are found in heavy-ion collisions in the low mass region from 0.15 to 0.6 GeV as well as in the intermediate mass regime from 1.1 to 3 GeV when employing the standard dilepton sources. We investigate, furthermore, the background from correlated dileptons that are not emitted as a pair from a parent hadron but emerge from semileptonic decays of two correlated daughter hadrons. Our calculations suggest a sizeable contribution of such sources in central heavy-ion collisions in the low mass region. However, even the upper limits of our calculations are found to be far below the dilepton mass spectra of the PHENIX Collaboration.

## Key claim
The authors find that even when including contributions from correlated semileptonic decays of kaon-antikaon pairs from heavy parent hadrons, their extended statistical hadronization model calculations fall far below the dilepton mass spectra measured by the PHENIX Collaboration in central heavy-ion collisions, indicating that standard hadronic sources cannot explain the observed excess.

## Method
The authors extended the statistical hadronization model (SHM) by incorporating transport calculations from the Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) framework to estimate uncorrelated electron-positron pairs from semileptonic D meson decays. They calculated hadron yields using the grand-canonical ensemble with temperature T=170 MeV, fitted the system volume and cluster transverse momentum width to PHENIX data on pion, kaon, and proton spectra, and then simulated dilepton production from hadronic decays including direct and Dalitz decays of light mesons, charmonia, and open charm mesons with acceptance cuts matching the PHENIX detector.

## Key numbers
- Temperature fixed at T=170 MeV for all collision systems based on SHM fits to proton-proton and Au+Au data at sqrt(s)=200 GeV
- Strangeness suppression factor gamma_S=0.6 for proton-proton collisions, increasing to unity in central Au+Au collisions
- Total J/psi production cross section sigma_{J/psi}^{NN}=3.0 microbarns used to normalize charmonium calculations
- PHENIX detector acceptance requires leptons with p_T greater than 200 MeV and rapidity near y=0, with angular coverage phi_min=-3pi/16 to phi_max=5pi/16 and phi_min=11pi/16 to phi_max=19pi/16
- Discrepancies between model and PHENIX data reach factors of 4 to 5 in the low mass region (0.15 to 0.6 GeV) and factors of 2 to 3 in the intermediate mass region (1.1 to 3 GeV) for central Au+Au collisions

## Context
This work extends earlier statistical hadronization model studies by incorporating transport calculations for open charm contributions and exploring correlated semileptonic decays from kaon-antikaon pairs. The results contradict the successful description of dilepton spectra at lower Super-Proton-Synchrotron (SPS) beam energies where vector-meson resonance broadening was observed, and highlight a persistent discrepancy with PHENIX data that had been noted in prior work by the HSD group (2009) and Dusling. The findings suggest that non-hadronic dilepton channels may be needed to explain the PHENIX measurements, motivating further investigation of possible beyond-hadronic sources.
