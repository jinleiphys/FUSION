# Six-dimensional Davidson potential as a dynamical symmetry of the symplectic Interacting Vector Boson Model

> A six-dimensional Davidson potential, introduced within the framework of the Interacting Vector Boson Model (IVBM), is used to describe nuclei that exhibit transitional spectra between the purely rotational and vibrational limits of the theory. The results are shown to relate to a new dynamical symmetry that starts with the $Sp(12,R) \supset SU(1,1) \times SO(6)$ reduction. Exact solutions for the eigenstates of the model Hamiltonian in the basis defined by a convenient subgroup chain of SO(6) are obtained. A comparison of the theoretical results with experimental data for heavy nuclei with transitional spectra illustrates the applicability of the theory.

# Six-dimensional Davidson potential as a dynamical symmetry of the symplectic Interacting Vector Boson Model

## Key claim

The six-dimensional Davidson potential, introduced within the Interacting Vector Boson Model (IVBM) framework, yields a new dynamical symmetry through the reduction chain $Sp(12,R) \supset Sp(2,R) \otimes SO(6)$ that provides exact algebraic solutions for nuclei with mixed rotational-vibrational spectra; the model reproduces experimental data for both well-deformed and transitional nuclei, but the five free parameters must be fitted individually for each nucleus using a $\chi^2$ procedure.

## Method

The authors constructed a Hamiltonian diagonal in the basis defined by the subgroup chain $Sp(12,R) \supset Sp(2,R) \otimes SO(6) \supset SU(1,1) \otimes SU(3) \otimes O(2) \supset SO(3)$, expressed in terms of Casimir operators of the subgroups. They classified basis states using quantum numbers $N$ (total boson number), $\omega$ ($SO(6)$ irrep label), $\nu$ ($O(2)$ label), $(\lambda,\mu)$ ($SU(3)$ labels), and $L$ (angular momentum). The energy eigenvalues were obtained analytically as $E = aN + bN^2 + \alpha_6 \omega(\omega+4) + \alpha_2 \nu^2 + \beta_3 L(L+1)$, and the five parameters were fitted to experimental data for four nuclei using a $\chi^2$ minimization procedure.

## Context

This work extends the application of the Davidson potential from the 5-dimensional Bohr-Mottelson collective model (Elliott et al.) and the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) to the 6-dimensional symplectic IVBM framework. The new dynamical symmetry chain through $Sp(2,R) \otimes SO(6)$ complements the previously known $Sp(12,R) \supset U(3) \supset SO(3)$ reduction used for deformed nuclei (Rowe, Rosensteel) and the $Sp(12,R) \supset Sp(2,R) \otimes SO(3)$ reduction for vibrational motion (Moshinsky). The results enable systematic description of nuclei across the rotational-vibrational transition region, including nuclei at the critical point of shape/phase transition such as $^{152}$Sm with $X(5)$ symmetry (identified experimentally by Casten and Zamfir).
