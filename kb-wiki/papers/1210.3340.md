# Thermodynamical second-order hydrodynamic coefficients

> Transport coefficients in non-conformal second-order hydrodynamics can be classified as either dynamical or thermodynamical. We derive Kubo formuale for the thermodynamical coefficients and compute them at leading perturbative order in a theory with general matter content. We also discuss how to approach their evaluation on the lattice.

## Key claim

The paper derives Kubo formulas for the thermodynamical second-order hydrodynamic coefficients (kappa, lambda_3, and lambda_4) and computes them at leading perturbative order in a general massless theory, but only for the case of vanishing quark-number chemical potentials and zero magnetic fields, and without assuming conformal symmetry.

## Method

The authors classify second-order hydrodynamic coefficients into dynamical and thermodynamical categories, where thermodynamical coefficients are those that can contribute to the stress tensor in a time-independent equilibrium state (even in curved geometry). They derive Euclidean Kubo relations for kappa, lambda_3, and lambda_4 in terms of zero-frequency Euclidean correlation functions of the stress tensor, including necessary contact terms. They then compute these coefficients at leading order in perturbation theory for free scalar fields, gauge fields, and fermions in curved space, using the free-theory actions and evaluating the relevant sum-integrals at finite temperature.

## Key numbers

* For one real scalar field: kappa = -T^2/72, lambda_3 = -T^2/12
* For a single color of gauge field: kappa = T^2/18, lambda_3 = T^2/3
* For fermions: the paper states it follows a similar calculation but does not provide explicit numerical results for the fermion contribution in the truncated text
* The scalar contribution to kappa is computed using the sum-integral identity: sumint_p (p^2)^n/(p^2)^(n+1) = (2n+1)!/(2^(2n) (n!)^2) * T^2/12

## Context

This work extends the classification of second-order hydrodynamic coefficients by Romatschke (2009) and Bhattacharyya (2012) by providing explicit Kubo formulas and leading-order perturbative calculations for the thermodynamical coefficients kappa, lambda_3, and lambda_4. The authors note that their result for kappa agrees with the earlier result of Romatschke and Son. The paper enables future lattice evaluations of these coefficients for QCD, particularly kappa, which the authors argue should be feasible with existing techniques for pure-glue QCD, while lambda_3 and lambda_4 may be prohibitively difficult due to the need for three-point functions.
