# Macroscopic properties of nuclei according to the relativistic mean field theory

> Self-consistent calculations within the relativistic mean field theory (RMFT) were performed for 150 spherical even-even nuclei. The macroscopic part of the binding energy was evaluated by subtracting the Strutinsky shell corrections from the RMFT energy. The parameters of a liquid-drop (LD), like mass formula which approximates the RMFT results, were determined. The mass and isospin dependence of the RMFT mean-square radii constant for the neutron, proton, charge and total density distributions were estimated. The RMFT liquid-drop parameters and the radii constants are compared with similar results obtained with the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with the Gogny force (HFB+Gogny) and phenomenological models.

# Macroscopic properties of nuclei according to the relativistic mean field theory

## Key claim

The macroscopic (liquid-drop) part of the binding energy extracted from self-consistent relativistic mean field theory (RMFT) calculations for 150 spherical even-even nuclei yields volume and surface energies smaller than those from the Gogny force or from phenomenological fits to experimental masses, and the isospin dependence of these terms is weaker than the phenomenological one. The RMFT results are most reliable for nuclei near the beta stability line because the NL3 parameter set was fitted to that region, and the macroscopic-microscopic method may be questionable near drip lines due to weak binding effects of purely quantal origin.

## Method

Self-consistent RMFT calculations with the NL3 parameter set were performed for 150 spherical even-even nuclei spanning isotopes and isotones between the proton and neutron drip lines, using 20 harmonic-oscillator shells with oscillator length constant b = 2.4 MeV. The Strutinsky shell correction (with a 6th-order correction polynomial, Gaussian width gamma = 1.2 hbar-omega, and hbar-omega = 40 A^{-1/3} MeV) was subtracted from the RMFT binding energy to obtain the macroscopic part, which was then fitted to a Myers-Swiatecki-type liquid-drop formula. For mean-square radii, pairing correlations were included via the BCS method using experimental proton and neutron pairing gaps, and the resulting radii constants were fitted to an isospin-dependent formula.

## Key numbers

* Volume coefficient: b_vol = 15.19 MeV (with isospin dependence kappa_vol = 1.66) from the RMFT fit to 150 spherical nuclei
* Surface coefficient: b_surf = 16.81 MeV (with isospin dependence kappa_surf = 1.21) from the same fit
* Coulomb coefficient: b_Coul = 0.68 (corresponding to charge radius constant r0_ch = 1.264 fm)
* Charge diffuseness parameter: C_4 = 1.3
* Root-mean-square deviation of the liquid-drop fit to RMFT macroscopic energies: 1.97 MeV
* Neutron radius constant: r0_n = 1.17 (1 + 0.27 I + 3.38/A) fm
* Proton radius constant: r0_p = 1.22 (1 - 0.15 I + 1.51/A) fm
* Charge radius constant: r0_ch = 1.23 (1 - 0.15 I + 2.47/A) fm
* Proton-to-neutron radius ratio: r_p/r_n = 1.04 (1 - 0.38 I - 1.52/A)
* Root-mean-square deviation of each radius fit: smaller than 0.01 fm

## Context

This work extends the macroscopic-microscopic decomposition previously applied to Skyrme forces (Ref. [4] in the paper) and Gogny forces (Kleb, 2000) to the relativistic mean field theory, using the same set of 150 spherical nuclei as the Gogny study for direct comparison. The RMFT liquid-drop parameters are compared with the traditional Myers-Swiatecki (1967) formula, a modern phenomenological fit (MS-2002) by Pomorski (2002) to experimental masses from Audi (1995), and the Gogny D1S results from Kleb (2000). The radii results are compared with experimental charge radii from Friebel (1995) and neutron radii from Friebel (1995) and Batty (1997), and with earlier RMFT radii calculations for 30 deformed nuclei by Warda (2001). The work enables future investigation of deformed nuclei with various RMFT parameter sets and provides a formula to predict neutron radii from measured charge radii.
