# Borromean binding

> A review is first presented of the Hall--Post inequalities relating $N$-body to $(N-1)$-body energies of quantum bound states. These inequalities are then applied to delimit, in the space of coupling constants, the domain of Borromean binding where a composite system is bound while smaller subsystems are unbound.

# Borromean binding

## Key claim

Borromean binding, where a composite system is bound while all smaller subsystems are unbound, can be rigorously delimited in the space of coupling constants using Hall–Post inequalities, but the actual window for three-body binding without two-body binding is about 20% of the coupling constant range, well above the rigorous lower bound of 2/3 for the ratio g₃/g₂.

## Method

The paper reviews Hall–Post inequalities that relate N-body to (N-1)-body ground-state energies for identical particles interacting via pairwise potentials, then applies these inequalities at zero energy to derive constraints on the critical coupling constants g_N required for N-body binding. For bosons with short-range forces, the decomposition of the translation-invariant Hamiltonian into (N-1)-body pieces yields the inequality N g_N ≥ (N-1) g_{N-1} ≥ ... ≥ 2 g₂, which is saturated only for harmonic oscillator potentials. For fermions, the antisymmetry requirement forces the use of excited two-body states in the decomposition, requiring group-theoretical averaging over spin states.

## Key numbers

- For simple monotonic potentials, g₃/g₂ ≈ 0.80, giving a window of about 20% in coupling constant where three-body systems are bound without two-body binding.
- For four-body systems, g₄/g₂ ≈ 0.67, giving a window of about 13% for four-body binding without three-body binding.
- The rigorous lower bound from Hall–Post inequalities gives g₃/g₂ ≥ 2/3.
- For asymmetric Coulomb three-body ions (m⁺, M⁻, m⁻), stability occurs in the mass ratio range 0.70 ≲ M/m ≲ 1.69 (Mitroy, 2000).
- For four-body Coulomb systems (M⁺, m⁺, M⁻, m⁻), stability occurs for 1/2.2 ≲ M/m ≲ 2.2 (Bressanini et al., 1997; Varga, 1999).

## Context

This review extends the Hall–Post inequality framework (Hall and Post, 1967) to systematically characterize Borromean binding, a phenomenon named after the Borromean rings (Bang, 1993) and exemplified by halo nuclei such as ⁶He, where the (α, n, n) three-body system is bound while neither (α, n) nor (n, n) have bound states. The work connects to the Efimov effect (Efimov, 1970), where vanishing two-body energy produces a myriad of three-body states, and the Thomas collapse (Thomas, 1935), where three-body energy diverges as the potential range shrinks. The inequalities enable rigorous delimitation of coupling-constant domains for Borromean binding in bosonic systems and, with additional group-theoretical machinery, for fermionic systems; the Coulomb case reveals a new class of four-body Borromean molecules where all three-body subsystems are unstable.
