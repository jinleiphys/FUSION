# Improved Determination of the $^{235}\text{U}$ and $^{239}\text{Pu}$ Reactor Antineutrino Cross Sections per Fission

> We present the results of a combined fit of the reactor antineutrino rates and the Daya Bay measurement of $σ_{f,235}$ and $σ_{f,239}$. The combined fit leads to a better determination of the two cross sections per fission: $σ_{f,235} = 6.29 \pm 0.08$ and $σ_{f,239} = 4.24 \pm 0.21 $ in units of $10^{-43} \, \text{cm}^2 / \text{fission}$, with respective uncertainties of about $1.2\%$ and $4.9\%$. Since the respective deviations from the theoretical cross sections per fission are $2.5σ$ and $0.7σ$, we conclude that, if the reactor antineutrino anomaly is not due to active-sterile neutrino oscillations, it is likely that it can be solved with a revaluation of the $^{235}\text{U}$ reactor antineutrino flux. However, the $^{238}\text{U}$, $^{239}\text{Pu}$, and $^{241}\text{Pu}$ fluxes, which have larger uncertainties, could also be significantly different from the theoretical predictions.

## Key claim

The combined fit of reactor antineutrino rates and the Daya Bay measurement yields improved cross sections per fission for 235U and 239Pu, with the 235U value deviating from the theoretical Saclay+Huber prediction by 2.5σ, suggesting that if the reactor antineutrino anomaly is not due to active-sterile neutrino oscillations, it is likely mainly caused by a miscalculation of the 235U flux.

## Method

The author performed a combined least-squares fit of reactor antineutrino rate data from 15 experiments (including Bugey-4, Rovno91, Bugey-3, Gosgen, ILL, Krasnoyarsk87, Krasnoyarsk94, Rovno88, SRP, Nucifer, Chooz, Palo Verde, Daya Bay, RENO, and Double Chooz) together with the Daya Bay measurement of σf,235 and σf,239. The fit used the Saclay+Huber theoretical cross sections per fission as a baseline, with correction coefficients rk for each fission isotope (235U, 238U, 239Pu, 241Pu), and included penalty terms for the minor isotopes 238U and 241Pu with uncertainties of 15% and 10% respectively.

## Key numbers

- σf,235 = 6.29 ± 0.08 (in units of 10⁻⁴³ cm²/fission), determined with 1.2% precision from the combined fit
- σf,239 = 4.24 ± 0.21 (in units of 10⁻⁴³ cm²/fission), determined with 4.9% precision from the combined fit
- The 235U cross section deviates from the theoretical Saclay+Huber value by 2.5σ
- The 239Pu cross section deviates from the theoretical Saclay+Huber value by 0.7σ
- The reactor-rates-only fit gave σf,235 = 6.35 ± 0.09 (1.4% precision) and σf,239 = 3.82 ± 0.43 (11.2% precision)
- The Daya Bay measurement alone gave σf,235 = 6.17 ± 0.17 and σf,239 = 4.27 ± 0.26
- The combined fit achieved χ²_min = 19.5 with 23 degrees of freedom (67% goodness of fit)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Giunti (2016) and the Daya Bay Collaboration (2017) by combining reactor rate data with the direct Daya Bay measurement of the 235U and 239Pu cross sections per fission, improving the precision of both determinations. The results confirm that the reactor antineutrino anomaly, first identified by Mention (2011) following the flux recalculation by Mueller (2011) and Huber (2011), is most likely attributable to the 235U flux rather than active-sterile neutrino oscillations. The improved determination of σf,239 and the constraints on 238U and 241Pu fluxes will inform upcoming experiments such as PROSPECT, SoLid, and STEREO, which aim to measure antineutrino fluxes from highly enriched 235U research reactors.
