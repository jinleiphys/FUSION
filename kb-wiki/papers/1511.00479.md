# Solution of the proton radius puzzle? Low momentum transfer electron scattering data are not enough

> In two recent papers it is argued that the 'proton radius puzzle' can be explained by truncating the electron scattering data to low momentum transfer and fit the rms radius in the low momentum expansion of the form factor. It is shown that this procedure is inconsistent and violates the Fourier theorem. The puzzle cannot be explained in this way.

## Key claim
The claim that the proton radius puzzle can be resolved by restricting electron scattering data analysis to low momentum transfer (Q² < 0.02 or 0.03 (GeV/c)²) is inconsistent and violates the Fourier theorem, because a truncated momentum-space fit cannot determine the charge distribution or rms radius without knowledge of the full Q² range.

## Method
The authors demonstrate analytically that the Fourier transform linking the charge distribution ρ(r) and the form factor G(Q²) requires integration over the full Q² range from 0 to ∞. They construct a "saw tooth" model form factor that is truncated at low Q² and show it produces unphysical oscillating charge distributions at large r, with all higher moments ⟨rⁿ⟩ for n≥4 equal to zero. They then compare the moments from this pathological model to experimental values from full-range fits by Arrington (2007, 2015) and Bernauer (2011, 2013), and critique the statistical methods (F-tests, covariance matrix handling) used in the two papers proposing the low-Q² truncation approach.

## Key numbers
- The electron scattering result for the proton rms radius is R^e_p = 0.877(5) fm (Bernauer 2011, 2013; Arrington 2015).
- The muonic hydrogen Lamb shift result is R^μ_p = 0.8409(4) fm (Pohl 2010, Antognini 2013), a difference of 0.036(5) fm or 7 standard deviations.
- The saw tooth model with ⟨r²⟩ = 0.7056 fm² (corresponding to an rms radius of 0.840 fm) yields ⟨r⁴⟩ = 0 fm⁴ and ⟨r⁶⟩ = 0 fm⁶, compared to experimental values of ⟨r⁴⟩ = 2.59 fm⁴ and ⟨r⁶⟩ = 29.8 fm⁶ from full-range fits.
- The correlation coefficient between linear and quadratic fit parameters in Higinbotham et al. is ρ₂₃ = -0.95; reducing the quadratic term to zero changes the extracted radius from R_p = 0.875 fm to 0.840 fm.
- The full Q² range used in the two independent studies extends to Q² ≤ 10 (GeV/c)².

## Context
This paper directly refutes the proposals of Griffioen, Carlson, and Maddox (2015) and Higinbotham et al. (2015), who argued that restricting electron scattering data to Q² < 0.02 or 0.03 (GeV/c)² could resolve the proton radius puzzle. The authors extend the earlier work of Sick (2014) on minimal Q² requirements and the full-range analyses of Arrington (2007, 2015) and Bernauer (2011, 2013), which already established the larger radius from electron scattering. The paper enables a clearer understanding that any resolution of the puzzle must respect the Fourier relation between form factor and charge distribution, and that low-Q² truncation alone cannot explain the discrepancy with muonic hydrogen results.
