# A finite-temperature Hartree-Fock code for shell-model Hamiltonians

> The codes HFgradZ.py and HFgradT.py find axially symmetric minima of a Hartree-Fock energy functional for a Hamiltonian supplied in a shell-model basis. The functional to be minimized is the Hartree-Fock energy for zero-temperature properties or the Hartree-Fock grand potential for finite-temperature properties. Various constraints may be imposed on the minima.

## Key claim

The paper presents two Python codes, HFgradZ.py and HFgradT.py, that find axially symmetric minima of a Hartree-Fock energy functional (zero temperature) or Hartree-Fock grand potential (finite temperature) using a gradient method with a preconditioner, but the abstract omits that the zero-temperature code can fail to converge when occupation numbers are allowed to change at each iteration step, as demonstrated for the nucleus 148Sm.

## Method

The codes minimize the Hartree-Fock energy (zero temperature) or grand potential (finite temperature) for a Hamiltonian supplied in a shell-model basis, using a hybrid minimization method that combines gradient descent with diagonalization of a modified single-particle Hamiltonian. The variational parameters are the elements of the orthogonal transformation matrix U between the shell-model basis and the orbital basis, updated via a Thouless transformation, and the occupation numbers f_kappa (for finite temperature) updated via a gradient method on auxiliary variables alpha_kappa. Constraints on the expectation value of a user-supplied single-particle operator Q can be imposed either by adding it with a Lagrange multiplier or by constraining its value directly.

## Key numbers

- For 162Dy at zero temperature, the final energy is E = -371.78, agreeing with Table II of Alhassid (2015).
- For 162Dy with a constraining field, the converged solution has E = -370.23 and quadrupole moment Q = 587.5.
- For 148Sm, two nearly degenerate minima have quadrupole moments of 314 and 341 fm^2 respectively.
- For 162Dy, a deformed-spherical phase transition is observed at inverse temperature beta approximately 0.83 MeV^-1.
- The codes require approximately 10 MBy of RAM and run in a few minutes.

## Context

This work extends the gradient method for Hartree-Fock minimization described in Ring and Schuck (1980) and adopted by Robledo (2010, 2011), providing an open-source Python implementation for shell-model Hamiltonians. The codes enable systematic exploration of the nuclear energy surface at both zero and finite temperature, including the study of shape phase transitions and near-degenerate minima, using Hamiltonians from the shell-model community such as those by Alhassid (2008) and Ozen (2013). The finite-temperature code allows investigation of thermal effects in nuclei, though the authors note that the observed phase transition is a well-known artifact of mean-field theory absent in more refined treatments.
