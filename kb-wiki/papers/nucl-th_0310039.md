# Meson loop effects on the pion electromagnetic form factor

> In this work we calculate the meson loop effects on the pion electromagnetic form factor and perform a comparison with experimental data. We show that, even though the meson cloud is not the only nonperturbative process to be considered, its contribution is significant.

# Meson loop effects on the pion electromagnetic form factor

## Key claim

The meson cloud contribution to the pion electromagnetic form factor is significant, but it is not the only nonperturbative process; the calculation provides an upper bound for the cloud contribution because the authors use a pointlike coupling for the virtual pion-photon vertex, which maximizes the cloud effect.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop meson cloud contributions to the pion electromagnetic form factor in the spacelike region by considering Fock state fluctuations of the pion into pseudoscalar-vector meson pairs (πρ and KK* states). They use interaction Lagrangians with monopole form factors at hadronic vertices, introduce seagull and vertex couplings via minimal substitution to maintain gauge invariance, and verify the Ward-Takahashi identity. The loop integrals are evaluated with cut-off parameters Λ chosen close to the corresponding vector meson masses, and the total vertex function is projected onto the form factor F_π(Q²).

## Key numbers

- The meson cloud contribution is evaluated up to Q² = 6 GeV².
- For a cut-off Λ = 0.9 GeV, the cloud contribution from |πρ> states and |KK*> states is shown separately, with the sum of all states plotted against experimental data.
- The mean square radius ⟨r_π²⟩ computed from the cloud contribution is much smaller than the experimental value of 0.439 ± 0.008 fm² (Amendolia et al.).
- At Q² around zero, the cloud contribution represents between 0.15 and 0.40 of the total form factor.
- The cut-off parameter is constrained to lie in the interval 0.5 < Λ < 1.0 GeV, with a preferred range of 0.6–0.7 GeV if the cloud contribution is of order 15% or less.
- Adding the pQCD asymptotic form F_π(Q²) = 16π f_π² α_s / Q² (with f_π = 92.4 MeV and α_s = 0.4) to the cloud contribution for Λ = 0.7 GeV gives a good description of data for Q² > 1 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the meson cloud approach previously applied to nucleon electromagnetic form factors (Friedrich, Miller, Korpa, Thomas, Mosel) and to the pion form factor in the timelike region (Nachmann) into the spacelike region. The authors had previously used similar cloud states to study D⁻/D⁺ asymmetry in πp → DX collisions (Carvalho et al., 2002). The calculation shows that the meson cloud contribution to the pion form factor is less important than for the nucleon, consistent with the larger virtuality required for pion-rho fluctuations compared to neutron-pion fluctuations. The results suggest that a hard cut-off above 0.7–0.8 GeV is difficult to accommodate if one wants to leave room for a sizeable bare pion (pQCD) component, and that another dynamics (such as constituent quark interactions) is needed to describe the low Q² region.
