# Investigation of constraints on few-neutron forces in neutron matter by empirical information on the neutron skin of 48Ca and 208Pb

> The neutron matter equation of state is calculated from two-neutron forces up to fifth order of the chiral expansion and the order-by-order convergence of the predictions is investigated. Based on these equations of state, the binding energies and the neutron and proton density distributions in 208Pb and 48Ca are derived, with particular attention to the neutron skins, the focal point of this paper. Anticipating future experiments which will provide reliable information on the weak charge density in nuclei, the theoretical uncertainties and the possibility of constraining the size of few-neutron forces in neutron matter are discussed.

## Key claim

The neutron skin thicknesses of 48Ca and 208Pb, calculated from chiral two-nucleon forces (2NFs) up to fifth order, are well converged with a theoretical uncertainty of about 0.01 fm; however, current empirical determinations of these skins carry uncertainties too large (around 0.03-0.04 fm) to constrain the size of three-neutron forces in neutron matter, and future experiments must achieve an uncertainty below 0.03 fm to provide useful constraints.

## Method

The neutron matter equation of state (EoS) was calculated using chiral two-nucleon forces from NLO (second order) through N4LO (fifth order) within the particle-particle ladder approximation, without including three-nucleon forces. These EoS were then used as input to a liquid droplet model, where the energy of a nucleus is minimized with respect to Thomas-Fermi density distributions for protons and neutrons, yielding binding energies, charge radii, point proton and neutron radii, and the neutron skin thickness for 48Ca and 208Pb. The analysis considered truncation errors (estimated from the N4LO correction), cutoff variations between 450 and 600 MeV, and uncertainties from the surface energy parameter f0 (60-70 MeV fm5) and from two different phenomenological symmetric matter EoS.

## Key numbers

- For 48Ca at N3LO, the predicted neutron skin from chiral 2NFs is S = 0.159 ± 0.009 fm.
- For 208Pb at N3LO, the predicted neutron skin from chiral 2NFs is S = 0.14 ± 0.01 fm.
- The truncation error from the chiral expansion of the 2NF is about 0.001 fm for both nuclei.
- The current empirical average for the neutron skin of 48Ca is 0.13 ± 0.03 fm.
- The current empirical average for the neutron skin of 208Pb is 0.16 ± 0.04 fm.
- The difference between the 2NF-based prediction and the empirical average is 0.03 ± 0.03 fm for 48Ca and 0.02 ± 0.04 fm for 208Pb.
- Future experiments must achieve an uncertainty ΔS_emp < 0.03 fm to provide a useful constraint on three-neutron forces.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the neutron matter EoS using chiral 2NFs up to fourth order (Sammarruca et al., 2015) by including the fifth-order (N4LO) interaction from Entem et al. (2015). The method connects microscopic EoS to finite-nucleus properties via a liquid droplet model previously used with relativistic meson-theoretic potentials (Alonso and Sammarruca, 2003). The results provide a baseline for upcoming parity-violating electron scattering experiments (PREX on 208Pb and C-REX on 48Ca at Jefferson Laboratory) to isolate the effects of three-neutron forces, complementing ab initio calculations of 48Ca (Hagen et al., 2016) and other chiral EFT studies of neutron matter (Kruger et al., 2013).
