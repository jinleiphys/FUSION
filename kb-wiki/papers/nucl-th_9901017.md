# Study of thermometers for measuring a microcanonical phase transition in nuclear fragmentation

> The aim of this work is to study how the thermodynamic temperature is related to the known thermometers for nuclei especially in view of studying the microcanonical phase transition. We find within the MMMC-model that the "S-shape" of the caloric equation of state e^*(T) which is the signal of a phase transition in a system with conserved energy, can be seen in the experimentally accessible slope temperatures T_slope for different particle types and also in the isotopic temperatures T_He-Li. The isotopic temperatures T_H-He are weaker correlated to the shape of the thermodynamic temperature and therefore are less favorable to study the signal of a microcanonical phase transition. We also show that the signal is very sensitive to variations in mass of the source.

# Study of thermometers for measuring a microcanonical phase transition in nuclear fragmentation

## Key claim

Within the MMMC statistical fragmentation model, the "S-shape" signal of a microcanonical phase transition in the caloric equation of state can be reproduced by experimentally accessible slope temperatures and isotopic temperatures, but the signal is very sensitive to variations in the mass of the source and requires energy binning finer than 0.2 MeV per nucleon to be detected.

## Method

The authors used the microcanonical Metropolis Monte Carlo (MMMC) model to simulate the fragmentation of hot compound nuclei, generating freeze-out configurations that respect conservation of mass, charge, linear and angular momentum, and total energy. They then computed slope temperatures by fitting calculated kinetic energy spectra of protons, deuterons, tritons, 3He, and alpha particles with a surface-evaporating Maxwellian source formula, and computed isotopic temperatures from the yields of 6Li/7Li and 3He/4He (T_He-Li) and 2H/3H and 3He/4He (T_H-He). The calculated events were also subjected to the software filter of the INDRA experimental setup to test the observability of the phase transition signal under realistic detection conditions.

## Key numbers

- The freeze-out volume radius used in MMMC simulations for the energy region of 1 to 4 MeV per nucleon is approximately r_f = 2.2 A^(1/3) fm, corresponding to about 6 times the normal nuclear volume.
- The shift between unfiltered slope temperatures for Z=1 particles and Z=2 particles is approximately 0.3 MeV, attributed to higher Coulomb repulsion.
- Increasing the mass of the source from 122Xe to 132Xe shifts the transition signal to higher excitation energies by approximately 0.5 MeV per nucleon.
- For 80Se, the evaporation-fission phase transition appears as a very weak signal at E* ≈ 1.5 A.MeV and T_thd ≈ 3.8 MeV, with a stronger signal at T_thd ≈ 4.6 MeV from a different fission-multifragmentation phase transition.
- The current INDRA setup can realize energy binning ΔE* ≈ 0.4 A.MeV for the energy region of 1 to 4 A.MeV, while detecting the transition requires binning less than 0.2 A.MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical predictions of microcanonical phase transitions in nuclear fragmentation from Gross (1990, 1996) and Hüller (1994), and the statistical multifragmentation models of Bondorf et al. (1995) and Sneppen. It directly tests the ability of nuclear thermometers, slope temperatures and isotopic temperatures from Albergo et al. (1985), to reproduce the "S-shape" caloric curve, building on earlier experimental indications from Chbihi et al. (1998). The results enable experimental searches for microcanonical phase transitions using the INDRA detector, while cautioning that the mass of the source must be kept constant across the energy range, contrary to the interpretation of the caloric curve in Pochodzalla et al. (1995) which the authors attribute to mass variation effects.
