# Nuclear Bag Model and Nuclear Magnetic Moments

> In 1991, we proposed a model in which nucleus is treated as a spherical symmetric MIT bag and nucleon satisfies the MIT bag model boundary condition. The model was employed to calculate nuclear magnetic moments. The results are in good agreement with experiment data. Now, we found this model is still interesting and illuminating.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a nucleus can be treated as a spherical MIT bag with a sharp boundary, containing nucleons moving in a uniformly distributed meson field, and that this simple model can reproduce experimental nuclear magnetic moments quite well, though the model is acknowledged as naive and further work is needed to reproduce single-particle energy levels correctly.

## Method

The nucleus is modeled as a spherical symmetric MIT bag of radius \(R = r_0 A^{1/3}\) with \(r_0 = 1.2\) fm, inside which nucleons satisfy the Dirac equation with an effective mass \(\tilde{M}\). The nucleon wave functions are determined by the MIT linear boundary condition at the bag surface, and the magnetic moment is calculated from the sum of a Dirac current term and an anomalous nucleon current term, with the effective nucleon mass ratio fixed to \(\tilde{M}/M = 0.68\) for all nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The wave number \(x_{n\kappa}\) for the \(0s_{1/2}\) orbit in \({}^{208}\)Pb with \(\tilde{M} = M = 938.5\) MeV is 3.10.
- The wave number \(x_{n\kappa}\) for the \(0h_{11/2}\) orbit in \({}^{208}\)Pb with \(\tilde{M} = M = 938.5\) MeV is 9.21.
- The calculated magnetic moment for \({}^{15}\)N is \(\mu = -0.205\) n.m., compared to the experimental value of \(-0.283\) n.m.
- The calculated magnetic moment for \({}^{17}\)O is \(\mu = -1.593\) n.m., compared to the experimental value of \(-1.894\) n.m.
- The calculated magnetic moment for \({}^{209}\)Bi is \(\mu = 3.920\) n.m., compared to the experimental value of 4.080 n.m.
- The calculated magnetic moment for \({}^{207}\)Pb is \(\mu = 0.696\) n.m., compared to the experimental value of 0.582 n.m.
- The calculated magnetic moment for \({}^{91}\)Zr is \(\mu = -5.712\) n.m., which deviates significantly from the experimental value of \(-1.304\) n.m.

## Context

This work extends the MIT bag model, originally developed for hadronic static properties by Chodos and others, to the nuclear scale, contrasting with the more extreme quark shell model (QSM) of Petry and others which treats the entire nucleus as a single bag of 3A quarks. The model provides a simpler calculation of nuclear magnetic moments compared to the core-polarization approach of Nishizaki and others, though it is less accurate for isoscalar magnetic moments than the models of Funstahl and Ichii. The results suggest that a sharp-boundary bag picture of the nucleus is not far from reality, but the model requires further development to correctly reproduce single-particle energy levels.
