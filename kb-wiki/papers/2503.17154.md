# Quantum fluctuations of quarks and gluons in nuclei

> Acceptance talk for the 2024 Kenneth G. Wilson Award for Excellence in Lattice Field Theory: For key contributions to lattice QCD studies of noise reduction in nuclear systems, the structure of nuclei, and transverse-momentum dependent hadronic structure functions.

## Key claim

The Collins-Soper kernel, which governs the evolution of transverse-momentum dependent hadronic structure functions, can be determined from lattice QCD with fully quantified uncertainties using the large momentum effective theory framework, and the resulting nonperturbative QCD determination is consistent with the most recent global fits while being more precise across the range \(b_T \lesssim 1\) fm constrained by data.

## Method

The Collins-Soper kernel was extracted from ratios of Euclidean quasi beam functions computed on the lattice, using the Ebert-Stewart-Zhao formula that cancels soft factor contributions and incorporates perturbative LaMET matching factors. Calculations employed quasi TMD wavefunctions from two-point correlation functions with clover valence fermions on MILC gauge ensembles at three lattice spacings (0.09, 0.12, and 0.15 fm), with perturbative matching performed at two-loop level and resummation of momentum logarithms, using an unexpanded next-to-next-to-leading-log scheme to account for power-suppressed effects at finite momentum.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit parameters for the Collins-Soper kernel parameterization: \(c_0 = 0.032(12)\) and \(k_1 = 0.22(8)\), achieving \(\chi^2 / \text{dof} = 0.39\) for \(b_T \in [0.09, 0.9]\) fm and \(a \in [0.09, 0.15]\) fm
- Three lattice spacings used: \(a \in \{0.09, 0.12, 0.15\}\) fm in a mixed-action setup with clover valence fermions and MILC gauge fields
- The signal-to-noise ratio for nucleon correlators decays exponentially with rate \(M_N - 3m_\pi/2\); for \(A\) nucleons the rate is \(A(M_N - 3m_\pi/2)\)
- For unphysically heavy quark masses, SNR degradation scales as \(M_N - 3m_\pi/2 \sim 800\text{ MeV} - m_\pi/2\), half as severe at \(m_\pi \sim 800\) MeV as in the chiral limit

## Context

This work extends the line of lattice QCD calculations of the Collins-Soper kernel initiated by Shanahan, Zhao, and Wagman (2020) in quenched QCD, and later refined by the same group (2021) and the LPC Collaboration (2022) using quasi TMD wavefunctions. The results are consistent with phenomenological global fits by groups such as BLNY (Landry et al., 2002), SV19 (Scimemi and Vladimirov, 2019), Pavia19 (Bacchetta et al., 2019), MAP22 (Bacchetta et al., 2022), ART23 (Moos et al., 2023), and IFY23 (Isaacson et al., 2023), while excluding some older fits. The hadron-state independence of the Collins-Soper kernel means these results apply universally to nucleon and nuclear scattering processes, and future work will incorporate them into global fits and cross-section predictions for Drell-Yan, semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering, and neutrino-nucleus scattering experiments including DUNE.
