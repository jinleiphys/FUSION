# Deuteron production from phase-space coalescence in the UrQMD approach

> UrQMD phase-space coalescence calculations for the production of deuterons are compared with available data for various reactions from the GSI/FAIR energy regime up to LHC. It is found that the production process of deuterons, as reflected in their rapidity and transverse momentum distributions in p+p, p+A and A+A collisions at a beam energies starting from the GSI energy regime around 1 AGeV and up to the LHC, are in good agreement with experimental data. We further explore the energy and centrality dependence of the d/p ratios. Finally, we discuss anti-deuteron production for selected systems. Overall, a good description of the experimental data is observed. The results are also compatible with thermal model estimates. Most importantly this good description is based only on a single set of coalescence parameters that is independent of energy system size and can also be applied for anti-deuterons.

## Key claim

Deuteron production in p+p, p+A, and A+A collisions from GSI/FAIR energies up to LHC can be described by a single set of phase-space coalescence parameters (Δp_max = 0.285 GeV/c and Δr_max = 3.575 fm) that is independent of energy, system size, and also applicable to anti-deuterons, provided the coalescence condition includes both a relative momentum and a relative distance cut.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model, which simulates hadronic scattering, resonance decays, and string dynamics, and added a phase-space coalescence module for deuteron formation. For each proton-neutron pair, the relative momentum Δp and relative distance Δr are evaluated in the two-particle rest frame at equal times after propagating the earlier-decoupled particle to the later freeze-out time; pairs satisfying Δp < 0.285 GeV/c and Δr < 3.575 fm are marked as deuteron candidates. A statistical spin-isospin factor of 3/8 is applied to account for the deuteron quantum numbers (S=1, I=0), and the constituent nucleons are removed from the phase-space distribution.

## Key numbers

- Coalescence parameters: Δp_max = 0.285 GeV/c and Δr_max = 3.575 fm, used for all systems and energies.
- Deuteron yield dN/dy in p+p collisions at √s_NN = 0.9 TeV: UrQMD (0.96 ± 0.05)×10⁻⁴, ALICE (1.12 ± 0.09 ± 0.09)×10⁻⁴.
- Deuteron yield dN/dy in p+p collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV: UrQMD (1.47 ± 0.06)×10⁻⁴, ALICE (1.53 ± 0.05 ± 0.13)×10⁻⁴.
- Deuteron yield dN/dy in p+p collisions at √s_NN = 7 TeV: UrQMD (2.05 ± 0.09)×10⁻⁴, ALICE (2.02 ± 0.02 ± 0.17)×10⁻⁴.
- Anti-deuteron yield dN/dy in p+p collisions at √s_NN = 0.9 TeV: UrQMD (1.00 ± 0.05)×10⁻⁴, ALICE (1.11 ± 0.10 ± 0.09)×10⁻⁴.
- Anti-deuteron yield dN/dy in p+p collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV: UrQMD (1.55 ± 0.07)×10⁻⁴, ALICE (1.37 ± 0.04 ± 0.12)×10⁻⁴.
- Anti-deuteron yield dN/dy in p+p collisions at √s_NN = 7 TeV: UrQMD (2.22 ± 0.09)×10⁻⁴, ALICE (1.92 ± 0.02 ± 0.15)×10⁻⁴.
- d/p and anti-d/anti-p ratios in p+p collisions are reported at √s_NN = 53, 900, 2760, and 7000 GeV (Figure 2).
- d/p ratio in central Au+Au collisions (b ≤ 4.6 fm, |y|<0.3) is calculated at √s_NN = 2, 5, 7.7, 11.5, 14.5, 17, and 19.6 GeV (Figure 10).

## Context

This work extends the phase-space coalescence approach previously applied in the Wigner-function formalism by Gyulassy, Frankel, and Remler (1982) and by Nagle (1996) to a wide range of collision systems and energies using the UrQMD transport model. It provides a benchmark for deuteron production from GSI/FAIR energies up to LHC, showing that coalescence with a single parameter set reproduces experimental data as well as thermal model fits (Andronic, 2012) do, and it enables systematic studies of cluster formation in future FAIR and LHC measurements. The results also complement HBT studies by providing information on the two-nucleon phase-space distribution at kinetic freeze-out.
