# Probing the chiral and $U(1)$ axial symmetry restoration via meson susceptibilities in holographic QCD

> We investigate the restoration patterns of chiral and $U(1)$ axial symmetries at finite temperature using a soft-wall holographic QCD model. The study employs two distinct parameter sets (Case I and Case II), both calibrated to reproduce a pseudocritical temperature $T_{\rm pc} \sim 155$ MeV and the physical pion mass. The temperature dependence of the light and strange quark condensates confirms a smooth chiral crossover transition, with pseudocritical temperatures of $T_{\rm pc}=0.157$ GeV and $T_{\rm pc}=0.154$ GeV for Cases I and II, respectively. The screening masses of chiral partner mesons ($π$-$σ$ and $η$-$a_0$) become degenerate near $T_{\rm pc}$, providing a clear signature of chiral symmetry restoration. Analysis of the corresponding meson susceptibilities further supports this conclusion. However, the indicator for $U(1)$ axial symmetry restoration, $χ_π- χ_{a_0}$, vanishes at a temperature $T \sim 0.190 $ GeV, which indicates a distinct restoration scale with chiral symmetry restoration scale within the present holographic framework. The temperature-dependent topological susceptibility $χ_{\rm top}^{1/4}$ is also computed, showing a sharp drop near $T_{\rm pc}$ and a subsequent slight decrease. While the model qualitatively captures established features of the chiral transition, the results highlight a limitation in the qualitative description of the $U(1)$ axial anomaly compared to LQCD in our work.

## Key claim

Using a soft-wall holographic QCD model with two parameter sets, the chiral crossover occurs near 155 MeV, but the $U(1)$ axial symmetry restoration indicator $\chi_\pi - \chi_{a_0}$ vanishes at a distinctly higher temperature of about 0.190 GeV, revealing a separation between the two restoration scales within this holographic framework, though the authors note a limitation in the model's qualitative description of the $U(1)$ axial anomaly compared to lattice QCD.

## Method

The authors employ a soft-wall AdS/QCD model with an AdS-Schwarzschild black hole background to introduce finite temperature, using two parameter sets (Case I with a hyperbolic-tangent dilaton profile and standard 5D mass, Case II with a quadratic dilaton and modified 5D mass) both calibrated to reproduce $T_{\rm pc} \sim 155$ MeV and the physical pion mass. They solve the equations of motion for the scalar field vacuum expectation values $\chi_l(z)$ and $\chi_s(z)$ numerically, then compute the two-point correlation functions and susceptibilities for the scalar mesons ($a_0$, $\sigma$) and pseudoscalar mesons ($\pi$, $\eta$) from the on-shell action, and also compute the topological susceptibility $\chi_{\rm top}^{1/4}$.

## Key numbers

- Pseudocritical temperatures for chiral crossover: $T_{\rm pc}=0.157$ GeV for Case I and $T_{\rm pc}=0.154$ GeV for Case II
- The $U(1)$ axial symmetry restoration indicator $\chi_\pi - \chi_{a_0}$ vanishes at $T \sim 0.190$ GeV, distinctly above the chiral pseudocritical temperature
- The model is calibrated to reproduce a pseudocritical temperature $T_{\rm pc} \sim 155$ MeV and the physical pion mass
- The topological susceptibility $\chi_{\rm top}^{1/4}$ shows a sharp drop near $T_{\rm pc}$ and a subsequent slight decrease with increasing temperature

## Context

This work extends previous holographic QCD studies of chiral phase transitions in soft-wall models by Colangelo (2011), Chelabi (2015), and Fang (2016), and connects to lattice QCD results on meson susceptibilities by the HotQCD collaboration (2012), Bhattacharya (2014), and Aoki (2021). The calculation of meson susceptibilities as probes of chiral and $U(1)$ axial symmetry restoration follows the theoretical framework developed by Cohen (1996) and Kawaguchi (2020, 2023). The results enable comparison between holographic predictions and lattice QCD for the temperature dependence of topological susceptibility and the separation of chiral and $U(1)$ axial restoration scales, while highlighting the need for improved holographic modeling of the $U(1)$ axial anomaly.
