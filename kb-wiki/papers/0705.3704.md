# Variation of fundamental constants: theory and observations

> Review of recent works devoted to the variation of the fundamental constants is presented including atomic clocks, quasar absorption spectra, and Oklo natural nuclear reactor data. Assuming linear variation with time we can compare different results. From the quasar absorption spectra: $\dotμ/μ=(1 \pm 3) \times 10^{-16}$ yr$^{-1}$. A combination of this result and the atomic clock results gives the best limt on variation of $α$: $\dotα/α=(-0.8 \pm 0.8) \times 10^{-16}$ yr$^{-1}$. The Oklo natural reactor gives the best limit on the variation of $m_s/Λ_{QCD}$ where $m_s$ is the strange quark mass. Huge enhancement of the relative variation effects happens in transitions between close atomic, molecular and nuclear energy levels. We suggest several new cases where the levels are very narrow. Large enhancement of the variation effects is also possible in cold atomic and molecular collisions near Feshbach resonance. Massive bodies (stars or galaxies) can also affect physical constants. They have large scalar charge $S$ proportional to number of particles which produces a Coulomb-like scalar field $U=S/r$. This leads to a variation of the fundamental constants proportional to the gravitational potential, e.g. $δα/ α= k_αδ(GM/ r c^2)$. We compare different manifestations of this effect.The strongest limit $k_α+0.17 k_e= (-3.5\pm 6) \times 10^{-7}$.

## Key claim

Assuming linear variation with time, the best limit on the variation of the fine structure constant is \(\dot{\alpha}/\alpha = (-0.8 \pm 0.8) \times 10^{-16}\) yr\(^{-1}\), obtained by combining quasar absorption spectra results with atomic clock data; the Oklo natural reactor provides the best limit on the variation of the strange quark mass parameter \(X_s = m_s/\Lambda_{QCD}\), but cannot give any limit on the variation of \(\alpha\) because the effect of \(\alpha\) there is much smaller than the effect of \(X_s\) and should be neglected.

## Method

The paper reviews observational constraints on the variation of fundamental constants from three main sources: atomic clock comparisons (optical and microwave), quasar absorption spectra (optical and microwave lines), and the Oklo natural nuclear reactor data. For quasar spectra, relativistic many-body calculations of the coefficients \(q\) relating atomic transition frequencies to \(\alpha^2\) are performed for a set of astrophysically relevant ions (Mg, Fe II, Cr II, etc.), enabling a measurement of \(\delta\alpha/\alpha\) by comparing cosmic and laboratory frequencies. For the Oklo analysis, the shift of a 0.1 eV neutron-capture resonance in \(^{149}\)Sm is estimated as a function of variations in \(\alpha\), the light quark mass parameter \(X_q = m_q/\Lambda_{QCD}\), and the strange quark mass parameter \(X_s = m_s/\Lambda_{QCD}\), with the measured bound on the resonance shift yielding a limit on \(\dot{X}_s/X_s\). The paper also discusses enhanced sensitivity from nearly degenerate levels in atoms (Dy), molecules (NH\(_3\), LaS, LaO, etc.), the \(^{229}\)Th nuclear clock, and cold collisions near Feshbach resonances, as well as spatial variations due to the scalar field of massive bodies parameterized by coefficients \(k_\alpha\), \(k_e\), and \(k_q\).

## Key numbers

- From quasar absorption spectra: \(\dot{\mu}/\mu = \dot{X}_e/X_e = (1 \pm 3) \times 10^{-16}\) yr\(^{-1}\) (assuming linear time variation).
- Combined quasar and atomic clock result: \(\dot{\alpha}/\alpha = (-0.8 \pm 0.8) \times 10^{-16}\) yr\(^{-1}\).
- From Oklo natural reactor: \(|\dot{X}_s/X_s| < 10^{-18}\) yr\(^{-1}\) (assuming linear time dependence over the last 2 billion years).
- From the Keck telescope data (143 absorption systems, redshift range \(0.2 < z < 4.2\)): \(\delta\alpha/\alpha = (-0.543 \pm 0.116) \times 10^{-5}\); assuming linear time dependence gives \(d\ln\alpha/dt = (6.40 \pm 1.35) \times 10^{-16}\) per year.
- Re-analysis of VLT data: \(\delta\alpha/\alpha = (-0.44 \pm 0.16) \times 10^{-5}\).
- From the Dy atom experiment: \(\dot{\alpha}/\alpha = (-2.7 \pm 2.6) \times 10^{-15}\) yr\(^{-1}\).
- From NH\(_3\) spectra at redshift \(z=0.6847\): \(\delta\mu/\mu = (-0.6 \pm 1.9) \times 10^{-6}\).
- From the \(^{199}\)Hg\(^+\) / \(^{133}\)Cs frequency ratio measurement: \(\dot{Y}/Y = (0.37 \pm 0.39) \times 10^{-15}\) yr\(^{-1}\).
- From the half-year variation of the Sun's gravitational potential on Earth, using the Cs/Hg ratio: \(k_\alpha + 0.17 k_e = (-3.5 \pm 6) \times 10^{-7}\).
- From the Cs/H ratio measurement (Ashby): \(k_\alpha + 0.13 k_q = (-1 \pm 17) \times 10^{-7}\).
- From the Cs/H ratio measurement (BW): \(|k_\alpha + 0.13 k_q| < 2.5 \times 10^{-5}\).

## Context

This review by Flambaum (2007) synthesizes results from atomic clock comparisons (e.g., Prestage 1995, Marion 2003, Bize 2005, Peik 2004), quasar absorption spectra (Murphy 2003, Chand 2004, Levshakov 2005), and the Oklo natural reactor (Flambaum and Shuryak 2002, Lam 2005) to place constraints on the variation of \(\alpha\), \(\mu\), and quark mass parameters. It extends earlier work on enhanced sensitivity from nearly degenerate levels in atoms (Dzuba 1999, Budker 2004) and molecules (DeMille 2002, Flambaum and Kozlov 2006), and introduces the concept of spatial variation of constants near massive bodies via scalar fields (Flambaum and Shuryak 2007). The paper enables future searches using the \(^{229}\)Th nuclear clock, cold collisions near Feshbach resonances, and space missions with atomic clocks to probe both temporal and spatial variations of fundamental constants.
