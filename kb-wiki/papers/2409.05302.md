# Strong decays of $a_0$, $f_0$, $f_2$, and $K^*_2$ resonances as dynamically generated states of two vector mesons

> The two-body strong decays of the $f_0(1500)$, $f_0(1710)$, $a_0(1710)$, $f_2(1270)$, $f_2'(1525)$, and $K_2^*(1430)$ resonances are investigated, assuming them as dynamically generated states of two vector mesons via $s$-wave interactions. The partial decay widths of all the possible two-body pseudoscalar meson-pseudoscalar meson final states are calculated considering the triangular diagrams. It is found that the ratios of branching fractions are similar to the previous results for most channels, which were obtained by using the real-axis method and considering the box diagrams. However, there are also differences. In addition, our focus is on the partial decay widths. More precise experimental measurements are needed to test the model calculations and determine the nature of these scalar and tensor mesons. It is anticipated that the BES\uppercase\expandafter{\romannumeral3}, Belle\uppercase\expandafter{\romannumeral2} and LHCb collaborations will conduct these measurements in the future.

## Key claim

The paper calculates two-body strong decay widths of scalar and tensor mesons (f₀(1500), f₀(1710), a₀(1710), f₂(1270), f₂'(1525), K₂*(1430)) assuming they are dynamically generated states from s-wave vector-vector interactions, finding that while many branching fraction ratios agree with previous real-axis method results using box diagrams, some partial decay widths deviate significantly from experimental data, particularly for f₀(1500) and f₂'(1525).

## Method

The authors use the chiral unitary approach to extract complex coupling constants g_{RVV} at the pole positions of each resonance from unitarized vector-vector scattering amplitudes. They then compute partial decay widths for R → PP (two pseudoscalar mesons) via triangular diagrams where the resonance couples to two vector mesons that decay into pseudoscalars by exchanging a pseudoscalar meson, using a cutoff Λ_t = 1350 MeV as the only free parameter fitted to f₂(1270) → ππ and f₂(1270) → ηη data.

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of scalar and tensor mesons as dynamically generated states from vector-vector interactions, previously studied by Molina (2008), Geng (2008), and Geng (2009) using the chiral unitary approach with box diagrams. The present calculation uses triangular diagrams instead of box diagrams and provides partial decay widths (not just ratios) for f₀(1500), f₀(1710), a₀(1710), f₂(1270), f₂'(1525), and K₂*(1430). The results enable future experimental tests by BESIII, Belle II, and LHCb collaborations to distinguish between molecular, q\bar{q}, and glueball interpretations of these resonances, particularly for the ambiguous f₀(1500)/f₀(1370) assignment and the a₀(1710) isovector partner.
