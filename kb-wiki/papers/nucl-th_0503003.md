# Thermal Recombination: Beyond the Valence Quark Approximation

> Quark counting rules derived from recombination models agree well with data on hadron production at intermediate transverse momenta in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. They convey a simple picture of hadrons consisting only of valence quarks. We discuss the inclusion of higher Fock states that add sea quarks and gluons to the hadron structure. We show that, when recombination occurs from a thermal medium, hadron spectra remain unaffected by the inclusion of higher Fock states. However, the quark number scaling for elliptic flow is somewhat affected. We discuss the implications for our understanding of data from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider.

## Key claim

When recombination occurs from a thermal medium, hadron spectra remain unaffected by the inclusion of higher Fock states (sea quarks and gluons), but the quark number scaling for elliptic flow is somewhat affected; however, a 30% gluon contribution is compatible with existing RHIC data.

## Method

The authors extended the constituent quark recombination model by including higher Fock states in the hadron wavefunction expansion on the light cone, using a Boltzmann approximation for the thermal medium. They computed the emission probability for hadrons from a thermal ensemble by summing over all Fock state components, and derived the elliptic flow \(v_2\) for hadrons with higher Fock states using a narrow wavefunction approximation and more realistic light-cone distribution amplitudes for mesons and baryons.

## Key numbers

- The relevant scale for recombination falls into the range \((\pi T)^2 \leq Q^2 \leq (2\pi T)^2\); for a quark-gluon plasma near hadronization (\(T_c \approx 170\) MeV), this implies \(Q\) is in the range \(0.5\ldots 1\) GeV.
- The ratio of prefactors \(n_\nu/n_1\) for mesons and baryons in the scaled elliptic flow equation is \(\frac{3}{2}\frac{s+2}{s+3}\), ranging between 1 (pure valence state) and 3/2 (infinite sea quarks and gluons).
- For the numerical estimate, the authors considered two cases: (i) lowest Fock state only (\(C_1=1, C_2=0\)) and (ii) moderate higher Fock state contribution (\(C_1=0.7, C_2=0.3\)), i.e., a 30% component containing an additional gluon.
- The relative difference \((\tilde v_2^{\rm (B)}-\tilde v_2^{\rm (M)})/(\tilde v_2^{\rm (B)}+\tilde v_2^{\rm (M)})\) was computed for three sizes of the higher Fock state component: 0%, 30%, and 50%.

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark recombination models of Voloshin (2002), Fries et al. (2003), and others, which successfully explained baryon enhancement and elliptic flow scaling at RHIC using only valence quarks. The paper addresses criticism that such models oversimplify hadron structure by showing that thermal recombination is "egalitarian", the emission probability per spin-flavor degree of freedom is independent of Fock state complexity. It enables future work to incorporate gluon degrees of freedom during hadronization while noting that the connection between measured elliptic flow and quark elliptic flow may be less straightforward than previously assumed.
