# Magnetic Properties of Quantized Vortices in Neutron $^3P_2$ Superfluids in Neutron Stars

> We discuss quantized vortices in neutron $^3P_2$ superfluids, which are believed to realize in high density neutron matter such as neutron stars. By using the Ginzburg-Landau free energy for $^3P_2$ superfluids, we determine the ground state in the absence and presence of the external magnetic field, and numerically construct $^3P_2$ quantized vortices in the absence and presence of the external magnetic field along the vortex axis (poloidal) or angular direction (toroidal). We find in certain situations the spontaneous magnetization of the vortex core, whose typical magnitude is about $10^{7-8}$ Gauss, but the net magnetic field in a neutron star is negligible because of the ratio of the vortex core size $\sim 10$fm and the intervortex distance $\sim 10^{-6}$m in a vortex lattice.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that quantized vortices in neutron \(^3P_2\) superfluids can exhibit spontaneous magnetization of the vortex core with a typical magnitude of about \(10^{7-8}\) Gauss, but the net magnetic field averaged over a vortex lattice in a neutron star is negligible because the vortex core size (\(\sim 10\) fm) is much smaller than the intervortex distance (\(\sim 10^{-6}\) m).

## Method

The authors used the Ginzburg-Landau (GL) free energy for \(^3P_2\) superfluids, including terms up to sixth order and magnetic field contributions, to determine the ground state in the absence and presence of external magnetic fields. They numerically constructed quantized vortex solutions by solving the GL equations with an Ansatz involving three integers (winding number \(l\), rotation index \(n\), and semi-topological winding \(m\)) and profile functions \(f_1, f_2, g\) depending on the radial coordinate, considering both cylindrical (\(n=1\)) and Cartesian (\(n=0\)) bases for the tensor order parameter.

## Key numbers

- Vortex core magnetization magnitude: about \(10^{7-8}\) Gauss
- Vortex core size: \(\sim 10\) fm
- Intervortex distance in a vortex lattice: \(\sim 1.7 \times 10^{-6}\) m (for typical neutron star parameters with period \(P = 1\) ms, effective mass \(M^* = 900\) MeV, radius \(R = 10\) km)
- Number of vortices in a rotating neutron star: \(N_v \sim 1.9 \times 10^{19}\) for the same typical parameters
- Ground state parameter \(r\) for the total free energy including sixth order and magnetic terms: \(r \sim -0.572\) for \(H = 10^{15}\) Gauss
- The UN phase (\(r = -1/2\)) is the ground state when only \(f_{2+4}+f_6\) is considered; the \(D_4\) BN phase (\(r = -1\)) is the ground state when only \(f_{2+4}+f_H\) is considered with magnetic field along the \(z\) axis
- The cross point between \(n=0\) and \(n=1\) configurations occurs at \(r = -5/8\)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of \(^3P_2\) superfluids by Richardson (1972), Muzikar (1980), and Sauls (1982) who first discussed vortex structures and spontaneous magnetization in these systems. The paper builds on the classification of ground states by Mermin (1974) and Sauls (1978) for the nematic phase, and incorporates the sixth order term and magnetic field effects that were not fully treated in previous vortex calculations. The results enable future studies of vortex dynamics and magnetic field evolution in neutron stars, particularly for understanding pulsar glitch mechanisms and cooling processes that involve superfluid components.
