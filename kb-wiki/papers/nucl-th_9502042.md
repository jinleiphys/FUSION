# Scissors Modes and Spin Excitations in Light Nuclei including $ΔN$=2 excitations: Behaviour of $^8Be$ and $^{10}Be$

> Shell model calculations are performed for magnetic dipole excitations in $^8{Be}$ and $^{10}{Be}$ in which all valence configurations plus $2\hbarω$ excitations are allowed (large space). We study both the orbital and spin excitations. The results are compared with the `valence space only' calculations (small space). The cumulative energy weighted sums are calculated and compared for the $J=0^+$ $T$=0 to $J=1^+$ $T$=1 excitations in $^8{Be}$ and for $J=0^+$ $T$=1 to both $J=1^+$ $T$=1 and $J$=$1^+$ $T$=2 excitations in $^{10}{Be}$. We find for the $J=0^+$ $T$=1 to $J=1^+$ $T$=1 isovector {\underline {spin}} transitions in $^{10}{Be}$ that the summed strength in the {\underline {large}} space is less than in the {\underline {small}} space. We find that the high energy energy-weighted isovector orbital strength is smaller than the low energy strength for transitions in which the isospin is changed, but for $J=0^+$ $T$=1 to $J=1^+$ $T$=1 in $^{10}{Be}$ the high energy strength is larger. We find that the low lying orbital strength in $^{10}{Be}$ is anomalously small, when an attempt is made to correlate it with the $B(E2)$ strength to the lowest $2^+$ states. On the other hand a sum rule of Zheng and Zamick which concerns the total $B(E2)$ strength is reasonably satisfied in both $^8{Be}$ and $^{10}{Be}$. The Wigner supermultiplet scheme is a useful guide in analyzing shell model results. In $^{10}Be$ and with a $Q \cdot Q$ interaction the T=1 and T=2 scissors modes are degenerate, with the latter carrying 5/3 of the T=1 strength.

## Key claim

Shell model calculations including $2\hbar\omega$ excitations (large space) show that for the $J=0^+$ $T=1$ to $J=1^+$ $T=1$ isovector spin transitions in $^{10}\mathrm{Be}$, the summed strength in the large space is actually *less* than in the valence-space-only (small space), contrary to the usual expectation that higher-shell admixtures move strength upward without depleting the total.

## Method

Shell model calculations were performed for $^{8}\mathrm{Be}$ and $^{10}\mathrm{Be}$ using two interactions: a short-range simplified realistic ($x,y$) interaction (with $x=1$, $y=1$, close to Bonn A G-matrix elements) and a long-range quadrupole-quadrupole ($Q \cdot Q$) interaction. The model spaces were either the valence $0\hbar\omega$ space (small space: $(0s)^4(0p)^4$ for $^{8}\mathrm{Be}$, $(0s)^4(0p)^6$ for $^{10}\mathrm{Be}$) or a large space including all $2\hbar\omega$ excitations. Cumulative energy-weighted sums of $B(M1)$ strength were computed for $J=0^+$ to $J=1^+$ transitions, separated into orbital and spin components and by isospin channel.

## Key numbers

- For $^{8}\mathrm{Be}$ isovector orbital strength: large space summed $B(M1) = 0.7283\ \mu_N^2$; small space summed $B(M1) = 0.6701\ \mu_N^2$.
- For $^{10}\mathrm{Be}$ $J=0^+$ $T=1$ to $J=1^+$ $T=1$ isovector spin transitions: large space summed $B(M1) = 2.08 \times 10^{-2}\ \mu_N^2$; small space summed $B(M1) = 2.34 \times 10^{-2}\ \mu_N^2$ (large space smaller).
- For $^{10}\mathrm{Be}$ total $M1$ (physical $g$-factors) to $J=1^+$ $T=1$ and $T=2$: large space $1.952\ \mu_N^2$; small space $2.09\ \mu_N^2$.
- Hindrance factor (low-energy large space / small space) for isovector orbital in $^{8}\mathrm{Be}$: $0.88$; for isovector spin in $^{8}\mathrm{Be}$: $0.73$.
- Large space summed orbital $B(M1)$ in $^{8}\mathrm{Be}$: $0.73\ \mu_N^2$; in $^{10}\mathrm{Be}$ (to $T=1$ and $T=2$ combined): $0.38\ \mu_N^2$.
- Energy-weighted isovector orbital sum for $^{8}\mathrm{Be}$ with ($x,y$) interaction: first plateau at about $12\ \mu_N^2\mathrm{MeV}$, second rise to about $20.8\ \mu_N^2\mathrm{MeV}$.
- For $^{10}\mathrm{Be}$ $J=0^+$ $T=1$ to $J=1^+$ $T=1$ with ($x,y$) interaction: high-energy rise reaches about $8\ \mu_N^2\mathrm{MeV}$, over three times the low-energy rise.
- Zheng-Zamick sum rule for $^{8}\mathrm{Be}$ large space with $Q \cdot Q$: left-hand side $14.040\ \mu_N^2\mathrm{MeV}$ vs. right-hand side $15.25\ \mu_N^2\mathrm{MeV}$ (reasonably close).
- For $^{10}\mathrm{Be}$ with $Q \cdot Q$ interaction, the $T=1$ and $T=2$ scissors modes are degenerate, with the $T=2$ mode carrying $5/3$ of the $T=1$ strength.

## Context

This work extends shell-model studies of magnetic dipole excitations in light $p$-shell nuclei, specifically $^{8}\mathrm{Be}$ and $^{10}\mathrm{Be}$, by including $2\hbar\omega$ excitations beyond the valence space. It builds on prior calculations by Fayache and Zamick (1994) for $^{8}\mathrm{Be}$ and on the energy-weighted sum rule developed by Zheng and Zamick (1990). The results contradict simple expectations that higher-shell admixtures always increase total summed strength, and they show that the low-lying orbital $M1$ strength in $^{10}\mathrm{Be}$ is anomalously small compared to its $B(E2)$ strength, despite $^{10}\mathrm{Be}$ being strongly deformed ($\beta \approx 1.13$ from Raman et al.). The Wigner supermultiplet scheme is used to interpret the degeneracy of $T=1$ and $T=2$ scissors modes in $^{10}\mathrm{Be}$ under the $Q \cdot Q$ interaction, providing a framework for understanding intermediate structure in scissors-mode states.
