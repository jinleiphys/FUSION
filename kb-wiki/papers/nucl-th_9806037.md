# Is SU(3) Chiral Perturbation Theory an Effective Field Theory?

> We argue that the difficulties associated with the convergence properties of conventional SU(3) chiral perturbation theory can be ameliorated by use of a cutoff, which suppresses the model-dependent short distance effects in such calculations.

## Key claim

Conventional SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory suffers from poor convergence because dimensional regularization retains large, model-dependent short-distance contributions from kaon and eta loops; using a momentum-space cutoff that suppresses these short-distance effects restores control over the chiral expansion without breaking chiral invariance.

## Method

The author replaces the dimensionally regularized loop integrals in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory with dipole-cutoff versions, where the cutoff Λ is of order the inverse baryon size (~300–600 MeV). The resulting modified integrals are then used to compute one-loop corrections to baryon masses, axial couplings, semileptonic and nonleptonic hyperon decay amplitudes, and magnetic moments. The cutoff-dependent pieces proportional to Λ³ and Λ are absorbed into renormalizations of the low-energy constants, verifying that chiral invariance is maintained in the limit m_P² ≪ Λ².

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass expansion in dimensional regularization (GeV): M_N = 0.711 + 0.202 – 0.272 + 0.298 + … (convergence poor).
- Ξ mass expansion in dimensional regularization (GeV): M_Ξ = 0.767 + 0.844 – 0.890 + 0.600 + … (convergence poor).
- Loop contributions L_B to baryon masses in dimensional regularization (GeV): L_N = –0.31, L_Λ = –0.66, L_Σ = –0.67, L_Ξ = –1.02 (using D = 0.806, F = 0.46, F_π = 93 MeV).
- With dipole cutoff Λ = 400 MeV, loop contributions become (GeV): N = –0.11, Σ = –0.08, Λ = –0.08, Ξ = –0.06.
- With dipole cutoff Λ = 500 MeV, loop contributions become (GeV): N = –0.22, Σ = –0.18, Λ = –0.18, Ξ = –0.15.
- Dimensionally regularized integral I(m_P²) (GeV³): I_π = 0.0025, I_K = 0.1213, I_η = 0.1815; with Λ = 400 MeV: Ī_π = 0.0157, Ī_K = 0.0077, Ī_η = 0.0069.

## Context

This work addresses the convergence crisis in SU(3) baryon chiral perturbation theory highlighted by Borasoy and Meissner (1996), where one-loop corrections to baryon masses, axial couplings, hyperon decays, and magnetic moments were found to be large (30–90%) and to destroy agreement with experiment. The author proposes a cutoff regularization scheme that retains only the long-distance, model-independent part of the loops, making the results consistent with the cloudy bag model of Thomas and collaborators. This enables future chiral calculations for baryons to be phenomenologically useful without requiring large higher-order counterterms.
