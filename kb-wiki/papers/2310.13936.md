# Isospin-dependence of the charge-changing cross-section shaped by the charged-particle evaporation process

> We present the charge-changing cross sections (CCCS) of $^{11-15}$C, $^{13-17}$N, and $^{15,17-18}$O at around 300 MeV/nucleon on a carbon target, which extends to $p$-shell isotopes with $N < Z$ for the first time. The Glauber model, which considers only the proton distribution of projectile nuclei, underestimates the cross sections by more than 10\%. We show that this discrepancy can be resolved by considering the contribution from the charged-particle evaporation process (CPEP) following projectile neutron removal. Using nucleon densities from the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum, we investigate the isospin-dependent CPEP contribution to the CCCS for a wide range of neutron-to-proton separation energy asymmetry. Our calculations, which include the CPEP contribution, agree well with existing systematic data and reveal an ``evaporation peak" at the isospin symmetric region where the neutron-to-proton separation energy is close to zero. These results suggest that analysis beyond the Glauber model is crucial for accurately determining nuclear charge radii from CCCSs.

## Key claim

The Glauber model systematically underestimates charge-changing cross sections (CCCS) for p-shell nuclei by 11-15% at around 300 MeV/nucleon, and this discrepancy can be resolved by including the contribution from charged-particle evaporation following projectile neutron removal, with the effect peaking at the isospin symmetric region where neutron-to-proton separation energy asymmetry is near zero.

## Method

The authors measured CCCS for 13 isotopes (11-15C, 13-17N, and 15,17-18O) on a carbon target at around 300 MeV/nucleon using the transmission method at the Heavy Ion Research Facility in Lanzhou (HIRFL-CSR). They calculated the charged-particle evaporation process (CPEP) contribution using the Gaimard-Schmidt approach for excitation energy distributions, with nucleon densities from the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc), and added this to zero-range optical-limit Glauber model (ZRGM) predictions to obtain corrected CCCS values.

## Key numbers

- ZRGM predictions underestimate experimental CCCS by 11-15% for stable p-shell isotopes at around 300 MeV/nucleon
- Measured CCCS values include: 12C at 294 MeV/nucleon: 731 +/- 52 mb; 14C at 339 MeV/nucleon: 732 +/- 22 mb; 15N at 315 MeV/nucleon: 815 +/- 11 mb; 17O at 308 MeV/nucleon: 866 +/- 11 mb; 18O at 368 MeV/nucleon: 887 +/- 39 mb
- CPEP contribution accounts for more than 10% of experimental CCCS for stable p-shell isotopes
- The CPEP contribution ratio shows a maximum at T_Z approximately 0 and is suppressed for very neutron-rich isotopes at T_Z greater than 5
- For Si, Ar, and Ca isotopes, the CPEP contribution maxima are about 20% smaller than for p-shell isotopes
- The CPEP contribution shows a sharp increase of more than 20% from 50 MeV/nucleon on, reaching peak values at around 200 MeV/nucleon accounting for 10% of CCCS

## Context

This work extends CCCS measurements to neutron-deficient p-shell isotopes (N less than Z) for the first time, completing systematic data for C, N, and O isotopes near the beta-stability line at 300 MeV/nucleon. The results contradict the assumption that the Glauber model alone can directly probe proton radii, as previously suggested by Meng (2002) and Abe (2004), and instead support the correction approach proposed by Tanaka (2021) for Ca isotopes. The findings enable more reliable extraction of nuclear charge radii from CCCS measurements by requiring subtraction of the CPEP contribution, and motivate further measurements on nuclei with extreme proton-neutron ratios and of evaporated particles near the reaction target.
