# Identification of mixed-symmetry states in an odd-mass nearly-spherical nucleus

> The low-spin structure of 93Nb has been studied using the (n,n' gamma) reaction at neutron energies ranging from 1.5 to 3.0 MeV and the 94Zr(p,2n gamma)93Nb reaction at bombarding energies from 11.5 to 19 MeV. States at 1779.7 and 1840.6 keV, respectively, are proposed as mixed-symmetry states associated with the coupling of a proton hole in the p_1/2 orbit to the 2+_1,ms state in 94Mo. These assignments are derived from the observed M1 and E2 transition strengths to the symmetric one-phonon states, energy systematics, spins and parities, and comparison with shell model calculations.

## Key claim

The 1779.7 and 1840.6 keV states in 93Nb are proposed as mixed-symmetry states arising from the coupling of a proton hole in the p1/2 orbit to the 2+1,ms state in 94Mo, with the assignments supported by observed M1 and E2 transition strengths, energy systematics, and shell model calculations, though the 1840.6 keV state allows equally for Jπ=3/2- or 5/2- assignments from angular correlation data.

## Method

The low-spin structure of 93Nb was studied using the (n,n'γ) reaction at neutron energies from 1.5 to 3.0 MeV at the University of Kentucky and the 94Zr(p,2nγ)93Nb reaction at bombarding energies from 11.5 to 19 MeV at the University of Cologne. Excitation functions, lifetimes via Doppler-shift attenuation, and branching ratios were measured from the (n,n'γ) reaction, while angular correlations from the (p,2nγ) reaction determined multipolarities and spin assignments. Shell model calculations using the low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction Vlow-k with 88Sr as the inert core were performed for comparison.

## Key numbers

- The 1779.7 keV state (assigned Jπ=5/2(-)) has a mean life of 105+43-28 fs, with a B(M1) value of 0.55+0.24-0.18 μN2 for the 969 keV transition to the 5/2-1 state and a B(E2) value of 0.5(2) W.u. for the same transition
- The 1840.6 keV state has a mean life of 103+35-24 fs, with B(M1) values of 0.29(8) μN2 (for Jπ=3/2-) and 0.28(9) μN2 (for Jπ=5/2-) for the 1153 keV transition
- The 1779.7 keV state shows a B(M1) of 0.03+0.04-0.02 μN2 for the 1092 keV transition to the 3/2-1 state and a B(E2) of 0.04+0.04-0.03 W.u.
- The summed B(M1) from the weak-coupling limit for transitions to the 3/2- state is 0.32+0.12-0.10 μN2 and to the 5/2- state is 0.63+0.27-0.21 μN2
- The shell model predicts the 5/2-2 state at 1734 keV with B(M1) values in good agreement with experiment, and the spin contribution to the B(M1) value is 0.155 μN2 when using vanishing orbital g-factors

## Context

This work extends the identification of mixed-symmetry states from even-even nuclei into odd-mass nuclei, specifically building on the characterization of 2+1,ms states in the N=52 isotones 92Zr and 94Mo by Fransen and collaborators. The identification of mixed-symmetry states in 93Nb provides evidence for weak-coupling of a fermion to collective excitations and affirms the effectiveness of the Interacting Boson Model-2 in separating proton and neutron representations. This enables future studies of mixed-symmetry states in other odd-mass nuclei and tests of the Interacting Boson Fermion Model in the weak-coupling limit.
