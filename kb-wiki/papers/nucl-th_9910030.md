# Uranium on uranium collisions at relativistic energies

> Deformation and orientation effects on compression, elliptic flow and particle production in uranium on uranium collisions (UU) at relativistic energies are studied within the transport model ART. The density compression in tip-tip UU collisions is found to be about 30% higher and lasts approximately 50% longer than in body-body or spherical UU reactions. The body-body UU collisions have the unique feature that the nucleon elliptic flow is the highest in the most central collisions and remain a constant throughout the reaction. We point out that the tip-tip UU collisions are more probable to create the QGP at AGS and SPS energies while the body-body UU collisions are more useful for studying properties of the QGP at higher energies.

# Uranium on uranium collisions at relativistic energies

## Key claim

Deformation and orientation effects in uranium-uranium collisions produce a 30% higher compression in tip-tip collisions compared to body-body or spherical configurations, but the paper hedges that this advantage is most critical at lower beam energies (AGS/SPS) where reaching QGP conditions is uncertain; at higher energies (RHIC/LHC), the body-body orientation offers superior diagnostic potential through sustained elliptic flow, even though its compression is lower.

## Method

The study used the relativistic transport model ART (A Relativistic Transport model) in cascade mode to simulate uranium-uranium collisions at beam energies from 1 to 20 GeV/nucleon, with uranium modeled as an ellipsoid having a deformation parameter delta = 0.27 and a long/short axis ratio of about 1.3. The authors compared tip-tip (long axes head-on), body-body (short axes head-on, long axes parallel), and spherical uranium configurations at impact parameters of 0 and 6 fm, tracking central baryon density evolution, nucleon elliptic flow (v2), and particle multiplicities.

## Key numbers

- Density compression in tip-tip UU collisions is about 30% higher than in body-body or spherical UU reactions at both 0 and 6 fm impact parameters at 20 GeV/nucleon.
- The high-density phase (rho/rho0 >= 5) in tip-tip collisions lasts about 3-5 fm/c longer than in body-body collisions at 20 GeV/nucleon.
- For uranium-238, the deformation parameter delta = 0.27, giving a long/short axis ratio of about 1.3.
- In the most central collisions at 10 GeV/nucleon, tip-tip UU collisions produce about 15% more pions and 40% more positive kaons than body-body or sphere-sphere UU collisions.
- The maximum impact parameter for tip-tip and body-body UU collisions are 2R_s and 2R_l, respectively (where R_s and R_l are the short and long semi-axes).

## Context

This work extends earlier schematic estimates by Braun-Munzinger (who found a factor of 1.8 gain in energy density for tip-tip UU over central Au+Au) and Shuryak (who re-estimated the factor as about 1.3 using particle production systematics), providing more quantitative transport-model results. The paper enables experimental planning for uranium beam programs at AGS/BNL, SPS/CERN, RHIC/BNL, and LHC/CERN, suggesting that tip-tip collisions are better for creating QGP at lower energies while body-body collisions offer cleaner probes of QGP properties at higher energies through sustained elliptic flow measurements.
