# Probing excited-state quantum phase transitions with trapped cold ions

> We propose concrete protocols to realize quantum criticality due to excited-state quantum phase transitions (ESQPTs) experimentally in presumably the simplest and most resilient system involving a single trapped ion oscillating in a radio-frequency Paul trap. We identify a specific class of excited states of the Extended Rabi Model (ERM) Hamiltonian, which occur between two critical ESQPT energies of the model in its (anti)Jaynes-Cummings superradiant phase. Properties of these states motivate the definition of several ESQPT witness observables. We study their critical scaling behaviors as well as various distinct state evolutions by driving the system across the quantum criticalities by changing the qubit-phonon coupling strength linearly in time at different finite rates. A mapping of the theoretical control parameters of the ERM to the experimental parameters of a trapped ion setup is provided, and simulations are performed for values referencing existing state-of-the-art setups, addressing both unitary state evolutions as well as relevant open-system corrections.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that excited-state quantum phase transitions (ESQPTs) can be experimentally realized using a single trapped ion, provided the system is tuned into the (anti)Jaynes-Cummings superradiant phase of the Extended Rabi Model (ERM) and the effective system size $\Delta$ is sufficiently large to approximate the semiclassical limit.

## Method
The authors map the control parameters of the Extended Rabi Model (ERM) Hamiltonian to the experimental parameters of a single trapped ion in a Paul trap, using a detuned bichromatic laser coupling scheme to reach the strong-coupling regime. They identify a specific class of excited states (S2 emergent states) in the ERM spectrum and define several ESQPT witness observables based on their properties. The study analyzes the critical scaling of these witnesses and simulates both unitary and open-system (Lindblad master equation) dynamics for driving protocols that linearly change the qubit-phonon coupling strength in time.

## Key numbers
*   The ground-state quantum phase transition (QPT) occurs at a critical coupling strength $\lambda_c \equiv 1$, independent of the interaction parameter $\delta$.
*   A second critical line for ESQPTs is identified at $\lambda_0 \equiv 1/|\delta|$, which separates the first (Rabi) superradiant phase (S1) from the second (Jaynes-Cummings) superradiant phase (S2).
*   The semiclassical density of states shows a logarithmic divergence at the saddle-point energy $e_{\rm sad}(\lambda, \delta)=-\frac{1}{4}\left[(\lambda\delta)^2+(\lambda\delta)^{-2}\right]$ in the S2 phase.
*   Numerical simulations of level dynamics are performed for a system size of $\Delta=15$ and coupling strengths $\lambda$ up to 4, for various values of $\delta$ (e.g., $\delta=0$, $\delta=0.5$, $\delta=1$).

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of excited-state quantum phase transitions (ESQPTs) reviewed by Cejnar (2021) and the specific analysis of the Extended Rabi Model by Stránský et al. (2021). It builds upon the experimental protocol for generating a synthetic Rabi Hamiltonian by Pedernales et al. (2016) and the subsequent experimental demonstration of a ground-state QPT in the Rabi model using a trapped ion by Duan et al. (2021). The paper provides a concrete experimental roadmap to observe ESQPT criticalities, which have so far remained experimentally untouched, and suggests potential applications in quantum sensing.
