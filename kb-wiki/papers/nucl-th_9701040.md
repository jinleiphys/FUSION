# Symmetries in nuclei and molecules

> Recent progress in two different fronts is reported. First, the concept of bisection of a harmonic oscillator (HO) or hydrogen atom (HA), used in the past in establishing the connection between U(3) and O(4), is generalized into multisection (trisection, tetrasection, etc). It is then shown that all symmetries of the N-dim anisotropic HO with rational ratios of frequencies (RHO), some of which are underlying the structure of superdeformed and hyperdeformed nuclei, can be obtained from the U(N) symmetry of the corresponding isotropic HO with an appropriate combination of multisections. Furthermore, it is seen that bisections of the N-dim HA, which possesses an O(N+1) symmetry, lead to the U(N) symmetry, so that further multisections of the HA lead to the symmetries of the N-dim RHO. Second, it is shown that there is evidence that the recently observed in superdeformed nuclear bands $ΔI=4$ bifurcation is also occuring in rotational bands of diatomic molecules. In addition there is evidence that a $ΔI=8$ bifurcation, of the same order of magnitude as the $ΔI=4$ one, is observed in superdeformed nuclear bands and rotational bands of diatomic molecules.

# Symmetries in nuclei and molecules

## Key claim
The authors show that all symmetries of the N-dimensional anisotropic harmonic oscillator with rational ratios of frequencies (RHO) can be obtained from the U(N) symmetry of the isotropic harmonic oscillator or the O(N+1) symmetry of the hydrogen atom through multisection operations, but these RHO symmetries are not themselves orthogonal symmetries except for the specific 2:2:1 case in three dimensions. Additionally, the paper presents evidence that the ΔI=4 bifurcation (ΔI=2 staggering) recently observed in superdeformed nuclear bands also occurs in rotational bands of diatomic molecules, and that a ΔI=8 bifurcation of comparable magnitude is observed in both systems.

## Method
The authors generalize the concept of bisection of a harmonic oscillator or hydrogen atom, previously used to connect U(3) and O(4) symmetries, to multisection (trisection, tetrasection, etc.), applying these operations to the completely symmetric irreducible representations of U(N) and O(N+1). They compute degeneracy patterns resulting from successive n-sections along different axes and identify which frequency ratios of the RHO each pattern corresponds to. For the staggering analysis, they apply discrete fourth-derivative operators to γ-ray transition energies in superdeformed nuclear bands and to rotational transition energies in diatomic molecules, examining the sign alternation and magnitude of the resulting quantities.

## Key numbers
- The degeneracy pattern from one bisection of the 3-dim isotropic HO yields the 2:2:1 RHO with degeneracies 1, 2, 4, 6, 9, 12, 16, 20, ...
- One trisection of the 3-dim isotropic HO yields the 3:3:1 RHO with degeneracies 1, 2, 3, 5, 7, 9, 12, 15, 18, 22, 26, 30
- One tetrasection of the 3-dim isotropic HO yields the 4:4:1 RHO with degeneracies 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, 15, 18, 21, 24
- Two bisections along different axes yield the 2:1:1 RHO with degeneracies 1, 1, 3, 3, 6, 6, 10, 10, ...
- Two trisections along different axes yield the 3:1:1 RHO with degeneracies 1, 1, 1, 3, 3, 3, 6, 6, 6, ...
- The magnitude of ΔI=2 staggering in superdeformed nuclear bands is of order 10^{-4} to 10^{-5} of the γ-ray transition energies
- The magnitude of ΔI=2 staggering in diatomic molecules is of order 10^{-3} to 10^{-5} of the interlevel separation energy
- The ΔI=8 bifurcation in superdeformed nuclear bands and diatomic molecules has the same order of magnitude as the ΔI=4 bifurcation in the same physical system
- Energy level separation in molecules is of order 10^{-2} eV, while in nuclei it is of order 10^{5} eV

## Context
This work extends the earlier bisection concept introduced by Ravenhall et al. for connecting U(3) and O(4) symmetries, generalizing it to arbitrary multisections that generate all RHO symmetries from a common U(N) or O(N+1) parent. The staggering analysis builds on the discovery of ΔI=4 bifurcation in superdeformed nuclear bands by Filbotte et al. and Cederwall et al., and the authors provide evidence that the same effect appears in molecular rotational bands of YD, YN, CS, and CrD, suggesting a common underlying mechanism possibly related to bandcrossings as proposed by Sun and Reviol. The results enable further investigation of whether these staggering effects arise from perturbations of a given symmetry and motivate searches for ΔI=12 bifurcations in longer rotational bands.
