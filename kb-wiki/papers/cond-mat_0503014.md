# The elementary excitations of the exactly solvable Russian doll BCS model of superconductivity

> The recently proposed Russian doll BCS model provides a simple example of a many body system whose renormalization group analysis reveals the existence of limit cycles in the running coupling constants of the model. The model was first studied using RG, mean field and numerical methods showing the Russian doll scaling of the spectrum, E(n) ~ E0 exp(-l n}, where l is the RG period. In this paper we use the recently discovered exact solution of this model to study the low energy spectrum. We find that, in addition to the standard quasiparticles, the electrons can bind into Cooper pairs that are different from those forming the condensate and with higher energy. These excited Cooper pairs can be described by a quantum number Q which appears in the Bethe ansatz equation and has a RG interpretation.

## Key claim

The Russian doll BCS model exhibits, in addition to the standard BCS quasiparticles, excited Cooper pairs that are distinct from those forming the condensate and have higher energy; these excited pairs are characterized by a quantum number Q that appears in the Bethe ansatz equations and has a renormalization group interpretation, with the condition that the integer Q labels distinct solutions of the gap equation corresponding to different winding numbers of the phase of the order parameter.

## Method

The authors used the exact Bethe ansatz solution of the Russian doll BCS model, obtained by Dunning and Links via the Quantum Inverse Scattering Method, to solve the Bethe ansatz equations (BAE) for the low-energy spectrum. They analyzed the BAE in the large-N limit (where N is the number of energy levels) by taking logarithms and identifying integer quantum numbers Q_a, then compared the exact solution to the mean-field BCS gap equation solution. They also performed a numerical solution of the BAE for the one-Cooper-pair problem and derived the BAE from a renormalization group approach using Glazek and Wilson's method of eliminating high-energy degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

- The RG period for the many-body case is lambda = pi/h, where h = eta N(0) is the dimensionless coupling (eta is the imaginary part of the scattering potential and N(0) is the density of states at the Fermi level).
- The gaps Delta_Q satisfy Russian doll scaling: Delta_Q ~ Delta_0 exp(-lambda Q) for Q = 0, 1, 2, ... in the weak coupling regime.
- The correlation length of the Q-th "doll" scales as xi_Q = xi_0 exp(n lambda), where n is the nesting number.
- For the one-Cooper-pair problem, the number of RG cycles n_C^(1) ~ (h/(2 pi)) log N for large N, while for the many-body case n_C ~ (h/pi) log N.
- The critical coupling for having at least one "doll" in a system of size N is h_c = pi / log N; for Avogadro's number this gives h_c = 0.057.

## Context

This work extends the Russian doll BCS model introduced by LeClair, Roman, and Sierra (2004), which showed that a modification of the BCS pairing Hamiltonian with an imaginary term breaking time-reversal symmetry leads to limit cycles in the renormalization group flow and Russian doll scaling of the spectrum. The exact solution by Dunning and Links (2004) using the Quantum Inverse Scattering Method is here exploited to go beyond the mean-field and numerical methods of the original paper. The results enable the detailed exploration of the spectrum of excited Cooper pairs and their characterization by the quantum number Q, which has a clear interpretation in both the RG and Bethe ansatz frameworks, and the work connects to the broader study of RG limit cycles in nuclear physics, quantum field theory, and few-body systems including Efimov states.
