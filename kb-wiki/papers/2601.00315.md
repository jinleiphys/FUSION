# Toward $\textit{Ab Initio}$ Quantum Simulations of Atomic Nuclei Using Noisy Qubits

> Quantum computers are expected to provide a ultimate solver for quantum many-body systems, although it is a tremendous challenge to achieve that goal on current noisy quantum devices. This work illustrated quantum simulations of ab initio no-core shell model calculations of $^3$H with chiral two-nucleon and three-nucleon forces. The measurement costs are remarkably reduced by using the general commutativity measurement together with the asymptotic optimization. In addition, the noise causes serious contaminations of configurations with undesired particle numbers, and the accuracies are much improved by applying the particle number projected measurement. By tackling the efficiency and noise issues, this work demonstrated a substantial step toward ab initio quantum computing of atomic nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) quantum simulations of $^3$H with chiral two-nucleon and three-nucleon forces are feasible on noisy quantum devices, provided that measurement costs are reduced by general commutativity grouping combined with order-by-order optimization, and that noise-induced particle-number contamination is suppressed by symmetry projection in measurements.

## Method

The authors performed variational quantum eigensolver (VQE) simulations of $^3$H in a 16-qubit $1s1p$ harmonic oscillator model space, using a chiral N$^3$LO NN interaction plus N$^2$LO 3N interaction evolved to a low-momentum scale of $\lambda=2.0$ fm$^{-1}$ via similarity renormalization group transformation. The wave function was represented with a unitary coupled cluster (UCC) ansatz up to triplet excitations, and the Hamiltonian was mapped to qubits using both Jordan-Wigner (JW) and Bravyi-Kitaev (BK) transformations. Measurement costs were reduced by partitioning Pauli strings into general commutativity (GC) groups and by optimizing UCC parameters using only second-order many-body perturbation theory (MBPT2) terms as a starting point. Noise effects were studied using a tunable noisy simulator based on IBM Kyoto device data, and particle-number projection was applied to the density matrix during measurement of diagonal Hamiltonian terms.

## Key numbers

- The $1s1p$ model space requires 16 qubits for $^3$H simulations.
- BK mapping requires 2196 single-qubit gates and 2575 two-qubit gates; JW mapping requires 3145 single-qubit gates and 3339 two-qubit gates.
- The GC grouping scheme reduces the number of measurement partitions by more than one order of magnitude compared to the naive case without grouping.
- At $e_{max}=3$, the MBPT2-level GC grouping reduces measurement groups by more than three orders of magnitude compared to full Hamiltonian terms.
- The overlap between MBPT2-guided variational wave functions and exact wave functions is 0.978.
- For $e_{max}=2$, the number of Pauli strings with 3N forces is larger than with NN forces by two orders of magnitude.
- For $e_{max}=2$, GC grouping reduces measurement groups by more than two orders of magnitude for the full 3N Hamiltonian.
- For BK mapping at noise scale $a=1.0$, the original energy deviation was -7.39 MeV; after symmetry projection the deviation improved to 0.98 MeV.
- The diagonal-part energy deviation for BK mapping decreased from -8.33 MeV to 0.04 MeV after symmetry projection.

## Context

This work extends prior quantum computing of configuration-interaction shell model (CISM) by Li et al. (2022) to the more challenging no-core shell model (NCSM) framework, which treats all nucleons as active degrees of freedom and includes three-nucleon forces. The general commutativity grouping method improves upon the qubitwise commutativity (QWC) scheme used in earlier simultaneous measurement approaches. The results enable practical ab initio quantum computing of light $p$-shell nuclei with cutoff $e_{max}=2$ on near-term devices, while also identifying particle-number contamination as a dominant noise issue that can be mitigated by symmetry projection.
