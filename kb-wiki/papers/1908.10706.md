# Moments of nucleon generalized parton distributions from lattice QCD simulations at physical pion mass

> We present results for the moments of nucleon isovector vector and axial generalised parton distribution functions computed within lattice QCD. Three ensembles of maximally twisted mass clover-improved fermions simulated with a physical value of the pion mass are analyzed. Two of these ensembles are generated using two degenerate light quarks. A third ensemble is used having, in addition to the light quarks, strange and charm quarks in the sea. A careful analysis of the convergence to the ground state is carried out that is shown to be essential for extracting the correct nucleon matrix elements. This allows a controlled determination of the unpolarised, helicity and tensor second Mellin moments. The vector and axial-vector generalised form factors are also computed as a function of the momentum transfer square up to about 1 GeV$^2$. The three ensembles allow us to check for unquenching effects and to assess lattice finite volume effects.

# Moments of nucleon generalized parton distributions from lattice QCD simulations at physical pion mass

## Key claim
The authors present lattice QCD results for the second Mellin moments of nucleon isovector unpolarized, helicity, and transversity parton distribution functions, as well as the associated generalized form factors, using three ensembles at physical pion mass. The results are conditional on a careful excited-state analysis showing that convergence to the ground state is essential for extracting correct nucleon matrix elements, and the three ensembles allow checks for unquenching effects and finite volume effects.

## Method
The authors compute nucleon two- and three-point correlation functions using maximally twisted mass clover-improved fermions on three ensembles at physical pion mass: two Nf=2 ensembles (light quarks only) with different volumes, and one Nf=2+1+1 ensemble (including strange and charm sea quarks). They extract the generalized form factors A20, B20, C20 (vector), Ã20, B̃20 (axial-vector), and AT20, ÃT20, BT20, B̃T20 (tensor) from the matrix elements of one-derivative operators using three complementary methods for excited-state removal: plateau fits, two-state fits, and the summation method, combined with singular value decomposition to handle the overconstrained system of kinematic equations.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.0801(4) fm for the Nf=2+1+1 ensemble (cB211.072.64), a = 0.0938(3)(1) fm for the Nf=2 ensembles (cA2.09.64 and cA2.09.48)
- Pion mass: mπ = 0.1393(7) GeV for the Nf=2+1+1 ensemble; mπ = 0.1303(4)(2) GeV for the large Nf=2 ensemble; mπ = 0.1306(4)(2) GeV for the small Nf=2 ensemble
- Volume: L = 5.12(3) fm for the Nf=2+1+1 ensemble; L = 6.00(2) fm for the large Nf=2 ensemble; L = 4.50(1) fm for the small Nf=2 ensemble
- Momentum transfer range: up to about 1 GeV^2 for the vector and axial-vector generalized form factors
- Statistics: up to 48000 measurements per sink-source separation for the Nf=2+1+1 ensemble (t_s/a = 20), with 750 configurations analyzed

## Context
This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of nucleon structure by using ensembles at the physical pion mass, eliminating the need for chiral extrapolation that was required in earlier studies such as Alexandrou (2015). The use of three ensembles with different volumes and sea quark content enables systematic assessment of finite volume effects and unquenching effects from strange and charm quarks. The results provide first-principles input for nucleon tomography and can be compared with experimental determinations from deeply virtual Compton scattering and deeply virtual meson production, as well as with phenomenological fits of parton distribution functions.
