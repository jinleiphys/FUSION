# RHIC Perfect Fluid sQGP- a Landau Chromo-Diamagnetic Liquid

> We speculate that the quark matter produced at RHIC, behaves like a perfect fluid may be considered as Landau chromo-diamagnetic material in presence of color magnetic field. We have shown that in such a system the viscosity coefficient can be small enough and in the extreme case of ultra-strong color magnetic field strength, it behaves like a super fluid.

## Key claim

The paper speculates that the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) produced at RHIC behaves as a Landau chromo-diamagnetic liquid in the presence of a self-consistently generated color magnetic field, and that in the extreme case of ultra-strong color magnetic field strength the shear viscosity coefficient can vanish, making the system a superfluid. The authors hedge that the quantitative results should not be taken too seriously due to uncertainties in parameters such as the strong coupling constant, densities, and temperatures.

## Method

The authors apply relativistic Boltzmann kinetic theory with a relaxation-time approximation to a system of quarks, anti-quarks, and gluons moving in a self-consistently generated color magnetic field. They assume the gauge \(A_a^\mu \equiv (0,0,xB_a,0)\) and compute single-particle energy eigenvalues with Landau quantization for both quarks (with spin projection \(\alpha = \pm 1/2\)) and gluons (with \(\alpha = \pm 1\)). Shear viscosity coefficients are derived separately for quarks/anti-quarks and gluons, then combined via an additive relaxation-rate formula, considering elementary processes such as \(qq\rightarrow qq\), \(q\bar q\rightarrow q\bar q\), \(qg\rightarrow qg\), and \(gg\rightarrow gg\).

## Key numbers

- The energy deposited in the RHIC fireball is about \(2.0 \times 10^4\) GeV.
- The pressure generated at impact is about \(10^{30}\) atmospheric pressure.
- The QED quantum critical magnetic field for the electron is \(4.4 \times 10^{13}\) G, used as a normalization for the color magnetic field strength.
- The strong coupling constant is taken as \(\alpha_s = 0.8\) (noted as arbitrary by the authors).
- The shear viscosity coefficient is shown to be almost insensitive to color magnetic field at low and moderate field strengths, but decreases when only a few low-lying Landau levels are occupied, and exactly vanishes when only the zeroth Landau level is occupied.

## Context

This work extends theoretical attempts to explain the low shear viscosity of the sQGP observed at RHIC by the BRAHMS, PHENIX, PHOBOS, and STAR collaborations, which contradicts earlier predictions of a weakly interacting gas. The authors contrast their approach with lattice QCD calculations, which they note are unable to reproduce jet quenching and low viscosity (citing work from 2006), and with an earlier proposal of anomalous viscosity from turbulent motion in color magnetic fields (citing a prior paper). The paper suggests that at LHC the same qualitative behavior should prevail, and speculates that chiral symmetry will not be restored in the presence of strong color magnetic fields due to dynamical mass generation.
