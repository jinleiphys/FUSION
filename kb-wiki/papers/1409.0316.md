# Charge-exchange excitations with finite range interactions including tensor terms

> We study charge-exchange excitations in doubly magic-nuclei by using a self-consistent Hartree-Fock plus Random Phase Approximation model. We use four Gogny-like finite-range interactions, two of them containing tensor forces. We investigate the effects of the various parts of the tensor forces in the two computational steps of our model, and we find that their presence is not negligible and improves the agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The inclusion of tensor terms in Gogny-like finite-range interactions improves the agreement with experimental data for charge-exchange excitations in doubly magic nuclei, but the improvement is not uniform across all observables and nuclei; the tensor force has a more relevant effect on Gamow-Teller excitations than on isobaric analog states, and the sign and magnitude of the effect depend on the specific nucleus and the part of the tensor force considered.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) plus Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations for the doubly magic nuclei ⁴⁸Ca, ⁹⁰Zr, and ²⁰⁸Pb, using four Gogny-like finite-range interactions: D1S, D1M, and two modified versions (D1ST2c and D1MT2c) that include tensor terms of the form V_tensor = (V_T1 + V_T2 P^τ_ij) S_ij exp(-r²/μ²_T) with μ_T = 1.2 fm. The tensor parameters were fitted to reproduce the experimental spin-orbit splitting of neutron 1f states in ⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁸Ca, and ⁵⁶Ni, while the spin-orbit strength W_LS was adjusted accordingly. The RPA secular equations were solved by diagonalization in a configuration space generated from HF single-particle states, with a box radius of 25 fm and a maximum single-particle energy of 100 MeV for ²⁰⁸Pb.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁸Ca, the IAS peak energy ω^F-_max increases from 5.67 MeV (D1M) to 6.26 MeV (D1MT2c), compared to the experimental value of 7.17 MeV.
- For ⁹⁰Zr, the GT main peak energy ω^GT-_max decreases from 17.36 MeV (D1M) to 15.64 MeV (D1MT2c), compared to the experimental value of 15.6 ± 0.3 MeV.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, the GT main peak energy ω^GT-_max decreases from 20.99 MeV (D1M) to 18.89 MeV (D1MT2c), compared to the experimental value of 19.2 ± 0.2 MeV.
- The IAS sum rule Δ_F (m_0^F- minus m_0^F+) is exactly 8.00 for ⁴⁸Ca, 10.00 for ⁹⁰Zr, and 44.00 for ²⁰⁸Pb, matching the expected N-Z values.
- The tensor parameters for D1ST2c are V_T1 = -135 MeV, V_T2 = 60 MeV, and W_LS = 103 MeV fm⁵; for D1MT2c they are V_T1 = -175 MeV, V_T2 = 40 MeV, and W_LS = 95 MeV fm⁵.

## Context

This work extends self-consistent HF+RPA studies of charge-exchange excitations, previously conducted mainly with zero-range Skyrme-type interactions (Bai et al., 2009-2011; Minato, 2013), to finite-range Gogny-like interactions including tensor terms. The authors follow the procedure of Gra13 and Ang12 to add tensor forces to the D1S and D1M parametrizations. The results enable systematic predictions for charge-exchange excitations in exotic nuclei that will be produced at radioactive ion beam facilities, where phenomenological approaches based on Woods-Saxon potentials (Osterfeld, 1992; Ichimura et al., 2006) cannot be applied due to lack of experimental ground-state data.
