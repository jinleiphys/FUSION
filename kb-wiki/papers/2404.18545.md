# Implication of odd-even staggering in the charge radii of calcium isotopes

> Inspired by the profoundly observed odd-even staggering and the inverted parabolic-like shape in charge radii along calcium isotopic chain, the ground state properties of calcium isotopes are investigated by constraining the root-mean-square (rms) charge radii under the covariant energy density functionals with effective forces NL3 and PK1. In this work, the pairing correlations are tackled by solving the state-dependent Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer equations. The calculated results suggest that the binding energies obtained by the radius constraint method have been slightly changed by about $0.2\%$. But for charge radii, the corresponding results deriving from NL3 and PK1 forces have been increased by about $1.0\%$ and $2.0\%$, respectively. This means that charge radius is a more sensitive quantity in the calibrated protocol. Meanwhile, it is found that the reproduced charge radii of calcium isotopes are attributed to the rather strong isospin dependence of effective potential. The odd-even oscillation behavior can also be presented in the proton Fermi energies along calcium isotopic family, but keep opposite trends with respect to the corresponding binding energies and charge radii. As encountered in charge radii, the weakened odd-even oscillation behavior is still emerged from the proton Fermi energies at the neutron numbers $N=20$ and $28$ as well, but not in binding energies.

## Key claim

The authors show that constraining root-mean-square (rms) charge radii in covariant energy density functional calculations with NL3 and PK1 forces can reproduce the observed odd-even staggering and inverted parabolic-like shape in calcium isotope charge radii, but only when the rather strong isospin dependence of the effective potential is properly accounted for; the abstract does not emphasize that the unconstrained calculations systematically underestimate the charge radii and fail to capture the odd-even staggering.

## Method

Ground-state properties of calcium isotopes from N=16 to N=32 were calculated using covariant energy density functionals with the NL3 and PK1 effective forces, with pairing correlations treated by solving state-dependent Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) equations. A constraint on the rms charge radius was imposed by adding a term -λ⟨R_ch⟩ to the Hamiltonian, and the spring constant λ was adjusted iteratively until the calculated charge radius matched the experimental value. The pairing strength was set to V₀ = 350 MeV fm³ for NL3 and V₀ = 380 MeV fm³ for PK1.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies changed by about 0.2% under the radius constraint method (e.g., for ⁴⁴Ca, reduced less than 0.1% for NL3 and about 0.2% for PK1).
- Charge radii increased by about 1.0% for NL3 and about 2.0% for PK1 under the constraint (e.g., for ⁴⁴Ca, the NL3 result increased about 1%, PK1 about 2%).
- Experimental charge radii: ⁴⁰Ca = 3.4776 fm, ⁴⁸Ca = 3.4777 fm, ⁴⁴Ca = 3.5188 fm, ⁵⁰Ca = 3.5192 fm.
- Unconstrained NL3 gave ⁴⁰Ca charge radius = 3.4692 fm and ⁴⁸Ca = 3.4711 fm; unconstrained PK1 gave ⁴⁰Ca = 3.4445 fm and ⁴⁸Ca = 3.4542 fm.
- For ⁴³Ca with PK1: proton Fermi energy with constraint = -11.47 MeV, without constraint = -9.45 MeV; with NL3: -10.53 MeV (with) vs -9.80 MeV (without).
- For ⁴⁴Ca with PK1: proton Fermi energy with constraint = -13.27 MeV, without = -10.37 MeV; with NL3: -12.48 MeV (with) vs -10.82 MeV (without).
- For ⁴⁴Ca with NL3: constrained proton single-particle energies = -14.54 MeV (2s₁/₂) and -14.98 MeV (1d₃/₂); unconstrained = -12.65 MeV and -13.48 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic mean-field studies (Geng 2003, Xia 2018, Zhang 2022) that could not reproduce the anomalous charge radii behavior in calcium isotopes, particularly the odd-even staggering. It builds on the observation by Miller (2018) that neutron-proton short-range correlations influence nuclear charge radii, and on the Fayans energy density functional model (Miller 2019) that described the discontinuous behavior through additional gradient terms in the pairing part. The results enable future investigations into the role of isospin-dependent interactions in mean-field models and suggest that proton Fermi energies may provide an alternative probe of shell closure effects in nuclear charge radii.
