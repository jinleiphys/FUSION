# Non-equilibrium cumulants within model A from crossover to first-order phase transition side

> We study the non-equilibrium cumulants of the chiral order parameter field (σ field) in different phase transition scenarios via Langevin dynamics. Cumulants up to fourth-order have been calculated based on the spacetimedependent σ configurations from the event-by-event numerical simulations. By limiting the cooling of the system in a Hubble-like way, the out-of-equilibrium cumulants illustrate clear memory effects during the evolution. Both the signs and the magnitudes of the high-order cumulants differ from the equilibrium ones below the phase transition temperature. Especially, the dynamical cumulants grow more intensively from the first-order phase transition side than they do from the crossover side. In addition, analysis of the high-order offequilibrium cumulants on the hypothetical freeze-out lines present non-monotonic curves in the large chemical potential region.

## Key claim

Non-equilibrium cumulants of the chiral order parameter field, computed via event-by-event Langevin simulations with a Hubble-like cooling, show that both the signs and magnitudes of high-order cumulants (C3, C4) differ from equilibrium values below the phase transition temperature, with dynamical cumulants growing more intensively from the first-order phase transition side than from the crossover side.

## Method

The authors solve the Langevin equation for the sigma field in a linear sigma model with a quark thermal contribution, using a Hubble-like temperature decrease T(t)/T0 = (t/t0)^(-0.45) in a finite volume V = 6.8^3 fm^3. They perform event-by-event simulations over 10^5 events, recording sigma field configurations at each time step, and compute cumulants up to fourth order from the event-averaged sigma field. The damping coefficient eta is treated as a free parameter with values 1, 3, and 7 fm^(-1), and the system evolves along constant baryon density trajectories at mu = 200 MeV (crossover) and mu = 240 MeV (first-order).

## Key numbers

- Critical point location: (mu_cp, T_cp) ~ (205, 100.2) MeV
- Simulation volume: V = 6.8^3 fm^3
- Space step size: dx = dy = dz = 0.2 fm; time step size: dt = 0.1 fm/c
- Number of events: 10^5
- Damping coefficient values used: eta = 1, 3, 7 fm^(-1)
- Hubble-like cooling exponent: -0.45 in T(t)/T0 = (t/t0)^(-0.45)
- Initial time: t0 = 1 fm
- Pion mass: m_pi = 138 MeV; pion decay constant: f_pi = 93 MeV; sigma mass: m_sigma ~ 600 MeV; constituent quark mass: ~310 MeV; proton mass: ~930 MeV
- Quark coupling: g = 3.3; lambda^2 = 20; quark degeneracy factor: d_q = 12

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of non-equilibrium cumulants using Fokker-Planck equations (Mukherjee, 2015; Jiang, 2017) and Langevin dynamics (Jiang, 2017) by including the impact of initial temperature on evolution and dynamical cumulants on hypothetical freeze-out lines. The results are relevant for interpreting STAR collaboration measurements of net-proton cumulants in Au+Au collisions from 7.7 to 200 GeV, and for future experiments at FAIR, NICA, HIAF, and J-PARC. The findings enable comparison with more sophisticated dynamical models such as chiral hydrodynamics and Hydro+, and highlight the need for proper freeze-out schemes near the critical point.
