# Thermodynamic properties of interacting bosons with zero chemical potential

> Thermodynamics properties of an interacting system of bosons are considered at finite temperatures and zero chemical potential within the Skyrme-like mean-field model. An interplay between attractive and repulsive interactions is investigated. As a particular example an equilibrium system of pions is discussed. Several modifications of thermodynamic properties in the considered system are found with increasing a strength of attractive forces. Different types of the first order phase transition are classified. Some of these transitions exist also in the Boltzmann approximation. However, effects of the Bose statistics introduce the notable additional changes in the thermodynamic quantities due to a possibility of the Bose-Einstein condensation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a Skyrme-like mean-field model for interacting bosons at zero chemical potential yields five distinct thermodynamic regimes depending on the strength of the attractive interaction parameter \(\kappa = A/(2\sqrt{mB})\), including cases where Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) coexists with a first-order liquid-gas phase transition (LGPT), but only when the attractive force exceeds a critical threshold \(A_{\rm cr} = 2\sqrt{Bm}\).

## Method

The authors adopt a self-consistent mean-field framework with a Skyrme-like density-dependent potential \(U(n) = -An + Bn^2\), where \(A\) and \(B\) are positive constants for attraction and repulsion, respectively. They solve coupled equations for pressure and particle number density using ideal Bose gas expressions with an effective chemical potential \(\mu^* = \mu - U(n)\), and classify phase transitions by varying the dimensionless parameter \(\kappa\) at fixed \(B/m = 21.6\ \text{fm}^{-6}\) and pion mass \(m_\pi = 140\ \text{MeV}\) with degeneracy \(g=3\).

## Key numbers

* Critical value for BEC onset: \(A_{\rm cr} = 2\sqrt{Bm}\); for the chosen parameters, the dimensionless threshold is \(\kappa = 1\).
* Five distinct \(\kappa\) intervals: \([0,\ \kappa_1]\) with \(\kappa_1 \cong 0.998\); \((\kappa_1,\ 1)\); \((1,\ \kappa_2)\) with \(\kappa_2 \cong 1.00017\); \((\kappa_2,\ \kappa_3)\) with \(\kappa_3 = 2/\sqrt{3} \cong 1.155\); and \((\kappa_3,\ +\infty)\).
* At \(\kappa = \kappa_1 \cong 0.998\) an inflection point in \(n(T)\) occurs at temperature \(T_c\), giving infinite particle-number fluctuations.
* At \(\kappa = 1\) the BEC onset point \(T = T_0\) with density \(n = n_0\) also yields infinite scaled variance \(\omega\).
* For \(\kappa > \kappa_3 = 2/\sqrt{3}\) the vacuum at \(T=0\) becomes unstable; the stable state at \(T=0\) has positive pressure \(p = B n_2^2 n_0 (\kappa - 2\sqrt{\kappa^2 - 1})/3 \ge 0\) and negative energy density \(\varepsilon = -p\).

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme-like mean-field approach previously applied to boson systems by Satarov et al. (2017) and Anchishkin et al. (2019), and generalizes the analysis to zero chemical potential. It classifies first-order phase transitions that can occur in interacting pion matter, relevant to the equation of state of strongly interacting matter at low temperatures where lattice QCD results (Borsanyi et al., 2010) are not directly applicable. The findings enable future studies of mesonic degrees of freedom in heavy-ion collisions and of boson stars, and they identify a regime where the zero-temperature equation of state mimics dark energy (\(p = -\varepsilon\)).
