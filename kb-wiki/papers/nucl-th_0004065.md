# Pertinent Dirac structure for QCD sum rules of meson-baryon coupling constants

> Using general baryon interpolating fields $J_B$ for $B= N, Ξ, Σ, $ without derivative, we study QCD sum rules for meson-baryon couplings and their dependence on Dirac structures for the two-point correlation function with a meson $i\int d^4x e^{iqx} \bra 0|{\rm T}[J_B(x)\bar{J}_B(0)] |{\cal M}(p)\ket$. Three distinct Dirac structures are compared: $iγ_5$, $iγ_5\fslash{p}$, and $γ_5σ_{μν}q^μp^ν$ structures. From the dependence of the OPE on general baryon interpolating fields, we propose criteria for choosing an appropriate Dirac structure for the coupling sum rules. The $γ_5σ_{μν}q^μp^ν$ sum rules satisfy the criteria while the $iγ_5$ sum rules beyond the chiral limit do not. For the $iγ_5\fslash{p}$ sum rules, the large continuum contributions prohibit reliable prediction for the couplings. Thus, the $γ_5σ_{μν}q^μp^ν$ structure seems pertinent for realistic predictions. In the SU(3) limit, we identify the OPE terms responsible for the $F/D$ ratio. We then study the dependence of the ratio on the baryon interpolating fields. We conclude the ratio $F/D \sim 0.6-0.8$ for appropriate choice of the interpolating fields.

# Pertinent Dirac structure for QCD sum rules of meson-baryon coupling constants

## Key claim

The $\gamma_5\sigma_{\mu\nu}q^\mu p^\nu$ (pseudotensor) Dirac structure is the most appropriate for QCD sum rule calculations of meson-baryon coupling constants, as it satisfies two criteria (insensitivity to continuum threshold and proper dependence on the baryon interpolating field parameter $t$) that the $i\gamma_5$ and $i\gamma_5\fslash{p}$ structures fail to meet; the $F/D$ ratio is constrained to $0.6-0.8$ when using this structure with appropriate choices of the interpolating field.

## Method

The authors constructed QCD sum rules for meson-baryon couplings using the two-point correlation function with a meson, $i\int d^4x e^{iq\cdot x} \bra 0|{\rm T}[J_B(x)\bar{J}_B(0)] |{\cal M}(p)\ket$, employing general baryon interpolating fields $J_B$ for $N$, $\Xi$, and $\Sigma$ parameterized by a real parameter $t$ (with $t=-1$ corresponding to the Ioffe current). Three distinct Dirac structures were compared: $i\gamma_5$ (pseudoscalar, PS), $i\gamma_5\fslash{p}$ (pseudovector, PV), and $\gamma_5\sigma_{\mu\nu}q^\mu p^\nu$ (pseudotensor, T), with the operator product expansion (OPE) evaluated at appropriate chiral orders for each structure. The sum rules were analyzed in the SU(3) symmetric limit to extract the $F/D$ ratio from the OPE terms.

## Key numbers

- The $F/D$ ratio is determined to be $0.6-0.8$ from the $\gamma_5\sigma_{\mu\nu}q^\mu p^\nu$ sum rules with appropriate choices of the interpolating field parameter $t$.
- For the Ioffe current ($t=-1$), the $F/D$ ratio is $0.76-0.81$.
- The continuum threshold sensitivity in the $i\gamma_5\fslash{p}$ structure produces a $\sim 15\%$ change at $M^2 = 1\ {\rm GeV}^2$ when varying $S_0$ from $2.07\ {\rm GeV}^2$ to $2.57\ {\rm GeV}^2$, while the $i\gamma_5$ and $\gamma_5\sigma_{\mu\nu}q^\mu p^\nu$ structures show only $2-3\%$ sensitivity at the same Borel mass.
- The Borel window used for fitting is $0.65 \leq M^2 \leq 1.24\ {\rm GeV}^2$ with continuum threshold $S_0 = 2.07\ {\rm GeV}^2$.
- Standard QCD parameters used: $\qq = -(0.23\ {\rm GeV})^3$, $\GG = (0.33\ {\rm GeV})^4$, $\delta^2 = 0.2\ {\rm GeV}^2$, $m_0^2 = 0.8\ {\rm GeV}^2$.
- The SU(6) quark model value for $F/D$ is $2/3$, and the value from hyperon semi-leptonic decay data is $F/D \sim 0.57$ (Ratcliffe).

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule studies of meson-baryon couplings by Shiomi and Hatsuda (1994), Kim (1996), and Kim, Doi, and Oka (1998, 1999) that reported conflicting $F/D$ ratios from different Dirac structures: $F/D \sim 0.2$ from pseudoscalar sum rules (Kim, Doi, Oka 1998) versus $F/D \sim 0.78$ from pseudotensor sum rules (Kim, Doi, Oka 1999). The authors resolve this discrepancy by establishing criteria for choosing the appropriate Dirac structure, demonstrating that the pseudotensor structure is the only one that satisfies both the continuum threshold insensitivity criterion (previously proposed by Kim 1996 and Kim, Doi, Oka 1999) and the new criterion of proper current-dependence in the SU(3) limit. The chirality analysis extends the work of Belyaev and Kogan (1984) on axial charges and the approach of Nishikawa and Kondo (1999) for $g_A$. The resulting $F/D$ ratio provides input for hyperon-baryon interaction potential models (Rijken, Stoks, Yamamoto 1999) and hyperon semi-leptonic decay analysis.
