# Dissipative or just Nonextensive hydrodynamics? - Nonextensive/Dissipative correspondence -

> We argue that there is correspondence between the perfect nonextensive hydrodynamics and the usual dissipative hydrodynamics, which we call nonextensive/dissipative correspondence (NexDC). It leads to simple expression for dissipative entropy current and allows for predictions for the ratio of bulk and shear viscosities to entropy density, $ζ/s$ and $η/s$.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a "nonextensive/dissipative correspondence" (NexDC) in which a perfect (non-dissipative) fluid described by Tsallis nonextensive statistics (parameterized by a single parameter \(q\)) is formally equivalent to a standard dissipative fluid with bulk and shear viscosities, provided the correspondence relations \(P(T)=P_q(T_q)\) and \(\varepsilon(T)=\varepsilon_q(T_q)+3\Pi\) hold.

## Method

The authors start from the nonextensive Boltzmann equation and derive a conserved \(q\)-entropy current and a perfect \(q\)-hydrodynamical equation for the \(q\)-energy-momentum tensor. They then decompose this tensor using the usual fluid four-velocity \(u^\mu\) instead of the \(q\)-modified flow \(u_q^\mu\), obtaining expressions for bulk pressure \(\Pi\), heat flow \(W^\mu\), and shear pressure \(\pi^{\mu\nu}\) in terms of the difference \(\delta u_q^\mu = u_q^\mu - u^\mu\) and the parameter \(x \equiv u_\mu \delta u_q^\mu\). By imposing the NexDC relations linking the \(q\)-thermodynamic quantities to standard ones, the perfect \(q\)-hydrodynamic equation is transformed into a standard dissipative hydrodynamic equation.

## Key numbers

* The ratio of bulk viscosity to entropy density \(\zeta/s\) and shear viscosity to entropy density \(\eta/s\) are predicted as functions of temperature \(T\) for a number of space-time rapidities \(y\), using \(q=1.08\) and initial energy density \(\varepsilon^{(in)} = 22.3\) GeV/fm\(^3\) (from a \(q\)-hydrodynamical model with a relativistic \(\pi\) gas equation of state).
* The sum rule \(\frac{1}{\zeta/s} + \frac{3}{\eta/s} = \frac{w \sigma^{\mu}_{\text{full};\mu}}{\Pi^2}\) connects the two viscosity ratios to the entropy production rate.
* The results are compared to the AdS/CFT conjecture that \(\eta/s \ge 1/4\pi\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier formulation of \(q\)-hydrodynamics (Osada 2007) and addresses ongoing debates about the necessity and formulation of dissipative hydrodynamics, including issues of causality and stability raised by Hiscock (1991) and Koide (2007). It offers a phenomenological alternative to standard second-order dissipative hydrodynamics (Israel 1976, Muronga 2004) by replacing multiple transport coefficients with a single nonextensivity parameter \(q\), which encodes intrinsic fluctuations and correlations in the quark-gluon plasma. The NexDC enables predictions for viscosity ratios that can be tested against RHIC data and AdS/CFT bounds (Kovtun 2005).
