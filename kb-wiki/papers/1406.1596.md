# The Fractal Geometrical Properties of Nuclei

> We present a new idea to understand the structure of nuclei, which is comparing to the liquid drop model. After discussing the probability that the nuclear system may be a fractal object with the characteristic of self-similarity, the nuclear irregular structure properties and the self-similarity characteristic are considered to be an intrinsic aspects of nuclear structure properties. For the description of nuclear geometric properties, nuclear fractal dimension is an irreplaceable variable similar to the nuclear radius. In order to determine these two variables, a new nuclear potential energy formula which is related to the fractal dimension is put forward and the phenomenological semi-empirical Bethe-Weizsacker binding energy formula is modified using the fractal geometric theory. And one important equation set with two equations is obtained, which is related to the conception that the fractal dimension should be a dynamical parameter in the process of nuclear synthesis. The fractal dimensions of the light nuclei are calculated and their physical meanings are discussed. We have compared the nuclear fractal mean density radii with the radii calculated by the liquid drop model for the light stable and unstable nuclei using rational nuclear fractal structure types. In the present model of fractal nuclear structure there is an obvious feature comparing to the liquid drop model, since the present model can reflect the geometric informations of the nuclear structure, especially for the nuclei with clusters, such as the α-cluster nuclei and halo nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that nuclei can be described as fractal objects with self-similarity, where the fractal dimension is a dynamical parameter that changes during nuclear synthesis; the model is presented as an alternative to the liquid drop model that can better describe geometric properties of nuclei with cluster structures, such as alpha-cluster nuclei and halo nuclei.

## Method

The authors modify the semi-empirical Bethe-Weizsacker binding energy formula using fractal geometric theory, introducing a nuclear fractal dimension D_f and a new nuclear potential energy formula that depends on D_f. They define nuclear fractal clusters (NFCs) as structural subunits within a nucleus and derive a set of equations relating binding energy, total potential energy, and fractal dimension, which are solved simultaneously to determine D_f and the nuclear fractal mean density radius for light nuclei in their ground states.

## Key numbers

- For ⁶Li, the calculated fractal mean density radius R_f = 2.217 fm, compared to experimental rms matter radius R_m = 2.32 ± 0.03 fm
- For ¹¹Li, R_f = 3.432 fm, compared to experimental R_m = 3.34_{-0.08}^{+0.04} fm
- For ¹⁴Be, R_f = 3.736 fm, compared to experimental R_m = 3.36 ± 0.19 fm
- For ¹²C, R_f = 2.848 fm, compared to experimental R_m = 2.48 ± 0.08 fm
- The coefficient of surface tension c_s = 0.98 MeV·fm²
- The minimum scale size parameter r_s is about 1.25 fm
- The fractal dimension D_f is constrained to the range 2 < D_f ≤ 3

## Context

This work extends the fractal approach to nuclear structure previously explored by Campi (1988) in nuclear fragmentation and by Adamenko et al. in super-heavy nuclear isomers. The model is presented as an improvement over the liquid drop model for describing light nuclei, halo nuclei, and alpha-cluster nuclei, though the authors note it cannot predict magic numbers and requires more realistic potential energy formulae for heavier nuclei. The paper enables future studies combining fractal nuclear structure with quantum mechanics and anisotropic descriptions of nuclear fractal systems.
