# High statistics study of in-medium S- and P-wave quarkonium states in lattice Non-relativistic QCD

> Many measurements of quarkonium suppression at the LHC, e.g. the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ of $J/Ψ$, are well described by a multitude of different models. Thus pinpointing the underlying physics aspects is difficult and guidance based on first principles is needed. Here we present the current status of our ongoing high precision study of in-medium spectral properties of both bottomonium and charmonium based on NRQCD on the lattice. This effective field theory allows us to capture the physics of quarkonium without modeling assumptions in a thermal QCD medium. In our study a first principles and realistic description of the QCD medium is provided by state-of-the-art lattices of the HotQCD collaboration at almost physical pion mass. Our updated results corroborate a picture of sequential modification of states with respect to their vacuum binding energy. Using a novel low-gain variant of the Bayesian BR method for reconstructing spectral functions we find that remnant features of the Upsilon may survive up to $T\sim400$MeV, while the $χ_b$ signal disappears around $T\sim270$MeV. The $c\bar{c}$ analysis hints at melting of $χ_c$ below $T\sim190$MeV while some $J/Ψ$ remnant feature might survive up to $T\sim245$MeV. An improved understanding of the numerical artifacts in the Bayesian approach and the availability of increased statistics have made possible a first quantitative study of the in-medium ground state masses, which tend to lower values as $T$ increases, consistent with lattice potential based studies.

## Key claim

Using a novel low-gain variant of the Bayesian BR method for spectral function reconstruction, the authors find that remnant features of the Upsilon may survive up to T ~ 400 MeV, while the chi_b signal disappears around T ~ 270 MeV; the charmonium analysis hints at melting of chi_c below T ~ 190 MeV while some J/Psi remnant feature might survive up to T ~ 245 MeV. These results are presented as the current status of an ongoing study, with the caveat that the charmonium analysis has lower statistics and is less precise.

## Method

The authors perform lattice Non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) simulations for both bottomonium and charmonium, using state-of-the-art HotQCD gauge configurations with almost physical pion mass (m_pi = 161 MeV) and crossover temperature T_pc = 159 MeV, spanning a temperature range T in [140-407] MeV. Spectral functions are reconstructed from Euclidean correlation functions using a novel low-gain variant of the Bayesian BR method that includes an additional penalty on the arc length of the spectrum to suppress numerical ringing artifacts, combined with Fourier-transformed correlator data (BRFT) for improved high-frequency constraints. The in-medium ground state masses are extracted and compared against a T ≈ 0 baseline that accounts for systematic shifts due to the finite number of Euclidean time points available at finite temperature.

## Key numbers

- Upsilon remnant feature may survive up to T ~ 400 MeV; chi_b signal disappears around T ~ 273 MeV
- Chi_c melts below T ~ 190 MeV; J/Psi remnant feature may survive up to T ~ 245 MeV
- For Upsilon at T = 407 MeV, the in-medium mass shift is Delta M = -50(1) MeV, small compared to the vacuum binding energy of 1.1 GeV
- For chi_b at T = 273 MeV, the in-medium mass shift is Delta M = -87(4) MeV, compared to vacuum binding energy of 0.6 GeV
- For J/Psi at T = 141 MeV, the in-medium mass shift is Delta M = -17(1) MeV; at T = 251 MeV, Delta M = -87(54) MeV
- The absolute change in correlators at T = 407 MeV is 1.75% for Upsilon and 6.5% for chi_b
- At T = 251 MeV, chi_b and J/Psi show similar correlator modification of 5%
- The lattice pion mass is m_pi = 161 MeV; crossover temperature is T_pc = 159 MeV
- The temperature range studied is T in [140-407] MeV for bottomonium; for charmonium the subset T in [140-251] MeV is used

## Context

This work extends the earlier lattice NRQCD study by Kim et al. (2014) with significantly increased statistics and an improved Bayesian reconstruction method that reduces numerical ringing artifacts. The results corroborate the sequential melting picture proposed by Karsch et al. (2005) and are consistent with lattice potential-based pNRQCD studies by Burnier et al. (2015), which found that melting is a gradual process. The improved understanding of systematic errors in the Bayesian approach enables a first quantitative study of in-medium ground state masses, which tend to lower values as temperature increases; future work aims to increase charmonium statistics to reach the same quantitative precision as for bottomonium.
