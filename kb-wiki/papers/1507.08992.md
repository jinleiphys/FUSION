# Flavor instabilities in the multi-angle neutrino line model

> Neutrino flavor oscillations in the presence of ambient neutrinos is nonlinear in nature which leads to interesting phenomenology that has not been well understood. It was recently shown that, in the two-dimensional, two-beam neutrino Line model, the inhomogeneous neutrino oscillation modes on small distance scales can become unstable at larger neutrino densities than the homogeneous mode does. We develop a numerical code to solve neutrino oscillations in the multi-angle/beam Line model with a continuous neutrino angular distribution. We show that the inhomogeneous oscillation modes can occur at even higher neutrino densities in the multi-angle model than in the two-beam model. We also find that the inhomogeneous modes on sufficiently small scales can be unstable at smaller neutrino densities with ambient matter than without, although a larger matter density does shift the instability region of the homogeneous mode to higher neutrino densities in the Line model as it does in the one-dimensional supernova Bulb model. Our results suggest that the inhomogeneous neutrino oscillation modes can be difficult to treat numerically because the problem of spurious oscillations becomes more severe for oscillations on smaller scales.

## Key claim
Inhomogeneous neutrino oscillation modes on small distance scales can become unstable at larger neutrino densities in the multi-angle Line model than in the two-beam model, and the presence of ambient matter can actually lower the neutrino density threshold for instability of sufficiently small-scale inhomogeneous modes, contrary to the suppression seen for homogeneous modes.

## Method
The authors developed a numerical code to solve the equations of motion for neutrino flavor evolution in the two-dimensional, multi-angle neutrino Line model with a continuous angular distribution of neutrino emission. They applied linear stability analysis to identify collective oscillation modes and their exponential growth rates, solving the characteristic equation for the Fourier moments of the neutrino density matrices. The study focused on the linear regime where flavor transformations remain small, using isotropic neutrino fluxes within a finite angular range and periodic boundary conditions along the neutrino source line.

## Context
This work extends the two-beam neutrino Line model of Duan (2014) to a multi-angle configuration with a continuous angular distribution, revealing that inhomogeneous modes can persist at even higher neutrino densities than previously found. The results contrast with the one-dimensional supernova Bulb model studied by Banerjee (2011) and Esteban-Pretel (2008), where high neutrino densities suppress inhomogeneous modes. The findings suggest that numerical simulations of collective neutrino oscillations in multi-dimensional astrophysical environments like core-collapse supernovae and black-hole accretion discs will face severe challenges from spurious oscillations, particularly on small spatial scales and at high neutrino densities.
