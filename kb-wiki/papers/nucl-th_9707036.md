# The Phase in Three-Pion Correlations

> We discuss the complex phase generated in three pion correlation functions. The lowest order contribution to the phase is of order $q^2 R/K$, where $q$ is a typical relative momentum, $K$ is a typical center of mass momentum and $R$ is a typical radius parameter. This contribution is of purely kinematic origin. At next order we find a generic contribution of order $(qR)^3$ which is a result of odd modifications to the source emission function. We argue, that the scale for typical HBT correlations in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions is $q/K \ll qR \sim 1$, so that the third order correction actually dominates the phase in the experimentally relevant momentum range. We study in detail such contributions which arise from source asymmetries generated by flow, the source geometry and resonance decays.

## Key claim

The complex phase in three-pion correlation functions receives a generic contribution of order \((qR)^3\) from odd space-time moments of the source emission function, which dominates over the kinematic contribution of order \(q^2 R/K\) in the experimentally relevant momentum range for ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, because \(q/K \ll qR \sim 1\).

## Method

The authors expand the Fourier transform of the source emission function \(S(x,K)\) in powers of the relative momentum \(q_{ij}\) to extract the phase \(\phi_{ij}\) of each two-pion pair. They then sum the three phases \(\phi_{12}+\phi_{23}+\phi_{31}\) and evaluate the resulting expression for several model source functions that incorporate flow, resonance decays, moving surfaces, and asymmetric bursts. The phase is computed analytically for each model, and the cosine of the summed phase is averaged over the relative-momentum triangle constraint to obtain the effective modification of the three-pion correlation function.

## Key numbers

- Typical heavy ion source size: \(R \sim 5\) fm; interference occurs predominantly when \(q \lesssim \hbar/R \sim 40\) MeV/\(c\).
- Typical single-particle momenta: \(k_i \simeq K \sim 300\) MeV/\(c\).
- For the \(\omega\) resonance, the phase becomes significant at outward momenta \(Q_{ij} \sim 50\) MeV, but the effect is suppressed by the resonance form factor and the small fraction of pions from \(\omega\) decays.
- For the burst model, a strong phase signal appears when \(\beta_\perp Q_{3,o} \Delta t/2 \sim 2\), corresponding to a temporal separation \(\Delta t \sim 10\) fm/\(c\) between bursts.

## Context

This work extends the earlier three-pion correlation formalism of Heiselberg and Vischer (1997) and the phase expansion of Heinz and Zhang (1997) by explicitly evaluating the phase for physically motivated asymmetric source models. The authors find that the phase is very small in realistic scenarios (flow, resonances, moving surfaces) and only becomes sizable in extreme cases such as two sudden bursts, requiring very high experimental resolution to detect. This suggests that the phase in three-pion correlations is elusive and that future measurements will need excellent statistics and momentum resolution to observe it.
