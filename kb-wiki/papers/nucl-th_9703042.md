# The nuclear liquid-gas phase transition within Fermionic Molecular Dynamics

> The time evolution of excited nuclei, which are in equilibrium with the surrounding vapour, is investigated. It is shown that the finite nuclear systems undergo a first oder phase transition. The caloric curve is presented for excited Oxygen, Magnesium, Aluminum and Calcium and the critical temperature is estimated for Oxygen.

# The nuclear liquid-gas phase transition within Fermionic Molecular Dynamics

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that finite nuclei (Oxygen, Magnesium, Aluminum, Calcium) undergo a first-order liquid-gas phase transition when the system is confined by a container potential to maintain equilibrium between the liquid drop and surrounding vapour, producing a caloric curve plateau at approximately T≈5 MeV that resembles experimental results from the ALADIN group (Poc95), though the calculation deals with a contained equilibrium system while the experiment addresses an expanding non-equilibrium system.

## Method

The time evolution of excited nuclei is simulated using Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD), where nucleons are described by antisymmetrized Gaussian wave packets with time-dependent parameters derived from the variational principle. To maintain thermal equilibrium between the liquid drop and evaporated nucleons, the system is confined by a harmonic oscillator container potential with frequency ω as a thermodynamic variable. A separate thermometer system of distinguishable particles in a harmonic oscillator potential is weakly coupled to the nuclear system, and temperature is extracted from the time-averaged energy of the thermometer using the Boltzmann statistics relation for an ideal gas in a common harmonic oscillator.

## Key numbers

- The caloric curve plateau appears at T≈5 MeV for Oxygen, Magnesium, Aluminum, and Calcium with container potential ℏω=1 MeV
- The plateau extends from excitation energy E*/A≈3 MeV per nucleon to approximately E*/A≈11 MeV per nucleon
- For Oxygen, the critical temperature Tc is estimated at about 10 MeV from the disappearance of the coexistence phase
- Jaqaman's result with Skyrme ZR3 interaction extrapolated to Oxygen gives Tc≈8 MeV; Bonche with SKM interaction gives Tc≈8 MeV, with SIII interaction gives Tc≈11 MeV; extrapolation from CaY96 with Gogny's D1 interaction gives Tc≈11.5 MeV
- Equilibration time for the system is 10000 fm/c, followed by a time-averaging interval of 10000 fm/c

## Context

This work extends mean-field predictions of a first-order phase transition for nuclear matter (JMZ84, GKM84, BLV84, SeW86, SCG89) to finite nuclei using a dynamical microscopic model. The calculated caloric curve shows striking similarity to the experimental curve from the ALADIN group (Poc95) for Au+Au collisions at E/A=600 MeV, suggesting that excited spectator matter may equilibrate faster into the coexistence region than it expands and cools. The approach enables future investigations of the time evolution of excited nuclei without a container to test assumptions about transient equilibrium situations (PaN95, BDM85, Gro90).
