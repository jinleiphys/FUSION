# Levinson's Theorem for Dirac Particles

> Levinson's theorem for Dirac particles constraints the sum of the phase shifts at threshold by the total number of bound states of the Dirac equation. Recently, a stronger version of Levinson's theorem has been proven in which the value of the positive- and negative-energy phase shifts are separately constrained by the number of bound states of an appropriate set of Schrödinger-like equations. In this work we elaborate on these ideas and show that the stronger form of Levinson's theorem relates the individual phase shifts directly to the number of bound states of the Dirac equation having an even or odd number of nodes. We use a mean-field approximation to Walecka's scalar-vector model to illustrate this stronger form of Levinson's theorem. We show that the assignment of bound states to a particular phase shift should be done, not on the basis of the sign of the bound-state energy, but rather, in terms of the nodal structure (even/odd number of nodes) of the bound state.

# Levinson's Theorem for Dirac Particles

## Key claim

The stronger form of Levinson's theorem for Dirac particles relates the individual positive-energy and negative-energy phase shifts at threshold directly to the number of bound states of the Dirac equation having an even or odd number of nodes, rather than to the number of bound states with the same sign of the energy. This assignment based on nodal structure remains valid even when the binding energy exceeds the rest mass of the particle, a condition under which the "naive" assignment by energy sign fails.

## Method

The author reduces the coupled Dirac equations to a pair of Schrodinger-like equations by eliminating either the upper or lower component of the Dirac wavefunction, then applies the nonrelativistic form of Levinson's theorem to these equations at threshold (E = +M and E = -M) following the approach of Poliatzky (1992). The boundary conditions on the ratio of lower to upper components at the origin and at infinity are analyzed to establish the connection between the threshold phase shifts and the nodal structure (even or odd combined number of nodes in both components) of Dirac bound states. A mean-field approximation to the Walecka model is used to illustrate the theorem numerically for the kappa = -1 channel in Ca-40, with scalar and vector potentials scaled by factors of 0.6, 1.0, and 1.4.

## Key numbers

- For the unscaled (lambda = 1.0) mean-field potentials for Ca-40, the scalar and vector potentials are approximately M/2 in magnitude, where M is the nucleon mass.
- For lambda = 1.4, one bound-state orbital (with one node) has binding energy exceeding the rest mass of the nucleon, causing the naive relation delta_kappa(E = -M) = N_kappa^{(-)} pi to fail.
- For a pure Lorentz-scalar square well of width cM = 20, the maximum number of bound states occurs at |S_0|/M = 1 (where M* = 0), and as the strength increases beyond this point, all but one bound state disappear.
- The shell-like bound state that remains for the scalar square well at |S_0|/M > 1 has an effective wavenumber p_0* that is purely imaginary, with 0 < E < |M*| = -M*.

## Context

This work extends the correct generalization of Levinson's theorem for Dirac particles established by Ma and Ni (1985), which constrained only the sum of phase shifts at threshold by the total number of bound states. It builds on the stronger version proven by Poliatzky (1992), which related individual phase shifts to bound states of Schrodinger-like equations, and shows that the nodal structure of Dirac bound states provides the proper classification criterion. The results enable correct interpretation of phase shifts in relativistic scattering calculations, particularly for high-energy nucleon propagation in nuclear media as relevant to coincidence (e,e'p) experiments at CEBAF investigating color transparency.
