# Strong CP problem, electric dipole moment, and fate of the axion

> Three hard problems! In this talk I investigate the long-distance properties of quantum chromodynamics in the presence of a topological theta term. This is done on the lattice, using the gradient flow to isolate the long-distance modes in the functional integral measure and tracing it over successive length scales. It turns out that the color fields produced by quarks and gluons are screened, and confinement is lost, for vacuum angles theta > 0, thus providing a natural solution of the strong CP problem. This solution is compatible with recent lattice calculations of the electric dipole moment of the neutron, while it excludes the axion extension of the Standard Model.

## Key claim

The paper claims that color fields produced by quarks and gluons are screened and confinement is lost for any nonzero vacuum angle theta, providing a natural solution to the strong CP problem, but this screening is a gradual process where the screening length decreases with increasing |theta|, and confinement is only fully lost at macroscopic distances.

## Method

The author uses lattice QCD with SU(3) Yang-Mills theory at beta=6.0 on 16^4, 24^4, and 32^4 lattices, applying the gradient flow to isolate long-distance modes and trace the running coupling over successive length scales. The topological charge distribution at theta=0 is computed, and the running coupling alpha_V(theta, mu) in the theta vacuum is obtained via discrete Fourier transform of the topological sector results. Flow equations for the running coupling are derived and solved to study the infrared behavior.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.082(2) fm at beta = 6.0
- String tension: sqrt(sigma) = 445(19) MeV, obtained from fitting Lambda_V to gradient flow data
- Gradient flow coupling at infrared scales follows alpha_GF(mu) = Lambda_GF^2/mu^2 for mu << 1 GeV
- Lambda_V = 0.854 Lambda_GF in the V scheme
- Polyakov loop and glueball correlator appear totally screened for theta greater than or approximately equal to 0.2 on the 32^4 lattice
- Current experimental upper limit on neutron electric dipole moment: |d_n| < 1.8 x 10^{-13} e fm

## Context

This work extends the theoretical arguments of Callan, Dashen and Gross (1979) that instantons would screen color fields for nonzero theta, and of 't Hooft (1981) that color-magnetic monopoles are responsible for confinement. It contradicts the prevailing paradigm that QCD remains in a single confinement phase for all |theta| < pi, and specifically excludes the Peccei-Quinn axion solution (1977) to the strong CP problem. The results are compatible with recent lattice calculations of the neutron electric dipole moment by Dragos (2019), Alexandrou (2020), Bhattacharya (2021), and the QCDSF collaboration, which find values consistent with zero. The work enables a reexamination of the axion extension of the Standard Model and suggests that the axion mass would be of order the eta' mass rather than light.
