# Bonn Potential and Shell-Model Calculations for N=126 Isotones

> We have performed shell-model calculations for the N=126 isotones 210Po, 211At, and 212Rn using a realistic effective interaction derived from the Bonn A nucleon-nucleon potential by means of a G-matrix folded-diagram method. The calculted binding energies, energy spectra and electromagnetic properties show remarkably good agreement with the experimental data. The results of this paper complement those of our previous study on neutron hole Pb isotopes, confirming that realistic effective interactions are now able to reproduce with quantitative accuracy the spectroscopic properties of complex nuclei.

## Key claim

Shell-model calculations for the N=126 isotones 210Po, 211At, and 212Rn using a realistic effective interaction derived from the Bonn A nucleon-nucleon potential via a G-matrix folded-diagram method achieve remarkably good agreement with experimental binding energies, energy spectra, and electromagnetic properties, though the agreement is not substantially better than that obtained with the older Kuo-Herling interaction (the rms deviation for 210Po is 87 keV versus 116 keV for the Kuo-Herling interaction).

## Method

The authors assumed 208Pb as a closed core with valence protons occupying six single-particle orbits (0h9/2, 1f7/2, 0i13/2, 1f5/2, 2p3/2, 2p1/2) with experimental single-particle energies taken from the 209Bi spectrum. They derived a realistic effective interaction from the Bonn A free NN potential by first calculating the Brueckner G-matrix with a Pauli operator defined by (n1,n2,n3) = (16,36,78) for protons and (22,45,78) for neutrons, then computing the Q-box containing irreducible valence-linked G-matrix diagrams through second order, and summing the folded-diagram series to all orders using the Lee-Suzuki iteration method. Shell-model calculations were performed with the OXBASH code, and electromagnetic observables were calculated using effective operators that include core-polarization effects via first-order diagrams in G.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy for 210Po relative to 208Pb: calculated value falls practically within the experimental error bar
- Binding energies for 211At and 212Rn: calculated values differ by less than 100 keV from experimental values
- Coulomb interaction strength between valence protons: V_C = 270 keV (matrix element between two h9/2 protons with J=0)
- Harmonic oscillator parameter: hbar-omega = 6.88 MeV (from expression hbar-omega = 45A^{-1/3} - 25A^{-2/3} for A=208)
- Single-particle energies (in MeV) from 209Bi: epsilon_h9/2 = 0.0, epsilon_f7/2 = 0.896, epsilon_i13/2 = 1.609, epsilon_f5/2 = 2.826, epsilon_p3/2 = 3.119, epsilon_p1/2 = 3.633
- 210Po: rms deviation sigma = 87 keV for 37 observed levels; 37 levels associated with predicted states, only 7 differ by more than 100 keV
- 211At: rms deviation sigma = 64 keV (excluding the 1/2+ state and two levels at 1.35 and 2.65 MeV)
- 212Rn: rms deviation sigma = 85 keV for 13 states; discrepancies smaller than 100 keV for 9 out of 13 states
- Kuo-Herling interaction comparison for 210Po: sigma = 116 keV for the same 37 levels (about 30 keV larger than the Bonn A result)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous realistic shell-model calculations on neutron-hole Pb isotopes (Coraggio et al., 1998) to the proton-rich N=126 isotones 210Po, 211At, and 212Rn, complementing their studies of nuclei around doubly magic 100Sn and 132Sn (Andreozzi et al., 1996, 1997; Covello et al., 1998, 1999). The calculations improve upon the pioneering work of McGrory and Kuo (1975), who used the Hamada-Johnston potential with only the 3p-1h core-polarization diagram multiplied by an empirical factor of 0.75, by employing a modern NN potential and a more complete folded-diagram method with no adjustable parameters. The results enable future tests of T=0 matrix elements in nuclei with both valence protons and neutrons, and motivate systematic comparisons of different NN potentials as input for microscopic nuclear structure calculations.
