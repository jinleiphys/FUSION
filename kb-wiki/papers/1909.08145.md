# Recent highlights with baryons from lattice QCD

> Highlights from recent computations in lattice QCD involving baryons are presented. Calculations of the proton mass and spin decompositions are discussed, a percent level determination of the nucleon axial coupling is described, and determinations of the proton and neutron electromagnetic form factors and light-cone parton distribution functions are outlined. Recent results applying the so-called Lüscher method to meson-baryon systems are presented. Key points emphasized are that much better precision with disconnected diagrams is being achieved, incorporating multi-hadron operators is now feasible, and more and more studies are being done with physical quark masses.

# Recent highlights with baryons from lattice QCD

## Key claim
The paper presents a selective overview of recent lattice QCD computations involving baryons, emphasizing that disconnected diagram contributions are now being estimated with unprecedented precision, multi-hadron operators are becoming feasible, and an increasing number of studies are being performed at physical quark masses. The author notes that many results remain preliminary or require further systematic checks, particularly regarding excited-state contamination and finite-volume effects.

## Method
The author reviews lattice QCD calculations that use Monte Carlo estimates of temporal correlation matrices constructed from covalently-displaced LapH-smeared quark fields, with group-theory projections onto irreducible representations of the lattice symmetry group. For scattering studies, the Luscher method is applied, relating finite-volume stationary-state energies to infinite-volume scattering amplitudes via a quantization condition involving a box matrix and a truncated partial-wave expansion of the K-matrix.

## Key numbers
- Proton mass decomposition: quark energy 32(4)(4)%, glue energy 36(5)(4)%, quark condensate 9(2)(1)%, trace anomaly 23(1)(1)%
- Nucleon axial coupling: g_A = 1.2711(103)^s(39)^chi(15)^a(19)^V(04)^I(55)^M, where errors are from statistical, chiral extrapolation, lattice spacing extrapolation, volume extrapolations, isospin corrections, and model selection
- Delta resonance study: m_Delta/m_pi = 4.738(47) and g_{Delta N pi} = 19.0(4.7) from a Breit-Wigner fit on a 48^3 x 128 lattice with m_pi approx 280 MeV and a approx 0.076 fm
- Delta resonance preliminary study: lattice length L = 4.2 fm, spacing a = 0.065 fm, pion mass m_pi = 200 MeV
- Lambda(1405) preliminary study: lattice length L = 3.2 fm, spacing a = 0.065 fm, pion mass m_pi = 280 MeV
- Timelike pion form factor study: CLS J303 ensemble on a 64^3 x 192 lattice with spacing a = 0.050 fm and m_pi = 260 MeV
- Nucleon spin decomposition: one ensemble at physical point on a 48^3 x 96 lattice with twisted mass clover-improved action, lattice spacing a = 0.0939(3) fm
- Parton distribution function study: 48^3 x 96 lattice with twisted mass N_f=2 action, lattice spacing a = 0.0938(3)(2) fm, m_pi L = 2.98(1) at physical point

## Context
This review extends the line of lattice QCD baryon studies following the successful determination of the low-lying QCD mass spectrum (Kronfeld) and addresses the next challenge of evaluating scattering amplitudes and extracting resonance information. The work builds on the LapH smearing method (Peardon et al. 2009) and the Luscher method for relating finite-volume energies to infinite-volume scattering. The proton mass decomposition follows the approach of Ji for the energy-momentum tensor, while the nucleon spin decomposition uses the Ji sum rule. The nucleon axial coupling determination uses a Feynman-Hellman method with MILC ensembles and domain-wall valence propagators. The parton distribution function extraction uses the method proposed by Ji for quasi-PDFs with subsequent refinements. The HAL QCD method for baryon-baryon interactions is discussed in the context of a controversy with direct methods, and the H-dibaryon study is presented as a warm-up calculation at the SU(3) flavor symmetric point. The timelike pion form factor calculation paves the way for future baryon form factor computations, particularly for the Delta baryon needed by the Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment (DUNE).
