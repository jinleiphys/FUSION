# Annihilation range and final-state interaction in the antiproton-proton annihilation into pi-pi+

> The large set of accurate data on differential cross section and analyzing power from the CERN LEAR experiment on $\bar pp \to π^+π^-$ in the range from 360 to 1550 MeV/c is well reproduced within a distorted wave approximation approach. The initial $\bar pp$ scattering wave functions originate from a recent $\bar N N$ model. The transition operator is obtained from a combination of the $^3P_0$ and $^3S_1$ quark-antiquark annihilation mechanisms. A good fit to the data, in particular the reproduction of the double dip structure observed in the analyzing powers, requires quark wave functions for proton, antiproton, and pions with radii slightly larger than the respective measured charge radii. This corresponds to an increase in range of the annihilation mechanisms and consequently the amplitudes for total angular momentum J=2 and higher are much larger than in previous approaches. The final state $ππ$ wave functions, parameterized in terms of $ππ$ phase shifts and inelasticities, are also a very important ingredient for the fine tuning of the fit to the observables.

# Annihilation range and final-state interaction in the antiproton-proton annihilation into pi-pi+

## Key claim

The large set of LEAR data on differential cross section and analyzing power for antiproton-proton annihilation into two pions can be reproduced only when both the annihilation range is increased (by using quark wave functions with radii slightly larger than measured charge radii) and the final-state pi-pi interaction is included; the double-dip structure in the analyzing power requires substantial amplitudes for total angular momentum J=2 and higher, which previous short-range models could not produce.

## Method

The authors used a distorted wave approximation (DWA) with initial antiproton-proton scattering wave functions from a recent antinucleon-nucleon model, a transition operator combining the 3P0 and 3S1 quark-antiquark annihilation mechanisms, and final-state pi-pi wave functions parameterized by phase shifts and inelasticities. The quark model parameters (alpha and beta controlling hadron sizes), the relative strength lambda of the two annihilation mechanisms, and the pi-pi phase shifts and inelasticities were varied to fit differential cross section and analyzing power data at five representative energies from 66.7 to 803.1 MeV laboratory kinetic energy (360 to 1467 MeV/c antiproton momentum).

## Key numbers

- Proton radius increased from 0.60 fm (core-only value) to 0.91 fm in the final fit, within 7% of the measured charge radius of 0.870 +/- 0.008 fm
- Pion radius increased from 0.48 fm to 0.71 fm in the final fit, within 7% of the measured charge radius of 0.663 +/- 0.006 fm
- The parameter alpha decreased by a factor of about 2.3 (from 2.80 fm^-2 to 1.11-1.20 fm^-2) and beta decreased by a factor of about 2.2 (from 3.23 fm^-2 to 1.00-1.54 fm^-2)
- The relative strength |lambda| of the 3S1 to 3P0 mechanism decreases smoothly with energy: from 1.200 at 66.7 MeV (sqrt(s)=1910 MeV) to 0.424 at 803.1 MeV (sqrt(s)=2242 MeV)
- The pi-pi inelasticity eta_2 ranges from 0.309 to 0.818 across the five energies, and the phase shift delta_2 ranges from 24.49 to 74.95 degrees
- Data were fitted at five laboratory kinetic energies: 66.7, 123.5, 219.9, 499.2, and 803.1 MeV (corresponding to antiproton momenta of 360, 497, 679, 1089, and 1467 MeV/c)

## Context

This work extends earlier DWA calculations by Bathas et al. (1993) and Muhm et al. (1996), which used short-range annihilation mechanisms and could not reproduce the double-dip structure in the analyzing power or the forward/backward peaks in the differential cross section. The authors show that the earlier models' use of quark wave functions describing only the qqq core (proton radius 0.60 fm) rather than the full hadron including the mesonic cloud was a key limitation. The results enable predictions for the as-yet-unmeasured spin observables A_ls and A_ss, and demonstrate that both increased annihilation range and final-state pi-pi interaction are necessary to describe the LEAR data.
