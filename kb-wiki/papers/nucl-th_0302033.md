# The reaction antirproton-proton into pi+pi-: relativistic aspects and final-state interaction in the pion wave functions

> We use a distorted wave approximation approach which includes $^3P_0$ and $^3S_1$ quark-antiquark annihilation mechanisms to reproduce the data set from LEAR on $\bar p p\to π^+π^-$ in the range from 360 to 1550 MeV/c. Improvements of the model are sought by implementing final-state interactions of the pions and by observing that the annihilation is too short-ranged in earlier attempts to describe the data. While the former improvement is due to to the final-state $ππ$ wave functions solely, the latter one originates from quark wave functions for proton, antiproton, and pions with radii slightly larger than the respective measured charge radii. This increase in hadron radius, as compared with typically much smaller radii used before in the quark model, increases the annihilation range and thereby the amplitudes for $J\ge2$ are much higher. Finally, given the very high kinetic energy of the final pions, we investigate the role of relativistic corrections in the pion wave functions when boosted into the center-of-mass frame.

# The reaction antiproton-proton into pi+pi-: relativistic aspects and final-state interaction in the pion wave functions

## Key claim

The authors show that reproducing the LEAR data on antiproton-proton annihilation into charged pions requires both (i) including final-state interactions in the pion wave functions and (ii) using hadron radii slightly larger than measured charge radii to increase the annihilation range, which enhances partial waves with J greater than or equal to 2. Relativistic boosts of the pion wave functions further modify the angular distributions, though these relativistic corrections had not yet been included in the results presented.

## Method

The authors use a distorted wave approximation with initial spin-triplet antiproton-proton wave functions from the Paris 1998 antinucleon-nucleon potential, combined with quark-antiquark annihilation mechanisms via effective vacuum (3P0) and gluon-like (3S1) states. The final-state pion-pion wave functions are parameterized using experimental phase shifts and inelasticities for J=0,1,2,3 extracted from pion-nucleon to pion-pion-nucleon reactions. The transition amplitudes are computed from quark and antiquark diagrams where momentum is transferred from the annihilation vertex to spectator quarks, with hadron wave functions described by Gaussian forms whose size parameters are fitted to data.

## Key numbers

- The data set covers antiproton laboratory momenta from 360 to 1550 MeV/c.
- The fitted proton radius is 0.91 fm (root-mean-square), within 7% of the measured charge radius.
- The fitted pion radius is 0.71 fm (root-mean-square), within 7% of the measured charge radius.
- For an incoming antiproton with p_lab = 800 MeV/c, the relativistic factor gamma for outgoing pions is 7.2 (E_cm divided by 2 m_pi c^2).
- Partial results are shown at T_lab = 123.5 and 219.9 MeV (p_lab = 497 and 679 MeV/c).

## Context

This work extends earlier quark-model calculations by Bathas (1993) and Muhm (1996) that used much smaller hadron radii and consequently produced amplitudes dominated by J=0 and J=1, which could not reproduce the LEAR data. The authors build on the Paris 1998 antinucleon-nucleon potential (El-Bennich, 1998) and use pion-pion phase shifts from analyses of pion-nucleon to pion-pion-nucleon reactions. The results enable improved descriptions of antiproton-proton annihilation at higher angular momenta and provide a framework for incorporating relativistic boosts of final-state meson wave functions in annihilation calculations.
