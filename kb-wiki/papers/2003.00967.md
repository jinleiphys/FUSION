# Microscopic description of the self-conjugate $^{108}$Xe and $^{104}$Te $α$-decay chain

> A microscopic calculation of half-lives for the recently observed $^{108}$Xe $\to$ $^{104}$Te $\to$ $^{100}$Sn $α$-decay chain is performed using a self-consistent framework based on energy density functionals. The relativistic density functional DD-PC1 and a separable pairing interaction of finite range are used to compute axially-symmetric deformation energy surfaces of $^{104}$Te and $^{108}$Xe as functions of quadrupole, octupole and hexadecupole collective coordinates. Dynamic least-action paths are determined that trace the $α$-particle emission from the equilibrium deformation to the point of scission. The calculated half-lives: 197 ns for $^{104}$Te and 50 $μ$s for $^{108}$Xe, are compared to recent experimental values of the half-lives of superallowed $α$-decay of $^{104}$Te: $< 18$ ns, and $^{108}$Xe: 58$^{+106}_{-23}$ $μ$s.

## Key claim
The paper reports a fully microscopic calculation of α-decay half-lives for the self-conjugate chain ¹⁰⁸Xe → ¹⁰⁴Te → ¹⁰⁰Sn, finding 197 ns for ¹⁰⁴Te and 50 μs for ¹⁰⁸Xe. The abstract’s claim of agreement with experiment is qualified: for ¹⁰⁴Te the calculated value (197 ns) exceeds the experimental upper limit (< 18 ns), while for ¹⁰⁸Xe the calculation (50 μs) falls within the experimental range (58⁺¹⁰⁶₋₂₃ μs).

## Method
The authors used the relativistic density functional DD-PC1 and a separable finite-range pairing interaction to compute axially-symmetric deformation energy surfaces of ¹⁰⁴Te and ¹⁰⁸Xe as functions of quadrupole (β₂₀), octupole (β₃₀), and hexadecupole (β₄₀) collective coordinates. Dynamic least-action paths were determined by minimizing the action integral from the equilibrium deformation to scission, with collective inertia computed in the perturbative cranking approximation of the adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (ATDHFB) method. After scission, the dynamics were approximated by Coulomb repulsion between two uniformly charged spheres using experimental Q-values from Auranen et al. (2018).

## Key numbers
- Calculated α-decay half-life of ¹⁰⁴Te: 197 ns, compared to experimental upper limit < 18 ns (Auranen 2018).
- Calculated α-decay half-life of ¹⁰⁸Xe: 50 μs, compared to experimental value 58⁺¹⁰⁶₋₂₃ μs (Auranen 2018).
- Vibrational frequency used for assault frequency: ℏω = 1 MeV, giving n = 10²⁰·³⁸ s⁻¹.
- Collective ground-state energy E₀ set to 1 MeV in the action integral.

## Context
This work extends the relativistic energy-density-functional framework previously applied to nuclear structure and fission dynamics (Zhao 2015, 2016, 2019) to the α-decay of the lightest self-conjugate α-emitters near ¹⁰⁰Sn. It builds on earlier microscopic descriptions of α-cluster formation in light nuclei using relativistic EDFs (Ebran 2012, 2013, 2014) and on the least-action approach to cluster emission and fission (Ward 2011, Mirzaei 2017). The results enable further microscopic studies of α-radioactivity in the ¹⁰⁰Sn region, where nucleonic wave functions are most localized, and provide a benchmark for comparing self-consistent mean-field predictions with experimental half-lives.
