# Efficient matrix-vector products for large-scale nuclear Shell-Model calculations

> A method to accelerate the matrix-vector products of j-scheme nuclear Shell-Model Configuration Interaction (SMCI) calculations is presented. The method takes advantage of the matrix product form of the j-scheme proton-neutron Hamiltonian matrix. It is shown that the method can speed up unrestricted large-scale pf-shell calculations by up to two orders of magnitude compared to previously existing related j-scheme method. The new method allows unrestricted SMCI calculations up to j-scheme dimension 10^10 to be made in more complex model spaces.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method to accelerate j-scheme nuclear Shell-Model Configuration Interaction (SMCI) matrix-vector products by exploiting the matrix product form of the proton-neutron Hamiltonian, achieving speedups of up to two orders of magnitude compared to the existing j-scheme method of Caurier (1999) and Nowacki (1995), but only for unrestricted large-scale pf-shell calculations; the actual speedup depends on the nucleus, angular momentum, and isospin projection.

## Method

The method reformulates the j-scheme Hamiltonian matrix-vector product as sums of triple matrix products involving proton and neutron reduced density matrix (RDM) elements, angular momentum recoupling factors, and transformed two-body interaction matrix elements. The key innovation is to express the Hamiltonian matrix as sums over direct products of proton and neutron density matrices, allowing the matrix-vector product to be computed using efficient matrix multiplication kernels rather than explicit construction of every Hamiltonian matrix element. The implementation is done in the SMCI code *eicode*.

## Key numbers

- For 0+ states of 56Ni in the pf-shell, the new method reduces floating point operations 35-fold compared to the original j-scheme method.
- The number of mathematical operations per matrix-vector product scales as d^1.46 for angular momenta J=0,2 in the pf-shell, compared to d^1.82 for the original j-scheme method.
- For 56Ni, the new method takes 1317 seconds per matrix-vector multiplication, while the m-scheme code *antoine* takes approximately 6700 seconds (five times slower).
- Compared to the original j-scheme method in *eicode*, the new method shortens calculation time 75-fold for 56Ni.
- The matrix-vector operation times scale as n^1.28 in the high-dimensional regime for pf-shell nuclei.
- For the sdg7/2h11/2 model space, estimated matrix-vector product times range from 550 seconds for (Nv,Zv)=(6,4) to 1.02e6 seconds for (8,8), with j-scheme dimensions up to 1.50e9.
- The new method achieves up to 70% of a processor's theoretical peak floating point capacity in the pf-shell, compared to 10-15% for the original j-scheme method.

## Context

This work extends the j-scheme SMCI method developed by Caurier (1999) and Nowacki (1995), which was used in the codes *nathan* and *antoine*, by reformulating the matrix-vector product to use efficient matrix multiplication kernels instead of explicit Hamiltonian element construction. The method enables unrestricted SMCI calculations up to j-scheme dimension 10^10 in complex model spaces such as sdg7/2h11/2 (for nuclei above 100Sn), pf5/2g7/2d5/2 (for deformed nuclei near N≈Z=40), and No-Core Shell-Model calculations for light nuclei. The approach can be combined with truncation methods showing exponential convergence, such as those of Horoi (1994), Andreozzi (2001), and Papenbrock (2004), to further increase achievable SMCI dimensions.
