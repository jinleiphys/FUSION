# $β$-decay half-lives and nuclear structure of exotic proton-rich waiting point nuclei under $rp$-process conditions

> We investigate even-even nuclei in the $A\sim70$ mass region within the framework of the proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (\mbox{pn-QRPA}) and the interacting boson model-1 (\mbox{IBM-1}). Our work includes calculation of the energy spectra and the potential energy surfaces $V(β,γ)$ of Zn, Ge, Se, Kr and Sr nuclei with the same proton and neutron number, $N = Z$. The parametrization of the \mbox{IBM-1} Hamiltonian was performed for the calculation of the energy levels in the ground state bands. Geometric shape of the nuclei was predicted by plotting the potential energy surfaces $V(β,γ)$ obtained from the \mbox{IBM-1} Hamiltonian in the classical limit. The \mbox{pn-QRPA} model was later used to compute half-lives of the neutron-deficient nuclei which were found to be in very good agreement with the measured ones. The \mbox{pn-QRPA} model was also used to calculate the Gamow-Teller strength distributions and was found to be in decent agreement with the measured data. We further calculate the electron capture and positron decay rates for these $N$ = $Z$ waiting point (WP) nuclei in the stellar environment employing the \mbox{pn-QRPA} model. For the $rp$-process conditions, our total weak rates are within a factor two compared with the Skyrme HF+BCS+QRPA calculation. All calculated electron capture rates are comparable to the competing positron decay rates under $rp$-process conditions. Our study confirms the finding that electron capture rates form an integral part of the weak rates under $rp$-process conditions and should not be neglected in the nuclear network calculations.

## Key claim

The authors find that for the rapid proton capture (rp-process) conditions, their total weak rates (electron capture plus positron decay) for N=Z waiting point nuclei in the A~70 mass region agree within a factor of two compared with Skyrme HF+BCS+QRPA calculations, and that electron capture rates are comparable to competing positron decay rates and should not be neglected in nuclear network calculations.

## Method

The authors used the interacting boson model-1 (IBM-1) to calculate energy spectra and potential energy surfaces V(β,γ) for the even-even N=Z nuclei ⁶⁰Zn, ⁶⁴Ge, ⁶⁸Se, ⁷²Kr, and ⁷⁶Sr. They then employed the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model with Nilsson single-particle states, BCS pairing, and separable particle-hole and particle-particle residual interactions to compute Gamow-Teller strength distributions, β-decay half-lives, and stellar electron capture and positron decay rates. The deformation parameters used in the pn-QRPA calculations were taken from relativistic mean-field calculations rather than from the IBM-1 model.

## Key numbers

- The energy ratio R₄/₂ = E(4⁺)/E(2⁺) for ⁶⁰Zn, ⁶⁴Ge, and ⁶⁸Se lies between the U(5) limit (2.0) and O(6) limit (2.5); for ⁷²Kr the ratio is below 2.0; for ⁷⁶Sr the ratio lies between O(6) and SU(3) (3.33).
- The IBM-1 deformation parameter β_IBM = 0.86 for ⁷⁶Sr.
- Total BGT⁺ strength calculated up to 10.8 MeV in daughter for ⁶⁰Zn is 5.08; up to 14.7 MeV for ⁶⁴Ge is 6.17; up to 14.4 MeV for ⁶⁸Se is 5.73.
- The particle-hole interaction strength parameter χ was set to 4.2/A as the optimum value reproducing experimental half-lives from AME2012.
- The quenching factor used in the pn-QRPA calculation was 0.6.

## Context

This work extends previous pn-QRPA calculations of β-decay half-lives for neutron-deficient nuclei by Hir93 and stellar weak rates by Nabi and Klapdor-Kleingrothaus (1999) to the specific N=Z waiting point nuclei in the A~70 mass region relevant to the rp-process. The IBM-1 calculations of potential energy surfaces are compared with earlier Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using Skyrme interactions by Yam01, GognyD1S interactions by Cor13 and Gir09, and pairing-plus-quadrupole interactions by Kob05 and Hin10. The calculated Gamow-Teller strength distributions are compared with those of Sarriguren (2011) and with experimental data from the ISOLDE PSB facility at CERN for ⁷²Kr. The results enable more reliable inclusion of electron capture rates in rp-process network calculations, which the authors argue have been previously neglected.
