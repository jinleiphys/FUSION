# Chiral dynamics in systems with strangeness

> In this talk a brief review of several problems involving systems with strangeness is made. In the first place one shows how the $Λ(1405)$, $Λ(1670)$ and $Σ(1620)$ states, for $S = -1$, and the $Ξ(1620)$ for $S= -2$ are generated dynamically in the context of unitarized chiral perturbation theory. The results for the $\bar{K}N$ interaction are then used to evaluate the $K^- d$ scattering length. Results obtained for the kaon selfenergy in a nuclear medium within this approach, with application to $K^-$ atoms, are also mentioned. Finally a few words are said about recent developments in the weak decay of $Λ$ hypernuclei and the puzzle of the $Γ_n/Γ_p$ ratio.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the $\Lambda(1405)$, $\Lambda(1670)$, $\Sigma(1620)$, and $\Xi(1620)$ resonances are dynamically generated by unitarized chiral perturbation theory, but the identification of the $\Xi(1620)$ is made by elimination because the found resonance is at odds with the properties of the $\Xi(1690)$.

## Method
The authors apply the N/D method (dispersion relation technique) to unitarize the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian for meson-baryon interactions in coupled channels. The scattering T-matrix is constructed as $T = [1 - V G]^{-1} V$, where $V$ is the chiral amplitude and $G$ is a dimensionally regularized loop function with subtraction constants fitted to data. Poles of the T-matrix in the complex plane are identified as dynamically generated resonances.

## Key numbers
- $K^- d$ scattering length: $A_{Kd} = -1.61 + i\, 1.91$ fm, differing from the earlier multichannel result of $-1.34 + i\, 1.04$ fm from Torres (1999).
- Kaon selfenergy in nuclear matter at $\rho = \rho_0$ and threshold: attraction of order $(40 - i\, 50)$ MeV.
- Deeply bound $K^-$ states: binding energy of 30-40 MeV with widths of 80-100 MeV.
- $\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p$ ratio for $\Lambda$ hypernuclei: theoretical value of 0.54 from Jido (2001), later corrected experimental values of 0.5-0.6 from Outa (2002).

## Context
This work extends the unitarized chiral perturbation theory approach previously applied to low-energy $K^-N$ scattering by Kaiser (1995, 1997) and Oset (1997) to generate new resonances and to compute the $K^-$ interaction in nuclear matter. The results contradict earlier claims of a 200 MeV attractive kaon potential needed to interpret $K^-$ atom data, and instead show that a moderate potential can describe those data (Zaki, 2001; Baca, 2001; Gal, 2001). The paper also resolves the long-standing puzzle of the $\Gamma_n/\Gamma_p$ ratio in $\Lambda$ hypernuclei by identifying a fatal error in the Monte Carlo code used by Hashimoto (2000) for final-state interactions.
