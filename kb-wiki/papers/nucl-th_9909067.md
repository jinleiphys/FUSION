# Microscopic analysis of a correlation between dipole transitions $1^-_1 \to 0^+_{g.s.}$ and $3^-_1 \to 2^+_{1}$ in spherical nuclei

> Correlation between B($E1, 1^-_1 \to 0^+_{g.s.}$) and B($E1, 3^-_1 \to 2^+_{1}$) values are considered within microscopic QRPA approach. General arguments for a dependence of a ratio between these values on a collectivity of the $2^+_1$ and $3^-_1$ phonons and ground state correlations are provided.

## Key claim

The ratio \( R = B(E1, 1^-_1 \to 0^+_{g.s.}) / B(E1, 3^-_1 \to 2^+_1) \) is not constant at 7/3 as a simple bosonic phonon model would suggest; instead, within the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) that accounts for the internal fermion structure of phonons, the ratio lies strictly between 0 and 7/3, with the upper limit being unreachable in realistic nuclei.

## Method

The author applied the QRPA to spherical nuclei, constructing phonon operators as superpositions of two-quasiparticle configurations with forward (\(X\)) and backward (\(Y\)) amplitudes. Analytical expressions for the \(E1\) transition matrix elements were derived for the two-phonon \(1^-_1 \to 0^+_{g.s.}\) decay and the one-phonon \(3^-_1 \to 2^+_1\) decay, using exact commutation relations between phonon and quasiparticle operators. The ratio \(R\) was then evaluated by comparing the two expressions under different assumptions about the relative sizes of \(X\) and \(Y\) amplitudes and the collectivity of the phonons.

## Key numbers

- The upper limit of the ratio \(R = B(E1, 1^-_1 \to 0^+_{g.s.}) / B(E1, 3^-_1 \to 2^+_1)\) is 7/3, obtained only if \(X^{\lambda}_{jj'} \equiv Y^{\lambda}_{jj'}\) and effective charges are equal; this corresponds to extremely collective vibrations where phonon energy is much smaller than two-quasiparticle energies.
- In spherical nuclei, the excitation energies of the lowest \(2^+\) and \(3^-\) states are approximately \(2/3\) of the energy of the lowest two-quasiparticle configuration; keeping only the main term in the sums yields \(R \approx 0.9\).
- The lower limit of \(R\) is 0, which occurs in the Tamm-Dankoff approximation (TDA) where \(Y^{\lambda}_{jj'} \equiv 0\) and the direct \(1^-_1 \to 0^+_{g.s.}\) transition is completely forbidden.

## Context

This work extends the experimental systematic of Pietralla (1999), who reported a nearly constant ratio \(R \approx 1\) across many medium-heavy spherical nuclei and derived \(R = 7/3\) from a simple bosonic phonon model. The present QRPA analysis shows that the bosonic value is an unreachable upper limit and that the experimental value \(R \approx 1\) provides evidence that the lowest \(2^+\) and \(3^-\) states are collective vibrations built on a correlated vacuum. The results enable future microscopic calculations of dipole transition strengths in spherical nuclei and clarify the role of ground-state correlations in two-phonon decays.
