# Nuclear Excitation by Near-Resonant Electron Transition in $^{229}$Th$^{39+}$ Ions

> Theoretical considerations are made for the nuclear excitation from the ground state to the 8 eV $^{229m}$Th isomer via near-resonant electron transitions in Sb-like ($q=39+$) thorium ions. The energy of the first excited atomic state ($J=7/2$) in the $^{229}$Th$^{39+}$ ion is estimated to be 8.308$\pm$0.069 eV, which is very close to the new reference value for the $^{229m}$Th nuclear isomer energy, 8.356 eV [Zhang et al., Nature 633, 63 (2024)]. Therefore, the $^{229}$Th$^{39+}$ ion provides a unique opportunity to study the nuclear excitation by electron transition process under well-defined atomic conditions in the regime of extremely low nuclear excitation energy. Our results indicate that the upper theoretical limit for the $^{229m}$Th isomer excitation rate reaches an enormous value of $1.27\times10^{16}$ s$^{-1}$ at resonance ($Δ=0$ meV), but drops many orders of magnitude for the value of electronic transition energy within its uncertainty interval. Additionally, it was shown that using an electron beam ion trap, the production of the $^{229}$Th isomer can reach rates ranging from a few to approximately $7\times10^{19}$ $^{-1}$.

## Key claim

The authors theoretically demonstrate that the atomic transition from the first excited state (J=7/2) to the ground state (J=5/2) in Sb-like 229Th39+ ions has an energy of 8.308±0.069 eV, which is very close to the newly established nuclear isomer energy of 8.356 eV from Zhang et al. (2024), but the NEET excitation rate drops by many orders of magnitude when the electronic transition energy lies within its uncertainty interval rather than exactly at resonance.

## Method

The authors employed the Multi-Configuration Dirac-Hartree-Fock (MCDHF) method with Configuration Interaction (CI) using the GRASP2018 code to calculate the atomic energy levels and transition probabilities for the [Kr]4d10 4f5 electronic configuration of Th39+ ions. They then computed the NEET rate using a multiconfigurational approach that accounts for the resonant transfer of energy from the atomic electron transition to the nuclear excitation, incorporating the natural widths of the atomic and nuclear levels and the reduced nuclear transition probabilities for M1 and E2 multipolarities.

## Key numbers

- The energy of the first excited atomic state (J=7/2) relative to the ground state (J=5/2) in 229Th39+ is 8.308±0.069 eV, with the total theoretical uncertainty of 69 meV derived from convergence and correlation contributions
- At exact resonance (Δ=0 meV), the upper theoretical limit for the 229mTh isomer excitation rate W_NEET reaches 2.50×10^16 s^-1
- For Δ=48 meV (when both E_if and E_GM are at the center of their uncertainty intervals), the NEET rate drops to 2.07×10^-4 s^-1
- Under EBIT conditions with 10^6 Th39+ ions and a steady-state population of 2.12% in the first excited atomic level, the achievable isomer excitation rate at resonance is 5.29×10^20 s^-1
- For Δ=48 meV under EBIT conditions, the achievable rate decreases to 4.40 s^-1
- The M1 transition from the J=7/2 to J=5/2 atomic state has a rate of 1.3×10^4 s^-1, while the E2 transition rate is 1.1×10^-2 s^-1
- The natural width of the J=7/2 atomic level is 8.7×10^-12 eV

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical investigations of nuclear excitation by electron transition (NEET) that have been studied for nuclei such as 235U, 237Np, 189Os, 197Au, and 193Ir, where experimental results have been highly inconsistent and vary by orders of magnitude. The authors build on recent proposals for exciting the 229Th isomer via electronic resonance in Th35+ ions by Bilous et al. (2020) and Porsev et al. (2021), and on the precise determination of the 229mTh isomer energy by Zhang et al. (2024) using a VUV frequency comb. The proposed EBIT experiment enables a rigorous test of NEET theory under well-defined atomic conditions at the lowest known nuclear excitation energy, and unlike the EBE mechanism in Th35+ ions studied by Bilous et al. (2020) which achieves maximum rates just over 10^5 s^-1, the NEET process in Th39+ could reach rates up to 5.29×10^20 s^-1 at resonance.
