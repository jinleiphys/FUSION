# Radiative corrections to elastic electron-carbon scattering cross sections in comparison with experiment

> The influence of dispersion on the differential scattering cross section in the vicinity of the first diffraction minimum is revisited for collision energies between 200 and 450 MeV. Transient nuclear excitations in the giant resonance region with angular momentum L \leq 3 are taken into consideration within updated numerics. Moreover, the deviations of the nonperturbative QED corrections from the conventionally used smooth Born predictions are accounted for. A qualitative agreement with the measurements is only obtained for the lowest energy, while dispersion within the present model is too small at the higher energies.

## Key claim

Qualitative agreement between theory and experiment for the radiative corrections to elastic electron-carbon scattering is only obtained at the lowest collision energy (238.1 MeV), while dispersion within the present model is too small at the higher energies of 300.5 and 431.4 MeV, indicating that nuclear excitations below 25 MeV are insufficient to describe dispersion above 200 MeV.

## Method

The differential cross section for elastic electron scattering from spin-zero ¹²C was calculated including nonperturbative QED corrections (Uehling potential for vacuum polarization and a vertex-plus-self-energy potential) and dispersive effects from transient nuclear excitations. Dispersion was treated in second-order Born approximation using the Feynman box diagram, summing over dipole states at 23.5 and 17.7 MeV and quadrupole states at 4.439 and 9.84 MeV, with transition densities from Hartree-Fock plus random phase approximation and the quasiparticle phonon model. The theoretical cross section change was compared to experimental data from Offermann et al. (1991) and Reuter et al. (1982) at collision energies of 238.1, 300.5, and 431.4 MeV.

## Key numbers

- At 238.1 MeV, the bremsstrahlung cutoff was ω₀ = 0.0476 MeV, corresponding to an experimental resolution of 2 × 10⁻⁴.
- At 300.5 MeV, the bremsstrahlung cutoff was ω₀ = 0.0751 MeV, matching a resolution of 2.5 × 10⁻⁴.
- At 431.4 MeV, the bremsstrahlung cutoff was ω₀ = 0.0863 MeV.
- The first diffraction minimum for 300.5 MeV electrons on ¹²C is located at 73.8°.
- The dispersion maximum decreases with increasing impact energy, in contrast to the experimental data.

## Context

This work extends earlier dispersion calculations by Friar and Rosen (1974) that used a closure approximation and by Jakubassa-Amundsen (2022) that included magnetic form factors, but corrects a spurious enhancement of form factors and adds the L=2 contribution at 9.84 MeV. The results show that above 200 MeV, the consideration of nuclear excited states below 25 MeV is insufficient, and hadronic excitations will play an important role, as has been suggested for beam-normal spin asymmetry calculations using experimental Compton scattering cross sections by Gorchtein (2008) and others. An experimental test at energies below the pion production threshold (135 MeV) is needed to validate the present approach.
