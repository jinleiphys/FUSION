# Shear viscosity in magnetized neutron star crust

> The electron shear viscosity due to Coulomb scattering of degenerate electrons by atomic nuclei throughout a magnetized neutron star crust is calculated. The theory is based on the shear viscosity coefficient calculated neglecting magnetic fields but taking into account gaseous, liquid and solid states of atomic nuclei, multiphonon scattering processes, and finite sizes of the nuclei albeit neglecting the effects of electron band structure. The effects of strong magnetic fields are included in the relaxation time approximation with the effective electron relaxation time taken from the field-free theory. The viscosity in a magnetized matter is described by five shear viscosity coefficients. They are calculated and their dependence on the magnetic field and other parameters of dense matter is analyzed. Possible applications and open problems are outlined.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the five coefficients of electron shear viscosity in a magnetized neutron star crust, but the results are strictly valid only where the relaxation time approximation holds and the Wiedemann-Franz law is fulfilled; at densities above about \(10^{12}~\mathrm{g~cm^{-3}}\) and temperatures below about \(10^8~\mathrm{K}\) the treatment becomes semi-quantitative because inelastic electron-nucleus collisions become important.

## Method

The authors solve the linearized Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation for degenerate electrons of any degree of relativity, using the effective electron relaxation time \(\tau\) from the field-free theory of Chugunov and Yakovlev (2005). This relaxation time accounts for Coulomb scattering off atomic nuclei in gaseous, liquid, and solid states, including multiphonon processes, plasma screening, and finite nuclear sizes, but neglects electron band structure. The magnetic field enters through the Hall parameter \(\xHall = \omega \tau\), where \(\omega = eBc/\mu\) is the electron gyrofrequency, and the five viscosity coefficients \(\eta_0\) through \(\eta_4\) are expressed analytically in terms of \(\eta_0\) and \(\xHall\).

## Key numbers

- The neutron drip density is \(\rho_\mathrm{ND} \approx 4.3 \times 10^{11}~\mathrm{g~cm^{-3}}\), dividing the outer and inner crust.
- The inner crust disappears (transforms into the liquid core) at \(\rho \approx 1.4 \times 10^{14}~\mathrm{g~cm^{-3}}\).
- For \(B = 10^{12}~\mathrm{G}\) and temperatures \(10^8\)–\(10^9~\mathrm{K}\), electrons remain weakly magnetized throughout most of the crust; for \(B = 10^{13}~\mathrm{G}\), electrons become strongly magnetized only at \(\rho \lesssim 10^{10}~\mathrm{g~cm^{-3}}\).
- For \(B = 10^{14}~\mathrm{G}\), electrons in the outer crust are mostly strongly magnetized, while in the inner crust they are strongly magnetized only at \(T \lesssim 10^8~\mathrm{K}\).
- At high magnetization (\(\xHall \gg 1\)), the transverse viscosities scale as \(\eta_1 \approx \eta_0/(2\xHall)^2\) and \(\eta_2 \approx \eta_0/\xHall^2\), while the Hall viscosities scale as \(\eta_3 \approx \eta_0/(2\xHall)\) and \(\eta_4 \approx \eta_0/\xHall\).
- At \(T \sim 10^8~\mathrm{K}\), the Wiedemann-Franz law is violated by a factor of about 10 at densities above \(10^{12}~\mathrm{g~cm^{-3}}\), limiting the quantitative reliability of the results in that regime.

## Context

This work extends the field-free electron shear viscosity calculations of Flowers and Itoh (1976) and Chugunov and Yakovlev (2005) to include magnetic fields, using the same formalism previously applied to strange quark stars by Haensel and Jerzak (1989). The results enable modeling of viscous damping of oscillations and instabilities (e.g., r-mode instabilities relevant to gravitational wave experiments) in magnetized neutron star crusts and magnetic white dwarfs, and they provide a framework for future work that must address inelastic collisions, electron band structure, nuclear pasta phases, and very strong fields that quantize electron states into Landau levels.
