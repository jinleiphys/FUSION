# Neutrino-induced forward meson-production reactions in nucleon resonance region

> As a first step toward developing a reaction model that enables a comprehensive description of neutrino-nucleon reactions in the nucleon resonance region, we have applied for the first time a dynamical coupled-channels model, which successfully describes πN, γN --> πN, ηN, ππN, KΛ, KΣreactions up to W = 2 GeV, to predict the neutrino-induced meson-production reactions with ΔS = 0 at the forward angle limit. This has been achieved by relating the divergence of the axial-current matrix elements at Q^2 = 0 to the πN --> X reaction amplitudes through the PCAC hypothesis. We present the contributions from each of the πN, ηN, ππN, KΛ, KΣchannels to the F_2 structure function at Q^2 --> 0 limit up to W = 2 GeV.

# Neutrino-induced forward meson-production reactions in nucleon resonance region

## Key claim

The authors apply for the first time a dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) model, previously validated against pion- and photon-induced reactions up to W = 2 GeV, to predict neutrino-induced meson-production cross sections at the forward angle limit (Q^2 → 0) using the PCAC hypothesis, but only for the ΔS = 0 (strangeness-conserving) channels and only at Q^2 = 0, not at finite momentum transfer.

## Method

The authors relate the divergence of the axial-current matrix elements at Q^2 = 0 to the πN → X reaction amplitudes through the PCAC (Partially Conserved Axial Current) hypothesis. Using total cross sections for πN → X reactions (X = πN, ηN, ππN, KΛ, KΣ) obtained from their DCC model, which satisfies two-body and three-body unitarity and has been fitted to πN and γN data up to W = 2 GeV, they compute the structure function F₂ = ωW₂ for charged-current and neutral-current neutrino-nucleon reactions at the forward angle limit.

## Key numbers

- The DCC model describes πN, γN → πN, ηN, ππN, KΛ, KΣ reactions up to W = 2 GeV.
- The F₂ structure function is computed for W from the 1π-production threshold up to 2 GeV.
- Contributions from ηN, KΛ, and KΣ reactions are O(10⁻¹) to O(10⁻²) times smaller than those from πN and ππN.
- Above W = 1.5 GeV, the ππN contribution becomes comparable to the πN contribution.
- Below W ≈ 1.3 GeV, the DCC πN-only results agree well with the Sato-Lee (SL) model, which directly calculates F₂ without PCAC.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical coupled-channels approach of Kamano et al. (2012) and the earlier Sato-Lee model (2005) from pion- and photon-induced reactions to the weak sector, specifically neutrino-induced meson production. Previous neutrino models for the resonance region were limited to single-pion production (e.g., the coherent resonance sum approach of Lalakulich et al., the tree-level models of Hernandez et al., and the unitarized Δ-region model of Sato and Lee) or to kaon production via tree-level approaches (e.g., Adera et al., Rafi et al.). This work is the first to include coupled-channels unitarity with ππN, ηN, and KY channels for neutrino reactions. The next step, extending the DCC model to finite Q² without relying on PCAC, is stated as underway.
