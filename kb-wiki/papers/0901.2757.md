# J/psi Transverse Momentum Distribution in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> The transverse momentum (pt) dependence of J/psi production in heavy ion collisions is investigated in a transport model with both initial production and continuous regeneration of charmonia. The competition between the two production mechanisms results in a pt suppression in central collisions, the gluon multi-scattering in the initial stage leads to a high pt enhancement, and the regeneration populates J/psis at low pt region and induces a minimum in R_AA(pt). These three phenomena are indeed observed in both 200 GeV Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the observed suppression of the average transverse momentum squared \(\langle p_t^2\rangle\) in central collisions and the minimum in the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}(p_t)\) at low \(p_t\) in Au+Au collisions at RHIC are signatures of charmonium regeneration, but these features depend on the competition between initial production and regeneration; the initial production alone cannot reproduce them, and the exact shape of \(R_{AA}(p_t)\) depends on the dissociation temperature of the \(J/\psi\) (set to \(T_d^{J/\psi}=1.92\,T_c\)) and the gluon rescattering parameter \(a_{gN}=0.1\) (GeV/c)\(^2\)/fm.

## Method

The authors solved a classical Boltzmann-type transport equation for \(J/\psi\), \(\psi'\), and \(\chi_c\) in the transverse phase space, coupled to \((2+1)\)-dimensional Bjorken hydrodynamic equations for the QGP evolution. The transport equation included a loss term from gluon dissociation (with a perturbative cross section and a dissociation temperature cutoff) and a gain term from \(c\bar{c}\) recombination via detailed balance, using a thermalized charm quark distribution. The initial condition incorporated gluon multi-scattering (Cronin effect) via a \(p_t\)-broadening term, and the final \(J/\psi\) distribution was obtained by summing contributions from direct production and feed-down from \(\psi'\) and \(\chi_c\), integrated over the freeze-out surface using the Cooper-Frye formula.

## Key numbers

* \(\langle p_t^2\rangle_{NN} = 4.14\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) for the intrinsic transverse momentum square in nucleon-nucleon collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV.
* Gluon rescattering parameter \(a_{gN} = 0.1\) (GeV/c)\(^2\)/fm used for the Cronin effect at RHIC (compared to 0.077 (GeV/c)\(^2\)/fm at SPS).
* Charm quark mass \(m_c = 1.87\) GeV; \(J/\psi\) binding energy \(\epsilon_{J/\psi} = 150\) MeV.
* Dissociation temperature for \(J/\psi\): \(T_d^{J/\psi} = 1.92\,T_c\) with critical temperature \(T_c = 165\) MeV; \(\psi'\) and \(\chi_c\) dissociate at \(T_d = T_c\).
* Charm quark pair production cross section at RHIC: \(d\sigma_{NN}^{c\bar{c}}/dy|_{y=0} = 120\ \mu\)b; \(J/\psi\) production cross section (times branching ratio) \(B_{ll}d\sigma_{NN}^{J/\psi}/dy|_{y=0} = 26.4\) nb.
* For central Au+Au collisions, the minimum in \(R_{AA}(p_t)\) occurs at \(p_t \sim 2.5\) GeV/c.
* At LHC, predicted \(d\sigma_{NN}^{J/\psi}/dy = 2\ \mu\)b and \(d\sigma_{NN}^{c\bar{c}}/dy = 0.7\) mb.

## Context

This work extends the transport model of Zhuang (2008) by including the full \(p_t\) dependence of \(J/\psi\) production and comparing to RHIC data from PHENIX (data1, data2) and STAR (data3). It contrasts with earlier SPS results where \(\langle p_t^2\rangle\) saturated rather than suppressed in central collisions (Hufner 1997, 2000) and with models that treat regeneration only on a hadronization hypersurface (Munzinger 2002) or via coalescence (Greco 2004). The findings enable predictions for LHC (Pb+Pb) where regeneration is expected to dominate, producing an increasing \(R_{AA}\) with centrality and a saturated \(\langle p_t^2\rangle\) in central collisions.
