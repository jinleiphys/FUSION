# Bremsstrahlung photons from stopping in heavy-ion collisions

> We examine the spectrum of bremsstrahlung photons that results from the stopping of the initial net charge distributions in ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collier (LHC). This effect has escaped detection so far since it becomes sizable only at very low transverse momentum and at sufficiently forward rapidity. We argue that it may be within reach of the next-generation LHC heavy-ion detector ALICE-3 that is currently under study, and we comment on the physics motivation for measuring it.

## Key claim
The paper argues that bremsstrahlung photons from the stopping of initial net charge distributions in ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions at the LHC, which have escaped detection so far because they become sizable only at very low transverse momentum and sufficiently forward rapidity, may be within reach of the next-generation ALICE-3 detector currently under study.

## Method
The authors model the incoming and outgoing charge currents of colliding nuclei using classical electrodynamics, with the outgoing current parameterized by different toy models of the final rapidity distribution of net charge: full transparency (no stopping), Bjorken boost-invariant stopping (flat rapidity distribution), Landau stopping (all charges stopped at mid-rapidity), and Gaussian distributions. They then compute the bremsstrahlung photon spectrum from these currents using the classical bremsstrahlung formula of Jackson (1998), including a transverse nuclear form factor for a fixed sphere of radius R = 6.8 fm for lead nuclei, and evaluate the resulting double-differential distributions in photon energy, pseudorapidity, and transverse momentum for central PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers
- For the Landau stopping scenario, a total of 64 GeV of energy is radiated per central Pb-Pb collision into the phase space region 10 MeV < p_T < 100 MeV and pseudorapidity eta in [4,5]; for the Gaussian scenario this is 47 GeV, and for the Bjorken scenario 18 GeV.
- For the Landau stopping scenario, 23.5 GeV (8.5 GeV) is radiated into eta in [3,4] ([2,3]) for the same p_T range.
- Between 5 and 20 bremsstrahlung photons per unit pseudorapidity are found in the p_T bin [10 MeV; 20 MeV], depending on the stopping scenario.
- In the p_T bin [50 MeV; 60 MeV], between 0.5 and 5 bremsstrahlung photons per unit pseudorapidity and per event are found.
- The photon number spectrum follows dN/dp_T proportional to 1/p_T for p_T less than about 40 MeV.
- For pseudorapidity eta = 3 (eta = 5), the condition q < 1 for the form factor translates into omega < 300 MeV (omega < 2.2 GeV), respectively.

## Context
This work extends classical bremsstrahlung calculations from the late 1990s by Jeon (1998), Kapusta (1999), and Wong (2000) that indicated measurability of stopping effects at RHIC, but which were never realized experimentally. The paper is motivated by the proposed ALICE-3 detector concept (Adamova, 2019) that promises access to unprecedentedly low transverse momentum (p_T ~ O(10 MeV)) and forward rapidity (y=4 or y=5). The results provide a baseline for distinguishing bremsstrahlung from the dominant background of photons from pi0 decays, which have a characteristically different p_T-dependence (flat versus 1/p_T) and centrality-dependence (proportional to N_part versus N_part^2).
