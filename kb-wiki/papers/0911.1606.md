# Probing the density dependence of symmetry energy at subsaturation density with HICs

> The reaction mechanism of the central collisions and peripheral collisions for $^{112,124}Sn+^{112,124}Sn$ at $E/A=50MeV$ is investigated within the framework of the Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics model. The results show that multifragmentation process is an important mechanism at this energy region, and the influence of the cluster emission on the double n/p ratios and the isospin transport ratio are important. Furthermore, three observables, double n/p ratios, isospin diffusion and the rapidity distribution of the ratio $R_{7}$ for $^{112,124}Sn+^{112,124}Sn$ at E/A=50MeV are analyzed with the Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics model. The results show that these three observables are sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy. By comparing the calculation results to the data, the consistent constraint on the density dependence of the symmetry energy from these three observables is obtained.

## Key claim

By comparing Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics (ImQMD) model calculations to experimental data for three observables (double n/p ratios, isospin diffusion, and the rapidity distribution of the ratio R7) in 112,124Sn+112,124Sn collisions at E/A=50 MeV, a consistent constraint on the density dependence of the symmetry energy is obtained, but the analysis shows that cluster emission effects are important and the data still carry large uncertainties that limit the precision of the constraint.

## Method

Central and peripheral collisions of 112,124Sn+112,124Sn at E/A=50 MeV were simulated using the ImQMD05 model, which includes cluster formation automatically. The density dependence of the symmetry energy was parameterized as Esym(ρ) = (1/3)(ħ2/2m)ρ0^(2/3)(3π2/2 * ρ/ρ0)^(2/3) + (Cs/2)(ρ/ρ0)^γi, with Cs=35.2 MeV and γi varied between 0.35 and 2.0. Three observables were computed: double neutron-to-proton ratios DR(n/p) for emitted nucleons, isospin transport ratios Ri, and the rapidity distribution of R7 (from yield ratios of 7Li and 7Be); these were compared to experimental data from NSCL via χ2 analysis.

## Key numbers

- For DR(n/p) data, within 2σ uncertainty, γi falls in the range 0.4 ≤ γi ≤ 1.05 for Cs = 35.2 MeV.
- For isospin transport ratios Ri(α), the χ2 analysis yields 0.45 ≤ γi ≤ 1.0 within 2σ.
- For the rapidity distribution of R7 at b=6 fm, the χ2 analysis favors 0.45 ≤ γi ≤ 0.95 within 2σ.
- The double ratio DR(n/p) peaks around γi = 0.7 due to competing effects; very soft (γi=0.35) and very stiff (γi=2.0) cases are ruled out.
- The heaviest fragments lose about 35% of their initial velocity for central collisions (b=2 fm), about 25% for b=6 fm, and about 10% for b=8 fm.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the symmetry energy density dependence using heavy-ion collisions, particularly the isoscaling work of Tsang (2001) and isospin diffusion measurements of Tsang (2004) and Liu (2007). It uses the ImQMD model to simultaneously constrain γi from three observables, whereas earlier transport model comparisons often used only one observable. The results enable future work to incorporate cluster emission effects more accurately and to combine constraints from giant dipole resonances, pygmy dipole resonances, mass data, and neutron star observations to reduce the remaining uncertainties.
