# Lattice QCD Constraints on the Nuclear Equation of State

> Based on the quasi-particle description of the QCD medium at finite temperature and density we formulate the phenomenological model for the equation of state that exhibits crossover or the first order deconfinement phase transition. The models are constructed in such a way to be thermodynamically consistent and to satisfy the properties of the ground state nuclear matter comply with constraints from intermediate heavy--ion collision data. Our equations of states show quite reasonable agreement with the recent lattice findings on temperature and baryon chemical potential dependence of relevant thermodynamical quantities in the parameter range covering both the hadronic and quark--gluon sectors. The model predictions on the isentropic trajectories in the phase diagram are shown to be consistent with the recent lattice results. Our nuclear equations of states are to be considered as an input to the dynamical models describing the production and the time evolution of a thermalized medium created in heavy ion collisions in a broad energy range from SIS up to LHC.

## Key claim

The paper constructs phenomenological equations of state for QCD matter that can describe either a crossover or a first-order deconfinement phase transition, and claims "quite reasonable agreement" with lattice QCD results, but only after the lattice data are multiplied by ad hoc constant factors (0.518 for zero baryon chemical potential and 0.446 for nonzero chemical potential) to approximately account for finite-size effects on the N_t=4 lattice.

## Method

The authors formulate two quasi-particle models based on a density-dependent mean-field Hamiltonian: a two-phase (2P) model with a first-order phase transition between a hadron resonance gas (with masses up to 1.6 GeV) and a quark-gluon plasma (with massive quarks and gluons in a bag), and a mixed-phase (MP) model where hadrons and unbound quarks/gluons coexist in a homogeneous phase with interactions between all constituents, producing a crossover transition. The hadronic sector uses a modified Zimanyi mean-field model with repulsive and attractive interactions scaled by constituent quark number, while the plasma sector incorporates a potential term mimicking hard thermal loop (HTL) masses and a confining term. Model parameters are fixed by ground-state nuclear matter properties (saturation density n_0 ≈ 0.15 fm⁻³, binding energy -16 MeV, incompressibility 210 MeV) and by tuning to reproduce the lattice transition temperature and thermodynamic quantities from Fodor et al. (2004) for (2+1)-flavor QCD with m_π ≈ 508 MeV.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state nuclear matter saturation density: n_0 ≈ 0.15 fm⁻³, with binding energy per nucleon of -16 MeV and incompressibility of 210 MeV.
* In the 2P model, the bag constant B^(1/4) = 223 MeV and parameter α = 2.1 yield a transition temperature T_c ≈ 176 MeV and ε/T⁴|_(T_c) = 7.84 at μ_B = 0.
* In the MP model, with parameters A^(1/(3γ+1)) = 270 MeV and γ = 0.3, the critical temperature (defined by the heat capacity maximum) is T_c = 188 MeV at μ_B = 0.
* The 2P model at T = 0 gives a coexistence region starting at n_B/n_0 ≈ 8, which is "by factor two larger" than in the conventional MIT bag model.
* Quark masses used in the plasma phase: m_u = m_d = 65 MeV, m_s = 135 MeV (2.08 × m_u), and gluon mass m_g = 700 MeV; bag constant B^(1/4) = 207 MeV yields T_c ≈ 170 MeV for physical pion masses.
* Lattice data from Fodor et al. (2004) are multiplied by correction factors c_0 = 0.518 (μ_B = 0) and c_μ = 0.446 (μ_B ≠ 0) to account for finite N_t = 4 lattice size.

## Context

This work extends the quasi-particle approach of Szabo et al. (2003) and the thermodynamic consistency framework of Toneev et al. (2003) to construct equations of state that simultaneously satisfy constraints from ground-state nuclear matter, heavy-ion collision flow analysis (Danielewicz et al., 2002), neutron star properties (Klahn et al., 2006), and lattice QCD results from Fodor et al. (2002, 2004) for (2+1)-flavor systems. The models are intended as input for dynamical simulations of heavy-ion collisions from SIS to LHC energies, and the comparison with lattice data uses the hadron mass scaling parametrization of Karsch et al. (2003) to account for the heavier pion masses (m_π ≈ 508 MeV) used in those lattice calculations.
