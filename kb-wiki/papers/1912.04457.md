# Towards a full solution of relativistic Boltzmann equation for quark-gluon matter on GPUs

> We have developed a numerical framework for a full solution of the relativistic Boltzmann equations for the quark-gluon matter using the multiple Graphics Processing Units (GPUs) on distributed clusters. Including all the $2 \to 2$ scattering processes of 3-flavor quarks and gluons, we compute the time evolution of distribution functions in both coordinate and momentum spaces for the cases of pure gluons, quarks and the mixture of quarks and gluons. By introducing a symmetrical sampling method on GPUs which ensures the particle number conservation, our framework is able to perform the space-time evolution of quark-gluon system towards thermal equilibrium with high performance. We also observe that the gluons naturally accumulate in the soft region at the early time, which may indicate the gluon condensation.

## Key claim

The authors develop a GPU-based numerical framework for solving the full relativistic Boltzmann equation for quark-gluon matter including all 2-to-2 scattering processes of 3-flavor quarks and gluons, but only as a first step that omits inelastic 2-to-3 and 3-to-2 processes which may be essential for thermalization.

## Method

The relativistic Boltzmann equation is discretized in phase space using central differences, and the collision integrals for all 2-to-2 scattering processes among gluons, u, d, s quarks and their antiparticles are computed via direct Monte Carlo integration using the ZMCintegral 5.0 package. A "symmetrical sampling" method is introduced on GPUs using CUDA atomic operations to enforce strict particle number conservation by reusing each Monte Carlo sample across all four related momentum grids. The code is implemented with Python libraries Numba and Ray to enable distributed GPU clusters.

## Key numbers

- Thermalization time scale expected from hydrodynamics is less than 1 fm/c (Heinz 2003) or even shorter than 0.25 fm/c (Bozek 2011, Broniowski 2009) in nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC and LHC.
- The "bottom-up scenario" (Baier 2001) estimates thermalization time of order tau_th ~ alpha_s^{-13/5} / Q_s, which is too large compared to hydrodynamics requirements.
- BAMPS simulations (El 2008) give thermal equilibration time scale of order alpha_s^{-2} ln(alpha_s)^{-2} Q_s^{-1}.
- The gluon helicity and color degeneracy factor N_g = 2 x 8 = 16.
- The quark helicity and color degeneracy factor N_q = 2 x 3 = 6.

## Context

This work extends numerical approaches to solving the relativistic Boltzmann equation for quark-gluon matter, building on prior parton cascade models such as BAMPS (Xu 2005) and ZPC (Zhang 1998), as well as effective kinetic theory implementations for pure gluons (Kurkela 2014, Kurkela 2015) and quark-gluon systems (Kurkela 2019, Keegan 2016). The framework enables full space-time evolution of distribution functions toward thermal equilibrium with GPU acceleration, and the observation of gluon accumulation in the soft region at early times may indicate gluon condensation, a mechanism that could help resolve the early thermalization puzzle in heavy-ion collisions. Future work should include inelastic 2-to-3 and 3-to-2 processes, which have been argued to be essential for thermalization (Wong 1996) and may strongly hinder gluon condensation (Blaizot 2017, Lenkiewicz 2019).
