# Clustering and pasta phases in nuclear density functional theory

> Nuclear density functional theory (DFT) is the tool of choice in describing properties of complex nuclei and intricate phases of bulk nucleonic matter. It is a microscopic approach based on an energy density functional representing the nuclear interaction. An attractive feature of nuclear DFT is that it can be applied to both finite nuclei and pasta phases appearing in the inner crust of neutron stars. While nuclear pasta clusters in a neutron star can be easily characterized through their density distributions, the level of clustering of nucleons in a nucleus can often be difficult to assess. To this end, we use the concept of nucleonic localization. We demonstrate that the localization measure provides us with fingerprints of clusters in light and heavy nuclei, including fissioning systems. Furthermore we investigate the rod-like pasta phase using twist-averaged boundary conditions, which enable calculations in finite volumes accessible by state of the art DFT solvers.

## Key claim

The nucleon localization function (NLF) can serve as a fingerprint of cluster structures in both finite nuclei and pasta phases, revealing shell effects that are not visible in particle density distributions alone; the authors demonstrate this for light and heavy nuclei including fissioning systems, and for rod-like pasta phases using twist-averaged boundary conditions to minimize finite-volume effects.

## Method

The authors use nuclear density functional theory (DFT) with Skyrme-type energy density functionals (SkM*, UNEDF1-HFB, SLy6, and TOV-min) to compute nucleonic densities and localization measures. For finite nuclei, they employ the DFT solvers HFBTHO and HFODD with harmonic oscillator basis expansions; for infinite rod-like pasta phases, they use the symmetry-unconstrained DFT solver Sky3d with a Cartesian 3D grid and twist-averaged boundary conditions (TABC) to average over Bloch angles between zero and pi.

## Key numbers

- For the rod phase calculations, the constant mean density is set to 0.0358 fm^-3
- The total energy minimum for rods at proton fraction X_P=1/3 occurs near a one-dimensional rod density of approximately 18 fm^-1
- At the lower proton fraction X_P=1/6, the total energy minimum shifts to a rod density of approximately 20 fm^-1
- For X_P=1/6, rods calculated with TOV-min are about 1 MeV lower in energy than those with SLy6
- The neutron background density at X_P=1/6 is approximately 0.01 nucleons/fm^3
- For the fission pathway of 264Fm, the neck develops at quadrupole moment Q_20 approximately 145 b and scission occurs at Q_20 approximately 265 b

## Context

This work extends the application of the localization measure, originally introduced for electronic systems by Becke and Savin and applied to nuclear structure by Reinhard (2011), to both finite nuclei and pasta phases. The DFT+TABC approach for pasta builds on prior work by Schuetrumpf (2015b) and others who studied pasta phases in semi-classical models (Dorso 2012, Watanabe 2009) and quantum mechanical models (Mag 2002, Schuetrumpf 2013a, Schuetrumpf 2014). The results enable future studies of alpha clustering in medium-mass nuclei and clustering in heavy fissioning systems, as well as NLF investigations of various nuclear pasta phases.
