# Flavour and Energy Dependence of Chemical Freeze-out Temperatures in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions from RHIC-BES to LHC Energies

> We present calculations of the chemical freeze-out temperature (T_ch) based on particle yields from STAR and ALICE measured at collision energies ranging from sqrt{s_{NN}} = 11.5 GeV to 5.02 TeV. Employing the Grand Canonical Ensemble approach using the Thermal-FIST Hadron Resonance Gas model package, we show evidence for a flavour-dependent chemical freeze-out in the crossover region of the QCD phase diagram. At a vanishing baryochemical potential, we calculate light and strange flavour freeze-out temperatures T_L = 150.2 +- 2.6 MeV and T_S = 165.1 +- 2.7 MeV, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper presents evidence that light and strange hadrons chemically freeze-out at different temperatures in the crossover region of the QCD phase diagram, with the two temperatures converging only at baryochemical potentials above approximately 300 MeV; the abstract's claim of flavour-dependent freeze-out is qualified by this convergence at high baryochemical potential.

## Method

The authors performed Grand Canonical Ensemble thermal fits using the Thermal-FIST Hadron Resonance Gas model package with the PDG2016+ hadronic spectrum (738 states) to particle yield data from STAR AuAu collisions at six energies (11.5 to 200 GeV) and ALICE PbPb collisions at 2.76 and 5.02 TeV. They separately fitted three sets of particle species: light (pions, kaons, protons), strange (kaons, lambdas, xis, omegas, K0S, phi), and full (all combined), with temperature and volume as free parameters and baryochemical potential either fixed to zero (ALICE) or free (STAR). Weak-decay feed-down contributions from lambda decays to proton yields were corrected using an interpolation method.

## Key numbers

- Light flavour freeze-out temperature at vanishing baryochemical potential: T_L = 150.2 ± 2.6 MeV
- Strange flavour freeze-out temperature at vanishing baryochemical potential: T_S = 165.1 ± 2.7 MeV
- Full fit freeze-out temperature at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV: T_ch = 150.4 ± 1.5 MeV with chi^2/dof = 78.9/12
- Light fit freeze-out temperature at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV: T_ch = 142.4 ± 1.7 MeV with chi^2/dof = 14.6/4
- Strange fit freeze-out temperature at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV: T_ch = 164.4 ± 2.28 MeV with chi^2/dof = 1.58/8
- Full fit freeze-out temperature at sqrt(s_NN) = 11.5 GeV: T_ch = 151.8 ± 1.89 MeV with mu_B = 310.7 ± 11.7 MeV
- Light and strange freeze-out temperatures converge within errors at mu_B approximately 300 MeV
- The two-temperature (2CFO) approach reduces chi^2/dof by at least a factor of two compared to single-temperature fits at all energies

## Context

This work extends prior lattice QCD susceptibility calculations by Ratti (2012) and the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration that showed flavour-dependent freeze-out temperatures differing by 15-20 MeV between light and strange quarks, as well as STAR Collaboration results (BES program) showing a 10-15 MeV lower freeze-out temperature when fitting only pions, kaons, and protons. The two-temperature approach improves upon the single chemical freeze-out (1CFO) method used in the Heidelberg-GSI model by Andronic (2018) and resolves the so-called "proton anomaly" in ALICE data. The results enable searches for a critical point in the QCD phase diagram above mu_B approximately 300 MeV and below T_ch approximately 150 MeV, and provide constraints for lattice QCD calculations of isentropic trajectories at finite density.
