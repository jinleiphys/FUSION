# Parity violation in quasielastic electron scattering from closed-shell nuclei

> The electromagnetic and weak neutral current matrix elements that enter in the analysis of parity-violating quasielastic electron scattering are calculated using a continuum nuclear shell model. New approximations to the on-shell relativistic one-body currents and relativistic kinematics for use in such models are developed and discussed in detail. Results are presented for three closed-shell nuclei of interest: $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb. The current work concludes with a study of the sensitivity of the resulting parity-violating asymmetries to properties of the nucleon form factors including the possible strangeness content of the nucleon.

## Key claim

The parity-violating asymmetry in quasielastic electron scattering from closed-shell nuclei, calculated using a continuum shell model (CSM) with new relativistic approximations to the one-body currents, shows sufficient stability against nuclear model uncertainties to allow extraction of nucleon form factor information, provided the appropriate observables are chosen.

## Method

The authors computed electromagnetic and weak neutral current matrix elements for quasielastic electron scattering using a continuum shell model (CSM) with Woods-Saxon mean-field potentials, restricting to one-body currents in the impulse approximation. They developed new approximations to the on-shell relativistic one-body currents and relativistic kinematics for use in the CSM, and applied these to calculate parity-violating response functions and asymmetries for three doubly-closed-shell nuclei: 16O, 40Ca, and 208Pb. The sensitivity of the resulting asymmetries to nucleon form factors, including possible strangeness content, was studied by varying parameters in the Galster parameterization of the electromagnetic form factors and the axial-vector dipole form factor.

## Key numbers

* For 16O, 40Ca, and 208Pb, the parity-violating asymmetry was calculated using the CSM and compared with results from the relativistic Fermi gas model.
* The axial-vector dipole mass was taken as 1 GeV, corresponding to λ_A^D = 3.32 in the dipole form factor G_A^D(τ) = [1 + λ_A^D τ]^{-2}.
* The electromagnetic form factors used the Galster parameterization with λ_V^D = 4.97 and λ_n = 5.6 for the neutron electric form factor.
* The axial-vector coupling constant was taken as g_A = 1.262.
* The study focused on the region τ < 0.3 for the strangeness form factors.
* At q = 550 MeV/c, the CSM and continuum RPA results were found to be practically indistinguishable for the parity-conserving responses.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of parity-violating quasielastic electron scattering that used the relativistic Fermi gas model (Donnelly et al., 1992) and more sophisticated models incorporating correlations and meson exchange currents (Alberico et al., 1988, 1990, 1993; Barbaro et al., 1994) by applying a continuum shell model that has been thoroughly tested for parity-conserving responses (Amaro et al., 1992, 1994a, 1994b). The results enable comparison of nuclear model uncertainties in extracting nucleon strangeness and axial-vector form factors from parity-violating asymmetry measurements, complementing existing experimental efforts at Bates on 12C and Mainz on 9Be.
