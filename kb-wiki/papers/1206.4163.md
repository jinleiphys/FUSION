# Chiral dynamics in form factors, spectral-function sum rules, meson-meson scattering and semilocal duality

> In this work, we perform the one-loop calculation of the scalar and pseudoscalar form factors in the framework of U(3) chiral perturbation theory with explicit tree level exchanges of resonances. The meson-meson scattering calculation from Ref.[1] is extended as well. The spectral functions of the nonet scalar-scalar (SS) and pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar (PP) correlators are constructed by using the corresponding form factors. After fitting the unknown parameters to the scattering data, we discuss the resonance content of the resulting scattering amplitudes. We also study spectral-function sum rules in the SS-SS, PP-PP and SS-PP sectors as well as semi-local duality from scattering. The former relate the scalar and pseudoscalar spectra between themselves while the latter mainly connects the scalar spectrum with the vector one. Finally we investigate these items as a function of Nc for Nc > 3. All these results pose strong constraints on the scalar dynamics and spectroscopy that are discussed. They are successfully fulfilled by our meson-meson scattering amplitudes and spectral functions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a combined framework of one-loop U(3) chiral perturbation theory with explicit tree-level resonance exchanges can simultaneously satisfy spectral-function sum rules and semilocal duality constraints, but only when the high-energy constraint for vector resonance couplings to pseudo-Goldstone bosons is properly imposed, particularly when studying the large-Nc evolution.

## Method

The authors performed a one-loop calculation of scalar and pseudoscalar form factors within U(3) chiral perturbation theory, including tree-level exchanges of scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector resonances from resonance chiral theory. They constructed spectral functions of the scalar-scalar and pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar correlators using these form factors, unitarizing the scalar ones via the N/D method. The unknown parameters were fitted to meson-meson scattering data, extending the previous calculation from Guo et al. (2011) by including pseudoscalar resonance exchanges and a delta-L8 operator.

## Key numbers

- The f0(500) resonance pole position is around 440 - i 250 MeV at Nc=3, playing a crucial role in opposing vector strength from the rho(770) resonance to fulfill semilocal duality
- A bare singlet scalar resonance S1 with bare mass around 1 GeV is found significant to reproduce pi-pi and K-Kbar scattering data around the f0(980) energy region
- The eta meson becomes much lighter with increasing Nc, having a strong impact on Nc pole trajectories of scalar resonances except for the f0(500) due to its weak couplings to channels with eta and eta'
- For Nc greater than 8, the f0(500) pole turns around moving back towards the real axis above 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the meson-meson scattering calculation from Guo et al. (2011) by including pseudoscalar resonance exchanges and a delta-L8 operator, and builds upon the U(3) chiral perturbation theory framework with resonance saturation of low-energy constants from Ecker et al. (1989). The study of semilocal duality follows the approach of Pelaez et al. (2011), who investigated how light resonances and Regge theory satisfy duality in pi-pi scattering using the Inverse Amplitude Method. The results enable simultaneous constraints on scalar dynamics from spectral-function sum rules, semilocal duality, and large-Nc evolution, revealing that the f0(980) resonance gradually evolves to the singlet S1 when increasing Nc, acting as the q-qbar seed described in Pelaez et al. (2011) and Llanes-Estrada (2011).
