# Squeezed back-to-back correlation of $\bqD^0{\bar \bqD}^0$ in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We investigate the squeezed back-to-back correlation (BBC) of $D^0\!{\bar D}^0$ in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, using the in-medium mass modification calculated with a self-energy in hot pion gas and the source space-time distributions provided by the viscous hydrodynamic code VISH2+1. It is found that the BBC of $D^0\!{\bar D}^0$ is significant in peripheral Au+Au collisions at the RHIC energy. A possible way to detect the BBC in experiment is presented.

## Key claim

The squeezed back-to-back correlation (BBC) of \(D^0{\bar D}^0\) is significant in peripheral Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy, but the effect is conditional on the in-medium mass modification of the \(D\) meson in a hot pion gas and is most observable when selecting \(D\) mesons with masses smaller than the peak of the in-medium mass distribution.

## Method

The authors calculated the in-medium mass shift and width of the \(D\) meson moving in a hot pion gas using the FMFK framework, which incorporates resonance interactions with pions via self-energy integrals over \(s\)-, \(p\)-, and \(d\)-wave resonances (\(D^*_0\), \(D^*\), \(D^*_2\)). They then computed the squeezed BBC function for \(D^0{\bar D}^0\) pairs using the space-time distributions from the viscous hydrodynamic code VISH2+1 for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, with the in-medium mass taken according to a Breit-Wigner distribution.

## Key numbers

- The mass of the \(D\) meson is taken as \(M_D = 1.865\) GeV/\(c^2\).
- The resonance masses used are: \(D^*\) at 2008.5 MeV/\(c^2\), \(D^*_0\) at \(2308 \pm 60\) MeV/\(c^2\), and \(D^*_2\) at \(2461.6 \pm 5.9\) MeV/\(c^2\).
- The on-mass-shell decay widths are: \(\Gamma^{D\pi}_{j0} \approx 0.1\) MeV/\(c^2\) for \(D^*\), \(276 \pm 99\) MeV/\(c^2\) for \(D^*_0\), and \(45.6 \pm 12.5\) MeV/\(c^2\) for \(D^*_2\).
- The squeezed BBC functions are calculated for momentum regions 0.55–0.65 GeV, 0.75–0.85 GeV, and 1.15–1.25 GeV, with the freeze-out temperature taken as 150 MeV.
- The maxima of the squeezed BBC functions for the \(m_* < M_*\) selection are about twice those for the full distribution.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of squeezed back-to-back correlations, originally developed by AsaCso (1996) and Padula (2006), to heavy \(D\) mesons in heavy-ion collisions. It uses the in-medium self-energy calculations of Fuchs, Martemyanov, Faessler, and Krivoruchenko (2006) for \(D\) mesons in a hot pion gas, combined with the viscous hydrodynamic code VISH2+1. The results enable experimental searches for the squeezed BBC of \(D^0{\bar D}^0\) at RHIC, particularly by selecting \(D\) mesons with masses below the peak of the in-medium mass distribution, and motivate further work on removing the background from heavy-quark correlations.
