# Femtoscopy can tell whether $Z_c(3900)$ and $Z_{cs}(3985)$ are resonances or virtual states

> There have been extended and heated discussions on the nature of the two exotic states, $Z_c(3900)$ and $Z_{cs}(3985)$, particularly whether they are near-threshold resonances, virtual states, or bound states. In this work, we demonstrate for the first time that the femtoscopic technique can be employed to distinguish between these three scenarios. More concretely, based on the Koonin-Pratt formula with a Gaussian source, we show that the low-momentum $D^0D^{*-}$/$D^0D_s^{*-}$ correlation functions significantly differ in the three scenarios. The high-momentum results exhibit distinct characteristics in the resonant and virtual state scenarios, especially in small collision systems of 1 fm, as produced in $pp$ collisions at the LHC. We hope that these discoveries will stimulate further experimental studies and help clarify the nature of the many exotic states that have been discovered.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate for the first time that the femtoscopic technique, using the Koonin-Pratt formula with a Gaussian source, can distinguish whether the exotic states \(Z_c(3900)\) and \(Z_{cs}(3985)\) are near-threshold resonances, virtual states, or bound states, based on the low-momentum \(D^0D^{*-}\) and \(D^0D_s^{*-}\) correlation functions. The claim is hedged by the assumption that these states are molecular states of \(D^0D^{*-}\) and \(D^0D_s^{*-}\), and that coupled-channel effects (e.g., \(\pi J/\psi - D\bar{D}^*\)) may offset some high-momentum features but not the low-momentum distinctions.

## Method

The authors model the \(D^0D^{*-}\) and \(D^0D_s^{*-}\) interactions using an S-wave potential up to next-to-leading order in momentum expansion, \(V(k) = a + b k^2\), with low-energy constants \(a\) and \(b\) determined by reproducing the pole positions of \(Z_c(3900)\) and \(Z_{cs}(3985)\) in three scenarios: resonant, virtual, and bound state. They then compute the femtoscopic correlation functions via the Koonin-Pratt formula with a Gaussian source of size \(R\), using the on-shell \(T\)-matrix and a regularized loop function with a sharp cutoff \(q_{\rm max}\) varied between 0.6 and 1.4 GeV to assess off-shell uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- For \(Z_c(3900)\): resonant pole mass \(M = 3887.1\) MeV, width \(\Gamma = 28.4\) MeV (PDG 2024); virtual state pole at \(M = 3871\) MeV; bound state pole at \(M = 3867\) MeV.
- For \(Z_{cs}(3985)\): resonant pole mass \(M = 3988\) MeV, width \(\Gamma = 14\) MeV (PDG 2024); virtual state pole at \(M = 3974\) MeV; bound state pole at \(M = 3972\) MeV.
- Scattering length \(a_0\) for \(Z_c(3900)\) resonant scenario: \(-0.6^{+0.2}_{-0.1}\) fm; virtual: \(-2.1^{+0.1}_{-0.0}\) fm; bound: \(1.7^{+0.1}_{-0.0}\) fm.
- Scattering length \(a_0\) for \(Z_{cs}(3985)\) resonant scenario: \(-0.4^{+0.2}_{-0.1}\) fm; virtual: \(-2.4^{+0.1}_{-0.0}\) fm; bound: \(2.1^{+0.1}_{-0.0}\) fm.
- Relative difference in correlation functions between resonant and virtual scenarios for \(R = 1\) fm: about \(7\%\) for \(D^0D^{*-}\) at \(k \approx 230\) MeV, and about \(17\%\) for \(D^0D_s^{*-}\) at \(k \approx 180\) MeV.
- For \(R = 0.9\) fm: relative difference about \(10\%\) for \(D^0D^{*-}\) at \(k \approx 230\) MeV, and about \(23\%\) for \(D^0D_s^{*-}\) at \(k \approx 180\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends femtoscopic studies of hadronic interactions, previously applied to light-quark systems (e.g., by ALICE) and to bound-state molecular scenarios for exotic hadrons (Liu 2023, Vidana 2023, Ikeno 2023), by explicitly addressing resonant and virtual states. It contradicts the common interpretation of \(Z_c(3900)\) and \(Z_{cs}(3985)\) as simple Breit-Wigner resonances (BESIII 2013, Belle 2013, BESIII 2020), and instead shows that femtoscopy can resolve the ambiguity left by invariant mass spectrum analyses (Guo 2013, Albaladejo 2015, Baru 2021). The work enables future experimental measurements in \(pp\), \(pA\), and \(AA\) collisions at the LHC to determine the nature of these and other near-threshold exotic states.
