# Chiral three-nucleon force at N^4LO I: Longest-range contributions

> We derive the sub-subleading two-pion exchange contributions to the three-nucleon force which appear at next-to-next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory. In order to determine the low-energy constants, a complete analysis of pion-nucleon scattering at the subleading-loop order in the heavy-baryon expansion is carried out utilizing the power counting scheme employed in the derivation of the nuclear forces. We discuss the convergence of the chiral expansion for this particular three-nucleon force topology and give the values of the low-energy constants which provide the most realistic description of the three-nucleon force when the chiral expansion is truncated at next-to-next-to-leading order.

## Key claim

The paper derives the sub-subleading two-pion-exchange contributions to the three-nucleon force at next-to-next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N⁴LO) in chiral effective field theory, but these contributions are only the longest-range part of the full N⁴LO three-nucleon force, and the convergence of the chiral expansion for this topology is expected to be slow because intermediate Δ(1232) isobar excitations are not yet fully accounted for at this order.

## Method

The authors used the method of unitary transformation to separate irreducible three-nucleon force contributions from reducible topologies, working within the heavy-baryon formulation of chiral effective field theory with pions and nucleons as explicit degrees of freedom. They calculated one-loop diagrams constructed from leading-order vertices with single insertions of subleading vertices proportional to the low-energy constants c_i, tree diagrams involving vertices from the fourth-order pion-nucleon Lagrangian, and relativistic 1/m-corrections. To determine the low-energy constants, they performed a complete analysis of pion-nucleon scattering at order Q⁴ in the heavy-baryon expansion using the power counting scheme employed in the derivation of nuclear forces, where Q/m ∼ Q²/Λ_χ² rather than m ∼ Λ_χ.

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory treatment of three-nucleon forces beyond the N²LO calculations by Epelbaum et al. (2002) and the N³LO parameter-free contributions by Bernard et al. (2007, 2011). The N⁴LO contributions derived here are the first step toward including Δ-isobar effects in the standard Δ-less approach, complementing the Δ-full approach where Δ-excitations appear already at N³LO. The results enable future calculations of nucleon-deuteron scattering and properties of light nuclei at N⁴LO, which may help resolve persistent puzzles such as the A_y-puzzle and the space-star configuration discrepancy in three-nucleon breakup.
