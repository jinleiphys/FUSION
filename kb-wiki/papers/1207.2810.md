# Superfluidity and collective modes in Rashba spin-orbit coupled Fermi gases

> We present a theoretical study of the superfluidity and the corresponding collective modes in two-component atomic Fermi gases with s-wave attraction and synthetic Rashba spin-orbit coupling. The general effective action for the collective modes is derived from the functional path integral formalism. By tuning the spin-orbit coupling from weak to strong, the system undergoes a crossover from an ordinary BCS/BEC superfluid to a Bose-Einstein condensate of rashbons. We show that the properties of the superfluid density and the Anderson-Bogoliubov mode manifest this crossover. At large spin-orbit coupling, the superfluid density and the sound velocity become independent of the strength of the s-wave attraction. The two-body interaction among the rashbons is also determined. When a Zeeman field is turned on, the system undergoes quantum phase transitions to some exotic superfluid phases which are topologically nontrivial. For the two-dimensional system, the nonanalyticities of the thermodynamic functions and the sound velocity across the phase transition are related to the bulk gapless fermionic excitation which causes infrared singularities. The superfluid density and the sound velocity behave nonmonotonically: they are suppressed by the Zeeman field in the normal superfluid phase, but get enhanced in the topological superfluid phase. The three-dimensional system is also studied.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a two-component atomic Fermi gas with s-wave attraction and synthetic Rashba spin-orbit coupling undergoes a crossover from an ordinary BCS/BEC superfluid to a Bose-Einstein condensate of rashbons as the spin-orbit coupling is tuned from weak to strong, and that at large spin-orbit coupling the superfluid density and sound velocity become independent of the s-wave attraction strength. This result is derived within a mean-field plus Gaussian fluctuation approximation at zero temperature, and the crossover is demonstrated through the behavior of the superfluid density and the Anderson-Bogoliubov mode.

## Method

The authors use a functional path integral formalism to derive the general effective action for collective modes in a homogeneous spin-1/2 Fermi gas with s-wave attraction and Rashba spin-orbit coupling. They first solve the saddle-point (mean-field) equations for the superfluid order parameter and chemical potential, then expand the action to Gaussian order around the saddle point to obtain the inverse boson propagator for collective excitations. The dispersion of the Anderson-Bogoliubov (Goldstone) mode and the Higgs mode are obtained from the determinant of this propagator, and the superfluid density is extracted from the phase stiffness coefficients in a small-momentum expansion.

## Key numbers

* For the two-dimensional system with nonzero Zeeman field, the superfluid density and sound velocity behave nonmonotonically: they are suppressed by the Zeeman field in the normal superfluid phase but enhanced in the topological superfluid phase. (No explicit numerical values given for the suppression or enhancement.)
* The critical Zeeman field for the Chandrasekhar-Clogston limit in s-wave weak-coupling superconductors is given as \(h_{\rm CC} = \Delta_0 / \sqrt{2}\), where \(\Delta_0\) is the zero-temperature gap at \(h=0\).
* The Fulde-Ferrell-Larkin-Ovchinnikov state may survive in a narrow window between \(h_{\rm CC}\) and \(h_{\rm FFLO} \approx 0.754 \Delta_0\).
* The spin-orbit coupling strength is characterized by the dimensionless ratio \(\lambda/k_{\rm F}\), and in cold atom systems this ratio can reach the order \(O(1)\).
* The total fermion density is defined as \(n = k_{\rm F}^3/(3\pi^2)\) for the three-dimensional system, with Fermi energy \(\epsilon_{\rm F} = k_{\rm F}^2/2\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of BCS-BEC crossover in Fermi gases by incorporating synthetic Rashba spin-orbit coupling, building on prior studies of spin-orbit coupled Fermi superfluids by groups such as SOC-BCSBEC, SOC-Hu, SOC-Yu, SOC-Gong, SOC-Iskin, SOC-He, SOC-Han, and SOC-Yi (all 2011-2012). It also connects to the study of topologically nontrivial superfluid phases in solid-state systems (TSC01-TSC09, 2008-2011) and to experimental work on spin-orbit coupled atomic Fermi gases (SOCF01, SOCF02, 2011-2012). The results enable future investigation of the interplay between spin-orbit coupling and Zeeman fields in ultracold Fermi gases, particularly the quantum phase transitions to topological superfluid phases and the associated nonanalyticities in thermodynamic functions.
