# Implications of local parity breaking in heavy ion collisions

> Recent data on dilepton production in heavy ion collisions revealed an abnormal excess in the region of invariant masses below 1 GeV. Our proposal is the creation of a slowly varying time-dependent pseudoscalar condensate within the hot nuclear fireball that comes from the very collision. The local parity breaking effect that immediately arises substantially modifies the vector meson properties leading to an excess of lepton pairs that could be a part of the explanation for the observed abnormal dilepton yield.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a time-dependent pseudoscalar condensate formed inside the hot nuclear fireball of a heavy ion collision creates local parity breaking (LPB), which modifies vector meson properties and produces an excess of dilepton pairs that could partially explain the abnormal dilepton yield observed by PHENIX below 1 GeV invariant mass. The authors explicitly state this is a "qualitative answer" and that the effect "could be a part of the explanation," not a complete solution.

## Method

The authors use a Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) framework in the SU(2) flavor sector to model the effect of a pseudoscalar condensate, parameterized by a chiral chemical potential ζ, on the ρ and ω meson spectral functions. They introduce a parity-odd mixing term in the Lagrangian that splits vector meson masses into three polarizations (ε = 0, ±1), then compute the dilepton production rate after applying experimental acceptance cuts (transverse momentum cut |p^e_t| > 200 MeV, rapidity cut |y_ee| < 0.35) and Gaussian smearing of 10 MeV bin width using the VEGAS numerical integration routine.

## Key numbers

- The abnormal dilepton yield observed by PHENIX in Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV is enhanced by a factor of approximately 4.7 compared to p+p collisions.
- The pseudoscalar condensate parameter ζ is estimated to be of order ζ ∼ α τ^{-1} ∼ 1 MeV, based on a condensate formation time τ ∼ 1 fm and condensate value of order f_π.
- For the isosinglet case relevant to RHIC and LHC (T ≫ μ), the vector meson mass splitting is given by m_V,ε^2 = m_V^2 − 360 ε ζ |k|, where the numerical factor 360 arises from the coupling constants (g ≃ 6, e^2/4π ≃ 1/137).
- The fireball lifetime is τ_fb ∼ 5–10 fm, while the oscillation time for u,d quarks is 1/m_q ∼ 40 fm (much longer than fireball) and for s quarks is 1/m_s ∼ 1 fm (much shorter than fireball).
- The average temperature used for PHENIX is T ≃ 220 MeV.
- The effective number of ρ mesons within the fireball is larger than expected from direct collision by a factor of about 1.8, according to PHENIX.
- The ρ and ω contributions are shown for ζ = 1 MeV and ζ = 2 MeV, with the latter producing visible polarization asymmetry in the spectral function.

## Context

This work extends the proposal of local parity breaking in QCD via metastable domains and pseudoscalar condensates, previously discussed in the context of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME). The authors argue that the CME and the unexplained dilepton excess may be complementary effects revealing two facets of a thermodynamic phase where parity is locally broken. The paper specifically addresses the PHENIX anomaly at RHIC, where standard hadron decay cocktails fail to explain the observed dilepton enhancement below 1 GeV. The authors note that future experiments at FAIR and NICA, where T ≪ μ, may probe an isotriplet pseudoscalar background rather than the isosinglet case studied here, and that incorporation of Dalitz ω and η decays is expected to provide additional excess in the 300–700 MeV region.
