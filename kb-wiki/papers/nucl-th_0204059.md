# Four-body cluster structure of $A=7-10$ double-$О›$ hypernuclei

> Energy levels of the double-$О›$ hypernuclei $_О›^{}$$_О›^7$He, $_О›^{}$$_О›^7$Li, $_О›^{}$$_О›^8$Li, $_О›^{}$$_О›^9$Li, $_О›^{}$$_О›^9$Be and $_О›^{}$$_О›^{10}$Be are predicted on the basis of the $О±+x+О›+О›$ four-body model with $x=n, p, d, t, ^3$He and $О±$, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper predicts energy levels of double-О› hypernuclei with A=7-10 using a four-body cluster model, with the key condition that the О›О› interaction is calibrated to reproduce the NAGARA event value for _О›_О›^6He, and then applied with no further adjustable parameters to heavier systems. The authors interpret the Demachi-Yanagi event as observation of the 2^+ excited state of _О›_О›^10Be rather than its ground state.

## Method

The authors employ an О± + x + О› + О› four-body cluster model (with x = n, p, d, t, ^3He, О±) solved using the Jacobian-coordinate coupled-rearrangement-channel Gaussian-basis variational method. Interactions between constituent particles are determined by fitting to observed low-energy properties of О±+x nuclei, existing О›-binding energies of single-О› hypernuclei, and the NAGARA event value for _О›_О›^6He, with no adjustable parameters remaining for the four-body calculations of A=7-10 systems.

## Key numbers

- _О›_О›^6He ground state B_О›О› = 7.25 В± 0.19 В± 0.18/0.11 MeV (NAGARA event, used as input to calibrate the О›О› interaction)
- _О›_О›^10Be(2^+) calculated B_О›О› = 12.28 MeV, agreeing with the Demachi-Yanagi event value of B_О›О› = 12.33 В± 0.35/0.21 MeV
- _О›_О›^10Be(2^+) excitation energy calculated as 2.86 MeV
- For _О›_О›^8Li: calculated B_О›О› = 6.80 MeV (matching the observed 6.80 В± 0.03 MeV for the 1^+ ground state of _О›^8Li)
- For _О›_О›^6He (_О›_О›^6Li): calculated energy relative to _О›^5He-N threshold is -0.17 MeV (0.57 MeV), matching observed values of -0.17 MeV (0.59 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous cluster-model calculations (Hiyama 1997) for _О›_О›^6He and _О›_О›^10Be to a comprehensive set of A=7-10 double-О› hypernuclei, updating those results with the NAGARA event as a new standard. The calculations improve upon earlier simplified cluster models by Takaki (1989) and variational Monte Carlo work by Bodmer (1984, 1987), and provide a more complete treatment than the s-wave Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations by Filikhin and Gal (2001). The predictions enable interpretation of future emulsion events from KEK-E373 and other experiments, and allow extraction of the О›О› interaction strength without the structural ambiguities present in frozen-core approximations.
