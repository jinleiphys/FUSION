# Four-body cluster structure of $A=7-10$ double-$Λ$ hypernuclei

> Energy levels of the double-$Λ$ hypernuclei $_Λ^{}$$_Λ^7$He, $_Λ^{}$$_Λ^7$Li, $_Λ^{}$$_Λ^8$Li, $_Λ^{}$$_Λ^9$Li, $_Λ^{}$$_Λ^9$Be and $_Λ^{}$$_Λ^{10}$Be are predicted on the basis of the $α+x+Λ+Λ$ four-body model with $x=n, p, d, t, ^3$He and $α$, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper predicts energy levels of double-Λ hypernuclei with A=7-10 using a four-body cluster model, with the key condition that the ΛΛ interaction is calibrated to reproduce the NAGARA event value for _Λ_Λ^6He, and then applied with no further adjustable parameters to heavier systems. The authors interpret the Demachi-Yanagi event as observation of the 2^+ excited state of _Λ_Λ^10Be rather than its ground state.

## Method

The authors employ an α + x + Λ + Λ four-body cluster model (with x = n, p, d, t, ^3He, α) solved using the Jacobian-coordinate coupled-rearrangement-channel Gaussian-basis variational method. Interactions between constituent particles are determined by fitting to observed low-energy properties of α+x nuclei, existing Λ-binding energies of single-Λ hypernuclei, and the NAGARA event value for _Λ_Λ^6He, with no adjustable parameters remaining for the four-body calculations of A=7-10 systems.

## Key numbers

- _Λ_Λ^6He ground state B_ΛΛ = 7.25 ± 0.19 ± 0.18/0.11 MeV (NAGARA event, used as input to calibrate the ΛΛ interaction)
- _Λ_Λ^10Be(2^+) calculated B_ΛΛ = 12.28 MeV, agreeing with the Demachi-Yanagi event value of B_ΛΛ = 12.33 ± 0.35/0.21 MeV
- _Λ_Λ^10Be(2^+) excitation energy calculated as 2.86 MeV
- For _Λ_Λ^8Li: calculated B_ΛΛ = 6.80 MeV (matching the observed 6.80 ± 0.03 MeV for the 1^+ ground state of _Λ^8Li)
- For _Λ_Λ^6He (_Λ_Λ^6Li): calculated energy relative to _Λ^5He-N threshold is -0.17 MeV (0.57 MeV), matching observed values of -0.17 MeV (0.59 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous cluster-model calculations (Hiyama 1997) for _Λ_Λ^6He and _Λ_Λ^10Be to a comprehensive set of A=7-10 double-Λ hypernuclei, updating those results with the NAGARA event as a new standard. The calculations improve upon earlier simplified cluster models by Takaki (1989) and variational Monte Carlo work by Bodmer (1984, 1987), and provide a more complete treatment than the s-wave Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations by Filikhin and Gal (2001). The predictions enable interpretation of future emulsion events from KEK-E373 and other experiments, and allow extraction of the ΛΛ interaction strength without the structural ambiguities present in frozen-core approximations.
