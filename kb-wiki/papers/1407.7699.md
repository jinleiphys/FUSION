# Toward global beyond-mean-field calculations of nuclear masses and low-energy spectra

> Self-consistent mean field (MF) and beyond-mean-field (BMF) calculations of masses, separation energies and $2^{+}_{1}$ excitation energies of even-even nuclei where experimental data is available are presented. The functionals used are based on the Gogny D1S and D1M parametrizations and the method includes beyond-mean-field corrections coming from both axial quadrupole shape mixing and symmetry restorations without assuming gaussian overlap approximations. A comparison between mean field, beyond-mean-field approaches and the experimental data is provided. Additionally, the convergence of the results and the possible reduction of the magic shell gaps by including BMF effects are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that beyond-mean-field corrections from axial quadrupole shape mixing and symmetry restorations, computed without Gaussian overlap approximations, can be added to mean-field results to improve global mass predictions, but the total energies remain not fully converged with respect to the number of oscillator shells, especially for heavy nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with Gogny D1S and D1M parametrizations using a spherical harmonic oscillator basis with 19 major shells. Beyond-mean-field corrections were computed using particle number and angular momentum projection combined with the generator coordinate method (GCM) for axial quadrupole shape mixing, using 11 oscillator shells. The total energy was obtained as the sum of the HFB energy at 19 shells and the BMF correction defined as the difference between the GCM energy and the HFB energy, both computed at 11 shells.

## Key numbers

- Root mean square deviation from experimental masses for existing global models: 0.57 MeV (FRDM), 0.298 MeV (WS), 0.36 MeV (DZ), 0.51 MeV (HFB-Skyrme), 0.798 MeV (HFB-Gogny)
- For the example nucleus 120Cd with Gogny D1S: HFB energy = -1011.786 MeV, PN-VAP gain = 1.96 MeV, PNAMP gain = 2.80 MeV, GCM shape mixing gain = 1.02 MeV, total BMF correction = 5.77 MeV, total energy = -1017.559 MeV
- BMF correlation energies for even-even nuclei with Z,N >= 10 range from 1.5 to 3.5 MeV, with a mean gain of approximately 2.3 MeV
- Differences between HFB results for N(s.h.o.)=19 and 17 are around 0.8 MeV on average
- Computational time per nucleus at GSI-Prometheus: HFB with 19 shells ~2.46 hours/core, GCM with 11 shells ~14.5 hours/core, total ~62 hours/core

## Context

This work extends global mass calculations beyond the mean-field level by including symmetry restorations and shape mixing without Gaussian overlap approximations, going beyond earlier approaches that used the five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) with Gogny interactions (Rodriguez 2010) or assumed Gaussian overlap approximations in Skyrme-based GCM calculations (Bender 2006, Bender 2008). The results enable systematic improvement of nuclear mass predictions for astrophysical applications, particularly the r-process, and provide a baseline for future inclusion of triaxial and octupole degrees of freedom. The authors note that 159 out of 5900 nuclei with triaxial ground states are found in the FRDM model, and that including triaxiality in the present framework would increase computational time prohibitively.
