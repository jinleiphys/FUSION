# The baryon-decuplet in the chiral dynamics of Lambda-hyperons in nuclear matter

> We study the long range part of the $Λ$-hyperon optical potential in nuclei using Quantum Many Body techniques and flavor-SU(3) Chiral Lagrangians as starting point. More precisely, we study the contributions to the $Λ$-hyperon optical potential due to the long-range two-pion exchange, with $Σ$ and $Σ^*$ baryons in the internal baryonic lines and considering Nh and $Δ$h excitations. We also consider the contribution to the spin-orbit potentials that comes out from these terms. Our results support a natural explanation of the smallness of the $Λ$-nuclear spin-orbit interaction and shows the importance of the $Σ^*$ and $Δ$ degrees of freedom for the hyperon-nucleus interactions.

## Key claim

The long-range two-pion exchange contributions to the \(\Lambda\)-hyperon optical potential, including \(\Sigma^*\) and \(\Delta\) baryons in intermediate states, support a natural explanation of the smallness of the \(\Lambda\)-nuclear spin-orbit interaction through cancellation between short- and long-range pieces, but the central potential's total size is not predictable because it requires a cut-off regularization whose value is adjusted to reproduce the empirical depth.

## Method

The authors used flavor-SU(3) chiral Lagrangians and quantum many-body techniques to compute the \(\Lambda\)-hyperon self-energy in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter. They evaluated two-pion exchange diagrams with \(\Sigma\) and \(\Sigma^*\) baryons in intermediate states, considering both nucleon-hole (Nh) and \(\Delta\)-hole (\(\Delta\)h) excitations, and extracted the central mean-field potential and spin-orbit potential from the resulting self-energy. A non-relativistic expansion in an average baryon mass was performed, and divergent integrals were regularized either by subtracting a divergent piece and introducing a cut-off \(\bar{\Lambda}\) or by direct cut-off regularization.

## Key numbers

- The depth of the \(\Lambda\) mean-field potential at saturation nuclear density \(\rho_0\) is around \(-30\) MeV.
- The coupling constants used are \(D=0.84\), \(F=0.46\), \(g_A = D+F\), \({\cal C}_{\Sigma^*} = 1.7\), and \({\cal C}_{\Delta} = 2.0\).
- The mass splittings are \(\Delta = 285\) MeV (for \(\Sigma-\Lambda\)), \(\Delta_f = 553\) MeV (for \(\Delta-N\)), and \(\Delta^* = 532\) MeV (for \(\Sigma^*-\Lambda\)).
- The convergent Nh-o contributions to the central potential at \(\rho=\rho_0\) are \(U_\Lambda(k_{f0})^{\rm Nh-o}=7.45\) MeV and \(U^*_\Lambda(k_{f0})^{\rm Nh-o}=5.83\) MeV.
- The cut-off \(\bar{\Lambda}=1077\) MeV was adjusted to produce a central potential of \(-30\) MeV at \(\rho=\rho_0\) using the analytical subtraction method; an alternative direct cut-off regularization used \(\bar{\Lambda}=600\) MeV.
- The spin-orbit potential from the long-range diagrams is convergent and requires no regularization; the short-range spin-orbit piece is parametrized as \(U_{\Lambda ls}^{\rm sr}(k_{f})=21.3\,C_l\) MeV fm\(^2\) \(\rho/\rho_0\) with \(C_l\) between 1/2 and 2/3.

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach of Kaiser (2004), who studied the \(\Lambda\) mean field and spin-orbit interaction using only nucleon-hole excitations in two-pion exchange, by including the baryon decuplet (\(\Delta\) and \(\Sigma^*\)) in intermediate states. The results show that the \(\Sigma^*\) and \(\Delta\) degrees of freedom are crucial for the hyperon-nucleus interaction, and the opposite sign of the spin-orbit contributions from \(\Sigma\) and \(\Sigma^*\) intermediate states preserves the cancellation mechanism that explains the small \(\Lambda\)-nuclear spin-orbit interaction. This enables further studies of short-range correlations and their effect on the total hyperon-nucleus potential, as in Tolos (2006).
