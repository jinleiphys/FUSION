# Threshold and jet radius joint resummation for single-inclusive jet production

> We present the first threshold and jet radius jointly resummed cross section for single-inclusive hadronic jet production. We work at next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy and our framework allows for a systematic extension beyond the currently achieved precision. Longstanding numerical issues are overcome by performing the resummation directly in momentum space within Soft Collinear Effective Theory. We present the first numerical results for the LHC and observe an improved description of the available data. Our results are of immediate relevance for LHC precision phenomenology including the extraction of parton distribution functions and the QCD strong coupling constant.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first numerical results for a cross section that jointly resums both threshold logarithms and jet-radius logarithms for single-inclusive jet production, achieving next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy; the abstract's claim of "improved description of the available data" is conditioned on the observation that the joint resummation decreases the cross section relative to NLO, thereby improving agreement with data that NLO and NNLO calculations had overshot.

## Method

The authors derive a factorization formula within Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) that separates the cross section into hard functions, an inclusive jet function, a global soft function, a signal-jet function, and a soft-collinear function, each evolved via renormalization group equations from their natural scales to a common scale. The resummation is performed directly in momentum space, avoiding the numerical difficulties that had prevented previous Mellin-space approaches from producing results for differential jet cross sections. The NLL-resummed result is matched to the full NLO calculation using the subtraction formula dσ = dσ_NLL − dσ_NLO_sin + dσ_NLO, where NLO_sin is the expansion of the factorized expression to NLO.

## Key numbers

* At √S = 13 TeV with pT > 700 GeV and 1.5 < |η| < 2, the ratio KR of the NLO_sin result to the full NLO QCD calculation shows very good agreement for R = 0.2, 0.4, and 0.6 (Fig. 1).
* For √S = 2.76 TeV with |η| < 2, the resummed calculation improves the description of CMS data for all jet radii R (Fig. 2), with the data and theory both normalized to the NLO result.
* For √S = 13 TeV with R = 0.4 and 1.5 < |η| < 2, the joint resummation decreases the cross section relative to NLO, improving agreement with preliminary ATLAS data that the NLO prediction overshot by about 7% to 10% (Fig. 3).

## Context

This work extends the threshold resummation efforts for inclusive jet production that had previously been limited to fixed-order expansions (Kidonakis 2000, deFlorian 2013) due to numerical issues in Mellin-space approaches, and it builds on the small-R resummation framework of Dasgupta (2014) and Kang (2016). The results enable improved precision for LHC phenomenology, including parton distribution function extraction and determination of the QCD strong coupling constant, and the framework is designed to be systematically extendable to NNLL accuracy and matching with the recently completed NNLO calculations (Currie 2016, 2017).
