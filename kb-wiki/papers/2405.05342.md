# Quasielastic Lepton-Nucleus Scattering and the Correlated Fermi Gas Model

> The neutrino research program in the coming decades will require improved precision. A major source of uncertainty is the interaction of neutrinos with nuclei that serve as targets for such experiments. Broadly speaking, this interaction often depends, e.g., for charge-current quasi-elastic scattering, on the combination of ``nucleon physics", expressed by form factors, and ``nuclear physics", expressed by a nuclear model. It is important to get a good handle on both. We present a fully analytic implementation of the Correlated Fermi Gas Model for electron-nucleus and charge-current quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering. The implementation is used to compare separately form factors and nuclear model effects for both electron-carbon and neutrino-carbon scattering data.

## Key claim

The paper presents a fully analytic implementation of the Correlated Fermi Gas (CFG) model for electron-nucleus and charge-current quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering, enabling separate comparison of nucleon form factor effects and nuclear model effects in scattering data, with the caveat that the CFG model fits some kinematic regions better than the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) model but not all.

## Method

The authors derive analytic expressions for the nuclear tensor Wμν by convolving the single-nucleon tensor Hμν with the CFG momentum distribution, which has a constant low-momentum region (below Fermi momentum pF) and a high-momentum tail proportional to 1/|p|^4 (between pF and λpF). They compute six possible transitions between initial and final nucleon momentum regions, each with distinct integration limits determined by energy and momentum conservation, and sum them to obtain the total cross section. The implementation is compared to electron-carbon scattering data at various beam energies and angles, and to flux-averaged MiniBooNE neutrino-carbon data, using different form factor parametrizations for both electromagnetic (BBBA and BHLT) and axial (dipole and z-expansion) form factors.

## Key numbers

- CFG model parameters: c0 = 4.16 ± 0.95, λ ≈ 2.75 ± 0.25, with pF as the Fermi momentum
- For electron-carbon at 480 MeV and 60° scattering angle, the maximum differential cross section at ω = 0.122 GeV is: RFG with BHLT = 9159 ± 37 nb/sr-GeV, RFG with BBBA = 8968 ± 146 nb/sr-GeV, CFG with BHLT = 7861 ± 32 nb/sr-GeV, CFG with BBBA = 7698 ± 125 nb/sr-GeV
- For the RFG model at 480 MeV and 60°, the kinematic range is 0.031 GeV ≤ ω ≤ 0.196 GeV; for the CFG model, ω can reach up to 0.298 GeV for the same kinematics
- Binding energy used: 25 MeV (with comparison to 52.2 MeV in appendix)
- Approximately 20% of nucleons have momentum greater than the Fermi momentum for Q² > 1.5 GeV², based on prior measurements cited

## Context

This work extends the CFG model originally suggested by Hen (2014) for studying short-range correlated nucleon pairs, which was previously applied to neutron star equations of state and the EMC effect. The paper directly compares to the RFG model used in earlier analyses by Bhattacharya (2011, 2015) that extracted axial form factors from MiniBooNE data, and addresses the long-standing issue of separating nuclear model effects from nucleon form factor effects that arose from the MiniBooNE axial mass puzzle. The fully analytic implementation enables future work to systematically disentangle nuclear and nucleon uncertainties in neutrino oscillation experiments, and the authors note that including final-state interactions as done by Ankowski (2014) could improve agreement at lower energies.
