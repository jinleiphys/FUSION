# Improving the Convergence of NN Effective Field Theory

> We study a low-energy effective field theory (EFT) describing the NN system in which all exchanged particles are integrated out. We show that fitting the residue of the 3S1 amplitude at the deuteron pole, rather than the 3S1 effective range, dramatically improves the convergence of deuteron observables in this theory. Reproducing the residue ensures that the tail of the deuteron wave function, which is directly related to NN scattering data via analytic continuation, is correctly reproduced in the EFT at next-to-leading order. The role of multi-nucleon-electroweak operators which produce deviations from effective-range theory can then be explicitly separated from the physics of the wave function tail. Such an operator contributes to the deuteron quadrupole moment, mu_Q, at low order, indicating a sensitivity to short-distance physics. This is consistent with the failure of impulse approximation calculations in NN potential models to reproduce mu_Q. The convergence of NN phase shifts in the EFT is unimpaired by the use of this new expansion.

## Key claim

Fitting the residue of the 3S1 amplitude at the deuteron pole (parameterized by Z_d) rather than the 3S1 effective range rho_d dramatically improves the convergence of deuteron observables in the pionless effective field theory EFT(pi-slash), while the convergence of NN phase shifts remains unimpaired.

## Method

The authors construct a low-energy effective field theory (EFT) for the NN system in which all exchanged particles, including pions, are integrated out, leaving only contact interactions. They compare two parameterizations of the EFT coefficients: the "rho-parameterization" which fits the effective range rho_d from the effective-range expansion, and the "z-parameterization" which instead fits the residue Z_d of the 3S1 amplitude at the deuteron pole. The coefficients are determined by matching to the effective-range expansion order-by-order in the momentum expansion parameter Q, with the difference between the two parameterizations being formally higher-order in Q.

## Key numbers

- Inverse deuteron binding momentum: gamma^{-1} = 4.319 fm
- 3S1 effective range: rho_d = 1.765(4) fm
- Effective-range expansion parameter w_2 = 0.389 fm^3
- Residue Z_d = 1.690(3), computed from Z_d = 1/(1 - gamma*rho_d)
- Asymptotic D-to-S ratio: eta_sd = 0.02544 +/- 0.0002
- In the z-parameterization, the deuteron charge radius squared at N2LO: <r_d^2> = 4.566 fm^2 (including nucleon charge radius r_N = 0.790 +/- 0.012 fm)
- Deuteron quadrupole moment at NLO: mu_Q = 0.286 fm^2 (fit to experiment)
- Quadrupole radius squared at N2LO: <r_Q^2> = 1.40 fm^4
- Deuteron electric polarizability in z-parameterization: alpha_E0^(z) = 0.6339 fm^3
- Ratio of isoscalar E2 to isovector M1 matrix element in np -> d gamma: -2.47 x 10^{-4}

## Context

This work extends the pionless EFT approach developed by Weinberg (1990-1992), Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1996-1998), and others, which treats nucleons interacting via contact interactions. The authors explicitly build on the observation by Phillips and Cohen (1999) and Rho (1999) that fitting Z_d rather than rho_d ensures correct reproduction of the long-distance deuteron wave function. The z-parameterization enables systematic separation of multi-nucleon-electroweak operators (such as the two-body quadrupole charge operator) from wave-function-tail physics, explaining why impulse-approximation calculations in NN potential models (Nijmegen, Reid, Bonn) persistently underpredict the deuteron quadrupole moment by about 5%. This approach can be extended to improve convergence of three-nucleon observables and the astrophysically important reaction pp -> d e+ nu_e.
