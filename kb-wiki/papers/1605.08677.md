# Induced Hyperon-Nucleon-Nucleon Interactions and the Hyperon Puzzle

> We present the first ab initio calculations for $p$-shell hypernuclei including hyperon-nucleon-nucleon (YNN) contributions induced by a Similarity Renormalization Group transformation of the initial hyperon-nucleon interaction. The transformation including the YNN terms conserves the spectrum of the Hamiltonian while drastically improving model-space convergence of the Importance-Truncated No-Core Shell Model, allowing a precise extraction of binding and excitation energies. Results using a hyperon-nucleon interaction at leading order in chiral effective field theory for lower- to mid-$p$-shell hypernuclei show a good reproduction of experimental excitation energies while hyperon binding energies are typically overestimated. The induced YNN contributions are strongly repulsive and we show that they are related to a decoupling of the $Σ$ hyperons from the hypernuclear system, i.e., a suppression of the $Λ$-$Σ$ conversion terms in the Hamiltonian. This is linked to the so-called hyperon puzzle in neutron-star physics and provides a basic mechanism for the explanation of strong $Λ$NN three-baryon forces.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first *ab initio* calculations for p-shell hypernuclei that include hyperon-nucleon-nucleon (YNN) interactions induced by a Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) transformation, showing that these induced YNN terms are strongly repulsive and arise from the suppression of Lambda-Sigma conversion, which provides a mechanism for explaining the hyperon puzzle in neutron-star physics; however, while excitation energies are reproduced well, hyperon separation energies are typically overestimated by 20 to 50 percent depending on the YN interaction cutoff.

## Method

The authors performed Importance-Truncated No-Core Shell Model (IT-NCSM) calculations for p-shell hypernuclei using a chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction at N3LO and a chiral three-nucleon interaction at N2LO (both with 500 MeV/c cutoff) combined with a chiral hyperon-nucleon interaction at leading order (with 600 and 700 MeV/c cutoffs). They applied an SRG transformation to the Hamiltonian, evaluating the flow equation in a three-body basis constructed from harmonic-oscillator wave functions with respect to three-body Jacobi coordinates, and disentangled the induced YNN terms by subtracting the Hamiltonian evolved in two-body space. The resulting evolved Hamiltonian was then used in the IT-NCSM framework to compute binding and excitation energies for hypernuclei including Li-7, Be-9, and C-13.

## Key numbers

* Lambda separation energy for Li-7: 6.40(16) MeV with YN cutoff 700 MeV/c and induced YNN terms, compared to experimental value 5.58(3) MeV
* Lambda separation energy for Be-9: 8.45(29) MeV with YN cutoff 700 MeV/c and induced YNN terms, compared to experimental value 6.71(4) MeV
* Lambda separation energy for C-13: 14.43(19) MeV with YN cutoff 700 MeV/c and induced YNN terms, compared to experimental value 11.69(12) MeV
* Inclusion of induced YNN terms changes the ground-state energy of Li-7 by about 3.5 MeV at alpha_Y = 0.08 fm^4, compared to a Lambda separation energy of about 7.7 MeV
* Overbinding of hyperons ranges from 20 to 50 percent depending on the YN interaction cutoff

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous *ab initio* calculations of p-shell hypernuclei (Wirth 2014) that suffered from slow convergence due to unevolved YN interactions, and it builds on SRG transformation techniques established for nucleonic systems (Roth 2011, Roth 2014). The findings connect to the hyperon puzzle in neutron-star physics, where phenomenological YN interactions cause equation-of-state softening that cannot support 2-solar-mass neutron stars (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013). The results are compared with Auxiliary-Field Diffusion Monte Carlo calculations (Lonardoni 2013, Lonardoni 2014, Lonardoni 2015) that show the importance of Lambda-NN interactions for the equation of state, and the paper demonstrates that the SRG-induced YNN terms provide a mechanism for understanding why phenomenological models using only Lambda hyperons necessarily need strong Lambda-NN interactions.
