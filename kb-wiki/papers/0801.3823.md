# Role of the $σ$-resonance in determining the convergence of chiral perturbation theory

> The dimensionless parameter $ξ= M_π^2/(16 π^2 F_π^2)$, where $F_π$ is the pion decay constant and $M_π$ is the pion mass, is expected to control the convergence of chiral perturbation theory applicable to QCD. Here we demonstrate that a strongly coupled lattice gauge theory model with the same symmetries as two-flavor QCD but with a much lighter $σ$-resonance is different. Our model allows us to study efficiently the convergence of chiral perturbation theory as a function of $ξ$. We first confirm that the leading low energy constants appearing in the chiral Lagrangian are the same when calculated from the $p$-regime and the $ε$-regime as expected. However, $ξ\lesssim 0.002$ is necessary before 1-loop chiral perturbation theory predicts the data within 1%. For $ξ> 0.0035$ the data begin to deviate dramatically from 1-loop chiral perturbation theory predictions. We argue that this qualitative change is due to the presence of a light $σ$-resonance in our model. Our findings may be useful for lattice QCD studies.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a light σ-resonance can cause chiral perturbation theory to break down at much smaller values of the expansion parameter ξ than in QCD, specifically finding that 1-loop predictions fail for ξ > 0.0035 when Mπ > Mσ/3, whereas QCD's heavier σ allows chiral perturbation theory to work at larger ξ.

## Method

The authors study a 4+1 dimensional lattice model with two flavors of staggered fermions interacting via U(1) gauge fields, tuned close to a second-order phase transition (T=1.7, Tc=1.73779) to produce a light σ-resonance. They measure the vector current susceptibility Yv, chiral current susceptibility Yc, and chiral condensate susceptibility χσ on lattices with L ranging from 12 to 32, fitting the finite-size scaling to 1-loop chiral perturbation theory predictions to extract Fπ, Mπ, and the chiral condensate as functions of the quark mass m (0.0002 ≤ m ≤ 0.01).

## Key numbers

- ξ ≤ 0.002 is required for 1-loop chiral perturbation theory to predict data within 1% error
- For ξ > 0.0035, data begin to deviate dramatically from 1-loop predictions
- The "knee" separating low-ξ and high-ξ regions occurs at ξ' ≈ 0.0035
- From the combined fit (0.0002 ≤ m ≤ 0.001): Σ = 0.4354(3), F = 0.2329(2), cS = 11.9(3), cF = 19.3(5), cM = 39(3)
- Using cS = 12 gives Mσ/F ∼ 2; using cM = 39 also gives Mσ/F ∼ 2
- At ξ' ∼ 0.0035, Mπ ∼ 60 MeV; scaling with F = 90 MeV gives Mσ ∼ 180 MeV
- Chiral perturbation theory breaks down when Mπ > Mσ/3 is roughly satisfied

## Context

This work extends studies of chiral perturbation theory convergence by Sharpe (2006), Bernard (2002), and Giusti (2007) by explicitly demonstrating that a light σ-resonance can cause early breakdown, a scenario previously considered less relevant for QCD where the σ is broad (Mσ ≈ 440 MeV, Γσ ≈ 544 MeV from Caprini (2005)). The results suggest that lattice QCD studies using 2-loop chiral perturbation theory in the region 0.02 < ξ < 0.1 (as done by Matsufuru (2007), Boyle (2007), Urbach (2007), and Kuramashi (2007)) may need to account for σ-resonance effects, and that the quark mass dependence of the σ studied by Pelaez (2007) and Hanhart (2008) could determine where chiral perturbation theory becomes reliable.
