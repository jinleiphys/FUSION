# Is nuclear matter perturbative with low-momentum interactions?

> The nonperturbative nature of inter-nucleon interactions is explored by varying the momentum cutoff of a two-nucleon potential. Conventional force models, which have large cutoffs, are nonperturbative because of strong short-range repulsion, the iterated tensor interaction, and the presence of bound or nearly-bound states. But for low-momentum interactions with cutoffs around 2 fm^{-1}, the softened potential combined with Pauli blocking leads to corrections in nuclear matter in the particle-particle channel that are well converged at second order in the potential, suggesting that perturbation theory can be used in place of Brueckner resummations. Calculations of nuclear matter using the low-momentum two-nucleon force V_{low k} with a corresponding leading-order three-nucleon (3N) force from chiral effective field theory (EFT) exhibit nuclear binding in the Hartree-Fock approximation, and become less cutoff dependent with the inclusion of the dominant second-order contributions. The role of the 3N force is essential to obtain saturation, and the contribution to the total potential energy is compatible with EFT power-counting estimates.

## Key claim

For low-momentum interactions with cutoffs around 2 fm⁻¹, the combined effects of a softened potential and Pauli blocking make the particle-particle channel in nuclear matter well converged at second order in the potential, suggesting that perturbation theory can replace Brueckner resummations, but only when a leading-order three-nucleon force from chiral EFT is included to obtain saturation.

## Method

The authors constructed low-momentum two-nucleon potentials (V_lowk) by evolving the Argonne v₁₈ potential via renormalization-group equations to cutoffs around 2 fm⁻¹, preserving phase shifts up to 330 MeV laboratory energy. They then performed many-body perturbation theory calculations of symmetric nuclear matter using V_lowk augmented with the leading-order chiral three-nucleon force (including 2π-exchange, 1π-exchange, and contact terms), evaluating the energy per particle at Hartree-Fock level and with second-order particle-particle contributions. The perturbative convergence was analyzed using Weinberg eigenvalues of the G₀V operator in free space and in medium.

## Key numbers

- For V_lowk with Λ = 2.1 fm⁻¹, the second-order in-medium contribution to the diagonal ¹S₀ T-matrix is very small over all momenta, in contrast to the Argonne v₁₈ potential where second-order contributions are several times larger than first-order.
- The repulsive Weinberg eigenvalue in the coupled ³S₁–³D₁ channel drops below |η_ν| = 1 for cutoffs Λ < 3.5 fm⁻¹ in free space.
- The empirical saturation Fermi momentum is k_F = 1.35 fm⁻¹, with empirical binding energy E/A = -16 MeV.
- The low-energy constants used for the chiral 3N force are c₁ = -0.76 GeV⁻¹, c₃ = -4.78 GeV⁻¹, c₄ = 3.96 GeV⁻¹, with g_A = 1.29, f_π = 92.4 MeV, and m_π = 138.04 MeV.
- The ¹S₀ neutron-proton scattering length is a = -23.7 fm.

## Context

This work extends the renormalization-group approach to low-momentum interactions (V_lowk) developed by Bogner, Kuo, and collaborators, and challenges the long-standing conclusion of Bethe (1971) that nuclear matter calculations must be nonperturbative. It contradicts earlier studies by Kuckei and Fujii that concluded low-momentum two-nucleon interactions are "wrong" because they overbind, by showing that including the leading chiral three-nucleon force is essential for saturation. The results enable systematic perturbative calculations of nuclear matter using low-momentum interactions with controlled truncation errors, and motivate future full RG evolution of combined two- and three-body forces.
