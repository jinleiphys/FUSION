# Restoration of chiral symmetry in cold and dense Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with tensor renormalization group

> We analyze the chiral phase transition of the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model in the cold and dense region on the lattice developing the Grassmann version of the anisotropic tensor renormalization group algorithm. The model is formulated with the Kogut--Susskind fermion action. We use the chiral condensate as an order parameter to investigate the restoration of the chiral symmetry. The first-order chiral phase transition is clearly observed in the dense region at vanishing temperature with $μ/T\sim O(10^3)$ on a large volume of $V=1024^4$. We also present the results for the equation of state.

## Key claim

The authors observe a first-order chiral phase transition in the cold and dense Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model using the Grassmann anisotropic tensor renormalization group algorithm, but only for a large four-fermi coupling of \(g_0=32\); the functional renormalization group analysis of Aoki (2017) indicates that the phase transition vanishes for smaller coupling values.

## Method

The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model is formulated on the lattice using the Kogut-Susskind fermion action with a four-fermi interaction. The Grassmann version of the anisotropic tensor renormalization group algorithm is developed to coarse-grain the tensor network representation of the partition function, employing a block diagonal representation based on Grassmann parity and randomized singular value decomposition with oversampling parameter \(p=4D\) and \(q=D\) iterations. The chiral condensate is computed via numerical differentiation of the thermodynamic potential with respect to mass, followed by chiral extrapolation using data at \(m=0.01\) and \(m=0.02\).

## Key numbers

- First-order chiral phase transition observed at critical chemical potential \(\mu_c = 3.0625 \pm 0.0625\) in the chiral limit on a \(1024^4\) lattice with bond dimension \(D=55\).
- Particle number density shows an abrupt jump from \(\langle n \rangle = 0\) to \(\langle n \rangle = 1\) at \(\mu_c = 2.9375 \pm 0.0625\) for \(m=0.01\).
- Thermodynamic potential converges to within \(\delta \lesssim 10^{-7}\) at \(\mu=4.0\) and to about \(\delta \sim 10^{-4}\) near the transition point at \(\mu=2.875\) for \(D=55\) on \(V=1024^4\).
- Heavy dense limit benchmark with \(m=10^4\) reproduces the analytical critical chemical potential \(\mu_c = \ln(2m) = 9.903\) on \(1024^4\) lattice with \(D=30\); difference between \(\ln Z(D=25)\) and \(\ln Z(D=30)\) is less than \(2.1\times10^{-3}\%\) near \(\mu_c\).

## Context

This work extends the tensor renormalization group method to four-dimensional fermionic systems, following previous successful applications to the four-dimensional Ising model by Akiyama (2019) and the complex \(\phi^4\) theory at finite density by Akiyama (2020). The results confirm the first-order chiral phase transition predicted by analytical methods such as the mean-field approximation of Buballa (2003) and the functional renormalization group of Aoki (2017), and demonstrate that the Grassmann anisotropic tensor renormalization group can overcome the sign problem that hinders Monte Carlo simulations in the cold and dense region with \(\mu/T \sim O(10^3)\). This enables future determination of the critical end point of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model.
