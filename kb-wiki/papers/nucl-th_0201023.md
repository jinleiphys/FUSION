# On the relation between the long-wave instability and the pion condensation in nuclear matter

> The solutions to the zero-sound frequency equation and pion dispersion equation are considered for the different values of the parameters of the effective particle-hole interaction. The branches of solutions, responsible for the long-wave instability [Pomeranchuk] of the nuclear matter ω_P(k) are presented for the both equations. It is shown that in the pion dispersion equation the solutions ω_P(k) result in the instability not only for values of the spin-isospin constant g'\leq -1/2, violating stability criteria, but for all g' < g'_p (|g'_p|\ll 1 and g'_p < 0). At g' > g'_p the branches ω_P(k) turn into the solutions responsible for pion condensation.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the long-wave instability branch ω_P(k) in the pion dispersion equation produces ground-state instability not only when the spin-isospin constant g' ≤ -1/2 (violating standard stability criteria), but for all g' < g'_p where |g'_p| ≪ 1 and g'_p < 0; at g' > g'_p these branches transform into the solutions responsible for pion condensation.

## Method

The author solves the zero-sound frequency equation and the pion dispersion equation for symmetric nuclear matter modeled as a normal Fermi liquid, using a simple effective particle-hole interaction with only l=0 Legendre components. The particle-hole loop A is computed with explicit expressions for the Migdal function, and the pion polarization operator is constructed including the one-pion exchange interaction renormalized by the spin-isospin constant g'. Solutions are tracked on both the physical and unphysical Riemann sheets as functions of the interaction parameters f, g', the Fermi momentum p_F, and the pion-nucleon coupling f_πNN.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum fixed at p_F = 268 MeV, corresponding to equilibrium density ρ_0 = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Effective nucleon mass m = 0.8 m_0, with m_0 = 0.94 GeV
- Pion mass m_π = 0.14 GeV (used as unit for all plotted quantities)
- Pion-nucleon form factor cutoff Λ = 0.667 GeV
- Critical density for g' = -0.4 and f_πNN = 1.0 is ρ_c = 0.07 ρ_0
- For the model including isobar-hole states, critical density ρ_c ≈ 1.2 ρ_0, corresponding to critical Fermi momentum p_Fc = 283 MeV
- Replacement of branches ω^π_P(k) by ω_c(k) occurs at g'_p between -0.00102 and -0.001
- At g' = -0.00102, the branch ω^π_P(k) has k_3 = 0.08 m_π, k_2 = 0.091 m_π, and k_min = 1.65 m_π
- At g' = -2.0, the branch ω_P(k) turns from imaginary to real at k_int = 4.8 m_π and terminates at k^P_fin = 5.4 m_π

## Context

This work extends the Migdal theory of pion condensation in nuclear matter (Migdal, 1978) by connecting it to the Pomeranchuk long-wave instability criterion. The author shows that the instability branch ω_P(k) from the zero-sound equation and the condensate branch ω_c(k) from the pion dispersion equation are related through a continuous interaction on the unphysical sheet, with a replacement occurring at a small negative g'_p. The results complement earlier studies by Migdal (1978) and Ericson and Weise (1988) on pion condensation, and the author notes that comparison with relativistic calculations by Liu (1990) demonstrates the importance of relativistic corrections. The analysis enables a unified understanding of how long-wave instabilities evolve into pion condensation as the spin-isospin interaction parameter varies.
