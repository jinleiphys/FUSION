# Fusion cross sections at deep subbarrier energies

> A recent publication reports that heavy-ion fusion cross sections at extreme subbarrier energies show a continuous change of their logarithmic slope with decreasing energy, resulting in a much steeper excitation function compared with theoretical predictions. We show that the energy dependence of this slope is partly due to the asymmetric shape of the Coulomb barrier, that is its deviation from a harmonic shape. We also point out that the large low-energy slope is consistent with the surprisingly large surface diffusenesses required to fit recent high-precision fusion data.

# Fusion cross sections at deep subbarrier energies

## Key claim

The continuous increase of the logarithmic slope of heavy-ion fusion cross sections at deep subbarrier energies is not anomalous behavior, but is partly due to the asymmetric shape of the Coulomb barrier deviating from a harmonic (parabolic) approximation, and is consistent with the large surface diffuseness parameters (0.8–1.4 fm) required to fit high-precision fusion data.

## Method

The authors performed exact one-dimensional potential calculations and coupled-channels calculations using the code CCFULL for the ⁵⁸Ni + ⁵⁸Ni system, including double quadrupole-phonon excitations in both projectile and target. They compared these results with the Wong fusion formula and with experimental data from Beckerman (1981), varying the surface diffuseness parameter of the Woods-Saxon nuclear potential while adjusting the depth to keep the barrier height fixed.

## Key numbers

- The parabolic approximation to the Coulomb barrier is adequate only for incident energies within μω²a²/2 = 2.62 MeV of the barrier height for the ⁵⁸Ni + ⁵⁸Ni system with a Woods-Saxon potential (V₀ = 160 MeV, r₀ = 1.1 fm, a = 0.65 fm).
- The dynamical quadrupole deformation parameter β₂ for Coulomb coupling was 0.177 (with r_coup = 1.2 fm), while a larger β₂ = 0.261 (with r_coup = 1.06 fm) was required for nuclear coupling to fit the data.
- Coupled-channels calculations with a = 0.65 fm underestimated the logarithmic slope at subbarrier energies, while a = 1.0 fm improved agreement considerably, and a = 1.3 fm further improved the fit to the logarithmic slope (though it somewhat worsened the fit to the cross section near 97 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Jiang (2002), who reported an abrupt decrease in fusion cross sections at deep subbarrier energies for the ⁶⁰Ni + ⁸⁹Y system and other systems including ⁵⁸Ni + ⁵⁸Ni and ⁹⁰Zr + ⁹²Zr. The authors show that the claimed anomaly arises from using the Wong formula (Wong, 1973) at energies where the parabolic approximation breaks down, and from using a surface diffuseness of about 0.63 fm typical of scattering analyses (Christensen and Winther, 1976; Chamon, 1996), whereas fusion data consistently require larger diffusenesses between 0.8 and 1.4 fm (Leigh, 1995; Hagino, 2001; Newton, 2001). The paper enables future work on whether the large diffuseness reflects the true nuclear potential or mocks up other effects, and motivates further experimental and theoretical studies of fusion at deep subbarrier energies, particularly for astrophysical reactions and exotic nuclei.
