# Born-Infeld magnetars: larger production of gravitational waves due to larger toroidal magnetic fields

> We discuss some aspects of Pereira et al. (2018) concerning magnetars described by nonlinear theories of the electromagnetism and make the case for the Born-Infeld Lagrangian. We focus on the increase of toroidal magnetic fields in these systems with respect to ordinary magnetars and the subsequent increase of gravitational wave production. In summary, nonlinear theories of the electromagnetism would make it more likely for the detection of gravitational waves with future detectors, which could constrain nonlinear aspects of electrodynamics not entirely possible on Earth-based particle accelerators.

## Key claim

Born-Infeld nonlinear electrodynamics predicts that toroidal magnetic fields in magnetars are larger than their Maxwellian counterparts, which increases gravitational wave emission; however, for typical giant flare energies up to 10^47 erg, the maximum increase in gravitational wave energy relative to Maxwell theory is only about 15%, making detection challenging.

## Method

The authors apply ideal magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) with the Born-Infeld Lagrangian to axisymmetric, rigidly rotating magnetars with purely toroidal magnetic fields. They derive the relation between the Born-Infeld toroidal field and the Maxwellian toroidal field from the nonlinear field equations, then compute the ratio of magnetic ellipticities and gravitational wave energy losses between the two theories. They estimate Maxwellian toroidal fields from observed giant flare energies using the relation (B_phi^Ma)^2 = 6 E_fl / R^3, and use these to place upper limits on Born-Infeld ellipticities and lower limits on the Born-Infeld scale field b.

## Key numbers

- For a giant flare energy E_fl = 10^47 erg (SGR 1806–20), with fiducial magnetar parameters R = 10 km and M = 1.4 M_sun, the Maxwellian magnetic ellipticity is |epsilon_Ma| = 1.20 × 10^-6.
- Using the absolute minimum Born-Infeld scale field b_min ≈ 3.96 × 10^15 G (from electron properties), the upper limit on the Born-Infeld ellipticity is |epsilon_ul| ≈ 1.24 × 10^-6, and the ratio of gravitational wave energies is Delta E_GW^BI / Delta E_GW^Ma ≲ 1.08.
- For a larger flare energy E_fl = 5 × 10^47 erg, the Maxwellian toroidal field is B_phi^Ma = 1.7 × 10^15 G, giving |epsilon_ul| ≈ 7.4 × 10^-6 and Delta E_GW^BI / Delta E_GW^Ma ≲ 1.53.
- For a Maxwellian toroidal field of 10^15 G, Delta E_GW^BI / Delta E_GW^Ma ≲ 1.14 and |epsilon_ul| ≈ 2 × 10^-6.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Pereira et al. (2018) on nonlinear electrodynamics in magnetars, specifically focusing on the Born-Infeld Lagrangian as a low-energy limit of string theory. The authors note that ATLAS experiments (2017) left a gap in probing the Born-Infeld scale field b, and that magnetar fields could fill this gap. The results suggest that future gravitational wave detectors could constrain nonlinear electrodynamics, complementing Earth-based particle accelerator experiments. The analysis is presented as first-order estimates, with the authors noting that realistic systems with both poloidal and toroidal fields would be more complex.
