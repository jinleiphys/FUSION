# Medium-induced parton splitting kernels from Soft Collinear Effective Theory with Glauber gluons

> We derive the splitting kernels for partons produced in large $Q^2$ scattering processes that subsequently traverse a region of strongly-interacting matter using a recently-developed effective theory \SCETG. We include all corrections beyond the small-$x$ approximation, consistent with the power counting of \SCETG. We demonstrate how medium recoil, geometry and expansion scenarios, and phase space cuts can be implemented numerically for phenomenological applications. For the simplified case of infinite transverse momentum kinematics and a uniform medium, we provide closed-form analytic results that can be used to validate the numerical simulations.

# Medium-induced parton splitting kernels from Soft Collinear Effective Theory with Glauber gluons

## Key claim

The authors derive complete medium-induced parton splitting kernels for all four branching processes (q→qg, g→gg, g→q¯q, q→gq) using SCET with Glauber gluons (SCET_G), including corrections beyond the small-x approximation that are consistent with the effective theory's power counting. The results are valid for arbitrary splitting parameter x and include contributions from in-medium parton scattering and their dominant interference with vacuum-like branching processes, but the analytic closed-form results are restricted to the simplified case of infinite transverse momentum kinematics and a uniform medium.

## Method

The authors use the SCET_G effective theory, which couples collinear SCET fields to Glauber gluons that mediate interactions with the QCD medium, to compute amplitudes for parton splitting processes at first order in opacity. They evaluate Feynman diagrams corresponding to single Glauber gluon exchange between the jet constituents and the medium, including both the splitting induced by interactions along the parent parton trajectory and the dominant interference with the splitting induced by the hard scattering process. The calculations are performed in the hybrid gauge (light-cone gauge for collinear gluons, covariant gauge for Glauber gluons), and the results are expressed in terms of universal transverse momentum vectors and interference phases.

## Key numbers

- For numerical studies, the medium parameters are set to: μ = 0.75 GeV (typical inverse range of parton scattering), λ_g = 1 fm (gluon mean free path), L = 5 fm (medium size), and parent parton energy E_0 = 100 GeV
- For intermediate x ∼ 0.5, the deviation between full in-medium splittings and the small-x approximation is on the order of a factor of 2
- The kinematic cut on q_⊥ can lead to a factor of 2 variation of the in-medium parton splitting intensities at intermediate x
- The function f[x] used in analytic formulas contains terms including γ_E (Euler-Mascheroni constant), sin(x), cos(x), and the special functions Ci(x) and Si(x)

## Context

This work extends the SCET_G framework developed by Ovanesyan and Vitev (2011) for quark jet broadening and the q→qg splitting, completing the derivation of all medium-induced branching processes without the assumption of a soft final-state parton. The results connect to the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev approach (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev, 2000-2007) for inelastic parton interactions in dense QCD matter, recovering the soft gluon emission results in the small-x limit. The full results provide a basis for improving jet quenching phenomenology by including finite x corrections, new splittings from coherent final-state interactions, exact parton recoil kinematics, and exact phase space cuts, all of which can be of the same order at high energies.
