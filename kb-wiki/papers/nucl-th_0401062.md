# Quasi-elastic knockout of pions and kaons from nucleons by high-energy electrons and quark microscopy of "soft" meson degrees of freedom in the nucleon

> Electro-production of pions and kaons at the kinematics of quasi-elastic knockout (which is well known in the physics of atomic nucleus and corresponds to the $t$-pole diagram) is proposed for obtaining their momentum distribution (MD) in various channels of virtual decay $N \to B+π$, $B=N$, $Δ$, $N^*$, $N^{**}$, and $N \to Y+K$, $Y=Λ$, $Σ$. It is a powerful tool for investigation of a quark microscopic picture of the meson cloud in the nucleon. A model of scalar $q \bar{q}$ ($^3P_0$) fluctuation in the non-trivial QCD vacuum is used to calculate pion and kaon momentum distributions (MD) in these channels.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that quasi-elastic knockout of pions and kaons from nucleons by high-energy electrons, at momentum transfers Q² ~ 1-3 (GeV/c)², can directly measure the momentum distributions of these mesons in the nucleon, and that these distributions can be calculated from a quark microscopic model of scalar q\bar{q} (³P₀) fluctuations in the QCD vacuum, though the relation of such phenomenological ³P₀ models to first-principles QCD has not been clearly established.

## Method

The authors analyze exclusive pion electro-production p(e,e'π⁺)n within the pole approximation, using the longitudinal cross section dσ_L/dt to extract the pion momentum distribution in the nucleon. They calculate the momentum distributions and spectroscopic factors for virtual decay channels p → B+π (B = N, Δ, N*(1/2⁻,3/2⁻), N**(1/2⁺ Roper)) and p → Y+K (Y = Λ, Σ) using a ³P₀ scalar fluctuation model with a Hamiltonian that creates q\bar{q} pairs, employing non-relativistic constituent quark model wave functions and recoupling of quark coordinates via fractional-parentage techniques.

## Key numbers

- The quasi-elastic knockout kinematics requires Q² ~ 1-3 (GeV/c)² for the t-pole and Z-diagram mechanisms to dominate.
- The pion electromagnetic form factor is parametrized as F_{ππγ}(Q²) = [1 + (Q²/0.54 (GeV/c)²)]⁻¹, corresponding to a charge pion radius [⟨r_π²⟩_{ch}]^{1/2} = 0.656 fm.
- The pion quark radius is taken as b_π ≈ 0.3 fm.
- The Q² values for the analyzed experimental data range from 0.7 to 3.3 (GeV/c)².
- The paper discusses the frontier problem of quark asymptotic degrees of freedom at Q² = 10-20 (GeV/c)², but focuses on the "soft" hadron degrees at Q² = 2-4 (GeV/c)².

## Context

This work extends the quasi-elastic knockout method, well-established in nuclear physics for probing nucleon and cluster momentum distributions in nuclei (e.g., Neudatchin et al. (1990s) on cluster knockout), to the investigation of meson clouds in the nucleon. It builds on pioneering analyses of exclusive pion electroproduction by authors such as those in Refs. [b8,b9] (2000s), who reconstructed the pion momentum distribution in the channel p → n+π⁺, but did not discuss suppression of non-pole diagrams. The paper enables future experiments at JLab to measure momentum distributions and spectroscopic factors for multiple virtual decay channels of the nucleon, including kaonic channels, using the ³P₀ model predictions as a microscopic benchmark.
