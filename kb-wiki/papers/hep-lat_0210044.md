# Neutron Electric Dipole Moment on the Lattice: a Theoretical Reappraisal

> We present a strategy for a lattice evaluation of the neutron electric dipole moment induced by the strong CP violating term of the QCD Lagrangian. Our strategy is based on the standard definition of the electric dipole moment, involving the charge density operator J0, in case of three flavors with non-degenerate masses. We present a complete diagrammatic analysis showing how the axial chiral Ward identities can be used to replace the opological charge operator with the flavor-singlet pseudoscalar density PS. Our final result is characterized only by disconnected diagrams, where the disconnected part can be either the single insertion of PS or the separate insertions of both PS and J0. The applicability of our strategy to the case of lattice formulations that explicitly break chiral symmetry, like the Wilson and Clover actions, is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a strategy for lattice QCD evaluation of the neutron electric dipole moment (EDM) induced by the strong CP-violating theta term, showing that the topological charge operator can be replaced by disconnected insertions of the flavor-singlet pseudoscalar density, but only when the axial chiral Ward identities are properly handled for three flavors with non-degenerate masses.

## Method

The authors start from the standard definition of the neutron EDM involving the charge density operator J0 and treat the QCD theta term as a first-order perturbation. Using the flavor-singlet and non-singlet axial Ward identities, they derive a master equation that replaces the topological charge operator G-tilde-G with the flavor-singlet pseudoscalar density PS, combined with chiral variation terms. A complete diagrammatic analysis using the FORM package is performed for all Wick contractions, and the non-singlet axial Ward identity is used to relate diagrams with different numbers of propagators, ultimately showing that only disconnected diagrams of types (a) and (b) survive in the final expression.

## Key numbers

* Experimental upper limit on neutron EDM: dN < 6.3 x 10^(-26) e·cm at 90% confidence level
* Theoretical estimate from bag model and chiral Lagrangian: dN ≈ 3 x 10^(-16) |θ| e·cm, leading to the bound |θ| ≲ 2 x 10^(-10)
* The final result is proportional to the average quark mass mbar, defined as mbar^(-1) = (mu^(-1) + md^(-1) + ms^(-1))/3 for three flavors

## Context

This work extends the earlier lattice attempt by Aoki and Gocksch (1989) which found a non-zero neutron EDM from connected diagrams, later shown by Aoki (1990) to be an O(a) lattice artifact. The present paper provides a more careful diagrammatic analysis than Aoki (1990), showing that connected diagrams indeed do not contribute, but also deriving the correct disconnected diagram structure when the charge density operator J0 is included. The strategy enables future lattice QCD calculations of the neutron EDM using standard three-point function techniques rather than the background electric field method, and is applicable to Wilson and Clover fermion formulations that break chiral symmetry explicitly.
