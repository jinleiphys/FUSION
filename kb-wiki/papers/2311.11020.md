# Production of Protons and Light Nuclei in Au+Au Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}$ = 3 GeV with the STAR Detector

> We report the systematic measurement of protons and light nuclei production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}$ = 3 GeV by the STAR experiment at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). The transverse momentum ($p_{T}$) spectra of protons ($p$), deuterons ($d$), tritons ($t$), $^{3}\mathrm{He}$, and $^{4}\mathrm{He}$ are measured from mid-rapidity to target rapidity for different collision centralities. We present the rapidity and centrality dependence of particle yields ($dN/dy$), average transverse momentum ($\langle p_{T}\rangle$), yield ratios ($d/p$, $t/p$,$^{3}\mathrm{He}/p$, $^{4}\mathrm{He}/p$), as well as the coalescence parameters ($B_2$, $B_3$). The 4$π$ yields for various particles are determined by utilizing the measured rapidity distributions, $dN/dy$. Furthermore, we present the energy, centrality, and rapidity dependence of the compound yield ratios ($N_{p} \times N_{t} / N_{d}^{2}$) and compare them with various model calculations. The physics implications of those results on the production mechanism of light nuclei and on QCD phase structure are discussed.

## Key claim
The paper reports that at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 3\) GeV, the compound yield ratio \(N_p \times N_t / N_d^2\) shows a monotonic decrease with increasing charged-particle multiplicity, independent of collision energy and centrality, consistent with coalescence models; however, the basic transport models (JAM, SMASH, UrQMD) cannot describe the experimental observations at this energy, implying that the production mechanism of light nuclei and the QCD phase structure at high baryon density require further theoretical refinement.

## Method
The STAR experiment measured transverse momentum (\(p_T\)) spectra of protons, deuterons, tritons, \(^3\)He, and \(^4\)He in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 3\) GeV using the fixed-target mode at RHIC. Particle identification was performed with the Time Projection Chamber (TPC) and Time-of-Flight (TOF) detectors, covering rapidity from mid-rapidity to target rapidity (\(-1.0 < y < 0\)) for four centrality bins (0-10%, 10-20%, 20-40%, 40-80%). Proton yields were corrected for weak-decay feed-down from hyperons (\(\Lambda\), \(\Sigma^+\), \(\Xi^-\)), while light nuclei yields are inclusive (no feed-down correction from excited states). The \(p_T\) spectra were fitted with a blast-wave model to extract \(dN/dy\) and \(\langle p_T \rangle\), and the \(4\pi\) yields were obtained by integrating the rapidity distributions.

## Key numbers
- The weak decay feed-down fraction of protons at mid-rapidity (\(-0.1 < y < 0\)) in 0-10% central collisions is \(1.838 \pm 0.017 \pm 0.214\)%, with the maximum contribution about 2% across all centralities and rapidity bins.
- The mean number of participating nucleons \(\langle N_{\mathrm{Part}}\rangle\) for the 0-10% centrality bin is \(310.7 \pm 0.1 \pm 8.3\).
- The systematic uncertainty for the compound yield ratio \(N_p \times N_t / N_d^2\) is about 2-15% for different rapidity and centrality bins, increasing to 25% when including \(^3\)He and \(^4\)He.
- The total systematic uncertainties for \(dN/dy\) are 6-8% for protons, 6-12% for deuterons, 6-11% for tritons, 7-11% for \(^3\)He, and 6-20% for \(^4\)He.
- The total systematic uncertainties for \(\langle p_T \rangle\) are 1-3% for protons, 3-9% for deuterons, 5-13% for tritons, 3-12% for \(^3\)He, and 3-12% for \(^4\)He.

## Context
This work extends the STAR Beam Energy Scan program to the lowest energy (\(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 3\) GeV) achieved via fixed-target mode, complementing previous measurements at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 7.7\)–200 GeV from STAR (2019, 2023). The observed enhancement in \(N_p \times N_t / N_d^2\) at 19.6 and 27 GeV (4.1\(\sigma\) significance) motivated this systematic study at higher baryon density (\(\mu_B\) up to \(\sim\)750 MeV). The results provide data to test coalescence models (e.g., Sun 2018, Shuryak 2019) and transport models (JAM, SMASH, UrQMD, PHQMD), enabling future searches for first-order phase transitions and the QCD critical point at high baryon density.
