# Wigner-Seitz cells in neutron star crust with finite range interactions

> The structure of Wigner-Seitz cells in the inner crust of neutron stars is investigated using a microcospic Hartree-Fock-BCS approach with finite range D1S and M3Y-P4 interactions. Large effects on the densities are found compared to previous predictions using Skyrme interactions. Pairing effects are found to be small, and they are attenuated by the use of finite range interactions in the mean field.

# Wigner-Seitz cells in neutron star crust with finite range interactions

## Key claim
The use of finite-range interactions (D1S and M3Y-P4) in Hartree-Fock-BCS calculations of neutron star inner crust Wigner-Seitz cells produces large differences in density profiles compared to previous Skyrme (SLy4) results, but pairing effects remain small and are further attenuated by the finite-range interactions in the mean field channel.

## Method
The authors solved the Hartree-Fock equations in a spherical Bessel function basis with Dirichlet-Neumann mixed boundary conditions for 11 representative Wigner-Seitz cells from Negele and Vautherin (1973), using finite-range D1S and M3Y-P4 interactions in the mean field channel. Pairing correlations were included via the BCS approximation using three different pairing interactions: the D1S and M3Y-P4 finite-range forces, and a zero-range density-dependent delta force with parameters from N. Chamel (2004).

## Key numbers
- The 11 Wigner-Seitz cells cover baryonic densities from 1.743 × 10⁻³ ρ₀ to 0.5 ρ₀, where ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ is nuclear matter saturation density
- Cell radii range from 53.6 fm (lowest density cell, ¹⁸⁰Zr) to 14.4 fm (highest density cell, ⁹⁸²Ge)
- For the ¹⁸⁰Zr cell, neutron density at the center with D1S and M3Y-P4 interactions is smaller by a factor of 2.3 than with SLy4 interaction
- The neutron gas density for ¹⁸⁰Zr with D1S and M3Y-P4 is more than 1.8 times greater than with SLy4
- For cells ¹³⁵⁰Sn, ¹⁸⁰⁰Sn, and ¹⁵⁰⁰Zr, the difference between HF and HF-BCS neutron densities at r=0 is around 10-11%
- The D1S Gogny interaction gives a maximum pairing gap in infinite neutron matter of about 2.4 MeV at Fermi momentum k_F ≈ 0.8 fm⁻¹
- The M3Y-P4 interaction gives a maximum pairing gap of about 3.2 MeV at similar Fermi momentum

## Context
This work extends the microscopic calculations of Wigner-Seitz cells originally performed by Negele and Vautherin (1973) using Skyrme interactions, and later studies by N. Chamel (2004) and M. Baldo et al. (2005) using energy functional methods. The authors note that no previous study had used finite-range interactions such as Gogny D1S or M3Y-P4 for the inner crust. The results enable future redetermination of the optimal (N,Z) numbers for each cell using finite-range interactions, and suggest that the highest-density cell (⁹⁸²Ge) likely belongs to the deformed pasta phase.
