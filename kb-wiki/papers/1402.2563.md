# Ab-initio coupled-cluster effective interactions for the shell model: Application to neutron-rich oxygen and carbon isotopes

> We derive and compute effective valence-space shell-model interactions from ab-initio coupled-cluster theory and apply them to open-shell and neutron-rich oxygen and carbon isotopes. Our shell-model interactions are based on nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon forces from chiral effective-field theory. We compute the energies of ground and low-lying states, and find good agreement with experiment. In particular our calculations are consistent with the N=14, 16 shell closures in oxygen-22 and oxygen-24, while for carbon-20 the corresponding N=14 closure is weaker. We find good agreement between our coupled-cluster effective-interaction results with those obtained from standard single-reference coupled-cluster calculations for up to eight valence neutrons.

## Key claim

The paper derives effective valence-space shell-model interactions from ab-initio coupled-cluster theory, finding good agreement with experimental ground and excited-state energies for neutron-rich oxygen and carbon isotopes. However, the CCEI results for carbon isotopes are systematically under-bound by 2.3–4.2 MeV relative to experiment, and the approach neglects induced three-body interactions that become significant when the number of valence neutrons exceeds about four.

## Method

The authors start from chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (3N) forces, evolve them via similarity renormalization group to a lower momentum scale, and solve the A-body problem using coupled-cluster singles-and-doubles with perturbative triples (Λ-CCSD(T)). They then construct a valence-space shell-model Hamiltonian by applying the Okubo-Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation to one- and two-particle-attached equation-of-motion coupled-cluster eigenstates, projecting onto the sd valence space (d5/2, s1/2, d3/2) above a ¹⁶O core for oxygen or a ¹⁴C core for carbon. The resulting non-Hermitian effective Hamiltonian is symmetrized via the metric operator to obtain a Hermitian shell-model interaction.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state energy of ²⁴O from CCEI is less bound by about 3.5 MeV compared to the Λ-CCSD(T) reference value, indicating the onset of neglected induced three-body interactions.
* In ²²O, the CCEI 2⁺ state lies at 2.7 MeV excitation energy (converged to within ~5 keV), while the 3⁺ state is at 4.0 MeV (not yet converged, moving down by ~150 keV when increasing model space from N=10 to N=12 oscillator shells).
* The CCEI 2⁺ energy in ²⁰C is 1.72 MeV, compared to 2.78 MeV in ²²O, consistent with a weaker N=14 sub-shell closure in carbon.
* Λ-CCSD(T) ground-state energies for ¹⁴C, ¹⁵C, and ¹⁶C are −104.0 MeV, −104.2 MeV, and −106.6 MeV respectively; the ¹⁴C value agrees well with the experimental −105.3 MeV, but ¹⁵C and ¹⁶C are under-bound by 2.3 MeV and 4.2 MeV.
* CCEI results are converged to within approximately 100 keV for the oxygen isotopes.

## Context

This work extends nonperturbative ab-initio shell-model derivations beyond the no-core shell model valence-cluster expansion (Navratil 1997, Lisetskiy 2008, Shukla 2011) and the in-medium similarity renormalization group (Tsukiyama 2012), applying coupled-cluster theory to construct effective interactions for open-shell nuclei. It confirms the pivotal role of three-nucleon forces in reproducing the N=14 and N=16 shell closures in oxygen, as previously shown by Otsuka (2010) and Holt (2012), and provides the first ab-initio shell-model description of neutron-rich carbon isotopes. The approach enables systematic improvements by including induced three-body interactions and extending to heavier nuclei where phenomenological Hamiltonians are less reliable, and was very recently complemented by the in-medium SRG method of Bogner (2014).
