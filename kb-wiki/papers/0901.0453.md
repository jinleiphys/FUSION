# Charge radii and electromagnetic moments of Li and Be isotopes from the ab initio no-core shell model

> Recently, charge radii and ground-state electromagnetic moments of Li and Be isotopes were measured precisely. We have performed large-scale ab initio no-core shell model calculations for these isotopes using high-precision nucleon-nucleon potentials. The isotopic trends of our computed charge radii and quadrupole and magnetic-dipole moments are in good agreement with experimental results with the exception of the 11Li charge radius. The magnetic moments are in particular well described, whereas the absolute magnitudes of the quadrupole moments are about 10% too small. The small magnitude of the 6Li quadrupole moment is reproduced, and with the CD-Bonn NN potential, also its correct sign.

## Key claim

The ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations using high-precision nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials reproduce the isotopic trends of charge radii, quadrupole moments, and magnetic dipole moments for Li and Be isotopes, with the exception of the 11Li charge radius; however, the absolute magnitudes of the computed quadrupole moments are consistently about 10% too small compared to experiment, and the 6Li quadrupole moment sign depends on which NN potential is used.

## Method

The authors solved the A-body problem using the ab initio NCSM with a harmonic-oscillator (HO) basis truncated by a maximal total HO energy (Nmax). They employed two high-precision NN potentials: the CD-Bonn 2000 (CDB) interaction and the INOY IS-M (INOY) interaction, using a two-body cluster approximation for the effective Hamiltonian. Ground-state observables were computed in large model spaces (Nmax up to 16 for A=6, down to 10 for A=11) with optimal HO frequencies selected by minimizing the dependence on the HO parameter, and convergence was estimated by varying both model-space size and HO frequency.

## Key numbers

- 6Li quadrupole moment: CDB gives -0.066(40) e fm^2, INOY gives +0.080(19) e fm^2, experiment is -0.0806(6) e fm^2
- 7Li quadrupole moment: CDB gives -3.20(22) e fm^2, INOY gives -2.79(17) e fm^2, experiment is -4.00(3) e fm^2
- 11Li quadrupole moment: CDB gives -2.81(27) e fm^2, INOY gives -2.32(13) e fm^2, experiment is -3.33(5) e fm^2
- 6Li magnetic dipole moment: CDB gives +0.843(5) mu_N, INOY gives +0.843(2) mu_N, experiment is +0.822 mu_N
- 11Li magnetic dipole moment: CDB gives +3.56(4) mu_N, INOY gives +3.54(4) mu_N, experiment is +3.671(1) mu_N
- 6Li charge radius: CDB gives 2.40(6) fm, INOY gives 2.29(4) fm, experiment is 2.540(28) fm
- 11Li charge radius: CDB gives 2.26(13) fm, INOY gives 2.06(6) fm, experiment is 2.426(34) fm
- 9Be quadrupole moment: CDB gives +4.12(26) e fm^2, INOY gives +3.67(23) e fm^2, experiment is +5.288(38) e fm^2
- 11Be magnetic dipole moment: CDB gives -1.55(6) mu_N, INOY gives -1.47(3) mu_N, experiment is -1.6813(5) mu_N

## Context

This work extends earlier NCSM studies by Navratil (2006) and Forssen (2005) to larger model spaces and compares two very different NN potentials (CD-Bonn 2000 and INOY IS-M) for Li and Be isotopes up to A=11. The calculations show that the INOY interaction produces anomalously high nuclear densities (as previously found in 4He calculations by Caurier (2006) and Lazauskas (2004)), leading to charge radii that are too small, while the CDB interaction gives better agreement with experiment except for the 11Li halo ground state. The results enable future work on extending the HO basis to describe dilute halo structures and on including three-body forces or effective operators to improve the quadrupole moment predictions.
