# Effects of momentum conservation on the analysis of anisotropic flow

> We present a general method for taking into account correlations due to momentum conservation in the analysis of anisotropic flow, either by using the two-particle correlation method or the standard flow vector method. In the latter, the correlation between the particle and the flow vector is either corrected through a redefinition (shift) of the flow vector, or subtracted explicitly from the observed flow coefficient. In addition, momentum conservation contributes to the reaction plane resolution. Momentum conservation mostly affects the first harmonic in azimuthal distributions, i.e., directed flow. It also modifies higher harmonics, for instance elliptic flow, when they are measured with respect to a first harmonic event plane such as one determined with the standard transverse momentum method. Our method is illustrated by application to NA49 data on pion directed flow.

# Effects of momentum conservation on the analysis of anisotropic flow

## Key claim

Momentum conservation induces azimuthal correlations between particles that can mimic or distort true anisotropic flow signals; these correlations can be corrected either by redefining the flow vector event-by-event or by explicitly subtracting the momentum-conservation contribution, but the correction depends on a dimensionless parameter \(f\) that scales as the square root of the fraction of detected particles, and the correction vanishes only when the detector acceptance is symmetric about midrapidity.

## Method

The authors derived analytic expressions for the correlation due to momentum conservation in both the two-particle correlation method and the flow-vector (event plane) method. For the flow-vector method, they showed that the correlation between a particle and the flow vector gains an additive term proportional to the particle's transverse momentum and the parameter \(f\), and that the event plane resolution is also modified. They provided corrected formulas for extracting the resolution parameter \(\chi\) from subevent correlations, including an exact formula for the fraction of events with subevent angle difference greater than \(\pi/2\) and a leading-order expression for \(\langle\cos(\Phi_a-\Phi_b)\rangle\). The method was applied to NA49 data on pion directed flow in Pb+Pb collisions at 158 GeV per nucleon.

## Key numbers

- The two-particle correlation due to momentum conservation is \(\langle\cos(\phi_1-\phi_2)\rangle_{\rm mom.\ cons.} = -{p_T}_1{p_T}_2 / (N\langle p_T^2\rangle)\), where \(N\) is the total number of produced particles.
- The order-of-magnitude estimate for the correlation between a particle and the flow vector due to momentum conservation is \(\sim -(p_T/\sqrt{N\langle p_T^2\rangle})\,f\), where \(f \equiv \langle w p_T\rangle / \sqrt{\langle w^2\rangle\langle p_T^2\rangle}\).
- The exact expression for that correlation is \(\langle\cos(\phi-\Phi)\rangle_{\rm mom.\ cons.} = -(p_T/\sqrt{N\langle p_T^2\rangle})\, (f/\sqrt{1-f^2})\, (\sqrt{\pi}/2)\, e^{-\chi^2/2} I_0(\chi^2/2)\).
- The corrected formula for the fraction of events with subevent angle difference \(|\Phi_a-\Phi_b| > \pi/2\) is \(P(|\Phi_a-\Phi_b|>\pi/2) = e^{-\chi_{\rm sub}^2} / (2-f^2)\).
- The relation between the full event resolution parameter \(\chi\) and the subevent parameter \(\chi_{\rm sub}\) is \(\chi = \chi_{\rm sub}\sqrt{(2-f^2)/(1-f^2)}\).
- For high resolution (\(\chi_{\rm sub} \gg 1\)), the width of the subevent angle distribution is \(\langle(\Phi_a-\Phi_b)^2\rangle = 2/[\chi_{\rm sub}^2 (2-f^2)]\).
- In the weak-correlation limit, the corrected resolution parameter satisfies \(\chi^2({\rm corrected}) = \chi^2({\rm uncorrected}) + f^2\).

## Context

This work extends the standard anisotropic flow analysis framework of Danielewicz and Odyniec (1985) and Poskanzer and Voloshin (1998) by providing a general, analytic treatment of momentum conservation effects that had previously been addressed only with approximate or case-specific corrections (Danielewicz 1988; Ogilvie 1990). The paper enables systematic correction of directed flow measurements at all collision energies, and shows that momentum conservation also biases higher harmonics (e.g., elliptic flow) when measured relative to a first-harmonic event plane. The method was illustrated on NA49 data (Poskanzer 2001) and provides the foundation for correcting flow analyses at RHIC and LHC where detector acceptance is often asymmetric.
