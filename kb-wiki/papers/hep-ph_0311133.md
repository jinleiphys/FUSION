# $ππ$ Invariant mass spectrum in $V'\to V~ππ$ and the $f_0(600)$ pole

> We consider the phenomenological description of the two pion invariant mass spectrum in the $V' \to V ππ$ decays. We study the parametrization of the amplitude involving both $S$ and $D$ wave contributions. From a fit to the two pion decays of the $ Υ(nS)$ and $Ψ(nS)$ we determine the $f_0 (600)$ mass and width to be $m_{f_0}=528 \pm 32$ MeV and $Γ_{f_0}=413\pm 45$ MeV. The mass and width values we report correspond to the real and imaginary part of the S matrix pole respectively.

## Key claim

From a joint fit to the di-pion invariant mass spectra of four heavy quarkonium decays (Upsilon and Psi families), the authors determine the f0(600) pole mass and width, but note that the fit is insensitive to the coupling constants of the resonance, so the pole position is obtained directly from a simplified Breit-Wigner parametrization rather than from a full self-energy calculation.

## Method

The authors perform a phenomenological analysis of the decays V' to V pi pi (V' = Upsilon(3S), Upsilon(2S), Psi(2S); V = Upsilon(1S), Upsilon(2S), J/Psi) using Lorentz-covariant amplitudes that include both S-wave and D-wave contributions for the di-pion system. The S-wave form factor is parametrized as a Breit-Wigner pole (representing the f0(600)) plus a soft background, while the D-wave is described by a purely soft background; a joint fit to 165 data points from four decay channels is performed with 33 parameters, yielding the pole mass and width directly.

## Key numbers

* f0(600) pole mass: m_p = 528 +/- 32 MeV, from a joint fit to Upsilon and Psi decays.
* f0(600) pole width: Gamma_p = 413 +/- 45 MeV, from the same joint fit.
* Chi-squared per degree of freedom for the joint fit: chi^2_d.o.f. = 1.12.
* Number of data points in the fit: 165, from four decay channels (Upsilon(3S) to Upsilon(1S) pi pi, Upsilon(3S) to Upsilon(2S) pi pi, Upsilon(2S) to Upsilon(1S) pi pi, and Psi(2S) to J/Psi pi pi).
* Chi^2_d.o.f. per process: Upsilon(2S) to Upsilon(1S) pi pi = 0.74 (48 data points); Upsilon(3S) to Upsilon(1S) pi pi = 1.3 (40 data points); Upsilon(3S) to Upsilon(2S) pi pi = 1.1 (32 data points); Psi(2S) to J/Psi pi pi = 1.5 (45 data points).

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of heavy quarkonium decays into pion pairs, which had been described using the scale anomaly (Voloshin, Novikov) or a Breit-Wigner parametrization (Komada). The authors show that a fit using only the scale anomaly yields chi^2_d.o.f. > 2, and that including the f0(600) pole with a strong interference between pole and background is required to describe the full dataset. The results are consistent with a light and broad f0(600) found in other analyses (Cola, Gray, Kamisnsky) and with the mass m=526^{+48}_{-37} MeV and width Gamma=301^{+145}_{-100} MeV reported by Komada, though the width obtained here is larger. The analysis enables future studies of the interplay between the f0(600) resonance and the scale anomaly in heavy quarkonium decays.
