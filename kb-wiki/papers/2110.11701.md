# Multi-Particle Tunneling Transport at Strongly-Correlated Interfaces

> We elucidate the multi-particle transport of pair- and spin-tunnelings in strongly correlated interfaces. Not only usual single-particle tunneling but also interaction-induced multi-particle tunneling processes naturally arise from a conventional microscopic model without any empirical parameters, through the overlap of the many-body wave functions around the interface. We demonstrate how anomalous tunneling currents occur in a strongly interacting system due to the pair-tunneling process which we derived microscopically. Our formulation is useful for junction systems in various disciplines, including atomtronics, spintronics, and nuclear reactions.

# Multi-Particle Tunneling Transport at Strongly-Correlated Interfaces

## Key claim

The authors derive a microscopic tunneling Hamiltonian for strongly correlated interfaces from a conventional contact-interaction model without empirical parameters, showing that interaction-induced pair-tunneling and spin-exchange tunneling arise naturally from the overlap of many-body wave functions around the interface. The claim is hedged by the assumption that the one-body potential barrier is weak (transmission coefficients are small) and that the long-wavelength limit for transmitted waves applies.

## Method

The authors start from a non-relativistic spin-1/2 Fermi gas with contact-type interaction and a potential barrier, then decompose the field operators into left and right reservoir components based on asymptotic scattering wave functions. Using the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism with truncation of tunnel couplings to second order, they derive tunneling current formulas for mass and spin transport in terms of retarded Green's functions and susceptibilities. The pair-tunneling and spin-tunneling coupling constants are expressed in terms of the transmission coefficients \(B_{\bm{0},\sigma}\) from the one-body scattering problem.

## Key numbers

- The pair-tunneling coupling constant is \(\mathcal{T}_{2,{\rm pair}} = 2g \, \mathrm{Re}[B_{\bm{0},\uparrow} B_{\bm{0},\downarrow}]\), where \(g\) is the two-body coupling constant and \(B_{\bm{0},\sigma}\) are transmission coefficients in the long-wavelength limit.
- The spin-tunneling coupling constant is \(\mathcal{T}_{2,{\rm spin}} = 2g \, \mathrm{Re}[B_{\bm{0},\uparrow}^* B_{\bm{0},\downarrow}]\).
- The induced interface interaction coupling is \(\mathcal{T}_{2,{\rm ind.}} = g [B_{\bm{0},\uparrow}^* B_{\bm{0},\uparrow} + B_{\bm{0},\downarrow}^* B_{\bm{0},\downarrow}]\).
- The molecular BEC critical temperature is \(T_{\rm c} \simeq 0.218 T_{\rm F}\), where \(T_{\rm F} = k_{\rm F}^2/(2m)\) is the Fermi temperature of an ideal Fermi gas at zero temperature.
- The ratio \(\mathcal{G}/\mathcal{S}\) (pair-tunneling mass conductance over Seebeck coefficient) shows a dramatic enhancement near \(T_{\rm c}\) in the strong-coupling limit (\(a^{-1} \rightarrow \infty\)).

## Context

This work extends the microscopic understanding of tunneling transport beyond the single-particle tunneling Hamiltonian with empirical parameters used in previous cold-atom studies such as Uchino (2017), Liu (2017), Yao (2018), Han (2019), Furutani (2020), and Sekino (2020). The derived pair-tunneling term connects to multi-neutron transfer in nuclear systems discussed by Potel (review) and Scamps, while the spin-tunneling term connects to magnonic spin transport in normal-metal-ferromagnet junctions studied by Matsuo (2018) and others in the spintronics community. The formulation enables a unified description of tunneling phenomena across atomtronics, spintronics, and nuclear reactions, and can be extended to long-range and three-body interactions.
