# Influence of Dark Matter on the Magnetized Neutron Star

> Over the past two decades, significant strides have been made in the study of Dark Matter (DM) admixed neutron stars and their associated properties. However, an intriguing facet regarding the effect of DM on magnetized neutron stars still remains unexplored. This study is carried out to analyze the properties of DM admixed magnetized neutron stars. The equation of state for the DM admixed neutron star is calculated using the relativistic mean-field model with the inclusion of a density-dependent magnetic field. Several macroscopic properties, such as mass, radius, particle fractions, tidal deformability, and the $f$-mode frequency, are calculated with different magnetic field strengths and DM configurations. The equation of state is softer with the presence of DM as well as for the parallel components of the magnetic field and vice-versa for the perpendicular one. Other macroscopic properties, such as mass, radius, tidal deformability, etc., are also affected by both DM and magnetic fields. The change in the magnitude of different neutron star observables is proportional to the amount of DM percentage and the strength of the magnetic field. We observe that the change is seen mainly in the core part of the star without affecting the crustal properties.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the combined effects of dark matter (DM) and a strong magnetic field (MF) on neutron star properties are competitive: DM softens the equation of state and reduces mass and radius, while the magnetic field produces anisotropic pressure that stiffens or softens the EOS depending on the pressure component (perpendicular or parallel). The authors hedge that for MF strengths up to \(10^{18}\) G, the deformation from spherical symmetry is less than 1%, justifying their use of spherically symmetric TOV equations as a preliminary assumption.

## Method

The authors used the effective relativistic mean-field (E-RMF) model with two parameter sets (BigApple and IOPB-I) to describe nuclear matter, coupled with a single-fluid fermionic dark matter model where Neutralino DM particles interact with nucleons via Higgs exchange. A density-dependent magnetic field parametrized from the surface (\(10^{15}\) G) to the core (up to \(10^{19}\) G) was included, with charged particle energies quantized into Landau levels. The equation of state was computed under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality, and the TOV equations were solved separately for parallel and perpendicular pressure components to obtain mass-radius relations, tidal deformability, and f-mode frequencies.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass without DM or MF: \(2.60 M_\odot\) (BigApple) and \(2.15 M_\odot\) (IOPB-I), with corresponding radii of 12.41 km and 11.91 km respectively.
- DM Fermi momenta varied: 0.00, 0.02, and 0.04 GeV, corresponding to DM number density \(\rho_{\rm DM} \sim 0.16 \times 10^{-3}\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Magnetic field range considered: \(10^{17}\) to \(10^{19}\) G at the core, with surface field \(B_{\rm surf} = 10^{15}\) G.
- Maximum mass decreases by approximately 4-5% with inclusion of DM and/or MF.
- For BigApple with \(k_f^{\rm DM} = 0.04\) GeV, the canonical radius does not satisfy NICER constraints; for IOPB-I with the same DM momentum, none of the observational constraints are satisfied.
- Tidal deformability \(\Lambda_{1.4}\) is insensitive to magnetic field for both EOSs, but changes considerably at maximum mass depending on MF strength.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of DM-admixed neutron stars (Panotopoulos 2017, Das 2019, 2021, 2022) and magnetized neutron stars (Broderick 2000, Strickland 2012, Patra 2020) by combining both effects in a unified hadronic model. It builds on the E-RMF formalism previously applied to finite nuclei and neutron stars (Kumar 2018, Fattoyev 2020) and uses the BigApple parameter set designed to accommodate the \(2.6 M_\odot\) secondary component of GW190814. The results enable future constraints on DM fraction and magnetic field strength in neutron stars by comparing with observational data from pulsars (PSR J0740+6620), NICER, and gravitational wave events (GW170817, GW190814).
