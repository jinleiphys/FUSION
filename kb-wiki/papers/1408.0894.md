# Properties and significance of the surface dipole mode

> A strong isoscalar dipole resonance is known to be excited in a variety of nuclei, including isospin symmetric ones, at approximately 6-7 MeV. A series of theoretical studies and accumulating experimental evidence support an interpretation of the above dipole resonance as an elementary surface vibration. Obviously, such a mode is potentially as interesting as any collective excitation for a variety of reasons. In addition, though, it is found to account for the observed isoscalar segment of pygmy dipole strength. As discussed here, this has important implications for pygmy-strength interpretations and searches for genuine neutron-skin oscillations.

# Properties and significance of the surface dipole mode

## Key claim
The strong isoscalar dipole resonance observed at approximately 6–7 MeV in a variety of nuclei, including isospin-symmetric ones, is interpreted as an elementary surface vibration rather than a neutron-skin oscillation; this mode accounts for the isoscalar segment of pygmy dipole strength, which has important implications for interpreting pygmy-strength data and for searches for genuine neutron-skin oscillations.

## Method
The paper surveys experimental data on low-energy isoscalar dipole transitions compiled from multiple nuclei (12C, 16O, 40Ca, 48Ca, 58Ni, 90Zr, 94Mo, 124Sn, 138Ba, 140Ce, 208Pb) and compares them with random-phase approximation (RPA) and quasiparticle RPA calculations using the Gogny D1S interaction in a harmonic-oscillator basis. The author analyzes transition densities extracted from electron scattering and computed within RPA, as well as velocity fields, to characterize the collective mode's dynamics as an oscillation of a surface layer against a denser core.

## Key numbers
- The isoscalar dipole resonance is observed at approximately 6–7 MeV across a wide range of nuclei, somewhat higher for the lightest nuclei or lower in open-shell nuclei.
- In 48Ca, a strong isoscalar dipole resonance was predicted at 7.6 MeV and subsequently confirmed via comparison of (α,α'γ) and (γ,γ') experiments.
- The portion of the isoscalar energy-weighted sum rule exhausted by low-lying dipole transitions amounts to a few percentage points, described as "a huge amount for states at such low energies."
- RPA calculations overestimate the excitation energy of the IS-LED modes by about 3 MeV for N=Z nuclei and Ca and Sn isotopes.
- The empirical 1ħω value is given as 41A^(−1/3) MeV, and the average empirical energy of low-lying 3− states is 30A^(−1/3) MeV.

## Context
This work extends a series of theoretical studies (Papakonstantinou et al. 2011, 2012, 2014) and experimental reports (Havel, Poelhekken 1992, Derya 2013, 2014, Crespi 2014, Savran 2013) on the isoscalar low-energy dipole mode. It contradicts interpretations that attribute the isoscalar pygmy dipole strength to neutron-skin oscillations, noting that such interpretations severely overestimate the isovector (E1) strength (Papakonstantinou et al. 2007, Chambers 1994) and cannot account for strong isoscalar modes in N=Z nuclei. The findings enable future searches for genuine neutron-skin oscillations, predicted to occur closer to particle threshold or in much more exotic nuclei beyond the shell closures of 48Ca and 132Sn, in concordance with Skyrme-based studies (Eberlein et al. 2013).
