# Effects of high-$j$ orbitals, pairing and deformed neutron shells on upbendings of ground-state bands in neutron-rich even-even isotopes $^{170-184}$Hf

> The ground-state bands (GSBs) in the even-even hafnium isotopes $^{170-184}$Hf are investigated by using the cranked shell model (CSM) with pairing correlations treated by the particle-number conserving (PNC) method. The experimental kinematic moments of inertia are reproduced very well by theoretical calculations. The second upbending of the GSB at high frequency $\hbarω\approx0.5$ MeV observed (predicted) in $^{172}$Hf ($^{170,174-178}$Hf) attributes to the sudden alignments of the proton high-$j$ orbitals $\pi1i_{13/2}$ $(1/2^{+}[660])$, $\pi1h_{9/2}$ $(1/2^{-}[541])$ and orbital $\pi1h_{11/2}$ $(7/2^{-}[523])$. The first upbendings of GSBs at low frequency $\hbarω=0.2-0.3$ MeV in $^{170-178}$Hf, which locate below the deformed neutron shell $N=108$, attribute to the alignment of the neutron orbital $\nu1i_{13/2}$. For the heavier even-even isotopes $^{180-184}$Hf, compared to the lighter isotopes, the first band-crossing is delayed to the high frequency due to the existence of the deformed shells $N=108,116$. The upbendings of GSBs in $^{180-184}$Hf are predicted to occur at $\hbarω\approx0.5$MeV, which come from the sharp raise of the simultaneous alignments of both proton $\pi1i_{13/2}$, $\pi1h_{9/2}$ and neutron $\nu2g_{9/2}$ orbitals. The pairing correlation plays a very important role in the rotational properties of GSBs in even-even isotopes $^{180-184}$Hf. Its effects on upbendings and band-crossing frequencies are investigated.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the different upbending behaviors observed in the ground-state bands of neutron-rich even-even hafnium isotopes 170-184Hf can be explained by the interplay of high-j orbital alignments, pairing correlations, and the location of the Fermi surface relative to deformed neutron shells N=98, 104, 108, and 116, with the specific mechanism depending on whether the nucleus lies at or between these shell gaps.

## Method

The authors used the cranked shell model (CSM) with pairing correlations treated by the particle-number conserving (PNC) method. The cranked Nilsson Hamiltonian was diagonalized in a cranked many-particle configuration space of about 800 dimensions for protons and 900 for neutrons, with monopole and quadrupole pairing strengths G0=0.3 MeV for both protons and neutrons. Deformation parameters ε2 and ε4 were chosen by referring to the finite range droplet model tables of Moller (1995) and Moller (2016), and the kinematic moments of inertia were calculated from the expectation value of the angular momentum operator Jx.

## Key numbers

* The second upbending of the GSB occurs at high frequency ℏω≈0.5 MeV in 172Hf (observed) and is predicted in 170,174-178Hf.
* The first upbendings of GSBs occur at low frequency ℏω=0.2-0.3 MeV in 170-178Hf, which lie below the deformed neutron shell N=108.
* Deformation parameters ε2 range from 0.254 (172Hf) down to 0.214 (184Hf), with ε4 ranging from 0.014 (170Hf) to 0.069 (182Hf).
* For the heavier isotopes 180-184Hf, the first band-crossing is delayed to high frequency ℏω≈0.5 MeV.
* In 172Hf, the highest observed state is at spin 44ℏ.
* In 180,182Hf, no nucleon alignment was observed in the GSB up to ℏω=0.43 MeV.
* The monopole effective pairing strengths used are G0=0.3 for both protons and neutrons.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of high-spin states in neutron-rich hafnium isotopes, including configuration-constrained calculations by Liang (2015) for 172-178Hf, PNC-CSM pairing studies by Zeng (1994) for 170-182Hf, and band-crossing frequency investigations by Miller (2019) in rare-earth nuclei around hafnium. It also complements experimental work by Ngijoi-Yogo (2007) on 180,182Hf and by Cullen (1995) on 172Hf. The systematic study across the long isotope chain 170-184Hf enables understanding of how deformed neutron shells at N=98, 104, 108, and 116 affect rotational properties, and provides predictions for upbendings in the heavier isotopes 180-184Hf that can be tested by future experiments.
