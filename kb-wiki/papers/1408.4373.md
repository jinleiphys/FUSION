# Coupling to two target-state bands in the study of the n+22Ne system at low energy

> One theoretical method for studying nuclear scattering and resonances is via the multi-channel algebraic scattering (MCAS) formalism. Studies to date with this method have used a simple collective-rotor prescription to model target states with which a nucleon couples. While generally these target states all belong to the same rotational band, for certain systems it is necessary to include coupling to states outside of that main band. Here, we extend MCAS to allow coupling of different strengths between such states and the rotor band. This is an essential consideration in studying the example examined herein, the scattering of neutrons from 22Ne.

## Key claim
The authors extend the multi-channel algebraic scattering (MCAS) formalism to allow different coupling strengths (via distinct deformation parameters \(\beta_L\)) between target states belonging to a main rotational band and those outside it, rather than using a single coupling strength for all states as in prior MCAS studies. This extension is essential for systems like \(n+^{22}\text{Ne}\), where the \(2^+_2\) state at 4.456 MeV decays to the ground state but lies outside the principal rotor band.

## Method
The MCAS coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equations are solved using finite-rank expansions of the nucleon-nucleus interaction, with deformed Woods-Saxon form factors expanded to second order in the deformation parameter \(\epsilon\). The formalism is generalized to allow multiple multipole deformations (\(L \ne L'\)) and to introduce a scaling factor \(s_L\) (with \(0 < |s_L| < 1\)) that multiplies \(\beta_L\) for channels involving target states outside the main rotational band. The method is applied to \(n+^{22}\text{Ne}\) scattering using four target states (\(0^+_1\), \(2^+_1\), \(4^+_1\), and the second \(2^+_2\) state), with Pauli blocking treated via orthogonalizing pseudo-potentials (OPP) and parameters fitted to reproduce the \(^{23}\text{Ne}\) spectrum.

## Key numbers
- The \(2^+_1\) state in \(^{22}\text{Ne}\) at 1.275 MeV has a half-life of 3.63 ps, giving \(B(E2, 1.275) = 46.06\ e^2\text{fm}^4\).
- The \(2^+_2\) state in \(^{22}\text{Ne}\) at 4.456 MeV has a half-life of 37 fs, giving \(B(E2, 4.456) = 8.67\ e^2\text{fm}^4\).
- The ratio of \(B(E2)\) values is 0.188, leading to \(\overline{\beta_2} = 0.43\beta_2\) for the \(2^+_2\) state coupling (with \(\beta_2 = 0.22\) for the main band).
- The best-fit MCAS deformation parameters are \(\beta_2 = 0.22\), \(\overline{\beta_2} = 0.1034\), and \(\beta_4 = -0.08\).
- The 3-state MCAS calculation (using only \(0^+_1, 2^+_1, 4^+_1\)) yields 63 states of \(^{23}\text{Ne}\); the 4-state calculation (adding \(2^+_2\)) yields 89 states.
- The potential parameters include \(V_0 = -65.20\) MeV (odd parity) and \(-51.30\) MeV (even parity), with radius \(R_0 = 3.1\) fm and diffusivity \(a = 0.75\) fm.

## Context
This work extends the MCAS formalism previously developed by Amos et al. (2003) and Canton et al. (2005), which used a single rotational band with uniform coupling strengths. The extension to two bands with distinct \(\beta_2\) values is motivated by the need to describe the \(n+^{22}\text{Ne}\) system, relevant to the \(^{22}\text{Na}\) puzzle in ONe white dwarf novae (Battino et al., 2011; Aliotta et al., 2010; Jose et al., 2013) and to the NeNa hydrogen-burning cycle in second-generation stars. The results enable future MCAS studies of the mirror \(p+^{22}\text{Mg}\) system and radiative capture reactions such as \(^{22}\text{Ne}(p,\gamma)^{23}\text{Na}\), which are being investigated experimentally at LUNA (Cavanna et al., 2012) at energies below 400 keV.
