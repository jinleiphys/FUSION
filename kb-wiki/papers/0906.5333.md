# The Y(4260) as a $J/ψK \bar{K}$ system

> A study of the $J/ψππ$ and $J/ψK \bar{K}$ systems, treating them as coupled channels, has been made by solving the Faddeev equations, with the purpose of investigating the possibility of generation of the $J^{PC} = 1^{--}$, Y(4260) resonance due to the interaction between these three mesons. In order to do this, we start by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the two pseudoscalar and for the vector-pseudocalar meson systems using the amplitudes obtained from the lowest order chiral Lagrangians as potentials. With the $t$-matrices generated from these potentials, which contain the poles of the $σ$, $f_{0}(980)$ and $a_{0}(980)$ resonances for the pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar system and the pole of the X(3872), alongwith other new charmed resonant states, for the vector-pseudoscalar system, we solve the Faddeev equations. As a result, we get a peak around 4150 MeV with a width $\sim$ 90 MeV when the invariant mass of the two pseudoscalars is close to that of the $f_0 (980)$.

## Key claim

The authors find a three-body resonance in the coupled J/ψππ and J/ψK̅K systems at 4150 MeV with a width of about 90 MeV, which they associate with the Y(4260) resonance. However, they note that the 100 MeV difference between their predicted mass and the nominal 4260 MeV is the largest discrepancy found across all three-body states they have previously studied, and they attribute this to uncertainties in SU(4) symmetry and the lack of data to tune the J/ψπ and J/ψK interactions.

## Method

The authors solve the Faddeev equations for the J/ψππ and J/ψK̅K systems as coupled channels in isospin 0, using S-wave interactions. They first obtain two-body t-matrices by solving Bethe-Salpeter equations: for the pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar system (ππ, K̅K) using potentials from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian, and for the vector-pseudoscalar system (including J/ψπ, J/ψK, and charmed channels) using an SU(4)-symmetric Lagrangian with symmetry breaking factors. The on-shell parts of these t-matrices are then used as input to the Faddeev equations, with the off-shell contributions shown to cancel against three-body contact terms.

## Key numbers

- The three-body resonance appears at √s = 4150 MeV with a full width at half maximum of 90 MeV.
- The peak occurs when the invariant mass of the two pseudoscalars is around that of the f₀(980) resonance (approximately 980 MeV).
- Varying the SU(4) symmetry breaking parameter γ by 15% changes the peak position by about 5 MeV and the width by about 5 MeV.
- The total energy √s is varied between 4 and 5 GeV, and the invariant mass of the two pseudoscalars √s₂₃ is varied between 400 and 1100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-body Faddeev studies, which successfully generated the X(2175) resonance from the φK̅K system (Torres et al., 2008) and various baryon resonances from two-meson one-baryon systems. The Y(4260) has been observed by BABAR (2005) and confirmed by CLEO and BELLE, with proposals including a tetraquark state, a hadronic molecule of D₁D or D₀D*, a hybrid charmonium, or a D*ₛD̅*ₛ cusp. The present calculation suggests the Y(4260) may be understood as a dynamically generated J/ψf₀(980) resonance, analogous to the X(2175) as a φf₀(980) resonance. The result enables further three-body calculations of charmonium-like states and predicts a strong coupling to the J/ψK̅K channel that could be tested experimentally.
