# Does non-monotonic behavior of directed flow signal the onset of deconfinement?

> We investigate the effects of nuclear mean-field as well as the formation and decay of nuclear clusters on the directed flow $v_1$ in high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions from $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ GeV to 27 GeV incident energies within a transport model. Specifically, we use the JAM transport model in which potentials are implemented based on the framework of the relativistic quantum molecular dynamics. Our approach reproduces the rapidity dependence of directed flow data up to $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\approx 8$ GeV showing the significant importance of mean-field. However, the slopes of $dv_1/dy$ at mid-rapidity are calculated to be positive at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=11.7$ and 19.6 GeV, and becomes negative above 27 GeV. Thus the result from the JAM hadronic transport model with nuclear mean-field approach is incompatible with the data. Therefore within our approach, we conclude that the excitation function of the directed flow cannot be explained by the hadronic degree of freedom alone.

## Key claim

The authors show that the JAM hadronic transport model with nuclear mean-field potentials cannot reproduce the beam energy dependence of proton directed flow data from the STAR collaboration, specifically because the model predicts positive slopes of \(dv_1/dy\) at mid-rapidity for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 11.5\) and 19.6 GeV where the data show negative slopes. They conclude that hadronic degrees of freedom alone cannot explain the excitation function of directed flow, implying that the observed non-monotonic behavior may signal the onset of deconfinement.

## Method

The authors used the JAM hadronic transport model, which includes resonance and string degrees of freedom for particle production and two-body collisions for secondary interactions. Baryon mean-field potentials were implemented using a simplified version of relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (RQMD/S) with a Skyrme-type density-dependent and Lorentzian-type momentum-dependent potential (incompressibility \(K = 272\) MeV). Nuclear cluster formation was modeled via a coalescence prescription (coalescence parameters \(R_0 = 4.0\) fm and \(P_0 = 0.3\) GeV/c), and the statistical decay of excited fragments was included using a statistical decay model (SDM) that emits nuclei up to mass number 4 and gamma rays.

## Key numbers

- The incompressibility of the nuclear mean-field potential is set to \(K = 272\) MeV, with parameters \(\alpha = -0.209\) GeV, \(\beta = 0.284\) GeV, \(\gamma = 7/6\), and saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.168\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The coalescence parameters for nuclear cluster formation are \(R_0 = 4.0\) fm and \(P_0 = 0.3\) GeV/c, chosen by fitting the proton rapidity distribution at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.7\) GeV for central Au+Au collisions.
- The effect of nuclear cluster formation on the proton directed flow \(v_1\) is about 15%, and the effect of statistical decay of nuclear clusters is very tiny.
- The baryon mean-field reduces the slope \(dv_1/dy\) by 20-30%.
- The effect of nuclear cluster formation on proton spectra and \(v_1\) is about 10-20%.
- The model predicts positive \(dv_1/dy\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 11.5\) and 19.6 GeV, while the data show negative slopes; the slope becomes negative in the model only above 27 GeV.
- The transition region where the sign of \(dv_1/dy\) reverses is around \(9 \lessapprox \sqrt{s_{NN}} \lessapprox 20\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies on the beam energy dependence of directed flow using hybrid transport approaches (Steinheimer 2014), three-fluid models (Ivanov 2014), and the PHDS transport model (Konchakovski 2014), none of which had reached a definite conclusion on the interpretation of the STAR data (Adamczyk 2014). The authors show that even with careful inclusion of mean-field potentials and nuclear cluster formation, a purely hadronic transport model cannot reproduce the observed negative slope of \(dv_1/dy\) at intermediate BES energies, suggesting that the softening of the equation of state associated with the quark-gluon plasma phase transition is needed. The authors note that the negative \(dv_1/dy\) at 11.5 and 19.6 GeV can be described by transport approaches that include the effects of EoS softening (NOS 2016).
