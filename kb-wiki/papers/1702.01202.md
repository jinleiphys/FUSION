# Fluctuations in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Fluctuations are one of the main probes of the physics of the new state of hot and dense nuclear matter called the Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) which is created in the ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. In this dissertation we extend and improve upon the existing descriptions of heavy ion collisions in three different directions: we study the new signatures of initial state fluctuations, the propagation of perturbations in the early stages of the collision, and the effect of thermal fluctuations on the hydrodynamic expansion of the QGP. First, we use Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to study event-by-event fluctuations in the spectrum of harmonic flow, $v_n(p_T)$ for $n=0\text{--}5$, in hydrodynamic simulations of Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\,{\rm TeV}$). The PCA procedure finds two dominant contributions to the two-particle correlation function: the leading component is identified with the standard event plane $v_n(p_T)$ and the subleading component is responsible for factorization breaking and reflects the radial excitation in the initial state. Second, we use leading order effective kinetic theory (accurate at weak coupling) to simulate the pre-equilibrium evolution of transverse energy and flow perturbations. We construct the linear response Green functions, which map initial perturbations to the energy momentum tensor at a time when hydrodynamics becomes applicable. Finally, we develop a set of kinetic equations for thermal noise correlators which are equivalent to nonlinear hydrodynamics with noise. We use the hydro-kinetic equations to analyze thermal fluctuations for a Bjorken expansion and determine the coefficient of the first fractional power ($\propto 1/(τT)^{3/2}$) of the nonlinear noise corrections to energy momentum tensor.

## Key claim

This dissertation extends and improves existing descriptions of heavy ion collisions in three directions: studying new signatures of initial state fluctuations via Principal Component Analysis (PCA), simulating pre-equilibrium evolution of perturbations using leading order effective kinetic theory, and analyzing thermal fluctuations in hydrodynamic expansion of the Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP). The work is limited to weak coupling regimes and hydrodynamic simulations, with the PCA analysis specifically applied to Pb+Pb collisions at a single center-of-mass energy.

## Method

The author applied Principal Component Analysis (PCA) to decompose event-by-event fluctuations in harmonic flow spectra v_n(p_T) for n=0-5 from hydrodynamic simulations of Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV. Leading order effective kinetic theory (accurate at weak coupling) was used to simulate pre-equilibrium evolution of transverse energy and flow perturbations, constructing linear response Green functions that map initial perturbations to the energy-momentum tensor at the time when hydrodynamics becomes applicable. A set of kinetic equations for thermal noise correlators was developed, equivalent to nonlinear hydrodynamics with noise, and applied to analyze thermal fluctuations for a Bjorken expansion.

## Key numbers

- Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV at the CERN Large Hadron Collider were used for PCA analysis of harmonic flow v_n(p_T) for n=0-5
- The coefficient of the first fractional power of the gradient expansion was determined as proportional to 1/(tau T)^(3/2), computed here for the first time
- Initial conditions for hydrodynamic evolution were set at tau_init ~ 0.6 fm
- The root mean squared charge radius of a Pb nucleus is R_rms ~ 5 fm
- Nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross-section diameter D = sqrt(sigma_NN/pi) ~ 1.5 fm
- Pion mass m_pi ~ 140 MeV is small compared to typical measured transverse momentum p_perp ~ 1 GeV
- Lorentz contraction factor gamma ~ 1500 for nuclei at LHC energies
- Nuclei passage time Delta t_passing ~ 2R_Pb/(c*gamma) ~ 0.01 fm

## Context

This work extends prior hydrodynamic simulations of heavy ion collisions that used Monte Carlo Glauber initial conditions (Alver, 2010; Qiu, 2011) and earlier studies of elliptic flow and viscous effects (Teaney, 2003; Romatschke, 2007). The PCA approach provides a systematic method for studying the full information of two-particle correlation matrices and identifying subleading flows responsible for factorization breaking in hydrodynamics. The formalism of hydro-kinetic equations can be applied to more general background flows and coupled to existing viscous hydrodynamic codes to incorporate the physics of hydrodynamic fluctuations, while the pre-equilibrium Green functions enable combining effective kinetic theory with weak coupling initial state models such as IP-Glasma to model complete pre-thermal evolution from saturated nuclei to hydrodynamics.
