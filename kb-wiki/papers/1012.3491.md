# Interesting,Surprising and Perhaps Amusing Results in Nuclear Physics Calculations Over the the Years

> We have compiled some sections of works by L. Zamick and collaborators which we hope will be of interest to the reader.

## Key claim
This compilation of selected works by L. Zamick and collaborators presents results that the authors find interesting, surprising, or amusing, often emerging unexpectedly from routine calculations; the paper is explicitly a curated collection rather than a single original study, with the stated criteria being educational value and adding "zest" to the field rather than presenting a unified new finding.

## Method
The paper reprints excerpts from several published works spanning 1973–1997, each employing distinct nuclear-structure methods: (1) a density-dependent delta interaction with a variable density-power parameter σ to derive a formula for nuclear compressibility using harmonic-oscillator wave functions; (2) the Inglis cranking model with harmonic-oscillator wave functions to compute the mass parameter of the breathing mode; (3) shell-model calculations with DME and SKII interactions to evaluate isospin mixing matrix elements in ¹²C; (4) random-phase approximation (RPA) calculations with a zero-range delta interaction in closed-shell nuclei, using the multipole condition to fix interaction strengths; (5) single-j-shell calculations in the f₇/₂ region for M1 transitions in titanium isotopes; (6) large-scale shell-model calculations in ⁴He including up to 6ħω admixtures; and (7) an SU(3) analysis of the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear compressibility K is expressed as K = (1/sA)[⟨T⟩ + qE_B + σ(3⟨T⟩ + qE_B)], where σ is the power of the density in the interaction, ⟨T⟩ is the mean kinetic energy per particle, and E_B is the binding energy per particle (positive quantities).
- For isospin mixing of 1⁺ states in ¹²C, the total off-diagonal matrix element is 170 keV with the DME interaction and 92 keV with the SKII interaction.
- The difference in the p₁/₂ proton orbit square radius between DME and SKII is 0.962 fm² (10%), while for the p₃/₂ orbit the difference is 0.126 fm² (1.7%).
- For M1 transitions in titanium isotopes (f₇/₂ shell): ⁴⁴Ti has B(M1)↑ = 2.70 μ_N² at 5.81 MeV; ⁴⁶Ti has B(M1)↑ = 1.70 μ_N² at 4.00 MeV; ⁴⁸Ti has B(M1)↑ = 0.69 μ_N² at 3.83 MeV.
- In ⁴He, with up to 2ħω admixtures and no spin-orbit or tensor interactions (x=0, y=0), the summed spin and orbital M1 strengths are both 0.8546 × 10⁻³ μ_N²; with up to 6ħω admixtures, spin sum is 0.8546 × 10⁻³ μ_N² and orbital sum is 0.8546 × 10⁻³ μ_N² (equal for the x=0, y=0 case).
- For the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction in Elliott's SU(3) model, only 2/3 of the single-particle splitting ε_0d − ε_1s comes from the diagonal term of Q·Q; the remaining 1/3 comes from the interaction of the valence nucleon with the core.

## Context
This compilation reprints results from Zamick (1973) on nuclear compressibility, Zamick (1973) on the breathing-mode mass parameter, Sato and Zamick (1977) on isospin mixing in ¹²C, Abbas and Zamick (1980) on nuclear vibrations with zero-range interactions, Zamick (1985) on M1 excitations in the f₇/₂ region, Fayache and Zamick (1995) on M1 spin and orbital strengths in ⁴He, and Fayache, Sharon, and Zamick (1997) on Elliott's SU(3) model. The work extends earlier approaches by Moszkowski (who fixed σ=2/3), Vautherin and Brink (who used σ=1), and Bethe's observation about compressibility differences. It also connects to the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly via charge-symmetry-breaking interactions and to the IBA model of Dieperink for M1 rates. The compilation enables future work on systematic trends of 1⁺ states across the periodic table and on the role of single-particle energies and radii in isospin mixing.
