# Space-like pion off-shell form factors in the Bethe-Salpeter approach

> In the paper, the off-shell electromagnetic pion form factors in the Bethe-Salpeter formalism are considered. The separable kernel of the first rank quark-antiquark interaction is used to solve the equation analytically. The semi-off-shell pion form factors \( F_1 \) and \( F_2 \), which are related to each other by the Ward-Takahashi identity, are calculated. The obtained off-shell form factors as well as static properties of the pion are compared with the results of other authors.

## Key claim

The paper calculates half-off-shell electromagnetic pion form factors using a Bethe-Salpeter approach with a separable kernel of rank one, and demonstrates that the Ward-Takahashi identity is satisfied for the model; the key hedge is that the results are model-dependent and rely on a specific monopole form for the vertex function's radial part.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the pion vertex function using a separable interaction kernel of rank one with a monopole radial dependence \( f(k^2) = 1/(k^2 - \Lambda^2 + i\epsilon) \). The half-off-shell form factors \( F_1(Q^2,t) \) and \( F_2(Q^2,t) \) are computed from four-dimensional integrals using Feynman parameterization, with contributions from both the relativistic impulse approximation and the interaction current. The form factor \( F_2 \) is calculated both directly and via the Ward-Takahashi identity relation \( F_2(Q^2,t) = (t - m_\pi^2)[F_1(0,t) - F_1(Q^2,t)]/Q^2 \).

## Key numbers

- Pion mass \( m_\pi = 140 \) MeV used as the on-shell mass.
- Constitutive quark mass \( m = 260 \) MeV and parameter \( \Lambda = 550 \) MeV from prior work (Bondarenko 2025).
- Charge radius \( \sqrt{\langle r_\pi^2 \rangle} = 0.679 \) fm from the model, compared to experimental value \( 0.672(8) \) fm.
- Weak decay constant \( f_\pi = 101.38 \) MeV from the model, compared to experimental value \( 92.28(7) \) MeV.
- Form factor \( g(0, m_\pi^2) = 1.97 \) GeV\(^{-2}\) from the model, compared to experimental value \( 1.93(5) \) GeV\(^{-2}\).
- The off-shell parameter \( t \) takes values \( t = -m_\pi^2 \), \( t = 0.2 \) (GeV/c)\(^2\), and \( t = -0.5 \) (GeV/c)\(^2\) in the calculations.

## Context

This work extends prior Bethe-Salpeter studies of pion form factors by Bondarenko (2025) and compares with the constant vertex function model (CON) of Choi (2019) and the symmetric vertex function model (SYM) of Leao (2024). The model reproduces experimental static pion properties more accurately than the CON and SYM models, and the authors plan to extend the approach to the time-like momentum transfer region and explore alternative functional forms for the vertex function.
