# Experiment summary

> The measurement of the production of particles coming from hard scattering processes covers a fundamental role in the characterization of the system formed in heavy-ion collisions, allowing to probe the microscopic processes underlying the interaction of high energy partons with the medium. An impressive amount of measurements related to jet, quarkonia, open heavy flavor, and electroweak signal production in nucleus-nucleus as well as p(d)-nucleus collisions was delivered by experiments at RHIC and LHC in past years. In these proceedings, the main experimental results presented during the Hard Probes conference are summarized.

## Key claim

This paper summarizes experimental results presented at the Hard Probes conference, covering measurements of hard scattering probes in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, with the caveat that the summary is not exhaustive and omits results on dilepton production and direct photons at low transverse momentum.

## Method

The author reviews experimental results from ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, LHCb, PHENIX, and STAR collaborations on electromagnetic probes (W/Z bosons, isolated photons), jet suppression and structure, heavy-flavor production (charm and beauty hadrons), quarkonia (J/psi and Upsilon states), and measurements in small collision systems (p-Pb, d-Au). The review covers measurements of nuclear modification factors (R_AA, R_pPb), azimuthal correlations, dijet momentum imbalances, and elliptic flow coefficients across multiple collision energies and centrality intervals.

## Key numbers

- ATLAS measured isolated photon production in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV for transverse momenta 22 < pT < 280 GeV/c in four centrality intervals from 0-80%
- Inclusive jet R_AA (R=0.4) in 0-10% most central Pb-Pb collisions shows suppression by a factor about two from pT=50 GeV/c up to 400 GeV/c, with R_AA about 0.8 in 60-80% centrality
- ALICE observed suppression by a factor about 3 down to pT=40 GeV/c for jets with R=0.2 in 0-10% most central collisions
- Charged particle R_AA in 0-5% most central Pb-Pb collisions shows minimum of about 0.15 at pT about 6 GeV/c, rising to about 0.65 at pT=200 GeV/c
- CMS measured b-jet suppression increasing with centrality up to more than a factor 2 in the 10% most central Pb-Pb collisions for pT>80 GeV/c
- 3.5 sigma difference between average R_AA values in 0-10% and 10-20% centrality for prompt D-mesons (8<pT<16 GeV/c) versus non-prompt J/psi (6.5<pT<30 GeV/c) indicates R_AA(B) > R_AA(D)
- Upsilon(1S) R_AA decreases with centrality down to 0.3 in central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC
- Upsilon(1S) R_AA reaches about 0.4 in central U-U collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=193 GeV at RHIC
- CMS measured dijet asymmetry coefficient averaged over 0-80% centrality: -0.010 +/- 0.004, suggesting stronger imbalance for out-of-plane jet pairs
- Constant energy loss of about 8 GeV/c suggested by both ALICE and STAR measurements of recoiling jets

## Context

This conference summary extends the extensive program of hard probe measurements at RHIC and LHC, building on foundational observations such as J/psi suppression at SPS (first observed in the 1980s) and the smaller J/psi suppression at LHC compared to RHIC (ALICE, 2012) that indicated recombination effects. The results enable further constraints on transport coefficients of the quark-gluon plasma, parton energy loss mechanisms (including mass dependence), and cold nuclear matter effects. The measurements also address open questions such as the charged-particle R_pPb puzzle at high pT (where CMS, ATLAS, and ALICE reported differing values) and the nature of double-ridge correlations in small collision systems first observed in p-Pb collisions.
