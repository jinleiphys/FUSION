# Fluctuations and correlations in scattering on a resonance coupled to a chaotic background

> We discuss and briefly overview recent progress with studying fluctuations in scattering on a resonance state coupled to the background of many chaotic states. Such a problem arises naturally, e.g., when dealing with wave propagation in the presence of a complex environment. Using a statistical model based on random matrix theory, we obtain a number of nonperturbative results for various statistics of scattering characteristics. This includes the joint and marginal distributions of the reflection and transmission intensities and phases, which are derived exactly at arbitrary coupling to the background with finite absorption. The intensities and phases are found to exhibit highly non-trivial statistical correlations, which remain essential even in the limit of strong absorption. In the latter case, we also consider the relevant approximations and their accuracy. As an application, we briefly discuss the statistics of the phase rigidity (or mode complexness) in such a scattering situation.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact, nonperturbative statistical distributions for scattering observables (intensities and phases) when a single resonance is coupled to a chaotic background, showing that strong statistical correlations between intensities and phases persist even in the limit of strong absorption, contrary to the expectation that such correlations would vanish.

## Method

The author models the chaotic background using the Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (GOE) of random matrices and employs the strength function formalism to account for coupling between the resonance and the background. The scattering matrix at the resonance energy is expressed in terms of a complex random variable \(K = u - iv\) (the local Green's function of the background), whose joint probability density function is known exactly at arbitrary absorption rate \(\gamma\). From this representation, the joint and marginal distributions of transmission and reflection intensities and phases are derived via Jacobian calculus for a two-channel scattering setup.

## Key numbers

- The model has two control parameters: the coupling strength \(\eta \equiv \Gamma_{\downarrow}/\Gamma_0\) (ratio of spreading width to escape width) and the absorption rate \(\gamma \equiv 2\pi\Gamma_{\mathrm{abs}}/\Delta\).
- At zero absorption (\(\gamma = 0\)), the transmission phase distribution is \(\mathcal{P}_0(\theta_T) = 1/[\pi(\eta\cos^2\theta_T + \eta^{-1}\sin^2\theta_T)]\).
- At strong absorption (\(\gamma \gg 1\)), the transmission intensity variance is \(\sigma^2_T = 4\eta^2/[\gamma(1+\eta)^4]\) and the phase variance is \(\sigma^2_\theta = 4\eta^2/[\gamma(1+\eta)^2]\).
- The unitarity deficit measure \(d = 1 - R - T\) satisfies \(d \leq 1/2\).
- The average transmission amplitude is \(\langle t \rangle = \sin 2\varphi/(1+\eta)\) and the average reflection amplitudes are \(\langle r_\pm \rangle = (\eta \pm \cos 2\varphi)/(1+\eta)\), where \(\varphi\) is the mixing angle.

## Context

This work extends the author's prior studies (Savin 2017a, 2017b) on resonance scattering coupled to a chaotic background, providing a unified exact treatment of joint statistics for both intensities and phases. The results enable quantitative comparison with microwave cavity experiments (Kuhl 2005, Kuhl 2005a, Dietz 2010, Kumar 2017) where full S-matrix measurements including phases are now possible. The formalism also applies to giant resonances and doorway states in nuclear physics (Bohr, Sokolov 1997) and to wave propagation in complex environments such as reverberation chambers.
