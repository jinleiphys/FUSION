# Reevaluating Reactor Antineutrino Anomalies with Updated Flux Predictions

> Hints for the existence of a sterile neutrino at nuclear reactors are reexamined using two updated predictions for the fluxes of antineutrinos produced in fissions. These new predictions diverge in their preference for the rate deficit anomaly, relative to previous analyses, but the anomaly in the ratios of measured antineutrino spectra persists. We comment on upcoming experiments and their ability to probe the preferred region of the sterile-neutrino parameter space in the electron neutrino disappearance channel.

## Key claim

The paper reevaluates the reactor antineutrino anomaly using two updated flux predictions and finds that these new predictions diverge in their preference for a sterile neutrino when analyzing total event rates, but the spectral anomaly (based on ratios of measured spectra) persists at the 3.1 sigma level regardless of which flux model is used.

## Method

The authors performed global fits to inverse beta decay (IBD) event rates from short-baseline and medium-baseline reactor experiments using three different antineutrino flux models: the standard Huber-Mueller (HM) conversion method, an updated ab initio calculation from Estienne (2019), and an updated conversion method called HKSS from Hayen (2019) that includes forbidden decays via nuclear shell model calculations. They also analyzed spectral ratios from Bugey, DANSS, Daya Bay, Double Chooz, NEOS, and RENO, which largely cancel flux model dependence. All analyses used the GLoBES software with a chi-squared minimization over sterile neutrino parameters (effective mixing angle sin^2 2theta_ee and mass-squared splitting Delta m_41^2) and nuisance parameters for isotopic flux uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- HM flux rates analysis: chi^2 for three-neutrino mixing = 41.4, minimum chi^2 = 33.5, significance = 2.3 sigma (p = 2.0 x 10^-2)
- Ab initio flux rates analysis: chi^2 for three-neutrino mixing = 39.2, minimum chi^2 = 37.0, significance = 0.95 sigma (p = 0.34)
- HKSS flux rates analysis: chi^2 for three-neutrino mixing = 58.1, minimum chi^2 = 47.5, significance = 2.8 sigma (p = 5.0 x 10^-3)
- Spectral analysis (all flux models): chi^2 for three-neutrino mixing = 184.9, minimum chi^2 = 172.2, significance = 3.1 sigma (p = 1.8 x 10^-3)
- DANSS + NEOS spectral analysis: chi^2 for three-neutrino mixing = 98.9, minimum chi^2 = 84.7, significance = 3.3 sigma (p = 8.1 x 10^-4)
- The ab initio calculation produces a roughly 10% reduction in the flux from ^235U relative to HM predictions

## Context

This work extends the original reactor antineutrino anomaly analysis of Mention (2011) and subsequent global fits by Dentler (2018) and Gariazzo (2018), using two new flux calculations that appeared in 2019. The ab initio calculation from Estienne (2019) reduces the sterile neutrino preference to below 1 sigma, while the HKSS calculation from Hayen (2019) increases it to 2.8 sigma, highlighting the need for improved beta decay data and treatment of forbidden decays. The spectral anomaly remains robust at 3.1 sigma, and the authors find that even a null result from the PROSPECT experiment would leave a significant fraction of the preferred parameter space unexplored, particularly at Delta m_41^2 around 0.1 to 0.5 eV^2.
