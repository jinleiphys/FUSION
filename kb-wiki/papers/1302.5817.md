# Odd-even staggerings on nuclear binding energy described by the covariant density functional theory

> The odd-even staggerings (OES) on nuclear binding energies are studied systematically within the covariant density functional (CDF) theories, specifically the relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) and the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theories. Taking the finite-range Gogny force D1S as an effective pairing interaction, both CDF models can provide appropriate descriptions on the OESs of nuclear binding energies for C, O, Ca, Ni, Zr, Sn, Ce, Gd and Pb isotopes as well as for N=50 and 82 isotones. However, due to the inconsistence between the non-relativistic pairing interaction and the relativistic effective Lagrangians, there exist some systematical discrepancies from the data, i.e., the underestimated OESs in light C and O isotopes and the overestimated ones in heavy region, respectively. Such discrepancies can be eliminated partially by introducing a $Z$- or $N$-dependent strength factor into the pairing force Gogny D1S. In addition, successful descriptions of the occupation numbers of Sn isotopes are achieved with the optimized Gogny pairing force. Furthermore, the analysis of the systematics of both pairing effects and nuclear binding energy indicate the requirement of an unified relativistic mechanism in both p-p and p-h channels to improve the quantitative precision of the theory.

## Key claim

The covariant density functional (CDF) theories, specifically the relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) and relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) models with the finite-range Gogny force D1S as the pairing interaction, can provide appropriate overall descriptions of odd-even staggerings (OES) on nuclear binding energies across a wide range of isotopes and isotones, but systematic discrepancies exist, underestimated OES in light nuclei and overestimated OES in heavy nuclei, due to the inconsistency between the non-relativistic pairing interaction and the relativistic effective Lagrangians.

## Method

The authors performed systematic calculations of neutron OES for isotopic chains of C, O, Ca, Ni, Zr, Sn, Ce, Gd, and Pb, and proton OES for N=50 and N=82 isotonic chains, using both RHFB and RHB theories. The finite-range Gogny force D1S was adopted as the effective pairing interaction, with four CDF effective interactions employed in the mean-field channels: PKA1 and PKO3 for RHFB, and PKDD and DD-ME2 for RHB. The three-point indicator formula was used to extract OES from single-nucleon separation energies, and a Z- or N-dependent strength factor was introduced into the Gogny pairing force to partially eliminate systematic discrepancies.

## Key numbers

- For C isotopes, the root-mean-square deviation sigma_rms with PKA1 was 0.792 (without strength factor) and improved to 0.665 (with strength factor zeta=1.20); the average error D changed from -0.467 to -0.125.
- For O isotopes, sigma_rms with PKA1 was 0.609 (without) and 0.582 (with zeta=1.15); D changed from -0.415 to -0.173.
- For Sn isotopes, sigma_rms with PKA1 was 0.230 (without) and 0.135 (with zeta=0.95); D changed from +0.186 to +0.043.
- For Pb isotopes, sigma_rms with PKA1 was 0.327 (without) and 0.118 (with zeta=0.90); D changed from +0.296 to +0.049.
- For N=50 isotones, sigma_rms with PKA1 was 0.250 (without) and 0.172 (with zeta=0.95); D changed from +0.196 to +0.037.
- For N=82 isotones, sigma_rms with PKA1 was 0.300 (without) and 0.123 (with zeta=0.90); D changed from +0.278 to -0.055.
- The strength factor zeta decreased monotonically with increasing proton number Z for isotopes: 1.20 (C), 1.15 (O), 1.00 (Ca, Ni, Zr), 0.95 (Sn), 0.93 (Ce), 0.90 (Gd, Pb).
- For Sn isotopes, the experimental OES values remained around 1.2 MeV except at N=65 and N=83.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pairing correlations in covariant density functional theory, building on the RHFB formalism developed by Long et al. (2010) and the RHB theory by Meng et al. (1998) and Vretenar et al. (2005). The systematic discrepancies found here, underestimated OES in light nuclei and overestimated OES in heavy nuclei when using the Gogny D1S force, are consistent with earlier findings by Gonzalez et al. (1996) and Afanasjev et al. (2003) regarding the need for adjusted pairing strengths in relativistic frameworks. The results indicate that a unified relativistic mechanism in both particle-particle and particle-hole channels is required to improve quantitative precision, and the optimized pairing force with Z- or N-dependent strength factors enables better descriptions of occupation numbers in Sn isotopes, particularly with the PKA1 interaction which includes rho-tensor couplings.
