# Time-scaled scenario of low-energy heavy-ion collisions

> The underlying scenario of low-energy heavy-ion collisions is presented based on time-dependent density-functional calculations. A classification of several types of reaction dynamics is given with respect to their time-scales.

# Time-scaled scenario of low-energy heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
Low-energy heavy-ion collisions proceed through three distinct time-scaled stages (fast charge equilibration, momentum equilibration, and thermal equilibration), but this classification applies only to the most probable time evolutions simulated by time-dependent density-functional theory (TDDFT), which cannot fully treat thermal effects or processes beyond 10⁻²⁰ s.

## Method
The author performed time-dependent density-functional theory (TDDFT) calculations to simulate low-energy heavy-ion collisions at incident energies of a few MeV per nucleon. Reaction dynamics were classified according to two criterion energies: the Coulomb energy (E_C) and the Fermi energy (E_F), which define thresholds for contact between nuclei and for fast charge equilibration, respectively. The time scales of three equilibration processes (charge, momentum, thermal) were estimated from the corresponding energies using the relation ħω = 0.65820 × 10⁻²¹ ω (MeV).

## Key numbers
- Fermi energy per nucleon in the centre-of-mass frame: E_F/(A₁+A₂) = E_{x=25} (MeV), where x=25% of the speed of light is the typical Fermi velocity; the typical Fermi energy value is 40 MeV.
- For mass-symmetric collision (A₁=A₂) with E_lab/A₁ = 29.3 MeV, the centre-of-mass Fermi energy per nucleon is 7.3 MeV.
- Coulomb energy per nucleon for two identical ²³⁸U nuclei: 1.7 MeV; for two identical ⁴He nuclei: 0.4 MeV.
- Typical time interval from Fermi energy (40 MeV): T_F = 1.0 × 10⁻²² s.
- Typical time interval from Coulomb energy (1 MeV): T_C = 4.1 × 10⁻²¹ s.
- Typical duration time of low-energy heavy-ion collisions: 1000 fm/c (3.3 × 10⁻²¹ s).
- TDDFT calculations are applicable for time scales from 10⁻²³ s to 10⁻²⁰ s, with a unit time step of order 10⁻²⁴ s and typically 10000 steps.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous TDDFT-based studies (Iwata 2010, Iwata 2012) by providing a systematic time-scale classification of low-energy heavy-ion reaction dynamics. The classification into three periods (fast charge equilibration at 10⁻²² s, momentum equilibration at 10⁻²⁰ s, and thermal equilibration beyond 10⁻²⁰ s) enables a framework for understanding when fusion, fragmentation, fission, and particle emission occur. The paper also clarifies the limitations of TDDFT for studying thermal equilibration and long-term evolution, pointing to the need for treatments that include particle emission and heat flow beyond the unitary time evolution of TDDFT.
