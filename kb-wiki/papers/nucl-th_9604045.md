# Angular Distributions for Knockout and Scattering of Protons in the Eikonal Approximation

> The advent of new electron accelerators with few-GeV beam energies makes the (e,e'p) reaction a promising tool for investigating new aspects of the electromagnetic interaction. To this purpose it is crucial to set the scale of Final-State Interactions (FSI) at high ejectile energies. We have analyzed the constraints which allow for a meaningful generalization to (e,e'p) of the Glauber method used in elastic (p,p) scattering. The FSI dominance at large deflection angles produces in the distributions a universal behaviour resembling the coherent diffractive scattering between the ejected proton and the (residual) nucleus. It is argued that the comparison between (e,e'p) and (p,p), when a meaningful one, may represent a convenient tool to disentangle (exotic) effects due to FSI from those related to the (hard) electromagnetic vertex.

## Key claim

The paper argues that for (e,e'p) reactions at high outgoing-proton momenta (around 4 GeV/c), the angular distributions at large deflection angles are dominated by final-state interactions (FSI) and exhibit a universal diffractive pattern resembling elastic (p,p) scattering, but only under the specific condition that the transverse missing momentum is large and the kinematics are chosen to make the comparison meaningful.

## Method

The authors compare the distorted spectral density for the 12C(e,e'p)11B reaction with the elastic scattering distribution for 11B(p,p), both calculated using the eikonal (Glauber) approximation for outgoing-proton momenta of 4 GeV/c. They solve the Schrodinger equation for the scattering wave function using both partial-wave expansion (method A, up to L_max = 120) and the linearized Glauber method (method B), employing a Woods-Saxon optical potential with parameters adjusted for 12C (R = 1.2 x A^(1/3) fm, a = 0.5 fm) and an imaginary well depth W = 50 x p'/1400 MeV chosen to reproduce NE18 data.

## Key numbers

* Outgoing-proton momenta in the range 1 to 4 GeV/c are selected for the analysis, with the main comparison at p' = 4 GeV/c.
* The optical potential uses R = 1.2 x A^(1/3) fm and a = 0.5 fm for the 12C nucleus.
* The imaginary well depth is set to W = 50 x p'/1400 MeV, with p' in MeV/c, to reproduce NE18 data.
* The partial-wave expansion requires L_max = 120 to satisfy the convergency criterion at these momenta.
* The transition to FSI dominance occurs for transverse missing momenta exceeding the Fermi momentum of the target nucleus, approximately at gamma = 6 degrees for p' = 4 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the Glauber method, previously applied successfully to elastic (p,p) scattering by Glauber (1959) and others, to the (e,e'p) reaction at high energies, addressing the nontrivial differences in kinematics and initial-state conditions. The analysis is motivated by data from the NE18 collaboration (1990s) which showed that straightforward application of the Glauber model overestimates damping at small angles, leading to various interpretations by Piasetzky and collaborators (1992) and Frankfurt and Strikman (1995). The paper enables a new strategy for disentangling exotic effects like color transparency from ordinary FSI by comparing the diffractive tails of (e,e'p) and (p,p) angular distributions at large transverse missing momenta.
