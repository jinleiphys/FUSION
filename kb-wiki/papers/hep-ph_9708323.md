# Determination of Decuplet Baryon Magnetic Moments from QCD Sum Rules

> A complete set of QCD sum rules for the magnetic moments of decuplet baryons are derived using the external field method. They are analyzed thoroughly using a Monte-Carlo based procedure. Valid sum rules are identified under the criteria of OPE convergence and ground state dominance and their predictions are obtained. The performances of these sum rules are further compared and a favorable sum rule is designated for each member. Correlations between the input and the output parameters are examined and large sensitivities to the quark condensate magnetic susceptibility $χ$ are found. Using realistic estimates of the QCD input parameters, the uncertainties on the magnetic moments are found relatively large and they can be attributed mostly to the poorly-known $χ$. It is shown that the accuracy can be improved to the 30% level, provided the uncertainties in the QCD input parameters can be determined to the 10% level. The computed magnetic moments are consistent with existing data. Comparisons with other calculations are made.

## Key claim

The paper derives a complete set of QCD sum rules for decuplet baryon magnetic moments and finds that the uncertainties on the computed moments are relatively large, attributable mostly to the poorly-known quark condensate magnetic susceptibility χ; the accuracy could be improved to the 30% level only if the uncertainties in the QCD input parameters can be determined to the 10% level.

## Method

The author uses the external field method to compute the linear response of two-point correlation functions for decuplet baryons to a constant background electromagnetic field, evaluating the QCD side via operator product expansion (OPE) up to dimension 8. The phenomenological side is constructed from the Rarita-Schwinger spin-3/2 formalism, isolating 18 tensor structures that contain the magnetic dipole form factor at zero momentum transfer. A Monte-Carlo based procedure with 1000 randomly generated QCD parameter sets is used to fit the sum rules, applying criteria of OPE convergence (highest-dimension operators contribute no more than 10%) and ground state dominance (excited state contributions no more than 50%) to identify valid sum rules.

- m₀² = 0.72 ± 0.08 GeV²

- Flavor symmetry breaking parameter for strange susceptibilities: φ = 0.60 ± 0.05
- κ = 0.75 ± 0.38, ξ = -1.5 ± 0.75

- Experimental magnetic moment of Δ⁺⁺: μ_Δ⁺⁺ = 4.5 ± 1.0 μ_N (Bosshard, 1991)

## Context

This work extends earlier QCD sum rule calculations of nucleon and octet baryon magnetic moments by Ioffe (1984), Balitsky (1983), Chiu (1986), and Pasupathy (1986) to the entire decuplet family, which had been less studied within the same approach. Previous unpublished reports on Δ⁺⁺ and Ω⁻ magnetic moments existed (Bely, 1984), and the magnetic form factor of Δ⁺⁺ was calculated using a different technique (Bely, 1993). The results enable systematic comparison with lattice QCD calculations (Derek, 1992), chiral perturbation theory (Butler, 1994), and various quark model approaches, and provide a framework for understanding the role of vacuum susceptibilities in determining baryon magnetic properties.
