# Entanglement as a probe of hadronization

> Recently, it was discovered that the proton structure at high energies exhibits maximal entanglement. This leads to a simple relation between the proton's parton distributions and the entropy of hadrons produced in high-energy inelastic interactions that has been experimentally confirmed. In this letter, we extend this approach to the production of jets. Here, the maximal entanglement predicts a relation between the jet fragmentation function and the entropy of hadrons produced in jet fragmentation. We test this relation using the ATLAS Collaboration data on jet production at the Large Hadron Collider and find good agreement between the prediction based on maximal entanglement within the jet and the data. This study represents the first use of the quantum entanglement framework in the experimental study of the hadronization process, offering a new perspective on the transition from perturbative to non-perturbative QCD. Our results open the door to a more comprehensive understanding of the quantum nature of hadronization.

## Key claim

The paper extends the maximal entanglement framework, previously applied to proton structure and minimum-bias collisions, to jet hadronization, predicting a relation between the jet fragmentation function and the entropy of hadrons produced in jet fragmentation. The authors find "good agreement" between this prediction and ATLAS data, but the agreement depends on the specific choice of fragmentation functions (JAM or NNFF1.1) and the hard scale used, with uncertainties that become larger at lower z values.

## Method

The authors derived an expression for the entanglement entropy of a jet from fragmentation functions (FFs) by assuming maximal entanglement, adding a constant term from the initial parton color state (ln(N_c) for quarks, ln(N_c^2-1) for gluons). They computed this entropy using JAM and NNFF1.1 fragmentation functions at next-to-leading order (NLO) for light quarks and gluons, then compared it to the hadron entropy extracted from charged-particle multiplicity distributions in jets measured by the ATLAS experiment at sqrt(s)=7 TeV and 13 TeV. The mapping between jet transverse momentum and the average momentum fraction <z> was determined through PYTHIA simulations.

## Key numbers

- ATLAS data at sqrt(s)=7 TeV: jet pT range 4-40 GeV, charged hadron pT > 0.3 GeV, rapidity |y| < 1.9
- ATLAS data at sqrt(s)=13 TeV: jet pT range 100-2500 GeV, charged hadron pT > 0.5 GeV, rapidity |y| < 2.1
- Entropy calculations used JAM FFs at NLO with mu^2 = 1300 GeV^2 for 13 TeV comparison and mu^2 = 22 GeV^2 for 7 TeV comparison
- Initial parton entropy: S_q = ln(3) for quarks (N_c=3), S_g = ln(8) for gluons (N_c^2-1=8)
- EIC prediction: PYTHIA simulation with 18 GeV electrons and 275 GeV protons, Q^2 > 10 GeV^2, jet radius 0.4, jet pT range 5-100 GeV, pseudorapidity -3.5 < eta < 3.5

## Context

This work extends the maximal entanglement framework previously established for proton structure at high energies by Kharzeev (2017) and experimentally confirmed in minimum-bias pp collisions by Tu (2019) and in inclusive and diffractive DIS by Hentschinski (2021-2024). It builds on the concept of local parton-hadron duality from Azimov (1985) and the crossing symmetry relation between PDFs and FFs from Gribov (1971). The results enable a new quantum information approach to studying the nonperturbative hadronization process, complementing perturbative QCD calculations, and motivate future measurements at the Electron-Ion Collider to constrain the initial conditions of jet fragmentation.
