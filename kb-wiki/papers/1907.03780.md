# Linearly polarized gluons at next-to-next-to leading order and the Higgs transverse momentum distribution

> We calculate the small-b (or large-qT) matching of transverse momentum dependent (TMD) distribution for linearly polarized gluons to the integrated gluon distributions at the next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). This is the last missing part for the complete NNLO prediction of the Higgs spectrum within TMD factorization. We discuss the numerical impact of the correction so derived to the qT-differential cross-section for Higgs boson production and to the positivity bound for linearly polarized gluon transverse momentum distribution.

## Key claim
The authors calculate the previously unknown next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) matching coefficient for the linearly polarized gluon transverse momentum dependent parton distribution function (lpTMDPDF) onto collinear parton distribution functions, which is the last missing piece for a complete NNLO prediction of the Higgs boson transverse momentum spectrum within TMD factorization; the numerical impact of this correction on the Higgs cross-section and on the positivity bound for the lpTMDPDF is discussed, but the abstract does not specify that the positivity bound is easily violated by loop corrections and could only be restored by non-perturbative effects.

## Method
The authors compute the two-loop (NNLO) parton-level matrix elements for the gluon TMD operator using dimensional regularization and \(\delta\)-regularization for rapidity divergences, following the same techniques as in previous NNLO evaluations for unpolarized gluons and pretzelosity distributions. The resulting unsubtracted expressions are renormalized using the known TMD renormalization factors and soft factor, and then matched to collinear PDFs via the operator product expansion (OPE) to extract the matching coefficients \(\delta^L C_{g\leftarrow f}\). The final finite parts of the coefficient functions are presented in closed analytic form.

## Key numbers
* The NLO matching coefficient for gluon-to-gluon is \(\delta^L C^{(1,0,0)}_{g\leftarrow g}(x) = -C_A \frac{4(1-x)}{x}\), with \(C_A = N_c = 3\).
* The NLO matching coefficient for gluon-to-quark is \(\delta^L C^{(1,0,0)}_{g\leftarrow q}(x) = -C_F \frac{4(1-x)}{x}\), with \(C_F = 4/3\).
* The NNLO finite part for gluon-to-gluon (Eq. C2gg) contains terms proportional to \(C_A^2\), \(C_F N_f\), and \(C_A N_f\), e.g., \(C_A^2 \left[ -16 \frac{1-x}{x}(\text{Li}_2(x)-\ln x) + \frac{124}{3}\ln x + \left(\frac{148}{9}+20\zeta_2\right)\frac{1-x}{x} -8\ln^2 x - \frac{100}{9}(1-x) - \frac{4}{9}x(11x-14) \right]\).
* The NNLO finite part for gluon-to-quark (Eq. C2gq) contains terms proportional to \(C_F(C_F-C_A)\), \(C_F C_A\), and \(C_F N_f\), e.g., \(C_F(C_F-C_A)\left[8\frac{1-x}{x}(\ln(1-x)+\ln^2(1-x))-20\ln x+4\ln^2 x+8(1-x)\right]\).

## Context
This work extends the NNLO matching calculation for the unpolarized gluon TMDPDF (Gehrmann et al., 2014; Echevarria et al., 2016) to the linearly polarized gluon case, which was previously known only at one-loop order (Becher et al., 2012; Echevarria et al., 2015; Gutierrez-Reyes et al., 2017). The result enables complete NNLO predictions for Higgs boson production within TMD factorization and is also relevant for other processes sensitive to lpTMDPDF, such as heavy-quark production at the LHC, EIC, or LHeC; it also allows a test of the positivity bound \(|h_{1,g}^\perp|/|f_{1,g}| \leq 1\) derived by Mulders and Rodrigues (2000), which the authors find is violated by loop corrections but could be restored by non-perturbative contributions.
