# Expansion, Thermalization and Entropy Production in High-Energy Nuclear Collisions

> The thermalization process is studied in an expanding parton gas using the Boltzmann equation with two types of collision terms. In the relaxation time approximation we determine the criteria under which a time-dependent relaxation time leads to thermalization of the partons. We calculate the entropy production due to collisions for the general time-dependent relaxation time. In a perturbative QCD approach on the other hand, we can estimate the parton collision time and its dependence on expansion time. The effective `out of equilibrium' collision time differs from the standard transport relaxation time, $τ_{\rm tr}\simeq(α_s^2\ln(1/α_s)T)^{-1}$, by a weak time dependence. It is in both cases Debye screening and Landau damping that regulate the singular forward scattering processes. We find that the parton gas does thermalize eventually but only after having undergone a phase of free streaming and gradual equilibration where considerable entropy is produced (``after-burning"). The final entropy and thus particle density depends on the collision time as well as the initial conditions (a ``memory effect"). Results for entropy production are presented based upon various model estimates of early parton production.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a parton gas produced in high-energy nuclear collisions does thermalize eventually, but only after a phase of free streaming and gradual equilibration ("after-burning") during which considerable entropy is produced; the final entropy and particle density depend on both the collision time and the initial conditions (a "memory effect"), contrary to the abstract's implication that thermalization is simply achieved.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann equation for a one-dimensionally expanding parton gas using two approaches: (1) a relaxation time approximation with a time-dependent relaxation time parametrized as θ ∝ τ^p, and (2) a perturbative QCD calculation of the full collision term to estimate the effective collision time and entropy production during the free-streaming phase. They match the entropy production from the perturbative QCD approach to that from the relaxation time approximation to determine an effective, momentum-averaged relaxation time.

## Key numbers

- For a constant relaxation time (p=0), the final entropy ratio is S_f/S_0 = 1.16 (θ_0/τ_0)^(1/4) (Baym result, adjusted by factor (π/4)^(3/4) for different initial conditions).
- The condition for thermalization and hydrodynamic expansion is p < 1, where θ = θ_0 (τ/τ_0)^p.
- From HIJING model at RHIC: τ_0 = 0.7 fm/c, T_0 = 0.57 GeV, λ_{0,g} = 0.09, λ_{0,q} = 0.02.
- From HIJING model at LHC: τ_0 = 0.5 fm/c, T_0 = 0.83 GeV, λ_{0,g} = 0.14, λ_{0,q} = 0.03.
- The approximate collision time at τ_0 is θ_0 ≈ α_s^(-2) λ_{0,g}^(-1) τ_0.
- For α_s ≈ 0.3, the final entropy increase factor is S_f/S_0 ≈ α_s^(-1/2) λ_{0,g}^(-1/4), giving a factor of 2-3 when λ_0 varies from 1 to 0.1.

## Context

This work extends the classic Bjorken hydrodynamic model (Bjorken, 1983) and the relaxation time solution of Baym (1984) by incorporating a time-dependent relaxation time and perturbative QCD estimates of collision times. It contrasts with the earlier assumption that hydrodynamic expansion begins at τ_0 ≈ 1 fm/c, showing instead that free streaming dominates at early times. The results enable more realistic modeling of the pre-equilibrium phase in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and provide a framework for comparing with parton cascade models such as Geiger (1992) and the HIJING model of Wang and collaborators.
