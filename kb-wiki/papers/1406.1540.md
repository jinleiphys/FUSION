# Double-pole nature of $Λ(1405)$ studied with coupled-channel complex scaling method using complex-range Gaussian basis

> The excited hyperon $Λ(1405)$ is the important building block for kaonic nuclei which are nuclear many-body system with anti-kaons. We have been investigating the $Λ(1405)$ resonance with the coupled-channel Complex Scaling Method (ccCSM) in which the $Λ(1405)$ is treated as a hadron-molecular state of a $\bar{K}N$-$πΣ$ coupled system. We use a $\bar{K}N$(-$πY$) potential based on the chiral SU(3) theory. In this article, we report the double-pole nature of the $Λ(1405)$, which is a characteristic property predicted by many studies with chiral SU(3)-based models. With the help of the complex-range Gaussian basis in ccCSM, we have found successfully another pole besides a pole near the $\bar{K}N$ threshold (called higher pole) which was found in our previous work with the real-range Gaussian basis. The new pole (called lower pole) is found far below $\bar{K}N$ threshold: $(M, -Γ/2)=(1395, -138)$ MeV when $f_π=110$ MeV. In spite of so broad width of the lower-pole state, the state is clearly identified with good separation from continuum states, since the oscillatory behavior of the continuum states is improved owing to the complex-range Gaussian basis. Analyzing the ccCSM wave function of the lower pole, we have revealed explicitly that the lower-pole state is dominated by the $πΣ$ component rather than the $\bar{K}N$ component. We have confirmed that the ccCSM wave function is correctly connected to the asymptotic form of the resonance wave function. We have estimated the meson-baryon mean distance for the lower-pole state as well as for the higher-pole state. In addition, we have investigated the origin of the appearance of the lower pole. The lower pole is confirmed to be generated by the energy dependence attributed to the chiral dynamics, by comparing the result of an energy-independent potential.

## Key claim

The paper confirms the double-pole nature of the Λ(1405) resonance using the coupled-channel Complex Scaling Method (ccCSM) with a complex-range Gaussian basis, finding a second pole (lower pole) far below the \(\bar{K}N\) threshold at \((M, -\Gamma/2) = (1395, -138)\) MeV when \(f_\pi = 110\) MeV, which was not clearly identified in previous work using a real-range Gaussian basis.

## Method

The authors applied the coupled-channel Complex Scaling Method (ccCSM) to the \(s\)-wave, isospin-zero \(\bar{K}N\)-\(\pi\Sigma\) coupled system, using a chiral SU(3)-based potential (non-relativistic versions NRv1 and NRv2) with Gaussian form in coordinate space. They expanded the complex-scaled wave function using complex-range Gaussian basis functions (with imaginary range parameter \(\omega\)) instead of the usual real-range Gaussian basis, which improved the description of oscillatory behavior at large distances and allowed clear identification of broad resonances. The complex energy of resonance poles was determined self-consistently with the energy-dependent potential, and the wave functions were compared to their asymptotic forms to verify reliability.

## Key numbers

- Lower pole complex energy: \((M, -\Gamma/2) = (1395.2, -137.9)\) MeV for NRv2 potential with \(f_\pi = 110\) MeV and \(\omega = 2.0\) at \(\theta = 40^\circ\).
- Higher pole complex energy: \((M, -\Gamma/2) = (1417.9, -16.6)\) MeV under the same conditions.
- Lower pole mass varies from \(\sim 1350\) MeV (at \(f_\pi = 90\) MeV) to 1424.7 MeV (at \(f_\pi = 120\) MeV); half-width varies from \(\sim -66\) MeV to \(-163.2\) MeV.
- Higher pole mass is stable around 1418-1420 MeV and half-width around \(-14\) to \(-23\) MeV across \(f_\pi = 90-120\) MeV.
- Connection points for asymptotic wave function: \(s_0^{(\bar{K}N)} = 5.96\) fm and \(s_0^{(\pi\Sigma)} = 4.66\) fm for the lower pole.
- Scaling angle \(\theta = 40^\circ\) was required to identify the lower pole; \(\theta\) trajectory shows stability at \(\theta \geq 39^\circ\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the Λ(1405) double-pole structure predicted by chiral SU(3)-based models (e.g., Kaiser, Weise, and Meissner; Jido et al.; Hyodo and Weise) by providing a direct wave-function analysis at the complex-energy pole rather than on the real-energy axis. The authors' earlier ccCSM study (Dote and Myo, 2013) using a real-range Gaussian basis could only identify the higher pole near the \(\bar{K}N\) threshold; the complex-range Gaussian basis, proposed by Kamimura et al. for \(^{12}\)C cluster states, enabled clear identification of the broad lower pole. This work enables future studies of kaonic nuclei (e.g., \(K^-pp\)) by establishing a method to treat broad resonances in coupled-channel systems with chiral dynamics.
