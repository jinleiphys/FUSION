# Effects of scalar mesons in a Skyrme model with hidden local symmetry

> We study the effects of light scalar mesons on the skyrmion properties by constructing and examining a mesonic model including pion, rho meson, and omega meson fields as well as two-quark and four-quark scalar meson fields. In our model, the physical scalar mesons are defined as mixing states of the two- and four-quark fields. We first omit the four-quark scalar meson field from the model and find that when there is no direct coupling between the two-quark scalar meson and the vector mesons, the soliton mass is smaller and the soliton size is larger for lighter scalar mesons; when direct coupling is switched on, as the coupling strength increases, the soliton becomes heavy, and the radius of the baryon number density becomes large, as the repulsive force arising from the $ω$ meson becomes strong. We then include the four-quark scalar meson field in the model and find that mixing between the two-quark and four-quark components of the scalar meson fields also affects the properties of the soliton. When the two-quark component of the lighter scalar meson is increased, the soliton mass decreases and the soliton size increases.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including light scalar mesons (both two-quark and four-quark states) in a Skyrme model with hidden local symmetry modifies the soliton mass and size, but the resulting soliton mass remains about 300 MeV larger than needed to reproduce empirical nucleon mass and Delta-N mass splitting, indicating the model requires further extensions.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral effective Lagrangian including pion, rho meson, omega meson, and scalar meson fields, where the physical scalar mesons f500 and f1370 are treated as mixing states of two-quark (sigma) and four-quark (phi) fields. They solve the equations of motion for the profile functions F(r), G(r), W(r), sigma-bar(r), and phi-bar(r) under the hedgehog ansatz for baryon number B=1, using parameters fixed from meson dynamics with f_pi = 92.4 MeV and m_pi = 139.57 MeV. The study systematically varies the scalar meson mass, the coupling parameter s0 between scalar and vector mesons, and the mixing angle theta between the two-quark and four-quark components.

## Context

This work extends previous Skyrme model studies that included vector mesons via hidden local symmetry (Harada 2003) and dilaton scalar mesons as Nambu-Goldstone bosons of scale symmetry (Park 2003, 2008; Ma 2013). The authors find that even with both two-quark and four-quark scalar mesons, the soliton mass remains too large, suggesting future extensions should include dilaton effects, O(p^4) terms of hidden local symmetry accounting for pi-rho interactions, and all homogeneous Wess-Zumino terms. The model is intended for future application to dense matter systems to study phase structure of nuclear matter.
