# The Salpeter plasma correction for solar fusion reactions

> We review five different derivations that demonstrate that the Salpeter formula for the plasma corrections to fusion rates is valid at the center of the sun with insignificant errors (~ percent). We point out errors in several recent papers that have obtained a variety of answers, some even with the wrong sign or the wrong functional dependence.

## Key claim

The Salpeter formula for plasma screening corrections to solar fusion rates is valid at the center of the sun with insignificant errors (approximately percent level), and any calculation that claims a different result must first demonstrate fatal errors in each of five independent derivations that all yield the same formula.

## Method

The authors review five distinct derivations of the Salpeter screening formula: the original electrostatic argument (Salpeter 1954), a WKB barrier penetration calculation (Bahcall, Chen, and Kamionkowski 1998), a density matrix evaluation using quantum statistical mechanics (Gruzinov and Bahcall 1998), a free-energy calculation (Dewitt, Graboske, and Cooper 1973), and a quantum field theory derivation (Brown and Sawyer 1997). They then identify specific errors in five alternative non-Salpeter screening calculations from the recent literature, including incorrect treatment of dynamic screening, misinterpretation of the Gibbs distribution, improper handling of cloud-cloud interactions, use of unconventional statistics, and a sign error in the Tsytovich suppression formula.

## Key numbers

- Plasma coupling parameter at the solar center: g = 0.04, where g = (e^2 / D)(1 / kT) with D the Debye length and T the plasma temperature.
- Higher-order screening corrections from the density matrix calculation are of order 1% for all important solar fusion reactions.
- The deviation from thermodynamic equilibrium in the solar core, delta = tau_Coulomb / tau_nuclear, is approximately 10^{-28} for typical parameters (tau_nuclear = 10^{10} yr, E = 20 keV, rho = 150 g cm^{-3}).
- Characteristic nuclear burning times for the most important solar fusion reactions range from 10^2 yr to 10^{10} yr.

## Context

This paper responds to a series of recent claims (Carraro, Schafer, and Koonin 1988; Shaviv and Shaviv 1996, 1997, 2000; Savchenko 1999; Tsytovich 2000; Opher and Opher 2000a, 2000b; Lavagno and Quarati 2000) that the standard Salpeter screening formula (Salpeter 1954) needs replacement, with some authors suggesting large changes that could affect solar neutrino predictions. The authors demonstrate that these alternative results are all incorrect and that the Salpeter formula remains the correct weak-screening limit, enabling solar modelers to confidently use the standard screening correction rather than invoking plasma effects to explain the solar neutrino problem.
