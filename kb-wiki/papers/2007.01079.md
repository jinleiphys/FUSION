# Instanton-motivated study of spontaneous fission of odd-A nuclei

> Using the idea of the instanton approach to quantum tunneling we try to obtain a method of calculating spontaneous fission rates for nuclei with the odd number of neutrons or protons. This problem has its origin in the failure of the adiabatic cranking approximation which serves as the basis in calculations of fission probabilities. Selfconsistent instanton equations, with and without pairing, are reviewed and then simplified to non-selfconsistent versions with phenomenological single-particle potential and seniority pairing interaction. Solutions of instanton-like equations without pairing and actions they produce are studied for the Woods-Saxon potential along realistic fission trajectories. Actions for unpaired particles are combined with cranking actions for even-even cores and fission hindrance for odd-A nuclei is studied in such a hybrid model. With the assumed equal mass parameters for neighbouring odd-A and even-even nuclei, the model shows that freezing the K π configuration leads to a large overestimate of the fission hindrance factors. Actions with adiabatic configurations mostly show not enough hindrance; instanton-like actions for blocked nucleons correct this, but not sufficiently.

## Key claim

The paper attempts to replace the adiabatic cranking approximation for spontaneous fission of odd-A nuclei with a non-adiabatic scheme motivated by the instanton method, but the resulting hybrid model, combining instanton-like actions for the odd nucleon with cranking actions for the even-even core, still underestimates the experimentally observed fission hindrance factors (three to five orders of magnitude) and does not yet provide a quantitative understanding.

## Method

The authors solve the imaginary-time-dependent Schrödinger equation (iTDSE) with a phenomenological Woods-Saxon single-particle potential along realistic fission trajectories, using the microscopic-macroscopic energy for the potential and the cranking mass parameter of the neighboring even-even nucleus to prescribe the collective velocity. They then combine these instanton-like solutions for the odd nucleon (without pairing) with the adiabatic cranking action (with pairing) for the even-even core in a hybrid model to study fission hindrance in odd-A nuclei, comparing results obtained with and without constraining the Ωπ ground-state configuration.

## Key numbers

- Spontaneous fission rates of odd nuclei are experimentally three to five orders of magnitude smaller than those of their even-even neighbours (reviewed in Hess).
- Typical cranking mass parameter for the Woods-Saxon potential is Bqq ≳ 200 ℏ²/MeV, and the fission barrier is ≲ 7 MeV, giving ℏq̇ ≲ 0.25 MeV for heavy nuclei.
- The lowest two-quasiparticle excitation in even-even nuclei has energy of at least twice the pairing gap 2Δ, which in heavy nuclei amounts to more than 1 MeV.
- The model shows that freezing the Kπ configuration leads to a large overestimate of the fission hindrance factors; actions with adiabatic configurations mostly show not enough hindrance, and instanton-like actions for blocked nucleons correct this but not sufficiently.

## Context

This work extends the instanton approach to nuclear fission formulated in the mean-field setting by LNP, Neg1, JS1, JS, and JS2, applying it to the long-standing problem of spontaneous fission in odd-A nuclei where the adiabatic cranking approximation fails due to near-singular mass parameters at avoided level crossings (as illustrated by Mirea (2019) for an odd nucleus). The approach enables future development of either a more refined non-selfconsistent method or ways to implement a selfconsistent instanton treatment of spontaneous fission half-lives, including odd-A nuclei and high-K isomers, but the present hybrid model still underestimates the experimentally observed hindrance.
