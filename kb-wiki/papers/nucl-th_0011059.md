# Charged particle rapidity distributions at RHIC

> Using a multiphase transport model (AMPT), which includes both initial partonic and final hadronic interactions, we study the rapidity distributions of charged particles such as protons, antiprotons, pions, and kaons in heavy ion collisions at RHIC. The theoretical results for the total charged particle multiplicity at midrapidity are consistent with those measured by the PHOBOS collaboration in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt s$ = 56 and 130 AGeV. We find that these hadronic observables are much more sensitive to the hadronic interactions than the partonic interactions.

## Key claim

The AMPT model, with parameters fixed using SPS data, reproduces the PHOBOS measurements of total charged particle multiplicity at midrapidity in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies of 56 and 130 AGeV, but the authors note that these hadronic observables are much more sensitive to final-state hadronic interactions than to initial partonic interactions, and that the results are consistent with none or a weak jet quenching at 130 AGeV.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model, which combines initial conditions from the HIJING model with parton cascade via Zhang's Parton Cascade (ZPC) for gluon elastic scatterings, Lund string fragmentation for hadronization, and the ART (A Relativistic Transport) model for final-state hadronic interactions. Model parameters in the Lund string fragmentation (a and b parameters) were adjusted to fit central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energy of 17 AGeV, then applied without change to Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies of 56 and 130 AGeV.

## Key numbers

- Total charged particle multiplicity at midrapidity from AMPT is consistent with PHOBOS data for central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 56 and 130 AGeV.
- Without final-state hadronic interactions, the antiproton-to-proton ratio at midrapidity is 0.80 and the K+/pi+ ratio is 0.13; with hadronic interactions, these become 0.66 and 0.18, respectively, for central Au+Au at 130 AGeV.
- Turning off the partonic cascade leads to less than about 5% change in final charged particle yields at sqrt(s) = 130 AGeV.
- Including jet quenching of dE/dx = 1 GeV/fm entails a 10% increase of the final yield of total charged particles at midrapidity at 130 AGeV.
- Without initial nuclear shadowing on parton production, the charged particle multiplicity at midrapidity at 130 AGeV increases by about 30%.
- The antiproton-to-proton ratio increases from about 0.1 at SPS (17 AGeV) to about 0.8 at RHIC energy of sqrt(s) = 200 AGeV.
- The K-/K+ ratio increases gradually from 0.7 at SPS to about 1.0 at sqrt(s) = 200 AGeV.
- Kaon yields in the default HIJING model are smaller than the AMPT final results by about 40% at SPS energies.

## Context

This work extends the HIJING model (which includes minijet production and jet quenching but no explicit parton or hadron interactions) and the LEXUS model (which overpredicted PHOBOS data) by incorporating both partonic and hadronic dynamics in a unified transport framework. The results enable future studies of observables more sensitive to partonic matter, such as J/psi suppression, elliptic flow, and high transverse momentum spectra, which the authors suggest may reveal the quark-gluon plasma expected at RHIC energies.
