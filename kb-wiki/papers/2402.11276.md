# The pervasiveness of shape coexistence in nuclear pair condensates

> We investigate nuclear shape coexistence for a wide range of even-even nuclides. By varying general pair condensates, which include Slater determinants as a limit but also allow for arbitrary pairing channels, we frequently find multiple coexisting mimina, and often more than two. This is consistent with recent experimental results. In order to measure general pairwise correlations beyond a simple Slater determinant, we introduce a novel entropy-like measure, which is smallest mid-shell and largest near shell closures; this is consistent with a picture of pairing-like behavior dominating near closed shells and deformation mid-shell. After surveying nuclides spanning from the $sd$ shell to nuclides between magic numbers 50 and 82, we focus on the six lightest nuclei with shape coexistence. Angular-momentum projected variational pair condensate (PVPC) calculations identify band structures, including two newly proposed coexisting bands in $^{26}$Si/Mg and $^{24}$Si/Ne. The PVPC results agree well with data, providing robust experimental support for the pervasiveness of coexistence in these light nuclei.

## Key claim

Shape coexistence is not rare but pervasive across a wide range of even-even nuclides, with many nuclei exhibiting more than two coexisting minima when general pair condensates (which include Slater determinants as a limit but allow arbitrary pairing channels) are varied; the abstract's claim of "frequently finding multiple coexisting minima" is hedged by the use of a phenomenological shell-model interaction for heavier nuclei that is "not finely tuned" and thus only "schematically demonstrates the possibility of coexistence."

## Method

The authors used variational pair condensates (VPC), a number-conserving approach that includes Slater determinants (Hartree-Fock) as a limit but also allows arbitrary pairing channels, to search for coexisting minima in even-even nuclides across four valence spaces (sd, pf, 20-50/50-82, and 50-82). For each nucleus, 1000 VPC runs with randomly initialized pair-structure coefficients were performed, and distinct minima were identified using three criteria: energy difference greater than 0.5 MeV, shape difference (in beta-gamma deformation space) greater than 0.05, and 0+ overlap less than 0.9. For the six lightest nuclei exhibiting two minima, angular-momentum projected VPC (PVPC) calculations were performed to generate low-lying level schemes and electric quadrupole transition rates.

## Key numbers

* For the six lightest nuclei with two minima (24Ne, 24,26Mg, 24-28Si), the VPC minima energies range from -130.1 MeV (28Si prolate minimum) to -67.1 MeV (24Si/Ne oblate minimum), with deformation parameters beta from 0.162 to 0.273 and gamma from 0 to 60 degrees.
* 28Si has two minima: one at -130.1 MeV with beta=0.195, gamma=60 degrees (oblate) and one at -122.8 MeV with beta=0.229, gamma=0 degrees (prolate), an energy difference of approximately 7 MeV.
* 24Mg has minima at -81.1 MeV (beta=0.273, gamma=10 degrees) and -75.7 MeV (beta=0.211, gamma=60 degrees).
* 26Si/Mg has minima at -100.1 MeV (beta=0.192, gamma=35 degrees) and -98.2 MeV (beta=0.224, gamma=0 degrees).
* 24Si/Ne has minima at -67.7 MeV (beta=0.162, gamma=32 degrees) and -67.1 MeV (beta=0.171, gamma=60 degrees).
* Nuclei with four or five minima ("super-coexistence") include 106Zr, 110,112,114Zr, 112Mo, 116Mo, 114Ru, 116Pd, 122,124Te, 126,130,132Ba, 126,130,132Ce, 134Ce, 136Sm, and 142Dy, with energies ranging from -12.1 MeV (114Ru) to -1.2 MeV (142Dy) and one-body entropies from 0.112 to 1.323.

## Context

This work extends the systematic theoretical study of shape coexistence beyond earlier density-functional calculations by Nomura (2017) and the macroscopic-microscopic global survey of shape isomers by Moller and collaborators, as well as the covariant density functional theory studies by the groups of Zhao (2021) and others. The VPC approach is more general than the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) method because it does not require time-reversal invariance of the pair condensate, and the authors show that allowing more pairing degrees of freedom yields more coexisting minima than either time-reversal-constrained VPC (TRVPC) or Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations. The PVPC results for the six lightest sd-shell nuclei agree well with experimental data, providing support for the pervasiveness of coexistence in light nuclei and enabling future systematic studies of band structures arising from shape coexistence across the nuclear chart.
