# Baryonic form factors of light pseudoscalar mesons

> Employing the Bethe-Salpeter formalism, we present a computation of the space-like baryonic form factor for the pion and kaon. In the exact isospin-symmetric limit this observable is forbidden by $G$-parity, so that any nonzero signal constitutes a direct probe of the quark mass difference $m_d - m_u$. The form factors are evaluated in the impulse approximation using fully dressed quark propagators, meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, and a dressed baryon-current vertex constrained by the vector Ward-Takahashi identity. The baryonic radius computed with this method for the pion is given by $\langle r_{\! B}^2\rangle_{π^+}^{1/2} = 0.043(2)$ fm, and is consistent with the available dispersive benchmarks. Our predictions for the kaons, namely $\langle r_{\!B}^2\rangle_{K^+}^{1/2} = 0.265(7)$ fm and $\langle r_{\!B}^2\rangle_{K^0}^{1/2} = 0.262(7)$ fm, indicate a larger spatial extent than in the pion case; these results have no dispersive counterparts, and are compatible with chiral QCD models.

# Baryonic form factors of light pseudoscalar mesons

## Key claim

The baryonic form factor of the pion, which vanishes in the exact isospin-symmetric limit, is computed to be nonzero due to the quark mass difference $m_d - m_u$; the result is consistent with dispersive benchmarks, while the kaon predictions have no dispersive counterparts and are compatible with chiral QCD models.

## Method

The Bethe-Salpeter formalism is employed in the impulse approximation using fully dressed quark propagators, meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, and a dressed baryon-current vertex constrained by the vector Ward-Takahashi identity. Separate quark gap equations are solved for the $u$, $d$, and $s$ flavours in an explicitly isospin-breaking setup, with the effective interaction taken from Gao (2024) without further adjustment. The baryon-current vertex is decomposed into a Ball-Chiu part that saturates the Ward-Takahashi identity and a transverse part that encodes vector-meson pole contributions.

## Key numbers

- Pion baryonic radius: $\langle r_{\! B}^2\rangle_{\pi^+}^{1/2} = 0.043(2)$ fm, consistent with the dispersive estimate of 0.041(1) fm from Sanchez-Puertas (2021)
- Charged kaon baryonic radius: $\langle r_{\!B}^2\rangle_{K^+}^{1/2} = 0.265(7)$ fm
- Neutral kaon baryonic radius: $\langle r_{\!B}^2\rangle_{K^0}^{1/2} = 0.262(7)$ fm
- Input quark masses: $m_u = 3.7$ MeV, $m_d = 6.2$ MeV, $m_s = 95$ MeV
- Computed pion mass: $m_{\pi} = 0.139$ GeV, decay constant: $f_{\pi} = 0.130$ GeV
- Computed charged kaon mass: $m_{K^{\pm}} = 0.493$ GeV, decay constant: $f_{K^{\pm}} = 0.157$ GeV
- Computed neutral kaon mass: $m_{K^0} = 0.499$ GeV, decay constant: $f_{K^0} = 0.157$ GeV
- Quark mass functions at origin: $\mathcal{M}_u(0)= 0.328$ GeV, $\mathcal{M}_d(0)= 0.341$ GeV, $\mathcal{M}_s(0)= 0.591$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the first quantitative study of the pion baryonic form factor by Sanchez-Puertas (2021), which used a constituent quark model and dispersive extraction from BaBar and KLOE data, and the kaon study by Broniowski (2021) using chiral quark models. The results are obtained within the Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter equation framework previously applied to light-meson electromagnetic form factors by Maris (1999), Cloet (2008), and Miramontes (2021), and to heavy-light systems by Gao (2024). The neutral kaon baryonic form factor is shown to satisfy $F_B^{K^0}(q^2) = -F_{\rm EM}^{K^0}(q^2)$ due to the flavour structure of the baryon-number current. The approach enables future inclusion of electromagnetic corrections via photonic loops, following the method of Miramontes (2022).
