# Quantum effects in the diffusion process to form a heavy nucleus in heavy-ion fusion reactions

> We discuss quantum effects in the diffusion process which is used to describe the shape evolution from the touching configuration of fusing two nuclei to a compound nucleus. Applying the theory with quantum effects to the case where the potential field, the mass and friction parameters are adapted to realistic values of heavy-ion collisions, we show that the quantum effects play significant roles at low temperatures which are relevant to the synthesis of superheavy elements.

## Key claim

Quantum effects in the diffusion process along a potential barrier, specifically the modified fluctuation-dissipation relation and non-Markovian colored noise, significantly enhance the compound nucleus formation probability at low temperatures (around 1 MeV or below) compared to classical diffusion theory, contrary to the high-temperature approximation used in prior work.

## Method

The authors developed a quantum diffusion theory incorporating a modified fluctuation-dissipation relation for a potential barrier (obtained by analytic continuation of the well formula) and a non-Markovian colored noise random force with a Gaussian cutoff function. They applied this theory to the \(^{100}\)Mo + \(^{100}\)Mo and \(^{110}\)Pd + \(^{110}\)Pd systems, using the liquid drop model for the potential, hydrodynamical mass, and one-body dissipation for friction, solving the Langevin equation for the separation distance with 100,000 trajectories initiated from the touching configuration.

## Key numbers

- The barrier curvature around the conditional saddle point is of the order of 1 MeV, and temperatures relevant to superheavy element synthesis are as low as 1 MeV or below.
- The cutoff energy in the spectral density of the heat bath was fixed to \(\Delta = 15\) MeV.
- The correlation function was shown for temperatures \(T = 0.5\) MeV (solid line) and \(T = 1.0\) MeV (dotted line), where non-Markovian effects are significant.
- The compound nucleus formation probability was calculated as a function of nuclear temperature for the \(^{100}\)Mo + \(^{100}\)Mo and \(^{110}\)Pd + \(^{110}\)Pd reactions, with quantum effects increasing the probability at low temperatures compared to classical diffusion theory.

## Context

This work extends the quantum diffusion theory developed by Takigawa et al. (2004) and Ayik et al. (2005), which previously used simplified potential and friction parameters, to realistic parameters for heavy-ion fusion. It contradicts the standard fluctuation-dissipation relation used in prior diffusion models for superheavy element formation by Aritomo (1999), Abe (2002), and Aritomo (2004), which assumed high-temperature limits. The results enable more accurate predictions of compound nucleus formation probabilities at low excitation energies, crucial for synthesizing superheavy elements, and motivate future extensions to multidimensional diffusion including mass partition to study competition between complete fusion and quasi-fission.
