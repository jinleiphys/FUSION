# Evolution of the nucleon structure in the lightest nuclei

> The evolution of the nucleon structure as a function of atomic mass A is considered for the first time for the lightest nuclei, D, 3H, 3He and 4He, with an approach based on the Bethe-Salpeter formalism. We show that the pattern of the oscillation of the structure functions ratio r^A(x) = F_2^A/F_2^N(D) varies with A by changing the position of the cross-over point x_3 in which r^A(x) = 1, unlike the pattern for nuclei with masses A larger than 4, where only the amplitude of the oscillation changes. In particular we find that the pattern of F_2(x) modifications is controlled with the values (1 - x_3) = 0.32 (D/N), 0.16 (3He/D) and 0.08 (4He/D). The obtained results follow from the relativistic consideration of the nuclear structure and allow us to define a whole class of modifications of the partonic distributions in the nucleon bound in a nucleus. The EMC effect is explained as a particular case of the considered class.

## Key claim

The pattern of the structure function ratio \(r^A(x) = F_2^A/F_2^{\rm N(D)}\) varies with atomic mass \(A\) for the lightest nuclei (D, \(^3\)H, \(^3\)He, \(^4\)He) by changing the position of the cross-over point \(x_3\) where \(r^A(x)=1\), unlike for nuclei with \(A > 4\) where only the amplitude of the oscillation changes. The EMC effect is explained as a particular case of a broader class of modifications of partonic distributions in bound nucleons.

## Method

The authors extended a Bethe-Salpeter formalism previously applied to the deuteron to three- and four-nucleon systems, deriving expressions for the nuclear structure function \(F_2^A\) in terms of structure functions of nuclear fragments and three-dimensional momentum distributions. The approach treats nuclear binding effects through the dependence of the bound-nucleon hadronic tensor on the relative time variable \(\tau_i\), leading to first-order derivative terms in the fragment structure functions. Numerical calculations were performed at fixed \(Q^2 = 10\) GeV\(^2\) using phenomenological momentum distributions from the literature and parametrizations of free proton and neutron structure functions.

## Key numbers

- \((1 - x_3) = 0.32\) for the ratio \(F_2^{\rm D}(x)/F_2^{\rm N}(x)\) (deuteron relative to free nucleon).
- \((1 - x_3) = 0.16\) for the ratio \(F_2^{A=3}(x)/F_2^{\rm D}(x)\) (helium-3 relative to deuteron).
- \((1 - x_3) = 0.08\) for the ratio \(F_2^{^4{\rm He}}(x)/F_2^{\rm D}(x)\) (helium-4 relative to deuteron).
- The calculated cross-over point for \(^4\)He relative to deuteron is \(x_3 = 0.913\).
- The calculated cross-over point for \(A=3\) relative to deuteron is \(x_3 = 0.845\), which agrees with the average \(x_3\)-value for nuclei in the range \(A = 9 \div 197\) found in Smirnov (1999) as \(0.84 \pm 0.01\).
- The displacement of \(x_3\) when replacing \(F_2^{\rm N}(x)\) with \(F_2^{\rm D}(x)\) for \(A=3\) is 0.08, eight times larger than the experimental error of \(\pm 0.01\) for \(\overline{x_3}\) in heavier nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter approach of Burov, Molochkov, and Smirnov (previous deuteron work) to \(A=3\) and \(A=4\) nuclei, providing the first consistent evaluation of nucleon structure evolution across the lightest nuclei. It contradicts the statement in Vanttinen (1998) that intrinsic nucleon structure is not much affected by nuclear binding, and instead supports the conclusions of Indumathi (1994) and Burov, Pervushin, and Smirnov (1995) that binding effects are crucial. The results enable future experimental tests at HERA or CEBAF to verify the predicted cross-over points for \(^3\)He and \(^4\)He, and suggest that the EMC effect in heavy nuclei can be understood as distortions of partonic distributions already present in \(^3\)He or \(^3\)H, modified by nuclear density effects.
