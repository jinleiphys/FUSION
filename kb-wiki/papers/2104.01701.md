# Neutrino energy reconstruction from semi-inclusive samples

> We study neutrino-nucleus charged-current reactions on finite nuclei for the situation in which an outgoing muon and a proton are detected in coincidence, i.e., we focus on semi-inclusive cross sections. We limit our attention to one-body current interactions (quasielastic scattering) and assess the impact of different nuclear effects in the determination of the neutrino energy. We identify the regions in phase space where the neutrino energy can be reconstructed relatively well, and study whether the cross section in those regions is significant. Our results indicate that it is possible to filter more than 50% of all events according to the muon and proton kinematics, so that for the DUNE and T2K fluxes the neutrino energy can be determined with an uncertainty of less than 1% and 3%, respectively. Furthermore, we find that the reconstructed neutrino energy does not depend strongly on how one treats the final-state interactions and is not much affected by the description of the initial state. On the other hand, the estimations of the uncertainty on the neutrino energy show important sensitivity to the modeling of the initial state.

## Key claim
For charged-current quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering with coincident detection of a muon and a proton (semi-inclusive CC1μ1p0π events), it is possible to filter more than 50% of events based on muon and proton kinematics such that the neutrino energy can be reconstructed with an uncertainty below 1% for the DUNE flux and below 3% for the T2K flux, provided the missing-energy distribution is dominated by a narrow, well-known region.

## Method
The authors computed semi-inclusive charged-current cross sections for neutrino-16O scattering within the impulse approximation, using one-body currents and restricting to quasielastic kinematics. They employed several nuclear models: relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA), a factorized spectral function approach (SFA), relativistic mean-field models with real optical potentials (rROP and ED-RMF), and a complex relativistic optical potential (ROP) that restricts the final-state proton to elastic-only propagation. For each model, they evaluated the flux-averaged six-fold differential cross section as a function of final muon and proton kinematics, then computed the average reconstructed neutrino energy and its standard deviation using the first and second statistical moments of the energy distribution weighted by the cross section and the DUNE or T2K flux.

## Key numbers
- For the DUNE flux, more than 50% of events can be filtered so that the neutrino energy uncertainty is less than 1%.
- For the T2K flux, more than 50% of events can be filtered so that the neutrino energy uncertainty is less than 3%.
- The ratio of the rROP peak cross section to the RPWIA peak cross section is 0.84 for the DUNE flux and 0.82 for the T2K flux.
- The ratio of the ROP peak cross section to the rROP peak cross section is 0.71 for the DUNE flux and 0.75 for the T2K flux.
- The missing-energy regions for 16O are divided as: 0-16.5 MeV (p1/2 shell, occupation 1.51), 16.5-25 MeV (p3/2 shell, occupation 3.47), 25-100 MeV (s1/2 plus background, total occupation 2.22), and 100-300 MeV (background only, occupation 0.80).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of VanOrden (2019) by examining the full phase space for semi-inclusive neutrino-nucleus reactions and including multiple models for final-state interactions. It builds on prior studies by Mosel (2014), Furmanski (2017), and Munteanu (2020) that explored neutrino energy reconstruction using final-state hadron information. The results enable experimental collaborations such as DUNE and T2K to design event selection strategies that minimize systematic uncertainties from nuclear modeling, though the authors note that the uncertainty estimates themselves show important sensitivity to the description of the initial nuclear state.
