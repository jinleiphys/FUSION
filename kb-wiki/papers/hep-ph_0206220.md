# Heavy Quark Photoproduction in Ultra-peripheral Heavy Ion Collisions

> Heavy quarks are copiously produced in ultra-peripheral heavy ion collisions. In the strong electromagnetic fields, c c-bar and b b-bar are produced by photonuclear and two-photon interactions; hadroproduction can occur in grazing interactions. We present the total cross sections, quark transverse momentum and rapidity distributions, as well as the Q Q-bar invariant mass spectra from the three production channels. We consider AA and pA collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and Large Hadron Collider. We discuss techniques for separating the three processes and describe how the AA to pA production ratios might be measured accurately enough to study nuclear shadowing.

## Key claim

Heavy quarks (charm and bottom) are produced in ultra-peripheral heavy ion collisions via three channels (photonuclear, two-photon, and hadroproduction), and the authors calculate that the resolved photon component contributes 2-6% of the total photoproduction cross section at RHIC and 15-20% at the LHC, with the caveat that these results depend on the choice of quark mass (m_c = 1.2 GeV, m_b = 4.75 GeV) and parton distribution functions.

## Method

The authors performed leading-order (LO) perturbative QCD calculations of heavy quark photoproduction (direct and resolved), two-photon production, and hadroproduction in AA and pA collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. They used the Weizsäcker-Williams virtual photon flux, MRST LO parton distributions, EKS98 nuclear shadowing parameterization, and Woods-Saxon nuclear density distributions, integrating over impact parameter with the constraint that no hadronic interactions occur (b > 2R_A for AA collisions).

## Key numbers

- Charm photoproduction cross section in Au+Au at RHIC: approximately 3.1 mb (direct) with shadowing reducing it by about 10%
- Bottom photoproduction cross section in Pb+Pb at LHC: approximately 0.7 mb (direct) with shadowing reducing it by about 5%
- Resolved photoproduction contribution to charm at RHIC: approximately 2% of total; at LHC: approximately 15% of total
- Resolved photoproduction contribution to bottom at RHIC: approximately 6% of total; at LHC: approximately 20% of total
- Charm photoproduction cross section in p+Au at RHIC (S^i=1): approximately 0.15 mb per nucleon
- Bottom photoproduction cross section in p+Pb at LHC (S^i=1): approximately 0.03 mb per nucleon
- The AA to pA photoproduction cross section ratio for charm at RHIC is 22-37% lower than 2A times the pA cross section due to different minimum impact parameters
- The AA to pA photoproduction cross section ratio for bottom at RHIC is 37-65% lower than 2A times the pA cross section

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of heavy quark photoproduction by Baron and Baur (1992) and Greiner et al. (1993) by including resolved photon processes for the first time, using modern parton distribution functions (MRST LO instead of Duke-Owens), and considering both AA and pA collisions at RHIC and LHC. The results enable experimental separation of the three production channels using rapidity gaps and nuclear dissociation signatures, and suggest that comparing AA to pA photoproduction ratios could measure nuclear shadowing to better than 10% accuracy if the photon flux is calibrated using known processes like rho meson photoproduction from HERA.
