# Robust Regularity in γ-Soft Nuclei and its Microscopic Realization

> γ-softness in atomic nuclei is investigated in the framework of energy density functionals. By mapping constrained microscopic energy surfaces for a set of representative non-axial medium-heavy and heavy nuclei to a Hamiltonian of the proton-neutron interacting boson model (IBM-2) containing up to three-body interactions, low-lying collective spectra and transition rates are calculated. Observables are analyzed that distinguish between the two limiting geometrical pictures of non-axial nuclei: the rigid-triaxial rotor and the γ-unstable rotor. It is shown that neither of these pictures is realized in actual nuclei, and that a microscopic description leads to results that are almost exactly in between the two geometrical limits. This finding points to the optimal choice of the IBM Hamiltonian for γ-soft nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that typical non-axial medium-heavy and heavy nuclei are neither rigid-triaxial rotors nor gamma-unstable rotors, but instead lie almost exactly in between these two geometrical limits, a robust regularity that only emerges in the interacting boson model when a three-body boson interaction is included.

## Method

The authors performed constrained self-consistent mean-field calculations using both relativistic (DD-PC1) and non-relativistic (Skyrme SkM*) energy density functionals to generate quadrupole energy surfaces for a set of non-axial nuclei in the mass regions A~110, 130, and 190. These microscopic energy surfaces were then mapped onto the Hamiltonian of the proton-neutron interacting boson model (IBM-2) containing up to three-body interactions, and the resulting Hamiltonian was diagonalized to obtain low-lying collective spectra and E2 transition rates.

## Key numbers

- The Wilets-Jean (gamma-unstable) limit for the energy ratio S(4,3,2) is -2.00, and the Davydov-Filippov (rigid-triaxial) limit is 1.67; the IBM results with three-body terms fall between these values for all nuclei studied.
- The Wilets-Jean model predicts 2.50 and the Davydov-Filippov model predicts -2.33 for the energy ratio S(5,4,3).
- The B(E2;3+1→2+2)/B(E2;2+1→0+1) ratio saturates between the gamma-rigid limit of 1.78 and the gamma-unstable limit of 1.19 for nuclei with NπNν ≥ 12.
- For 190Os, inclusion of the three-body term lowers the 3+1 state by 473 keV and the 5+1 state by 663 keV compared to the two-body IBM calculation.
- The ratio |θ3/κ| is approximately 1 for total boson numbers 1 to 5, and approximately 0.5 for total boson numbers 5 to 10.

## Context

This work extends the framework of energy density functionals (using both relativistic functionals like DD-PC1 and non-relativistic ones like Skyrme SkM*) to address the longstanding question of whether axially-asymmetric nuclei are gamma-rigid or gamma-unstable, previously studied through the rigid-triaxial rotor model of Davydov and Filippov (1958) and the gamma-unstable rotor model of Wilets and Jean (1956). The results enable a fully microscopic determination of the optimal IBM Hamiltonian for gamma-soft nuclei and resolve the problem of the energy-level pattern of odd-spin states in the quasi-gamma band.
