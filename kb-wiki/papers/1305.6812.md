# Toroidal, compression, and vortical dipole strengths in $^{144-154}$Sm: Skyrme-RPA exploration of deformation effect

> A comparative analysis of toroidal, compressional and vortical dipole strengths in the spherical $^{144}$Sm and the deformed $^{154}$Sm is performed within the random-phase-approximation using a set of different Skyrme forces. Isoscalar (T=0), isovector (T=1), and electromagnetic excitation channels are considered. The role of the nuclear convection $j_{\text{con}}$ and magnetization $j_{\text{mag}}$ currents is inspected. It is shown that the deformation leads to an appreciable redistribution of the strengths and causes a spectacular deformation splitting (exceeding 5 MeV) of the isoscalar compressional mode. In $^{154}$Sm, the $μ$=0 and $μ$=1 branches of the mode form well separated resonances. When stepping from $^{144}$Sm to $^{154}$Sm, we observe an increase of the toroidal, compression and vortical contributions in the low-energy region (often called pygmy resonance). The strength in this region seems to be an overlap of various excitation modes. The energy centroids of the strengths depend significantly on the isoscalar effective mass $m_0$. Skyrme forces with a large $m_0$ (typically $m_0/m \approx 0.8 - 1$) seem to be more suitable for description of experimental data for the isoscalar giant dipole resonance.

## Key claim

Nuclear deformation in the transition from spherical $^{144}$Sm to deformed $^{154}$Sm causes a dramatic redistribution of toroidal, compression, and vortical dipole strengths, with the isoscalar compressional mode exhibiting a deformation splitting exceeding 5 MeV; however, the strength in the low-energy pygmy dipole resonance region appears to be an overlap of various excitation modes rather than a single pure mode.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent separable random-phase-approximation (SRPA) calculations using five different Skyrme forces (SkT6, SVbas, SkM*, SLy6, and SkI3) covering a range of isoscalar effective masses from $m_0/m = 0.58$ to 1.0. They computed toroidal, compressional, and vortical (Ravenhall-Wambach type) dipole strength functions in the isoscalar (T=0), isovector (T=1), and electromagnetic channels for spherical $^{144}$Sm and axially deformed $^{154}$Sm, using a configuration space with excitation energy cutoff at approximately 175 MeV and a 2D cylindrical coordinate mesh of 0.3 fm spacing.

## Key numbers

- Deformation splitting of the isoscalar compressional mode exceeds 5 MeV in $^{154}$Sm, reduced from approximately 10 MeV in the HFB approximation to about 5 MeV in the RPA
- The quadrupole deformation parameter for $^{154}$Sm is $\beta = 0.33-0.34$, with internal quadrupole moment $Q = 6.4-6.6$ b, in agreement with experimental values $\beta_{\text{exp}} = 0.34$ and $Q_{\text{exp}} = 6.6$ b (Raman 1987)
- The ISGDR energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) values for CM(T=0) range from 24.3 to 25.1 $\times 10^3$ $e^2$fm$^6$ MeV (ISGDR formula) and RPA values from 23.1 to 25.4 $\times 10^3$ $e^2$fm$^6$ MeV for $^{144}$Sm, and from 33.2 to 34.1 $\times 10^3$ $e^2$fm$^6$ MeV (ISGDR) and 32.6 to 34.8 $\times 10^3$ $e^2$fm$^6$ MeV (RPA) for $^{154}$Sm
- Experimental ISGDR in $^{144}$Sm shows two bumps at energies E=14.2 MeV (width $\Gamma$=4.8 MeV) and E=25.0 MeV (width $\Gamma$=19.9 MeV) from Itoh (2003) and Uchida (2004)
- The spurious E1(T=0) peak in spherical $^{144}$Sm lies at 1.5-2.0 MeV depending on the Skyrme parametrization

## Context

This work extends previous SRPA explorations of toroidal, compression, and vortical strengths in $^{208}$Pb (Kvasil 2011) and Sn isotopes (Kvasil 2013) to the Sm isotopic chain, specifically examining the effect of quadrupole deformation. The study addresses the ongoing debate about the nature of the low-energy isoscalar giant dipole resonance, where earlier work by Bastrukov (1993), Vretenar (2002), and Kvasil (2003) associated the low-energy bump with toroidal vortical flow, while Vretenar (2000) and Colo (2000) attributed the high-energy peak to compressional vibrations. The results enable future comparisons with $(\alpha,\alpha')$ experiments on deformed nuclei and motivate more advanced treatments incorporating coupling to complex configurations, such as the quasiparticle-phonon model (Soloviev 1992) or relativistic time-blocking approximation (Litvinova 2008).
