# Approximate boost of the relativistic two-body problem in instant formalism

> Considering two spinless particles, a simple, approximate boost rule is derived that is sufficient to keep the mass invariant and to relate interactions, vertex functions, wave functions and t-matrices of the instant two-body problem in an arbitrary frame to those in the c.m. frame. The boost generator ${\bf K}$ that is used provides a solution to the Poincaré algebra except for the commutator between two components of ${\bf K}$, for which it provids an approximate solution.

## Key claim
A simple, approximate boost rule for the instant two-body problem with spinless particles is derived that keeps the mass eigenvalue constant and relates interactions, vertex functions, wave functions, and t-matrices in an arbitrary frame to those in the center-of-mass frame, but the boost generator provides only an approximate solution to the Poincaré algebra because the commutator between two components of the boost generator has an error term of order 1/m².

## Method
The author starts from the instant form of relativistic quantum mechanics for two spinless particles with equal masses, where the Hamiltonian is H = ε₁ + ε₂ + v. By taking a diagonal matrix element of the commutation relation [K, H] = iP in an eigenstate of H with eigenvalue E_P, and using the eigenvalue condition to eliminate v² terms, a linear equation for the interaction v is obtained. This equation is solved in momentum space by factorizing v into a product of a function f(p;P) and the center-of-mass frame interaction v_c evaluated with Lorentz-contracted relative momenta p_c, where the relative momentum components perpendicular to the total momentum are unchanged while the parallel component is contracted by γ = E_P/M.

## Key numbers
- The boost generator K has an error term of order 1/m² in the commutator [K_i, K_j] = -iε_ijk J_k.
- The relative momentum transformation is p_cz = p_z/γ, where γ = E_P/M, and p_c⊥ = p_⊥.
- For relative momenta |p| < m and |p'| < m, the ratio |Δ|/E_P = |2p·P|/[(ε₁+ε₂)E_P] is much less than 1, and f ≈ M/E_P.
- The phase space transformation gives d³p = (E_P/M) d³p_c.

## Context
This work extends the Bakamjian-Thomas (1953) approach to the two-body boost problem in instant-form dynamics, which introduced the interaction in a mass operator but produced a nonlinear relation between interactions in different frames. It provides a simpler alternative to the linear boost relations of Foldy (1961) and Krajcik (1974) that worked to leading order in an expansion in powers of momenta to mass. The result is similar to the approximate boost rule of Coester, Pieper, and Serduke (1975) but differs in the f factors and provides an exactly constant mass eigenvalue, whereas the Coester et al. rule does not. The boost rule enables form factor calculations in the equal-time formalism where initial or final states have nonzero total momentum.
