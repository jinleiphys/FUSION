# Compressibility and equation of state of finite nuclei

> We present a new approach for calculating the nuclear equation of state and compressibility for finite nuclei using the density-constrained Hartree-Fock method.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new method for calculating the equation of state and compressibility of finite nuclei using the density-constrained Hartree-Fock (DCHF) method, but the results depend on the choice of reference density (central density vs. 0.16 fm⁻³) and the incompressibility values are about 5-10% higher if the full EOS curve is used in the fit rather than a quadratic expansion around equilibrium.

## Method

The authors apply a scale transformation to the ground-state density profile of spherical nuclei, compressing or stretching the density by a factor s (typically 0.8 to 1.2), then renormalize to preserve the correct mass number. For each scaled density, they perform a density-constrained Hartree-Fock calculation using the Skyrme SLy4 and SkM* forces, extracting the binding energy per particle as a function of density. The incompressibility coefficient K_A is obtained by fitting a quadratic expansion of the EOS around the equilibrium density, and the symmetry energy is extracted by comparing EOS curves for nuclei with different isospin asymmetry α = (N-Z)/A.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁶O with SLy4 and SkM* forces (no Coulomb): K_A = 116 MeV
- For ⁴⁰Ca with SLy4 and SkM* forces (no Coulomb): K_A = 141 MeV
- For ⁴⁸Ca with SLy4 (no Coulomb): K_A = 155 MeV
- For ⁶⁰Ca with SLy4 (no Coulomb): K_A = 136.5 MeV
- For ⁴⁰Ca with SLy4 (including Coulomb): K_A = 138.8 MeV
- For ⁴⁸Ca with SLy4 (including Coulomb): K_A = 147.7 MeV
- For ⁶⁰Ca with SLy4 (including Coulomb): K_A = 123.5 MeV
- For ⁴⁸Ca: symmetry energy parameters L = 66.6 MeV and K_sym = -403 MeV
- For ⁶⁰Ca: symmetry energy parameters L = 1.55 MeV and K_sym = -193 MeV
- Symmetry energy S(ρ) around equilibrium density is about 10-14 MeV for ⁴⁸Ca and ⁶⁰Ca, considerably lower than the nuclear matter value of 32 MeV for SLy4

## Context

This work extends the density-constrained Hartree-Fock method, previously used for shape resonances (Cusson and Reinhard, 1985; Umar and Strayer, 1985) and heavy-ion interaction potentials (Umar and Oberacker, 2006), to calculate the equation of state for finite nuclei. The results for incompressibility generally agree with empirical methods relating finite-nucleus compressibility to infinite nuclear matter (Ma and Stoitsov, 1995) and with values deduced from experimental giant monopole resonances (J.P. Blaizot, 1980). The method enables systematic study of the EOS and symmetry energy along isotope chains of exotic neutron-rich nuclei, which is increasingly relevant with the availability of radioactive ion beams.
