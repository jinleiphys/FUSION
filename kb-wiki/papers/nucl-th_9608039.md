# From $K^+$ in heavy-ion collisions to $K^-$ in kaonic atoms

> Kaon production and kaon flow in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies are analyzed using the relativistic transport model. The experimental data are found to be consistent with the scenario that kaon scalar (before being cut down by the range term) and vector mean field potentials are about 1/3 of that of nucleons in the Walecka model. This also explains the strong attraction found for $K^-$ in kaonic atoms, and gives information on the possibility of $K^-$ condensation in dense matter.

# From $K^+$ in heavy-ion collisions to $K^-$ in kaonic atoms

## Key claim

The experimental data on kaon production and flow in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies are consistent with the scenario that the kaon scalar and vector mean-field potentials are about 1/3 of those of nucleons in the Walecka model, *provided that the energy-dependent range term is included*, this correction is significant for $K^+$ but relatively unimportant for $K^-$, and it reconciles the apparently smaller scalar attraction seen in heavy-ion collisions with the stronger attraction observed in kaonic atoms.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic transport model, previously generalized to include kaon mean-field potentials, to reanalyze kaon production and flow in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies. They incorporated the kaon vector potential from the Weinberg-Tomozawa term and the scalar potential from the Kaplan-Nelson term, with the pion decay constant scaled in medium according to the Brown-Rho scaling relation ($f_\pi^{*2}/f_\pi^2 \approx 0.6$ at normal density), and included the energy-dependent range term correction. The calculations were compared to KaoS data for Au+Au collisions at 1 GeV/nucleon and FOPI data for Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93 GeV/nucleon, and the resulting $K^-$ potential was compared to kaonic atom data from Friedman, Gal and Batty.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear matter density $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$, the scaled pion decay constant gives $f_\pi^{*2}/f_\pi^2 \approx 0.6$, yielding a kaon vector potential $V_{K^+} \approx 88$ MeV and scalar potential $S_K \approx 100$ MeV.
- The nucleon mean-field potentials in the Walecka model are $V_N \approx 270$ MeV and $V_S \approx 330$ MeV, giving the relation $V_{K^+} \approx 1/3 V_N$ and $S_K \approx 1/3 S_N$.
- With the range term included, the overall kaon potential at $\rho_0$ is about 28 MeV, agreeing with the empirical value of 30 MeV from the impulse approximation using the isospin-averaged KN scattering length ${\bar a}_{KN} \approx -0.255$ fm.
- For $K^-$ at $\rho_0$, the scalar potential is $S_{K^-}(\rho_0) \approx -94$ MeV $(\rho_S/\rho_0)$ and the vector potential is $V_{K^-}(\rho_0) \approx -88$ MeV, giving a total $U_{K^-}(\rho_0) \approx -175$ MeV, consistent with the $-200 \pm 20$ MeV found by Friedman, Gal and Batty for a $K^-$ around $^{56}$Ni.
- The $KN$ sigma term used is $\Sigma_{KN} \approx 450 \pm 30$ MeV, based on lattice QCD calculations of nucleon strangeness content.

## Context

This work extends the effective chiral Lagrangian approach of Brown and Rho (1996), who showed that with a dropping pion decay constant in medium the kaon mean fields are about 1/3 of the nucleon mean fields in the Walecka model. It reconciles earlier transport model analyses by Fang et al. (1994) and Li et al. (1995), which used a smaller kaon potential, with the same experimental data by including both the scaled pion decay constant and the range term correction. The results also explain the strong $K^-$ attraction found in kaonic atoms by Friedman, Gal and Batty (1993), and provide input for calculations of kaon condensation in neutron stars as discussed by Lee (1995) and Brown and Rho (1996).
