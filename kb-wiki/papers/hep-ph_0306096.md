# Spontaneous CP Violation in the U(3) NJL Model

> With the help of the functional integration method, the formation of scalar, pseudoscalar condensates, and dynamical symmetry breaking in the U(3) four-fermion model has been investigated. We show the possibility of spontaneous CP symmetry violation in the model under consideration. The bosonization procedures of the model are performed; the propagators of quarks, scalar and pseudoscalar fields are calculated in one loop approximation. The masses of the scalar and pseudoscalar mesons are evaluated.

# Spontaneous CP Violation in the U(3) NJL Model

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that spontaneous CP symmetry violation can occur in the U(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model through the formation of pseudoscalar condensates, but only under the restrictive condition that the light quark current masses vanish (chiral limit $m_u = m_d = 0$), making the CP-violating scenario incompatible with nonzero current quark masses for the up and down quarks.

## Method

The author uses functional integration methods to bosonize the U(3) four-fermion interaction, introducing scalar and pseudoscalar auxiliary fields. The vacuum is shifted by constant scalar ($\sigma_0, \sigma_3, \sigma_8$) and pseudoscalar ($\widetilde{\sigma}_0, \widetilde{\sigma}_3, \widetilde{\sigma}_8$) condensates, and the effective potential is minimized to obtain gap equations. Quark propagators and meson inverse propagators are computed in the one-loop approximation using a cutoff regularization scheme, and the mass matrices for scalar and pseudoscalar meson nonets are derived from the quadratic terms in the effective action.

## Key numbers

- Constituent masses of light quarks (u, d) in the CP-conserving case: $m_1 = m_2 = 342.7$ MeV, obtained from the Goldberger-Treiman relation with $f_\pi = 93$ MeV and coupling $g = 3.628$
- Constituent mass of the strange quark: $m_3 = 570$ MeV, obtained with current strange quark mass $m_s = 166$ MeV
- Cutoff parameter: $\Lambda = e m_1 = 931.5$ MeV
- Pion mass: $m_\pi = 139$ MeV
- Kaon mass: $m_K = 494$ MeV
- Light quark condensate: $\langle\overline{u}u\rangle = \langle\overline{d}d\rangle = (-252\text{ MeV})^3$
- Strange quark condensate: $\langle\overline{s}s\rangle = (-268\text{ MeV})^3$
- Scalar meson masses (CP-conserving case): $m_{00} = 938$ MeV, $m_{11}=m_{22}=m_{33}=699$ MeV, $m_{44}=m_{55}=m_{66}=m_{77}=1013$ MeV, $m_{88}=1128$ MeV
- Pseudoscalar $\eta$-$\eta'$ mixing angle: $\theta_P = -35^\circ$
- Scalar meson mixing angle: $\theta_S = -35^\circ$

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study (Kruglov, 2003) from the SU(3) to the U(3) group, using standard cutoff regularization instead of a specific constraint between diverging integrals. The model builds on the instanton vacuum theory of Shuryak (1982) and the 't Hooft effective four-quark interaction (1976), as well as earlier NJL model studies by Bernard (1988) and Eguchi (1976). The paper shows that CP-violating condensates require the chiral limit for light quarks, and that the model without CP violation reproduces reasonable phenomenological values for pion and kaon masses and quark condensates. The $\eta$ and $\eta'$ masses are not correctly described because the $U_A(1)$ anomaly is not included; the author suggests generalizing the model with a six-quark determinant interaction (due to instantons) to break the $U_A(1)$ symmetry, following Bernard (1988).
