# Isospin dependent properties of the isotopic chain of Scandium and Titanium nuclei within the relativistic mean-field formalism

> The density-dependent nuclear symmetry energy is directly related to the isospin asymmetry for finite and infinite nuclear systems. It is critical to determine the coefficients of symmetry energy and its related observables as it holds great importance in different areas of nuclear physics, such as analyzing the structure of ground state exotic nuclei and neutron star study. The ground state bulk properties such as nuclear binding energy, quadrupole deformation, two-neutron separation energy, the differential variation of two-neutron separation energy, and root-mean-square charge radius for Scandium (Z = 21), and Titanium (Z = 22) nuclei are calculated. The coherent density fluctuation model is used to estimate the isospin-dependent properties of finite nuclei such as symmetry energy and its surface and volume components from its corresponding value in infinite nuclear matter system. Finally, we correlate the neutron-skin thickness with the coefficient of symmetry energy and its related observables corresponding to these isotopic chains of nuclei. The relativistic mean-field formalism with non-linear NL3 and Relativistic-Hartree-Bogoliubov with density-dependent DD-ME2 interaction parameters are employed in the present analysis. A shape transition is observed from spherical to prolate near N $\geq$ 44 and N $\geq$ 40 for Sc- and Ti- isotopic chain, respectively. Notable signatures of shell and/or sub-shell closures have been found for the magic neutron numbers at N = 20 and 28 for both the isotopic chain using the nuclear bulk and isospin quantities. In addition to these, a few signatures of shell/sub-shell closure are observed near the drip-line region, at N = 34 and 50 by following the surface/isospin dependent observables, namely symmetry energy and its component for both the isotopic chain of odd-A Sc- and even-even Ti- nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that isospin-dependent observables, specifically the symmetry energy and its surface and volume components derived via the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM), can reveal shell and sub-shell closures in neutron-rich Sc and Ti isotopes where traditional bulk properties (binding energy, separation energies) show only weak or ambiguous signatures, particularly at N = 34 and 50 near the drip-line region.

## Method

The authors employed the relativistic mean-field (RMF) formalism with the non-linear NL3 parameter set and the Relativistic-Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) approach with the density-dependent DD-ME2 interaction to calculate ground-state bulk properties (binding energy, quadrupole deformation, two-neutron separation energy, charge radius) for odd-A Sc (Z=21) and even-even Ti (Z=22) isotopic chains. They then used the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) to transform the nuclear matter symmetry energy into finite-nucleus symmetry energy, separating it into volume and surface components via the Danielewicz method, and correlated these with neutron-skin thickness.

## Key numbers

- Standard deviation of binding energy for NL3, DD-ME2, and FRDM predictions relative to experimental data: 3.66, 3.16, and 1.43 MeV for Sc isotopes; 2.47, 3.41, and 1.34 MeV for Ti isotopes.
- Shape transition from spherical to prolate observed near N >= 44 for Sc and N >= 40 for Ti isotopic chains.
- Shell/sub-shell closure signatures identified at magic neutron numbers N = 20 and 28 for both isotopic chains using bulk and isospin quantities.
- Additional shell/sub-shell closure signatures observed near the drip-line at N = 34 and 50 using surface/isospin dependent observables (symmetry energy and its components) for both Sc and Ti nuclei.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of shell evolution in the Ca region, particularly the emergence of N = 32 and 34 sub-shell closures discussed by Otsuka (2020) and Xu (2019), and experimental evidence from Steppenbeck (2013) for N = 34 in Ca isotopes. The study applies the CDFM approach, previously used by Bhuyan (2018) and Antonov (2004, 2009), to Sc and Ti isotopic chains, demonstrating that isospin-dependent quantities can reveal magicity where traditional bulk properties become ambiguous near the drip-line. This enables future systematic searches for new shell closures in neutron-rich nuclei using symmetry energy components.
