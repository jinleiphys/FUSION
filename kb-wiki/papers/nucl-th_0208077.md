# Periodic Orbits and Deformed Shell Structure

> Relationship between quantum shell structure and classical periodic orbits is briefly reviewed on the basis of semi-classical trace formula. Using the spheroidal cavity model, it is shown that three-dimensional periodic orbits, which are born out of bifurcation of planar orbits at large prolate deformations, generate the superdeformed shell structure.

# Periodic Orbits and Deformed Shell Structure

## Key claim

Three-dimensional periodic orbits, which are absent in spherical and normal deformed systems and are born out of bifurcations of planar orbits at large prolate deformations, generate a new shell structure that may be called the superdeformed shell structure; these 3D orbits continue to exist for a wide range of deformation once they are born, in contrast to the harmonic-oscillator potential where 3D periodic orbits exist only at the exact 2:1 shape.

## Method

The authors use the spheroidal cavity model (a simplified model of the Woods-Saxon potential for heavy nuclei) and apply the semiclassical periodic-orbit theory based on the trace formula, which expresses the oscillating part of the single-particle level density as a sum over classical periodic orbits. They perform Fourier transforms of quantum spectra to obtain length spectra and compare these with classical orbit lengths as functions of the axis ratio deformation parameter eta, and develop an improved stationary phase approximation to handle the divergence of the standard trace formula at bifurcation points.

## Key numbers

- The bifurcation of butterfly-shaped planar orbits from the short diameter on the meridian plane occurs at axis ratio eta = sqrt(2)
- Three-dimensional orbits emerge through bifurcation of five-point star shaped orbits on the equatorial plane at eta approximately 1.62
- The 3D orbits responsible for superdeformed shell structure appear at axis ratio eta >= 1.62, which corresponds to the ordinary deformation parameter delta >= 0.44
- Additional 3D orbits appear at eta = sqrt(3) through bifurcation of second repetitions of triangular orbits on the equatorial plane

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical periodic-orbit theory originally developed by Balian and Bloch (1972) and the ridge-valley structure analysis of Strutinsky, Magner, Ofengenden, and Dossing (1977) for the spheroidal cavity model. It provides a general explanation for superdeformed shell structure that is not restricted to the harmonic-oscillator potential with axis ratio 2:1, which is important because more than 200 superdeformed bands have been found in various regions of the nuclear chart with shapes deviating from the 2:1 shape. The results enable a deeper understanding of the microscopic origin of symmetry breaking in the mean field, including the reason for prolate dominance in normal deformed nuclei and the origin of left-right asymmetric shapes, and are applicable to finite many-fermion systems such as metallic clusters and quantum dots.
