# Direct photon emission and influence of dynamical wave packets in an extended quantum molecular dynamics model

> Direct photon produced from first proton-neutron ($p$-$n$) collision during the early stage of heavy ion reaction is a sensitive probe to reflect energy and momentum distribution of nucleons. In this work, we embedded the hard photon production channel in an extended quantum molecular dynamics (EQMD) model, and took the direct photon as a possible probe to improve namely the Fermi motion in the EQMD model. A possible scheme is offered to handle the dynamical wave packet width within incoherent bremsstrahlung process. Direct photons calculated by our modified EQMD were compared with data of $^{14}$N + $^{12}$C at beam energies $E/A$ = 20, 30 and 40 MeV, and it is found that the yield, inverse slope and angular distribution of direct photons could be reasonably reproduced. In addition, asymmetric reaction systems of $^{4}$He + C and $^{4}$He + Zn at $E/A$ = 53 MeV are also simulated in this work. It is found that the symmetric angular distribution in the nucleon-nucleon ($N$-$N$) center-of-mass (c.m.) frame and the velocity of $ the γ$-emission source can be reasonably obtained from our method although there is some quantitative differences.

## Key claim

The authors claim that embedding the hard photon production channel in the extended quantum molecular dynamics (EQMD) model, with a new scheme to handle the dynamical wave packet width within the incoherent bremsstrahlung process, can reasonably reproduce the yield, inverse slope, and angular distribution of direct photons, although some quantitative differences remain for asymmetric reaction systems.

## Method

The authors embedded the incoherent proton-neutron bremsstrahlung channel (\(pn \rightarrow pn\gamma\)) as a perturbative process in the EQMD model, which treats each nucleon as a Gaussian wave packet with a dynamical complex width. To account for the momentum variance of wave packets during inelastic scattering, they introduced a method to re-extract the nucleon's kinetic energy by sampling the momentum from the conditional probability distribution derived from the Wigner function, and modified the Pauli-blocking treatment to use the phase-space density of points in the final state. The model was applied to simulate direct photon emission from the first proton-neutron collisions in heavy ion reactions, selecting only the early-stage direct photons to avoid contributions from thermal photon emission.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{14}\)N + \(^{12}\)C at beam energies \(E/A\) = 20, 30, and 40 MeV, the original EQMD model produced a limit of the highest energy of direct photons of about 21, 36, and 45 MeV, respectively, while the modified EQMD model reasonably reproduced the experimental data.
* For \(^{14}\)N + \(^{12}\)C at \(E/A\) = 40 MeV, the laboratory angular distributions of photons were reproduced for photon energies of \(E_\gamma = 20 \pm 3\), \(40 \pm 3\), \(60 \pm 3\), and \(80 \pm 3\) MeV.
* For \(^{4}\)He + C and \(^{4}\)He + Zn at \(E/A\) = 53 MeV, the calculated yields needed to be scaled upward by factors of 1.5 and 3, respectively, to match experimental data, but the inverse slope of the spectra for photon energy above 30 MeV (\(E_\gamma > 30\) MeV) was in good agreement.

## Context

This work extends the EQMD model, which has previously succeeded in describing multifragmentation, giant dipole resonance, photonuclear reactions, and collective flow (He et al., 2014; He et al., 2015; Wang et al., 2015; Guo et al., 2017), by adding the capability to handle inelastic processes such as hard photon bremsstrahlung. The study addresses a known deficiency in the original EQMD model where the kinetic energy stored in the dynamical wave packet width was not accounted for in two-body collisions, which is critical for inelastic processes. The results enable future investigations of the effect of short-range correlations on hard photon production, as previously studied in BUU-type models (Yong et al., 2010; Ren et al., 2016).
