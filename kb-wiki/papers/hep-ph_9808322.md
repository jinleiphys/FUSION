# Chiral Perturbation Theory for |Delta I|=3/2 Hyperon Decays

> We study the |Delta I|=3/2 amplitudes of hyperon non-leptonic decays of the form B->B'+pi in the context of chiral perturbation theory. The lowest-order predictions are determined in terms of only one unknown parameter and are consistent within errors with current data. We investigate the theoretical uncertainty of these predictions by calculating the leading non-analytic corrections. We also present an estimate for the size of the S-wave Lambda and Cascade decays which vanish at leading order. We find that the corrections to the lowest-order predictions are within the expectations of naive power counting and, therefore, that this picture can be tested more accurately with improved measurements.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the leading non-analytic corrections of order \(m_s \ln m_s\) to the \(|\Delta I|=3/2\) amplitudes of hyperon non-leptonic decays within chiral perturbation theory, finding that these corrections are within the expectations of naive power counting and that the lowest-order predictions, which depend on only one unknown parameter, are consistent within errors with current data. The authors caution that the S-wave amplitudes for \(\Lambda\) and \(\Xi\) decays vanish at leading order and remain zero after including the non-analytic terms, so they provide only an estimate for the size of these amplitudes from non-vanishing contributions.

## Method

The authors used heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, including both octet and decuplet baryon fields explicitly in the Lagrangian, to compute the \(|\Delta I|=3/2\) S-wave and P-wave amplitudes for hyperon decays of the form \(B \rightarrow B' \pi\) at one-loop order. They calculated only the terms of order \(m_s \ln m_s\) (the leading non-analytic corrections) because these are uniquely determined from one-loop diagrams and cannot arise from local counterterm Lagrangians, following the approach used previously for \(|\Delta I|=1/2\) amplitudes by Bijnens and Jenkins. The experimental values of the amplitudes were extracted using the latest decay rates and asymmetry parameters from the Particle Data Book.

## Key numbers

- The pion-decay constant used is \(f_\pi \approx 92.4\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The constant \(g_{27}\) extracted from \(K \rightarrow \pi\pi\) decays is approximately \(g_{27} \approx 0.16\), while the analogous constant for \(|\Delta I|=1/2\) transitions is \(g_8 \approx 5.1\).
- The leading-order S-wave amplitudes are: \(\alpha^{(S)}_{\Sigma^+ n} = -\frac{3}{2} \beta_{27}\), \(\alpha^{(S)}_{\Sigma^- n} = \beta_{27}\), and \(\alpha^{(S)}_{\Lambda p} = \alpha^{(S)}_{\Xi^-\Lambda} = 0\).
- The leading-order P-wave amplitudes are expressed in terms of \(\beta_{27}\), the strong couplings \(D\) and \(F\), and baryon mass differences such as \(m_\Sigma - m_N\) and \(m_\Xi - m_\Sigma\).
- The kaon mass \(m_K\) and the relation \(m_{\eta_8}^2 = \frac{4}{3} m_K^2\) are used in the chiral limit where the pion is massless.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory treatment of hyperon non-leptonic decays from the dominant \(|\Delta I|=1/2\) sector, studied by Bijnens, Jenkins, and others, to the \(|\Delta I|=3/2\) sector. It builds on the leading-order analysis by He and Valencia, who showed that the \(|\Delta I|=3/2\) amplitudes can be described by a single parameter. The calculation enables future tests of the chiral perturbation theory picture with improved measurements from experiments such as Fermilab E871 (searching for CP violation in hyperon decays) and KTeV (studying CP violation in \(K \rightarrow \pi\pi\) and rare kaon decays), which could improve the measurements of \(\Lambda\) and \(\Xi\) decays with charged or neutral pions.
