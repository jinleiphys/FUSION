# Ground state octupole correlation energies with effective forces

> The ground state octupole correlation energy is computed with the D1M variant of the Gogny force in different theoretical frameworks and analyzed in detail. First I consider the correlation energy gained at the mean field level by breaking reflection symmetry. Next I consider the energy gain coming from symmetry (parity) restoration and finally I analyze the ground state correlation energy after configuration mixing with axially symmetric octupole states. The impact of the latter on theoretical binding energies indicates that octupole correlations do not affect in a significant way the trend and systematic of binding energies and therefore can not improve the performance of theoretical models in this respect. In particular, the too-large "shell gaps" predicted by self-consistent mean field models and relevant in astrophysics scenarios are not altered by the octupole correlations.

## Key claim

Octupole correlation energies computed with the Gogny D1M force, while reaching up to 2.5 MeV, do not substantially improve the agreement between theoretical and experimental binding energies and do not alter the too-large "shell gaps" predicted by self-consistent mean field models that are relevant for astrophysical r-process simulations.

## Method

The author performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with a constraint on the axial octupole moment using the Gogny D1M effective interaction for 818 even-even nuclei from oxygen to copernicium. Three levels of correlation energy were computed: the mean-field energy gain from breaking reflection symmetry (octupole-deformed HFB), the energy gain from parity restoration via restricted variation after projection (RVAP), and the full ground state correlation energy from Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) configuration mixing over axially symmetric octupole states. The resulting binding energies and two-neutron separation energies were compared to the 2012 experimental binding energy compilation.

## Key numbers

- Octupole GCM correlation energy reaches up to 2.5 MeV in regions showing octupole deformation at the HFB level
- HFB octupole correlation energy (reflection symmetry breaking) never exceeds 1.2 MeV, occurring only in Ra, Ba, and Zr regions
- Parity RVAP correlation energy reaches up to 1.5 MeV and is non-zero in all nuclei considered
- The D1M parametrization yields an rms deviation for binding energy of 0.79 MeV when fitted with HFB plus approximate rotational and quadrupole zero-point energy corrections
- The zero-point energy correction from quadrupole degrees of freedom is typically of order 2-3 MeV

## Context

This work extends the systematic octupole calculations of Robledo (2011) by focusing specifically on the impact of octupole correlations on binding energies, following the study of other correlation energies (particle number projection, angular momentum projection, and quadrupole configuration mixing) by Tom. (2014) with the same Gogny D1M force. The results show that, unlike quadrupole correlations which reach tens of MeV, octupole correlations do not improve the performance of mean-field mass models for astrophysical simulations, contrary to expectations discussed in Arc. (2012). The work enables future studies of non-axial tetrahedral correlations (Q32) and their impact on ground state correlation energies across the nuclear chart.
