# Three body dwell time

> The lifetime of an unstable state or resonance formed as an intermediate state in two body scattering is known to be related to the dwell time or the time spent within a given region of space by the two interacting particles. This concept is extended to the case of three body systems and a relation connecting the three body dwell time with the two body dwell times of the substructures of the three body system is derived for the case of separable wave functions. The Kapur Peierls formalism is revisited to discover one of the first definitions of dwell time in literature. An extension of the Kapur Peierls formalism to the three body case shows that the lifetime of a three body resonance can indeed be given by the three body dwell time.

## Key claim

The paper extends the concept of dwell time (the average time a particle spends in a given spatial region) from two-body scattering to three-body systems, deriving a relation that connects the three-body dwell time to the two-body dwell times of the system's substructures, but only under the restrictive assumptions of separable wave functions and s-wave (spherically symmetric) interactions.

## Method

The author first revisits the Kapur-Peierls (K-P) formalism for two-body resonances, showing that the K-P boundary condition leads to a dwell time equal to the phase time and yielding a new expression for the dwell time. This K-P approach is then extended to a three-body system with a separable wave function, where the Hamiltonian is written in terms of relative coordinates for a particle and a two-body subsystem. The three-body dwell time is derived both from the K-P extension and from a standard definition using a three-body current density, with both approaches giving the same result: the inverse of the three-body dwell time equals the sum of the inverses of the two-body dwell times of the substructures.

## Key numbers

* The lifetime of a three-body resonance, τ^R, is related to the two-body dwell times by 1/τ^R = 1/τ_D^χ + 1/τ_D^Φ, where τ_D^χ is the dwell time of particle 1 with the composite (2,3) system and τ_D^Φ is the dwell time of particles 2 and 3.
* The two-body dwell time relation from the K-P formalism is given by ℏ/Γ_n = ∫₀^{r₀} φ_n* φ_n dr / j(r₀), where Γ_n is the width of the unstable state and j(r₀) is the current density at the boundary radius r₀.
* A new definition of the two-body dwell time within the K-P formalism is τ_D(E) = -iℏ d/dE [ln φ(r₀)], where φ(r₀) is the radial wave function at the boundary.

## Context

This work extends the dwell time concept, originally introduced by Smith (1960) for quantum collisions and by Kapur and Peierls (1938) in nuclear reaction theory, to three-body systems for the first time. The author notes that the phase time delay (Wigner time delay) is singular near threshold for s-wave resonances, making the dwell time a more useful tool for characterizing resonances in systems such as the unstable nuclei ⁹Be and ⁹B, which can be treated as three-body resonances (e.g., α+α+n). The derived inverse-addition relation for dwell times parallels a similar relation found by the author in earlier work for reflection, transmission, and average dwell times in one-dimensional tunneling. This formalism enables future studies of three-body resonances in nuclear, hadronic, and halo-nuclei systems.
