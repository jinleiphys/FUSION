# Covariance analysis of finite temperature density functional theory: symmetric nuclear matter

> We study symmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature, with particular emphasis on the liquid-gas phase transition. We use a standard covariance analysis to propagate statistical uncertainties from the density functional to the thermodynamic properties. We use four functionals with known covariance matrices to obtain as wide a set of results as possible. Our findings suggest that thermodynamical properties are very well constrained by fitting data at zero temperature. The propagated statistical errors in the liquid-gas phase transition parameters are relatively small.

## Key claim

The paper finds that thermodynamic properties of symmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature are very well constrained by fitting data at zero temperature, with propagated statistical errors in the liquid-gas phase transition parameters being relatively small; however, this conclusion is conditional on the specific choice of density functional and depends critically on whether the isoscalar effective mass is fixed or allowed to vary in the fitting procedure.

## Method

The authors performed finite-temperature Skyrme energy density functional (EDF) calculations for symmetric nuclear matter using four parametrizations (SLy5min, UNEDF0, UNEDF1, UNEDF2) that have published covariance matrices. They propagated statistical uncertainties from the EDF parameters to thermodynamic properties (equation of state, pressure isotherms, phase transition parameters) using a standard covariance analysis based on a chi-squared objective function with Gaussian likelihood, computing numerical derivatives at the central parameter values plus or minus one-tenth of the standard deviation.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density constrained to better than 0.002 fm^-3 for all functionals
- Critical temperatures: SLy5min 14.6 ± 0.1 MeV, UNEDF0 17.99 ± 0.03 MeV, UNEDF1 16.8 ± 1.2 MeV, UNEDF2 16.4 ± 0.5 MeV
- Critical densities: SLy5min 0.055 ± 0.001 fm^-3, UNEDF0 0.0557 ± 0.0003 fm^-3, UNEDF1 0.053 ± 0.002 fm^-3, UNEDF2 0.0526 ± 0.0005 fm^-3
- Critical pressures: SLy5min 0.22 ± 0.01 MeV fm^-3, UNEDF0 0.283 ± 0.002 MeV fm^-3, UNEDF1 0.26 ± 0.03 MeV fm^-3, UNEDF2 0.24 ± 0.01 MeV fm^-3
- Flashing temperatures: SLy5min 11.74 ± 0.06 MeV, UNEDF0 13.50 ± 0.02 MeV, UNEDF1 12.9 ± 0.6 MeV, UNEDF2 12.7 ± 0.3 MeV
- Flashing densities: SLy5min 0.085 ± 0.001 fm^-3, UNEDF0 0.0854 ± 0.0005 fm^-3, UNEDF1 0.083 ± 0.001 fm^-3, UNEDF2 0.0818 ± 0.0004 fm^-3
- Maximum latent heat values: SLy5min 29.8 ± 0.4 MeV, UNEDF0 29.59 ± 0.07 MeV, UNEDF1 29.26 ± 0.03 MeV, UNEDF2 29.5 ± 0.2 MeV
- Pearson correlation coefficients between flashing and critical densities: c > 0.9998; between flashing and critical temperatures: c > 0.997

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of error propagation in nuclear energy density functionals by Reinhard (2010) and Dobaczewski (2014) to the finite-temperature regime, and builds on the finite-temperature Skyrme calculations of Rios (2010) which identified empirical correlations between flashing and critical points. The results enable quantitative uncertainty estimates for the nuclear matter liquid-gas phase transition, which can be compared to experimental extractions such as those by Elliott (2013), and provide guidance for future EDF fitting protocols by demonstrating that the isoscalar effective mass must be accurately determined if finite-temperature properties are to be constrained.
