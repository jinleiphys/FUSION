# Progress in calculation of the fourth Mellin moment of the pion light-cone distribution amplitude using the HOPE method

> The pion light-cone distribution amplitude (LCDA) is a central non-perturbative object of interest for the calculation of high-energy exclusive processes in quantum chromodynamics. This article describes the progress in the lattice QCD calculation of the fourth Mellin moment of the pion LCDA using a heavy-quark operator product expansion (HOPE).

## Key claim

The authors report progress toward a lattice QCD determination of the fourth Mellin moment of the pion light-cone distribution amplitude using the heavy-quark operator product expansion (HOPE) method, but state that with only two lattice spacings and up to three heavy quark masses, a reliable combined continuum and twist-two extrapolation is not yet possible.

## Method

The HOPE method relates Euclidean hadronic matrix elements of conserved currents to the Mellin moments of the pion LCDA via an operator product expansion that includes a fictitious heavy quark. The authors compute three-point correlation functions on two ensembles of gauge configurations with lattice spacings 0.0813 fm and 0.0600 fm, at a pion mass of approximately 0.55 GeV, and extract the second and fourth Mellin moments by fitting the numerical data to the one-loop HOPE expression. A ratio of three-point correlators is constructed to avoid the need for precise two-point parameter extraction and current renormalization, and operator smearing combined with a variational basis is used to reduce excited-state contamination.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacings: 0.0813 fm (24^3 x 48 lattice, 6500 configurations) and 0.0600 fm (32^3 x 64 lattice, 4500 configurations)
- Pion mass: m_pi ~ 0.55 GeV
- Physical volume: L = 1.92 fm
- Heavy quark masses in the MSbar scheme: 2.0 GeV, 2.6 GeV, and 3.4 GeV
- At L/a=24 with m_Psi=2.0 GeV and renormalization scale mu=2.0 GeV: <xi^2> = 0.17 +/- 0.04, <xi^4> = 0.07 +/- 0.02 (purely statistical uncertainties)
- The only existing lattice determination of the fourth moment is <xi^4>(mu=2 GeV) = 0.124(11)(20) at a single lattice spacing a=0.076 fm from Gao (2022)

## Context

This work extends the HOPE method previously applied to the second Mellin moment by Detmold (2021) to the fourth Mellin moment, for which little is known from lattice QCD. The approach avoids the power-divergent operator mixing that limited conventional methods to the second moment, as discussed by Martinelli (1987). The results are broadly consistent with the single existing determination of the fourth moment by Gao (2022) at finite lattice spacing, and the authors plan to perform a combined continuum and twist-two extrapolation once more data are available.
