# Fission Cycling in Supernova Nucleosynthesis: Active-Sterile Neutrino Oscillations

> We investigate nucleosynthesis in the supernovae post-core bounce neutrino-driven wind environment in the presence of active-sterile neutrino transformation. We consider active-sterile neutrino oscillations for a range of mixing parameters: vacuum mass-squared differences of 0.1 eV^2 < dm^2 < 100 eV^2, and vacuum mixing angles of sin^2(2 theta_v) > 10^-4. We find a consistent r-process pattern for a large range of mixing parameters that is in rough agreement with the halo star CS 22892-052 abundances and the pattern shape is determined by fission cycling. We find that the allowed region for the formation of the r-process peaks overlaps the LSND and NSBL (3+1) allowed region.

## Key claim

Active-sterile neutrino oscillations in the supernova neutrino-driven wind can produce a consistent r-process pattern that reproduces the second and third r-process peaks, but only when the electron fraction at the onset of neutron capture is below 0.3; the pattern shape is determined by fission cycling rather than by the specific neutrino mixing parameters.

## Method

The authors used a one-dimensional parameterized model of the neutrino-driven wind following a mass element from the protoneutron star surface through three linked network calculations: a nuclear statistical equilibrium code, a full reaction rate network for intermediate nuclides (A less than 120), and an r-process reaction network for the neutron capture regime. They self-consistently solved the matter-enhanced active-sterile neutrino oscillation equations for the nu_e to nu_s and anti-nu_e to anti-nu_s channels, using vacuum mass-squared differences from 0.1 eV^2 to 100 eV^2 and vacuum mixing angles with sin^2(2 theta_v) greater than 10^-4. The network included spontaneous fission for nuclei with A greater than or equal to 270 and beta-delayed fission for nuclei with Z from 81 to 100 and N from 140 to 164.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum mass-squared difference range considered: 0.1 eV^2 less than or equal to delta m^2 less than or equal to 100 eV^2
- Vacuum mixing angle threshold: sin^2(2 theta_v) greater than 10^-4
- Electron fraction threshold for r-process occurrence: Y_e less than or equal to 0.35 at T_keV approximately 200
- Electron fraction threshold for matching observed peak ratios: Y_e less than or equal to 0.3
- Wind parameters used: entropy per baryon S/k = 100, expansion timescale tau = 0.3 s, neutrino luminosities L_nu = 10^51 ergs and L_anti-nu = 1.3 x 10^51 ergs, neutrino temperatures T_nu = 3.5 MeV and T_anti-nu = 4.5 MeV
- Solar data scaled such that sum of A=195 nuclides is 2.3 x 10^-3
- Halo star data scaled such that sum of Z=70 isotopes has abundance of 10^-3
- Network termination boundary for spontaneous fission: A greater than or equal to 270
- Beta-delayed fission range: Z from 81 to 100, N from 140 to 164

## Context

This work extends the proposal by McLaughlin et al. (1999) that active-sterile neutrino transformation could circumvent the alpha-effect problem that impedes r-process production in the neutrino-driven wind. It contrasts with earlier models such as Woosley et al. (1994) that reproduced r-process abundances without significant fission cycling, and with later models that produced lower entropy per baryon and insufficient neutronization. The results show that the allowed parameter region for r-process formation overlaps the LSND and NSBL (3+1) allowed region, and the consistent pattern produced by fission cycling enables future work to compare with time-integrated histories over multiple wind conditions to determine if a significant first r-process peak at A approximately 80 can be produced.
