# Is the nuclear force short range ?

> Possible strong Van der Waals force is searched in the nuclear interaction, whose existence is expected when the fundamental interaction between quarks is strong or superstrong Coulombic type. The relation between the type of the extra singularity at t=0 of the scattering amplitude and the power of the asymptotic behavior of the long range potential is derived. The square root cusp in the once subtracted S-wave amplitude, whose existence is expected when the Van der Waals force of the London type is acting, is observed when we analyze the high precision phase shift data of the low energy proton-proton scattering.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a strong Van der Waals force of the London type, with an asymptotic potential falling as \(V(R) \sim -C/R^{\alpha}\) where \(\alpha \approx 6.09\), is acting between nucleons, contradicting the standard assumption that the nuclear force is purely short range; the claim is hedged by the observation that the extracted parameters depend on fitting the once-subtracted Kantor amplitude to Nijmegen phase shift data in the range \(0.6\ \mathrm{MeV} < T_{\mathrm{lab}} < 125\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Method

The author derives a relation between the power \(\gamma\) of the extra singularity at \(t=0\) of the scattering amplitude and the power \(\alpha\) of the asymptotic long-range potential, \(\alpha = 2\gamma + 3\). Using high-precision low-energy proton-proton phase shift data from the Nijmegen group, the once-subtracted S-wave Kantor amplitude \(K_0^{\mathrm{once}}(\nu)\) is constructed after removing the unitarity cut and the one-pion exchange contribution, and a chi-square fit of the spectral function \(A_t(s,t) = \pi C' t^\gamma e^{-\beta t}\) is performed to extract \(\gamma\), \(C'\), and \(\beta\).

## Key numbers

* The best-fit spectral parameters in the range \(0.6\ \mathrm{MeV} < T_{\mathrm{lab}} < 125\ \mathrm{MeV}\) are \(\gamma = 1.54\), \(C' = 0.18\), and \(\beta = 0.063\) in units of the neutral pion mass.
* The asymptotic potential parameters derived from the fit are \(\alpha = 6.09\) and \(C = 0.18\) in units of the neutral pion mass.
* The lower bound on the fundamental Coulomb interaction strength is \(^{*}e^2 > 3.06\), obtained using the nucleon radius \(\bar{r} = 0.5\) (in pion mass units), the first excitation energy \(\Delta E_1 = m_\Delta - m_N = 2.17\), and the fitted Van der Waals strength \(C = 0.18\).
* The upper and lower bounds on the Van der Waals coefficient \(C\) for a two-body composite system are given by \(\frac{2}{3}\frac{^{*}e^4}{\Delta E_1} \hat{r}_1^2 \hat{r}_2^2 \leq C \leq \frac{2}{3}\frac{^{*}e^4}{\Delta E_1} \bar{r}_1^2 \bar{r}_2^2\), with the nucleon radius estimates \(\bar{r} = 1/2\) (charge radius) to \(1/6\) (repulsive core distance) in pion mass units.

## Context

This work extends the composite model of hadrons by arguing that if the fundamental quark interaction is Coulombic, a Van der Waals force must appear between nucleons, contradicting the standard meson-exchange picture where the nuclear force is short range. The author contrasts the extracted lower bound \(^{*}e^2 > 3.06\) with the QCD value \(^{*}e^2 \sim 0.3\) and the magnetic monopole model value \(^{*}e^2 = 137.0/4\), concluding that the result supports the magnetic monopole model of hadrons over QCD. The analysis enables a new approach to constructing nuclear potentials that include a strong Van der Waals component alongside short-range meson-exchange terms, and suggests that similar cusp singularities should be observable in other hadron-hadron reactions such as low-energy pion-pion scattering.
