# $f$ and $p$ mode oscillation of proto-neutron stars with systematic variation of the nucleon effective mass

> We develop equation of state (EoS) of proto-neutron stars (PNSs) at various stages of evolution by varying entropy per baryon $S$, using the Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU density functional model. With finite values for both temperature and density, we systematically investigate the influence of nucleon effective mass on EoS of PNSs, for different values of isoscalar effective mass $μ_S^*$. For high entropy values, we aim to replicate conditions of failed core-collapse supernovae forming black holes. At each stage of evolution, structural and non-radial oscillation (fundamental $f$-mode and first pressure $p_1$-mode) properties are computed under isentropic conditions by varying $μ_S^*$. We focus on the effects of $S$ and $μ_S^*$ on oscillation frequencies $f_f$ and $f_{p_1}$ adopting complete general relativistic formalism and Cowling approximation. Thermal effects reduce the values of $f_f$ and $f_{p_1}$ of PNSs compared to those of cold NSs, consequently detection of the former gets facilitated. For high-mass PNSs, this reduction is more pronounced for $f_{p_1}$ than $f_f$. Moreover, lower values of $μ_S^*$ reduce $f_f$ and $f_{p_1}$ further. Universality of mass-scaled angular frequency ($ω_fM$) with compactness ($C$) and tidal deformability ($Λ$) are obtained as non-linear fits that shift upwards (downwards) in $ω_fM-C$ ($ω_fM-Λ$) plane for increasing values of $S$. For fixed $S$, the universality is also retained for variation of $μ_S^*$. $S$ shows stronger correlation than $μ_S^*$ with structural and oscillation properties of (P)NSs. Strength of correlation of $S$ is more prominent with $f_{p_1}$ than $f_f$ while the trend is opposite for $μ_S^*$. These findings suggest that detection of oscillation frequencies by upcoming GW detectors, could potentially indicate the evolutionary stage of a star during its transition from supernova to cold NS.

## Key claim

The paper systematically investigates how variations in the isoscalar nucleon effective mass ($\mu_S^*$) and entropy per baryon ($S$) affect the fundamental $f$-mode and first pressure $p_1$-mode oscillation frequencies of proto-neutron stars, finding that thermal effects reduce these frequencies compared to cold neutron stars and that lower effective mass further reduces them, but the abstract's claim that detection "could potentially indicate the evolutionary stage" is hedged by the fact that the study uses static, isentropic models rather than full dynamical simulations.

## Method

The authors use the Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU (KIDS) density functional model to construct equations of state for proto-neutron star matter at fixed entropy per baryon values of $S = 1, 2, 4$, with four variations of the isoscalar effective mass ratio $\mu_S^* = 0.7, 0.8, 0.9, 1.0$ while fixing the isovector effective mass to $\mu_V^* = 1$. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for stellar structure and compute non-radial oscillation frequencies using both the full general relativistic formalism and the Cowling approximation, with the Zerilli equation solved for the exterior spacetime.

## Key numbers

- Four values of isoscalar effective mass ratio are considered: $\mu_S^* = 0.7, 0.8, 0.9, 1.0$
- Three entropy per baryon values are used: $S = 1, 2, 4$, where $S = 4$ models failed core-collapse supernovae forming black holes
- The KIDS0 model has saturation density $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$, binding energy $E_B = 16.0$ MeV, incompressibility $K_0 = 240$ MeV, symmetry energy constant $J = 32.8$ MeV, slope $L = 49.1$ MeV
- The lepton fraction is fixed at $Y_l = 0.3$ for all proto-neutron star models
- The Cowling approximation overestimates the full GR $f$-mode frequency by about 20 percent

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Togashi et al., 2025) on the effects of nucleon effective mass on proto-neutron star structural properties using the KIDS model, and follows their calculation of non-radial oscillation frequencies for cold neutron stars (Guha et al., 2024). The paper builds on prior work by Kumar et al. (2024) showing non-linear deviations in universal relations due to temperature changes, and by Barman et al. (2024) and Zheng et al. (2025) demonstrating that universality holds for the $\omega_f M - \Lambda$ relation. The results enable future gravitational wave detectors such as LIGO O4, the Einstein Telescope, and the Cosmic Explorer to potentially distinguish the evolutionary stage of a proto-neutron star based on its oscillation frequencies.
