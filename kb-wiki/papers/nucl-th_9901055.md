# Unitary-model-operator approach to $Λ$ hypernuclei

> A method is formulated for the description of lambda hypernuclei in the framework of the unitary-model-operator approach (UMOA). The method is applied to $_Λ^{17}$O. A lambda-nucleon effective interaction is derived, taking the coupling of the sigma-nucleon channel into account. The lambda single-particle energies are calculated for the 0s_{1/2}, 0p_{3/2} and 0p_{1/2} states employing the Nijmegen soft-core (NSC), Jülich model-Ã (JÃ) and model-B (JB) hyperon-nucleon potentials.

## Key claim

The unitary-model-operator approach (UMOA) is extended to describe Λ hypernuclei by deriving a hermitian, energy-independent ΛN effective interaction that explicitly includes the coupling to the ΣN channel, and the method is applied to \(^{17}_{\Lambda}\)O using three realistic hyperon-nucleon potentials.

## Method

The authors formulate the UMOA for a closed-shell nucleus plus one Λ system by introducing a unitary transformation that decouples a model space of low-momentum ΛN two-particle states from its complement (high-momentum ΛN states and all ΣN states). The ΛN effective interaction is derived self-consistently with the Λ single-particle potential through a two-step procedure using harmonic oscillator basis states, where the first step uses a large model space and the second step a small model space of low-lying ΛN states. The calculation employs the Nijmegen soft-core (NSC), Jülich model-Ã (JÃ), and model-B (JB) hyperon-nucleon potentials, with the Σ single-particle potential set to zero and the nucleon single-particle potential taken from a previous UMOA calculation for \(^{16}\)O.

## Key numbers

- The mass difference between Λ and Σ used in the Hamiltonian is about 77 MeV.
- The Λ single-particle energies are calculated for the \(0s_{1/2}\), \(0p_{3/2}\), and \(0p_{1/2}\) states in \(^{17}_{\Lambda}\)O using the NSC, JÃ, and JB potentials (explicit numerical values for these energies are not provided in the truncated text).
- The model space boundary in the first-step calculation is defined by the quantum number \(\rho_1\), where \((\rho_1 + 3)\hbar\Omega/2\) gives the maximum kinetic energy of ΛN two-particle states in the \(P^{(1)}_{\Lambda N}\) space.
- The Pauli-exclusion principle for nucleons is handled using the number \(\rho_F = 2n_N + l_N\) for the highest occupied orbit in the closed-shell core nucleus.

## Context

This work extends the unitary-model-operator approach, previously applied to ordinary nuclei like \(^{16}\)O and \(^{40}\)Ca by Suzuki and Okamoto (1994), to hypernuclei by incorporating the ΛN-ΣN channel coupling. It offers an alternative to the G-matrix approach used by Bando (1981), Halderson (1993), and others, which yields energy-dependent and non-hermitian effective interactions; the UMOA instead produces an energy-independent, hermitian effective interaction with decoupling properties. The method enables microscopic structure calculations of heavier hypernuclei and can help constrain hyperon-nucleon interactions by comparing calculated Λ single-particle energies with future experimental data on spin-orbit splitting.
