# The effect of uu diquark suppression in proton splitting in Monte Carlo event generators

> Monte Carlo event generators assume that protons split into (uu)-diquarks and d-quarks with a probability of 1/3 in strong interactions. It is shown in this paper that using a value of 1/6 for the probability allows one to describe at a semi-quantitative level the NA49 Collaboration data for $p+p\rightarrow p+X$ reactions at 158 GeV/c. The Fritiof (FTF) model of Geant4 was used to simulate the reactions. The reduced weight of the (uu)-diquarks in protons is expected in the instanton model.

# The effect of uu diquark suppression in proton splitting in Monte Carlo event generators

## Key claim

The standard Monte Carlo assumption that protons split into (uu)-diquarks and d-quarks with probability 1/3 in strong interactions should be reduced to 1/6, which allows a semi-quantitative description of NA49 proton spectra at 158 GeV/c, though the description is not perfect and requires further fine-tuning.

## Method

The authors used the Fritiof (FTF) model of Geant4 (release geant4.10.0) to simulate p+p → p+X reactions at 158 GeV/c, testing various diquark fragmentation functions (1/z, 1/√z, 1, √z, z, z²) and introducing string inversion (quark as leading particle in 25% of cases). The standard proton splitting probability of 1/3 for the (uu)-d configuration was changed to 1/6, and the resulting xF distributions were compared to NA49 Collaboration data.

## Key numbers

- The standard probability for protons splitting into (uu)-diquarks and d-quarks is 1/3; the proposed value is 1/6.
- The optimal diquark fragmentation function found was f(z) ∝ √z.
- String inversion assumes a quark is the leading particle in 25% of cases.
- High mass diffraction dissociation was simulated with a probability of 10 mb/σᵢₙₚₚ.
- The beam momentum was 158 GeV/c for p+p interactions.

## Context

This work extends the Fritiof model (Andersson et al., 1987; Nilsson-Almqvist and Stenlund, 1987) as implemented in Geant4, and addresses discrepancies with NA49 Collaboration data (2002) that previous Monte Carlo event generators could not satisfactorily describe. The suppression of (uu)-diquarks is motivated by the instanton model of the QCD vacuum (Shuryak and Schafer, 1998; Kochelev, 1998), which predicts flavor-dependent quark-quark interactions. The approach also improves description of neutron and Λ-hyperon spectra, and suggests that weights of (uu)-d and (dd)-u configurations in protons and neutrons respectively need re-consideration in all Monte Carlo event generators.
