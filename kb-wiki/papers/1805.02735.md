# Consistent relativistic mean-field models: critical parameters values

> We revisit the study published in [1], related to the behavior of 34 relativistic mean-field models, previously selected because they satisfy bulk nuclear matter properties, here used to compute the critical parameters of the symmetric nuclear matter. We evaluate their critical temperature, pressure, and density and compare with some values encountered in the literature. We also show that these parameters are correlated with the incompressibility calculated at the zero temperature regime.

## Key claim

The critical temperature, pressure, and density of symmetric nuclear matter computed from 34 consistent relativistic mean-field (CRMF) models show a clear increasing correlation with the incompressibility at zero temperature and saturation density, but no such correlation with the symmetry energy or its slope. Only the Z271 family of nonlinear models and the density-dependent models produce critical parameters compatible with the most recent experimental data from Elliott (2013).

## Method

The authors computed the critical point (temperature T_c, density ρ_c, and pressure P_c) for 34 CRMF parametrizations that had previously passed bulk nuclear matter constraints. They solved the conditions ∂P/∂ρ = 0 and ∂²P/∂ρ² = 0 at the critical point using the finite-temperature equation of state derived from either nonlinear RMF Lagrangians (with σ, ω, ρ mesons and cross-coupling terms) or density-dependent RMF Lagrangians (with density-dependent coupling functions Γ_i(ρ)). The critical parameters were then compared with eight experimental data sets and tested for correlations with the symmetry energy J, its slope L_0, and the incompressibility K_0 at saturation density.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature T_c ranges from 13.83 MeV (BKA24) to 17.97 MeV (all Z271 family models)
- Critical density ρ_c ranges from 0.0439 fm⁻³ (BSR10) to 0.0509 fm⁻³ (Z271 family and TW99)
- Critical pressure P_c ranges from 0.176 MeV/fm³ (BSR10) to 0.303 MeV/fm³ (Z271 family)
- The Z271 family (8 parametrizations) gives T_c = 17.97 MeV, ρ_c = 0.0509 fm⁻³, P_c = 0.303 MeV/fm³
- Experimental reference from Elliott (2013): T_c = 17.9 ± 0.4 MeV, ρ_c = 0.06 ± 0.01 fm⁻³, P_c = 0.31 ± 0.07 MeV/fm³
- The incompressibility constraint used for model selection was K_0 = 190–270 MeV at saturation density

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Dutra et al. (2014) that screened 263 RMF models against bulk nuclear matter constraints, leaving 35 consistent parametrizations (34 used here). The critical parameters are compared with experimental data from Karn (1981), Natowitz (2002), and Elliott (2013). The finding that critical parameters correlate with incompressibility but not with symmetry energy or its slope provides guidance for selecting RMF models for finite-temperature applications such as heavy-ion collisions and nuclear multifragmentation. The Z271 family and density-dependent models are identified as the most suitable for describing the liquid-gas phase transition in symmetric nuclear matter.
