# Centrality dependence of forward $J/ψ$ suppression in high energy proton-nucleus collisions

> The production of forward $J/ψ$ mesons in proton-nucleus collisions can provide important information on gluon saturation. In a previous work we studied this process in the Color Glass Condensate framework, describing the target using a dipole cross section fitted to HERA inclusive data and extrapolated to the case of a nuclear target using the optical Glauber model. In this work we study the centrality dependence of the nuclear suppression in this model and compare our results with recent LHC data for this observable.

## Key claim

The Color Glass Condensate framework, using an optical Glauber model to relate the nuclear dipole cross section to the proton one, predicts a centrality dependence of forward \(J/\psi\) suppression that is too strong compared to ALICE data, with the nuclear modification factor approaching unity too quickly as the number of binary collisions decreases.

## Method

The authors compute the cross section for \(c\bar{c}\) pair production in the dilute-dense limit of the Color Glass Condensate, using the color evaporation model to hadronize pairs below the \(D\)-meson threshold into \(J/\psi\) mesons. The proton target is described by the MV\(^e\) parametrization fitted to HERA inclusive data, evolved via the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov equation; the nuclear target is obtained by applying the optical Glauber model with a Woods-Saxon distribution, relating the impact parameter to the experimental mean number of binary collisions \(\langle N_\text{coll} \rangle\) in each centrality class.

## Key numbers

- The \(D\)-meson mass used as the upper integration limit for the \(c\bar{c}\) invariant mass is \(m_D = 1.864\) GeV.
- The Woods-Saxon parameters are \(d = 0.54\) fm and \(R_A = (1.12 A^{1/3} - 0.86 A^{-1/3})\) fm.
- The nuclear modification factor \(Q_\text{pPb}\) is computed for proton-lead collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5\) TeV, compared with ALICE data across five centrality bins.
- The uncertainty band in the calculation includes variation of the charm quark mass between 1.2 and 1.5 GeV and of the factorization scale between \(M_\perp/2\) and \(2M_\perp\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous minimum-bias study (Ducloué, Lappi, Mäntysaari, 2015) which found nuclear suppression closer to LHC data than earlier CGC calculations such as Fujii et al. (2013). The present centrality-dependent results show that the optical Glauber model overestimates the centrality dependence compared to ALICE data (Adam et al., 2015), motivating future studies using full impact-parameter distributions rather than fixed values tied to \(\langle N_\text{coll} \rangle\).
