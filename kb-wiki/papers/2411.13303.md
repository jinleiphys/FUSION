# Two-pion exchange for coupled-channel scattering of two heavy mesons

> To improve the theoretical understanding of multiquark states like $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$, we calculate the heavy-meson heavy-(anti)meson scattering potential up to next-to-leading order, $O(Q^2)$, within chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) employing a power counting scheme that explicitly keeps track with the large momentum scale $Q \sim \sqrt{2μδ}$ (where $δ= m_V - m_P$ is the vector-pseudoscalar mass difference and $μ$ their reduced mass) introduced by the coupled channel dynamics. We provide expressions for the two-pion exchange (TPE) terms up to $O(Q^2)$ and their partial-wave decomposition. We show that these potentials are well-approximated by contact terms at $O(Q^2)$, with minor residual non-analytic TPE contributions, supporting $χ$EFT convergence in the theoretical predictions for $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$, as well as their spin partners. These findings are also relevant for $D^{(*)}D^{(*)}$ scattering, especially for the $T_{cc}$ state, for both physical and lattice QCD data with moderately larger pion masses. We further demonstrate that the differences between isovector and isoscalar potentials for heavy mesons are naturally explained by the TPE contributions.

## Key claim

The paper calculates two-pion exchange (TPE) contributions to heavy-meson heavy-(anti)meson scattering potentials up to next-to-leading order, O(Q²), within chiral effective field theory using a momentum counting scheme that treats the large momentum scale Q ~ √(2μδ) (where δ = m_V - m_P is the vector-pseudoscalar mass difference) as a soft scale; the authors show these TPE potentials are well-approximated by contact terms at O(Q²) with only minor residual non-analytic contributions, supporting convergence of the chiral expansion despite the expansion parameter χ ~ 1/2 being relatively large.

## Method

The authors derived the two-pion exchange contributions to the B(*)B̅(*) scattering potential from the chiral effective Lagrangian respecting both chiral symmetry and heavy-quark spin symmetry, evaluating one-loop diagrams in the momentum counting scheme that keeps track of the large momentum scale from coupled-channel dynamics. They provided analytic expressions for the TPE potentials up to O(Q²) and performed their partial-wave decomposition, then compared these potentials to contact-term approximations and to results from previous works that omitted coupled-channel transitions or used different power counting schemes.

## Key numbers

- The vector-pseudoscalar mass difference δ = m_B* - m_B ≈ 45 MeV, which defines the momentum scale p_typ = √(m_B δ) ≈ 500 MeV
- The expansion parameter χ = Q/Λ_χ ~ p_typ/Λ_χ ≈ 1/2, where Λ_χ ≈ 1 GeV is the chiral symmetry breaking scale
- The pion decay constant f_π = 92.4 MeV used in the Lagrangian
- The pion-heavy meson coupling constant g_Q ≈ g = 0.57, extracted from D* → Dπ decay width
- The pion mass m_π ≈ 140 MeV, with m_π/p_typ ~ O(χ) in the power counting scheme

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT approach for Z_b(10610) and Z_b(10650) states developed by Wang et al. (2018) and Baru et al. (2019), which previously omitted two-pion exchange contributions. The TPE potentials derived here are necessary to test convergence of the chiral expansion and provide systematic uncertainty estimates for pole positions extracted from experimental data. The results are also applicable to D(*)D(*) scattering for the T_cc state, complementing lattice QCD studies by Padmanath and Prelovsek (2022) and Meng et al. (2023) that showed OPE effects shift the T_cc pole into the complex plane for pion masses above 230 MeV. Previous EFT calculations by Wang et al. (2018) and Wang (2020) derived TPE operators ignoring coupled-channel transitions based on Weinberg's counting, which the present work overcomes.
