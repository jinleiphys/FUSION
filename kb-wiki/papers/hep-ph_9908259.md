# Drell-Yan and J/psi Production in High Energy Proton-Nucleus and Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

> The distributions of outgoing protons and charged hadrons in high energy proton-nucleus collisions are described rather well by a linear extrapolation from proton-proton collisions. This linear extrapolation is applied to precisely measured Drell-Yan cross sections for 800 GeV protons incident on a variety of nuclear targets. The deviation from linear scaling in the atomic number A can be accounted for by energy degradation of the proton as it passes through the nucleus if account is taken of the time delay of particle production due to quantum coherence. We infer an average proper coherence time of 0.4 +/- 0.1 fm/c. Then we apply the linear extrapolation to measured J/psi production cross sections for 200 and 450 GeV/c protons incident on a variety of nuclear targets. Our analysis takes into account energy loss of the beam proton, the time delay of particle production due to quantum coherence, and absorption of the J/psi on nucleons. The best representation is obtained for a coherence time of 0.5 fm/c, which is consistent with Drell-Yan production, and an absorption cross section of 3.6 mb, which is consistent with the value deduced from photoproduction of the J/psi on nuclear targets. Finally, we compare to recent J/psi data from S+U and Pb+Pb collisions at the SPS. The former are reproduced reasonably well with no new parameters, but not the latter.

## Key claim

The deviation from linear scaling in atomic number A for Drell-Yan cross sections in 800 GeV proton-nucleus collisions can be accounted for by energy degradation of the proton combined with a finite quantum coherence time, yielding a proper coherence time of 0.4 ± 0.1 fm/c; for J/ψ production, the best representation requires both this coherence effect and a nuclear absorption cross section of 3.6 mb, consistent with photoproduction values.

## Method

The authors used a linear extrapolation from proton-proton collisions (implemented via the LEXUS model) to describe proton-nucleus collisions, incorporating energy loss of the beam proton as it traverses the nucleus and a time delay for particle production due to quantum coherence (Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect). For J/ψ production, they additionally included absorption of the J/ψ on nucleons, parametrized by an absorption cross section. The coherence time was inferred by comparing calculated Drell-Yan cross sections to E772 data at 800 GeV, then applied to J/ψ data from NA38 at 200 and 450 GeV/c to extract the absorption cross section. Finally, the model was applied to S+U and Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies with no new parameters.

## Key numbers

- Proper coherence time for Drell-Yan production: 0.4 ± 0.1 fm/c, inferred from 800 GeV proton-nucleus data (E772) with targets C, Ca, Fe, W, Pb.
- Best-fit coherence time for J/ψ production: 0.5 fm/c, consistent with Drell-Yan value.
- Best-fit J/ψ absorption cross section on nucleons: 3.6 mb, from combined fit to 200 and 450 GeV/c proton-nucleus data.
- For S+U collisions at 200 GeV/c: measured Drell-Yan cross section 310 ± 10 ± 25 nb; measured J/ψ cross section 7.78 ± 0.04 ± 0.62 μb.
- For Pb+Pb collisions at 158 GeV/c: measured Drell-Yan cross section 1.49 ± 0.01 ± 0.11 μb; measured J/ψ cross section 21.9 ± 0.02 ± 1.6 μb.
- Nucleon-nucleon J/ψ production parametrization: B σ_{NN→J/ψ}(x_F > 0) = 37 (1 - m_{J/ψ}/√s)^{12} nb, with x_F dependence dσ/dx_F ∝ (1-|x_F|)^5.

## Context

This work extends the LEXUS model (developed by the authors in prior papers) to simultaneously describe Drell-Yan and J/ψ production in proton-nucleus collisions, resolving a long-standing discrepancy between J/ψ absorption cross sections extracted from proton-nucleus experiments (6-7 mb in earlier Glauber analyses by Dima et al.) and those from photoproduction experiments. The analysis enables parameter-free calculations of J/ψ production in nucleus-nucleus collisions; the model reproduces S+U data reasonably well but fails for Pb+Pb data, suggesting the need for additional physics such as absorption on co-movers or quark-gluon plasma formation, as originally proposed by Matsui and Satz. The authors note that parton energy loss calculations by Gavin and Milana and nuclear shadowing effects also need to be considered in future work.
