# Alpha-decay half-lives and Q_alpha values of superheavy nuclei

> The alpha-decay half-lives of recently synthesized superheavy nuclei (SHN) are investigated based on a unified fission model (UFM) where a new method to calculate the assault frequency of alpha-emission is used. The excellent agreement with the experimental data indicates the UFM is a useful tool to investigate these alpha-decays. It is found that the half-lives become more and more insensitive to the Q_alpha values as the atomic number increases on the whole, which is favorable for us to predict the half-lives of SHN. In addition, a formula is suggested to compute the Q_alpha values for the nuclei with Z > 92 and N > 140 with a good accuracy, according to which the long-lived SHN should be neutron rich. With Q_alpha values from this formula as inputs, we predict the half-lives of isotopes of Z = 117, which may be useful for experimental identication in the future.

## Key claim
The authors show that the Unified Fission Model (UFM) with a new phenomenological assault frequency reproduces experimental alpha-decay half-lives of superheavy nuclei (SHN) well, but they note that for some odd-A and odd-odd nuclei in specific decay chains the half-lives are underestimated by a few times, possibly due to unaccounted nonzero angular momentum transfers.

## Method
The alpha-decay half-life is calculated using the WKB barrier penetration probability within the UFM, where the potential includes Coulomb, nuclear proximity, and centrifugal terms. The assault frequency is derived from a harmonic oscillator model using the Virial theorem, relating it to the principal quantum number and nuclear radius. A separate five-parameter formula for Q_alpha values is fitted to 154 experimental data points for nuclei with Z >= 92 and N >= 140, based on a simplified liquid-drop-type binding energy expression.

## Key numbers
- For 294118 (Z=118), the UFM gives a half-life of 0.59 (+0.23/-0.16) ms compared to the experimental 0.89 (+1.07/-0.31) ms, using Q_alpha = 11.81 +/- 0.06 MeV.
- For 293116 (Z=116), the UFM gives 93.2 (+42.2/-28.8) ms versus experimental 53 (+62/-19) ms with Q_alpha = 10.67 +/- 0.06 MeV.
- The Q_alpha formula yields a standard deviation of 0.183 MeV and an average absolute deviation of 0.137 MeV across 154 nuclei.
- For 294118, an increase in Q_alpha of 0.4 MeV reduces the half-life by only one order of magnitude; for 147Sm (Z=62), the same increase reduces it by five orders of magnitude.
- Predicted half-lives for Z=117 isotopes range from 0.17 ms (288117) to 0.77 s (299117), with Q_alpha values from 11.94 MeV to 10.43 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the Unified Fission Model previously applied to proton radioactivity and cluster radioactivity by Balasubramaniam and Arunachalam (2001) and the authors' own prior work (2009). It provides an alternative to the Density-Dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective interaction and the Generalized Liquid Drop Model (GLDM) for alpha-decay calculations, which the authors note overestimate and underestimate half-lives respectively. The Q_alpha formula enables predictions for unobserved nuclei such as Z=117 isotopes and suggests that long-lived superheavy nuclei should be neutron-rich, guiding future experiments at upcoming radioactive-ion-beam facilities.
