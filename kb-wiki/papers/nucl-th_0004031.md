# Hyperon-hyperon interactions and properties of neutron matter

> We present results from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculatons for beta stable neutron star matter with nucleonic and hyperonic degress degrees of freedom, employing the most recent parametrizations of the baryon-baryon interaction of the Nijmegen group. It is found that the only strange baryons emergin in beta stable matter up to total barionic densities of 1.2 fm^-3 are $Σ^-$ and $Λ$. The corresponding equations of state are then used to compute properties of neutron stars such as masses and radii.

# Hyperon-hyperon interactions and properties of neutron star matter

## Key claim

The inclusion of hyperon-hyperon (YY) interactions, which are attractive over the entire density range explored, produces a further softening of the equation of state beyond that from hyperon-nucleon (YN) interactions alone, reducing the maximum neutron star mass to approximately 1.34 solar masses (without rotation), a result that depends on the specific Nijmegen NSC97e parametrization and the choice of nucleonic equation of state (APR98 or NSC97e).

## Method

The authors performed Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations for beta-stable neutron star matter including nucleons (n, p) and hyperons from the full baryon octet, using the Nijmegen NSC97e baryon-baryon potential for all strangeness sectors (S=0 to S=-4). The nucleonic sector was treated either with the same NSC97e interaction or with the variational APR98 equation of state (which includes three-body forces and relativistic boost corrections), while hyperonic contributions (YN and YY) were always computed from NSC97e. The G-matrix was solved self-consistently for single-particle energies, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation was solved to obtain neutron star masses and radii, with and without rotational corrections following the Hartle approach.

## Key numbers

- Onset density for Sigma- hyperons: 0.27 fm^-3 (APR98 nucleonic EoS) versus 0.34 fm^-3 (NSC97e nucleonic EoS)
- Onset density for Lambda hyperons: 0.67 fm^-3 (APR98 case); no Lambda appeared up to 1.2 fm^-3 in the NSC97e case
- Maximum neutron star mass with nucleons only (APR98): 1.89 solar masses (no rotation), 2.11 solar masses (with rotation)
- Maximum neutron star mass with nucleons + hyperons including YN but not YY: 1.47 solar masses (no rotation), 1.60 solar masses (with rotation)
- Maximum neutron star mass with nucleons + hyperons including both YN and YY: 1.34 solar masses (no rotation), 1.44 solar masses (with rotation)
- Reduction in maximum mass due to hyperonic degrees of freedom: approximately 0.5 to 0.7 solar masses
- Only Sigma- and Lambda hyperons appear up to total baryonic density of 1.2 fm^-3

## Context

This work extends earlier microscopic calculations by Schulze et al. (1998, 2000) that included hyperon-nucleon interactions but omitted hyperon-hyperon interactions, and differs from relativistic mean field calculations by Prakash et al. (1997) and others where all hyperon species appear at the densities considered. The results show that the YY interaction, particularly the strongly attractive Sigma-Sigma channel, further softens the equation of state, reducing maximum masses below the approximately 1.4 solar mass threshold. This enables a direct comparison with observed neutron star masses from binary pulsars (approximately 1.35 solar masses) and from kilohertz QPO observations (up to approximately 2.2 solar masses), highlighting the tension between hyperon softening and the need for a stiffer EoS to support the heaviest observed neutron stars.
