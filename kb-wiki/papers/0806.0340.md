# Chiral Unitary Dynamics of Hadrons and Hadrons in a Nuclear Medium

> In this talk I summarize recent findings around the description of axial vector mesons as dynamically generated states from the interaction of pseudoscalar mesons and vector mesons, dedicating some attention to the two $K_1(1270)$ states. Then I review the generation of open and hidden charm scalar and axial states, and how some recent experiment supports the existence of the new hidden charm scalar state predicted. I present recent results showing that the low lying $1/2^+$ baryon resonances for S=-1 can be obtained as bound states or resonances of two mesons and one baryon in coupled channels. Then show the differences with the S=0 case, where the $N^*(1710)$ appears also dynamically generated from the two pion one nucleon system, but the $N^*(1440)$ does not appear, indicating a more complex structure of the Roper resonance. Finally I shall show how the state X(2175), recently discovered at BABAR and BES, appears naturally as a resonance of the $ϕK \bar{K}$ system.

## Key claim

The paper argues that many low-lying meson and baryon resonances can be understood as dynamically generated states arising from the unitary interactions of their constituent hadrons in coupled channels, rather than as elementary quark-model states. The key hedge is that this mechanism successfully describes the two \(K_1(1270)\) states, the hidden charm scalar X(3700), and several \(1/2^+\) baryon resonances, but explicitly fails for the Roper \(N^*(1440)\), indicating a more complex structure for that resonance.

## Method

The authors combine chiral Lagrangians with nonperturbative unitary techniques (Bethe-Salpeter equation, N/D method, or Inverse Amplitude Method) in coupled channels to compute scattering amplitudes for meson-meson, meson-baryon, and three-body systems. For three-body systems (two mesons plus one baryon, or three mesons), they develop a new formalism based on Faddeev equations where the off-shell parts of two-body t-matrices cancel with three-body contact forces from the same chiral Lagrangians, allowing the use of on-shell amplitudes. The resulting algebraic equations are solved in up to twenty-two coupled channels to identify poles corresponding to resonances.

## Key numbers

- Two \(K_1\) states found with masses 1197 MeV and 1284 MeV, and widths of about 240 MeV and 140 MeV respectively, after fine tuning.
- Hidden charm scalar state X(3700) predicted with pole position 3718.93 - i0.06 MeV for \(\alpha_H = -1.3\), with \(\chi^2/d.o.f = 0.85\) when compared to Belle data for \(e^+ e^- \to J/\psi D \bar D\).
- \(D_{s0}^*(2317)\) appears at 2317.25 MeV with zero width; \(D_0^*(2400)\) appears at 2129.26 - i157.00 MeV.
- Hidden charm axial states: X(3872) at 3837.57 MeV (positive C-parity) and a nearly degenerate state at 3840.69 - i1.60 MeV (negative C-parity).
- \(\Lambda\) resonance found at 1568 MeV in the \(\pi \bar K N\) amplitude for \(I=0\), \(I_{\pi \bar K}=1/2\).
- X(2175) resonance found around 2150 MeV from the \(\phi K \bar K\) three-body system, with width compatible with experiment.
- Breit-Wigner fit to the Belle \(D\bar D\) data gives \(\chi^2/d.o.f = 2.10\) for \(M_X=3878\) MeV, \(\Gamma_X=347\) MeV, improving to 1.12 for \(M_X=3750\) MeV, \(\Gamma_X=250\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to scalar mesons like \(\sigma(600)\), \(f_0(980)\), \(a_0(980)\), and \(\kappa(800)\) (Gasser and Leutwyler, 1984; Oller and Oset, 1997) and to \(1/2^-\) baryon resonances (Kaiser et al., 1995; Oset and Ramos, 1998) into the axial vector meson sector, the charm sector, and three-body systems. It directly builds on prior studies of axial vector mesons (Lutz and Kolomeitsev, 2004; Roca et al., 2005) and scalar charm states (Kolomeitsev and Lutz, 2004; Hofmann and Lutz, 2005; Guo et al., 2006). The three-body formalism enables the interpretation of the X(2175) discovered at BABAR (Aubert et al., 2006, 2007) and BES (2007) as a \(\phi K \bar K\) resonance, and provides a new picture for low-lying \(1/2^+\) baryon resonances with \(S=-1\) as two-meson-one-baryon bound states. The failure to generate the Roper \(N^*(1440)\) in the \(\pi \pi N\) system points toward more complex dynamics, consistent with the multi-channel study of Krehl et al. (2000).
