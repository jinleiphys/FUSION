# Heavy Neutron Star Phenomenology with an H-dibaryon

> The equation of state for dense nuclear matter in $β$-equilibrium is explored including the possibility of a doubly-strange H-particle. Consistent with experimental constraints, the mass of the H in free space is taken to be near the $Λ\, Λ$ threshold. Within the quark-meson coupling model, which we use, no new parameters are required to describe the interaction between the H-dibaryon and the other baryons. The maximum mass is only slightly reduced, and the tidal deformability is essentially unchanged with this addition. In heavy neutron stars the H is abundant and extends as far as 6 km from the center of the core.

## Key claim

The H-dibaryon, with a free-space mass near the ΛΛ threshold (consistent with experimental constraints that rule out a deeply bound state), can appear in heavy neutron stars without requiring new parameters in the quark-meson coupling model; its presence only slightly reduces the maximum mass and leaves the tidal deformability essentially unchanged, provided the H is unbound in free space.

## Method

The authors extended the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, previously applied to hyperonic neutron star matter, to include the H-dibaryon as a spin-0, isospin-0 boson that forms a condensate at zero momentum. The meson couplings to the H were determined from the model's quark-level couplings with no free parameters, using a free bag radius for the H that is 1.2 times the nucleon bag radius. The equation of state for β-equilibrium matter was computed including neutrons, protons, Λ, Ξ⁻, Ξ⁰, and the H-dibaryon, with an overlap term to model short-distance repulsion at high densities, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved to obtain neutron star masses and radii.

## Key numbers

- The H-dibaryon free mass is taken as M_H = 2247, 2258, and 2269 MeV, corresponding to binding energies B_H = -26 ± 11 MeV relative to the ΛΛ threshold (2M_Λ ≈ 2231 MeV).
- The H is bound by 85 MeV in neutron star matter and 79 MeV in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density.
- The maximum neutron star masses are 2.08 M_⊙ (M_H = 2247 MeV), 2.09 M_⊙ (M_H = 2258 MeV), and 2.10 M_⊙ (M_H = 2269 MeV), compared to 2.12 M_⊙ without the H-dibaryon.
- The corresponding radii at maximum mass are 10.91 km (M_H = 2247 and 2258 MeV) and 10.94 km (M_H = 2269 MeV), versus 11.0 km without the H.
- The tidal deformability for a 1.4 M_⊙ star remains Λ_1.4 = 430, unchanged from the case without the H-dibaryon.
- The H-dibaryon extends up to approximately 6.5 km from the center of the core in a maximum mass star.
- The H appears at a number density around 0.6 fm⁻³, well above three times nuclear saturation density.

## Context

This work extends the QMC model calculations of Leong et al. (2023) for hyperonic neutron star matter to include the H-dibaryon, and contrasts with earlier studies such as Glendenning (1998) that assumed a bound H with adjustable couplings. The results are consistent with the lattice QCD extrapolation by Shanahan et al. (2013) suggesting the H is unbound by about 26 ± 11 MeV, and with the experimental lower bound from the NAGARA event (M_H > 2224 MeV). The paper enables future comparisons with NICER and gravitational wave observations of heavy neutron stars, showing that an H-dibaryon condensate in the core is compatible with current constraints.
