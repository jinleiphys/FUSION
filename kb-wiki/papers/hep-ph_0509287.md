# Model study of generalized parton distributions with helicity flip

> Generalized parton distributions with helicity flip are studied in the quark sector, within a simple version of the MIT bag model, assuming an SU(6) wave function for the proton target. In the framework under scrutiny it turns out that only the generalized transversity distribution, H_T^q, is non vanishing. For this quantity, the forward limit is properly recovered and numerical results are found to underestimate recent lattice data for its first moment. Positivity bounds recently proposed are fulfilled by the obtained distribution. The relevance of the analysis for the planning of measurements of the quark generalized transversity is addressed.

# Model study of generalized parton distributions with helicity flip

## Key claim
Within a simple MIT bag model assuming SU(6) symmetry for the proton, only the generalized transversity distribution H_T^q is non-vanishing among the four helicity-flip generalized parton distributions; the numerical results underestimate recent lattice data for the first moment of H_T^q, but satisfy recently proposed positivity bounds.

## Method
The author evaluates the quark helicity-flip GPD matrix elements in the Breit frame using the MIT bag model with quarks in the lowest energy state, following the approach of Ji (1998) for helicity-conserving GPDs. The calculation employs an SU(6) spin-flavor wave function for the proton and a prescription to partially restore momentum conservation by taking the effective momentum transfer through the active quark as ηΔ, with η fixed by fitting nucleon electromagnetic form factors. The resulting expression for the non-vanishing amplitude A^q_{++,--} is integrated to obtain H_T^q(x,ξ,Δ²) at a low factorization scale μ₀ = 0.4 GeV.

## Key numbers
- The bag radius R is related to the quark energy ε₀ = ω₀/R with ω₀ = 2.04, and the relation R m = 4 ω₀ is used.
- Results are shown for Δ² = -0.5 GeV² and Δ² = -1.0 GeV², with the parameter η taken as 0.55 for the presented figures.
- The isovector tensor form factor calculated with η = 0.35 and η = 0.55 lies below the dipole fit of lattice data from Göckeler et al. (2005).
- The calculated isovector tensor form factor is divided by 1.35, the isovector tensor charge predicted by the MIT bag model with SU(6) symmetry; the same lattice calculation yields the value 1.09 for this quantity.
- The SU(6) u-flavor distribution is obtained by multiplying the results by 4/3; the d-flavor by multiplying by -1/3.

## Context
This work extends the MIT bag model approach of Ji (1998) for helicity-conserving GPDs to the helicity-flip sector, providing the first model calculation of these quantities. The results are compared to lattice data from Göckeler et al. (2005) for the tensor form factor and to positivity bounds derived by Goeke et al. (2005). The analysis is motivated by the need to estimate cross sections for diffractive double meson production, the process proposed by Pire (2005) to access the quark generalized transversity in the ERBL region. The study enables future phenomenological work by relaxing the SU(6) assumption, including antiquark degrees of freedom, improving translational invariance restoration, and evolving results to experimental scales.
