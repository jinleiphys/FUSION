# Applicability of Relativistic Point-Coupling Models to Neutron Star Physics

> Comparing with a wide range of covariant energy density functional models based on the finite-range meson-exchange representation, the relativistic mean-field models with the zero-range contact interaction, namely the relativistic point-coupling models, are still infrequent to be utilized in establishing nuclear equation of state (EoS) and investigating neutron star properties, although comprehensive applications and achievements of them in describing many nuclear properties both in ground and exited states are mature. In this work, the EoS of neutron star matter is established constructively in the framework of the relativistic point-coupling models to study neutron star physics. Taking two selected functionals DD-PC1 and PC-PK1 as examples, nuclear symmetry energies and several neutron star properties including proton fractions, mass-radius relations, the core-crust transition density, the fraction of crustal moment of inertia and dimensionless tidal deformabilities are discussed. A suppression of pressure of neutron star matter found in the functional PC-PK1 at high densities results in the difficulty of its prediction when approaching to the maximum mass of neutron stars. In addition, the divergences between two selected functionals in describing neutron star quantities mentioned above are still large, ascribing to the less constrained behavior of these functionals at high densities. Then it is expected that the constraints on the dense matter EoS from precise and massive modern astronomical observations, such as the tidal-deformabilities taken from gravitational-wave events, would be essential to improve the parameterizing of the relativistic point-coupling models.

# Applicability of Relativistic Point-Coupling Models to Neutron Star Physics

## Key claim
The relativistic point-coupling (PC-RMF) models can be used to construct neutron star equations of state, but the two selected functionals (DD-PC1 and PC-PK1) show large divergences in predicting neutron star properties at high densities, and the PC-PK1 functional exhibits a suppression of pressure at high densities that prevents it from reaching a maximum mass limit, indicating that these models are not yet sufficiently constrained for reliable neutron star predictions without additional astronomical data.

## Method
The authors constructed the equation of state (EoS) of neutron star matter under beta-equilibrium using two relativistic point-coupling models: the nonlinear PC-RMF functional PC-PK1 and the density-dependent PC-RMF functional DD-PC1. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations for static, spherically symmetric neutron stars, matching the core EoS with the BPS and BBP EoS for the crust region. The core-crust transition density was determined using the thermodynamical method by finding where the stability condition V_ther(ρ)=0 is satisfied.

## Key numbers
- DD-PC1: saturation density ρ₀ = 0.152 fm⁻³, symmetry energy S₂(ρ₀) = 33.01 MeV, symmetry energy slope L = 70.1 MeV
- PC-PK1: saturation density ρ₀ = 0.153 fm⁻³, symmetry energy S₂(ρ₀) = 35.61 MeV, symmetry energy slope L = 112.9 MeV
- PC-PK1 predicts the DUrca process threshold at baryon density ρ_DU ≈ 0.25 fm⁻³, corresponding to a star mass of 1.06 M_⊙
- FSUGold predicts the DUrca process for masses larger than 1.49 M_⊙ and central density ρ ≳ 0.58 fm⁻³
- DD-PC1 and TW99 do not support the onset of the DUrca process at any density
- PC-PK1 mass-radius curve stops around 2.30 M_⊙ and cannot reach a maximum mass limit
- Core-crust transition density: DD-PC1 ρ_t = 0.0794 fm⁻³, PC-PK1 ρ_t = 0.0629 fm⁻³
- Core-crust transition pressure: DD-PC1 P_t = 0.4850 MeV fm⁻³, PC-PK1 P_t = 0.3184 MeV fm⁻³
- The DUrca process threshold proton fraction is χ_p = 14.8% (assuming massless muons)

## Context
This work extends the application of relativistic point-coupling models, which have been widely used for finite nuclei properties (e.g., PC-PK1 by Zhao (2010) for nuclear masses and charge radii), into neutron star physics where finite-range meson-exchange models (such as DD-RMF functionals PKDD and TW99, NL-RMF functionals FSUGold and PK1, and DD-RHF functionals PKA1 and PKO1) have been more commonly employed. The results show that the PC-RMF functionals require additional constraints from modern astronomical observations, particularly the tidal deformabilities from gravitational-wave events like GW170817 (Abbott 2017), to improve their parameterization at high densities. The work enables future studies to use gravitational-wave data to calibrate point-coupling models for dense matter EoS.
