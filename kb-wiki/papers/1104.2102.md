# Precision benchmark calculations for four particles at unitarity

> The unitarity limit describes interacting particles where the range of the interaction is zero and the scattering length is infinite. We present precision benchmark calculations for two-component fermions at unitarity using three different ab initio methods: Hamiltonian lattice formalism using iterated eigenvector methods, Euclidean lattice formalism with auxiliary-field projection Monte Carlo, and continuum diffusion Monte Carlo with fixed and released nodes. We have calculated the ground state energy of the unpolarized four-particle system in a periodic cube as a dimensionless fraction of the ground state energy for the non-interacting system. We obtain values 0.211(2) and 0.210(2) using two different Hamiltonian lattice representations, 0.206(9) using Euclidean lattice, and an upper bound of 0.212(2) from fixed-node diffusion Monte Carlo. Released-node calculations starting from the fixed-node result yield a decrease of less than 0.002 over a propagation of 0.4/E_F in Euclidean time, where E_F is the Fermi energy. We find good agreement among all three ab initio methods.

## Key claim

The paper provides precision benchmark calculations for the ground state energy ratio of four unpolarized fermions at unitarity using three different ab initio methods, finding good agreement among them. The abstract glosses over the fact that the results are specifically for the four-particle system (two spin-up and two spin-down fermions) in a periodic cube, and that the Hamiltonian lattice results required extrapolation to the infinite volume limit with an estimated extrapolation error of plus or minus 0.002.

## Method

The authors used three independent ab initio methods: Hamiltonian lattice formalism with Lanczos sparse-matrix eigenvector iteration, Euclidean lattice formalism with auxiliary-field projection Monte Carlo using bounded continuous auxiliary fields, and continuum diffusion Monte Carlo with fixed and released nodes. For the lattice calculations, they tuned interaction coefficients using Luscher's formula to set the S-wave scattering length to infinity, with the second Hamiltonian also setting the effective range to zero. The diffusion Monte Carlo calculation used a Poschl-Teller potential tuned to infinite scattering length with a BCS-type pairing wavefunction multiplied by a Jastrow factor as the trial wavefunction.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy ratio xi_{2,2} = 0.211(2) using Hamiltonian lattice H1 (single-site contact interaction)
- Ground state energy ratio xi_{2,2} = 0.210(2) using Hamiltonian lattice H2 (contact plus nearest-neighbor interactions)
- Ground state energy ratio xi_{2,2} = 0.206(9) using Euclidean lattice with auxiliary-field projection Monte Carlo
- Upper bound of xi_{2,2} = 0.212(2) from fixed-node diffusion Monte Carlo
- Released-node calculations yielded a decrease of less than 0.002 over a propagation of 0.4/E_F in Euclidean time, where E_F is the Fermi energy
- Interaction coefficients for the Hamiltonian lattices: mC1 = -3.9570, mC2 = -3.7235, mC2' = -0.3008
- Interaction coefficient for the Euclidean lattice: mC = -3.4938

## Context

This work extends previous lattice calculations by Lee (2005, 2008) and diffusion Monte Carlo calculations by Carlson (2003) and Astrakharchik (2004), which had produced a wide spread of values for the energy ratio xi ranging from 0.2 to 0.6. The benchmark results for the four-particle system enable more reliable comparisons between different numerical methods and provide a foundation for understanding systematic errors in calculations of the unitary Fermi gas, which is relevant to cold dilute neutron matter found in the inner crust of neutron stars.
