# Mass corrections for the bound states in the muonic molecular ions

> The mass corrections to the bound state energies of the three-body muonic molecular ions $ppμ, pdμ, ptμ, ddμ, dtμ$ and $ttμ$ are determined numerically from the results of highly accurate computations. The total energies and some other bound state properties of these ions are evaluated to very high accuracy for the bound $S(L = 0)-, P(L = 1)-$ and $D(L = 2)-$states. In these highly accurate calculations we used the most recent and accurate masses of particles $m_p, m_d, m_t$ and $m_μ$ known from high energy experiments.

# Mass corrections for the bound states in the muonic molecular ions

## Key claim
The mass corrections to bound state energies of six muonic molecular ions (ppμ, pdμ, ptμ, ddμ, dtμ, ttμ) are computed using updated particle masses from recent high-energy experiments, yielding total energies with up to 21-22 correct decimal digits for some states. The hyperfine structure splittings for the ground states of pdμ, ptμ, and dtμ are determined from highly accurate expectation values of interparticle delta-functions, with the caveat that the F(L=3) state in ttμ could not be computed due to loss of the specialized code.

## Method
The non-relativistic three-body Schrodinger equation was solved using an exponential variational expansion in perimetric coordinates with basis sets of up to 3840 functions, employing 64-108 decimal digit arithmetic. Particle masses in MeV/c² (m_μ = 105.65836668, m_p = 938.272046, m_d = 1875.612859, m_t = 2808.290906) were taken as exact, with experimental uncertainties handled by repeating calculations with varied masses. Hyperfine structure splittings were computed from expectation values of interparticle delta-functions using the general formula involving g-factors and fine structure constant.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy of the P*(L=1) state in ddμ: ε = -1.9749828376301(2) eV
- Binding energy of the P*(L=1) state in dtμ: ε = -0.6603325645(2) eV
- Hyperfine structure splitting Δ₁₂ for ptμ ground state: 1.3400149 × 10⁷ MHz
- Hyperfine structure splitting Δ₂₃ for ptμ ground state: 3.3518984 × 10⁷ MHz
- Total energies determined to accuracy ≈ 1 × 10⁻²¹ to 1 × 10⁻²³ muon-atomic units for S- and P-states
- Hyperfine energy of the J=1/2 level in ttμ ground state: ≈ 11.0591 MHz
- Hyperfine energy of the J=1/2 level in ttμ first excited state: ≈ 12.4307 MHz

## Context
This work extends the author's prior calculations (Frolov and Wardlaw, 2011) which used older particle masses from the mid-1990s, updating them to the most recent high-energy experimental values. The mass corrections are obtained by direct subtraction of total energies from the earlier tables, avoiding interpolation formulas that are inaccurate for muonic molecular ions due to multiple non-small mass ratios. The hyperfine structure results enable comparison with modern experiments and provide input for calculations of more complex four-, five-, and six-body quasi-atoms containing muonic molecular ions. The classification of bound state spectra follows the general theory developed by Frolov and Bishop (1991) for three-body Coulomb systems with unit charges.
