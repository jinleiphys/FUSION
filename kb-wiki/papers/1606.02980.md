# Strangeness suppression in the unquenched quark model

> In this contribution, we discuss the strangeness suppression in the proton in the framework of the unquenched quark model. The theoretical results are in good agreement with the values extracted from CERN and JLab experiments.

## Key claim

The unquenched quark model (UQM) reproduces the observed ratios of baryon-meson electroproduction cross sections and the strangeness suppression in the proton, with the theoretical results agreeing with values extracted from both exclusive CERN/JLab reactions and high-energy production, without any optimization of the model's free parameter.

## Method

The authors applied the unquenched quark model, which incorporates quark-antiquark pair effects via a $^{3}P_{0}$ mechanism, to compute production ratios of baryon-meson final states. The ratios were expressed as the product of a spin-flavor-isospin factor and a radial integral over the momentum of the emitted meson, with the energy denominator representing the energy difference between initial and final hadrons in the rest frame of the initial baryon. The single free parameter $F^2$, which depends on the harmonic oscillator wave function sizes and the Gaussian smearing of the pair-creation vertex, was taken from prior work (Geiger, 1994) as $F^2=2.275$ GeV$^{-2}$.

## Key numbers

- Ratio $p \rightarrow \Lambda K^+ / p \rightarrow n\pi^+$: UQM = 0.227, experiment = $0.19 \pm 0.01 \pm 0.03$ (CLAS Collaboration, 2016)
- Ratio $p \rightarrow \Lambda K^+ / p \rightarrow p\pi^0$: UQM = 0.454, experiment = $0.50 \pm 0.02 \pm 0.12$ (CLAS Collaboration, 2016)
- Ratio $p \rightarrow p\pi^0 / p \rightarrow n\pi^+$: UQM = 0.500, experiment = $0.43 \pm 0.01 \pm 0.09$ (CLAS Collaboration, 2016)
- Strangeness suppression $s\bar{s}/d\bar{d}$: UQM = 0.265, experiment = $0.22 \pm 0.07$ (CLAS Collaboration, 2016)
- Strangeness suppression $u\bar{u}/d\bar{d}$: UQM = 0.568, experiment = $0.74 \pm 0.18$ (CLAS Collaboration, 2016)
- Strangeness suppression $2s\bar{s}/(u\bar{u}+d\bar{d})$: UQM = 0.338, experiment = $0.25 \pm 0.08$ (CLAS Collaboration, 2016) and $0.29 \pm 0.02$ (Bocquet, 1996)

## Context

This work extends the unquenched quark model framework, previously applied to hadron spectroscopy, to the analysis of strangeness suppression in exclusive electroproduction reactions measured by the CLAS Collaboration (Mestayer, 2016). The results confirm the importance of $qqq-q\bar{q}$ multiquark configurations in the proton, which were previously inferred from the violation of the Gottfried sum rule and the proton spin crisis. The UQM predictions for strangeness suppression agree with both the exclusive reaction data from JLab and the high-energy production results from Bocquet (1996), demonstrating the model's consistency across different energy regimes.
