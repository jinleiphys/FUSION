# Possible existence of bound nuclei beyond neutron drip lines driven by deformation

> Based on the relativistic calculations of the nuclear masses in the transfermium region from No $(Z=102)$ to Ds $(Z=110)$ by the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum, the possible existence of the bound nuclei beyond the neutron drip lines is studied. The two-neutron and multi-neutron emission bound nuclei beyond the primary neutron drip line of $N=258$ are predicted in $Z=106,108$ and $110$ isotopes. Detailed microscopic mechanism investigation reveals that nuclear deformation plays a vital role in the existence of the bound nuclei beyond the drip line. Furthermore, not only the quadrupole deformation $β_{2}$, but also the higher orders of deformation are indispensible in the reliable description of the phenomenon of the reentrant binding.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that bound nuclei can exist beyond the primary neutron drip line at N=258 for isotopes of Sg (Z=106), Hs (Z=108), and Ds (Z=110), but only if the nuclei are deformed; the abstract's claim of "possible existence" is hedged by the condition that these nuclei are bound against two-neutron emission while remaining unbound against multi-neutron emission.

## Method

The authors used the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) with the density functional PC-PK1 to calculate nuclear masses for even-even isotopes from No (Z=102) to Ds (Z=110). The relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equations were solved in a spherical Dirac Woods-Saxon basis with a box size of 20 fm, and the neutron drip line was defined by the two-neutron separation energy S2n becoming negative. The calculations included reflection-symmetric nuclear shapes with Legendre expansion up to λmax = 10.

## Key numbers

- The primary neutron drip line is predicted at N=258 for all isotopes from No to Ds, consistent with prior calculations by Erler (2012) and Afanasjev (2013).
- For Sg (Z=106), nuclei with 266 ≤ N ≤ 284 are bound against two-neutron emission but unbound against multi-neutron emission.
- For Hs (Z=108), nuclei with 266 ≤ N ≤ 288 are bound against two-neutron emission, with those having 266 ≤ N ≤ 274 unbound against multi-neutron emission.
- For Ds (Z=110), nuclei with 266 ≤ N ≤ 290 are bound against two-neutron emission, with N=266 and N=268 unbound against multi-neutron emission.
- For Ds isotopes, the two-neutron separation energy S2n for 370Ds (N=260) is -0.576 MeV, for 372Ds (N=262) is -0.552 MeV, and for 374Ds (N=264) is -0.293 MeV; S2n becomes positive at 376Ds (N=266) with 0.517 MeV.
- The quadrupole deformation β2 for Ds isotopes increases from 0.000 at N=258 to 0.277 at N=294.

## Context

This work extends prior predictions of "reentrant binding" beyond the neutron drip line, which were previously indicated for light nuclei by Stoitsov (2000) and later found throughout the nuclear landscape for Z=60-100 by various density functional theories including Delaroche (2010), Goriely (2010), Erler (2012), and Afanasjev (2013). The calculations confirm the spherical neutron shell closure at N=258 predicted by Erler (2012) and Zhang (2005). The results enable future investigation of heavier elements along this isotopic chain, as noted by Zhang (2021), and provide guidance for experimental searches with next-generation radioactive ion-beam facilities.
