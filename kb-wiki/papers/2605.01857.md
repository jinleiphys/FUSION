# Boost-invariant and cylindrically symmetric perfect spin hydrodynamics

> Equations of a boost-invariant and cylindrically symmetric perfect hydrodynamics are solved numerically for initial conditions inspired by the wounded nucleon model. The energy-momentum and spin tensors are used in the form that describes a relativistic massive gas governed by Boltzmann statistics. In contrast to one dimensional boost-invariant expansion, we find a coupling between the azimuthal and longitudinal components of the electric and magnetic components of the spin polarization tensor. This feature is similar to that found earlier for the Gubser symmetry, however, our treatment allows for a more general form of initial conditions and expansion geometry. Defining the freeze-out hypersurface by the constant temperature condition, we evaluate the Pauli-Lubański four-vector and find that for the assumed geometry the only nonzero total polarization may be induced by the longitudinal component of the magnetic part of the spin polarization tensor coupled with the azimuthal electric component. The obtained results may serve as a reference point for more realistic models of hydrodynamic expansion.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in boost-invariant and cylindrically symmetric perfect spin hydrodynamics, a coupling emerges between the azimuthal and longitudinal components of the electric and magnetic parts of the spin polarization tensor, a feature absent in one-dimensional boost-invariant expansion; however, the authors note that this geometry cannot address global or longitudinal polarization phenomena commonly discussed in spin physics.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the conservation equations for baryon number, energy-momentum, and spin for a boost-invariant and cylindrically symmetric system, using the GLW (de Groot–van Leeuwen–van Weert) forms of the energy-momentum and spin tensors for a relativistic massive gas obeying Boltzmann statistics. The spinless hydrodynamic background was solved first using initial conditions inspired by the wounded nucleon model, with transverse profiles of entropy density proportional to Woods–Saxon nucleon distributions, and then the spin polarization tensor evolution was computed in that background. The freeze-out hypersurface was defined by a constant temperature condition to evaluate the Pauli–Lubański four-vector.

## Key numbers

- Initial proper time set to τ₀ = 1.0 fm; nuclear radius R = 7.0 fm; skin thickness a = 0.54 fm
- Central initial temperature T₀ = 0.3 GeV; vacuum temperature T_vac = 0.03 GeV; central baryon chemical potential μ₀ = 0.03 GeV
- Particle masses considered: m = 1 GeV and m = 0.3 GeV
- Radial grid extended to r_max = 20.0 fm with spatial resolution Δr = 0.05 fm; temporal evolution computed up to τ_max = 10.0 fm
- Initial transverse fluid rapidity set to θ(τ₀, r) = 0.01

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of one-dimensional boost-invariant expansion (Florkowski 2019, Singh 2021, Drogosz 2024) and Gubser-symmetric flow (Singh 2020, Singh 2026) by introducing cylindrical symmetry with numerical solutions in the transverse plane, filling a gap between 1D and full 3D spin hydrodynamics (Singh 2024, Sapna 2025). The results provide a reference point for more realistic models of hydrodynamic expansion and may inform future less restrictive analyses that should reduce to these findings in special cases.
