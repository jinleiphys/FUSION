# Parity and time-reversal violating nuclear forces with explicit $Δ$-excitations

> We emphasize the usefulness of treating delta resonances as explicit degrees of freedom in applications of chiral effective field theory (EFT) to parity-violating and time-reversal-violating (PVTV) nuclear interactions. Compared with the delta-less framework, the explicit inclusion of the delta isobar allows one to resum certain types of contributions to the PVTV two-pion exchange two- and three-nucleon potentials without at the same time introducing any unknown parameters up to next-to-next-to-leading order in the EFT expansion. We provide the corresponding expressions for the delta contributions in momentum and coordinate spaces and compare the convergence of the EFT expansion in both formulations.

## Key claim

Treating delta resonances as explicit degrees of freedom in chiral effective field theory allows one to resum certain types of contributions to the parity-violating and time-reversal-violating (PVTV) two-pion exchange two- and three-nucleon potentials without introducing any unknown parameters up to next-to-next-to-leading order, but the delta contributions to the N$^2$LO potentials are generally smaller in magnitude than the corresponding nucleonic contributions and become negligible beyond about 1.5 fm.

## Method

The authors derived the long-range PVTV nuclear forces up to N$^2$LO in chiral EFT with explicit delta-isobar degrees of freedom, using the effective chiral Lagrangian with pions, nucleons, and delta resonances. They calculated the two-nucleon and three-nucleon potentials from time-ordered perturbation theory diagrams involving intermediate delta excitations, regularized ultraviolet divergences using dimensional regularization, and compared the convergence of the EFT expansion between the delta-less and delta-full formulations by evaluating the coordinate-space potentials numerically.

## Key numbers

- Delta-nucleon mass splitting: $\Delta = 293$ MeV
- Pion mass: $M_\pi = 138$ MeV; pion decay constant: $F_\pi = 92.4$ MeV; nucleon mass: $m_N = 939$ MeV
- Axial coupling constant of the nucleon: $g_A = 1.29$ (effective value accounting for Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy)
- Pion-nucleon-delta coupling constant: $h_A = 1.34$ (fixed from the width of the delta resonance)
- LECs in delta-less formulation: $c_1 = -0.74$ GeV$^{-1}$, $c_2 = 1.81$ GeV$^{-1}$, $c_3 = -3.61$ GeV$^{-1}$ (from order-$Q^2$ heavy-baryon-NN fit of Siemens (2016))
- LECs in delta-full formulation (after subtracting delta contributions): $c_1 = -0.74$ GeV$^{-1}$, $c_2 = -0.91$ GeV$^{-1}$, $c_3 = -0.89$ GeV$^{-1}$
- Delta-resonance saturation values of LECs: $c_1 = 0$, $c_2 = -c_3 = 4h_A^2/(9\Delta)$

## Context

This work extends previous derivations of PVTV nuclear forces in chiral EFT up through N$^2$LO by Maekawa (2011), Bsaisou (2012, 2014), and Gnech (2019), which were all based on a formulation with only pions and nucleons as degrees of freedom. The explicit inclusion of the delta isobar is motivated by well-known convergence issues in the parity-conserving sector studied by Ordonez (1995), Kaiser (1998), Krebs (2007), and Epelbaum (2007, 2008), where implicit treatment of the delta resonance leads to large numerical values of subleading pion-nucleon LECs. The results enable parameter-free resummation of dominant higher-order contributions to PVTV nuclear forces and provide a framework for analyzing PVTV signals in nuclear systems, including searches for permanent electric dipole moments of nucleons and nuclei.
