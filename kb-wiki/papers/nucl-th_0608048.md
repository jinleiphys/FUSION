# Pairing collectivity in medium-mass neutron-rich nuclei near drip-line

> We look for collective excitations originating from the strong surface pairing in unstable nuclei near the neutron drip-line. The soft dipole excitation is such a pairing mode as it exhibits a character of core-vs-dineutron motion. Possibility of the hydrodynamic phonon mode (the Anderson-Bogoliubov mode) is also discussed.

# Pairing collectivity in medium-mass neutron-rich nuclei near drip-line

## Key claim

The soft dipole excitation in neutron-rich nuclei near the drip-line is a pairing mode exhibiting core-vs-dineutron motion, and this mode persists even with realistic pairing strengths, whereas the Anderson-Bogoliubov hydrodynamic phonon mode emerges only under unrealistically strong pairing conditions (pair gap Δ > 7 MeV).

## Method

The authors performed selfconsistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations for the ground state and static mean fields, combined with continuum quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) for the response. They used the Skyrme SLy4 interaction for the mean field and a density-dependent delta interaction (DDDI) for the pairing channel, with the DDDI parameters tuned to reproduce the pair gap and coherence length from bare-force calculations in uniform neutron matter. The pairing strength was varied artificially by changing the density-dependence parameter η to explore regimes from normal pairing (Δ ~ 1-2 MeV) to extreme strong pairing (Δ ~ 15 MeV) in the nuclei ¹²⁰Sn and ¹⁵⁸Sn.

## Key numbers

- The pair gap in stable Sn isotopes is Δ ~ 1.1-1.4 MeV (experimental value), reproduced with η = 0.71
- The extreme strong pairing case (η = 0) gives a pair potential Δ(r) ~ 15 MeV, which is unrealistically large
- In ¹²⁰Sn with strong pairing (η = 0), a sharp resonance appears at E = 14.4 MeV, identified as the Anderson-Bogoliubov mode
- In ¹⁵⁸Sn with normal pairing (η = 0.71), the soft dipole excitation appears as a low-lying bump around E = 2-8 MeV
- The core-vs-dineutron mode in the quadrupole response emerges only when Δ > 3 MeV (η < 0.5), while in the dipole case it is present even with realistic pairing
- In uniform neutron matter, the coherence length ξ ~ 5 fm at low density ρ/ρ₀ = 1/8 to 1/64, compared to ξ ≳ 10 fm at normal density ρ/ρ₀ = 1 to 1/2

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Matsuo 2005) suggesting di-neutron correlation persists in medium-mass neutron-rich nuclei beyond light two-neutron halo nuclei like ¹¹Li and ⁶He studied by Nakamura, Hansen, Bertsch (1991), Ikeda, Zhukov, Barranco (2001), Aoyama, and Hagino (2005). The paper connects the di-neutron correlation to the BCS-BEC crossover framework developed by Leggett, Nozieres, Randeria, and Engelbrecht, which has been observed in ultracold Fermi gases (Regal). The results enable future experimental study of the soft dipole excitation as a probe of strong surface pairing in nuclei near the drip-line, and suggest that the Anderson-Bogoliubov mode, while theoretically interesting, is unlikely to be observed in realistic nuclei.
