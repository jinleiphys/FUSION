# The angular momentum decomposition in the scalar diquark model

> One of the challenges of hadronic physics is to fully understand the structure of the proton. In particular, there is nowadays a great interest in the decomposition of its total angular momentum into orbital angular momentum and intrinsic spin, as well as identifying contributions from valence quarks, sea quarks and gluons. The most common decompositions of angular momentum are the Jaffe-Manohar (canonical) and Ji (kinetic) decompositions, which differ in the way contributions are attributed to quarks and gluons. Using perturbation theory, explicit one-loop calculations found that the difference between such decompositions vanishes. We justify within the diquark model in QED that the difference appears at two-loop level, supporting the interpretation of such a difference as originating from the torque exerted by the spectator system on the struck quark.

# The angular momentum decomposition in the scalar diquark model

## Key claim

Using perturbation theory within the scalar diquark model in QED, the difference between the Jaffe-Manohar (canonical) and Ji (kinetic) decompositions of angular momentum vanishes at one-loop order but appears at two-loop order, supporting the interpretation that this difference originates from the torque exerted by the spectator system on the struck quark via initial or final state interactions.

## Method

The authors computed the potential momentum (the difference between Ji and Jaffe-Manohar transverse momentum operators) and the potential orbital angular momentum (the difference between Ji and Jaffe-Manohar OAM operators) in a scalar diquark model of the nucleon, using one-loop and two-loop perturbative calculations in an abelian gauge theory. The calculations were performed in light-cone gauge with dimensional regularization, retaining only the divergent pieces of the amplitudes. The model assumes the nucleon splits into a quark and a scalar diquark, with the target having no net electromagnetic charge to emulate color neutrality in QCD.

## Context

This work extends previous one-loop perturbative calculations by Ji (1997) which found that the difference between the Jaffe-Manohar and Ji decompositions vanishes at that order. The authors build on the scalar diquark model framework used by Brodsky and collaborators for studying final state interactions, and on the Chen et al. field splitting formalism for separating pure and physical gauge fields. The results support the lensing mechanism interpretation proposed by Burkardt (2004) connecting the Sivers function to the GPD E, and suggest that a non-vanishing Sivers function is a necessary condition for a non-vanishing difference between the two decompositions. The work enables future two-loop calculations of the potential orbital angular momentum and further exploration of the relationship between transverse-momentum distributions and generalized parton distributions.
