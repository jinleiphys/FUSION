# Magnetically induced QCD Kondo effect

> The "QCD Kondo effect" stems from the color exchange interaction in QCD with non-Abelian property, and can be realized in a high-density quark matter containing heavy-quark impurities. We propose a novel type of the QCD Kondo effect induced by a strong magnetic field. In addition to the fact that the magnetic field does not affect the color degrees of freedom, two properties caused by the Landau quantization in a strong magnetic field are essential for the "magnetically induced QCD Kondo effect"; (1) dimensional reduction to 1+1-dimensions, and (2) finiteness of the density of states for lowest energy quarks. We demonstrate that, in a strong magnetic field $B$, the scattering amplitude of a massless quark off a heavy quark impurity indeed shows a characteristic behavior of the Kondo effect. The resulting Kondo scale is estimated as $Λ_{\rm K} \simeq \sqrt{e_qB}\ α_{s}^{1/3} {\rm{exp}}\{-{4}π/N_{c} α_{s} {\rm{log}}( 4 π/α_{s}) \}$ where $α_{s}$ and $N_c$ are the fine structure constant of strong interaction and the number of colors in QCD, and $e_q$ is the electric charge of light quarks.

## Key claim

The authors propose that a strong magnetic field can induce a QCD Kondo effect even without a Fermi surface, because Landau quantization produces a finite density of states at the lowest energy and dimensional reduction to 1+1 dimensions, analogous to the role of a Fermi surface in the conventional Kondo effect. The claim is conditional on the magnetic field being strong enough that the QCD coupling is small and the lowest Landau level approximation is valid, and on the assumption that magnetic catalysis (which also occurs in strong magnetic fields) does not significantly interfere with the Kondo effect at the scales of interest.

## Method

The authors compute the scattering amplitude of a massless light quark off a heavy quark impurity in the presence of a strong constant magnetic field, using perturbative QCD within the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation. They evaluate the tree-level and one-loop diagrams, project the amplitude onto 1+1 dimensions by integrating out transverse momenta, and derive a renormalization group equation for the effective 1+1-dimensional coupling strength as a function of the energy scale measured from the Fermi level. The Kondo scale is identified as the Landau pole where the effective coupling diverges.

## Key numbers

* The Kondo scale is estimated as $\Lambda_{\rm K} \simeq \sqrt{e_qB}\ \alpha_{s}^{1/3} \exp\{-4\pi/N_{c} \alpha_{s} \log(4\pi/\alpha_{s})\}$, where $e_q$ is the electric charge of the light quark, $\alpha_s$ is the strong fine structure constant, and $N_c$ is the number of colors.
* The gluon screening mass is given by $m_g^2 = (\alpha_s/\pi) e_q B$, which is parametrically smaller than $\sqrt{e_qB}$ but larger than the dynamical quark mass $m_{\rm dyn}$ from magnetic catalysis.
* The hierarchy of scales assumed is $\Lambda_{\rm QCD} \ll m_{\rm dyn} \ll m_g \ll \sqrt{e_qB}$, with the chemical potential $\mu$ satisfying $\mu \ll \sqrt{e_qB}$ and $\mu \gg m_{\rm dyn}$.
* The effective 1+1-dimensional coupling $G$ in the regime $m_g < \Lambda$ is $G \simeq \alpha_s \log(4 e_q B / \Lambda^2)$, and in the regime $\Lambda < m_g$ it is $G \simeq \alpha_s \log(4 e_q B / m_g^2)$.

## Context

This work extends the concept of the QCD Kondo effect, previously proposed for high-density quark matter with a Fermi surface by Hattori et al. (2015), to a new regime where a strong magnetic field replaces the Fermi surface as the source of finite density of states and gapless excitations. The mechanism is analogous to magnetic catalysis of chiral symmetry breaking in QCD, as studied by Gusynin, Miransky, and Shovkovy (1994, 1999) and Miransky and Shovkovy (2002), where a strong magnetic field also produces dimensional reduction and a finite density of states. The result enables future study of competition between the magnetically induced QCD Kondo effect and magnetic catalysis, potentially affecting the QCD phase diagram in strong magnetic fields relevant to heavy-ion collisions and neutron star cores.
