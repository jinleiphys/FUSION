# Towards the three-dimensional parton structure of the pion: Integrating transverse momentum data into global QCD analysis

> We perform a new Monte Carlo QCD analysis of pion parton distribution functions, including, for the first time, transverse momentum dependent pion-nucleus Drell-Yan cross sections together with $p_{\rm T}$-integrated Drell-Yan and leading neutron electroproduction data from HERA. We assess the sensitivity of the Monte Carlo fits to kinematic cuts, factorization scale, and parametrization choice, and we discuss the impact of the various datasets on the pion's quark and gluon distributions. This study provides the necessary step towards the simultaneous analysis of collinear and transverse momentum dependent pion distributions and the determination of the pion's three-dimensional structure.

## Key claim

This study demonstrates that, contrary to proton-induced Drell-Yan reactions, the large transverse momentum region of pion-induced Drell-Yan data can be consistently described within a global QCD analysis of pion parton distribution functions using only leading-order (O(alpha_s)) perturbative QCD calculations, provided a lower cut on transverse momentum of pT_min = 2.7 GeV is applied.

## Method

The authors performed a Monte Carlo global QCD analysis of pion parton distribution functions using Bayesian inference, fitting for the first time both pT-differential and pT-integrated Drell-Yan cross sections from pion-tungsten scattering (NA10 at CERN and E615 at Fermilab) together with leading neutron electroproduction data from HERA (H1 and ZEUS). The pT-dependent cross sections were described using the fixed-order (FO) term of the Collins-Soper-Sterman W+Y framework at O(alpha_s), while the pT-integrated Drell-Yan and leading neutron data were treated within standard collinear factorization. The pion PDFs were parametrized at an input scale mu_0 = 1.27 GeV using a three-parameter form for valence, sea, and gluon distributions, with 7 shape parameters and 8 additional parameters for data normalizations and the pion splitting function.

## Key numbers

- Lower transverse momentum cut: pT_min = 2.7 GeV, determined phenomenologically to ensure applicability of the collinear approximation for the fixed-order term
- Drell-Yan data selection: Q range 4.16 < Q < 7.68 GeV, with xF < 0.9, yielding 61 data points from E615 and 56 from NA10 for pT-integrated cross sections
- pT-dependent E615 data: 34 data points for xF-integrated cross section (4.3 < Q < 7.2 GeV) and 49 data points for Q-integrated cross section (4.05 < Q < 8.55 GeV), with pT up to 4.875 GeV
- Leading neutron data cut: xL > 0.8, following systematic chi-squared studies from McKenney and Barry (2018)
- Pion beam energies: 194 and 286 GeV (NA10), 252 GeV (E615)
- Input scale for PDF parametrization: mu_0 = m_c = 1.27 GeV
- Axial charge g_A = 1.267, pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV

## Context

This work extends the previous JAM collaboration analysis of pion PDFs by Barry (2018) by incorporating transverse momentum dependent Drell-Yan data for the first time, building on the theoretical framework developed by Collins, Soper, and Sterman (1984). The study contrasts with proton-induced Drell-Yan analyses, where Bacchetta (2019) found that even O(alpha_s^2) corrections significantly underestimate measured cross sections at low energies. This analysis enables the next step toward a simultaneous determination of collinear and transverse momentum dependent pion distributions, which would allow extraction of the pion's three-dimensional parton structure.
