# Bulk Properties of the Matter Produced at Energies of the Beam Energy Scan Program

> Recent STAR data on the bulk observables in the energy range of the Beam-Energy Scan Program at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider are analyzed within the model of the three-fluid dynamics (3FD). The simulations are performed with different equations of state (EoS). The purely hadronic EoS fails to reproduce the data. A good, though imperfect, overall reproduction of the data is found within the deconfinement scenarios. The crossover EoS turns out to be slightly preferable. For this reproduction a fairly strong baryon stopping in the quark-gluon phase is required. The 3FD model does not need two separate freeze-outs (i.e. kinetic and chemical ones) to describe the STAR data. A unified freeze-out is applied at all energies.

## Key claim

The three-fluid dynamics (3FD) model achieves a good, though imperfect, overall reproduction of STAR bulk observables at Beam-Energy Scan (BES) energies when using deconfinement equations of state, with the crossover EoS slightly preferable; however, the model fails to reproduce the horn anomaly in the K+/π+ ratio, which may require inclusion of the chiral phase transition absent in the present scenarios.

## Method

The authors performed 3FD simulations of Au+Au collisions at BES-RHIC energies (√sNN = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, and 39 GeV) using three equations of state: a purely hadronic EoS, a first-order phase transition EoS, and a smooth crossover EoS. The friction forces in the quark-gluon phase were fitted phenomenologically to reproduce baryon stopping at high incident energies, and a unified freeze-out (without separate kinetic and chemical freeze-outs) was applied at all energies. The simulations were compared with final STAR data on transverse-momentum spectra, mean transverse mass, midrapidity densities, and particle ratios.

## Key numbers

- Collision energies studied: √sNN = 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, and 39 GeV
- At √sNN = 39 GeV, approximately 30% of the baryon charge is located in "true" fragmentation regions while 70% is located in the central fireball
- The equilibration time in a small box (0.5 fm × 0.5 fm × 0.5 fm) within QGSM and UrQMD is very similar to the 3FD time at √sNN = 7.7 GeV (or 30A GeV in the lab frame)
- The thermalization criterion used: difference between longitudinal and transverse pressures drops below 10%
- The K+ spectra are underestimated at √sNN = 7.7 GeV and less spectacularly at 11.5 GeV
- The K- densities are overestimated at √sNN ≥ 8 GeV
- The purely hadronic EoS fails to reproduce data at √sNN ≥ 5 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous 3FD model analyses of bulk observables at AGS and SPS energies (Ivanov 2013, 2014) to the BES-RHIC energy range, providing systematic comparison with final STAR data (Adamczyk 2017). The results contradict conclusions from Bialas (2016) and Shen (2017) regarding thermalization and baryon stopping, showing that equilibration in the central region is achieved at rather high baryon densities even at high collision energies. The failure to reproduce the horn anomaly in the K+/π+ ratio suggests that future work should incorporate chiral phase transition effects as explored in the PHSD model by Palmese (2016) or the PNJL model by Dubinin (2016), and the authors recommend studying hadronic afterburner effects using the THESEUS event generator (Batyuk 2016).
