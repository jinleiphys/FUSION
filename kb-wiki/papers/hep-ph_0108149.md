# Superfluid phases of quark matter, II: Phenomenology and sum rules

> We derive sum rules for a uniform, isotropic superfluid quark-gluon plasma with massless quarks, first laying out the phenomenological equations obeyed by a color superconductor in terms of macroscopic observables such as the superfluid mass and baryon densities, and the electric and magnetic gluon masses, and then expressing these quantities in terms of equilibrium correlation functions. From the transverse part of the long wavelength baryon current-momentum correlation function we derive an exact expression for the superfluid baryon density, and from the longitudinal part, an f-sum rule. From the transverse part of the long wavelength color current-current correlation function we derive the superfluid Meissner mass, and from the longitudinal part, the Debye mass. These masses constrain integrals of the transverse and longitudinal parts of the gluon propagator over frequencies, and provide self-consistent conditions for a solution to the gap equation beyond weak coupling.

# Superfluid phases of quark matter, II: Phenomenology and sum rules

## Key claim

The paper derives exact sum rules for a uniform, isotropic color superconductor with massless quarks that connect macroscopic observables (superfluid baryon density, Meissner mass, Debye mass) to equilibrium correlation functions, providing self-consistency conditions that must be satisfied by any approximate theory of the pairing gap beyond weak coupling. The authors note that weak coupling calculations of the gap are not self-consistent because they use masses corresponding to the normal state rather than the superconducting state.

## Method

The authors first lay out the phenomenological hydrodynamic equations for a relativistic superfluid plasma in terms of macroscopic observables including superfluid mass density, superfluid baryon density, and normal densities. They then express these quantities microscopically in terms of equilibrium correlation functions: the transverse and longitudinal parts of the baryon current-momentum correlation function yield the superfluid baryon density and an f-sum rule, while the transverse and longitudinal parts of the color current-current correlation function yield the Meissner mass and Debye mass respectively. These masses are then related to sum rules for the gluon propagator via its spectral representation.

## Key numbers

- Superfluid baryon density relation: $\rho_s = \mu_b n_s$ (relativistic relation between superfluid mass density and superfluid baryon density)
- For color-flavor locked phase at zero temperature: $(m_E^2)_{\alpha\alpha}=3(m_M^2)_{\alpha\alpha}= \frac{21-8\ln 2}{18}\omega_p^2$ for $\alpha=1,\ldots,8$, where $\omega_p^2 = g^2 \mu_b^2/18\pi^2 + g^2 T^2 /2$ in weak coupling
- For two-flavor channel at zero temperature: $(m_M^2)_{\alpha\alpha}=0$ for $\alpha=1,2,3$; $(m_M^2)_{\alpha\alpha}=\omega_p^2/3$ for $\alpha=4,5,6,7$; $(m_M^2)_{88}=2\omega_p^2/9$
- For two-flavor channel at zero temperature: $(m_E^2)_{\alpha\alpha}=\omega_p^2$ for $\alpha=1,2,3$; $(m_E^2)_{\alpha\alpha}=2\omega_p^2$ for $\alpha=4,5,6,7$; $(m_E^2)_{88}=3\omega_p^2$

- Near $T_c$, coefficient $K_T = 7\zeta(3)n_b/16\pi^2 T_c^2 \mu_b$ in weak coupling, where $\zeta(3)=1.202\ldots$

## Context

This paper extends the authors' prior Ginzburg-Landau analysis (Iida and Baym, 2001) of color superconducting phases to derive exact sum rules that hold beyond the weak coupling regime. The sum rules connect to earlier work by Baym (1969) on sum rules in superfluid systems and by Pisarski and Rischke (2000) on gluon propagators in the normal state. The results provide constraints that must be satisfied by self-consistent solutions to the gap equation, going beyond the one-loop calculations of Rischke (2000) which showed that superconducting medium effects modify the gluon self-energy only in the energy range $|\omega| \lesssim \Delta$. The sum rules enable future checks on approximate theories for the pairing gap in strongly coupled regimes relevant to neutron stars and heavy-ion collisions.
