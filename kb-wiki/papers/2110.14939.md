# Nonlinear Klein-Gordon equation and the Bose-Einstein condensation

> The interest in the Klein-Gordon equation with different potentials has increased in recent years due to its possible applications in Cosmology, Hadron Physics and High-Energy Physics. In this work we investigate the solutions of the Klein-Gordon equation for bosons under the influence of an external potential by using the Feshbach-Villars method. We present detailed results for two cases: the Coulombic potential and the harmonic potential. For the latter case, we studied the effects of self-interacting particles by adopting a mean-field approach. We show that our results converge smoothly to the solution of the Schrödinger equation for the same systems as the relativistic effects diminish.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that solutions of the nonlinear Klein-Gordon equation for bosons under external potentials, obtained via the Feshbach-Villars formalism, converge smoothly to the corresponding Schrödinger equation solutions as relativistic effects diminish, but only under the condition that the chemical potential is identified with the ground-state eigenvalue and the system parameters satisfy specific bounds (e.g., \(g < \hbar c/2\) for the Coulombic potential).

## Method

The authors solve the time-independent Klein-Gordon equation for bosons using the Feshbach-Villars spinor formalism, which recasts the equation into a Schrödinger-like form. They treat two external potentials analytically: a Coulombic potential \(V(r) = -g/r\) (for zero angular momentum) and a harmonic potential \(V(r) = \frac{1}{2} m \omega^2 r^2\). For the harmonic case, they include a mean-field self-interaction term proportional to the scattering length \(a\) and the density \(|\psi|^2\), leading to a relativistic Gross-Pitaevskii-type equation. The chemical potential \(\mu\) is introduced as the ground-state eigenvalue, and the non-relativistic limit is taken by expanding in powers of \(1/c^2\) or the dimensionless parameter \(\alpha = \hbar\omega/(mc^2)\).

## Key numbers

- For the Coulombic potential, the dimensionless eigenvalue is \(M_n^{\text{KG}} = \mu_n^{\text{KG}}/(m c^2) = (n-1+\beta_+)/\sqrt{\lambda^2 + (n-1+\beta_+)^2}\), where \(\lambda = g/(\hbar c)\) and \(\beta_+ = \frac{1}{2}(1 + \sqrt{1-4\lambda^2})\); the non-relativistic limit gives \(\bar\mu_n^{\text{NR}} = - m g^2/(2\hbar^2 n^2)\).
- The relativistic correction to the chemical potential for the Coulombic case at order \(\mathcal{O}(c^{-4})\) is \(\delta M_n = -\lambda^4 (1/n^3 - 3/(8 n^4))\).
- For the harmonic potential with self-interaction, the dimensionless chemical potential \(\bar\beta = \bar\mu/(\hbar\omega)\) is computed as a function of the dimensionless charge \(q = Q |a|/\ell_s\) (with \(\ell_s = \frac{1}{2}\sqrt{\hbar/(2m\omega)}\)) for values of the relativistic parameter \(\alpha = 0.1, 0.2, 0.3\) and compared to the non-relativistic case \(\alpha = 0\).
- The ratio \(N/Q\) (particle number over conserved charge) is shown for \(\Phi'(0) = 2.5\) as a function of \(\alpha\), with \(N < Q\) in the relativistic regime and \(N/Q \to 1\) as \(\alpha \to 0\).

## Context

This work extends the Feshbach-Villars treatment of the Klein-Gordon equation to include external potentials and self-interactions relevant to Bose-Einstein condensation, building on prior studies of relativistic condensates in axion stars (Eby et al., 2015) and hadronic matter (Rezaeian, 2017; Jankowski, 2014; Fujii, 2016). The non-relativistic limit is benchmarked against the Gross-Pitaevskii equation results of Gammal (1999) and Adhikari (2000) for harmonic traps with scattering-length interactions. The formalism enables future investigation of condensation in relativistic hadronic matter and extensions to fractional-derivative Gross-Pitaevskii equations.
