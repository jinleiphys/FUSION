# Medium Effects on Binary Collisions with the Delta Resonance

> To facilitate the relativistic heavy-ion calculations based on transport equations, the binary collisions involving a $Δ$ resonance in either the entrance channel or the exit channel are investigated within a Hamiltonian formulation of $πNN$ interactions. An averaging procedure is developed to define a quasi-particle $Δ^*$ and to express the experimentally measured $NN\rightarrow πNN$ cross section in terms of an effective $NN\rightarrow NΔ^\ast$ cross section. In contrast to previous works, the main feature of the present approach is that the mass and the momentum of the produced $Δ^*$'s are calculated dynamically from the bare $Δ\leftrightarrow πN$ vertex interaction of the model Hamiltonian and are constrained by the unitarity condition. The procedure is then extended to define the effective cross sections for the experimentally inaccessible $NΔ^\ast \rightarrow NN$ and $NΔ^\ast \rightarrow NΔ^\ast$ reactions. The predicted cross sections are significantly different from what are commonly assumed in relativistic heavy-ion calculations. The $Δ$ potential in nuclear matter has been calculated by using a Bruckner-Hartree-Fock approximation. By including the mean-field effects on the $Δ$ propagation, the effective cross sections of the $NN\rightarrow NΔ^\ast$, $NΔ^\ast \rightarrow NN$ and $NΔ^\ast \rightarrow NΔ^\ast$ reactions in nuclear matter are predicted. It is demonstrated that the density dependence is most dramatic in the energy region close to the pion production threshold.

# Medium Effects on Binary Collisions with the Delta Resonance

## Key claim

The paper develops a Hamiltonian-based averaging procedure to define effective cross sections for binary collisions involving a Delta resonance, where the mass and momentum of the produced quasi-particle Delta* are calculated dynamically from the bare Delta-pi-N vertex interaction and constrained by unitarity, in contrast to previous works that used simpler parameterizations. The predicted cross sections are significantly different from what is commonly assumed in relativistic heavy-ion calculations, with density dependence most dramatic near the pion production threshold.

## Method

The author employs a Hamiltonian model of pi-N-N interactions previously developed to describe N-N scattering up to 2 GeV and Delta-nucleus dynamics in pion-nucleus reactions. An averaging procedure is developed to define a quasi-particle Delta* and express the experimentally measured N-N -> pi-N-N cross section in terms of an effective N-N -> N-Delta* cross section, using a factorization approximation where the transition t-matrix is evaluated at an average momentum. The Delta potential in nuclear matter is calculated using a Bruckner-Hartree-Fock approximation, and the medium-corrected cross sections for N-N -> N-Delta*, N-Delta* -> N-N, and N-Delta* -> N-Delta* reactions are obtained by replacing the scattering t-matrix with the G-matrix.

## Key numbers

- The cutoff parameter for the monopole form factor in the one-pion-exchange model of transition potentials is Lambda = 650 MeV/c
- The bare mass of the Delta is m0_Delta = 1280 MeV, determined from fitting pi-N phase shifts in the P33 channel
- The subtraction energy for defining the N-N interaction from the Paris potential is E_s = 10 MeV laboratory energy
- The ranges of the pi-N-N and pi-N-Delta vertices in the N-N <-> N-Delta transition potential must be less than about 750 MeV for a monopole form to reasonably describe N-N -> pi-N-N reaction cross sections
- The invariant mass M of the Delta* is restricted to the region (m_N + m_pi) less than or equal to M less than or equal to (E - m_N)

## Context

This work extends the Hamiltonian formulation of pi-N-N interactions developed by Lee and collaborators (Lee 1991, 1992, 1993) and the Delta-nucleus dynamics approach of Lee (1994) to provide theoretical input for relativistic heavy-ion calculations using transport equations. The approach contrasts with previous works by Bertsch and collaborators (Bertsch 1988, 1992) and Mosel and collaborators (Mosel 1992) which used simpler parameterizations of the N-Delta phase space. The results enable more rigorous treatment of Delta-rich matter in transport equation calculations and identify the need for future extension to include two-pion channels and higher mass N* resonances, requiring improved experimental data from the proposed pion facility at GSI and the N* program at CEBAF.
