# Strong $Λπ$ Phase Shifts for CP Violation in Weak $Ξ\rightarrow Λπ$ Decay

> Strong interaction $Λπ$ phase shifts relevant for the weak nonleptonic decay $Ξ\rightarrow Λπ$ are calculated using baryon chiral perturbation theory. We find in leading order that the S-wave phase shift vanishes and the $J={1 \over 2}$ P-wave phase shift is $-1.7 ^{\rm o} $. The small phase shifts imply that CP violation in this decay will be difficult to observe. Our results follow from chiral $SU(2)_L\times SU(2)_R$ symmetry.

## Key claim

Using baryon chiral perturbation theory at leading order, the authors find that the S-wave Λπ phase shift vanishes and the J=1/2 P-wave phase shift is only -1.7°, implying that CP violation in Ξ→Λπ decay will be difficult to observe because the CP-violating asymmetry A is proportional to tan(δ₀−δ₁), which is small.

## Method

The authors compute the strong Λπ phase shifts relevant for the weak nonleptonic decay Ξ→Λπ using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory with chiral SU(2)L×SU(2)R symmetry. The ΣΛπ coupling gΣΛ is determined from the measured Σ⁻→Λeν̄e decay rate, and the Σ*Λπ coupling gΣ*Λ is determined from the Σ*⁺→Λπ⁺ decay width. The leading-order Feynman diagrams involving Σ and Σ* intermediate states are evaluated to obtain the S-wave and J=1/2 P-wave phase shifts as functions of pion energy.

## Key numbers

- The J=1/2 P-wave Λπ phase shift at the Ξ decay energy Eπ ≈ mΞ−mΛ = 206 MeV is δ₁ = −1.7°.
- The S-wave phase shift vanishes at leading order: δ₀(Eπ) = 0.
- The Σ*Λπ coupling squared is gΣ*Λ² ≈ 1.49, extracted from the Σ*⁺→Λπ⁺ decay width.
- The ΣΛπ coupling squared is gΣΛ² ≈ 1.44, extracted from the Σ⁻→Λeν̄e decay rate.
- The pion decay constant used is f ≈ 132 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of strong phase shifts for Ξ→Λπ decay by Martin (1965), Abers and Zumach (1963), and Nath and Kumar (1965), but differs from those approaches by not relying on SU(3) symmetry. The small phase shifts indicate that the proposed Fermilab experiment (Gidal et al., 1993) to observe direct CP violation in the Ξ→Λπ→pππ decay chain will likely be dominated by CP violation in the Λ→pπ part rather than the Ξ→Λπ part. The results enable quantitative estimates of the feasibility of observing direct CP violation in hyperon decays.
