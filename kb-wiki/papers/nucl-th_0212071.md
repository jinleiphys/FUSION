# Density Functional Theory for a Confined Fermi System with Short-Range Interaction

> Effective field theory (EFT) methods are applied to density functional theory (DFT) as part of a program to systematically go beyond mean-field approaches to medium and heavy nuclei. A system of fermions with short-range, natural interactions and an external confining potential (e.g., fermionic atoms in an optical trap) serves as a laboratory for studying DFT/EFT. An effective action formalism leads to a Kohn-Sham DFT by applying an inversion method order-by-order in the EFT expansion parameter. Representative results showing the convergence of Kohn-Sham calculations at zero temperature in the local density approximation (LDA) are compared to Thomas-Fermi calculations and to power-counting estimates.

## Key claim

Effective field theory (EFT) methods can be systematically merged with Kohn-Sham density functional theory (DFT) to go beyond mean-field approaches for finite Fermi systems, but the present work is limited to a dilute, confined Fermi gas with short-range natural interactions as a laboratory test case, and the authors assume a gradient expansion parameter that justifies a local density approximation without verifying this assumption.

## Method

The authors apply an effective action formalism to construct a Kohn-Sham energy functional for a dilute Fermi gas with short-range interactions confined by an external harmonic potential. They use an inversion method to expand the effective action order-by-order in the EFT expansion parameter (the local Fermi momentum times the scattering length), and evaluate the functional at zero temperature using the local density approximation (LDA). The Kohn-Sham calculations through next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) are compared to Thomas-Fermi calculations and to power-counting estimates.

## Key numbers

- Non-interacting energy density for uniform system: \( \edens_0 = \rho \frac{3}{5} \frac{k_F^2}{2M} \), with density \( \rho = g k_F^3 / (6\pi^2) \) for spin degeneracy \( g \)
- Leading order (LO, order \( k_F^6 \)) Hartree-Fock contribution: \( \edens_1 = \rho (g-1) \frac{k_F^2}{2M} \frac{2}{3\pi} k_F a_s \)
- Next-to-leading order (NLO, order \( k_F^7 \)) "beach ball" diagram contribution: \( \edens_2 = \rho (g-1) \frac{k_F^2}{2M} (k_F a_s)^2 \frac{4}{35\pi^2} (11 - 2\ln 2) \)
- Next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO, order \( k_F^8 \)) correction includes terms proportional to \( (k_F a_s)^2 k_F r_s \), \( (k_F a_p)^3 \), and \( (k_F a_s)^3 \) with numerical coefficients \( 0.07550 \pm 0.00003 \) and \( 0.05741 \pm 0.00002 \)

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to dilute Fermi systems developed by Hammer and collaborators (2000) by merging it with the Kohn-Sham DFT framework, building on the effective action formalism for DFT pioneered by Fukuda and collaborators (1994, 1995) and further developed by Valiev and Fernando (1997). The paper serves as a prototype for ultimately applying EFT-based DFT to heavier nuclei, where systematic improvements beyond Skyrme mean-field models are needed, and also has direct relevance to experiments on fermionic atoms in optical traps as described by Granade and collaborators (2002).
