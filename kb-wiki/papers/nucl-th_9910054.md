# Isoscalar compression modes in relativistic random phase approximation

> A fully consistent relativistic RPA calculation is performed for the monopole and dipole compression modes in nuclei. The emphasis is put on the effects of Dirac sea states which are generally neglected in relativistic RPA calculations. It is found that these effects can be quite important for the isoscalar monopole mode. The main contributions from the pairs of Fermi to Dirac sea states are through the exchange of the scalar meson, while the vector mesons play a negligible role. Numerical results of relativistic RPA are checked with the constrained relativistic mean field model in the monopole case. A good agreement beteween monopole energies calculated in RRPA and in time-dependent relativistic mean field approach is achieved. For the monopole compression mode, a comparison of experimental and calculated energies gives a value of 250 $\sim$ 270 MeV for the nuclear matter incompressibility. A large discrepancy remains between theory and experiment in the case of the dipole compression mode.

## Key claim

The inclusion of Dirac sea states in relativistic RPA calculations is essential for a consistent description of isoscalar compression modes; without them, the residual interaction in the isoscalar channel becomes too attractive and the calculated giant resonance energies are too low, but the effect is negligible for isovector modes.

## Method

Fully consistent relativistic RPA (RRPA) calculations were performed for isoscalar giant monopole and dipole resonances in finite nuclei, using effective Lagrangians with nonlinear meson self-interactions (NL1, NL3, TM1, NLSH, HS). The single-particle basis and particle-hole couplings were derived from the same Lagrangian, and the RRPA response included both positive-energy particle-hole pairs and pairs formed from occupied Fermi-sea and empty Dirac-sea states. The static polarizability from RRPA was checked against constrained relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations using a spherical constraint.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter incompressibility extracted from comparison of calculated and experimental ISGMR energies in 208Pb and 144Sm: \(K_\infty = 250 \sim 270\) MeV.
- Experimental ISGMR energies used for comparison: 208Pb at \(14.2 \pm 0.3\) MeV; 144Sm at \(15.4 \pm 0.3\) MeV.
- Compression moduli of the five effective Lagrangians used: NL1 (211 MeV), NL3 (272 MeV), TM1 (281 MeV), NLSH (355 MeV), HS (545 MeV).
- For the ISGDR in 208Pb, the calculated peak energy with NL3 is about 26 MeV, while the experimental centroid energy is 20.3 MeV.
- The RRPA static polarizability (inverse energy-weighted sum rule) for 208Pb agrees with constrained RMF to within 1% when Dirac states are included; without Dirac states the discrepancy is dramatic (polarizability can become negative for some Lagrangians).
- The cut-off for Dirac sea states was varied between -300 MeV and -900 MeV, and results were stable.

## Context

This work extends earlier relativistic RPA calculations that omitted Dirac sea states (e.g., Daw (1990), L'Huillier and Van Giai (1989)) and resolves a long-standing discrepancy between RRPA and time-dependent RMF results for the isoscalar monopole mode. The finding that Dirac states contribute mainly through scalar-meson exchange explains why the effect is large for isoscalar modes but negligible for isovector modes. The extracted incompressibility range (250–270 MeV) is somewhat higher than values from non-relativistic RPA with Skyrme and Gogny forces (Blaizot (1995)). The large remaining discrepancy for the isoscalar giant dipole resonance (about 5 MeV in 208Pb) is identified as an open problem that likely cannot be resolved by coupling to 2p-2h configurations (Colo et al. (1999)).
