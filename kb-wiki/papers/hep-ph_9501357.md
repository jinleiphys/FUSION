# Chiral perturbation theory

> The main elements and methods of chiral perturbation theory, the effective field theory of the Standard Model below the scale of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, are summarized. Applications to the interactions of mesons and baryons at low energies are reviewed, with special emphasis on developments of the last three years. Among the topics covered are the strong, electromagnetic and semileptonic weak interactions of mesons at and beyond next--to--leading order in the chiral expansion, nonleptonic weak interactions of mesons, virtual photon corrections and the meson--baryon system. The discussion is limited to processes at zero temperature, for infinite volume and with at most one baryon.

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) is the effective field theory of the Standard Model below the scale of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, but its validity is limited to processes at zero temperature, for infinite volume, and with at most one baryon; the paper reviews applications to meson and baryon interactions at low energies, with emphasis on developments from 1992 through 1994.

## Method

The paper reviews the construction of effective chiral Lagrangians organized in a derivative expansion based on chiral counting rules, where Goldstone boson fields are collected in a unitary matrix field \(U(\varphi)\) transforming under chiral rotations. The method introduces external hermitian matrix fields (vector, axial-vector, scalar, pseudoscalar) to generate Green functions of quark currents, and uses spurion fields to incorporate nonleptonic weak interactions and virtual photon corrections. The review covers calculations at leading order \(O(p^2)\), next-to-leading order \(O(p^4)\), and beyond, including two-loop diagrams at \(O(p^6)\), for strong, electromagnetic, semileptonic, and nonleptonic weak interactions of mesons and baryons.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant in the chiral limit: \(F_\pi = 92.4\ \text{MeV}\) (to leading order in quark masses)
- Leading-order meson mass relations: \(M^2_{\pi^+} = 2\hat{m}B\), \(M^2_{K^+} = (m_u + m_s)B\), \(M^2_{K^0} = (m_d + m_s)B\), \(M^2_{\eta_8} = \frac{2}{3}(\hat{m} + 2m_s)B\) (with \(\hat{m} = \frac{1}{2}(m_u + m_d)\))
- Gell-Mann–Okubo relation: \(3M^2_{\eta_8} = 4M_K^2 - M_\pi^2\)
- Goldberger–Treiman relation in the chiral limit: \(g_{\pi NN} = g_A m / F\), with axial-vector coupling \(g_A = f + d\) for \(SU(3)\)

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on chiral perturbation theory by Gasser and Leutwyler (1984, 1985) and summarizes developments in the three years up to 1994, including applications to pion-pion scattering, semileptonic kaon decays, and the meson-baryon system. It contrasts the standard CHPT approach with the linear sigma model (Gell-Mann and Levy, 1960), which is shown to be too restrictive for a realistic low-energy approximation of QCD. The review enables further work on higher-order chiral corrections, virtual photon effects, and the application of CHPT to nuclear forces as pioneered by Weinberg (1990, 1991).
