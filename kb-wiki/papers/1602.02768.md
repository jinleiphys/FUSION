# $\mathbf{γ_{v} NN^{\ast}}$ Electrocouplings in Dyson-Schwinger Equations

> A symmetry preserving framework for the study of continuum Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) is obtained from a truncated solution of the QCD equations of motion or QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs). A nonperturbative solution of the DSEs enables the study of, e.g., hadrons as composites of dressed-quarks and dressed-gluons, the phenomena of confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB), and therefrom an articulation of any connection between them. It is within this context that we present a unified study of Nucleon, Delta and Roper elastic and transition electromagnetic form factors, and compare predictions made using a framework built upon a Faddeev equation kernel and interaction vertices that possess QCD-like momentum dependence with results obtained using a symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector$\,\otimes\,$vector contact-interaction. The comparison emphasises that experiment is sensitive to the momentum dependence of the running coupling and masses in QCD and highlights that the key to describing hadron properties is a veracious expression of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in the bound-state problem.

## Key claim

A unified Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) framework, using a Faddeev equation kernel with QCD-like momentum dependence, can simultaneously describe elastic and transition form factors of the Nucleon, Delta, and Roper resonances, but only when the dressed-quark core is augmented by meson-cloud contributions that dominate at low momentum transfer (below approximately \(Q^2 \sim 3 m_N^2\)).

## Method

The authors solved a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation for baryons using a leading-order (rainbow-ladder) truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations, incorporating dressed-quark propagators and diquark correlations (scalar-isoscalar and pseudovector-isotriplet). They computed elastic and transition electromagnetic form factors for the Nucleon, Delta, and Roper states, and compared these results with those obtained from a symmetry-preserving vector\(\otimes\)vector contact-interaction (CI) model.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass from DSEs: \(M_{\text{nucleon}} = 1.18\ \text{GeV}\); Roper mass: \(M_{\text{Roper}} = 1.73\ \text{GeV}\) (dressed-quark core values; the empirical Roper pole is \(1.36 - i\,0.091\ \text{GeV}\), while the bare Roper core from coupled-channel models is \(1.76\ \text{GeV}\)).
- The scalar diquark component produces \(62\%\) of the proton's normalisation.
- The QCD-kindred prediction for the magnetic transition form factor \(G_M^\ast\) in the \(\gamma^\ast N \to \Delta\) transition agrees with data for \(x = Q^2/M_N^2 \gtrsim 0.4\).
- The zero in the Pauli transition form factor \(F_2^\ast\) for the \(\gamma^\ast N \to \text{Roper}\) transition is not influenced by meson-cloud effects, though its precise location is.
- Meson-cloud contributions provide up to \(35\%\) of the \(\gamma^\ast N \to \Delta\) magnetic form factor for \(x \lesssim 2\) and fall to \(20\%\) of their maximum by \(x=2\) for the Roper transition.

## Context

This work extends prior DSE-based studies of baryon structure (Segovia 2013, 2014, 2015) by providing a unified treatment of Nucleon, Delta, and Roper elastic and transition form factors. It contrasts predictions from a QCD-kindred framework with those from a contact-interaction model, showing that experiment is sensitive to the momentum dependence of QCD's running coupling and masses. The results enable future extraction of dressed-quark core properties from high-\(Q^2\) electroproduction data from CLAS at Jefferson Lab, and highlight the need to incorporate meson-cloud effects (as in the Sato-Lee dynamical model) for low-momentum comparisons.
