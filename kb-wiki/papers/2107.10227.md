# A method to remove lower order contributions in multi-particle femtoscopic correlation functions

> In recent years the femtoscopy technique has been used by the ALICE Collaboration in small colliding systems at the LHC to investigate the strong-interaction of hadron pairs in the low-energy regime. The extension of this technique to the study of many-body correlations aims to deliver in the next years the first experimental measurements of the genuine many-hadron interactions, provided that the contributions due to the lower order terms are properly accounted for. In this paper we present a method that allows to determine the residual lower order contributions to the three-body correlation functions, based on the cumulant decomposition approach and on kinematic transformations. A procedure to simulate genuine three-body correlations in three-baryon correlation functions is also developed. A qualitative study of the produced correlation signal is performed by varying the strength of the adopted three-body interaction model and comparisons with the expectations for the lower order contributions to the correlation function are shown. The method can be also applied to evaluate the combinatorial background in the two-body correlation functions, providing an improved statistical accuracy with respect to the standard techniques. The example of the contribution by the pK$^+$K$^-$ channel to the recently measured p$ϕ$ correlation is discussed.

# A method to remove lower order contributions in multi-particle femtoscopic correlation functions

## Key claim
The paper presents a method (the "projector method") to calculate and subtract lower-order two-body contributions from three-body femtoscopic correlation functions using analytical kinematic transformations, but the method is validated only through toy Monte Carlo simulations assuming a specific Gaussian source and a particular pΛ interaction model, not yet with experimental data.

## Method
The authors derive an analytical projector function W(k₁,Q₃) that transforms a two-body correlation function C₂(k*) from the relative momentum k* of an interacting pair into the three-body hyper-momentum Q₃ space, using the cumulant decomposition approach and Jacobi coordinate transformations. They validate the method by generating toy Monte Carlo events where only one pair (1,2) is correlated using a multivariate Gaussian distribution, extracting the correlation parameters from a Lednický-model fit with pΛ scattering parameters (f₀ = 2.91 fm, d₀ = 2.78 fm) and source radius r₀ = 1.25 fm, then comparing the projected three-body correlation to the directly simulated three-body correlation. The method is also applied to evaluate combinatorial background from pK⁺K⁻ channels in the pφ correlation function.

## Key numbers
- Scattering parameters used for the test: f₀ = 2.91 fm, d₀ = 2.78 fm (spin-0 pΛ interaction from χEFT at NLO)
- Source radius assumed: r₀ = 1.25 fm (typical for small colliding systems at the LHC)
- Fit parameters extracted from the same-event distribution: q = (99.734 ± 0.002)%, p = (0.266 ± 0.002)%, σ₂ = (27.7 ± 0.1) MeV/c
- The projector function W(k₁,Q₃) is given analytically as: W(k₁,Q₃) = [16(αγ - β²)^(3/2) k₁²] / [π Q₃⁴ γ²] × √[γ Q₃² - (αγ - β²) k₁²]

## Context
This work extends the femtoscopy technique from two-body correlations, previously used by ALICE to study hadron pairs such as p-p (Acharya 2019), pΛ (Acharya 2019), ΛΛ (Acharya 2019), and pφ (ALICE 2021), toward genuine three-body correlation measurements. The method builds on the Kubo cumulant expansion approach previously applied to pion triplets (PhysRevC.89.024911) but provides an analytical alternative to the data-driven mixed-event subtraction technique. The projector method enables future experimental measurements of genuine three-hadron interactions at the LHC and can also improve background modeling for two-body correlations involving unstable hadrons, such as the pφ channel.
