# Quantum Kinetic Theory for Quantum Electrodynamics

> We derive a quantum kinetic theory for QED based on Kadanoff-Baym equations for Wigner functions. By assuming parity invariance and considering a complete set of self-energy diagrams, we find the resulting kinetic theory expanded to lowest order in $\hbar$ generalizes the well-known classical kinetic theory to massive case. It contains elastic and inelastic collision terms and integrates screening effect naturally. For a given solution to the classical kinetic theory, we find at next order in $\hbar$ a non-dynamical quantum correction to Wigner functions for both fermions and photons, which gives rise to spin polarization for fermion and photon respectively. The approach allows us to study the non-dynamical part of collisional effect on spin polarization phenomenon.

## Key claim

The paper derives a quantum kinetic theory for QED from Kadanoff-Baym equations, finding that under the assumption of parity invariance the lowest-order expansion in \(\hbar\) generalizes the classical Boltzmann equations of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (AMY) to the massive case, while the next order in \(\hbar\) yields non-dynamical quantum corrections that produce spin polarization for both fermions and photons.

## Method

The author starts from the Kadanoff-Baym equations for fermion and photon Wigner functions in QED, performs a Wigner transform, and applies the quasi-particle approximation. By assuming parity invariance, the degrees of freedom reduce to unpolarized fermion and photon distribution functions. The self-energies are evaluated using a complete set of two-loop diagrams, which incorporate both elastic \(2\leftrightarrow2\) and inelastic \(1\leftrightarrow2\) collision terms with screening effects included naturally. The kinetic equations are then expanded systematically in \(\hbar\) to obtain the classical (zeroth-order) and quantum (first-order) contributions.

## Key numbers

* Characteristic momentum scale of quasi-particles: \(\Lambda\); thermal/screening mass: \(e\Lambda\); damping rate: \(e^2\Lambda\).
* Coarse-graining scale for the kinetic theory: \(1/(e^4\Lambda)\).
* Fluctuation of electromagnetic gauge fields over the coarse-graining scale: \(|A_i| \sim e^5 T\) (with \(T\) the temperature).
* Condition for validity of \(\hbar\) expansion: \(\hbar \partial_X f \ll \Lambda\).
* Condition for weak anisotropy to avoid filamentation instability: \(\xi^{3/2} \ll e^2\), where \(\xi\) is the magnitude of anisotropy.

## Context

This work extends the classical Boltzmann equations for QCD developed by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (AMY) to the massive QED case, bridging the gap between realistic classical kinetic theory and formal quantum kinetic theory. It enables the study of collisional contributions to spin polarization phenomena in a systematic \(\hbar\) expansion, complementing earlier spin kinetic theory frameworks such as those by Hattori (2019), Weickgenannt (2019), and Gao (2019), and providing a foundation for future work on spin hydrodynamics and non-equilibrium spin transport.
