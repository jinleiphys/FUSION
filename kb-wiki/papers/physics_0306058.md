# Variational RPA for the Mie resonance in jellium

> The surface plasmon in simple metal clusters is red-shifted from the Mie frequency, the energy shift being significantly larger than the usual spill-out correction. Here we develop a variational approach to the RPA collective excitations. Using a simple trial form, we obtain analytic expressions for the energy shift beyond the spill-out contribution. We find that the additional red shift is proportional to the spill-out correction and can have the same order of magnitude.

# Variational RPA for the Mie resonance in jellium

## Key claim

The additional red shift of the surface plasmon frequency beyond the standard spill-out correction is proportional to the spill-out correction and can have the same order of magnitude, but the variational approach yields a coefficient that is too small by about a factor of two compared to full numerical RPA calculations.

## Method

The authors develop a variational perturbation theory for the RPA operator, which is non-Hermitian, using a trial wave function constructed from the x-component of the perturbation vector applied to the unperturbed collective state. They apply this formalism to the surface plasmon in jellium clusters, taking the harmonic oscillator potential as the zeroth-order Hamiltonian and treating the deviation of the jellium background potential from harmonic shape as the perturbation, then evaluate the integrals analytically using an exponential approximation for the surface density.

## Key numbers

- For Na$_{20}$ with $r_s=3.93$ a.u., the Mie frequency is 3.5 eV, while the measured resonance peak is at 2.65 eV with width about 0.3 eV FWHM, giving a red shift of 24%
- The spill-out formula (Eq. 3) predicts a 9% red shift for Na$_{21}^+$ using jellium wave functions
- For Na$_{20}$, the numerical RPA red shift is 0.166 $\omega_{\rm Mie}$, while the spill-out formula gives only 0.058 $\omega_{\rm Mie}$
- The ionization potential for Na$_{20}$ is 2.84 eV in the usual jellium model and 4.11 eV in the simplified s-wave treatment
- The variational formula (Eq. 9) gives a red shift with the same functional N-dependence as the numerical calculation but underestimates the magnitude by a factor of two

## Context

This work extends the approaches of Gerchikov et al. (2002) and Kurasawa et al. (1997), who studied the coupling between center-of-mass motion and intrinsic excitations in metal clusters, by providing an analytic variational estimate of the additional red shift beyond the spill-out correction. The method is general and could be applied to the giant dipole resonance in atomic nuclei, where the mass dependence of the isovector dipole frequency deviates from the Goldhaber-Teller model prediction as studied by Bertsch and Stricker (1976) and Myers et al. (1977).
