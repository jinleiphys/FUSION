# Instanton content of finite temperature QCD matter

> We investigate the temperature dependence of the instanton content of gluon fields and their contribution to quark correlation using quenched lattice QCD and the cooling method. We found a suppression of the topological susceptibility at finite temperature, agreeing with the PCAC expectation at low temperature and enhanced suppression for temperatures at and above the deconfinement transition. For temperatures up to about 334 MeV, the topological charge correlation agrees well with a single instanton profile, though the size parameter seems to change across the phase transition. The screening wave functions for the pion and the rho become slightly more compact at higher temperatures. Lattice cooling shows no contribution from the instantons to the screening wave functions at temperatures above the phase transition.

# Instanton content of finite temperature QCD matter

## Key claim
The topological susceptibility is suppressed at finite temperature, with a mild decrease below the critical temperature consistent with PCAC expectations, followed by enhanced suppression at and above the deconfinement transition; however, the agreement with PCAC is probably accidental because the quenched configurations should not reproduce the physics of a thermal pion gas.

## Method
The authors performed quenched lattice QCD simulations on a 16³ spatial volume with β=6.0 (lattice spacing a≈0.1 fm), varying the temperature by changing the number of time slices Nt from 4 to 16, corresponding to a temperature range of 125 to 500 MeV. They applied the cooling method to remove short-range fluctuations and extracted the topological susceptibility, topological charge correlation functions, plaquette expectation values, and screening wave functions for the pion and rho mesons using Wilson fermions with κ=0.1541.

## Key numbers
- Topological susceptibility at zero temperature: approximately (180 MeV)⁴ from the Witten-Veneziano formula
- Critical temperature: Tc ≈ 250 MeV for β=6.0
- Instanton size parameter below Tc: ρ = 3.3a ≈ 0.33 fm, fitting data up to Tc (Nt=8)
- Instanton size parameter at T ≈ 334 MeV (Nt=6): ρ = 2.6a ≈ 0.26 fm
- Fraction of latent heat contributed by instantons: upper bound of 0.15 ± 0.04 at T=334 MeV and 0.056 ± 0.004 at T=500 MeV
- Quark mass used for screening wave functions: m ≈ 100 MeV (κ=0.1541)

## Context
This work extends previous lattice studies of topological susceptibility in pure SU(2) by Teper et al. (1990) and Di Giacomo et al. (1990) and pure SU(3) by Teper et al. (1990), as well as unquenched SU(3) calculations by Gottlieb et al. (1990). The authors avoid the ambiguity in the cooling method reported for SU(2) by Di Giacomo et al. and vary temperature by changing Nt at fixed β to avoid scale uncertainties. The results enable comparison with the Pisarski-Yaffe perturbative formula for Debye-screening suppression of instantons and provide constraints on the temperature of UA(1) restoration relative to the deconfinement transition, which has implications for the order of the phase transition as discussed by Pisarski and Wilczek (1984) and Shuryak (1993).
