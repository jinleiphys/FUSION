# Understanding the $D^+_{sJ}(2317)$ and $D^+_{sJ}(2460)$ with Sum Rules in HQET

> In the framework of heavy quark effective theory we use QCD sum rules to calculate the masses of the $\bar c s$ $(0^+, 1^+)$ and $(1^+, 2^+)$ excited states. The results are consistent with that the states $D_{sJ}(2317)$ and $D_{sJ}(2460)$ observed by BABAR and CLEO are the $0^+$ and $1^+$ states in the $j_l={1\over 2}^+$ doublet.

# Understanding the $D^+_{sJ}(2317)$ and $D^+_{sJ}(2460)$ with Sum Rules in HQET

## Key claim
The $D_{sJ}(2317)$ and $D_{sJ}(2460)$ states observed by BABAR and CLEO can be consistently identified as the $0^+$ and $1^+$ members of the $j_l = 1/2^+$ doublet in the heavy quark effective theory, but only within the theoretical uncertainty of the sum rule approach; the central value for the $0^+$ mass is about 100 MeV larger than the experimental value of 2.317 GeV.

## Method
The authors used QCD sum rules in the framework of heavy quark effective theory (HQET) to compute the masses of excited $\bar c s$ meson states. They employed interpolating currents with derivatives for the $(0^+, 1^+)$ and $(1^+, 2^+)$ doublets, calculated the leading-order parameter $\bar\Lambda = \lim_{m_Q\to\infty} (m_M - m_Q)$, and included $1/m_Q$ corrections from the kinetic and chromo-magnetic operators. The strange quark mass was included in the perturbative spectral densities, and the analysis required that power corrections be less than 30% of the perturbative term and the pole contribution exceed 60% of the perturbative term to define the Borel parameter working window.

## Key numbers
- $\bar\Lambda(1/2^+) = (0.86 \pm 0.10)$ GeV for the $(0^+, 1^+)$ doublet using derivative currents, with central value at $T = 0.52$ GeV and $\omega_c = 2.9$ GeV
- $\bar\Lambda(3/2^+) = (0.83 \pm 0.10)$ GeV for the $(1^+, 2^+)$ doublet, with central value at $T = 0.62$ GeV and $\omega_c = 3.0$ GeV
- $K_{1/2} = (-1.60 \pm 0.30)$ GeV$^2$ and $\Sigma_{1/2} = (0.28 \pm 0.05)$ GeV$^2$ for the $(0^+, 1^+)$ doublet
- $K_{3/2} = (-1.64 \pm 0.40)$ GeV$^2$ and $\Sigma_{3/2} = (0.058 \pm 0.01)$ GeV$^2$ for the $(1^+, 2^+)$ doublet
- Predicted $0^+$ mass: $m_{0^+} = (2.42 \pm 0.13)$ GeV, compared to experimental $D_{sJ}(2317)$ mass of $2317 \pm 3$ MeV
- Predicted mass splitting in $(0^+, 1^+)$ doublet: $m_{D^*_{s1}} - m_{D_{s0}^*} = (0.19 \pm 0.04)$ GeV, compared to observed splitting of $0.143$ GeV
- Predicted mass splitting in $(1^+, 2^+)$ doublet: $m_{D_{s2}^*} - m_{D_{s1}} = (0.080 \pm 0.042)$ GeV, compared to experimental value of $37$ MeV

## Context
This work extends the HQET sum rule formalism previously applied to non-strange heavy mesons by Huang et al. (2000) and Dai et al. (2000) to include the strange quark mass for the $\bar c s$ system. It addresses the puzzle that the $D_{sJ}(2317)$ mass is significantly lower than predictions from quark models (which gave $2.4-2.6$ GeV) and lattice calculations by Bali (2003) which suggested around $2.57$ GeV. The results support the conventional quark-antiquark interpretation advocated by Colangelo and Fazio (2003), Cahn and Jackson (2003), and Godfrey (2003), rather than the four-quark or molecular interpretations proposed by Cheng and Hou (2003) and Barnes, Close and Lipkin (2003). The authors note that the repulsion between these states and the $DK$ and $DK^*$ continuum may help lower their masses, and that the non-derivative current sum rules for the $(0^+, 1^+)$ doublet fail to produce a stable working window, making the derivative current results more reliable.
