# Impact of the $^5$Li resonance in $\alpha$-$p$ elastic scattering on precision measurements of neutrino oscillation parameters

## Key claim
The $^5$Li resonance in $\alpha$-$p$ elastic scattering, previously omitted from Geant4 simulations, explains the factor-of-ten discrepancy between simulated and measured misidentification probabilities for $^{214}$Bi-$^{214}$Po cascade decays mimicking inverse beta decay signals in liquid scintillator detectors. After incorporating the correct differential cross section, the recalculated misidentification probability of 1.9$\times$10$^{-4}$ aligns with the order of magnitude reported by SNO+ and JUNO, though the exact experimental values (0.014% and 0.024%) still differ from this single simulation result.

## Method
The authors incorporated the $^5$Li resonance differential cross section, derived from experimental data using the $R$-matrix formalism, into Geant4 simulations of $\alpha$-$p$ elastic scattering for alpha particles from $^{214}$Po (7.68 MeV) and $^{212}$Po (8.78 MeV) decays. They simulated the visible energy spectra of alpha particles in linear alkylbenzene-based liquid scintillator using Birks' law with proton quenching parameter $k$B=7.5$\times$10$^{-3}$ g/cm$^2$/MeV and alpha quenching parameter $k$B=3.5$\times$10$^{-3}$ g/cm$^2$/MeV, and compared the resulting misidentification probabilities and energy tail shapes with default Geant4 predictions and experimental data from JUNO and SNO+.

## Key numbers
- Misidentification probability for $^{214}$Po alpha to mimic IBD: 0.019% (Geant4 with $^5$Li resonance), compared to 0.001% (default Geant4), 0.014% (JUNO), and 0.024% (SNO+)
- For $^{212}$Po alpha (8.78 MeV): misidentification probability 0.059% with $^5$Li resonance vs. 0.003% with default Geant4
- Daya Bay nH analysis: overlooked background rate of 0.5 events per detector per day, leading to an increase of $\sin^22\theta_{13}$ by approximately 0.012
- PDG $\sin^2\theta_{13}$ value (0.0216$\pm$0.006) would increase by about 0.006 (1$\sigma$) to 0.0222
- $^5$Li resonance occurs at incident alpha energy of 8.8 MeV with width approximately 1.5 MeV
- For alpha energies above 5 MeV, the cross section is hundreds of times larger than Rutherford scattering

## Context
This work resolves a discrepancy between Geant4 simulations and experimental findings from JUNO (2025) and SNO+ (2025), which reported $^{214}$Bi-$^{214}$Po cascade decay misidentification probabilities ten times higher than default Geant4 predictions. The $^5$Li resonance in $\alpha$-$p$ scattering, a classic nuclear physics process described by the $R$-matrix formalism using experimental data from PUSA (2004), produces forward-peaked proton angular distributions that transfer more energy to protons than Rutherford scattering or the GHEISHA model used in default Geant4. The results enable JUNO and SNO+ to adopt more accurate delayed-energy shapes for background estimation, and imply that previous $\theta_{13}$ measurements from Daya Bay's neutron-hydrogen capture analysis (2024) underestimated $\sin^22\theta_{13}$ by approximately 0.012, while gadolinium-based analyses remain unaffected.
