# Quarkyonic matter with chiral symmetry restoration

> We present a novel unified approach to describe the dense symmetric nuclear matter by combining the quarkyonic matter framework with the parity doublet model. This integration allows for a consistent treatment of the transition from hadronic to quark degrees of freedom while incorporating chiral symmetry restoration effects. Our model introduces a chiral invariant mass for both baryons and constituent quarks, enabling a smooth crossover between hadronic and quark matter in symmetric nuclear matter. We derive the equation of state (EOS) for this hybrid system and investigate its thermodynamic properties. The model predicts a gradual onset of quark degrees of freedom at high densities while maintaining aspects of confinement.

## Key claim
The paper presents a unified model combining the quarkyonic matter framework with the parity doublet model (PDM) to describe dense symmetric nuclear matter, claiming a smooth crossover between hadronic and quark degrees of freedom. The key hedge is that the quarkyonic picture is valid only within a limited window of quark chemical potential (Λ_QCD < μ_q < √N_c Λ_QCD), and the negative parity state N(1535) is excluded from the density range studied because its mass lies near the upper bound of this window.

## Method
The authors construct a relativistic mean-field model where the thermodynamic potential combines the parity doublet model (with scalar σ and vector ω meson fields) with a quarkyonic description that partitions the Fermi sea into a hadronic shell near the Fermi surface and a quark core at lower momenta. The nucleon mass is split into a chiral invariant part m_0 and a chiral-symmetry-breaking part, and the constituent quark mass is defined as M_Q = m_+/3 in the simplest case, with a more general parametrization M_Q = m_+/w(σ) allowing adjustment of the quark mass after chiral restoration. The gap equations for σ and ω are solved at finite density, and the equation of state is derived from the thermodynamic potential.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} is used as the reference density for normal nuclear matter.
- The pion decay constant f_π = 92.4 MeV is used in the scalar potential.
- The quarkyonic phase onset occurs at approximately 1.4 n_0, signaled by a non-zero quark Fermi momentum.
- The chiral invariant mass m_0 is varied among 600, 700, and 800 MeV in the numerical results.
- The constituent quark mass in vacuum is M_Q = m_+/3, where m_+ is the nucleon mass (939 MeV in vacuum).
- The parameter w_0 in the generalized quark mass parametrization satisfies w_0 ≥ 3, with w_0 = 3 giving M_Q = m_0/3 in the chirally restored phase.

## Context
This work extends the quarkyonic matter framework of McLerran (2007, 2008, 2018) and the parity doublet model of Detar (1988) and Jido (2001) by introducing a chiral invariant mass for both baryons and constituent quarks. Previous quarkyonic models (McLerran 2018) used non-interacting matter with constant nucleon and quark masses, while this work incorporates interactions and density-dependent masses. The model enables future comparisons with neutron star observations (mass-radius, tidal deformabilities, cooling) and can be extended to beta-equilibrium and charge-neutral matter for direct astrophysical tests.
