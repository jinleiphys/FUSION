# Exposing the dead-cone effect of jet quenching in QCD medium

> When an energetic parton traverses the hot QCD medium it may suffer multiple scattering and lose its energy. The medium-induced gluon radiation for a massive quark will be suppressed relative to that of a light quark due to the dead-cone effect. The development of new declustering techniques of jet evolution makes a direct study of the dead-cone effect in the QCD medium possible for the first time. In this work, we compute the emission angle distribution of the charm-quark initiated splittings in $\rm D^0$ meson tagged jet and that of the light parton initiated splittings in an inclusive jet in p+p and Pb+Pb at $\rm 5.02$~TeV by utilizing the declustering techniques of jet evolution. The heavy quark propagation and indued energy loss in the QCD medium are simulated with the SHELL model based on the Langevin equation. By directly comparing the emission angle distributions of charm-quark-initiated splittings with those of light parton-initiated splittings at the same energy intervals of the initial parton, we provide insights into the fundamental splitting structure in A+A collisions, thereby exploring the possible observation of the dead-cone effect in medium-induced radiation. We further investigate the case of the emission angle distributions normalized to the number of splittings and find the dead-cone effect will broaden the emission angle of the splitting and reduce the possibility to occur such splitting, therefore leading the massive parton to lose less energy. We also find the collisional energy loss mechanism has a negligible impact on the medium modification to the emission angle distribution of the charm-quark initiated splittings for $\rm D^0$-meson tagged jets.

# Exposing the dead-cone effect of jet quenching in QCD medium

## Key claim

The dead-cone effect in medium-induced gluon radiation can be exposed in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV by comparing the emission angle distributions of charm-quark-initiated splittings in D0-meson tagged jets with those of light parton-initiated splittings in inclusive jets, using iterative jet declustering techniques; however, the observable R(θ) alone cannot be compared with unity because the numbers of D0-tagged jets and inclusive jets differ, and the averaged splitting angles per jet for D0 jets are smaller than those for inclusive jets at higher radiator energies, pointing to an important caveat in interpreting the suppression.

## Method

The authors used the SHELL model, which combines a modified Langevin equation for heavy quark propagation with the higher-twist approach for medium-induced gluon radiation, to simulate in-medium parton evolution in a (3+1)D viscous hydrodynamic medium (CLVisc). Jet constituents were reclustered using the Cambridge-Aachen algorithm, and the splitting tree was iteratively declustered to record the emission angle θ, relative momentum transfer kT, and radiator energy ERadiator for each splitting. The observable R(θ) was constructed as the ratio of splitting-angle distributions normalized to the number of jets for D0-meson tagged jets versus inclusive jets, computed at the same radiator energy intervals in both p+p and Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers

- The jet transport coefficient was set to q̂0 = 1.5 GeV²/fm, extracted from χ² fits to final-state hadron productions in Pb+Pb at √s = 5.02 TeV.
- The spatial diffusion coefficient was Ds(2πT) = 4, consistent with lattice QCD calculations giving Ds(2πT) = 3.7–7.
- The charm quark mass was assumed to be 1.275 GeV/c² for defining the dead-cone angle θdc < mc/ERadiator.
- The averaged splitting angles per splitting for D0-meson tagged jets in p+p ranged from 0.232 rad (20–35 GeV radiator) to 0.277 rad (5–10 GeV radiator); in Pb+Pb they ranged from 0.251 rad to 0.280 rad.
- The number of splittings per jet for D0-meson tagged jets in p+p ranged from 1.233 (5–10 GeV) to 1.822 (20–35 GeV); in Pb+Pb from 1.280 to 1.860.
- The averaged splitting angles per splitting for inclusive jets in p+p ranged from 0.220 rad (10–20 GeV) to 0.232 rad (20–35 GeV); in Pb+Pb from 0.249 rad to 0.256 rad.
- The number of splittings per jet for inclusive jets in p+p ranged from 1.358 (5–10 GeV) to 2.040 (20–35 GeV); in Pb+Pb from 1.405 to 1.977.
- The averaged splitting angles per jet for D0-meson tagged jets in p+p ranged from 0.34 rad (5–10 GeV) to 0.42 rad (20–35 GeV); in Pb+Pb from 0.36 rad to 0.47 rad.
- The averaged splitting angles per jet for inclusive jets in p+p ranged from 0.31 rad (5–10 GeV) to 0.47 rad (20–35 GeV); in Pb+Pb from 0.36 rad to 0.49 rad.

## Context

This work extends the ALICE (2021) measurement of the dead-cone effect in p+p collisions at 13 TeV to the QCD medium in Pb+Pb collisions, using the same iterative jet declustering techniques developed by Frye (2017), Dreyer (2018), and Cunqueiro (2018). The SHELL model has previously been used to predict heavy flavor jet observables measured by ATLAS (2022), CMS (2019), and ALICE (2021). The results enable experimental searches for the in-medium dead-cone effect using D0-meson tagged jets at the LHC, and can help constrain radiative energy loss descriptions in jet quenching models such as the higher-twist approach of Guo (2000), Zhang (2003), and Majumder (2009).
