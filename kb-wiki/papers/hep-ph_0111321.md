# On the equivalence of regularization schemes

> We illustrated via the sunset diagram that dimensional regularization 'deforms' the nonlocal contents of multi-loop diagrams with its equivalence to cut-off regularization scheme recovered only after sub-divergence were subtracted. Then we employed a differential equation approach for calculating loop diagrams to verify that dimensional regularization deformed the 'low energy' contents before subtraction. The virtues of the differential equation approach were argued especially in non-perturbative perspective.

# On the equivalence of regularization schemes

## Key claim
Dimensional regularization "deforms" the nonlocal (low-energy) content of multi-loop diagrams before any subtraction is performed, and its equivalence to cutoff regularization is only recovered after sub-divergence subtraction that itself alters the leading finite nonlocal term, a subtlety that becomes problematic in nonperturbative contexts where perturbative subtraction procedures may not be straightforwardly applicable.

## Method
The author compared the sunset diagram (two-loop self-energy at zero external momentum) in massive $\lambda\phi^4$ theory calculated using dimensional regularization versus cutoff regularization, extracting expressions from prior work by Collins and by Fleischer and Jegerlehner. A differential equation approach was then applied: the diagram was differentiated twice with respect to $m^2$ to reduce the divergence degree, the resulting well-defined integrals were computed, and the original diagram was recovered by integration with undetermined constants parametrizing the UV ill-definedness.

## Key numbers
- In dimensional regularization, the leading finite double-log term in $(4\pi)^4 I_{\theta}^{(D)}(m^2)$ is $-\frac{3m^2}{(4\pi)^4} \overline{L}^2(m)$, where $\overline{L}(m) \equiv \ln(m^2/\mu^2) + \gamma_E - \ln 4\pi$.
- In cutoff regularization, the corresponding leading finite double-log term in $(4\pi)^4 I_{\theta}^{(\Lambda)}(m^2)$ is $-\frac{3m^2}{2(4\pi)^4} \overline{L}^2(m)$, a factor of 1/2 smaller.
- The cutoff result includes a quadratic divergence $2\Lambda^2$ (coefficient $C=2$).
- After sub-divergence subtraction in dimensional regularization (Eq. 10), the double-log coefficient changes to agree with the cutoff scheme result.
- The differential equation solution (Eq. 16) gives $I_{\theta}(m^2) = -\frac{3m^2}{2(4\pi)^4}\{\ln^2 m^2 + 2(c_{(1;1)}-2)\ln m^2 - 2c_{(1;1)}+4+2c_{\theta;1}\} - \frac{3c_{\theta;2}}{(4\pi)^4}$, with the leading double log $-\frac{3m^2}{2(4\pi)^4}\ln^2 m^2$ matching the cutoff scheme.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of regularization scheme equivalence in effective field theory for nucleon interactions, referencing prior analyses by Jackiw (1974), Collins (1984), and Fleischer and Jegerlehner (1981) for the sunset diagram expressions. The author argues that while the cutoff scheme faithfully preserves low-energy information but introduces power divergences, dimensional regularization removes power divergences implicitly but deforms nonlocal content. The differential equation approach, building on ideas from 't Hooft and Veltman (1973) and Chetyrkin, Kataev, and Tkachov (1980), is proposed as a more natural method for nonperturbative contexts, replacing divergence subtraction with the fixing of undetermined constants through physical boundary conditions.
