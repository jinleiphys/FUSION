# Microscopic calculation of 6Li elastic and transition form factors

> Variational Monte Carlo wave functions, obtained from a realistic Hamiltonian consisting of the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana-IX three-nucleon interactions, are used to calculate the 6Li ground-state longitudinal and transverse form factors as well as transition form factors to the first four excited states. The charge and current operators include one- and two-body components, leading terms of which are constructed consistently with the two-nucleon interaction. The calculated form factors and radiative widths are in good agreement with available experimental data.

## Key claim

Variational Monte Carlo wave functions from the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana-IX three-nucleon interactions, combined with consistent two-body charge and current operators, produce form factors and radiative widths for 6Li that are in good agreement with experiment, though the ground state is underbound by nearly 5 MeV compared to experiment and the variational energies are an additional 10% above the more accurate Green's function Monte Carlo results.

## Method

The authors constructed six-body variational Monte Carlo wave functions for 6Li using a realistic Hamiltonian (AV18/UIX) with two- and three-body correlation operators, then computed longitudinal and transverse form factors for the ground state and transitions to the first four excited states. Charge and current operators included one-body impulse approximation terms plus two-body meson-exchange contributions (π-like, ρ-like, ωπγ, ρπγ, and Δ-isobar currents) constructed consistently with the two-nucleon interaction. Matrix elements were evaluated via Metropolis Monte Carlo integration.

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energy: 27.0(1) MeV (VMC), 31.2(1) MeV (GFMC), 31.99 MeV (experiment); underbound by nearly 5 MeV
- Ground state point-nucleon radius constrained to experimental value of 2.43 fm
- Ground state quadrupole moment: -0.23(9) fm² (calculated) vs -0.08 fm² (experimental)
- Ground state magnetic moment: 0.829 μN (IA) and 0.832 μN (with two-body currents), about 1% larger than experiment
- Radiative width of (3⁺;0) state: 3.38(9)×10⁻⁴ eV (IA and MEC) vs (4.40 ± 0.34)×10⁻⁴ eV (experiment)
- Radiative width of (0⁺;1) state: 7.49(2) eV (IA) and 9.06(7) eV (with MEC) vs 8.19 ± 0.17 eV (experiment); isovector two-body currents increase γ-width by 20%
- Radiative width of (2⁺;0) state: 8.0(5)×10⁻³ eV (IA and MEC) vs (5.4 ± 2.8)×10⁻³ eV (experiment)
- Radiative width of (2⁺;1) state: 0.050(9) eV (IA) and 0.075(26) eV (with MEC) vs 0.27 ± 0.05 eV (experiment); calculation several times smaller
- Monopole cutoff values: Λπ = 3.8 fm⁻¹ at πNN and πNΔ vertices; Λρ = Λω = 6.3 fm⁻¹ at ρNN and ωNN vertices

## Context

This work extends realistic microscopic calculations of electromagnetic structure from the A=2-4 systems (reviewed by Carlson and Schiavilla, 1998) to the p-shell nucleus 6Li, using the same AV18/UIX Hamiltonian that reproduces binding energies and charge radii of ³H, ³He, and ⁴He. Previous calculations relied on simpler shell-model approaches (Donnelly and Walecka, 1973; Vergados, 1974; Bergstrom, 1975), α-d cluster models (Bergstrom, 1979), αNN clusterization (Eskandarian, 1988; Kukulin, 1990; Ryzhikh, 1993), or large-space multi-ħω shell models (Karataglidis, 1997), but these had tenuous connections to the underlying two- and three-nucleon dynamics. The results demonstrate that nuclear many-body theory can provide a quantitatively satisfactory description of electromagnetic structure for both s- and p-shell nuclei across a wide range of momentum transfers, enabling future tests on heavier p-shell systems.
