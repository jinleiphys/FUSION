# Freeze-out Configuration in Multifragmentation

> The excitation energy and the nuclear density at the time of breakup are extracted for the $α+ ^{197}Au$ reaction at beam energies of 1 and 3.6 GeV/nucleon. These quantities are calculated from the average relative velocity of intermediate mass fragments (IMF) at large correlation angles as a function of the multiplicity of IMFs using a statistical model coupled with many-body Coulomb trajectory calculations. The Coulomb component $\vec{v}_{c}$ and thermal component $\vec{v}_{0}$ are found to depend oppositely on the excitation energy, IMFs multiplicity, and freeze-out density. These dependencies allow the determination of both the volume and the mean excitation energy at the time of breakup. It is found that the volume remained constant as the beam energy was increased, with a breakup density of about $ρ_{0}/7$, but that the excitation energy increased $25\%$ to about 5.5 MeV/nucleon.

# Freeze-out Configuration in Multifragmentation

## Key claim

The freeze-out density remains constant at about ρ₀/7 for both beam energies, while the mean excitation energy increases from 4.5 MeV/nucleon to 5.5 MeV/nucleon when the beam energy is raised from 1 to 3.6 GeV/nucleon. This extraction relies on the opposite dependence of the Coulomb and thermal velocity components on excitation energy, IMF multiplicity, and freeze-out density, and is specific to the highly asymmetric α + ¹⁹⁷Au system where the IMF multiplicity fluctuates at a relatively narrow distribution of excitation energies.

## Method

The authors used the Berlin version of a statistical model coupled with many-body Coulomb trajectory calculations to simulate multifragmentation of the target residue formed in α + ¹⁹⁷Au reactions. They calculated the average relative velocity ⟨v_rel⟩ of intermediate mass fragments (IMFs, defined as 3 ≤ Z ≤ 15 or 6 ≤ A ≤ 30) at large correlation angles (105°–180°) as a function of the IMF multiplicity, and compared these calculations to experimental data from the FASA 4π detector at JINR Dubna. The residue masses and charges (A=150, Z=62 for 3.6 GeV/nucleon; A=170, Z=70 for 1 GeV/nucleon) were determined from cascade model calculations.

## Key numbers

- Breakup density: ρ_c ≈ ρ₀/7 for both beam energies (1 and 3.6 GeV/nucleon)
- Mean excitation energy at 1 GeV/nucleon: 4.5 MeV/nucleon
- Mean excitation energy at 3.6 GeV/nucleon: 5.5 MeV/nucleon (a 25% increase)
- Residue mass and charge at 3.6 GeV/nucleon: A=150, Z=62
- Residue mass and charge at 1 GeV/nucleon: A=170, Z=70
- Uncertainty in breakup density: 15%, estimated from fluctuations in residue charge and excitation energy (⟨v_rel⟩ varies by about 4%, corresponding to a 5% variation in freeze-out radius R_c)

## Context

This work extends the study of freeze-out conditions beyond the three-body dynamics approach used by the ALADIN collaboration (Lindenstruth, 1993) by analyzing the dependence of ⟨v_rel⟩ on IMF multiplicity across many-fragment events. The method exploits the advantage of the highly asymmetric α + Au system, where IMF multiplicity fluctuations arise primarily from statistical fluctuations at nearly constant excitation energy, unlike heavy-ion induced reactions where multiplicity variation is driven by broad excitation energy distributions. The extracted freeze-out density is similar to values deduced from nucleus-nucleus collisions, suggesting that compression effects in the latter play a minor role. The results constrain dynamical models of hot nucleus formation and the nuclear equation of state at low density.
