# Isospin Dependence of Proton and Neutron Radii within Relativistic Mean Field Theory

> The binding energies, shapes and sizes of even-even beta-stable nuclei with A >= 40 and a few chains of isotopes with Z=50, 56, 82, 94 protons and isotones with N=50, 82, 126 neutrons are analyzed. The average isospin dependence of the radii of protons and neutrons evaluated within the relativistic mean field theory is studied. A simple, phenomenological formula for neutron radii is proposed.

# Isospin Dependence of Proton and Neutron Radii within Relativistic Mean Field Theory

## Key claim

The relativistic mean field theory (RMFT) with the NL-3 parameter set predicts that the renormalized proton and neutron radius constants depend almost linearly on the relative neutron excess I = (N-Z)/A, with the proton radius constant decreasing and the neutron radius constant increasing with I; however, the isospin dependence of the RMFT charge radii is about 20% weaker than the global experimental trend described by the phenomenological formula of Nerlo-Pomorska (1994), suggesting that the NL-3 parameters should be slightly changed.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Hartree equations iteratively using the NL-3 parameter set of the mean field Lagrangian, with pairing treated via a BCS-type wave function using experimental energy gaps from Wapstra-Audi tables (or 12/√A MeV where data were unavailable). They calculated root mean square radii, quadrupole moments, binding energies, and separation energies for even-even beta-stable nuclei with A ≥ 40, plus isotope chains with Z = 50, 56, 82, 94 and isotone chains with N = 50, 82, 126, extending calculations up to the proton or neutron drip line. The raw RMSR values were divided by √(3/5) A^(1/3) and renormalized to spherical shape using a volume conservation factor g(β₂) = 1 + (5/4π)β₂² to extract the isospin dependence of the radius constants.

## Key numbers

- Proton radius constant formula: R^p_0 = 1.237 (1 - 0.157·(N-Z)/A - 0.646/A) A^(1/3) fm
- Neutron radius constant formula: R^n_0 = 1.176 (1 + 0.250·(N-Z)/A + 2.806/A) A^(1/3) fm
- Charge radius formula from RMFT: R^ch_0 = 1.241 (1 - 0.154·(N-Z)/A + 0.580/A) A^(1/3) fm
- Ratio formula: r_p/r_n = 1.048 (1 - 0.364·(N-Z)/A - 3.148/A)
- The RMFT binding energy error is at most 5 MeV (for A = 150)
- For Sn isotopes, neutron and proton RMSR differ by up to 0.5 fm for the heaviest isotopes
- The renormalized radius constants of protons and neutrons cluster around 1.25 fm and 1.20 fm respectively as functions of I
- The term 0.64 fm² in the charge-proton radius relation originates from the finite size of the proton

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological charge radius formula of Nerlo-Pomorska (1994) by providing separate proton and neutron radius formulae derived from RMFT calculations, and it confirms the isospin dependence trends suggested by Dobaczewski (1996) from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with Skyrme forces. The proposed formula (18) allows estimation of neutron RMSR from measured charge RMSR, which is useful because over 250 charge radii are measured while only few neutron radii exist experimentally. The results will be used to develop a liquid drop model with different proton and neutron density distributions, as noted by Dieperink (in progress).
