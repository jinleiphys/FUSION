# Examinations of the consistency of the quasiparticle random-phase approximation approach to double-$β$ decay of $^{48}$Ca

> The nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) of the neutrinoless and two-neutrino double-$β$ decays of $^{48}$Ca are calculated by the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) with emphasis on the consistency examinations of this calculation method. The main new examination points are the consistency of two ways to treat the intermediate-state energies in the two-neutrino double-$β$ NME and comparison with the experimental charge-exchange strength functions obtained from $^{48}$Ca$(p,n)$ and $^{48}$Ti$(n,p)$ reactions. No decisive problem preventing the QRPA approach is found. The obtained neutrinoless double-$β$ NME adjusted by the ratio of the effective and bare axial-vector current couplings is lowest in those calculated by several groups and close to one of the QRPA values obtained by another group.

## Key claim

The quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) approach to double-beta decay of ⁴⁸Ca passes two new consistency checks, the treatment of intermediate-state energies in the two-neutrino matrix element and comparison with experimental charge-exchange strength functions, but the effective axial-vector coupling g_A must be reduced to 0.48–0.49 to reproduce the experimental half-life, which is 58% of the value needed for ¹⁵⁰Nd in the same framework.

## Method

The author performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Skyrme interaction SkM* with contact pairing interactions, then constructed proton-neutron QRPA and like-particle QRPA states for both ⁴⁸Ca and ⁴⁸Ti. The two-neutrino double-beta nuclear matrix element was computed using two different sets of intermediate-state energies (based on the initial and final ground states), and the consistency of the two results was examined. The Gamow-Teller strength function was compared with experimental data from ⁴⁸Ca(p,n) and ⁴⁸Ti(n,p) reactions. The 0νββ nuclear matrix element was calculated using the same framework with the g_A value fixed by the 2νββ half-life.

## Key numbers

- Experimental 2νββ half-life of ⁴⁸Ca: T_{1/2}^{(2ν)exp} = (6.4 ± 1.2) × 10¹⁹ yr (Arn16)
- Reproduced with g_A = 0.48 giving M^{(2ν)}(I) = 0.138 and T_{1/2}^{(2ν)} = 6.339 × 10¹⁹ yr
- Reproduced with g_A = 0.49 giving M^{(2ν)}(F) = 0.133 and T_{1/2}^{(2ν)} = 6.274 × 10¹⁹ yr
- Relative difference between M^{(2ν)}(I) and M^{(2ν)}(F) is approximately 4%
- Obtained 0νββ NME M^{(0ν)} = 3.054 with g_A = 0.49
- Reduced half-life R_{1/2}^{(0ν)} = 19.572 × 10¹² MeV² yr for the author's calculation
- Pairing gaps from HFB: Δ_p = 2.200 MeV and Δ_n = 1.671 MeV for ⁴⁸Ti; Δ_p = 1.731 MeV and Δ_n = 0 MeV for ⁴⁸Ca
- Pairing interaction strengths: −258.4 MeV fm³ (protons) and −224.5 MeV fm³ (neutrons)
- Isovector pn pairing strength g_{(T,T_z)=(1,0)}^pair = −241.43 MeV fm³
- T=0 pairing strength g_{T=0}^pair = −180.0 MeV fm³

## Context

This work extends the QRPA approach of Terasaki (2015, 2016) for ¹⁵⁰Nd → ¹⁵⁰Sm to the ⁴⁸Ca → ⁴⁸Ti system, which is less commonly studied in QRPA due to uncertain pairing gaps. The consistency check of two intermediate-state energy treatments is new, and the comparison with experimental charge-exchange data from Yako (2009) tests the quenching of Gamow-Teller strength. The obtained 0νββ NME is lowest among calculations by different groups and close to one QRPA value from another group; the reduced half-life is largest among all methods, implying a longer predicted half-life than shell-model calculations such as Iwata (2016) by a factor of five. This work enables more reliable predictions for the 0νββ decay of ⁴⁸Ca, which is a candidate for experiments including CANDLES, NEMO-3, and SuperNEMO.
