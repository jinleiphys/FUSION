# Net Charge Fluctuations as a signal of QGP from Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

> We report the first model study of the net charge fluctuations in terms of D-measure within the framework of the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. Net charge fluctuation is estimated from the charge susceptibility evaluated using PNJL model. A parameterization of the freeze-out curve has been used to obtain D as a function of sqrt(s). We have discussed our results vis-a-vis recent experimental findings from ALICE collaboration.

## Key claim

The Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model can reproduce the general features and numerical range of the net charge fluctuation observable \(D\) as a function of center-of-mass energy \(\sqrt{s}\) along the freeze-out curve, but the entire curve lies in the hadronic phase, meaning that if fully consistent with experiment, there would be no signature of a partonic phase in \(D\).

## Method

The authors computed the charge susceptibility \(\chi_Q\) from the pressure of the PNJL model and the total charge density \(n_{ch}\) from quark distribution functions, for both 2-flavor and 2+1-flavor systems. They then evaluated the \(D\)-measure, defined as \(D = 4\,\chi_Q / (n_{ch}/T^3)\), as a function of temperature and baryon chemical potential. To connect with experiments, they used a parameterization of the freeze-out curve from Cleymans et al. (2006) to obtain \(D\) as a function of \(\sqrt{s}\) from 5 GeV to 3 TeV.

## Key numbers

* For 2 flavor at low \(\sqrt{s}\), \(D\) takes a value of \(\sim 4\); for 2+1 flavor, \(\sim 3.5\).
* Around \(\sqrt{s} \sim 200\) GeV, \(D\) drops to 2.6 for 2 flavor and 2.4 for 2+1 flavor, then saturates at these values for increasing \(\sqrt{s}\).
* The ratio \(\langle Q \rangle / \langle N_{ch} \rangle\) varied from 0.025 for high temperature and low \(\mu_B\) to 0.3 at the other extreme.
* The freeze-out parameterization used \(a = 0.166 \pm 0.002\) GeV, \(b = 0.139 \pm 0.016\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(c = 0.053 \pm 0.021\) GeV\(^{-3}\) for the temperature curve.

## Context

This work extends prior PNJL model studies of fluctuations and correlations of conserved charges by Ray et al. (2010) and Deb et al. (2009) to the specific observable of net charge fluctuations measured via the \(D\)-measure. It provides the first model comparison to experimental results from the ALICE collaboration (2012) at \(\sqrt{s} = 2.76\) TeV and STAR collaboration data in the range \(19.6 < \sqrt{s} < 200\) GeV. The results enable future comparisons with complete experimental data to determine whether the \(D\)-measure can serve as a signature of quark-gluon plasma formation.
