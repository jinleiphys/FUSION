# Some remarks on the interpretation of degree of nonextensivity

> Recently we have demostrated that the nonextensitivity parameter q occuring in some applications of Tsallis statistics (known also as index of the corresponding Lévy distribution) is, in the q>1 case, given entirely by the fluctuations of the parameters of the usual exponential distribution. We show here that this interpretation is valid also for the q<1 case. The parameter q is therefore a measure of fluctuations of the parameters of the usual exponential distribution.

# Some remarks on the interpretation of degree of nonextensivity

## Key claim

The nonextensivity parameter \(q\) in Tsallis statistics (also the index of the corresponding Lévy distribution) is entirely given by the relative variance of fluctuations of the parameter \(1/\chi\) of the usual exponential distribution, and this interpretation holds for both \(q>1\) and \(q<1\) cases. For \(q<1\), the fluctuations of \(\chi\) become dependent on the variable \(\varepsilon\) itself, but the relative variance remains \(\varepsilon\)-independent and equals \(1-q\).

## Method

The authors start from an exponential distribution \(L_{q=1}(\varepsilon) = C \exp(-\varepsilon/\chi)\) and allow the parameter \(\chi\) to fluctuate according to a Gamma distribution. They show analytically, using the Euler gamma function representation, that averaging the exponential over these fluctuations yields the power-like Lévy distribution \(L_q(\varepsilon) = C_q [1 - (1-q)\varepsilon/\chi_0]^{1/(1-q)}\) for both \(q>1\) and \(q<1\). For the \(q<1\) case, they derive the resulting Gamma distribution for \(f(1/\chi)\) and compute the mean, variance, and relative variance of \(1/\chi\), demonstrating that the relative variance \(\omega = 1-q\) is independent of \(\varepsilon\). They then connect this to a Langevin-type equation for temperature fluctuations, showing that the \(q<1\) case corresponds to the presence of an internal heat source term.

## Key numbers

- For \(q>1\): relative variance \(\omega = q - 1\).
- For \(q<1\): relative variance \(\omega = 1 - q\).
- For \(q<1\), the Lévy distribution \(L_q(\varepsilon)\) is defined only for \(\varepsilon \in [0, \chi_0/(1-q)]\).
- The mean value of \(1/\chi\) for \(q<1\) is \(\langle 1/\chi \rangle = 1/(\chi_0 - \varepsilon/\alpha')\), where \(\alpha' = 1/(1-q)\).
- The variance of \(1/\chi\) for \(q<1\) is \(\langle (1/\chi)^2 \rangle - \langle 1/\chi \rangle^2 = (1/\alpha') \cdot 1/(\chi_0 - \varepsilon/\alpha')^2\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous result (Wilk and Wlodarczyk, 2000) which demonstrated that for \(q>1\) the parameter \(q\) is given by fluctuations of the exponential distribution parameter. It shows that the same interpretation applies to the \(q<1\) case, unifying the physical meaning of \(q\) across both regimes. The derivation uses a Langevin-type equation and Fokker-Planck formalism (Gardiner, 1985) to connect the Gamma distribution of fluctuations to stochastic processes. This enables a physical interpretation where \(q>1\) corresponds to temperature fluctuations without an internal heat source, while \(q<1\) corresponds to fluctuations with an energy source term, relevant for thermal models in nuclear physics and nonextensive statistics.
