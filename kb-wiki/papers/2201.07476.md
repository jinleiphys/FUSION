# Competition between weak and alpha-decay modes in superheavy nuclei

> The competition between alpha- and beta+/EC-decay modes is studied systematically in nuclei along the alpha-decay chains following the synthesis of superheavy nuclei with Z = 119 and Z = 120. A microscopic approach based on deformed self-consistent Hartree-Fock mean-field calculations with Skyrme forces and pairing correlations is used to describe the beta+ and electron capture weak decays, whereas the alpha-decay is estimated from existing phenomenological expressions. It is shown that alpha-decay is in most cases the dominant decay mode, but interesting instances are identified where the half-lives are comparable, opening the possibility of new pathways towards more neutron-rich nuclei in this region.

# Competition between weak and alpha-decay modes in superheavy nuclei

## Key claim
Alpha decay is the dominant decay mode in most superheavy nuclei along the decay chains following synthesis of Z=119 and Z=120, but the author identifies specific instances where beta+/EC half-lives become comparable to or shorter than alpha half-lives, particularly for nuclei with atomic number Z below 108 and neutron-proton difference N-Z below 55, opening potential pathways toward more neutron-rich superheavy nuclei.

## Method
The author performs deformed self-consistent Hartree-Fock mean-field calculations with Skyrme SLy4 interaction and BCS pairing correlations to describe the nuclear structure of parent and daughter nuclei, then calculates Gamow-Teller strength distributions using the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) with a spin-isospin separable residual interaction. Alpha-decay half-lives are estimated using the phenomenological Royer formula, and Q-values are taken from experimental masses (AME2020) where available or from the WS4+RBF mass model otherwise.

## Key numbers
- For even-Z, even-N nuclei: $^{244}$Cf (N-Z=48) has $T_{\alpha} = 2.16 \times 10^{3}$ s and $T_{\beta^+/EC} = 2.53 \times 10^{3}$ s, giving a ratio $\mathcal{R} = 1.2 \times 10^{0}$
- For even-Z, odd-N nuclei: $^{247}$Cf (N-Z=51) has $T_{\alpha} = 2.50 \times 10^{7}$ s and $T_{\beta^+/EC} = 8.49 \times 10^{3}$ s, giving $\mathcal{R} = 3.4 \times 10^{-4}$ (weak decay faster)
- For odd-Z, even-N nuclei: $^{249}$Es (N-Z=51) has $T_{\alpha} = 7.12 \times 10^{5}$ s and $T_{\beta^+/EC} = 2.10 \times 10^{2}$ s, giving $\mathcal{R} = 2.9 \times 10^{-4}$ (weak decay faster)
- For even-Z, odd-N nuclei: $^{235}$Pu (N-Z=47) has $T_{\alpha} = 1.62 \times 10^{8}$ s and $T_{\beta^+/EC} = 1.23 \times 10^{3}$ s, giving $\mathcal{R} = 7.6 \times 10^{-6}$
- The quenching factor used for the axial-vector coupling constant is $(g_A/g_V)_{\rm eff} = 0.77 \times (g_A/g_V)_{\rm free}$ with $(g_A/g_V)_{\rm free} = -1.270$
- The constant D in the half-life formula is 6143 s

## Context
This work extends the author's previous microscopic calculations of beta-decay half-lives in superheavy nuclei (Sarriguren, 2019-2021) to a systematic survey of entire alpha-decay chains starting from Z=119 and Z=120. It contrasts with purely phenomenological approaches such as those by Zhang (2006), Karpov (2012), and Singh (2020) that neglect nuclear structure effects, and with the folded-Yukawa pnQRPA calculations by Moller (2019). The identification of regions where weak decay competes with or dominates over alpha decay could guide experimental searches using delayed X-ray coincidence techniques, as demonstrated for $^{257}$Rf, $^{258}$Db, and $^{244}$Md by Hessberger and Khuyagbaatar.
