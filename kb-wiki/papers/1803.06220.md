# Collective motion in prolate γ-rigid nuclei within minimal length concept via a quantum perturbation method

> Based on the minimal length concept, inspired by Heisenberg algebra, a closed analytical formula is derived for the energy spectrum of the prolate γ-rigid Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian of nuclei, within a quantum perturbation method (QPM), by considering a scaled Davidson potential in \b{eta} shape variable. In the resulting solution, called X(3)-D-ML, the ground state and the first \b{eta}-band are all studied as a function of the free parameters. The fact of introducing the minimal length concept with a QPM makes the model very flexible and a powerful approach to describe nuclear collective excitations of a variety of vibrational-like nuclei. The introduction of scaling parameters in the Davidson potential enables us to get a physical minimum of this latter in comparison with previous works. The analysis of the corrected wave function, as well as the probability density distribution, shows that the minimal length parameter has a physical upper bound limit.

## Key claim

The paper derives an approximate analytical formula for the energy spectrum of prolate gamma-rigid nuclei by combining the minimal length concept with a scaled Davidson potential, using a quantum perturbation method (QPM) rather than an exact solution, and claims the minimal length parameter has a physical upper bound limit beyond which the wave function becomes unphysical.

## Method

The authors start from the Bohr-Mottelson Hamiltonian for gamma-rigid nuclei (gamma fixed to 0 degrees) and incorporate the minimal length concept by modifying the canonical commutation relation to include a minimal length parameter alpha. They treat the Davidson potential in the beta shape variable and solve the resulting differential equation using the asymptotic iteration method (AIM) for the unperturbed case (alpha=0), then apply first-order quantum perturbation theory to compute the energy correction from the minimal length term. The model, called X(3)-D-ML, has three adjustable parameters: the minimal length parameter alpha and the two Davidson potential parameters a and b.

## Key numbers

* The ground state bandhead ratio R4/2 for 150Nd is predicted as 3.110, compared to the observed value 2.929, with a root-mean-square deviation sigma = 0.2652.
* For 186Pt, the minimal length parameter alpha takes its largest fitted value of 0.2045, with a=0.9662 and b=0.0000, yielding R4/2(Predicted)=2.412 versus R4/2(Observed)=2.560.
* For 100Pd, the model achieves the smallest sigma = 0.0568, with a=3.0368, b=0.0000, and alpha=0.0049.
* The cross-correlation coefficient between observed and predicted signature ratios R4/2 for all fitted nuclei is rho = 0.95.
* The potential minimum beta_0 ranges from 0.0000 (for many vibrator-like nuclei) up to 0.9975 for 184Os.

## Context

This work extends the X(3)-ML model previously introduced by the same group (Chabab et al., 2015) by replacing the infinite square well potential with a Davidson potential containing scaling parameters. It also builds on earlier treatments of gamma-rigid nuclei with various beta potentials, including X(3)-beta^2, X(3)-beta^4, X(3)-beta^6, and the sextic potential models. The introduction of scaling parameters in the Davidson potential resolves a problem from earlier works (Budaca et al., various years) where the potential minimum was unphysically large (beta_0 > 1). The model enables description of both vibrational-like nuclei (R4/2 below 2.4) and rotational-like nuclei (R4/2 above 3.0) within a single framework, and the authors note that calculations of electromagnetic transition rates using the corrected wave functions remain for future work.
