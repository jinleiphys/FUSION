# On the limit cycle for the 1/r^2 potential in momentum space

> The renormalization of the attractive 1/r^2 potential has recently been studied using a variety of regulators. In particular, it was shown that renormalization with a square well in position space allows multiple solutions for the depth of the square well, including, but not requiring a renormalization group limit cycle. Here, we consider the renormalization of the 1/r^2 potential in momentum space. We regulate the problem with a momentum cutoff and absorb the cutoff dependence using a momentum-independent counterterm potential. The strength of this counterterm is uniquely determined and runs on a limit cycle. We also calculate the bound state spectrum and scattering observables, emphasizing the manifestation of the limit cycle in these observables.

## Key claim

The renormalization of the attractive 1/r² potential in momentum space, using a momentum cutoff and a momentum-independent counterterm, uniquely determines the counterterm strength to run on a limit cycle, in contrast to position-space square-well regularization where a continuous (non-limit-cycle) dependence on the cutoff can be chosen.

## Method

The authors Fourier-transformed the attractive 1/r² potential to momentum space, obtaining V(q) = 2π²c/q. They regulated the S-wave Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a sharp momentum cutoff Λ and added a momentum-independent counterterm δV ∝ H(Λ)/Λ. They determined H(Λ) analytically by demanding that the relative phase of the zero-energy bound-state solution remain invariant under variations of Λ, then computed bound-state energies and S-wave scattering phase shifts and cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The preferred scaling factor for the limit cycle is λ₀ = exp(π/ν), where ν > 0 parameterizes the potential strength via c = -1/4 - ν².
- For the bound-state spectrum, the binding energies satisfy ln B_N = c₁ - N(2π/ν) + 2 ln Λ, with c₁ = -2.07 ± 0.03 for the deepest state labeled N = 0.
- The β function for H is β(H) = -(1/4)(1-H)² - ν²(1+H)², which is negative definite for ν > 0 and has no real fixed points.
- The phase shift has the form δ(k) = β(ν) - ν ln(k/Λ_*), where Λ_* is a free dimensionful parameter.

## Context

This work extends the study of renormalization group limit cycles for the 1/r² potential, previously treated with a square-well regulator in position space by Beane et al. (2000) and Bawin and Coon (2003), and with a delta-shell regulator by Braaten and Phillips (2004). The momentum-space approach mirrors the effective field theory treatment of the three-body Efimov effect (Efimov, 1970; Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck, 1999) and shows that the limit cycle is unavoidable with a momentum cutoff, unlike the square-well case. The results enable further study of discrete scale invariance in scattering observables and connect to experimental searches for Efimov trimers in cold-atom systems near Feshbach resonances.
