# The $0νββ$-decay nuclear matrix element for light and heavy neutrino mass mechanisms from deformed QRPA cacluations for $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{130}$Te, $^{136}$Xe and $^{150}$Nd with isospin restoration

> In this work, with restored isospin symmetry, we evaluated the neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear matrix elements for $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{130}$Te, $^{136}$Xe and $^{150}$Nd for both the light and heavy neutrino mass mechanisms using the deformed QRPA approach with realistic forces. We give detailed decompositions of the nuclear matrix elements over different intermediate states and nucleon pairs, and discuss how these decompositions are affected by the model space truncations. Compared to the spherical calculations, our results show reductions from $30\%$ to about $60\%$ of the nuclear matrix elements for the calculated isotopes mainly due to the presence of BCS overlap factor between the initial and final ground states. The comparison between different nucleon-nucleon forces with corresponding Short-Range-Correlations (src) shows, that the choice of the NN force gives roughly $20\%$ deviations for light exchange neutrino mechanism and much larger deviations for the heavy neutrino exchange mechanism.

## Key claim

The deformed QRPA calculations with isospin restoration yield reductions of the neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear matrix elements by 30% to about 60% compared to spherical calculations for the five isotopes studied, primarily due to the BCS overlap factor between initial and final ground states rather than deformation effects on the wave functions themselves.

## Method

The authors used the deformed Quasi-Particle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) with realistic G-matrix forces (Argonne V18 and CD-Bonn) obtained from the Brueckner equation, starting from single-particle wave functions solved from a Coulomb-corrected Woods-Saxon potential with axial symmetry. Isospin symmetry was restored by separating the fitting of T=0 and T=1 particle-particle residual interactions, with the T=1 strength fixed by requiring the Fermi two-neutrino double beta decay matrix element to be zero, and the T=0 strength fixed by reproducing experimental two-neutrino double beta decay matrix elements. The nuclear matrix elements were computed for both light and heavy neutrino exchange mechanisms, with detailed decompositions over intermediate states and nucleon pairs.

## Key numbers

- BCS overlap factors between initial and final ground states: 0.72 for 76Ge/76Se, 0.71 for 82Se/82Kr, 0.73 for 130Te/130Xe, 0.43 for 136Xe/136Ba, and 0.51 for 150Nd/150Sm (using AV18 interaction)
- Total 0νββ nuclear matrix elements for light neutrino mechanism (AV18, with short-range correlations, gA = gA0): 3.12 for 76Ge, 2.86 for 82Se, 2.90 for 130Te, 1.11 for 136Xe, and 3.01 for 150Nd
- Total 0νββ nuclear matrix elements for light neutrino mechanism (AV18, with short-range correlations, gA = 0.75 gA0): 2.48 for 76Ge, 2.26 for 82Se, 2.13 for 130Te, 0.86 for 136Xe, and 2.33 for 150Nd
- The tensor part reduces the Gamow-Teller matrix elements by about 15% for the light neutrino mechanism
- The choice of NN force gives roughly 20% deviations for the light neutrino exchange mechanism and much larger deviations for the heavy neutrino exchange mechanism
- Short-range correlations from CD-Bonn give enhancement of several percent to total NME, while Argonne V18 short-range correlations reduce NME by several percent, leading to about 10% difference between the two potentials

## Context

This work extends the deformed QRPA approach with realistic forces previously developed by the same group (Fang et al., 2015) and applies it to five candidate nuclei for neutrinoless double beta decay with isospin symmetry restoration following the method of Rodin and Faessler (2011) and Simkovic et al. (2013). The results show that spherical calculations (Simkovic et al., 2013) overestimate the nuclear matrix elements by 30-60% because they neglect the BCS overlap factor between initial and final ground states. The calculations enable more reliable predictions for experimental searches by providing nuclear matrix elements that account for deformation effects, and the detailed decomposition over intermediate states helps understand the sensitivity to model space truncations and different nucleon-nucleon forces.
