# The n-n-alpha System in a Continuum Faddeev Formulation

> The continuum Faddeev equations for the neutron-neutron-alpha (n-n-$α$) system are formulated for a general interaction as well as for finite rank forces. In addition, the capture process n+n+$α\rightarrow ^6$He+$γ$ is derived.

## Key claim

The paper formulates continuum Faddeev equations for the n+n+α system, including the capture process n+n+α → ⁶He+γ, but does not present numerical solutions; it only derives the formal framework and multiple scattering series, noting that the third-order amplitudes in the two-body t-matrix are smooth enough to serve as driving terms for iterative solution via Padé summation.

## Method

The authors derive coupled Faddeev equations for the three-to-three scattering amplitude in the n+n+α system using Jacobi momenta and partial wave decomposition, incorporating antisymmetrization of the two neutrons via the transposition operator P₂₃ to reduce the three coupled equations to two. They then develop the multiple scattering series by iterating the kernel, showing that terms up to second order in the two-body t-matrix contain logarithmic singularities while third-order terms are smooth, and also present a formulation using rank-1 separable (finite rank) forces that reduces the equations to coupled one-dimensional integral equations.

## Key numbers

- The mass ratio parameters are defined as α = 1/2, β = m_α/(m + m_α), γ = (2m + m_α)/(2(m + m_α)), and β̄ = m/(m + m_α), where m is the neutron mass and m_α the mass of the ⁴He nucleus.
- The two-neutron t-matrix is denoted t_{α₁′}(p₁′ p₁, E_{q₁}) and the neutron-α t-matrix as t_{α₂′}(p₂′ p₂, E_{q₂}), with no numerical values given for these interactions.
- The paper contains no numerical results for cross sections, resonance energies, or widths; it is a formal derivation only.

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev approach, previously applied to three-nucleon systems and to d-α scattering by Koike (1980s) and Eskandarian (1992), to the n+n+α continuum where the predominant existing method is the Hyper-spherical Harmonics (HH) approach used by Danilin (1997–2007), Fedorov (2003), and others. The authors argue that the Faddeev method using Jacobi momenta avoids convergence problems of HH in mapping out strong final-state interaction peaks, and the derived formalism enables future calculation of the three-body break-up amplitude and the capture process n+n+α → ⁶He+γ, which is relevant for astrophysical production of ⁶He in supernova shock environments as discussed by de Diego (2010).
