# An Area Law for Entanglement Entropy in Particle Scattering

> The scattering cross section is the effective area of collision when two particles collide. Quantum mechanically, it is a measure of the probability for a specific process to take place. Employing wave packets to describe the scattering process, we compute the entanglement entropy in 2-to-2 scattering of particles in a general setting using the $S$-matrix formalism. Applying the optical theorem, we show that the linear entropy $\mathcal{E}_2$ is given by the elastic cross section $σ_{\text{el}}$ in unit of the transverse size $L^2$ of the wave packet, $\mathcal{E}_2 \sim σ_{\text{el}}/L^2$, when the initial states are not entangled. The result allows for dual interpretations of the entanglement entropy as an area and as a probability. Since $σ_{\text{el}}$ is generally believed, and observed experimentally, to grow with the collision energy $\sqrt{s}$ in the high energy regime, the result suggests a "second law" of entanglement entropy for high energy collisions. Furthermore, the Froissart bound places an upper limit on the entropy growth.

# An Area Law for Entanglement Entropy in Particle Scattering

## Key claim

The linear entanglement entropy in 2-to-2 particle scattering is given by the elastic cross section divided by the transverse area of the incoming wave packets, $\mathcal{E}_2 \sim \sigma_{\text{el}}/L^2$, but only when the initial states are not entangled and in the plane-wave limit where the momentum spread $\delta p$ goes to zero. This relation holds for any quantum field theory without committing to a specific theory, using only the S-matrix formalism and the optical theorem.

## Method

The authors construct a bipartite system from two distinguishable particles A and B undergoing 2-to-2 elastic scattering, described by momentum wave packets with transverse size $L$ and momentum width $\delta p$. They compute the reduced density matrix for particle A by tracing over particle B in the final state, then evaluate the linear entropy $\mathcal{E}_2 = 1 - \text{Tr}(\rho_A)^2$ to leading order in the small parameter $\delta p/|\vec{k}|$. The optical theorem is applied to relate the imaginary part of the forward scattering amplitude to the total cross section, and the inelastic probability is expressed in terms of the inelastic cross section times the inverse transverse area $1/L^2$.

## Key numbers

- The linear entropy for unentangled initial states is $\mathcal{E}_2^{\text{f}} = 2\sigma_{\text{el}}/L^2 + \mathcal{O}(\delta p^5/|\vec{k}|^5)$, where $\sigma_{\text{el}}$ is the elastic cross section and $L^2$ is the transverse area of the wave packet.
- The inelastic probability is $\mathcal{P}_{\text{inel}} = I_0(|\vec{k}|)[\sigma_{\text{inel}} + \mathcal{O}(\delta p/|\vec{k}|)]$, with $I_0(|\vec{k}|) = 1/L^2 + \mathcal{O}(\delta p/|\vec{k}|)$.
- For the n-th Tsallis entropy, $\mathcal{E}_n(\rho^{\text{f}}_A) = \frac{n}{n-1}\frac{\sigma_{\text{el}}}{L^2} + \mathcal{O}(\delta p^5/|\vec{k}|^5)$.
- For mixed initial states with unpolarized scattering, $\mathcal{E}_{2,A}^{\text{f}} = \frac{2}{n_A}\frac{\overline{\sigma_{\text{el}}}}{L^2} + \mathcal{O}(\delta p^5/|\vec{k}|^5)$, where $n_A$ counts the number of nonzero entries in the initial density matrix.

## Context

This work extends recent calculations of entanglement entropy in particle scattering that were performed in specific quantum field theories (Dvali 2014, Beane 2018, Low 2021, Cheung 2023) by providing a general, non-perturbative S-matrix derivation. The result connects to the classic Froissart bound (Froissart 1961, Martin 1962) on total cross sections and the Cheng-Wu argument (Cheng 1969, Cheng 1970) that elastic cross sections grow with collision energy, suggesting a "second law" where entanglement entropy increases with center-of-mass energy. The area-law interpretation parallels the Bekenstein-Hawking entropy (Bekenstein 1973, Hawking 1975) and area laws in many-body systems (Srednicki 1993, Eisert 2008), but here the "area" is the scattering cross section rather than a spatial boundary.
