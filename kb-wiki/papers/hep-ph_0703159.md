# BEC-BCS Crossover in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio Model of QCD

> The BEC-BCS crossover in QCD at finite baryon and isospin chemical potentials is investigated in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. The diquark condensation in two color QCD and the pion condensation in real QCD would undergo a BEC-BCS crossover when the corresponding chemical potential increases. We determined the crossover chemical potential as well as the BEC and BCS regions. The crossover is not triggered by increasing the strength of attractive interaction among quarks but driven by changing the charge density. The chiral symmetry restoration at finite temperature and density plays an important role in the BEC-BCS crossover. For real QCD, strong couplings in diquark and vector meson channels can induce a diquark BEC-BCS crossover in color superconductor, and in the BEC region the chromomagnetic instability is fully cured and the ground state is a uniform phase.

## Key claim

The BEC-BCS crossover for diquarks in two-color QCD and pions in real QCD is not driven by increasing the attractive coupling among fermions (as in ultracold atomic gases) but instead by increasing the baryon or isospin chemical potential, which changes the charge density and restores chiral symmetry; the crossover chemical potential is defined by the condition where the non-relativistic chemical potential \(\mu_N = \mu_B/2 - m\) changes sign.

## Method

The authors use the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with two colors and two flavors, employing a hard three-momentum cutoff \(\Lambda = 653\) MeV and coupling \(G = 1.5 \times 4.93\) GeV\(^{-2}\) to fit vacuum pion properties. They compute the mean-field thermodynamic potential and gap equations at both finite temperature (above \(T_c\)) and zero temperature (in the superfluid phase), extracting the diquark spectral function, quark momentum distribution, and the effective quark mass as functions of baryon chemical potential. The crossover point is identified analytically from the condition \(\mu_B/2 < m(\mu_B)\) using the condensate scaling relations.

## Key numbers

- The crossover chemical potential for diquarks in two-color QCD is \(\mu_B^0 \simeq 230\) MeV at zero temperature, derived analytically as \(\mu_B^0 = [2 m(0) m_\pi^2]^{1/3}\) with vacuum quark mass \(m(0)=300\) MeV and pion mass \(m_\pi=134\) MeV.
- The critical temperature for diquark condensation at \(\mu_B=0\) is \(T_0 = 185\) MeV.
- The diquark condensed phase starts at \(\mu_B = m_\pi = 134\) MeV, and the critical temperature follows \(T_c = T_0 \sqrt{1 - (m_\pi/\mu_B)^4}\) at moderate chemical potential.
- The effective quark mass at the critical temperature scales as \(m(\mu_B)/m(0) \simeq (m_\pi/\mu_B)^2\) for \(\mu_B\) near the critical value.
- When the vacuum quark mass varies from 300 MeV to 500 MeV, the crossover chemical potential \(\mu_B^0\) changes from 230 MeV to 270 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the BEC-BCS crossover concept, previously studied in non-relativistic fermion gases (BEC1–BEC6) and relativistic fermion superfluids (RBEC1–RBEC3), to QCD at finite baryon and isospin density using the NJL model. The density-driven crossover mechanism differs from the coupling-driven crossover in ultracold atoms and is analogous to the crossover found in nuclear matter (NM) and non-dilute Fermi gas (NDFG). The results agree with lattice simulations of two-color QCD (2QCD1–2QCD4) and with chiral effective theory (CBT2), and they enable future studies of chromomagnetic instability in color superconductors, which is fully cured in the BEC region.
