# On the origin of trigger-angle dependence of di-hadron correlations

> The STAR Collaboration reported measurements of di-hadron azimuthal correlation in medium-central Au+Au collisions at 200 A$\,$GeV, where the data are presented as a function of the trigger particle's azimuthal angle relative to the event plane $ϕ_s $. In particular, it is observed that the away-side correlation evolves from single- to double-peak structure with increasing $ϕ_s$. In this work, we present the calculated correlations as functions of both $ϕ_s$ and particle transverse momentum $p_T $, using the hydrodynamic code NeXSPheRIO. The results are found to be in reasonable agreement with the STAR data. We further argue that the above $ϕ_s$ dependence of the correlation structure can be understood in terms of one-tube model, as due to an interplay between the background elliptic flow caused by the initial state global geometry and the flow produced by fluctuations.

## Key claim

The observed trigger-angle dependence of di-hadron correlations, where the away-side structure evolves from a single peak to a double peak as the trigger particle moves from in-plane to out-of-plane, can be explained by the interplay between the background elliptic flow from the initial global geometry and the flow produced by fluctuations, specifically a peripheral energetic tube. This explanation is argued to hold even though the hydrodynamic simulations reproduce the data only in "reasonable agreement" and the analytic model is simplified to qualitative behavior.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations using the NeXSPheRIO code for 20-60% centrality Au+Au collisions at 200 A GeV, generating 1200 NeXuS events and solving relativistic ideal hydrodynamics for each. Di-hadron azimuthal correlations were computed both with and without a pseudo-rapidity cut of |Δη| > 0.7, using the two-particle cumulant method for background subtraction. Separately, an analytic one-tube model was developed where the single-particle distribution is written as a sum of a background elliptic flow term and a tube term with v₂ and v₃ components, and the resulting di-hadron correlation was derived using both cumulant and ZYAM background subtraction methods.

## Key numbers

- 1200 NeXuS events generated for 20-60% centrality Au+Au collisions at 200 A GeV
- Monte-Carlo decoupling invoked 300 times for more central to 500 times for more peripheral collisions
- The Δη cut of |Δη| > 0.7 was applied to isolate ridge from jet contributions
- In the analytic model, the background elliptic flow parameter v₂^b = 0.25 and tube flow parameters v₂^t = v₃^t = 0.1 were used
- The multiplicity fluctuation parameter ⟨N_b²⟩ - ⟨N_b⟩² = 0.022 was used in the analytic model
- The tube multiplicity squared ⟨N_t²⟩ = 0.45 was used in the analytic model
- The background multiplicity squared ⟨N_b²⟩ = 100 was used for the ZYAM calculation
- The scale factor B(φ_s=0) = 1.000053 and B(φ_s=π/2) = 1 were determined by the ZYAM minimum condition

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic studies of di-hadron correlations and the "ridge" phenomenon by the same group (Qian et al., 2011; Andrade et al., 2011) and directly addresses STAR measurements (STAR Collaboration, 2010, 2011) of trigger-angle-dependent correlations in Au+Au collisions. The one-tube model was previously introduced by the authors to explain ridge and shoulder structures, and here it is adapted to non-central collisions to explain the in-plane/out-of-plane effect. The results enable a physical understanding of how event-by-event fluctuations and collective flow combine to produce the observed correlation patterns, and the analytic model provides a framework that can be extended to include higher-order harmonics or multiple tubes.
