# (3+1)D dilute Glasma initial conditions in simulations of heavy-ion collisions

> In this thesis, an approximation for the full (3+1)D dynamics of the Glasma is presented, which breaks boost-invariance on the level of the nuclear fields and leads to rapidity dependence in the final results. For this treatment, the Yang-Mills equations are linearized in covariant gauge, where lower-order, nonlinear contributions are neglected and the dynamics are captured by the (3+1)D dilute Glasma. The analytic solutions of the (3+1)D dilute Glasma are derived in both position and momentum space formulations, providing a comprehensive understanding of the involved (3+1)D dynamics. In position space, the field strength tensor results from the integration of free-streaming gluons that are produced in $2\rightarrow1$ scattering processes where the initial nuclear fields overlap. In momentum space, the event-averaged gluon number distribution for the (3+1)D dilute Glasma is derived in Coulomb gauge. A generalized, three-dimensional McLerran-Venugopalan nuclear model is developed for nuclei with realistic envelopes and intrinsic longitudinal correlations. Numerical results are presented for the rapidity structure of the energy-momentum tensor, the gluon number distribution, and the transverse energy of the (3+1)D dilute Glasma. In position space, the extended longitudinal collision geometry and finite longitudinal correlation length break boost-invariance. In momentum space, the results each follow universal parametrizations and are fixed by the values of two scaling parameters. Furthermore, the numerical results exhibit limiting fragmentation where the rapidity profiles approach a limiting distribution at large rapidities. This feature is also derived locally in position space for the analytic expressions of the field strength tensor and, in momentum space, for the transverse energy of the (3+1)D dilute Glasma.

## Key claim

The paper presents an approximation for the full (3+1)D dynamics of the Glasma that breaks boost-invariance at the level of the nuclear fields, but the treatment is limited to a linearized regime where lower-order nonlinear contributions are neglected, meaning the results are strictly valid only for the dilute Glasma.

## Method

The Yang-Mills equations are linearized in covariant gauge, neglecting lower-order nonlinear contributions, to capture the dynamics of the (3+1)D dilute Glasma. Analytic solutions are derived in both position space, where the field strength tensor results from integrating free-streaming gluons produced in 2→1 scattering processes, and in momentum space, where the event-averaged gluon number distribution is derived in Coulomb gauge. A generalized three-dimensional McLerran-Venugopalan nuclear model is developed for nuclei with realistic envelopes and intrinsic longitudinal correlations, and an efficient numerical implementation calculates the energy-momentum tensor on an event-by-event basis.

## Context

This work extends the established (2+1)D boost-invariant Glasma framework by introducing genuine rapidity dependence through a linearized approximation, breaking the boost-invariance that characterizes standard Color Glass Condensate simulations. The generalized three-dimensional McLerran-Venugopalan model with realistic nuclear envelopes and intrinsic longitudinal correlations enables state-of-the-art heavy-ion collision simulation frameworks to incorporate rapidity-dependent initial conditions. The results exhibit limiting fragmentation, a feature previously observed in experimental data, which is derived both locally in position space for the field strength tensor and in momentum space for the transverse energy.
