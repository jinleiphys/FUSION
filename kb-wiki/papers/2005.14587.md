# Energy dependence of the optical potential of the weakly bound 9Be projectile on the 197Au target

> In this work we measured elastic and inelastic angular distributions of the weakly bound 9Be projectile on the 197Au target at several bombarding energies from 84% up to 140% of the Coulomb barrier. The elastic angular distributions were analyzed using a phenomenological Woods-Saxon potential and a double folding Sao Paulo potential and the energy dependence was extracted. Angular distributions from two inelastic peaks were compared with coupled channel calculations using reduced transition probabilities available in the literature. The energy dependence of the two interaction potential models show a similar trend in the region of the Coulomb barrier. Dispersion relation calculation demonstrates the presence of the breakup threshold anomaly proposed for weakly bound systems.

## Key claim

The energy dependence of the optical potential for the weakly bound 9Be projectile on the 197Au target shows the breakup threshold anomaly (BTA) rather than the usual threshold anomaly (TA), as demonstrated by dispersion relation calculations; however, the authors note that the physical conclusions depend on the potential model used, and the results are consistent with BTA only under the condition that the dispersion relation is verified using a statistical method on random sampling.

## Method

Elastic and inelastic angular distributions were measured for the 9Be + 197Au system at twelve bombarding energies from 34 to 48 MeV (84% to 140% of the Coulomb barrier). The elastic data were analyzed using two optical model potentials: a phenomenological Woods-Saxon potential (with energy-independent, energy-dependent fixed geometry, and energy-dependent variable geometry variants) and a double folding Sao Paulo potential. Inelastic angular distributions for two excited states of 197Au (at approximately 274 keV and 540 keV) were analyzed using coupled channel calculations with reduced transition probabilities from the literature, and the dispersion relation was tested to determine the presence of the breakup threshold anomaly.

## Key numbers

- Bombarding energies: 34, 35, 36, 37, 38, 39, 40, 41, 42, 44, 46, and 48 MeV (laboratory frame)
- Coulomb barrier estimated at 40.5 MeV (laboratory) or 38.7 MeV (center-of-mass)
- Target thickness: 250 microgram/cm2 self-supporting 197Au foil
- Energy resolution of detectors: 0.2% to 0.5%
- Inelastic peaks observed at excitation energies of 274(40) keV and 540(50) keV
- Overall experimental uncertainties: 2-6%, rising to 10% at higher energies and backward angles
- Global estimator for Woods-Saxon energy-dependent variable geometry fit: 0.575
- Global estimator for Sao Paulo potential fit: 0.760
- Neutron separation energy of 9Be: 1.67 MeV
- Alpha separation energy of 9Be: 2.47 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies on the breakup threshold anomaly for weakly bound systems, particularly for 9Be projectiles on various targets such as 80Se (Gollan, 2018) and 64Zn (Gomezcamacho, 2007), where BTA was observed. It contrasts with results for 7Li projectiles on targets like 59Co (Souza, 2007), 80Se (Fimiani, 2012), 138Ba (Maciel, 1999), 144Sm (Figueira, 2010), and 208Pb (Keeley, 1994), which showed the usual threshold anomaly. The use of both Woods-Saxon and Sao Paulo potentials addresses the model dependence ambiguity noted in prior work, and the statistical method for dispersion relation verification provides a more rigorous criterion than earlier analyses.
