# Quasifission dynamics in TDHF

> For light and medium mass systems the capture cross-section may be considered to be the same as that for complete fusion, whereas for heavy systems leading to superheavy formations the evaporation residue cross-section is dramatically reduced due to the quasifission (QF) and fusion-fission processes thus making the capture cross-section to be essentially the sum of these two cross-sections, with QF occurring at a much shorter time-scale. Consequently, quasifission is the primary reaction mechanism that limits the formation of superheavy nuclei. Within the last few years the time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) approach has been utilized for studying the dynamics of quasifission. The study of quasifission is showing a great promise to provide insight based on very favorable comparisons with experimental data. In this article we will focus on the TDHF calculations of quasifission observables for the $^{48}$Ca+$^{249}$Bk system.

## Key claim

The paper argues that TDHF calculations of quasifission for the $^{48}$Ca+$^{249}$Bk system show promising agreement with experimental data, but the authors acknowledge that TDHF is a deterministic theory that yields only the most probable outcome rather than a full distribution of mass-angle distributions.

## Method

The authors performed three-dimensional TDHF calculations on a Cartesian grid with no symmetry restrictions using the Skyrme SLy4d energy density functional including time-odd terms. They generated static HF wave functions for the two nuclei, applied a boost operator, and propagated in time using a Taylor series expansion up to orders 10-12 with a time step of 0.4 fm/c. They also used density-constrained TDHF (DC-TDHF) to calculate microscopic potential barriers and fragment excitation energies directly from the TDHF density evolution.

## Key numbers

- Initial separation of the two nuclei was 30 fm
- Time step used for propagation was Delta t = 0.4 fm/c
- For side orientation of $^{249}$Bk, quasielastic collisions occur below E_c.m. = 204 MeV, while fusion (contact times exceeding 35 zs) occurs above 211 MeV
- For side orientation, quasifission is limited to a small energy range E_c.m. = 209-211 MeV
- For tip orientation of $^{249}$Bk, quasifission ranges from E_c.m. = 191 MeV to E_c.m. = 218 MeV
- Heavy fragments contain up to 60 MeV (side orientation) and up to about 55 MeV (tip orientation) of excitation energy
- Light fragments contain up to 45 MeV (side orientation) and up to about 30 MeV (tip orientation) of excitation energy
- Contact times for quasifission are 8-30 zs at E_c.m. = 218 MeV
- At E_c.m. = 218 MeV, quasifission corresponds to charge transfer from 20 to 40 and mass transfer from 48 to 100
- At impact parameter b = 2 fm, the light fragment is a neutron rich Zr isotope with A approximately 102-106

## Context

This work extends previous TDHF studies of quasifission for systems such as $^{40,48}$Ca+$^{238}$U (Oberacker 2014) and builds on earlier dynamical models (Fazio 2005, Adamian 2003, 2009, Nasirov 2009, Feng 2009) that argued for dinuclear complex formation in heavy systems. The results enable microscopic predictions of fragment mass-angle distributions, excitation energy repartition, and contact times for superheavy element formation reactions, with further calculations underway for a full range of observables including fragment total kinetic energies.
