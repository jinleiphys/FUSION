# Upsilon Production as a Probe for Early State Dynamics in High Energy Nuclear Collisions at RHIC

> Upsilon production in heavy ion collisions at RHIC energy is investigated. While the transverse momentum spectra of the ground state Upsilon(1s) are controlled by the initial state Cronin effect, the excited $b\bar b$ states are characterized by the competition between the cold and hot nuclear matter effects and sensitive to the dissociation temperatures determined by the heavy quark potential. We emphasize that it is necessary to measure the excited heavy quark states in order to extract the early stage information in high energy nuclear collisions at RHIC.

## Key claim

The observed Upsilon(1s) yield in Au+Au collisions at RHIC is dominated by direct production and is not sensitive to the hot medium, but the excited bottomonium states (Upsilon(1p), Upsilon(2s)) are sensitive to the heavy quark potential and the initial temperature of the fireball, provided that the dissociation temperatures are determined by whether the internal energy U or the free energy F is used as the potential.

## Method

The authors solved a classical Boltzmann transport equation for the phase-space distribution of Upsilon(1s), Upsilon(1p), and Upsilon(2s) states moving through a hydrodynamic background medium described by ideal hydrodynamics with a Hubble-like longitudinal expansion. The suppression mechanism was gluon dissociation, with the dissociation cross section taken as temperature-independent below a state-specific dissociation temperature T_Upsilon, which was obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation for the b-bbar system using either the internal energy U or the free energy F as the heavy quark potential. The initial transverse momentum distribution was generated by PYTHIA with the Cronin effect included via a transverse momentum broadening parameter a_gN = 0.2 (GeV/c)^2/fm.

## Key numbers

- Dissociation temperatures for V=U: T_Upsilon/T_c = 4 (Upsilon(1s)), 1.8 (Upsilon(1p)), 1.6 (Upsilon(2s)), with T_c = 165 MeV.
- Dissociation temperatures for V=F: T_Upsilon/T_c = 3 (Upsilon(1s)), 1.1 (Upsilon(1p)), 1 (Upsilon(2s)).
- Initial temperature of the fireball: T_i = 340 MeV.
- Feed-down fractions to observed Upsilon(1s): a_Upsilon = 51% (direct), 37% (Upsilon(1p)), 12% (Upsilon(2s)).
- Minimum bias R_AA for total Upsilon(1s): 0.53 for V=F, 0.63 for V=U, compared to preliminary PHENIX data R_AA < 0.64.
- In central Au+Au collisions, R_AA ~ a_1s = 0.51.
- The Cronin broadening parameter a_gN = 0.2 (GeV/c)^2/fm; the p+p average transverse momentum squared from PYTHIA is 7.1 (GeV/c)^2.
- The ratio of Cronin effects between Upsilon at RHIC and J/psi at SPS is 2.4, based on a_gN^SPS = 0.08 GeV^2/fm and nuclear radii.

## Context

This work extends the transport model approach previously applied to J/psi production (Liu et al.) to the Upsilon system at RHIC energy (sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV). It contrasts with J/psi studies at SPS and RHIC (Matsui, 1986; Topilskaya; Alessandro; Adare) where regeneration complicates the interpretation, while Upsilon regeneration is negligible (Grandchamp, 2004). The results enable extraction of the initial temperature of the quark-gluon plasma by measuring the nuclear modification factor of excited Upsilon states, and show a quantitative relation between the Cronin effect for Upsilon at RHIC and J/psi at SPS.
