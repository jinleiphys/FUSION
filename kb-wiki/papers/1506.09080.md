# Multihadron production dynamics exploring the energy balance in hadronic and nuclear collisions

> The multihadron production in nucleus-nucleus collisions and its interrelation with that in (anti)proton-proton interactions are studied by exploring the charged particle mean multiplicity collision-energy and centrality dependencies in the measurements to date. The study is performed in the framework of the recently proposed effective-energy approach which, under the proper scaling of the collision energy, combines the constituent quark picture with Landau relativistic hydrodynamics counting for the centrality-defined effective energy of participants and relating different types of collisions. Within this approach, the multiplicity energy dependence and the pseudorapidity spectra from the most central nuclear collisions are well reproduced. The study of the multiplicity centrality dependence reveals a new scaling between the measured pseudorapidity spectra and the calculations. By means of this scaling, called the energy balanced limiting fragmentation scaling, one reproduces the pseudorapidity spectra for all centralities. The scaling elucidates some differences in the multiplicity and midrapidity density centrality dependence obtained at RHIC and LHC. These findings reveal an inherent similarity in the multiplicity energy dependence from the most central collisions and centrality data. A new regime in heavy-ion collisions to occur at about a TeV energy is indicated, similar to that observed in the earlier studies of the midrapidity densities. Predictions are made for the mean multiplicities to be measured in proton-proton and heavy-ion collisions at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the charged particle mean multiplicity in nucleus-nucleus collisions and (anti)proton-proton collisions can be unified through an effective-energy approach that combines constituent quark degrees of freedom with Landau relativistic hydrodynamics, provided the collision energy is scaled by the number of participating constituent quarks and the centrality-defined effective energy. The claim is hedged by the observation that this universality holds well for LHC data but shows visible deviations for RHIC data at medium centralities, and that a new scaling called "energy-balanced limiting fragmentation scaling" is needed to reproduce pseudorapidity spectra for all centralities.

## Method

The authors apply the participant dissipating energy (PDE) approach, which treats particle production as driven by the effective energy deposited by constituent quark participants into the Lorentz-contracted overlap volume. For head-on heavy-ion collisions, all three constituent quarks from each nucleon participate, making the full nucleon energy available, while in pp collisions only one quark pair interacts, giving an effective energy of 1/3 of the nucleon energy. The centrality dependence is incorporated by defining an effective c.m. energy ε_NN = √s_NN (1 − α), where α is the average centrality fraction, and then applying the same scaling relations that connect pp and AA collisions at the corresponding scaled energies.

## Key numbers

- The hybrid fit to head-on AA multiplicity per participant pair is: 2N_ch/N_part = (−0.577 ± 0.177) + (0.394 ± 0.094) ln(s_NN) + (0.213 ± 0.014) ln²(s_NN) + (0.005 ± 0.009) (s_NN)^(0.55 ± 0.11), with s_0 = 1 GeV²
- The power-law fit to head-on AA multiplicity per participant pair is: 2N_ch/N_part = (−6.72 ± 1.44) + (5.42 ± 1.11) (s_NN)^(0.18 ± 0.02)
- Predicted pp mean multiplicities: 47 at √s_pp = 2.36 TeV, 67 at 7 TeV, and 79 at 13 TeV, with 5% uncertainties
- The hybrid fit to pp multiplicity is: N_ch^pp = (1.60 ± 0.23) + (−0.03 ± 0.10) ln(s_pp) + (0.18 ± 0.01) ln²(s_pp) + (0.03 ± 0.02) (s_pp)^(0.29 ± 0.06)
- The power-law fit to pp multiplicity is: N_ch^pp = (−7.36 ± 0.16) + (6.97 ± 0.12) (s_pp)^(0.133 ± 0.001)
- A new regime in heavy-ion collisions is indicated at about √s_NN ≈ 1 TeV, based on the transition from log² to power-law behavior

## Context

This work extends the participant dissipating energy approach previously developed by Sarkisyan et al. (2010, 2011) and applied to midrapidity densities, now covering the full pseudorapidity spectra and centrality dependence up to LHC energies. The study reveals a complementarity between central collision data and centrality data when expressed in terms of effective energy, and introduces the energy-balanced limiting fragmentation scaling to describe pseudorapidity spectra for all centralities. The findings enable predictions for mean multiplicities in pp collisions at √s_pp = 13 TeV and in heavy-ion collisions at √s_NN = 5.13 TeV, and clarify differences in the centrality dependence of multiplicities measured at RHIC (where multiplicity per participant pair is independent of centrality) versus LHC (where it decreases with centrality).
