# Saturation and Scaling of Multiplicity, Mean p_T and p_T Distributions from 200 GeV < sqrt{s} < 7 TeV

> The multiplicity, average transverse momentum, and charged particle transverse momentum distributions have recently been measured in LHC experiments. The multiplicity and average transverse momentum grow with beam energy. Such growth is expected in the theory of the Color Glass Condensate, a theory that incorporates the physics of saturation into the evolution of the gluon distribution. We show that the energy dependence of the $p\overline{p}$ data and the LHC data for $pp$ scattering at \sqrt{s} > 200 GeV may be simply described using a minimal amount of model input. Such a description uses parameters consistent with the Color Glass Condensate descriptions of HERA and RHIC experimental data.

## Key claim

The energy dependence of charged particle multiplicity, average transverse momentum, and transverse momentum distributions in proton-proton and proton-antiproton collisions from 200 GeV to 7 TeV can be described using a minimal Color Glass Condensate model, with parameters consistent with those extracted from HERA deep inelastic scattering and RHIC data, though the optimal saturation exponent from hadron data may be slightly larger than the DIS value of λ ≈ 0.23.

## Method

The authors fit the charged particle pseudorapidity density to a power law dN_ch/dη = A E^λ, and the average transverse momentum to ⟨p_T⟩ = B + C E^(λ/2), using data from LHC, UA1, UA5, and CDF experiments. They test geometric scaling of transverse momentum distributions by rescaling CMS data using the scaling variable τ = p_T² / Q_sat²(p_T/√s) with λ fixed from DIS, and quantify the scaling quality by computing normalized deviations between distributions at different energies using a Tsallis parametrization of the CMS data.

## Key numbers

- Charged particle pseudorapidity density follows dN_ch/dη = 3.7043 × E^0.23, where E is in TeV, based on a fit to data from √s = 0.9 to 7 TeV.
- Average transverse momentum follows ⟨p_T⟩ = B + C E^0.115, with the same power law exponent for both pp and p̅p data.
- Tsallis fit parameters for CMS p_T distributions: at √s = 0.9 TeV, T = 0.130 GeV and n = 7.7; at 2.36 TeV, T = 0.140 GeV and n = 6.7; at 7.0 TeV, T = 0.145 GeV and n = 6.6.
- Predicted multiplicity at √s = 10 TeV: dN_ch/dη|_(η=0) = 6.29 ± 0.25; at 14 TeV: 6.80 ± 0.27.
- Predicted average p_T at √s = 10 TeV: 0.562 ± 0.017 GeV; at 14 TeV: 0.579 ± 0.017 GeV.
- Predicted Tsallis parameters for √s = 10 TeV: T = 0.153 GeV, n = 6.6; for 14 TeV: T = 0.162 GeV, n = 6.7.

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate framework, previously applied to HERA deep inelastic scattering data by Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff (1998) and to RHIC data, to LHC pp collisions. It provides a simpler, parameter-minimal alternative to more detailed saturation-based predictions by Kharzeev, Levin, and Nardi (2004) and Rezaeian et al. (2009-2010). The results enable predictions for higher LHC energies and suggest that saturation effects may influence transverse momentum distributions out to very high p_T, which could affect jet quenching measurements in heavy ion collisions.
