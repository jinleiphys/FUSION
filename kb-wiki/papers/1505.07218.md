# Few-nucleon systems with state-of-the-art chiral nucleon-nucleon forces

> We apply improved nucleon-nucleon potentials up to fifth order in chiral effective field theory, along with a new analysis of the theoretical truncation errors, to study nucleon-deuteron (Nd) scattering and selected low-energy observables in 3H, 4He, and 6Li. Calculations beyond second order differ from experiment well outside the range of quantified uncertainties, providing truly unambiguous evidence for missing three-nucleon forces within the employed framework. The sizes of the required three-nucleon force contributions agree well with expectations based on Weinberg's power counting. We identify the energy range in elastic Nd scattering best suited to study three-nucleon force effects and estimate the achievable accuracy of theoretical predictions for various observables.

## Key claim

Calculations using improved chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials up to fifth order (N4LO), without including three-nucleon forces, disagree with experimental data for few-nucleon observables well outside the quantified theoretical uncertainties, providing unambiguous evidence that three-nucleon forces are missing within the chiral effective field theory framework. The size of the required three-nucleon force contributions agrees well with expectations based on Weinberg's power counting.

## Method

The authors applied a new generation of chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials up to N4LO (fifth order) using a novel ultraviolet regularization scheme with regulator R = 1.0 fm, and employed a new procedure for estimating theoretical truncation errors that accounts for the neglect of many-body forces. They solved the Faddeev equations for nucleon-deuteron (Nd) scattering observables and used both Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations and the no-core shell model (NCSM) for bound states of 3H, 4He, and 6Li, with Similarity Renormalization Group evolution applied for the 6Li calculations to enhance convergence.

## Key numbers

- 3H ground state energy at N3LO (N4LO) is expected to be accurate at the level of about 50 keV (about 10 keV) for regulator choices R = 0.8, 0.9, and 1.0 fm
- For 6Li at N4LO, the ground state energy is -26.9(4) MeV at SRG scale alpha = 0.04 fm4 and -26.9(2) MeV at alpha = 0.08 fm4
- The discrepancy between NN-only predictions and data for the Nd total cross section agrees within 1.5 times the estimated size of N2LO corrections
- At the lowest energy of 10 MeV in Nd scattering, the discrepancy in the total cross section is much smaller than the estimated size of N2LO contributions
- Nd scattering observables at N4LO are expected to be accurately described up to energies of at least 200 MeV
- The energy range best suited for studying three-nucleon force effects in elastic Nd scattering is Elab approximately 70 to 200 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory program of Epelbaum et al. (2014) by applying improved NN potentials beyond the two-nucleon system for the first time, using a new error analysis procedure. The results contradict earlier calculations using older N3LO potentials from Entem and Machleidt (2003) and Epelbaum et al. (2004), which showed larger finite-cutoff artifacts and distortions in the cross section minimum as found by Witala et al. (2013). The paper enables future work on the explicit inclusion of consistent three-nucleon forces at N3LO and N4LO, which the authors state is in progress, and suggests that chiral EFT at these orders should be capable of resolving the long-standing three-nucleon force problem.
