# Universal spectrum of isolated three-body resonances

> The exact wavefunction of an isolated three-body resonance at finite scattering length is obtained for two identical particles interacting with another one via a pairwise zero-range potential. The corresponding universal spectrum is studied as a function of the scattering length. The universality of the results is illustrated by considering a model with finite-range interactions.

## Key claim

The paper derives the universal spectrum of isolated three-body resonances (ITBR) as a function of the two-body scattering length, but only for configurations where the scaling exponent is real and positive (i.e., where the Efimov effect does not occur), and the results are valid in the limit of small three-body energy and small detuning from the unitary threshold.

## Method

The author solves the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian (STM) equation for three-body bound states using a contact model with zero-range pairwise interactions, imposing Wigner-Bethe-Peierls contact conditions. The universal functions are obtained from their expressions at unitarity via a Lippmann-Schwinger-like equation, and the spectrum is derived from an energy-dependent log-derivative condition applied at a chosen hyperradius. The universality is tested by comparing with a finite-range separable potential model for two identical fermions interacting with an impurity.

## Key numbers

- For a 2FI system with scaling exponent s = 0.25 (mass ratio M/m = 13.28...), the binding wavenumber at unitarity is q_u = 1.34... x 10^(-3)/epsilon, where epsilon is the range of the separable potential.
- For the same system, the endpoint of the universal spectrum is at Radius/a^- approximately -1.56 x 10^(-3), compared to the reference model value Radius/a^- approximately -1.57 x 10^(-3).
- For a 2FI system with scaling exponent s = 1.25 (mass ratio M/m = 6.093...), the binding wavenumber at unitarity is q_u epsilon = 1.39... x 10^(-4), and the endpoint is at Radius/a^- approximately -1.19 x 10^(-5).
- The critical mass ratio for an ITBR in the p-wave sector (l=1) with a pure Gaussian separable potential (alpha=0) is M/m >= 13.10418..., corresponding to scaling exponent s < 0.3106...

## Context

This work extends the author's previous results on the universal spectrum of isolated three-body resonances at unitarity (Pricoupenko 2023, 2024) to finite scattering length, and generalizes the one-parameter law found at unitarity by Werner and Castin (2006) and Gao (2015) for small scaling exponents. The results contrast with the Efimov effect, which produces an infinite number of states at unitarity and occurs when the scaling exponent is imaginary. The paper enables the description of ITBR in systems such as ultracold atoms with Feshbach resonances and neutron-deuteron scattering, where the two-body scattering length is large but finite.
