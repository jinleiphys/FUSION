# Nucleon Gravitational Form Factors

> A symmetry-preserving analysis of strong interaction quantum field equations is used to complete a unified treatment of pion, kaon, nucleon electromagnetic and gravitational form factors. Findings include a demonstration that the pion near-core pressure is roughly twice that in the proton, so both are significantly greater than that of a neutron star; parton species separations of the nucleon's three gravitational form factors, in which, inter alia, the glue-to-quark ratio for each form factor is seen to take the same constant value, independent of momentum transfer; and a determination of proton radii orderings, with the mechanical (normal force) radius being less than the mass-energy radius, which is less than the proton charge radius. This body of predictions should prove useful in an era of anticipated experiments that will enable them to be tested.

## Key claim

Using a symmetry-preserving, systematically-improvable truncation of quantum field equations (rainbow-ladder truncation), the authors provide parameter-free predictions for nucleon gravitational form factors and their parton decompositions, with the hedge that the glue-to-quark ratio for each form factor is a constant 0.71(4) independent of momentum transfer, a prediction consistent with available lattice QCD results within mutual uncertainties.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled rainbow-ladder gap and Faddeev equations for the nucleon, using a dressed quark+quark scattering kernel whose single parameter (ω = 0.8 GeV, ωD = 0.8 GeV³) is fixed by gauge-sector dynamics. The graviton+quark vertex was obtained from the defining RL equation following Xu (2023), and the species decomposition of form factors was achieved using an all-orders evolution scheme that defines a hadron scale ζ_H = 0.331(2) GeV where glue and sea contributions vanish.

## Key numbers

* Nucleon D-term D(0) = -3.114(10) (total), with quark contribution D^q(0) = -1.820(43) and glue contribution D^g(0) = -1.294(33) at ζ = 2 GeV
* Glue-to-quark ratio for all three gravitational form factors: g/q = 0.71(4), independent of Q²
* Proton mass radius r_mass = 0.81(5) r_ch, mechanical radius r_mech = 0.72(2) r_ch, where r_ch = 0.887(3) fm (charge radius from same framework)
* Pion near-core pressure is roughly twice that in the proton; both are an order of magnitude greater than neutron star core pressures
* Light-quark D-term D^(u+d)(0; ζ_2) = -1.73(5), consistent with phenomenological inference D^(u+d)(0) = -1.63(29) from Burkert (2018)

## Context

This work extends the unified continuum Schwinger function method treatment previously applied to pion, kaon, and nucleon electromagnetic form factors (Yao 2024) and pion/kaon gravitational form factors (Xu 2023) to the nucleon gravitational sector. It provides the first single-framework calculation of all these form factors, enabling direct comparisons with lattice QCD results from Hackett (2023) and phenomenological extractions from deeply virtual Compton scattering data by Burkert (2018). The predictions serve as benchmarks for future experiments and lattice QCD analyses at physical pion mass.
