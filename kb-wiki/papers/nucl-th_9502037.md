# Tables of SU(3) Isoscalar Factors

> The Clebsch-Gordan coefficients of $SU(3)$ are useful in calculations involving baryons and mesons, as well as in calculations involving arbitrary numbers of quarks. For the latter case, one needs the coupling constants between states of nonintegral hypercharges. The existing published tables are insufficient for many such applications, and therefore we have compiled this collection. This report supplies the isoscalar factors required to reconstruct the Clebsch-Gordan coefficients for a large set of products of representations.

## Key claim
This paper provides a comprehensive set of tables of SU(3) isoscalar factors (Racah coefficients) for a large set of products of representations, specifically extending the available tables to cover cases needed for calculations involving arbitrary numbers of quarks where states have nonintegral hypercharges. The author notes that the tables were generated by computer using a simple-minded approach of explicitly constructing representations with integers for exact precision, and that any omissions should be attributed to limitations of computing power.

## Method
The author generated the Clebsch-Gordan coefficients by computer using routines that explicitly construct the representations with integer arithmetic for exact precision, then extracted the isoscalar factors from these coefficients. The Condon-Shortley phase convention was adopted for isomultiplets, and the de Swart phase convention was used for relative phases between isomultiplets in a given representation. The isoscalar factors are presented in tables where each entry is given as a signed square root, and the full Clebsch-Gordan coefficients can be reconstructed by multiplying the isoscalar factor by the appropriate SU(2) Clebsch-Gordan coefficient.

## Context
This work extends the available set of tables of SU(3) Clebsch-Gordan coefficients and isoscalar factors previously compiled by de Swart (1963), McNamee and Chilton (1964), Rabl et al. (1975), Hecht (1965), and Rashid (1962), as well as computer programs distributed by Akiyama and Draayer (1973) and Williams (1993, 1994). The paper enables calculations involving large or arbitrary numbers of quarks by providing the isoscalar factors needed to reconstruct Clebsch-Gordan coefficients for a large set of products of representations, particularly for states with nonintegral hypercharges where existing published tables were insufficient.
