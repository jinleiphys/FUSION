# Odd Parity Light Baryon Resonances

> We use a consistent SU(6) extension of the meson-baryon chiral Lagrangian within a coupled channel unitary approach in order to calculate the T-matrix for meson-baryon scattering in s-wave. The building blocks of the scheme are the pion and nucleon octets, the rho nonet and the Delta decuplet. We identify poles in this unitary T-matrix and interpret them as resonances. We study here the non exotic sectors with strangeness S=0,-1,-2,-3 and spin J=1/2, 3/2 and 5/2. Many of the poles generated can be associated with known N, Delta, Sigma, Lambda and Xi resonances with negative parity. We show that most of the low-lying three and four star odd parity baryon resonances with spin 1/2 and 3/2 can be related to multiplets of the spin-flavor symmetry group SU(6). This study allows us to predict the spin-parity of the Xi(1620), Xi(1690), Xi(1950), Xi(2250), Omega(2250) and Omega(2380) resonances, which have not been determined experimentally yet.

## Key claim

Most low-lying three- and four-star odd parity baryon resonances with spin 1/2 and 3/2 can be related to multiplets of the spin-flavor symmetry group SU(6), but this classification is qualitative because each resonance displays mixing of SU(6) and SU(3) configurations, and poles from the weakly bound 1134-plet might be subject to larger corrections or even disappear when higher-order terms are considered.

## Method

The authors used a consistent SU(6) extension of the meson-baryon chiral Lagrangian within a coupled channel unitary approach to calculate the s-wave T-matrix for meson-baryon scattering. The building blocks were the pion and nucleon octets, the rho nonet, and the Delta decuplet, with the interaction derived from a contact Lagrangian coupling the mesonic current (35-plet) to the baryonic current (56-plet) through an implicit 35-like exchange in the t-channel. Poles in the unitary T-matrix were identified as resonances, with the loop function renormalized by a subtraction constant fixed at the lowest threshold for each strangeness-isospin sector.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of meson-baryon scattering using chiral symmetry constraints, such as the works that found poles for N(1535) and Δ(1620) from pseudoscalar-baryon scattering (Inoue et al.) and the studies of vector meson interactions with baryons using the hidden gauge formalism (Bao et al., Angels et al.). The key advance is coupling the pseudoscalar-baryon and vector-baryon sectors that had been treated independently, using an SU(6) framework that combines spin and flavor symmetries. This enables predictions for the spin-parity of the Ξ(1620), Ξ(1690), Ξ(1950), Ξ(2250), Ω(2250), and Ω(2380) resonances, which had not been determined experimentally.
