# Sudden freeze-out vs continuous emission: duality in hydro-kinetic approach to A+A collisions

> The problem of spectra formation in hydrodynamic approach to A+A collisions is discussed. It is analyzed in terms of the two different objects: distribution and emission functions. We show that though the process of particle liberation, described by the emission function, is, usually, continuous in time, the observable spectra can be also expressed by means of the Landau/Cooper-Frye prescription. # Sudden freeze-out vs continuous emission: duality in hydro-kinetic approach to A+A collisions

## Key claim
The observable particle spectra in A+A collisions can be approximately described either by a sudden freeze-out prescription (Landau/Cooper-Frye) using thermal distribution functions or by a continuous emission function, despite the emission process being continuous in time. This duality holds approximately because of symmetry properties that systems reach at the end of hydrodynamic evolution and the reduction of collision rate at the post-hydrodynamic stage, but the emission function loses all proportionality to the distribution function in realistic cases without sudden freeze-out.

## Method
The authors analyze the Boltzmann equation for the distribution function f(x,p) by splitting it into interacting and escaping components, defining the escape probability P(x,p) via an opacity integral along the particle's world line. They derive two equivalent expressions for the single-particle spectra: one in terms of the emission function S = p^0 P F^{gain} integrated over space-time, and another in terms of the distribution function f integrated over a hypersurface plus the difference F^{gain} - F^{loss} integrated over the 4-volume. They then compare these formal expressions to the Landau/Cooper-Frye sudden freeze-out prescription and examine conditions under which the two descriptions yield approximately equivalent spectra.

## Key numbers
- The Landau freeze-out temperature is T_f.o. = m_pi (pion mass), approximately 0.12 to 0.15 GeV, which corresponds to the lower boundary of hydrodynamic applicability.
- Thermalization times tau_0 at RHIC energies are estimated between 0.6 and 3 fm/c from phenomenological and pQCD estimates.
- The approximate duality is argued to hold for low p_T spectra, while for high p_T above approximately 0.8 to 1 GeV, particles are radiated mainly from the periphery at earlier times, requiring a p-dependent freeze-out hypersurface.
- The mean free path of pions starts to increase exponentially as ~ exp(beta m_pi) with beta = 1/T(x) when temperature falls below m_pi, based on the analytic representation of thermal density n(T).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Sinyukov (1994) on the relationship between distribution and emission functions in expanding fireballs, and addresses the puzzle of why the Landau freeze-out criterion (Landau, 1953) at T ~ m_pi works across a wide range of collision systems from p+p(bar p) to heavy ion A+A. The authors contrast their approach with hybrid "hydro + cascade" models (Teaney, 2001) that fail to describe HBT radii, and with the perfect hydrodynamics picture advocated by Shuryak (2004) for the QGP stage. The paper enables development of a hydro-kinetic approach combining hydrodynamic evolution with escape probabilities and generalized relaxation time approximation to coherently explain hadronic observables across experiments.
