# Exploring sd-shell nuclei from two- and three-nucleon interactions with realistic saturation properties

> We study ground- and excited-state properties of all sd-shell nuclei with neutron and proton numbers 8 <= N,Z <= 20, based on a set of low-resolution two- and three-nucleon interactions that predict realistic saturation properties of nuclear matter. We focus on estimating the theoretical uncertainties due to variation of the resolution scale, the low-energy couplings, as well as from the many-body method. The experimental two-neutron and two-proton separation energies are reasonably well reproduced, with an uncertainty range of about 5 MeV. The first excited 2+ energies also show overall agreement, with a more narrow uncertainty range of about 500 keV. In most cases, this range is dominated by the uncertainties in the Hamiltonian.

## Key claim

Ground- and excited-state properties of all sd-shell nuclei (8 ≤ N,Z ≤ 20) can be reasonably reproduced using low-resolution two- and three-nucleon interactions that predict realistic nuclear matter saturation, with theoretical uncertainty estimates dominated by variations in the Hamiltonian rather than the many-body method, though the agreement is notably better for proton-neutron dominated systems than for neutron-rich systems.

## Method

The authors start from the N³LO 500 MeV Entem-Machleidt nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential, evolve it via similarity renormalization group (SRG) to resolution scales λ_NN = 1.8, 2.0, 2.2 fm⁻¹, and combine each with leading N²LO three-nucleon (3N) forces. They construct microscopic valence-space Hamiltonians using many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) at second and third order for the sd-shell valence space on top of a ¹⁶O core, then perform exact valence shell-model diagonalizations to obtain ground-state energies, two-neutron and two-proton separation energies, and first excited 2⁺₁ energies.

## Key numbers

- Two-neutron separation energies (S₂n) for sd-shell isotopic chains show an uncertainty range of approximately 5 MeV
- Two-proton separation energies (S₂p) show an uncertainty range of approximately 5 MeV
- First excited 2⁺₁ energies show an uncertainty range of approximately 500 keV, except near shell closures where uncertainty can reach approximately 1 MeV
- For N<Z isotopes, total uncertainty in S₂n and S₂p can reach approximately 10 MeV due to comparable Hamiltonian and MBPT uncertainties
- Resolution-scale dependence yields approximately 1.0 MeV per valence particle in ³²Mg and ³⁷Cl; including PWA c_i values doubles this to approximately 2.0 MeV per valence particle
- Total uncertainty including MBPT contributions reaches 2.1 MeV per valence particle in ³²Mg and 2.8 MeV per valence particle in ³⁷Cl

## Context

This work extends prior ab initio studies of oxygen and calcium isotopes (Holt 2013, Cipollone 2013, Hergert 2014) to the full sd shell, providing the first comprehensive uncertainty quantification across all nuclei from oxygen to calcium. The results enable systematic comparisons with phenomenological interactions like USDA/B (Brown 2006) and establish a baseline for future studies incorporating consistently SRG-evolved NN and 3N interactions, different regulator forms, and nonperturbative valence-space methods (Tsukiyama 2012, Bogner 2014, Jansen 2014, Dikmen 2015).
