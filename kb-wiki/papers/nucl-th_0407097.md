# Analysis of the JINR p(660 MeV) + 129I, 237Np, and 241Am Measurements with Eleven Different Models

> We have analyzed the recent JINR measurements on nuclide production cross sections from interaction of 660 MeV proton beams with radioactive targets of enriched 129I (85% 129I and 15% 127I), 237Np, and 241Am with eleven different models, realized in eight transport codes and event-generators: LAHET (Bertini, ISABEL, INCL+ABLA, and INCL+RAL options), CASCADE, CEM95, CEM2k, LAQGSM+GEM2, CEM2k+GEM2, LAQGSM+GEMINI, and CEM2k+GEMINI. We found out that all these models have problems in a correct description of many of these cross sections, though some of these models describe very well most of the recent measurements done at GSI using inverse kinematics, as well as many other reactions. None of the tested here models is able to reproduce well all the JINR data and all of them should be further improved. Development of a better universal evaporation/fission model should be of a highest priority. We conclude that it is impossible to make a correct choice between fission and fragmentation reaction mechanisms analyzing only measurements on product cross sections; addressing this question would require analysis of two- or multi-particle correlation measurements.

## Key claim

None of the eleven tested models is able to reproduce well all the JINR data for p(660 MeV) + 129I, 237Np, and 241Am, even though some of these models describe very well most of the recent GSI inverse-kinematics measurements; the authors conclude that it is impossible to make a correct choice between fission and fragmentation reaction mechanisms by analyzing only product cross sections.

## Method

The authors analyzed 207 measured residual-product cross sections (74 from 129I, 53 from 237Np, and 80 from 241Am) from 660 MeV proton irradiations at the JINR Phasotron, using eleven different models implemented in eight transport codes and event-generators: LAHET (with Bertini, ISABEL, INCL+ABLA, and INCL+RAL options), CASCADE, CEM95, CEM2k, LAQGSM+GEM2, CEM2k+GEM2, LAQGSM+GEMINI, and CEM2k+GEMINI. The agreement was quantified by the mean simulated-to-experimental ratio ⟨F⟩ and its standard deviation, calculated for selected isotopes that satisfy rules based on physical principles realized in the models.

## Key numbers

- For the 129I target, 42 of 74 measured cross sections were selected for quantitative comparison; the best overall mean deviation factor ⟨F⟩ was 2.74 (CEM2k+GEMINI) for all 42 selected isotopes, and 1.31 (LAQGSM+GEMINI) for the 22 spallation products with A ≥ 95.
- For the 237Np target, 37 of 53 measured isotopes were selected; the best ⟨F⟩ for all selected isotopes was 3.18 (Bertini+Dresner/RAL and ISABEL+Dresner/RAL), and for the 32 fission products with A < 175 the best ⟨F⟩ was 3.29 (Bertini+Dresner/RAL).
- For the 241Am target, 45 of 80 measured isotopes were selected; the best ⟨F⟩ for all selected isotopes was 2.26 (Bertini+Dresner/RAL), and for the 44 fission products with A < 175 the best ⟨F⟩ was 2.28 (Bertini+Dresner/RAL).
- For the 129I target, the codes CEM2k+GEMINI and LAQGSM+GEMINI agreed better than all other models in the A = 40-80 mass region because only GEMINI considers fission of nuclei as light as iodine.
- For the 237Np and 241Am targets, deviations between some calculated and measured cross sections reached two orders of magnitude or higher.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of JINR measurements on 129I, 237Np, and 241Am targets (Adam et al., 2004; Balabekyan et al., 2004) by testing eleven models relevant for Accelerator Driven System (ADS) calculations and nuclear waste transmutation. The results contrast with the good performance of these same models on GSI inverse-kinematics measurements (Schmidt, 2004) and highlight the need for a better universal evaporation/fission model. The finding that fission and fragmentation cannot be distinguished from product cross sections alone motivates future two- or multi-particle correlation measurements.
