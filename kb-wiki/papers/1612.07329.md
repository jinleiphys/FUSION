# Quasiparticle second-order viscous hydrodynamics from kinetic theory

> We present the derivation of second-order relativistic viscous hydrodynamics from an effective Boltzmann equation for a system consisting of quasiparticles of a single species. We consider temperature-dependent masses of the quasiparticles and devise a thermodynamically-consistent framework to formulate second-order evolution equations for shear and bulk viscous pressure corrections. The main advantage of this formulation is that one can consistently implement realistic equation of state of the medium within the framework of kinetic theory. Specializing to the case of one-dimensional purely-longitudinal boost-invariant expansion, we study the effect of this new formulation on viscous hydrodynamic evolution of strongly-interacting matter formed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors derive second-order relativistic viscous hydrodynamics from an effective Boltzmann equation for a single-species quasiparticle system with temperature-dependent masses, enabling consistent implementation of realistic lattice QCD equations of state within kinetic theory; however, the derivation relies on a specific ansatz for the non-equilibrium mean field and the relaxation-time approximation for the collision kernel.

## Method

The authors start from an effective Boltzmann equation with temperature-dependent quasiparticle masses and a relaxation-time collision kernel, introducing a non-equilibrium mean field contribution to the energy-momentum tensor to maintain thermodynamic consistency and energy-momentum conservation. Using a Chapman-Enskog-like iterative solution, they derive first-order Navier-Stokes relations for shear and bulk viscosities, then obtain second-order relaxation-type evolution equations for the bulk viscous pressure and shear-stress tensor. The formalism is specialized to one-dimensional boost-invariant expansion to study viscous hydrodynamic evolution of strongly-interacting matter in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- Degeneracy factor fixed to \( g = \frac{\pi^4}{180}\left(4 (N_c^2-1)+7 N_c N_f\right) \) with \( N_c = 3 \) and \( N_f = 3 \)
- First-order shear viscosity: \( \eta = \beta_\pi \tau_R \) where \( \beta_\pi = \beta I_{3,2} \)
- First-order bulk viscosity: \( \zeta = \beta_\Pi \tau_R \) where \( \beta_\Pi = \frac{5}{3} \beta I_{3,2} - c_s^2(\mathcal{E}_0 + \mathcal{P}_0) + \kappa c_s^2 m^2 \beta I_{1,1} \)
- At first order in gradients: \( b_0 = 0 \) and \( b^\mu = 0 \)
- At second order: \( b_0 = -3 \tau_R \kappa c_s^2 \Pi \theta \) and \( b^\mu = 3 \tau_R \kappa \Pi \dot{u}^\mu \)

## Context

This work extends prior derivations of second-order viscous hydrodynamics from kinetic theory (Denicol 2012, Jaiswal 2014) to the case of quasiparticles with temperature-dependent masses, following the thermodynamic consistency framework of Gorenstein (1995) and Romatschke (2011). The formalism enables the use of realistic lattice QCD equations of state (Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration, Borsanyi 2010) within a kinetic-theory-based hydrodynamic framework, addressing inconsistencies that arise when lattice QCD results are naively inserted into hydrodynamics derived for constant-mass particles. This enables more consistent extraction of transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma from heavy-ion collision data.
