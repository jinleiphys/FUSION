# Scattering length and effective range of microscopic two-body potentials

> Scattering processes are a fundamental way of experimentally probing distributions and properties of systems in several areas of physics. Considering two-body scattering at low energies, when the de Broglie wavelength is larger than the range of the potential, partial waves with high angular momentum are typically unimportant. The dominant contribution comes from $l=0$ partial waves, commonly known as $s$-wave scattering. This situation is very relevant in atomic physics, e.g. cold atomic gases, and nuclear physics, e.g. nuclear structure and matter. This manuscript is intended as a pedagogical introduction to the topic while covering a numerical approach to compute the desired quantities. We introduce low-energy scattering with particular attention to the concepts of scattering length and effective range. These two quantities appear in the effective-range approximation, which universally describes low-energy processes. We outline a numerical procedure for calculating the scattering length and effective range of spherically symmetric two-body potentials. As examples, we apply the method to the spherical well, modified Pöschl-Teller, Gaussian, and Lennard-Jones potentials. We hope to provide the tools so students can implement similar calculations and extend them to other potentials.

## Key claim

This paper presents a pedagogical numerical method for computing the scattering length and effective range of spherically symmetric two-body potentials, demonstrating that any potential with at least two parameters can be tuned to reproduce desired low-energy scattering properties, but the method is limited to potentials where the functional form is specified and the numerical integration is stable.

## Method

The authors solve the radial Schrödinger equation for s-wave scattering using Numerov's method, matching the interior numerical solution to the exterior free-particle solution at the potential range R via logarithmic derivatives. The scattering length is extracted from the zero-energy wave function's asymptotic form u_0(r) = 1 - r/a, while the effective range is obtained from the low-energy expansion of k cot δ_0(k) = -1/a + (1/2) r_0 k^2 + O(k^4). The procedure is applied to four example potentials: spherical well, modified Pöschl-Teller, Gaussian, and Lennard-Jones.

## Key numbers

- For the spherical well potential with depth V_0 and range R, the scattering length diverges when the dimensionless parameter α = sqrt(2m|V_0|)R/ℏ equals π/2, 3π/2, 5π/2, etc., corresponding to the appearance of new bound states.
- The modified Pöschl-Teller potential V(r) = -V_0 / cosh^2(r/R) has an analytical scattering length a = R [ψ(1/2 + √(1 + 2m V_0 R^2/ℏ^2)) - ψ(1/2)] where ψ is the digamma function, with numerical examples shown for various V_0 values.
- For the Gaussian potential V(r) = -V_0 exp(-r^2/R^2), the scattering length varies from negative to positive values as V_0 increases, crossing zero at specific strengths (e.g., a ≈ 0 when 2m V_0 R^2/ℏ^2 ≈ 2.7).
- The Lennard-Jones potential V(r) = 4ε[(σ/r)^12 - (σ/r)^6] yields scattering lengths that depend on the reduced mass and the parameters ε and σ, with numerical results shown for several combinations.

## Context

This work extends pedagogical treatments of low-energy scattering such as those in standard quantum mechanics textbooks (Griffiths 2018, Sakurai 2014) and specialized references on scattering length and effective range (Bethe 1949, Madsen 2002). It provides a numerical toolkit that enables students to compute scattering parameters for arbitrary spherically symmetric potentials, complementing analytical results for specific potentials like the hard-sphere, soft-sphere, and spherical well studied by Pera (2023). The method enables systematic construction of microscopic two-body potentials with desired scattering properties for applications in cold atomic gases and nuclear physics, where Feshbach resonances allow tuning of interactions as demonstrated by Moerdijk (1995) and Randeria (2014).
