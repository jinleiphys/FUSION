# Event-by-event vortex rings in fixed-target p+Ar collisions

> We present event-by-event simulations for central asymmetric p+Ar collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 68$ GeV to investigate the formation and evolution of vortex-ring structures from the early-stage longitudinal flow velocity profile. Our predictions for their imprints on Lambda hyperon's polarization observables are complementary to those presented in Ref. [Phys.Rev.C 110 (2024) 5, 054908] and can be explored in the future fixed-target collisions at the Large Hadron Collider beauty (LHCb) experiment.

## Key claim
Event-by-event simulations predict that vortex-ring structures in fixed-target p+Ar collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 68\) GeV produce a measurable signal in the \(\mathcal{R}_{\Lambda}^{\hat{z}}\) polarization observable for Lambda hyperons, but the magnitude depends strongly on the assumed fraction \(f\) of initial longitudinal flow velocity, the signal is a factor of six larger when full initial longitudinal flow (\(f=1\)) is used compared to the Bjorken-flow limit (\(f=0\)).

## Method
The authors used a (3+1)D hydrodynamic model (iEBE-MUSIC framework) with a geometric-based 3D initial condition that maps local energy and longitudinal momentum from colliding nucleons to hydrodynamic fields, conserving orbital angular momentum. The initial longitudinal flow profile was parameterized by a fraction \(f\) of the center-of-mass rapidity derived from nuclear thickness functions. The spin vector for Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons was computed from the thermal vorticity tensor over the freeze-out hypersurface, and the ring observable \(\mathcal{R}_{\Lambda}^{\hat{z}}\) was evaluated as a function of charged hadron multiplicity, pseudo-rapidity, and transverse momentum.

## Key numbers
- The ring observable \(\mathcal{R}_{\Lambda}^{\hat{z}}\) is a factor of six larger with strong initial longitudinal flow (\(f=1\)) compared to Bjorken flow (\(f=0\)) in p+Ar collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 68\) GeV.
- For \(p_T > 1\) GeV, \(\mathcal{R}_{\Lambda}^{\hat{z}}(p_T)\) near mid-rapidity shows a linear \(p_T\) dependence whose slope is proportional to the thermal vorticity tensor.
- The magnitude of \(\mathcal{R}_{\Lambda}^{\hat{z}}\) increases with charged hadron multiplicity, indicating that the vortex-ring flow structure develops with the fireball lifetime.

## Context
This work extends the study of vortex-ring structures from asymmetric Cu+Au collisions (Voloshin, 2017) and p+Au/Pb collisions (Lisa, 2021) to the p+Ar system at fixed-target LHCb energies. It provides complementary predictions to those in Phys.Rev.C 110 (2024) 5, 054908, using the same (3+1)D hydrodynamic framework (Shen, 2017; Shen, 2020; Ryu, 2021; Alzhrani, 2022). The results enable experimental searches for vortex-ring signatures in future LHCb SMOG fixed-target runs and can help constrain the early-time longitudinal dynamics and vorticity transport in the quark-gluon plasma.
