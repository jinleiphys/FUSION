# Constraint to chiral invariant masses of nucleons from GW170817 in an extended parity doublet model

> We construct nuclear matter based on an extended parity doublet model including four light nucleons $N(939)$, $N(1440)$, $N(1535)$, and $N(1650)$. We exclude some values of the chiral invariant masses by requiring the saturation properties of normal nuclear matter; saturation density, binding energy, incompressibility, and symmetry energy. We find further constraint to the chiral invariant masses from the tidal deformability determined by the observation of the gravitational waves from neutron star merger GW170817. Our result shows that the chiral invariant masses are larger than about $600\,$MeV. We also give some predictions on the symmetry energy and the slope parameters in the high density region, which will be measured in future experiments.

## Key claim

The authors show that the chiral invariant masses of nucleons must be larger than about 600 MeV when combining constraints from normal nuclear matter saturation properties and the tidal deformability measured from the neutron star merger GW170817, whereas earlier vacuum phenomenology alone allowed values below 500 MeV.

## Method

The authors construct nuclear matter using an extended parity doublet model that includes four nucleon states (N(939), N(1440), N(1535), N(1650)) with two chiral representations and two independent chiral invariant masses. They determine model parameters by fitting vacuum properties of these nucleons, then fix additional parameters (g_omega, g_rho, lambda_4, lambda_6) to reproduce saturation properties of normal nuclear matter. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations to compute neutron star mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities, and compare the binary tidal deformability to the GW170817 constraint.

## Key numbers

- Chiral invariant masses must be larger than about 600 MeV to satisfy the GW170817 tidal deformability constraint
- The upper bound on binary dimensionless tidal deformability used is Lambda_tilde = 800 from the GW170817 observation
- Saturation properties used as inputs: saturation density rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}, binding energy E_bind = -16 MeV, incompressibility K = 240 MeV, symmetry energy E_sym = 31 MeV
- The chirp mass used for the binary system is M_chirp = 1.118 solar masses
- The mass ratio constraint used is 0.7 < M_2/M_1 < 1 from GW170817
- For Group 2, the combination (m_0^(1), m_0^(2)) = (600, 695) MeV is excluded, while (650, 705), (705, 790), and (795, 860) MeV satisfy the constraint
- For Group 3, combinations (800, 500) and (800, 550) MeV are excluded
- For Group 4, combination (550, 800) MeV is excluded
- The omega meson mass is taken as m_omega = 783 MeV and rho meson mass as m_rho = 775 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous parity doublet model (Yamazaki and Harada, 2018) that included four nucleons with two chiral representations. It builds on earlier studies by Motohiro and Harada (2015) who showed saturation properties could be reproduced for chiral invariant masses 500-900 MeV using six-point scalar interactions, and by Marczenko and collaborators (2018) who obtained a constraint of 780-810 MeV from neutron star properties including GW170817. The result that chiral invariant masses exceed about 600 MeV is consistent with lattice analyses by Aarts and collaborators (2017, 2018) and with finite nuclei studies by Shin and collaborators (2018) favoring m_0 ~ 700 MeV. The predictions for symmetry energy and slope parameter at high density are intended for future experimental measurements.
