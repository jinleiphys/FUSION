# Linear response calculation using the canonical-basis TDHFB with a schematic pairing functional

> A canonical-basis formulation of the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) theory is obtained with an approximation that the pair potential is assumed to be diagonal in the time-dependent canonical basis. The canonical-basis formulation significantly reduces the computational cost. We apply the method to linear-response calculations for even-even nuclei. E1 strength distributions for proton-rich Mg isotopes are systematically calculated. The calculation suggests strong Landau damping of giant dipole resonance for drip-line nuclei.

## Key claim

The canonical-basis TDHFB (Cb-TDHFB) method, which approximates the pair potential as diagonal in the time-dependent canonical basis, significantly reduces computational cost compared to full TDHFB. The calculation suggests strong Landau damping of the giant dipole resonance for drip-line nuclei, but this conclusion is based on a schematic pairing functional that violates gauge invariance unless a specific gauge condition is imposed.

## Method

The authors derive Cb-TDHFB equations from the generalized density-matrix formalism of TDHFB, introducing the approximation that the pair potential has only diagonal elements in the time-dependent canonical basis (Eq. 13). They use a Skyrme functional with the SkM* parameter set for particle-hole channels and a simple pairing functional \(E_g(t)=-\sum_{k,l>0} G_{kl} \kappa_k^*(t) \kappa_l(t)\) with \(G=0.6\) MeV. The ground state is constructed by HF+BCS, then the Cb-TDHFB equations are solved in real time under a weak isovector dipole impulse, and the E1 strength distribution is obtained via spectral analysis with exponential smoothing at \(\Gamma=1\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- \(^{18}\)Mg is bound with a small binding energy of 200 keV, treated as a "fictitious" proton halo nucleus.
- For \(^{22,24}\)Mg, the \(K=0\) GDR peak is located around 15 MeV and the \(K=1\) peak near 22 MeV.
- Proton separation energies for \(^{18,20}\)Mg are less than 3 MeV.
- The pairing strength parameter is \(G=0.6\) MeV.
- The smoothing parameter used in spectral analysis is \(\Gamma=1\) MeV.
- For \(^{28}\)Mg, the neutron separation energy is about 9 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior development of the Cb-TDHFB method (Ebata 2010) from neutron-rich to proton-rich Mg isotopes. It contrasts with full TDHFB calculations (Hashimoto and Nakatsukasa 2007, Avez et al. 2008) which are computationally limited, and with earlier TDHF studies (Nakatsukasa and Yabana 2005) that neglect pairing correlations. The results enable systematic linear-response calculations for open-shell nuclei near drip lines, and the observed strong Landau damping near drip lines motivates further study of level densities of 1p1h states.
