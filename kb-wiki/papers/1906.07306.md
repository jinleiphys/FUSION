# Frame dependence of transition form factors in light-front dynamics

> We calculate the transition form factor between vector and pseudoscalar quarkonia in both the timelike and the spacelike region using light-front dynamics. We investigate the frame dependence of the form factors for heavy quarkonia with light-front wavefunctions calculated from the valence Fock sector. This dependence could serve as a measure for the Lorentz symmetry violation arising from the Fock-space truncation. We suggest using frames with minimal longitudinal momentum transfer for calculations in the valence Fock sector, namely the Drell-Yan frame for the space-like region and a specific longitudinal frame for the timelike region; at $q^2=0$ these two frames give the same result. We also use the transition form factor in the timelike region to investigate the electromagnetic Dalitz decay $ψ_A\to ψ_B l^+l^-$ ($l = e,μ$) and predict the effective mass spectrum of the lepton pair.

## Key claim

The frame dependence of transition form factors in light-front dynamics, arising from Fock-space truncation, can be minimized by using frames with minimal longitudinal momentum transfer, specifically the Drell-Yan frame for the spacelike region and the longitudinal-II frame for the timelike region, though the spread of results across different frames serves as a measure of Lorentz symmetry violation from the truncation.

## Method

The authors calculated the M1 transition form factor between vector and pseudoscalar heavy quarkonia using light-front wavefunctions from the basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) approach, with an effective Hamiltonian based on light-front holographic QCD and one-gluon exchange. They sampled various reference frames by apportioning the transferred momentum \(q^2\) between longitudinal and transverse components, parameterized by the boost invariants \(z\) and \(\vec\Delta_\perp\), and evaluated the form factor using the transverse current \(J^R\) with the \(m_j=0\) state of the vector meson. The transition form factor was then used to compute the effective mass spectrum of the lepton pair in electromagnetic Dalitz decays \(\psi_A\to\psi_B l^+l^-\).

## Key numbers

- The maximal value of \(q^2\) in the timelike region for the transition is \(q_{\text{max}}^2 = (m_A - m_B)^2\), occurring at \(z_{\text{turn}} = 1 - m_B/m_A\) and \(\vec\Delta_\perp = 0\).
- The Drell-Yan frame corresponds to \(z = 0\) and \(q^2 = -\vec\Delta_\perp^2\), restricted to the spacelike region (\(q^2 \le 0\)).
- The longitudinal-II frame exists only in the timelike region with \(0 \le z \le z_{\text{turn}}\), joining the Drell-Yan frame at \(q^2 = 0\) with \(z = 0\) and \(\vec\Delta_\perp = 0\).
- The longitudinal-I frame covers both regions: \(z_{\text{turn}} \le z < z_{\text{node}}\) for timelike and \(z_{\text{node}} \le z < 1\) for spacelike, where \(z_{\text{node}} \equiv 1 - m_B^2/m_A^2\).
- For the transition \(J/\psi \to \eta_c + \gamma^{(*)}\) at \(q^2 = -3\ \text{GeV}^2\), the largest \(z\) in the longitudinal frame is \(z = 0.45\) when \(\Delta_\perp = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study on current-component dependence of M1 transition form factors (Li, 2018) to investigate frame dependence, and builds on studies of elastic form factor frame dependence for pseudoscalar mesons (Li, 2017). The finding that frames with minimal longitudinal momentum transfer suppress non-valence contributions agrees with the study by Bakker and collaborators on semileptonic decays, where the transition form factor in the timelike region from a specific frame (\(\vec q_\perp = 0\) with negative recoil) was closest to the full solution including non-valence contributions. The results enable more reliable predictions for electromagnetic Dalitz decays of heavy quarkonia using valence-sector light-front wavefunctions.
