# Baryon-Strangeness Correlations in Au+Au Collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}$=7.7-200 GeV from the UrQMD model

> Fluctuations and correlations of conserved charges are sensitive observables for studying the QCD phase transition and critical point in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. We have studied the centrality and energy dependence of mixed-cumulants (up to fourth order) between net-baryon and net-strangeness in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$= 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, 200 GeV from UrQMD model. To compare with other theoretical calculations, we normalize these mixed-cumulants by various order cumulants of net-strangeness distributions. We found that the results obtained from UrQMD calculations are comparable with the results from Lattice QCD at low temperature and hadron resonance gas model. The ratios of mixed-cumulants ($R_{11}^{BS},R_{13}^{BS},R_{22}^{BS}$,~$R_{31}^{BS}$) from UrQMD calculations show weak centrality dependence. However, the mixed-cumulant ratios $R_{11}^{BS}$ and $R_{31}^{BS}$ show strong increase at low energy, while the $R_{13}^{BS}$ snd $R_{22}^{BS}$ are similar at different energies. Furthermore, we have also studied the correlations between different hadron species and their contributions to the net-baryon and net-strangeness correlations. These models studies can provide baselines for searching for the signals of QCD phase transition and critical point in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The UrQMD model, which does not include a quark-hadron phase transition or QCD critical point, produces baryon-strangeness correlation ratios that are comparable to Lattice QCD results at low temperature and to the hadron resonance gas model, providing a non-critical baseline for experimental searches; however, the ratios \(R_{11}^{BS}\) and \(R_{31}^{BS}\) show a strong increase at low collision energies, while \(R_{13}^{BS}\) and \(R_{22}^{BS}\) remain similar across energies.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD model (version 2.3) to simulate Au+Au collisions at seven beam energies from \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\) to 200 GeV, with event statistics ranging from 35 to 133 million per energy. They computed mixed-cumulants up to fourth order between net-baryon and net-strangeness distributions, then normalized them by various order cumulants of net-strangeness to form the ratios \(R_{11}^{BS}, R_{13}^{BS}, R_{22}^{BS}, R_{31}^{BS}\). The analysis was performed within pseudo-rapidity \(|\eta| < 0.5\), with centrality defined by charged particles in \(0.5 < |\eta| < 1\) to avoid auto-correlations, and included ten different combinations of hadron species to isolate contributions from strange baryons, non-strange baryons, and strange mesons.

## Key numbers

*   Event statistics for Au+Au collisions: 35 million at 7.7 GeV, 105 million at 11.5 GeV, 106 million at 19.6 GeV, 81 million at 27 GeV, 133 million at 39 GeV, 38 million at 62.4 GeV, and 56 million at 200 GeV.
*   The mixed-cumulant ratios \(R_{11}^{BS}, R_{13}^{BS}, R_{22}^{BS}, R_{31}^{BS}\) from UrQMD show weak centrality dependence across all energies.
*   At low energy, the ratio \(R_{31}^{BS}\) decreases for the case including all particles (Net-B vs. Net-S) and for the Net-proton vs. Net-kaon case.
*   When strange baryons are excluded from the B-S correlations, the mixed-cumulant ratios are much smaller than the case with all particles and have values close to zero at high energies.
*   When non-strange baryons are excluded from the B-S correlations, the ratios \(R_{11}^{BS}, R_{13}^{BS}, R_{22}^{BS}, R_{31}^{BS}\) are finite and monotonically increase with decreasing collision energy.

## Context

This work extends the study of conserved-charge fluctuations and correlations, which have been proposed as sensitive observables for the QCD phase transition and critical point in heavy-ion collisions, as discussed in reviews by Misha, Volker, Asakawa, and others. The results are compared to Lattice QCD calculations (e.g., from the 2013 work by the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration) and the hadron resonance gas model, showing agreement at low temperature. The study provides a baseline from a model without a phase transition or critical point, enabling experimental searches by the STAR collaboration at RHIC to distinguish signals of the QCD phase transition from hadronic background effects such as baryon stopping and associated strangeness production.
