# Critical behavior of charmonia across the phase transition: A QCD sum rule approach

> We investigate medium-induced change of mass and width of J/psi and eta_c across the phase transition in hot gluonic matter using QCD sum rules. In the QCD sum rule approach, the medium effect on heavy quarkonia is induced by the change of both scalar and twist-2 gluon condensates, whose temperature dependences are extracted from the lattice calculations of energy density and pressure. Although the stability of the operator product expansion side seems to break down at T > 1.06Tc for the vector channel and T>1.04Tc for the pseudoscalar channel, we find a sudden change of the spectral property across the critical temperature Tc, which originates from an equally rapid change of the scalar gluon condensate characterized by e-3p. By parameterizing the ground state of the spectral density by the Breit-Wigner form, we find that for both J/psi and eta_c, the masses suddenly decrease maximally by a few hundreds of MeV and the widths broaden to ~100 MeV slightly above Tc. Implications for recent and future heavy ion experiments are discussed. We also carry out a similar analysis for charmonia in nuclear matter, which could serve as a testing ground for observing the precursor phenomena of the QCD phase transition. We finally discuss the possibility of observing the mass shift at nuclear matter at the FAIR project at GSI.

## Key claim

The paper finds a sudden change in the spectral properties of J/ψ and ηc across the critical temperature Tc, originating from the rapid change of the scalar gluon condensate characterized by ε-3p, but the stability of the operator product expansion side breaks down at T > 1.06Tc for the vector channel and T > 1.04Tc for the pseudoscalar channel, limiting the analysis to temperatures just above Tc.

## Method

The authors use QCD sum rules with moment ratios to study charmonia in hot gluonic matter, extracting the temperature dependence of the scalar and twist-2 gluon condensates from lattice QCD calculations of energy density and pressure for pure SU(3) gauge theory. The spectral function is parametrized by a relativistic Breit-Wigner form, and the sum rule is solved to find pairs of mass and width that satisfy the moment ratio equation. The analysis is performed for two different temperature-dependent coupling constants extracted from lattice data: αqq(r_screen,T) and a rescaled perturbative running coupling α̃(T).

## Key numbers

- The scalar gluon condensate G0 decreases to less than half of its vacuum value (G0^vac = (0.35 GeV)^4 ≈ 0.015 GeV^4) at T/Tc ≈ 1.04
- For J/ψ, the moment ratio remains stable up to T = 1.06Tc for ξ = 3 with αqq coupling, and up to T = 1.06Tc for ξ = 3 with α̃ coupling
- For ηc, the moment ratio remains stable only up to T = 1.04Tc for ξ = 3 with α̃ coupling, and up to T = 1.03Tc for ξ = 3 with αqq coupling
- The masses decrease maximally by a few hundreds of MeV and the widths broaden to approximately 100 MeV slightly above Tc
- The charm quark mass is set to mc = 1.24 GeV at ξ = 1, and αs = 0.21
- The critical temperature is taken as Tc = 264 MeV from lattice data (Boyd et al., 1996)

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach for charmonia in medium previously developed by Furnstahl, Hatsuda, and Lee (1990) for hot hadronic matter, and by Klingl et al. (1999) and Hayashigaki (1999) for nuclear matter. The analysis uses exact temperature dependencies of gluon condensates from lattice QCD calculations, in contrast to earlier perturbative treatments. The results are relevant for interpreting J/ψ suppression in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and for planning future experiments at FAIR at GSI, where charmonia in nuclear matter could serve as a precursor signal of the QCD phase transition.
