# Neutron/proton ratio of nucleon emissions as a probe of neutron skin

> The dependence between neutron-to-proton yield ratio ($R_{np}$) and neutron skin thickness ($δ_{np}$) in neutron-rich projectile induced reactions is investigated within the framework of the Isospin-Dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model. The density distribution of the Droplet model is embedded in the initialization of the neutron and proton densities in the present IQMD model. By adjusting the diffuseness parameter of neutron density in the Droplet model for the projectile, the relationship between the neutron skin thickness and the corresponding $R_{np}$ in the collisions is obtained. The results show strong linear correlation between $R_{np}$ and $δ_{np}$ for neutron-rich Ca and Ni isotopes. It is suggested that $R_{np}$ may be used as an experimental observable to extract $δ_{np}$ for neutron-rich nuclei, which is very significant to the study of the nuclear structure of exotic nuclei and the equation of state (EOS) of asymmetric nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The neutron-to-proton yield ratio ($R_{np}$) from peripheral collisions of neutron-rich projectiles on a light target shows a strong linear correlation with the projectile's neutron skin thickness ($\delta_{np}$) in IQMD simulations, suggesting $R_{np}$ can serve as an experimental observable to extract $\delta_{np}$, but the method requires well-defined experimental determination of collision centrality and the estimated uncertainty of $\delta_{np}$ is around 0.1 fm when the uncertainty of $R_{np}$ is 5%.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin-Dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate collisions of neutron-rich Ca and Ni isotopes with a $^{12}$C target at 50A MeV. The density distributions of neutrons and protons in the projectile were initialized using the Droplet model, with a variable factor $f_n$ (ranging from 1.0 to 1.6) applied to the neutron diffuseness parameter to generate different neutron skin thicknesses. Emitted neutrons and protons were collected between 150 fm/c and 200 fm/c using a coalescence model, and the yield ratio $R_{np}$ was calculated with a rapidity cut ($Y>0$) and a reduced impact parameter gate ($0.8 < b/b_{\mathrm{max}} < 1.0$) to isolate projectile contributions.

## Key numbers

- For $^{50}$Ca+$^{12}$C and $^{68}$Ni+$^{12}$C at 50A MeV, the mean slope of the linear fit ($R_{np}=a+b\cdot\delta_{np}$) is 1.06 for Ca isotopes and 0.83 for Ni isotopes.
- The estimated error of $\delta_{np}$ may be less than 0.08 fm if the uncertainty of $R_{np}$ is less than 5%.
- For $0.6<b/b_{\mathrm{max}}<1.0$, the uncertainty of $\delta_{np}$ is estimated to be around 0.1 fm when the uncertainty of $R_{np}$ is 5%.
- The IQMD model uses a soft equation of state with incompressibility $K=200$ MeV, symmetry energy strength $C_{\mathrm{sym}}=32$ MeV, and parameters $\alpha=-356$ MeV, $\beta=303$ MeV, $\gamma=1.17$.
- The coalescence model uses $P_0=300$ MeV/c and $R_0=3.5$ fm.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of neutron skin thickness using proton scattering, interaction cross sections (Suzuki, 1995), parity-violating measurements (Horowitz, 1998; Danielewicz, 2003), and neutron abrasion cross sections (Ma, 2007), as well as studies of neutron-proton emission ratios as probes of the asymmetry term of the nuclear equation of state (MSU experiments; Li, 2005; Toro, 2006). The proposed method enables extraction of $\delta_{np}$ from $R_{np}$ measurements in heavy-ion collisions, which could help distinguish between different theoretical predictions (e.g., Skyrme Hartree-Fock, relativistic mean-field, BUU model) for the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter, particularly for nuclei with neutron excess $I>0.11$ where predictions vary from 0.01 fm to 0.11 fm (Warda, 2009).
