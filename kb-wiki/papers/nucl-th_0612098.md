# Predictions for two-pion correlations for sqrt{s}=14 TeV proton-proton collisions

> A simple model based on relativistic geometry and final-state hadronic rescattering is used to predict pion source parameters extracted in two-pion correlation studies of proton-proton collisions at sqrt{s}=14 TeV. By comparing the results of these model studies with data, it might be possible to obtain information on the hadronization time in these collisions. As a test of this model, comparisons between existing two-pion correlation data at sqrt{s}= 1.8 TeV and results from the model are made. It is found at this lower energy that using a short hadronization time in the model best describes the trends of the data.

## Key claim

A simple model based on relativistic geometry and final-state hadronic rescattering predicts that two-pion correlation parameters at sqrt(s)=14 TeV will show strong variations with transverse momentum and multiplicity if the hadronization proper time is short (tau ~ 0.1 fm/c), but the model's validity depends on future LHC data confirming the qualitative trends seen in comparisons with Tevatron data at sqrt(s)=1.8 TeV.

## Method

The author used PYTHIA 6.326 to generate minimum-bias proton-proton and proton-antiproton events, then assigned space-time coordinates to each hadron assuming a uniform proper-time hadronization from a thin disk of radius 1 fm. A semi-classical Monte Carlo rescattering calculation with isospin-averaged cross sections evolved the system in 0.1 fm/c time steps to 20 fm/c, after which Bose-Einstein correlations were imposed via pairwise symmetrization of pions in a plane-wave approximation. Gaussian fits (Eq. 5) were applied to correlation functions for comparison with Tevatron data, while a more general function (Eq. 6) with parameters R, lambda, alpha, and delta was used for LHC predictions.

## Key numbers

- For sqrt(s)=1.8 TeV proton-antiproton collisions, the model with tau=0.1 fm/c and rescattering best reproduces the E735 data trends, showing R decreasing with increasing pT and increasing with increasing charged multiplicity N_C.
- For sqrt(s)=14 TeV proton-proton collisions with tau=0.1 fm/c, the R parameter can increase by a factor of three for increasing total multiplicity m or decrease by a factor of three for increasing pT.
- For tau=0.1 fm/c at 14 TeV, the alpha parameter (related to oscillatory behavior) is small (alpha < 0.2), while for tau=0.5 fm/c it is large (alpha ~ 0.5-0.6).
- The delta parameter (exponential vs. Gaussian shape) at 14 TeV falls in the range 0.7 < delta < 1.5 for both tau=0.1 and 0.5 fm/c cases.
- The lambda parameter at 14 TeV is consistently below 0.5 for both tau values, attributed to long-lived resonances.
- The fraction of minimum-bias events at 14 TeV for the highest multiplicity bin (m > 300) is 0.093, with an average detectable charged particle multiplicity at mid-rapidity of 47.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Paic and Skowronski (2005) by using a simpler geometric hadronization picture without explicit jet identification, studying only 1-dimensional invariant correlation functions, and including final-state hadronic rescattering effects. The model is tested against Tevatron experiment E735 data at sqrt(s)=1.8 TeV, and the predictions for sqrt(s)=14 TeV are intended as baseline expectations for LHC measurements. Future comparisons with LHC data could determine whether the hadronization time is short (tau ~ 0.1 fm/c) or longer, and whether rescattering effects are significant at the higher energy.
