# Antineutrino induced Lambda(1405) production off the proton

> We have studied the strangeness changing antineutrino induced reactions $\barν_{l} p \rightarrow l^+ ϕB $, with $ϕB = K^-p$, $\bar{K}^0n$, $π^0Λ$, $π^0Σ^0$, $ηΛ$, $ηΣ^0$, $π^+Σ^-$, $π^-Σ^+$, $K^+Ξ^-$ and $K^0Ξ^0$, using a chiral unitary approach. These ten coupled channels are allowed to interact strongly, using a kernel derived from the chiral Lagrangians. This interaction generates two $Λ(1405)$ poles, leading to a clear single peak in the $πΣ$ invariant mass distributions. At backward scattering angles in the center of mass frame, $\barν_μ p \rightarrow μ^+ π^0 Σ^0$ is dominated by the $Λ(1405)$ state at around 1420~MeV while the lighter state becomes relevant as the angle decreases, leading to an asymmetric line shape. In addition, there are substantial differences in the shape of $πΣ$ invariant mass distributions for the three charge channels. If observed, these differences would provide valuable information on a claimed isospin I=1, strangeness S=-1 baryonic state around 1400 MeV. Integrated cross sections have been obtained for the $πΣ$ and $\bar K N$ channels, investigating the impact of unitarization in the results. The number of events with $Λ(1405)$ excitation in $\barν_μp$ collisions in the recent antineutrino run at the MINERvA experiment has also been obtained. We find that this reaction channel is relevant enough to be investigated experimentally and to be taken into account in the simulation models of future experiments with antineutrino beams.

## Key claim

The paper claims that antineutrino-induced reactions off protons can produce the Λ(1405) resonance, and that the two-pole structure of this resonance (a heavier state around 1420 MeV and a lighter state around 1385 MeV) manifests differently depending on the scattering angle, with the heavier pole dominating at backward angles and the lighter state becoming relevant at forward angles, leading to an asymmetric line shape in the πΣ invariant mass distributions.

## Method

The authors used a chiral unitary approach (UChPT) to study the strangeness-changing antineutrino-induced reactions ν̄_l p → l⁺ φB, where φB represents ten coupled channels (K⁻p, K̄⁰n, π⁰Λ, π⁰Σ⁰, ηΛ, ηΣ⁰, π⁺Σ⁻, π⁻Σ⁺, K⁺Ξ⁻, K⁰Ξ⁰). The hadronic current was derived from leading-order chiral Lagrangians, and the strong final-state interactions were described by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels with a kernel from the chiral Lagrangians, using a cutoff regularization of q_max = 630 MeV for the loop function. The cross sections were obtained by integrating over the phase space with a global dipole form factor (M_F ≈ 1 GeV) to account for the q² dependence of the weak vertices.

## Key numbers

- The two Λ(1405) poles appear at around 1420 MeV (width about 40 MeV) and around 1385 MeV (width about 150 MeV)
- The differential cross section dσ/dM_inv for π⁰Σ⁰ production peaks around 1420 MeV at antineutrino laboratory energies of 900, 1100, and 1300 MeV
- The cutoff used for the loop function regularization is q_max = 630 MeV
- The axial-vector coupling constants used are D = 0.804 and F = 0.463
- The Cabibbo angle parameters are |V_ud| = cos θ_c = 0.97425 ± 0.00022 and |V_us| = sin θ_c = 0.2252 ± 0.0009
- The Fermi constant is G_F = 1.16639 × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻²
- The meson decay constant used is F_φ = 1.15 f_π with f_π = 93 MeV
- The K⁻p threshold is at √s = 1430 MeV and the K̄⁰n threshold at √s = 1437 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to Λ(1405) studies in strong and electromagnetic processes (such as K⁻p scattering, photoproduction by CLAS, and electroproduction) to the weak interaction sector for the first time. The two-pole structure of the Λ(1405) was originally predicted by Oller and Meissner and later confirmed in photoproduction fits by Roca and Oset (2013). The paper enables experimental investigation of Λ(1405) production in antineutrino beams, specifically noting that the MINERvA experiment at FNAL could observe this reaction, and that the differences between the three πΣ charge channels could provide information on a claimed I=1, S=-1 baryonic state around 1400 MeV.
