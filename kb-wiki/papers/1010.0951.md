# Centre symmetric 3d effective actions for thermal SU(N) Yang-Mills from strong coupling series

> We derive three-dimensional, Z(N)-symmetric effective actions in terms of Polyakov loops by means of strong coupling expansions, starting from thermal SU(N) Yang-Mills theory in four dimensions on the lattice. An earlier action in the literature, corresponding to the (spatial) strong coupling limit, is thus extended by several higher orders, as well as by additional interaction terms. We provide analytic mappings between the couplings of the effective theory and the parameters $N_τ,β$ of the original thermal lattice theory, which can be systematically improved. We then investigate the deconfinement transition for the cases SU(2) and SU(3) by means of Monte Carlo simulations of the effective theory. Our effective models correctly reproduce second order 3d Ising and first order phase transitions, respectively. Furthermore, we calculate the critical couplings $β_c(N_τ)$ and find agreement with results from simulations of the 4d theory at the few percent level for $N_τ=4-16$.

## Key claim
The paper derives three-dimensional Z(N)-symmetric effective actions for thermal SU(N) Yang-Mills theory using strong coupling expansions on the lattice, extending earlier work beyond the spatial strong coupling limit by including higher orders and additional interaction terms. The effective models reproduce the correct order of the deconfinement phase transition (second order for SU(2), first order for SU(3)) and yield critical couplings β_c(N_τ) that agree with full 4d simulations at the few percent level for N_τ = 4-16, though the strong coupling series has a finite radius of convergence given by the deconfinement transition itself, limiting validity to the confined phase.

## Method
Starting from the 4d lattice Wilson action for SU(N) Yang-Mills at finite temperature, the authors integrate out spatial degrees of freedom using a strong coupling (small β) expansion via character expansion and the method of moments and cumulants. This yields a 3d effective action in terms of Polyakov loop variables, with analytic mappings between the effective couplings λ_n and the original lattice parameters (N_τ, β) computed through order u^10 in the strong coupling parameter u = a_f(β). The effective theories for SU(2) and SU(3) are then simulated using Monte Carlo methods, with a Taylor-expansion technique to handle a sign problem that appears for SU(3) near the critical coupling.

## Key numbers
- For SU(2), the leading effective coupling λ_1(u, N_τ) is given by u^{N_τ} exp[N_τ(4u^4 - 4u^6 + (140/3)u^8 - (36044/405)u^10)] for N_τ ≥ 5.
- For SU(3), λ_1(N_τ ≥ 6, u) = u^{N_τ} exp[N_τ(4u^4 + 12u^5 - 14u^6 - 36u^7 + (295/2)u^8 + (1851/10)u^9 + (1055797/5120)u^10)].
- The next-to-nearest neighbour coupling λ_2 for SU(3) with N_τ ≥ 8 is λ_2 = u^{2N_τ}[N_τ(N_τ-1)u^2].
- The critical couplings β_c(N_τ) from the effective theory agree with 4d simulation results at the few percent level for N_τ = 4-16, with deviations less than 6%.
- For SU(3), the critical coupling λ_{1,c} in the one-coupling model is approximately 0.2 for SU(2) and close to the threshold λ^T = 1/9 for SU(3).

## Context
This work extends earlier strong coupling approaches to finite-temperature gauge theories, such as those by Polonyi (1982), Green (1983), and Gross (1984), which typically neglected spatial plaquettes. It goes beyond the spatial strong coupling limit by including higher-order corrections and next-to-nearest neighbour interactions, complementing perturbative dimensional reduction methods (Linde, 1980) that lose Z(N) symmetry near the deconfinement transition. The effective models enable systematic improvement of the mapping between effective and original couplings, and the results suggest these models are useful for studying continuum physics and potentially for investigating QCD phase structure where sign problems hinder direct lattice simulations.
