# Superfluid Local Density Approximation: A Density Functional Theory Approach to the Nuclear Pairing Problem

> I describe the foundation of a Density Functional Theory approach to include pairing correlations, which was applied to a variety of systems ranging from dilute fermions, to neutron stars and finite nuclei. Ground state properties as well as properties of excited states and time-dependent phenomena can be achieved in this manner within a formalism based on microscopic input.

## Key claim

The Superfluid Local Density Approximation (SLDA) provides a Density Functional Theory (DFT) framework for describing pairing correlations in fermionic systems, with the claim that it can accurately reproduce ground-state energies of strongly interacting superfluid systems without needing gradient corrections, provided the ultraviolet divergences in the kinetic energy and anomalous densities are handled through a proper renormalization scheme.

## Method

The author constructs a local Energy Density Functional (EDF) in terms of the normal density, kinetic energy density, and anomalous (pairing) density, using a zero-range interaction with a renormalized coupling constant to avoid ultraviolet divergences. The functional is parameterized using Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) results for infinite homogeneous unitary Fermi gas (UFG) to determine three dimensionless constants (alpha, beta, gamma), and then tested against independent QMC calculations for finite systems in harmonic traps. The approach is extended to nuclear systems by including separate proton and neutron densities, Coulomb interaction, and isospin-symmetric pairing terms.

## Key numbers

* For the unitary Fermi gas (UFG), the QMC pairing gap is Delta_UFG(QMC) = 0.504(24) epsilon_F, and the experimental value is Delta_UFG(exp.) approx 0.45(5) epsilon_F.
* For (N_up, N_down) = (2,1) in a harmonic trap: E_QMC = 4.281 +/- 0.004 (in trap units), E_SLDA = 4.417, error = 3.2%.
* For (N_up, N_down) = (3,2): E_QMC = 7.61 +/- 0.01, E_SLDA = 7.602, error = 0.1%.
* For (N_up, N_down) = (4,4): E_QMC = 12.573 +/- 0.03, E_SLDA = 12.63, error = 0.48%.
* For (N_up, N_down) = (6,6): E_QMC = 21.278 +/- 0.05, E_SLDA = 21.13, error = 0.69%.
* For (N_up, N_down) = (10,10): E_QMC = 41.302 +/- 0.08, E_SLDA = 40.54, error = 1.8%.
* For (N_up, N_down) = (11,11): E_QMC = 46.889 +/- 0.09, E_SLDA = 45.00, error = 4%.
* Pairing gaps in infinite matter are reduced by a factor (4e)^(1/3) approx 2.2 when accounting for induced interactions, compared to weak-coupling BCS approximation.

## Context

This work extends the DFT approach of Kohn, Hohenberg, and Sham (1960s) to superfluid systems, building on earlier extensions by Gross and collaborators (Oliveira, 1988) and the pioneering nuclear DFT work of Fayans (1990s). It contrasts with Skyrme-type functionals (Vautherin, 1972) and Gogny interactions (Gogny, 1975) by providing a theoretically consistent treatment of pairing correlations without arbitrary cutoffs or separate particle-hole and particle-particle channels. The SLDA enables time-dependent simulations (TDSLDA) of phenomena such as giant dipole resonances in heavy nuclei, vortex dynamics in neutron stars, and low-energy nuclear collisions, which were previously inaccessible to microscopic theory.
