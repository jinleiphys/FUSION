# Norm-overlap formula of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov states with odd number parity

> A formula to calculate a norm overlap between Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) states with the odd number parity (one quasi-particle excited states) is derived with help of the Grassmann numbers and the Fermion coherent states. The final form of the formula is expressed in terms of a product of the Pfaffian for a neighboring even-even system (the zero quasi-particle state), and an extra factor consisting of the Bogoliubov transformation matrix and the anti-symmetric matrix in Thouless' HFB ansatz for the even-even system.

# Norm-overlap formula of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov states with odd number parity

## Key claim

The authors derive a Pfaffian-based formula for the norm overlap between two Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) states with odd number parity (one-quasi-particle excited states), expressed as a product of the Pfaffian for the neighboring even-even system and an extra factor involving the Bogoliubov transformation matrix and the inverse of the anti-symmetric matrix from Thouless' HFB ansatz. The formula is independent of the basis transformation matrix R used in the derivation, but its practical application requires computing the inverse of the matrix X, which itself is constructed from quantities already calculated for the even-even system.

## Method

The authors employ Grassmann numbers and Fermion coherent states to evaluate the norm overlap integral, following the Pfaffian approach introduced by Robledo (2009) for even-number-parity states. They decompose the one-quasi-particle creation-annihilation product into normal and dangerous operators, then evaluate each term using Grassmann integration with a specific ordering convention for the Grassmann vectors (following Negele and Orland, 1985). The final expression is obtained by relating the quantities derived from the inverse of the transformation matrix R directly to the inverse of the anti-symmetric matrix X.

## Key numbers

- The dimension of the configuration space is given as M, and the Pfaffian formula for even-number-parity states yields ⟨Φ^(0)|Φ^(1)⟩ = Pf(X), with the phase factor (−1)^(M(M+2)/2) = +1 for even M.
- The identity for the non-vanishing Grassmann integral gives ∫∏_α dη_α* dη_α ∏_β η_β* η_β = (−1)^M.
- The Pfaffian of the canonical form J is Pf(J) = (−1)^(M(M−1)/2).
- The final formula for odd-number-parity overlap is ⟨Φ^(0)_k|Φ^(1)_(k')⟩ = ⟨Φ^(0)|Φ^(1)⟩ Tr(W^(kk') X^(−1) + S^(kk')), where the even-number-parity overlap ⟨Φ^(0)|Φ^(1)⟩ = Pf(X).

## Context

This work extends the Pfaffian-based approach to norm overlaps originally developed by Robledo (2009) for even-even HFB states, applying it to odd-mass nuclei described by one-quasi-particle excited states. The formula addresses the sign problem in angular momentum projection that was previously treated by Onishi and Yoshida (1966) with a square root formula, and later by Hara, Hayashi and Ring (1982) using continuity methods, which encountered difficulties with nodal lines as discovered by Oi and Takada (2005). The result enables simultaneous angular momentum projection for both even-even and neighboring odd-mass nuclear systems without significant additional computational effort, and is related to concurrent work by Avez and Bender (2011) and Bertsch and Robledo (2011), though those works assume fully blocked unpaired particles rather than the UV exchange approximation used here.
