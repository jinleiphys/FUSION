# Complex-energy eigenvector continuation for nuclear many-body broad resonances

> Broad resonances are a unique phenomenon in nuclear many-body systems. Theoretical studies usually involve the continuum degree of freedom, which drastically increases the model space of calculations, and may lead to non-convergence or instability of computations. In this paper, we present the extension of the eigenvector continuation (EC) method to the complex-energy space to treat the broad resonances of open quantum systems of nuclei. EC provides an efficient method to predict the solution of a large-space many-body problem within a small subspace. Using only a few bound and narrow resonance solutions as input in EC, we can obtain the solution of a broad resonance. We have applied the complex-energy EC to the broad resonances of $^4$H, four-neutron $^4n$, $^6$He and $^7$He systems.

# Complex-energy eigenvector continuation for nuclear many-body broad resonances

## Key claim

The eigenvector continuation (EC) method can be extended to complex-energy space to predict broad resonance solutions of open quantum systems, but only if the training subspace includes narrow resonance snapshots (or complex conjugates of bound snapshots) in addition to bound-state snapshots; using only bound snapshots fails to reproduce the imaginary part (width) of broad resonances.

## Method

The authors scale the nuclear Hamiltonian as \(H(\alpha) = H_0 + \alpha H_1\) with \(\alpha \geq 1\), where \(\alpha=1\) is the physical target. They compute converged eigenvectors at several training points \(\alpha_i > 1\) using the Gamow shell model (GSM) or no-core Gamow shell model (NCGSM) with the complex-energy Berggren basis, obtaining bound or narrow resonance solutions. These training vectors define a subspace into which the target Hamiltonian at \(\alpha_*=1\) is projected, yielding a small generalized eigenvalue problem whose solution approximates the broad resonance.

## Key numbers

- For \(^4\)H \(2^{-}\) ground state: EC gives energy \(E_R = 2.65\) MeV and width \(\Gamma = 0.7\) MeV (relative to \(^3\)H + \(n\) threshold).
- For \(^6\)He \(2^+_1\) excited state: EC gives energy \(E_R = 0.947\) MeV (relative to \(^4\)He) and width \(\Gamma = 174\) keV.
- For \(^7\)He: EC gives \(3/2^{-}\) ground state \(E_R = 0.50\) MeV, \(\Gamma = 0.23\) MeV; \(1/2^{-}\) excited state \(E_R = 1.48\) MeV, \(\Gamma = 1.51\) MeV; \(5/2^{-}\) excited state \(E_R = 3.23\) MeV, \(\Gamma = 1.40\) MeV.
- EC uncertainty from model space and basis parameters is about 50 keV for both energy and width in \(^4\)n and \(^4\)H calculations.
- EC extrapolation uncertainty is about 60 keV for both energy and width in \(^4\)n and \(^4\)H, and about 10 keV in \(^6\)He and \(^7\)He.
- Using different chiral nuclear forces, the calculated \(^4\)n energy and width vary by at most 0.3 MeV and 0.5 MeV, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the eigenvector continuation method, previously applied to bound states and two- or three-body resonances with schematic potentials (Frame 2018; Yapa 2023, 2025), to realistic many-body nuclear broad resonances. It addresses the long-standing difficulty that broad resonances in the Berggren basis cause non-convergence or instability in direct GSM calculations, which previously required external potentials (PhysRevLett.90.252501) or polynomial extrapolations (PhysRevLett.117.182502; PhysRevLett.119.032501; PhysRevC.100.054313). The complex-energy EC enables reliable extrapolation to broad resonances in \(^4\)H, \(^4\)n, \(^6\)He, and \(^7\)He, and provides predictions for the \(^7\)He \(1/2^{-}\) broad resonance where experimental data remain uncertain.
