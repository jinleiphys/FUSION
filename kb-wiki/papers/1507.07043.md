# Short-range correlations in the deuteron: chiral effective field theory, meson-exchange, and phenomenology

> We study high-momentum distributions and short-range correlation probabilities in the deuteron with a variety of modern potentials based on chiral effective field theory up to fifth order in the chiral expansion. We also consider some conventional (meson-exchange and phenomenological) interactions. We examine our predictions in the context of short-range correlation probabilities as extracted from analyses of inclusive electron scattering data and discuss whether modern interac- tions can be reconciled with the latter.

# Short-range correlations in the deuteron: chiral effective field theory, meson-exchange, and phenomenology

## Key claim

Predictions of high-momentum distributions and short-range correlation probabilities in the deuteron from modern chiral effective field theory potentials and non-local meson-exchange forces are systematically lower than the empirical value of 0.041 ± 0.008 used in analyses of inclusive electron scattering data; however, when these lower values are used to extrapolate to nuclear matter, the resulting short-range correlation probabilities become more consistent with theoretical predictions for the wound integral.

## Method

The author calculated the deuteron momentum distribution ρ(k) as the Fourier transform squared of the coordinate-space wave function for a variety of nucleon-nucleon potentials, including three high-precision phenomenological potentials (CD-Bonn, Nijmegen II, AV18) and chiral EFT potentials from LO through N⁴LO (fifth order) with a 500 MeV cutoff. The short-range correlation probability a₂N(d) was computed by integrating the momentum distribution from k_min = 1.4 fm⁻¹ (276 MeV) to infinity, following the definition used in experimental analyses from the CLAS collaboration.

## Key numbers

- CD-Bonn potential: a₂N(d) = 0.032, with D-state probability P_D = 0.0485
- AV18 potential: a₂N(d) = 0.042, with P_D = 0.0578
- Nijmegen II potential: a₂N(d) = 0.041, with P_D = 0.0565
- Chiral N³LO potential (500 MeV cutoff): a₂N(d) = 0.030 ± 0.004, with P_D = 0.0451
- Chiral N⁴LO potential (500 MeV cutoff): a₂N(d) = 0.026 ± 0.004, with P_D = 0.0414
- Empirical deuteron SRC probability from CLAS analysis: 0.041 ± 0.008
- Using a₂N(d) = 0.032 (CD-Bonn) gives 20% for empirical SRC in nuclear matter; using a₂N(d) = 0.026 (converged chiral) gives 17%, compared to the cited empirical extrapolation of about 25%

## Context

This work extends the analysis of short-range correlations from the CLAS collaboration (Egiyan et al., 2006) and Alvioli et al. (2013) by systematically comparing chiral EFT potentials up to fifth order with conventional meson-exchange potentials (CD-Bonn, Nijmegen II, AV18). The study addresses the long-standing tension between non-local potentials (which predict lower SRC probabilities) and the empirical values used to extract absolute SRC probabilities in heavier nuclei. The results suggest that the discrepancy points to the role of non-locality in the tensor force rather than simply the "low-momentum" nature of some potentials, and the author plans to extend the analysis to the A=3 system to further reconcile theory with empirical analyses.
