# Glueballs, closed fluxtubes and eta(1440)

> The $eta_L(1410)$ component of the $η(1440)$ pseudoscalar has strong affinity for glue. But its mass is incompatible with lattice simulations that predict a much higher value for the $0^{-+}$ glueball. As a consequence it has been suggested that $η_L(1410)$ could signal physics beyond the Standard Model. Here we argue that if glueballs are closed gluonic fluxtubes then $η_L(1410)$ is a prime candidate for the $0^{-+}$ glueball. Furthermore, in the absence of parity violating terms its mass should be degenerate with that of the $0^{++}$ glueball. We also suggest that the decay properties of such glueballs could be employed as probes for extra dimensions.

## Key claim

The authors argue that if glueballs are interpreted as closed gluonic fluxtubes (QCD strings), then the $\eta_L(1410)$ component of $\eta(1440)$ is a natural candidate for the $0^{-+}$ glueball, contradicting lattice simulations that predict a much higher mass near 2.5 GeV for this state. They further claim that in a parity-invariant QCD, the $0^{++}$ glueball $f_0(1500)$ and the $0^{-+}$ glueball $\eta_L(1410)$ should be mass-degenerate, with the observed ~90 MeV mass difference possibly arising from parity-invariant terms in the Hamiltonian or from experimental uncertainties.

## Method

The authors compare the experimental production rates and decay properties of the $f_0(1500)$ and $\eta_L(1410)$ states in $p \bar p$ annihilation and radiative J/$\psi$ decay, using published branching fractions from Crystal Barrel and other experiments. They then develop a theoretical model where glueballs are identified with twisted toroidal closed gluonic fluxtubes, using a decomposition of the SU(2) Yang-Mills gauge field into a U(1) Cartan component and charged vector fields, which yields an effective action supporting knotted soliton solutions with left-right twisting degeneracy.

## Key numbers

- Lattice prediction for the lowest $0^{++}$ glueball mass: $1611 \pm 163$ MeV
- Lattice prediction for the $2^{++}$ glueball mass: $2232 \pm 310$ MeV
- Measured branching fraction for $p \bar p \rightarrow f_0(1500)$: $(7.7 \pm 3.8) \times 10^{-3}$
- Measured branching fraction for $p \bar p \rightarrow \eta_L(1410)\pi\pi$: $(5.3 \pm 1.7) \times 10^{-3}$
- Measured branching fraction for $J/\psi \rightarrow \gamma f_0(1500)$: $(1.3 \pm 0.3) \times 10^{-3}$
- Measured branching fraction for $J/\psi \rightarrow \gamma \eta_L(1410)$: $(1.0 \pm 0.46) \times 10^{-3}$
- Observed mass range for $f_0(1500)$: 1445-1560 MeV depending on reaction channel
- Estimated mass of the next ($|Q_H|=2$) glueball from the soliton model: 2500-2600 MeV
- Estimated mass for $Q_H = \pm 7$ (trefoil knot): 6.4-6.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of glueballs as closed gluonic fluxtubes, building on the decomposition of Yang-Mills fields developed by Faddeev and Niemi (2002). It directly contradicts quenched lattice simulations (Morningstar and Peardon, 1999) that predict a $0^{-+}$ glueball mass near 2.5 GeV, and challenges the suggestion by Farrar and collaborators (1998) that $\eta_L(1410)$ could be a bound state of light gluinos beyond the Standard Model. The proposal enables experimental tests at the planned GSI antiproton facility and at CLEO-II and BES through radiative charmonium decays, and suggests that glueball stability and decay properties could probe the dimensionality of space-time.
