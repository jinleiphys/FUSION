# Extracting $pΛ$ scattering lengths from heavy ion collisions

> The source radii, previously extracted by STAR Collaboration from the $p-Λ\oplus \bar{p}-\barΛ$ and $\bar{p}-Λ\oplus p-\barΛ$ correlation functions measured in 10% most central Au+Au collisions at top RHIC energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, differ by a factor of 2. The probable reason for this is the neglect of residual correlation effect in the STAR analysis. In the present paper we analyze baryon correlation functions within Lednicky and Lyuboshitz analytical model, extended to effectively account for the residual correlation contribution. Different analytical approximations for such a contribution are considered. We also use the averaged source radii extracted from the hydrokinetic model (HKM) simulations to fit the experimental data. In contrast to the STAR experimental study, the calculations in HKM show both $pΛ$ and $p\barΛ$ radii to be quite close, as expected from theoretical considerations. Using the effective Gaussian parametrization of residual correlations we obtain a satisfactory fit to the measured baryon-antibaryon correlation function with the HKM source radius value 3.28 fm. The baryon-antibaryon spin-averaged strong interaction scattering length is also extracted from the fit to the experimental correlation function.

# Extracting \(p\Lambda\) scattering lengths from heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The factor-of-2 discrepancy between the STAR Collaboration's extracted source radii for \(p\Lambda\) and \(p\bar{\Lambda}\) pairs in central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV can be resolved by accounting for residual correlation effects, which were neglected in the STAR analysis. Using the hydrokinetic model (HKM) to fix the source radius, the authors obtain a satisfactory fit to the baryon-antibaryon correlation function only when an effective Gaussian parametrization of residual correlations is included.

## Method
The authors analyze baryon-baryon (\(p-\Lambda \oplus \bar{p}-\bar{\Lambda}\)) and baryon-antibaryon (\(\bar{p}-\Lambda \oplus p-\bar{\Lambda}\)) correlation functions measured by STAR in 10% most central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, using the Lednicky-Lyuboshitz analytical model extended to include residual correlation contributions. Source radii are extracted from Gaussian fits to angle-averaged source functions calculated in the hydrokinetic model (HKM), which simulates the full collision evolution using ideal hydrodynamics matched to a hadronic cascade (UrQMD). For the baryon-antibaryon case, the authors test two residual correlation parametrizations: one following Kisiel (2014) and a simpler effective Gaussian form.

## Key numbers
- The HKM source radius for \(p\Lambda\) pairs is \(r_0^{\mathrm{HKM}} = 3.23\) fm, consistent with the STAR extracted value \(r_0^{exp}=3.09 \pm 0.30_{-0.25}^{+0.17} \pm 0.2\) fm for the baryon-baryon system.
- The HKM source radius for baryon-antibaryon pairs is \(r_0^{\mathrm{HKM}} = 3.28\) fm, in contrast to the STAR extracted value \(r_0 = 1.50 \pm 0.05^{+0.10}_{-0.12} \pm 0.3\) fm which is a factor of \(\sim 2\) smaller.
- Using the residual correlation parametrization of Kisiel (2014), the fit to the purity-uncorrected \(\bar{p}\Lambda\) correlation function yields \(r_0 = 2.76 \pm 0.13\) fm, \(\Re f_0 = 0.59 \pm 0.19\) fm, \(\Im f_0 = 0.85 \pm 0.14\) fm, with \(\chi^2/\mathrm{ndf} = 1.37\).
- Using the effective Gaussian residual correlation parametrization with the HKM source radius fixed at 3.28 fm, the extracted spin-averaged scattering lengths are \(\Re f_0 = 0.14 \pm 0.66\) fm and \(\Im f_0 = 1.53 \pm 1.31\) fm, with \(\chi^2/\mathrm{ndf} = 0.87\).
- The mean pair purity in the STAR experiment is \(\lambda = 17.5 \pm 2.5\%\).

## Context
This work extends the STAR experimental analysis (STAR Collaboration, 2014) of baryon correlation functions by incorporating residual correlation effects that were neglected in the original study. The authors use source radii from the hydrokinetic model (HKM), which successfully describes bulk observables at RHIC and LHC (Sinyukov et al., various years) and reproduces non-Gaussian tails in pion source functions observed by PHENIX (PHENIX Collaboration, 2014). The approach contrasts with the detailed residual correlation treatment of Kisiel (2014) by proposing a simpler effective Gaussian parametrization. The results enable improved extraction of baryon-antibaryon strong interaction parameters from existing and future high-statistics data at RHIC and LHC, and can help constrain hadron-hadron interaction models and cascade codes like UrQMD.
