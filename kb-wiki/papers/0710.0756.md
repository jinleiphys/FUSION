# Quark-Gluon Plasma/Black Hole duality from Gauge/Gravity Correspondence

> The Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) is the QCD phase of matter expected to be formed at small proper-times in the collision of heavy-ions at high energy. Experimental observations seem to favor a strongly coupled QCD plasma with the hydrodynamic properties of a quasi-perfect fluid, i.e. rapid thermalization (or isotropization) and small viscosity. The theoretical investigation of such properties is not obvious, due to the the strong coupling. The Gauge/Gravity correspondence provides a stimulating framework to explore the strong coupling regime of gauge theories using the dual string description. After a brief introduction to Gauge/Gravity duality, and among various existing studies, we focus on challenging problems of QGP hydrodynamics, such as viscosity and thermalization, in terms of gravitational duals of both the static and relativistically evolving plasma. We show how a Black Hole geometry arises naturally from the dual properties of a nearly perfect fluid and explore the lessons and prospects one may draw for actual heavy ion collisions from the Gauge/Gravity duality approach.

## Key claim

The gauge/gravity correspondence provides a framework to explore the strong coupling regime of gauge theories, where a nearly perfect fluid in (3+1) dimensions is dual to a black hole geometry in five dimensions; however, the quantitative results are derived specifically for the AdS/CFT case with N=4 supersymmetry and may not directly apply to QCD.

## Method

The paper reviews the application of the AdS/CFT correspondence to the Quark-Gluon Plasma, using holographic renormalization to relate the boundary energy-momentum tensor of a gauge theory to the bulk metric in five-dimensional anti-de Sitter space. For static configurations, the perfect fluid energy-momentum tensor is shown to be dual to a static black brane metric, and the shear viscosity is derived via the Kubo formula relating graviton absorption cross-section to energy-momentum correlators. For the dynamical case, the Bjorken boost-invariant flow is implemented with a proper-time dependent energy-momentum tensor, and the dual geometry is solved asymptotically to identify the nonsingular solution corresponding to a moving black hole.

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio from the static black hole calculation is η/S = 1/(4π), derived from the relation σ_abs(0)/(16πG) divided by A/(4G).
- The only nonsingular solution for the expanding plasma corresponds to the perfect fluid case with exponent s = 4/3 in the energy density scaling f(τ) ∝ τ^{-s}.
- The black hole horizon in the dynamical case moves as z_h(τ) ∝ τ^{1/3}, the temperature scales as T(τ) ∼ τ^{-1/3}, and the entropy remains constant.
- The estimated isotropization time from matching large and small proper-time regimes is τ_iso ∼ 0.3 fermi, using an energy density of 15 GeV fm^{-3} at proper-time τ = 0.6 fm.

## Context

This review extends the seminal work of Policastro, Son, and Starinets (2001) on viscosity in strongly coupled gauge theories via the AdS/CFT correspondence, and the work of Janik and Peschanski (2005) on dual geometries for the Bjorken flow. It also discusses the thermalization problem addressed by Janik (2006) using quasi-normal modes and by Kovchegov and Taliotis (2007) for small proper-time expansions. The approach enables quantitative connections between black hole thermodynamics and QGP hydrodynamics, and suggests directions for more realistic dual frameworks including finite color number, flavor degrees of freedom, and broken supersymmetry.
