# Form factors and exclusive processes -- Introduction and Overview

> Form factors, polarizabilities and excitation spectra are different aspects of many-body structures. These aspects are constrained by sum rules based on general principles like relativity, gauge invariance, causality and unitarity. Several such structure effects of baryons and mesons will be reviewed, with particular emphasis on the neutron charge form factor, the strangeness content of the nucleon, polarizabilities, and the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn and related sum rules.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that form factors, polarizabilities, and excitation spectra of baryons and mesons are interconnected aspects of hadronic structure, constrained by sum rules derived from general principles; however, the abstract's claim of a review "with particular emphasis" glosses over the fact that many of the presented results are preliminary, model-dependent, or contradictory, such as the unresolved discrepancy between neutron charge form factor measurements from different targets and the puzzling disagreement between experimental and theoretical values for the backward spin polarizability.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental and theoretical work on nucleon and meson structure, focusing on form factors measured via electron scattering (including double-polarization experiments and time-like measurements), parity-violating electron scattering for strangeness content, Compton scattering and virtual Compton scattering for polarizabilities, and sum rules (Baldin, Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn, Burkhardt-Cottingham, Bjorken) derived from dispersion relations and low-energy theorems. Specific experiments discussed include MAMI measurements of the neutron charge form factor using polarized 3He and deuterium targets, the FENICE experiment for time-like neutron form factors, the SAMPLE and HAPPEX experiments for strange form factors, and the GDH collaboration experiment at MAMI for helicity structure of photoabsorption.

## Key numbers

- Neutron charge form factor zero-crossing radius: approximately 0.7 fm from recent deuteron data, compared to the previously preferred "Platchkov fit" value of about 0.9 fm
- Sigma-minus charge radius: <r^2>_Sigma- = (0.60 ± 0.08 ± 0.08) fm^2, markedly smaller than for the proton
- HAPPEX asymmetry at Q^2 = 0.48 GeV^2: A_raw = -5.64 ± 0.75 ppm; extracted strange form factor combination: G_E^s + 0.39 G_M^s = 0.019 ± 0.035 ± 0.023 ± 0.026
- SAMPLE experiment central value leads to strange magnetic moment range: 0.03 ≤ μ_p^s ≤ 0.18 from HBChPT extrapolation
- Backward spin polarizability gamma_pi: experimental value -27.1 ± 3.4 (Tonnison et al.), theoretical predictions range -40 to -34, with L'vov and Nathan predicting -39.5
- Forward spin polarizability gamma_0: approximately -0.6 in units of 10^-4 fm^4 (from s-wave and Delta contributions)
- GDH integral predictions: Karliner predicted I^p = -261 μb and I^n = -183 μb, versus GDH result I^p = -205 μb and I^n = -233 μb
- Generalized GDH integral zero-crossing: Q^2_0 ≈ 0.8 GeV^2 (one-pion only), lowered to about 0.5 GeV^2 (including eta and multi-pion channels)
- SLAC experiment at Q^2 = 0.5 GeV^2: I_1 = 0.10 ± 0.06 (statistical error only)
- Ratio E_{1+}/M_{1+} for Delta transition: remains of order -5% even at highest Q^2 values studied at Jefferson Lab
- Virtual Compton scattering pilot experiment at MAMI: real photon momenta 33.6 MeV < q' < 111.5 MeV, effects of order 10^-38 cm^2/MeV sr^2 in differential cross section
- Pion polarizabilities: experimental result α - β = 40 ± 24 (Lebedev Institute 1970s), ChPT predicts 5.3 (in units of 10^-4 fm^3)

## Context

This review extends the long-standing program of understanding nucleon structure through electromagnetic probes, building on the foundational sum rules of Baldin (1960), Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (1965), Burkhardt-Cottingham (1970), and Bjorken. It presents results that challenge earlier theoretical expectations, such as the FENICE experiment showing time-like neutron form factors about twice the space-like values, and the discrepancy between the GDH sum rule prediction and calculations by Karliner (1973) and Sandorfi et al. (1994). The work enables future experiments at Jefferson Lab, ELSA, MIT/Bates, and MAMI to explore the transition from resonance-dominated coherent processes at low Q^2 to the incoherent parton dynamics of deep inelastic scattering at high Q^2, particularly through the Q^2 evolution of the generalized GDH integral and the measurement of small Delta multipoles that affect spin structure at intermediate Q^2.
