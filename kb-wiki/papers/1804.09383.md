# Hidden-strange molecular states and the $Nϕ$ bound state via a QCD van der Waals force

> In this work, we study the hidden-strange molecular states composed of a baryon and a vector meson in a coupled-channel $Nρ-Nω-Nϕ-ΛK^*-ΣK^*$ interaction. With the help of the effective Lagrangians which coupling constants are determined by the SU(3) symmetry, the interaction is constructed and inserted into the quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation to search for poles in the complex plane, which correspond to molecular states. Two poles are found with a spin parity $3/2^-$ near the $Nρ$ and the $ΣK^*$ thresholds, which can be related to the $N(1700)$ and the $N(2100)$, respectively. No pole near the $Nϕ$ threshold can be found if direct interaction between a nucleon and $ϕ$ meson is neglected according to the OZI rule. After introducing the QCD van der Waals force between a nucleon and $ϕ$ meson, a narrow state can be produced near the $Nϕ$ threshold. Inclusion of the QCD van der Waals force changes the line shape of the invariant mass spectrum in the $Nϕ$ channel leading to a worse agreement with the present low-precision data. Future experiments at BelleII, JLab, and other facilities will be very helpful to clarify the existence of these possible hidden-strange molecular states.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a narrow hidden-strange molecular state near the \(N\phi\) threshold can be produced only after introducing a QCD van der Waals force between a nucleon and a \(\phi\) meson, but the inclusion of this force worsens agreement with existing low-precision experimental data on the \(N\phi\) invariant mass spectrum.

## Method

The authors constructed a coupled-channel \(N\rho-N\omega-N\phi-\Lambda K^*-\Sigma K^*\) interaction using effective Lagrangians with coupling constants determined by SU(3) symmetry. The interaction was inserted into a quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation to search for poles in the complex plane corresponding to molecular states, with both exponential and cutoff regularizations applied. The QCD van der Waals force was modeled as a nonrelativistic Yukawa-type attractive potential and transformed into momentum space for inclusion in the formalism.

## Key numbers

- Two poles with spin parity \(3/2^-\) were found: a lower pole near the \(N\rho\) threshold at about 1710 MeV and a higher pole near the \(\Sigma K^*\) threshold at about 2050 MeV, with the higher pole showing a width of about 19 MeV at a cutoff of \(p_{max}=0.65\) GeV.
- With the QCD van der Waals force strength \(\alpha=1.2\) and cutoff \(p_{max}=0.65\) GeV, an additional narrow pole appeared at \(1934+1i\) MeV near the \(N\phi\) threshold.
- The higher pole was found at \(2024+32i\) MeV at a cutoff \(p_{max}=0.55\) GeV or at \(1973+24i\) MeV at a cutoff \(p_{max}=0.75\) GeV when using a coupling constant corresponding to the standard value of \(f=93\) MeV as in the chiral unitary approach.
- The Belle experiment best fitted result suggested a resonance at 2025 MeV with a width of 22 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hidden-strange molecular states, particularly the \(\Sigma K^*\) and \(\Sigma^* K\) interactions studied by He (2017), and the chiral unitary approach calculation by Oset (2009) which found a pole at \(1977+i53\) MeV with large coupling to \(\Sigma K^*\). The paper also builds on the suggestion by Brodsky (1989) and Gao (2000) that the QCD van der Waals force from multigluon exchange could produce a \(N\phi\) bound state, and on constituent quark model calculations by Huang (2005) and Gao (2017) that required couplings to other channels to produce a \(N\phi\) bound state. The results enable future experimental searches at BelleII and JLab to clarify the existence of these hidden-strange pentaquark candidates.
