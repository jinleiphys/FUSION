# Elliptic flow of thermal photons and dileptons

> In this talk we describe the recently discovered rich phenomenology of elliptic flow of electromagnetic probes of the hot matter created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Using a hydrodynamic model for the space-time dynamics of the collision fireball created in Au+Au collisions at RHIC, we compute the transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flow of thermal photons and dileptons. These observables are shown to provide differential windows into various stages of the fireball expansion.

## Key claim

The elliptic flow of thermal photons and dileptons can provide differential windows into various stages of the fireball expansion in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, but the hydrodynamic predictions for photon elliptic flow at high transverse momentum (above 1 GeV/c) must be regarded as an upper limit due to the gradual breakdown of ideal fluid dynamics and the expected suppression from viscous corrections and prompt photon contributions.

## Method

The authors used the boost-invariant hydrodynamic code AZHYDRO to model the space-time dynamics of Au+Au collisions at RHIC at sqrt(s) = 200 A GeV. They computed the transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flow of thermal photons and dileptons by integrating thermal emission rates from the QGP and hadronic matter over the hydrodynamic evolution. The initial entropy density was extrapolated from the usual initial time of 0.6 fm/c to a 3 times smaller value of 0.2 fm/c, assuming 1-dimensional boost-invariant expansion between these times, to account for pre-equilibrium photon production at very early times.

## Key numbers

- Initial time for hydrodynamic evolution: tau_0 = 0.2 fm/c (extrapolated from the standard tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c)
- Collision system: Au+Au at sqrt(s) = 200 A GeV, impact parameter b = 7 fm
- Photon elliptic flow shows a distinctive peak-valley structure around p_T = 0.4-0.5 GeV/c
- For dileptons with invariant mass M = 2 GeV, QGP dileptons outshine hadronic dileptons by three orders of magnitude or more for all p_T
- The hadron gas phase duration is approximately 7 fm/c
- The omega and phi meson lifetimes are 23 and 45 fm/c, respectively, significantly exceeding the hadron gas phase duration
- Thermal freeze-out temperature: T_dec = 130 MeV
- For dileptons with M = m_phi, hadronic dileptons dominate the p_T-spectrum up to p_T = 4 GeV/c
- The total dilepton elliptic flow at M = m_phi follows hadronic v_2 almost perfectly below p_T = 2 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic modeling of hadron elliptic flow at RHIC, which established the "perfect liquidity" paradigm for the QGP, to electromagnetic probes that directly access earlier expansion stages. The authors note that previous work by Hirano and Lappi had highlighted uncertainties about initial conditions, while studies by Pasi had raised questions about equation of state effects. The paper builds on the AZHYDRO code and prior photon emission rate calculations by Guy and Simon, and enables future studies using two-photon correlations to directly measure the space-time structure of the early collision fireball.
