# Constrained Gaussian-process bridge prior for neutron-star equation-of-state inference

> We set forth a new method for generating model-agnostic, nonparametric priors for neutron star equation-of-state inference that are stable, causal and thermodynamically consistent by construction. This generalizes Gaussian processes to include global thermodynamic constraints, specifically allowing the inclusion of any number of training points in the form $(μ, n, p)$ while retaining thermodynamic consistency between them. The method is based on constructing constrained Gaussian-process bridges, whose correlation properties can be tuned at will allowing flexibility between a conservative prior and a theory-informed prior. The method does not require any shooting to obey multiple constraints and provides an efficient and informed way to include both chiral effective field theory and perturbative quantum chromodynamics constraints within the same framework.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new method for generating nonparametric priors for neutron-star equation-of-state inference that enforces thermodynamic consistency, stability, and causality by construction, while allowing tunable correlation lengths. The key advance over previous Gaussian-process approaches is that the method does not require any shooting procedure to satisfy multiple constraints and can incorporate both chiral effective field theory and perturbative quantum chromodynamics constraints within the same framework.

## Method

The method constructs constrained Gaussian-process bridge priors in two steps: first, a self-similar (fractal) equation of state is generated by recursively sampling intermediate thermodynamic points (chemical potential, number density, pressure) within the allowed volume defined by physical constraints between low- and high-density limits; second, local Gaussian correlations are imposed by applying a heat-kernel diffusion equation to the chemical potential as a function of number density, with a density-dependent diffusion coefficient that controls the correlation length. The low-density limit is taken from chiral effective field theory calculations up to 2 nuclear saturation densities, and the high-density limit from perturbative quantum chromodynamics down to 30 nuclear saturation densities, with diffusion performed over the extended range from 0.34 to 40 nuclear saturation densities to ensure smooth transitions.

## Key numbers

- Low-density limit set at n_L = 2 n_s ≈ 0.32 fm^{-3} from chiral effective field theory; high-density limit set at n_H = 30 n_s ≈ 4.8 fm^{-3} from perturbative quantum chromodynamics
- Diffusion performed over density range [0.34, 40] n_s to ensure smooth transitions of the speed of sound
- Correlation lengths tested: σ/n = 1%, 10%, 20%, and 40% of the local density
- Hierarchical model combines correlation lengths from a uniform distribution σ/n ∈ [0.2, 0.4]
- Energy density deviation at the high-density boundary from diffusion is at the percent level, below the theoretical uncertainty of chiral effective field theory and perturbative quantum chromodynamics calculations
- For the largest correlations (σ/n = 40%), the speed of sound exhibits a clear softening onset from approximately 1 fm^{-3} to 4-6 fm^{-3}
- At the highest densities reached in stable neutron stars, central densities in most equations of state satisfy d_c < 0.2, a value indicative of quark matter
- Pressure normalized to free quark gas approximately plateaus above chemical potentials of μ ∼ 1.25 GeV

## Context

This work extends the line of model-agnostic equation-of-state inference that began with Hebeler (2013) and Kurkela (2014), and addresses shortcomings of previous Gaussian-process approaches such as those by Landry (2018) and Gorda (2022) that could not simultaneously enforce all physical constraints. The method enables unified inference of the equation of state across all densities from nuclear to perturbative quantum chromodynamics regimes, and allows studying how individual observations constrain the high-density part of the equation of state beyond the stable neutron-star branch. The results are consistent with earlier findings by Annala (2019, 2023) regarding softening at high densities and the behavior of the trace anomaly and conformal distance as indicators of quark matter.
