# Pairing theory of the symmetry energy

> As a model which displays a picture of the symmetry energy as an energy of rotation in isospace of a Cooper pair condensate, a Hamiltonian with a pairing force and an interaction of isospins is analyzed in the Hartree-Bogolyubov (HB) plus Random Phase (RPA) approximation. The HB energy minus Lagrangian multiplier terms is shown to be locally minimized by a product of neutron and proton Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer states. Nambu-Goldstone RPA solutions appear due to global gauge invariance and isobaric invariance. In an idealized case of infinitely many equidistant single-nucleon levels, the symmetry energy is composed of contributions from the single-nucleon and isospin interaction energies and the RPA correlation energy. The contribution of the latter is dominated by a neutron-proton Nambu-Goldstone solution, which makes the total symmetry energy nearly proportional to T(T+1). Observations reported from Skyrme force calculations are discussed in the light of these results. Calculations with deformed Woods-Saxon single-nucleon levels give results similar to those of the idealized case, whereas a somewhat different behavior is found with spherical Woods-Saxon levels. The calculations with Woods-Saxon single-nucleon levels reproduce surprisingly well the empirical symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The symmetry energy can be understood as an energy of rotation in isospace of a Cooper pair condensate ("superfluid isorotation"), and in an idealized case of infinitely many equidistant single-nucleon levels, the total symmetry energy becomes nearly proportional to \(T(T+1)\) rather than \(T^2\), with the neutron-proton Nambu-Goldstone RPA solution dominating the correlation contribution.

## Method

The author analyzes a Hamiltonian with an isobarically invariant isovector pairing force and a separable isospin interaction (symmetry force) in the Hartree-Bogolyubov (HB) plus Random Phase Approximation (RPA). The HB Routhian (energy minus Lagrangian multiplier terms for valence nucleon number and isospin \(z\)-component) is minimized by a product of neutron and proton Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) states. The RPA equations are reduced to independent equations for two-neutron, two-proton, and neutron-proton quasiparticle pairs, and calculations are performed for an idealized equidistant level spectrum and for deformed and spherical Woods-Saxon single-nucleon levels.

## Key numbers

- In the idealized case of infinitely many equidistant single-nucleon levels, the total symmetry energy is proportional to \(T(T+1)\), with the neutron-proton Nambu-Goldstone solution giving a linear term that makes the total symmetry energy nearly proportional to \(T(T+1)\).
- Calculations for mass numbers \(A = 56\) and \(A = 100\) with spherical Woods-Saxon levels show that promotion of nucleons across magic gaps and the onset of superfluidity give rise to large linear terms in the symmetry energy.
- The calculations with Woods-Saxon single-nucleon levels reproduce surprisingly well the empirical symmetry energy (no explicit numerical value for the empirical symmetry energy is given in the text).

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier brief articles (Neergård, 2008) and is inspired by Goswami's observation that the pair potential in a superfluid nucleus is isobarically noninvariant, and by Frauendorf and Sheikh's suggestion that the symmetry energy may be conceived as an energy of rotation of the condensate in isospace. The model contrasts with earlier approaches such as the Fermi gas model (Bethe and Bacher, 1936) and the shell-model result of Talmi and Unna (1960) for a single \(j\)-shell, and it provides a microscopic basis for the \(T(T+1)\) dependence seen in Skyrme force calculations by Satula and Wyss (2001). The results enable a unified understanding of the symmetry energy as a superfluid isorotational phenomenon and suggest an experimental signature for such rotation.
