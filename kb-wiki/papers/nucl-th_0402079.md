# New effective interaction for $pf$-shell nuclei and its implications for the stability of the $N$=$Z$=28 closed core

> The effective interaction GXPF1 for shell-model calculations in the full $pf$ shell is tested in detail from various viewpoints such as binding energies, electro-magnetic moments and transitions, and excitation spectra. The semi-magic structure is successfully described for $N$ or Z=28 nuclei, $^{53}$Mn, $^{54}$Fe, $^{55}$Co and $^{56,57,58,59}$Ni, suggesting the existence of significant core-excitations in low-lying non-yrast states as well as in high-spin yrast states. The results of $N=Z$ odd-odd nuclei, $^{54}$Co and $^{58}$Cu, also confirm the reliability of GXPF1 interaction in the isospin dependent properties. Studies of shape coexistence suggest an advantage of Monte Carlo Shell Model over conventional calculations in cases where full-space calculations still remain too large to be practical.

## Key claim

The GXPF1 effective interaction, fitted to 699 energy data points in the mass range A=47-66, successfully describes semi-magic nuclei near N or Z=28, but this success depends on allowing significant core-excitations across the N=Z=28 shell gap in low-lying non-yrast states, not just in high-spin yrast states as previously assumed.

## Method

The authors derived GXPF1 by starting from a microscopic G-matrix interaction based on the Bonn-C potential and empirically fitting 70 well-determined linear combinations of the 195 two-body matrix elements and four single-particle energies to 699 experimental binding and excitation energies from 87 nuclei in the pf shell (mass range A=47-66). They used a few-dimensional approximation with empirical corrections (FDA*) within the Monte Carlo Shell Model framework for the fitting, then performed conventional Lanczos diagonalization for most subsequent calculations, comparing results with experimental data on binding energies, electromagnetic moments and transitions, and excitation spectra.

## Key numbers

- The GXPF1 interaction was fitted to 699 experimental energy data points (490 yrast, 198 yrare, and 11 higher states) from 87 nuclei in the mass range A=47-66
- The estimated rms error of GXPF1 within FDA* is 168 keV
- For the group of nuclei with Z or N=28, GXPF1 gives rms deviations of 0.184 MeV (yrast) and 0.195 MeV (yrare), compared to KB3G which gives 0.647 MeV (yrast) and 0.802 MeV (yrare)
- Single-particle energies in GXPF1 are -8.6240 MeV (f7/2), -5.6793 MeV (p3/2), -1.3829 MeV (f5/2), and -4.1370 MeV (p1/2)
- The collective quadrupole-quadrupole strength between protons and neutrons in GXPF1 is -7.82 MeV, while that between like nucleons is -0.96 MeV
- Seven two-body matrix elements differ between G and GXPF1 by more than 500 keV, with the largest difference being -0.7637 MeV for the 7373 J=5, T=0 matrix element

## Context

This work extends the empirical fitting approach previously successful for lighter nuclei (Cohen-Kurath for the p shell and USD for the sd shell) to the full pf shell, addressing failures of earlier microscopic interactions like KB3 and KB3G (Otsuka 2001) near 56Ni. The GXPF1 interaction enables systematic shell-model calculations across the entire pf shell and reveals that the N=Z=28 closed core is softer than previously assumed, with significant core-excitations appearing in low-lying states. The results also demonstrate the advantage of the Monte Carlo Shell Model (Otsuka 1998) over conventional calculations for cases where full-space calculations remain too large to be practical.
