# Cumulants of multiple conserved charges and global conservation laws

> We analyze the behavior of cumulants of conserved charges in a subvolume of a thermal system with exact global conservation laws by extending a recently developed subensemble acceptance method (SAM) [V. Vovchenko et al., arXiv:2003.13905] to multiple conserved charges. Explicit expressions for all diagonal and off-diagonal cumulants up to sixth order that relate them to the grand canonical susceptibilities are obtained. The derivation is presented for an arbitrary equation of state with an arbitrary number of different conserved charges. The global conservation effects cancel out in any ratio of two second order cumulants, in any ratio of two third order cumulants, as well as in a ratio of strongly intensive measures $خ£$ and $خ”$ involving any two conserved charges, making all these quantities particularly suitable for theory-to-experiment comparisons in heavy-ion collisions. We also show that the same cancellation occurs in correlators of a conserved charge, like the electric charge, with any non-conserved quantity such as net proton or net kaon number. The main results of the SAM are illustrated in the framework of the hadron resonance gas model. We also elucidate how net-proton and net-$خ›$ fluctuations are affected by conservation of electric charge and strangeness in addition to baryon number.

## Key claim

The paper extends the subensemble acceptance method (SAM) to systems with multiple globally conserved charges, deriving explicit expressions for cumulants up to sixth order that relate subvolume cumulants to grand canonical susceptibilities; the key finding is that global conservation effects cancel in ratios of second-order cumulants, ratios of third-order cumulants, and in strongly intensive measures خ£ and خ”, but not in ratios of fourth-order cumulants except at vanishing chemical potentials.

## Context

This work extends the SAM framework developed by Vovchenko et al. (2020) for a single conserved charge to multiple conserved charges, generalizing earlier studies of global conservation effects in uncorrelated hadron gases by Bleicher (2000), Begun (2006), Bzdak (2012), and Braun-Munzinger (2016). The results enable direct comparison of cumulant ratios from heavy-ion experiments to grand canonical susceptibilities from lattice QCD, particularly for ratios of second-order cumulants, third-order cumulants, and strongly intensive measures خ£ and خ”, where global conservation effects cancel. The formalism also shows how net-proton and net-خ› fluctuations are affected by conservation of electric charge and strangeness in addition to baryon number, providing a framework for understanding acceptance corrections in experimental fluctuation measurements at facilities such as RHIC and LHC.
