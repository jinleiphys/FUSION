# Deep Learning Detection and Classification of Gravitational Waves from Neutron Star-Black Hole Mergers

> The Laser Interferometer Gravitational-Wave Observatory (LIGO) and Virgo Interferometer Collaborations have now detected all three classes of compact binary mergers: binary black hole (BBH), binary neutron star (BNS), and neutron star-black hole (NSBH). For coalescences involving neutron stars, the simultaneous observation of gravitational and electromagnetic radiation produced by an event, has broader potential to enhance our understanding of these events, and also to probe the equation of state (EOS) of dense matter. However, electromagnetic follow-up to gravitational wave (GW) events requires rapid real-time detection and classification of GW signals, and conventional detection approaches are computationally prohibitive for the anticipated rate of detection of next-generation GW detectors. In this work, we present the first deep learning based results of classification of GW signals from NSBH mergers in \textit{real} LIGO data. We show for the first time that a deep neural network can successfully distinguish all three classes of compact binary mergers and separate them from detector noise. Specifically, we train a convolutional neural network (CNN) on $\sim 500,000$ data samples of real LIGO noise with injected BBH, BNS, and NSBH GW signals, and we show that our network has high sensitivity and accuracy. Most importantly, we successfully recover the two confirmed NSBH events to-date (GW200105 and GW200115) and the two confirmed BNS mergers to-date (GW170817 and GW190425), together with $\approx 90\%$ of all BBH candidate events from the third Gravitational Wave Transient Catalog, GWTC-3. These results are an important step towards low-latency real-time GW detection, enabling multi-messenger astronomy.

# Deep Learning Detection and Classification of Gravitational Waves from Neutron Star-Black Hole Mergers

## Key claim
A convolutional neural network trained on approximately 500,000 samples of real LIGO noise with injected gravitational wave signals can successfully distinguish all three classes of compact binary mergers (binary black hole, binary neutron star, and neutron star-black hole) from each other and from detector noise, but only when the training dataset includes the neutron star-black hole class, previous models lacking this class misclassified real NSBH events as noise or BBH signals.

## Method
The authors constructed a dataset of real LIGO noise from the Livingston detector (O2 and O3b runs, sampled at 4096 Hz) with injected simulated waveforms generated using LALSuite: SEOBNRv4 for BBH, TaylorF2 for BNS, and SEOBNRv4_ROM_NRTidalv2_NSBH for NSBH signals. Component masses were sampled uniformly (BBH: 2-95 solar masses, mass ratio ≤10; BNS: 1-2 solar masses; NSBH: NS 1-2 solar masses, BH 2-35 solar masses) with zero spin and zero eccentricity, using the APR equation of state for tidal deformability. A convolutional neural network with 4 convolutional blocks (each with 3 convolutional layers, ReLU activation, batch normalization, and max pooling), 2 dense hidden layers (128 and 64 neurons), and a 4-class softmax output (3,441,380 total parameters) was trained on 480,000 templates, validated on 16,000, and tested on 1,600,000 disjoint samples, with signal-to-noise ratios sampled from a truncated triangular distribution (mode 5, range 3-27.5) for training to emphasize low-SNR events.

## Key numbers
- Training dataset: approximately 500,000 data samples (480,000 training, 16,000 validation, 1,600,000 testing), each approximately 1/4 noise, 1/4 noise+BBH, 1/4 noise+BNS, 1/4 noise+NSBH
- Component mass ranges: BBH 2-95 solar masses, BNS 1-2 solar masses, NSBH NS 1-2 solar masses and BH 2-35 solar masses
- At a false alarm probability of 0.001, the model saturates sensitivity (100% detection) for BBH at SNR ≥ 8, for NSBH at SNR ≥ 10, and for BNS at SNR ≥ 13
- All signals are detected and correctly classified for optimal SNR ≥ 13 across all three classes
- The model successfully recovers 64 of 72 BBH events from GWTC-2 and GWTC-3 catalogs, plus all 2 BNS events (GW170817, GW190425) and all 2 confirmed NSBH events (GW191219, GW200115)
- All misclassified BBH events from O3b have single-detector SNR < 7.0
- The model has 3,441,380 parameters total
- Training used initial learning rate α = 3.986 × 10^(-3), batch size 256, 50 epochs on 4 NVidia A100 GPUs

## Context
This work extends previous deep learning approaches by Krastev (2019) and Krastev et al. (2020) that detected BNS and BBH signals but did not include the NSBH event class, which caused those earlier models to misclassify real NSBH events as BBH signals or noise. The results enable low-latency real-time gravitational wave detection for multi-messenger astronomy, particularly for prompt electromagnetic follow-up of neutron star mergers, which is critical for next-generation detectors like Einstein Telescope and Cosmic Explorer where millions of events per year are anticipated. The approach contrasts with conventional matched-filtering methods (Gabbard 2017, Dal Canton 2014) that require large banks of pre-calculated waveform templates and become computationally prohibitive at high detection rates.
