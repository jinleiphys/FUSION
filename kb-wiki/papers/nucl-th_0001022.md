# Dressing the nucleon in a dispersion approach

> We present a model for dressing the nucleon propagator and vertices. In the model the use of a K-matrix approach (unitarity) and dispersion relations (analyticity) are combined. The principal application of the model lies in pion-nucleon scattering where we discuss effects of the dressing on the phase shifts.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model for dressing the nucleon propagator and vertices that combines a K-matrix approach (for unitarity) with dispersion relations (for analyticity), but the authors note that analyticity is only obeyed "approximately" because the use of phenomenological bare form factors with singularities at infinity invalidates rigorous dispersion relations, requiring neglect of additional residue contributions.

## Method

The model proceeds in two stages: first, effective 2- and 3-point Green's functions (propagators and vertices) are built incorporating non-perturbative dressing from regular (non-pole) parts of loop diagrams, using dispersion relations to obtain real parts from imaginary parts calculated via cutting rules. Second, a K-matrix formalism is used to calculate the T-matrix, where the kernel (K-matrix) is constructed from tree-level diagrams using the dressed vertices and propagators, with pole contributions included through the K-matrix equation. The dressing procedure is iterated until convergence (normalized root-mean-square difference below 10^{-4} for at least 100 steps), and includes the nucleon, pion, Delta-resonance, rho-meson, and sigma-meson degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

- The physical pion-nucleon coupling constant is taken as 13.02 (dimensionless, from Korschtim reference)
- The bare pi-N-N form factor uses an exponential form with half-width Lambda_N^2, where the form factor reduces to 1/2 at p^2 = M^2 + Lambda_N^2
- The convergence criterion for the dressing iteration is d_n < 10^{-4} for at least 100 iteration steps
- The model achieves good agreement with pion-nucleon phase shifts for pion energies exceeding 400 MeV, adjusting only 5 parameters
- The nucleon and pion masses are denoted M and mu respectively, with the dispersion integral threshold at (M+mu)^2

## Context

This work extends the method of Kondratyuk and Scholten (1999) by including consistent dressing of interacting nucleons with a broader set of meson and baryon degrees of freedom (rho, sigma, Delta). It contrasts with earlier K-matrix models by Goudsmit, Schkorpian, Feuster, and Korschtim that set K=V and thus ignore principal value parts of loop integrals, thereby violating analyticity. The approach enables calculation of pion-nucleon phase shifts with explicit inclusion of principal value parts through dispersion integrals, differing from the work of Pearce (who used three-dimensional reductions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation) by keeping the most general Lorentz structure for the pi-N-N vertex.
