# Extended formalism for the study of final-state interaction in $γp\,\rightarrow\,K^{+}π\,Σ$ photoproduction

> We extend the formalism for the low-energy analysis of the $γp\rightarrow K^{+}πΣ$ photoproduction reaction to include the strangeness $S=-1$ meson-baryon p-wave final-state interaction with total angular momentum $J=\frac{3}{2}\,$. As an application, and a check of our method, we evaluate contributions due to the exchange of the $Σ^{\ast}(1385)$ resonance as derived from a chiral Lagrangian.

## Key claim
The paper extends a previously developed theoretical formalism for analyzing $\gamma p\rightarrow K^{+}\pi\Sigma$ photoproduction to include p-wave ($\ell=1$, $J=3/2$) final-state interactions in the strangeness $S=-1$ meson-baryon system, whereas earlier work had only treated s-wave interactions. The extension is necessary because earlier predictions overshot measured spectra at higher center-of-mass energies, and the authors identify the neglect of higher partial waves as one possible reason for this discrepancy.

## Method
The authors decompose the invariant amplitude for $\gamma p\rightarrow K^{+}\pi\Sigma$ into Lorentz structures and perform a partial-wave projection of the meson-baryon scattering amplitude, expanding the photoproduction amplitudes in spherical harmonics up to $\ell=1$. They derive unitarity relations connecting the photoproduction partial-wave projections to the meson-baryon partial-wave scattering amplitudes $f_{\ell\pm}$, and construct gauge-invariant combinations of amplitude projections that satisfy these unitarity relations. As a consistency check, they evaluate contributions from $\Sigma^{\ast}(1385)$ exchange using a chiral Lagrangian with decuplet fields and minimal coupling of the photon, verifying that the resonance pole appears only in the $J=3/2$, $\ell=1$ partial wave.

## Key numbers
- The $\Sigma^{\ast}(1385)$ resonance mass is taken as $m_{\Sigma^{\ast 0}} = 1.384$ GeV with width $\Gamma_{\Sigma^{\ast 0}} = 36$ MeV.
- The pion decay constant is set to $F_{\pi} = 92.2$ MeV and the kaon decay constant to $F_{K} = 110.1$ MeV.
- The decuplet coupling constant is taken as $|\mathcal{C}| = 3/2$.
- The numerical evaluation is performed at a center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s} = 2$ GeV.
- The cross section results are shown in units of $\mu$b/GeV for the mass spectrum and $\mu$b for the angular distribution.

## Context
This work extends the formalism developed by Bruns (2020) and Bruns (2022) for analyzing $\Lambda(1405)$ lineshape measurements from the CLAS collaboration (2013). Earlier work by Cieply (2023) introduced a form factor for $K^{+}$ emission but still found unsatisfactory fits, especially for the $\pi^{-}\Sigma^{+}$ channel. The new formalism enables unitarized models that include both s-wave and p-wave final-state interactions, which is necessary for a proper treatment of resonances like the $\Sigma^{\ast}(1385)$ and potentially the $\Lambda(1520)$ in photoproduction reactions. The author notes that a full treatment of decuplet contributions beyond the minimal-coupling scheme used here would be needed for a more realistic description.
