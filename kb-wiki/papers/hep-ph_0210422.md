# Catastrophic rearrangement of a compact star due to the quark core formation

> We study properties of compact stars with the deconfinement phase transition in their interiors. The equation of state of cold baryon-rich matter is constructed by combining a relativistic mean-field model for the hadronic phase and the MIT Bag model for the deconfined phase. In a narrow parameter range two sequences of compact stars (twin stars), which differ by the size of the quark core, have been found. We demonstrate the possibility of a rapid transition between the twin stars with the energy release of about $10^{52}$ ergs. This transition should be accompanied by the prompt neutrino burst and the delayed gamma-ray burst.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that, within a narrow parameter range of a specific hybrid equation-of-state model (NLZY for hadronic matter and MIT Bag model for quark matter), two distinct sequences of stable compact stars (twin stars) can exist, and a rapid transition between them can release about 10^52 erg of energy. The authors explicitly note that this picture is not guaranteed to correspond to reality, as such twin-star solutions appear only in very exceptional cases among the many models they studied.

## Method

The authors constructed the equation of state of cold baryon-rich matter by combining the NLZ relativistic mean-field model (including hyperons and hyperon-hyperon interactions via additional σ* and φ meson fields) for the hadronic phase with the MIT Bag model for the deconfined quark phase. They applied the Gibbs conditions for charge-neutral, β-equilibrated matter at zero temperature to construct a mixed phase, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain static, spherically symmetric stellar configurations for various values of the bag constant B.

## Key numbers

- The mixed phase exists between baryon densities ρ_B ≈ 1.5 ρ_0 and ρ_B ≈ 5.1 ρ_0 (for B^{1/4} = 180 MeV), where ρ_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3} is nuclear saturation density.
- The first sequence of hybrid stars has maximum mass M_max = 1.36 M_⊙ and radii R > 13 km, with central densities up to 0.77 fm^{-3}.
- The second sequence of hybrid stars has masses in the narrow interval 1.35 < M/M_⊙ < 1.38 and radii 10.2 km < R < 12.3 km, with central densities between 0.9 fm^{-3} and 1.53 fm^{-3}.
- The energy released in the transition between twin stars is ΔE ≈ 6 × 10^{-3} M_⊙ ≈ 10^{52} erg.
- The prompt neutrino burst is estimated to contain N_ν ≈ 2 × 10^{55} neutrinos, with mean energy ∼ 100 MeV and maximum energy E_ν^{max} ≈ 150 MeV.
- The quark core is estimated to be heated to temperature T_0 ≈ 40 MeV, while the emission temperature at the stellar surface is T_em ∼ 2 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hybrid stars containing both hadrons and quarks (e.g., Glendenning 1997, Steiner et al. 2000, Hanauske et al. 2001, Schaffner-Bielich et al. 2002, Burgio et al. 2002) by identifying a specific model combination (NLZY and MIT Bag) that produces twin-star solutions. The instability associated with a first-order phase transition in hadronic matter was first studied by Migdal (1979) and later by Kämpfer (1981) and Haensel (1989). The paper proposes that the twin-star collapse could serve as an engine for gamma-ray bursts, and notes that the predicted maximum masses (1.35–1.38 M_⊙) agree with observed pulsar masses clustered near 1.4 M_⊙.
