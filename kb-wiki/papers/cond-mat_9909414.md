# Perturbation of a lattice spectral band by a nearby resonance

> A soluble model of weakly coupled "molecular" and "nuclear" Hamiltonians is studied in order to exhibit explicitly the mechanism leading to the enhancement of fusion probability in case of a narrow near-threshold nuclear resonance. We, further, consider molecular cells of this type being arranged in lattice structures. It is shown that if the real part of the narrow nuclear resonance lies within the molecular band generated by the intercellular interaction, an enhancement, proportional to the inverse width of the nuclear resonance, is to be expected.

## Key claim

The paper shows that if the real part of a narrow nuclear resonance lies within a molecular spectral band generated by intercellular interaction in a lattice, the decay rate (fusion probability) of molecular states is enhanced proportionally to the inverse width of the nuclear resonance, but this enhancement depends on the quasimomentum distribution of the initial lattice state and the condition that the nuclear resonance position falls inside the band.

## Method

The authors construct a soluble two-channel Friedrichs-type model Hamiltonian consisting of a nuclear channel with continuous spectrum and a one-dimensional molecular channel with a discrete eigenvalue embedded in that continuum. They first analyze the two-channel case analytically, then extend the model to infinite one-dimensional and multi-dimensional lattices where molecular cells interact via the molecular channel only, using Fourier transform and resolvent techniques to study the time evolution of initially pure molecular states.

## Key numbers

- The molecular resonance width in the presence of a nearby nuclear resonance is given by \(\Gamma_R^{(m)} \cong 4\,\Real a / \Gamma_R^{(1)}\) when the molecular energy \(\lambda_2\) coincides with the real part \(E_R^{(1)}\) of the nuclear resonance (Eq. GmFin).
- The molecular width enhancement ratio relative to the case without a nearby nuclear resonance is \(\Gamma_R^{(m)} \sim \Gamma_R^{(2)} \cdot (C_a/c_\mathcal{D}) / \Gamma_R^{(1)}\) (Eq. gmg2).
- The exponential decay of the molecular state occurs in the time interval \(0 \leq t < T\) with \(T \sim (2/\Gamma_R^{(m)}) \, |\ln \max |\widetilde{\varepsilon}(t)||\) (text after Eq. ProbGamma).
- The molecular survival probability decays as \(P_{\rm mol}(t) \cong \exp\{-\Gamma_R^{(m)} t\} \cong \exp\{-4|\Real a| t / \Gamma_R^{(1)}\}\) (Eq. Exponent).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier studies (Motovilov, Belyaev 1998; Belyaev, Motovilov 1999) on near-threshold nuclear resonances enhancing molecular fusion, previously applied to specific systems like LiD and H₂O with ¹⁷O (Belyaev, Motovilov, Sandhas 1996-1998) and the muon-catalyzed fusion case in dtμ (Breunlich et al. 1989). The lattice generalization enables the possibility of fine-tuning molecular energies by exciting crystalline structures to match nuclear resonance positions, which could apply to molecular crystals where nuclear constituents exhibit narrow near-threshold resonances.
