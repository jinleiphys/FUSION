# The Unitary Fermi Gas: From Monte Carlo to Density Functionals

> In this chapter, we describe three related studies of the universal physics of two-component unitary Fermi gases with resonant short-ranged interactions. First we discuss an ab initio auxiliary field quantum Monte Carlo technique for calculating thermodynamic properties of the unitary gas from first principles. We then describe in detail a Density Functional Theory (DFT) fit to these thermodynamic properties: the Superfluid Local Density Approximation (SLDA) and its Asymmetric (ASLDA) generalization. We present several applications, including vortex structure, trapped systems, and a supersolid Larkin-Ovchinnikov (FFLO/LOFF) state. Finally, we discuss the time-dependent extension to the density functional (TDDFT) which can describe quantum dynamics in these systems, including non-adiabatic evolution, superfluid to normal transitions and other modes not accessible in traditional frameworks such as a Landau-Ginzburg, Gross-Pitaevskii, or quantum hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The unitary Fermi gas is stable and its universal physics can be quantitatively described by a single dimensionless parameter (the Bertsch parameter ξ) only in the formal limit where the range of the interaction goes to zero and the inverse scattering length goes to zero; at finite temperatures all thermodynamic properties become universal functions of the dimensionless variable T/εF.

## Method

The authors perform ab initio auxiliary field quantum Monte Carlo (AFQMC) simulations of a two-component unitary Fermi gas on a three-dimensional cubic spatial lattice with periodic boundary conditions, using a contact (zero-range) interaction regularized by the lattice momentum cutoff. They apply a discrete Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to convert the interacting fermion problem into a sum over auxiliary fields, then use the Metropolis algorithm to sample field configurations, with singular value decomposition (SVD) used to stabilize the algorithm at low temperatures. The resulting thermodynamic data are used to fit a Density Functional Theory (DFT) called the Superfluid Local Density Approximation (SLDA) and its asymmetric generalization (ASLDA), which are then extended to time-dependent phenomena.

## Key numbers

- The ground state energy of the unitary gas is given by E_gs = (3/5) ε_F N ξ, where ξ is the Bertsch parameter (a single dimensionless constant describing all non-perturbative effects in the unitary limit)
- The Fermi momentum is defined as p_F = ħ k_F = ħ (3π² n)^(1/3), where n is the total density
- The effective range generated by the lattice interaction is r_eff = 4/(π k_c), where k_c is the momentum cutoff
- The condition for the unitary regime requires r_0 ≪ n^(-1/3) ≪ |a|, where r_0 is the interaction range, n is the density, and a is the scattering length
- The total energy of the normal phase above the critical temperature T_c exceeds the ground state energy by only about 20%

## Context

This work extends the initial mean-field studies of the BCS-BEC crossover by Eagles (1969) and Leggett (1980), which neglected the dominant particle-hole (Hartree-Fock) contributions to the total energy. The authors build on the stability proof by Baker (1999) and the first energy calculations by Carlson and collaborators (2003), as well as the experimental realization of a degenerate unitary gas by O'Hara and Thomas (2002). The DFT approach (SLDA/ASLDA) enables studies of phenomena inaccessible to quantum Monte Carlo, including vortex structure, trapped systems, supersolid Larkin-Ovchinnikov (FFLO/LOFF) states, and time-dependent dynamics such as superfluid-to-normal transitions.
