# Surface diffuseness correction in global mass formula

> By taking into account the surface diffuseness correction for unstable nuclei, the accuracy of the macroscopic-microscopic mass formula is further improved. The rms deviation with respect to essentially all the available mass data falls to 298 keV, crossing the 0.3 MeV accuracy threshold for the first time within the mean-field framework. Considering the surface effect of the symmetry potential which plays an important role in the evolution of the "neutron skin" toward the "neutron halo" of nuclei approaching the neutron drip line, we obtain an optimal value of the symmetry energy coefficient J=30.16 MeV. With an accuracy of 258 keV for all the available neutron separation energies and of 237 keV for the alpha-decay Q-values of super-heavy nuclei, the proposed mass formula is particularly important not only for the reliable description of the r-process of nucleosynthesis but also for the study of the synthesis of super-heavy nuclei.

## Key claim

By introducing a surface diffuseness correction for unstable nuclei into the macroscopic-microscopic Weizsäcker-Skyrme (WS) mass formula, the root-mean-square (rms) deviation with respect to all available mass data falls to 298 keV, crossing the 0.3 MeV accuracy threshold for the first time within the mean-field framework; the improvement is most pronounced for nuclei far from stability, where the correction factor is largest.

## Method

The authors modified the WS mass formula by making the surface diffuseness parameter \(a\) of the Woods-Saxon single-particle potential isospin-dependent, using a correction factor \(\varepsilon = (I-I_0)^2 - I^4\) where \(I=(N-Z)/A\) and \(I_0\) is the isospin asymmetry along the beta-stability line. They also introduced corresponding correction factors \(f_s\) and \(f_d\) for the macroscopic symmetry energy and the microscopic shell correction, respectively. The 18 model parameters were optimized by minimizing the rms deviation against 2353 measured nuclear masses from the AME2012 atomic mass evaluation, using a combination of downhill searching and simulated annealing algorithms.

## Key numbers

* Rms deviation for all 2353 measured masses: 298 keV (WS4 formula), reduced from 335 keV for the previous WS3 formula.
* Rms deviation for the 219 "new" nuclei first appearing in AME2012: 346 keV (WS4), compared to 424 keV (WS3), an improvement of 78 keV.
* Rms deviation for 286 nuclei with \(|I-I_0| > 0.1\): 444 keV (WS4), compared to 516 keV (WS3).
* Rms deviation for all 2199 measured neutron separation energies: 258 keV (WS4).
* Rms deviation for alpha-decay Q-values of 46 super-heavy nuclei (Z >= 106): 237 keV (WS4).
* Optimal symmetry energy coefficient at saturation: \(J = 30.16\) MeV.
* With additional radial basis function (RBF) corrections, the rms deviation for all 2353 masses falls to 170 keV and for the 219 new nuclei to 155 keV.

## Context

This work extends the macroscopic-microscopic WS mass formula series (Wang 2010, Wang 2011, Liu 2011) by incorporating a surface diffuseness correction for the first time, addressing a limitation noted in all previous global mass formulas including the UNEDF (Erler 2012) and the Duflo-Zuker formula (Duflo and Zuker 1995). The resulting WS4 formula achieves the best accuracy within the mean-field framework, with the symmetry energy coefficient \(J=30.16\) MeV consistent with the value of 30.0 MeV from the latest Skyrme HFB mass formulas (Goriely 2013, HFB-27). The improved description of neutron-rich nuclei enables more reliable predictions for the r-process of nucleosynthesis and for the synthesis and decay properties of super-heavy nuclei.
