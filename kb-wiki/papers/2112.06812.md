# Jet quenching in glasma

> We discuss the transverse momentum broadening of hard probes traversing an evolving glasma, which is the earliest phase of the matter produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The coefficient $\hat q$ is calculated using the Fokker-Planck equation, and an expansion in the proper time $τ$ which is applied to describe the temporal evolution of the glasma. The correlators of the chromodynamic fields that determine the Fokker-Planck collision term, which in turn provides $\hat q$, are computed to fifth order in $τ$. The momentum broadening is shown to rapidly grow in time and reach a magnitude of several ${\rm GeV^2/fm}$. We show that the transient pre-equilibrium phase provides a contribution to the energy loss of hard probes which is comparable to that of the long lasting, hydrodynamically evolving, equilibrium phase.

# Jet quenching in glasma

## Key claim

The transient glasma phase contributes to the transverse momentum broadening of hard probes at a level comparable to the entire equilibrium quark-gluon plasma phase, contradicting the previous belief that pre-equilibrium effects can be safely neglected. The claim is hedged by the use of a proper-time expansion to fifth order, an assumed linear interpolation between the glasma and hydrodynamic phases, and the neglect of the gauge-linking operator in the field correlators (estimated to produce a 16% effect).

## Method

The authors compute the jet quenching coefficient $\hat{q}$ for hard probes traversing the glasma by solving the Fokker-Planck equation whose collision term depends on correlators of chromodynamic fields. The glasma fields are expanded in powers of proper time $\tau$ using a recursive solution of the classical Yang-Mills equations, with the correlators of pre-collision gauge potentials computed in the Glasma Graph approximation (Wick's theorem applied to products of pre-collision potentials). The field correlators entering the Fokker-Planck collision term are evaluated to fifth order in $\tau$, and the resulting $\hat{q}$ is obtained as a function of proper time for a quark moving at the speed of light perpendicular to the collision axis.

## Key numbers

- The momentum broadening coefficient reaches $\hat{q} \approx 6~\mathrm{GeV^2/fm}$ at $\tau = 0.06~\mathrm{fm}$ for a quark with $N_c=3$, $g=1$, $Q_s=2~\mathrm{GeV}$, and infrared regulator $m=0.2~\mathrm{GeV}$.
- The ratio of non-equilibrium to equilibrium accumulated transverse momentum broadening is $\Delta p_T^2|^{\mathrm{neq}} / \Delta p_T^2|^{\mathrm{eq}} = 0.93$ for a path length $L = 10~\mathrm{fm}$, using $\hat{q}_{\mathrm{max}} = 6~\mathrm{GeV^2/fm}$, $t_{\mathrm{max}} = 0.06~\mathrm{fm}$, $t_0 = 0.6~\mathrm{fm}$, and $\hat{q}_0 = 1.4~\mathrm{GeV^2/fm}$.
- The equilibrium quark-gluon plasma value from JETSCAPE (2021) is $2 < \hat{q}/T^3 < 4$, giving $\hat{q} \approx 1.0~\mathrm{GeV^2/fm}$ at $T=450~\mathrm{MeV}$ and $\hat{q} \approx 0.05~\mathrm{GeV^2/fm}$ at $T=150~\mathrm{MeV}$.
- The ensemble average of the link operator per color degree of freedom differs from unity by $0.16$ at $t' = 0.06~\mathrm{fm}$ with $N_c=3$, $Q_s=2~\mathrm{GeV}$, $m=200~\mathrm{MeV}$, and ${\bf v}^2=1$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous lowest-order calculation (Carrington 2020) to fifth order in the proper-time expansion and provides a systematic comparison of glasma and equilibrium contributions to jet quenching. It contradicts the standard assumption that pre-equilibrium effects can be ignored (JETSCAPE 2021) and supports recent kinetic theory calculations (Das 2015, Das 2017, Li 2020, Song 2019, Song 2020, Kumar 2021) suggesting sizable pre-equilibrium contributions. The results are consistent with real-time CGC lattice simulations (Ipp 2020) that show similar growth and magnitude of $\hat{q}$ over the same proper-time range. The work enables quantitative estimates of the glasma contribution to jet quenching observables at RHIC and the LHC, and provides a benchmark for future non-perturbative calculations.
