# Quadrupole and Hexadecapole Correlations in Rotating Nuclei Studied within the Single-j Shell Model

> The influence of quadrupole and hexadecapole residual interactions on rotational bands is investigated in a single-j shell model. An exact shell-model diagonalization of quadrupole-plus-hexadecapole interaction can sometimes produce a staggering of energy levels in the yrast bands.

# Quadrupole and Hexadecapole Correlations in Rotating Nuclei Studied within the Single-j Shell Model

## Key claim
The hexadecapole-hexadecapole residual interaction can sometimes produce a staggering of energy levels in the yrast sequence of a single-j shell model, but long and regular ΔI=2 staggering sequences are not obtained. This negative result suggests that the experimental ΔI=2 staggering observed in superdeformed bands probably cannot be explained in terms of coupling between rotation and hexadecapole vibrations.

## Method
The authors performed exact shell-model diagonalization of a quadrupole-plus-hexadecapole Hamiltonian for identical nucleons in a single-j shell (j=19/2, N=8 and 10 particles), with the hexadecapole coupling strength χ4 varied from 0 to the self-consistent limit of 1. They also solved the same Hamiltonian using the cranking Hartree-Fock (HF) method to extract intrinsic quadrupole and hexadecapole moments Q2μ and Q4μ as functions of angular momentum, and analyzed the staggering using a second-difference filter Δ²Eγ.

## Key numbers
- The self-consistent ratio χ4 = χ̄4/χ̄2 is equal to 165/176, very close to unity, estimated from the harmonic oscillator approximation with nuclear radius R = 1.2 A^(1/3) fm.
- For j=19/2 and N=10, the maximum angular momentum Imax = 50; for N=8, Imax = 48.
- For χ4=1, values of Δ²Eγ are an order of magnitude larger than for χ4=0.5.
- The amplitude of staggering observed in experimental superdeformed bands, |ε(I)|, is of the order of 100 eV.
- At the fully aligned state for N=8 (I=48), the quadrupole deformation parameters approach γ2 = -60° and β2 = |Q20x|, with hexadecapole parameters approaching δ4^(x) and γ4^(x) (axial with respect to the x-axis, δ4 ≈ 0.7017 rad or 40°20').

## Context
This work extends the single-j shell model previously used for quadrupole collectivity by Bel59, Mul64, and Bur95, and directly addresses the conjecture from Fli93 and Ced94 that hexadecapole (C4) symmetry might explain the ΔI=2 staggering observed in superdeformed bands of nuclei such as 149Gd, 153Dy, 194Hg, and 192Tl. The results contradict phenomenological models by Ham94, Mag95, and Mac95 that assumed a C4 symmetry axis, and are consistent with microscopic shell-correction calculations by Rag94, Fra94, and Don96 that predicted very weak collectivity for non-axial λ=4 fields. The study enables future work to focus on alternative mechanisms for staggering, such as band crossings or pairing correlations, rather than hexadecapole vibrations.
