# Far-from-equilibrium attractors in kinetic theory with two different relaxation times

> We solve a Boltzmann equation for massless quark and gluon fluids in a transversally homogeneous, longitudinally boost-invariant expansion. Quarks can be out of chemical equilibrium and the relaxation times of the two species are assumed to be connected by Casimir scaling. We numerically calculate moments of the distribution functions, identifying their early- and late-time attractors and reconstructing also the full distributions. These attractors appear when the system is still far from local thermalization, before hydrodynamics traditionally would be expected to apply. We also analyze the evolution of entropy production for different initial momentum anisotropies and quark abundances.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that far-from-equilibrium attractors exist in a two-component (quark-gluon) kinetic theory with different relaxation times related by Casimir scaling, but only moments with sensitivity to longitudinal momentum (m > 0) exhibit early-time attractors, while the scaled energy density shows no significant attractor behavior.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann equation in relaxation time approximation (RTA) for massless quarks and gluons undergoing Bjorken flow (longitudinally boost-invariant expansion), with quark and gluon relaxation times related by the Casimir scaling factor C_R = 4/9. They initialize the system using the Strickland-Romatschke form with a quark fugacity parameter γ_q and anisotropy parameter ξ_0, then numerically compute the exact solution for the one-particle distribution functions and their moments M^{nm} using an integral formulation with damping functions D(τ_2, τ_1). The temperature evolution is determined self-consistently through a Landau matching condition that couples the quark and gluon energy densities.

## Key numbers

- Casimir scaling factor C_R ≡ τ_eq,g/τ_eq,q = 4/9, with gluon internal degrees of freedom g_g = 16 and quark internal degrees of freedom g_q = 18
- Initial conditions: τ_0 = 0.15 fm/c, T_0 = 600 MeV, η/s = 0.2
- Ratio of gluon to quark degrees of freedom r ≡ g_g/g_q = 8/9
- Two relaxation times result in a modest +15% increase in entropy production by the end of the simulation compared to single relaxation time
- Variation of initial quark abundance from γ_q,0 = 0.1 to γ_q,0 = 1 produces a final 40% increase in entropy production
- Hydrodynamization expected when scaled time ω̄ ≡ τ/τ_eq ≳ 5, where individual runs collapse onto the late-time attractor

## Context

This work extends the single-relaxation-time RTA attractor studies of Strickland (2017) and Florkowski (2012) to a two-component system with quarks and gluons having different relaxation times, motivated by the chemical non-equilibrium in heavy-ion collisions where gluons dominate initially as described by Biro (1993). The results enable more realistic modeling of the pre-hydrodynamic phase in heavy-ion collisions, showing that attractor behavior persists even with species-dependent relaxation times, and that entropy production depends strongly on initial quark abundance rather than just the relaxation time ratio.
