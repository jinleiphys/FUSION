# Electric conductivity with the magnetic field and the chiral anomaly in a holographic QCD model

> We calculate the electric conductivity $σ$ in deconfined QCD matter using a holographic QCD model, i.e., the Sakai-Sugimoto Model with varying magnetic field $B$ and chiral anomaly strength. After confirming that our estimated $σ$ for $B=0$ is consistent with the lattice-QCD results, we study the case with $B\neq 0$ in which the coefficient $α$ in the Chern-Simons term controls the chiral anomaly strength. Our results imply that the transverse conductivity, $σ_\perp$, is suppressed to be $\lesssim 70\%$ at $B\sim 1\,\mathrm{GeV}^2$ as compared to the $B=0$ case when the temperature is fixed as $T= 0.2\,\mathrm{GeV}$. Since the Sakai-Sugimoto Model has massless fermions, the longitudinal conductivity, $σ_\parallel$, with $B\neq 0$ should diverge due to production of the matter chirality. Yet, it is possible to extract a regulated part out from $σ_\parallel$ with an extra condition to neutralize the matter chirality. This regulated quantity is interpreted as an Ohmic part of $σ_\parallel$. We show that the longitudinal Ohmic conductivity increases with increasing $B$ for small $α$, while it is suppressed with larger $B$ for physical $α=3/4$ due to anomaly induced interactions.

## Key claim

The longitudinal electric conductivity in the Sakai-Sugimoto Model diverges at zero frequency due to massless fermions and chirality production, but a finite Ohmic part can be extracted by imposing an extra condition to neutralize the matter chirality; this Ohmic part shows nonmonotonic dependence on magnetic field and the chiral anomaly strength, with suppression at large B for the physical anomaly parameter α = 3/4.

## Method

The authors use the Sakai-Sugimoto Model, a holographic QCD model with D8/anti-D8 branes for left- and right-handed quarks and D4 branes for gluons, in the deconfined phase above the critical temperature Tc = MKK/(2π). They solve the equations of motion derived from the Dirac-Born-Infeld action and Chern-Simons term for the gauge fields in the linear response regime, imposing infalling boundary conditions at the black hole horizon and extracting the electric conductivity from the on-shell action via the GKP-W relation. For the longitudinal case, they remove the divergent zero-mode contribution by setting the net chirality charge to zero, isolating a regulated Ohmic part.

## Key numbers

* At B = 0, the dimensionless conductivity σ/(Ce T) at T = 1.1 Tc is 0.206, at T = 1.3 Tc is 0.243, and at T = 1.5 Tc is 0.281, consistent with lattice-QCD results from Ding (2016) which give ranges 0.201-0.703, 0.203-0.388, and 0.218-0.413 respectively.
* The transverse conductivity σ⊥ is suppressed to ≲ 70% at B ∼ 1 GeV² compared to the B = 0 case at fixed temperature T = 0.2 GeV.
* The model parameters are fixed as λ = 16.63 and MKK = 0.95 GeV from the ρ meson mass and pion decay constant.
* The zero-field conductivity formula is σ/(Ce T) = (λ/(9π²)) (T/Tc), giving the T² scaling behavior.

## Context

This work extends holographic conductivity calculations in the Sakai-Sugimoto Model from Bergman (2008) at B = 0 and Lifschytz (2009) at B ≠ 0, where the longitudinal conductivity was found to diverge with massless fermions. It provides nonperturbative estimates that complement perturbative QCD calculations in the lowest Landau level approximation by Hattori (2016) and full Landau level sums by Fukushima (2017, 2019). The results enable quantitative comparison between anomaly-induced negative magnetoresistance and ordinary Ohmic contributions, addressing the gap between theory and experimental observations of the Chiral Magnetic Effect in heavy-ion collisions and Weyl semimetals.
