# Light clusters and the pasta phase

> The effects of including light clusters in nuclear matter at low densities are investigated within four different parametrizations of relativistic models at finite temperature. Both homogeneous and inhomogeneous matter (pasta phase) are described for neutral nuclear matter with fixed proton fractions. We discuss the effect of the density dependence of the symmetry energy, the temperature and the proton fraction on the non-homogeneous matter forming the inner crust of proto-neutron stars. It is shown that the number of nucleons in the clusters, the cluster proton fraction and the sizes of the Wigner Seitz cell and of the cluster are very sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy.

# Light clusters and the pasta phase

## Key claim
The inclusion of light clusters (deuterons, tritons, helions, and alpha particles) in relativistic mean-field models lowers the free energy of nuclear matter at subsaturation densities, but the effect is small (less than about 1 MeV per particle) and is most visible only at very low densities, below the onset of the pasta phase; the pasta phase itself is more strongly affected by the density dependence of the symmetry energy than by the presence of light clusters.

## Method
The authors use four parametrizations of the non-linear Walecka model (NL3, NL3ωρ, FSUGold, and IU-FSU) within a relativistic mean-field and Thomas-Fermi approximation at finite temperature. They describe both homogeneous matter and inhomogeneous pasta structures (droplets, rods, slabs, tubes, bubbles) using the coexisting-phases method with a sharp interface, surface tension parametrized as a function of neutron excess and temperature, and a Wigner-Seitz cell geometry. Light clusters are included as bosons (alpha particles and deuterons) and fermions (tritons and helions) with fixed binding energies and meson-cluster couplings proportional to mass number.

## Key numbers
- Binding energies used: B_t = 8.482 MeV (triton), B_h = 7.718 MeV (helion), B_α = 28.296 MeV (alpha), B_d = 2.224 MeV (deuteron)
- Saturation densities of the models: ρ₀ = 0.148 fm⁻³ for NL3, NL3ωρ, and FSUGold; ρ₀ = 0.155 fm⁻³ for IU-FSU
- Symmetry energy slope L: 118.32 MeV (NL3), 55.23 MeV (NL3ωρ), 60.39 MeV (FSUGold), 47.2 MeV (IU-FSU)
- Pasta phase onset density for NL3 at T = 5 MeV and Y_pg = 0.2 occurs at approximately ρ ∼ 0.001 fm⁻³
- Transition to dense homogeneous matter occurs above approximately 0.08 fm⁻³ for the same conditions
- Surface tension parametrization is valid up to T = 10 MeV for Y_pg > 0.2

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Avancini et al., 2010) that included only alpha particles in the pasta phase, by adding deuterons, tritons, and helions. It builds on the single-nucleus approximation used by Lattimer and Swesty (1991) and Shen et al. (1998), and contrasts with statistical models such as those of Hempel et al. (2010) and Raduta and Gulminelli (2010) that use an excluded-volume approach. The results enable improved modeling of the inner crust of proto-neutron stars and supernova matter, particularly for understanding how the symmetry energy slope affects cluster sizes, proton fractions, and Wigner-Seitz cell dimensions.
