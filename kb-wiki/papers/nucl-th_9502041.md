# Relativistic treatment of the charged-current reaction ${}^{12}{\rm C}(ν_μ,μ^{-})$ near threshold

> We compute the cross section for the charged-current reaction ${}^{12}{\rm C}(ν_μ,μ^{-})$ near threshold using a relativistic mean-field formalism. A reduced value of the nucleon mass in the medium --- coupled to the finite muon mass --- leads to a 30\% reduction in the cross section relative to a free Fermi-gas estimate. Isovector RPA correlations, which are strongly repulsive at these momentum transfers, reduce the cross section by an additional 15-30\%. Hence, relativistic nuclear-structure effects can account for the more than a factor-of-two reduction in the cross section recently reported by the LSND collaboration.

# Relativistic treatment of the charged-current reaction ${}^{12}{\rm C}(ν_μ,μ^{-})$ near threshold

## Key claim
Relativistic nuclear-structure effects, specifically an in-medium reduction of the nucleon mass combined with repulsive isovector RPA correlations, can account for the more than factor-of-two reduction in the inclusive cross section reported by the LSND collaboration, but only when the finite muon mass prevents the quasielastic strength from being fully exhausted.

## Method
The authors computed the inclusive charged-current cross section for ${}^{12}{\rm C}(ν_μ,μ^{-})$ near threshold using a relativistic mean-field approximation to the Walecka model, treating the nucleus as a Fermi gas with Fermi momentum $k_F=225$ MeV. They employed the impulse approximation with single-nucleon form factors parameterized from on-shell data, and incorporated nuclear-structure effects through a reduced effective nucleon mass $M^*$ and relativistic random-phase approximation (RPA) correlations with two choices of residual interaction: a conventional $\pi+\rho+g'$ interaction and a less repulsive alternative motivated by earlier work.

## Key numbers
- The flux-averaged inclusive cross section in the free Fermi-gas limit is $\langle\sigma_{FG}\rangle = 8.11 \times 10^{-40} \text{ cm}^2$ for muons and $23.13 \times 10^{-40} \text{ cm}^2$ for electrons.
- With a reduced nucleon mass ($M^*/M=0.68$) and the conventional RPA interaction [RPA(I)], the flux-averaged cross section for muons is $\langle\sigma\rangle = 3.16 \times 10^{-40} \text{ cm}^2$, or 0.39 of the Fermi-gas value.
- The impulse approximation with reduced mass ($M^*/M=0.68$) alone gives a cross section ratio of 0.70 for muons and 0.84 for electrons, relative to the free Fermi gas.
- The conventional RPA interaction [RPA(I)] with full mass ($M^*/M=1.00$) gives a cross section ratio of 0.56 for muons and 0.64 for electrons.
- The less repulsive RPA interaction [RPA(II)] with reduced mass gives a cross section ratio of 0.53 for muons and 0.71 for electrons.
- The threshold neutrino energy for the reaction is $E_\nu = 111.6$ MeV in the Fermi gas model.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier relativistic mean-field study of neutrino-nucleus scattering (Kim, Piekarewicz, and Horowitz, 1994) to low momentum transfers near threshold, where Pauli blocking, binding-energy corrections, and RPA correlations become important. The calculations aim to explain the factor-of-two reduction in the inclusive cross section reported by the LSND collaboration (Albert et al., 1994), which could not be accounted for by simple Fermi-gas estimates. The results have implications for the atmospheric neutrino anomaly observed by the Kamiokande collaboration (Hirata et al., 1992), as similar relativistic nuclear-structure effects may affect the $\nu_\mu/\nu_e$ ratio used in neutrino oscillation searches.
