# Finite-size effects and the search for the critical endpoint of QCD

> Taking into account the finiteness of the system created in heavy ion collisions, we show sizable results for the modifications of the chiral phase diagram at volume scales typically encountered in current experiments and demonstrate the applicability of finite-size scaling as a tool in the experimental search for the critical endpoint. Using data from RHIC and SPS and assuming finite-size scaling, we find that RHIC data from 200 GeV down to 19.6 GeV is only consistent with a critical point at μ\gtrsim 510 MeV. We also present predictions for the fluctuations at lower energies currently being investigated in the Beam Energy Scan program.

## Key claim

Finite-size effects in heavy-ion collisions shift the apparent critical endpoint to a pseudocritical point whose location depends on system size and boundary conditions; using finite-size scaling analysis of RHIC data, the authors find that data from 200 GeV down to 19.6 GeV are consistent with a critical point only at baryochemical potential μ ≳ 510 MeV, but this conclusion depends on assuming a quadratic polynomial fit for the scaling function.

## Method

The authors used the linear sigma model coupled to two-flavor quarks to compute finite-size modifications of the chiral phase diagram for system sizes L between 2 and 12 fm with periodic and antiperiodic boundary conditions. They then applied finite-size scaling (FSS) analysis to STAR data on transverse momentum fluctuations σ_{p_T}/⟨p_T⟩ from RHIC collisions at √s_{NN} = 19.6, 62.4, 130, and 200 GeV, binning events by centrality to obtain eight system sizes from L = 3.4 to 12.4 fm. The scaled observable L^{-γ_x/ν} × (σ_{p_T}/⟨p_T⟩) was plotted versus μ, with ν = 2/3 (Ising universality class) and γ_x ≈ 1, and polynomial fits were constrained to force all curves to cross at a common μ = μ_crit.

## Key numbers

- For periodic boundary conditions, the critical endpoint chemical potential μ_CEP is about 30% larger than for antiperiodic boundary conditions.
- At μ = 0, the crossover temperature increases by up to ~80% when L = 2 fm with periodic boundary conditions.
- System sizes used in the FSS analysis: L = 12.4, 11.1, 9.6, 8.0, 6.8, 5.6, 4.5, and 3.4 fm, corresponding to centrality bins from 0-5% to 60-70%.
- With a quadratic polynomial fit, the data are consistent with a critical point at μ ∼ 510 MeV, corresponding to √s_{NN} = 5.75 GeV.
- With a linear fit, the crossing occurs at μ ∼ 3 GeV.
- The analysis excludes a critical point at μ below 450 MeV.
- Predictions are given for σ_{p_T}/⟨p_T⟩ at √s_{NN} = 11.5, 7.7, and 5 GeV as a function of number of participants N_part.

## Context

This work extends earlier finite-size studies of the QCD phase diagram using effective models (Palhares 2009) and lattice simulations (Bazavov 2007) by demonstrating that finite-size scaling can be applied pragmatically to heavy-ion collision data. It builds on the theoretical framework for critical fluctuations developed by Stephanov (1998, 2008) and the finite-size scaling formalism of Fisher and Brezin (1981, 1985). The results enable experimental searches for the QCD critical endpoint in the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC by providing a method to extract critical point location from centrality-dependent fluctuation measurements, and by making testable predictions for lower beam energies (11.5, 7.7, and 5 GeV).
