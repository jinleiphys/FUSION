# Detection of Supernova Neutrinos by Neutrino-Proton Elastic Scattering

> We propose that neutrino-proton elastic scattering, $őĹ+ p \to őĹ+ p$, can be used for the detection of supernova neutrinos in scintillator detectors. Though the proton recoil kinetic energy spectrum is soft, with $T_p \simeq 2 E_őĹ^2/M_p$, and the scintillation light output from slow, heavily ionizing protons is quenched, the yield above a realistic threshold is nearly as large as that from $\barőĹ_e + p \to e^+ + n$. In addition, the measured proton spectrum is related to the incident neutrino spectrum, which solves a long-standing problem of how to separately measure the total energy and temperature of $őĹ_őľ$, $őĹ_ŌĄ$, $\barőĹ_őľ$, and $\barőĹ_ŌĄ$. The ability to detect this signal would give detectors like KamLAND and Borexino a crucial and unique role in the quest to detect supernova neutrinos.

## Key claim

The authors propose that neutrino-proton elastic scattering (őĹ + p ‚Üí őĹ + p) can serve as a viable detection channel for supernova neutrinos in scintillator detectors, and crucially that the measured proton recoil spectrum allows separate determination of the total energy and temperature of őĹ_őľ, őĹ_ŌĄ, anti-őĹ_őľ, and anti-őĹ_ŌĄ, a long-standing problem in supernova neutrino detection, provided the detector threshold is low enough to overcome the soft proton recoil spectrum and scintillation light quenching.

## Method

The authors calculated the differential cross section for neutrino-proton elastic scattering using Standard Model neutral-current couplings, then convolved this with Fermi-Dirac neutrino spectra characteristic of supernova models (temperatures of 3.5, 5, and 8 MeV for different flavors). They applied Birk's Law with a quenching constant k_B = 0.015 cm/MeV (appropriate for KamLAND scintillator) to convert true proton kinetic energies into observable electron-equivalent energies, and performed Monte Carlo simulations with chi-squared fits to determine how well the total energy and temperature of the four heavy-flavor neutrino species could be extracted from the measured proton spectrum.

## Context

This work extends the problem of measuring supernova neutrino spectra from neutral-current reactions, which had previously been considered impossible because most neutral-current channels (such as őĹ + d ‚Üí őĹ + p + n in SNO or nuclear excitations) provide no information on the incident neutrino energy. The authors show that neutrino-proton elastic scattering in scintillator detectors like KamLAND (already operating) and Borexino (soon to operate) provides a unique solution, enabling separate measurement of the total energy and temperature of the heavy-flavor neutrinos that carry about 2/3 of the supernova binding energy. This capability is crucial for determining the neutron star mass from the total energy and for studying neutrino oscillations from the temperature, without requiring model-dependent assumptions as in prior approaches.
