# On the quark mass dependence of nucleon-nucleon S-wave scattering lengths

> In the framework of a Chiral effective theory with dibaryon fields, we calculate the pion mass dependence of the inverse scattering length of the nucleon-nucleon system in the $^3S_1$ channel at order ${(m_π^3 / Λ_χ^2)}\times (m_π^{1/2}m_N^{3/2}/8πf_π^2)^n$ for all $n\ge 0$. We show that certain sets of potentially large higher order contributions vanish. We discuss the difficulties of extending the proof to the $^1S_0$ channel. We apply our results to chiral extrapolations of current lattice data.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the $^3S_1$ channel, the resummation of multiple potential pion exchanges inside radiation pion loops, which naively contribute at order $\sqrt{m_\pi/\Lambda_\chi}$ and are numerically of order 1, can be performed and their total contribution to the inverse scattering length vanishes. This cancellation is attributed to a field redefinition of the dibaryon field, and the proof does not extend to the $^1S_0$ channel, where the potential remains non-local and singular.

## Method

The authors work within a chiral effective field theory for nucleon-nucleon interactions that includes dibaryon fields (isovector for $^1S_0$ and isoscalar for $^3S_1$). They match this theory to a pionful effective theory (pNNEFT) for energies below the pion mass, and then to a pionless effective theory ($\slashed{\pi}$NNEFT), extracting the scattering lengths from the dibaryon residual masses and nucleon-dibaryon vertices. The key technical step is the resummation of an infinite series of potential pion exchanges inside loops containing a radiation pion, which collapses into an effective energy-dependent four-nucleon vertex and an effective nucleon-dibaryon vertex.

## Key numbers

* The size of each bubble in the resummation series is $\sqrt{m_\pi}/\alpha \sim 1.19$, where $\alpha = 8\pi f_\pi^2 / (g_A^2 m_N^{3/2})$, making the series of order $\mathcal{O}(1)$ and requiring resummation.
* The LO fit parameters for the $^3S_1$ channel are $\zeta_1 = -98$ MeV and $\zeta_2 = 1.59 \times 10^{-3}$ MeV$^{-1}$, with a chi-squared per degree of freedom of 0.91.
* The NLO fit parameters for the $^3S_1$ channel are $\zeta_1 = -155$ MeV, $\zeta_2 = 3.83 \times 10^{-3}$ MeV$^{-1}$, and $\zeta_3 = 10.1 \times 10^{-6}$ MeV$^{-2}$, with a chi-squared per degree of freedom of 0.4.
* The NLO fit parameters for the $^1S_0$ channel are $\zeta_1 = -246$ MeV, $\zeta_2 = 4.56 \times 10^{-3}$ MeV$^{-1}$, and $\zeta_3 = 9.21 \times 10^{-6}$ MeV$^{-2}$, with a chi-squared per degree of freedom of 2.4.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the quark mass dependence of nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths using chiral effective theories, particularly the KSW approach (Kaplan, Savage, and Wise, 1998) and the dibaryon formulation (Soto and Tarrús, 2009). It addresses the problem that corrections of order $\sqrt{m_\pi/\Lambda_\chi}$ were found to be numerically large (Mondejar and Soto, 2006). The paper provides a chiral extrapolation formula for the $^3S_1$ channel that sums these contributions and shows they vanish, enabling more reliable fits to lattice data from the NPLQCD Collaboration (Beane et al., 2006, 2009). The results for the $^3S_1$ channel in the chiral limit (negative scattering length, unbounded system) agree with the later work of Chen et al. (2010) but differ from earlier predictions by Epelbaum et al. (2002) and Beane et al. (2002).
