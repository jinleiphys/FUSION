# Updated Astrophysical Equation-of-State Constraints on the Color-Superconducting Gap

> We summarize and update using new NICER measurements the results of arXiv:2401.16253, in which we used various astrophysical neutron-star observations to set an upper bound on the CFL color-superconducting gap in a range of baryon chemical potentials $μ_B \in [2.1,3.2]$, above those reached within neutron stars. We also corroborate the ``reasonable" constraint from arXiv:2401.16253 on the maximum value of the color-superconducting gap by performing a new Bayesian analysis using a prior that extends a two-segment Gaussian process connecting the whole density range between CEFT and pQCD.

## Key claim

The paper updates earlier astrophysical constraints on the CFL color-superconducting gap using new NICER measurements, finding that the 95% upper bounds remain very similar to those obtained before the new data were available, and corroborating the "reasonable" constraint through a new Bayesian analysis that uses a two-segment Gaussian process connecting the full density range between chiral effective field theory (CEFT) and perturbative QCD (pQCD).

## Method

The authors take the baryon density and pressure at a high chemical potential as the sum of the N2LO pQCD prediction and the leading-order CFL correction, then use thermodynamic consistency and a maximum sound speed to derive an upper bound on the CFL gap. They perform a Bayesian analysis using a Gaussian process to infer the neutron star equation of state from astrophysical observations, updating the posterior with new NICER mass-radius measurements of PSR J0740+6620 and J0614-3329, and corroborate the result using a separate two-Gaussian-process approach that matches low-density and high-density ensembles without explicitly imposing a sound speed constraint.

## Key numbers

- The CFL gap values from model estimates range between 20 and 250 MeV, typically around 50-100 MeV
- The analysis constrains the CFL gap in a range of baryon chemical potentials μ_B ∈ [2.1, 3.2] GeV
- The "reasonable" scenario uses a maximum sound speed squared c_s,max^2 = 1/2 above the matching point
- The CEFT matching point uses μ_L = 0.97 GeV and n_L = 1.1 n_s (where n_s is nuclear saturation density)
- The two-GP matching is performed at n_L = 10 n_s
- The prior on Δ is uniform between 0 and 1 GeV
- The pQCD renormalization scale uncertainty is marginalized over X ∈ (1/2, 2)

## Context

This work updates the analysis of Kurkela (2024) by incorporating new NICER measurements of PSR J0740+6620 from Dittmann (2024) and of J0614-3329 from Mauviard (2025), as well as MeerKAT mass data. The method extends the approach of Komoltsev (2021) who used pQCD to constrain the neutron star equation of state, but inverts it to constrain non-perturbative corrections to pQCD. The results enable future improvements from higher-order pQCD calculations (N3LO from Gorda (2023)) and from the recently calculated O(g^2 Δ^2) CFL correction by Geissel (2024), which yields a stronger constraint on the gap.
