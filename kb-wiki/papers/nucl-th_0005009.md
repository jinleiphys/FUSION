# One-body density matrix and momentum distribution in s-p and s-d shell nuclei

> Analytical expressions of the one- and two- body terms in the cluster expansion of the one-body density matrix and momentum distribution of the s-p and s-d shell nuclei with N=Z are derived. They depend on the harmonic oscillator parameter b and the parameter $β$ which originates from the Jastrow correlation function. These parameters have been determined by least squares fit to the experimental charge form factors. The inclusion of short-range correlations increases the high momentum component of the momentum distribution, $n({\bf k})$ for all nuclei we have considered while there is an A dependence of $n({\bf k})$ both at small values of k and the high momentum component. The A dependence of the high momentum component of $n({\bf k})$ becomes quite small when the nuclei $^{24}$Mg, $^{28}$Si and $^{32}$S are treated as 1d-2s shell nuclei having the occupation probability of the 2s-state as an extra free parameter in the fit to the form factors.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytical expressions for the one-body density matrix and momentum distribution of s-p and s-d shell nuclei with N=Z using a cluster expansion with Jastrow correlations, finding that the A-dependence of the high-momentum component of the momentum distribution becomes quite small when the nuclei 24Mg, 28Si and 32S are treated as 1d-2s shell nuclei with the occupation probability of the 2s-state as an extra free parameter in the fit to the form factors.

## Method

The authors employed a low-order approximation (LOA) within the Jastrow formalism, using a state-independent correlation function f(r)=1-exp(-βr²) and a factor cluster expansion to derive analytical expressions for the one-body density matrix and momentum distribution. The harmonic oscillator parameter b and the correlation parameter β were determined by least-squares fits to experimental charge form factors for closed shell nuclei (4He, 16O, 40Ca) and open shell nuclei (12C, 24Mg, 28Si, 32S). For the open shell nuclei 24Mg, 28Si and 32S, the analysis was performed both treating them as pure 1d shell nuclei and as 1d-2s shell nuclei with the 2s occupation probability as an additional free parameter.

## Key numbers

- The high-momentum tail of the momentum distribution appears for k > 2 fm⁻¹ for all nuclei considered
- The A-dependence of n(k) is studied in the region 2 fm⁻¹ < k < 5 fm⁻¹
- The correlation function parameter β and harmonic oscillator parameter b were determined by fitting to experimental charge form factors for 4He, 16O, 40Ca (closed shell) and 12C, 24Mg, 28Si, 32S (open shell)
- The depletion of single-particle states below the Fermi energy can be of order 15% or more, as cited from Quint (1987)

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Bohigas and Stringari (1980) and Dal Ri et al (1982) who evaluated short-range correlation effects on one- and two-body densities using LOA in the Jastrow formalism, and by Stoitsov et al (1993) who generalized the Jastrow correlation model to heavier nuclei. The present paper is a continuation of the authors' previous study (Massen, 1999) on form factors and densities of s-p and s-d shell nuclei, and provides analytical expressions that can be used for both closed and open shell nuclei, enabling calculations of overlap integrals and reactions beyond mean field theories as noted by Gaidarov (1999).
