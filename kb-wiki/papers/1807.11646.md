# Shells in a Toroidal Nucleus in the Intermediate Mass Region

> Attention is fixed on shells in toroidal nuclei in the intermediate mass region using a toroidal single-particle potential. We find that there are toroidal shells in the intermediate mass region with large single-particle energy gaps at various nucleon numbers located at different toroidal deformations characterized by the aspect ratios of toroidal major to minor radius. These toroidal shells provide extra stability at various toroidal deformations. Relative to a toroidal core, Bohr-Mottelson spin-aligning particle-hole excitations may be constructed to occupy the lowest single-particle Routhian energies to lead to toroidal high-spin isomers with different spins. Furthermore, as a nucleon in a toroidal nucleus possesses a vorticity quantum number, toroidal vortex nuclei may be constructed by making particle-hole excitations in which nucleons of one type of vorticity are promoted to populate un-occupied single-particle orbitals of the opposite vorticity. Methods for producing toroidal high-spin isomers and toroidal vortex nuclei are discussed.

## Key claim

Toroidal shells with large single-particle energy gaps exist in the intermediate mass region (nucleon numbers 30 to 96) at various toroidal deformations, but the authors note that these results come from a simplified harmonic oscillator toroidal shell model and that whether these states actually form local energy minima will require verification with more reliable microscopic models such as non-relativistic or relativistic mean-field calculations.

## Method

The authors used a toroidal single-particle harmonic oscillator potential with spin-orbit coupling, solved analytically in the large major radius approximation (R >> d) by expanding about the major radius and keeping terms up to second order. They calculated single-particle energies as a function of the aspect ratio R/d for nucleon numbers in the intermediate mass region, and then constructed single-particle Routhian energies as a function of cranking frequency to identify favorable configurations for toroidal high-spin isomers.

## Key numbers

- The toroidal shell model uses a harmonic oscillator frequency of ℏω₀ = 41 MeV / A^(1/3), modified by a density factor (ρ_toroidal/ρ₀) ≈ 0.82 for the intermediate mass region (A ≈ 60-160), with A = 110 used for numerical purposes
- Toroidal shells occur at aspect ratios R/d ranging from approximately 1.8 to 4.2, with nucleon numbers including (30), (42), (46), (50) at R/d ≈ 1.8; (48), (58), (68), (78) at R/d ≈ 2.9; and (56), (66), (76), (86), (96) at R/d ≈ 4.2
- At R/d = 2.9 and cranking frequency ℏω ≈ 0.4 MeV, favorable (N,I) configurations include (52,26), (68,26), and (74,37); at ℏω ≈ 0.6 MeV, favorable configurations include (54,39), (64,44), (72,50), and (74,52); at ℏω ≈ 1.0 MeV, favorable configurations include (48,59), (68,73), (68,82), and (80,100)
- The gross structure energy scale of ℏω_⊥ is of order 10 MeV, while the single-particle orbiting energies Λ_z²ℏ²/2mR² are of order 1 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of toroidal nuclei in light nuclei (such as Wong (1972, 1973) and self-consistent calculations for ²⁸Si by Cao (2018)) and in the superheavy region (such as Staszczak (2017, 2018) and Kosior (2017, 2018)) to the previously unexplored intermediate mass region. The results provide guidance for future self-consistent mean-field calculations and experimental searches for toroidal high-spin isomers and toroidal vortex nuclei, building on recent experimental evidence from Cao (2018) for resonances in the 7α disassembly of ²⁸Si that may indicate toroidal high-spin isomers.
