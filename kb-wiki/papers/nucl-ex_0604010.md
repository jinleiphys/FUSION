# Extracting nucleon strange and anapole form factors from world data

> The complete world set of parity violating electron scattering data up to Q^2~0.3 GeV^2 is analysed. We extract the current experimental determination of the strange electric and magnetic form factors of the proton, as well as the weak axial form factors of the proton and neutron, at Q^2 = 0.1 GeV^2. Within experimental uncertainties, we find that the strange form factors are consistent with zero, as are the anapole contributions to the axial form factors. Nevertheless, the correlation between the strange and anapole contributions suggest that there is only a small probability that these form factors all vanish simultaneously.

## Key claim

The strange electric and magnetic form factors of the proton, as well as the anapole contributions to the axial form factors, are each individually consistent with zero within experimental uncertainties at Q^2 = 0.1 GeV^2; however, the correlation between the strange and anapole contributions implies only an 8% probability that all these form factors vanish simultaneously.

## Method

The authors performed a global fit of all available parity-violating electron scattering (PVES) data up to Q^2 ~ 0.3 GeV^2 from the SAMPLE, HAPPEx, PVA4, and G0 collaborations, including both proton and deuteron targets. The theoretical asymmetry for each measurement was reconstructed using a linear combination of the weak axial form factors of the proton and neutron, the strange electric form factor, and the strange magnetic form factor, with coefficients that include the latest vector form factors and PDG radiative corrections. The Q^2 dependence of the form factors was parameterized by a Taylor series expansion (for the strange form factors) and a dipole form (for the axial form factors), allowing a simultaneous extraction of all four form factors from data for the first time.

## Key numbers

- At Q^2 = 0.1 GeV^2, the extracted strange electric form factor is G_E^s = -0.006 ± 0.041 (statistical only, from the full fit including all data up to Q^2 ~ 0.3 GeV^2)
- At Q^2 = 0.1 GeV^2, the extracted strange magnetic form factor is G_M^s = 0.12 ± 0.55 ± 0.07 (first error statistical, second error from correlated G0 systematic)
- The extracted proton weak axial form factor at Q^2 = 0.1 GeV^2 is tilde{g}_A^p = 0.05 ± 1.38 ∓ 0.29 (leading-order fit, Q^2 < 0.3 GeV^2)
- The extracted neutron weak axial form factor at Q^2 = 0.1 GeV^2 is tilde{g}_A^n = 2.61 ± 2.27 ∓ 0.37 (leading-order fit, Q^2 < 0.3 GeV^2)
- The probability that strange and anapole form factors are simultaneously zero is 8%
- The reduced chi-squared for the leading-order fit is chi^2 = 19.7/15 = 1.3

## Context

This work extends previous analyses that could fit at most two independent form factors simultaneously, by performing the first combined extraction of all four form factors (strange electric, strange magnetic, and the proton and neutron weak axial form factors including anapole contributions) from world PVES data. The results are compared with theoretical calculations of the strange form factors by Leinweber et al. (2004, 2006) and of the anapole contributions by Zhu et al. (2000), finding consistency within uncertainties. The analysis enables future measurements from HAPPEx, G0 backward-angle, and PVA4 backward-angle experiments to potentially distinguish between strange and anapole contributions and reveal a nonzero signal in at least one of these form factors.
