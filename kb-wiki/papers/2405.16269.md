# Pion gravitational form factors in the QCD instanton vacuum II

> We revisit the hard QCD contributions to the pion gravitational form factors (GFFs), in terms of the twist-2,3 pion distribution amplitudes (DA), including novel semi-hard contributions from the instantons. The pion DAs are evaluated in the QCD instanton vacuum, and then properly evolved to higher resolution. The results are compared to our recent results from the QCD instanton vacuum, as well as Bethe-Salpeter calculations and recent lattice data. The interpolated hard and soft contributions to the pion D-form-factor, are used to derive the (gravitational) pressure and shear within the pion, with a clear delineation of their range.

## Key claim

The paper extends previous calculations of pion gravitational form factors (GFFs) by including full twist-3 contributions alongside twist-2 contributions, and adds novel semi-hard contributions from instanton non-zero-modes, with the caveat that QCD factorization does not hold in the semi-hard regime and must be supplemented with non-perturbative contributions.

## Method

The authors evaluate the twist-2 and twist-3 pion distribution amplitudes (DAs) using the instanton liquid model (ILM) of the QCD vacuum at a low resolution scale of mu = 1/rho approximately 0.63 GeV, then evolve them to higher resolution using the Efremov-Radyushkin-Brodsky-Lepage (ERBL) procedure with Gegenbauer polynomial expansions. They compute hard perturbative QCD contributions to the pion energy-momentum tensor (EMT) and gravitational form factors A and D up to twist-3, and add semi-hard contributions from instanton non-zero-mode propagators. The soft contributions from a previous ILM analysis are interpolated with the hard and semi-hard contributions through a linear combination with matching bands.

## Key numbers

- Instanton size rho = 0.313 fm, packing fraction kappa approximately 0.1, and mean instanton density n_{I+A} approximately 1/fm^4
- Pion decay constant F_pi = sqrt(2) f_pi approximately 133 MeV, pion mass m_pi = 135 MeV
- Quark constituent mass M = 398 MeV, pseudoscalar strength chi_pi = 0.551 GeV at mu = 1/rho
- Lambda_QCD = 250 MeV used in the running coupling
- Mean vacuum EMT trace <T^mu_mu> approximately -10 fm^{-4}
- Gluon transverse energy m_gluon^2 = 1 GeV^2, quark transverse energy E_perp^2 = 0.3 GeV^2
- Matching band for 00-EMT is 3-6 GeV^2, for trace-EMT is 3-8 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of pion GFFs in the QCD instanton vacuum (Liu, Shuryak, Zahed 2024) and earlier partial calculations by Shuryak and Zahed (2020) and Tong et al. (2021, 2022). The results are compared to recent lattice data from Hackett et al. (2023) and Bethe-Salpeter calculations by Xu et al. (2023). The interpolated hard and soft contributions enable derivation of the gravitational pressure and shear distributions within the pion across all kinematic ranges.
