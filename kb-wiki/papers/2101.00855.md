# $Œû$-nucleus potential for $Œû^-$ quasifree production in the $^9$Be($K^-$, $K^+$) reaction

> We study phenomenologically a $Œû^-$ production spectrum of the $^9$Be($K^-$, $K^+$) reaction at 1.8 GeV/$c$ within the distorted-wave impulse approximation using the optimal Fermi-averaged $K^- p \to K^+ Œû^-$ amplitude. We attempt to clarify properties of a $Œû$-nucleus potential for $Œû^-$-$^8$Li, comparing the calculated spectrum with the data of the BNL-E906 experiment. The results show a weak attraction in the $Œû$-nucleus potential for $Œû^-$-$^8$Li, which can sufficiently explain the data in the $Œû^-$ quasifree region. The strength of $V_0^Œû=$ $-17 \pm 6$ MeV is favored within the Woods-Saxon potential, accompanied by the reasonable absorption of $W_0^Œû=$ $-5$ MeV for $Œû^-p \to Œû^0n$, $ŒõŒõ$ transitions in nuclear medium. It is difficult to determine the value of $W_0^Œû$ from the data due to the insufficient resolution of 14.7 MeV FWHM. The energy dependence of the Fermi-averaged $K^- p \to K^+ Œû^-$ amplitude is also confirmed by this analysis, and its importance in the nuclear ($K^-$, $K^+$) reaction is emphasized.

## Key claim
The $\Xi$-nucleus potential for $\Xi^-$-$^8$Li exhibits a weak attraction with real strength $V_0^\Xi = -17 \pm 6$ MeV in a Woods-Saxon form, but the imaginary part $W_0^\Xi$ cannot be reliably determined from the BNL-E906 data due to the insufficient detector resolution of 14.7 MeV FWHM.

## Method
The authors performed distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) calculations using the Green's function method to compute $\Xi^-$ production spectra for the $^9$Be($K^-$, $K^+$) reaction at an incident $K^-$ momentum of 1.8 GeV/$c$. They employed optimal Fermi-averaged $K^- p \to K^+ \Xi^-$ amplitudes and eikonal distorted waves, fitting the calculated average cross section $\bar{\sigma}_{1.5^\circ-8.5^\circ}$ to the BNL-E906 experimental data by varying the Woods-Saxon potential parameters $V_0^\Xi$ and $W_0^\Xi$ for the $\Xi^-$-$^8$Li system, with a renormalization factor $f_s$ to account for absolute normalization uncertainties.

## Key numbers
- Real potential strength: $V_0^\Xi = -17 \pm 6$ MeV in the Woods-Saxon potential with $r_0 = 0.783$ fm and $a = 0.722$ fm for $\Xi^-$-$^8$Li
- Imaginary potential strength: $W_0^\Xi = -5$ MeV (favored value) for $\Xi^-p \to \Xi^0n$, $\Lambda\Lambda$ transitions
- Minimum $\chi^2$ value: $\chi^2_{\rm min} = 15.2$ for $N=17$ data points ($\chi^2/N = 0.89$) at $V_0^\Xi = -17$ MeV, $W_0^\Xi = -5$ MeV, with renormalization factor $f_s = 0.940$
- Detector resolution: 14.7 MeV FWHM, which prevents determination of $W_0^\Xi$
- $\Xi^-$-$^8$Li $1S$ bound state: binding energy $B_{\Xi^-}(1S) = 1.85$ MeV and width $\Gamma_{\Xi^-}(1S) = 1.12$ MeV (with $W_0^\Xi = -2.5$ MeV); or $B_{\Xi^-}(1S) = 1.88$ MeV and $\Gamma_{\Xi^-}(1S) = 0.669$ MeV (with $W_0^\Xi = -1.5$ MeV)
- Momentum transfer range: $q \simeq 390$‚Äì$600$ MeV/$c$ for $K^+$ forward angles $\theta_{\rm lab} = 1.5^\circ$‚Äì$8.5^\circ$
- Estimated imaginary part from optical potential: $W_0^\Xi \approx -6.2$ MeV from $\langle v_{\Xi^-p}\sigma \rangle = 7.02$ mb using the Fermi gas model

## Context
This work extends previous analyses of $\Xi$-nucleus potentials from the $^{12}$C($K^-$, $K^+$) reaction by Fukuda (1998) and Khaustov (2000), who suggested $V_0^\Xi \simeq -14$ MeV, and is consistent with the weak attraction inferred from the KISO event in the KEK-E373 experiment reported by Nakazawa (2015). The results provide input for understanding $\Xi$ hypernuclei and neutron star properties, and support the ongoing J-PARC E05 experiment by Nagae (2018) on $^{12}$C targets. The study also confirms the importance of energy-dependent Fermi-averaged amplitudes found by Harada (2020) for nuclear ($K^-$, $K^+$) reactions.
