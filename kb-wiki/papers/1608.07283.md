# Probing the hardest branching of jets in heavy ion collisions

> We present the first calculation of the momentum sharing and angular separation distributions between the leading subjets inside a reconstructed jet in heavy ion collisions. These observables are directly sensitive to the hardest branching in the process of jet formation and are, therefore, ideal for studying the early stage of the in-medium parton shower evolution. The modification of the momentum sharing and angular separation distributions in lead-lead relative to proton-proton collisions is evaluated using the leading-order medium-induced splitting functions obtained in the framework of soft-collinear effective theory with Glauber gluon interactions. Qualitative and in most cases quantitative agreement between theory and preliminary CMS measurements suggests that the parton shower in heavy ion collisions can be dramatically modified early in the branching history. We propose a new measurement which will illuminate the angular distribution of the hardest branching within jets in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first calculation of the momentum sharing and angular separation distributions between the leading subjets inside a reconstructed jet in heavy ion collisions, showing that qualitative and in most cases quantitative agreement with preliminary CMS data suggests the parton shower can be dramatically modified early in the branching history, though the theory's transverse momentum dependence is not as strong as suggested by the preliminary measurements.

## Method

The authors calculate the groomed momentum sharing distribution \(p(z_g)\) and angular separation distribution \(p(r_g)\) for jets in proton-proton and lead-lead collisions using leading-order medium-induced splitting functions obtained from soft-collinear effective theory with Glauber gluon interactions (SCET\(_G\)). Jets are reconstructed with the anti-\(k_T\) algorithm (radius \(R=0.4\)), then reclustered with the Cambridge-Aachen algorithm and groomed using the soft-drop procedure with parameters \(\beta=0\) and \(z_{cut}=0.1\). The momentum sharing distribution is computed by integrating the splitting functions over partonic phase space constrained by the jet radius \(R\), angular resolution \(\Delta\), and \(z_{cut}\), then weighted by jet production cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Jets selected with transverse momentum \(p_T > 140\) GeV and pseudorapidity \(|\eta| < 1.3\) for comparison with CMS data at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02\) TeV.
- For typical jets with \(\omega = 2 p_T = 400\) GeV, \(r_g = 0.1\) and \(z_g = 0.1\), the branching time \(\tau_{\rm br} < 2\) fm, much smaller than the QGP size in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC.
- The ratio \(R^{p(z_g)}_{AA} = p(z_g)^{PbPb} / p(z_g)^{pp}\) is evaluated for two \(p_T\) bins: \(140~{\rm GeV} < p_T < 160~{\rm GeV}\) and \(250~{\rm GeV} < p_T < 300~{\rm GeV}\) in 0-10% central collisions, and for \(140~{\rm GeV} < p_T < 160~{\rm GeV}\) in 30-50% mid-peripheral collisions.
- Theoretical predictions for the momentum sharing ratio are provided for \(60~{\rm GeV} < p_T < 80~{\rm GeV}\) and \(250~{\rm GeV} < p_T < 300~{\rm GeV}\) with a more stringent cut \(\Delta R_{12} > 0.2\).
- Predictions for the groomed jet radius modification are shown for four \(p_T\) bins: \(60-80\) GeV, \(100-120\) GeV, \(140-160\) GeV, and \(250-300\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of SCET\(_G\) medium-induced splitting functions, previously used to describe hadron and jet observables in heavy ion collisions by Kang (2014) and Chien (2015), to the groomed momentum sharing observable. The observable is designed to probe the hardest branching in jet formation, complementing earlier jet substructure measurements such as jet shapes (Ellis, 1992) and jet fragmentation functions (Procura, 2009) which are sensitive to the partonic origin of jets. The results enable future studies of jet substructure observables more sensitive to soft radiation, such as jet mass, to map out the complete jet formation history in the quark-gluon plasma.
