# Determining the Meson Cloud Contribution of Nucleon Electromagnetic Form Factor Using Dispersion Relation

> We explore the meson cloud contribution to nucleon electromagnetic form factors in dispersion relation approach. In our calculations, experimental data on transition amplitudes for pion-nucleon scatterings are taken directly as inputs, with the assumption that the photon-pion interaction dominates over other meson-photon couplings. Combining with the quark core contribution evaluated in quark model, the proton electromagnetic form factors are well reproduced in the work. The resulting mean-square charge radius of the proton agrees well with experimental data, and the quark core radius is reasonable.

## Key claim

The meson cloud contribution to nucleon electromagnetic form factors can be extracted from experimental data on pion-nucleon scatterings using dispersion relations, but the resulting meson cloud contribution appears too large and the quark core too small unless an empirical scaling factor of \(1/\sqrt{2}\) is applied to the nucleon pole terms.

## Method

The authors used dispersion relations to compute the isovector electromagnetic form factors from the imaginary parts of the two-pion continuum spectral function, using experimental data on \(\pi\pi \rightarrow N\bar{N}\) partial wave transition amplitudes from Landolt-Bornstein (1983). The pion vector form factor was fitted to Belle experiment data using an Omnès function with a fitted parameter \(\alpha_V = 0.116\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\). The meson cloud contribution was isolated by taking the nucleon pole terms \(f^1_{\pm N}\) from the total partial wave amplitudes and scaling them against the Roy-Steiner equation solutions of Hoferichter (2016). The quark core contribution was calculated in a constituent quark model with harmonic oscillator basis, and the combined form factor was fitted to experimental proton electric form factor data.

## Key numbers

- \(\alpha_V = 0.116\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) for the pion vector form factor parameterization
- Integral cut-off \(\Lambda = 0.8\ \text{GeV}^2\) applied to the dispersion integral
- \(G_E(0) = 0.766\) for the meson cloud contribution to the isovector electric form factor (ideal value is 0.5)
- Proton charge radius \(r_E^p = 0.888\ \text{fm}\) with the unmodified \(G_{EN}\) from the dispersion relation
- Proton charge radius \(r_E^p = 0.874\ \text{fm}\) with \(G_{EN}\) scaled by \(1/\sqrt{2}\)
- Quark core charge radius \(r_E^{p,q^3} = 0.586\ \text{fm}\) with unmodified \(G_{EN}\)
- Quark core charge radius \(r_E^{p,q^3} = 0.669\ \text{fm}\) with \(G_{EN}\) scaled by \(1/\sqrt{2}\)

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relation approach to nucleon form factors previously developed by Hoferichter (2016) using Roy-Steiner equations, and by Belushkin (2006) for proton radius analyses. The authors combine this with the cloudy bag model tradition of Brown (1985) and Thomas (2001), and their own previous quark model studies of nucleon resonances \(N(1440)\), \(N(1520)\), and \(N(1535)\) (Atthaphon 2021, Kaewsnod 2022). The results enable a decomposition of the proton charge radius into quark core and meson cloud contributions, though the authors note that the meson cloud contribution appears too large unless corrected, and the resulting quark core radius is rather small.
