# Coupling hydrodynamics to nonequilibrium degrees of freedom in strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma

> Relativistic hydrodynamics simulations of quark-gluon plasma play a pivotal role in our understanding of heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. They are based on a phenomenological description due to Mueller, Israel, Stewart (MIS) and others, which incorporates viscous effects and ensures a well-posed initial value problem. Focusing on the case of conformal plasma we propose a generalization which includes, in addition, the dynamics of the least damped far-from-equilibrium degree of freedom found in strongly coupled plasmas through the AdS/CFT correspondence. We formulate new evolution equations for general flows and then test them in the case of N=4 super Yang-Mills plasma by comparing their solutions alongside solutions of MIS theory with numerical computations of isotropization and boost-invariant flow based on holography. In these tests the new equations reproduce the results of MIS theory when initialized close to the hydrodynamic stage of evolution, but give a more accurate description of the dynamics when initial conditions are set in the pre-equilibrium regime.

# Coupling hydrodynamics to nonequilibrium degrees of freedom in strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim
The authors propose a generalization of Mueller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) relativistic viscous hydrodynamics that incorporates the dynamics of the least damped far-from-equilibrium quasinormal mode (QNM) found in strongly coupled plasmas via the AdS/CFT correspondence. The new equations reproduce MIS results when initialized close to the hydrodynamic stage, but provide a more accurate description of the dynamics when initial conditions are set in the pre-equilibrium regime, though the improvement is limited by the presence of higher QNMs not captured by the single-mode approximation.

## Method
The authors extract the complex quasinormal mode frequencies of the stress tensor for N=4 super Yang-Mills plasma from holography and, assuming ultralocality (neglecting momentum dependence of QNM frequencies up to k ≈ 2πT), write a second-order differential equation for the QNM contribution to the shear stress tensor. They then formulate a Weyl-covariant generalization of this equation for arbitrary flows and couple it to the MIS relaxation equation via conservation of the stress tensor, with the total shear stress decomposed as Π^{μν} = Π_{MIS}^{μν} + Π_{QNM}^{μν}. The theory is tested against full numerical solutions of 5-dimensional Einstein equations for homogeneous isotropization and boost-invariant flow.

## Key numbers
- QNM frequencies for N=4 SYM at zero momentum: ω_R ≈ 9.800 and ω_I ≈ 8.629 (dimensionless, in units of temperature T)
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: η/s = 1/(4π)
- MIS relaxation parameter set to τ_Π = 1/(2π), the smallest value allowed by causality
- Ultralocality assumption holds for momenta up to k ≈ 2πT
- For the alternative formulation (Eq. 6), causality requires c_σ ≤ 2π, and unstable modes appear for wave vectors k > 18.5 T when c_σ = 2π
- Initialization times tested: t T = 0.4, 0.5, and 0.6 for isotropization; τ T = 0.4, 0.5, and 0.6 for boost-invariant flow

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological MIS framework (Muller 1967, Israel 1979) by incorporating insights from holographic dualities, specifically the quasinormal mode structure of black branes in asymptotically AdS space (Kovtun 2005). It builds on earlier observations by Denicol (2011) and Noronha (2011) regarding the significance of the analytic structure of retarded correlators in theories with gravity duals. The new equations enable more accurate modeling of pre-equilibrium dynamics in heavy ion collisions, which is relevant for observables sensitive to early-time evolution such as photon emission (Schenke 2006, McLerran 2014) and dilepton production (Martinez 2008, Shuryak 2012). Future work could involve extracting initial conditions for the nonhydrodynamic modes from early post-collision states simulated by Gelis (2013) or Berges (2013).
