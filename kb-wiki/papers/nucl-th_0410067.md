# Cold Strongly Coupled Atoms Make a Near-perfect Liquid

> Feshbach resonances of trapped ultracold alkali atoms allow to vary the atomic scattering length a. At very large values of a the system enters an universal strongly coupled regime in which its properties--the ground state energy, pressure {\it etc.}--become independent of a. We discuss transport properties of such systems. In particular, the universality arguments imply that the shear viscosity of ultracold Fermi atoms at the Feschbach resonance is proportional to the particle number density n, and the Plank constant \hbar η=\hbar n α_η, where α_ηis a universal constant. Using Heisenberg uncertainty principle and Einstein's relation between diffusion and viscosity we argue that the viscosity has the lower bound given by α_η \leq (6π)^{-1}. We relate the damping of low-frequency density oscillations of ultracold optically trapped ^{6}Li atoms to viscosity and find that the value of the coefficient α_ηis about 0.3. We also show that such a small viscosity can not be explained by kinetic theory based on binary scattering. We conclude that the system of ultracold atoms near the Feshbach resonance is a near-ideal liquid.

## Key claim

The paper argues that ultracold Fermi atoms near a Feshbach resonance behave as a near-ideal liquid with a shear viscosity close to a fundamental lower bound, based on the observation that the damping rate of collective oscillations is much smaller than predictions from kinetic theory based on binary scattering.

## Method

The authors used linearized viscous hydrodynamics to relate the damping rate of low-frequency collective density oscillations of ultracold optically trapped ⁶Li atoms to the shear viscosity coefficient. They extracted the dimensionless universal viscosity coefficient α_η from experimental damping rates of axial and radial modes in cigar-shaped harmonic traps, and compared these values to predictions from kinetic theory using the unitary-limited cross section and including Pauli blocking effects.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless viscosity coefficient extracted from the axial mode damping is α_η^(z)|_min ≈ 0.3, using Γ_z/ω_z = 0.0015 at the minimal damping point and ω_z ≈ 140 Hz
- The coefficient from the radial mode in the Bartenstein experiment is α_η^(r) ≈ 1.1, using Γ_r = 0.0625 ω_r with ω_r ≈ 4700 Hz
- The coefficient from the radial mode in the Kinast experiment is α_η^(r) ≈ 0.2
- The axial mode frequency ratio at resonance is Ω_z/ω_z = √(12/5) = 1.549, matching the measured value of approximately 1.55
- The radial mode frequency ratio at resonance is Ω_r/ω_r = √(10/3) = 1.826, matching the Kinast measurement of 1.829
- Kinetic theory predicts Γ_z/ω_z ≈ 0.56 for the axial mode, much larger than the observed damping
- Kinetic theory predicts Γ_r/ω_r ≈ 0.17 for the radial mode in the Kinast experiment, compared to the observed 0.014

## Context

This work extends the concept of universal transport properties from strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) studied at RHIC and from supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory via the AdS/CFT correspondence to ultracold atomic systems. The authors relate their findings to the universal bound η/s ≥ 1/(4π) from the AdS/CFT correspondence (Policastro, Son, Starinets, 2001) and to the equation of state parameter β ≈ 0.5 for unitary fermions (Heiselberg, 2001). The results enable experimental determination of universal transport coefficients in strongly coupled Fermi systems and suggest that ultracold atoms can serve as a laboratory for studying near-perfect liquid behavior analogous to the quark-gluon plasma.
