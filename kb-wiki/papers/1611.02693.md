# SU($4$) Polyakov linear-sigma model at finite temperature and density

> In mean-field approximation, the SU($4$) Polyakov linear - sigma model (PLSM) is constructed in order to characterize the quark-hadron phase structure in a wide range of temperatures and densities. The chiral condensates $σ_l$, $σ_s$ and $σ_c$ for light, strange and charm quarks, respectively, and the deconfinement order-parameters $ϕ$ and $ϕ^*$ shall be analyzed at finite temperatures and densities. We conclude that the critical temperatures corresponding to charm condensates are greater than that to strange and light ones, respectively. Thus, the charm condensates are likely not affected by the QCD phase transition. Furthermore, increasing the chemical potentials decreases the corresponding critical temperatures.

## Key claim

The SU(4) Polyakov linear-sigma model predicts that the critical temperature for charm quark chiral condensate (575 MeV) is substantially higher than those for strange (283.4 MeV) and light (174.6 MeV) quarks at zero chemical potential, implying that charm condensates are likely not affected by the QCD phase transition.

## Method

The authors constructed an SU(4) Polyakov linear-sigma model (PLSM) in mean-field approximation, extending the SU(3) framework to include charm quarks alongside light and strange quarks. They derived the thermodynamic potential from the grand-canonical partition function, incorporating chiral condensates for light, strange, and charm quarks, deconfinement order-parameters (Polyakov-loop fields), and a polynomial logarithmic parametrization for the Polyakov-loop potential. The chiral condensates and deconfinement order-parameters were obtained by globally minimizing the real part of the thermodynamic potential at the saddle point for various temperatures and baryon chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum chiral condensates: light quark sigma_l0 = 92.4 MeV (from pion decay constant), strange quark sigma_s0 = 94.5 MeV (from kaon decay constant), charm quark sigma_c0 = 293.87 MeV (from D-meson decay constant)
- Critical chiral temperatures at zero baryon chemical potential: T_chi^l = 174.6 MeV for light quarks, T_chi^s = 283.4 MeV for strange quarks, T_chi^c = 575 MeV for charm quarks
- The charm quark vacuum condensate sigma_c0 = 295 MeV is also cited in the results section

## Context

This work extends previous SU(2) and SU(3) Polyakov linear-sigma model studies (Lenaghan, Schaefer (2008), and others) to include charm quarks in an SU(4) framework, enabling the study of heavy meson phase structure up to masses of approximately 3.5 GeV. The results contradict the assumption that all quark flavors undergo chiral transition at similar temperatures, and enable future analysis of quark number susceptibilities and correlations involving charm quarks in thermal and dense QCD medium, which is relevant for interpreting experimental results from heavy-ion collisions.
