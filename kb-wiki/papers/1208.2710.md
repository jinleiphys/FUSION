# Multichannel parametrization of πN scattering amplitudes and extraction of resonance parameters

> We present results of a new multichannel partial-wave analysis for πN scattering in the c.m. energy range 1080 to 2100 MeV. This work explicitly includes ηN and K Λchannels and the single pion photoproduction channel. Resonance parameters were extracted by fitting partial-wave amplitudes from all considered channels using a multichannel parametrization that is consistent with S-matrix unitarity. The resonance parameters so obtained are compared to predictions of quark models.

## Key claim

The paper presents a multichannel partial-wave analysis of πN scattering that explicitly includes ηN, KΛ, and single pion photoproduction channels, using a parametrization consistent with S-matrix unitarity to extract resonance parameters; the abstract glosses over the fact that the analysis is limited to the center-of-mass energy range 1080 to 2100 MeV and that some partial waves required dummy channels (ωN and ρΔ) without data to satisfy unitarity.

## Method

The authors performed a multichannel energy-dependent partial-wave analysis by fitting amplitudes from πN→πN (SAID SP06 solution), γN→πN (SAID FA07 solution), πN→ππN (Manley et al. 1984 solution), and their own single-energy amplitudes for πN→ηN and πN→KΛ. The KSU model was used, employing a generalized energy-dependent Breit-Wigner parametrization of amplitudes with unitary background matrices B and a resonant K-matrix, treating all channels on an equal footing. Fits were performed in the c.m. energy range 1080 to 2100 MeV, with dummy ωN and ρΔ channels included in some partial waves to satisfy unitarity.

## Key numbers

- Fitted mass of S11(1535): M = 1538 ± 1 MeV with total width Γ = 141 ± 4 MeV
- Fitted mass of P11(1440): M = 1412 ± 2 MeV with Γ = 248 ± 5 MeV
- Fitted mass of D13(1520): M = 1512.6 ± 0.5 MeV with Γ = 117 ± 1 MeV
- Fitted mass of F15(1680): M = 1682.7 ± 0.5 MeV with Γ = 126 ± 1 MeV
- Fitted mass of P33(1232): M = 1231.1 ± 0.2 MeV with Γ = 113.0 ± 0.5 MeV, elasticity 99.4%
- Pole mass for S11(1535): Mp = 1515 MeV with pole width Γp = 123 MeV
- Pole mass for P33(1232): Mp = 1212 MeV with pole width Γp = 98 MeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier KSU model analysis by Manley and Saleski (1992) by explicitly including ηN and KΛ channels and single pion photoproduction, superseding that prior analysis. The results are compared with predictions of quark models and with prior analyses by Arndt et al. (2006), Cutkosky et al. (1980), and Hohler (1979). The analysis enables improved extraction of N* and Δ* resonance parameters by treating multiple channels simultaneously, addressing the longstanding discrepancy between the number of predicted and experimentally confirmed baryon resonances.
