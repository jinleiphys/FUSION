# Dynamical Renormalization Group Approach to Quantum Kinetics in Scalar and Gauge Theories

> We derive quantum kinetic equations from a quantum field theory implementing a diagrammatic perturbative expansion improved by a resummation via the dynamical renormalization group. The method begins by obtaining the equation of motion of the distribution function in perturbation theory. The solution of this equation of motion reveals secular terms that grow in time, the dynamical renormalization group resums these secular terms in real time and leads directly to the quantum kinetic equation. We used this method to study the relaxation in a cool gas of pions and sigma mesons in the O(4) chiral linear sigma model. We obtain in relaxation time approximation the pion and sigma meson relaxation rates. We also find that in large momentum limit emission and absorption of massless pions result in threshold infrared divergence in sigma meson relaxation rate and lead to a crossover behavior in relaxation. We then study the relaxation of charged quasiparticles in scalar electrodynamics (SQED). While longitudinal, Debye screened photons lead to purely exponential relaxation, transverse photons, only dynamically screened by Landau damping lead to anomalous relaxation, thus leading to a crossover between two different relaxational regimes. We emphasize that infrared divergent damping rates are indicative of non-exponential relaxation and the dynamical renormalization group reveals the correct relaxation directly in real time. Finally we also show that this method provides a natural framework to interpret and resolve the issue of pinch singularities out of equilibrium and establish a direct correspondence between pinch singularities and secular terms. We argue that this method is particularly well suited to study quantum kinetics and transport in gauge theories.

## Key claim

The dynamical renormalization group method derives quantum kinetic equations by resumming secular terms (terms that grow in time) that appear in perturbative solutions of the distribution function's equation of motion, with the caveat that the method assumes a wide separation between microscopic and relaxational time scales, which is generally justified only in weakly coupled theories.

## Method

The method begins by writing the Hamiltonian with an effective mass that incorporates medium effects (analogous to hard thermal loop resummation), then obtains the equation of motion for the quasiparticle distribution function via the Heisenberg field equations. The perturbative solution of this equation reveals secular terms that grow in time, and the dynamical renormalization group resums these secular terms in real time, yielding the quantum kinetic equation as the renormalization group equation. The approach uses nonequilibrium (Schwinger-Keldysh) Green's functions and a diagrammatic perturbative expansion with the initial density matrix diagonal in the basis of free quasiparticles.

## Key numbers

* For the O(4) chiral linear sigma model in the relaxation time approximation, the pion and sigma meson relaxation rates are obtained; in the large momentum limit, emission and absorption of massless pions produce a threshold infrared divergence in the sigma meson relaxation rate.
* For scalar electrodynamics (SQED), longitudinal Debye screened photons lead to purely exponential relaxation, while transverse photons (dynamically screened only by Landau damping) lead to anomalous (non-exponential) relaxation, producing a crossover between two different relaxational regimes.
* For the scalar field theory at high temperature, the effective thermal mass is given by \( m^2_{\rm eff} = \frac{\lambda T^2}{24} + \mathcal{O}(\lambda^{3/2}T^2) \) for \( T \gg \sqrt{\lambda} T \gg m \), with the longest microscopic time scale being \( t_{\rm micro} \sim 1/\sqrt{\lambda} T \sim 1/m_{\rm eff} \).

## Context

This work extends the dynamical renormalization group approach previously introduced by Boyanovsky, de Vega, and Wang (1999) for studying relaxation of mean fields in gauge theories, now applied to quantum kinetics in scalar and gauge theories. The method provides an alternative to Wigner transform approaches (e.g., Blaizot and Iancu, 1999) that often yield infrared divergent damping rates, and it resolves the issue of pinch singularities (Landsman, Altherr) by establishing their equivalence to secular terms in time-dependent perturbation theory. The approach enables systematic study of transport phenomena in gauge theories, including non-exponential relaxation and crossover behavior, with direct relevance to understanding kinetic equilibration in quark-gluon plasma formation at RHIC and LHC.
