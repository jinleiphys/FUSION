# The impact of dissipation and noise on fluctuations in chiral fluid dynamics

> We investigate the nonequilibrium evolution of the sigma field coupled to a fluid dynamic expansion of a hot fireball to model the chiral phase transition in heavy-ion collisions. The dissipative processes and fluctuations are allowed under the assumption that the total energy of the coupled system is conserved. We use the linear sigma model with constituent quarks to investigate the effects of the chiral phase transition on the equilibration and excitation of the sigma modes. The quark fluid acts as a heat bath in local thermal equilibrium and the sigma field evolves according to a semiclassical stochastic Langevin equation of motion. The effects of supercooling and reheating in a first order phase transition are observed when the sigma field relaxes to equilibrium with the quark fluid. Nonequilibrium fluctuations at the first order phase transition lead to an increase in the intensity of sigma fluctuations in comparison to a scenario with a critical point.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nonequilibrium fluctuations of the sigma field at a first-order chiral phase transition are significantly stronger than those at a critical point, but this result depends on the specific temperature-dependent damping coefficient and the assumption that the total energy of the coupled system is conserved.

## Method

The authors couple a semiclassical stochastic Langevin equation for the sigma field to (3+1)-dimensional ideal relativistic fluid dynamics of a quark-antiquark fluid, using the linear sigma model with constituent quarks as the effective theory. The damping coefficient and noise correlation are derived from the dissipation-fluctuation theorem, and the energy-momentum exchange between the field and fluid is implemented via a source term that accounts for both dissipative and stochastic energy transfer. The system is evolved numerically using the SHASTA algorithm with initial conditions mimicking a heavy-ion collision fireball at an initial temperature of 160 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The critical point in the linear sigma model occurs at \(T_c \simeq 99\) MeV and \(\mu_c \simeq 207\) MeV (from prior work by Scavenius et al., 2000).
- For the first-order phase transition scenario, the coupling is set to \(g = 5.5\), yielding a critical temperature \(T_c = 123.27\) MeV where two degenerate minima exist.
- For the critical point scenario, the coupling is \(g = 3.63\), giving \(T_c = 139.88\) MeV where the effective potential becomes flat.
- The vacuum sigma mass is \(m_\sigma \approx 604\) MeV, the pion mass is \(m_\pi = 138\) MeV, and the pion decay constant is \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV.
- The damping coefficient \(\eta\) is supplemented by a constant value of \(2.2\)/fm from \(\sigma \leftrightarrow 2\pi\) reactions when the sigma mass is above \(2m_\pi\).
- The numerical grid uses \(\Delta x = 0.2\) fm, \(\Delta t = 0.08\) fm, and a grid volume of \((128 \Delta x)^3\).
- The total energy conservation is violated by \(\lesssim 10\%\) in the first-order phase transition scenario, while it is well conserved for the critical point scenario.

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral fluid dynamics models by Mishustin (1998) and Paech (2003) by including dissipation and noise via a Langevin equation, going beyond deterministic equations of motion for the chiral fields. It also builds on the derivation of the Langevin equation for the sigma field within the 2PI effective action formalism by Nahrgang (2011). The results enable future studies of nonequilibrium signals at the first-order phase transition, such as enhanced sigma fluctuations, which are relevant for experiments at the low-energy RHIC program, the CBM project at FAIR, and NICA.
