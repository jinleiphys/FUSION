# The influence of chiral chemical potential, parallel electric and magnetic fields on the critical temperature of QCD

> We study the influence of external electric, $E$, and magnetic, $B$, fields parallel to each other, and of a chiral chemical potential, $μ_5$, on the chiral phase transition of Quantum Chromodynamics. Our theoretical framework is a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with a contact interaction. Within this model we compute the critical temperature of chiral symmetry restoration, $T_c$, as a function of the chiral chemical potential and field strengths. We find that the fields inhibit and $μ_5$ enhances chiral symmetry breaking, in agreement with previous studies.

## Key claim

The parallel electric and magnetic fields inhibit chiral symmetry breaking (lowering the critical temperature), while the chiral chemical potential enhances it; however, the perturbative expansion used in Ruggieri (2016) underestimates the effect of μ₅ on T_c, with the numerical error becoming sizeable for larger values of μ₅.

## Method

The authors use a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a contact four-fermion interaction to compute the thermodynamic potential and gap equation for quark matter in the presence of parallel electric and magnetic fields and a chiral chemical potential. They solve the gap equation self-consistently without resorting to the small-field/small-chemical-potential expansion used in prior work, and also implement inverse magnetic catalysis via a B-dependent coupling constant tuned to lattice data.

## Key numbers

- For eE = eB = 8m_π² and μ₅ = 300 MeV, the critical temperature increases by approximately 50% relative to the μ₅ = 0 case when inverse magnetic catalysis is included, compared to about 8% for eE = eB = 5m_π² and about 4% for E = B = 0.
- At T = 100 MeV with eE = eB = 8m_π² and an average quark mass M = 330 MeV, the equilibrium chiral density n₅^eq ≈ 0.01 fm⁻³ corresponds to μ₅^eq ≈ 5 MeV.
- At T = 200 MeV with eE = eB = 8m_π² and an average quark mass M = 100 MeV, n₅^eq ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³ corresponds to μ₅^eq ≈ 15 MeV.
- The inverse magnetic catalysis parameters are a = 0.0109, b = -1.013 × 10⁻⁴, c = 0.022, d = 1.846 × 10⁻⁴, with Λ_QCD = 300 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative calculation of Ruggieri (2016) by solving the full gap equation beyond the expansion in powers of μ₅²/T², E/T², and B/T², finding that the earlier perturbative approach underestimates the effect of μ₅ on the critical temperature. The results are in qualitative agreement with prior studies by Yu (2015), Cui (2016), and Frasca (2016) regarding the enhancement of chiral symmetry breaking by μ₅, and with Cao (2015) for the treatment of electromagnetic fields. The study enables more accurate predictions for the chiral phase diagram in heavy-ion collision conditions where parallel electric and magnetic fields and chiral imbalance may coexist.
