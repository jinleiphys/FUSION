# Electron scattering with a short-range correlation model

> The inclusive electromagnetic responses in the quasi-elastic region are calculated with a model which considers the terms of the cluster expansion containinga single correlation line. The validity of this model is studied by comparing, in nuclear matter, its results with those of a complete calculation. Results in finite nuclei for both one-and two-nucleon emission are presented.

## Key claim

The authors show that a cluster expansion truncated to first order in the correlation function \(h(r_{ij})\) (i.e., retaining only diagrams with a single correlation line) can accurately reproduce full Fermi Hypernetted Chain (FHNC) results for both one-body densities and inclusive electromagnetic responses in the quasi-elastic region, provided that both two-point and three-point diagrams are included to maintain correct normalization.

## Method

The authors use a variational many-body approach where the ground state wave function is a Slater determinant multiplied by a product of two-body scalar correlation functions \(f(r_{ij})\). They perform a cluster expansion of operator mean values, retaining only terms containing a single correlation function \(h(r_{ij}) = f^2(r_{ij}) - 1\) after eliminating unlinked diagrams. For excited states, they assume the same correlation function describes both ground and excited states, and evaluate the inclusive nuclear response for one-particle one-hole (1p-1h) and two-particle two-hole (2p-2h) excitations. The model is tested by comparing its results for nuclear matter and finite nuclei (\({}^{12}\)C and \({}^{48}\)Ca) against full FHNC calculations using the semi-realistic Afnan-Tang S3 interaction.

## Key numbers

- The 2p-2h longitudinal response is approximately three orders of magnitude smaller than the 1p-1h response.
- For the 2p-2h longitudinal response, the contribution of three-point diagrams is close to 40% for some values of the excitation energy.
- The comparison between the model and FHNC calculations for one-body densities and momentum distributions in \({}^{12}\)C and \({}^{48}\)Ca shows "very good" and "rather satisfactory" agreement, respectively (no numerical precision given).
- The nuclear matter charge responses calculated with the model "almost exactly overlapped" the FHNC results (no numerical deviation quoted).

## Context

This work extends the variational approach of Fantoni and Pandharipande (1987) for nuclear matter responses to finite nuclei, using a simplified cluster expansion. It builds on the authors' previous development of a truncated model for one-body density distributions (Co' et al., 1995; Arias de Saavedra et al., 1997) and on FHNC ground-state calculations for doubly closed shell nuclei (Co' et al., 1992; Fabrocini et al., 1998). The model enables calculation of inclusive quasi-elastic responses in finite nuclei including both one- and two-nucleon emission, with the authors planning to extend it to exclusive processes.
