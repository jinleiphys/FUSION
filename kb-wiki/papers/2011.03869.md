# Providing physics guides in Bayesian neural networks from input layer: case of giant dipole resonance predictions

> The Bayesian neural network (BNN) has been applied to evaluate and predict the nuclear data. However, how to provide physics guides in BNN is a key but an open question. In this work, the case study on giant dipole resonance (GDR) energy is presented to illustrate the effectiveness and maneuverability of the method to provide physics guides in BNN from input layer. The Spearman's correlation coefficients are applied to assess the statistical dependence between nuclear properties in the ground state and the GDR energies. Then the optimal ground-state properties are employed as the input layer in the BNN for evaluating and predicting the GDR energies. Those selected ground-state properties actively contributes to reduce the predicted errors and avoid the risk of the non-physics divergence. This work gives a demonstration to find effects of the GDR energy by using the BNN without the physics motivated model, which may be helpful for discovering physics effects from the complex nuclear data.

# Providing physics guides in Bayesian neural networks from input layer: case of giant dipole resonance predictions

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that physics guidance can be provided to a Bayesian neural network (BNN) from the input layer alone, without requiring a physics-motivated model for initial predictions, by using Spearman's correlation coefficients to select optimal ground-state nuclear properties as input neurons for predicting giant dipole resonance (GDR) energies. The method is presented as a case study, and the authors hedge that while the selected inputs reduce prediction errors and avoid non-physics divergence, the approach is a demonstration for discovering physics effects from complex nuclear data rather than a replacement for physics-motivated models.

## Method

The authors calculated Spearman's correlation coefficients between 18 ground-state nuclear properties (derived from atomic masses and the Weizsacker-Skyrme mass formula) and the low and high GDR peak energies (E_r1 and E_r2) from 445 data sets. They then trained a Bayesian neural network with one hidden layer of 10 neurons using variational inference, sequentially adding ground-state properties as input neurons based on minimizing the root-mean-square (RMS) deviation between BNN predictions and experimental GDR energies. The optimal input layer was selected as the five-dimensional set {A, beta_2, Q_n_alpha, Q_beta, B}.

## Key numbers

* Spearman's correlation coefficient between mass number A and average GDR energy: rho = -0.92; between charge number Z and average GDR energy: rho = -0.92.
* Spearman's correlation coefficient between isospin asymmetry delta and average GDR energy: rho = -0.89.
* Spearman's correlation coefficient between quadrupole deformation beta_2 and the difference of resonance energies Delta E_r: rho = 0.72.
* RMS deviation for one-dimensional input using mass number A: 0.96 MeV; using charge number Z: 0.98 MeV.
* RMS deviation for two-dimensional input {A, beta_2}: 0.78 MeV, which is 19 percent lower than using {A} alone.
* RMS deviation for optimal five-dimensional input {A, beta_2, Q_n_alpha, Q_beta, B}: 0.65 MeV.
* Empirical formula parameters from fitting low GDR energy E_r1 with (a + b*delta)*A^{-1/3}: a = 61.6 +/- 0.5, b = 66.9 +/- 4.0.

## Context

This work extends the residual-approach developed by Utama et al. (2016) for nuclear masses and charge radii, where physics-motivated models provide initial predictions that BNNs then fine-tune by modeling residuals. The authors address the open question of whether physics guidance can be provided without such a model, as posed in Utama et al. (2016). The method is demonstrated on GDR energies, which have been studied experimentally for decades and modeled with approaches including Hartree-Fock-based random phase approximation (Lyutorovich et al., 2015; Bonasera et al., 2018) and time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory (Fracasso et al., 2012; Mennana et al., 2020). The work enables future efforts to train BNNs directly on GDR spectra rather than extracted energies, and suggests that the importance-ordering of effects from the BNN input selection can guide further physics-motivated model development.
