# Interpretation of Coulomb breakup of 31Ne in terms of deformation

> The recent experimental data on Coulomb breakup of the nucleus $^{31}$Ne are interpreted in terms of deformation. The measured large one-neutron removal cross-section indicates that the ground state of $^{31}$Ne is either s- or p-halo. The data can be most easily interpreted as the spin of the ground state being 3/2$^-$ coming from either the Nilsson level [330 1/2] or [321 3/2] depending on the neutron separation energy $S_n$. However, the possibility of 1/2$^{+}$ coming from [200 1/2] is not excluded. It is suggested that if the large ambiguity in the measured value of $S_n$ of $^{31}$Ne, 0.29$\pm1.64$ MeV, can be reduced by an order of magnitude, say to be $\pm$100 keV, one may get a clear picture of the spin-parity of the halo ground state.

# Interpretation of Coulomb breakup of 31Ne in terms of deformation

## Key claim
The measured large one-neutron removal cross-section from Coulomb breakup of 31Ne can be most easily interpreted as the ground state having spin 3/2- arising from either the [330 1/2] or [321 3/2] Nilsson level, depending on the neutron separation energy Sn; however, the possibility of 1/2+ from [200 1/2] is not excluded, and a reduction of the large uncertainty in Sn (currently 0.29 ± 1.64 MeV) to ±100 keV would clarify the spin-parity.

## Method
The author calculated one-particle neutron wave-functions in a deformed Woods-Saxon mean-field, using the eigenphase formalism for continuum states and Nilsson diagram analysis for bound states. The B(E1) values for soft E1 excitation from bound halo-neutron levels to continuum states were computed, with the radial integrals evaluated far outside the 30Ne core nucleus (r = 8-40 fm). The probabilities of s, p, and higher partial-wave components in the relevant Nilsson levels ([330 1/2], [321 3/2], [200 1/2]) were extracted at deformations β = 0.3 and 0.5, and the resulting Coulomb breakup cross-sections were compared to the experimental data of Nakamura (2009).

## Key numbers
- The measured neutron separation energy Sn of 31Ne is 0.29 ± 1.64 MeV, with the author suggesting this uncertainty should be reduced to ±100 keV.
- At εΩ = -300 keV, the probability of the p3/2 component in the [330 1/2] level at β = 0.3 is 0.5225, and in the [321 3/2] level at β = 0.5 is 0.2534.
- At εΩ = -300 keV, the probability of the s1/2 component in the [200 1/2] level at β = 0.5 is 0.67.
- The 1f7/2 one-neutron resonance is found at 2.40 MeV with a width of 0.224 MeV for the spherical Woods-Saxon potential.
- The difference in integrated dB(E1)/dE values (integrated over continuum energy up to 2 MeV) between using the 2p3/2 spherical wave-function and the p3/2 component of [330 1/2] is about 15 percent.
- The estimated magnetic moment for possible states with Iπ = 3/2- or 1/2+ lies in the range -0.4 ≤ μ/μN ≤ -1.0.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Nakamura (2009), who interpreted Coulomb breakup data on 31Ne using spherical Woods-Saxon wave-functions and concluded the ground state is a p3/2 or s1/2 neutron halo. The author argues that since the 21st neutron cannot have the spherical 7/2- assignment, deformation must be considered, following the deformed mean-field approach successfully applied to mirror nuclei 25Mg and 25Al by Bohr and Mottelson (1975). The analysis is enabled by the observations that 30Ne and 32Ne lie inside the island of inversion (Yamamoto 2003, Doornenbal 2009) and that 33Mg (N=21) has a measured magnetic moment consistent with Iπ = 3/2- (Yordanov 2007). The results suggest that improved measurement of Sn could resolve whether 31Ne has a p-wave or s-wave halo ground state, and that magnetic moment measurements alone may not distinguish between the candidates.
