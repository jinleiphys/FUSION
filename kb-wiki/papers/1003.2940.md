# Finite-size effects and collective vibrations in the inner crust of neutron stars

> We study the linear response of the inner crust of neutron stars within the Random Phase Approximation, employing a Skyrme-type interaction as effective interaction. We adopt the Wigner-Seitz approximation, and consider a single unit cell of the Coulomb lattice which constitutes the inner crust, with a nucleus at its center, surrounded by a sea of free neutrons. With the use of an appropriate operator, it is possible to analyze in detail the properties of the vibrations of the surface of the nucleus and their interaction with the modes of the sea of free neutrons, and to investigate the role of shell effects and of resonant states.

## Key claim

The collective surface vibrations of nuclei in the inner crust of neutron stars persist even when the neutron Fermi energy lies in the continuum, but their properties are significantly modified by the coupling with the surrounding neutron gas. The authors show that the response is largely driven by transitions involving deeply bound protons and resonant neutron levels, while the coupling with continuum states shifts the peaks to lower energy and increases their width.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations using the Skyrme-type SLy4 interaction on a grid with 0.1 fm mesh, imposing that single-particle wavefunctions vanish at the edges of the Wigner-Seitz cell. They then calculated excited states via Random Phase Approximation (RPA) in configuration space, using the same Skyrme force for the residual interaction (excluding two-body Coulomb and spin-orbit terms). The response was studied using the operator dU/dr Y_LM, which is specifically sensitive to nuclear surface vibrations, for quadrupole (L=2) and octupole (L=3) modes.

## Key numbers

- Two Wigner-Seitz cells were studied: (1) R_WS = 33.1 fm, containing 1314 neutrons and 50 protons (1364Sn), corresponding to nucleon density rho ≈ 1.5 × 10^13 g cm^-3 (0.05 rho_0); (2) R_WS = 42.2 fm, containing 458 neutrons and 40 protons (498Zr), corresponding to rho ≈ 2.7 × 10^12 g cm^-3 (0.01 rho_0)
- Neutron Fermi energies: E_F = 5.5 MeV for 1364Sn and E_F = 2.1 MeV for 498Zr; proton Fermi energies: E_F = -35.1 MeV for 1364Sn and E_F = -31.5 MeV for 498Zr
- The continuum portion of the single-particle spectrum starts at E_cont = -3.5 MeV (1364Sn) and E_cont = -0.5 MeV (498Zr)
- The RPA 2+ response for 1364Sn displays two main peaks at about 3.5 MeV (width ~3.3 MeV) and 10.0 MeV (width ~4.0 MeV); the 3- response shows a strong peak around 3.6 MeV (width ~2 MeV) and a broader bump around 18.6 MeV (width ~4 MeV)
- To satisfy the Energy Weighted Sum Rule within 93%, single-particle states up to E_cut = 90 MeV were included; the ph basis contained about 8000 states for quadrupole and about 10000 for octupole response

## Context

This work extends previous mean-field studies of the inner crust by groups such as Pizzo (2005), Sandulescu (2004), and Montani (2004), who considered the interplay between nuclear and free-neutron degrees of freedom at the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov level. The authors note that earlier studies by Gori (2007), Khan (2005), and Grasso (2008) found that responses to r^L Y_LM operators closely resemble those in neutron matter, whereas the present work focuses specifically on surface modes using the dU/dr Y_LM operator. The results enable future studies of how these collective vibrations affect pairing correlations in the inner crust, building on exploratory calculations by Simone (2007) that found density fluctuations associated with nuclear surface dynamics can partially dequench the pairing gap.
