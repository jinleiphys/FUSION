# Quenching of $1^+$ excitations in the double giant resonance

> The electromagnetic excitation of the two-phonon isovector giant dipole resonance in relativistic projectiles incident on heavy targets can be proceed via several intermediate $1^-$ one-phonon giant resonance states. In two step electric dipole transitions the population of $0^+$, $1^+$, and $2^+ $ two-phonon states are possible. We calculate the amplitude distribution of $1^-$ excitations with an RPA formalism, and use it to calculate the electromagnetic excitation of two-phonon states in second order perturbation theory and coupled-channels. We show that a conspiracy between angular momentum coupling and the strength of the electromagnetic fields suppresses contributions of $1^+$ states to the total cross sections.

## Key claim

The authors show that the $1^+$ component of the double giant dipole resonance (DGDR) is strongly suppressed in electromagnetic excitation, contributing negligibly to the total cross section, and therefore cannot explain the missing theoretical strength compared to experimental DGDR cross sections. This suppression arises from a combination of angular momentum coupling rules and the properties of the electromagnetic field, making the second-order perturbation theory amplitude identically zero, with only small nonzero contributions appearing in coupled-channels calculations.

## Method

The authors performed an RPA calculation for $1^-$ states in $^{208}$Pb using the quasiparticle-phonon model with a Woods-Saxon average field and separable residual interaction. They constructed two-phonon DGDR states with $J^\pi = 0^+, 1^+, 2^+$ by coupling the six $1^-$ states with the largest reduced matrix elements (exhausting 90.6% of the classical energy-weighted sum rule). They then calculated electromagnetic excitation cross sections for $^{208}$Pb (640 MeV/nucleon) $+^{208}$Pb collisions using both second-order perturbation theory and a coupled-channels approach.

## Key numbers

- The six selected $1^-$ states exhaust 90.6% of the classical EWSR; all $1^-$ states up to 25 MeV exhaust 94.3% of the EWSR
- Total DGDR cross section in second-order perturbation theory: 244.9 mb
- Coupled-channels cross sections for DGDR components: $0^+$ = 33.0 mb, $2^+$ = 163.0 mb, $1^+$ = 6.3 mb
- Direct excitation of $2^+$ two-phonon states from ground state: 0.11 mb; for $1^+$ states: less than 0.01 mb
- GDR cross section in coupled-channels: 2830 mb; in first-order perturbation theory: 3275 mb
- Ratio $R = \sigma_{DGDR}/\sigma_{GDR}$ from coupled-channels: 0.071; from perturbation theory: 0.075
- Experimental ratio $R_{exp} = 0.116 \pm 0.014$ from Stroth et al. (1996)
- Energy shift of GDR centroid relative to B(E1) centroid: 0.26 MeV; DGDR shift: 0.33 MeV (coupled-channels) and 0.28 MeV (perturbation theory)

## Context

This work extends microscopic studies of the DGDR by Ponomarev et al. (1994) and Catara, Chomaz, and Van Giai (1989) by explicitly calculating the previously neglected $[1^- \otimes 1^-]_{1^+}$ component. It contradicts the possibility that $1^+$ states could explain the 30% discrepancy between theory and experiment reported by Stroth et al. (1996) for $^{208}$Pb. The results enable refined calculations requiring precise description of both GDR and DGDR to resolve the remaining experiment-theory disagreement, with the authors noting that a primitive scaling to 122% of the EWSR (from Vessiere et al., 1970) yields $R_{calc} \approx 0.096-0.101$, still below the experimental value.
