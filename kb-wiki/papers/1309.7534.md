# Mechanism of production of light complex particles in nucleon-induced reactions

> The Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics (ImQMD) model incorporated with the statistical decay model is successful in describing emission of nucleons in the intermediate energy spallation reactions, but not good enough in describing productions of light complex particles, i.e. $d$, $t$, $^3$He and $^4$He. To improve the description on emission of light complex particles, a phenomenological mechanism called surface coalescence and emission is introduced into ImQMD model: nucleon ready to escape from the compound nuclei can coalesce with the other nucleon(s) to form light complex particle and be emitted. With updated ImQMD model, the description on the experimental data of light complex particles produced in nucleon-induced reactions are great improved.

## Key claim
Introducing a phenomenological surface coalescence and emission mechanism into the Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics (ImQMD) model significantly improves the description of light complex particle (deuteron, triton, helium-3, helium-4) production in nucleon-induced reactions, but the improvement is conditional on adjusting the coalescence parameter \(h_0\) as a function of incident energy.

## Method
The authors modified the ImQMD05 model by adding a surface coalescence mechanism: at each time step, any fast nucleon passing through a surface layer (defined by a core radius \(R_0\) and surface width \(D_0\)) is considered a leading nucleon; it can coalesce with other nucleons close in phase space (satisfying \(R_{im} \times P_{im} \leq h_0\)) to form a light complex particle (LCP). Candidate LCPs are tested for emission in priority order (⁴He > ³He > t > d) and are emitted only if their kinetic energy is sufficient to tunnel through the Coulomb barrier; otherwise, the constituent nucleons are released. The excited residues from the ImQMD simulation are then passed to the statistical decay model GEM2 for the evaporation stage.

## Key numbers
- The surface coalescence parameter \(h_0\) is set to 200 MeV fm/\(c\) for \(E_{\text{lab}} \leq 300\) MeV, 260 MeV fm/\(c\) for \(300 < E_{\text{lab}} \leq 500\) MeV, and 330 MeV fm/\(c\) for \(E_{\text{lab}} > 500\) MeV.
- The core radius is set to \(R_0 \simeq 1.4 A^{1/3}\) fm (where \(A\) is the target mass number), and the surface width is fixed at \(D_0 = 2.3\) fm.
- The switching time from ImQMD05 to GEM2 is 100 fm/\(c\) for light nuclei (\(A \leq 27\)), 125 fm/\(c\) for intermediate nuclei (\(27 < A \leq 58\)), and 150 fm/\(c\) for heavy nuclei (\(A > 58\)).
- The cluster recognition parameters in the minimum spanning tree algorithm are \(R_c = 4.5\) fm and \(P_c = 250\) MeV/\(c\).
- For each impact parameter, 50,000 events are calculated; maximum impact parameters range from 5.0 fm (¹⁶O) to 9.5 fm (²⁰⁸Pb, ²⁰⁹Bi).

## Context
This work extends the ImQMD model (previously successful for nucleon emission in spallation reactions) to address its known deficiency in describing light complex particles, a problem noted in IAEA benchmark comparisons of spallation models. The surface coalescence approach follows similar implementations in the INCL model (Letourneau et al., Boudard et al.) and the JQMD code (Watanabe et al., 2005). The improved model enables unified description of both free nucleons and light complex particles (d, t, ³He, ⁴He) across a wide energy range (62–1200 MeV) and various targets, which is important for applications such as accelerator-driven subcritical reactors and spallation neutron source design.
