# Production and rescattering of strange baryons at SPS energies in a transport model with hadron potentials

> A mean-field potential version of the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model is used to investigate the production of strange baryons, especially the $Λ$s and $\overlineΛ$s, from heavy ion collisions at SPS energies. It is found that, with the consideration of both formed and pre-formed hadron potentials in UrQMD, the transverse mass and longitudinal rapidity distributions of experimental data of both $Λ$s and $\overlineΛ$s can be quantitatively explained fairly well. Our investigation also shows that both the production mechanism and the rescattering process of hadrons play important roles in the final yield of strange baryons.

## Key claim

The mean-field potential version of the UrQMD model, which includes potentials for both formed and pre-formed hadrons, can quantitatively explain the transverse mass and longitudinal rapidity distributions of Λ and \(\overline{\Lambda}\) data from SPS energies, but the agreement is qualified by the fact that the cascade version 2.1 already reproduces the data well within error bars except at very low and high transverse masses, and the potential version further flattens the slope to improve the fit at those extremes.

## Method

The authors used the mean-field potential version of the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model, based on cascade version 2.1, to simulate central and semi-peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at 40A GeV and 158A GeV. They incorporated Skyrme-like density-dependent mean-field potentials for both formed baryons and pre-formed hadrons from string fragmentation, with a reduction factor of 2/3 for pre-formed mesons, and applied relativistic corrections to the two-body potentials. The calculated transverse mass spectra and rapidity distributions of Λ, \(\overline{\Lambda}\), \(\Xi^-\), and anti-protons were compared to experimental data from the NA49 collaboration.

## Key numbers

- The normal nuclear density used in the potential is \(\rho_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
- The Skyrme parameters for the SM-EoS are \(a = -110\ \text{MeV}\), \(b = 182\ \text{MeV}\), and \(g = 7/6\).
- The average hadronic density in the central zone (\(R_{c.m.} < 5\ \text{fm}\)) from central Pb+Pb reactions at SPS energies reaches 4–8 times normal nuclear density at the early stage.
- For central Pb+Pb collisions at 40A GeV and 158A GeV, the transverse mass spectra of Λs at mid-rapidity (\(|y| < 0.4\)) are shown for the centrality bin \(\sigma/\sigma_T < 5\%\).
- The rapidity distributions of Λs are shown for five centrality bins from \(\sigma/\sigma_T < 5\%\) (most central) to \(33.5\% < \sigma/\sigma_T < 43.5\%\) (semi-peripheral) at both 40A GeV and 158A GeV.
- The time evolution of the ratio of pre-formed hadrons to all hadrons in the zone \(R_{c.m.} < 5\ \text{fm}\) is shown at \(t < 5\ \text{fm}/c\) and up to \(t \sim 10\ \text{fm}/c\) for both 40A GeV and 158A GeV.
- For central Pb+Pb collisions at 158A GeV, the total yield of \(\Xi^-\) doubles from \(t = 3\ \text{fm}/c\) to \(t = 30\ \text{fm}/c\) in cascade mode, while the yield of anti-protons decreases by about 30% due to annihilation.

## Context

This work extends previous UrQMD studies (Bass 1998, Bleicher 1999, Bratkovskaya 2004) that showed overestimation of hyperon yields and underestimation of mean transverse momenta at AGS and SPS energies, problems partly cured by version 2.3 (Petersen 2008). It also builds on the mean-field potential version of UrQMD (Li 2007) that was previously applied to HBT correlations. The results enable further investigation of the role of hadronic rescattering and medium effects on multi-strange baryon production, which remains unsatisfactory, and motivate theoretical breakthroughs in describing the dynamic process of particles in dense medium.
