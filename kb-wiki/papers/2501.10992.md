# Direct Evidence for the $\bar{D}D^*/D\bar{D}^*$ Molecular Nature of $G(3900)$ Through Triangular Singularity Mechanisms

> The exotic hadron $G(3900)$, initially observed in the process $e^+ e^- \to D\bar{D}$, has been further supported by analyses from the BESIII Collaboration, which classify it as a $P$-wave molecular state of $D^{+}D^{*-}/D^{-}D^{*+}$. However, theoretical discussions raise concerns about its status as a true particle, emphasizing the need for additional studies. In this Letter, we employ the triangular singularity mechanism to investigate $G(3900)$ across various reaction channels, allowing us to produce significant peaks without relying on the existence of a real particle. We identify $X(4020)$, $Y(4320)$, and the tentative $X(4014)$ as potential sources of these peaks via decay processes to $γG(3900)$ or $πG(3900)$. We stress the importance of experimental explorations of $e^+ e^- \to X/Y \to γ(π) G(3900)$, which are essential for confirming the molecular composition of $D^{+}D^{*-}/D^{-}D^{*+}$ and refining the mass measurement of $G(3900)$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the exotic hadron \(G(3900)\) can be confirmed as a genuine \(P\)-wave \(\bar{D}D^*/D\bar{D}^*\) molecular state not by direct observation in \(e^+e^- \to D\bar{D}\), but by detecting triangular singularity peaks in alternative reactions \(e^+e^- \to \gamma(\pi) G(3900)\); the claim is conditional on the existence of specific parent states \(X(4020)\), \(Y(4320)\), or \(X(4014)\) that decay via triangle diagrams.

## Method

The authors apply the Landau equation and the Coleman-Norton theorem to identify kinematic conditions under which a triangular singularity produces a peak in the invariant mass distribution of \(G(3900)\) without requiring a genuine resonance. They assign the intermediate particles in the triangle loop as the molecular constituents of \(G(3900)\) (\(D^{*0}\bar{D}^0\) or \(D^{*+}D^{-}\)) and compute the allowed mass ranges for the initial particle \(A\) and the final particle \(B\) (photon or pion) that satisfy the singularity conditions. They then calculate the cross-section line shape for \(e^+e^- \to \gamma G(3900)\) using a non-relativistic amplitude from prior work, proportional to \(\mathcal{F}^2\), and identify candidate parent states from the Particle Data Group that fall within the required mass windows.

## Key numbers

- Mass of \(G(3900)\) reported by BESIII: \(M_{G(3900)} = 3872.5 \pm 14.2 \pm 3.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\), width \(\Gamma_{G(3900)} = 179.7 \pm 14.1 \pm 7.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- For the \(D^{*0}\bar{D}^0\) assignment, the mass of \(G(3900)\) must exceed the threshold \(m_{D^{*0}} + m_{D^0} = 2006.85 + 1864.84 = 3871.69\ \mathrm{MeV}\); for the \(D^{*+}D^{-}\) assignment, the threshold is \(2010.26 + 1869.66 = 3879.92\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- For the photon channel with \(D^{*0}\bar{D}^0\) constituents, the initial particle \(A\) mass range is \(M_A = 4013.7 - 4016.4\ \mathrm{MeV}\), consistent with the tentative \(X(4014)\) (mass \(4014.3 \pm 4.0 \pm 1.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\), width \(4 \pm 11 \pm 6\ \mathrm{MeV}\), significance \(2.8\sigma\)).
- For the photon channel with \(D^{*+}D^{-}\) constituents, the \(G(3900)\) mass range is \(M_C = 3879.92 - 3882.47\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and the initial particle mass range is \(M_A = 4020.52 - 4023.16\ \mathrm{MeV}\), consistent with \(X(4020)\).
- For the pion channel with \(\bar{D}^0 D^{*0}\) constituents, the initial particle mass range is \(M_A = 4286.94 - 4303.62\ \mathrm{MeV}\), consistent with \(Y(4320)\) (mass \(4298 \pm 12 \pm 26\ \mathrm{MeV}\), width \(127 \pm 17 \pm 10\ \mathrm{MeV}\)).
- The \(D^{*+}\) width used in the amplitude is \(\Gamma_{D^{*+}} = 83.4\ \mathrm{KeV}\).

## Context

This work addresses the controversy over whether \(G(3900)\) is a genuine hadronic state or a kinematic artifact, following the BESIII observation (BESIII, 2024) and the molecular interpretation by Lin (2024). It directly counters the conclusions of Salnikov (2024) and Husken (2024), who argued that \(G(3900)\) is not a true resonance. The triangular singularity method extends the approach previously applied to \(X(3872)\) by Guo (2019) and Braaten (2019, 2020) to the \(P\)-wave molecular candidate \(G(3900)\). The proposed experiments \(e^+e^- \to \gamma(\pi) G(3900)\) near the \(\bar{D}^*D^*\) and \(\bar{D}_1D\) thresholds would provide a decisive test of the molecular nature of \(G(3900)\) and enable a more precise mass measurement.
