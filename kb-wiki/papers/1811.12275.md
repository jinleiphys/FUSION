# Jet fragmentation in a dense QCD medium

> We study the fragmentation of a jet propagating in a dense quark-gluon plasma. Using a leading, double-logarithmic approximation in perturbative QCD, we compute for the first time the effects of the medium on the vacuum-like emissions. We show that, due to the scatterings off the plasma, the in-medium parton showers differ from the vacuum ones in two crucial aspects: their phase-space is reduced and the first emission outside the medium can violate angular ordering. We compute the jet fragmentation function and find results in qualitative agreement with measurements at the LHC.

## Key claim

The authors compute for the first time the effects of a dense quark-gluon plasma on vacuum-like emissions within a leading double-logarithmic approximation, showing that the in-medium parton shower phase space is reduced and that the first emission outside the medium can violate angular ordering. The results are in qualitative agreement with LHC measurements, but the approximation is not accurate enough for observables relying on energy conservation.

## Method

The authors use the double-logarithmic approximation (DLA) of perturbative QCD to compute the phase space for vacuum-like emissions (VLEs) inside and outside a dense quark-gluon plasma characterized by the jet quenching parameter \(\hat{q}\) and medium length \(L\). They derive conditions for VLEs based on formation time constraints: inside the medium, VLEs require \(t_f \ll \sqrt{2\omega/\hat{q}}\) (equivalently \(\omega \gg \omega_0(\theta) = (2\hat{q}/\theta^4)^{1/3}\)), while outside the medium they require \(t_f \ge L\) (equivalently \(\omega \le \omega_L(\theta) = 2/(\theta^2 L)\)). They then iterate these emissions to construct the parton shower and compute the jet fragmentation function by integrating the two-dimensional gluon distribution over the unmeasured angle.

## Key numbers

- Critical energy \(\omega_c = 1/(2\hat{q}L^2)\) separates emissions that behave as in vacuum (\(\omega \ge \omega_c\)) from those affected by the medium.
- Critical angle \(\theta_c = 2/\sqrt{\hat{q}L^3}\) satisfies \(t_{coh}(\theta_c) = L\); antennas with \(\theta_{i-1} \ge \theta_c\) can lose coherence.
- For intermediate energies and small angles \(\theta \simeq 0.1\theta_{q\bar{q}}\), the two-dimensional gluon distribution shows suppression up to 15% relative to vacuum.
- The fragmentation function ratio \(D(\omega)/D_{vac}(\omega)\) shows enhancement at low energies \(\omega \lesssim 2\) GeV and slight suppression from roughly 2 GeV up to \(\omega_c\).
- For a jet of energy \(E = 200\) GeV and opening angle \(\bar{\theta} = 0.4\), the phase space diagram is computed with \(\hat{q}\) and \(L\) as free parameters.

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS-Z formalism (Baier 1996, Zakharov 1996, Wiedemann 2000) for medium-induced radiation and the DLA treatment of vacuum jets (Dokshitzer 1991) to construct a unified picture of jet evolution in a quark-gluon plasma. It builds on studies of color decoherence in a medium (Mehtar-Tani 2010, Casalderrey-Solana 2011, 2012) and provides a probabilistic framework suitable for Monte-Carlo implementations. The results enable more quantitative predictions for jet quenching observables such as the fragmentation function measured at the LHC (Chatrchyan 2014, Aaboud 2018) and the \(R_{AA}\) ratio for jets.
