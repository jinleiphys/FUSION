# A revised $B(E2; 2^+_1 \to 0^+_1)$ value in the semi-magic nucleus $^{210}$Po

> The lifetimes of the $2^+_1$, the $2^+_2$ and the $3^-_1$ states of $^{210}$Po have been measured in the $^{208}$Pb($^{12}$C,$^{10}$Be)$^{210}$Po transfer reaction by the Doppler-shift attenuation method. The results for the lifetime of the $2^+_1$ state is about three times shorter than the adopted value. However, the new value still does not allow for consistent description of the properties of the yrast $2^+_1$, $4^+_1$, $6^+_1$, and $8^+_1$ states of $^{210}$Po in the framework of nuclear shell models. The Quasi-particle Phonon Model (QPM) calculations also cannot overcome this problem thus indicating the existence of a peculiarity which is neglected in both theoretical approaches.

## Key claim

The newly measured lifetime of the \(2^+_1\) state in \(^{210}\)Po is about three times shorter than the adopted value, yielding a \(B(E2;2^+_1 \to 0^+_1)\) value that is three times larger; however, even this revised value is still insufficient to resolve the long-standing discrepancy with shell-model predictions, and the Quasi-particle Phonon Model (QPM) also fails to reproduce the observed \(E2\) transition strengths for the yrast cascade.

## Method

Lifetimes of the \(2^+_1\), \(2^+_2\), and \(3^-_1\) states in \(^{210}\)Po were measured using the Doppler-shift attenuation method (DSAM) following the \(^{208}\)Pb(\(^{12}\)C,\(^{10}\)Be)\(^{210}\)Po transfer reaction at 62 MeV beam energy, about 2 MeV below the Coulomb barrier. Gamma rays were detected by HPGe detectors at forward (35°) and backward (142.3°) angles in coincidence with recoiling light fragments registered by a solar-cell array. Line-shape analysis was performed with the APCAD software package, which simulates the slowing-down process via GEANT4 and accounts for detector response, geometry, and feeding contributions from higher-lying states.

## Key numbers

- Lifetime of the \(2^+_1\) state: \(\tau = 2.6(4)\) ps, leading to \(B(E2;2^+_1 \to 0^+_1) = 136(21)~e^2\text{fm}^4\) (1.83(28) W.u.).
- Lifetime of the \(2^+_2\) state: \(\tau = 0.22(4)\) ps, with \(B(E2;2^+_2 \to 0^+_1) = 53(11)~e^2\text{fm}^4\).
- Lifetime of the \(3^-_1\) state: \(\tau = 0.50(6)\) ps, with \(B(E3;3^-_1 \to 0^+_1) = 70(31) \times 10^3~e^2\text{fm}^6\).
- The new \(B(E2;2^+_1 \to 0^+_1)\) value is about three times larger than the adopted value from Ellegaard (based on \((d,d')\) scattering), but shell-model calculations (Coraggio; Caurier) still overestimate it by a factor of two.
- QPM calculations, adjusted to reproduce the \(B(E2;2^+_1 \to 0^+_1)\) value, underestimate the \(E2\) strengths for the \(8^+_1 \to 6^+_1 \to 4^+_1\) cascade by a factor of about 8.

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of the \(2^+_1\) lifetime in \(^{210}\)Po, which had been deduced from cross-section ratios in \((d,d')\) and \((p,p')\) scattering by Ellegaard. The new DSAM result is model-independent and resolves the earlier ambiguity, but the persistent discrepancy with large-scale shell-model calculations (Coraggio; Caurier) and with QPM calculations indicates that both theoretical approaches neglect a peculiarity, likely the softness of the \(^{208}\)Pb core and its influence on particle-hole excitations. The findings motivate further theoretical investigation of core excitations and mixing between two-quasiparticle and phonon configurations in this nominally simple two-valence-proton nucleus.
