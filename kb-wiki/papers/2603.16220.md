# Nuclear pasta in hot neutron-star matter and proto-neutron stars

> We investigate nuclear pasta phases appearing in hot neutron-star matter based on the compressible liquid-drop model, where the matter consists of a dense liquid phase and a dilute gas phase separated by a sharp interface. The surface tension is calculated self-consistently from the Thomas-Fermi approximation, and it depends on temperature and isospin asymmetry. We employ relativistic mean-field models with different symmetry energy slopes to describe nuclear interactions. It is found that the TM1e model with a small symmetry energy slope of $L=40$ MeV predicts various pasta shapes at low temperatures, while the TM1 model with $L=110.8$ MeV yields only the droplet configuration up to the crust-core transition density. We examine the occurrence and influence of pasta phases in proto-neutron stars with a constant entropy per baryon. These pasta phases may occur in the inner crust with a thickness of about $1.2$ km, playing an important role in the thermal evolution of the star.

## Key claim

The paper claims that nuclear pasta phases (nonspherical nuclear shapes) can appear in hot neutron-star matter and proto-neutron stars, but only when the symmetry energy slope parameter is small (L=40 MeV in the TM1e model); the TM1 model with a large slope (L=110.8 MeV) yields only spherical droplet configurations up to the crust-core transition density.

## Method

The authors employ the compressible liquid-drop model with a sharp interface between a dense liquid phase and a dilute gas phase within the Wigner-Seitz approximation. The surface tension is parameterized from Thomas-Fermi calculations and depends on temperature and isospin asymmetry. Nuclear interactions are described by two relativistic mean-field models (TM1 and TM1e) that share identical isoscalar properties but differ in their symmetry energy slopes. For each temperature and average baryon density, the free energy density is minimized across all pasta configurations (droplet, rod, slab, tube, bubble) and uniform matter to identify the thermodynamically stable phase.

## Key numbers

- TM1e model: symmetry energy slope L = 40 MeV, symmetry energy at saturation E_sym = 31.38 MeV
- TM1 model: symmetry energy slope L = 110.8 MeV, symmetry energy at saturation E_sym = 36.89 MeV
- Pasta phases may occur in the inner crust with a thickness of about 1.2 km
- Surface tension parameter tau_s = 1.071 MeV/fm^2 for both models
- Critical temperature for symmetric nuclear matter T_c0 = 15.6 MeV for both models
- TM1e model predicts various pasta shapes at low temperatures; TM1 model yields only droplet configurations

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nuclear pasta phases in cold neutron-star matter (Bao 2015, Ji 2020) to finite-temperature conditions relevant to proto-neutron stars. The compressible liquid-drop method with self-consistent surface tension follows the approach of Avancini (2010) and Bao (2016). The results enable improved modeling of proto-neutron star thermal evolution, as pasta phases can alter thermal conductivity and neutrino transport in the inner crust. The TM1e model with L=40 MeV is compatible with nuclear experimental data and recent astrophysical observations from NICER and gravitational-wave events.
