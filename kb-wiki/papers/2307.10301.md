# Sensitivity of finite size effects to the boundary conditions and the vacuum term

> Finite volume effects are studied both with low-momentum cutoff and with momentum discretization in the framework of an (axial)vector meson extended quark-meson model with Polyakov-loop variables. In the momentum cutoff scenario, the CEP moves to lower temperatures and larger quark chemical potentials as the characteristic system size is reduced, however, the treatment of the vacuum term significantly affects its trajectory. The size dependence of the baryon fluctuations is also studied by the kurtosis and the skewness, both of which show moderate dependence on temperature and some dependence on quark chemical potential. The order of the phase transition is also studied near the chiral limit at finite system size and found to be second-order only at vanishing explicit breaking. The implementation of the finite size effect with momentum discretization is more complicated and shows peculiar behavior due to the different modes dropping below the Fermi surface and strong dependence on the type of the boundary condition chosen. We found that both the different boundary conditions and the treatment of the vacuum term cause significant changes in the trajectory of the CEP as the characteristic system size is changed.

## Key claim

The trajectory of the critical endpoint (CEP) as system size is reduced depends significantly on whether the fermionic vacuum term is treated as finite-size or kept at infinite size, and on the choice of boundary conditions in momentum discretization; these choices can produce qualitatively different behavior, including opposite shifts of the pseudocritical temperature at zero chemical potential.

## Method

The authors study finite volume effects in a 2+1 flavor (axial)vector meson extended Polyakov quark-meson (ePQM) model, using two implementations: a low-momentum cutoff lambda = pi/L and momentum discretization with periodic (PBC) and antiperiodic (APBC) boundary conditions. The grand potential is computed in a hybrid approximation with tree-level mesons and one-loop fermions, including a properly renormalized vacuum contribution; the field equations are solved for the nonstrange and strange condensates and Polyakov-loop variables to map the phase diagram and compute baryon number fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- The pseudocritical temperature at mu_q=0 decreases with decreasing system size L when the vacuum term is treated as finite-size; for L=2 fm the pion and sigma masses become degenerate, signaling chiral symmetry restoration.
- The CEP disappears around L approximately 2.5 fm in the low-momentum cutoff scenario with finite-size vacuum term.
- When the vacuum term is kept at infinite size, the CEP does not disappear until L approximately 0.5 fm, and the pseudocritical temperature increases with decreasing L.
- System sizes studied range from L=2 fm to L=6 fm, corresponding to low-momentum cutoffs lambda = pi/L (converted using 1 fm^-1 = 197.3 MeV).
- For L=3 fm, the CEP temperature T^CEP is approximately 10 MeV, which is about one-fifth of the infinite-volume T^CEP.

## Context

This work extends previous finite-volume studies in linear sigma models (Palhares 2009, Fraga 2010, Magdy 2015, Magdy 2019) that neglected the vacuum contribution, and in functional renormalization group approaches (Braun 2004, Braun 2005, Tripolt 2013, Almasi 2016, Klein 2017) that included a renormalized fermionic vacuum integral. The results show that the treatment of the vacuum term qualitatively changes the size dependence of the phase boundary and CEP trajectory, and that momentum discretization with different boundary conditions introduces additional complications. The baryon fluctuation results (kurtosis and skewness) are compared with Dyson-Schwinger approach calculations (Bernhardt 2021, Bernhardt 2022).
