# Recent results on the nonmesonic weak decay of hypernuclei within a one-meson-exchange model

> We update our previous results for the nonmesonic decay of $^{12}_Λ$C and $^5_Λ$He. We pay special attention to the role played by Final State Intreractions on the decay observables. We follow a One-Meson-Exchange model which includes the exchange of the $π, ρ, K, K^*, η$ and $ω$ mesons. We also present recent predictions for different observables concerning the decay of the doubly strange $^6_{ΛΛ}$He hypernucleus.

# Recent results on the nonmesonic weak decay of hypernuclei within a one-meson-exchange model

## Key claim
The one-meson-exchange model, including exchange of π, ρ, K, K*, η, and ω mesons, can reproduce the nonmesonic decay rates of ¹²_ΛC and ⁵_ΛHe in reasonable agreement with experiment, but it fails to explain the experimentally observed difference in the parity-violating asymmetry parameter between ⁵_ΛHe (small and positive) and ¹²_ΛC (large and negative), which remains an open challenge.

## Method
The authors perform a nonrelativistic reduction of Feynman diagrams for one-meson exchange to construct a transition potential for the weak decay ΛN → NN (and ΛΛ → YN for double-strangeness hypernuclei). They include pseudoscalar mesons (π, η, K) for the long-range part and vector mesons (ρ, ω, K*) for the short-range part, with monopole form factors at each vertex. Strong interaction effects are incorporated using the Nijmegen NSC97a–f potentials for initial-state correlations (via G-matrix) and final-state interactions (via T-matrix solution of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with realistic NN scattering wave functions). Weak coupling constants are obtained from SU(6)ₓ for parity-violating amplitudes and a pole model for parity-conserving amplitudes.

## Key numbers
- Nonmesonic decay rate for ⁵_ΛHe: 0.425 (NSC97a) and 0.317 (NSC97f) in units of Γ_Λ = 3.8 × 10⁹ s⁻¹; experimental value 0.41 ± 0.14
- Nonmesonic decay rate for ¹²_ΛC: 0.726 (NSC97a) and 0.554 (NSC97f); experimental values range from 0.89 ± 0.15 ± 0.03 to 1.14 ± 0.2
- Neutron-to-proton ratio Γₙ/Γₚ for ⁵_ΛHe: 0.343 (NSC97a) and 0.457 (NSC97f); experimental value 0.93 ± 0.55
- Neutron-to-proton ratio Γₙ/Γₚ for ¹²_ΛC: 0.288 (NSC97a) and 0.341 (NSC97f); experimental values range from 0.52 ± 0.16 to 1.87 ± 0.59⁺⁰·³²₋₁.₀₀
- Proton-induced partial rate Γₚ for ⁵_ΛHe: 0.317 (NSC97a) and 0.218 (NSC97f); experimental value 0.21 ± 0.07
- Proton-induced partial rate Γₚ for ¹²_ΛC: 0.564 (NSC97a) and 0.413 (NSC97f); experimental value 0.31⁺⁰·¹⁸₋₀.₁₁
- Asymmetry parameter Aₚ for ⁵_ΛHe: −0.675 (NSC97a) and −0.682 (NSC97f); experimental value 0.24 ± 0.22
- Asymmetry parameter Aₚ for ¹²_ΛC: 0.358 (NSC97a) and 0.367 (NSC97f); experimental value 0.05 ± 0.53

## Context
This work updates the authors' previous calculations (Parreno, Ramos, Bennhold 1997) using the new Nijmegen NSC97a–f baryon-baryon potentials (Nijmegen group 1999) for both strong interaction vertices and correlated wave functions. The results extend earlier one-meson-exchange models by systematically comparing six different strong interaction parametrizations and by demonstrating that proper treatment of final-state interactions via T-matrix solution is essential, simpler approaches (no FSI, phenomenological correlation functions, or K-matrix approximations) produce decay rates differing by more than a factor of two. The predictions for the doubly-strange hypernucleus ⁶_ΛΛHe provide a new testing ground for chiral perturbation theory in the weak SU(3) sector, since the ΛΛ → Λn mode is sensitive to one-loop log corrections at the 50% level and allows access to the ΛΛK vertex without dominant pion-exchange contamination. The failure to reproduce the experimental asymmetry difference between ⁵_ΛHe and ¹²_ΛC motivates further theoretical and experimental work on the weak decay of hypernuclei.
