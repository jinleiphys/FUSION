# Approaching nuclear interactions with lattice QCD

> Nuclei make up the majority of the visible matter in the Universe; obtaining a first principles description of the nuclear properties and interactions between nuclei directly from the underlying theory of the strong interaction, Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD), is one of the main goals of the nuclear physics community. Although the theory was established nearly fifty years ago, the complexities of QCD at low energies precludes analytical solutions of the simplest hadronic systems, let alone the features of the nuclear forces. In this thesis we follow the lattice QCD approach, according to which QCD is solved non-perturbatively in a discretized space-time via large-scale numerical calculations. Specifically, the interactions between two octet baryons, with strangeness ranging from 0 to -4, are studied at low energies with larger-than-physical quark masses, and the low-energy coefficients in pionless effective field thepry relevant for two-baryon interactions, including those responsible for SU(3) flavor-symmetry breaking, are constrained.

## Key claim

This thesis demonstrates that lattice QCD can be used to constrain the low-energy coefficients of pionless effective field theory for two-baryon interactions across strangeness sectors from 0 to -4, but only at larger-than-physical quark masses, meaning the results do not yet correspond to the physical point where direct comparison with experimental nuclear data is possible.

## Method

The author performed lattice QCD calculations of two-octet-baryon correlation functions on a discretized space-time, extracting energy eigenvalues from the Euclidean time dependence of these correlators. The resulting finite-volume energy shifts were then matched to the Luscher formalism to determine scattering phase shifts and low-energy constants in pionless effective field theory, including those responsible for SU(3) flavor-symmetry breaking. The analysis employed a systematic fitting protocol with excited-state model selection, covariance matrix estimation with shrinkage, and bootstrap resampling for confidence intervals.

## Key numbers

- The analysis used a shrinkage parameter lambda* for the covariance matrix estimation, with a tolerance parameter tol_noise for the signal-to-noise ratio cutoff and tol_temp for the maximum temporal extent.
- The excited-state fit function used e=0 as the starting number of exponentials, with model selection based on the Akaike Information Criterion (Delta AIC) and a threshold parameter A for the number of degrees of freedom.
- Fit acceptance criteria included a chi-squared per degree of freedom threshold tol_chi2, a solution stability tolerance tol_sol, and a correlation tolerance tol_corr.
- The median tolerance for bootstrap distributions was set to tol_med, and confidence intervals were computed using the 1/6 and 5/6 quantiles of the bootstrap ensemble.

## Context

This work extends the program of applying lattice QCD to nuclear forces pioneered by Luscher (1986, 1990) and later developed by Rummukainen and Gottlieb (1995) for boosted systems. It builds on prior lattice studies of two-baryon systems by Beane et al. (2011), Yamazaki et al. (2004), and Leskovec and Prelovsek (2012), while incorporating the pionless effective field theory framework. The results enable future lattice calculations at physical quark masses and provide constraints for nuclear structure calculations based on chiral effective field theory.
