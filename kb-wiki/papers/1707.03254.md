# Centrality dependence of charmed-meson photoproduction in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions

> We calculate the centrality dependence of inclusive cross section of large-pT charmed-meson ($D^{0}$, $D^{*}$, $D^{*+}$, and $D_{s}^{+}$) from heavy quark fragmentation by the hard photoproduction processes for the nucleus-nucleus collisions in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. The numerical results indicate that the modification of the hard photoproduction processes cannot be negligible for the charmed-meson production in Au-Au collisions at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and Pb-Pb collisions at Large Hadron Collider (LHC).

## Key claim

The hard photoproduction processes (direct and resolved) provide a non-negligible contribution to large-\(p_T\) charmed-meson production in Au-Au collisions at RHIC and Pb-Pb collisions at LHC, but only when the collision energy is high enough that the equivalent photon spectrum for the charged parton, which scales as \(\ln(\sqrt{s}/2m_q)\), becomes prominent.

## Method

The authors extended the hard photoproduction mechanism from electron-proton deep inelastic scattering at HERA to nucleus-nucleus collisions. They calculated the inclusive cross section for large-\(p_T\) charmed-mesons (\(D^0\), \(D^*\), \(D^{*+}\), and \(D_s^{+}\)) from heavy quark fragmentation, including both inelastic direct photoproduction (photon-gluon fusion) and inelastic resolved photoproduction (where the hadron-like photon fluctuates into a quark-antiquark pair). The calculation used the Peterson heavy quark fragmentation function, nuclear thickness functions, parton distribution functions with nuclear shadowing, and the equivalent photon spectrum of the charged parton, with centrality dependence included via the nuclear overlap geometry.

## Key numbers

- The equivalent photon spectrum for the charged parton scales as \(f_{\gamma/q} \propto \ln(E/m_q) = \ln(\sqrt{s}/2m_q) + \ln(x)\), making the photoproduction contribution prominent at LHC energies due to the large \(\sqrt{s}\).
- The calculation was performed for Au-Au collisions at RHIC and Pb-Pb collisions at LHC, with comparisons to data from STAR Collaboration (2014) and ALICE Collaboration (2012, 2013).
- The differential cross section formula includes the Peterson fragmentation function parameter \(\epsilon_c\) from Peterson et al. (1995) and the subprocess cross sections for \(\gamma g \rightarrow Q\bar{Q}\) from Babcock et al. (1978).

## Context

This work extends the hard photoproduction mechanism previously applied to electron-proton collisions at HERA (Forshaw, 1997; Nisius, 2000; Krawczyk et al., 2001) to ultrarelativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions, building on the authors' prior studies (Yu and Li, 2015; Yu et al., 2017). It complements existing approaches such as the color glass condensate effective theory (Fujii and Watanabe, 2013), the general-mass variable-flavor-number scheme (Kniehl et al., 2009), and the \(k_T\)-factorization approach (Maciula and Szczurek, 2013). The results enable future studies that include photoproduction contributions alongside standard parton hard scattering processes in heavy-ion phenomenology.
