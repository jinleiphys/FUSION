# Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of A=8 nuclei

> We report quantum Monte Carlo calculations of ground and low-lying excited states for A=8 nuclei using a realistic Hamiltonian containing the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon potentials. The calculations begin with correlated eight-body wave functions that have a filled alpha-like core and four p-shell nucleons LS-coupled to the appropriate (J+/-;T) quantum numbers for the state of interest. After optimization, these variational wave functions are used as input to a Green's function Monte Carlo calculation made with a new constrained path algorithm. We find that the Hamiltonian produces an 8Be ground state that is within 2 MeV of the experimental resonance, but the other eight-body energies are progressively worse as the neutron-proton asymmetry increases. The 8Li ground state is stable against breakup into subclusters, but the 8He ground state is not. The excited state spectra are in fair agreement with experiment, with both the single-particle behavior of 8He and 8Li and the collective rotational behavior of 8Be being reproduced. We also examine energy differences in the T=1 and 2 isomultiplets and isospin-mixing matrix elements in the excited states of 8Be. Finally, we present densities, momentum distributions, and studies of the intrinsic shapes of these nuclei, with 8Be exhibiting a definite 2-alpha cluster structure.

## Key claim

The Argonne v18 plus Urbana IX Hamiltonian reproduces the 8Be ground state within 2 MeV of the experimental resonance, but the accuracy of calculated energies for other A=8 nuclei degrades progressively as neutron-proton asymmetry increases, with the 8He ground state actually being unstable against breakup into subclusters despite being experimentally stable.

## Method

The authors constructed variational wave functions for A=8 nuclei with a filled alpha-like core and four p-shell nucleons LS-coupled to the appropriate (Jπ;T) quantum numbers, then optimized these wave functions using variational Monte Carlo. These optimized wave functions served as input to Green's function Monte Carlo calculations using a new constrained path algorithm to project out the exact lowest-energy state by Euclidean propagation. The Hamiltonian included the Argonne v18 two-nucleon potential and the Urbana IX three-nucleon potential, with charge-dependent and charge-symmetry-breaking terms treated as first-order perturbations.

## Key numbers

- 8Be ground state energy is within 2 MeV of the experimental resonance
- 8He ground state is unstable against breakup into subclusters (experimentally it is strong stable with 119 ms half-life)
- 8Li ground state is stable against breakup into subclusters (experimentally decays by beta emission with 838 ms half-life)
- 8Be first excited (2+;0) state at 3.04 MeV excitation, second excited (4+;0) state at 11.4 MeV excitation
- 8He first excited state identified experimentally at 3.59 ± 0.05 MeV
- 8Li first excited (1+;1) state at 0.98 MeV excitation, second excited (3+;1) state at 2.26 MeV excitation with width 33 keV
- 8Li (4+;1) stretched state at 6.53 MeV excitation with width 35 keV
- 8Li (0+;2) isobaric analog of 8He at 10.82 MeV excitation with width less than 12 keV
- 8Be (0+;2) isobaric analog of 8He at 27.49 MeV excitation
- 8Be ground state is 92 keV above threshold for breakup into two alpha particles, with width 7 eV
- 8B ground state decays by beta+ emission with half-life 770 ms
- 8C ground state has width approximately 230 keV

## Context

This work extends previous variational Monte Carlo and Green's function Monte Carlo calculations for A=6,7 nuclei reported by Pieper, Carlson, Pandharipande, and Wiringa (1997) to the A=8 system. The A=8 nuclei provide a sensitive testing ground for nuclear forces, particularly the isospin dependence of the three-nucleon force in the neutron-rich 8He nucleus. The results lay groundwork for studies of more sophisticated three-nucleon forces and enable investigation of isomultiplet energy differences, isospin-mixing matrix elements, and the intrinsic 2-alpha cluster structure of 8Be.
