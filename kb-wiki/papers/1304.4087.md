# Heavy Quarkonium moving in a Quark-Gluon Plasma

> By means of effective field theory techniques, we study the modifications of some properties of weakly coupled heavy quarkonium states propagating through a quark-gluon plasma at temperatures much smaller than the heavy quark mass, m_Q. Two different cases are considered, corresponding to two different hierarchies between the typical size of the bound state, r, the binding energy, E, the temperature, T, and the screening mass, m_D. The first case corresponds to the hierarchy m_Q >> 1/r >> T >> E >> m_D, relevant for moderate temperatures, and the second one to the hierarchy m_Q >> T >> 1/r, m_D >> E, relevant for studying the dissociation mechanism. In the first case we determine the perturbative correction to the binding energy and to the decay width of states with arbitrary angular momentum, finding that the width is a decreasing function of the velocity. A different behavior characterizes the second kinematical case, being the width of s-wave states a non-monotonic function of the velocity, increasing at moderate velocities and decreasing in the ultra-relativistic limit. We obtain a simple analytical expression of the decay width for T >> 1/r >> m_D >> E at moderate velocities, and we derive the s-wave spectral function for the more general case T >> 1/r, m_D >> E. A brief discussion of the possible experimental signatures as well as a comparison with the relevant lattice data are also presented.

## Key claim

For heavy quarkonium moving through a quark-gluon plasma, the thermal width depends on velocity in opposite ways depending on the hierarchy of scales: in the moderate-temperature regime (Case I) the width decreases monotonically with velocity, while in the dissociation regime (Case II) the width of s-wave states is non-monotonic, increasing at moderate velocities and decreasing in the ultra-relativistic limit. The authors note that the ultra-relativistic results are on less firm ground for QCD than for QED.

## Method

The authors use effective field theory (EFT) techniques, starting from pNRQCD at zero temperature and integrating out thermal scales to construct pNRQCD$_{\rm HTL}$ for a moving thermal bath. For Case I ($m_Q \gg 1/r \gg T \gg E \gg m_D$), they compute the thermal correction to the singlet potential and the singlet self-energy via matching, then evaluate first-order perturbation theory on hydrogen-like wavefunctions. For Case II ($m_Q \gg T \gg 1/r, m_D \gg E$), they use the potential derived from NRQCD$_{\rm HTL}$ and solve the Schrödinger equation numerically for the s-wave spectral function, with an analytic treatment for the subcase $T \gg 1/r \gg m_D \gg E$.

## Key numbers

- For $\Upsilon(1s)$ at rest in the QGP, the Bohr radius is taken as $a_0 \simeq 0.74$ GeV$^{-1}$ and the bottom quark mass as $m_Q = 4.881$ GeV, with $\Lambda_{\overline{\rm MS}} = 250$ MeV.
- In Case I, the s-wave binding energy shift is $\delta E_n^{s\text{-wave}} = \frac{2\pi C_F\alpha_s T^2}{3m_Q} + \frac{\pi N_c C_F\alpha_s^2 T^2 a_0 n^2}{2}$, independent of velocity at leading order.
- In Case I, the s-wave width is $\Gamma_n^{s\text{-wave}} = \frac{4\alpha_s C_F T\sqrt{1-v^2}}{3v} \left(-\frac{2E_n^c}{m_Q} + \frac{\alpha_s N_c}{m_Q a_0 n^2} + \frac{\alpha_s^2 N_c^2}{8}\right) \log\left(\frac{1+v}{1-v}\right)$, which vanishes as $v \to 1$.
- For Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{S_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV, the CMS collaboration measured $R_{AA}(\Upsilon(1s)) \simeq 0.6$, $R_{AA}(\Upsilon(2s)) \simeq 0.1$, and $R_{AA}(\Upsilon(3s)) < 0.1$.

## Context

This work extends the QED analysis of Escobedo (2011) to QCD, where the color-singlet and color-octet structure introduces new velocity-dependent terms absent in the hydrogen-atom case. It builds on the EFT framework for quarkonium in a thermal medium developed by Brambilla (2008, 2010) and Escobedo (2008, 2010), and on the finding by Laine (2006) that dissociation occurs via an imaginary part in the potential (Landau damping) rather than Debye screening. The results enable quantitative predictions for the velocity-dependent suppression of bottomonium states in heavy-ion collisions, and provide spectral functions that can be compared with lattice data from Rothkopf (2011) and Burnier (2012).
