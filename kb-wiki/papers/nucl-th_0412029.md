# Coupled-channel analysis of the $ω$-meson production in $πN$ and $γN$ reactions for c.m. energies up to 2 GeV

> The pion- and photon induced reactions for the final states $γN$, $πN$, $2πN$, $ηN$, and $ωN$ are studied within a coupled-channel effective Lagrangian approach in the energy region from the pion threshold up to 2 GeV. To investigate the role of the nucleon resonances in the different reactions we include all known states with spin-$\foh$,-$\fth$, and -$\ffh$ and masses below 2 GeV. We find a strong contribution from the $D_{15}(1675)$ resonance to the $πN \to ωN$ reaction. While the $F_{15}(1680)$ state only slightly influences the $ω$ meson production in the $πN$ scattering its role is enhanced in the $ω$ photoproduction due to the large electromagnetic coupling of this resonance. We predict the beam asymmetry $Σ_X$ to be a negative in the $γp \to ωp$ reaction near to the threshold. Above the 1.85 GeV the asymmetry is found to change its sign and becomes positive at forward directions. The presented findings can be experimentally tested at GRAAL, CLAS, and CB-ELSA facilities.

## Key claim

The authors find that the $D_{15}(1675)$ resonance contributes strongly to the $\pi N \to \omega N$ reaction, while the $F_{15}(1680)$ state only slightly influences $\omega$ meson production in $\pi N$ scattering but its role is enhanced in $\omega$ photoproduction due to large electromagnetic coupling; these findings depend on the inclusion of spin-5/2 resonances in a coupled-channel framework that satisfies unitarity.

## Method

The authors employ a coupled-channel effective Lagrangian approach using the K-matrix approximation to the Bethe-Salpeter equation, including the $\gamma N$, $\pi N$, $2\pi N$, $\eta N$, and $\omega N$ final states. They include all known nucleon resonances with spin-1/2, -3/2, and -5/2 and masses below 2 GeV, fixing resonance couplings by simultaneously fitting experimental data from all open channels in the energy region from pion threshold up to 2 GeV center-of-mass energy. The photoproduction reactions are treated perturbatively, with the interaction potential built from $s$-, $u$-, and $t$-channel tree-level Feynman diagrams using effective Lagrangians.

## Key numbers

* The $\pi NN$ coupling constant is found to be $g_{\pi NN} = 12.85$, slightly lower than the SAID group value of 13.13
* The $\eta NN$ coupling constant is found to be $g_{NN\eta} = 0.41$, corroborating previous small values
* The $\omega NN$ coupling constant is found to be $g_{NN\omega} = 4.19$, smaller than the Bonn model value of 15.9 for nucleon-nucleon scattering
* The Roper resonance $P_{11}(1440)$ is found to have mass $M = 1517$ MeV and total width $\Gamma = 608$ MeV
* The fit yields improved $\chi^2$ values for photon-induced reactions: $\chi^2 = 4.2$ (previously 6.25) for $\omega N$, $\chi^2 = 2.1$ (previously 3.95) for $K\Lambda$, and $\chi^2 = 1.6$ (previously 2.74) for $K\Sigma$ final states
* The beam asymmetry $\Sigma_X$ in $\gamma p \to \omega p$ is predicted to be negative near threshold and to change sign above 1.85 GeV, becoming positive at forward directions

## Context

This work extends the Giessen coupled-channel model previously developed by Penner et al. (2002) and Shklyar et al. (2004) by including spin-5/2 resonances, which were omitted in earlier analyses restricted to resonances with spin $J \leq 3/2$. The study addresses discrepancies among single-channel calculations by Zhao (2000), Titov and Lee (2002), Oh et al. (2000), and Babacan et al. (2001), which disagree on which resonances dominate $\omega$ photoproduction. The results enable experimental tests at GRAAL, CLAS, and CB-ELSA facilities, and provide constraints for extracting $\omega N$ elastic scattering information that cannot be obtained experimentally.
