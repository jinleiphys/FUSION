# Collective model with isovector pair and alpha-particle type correlations

> The collective Hamiltonian including isovector pairing and $α$-particle type correlation degrees of freedom is constructed. The Hamiltonian is applied to description of the relative energies of the ground states of even-even nuclei around $^{56}$Ni. A satisfactory description of the experimental data is obtained. A significant improvement of the agreement with the experimental data compared to our previous calculations is explained by inclusion in the Hamiltonian of the dynamical variables describing $α$-particle type correlations.

## Key claim

The collective Hamiltonian that includes both isovector pairing and α-particle type correlation degrees of freedom provides a satisfactory description of the relative ground-state energies of even-even nuclei around ⁵⁶Ni, with a significant improvement over previous calculations that included only isovector pairing, particularly for T=0 states (nuclei composed of α-particle-like quartets).

## Method

The authors constructed a collective Hamiltonian with dynamical variables for isovector pair correlations (a complex isovector z₁μ) and α-particle type correlations (a scalar variable α). The kinetic energy was quantized using the Pauli prescription, and the potential energy was taken as a Davidson potential for the pairing mode plus a harmonic oscillator for the α-mode. The Hamiltonian was applied to calculate relative ground-state energies of even-even nuclei around ⁵⁶Ni, with parameters fixed by the experimental energy of the N=1, T=1 state (4.5 MeV) and the average excitation energy of 0⁺ states with 4p-4h structure (taken as 3 MeV for the α-oscillation energy).

## Key numbers

* The experimental relative energy of the nucleus with N=1 and T=1 is 4.5 MeV, used to fix the relation between the parameters √(CΔ/BΔ), √(Cα/Bα), and ρ₀.
* The average excitation energy of 0⁺ states with 4p-4h structure in nuclei around ⁵⁶Ni is approximately 3.4 MeV, with values ranging from 2.56 MeV (⁵⁶Fe) to 4.28 MeV (⁴⁸Ca); the authors used √(Cα/Bα) = 3 MeV for their calculations.
* The best agreement with experimental data was obtained with the nondimensional parameter ρ₀⁴ = 6.
* Even for N=4, the ratio C̃Δ/CΔ is ≥ 0.99, indicating that the coupling correction to the Davidson potential is small.

## Context

This work extends the collective model for isovector pair correlations developed by Dussel et al. and Jolos et al., which was previously applied to ground-state energies around ⁵⁶Ni in Jolos (previous paper). The inclusion of α-particle type correlation degrees of freedom addresses the large deviations from experimental data seen for nuclei with even Z and N (α-particle-like systems) in those earlier calculations. The approach enables improved description of both T=0 and T≠0 states and provides expressions for two-nucleon and α-particle transfer reaction amplitudes, which can be tested against future experimental data.
