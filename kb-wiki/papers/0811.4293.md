# Turbulence in nonabelian gauge theory

> Kolmogorov wave turbulence plays an important role for the thermalization process following plasma instabilities in nonabelian gauge theories. We show that classical-statistical simulations in SU(2) gauge theory indicate a Kolmogorov scaling exponent known from scalar models. In the range of validity of resummed perturbation theory this result is shown to agree with analytical estimates. We study the effect of classical-statistical versus quantum corrections and demonstrate that the latter lead to the absence of turbulence in the far ultraviolet.

## Key claim

Classical-statistical simulations of SU(2) gauge theory following plasma instabilities indicate a Kolmogorov scaling exponent of κ = 4/3, matching the particle cascade exponent known from scalar models, but only in the quasi-stationary period before the system approaches classical thermal equilibrium; quantum corrections are shown to eliminate turbulence in the far ultraviolet.

## Method

The authors performed classical-statistical lattice gauge theory simulations of SU(2) Yang-Mills theory in Minkowski space-time using the Wilsonian lattice action, with oblate initial conditions mimicking the anisotropy after heavy-ion collisions. They solved the classical equations of motion in temporal axial gauge on 128³ lattices (with checks on 64³ and 256³ lattices), employed Coulomb gauge fixing, and extracted the equal-time correlation function of gauge fields as a function of momentum. Separately, they derived stationarity conditions from the two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action to identify scaling exponents analytically.

## Key numbers

- The best-fit occupation number exponent from classical-statistical simulations is κ = 1.35 ± 0.02, obtained by time-averaging over the quasi-stationary period 1000 ε⁻¹/⁴ ≲ t ≲ 4500 ε⁻¹/⁴.
- The fit range for longitudinal momenta was p_z ∈ [0.6, 2] ε¹/⁴ and for transverse momenta p_⊥ ∈ [0.6, 3] ε¹/⁴, where ε is the energy density.
- The exponential growth from plasma instabilities stops around t ≃ (200–250) ε⁻¹/⁴.
- The analytical Kolmogorov exponents for energy and particle cascades are κ = 5/3 and κ = 4/3, respectively, derived from the condition Δ = 4 − 3κ = 0 (particle cascade) or Δ = −1 (energy cascade).

## Context

This work extends the study of Kolmogorov wave turbulence from scalar field theories (Micha and Tkachev, 2004; Berges et al., 2008) to nonabelian gauge theories, specifically SU(2). The numerical result κ ≈ 1.35 disagrees with the value κ ≈ 2 reported by Arnold and Moore (2005) and Strickland (2007) based on Vlasov equations; the authors suggest that larger lattices may be needed to resolve this discrepancy. The finding enables future investigation of whether a nonperturbative infrared fixed point exists in nonequilibrium QCD, analogous to that observed in scalar theories by Berges et al. (2008).
