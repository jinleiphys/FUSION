# TMD Soft Function from Large-Momentum Effective Theory

> We study Euclidean formulations of the transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) soft function, which is a cross section for soft gluon radiations involving color charges moving in two conjugate lightcone directions in quantum chromodynamics. We show it is related to a special form factor of a pair of color sources traveling with nearly-lightlike velocities, which can be matched to TMD physical observables in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan process in the framework of large momentum effective theory. It can also be extracted by combining a large-momentum form factor of light meson and its leading TMD wave function. These formulations are useful for initiating nonperturbative calculations of this useful quantity.

# TMD Soft Function from Large-Momentum Effective Theory

## Key claim

The transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) soft function in the off-lightcone regularization scheme can be formulated as a Euclidean-time observable, either as a form factor of a boosted heavy-quark pair in heavy quark effective theory (HQET), or by combining a large-momentum form factor of a light meson with its leading TMD wave function, enabling nonperturbative lattice QCD calculations, provided that the rapidity separation is taken large while keeping the velocities timelike rather than strictly lightlike.

## Method

The authors show that the off-lightcone TMD soft function $S(b_\perp, Y, Y')$ equals the equal-time matrix element ${}_{v'}\langle\overline{Q}Q,\vec{b}_\perp|J(\vec{b}_\perp,v',v)|\overline{Q}Q,\vec{b}_\perp\rangle_v$ of a transition current between heavy-quark pair ground states with fixed transverse separation $\vec{b}_\perp$ and opposite nearly-lightlike velocities $v$ and $v'$. This matrix element is obtained by taking the large-time limit of a Wilson-loop correlator $W(t,t',b_\perp,Y,Y')$ and dividing by the square root of rectangular Wilson-loop vacuum expectations $Z(2t,b_\perp,Y)$ that subtract pinch-pole singularities. The Euclidean version is realized by a lattice HQET Lagrangian ${\cal L}_{{\rm HQET},E}=\psi_v^\dagger(x)(i\tilde v\cdot D_E)\psi_v(x)+\eta_v^\dagger(x)(i\tilde v\cdot D_E)\eta_v(x)$ with $\tilde v^\mu=\gamma(-i,-\beta,\vec 0_\perp)$. Alternatively, the intrinsic soft function $S_I(b_\perp)$ can be extracted from the ratio of a large-momentum light-meson form factor $F(b_\perp,P\!\cdot\!P')$ to the product of two quasi-TMDWFs $\widetilde\phi(x,b_\perp,P)$, with all perturbative hard kernels computed in matching.

## Key numbers

- The soft function in the off-lightcone scheme has the asymptotic form $S(b_\perp,Y,Y')=e^{(Y+Y')K(b_\perp)+{\cal D}(b_\perp)+{\cal O}(\exp[-(Y+Y')])}$, where $K(b_\perp)$ is the nonperturbative Collins-Soper kernel and ${\cal D}(b_\perp)$ is a scheme-dependent function.
- The on-lightcone soft functions have asymptotic forms $S_1(b_\perp,\delta,Y')=e^{(Y'-\ln \delta)K(b_\perp)+{\cal D}_1(b_\perp)+{\cal O}(\delta \exp(-Y))}$ and $S_2(b_\perp,\delta,\delta')=e^{-(\ln\delta\delta')K(b_\perp)+{\cal D}_2(b_\perp)+{\cal O}(\delta\delta')}$.
- The relation $2{\cal D}_1-{\cal D}_2={\cal D}$ holds between the scheme-dependent functions, and the intrinsic soft function satisfies $S_I=e^{-{\cal D}}$ in the off-lightcone scheme.
- The authors explicitly verified Eq. (13) (the ratio defining $S_I$) and the relation $2{\cal D}_1-{\cal D}_2={\cal D}$ at one-loop level.

## Context

This work extends the program of large-momentum effective theory (LaMET) (Ji, 2013; Ji, 2014) to the TMD soft function, which previously lacked a Euclidean formulation suitable for nonperturbative lattice calculations. It contrasts with earlier proposals by Ji (2014), Ji (2018), and Ebert (2019) for lattice soft functions, which used Euclidean gauge links with circular angles that cannot produce the arbitrarily large hyperbolic angles needed to match the cusp anomalous dimension of the physical soft function; the authors note that while those bent soft functions coincide with $S_I$ at one-loop order, they differ beyond one loop (Korchemsky, 1987). The results enable first-principle predictions of the Drell-Yan cross section at low transverse momentum via Eq. (18), combining quasi-TMDPDFs with the intrinsic soft function $S_I$, and open the door to nonperturbative calculations of the Collins-Soper kernel $K(b_\perp)$ and the scheme-dependent function ${\cal D}(b_\perp)$.
