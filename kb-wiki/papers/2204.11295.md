# Quark Nuclear Physics with Heavy Quarks

> Heavy quarks have been instrumental for progress in our exploration of strong interactions. Quarkonium in particular, a heavy quark-antiquark nonrelativistic bound state, has been at the root of several revolutions. Quarkonium is endowed with a pattern of separated energy scales qualifying it as special probe of complex environments. Its multiscale nature has made a description in Quantum Field Theory particularly difficult up to the advent of Nonrelativistic Effective Field Theories. We will focus on systems made by two or more heavy quarks. After considering some historical approaches based on the potential models and the Wilson loop approach, we will introduce the contemporary nonrelativistic effective field theory descriptions, in particular potential Nonrelativistic QCD which entails the Schoedinger equation as zero order problem, define the potentials as matching coefficients and allows systematic calculations of the physical properties. The effective field theory allows us to explore quarkonium properties in the realm of QCD. In particular it allows us to make calculations with unprecedented precision when high order perturbative calculations are possible and to systematically factorize short from long range contributions where observables are sensitive to the nonperturbative dynamics of QCD. Such effective field theory treatment can be extended at finite temperature and in presence of gluonic and light quark excitations. We will show that in this novel theoretical framework, quarkonium can play a crucial role for a number of problems at the frontier of our research, from the investigation of the confinement dynamics in strong interactions to the study of deconfinement and the phase diagram of nuclear matter, to the precise determination of Standard Model parameters up to the emergence of exotics X Y Z states of an unprecedented nature.

## Key claim

Heavy quarkonium systems, being endowed with a hierarchy of separated energy scales (hard scale \(m\), soft scale \(mv\), and ultrasoft scale \(mv^2\)), can be described systematically in quantum field theory only through nonrelativistic effective field theories (NREFTs) such as potential nonrelativistic QCD (pNRQCD), which implements the Schrödinger equation as the zero-order problem and defines potentials as matching coefficients, thereby enabling calculations with unprecedented precision when high-order perturbative calculations are possible and systematic factorization of short- from long-range contributions where observables are sensitive to nonperturbative QCD dynamics.

## Method

The paper reviews the construction of a hierarchy of NREFTs for heavy quark systems: nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) is obtained by integrating out modes at the hard scale \(m\), and potential nonrelativistic QCD (pNRQCD) is obtained by further integrating out the soft scale \(mv\), leaving ultrasoft gluons and light quarks as dynamical degrees of freedom. The static potential is derived from the Wilson loop via the Feynman–Kac formula \(V_0(r) = -\lim_{T\to\infty} (1/T) \log \langle W(\Gamma_0) \rangle\), and spin-dependent and velocity-dependent relativistic corrections are expressed in terms of generalized static Wilson loops containing field-strength insertions. The matching procedure enforces equivalence between QCD and the EFT at a given order in the expansion in the heavy-quark velocity \(v\), with the EFT Lagrangian factorized into matching coefficients encoding high-energy degrees of freedom and low-energy operators.

## Key numbers

- The \(J/\psi\) has a mass of 3 GeV and a width of 90 keV, at variance with typical hadron widths of several tens to hundreds of MeV.
- The charm quark mass \(m_c \sim 1.5\) GeV and the bottom quark mass \(m_b \sim 5\) GeV, both larger than the QCD confinement scale \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}\).
- The heavy-quark velocity squared is \(v^2 \sim 0.1\) for \(b\bar{b}\) systems and \(v^2 \sim 0.3\) for \(c\bar{c}\) systems.
- The Cornell potential parameters from a fit to the states are \(\kappa = 0.52\) and string tension \(\sigma = 0.182\) GeV\(^2\).
- Relativistic corrections are of order \(20\text{--}30\%\) for the charmonium spectrum and up to \(10\%\) for the bottomonium spectrum.

## Context

This review extends the historical approaches of potential models (Cornell potential by Eichten et al., 1978–1979) and the Wilson loop formalism (Eichten and Feinberg, 1980; Gromes, 1984; Barchielli et al., 1986–1988) by introducing the modern NREFT framework of NRQCD (Bodwin, Braaten, Lepage, 1995) and pNRQCD (Pineda and Soto, 1997; Brambilla et al., 1999). It enables systematic, model-independent calculations of quarkonium properties including spectrum, decays, transitions, and production, and extends to finite temperature, deconfinement studies, the phase diagram of nuclear matter, precise determination of Standard Model parameters, and the description of exotic X Y Z states.
