# Unraveling Generalized Parton Distributions Through Lorentz Symmetry and Partial DGLAP Knowledge

> Relying on the polynomiality property of generalized parton distributions, which roots on Lorentz covariance, we prove that it is enough to know them at vanishing- and low-skewness within the DGLAP region to obtain a unique extension to their entire support up to a D-term. We put this idea in practice using two methods: Reconstruction using artificial neural networks and finite-elements methods. We benchmark our results against standard models for generalized parton distributions. In agreement with the formal expectation, we obtain a very accurate reconstructions for a maximal value of the skewness as low as 20% of the longitudinal momentum fraction. This result might be relevant for reconstruction of generalized parton distribution from experimental and lattice QCD data, where computations are for now, restricted in skewness.

## Key claim

The authors prove that a generalized parton distribution (GPD) can be uniquely extended from the DGLAP region to the full kinematic domain (up to a D-term ambiguity) using only knowledge at vanishing and low skewness, specifically for skewness values as low as 20% of the longitudinal momentum fraction, relying on the polynomiality property rooted in Lorentz covariance.

## Method

The authors apply the covariant extension technique, which represents a GPD as the Radon transform of a double distribution (DD), and invert this transform using two numerical methods: artificial neural networks (ANNs) and finite-elements method (FEM) with second-order polynomial interpolation on a Delaunay triangulation of the DD domain. They benchmark the reconstruction against two standard GPD models: the algebraic model for the pion (Chouika 2017) and the Goloskokov-Kroll phenomenological parametrization (Goloskokov 2005), using GPD data restricted to the subset O_lambda = {|x| > xi} ∩ {x > 0} ∩ {0 ≤ xi < lambda x} for various lambda values.

## Key numbers

- Reconstruction achieves "very accurate" results for a maximal skewness value as low as lambda = 0.2 (20% of the longitudinal momentum fraction x)
- The FEM mesh used 17 elements with average area 0.03 (arbitrary units)
- The number of sampling lines was set to N_sample = 6n, where n is the number of interpolation nodes
- 250 replica sets of sampling lines were generated for uncertainty estimation, with outliers removed following a 5-sigma rule
- For the algebraic model with lambda = 1, 0.5, and 0.2, the reconstructed GPD at xi = 0.5 shows "astonishing" agreement with the analytical expression

## Context

This work extends the covariant extension technique previously developed by Chouika (2017) and Chavez (2021), which originally recovered the ERBL kinematic region from the full DGLAP region, to show that reconstruction is possible from only a restricted low-skewness subset of the DGLAP region. This result is relevant for extracting GPDs from experimental data (e.g., DVCS measurements) and lattice QCD computations, which are currently restricted to small skewness values, and enables the use of t-dependent PDFs (vanishing-xi GPDs) from lattice calculations as inputs for full GPD reconstruction.
