# Anisotropic transport properties of Hadron Resonance Gas in magnetic field

> An intense transient magnetic field is produced in high energy heavy-ion collisions mostly due to the spectator protons inside the two colliding nucleus. The magnetic field introduces anisotropy in the medium and hence the isotropic scalar transport coefficients become anisotropic and split into multiple components. Here we calculate the anisotropic transport coefficients shear, bulk viscosity, electrical conductivity, and the thermal diffusion coefficients for a multicomponent Hadron- Resonance-Gas (HRG) model for a non-zero magnetic field by using the Boltzmann transport equation in a relaxation time approximation (RTA). The anisotropic transport coefficient component along the magnetic field remains unaffected by the magnetic field, while perpendicular dissipation is governed by the interplay of the collisional relaxation time and the magnetic time scale, which is inverse of the cyclotron frequency. We calculate the anisotropic transport coefficients as a function of temperature and magnetic field using the HRG model. The neutral hadrons are unaffected by the Lorentz force and do not contribute to the anisotropic transports, we estimate within the HRG model the relative contribution of isotropic and anisotropic transports as a function of magnetic field and temperature. We also give an estimation of these anisotropic transport coefficients for the hadronic gas at finite baryon chemical potential.

## Key claim

The paper calculates anisotropic transport coefficients (shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, electrical conductivity, and baryon diffusion) for a multicomponent Hadron Resonance Gas model in a nonzero magnetic field using the Boltzmann transport equation in relaxation time approximation, but treats the relaxation time as a free parameter rather than estimating it from hadronic cross sections as done in prior work.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann transport equation in relaxation time approximation for a multicomponent Hadron Resonance Gas, using a Chapman-Enskog expansion with projection tensors constructed from magnetic and hydrodynamic degrees of freedom to systematically expand the off-equilibrium distribution function. The transport coefficients are decomposed into components parallel, perpendicular, and transverse (Hall) to the magnetic field direction, with the parallel component remaining unaffected by the magnetic field while perpendicular components depend on the ratio of collisional relaxation time to cyclotron time. The calculation includes all hadronic species in the HRG model, with neutral hadrons contributing only to isotropic transport and charged hadrons producing the anisotropic components.

## Key numbers

- The initial magnetic field in heavy-ion collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=200\) GeV is estimated to be \(eB\sim (1-10) m_{\pi}^2\)
- For massless QGP in the Stefan-Boltzmann limit, the degeneracy factor is \(g=47.5\) yielding \(P/T^4=5.2\), \(\epsilon/T^4=15.6\), and \(s/T^3=20.8\)
- The perpendicular electrical conductivity \(\sigma_\perp\) contains the factor \(\frac{\tau_c}{1+(\tau_c/\tau_{iB})^2}\) where \(\tau_{iB}=p_i^0/(eB)\) is the cyclotron time
- The perpendicular shear viscosity \(\eta_\perp\) contains the factor \(\frac{\tau_c}{1+(\tau_c/\tau_{iB})^2}\) while \(\eta'_\perp\) contains \(\frac{\tau_c}{1+(2\tau_c/\tau_{iB})^2}\)
- The Hall shear viscosity \(\eta_\times\) contains the factor \(\frac{\tau_c^2/\tau_{iB}}{1+(\tau_c/\tau_{iB})^2}\) and \(\eta'_\times\) contains \(\frac{\tau_c^2/\tau_{iB}}{\frac{1}{2}+2(\tau_c/\tau_{iB})^2}\)
- Bulk viscosity components satisfy \(\zeta_\parallel=\zeta_\perp\) and \(\zeta_\times=0\)

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of transport coefficients in hadronic matter with magnetic fields by Tuchin, Li, and others (shear viscosity), and by Sedrakian, Kerbikov, Hattori, and others (electrical conductivity), using a more systematic Chapman-Enskog expansion with projection tensors rather than the heuristic basis used in earlier works such as the HRG study by HM_TrB. The formalism enables systematic derivation of second and higher order non-resistive magnetohydrodynamic equations and provides estimates of anisotropic transport coefficients for hadronic gas at finite baryon chemical potential, which can serve as input for relativistic magnetohydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions.
