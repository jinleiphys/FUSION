# A note on scalar meson dominance

> We consider chiral perturbation theory with an explicit broad $σ$-meson and study its contribution to the scalar form factors of the pion and the nucleon. Our goal is to learn more about resonance saturation in the scalar sector.

# A note on scalar meson dominance

## Key claim
The broad σ-meson (f0(500)) contributes visibly to the scalar form factors of the pion and nucleon at low momentum transfer, but it does not saturate the imaginary part of the nucleon scalar form factor; the σ contribution drops faster than the two-loop chiral perturbation theory result, and for the pion form factor the f0(980) contribution (not captured in this approach) is needed to reproduce the dip near √t ≈ 1 GeV.

## Method
The authors supplement chiral perturbation theory with an explicit broad σ-meson propagator (with a co-moving energy-dependent width) and compute the one-loop imaginary parts of the scalar form factors of the pion and the nucleon. For the pion, the calculation uses lowest-order ππ vertices (chiral order O(q³)); for the nucleon, the calculation uses relativistic two-flavor baryon chiral perturbation theory at O(q⁴), including πN vertices from the dimension-one and dimension-two Lagrangians. The resulting spectral functions are compared to existing dispersion-theoretical and two-loop heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory results.

## Key numbers
- Mσ = 478 MeV, Γσ = 324 MeV (σ-meson mass and width used in the calculation)
- Mπ = 0.139 GeV, Fπ = 0.092 GeV, gσππ = 2.52 GeV (pion mass, decay constant, and σππ coupling)
- B = 0.7 GeV (scalar quark condensate parameter)
- For the nucleon case, low-energy constants used: c2 = 3.13 mN⁻¹, c3 = -5.61 mN⁻¹, c4 = 4.26 mN⁻¹ (from Hoferichter (2015)); two extreme cases for c1 are considered: c1 = 0 (complete saturation by σ) and c1 = -0.55 mN⁻¹ (partial saturation)
- The normalized scalar pion form factor at zero momentum transfer is FπS(0) = (0.99 ± 0.02) Mπ² (from Gasser (1990))
- The σ contribution to the pion form factor matches the dispersion-theoretical result up to √t ≈ 0.6 GeV

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of resonance saturation in the scalar sector, particularly the observation by Bernard (1996) that the low-energy constant c1 can be saturated by scalar meson exchange only for a specific ratio Mσ/gσNN. It contrasts with the vector and axial-vector sectors, where mesons clearly saturate low-energy constants (Ecker (1988), Donoghue (1988)). The approach differs from scale-chiral perturbation theory (Crewther (2013), Isham (1970), Schechter (1993)) and does not include the f0(980) contribution, which is needed for a complete description of the pion scalar form factor near 1 GeV. The results enable further study of whether the broad σ can be understood as a rescattering effect through non-perturbative pion loop resummation rather than as an explicit degree of freedom.
