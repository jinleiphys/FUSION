# Benchmark calculations for 3H, 4He, 16O and 40Ca with ab-initio coupled-cluster theory

> We present ab-initio calculations for 3H, 4He, 16O, and 40Ca based on two-nucleon low-momentum interactions Vlowk within coupled-cluster theory. For 3H and 4He, our results are within 70 keV and 10 keV of the corresponding Faddeev and Faddeev-Yakubovsky energies. We study in detail the convergence with respect to the size of the model space and the single-particle basis. For the heavier nuclei, we report practically converged binding energies and compare with other approaches.

## Key claim

Coupled-cluster theory at the CCSD(T) level reproduces exact Faddeev and Faddeev-Yakubovsky ground-state energies for 3H and 4He to within 70 keV and 10 keV respectively, but only when using low-momentum interactions Vlowk with a sharp cutoff and sufficiently large model spaces; the method also yields practically converged binding energies for 16O and 40Ca, though these are overbound compared to experiment because three-nucleon forces are omitted.

## Method

The authors employed the coupled-cluster method with single and double excitations (CCSD) and perturbative triples corrections (CCSD(T)), using a harmonic-oscillator single-particle basis. The two-nucleon low-momentum interaction Vlowk derived from the Argonne v18 potential was used, with a sharp cutoff of 1.9 fm^-1 for 3H and 4He and 2.1 fm^-1 for 16O and 40Ca. Convergence was studied by varying the maximum orbital angular momentum l, the model-space size N = 2n + l, and the oscillator frequency hbar-omega, with exponential extrapolations to infinite model space for the heavier nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For 3H, CCSD(T) energy = -8.40 MeV; exact Faddeev result = -8.470(2) MeV; agreement within 70 keV.
- For 4He, CCSD(T) energy = -29.18 MeV; exact Faddeev-Yakubovsky result = -29.19(5) MeV; agreement within 10 keV.
- For 16O, extrapolated infinite-model-space CCSD energy = -142.78 MeV; largest N=13 result = -142.40 MeV; finite-model-space error estimate about 0.5 MeV.
- For 16O, extrapolated triples corrections: CCSD(T) gives -5.45 MeV, CCSDT-1 gives -5.00 MeV; combined error estimate about 1 MeV.
- For 40Ca, extrapolated infinite-model-space CCSD energy = -492.6 MeV; N=8 result is about 4 MeV from convergence.
- For 40Ca, extrapolated CCSD(T) triples correction = -11.70 MeV; largest N=7 result = -10.21 MeV; total error estimate about 5 MeV.
- Table: extrapolated CCSD(T) binding energies: 4He = -29.2 MeV, 16O = -148.2 MeV, 40Ca = -502.9 MeV.

## Context

This work benchmarks coupled-cluster theory against exact Faddeev and Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations for light nuclei (Nogga et al., 2004) and extends the method to medium-mass nuclei 16O and 40Ca, where it is compared to importance-truncated no-core shell model results by Roth and Navratil (2007). The findings demonstrate that coupled-cluster theory meets the accuracy of established few-body methods for light systems and can provide benchmark energies for heavier nuclei, enabling future inclusion of three-nucleon forces (as initiated by Hagen et al., 2007) and studies with smooth cutoffs.
