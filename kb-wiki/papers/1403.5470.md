# From Kuo-Brown to today's realistic shell-model calculations

> This paper is an homage to the seminal work of Gerry Brown and Tom Kuo, where shell model calculations were performed for 18O and 18F using an effective interaction derived from the Hamada-Johnston nucleon-nucleon potential. That work has been the first successful attempt to provide a description of nuclear structure properties starting from the free nucleon-nucleon potential. We shall compare the approach employed in the 1966 paper with the derivation of a modern realistic shell-model interaction for sd-shell nuclei, evidencing the progress that has been achieved during the last decades.

## Key claim

This paper compares the original Kuo-Brown (1966) shell-model calculations for 18O and 18F, which used an effective interaction derived from the Hamada-Johnston potential, with modern calculations using a chiral N3LOW potential, showing that the modern approach leads to only moderately improved agreement with experiment, thereby confirming the substantial soundness of the original Kuo-Brown approach.

## Method

The authors constructed a shell-model effective Hamiltonian starting from the N3LOW chiral nucleon-nucleon potential (a low-momentum potential derived from chiral perturbation theory at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order with a sharp momentum cutoff at 2.1 fm-1). They employed the Q-box plus folded-diagram method within time-dependent degenerate linked-diagram perturbation theory, including all diagrams up to third order in the Q-box and summing the folded-diagram series to all orders using the Lee-Suzuki iteration method. For the Kuo-Brown comparison, they recalculated the spectra using the original approach where the effective interaction was derived from the Hamada-Johnston potential via the Brueckner G-matrix, including only the second-order "bubble" diagram (1p-1h core-polarization corrections) without the folded-diagram expansion.

## Key numbers

- For 18O with the Kuo-Brown interaction: the binding energy is overestimated by about 300 keV, and differences between observed and calculated spacings do not exceed 500 keV.
- For 18O with the N3LOW interaction: the energy of the 0_2+ state is overestimated by about 1.2 MeV above the experimental value.
- For 17O single-particle energies: the N3LOW calculation gives epsilon_{1/2+} - epsilon_{5/2+} = 0.835 MeV (experiment: 0.871 MeV); the Kuo-Brown calculation gives 0.11 MeV for the same spacing.
- For 17O spin-orbit splitting: the N3LOW calculation gives epsilon_{3/2+} - epsilon_{5/2+} = 6.281 MeV (experiment: 5.085 MeV); the Kuo-Brown calculation gives 5.51 MeV.
- The N3LOW ground-state energy of 17O relative to 16O is -3.211 MeV (experiment: -4.144 MeV); the Kuo-Brown value is -2.64 MeV.
- The N3LOW calculation with a Q-box up to second order only (matching the Kuo-Brown level of approximation) gives epsilon_{3/2+} - epsilon_{5/2+} = 4.945 MeV, close to the Kuo-Brown value of 5.51 MeV.

## Context

This paper commemorates the seminal work of Kuo and Brown (1966), which was the first successful attempt to describe nuclear structure properties starting from the free nucleon-nucleon potential, specifically using the Hamada-Johnston potential for 18O and 18F. The modern calculations extend this approach by using chiral perturbation theory potentials (Weinberg, 1979, 1990, 1991) and the Q-box plus folded-diagram method developed by Kuo and coworkers through the 1970s, which improves upon the original second-order perturbative expansion. The work shows that while modern techniques provide a more solid theoretical foundation, the original Kuo-Brown approach captured the main physics correctly, and the results enable future applications of realistic shell-model calculations to nuclei in different mass regions.
