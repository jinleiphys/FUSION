# Roles of $Δ(1232)$, $N^*(1520)$, and $N^*(1650)$ resonances in $γp\to π^0 π^0 p$ reaction within an effective Lagrangian approach

> Roles of the $Δ(1232)$, ${N}^{*}(1520)$, and ${N}^{*}(1650)$ resonances in the $γp\to{π}^{0}{π}^{0}p $ reaction near threshold is investigated within an effective Lagrangian approach. We have calculated the differential cross sections of the $γp\to{π}^{0}{π}^{0}p$ reaction by including the contributions from the $Δ(1232)$, ${N}^{*}(1520)$, and ${N}^{*}(1650)$ intermediate states decaying into $π^0 p$ via the $s$-channel nucleon pole and $t$-channel $ρ$ exchange, and found that the current experimental measurements can be well reproduced. The production of $Δ(1232)$ is mainly from the mechanism of the $s$-channel nucleon pole, while the ${N}^{*}(1520)$ and ${N}^{*}(1650)$ are produced from the mechanism of the $t$-channel $ρ$ exchange. It is expected that more experimental data on the $γp \to π^0 π^0 p$ reaction can be used to explore the properties of the low-lying excited baryon states and also the scalar $f_0(500)$ and $f_0(980)$ mesons.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the LEPS2/BGOegg experimental data on the $\gamma p \to \pi^0 \pi^0 p$ reaction can be reproduced by including only the $\Delta(1232)$, $N^*(1520)$, and $N^*(1650)$ resonances decaying into $\pi^0 p$, without needing the scalar mesons $f_0(500)$ and $f_0(980)$ that were the original focus of the experiment. The reproduction is achieved with the hedge that interference terms between different resonances are ignored and a constant background term is added.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach with tree-level Feynman diagrams, considering $s$-channel nucleon pole exchange for $\Delta(1232)$ production and $t$-channel $\rho^0$ meson exchange for $N^*(1520)$ and $N^*(1650)$ production. The coupling constants for the $\pi N R$ vertices were fixed from known partial decay widths, while the $\rho N R$ coupling constants for $N^*(1520)$ and $N^*(1650)$ were determined by fitting to the experimental $\pi^0 p$ invariant mass distributions. A contact term was included to maintain gauge invariance, and form factors with cutoff parameters were applied to account for the internal structure of hadrons.

## Key numbers

- The $\Delta(1232)$ resonance mass used is 1210 MeV with width 100 MeV and branching fraction to $\pi N$ of 0.994, giving $g^2_{\pi N \Delta} = 1.59$
- The $N^*(1520)$ resonance mass used is 1505 MeV with width 110 MeV and branching fraction to $\pi N$ of 0.60, giving $g^2_{\pi N N^*(1520)} = 2.55$
- The $N^*(1650)$ resonance mass used is 1670 MeV with width 110 MeV and branching fraction to $\pi N$ of 0.60, giving $g^2_{\pi N N^*(1650)} = 0.41$
- The fitted coupling constants are $g_{\rho N N^*(1520)} = 33.34$ and $g_{\rho N N^*(1650)} = 30.37$
- The scaling factors used are $c_1 = 1.08 \times 10^6$ and $c_2 = 5.14 \times 10^2$
- The cutoff parameters are $\Lambda = 1.0$ GeV for $\Delta(1232)$, $\Lambda = 2.0$ GeV for $N^*(1520)$ and $N^*(1650)$, and $\Lambda = 1.1$ GeV for the proton pole
- The $\rho$ meson exchange cutoff is $\Lambda_\rho = 1.3$ GeV
- The total energy range of the experimental data is $1898 < W < 2320$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the LEPS2/BGOegg Collaboration (2023) data on $\gamma p \to \pi^0 \pi^0 p$, which was originally aimed at studying the $f_0(980)$ scalar meson. The paper shows that the bump structures in the $\pi^0 p$ invariant mass distributions correspond to the $\Delta(1232)$, $N^*(1520)$, and $N^*(1650)$ resonances, consistent with the known resonance spectrum from the Particle Data Group (2024). The results enable future studies of the $\rho N N^*(1520)$ and $\rho N N^*(1650)$ couplings and provide a baseline for separating the contributions of scalar mesons $f_0(500)$ and $f_0(980)$ when more precise experimental data become available.
