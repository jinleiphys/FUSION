# A Relativistic Model for the Electromagnetic Structure of Baryons from the 3rd Resonance Region

> We present some predictions for the $γ^\ast N \to N^\ast$ transition amplitudes, where $N$ is the nucleon, and $N^\ast$ is a nucleon excitation from the third resonance region. First we estimate the transition amplitudes associated with the second radial excitation of the nucleon, interpreted as the $N(1710)$ state, using the covariant spectator quark model. After that, we combine some results from the covariant spectator quark model with the framework of the single quark transition model, to make predictions for the $γ^\ast N \to N^\ast$ transition amplitudes, where $N^\ast$ is a member of the $SU(6)$-multiplet $[70,1^-]$. The results for the $γ^\ast N \to N(1520)$ and $γ^\ast N \to N(1535)$ transition amplitudes are used as input to the calculation of the amplitudes $A_{1/2}$, $A_{3/2}$, associated with the $γ^\ast N \to N(1650)$, $γ^\ast N \to N(1700)$, $γ^\ast N \to Δ(1620)$, and $γ^\ast N \to Δ(1700)$ transitions. Our estimates are compared with the available data. In order to facilitate the comparison with future experimental data at high $Q^2$, we derived also simple parametrizations for the amplitudes, compatible with the expected falloff at high $Q^2$.

# A Relativistic Model for the Electromagnetic Structure of Baryons from the 3rd Resonance Region

## Key claim

The covariant spectator quark model provides predictions for the gamma* N to N(1710) transition amplitudes that differ from recent data in magnitude for A_{1/2} and in sign for S_{1/2} at Q^2 less than or equal to 4 GeV^2, suggesting either that the estimate is valid only for larger Q^2 values or that the interpretation of N(1710) as the second radial excitation of the nucleon is not valid. For the negative parity resonances from the [70,1^-] SU(6)-multiplet, the combination of the covariant spectator quark model with the single quark transition model yields predictions that compare well with data for N(1650) and Delta(1620) at Q^2 greater than 2 GeV^2.

## Method

The covariant spectator quark model treats baryons as three-quark systems reduced to a quark-diquark system, with the diquark as an on-mass-shell spectator particle of effective mass m_D. The electromagnetic interaction is described by photon coupling to constituent quarks in the relativistic impulse approximation, with quark form factors parametrized by a vector meson dominance mechanism calibrated to nucleon form factor data and lattice QCD data for decuplet baryons. For the [70,1^-] multiplet, the single quark transition model parametrizes the helicity amplitudes A_{1/2} and A_{3/2} for six resonances using three Q^2-dependent coefficients A, B, and C, which are determined from the covariant spectator quark model results for the gamma* N to N(1520) and gamma* N to N(1535) transitions.

## Key numbers

- The mixing angle for the N 1/2- states (S_{11}) is theta_S = 31 degrees; the mixing angle for the N 3/2- states (D_{13}) is theta_D = 6 degrees.
- For the N(1700) state, the high Q^2 parametrization gives D = -8.51 x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2} with Lambda^2 = 2.82 GeV^2 for A_{1/2}, and D = 4.36 x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2} with Lambda^2 = 3.61 GeV^2 for A_{3/2}.
- For the Delta(1700) state, the high Q^2 parametrization gives D = 39.22 x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2} with Lambda^2 = 2.69 GeV^2 for A_{1/2}, and D = 42.15 x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2} with Lambda^2 = 8.42 GeV^2 for A_{3/2}.
- For the N(1650) state, the high Q^2 parametrization gives D = 68.90 x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2} with Lambda^2 = 3.35 GeV^2 for A_{1/2}.
- The expected falloff for large Q^2 is A_{1/2} proportional to 1/Q^3 and A_{3/2} proportional to 1/Q^5, based on perturbative QCD arguments from Carlson.
- For the Delta(1620) amplitude A_{1/2}, a parametrization proportional to (Lambda^2/(Lambda^2 + Q^2))^{5/2} with Lambda^2 = 1 GeV^2 is used instead of the expected 1/Q^3 behavior.

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator quark model previously applied to the nucleon, the Roper resonance N(1440), and the N(1520) and N(1535) states (Ramalho, 2015; Ramalho, 2016) to the third resonance region (W = 1.4 to 1.8 GeV). The combination with the single quark transition model (Burkert, 2003) enables predictions for six resonances from the [70,1^-] SU(6)-multiplet. The results are compared with data from CLAS, MAID, and the Particle Data Group, and are intended to guide future experiments at the Jefferson Lab 12 GeV upgrade. The interpretation of N(1710) as the second radial excitation is contrasted with alternative interpretations including the hypercentral quark model (Santopinto, 2012) and the interacting quark diquark model (Santopinto, 2005; Santopinto, 2015).
