# The line shapes of the $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ in the elastic and inelastic channels revisited

> The most recent experimental data for all measured production and decay channels of the bottomonium-like states $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ are analysed simultaneously using solutions of the Lippmann-Schwinger equations which respect constraints from unitarity and analyticity. The interaction potential in the open-bottom channels $B^{(*)}\bar{B}^{*}+\mbox{c.c.}$ contains short-range interactions as well as one-pion exchange. It is found that the long-range interaction does not affect the line shapes as long as only $S$ waves are considered. Meanwhile, the line shapes can be visibly modified once $D$ waves, mediated by the strong tensor forces from the pion exchange potentials, are included. However, in the fit they get balanced largely by a momentum dependent contact term that appears to be needed also to render the results for the line shapes independent of the cut-off. The resulting line shapes are found to be insensitive to various higher-order interactions included to verify stability of the results. Both $Z_b$ states are found to be described by the poles located on the unphysical Riemann sheets in the vicinity of the corresponding thresholds. In particular, the $Z_b(10610)$ state is associated with a virtual state residing just below the $B\bar{B}^{*}/\bar B{B}^{*}$ threshold while the $Z_b(10650)$ state most likely is a shallow state located just above the $B^*\bar{B}^{*}$ threshold.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the line shapes of the Zb(10610) and Zb(10650) are insensitive to various higher-order interactions once a momentum-dependent contact term is promoted to leading order to balance the strong modifications from D-wave tensor forces in the one-pion exchange potential, with the Zb(10610) identified as a virtual state just below the B Bbar* threshold and the Zb(10650) as a shallow state just above the B* Bbar* threshold.

## Method

The authors performed a simultaneous fit to all measured production and decay channels of the Zb states using numerical solutions of coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equations that respect unitarity and analyticity. The interaction potential in the open-bottom channels B(*) Bbar* included short-range contact interactions at leading order (momentum-independent) and next-to-leading order (momentum-dependent), plus one-pion exchange and one-eta exchange potentials decomposed into partial waves including S-wave and D-wave channels. The production amplitudes were solved numerically and used to compute differential widths for elastic channels (B Bbar*, B* Bbar*) and inelastic channels (Upsilon(nS) pi, hb(mP) pi), with the chi-squared function built from both line shapes and total branching fractions.

## Key numbers

- The typical on-shell relative momentum in the B* Bbar channel near the B* Bbar* threshold is p_typ = sqrt(m_B * delta) ~ 500 MeV, where delta = m_B* - m_B = 45 MeV
- The expansion parameter controlling convergence is chi = p_typ / Lambda_h ~ 0.5, with the hard scale Lambda_h ~ 1 GeV
- The pion decay constant used is f_pi = 92.4 MeV
- The heavy-meson-pion coupling constant is g_b = g_c ~ 0.57, extracted from the D*+ partial decay width Gamma(D*+ -> D0 pi+) = 56.46 keV

## Context

This work extends previous analyses by Hanhart (2015) and Guo (2016) that used analytic parameterizations with only S-wave short-range interactions, by now including nonperturbative one-pion exchange and D-wave contributions via direct numerical solution of Lippmann-Schwinger equations. The results contradict the claim by Swanson (2014) that the Zb states could be simple kinematical cusps, instead finding near-threshold poles consistent with the molecular interpretation. The analysis enables future studies of the spin partners of the Zb states predicted by heavy-quark spin symmetry, where reliable predictions require nonperturbative treatment of pion exchange as argued by Baru (2016, 2017).
