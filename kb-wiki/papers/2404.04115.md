# Significance of the refraction effect on the $p$-$d$ elementary process in the ($p$,$pd$) reaction

> The proton-induced deuteron knockout reaction, ($p$,$pd$), is one of the interests in the studies for probing the deuteron-like $p$-$n$ correlation in nuclei. According to a recent study of the inclusive deuteron-induced reaction, $(d,d'x)$, the refraction effect of the deuteron has a significant effect on the elementary process, nucleon-deuteron ($N$-$d$) binary scattering inside a nucleus, of the reaction. In the paper, it is shown that proper treatment of the local $N$-$d$ relative momentum in the elementary process is crucial in $(d,d'x)$ reactions at $100$ MeV and below. In the present work, we investigate the deuteron refraction effect in the exclusive ($p$,$pd$) reactions. We also discuss the incident energy dependence of the refraction effect. The refraction effect on the $p$-$d$ elementary process is taken into account by the local semiclassical approximation to the distorted waves. The results are compared with those obtained with the asymptotic momentum approximation, which is standardly applied to the distorted wave impulse approximation framework. It is shown that the refraction effect drastically changes the energy sharing distribution of the $^{16}$O($p$,$pd$)$^{14}$N reaction at 101.3 MeV and gives a better agreement with experimental data. In contrast, it is confirmed that the effect is negligibly small at 250 MeV. We have clarified that the deuteron refraction effect is significant in the $^{16}$O($p$,$pd$)$^{14}$N reaction at 101.3 MeV and the experimental data are well reproduced. The refraction effect plays a significant role in both the shape and magnitude of the ($p$,$pd$) cross section, while the effect is negligible at 250 MeV.

## Key claim

The deuteron refraction effect, implemented via the local semiclassical approximation (LSCA) to distorted waves, significantly changes the shape and magnitude of the energy sharing distribution in the $^{16}$O($p$,$pd$)$^{14}$N reaction at 101.3 MeV, but this effect is negligibly small at 250 MeV.

## Method

The authors applied the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) framework to the ($p$,$pd$) reaction, incorporating the deuteron refraction effect through the local semiclassical approximation (LSCA) which evaluates the local $p$-$d$ relative momentum at each point in the nuclear interior. They compared these results with the standard asymptotic momentum approximation (AMA), which uses only asymptotic momenta, for the $^{16}$O($p$,$pd$)$^{14}$N reaction at two incident energies. The $p$-$d$ elementary cross section was prepared by fitting experimental elastic scattering data from 5 to 800 MeV, and the distorted waves were generated using phenomenological optical potentials.

## Key numbers

* Spectroscopic factor $S_d = 1.74$ extracted from the LSCA analysis of $^{16}$O($p$,$pd$)$^{14}$N at 101.3 MeV, consistent with the 1$p$ shell model prediction of 1.75 from Cohen (1970)
* Deuteron separation energy of $^{16}$O is 20.74 MeV, with an additional 3.95 MeV excitation energy of the $^{14}$N residue, giving an effective separation energy of 24.7 MeV
* At 250 MeV, the LSCA and AMA results are nearly equivalent, showing negligible refraction effect
* The $p$-$d$ elastic cross section fitting covers energies from 5 to 800 MeV, using 2 Gaussian terms for 5-20 MeV, 3 terms for 35-250 MeV, and 4 terms for 425-800 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of deuteron refraction effects from inclusive $(d,d'x)$ reactions by Nakada (2023) to exclusive ($p$,$pd$) knockout reactions, and contrasts with the earlier finding by Yoshida (2016) that refraction was small in $^{120}$Sn($p$,$p\alpha$)$^{116}$Cd due to strong absorption of alpha particles. The results enable future combination with the CDCCIA framework by Chazono (2022), which accounts for deuteron breakup and reformation, for more accurate description of ($p$,$pd$) reactions. The analysis also resolves discrepancies with earlier work by Samanta (1982) by using modern optical potentials and a wider coverage of $p$-$d$ elastic scattering data.
