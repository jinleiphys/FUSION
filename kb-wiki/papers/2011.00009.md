# Deuteron Uncertainties in the Determination of Proton PDFs

> We evaluate the uncertainties due to nuclear effects in global fits of proton parton distribution functions (PDFs) that utilise deep-inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan data on deuterium targets. To do this we use an iterative procedure to determine proton and deuteron PDFs simultaneously, each including the uncertainties in the other. We apply this procedure to determine the nuclear uncertainties in the SLAC, BCDMS, NMC and DYE866/NuSea fixed target deuteron data included in the NNPDF3.1 global fit. We show that the effect of the nuclear uncertainty on the proton PDFs is small, and that the increase in overall uncertainties is insignificant once we correct for nuclear effects.

## Key claim

The effect of nuclear uncertainties in deuterium data on proton PDFs is small, and the increase in overall uncertainties is insignificant once nuclear effects are corrected for, provided that the deuteron and proton PDFs are determined simultaneously in a self-consistent iterative procedure that avoids reliance on external nuclear PDF models.

## Method

The authors split the NNPDF3.1 global dataset into proton-only and deuteron-only subsets, then performed an iterative procedure: first fitting proton PDFs from proton data alone, then fitting deuteron PDFs from deuteron data using a theoretical covariance matrix that accounts for the proton PDF uncertainty in mixed deuteron data (such as NMC ratios and DYE866/NuSea Drell-Yan ratios). A deuteron theoretical covariance matrix was then constructed from the difference between predictions using deuteron PDFs and isoscalar PDFs, and added to the experimental covariance matrix of the deuteron data in a subsequent global proton fit; this process was iterated to convergence.

## Key numbers

- The NNPDF3.1 global dataset contains 3978 data points, of which 418 (about 10%) come from experiments using deuterium targets: SLAC, BCDMS, NMC, and DYE866/NuSea.
- The global chi-squared per data point was 1.16 for both the first and second iteration global fits (global-ite1-dw and global-ite2-dw), indicating convergence after one iteration.
- The deuteron correction generally amounts to a few percent for all experiments considered, with the ratio of deuteron to isoscalar predictions mostly compatible with one except at higher x and Q where the correction is negative.
- Distances between central values of PDFs from successive iterations were at most of order two or three, confirming statistical equivalence between iterations.

## Context

This work extends the approach developed for heavy nuclear targets in Ball et al. (2018) to deuterium, avoiding the dependence on externally determined nuclear PDF sets (such as DSSZ, nCTEQ15, and EPPS16) by fitting deuteron PDFs directly from the deuteron data. The procedure is similar to the self-consistent method set out in Ball et al. (2018) for simultaneous determination of PDFs and fragmentation functions. The resulting deuteron PDFs are compared with the nNNPDF2.0 determination (AbdulKhalek et al., 2020) and the parametric correction used in MMHT2014 (Harland-Lang et al., 2014); the new estimate is the most conservative, being free from model dependence, and enables a faithful quantification of nuclear uncertainties in future proton PDF determinations.
