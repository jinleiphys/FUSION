# Observables from a solution of 1+3 dimensional relativistic hydrodynamics

> In this paper we analyze a 1+3 dimensional solution of relativistic hydrodynamics. We calculate momentum distribution and other observables from the solution and compare them to measurements from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). We find that the solution we analyze is compatible with the data. In the last several years many numerical models were tested, but it is the first time that an exact, parametric, 1+3 dimensional relativistic solution is compared to data.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first comparison of an exact, parametric, 1+3 dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic solution to experimental data from RHIC, finding that the solution is compatible with the measured observables. However, the solution is accelerationless (constant expansion rates) and assumes instantaneous freeze-out, which are significant simplifications.

## Method

The authors extract observables from the ellipsoidally symmetric, relativistic, exact hydrodynamic solution of Csörgő (2003) by calculating the invariant momentum distribution, elliptic flow, and Bose-Einstein correlation (HBT) radii using a second-order Gaussian approximation. They fit these analytic formulas to PHENIX data from 200 GeV Au+Au collisions: spectra and HBT radii for positive pions at 0-30% centrality, and elliptic flow for π±, K±, protons, and antiprotons at 0-92% centrality. The fit parameters include central freeze-out temperature T₀, freeze-out proper time τ₀, temperature gradient b, expansion rates, and expansion anisotropy ε.

## Key numbers

- Central freeze-out temperature T₀ = 199 ± 3 MeV from spectra and HBT fits (0-30% centrality); T₀ = 204 ± 7 MeV from elliptic flow fits (0-92% centrality)
- Freeze-out proper time τ₀ = 7.7 ± 0.1 fm/c from spectra and HBT fits
- Expansion eccentricity ε = 0.80 ± 0.02 (spectra/HBT) and ε = 0.34 ± 0.03 (elliptic flow)
- Temperature gradient parameter u_t²/b = -0.84 ± 0.08 (spectra/HBT) and u_t²/b = -0.34 ± 0.01 (elliptic flow)
- Assuming an average equation of state parameter κ ≈ 10, the initial central temperature is approximately 370 MeV at τ_initial = 1 fm/c
- χ²/NDF = 171/41 for spectra and HBT fits; χ²/NDF = 256/34 for elliptic flow fits; with an estimated 3% theory error these reduce to 24/41 and 66/34 respectively

## Context

This work extends the search for exact hydrodynamic solutions beyond the 1+1 dimensional Landau-Khalatnikov (1953) and Hwa-Bjorken (1974, 1982) solutions, which cannot describe transverse dynamics. It is the first test of the relativistic, 1+3 dimensional exact solution of Csörgő (2003) against RHIC data, complementing numerical hydrodynamic models that have been compared to data previously. The results enable extraction of freeze-out parameters directly from analytic formulas and, when combined with an assumed equation of state, allow estimation of initial conditions such as the initial temperature.
