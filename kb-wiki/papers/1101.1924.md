# Astrophysical $S$ factor for the ${}^{15}{\rm N}(p,γ){}^{16}{\rm O}$ reaction from $R$-matrix analysis and asymptotic normalization coefficient for ${}^{16}{\rm O} \to {}^{15}{\rm N} + p$. Is any fit acceptable?

> The $^{15}{\rm N}(p,γ)^{16}{\rm O}$ reaction provides a path from the CN cycle to the CNO bi-cycle and CNO tri-cycle. The measured astrophysical factor for this reaction is dominated by resonant capture through two strong $J^π=1^{-}$ resonances at $E_{R}= 312$ and 962 keV and direct capture to the ground state. Recently, a new measurement of the astrophysical factor for the $^{15}{\rm N}(p,γ)^{16}{\rm O}$ reaction has been published [P. J. LeBlanc {\it et al.}, Phys. Rev. {\bf C 82}, 055804 (2010)]. The analysis has been done using the $R$-matrix approach with unconstrained variation of all parameters including the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC). The best fit has been obtained for the square of the ANC $C^{2}= 539.2$ fm${}^{-1}$, which exceeds the previously measured value by a factor of $\approx 3$. Here we present a new $R$-matrix analysis of the Notre Dame-LUNA data with the fixed within the experimental uncertainties square of the ANC $C^{2}=200.34$ fm${}^{-1}$. Rather than varying the ANC we add the contribution from a background resonance that effectively takes into account contributions from higher levels. Altogether we present 8 fits, five unconstrained and three constrained. In all the fits the ANC is fixed at the previously determined experimental value $C^{2}=200.34$ fm${}^{-1}$. For the unconstrained fit with the boundary condition $B_{c}=S_{c}(E_{2})$, where $E_{2}$ is the energy of the second level, we get $S(0)=39.0 \pm 1.1 $ keVb and normalized ${\tilde χ}^{2}=1.84$, i.e. the result which is similar to [P. J. LeBlanc {\it et al.}, Phys. Rev. {\bf C 82}, 055804 (2010)]. From all our fits we get the range $33.1 \leq S(0) \leq 40.1$ keVb which overlaps with the result of [P. J. LeBlanc {\it et al.}, Phys. Rev. {\bf C 82}, 055804 (2010)]. We address also physical interpretation of the fitting parameters.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the best-fit asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) obtained by LeBlanc et al. (2010) from unconstrained R-matrix fitting of the 15N(p,γ)16O reaction, C² = 539.2 fm⁻¹, is unphysical because it exceeds the previously measured experimental value by a factor of approximately 3; the authors demonstrate that equally good fits can be obtained by fixing the ANC at the experimentally determined value (C² = 200.34 fm⁻¹) and instead adding a background resonance to account for missing physics.

## Method

The authors performed a two-level, two-channel R-matrix analysis of the Notre Dame-LUNA experimental data from LeBlanc et al. (2010), using the AZURE R-matrix code. Rather than allowing the ANC to vary as an unconstrained parameter, they fixed the square of the ANC at C² = 200.34 fm⁻¹ (C = 14.154 fm⁻¹/2) and added a coherent contribution from a background resonance at 5.07 MeV to effectively account for contributions from higher-lying 1⁻ levels. They produced eight total fits: five unconstrained fits (varying all other parameters) and three constrained fits (where proton and alpha reduced width amplitudes were fixed to values from prior analysis of the 15N(p,α)12C reaction), using two different boundary conditions (Bc = Sc(E₂) and Bc = Sc(E₁)) and either 113 data points or 70 low-energy data points.

## Key numbers

- Square of the ANC fixed at C² = 200.34 fm⁻¹ (C = 14.154 fm⁻¹/2), within the experimental interval 13.86 ± 0.91 fm⁻¹/2 from Mukhamedzhanov et al. (2008)
- For the unconstrained fit A(113) with boundary condition Bc = Sc(E₂): S(0) = 39.0 ± 1.1 keVb with normalized χ̃² = 1.84
- For the unconstrained fit A(113) with boundary condition Bc = Sc(E₁): S(0) = 37.2 keVb with normalized χ̃² = 1.76
- Range from all fits: 33.1 ≤ S(0) ≤ 40.1 keVb
- For the unconstrained fit without background resonance with Bc = Sc(E₂): S(0) = 34.2 keVb with χ̃² = 2.6
- For the unconstrained fit without background resonance with Bc = Sc(E₁): S(0) = 34.6 keVb with χ̃² = 2.5
- Observable partial widths from fit A(113) for the second resonance: proton width Γ̃₂p = 82.8 keV, alpha width Γ̃₂α = 63.2 keV, radiative width Γ₂γ = 63.6 eV
- Background resonance radiative width from fit A(113) with Bc = Sc(E₂): Γγ(BG) = 354.9 eV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the 15N(p,γ)16O reaction, which provides the path from the CN cycle to the CNO bi-cycle and tri-cycle in stellar hydrogen burning. The authors directly challenge the unconstrained R-matrix fit of LeBlanc et al. (2010), which obtained S(0) = 39.6 ± 2.6 keVb but with an ANC (C² = 539 ± 138 fm⁻¹) that the authors argue is unphysical based on their prior measurement of the ANC from the 15N(³He,d)16O reaction (Mukhamedzhanov et al., 2008) and on spectroscopic factors from 16O(e,e'p)15N measurements (Leuschner et al.; Kramer et al.). The work parallels a similar situation in the 14N(p,γ)15O reaction analysis (Mukhamedzhanov et al., 2003; Solar Fusion paper), where the best fit required an unphysical ANC and was rejected in favor of fits with the recommended ANC. The results enable more reliable determination of the CNO cycle branching ratio, with the authors finding that for every 2200 ± 300 cycles of the main CN cycle, one CN catalyst is lost due to this reaction, rather than 1200 ± 100 cycles from earlier data (Rolfs and Rodney, 1974).
