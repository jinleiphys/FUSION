# Proton-proton and deuteron-gold collisions at RHIC

> We try to understand recent data on proton-proton and deuteron-gold collisions at RHIC, employing a modified parton model approach.

## Key claim

The authors attempt to understand recent RHIC data on proton-proton and deuteron-gold collisions using a modified parton model approach, but the work is presented as a qualitative exploration rather than a definitive quantitative fit, with the explicit hedge that "at this point there is no fine-tuning employed" and they "first want to understand the qualitative features" of their effective treatment.

## Method

The authors employ a modified parton model based on cut diagram techniques with strict energy conservation and Markov chain numerics, where parton ladders (Pomerons) between projectile and target are treated as effective projectile/target excitations. Screening corrections are implemented by modifying the power-law behavior of simple diagrams via a parameter epsilon that depends on the number Z of "close" partons, which increases with decreasing impact parameter and increasing energy. The formalism represents multiple, interacting ladders through string excitations whose properties (specifically the average transverse momentum of string breaks) depend on Z, with the excitation mass distributed as 1/M^(2*alpha).

## Key numbers

- For pp collisions, the parameter Z (number of close partons) is less than or approximately 2; for d-Au collisions, Z is less than or approximately 6.
- The function f(Z) controlling the average transverse momentum of string breaks uses f_max = 3 and alpha = 0.3, with f(Z) = min(f_max, 1 + alpha * Z).
- The screening parameter epsilon is given by epsilon = epsilon_max * (1 - 1/sqrt(1 - (log(1 + 3*Z))^2)).
- The nuclear modification factor R for d-Au collisions is greater than one for central collisions, approaches one with decreasing centrality, and goes below one for peripheral collisions.

## Context

This work extends the NEXUS model approach to describe RHIC data on the nuclear modification factor, which shows different behavior in Au-Au collisions (R much smaller than one for central collisions) versus d-Au collisions (R greater than one for central collisions). The authors reference data from PHENIX (2004) and BRAHMS (2004) for the rapidity dependence of the nuclear modification factor, and PHOBOS (2004) for pp pseudorapidity distributions. The approach enables a qualitative understanding of how interacting parton ladders and screening corrections affect the centrality and pseudorapidity dependence of particle production in nuclear collisions at RHIC energies.
