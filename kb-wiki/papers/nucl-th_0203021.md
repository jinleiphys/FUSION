# Matrix Elements of Random Operators and Discrete Symmetry Breaking in Nuclei

> It is shown that several effects are responsible for deviations of the intensity distributions from the Porter-Thomas law. Among these are genuine symmetry breaking, such as isospin; the nature of the transition operator; truncation of the Hilbert space in shell model calculations and missing transitions

## Key claim

Deviations from the Porter-Thomas law for transition strength distributions can arise not only from genuine discrete symmetry breaking (such as isospin mixing) but also from the nature of the transition operator itself, truncation of the Hilbert space in shell model calculations, and missing transitions in experimental data; these effects can mimic or obscure true symmetry violation.

## Method

The authors construct a random matrix model based on the deformed Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (DGOE), where the Hamiltonian is written as H = H0 + λ H1, with λ controlling the transition from two uncoupled GOE blocks (λ=0, conserved symmetry) to a single GOE (λ=1, fully chaotic). They define two classes of random operators: symmetry-conserving operators OC that act only within blocks, and symmetry-breaking operators OB that couple blocks, plus a generic operator O = (1-q)OC + qOB with mixing parameter q. Numerical simulations of the distribution of squared matrix elements |Tkl|^2 are performed for matrices of dimension N=150 with block sizes M1=80 and M2=70, and the results are compared to the Porter-Thomas distribution.

## Key numbers

- Matrix dimension N=150 with block sizes M1=80 and M2=70 used for numerical simulations of the transition GOE → 2GOE
- Chaoticity parameter λ = 0.032 fixed to account for isospin breaking seen in the eigenvalue distribution of 26Al from Mitchell et al. (1988)
- For the symmetry-conserving operator OC, the distribution evolves with λ as Porter-Thomas → non-Porter-Thomas → Porter-Thomas as λ increases
- Missing transitions simulated by eliminating matrix elements below a threshold produce a distribution with enhanced maximum shifted left, matching the qualitative trend of data from Adams, Mitchell, and Shriner (1998)

## Context

This work extends the deformed Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (DGOE) approach developed by Hussein and Pato (1993, 1998) and provides a theoretical framework to interpret deviations from the Porter-Thomas law observed in experimental data. The authors specifically address the isospin mixing measurements in 26Al by Mitchell et al. (1988) and the subsequent transition distribution analysis by Adams, Mitchell, and Shriner (1998), where a large deviation from Porter-Thomas was seen but lacked clear explanation. The model also offers an interpretation of shell model calculations by Hamoudi, Nazmitdinov, and Alhassid (1998), who found that B(M1) distributions in A=60 nuclei depend strongly on Tz, suggesting that the deviation for Tz=0 nuclei may be a "false" symmetry breaking effect from basis truncation rather than genuine isospin violation.
