# A novel probe of chiral restoration in nuclear medium

> We propose measuring the mass shift and width broadening of the $f_1(1285)$ meson together with those of the $ω$ from a nuclear target as a means to experimentally probe the partial restoration of chiral symmetry inside the nuclear matter. The relation between the order parameter of chiral symmetry and the difference in the correlation functions of the $f_1(1285)$ current and the $ω$ current is discussed in the limit where the disconnected diagrams are neglected. A QCD sum rule analysis of the $f_1(1285)$ meson mass leads to about 100 MeV attraction in nuclear matter, which can be probed in future experiments.

# A novel probe of chiral restoration in nuclear medium

## Key claim

Measuring the mass shift and width broadening of the $f_1(1285)$ meson together with those of the $\omega$ meson from a nuclear target can serve as an experimental probe of partial chiral symmetry restoration in nuclear matter, provided that disconnected diagrams are neglected and the $f_1(1285)$ and $\omega$ are treated as chiral partners under that approximation.

## Method

The authors perform a QCD sum rule analysis of the $f_1(1285)$ meson at finite nuclear density, using the trace part of the polarization function for the axial vector current composed of light quarks. They apply the Borel transformation to the operator product expansion (OPE) and extract the mass from a simple pole-plus-continuum ansatz for the spectral density, neglecting the width. The density dependence enters through the in-medium chiral condensate, gluon condensate, and higher-twist operators, with the $\pi N$ sigma term $\sigma_{\pi N}$ as the dominant source of uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- The QCD sum rule analysis predicts a mass shift of the $f_1(1285)$ in nuclear matter of about $96 \pm 38$ MeV (attraction), with the central value obtained using $\sigma_{\pi N} = 45$ MeV and the range from $\sigma_{\pi N} = 30$ MeV to $60$ MeV.
- The vacuum threshold parameter for the $J^q_{\mu}$ correlator (coupling to $f_1(1285)$) is $\sqrt{s_0} = 1.61$ GeV; for the $J^s_{\mu}$ correlator (coupling to $f_1(1420)$) it is $\sqrt{s_0} = 1.925$ GeV.
- The vacuum mass extracted from the $J^q_{\mu}$ correlator agrees almost perfectly with the experimental $f_1(1285)$ mass of 1285 MeV; the $J^s_{\mu}$ result lies about 70 MeV above the $f_1(1420)$ mass.
- The vacuum width of the $f_1(1285)$ is $24.2$ MeV, compared to the $\omega$ width of $8.49$ MeV (from Table I).
- The $\omega$ meson in nuclear matter shows a mass shift of $-29$ MeV and a width increase of $70$ MeV, as reported by the CBELSA/TAPS collaboration (Metag 2015).

## Context

This work extends the program of measuring narrow mesons in nuclear matter pioneered by the CBELSA/TAPS collaboration, which successfully measured the $\omega$ and $\eta'$ in nuclear targets (Metag 2015, Nanova 2016). The authors build on the theoretical framework relating the chiral condensate to differences in correlation functions of chiral partners (Cohen 1996, Lee 1996) and on earlier finite-temperature QCD sum rules for vector and axial-vector mesons (Hatsuda 1992). The recent identification of the $f_1(1285)$ in photoproduction from a proton target by the CLAS collaboration (Dickson 2016) provides the experimental prerequisite. If successful, combined measurements of $\eta'$, $\omega$, and $f_1(1285)$ in nuclear matter could disentangle chiral symmetry restoration effects from $U_A(1)$ anomaly effects.
