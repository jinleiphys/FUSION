# Successful prediction of total $α$-induced reaction cross sections at astrophysically relevant sub-Coulomb energies using a novel approach

> The prediction of stellar ($γ$,$α$) reaction rates for heavy nuclei is based on the calculation of ($α$,$γ$) cross sections at sub-Coulomb energies. These rates are essential for modeling the nucleosynthesis of so-called $p$-nuclei. The standard calculations in the statistical model show a dramatic sensitivity to the chosen $α$-nucleus potential. The present study explains the reason for this dramatic sensitivity which results from the tail of the imaginary $α$-nucleus potential in the underlying optical model calculation of the total reaction cross section. As an alternative to the optical model, a simple barrier transmission model is suggested. It is shown that this simple model in combination with a well-chosen $α$-nucleus potential is able to predict total $α$-induced reaction cross sections for a wide range of heavy target nuclei above $A \gtrsim 150$ with uncertainties below a factor of two. The new predictions from the simple model do not require any adjustment of parameters to experimental reaction cross sections whereas in previous statistical model calculations all predictions remained very uncertain because the parameters of the $α$-nucleus potential had to be adjusted to experimental data. The new model allows to predict the reaction rate of the astrophysically important $^{176}$W($α$,$γ$)$^{180}$Os reaction with reduced uncertainties, leading to a significantly lower reaction rate at low temperatures. The new approach could also be validated for a broad range of target nuclei from $A \approx 60$ up to $A \gtrsim 200$.

## Key claim
The paper identifies that the dramatic sensitivity of statistical model predictions for α-induced reaction cross sections at sub-Coulomb energies originates from the tail of the imaginary α-nucleus potential at large radii, not from the real potential. A simple barrier transmission model (pBTM) using only the real part of the ATOMKI-V1 potential can predict total α-induced reaction cross sections for heavy targets (A ≳ 150) with uncertainties below a factor of two, without any parameter adjustment to experimental reaction data.

## Method
The authors first demonstrate that truncating or reshaping the imaginary part of the standard McFadden-Satchler (MCF) Woods-Saxon potential at radii beyond 12 fm reduces the calculated total reaction cross section at low energies by an order of magnitude, while changes to the real potential have negligible effect. They then implement a pure barrier transmission model (pBTM) using the code CCFULL, which calculates transmission through the Coulomb barrier using only the real part of the ATOMKI-V1 double-folding potential, assuming complete absorption once the α-particle tunnels through the barrier. The pBTM predictions are compared to experimental total reaction cross sections (sum of (α,γ), (α,n), and (α,2n) channels) for ¹⁹⁷Au and a series of heavy targets above A = 150.

## Key numbers
- For ¹⁹⁷Au + α at 10 MeV (center of Gamow window at T₉ ≈ 2.5), predicted cross sections from different optical model potentials vary by about four orders of magnitude; the pBTM reduces this to agreement within a factor of two with experimental data.
- At 25 MeV (above the Coulomb barrier), different optical model potentials give total reaction cross sections for ¹⁹⁷Au + α within a factor of two of each other.
- The pBTM predictions for all heavy targets above A = 150 (including ¹⁵¹Eu, ¹⁶⁵Ho, ¹⁶⁶Er, ¹⁶²Er, ¹⁶⁹Tm, ¹⁶⁸Yb, ¹⁸⁷Re, and Ir isotopes) agree with experimental data within a factor of two (grey-shaded uncertainty band in Fig. 2).
- The dispersion relation correction to the pBTM has only minor influence of less than 30% on the total cross section for all energies studied.
- The new reaction rate for ¹⁷⁶W(α,γ)¹⁸⁰Os is significantly lower than previous predictions from literature (Cyburt 2010, Rauscher 2000, Sallaska 2013), which vary by orders of magnitude; the pBTM rate is claimed with uncertainty less than a factor of two.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of α-induced reactions for p-nuclei nucleosynthesis, which has been plagued by order-of-magnitude uncertainties in statistical model calculations using global α-nucleus optical model potentials (AOMPs) such as McFadden and Satchler (1966), Watanabe (1958), Avrigeanu et al. (2014), and Demetriou et al. (2002). The pBTM approach is similar to models used for heavy-ion fusion reactions far below the Coulomb barrier (Back et al. 2014, Hagino et al. 2012, Balantekin et al. 1998), where Woods-Saxon potentials are known to be inadequate. The new method enables reliable prediction of the ¹⁷⁶W(α,γ)¹⁸⁰Os reaction rate, which governs the production of the p-nucleus ¹⁸⁰W (Rauscher 2016), and is validated for targets from A ≈ 60 up to A ≳ 200, potentially improving the entire γ-process nucleosynthesis network.
