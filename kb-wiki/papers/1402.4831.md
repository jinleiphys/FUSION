# Exclusive vector meson production at high energies and gluon saturation

> We systematically study exclusive diffractive (photo) production of vector mesons ($J/ψ$, $ψ(2s)$, $ϕ$ and $ρ$) off protons in high-energy collisions and investigate whether the production is a sensitive probe of gluon saturation. We confront saturation-based results for diffractive $ψ(2s)$ and $ρ$ production at HERA and $J/ψ$ photoproduction with all available data including recent ones from HERA, ALICE and LHCb, finding good agreement. In particular, we show that the $t$-distribution of differential cross sections of photoproduction of vector mesons offers a unique opportunity to discriminate among saturation and non-saturation models. This is due to the emergence of a pronounced dip (or multiple dips) in the $t$-distribution of diffractive photoproduction of vector mesons at relatively large, but potentially accessible $|t|$ that can be traced back to the unitarity features of colour dipole amplitude in the saturation regime. We show that in saturation models the dips in $t$-distribution recede towards lower $|t|$ with decreasing mass of the vector meson, increasing energy or decreasing Bjorken-$x$, and decreasing virtuality $Q$. We provide various predictions for exclusive (photo) production of different vector mesons including the ratio of $ψ(2s)/J/ψ$ at HERA, the LHC, and future colliders.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the differential cross-section in momentum transfer \(t\) for exclusive diffractive photoproduction of vector mesons can discriminate between saturation and non-saturation models, because saturation models predict a pronounced dip (or multiple dips) at relatively large but potentially accessible \(|t|\) that arises from unitarity features of the colour dipole amplitude in the saturation regime; this claim is hedged by noting that the dip position depends on the vector meson mass, energy, Bjorken-\(x\), and virtuality \(Q\), and that the predictions are made for kinematics accessible in future experiments.

## Method

The authors compute exclusive diffractive vector meson production (\(\gamma^* + p \to V + p\) for \(V = J/\psi, \psi(2s), \phi, \rho\)) using the colour dipole formalism, where the scattering amplitude is a convolution of the dipole-proton forward scattering amplitude \(\mathcal{N}(x, r, b)\) and the overlap of the photon and vector meson wave functions. They employ two impact-parameter dependent saturation models (IP-Sat and b-CGC), whose parameters were previously fitted to combined HERA data for inclusive reduced cross-sections and to the \(t\)-dependence of exclusive \(J/\psi\) production at HERA. The vector meson wave functions are constructed using boosted Gaussian wave functions with parameters determined from normalisation, orthogonality, and fits to leptonic decay widths.

## Key numbers

- Total \(J/\psi\) photoproduction cross-section as a function of photon-proton centre-of-mass energy \(W_{\gamma p}\) is compared with data from HERA, ALICE, and LHCb up to \(W_{\gamma p} \approx 1.3\) TeV; the CGC band (IP-Sat and b-CGC with charm mass \(m_c = 1.2 \div 1.4\) GeV) agrees with LHCb data, while pQCD fits at LO and NLO show tensions at high \(W_{\gamma p}\).
- For \(J/\psi\) with \(m_c = 1.27\) GeV, the IP-Sat and b-CGC models give consistent results with LHCb data; the b-CGC model fitted to old \(F_2\) data underestimates recent LHCb data.
- The leptonic decay width \(\Gamma_{e^+e^-}\) for \(J/\psi\) is fitted to \(5.54\) KeV (experimental value \(5.55 \pm 0.14\) KeV) with \(m_c = 1.4\) GeV; for \(\rho\) it is fitted to \(7.02\) KeV (experimental \(7.04 \pm 0.06\) KeV) with light quark mass \(m_{u,d,s} = 0.14\) GeV.
- The \(\psi(2s)\) leptonic decay width is fitted to \(2.39\) KeV (experimental \(2.37 \pm 0.04\) KeV) with \(m_c = 1.4\) GeV.
- The parameters of the boosted Gaussian wave function for \(J/\psi\) with \(m_c = 1.4\) GeV are \(\mathcal{N}_L = \mathcal{N}_T = 0.57\), \(\mathcal{R}^2 = 2.45\) GeV\(^2\); for \(\psi(2s)\) with \(m_c = 1.4\) GeV, \(\mathcal{N}_L = \mathcal{N}_T = 0.67\), \(\mathcal{R}^2 = 3.72\) GeV\(^2\), \(\alpha_{2s} = -0.61\).

## Context

This work extends previous saturation model studies of exclusive vector meson production (e.g., IP-Sat and b-CGC fits by Rezaeian et al. (2013) and Watt and Kowalski (2008)) by incorporating new high-precision combined HERA data and recent LHC data from ALICE and LHCb, and by systematically studying \(\psi(2s)\) production and the ratio \(\psi(2s)/J/\psi\). It goes beyond existing works on \(J/\psi\) and \(\psi(2s)\) production in the dipole model (Ducati et al., 2013; Goncalves et al., 2014) and in pQCD (Jones et al., 2013; Guzey et al., 2014) by predicting dips in the \(t\)-distribution as a unique signature of saturation, which could be tested at future experiments such as the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and the LHeC.
