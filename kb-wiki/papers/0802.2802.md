# Coupling Constant for $\mathbf{N^*(1535)NœÅ}$

> The value of the $N^*(1535)NœÅ$ coupling constant $g_{N^*NœÅ}$ derived from the $N^*(1535) \to NœÅ\to N œÄœÄ$ decay is compared with that deduced from the radiative decay $N^*(1535) \to N Œ≥$ using the vector-meson-dominance model. On the basis of an effective Lagrangian approach, we show that the value of $g_{N^*NœÅ}$ extracted from the available experimental data on the two decays are consistent, though the error bars are rather large.

## Key claim
The values of the $N^*(1535)N\rho$ coupling constant $g_{N^*N\rho}$ extracted from the $N^*(1535) \to N\rho \to N\pi\pi$ decay and from the radiative decay $N^*(1535) \to N\gamma$ using vector-meson-dominance are consistent within large error bars, but the uncertainties are too large to discriminate between pure S-wave, pure vector, or pure tensor forms of the coupling.

## Method
The authors used an effective Lagrangian approach with Lorentz covariant orbital-spin coupling scheme to compute the $N^*(1535) \to N\rho^0 \to N\pi^+\pi^-$ decay width and the $N^*(1535) \to N\rho^0 \to N\gamma$ decay width via vector-meson-dominance. They adjusted the coupling constant $g_{N^*N\rho}$ and a monopole form factor cut-off parameter $\Lambda$ to reproduce the experimental partial width of $(3.0\pm1.6)$ MeV for the two-pion decay and the isovector helicity-1/2 decay amplitude $A^{I=1}_{1/2} = (0.068\pm0.020)\,(\text{GeV})^{-1/2}$ for the radiative decay. The analysis was repeated for pure vector and pure tensor coupling forms using a dipole form factor.

## Key numbers
- Partial decay width for $N^*(1535) \to N\rho \to N\pi\pi$: $(3.0\pm1.6)$ MeV, obtained from total width $150\pm 25$ MeV and branching ratio $0.02\pm 0.01$ (PDG 2006).
- Isovector helicity-1/2 decay amplitude: $A^{I=1}_{1/2} = (0.068\pm0.020)\,(\text{GeV})^{-1/2}$, derived from $A^{p\gamma}_{1/2}=0.090\pm 0.030\,(\text{GeV})^{-1/2}$ and $A^{n\gamma}_{1/2}=-0.046\pm 0.027\,(\text{GeV})^{-1/2}$ (PDG 2006).
- $\rho\pi\pi$ coupling constant: $g^2_{\rho\pi\pi}/4\pi = 2.91$, from the $\rho^0 \to \pi^+\pi^-$ decay width.
- $\rho\gamma$ coupling constant: $f^2_{\rho}/4\pi = 2.7$, from vector-meson-dominance (Sakurai compilation).
- The extracted $g^2_{N^*N\rho}/4\pi$ values from the two decays are compatible for cut-off parameter $\Lambda$ ranging from 0.5 to 2.0 GeV for pure S-wave and pure vector coupling, and for $\Lambda > 0.6$ GeV for pure tensor coupling.

## Context
This work addresses the controversy in $\eta$ production in nucleon-nucleon scattering near threshold, where some authors (Batinic (1995), Nakayama (2002), Shyam (2007)) find pseudoscalar exchanges dominate while others (Gedalin (1993), Santra (1995), Faldt (1998), Nakayama (2002), Vetter (1998)) claim $\rho$-meson exchange plays an important role. The paper constrains the $N^*(1535)N\rho$ coupling constant from two independent decay channels, showing that the coupling is strong enough that $\rho$-exchange should not be neglected in $\eta$ production models. Improved experimental data on both decays could eventually identify the correct form of the $N^*N\rho$ coupling.
