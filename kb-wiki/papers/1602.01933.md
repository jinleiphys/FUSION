# Rotational Properties of the Odd-$Z$ Transfermium Nucleus $^{255}$Lr by a Particle-number-conserving Method in the Cranked Shell Model

> Experimentally observed ground state band based on the $1/2^{-}[521]$ Nilsson state and the first exited band based on the $7/2^{-}[514]$ Nilsson state in the odd-$Z$ nucleus $^{255}$Lr are studied by the cranked shell model (CSM) with the paring correlations treated by the particle-number-conserving (PNC) method. This is the first time the detailed theoretical investigations being performed on these rotational bands. Both the experimental kinematic and dynamic moment of inertia ($\mathcal{J}^{(1)}$ and $\mathcal{J}^{(2)}$) versus rotational frequency are reproduced quite well by the PNC-CSM calculations. By comparing the theoretical kinematic moment of inertia $\mathcal{J}^{(1)}$ with the experimental ones extracted from different spin assignments, the spin $17/2^{-}\rightarrow13/2^{-}$ is assigned to the lowest-lying $196.6(5)$ keV transition of the $1/2^{-}[521]$ band, and $15/2^{-}\rightarrow11/2^{-}$ to the $189(1)$ keV transition of the $7/2^{-}[514]$ band, respectively. The proton $N=7$ major shell is included in the calculations. The intruder of the high$-j$ low$-Ω$ orbitals $1j_{15/2}$ $ (1/2^{-}[770])$ at the high spin leads to the band-crossing at $\hbarω\approx0.20$ ($\hbarω\approx0.25$) MeV for the $7/2^{-}[514]$ $α=-1/2$ ($α=+1/2$) band, and at $\hbarω\approx0.175$ MeV for the $1/2^{-}[521]$ $α=-1/2$ band, respectively. Further investigations show that the band-crossing frequencies are quadrupole deformation dependent.

# Rotational Properties of the Odd-Z Transfermium Nucleus 255Lr by a Particle-number-conserving Method in the Cranked Shell Model

## Key claim

This is the first detailed theoretical investigation of the ground-state band based on the 1/2−[521] Nilsson state and the first excited band based on the 7/2−[514] Nilsson state in 255Lr. The particle-number-conserving cranked shell model (PNC-CSM) reproduces both experimental kinematic and dynamic moments of inertia, but the agreement depends on including the proton N=7 major shell and the high-j intruder orbital 1j15/2 (1/2−[770]), whose effect is deformation-dependent and becomes significant only at rotational frequencies beyond the currently observed experimental range.

## Method

The cranked shell model Hamiltonian with monopole and quadrupole pairing correlations was diagonalized directly in a truncated Fock space using the particle-number-conserving (PNC) method, which exactly conserves particle number and treats Pauli blocking effects. The cranked Nilsson orbitals were obtained by diagonalizing h0(ω)=hNil−ωjx, then the full Hamiltonian was diagonalized in a cranked many-particle configuration (CMPC) space of about 1000 dimensions for both protons and neutrons. The proton N=4,5,6,7 shells and neutron N=6,7 shells were included, with deformation parameters ε2=0.27, ε4=0.02, and ε6=0.02, and the new set of Nilsson parameters (κ,μ) optimized for transfermium nuclei from Zhang et al. (2011, 2012).

## Key numbers

- The lowest-lying transition of the 1/2−[521] band at 196.6(5) keV is assigned as 17/2−→13/2− based on comparison of theoretical and experimental kinematic moments of inertia.
- The lowest-lying transition of the 7/2−[514] band at 189(1) keV is assigned as 15/2−→11/2−.
- Band-crossing occurs at ħω≈0.20 MeV for the 7/2−[514] α=−1/2 band and at ħω≈0.25 MeV for the 7/2−[514] α=+1/2 band.
- Band-crossing occurs at ħω≈0.175 MeV for the 1/2−[521] α=−1/2 band.
- The band-crossing frequency for the 1/2−[521] α=−1/2 band decreases from ħω≈0.225 MeV at ε2=0.26 to ħω≈0.150 MeV at ε2=0.28-0.29.
- For the 7/2−[514] α=−1/2 band, the band-crossing frequency remains at ħω≈0.20 MeV for ε2=0.26-0.28, dropping to ħω≈0.175 MeV at ε2=0.29.
- Effective pairing strengths used: G0p=0.25 MeV, G2p=0.01 MeV, G0n=0.25 MeV, G2n=0.02 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the PNC-CSM approach previously applied to 251Md and 253No by He et al. (2009), which was the first PNC-CSM study of transfermium nuclei including the proton N=7 major shell. The present study addresses the open spin assignments for the two rotational bands in 255Lr observed experimentally by Ketelhut et al. (2009), where no spin assignments had been made. The calculations demonstrate that the high-j intruder orbital 1j15/2 (1/2−[770]) from the N=7 shell, which is often neglected in theoretical studies due to its distance from the Fermi surface at zero frequency, becomes important at high rotational frequencies and produces deformation-dependent band-crossings. This enables future experimental tests of whether this orbital plays a significant role in transfermium nuclei, as the predicted band-crossing frequencies lie beyond the currently observed frequency range.
