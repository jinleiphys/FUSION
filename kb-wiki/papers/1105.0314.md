# In-Medium Properties of Vector Mesons in a Transport Approach

> We present dilepton spectra from p+p and p+Nb collisions at a kinetic beam energy of 3.5 GeV, which were simulated with the GiBUU transport model assuming different in-medium scenarios. We compare these spectra to preliminary HADES data and show that GiBUU can describe the data reasonably well. Our simulations indicate that the intermediate dilepton-mass region is sensitive to the N-Delta electromagnetic transition form factor, which up to now is unmeasured in the time-like region.

## Key claim

GiBUU transport simulations can describe HADES dilepton spectra from p+p and p+Nb collisions at 3.5 GeV kinetic beam energy reasonably well, but the intermediate dilepton-mass region (500-600 MeV) is sensitive to the N-Delta electromagnetic transition form factor in the time-like region, which remains unmeasured and prevents firm conclusions on in-medium vector meson properties.

## Method

The authors used the GiBUU hadronic transport model, which solves the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck equation via test particles, to simulate dilepton production in p+p and p+Nb collisions at 3.5 GeV kinetic beam energy. Elementary collisions were modeled using a resonance model at low energies (below sqrt(s)=2.6 GeV for baryon-baryon) and the Pythia string model at higher energies, with tuned parameters for HADES energies. The simulated dilepton events were passed through the HADES acceptance filter (HAFT version 2.0) for direct comparison to experimental data, testing several in-medium scenarios including collisional broadening (Gamma_0 = 150 MeV at normal nuclear density rho_0 = 0.168 fm^-3) and a 16% pole mass shift.

## Key numbers

- Beam kinetic energy: 3.5 GeV for both p+p and p+Nb collisions
- Center-of-mass energy for p+p: sqrt(s) = 3.18 GeV
- Normal nuclear matter density: rho_0 = 0.168 fm^-3
- Collisional broadening parameter for rho and omega mesons: Gamma_0 = 150 MeV
- Mass shift scaling parameter: alpha = 16% for the relation m*(rho) = m_0(1 - alpha * rho/rho_0)
- Transition energy between resonance model and Pythia for baryon-baryon collisions: sqrt(s) = 2.6 GeV (with +/-0.2 GeV smoothing window)
- Transition energy for meson-baryon collisions: sqrt(s) = 2.2 GeV (with +/-0.2 GeV smoothing window)
- N-Delta transition form factor at real-photon point: |F_Delta(0)| = 3.029
- Upper limit for BR(eta -> e+e-) < 2.7e-5
- Systematic error of p+Nb data: approximately 28%

## Context

This work extends the application of the GiBUU transport model (Cassing 1999) to dilepton production at HADES energies, following earlier studies of in-medium vector meson properties via QCD sum rules and experiments such as CLAS at JLAB (Wood 2008), E325 at KEK (Naruki 2005), and CB/TAPS at ELSA (Nanova 2010). The results indicate that the HADES data at 3.5 GeV cannot resolve in-medium effects without first fixing the N-Delta transition form factor in the time-like region, which is poorly constrained compared to the space-like region where electron scattering data exist. The tuned Pythia parameters (suppressing vector meson production via PARJ(11)=0.15 and PARJ(12)=0.2) provide a baseline for future studies of heavier nuclear systems and higher beam energies.
