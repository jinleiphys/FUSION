# Chiral symmetry aspects of positive and negative parity baryons

> Chiral symmetry aspects for baryon properties are studied. After a brief discussion on general framework, we introduce two distinctive chiral group representations for baryons: the naive and mirror assignments. Using linear sigma models, nucleon properties are studied in both representations. Finally, we propose an experiment to distinguish the two assignments in the reactions of pion and eta productions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that positive and negative parity baryons can be assigned to chiral symmetry representations in two distinct ways (naive and mirror assignments), and that these assignments can be distinguished experimentally by the relative sign of their axial couplings, which affects interference patterns in pion and eta production reactions.

## Method

The authors construct linear sigma models for nucleons in both the naive assignment (where both parity states transform identically under chiral rotations) and the mirror assignment (where the right- and left-handed components are interchanged for the negative parity state). They derive mass matrices, mixing angles, and axial couplings for both models, then compute cross sections for the reactions π⁻ + p → π⁻ + η + p and γ + p → π⁰ + η + p using resonance-pole dominance with phenomenological Lagrangians.

## Key numbers

- Masses used for the two nucleon states: m₊ = 939 MeV (N(939)) and m₋ = 1535 MeV (N(1535))
- Pion decay constant: σ₀ = fπ = 93 MeV
- Mirror model parameters from fit: m₀ = 270 MeV, g₁ = 9.8, g₂ = 16, mixing angle θ = 6.3°
- Diagonal axial charges in mirror model: gA⁺⁺ = -gA⁻⁻ = 0.98
- Coupling constants used: gπNN ≈ 13, gπNN* ≈ 0.7, gηNN* ≈ 2
- Partial decay width of N(1535) → πN: Γ ≈ 75 MeV
- Transition magnetic moments from mirror model: μpp* = 8.42, μnn* = -8.99 (in nuclear magnetons)
- Empirical helicity amplitudes: A₁/₂ᵖ ≈ 95 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/², A₁/₂ⁿ ≈ -80 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/²
- Anomalous magnetic moments used: κS = -0.12, κV = 3.7, κV* = 0.9

## Context

This work extends the chiral representation approach of Weinberg (1969, 1990) to baryons with opposite parity, building on earlier formulations by DeTar and Kunihiro for the mirror model. The authors distinguish their naive and mirror assignments from prior work by Jido (2000, 2001) and show that the mirror assignment allows a chiral-scalar mass term m₀ that survives chiral restoration. The proposed experiments in pion and eta production could distinguish the two assignments and enable studies of how baryon properties change when chiral symmetry is restored at finite density or temperature, as previously discussed by Kim and others for the width of N(1535) in nuclear medium.
