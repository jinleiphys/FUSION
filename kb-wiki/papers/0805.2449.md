# Asymmetric nuclear matter : a variational approach

> We discuss here a self-consistent method to calculate the properties of the cold asymmetric nuclear matter. In this model, the nuclear matter is dressed with s-wave pion pairs and the nucleon-nucleon (N-N) interaction is mediated by these pion pairs, $ω$ and $ρ$ mesons. The parameters of these interactions are calculated self-consistently to obtain the saturation properties like equilibrium binding energy, pressure, compressibility and symmetry energy. The computed equation of state is then used in the Tolman- Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation to study the mass and radius of a neutron star in the pure neutron matter limit.

## Key claim
The paper presents a non-relativistic variational model for cold asymmetric nuclear matter where the attractive scalar interaction usually mediated by σ mesons is instead simulated by s-wave pion pairs, while ω and ρ mesons provide repulsion and isospin dependence; the model's parameters are fixed self-consistently to reproduce saturation properties, but the compressibility modulus must be chosen at 260 MeV because the calculation does not converge for values below approximately 258 MeV.

## Method
The authors construct a non-relativistic Hamiltonian density with πN, ω, and ρ interactions, dress the nuclear matter with s-wave pion pairs via a Bogoliubov transformation, and introduce a phenomenological repulsion between pions in a pair (parameters a and Rπ). The four model parameters (a, Rπ, λω, λρ) are determined self-consistently by solving for binding energy per nucleon (−16 MeV), symmetry energy (31 MeV), and vanishing pressure at saturation density ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³, with the compressibility modulus fixed at K = 260 MeV. The resulting equation of state (EOS) for pure neutron matter is then used in the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation to compute neutron star mass and radius.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³.
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: −16 MeV.
- Symmetry energy at saturation: 31 MeV.
- Compressibility modulus: K = 260 MeV.
- Effective mass ratio at saturation: M*/M = 0.81.
- Model parameters: a = 16.98 MeV, Rπ = 1.42 fm, λω = 3.10 fm², λρ = 0.65 fm².
- Symmetry energy density dependence exponent: γ = 0.85 (for E_sym = 31 MeV at ρ₀).
- Neutron star mass (pure neutron matter): 2.25 M⊙.
- Neutron star radius (pure neutron matter): 11.7 km.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Mishra (1990, 1992), who showed that s-wave pion pairs can simulate the σ-meson attraction in relativistic mean-field (RMF) models, and applies it to asymmetric nuclear matter. The results are compared with DBHF calculations using the Bonn A potential (Li, 1992), the variational A18+δv+UIX* model of Akmal (1998), and the Walecka model (1974); the EOS is softer than the relativistic models but stiffer than the variational results. The computed symmetry energy supports a moderately stiff density dependence, consistent with the analysis of Shetty (2007), and the neutron star mass of 2.25 M⊙ provides a prediction for pure neutron matter that can be tested against future astrophysical observations.
