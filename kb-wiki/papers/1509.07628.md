# Nucleosynthesis in the Ejecta of Neutron Star Mergers

> Heavy elements like gold, platinum or uranium are produced in the r-process, which needs neutron-rich and explosive environments. Neutron star mergers are a promising candidate for an r-process site. They exhibit three different channels for matter ejection fulfilling these conditions: dynamic ejecta due to tidal torques, neutrino-driven winds and evaporating matter from the accretion disk. We present a first study of the integrated nucleosynthesis for a neutrino-driven wind from a neutron star merger with a hyper-massive neutron star. Trajectories from a recent hydrodynamical simulation are divided into four different angle regions and post-processed with a reaction network. We find that the electron fraction varies around $Y_e \approx 0.1 - 0.4$, but its distribution differs for every angle of ejection. Hence, the wind ejecta do not undergo a robust r-process, but rather possess distinct nucleosynthesis yields depending on the angle range. Compared to the dynamic ejecta, a smaller amount of neutron-rich matter gets unbound, but the production of lighter heavy elements with $A \lesssim 130$ in the neutrino-driven wind can complement the strong r-process of the dynamic ejecta.

## Key claim

The neutrino-driven wind ejecta from a neutron star merger with a hyper-massive neutron star do not undergo a robust r-process across all ejection angles; instead, their nucleosynthesis yields vary strongly with the polar angle of ejection, producing lighter heavy elements up to A ~ 130 in all cases but only reaching the third abundance peak (A ~ 195) for the highest angles where electron fractions drop to Y_e ~ 0.1.

## Method

Trajectories from a 3D Eulerian hydrodynamical simulation of a neutron star merger aftermath (Perego 2014) were divided into four polar angle regions. For each region, representative tracers were selected by binning in the electron fraction-entropy plane, yielding 124 trajectories total. These trajectories were post-processed with a reaction network containing over 5800 isotopes up to Z = 111, including neutrino absorption effects, to compute integrated final abundances.

## Key numbers

- Electron fraction in the neutrino-driven wind varies in the range Y_e ≈ 0.1 – 0.4, with distribution differing for each angle region
- Expansion velocity of the wind ejecta is v = 0.06c – 0.08c
- Neutrino luminosities from the remnant are about 10^53 erg/s with energies of ~10 – 15 MeV
- Dynamic ejecta have electron fractions Y_e ~ 0.04 and densities ρ = 10^12 – 10^13 g/cm^3 at T = 10 GK
- Neutron-rich matter unbound in the neutrino-driven wind exceeds 2 × 10^−3 M_⊙, compared to 10^−2 M_⊙ for dynamic ejecta
- Lighter heavy elements with A ≲ 130 are produced in all angle regions; the third abundance peak (A ~ 195) is only produced for the highest angles

## Context

This work extends the study of neutron star merger nucleosynthesis beyond the dynamic ejecta (Korobkin 2012; Bauswein 2013) by providing the first integrated nucleosynthesis calculation for the neutrino-driven wind component. The wind ejecta produce lighter heavy elements (A ≲ 130) that complement the strong r-process from dynamic ejecta, which primarily produce isotopes with A > 130 and negligible contributions below A ~ 110. The results enable more complete models of the total r-process yield from neutron star mergers and motivate further work with full general-relativistic simulations and improved neutrino transport.
