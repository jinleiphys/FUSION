# Splitting of elliptic flow in non-central relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We predict a new effect due to the presence of the global vorticity in non-central relativistic heavy-ion collisions, namely a splitting of the elliptic flow parameter $v_2$ at non-zero rapidity. The size of the splitting is proposed as a new observable that can be used to constrain the initial vortical configuration of the produced QCD matter in experiments. The new findings are demonstrated by numerical calculations employing the parton cascade model, Boltzmann Approach of MultiParton Scatterings (BAMPS), for non-central Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200 \ GeV$.

# Splitting of elliptic flow in non-central relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The paper predicts that in non-central relativistic heavy-ion collisions, the elliptic flow parameter \(v_2\) at non-zero rapidity will split into different values for particles with positive versus negative \(p_x\), provided a global vorticity is present. This splitting is proposed as a new observable to constrain the initial vortical configuration of QCD matter, but the authors note that the effect depends on the magnitude of the global orbital angular momentum and that initial fluctuations could also produce a splitting, though with random sign on an event-by-event basis.

## Method
The authors employed the parton cascade model BAMPS (Boltzmann Approach of MultiParton Scatterings) to simulate Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200 \ \text{GeV}\) with impact parameter \(b = 7 \ \text{fm}\). They modified the default initialization (which gives zero orbital angular momentum) by randomly selecting 1/5 of the particles and adjusting their longitudinal momenta to produce a global orbital angular momentum \(J_y = 1.4 \times 10^4 \hbar\), then calculated the time evolution and final \(p_T\) dependence of \(v_2\) for particles in two rapidity windows, separating particles by the sign of \(p_x\).

## Key numbers
- Global orbital angular momentum from the modified initialization: \(J_y = 1.4 \times 10^4 \hbar\) for Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200 \ \text{GeV}\) with \(b = 7 \ \text{fm}\).
- Impact parameter used in all simulations: \(b = 7 \ \text{fm}\).
- Nucleon-nucleon cross section used: \(\sigma_{p+p} \approx 42 \ \text{mb}\) for Au+Au at top RHIC energy.
- Splitting magnitude at final time for the 1/5 soft-particle initialization: approximately \(0.024\) in the rapidity window \([0.5:1.5]\).
- Splitting magnitude for a reduced initialization (1/10 soft particles, \(J_y = 0.63 \times 10^4 \hbar\)): approximately \(0.014\) at final time.
- Test particle number increased from 250 to 25000 for higher statistics; cell length in transverse plane reduced from \(0.4 \ \text{fm}\) to \(0.05 \ \text{fm}\).

## Context
This work extends studies of global vorticity in non-central heavy-ion collisions, following experimental observations of global spin polarization at RHIC and LHC (STAR Collaboration, 2017; Adam et al., 2018) and theoretical developments in spin kinetic theory and spin hydrodynamics. The authors note that prior BAMPS calculations (Uphoff et al., 2014) neglected soft particle production from wounded nucleons, which is essential for global vorticity. The proposed splitting observable could help resolve the "spin sign problem" between experimental and theoretical results on local polarization (Becattini et al., 2020; Liu et al., 2020; Gao et al., 2020), and the authors suggest experimental confirmation could constrain the initial vortical configuration of QCD matter.
