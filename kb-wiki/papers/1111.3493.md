# Proton radioactivity half lives with Skyrme interactions

> The potential barrier impeding the spontaneous emission of protons in the proton radioactive nuclei is calculated as the sum of nuclear, Coulomb and centrifugal contributions. The nuclear part of the proton-nucleus interaction potential is obtained in the energy density formalism using Skyrme effective interaction that results into a simple algebraic expression. The half-lives of the proton emitters are calculated for the different Skyrme sets within the improved WKB framework. The results are found to be in reasonable agreement with the earlier results obtained for more complicated calculations involving finite range interactions.

## Key claim

The half-lives of proton emitters calculated using Skyrme effective interactions in the energy density formalism are in reasonable agreement with earlier results from more complicated finite-range interaction calculations, but the authors note that the results show marginal sensitivity to the different Skyrme parametrizations despite widely varying nuclear matter properties such as incompressibility and effective mass.

## Method

The nuclear part of the proton-nucleus interaction potential was obtained analytically in the energy density formalism using Skyrme effective interactions, yielding a simple algebraic expression. The total potential barrier was constructed as the sum of this nuclear potential, the Coulomb potential (including direct and exchange parts), and the centrifugal potential. Half-lives were then calculated using the improved WKB framework for 31 proton-emitting nuclei across multiple Skyrme parameter sets, using Wood-Saxon density distributions and semiclassical kinetic energy densities.

## Key numbers

- For $^{185}$Bi (l=0, Q=1.624 MeV), the logarithmic half-life log10(T/s) ranges from -5.28 (SVI set) to -5.40 (SkM* set), compared to the measured value of -4.229
- For $^{155}$Ta (l=5, Q=1.791 MeV), the SLy230a set gives log10(T/s) = -4.75(7) and DDM3Y gives -4.80(7), compared to the measured value of -4.921
- The SVI Skyrme set has incompressibility 364 MeV and effective mass 0.95; SkM* has incompressibility 217 MeV and effective mass 0.79
- For $^{185}$Bi with SVI set, the second turning point Ra is at 7.63 fm and the third turning point Rb is at 65.71 fm, giving a barrier width of 58.08 fm; for SkM* the corresponding values are Ra=7.77 fm, Rb=65.71 fm, barrier width 57.94 fm
- For $^{185}$Bi using SkM*, the logarithmic half-life from Wood-Saxon densities is -5.40 and from Hartree-Fock densities is -5.52

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical WKB approach to proton radioactivity half-life calculations by applying it to Skyrme effective interactions, whereas previous folding model calculations used finite-range interactions such as JLM (used by Mohanty et al. 2007), DDM3Y (used by Basu et al. 2008), and YENI (used by Yeni et al. 2011). The results are compared with the earlier DDM3Y folding model calculations of Basu et al. (2008). The authors note that their finding of marginal sensitivity to Skyrme parametrizations contradicts the results of Khan et al. (2007) for $^{161}$Re obtained from quantum mechanical tunneling calculations of single particle resonance, where the half-lives depended sensitively on the potential and wave functions.
