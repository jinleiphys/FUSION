# Rare two-body decays of the top quark into a bottom meson plus an up or charm quark

> Rare two-body decays of the top quark into a neutral bottom-quark meson plus an up- or charm-quark: $t\to {\overline B}^0+ u, c$; $t\to {\overline B}^0_{s}+ c,u$; and $t \to Υ(nS)+ c,u$, are studied for the first time. The corresponding partials widths are computed at leading order in the non-relativistic QCD framework. The sums of all two-body branching ratios amount to $\mathcal{B}(t \to {\overline B}^0+ {\rm jet}) \approx \mathcal{B}(t \to {\overline B}^0_{s}+ {\rm jet}) \approx 4.2\cdot 10^{-5}$ and $\mathcal{B}(t \to Υ(nS)+ {\rm jet}) \approx 2\cdot 10^{-9}$, respectively. The feasibility to observe the $t\to {\overline B}^0_{(s)}+{\rm jet}$ decay is estimated in top-pair events produced in proton-proton collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 14, 100$ TeV at the LHC and FCC, respectively. Combining many exclusive hadronic ${\overline B}^0_{(s)}$ decays, with $J/ψ$ or $D^{0,\pm}$ final states, about 50 (16000) events are expected in 3 (20) ab$^{-1}$ of integrated luminosity at the LHC (FCC), after typical selection criteria, acceptance, and efficiency losses. An observation of the two-body top-quark decay can also be achieved in the interesting $t\to b(\rm{jet})+c(\rm{jet})$ dijet final state, where the ${\overline B}^0_{(s)}$ decay products are reconstructed as a jet, with 5300 and 1.4 million signal events above backgrounds expected after selection criteria at the LHC and FCC, respectively. Such unique final states provide a new direct method to precisely measure the top-quark mass via simple 2-body invariant mass analyses.

## Key claim

The paper claims that rare two-body decays of the top quark into a neutral bottom meson plus a jet have branching ratios of order 10⁻⁵, making them potentially observable at the HL-LHC and FCC, but the theoretical predictions carry approximately 100% uncertainty dominated by poorly known non-perturbative transition probabilities for the light antiquark to bind with the bottom quark.

## Method

The authors compute partial widths at leading order in the non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) framework, treating the formation of \(\overline{B}^0\) and \(\overline{B}^0_s\) mesons via a quark recombination mechanism where a down or strange antiquark from the off-shell \(W\) boson combines with the bottom quark from the top decay. For \(\Upsilon(nS)\) production, standard NRQCD long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) from four different sets in the literature are used. The expected event yields are estimated by combining NNLO+NNLL top-pair cross sections with the computed branching ratios and applying typical detector acceptance, selection, and efficiency losses for pp collisions at 14 TeV (LHC) and 100 TeV (FCC).

## Key numbers

- Summed branching ratio \(\mathcal{B}(t \to \overline{B}^0 + \text{jet}) \approx \mathcal{B}(t \to \overline{B}^0_s + \text{jet}) \approx 4.2 \times 10^{-5}\) (each), with total \(\mathcal{B}(t \to \overline{B}^0_{(s)} + \text{jet}) = 8.4 \times 10^{-5}\)
- Branching ratio \(\mathcal{B}(t \to \Upsilon(nS) + \text{jet}) \approx 2 \times 10^{-9}\), about four orders of magnitude smaller than the \(\overline{B}^0_{(s)}\) modes
- Individual branching fractions: \(\mathcal{B}(t \to \overline{B}^0 + u) = 4.0 \times 10^{-5}\), \(\mathcal{B}(t \to \overline{B}^0_s + c) = 4.0 \times 10^{-5}\), \(\mathcal{B}(t \to \overline{B}^0 + c) = 2.1 \times 10^{-6}\), \(\mathcal{B}(t \to \overline{B}^0_s + u) = 2.1 \times 10^{-6}\)
- About 50 signal events expected at the LHC with 3 ab⁻¹ integrated luminosity, and 16,000 events at the FCC with 20 ab⁻¹, combining exclusive hadronic \(\overline{B}^0_{(s)}\) decays with \(J/\psi\) or \(D^{0,\pm}\) final states
- For the dijet final state \(t \to b(\text{jet}) + c(\text{jet})\), 5,300 signal events above backgrounds expected at the LHC and 1.4 million at the FCC after selection criteria
- Top-pair cross sections: \(\sigma_{t\bar{t}} = 980 \pm 17\,(\text{PDF})^{+24}_{-35} (\text{scale})\) pb at 14 TeV LHC; \(\sigma_{t\bar{t}} = 34.80 \pm 1.20\,(\text{PDF})^{+1.00}_{-1.65} (\text{scale})\) nb at 100 TeV FCC

## Context

This work presents the first study of rare two-body top-quark decays into bottom mesons plus a jet, extending the known phenomenology of top-quark decays beyond the dominant \(t \to Wb\) channel. The theoretical framework builds on the NRQCD recombination approach previously applied to \(B_c^\pm\) production by Braaten (2001) and to \(D^*\)-meson fragmentation by Berezhnoy (1999). The predicted branching ratios of order \(10^{-5}\) are comparable to or larger than those for rare flavour-changing neutral current decays such as \(t \to q\gamma\) or \(t \to qZ\) studied by Mandrik (2018) for the HL-LHC and by Mangano (2016) for the FCC. The results enable a new direct method to measure the top-quark mass via simple two-body invariant mass analyses of \(\overline{B}^0_{(s)}\)-plus-jet pairs, providing an independent cross-check of the top mass determined from other channels.
