# Neutron star cooling and GW170817 constraint within quark-meson coupling models

> In the present work we used five different versions of the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model to compute astrophysical quantities related to the GW170817 event and to neutron star cooling process. Two of the models are based on the original bag potential structure and three versions consider a harmonic oscillator potential to confine the quarks. The bag-like models also incorporate the pasta phase used to describe the inner crust of neutron stars. We show that the pasta phase always play a minor or negligible role in all studies. Moreover, while no clear correlation between the models that satisfy the GW170817 constraints and the slope of the symmetry energy is found, a clear correlation is observed between the slope and the fact that the cooling is fast or slow, i.e., fast (slow) cooling is related to higher (lower) values of the slope. We did not find one unique model that can describe, at the same time, GW170817 constraints and give a perfect description of the possible cooling processes.

## Key claim

The authors find that no single quark-meson coupling (QMC) model simultaneously satisfies the GW170817 tidal deformability constraints and perfectly reproduces observed neutron star cooling data, but a clear correlation exists between the slope of the symmetry energy and cooling speed: fast (slow) cooling corresponds to higher (lower) values of the slope.

## Method

The authors constructed five QMC equations of state: two bag-based models (QMC and QMCωρ, with the latter including an ω-ρ meson interaction) and three harmonic-oscillator confining potential models (MQMC1, MQMC2, MQMC3). They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute neutron star masses, radii, and tidal deformabilities, and solved the general relativistic thermal evolution equations to simulate cooling curves. The bag-based models additionally incorporated pasta-phase inner crust structures via the coexisting phases method.

## Key numbers

- QMC model: maximum mass 2.14 M⊙, radius at maximum mass 11.51 km, radius at 1.4 M⊙ is 13.55 km
- QMCωρ model: maximum mass 2.07 M⊙, radius at maximum mass 10.96 km, radius at 1.4 M⊙ is 12.83 km
- MQMC1 model: maximum mass 1.97 M⊙, radius at maximum mass 11.43 km, radius at 1.4 M⊙ is 13.55 km
- MQMC2 model: maximum mass 1.97 M⊙, radius at maximum mass 11.34 km, radius at 1.4 M⊙ is 13.32 km
- MQMC3 model: maximum mass 1.97 M⊙, radius at maximum mass 11.18 km, radius at 1.4 M⊙ is 12.94 km
- Symmetry energy slope L0 values: QMC 90.00 MeV, QMCωρ 69.17 MeV, MQMC1 93.20 MeV, MQMC2 82.46 MeV, MQMC3 64.70 MeV
- Only QMCωρ and MQMC3 produce tidal deformabilities within the 90% confidence region of Λ1.4 = 190+390−120 from LIGO/Virgo (PRL121_161101)

## Context

This work extends the application of QMC models to the multimessenger era by confronting them with both GW170817 tidal deformability constraints and neutron star cooling observations. The results contrast with earlier relativistic mean-field studies (Lourenco et al., 2018) that found consistent tidal deformabilities, and with the crust-effect analysis of Jorge et al. (2018) which showed inner crust sensitivity in Love numbers. The findings enable future work to refine QMC parametrizations that simultaneously satisfy gravitational-wave and thermal evolution constraints, particularly by tuning the symmetry energy slope to intermediate values.
