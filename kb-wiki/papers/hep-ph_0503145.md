# Relativistic Calculation of the Width of the Theta (1540) Pentaquark

> We calculate the width of the Theta(1540) pentaquark in a relativistic model in which the pentaquark is considered to be composed of a scalar diquark and a spin 1/2 triquark. We consider both positive and negative parity for the pentaquark. There is a single parameter in our model which we vary and which describes the size of the pentaquark. If the pentaquark size is somewhat smaller than that of the nucleon, we find quite small widths for the pentaquark of about 1 MeV or less. Our model of confinement plays an important role in our analysis and makes it possible to use Feynman diagrams to describe the decay of the pentaquark.

## Key claim
The width of the Theta(1540) pentaquark can be as small as about 1 MeV or less, but only if the pentaquark size parameter (w_Theta) is somewhat larger than that of the nucleon; the abstract's claim of "quite small widths" depends on this specific condition.

## Method
The authors model the pentaquark as a bound state of a scalar diquark (mass 400 MeV) and a spin-1/2 triquark (mass 800 MeV), using a covariant confinement model that prevents the pentaquark from decaying into its constituents. They calculate the decay width via Feynman diagrams for both negative and positive parity assignments, representing the nucleon and triquark vertices with Gaussian wave functions fitted to earlier work (Shakin et al. 1996). The width is computed as a function of a single free parameter w_Theta, which governs the momentum-space extent of the pentaquark wave function.

## Key numbers
- The phase space factor rho = 0.193 GeV, derived from |P_N| = 0.199 GeV for the decay Theta(1540) -> N + K.
- For negative parity, the width reaches a maximum of about 15 MeV in the range w_Theta = 0.6 to 1.0 GeV.
- For negative parity, widths significantly less than 1 MeV are obtained for w_Theta in the range 0.7 to 0.9 GeV.
- For positive parity, widths less than 1 MeV are found for w_Theta greater than 0.72 GeV.
- The nucleon wave function parameter w_N = 0.66 GeV from prior work (Shakin et al. 1996).

## Context
This work extends the diquark-triquark model of Karliner and Lipkin (2003) to a relativistic calculation with explicit confinement, addressing the puzzle of the extremely narrow width of the Theta(1540) pentaquark. The results contradict expectations from simple constituent models that would predict larger widths, and the authors note that lattice QCD studies (Lasscock et al. 2005, Csikor et al. 2005) have not observed a 1540 MeV pentaquark signal, though they cannot rule out its existence at physical quark masses. The calculation enables future work to test whether the pentaquark's small width can be reconciled with experimental searches and lattice constraints.
