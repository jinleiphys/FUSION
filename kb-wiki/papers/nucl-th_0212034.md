# Low-momentum Nucleon-Nucleon Potential and Hartree-Fock Calculations

> A low-momentum nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential V-low-k is derived from modern realistic NN potentials by integrating out the high-momentum modes. The smooth V-low-k may be used as input for nuclear structure calculations instead of the usual Brueckner G matrix. Such an approach eliminates the energy dependence one finds in the G-matrix approach, allowing this interaction to be used directly in Hartree-Fock calculations. Bulk properties of 16O have been calculated starting from different NN potentials. Our results, obtained including up to second order contributions in the Goldstone expansion, are in good agreement with experiment.

# Low-momentum Nucleon-Nucleon Potential and Hartree-Fock Calculations

## Key claim
A low-momentum nucleon-nucleon potential (V_low-k) derived from realistic NN potentials by integrating out high-momentum modes can replace the energy-dependent Brueckner G matrix in nuclear structure calculations, and when used in Hartree-Fock calculations for 16O with up to second-order Goldstone expansion corrections, the results agree well with experiment. The hedge is that the agreement is achieved only after including second-order contributions, not at the mean-field level alone.

## Method
The authors derive V_low-k from realistic NN potentials (Bonn, CD-Bonn, Paris, Nijmegen, and Idaho) by integrating out momentum components above a cutoff Lambda = 2.1 fm^-1 using a renormalization group approach, implemented via the Andreozzi-Lee-Suzuki iterative method. They then perform self-consistent Hartree-Fock calculations for 16O using a harmonic-oscillator basis, with the Coulomb potential included exactly, and evaluate binding energy and root-mean-square mass radius at both first and second order in the Goldstone linked-cluster perturbation expansion.

## Key numbers
- Cutoff momentum used for V_low-k: Lambda = 2.1 fm^-1
- The minimum cutoff for nuclear structure calculations is argued to be around 2 fm^-1, consistent with NN potentials fitting phase shifts up to E_lab approximately 350 MeV
- Binding energy per nucleon and inverse r.m.s. mass radius for 16O are calculated and compared to experimental data from Refs. 15 and 16 (specific numerical values are not given in the truncated text)
- Second-order corrections improve agreement with experiment compared to first-order Hartree-Fock results

## Context
This work extends the renormalization group and effective field theory approach of Lepage (1997), Kaplan et al. (1998), and Epelbaum et al. (1998, 1999) to finite nuclei, providing an alternative to the traditional Brueckner G-matrix method that avoids energy dependence. The V_low-k approach had previously been applied to deuteron and scattering observables by Bogner et al. (2001, 2002) and to shell-model calculations by the same authors and Coraggio et al. (2002). The results confirm the need to go beyond mean-field descriptions of ground-state properties in finite nuclei, as noted by Suzuki (1994), Heisenberg (1999), Muther (2000), and Fabrocini (2000). This enables direct use of realistic NN potentials in Hartree-Fock and other many-body calculations without the complications of energy-dependent interactions.
