# The charmed strange meson system

> Motivated by the recent surprising discovery of two new meson states with c s-bar quark content but unexpectedly low masses and narrow total decay widths, we work out, in a nonrelativistic potential-model approach developed already some two decades ago, the predictions for the energy levels of the corresponding charm-antistrange quark bound states. In spite of the fact that this simple quark model reproduces the mass spectrum of the previously observed hadrons remarkably well, we are led to the conclusion that, without considerable modifications, both the new states do not fit into this framework.

## Key claim

Despite the nonrelativistic potential model reproducing the mass spectrum of previously observed hadrons with high accuracy (quality measure Q = 0.019%), the two newly discovered charmed strange meson states D_sJ*(2317)± and D_sJ(2463)± cannot be accommodated as (c s-bar) bound states within this framework without considerable modifications.

## Method

The authors employed a nonrelativistic quark-antiquark potential model with a Coulomb-plus-linear "funnel-like" shape improved by an intermediate exponential damping, originally developed by Ono and Schöberl (1982). The Schrödinger equation was solved numerically using the Breit-Fermi Hamiltonian with relativistic corrections up to second order in inverse quark masses, including a smeared delta function for the spin-spin interaction term. Model parameters were fixed from a 1982 fit to mesons known at that time, with constituent quark masses m_u = m_d = 336 MeV, m_s = 575 MeV, m_c = 1845 MeV, and potential parameters α_s = 0.31, a = 0.15 GeV², b = 0.956 GeV, c = 2.05 GeV⁻¹.

## Key numbers

- Weighted average mass of D_sJ*(2317)±: 2317.6 ± 0.8 MeV (from BaBar, CLEO, and Belle measurements)
- Weighted average mass of D_sJ(2463)±: 2459.0 ± 1.1 MeV (from BaBar, CLEO, and Belle measurements)
- Mass difference M(D_sJ*(2317)±) - M(D_s±) = 350.0 ± 1.2 ± 1.0 MeV (CLEO)
- Mass difference M(D_sJ(2463)±) - M(D_s*±) = 351.2 ± 1.7 ± 1.0 MeV (CLEO)
- Upper limit on total decay width for both states: Γ < 7 MeV at 90% confidence level (CLEO)
- Model prediction for 1³P_0 state: 2446 MeV; for D_s1^(l) (lower J^P=1+): 2515 MeV; for D_s1^(h) (higher J^P=1+): 2527 MeV; for 1³P_2: 2561 MeV
- Quality measure Q for this model across 24 well-established mesons: 0.019%, compared to 0.041% for Godfrey-Isgur-Kokoski (1985, 1991) and 0.28% for the Bethe-Salpeter approach of Koll et al. (2000) and Merten et al. (2001)
- 1¹P_1-³P_1 mixing angle θ = 44.7°
- D_sJ*(2317)± mass is 40.8 MeV below the threshold for D^0 + K+ (2358.4 MeV) and 49.4 MeV below the threshold for D+ + K^0 (2367.0 MeV)
- D_sJ(2463)± mass is 41.5 MeV below the threshold for D*(2007)^0 + K+ (2500.5 MeV) and 48.7 MeV below the threshold for D*(2010)+ + K^0 (2507.7 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic potential model approach of Ono (1979, 1982) and Schöberl (1984) to the newly discovered D_sJ*(2317)± and D_sJ(2463)± states reported by BaBar (2003), CLEO (2003), and Belle (2003). The failure of this and other quark models (Godfrey-Isgur-Kokoski 1985, 1991; Gupta 1995; Zeng 1995; Di Pierro 2001) to reproduce the low masses prompted numerous alternative interpretations, including (c s-bar) bound states, (DK) molecules, four-quark bound states, or Dπ atoms. The authors note that the experimental center-of-gravity mass for P-wave states (2531.7 MeV) being larger than the D_sJ(2463)± mass (2459.0 MeV) is consistent with identifying D_sJ(2463)± as predominantly a ¹P_1 state, while the alternative identification would contradict theoretical expectations.
