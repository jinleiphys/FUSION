# Consequences of conformal anomaly on fluid dynamics

> We review a recent progress on fluid dynamics applied to strongly interacting nuclear matter. The efforts are made to highlight consequences of scale invariance breaking on the hydrodynamic description of a nuclear medium. Both phenomenological and analytical findings are summarized.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how the breaking of conformal symmetry (scale invariance) in QCD significantly affects the hydrodynamic description of strongly interacting nuclear matter, particularly through the bulk viscosity and its consequences for experimental observables; the abstract's claim of "recent progress" is hedged by the fact that many results are model-dependent and the precise temperature dependence and magnitude of bulk viscosity remain uncertain.

## Method

The paper surveys both phenomenological and analytical approaches to nonconformal fluid dynamics. Phenomenologically, it reviews hydrodynamic simulations that incorporate a temperature-dependent bulk viscosity peaked near the critical temperature, comparing computed observables (flow harmonics, multiplicity, mean transverse momentum, photon spectra, interferometry radii) with experimental data from ALICE. Analytically, it examines kinetic theory derivations (Boltzmann equation with mean-field effects, relaxation time approximation, 14-moment approximation) and holographic methods to extract the bulk viscosity coefficient and its relation to the conformality breaking parameter (1/3 - c_s^2).

## Key numbers

- Bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio at the peak: ζ/s ≈ 0.3 near the critical temperature, used in phenomenological studies of Pb+Pb collisions at 2760 GeV.
- For a Bose-Einstein system in the relaxation time approximation: ζ/τ_R ∝ T^4 (1/3 - c_s^2)^2 (T/m_x), where m_x includes zero-temperature and thermal mass.
- For Boltzmann statistics: ζ_Boltz/τ_R ∝ T^4 (1/3 - c_s^2)^2.
- In the weak-coupling (perturbative QCD) limit: ζ/η ∝ (1/3 - c_s^2)^2, with η ∝ T^3/(α_s^2 log(1/α_s)).
- In the strong-coupling (holographic) limit: ζ/η ∝ (1/3 - c_s^2), with η/s = 1/(4π).
- Lower bound from holography: ζ/η ≥ 2(1/3 - c_s^2) (Buchel bound).
- Direct lattice QCD simulations suggest bulk viscosity values in the range O(10^{-2}) to O(10^{-1}).

## Context

This review extends the line of work initiated by Karsch et al. (2007) on relating bulk viscosity to the lattice QCD trace anomaly, and by Noronha-Hostler et al. (2008) on bulk viscosity in the hadron resonance gas. It synthesizes recent phenomenological studies (Ryu et al. 2015, 2017; Paquet et al. 2015; Bozek et al. 2017) that incorporate a peaked bulk viscosity near the critical temperature, and analytical developments (Czajka et al. 2017, 2018) that provide a self-consistent kinetic theory treatment with mean-field effects. The work enables more precise constraints on the bulk viscosity of hot QCD matter and tests of the Buchel bound across coupling regimes.
