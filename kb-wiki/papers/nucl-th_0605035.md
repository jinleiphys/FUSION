# Open-charm enhancement at FAIR?

> We have calculated the D-meson spectral density at finite temperature within a self-consistent coupled-channel approach that generates dynamically the $Λ_c$ (2593) resonance. We find a small mass shift for the D-meson in this hot and dense medium while the spectral density develops a sizeable width. The reduced attraction felt by the D-meson in hot and dense matter together with the large width observed have important consequences for the D-meson production in the future CBM experiment at FAIR.

# Open-charm enhancement at FAIR?

## Key claim

The authors find that in hot and dense matter at conditions expected for the CBM experiment at FAIR, the D-meson experiences almost no mass shift (reduced attraction) but develops a sizeable width, contrary to earlier mean-field predictions of large attractive mass shifts. The width alone, rather than a mass shift, is identified as the only source of possible open-charm enhancement in this environment.

## Method

The D-meson spectral density at finite temperature is calculated using a self-consistent coupled-channel approach (G-matrix) with a separable potential whose parameters are fixed to reproduce the position and width of the Λc(2593) resonance. The in-medium DN interaction is solved self-consistently including Pauli blocking for nucleons, Bose enhancement for pions, and dressing of intermediate nucleon and pion propagators at finite temperature using a Walecka-type σ-ω model for nucleons and a pion self-energy in nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- At T=0 and ρ₀=0.17 fm⁻³, the D-meson potential is -23 MeV when D-mesons and nucleons are dressed; for the full calculation including pion dressing, it is -5 MeV at ρ₀ and -48 MeV at 3ρ₀.
- At T=120 MeV and zero momentum, the width of the D-meson spectral density increases from 52 MeV at ρ₀ to 163 MeV at 3ρ₀ for the first approach, and from 36 MeV at ρ₀ to 107 MeV at 3ρ₀ for the full approach including pion dressing.
- The in-medium D-meson cross sections at threshold are expected on the order of 1-20 mb for the range of densities studied.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous zero-temperature calculation (Tolos, 2004) to finite temperature, adapting to conditions expected for the CBM experiment at FAIR. It contradicts earlier mean-field predictions from the quark-meson coupling model (QMC), QCD sum rules (Arata, 1993), and chiral models (Amruta, 2002) that obtained attractive mass shifts of -50 to -200 MeV at saturation density. The results are supported by a recent study (Lutz, 2005) suggesting a two-mode structure with a repulsive main branch at T=0. The finding that only the width contributes to open-charm enhancement implies that off-shell transport theory and in-medium cross sections are needed for future CBM data interpretation.
