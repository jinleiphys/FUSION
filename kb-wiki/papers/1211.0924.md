# Theories of Low Energy Nuclear Transmutations

> Employing concrete examples from nuclear physics it is shown that low energy nuclear reactions can and have been induced by all of the four fundamental interactions (i) (stellar) gravitational, (ii) strong, (iii) electromagnetic and (iv) weak. Differences are highlighted through the great diversity in the rates and similarity through the nature of the nuclear reactions initiated by each.

## Key claim

The paper argues that low energy nuclear transmutations (LENT) can be induced by all four fundamental interactions (gravitational, strong, electromagnetic, and weak), but the abstract glosses over the crucial condition that for weak-interaction-induced neutron production in condensed matter, an external energy input of at least 0.78 MeV must be supplied via electromagnetic means (such as high electric or magnetic fields) because thermal excitation alone is insufficient even at the core of most stars.

## Method

The authors review and classify low energy nuclear reactions from stellar astrophysics and laboratory experiments, analyzing the interplay of the four fundamental interactions. They decompose reaction rates into a Coulomb Gamow suppression factor and a cross section term, and discuss how collective effects (such as many-electron Darwin interactions in condensed matter or plasmon polaritons on metallic hydride surfaces) can renormalize electron mass and enhance weak interaction rates for neutron production via inverse beta decay.

## Key numbers

- Gravitational potential energy released per gram when matter falls on the Sun's surface: \(1.91 \times 10^{15}\) erg/gm (Eq. G1)
- Present solar radiation flux: \(1.96\) erg/gm/sec (Eq. G2)
- Minimum energy gain per gram required to fuel the Sun for 4.5 billion years: \(\gtrsim 3 \times 10^{17}\) erg/gm (Eq. G3)
- Bethe-Critchfield S-factor for the pp chain reaction \(p^+ + p^+ \to d^+ + e^+ + \nu_e\): \(3.36 \times 10^{-25}\) MeV barn (Eq. K8)
- Typical S-factor for purely nuclear (non-leptonic) reactions: \(1\) KeV barn to \(10\) MeV barn (Eq. K9)
- Half-life of \(^7\)Be electron capture (\(e^- + ^7\)Be \(\to\) \(^7\)Li + \(\nu_e\)): \((4.61 \pm 0.001) \times 10^6\) sec (Eq. A6)
- Rate of \(^7\)Be electron capture: \((2.169 \pm 0.005) \times 10^{-7}\) Hz (from Eq. A6)
- Neutron energy from \(d + d \to n + ^3\)He reaction: \(2.5\) MeV (Eq. W1)
- Integrated neutron yield from deuterated fibers (diameter \(\sim 20\) micron): \(\sim 10^{10}\) neutrons of \(2.5\) MeV
- Natural deuterium isotopic abundance in normal matter: \(1.5 \times 10^{-4}\)
- Normal fiber neutron yields are 4 to 5 orders of magnitude higher than expected from deuterium contamination
- Typical explosion field for Coulomb explosion of clusters: \(\sim 10^{11}\) Volts/m (Eq. E3)
- Cluster radii measured via light scattering: from about \(55\) Angstrom to about \(12\) Angstrom as gas jet temperature increased from \(85\) K to \(120\) K at \(55\) atmospheres pressure
- Threshold energy for weak neutron production from \(e^- + p^+\): \(0.78\) MeV (from Q value)
- Bethe's estimate for the timescale of the \(e^- + p^+ \to n + \nu_e\) reaction in the Sun's core: \(10^{30}\) years

## Context

This review extends the classic work of Bethe and Critchfield (1938) on the pp chain and von Weizsacker's proposal for proton-proton fusion, placing stellar nuclear reactions and laboratory experiments into a unified framework based on the four fundamental interactions. It connects the exploding wire experiments of Stephanakis (1970s) and Coulomb explosion experiments of Zweiback (2000s) with weak-interaction neutron production mechanisms proposed by Widom and Larsen (2006), and discusses implications for neutron production in lightning (Shah, 1985) and thunderstorms (Gurevich, 2004). The paper enables future work on electroweak LENT via plasmon polaritons on metallic hydride surfaces and piezoelectric materials, where collective electron effects can overcome the 0.78 MeV threshold for neutron production.
