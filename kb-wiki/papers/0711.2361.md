# Reflection symmetry instability at high spins in 162,164Yb

> A shape evolution of 162,164Yb in yrast states is traced using the self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations. We found that nonaxial octupole deformations (in particular, Y_{31} term) become favorable at large rotational frequencies (> 0.4 MeV) in 162Yb, while in 164Yb a nonaxial quadrupole shape is dominant at fast rotation. The cranked Nilsson model and random phase approximation are used to understand the dynamics of octupole correlations in both nuclei. We demonstrate that the disappearance of one of the octupole vibrational modes in the rotating frame gives rise to the nonaxial octupole deformations in 162Yb, while the octupole modes are nonzero in 164Yb.

## Key claim

Nonaxial octupole deformations (specifically the Y₃₁ term) become favorable in ¹⁶²Yb at rotational frequencies above 0.4 MeV, while in ¹⁶⁴Yb a nonaxial quadrupole shape remains dominant at fast rotation; the authors demonstrate that the disappearance of one octupole vibrational mode in the rotating frame gives rise to the nonaxial octupole deformations in ¹⁶²Yb, whereas the octupole modes remain nonzero in ¹⁶⁴Yb.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent cranked Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations using the SkP interaction (and SKIII parametrization for comparison) to trace shape evolution of yrast states in ¹⁶²,¹⁶⁴Yb, neglecting pairing correlations as unimportant at large rotational frequencies. They also employed a cranked Nilsson model combined with random phase approximation (CRPA) to analyze the dynamics of octupole correlations, using separable multipole interactions and strength constants adjusted to satisfy conservation laws and separate spurious modes from physical excitations.

## Key numbers

- Nonaxial octupole deformations become favorable at ℏΩ > 0.4 MeV in ¹⁶²Yb
- In ¹⁶²Yb, the band B4 crosses the yrast band B1 at rotational frequency ℏΩ = 0.265 MeV
- In ¹⁶⁴Yb, the band B11 crosses the ground band B1 at ℏΩ ≈ 0.27 MeV
- B(E3, ΔI=3) transition probability in ¹⁶²Yb increases from ~0.7 W.u. at ℏΩ ≈ 0.3 MeV to ~4.5 W.u. at ℏΩ ≈ 0.45 MeV
- B(E3, ΔI=3) transition probability in ¹⁶⁴Yb increases from ~1.2 W.u. at ℏΩ ≈ 0.3 MeV to ~4.4 W.u. at ℏΩ ≈ 0.45 MeV
- The maximal two-quasiparticle component weight in the lowest negative parity one-phonon states of ¹⁶²Yb is ~65%
- The maximal two-quasiparticle component in the octupole phonons of ¹⁶⁴Yb is ~75%
- Good agreement between calculated and experimental kinematic momenta of inertia holds till ~0.6 MeV in ¹⁶⁴Yb and till ~0.45 MeV in ¹⁶²Yb

## Context

This work extends previous studies of octupole correlations in rotating nuclei, particularly calculations with Skyrme forces predicting nonaxial Y₃₁ octupole deformation in light nuclei at high spins (Yamagami 2001) and nonaxial Y₃₂ octupole deformation in actinide nuclei (Tsvetkov 2002). It also builds on cranking plus Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with Gogny forces in the Ba-Sm region (Garrote 1998). The results enable predictions of octupole band formation with strong B(E1) and B(E3) transitions to the yrast line at high spins in the Z~70, N~90 transitional region, and suggest that octupole deformations arise from octupole phonon condensation at fast rotation.
