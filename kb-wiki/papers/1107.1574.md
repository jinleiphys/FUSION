# Validity of the Hadronic Freeze-Out Curve

> We analyze hadro-chemical freeze-out in central Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS energies, employing the hybrid version of UrQMD which models hadronization by the Cooper-Frye mechanism, and matches to a final hadron-resonance cascade. We fit the results both before and after the cascade stage using the Statistical Hadronization Model, to assess the effect of the cascade phase. We observe a strong effect on antibaryon yields except anti-Ω, resulting in a shift in T and μ_B. We discuss the implications for the freeze-out curve.

## Key claim

The hadron-resonance cascade stage after hadronization does not cool the system in chemical equilibrium from the phase coexistence line down to the freeze-out curve; instead, it distorts the hadron yield distribution away from equilibrium, particularly suppressing antibaryons except anti-Ω, which shifts the fitted freeze-out parameters (T, μ_B) downward but with poor fit quality.

## Method

The authors used the hybrid version of the UrQMD transport model, which employs a 3+1 hydrodynamic expansion during the high-density stage followed by Cooper-Frye hadronization at a critical energy density, and then attaches a hadron-resonance cascade afterburner. They fitted the hadron yields from central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies (40A and 158A GeV) using the Statistical Hadronization Model (SM), both before the cascade stage (directly after hadronization) and after the cascade stage, to compare the resulting temperature T and baryochemical potential μ_B.

## Key numbers

- For central Pb+Pb at 158A GeV, the SM fit to UrQMD results shifts from (T = 162 MeV, μ_B = 250 MeV) before the cascade to (T = 153 MeV, μ_B = 261 MeV) after the cascade.
- The χ² value for the SM fit at 158A GeV increases from 4.4 before the cascade to 21.5 after the cascade, indicating a dramatic decrease in fit quality.
- At 158A GeV, when antibaryons (p̄, Λ̄, Ξ̄) are excluded from the SM fit to the afterburner output, the fit yields a tolerable χ² and shows no cooling relative to the pre-cascade parameters.
- For real NA49 data at 158A GeV, excluding p̄, Λ̄, and Ξ̄ from the SM fit moves the temperature from 158 MeV to above 170 MeV at reasonable χ².

## Context

This work tests the scenario that the hadron-resonance cascade stage maintains chemical equilibrium while cooling from the parton-hadron coexistence boundary (inferred from lattice QCD at low μ_B and chiral restoration theory at high μ_B) down to the conventional freeze-out curve. The results contradict the assumption that the freeze-out curve represents an equilibrium cooling path, and instead suggest that the cascade stage distorts yields, particularly for antibaryons. This enables a re-evaluation of the freeze-out curve at μ_B from about 280 to 430 MeV, where previous statistical model analyses (e.g., by Becattini (2003) and others) may have been affected by cascade-stage distortions, while at RHIC energies with μ_B near zero the effect is expected to be smaller due to approximate baryon-antibaryon symmetry.
