# Chiral symmetry in nuclei: partial restoration and its consequences

> Partial restoration in nuclear matter of the chiral symmetry of QCD is discussed together with some of its possible signals. Estimates of corrections to the leading, linear dependence of the quark condensate are found to be small, implying a significant reduction of that condensate in matter. The importance of the pion cloud for the scalar quark density of a single nucleon indicates a close connection between chiral symmetry restoration and the attractive two-pion exchange interaction between nucleons. This force is sufficiently long-ranged that nucleons in nuclear matter will feel a significant degree of symmetry restoration despite the strong correlations between them. Expected consequences of this include reductions in hadron masses and decay constants. Various signals of these effects are discussed, in particular the enhancement of the axial charge of a nucleon in matter.

## Key claim

Partial restoration of chiral symmetry in nuclear matter is expected at ordinary nuclear densities, with the leading-order linear density dependence of the quark condensate receiving only small corrections, but the quantitative consequences for hadron properties remain uncertain and model-dependent.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical approaches to chiral symmetry restoration in nuclear matter, including the linear sigma model, the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, and QCD sum rules. It examines the quark condensate's density dependence via the Feynman-Hellmann theorem and the pion-nucleon sigma commutator, and evaluates corrections from meson-exchange contributions, particularly two-pion exchange, to the leading linear term.

## Key numbers

* The leading density dependence of the quark condensate in nuclear matter is given by ⟨q̄q⟩_ρ / ⟨q̄q⟩_0 = 1 − (σ_πN / f_π² m_π²) ρ, where σ_πN = 45 ± 7 MeV, f_π = 92.5 ± 0.2 MeV, and m_π is the pion mass.
* The vacuum quark condensate per flavour is approximately −(210 MeV)³ to −(260 MeV)³.
* The pion-nucleon sigma commutator σ_πN = 45 ± 7 MeV, with a scalar form factor radius of about 1.3 fm and a roughly 15 MeV difference between the value at q²=0 and at the Cheng-Dashen point (q²=2m_π²).
* In the linear sigma model at tree level, with a σ mass of 1200 MeV, the sigma commutator is σ_πN ≃ 13 MeV.
* The kaon-nucleon sigma commutator σ_KN is at least ∼ 280 MeV, estimated as (m_s / 4m̄) times σ_πN.

## Context

This review extends earlier work on chiral symmetry restoration in nuclei by Brown (1988), Weise (1988), and Barshay and Brown (1989), and addresses the model-independent leading-order result of Drukarev and Levin (1988) and Cohen, Furnstahl, and Griegel (1992). It enables further investigation into observable signals of partial restoration, particularly the enhancement of the axial charge of a nucleon in matter, and motivates studies of hadron mass reductions and modifications of nuclear forces.
