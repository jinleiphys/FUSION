# Skyrme-Model $πNN$ Form Factor and Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction

> We apply the strong $πNN$ form factor, which emerges from the Skyrme model, in the two-nucleon system using a one-boson-exchange (OBE) model for the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction. Deuteron properties and phase parameters of NN scattering are reproduced well. In contrast to the form factor of monopole shape that is traditionally used in OBE models, the Skyrme form factor leaves low momentum transfers essentially unaffected while it suppresses the high-momentum region strongly. It turns out that this behavior is very appropriate for models of the NN interaction and makes possible to use a soft pion form factor in the NN system. As a consequence, the $πN$ and the $NN$ systems can be described using the same soft $πNN$ form factor, which is impossible with the monopole.

# Skyrme-Model πNN Form Factor and Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction

## Key claim

The Skyrme-model πNN form factor, when applied in a one-boson-exchange (OBE) model for the nucleon-nucleon interaction, reproduces deuteron properties and NN scattering phase shifts well, *provided* the Skyrme term has sufficient strength (Skyrme parameter e ≈ 4 or less); the form factor leaves low momentum transfers essentially unaffected while strongly suppressing high-momentum transfers, which allows a soft pion form factor to work in the NN system, something impossible with the conventional monopole shape.

## Method

The authors extract the strong πNN form factor from the Skyrme model by redefining the pion field to absorb the local metric in the kinetic energy of the fluctuating field, then compute the form factor via the Fourier transform of the classical soliton profile. They insert this Skyrme-model form factor into the Bonn-B OBE potential (replacing only the pion monopole form factor), adjust the vector-meson coupling constants and the sigma-boson coupling to optimize the fit, and solve the Blankenbecler-Sugar three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for NN scattering.

## Key numbers

- Skyrme-model πNN coupling constant at zero momentum transfer: GπNN(0) = 0.99 (2 MN/mπ) = 13.5, with axial coupling constant gA = 1.30.
- For the pure Skyrme model (e = 3.5, gω = 0), the form factor shows very small slope near q² = 0 and negative curvature, then falls off strongly above q² > 50 mπ².
- The vector-meson model yields a form factor close to dipole shape with cutoff mass Λα ≈ 1.5 GeV.
- Deuteron quadrupole moment predicted with Skyrme FF and g²π/4π = 13.5: Qd = 0.274 fm², within the empirical range.
- For comparison, a soft monopole with Λπ = 0.8 GeV gives disastrous results for NN mixing parameters ε1 and ε2 and deuteron properties.
- The original Bonn-B potential uses Λπ = 1.7 GeV and g²π/4π = 14.4 for the monopole form factor.

## Context

This work extends the Bonn-B OBE potential (Machleidt, 1989) by replacing its phenomenological monopole πNN form factor with a form factor derived from the Skyrme model, a QCD-inspired effective meson theory. It resolves a long-standing puzzle: OBE models seemingly required a very hard pion form factor (Λπ ≈ 1.3–2 GeV) because the monopole shape suppresses low momentum transfers, which the Skyrme form factor avoids. The result enables consistent description of both πN and NN systems using the same soft πNN form factor, which was impossible with monopole shapes (as shown by the failure of cloudy-bag form factors with R ≈ 0.8 fm or Λπ ≈ 800 MeV). The work also addresses the discrepancy between the large πNN coupling constant (g²π/4π = 14.4) used in Bonn-B and the modern determination (g²π/4π = 13.5 ± 0.1 from Stoks et al., 1993).
