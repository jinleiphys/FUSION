# $Δ(1232)$ production in the $γp \to ϕπp$ reaction

> In this work, the production of baryon resonances in the $γp \to ϕπp$ reaction near threshold is investigated within an effective Lagrangian approach. incorporates intermediate resonances, including $Δ(1232)$, $N(1440)$, $N(1520)$, and $N(1535)$, alongside non-resonant background contributions. The results indicate that the reaction is dominated by the $Δ(1232)$ excitation near threshold. A key focus of this study is the exploration of the $a_0(980)$ meson exchange in the $Δ(1232)$ production, as this reaction offers a unique opportunity to probe the poorly known $Δ(1232)N a_0$ coupling. We demonstrate that the parity asymmetry ($P_σ$), accessible through the $ϕ$-meson's spin density matrix elements, serves as a suitable experimental observable to identify the contribution from the $a_0(980)$ exchange.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $\gamma p \to \phi \pi p$ reaction near threshold is dominated by $\Delta(1232)$ excitation, and that the parity asymmetry $P_\sigma$ of the $\phi$ meson can serve as an experimental observable to identify the contribution from $a_0(980)$ meson exchange, thereby probing the poorly known $\Delta(1232) N a_0$ coupling. The hedge is that this identification works only if the coupling is sufficiently large; for very weak couplings (e.g., $g_{\Delta(1232)Na_0} = 2.9$ or smaller), only an upper limit on the coupling may be estimable.

## Method

The authors use an effective Lagrangian approach to compute the scattering amplitudes for the $\gamma p \to \phi \pi p$ reaction, including intermediate baryon resonances $\Delta(1232)$, $N(1440)$, $N(1520)$, and $N(1535)$ with $\pi$ and $\eta$ exchanges, plus a $t$-channel background via $\rho$ exchange. For the $\Delta(1232)$ excitation specifically, they include both $\pi$ and $a_0(980)$ exchange contributions, with coupling constants extracted from PDG decay widths and form factors applied at vertices. The total cross section, invariant mass spectra, angular distributions, and the parity asymmetry $P_\sigma$ (derived from $\phi$ meson spin density matrix elements) are calculated and compared for different values of the unknown $\Delta(1232) N a_0$ coupling constant.

## Key numbers

- The $\Delta(1232)$ resonance dominates the reaction near threshold, with a clear peak in the $p\pi^0$ invariant mass spectrum at $M_{p\pi^0} \approx 1.23$ GeV.
- The $\phi$ meson total width is 4.25 MeV, with branching ratios used: $\phi \to \pi \gamma$ at $1.32 \times 10^{-3}$, $\phi \to \eta \gamma$ at $1.3 \times 10^{-2}$, $\phi \to \rho \pi$ at 0.15, and $\phi \to a_0 \gamma$ at $7.6 \times 10^{-5}$.
- The $\Delta(1232)$ has a width of 117 MeV and a branching ratio to $N\pi$ of 0.994, yielding $g^2/4\pi = 0.12$ for the $\Delta N\pi$ coupling.
- Three test values of the $\Delta(1232) N a_0$ coupling constant are used: $g_{\Delta(1232)Na_0} = 20.8$, $9.3$, and $2.9$, chosen so that the $a_0$ exchange contribution is roughly 1/2, 1/10, and 1/100 of the $\pi$ exchange contribution, respectively.
- For $g_{\Delta(1232)Na_0} = 20.8$, the parity asymmetry $P_\sigma$ ranges from about 0.1 at $\cos\theta_\phi = -1$ (backward angles) to about -0.8 at $\cos\theta_\phi = 1$ (forward angles); for $g_{\Delta(1232)Na_0} = 2.9$, $P_\sigma$ varies from approximately -0.98 to -1.

## Context

This work extends the study of baryon resonances in photoproduction reactions beyond single-meson final states, following the approach used for $\gamma p \to \phi K^+\Lambda$ by Lv et al. to study the $N(1535)K\Lambda$ coupling. The method of using $\phi$ meson spin density matrix elements to identify exchanged meson parity was previously applied by Oh et al. (2006, 2012) to identify scalar $\kappa$ meson exchange. The results enable future experimental measurements of $P_\sigma$ in the $\gamma p \to \phi \pi p$ reaction at facilities such as CLAS or LEPS to extract the poorly known $\Delta(1232) N a_0$ coupling, which is inaccessible through direct $\Delta(1232)$ decays due to kinematic constraints.
