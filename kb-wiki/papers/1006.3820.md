# Three and Four Harmonically Trapped Particles in an Effective Field Theory Framework

> We study systems of few two-component fermions interacting via short-range interactions within a harmonic-oscillator trap. The dominant interactions, which are two-body, are organized according to the number of derivatives and defined in a two-body truncated model space made from a bound-state basis. Leading-order (LO) interactions are solved for exactly using the formalism of the No-Core Shell Model, whereas corrections are treated as many-body perturbations. We show explicitly that next-to-LO and next-to-next-to-LO interactions improve convergence as the model space increases. We present results at unitarity for three- and four-fermion systems, which show excellent agreement with the exact solution (for the three-body problem) and results obtained by others methods (in the four-body case). We also present results for finite scattering lengths and non-zero range of the interaction, including (at positive scattering length) observation of a change in the structure of the three-body ground state and extraction of the atom-dimer scattering length.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that an effective field theory (EFT) approach for harmonically trapped two-component fermions, when solved with a No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) at leading order and perturbative corrections at next-to-leading order (NLO) and next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), systematically improves convergence of few-body energies as the model space increases, provided the cutoff for the many-body system is decoupled from the cutoff for the two-body subsystem.

## Method

The authors construct two-body short-range interactions in a harmonic-oscillator trap using an EFT expansion in derivatives of contact interactions, organized by the number of derivatives. Leading-order (LO) interactions are solved exactly via NCSM diagonalization in a truncated harmonic-oscillator basis, while NLO and NNLO corrections are treated using many-body perturbation theory. The two-body interaction is renormalized at each two-body cutoff \(N_2^{max}\) by fitting to exact energies from the effective range expansion, and the many-body system is solved with a separate, larger cutoff \(N_A^{max}\) to account for spectator particles carrying excitation energy.

## Key numbers

* Ground state energy of the three-fermion system at unitarity: at NNLO with \(N_2^{max}=22\), \(E/\omega = 2.7715\), compared to the exact value \(E/\omega = 2.7727\) from Werner (2006).
* First excited state (\(L^\pi=1^-\)) energy of the three-fermion system at unitarity: at NNLO with \(N_2^{max}=22\), \(E/\omega = 4.7721\), compared to the exact value \(E/\omega = 4.7727\) from Werner (2006).
* Lowest \(L^\pi=0^+\) state energy at unitarity: at NLO with \(N_2^{max}=26\), \(E/\omega = 3.1652\), compared to the exact value \(E/\omega = 3.1662\) from Werner (2006).
* First excited \(L^\pi=0^+\) state energy at unitarity: at NNLO with \(N_2^{max}=26\), \(E/\omega = 5.1614\), compared to the exact value \(E/\omega = 5.1662\) from Werner (2006).
* Ground-state parity inversion occurs at \(b/a_2 \simeq 1.34\) for the three-fermion system with \(r_2/b=0\).

## Context

This work extends the EFT for trapped two-body systems developed by Rotureau et al. (2010) to three- and four-fermion systems, improving upon the LO-only NCSM results of Stetcu et al. (2007) by including NLO and NNLO corrections that accelerate convergence. The method enables systematic extraction of atom-dimer scattering lengths and identification of universal features such as the ground-state parity inversion, and provides a framework that can be applied to larger fermion systems with increased computational resources. The results at unitarity agree with semi-analytical solutions from Werner (2006) and with other numerical methods such as those of Chang (2008) and Alhassid et al. (2008).
