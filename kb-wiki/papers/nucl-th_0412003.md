# Effective Field Theory and Isospin Violation in Few-Nucleon Systems

## Key claim
The leading and subleading isospin-breaking three-nucleon forces (3NFs) can be systematically derived within chiral effective field theory (EFT) using a consistent power counting where the quark mass difference parameter epsilon and the electric charge e are both treated as order q/Lambda. The paper presents explicit analytic forms for these forces but does not provide numerical predictions for observables, only estimating that the isospin-violating 3NFs are expected to give a small but non-negligible contribution to the 3He-3H binding-energy difference.

## Method
The author uses chiral EFT with the power counting rules epsilon ~ e ~ q/Lambda and e^2/(4 pi)^2 ~ q^4/Lambda^4 to classify isospin-breaking vertices. The leading (nu=4) and subleading (nu=5) isospin-violating 3NFs are derived using the method of unitary transformation, evaluating three-nucleon matrix elements of operators built from the effective Lagrangian. The resulting forces are expressed in terms of one-pion-exchange and two-pion-exchange topologies with strengths determined by the strong neutron-proton mass difference, the charged-to-neutral pion mass difference, and electromagnetic low-energy constants.

## Key numbers
- The quark mass ratio parameter epsilon = (m_d - m_u)/(m_d + m_u) is approximately 1/3.
- The leading isospin-breaking 3NFs appear at order nu = 4 in the chiral expansion, with subleading corrections at nu = 5.
- The strong part of the proton-neutron mass difference is given by (delta m)^str = -4 c_5 epsilon M_pi^2, and the electromagnetic part by (delta m)^em = -f_2 e^2 F_pi^2.
- The charged-to-neutral pion mass-squared difference delta M_pi^2 = M_pi+^2 - M_pi0^2 enters the subleading 3NFs.

## Context
This work extends the chiral EFT treatment of isospin violation from two-nucleon forces (previously studied by Van Kolck (1993), van Kolck (1996-1997), Epelbaum (1999), Walzl (2000), and Friar (1999-2004)) to three-nucleon forces. The derived isospin-breaking 3NFs are intended for future use in analyzing few-nucleon systems within chiral EFT, particularly to compute the 3He-3H binding-energy difference. The paper is a conference proceedings contribution that presents the formalism and analytic results without numerical applications, leaving the numerical implementation and comparison with experiment as open tasks.
