# Wavefunction localization and its semiclassical description in a 3-dimensional system with mixed classical dynamics

> We discuss the localization of wavefunctions along planes containing the shortest periodic orbits in a three-dimensional billiard system with axial symmetry. This model mimicks the self-consistent mean field of a heavy nucleus at deformations that occur characteristically during the fission process [1,2]. Many actinide nuclei become unstable against left-right asymmetric deformations, which results in asymmetric fragment mass distributions. Recently we have shown [3,4] that the onset of this asymmetry can be explained in the semiclassical periodic orbit theory by a few short periodic orbits lying in planes perpendicular to the symmetry axis. Presently we show that these orbits are surrounded by small islands of stability in an otherwise chaotic phase space, and that the wavefunctions of the diabatic quantum states that are most sensitive to the left-right asymmetry have their extrema in the same planes. An EBK quantization of the classical motion near these planes reproduces the exact eigenenergies of the diabatic quantum states surprisingly well.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the wavefunctions of diabatic quantum states most sensitive to left-right asymmetry in a deformed cavity model of an actinide nucleus are localized in the same planes that contain the shortest periodic orbits, and that an EBK quantization of the classical motion near these planes reproduces the exact eigenenergies of those states "surprisingly well", though the agreement is perfect only for states with the smallest quantum number n=1 and degrades for n=2.

## Method

The authors studied a three-dimensional axially symmetric billiard cavity whose shape mimics the mean field of a heavy nucleus at deformations characteristic of the fission process, parametrized by an elongation c and an octupole-type asymmetry alpha. They computed the quantum spectrum with Dirichlet boundary conditions for states with fixed angular momentum L_z, identified diabatic states by their energy variation with alpha, and compared their probability distributions to the positions of the shortest periodic orbits. A quasimode approximation was constructed by linearizing the classical motion around stable equatorial planes, replacing the cavity locally by an axially symmetric ellipsoid with matching curvature radii, and applying EBK quantization conditions to the resulting action variables.

## Key numbers

- The outer fission barrier is located at elongation c=1.53; by varying alpha from zero to approximately 0.13, the shell energy delta E is lowered by about 2.5–3 MeV.
- In the symmetric case (alpha=0), the shortest orbits at z_0=0 are slightly unstable, while those at -z_2=z_1=0.414 (in units of R) are stable.
- In the asymmetric case (alpha=0.13), there is only one plane at z_0=0.671 containing stable orbits.
- For a model calculation of Pu-240 with fixed Fermi energy k_F=12.1/R, the minimum of the shell energy occurs at alpha_0 approximately 0.13.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous semiclassical periodic orbit theory calculations (Brack, Reimann, Sieber 1997; Brack et al. 1998) that showed the onset of left-right asymmetry in actinide fission can be explained by a few short periodic orbits. It connects those results to the earlier quantum-mechanical finding by Gustafsson, Moller, and Nilsson (1971) that specific quantum states with probability maxima in the equatorial plane are responsible for the instability against asymmetric deformations. The paper enables a unified semiclassical description of both the shell energy and the wavefunction localization, and shows that the shell effect driving the asymmetry simultaneously increases the chaoticity of the classical phase space.
