# Hadron Chemistry at High-PT with Identified Particles

> We discuss mechanisms that change the hadron chemistry for high momentum particles emitted in high energy nuclear collisions. We argue that particle ratios naturally tend to be different from jets in the vacuum. We show results of computations in a model that propagates leading particles through a quark gluon plasma and permits elastic flavor changing processes. We predict less suppression for kaons compared to pions in central collision. We also discuss elliptic flow resulting from flavor changing processes.

## Key claim

Particle ratios at high transverse momentum in heavy ion collisions are expected to differ from those in vacuum jets because flavor-changing elastic scatterings of leading partons in the quark gluon plasma drive the jet ensemble toward chemical equilibration, though this mechanism alone cannot explain the observed excess of protons over pions at momenta up to 10 GeV/c.

## Method

The author models an ensemble of jets described by their leading parton which propagates through a quark gluon plasma, allowing elastic flavor-changing processes such as Compton scattering (q+g ↔ g+q) and annihilation (q+anti-q ↔ g+g) to alter the parton chemistry. Leading partons are then fragmented once they exit the medium, translating the modified parton chemistry into hadron chemistry that differs from that in proton-proton collisions.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear modification factor R_AA for protons relative to pions approaches one when conversions are allowed, but STAR data shows even less suppression for protons at momenta of 10 GeV/c or more.
- The model predicts less suppression for kaons compared to pions in central collisions, with R_AA for neutral kaons shown with and without conversion processes.
- The azimuthal asymmetry v_2 for strange quarks from jet conversions is predicted to be negative, leading to a suppression of kaon v_2 compared to pion v_2.
- Heavy charm and bottom quarks at high momentum show negligible enhancement both at RHIC and LHC energies due to large thresholds for pair creation and small amounts of primordial heavy quarks in the medium.

## Context

This work extends the leading particle conversion model first applied to photon and dilepton production by Fries, Muller, and Srivastava (2003) and later developed by Liu, Ko, and Zhang (2007) and Liu and Fries (2008). It contrasts with the approach of Sapeta and Wiedemann (2008) which studies chemical composition changes inside a single jet cone from increased multiplicities. The results enable predictions for strange hadron elliptic flow measurements at high transverse momentum, and suggest that soft physics like quark recombination (Fries, Muller, Nonaka, and Bass, 2003) may still contribute to baryon production at large momenta.
