# Thermal and chemical equilibration in a gluon plasma

> We show the evolution of a gluon plasma towards equilibrium starting at some early moment when the momentum distribution in the central region is momentaneously isotropic. Using HIJING results for Au+Au collision as initial input, we consider thermalization and chemical equilibration simultaneously at both LHC and RHIC energies. Thermalization is shown to be driven chiefly by inelastic process in our scenario contradicting common assumption that this is the role of elastic process. We argue that only the inelastic dominancy depends on the initial conditions but not the dominance itself.

## Key claim

Thermalization in a gluon plasma is driven chiefly by inelastic processes (gg ↔ ggg), contradicting the common assumption that elastic processes dominate; the authors argue that only the degree of inelastic dominance depends on initial conditions, not the dominance itself.

## Method

The evolution of a gluon plasma towards equilibrium was modeled using the Boltzmann equation with a relaxation-time approximation, starting from an isotropic momentum distribution at an early time. Initial conditions (energy density, number density) were taken from HIJING Monte Carlo results for Au+Au collisions at both LHC and RHIC energies. The collision time was determined entirely from the inelastic gluon multiplication process gg ↔ ggg, using the Bertsch-Gunion formula for the amplitude with a time-dependent Debye screening mass and including Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal suppression via a theta-function cutoff.

## Key numbers

- At LHC: initial time τ₀ ≈ 0.5 fm/c, initial collision time θ₀ ≈ 0.84 fm/c, ratio θ₀/τ₀ ≈ 1.6
- At RHIC: initial time τ₀ ≈ 0.7 fm/c, initial collision time θ₀ ≈ 2.7 fm/c, ratio θ₀/τ₀ ≈ 4
- Estimated fugacity at end of calculation: l_est ≈ 0.94 at LHC (τ = 8.75 fm/c), l_est ≈ 0.40 at RHIC (τ = 3.55 fm/c)
- Maximum anisotropy in momentum distribution reached at about 1.35 fm/c at LHC and 2.2 fm/c at RHIC
- Using α_s = 0.3 and time step Δτ = 0.05 fm/c; varying Δτ to 0.02 fm/c shifts values by up to 6.25%
- Computation stopped when estimated temperature fell below 200 MeV
- For elastic-only estimate at RHIC (using Heiselberg and Wang formula), θ varied between 4.28 and 3.53 fm/c over τ ≈ 1.40 to 1.76 fm/c, about 2.0 times higher than the inelastic-derived θ in the same range

## Context

This work extends the approach of Heiselberg and Wang (1995), who considered only elastic interactions and only in the time range τ₀ ≲ τ ≪ θ, by providing a more complete treatment that includes inelastic processes and covers longer timescales. It uses initial conditions from the HIJING model of Gyulassy and Wang (1994) and follows the earlier studies of thermalization and chemical equilibration by Biro (1993), Levai (1995), and Wang (1995). The results enable future investigation of quark-gluon plasma evolution and show that inelastic processes must be included for realistic equilibration studies, contrary to the common assumption that elastic scattering dominates thermalization.
