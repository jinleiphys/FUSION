# Exactly Solvable Pairing Model Using an Extension of Richardson-Gaudin Approach

> We introduce a new class of exactly solvable boson pairing models using the technique of Richardson and Gaudin. Analytical expressions for all energy eigenvalues and first few energy eigenstates are given. In addition, another solution to Gaudin's equation is also mentioned. A relation with the Calogero-Sutherland model is suggested.

# Exactly Solvable Pairing Model Using an Extension of Richardson-Gaudin Approach

## Key claim
The authors introduce a new class of exactly solvable boson pairing models using the Richardson-Gaudin technique, claiming that all energy eigenvalues can be expressed analytically without solving the Bethe ansatz equations. However, this analytical result is conditional on the specific choice of two real parameters $x_1$ and $x_2$ and the particular form of the lowest-weight function $W(\lambda)$ for the boson realization; the corresponding eigenstates still require solving the Bethe ansatz equations or finding roots of a polynomial.

## Method
The authors construct a realization of the rational Gaudin algebra using boson creation and annihilation operators with two types of bosons ($b_\beta$ and $a_\alpha$) and two real parameters $x_1$ and $x_2$. They derive the pairing Hamiltonian $\tilde{H}(\lambda)$ from the Gaudin algebra generators and compute its energy eigenvalues by evaluating sums $S_1$ and $S_2$ using symmetries of the Bethe ansatz equations, bypassing explicit solution of the equations. For the eigenstates, they transform the Bethe ansatz equations into a hypergeometric differential equation whose polynomial solutions yield the rapidities $\xi_\alpha$ as roots of an $n$th-order polynomial. Additionally, they present a new solution to Gaudin's equation by relaxing the antisymmetry condition, yielding a new set of commuting Hamiltonians $h_i^{(q)}$ related to the spin-Sutherland model.

## Key numbers
- The analytical energy eigenvalue formula for $\tilde{H}(\lambda)$ is $\tilde{E}_n(\lambda) = \frac{1}{(x_1-\lambda)(x_2-\lambda)}\left[n(n-1)+(n_1+n_2)\frac{n}{2}\right]$, where $n$ is the number of Bethe ansatz rapidities, $n_1$ and $n_2$ are the numbers of boson types, and $x_1$, $x_2$ are real parameters.
- The Bethe ansatz equations after change of variables become $\sum_{\beta\neq\alpha}^n \frac{1}{\zeta_\alpha-\zeta_\beta} + \frac{n_2/4}{\zeta_{\alpha}} -\frac{n_1/4}{1-\zeta_{\alpha}} = 0$ for $\alpha=1,2,\dots,n$.
- The polynomial whose roots give the rapidities is $\sum_{k=1}^\infty \frac{(-n)_k (n+n_1/2+n_2/2-1)_k}{k!(n_2/2)_k } \zeta^k=0$, which terminates at $k=n$.
- The new solution to Gaudin's equation is $w_{ij}^\alpha = q\coth[q(u_i-u_j)]+q$ for $\alpha=1,2,3$, where $q$ is a complex parameter.

## Context
This work extends the Richardson-Gaudin technique, originally developed for fermionic BCS pairing (Richardson 1963, Gaudin 1976), to bosonic systems, specifically generalizing the $sd$-version of the interacting boson model (Arima and Iachello 1976; Balantekin 2003, 2004). The analytical energy formula without solving Bethe ansatz equations is a significant simplification over standard Richardson-Gaudin approaches. The new solution to Gaudin's equation, obtained by relaxing the antisymmetry condition, provides a connection to the spin-Sutherland model (Sutherland 1971) analogous to the known relation between rational Gaudin magnets and the spin-Calogero model (Calogero 1969). This enables future work on exactly solvable boson pairing models with more general interactions and suggests a Lax-pair formulation for the associated many-body systems.
