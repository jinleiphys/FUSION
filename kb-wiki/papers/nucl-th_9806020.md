# Alternative Linear Chiral Models for Nuclear Matter

> The equation of state of a family of alternative linear chiral models in the mean field approximation is discussed. We investigate the analogy between some of these models with current models in the literature, and we show that it is possible to reproduce very well the saturation properties of nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The authors show that a family of linear chiral models with field-dependent coupling constants (scaling with the scalar field) can reproduce nuclear matter saturation properties in the mean-field approximation, contrary to the original linear sigma model which fails to do so. The key hedge is that this success depends on allowing the coupling constants to depend on the scalar field density, and the models are only analyzed in the mean-field approximation without surface terms for finite nuclei.

## Method

The authors start from the linear sigma model Lagrangian with nucleon, scalar sigma, pseudoscalar pion, and vector meson fields, but replace the constant meson-nucleon coupling constants with effective couplings that depend on the chiral invariant combination sigma^2 + pi^2 via parameters alpha and beta. They then shift the scalar field to its vacuum expectation value, apply the mean-field approximation, derive the energy density and gap equation for the effective nucleon mass M*, and fix the coupling constants C_v^2 and C_sigma^2 to reproduce the empirical saturation point (E/N = -15.75 MeV at k_F = 1.3 fm^{-1}) for various choices of alpha and beta.

## Key numbers

- Saturation energy per nucleon: E/N = -15.75 MeV at Fermi momentum k_F = 1.3 fm^{-1} (used as the fitting constraint)
- Pion-nucleon coupling constant fixed at experimental value: g_pi^2/(4 pi) = 13.6
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation M_0*/M: values range from near unity (for beta=0) down to approximately 0.6 (for alpha=beta and beta=1 with alpha around 1.5), with the phenomenologically successful range given as 0.58 <= M_0*/M <= 0.70
- Incompressibility K: generally small values compared to QHD, with specific values shown as functions of alpha for different beta choices
- Scalar meson mass m_s: varies with alpha and beta; for beta=alpha the values are much smaller than for other choices, with the ZM and ZM3 results marked at specific points
- Vector-scalar potential difference V-S: typical values giving reasonable spin-orbit splitting lie in the region 550 to 650 MeV, achievable for alpha=beta and beta=1

## Context

This work extends the linear sigma model of Gell-Mann and Levy (1960) by introducing density-dependent coupling constants, motivated by the quark-meson coupling model of Guichon (1988) and the chiral confining model of Banerjee (1992). It shows that in mean-field approximation, the model can reproduce the Walecka model (QHD), the Zimanyi-Moszkowski (ZM) model, and the ZM3 model for specific choices of the parameters alpha and beta, unifying these approaches under a chiral framework. The results enable future calculations for finite nuclei, which the authors state are under way.
