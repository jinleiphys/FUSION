# Delta isobar masses, large N_c relations, and the quark model

> Motivated by recent remarks on the Delta+ mass and comparisons between the quark model and relations based on large-N_c with perturbative flavor breaking, two sets of Delta masses consistent with these constraints are constructed. These two sets, based either on an experimentally determined mass splitting or a quark model of isospin symmetry breaking, are shown to be inconsistent. The model dependence of this inconsistency is examined, and suggestions for improved experiments are made. An explicit quark model calculation and mass relations based on the large-N_c limit with perturbative flavor breaking are compared. The expected level of accuracy of such relations is realized in the quark model, except for mass relations spanning more than one SU(6) representation. It is shown that the Delta0 and Delta++ pole masses and Delta0 - Delta+ = (Delta- - Delta++)/3 about 1.5 MeV are more consistent with model expectations than the analogous Breit-Wigner masses and their splittings.

# Delta isobar masses, large N_c relations, and the quark model

## Key claim
Two different constructions of Delta isobar masses, both based on the well-determined Delta0 and Delta++ masses and a large-N_c relation expected to be accurate to order 10^{-3} MeV, are shown to be inconsistent when Breit-Wigner masses are used; the inconsistency is resolved if pole masses are adopted instead, with Delta0 - Delta+ = (Delta- - Delta++)/3 approximately 1.5 MeV being more consistent with model expectations than the analogous Breit-Wigner mass splittings.

## Method
The authors construct two sets of Delta masses using the large-N_c relation Delta3 = Delta++ - 3Delta+ + 3Delta0 - Delta- = 0 as a common constraint, combined with either (1) the experimentally extracted linear combination D = Delta- - Delta++ + (Delta0 - Delta+)/3 from Pedroni et al. (1978), or (2) the large-N_c relation Delta2 = 2 Sigma*2 together with a quark model estimate of Sigma*2 - Sigma2 = 0.074 MeV from a dynamical quark model that includes SU(6) symmetry breaking and non-spectator effects. The quark model uses a smeared contact interaction with sigma_{ij} = 0.9 GeV for light quark pairs, delta m = m_d - m_u = 3.6 MeV, and gamma_{em} = 1.0 GeV, fitted to the baryon spectrum, nucleon form factors, and measured isospin splittings.

## Key numbers
- Delta0 - Delta+ = 1.38 +/- 0.06 MeV and Delta- - Delta++ = 4.14 +/- 0.18 MeV from the Pedroni et al. D value combined with Delta3 = 0
- Delta0 Breit-Wigner mass = 1233.6 +/- 0.5 MeV; Delta++ Breit-Wigner mass = 1230.9 +/- 0.3 MeV
- Delta+ = 1232.2 +/- 0.5 MeV and Delta- = 1235.0 +/- 0.35 MeV from the Breit-Wigner masses combined with the Pedroni et al. constraint
- Delta2 = 3.57 +/- 0.36 MeV from the quark model estimate, using Sigma2 = 1.71 +/- 0.18 MeV from data plus Sigma*2 - Sigma2 = 0.074 MeV
- Delta0 - Delta+ = 2.2 +/- 0.3 MeV when using the quark model Delta2 value with Breit-Wigner Delta0 and Delta++ masses
- Quark model values: n-p = 1.3 MeV, D = 4.9 MeV, Delta2 = 3.5 MeV, Delta3 = 0.002 MeV, Delta2 - 2 Sigma*2 = -0.082 MeV
- Pole mass splitting Delta0 - Delta++ approximately 1 MeV
- Delta0 - Delta+ approximately 1.5 MeV and Delta- - Delta++ approximately 4.5 MeV from pole masses combined with the quark model Delta2

## Context
This work extends comparisons between large-N_c mass relations from Jenkins and Lebed (1995) and quark model predictions by Rosner (1998) and Cutkosky (1993), using a dynamical quark model that allows SU(6) symmetry breaking and non-spectator effects. The study finds that the expected accuracy of large-N_c relations is realized in the quark model except for relations spanning more than one SU(6) representation, such as Delta1 - 3 Sigma*1 - 4 Xi*1 = 0 which is violated by 6.1 MeV. The results enable improved experimental determinations of Delta+, Sigma*, and Xi* masses to sharpen comparisons between large-N_c and quark model predictions, and suggest that pole masses rather than Breit-Wigner masses should be used in mass relations.
