# Mean-field approach to nuclear structure with semi-realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions

> Semi-realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions applicable to the self-consistent mean-field (both Hartree-Fock and Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov) calculations are developed, by modifying the M3Y interaction. The modification is made so as to reproduce binding energies and rms matter radii of doubly magic nuclei, single-particle levels in $^{208}$Pb, and even-odd mass differences of the Sn isotopes. We find parameter-sets with and without the tensor force. The new interactions are further checked by the saturation properties of the uniform nuclear matter including the Landau-Migdal parameters. By the mean-field calculations, interaction-dependence of the neutron drip line is investigated for the O, Ca and Ni isotopes, and of the single-particle energies for the N=16, 32, 50, 82 and Z=50 nuclei. Results of the semi-realistic interactions including the tensor force are in fair agreement with available experimental data for all of these properties.

## Key claim

Semi-realistic M3Y-type interactions can be developed that simultaneously reproduce binding energies, radii, single-particle levels, and pairing properties within the mean-field framework, but the tensor force included in the M3Y-P3 and M3Y-P5 parameter sets narrows the shell gaps in $^{40}$Ca compared to tensor-free interactions, though without collapsing them as seen with zero-range tensor forces in prior work.

## Method

The author modifies the M3Y-Paris interaction by adding a density-dependent contact term and adjusting strength parameters in the central, spin-orbit, and tensor channels to fit binding energies and rms matter radii of doubly magic nuclei, single-particle levels in $^{208}$Pb, and even-odd mass differences of Sn isotopes. Hartree-Fock (HF) and Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations are performed using a Gaussian expansion method basis that is applicable across the nuclear mass table without nuclide-specific parameters.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density $k_{\mathrm{F}0} = 1.340$ fm$^{-1}$ for M3Y-P3, P4, and P5 (compared to $1.342$ fm$^{-1}$ for D1S and $1.340$ fm$^{-1}$ for M3Y-P2)
- Saturation energy $\mathcal{E}_0 = -16.51$ MeV (M3Y-P3), $-16.13$ MeV (M3Y-P4), $-16.12$ MeV (M3Y-P5) compared to $-16.01$ MeV (D1S) and $-16.14$ MeV (M3Y-P2)
- Incompressibility $\mathcal{K} = 245.8$ MeV (M3Y-P3), $235.3$ MeV (M3Y-P4), $235.6$ MeV (M3Y-P5) compared to $202.9$ MeV (D1S) and $220.4$ MeV (M3Y-P2)
- Volume symmetry energy $a_t = 29.75$ MeV (M3Y-P3), $28.71$ MeV (M3Y-P4), $29.59$ MeV (M3Y-P5) compared to $31.12$ MeV (D1S) and $30.61$ MeV (M3Y-P2)
- Binding energy of $^{208}$Pb: $-1635.2$ MeV (M3Y-P3), $-1632.1$ MeV (M3Y-P4), $-1633.2$ MeV (M3Y-P5) compared to experimental $-1636.4$ MeV
- Binding energy of $^{16}$O: $-118.6$ MeV (M3Y-P3), $-126.3$ MeV (M3Y-P4), $-126.1$ MeV (M3Y-P5) compared to experimental $-127.6$ MeV
- Proton shell gap in $^{40}$Ca: $5.2$ MeV for M3Y-P5 (with tensor force) compared to experimental values
- Neutron shell gap in $^{40}$Ca: $7.7$ MeV for M3Y-P5 (with tensor force)

## Context

This work extends the author's prior development of M3Y-type semi-realistic interactions (Nakada 2003, 2004) by incorporating pairing properties that were not accounted for in the M3Y-P2 parameter set, and by systematically investigating the role of the tensor force. The new interactions (M3Y-P3, P4, P5) are compared with the Gogny D1S interaction (Berger et al.) and with the earlier M3Y-P2 set. The results enable future mean-field studies of neutron-rich nuclei, including investigation of the neutron drip line for O, Ca, and Ni isotopes, and assessment of shell evolution at magic numbers N=16, 32, 50, 82 and Z=50.
