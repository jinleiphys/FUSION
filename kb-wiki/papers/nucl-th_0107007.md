# Pionic Modes Studied by Quasielastic (\vec{p}, \vec{n}) Reactions

> It has long been expected that the pionic modes show some collective phenomena such as the pion condensation in the high density nuclear matter and its precursor phenomena in the ordinary nuclei. Here we show an evidence of the precursor observed in the isovector spin longitudinal cross sections ID_q of the quasielastic 12C, 40Ca (\vec{p}, \vec{n}) reactions at T_p = 346 and 494MeV with the momentum transfer q = 1.7fm-1. Another aim of this report is to evaluate the Landau-Migdal parameters g'_{NN}, g'_{NΔ} and g'_{ΔΔ} at the large momentum region from the above reactions. We obtained g'_{NN} \approx 0.6-0.7, g'_{NΔ} \approx 0.3-0.4. The results are consistent with those at the small momentum region, which are obtained from the Gamov-Teller strength distribution.

# Pionic Modes Studied by Quasielastic (p, n) Reactions

## Key claim

The authors report the first experimental evidence of precursor phenomena of pion condensation, observed as an enhancement of the isovector spin longitudinal cross section ID_q in quasielastic (p, n) reactions on 12C and 40Ca. However, the authors caution that the observed spin transverse response functions from (p, n) are much larger than those from (e, e') measurements, and this contradiction must be resolved before reaching a definite conclusion.

## Method

The authors analyzed quasielastic (p, n) reactions on 12C and 40Ca using a distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) combined with continuum random phase approximation (RPA) in angular momentum representation, including Delta-hole configurations. They employed the pi + rho + g' model interaction with three independent Landau-Migdal parameters (g'_NN, g'_NΔ, g'_ΔΔ), removing the universality ansatz that sets all three equal. The spin longitudinal cross sections ID_q were extracted from polarization transfer coefficients measured with polarized proton beams and neutron polarimeters.

## Key numbers

- Proton beam energies: T_p = 346 MeV and 494 MeV, with momentum transfer q = 1.7 fm^-1 for energy transfers ω < 120 MeV
- Landau-Migdal parameters extracted at q = 1.7 fm^-1: g'_NN ≈ 0.6-0.7, g'_NΔ ≈ 0.3-0.4
- Effective mass at nuclear center: m*(0) = 0.7 m (with m the free nucleon mass)
- g'_ΔΔ fixed at 0.5, with the authors noting its dependence is very weak
- For comparison, Suzuki and Sakai (1999) obtained at q ≈ 0: 0.58 < g'_NN < 0.59, 0.18 < g'_NΔ < 0.23 (if g'_ΔΔ ≤ 1.0)

## Context

This work extends the estimation of Landau-Migdal parameters from the small momentum region (q ≈ 0), where they were determined from Gamow-Teller strength distributions by Suzuki and Sakai (1999) and Arima et al. (2001), to the large momentum region q = 1.7 fm^-1. The results show that the pi + rho + g' model with constant g' parameters works well across a wide range of q, implying that previous calculations of the critical density for pion condensation based on the universality ansatz (g'_NN = g'_NΔ = g'_ΔΔ) should be revisited, with potential implications for neutron star equations of state and cooling scenarios.
