# Comparaison between Coulomb and Hulthèn potentials within Bohr Hamiltonian for $γ$-rigid nuclei in the presence of minimal length

> In this work we solve the Schrödinger equation for Bohr Hamiltonian with Coulomb and Hulthén potentials within the formalism of minimal length in order to obtain analytical expressions for the energy eigenvalues and eigenfunctions by means of asymptotic iteration method. The obtained formulas of the energy spectrum and wave functions, are used to calculate excitation energies and transition rates of $γ$-rigid nuclei and compared with the experimental data at the shape phase critical point X(3) in nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for γ-rigid nuclei at the X(3) critical point, the Bohr Hamiltonian with a Hulthén potential, solved within the minimal length formalism, reproduces experimental excitation energies and B(E2) transition rates better than the Coulomb potential does, but only for a specific set of fitted parameters and for certain nuclei.

## Method

The authors solve the deformed Schrödinger equation for the Bohr Hamiltonian, including a minimal length correction to first order in the parameter α, with both Coulomb and Hulthén potentials. They use the asymptotic iteration method (AIM) to obtain analytical expressions for energy eigenvalues and eigenfunctions. These formulas are then used to calculate excitation energies and B(E2) transition rates for a set of even-even γ-rigid nuclei, and the results are compared to experimental data, with the free parameters (α, c for Coulomb; α, δ for Hulthén) fitted to the energy of the first excited 2⁺ state.

## Key numbers

- For the Hulthén potential, the best-fit minimal length parameter α is 0.00003 for most nuclei (e.g., ¹⁰⁴Ru, ¹²⁰Xe, ¹⁴⁸Nd), with δ = 0.003, yielding root-mean-square deviations σ for energy ratios ranging from 0.16 (¹⁹⁰Hg) to 0.70 (¹²⁰Xe).
- For the Coulomb potential, the best-fit parameters are typically α = 0.58 and c = -1.31, yielding σ values for energy ratios ranging from 0.66 (¹⁹⁰Hg) to 1.53 (¹²²Xe).
- For B(E2) transition rates in ¹⁰⁸Ru, the Hulthén potential gives σ = 0.05, while the Coulomb potential gives σ = 0.96.
- The energy ratio R₀,₄ (E(4⁺)/E(2⁺)) for ¹⁰⁴Ru is 2.48 experimentally; the Hulthén model gives 2.82 and the Coulomb model gives 4.121.

## Context

This work extends the X(3) model of Bonatsos (2005), which describes γ-rigid nuclei at the critical point of the transition from vibratory to axially symmetric shapes, by introducing a minimal length formalism previously applied to the infinite square well and other potentials. The paper compares two potentials (Coulomb and Hulthén) within this framework, finding that the Hulthén potential yields better agreement with experimental data for several candidate nuclei, including a newly predicted candidate, ¹⁹⁰Hg. This enables future studies of other potentials or the inclusion of the minimal length in more complex nuclear models.
