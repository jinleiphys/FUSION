# Leading-order calculation of hadronic contributions to the muon $g-2$ using the Dyson-Schwinger approach

> We present a calculation of the hadronic vacuum polarization (HVP) tensor within the framework of Dyson--Schwinger equations. To this end we use a well-established phenomenological model for the quark-gluon interaction with parameters fixed to reproduce hadronic observables. From the HVP tensor we compute both the Adler function and the HVP contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon, $a_μ$. We find $a_μ^{HVP}= 6760\times 10^{-11}$ which deviates about two percent from the value extracted from experiment. Additionally, we make comparison with a recent lattice determination of $a_μ^{HVP}$ and find good agreement within our approach. We also discuss the implications of our result for a corresponding calculation of the hadronic light-by-light scattering contribution to $a_μ$.

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger approach, using a phenomenological model for the quark-gluon interaction with parameters fixed to hadronic observables, yields a value for the hadronic vacuum polarization contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment that deviates by about two percent from the value extracted from experiment, but only when the quark masses are tuned to the vector meson sector rather than the pseudoscalar sector.

## Method

The authors solved the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator and the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the quark-photon vertex in a rainbow-ladder truncation, using the Maris-Tandy model for the quark-gluon interaction. From these fully dressed Green's functions, they computed the hadronic vacuum polarization tensor for five quark flavors (u, d, s, c, b) in the isospin symmetric limit, and then evaluated the Adler function and the leading-order HVP contribution to the muon g-2 via the standard integral formula.

## Key numbers

*   $a_{\mu}^{HVP} = 6760 \times 10^{-11}$ for parameter set II (quark masses tuned to vector meson sector), compared to the experimental extraction of $6903.0(52.6) \times 10^{-11}$.
*   $a_{\mu}^{HVP} = 7440 \times 10^{-11}$ for parameter set I (quark masses tuned to pseudoscalar meson sector), which is about eight percent above the experimental value.
*   The transverse (vector meson pole) part of the quark-photon vertex contributes $6160 \times 10^{-11}$ (set I) or $5640 \times 10^{-11}$ (set II) to $a_{\mu}^{HVP}$, while the Ball-Chiu (gauge) part contributes $1280 \times 10^{-11}$ (set I) or $1120 \times 10^{-11}$ (set II).
*   The bare vertex (no dressing) contributes only $760 \times 10^{-11}$ (set I) or $720 \times 10^{-11}$ (set II).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Dyson-Schwinger calculation of the hadronic light-by-light scattering contribution to $a_{\mu}$ (Fischer 2010, Goecke 2010) by providing a validation of the same truncation scheme on the hadronic vacuum polarization. The results agree well with dispersion-relation-based extractions from experiment (Jegerlehner 2009) and with lattice determinations from the ETMC collaboration (Feng 2011). The paper also addresses and disagrees with arguments made by Boughezal (2011) that vertex dressing effects should be small, demonstrating that non-perturbative vector meson pole contributions are essential and constitute roughly 80% of the HVP result.
