# Short-range three-nucleon forces and low-energy constants

> The 3N forces due to pi-rho, pi-sigma and pi-omega exchanges following from the nucleon Born diagrams and diagrams with an intermediate N^{*}(1440) are re-analyzed. The cancellation between pi-sigma and pi-omega forces is rather sensitive to the values of the coupling constants and to the form of the piNN vertex. Experimental uncertainties in the parameters of the TM pi-pi potential are assessed. They lead to uncertainties in theoretical predictions of the triton binding energy of about 0.4 MeV. The low-energy limit of pi-sigma and pi-omega potentials is performed. It defines the coupling constants of effective contact piNNNN vertices, which are compared with the corresponding contact vertices of Chiral Perturbation Theory.

## Key claim

The paper re-analyzes three-nucleon (3N) forces from pi-rho, pi-sigma, and pi-omega exchanges, finding that the cancellation between pi-sigma and pi-omega forces is sensitive to the values of coupling constants and the form of the piNN vertex, and that experimental uncertainties in the parameters of the Tucson-Melbourne (TM) pi-pi potential lead to uncertainties in theoretical predictions of the triton binding energy of about 0.4 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed Faddeev calculations for the trinucleon system including the TM pi-pi 3N force (in its modified TM'(99) version) together with pi-rho, pi-sigma, and pi-omega exchange 3N forces derived from nucleon Born diagrams and diagrams with an intermediate N*(1440) Roper resonance. They computed partial derivatives of the triton binding energy with respect to the TM force parameters a', b, and d for four different NN potentials (Reid, Paris, Nijmegen 93, Bonn B), then propagated experimental uncertainties from the underlying piN scattering data (sigma term, coupling constants, amplitudes) through the error propagation formula to estimate the resulting uncertainty in the binding energy. They also performed the low-energy limit of the pi-sigma and pi-omega potentials to extract effective contact piNNNN vertex constants and compared these with chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) constants.

## Key numbers

- The uncertainty in the triton binding energy from experimental uncertainties in TM pi-pi force parameters is about 0.4 MeV, with values of Delta E_t = 0.377 MeV (Reid), 0.389 MeV (Paris), 0.440 MeV (Nijmegen 93), and 0.393 MeV (Bonn B)
- The approximate uncertainty formula Delta E_t^app = 2 (partial E_t / partial b) Delta x gives values of 0.362 MeV (Reid), 0.372 MeV (Paris), 0.423 MeV (Nijmegen 93), and 0.375 MeV (Bonn B), confirming the pion-nucleon sigma term uncertainty dominates
- The partial derivatives of triton binding energy with respect to TM force parameters for the Nijmegen 93 potential are: partial E_t / partial a' = 0.095, partial E_t / partial b = 0.845, partial E_t / partial d = 0.540
- The TM'(99) force parameters at Lambda_piNN = 5.8 m_pi are: m_pi a' = -1.127, m_pi^3 b = -2.801, m_pi^3 d = -0.754
- The coefficient a' varies with cut-off from -1.203 (Lambda_piNN = 4.1 m_pi) to -1.101 (Lambda_piNN = 7.1 m_pi)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of meson-exchange 3N forces by the Tucson-Melbourne group (TM, 1980s) and the Brazilian group (Robillotta, 1980s), specifically addressing the chiral consistency issues raised by Friar and van Kolck (1999) who showed the TM force contained a spurious c-term. The paper complements studies by Coon and Han (1999) who used less realistic NN interactions and variational methods, and provides the first exact Faddeev calculations including pi-sigma and pi-omega exchange 3N forces with intermediate Roper resonance as proposed by CPR (1990s). The results enable comparison of phenomenological meson-exchange 3N forces with the NLO chiral 3N forces of Epelbaum et al. (2002) and Friar and van Kolck, and provide theoretical error bars for triton binding energy predictions that are needed for interpreting the Ay puzzle in nd scattering.
