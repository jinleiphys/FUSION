# $p Ω$ femtoscopy using baryon-baryon effective potentials

> We have generated an updated version of the $pΩ$ potential for low-energy interactions based on an effective field theory approach at leading order. This potential, together with other potentials based either on different parametrizations or lattice QCD calculations, have been used to solve the Schrödinger equation numerically, obtaining the scattering wave functions for different values of the relative momentum. Using these wave functions, we have computed the $p Ω$ femtoscopic correlation functions, comparing the results with those published by the ALICE collaboration.

## Key claim
The authors generate an updated leading-order effective field theory potential for the pΩ system and show that their best-fit "Free FF" parametrization can describe the ALICE femtoscopic correlation data, though it slightly overestimates the low-momentum region and underestimates around k = 150 MeV due to bound-state effects not seen in the experimental data.

## Method
The authors construct a pΩ potential at leading order in baryon chiral perturbation theory, including contact terms and one-meson-exchange diagrams for η and σ mesons, with Gaussian regularization and form factors. They fit the free parameters to the lattice QCD ⁵S₂ potential from HAL QCD (Ref. [HALQCD]), then solve the Schrödinger equation numerically to obtain scattering wave functions for different relative momenta. Using these wave functions, they compute the femtoscopic correlation function via the Koonin-Pratt formula, assuming only the L=0 partial wave contributes to the strong interaction, and compare with ALICE data.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy in the ⁵S₂ channel from the "Free FF" fit: E = -2.13 MeV
- Binding energy in the ³S₁ channel from the "Free FF" fit: E = -0.48 MeV
- Gaussian source radius used for correlation functions: r₀ = 0.95 fm
- Coupling constants from baryon χPT: C_{pηp̄} ≈ 2.17 × 10⁻³ MeV⁻¹, C_{ΩηΩ̄} ≈ 2.36 × 10⁻² MeV⁻¹
- Coupling constant from Walecka-Serot model: C_{pσp̄} ≈ 8.71
- Best-fit form factor cutoffs: Λ_η = 917.4(7) MeV, Λ_σ = 622(6) MeV

## Context
This work extends the baryon-baryon effective field theory approach of Weinberg (1979) and the earlier strangeness S=-1,-2 work by Florit and collaborators (Martí), applying it to the pΩ system. The results are compared with the lattice QCD potential from HAL QCD (Ref. [HALQCD]) and the experimental correlation data from the ALICE collaboration (Ref. [ALICE]). The authors' best fit slightly improves over the HAL QCD results when compared with ALICE data, and the inclusion of the ³S₁ channel (absent in the HAL QCD calculation) leads to overestimation at low momentum, suggesting possible negative contributions from inelastic channels that warrant future study.
