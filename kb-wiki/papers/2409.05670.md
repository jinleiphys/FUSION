# Emergent chirality and superfluidity of parity-doubled baryons in neutron stars

> We propose novel superfluids induced by the parity-doubled baryons. The parity-doubled baryons, i.e., a nucleon $N(940)$ with spin-parity $J^{P}=1/2^{+}$ and an excited nucleon $N^{\ast}(1535)$ with $J^{P}=1/2^{-}$ in vacuum, become degenerate at sufficiently high density where the chiral symmetry is restored. In this study, we extend the conventional $\mathrm{U}(1)$ chiral symmetry to the higher dimensional symmetries, dubbed emergent chiral symmetries, including the naive and mirror assignments as their subgroups. Starting with the Lagrangian up to four-point interactions among the neutron $n$ and its chiral partner $n^{\ast}$, neutral components in $N$ and $N^{\ast}$, in pure neutron matter, we investigate the properties of the ground state with a pairing gap generated by the $n$ and $n^{\ast}$ in the mean-field approximation. We find vector-type condensates that induce the dynamical breaking of a new class of internal symmetries, emergent chiral symmetries, as well as the baryon number and the rotational symmetries of the real space, indicating the appearance of massless Nambu-Goldstone bosons consisting of six quarks: emergent pions, superfluid phonons, and magnons, respectively. We also study the fermionic excitation modes at low-energy scales, and show that they exhibit a spatial anisotropy of the propagation at the Dirac cone in momentum space. Some phenomenological implications are advocated, shedding new light on the properties of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The authors propose that at sufficiently high baryon density inside neutron stars, where chiral symmetry is restored, parity-doubled neutrons (the neutron \(n\) and its chiral partner \(n^{\ast}\)) can form novel superfluids characterized by vector-type condensates that break emergent chiral symmetries, baryon number, and rotational symmetry, producing massless Nambu-Goldstone bosons made of six quarks. The claim is conditional on the existence of densities high enough for chiral symmetry restoration while the matter remains hadronic, which the authors estimate may occur at baryon densities above those of conventional \(^{3}P_{2}\) neutron superfluids (up to \(3n_0\) or a few more).

## Method

The authors constructed a Lagrangian with four-point interactions among the neutron \(n\) and its chiral partner \(n^{\ast}\) (neutral components of \(N(940)\) and \(N^{\ast}(1535)\)) in pure neutron matter, extending the conventional U(1) chiral symmetry to higher-dimensional emergent chiral symmetries: \(\mathrm{U}(1)_{(1-2)\mathrm{L}} \times \mathrm{U}(1)_{(1-2)\mathrm{R}}\) and \(\mathrm{SU}(2)_{\mathrm{L}} \times \mathrm{SU}(2)_{\mathrm{R}}\). They applied the mean-field approximation using the Nambu-Gor'kov formalism to study the ground state with pairing gaps generated by \(nn\), \(nn^{\ast}\), and \(n^{\ast}n^{\ast}\) Cooper pairs, and analyzed the resulting fermionic excitation modes.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon \(N(940)\) has mass 940 MeV and spin-parity \(J^{P}=1/2^{+}\); the excited nucleon \(N^{\ast}(1535)\) has mass 1535 MeV and spin-parity \(J^{P}=1/2^{-}\) in vacuum.
- Normal nuclear matter density is \(n_{0}=0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\); the baryon number density of hadronic matter could reach maximally \(3\)-\(5n_{0}\).
- Neutron \(^{3}P_{2}\) pairings can exist at baryon number densities up to \(3n_{0}\) or a few more, based on prior work by Tamagaki (1970) and Takatsuka (1971, 1972).

## Context

This work extends the parity-doublet model of Detar and Kunihiro (1989) and Jido et al. (2001) to include superfluidity, going beyond previous applications of the parity-doublet model to neutron star matter by Marczenko et al. (2018-2022) and Minamikawa et al. (2020-2023), which used relativistic mean-field models with \(\sigma\) and \(\omega\) mesons. The proposed emergent chiral superfluids are expected to exist in deeper interior regions of neutron stars than the conventional neutron \(^{1}S_{0}\) and \(^{3}P_{2}\) superfluids studied extensively since Migdal (1960) and Tamagaki (1970), and may provide new phenomenological implications for neutron star properties.
