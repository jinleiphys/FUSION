# Event-shape dependence of symmetry plane correlations using the Gaussian estimator in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC using a multiphase transport model

> The study of symmetry plane correlations (SPCs) can be useful in characterizing the direction of the anisotropic emission of produced particles in the final state. The study of SPCs provides an independent method to understand the transport properties of the system formed in heavy-ion collisions. Similar to anisotropic flow coefficients, which are largely influenced by the initial spatial anisotropy, SPCs also depend upon the participant plane correlations measured using the participating nucleons of the collision overlap region. In this paper, SPCs have been studied in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV using the event generator AMPT. In addition to their behaviour with the changing centrality of the collision, their event shape dependence has also been studied for the first time, using the event shape classifier transverse spherocity. The Gaussian estimator has been used to evaluate the correlations, and these have been compared to the participant plane correlations defined in an analogous way to the symmetry plane correlations, and a qualitative match has been found between them. These event-shape differentiated symmetry plane correlations can be used to deduce the presence of higher-order anisotropies in the initial energy distribution, thus giving insight into the initial geometry of the colliding system, among other applications like model development and model testing using Bayesian analyses.

## Key claim

The paper claims that event-shape-differentiated symmetry plane correlations (SPCs) evaluated using the Gaussian estimator in AMPT simulations of Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02\) TeV can reveal the presence of higher-order anisotropies in the initial energy distribution, but the results are limited to qualitative agreement with experimental data and the model systematically underestimates the measured SPC values.

## Method

The authors generated 210,000 minimum-bias Pb-Pb collision events at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02\) TeV using the string-melting version of the AMPT event generator with Lund parameters \(a=0.30\) and \(b=0.15\) and parton screening mass 1.2408 fm\(^{-1}\). Events were classified by centrality using impact parameter from the Glauber Monte Carlo scheme and further subdivided into event shape classes (low-\(S_0\) pencil-like, high-\(S_0\) isotropic, and \(S_0\)-integrated) using transverse spherocity. Symmetry plane correlations were computed using the Gaussian estimator formula, which removes bias from flow coefficient magnitudes, and were compared to participant plane correlations calculated from the initial-state participant nucleon positions.

## Key numbers

* 210,000 minimum-bias Pb-Pb collision events were generated at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02\) TeV using AMPT
* Transverse momentum cuts for spherocity evaluation: \(p_{\rm T} > 0.15\) GeV/c; for SPC evaluation: \(0.2 < p_{\rm T} < 5.0\) GeV/c with pseudorapidity range \(|\eta| < 0.8\)
* Low-\(S_0\) (pencil-like) events in 0-5% centrality: spherocity range 0-0.897; high-\(S_0\) (isotropic) events: 0.958-1
* Low-\(S_0\) events in 50-60% centrality: spherocity range 0-0.718; high-\(S_0\) events: 0.876-1
* \(\langle\cos[4(\psi_4-\psi_2)]\rangle_{\rm GE}\) shows the strongest correlation among two-plane SPCs, with values decreasing from mid-central toward peripheral collisions
* \(\langle\cos[6(\psi_3-\psi_2)]\rangle_{\rm GE}\) is consistent with zero within uncertainties across all centralities
* \(\langle\cos(2\psi_2+3\psi_3-5\psi_5)\rangle_{\rm GE}\) shows the strongest correlation among three-plane SPCs, while \(\langle\cos(8\psi_2-3\psi_3-5\psi_5)\rangle_{\rm GE}\) has the weakest
* \(\langle\cos(2\psi_2+3\psi_3-6\psi_6)\rangle_{\rm GE}\) has a negative sign, unlike other correlators which are positive or consistent with zero

## Context

This work extends previous studies of symmetry plane correlations in heavy-ion collisions by introducing event-shape dependence via transverse spherocity, which had not been done before. The authors compare their AMPT results to experimental measurements from ALICE (2023, 2024) and to hydrodynamic model calculations including T\(_R\)ENTO, EKRT+hydrodynamics (Niemi 2012, Hirvonen 2022), and T\(_R\)ENTO+iEBE-VISHNU (Bass 1998, Bleicher 1999, Song 2007, Shen 2014). The study finds that AMPT qualitatively reproduces experimental SPC trends but systematically underestimates values, while hydrodynamic models overestimate them, suggesting SPCs provide strong constraints on model details. The event-shape-differentiated SPCs enable future Bayesian analyses for model development and testing, and can probe whether correlations like \(\langle\cos[6(\psi_3-\psi_2)]\rangle\) that appear zero in inclusive measurements have non-zero values in specific event shape classes.
