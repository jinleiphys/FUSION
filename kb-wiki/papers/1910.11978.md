# Theoretical analysis of $^8$Li + $^{208}$Pb reaction and the critical angular momentum for complete fusion

> In a theoretical approach, the complete and incomplete fusions are investigated by considering the $^8$Li+$^{208}$Pb reaction. By decreasing the projectile ground-state binding energy $\varepsilon_b$ from its known experimental value, the complete fusion is shown to have insignificant dependence on such variations, whereas the incomplete fusion strongly depends on that. The complete and incomplete fusion cross sections are calculated by using a combination of both continuum-discretized coupled-channel and sum-rule models. To this end, an incident-energy dependent cut-off angular momentum $L_c$ is first obtained by using the available complete fusion experimental data, within an approach which is extended to model results obtained for other incident-energies. An approximated fitted expression linking $L_c$ to the well-known critical value $L_{\rm crit}$ derived by Wilczyński [Nucl. Phys. A 216 (1973) 386] suggests a generalization of the corresponding sum-rule model to energies around and below the Coulomb barrier.

## Key claim

The complete fusion cross section for the $^8$Li + $^{208}$Pb reaction shows insignificant dependence on variations of the projectile ground-state binding energy below its experimental value, whereas the incomplete fusion cross section strongly depends on such variations; this supports the conclusion that weak binding energy alone is not sufficient to explain complete fusion suppression.

## Method

The authors combined the Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channel (CDCC) method with the sum-rule model of Wilczynski (1973). They first calculated total fusion cross sections via CDCC, then used experimental complete fusion data to determine an energy-dependent cutoff angular momentum $L_c$, summing partial total fusion cross sections from $L=0$ up to $L_c$ to obtain complete fusion, with the remainder assigned to incomplete fusion. The projectile ground-state binding energy was artificially varied from the experimental value of 2.033 MeV down to 0.5 MeV by adjusting the depth of the Woods-Saxon potential.

## Key numbers

* Experimental ground-state binding energy of $^8$Li: $\varepsilon_b = 2.033$ MeV; three additional values considered: 1.5, 1.0, and 0.5 MeV.
* Cutoff angular momenta $L_c$ (in units of $\hbar$) for incident energies from 32.10 to 37.50 MeV: 9, 11, 12, 13, 14, 15, and 16, respectively (Table 1).
* For $E_{\rm c.m.}=37.5$ MeV, $L_c$ represents about 3% of $L_{\rm max}=500$.
* Critical angular momentum from the sum-rule model: $L_{\rm crit} \approx 41$ for the $^8$Li + $^{208}$Pb reaction.
* Coulomb barrier $V_B = 28.65$ MeV from the Sao Paulo Potential.
* At $E_{\rm c.m.}=37.5$ MeV with $\varepsilon_b=2.03$ MeV, complete fusion accounts for over 60% of total fusion, representing a suppression of about 35%; at $\varepsilon_b=0.50$ MeV, this drops to about 25% (suppression of about 75%).

## Context

This work extends the sum-rule model of Wilczynski (1973), which previously applied only to energies well above the Coulomb barrier with energy-independent $L_{\rm crit}$, to energies around and below the Coulomb barrier by introducing an energy-dependent cutoff $L_c$. The results provide theoretical support for the experimental conclusions of Cook (2020, 2021) on $^7$Li and $^8$Li reactions with $^{209}$Bi, which suggested charge clustering rather than weak binding as the crucial factor in complete fusion suppression. The study also parallels the artificial binding-energy variation approach of Lei (2019) for $^{6,7}$Li projectiles, confirming that $^8$Li complete fusion behaves similarly to $^7$Li in showing insignificant dependence on binding energy variations.
