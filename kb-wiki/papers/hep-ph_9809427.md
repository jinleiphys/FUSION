# Fock Space Distributions, Structure Functions, Higher Twists and Small x

> We compute quark structure functions and the intrinsic Fock space distribution of sea quarks in a hadron wavefunction at small x. The computation is performed in an effective theory at small x where the gluon field is treated classically. At $Q^2$ large compared to an intrinsic scale associated with the density of gluons $μ^2$, large compared to the QCD scale $Λ^2_{QCD}$, and large compared to the quark mass squared $M^2$, the Fock space distribution of quarks is identical to the distribution function measured in deep inelastic scattering. For $Q^2 \le M^2$ but $Q^2 >> μ^2$, the quark distribution is computed in terms of the gluon distribution function and explicit expressions are obtained. For $Q^2 \le μ^2$ but $Q^2 >> Λ_{QCD}^2$ we obtain formal expressions for the quark distribution functions in terms of the glue. An evaluation of these requires a renormalization group analysis of the gluon distribution function in the regime of high parton density. For light quarks at high $Q^2$, the DGLAP flavor singlet evolution equations for the parton distributions are recovered. Explicit expressions are given for heavy quark structure functions at small x.

## Key claim

The authors compute quark structure functions and intrinsic Fock space distributions of sea quarks at small x using an effective theory where the gluon field is treated classically, showing that these two quantities are identical only at high Q² (large compared to the gluon density scale μ², the QCD scale Λ²_QCD, and the quark mass squared M²), while they differ for massive quarks when Q² ≤ M² even if Q² >> Λ²_QCD.

## Method

The computation uses an effective field theory approach to QCD at small x, where the gluon field is treated as a classical background field obtained from the saddle point solution of the Yang-Mills equations in the presence of a color charge density source ρ. The fermion propagator is solved exactly in this classical background field by solving the Dirac equation across a discontinuity at x⁻=0, and the sea quark distribution function is expressed as an integral over the trace of the fermion propagator. The color averaging over the source ρ is performed using a functional F[ρ] that satisfies a non-linear Wilson renormalization group equation, with a Gaussian form used when transverse momentum scales are large compared to α_S μ.

## Key numbers

- μ² = (1/σ) dN/dy, where σ is the hadronic cross section and y = y₀ - ln(1/x); for large nuclei A>>1, μ² = (1/(πR²))(N_q/(2N_c)) ~ A^(1/3)/6 fm⁻²
- For Q² >> μ², Q² >> Λ²_QCD, and Q² >> M², the Fock space distribution of quarks is identical to the distribution function measured in deep inelastic scattering
- For Q² ≤ M² but Q² >> μ², the quark distribution is computed in terms of the gluon distribution function with explicit expressions obtained
- For Q² ≤ μ² but Q² >> Λ²_QCD, formal expressions for quark distribution functions in terms of the glue are obtained, requiring a renormalization group analysis of the gluon distribution function in the high parton density regime

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to small x QCD developed by McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and collaborators (Jalilian-Marian, Kovner, Leonidov, Weigert), which is an alternative to the BFKL approach of Lipatov and collaborators. The method does not rely on a high twist expansion but uses the weak coupling nature of the theory at high gluon density, allowing non-trivial relations between parameters in a region where perturbation theory and leading order operator product expansion methods are not valid. This enables future computation of heavy quark structure functions at small x and connections to diffractive cross sections, with the formalism treating heavy quark evolution on the same footing as light quarks.
