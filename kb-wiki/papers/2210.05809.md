# Constraining Neutrinoless Double-Beta Decay Matrix Elements from Ab Initio Nuclear Theory

> As experimental searches for neutrinoless double-beta ($0νββ$) decay are entering a new generation, with hopes to completely probe the inverted mass hierarchy, the need for reliable nuclear matrix elements, which govern the rate of this decay, is stronger than ever. Since a large discrepancy in results is typically found with nuclear modela, a large unknown still exists on the sensitivity of these experiments to the effective neutrino mass. We consider this problem from a first-principles perspective, using the ab initio valence-space in medium similarity renormalization group. In particular, we study correlations of the $0νββ$-decay matrix elements in $^{76}$Ge with other observables, such as the double Gamow-Teller giant resonance, from 34 input chiral interactions in an attempt to constrain our uncertainties and investigate the interaction dependence of the nuclear matrix element.

# Constraining Neutrinoless Double-Beta Decay Matrix Elements from Ab Initio Nuclear Theory

## Key claim

The nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay in 76Ge show a large spread across 34 chiral interactions, indicating that the low-energy constants relevant for this process are not well constrained at next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory. However, the double Gamow-Teller transition could potentially help reduce this interaction uncertainty if observed, though the correlation is weaker for isospin-changing transitions relevant to experimental candidates.

## Method

The authors used the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) method to approximately solve the Schrodinger equation and obtain neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements from 34 different chiral effective field theory potentials based on Delta-full chiral EFT at N2LO with a cutoff of 394 MeV. The valence-space Hamiltonian was diagonalized using the shell-model code KSHELL, with operators evolved consistently and truncated at the normal-ordered two-body level [IMSRG(2)]. The authors studied correlations between the 0νββ-decay matrix elements and other observables including ground-state energies, Q-values, 2+ excitation energies, charge radius, single beta decay matrix elements, and double Gamow-Teller matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- 34 non-implausible sample interactions based on N2LO Delta-full chiral EFT with a cutoff of 394 MeV were used to probe the dependence of the 0νββ-decay nuclear matrix element on low-energy constants
- The contact operator coupling constant gNN can be estimated to an accuracy of approximately 30% when fitting to synthetic two-body amplitude data
- The e_max truncation limits states to those with e = 2n + l ≤ e_max, and 3N forces are truncated via e1 + e2 + e3 ≤ E3max
- The study focused on the transition 76Ge → 76Se

## Context

This work extends previous ab initio calculations of neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements for 48Ca (Yao 2020, Belley 2021, Novario 2021), 76Ge, and 82Se (Belley 2021) to include a systematic study of interaction uncertainty using 34 chiral interactions. It builds on the development of emulators based on eigenvector continuation (Frame 2018, Ekstrom 2019) and history matching (Hu 2021) to select non-implausible sets of low-energy constants. The correlation between 0νββ-decay matrix elements and double Gamow-Teller transitions, previously suggested by phenomenology (Shimizu 2018, Brase 2022), was found to be significantly weaker for isospin-changing transitions relevant to experimental candidates, contradicting earlier expectations. Recent advances (Miyagi 2022) have overcome the E3max truncation bottleneck, enabling first ab initio calculations for 130Te and 136Xe (Belley in preparation).
