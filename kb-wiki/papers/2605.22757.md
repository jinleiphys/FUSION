# Nuclear Rainbow of Core-Symmetric Systems

> The nearside-farside (NF) decomposition method developed originally by Fuller for elastic scattering of a nonidentical nucleus-nucleus system was generalized to study the nuclear rainbow pattern in a symmetric or core-symmetric dinuclear system. It has been shown that the projectile-target identity of an identical system implies a symmetric interchange of the nearside and farside components of elastic scattering amplitude around $θ_{\mathrm{c.m.}}=90^\circ$. A similar interchange appears also in a nonidentical core-symmetric system due to elastic transfer of cluster or nucleon between two identical cores. The analysis of the ${}^{12}\mathrm{C}+{}^{12}\mathrm{C}$, ${}^{16}\mathrm{O}+{}^{12}\mathrm{C}$, and ${}^{13}\mathrm{C}+{}^{12}\mathrm{C}$ systems shows how the generalized NF decomposition method reveals the nuclear rainbow pattern in these systems, which can be helpful in probing the real optical potential and nuclear clustering.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes Fuller's nearside-farside (NF) decomposition method to elastic scattering of identical and core-identical dinuclear systems, showing that the projectile-target identity implies a symmetric interchange of nearside and farside components around θ_c.m. = 90°, but the exact NF symmetry is broken in core-identical systems because the elastic scattering and elastic transfer strengths differ and there is no Rutherford amplitude in the transfer channel.

## Method

The authors extended Fuller's NF decomposition by splitting the symmetrized elastic scattering amplitude for identical spin-zero nuclei into direct and exchange NF subamplitudes using Legendre functions of the second kind, with the partial-wave summation restricted to even L. For core-identical systems such as 16O+12C and 13C+12C, they performed two-channel coupled reaction channel (CRC) calculations to obtain the elastic scattering and elastic transfer S-matrix elements separately, then applied the generalized NF decomposition to the total amplitude f_total(θ) = f_ES(θ) + f_ET(π-θ).

## Key numbers

- Elastic 16O+12C scattering data at E_lab = 200 MeV show the first Airy minimum A1 at θ_c.m. ≈ 64° and the second Airy minimum A2 at θ_c.m. ≈ 43°
- Elastic 12C+12C data analyzed at E_lab = 112 MeV and E_lab = 121.6 MeV, with the NF decomposition revealing symmetric Airy minima at θ and π-θ
- Elastic 16O+12C data analyzed at E_lab = 100 MeV, 132 MeV, and 300 MeV, with the two-channel CRC calculation reproducing the enhanced oscillation at backward angles
- Elastic 13C+12C scattering analyzed at E_c.m. = 120 MeV, showing the NF pattern evolving smoothly from the symmetric 12C+12C system

## Context

This work extends Fuller's original NF decomposition method (Fuller, 1975) for nonidentical nucleus-nucleus systems to identical and core-identical systems where boson symmetry or elastic transfer distorts the rainbow pattern. The authors' prior CRC calculations of elastic α transfer in 16O+12C (Phuc, 2018, 2019, 2021a, 2024a) showed that elastic transfer strongly enhances the cross section at backward angles and obscures the Airy oscillation pattern. The generalized NF method enables probing the real optical potential at small internuclear distances and nuclear clustering in systems where the standard Fuller decomposition was previously inapplicable.
