# Connection formulas between Coulomb wave functions

> The mathematical relations between the regular Coulomb function $F_{η\ell}(ρ)$ and the irregular Coulomb functions $H^\pm_{η\ell}(ρ)$ and $G_{η\ell}(ρ)$ are obtained in the complex plane of the variables $η$ and $ρ$ for integer or half-integer values of $\ell$. These relations, referred to as "connection formulas", form the basis of the theory of Coulomb wave functions, and play an important role in many fields of physics, especially in the quantum theory of charged particle scattering. As a first step, the symmetry properties of the regular function $F_{η\ell}(ρ)$ are studied, in particular under the transformation $\ell\mapsto-\ell-1$, by means of the modified Coulomb function $Φ_{η\ell}(ρ)$, which is entire in the dimensionless energy $η^{-2}$ and the angular momentum $\ell$. Then, it is shown that, for integer or half-integer $\ell$, the irregular functions $H^\pm_{η\ell}(ρ)$ and $G_{η\ell}(ρ)$ can be expressed in terms of the derivatives of $Φ_{η,\ell}(ρ)$ and $Φ_{η,-\ell-1}(ρ)$ with respect to $\ell$. As a consequence, the connection formulas directly lead to the description of the singular structures of $H^\pm_{η\ell}(ρ)$ and $G_{η\ell}(ρ)$ at complex energies in their whole Riemann surface. The analysis of the functions is supplemented by novel graphical representations in the complex plane of $η^{-1}$.

## Key claim

The paper derives connection formulas between regular and irregular Coulomb wave functions for integer or half-integer angular momentum \(\ell\) in the complex plane of \(\eta\) and \(\rho\), showing that the irregular functions \(H^\pm_{\eta\ell}\) and \(G_{\eta\ell}\) can be expressed in terms of derivatives of the modified Coulomb function \(\Phi_{\eta\ell}\) with respect to \(\ell\), but this derivation relies on the use of the regularized function \(\Phi_{\eta\ell}\) rather than \(F_{\eta\ell}\) because the latter has a tangled branch-cut structure that makes symmetry relations discontinuous.

## Method

The author first defines a modified Coulomb function \(\Phi_{\eta\ell}(\rho)\) that is entire in the dimensionless energy \(\eta^{-2}\) and angular momentum \(\ell\), using the regularized confluent hypergeometric function \(\regm(\alpha,\beta,z)\). Symmetry properties of \(\Phi_{\eta\ell}\) under the transformation \(\ell \mapsto -\ell-1\) are established via the reflection formula of the regularized Kummer function, yielding \(\Phi_{\eta,-\ell-1}(\rho) = w_{\eta\ell}\,\Phi_{\eta,\ell}(\rho)\) for half-integer \(\ell\). The irregular Coulomb functions \(H^\pm_{\eta\ell}\) and \(G_{\eta\ell}\) are then expressed in terms of derivatives of \(\Phi_{\eta,\ell}\) and \(\Phi_{\eta,-\ell-1}\) with respect to \(\ell\), and the singular structures of these functions in the complex energy plane are analyzed using these connection formulas.

## Key numbers

* The modified Coulomb function \(\Phi_{\eta\ell}(\rho)\) behaves as \(\Phi_{\eta\ell}(\rho) = x\,I_{2\ell+1}(2x) + \mathcal{O}(\energy)\) as \(\energy \to 0\), where \(x = \sqrt{2\eta\rho} = \sqrt{2r/\anbohr}\) and \(I_{\nu}\) is the modified Bessel function of the first kind.
* The polynomial \(w_{\eta\ell}\) for positive integer \(\ell\) is given by \(w_{\eta\ell} = \prod_{j=0}^\ell \left(1 + \frac{j^2}{\eta^2}\right)\) for all \(\ell \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}\), and for half-odd-integer \(\ell\) by \(w_{\eta\ell} = \prod_{j=1/2}^\ell \left(1 + \frac{j^2}{\eta^2}\right)\) for \(\ell \in \{\frac{1}{2}, \frac{3}{2}, \frac{5}{2}, \ldots\}\).
* The branch points of the Coulomb normalization coefficient \(C_{\eta\ell}\) are located at \(\anbohr k = \pm \I/(n+\ell+1)\) for all \(n \in \mathbb{Z}_{\geq 0}\), and the point \(\anbohr k = 0\) is an essential singularity.

## Context

This work extends the mathematical theory of Coulomb wave functions developed by Yost, Wheeler, and Breit (1936), Breit and Hull (1950, 1959), Abramowitz and Stegun (1954, 1964), and later by Humblet (1984, 1985) and Dzieciol (1999), by providing explicit connection formulas valid for complex arguments and integer or half-integer \(\ell\). The results enable the analysis of singular structures of irregular Coulomb functions at complex energies, which is relevant for charged-particle scattering in nuclear and atomic physics, quantum defect theory (Seaton, 1983), and Regge pole theory (Takemasa, 1979). The paper also introduces novel graphical representations in the complex plane of \(\eta^{-1}\) to illustrate the analytic properties.
