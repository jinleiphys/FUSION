# Proton spin, topology and confinement: lessons from QCD$_2$

> We investigate the relation between the topology of a nucleon and its spin composition. We approach this question in (1+1) dimensional single-flavor QCD with a large number of color. In this limit the theory can be shown to be dual to the exactly solvable sine-Gordon model. The spectrum of baryons and mesons is known analytically, and the baryon is a topological kink of the sine-Gordon model. Using the method of solitonic constituents we construct the state of the baryon and extract its $g_1$ structure function. Due to the topological nature of the baryon state this structure function is enhanced at low Bjorken $x$. We propose this enhancement as an experimental probe of the topological structure of the nucleon state.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the enhancement of the polarized structure function \(g_1(x_B)\) at low Bjorken \(x\) in a baryon arises entirely from the topological nature of the baryon state, and that this enhancement could serve as an experimental probe of the topological structure of the nucleon state in QCD\(_4\).

## Method

The authors work in (1+1)-dimensional QCD with one flavor and a large number of colors (the 't Hooft limit), which is dual to the exactly solvable sine-Gordon model. In this dual description, the baryon is a topological kink. Using the method of solitonic constituents, the quantum state of the kink is constructed as a coherent state of constituents that encode the topology. The matrix element of the axial current over this kink state is then computed exactly, and the chirality distribution is extracted as a function of Bjorken \(x\).

## Key numbers

- The classical energy (mass) of the kink is \(M_{kink} = 4\sqrt{\frac{N}{\pi}} m'\).
- The total vector charge of the kink is \(Q = N\), identifying it as a baryon composed of \(N\) quarks.
- The matrix element of the axial current in the baryon is \(\langle kink |j_{5+}(x_B) | kink \rangle = N \frac{1}{\cosh\left[N x_B\right]}\), showing enhancement at \(x_B \sim 1/N\).
- For a non-topological "meson" state, the chirality distribution is \(\langle non-top.|j_{5+}(x_B)|non-top.\rangle (x_B) = \frac{2N^2 x_B i}{\pi \cosh(N x_B)}\), which vanishes at \(x_B \rightarrow 0\).

## Context

This work extends the study of the "proton spin crisis" by investigating the role of baryon topology in spin composition, using a simplified model (QCD\(_2\)) that shares confinement and mass gap generation with real QCD. It builds on the bosonization duality established by Coleman (1975) and the solitonic constituent approach of Dvali (2015). The results suggest that the topological structure of the baryon leads to a distinct enhancement of \(g_1(x_B)\) at low \(x\) compared to mesons, which could be tested experimentally via diffractive deep-inelastic scattering or lattice QCD calculations.
