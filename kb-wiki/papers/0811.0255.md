# Pomeron-Odderon interference in production of pi^ + pi^- pairs in ultraperipheral collisions

> In this contribution we discuss the production of two pion pairs in high energy photon collisions as they can be produced in ultraperipheral collisions at hadron colliders such as the Tevatron, RHIC or LHC. We find that charge asymmetries may reveal the existence of the perturbative Odderon.

## Key claim

Charge asymmetries in the production of two pion pairs in photon-photon collisions may reveal the existence of the perturbative Odderon, but the asymmetry is sizable yet dependent on the model chosen for the generalized distribution amplitude (GDA) of the pion pair.

## Method

The authors compute the amplitude for the reaction γγ → π⁺π⁻ π⁺π⁻ using k_T-factorization, expressing the amplitudes as convolutions of impact factors over the transverse momenta of exchanged gluons. The Pomeron exchange amplitude uses a C-odd GDA (Φ^{I=1}) for the pion pair, while the Odderon exchange uses a C-even GDA (Φ^{I=0}); the charge asymmetry is defined by weighting the differential cross section with cosθ cosθ' before angular integration, so that the Odderon amplitude enters linearly through interference with the Pomeron. The matrix elements are evaluated numerically via Monte Carlo methods using a modified version of the Vegas algorithm from the Cuba library.

## Context

This work extends the approach initiated by Brodsky (1999) for using charge asymmetries to isolate Odderon effects linearly rather than quadratically in observables. The calculations rely on phenomenological GDAs from Hagler (2002) and Warkentin (2007), with four different models for the I=0 component; HERMES measurements (Airapetian, 2004) disfavor models with a strong f₀ coupling to the π⁺π⁻ state. The results enable searches for the perturbative Odderon at the Tevatron and LHC, with higher-statistics data from JLab at 6 or 12 GeV needed before a definite conclusion.
