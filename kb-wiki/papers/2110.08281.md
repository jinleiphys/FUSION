# Holographic modeling of nuclear matter and neutron stars

> I review holographic models for (dense and cold) nuclear matter, neutron stars, and their mergers. I start by a brief general discussion on current knowledge of cold QCD matter and neutron stars, and go on discussing various approaches to model cold nuclear and quark matter by using gauge/gravity duality, pointing out their strengths and weaknesses. Then I focus on recent results for a complex bottom-up holographic framework (V-QCD), which also takes input from lattice QCD results, effective field theory, and perturbative QCD. Dense nuclear matter is modeled in V-QCD through a homogeneous non-Abelian bulk gauge field. Feasible "hybrid" equations of state for cold nuclear (and quark) matter can be constructed by using traditional methods (e.g., effective field theory) at low densities and the holographic V-QCD model at higher densities. I discuss the constraints from this approach to the properties of the nuclear to quark matter transition as well as to properties of neutron stars. Using such hybrid equations of state as an input for numerical simulations of neutron star mergers, I also derive predictions for the spectrum of produced gravitational waves.

## Key claim

This review argues that holographic (gauge/gravity duality) models, particularly the bottom-up V-QCD framework, can provide reliable predictions for the equation of state of cold dense QCD matter in the intermediate-density regime where lattice QCD, effective field theory, and perturbative QCD are not applicable, but the author notes that the existence of a nuclear-to-quark matter transition remains a conjecture and that the V-QCD model requires matching to multiple data sources.

## Method

The author reviews the construction of hybrid equations of state for cold nuclear and quark matter by combining traditional methods (effective field theory) at low densities with the holographic V-QCD model at higher densities. Dense nuclear matter is modeled in V-QCD through a homogeneous non-Abelian bulk gauge field, with input from lattice QCD results, effective field theory, and perturbative QCD. These hybrid equations of state are then used as input for numerical simulations of neutron star mergers to derive predictions for gravitational wave spectra.

## Key numbers

- The QCD crossover temperature is approximately 155 MeV, with lattice simulations reliable up to μ/T ≈ 1 near this temperature.
- The pulsar J0740+6620 has mass M = 2.08 ± 0.07 M⊙ (solar masses), setting a lower bound on the maximum neutron star mass MTOV around 2 M⊙.
- The tidal deformability from GW170817 is Λ1.4 = 190+390-120 at 90% confidence level, with an upper bound of Λ1.4 < 580 used to constrain the equation of state.
- Neutron star radii lie between 10 and 15 km.
- The LIGO/Virgo bound from GW170817 excludes equations of state with Λ1.4 > 580, while the mass constraint MTOV > 2 M⊙ excludes soft equations of state.

## Context

This review extends the line of work using gauge/gravity duality for QCD, building on earlier holographic approaches such as the hard wall model (Erlich 2005, DaRold 2005), soft wall model (Karch 2006), Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model (Sakai 2004, 2005), and D3-D7 models (Karch 2002, Kruczenski 2003, Mateos 2007). The V-QCD model (Jarvinen 2011) is an extension of improved holographic QCD (Gursoy 2007) with dynamical flavors. The review enables future work by providing a framework to extrapolate QCD predictions to the intermediate-density regime of neutron star cores, where constraints from neutron star mass measurements (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013, Cromartie 2019) and gravitational wave observations (LIGO/Virgo 2017) can be applied.
