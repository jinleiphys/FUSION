# Dynamical collective potential energy landscape: its impact on the competition between fusion and quasi-fission in a heavy fusing system

> A realistic microscopically-based quantum approach to the competition between fusion and quasi-fission in a heavy fusing system is applied to several reactions leading to $^{256}$No. Fusion and quasi-fission are described in terms of a diffusion process of nuclear shapes through a dynamical collective potential energy landscape which is initially diabatic and gradually becomes adiabatic. The microscopic ingredients of the theory are obtained with a realistic two-center shell model based on Woods-Saxon potentials. The results indicate that (i) the diabatic effects play a very important role in the onset of fusion hindrance for heavy systems, and (ii) very asymmetric reactions induced by closed shell nuclei seem to be the best suited to synthesize the heaviest compound nuclei.

## Key claim

Diabatic effects (coherent particle-hole excitations that destroy the Fermi distribution of single-particle occupation numbers) play a very important role in the onset of fusion hindrance for heavy systems, and very asymmetric reactions induced by closed shell nuclei appear best suited to synthesize the heaviest compound nuclei because diabatic effects are minimized and the contact configuration is compact and well inside the capture barrier radius.

## Method

The competition between fusion and quasi-fission is modeled as a diffusion process of nuclear shapes through a dynamical collective potential energy landscape that is initially diabatic and gradually becomes adiabatic. The microscopic ingredients are obtained from a realistic two-center shell model based on Woods-Saxon potentials, and the evolution is described by solving a set of master equations coupled to a relaxation equation for the single-particle occupation numbers on a 2D mesh of the separation between nuclei R and the mass asymmetry coordinate eta.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction $^{48}$Ca + $^{208}$Pb $\to$ $^{256}$No with a central collision at total incident energy 30 MeV in the center-of-mass frame, the relaxation time of the diabatic collective potential energy surface to the adiabatic one is about $5 \times 10^{-22}$ s.
- The quasi-fission time tau_qf remains around $10^{-22}$ s for the reactions studied.
- For a central symmetric collision, the maximum of the compound nucleus formation probability P_CN is around the capture barrier energy of 30 MeV.
- For a symmetric collision at E_cm = 30 MeV, P_CN weakly depends on angular momentum for partial waves up to 40 hbar.

## Context

This work extends the dissipative diabatic dynamics approach suggested by Norenberg and reconciles conflicting aspects of current models for compound nucleus formation by showing that both the mass asymmetry coordinate eta (important at the beginning of the reaction) and the internuclear distance R (important later when the diabatic potential energy surface has relaxed) play crucial roles. The approach contrasts with classical trajectory models such as those of Aritomo and Zagrebaev by using master equations without equations of motion for individual collective coordinates, and it enables predictions of the dependence of fusion on the nuclear structure of the interacting nuclei.
