# Omega meson propagation in dense nuclear matter and collective excitations

> The bosonic excitations induced by the omega meson propagation in dense nuclear matter is studied within the framework of random phase approximation. The collective modes are then analyzed by finding the zeros of the relevant dielectric functions. Subsequently we present closed form analytical expressions for the dispersion relations in different kinematical regime. Next, the analytical behaviour of the in-medium effective propagator for the omega meson is examined. This is exploited to calculate the full spectral function for the transverse (T) and longitudinal (L) mode of the omega meson. In addition, various sum rules are constructed for the omega meson spectral density in nuclear medium. Results are then discussed by calculating residues at the poles and discontinuities across the cuts.

# Wiki Page: Omega Meson Propagation in Dense Nuclear Matter and Collective Excitations

## Key claim
The omega meson in dense nuclear matter induces collective bosonic excitations whose dispersion relations can be derived analytically within the hard nucleon loop (HNL) approximation, but only when the meson momentum is small compared to the Fermi momentum of the nucleons. The results depend crucially on including both the density-dependent (Fermi sea) and vacuum (Dirac sea) contributions to the polarization tensor, which have opposite signs and together determine the net in-medium mass shift.

## Method
The author calculates the omega meson polarization tensor in nuclear matter using the random phase approximation (RPA) with repeated nucleon-nucleon loops. The in-medium nucleon propagator is split into a free part (Dirac sea) and a density-dependent part (Pauli blocking). The hard nucleon loop (HNL) approximation is applied, assuming all loop momenta are hard (p ~ p_F) and external meson momenta are soft (|q| << p_F), which allows analytical evaluation of the polarization insertions. The dispersion relations are obtained by finding zeros of the longitudinal and transverse dielectric functions, and sum rules are constructed from the analytic properties of the effective propagators.

## Key numbers
- Plasma frequency defined as Ω² = (g_V²/π²)(1/3)(p_F³/ε_F), where p_F is the Fermi momentum and ε_F = √(p_F² + M²)
- For k << Ω (small meson momentum), the dispersion relation gives ω_{L(T)}² = m_ω*² + Ω², where m_ω*² = m_ω²(1 + (g_V²/(3π²))((M* - M)/M))
- For k << Ω, the transverse mode dispersion: ω_T² = m_ω*² + Ω² + k² + (1/5)k²v_F²(Ω²/(m_ω*² + Ω²))
- For k << Ω, the longitudinal mode dispersion: ω_L² = m_ω*² + Ω² + (3/5)k²v_F²(Ω²/(m_ω*² + Ω²))
- For k > Ω, the transverse mode: ω_T² = k² + m_ω*² + (3/2)Ω²
- The first transverse sum rule: ∫(ω dω/2π)ρ_T(ω,k) = 1 (independent of k)
- The second transverse sum rule: ∫(ω³ dω/2π)ρ_T(ω,k) = k² + m_ω*² + Ω²
- For large k (k > Ω), the transverse residue Z_T(k) ≈ 1/(2√(k² + m_ω*²))

## Context
This work extends the pioneering study by Chin (1977) on omega meson collective modes in nuclear matter, which neglected the Dirac sea contribution. It also builds on the work of Jean, Piekarewicz, and Williams (1994), who showed that the vacuum part contributes substantially with opposite sign to the density-dependent part. The paper provides closed-form analytical expressions for dispersion relations in different kinematic regimes and constructs sum rules for the omega meson spectral density, enabling future studies of rho meson propagation (which requires including tensor interactions) within the same HNL approximation framework.
