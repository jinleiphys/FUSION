# Correlation function for the $p ~f_1(1285)$ interaction

> We have addressed here the problem of calculating the correlation function of a stable particle with a resonance, in particular one resonance that qualifies as a molecular state of two components. The formalism used requires to evaluate the scattering matrix of the stable particle with the molecule, a nuclear problem which we address by means of the fixed center approximation. We have applied the method to the interaction of a proton with the $f_1(1285)$ resonance, presently under investigation by the ALICE collaboration, where the $f_1(1285)$ is taken as a $K^*\bar{K}-\bar{K}^*K$ molecule. We find that the $p ~ f_1(1285)$ interaction develops a resonance state below the $p ~ f_1(1285)$ threshold, which leads to a depletion in the $p ~ f_1(1285)$ correlation function for small values of the proton momentum. The discussion presented shows that these type of studies can provide much information on the nature of some resonances and the existence of three-body bound states involving mesons and baryons.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the p f1(1285) interaction develops a resonance state below the p f1(1285) threshold, which leads to a depletion in the p f1(1285) correlation function for small values of the proton momentum. This result depends on treating the f1(1285) as a molecular state of K*Kbar - Kbar*K components and using the fixed center approximation, with the caveat that the elastic unitarity of the T-matrix is restored by a multiplicative factor of s/Mc^2 A|th (found to be 1.52) to correct for static approximations.

## Method
The authors calculate the scattering amplitude for a proton interacting with the f1(1285) resonance, treating the f1(1285) as a molecular state of K*Kbar - Kbar*K components. They use the fixed center approximation (FCA) to evaluate the three-body scattering of the nucleon with the two-body cluster, solving coupled equations for the partitions T1 and T2 where the nucleon interacts first with either the vector or pseudoscalar meson component. The correlation function is then computed using the formalism of Vidana (2023) with a Gaussian source function, integrating over the relative distance r with the scattering amplitude and a loop function Gtilde(s,r) regularized with a cutoff q'max = 630 MeV.

## Key numbers
- The squared absolute value of the amplitude |Ttilde|^2 peaks around 2180 MeV, about 40 MeV below the p f1(1285) threshold.
- The cutoff momentum used to regularize loops in the chiral unitary approach is qmax = 1000 MeV, ensuring the f1(1285) state is generated at its experimental mass.
- The elastic unitarity correction factor s/Mc^2 A|th is found to be 1.52, with the imaginary part only 1% of the real part.
- The cutoff q'max for the correlation function loop is chosen as 630 MeV, and changes to 800 MeV result in changes in the correlation function of the level of 3%.

## Context
This work extends the femtoscopic correlation function technique, previously applied to pairs of stable particles (e.g., DK and D*D systems by Ikeno (2023) and Albaladejo (2023)), to the case of a stable particle interacting with a resonance. It builds on the molecular picture of the f1(1285) as a K*Kbar - Kbar*K state, supported by prior studies of its decays (Aceti 2015) and production reactions (Xie 2015, Molina 2016). The method enables future experimental studies by the ALICE collaboration to extract information about the nature of resonances and the existence of three-body bound states involving mesons and baryons.
