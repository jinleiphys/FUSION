# Relativistic quasiparticle time blocking approximation. Dipole response of open-shell nuclei

> The self-consistent Relativistic Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (RQRPA) is extended by the quasiparticle-phonon coupling (QPC) model using the Quasiparticle Time Blocking Approximation (QTBA). The method is formulated in terms of the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) in the two-quasiparticle space with an energy-dependent two-quasiparticle residual interaction. This equation is solved either in the basis of Dirac states forming the self-consistent solution of the ground state or in the momentum representation. Pairing correlations are treated within the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) model with a monopole-monopole interaction. The same NL3 set of the coupling constants generates the Dirac-Hartree-BCS single-quasiparticle spectrum, the static part of the residual two-quasiparticle interaction and the quasiparticle-phonon coupling amplitudes. A quantitative description of electric dipole excitations in the chain of tin isotopes (Z=50) with the mass numbers A = 100, 106, 114, 116, 120, and 130 and in the chain of isotones with (N=50) 88-Sr, 90-Zr, 92-Mo is performed within this framework. The RQRPA extended by the coupling to collective vibrations generates spectra with a multitude of '2q+phonon' (two quasiparticles plus phonon) states providing a noticeable fragmentation of the giant dipole resonance as well as of the soft dipole mode (pygmy resonance) in the nuclei under investigation. The results obtained for the photo absorption cross sections and for the integrated contributions of the low-lying strength to the calculated dipole spectra agree very well with the available experimental data.

## Key claim

The Relativistic Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (RQRPA) is extended by coupling to low-lying collective vibrations via the Quasiparticle Time Blocking Approximation (QTBA), producing dipole spectra with strong fragmentation of both the giant dipole resonance and the pygmy resonance; the method is restricted to the linearized version of the quasiparticle-phonon coupling model, where the phonon transition densities are computed within the standard RQRPA without self-consistent iteration of the energy-dependent interaction.

## Method

The Bethe-Salpeter equation for the response function is formulated in the two-quasiparticle space of Dirac-Hartree-Bogoliubov eigenstates, using an energy-dependent residual interaction that sums a static part derived from the NL3 covariant density functional and an induced part from quasiparticle-phonon coupling. Pairing correlations are treated with the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) model using a monopole-monopole interaction, and the phonon vertices are obtained from the static effective interaction and the RQRPA transition densities. The resulting equation is solved within the quasiparticle time blocking approximation, which truncates the configuration space to two-quasiparticle and two-quasiparticle-plus-phonon states and includes a subtraction procedure to avoid double counting of ground-state correlations.

## Key numbers

- Photoabsorption cross sections and integrated low-lying dipole strength are calculated for tin isotopes with mass numbers A = 100, 106, 114, 116, 120, and 130 (Z=50) and for the N=50 isotones 88-Sr, 90-Zr, and 92-Mo.
- The NL3 parameter set is used for the coupling constants generating the Dirac-Hartree-BCS single-quasiparticle spectrum, the static residual interaction, and the quasiparticle-phonon coupling amplitudes.
- The method produces a multitude of "2q+phonon" states that cause noticeable fragmentation of the giant dipole resonance and the soft dipole (pygmy) mode in all nuclei studied.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic RPA with quasiparticle-phonon coupling (Litvinova, Ring, and Tselyaev 2007; Litvinova, Ring, and Vretenar 2007) from doubly closed-shell nuclei to open-shell nuclei by incorporating pairing correlations via the BCS model. It builds on the non-relativistic quasiparticle time blocking approximation developed by Tselyaev (2007) and Litvinova and Tselyaev (2007), and on earlier non-relativistic approaches by Soloviev (1992), Bortignon and Peres (1999), and Colo and Bortignon (2001). The method enables microscopic description of dipole strength fragmentation in semi-magic nuclei across isotopic and isotonic chains, providing a framework for future studies of giant resonances and pygmy modes in open-shell systems.
