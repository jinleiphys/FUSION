# Effects of isospin and momentum dependent interactions on liquid-gas phase transition in hot asymmetric nuclear matter

> The liquid-gas phase transition in hot neutron-rich nuclear matter is investigated within a self-consistent thermal model using an isospin and momentum dependent interaction (MDI) constrained by the isospin diffusion data in heavy-ion collisions, a momentum-independent interaction (MID), and an isoscalar momentum-dependent interaction (eMDYI). The boundary of the phase-coexistence region is shown to be sensitive to the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy with a softer symmetry energy giving a higher critical pressure and a larger area of phase-coexistence region. Compared with the momentum-independent MID interaction, the isospin and momentum-dependent MDI interaction is found to increase the critical pressure and enlarge the area of phase-coexistence region. For the isoscalar momentum-dependent eMDYI interaction, a limiting pressure above which the liquid-gas phase transition cannot take place has been found and it is shown to be sensitive to the stiffness of the symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The liquid-gas phase coexistence boundary in hot neutron-rich nuclear matter is sensitive to both the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy and the momentum dependence of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, with the isospin and momentum-dependent MDI interaction constrained by isospin diffusion data providing a realistic prediction of the phase boundary.

## Method

The authors used a self-consistent thermal model with three different nuclear effective interactions: the isospin and momentum-dependent MDI interaction (constrained by isospin diffusion data from heavy-ion collisions), a momentum-independent MID interaction, and an isoscalar momentum-dependent eMDYI interaction. At zero temperature, all three interactions give the same equation of state for asymmetric nuclear matter. The phase coexistence was determined by solving the Gibbs conditions (equal pressures and chemical potentials for the liquid and gas phases) using a geometrical construction method on the chemical potential isobars as functions of isospin asymmetry.

## Key numbers

- Critical pressure for MDI interaction with x=0 (softer symmetry energy): 0.265 MeV/fm³ at T=10 MeV
- Critical pressure for MID interaction with x=0: 0.230 MeV/fm³ at T=10 MeV
- Critical pressure for MDI interaction with x=-1 (stiffer symmetry energy): 0.195 MeV/fm³ at T=10 MeV
- Critical pressure for MID interaction with x=-1: 0.154 MeV/fm³ at T=10 MeV
- Limiting pressure for eMDYI interaction with x=0: 0.205 MeV/fm³ at T=10 MeV
- Limiting pressure for eMDYI interaction with x=-1: 0.175 MeV/fm³ at T=10 MeV
- Binding energy per nucleon of -16 MeV, incompressibility K0=211 MeV, and symmetry energy of 31.6 MeV for cold symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density ρ0=0.16 fm⁻³ (for MDI interaction)
- Zero-temperature symmetry energy parameterized as 31.6(ρ/ρ0)^0.69 MeV for x=0 (softer) and 31.6(ρ/ρ0)^1.05 MeV for x=-1 (stiffer)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the liquid-gas phase transition in symmetric nuclear matter (Lamb 1978, Finn 1982, Berdahl 1983, Jaqaman 1983) and asymmetric nuclear matter (Muller 1995, Li 2001, Natowitz 2002) by incorporating momentum-dependent interactions constrained by recent isospin diffusion data from NSCL/MSU (Chen 2005, Li 2005). The results enable more reliable predictions of the liquid-gas phase boundary in neutron-rich matter relevant for both nuclear physics and astrophysics, particularly for experiments planned at radioactive beam facilities. The finding of a limiting pressure in the eMDYI interaction is similar to that found by Su (2000) using different models.
