# On the Relativistic Description of the Nucleus

> We present here a formalism able to generalise to a relativistically covariant scheme the standard nuclear shell model. We show that, using some generalised nuclear Green's functions and their Lehmann representation we can define the relativistic equivalent of the non relativistic single particle wave function (not loosing, however, the physical contribution of other degrees of freedom, like mesons and antinucleons). It is shown that the mass operator associated to the nuclear Green's function can be approximated with the equivalent of a shell-model potential and that the corresponding ``single particle wave functions'' can be easily derived in a specified frame of reference and then boosted to any other system, thus fully restoring the Lorentz covariance

## Key claim

The paper presents a formalism that generalizes the non-relativistic nuclear shell model to a relativistically covariant scheme by using generalized nuclear Green's functions with different initial and final nuclear momenta, thereby restoring Lorentz covariance while retaining contributions from mesons and antinucleons. The authors show that the mass operator can be approximated as a shell-model potential and that the corresponding "single particle wave functions" can be derived in one frame and boosted to any other, but this construction depends on introducing a "shell" ground state and choosing a specific frame of reference for the initial derivation.

## Method

The authors define a generalized single-particle Green's function with different initial and final nuclear momenta (non-diagonal in the nuclear momentum), then derive its Lehmann representation and a Dyson-like equation with separate mass operators for particle and hole channels. They introduce a phenomenological shell-model potential V(p,p';q) that is independent of the energy variable q0, chosen to approximate the full mass operator, and formulate eigenvalue equations for relativistic "hole wave functions" and "particle wave functions" that serve as the relativistic analogues of shell-model single-particle wave functions. The formalism is developed for a simplified model of a fermionic field interacting with a scalar bosonic field (sigma meson), neglecting self-interactions.

## Key numbers

- The normalization condition for nuclear states is given as <p'|p> = (2π)^3 2p0 δ^3(p - p'), where p0 = sqrt(p^2 + M^2) for a nucleus of mass M.
- The completeness equation for the relativistic wave functions reads: Σ_λ φ_λ(p,q) φ^†_λ(p',q) + Σ_ν ψ_ν(p',q') ψ^†_ν(p,q') = (2π)^3 δ(p - p'), where q' = p + p' - q.
- The number of baryons A is given by A = Tr ∫ d^4q/(2π)^4 i γ^0 G_h(p,p;q) e^{-iq0α}, and the sum over occupied levels satisfies Σ_ℓ N_ℓ = A, where N_ℓ = θ(ℓ_F - ℓ).

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic Feshbach optical potential formalism (Feshbach, 1958, 1962) and the Bell-Squires connection between Green's functions and optical potentials (Bell and Squires, 1959) to a relativistic covariant framework for finite nuclei. It addresses difficulties in reconciling relativity, translational invariance, and the shell model that were noted in reviews by Serot and Walecka (1986) and more recent relativistic shell-model approaches (Rath, 2001). The formalism enables future treatment of exclusive and inclusive electron scattering reactions (such as (e,e'p) and (e,e'n)) in a fully Poincaré-invariant way, avoiding the multiple-counting problems identified by the authors in earlier work (Cenni and Vagradov, 1995), and can be extended to quark dynamics in nucleons.
