# Sound velocity peak and conformality in isospin QCD

> We study zero temperature equations of state (EOS) in isospin QCD within a quark-meson model which is renormalizable and hence eliminates high density artifacts in models with the ultraviolet cutoff (e.g., NJL models). The model exhibits a crossover transition of pion condensations from the Bose-Einstein-Condensation regime at low density to the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer regime at high density. The EOS stiffens quickly and approaches the quark matter regime at density significantly less than the density for pions to spatially overlap. The sound velocity develops a peak in the crossover region, and then gradually relaxes to the conformal value $1/3$ from above, in contrast to the perturbative QCD results which predicts the approach from below. In the context of QCD computations, this opposite trend is in part due to the lack of gluon exchanges in our model, and also due to the non-perturbative power corrections arising from the condensates. We argue that with large power corrections the trace anomaly can be negative. In quantitative level, our EOS is consistent with the lattice results in the BEC regime but begins to get stiffer at higher density. The sound velocity peak also appears at higher density. The BCS gap in our model is $Δ\simeq 300$ MeV in the quark matter domain, and naive application of the BCS relation for the critical temperature $T_c \simeq 0.57Δ$ yields the estimate $T_c \simeq 170$ MeV, in good agreement with the lattice data.

## Key claim

The sound velocity squared in isospin QCD develops a peak in the BEC-BCS crossover region and then relaxes to the conformal value 1/3 from above, opposite to perturbative QCD predictions which approach from below; this opposite trend is attributed to the lack of gluon exchanges in the quark-meson model and to non-perturbative power corrections from condensates.

## Method

The authors study zero-temperature equations of state in isospin QCD using a renormalizable two-flavor quark-meson model, computing the one-loop effective potential within the leading order of the 1/Nc expansion. They fix parameters using vacuum quantities (fπ, M0, mσ, mπ) and renormalization conditions in the MS scheme, then evaluate the thermodynamic potential at finite isospin chemical potential μI to describe the crossover from BEC to BCS regimes.

## Key numbers

- BCS gap in the quark matter domain: Δ ≃ 300 MeV
- Critical temperature estimate from BCS relation Tc ≃ 0.57Δ yields Tc ≃ 170 MeV, in agreement with lattice data
- Nuclear saturation density used as unit: n0 = 0.16 fm⁻³
- The sound velocity squared cs² approaches the conformal value 1/3 from above, not from below as in perturbative QCD

## Context

This work extends model studies of the BEC-BCS crossover in isospin QCD previously analyzed by Adhikari (2016, 2018) and Ayala (2023), using a renormalizable quark-meson model that avoids high-density artifacts present in NJL models with UV cutoffs. The results are compared with lattice data from Brandt (2022) and Abbott (2023), and the discussion of sound velocity peaks connects to phenomenological interpolation studies by Masuda (2012) and general arguments by Bedaque (2014) and Kojo (2014, 2015). The approach from above to the conformal limit contrasts with perturbative QCD results by Freedman (1977) and Kurkela (2009), and the analysis of power corrections and trace anomaly provides a framework for understanding non-perturbative effects in dense quark matter relevant to neutron star equations of state.
