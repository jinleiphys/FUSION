# All Order Linearized Hydrodynamics from Fluid/Gravity Correspondence

> Using fluid/gravity correspondence, we determine the (linearized) stress energy tensor of $\mathcal{N}=4$ super-Yang-Mills theory at strong coupling with all orders in derivatives of fluid velocity included. We find that the dissipative effects are fully encoded in the shear term and a new one, which emerges starting from the third order. We derive, for the first time, closed linear holographic RG flow-type equations for (generalized) momenta-dependent viscosity functions. In the hydrodynamic regime, we obtain the stress tensor up to third order in derivative expansion analytically. We then numerically determine the viscosity functions up to large momenta. As a check of our results, we also derive the generalized Navier-Stokes equations from the Einstein equations in the dual gravity.

## Key claim
The paper derives the linearized stress-energy tensor of strongly coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) super-Yang-Mills theory with all orders in derivatives of fluid velocity included, but only for linearized (small-amplitude) perturbations around equilibrium. The dissipative part is fully encoded in two momentum-dependent viscosity functions, \(\eta(\omega,q^2)\) and a new function \(\zeta(\omega,q^2)\) that emerges starting at third order in derivatives.

## Method
Using the fluid/gravity correspondence, the authors linearize the fluid velocity and temperature fields around equilibrium and solve the five-dimensional Einstein equations in asymptotically anti-de Sitter spacetime for metric corrections. The metric perturbations are decomposed into scalar, vector, and tensor channels in momentum space, leading to a system of ordinary differential equations for the coefficient functions \(a,b,c,d\) that encode the viscosity functions. These equations are solved both analytically via a small-momentum (hydrodynamic) expansion and numerically for arbitrary momenta using a shooting method with boundary conditions at the horizon and asymptotic infinity.

## Key numbers
- The shear viscosity function at zero momentum is \(\eta(0,0)=2\) (in units where \(\pi T=1\)), corresponding to the universal ratio \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\).
- The first-order (relaxation time) correction to \(\eta\) is \((2-\ln 2)i\omega\).
- The second-order (third-order derivative) correction to \(\eta\) includes a term \(-\frac{1}{24}[6\pi-\pi^2+12(2-3\ln 2+\ln^2 2)]\omega^2\).
- The new viscosity function \(\zeta\) at zero momentum is \(\zeta(0,0)=\frac{1}{12}(5-\pi-2\ln 2)\).
- The temperature of the dual fluid is \(T = 1/(\pi \mathbf{b})\), with \(\mathbf{b}_0\) set to 1 by conformal invariance.

## Context
This work extends the linearized all-orders hydrodynamics program of Baier et al. (2008) and Lublinsky and Shuryak (2009) by deriving the viscosity functions directly from the dual gravity, rather than from retarded correlators which were insufficient to determine all transport coefficient functions. It generalizes the order-by-order derivative expansion of Bhattacharyya et al. (2008) to a resummation of all linear derivative terms. The results enable improved hydrodynamic models for strongly coupled plasmas, such as those produced in heavy-ion collisions, and provide a framework for studying thermalization and entropy production at early times.
