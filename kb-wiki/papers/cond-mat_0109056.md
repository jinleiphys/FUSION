# Fingerprints of nonextensive thermodynamics in a long-range Hamiltonian system

> We study the dynamics of a Hamiltonian system of N classical spins with infinite-range interaction. We present numerical results which confirm the existence of metaequilibrium Quasi Stationary States (QSS), characterized by non-Gaussian velocity distributions, anomalous diffusion, Lévy walks and dynamical correlation in phase-space. We show that the Thermodynamic Limit (TL) and the Infinite-Time Limit (ITL) do not commute. Moreover, if the TL is taken before the ITL the system does not relax to the Boltzmann-Gibbs equilibrium, but remains in this new equilibrium state where nonextensive thermodynamics seems to apply.

# Fingerprints of nonextensive thermodynamics in a long-range Hamiltonian system

## Key claim

The authors show that for a Hamiltonian system of N classical spins with infinite-range interaction, the thermodynamic limit (N → ∞) and the infinite-time limit (t → ∞) do not commute; if the thermodynamic limit is taken before the infinite-time limit, the system does not relax to the Boltzmann-Gibbs equilibrium but instead remains indefinitely in a Quasi Stationary State (QSS) where nonextensive thermodynamics appears to apply. The claim is hedged by the observation that this behavior occurs only for a specific range of initial energy densities (0.5 < U < U_c = 0.75) and for a class of off-equilibrium initial conditions.

## Method

The authors numerically integrate the equations of motion of the Hamiltonian Mean Field (HMF) model, consisting of N planar classical spins with infinite-range coupling, starting from Water Bag (WB) initial conditions (all angles zero, velocities uniformly distributed) at energy density U = 0.69. They compute the time evolution of temperature (2⟨K⟩/N), magnetization, velocity probability distribution functions (pdfs), and the largest Lyapunov exponent (LLE) for system sizes up to N = 1000, and fit the non-Gaussian velocity pdfs using the one-particle prescription of nonextensive thermodynamics.

## Key numbers

- The critical energy density of the HMF model is U_c = 0.75, corresponding to a critical temperature T_c = 0.5.
- The canonical equilibrium temperature at U = 0.69 is T_can = 0.476, with magnetization M_can = 0.307.
- The QSS temperature T_QSS(N) approaches T_∞ = 0.380 as N → ∞, with scaling [T_QSS(N) − T_∞] ∝ N^(−1/3).
- The QSS magnetization scales as M_QSS ∝ N^(−1/6).
- The lifetime τ of the QSS scales as τ ∝ N.
- The best fit to the non-Gaussian velocity pdf in the QSS uses the nonextensive index q = 7 and T = 0.38.
- The largest Lyapunov exponent in the QSS scales as LLE ∝ N^(−1/9).

## Context

This work extends the study of the HMF model previously investigated by Antoni and Ruffo (1995) and by Latora, Rapisarda, and collaborators in earlier papers, where anomalous diffusion, Lévy walks, and negative specific heat were reported in the same energy range. The results provide the first evidence of nonextensive statistical mechanics, as proposed by Tsallis (1988), in a Hamiltonian system with long-range interactions, and support the criticism by Einstein (1910) regarding the nonuniversality of Boltzmann thermostatistics. The findings enable further investigation of metastable states and ensemble inequivalence in long-range interacting systems, with connections to self-gravitating systems studied by Thirring (1970) and Lynden-Bell (1967), as well as to nuclei and atomic clusters.
