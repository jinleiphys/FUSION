# Effects of fluctuations on the initial eccentricity from the Color Glass Condensate in heavy ion collisions

> We introduce a modified form of the Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (KLN) approach for nuclear collisions. The new ansatz for the unintegrated gluon distribution function preserves factorization, and the saturation scale is bound from below by that for a single nucleon. It also reproduces the correct scaling with the number of collisions at high transverse momentum. The corresponding Monte Carlo implementation allows us to account for fluctuations of the hard sources (nucleons) in the transverse plane. We compute various definitions of the eccentricity within the new approach, which are relevant for the interpretation of the elliptic flow. Our approach predicts breaking of the scaling of the eccentricity with the Glauber eccentricity at the level of about 30%.

# Effects of fluctuations on the initial eccentricity from the Color Glass Condensate in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The modified factorized KLN (fKLN) approach predicts that the scaling of the initial eccentricity with the Glauber eccentricity is broken at the level of about 30%, with the enhancement relative to the Glauber model reaching more than 50% for central Au+Au collisions but not exceeding 30% when using the participant eccentricity definition that accounts for fluctuations of the major axes.

## Method

The authors introduced a modified form of the Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (KLN) approach where the unintegrated gluon distribution function is constructed to preserve factorization by averaging the uGDF itself rather than its argument, with the saturation scale bound from below by that for a single nucleon. They implemented this factorized KLN (fKLN) model both as a mean-field calculation and as a Monte Carlo implementation (MC-KLN) that accounts for fluctuations of nucleon positions in the transverse plane, using the k-perpendicular factorization formula to compute gluon production and various definitions of the eccentricity.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale of a single nucleon is equal to Q_{s,n}^2 = 2.0 GeV^2 / (4.2 x 1.53) = 0.31 GeV^2
- The ratio epsilon_{MC-KLN} / epsilon_{Glauber} increases by more than 50% for central Au+Au collisions and 10% for peripheral collisions
- Using the participant eccentricity definition, the enhancement is not more than 30% and both Au+Au and Cu+Cu systems follow the same curve
- The eccentricity from fKLN is somewhat lower than from KLN, especially for more peripheral collisions
- The multiplicity from MC-KLN is about 5% lower in overall normalization compared to the original KLN approach
- Including simple Poissonian fluctuations in gluon number increased epsilon{2} by only about 0.02

## Context

This work extends the KLN approach (Kharzeev, Levin, Nardi 2001) for gluon production in nuclear collisions and continues the authors' previous study (Drescher and Nara 2006) of eccentricity in the initial stage of heavy ion collisions. The results are compared to the classical Yang-Mills approach on the lattice (Lappi and Venugopalan 2006), which gave somewhat smaller eccentricity values than the original KLN model but still larger than the Glauber wounded nucleon model. The Monte Carlo implementation enables study of event-by-event fluctuations previously examined within the Glauber approach (Miller et al. 2003, Manly et al. 2005, Voloshin 2006, Bhalerao et al. 2006), and the different eccentricity definitions are relevant for interpreting elliptic flow measurements and extracting properties of the quark-gluon plasma.
