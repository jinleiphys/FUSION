# Estimate on Spin Asymmetry for Drell-Yan Process at Fermilab with Tensor-Polarized Deuteron

> There are four new structure functions for the spin-1 deuteron in comparison with the ones for the spin-1/2 proton, and they are called $b_1$, $b_2$, $b_3$, and $b_4$. The twist-2 structure functions $b_1$ and $b_2$ are expressed by tensor-polarized parton distribution functions in the deuteron. HERMES measurements of $b_1$ are much different from the prediction of the standard deuteron model with D-state admixture. It indicates that the structure functions $b_1$ and $b_2$ probe an interesting new aspect in the deuteron. There is an approved experiment at JLab to measure $b_1$ and it is expected to start in 2019. On the other hand, the measurement of tensor-polarized distributions is under consideration at Fermilab by the Drell-Yan process with the unpolarized proton beam and tensor-polarized deuteron target. It is expected to provide crucial information on tensor-polarized antiquark distributions. Since the distributions are small quantities, it is important to estimate the tensor-polarized spin asymmetry theoretically to find experimental feasibility for an actual proposal at Fermilab.

# Estimate on Spin Asymmetry for Drell-Yan Process at Fermilab with Tensor-Polarized Deuteron

## Key claim
The tensor-polarized spin asymmetry \(A_Q\) in the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process is estimated to be of the order of a few percent, which suggests that a measurement at Fermilab is feasible; however, the asymmetry depends on whether tensor-polarized antiquark distributions are included (set 2) or not (set 1), with set 2 giving more reliable results because it better explains the HERMES \(b_1\) data.

## Method
The authors calculated the tensor-polarized spin asymmetry \(A_Q\) for the Drell-Yan process \(p + d \rightarrow \mu^+ \mu^- + X\) at leading order in the parton model, using unpolarized MSTW parton distribution functions (PDFs) for the proton and deuteron, and two parameterizations (set 1 and set 2) of tensor-polarized PDFs for the deuteron at an initial scale \(Q^2 = 2.5\) GeV\(^2\). The tensor-polarized PDFs were evolved to higher \(Q^2\) scales using DGLAP evolution equations, and the asymmetry was evaluated at fixed momentum fractions \(x_1 = 0.2, 0.4, 0.6\) as a function of \(x_2\).

## Key numbers
- The HERMES integral of \(b_1\) over \(0.002 < x < 0.85\) is \([1.05 \pm 0.34(\text{stat}) \pm 0.35(\text{sys})] \times 10^{-2}\) in the measured range, and \([0.35 \pm 0.10(\text{stat}) \pm 0.18(\text{sys})] \times 10^{-2}\) in the range \(Q^2 > 1\) GeV\(^2\).
- The estimated spin asymmetry \(A_Q\) for both set 1 and set 2 is a few percent at momentum fractions \(x_1 = 0.2, 0.4, 0.6\).
- The tensor-polarized PDFs were evolved to \(Q^2 = 30\) GeV\(^2\) as an example scale for the Drell-Yan process.
- The initial scale for the tensor-polarized PDF parameterizations is \(Q^2 = 2.5\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context
This work extends the study of tensor-polarized structure in the deuteron, motivated by the HERMES measurement (Airapetian 2005) of \(b_1\) which disagreed with the standard S-D mixture model predictions (Hoodbhoy 1988, Jaffe 1988, Khan 1991). The authors propose the Drell-Yan process at Fermilab (E1309 experiment) as a complementary method to deep inelastic scattering for directly probing tensor-polarized antiquark distributions, which could help resolve the deuteron tensor structure puzzle. The results enable experimental feasibility studies for the proposed Fermilab measurement and complement the approved JLab \(b_1\) experiment expected to start in 2019.
