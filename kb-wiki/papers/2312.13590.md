# Quarkonium spectral functions in a bulk-viscous quark-gluon plasma

> We study the interplay of non-equilibrium properties of a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and heavy quarkonia. For this purpose, we compute the quarkonium spectral functions in a bulk-viscous QGP. We take into account the bulk viscous nature of the medium by modifying the distribution functions of thermal quarks and gluons. This modification affects the dielectric permittivity, which is used to calculate the in-medium heavy quark potential. With this modified complex potential, we calculate the quarkonium spectral functions and extract their physical properties. We discuss the impact of bulk viscosity on quarkonia properties such as decay widths and binding energies. We also estimate the relative production yield of $ ψ' $ to $ J/ψ$ and discuss the bulk viscous effects on it.

# Quarkonium spectral functions in a bulk-viscous quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim

The authors show that bulk viscous corrections to the quark-gluon plasma modify quarkonium spectral functions, with excited states (such as ψ′) exhibiting a clearly decreasing decay width as the bulk viscosity parameter Φ increases, while ground states (J/ψ and Υ) show no monotonic trend; this differential response could serve as a signature of the QCD critical point where bulk viscosity is expected to be enhanced.

## Method

The authors introduce bulk viscous corrections by modifying the thermal distribution functions of quarks and gluons with a parameter Φ, which alters the dielectric permittivity of the medium. From this permittivity they compute a complex in-medium heavy quark potential via a modified Fourier transform of the Cornell potential, then solve a Schrödinger equation for the unequal-time meson-meson correlator to obtain vector-channel spectral functions. The spectral function peaks are fitted with a skewed Breit-Wigner form to extract in-medium masses and decay widths.

## Key numbers

- Spectral functions are computed at temperature T = 0.2 GeV for charmonium and bottomonium for different values of the bulk viscosity parameter Φ.
- The ψ′/J/ψ ratio is computed at freeze-out temperatures of 170 MeV and 200 MeV and compared with experimental data from the CMS Collaboration.
- The in-medium masses of both ground and excited charmonium and bottomonium states decrease as functions of both temperature T and the bulk viscosity parameter Φ.
- The decay widths of excited charmonium and bottomonium states are clearly decreasing functions of Φ, while ground state decay widths show no monotonic tendency with Φ.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Thakur 2021) on quarkonium in a bulk-viscous medium by computing full spectral functions rather than just the potential. It builds on the seminal proposal by Matsui and Satz (1986) that quarkonium suppression signals QGP formation, and on the recognition that bulk viscosity is enhanced near the QCD critical point (Kharzeev 2007, Karsch 2007, Ryu 2015, Bzdak 2019). The results enable experimental searches for non-monotonic behavior in the nuclear modification factor RAA of excited quarkonium states as a function of collision energy, particularly in the beam energy scan program.
