# Diquarks and the production of charmed baryons

> Utilizing a quark model characterized by parameters that effectively replicate the masses of ground state hadrons, we illustrate that $(us)$ or $(ds)$ diquarks exhibit greater compactness in comparison to $(ud)$ diquarks. Concretely, the binding energy of the $(us)$ diquark - defined as the diquark's mass minus the combined masses of its individual quarks - is found to be stronger than that of the $(ud)$ diquark. This heightened attraction present in $(us)$ diquarks could lead to enhanced production of $Ξ_c/D$ particles in high-energy pp or ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Using a non-relativistic quark model whose parameters are fitted to reproduce ground-state baryon masses, the authors show that $(us)$ or $(ds)$ diquarks have stronger binding energy than $(ud)$ diquarks, which could lead to enhanced production of $\Xi_c/D$ particles in high-energy collisions, but the effect is presented as a possible additional mechanism rather than a definitive prediction.

## Method

The authors employ a non-relativistic quark model Hamiltonian with Coulomb, confining, and color-spin interactions, fitting its parameters to reproduce the masses of 15 ground-state baryons (standard deviation 11.9 MeV). They calculate diquark masses, binding energies, and sizes for $(ud)$, $(us)$, and $(uc)$ configurations in color anti-triplet spin-0 states, then extend the analysis to five schemes incorporating thermal masses and screened potentials at finite temperature. Finally, they use a two-dimensional coalescence model with blast-wave flow profiles to estimate the additional $\Xi_c/D^0$ production enhancement from $(us)$ diquark correlations.

## Key numbers

- $(us)$ diquark binding energy: -16.30 MeV (defined as $M_{Diquark} - m_u - m_s$), compared to $(ud)$ binding energy: +10.22 MeV (unbound)
- $(us)$ diquark mass: 970.7 MeV and size: 0.714 fm at zero temperature; mass ranges from 580.2 to 970.7 MeV across five thermal schemes
- $(ud)$ diquark mass: 680.3 MeV and size: 0.761 fm
- Difference in binding energies $B_{(us)} - B_{(ud)}$ ranges from -26.52 MeV (Scheme 1) to -6.93 MeV (Scheme 5)
- Standard deviation of fitted baryon masses: $\sigma = 11.9$ MeV
- Coalescence area for pp collisions at 7 TeV: $A = 2.52$ fm$^2$; hadronization volume: $V_H = 20$ fm$^3$
- $\Xi_c^+$ radius from quark model: $r_{\Xi_c^+} = 0.222$ fm

## Context

This work extends the study of diquark correlations in hadron production, motivated by experimental observations from ALICE that the $\Lambda_c/D$ enhancement models underestimate the measured $\Xi_c/D$ ratio in pp collisions at 5.02 TeV and 13 TeV (ALICE 2021). The authors build on coalescence models used for heavy baryon production (Greco 2003, Plumari 2017, Cho 2019, Beraudo 2022) and on prior quark model studies of diquarks (Park 2018, Noh 2021). The finding that $(us)$ diquarks are more bound than $(ud)$ diquarks provides a mechanism that could explain the missing $\Xi_c/D$ enhancement, and the framework enables future calculations of charmed baryon production in pp, pPb, and PbPb collisions using the coalescence approach with explicit diquark degrees of freedom.
