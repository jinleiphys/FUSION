# Distinguishing cusp effects and near-threshold-pole effects

> We make use of a unitarized coupled-channel model to analyze the mass distribution data of final states in production processes of $X(4260)$. By analyzing the analytical structures of the decay amplitudes, we find that the line shape of $Z_c(3900)$ signal is related to the combined effect of a pair of near-threshold "shadow" poles and the $(D\bar{D}^*)^\pm$ thresholds, in which the third-sheet pole might provide a dominant contribution. As all the coupled channels effects are tuning off, the trajectories of these two poles suggest that the $Z_c(3900)$ might originate from the attractive interaction of $(D^*\bar{D}^*)^\pm$ through a long-distance interaction, $e.g.$ $π$-exchange interaction, as a "deuteron-like" state. There is no nearby pole structure corresponding to the $Z_c(4025)$ signal in the $(D^*\bar{D}^*)^\pm$ mass distribution.

## Key claim

The line shape of the $Z_c(3900)$ signal is produced by the combined effect of a pair of near-threshold "shadow" poles and the $(D\bar{D}^*)^\pm$ thresholds, with the third-sheet pole providing the dominant contribution; there is no nearby pole structure corresponding to the $Z_c(4025)$ signal in the $(D^*\bar{D}^*)^\pm$ mass distribution.

## Method

The authors constructed a unitarized coupled-channel model that sums all two-hadron bubble-chain contributions while respecting coupled-channel unitarity, with no $s$-channel bare states introduced. They fitted the model simultaneously to three invariant mass distribution data sets from BES: $J/\psi\pi^-$, $(D\bar{D}^*)^-$, and $(D^*\bar{D}^*)^+$ in $e^+e^-$ production processes around 4.26 GeV, using 14 parameters (ten coupling constants, one regularization parameter, and three normalization factors). The pole structures of the amplitude were then extracted by analytically continuing the amplitude into the complex $s$-plane and searching on the appropriate Riemann sheets.

## Key numbers

- Best fit achieved $\chi^2/d.o.f = 125.9/(60+33+43-14) \approx 1.03$ for the combined data sets.
- A second-sheet pole was found at $\sqrt{s^{II}} = 3.846 \pm 0.019 i$ GeV.
- A third-sheet pole was found at $\sqrt{s^{III}} = 3.875 \pm 0.016 i$ GeV.
- The regularization parameter $k_0$ was fixed at 0.36 (in units of GeV, from the range 0.3 to 0.4).
- The fitted coupling constants include $\lambda_{33} = 71.9 \pm 1.1$ (attractive $D^*\bar{D}^*$ interaction) and $\lambda_{22} = -30.2 \pm 4.0$ (repulsive $D\bar{D}^*$ interaction).
- When all coupled-channel effects are switched off, the two poles converge to a bound state of $D^*\bar{D}^*$ at $\sqrt{s} = 3.862$ GeV, corresponding to a binding energy of about 140 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the debate on whether near-threshold structures like $Z_c(3900)$ are genuine resonances or threshold cusp effects, directly addressing the challenge posed by Swanson (2015) that cusp models could explain the signals without introducing poles. The authors' unitarized coupled-channel approach improves upon earlier rescattering models criticized by Guo (2014) for neglecting higher-order contributions. The finding that $Z_c(3900)$ arises from a pair of shadow poles originating from $D^*\bar{D}^*$ attraction via long-distance $\pi$-exchange supports the molecular interpretation, similar to the deuteron, and the scheme can be generalized to analyze other near-threshold structures in future experimental data.
