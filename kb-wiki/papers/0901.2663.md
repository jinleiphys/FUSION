# Subtractive renormalization of the chiral potentials up to next-to-next-to-leading order in higher NN partial waves

> We develop a subtractive renormalization scheme to evaluate the P-wave NN scattering phase shifts using chiral effective theory potentials. This allows us to consider arbitrarily high cutoffs in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation (LSE). We employ NN potentials computed up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in chiral effective theory, using both dimensional regularization and spectral-function regularization. Our results obtained from the subtracted P-wave LSE show that renormalization of the NNLO potential can be achieved by using the generalized NN scattering lengths as input--an alternative to fitting the constant that multiplies the P-wave contact interaction in the chiral effective theory NN force. However, in order to obtain a reasonable fit to the NN data at NNLO the generalized scattering lengths must be varied away from the values extracted from the so-called high-precision potentials. We investigate how the generalized scattering lengths extracted from NN data using various chiral potentials vary with the cutoff in the LSE. The cutoff-dependence of these observables, as well as of the phase shifts at $T_{lab} \approx 100$ MeV, suggests that for a chiral potential computed with dimensional regularization the highest LSE cutoff it is sensible to adopt is approximately 1 GeV. Using spectral-function regularization to compute the two-pion-exchange potentials postpones the onset of cutoff dependence in these quantities, but does not remove it.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that subtractive renormalization of the chiral NN potential in P-waves can be achieved at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) by using generalized scattering lengths as input instead of fitting the contact-interaction constants, but only if those scattering lengths are varied away from values extracted from high-precision potentials; the approach reveals that for dimensionally regularized potentials the highest sensible cutoff in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation is approximately 1 GeV.

## Method

The authors developed a subtractive renormalization scheme for the Lippmann-Schwinger equation (LSE) in P-waves, where the unknown contact-interaction constants are eliminated by subtracting the zero-energy LSE and using the generalized P-wave scattering length \(\alpha_{11}\) as input. They solved the subtracted LSE for the four NN P-wave channels (\(^{1}\mathrm{P}_1\), \(^{3}\mathrm{P}_0\), \(^{3}\mathrm{P}_1\), and the coupled \(^{3}\mathrm{P}_2\)-\(^{3}\mathrm{F}_2\)) using chiral potentials computed up to NNLO with both dimensional regularization and spectral-function regularization, examining cutoffs from 500 MeV to 10 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The highest sensible LSE cutoff for a chiral potential computed with dimensional regularization is approximately 1 GeV, based on cutoff-dependence of phase shifts at \(T_{\mathrm{lab}} \approx 100\) MeV.
- For the one-pion-exchange potential alone, phase shifts in \(^{1}\mathrm{P}_1\) and \(^{3}\mathrm{P}_1\) become independent of cutoff once \(\Lambda > 2000\) MeV.
- The spectral-function regularization cutoff used for the two-pion-exchange potential was \(\tilde{\Lambda} = 800\) MeV.
- The nucleon mass used was \(M = 938.926\) MeV, the pion mass \(m_\pi = 138.03\) MeV, the axial coupling constant \(g_A = 1.29\) (for OPE) and \(g_A = 1.26\) (for TPE), and the pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV (for OPE) and \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV (for TPE).
- The \(\pi\)N low-energy constants used in the NNLO potential were \(c_1 = -0.81\), \(c_3 = -4.70\), and \(c_4 = 3.4\) (all in GeV\(^{-1}\)).

## Context

This work extends the leading-order analysis of Nogga, Timmermans, and van Kolck (2005), who showed that chiral effective theory is properly renormalized at LO in some P-waves but not in attractive singular tensor-force channels like \(^{3}\mathrm{P}_0\) and \(^{3}\mathrm{P}_2\)-\(^{3}\mathrm{F}_2\). The subtractive renormalization method allows the authors to examine cutoff dependence up to 10 GeV for NLO and NNLO potentials, going beyond the coordinate-space analysis of Pavon Valderrama and Ruiz Arriola (2006) and Entem (2007). The results enable more reliable error estimates for chiral potentials used in nuclear structure and reaction calculations by establishing the maximum sensible cutoff for non-perturbative treatment.
