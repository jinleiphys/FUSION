# On the off-diagonal Wick's theorem and Onishi formula

> The projected generator coordinate method based on the configuration mixing of non-orthogonal Bogoliubov product states, along with more advanced methods based on it, require the computation of off-diagonal Hamiltonian and norm kernels. While the Hamiltonian kernel is efficiently computed via the off-diagonal Wick theorem of Balian and Brezin, the norm kernel relies on the Onishi formula (or equivalently the Pfaffian formula by Robledo or the integral formula by Bally and Duguet). Traditionally, the derivation of these two categories of formulae rely on different formal schemes. In the present work, the formulae for the operator and norm kernels are computed consistently from the same diagrammatic method. The approach further offers the possibility to address kernels involving more general states in the future.

## Key claim

The paper provides a consistent derivation of both the off-diagonal Wick theorem (for connected operator kernels) and the Onishi/Pfaffian formula (for norm kernels) using a common diagrammatic method, whereas traditionally these two categories of formulae relied on different formal schemes. The symmetric approach used here, where both Bogoliubov states are expressed relative to a common reference state, is more general than the asymmetric approach but requires showing that the infinite series of terms generated can be factorized to recover the standard results.

## Method

The authors express two non-orthogonal Bogoliubov states \(|\Phi(l)\rangle\) and \(|\Phi(r)\rangle\) with respect to a common reference Bogoliubov vacuum \(|\Phi\rangle\) via Thouless' theorem, yielding exponentials of one-body excitation and de-excitation operators. They then compute the operator kernel \(\langle \Phi(l)|O|\Phi(r)\rangle\) and norm kernel \(\langle \Phi(l)|\Phi(r)\rangle\) by expanding these exponentials and applying standard Wick's theorem with respect to the reference state, using diagrammatic rules to organize the contractions. The norm kernel is shown to factorize out of the operator kernel, and the connected operator kernel is expressed as a sum over connected diagrams whose topology is constrained by the one-body character of the Thouless operators, ultimately recovering the off-diagonal Wick theorem.

## Key numbers

* The condition \(s - t = (i - j)/2\) (Eq. 4.8) must be satisfied for each connected matrix element \(\langle _s|^i \, _j| ^t \rangle_c\), where \(s\) and \(t\) count the number of \(\textbf{L}\) and \(\textbf{R}\) operators respectively, and \(i\) (\(j\)) is the number of creation (annihilation) operators in the normal-ordered component \(\textbf{O}^{ij}\).
* The combinatorial factor for a diagram with \(k\) normal strings is \(c(i,j,k) \equiv \frac{1}{k!}\frac{1}{(i-k)!}\frac{1}{(j-k)!}(i-k-1)!!(j-k-1)!!\), where \(i\) and \(j\) are the numbers of creation and annihilation operators in \(\textbf{O}^{ij}\).
* For the diagonal case where \(|\Phi(l)\rangle = |\Phi(r)\rangle = |\Phi\rangle\), the generalized density matrix reduces to \(\mathbf{R}_{k_1k_2} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 0 \\ 0 & \delta_{k_1k_2} \end{pmatrix}\) (Eq. 2.24).

## Context

This work extends the formal foundation of the projected generator coordinate method (PGCM), which requires off-diagonal Hamiltonian and norm kernels between non-orthogonal Bogoliubov product states. The authors provide a unified diagrammatic derivation that connects the off-diagonal Wick theorem of Balian and Brezin (1969) with the Onishi formula (1966), the Pfaffian formula by Robledo (2009), and the integral formula by Bally and Duguet (2017). The approach enables future extension to kernels involving more general states beyond the Bogoliubov vacuum manifold, which is relevant for advanced methods such as multi-reference perturbation theory based on a PGCM unperturbed state.
