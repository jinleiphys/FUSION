# Quarkonium Interactions in QCD

> Contents: 1. Introduction 2. Operator Product Expansion for Quarkonium Interactions 3. Scale Anomaly, Chiral Symmetry and Low-Energy Theorems 4. Quarkonium Interactions in Matter 5. Conclusions and Outlook

## Key claim

The paper argues that the absorption cross section of heavy quarkonia (J/ψ, Υ) by light hadrons rises very slowly from threshold, reaching its geometrical value only at very high energy, contrary to the common assumption that it is equal to its geometrical value even at low energy. This conclusion is derived from QCD operator product expansion and low-energy theorems, with the caveat that the analysis applies to heavy and strongly bound quarkonium states where both the quark masses and binding energies are much larger than ΛQCD.

## Method

The author uses the operator product expansion (OPE) to compute the forward scattering amplitude of heavy quarkonium with light hadrons, expanding the amplitude in terms of twist-two gluon field operators with Wilson coefficients computed for 1S, 2S, and 2P states. Sum rules are derived relating the quarkonium-hadron cross section to the gluon structure function of the light hadron, and low-energy theorems based on the scale anomaly and chiral symmetry are used to evaluate the amplitude in the limit where the incident hadron energy is much smaller than the quarkonium binding energy.

## Key numbers

- J/ψ radius: r_J/ψ ≃ 0.2 fm = (1 GeV)^{-1}; binding energy: E_J/ψ = 2 M_D - M_J/ψ ≃ 0.64 GeV
- J/ψ-nucleon total cross section near threshold: σ_JN(λ) ≃ 2.5 mb × (1 - λ_0/λ)^{6.5}, with λ_0 ≃ (M_N + ε_0)
- J/ψ-nucleon cross section at P_N ≃ 5 GeV: around 0.1 mb, more than an order of magnitude below its asymptotic value
- Υ-nucleon high-energy cross section: σ_ΥN ≃ 0.37 mb
- Wilson coefficient d_2 for 1S states: d_n^{(1S)} = (32/N)^2 √π Γ(n+5/2)/Γ(n+5)
- Pion-nucleon Σ-term: Σ_πN = 49 ± 7 MeV
- Strange quark contribution to proton: ⟨p| m_s s̄s |p⟩ ≃ 127 MeV (from m_s/(2m̂) × y × Σ_πN with y ≃ 0.2)

## Context

This work extends the short-distance QCD formalism developed by Bhanot and Peskin (1979) for quarkonium-hadron interactions, and applies it to the problem of quarkonium suppression in heavy ion collisions originally proposed by Matsui and Satz (1986). The paper provides a dynamical treatment that unifies quarkonium interactions with vacuum gluon fields, gluon fields confined inside light hadrons, and deconfined gluons, enabling a clear diagnostic test for deconfined matter based on the hardness of gluon distributions. The low-energy theorems derived here build on the work of Voloshin and Zakharov (1980) and Leutwyler, et al. on the scale anomaly and chiral symmetry.
