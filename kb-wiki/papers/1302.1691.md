# Viscous potential flow analysis of peripheral heavy ion collisions

> The conditions for the development of a Kelvin-Helmholtz Instability (KHI) for the Quark-gluon Plasma (QGP) flow in a peripheral heavy-ion collision is investigated. The projectile and target side particles are separated by an energetically motivated hypothetical surface, characterized with a phenomenological surface tension. In such a view, a classical potential flow approximation is considered and the onset of the KHI is studied. The growth rate of the instability is computed as function of phenomenological parameters characteristic for the QGP fluid: viscosity, surface tension and flow layer thickness.

## Key claim

The paper presents a strongly idealized analytic model for the development of the Kelvin-Helmholtz Instability (KHI) in ultra-relativistic heavy ion reactions, compressing the shear zone into a central infinitesimal layer and assuming the remaining flow can be approximated as potential flow; the model shows that critical size KHI may occur for low viscosity QGP, but only under the condition that the relative velocity squared exceeds a threshold set by surface tension, wave number, and density.

## Method

The authors model the initial state of a peripheral heavy ion collision as two fluid layers (top and bottom) with uniform velocities \(U_t\) and \(U_b\) separated by a hypothetical dividing surface with a phenomenological surface tension \(\gamma\). They assume a two-dimensional, non-relativistic potential flow in the reaction plane, with the shear and vorticity constrained to the dividing plane, and solve the Laplace equation for the velocity potential in each layer. The growth rate \(\sigma\) of a wave-like perturbation on the interface is derived from the linearized equations of motion, including viscosity \(\eta\) and surface tension, leading to a dispersion relation that yields the real part of the growth rate \(\sigma_R\) as a function of \(\eta\), \(\gamma\), wave number \(k\), and layer half-thickness \(l\).

## Key numbers

- The effective mass density of the QGP is taken as \(\rho = 10\ \mathrm{GeV/fm^3 c^2}\).
- For the dominant wave number, the paper uses \(k \approx 0.6\ \mathrm{fm^{-1}}\) (initially \(k \approx 1\ \mathrm{fm^{-1}}\)) and a relative velocity \(|U_t - U_b| = 0.8c\).
- The critical surface tension above which the growth rate becomes negative is \(\gamma_{crit} \approx 5.3\ \mathrm{GeV/fm^2}\), independent of viscosity.
- For impact parameter \(b = 0.5 b_{max}\) (with \(b_{max} = 14\ \mathrm{fm}\) for Pb+Pb), the minimum wave number is \(k_{min} = 0.598\ \mathrm{fm^{-1}}\) and the maximum wave number is \(k_{max} = 1.79\ \mathrm{fm^{-1}}\); for \(b = 0.7 b_{max}\), \(k_{min} = 0.479\ \mathrm{fm^{-1}}\) and \(k_{max} = 2.51\ \mathrm{fm^{-1}}\).
- The surface tension is estimated from an energy balance argument as \(\gamma = 0.4\ \mathrm{GeV/fm^2}\) for the examples shown.
- The growth rate \(\sigma_R\) is shown as a function of viscosity \(\eta\) for \(\gamma = 0.4\ \mathrm{GeV/fm^2}\) and \(\gamma = 3.5\ \mathrm{GeV/fm^2}\) at \(k = 0.6\ \mathrm{fm^{-1}}\); the growth rate decreases with increasing viscosity.

## Context

This work extends the numerical fluid dynamical studies of KHI in heavy ion collisions by Csernai et al. (2011) by providing an analytic potential-flow model that isolates the roles of viscosity, surface tension, and layer thickness. The approach follows the idealization of Funada and Joseph (2001) for classical KHI, compressing the shear zone into a plane. The results confirm that KHI can develop in low-viscosity QGP under conditions similar to those seen in high-resolution relativistic fluid dynamical simulations, and they enable future studies of how the effective surface tension from shear energy influences the instability threshold and growth rate in expanding systems.
