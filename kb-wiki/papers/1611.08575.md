# Event Shape Sorting: selecting events with similar evolution

> We present novel method for the organisation of events. The method is based on comparing event-by-event histograms of a chosen quantity Q that is measured for each particle in every event. The events are organised in such a way that those with similar shape of the Q-histograms end-up placed close to each other. We apply the method on histograms of azimuthal angle of the produced hadrons in ultrarelativsitic nuclear collisions. By selecting events with similar azimuthal shape of their hadron distribution one chooses events which are likely that they underwent similar evolution from the initial state to the freeze-out. Such events can more easily be compared to theoretical simulations where all conditions can be controlled. We illustrate the method on data simulated by the AMPT model.

# Event Shape Sorting: selecting events with similar evolution

## Key claim

The authors propose a novel method for sorting heavy-ion collision events so that events with similar azimuthal hadron distributions are placed close together, enabling more exclusive comparisons to theoretical simulations. However, the method does not determine *how* the selected events actually look; it only guarantees similarity within a group, and the authors have not yet quantified the degree of similarity between events.

## Method

Each event is represented by a histogram of the azimuthal angle distribution of produced hadrons. An iterative Bayesian algorithm divides events into deciles, computes average histograms per decile, calculates for each event the conditional probability of belonging to each decile, and re-sorts events by the mean decile index until convergence. Before sorting, events are rotated to align their second-order event planes ψ₂.

## Key numbers

- 5000 artificial Monte Carlo events were generated with elliptic anisotropy only, multiplicities between 300 and 3000, and v₂ correlated with multiplicity via parameters a = -7.099×10⁻⁸, b = 20.06×10⁻⁵, c = 0.07874, with a Gaussian random addition of width 0.25.
- 2000 AMPT events of Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV and 0–20% centrality class were analyzed.
- The correlation between the sorting variable μ̄ and v₂ is pronounced for μ̄ above 8; below that, ordering is determined by other features.

## Context

This work extends the spirit of Event Shape Engineering (ESE), which selects events based on a single variable such as the flow vector q₂, by instead considering the full shape of the azimuthal distribution. The method was previously used for statistical analysis of other data types (Andy, Syne). It enables more exclusive studies where higher-order anisotropies remain visible and can be compared directly to simulations without averaging over many events, and may eventually support single-event femtoscopy by providing similar events for mixed-event background construction.
