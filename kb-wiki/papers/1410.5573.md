# Dissipation dynamics and spin-orbit force in time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory

> We investigate the one-body dissipation dynamics in heavy-ion collisions of $^{16}{\rm O}$+$^{16}{\rm O}$ using a fully three-dimensional time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory with the modern Skyrme energy functional and without any symmetry restrictions. The energy dissipation is revealed to decrease in deep-inelastic collisions of the light systems as the bombarding energy increases owing to the competition between collective motion and single-particle degrees of freedom. The role of spin-orbit force is given particular emphasis in deep-inelastic collisions. The spin-orbit force causes a significant enhancement of the dissipation. The time-even coupling of spin-orbit force plays a dominant role at low energies, while the influence of time-odd terms is notable at high energies. About 40-65\% of the total dissipation depending on the different parameter sets is predicted to arise from the spin-orbit force. The theoretical fusion cross section has a reasonably good agreement with the experimental data, considering that no free parameters are adjusted to reaction dynamics in the TDHF approach.

## Key claim

The spin-orbit force causes a significant enhancement of one-body energy dissipation in deep-inelastic collisions of light systems, with about 40-65% of the total dissipation arising from the spin-orbit force depending on the Skyrme parameter set; however, this result is obtained within a framework that neglects two-body dissipation, which is likely to play a significant role at the higher energies studied.

## Method

Fully three-dimensional time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations were performed for head-on collisions of 16O+16O using the Sky3D code with three Skyrme energy functionals (SLy4, SkM*, and UNEDF1) and no symmetry restrictions. The final relative kinetic energy of the separating ions was extracted as a function of initial center-of-mass energy, and the percentage of energy dissipation was computed by comparing final to initial kinetic energy. The contributions of the time-even and time-odd terms of the spin-orbit force were isolated by performing separate TDHF calculations with full spin-orbit, time-even-only spin-orbit, and no spin-orbit force.

## Key numbers

- For head-on collisions of 16O+16O, the percentage of dissipated energy coming from the spin-orbit force reaches a maximum of 65% for SkM*, 63% for SLy4, and 48% for UNEDF1.
- The fusion cross section at a center-of-mass energy of 70.5 MeV is 1282 mb (SLy4), 1287 mb (SkM*), and 1327 mb (UNEDF1), compared to the experimental value of 1056 ± 125 mb.
- For SLy4 at 70.5 MeV, the fusion window for impact parameter is b_min = 1.2 fm and b_max = 6.5 fm, with a transparency cross section of 45 mb.
- The numerical box size is 32×24×24 fm³, with grid spacing 1 fm and time step 0.2 fm/c; the shift of particle number and total energy during time evolution is less than 0.01 and 0.1 MeV, respectively.

## Context

This work extends earlier TDHF studies of heavy-ion collisions that systematically underestimated dissipation due to simplified effective interactions and symmetry restrictions, such as those by Krieger (1978), Davies (1978, 1981), and Maruhn (1985). The inclusion of the full spin-orbit force (both time-even and time-odd terms) in a fully three-dimensional calculation without symmetry restrictions resolves the earlier puzzle of the fusion window anomaly identified by Umar (1986). The results enable systematic comparison with experimental fusion cross sections and provide a baseline for future beyond-TDHF calculations that include two-body dissipation and dynamical fluctuations, such as the stochastic TDHF approach of Ayik (2009) and the Balian-Veneroni variational principle applied by Simenel (2011).
