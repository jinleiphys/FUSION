# Benchmarking $Λ$NN three-body forces and first predictions for A=3-5 hypernuclei

> Explicit expressions for the leading chiral hyperon-nucleon-nucleon three-body forces have been derived by Petschauer et al [Phys. Rev. C93.014001 (2016)]. An important prerequisite for including these three-body forces in few- and many-body calculations is the accuracy and efficiency of their partial-wave decomposition. A careful benchmark of the ΛNN potential matrix elements, computed using two robust and efficient partial-wave decomposition methods, is presented. In addition, results of a first quantitative assessment for the contributions of $Λ$NN forces to the separation energies in A=3-5 hypernuclei are reported.

## Key claim

The paper benchmarks two independent partial-wave decomposition methods for chiral ΛNN three-body forces and provides a first quantitative assessment of their contributions to separation energies in A=3-5 hypernuclei, but the low-energy constants (LECs) characterizing the ΛNN force are estimated via decuplet saturation rather than fitted to experimental data, leaving the actual strength of the three-body force as an open parameter.

## Method

The authors compare two partial-wave decomposition approaches for the chiral ΛNN potential at next-to-next-to-leading order: a "local" method (lPWD) that exploits the locality of the potentials to reduce the eight-fold angular integration to two-fold, and an "automatic" method (aPWD) that uses symbolic computation to generate analytic spin-isospin matrix elements and numerically performs a five-fold angular integration. The benchmarked matrix elements are then used in Faddeev (KKM group) and Jacobi no-core shell model (JBG group) calculations to compute separation energies for hypertriton, \(^4_\Lambda\)H, \(^4_\Lambda\)He, and \(^5_\Lambda\)He.

## Key numbers

- The 2π-exchange ΛNN matrix elements at fixed momenta \(p'_{12}=p_{12}=q_3=0.205507\) fm\(^{-1}\) and \(q'_3=0.306967\) fm\(^{-1}\) agree between aPWD and lPWD to within 0.06% or better for all tested partial-wave states with \((J^\pi,T)=(1/2^+,0)\) and \((3/2^+,0)\)
- The 1π-exchange and contact ΛNN matrix elements at the same momenta agree to within 0.4% or better for \((J^\pi,T)=(1/2^+,0)\)
- The sub-leading meson-baryon LECs in the 2π-exchange potential are set to \(3b_0+b_D=0\) and \(2b_2+3b_4=-3.0\times10^{-3}\) MeV\(^{-1}\) for the benchmark
- The LECs in the decuplet saturation scheme are set to \(D'_2=0.6268\) fm\(^3\) and \(C'_1=C'_3=0.1852\) fm\(^5\) with \(C=3/4\,g_A=0.9525\), \(f_0=93\) MeV, and \(\Delta=300\) MeV
- The non-local regularization cutoff is set to \(\Lambda=550\) MeV for the matrix element comparisons

## Context

This work extends the benchmark tradition in few-nucleon physics (Nogga 2002, Kamada 2001) to the hypernuclear sector, where previous comparisons were limited to simple NN and YN interactions (Ferrari Ruffino 2017) or to two-body forces only (Le 2020). The benchmarked partial-wave decomposition methods and the first quantitative assessment of chiral ΛNN forces in A=3-5 hypernuclei enable future fits of the unknown LECs to s-shell hypernuclei binding energies, which the authors state is beyond the scope of this paper and will be studied in a separate application.
