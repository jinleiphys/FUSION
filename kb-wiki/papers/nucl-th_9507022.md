# Bulk properties of nuclear matter in the relativistic Hartree approximation with cut-off regularization

> A method of cut-off regularization is proposed to evaluate vacuum corrections in nuclear matter in the framework of the Hartree approximation. Bulk properties of nuclear matter calculated by this method are a good agreement with results analyzed by empirical values. The vacuum effect is quantitatively evaluated through a cut-off parameter and its role for saturation property and compressional properties is clarified.

## Key claim

The authors propose a cut-off regularization method for evaluating vacuum corrections in nuclear matter within the relativistic Hartree approximation, claiming that this method can produce nuclear incompressibility values ranging from 200 MeV to 546 MeV, but only if the cut-off parameter is chosen to satisfy the saturation condition at normal density.

## Method

The authors introduce a cut-off parameter Lambda into the divergent Feynman integrals for the scalar self-energy and baryon energy density in the Walecka sigma-omega model, then renormalize the bare nucleon mass so that the physical mass is independent of Lambda. They enforce the Hugenholtz-von Hove theorem to relate the scalar self-energy and baryon energy density in the Feynman part, and determine the cut-off parameter by requiring saturation at normal density (binding energy -15.75 MeV at Fermi momentum 1.35 fm^-1).

## Key numbers

* Nuclear incompressibility K ranges from 546 MeV (Lambda/M = 0.0000, no vacuum effect) down to 200 MeV (Lambda/M = 0.5624)
* Effective nucleon mass M*/M varies from 0.5470 (K = 546 MeV) to 0.5044 (K = 200 MeV), with a maximum of 0.5924 at K = 450 MeV (Lambda/M = 0.3884)
* Symmetry energy a4 ranges from 29.0 MeV to 31.6 MeV across the parameter sets, with C_rho^2 = 54.71
* The Coulomb coefficient K_c ranges from -8.971 MeV (K = 546 MeV) to +3.972 MeV (K = 200 MeV)
* The volume-symmetry coefficient K_vs ranges from -890.4 MeV (K = 546 MeV) to +1142.4 MeV (K = 200 MeV)
* The cut-off parameter Lambda is related to a monopole form factor cut-off by Lambda_N^2 = M^2 + Lambda^2

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA) of Chin (1977) by replacing dimensional regularization with a cut-off regularization that accounts for the finite size of hadrons, addressing the problem that dimensional regularization may overestimate vacuum corrections. The method provides a phenomenological alternative to introducing form factors at vertices as done by Prakash, Ellis and Kapusta (1992) and others, and enables systematic study of the correlation between effective mass, incompressibility, and the Coulomb and symmetry coefficients K_c and K_vs, which can be compared to empirical giant monopole resonance data from Pearson (1991) and Shlomo and Youngblood (1993).
