# Test of OPE and OGE through mixing angles of negative parity N* resonances in electromagnetic transitions

> In this report, by using the mixing angles of one-gluon-exchange model(OGE) and one-pion-exchange model(OPE), and by using the electromagnetic Hamiltonian of Close and Li, we calculate the amplitudes of L=1 N* resonances for photoproduction and electroproduction. The results are compared to experimental data. It's found that the data support OGE, not OPE.

## Key claim

The paper finds that photoproduction and electroproduction amplitudes of negative-parity N* resonances, calculated using mixing angles from the one-gluon-exchange (OGE) model, fit experimental data significantly better than those calculated using mixing angles from the one-pion-exchange (OPE) model, with the conclusion holding even when mixing angles are varied by ±10 degrees.

## Method

The authors used the mixing angles for L=1 N* resonances derived by Chizma and Karl from OGE (θ₁/₂ = -32°, θ₃/₂ = 6°) and OPE (θ₁/₂ = 25.5°, θ₃/₂ = -52.7°) models, combined with the electromagnetic Hamiltonian of Close and Li, to calculate transition amplitudes from the nucleon ground state to negative-parity N* resonances in both photoproduction (Q²=0) and electroproduction (Q²>0). The calculations were performed in the Breit-frame using harmonic-oscillator wavefunctions with parameters α=0.5 GeV and m*=0.437 GeV, and the results were compared to experimental data from the Particle Data Group using χ² statistics.

## Key numbers

- OPE mixing angles: θ₁/₂ = 25.5°, θ₃/₂ = -52.7°
- OGE mixing angles: θ₁/₂ = -32°, θ₃/₂ = 6°
- Sum χ² for photoproduction amplitudes: no-admixture = 8.0, OPE = 84.6, OGE = 6.2
- For N(1520) Aᵖ₃/₂: OGE gives 142 (expt. 166±5), OPE gives 55 (expt. 166±5), in units of 10⁻³ GeV¹/²
- For N(1650) Aᵖ₁/₂: OGE gives 81 (expt. 53±16), OPE gives -35 (expt. 53±16), in units of 10⁻³ GeV¹/²
- Sum χ² for OGE varies between 5.1 and 11.3 when mixing angles are changed by ±10°
- Sum χ² for OPE varies between 57.5 and 117.5 when mixing angles are changed by ±10°
- Parameters used: α = 0.5 GeV, m* = 0.437 GeV, M_T = 3m*

## Context

This work extends the debate between Isgur (2000) and Glozman and Riska over whether baryon spectroscopy is better described by one-gluon-exchange or one-pion-exchange models, moving beyond spectrum predictions to test the models against electromagnetic transition data. The authors use mixing angles from Chizma and Karl (2002) and the electromagnetic Hamiltonian of Close and Li (1990), finding that OGE provides consistent mixing angles to explain spectrum, decay branching, and electromagnetic production amplitudes simultaneously, while OPE fails to reproduce the photoproduction data. This enables future work to use electromagnetic transitions as a more stringent test of quark interaction models than spectroscopy alone.
