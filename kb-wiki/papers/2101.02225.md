# Forward scattering in a thermal Plasma

> I examine the regime of forward scattering of an energetic particle in a Plasma medium in thermal equilibrium. Treating the particle as an open quantum system interacting with a bath, I look at the time evolution of the reduced density matrix of the system. The kinematic and dynamical time scales that emerge can exist in several possible hierarchies which can lead to different EFT formulations. I show that in certain hierarchies, it becomes necessary to account for arbitrary number of coherent exchanges between the system and the bath going beyond the independent scattering paradigm. Analytic results are obtained in certain limits and the formalism is applied for the measurement of transverse momentum broadening of a quark in a Quark Gluon Plasma medium.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for forward scattering of an energetic particle in a thermal plasma, the hierarchy between the interaction time scale of the system with the medium and other dynamical time scales determines whether the independent scattering approximation suffices or whether arbitrary numbers of coherent Glauber exchanges must be resummed, going beyond the independent scattering paradigm.

## Method

The author treats the energetic particle as an open quantum system interacting with a thermal bath, using the real-time formalism of thermal field theory within an effective field theory (EFT) for forward scattering developed in Rothstein (2016). The Glauber mode mediates forward interactions between collinear jet partons and soft medium partons, and the time evolution of the reduced density matrix of the jet is computed order by order in the Glauber Hamiltonian, focusing on the transverse momentum broadening observable for a quark in a Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) medium.

## Key numbers

* The collinear momentum scales as \(p_c^\mu \sim Q(1, \lambda^2, \lambda)\) and the soft momentum scales as \(p_s^\mu \sim Q(\lambda, \lambda, \lambda)\), where \(\lambda = \theta \ll 1\) is the scattering angle expansion parameter and \(Q\) is the hard scale (quark energy).
* The medium-induced gluon mass is \(m_D \sim gT\), where \(T\) is the QGP temperature, with \(T \ll Q\).
* The correlation time scale of the thermal bath is \(t_e \sim 1/T\).
* The inverse interaction time of the system with the medium is defined as \(t_I = \left( \int d^2 k_\perp F(k_\perp) \right)^{-1}\), where \(F(k_\perp)\) is derived from the \(O(H_G^2)\) Glauber exchange kernel.

## Context

This work extends the EFT formulation for forward scattering developed by Rothstein (2016) and the subsequent application to transverse momentum broadening by Vaidya (2020), which used the Lindblad equation to resum multiple interactions under the independent scattering approximation. The paper explores all classes of Glauber exchanges beyond that approximation, examining how different hierarchies of time scales (interaction time, propagation time, and thermal correlation time) lead to different EFT formulations. This enables future incorporation into factorization formulas for jet substructure observables in heavy ion collisions, where jets serve as hard probes of the QGP medium.
