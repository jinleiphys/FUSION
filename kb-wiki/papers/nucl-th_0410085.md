# Hadron Correlations from Recombination and Fragmentation

> We review the formalism of quark recombination applied to the hadronization of a quark gluon plasma. Evidence in favor of the quark recombination model is outlined. Recent work on parton correlations, leading to detectable correlations between hadrons, is discussed. Hot spots from completely quenched jets are a likely source of such correlations which appear to be jet-like. It will be discussed how such a picture compares with measurement of associated hadron yields at RHIC.

# Hadron Correlations from Recombination and Fragmentation

## Key claim
Quark recombination can produce jet-like hadron correlations at intermediate transverse momentum (2–5 GeV/c) without contradicting the recombination picture, provided that small (~8%) parton-level correlations are introduced; the observed near-side associated yields in Au+Au collisions at RHIC can be reproduced by a combination of fragmentation and recombination with correlated soft partons, without requiring soft-hard recombination as the dominant source.

## Method
The authors extend the standard recombination formalism by introducing a 2-parton correlation function C_{ij} into the 4-parton Wigner function, using a Gaussian ansatz in azimuthal angle and rapidity motivated by hot spots from quenched jets. They compute 2-meson yields from three production channels, fragmentation-fragmentation (F-F), soft-soft recombination (SS-SS), and fragmentation-soft-hard recombination (F-SH), and compare the resulting near-side associated yields per trigger particle to PHENIX data for central and peripheral Au+Au collisions at √s=200 GeV.

## Key numbers
- Proton/pion ratio ≈ 1 between 2 and 4 GeV/c in central Au+Au collisions, compared to ≈ 0.2 predicted by leading-twist pQCD and fragmentation.
- Recombination dominates hadron production up to about 4 GeV/c for mesons and up to about 6 GeV/c for baryons in central Au+Au collisions.
- The amplification factor for 2-parton correlations in dimeson production is Q = n_A n_B = 4 for meson-meson pairs, 6 for meson-baryon pairs, and 9 for baryon-baryon pairs.
- A parton-level correlation strength of ĉ₀ ≈ 0.08 reproduces the observed near-side associated yields when the correlation volume is approximately constant (V_c ∼ constant) rather than scaling with N_part.
- The azimuthal correlation width is fixed to φ₀ = 0.2 in all channels, in rough agreement with experimental data.
- Energy loss for a 10 GeV parton is estimated to be up to 14 GeV/fm in the medium.

## Context
This work extends the recombination formalism of Fries, Müller, Nonaka, and Bass (2003) by introducing parton correlations that were previously neglected due to the factorization approximation W_{1,…,n} = ∏ w_i. It addresses the challenge posed by dihadron correlation measurements from STAR (2002) and PHENIX (2004), which some argued contradicted the recombination picture. The results enable a unified description of single hadron spectra, elliptic flow, and hadron correlations within the same framework, and suggest that hot spots from completely quenched jets are a likely source of jet-like correlations in the medium. The quark counting rule for elliptic flow (v₂/n scaling with P_T/n) is identified as a potential cornerstone for establishing the existence of a quark-gluon plasma at RHIC.
