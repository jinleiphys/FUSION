# QCD Vacuum Energy and Its Implication for Quark Nugget Stability

> In this work, we employ both theoretical and data-driven methods to derive the QCD vacuum energy, utilizing the GMOR relation, the low-energy theorem, and the equation of state from Lattice QCD. The QCD vacuum energy is determined to be between around $(163\,\mbox{MeV})^4$ and $(190\,\mbox{MeV})^4$. With the assumptions of complete deconfinement, vanishing gluon condensate, full chiral-symmetry restoration, and the validity of perturbative QCD at baryon chemical potentials of order of the proton mass, a very specific kind of quark nugget is found to be less stable than ordinary nuclei.

## Key claim

Using the GMOR relation, the NSVZ low-energy theorem, and fits to lattice QCD equations of state, the QCD vacuum energy is constrained to lie between approximately (163 MeV)^4 and (190 MeV)^4; under the specific assumptions of complete deconfinement, vanishing gluon condensate, full chiral-symmetry restoration, and the validity of perturbative QCD at baryon chemical potentials of order the proton mass, quark nuggets of a particular type are found to be less stable than ordinary nuclei.

## Method

The authors combined three approaches to bound the QCD vacuum energy: (1) the GMOR relation to extract the fermionic contribution from quark condensates using measured pion mass and decay constant; (2) the NSVZ low-energy theorem together with lattice QCD calculations of the topological susceptibility to derive a lower bound on the gluonic contribution; and (3) fits of the hard-thermal-loop perturbative QCD (HpQCD) equation of state to finite-temperature lattice QCD trace anomaly data from the Wuppertal-Budapest, HotQCD, and Bazavov et al. collaborations, with a fitted nonperturbative parameter at order g^6. For quark nugget stability, the authors used the O(g^4) perturbative QCD formalism at finite baryon chemical potential to compute the energy per baryon under the assumption of complete deconfinement and zero condensates inside the nugget.

## Key numbers

* QCD vacuum energy lower bound from GMOR relation (lattice strange condensate) plus low-energy theorem: (163 MeV)^4
* QCD vacuum energy lower bound from GMOR relation (sum rule average strange condensate) plus low-energy theorem: (150 MeV)^4
* QCD vacuum energy upper bound from finite-temperature lattice QCD plus HpQCD fit: (190 MeV)^4
* Two-flavor QCD vacuum energy lower bound from GMOR plus low-energy theorem: (119 MeV)^4
* Two-flavor QCD vacuum energy upper bound from isospin-dense lattice QCD plus pQCD plus color superconductivity: (160 MeV)^4
* Topological susceptibility from lattice QCD used in the low-energy theorem bound: (75.6 ± 2.0 MeV)^4
* Fermionic contribution from light quarks via GMOR relation: B^{con}_{F,2} = (76.8 MeV)^4
* Fermionic contribution from 2+1 flavors via GMOR relation (lattice): (153 MeV)^4; (sum rule average): (136 ± 5.0 MeV)^4
* Gluonic contribution lower bound from low-energy theorem: B^{con}_G ≥ (113.4 MeV)^4
* Stability threshold for quark nuggets: energy per baryon ε/n_B ≤ 930 MeV (the averaged mass per nucleon of iron)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Bai and collaborators (2024) on the QCD vacuum energy and its relation to quark matter stability, using the GMOR relation and isospin-dense lattice QCD fits. It provides combined lower bounds from the GMOR relation and the NSVZ low-energy theorem, and upper bounds from finite-temperature lattice QCD fits, which together constrain the QCD vacuum energy more tightly than previous individual determinations. The results enable a quantitative assessment of whether quark nuggets of the type proposed by Bodmer (1971), Lee and collaborators (1974-1977), and Witten (1984) can be stable, and suggest that under the assumptions of complete deconfinement and full chiral symmetry restoration, such nuggets are less stable than ordinary nuclei, contrary to some earlier expectations.
