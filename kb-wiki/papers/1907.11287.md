# Study of collective anisotropies $v_2$ and $v_3$ and their fluctuations in $pA$ collisions at LHC within a relativistic transport approach

> We have developed a relativistic transport approach at fixed $η/s(T)$ that incorporates initial space fluctuations generated by wounded quark model to study the hadron observables in 5.02 TeV p+Pb collisions. We find that our approach is able to correctly predict quite well several existing experimental measurements assuming a matter with $η/s=1/4π$, a result similar to previous studies within a viscous hydrodynamics approach. Besides, we further discuss the sensitivity of the results on both $η/s(T)$ and the smearing width. Our transport approach has the possibility to include in initial conditions the power law tail associated to minijet, and this improvement extends the agreement with the experimental data to higher $p_T$ ranges. We also perform a comparison to Pb+Pb collisions pointing out that even if the collective flows have a similar magnitude the features of the matter created are different. By studying the correlation between collective flows and initial geometry, we find that the correlation decreases faster in small systems with the increase of $n$ and centrality. In particular we show that the variance of $σ_{v_n}/\langle v_n\rangle$ has a quite different evolution with centrality for p+Pb, so their measurement could provide some further hint about the correctness of current modelling.

## Key claim

The authors show that a relativistic transport approach with a fixed shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of η/s = 1/4π can reproduce experimental measurements of collective flow in 5.02 TeV p+Pb collisions, but they note that the quantitative agreement can be tuned by the size of the spatial fluctuations (the Gaussian width σ), which limits the ability to constrain η/s without independent constraints on initial state fluctuations.

## Method

The authors solve the Relativistic Boltzmann Transport equation using a test particle method with a collision integral tuned locally to a specific η/s value via the Chapmann-Enskog approximation. Initial conditions are generated event-by-event using a wounded quark model with a Monte Carlo Glauber approach, where three constituent quarks are placed within each nucleon and the transverse density is smeared with a Gaussian width σ. The system is evolved from a thermalization time τ₀ = 0.4 fm/c, and final hadrons are recovered using a parton-hadron duality ansatz.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s = 1/4π (approximately 0.08) is used as the primary value, with comparisons to η/s = 2/4π
- Gaussian width σ for parton transverse density fluctuations is varied between 0.4 and 0.6 fm, with σ = 0.55 fm used for the main results
- Thermalization time τ₀ = 0.4 fm/c
- Constituent quark Gaussian width within nucleons: r₀ = 0.3 fm
- Woods-Saxon parameters for Pb nuclei: R₀ = 6.5 fm, a = 0.54 fm
- Quark-quark cross section σ_qq = 13.6 mb for 5.02 TeV p+Pb collisions
- Negative binomial distribution parameters: κ = 0.54, n̄ = 3.9, n₀ ≈ 2.352
- Parton degrees of freedom: g = 52 (three flavor quarks and gluons)
- Parton mass m = 0.3 GeV
- Simulation convergence requires N_test = 4000 test particles and grid spacing Δx = Δy = 0.15 fm
- The ratio v_n(4πη/s=2)/v_n(4πη/s=1) shows about 10% effect for v₂ and 20% for v₃ in Pb+Pb, while in p+Pb the effect is about twice as large at p_T ≈ 1 GeV/c
- ⟨ε₂⟩ ≈ 0.28 in p+Pb collisions, showing a flat behavior with centrality

## Context

This work extends previous studies of collective flow in small systems using viscous hydrodynamics (Bozek 2011, Shen 2016) and transport approaches (Bzdak 2014, Greif 2017) by employing a relativistic transport approach that can naturally include non-equilibrium minijet distributions. The authors compare their results to Pb+Pb collisions studied in their previous work (Plumari 2015) and find that the correlation between final flow and initial geometry decreases faster in p+Pb than in Pb+Pb collisions. The approach enables future studies of whether the expanding phase in pA collisions is fully hydrodynamical or consistent with a transport evolution where non-hydrodynamical collective excitations are relevant, as recently analyzed by Kurkela (2019) using conformal kinetic theory.
