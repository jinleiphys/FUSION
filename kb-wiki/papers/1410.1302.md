# Regularization of zero-range effective interactions in finite nuclei

> The problem of the divergences which arise in beyond mean-field calculations, when a zero-range effective interaction is employed, has not been much considered so far. Some of us have proposed, quite recently, a scheme to regularize a zero-range Skyrme-type force when it is employed to calculate the total energy, at second-order perturbation theory level, in uniform matter. Although this scheme looked promising, the extension for finite nuclei is not straightforward. We introduce such procedure in the current paper, by proposing a regularization procedure that is similar, in spirit, to the one employed to extract the so-called V_{\rm low-k} from the bare force. Although this has been suggested already by B.G. Carlsson and collaborators, the novelty of our work consists in setting on equal footing uniform matter and finite nuclei; in particular, we show how the interactions that have been regularized in uniform matter behave when they are used in a finite nucleus with the corresponding cutoff. We also address the problem of the validity of the perturbative approach in finite nuclei for the total energy.

## Key claim

The authors propose a regularization procedure for zero-range Skyrme-type effective interactions in finite nuclei that is analogous to the $V_{\rm low-k}$ approach, showing that interactions regularized in uniform matter can be consistently applied to finite nuclei with a corresponding cutoff, though the extension from uniform matter to finite systems is not straightforward.

## Method

The authors introduce a cutoff $\lambda$ on the relative momenta of initial and final two-particle states, defined through center-of-mass and relative coordinate transformations, and implement this regularization in a harmonic oscillator basis using Brody-Moshinsky transformations. They compute the Hartree-Fock and second-order perturbative contributions to the total energy of $^{16}$O using a simplified Skyrme interaction (keeping only $t_0$, $t_3$, and $\alpha$ parameters) that had been previously fitted with a cutoff regularization in uniform symmetric nuclear matter. The relationship between the cutoff on relative momenta $\lambda$ and the cutoff on transferred momentum $\Lambda$ used in uniform matter is established as $\lambda = \sqrt{2}\Lambda$.

## Context

This work extends the regularization scheme previously developed for uniform matter by the same group (Mog. 1, Mog. 2) to finite nuclei, addressing divergences that arise in beyond-mean-field calculations like particle-vibration coupling (PVC) when zero-range Skyrme-type forces are used. The approach is similar in spirit to the $V_{\rm low-k}$ method and follows suggestions by Carlsson and collaborators (2013), but is novel in treating uniform matter and finite nuclei on equal footing. This enables future work where effective forces could be fitted at second order and beyond, rather than relying on interactions fitted only at the mean-field level.
