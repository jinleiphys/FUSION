# $K^-d\rightarrow πΣn$ reactions and structure of the $Λ(1405)$

> We report on the first results of a full three-body calculation of the $\bar{K}NN$-$πYN$ amplitude for the $K^-d\rightarrowπΣn$ reaction, and examine how the $Λ(1405)$ resonance manifests itself in the neutron energy distributions of $K^-d\rightarrowπΣn$ reactions. The amplitudes are computed using the $\bar{K}NN$-$πYN$ coupled-channels Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations. Two types of models are considered for the two-body meson-baryon interactions: an energy-independent interaction and an energy-dependent one, both derived from the leading order chiral SU(3) Lagrangian. These two models have different off-shell properties that cause correspondingly different behaviors in the three-body system. As a remarkable result of this investigation, it is found that the neutron energy spectrum, reflecting the $Λ(1405)$ mass distribution and width, depends quite sensitively on the (energy-dependent or energy-independent) model used. Hence accurate measurements of the $πΣ$ mass distribution have the potential to discriminate between possible mechanisms at work in the formation of the $Λ(1405)$.

## Key claim

The neutron energy spectrum in the $K^-d\rightarrow \pi\Sigma n$ reaction, which reflects the $\Lambda(1405)$ mass distribution and width, depends quite sensitively on whether an energy-dependent or energy-independent two-body interaction model is used, meaning that accurate measurements of the $\pi\Sigma$ mass distribution can discriminate between the single-pole and two-pole descriptions of the $\Lambda(1405)$.

## Method

The authors solved the $\bar{K}NN$-$\pi YN$ coupled-channels Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations for the three-body $K^-d\rightarrow \pi\Sigma n$ reaction, using separable two-body interactions derived from the leading-order chiral SU(3) Lagrangian. Two models were compared: an energy-dependent interaction (E-dep) and an energy-independent interaction (E-indep), which differ in their off-shell properties and produce different pole structures for the $\Lambda(1405)$. The differential cross section $d\sigma/dE_n$ was computed for an initial kaon laboratory momentum of $p_{K^-}^{lab}=1000$ MeV, with the neutron energy $E_n$ defined in the $\pi\Sigma$ center-of-mass frame.

## Key numbers

- Initial kaon laboratory momentum: $p_{K^-}^{lab}=1000$ MeV, chosen in accordance with the planned J-PARC experiment.
- For the E-dep model, peak structures appear at $E_n\sim 17$–$30$ MeV (depending on the $\pi\Sigma$ charge combination), with a calculated $\Lambda(1405)$ binding energy of $E_B\sim 13$ MeV.
- For the E-indep model, peaks or bumps appear at $E_n\sim 32$–$38$ MeV, with a calculated $\Lambda(1405)$ binding energy of $E_B\sim 28$ MeV.
- The magnitude of the differential cross section for the E-dep model is twice larger than that for the E-indep model.
- Cutoff parameters for the $\bar{K}N$-$\pi Y$ interaction: for the E-dep model, $\Lambda^{I=0}_{\bar{K}N}=1000$ MeV, $\Lambda^{I=0}_{\pi\Sigma}=700$ MeV, $\Lambda^{I=1}_{\bar{K}N}=725$ MeV, $\Lambda^{I=1}_{\pi\Sigma}=725$ MeV, $\Lambda^{I=1}_{\pi\Lambda}=725$ MeV; for the E-indep model, $\Lambda^{I=0}_{\bar{K}N}=1000$ MeV, $\Lambda^{I=0}_{\pi\Sigma}=700$ MeV, $\Lambda^{I=1}_{\bar{K}N}=920$ MeV, $\Lambda^{I=1}_{\pi\Sigma}=960$ MeV, $\Lambda^{I=1}_{\pi\Lambda}=640$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of the $K^-d\rightarrow \pi\Sigma n$ reaction that used simplified two-step process models (Jido 2009, Miyagawa 2012, Jido 2012, Yamagata-Sekihara 2012) by performing the first full three-body calculation incorporating the $\bar{K}NN$-$\pi YN$ coupled-channel dynamics. The results build on the AGS-equation framework developed by Ikeda (2007, 2008, 2010) and Ohnishi (2013) for the $\bar{K}NN$ system. The paper shows that the $K^-d\rightarrow \pi\Sigma n$ reaction can discriminate between the single-pole description of the $\Lambda(1405)$ from phenomenological models (Akaishi 2002, Shevchenko 2011) and the two-pole description from chiral SU(3) dynamics (Oller 2000, Jido 2003), enabling future experiments at J-PARC (Noumi) to resolve the long-standing structure of the $\Lambda(1405)$.
