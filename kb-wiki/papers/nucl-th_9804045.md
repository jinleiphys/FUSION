# Au+Au central collisions at 150, 250 and 400 AMeV energies in QMD with relativistic forces

> An extensive comparison of the recent experimental data published by the FOPI collaboration at GSI with the results of a relativistically covariant formulation of a QMD code is presented. For most of the quantities we find agreement with the experimental results showing that the derived force has a reasonable momentum dependence.

## Key claim

The authors derive a relativistically covariant mean-field force from a modified Zimanyi-Moszkowski Lagrangian and implement it in a Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) code, finding agreement with FOPI experimental data for most observables in central Au+Au collisions at 150, 250, and 400 AMeV, but only within the "small acceleration approximation" which limits the validity to mean-field interactions where accelerations are small compared to the meson mass.

## Method

The authors start from a field-theoretical Lagrangian with scalar (\(\sigma\)), vector (\(\omega\)), and isovector vector (\(\rho\)) meson exchanges, using the Zimanyi-Moszkowski non-linear scalar coupling to avoid stiff equations of state. They derive covariant equations of motion for nucleon world lines using the Wheeler-Feynman action-at-a-distance formulation, then apply the "small acceleration approximation" to obtain an instantaneous, equal-time Lagrangian and Hamiltonian that yields Lorentz-boosted Yukawa potentials. The resulting relativistic forces are implemented in a QMD code, and the code is used to simulate central Au+Au collisions at beam energies of 150, 250, and 400 AMeV, with results compared to experimental data from the FOPI collaboration at GSI.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: 150, 250, and 400 AMeV for central Au+Au collisions
- The scalar field coupling uses the form \(f(\phi) = 1/(1 + \frac{g_\sigma}{m} \phi)\), with coupling constants determined by fitting nuclear saturation density and energy
- The incompressibility from the Zimanyi-Moszkowski Lagrangian is "two times smaller" than from linear scalar coupling (see Table 1 in the paper)
- The range of validity for the small acceleration approximation requires \(|a_\mu a^\mu| \ll \mu^2\), where \(\mu\) is the meson mass
- The effective mass of each nucleon is given by \(m^*_i(\theta) = m/(1 + \frac{g_\sigma}{m} \phi(r_i(\theta)))\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QMD code (Nemeth, Papp, Feldmeier, earlier publication) which used a Walecka-type relativistic Lagrangian but was found unsatisfactory for QMD calculations due to large scalar and vector couplings. The paper uses the Zimanyi-Moszkowski (ZM) derivative coupling (Zimanyi and Moszkowski, 1990) instead of adding third and fourth powers of the scalar field as done by Boguta and Bodmer (1977). The results are compared with experimental data from the FOPI collaboration at GSI, and the approach enables testing of relativistic mean-field effects in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies (100 AMeV to 2 AGeV) where particle creation is negligible, helping to disentangle mean-field effects from medium-dependent cross sections.
