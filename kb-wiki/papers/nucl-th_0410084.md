# Hybrid and Conventional Baryons in the Flux-Tube and Quark Models

> The status of conventional baryon flux-tubes and hybrid baryons is reviewed. Recent surprises are that a model prediction indicates that hybrid baryons are very weakly produced in glue-rich Psi decays, and an analysis of electro-production data concludes that the Roper resonance is not a hybrid baryon. The baryon decay flux-tube overlap has been calculated in the flux-tube model, and is discussed here. The behavior of the overlap follows naive expectations.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the status of conventional and hybrid baryons in the flux-tube and quark models, with the key hedged claim that a recent model calculation indicates hybrid baryons are very weakly produced in glue-rich Psi decays, contrary to naive expectations, and that an analysis of electro-production data concludes the Roper resonance is not a hybrid baryon.

## Method

The paper reviews quenched lattice QCD calculations of the baryon potential and the low-lying hybrid baryon potential, comparing them to flux-tube model predictions and QCD sum rule results. It also presents a first preliminary calculation of the baryon decay flux-tube overlap in the flux-tube model of Isgur and Paton, where the overlap of initial and final flux-tube wavefunctions is evaluated analytically for OZI-allowed baryon decay via quark-antiquark pair creation.

## Key numbers

- The lowest gluonic excitation energy in quenched lattice QCD is consistently greater than 1 GeV, several hundred MeV larger than the flux-tube model prediction
- The lightest N 1/2+ hybrid baryon mass is predicted above 2 GeV if the lattice calculation is correct
- QCD sum rules predict the Lambda 1/2+ hybrid baryon mass at 1.6(2) GeV
- A 2-sigma peak at 1834+46-55 MeV is reported in Psi -> p pbar eta decay
- A peak at 2068 +/- 3 +15-40 MeV is reported in Psi -> N Nbar pi decay
- Constituent quark masses taken as 330 MeV, string tension 0.18 GeV^2 (approximately 1 GeV/fm)
- The flux-tube overlap shows a Gaussian fall-off with flattened top with diameter approximately 0.4 fm
- Fixed parameters for overlap calculations: y2 = 1.25 GeV^-1 = 0.25 fm, li = 2.5 GeV^-1 = 0.5 fm, N^A_i = 3

## Context

This review extends the author's previous 2002 review on baryon flux-tubes and hybrid baryons, and discusses recent lattice QCD calculations by Takahashi (2004) and Woloshyn (2004) that confirm the Y-shaped baryon potential. The paper contradicts earlier expectations that glue-rich Psi decays would be promising for hybrid baryon production, and provides the first calculation of the baryon decay flux-tube overlap, enabling future incorporation of Y-shaped baryon confinement in decay models, following similar work for meson decays by Perantonis (1990) and Kokoski (1985).
