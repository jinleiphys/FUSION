# Break-up mechanisms in heavy ion collisions at low energies

> We investigate reaction mechanisms occurring in heavy ion collisions at low energy (around 20 MeV/u). In particular, we focus on the competition between fusion and break-up processes (Deep-Inelastic and fragmentation) in semi-peripheral collisions, where the formation of excited systems in various conditions of shape and angular momentum is observed. Adopting a Langevin treatment for the dynamical evolution of the system configuration, described in terms of shape observables such as quadrupole and octupole moments, we derive fusion/fission probabilities, from which one can finally evaluate the corresponding fusion and break-up cross sections. The dependence of the results on shape, angular momentum and excitation energy is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a hybrid model coupling a microscopic Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) transport approach for the early collision stage with a macroscopic Langevin treatment of shape fluctuations (quadrupole and octupole moments) can describe the competition between fusion and binary break-up in semi-peripheral heavy-ion collisions around 20 MeV/u, but only for systems that form quasi-stationary elongated configurations with density close to normal value, where surface rather than volume instabilities dominate.

## Method

The authors first simulate the early stage of the collision (up to ~200 fm/c) using the Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) model, a Boltzmann-Langevin-type transport approach with a soft isoscalar equation of state (compressibility modulus K = 200 MeV) and a stiff density dependence of the symmetry energy. At the point when a quasi-stationary elongated composite system forms, they switch to a macroscopic Langevin treatment of the shape degrees of freedom (quadrupole moment β₂, octupole moment β₃, and rotation angle ω), solving stochastic Rayleigh-Lagrange equations with one-body dissipation and thermal fluctuations. The nuclear potential energy is computed using a Yukawa-plus-exponential folding function, and the Coulomb energy includes a sharp-surface term plus a diffuseness correction.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies: 9 MeV/u and 16 MeV/u for the system ³⁶Ar + ⁹⁶Zr
- Impact parameter ranges: b = 5-7 fm at 9 MeV/u; b = 4-6 fm at 16 MeV/u
- Composite system mass A ≈ 122, charge Z ≈ 53 after pre-equilibrium emission
- Excitation energy of composite system: approximately 250 MeV, corresponding to temperatures of order 4 MeV
- Intrinsic angular momentum of composite system: ranging from 50 ħ to 100 ħ
- Quadrupole moment β₂ values of composite system: from 0.2 to 1.14
- Octupole moment β₃ values of composite system: from -0.76 to 0.47
- Time step for stochastic kicks in Langevin treatment: 3 fm/c
- Nucleon emission rate: approximately 2.5 MeV per 30 fm/c
- Fission probability for b = 7 fm at 9 MeV/u: 0.990 (for two sample events)
- Fission probability for b = 5 fm at 9 MeV/u: 0.375 and 0.145 (for two sample events)
- Fission probability for b = 6 fm at 16 MeV/u: 1.000 (for two sample events)
- Fission probability for b = 4 fm at 16 MeV/u: 0.110 and 0.035 (for two sample events)

## Context

This work extends the Stochastic Mean Field approach developed by Colonna et al. (1995) for describing dissipative collisions at Fermi energies, adapting it to lower energies (around 20 MeV/u) where elongated rather than dilute systems form. The hybrid treatment addresses the longstanding problem of competition between fusion and binary processes in semi-central collisions, which has seen renewed interest due to experiments with neutron-rich systems (Amo09). The Langevin treatment of shape fluctuations follows the formalism of David (1980) and allows the authors to observe a bimodal behavior in shape observables, analogous to bimodality seen in liquid-gas phase transitions (Eric), enabling future evaluation of fusion cross sections and characterization of fast-fission processes of projectile-like and target-like fragments.
