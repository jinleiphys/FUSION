# Diffraction dissociation, an important background to photon-photon collisions via heavy ion beams at LHC

> The cross sections for the production of hadrons in quasi-real photon-photon collisions in proton-proton and heavy ion interactions are compared with the corresponding cross sections for central diffraction and for photon-pomeron collisions. The signatures, heavy ions or protons with only slightly changed momenta together with two large rapidity gaps and a cluster of produced hadrons in the central region, are nearly identical in all three processes. Therefore, it will be rather difficult to distinguish the reactions experimentally. It is found, that central diffraction is the dominant process in collisions of protons, light and medium-heavy ions. The photon-pomeron and photon-photon processes have quite similar cross sections in collisions of heavy ions like lead.

## Key claim

Central diffraction dissociation produces nearly identical experimental signatures (two large rapidity gaps with a central hadron cluster) as quasi-real photon-photon collisions in proton-proton and heavy ion interactions at LHC energies, making it a severe background that will be "rather difficult to distinguish" experimentally, with the relative importance of the three processes depending strongly on the nuclear mass of the colliding ions.

## Method

The authors used the two-component Dual Parton Model as implemented in the Phojet event generator to calculate cross sections for hadron production in double-photon, double-pomeron (central diffraction), and photon-pomeron collisions. For heavy ion collisions, they applied the "criterion C" formalism within the Glauber model to account for nuclear shadowing and absorption effects, using Woods-Saxon nuclear densities with standard parameters. The photon fluxes for heavy ions were calculated using both form factor and semi-classical geometric approximations.

## Key numbers

- For proton-proton collisions at LHC energy (sqrt(s) = 6 TeV), central diffraction dominates over photon-photon and photon-pomeron processes
- For Pb-Pb collisions, the photon-pomeron and photon-photon cross sections are "quite similar" in magnitude
- The effective atomic number A^2_eff for central diffraction in nucleus-nucleus collisions scales approximately as A^(1/3), much smaller than A^2
- Luminosity for Ca-Ca collisions at LHC: approximately 5 x 10^30 cm^(-2)s^(-1), compared to 5 x 10^26 cm^(-2)s^(-1) for Pb-Pb
- The model predicts a photon single diffraction cross section of 19 microbarns at sqrt(s)_(gamma p) = 200 GeV, compared to 23 +/- 11 microbarns from H1 data (Aid 1995)
- The nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section used for heavy ion calculations is 73 mb at sqrt(s) = 6 TeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of photon-photon collisions in heavy ion reactions (Baur 1988, Grabiak 1989, Papageorgiu 1990, Vidovic 1993, Hencken 1995) and central diffraction studies (Ingelman and Schlein 1985, UA8 Collaboration) to the LHC energy regime. The calculations use the Phojet event generator framework previously applied to photon-photon collisions at electron-positron colliders (Engel and Ranft 1995). The results show that isolating pure photon-photon events from diffractive backgrounds will be extremely challenging, particularly for light and medium-heavy ions where central diffraction dominates, and that the photon-pomeron process provides an additional irreducible background for heavy ion collisions.
