# Conundrum for the free energy of a holonomous gluonic plasma at cubic order

> We compute the term $\sim g^3$ in the free energy for a $SU(N)$ gauge theory with nonzero holonomy at nonzero temperature. If the holonomy is generated kinematically by the introduction of gauge invariant sources coupled to Polyakov loops, the contribution of charged (off-diagonal) gluons to the free energy at order $g^3$, ${\cal F}^{\left( 3\rm{:c.g.} \right)}$, is singular: ${\cal F}^{\left( 3\rm{:c.g.} \right)} \neq 0 $ without holonomy, but ${\cal F}^{\left( 3 \rm{:c.g.} \right)}= 0$ when the holonomy is nonzero, even infinitesimally. We show that the absence of the charged gluon contribution is required by gauge invariance alone and is therefore a universal feature.

# Conundrum for the free energy of a holonomous gluonic plasma at cubic order

## Key claim
The free energy of a SU(N) gauge theory at order g^3 is singular when holonomy is generated kinematically by gauge-invariant sources coupled to Polyakov loops: the charged (off-diagonal) gluon contribution vanishes for any nonzero holonomy, even infinitesimally, but is nonzero at zero holonomy. This absence of the charged gluon contribution is required by gauge invariance and is therefore a universal feature, not a model-dependent artifact.

## Method
The authors compute the free energy of a SU(N) gauge theory at nonzero temperature to order g^3 using the background field method in R_xi gauge, with a constant diagonal background field A_4 that introduces nonzero holonomy. They add a gauge-invariant deformation term Delta S = T^3 integral d^3 x h U(L_n) to the Yang-Mills action, where h is a source coupled to Polyakov loops, and compute the effective potential for the holonomy by expanding the deformation to order g^2 and evaluating one-loop self-energies and ring diagrams. The saddle point of the effective potential gives the free energy, which is evaluated in the static limit (p_4 = 0, p -> 0) for both neutral and charged gluons.

## Key numbers
- The free energy density from charged gluons at order g^3 satisfies: lim_{h -> 0} F(h) - F(0) = (N^2 - N)/(12 pi) T m_D^3 + O(g^4), where m_D ~ g T is the Debye mass at h = 0.
- At order g^2, the free energy in the limit h -> 0 approaches F^(2) -> N g^2 (N^2 - 1) T^4 / 144, matching the standard result of Shuryak (1977).
- The Debye mass squared in pure Yang-Mills without holonomy is m_D^2 = (1/3) N g^2 T^2.
- The standard g^3 contribution to the free energy without holonomy is F^(3)(h=0) = -(N^2 - 1)/(12 pi) T m_D^3.

## Context
This work extends perturbative calculations of the QCD free energy at high temperature, which have been computed to order g^6 by Collins and Perry (1974), Shuryak (1977), Kapusta (1979), Toimela (1982), Arnold and Zhai (1994), Zhai and Kastening (1995), and Kajantie et al. (2002). It specifically addresses the effect of nonzero holonomy, which was previously studied at order g^2 using gauge-invariant sources by Bhattacharya et al. (1990, 1992), Belyaev (1991), Korthals Altes (1993), Giovannangeli and Korthals Altes (2002, 2004), Dumitru et al. (2013), and Guo et al. (2014, 2018). The singular behavior at order g^3 contradicts the smooth continuity found at order g^2 and implies that the deformed theory with arbitrarily small source h is not perturbatively connected to pure Yang-Mills theory. This result has implications for lattice studies of Polyakov loop probability distributions and for phenomenological models of the deconfining phase transition using double-trace deformations as in Meisinger et al. (2001) and Dumitru et al. (2010).
