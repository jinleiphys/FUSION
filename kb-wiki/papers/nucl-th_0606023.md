# Shell model study of $^{40}$Ca muon capture and the $(0^+, 0)$$\to$$(0^-, 2626)$ axial charge transition

> We report results from shell model studies of muon capture on $^{40}$Ca to low-lying levels of $^{40}$K. We discuss the comparison between calculated capture rates, measured capture rates and analogous transitions in ($e$,$e^{\prime}$) scattering in terms of the particle-hole structure of the $^{40}$Ca-$^{40}$K nuclei. We highlight the $^{40}$Ca$(0^+, 0)$$\to$$^{40}$K$(0^-, 2626)$ axial charge transition and its sensitivity to the induced pseudoscalar coupling $g_p$ of the proton's weak interaction. In addition, we address the hindrance of unique first-forbidden transitions due to particle-hole interactions and the emergence of allowed Gamow-Teller transitions due to ground state correlations. Lastly, we examine the longitudinal alignment of $^{40}$K recoils following muon capture, and discuss this possibility for independently determining the induced coupling $g_p$.

## Key claim

The paper reports that shell model calculations of muon capture on $^{40}$Ca to low-lying $^{40}$K states show fair agreement with experimental data for the global distribution of capture rates, but significant model-dependent discrepancies exist, particularly for the $(0^+,0)\to(0^-,2626)$ axial charge transition where the extracted induced pseudoscalar coupling $g_p$ ranges from $10.3^{+2.1}_{-1.9}$ to $14.3^{+1.8}_{-1.6}$ depending on the treatment of ground state correlations, compared to the theoretical prediction of $g_p = 8.23$.

## Method

Shell model calculations were performed using the OXBASH code with two model spaces: the sd-pf space with the WBMB interaction (in both 0 $\hbar\omega$ and (0+2) $\hbar\omega$ versions) and the smaller $d_{3/2}$-$f_{7/2}$ space with the SAS interaction. Muon capture rates and nuclear matrix elements were computed using the formalism of Walecka and Donnelly and Haxton, assuming harmonic oscillator wavefunctions with oscillator parameter $b = 1.94$ fm, a constant muon wavefunction over the nuclear volume with reduction factor $R = 0.44$, and fixed weak coupling constants $g_v = 1.0$, $g_m = 3.706$, $g_a = 1.27$, and $g_p = 8.23$ (with $g_p$ varied for the axial charge transition analysis).

## Key numbers

- Experimental capture rate for the $(0^+,0)\to(0^-,2626)$ transition: $\Lambda = (15 \pm 2) \times 10^3$ s$^{-1}$
- Extracted $g_p$ from 0 $\hbar\omega$ WBMB calculation: $g_p = 14.3^{+1.8}_{-1.6}$
- Extracted $g_p$ from (0+2) $\hbar\omega$ WBMB calculation: $g_p = 10.3^{+2.1}_{-1.9}$
- Theoretical prediction for $g_p$ from chiral symmetry: $g_p = 8.23$
- Measured capture rate for $(0^+,0)\to(1^+,2290)$ allowed Gamow-Teller transition: $(13 \pm 5) \times 10^3$ s$^{-1}$
- SAS model prediction for $(0^+,0)\to(1^+,2290)$ rate: $19.2 \times 10^3$ s$^{-1}$
- (0+2) $\hbar\omega$ WBMB prediction for $(0^+,0)\to(1^+,2290)$ rate: $0.08 \times 10^3$ s$^{-1}$
- Measured B(M1) for $^{40}$Ca(e,e')$^{40}$Ca($1^+, 9.87$ MeV) analog transition: $0.32 \pm 0.09$ $\mu_N^2$
- SAS prediction for B(M1): $0.86$ $\mu_N^2$
- (0+2) $\hbar\omega$ WBMB prediction for B(M1): $\simeq 0.01$ $\mu_N^2$
- For the $(0^+,0)\to(2^-,800)$ transition, the $d_{3/2}\to f_{7/2}$ single-particle estimate exceeds the measured B(M2) by a factor of 6.3 and the measured capture rate $\Lambda$ by a factor of 4.6
- Measured rate for $(0^+,0)\to(1^-,2104)$ transition: $(18 \pm 5) \times 10^3$ s$^{-1}$
- 0 $\hbar\omega$ WBMB rate for $(0^+,0)\to(1^-,2104)$: $14.7 \times 10^3$ s$^{-1}$
- (0+2) $\hbar\omega$ WBMB rate for $(0^+,0)\to(1^-,2104)$: $9.0 \times 10^3$ s$^{-1}$
- Closed-shell occupancy of $^{40}$Ca ground state: 72% in (0+2) $\hbar\omega$ WBMB calculation, 46% in SAS calculation

## Context

This work extends shell model studies of muon capture on $^{40}$Ca, building on the experimental results of Measday and Stocki (2006) who published new $\gamma$-ray spectra from muon capture on $^{40}$Ca including partial rates for numerous transitions. The paper compares calculated capture rates with earlier data from Igo-Kemenes et al. (1971) and with analog transitions in electron scattering studied by Buenerd et al. (1982), Ogata et al. (1984), and Petraitis et al. (1995). The $(0^+,0)\to(0^-,2626)$ axial charge transition provides a new data point for accessing the induced pseudoscalar coupling $g_p$ in medium-weight nuclei, following the celebrated $^{16}$O$(0^+,0)\to^{16}$N$(0^-,120)$ transition studied by Haxton et al. (1990) and Warburton et al. (1994). The work also addresses the hindrance of unique first-forbidden transitions due to particle-hole interactions, as documented by Towner et al. (1971) and Warburton et al. (1988), and the emergence of allowed Gamow-Teller transitions from ground state correlations. The results enable future measurements of $^{40}$K recoil longitudinal alignment following muon capture as a potential independent determination of $g_p$.
