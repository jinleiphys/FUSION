# Equation of state of QCD matter within the Hagedorn bag-like model

> The QCD equation of state at finite temperature and densities of conserved charges is considered in the framework of a Hagedorn bag-like model, incorporating both the finite sizes of hadrons as well as their exponential mass spectrum. Augmented with non-zero masses of quarks and gluons in the bag spectrum, the model yields a fair quantitative description of lattice data on thermodynamic functions, the conserved charges susceptibilities, and Fourier coefficients of net-baryon density. Both at zero and finite baryon densities a broad crossover transition between hadronic and quark-gluon matter is observed. The model thus provides a thermodynamically consistent construction of a crossover equation of state for finite baryon number, electric charge and strangeness chemical potentials, which can be used in fluid dynamical simulations of heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim
The Hagedorn bag-like model, augmented with non-zero masses for quarks and gluons in the bag spectrum, provides a thermodynamically consistent crossover equation of state for finite baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness chemical potentials, but the quantitative comparison with lattice data was previously only performed at zero baryon chemical potential; this work extends the analysis to finite baryon density for the first time within this framework.

## Method
The authors construct a statistical mechanics model of non-overlapping hadrons and quark-gluon bags with finite eigenvolumes, using an exponential mass spectrum for the bags. The discrete hadron spectrum is taken from the Particle Data Tables, while the continuum part uses the MIT bag model with massive quarks (u,d at 300 MeV, s at 350 MeV) and gluons (800 MeV). The system pressure is obtained by solving a transcendental equation derived from the isobaric ensemble, with parameters previously constrained to lattice QCD thermodynamics at zero baryon chemical potential.

## Key numbers
- Bag constant: B^(1/4) = 250 MeV
- Quark masses: m_u = m_d = 300 MeV, m_s = 350 MeV; gluon mass: m_g = 800 MeV
- Model parameters: gamma = 0, delta = -2, C = 0.03 GeV^4, V_0 = 4 fm^3
- The pressure along the T = 155 MeV isotherm is compared to a Taylor expansion truncated at O(mu_B^4); the Hagedorn model approaches the Stefan-Boltzmann limit at large mu_B/T, while the truncated expansion overshoots that limit at mu_B/T approximately 8
- The Hagedorn temperature is defined through T_H = [3B / sigma_Q(T_H)]^(1/4), where sigma_Q is three times the pressure of an ideal gas of massive quarks and gluons

## Context
This work extends the Hagedorn bag-like model framework previously developed by Gorenstein (1981) and others, which offered one of the first examples of a single partition function describing the hadron-to-QGP transition. It improves upon the earlier study by Ferroni (2008) that found only qualitative agreement with lattice QCD, and follows the semi-quantitative description achieved by Vovchenko (2018) at zero baryon density. The model enables fluid dynamical simulations of heavy-ion collisions at finite baryon densities and can be extended to incorporate a first-order phase transition in the baryon-rich region as outlined by Gorenstein (2005).
