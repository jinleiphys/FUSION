# Non-perturbative renormalization of the average color charge and multi-point correlators of color charge from a non-Gaussian small-$x$ action

> The McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model is a Gaussian effective theory of color charge fluctuations at small-$x$ in the limit of large valence charge density, {\it i}.{\it e}., a large nucleus made of uncorrelated color charges. In this work, we explore the effects of the first non-trivial (even C-parity) non-Gaussian correction on the color charge density to the MV model ("quartic" term) in SU(2) and SU(3) color group in the non-perturbative regime. We compare our (numerical) non-perturbative results to (analytical) perturbative ones in the limit of small or large non-Gaussian fluctuations. The couplings in the non-Gaussian action, $\barμ$ for the quadratic and $κ_4$ for the quartic term, need to be renormalized in order to match the two-point function in the Gaussian theory. We investigate three different choices for the renormalization of these couplings: i) $κ_{4}$ is proportional to a power of $\barμ$; ii) $κ_4$ is kept constant and iii) $\barμ$ is kept constant. We find that the first two choices lead to a scenario where the small-$x$ action evolves towards a theory dominated by large non-Gaussian fluctuations, regardless of the system size, while the last one allows for controlling the deviations from the MV model.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the first non-trivial (even C-parity) non-Gaussian correction to the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model can be renormalized in three different schemes, and that two of these schemes (keeping $\kappa_4$ proportional to a power of $\bar\mu$ or keeping $\kappa_4$ constant) force the small-$x$ action toward a theory dominated by large non-Gaussian fluctuations regardless of system size, while the third scheme (keeping $\bar\mu$ constant) allows control over deviations from the MV model.

## Method

The authors performed non-perturbative lattice calculations of color charge correlators in SU(2) and SU(3) color groups using a non-Gaussian weight function that includes a quartic term in the color charge density, comparing these numerical results to analytical perturbative expansions in the limits of small and large non-Gaussian fluctuations. They imposed the renormalization condition that the two-point function of color charges must match the Gaussian MV model result, and investigated three different renormalization schemes for the couplings $\bar\mu$ (quadratic term) and $\kappa_4$ (quartic term) on a transverse lattice with spacing $\mathtt{a}$.

## Key numbers

- For SU(2) in the limit of large non-Gaussian fluctuations ($Z \to 0$), the ratio of the four-point function of color charges at a single site in the non-Gaussian theory to the Gaussian theory is 0.822504.
- For SU(3) in the same limit, this ratio is 0.905415.
- In the first renormalization scheme ($\kappa_4/\bar\mu^6 = \lambda$ constant), the renormalization factor $Z = \mu^2/\bar\mu^2$ scales as $Z^3 \propto a^2 \mu^2$ at leading order in the continuum limit, where $a$ is the lattice spacing and $\mu$ is the average color charge squared per unit area.
- The perturbative expression for the four-point function ratio in the dilute limit is $1 - 24\mu^4/(\kappa_4 a^2)$ for the local configuration.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative calculations of non-Gaussian corrections to the MV model by Jeon and Venugopalan (2004) and Dumitru et al. (2011) into the fully non-perturbative regime, where all orders in $1/\kappa_4$ are included. The results provide initial conditions at $Y=0$ for the JIMWLK renormalization group evolution equation that go beyond the Gaussian approximation, enabling future studies of how non-Gaussian initial conditions affect observables such as multi-particle correlations in nuclear collisions and di-jet production in proton-nucleus collisions.
