# On The pp->ppη(eta') Reactions Near Threshold

> The production rate for η' in pp ->pp η' at rest is calculated in a covariant one boson exchange model, previously applied to study pi^0 and ηproduction in NN collisions. The transition amplitudes for the elementary BN -> eta' N processes with B being the meson exchanged (B = pi, sigma, eta, rho, omega and delta) are taken to be the sum of s and u channels with a nucleon in the intermediate states, and a delta meson pole in a t-channel. The couplings of the eta' to hadrons are a factor 0.437 weaker than the respective eta-hadron couplings, as suggested by a quark model and a singlet-octetmixing angle theta = -23 degree. The model reproduces near threshold cross sections for the quasielastic processes pi- p ->n eta (eta') and pp -> pp eta (eta') reactions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that near-threshold cross sections for the $pp \rightarrow pp \eta'$ reaction can be reproduced without invoking resonant production via an intermediate baryon resonance, in marked contrast to $\eta$ production which relies on the N$^*$(1535) S11 resonance. The model's success depends on the assumption that the $\eta'$ couplings to hadrons are a factor 0.437 weaker than the corresponding $\eta$-hadron couplings, as derived from a quark model with a singlet-octet mixing angle $\theta = -23^\circ$.

## Method

The authors apply a relativistic covariant one-boson-exchange (OBE) model, previously used for $\pi^0$ and $\eta$ production, to calculate the $pp \rightarrow pp \eta'$ reaction near threshold. The transition amplitudes for the elementary $BN \rightarrow \eta' N$ conversion processes (with B = $\pi$, $\sigma$, $\eta$, $\rho$, $\omega$, $\delta$) are constructed as sums of s-channel and u-channel nucleon pole terms, plus a t-channel $\delta$ meson pole term; resonant contributions from nucleon isobars are omitted for $\eta'$ production. The half off-mass-shell amplitudes are then folded with the NN initial state to obtain the $pp \rightarrow pp \eta'$ cross section, with final-state interactions included via the procedure of Gedalin (1998).

## Key numbers

- The ratio of coupling constants is $g_{\eta' NN} / g_{\eta NN} = 0.437$, derived from a mixing angle $\theta = -23^\circ$ and the assumption $g_8 = g_1$.
- The extracted $\eta'$ coupling constants are: $g_{\eta' NN} = 2.68$, $g_{0\eta'} = 0.02 \pm 0.01$, $g_{1\eta'} = -2.6 \pm 0.20$, $g_{2\eta'} = -1.51 \pm 0.12$, $g_{3\eta'} = 1.44 \pm 0.11$.
- For the quasielastic process $\pi^0 p \rightarrow \eta' p$ at threshold, the model predicts $|f|^2 = (15 \pm 7) \mu b/sr$, compared to the experimental value of $10 \pm 1 \mu b/sr$ from Binnie (1973).
- For $\pi^0 p \rightarrow \eta p$ at threshold, the model predicts $|f|^2 = (346 \pm 40) \mu b/sr$, compared to the experimental value of $365 \pm 30 \mu b/sr$ from Binnie (1973).
- The relative importance of meson exchanges in the $pp \rightarrow pp \eta'$ amplitude at threshold is given by the ratios $M_{\sigma} : M_{\rho} : M_{\omega} : M_{\pi} : M_{\delta} : M_{\eta} \approx 14 : 10 : 8 : 7 : 2.5 : 1$.

## Context

This work extends the covariant OBE model of Gedalin (1998) and Gedalin (1998a), which successfully described $pp \rightarrow pp \pi^0$ and $pp \rightarrow pp \eta$ reactions, to the heavier $\eta'$ meson. The model reproduces near-threshold cross section data from the SPESIII group at Saturne (Hibou 1998) and COSY-Julich (Moskal 1998), as well as earlier data from Saclay (Bergdolt 1993, Chiavassa 1994) and Uppsala (Calen 1996, Haggstrom 1997). The key difference from $\eta$ production is that $\eta'$ production does not require resonant contributions from the N$^*$(1535) S11 isobar, which dominates $\eta$ production. The approach enables a consistent description of pseudoscalar meson ($\pi^0$, $\eta$, $\eta'$) production in NN collisions within a single OBE framework, but the authors note that further verification of the off-mass-shell behavior of the conversion amplitudes is needed.
