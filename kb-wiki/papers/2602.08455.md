# First Extraction of the Matter Radius of $^{132}$Sn via Proton Elastic Scattering at 200 MeV/Nucleon

> The angular distribution of the differential cross sections for proton elastic scattering from $^{132}$Sn at 196-210 MeV/nucleon was successfully measured over a momentum transfer range of 0.80 to 2.1 fm$^{-1}$. Using a relativistic impulse approximation, the root-mean-square matter radius of $^{132}$Sn was extracted to be $4.758^{+0.023}_{-0.024}$ fm, which was compared with the state-of-the-art ab initio calculations. Combined with the charge radius measured at ISOLDE, there are no theoretical calculations consistent with both matter and charge radii within the experimental errors.

## Key claim

The root-mean-square matter radius of the doubly magic neutron-rich nucleus $^{132}$Sn was extracted for the first time from proton elastic scattering data, but no existing theoretical calculation simultaneously reproduces both this matter radius and the previously measured charge radius within experimental errors.

## Method

The experiment was performed at the RIKEN RI Beam Factory using a secondary $^{132}$Sn beam at 196–210 MeV/nucleon produced by bombarding a $^{9}$Be target with a 345 MeV/nucleon $^{238}$U primary beam. Recoil protons from a solid hydrogen target were detected by the Recoil Proton Spectrometer (RPS) over a center-of-mass angular range of $14^\circ$ to $38^\circ$, corresponding to momentum transfers of 0.80 to 2.1 fm$^{-1}$. The angular distribution of differential cross sections was analyzed using a medium-modified relativistic impulse approximation (mm-RIA), where density-dependent parameters were calibrated against $^{58}$Ni(p,p) data at 200 MeV, and the matter radius was extracted by fitting two-parameter Fermi distributions for protons and neutrons.

## Key numbers

* Root-mean-square matter radius of $^{132}$Sn: $4.758^{+0.023}_{-0.024}$ fm, extracted via mm-RIA analysis of proton elastic scattering at 196–210 MeV/nucleon over momentum transfer 0.80 to 2.1 fm$^{-1}$.
* Charge radius of $^{132}$Sn previously measured at ISOLDE: 4.7093(76) fm (Blanc 2005, Gorges 2019).
* Beam energy spread: 196 to 210 MeV/nucleon, determined event-by-event from beam position at the dispersive fifth focus.
* Excitation energy resolution: 0.7 MeV ($\sigma$), enabling clear separation of elastic scattering from the first excited state at 4.04 MeV.
* Solid hydrogen target thickness: $6.76(35)\times10^{21}$ protons/cm$^2$, calibrated using $^{48}$Ca(p,p) at 225–245 MeV/nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the ESPRI project's previous proton elastic scattering measurements on $^{9}$C (Matsuda 2013) and $^{6}$He (Chebotayov 2018) to a heavy doubly magic nucleus far from stability, $^{132}$Sn, which has a larger isospin asymmetry ($(N-Z)/A=0.242$) than $^{208}$Pb ($(N-Z)/A=0.221$). The extracted matter radius, combined with the charge radius from ISOLDE, provides a stringent test for state-of-the-art ab initio in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) calculations using chiral effective field theory interactions (Arthuis 2020, Miyagi 2022, Hu 2022, Arthuis 2024). The result that no theoretical calculation simultaneously matches both radii indicates the need for further refinement of nuclear forces and many-body methods, and demonstrates that $^{132}$Sn can impose tighter constraints on the symmetry energy parameters of the nuclear equation of state than $^{208}$Pb (Chen 2005).
