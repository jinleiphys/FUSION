# Pseudoscalar-Meson Form Factors: A Fresh Look by QCD Sum Rules

> Confronted with some surprising claims about experimentally measured or theoretically expected dependencies on the involved momentum transfer of various form factors of pseudoscalar mesons, we reassess the present status of these quantities by means of QCD sum rules. This approach provides well-developed and very efficient tools to relate in an analytical manner the parameters of QCD to the empirical properties of hadrons. Within this framework, we show that a few theoretical findings for the pion elastic form factor and one experimental result for the pion-to-photon transition form factor are at odds with rather general fundamental considerations.

## Key claim

The authors use QCD sum rules to show that certain theoretical predictions for the charged-pion elastic form factor and one experimental result from BaBar for the neutral-pion-to-photon transition form factor are inconsistent with general fundamental considerations, specifically with the behavior expected from local-duality (LD) sum rules.

## Method

The authors analyze the momentum-transfer-squared (\(Q^2\)) dependence of the charged-pion elastic form factor \(F_\pi(Q^2)\) and the neutral-pion-to-photon transition form factor \(F_{\pi\gamma}(Q^2)\) using dispersive QCD sum rules derived from vacuum expectation values of products of interpolating and electromagnetic currents. In the local-duality limit, the sum rules relate the form factors to dispersion integrals over perturbatively calculable spectral densities, with the nonperturbative effects encoded in a \(Q^2\)-dependent effective threshold \(s_{\rm eff}(Q^2)\). The authors define equivalent effective thresholds that exactly reproduce experimental or theoretical results, and then compare the \(Q^2\) behavior of these thresholds with a model that interpolates between the large-\(Q^2\) asymptote and empirical low-\(Q^2\) behavior.

## Key numbers

* The pion decay constant is \(f_\pi = 130\ \text{MeV}\).
* For \(Q^2 \to \infty\), factorization implies \(Q^2 F_\pi(Q^2) \to 8\pi \alpha_{\rm s}(Q^2) f_\pi^2\) and \(Q^2 F_{\pi\gamma}(Q^2) \to \sqrt{2} f_\pi\).
* The asymptotic effective threshold is \(s_{\rm eff}(\infty) = 4\pi^2 f_\pi^2\).
* The BaBar claim of \(Q^2 F_{\pi\gamma}(Q^2)\) rising with \(Q^2\) beyond the LD asymptote \(\sqrt{2} f_\pi\) is not reproduced by the LD sum rules; recent Belle observations support the authors' point of view.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous local-duality sum-rule analyses (Balakireva, Lucha, Melikhov, 2008) and confronts specific theoretical predictions for the pion elastic form factor from Brodsky and de Teramond (2008), Grigoryan and Radyushkin (2008), and Bakulev, Pimikov, and Stefanis (2009), which are found to conflict with LD model expectations. The analysis also contradicts the BaBar measurement (2009) of the pion-to-photon transition form factor, while noting that the LD sum rules perform satisfactorily for \((\eta, \eta', \eta_c) \leftrightarrow \gamma \gamma^*\) transitions. The results enable further scrutiny of both experimental data and alternative theoretical approaches to meson form factors.
