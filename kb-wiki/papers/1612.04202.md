# Dijet Asymmetry in the Resummation Improved Perturbative QCD Approach

> We develop the first systematic theoretical approach to dijet asymmetries in hadron-hadron collisions based on the perturbative QCD (pQCD) expansion and the Sudakov resummation formalism. We find that the pQCD calculation at next-to-leading order is indispensable to describe the experimental data, while the Sudakov resummation formalism is vital near the end points where the pQCD expansion fails to converge due to the appearance of large Sudakov logarithms. Utilizing our resummation improved pQCD approach, we obtain good agreement with the most up-to-date fully corrected ATLAS data on dijet asymmetry in $pp$ collisions. Combining with the BDMPS jet energy loss formalism, we extract the value of jet transport coefficient $\hat{q}_0 \sim 2$-$6~\textrm{GeV}^2/\textrm{fm}$ for the quark-gluon-plasma created in $PbPb$ collisions at 2.76A TeV. This work paves the way for a more complete and deeper understanding of the properties of strongly-coupled QCD medium via the studies of dijet asymmetries in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first systematic theoretical approach to dijet asymmetries in hadron-hadron collisions that combines next-to-leading-order (NLO) perturbative QCD with Sudakov resummation; the pQCD expansion alone fails near the back-to-back endpoint (x_J ~ 1) where large Sudakov logarithms appear, and the resummation formalism is vital there.

## Method

The authors computed dijet asymmetry distributions in pp collisions using NLO pQCD (2→3 and 2→4 processes) for the region away from the back-to-back endpoint, and replaced the divergent pQCD expansion near x_J ~ 1 with a Sudakov resummation formalism performed in coordinate space. For PbPb collisions, they embedded this pp baseline into a (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulation of the QGP medium and applied the BDMPS energy loss formalism to compute the energy loss of each jet traversing the medium, assuming all jets are gluon jets and parameterizing the jet transport coefficient as \(\hat{q} = \hat{q}_0 T^3/T_0^3\).

## Key numbers

- The jet transport coefficient extracted for the quark-gluon plasma created in PbPb collisions at 2.76A TeV is \(\hat{q}_0 \sim 2\)–\(6~\textrm{GeV}^2/\textrm{fm}\).
- For the highest \(p_\perp\) bin (\(p_{1\perp} > 200~\textrm{GeV}\)), the data favors \(\hat{q}_0 \sim 2~\textrm{GeV}^2/\textrm{fm}\).
- Assuming the scaling \(\hat{q} = \hat{q}_0 T^3/T_0^3\) with \(T_0 = 481~\textrm{MeV}\), the extracted \(\hat{q}\) is \(\sim 0.3\)–\(0.8~\textrm{GeV}^2/\textrm{fm}\) at \(T = 250~\textrm{MeV}\).
- The typical energy loss for gluon jets is about 20–30 GeV.
- For \(2 \to n\) processes, the lower bound for \(x_J\) is \(\frac{1}{n-1}\) and the upper bound for \(A_J\) is \(\frac{n-2}{n}\); for \(2 \to 3\) (LO) this gives \(x_J \geq 1/2\) and for \(2 \to 4\) (NLO) \(x_J \geq 1/3\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier studies of transverse momentum broadening via back-to-back dijet azimuthal angular correlations using Sudakov resummation (Mueller 2016, Chen 2016) to the dijet asymmetry observable. Previous theoretical studies of dijet asymmetries were mostly based on Monte Carlo simulations and compared with uncorrected data (Aad 2010, Chatrchyan 2011, Chatrchyan 2012); this paper provides the first systematic pQCD-based calculation that can be compared to fully corrected ATLAS data (Perepelitsa 2016). The extracted \(\hat{q}_0\) is consistent with the JET collaboration's extraction from single hadron production (Burke 2013) and with the authors' previous finding via dihadron and hadron-jet angular correlation calculations (Chen 2016).
