# Correlations and neutrinoless $ββ$ decay nuclear matrix elements of $pf$-shell nuclei

> We calculate the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for neutrinoless double-beta decays ($0νββ$) of $pf$-shell nuclei using the shell model (SM) and energy density functional (EDF) methods. The systematic study of non-physical decays (except for $^{48}$Ca) of Ca$\rightarrow$Ti, Ti$\rightarrow$Cr and Cr$\rightarrow$Fe allows for a detailed comparison between the two nuclear structure approaches. We observe that while the dominant Gamow-Teller part of the NME differs roughly by a factor of two between SM and EDF, when we restrict the calculations to spherical EDF states and seniority-zero SM configurations, the NMEs obtained by both methods are strikingly similar. This points out to the important role of nuclear structure correlations for $0νββ$ decay NMEs. We identify correlations associated with high-seniority components in the initial and final states of the decay as one of the reasons for the discrepancies between SM and EDF results. We also explore exact projection to good isospin, and conclude that it has only a moderate effect in the Gamow-Teller part of the NMEs but strongly affects the Fermi contribution. This work opens up the door for NME benchmarks between different theoretical approaches, and constitutes a step forward towards more reliable estimations of the NMEs.

## Key claim

When full shell-model (SM) and energy density functional (EDF) calculations are compared, the SM Gamow-Teller nuclear matrix elements are about half the EDF values; however, if the calculations are restricted to spherical EDF states and seniority-zero SM configurations, the matrix elements from both methods become strikingly similar, indicating that the discrepancies arise from nuclear structure correlations, specifically high-seniority components in the SM and collective deformation in the EDF, that are not equivalently captured by the two approaches.

## Method

The authors calculated neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements for non-physical decays along the Ca→Ti, Ti→Cr, and Cr→Fe isotopic chains in the pf shell using the shell model (with KB3G and GXPF1A effective interactions in the full pf valence space) and the energy density functional method (with the Gogny D1S functional, including particle-number and angular-momentum projection and axial quadrupole configuration mixing via the generator coordinate method). The same transition operator was used for both methods, assuming the closure approximation with a closure energy of 7.72 MeV and including short-range correlations via the UCOM framework. Truncated SM calculations limiting seniority and spherical EDF calculations were performed to isolate the role of correlations, and exact isospin projection was applied to the SM states.

## Key numbers

- SM Gamow-Teller NMEs are about half of the EDF values in full calculations for all studied decays (Ca→Ti, Ti→Cr, Cr→Fe).
- With spherical EDF and seniority-zero SM states, SM GXPF1A results lie within 10% of EDF values, while SM KB3G results are about 25% larger.
- SM results with the two effective interactions (KB3G and GXPF1A) differ by around 10%–20% in the full calculations.
- For the ⁵⁰Ca→⁵⁰Ti decay, seniority components up to s=4 are necessary for a reliable calculation; the full SM states are 97%/3%/0% (s=0/s=4/s>4) for ⁵⁰Ca and 77%/21%/2% for ⁵⁰Ti.
- For the ⁴⁸Ti→⁴⁸Cr decay, higher seniority components up to s=8 are needed; the seniority decomposition is 58%/37%/5% for ⁴⁸Ti and 27%/42%/31% for ⁴⁸Cr (s=0/s=4/s>4).
- Isospin projection reduces the Fermi component of the NME strongly but has only a moderate effect on the Gamow-Teller part, with the reduction maximal at N=Z nuclei and very minor in the most neutron-rich systems.

## Context

This work extends systematic comparisons of neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements between shell-model and energy density functional methods, previously applied to actual decay candidates (e.g., by Menendez et al. (2009) and Rodriguez et al. (2010)), by studying non-physical decays in the pf shell to isolate the role of nuclear structure correlations. The finding that spherical EDF and seniority-zero SM calculations yield very similar NMEs opens the door for benchmarking with other approaches such as the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) and the interacting boson model (IBM), as used by Simkovic et al. and Iachello (2013), respectively. The identification of high-seniority correlations as a key source of discrepancy between SM and EDF results provides a path toward reducing theoretical uncertainties in matrix elements for experimentally relevant nuclei.
