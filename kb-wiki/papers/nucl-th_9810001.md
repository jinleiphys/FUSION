# Production of e+e- pairs in proton-deuteron capture to 3He

> The process p+d \leftrightarrow 3He + γ* at intermediate energies is described using a covariant and gauge-invariant model, and a realistic pd3He vertex. Both photodisintegration of 3He and proton-deuteron capture with production of e+e- pairs are studied, and results for cross sections and response functions are presented. The effect of time-like formfactors on the dilepton cross sections is investigated as well.

## Key claim

The paper presents a covariant and gauge-invariant model for p+d ↔ ³He+γ* reactions at intermediate energies, but the agreement with data is only "reasonable" and the model does not include explicit initial- or final-state pd rescattering, which is acknowledged as a missing mechanism.

## Method

The authors construct a covariant impulse approximation model for the p+d ↔ ³He+γ* reaction, making it gauge invariant by introducing an additional internal amplitude constrained by the Ward-Takahashi identity. The pd³He vertex function is related to nonrelativistic wave functions from realistic NN interactions (Argonne v14 and v18), including both the dominant deuteron-state pn pair and the ¹S₀ (d*) component. The cross section for e+e- pair production is decomposed into four response functions (transverse, longitudinal, and two interference terms) in analogy with (e,e'p) reactions.

## Key numbers

- Proton lab energy T_p = 190 MeV used for e+e- production calculations (corresponding to KVI, Groningen kinematics)
- Maximal photon invariant mass at T_p = 190 MeV is approximately 2/3 × T_p, giving m_γ^max ≈ 127 MeV
- At T_p = 350 MeV, m_γ^max = 229.8 MeV
- Fixed m_γ = 65 MeV used for angular dependence studies (about 1/2 of m_γ^max)
- The effect of electromagnetic form factors on the conversion factor is of order 1% at T_p = 190 MeV and 5-7% at T_p = 350 MeV
- At √q² = 230 MeV, time-like form factors of deuteron and ³He are increased by a factor of two compared to their values at q² = 0
- Deuteron quadrupole moment Q_d = 0.2859 fm²
- Magnetic moments (in nuclear magnetons): μ_p = 2.7928, μ_d = 0.85774, μ_h = -2.12755

## Context

This work extends the covariant impulse approximation model of Falter (1993) by enforcing gauge invariance through an internal amplitude, and uses realistic ³He wave functions from Schiavilla (1986) and Forest (1996) with Argonne v14/v18 NN interactions plus Urbana VII/IX three-nucleon forces. The model is tested against photodisintegration data from Bonn and Saclay at E_γ = 245 MeV, and predictions are made for ongoing dilepton production experiments at TSL (Uppsala) and KVI (Groningen). The approach enables study of the time-like region (q² > 0) accessible via dilepton production, particularly the longitudinal response and time-like form factors in the "unphysical" region between 4m_e² and 4m_p².
