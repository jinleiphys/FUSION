# Chiral $3π$-exchange potential using the method of unitary transformation

> Nuclear potentials are known to exhibit a considerable degree of scheme dependence. For one- and two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials, unitary ambiguities start showing up at the level of the leading relativistic corrections to the dominant static contributions. However, for the three-pion exchange potential, scheme-dependent contributions are expected to appear already at the static level. Here, we analyze the leading and subleading chiral $3π$-exchange NN potentials using the method of unitary transformation. In line with the expectations, our results for selected classes of contributions differ from those obtained by Kaiser using S-matrix matching. We present analytical expressions for the $3π$-exchange potential, which are off-shell consistent with the interactions used by the Bochum group, and discuss the numerical importance of the observed differences.

## Key claim
The static (leading-order) three-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potential is scheme-dependent, contrary to the static two-pion exchange potential; the authors show that results from the method of unitary transformation differ from those obtained by Kaiser using S-matrix matching for certain classes of reducible-like diagrams, while other classes coincide.

## Method
The authors rederive the leading (N³LO) and subleading (N⁴LO) chiral three-pion exchange NN potentials using the method of unitary transformation (MUT), which yields off-shell consistent potentials compatible with the Bochum group's nuclear forces and currents. For scheme-dependent contributions from reducible-like diagrams (classes VI, VIII, IX), they compare results from the S-matrix method (SMM) used by Kaiser with those from the MUT, employing either dispersive representations via Cutkosky cutting rules or direct coordinate-space calculations after Wick rotation.

## Key numbers
- The leading three-pion exchange contributions appear at chiral order Q⁴ (N³LO) and subleading at Q⁵ (N⁴LO), with the expansion parameter Q ∈ {|p|/Λ_b, M_π/Λ_χ}.
- The static two-pion exchange potential is unambiguously determined at order O(1) in the 1/m expansion, while the three-pion exchange potential shows scheme dependence already at the static level.
- The phases α_i for the additional unitary transformations are constrained: α₃ = -α₅, α₄ = 1/2 + 2α₅, and α₁ = -2α₂ = -1/2.
- The three-pion exchange potential is parameter-free within chiral EFT, with no low-energy constants appearing at the orders considered.

## Context
This work extends the derivation of chiral nuclear forces by the Bochum group (Epelbaum, Krebs, and collaborators, 1998–2020) to include three-pion exchange contributions that are off-shell consistent with their existing NN potentials, three-nucleon forces, and exchange currents. It contradicts the earlier results of Kaiser (1999–2001) for classes VI, VIII, and IX of the three-pion exchange diagrams, while confirming Kaiser's results for classes IV, XI, and XIII. The derived potentials enable consistent inclusion of three-pion exchange in future high-precision NN potentials and many-body calculations using the Bochum group's framework.
