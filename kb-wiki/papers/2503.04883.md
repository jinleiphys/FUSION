# Improved evaluation of the electroweak contribution to muon $g-2$

> A precise evaluation of the electroweak contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon requires control over all aspects of the Standard Model, ranging from Higgs physics, over multi-loop computations for bosonic and (heavy-)fermion diagrams, to non-perturbative effects in the presence of light quarks. Currently, the dominant uncertainties arise from such hadronic effects in the vector-vector-axial-vector three-point function, an improved understanding of which has recently emerged in the context of hadronic light-by-light scattering. Profiting from these developments as well as new perturbative and non-perturbative input for the charm contribution, we obtain $a_μ^\text{EW}=154.4(4)\times 10^{-11}$.

# Improved evaluation of the electroweak contribution to muon $g-2$

## Key claim
The electroweak contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment is evaluated as $a_\mu^\text{EW}=154.4(4)\times 10^{-11}$, which is almost $1\sigma$ larger than the previous evaluation from Aoyama (2020) and Czarnecki (2002), with the shift driven primarily by improved treatment of hadronic effects in the vector-vector-axial-vector (VVA) correlator, charm-loop corrections, and eta-eta' mixing, all of which happen to point in the same direction.

## Method
The authors evaluate the electroweak contribution by decomposing it into one-loop, two-loop bosonic, two-loop Higgs, two-loop VVA correlator contributions for each fermion generation, two-loop fermionic remainder (including gamma-Z mixing), and three-loop next-to-leading-logarithm terms. For the VVA correlator, they use a dispersive evaluation for the first generation (u,d,e) from Ludtke (2024), construct a simplified parametrization for the strange quark using eta-eta' and f1-f1' mixing weights, apply perturbative QCD corrections from Melnikov (2006) for heavy quarks, and model non-perturbative charm effects via eta_c(1S) and eta_c(2S) poles. The gamma-Z mixing is updated using lattice QCD input from Ce (2022) and data-driven evaluations of Delta_alpha_had.

## Key numbers
- Total electroweak contribution: $a_\mu^\text{EW} = 154.4(4) \times 10^{-11}$
- One-loop contribution: $194.79(1) \times 10^{-11}$ (unchanged from Aoyama 2020)
- Two-loop bosonic: $-19.96(0) \times 10^{-11}$ (unchanged)
- Two-loop Higgs: $-1.50(0) \times 10^{-11}$ (unchanged)
- Two-loop VVA [u,d,e]: $-2.08(3) \times 10^{-11}$ (previously $-2.28(20) \times 10^{-11}$)
- Two-loop VVA [c,s,mu]: $-4.14(28) \times 10^{-11}$ (previously $-4.63(30) \times 10^{-11}$)
- Two-loop VVA [t,b,tau]: $-8.12(1) \times 10^{-11}$ (previously $-8.21(10) \times 10^{-11}$)
- Two-loop fermionic remainder: $-4.58(10) \times 10^{-11}$ (previously $-4.64(10) \times 10^{-11}$)
- Three-loop NLL: $0.00(20) \times 10^{-11}$ (unchanged)
- Charm-loop alpha_s correction: $+0.48(24) \times 10^{-11}$
- First-generation VVA longitudinal component: $-0.892(10) \times 10^{-11}$
- First-generation VVA transversal component: $-1.192(29) \times 10^{-11}$
- Third-generation VVA total: $-8.12(1) \times 10^{-11}$
- Gamma-Z mixing value: $8\pi^2 \bar\Pi^{\gamma Z}(-M_Z^2) = 6.0(1)$

## Context
This work updates the electroweak contribution to the muon g-2, which was last evaluated comprehensively by Czarnecki (2002) and Gnendiger (2013) and included in the 2020 White Paper review by Aoyama (2020). The improvements leverage recent developments in the dispersive evaluation of hadronic light-by-light scattering by Hoferichter (2024), particularly the VVA correlator analysis by Ludtke (2024), as well as new perturbative QCD corrections from Melnikov (2006) and lattice QCD input for gamma-Z mixing from Ce (2022). The result enables a more precise Standard Model prediction for the muon anomalous magnetic moment, which is needed to match the experimental precision from the Fermilab experiment (projected to reach Delta a_mu_exp ~ 13 x 10^{-11}). Further refinements could target the second-generation VVA contribution and three-loop next-to-leading-logarithm estimates using modern effective field theory techniques.
