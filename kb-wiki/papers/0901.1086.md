# K- absorption in nuclei by two and three nucleons

> It will be shown that the peaks in the (Lambda p) and (Lambda d) invariant mass distributions, observed in recent FINUDA experiments and claimed to be signals of deeply bound kaonic states, are naturally explained in terms of K- absorption by two or three nucleons leaving the rest of the original nuclei as spectator. For reactions on heavy nuclei, the subsequent interactions of the particles produced in the primary absorption process with the residual nucleus play an important role. Our analyses leads to the conclusion that at present there is no experimental evidence of deeply bound K- state in nuclei. Although the FINUDA experiments have been done for reasons which are not supported a posteriori, some new physics can be extracted from the data.

## Key claim

The peaks in the (Λp) and (Λd) invariant mass distributions observed by FINUDA, previously claimed as evidence for deeply bound kaonic states, are naturally explained by K⁻ absorption on two or three nucleons with the rest of the nucleus acting as spectator, plus final-state interactions; the authors conclude that there is currently no experimental evidence for deeply bound K⁻ states in nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed detailed calculations of K⁻ absorption by two nucleons (producing Λp pairs) and by three nucleons (producing Λd pairs) in light nuclei such as ⁶Li, using known kaon atomic wave functions, nucleon binding energies, Fermi motion, and experimental cross sections for final-state rescattering of the produced particles with the residual nucleus. The calculations contain no free parameters and reproduce the observed invariant mass distributions by modeling the primary absorption kinematics and subsequent interactions.

## Key numbers

- The peak in the (Λp) invariant mass from FINUDA was previously interpreted as a K⁻pp bound state with 115 MeV binding and 67 MeV width; the authors show this peak is explained by two-nucleon absorption with final-state interactions.
- The peak in the (Λd) invariant mass from FINUDA on ⁶Li was interpreted as a bound K̄NNN state with 58 MeV binding and 37 MeV width; the authors show this peak is explained by three-nucleon absorption.
- The K⁻ absorption widths scale as Γ_NN ∝ ∫ d³r |Ψ_K⁻(r)|² ρ²(r) and Γ_NNN ∝ ∫ d³r |Ψ_K⁻(r)|² ρ³(r), where ρ(r) is the nuclear density.
- For light nuclei (⁶Li, ⁷Li, ⁹Be, ¹²C, ¹³C, ¹⁶O) K⁻ absorption takes place from the 2p orbit; for ²⁷Al from 3d; for ⁵¹V from 4f.
- The Λ-nucleon cross section parameterization used is σ_Λ = (39.66 − 100.45x + 92.44x² − 21.40x³)/p_LAB mb, where x = Min(2.1 GeV, p_LAB).

## Context

This work extends the earlier alternative explanation by Toki (2004) for the (K⁻_stop, p) peak on ⁴He, which was later confirmed by KEK (Sato, 2007) to be a broad bump rather than a narrow peak. It directly contradicts the FINUDA collaboration's interpretation (Agnello, 2005; 2007) of peaks as deeply bound kaonic states, and instead supports the three-body absorption mechanism suggested by Suzuki (2007) for the Λd channel. The results enable future experiments such as AMADEUS to properly account for absorption dynamics and final-state interactions before searching for possible K⁻ bound states.
