# N3LO NN interaction adjusted to light nuclei in ab exitu approach

> We use phase-equivalent transformations to adjust off-shell properties of similarity renormalization group evolved chiral effective field theory NN interaction (Idaho N3LO) to fit selected binding energies and spectra of light nuclei in an ab exitu approach. We then test the transformed interaction on a set of additional observables in light nuclei to verify that it provides reasonable descriptions of these observables with an apparent reduced need for three- and many-nucleon interactions.

## Key claim

The authors construct a two-nucleon (NN) interaction, Daejeon16, by applying phase-equivalent transformations (PETs) to an SRG-evolved chiral N3LO interaction, and show that it can describe binding energies and spectra of light nuclei up to A=16 without explicitly including three-nucleon (NNN) forces, though they acknowledge that such forces cannot be eliminated completely and that reduction below their natural chiral EFT size may constitute fine tuning that succeeds on a limited scale.

## Method

Starting from the Idaho N3LO chiral EFT NN interaction SRG-evolved with flow parameter λ=1.5 fm⁻¹, the authors apply phase-equivalent transformations (PETs) that mix the lowest oscillator components in each NN partial wave using an oscillator basis with ħΩ=25 MeV. The PET parameters are fitted to binding energies of ³H, ⁴He, ⁶Li, ⁸He, ¹⁰B, ¹²C, and ¹⁶O, plus excitation energies of selected narrow states, using the POUNDerS derivative-free algorithm within no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations performed with the MFDn code.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of ⁴He: 28.372(0) MeV (Daejeon16) vs. 28.296 MeV experimental
- Binding energy of ¹²C: 92.9(1) MeV (Daejeon16) vs. 92.162 MeV experimental
- Binding energy of ¹⁶O: 131.4(7) MeV (Daejeon16) vs. 127.619 MeV experimental
- Excitation energy of ²⁺ state in ¹²C: 4.57(15) MeV (Daejeon16) vs. 4.439 MeV experimental
- Excitation energy of ³⁺ state in ⁶Li: 1.91(1) MeV (Daejeon16) vs. 2.186 MeV experimental
- ¹²C point-proton rms radius from Daejeon16 crossing point: ≈2.29 fm vs. experimental 2.32(2) fm
- PET angles in degrees: ¹s₀ = -2.997, ³sd₁ = 4.461, ¹p₁ = 5.507, ³p₀ = 1.785, ³p₁ = 4.299, ³pf₂ = -2.031, ³d₂ = 7.833

## Context

This work extends the approach used to construct the JISP16 and JISP6 interactions (Shirokov et al., 2006-2007) by starting from a chiral EFT interaction (Entem and Machleidt, 2003) rather than a purely phenomenological inverse-scattering potential, and by employing SRG softening and improved extrapolation techniques (Maris et al., 2009). The Daejeon16 interaction provides better convergence and more accurate binding energies for p-shell nuclei than JISP16, particularly reducing the overbinding of ¹⁶O and the underbinding of neutron-rich helium isotopes, and enables future applications where consistent SRG- and PET-transformed electroweak operators can be derived from the underlying chiral EFT.
