# Anisotropic flow predictions for identified and strange hadrons in $O+O$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}$ = 7 TeV using model approaches

> In this study, we report the predictions for the flow observables for different centrality classes in $O+O$ collisions. Our predictions utilize two different approaches, hydrodynamic and transport models, to analyze the behavior of the flow coefficients for identified ($蟺^\pm$, $K^\pm$ and $p (\overline{p})$) and strange ($\mathrm{K}^{0}_{\mathrm S}$, $螞$ ($\overline螞$), $惟^{-}$ ($\overline惟^{+}$), $螢^{-}$ ($\overline螢^{+}$), $蠒$) hadrons. We explore particle-by-particle flow and compare the response of the system to initial conditions across various models, which provide insights into the underlying partonic and hadronic dynamics. The study presents comparisons of flow harmonics with the existing experimental measurements and demonstrates how $O+O$ collisions can serve as a benchmark to understand the transition from small to large systems, contributing to our knowledge of the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) and collective phenomena in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that oxygen-oxygen (O+O) collisions at 7 TeV will show anisotropic flow coefficients (v2, v3, v4) that overlap in final-state multiplicity with existing data from small systems (p+p, p+Pb) and large systems (Xe+Xe, Pb+Pb), suggesting that O+O collisions can serve as a benchmark to study the transition from small to large systems and the possible formation of a quark-gluon plasma (QGP). The hedge is that these are model predictions using EPOS4 and AMPT, not experimental measurements, and the overlap "indicates strong final-state interactions and collective behavior, which may hint at the possible formation of a strongly interacting medium with QGP-like properties."

## Method

The authors simulated O+O collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7 TeV using three model approaches: the EPOS4 event generator (which uses 3+1D viscous hydrodynamics), the default AMPT (AMPT-Def) transport model, and the string-melting AMPT (AMPT-SM) version. They computed anisotropic flow harmonics v2, v3, and v4 using the Q-cumulant method for multi-particle correlations, analyzing results as functions of charged-particle multiplicity and transverse momentum (pT) for identified hadrons (pions, kaons, protons) and strange hadrons (K0_S, Lambda, Omega, Xi, phi). Approximately 4 million minimum-bias events were generated from each AMPT version and 1.5 million from EPOS4.

## Key numbers

- O+O collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7 TeV; approximately 4 million minimum-bias events from AMPT-SM and AMPT-Def, and approximately 1.5 million from EPOS4.
- v2 and v3 for all charged hadrons were shown as functions of pT in 0-5% central O+O collisions; both models predict an increasing trend in v2 with increasing pT, with AMPT-SM giving higher v2 than EPOS4 and AMPT-Def.
- v2 for identified hadrons (pi+, pi-, K+, K-, p, antiproton) and strange hadrons (Lambda, anti-Lambda) was shown in 0-10% central O+O collisions; all particles show an increasing trend with pT, with higher v2 at intermediate pT.

## Context

This work extends the study of anisotropic flow from small systems (p+p, p+Pb) and large systems (Xe+Xe, Pb+Pb) to the intermediate O+O system, using the same model frameworks (EPOS4 and AMPT) that have been applied at RHIC and LHC energies. The predictions are compared with experimental data from STAR (2005), PHENIX (2004), and ALICE (2010) collaborations. The results enable future comparisons with upcoming LHC O+O data, which will help disentangle initial-state effects from final-state collective behavior and improve understanding of QGP formation across system sizes.
