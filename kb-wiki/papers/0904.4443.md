# Contribution from Duality Violations to the theoretical error on alpha_s

> Duality Violations (DVs) is a nickname for the failure of the Operator Product Expansion to describe QCD correlators on the physical axis. Using a physically motivated ansatz, a fit to the spectral functions allows us to get a quantitative estimate for the amount of DVs present in tau data. The quality of the fit turns out to be better than expected. Since DVs have not been included in the past in the determination of alpha_s, they amount to an additional theoretical error which we estimate could be δalpha_s(m_{tau}) \sim 0.003-0.010 . Our ansatz satisfies, in particular, the 1st Weinberg sum rule, which shows that this sum rule is not enough to force DVs to vanish.

## Key claim

Duality violations (DVs) contribute an additional theoretical error to the determination of \(\alpha_s\) from tau decay, estimated as \(\delta\alpha_s(m_{\tau}) \sim 0.003-0.010\), which has not been included in previous analyses; the first Weinberg sum rule alone is insufficient to force DVs to vanish at the tau mass scale.

## Method

The authors fit a physically motivated ansatz for the DV contribution, an exponential times a sinusoidal function, to the vector and axial-vector spectral functions from ALEPH tau decay data in the window \(1.1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2 \leq s \leq m_{\tau}^2\). The fit combines fixed-order perturbation theory with the DV parametrization, and the resulting parameters are used to extrapolate the spectral functions to infinite energy, enabling evaluation of the first Weinberg sum rule and estimation of the DV-induced shift in \(\alpha_s\).

## Key numbers

- Fit to vector spectral function: \(\kappa_V = 0.018 \pm 0.004\), \(\gamma_V = 0.15 \pm 0.15\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\), \(\alpha_V = 2.2 \pm 0.3\), \(\beta_V = 2.0 \pm 0.1\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\), with \(\chi^2/\mathrm{dof} = 10/79 \approx 0.13\).
- Fit to axial-vector spectral function: \(\kappa_A = 0.20 \pm 0.06\), \(\gamma_A = 1.7 \pm 0.2\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\), \(\alpha_A = -0.4 \pm 0.1\), \(\beta_A = -3.0 \pm 0.1\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\), with \(\chi^2/\mathrm{dof} = 17/78 \approx 0.22\).
- Estimated theoretical error from DVs on \(\alpha_s(m_{\tau})\): \(\delta\alpha_s(m_{\tau}) \sim 0.003-0.010\).
- Simultaneous fit to tau and \(e^+e^-\) vector data (above \(4\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\)): \(\kappa_V = 0.024 \pm 0.004\), \(\gamma_V = 0.40 \pm 0.12\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\), \(\alpha_V = 1.82 \pm 0.19\), \(\beta_V = 2.14 \pm 0.11\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\), \(\alpha'_V = 5.2 \pm 1.4\), with \(\chi^2/\mathrm{dof} = 22/87 \approx 0.25\); this reduces the DV error by a factor of about 3.

## Context

This work extends earlier determinations of \(\alpha_s\) from tau decay (e.g., by the ALEPH collaboration and analyses by Davier et al.) by explicitly quantifying the contribution of duality violations, which had been assumed negligible in all prior extractions. The authors challenge the assumption that the operator product expansion can be analytically continued to the Minkowski axis without significant error, a practice dating back to Shankar (1977) and the pioneering work of Braaten, Narison, and Pich. The results enable a more complete theoretical error budget for \(\alpha_s\) from tau data and motivate further study of DVs, possibly using \(e^+e^-\) data or lattice QCD.
