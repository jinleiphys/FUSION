# Evolution of particle density in high-energy pp collisions

> We study the evolution of the particle density, dn/dηat fixed ηwith the beam rapidity Y in the framework of string percolation model. Our main results are: (i) The width of the "plateau" increases proportionally to Y, (ii) limiting fragmentation is violated, and (iii) the particle density, reduces to a step function.

# Evolution of particle density in high-energy pp collisions

## Key claim
Within the string percolation model, the width of the central pseudorapidity plateau in pp collisions grows proportionally to beam rapidity Y (Δy ≈ 1.4 Y), leading to violation of limiting fragmentation and an eventual evolution of the particle density toward a step function, provided the transverse string density exceeds the percolation threshold ηᵗ_c ≈ 1.15–1.5.

## Method
The authors model pp collisions using string percolation, where strings formed between projectile and target overlap and fuse into clusters above a critical transverse density. They parameterize the single-string rapidity distribution as a Fermi-like function and multiply it by an exponential growth factor e^{λY} (with λ ≈ 0.23) to obtain the full pp pseudorapidity density dn/dη, then fit this expression to charged-hadron data from √s = 53 GeV to 7 TeV. The evolution of dn/dη at fixed η is studied analytically as Y increases, comparing the plateau region (η ≤ (1−α)Y) and the fragmentation region (η ≥ (1−α)Y).

## Key numbers
- Plateau width Δy ≈ 1.4 Y, where Y = ln(√s/m_p) is the beam rapidity.
- Single string transverse radius r₀ ≈ 0.2–0.3 fm; proton transverse radius R_p ≈ 1 fm.
- Critical transverse density for percolation ηᵗ_c ≈ 1.15–1.5.
- Fitted parameters: λ = 0.23 ± 0.005, δ = 0.61 ± 0.15, α = 0.27 ± 0.03, a = 0.8 ± 0.2.
- Ratio α/λ = 1.17 ± 0.15, compared to δ = 0.61 ± 0.15, showing violation of the limiting fragmentation condition α/λ = δ.
- Effective Jacobian parameter k fixed at 1.2, corresponding to m_π ≈ 0.14 GeV and average p_T ≈ 0.3 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the string percolation model of Dias de Deus and Ugoccioni (2000) and Brogueira, Dias de Deus, and Pajares (2007) to pp collisions, showing that the plateau growth and limiting fragmentation violation seen in heavy-ion collisions also occur in pp systems. The results favor saturation models such as the Color Glass Condensate (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1994) and explain long-range rapidity correlations and the ridge phenomenon observed at RHIC and LHC. The approach enables predictions for forward-backward correlation parameters and ridge structure in pp, pA, and AA collisions at higher energies.
