# Cross Section Oscillations in the Coherent Charmonium Photoproduction off Nuclei at Moderate Energies

> We calculate the coherent charmonium photoproduction at intermediate energies accounting for the physics of the charmonium bound states and the dependence of the cross section on the region occupied by color using a correspondingly adjusted generalized vector dominance model (GVDM). In the photon energy domain where the coherence lengths are comparable to the average internucleon distances in nuclei and the nuclear radii we found that significant oscillations of the total and forward photoproduction cross sections governed by the longitudinal nuclear form factor are strongly modified by the charmonium rescatterings accounting for the nondiagonal transitions related to the color screening phenomenon. We discuss how these oscillations can influence the determination of the genuine charmonium-nucleon cross sections in the forthcoming SLAC E160 experiment on low energy J/psi and psi' photoproduction off nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that at moderate photon energies (20-60 GeV), where the coherence length for charmonium photoproduction is comparable to internucleon distances, the total and forward cross sections for coherent J/ψ and ψ' photoproduction off nuclei exhibit significant oscillations governed by the longitudinal nuclear form factor, which are strongly modified by charmonium rescatterings including nondiagonal transitions related to color screening.

## Method

The authors constructed a Generalized Glauber Model (GGM) combining a multistep production Glauber model with a Generalized Vector Dominance Model (GVDM) adjusted to account for color screening. They solved coupled two-channel eikonal equations for J/ψ and ψ' states using a Hartree-Fock-Skyrme nuclear density for lead, with elementary forward amplitudes fixed by fitting to SLAC and HERA photoproduction data and the optical theorem. The model accounts for diagonal (ψN → ψN) and nondiagonal (ψN ⇄ ψ'N) rescatterings, with the longitudinal momentum transfer in the γ → V transition producing the oscillatory behavior.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy range considered: 20 GeV ≤ ω ≤ 60 GeV
- At s₀ = 40.4 GeV², dσ(γN → J/ψN)/dt at t-t_min=0 = 17.8 ± 1.5 nb GeV⁻²
- At s₀ = 188.9 GeV², dσ(γN → J/ψN)/dt at t-t_min=0 = 40 ± 13 nb GeV⁻²
- σ(γN → ψ'N)/σ(γN → J/ψN) = 0.15 for forward photoproduction
- σ(J/ψ N)(s=s₀) = 3.5 ± 0.8 mb at s₀ = 38.5 GeV²
- σ(ψ' N) ≈ 8 mb as found from the GVDM
- The ratio σ(ψ' N)/σ(J/ψ N) ≈ 5-6 from SLAC data combined with nucleus-nucleus collision data
- The mixing angle between ψ' and ψ'' states: θ = 19° ± 2°
- Γ(ψ → e⁺e⁻) = 5.26 ± 0.37 keV and Γ(ψ' → e⁺e⁻) = 2.12 ± 0.18 keV
- f_ψ²/4π = 10.5 ± 0.7 and f_ψ'²/4π = 30.9 ± 2.6
- The suppression of J/ψ yield in GGM vs. Impulse Approximation is ≈ 15-20% at ω ≤ 40 GeV and ≈ 6-7% at ω ≥ 40 GeV

## Context

This work extends the Generalized Vector Dominance Model approach previously applied to light meson production (Shaw; Frankfurt, Strikman, Zhalov) to charmonium photoproduction at intermediate energies, where the coherence length is comparable to nuclear dimensions. The paper addresses the long-standing problem of extracting genuine J/ψN and ψ'N cross sections from nuclear photoproduction data, which have large uncertainties (1-8 mb for J/ψN and 0.8-20 mb for ψ'N as reviewed by Vogt). The results are directly relevant to the planned SLAC E160 experiment, and the authors suggest that measuring the ψ'-to-J/ψ ratio removes nuclear model dependence, while a double ratio using two different nuclei can also remove dependence on elementary photoproduction cross sections.
