# Properties of hot finite nuclei and associated correlations with infinite nuclear matter

> This work aim to study the various thermal characteristics of nuclei in view of the saturation and critical behavior of infinite nuclear matter. The free energy of a nucleus is parametrized using the density and temperature-dependent liquid-drop model and interaction among nucleons is worked out within the effective relativistic mean-field theory (E-RMF). The effective mass (m,$^*$) and critical temperature of infinite symmetric nuclear matter ($T_c$) of a given E-RMF parameter force play a seminal role in the estimation of thermal properties. A larger (m$^*$) and $T_c$ of the E-RMF set estimate larger excitation energy, level density, and limiting temperature $(T_l)$ for a given nucleus. The limiting temperature of a nucleus also depends on the behavior of the nuclear gas surrounding the nucleus, making the equation of state (EoS) at subsaturation densities an important input. A stiff EoS in the subsaturation region estimates a higher pressure of the nuclear gas making it less stable. Since the $T_c$ plays an important part in these calculations, we perform a Pearson correlation statistical study of fifteen E-RMF parameter sets, satisfying the relevant constraint on EoS. Effective mass seems to govern the thermal characteristics of infinite as well as finite nuclear matter in the framework of E-RMF theory.

## Key claim

The limiting temperature of a hot nucleus depends not only on the effective mass and critical temperature of infinite symmetric nuclear matter from a given E-RMF parameter set, but also on the stiffness of the equation of state at subsaturation densities, because a stiffer EoS in that region produces higher nuclear gas pressure and makes the nucleus less stable.

## Method

The free energy of a finite nucleus was parametrized using a density- and temperature-dependent liquid-drop model, with the volume term computed from infinite symmetric nuclear matter within effective relativistic mean-field (E-RMF) theory. Finite-size corrections for surface, symmetry, and Coulomb energies were added, and the limiting temperature was found by solving modified Gibbs phase-equilibrium conditions between a spherical liquid drop and a surrounding nucleon gas. Fifteen E-RMF parameter sets satisfying relevant EoS constraints were used, and a Pearson correlation study was performed among saturation properties, critical temperature, and limiting temperature.

## Key numbers

* The limiting temperature for mass region A ≈ 90 was found to be ≈ 5.6 MeV in the ALADIN experiment (cited in the paper).
* The shell melting temperature T_m is approximately 1–2 MeV.
* The freeze-out volume for multi-fragmentation is ≈ 7 V_0.
* The surface energy coefficient α_s(ρ_0, T=0) was taken as 1.15 MeV fm⁻².
* The symmetry energy J of cold symmetric nuclear matter was taken as 31 MeV.
* The surface thickness parameter b ≈ 0.72(1 + 0.009 T²) fm.
* The G3 parameter set has the largest effective mass m*/m = 0.699; FSUGarnet has the smallest m*/m = 0.578.
* The critical temperature T_c for G3 is 15.3 MeV; for NL3 it is 13.75 MeV.
* Level density parameters for ²⁰⁸Pb using Eq. (s1) range from 15.752 MeV⁻¹ (FSUGarnet) to 17.126 MeV⁻¹ (G3) for the E*/T² definition.
* The fission barrier for ²³⁶U almost vanishes at T/T_c ≈ 0.4 for all forces.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of critical properties of infinite symmetric nuclear matter (Parmar et al., 2022) to finite nuclei, using the same E-RMF parameter sets. It compares with microscopic calculations by Bonche et al. (1984) and Quddus et al. (2018), and with experimental limiting temperatures from Natowitz et al. and the ALADIN collaboration. The results enable systematic correlation of infinite-matter saturation properties (especially effective mass and critical temperature) with finite-nucleus thermal properties such as excitation energy, level density, and limiting temperature, and can inform models of nuclear multi-fragmentation and neutron star crust structure.
