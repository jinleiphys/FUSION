# Non-relativistic trace anomaly and equation of state in dense fermionic matter

> We theoretically investigate a non-relativistic trace anomaly and its impact on the low-temperature equation of state in spatially one-dimensional three-component fermionic systems with a three-body interaction, which exhibit a non-trivial three-body crossover from a bound trimer gas to dense fermionic matter with increasing density. By applying the $G$-matrix approach to the three-body interaction, we obtain the analytical expression for the ground-state equation of state relevant to the high-density degenerate regime and thereby address how the three-body contact or, equivalently, the trace anomaly emerges. The analytical results are compared with the recent quantum Monte Carlo data. Our study of the trace anomaly and the sound speed could have some relevance to the physics of hadron-quark crossover in compact stars.

# Non-relativistic trace anomaly and equation of state in dense fermionic matter

## Key claim

The authors derive an analytical expression for the ground-state equation of state of non-relativistic one-dimensional three-component fermions with a three-body interaction, showing that the trace anomaly (quantified by the three-body contact) increases with chemical potential in the high-density regime where the system is asymptotically free. The results are qualitatively consistent with quantum Monte Carlo data, but the authors note that their simplified approach underestimates the three-body contact and does not fully capture the sound velocity behavior without including phenomenological corrections for nonzero center-of-mass momentum correlations.

## Method

The authors apply the Brueckner G-matrix approach to a one-dimensional three-component Fermi gas with a contact-type three-body interaction, summing an infinite series of three-body ladder diagrams to obtain an in-medium effective interaction. They evaluate the internal energy as a Hartree-Fock-like expectation value using the G-matrix at zero center-of-mass momentum and the three-body binding energy, then derive the chemical potential, pressure, three-body contact, and sound velocity via thermodynamic identities. The results are compared with quantum Monte Carlo data from PhysRevA.102.023313 at finite temperature.

## Key numbers

- The three-body coupling constant is regularized using the three-body binding energy E_b, with 1/g_3 = -(m/(2√3 π)) ln((mE_b + Λ²)/(mE_b)), where Λ is the momentum cutoff.
- The internal energy in the high-density limit (E_F >> E_b) is E ≈ (1/3)ρE_F - (4√3/(3π))ρE_F / ln(3E_F/E_b).
- The three-body contact C_3 = (8√3/(3π)) ρE_F × (3E_F/E_b) / [(1 + 3E_F/E_b)(ln(1 + 3E_F/E_b))²].
- The squared sound velocity in the high-density limit follows c_s²/v_F² ≈ 1 - (4√3)/(π ln(3E_F/E_b)).
- The effective trimer Fermi momentum used for phenomenological corrections is K_T = √(6m(3E_F + E_b)).

## Context

This work extends the G-matrix approach previously applied to two-dimensional two-component Fermi gases with two-body interactions (PhysRevA.84.033607, klawunn2016equation, PhysRevA.107.053313) to a one-dimensional three-component system with three-body interactions. The system exhibits a three-body crossover from a bound trimer gas to dense fermionic matter, analogous to the BEC-BCS crossover in two-component Fermi gases. The study is motivated by the hadron-quark crossover scenario in compact stars (PhysRevLett.82.3956, baym2018hadrons, kojo2021qcd), where a similar trace anomaly and sound velocity peak appear in dense QCD (PhysRevLett.129.252702). The results enable future investigation of three-body correlations with nonzero center-of-mass momenta and trimer-trimer interactions, and could be applied to constituent quark models with three-body confining forces.
