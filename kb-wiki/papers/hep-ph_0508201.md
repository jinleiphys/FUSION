# Azimuthal Asymmetry of Direct Photons in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> We show that a sizeable azimuthal asymmetry, characterized by a coefficient v_2, is to be expected for direct photons produced in non-central high energy nuclear collisions. This signal is generated by photons radiated by jets interacting with the surrounding hot plasma. The anisotropy is out of phase by an angle $π/2$ with respect to that associated with the elliptic anisotropy of hadrons, leading to negative values of v_2. Such an asymmetry, if observed, could be a signature for the presence of a quark gluon plasma and would establish the importance of jet-plasma interactions as a source of electromagnetic radiation.

# Azimuthal Asymmetry of Direct Photons in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim
The authors predict that direct photons produced in non-central high energy nuclear collisions will exhibit a sizeable azimuthal asymmetry (v₂) that is out of phase by π/2 with respect to hadron elliptic flow, leading to negative v₂ values. This negative v₂ arises specifically from jet-plasma interactions (induced bremsstrahlung and jet-photon conversion) and would serve as a signature for quark-gluon plasma formation, but only if the signal can be disentangled from the positive v₂ contributions from fragmentation photons and the dilution from isotropic primary hard and thermal photons.

## Method
The authors calculate direct photon v₂ for Au+Au collisions at RHIC (√s=200 GeV) by modeling four photon sources: primary hard Compton/annihilation processes, jet fragmentation photons, jet-photon conversion, and medium-induced bremsstrahlung from jets. Jet energy loss is implemented using the complete leading-order AMY (Arnold, Moore, Yaffe) formalism, and the spatial anisotropy of the nuclear overlap zone is translated into momentum anisotropy through path-length-dependent photon production. The calculation uses initial conditions constrained by charged particle pseudorapidity densities from PHOBOS, with initial time τᵢ=0.26 fm/c and initial temperatures varying by centrality class.

## Key numbers
- Initial temperatures: Tᵢ=370 MeV (0-20% centrality), 360 MeV (20-40% centrality), and 310 MeV (40-60% centrality) for Au+Au collisions at √s=200 GeV
- Direct photon v₂ reaches about 2-3% for the 20-40% centrality bin around p_T=4 GeV/c
- Direct photon v₂ reaches up to 5% for the more peripheral (40-60%) centrality bin
- Above 8 GeV/c, the direct photon v₂ becomes positive again as fragmentation photons dominate over medium-induced bremsstrahlung
- A 40% change in initial temperature Tᵢ generates a shift in v₂ of less than 20%
- Isolated direct photon processes (including jet-photon conversion) show v₂ up to 5% negative at large p_T

## Context
This work extends the study of jet-medium interactions in heavy ion collisions, building on prior calculations of photon production from jets (FMS:02, Zakharov:04, TGJM:05) and thermal radiation from QGP (KaLiSei:91). The authors introduce a novel "inverse optical" mechanism for photon v₂ that is distinct from the hydrodynamic flow mechanism for hadrons (Olli:92) and the optical absorption mechanism for hadrons from jets (STAR:02v2). The calculation is compared to PHENIX inclusive photon v₂ data (Adler:2005rg) and shows good agreement when decays from recombined pions are included. The results suggest that separating jet-associated from isolated direct photons could provide a clean signature for QGP formation and constrain models of jet energy loss, and the same arguments apply to dilepton production from jets in the medium.
