# Dynamics of 1S0 diproton formation in the reactions pp-> (pp)_s+pi0$ and pp-> (pp)_s+gamma in the GeV region

> COSY-ANKE data on the cross section of the reactions $pp\to \{pp\}_sπ^0$ and $pp\to \{pp\}_sγ$, where $\{pp\}_s$ is the proton pair in the $^1S_0$ state at small excitation energy $E_{pp}<3$ MeV, are analyzed at beam energies $0.5 - 2.0$ GeV within the one-pion exchange model. The model includes the subprocesses $π^0 p\to π^0 p$ and $π^0 p\to γp$ for the pion- and photo-production, respectively, and accounts for the final state pp-interaction. A broad maximum in the energy dependence of the $π^0$ production at $\sim 0.5 -0.6$ GeV and fast increase of the $γ$-production cross section at $0.3 - 0.55$ GeV observed in the data are explained by the $Δ$-isobar contribution. An analogy with the dynamics of the deuteron breakup reaction $pd\to \{pp\}_sn$ in the $Δ$-region is outlined.

# Dynamics of 1S0 diproton formation in the reactions pp→ (pp)_s+π0 and pp→ (pp)_s+γ in the GeV region

## Key claim
Despite parity and angular momentum conservation forbidding S-wave ΔN intermediate states in pp→{pp}_sπ0 and M1 transitions in pp→{pp}_sγ, the Δ(1232)-isobar contribution remains significant in both reactions at beam energies 0.5–2.0 GeV, indicating that short-range NN-dynamics effects are minor in this energy region rather than being revealed as expected.

## Method
The author analyzes COSY-ANKE data using a one-pion exchange (OPE) model that includes the subprocesses π0p→π0p for pion production and π0p→γp for photoproduction, with final-state pp(1S0) interaction included via the CD Bonn potential. The impulse approximation is employed, taking the forward π0p→π0p and π0p→γp amplitudes from data, with a monopole formfactor cutoff Λ=0.65 GeV/c at the πNN vertex. The formalism is analogous to that previously developed for the pd→{pp}_sn reaction.

## Key numbers
- The cross section of pp→{pp}_sπ0 shows a broad maximum at beam energies 0.5–1.4 GeV, similar in shape and position to the Δ-maximum in pp→dπ+.
- The OPE model overestimates the pp→{pp}_sπ0 absolute cross section by a factor of 6 when using the impulse approximation.
- For pp→{pp}_sγ, the OPE model (scaled by factor 0.8) explains the observed rise in cross section at 0.3–0.55 GeV and a second bump at 1.6 GeV related to heavier nucleon isobars.
- The excitation energy of the diproton is restricted to E_pp < 3 MeV.
- The ONE mechanism in pd→{pp}_sn has a minimum at 0.8 GeV due to the repulsive core at r_NN ~ 0.5 fm, while the Δ contribution has a maximum at 0.6 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the OPE model previously applied to pd→{pp}_sn (Uzikov and collaborators, 2003–2008) to the diproton production channels pp→{pp}_sπ0 and pp→{pp}_sγ. It contradicts the expectation from Niskanen (earlier work) that Δ-isobar contributions should be strongly suppressed in these reactions compared to pp→dπ+ and γd→pn. The analysis suggests that the CD Bonn soft NN potential provides agreement with data, while harder potentials like Paris or RSC overestimate high-momentum components. The results enable future studies of short-range NN dynamics at higher energies where Δ contributions may diminish.
