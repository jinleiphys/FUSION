# Minijet Initial Conditions For Non-Equilibrium Parton Evolution at RHIC and LHC

> An important ingredient for the non-equilibrium evolution of partons at RHIC and LHC is to have some physically reasonable initial conditions for the single particle phase space distribution functions for the partons. We consider several plausible parametrizations of initial conditions for the single particle distribution function f_i(x,p) and fix the parameters by matching \int f(x,p)p^μd σ_μto the invariant momentum space semi-hard parton distributions obtained using perturbative QCD (pQCD), as well as fitting low order moments of the distribution function. We consider parametrizations of f_i(x,p) with both boost invariant and boost non-invariant assumptions. We determine the initial number density, energy density and the corresponding (effective) temperature of the minijet plasma at RHIC and LHC energies. For a boost non-invariant minijet phase-space distribution function we obtain ~ 30(140) /fm^3 as the initial number density, ~ 50(520) GeV/fm^3 as the initial energy density and ~ 520(930) MeV as the corresponding initial effective temperature at RHIC(LHC).

## Key claim

The paper provides physically reasonable initial conditions for the single-particle phase-space distribution function of minijet partons at RHIC and LHC, but the results depend on whether one assumes boost invariance or a boost non-invariant parametrization; the boost non-invariant case is argued to be more realistic because the actual pQCD minijet rapidity distribution is not flat over the entire rapidity range.

## Method

The authors used perturbative QCD (pQCD) with 2-to-2 parton scattering processes (gluon-gluon and gluon-quark) to compute the minijet cross section, number distribution, and transverse energy distribution for central Au-Au collisions. They then parametrized the phase-space distribution function f(x,p) with a Gaussian correlation between coordinate rapidity eta and momentum rapidity y, fixing the width sigma^2 by matching the first p_T moment of the distribution to the pQCD-predicted E_T distribution. Both boost-invariant and boost-non-invariant forms were considered, using GRV98 parton distribution functions with EKS98 nuclear shadowing and a K factor of 2.

## Key numbers

- Minimum transverse momentum cut-off p0 = 1 GeV at RHIC and 2 GeV at LHC
- For the boost non-invariant case at mid-rapidity (eta=0): initial number density ~ 30/fm^3 at RHIC and ~ 140/fm^3 at LHC
- For the boost non-invariant case at mid-rapidity (eta=0): initial energy density ~ 50 GeV/fm^3 at RHIC and ~ 520 GeV/fm^3 at LHC
- For the boost non-invariant case at mid-rapidity (eta=0): initial effective temperature ~ 520 MeV at RHIC and ~ 930 MeV at LHC
- For the boost invariant case: initial number density 20/fm^3 at RHIC and 85/fm^3 at LHC
- For the boost invariant case: initial energy density 31 GeV/fm^3 at RHIC and 287 GeV/fm^3 at LHC
- For the boost invariant case: effective temperature 460 MeV at RHIC and 800 MeV at LHC
- Width parameter sigma^2 = 0.28 for the boost non-invariant case at both RHIC and LHC; sigma^2 = 0.0034 at RHIC and 0.0015 at LHC for the boost invariant case

## Context

This work extends earlier approaches that used the Bjorken (1983) scaling formula to extract energy density from transverse energy distributions, which assumes uniform parton distribution in coordinate space and flat rapidity distributions. It differs from the parton cascade model of Geiger and Muller (1992) which used momentum space structure functions with coordinate distributions of nucleons directly in transport equations, and from the earlier work of Nayak (2002) which used a boost-invariant Boltzmann form. The results provide initial conditions for non-equilibrium parton evolution that can be used to study thermalization of the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC, with the boost non-invariant case minimally altering the pQCD p_T and y distributions.
