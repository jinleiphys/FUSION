# Correcting for neutron width fluctuations in Hauser-Feshbach gamma branching ratios

> Porter-Thomas fluctuations of neutron widths skew compound nuclear decay probabilities from their statistical Hauser-Feshbach values. We present a straightforward method to correct Hauser-Feshbach calculations for these fluctuations, useful for modeling near-threshold competition between gamma and neutron emission following beta decay or when standard width fluctuation corrections are inadequate.

# Correcting for neutron width fluctuations in Hauser-Feshbach gamma branching ratios

## Key claim

Porter-Thomas fluctuations of neutron partial widths can significantly enhance gamma branching ratios relative to standard Hauser-Feshbach predictions when there are few final states available in the neutron exit channel, and this enhancement can be captured by a simple analytic correction factor without performing expensive Monte Carlo cascade simulations.

## Method

The authors performed numerical Monte Carlo simulations (10^6 samples) of gamma branching ratios by randomly generating neutron total widths from chi-squared distributions with k degrees of freedom (where k is the number of neutron partial widths), assuming gamma total widths are constant due to many contributing transitions. They then defined a Porter-Thomas width fluctuation correction (PT WFC) factor W(k, y) that relates the exact simulated gamma branching ratio to the Hauser-Feshbach estimate, and applied this correction factor to a Hauser-Feshbach calculation of beta-delayed gamma emission from Valencia et al. (2017).

## Key numbers

- For k=1 neutron partial width with equal average neutron and gamma widths, the simulated gamma branching ratio is 0.66, a 33% enhancement over the Hauser-Feshbach prediction of 0.5.
- For k=100 neutron partial widths with equal average widths, the simulated gamma branching ratio is 0.503, close to the Hauser-Feshbach prediction.
- The PT WFC factor can produce up to two orders of magnitude enhancement at the smallest Hauser-Feshbach gamma branching ratio y=10^-5 when k=1.
- For all curves to have an enhancement below 1.1, one requires k > 22 neutron partial widths.
- By k=5 neutron partial widths, all correction curves are below an enhancement factor of 2.

## Context

This work extends the findings of Valencia et al. (2017), who showed that Hauser-Feshbach theory greatly under-predicts observed gamma branching ratios in beta-delayed neutron emission and that including Porter-Thomas fluctuations via a custom Monte Carlo program (similar to DICEBOX by Becvar (1998)) significantly improves agreement. The authors provide a simpler alternative to such costly Monte Carlo cascade calculations by introducing a Moldauer-type correction factor (inspired by Moldauer (1976, 1980) and Hilaire (2003)) that can be applied directly to existing Hauser-Feshbach codes. This enables modeling of near-threshold competition between gamma and neutron emission following beta decay without requiring dedicated simulation programs.
