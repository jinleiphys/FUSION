# Analysis of the data on spin density matrix elements for $γp \to K^{*+}Λ$

> In our previous work [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 96}, 035206 (2017)], the high-precision differential cross-section data for $γp \to K^{*+}Λ$ reported by the CLAS Collaboration has been analyzed within an effective Lagrangian approach. It was found that apart from the $t$-channel $K$, $K^*$, and $κ$ exchanges, the $u$-channel $Λ$, $Σ$, and $Σ^*$ exchanges, the $s$-channel $N$ exchange, and the interaction current, one needs to introduce at least two nucleon resonances in the $s$ channel in constructing the reaction amplitudes to describe the cross-section data. One of the needed resonances is $N(2060)5/2^-$, and the other one could be one of the $N(2000)5/2^+$, $N(2040)3/2^+$, $N(2100)1/2^+$, $N(2120)3/2^-$, and $N(2190)7/2^-$ resonances. In this paper, we further include in our analysis the data on spin density matrix elements for $K^*$ meson reported recently by the CLAS Collaboration, with the purpose being to impose further constraints on extracting the resonance contents and to gain a better understanding of the reaction mechanism. It turns out that with the new data on spin density matrix elements taken into account, only the set with the $N(2060)5/2^-$ and $N(2000)5/2^+$ resonances among those five possible solutions extracted from the analysis of the differential cross-section data can satisfactorily describe the data on both the differential cross sections and the spin density matrix elements. Further analysis shows that this reaction is dominated by the $t$-channel $K$ exchange and $s$-channel $N(2060)5/2^-$ and $N(2000)5/2^+$ exchanges.

# Analysis of the data on spin density matrix elements for γp → K*+Λ

## Key claim

When spin density matrix element data from CLAS are included alongside differential cross-section data, only one of the five previously viable resonance combinations, the set containing N(2060)5/2⁻ and N(2000)5/2⁺, can satisfactorily describe both observables simultaneously, whereas the abstract's mention of five possible solutions applies only to the cross-section-only analysis.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach to compute the full photoproduction amplitude for γp → K*+Λ, including t-channel K, K*, and κ exchanges; u-channel Λ, Σ, and Σ* exchanges; s-channel N exchange and nucleon resonance exchanges; and an interaction current constructed to satisfy gauge invariance via the generalized Ward-Takahashi identity. They fitted model parameters to the combined dataset of differential cross sections (191 data points) and spin density matrix elements ρ₀₀, ρ₁₋₁, and Re ρ₁₀ (180 data points each) using MINUIT, testing all 15 combinations of the six nucleon resonances near the K*Λ threshold.

## Key numbers

- The preferred fit includes N(2000)5/2⁺ and N(2060)5/2⁻ resonances, with fitted mass M_R = 2010 ± 1 MeV for N(2000)5/2⁺ and M_R = 2043 ± 12 MeV for N(2060)5/2⁻.
- The fitted width of N(2060)5/2⁻ is Γ_R = 79 ± 3 MeV, much smaller than the PDG range of 300–450 MeV.
- The reduced helicity amplitude √β_{ΛK*} A_{3/2} for N(2060)5/2⁻ is −1.40 ± 0.05 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/².
- The t-channel K exchange cutoff mass is Λ_K = 1009 ± 2 MeV.
- The global χ²/N for the preferred fit is 5.3 over N = 731 total data points; the χ²_{dσ}/N_{dσ} for differential cross sections in the energy region W ≤ 2217 MeV is 1.2 (45 data points).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous effective Lagrangian analysis of γp → K*+Λ (Wei et al., Phys. Rev. C 96, 035206, 2017), which had identified five equally viable resonance combinations from differential cross-section data alone. By incorporating the spin density matrix element data recently reported by the CLAS Collaboration (Anisovich et al., 2017), the present analysis narrows the resonance content to a unique combination. The results enable future studies to focus on the N(2000)5/2⁺ and N(2060)5/2⁻ resonances, though the authors note that the available data remain insufficient to constrain models with three or more resonances.
