# Microscopic optical potentials for $^4$He scattering

> We present a reliable double-folding (DF) model for $^{4}$He-nucleus scattering, using the Melbourne $g$-matrix nucleon-nucleon interaction that explains nucleon-nucleus scattering with no adjustable parameter. In the DF model, only the target density is taken as the local density in the Melbourne $g$-matrix. For $^{4}$He elastic scattering from $^{58}$Ni and $^{208}$Pb targets in a wide range of incident energies from 20~MeV/nucleon to 200~MeV/nucleon, the DF model with the target-density approximation (TDA) yields much better agreement with the experimental data than the usual DF model with the frozen-density approximation in which the sum of projectile and target densities is taken as the local density. We also discuss the relation between the DF model with the TDA and the conventional folding model in which the nucleon-nucleus potential is folded with the $^{4}$He density.

## Key claim

The double-folding (DF) model using the Melbourne *g*-matrix with the target-density approximation (TDA) yields much better agreement with experimental data for ⁴He elastic scattering from ⁵⁸Ni and ²⁰⁸Pb than the usual frozen-density approximation (FDA), but only when the projectile is hardly excited and the target is easily excited, as is the case for ⁴He scattering from heavier nuclei.

## Method

The authors constructed a double-folding (DF) optical potential for ⁴He-nucleus scattering using the Melbourne *g*-matrix effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. Instead of the usual frozen-density approximation (FDA), which uses the sum of projectile and target densities as the local density in the *g*-matrix, they proposed the target-density approximation (TDA), where only the target density is taken as the local density. They then compared the DF-TDA model, the DF-FDA model, and the nucleon-nucleus folding (NAF) model against experimental differential cross sections and total reaction cross sections for ⁴He elastic scattering from ⁵⁸Ni and ²⁰⁸Pb targets.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴He+⁵⁸Ni scattering at incident energies from 20 to 175 MeV/nucleon, the DF-TDA model reproduces experimental differential cross sections, while the DF-FDA model overestimates data at lower energies (20–60 MeV/nucleon) and underestimates data at higher energies (around 175 MeV/nucleon).
- For ⁴He+⁵⁸Ni scattering, the DF-TDA model yields better agreement with total reaction cross section data from Ingemarsson (2000) and Bonin (1985) than the DF-FDA model across the energy range 20–175 MeV/nucleon.
- For ⁴He+²⁰⁸Pb scattering at incident energies from 26 to 175 MeV/nucleon, the DF-TDA model yields better agreement with experimental data from Hauser (1969), Goldberg (1973), Uchida (2004), and Bonin (1985) than the DF-FDA model.
- The NAF model well simulates the DF-TDA model for ⁴He+⁵⁸Ni scattering at lower energies around 30 MeV/nucleon and higher energies around 175 MeV/nucleon, but deviates sizably at intermediate energies around 85 MeV/nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the Melbourne *g*-matrix folding model, previously successful for nucleon-nucleus scattering with no adjustable parameter (Amos, 2003) and for ¹²C+¹²C and ²⁰⁻³²Ne+¹²C scattering (Minomo, 2011; Minomo, 2012; Sumi, 2012), to ⁴He scattering from heavier targets. It contradicts the earlier JLM *g*-matrix folding model (Jeukenne, Lejeune, and Mahaux, 1977) which required normalization factors of about 25% and 35% for the real and imaginary parts, respectively, to reproduce ⁴He scattering data. The DF-TDA model enables parameter-free microscopic optical potentials for ⁴He scattering and validates the practical NAF model, which can use phenomenological nucleon-nucleus optical potentials instead of microscopic ones.
