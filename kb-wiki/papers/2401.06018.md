# Implications of comprehensive nuclear and astrophysics data on the equations of state of neutron star matter

> The equations of state (EoSs) governing neutron star (NS) matter obtained for both non-relativistic and relativistic mean-field models are systematically confronted with a diverse set of terrestrial data and astrophysical observations within the Bayesian framework. The terrestrial data, spans from bulk properties of finite nuclei to the heavy-ion collisions, constrain the symmetric nuclear matter EoS and the symmetry energy up to twice the saturation density ($ρ_0$= 0.16 fm$^{-3}$). The astrophysical observations encompass the NS radius, the tidal deformability, and the lower bound on maximum mass. Three distinct posterior distributions of EoSs are generated by gradually updating the priors with different constraints: (i) only the maximum NS mass, (ii) incorporating additional terrestrial data, (iii) combining both the terrestrial data and astrophysical observations. These EoS distributions are then compared using the Kullback-Liebler divergence which highlights the significant constraints imposed on the EoSs by the currently available lower bound of NS maximum mass and terrestrial data. The remaining astrophysical observations marginally refine the EoS within the density range $\sim$ 2-3$ρ_0$. It is observed that the relativistic mean field model yields stiffer EoS around the saturation density, but predict smaller values of the speed of sound and proton fraction in the interior of massive stars.

## Key claim

The paper systematically confronts both non-relativistic (Skyrme) and relativistic mean-field (RMF) equations of state with terrestrial nuclear data and astrophysical observations using Bayesian inference, finding that the currently available lower bound on neutron star maximum mass and terrestrial data provide the dominant constraints, while additional astrophysical observations (radii and tidal deformability) only marginally refine the EoS within the density range of approximately 2-3 times saturation density.

## Method

The authors generated three distinct posterior distributions of EoSs by gradually updating priors with different constraints: (i) only the maximum neutron star mass from PSR J0740+6620, (ii) incorporating 12 terrestrial experimental data points from finite nuclei properties and heavy-ion collisions, and (iii) combining both terrestrial data and astrophysical observations including NICER mass-radius measurements and GW170817 tidal deformability. The EoSs were computed using Skyrme-based non-relativistic mean-field models and non-linear relativistic mean-field models, with the Kullback-Leibler divergence used to compare the resulting posterior distributions.

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian inference approach of Huth (2021) and Tsang (2024) by applying the same diverse dataset to both non-relativistic and relativistic mean-field models, rather than using Taylor-expanded EoSs or chiral effective field theory calculations. The analysis demonstrates that the lower bound on maximum neutron star mass (from pulsars PSR J0740+6620 and PSR J0348+0432) combined with terrestrial nuclear data provides the strongest constraints on the EoS, while NICER radius measurements and GW170817 tidal deformability only marginally refine the EoS at densities around 2-3 rho_0. The results enable future work to focus on which specific nuclear observables most effectively reduce uncertainties in the high-density EoS.
