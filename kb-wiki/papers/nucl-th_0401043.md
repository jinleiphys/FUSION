# Quantum-tail effect in low energy d+d reaction in deuterated metals

# Quantum-tail effect in low energy d+d reaction in deuterated metals

## Key claim

The anomalously large enhancement of the d(d,p)t fusion cross section observed in deuterated metal targets at beam energies below a few keV can be explained by quantum broadening of the momentum-energy dispersion relation, which modifies the high-momentum tail of the target deuteron distribution from an exponential (Maxwellian) to a power-law form, without requiring unphysically large electron screening potentials.

## Method

The authors compute the fusion reaction rate by averaging the cross section over a momentum distribution function for target deuterons that incorporates a Lorentzian broadening of the energy-momentum relation, as proposed by Galitskij and Yakimets (1967). The width of this Lorentzian is set by the collisional frequency, estimated using the bare Coulomb cross section and the density of deuterated metal. The resulting momentum distribution develops a power-law tail at energies much larger than thermal, and the authors evaluate the enhancement factor relative to the zero-temperature cross section using an approximate parameterization with an upper cutoff energy Emax.

## Key numbers

- The theoretical adiabatic electron screening potential for d+d reactions is U_ad = 28 eV, while the experimentally inferred screening energy needed to fit the data is U_ex = 309 ± 12 eV (Raiola 2002).
- The bare cross section parameters are S_0 = 43 keV·b and S_1 = 0.53 b, extrapolated from center-of-mass energies above 20 keV.
- The thermal energy of the target at 10 °C is kT = 2.44 × 10^(-2) eV; the Maxwellian thermal correction factor f_T is approximately 1 even at the lowest beam energy of about 1 keV because π^2 E_G = 986 keV for d+d reactions.
- The quantum enhancement factor f_Q shows an evident increase starting from beam energies of 1–2 keV, in qualitative agreement with the experimental enhancement.

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing puzzle of anomalously large electron screening potentials inferred from sub-barrier fusion experiments in deuterated metals (Raiola 2002, Raiola 2002a), where the required U_e values exceed the adiabatic upper limit U_ad by an order of magnitude. The authors apply the quantum uncertainty broadening of the energy-momentum relation originally proposed by Galitskij and Yakimets (1967) and recently developed by Starostin et al. (2000, 2002). This mechanism provides an alternative to the electron screening explanation and could have implications for nuclear fusion in astrophysical environments (Coraddu 1998, Coraddu 2000). The authors note that a more quantitative comparison requires further analytical or numerical work and inclusion of screening effects on both the fusion and ion-collision cross sections.
