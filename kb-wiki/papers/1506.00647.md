# Perturbative Renormalization of Neutron-Antineutron Operators

> Two-loop anomalous dimensions and one-loop renormalization scheme matching factors are calculated for six-quark operators responsible for neutron-antineutron transitions. When combined with lattice QCD determinations of the matrix elements of these operators, our results can be used to reliably predict the neutron-antineutron vacuum transition time, $τ_{n\bar{n}}$, in terms of basic parameters of baryon-number violating beyond-the-Standard-Model theories. The operators are classified by their chiral transformation properties, and a basis in which there is no operator mixing due to strong interactions is identified. Operator projectors that are required for non-perturbative renormalization of the corresponding lattice QCD six-quark operator matrix elements are constructed. A complete calculation of $δm = 1/τ_{n\bar{n}}$ in a particular beyond-the-Standard-Model theory is presented as an example to demonstrate how operator renormalization and results from lattice QCD are combined with experimental bounds on $δm$ to constrain the scale of new baryon-number violating physics. At the present computationally accessible lattice QCD matching scale of $\sim$ 2 GeV, the next-to-next-to-leading-order effects calculated in this work correct the leading-order plus next-to-leading-order $δm$ predictions of beyond-the-Standard-Model theories by $< 26\%$. Next-to-next-to-next-to-leading-order effects provide additional unknown corrections to predictions of $δm$ that are estimated to be $< 7\%$.

## Key claim

Two-loop anomalous dimensions and one-loop renormalization scheme matching factors are calculated for six-quark operators responsible for neutron-antineutron transitions, providing the first NNLO corrections to the renormalization group evolution that relate lattice QCD matrix elements at ~2 GeV to high-scale BSM theories; these NNLO effects correct leading-order plus next-to-leading-order predictions of the neutron-antineutron transition rate by less than 26%, with estimated N3LO corrections below 7%.

## Method

The authors constructed a chiral operator basis for six-quark operators using SU(2)L × SU(2)R chiral symmetry, identifying operators that do not mix under perturbative QCD interactions. They calculated two-loop anomalous dimensions in the MSbar scheme and one-loop matching factors between the RI-MOM scheme (used for non-perturbative lattice QCD renormalization) and the MSbar scheme, using dimensional regularization with naive dimensional regularization (NDR) for γ5 and a specified evanescent operator basis. Operator projectors required for non-perturbative renormalization of lattice QCD matrix elements were also constructed.

## Key numbers

* NNLO corrections to leading-order plus next-to-leading-order δm predictions are less than 26% at the lattice QCD matching scale of ~2 GeV
* Estimated N3LO corrections to δm predictions are less than 7%
* One-loop MSbar anomalous dimensions γI(0): Q1 = 4, Q2 = -4, Q3 = 0, Q4 = 24, Q5 = 12, Q̃1 = 4, Q̃3 = 0
* Two-loop MSbar anomalous dimensions γI(1): Q1 = 335/3 - 34Nf/9, Q2 = 91/3 - 26Nf/9, Q3 = 64 - 10Nf/3, Q4 = 229 - 46Nf/3, Q5 = 238 - 14Nf, Q̃1 = 797/3 - 118Nf/9, Q̃3 = 218 - 38Nf/3
* One-loop Landau gauge RI-MOM matching factors rI(0): Q1 = 101/30 + 8/15 ln 2, Q2 = -31/6 + 88/15 ln 2, Q3 = -9/10 + 16/5 ln 2, Q4 = 177/10 - 64/5 ln 2, Q5 = 49/10 - 24/5 ln 2, Q̃1 = -109/30 + 8/15 ln 2, Q̃3 = -79/10 + 16/5 ln 2
* In a simplified BSM model from Arnold (2012), the relative size of NNLO to NLO corrections to δm is 14%, with estimated N3LO corrections of 2%

## Context

This work extends the one-loop running calculations for n-nbar operators performed by Caswell (1982) to NNLO, providing the first two-loop anomalous dimensions and one-loop matching factors needed to reliably combine lattice QCD matrix elements with perturbative BSM matching calculations. The results enable testable predictions for the neutron-antineutron vacuum transition time τnnbar from lattice QCD calculations underway by Buchoff (2012), allowing experimental bounds from the ILL experiment (τnnbar > 2.7 years) and Super-K (τO16 > 1.89 × 10^32 years) to constrain the scale of baryon-number violating physics. Similar two-loop matching and three-loop running calculations have been performed for proton decay operators by Gracey (2012) and for four-quark weak matrix elements by Buras and collaborators.
