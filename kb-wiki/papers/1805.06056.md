# Large-$N_c$ Relationships Among Two-Derivative Pionless Effective Field Theory Couplings

> We analyze two-derivative two-nucleon interactions in a combined pionless effective field theory and large-$N_c$ expansion. At leading order in the large-$N_c$ expansion, relationships among low-energy constants emerge. We find these to be consistent with experiment. However, it is critical to correctly address the subtraction-point dependence of the low-energy constants. These results provide additional confidence that the dual-expansion procedure is useful for analyzing low-energy few-body observables.

## Key claim

The large-\(N_c\) expansion predicts that the two-derivative low-energy constants in pionless effective field theory satisfy specific relationships at leading order in \(N_c\), but these relationships are only consistent with experiment when the renormalization subtraction point \(\mu\) is chosen appropriately, otherwise the fine-tuning of the \(S\)-wave scattering lengths can obscure the large-\(N_c\) symmetry.

## Method

The authors combined the pionless effective field theory (EFT\(_{\pi\!/}\)) expansion with the large-\(N_c\) expansion of QCD, analyzing two-nucleon contact operators with up to two derivatives. They constructed an overcomplete set of 14 operators in a "large-\(N_c\)-counting basis" where the \(N_c\) scaling is manifest, then used Fierz transformations to map these onto the seven independent partial-wave operators (\(^1S_0\), \(^3S_1\), \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) mixing, \(^1P_1\), and \(^3P_{0,1,2}\)). The large-\(N_c\) scaling of each partial-wave coupling was extracted as a linear combination of leading-order (LO-in-\(N_c\)) and next-to-next-to-leading-order (N\(^2\)LO-in-\(N_c\)) coefficients from the counting basis.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of the two-derivative \(S\)-wave couplings at LO in \(N_c\) is predicted to be \(\Ctrip/\Csing = 1\); with experimental scattering lengths \(a^{(^1S_0)} = -23.7148 \pm 0.0043\ \mathrm{fm}\) and \(a^{(^3S_1)} = 5.424 \pm 0.004\ \mathrm{fm}\), and effective ranges \(r^{(^1S_0)} = 2.750 \pm 0.018\ \mathrm{fm}\) and \(r^{(^3S_1)} = 1.759 \pm 0.005\ \mathrm{fm}\), the ratio in the PDS scheme is \(\Ctrip/\Csing = (r^{(^3S_1)}/r^{(^1S_0)}) \cdot (\mu - 1/a^{(^1S_0)})^2/(\mu - 1/a^{(^3S_1)})^2\).
- The ratio \(\Czerotrip/\Czerosing}\) for the leading (no-derivative) \(S\)-wave couplings reaches 0.9 (within 10% of the LO-in-\(N_c\) prediction of 1) at \(\mu \approx 440\ \mathrm{MeV}\), and reaches 0.7 (within the 30% expected from \(1/N_c\) corrections) at \(\mu = 140\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The ratio \(C_T/C_S\) for the leading \(S\)-wave operators is approximately 0.08 at \(\mu = 140\ \mathrm{MeV}\), consistent with the expected \(1/N_c^2\) suppression.
- The \(^3P_J\) phase shifts modified by only 15% can yield changes in the nucleon-deuteron analyzing power \(A_y\) by 50%.

## Context

This work extends the combined EFT\(_{\pi\!/}\) and large-\(N_c\) analysis of Schindler, Springer, and collaborators (2015) from parity-violating interactions to parity-conserving two-derivative two-nucleon operators. It builds on the large-\(N_c\) analysis of the two-nucleon potential by Kaplan and Manohar (1996) and the demonstration by Kaplan and Savage (1995) that the leading \(S\)-wave couplings are identical in the large-\(N_c\) limit. The results provide a framework for predicting the relative sizes of \(P\)-wave and \(SD\)-mixing couplings in EFT\(_{\pi\!/}\), which is relevant for resolving the longstanding \(A_y\) puzzle in low-energy nucleon-deuteron scattering (reviewed by Glöckle et al., 1995).
