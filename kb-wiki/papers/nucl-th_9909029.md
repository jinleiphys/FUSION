# Light-Front Bethe-Salpeter Equation

> A three-dimensional reduction of the two-particle Bethe-Salpeter equation is proposed. The proposed reduction is in the framework of light-front dynamics. It yields auxiliary quantities for the transition matrix and the bound state. The arising effective interaction can be perturbatively expanded according to the number of particles exchanged at a given light-front time. An example suggests that the convergence of the expansion is rapid. This result is particular for light-front dynamics. The covariant results of the Bethe-Salpeter equation can be recovered from the corresponding auxiliary three-dimensional ones. The technical procedure is developed for a two-boson case; the idea for an extension to fermions is given. The technical procedure appears quite practicable, possibly allowing one to go beyond the ladder approximation for the solution of the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The relation between the three-dimensional light-front reduction of the field-theoretic Bethe-Salpeter equation and a corresponding quantum-mechanical description is discussed.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation using light-front dynamics, claiming that the resulting perturbative expansion of the effective interaction converges rapidly. The authors hedge that this rapid convergence is particular to light-front dynamics and that the covariant results of the full Bethe-Salpeter equation can be recovered from the auxiliary three-dimensional quantities.

## Method
The authors construct an auxiliary four-dimensional resolvent \(\widetilde{G}_0(K)\) that satisfies specific projection conditions onto the light-front hyperplane, then derive a three-dimensional integral equation for an auxiliary transition matrix \(t(K)\) and bound-state vertex \(|\gamma_B\rangle\). The effective interaction \(w(K)\) in the three-dimensional equation is obtained by expanding the full interaction \(W(K)\) in powers of the original Bethe-Salpeter interaction \(V(K)\), with the expansion truncated at a given order of exchanged particles. The method is tested on a two-boson bound-state system with scalar exchange, comparing approximations up to second and fourth order in the coupling constant against exact four-dimensional Euclidean-space solutions.

## Key numbers
- The test case uses boson masses \(m_1=m_2=m\) and exchanged boson mass \(\mu = 0.5 m\).
- For strong binding (\(M_B = 0\)), the approximation based on \(w^{(2)}(K_B)\) already reproduces the exact bound-state projection accurately, with improvement from including \(w^{(4)}(K_B)\).
- For weak binding (\(M_B = 1.98 m\), i.e., 2% of the particle masses), the \(w^{(2)}(K_B)\) approximation is already quite accurate.
- The exact relation between coupling constant \(g_S\) and bound-state mass \(M_B\) is satisfactorily reproduced by the \(w^{(2)}(K_B)\) approximation, with further improvement from \(w^{(2)}(K_B)+w^{(4)}(K_B)\).

## Context
This work extends the three-dimensional reduction techniques for the Bethe-Salpeter equation, previously explored in instant-form dynamics by Woloshyn and Jackson (1973), to the light-front framework. The authors note that the analogous approximation scheme in instant-form dynamics has much poorer convergence properties with respect to the number of exchanged bosons, as shown by Nico and collaborators. The method enables practical calculations beyond the ladder approximation for the Bethe-Salpeter equation, with potential applications to fermion systems as sketched in the paper, and provides a connection to light-front quantum-mechanical descriptions of two-particle systems.
