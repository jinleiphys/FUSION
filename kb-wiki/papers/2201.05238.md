# Theoretical description of fission yields: towards a fast and efficient global model

> Background: A quantitative microscopic understanding of the fission-fragment yield distributions represents a major challenge for nuclear theory as it involves the intricate competition between large-amplitude nuclear collective motion and single-particle nucleonic motion. Purpose: A recently proposed approach to global modeling of fission fragment distributions is extended to account for odd-even staggering in charge yields and for neutron evaporation. Method: Fission trajectories are obtained within the density functional theory framework, allowing for a microscopic determination of the most probable fission prefragment configurations. Mass and charge yields distributions are constructed by means of a statistical approach rooted in a microcanonical ensemble. Result: We show that the proposed hybrid model can reproduce experimental mass and charge fragment yields, including the odd-even staggering, for a wide range of fissioning nuclei. Experimental isotopic yields can be described within a simple neutron evaporation scheme. We also explore fission fragment distributions of exotic neutron-rich and superheavy systems, and compare our predictions with other state-of-the art global calculations. Conclusion: Our study suggests that the microscopic rearrangement of nucleons into fission fragments occurs well before the scission, and that the subsequent dynamics is mainly driven by the thermal excitations and bulk features of the nuclear binding. The proposed simple hybrid approach is well suited for large-scale calculations involving hundreds of fissioning nuclei.

## Key claim

The hybrid model combining microscopic density functional theory (DFT) input with a statistical microcanonical treatment can reproduce experimental mass and charge fission-fragment yields, including odd-even staggering, for a wide range of fissioning nuclei, but the agreement depends on using liquid-drop-model binding energies rather than ground-state mass tables, and the odd-even staggering magnitude is sensitive to the chosen pairing strength parameter.

## Method

Fission trajectories are computed on a two-dimensional potential energy surface (quadrupole and octupole moments) using the Skyrme SkM* functional with mixed-type pairing, and the pre-scission configuration is identified via the minimum action path (spontaneous fission) or minimum-potential path (induced fission). Prefragment proton and neutron numbers are determined from the nucleon localization function at that configuration, and the remaining neck nucleons (about 10% of the total mass) are distributed statistically using a microcanonical probability formula that incorporates pairing corrections via a smooth pairing term. Neutron evaporation from excited primary fragments is treated by allowing sequential neutron emission until the excitation energy falls below the neutron separation energy.

## Key numbers

- The pairing term in the liquid drop model uses parameters alpha = 0.31 and c = 4.66 (neutrons) or 4.31 (protons) to reproduce odd-even staggering in charge yields.
- The residual thermal energy upper limit is set to E_r^max = 40 MeV based on typical maximum excitation energy of fission fragments in low-energy fission.
- Prefragment quadrupole deformations are found to be beta_2^{H,L} less than or approximately 0.1 for all systems studied, and yield distributions are insensitive to such small deformations.
- For the superheavy system 264Fm, the prefragments strongly favor symmetric fission into two doubly-magic 132Sn, with only 2 protons and 8 neutrons available for redistribution in the neck.
- The odd-even difference delta_Y in charge yields decreases exponentially with the Coulomb parameter Z^2/A^{1/3} of the fissioning system, with the default alpha=0.31 giving a broad band that overlaps experimental data.
- For 240Pu thermal neutron fission, a spread in E_r^max of 8 MeV (values 28, 32, and 36 MeV) is used to account for dissipative energy fluctuations, which reduces odd-even staggering in secondary isotopic yields.

## Context

This work extends the hybrid DFT-statistical approach of Sadhukhan (2020) by incorporating odd-even staggering in charge yields and neutron evaporation, enabling global calculations for hundreds of fissioning nuclei. It contrasts with scission-point models (SPM) like SPY2 (Lemaître 2021) that reproduce odd-even staggering but lack dynamical correlations, and with Brownian shape-motion (BSM) models (Mumpower 2020) that include dynamics but overestimate widths for heavy systems and lack odd-even staggering in charge yields. The results suggest that microscopic nucleon rearrangement occurs well before scission, enabling large-scale predictions for exotic neutron-rich and superheavy systems relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis.
