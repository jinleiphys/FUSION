# Extracting the jet transport coefficient of cold nuclear matter from world data

> We present the first global extraction of the jet transport coefficient ($\hat q$) for cold nuclear matter within the framework of higher-twist expansion. The analysis takes into account the world data on transverse momentum broadening in semi-inclusive $e$-A deep inelastic scattering (DIS) and in Drell-Yan dilepton and heavy quarkonium production in $p$-A collisions, as well as the nuclear modification of the structure functions in DIS. The results of this work provide the first quantitative evidence for the universality and non-trivial scale dependence of the medium transport property, similar to that for nucleon structure as encoded in the standard parton distribution functions. We expect the extracted scale dependence of $\hat q$ for cold nuclear matter can be extended to precisely identify the fundamental property of quark gluon plasma.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first global extraction of the jet transport coefficient (\(\hat q\)) for cold nuclear matter, providing quantitative evidence that \(\hat q\) is a probing-scale dependent quantity rather than a constant, as had been commonly assumed in heavy ion studies. The authors hedge that this is a leading-order (LO) result that may be improved when next-to-leading order (NLO) theoretical frameworks become available.

## Method
The authors performed a global analysis within the higher-twist expansion framework of QCD factorization, using a parametrization \(\hat q(x_B,Q^2) = \hat q_0 \,\alpha_s(Q^2) \,x_B^{\alpha}(1-x_B)^{\beta} \left[\ln(Q^2/Q_0^2)\right]^{\gamma}\) to describe the kinematic dependence. They fitted this parametrization to world data on transverse momentum broadening from semi-inclusive \(e\)-A deep inelastic scattering (HERMES), Drell-Yan dilepton production (FNAL-E772, SPS-NA10), heavy quarkonium production (FNAL-E772, FNAL-E866, RHIC, LHC), and nuclear modification of structure functions in DIS (FNAL-E665), totaling 215 data points.

## Key numbers
- Total \(\chi^2\) reduced from 387.9 (constant \(\hat q\) fit) to 260.2 (scale-dependent \(\hat q(x_B,Q^2)\) fit) across 215 data points.
- Extracted parameters for \(\hat q(x_B,Q^2)\): \(\hat{q}_0 = 0.0195^{+0.0020}_{-0.0020}\) GeV\(^2\)/fm, \(\alpha = -0.174^{+0.031}_{-0.025}\), \(\beta = -2.79^{+0.91}_{-0.80}\), \(\gamma = 0.254^{+0.132}_{-0.143}\), all at 90% confidence level.
- For HERMES SIDIS data (156 points), \(\chi^2\) improved from 218.5 (constant \(\hat q\)) to 189.7 (scale-dependent \(\hat q\)).
- For LHC \(J/\psi\) production data (12 points), \(\chi^2\) improved dramatically from 87.3 (constant \(\hat q\)) to 4.8 (scale-dependent \(\hat q\)).

## Context
This work extends the theoretical developments on renormalization group equations for \(\hat q\) by Kang (2013, 2014, 2016) and others (Blaizot 2014, Iancu 2014, Liou 2013), and the NLO calculations of transverse momentum broadening in cold nuclear matter. It provides the first quantitative evidence for the universality and scale dependence of \(\hat q\) in cold nuclear matter, analogous to the scale dependence of parton distribution functions. The results enable future precise identification of the jet transport property of quark-gluon plasma and motivate further measurements at facilities like the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) to extend the kinematic coverage.
