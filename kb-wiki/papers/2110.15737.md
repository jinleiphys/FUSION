# Multi-reference many-body perturbation theory for nuclei I -- Novel PGCM-PT formalism

> Perturbative and non-perturbative expansion methods already constitute a tool of choice to perform ab initio calculations over a significant part of the nuclear chart. In this context, the categories of accessible nuclei directly reflect the class of unperturbed state employed in the formulation of the expansion. The present work generalizes to the nuclear many-body context the versatile method of Ref. \cite{burton20a} by formulating a perturbative expansion on top of a multi-reference unperturbed state mixing deformed non-orthogonal Bogoliubov vacua, i.e. a state obtained from the projected generator coordinate method (PGCM). Particular attention is paid to the part of the mixing taking care of the symmetry restoration, showing that it can be exactly contracted throughout the expansion, thus reducing significantly the dimensionality of the linear problem to be solved to extract perturbative corrections. While the novel expansion method, coined as PGCM-PT, reduces to the PGCM at lowest order, it reduces to single-reference perturbation theories in appropriate limits. Based on a PGCM unperturbed state capturing (strong) static correlations in a versatile and efficient fashion, PGCM-PT is indistinctly applicable to doubly closed-shell, singly open-shell and doubly open-shell nuclei. The remaining (weak) dynamical correlations are brought consistently through perturbative corrections. This symmetry-conserving multi-reference perturbation theory is state-specific and applies to both ground and excited PGCM unperturbed states, thus correcting each state belonging to the low-lying spectrum of the system under study.

## Key claim

The paper formulates a novel multi-reference perturbation theory (PGCM-PT) that uses a projected generator coordinate method (PGCM) state as the unperturbed reference, such that strong static correlations are captured at zeroth order and weak dynamical correlations are added via perturbative corrections; the method is state-specific and applies to both ground and excited states of doubly closed-shell, singly open-shell, and doubly open-shell nuclei, with the symmetry-restoring part of the mixing exactly contracted throughout the expansion.

## Method

The authors generalize the non-orthogonal configuration interaction perturbation theory of Burton (2020) to the nuclear many-body context by constructing the unperturbed state as a linear combination of deformed, non-orthogonal Bogoliubov vacua obtained from the projected generator coordinate method (PGCM). The PGCM state is built by solving Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equations for the mixing coefficients over a collective deformation coordinate, with symmetry projection performed via rotation operators. Rayleigh-Schrodinger perturbation theory is then formulated on top of this multi-reference unperturbed state, with the symmetry-restoration part of the mixing contracted exactly to reduce the dimensionality of the linear system needed for perturbative corrections.

## Key numbers

* The PGCM unperturbed state mixes from several tens to a few hundred non-orthogonal Bogoliubov states, providing a low-dimensional configuration mixing that efficiently captures static correlations as the mass A of the system grows.
* The method reduces to the PGCM at lowest order (zeroth order) and reduces to single-reference perturbation theories in appropriate limits.
* Second-order calculations, i.e. PGCM-PT(2), are performed in both closed- and open-shell nuclei and are the subject of the third paper in the series.

## Context

This work generalizes the multi-reference perturbation theory of Burton (2020) from quantum chemistry to nuclear physics, extending prior single-reference expansion methods (such as those of Soma 2011, 2013; Tichai 2017, 2018) that break symmetries to capture static correlations. The PGCM-PT formalism follows a "project-partition-and-expand" philosophy that maintains exact symmetries throughout the expansion, in contrast to the "partition-and-project-expand" approach of projected coupled cluster methods (Qiu 2017, 2018; Sun 2021). The method enables systematic inclusion of dynamical correlations on top of PGCM states that already capture static correlations from symmetry breaking, restoration, and large-amplitude collective fluctuations, making it applicable across the nuclear chart.
