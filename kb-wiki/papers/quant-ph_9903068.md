# Reply on `comment on our paper `Single two-level ion in an anharmonic-oscillator trap: Time evolution of the Q function and population inversion ''

> We show here that the model Hamiltonian used in our paper for ion vibrating in a q-analog harmonic oscillator trap and interacting with a classical single-mode light field is indeed obtained by replacing the usual bosonic creation and annihilation operators of the harmonic trap model by their q-deformed counterparts. The approximations made in our paper amount to using for the ion-laser interaction in a q-analog harmonic oscillator trap, the operator $F_{q}=exp{-(|ε|^2}/2)}exp{iεA^{\dagger}}exp{iεA}$, which is analogous to the corresponding operator for ion in a harmonic oscillator trap that is $F=exp{-(|ε|^2 /2)}exp{iεa^{\dagger }}exp{iεa}$. In our article we do not claim to have diagonalized the operator, $F_q = exp{i ε(A^{\dagger}+A)}$, for which the basis states |g,m> and |e,m> are not analytic vectors.

## Key claim

The authors defend their original paper (Sharma, Sharma, and Zamick, 1997) by clarifying that their model Hamiltonian for an ion in a q-deformed harmonic oscillator trap is obtained by replacing standard bosonic operators with q-deformed counterparts, and that their numerical calculations are valid because they used the operator \(F_q = e^{-|\epsilon|^2/2} e^{i\epsilon A^\dagger} e^{i\epsilon A}\) rather than the non-diagonalizable operator \(e^{i\epsilon(A^\dagger + A)}\), with the approximation effectively treating the commutator \([A^\dagger, A]\) as \(-1\).

## Method

The authors compare the standard harmonic oscillator trap Hamiltonian (Eq. 1) with the q-deformed analog (Eq. 11a), where creation and annihilation operators \(A, A^\dagger\) satisfy the q-commutation relation \(AA^\dagger - q A^\dagger A = q^{-N}\). They show that the ion-laser interaction operator \(F_q\) is defined as \(e^{-|\epsilon|^2/2} e^{i\epsilon A^\dagger} e^{i\epsilon A}\) (Eq. 11), which can be expanded as a double sum over \(A^{\dagger n} A^k\) terms with coefficients identical to the standard case. The matrix elements of \(F_q\) are given explicitly (Eq. 14), and the effective Lamb-Dicke parameter becomes \(\epsilon f(N)\), where \(f(N) = \sqrt{[N]_q/N}\).

## Key numbers

- For the initial coherent-like state with \(\alpha = 4\), \(\epsilon = 0.05\), and \(\tau = 0.003\), the calculated value of \(_q\langle \alpha | f^2(N) | \alpha \rangle_q\) is 1.0004.
- The effective q-deformed Lamb-Dicke parameter for this case is \(\epsilon_q = 0.05001\).
- Parameters used for numerical calculations: \(\epsilon = 0.05\), \(\overline{\omega} = \omega/\Omega = 50\), and \(\overline{\Delta} = \Delta/\Omega = -50\).

## Context

This reply addresses a comment on the authors' earlier work (Sharma, Sharma, and Zamick, 1997) which extended the standard ion-trap laser interaction model of Blockley, Walls, and Risken (1992) to q-deformed harmonic oscillator traps. The paper clarifies that the Baker-Hausdorff theorem cannot be applied exactly for q-deformed operators, and that the approximation used amounts to setting \([A^\dagger, A] = -1\) at a certain level. This work enables further study of q-deformed quantum optics systems where the energy level spacing depends on the quantum number \(n\), leading to \(n\)-dependent entanglement between center-of-mass motion and internal atomic degrees of freedom.
