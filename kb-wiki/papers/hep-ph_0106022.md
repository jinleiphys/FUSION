# Constraining Quark-Hadron Duality at Large-Nc

> Quark-meson duality for two-point functions of vector and axial-vector QCD currents is investigated in the large-Nc approximation. We find that the joint constraints of duality and chiral symmetry imply degeneracy of excited vector and axial-vector mesons in the large-Nc limit. We compare model-independent constraints with expectations based on the Veneziano-Lovelace-Shapiro string model. Several models of duality are constructed, and phenomenological implications are discussed.

## Key claim

The joint constraints of quark-hadron duality and chiral symmetry force the excited vector and axial-vector mesons to become degenerate in the large-Nc limit, and no solution exists with non-degenerate masses if the squared masses are linear in the excitation number n.

## Method

The author writes the vector and axial-vector two-point functions as infinite sums over zero-width meson states in the large-Nc limit, imposes duality matching to the perturbative QCD logarithm and the operator product expansion, and enforces chiral symmetry through spectral-function sum rules derived from the chiral algebra and from the infinite-momentum-frame commutation relations of axial charges. The constraints are then analyzed for linear mass spectra, and explicit ansatze are constructed and tested against the Veneziano-Lovelace-Shapiro string model and its generalizations.

## Key numbers

* The first spectral-function sum rule in the large-Nc limit: sum over n of F_V^2(n) minus sum over n of F_A^2(n) equals F_pi^2 (Equation sfsr;a).
* The second spectral-function sum rule in the large-Nc limit: sum over n of F_V^2(n) M_V^2(n) minus sum over n of F_A^2(n) M_A^2(n) equals 0 (Equation sfsr;b).
* In the LSV string model, the Regge intercept is fixed to alpha_0 = 1/2 by chiral symmetry, and the squared masses are M_V^2(n) = M_rho^2 (1 + 2n) for n = 0,1,... (Equation venecinque2).
* The LSV coupling chi_LSV(n) = (F_pi^2 / pi) Gamma(1/2 + n) / [Gamma(1/2) Gamma(1+n) (n+1/2)] satisfies the sum rule sum_{n=0}^infty chi_LSV(n) = F_pi^2 (Equation veneseis).
* For the generalized coupling chi(n, N_M, alpha_0), the asymptotic behavior is n^{-(N_M+1)}; for N_M > 1 the sums converge and satisfy sum_{n=0}^infty chi = F_pi^2 and sum_{n=0}^infty n chi = -alpha_0 F_pi^2 (Equations theoremstup;a,b).
* In the string-inspired model with degenerate vector and axial-vector mesons, matching to the OPE gives F^2 = (N_c / (24 pi^2)) Lambda^2 (Equation opejunkmod;a).

## Context

This work extends the investigation of quark-hadron duality in large-Nc QCD begun by Golterman and Peris (2001) and builds on the classic spectral-function sum rules of Weinberg (1967). It compares model-independent chiral constraints with the predictions of the Veneziano-Lovelace-Shapiro string model (Veneziano 1968; Lovelace 1968; Shapiro 1969) and constructs explicit models that satisfy both duality and chiral symmetry. The results enable future studies of how chiral symmetry breaking and the OPE can be reconciled in large-Nc QCD, and they highlight the difficulty of constructing realistic models with non-degenerate vector and axial-vector mesons.
