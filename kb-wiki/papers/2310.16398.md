# Muonic hyperfine structure and the Bohr-Weisskopf effect

> An update is given on the experimental values of the magnetic hyperfine structure and the Bohr-Weisskopf effect in muonic atoms. The need for more measurements and systematic calculations is discussed to allow the differentiation of different models of the Bohr-Weisskopf effect in nuclei.

# Muonic hyperfine structure and the Bohr-Weisskopf effect

## Key claim

The paper argues that while existing measurements of the magnetic hyperfine structure in muonic atoms are limited in number and precision, they can still differentiate between nuclear models of magnetization distribution, but only if systematic calculations are performed across all measured isotopes. The author hedges that the experimental errors are large enough that most calculated values fall within error bars, with only a few exceptions where models clearly fail.

## Method

The author compiles and updates experimental A constants for the magnetic hyperfine splitting in muonic 1s₁/₂ states from measurements performed primarily in the 1960s-1970s, taking weighted arithmetic averages to produce recommended values. The experimental Bohr-Weisskopf effect (ε_BW) is extracted by dividing the experimental A constant by the calculated point-dipole A constant (A₀,ₚ.ₔ) and subtracting 1. These experimental ε values are then compared against calculations from several nuclear models: the configuration mixing model (CM), pairing model (PM), pairing plus quadrupole model (PQM), microscopic models (MT I and II), homogeneous magnetization (ball) model, and single-particle models with and without Woods-Saxon potentials.

## Key numbers

- The muon mass is approximately 207 times the electron mass
- Muon lifetime is 2.2 μs
- Transition energies in muonic atoms are in the MeV region, while A constants are in the keV region
- Recommended A constant for ²⁰⁵Tl: 2.309(35) keV
- Recommended A constant for ²⁰⁹Bi: 0.905(27) keV
- Experimental ε_BW for ²⁰⁵Tl: -0.515(15) (using A₀ = 4.760 keV from Johnson and Sorensen)
- Experimental ε_BW for ²⁰⁹Bi: -0.330(30) (using A₀ = 1.351 keV from Johnson and Sorensen)
- Experimental ε_BW for ²⁰³Tl: -0.508(34) (using A₀ = 4.760 keV from Johnson and Sorensen)
- Experimental ε_BW for ¹³³Cs: -0.172(162) (using A₀ = 0.766 keV from Johnson and Sorensen)
- For ¹⁵¹Eu, the experimental ε_BW is -0.608(334), while the CM model predicts -0.397, showing a deviation attributed to nuclear deformation
- For ¹⁹¹Ir (I=3/2), the experimental ε_BW is 2.143(182), while MT I predicts 2.910 and MT II predicts 3.058

## Context

This paper updates the previous reviews by Engfer et al. (1974) and Buttgenbach (1984) on muonic atom hyperfine structure. The work extends the comparison of experimental Bohr-Weisskopf effect values against nuclear models including the configuration mixing model of Johnson and Sorensen (1969), the microscopic model of Fujita and Arima (1975), and recent single-particle calculations by Sanamyan et al. (2023) and Elizarov et al. (2005). The author identifies the muX project at PSI (Wauters et al., 2021) as a promising new initiative that could enable measurements in the lead and trans-lead regions. The paper calls for systematic calculations using multiple nuclear models across all measured isotopes to determine which models are valid and for which nuclei, which would then inform the choice of model for calculations in electronic atoms.
