# Medium induced mixing, spatial modulations and critical modes in QCD

> The mixing between the chiral condensate and the density in hot and dense QCD matter is familiar. We show that the mixing relevant for the ground state is considerably more extensive, and in particular also involves gluonic degrees of freedom. As a result, the Hessian of the QCD effective action is non-Hermitian, but retains a symmetry under combined charge- and complex conjugation. This can lead to complex-conjugate pairs of eigenvalues of this Hessian, signaling regimes with spatially modulated correlations. Furthermore, based on the analytic structure of the quark determinant at a chiral critical point, we demonstrate that the corresponding massless critical mode is composed of the chiral condensate, the density and the Polyakov loops. Due to an avoided crossing, the critical mode turns out to be disconnected from the chiral condensate in vacuum. We present general arguments for all these features and illustrate them through explicit model calculations.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the mixing relevant for the ground state of hot and dense QCD matter is more extensive than previously recognized, involving not only the chiral condensate and the density but also gluonic degrees of freedom (Polyakov loops), leading to a non-Hermitian Hessian that can produce complex-conjugate eigenvalue pairs and spatially modulated correlations; the critical mode at a chiral critical point is composed of the chiral condensate, the density, and the Polyakov loops, but due to an avoided crossing it is disconnected from the chiral condensate in vacuum.

## Method

The authors derive medium-induced mixing in QCD using a saddle-point expansion of the functional quark determinant in the Matsubara formalism, computing the two-point functions (linear couplings) at zero momentum exchange between the chiral condensate (sigma), the temporal isoscalar vector meson (omega_0), and the Polyakov loops (Phi and anti-Phi) via their parametrization in terms of temporal gluon background field eigenvalues a_3 and a_8. They then construct the 4x4 Hessian matrix of the QCD effective action and analyze its symmetry properties under charge conjugation and complex conjugation, illustrating the general arguments with explicit calculations in Polyakov-Quark-Meson models with and without vector repulsion and vacuum fluctuations.

## Key numbers

* The chiral crossover becomes steeper as chemical potential increases, with a predicted critical endpoint (CEP) roughly around T approx 100 MeV and baryon chemical potential mu_B approx 600 MeV (from prior work cited in the introduction).
* The mixing term Gamma_{sigma omega_0} is proportional to the constituent quark mass m_q and involves an integral over the derivative of modified Fermi-Dirac distribution functions with respect to E_p^2, vanishing at mu=0 for all temperatures.
* The mixing terms Gamma_{sigma Phi} and Gamma_{sigma anti-Phi} are proportional to the constituent quark mass m_q and vanish in the chiral limit (m_q -> 0).
* The mixing terms Gamma_{omega_0 Phi} and Gamma_{omega_0 anti-Phi} are pure imaginary at finite density, reflecting the repulsive nature of the vector interaction.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of sigma-omega_0 mixing in nuclear matter (Lim 1989, Kunihiro 1991) and the discovery of a moat regime near the QCD critical point (Fu 2019, Pisarski 2020, Rennecke 2021) by showing that the mixing also involves Polyakov loops, linking confinement and chiral symmetry breaking. It builds on the observation that the C K symmetry of finite-density QCD is a form of PT symmetry (Nishimura 2014, 2015, 2016) and that the Hessian can become non-Hermitian with complex-conjugate eigenvalue pairs signaling spatially modulated phases. The results enable a systematic investigation of how mixing affects the nature of the critical mode at a chiral critical point and the dynamic universality class (Model H of Son 2004), with implications for heavy-ion collision observables and the possible replacement of the CEP by a Lifshitz point or regime.
