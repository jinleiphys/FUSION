# Sound velocity bound and neutron stars

> It has been conjectured that the velocity of sound in any medium is smaller than the velocity of light in vacuum divided by $\sqrt{3}$. Simple arguments support this bound in non-relativistic and/or weakly coupled theories. The bound has been demonstrated in several classes of strongly coupled theories with gravity duals and is saturated only in conformal theories. We point out that the existence of neutron stars with masses around two solar masses combined with the knowledge of the equation of state of hadronic matter at "low" densities is in strong tension with this bound.

## Key claim

The existence of neutron stars with masses around two solar masses is in strong tension with the conjectured bound that the speed of sound in any medium is less than the speed of light divided by √3, assuming the low-density equation of state of hadronic matter is known up to about twice nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The authors constructed a family of equations of state for neutron star matter that satisfy the known low-density constraints (up to 2n₀, where n₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³) and then transition to the stiffest possible behavior allowed by the v_s² < 1/3 bound at higher densities. They used a Skyrme-like parametrization fitted to microscopic neutron matter calculations from chiral effective field theory and quantum Monte Carlo methods, with empirical constraints on nuclear saturation properties (binding energy, incompressibility, symmetry energy, and its slope). For each equation of state, they solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations to determine the maximum possible neutron star mass.

## Key numbers

- The maximum neutron star mass achievable under the v_s² < 1/3 bound is approximately 2 M⊙ (solar masses), with an abrupt disappearance of viable models above this value.
- The transition density between the known low-density regime and the conjectured bound is set at 2n₀, where n₀ = 0.16 ± 0.01 fm⁻³.
- The nuclear incompressibility is K = 235 ± 25 MeV, the symmetry energy is S = 32 ± 2 MeV, and the slope parameter is L = 50 ± 15 MeV.
- The proton fraction in β-equilibrated matter remains below 6% at all densities considered.
- Observation of a 1.0 M⊙ neutron star with radius smaller than 13 km, or any neutron star with radius less than 11.8 km, would imply violation of the sound speed bound.

## Context

This work extends earlier arguments by Rhoades and Ruffini (1974) and Koranda, Stergioulas, and Friedman (1997) that used causality (v_s < 1) to bound neutron star masses, now applying the stronger conjectured bound v_s² < 1/3. The results contradict the sound speed bound conjecture supported by holographic calculations in models such as the Sakai-Sugimoto model (Benincasa, 2006) and the D3/D7 system (Mateos, 2007). The paper enables two possible paths forward: either the sound speed bound is violated, implying a specific shape for the equation of state with a bump in the speed of sound at intermediate densities, or the bound is proven correct, which would tightly constrain the equation of state of dense QCD matter and rule out significant hyperon populations in neutron stars.
