# Mean Field Theoretical Structure of He and Be Isotopes

> The structures of He and Be even-even isotopes are investigated using an axially symmetric Hartree-Fock approach with a Skyrme-IIIls mean field potential. In these simple HF calculations, He and Be isotopes appear to be prolate in their ground states and Be isotopes have oblate shape isomeric states. It is also shown that there exists a level crossing when the nuclear shape changes from the prolate state to the oblate state. The single neutron levels of Be isotopes exhibit a neutron magic number 6 instead of 8 and show that the level inversion between 1/2- and 1/2+ levels occurs only for a largely deformed isotope. Protons are bound stronger in the isotope with more neutrons while neutron levels are somewhat insensitive to the number of neutrons and thus the nuclear size and also the neutron skin become larger as the neutron number increases. In these simple calculations with Skyrme-IIIls interaction no system with a clear indication of neutron halo was found among He and Be isotopes. Instead of it we have found 8He+2n, 2n+8He+2n, and 16Be+2n like chain structures with clusters of two correlated neutrons. It is also shown that 8He and 14Be in their ground states are below the neutron drip line in which all nucleons are bound with negative energy and that 16Be in its ground state is beyond the neutron drip line with two neutrons in positive energy levels.

# Mean Field Theoretical Structure of He and Be Isotopes

## Key claim

Using a simple axially symmetric Hartree-Fock approach with the Skyrme-IIIls interaction, the authors find that He and Be isotopes have prolate ground states and that Be isotopes exhibit oblate shape isomeric states, but they caution that no system with a clear indication of neutron halo was found among these isotopes, instead, chain structures with clusters of two correlated neutrons appear for the most neutron-rich isotopes.

## Method

The authors performed nonrelativistic Hartree-Fock calculations using a Skyrme-IIIls mean field potential in an axially symmetric two-dimensional coordinate space (body-fixed symmetry axis z and radial coordinate r). Single nucleon levels were identified by quantum numbers (Ω, π_z, π_r) and occupied from the lowest level upward to obtain the lowest energy state for each even-even isotope. The self-consistent iteration determined nuclear and charge densities, from which binding energies, root-mean-square radii, and intrinsic quadrupole moments were computed for He isotopes (⁴He through ¹²He) and Be isotopes (⁸Be through ¹⁸Be).

## Context

This work extends earlier mean-field studies of light unstable nuclei, including Hartree-Fock calculations of Be and B isotopes (Zamick and collaborators, early 2000s) and relativistic mean field studies of alpha chain structures in ⁸Be and ¹²C (Lee and collaborators, 2000). The authors compare their results to experimental data from the nuclear data compilation of Audi and Wapstra (2003) and to AMD-HF calculations (Beam and collaborators, 2002). The finding of a neutron magic number 6 instead of 8 in Be isotopes supports earlier predictions from shell model configuration mixing (Talmi and collaborators, 1990) and experimental studies of the ¹⁰Be(t,p)¹²Be reaction (Fortune and collaborators, 1997). The chain structures found for ¹⁰He, ¹²He, and ¹⁸Be suggest possible Borromean or resonant systems, following the suggestion by Jensen and Riisager (2001) that correlations between many neutrons could produce stable exotic structures beyond the neutron drip line. The authors note that quantitative improvement requires more self-consistent center-of-mass energy correction and investigation of the dependence on the chosen Skyrme interaction.
