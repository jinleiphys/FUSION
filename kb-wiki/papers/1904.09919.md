# Intertwined Quantum Phase Transitions in the Zr Isotopes

> We explore the situation of intertwined quantum phase transitions (IQPTs), for which a QPT involving a crossing of two configurations is accompanied by a shape evolution of each configuration with its own separate QPT. We demonstrate the relevance of IQPTs to the Zr isotopes, with such coexisting Type I and Type II QPTs, and ground state shapes changing from spherical to prolate axially deformed and finally to gamma-unstable. Evidence for this scenario is provided by a detailed comparison with experimental data, using a definite symmetry-based conceptual framework.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Zr isotopes provide the first empirical evidence for "intertwined quantum phase transitions" (IQPTs), where a configuration-changing Type II QPT (crossing of normal and intruder configurations) occurs simultaneously with shape-changing Type I QPTs (U(5)-SU(3) and SU(3)-SO(6)) within the intruder configuration, but this claim is hedged by the fact that the analysis is phenomenological and the Hamiltonian parameters are determined from a combined fit to data, not from first principles.

## Method

The authors used the Interacting Boson Model with Configuration Mixing (IBM-CM), where the normal configuration (A) is described by N bosons and the intruder configuration (B) by N+2 bosons, representing a proton excitation across the subshell closure at proton number 40. Hamiltonian parameters for each isotope from 92Zr to 110Zr were determined from a combined fit to experimental energy levels and E2 transitions, allowing gradual changes between adjacent isotopes. The resulting eigenstates are linear combinations of wave functions in the two boson spaces, and the analysis includes both quantum calculations of spectra and observables and semi-classical analysis using energy functionals in the (β,γ) deformation plane.

## Key numbers

- In 104Zr, the intruder configuration (B) shows strong deformation with E(2₁⁺) = 139.3 keV and R₄/₂ = 3.24
- In 106Zr, the ground state band becomes γ-unstable with E(2₂⁺) = 607.0 keV and E(4₁⁺) = 476.5 keV
- In 110Zr, the SO(6) signature is seen with E(4₁⁺) = 565 keV and E(2₂⁺) = 485 keV
- The ratio R₄/₂ for configuration A at neutron numbers 52-56 is approximately 1.6, and for configuration B at the same neutron numbers is approximately 2.3
- The energy offset parameter Δp(B) decreases sharply by 1 MeV beyond neutron number 56
- The boson effective charges used are e(A) = 0.9 (W.u.)¹/² and e(B) = 2.24 (W.u.)¹/²
- The isotope shift parameters are α = 0.235 fm² and η = 0.264 fm²
- The two-neutron separation energy parameters are Ã = -16.5 MeV and B̃ = 0.758 MeV, with Δn = 2 MeV for neutron numbers 58-70

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum phase transitions in nuclei, previously established as Type I (single-configuration shape transitions, e.g., in Nd-Sm-Gd isotopes near neutron number 90) and Type II (configuration coexistence transitions, e.g., in light Pb-Hg isotopes), by showing that both types can coexist and intertwine in the Zr chain. The results contrast with mean-field based calculations by Nomura (2016) and others, which smooth out the phase transitional behavior and show no jump at the critical point, and also differ from Monte-Carlo Shell Model calculations by Taka (which identify the spherical state in 100Zr as 0₄⁺ rather than 0₂⁺). The work enables future application to the Sr isotopes and may stimulate searches for IQPTs in other fields of physics.
