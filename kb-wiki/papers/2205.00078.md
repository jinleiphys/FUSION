# Perturbative approaches in relativistic kinetic theory and the emergence of first-order hydrodynamics

> Hydrodynamics can be formulated in terms of a perturbative series in derivatives of the temperature, chemical potential, and flow velocity around an equilibrium state. Different formulations for this series have been proposed over the years, which consequently led to the development of various hydrodynamic theories. In this work, we discuss the relativistic generalizations of the perturbative expansions put forward by Chapman and Enskog, and Hilbert, using general matching conditions in kinetic theory. This allows us to describe, in a comprehensive way, how different out-of-equilibrium definitions for the hydrodynamic fields affect the development of the hydrodynamic perturbative series. We provide a perturbative method for systematically deriving the hydrodynamic formulation recently proposed by Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha, and Kovtun (BDNK) from relativistic kinetic theory. The various transport coefficients that appear in BDNK (at first-order) are explicitly computed using a new formulation of the relaxation time approximation for the Boltzmann equation. Assuming Bjorken flow, we also determine the hydrodynamic attractors of BDNK theory and compare the overall hydrodynamic evolution obtained using this formulation with that generated by the Israel-Stewart equations of motion and also kinetic theory.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the recently proposed first-order relativistic hydrodynamic theory by Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha, and Kovtun (BDNK) can be systematically derived from the relativistic Boltzmann equation using a novel perturbative expansion, with the caveat that the derivation requires specific matching conditions (choices of out-of-equilibrium definitions for temperature, chemical potential, and flow velocity) that differ from the standard Landau or Eckart prescriptions.

## Method

The authors formulate three distinct perturbative schemes for deriving hydrodynamics from the relativistic Boltzmann equation: the Chapman-Enskog expansion, the Hilbert expansion, and a new expansion tailored to BDNK theory. They compute the first-order transport coefficients for each scheme using a new formulation of the relaxation time approximation (RTA) for the Boltzmann equation proposed in Rocha (2021), assuming an ultrarelativistic classical gas. For Bjorken flow, they determine the hydrodynamic attractors of BDNK theory and compare the evolution with Israel-Stewart equations and kinetic theory solutions.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative approaches of Hilbert (1912) and Chapman-Enskog (1916-1917) to the relativistic regime with general matching conditions, and provides the first kinetic-theory derivation of the BDNK formalism proposed by Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha, and Kovtun (2018-2020). The BDNK theory solves the causality and stability problems that plague relativistic Navier-Stokes theory derived from Chapman-Enskog, as identified by Hiscock and Lindblom (1983-1985). The results enable systematic comparison between BDNK, Israel-Stewart (1979), and kinetic theory solutions in Bjorken flow, and open the way for further investigation of how different matching conditions affect hydrodynamic attractors.
