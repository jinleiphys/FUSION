# The Role of the Roper in QCD

> We show that existing data suggest a simple scenario in which the nucleon, and the Delta and Roper resonances act as chiral partners in a reducible representation of the full QCD chiral symmetry group. We discuss the peculiar interpretation of this scenario using spin-flavor symmetries of the naive constituent quark model, as well as the consistency of the scenario with large-Nc expectations.

## Key claim

Existing data suggest a simple scenario in which the nucleon, the Delta resonance, and the Roper resonance act as chiral partners in a reducible representation of the full QCD chiral symmetry group, specifically the \((0, 1/2) \oplus (1/2, 1)\) representation of \(SU(2) \times SU(2)\) with approximately maximal mixing, though the authors note that the remarkable agreement of the Adler-Weisberger sum rule with experiment must to some degree be fortuitous given uncertainties.

## Method

The authors performed an updated analysis of the Adler-Weisberger sum rule for pion-nucleon scattering, saturating it with established \(N\) and \(\Delta\) resonances from the Particle Data Group using central values and estimates. They then rederived the sum rule from the chiral \(SU(2) \times SU(2)\) algebra using the Wigner-Eckart theorem in helicity-conserving frames, and used group representation theory to construct chiral multiplets that reproduce the observed axial couplings and masses.

## Key numbers

- The Adler-Weisberger sum rule saturated with all established resonances gives \(g_A = 1.26\), compared to the experimental value of \(1.2670 \pm 0.0035\).
- Saturating the sum rule with only \(\Delta(1232)\) and \(N(1440)\) gives \(g_A = 1.34\).
- The \(\Delta(1232)\) contributes \(\mathcal{I}_\Delta = 1.02\) and the \(N(1440)\) contributes \(\mathcal{I}_N = 0.23\) to the sum rule.
- In the minimal realistic model with maximal mixing (\(\theta = 45^\circ\)), the predicted Roper mass is \(M_{N'} = 1467\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and the nucleon axial coupling is \(g_A = 1.33\).
- Fitting the mixing angle to \(g_A = 1.26\) gives \(\theta = 51^\circ\), predicting \(M_{N'} = 1386\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(g_A' = 0.33\), \(\mathcal{C}_{\Delta N} = 1.25\), and \(\mathcal{C}_{\Delta N'} = 1.56\).
- The experimental Roper mass is \(1440 \pm 30\ \mathrm{MeV}\); the experimental \(\mathcal{C}_{\Delta N}\) is \(1.51 \pm 0.10\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral symmetry analysis of baryons originally developed by Weinberg (1969) and others (Harari, 1966; Gilman and Harari, 1968), applying it to the Roper resonance which has been problematic for quark models (Isgur and Karl, 1979) and lattice QCD calculations (Sasaki, Blum and Ohta, 2002). The proposed chiral representation offers an alternative to the interpretation of the Roper as a hybrid state (Li, Burkert and Li, 1992; Carlson and Mukhopadhyay, 1991) and is consistent with large-\(N_c\) expectations (Carone et al., 1994; Carlson and Carone, 2000), though the authors find that consistency with large \(N_c\) would require the Roper to be in the fundamental representation of \(SU(4)\) rather than the \({\bf 20}\)-dimensional representation usually assumed. This work enables future chiral perturbation theory calculations of the continuum corrections to the multiplet, as the \(N\)-\(N'\) mass splitting is of order the kaon mass.
