# Foundations and Applications of Quantum Kinetic Theory

> Many novel quantum phenomena emerge in non-equilibrium relativistic quantum matter under extreme conditions such as strong magnetic fields and rotations. The quantum kinetic theory based on Wigner functions in quantum field theory provides a powerful and effective microscopic description of these quantum phenomena. In this article we review some of recent advances in the quantum kinetic theory and its applications in describing these quantum phenomena.

## Key claim

This review paper surveys recent advances in quantum kinetic theory (QKT) based on the covariant Wigner function approach in quantum field theory, emphasizing that QKT provides a unified microscopic framework for describing chiral transport phenomena and spin polarization effects in non-equilibrium relativistic quantum matter under extreme conditions such as strong magnetic fields and rotations, though many open questions remain regarding off-equilibrium contributions to spin polarization and the disentanglement of the chiral magnetic effect from background signals in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors review the derivation of master equations for Wigner functions of massless and massive fermions in background electromagnetic fields using the closed-time-path formalism, solving these equations order by order in the Planck constant to obtain chiral kinetic theory (CKT) and quantum kinetic theory for massive fermions. They also discuss the construction of spin Boltzmann equations with non-local collisions, the decomposition of chiral magnetic effect and chiral vortical effect currents, and the development of relativistic spin hydrodynamics from kinetic approaches and the entropy principle.

## Key numbers

- Strong electromagnetic fields of order \(10^{18}\) Gauss can be generated in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, with peak values of a few \(m_{\pi}^{2}\) where \(m_{\pi}\) is the pion mass
- Global orbital angular momentum of two colliding Au nuclei at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV and impact parameter \(b=10\) fm is estimated as \(J_{0}\sim 10^{5}\hbar\)
- STAR experiments measured global polarization of \(\Lambda\) and \(\overline{\Lambda}\) hyperons indicating an average angular velocity or vorticity of the quark-gluon plasma as \(\omega\sim 10^{22}\,\mathrm{s}^{-1}\)
- The chiral magnetic effect current is given by \(\mathbf{J} = \frac{e^{2}}{2\pi^{2}}\mu_{5}\mathbf{B}\), where \(\mu_{5}\) is the chiral chemical potential

## Context

This review extends and systematizes the Wigner-function-based quantum kinetic theory developed over the past decade, building on earlier work on chiral kinetic theory from Hamiltonian approaches (Son and Yamamoto, 2012), path integrals (Stephanov and Yin, 2012), and effective theories (Manuel and Torres-Rincon, 2013). It connects to experimental searches for the chiral magnetic effect at RHIC and LHC, the measurement of global and local spin polarization of hyperons by STAR, and the development of spin hydrodynamics (Florkowski et al., 2017; Hattori et al., 2019; Fukushima and Pu, 2020). The review enables further theoretical work on off-equilibrium spin polarization, chiral transport in astrophysical systems such as core-collapse supernovae and magnetars, and applications to Weyl and Dirac semimetals.
