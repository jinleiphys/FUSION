# Theoretical understanding of the nuclear incompressibility: where do we stand ?

> The status of the theoretical research on the compressional modes of finite nuclei and the incompressibility $K_\infty$ of nuclear matter, is reviewed. It is argued that the recent experimental data on the Isoscalar Giant Monopole Resonance (ISGMR) allow extracting the value of $K_\infty$ with an uncertainity of about $\pm$ 12 MeV. Non-relativistic (Skyrme, Gogny) and relativistic mean field models predict for $K_\infty$ values which are significantly different from one another, namely $\approx$ 220-235 and $\approx$ 250-270 MeV respectively. It is shown that the solution of this puzzle requires a better determination of the symmetry energy at, and around, saturation. The role played by the experimental data of the Isoscalar Giant Dipole Resonance (ISGDR) is also discussed.

## Key claim

The nuclear incompressibility \(K_\infty\) can be extracted from experimental ISGMR data with an uncertainty of about \(\pm 12\) MeV, but the extracted value depends on the theoretical model used: non-relativistic models (Skyrme, Gogny) yield \(K_\infty \approx 220-235\) MeV while relativistic mean field models yield \(K_\infty \approx 250-270\) MeV, and this discrepancy likely originates from differences in the predicted density dependence of the symmetry energy rather than from fundamental inconsistencies in the data.

## Method

The authors review the microscopic approach to extracting \(K_\infty\), which uses energy functionals that describe both nuclear matter and finite nuclei on the same footing. Self-consistent Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations of the Isoscalar Giant Monopole Resonance (ISGMR) energy are performed in nuclei like \(^{208}\)Pb using different parametrizations of Skyrme, Gogny, and relativistic mean field interactions, and the resulting linear relation between \(E_{\text{ISGMR}}\) and \(\sqrt{K_\infty}\) is used to deduce \(K_\infty\) from experimental centroid energies. The analysis also examines the Isoscalar Giant Dipole Resonance (ISGDR) as an alternative probe, comparing theoretical calculations with recent experimental data.

## Key numbers

* The experimental ISGMR centroid energy in \(^{208}\)Pb is \(14.17 \pm 0.28\) MeV (Youngblood et al., 1999), yielding an experimental uncertainty on \(K_\infty\) of \(\delta K_\infty^{\text{exp.}} \sim \pm 10\) MeV.
* The total uncertainty on \(K_\infty\) from combining experimental and theoretical errors is \(\delta K_\infty \sim \pm 12\) MeV.
* Non-relativistic models (Skyrme, Gogny) predict \(K_\infty \approx 220-235\) MeV; relativistic mean field models predict \(K_\infty \approx 250-270\) MeV.
* From Skyrme-CHF calculations, the extracted \(K_\infty\) values are 235 MeV from \(^{208}\)Pb and 220 MeV from \(^{90}\)Zr.
* The ISGDR high-energy centroid in \(^{208}\)Pb from Skyrme-RPA calculations is 23.9 MeV (Colo et al., 2000), while the most recent experimental measurement gives \(23 \pm 0.3\) MeV (Uchida et al., 2003).
* Coupling RPA states with 2p-2h configurations shifts the ISGDR centroid in \(^{208}\)Pb down to 22.9 MeV, with a spreading width of about 6 MeV.

## Context

This review extends the work of Blaizot (1998) and Van Giai et al. (2001) from previous Giant Resonance conferences, and addresses the persistent puzzle that non-relativistic and relativistic models give different values for \(K_\infty\). The authors show that the apparent discrepancy between Skyrme and Gogny results is resolved when fully self-consistent constrained Hartree-Fock calculations are used, yielding consistent values around 220-235 MeV. The work of Piekarewicz (2003) is discussed as suggesting that the relativistic-nonrelativistic discrepancy may be resolved by softening the density dependence of the symmetry energy, though Vretenar et al. (2003) argue that such softened models cannot describe \(N \neq Z\) nuclei. The review enables future work to focus on better determining the symmetry energy and its derivatives, particularly \(K_{sym}\), as the key to resolving the remaining puzzle.
