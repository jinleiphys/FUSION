# Elliptic and triangular flow of heavy flavor in heavy-ion collisions

> We investigate the elliptic and the triangular flow of heavy mesons in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC. The dynamics of heavy quarks is coupled to the locally thermalized and fluid dynamically evolving quark-gluon plasma. The elliptic flow of $D$ mesons and the centrality dependence measured at the LHC is well reproduced for purely collisional and bremsstrahlung interactions. Due to the event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions from the EPOS2 model, the $D$ meson triangular flow is predicted to be nonzero at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV and $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV. We study the centrality dependence and quantify the contributions stemming from flow of the light bulk event and the hadronization process. The flow coefficients as response to the initial eccentricities behave differently for heavy mesons than for light hadrons due to their inertia. Higher-order flow coefficients of heavy flavor become important in order to quantify the degree of thermalization.

# Elliptic and triangular flow of heavy flavor in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The elliptic flow of D mesons at LHC is well reproduced by both purely collisional and collisional+radiative energy loss models, but the triangular flow of D mesons is predicted to be nonzero at both RHIC and LHC energies due to event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions from the EPOS2 model. The flow coefficients as a response to initial eccentricities behave differently for heavy mesons than for light hadrons due to inertia effects, and higher-order flow coefficients of heavy flavor become important for quantifying the degree of thermalization.

## Method
Heavy quarks are propagated via a Monte-Carlo Boltzmann approach (MC@sHQ) coupled to a 3+1 dimensional ideal fluid dynamical evolution of the light bulk sector, with initial conditions from the EPOS2 model that include event-by-event fluctuations. The interactions of heavy quarks with thermal partons are modeled using either purely collisional scatterings (Born approximation with running coupling and Debye mass regularization) or including gluon bremsstrahlung with Landau-Migdal-Pomeranchuk suppression, with cross sections rescaled by a global K-factor calibrated to reproduce R_AA data at high pT.

## Key numbers
- D meson elliptic flow at LHC: purely collisional model yields v2 enhanced by 10-20% over collisional+radiative(LPM) model for pT between 2-4 GeV in the 30-50% most peripheral centrality class
- D meson triangular flow at LHC: purely collisional model yields v3 enhanced by 20-30% over collisional+radiative(LPM) model for pT between 2-4 GeV in the 30-50% most peripheral centrality class
- D meson triangular flow at RHIC: purely collisional model yields v3 enhanced by 30-40% over collisional+radiative(LPM) model for pT between 2-4 GeV in the 20-40% most peripheral centrality class
- Charm quark v2 is about 75% of D meson v2 for pT greater than about 2 GeV in 30-50% most central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV
- Charm quark v3 accounts for only 50% of D meson v3 for pT greater than about 2 GeV in 30-50% most central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV
- At pT approximately 4 GeV, charm quark v2 from path length differences is about 50% of charm quark v2 produced in a medium with bulk flow
- K-factors for energy loss models: K_c^LHC=1.5 (purely collisional at LHC), K_c+r^LHC=0.8 (collisional+radiative at LHC), K_c^RHIC=1.8 (purely collisional at RHIC), K_c+r^RHIC=1.0 (collisional+radiative at RHIC)

## Context
This work extends studies of heavy quark flow in heavy-ion collisions by coupling the MC@sHQ heavy quark transport to EPOS2 initial conditions with event-by-event fluctuations, enabling the first predictions of nonzero triangular flow for D mesons. It builds on prior work by Gossiaux (2010) and Gossiaux (2008) on the MC@sHQ approach, and by Werner (2010, 2012) on EPOS2 initial conditions. The results contrast with Langevin-dynamics approaches by Moore (2004), van Hees (2004), Alberico (2011), and Cao (2013), and with parton cascade studies by Uphoff (2011). The findings enable future studies using the upcoming EPOS3 viscous fluid dynamics and inclusion of hadronic rescatterings for more realistic comparisons with experimental data from ALICE, and open the possibility of using higher-order flow coefficients of heavy flavor as probes of heavy quark thermalization in the quark-gluon plasma.
