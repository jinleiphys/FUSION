# Disentangling nuclear structure through multiparticle azimuthal correlations in high-energy isobar collisions

> Event-by-event fluctuations in the amplitudes of flow harmonics offer a novel approach to probing the initial-state characteristics in heavy-ion collisions. In this study, we conduct a systematic investigation of correlations among various flow harmonics utilizing multiparticle cumulants in $^{96}$Ru+$^{96}$Ru and $^{96}$Zr+$^{96}$Zr collisions at $\sqrtsnn =$ 200 GeV within the framework of a multiphase transport model. Correlated nuclear density distributions specific to the isobar systems are incorporated to evaluate the sensitivity of selected observables to variations in nuclear deformation and neutron skin thickness. The analysis reveals that multiparticle azimuthal correlations are responsive to these nuclear structure features, predominantly in the most central collision events. Furthermore, the examined correlations exhibit shallow dependence on the assumed shear viscosity values. These findings provide a quantitative evaluation of the extent to which multiparticle flow observables can discern nuclear structure effects in isobar collisions and offer valuable guidance for future detailed dynamical investigations and experimental measurements.

## Key claim

Multiparticle azimuthal correlations in isobar collisions at 200 GeV, simulated with the AMPT model, are sensitive to differences in nuclear structure parameters (quadrupole deformation, octupole deformation, neutron skin thickness, and nuclear radius) primarily in the most central collision events, while exhibiting a shallow dependence on the assumed shear viscosity values.

## Method

The authors performed simulations of 96Ru+96Ru and 96Zr+96Zr collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV using the AMPT transport model (v2.26t5, string-melting mode) with deformed Woods-Saxon density profiles. They generated a minimum of 300 million minimum-bias events for each of five nuclear parameter configurations (varying β2, β3, a0, and R0) and computed multiparticle cumulants including asymmetric cumulants, symmetric cumulants, nonlinear coupling coefficients, and correlations between flow harmonics and mean transverse momentum, using a two-subevent method with pseudorapidity gaps to suppress non-flow correlations.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of nuclear structure effects in heavy-ion collisions, including measurements in 238U+238U and 197Au+197Au collisions at RHIC (STAR Collaboration, 2024) and 129Xe+129Xe and 208Pb+208Pb collisions at the LHC (ATLAS, 2022; ALICE, 2021, 2024), which reported patterns consistent with nuclear quadrupole and triaxial deformations. It builds on previous isobar collision analyses by Jia, Zhang, and collaborators (2021-2022) that demonstrated nuclear structure differences in ratios of bulk observables, and provides the first systematic assessment of multiparticle flow observables in isobar collisions using a transport model. The results enable future experimental measurements by the STAR Collaboration (which has collected approximately two billion events per isobar species) to disentangle competing influences of quadrupole and octupole deformations, and could be extended to other isobar systems such as 16O+16O and 20Ne+20Ne in conjunction with modern ab initio nuclear structure calculations.
