# Hydrodynamic attractor of a hybrid viscous fluid in Bjorken flow

> The nonequilibrium evolution in a boost-invariant Bjorken flow of a hybrid viscous fluid model containing two interacting components with different viscosities, such that they represent strongly and weakly self-coupled sectors, is shown to be characterized by a hydrodynamic attractor which has an early-time behavior that is reminiscent of the so-called bottom-up thermalization scenario in heavy-ion collisions. The hydrodynamization times for the two sectors can differ strongly, with details depending on the curve realized on the two-dimensional attractor surface, which might account for different scenarios for small and large systems in nuclear collisions. The total system behaves like a single viscous fluid with a dynamically determined effective shear viscosity.

## Key claim

The nonequilibrium evolution of a hybrid two-fluid system in Bjorken flow possesses a hydrodynamic attractor that is a two-dimensional surface, not a single curve; the early-time behavior of this attractor is reminiscent of the bottom-up thermalization scenario, but the ratio of hydrodynamization times between the two sectors depends strongly on which curve on the attractor surface the system follows, potentially accounting for different scenarios in small and large nuclear collision systems.

## Method

The authors model a boost-invariant Bjorken flow of two conformal fluids coupled via mutual deformations of their effective metrics, as introduced by Kurkela et al. (2018). Each fluid obeys Müller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) relaxation equations with different shear viscosities and relaxation times, chosen to represent a strongly coupled sector (AdS/CFT values) and a weakly coupled sector (ten times higher specific viscosity). The six nonlinear algebraic coupling equations and the dynamical MIS equations are solved numerically for the scale factors of the effective metrics and the anisotropy variables.

## Key numbers

- The less viscous (strong-coupling) sector has shear viscosity parameter \(\tilde C_\eta = 1/(4\pi)\) and relaxation time \(\tilde C_\tau = (2-\ln 2)/(2\pi)\); the more viscous sector has \(C_\eta = 10\tilde C_\eta\) and \(C_\tau = 5 C_\eta\).
- For the chosen parameters, the limiting early-time anisotropy values are \(\sigma \approx 0.45\) for the more viscous fluid and \(\tilde\sigma \approx 0.62\) for the less viscous fluid.
- For one attractor solution, the hydrodynamization time ratio is \(R_\mathrm{hd} = \tau_\mathrm{hd}/\tilde\tau_\mathrm{hd} \approx 5.76\), with \(\tau_\mathrm{hd} = 12.0\) (in units of \(\gamma^{1/4}\)) for the hard sector and \(\tilde\tau_\mathrm{hd} = 2.08\) for the soft sector.
- Varying the total energy at the reference time \(\gamma^{-1/4}\tau = 1\) changes the ratio from \(R_\mathrm{hd} \approx 2.62\) (higher energy) to \(R_\mathrm{hd} \approx 18.4\) (lower energy).
- At its hydrodynamization time, the hard sector is still strongly anisotropic with \(P_L/P \approx 0.14\) (from \(\chi_\mathrm{hd} = 0.215\)), while the soft sector at its earlier hydrodynamization time has \(\tilde P_L/\tilde P \approx 0.6\) (from \(\tilde\chi_\mathrm{hd} = 0.101\)).

## Context

This work extends the equilibrium analysis of the hybrid two-fluid model by Kurkela et al. (2018) into nonequilibrium dynamics, and is inspired by the semi-holographic approach of Faulkner et al. (2010) and Mukhopadhyay et al. (2013) for combining weak-coupling glasma with holographic AdS/CFT. It builds on the hydrodynamic attractor concept established for single conformal fluids by Heller et al. (2015) and Denicol et al. (2017), and is the first demonstration of a hydrodynamic attractor in a two-fluid system. The results enable future studies of how the attractor surface parameters might connect to different hydrodynamization scenarios for small systems (p-p, p-A) versus large systems (A-A) in heavy-ion collisions, and suggest generalizations to include first-order phase transitions or non-conformal equations of state.
