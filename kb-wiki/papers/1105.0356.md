# η' Multiplicity and the Witten-Veneziano relation at finite temperature

> We discuss and propose the minimal generalization of the Witten-Veneziano relation to finite temperatures, prompted by STAR and PHENIX experimental results on the multiplicity of eta' mesons. After explaining why these results show that the zero-temperature Witten-Veneziano relation cannot be straightforwardly extended to temperatures T too close to the chiral restoration temperature T_Ch and beyond, we find the quantity which should replace, at T>0, the Yang-Mills topological susceptibility appearing in the T=0 Witten-Veneziano relation, in order to avoid the conflict with experiment at T>0. This is illustrated through concrete T-dependences of pseudoscalar meson masses in a chirally well-behaved, Dyson-Schwinger approach, but our results and conclusions are of a more general nature and, essentially, model-independent.

## Key claim

The straightforward extension of the zero-temperature Witten-Veneziano relation to finite temperatures, using the Yang-Mills topological susceptibility χ_YM(T), leads to an increase of the η' mass near the chiral restoration temperature T_Ch, which contradicts experimental observations of η' mass reduction at RHIC. The authors propose that replacing χ_YM(T) with an effective susceptibility χ̃(T) derived from the Leutwyler-Smilga relation resolves this conflict while preserving the successful zero-temperature relation.

## Method

The authors used a Dyson-Schwinger (DS) approach with a rank-2 separable interaction to compute the nonanomalous parts of pseudoscalar meson masses and decay constants at finite temperature, including the chiral quark condensate ⟨q̄q⟩_0(T). The anomalous contributions to the η'-η complex were then incorporated through the Witten-Veneziano relation, but with the Yang-Mills topological susceptibility χ_YM(T) replaced by an effective susceptibility χ̃(T) derived from the Leutwyler-Smilga relation, using a parametrized Ansatz for the temperature dependence of the correction term with a power δ.

## Key numbers

- The η' mass in vacuum is M_η' = 957.8 MeV; at RHIC it is reduced by at least 200 MeV inside the fireball at 99.9% confidence level, based on analysis of PHENIX and STAR data at √s_NN = 200 GeV central Au+Au collisions
- The Yang-Mills topological susceptibility at T=0 is χ_YM = (0.1757 GeV)^4, taken as a weighted average of various lattice results
- The "melting temperature" where χ_YM(T) starts to decrease appreciably is T_YM ≈ 260 MeV from lattice (Alles 1996, Boyd 1996) or T_YM ≈ 300 MeV (Gattringer 2002), while the pseudocritical temperature for chiral restoration T_Ch is lower by some 100 MeV or more
- For δ=0 (temperature-independent correction term) and δ=1 (correction term proportional to ⟨q̄q⟩_0(T)), the resulting η' mass decreases are consistent with experimental findings

## Context

This work extends the earlier theoretical study by Horvatic et al. (2007) on the thermal behavior of the η'-η complex from the Witten-Veneziano relation, which had predicted η' mass enhancement near T_Ch. The authors reconcile this with the experimental analysis by Csörgő et al. (2009) and Vértesi et al. (2009) showing η' mass reduction at RHIC. The approach differs from model predictions for topological susceptibility by Contrera et al. (2009) and Costa et al. (2008); instead, it uses the Leutwyler-Smilga relation (Leutwyler and Smilga 1992) to define an effective susceptibility that ties U_A(1) restoration to chiral restoration, consistent with functional methods analysis by Alkofer et al. (2011) finding that anomalous U_A(1) breaking is related to dynamical chiral symmetry breaking.
