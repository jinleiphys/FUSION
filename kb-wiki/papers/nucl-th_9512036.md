# The $p p \to p pπ^0$ Reaction near Threshold: A Chiral Power Counting Approach

> We use power-counting arguments as an organizing principle to apply chiral perturbation theory, including an explicit $Δ$, to the $p p \rightarrow p p π^0$ reaction near threshold. There are two lowest-order leading mechanisms expected to contribute to the amplitude with similar magnitudes: an impulse term, and a $Δ$-excitation mechanism. We examine formally sub-leading but potentially large mechanisms, including pion-rescattering and short-ranged contributions. We show that the pion-rescattering contribution is enhanced by off-shell effects and has a sign opposite to that of a recent estimate based on a PCAC pion interpolating field. Our result is that the impulse term interferes destructively with the pion rescattering and $Δ$-excitation terms. In addition, we have modeled the short-ranged interaction using $σ$ and $ω$ exchange mechanisms. A recoil correction to the impulse approximation is small. The total amplitude obtained including all of these processes is found to yield cross sections substantially smaller than the measured ones.

## Key claim

The authors use chiral perturbation theory with an explicit Delta isobar as an organizing principle to show that the total amplitude for the $p p \rightarrow p p \pi^0$ reaction near threshold, including impulse, Delta-excitation, pion-rescattering, recoil, and short-range terms, yields cross sections substantially smaller than the measured ones, contrary to earlier claims that either pion-rescattering or short-range exchanges alone could explain the data.

## Method

The authors apply Weinberg's power-counting scheme to chiral perturbation theory, including an explicit Delta isobar as a light degree of freedom, to classify irreducible diagrams for the $p p \rightarrow p p \pi^0$ reaction near threshold. They evaluate the leading-order impulse term, Delta-excitation term, and formally sub-leading but potentially large pion-rescattering and short-range contributions (modeled by sigma and omega exchanges) using three different phenomenological nucleon-nucleon potentials to describe initial and final state interactions. The operators are derived from the effective chiral Lagrangian up to second order, and the amplitudes are computed in momentum space with the outgoing pion in an s-wave.

## Key numbers

- The typical internal nuclear momentum in the reaction is $p_{\rm typ} \sim \sqrt{m_N m_\pi}$, which yields an expansion parameter $\sqrt{m_\pi/M} \sim 0.4$ rather than the usual $m_\pi/M \sim 0.1$ to 0.2.
- The impulse term alone gives a total cross-section about a factor of five below the data, as established by earlier work (Meyer 1990, Miller and Sauer 1991, Niskanen 1992).
- The Delta-excitation term is of order $m_\pi/(m_\pi - \delta)$ relative to the impulse term, where $\delta = m_\Delta - m_N \simeq 2 m_\pi$, making it comparable in magnitude to the impulse term in the physical world.
- The pion-rescattering (seagull) term is formally suppressed by relative order $m_\pi/M$ compared to the leading terms, with an amplitude of order $f_\pi^{-3} (m_\pi/M)^{3/2}$.
- The total amplitude including all processes yields cross sections substantially smaller than the measured ones, even when the sigma and omega exchange term is included.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory framework of Weinberg (1990) and Ordonez, Ray, and van Kolck (1994) to the $p p \rightarrow p p \pi^0$ reaction, addressing the long-standing discrepancy between early impulse approximation calculations and data from Meyer (1990) and the Uppsala group. The authors contradict the explanation of Lee and Riska (1992) that sigma and omega exchanges alone can account for the data, and also the explanation of Hernandez and Oset (1995) that off-shell pion-rescattering effects are sufficient. The paper enables future systematic chiral perturbation theory calculations of pion production reactions, though the authors note that one-loop diagrams and contact terms of the same order as the seagull term could be significant and require further study.
