# Nucleon Resonance Excitation with Virtual Photons

> The unitary isobar model MAID is used for a partial wave analysis of pion photoproduction and electroproduction data on the nucleon. In particular we have taken emphasis on the region of the Delta(1232) resonance and have separated the resonance and background amplitudes with the K-matrix approach. This leads to electromagnetic properties of the dressed Delta resonance, where all multipole amplitudes become purely imaginary and all form factors and helicity amplitudes become purely real at the K-matrix pole of W=M_Delta=1232 MeV. The REM=E2/M1 and RSM=C2/M1 ratios of the quadrupole excitation are compared to recent data analysis of different groups. The REM ratio of MAID2005 agrees very well with the data and has a linear behavior over the whole experimentally explored Q^2 region with a small positive slope that predicts a zero crossing around 3.5 GeV^2. The recent RSM data for Q^2 < 0.2 GeV^2 indicate a qualitative change in the shape of the ratio which can be explained by the impact of the Siegert theorem at pseudothreshold (Q^2=-0.086 GeV^2) in the unphysical region.

## Key claim
The unitary isobar model MAID provides a partial wave analysis of pion photoproduction and electroproduction that separates resonance and background amplitudes using the K-matrix approach; the claimed linear behavior of the REM ratio over the entire experimentally explored Q² region with a zero crossing predicted around 3.5 GeV² is conditional on the specific MAID2005 parametrization, and the RSM data for Q² < 0.2 GeV² show a qualitative shape change that the model explains via the Siegert theorem at pseudothreshold (Q² = -0.086 GeV²) in the unphysical region.

## Method
The authors used the unitary isobar model MAID to perform a partial wave analysis of the world data on pion photoproduction and electroproduction, fitting background parameters and transverse normalization constants for nucleon resonance excitation. They employed a K-matrix approximation to separate resonance and background amplitudes, with resonance contributions described by a Breit-Wigner form, and performed both single-Q² fits and a superglobal fit combining all electroproduction data to determine the Q² evolution of transition form factors and helicity amplitudes for 13 four-star resonances below W = 2 GeV.

## Key numbers
- The Delta(1232) resonance mass is W = M_Delta = 1232 MeV with a total width Gamma_Delta = 115 MeV.
- The REM ratio from MAID2005 shows a linear behavior with a small positive slope predicting a zero crossing around Q² = 3.5 GeV².
- The pseudothreshold for the Siegert theorem occurs at Q² = -0.086 GeV² in the unphysical region.
- The fitted parameters for the N → Delta transition include: M1 amplitude at Q²=0 of 294 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/², E2 amplitude of -6.37 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/², and C2 amplitude of -19.5 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/², all with an exponential factor gamma = 0.21 GeV⁻² and dipole form factor G_D(Q²) = 1/(1+Q²/0.71 GeV²)².
- The MAID2003 re-fit at W = 1232 MeV and Q² = 0.2 GeV² yields Re{S₁₊M₁₊*}/|M₁₊|² = -5.45 ± 0.42% and Im{S₁₊M₁₊*}/|M₁₊|² = -2.92 ± 0.48%.
- For the Roper resonance P₁₁(1440), the transverse helicity coupling shows a zero crossing around Q² ≈ 0.7 GeV² and a maximum at Q² ≈ 2.5 GeV², with the longitudinal excitation reaching very large values around Q² ≈ 0.5 GeV².
- The S₁₁(1535) resonance transverse form factor falls off very weakly and at Q² ≈ 3 GeV² is much stronger than the Delta or D₁₃ resonances.

## Context
This work extends the unitary isobar model MAID (previously developed by the Mainz group) to analyze new electroproduction data from MAMI, ELSA, Bates, Bonn, and JLab, covering a Q² range from 0.1 to 4.0 GeV² and an energy range from 1.1 to 2.0 GeV. The results are compared with the GW/SAID analysis (SM02 solution), dynamical models such as DMT2001 (Kamalov and Yang, 1999) and Sato/Lee (2001), and experimental analyses from Beck et al. (1997) at Mainz, Frolov et al. (1999) at JLab Hall C, and Kelly et al. (2005) at JLab Hall A. The findings enable improved determination of electromagnetic transition form factors for nucleon resonances, particularly addressing the role of pion cloud effects and the Siegert theorem at pseudothreshold, with implications for distinguishing between quark model and hybrid state interpretations of the Roper resonance as raised by Li and Burkert (1992).
