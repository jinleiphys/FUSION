# Predicting Quadrupole deformation via anisotropic flow and transverse momentum spectra in isotopic $\mathbf{\prescript{128-135}{54}{\mathrm{Xe}}}$ collisions at LHC

> In the hydrodynamical description of heavy-ion collisions, the elliptic flow $\mathrm{v_{2}}$ and triangular flow $\mathrm{v_{3}}$ are sensitive to the quadrupole deformation $\mathrm{β_{2}}$ of the colliding nuclei. We produce $\mathrm{v_{2}}$ and $\mathrm{v_{3}}$ ratios qualitatively and quantitatively in most-central Xe-Xe collisions at 5.44 TeV. By employing HYDJET++ model, we study the sensitivity of anisotropic flow coefficients and mean transverse momentum to the quadrupole deformation and system-size in isotopic Xe-Xe collisions. Flow observables strongly depend on the strength of nucleon-nucleon scattering occuring in even-A and odd-A nuclei. Flow for odd-A nuclei is suppressed in comparison to flow in even-A collisions. There exists a linear inter-dependence between $\mathrm{p_{T}}$ integrated anisotropic flow and nuclear deformation. Mean transverse momentum signifies the fireball temperature in body-body and tip-tip collisions. There exists a negative linear correlation of $\mathrm{\langle p_{T} \rangle}$ with collision system-size and a positive correlation with nuclear deformation. Flow measurements in high-energy, heavy-ion collisions using isotopic collision systems, offer a new precision tool to study nuclear structure physics. Observation of nuclear structure properties like nuclear deformation in a heavy-ion collision such as this would be very interesting.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that anisotropic flow coefficients (v₂, v₃) and mean transverse momentum in most-central Xe-Xe collisions at 5.44 TeV are sensitive to the quadrupole deformation β₂ of the colliding nuclei, but the relationship between flow and deformation is opposite for even-A versus odd-A isotopes due to differences in nucleon-nucleon scattering strength.

## Method

The authors employed the Monte Carlo HYDJET++ event generator to simulate 10⁵ minimum bias, symmetric, most-central (0–5)% collisions of eight xenon isotopes (A=128–135) at 5.44 TeV center-of-mass energy. They used a modified Woods-Saxon nuclear density profile incorporating quadrupole deformation parameters β₂ taken from low-energy nuclear structure models, with kinematic cuts of |η| < 0.8 and 0 < pT < 5.0 GeV/c. Anisotropic flow coefficients v₂ and v₃ were computed from the azimuthal distribution of produced particles relative to the reaction plane.

## Key numbers

- β₂ values for the eight xenon isotopes range from 0.1837 (¹²⁸Xe) down to 0.0830 (¹³⁵Xe), as listed in Table 1 of the paper
- Model parameters for ¹²⁹Xe-¹²⁹Xe collisions: chemical freeze-out temperature Tch = 151 MeV, thermal freeze-out temperature Tth = 105 MeV, pTmin = 10.5 GeV/c
- Nuclear radius parameter R₀ = 1.15 fm, surface thickness a = 0.59 ± 0.07 fm
- The peak of elliptic flow v₂ occurs around 3.0 GeV/c in transverse momentum
- The ratio (v₂²)Xe/(v₂²)Pb varies linearly with (β₂²)Xe, while (v₃²)Xe/(v₃²)Pb remains approximately constant across the isotopic chain

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear deformation effects in heavy-ion collisions from earlier measurements in Au+Au and U+U collisions at RHIC (PhysRevLett.115.222301, 2015) and Pb+Pb versus Xe+Xe collisions at LHC (elliptic_2022, 201882). The authors build on previous HYDJET++ studies of deformed ¹²⁹Xe collisions (Pandey_2022, PhysRevC.103.014903) by systematically scanning the full isotopic chain of xenon (A=128–135) to probe how flow observables respond to varying quadrupole deformation. The results enable future comparisons with experimental data from the LHC Xe-Xe run and suggest that isotopic collision systems can serve as a precision tool for nuclear structure physics, complementing low-energy nuclear spectroscopy measurements.
