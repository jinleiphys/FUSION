# The role of the Dirac sea in the quenching of Gamow-Teller strength

> The issue of Gamow-Teller sum rule in atomic nuclei is reexamined in the framework of a relativistic description of nuclei. It is shown that the total Gamow-Teller strength in the nucleon sector is reduced by about 12% as compared to the prediction of a non-relativistic description. This reduction is due to the antinucleon degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

The total Gamow-Teller (GT) strength in the nucleon sector is reduced by about 12% compared to the non-relativistic prediction, but this reduction depends on the nucleon effective mass and Fermi velocity; for a free nucleon mass the quenching is only about 5%.

## Method

The authors compute the Ikeda-Fujii-Fujita GT sum rule in a relativistic mean-field model where the nucleus consists of Dirac particles bound in Lorentz scalar and vector potentials. They evaluate the matrix elements of the GT operators \(F_+\) and \(F_-\) including both nucleon and antinucleon (Dirac sea) degrees of freedom, and then derive the sum rule value restricted to the nucleon sector by isolating the nucleon-antinucleon contributions. The effect of Pauli blocking terms on the RPA correlation function and the giant GT resonance strength is also examined.

## Key numbers

- The total GT strength in the nucleon sector is quenched by about **12%** relative to the non-relativistic sum rule, for a nucleon effective mass of about \(0.6M\) and Fermi momentum \(k_F = 1.36\,\text{fm}^{-1}\) (Fermi velocity \(v_F = 0.43\)).
- For a free nucleon mass, the quenching is about **5%**.
- The fraction of the sum rule taken by nucleon-antinucleon excitations is approximately \(2 \cdot \frac{2}{3} v_F^2 (N-Z)\).
- In the limit \(k_F \rightarrow \infty\), the ratio of GT strength from nucleon excitations to antinucleon excitations is **1 to 2**.
- The excitation energy of the giant GT resonance in the relativistic RPA is given by \(\omega_{\text{GT}} = \frac{1-2v_F^2/3}{1-g_5 P(k_F)} \, g_5 \frac{8k_F^3}{3\pi^2} \frac{N-Z}{2A}\), where \(P(k_F)\) is a Pauli blocking term; the Pauli blocking effect on the excitation energy is less than **0.5%** for \(g' = 0.6\).
- The strength of the giant GT resonance \(S_{\text{GT}}\) is almost equal to the nucleon-space sum rule value \(\left(1-\frac{2}{3}v_F^2\right)(N-Z)\), since \(P(k_F)\) is negligible.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing discussion of GT quenching in atomic nuclei, which has been observed experimentally at about 10% in medium-heavy nuclei (experimental measurements cited as "s"). The authors identify a new quenching mechanism from antinucleon degrees of freedom, distinct from previously proposed sources such as multiconfiguration spreading (cited as "mult") and coupling to the \(\Delta\)-hole sector (cited as "delta"). The result enables future studies to disentangle the contributions of \(\Delta\)-hole and nucleon-antinucleon states to the observed quenching, and suggests that antinucleon degrees of freedom may also modify other sum rules, such as the Coulomb sum rule (cited as "cos" and "m").
