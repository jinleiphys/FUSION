# Multi-messenger heavy-ion collision physics

> This work studies the production of direct photons in relativistic nuclear collisions, along with the production of hadrons. Radiation from the very first instants to the final moments of the evolution is included. The hybrid model used here describes all stages of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Chronologically, those are an initial state reflecting the collision of nuclei described within the Color Glass Condensate effective theory; a pre-equilibrium phase based on non-equilibrium linear response; relativistic viscous hydrodynamics, and a hadronic afterburner. The effect of the pre-equilibrium phase on both photonic and hadronic observables is highlighted for the first time. The potential of photon observables -- spectrum, differential elliptic and triangular flow -- to reveal the chemical equilibration time is studied. Finally, we consider "small collision systems", including proton+nucleus collisions and collisions of light nuclei, as probed by hadronic and electromagnetic observables. We demonstrate how photon production can signal the formation of quark-gluon plasma in such small systems.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that including a pre-equilibrium phase (KøMPøST) between the IP-Glasma initial state and relativistic viscous hydrodynamics modifies the extracted transport coefficients of QCD matter, particularly the bulk viscosity, when fitting hadronic data from RHIC and LHC. The authors show that photon observables can reveal the chemical equilibration time of quarks in an initially gluonic system, and that photon production can signal quark-gluon plasma formation in small collision systems such as p+Pb and O+O.

## Method
The authors use a hybrid model combining IP-Glasma initial conditions with classical Yang-Mills evolution, followed by either a KøMPøST pre-equilibrium phase (scenario I) or direct transition to relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (scenario II). The hydrodynamic phase uses the MUSIC code with shear viscosity η/s = 0.12 and a tuned bulk viscosity, followed by a hadronic afterburner (UrQMD). Photon production includes prompt photons from NLO pQCD, thermal photons from the hydrodynamic phase, and photons estimated from the KøMPøST phase by decomposing the energy-momentum tensor into temperature, flow, and viscous components.

## Key numbers
- Switching time from IP-Glasma to KøMPøST: τ_IPG^EKT = 0.1 fm/c (corresponding to gluon saturation scale Q_s ~ 2 GeV)
- Default switching time from KøMPøST to hydrodynamics: τ_EKT^hydro = 0.8 fm/c
- Switching time from IP-Glasma directly to hydrodynamics (scenario II): τ_IPG^hydro = 0.4 fm/c
- Switching temperature to hadronic afterburner: T_switch = 145 MeV
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: η/s = 0.12
- The KøMPøST phase generates approximately 20% less entropy during the pre-equilibrium phase compared to scenario II
- Photon production from late stages uses hydrodynamic profiles down to T = 105 MeV

## Context
This work extends the hybrid modeling approach of Gale (2013) and Schenke (2020) by incorporating the KøMPøST pre-equilibrium framework of Kurkela (2018) into simultaneous calculations of hadronic and electromagnetic observables. It directly compares scenarios with and without this pre-equilibrium phase, finding that the extracted bulk viscosity profile differs between the two cases. The study enables future investigations of chemical equilibration times using photon flow observables and provides predictions for photon production in small systems (p+Pb and O+O) that could be tested at the LHC.
