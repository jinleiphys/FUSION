# Numerical Study of Dense Adjoint Matter in Two Color QCD

> We identify the global symmetries of SU(2) lattice gauge theory with N flavors of staggered fermion in the presence of a quark chemical potential mu, for fermions in both fundamental and adjoint representations, and anticipate likely patterns of symmetry breaking at both low and high densities. Results from numerical simulations of the model with N=1 adjoint flavor on a 4^3x8 lattice are presented, using both hybrid Monte Carlo and Two-Step Multi-Boson algorithms. It is shown that the sign of the fermion determinant starts to fluctuate once the model enters a phase with non-zero baryon charge density. HMC simulations are not ergodic in this regime, but TSMB simulations retain ergodicity even in the dense phase, and in addition appear to show superior decorrelation. The HMC results for the equation of state and the pion mass show good quantitative agreement with the predictions of chiral perturbation theory, which should hold only for N>=2. The TSMB results incorporating the sign of the determinant support a delayed onset transition, consistent with the pattern of symmetry breaking expected for N=1.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for SU(2) lattice gauge theory with one flavor of adjoint staggered fermion, the sign of the fermion determinant begins to fluctuate once the system enters a phase with non-zero baryon charge density, and that the Hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm is not ergodic in this regime while the Two-Step Multi-Boson algorithm retains ergodicity. The HMC results for the equation of state and pion mass agree with chiral perturbation theory predictions that should only hold for N≥2, while the TSMB results incorporating the sign of the determinant support a delayed onset transition consistent with the expected symmetry breaking pattern for N=1.

## Method

The authors performed numerical simulations of SU(2) lattice gauge theory with N=1 flavor of adjoint staggered fermion on a 4³×8 lattice, using both Hybrid Monte Carlo and Two-Step Multi-Boson algorithms. They identified the global symmetries of the model and analyzed the eigenvalue spectrum of the Dirac operator to understand the sign problem and algorithm performance, then measured the equation of state, pion mass, and baryon number density as functions of the chemical potential μ.

## Key numbers

- Simulations were performed on a 4³×8 lattice volume
- The onset chemical potential from HMC simulations was found at μₒ ≃ m_π/2, where m_π is the pion mass
- The HMC results for the equation of state and pion mass showed "good quantitative agreement" with chiral perturbation theory predictions
- For N=1 adjoint quarks, the Goldstone mode count is N(2N-1) = 1, yielding only the 0⁻ mesonic pion as a Goldstone boson
- For fundamental quarks with N=1, the Goldstone count is N(2N+1) = 3, giving one pion and two scalar diquark states

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Two Color QCD by Hands, Kogut, Lombardo, and others (1999-2000) who established the symmetry patterns for fundamental quarks, and by Kogut, Stephanov, Toublan, Verbaarschot, and Zhitnitsky (1999-2000) who developed chiral perturbation theory predictions for these models. The paper specifically addresses the case of adjoint quarks with odd flavor number, which has a sign problem absent in the fundamental case, and demonstrates that the TSMB algorithm can handle this sign problem while HMC cannot. The results enable future studies of dense matter in a controlled lattice setting where the sign problem is tractable, potentially providing insight into the behavior of QCD at high baryon density.
