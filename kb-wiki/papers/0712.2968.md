# Constraints on the variability of quark masses from nuclear binding

> Based on recent work on nuclear binding, we update and extend the anthropic constraints on the light quark masses, with results that are more tightly constrained than previously obtained. We find that heavy nuclei would fall apart (because the attractive nuclear central potential becomes too weak) if the sum of the light quark masses m_u+m_d would exceed their physical values by 64% (at 95% confidence level). We summarize the anthropic constraints that follow from requiring the existence both of heavy atoms and of hydrogen. With the additional assumption that the quark Yukawa couplings do not vary, these constraints provide a remarkably tight anthropic window for the Higgs vacuum expectation value: 0.39 < v/v_physical < 1.64.

## Key claim

Heavy nuclei would become unbound (the attractive nuclear central potential becomes too weak) if the sum of the light quark masses \(m_u+m_d\) exceeds their physical values by 64% at 95% confidence level, a tighter constraint than previously obtained by Agrawal et al. (ABDS).

## Method

The authors used effective field theory to parameterize the dominant isoscalar-scalar and isoscalar-vector contact interactions responsible for nuclear binding in heavy nuclei, employing results from Furnstahl, Serot and co-workers for the binding energy per nucleon in \(^{16}\text{O}\) and \(^{208}\text{Pb}\). They connected the scalar coupling strength \(G_S\) to the light quark masses via a dispersive sum rule for two-pion exchange, where the threshold at \(2 m_\pi\) depends on the pion mass through \(m_\pi^2 = B_0 (m_u+m_d)\), and used chiral perturbation theory and unitarized amplitudes from prior work (Damour and Donoghue, 2007) to compute the variation of \(G_S\) with \(m_\pi\). A complementary nuclear matter model based on Walecka-type mean-field theory was also employed to confirm the sensitivity.

## Key numbers

- The scalar strength in the chiral limit is \(G_S|_{\text{chiral}} / G_S|_{\text{physical}} = 1.37 \pm 0.10\).
- The binding energy per nucleon for \(^{16}\text{O}\) is \(\text{B.E.}/A \simeq -82 \eta_S + 44\eta_V + 30\) (in MeV), and for \(^{208}\text{Pb}\) is \(\text{B.E.}/A \simeq -104 \eta_S + 57\eta_V + 36\) (in MeV).
- The critical scalar strength for unbinding is \(\eta_S|_{\text{critical}} = 0.90\) for \(^{16}\text{O}\) and \(0.89\) for \(^{208}\text{Pb}\).
- The pion mass-squared must be \(36 \pm 14\%\) larger than the physical value for nuclei to become unbound.
- The final 95% confidence upper bound on the sum of light quark masses is \((m_u+m_d)/(m_u+m_d)_{\text{phys}} < 1.64\), corresponding to \(m_u+m_d \le 18\) MeV (at a scale of 1 GeV).
- The lower bound from hydrogen stability gives \(m_d - m_u - 1.67 m_e \ge 0.83\) MeV.
- Under the assumption of fixed Yukawa couplings, the Higgs vacuum expectation value is constrained to \(0.39 < v/v_{\text{physical}} < 1.64\).

## Context

This work extends the anthropic constraints on quark masses and the Higgs vev originally derived by Agrawal et al. (ABDS), who used the atomic principle (the need for atoms to exist) to bound the Higgs vev. The present paper refines the nuclear binding constraint using modern effective field theory results from Furnstahl, Serot and co-workers, and chiral perturbation theory calculations by Damour and Donoghue (2007). The results enable tighter anthropic windows for fundamental parameters, which can inform discussions of the string landscape, multiverse scenarios, and the fine-tuning problem of the Higgs vev; the authors note that a companion paper will address implications for tests of the equivalence principle.
