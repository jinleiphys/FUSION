# Formation spectra of charmed meson--nucleus systems using an antiproton beam

> We investigate the structure and formation of charmed meson--nucleus systems, with the aim of understanding the charmed meson--nucleon interactions and the properties of the charmed mesons in the nuclear medium. The $\bar{D}$ mesic nuclei are of special interest, since they have tiny decay widths due to the absence of strong decays for the $\bar{D} N$ pair. Employing an effective model for the $\bar{D} N$ and $D N$ interactions and solving the Klein--Gordon equation for $\bar{D}$ and $D$ in finite nuclei, we find that the $D^{-}$-${}^{11}\rm{B}$ system has $1 s$ and $2p$ mesic nuclear states and that the $D^{0}$-${}^{11}\rm{B}$ system binds in a $1s$ state. In view of the forthcoming experiments by the PANDA and CBM Collaborations at the future FAIR facility and the J-PARC upgrade, we calculate the formation spectra of the $[D^{-}$-${}^{11}\rm{B}]$ and $[D^{0}$-${}^{11}\rm{B}]$ mesic nuclei for an antiproton beam on a ${}^{12} \rm{C}$ target. Our results suggest that it is possible to observe the $2 p$ $D^{-}$ mesic nuclear state with an appropriate experimental setup.

## Key claim

The authors find that the $2p$ $D^{-}$ nuclear state in $[D^{-}$-${}^{11}\rm{B}]$ may be observable as a small peak in the formation spectrum of the ${}^{12}\rm{C}(\bar{p}, D^{+})$ reaction, but only with an appropriate experimental setup; the $1s$ $D^{-}$ state and the $D^{0}$ nuclear bound state do not produce visible signatures due to the large momentum transfer inherent to the reaction.

## Method

The authors solve the Klein-Gordon equation for $D^{-}$ and $D^{0}$ mesons in ${}^{11}\rm{B}$ using an energy-dependent optical potential derived from a self-consistent unitarized coupled-channels model of the charmed meson-nucleon interaction that incorporates heavy-quark spin symmetry constraints. They then calculate the formation spectra for the ${}^{12}\rm{C}(\bar{p}, D^{+})$ and ${}^{12}\rm{C}(\bar{p}, \bar{D}^{0})$ reactions at an antiproton beam momentum of $8\ \mathrm{GeV}/c$ using the Green's function method with the eikonal approximation for distorted waves.

## Key numbers

- $D^{-}$ atomic $1s$ state in ${}^{11}\rm{B}$: binding energy $B_{\rm E} = 446.1\ \mathrm{keV}$, width $\Gamma = 77.0\ \mathrm{keV}$ (with Coulomb plus optical potential)
- $D^{-}$ nuclear $1s$ state in ${}^{11}\rm{B}$: $B_{\rm E} = 21.7\ \mathrm{MeV}$, $\Gamma = 0.5\ \mathrm{MeV}$
- $D^{-}$ nuclear $2p$ state in ${}^{11}\rm{B}$: $B_{\rm E} = 14.5\ \mathrm{MeV}$, $\Gamma = 2.4\ \mathrm{MeV}$
- $D^{0}$ nuclear $1s$ state in ${}^{11}\rm{B}$: $B_{\rm E} = 6.5\ \mathrm{MeV}$, $\Gamma = 10.8\ \mathrm{MeV}$
- Elementary cross sections at $P_{\bar p} = 8\ \mathrm{GeV}/c$ and forward angle: $760\ \mathrm{nb/sr}$ for $p\bar{p}\to D^{+}D^{-}$, $40\ \mathrm{nb/sr}$ for $p\bar{p}\to D^{0}\bar{D}^{0}$
- Momentum transfer in the reaction is approximately $1\ \mathrm{GeV}/c$
- Total cross sections used for distortion: $\sigma_{\bar{p}N} = 59\ \mathrm{mb}$, $\sigma_{\bar{D}N} = 10\ \mathrm{mb}$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of charmed meson-nucleus bound states (Tolos 2009, Garcia-Recio 2010, Garcia-Recio 2011) by calculating for the first time the formation spectra for charmed mesic nuclear states using an energy-dependent optical potential. It builds on earlier predictions of $D^{-}$ atoms by Tsushima (1998) using the quark-meson coupling model and on studies of $\bar{D}$ meson levels in nuclei by Yasui (2012). The results are intended to guide upcoming experiments at the PANDA and CBM Collaborations at FAIR and the J-PARC upgrade, and the authors suggest that alternative reactions involving $D^{*}$ mesons or pion emission could achieve smaller momentum transfers and thus more favorable formation conditions.
