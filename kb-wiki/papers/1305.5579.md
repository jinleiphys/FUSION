# Influence of the Nucleon Hard Partons Distribution on J/ΨSuppression in a GMC Framework

> In a Glauber Monte Carlo framework, taking account of the transverse spatial distribution of hard partons in the nucleon, we analyse the nuclear modification factor $R_{dAu}$ for $J/ψ$ in d+Au collisions with the EPS09 shadowing parametrization. After the influence of nucleon hard partons distribution is considered, a clearly upward correction is revealed for the dependence of $R_{dAu}$ on $N_{coll}$ in peripheral d+Au collisions, however, an unconspicuous correction is shown for the results versus $p_{T}$. The theoretical results are in good agreement with the experimental data from PHENIX.

# Influence of the Nucleon Hard Partons Distribution on J/Ψ Suppression in a GMC Framework

## Key claim
In a Glauber Monte Carlo (GMC) framework using the EPS09 shadowing parametrization, including the transverse spatial distribution of hard partons within the nucleon produces a clearly upward correction to the nuclear modification factor R_dAu for J/ψ in d+Au collisions when plotted against N_coll in peripheral collisions, but only an unconspicuous correction when plotted against p_T. The improvement in agreement with PHENIX data is most visible for the N_coll dependence, not for the p_T dependence.

## Method
The authors implement a GMC framework for J/ψ production in d+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV, considering only the gluon-gluon fusion subprocess g+g → c\bar{c}. They incorporate the EPS09 shadowing parametrization with an inhomogeneous shadowing ratio proportional to the nuclear thickness function, a nuclear absorption cross section σ_abs = 3.1 mb, and two alternative transverse spatial distributions for hard partons in the nucleon: a uniform hard-sphere distribution and a dipole form derived from J/ψ photo-production data at HERA and FNAL. The number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions is recalculated using a normalized overlap function that convolves the nucleon hard parton transverse distributions.

## Key numbers
- Charm quark mass m_c = 1.2 GeV; center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair √s = 200 GeV (RHIC energies).
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section σ_nn^in = 42 mb at RHIC energies, 72 mb at LHC energies.
- Absorption cross section σ_abs = 3.1 mb, obtained from a global χ² analysis with PHENIX data.
- Mass parameter for the dipole distribution m_g² ∼ 1.1 GeV².
- Average number of inelastic n+n collisions at RHIC energies ⟨N_coll(b)⟩ ∼ 1654 for all methods considered.
- Ratios of N_coll^(nps) to N_coll are less than 1 for d+Au impact parameters b_dAu > 6 fm (peripheral collisions).

## Context
This work extends the GMC approach of Miller et al. (2007) and earlier studies of cold nuclear matter effects in d+Au collisions at RHIC, specifically the treatment of shadowing via the EPS09 parametrization of Eskola, Paukkunen, and Salgado (2009). It addresses the limited experimental knowledge of the transverse spatial distribution of partons in the nucleon, which has been studied via J/ψ photo-production data at HERA and FNAL. The results show that the nucleon hard parton distribution must be properly considered at LHC energies, where the bias in N_coll is predicted to be much larger than at RHIC energies.
