# Topological Susceptibility in the Superconductive Phases of Quantum Chromodynamics: a Dyson-Schwinger Perspective

> We test non-perturbative gluon propagators recently studied in the literature, by computing the topological susceptibility, $χ$, of the superconductive phases of Quantum Chromodynamics at high density. We formulate the problem within the High-Density Effective Theory, and use the 2-particle irreducible formalism to compute the effective potential of the dense phases. We focus on superconductive phases with two and three massless flavors. Within this formalism, we write a Dyson-Schwinger equation in the rainbow approximation for the anomalous part of the quark propagator in the superconductive phases, in which the non-perturbative gluon propagator plays its role. We complete the model by adding a $U(1)_A$-breaking term whose coupling is fixed perturbatively at large quark chemical potential. We then use the effective potential to compute $χ$ in the superconductive phases. We finally discuss implications of the results for the axion mass in superdense phases of Quantum Chromodynamics.

## Key claim

The topological susceptibility in color-superconducting phases of QCD is computed using non-perturbative gluon propagators within the High-Density Effective Theory, but the results depend on the coupling of the $U(1)_A$-breaking term which is fixed perturbatively only at asymptotically large chemical potential, leaving quantitative predictions conditional on the choice of this coupling at finite density.

## Method

The authors formulate the problem within the High-Density Effective Theory (HDET) and use the 2-particle-irreducible (2PI) Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis formalism to compute the effective potential of dense QCD phases. They write a Dyson-Schwinger equation in the rainbow approximation for the anomalous part of the quark propagator in color-superconducting phases with two and three massless flavors, incorporating non-perturbative gluon propagators obtained from the screened massive expansion. The model is completed by adding a $U(1)_A$-breaking term describing one-instanton exchange, whose coupling is fixed perturbatively at large quark chemical potential, and the topological susceptibility is extracted from the effective potential.

## Key numbers

* For the three-flavor model, the instanton-induced coupling is given by $\zeta = 6.106 \times 10^9 \times (1/g)^{10} e^{-8\pi^2/g^2} (1/g^2 \mu^2) (M/\mu)$, where $g$ is the QCD coupling, $\mu$ is the quark chemical potential, and $M$ is the degenerate quark mass.
* The gluon propagator parameters are fitted at $\mu = 400$ MeV, $500$ MeV, and $600$ MeV, with the transverse component at $\mu=400$ MeV having $Z^T = 1.06518$, $m_1^T = 1086.16$ MeV, $m_2^T = 301.016$ MeV, $m_3^T = 621.807$ MeV.
* At $\mu=400$ MeV, the longitudinal component has $Z^L = 0.994707$, $m_1^L = 1376.83$ MeV, $m_2^L = 345.026$ MeV, $m_3^L = 870.951$ MeV.
* The gluon mass parameter at $\mu=0$ is fixed to $m = 656$ MeV from pure Yang-Mills lattice data.

## Context

This work extends studies of topological susceptibility in QCD, which have been extensively performed in vacuum and at finite temperature using effective models (Zhang 2023, Ruggieri 2020, Gatto 2011), chiral perturbation theory (Grilli di Cortona 2015, Landini 2019), and lattice QCD (Borsanyi 2016, Bonati 2015), but remain scarce at finite chemical potential due to the sign problem. The authors test non-perturbative gluon propagators from Comitini (2024) that had previously only been used for the QCD phase diagram, and their results enable future calculations of the QCD axion mass in superdense phases by relating the topological susceptibility to the axion potential via the replacement $\theta \rightarrow a/f_a$.
