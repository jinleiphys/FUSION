# Partial and quasi dynamical symmetries in quantum many-body systems

> We introduce the notions of partial dynamical symmetry (PDS) and quasi dynamical symmetry (QDS) and demonstrate their relevance to nuclear spectroscopy, to quantum phase transitions and to mixed systems with regularity and chaos. The analysis serves to highlight the potential role of PDS and QDS towards understanding the emergent "simplicity out of complexity" exhibited by complex many-body systems.

## Key claim

Partial dynamical symmetries (PDS) and quasi dynamical symmetries (QDS) represent intermediate symmetry concepts where some, but not all, of the virtues of an exact dynamical symmetry are retained; these notions are relevant for understanding the emergent "simplicity out of complexity" in quantum many-body systems, particularly in nuclear spectroscopy, quantum phase transitions, and mixed regular-chaotic systems.

## Method

The paper employs the interacting boson model (IBM) with U(6) spectrum-generating algebra to construct Hamiltonians that exhibit PDS or QDS. For PDS, the author identifies n-boson annihilation operators that annihilate selected states in a given irreducible representation of a subalgebra (e.g., SU(3) or O(6)), then builds number-conserving normal-ordered interactions from these operators that preserve solvability for those states while breaking symmetry for others. For QDS, the author studies Hamiltonians that interpolate between two dynamical symmetry limits (e.g., U(5) and SU(3)) and examines the coherent mixing of symmetry irreps in eigenstates, quantified by Shannon entropies, quantum number fluctuations, and Pearson correlation coefficients between states of different angular momenta.

- For the SU(3)-PDS Hamiltonian applied to 168Er (N=16), the parameters used are h0=8, h2=4, C=13 keV (Fig. 2 caption).
- For O(6)-PDS type III in the ECQF Hamiltonian, the ground-state sigma fluctuations Delta_sigma_0 reach a saturation value of approximately 2.47 at the U(5) limit and approximately 1.25 at the SU(3) limit, while in the valley of enhanced purity they are two orders of magnitude lower (Fig. 4).
- For 160Gd (N=14) with ECQF parameters xi=0.84, chi=-0.53, the ground-state wave function contains more than 99% O(6) basis states with sigma=N (Fig. 5 and Table 1).

## Context

This work extends the concepts of dynamical symmetry (DS) introduced in the IBM by Arima and Iachello (1975) and the geometric model of Bohr and Mottelson (1975). It builds on prior formulations of PDS by Leviatan (1992, 2011) and QDS by Bahri, Rowe, Turner, and Rosensteel (2000-2005). The paper demonstrates that PDS and QDS provide a framework for understanding nuclear spectra in the interior of the ECQF symmetry triangle (Casten, 1983), where most realistic nuclei lie, and enables systematic identification of approximate symmetries in deformed rare-earth nuclei such as 160Gd, 162Dy, and 166Er.
