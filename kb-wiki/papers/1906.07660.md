# Sensitivity of one-neutron knockout to the nuclear structure of halo nuclei

> Background: Information about the structure of halo nuclei are often inferred from one-neutron knockout reactions. Typically the parallel-momentum distribution of the remaining core is measured after a high-energy collision of the exotic projectile with a light target. Purpose: We study how the structure of halo nuclei affects knockout observables considering an eikonal model of reaction. Method: To evaluate the sensitivity of both the diffractive and stripping parallel-momentum distributions to the structure of halo nuclei, we consider several descriptions of the projectile within a halo effective field theory. We consider the case of 11Be, the archetypical one-neutron halo nucleus, impinging on 12C at 68 MeV/nucleon, which are usual experimental conditions for such measurements. The low-energy constants of the description of 11Be are fitted to experimental data as well as to predictions of an ab initio nuclear-structure model. Results: One-neutron knockout reaction is confirmed to be purely peripheral, the parallel-momentum distribution of the remaining core is only sensitive to the asymptotics of the ground-state wavefunction and not to its norm. The presence of an excited state in the projectile spectrum reduces the amplitude of the breakup cross section; the corresponding probability flux is transferred to the inelastic-scattering channel. Although the presence of a resonance in the core-neutron continuum significantly affects the energy distribution, it has no impact on the parallel-momentum distribution. Conclusions: One-neutron knockout cross section can be used to infer information about the tail of the ground-state wavefunction, viz. its asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC). The independence of the parallel-momentum distribution on the continuum description makes the extraction of the ANC from this observable very reliable.

## Key claim

One-neutron knockout reactions are confirmed to be purely peripheral: the parallel-momentum distribution of the remaining core is sensitive only to the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) of the ground-state wavefunction, not to its norm (spectroscopic factor), and this independence from the continuum description makes ANC extraction from this observable very reliable.

## Method

The authors model the one-neutron halo nucleus 11Be as a two-body system (10Be core plus neutron) using a halo effective field theory (Halo-EFT) potential truncated at next-to-leading order, with low-energy constants fitted to experimental binding energies and to ab initio predictions of Calci et al. (2016). They simulate the inclusive breakup (diffractive and stripping) of 11Be on a 12C target at 68 MeV/nucleon within a Coulomb-corrected eikonal model, and systematically vary the ground-state wavefunction, the presence of an excited bound state, and the description of the continuum to test sensitivity.

## Key numbers

* The 11Be ground state (1/2+) has a binding energy of 0.504 MeV; the 1/2- excited bound state has a binding energy of 0.184 MeV.
* The single-particle ANC (SPANC) for the 1s1/2 ground state from ab initio calculations is b_{1s1/2} = 0.786 fm^{-1/2}; when scaled to a spectroscopic factor of 0.9, the SPANC becomes 0.829 fm^{-1/2}.
* The SPANC for the 0p1/2 excited state is b_{0p1/2} = 0.129 fm^{-1/2}.
* Including the 1/2- excited state reduces the diffractive-breakup parallel-momentum distribution peak amplitude by about 4.4% compared to the calculation without it.
* The total diffractive breakup cross section with both bound states is 122.8 mb; without the p1/2 interaction (plane waves) it is 126.1 mb, and the sum of breakup and inelastic cross sections remains nearly constant (126.4 mb vs. 126.1 mb).
* A d5/2 resonance at 1.27 MeV with width 98 keV (matching the experimental 5/2+ state) changes the total breakup cross section negligibly (from 122.8 mb with plane waves to 122.6 mb).

## Context

This work extends the peripheral-reaction analysis of Hansen (1996) and Bonaccorso and Hencken (2002), who showed stripping cross sections are proportional to the square of the SPANC, and confirms within a full eikonal framework that inclusive breakup is sensitive only to the wavefunction tail. The results enable reliable extraction of ANCs from knockout measurements, as done by Terry et al. (2002), and clarify that spectroscopic factors extracted from such data are unreliable. The study also confirms the flux-conservation effect observed by Moro et al. (2019) in Coulomb breakup, where the presence of an excited bound state transfers probability from breakup to inelastic scattering.
