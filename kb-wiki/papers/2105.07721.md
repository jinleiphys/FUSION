# Incompressibility and Symmetry Energy of Neutron Star

> We trace a systematic and consistent method to precisely numerate the magnitude range for various structural and isospin compositional properties of the neutron star. Incompressibility, symmetry energy, slope parameter and curvature of a neutron star are investigated using the relativistic energy density functional within the framework of coherent density fluctuation model. The analytical expression for the energy density functional of the neutron star matter is motivated from the Br$\ddot{u}$ckner functional and acquired by the polynomial fitting of the saturation curves for three different relativistic mean-field parameter sets (NL3, G3 and IU-FSU). The modified functional is folded with the neutron star's density-dependent weight function to calculate the numerical values for incompressibility and symmetry energy using the coherent density fluctuation model. NL3 parameter set, being the stiffest equation of state, endue us with a higher magnitude of all the properties compared to the other two parameter sets.

## Key claim

The authors claim to provide the first systematic calculation of incompressibility, symmetry energy, slope parameter, and curvature for neutron stars using the Coherent Density Fluctuation Model (CDFM) applied to relativistic mean-field equations of state, but the results depend strongly on which RMF parameter set is used, with the stiffest set (NL3) giving systematically higher values than the softer sets (G3 and IU-FSU).

## Method

The authors construct equations of state for neutron star matter (including neutrons, protons, electrons, and muons) using three relativistic mean-field parameter sets (NL3, G3, and IU-FSU). They fit the numerical RMF binding energy curves to an analytical energy density functional motivated by the Brückner functional, then use the Coherent Density Fluctuation Model to fold this functional with a density-dependent weight function derived from the neutron star's density profile obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations. The weight function is computed from the density-radius curve of the maximum-mass neutron star for each parameter set, and the nuclear properties are obtained by integrating the infinite-matter expressions weighted by this function.

## Key numbers

* Incompressibility of maximum-mass neutron star: 44.956 MeV (NL3), 29.480 MeV (G3), 29.827 MeV (IU-FSU)
* Symmetry energy of maximum-mass neutron star: 146.002 MeV (NL3), 66.813 MeV (G3), 60.758 MeV (IU-FSU)
* Slope parameter of maximum-mass neutron star: 615.854 MeV (NL3), 307.015 MeV (G3), 320.321 MeV (IU-FSU)
* Curvature of maximum-mass neutron star: -688.514 MeV (NL3), -360.127 MeV (G3), -228.012 MeV (IU-FSU)
* Incompressibility of canonical (1.4 solar mass) neutron star: 3.934 MeV (NL3), 3.590 MeV (G3), 3.389 MeV (IU-FSU)
* Symmetry energy of canonical neutron star: 15.911 MeV (NL3), 8.904 MeV (G3), 7.228 MeV (IU-FSU)
* Maximum neutron star masses: 2.850 solar masses (NL3), 2.004 solar masses (G3), 1.940 solar masses (IU-FSU)
* Symmetric nuclear matter incompressibility at saturation: 271.38 MeV (NL3), 243.96 MeV (G3), 231.31 MeV (IU-FSU)
* Symmetric nuclear matter symmetry energy at saturation: 37.43 MeV (NL3), 31.84 MeV (G3), 32.71 MeV (IU-FSU)

## Context

This work extends the Coherent Density Fluctuation Model, previously applied to finite nuclei by Antonov et al. (1980), to neutron star matter for the first time. The results contrast with the symmetric nuclear matter values at saturation density, showing that incompressibility decreases dramatically with increasing density and asymmetry. The G3 and IU-FSU parameter sets satisfy observational constraints from pulsar masses (PSR J1614-2230, PSR J0348+0432, PSR J0740+6620), NICER radius measurements, and the GW170817 tidal deformability constraint, while NL3 overpredicts the maximum mass. This formalism enables future systematic studies of the correlation between neutron star mass and incompressibility.
