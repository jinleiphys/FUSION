# Parity Violation in gamma proton Compton Scattering

> A measurement of parity-violating spin-dependent gamma proton Compton scattering will provide a theoretically clean determination of the parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling constant $h_{πNN}^{(1)}$. We calculate the leading parity-violating amplitude arising from one-loop pion graphs in chiral perturbation theory. An asymmetry of ~5 10^{-8} is estimated for Compton scattering of 100 MeV photons.

## Key claim

A measurement of parity-violating spin-dependent gamma-proton Compton scattering can provide a theoretically clean determination of the parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling constant \(h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}\), with the leading contribution arising from one-loop pion graphs in chiral perturbation theory; however, the asymmetry is estimated at only \(\sim 5 \times 10^{-8}\) for 100 MeV photons, and the extraction assumes that \(h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}\) is of its naively estimated size, with corrections suppressed by \(m_\pi/\Lambda_\chi\) and \(\omega/\Lambda_\chi\) at the \(\sim 15\%\) level for photon energies below pion photo-production threshold.

## Method

The authors computed the leading parity-violating amplitude for gamma-proton Compton scattering using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB\(\chi\)PT) at one-loop order, with a single insertion of the \(\Delta I=1\) parity-violating pion-nucleon interaction described by \(h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}\). They evaluated the one-loop pion graphs shown in Figure 1 of the paper, expressed the loop functions as integrals over two Feynman parameters, and derived the parity-violating asymmetry \(A^{\gamma\gamma}(\omega,\theta)\) from the difference in cross sections for proton spin parallel versus anti-parallel to the incident photon direction.

## Key numbers

- The estimated parity-violating asymmetry for 100 MeV photons at forward scattering (\(\theta=0\)) is \(A^{\gamma\gamma}(100,0) \sim 5 \times 10^{-8}\), assuming the naive value for \(h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}\).
- The asymmetry scales as \(A^{\gamma\gamma}(\omega,0) = 1.5 \times 10^{-9} \left( h_{\pi NN}^{(1)} / 5 \times 10^{-7} \right) \left( \omega / 20\ {\rm MeV} \right)^2 \tilde{\cal F}_1(\omega,0)\).
- The leading-order parity-violating amplitude is reproduced by a local operator with isovector coupling constant \(W_{\gamma\gamma}^{(1)} = e^2 g_A h_{\pi NN}^{(1)} / (12\pi^2 f m_\pi^2)\), where \(f = 132\ {\rm MeV}\) is the pion decay constant and \(g_A \sim +1.25\) is the axial coupling constant.
- The current experimental limit on the related forward-backward asymmetry in \(\overrightarrow n + p \rightarrow d + \gamma\) is \(A_\gamma = -(1.5 \pm 4.8) \times 10^{-8}\), with a proposed experiment aiming for \(\pm 5 \times 10^{-9}\) precision.

## Context

This work extends the program of extracting \(h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}\) from few-nucleon parity-violating observables, which has been complicated by nuclear structure uncertainties in measurements from light nuclei such as \(^{18}\)F (Barnes et al., 1978; Bini et al., 1985) and in the radiative capture reaction \(\overrightarrow n + p \rightarrow d + \gamma\) (Danilov, 1971; Kaplan, Savage, Springer, and Wise, 1999). The proposed Compton scattering measurement avoids the direct \(Z^0\)-exchange background that complicates electron-deuteron scattering measurements (Savage and Springer, 1998; Khriplovich and Korkin, 1999), and the authors note that high-intensity photon sources such as the FEL at Duke may enable a measurement despite the small \(\sim 10^{-8}\) asymmetry.
