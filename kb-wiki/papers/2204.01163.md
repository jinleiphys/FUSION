# Inclusive jet and hadron suppression in a multistage approach

> We present a new study of jet interactions in the quark-gluon plasma created in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, using a multistage event generator within the JETSCAPE framework. We focus on medium-induced modifications in the rate of inclusive jets and high transverse momentum (high-$p_{\mathrm{T}}$) hadrons. Scattering-induced jet energy loss is calculated in two stages: A high virtuality stage based on the MATTER model, in which scattering of highly virtual partons modifies the vacuum radiation pattern, and a second stage at lower jet virtuality based on the LBT model, in which leading partons gain and lose virtuality by scattering and radiation. Coherence effects that reduce the medium-induced emission rate in the MATTER phase are also included. The TRENTo model is used for initial conditions, and the (2+1)dimensional VISHNU model is used for viscous hydrodynamic evolution. Jet interactions with the medium are modeled via 2-to-2 scattering with Debye screened potentials, in which the recoiling partons are tracked, hadronized, and included in the jet clustering. Holes left in the medium are also tracked and subtracted to conserve transverse momentum. Calculations of the nuclear modification factor ($R_{\mathrm{AA}}$) for inclusive jets and high-$p_{\mathrm{T}}$ hadrons are compared to experimental measurements at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC). Within this framework, we find that with one extra parameter which codifies the transition between stages of jet modification -- along with the typical parameters such as the coupling in the medium, the start and stop criteria etc. -- we can describe these data at all energies for central and semicentral collisions without a rescaling of the jet transport coefficient $\hat{q}$.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a multistage jet energy-loss framework within JETSCAPE, with a single additional parameter governing the transition between high- and low-virtuality stages (along with standard parameters such as medium coupling and start/stop criteria), can simultaneously describe the nuclear modification factor R_AA for inclusive jets and high-pT hadrons at both RHIC and LHC energies for central and semicentral collisions without requiring a rescaling of the jet transport coefficient q-hat across collision energies.

## Method

The simulation uses a two-stage parton energy-loss approach: the MATTER model handles high-virtuality partons where medium scattering modifies the vacuum radiation pattern, and the LBT model handles lower-virtuality partons where leading partons gain and lose virtuality through scattering and radiation. The TRENTo model provides initial conditions, (2+1)D VISHNU viscous hydrodynamics evolves the bulk medium, and jet-medium interactions are modeled via 2-to-2 scattering with Debye screened potentials, with recoiling partons tracked, hadronized, and included in jet clustering while holes in the medium are subtracted to conserve transverse momentum. The switching virtuality Q_sw between stages is treated as a tunable parameter.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear modification factor R_AA is calculated for inclusive jets and charged hadrons at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV (LHC) and sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (RHIC), compared to data from ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, PHENIX, and STAR experiments.
- Jet-medium interactions begin at proper time tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c, with the energy-loss termination temperature T_c = 160 MeV (varied between 150 MeV and 170 MeV for uncertainty quantification).
- The hydrodynamic evolution starts at tau_hydro = 1.2 fm/c at LHC and 0.5 fm/c at top RHIC energy.
- The Cooper-Frye particlization temperature is T_sw = 151 MeV.
- The medium-generated virtuality scale is given by Q^2_Med = integral of q-hat over the parton lifetime, approximately q-hat * tau.
- The switching time between stages is estimated as tau_sw approximately sqrt(2E/q-hat).

## Context

This work extends the JETSCAPE framework's multistage approach (Putschke 2019, JETSCAPE 2020, JETSCAPE 2021) to simultaneously describe inclusive jet and hadron suppression across multiple collision energies and centralities. It builds on earlier JET Collaboration findings (JET 2013) that required a reduction in q-hat normalization from RHIC to LHC, and on prior Bayesian parameter estimation of q-hat (JETSCAPE 2021). The results enable future Bayesian parameter estimation over a wider range of observables and parameters, and demonstrate that a single transition scale between energy-loss stages can unify the description of jet quenching across energies without energy-dependent rescaling of the transport coefficient.
