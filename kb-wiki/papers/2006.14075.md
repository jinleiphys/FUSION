# Higgs modulation of emergent mass as revealed in kaon and pion parton distributions

> Strangeness was discovered roughly seventy years ago, lodged in a particle now known as the kaon, $K$. Kindred to the pion, $π$; both states are massless in the absence of Higgs-boson couplings. Kaons and pions are Nature's most fundamental Nambu-Goldstone modes. Their properties are largely determined by the mechanisms responsible for emergent mass in the standard model, but modulations applied by the Higgs are crucial to Universe evolution. Despite their importance, little is known empirically about $K$ and $π$ structure. This study delivers the first parameter-free predictions for all $K$ distribution functions (DFs) and comparisons with the analogous $π$ distributions, i.e. the one-dimensional maps that reveal how the light-front momentum of these states is shared amongst the gluons and quarks from which they are formed. The results should stimulate improved analyses of existing data and motivate new experiments sensitive to all $K$ and $π$ DFs.

## Key claim

This study delivers the first parameter-free predictions for all kaon distribution functions (valence, glue, and sea) and comparisons with analogous pion distributions, based on the existence of a process-independent effective charge and a factorised form for hadron light-front wave functions; the results reveal that Higgs-induced quark mass splittings introduce differences between kaon and pion distributions on the domain of valence-quark dominance, while glue and sea-quark distributions remain similar.

## Method

The authors compute kaon and pion parton distribution functions (DFs) by first determining the meson's two-particle light-front wave function from a Poincaré-covariant Bethe-Salpeter wave function, then using a factorised expression relating the valence-quark DF to the square of the distribution amplitude (DA) at the hadronic scale ζ_H = 0.331 GeV. The DAs are obtained from previously calculated moments, and the DFs are evolved from ζ_H to the experimental scale ζ_5 = 5.2 GeV using one-loop DGLAP equations integrated with a process-independent effective charge, with mass-dependent splitting functions introduced to account for the heavier strange quark.

## Key numbers

- The hadronic scale is identified as ζ_H = m_G = 0.331(2) GeV, defined by the screening mass from the process-independent effective charge.
- At the hadronic scale, the pion DA moments are ⟨[ξ, ξ²]⟩_π^{u_{ζ_H}} = [0, 0.25]; the kaon DA moments are ⟨[ξ, ξ²]⟩_K^{u_{ζ_H}} = [0.035(5), 0.24(1)].
- At ζ_5 = 5.2 GeV, the kaon's valence-quark momentum fraction is ⟨x[u^K(x;ζ_5) + \bar{s}^K(x;ζ_5)]⟩ = 0.42(3) (from Table B: u + \bar{s}_m).
- The kaon's valence-quark DF at ζ_5 behaves as (1-x)^{β} with β(ζ_5) = 2.73(7), consistent with QCD predictions.
- The ratio u^K(x;ζ_5)/u^π(x;ζ_5) shows best agreement with data from Badier (1980) using the middle parameter set, with χ²/degree-of-freedom = 0.86.
- The strange quark mass function at zero momentum is M_s(0) = 0.55 GeV, compared to M_u(0) = 0.46 GeV; the ratio M_s(0)/M_u(0) = 1.20 agrees with f_K/f_π = 1.19.

## Context

This work extends the framework developed by Chang (2013) for pion DAs and by Ding (2019) for pion DFs using all-orders evolution, now applied to the kaon for the first time. The predictions contradict the harder kaon DFs obtained from lattice QCD by Lin (2020), which show systematically larger low-order moments, especially for the anti-strange quark. The results enable improved analyses of existing Drell-Yan data from Badier (1980) and motivate new experiments sensitive to all kaon and pion distribution functions, such as those proposed by Keppel (2015) and Denisov (2018).
