# Nucleon strange electromagnetic form factors

> The role of the strange quarks on the low-energy interactions of the proton can be probed through the strange electromagnetic form factors. Knowledge of these form factors provides essential input for parity-violating processes and contributes to the understanding of the sea quark dynamics. We determine the strange electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon within the lattice formulation of Quantum Chromodynamics using simulations that include light, strange and charm quarks in the sea all tuned to their physical mass values. We employ state-of-the-art techniques to accurately extract the form factors for values of the momentum transfer square up to 0.8~GeV$^2$. We find that both the electric and magnetic form factors are statistically non-zero. We obtain for the strange magnetic moment $μ^s=-0.017(4)$, the strange magnetic radius $\langle r^2_M \rangle^s=-0.015(9)$~fm$^2$, and the strange charge radius $\langle r^2_E \rangle^s=-0.0048(6)$~fm$^2$.

## Key claim

The strange electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon are statistically non-zero at the physical point, determined directly from lattice QCD simulations with physical light quark masses rather than relying on chiral extrapolations from heavier pion masses.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with \(N_f=2+1+1\) twisted mass fermions including a clover term, using an ensemble with physical pion mass \(m_\pi=0.1393(7)\) MeV, lattice spacing \(a=0.0801(4)\) fm, and volume \(64^3 \times 128\). Strange quark loops were computed using stochastic methods with hierarchical probing and full dilution in spin and color, combined with the one-end trick, and correlated with 200 nucleon two-point functions per configuration across 750 gauge configurations for a total of 600,000 measurements. The form factors were extracted using plateau, two-state, and summation methods, and fitted with a model-independent z-expansion to obtain the magnetic moment and radii.

## Key numbers

- Strange magnetic moment: \(\mu^s = -0.017(4)\) (statistical error only)
- Strange magnetic radius: \(\langle r^2_M \rangle^s = -0.015(9)\) fm\(^2\)
- Strange charge radius: \(\langle r^2_E \rangle^s = -0.0048(6)\) fm\(^2\)
- Lattice spacing: \(a = 0.0801(4)\) fm determined from the nucleon mass
- Pion mass: \(m_\pi = 0.1393(7)\) MeV
- Renormalization constant: \(Z_V = 0.728(1)(4)\) (statistical and systematic errors)
- Maximum momentum transfer squared explored: up to 0.8 GeV\(^2\)

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations by the same collaboration using an \(N_f=2\) ensemble with near-physical pion mass (Alexandrou 2018), achieving approximately four times higher accuracy through improved loop computation methods. It provides the first direct lattice determination at the physical point without chiral extrapolation, in contrast to prior work by the \(\chi\)QCD collaboration (Sufian 2016), LHPC (Green 2015), and Djukanovic (2019) which used ensembles with heavier-than-physical pions and extrapolated. The results provide stringent constraints for ongoing parity-violating electron scattering experiments such as Q-weak and the future MESA facility at Mainz, and can aid in separating Standard Model contributions from potential new physics signals.
