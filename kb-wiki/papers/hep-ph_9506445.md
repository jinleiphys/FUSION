# Quark and Gluon Propagators from Meson Data

> We report robust calculations of various low energy QCD hadronic properties. We use a multi-rank separable expansion for the gluon propagator which greatly facilitates the numerical computations within the Global Colour Model for QCD. The parameters for the propagators are determined by fitting experimental values for $f_π$ and the $π$ and $a_1$ meson masses.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a multi-rank separable expansion of the gluon propagator, with parameters fitted to only three experimental observables (the pion decay constant and the pion and a1 meson masses), yields a universal effective gluon propagator that successfully predicts a wide range of other low-energy hadronic properties within the Global Colour Model (GCM) approximation to QCD.

## Method

The authors use the Global Colour Model (GCM) truncation of QCD, retaining only the gluon two-point function and neglecting higher-order n-point functions. They employ a multi-rank separable expansion of the gluon propagator in O(4) hyperspherical harmonics, which reduces the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSE) and Bethe-Salpeter equations (BSE) to algebraic form. Rather than parametrizing the gluon propagator directly, they parametrize the chiral-limit quark propagator with entire functions to ensure confinement, then use the DSE in an inverse manner to compute the gluon propagator parameters, fitting to the pion decay constant fπ and the π and a1 meson masses.

## Context

This work extends the Global Colour Model approach of Cahill and Roberts (1985) by introducing a robust multi-rank separable expansion technique for the gluon propagator, which greatly simplifies numerical computations of Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations. The results are consistent with and complementary to contemporaneous work by Frank and Roberts (1995) and Burden et al. (1995), who also used separable expansion techniques for model gluon propagators fitted to meson data. The reconstructed translation-invariant gluon propagator can be compared with direct QCD computations of the gluon propagator (Pennington and others), and the predicted hadronic observables provide a benchmark for future lattice QCD calculations and more complete GCM computations that include meson dressing of the nucleon core.
