# Comparison of model predictions for elliptic flow with experiment for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt{sNN}=2.76 TeV

> A simple kinematic model based on the superposition of p+p collisions, relativistic geometry and hadronic rescattering is used to predict the elliptic flow observable in sqrt{sNN} = 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions. A short proper time for hadronization is assumed. The predictions are compared with recent experimental measurements of elliptic flow which have been made for this colliding system and energy. It is found that the model predictions do a reasonable job in describing the experimental results, suggesting that the parton phase in these collisions may be short-lived.

## Key claim

A simple kinematic model using only hadronic degrees of freedom (no parton phase) with a short hadronization proper time of 0.1 fm/c does a reasonably good job describing the main features of ALICE elliptic flow measurements for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, suggesting the parton phase in these collisions may be short-lived. The model under-predicts the integrated V2 measurements by approximately 14% on average.

## Method

The model superposes PYTHIA-generated p+p collisions within the collision geometry of the Pb nuclei, assumes all particles hadronize at a common proper time tau=0.1 fm/c from a thin uniform disk of radius 1 fm, and then simulates hadronic rescattering using a semi-classical Monte Carlo calculation with isospin-averaged cross sections for pions, kaons, nucleons, lambdas, and resonances (rho, omega, eta, eta', phi, Delta, K*). Elliptic flow V2 is calculated from the final-state particle momenta using the reaction plane (model x-z plane) and compared with ALICE experimental V2{4} values from the 4-particle cumulant method to minimize non-flow correlations.

## Key numbers

- Model under-predicts integrated V2 (0.2 < pT < 5.0 GeV/c) by approximately 14% on average compared to ALICE measurements for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV
- Hadronization proper time tau set to 0.1 fm/c, same value used in previous model studies of RHIC Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=0.20 TeV
- Model used 3200 event minimum bias run for sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions
- Lower multiplicity cut rejected the lowest approximately 20% of p+p events to compensate for no re-interaction of primary nucleons
- Rescattering calculation carried out to 400 fm/c with time steps of Delta t=0.5 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the author's previous kinematic model studies that described hadronic observables in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=0.20 TeV from RHIC (Humanic, 2008) and Tevatron p+pbar collisions (Humanic, 2006). The model predictions are compared with recent ALICE experimental measurements (Aamodt, 2010) for Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies. The model reproduces the "high-pT flattening" feature of V2 that pure hydrodynamic models based on Quark Matter formation (Kestin, 2009) cannot reproduce, suggesting that hadronic rescattering plays an important role in determining elliptic flow properties and that the parton phase may be short-lived.
