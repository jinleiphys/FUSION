# Baryon Masses and Axial Couplings in the Combined 1/Nc and Chiral Expansions

> The effective theory for baryons with combined 1/Nc and chiral expansions is analyzed for non-strange baryons. Results for baryon masses and axial couplings are obtained in the small scale expansion, to be coined as the ξ-expansion, in which the 1/Nc and the low energy power countings are linked according to 1/Nc=O(ξ)=O(p). Masses and axial couplings are analyzed to O(ξ^3) and O(ξ^2) respectively, which correspond to next-to-next to leading order evaluations, and require one-loop contributions in the effective theory. The role of the spin-flavor approximate symmetry in baryons, consequence of the large Nc limit, is manifested in the physical world with Nc=3 in a significant way, as the analysis of its breaking in the masses and the axial couplings shows. Applications to the recent lattice QCD results on baryon masses and the nucleon's axial coupling are presented. It is shown that those results are naturally described within the effective theory at the order considered in the ξ-expansion.

## Key claim

The paper shows that baryon masses and axial couplings can be consistently described to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) using a combined expansion where the \(1/N_c\) and chiral expansions are linked by counting \(1/N_c = O(\xi) = O(p)\), but only if the spin-3/2 baryons are included and the large-\(N_c\) consistency cancellations between loop diagrams are respected.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory for non-strange baryons using Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) combined with the \(1/N_c\) expansion, where the baryon mass splitting (e.g., \(\Delta\)-\(N\)) is taken to be of order \(p\) in the chiral expansion, this linked counting is called the \(\xi\)-expansion. They evaluate baryon masses to \(O(\xi^3)\) and axial couplings to \(O(\xi^2)\), which requires calculating one-loop diagrams with intermediate baryons (including the \(\Delta\)) and renormalizing the ultraviolet divergences with appropriate counter-terms. The spin-flavor algebra of the contracted \(SU(4)\) symmetry is used to organize the operators and to verify that the large-\(N_c\) consistency conditions (cancellations between diagrams) are satisfied.

## Key numbers

* The \(\Delta\)-\(N\) mass splitting in the real world (\(N_c=3\)) is about 300 MeV, which motivates counting it as \(O(p)\) in the \(\xi\)-expansion.
* The pion decay constant is \(F_\pi = 92.4\) MeV, used as the scale for pion fields in the effective Lagrangian.
* The nucleon axial coupling in the physical world is \(g_A = 1.2701 \pm 0.0025\) (from Beringer (2012)), which defines the normalization of the axial couplings in the paper.
* Baryon masses at leading order scale as \(O(N_c)\), with the hyperfine splitting term proportional to \(C_{HF} S(S+1)/N_c\) and a sigma-term contribution \(c_1 N_c M_\pi^2\).
* The one-loop correction to baryon masses contributes at \(O(\xi^2)\) and \(O(\xi^3)\), with the leading ultraviolet divergence proportional to \(M_\pi^2 N_c\) in the wave-function renormalization.
* The difference \(\sigma_\Delta - \sigma_N\) (sigma-terms) is \(O(1/N_c^2)\) in both the large-\(N_c\) limit and the \(\xi\)-expansion.

## Context

This work extends the combined \(1/N_c\) and chiral expansion framework originally developed by Jenkins (1995) and Flores-Mendieta (1998, 2006), by providing a detailed renormalization procedure and explicit one-loop calculations for masses and axial couplings in the \(\xi\)-expansion (small scale expansion). The results enable direct comparison with lattice QCD data for baryon masses and the nucleon axial coupling \(g_A\) from groups such as Durr (2008), Walker-Loud (2008), and Alexandrou (2011), and can help determine the range of validity of the effective theory in quark mass and test the role of the spin-3/2 baryons.
