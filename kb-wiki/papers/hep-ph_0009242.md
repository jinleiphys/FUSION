# On the number of colours in Quantum Chromodynamics

> It is commonly believed that $π^0 \to 2 γ$ decay shows that there are three colours in Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). It is shown here that this is not correct. When correct colour dependent charges of the quarks are considered then it is shown that this decay does not make any statement about the number of colours in QCD.

# On the number of colours in Quantum Chromodynamics

## Key claim
The commonly held belief that the decay rate of π⁰ → 2γ proves there are three colours in QCD is incorrect. When the colour-dependent electric charges of quarks (as derived from the Standard Model) are used instead of static charges of 2/3 and -1/3, the decay rate agrees with experiment for any number of colours, not just N_c = 3.

## Method
The author re-examines the standard anomaly-based formula for the π⁰ → 2γ decay rate, which contains the factor N_c² (Q_u² − Q_d²)². Instead of using the canonical static quark charges Q_u = 2/3 and Q_d = −1/3, the author substitutes colour-dependent charges derived from the SU(N_c) ⊗ SU(2)_L × U(1)_Y Standard Model spontaneously broken to SU(N_c) ⊗ U(1)_em: Q_u = ½(1 + 1/N_c) and Q_d = ½(−1 + 1/N_c). The resulting factor is shown to equal 1 for any N_c, making the decay rate independent of the number of colours.

## Key numbers
- The experimental decay rate of π⁰ → 2γ is approximately 7.48 eV.
- With static charges (Q_u = 2/3, Q_d = −1/3) and N_c = 1, the formula gives a decay rate of 0.84 eV, which disagrees with experiment.
- With static charges and N_c = 3, the factor N_c² (Q_u² − Q_d²)² equals 1, yielding agreement with experiment.
- With the author's colour-dependent charges, the same factor equals 1 for any N_c, so the decay rate always matches experiment.
- The pion decay constant F_π is taken as 91 MeV.

## Context
This paper challenges the standard textbook argument (found in works such as Muta (1987), Cheng and Li (1989), Close (1979), and Dodd (1984)) that π⁰ → 2γ decay provides a clean proof of three colours in QCD. The author extends his own prior work (Abbas, 1990; Abbas, 1990) on colour-dependent quark electric charges to show that this decay is actually insensitive to the number of colours. The result implies that the number of colours must be determined by other experimental methods, such as the R-ratio in e⁺e⁻ → hadrons, and that the large-N_c limit of QCD remains a valid and important theoretical tool.
