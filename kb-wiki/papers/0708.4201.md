# A sticky business: the status of the conjectured viscosity/entropy density bound

> There have been a number of forms of a conjecture that there is a universal lower bound on the ratio, eta/s, of the shear viscosity, eta, to entropy density, s, with several different domains of validity. We examine the various forms of the conjecture. We argue that a number of variants of the conjecture are not viable due to the existence of theoretically consistent counterexamples. We also note that much of the evidence in favor of a bound does not apply to the variants which have not yet been ruled out.

## Key claim

The paper argues that many variants of the conjectured universal lower bound on the ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density (η/s ≥ ħ/(4πkB)) are not viable due to theoretically consistent counterexamples, and that much of the evidence in favor of a bound does not apply to the variants which have not yet been ruled out.

## Method

The authors classify the various forms of the KSS conjecture by two criteria: the class of underlying theories (from any quantum mechanical system to sensible quantum field theories) and the stability class of fluids (absolutely stable versus metastable). They then examine each variant by constructing theoretical counterexamples, including a nonrelativistic fluid with many degenerate species that violates the bound through Gibbs mixing entropy, and a heavy meson system based on a UV-complete quantum field theory.

## Key numbers

- The conjectured bound is η/s ≥ ħ/(4πkB), which in natural units (ħ = kB = 1) becomes η/s ≥ 1/(4π)
- For a dilute gas, kinetic theory gives η/s ∼ pT/(nσ), where pT is thermal momentum, n is density, and σ is scattering cross-section
- The density at which mean-free path equals thermal wavelength occurs when η/s ∼ 1 (order unity)
- For QCD with Nf flavors and Nc colors, asymptotic freedom requires 11Nc > 2Nf; increasing Nf to violate the bound pushes the theory outside the domain of sensible theories
- In a pion gas with large Nf and Nc scaled together to maintain asymptotic freedom, η/s ∼ Nf²/log(Nf) for large Nf, driving the ratio to infinity

## Context

This work extends the critical examination of the KSS bound originally proposed by Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2005) for theories with super-gravity duals. The authors build on earlier counterexample constructions by Cohen (2007) and by Spain (2007) showing that nonrelativistic fluids with many species can violate the bound. The paper enables a clearer understanding of which formulations of the conjecture remain viable, and suggests that if the bound is correct, it would require physics beyond quantum mechanics and quantum field theory, possibly related to quantum gravity as suggested by Bekenstein (2003).
