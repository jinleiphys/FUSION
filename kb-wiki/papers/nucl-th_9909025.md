# A light-front description of electromagnetic form factors for $J \leq {3/2}$ hadrons

> A review of the hadron electromagnetic form factors obtained in a light-front constituent quark model, based on the eigenfunctions of a mass operator, is presented. The relevance of different components in the q-q interaction for the description of hadron experimental form factors is analysed.

# A light-front description of electromagnetic form factors for $J \leq {3/2}$ hadrons

## Key claim

The light-front constituent quark model, using eigenfunctions of a mass operator with the Capstick-Isgur potential, can reproduce experimental elastic and transition electromagnetic form factors for hadrons with spin up to 3/2, but the quality of agreement depends on including both the spin-dependent part of the quark-quark interaction and effective constituent quark form factors; the ratio $G_E^p \mu_p / G_M^p$ can be reproduced even with pointlike constituent quarks, indicating it is directly linked to the nucleon wave function structure rather than to quark substructure.

## Method

The authors solved a mass operator with the Capstick-Isgur quark-quark potential, which includes a linear confining term and an effective one-gluon-exchange term with central Coulomb-like, spin-dependent, and tensor parts, to obtain baryon eigenstates. They evaluated elastic and transition electromagnetic form factors using the plus component of the electromagnetic current as a sum of one-body constituent quark currents with phenomenological Dirac and Pauli form factors, in the light-front dynamics framework where $q^+ = 0$.

## Key numbers

- The $D$-wave component probability in the $\Delta$ eigenstate is $P_D^{\Delta} = 1.1\%$, showing the tensor part of the quark-quark interaction has a small effect on $G_M^{N-\Delta}$.
- The ratio $G_M^{N-\Delta}(Q^2) / (G_M^p(Q^2) - G_M^n(Q^2))$ is largely insensitive to constituent quark form factors but sharply affected by the spin-dependent part of the Capstick-Isgur potential.
- The faster-than-dipole fall-off of $G_M^{N-\Delta}(Q^2)$ requires the spin-dependent part of the quark-quark interaction responsible for the $N-\Delta$ mass splitting and the different high-momentum tails of the nucleon and $\Delta$ wave functions.

## Context

This work extends the light-front constituent quark model approach of Cardarelli et al. (1990s) to a systematic review of form factors for hadrons with $J \leq 3/2$, updating the fit of constituent quark form factors to incorporate the most recent data for the ratio $G_{Ep}\mu_p/G_{Mp}$ from Perdrisat (1999). The results are compared with non-relativistic constituent quark model calculations by Aiello (1990s) and with experimental data from the Particle Data Group (1996) and Burkert (1990s). The finding that the spin-dependent part of the interaction is essential for the $N-\Delta$ transition form factor fall-off parallels results obtained with Goldstone-boson exchange interactions by Glozman (1990s), suggesting the hadron spectrum itself dictates the high-momentum behavior in wave functions.
