# Nucleon Form Factors and Distribution Amplitudes in QCD

> We derive light-cone sum rules for the electromagnetic nucleon form factors including the next-to-leading-order corrections for the contribution of twist-three and twist-four operators and a consistent treatment of the nucleon mass corrections. The essence of this approach is that soft Feynman contributions are calculated in terms of small transverse distance quantities using dispersion relations and duality. The form factors are thus expressed in terms of nucleon wave functions at small transverse separations, called distribution amplitudes, without any additional parameters. The distribution amplitudes, therefore, can be extracted from the comparison with the experimental data on form factors and compared to the results of lattice QCD simulations. A selfconsistent picture emerges, with the three valence quarks carrying 40%:30%:30% of the proton momentum.

## Key claim

The paper derives light-cone sum rules (LCSRs) for nucleon electromagnetic form factors that include next-to-leading-order (NLO) QCD corrections for twist-three and twist-four operators, along with a consistent treatment of nucleon mass corrections, enabling a direct relation between form factors and nucleon distribution amplitudes (DAs) without additional nonperturbative parameters. The approach assumes that soft contributions dominate at experimentally accessible momentum transfers, with the collinear factorization regime approached very slowly due to suppression by powers of alpha_s/pi.

## Method

The authors compute the correlation function of the Ioffe interpolating current with the electromagnetic current between the vacuum and a nucleon state, using the operator product expansion (OPE) on the light cone. They match this QCD calculation to a dispersion representation with a nucleon pole and continuum, applying Borel transformation and quark-hadron duality with a continuum threshold s_0 to obtain sum rules for the Dirac form factor F_1 and Pauli form factor F_2. The NLO corrections are calculated for contributions of twist-three and twist-four operators, and the Wandzura-Wilczek-type contributions to higher-twist DAs are derived systematically.

## Key numbers

* The three valence quarks carry 40%:30%:30% of the proton momentum, emerging as a self-consistent picture from the analysis.
* The continuum threshold s_0 is approximately (1.5 GeV)^2, used to separate the nucleon contribution from higher-mass states in the dispersion integrals.
* The normalization constant f_N scales with the strong coupling as f_N(mu) = f_N(mu_0) (alpha_s(mu)/alpha_s(mu_0))^{2/(3 beta_0)} where beta_0 = 11 - (2/3) n_f.
* The leading-twist DA shape parameters have anomalous dimensions: gamma_10 = 20/9, gamma_11 = 8/3, gamma_20 = 32/9, gamma_21 = 40/9, gamma_22 = 14/3.
* The twist-4 constants lambda^N_1 and lambda^N_2 scale as (alpha_s(mu)/alpha_s(mu_0))^{-2/beta_0} to one-loop accuracy.
* The twist-4 shape parameters have anomalous dimensions: gamma_10^{(eta)} = 20/9, gamma_11^{(eta)} = 4, gamma_10^{(xi)} = 10/3.

## Context

This work extends the LCSR approach for baryon form factors first formulated by Braun et al. (2001) and refined by Braun et al. (2006), adding NLO corrections that are standard in B-decay applications but were missing for nucleons. The calculation partially addresses the task begun by Passek-Kumericki et al. (2008) and provides a more systematic treatment of higher-twist contributions related to nucleon mass corrections. The results enable extraction of nucleon distribution amplitudes from experimental form factor data and comparison with lattice QCD simulations, as well as application to other baryon form factors and transitions such as N to gamma Delta and weak Lambda_b decays.
