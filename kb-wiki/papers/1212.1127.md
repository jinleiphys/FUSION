# Head shock vs Mach cone: azimuthal correlations from 2 to 3 parton processes in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We study the energy-momentum deposited by fast moving partons within a medium using linearized viscous hydrodynamics. The particle distribution produced by this energy-momentum is computed using the Cooper-Frye formalism. We show that for the conditions arising in heavy-ion collisions, energy momentum is preferentially deposited along the head shock of the fast moving partons. We also show that the double hump in the away-side of azimuthal correlations can be produced by two (instead of one) away-side partons that deposit their energy-momentum along their directions of motion. These partons are originated in the in-medium hard scattering in 2 to 3 processes. We compare the results of the analysis to azimuthal angular correlations from PHENIX and show that the calculation reproduces the data systematics of a decreasing away-side correlation when the momentum of the associated hadron becomes closer to the momentum of the leading hadron. This scenario seems to avoid the shortcomings of the Mach cone as the origin of the double-hump structure in the away-side

## Key claim

The double-hump structure observed in the away-side of azimuthal correlations in heavy-ion collisions can be explained by two away-side partons from 2→3 processes depositing energy-momentum preferentially along their directions of motion (head shock), rather than by a single parton exciting Mach cone sound modes, provided that the transverse (wake) modes dominate over longitudinal (sound) modes under conditions where the parton velocity is larger than the sound velocity squared divided by the parton velocity squared is less than one.

## Method

The authors used linearized viscous hydrodynamics to compute the energy-momentum deposited by fast moving partons in a medium, solving the hydrodynamic equations for transverse (wake) and longitudinal (sound) modes in momentum space. They computed the resulting particle distributions using the Cooper-Frye formalism with a Boltzmann equilibrium distribution expanded to linear order in the perturbations. For the azimuthal correlations, they generated 2→3 hard scattering parton events using MadGraph5, selected the highest-momentum parton as the leading hadron via collinear fragmentation with KKP fragmentation functions, and treated the other two partons as sources depositing energy-momentum into the medium over 6 fm with dE/dx = 2 GeV/fm.

## Key numbers

- Sound attenuation length: Γ_s = 1/(3πT_0), with T_0 = 350 MeV and η = 1/(4π)
- Sound velocity: c_s = 1/√3
- Parton speed: v = 0.9995c
- Average parton travel distance: 6 fm
- Energy loss per unit length: dE/dx = 2 GeV/fm
- Leading hadron momentum range: 3 GeV ≤ p_T ≤ 4 GeV
- Associated hadron momentum ranges: 0.4 GeV ≤ p_T ≤ 1 GeV, 1 GeV ≤ p_T ≤ 2 GeV, 2 GeV ≤ p_T ≤ 3 GeV, and 3 GeV ≤ p_T ≤ 4 GeV
- Hard scattering parton momentum window: 10 GeV ≤ p_T ≤ 12 GeV
- Correction factor for data comparison: f ≈ 30 (same for all bins)
- Angular difference between peaks in the away side: close to 2π/3 rad

## Context

This work extends the approach of Ayala et al. (2011, 2012) who computed hadron events from 2→3 parton processes for fragmentation outside the medium, now incorporating in-medium energy deposition via linearized viscous hydrodynamics. It contradicts the Mach cone explanation proposed by Casalderrey-Solana et al. (2005) and Renk et al. (2007), which was argued to be fragile by Casalderrey-Solana et al. (2006) and Torrieri et al. (2008), and shown unlikely by Bouras et al. (2012) for systems of finite size and viscosity relevant to heavy-ion collisions. The results enable a final-state interpretation of the away-side double-peak structure that avoids the shortcomings of the Mach cone scenario, and the calculation reproduces the PHENIX data systematics of a decreasing away-side correlation when the associated hadron momentum approaches the leading hadron momentum.
