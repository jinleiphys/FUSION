# Microscopic triaxial quadrupole-octupole collective Hamiltonian for low-energy nuclear excitations

> We present a microscopic triaxial quadrupole-octupole collective Hamiltonian (TQOCH) that unifies collective rotations, quadrupole-octupole vibrations, and their couplings to model low-lying nuclear states of both parities. The TQOCH's dynamics are governed by collective parameters derived from multidimensionally constrained covariant density functional theory. The model's validity is demonstrated through calculations of $^{152}$Sm, including its deformation energy surfaces, excitation spectra, and transition probabilities. As a predictive tool, the TQOCH probes complex nuclear phenomena like shape coexistence and phase transitions, directly connecting their microscopic origins to spectroscopic observables.

## Key claim

The paper presents a microscopic triaxial quadrupole-octupole collective Hamiltonian (TQOCH) that unifies collective rotations, quadrupole-octupole vibrations, and their couplings to model low-lying nuclear states of both parities, with the caveat that the model's validity is demonstrated only through calculations of a single nucleus, $^{152}$Sm, and the theoretical bands are generally overextended in energy due to the cranking approximation underestimating moments of inertia.

## Method

The TQOCH is defined using four deformation parameters ($\beta_{20}, \beta_{22}, \beta_{30}, \beta_{32}$) and three Euler angles as collective coordinates, with dynamics governed by a collective potential, ten mass parameters, and three moments of inertia derived from multidimensionally constrained covariant density functional theory (MDC-CDFT) using the PC-PK1 energy density functional and a separable pairing force in the BCS approximation. The collective Hamiltonian is diagonalized by expanding eigenfunctions in a complete basis of one-dimensional harmonic oscillator wave functions and symmetrized Wigner D-functions, yielding energy spectra and collective wave functions for each total angular momentum and parity. The model incorporates quadrupole-octupole couplings at both the static mean-field and dynamical levels, and self-consistently minimizes the 4D collective potential by including higher-order deformations.

## Context

This work extends the multidimensionally constrained covariant density functional theory (MDC-CDFT) framework developed by Lu et al. (2012) and Zhou et al. (2016) by incorporating shape fluctuations through a collective Hamiltonian, going beyond previous static mean-field calculations. The TQOCH improves upon the quadrupole-octupole collective Hamiltonian (QOCH) by Dobrowolski et al. (2016, 2018), which used a macroscopic-microscopic approach but lacked coupling between quadrupole and octupole degrees of freedom. The model enables future microscopic descriptions of shape coexistence and phase transitions in nuclei, and provides predictions for a triaxial octupole band in $^{152}$Sm that could guide experimental searches for tetrahedral deformation, following the recent claim by Basak et al. (2025) of a tetrahedral band in this nucleus.
