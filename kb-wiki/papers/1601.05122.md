# Superfluid fission dynamics with microscopic approaches

> Recent progresses in the description of the latter stage of nuclear fission are reported. Dynamical effects during the descent of the potential towards scission and in the formation of the fission fragments are studied with the time-dependent Hartree-Fock approach with dynamical pairing correlations at the BCS level. In particular, this approach is used to compute the final kinetic energy of the fission fragments. Comparison with experimental data on the fission of 258Fm are made.

## Key claim

The time-dependent Hartree-Fock approach with dynamical BCS pairing correlations can reproduce the experimental mass and total kinetic energy distributions in \(^{258}\)Fm fission, but only if the initial configuration is chosen in a specific range (\(210\) b \(< Q_{20} < 270\) b for the asymmetric mode) and pairing is treated dynamically rather than in the frozen occupation approximation.

## Method

The authors performed static Hartree-Fock+BCS calculations using the SLy4\(d\) Skyrme functional with surface pairing on a Cartesian grid (mesh size 0.8 fm) to obtain adiabatic potential energy surfaces for \(^{258}\)Fm and \(^{264}\)Fm. They then solved the time-dependent Hartree-Fock equations simultaneously with the time-dependent BCS equations for the single-particle occupation numbers, starting from configurations along the adiabatic fission path, to simulate the dynamical descent from the saddle to scission and beyond.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{258}\)Fm asymmetric fission, the final fragment properties were essentially independent of the initial condition when the initial quadrupole moment was chosen in the range \(210\) b \(< Q_{20} < 270\) b.
- The adiabatic potential for \(^{264}\)Fm shows the formation of spherical magic gaps at \(Z=50\) and \(N=82\) occurring at a fragment separation distance \(R \sim 10.5\) fm.
- The calculated total kinetic energy (TKE) for the symmetric fission mode of \(^{258}\)Fm corresponds to the high-TKE peak in the experimental distribution, while the asymmetric mode yields a much lower TKE, matching the low-energy tail in the experimental data from Hulet (1989).
- In \(^{264}\)Fm symmetric fission, the low-lying \(2^+\) and \(3^-\) vibrational modes in the \(^{132}\)Sn fragments are excited, but the giant quadrupole resonance (GQR) and high-energy octupole resonance (HEOR) are not populated.

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent Hartree-Fock studies of fission dynamics by Simenel (2014) and Scamps (2015) to include dynamical pairing correlations at the BCS level, which were previously treated only statically or neglected. The results contradict the adiabatic approximation of Moretto (1991) for the later stages of fission, showing that non-adiabatic effects near scission are crucial. The approach enables future incorporation of beyond-mean-field fluctuations via the time-dependent random phase approximation (TDRPA), which has already been applied to heavy-ion collisions by Balian and Vénéroni (1984) and others, but not yet to fission with pairing.
