# Where is the jet quenching in Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV?

> Because of the rapidly falling particle spectrum at large $p_T$ from jet fragmentation at the CERN SPS energy, the high-$p_T$ hadron distribution should be highly sensitive to parton energy loss inside a dense medium as predicted by recent perturbative QCD (pQCD) studies. A careful analysis of recent data from CERN SPS experiments via pQCD calculation shows little evidence of energy loss. This implies that either the life-time of the dense partonic matter is very short or one has to re-think about the problem of parton energy loss in dense matter. The hadronic matter does not seem to cause jet quenching in $Pb+Pb$ collisions at the CERN SPS. High-$p_T$ two particle correlation in the azimuthal angle is proposed to further clarify this issue.

# Where is the jet quenching in Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV?

## Key claim
Despite theoretical predictions of significant parton energy loss in dense matter formed in Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS energies, a pQCD analysis of high-pT hadron spectra shows little evidence of such energy loss. The absence of jet quenching implies either that the dense partonic matter has a very short lifetime (below approximately 2.2–2.9 fm/c) or that current models of parton energy loss in dense matter require revision.

## Method
The author performed pQCD parton model calculations of single-inclusive pion spectra in p+p, p+A, and A+A collisions, incorporating initial parton transverse momentum (kT) distributions, nuclear modification of parton distributions (EMC effect), and nuclear kT-broadening (Cronin effect) via a multiple scattering model. The calculated spectra were compared to WA80 data for S+S at 200 GeV and WA98 data for Pb+Pb at 158 GeV, with the kT-broadening parameter tuned to reproduce p+A data. The effect of parton energy loss was simulated by shifting the pT scale of the calculated Pb+Pb spectrum by 0.2 GeV/c.

## Key numbers
- The calculated π0 spectrum in central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV, when shifted by 0.2 GeV/c, deviates from the WA98 data; assuming 20% uncertainty in the calculated spectrum, a total energy loss ΔE < 0.1 GeV is excluded.
- The corresponding upper limit on the energy loss per unit length is dE/dx < 0.02 GeV/fm.
- The measured dET/dη ≈ 405 GeV in the central rapidity region of the most central Pb+Pb collisions (2% of total inelastic cross section) gives an initial energy density ε0 ≈ 2.9 GeV/fm³ at τ0 = 1 fm/c.
- Assuming critical energy density εc ≈ 1 GeV/fm³, the system lives above this density for approximately 2.2–2.9 fm/c.
- The kT-broadening parameter Δ² ranges from 0.064 to 0.129 GeV²/c² for Q = 2–3 GeV, consistent with J/Ψ production analyses in p+A collisions.

## Context
This work extends pQCD parton model calculations previously applied to p+p and p+A collisions (Feynman, Owens, Field) to A+A collisions at SPS energies, using the HIJING parameterization for nuclear parton distributions and a multiple scattering model for the Cronin effect (Wang, 1998). The absence of jet quenching contradicts theoretical predictions of parton energy loss in dense partonic matter (Gyulassy and Wang, 1994; Baier et al., 1997; Zakharov, 1996) and suggests that the dense matter at SPS is too short-lived for such energy loss to occur. The paper proposes azimuthal two-particle correlations (Wang, 1998) to distinguish jet fragmentation from hydrodynamic flow, and notes that if significant suppression of high-pT hadrons is observed at RHIC, it would indicate a dramatically different initial condition from the CERN SPS.
