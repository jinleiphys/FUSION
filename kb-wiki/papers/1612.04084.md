# Beta-decay properties of neutron-rich rare-earth isotopes

> In this paper, beta-decay properties of even-even neutron-rich isotopes in the rare-earth mass region are studied within a microscopic theoretical approach based on a proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation. The underlying mean field is constructed selfconsistently from a deformed Hartree-Fock calculation with Skyrme interactions and pairing correlations to which particle-hole and particle-particle residual interactions are added. Nuclei in this mass region participate in the astrophysical rapid neutron capture process and are directly involved in the generation of the rare-earth peak in the isotopic abundance pattern centered at A=160. The energy distributions of the Gamow-Teller strength as well as the beta-decay half-lives and the beta-delayed neutron-emission probabilities are discussed and compared with the available experimental information and with calculations based on different approaches.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a deformed Skyrme HF+BCS+QRPA approach with separable residual forces can reproduce the available experimental beta-decay half-lives of neutron-rich rare-earth isotopes, but the agreement is qualified by the fact that the same coupling strengths for the residual interactions are used for all nuclei without individual fitting, and the results fall within the spread of other theoretical approaches that can differ by as much as one order of magnitude depending on the nucleus.

## Method

The author performs deformed Hartree-Fock calculations with the Skyrme SLy4 interaction and BCS pairing to obtain selfconsistent equilibrium deformations for even-even neutron-rich isotopes of Xe, Ba, Ce, Nd, Sm, Gd, and Dy with neutron numbers between 86 and 126. A proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) with separable particle-hole and particle-particle residual interactions is then applied to compute Gamow-Teller strength distributions, from which beta-decay half-lives and beta-delayed neutron-emission probabilities are calculated by weighting the strength with phase-space factors and integrating over the Q-beta window.

## Key numbers

* Quadrupole deformations reach values close to beta_2 = 0.35 in the prolate sector and close to beta_2 = -0.25 in the oblate sector around mid-shell (N=104).
* Shape transitions from prolate to oblate are predicted between N=116 and N=118 in Sm, Gd, and Dy isotopes.
* The coupling strength for the particle-hole residual interaction is taken as chi^{ph}_{GT} = 0.15 MeV, and for the particle-particle channel as kappa^{pp}_{GT} = 0.03 MeV.
* A quenching factor of 0.77 is applied to the axial-vector coupling constant (g_A/g_V)_eff = 0.77 (g_A/g_V)_free, with D = 6200 s in the half-life formula.
* The various theoretical calculations for half-lives in this mass region differ by as much as one order of magnitude depending on the nucleus.

## Context

This work extends previous deformed QRPA calculations by the same author (Sarriguren, various years) on medium-mass nuclei from Ge to Pd into the rare-earth region (Xe through Dy, 86 < N < 126), which is crucial for understanding the rare-earth peak at A ~ 160 in the r-process abundance pattern. The results are compared with global calculations by Moller et al. (2003) combining QRPA with statistical gross theory, Fang et al. (2016) using Woods-Saxon potentials and realistic CD-Bonn forces, Mustonen et al. (2016) using the finite-amplitude method with Skyrme QRPA, and Marketin et al. (2016) using a spherical relativistic formalism. The paper enables future experimental measurements at facilities such as FAIR, RIBF-RIKEN, and FRIB-MSU by providing predictions for half-lives and GT strength distributions in nuclei at the borderline of present capabilities.
