# Vacuum-like jet fragmentation in a dense QCD medium

> We study the fragmentation of a jet propagating in a dense quark-gluon plasma. Using a leading, double-logarithmic approximation in perturbative QCD, we compute for the first time the effects of the medium on the vacuum-like emissions. We show that, due to the scatterings off the plasma, the in-medium parton showers differ from the vacuum ones in two crucial aspects: their phase-space is reduced and the first emission outside the medium can violate angular ordering. We compute the jet fragmentation function and find results in qualitative agreement with measurements at the LHC.

## Key claim

The authors show that, within a leading double-logarithmic approximation (DLA) of perturbative QCD, vacuum-like parton cascades inside a dense quark-gluon plasma can be factorized from medium-induced emissions, with the medium only modifying the allowed phase-space and allowing the first emission outside the medium to violate angular ordering, but only for sources with opening angles larger than a critical angle \(\theta_c\).

## Method

The authors consider a color-singlet quark-antiquark antenna with small opening angle \(\theta_{q\bar q}\) propagating through a dense QGP of length \(L\) with jet quenching parameter \(\hat q\). They compute the double-differential gluon distribution \(T(\omega,\theta) = \omega \theta^2 \, d^2N/(d\omega\,d\theta^2)\) in DLA, separating emissions into three stages: inside the medium, the first emission outside the medium, and subsequent vacuum-like emissions. The medium effects enter only via integration boundaries in the phase-space, and the vacuum-like splitting functions are used throughout. The fragmentation function \(D(\omega)\) is obtained by integrating over angles above the hadronisation scale \(\Lambda\).

## Key numbers

* The critical angle for color decoherence is \(\theta_c \equiv 2/\sqrt{\hat q L^3}\), which is also the emission angle for the hardest medium-induced emission with energy \(\omega_c = \hat q L^2/2\).
* The decoherence time for the antenna is \(t_{\rm coh}(\theta_{q\bar q}) = (4/(\hat q \theta_{q\bar q}^2))^{1/3}\).
* The condition for vacuum-like emissions inside the medium is \(\omega \gg (2\hat q/\theta^4)^{1/3} \equiv \omega_0(\theta)\).
* The upper energy limit for the first emission outside the medium is \(\omega_L(\theta) \equiv 2/(L\theta^2)\).
* For intermediate energies below \(\omega_c\), the fragmentation function shows a suppression of up to 15% relative to vacuum at small angles \(\theta \lesssim 0.1\theta_{q\bar q}\), and a strong enhancement at larger angles \(\theta > 0.2\theta_{q\bar q}\) and lower energies.
* The suppression at intermediate energies grows with both \(\hat q\) and \(L\); the logarithmic width of the excluded region is \(\ln(\omega_0/\omega_L) = (1/3)\ln(\theta^2/\theta_c^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS-Z formalism (Baier, 1997; Zakharov, 1997; Wiedemann, 2000) for medium-induced radiation by computing for the first time the effect of the medium on vacuum-like emissions in a controlled perturbative approximation. It contradicts heuristic models that assume vacuum showers develop first and then interact with the medium (Schenke, 2009; Casalderrey-Solana, 2014), or that use postulated medium-modified splitting functions (Wang, 2001; Majumder, 2009; Armesto, 2009). The results enable systematic refinements including energy conservation and energy loss, and can be implemented in Monte-Carlo event generators for comparison with LHC jet fragmentation and jet shape measurements (Chatrchyan, 2014; Aad, 2014).
