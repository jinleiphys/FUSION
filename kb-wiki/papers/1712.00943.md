# Secular Terms in Dyson Series to All-Orders of Perturbation

> In classical and quantum systems, perturbation of an evolution equation is often invalidated by secular terms which diverge at late times. The diverging behavior of evolution can be remedied by various techniques of resumma- tion such as renormalization group or multi-scale analysis. In this paper, we prove that, in a generic quantum mechanical system, secular terms can be systematically removed to all orders in the Dyson series by the method of improved (renormalized) perturbation. A recurrence relation to provide an explicit method to remove the secular terms is given. As a byproduct, we give a simple method to obtain energy eigenvalues and decay rates to all orders of perturbation.

## Key claim

The paper proves that secular terms in the Dyson series of a generic quantum mechanical system can be systematically removed to all orders of perturbation theory by renormalizing the unperturbed Hamiltonian, provided that the renormalized Hamiltonian commutes with the original unperturbed Hamiltonian and that the recurrence relations given in the paper are followed.

## Method

The authors introduce a modified interaction picture where the full Hamiltonian is decomposed as \(H = H_0(\lambda) + \lambda V(\lambda)\), with \(H_0(\lambda)\) containing all-order corrections. They derive a recurrence relation for the operators \(U_n(t)\) and the correction terms \(H_n(0)\) that eliminates secular terms order by order without performing complicated multiple time integrations. The method is then extended to the \(\lambda\)-dependent case by renormalizing the energy eigenvalues of \(H_0(\lambda)\).

## Key numbers

* The first-order correction to the unperturbed Hamiltonian is \(H_1(0) = V_0\), where \(V_0\) is the part of the potential that commutes with \(H_0\).
* The second-order correction is \(H_2(0) = -\sum_{a,b\neq 0} \frac{1}{\omega_a} \delta_{a+b} V_b V_a\), where \(\omega_a\) are differences of eigenvalues of \(H_0\) and \(\delta_{a+b}=1\) when \(\omega_a+\omega_b=0\).
* The third-order correction is \(H_3(0) = \sum_{a,b,c\neq 0} \frac{1-\delta_{a+b}}{\omega_a(\omega_a+\omega_b)} \delta_{a+b+c} V_c V_b V_a\).
* The fourth-order correction \(H_4(0)\) is given by \(H_4(0) = -i\sum_{a,b,c,d\neq 0} \frac{1-\delta_{a+b}-\delta_{a+b+c}}{\omega_a(\omega_a+\omega_b)(\omega_a+\omega_b+\omega_c)} \delta_{a+b+c+d} V_d V_c V_b V_a + \sum_{a,b\neq 0} \frac{1}{\omega_a^2} \delta_{a+b} V_b H_2 V_a\).

## Context

This work extends the renormalization group method for resumming secular terms, previously applied to classical systems and quantum field theories by Chen, Goldenfeld, and Oono (1996) and to de Sitter space by Burgess et al., to generic quantum mechanical systems to all orders. The method provides an explicit recurrence relation that avoids the complexity of multiple time integrations, and as a byproduct yields a simple perturbative algorithm for calculating energy eigenvalues and decay rates (higher-order generalizations of Fermi's Golden Rule) without requiring diagonalization of the full Hamiltonian.
