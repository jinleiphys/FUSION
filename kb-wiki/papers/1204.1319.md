# Prompt photon production and photon-hadron correlations at RHIC and the LHC from the Color Glass Condensate

> We investigate inclusive prompt photon and semi-inclusive prompt photon-hadron production in high energy proton-nucleus collisions using the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism which incorporates non-linear dynamics of gluon saturation at small x via Balitsky-Kovchegov equation with running coupling. For inclusive prompt photon production, we rewrite the cross-section in terms of direct and fragmentation contributions and show that the direct photon (and isolated prompt photon) production is more sensitive to gluon saturation effects. We then analyze azimuthal correlations in photon-hadron production in high energy proton-nucleus collisions and obtain a strong suppression of the away-side peak in photon-hadron correlations at forward rapidities, similar to the observed mono-jet production in deuteron-gold collisions at forward rapidity at RHIC. We make predictions for the nuclear modification factor R_{p(d)A} and photon-hadron azimuthal correlations in proton(deuteron)-nucleus collisions at RHIC and the LHC at various rapidities.

## Key claim
The authors claim that direct (and isolated) prompt photon production is more sensitive to gluon saturation effects than fragmentation photon production, and that gluon saturation leads to a strong suppression of the away-side peak in photon-hadron azimuthal correlations at forward rapidities in proton-nucleus collisions. This suppression is similar to the observed mono-jet production in deuteron-gold collisions at RHIC, but the authors note that the separation between direct and fragmentation contributions depends on the hard scale and involves a cutoff that introduces a parametrically small higher-order mismatch.

## Method
The authors computed inclusive prompt photon and semi-inclusive photon-hadron production cross sections in proton-nucleus collisions using the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism, with the dipole-target scattering amplitude evolved via the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) equation. They separated the inclusive prompt photon cross section into direct and fragmentation contributions by adding and subtracting a term in the momentum integral, then convoluted the partonic cross sections with parton distribution functions (MSTW 2008) and fragmentation functions (KKP for hadrons, leading-log parametrization for photons). The nuclear modification factor \(R_{p(d)A}\) was computed for minimum-bias collisions using initial saturation scales for protons (\(Q_{0p}^2 = 0.168\) or \(0.2\) GeV\(^2\)) and nuclei (\(Q_{0A}^2 = 3\)–\(4\, Q_{0p}^2\)) fitted to HERA and RHIC data.

## Key numbers
- Initial proton saturation scale: \(Q_{0p}^2 = 0.168\) GeV\(^2\) (set II) or \(0.2\) GeV\(^2\) (set I), with parameter \(\gamma = 1.119\) or \(1.0\) respectively, fitted to HERA data.
- Initial nuclear (gold) saturation scale: \(Q_{0A}^2 = 3\)–\(4\, Q_{0p}^2\), consistent with RHIC inclusive hadron data.
- Number of binary collisions \(N_{coll} = 3.6\) at \(\sqrt{s} = 0.2\) TeV, \(6.5\) at \(4.4\) TeV, and \(7.4\) at \(8.8\) TeV.
- The nuclear modification factor for direct photons at \(\eta = 3\) shows stronger suppression at LHC energies (\(\sqrt{S} = 4.4\) and \(8.8\) TeV) compared to RHIC (\(\sqrt{S} = 0.2\) TeV), persisting to higher transverse momentum.
- Fragmentation photon \(R_{p(d)A}^{\gamma}\) is larger than direct photon \(R_{p(d)A}^{\gamma}\) at RHIC, but both become suppressed at LHC energies.

## Context
This work extends previous CGC calculations of inclusive prompt photon production (Jalilian-Marian and Rezaeian, 2012, earlier work) and photon-hadron correlations (Jalilian-Marian and Rezaeian, 2012, earlier work) by explicitly separating direct and fragmentation contributions and providing detailed predictions for RHIC and LHC kinematics. It contrasts with alternative phenomenological approaches that combine nuclear shadowing, transverse momentum broadening, and cold matter energy loss (described in references within the paper) to describe RHIC data. The results enable experimental tests of gluon saturation via measurements of the nuclear modification factor \(R_{pA}^{\gamma}\) and photon-hadron azimuthal correlations at forward rapidities, which can help distinguish CGC dynamics from other models.
