# Poincaré Invariance, Cluster Properties, and Particle Production

## Key claim
The paper presents a method for constructing Poincaré invariant quantum mechanical models with a finite number of degrees of freedom that satisfy cluster separability and the spectral condition while allowing particle number non-conservation, but only for reactions where a conserved set of "atomic parton" quantum numbers limits the number of degrees of freedom.

## Method
The construction extends the relativistic quantum theory of N-particles (Polyzou, previous work) by introducing conserved "atomic parton" quantum numbers and additional bare particles with composite atomic-parton quantum numbers. The model Hilbert space is a direct sum of tensor products of bare and atomic particle subspaces with the same atomic-parton numbers. Interactions are added to the non-interacting mass operator using Wigner's forms of the dynamics and Clebsch-Gordan coefficients of the Poincaré group, with a recursive many-body construction that uses Ursell cluster expansions and Cayley transforms to enforce cluster properties.

## Key numbers
- The Hilbert space for a model with atomic partons of nucleon and pion is given as ${\cal H}_{\{1,1\}} = ({\cal H}_{N}\otimes {\cal H}_{\pi})\oplus{\cal H}_{\Delta}$, where the conserved atomic-parton numbers are ${\cal N} := \{ N_N, N_\pi\}$.
- The mass operator combining different partition dynamics is defined as $\bar{M} := \sum_b (-)^{n_b} (n_b-1)! \hat{B}_b \hat{M}_b \hat{B}_b^{\dagger}$, where $n_b$ is the number of equivalence classes in partition $b$.
- The scattering equivalence operator is constructed as $\hat{B}= {1 - i \hat{\beta} \over 1+i \hat{\beta}}$ with $\hat{\beta} = \sum_b (-1)^{n_b} (n_b-1)! \hat{\beta}_b$.

## Context
This work extends the relativistic quantum theory of N-particles developed by Polyzou in prior papers (1990s) to treat a class of models with particle production, including the relativistic Lee model and relativistic isobar models. The approach separates the difficulties of particle production from those of infinite degrees of freedom by conserving atomic-parton number, enabling future construction of well-defined mathematical models for realistic strongly interacting systems that require high-precision ab-initio calculations.
