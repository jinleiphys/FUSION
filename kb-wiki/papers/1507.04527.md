# Net baryon number fluctuations across the chiral phase transition at finite density in the strong coupling lattice QCD

> We investigate the net-baryon number fluctuations across the chiral phase transition at finite density in the strong coupling and chiral limit. Mesonic field fluctuations are taken into account by using the auxiliary field Monte-Carlo method. We find that the higher-order cumulant ratios, $Sσ$ and $κσ^2$, show oscillatory behavior around the phase boundary at $μ/T\gtrsim 0.2$, and there exists the region where the higher-order cumulant ratios are negative. The negative region of $κσ^2$ is found to shrink with increasing lattice size. This behavior agrees with the expectations from the scaling analysis.

## Key claim

The higher-order cumulant ratios of net-baryon number, \(S\sigma\) and \(\kappa\sigma^2\), show oscillatory behavior around the chiral phase boundary at \(\mu/T \gtrsim 0.2\) in the strong coupling and chiral limit, and the negative region of \(\kappa\sigma^2\) shrinks with increasing lattice size, consistent with scaling analysis expectations.

## Method

The authors use the auxiliary field Monte-Carlo (AFMC) method to simulate lattice QCD in the strong coupling limit (\(g \to \infty\)) with one species of unrooted staggered fermion in the chiral limit on anisotropic Euclidean lattices. They integrate out spatial link variables in the leading order of the strong coupling and \(1/d\) expansion, then apply an extended Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to bosonize the four-Fermi interactions, and finally integrate over Grassmann and temporal link variables to obtain an effective action for auxiliary \(\sigma\) and \(\pi\) fields. Monte-Carlo configurations are generated using the phase quenched action at finite \(\mu\) and \(T\), and observables are calculated via reweighting.

## Key numbers

* On a \(6^3 \times 6\) lattice, the critical temperature at \(\mu=0\) is \(T_c \simeq 1.46862\) (in lattice units), defined by the peak position of chiral susceptibility.
* The average phase factor \(\langle \exp(i\theta) \rangle_\mathrm{pq}\) is \(\gtrsim 0.9\) for \(\mu/T \lesssim 0.8\) on a \(4^3 \times 4\) lattice and \(\gtrsim 0.4\) on an \(8^3 \times 8\) lattice around the phase boundary.
* The negative region of the normalized skewness \(S\sigma\) starts to appear between \(\mu/T = 0.3\) and \(\mu/T = 0.5\) on a \(6^4\) lattice.
* Oscillatory behavior in \(\kappa\sigma^2\) appears for \(\mu/T > 0.2\) on a \(6^4\) lattice.

## Context

This work extends the strong coupling lattice QCD approach using the AFMC method developed by Ichihara et al. (2014) to study net-baryon number fluctuations, complementing studies using chiral models with functional renormalization group (FRG) by Schaefer and Wambach and by Morita et al. The results are consistent with expectations from scaling analysis of the O(2) universality class, as previously studied in the monomer-dimer-polymer (MDP) simulation framework. This enables further investigation of critical behavior at finite density in a regime where standard lattice QCD faces the sign problem.
