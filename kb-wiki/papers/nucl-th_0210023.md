# Muon catalysis of superheavy element production in nucleus-nucleus fusion reaction

> It is shown that muon bound with light projectile induces the superheavy elements production in nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

Muon bound to a light projectile nucleus can induce superheavy element (SHE) production in nucleus-nucleus fusion reactions by reducing the Coulomb repulsion between protons, but the effect is strongest for systems with a shallow capture pocket, such as the reaction \(^{86}\)Kr\(_\mu\) + \(^{208}\)Pb \(\rightarrow\) \(^{294}\)118.

## Method

The author models the fusion process in three steps: capture of two nuclei in an entrance-channel potential well, shape evolution from touching nuclei to a spherical compound nucleus, and survival of the excited compound nucleus against fission. The potential energy of the muon-nuclear system is decomposed into pure nuclear and muon-nuclear contributions, and the muon binding energies are computed using the Pustovalov parametrization with a nuclear charge radius of \(R = 1.2 A^{1/3}\) fm. The entrance-channel fusion barriers, pocket depths, and capture pocket bottoms are evaluated for both pure nuclear and muon-nuclear systems for three representative reactions.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{70}\)Zn + \(^{208}\)Pb \(\rightarrow\) \(^{278}\)112: pure nuclear barrier height \(B_{gs} = 16.7\) MeV; muon-nuclear barrier height \(B_{gs\; \mu} = 20.3\) MeV; pure nuclear pocket depth \(D_{pw} = 4.0\) MeV; muon-nuclear pocket depth \(D_{pw\; \mu} = 5.3\) MeV.
- For \(^{78}\)Ge + \(^{208}\)Pb \(\rightarrow\) \(^{286}\)114: pure nuclear barrier height \(B_{gs} = 12.8\) MeV; muon-nuclear barrier height \(B_{gs\; \mu} = 16.4\) MeV; pure nuclear pocket depth \(D_{pw} = 2.9\) MeV; muon-nuclear pocket depth \(D_{pw\; \mu} = 3.8\) MeV.
- For \(^{86}\)Kr + \(^{208}\)Pb \(\rightarrow\) \(^{294}\)118: pure nuclear barrier height \(B_{gs} = 5.3\) MeV; muon-nuclear barrier height \(B_{gs\; \mu} = 9.1\) MeV; pure nuclear pocket depth \(D_{pw} = 1.1\) MeV; muon-nuclear pocket depth \(D_{pw\; \mu} = 1.9\) MeV.
- The muon lifetime is approximately \(2.2 \times 10^{-6}\) s, sufficient for forming a 1s bound state with a light projectile before collision.

## Context

This work extends the concept of muon catalysis, previously studied for thermonuclear reactions between hydrogen isotopes (see Kim and references therein), to the synthesis of superheavy elements. The author shows that muon binding modifies the capture pocket, potential-energy landscape, and fission barrier height, which are key factors in SHE production as described in prior work by Denisov and collaborators (Denisov, 2002; Denisov and Hofmann, 2000). The results suggest that muon catalysis could enable the use of reactions with shallow capture pockets, such as \(^{86}\)Kr + \(^{208}\)Pb, which are otherwise marginal for SHE production.
