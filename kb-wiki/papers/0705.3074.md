# Covariant Hamiltonian Dynamics with Negative Energy States

> A relativistic quantum mechanics is studied for bound hadronic systems in the framework of the Point Form Relativistic Hamiltonian Dynamics. Negative energy states are introduced taking into account the restrictions imposed by a correct definition of the Poincaré group generators. We obtain nonpathological, manifestly covariant wave equations that dynamically contain the contributions of the negative energy states. Auxiliary negative energy states are also introduced, specially for studying the interactions of the hadronic systems with external probes.

## Key claim

Negative energy states can be incorporated into Point Form Relativistic Hamiltonian Dynamics (PF RHD) for bound hadronic systems, but only if all particles in a given state share the same energy sign; states with mixed energy signs are relegated to auxiliary states that do not directly participate in the dynamics.

## Method

The author generalizes the Poincaré group generators for free particles by introducing an energy-sign operator λ, modifying the Hamiltonian to h = λ ε(p) and the boost generator to t = λ (d + g) while leaving momentum and angular momentum unchanged. For interacting particles, the construction of the four-velocity operator V^μ requires all particles in a dynamical state to have the same energy sign, and the resulting wave equation is written in manifestly covariant form using projection states that select the three-momenta of N−1 particles and the spatial part of the system's four-velocity. Auxiliary states, representing configurations with mixed energy signs, are defined covariantly via the interaction operator without introducing new parameters, using the Mandelzweig-Wallace relativistic wave equation formalism as a link to field theory.

## Key numbers

- The free-particle Hamiltonian for a single particle is h(λ, p) = λ ε(p), where ε(p) = [p² + m²]^{1/2} and λ = +1 or −1 for positive or negative energy, respectively.
- The boost generator for a single particle is t = λ (d + g), with d = ½(r ε(p) + ε(p) r) and g = (p × s)/(ε(p) + m).
- The four-velocity v^μ satisfies v_μ v^μ = 1, with v⁰ = [1 + v²]^{1/2}.

## Context

This work extends the Point Form of Relativistic Hamiltonian Dynamics, originally formulated by Dirac (1949) and developed by others [5-7], to include negative energy states that are present in quantum field theories like QCD but were previously excluded from standard RHD models. The approach addresses the "continuum dissolution" problem identified in Dirac-like equations by Sucher (1985) and others [11,12], and establishes a connection with the Mandelzweig-Wallace equation [11] and the Gross equation [13] for relativistic bound states. The formalism enables future calculations of electroweak form factors and Compton scattering amplitudes for hadronic systems, where negative energy contributions (z-graphs) are expected to be significant.
