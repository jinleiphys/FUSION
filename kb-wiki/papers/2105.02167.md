# Heavy quark dynamics in a strongly magnetized quark-gluon plasma

> We present a calculation of the heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficients in a quark-gluon plasma under the presence of a strong external magnetic field, within the Lowest Landau Level (LLL) approximation. In particular, we apply the Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) technique for the resummed effective gluon propagator, generalized for a hot and magnetized medium. Using the derived effective HTL gluon propagator and the LLL quark propagator we analytically derive the full results for the longitudinal and transverse momentum diffusion coefficients as well as the energy losses for charm and bottom quarks beyond the static limit. We also show numerical results for these coefficients in two special cases where the heavy quark is moving either parallel or perpendicular to the external magnetic field.

## Key claim

The paper presents an analytic calculation of heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficients in a quark-gluon plasma under a strong external magnetic field, using the Lowest Landau Level (LLL) approximation and Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) resummation, but the results are valid only under the specific scale hierarchy \(M \gg \sqrt{eB} \gg T\) and the additional constraint \(\alpha_s eB \ll T^2\) that allows neglect of soft self-energy corrections for LLL quarks and gluons.

## Method

The authors compute the heavy quark scattering rate via the imaginary part of the heavy quark self-energy, using the HTL-resummed effective gluon propagator generalized for a hot and magnetized medium, combined with the LLL quark propagator. They analytically derive the longitudinal and transverse momentum diffusion coefficients for charm and bottom quarks beyond the static limit, evaluating numerical results for two special cases where the heavy quark moves either parallel or perpendicular to the external magnetic field.

## Key numbers

* The magnetic field strength at RHIC is \(eB \sim \mathcal{O}(1) m_\pi^2\) and at LHC is \(eB \sim \mathcal{O}(10) m_\pi^2\), based on numerical simulations cited from prior work.
* The Debye screening mass without magnetic field is \((m_D^2)_g = \frac{g^2 N_c T^2}{3}\), with \(N_c\) the number of colors.
* The scale hierarchy assumed is \(M \gg \sqrt{eB} \gg T\), with the additional condition \(\alpha_s eB \ll T^2\) for neglecting soft self-energy corrections.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of heavy quark transport coefficients in a magnetized medium by Fukushima (2015), Sadofyev (2015), Kurian (2019), and Singh (2020), which were mostly limited to the static limit or used the LLL approximation. The paper goes beyond the static limit to treat relativistic heavy quarks with finite velocity, enabling more realistic phenomenological modeling of heavy quark dynamics in non-central heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where strong magnetic fields are present.
