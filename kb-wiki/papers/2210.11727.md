# Minimal Length, Nuclear Matter, and Neutron Stars

> In this paper, we employ one variant of the Generalized Uncertainty Principle (GUP) model, i.e., the Kempf-Mangano-Mann (KMM) model, and discuss the impact of GUP on the EoS of nuclear and neutron star matter based on the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model. We input the result in the Serrano-Liška (SL) gravity theory to discuss the corresponding Neutron Star (NS) properties. We have shown that the upper bound for the GUP parameter from nuclear matter properties is $β\leq 2\times10^{-7}$ MeV$^{-2}$. If we used this $β$ upper bound to calculate NS matter, and considering SL parameter $\tilde{c}$ as an independent parameter, we have found that the upper bound for the SL parameter, which modifies the Einstein field equation, is $\tilde{c} \leq 10^7$ m$^2$. This beta upper bound is determined by considering the anisotropy magnitude smaller than the pressure magnitude. By employing $β=2\times10^{-7}$ MeV$^{-2}$ and $\tilde{c} = 10^7$ m$^2$, we obtain the mass-radius relation that satisfies NICER data for both PSR J0740+6620 (whose mass is $\sim 2.1M_\odot$) and PSR J0030+0451 ($M\sim 1.4M_\odot$). Our GUP parameter upper bound perfectly matches the constraint from $^{87}$Rb cold-atom-recoil experiment. If we consider that the same strength from the additional logarithmic term in the entropy from both GUP and SL model are dependent, for $β< 2\times10^{-7}$ MeV$^{-2}$, it is clear that SL parameter lower bound is $\tilde{c} > -16\times 10^{-34}$ m$^2$. The magnitude of this bound is $10^{-40}$ smaller than the upper bound magnitude of SL parameter considering as independent parameter i.e., $\tilde{c} \leq 10^7$ m$^2$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the Generalized Uncertainty Principle (GUP) parameter beta from the Kempf-Mangano-Mann model can be constrained using nuclear matter properties, but only if one introduces an ad hoc anisotropic pressure to preserve the first law of thermodynamics; the resulting upper bound beta <= 2 x 10^{-7} MeV^{-2} matches the constraint from a cold-atom-recoil experiment.

## Method

The authors incorporate the KMM quadratic GUP into the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) model for symmetric nuclear matter, pure neutron matter, and neutron star matter by deforming the phase-space integration measure and modifying the dispersion relation. They then use the resulting equation of state as input to the Serrano-Liska gravity theory, which modifies Einstein's field equations via an additional logarithmic term in the Bekenstein entropy, to compute neutron star mass-radius relations. The beta stability and charge neutrality conditions are solved self-consistently for neutron star matter, and the first law of thermodynamics is enforced by introducing an anisotropic pressure component.

## Key numbers

- Upper bound on the GUP parameter from nuclear matter properties: beta <= 2 x 10^{-7} MeV^{-2}
- Upper bound on the Serrano-Liska parameter (treated as independent): c-tilde <= 10^{7} m^{2}
- Lower bound on the Serrano-Liska parameter (when GUP and SL parameters are dependent, for beta < 2 x 10^{-7} MeV^{-2}): c-tilde > -16 x 10^{-34} m^{2}
- The magnitude of this lower bound (1.6 x 10^{-33} m^{2}) is 10^{-40} times smaller than the upper bound magnitude (10^{7} m^{2})
- With beta = 2 x 10^{-7} MeV^{-2} and c-tilde = 10^{7} m^{2}, the mass-radius relation satisfies NICER data for PSR J0740+6620 (mass ~ 2.1 solar masses) and PSR J0030+0451 (mass ~ 1.4 solar masses)

## Context

This work extends prior studies of GUP effects on compact objects, notably the only previous discussion of GUP impacts on neutron stars by Abac (2021) which used the linear RMF (Walecka) model and general relativity. The authors build on the Serrano-Liska gravity theory (Alonso-Serrano, 2020) which derives modified field equations from the thermodynamics of spacetime formalism with an additional logarithmic area term in entropy. The GUP parameter upper bound matches the constraint from a rubidium-87 cold-atom-recoil experiment, and the results enable simultaneous satisfaction of NICER observations for both a massive (~2.1 solar masses) and a canonical (~1.4 solar masses) neutron star.
