# Alpha decay half-lives of new superheavy elements

> The lifetimes of $α$ decays of the recently produced isotopes of the elements 112, 114, 116 and the element $^{294}118$ and of some decay products have been calculated theoretically within the WKB approximation using microscopic $α$-nucleus interaction potentials. These nuclear potentials have been obtained by folding the densities of the $α$ and the daughter nuclei with the M3Y effective interaction, supplemented by a zero-range pseudo-potential for exchange along with the density dependence. Spherical charge distributions have been used for calculating the Coulomb interaction potentials. These calculations provide reasonable estimates for the observed $α$ decay lifetimes and thus provide reliable predictions for other superheavies.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their WKB-based calculations using microscopic double-folded α-nucleus potentials (DDM3Y) provide "reasonable estimates" for the observed α-decay half-lives of the new superheavy elements, but the agreement is qualified: the result for ²⁹⁴118 is "almost underestimated" because the centrifugal barrier for spin-parity conservation could not be included, and the theoretical half-lives are sensitive to uncertainties in the experimental Q-values.

## Method

The authors computed α-decay half-lives using the WKB barrier penetration approximation. The nuclear interaction potential was obtained by double-folding the density distributions of the α particle (Gaussian form) and the spherical daughter nucleus (two-parameter Fermi function) with the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, supplemented by a zero-range pseudo-potential for single-nucleon exchange. The Coulomb potential was calculated assuming spherical charge distributions, and the assault frequency was derived from the zero-point vibration energy, which was taken proportional to the experimental Q-value.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical predictions for superheavy element α-decay half-lives, such as those from the generalized liquid drop model (GLDM) by Royer (2002, 2004) and the Viola-Seaborg systematics with Sobiczewski constants (VSS, 1989). The authors compare their DDM3Y results to these prior approaches and find that VSS largely overestimates half-lives for eight of the fourteen measured decays, while the present calculations overestimate only three. The study enables predictions for about eighty additional superheavy isotopes, with half-lives ranging from years to microseconds, guiding future experimental searches using recoil separation, chemical studies, or trapped-ion techniques.
