# Realistic classical binding energies in the $ω$-Skyrme model

> An omega-meson extension of the Skyrme model - without the Skyrme term but including the pion mass - first considered by Adkins and Nappi is studied in detail for baryon numbers 1 to 8. The static problem is reformulated as a constrained energy minimisation problem within a natural geometric framework and studied analytically on compact domains, and numerically on Euclidean space. Using a constrained second-order Newton flow algorithm, classical energy minimisers are constructed for various values of the omega-pion coupling. At high coupling, these Skyrmion solutions are qualitatively similar to the Skyrmions of the standard Skyrme model with massless pions. At sufficiently low coupling, they show similarities with those in the lightly bound Skyrme model: the Skyrmions of low baryon number dissociate into lightly bound clusters of distinct 1-Skyrmions, and the classical binding energies for baryon numbers 2 through 8 have realistic values.

## Key claim

The omega-meson extension of the Skyrme model (without the Skyrme term but with pion mass) can produce classical binding energies for baryon numbers 2 through 8 that are realistic, but only at sufficiently low values of the omega-pion coupling; at high coupling the solutions resemble the standard Skyrme model with massless pions rather than realistic nuclei.

## Method

The static problem was reformulated as a constrained energy minimization problem where the temporal component of the omega field is uniquely determined by the Skyrme field through a linear inhomogeneous PDE. A constrained second-order Newton flow algorithm was used to construct classical energy minimizers for baryon numbers 1 to 8, updating the omega field after each time step by solving the constraint equation via a conjugate gradient method.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies for baryon numbers 2 through 8 were found to have realistic values at sufficiently low omega-pion coupling, addressing the problem that Skyrme-type models are typically too strongly bound.
- The model has a line of vanishing classical binding energy, beyond which solutions become metastable (local minima rather than global minima of the energy functional).
- The pion mass parameter m appears in the potential V(φ) = (m²/4)(1 - φ₀), giving the pions mass in units of the omega mass.
- The coupling constant g appears in the action term g∫ ω ∧ φ*Ω, where Ω is the normalized volume form on S³ (normalized so that ∫_{S³} Ω = 1).

## Context

This work extends the omega-meson extension of the Skyrme model first considered by Adkins and Nappi (1983), which had received little attention in the following 36 years. Sutcliffe (2008) had previously constructed solutions only for degrees 1 to 4 within a rational map approximation (approximating field equations by ODEs), while Speight (2018) considered only the degree 1 sector with an additional isospin symmetry breaking term. The present work enables, for the first time, extensive simulation across a wide selection of topological sectors for a range of coupling values, and shows that the model can produce realistic binding energies that address the long-standing problem of Skyrme-type models being too strongly bound.
