# Factorization at the LHC: From PDFs to Initial State Jets

> We study proton-(anti)proton collisions at the LHC or Tevatron in the presence of experimental restrictions on the hadronic final state and for generic parton momentum fractions. At the scale Q of the hard interaction, factorization does not yield standard parton distribution functions (PDFs) for the initial state. The measurement restricting the hadronic final state introduces a new scale μ_B << Q and probes the proton prior to the hard collision. This corresponds to evaluating the PDFs at the scale μ_B. After the proton is probed, the incoming hard parton is contained in an initial-state jet, and the hard collision occurs between partons inside these jets rather than inside protons. The proper description of such initial-state jets requires "beam functions". At the scale μ_B, the beam function factorizes into a convolution of calculable Wilson coefficients and PDFs. Below μ_B, the initial-state evolution is described by the usual PDF evolution which changes x, while above μ_B it is governed by a different renormalization group evolution which sums double logarithms of μ_B/Q and leaves x fixed. As an example, we prove a factorization theorem for "isolated Drell-Yan", pp -> Xl+l- where X is restricted to have no central jets. We comment on the extension to cases where the hadronic final state contains a certain number of isolated central jets.

## Key claim

The paper proves that for proton-(anti)proton collisions with restrictions on the hadronic final state (such as vetoing central jets), the initial state cannot be described by standard parton distribution functions (PDFs) alone; instead, process-independent "beam functions" are required, which reduce to PDFs at leading order but evolve differently and sum double logarithms of the ratio between the beam scale μ_B and the hard scale Q.

## Method

The authors use soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) to derive a factorization theorem for "isolated Drell-Yan" production, pp → Xℓ⁺ℓ⁻, where the hadronic final state X is restricted to have no central jets by imposing cuts on the hemisphere variables B_a⁺ and B_b⁺ (the light-cone momentum components of hadronic radiation in each beam hemisphere). They define beam functions B_i(t, x, μ_B) as matrix elements that describe the initial-state jet, show that these factorize into convolutions of calculable Wilson coefficients with standard PDFs at the scale μ_B, and derive the renormalization group evolution that sums Sudakov double logarithms above μ_B while leaving x fixed.

## Key numbers

- The beam scale μ_B is parametrically smaller than the hard scale Q, with μ_B ≪ Q.
- The cut on hemisphere variables is given by B_{a,b}^+ ≤ Q e^{-2 y_cut}, where a typical experimental value might be y_cut = 2, vetoing energetic jets in the central region |y| ≤ 1.
- The hard interaction scale Q = √(q²) ≫ Λ_QCD, where q is the dilepton momentum.
- The invariant mass of the hadronic final state is bounded by m_X² ≤ E_cm² (1 - √τ)², where τ = q²/E_cm².
- The beam function at leading order is B_i(t, ξ, μ_B) = δ(t) f_i(ξ, μ_B) + O[α_s(μ_B)].

## Context

This work extends the classic factorization theorem for inclusive Drell-Yan (Collins 1985, Bodwin 1984) and threshold resummation (Sterman 1986, Catani 1989) to the case of generic parton momentum fractions with restrictions on the hadronic final state, which is the dominant kinematic region at the LHC. The beam functions introduced here are related to the same matrix element of gluon fields found by Fleming (2006) in γp → J/ψ X, and the formalism enables rigorous tests of initial-state parton showers in Monte Carlo programs like Pythia (Sjöstrand 2006) and Herwig (Corcella 2000, Bahr 2008), as well as providing a framework for resummation in processes with isolated jets.
