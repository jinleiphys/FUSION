# Hot quark matter and merger remnants

> This work investigates hot quark matter under the thermodynamic conditions characteristic of a binary neutron star (BNS) merger remnants. We used the density-dependent quark mass model (DDQM) to access the microscopic nuclear equation of state (EoS) in a series of snapshots. The strange quark matter (SQM) is studied at finite temperature and entropy, in the presence of electrons and muons and their corresponding neutrinos to simulate the BNS merger conditions. For the first time, we introduced temperature into the DDQM model using a lattice QCD-motivated approach to construct both isentropic and isothermal EoSs. We observe that as the entropy of the SQM increases, the merger remnant becomes more massive and increases in size, whereas the neutrino abundance also increases. In the fixed-temperature case, on the other hand, we observe that the entropy spreads from the surface towards the center of the remnant. We determine the particle distribution in the core of the remnants, the structure of the remnant, the temperature profile, sound velocity, and the polytropic index, and discuss their effects. The strange-quark star (SQS) remnants satisfy the $2\,{\rm M_\odot}$ mass constraint associated with neutron stars (NS).

## Key claim

The paper claims that strange quark matter (SQM) in binary neutron star merger remnants, modeled with a temperature-dependent density-dependent quark mass model (DDQM), can produce strange-quark star remnants that satisfy the 2 M⊙ mass constraint, but only under the specific condition that the model parameters are chosen from a Bayesian analysis at T = 0 MeV (√D = 127.40 MeV, C = 0.8) and the temperature dependence is introduced via a lattice QCD-motivated string tension that vanishes at T_C = 270 MeV.

## Method

The authors extended the density-dependent quark mass model (DDQM) to finite temperature by incorporating a lattice QCD-motivated temperature dependence into the quark mass scaling formula, where the confining term scales as [1 − T²/T_C²] and the Coulomb-like term scales as its inverse. They constructed both isentropic (fixed entropy per baryon) and isothermal (fixed temperature) equations of state for SQM in β-equilibrium with electrons, muons, and their corresponding neutrinos, with lepton fractions fixed at Y_{L,e} = Y_{L,μ} = 0.1 to simulate BNS merger conditions. The stellar structure was then solved to obtain mass, radius, temperature profiles, sound velocity, and polytropic index for the merger remnants.

## Key numbers

- Model parameters: √D = 127.40 MeV (confining strength) and C = 0.8 (deconfinement strength), determined from Bayesian analysis at T = 0 MeV in daSilva (2023)
- Critical temperature for deconfinement: T_C = 270 MeV, taken from quenched lattice QCD results
- Current quark masses used: m_u = 2.16 MeV, m_d = 4.67 MeV, m_s = 93.4 MeV (from Particle Data Group 2022)
- Nuclear saturation density: n_0 = 0.152 fm⁻³
- Baryon density in merger remnants: n_b ∼ 5–6 n_0 (from prior simulations cited)
- Temperature in postmerger remnant core: 20 to 60 MeV initially, up to ∼150 MeV in contact layer (from prior simulations cited)
- Lepton fractions fixed at Y_{L,e} = Y_{L,μ} = 0.1
- The strange-quark star remnants satisfy the 2 M⊙ mass constraint

## Context

This work extends the DDQM framework, previously applied to cold quark stars by daSilva (2023) and to proto-neutron stars by Issifu (2023), to the finite-temperature conditions of BNS merger remnants. It builds on numerical relativity simulations by Perego (2019), Baiotti (2016), and others that predict temperatures of 20–150 MeV and densities of 5–6 n_0 in postmerger remnants. The inclusion of muons and their neutrinos follows the recent work of Loffredo (2022), who showed muons significantly affect the EoS in merger simulations. This enables future comparisons with postmerger gravitational wave signals expected from next-generation detectors such as the Einstein Telescope and Cosmic Explorer.
