# Thermal and Hadrochemical Equilibration in Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions at the SPS

> The currently available set of hadron abundances at the SPS for central S+Au(W,Pb) collisions is compared to predictions from a scenario assuming local thermal and hadrochemical equilibrium. The data are consistent with a freeze-out temperature T = 160-170 MeV. Spectra are consistent with this temperature range and a moderate transverse expansion. The freeze-out points at the AGS and SPS are found to be close to the phase boundary between a hadron gas and an ideal quark-gluon phase.

## Key claim

The hadron abundances measured in central S+Au(W,Pb) collisions at the SPS are consistent with a system in local thermal and hadrochemical equilibrium at freeze-out, with a temperature of 160-170 MeV and baryon chemical potential of 170-180 MeV, provided that finite-size and excluded-volume corrections are applied and that the analysis uses data integrated over the maximum available rapidity and transverse momentum range.

## Method

The authors compared 27 measured particle ratios from central 200 A GeV/c S+Au(W,Pb) collisions to a grand-canonical thermal model including all known baryons up to 2 GeV and mesons up to 1.5 GeV, with finite-size and excluded-volume corrections applied. The baryon chemical potential was fixed for each temperature by fitting the proton-to-pion ratio, while the strangeness chemical potential was determined by the strangeness neutrality condition, assuming full strangeness saturation. Transverse momentum spectra were analyzed using a blast-wave formalism with a linear radial flow profile to extract the average transverse expansion velocity.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature range: T = 160-170 MeV
- Baryon chemical potential range: μ_b = 170-180 MeV
- Strangeness chemical potential: μ_s = 38.0 MeV (at T=160 MeV) and 47.0 MeV (at T=170 MeV)
- Average transverse expansion velocity: ⟨β_t⟩ = 0.27 (for SPS), compared to 0.33-0.39 at AGS
- Entropy per net baryon at freeze-out: S/(B-‾B) = 45.4 (T=160 MeV) and 36.7 (T=170 MeV)
- Pion density at freeze-out from thermal model: ρ_π ≈ 0.30/fm³ (T=160 MeV), consistent with experimental estimate of 0.27/fm³ from RQMD volume of 1940 fm³ and total pion multiplicity of 510
- Strange quark chemical potential: μ_qs = 18.6 MeV (T=160 MeV) and 13.0 MeV (T=170 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the thermal model analysis previously applied to AGS data by Braun-Munzinger et al. (1995) to SPS energies, finding freeze-out points at both energies close to the phase boundary between a hadron gas and an ideal quark-gluon plasma. The results contradict the higher freeze-out temperature of 190 MeV proposed by Rafelski (1991) and Heinz (1994), which significantly underpredicted pion abundances, and instead support a lower temperature that avoids unphysically high pion densities. The analysis enables systematic mapping of the freeze-out curve in the QCD phase diagram across beam energies and provides a baseline for testing whether strangeness saturation is achieved in heavy-ion collisions without requiring an additional strangeness suppression factor.
