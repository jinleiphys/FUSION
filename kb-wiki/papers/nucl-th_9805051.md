# New Discrete Basis for Nuclear Structure Studies

> A complete discrete set of spherical single-particle wave functions for studies of weakly-bound many-body systems is proposed. The new basis is obtained by means of a local-scale point transformation of the spherical harmonic oscillator wave functions. Unlike the harmonic oscillator states, the new wave functions decay exponentially at large distances. Using the new basis, characteristics of weakly-bound orbitals are analyzed and the ground state properties of some spherical doubly-magic nuclei are studied. The basis of the transformed harmonic oscillator is a significant improvement over the harmonic oscillator basis, especially in studies of exotic nuclei where the coupling to the particle continuum is important.

# New Discrete Basis for Nuclear Structure Studies

## Key claim
The transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis, obtained via a local-scale point transformation of standard harmonic oscillator wave functions, provides a complete discrete single-particle basis with exponential asymptotic decay that is significantly more efficient than the harmonic oscillator basis for describing weakly-bound nuclear systems, though the improvement is most pronounced for loosely-bound halo states and the basis requires optimization of three parameters.

## Method
The authors construct the THO basis by applying a local-scale point transformation (LST) to spherical harmonic oscillator wave functions, using a three-parameter LST function f(r) that ensures exponential decay at large distances while maintaining continuity up to fourth derivatives. They test the basis by diagonalizing a finite square well Hamiltonian for s-wave states at various binding energies, comparing convergence with the standard HO basis, and perform restricted variational Hartree-Fock calculations using the Skyrme SkP force for three doubly-magic nuclei (16O, 40Ca, 208Pb), minimizing the energy functional with respect to the THO parameters.

## Key numbers
- For a 3s halo state at -200 keV binding energy, N_max=20 THO shells reproduce the energy within 50-60 keV accuracy, while N_max≈30 HO shells are required for comparable accuracy.
- For a 3s halo state at -40 keV binding energy, the same relative improvement of THO over HO is observed.
- The optimized single-particle THO wave function for a 1s state at -14 keV has a squared overlap with the exact square well solution of |⟨SQW|THO⟩|²=0.994, compared to |⟨SQW|HO⟩|²=0.877 for the HO wave function.
- In variational HF calculations for 208Pb, THO binding energies are within 1.1% of full self-consistent HF results, while HO results deviate up to 5%.
- The square well potential used for testing had radius R₀=7.11 fm.

## Context
This work extends the local-scale transformation method developed by Petkov and Stoitsov (1983) and earlier density functional applications by Dimitrova et al. (1986, 1988) to create a practical discrete basis for nuclear structure calculations. The THO basis directly addresses the well-known deficiency of harmonic oscillator bases in describing weakly-bound nuclei near the drip lines, as discussed in reviews by Mueller (1993), Riisager (1994), Hansen (1995), and Tanihata (1996). The approach offers an alternative to continuum shell model methods, Sturmian function expansions, Gamow (Berggren) states, and coordinate-space HFB methods, and is particularly relevant for future HFB calculations of exotic nuclei where the coupling to the particle continuum is important.
