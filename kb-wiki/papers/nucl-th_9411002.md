# Comparison of potential models with the np scattering data below 350 MeV

> As a follow-up on our earlier paper we calculate the $χ^{2}$ of various $N\!N$ potential models with respect to the $np$ scattering data. We find that only the most recent potential models give a reasonably good description of these data. Almost none of the potentials is truly an $N\!N$ potential in the sense that it gives a good description of both $pp$ and $np$ scattering simultaneously.

## Key claim

Only the most recent potential models give a reasonably good description of the np scattering data below 350 MeV, but almost none of the potentials is truly an NN potential in the sense that it gives a good description of both pp and np scattering simultaneously. The authors explicitly show that a good fit to np data does not automatically guarantee a good fit to pp data, contradicting the common assumption that fitting only to np data and adding electromagnetic corrections yields a valid NN potential.

## Method

The authors calculated the chi-squared of nine NN potential models (HJ62, Reid68, TRS75, Paris80, Urb81, Arg84, BonnR, Bonn89, and Nijm93) against the Nijmegen np database of 2458 data points between 5 and 350 MeV. They computed phase shifts for partial waves up to total angular momentum J=6 from the nuclear potentials, used one-pion exchange for higher partial waves, constructed scattering amplitudes, and calculated observables to compare with experimental data. Low-energy data below 5 MeV were excluded to avoid the dominant effect of the scattering length difference between pp and np systems.

## Key numbers

- The Nijm93 potential achieved chi-squared per data point of 1.9 on np data (5-350 MeV) and 1.9 on pp data (5-350 MeV), making it the only model giving good simultaneous description of both systems
- The Arg84 potential gave chi-squared per data point of 2.1 on np data (5-350 MeV) but 6.9 on pp data (5-350 MeV), classifying it as an np-only potential
- The Bonn89 potential gave chi-squared per data point of 3.0 on np data and 1.8 on pp data (5-350 MeV)
- The Reid68 potential gave chi-squared per data point of 10.7 on np data and 2.5 on pp data (5-350 MeV), classifying it as a pp-only potential
- At T_lab=300 MeV, the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis gives delta(1P1) = -27.58(22) degrees and epsilon_1 = 4.03(17) degrees
- The Bonn A and Bonn B coordinate-space potentials gave chi-squared per data point of 8.3 and 8.9 respectively on np data (5-350 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous comparison of NN potential models against pp scattering data (Stoks and de Swart, 1993) by now testing the isoscalar (I=0) partial waves against the newly completed Nijmegen np database (Stoks et al., 1993). The paper contradicts the common practice exemplified by the full Bonn potential (Machleidt et al., 1987), which was fitted only to np data and later had to be adjusted for pp scattering (Haidenbauer and Holinde, 1989). The results enable the construction of truly simultaneous NN potentials, such as the subsequently developed Nijm I, Nijm II, a regularized Reid68 update, and an updated Arg84 potential, all achieving chi-squared per data point near 1 on both pp and np data.
