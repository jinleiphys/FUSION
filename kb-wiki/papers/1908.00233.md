# Centrality Dependence of Multiplicity Fluctuations in Ion-Ion Collisions from the Beam Energy Scan at FAIR

> Multiplicity distributions and event-by-event multiplicity fluctuations in AuAu collisions at energies in future heavy-ion experiment at the Facility for Anti-proton and Ion Research (FAIR) are investigated. Events corresponding to FAIR energies are simulated in the frame work of Ultra Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (URQMD) model. It is observed that the mean and the width of multiplicity distributions monotonically increase with beam energy. The trend of variations of dispersion with mean number of participating nucleons for the centrality-bin width of 5\% are in accord with the Central Limit Theorem. The multiplicity distributions in various centrality bins as well as for full event samples are observed to obey Koba, Nielsen and Olesen (KNO) scaling. The trends of variations of scaled variance with beam energy are also found to support the KNO scaling predictions for larger collision centrality. The findings also reveal that the statistical fluctuations in 5\% centrality-bin width appear to be under control.

## Key claim

Multiplicity distributions and event-by-event multiplicity fluctuations in AuAu collisions at FAIR energies, simulated with the URQMD model, obey KNO scaling and show that statistical fluctuations in 5% centrality-bin width appear to be under control, though the URQMD model does not explicitly include phase transitions from hadronic to partonic matter, so a clear suggestion about the location of the QCD critical point cannot be made.

## Method

AuAu collisions at beam energies of 10, 20, 30, and 40A GeV were simulated using the URQMD model (urqmd-v3.4), generating 2.3, 2.3, 2.1, and 2.2 million events respectively. The analysis was performed in the pseudorapidity interval -1.0 < eta < 1.0 and transverse momentum range 0.2 < pT < 5.0 GeV/c, with centrality determined from the impact parameter on an event-by-event basis using the Glauber model. Multiplicity distributions, dispersion, and scaled variance were computed for centrality bins of 2%, 5%, and 10% width.

## Key numbers

- Mean charged particle multiplicity <N_ch> for the 5% most central collisions at 10A GeV: 231.03 +/- 0.07; at 40A GeV: 353.86 +/- 0.10
- Dispersion (sigma) for the 5% most central collisions at 10A GeV: 25.07 +/- 0.05; at 40A GeV: 35.23 +/- 0.07
- Scaled variance (omega) for the 5% most central collisions at 10A GeV: 2.7198 +/- 0.0009; at 40A GeV: 3.5065 +/- 0.0010
- Mean number of participating nucleons <N_part> for the 5% most central collisions: 348.00 +/- 0.0020 at all four beam energies
- Scaled variance for the 80% centrality bin at 10A GeV: 2.6062 +/- 0.0111; at 40A GeV: 4.3207 +/- 0.0620

## Context

This work extends studies of multiplicity fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, following the approach of Mukherjee (2016) who examined similar fluctuations at RHIC and lower energies using the CLT framework. The results provide baseline predictions for the future FAIR beam energy scan, enabling subtraction of simple dynamical and geometrical effects from expected QGP signals as discussed by Konchakovski (2008). The study also supports the KNO scaling behavior previously observed in pp collisions by the NA61 collaboration (2014) and extends it to AuAu collisions at FAIR energies.
