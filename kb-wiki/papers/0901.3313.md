# Spin observables in three-body direct nuclear reactions

> Direct nuclear reactions $\vec{d}+A$ and $\vec{p}+(An)$ are described in the framework of three-body Faddeev-type equations. Differential cross section and analyzing powers are calculated using several optical potential models and compared with the experimental data. Quite satisfactory agreement is found except for few systematic discrepancies.

## Key claim

The Faddeev/AGS framework, using several nucleon-nucleus optical potentials, yields a "quite satisfactory" description of deuteron and proton analyzing powers in three-body direct nuclear reactions, but with "few systematic discrepancies" that the abstract glosses over: these include failures in the vector analyzing power \(A_y\) at small angles for light nuclei and in the tensor analyzing power \(A_{xz}\) at large angles, as well as poor agreement for the \(\vec{p} + \Cn \to d + \C\) transfer reaction.

## Method

The author solved the momentum-space Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) equations, a set of coupled integral equations for transition operators, in a three-body \((p,n,A)\) model. The Coulomb interaction was included via the method of screening and renormalization. The dynamic input used the realistic CD Bonn potential for the \(np\) pair and several different nucleon-nucleus optical potentials (Watson et al., Menet et al., Becchetti and Greenlees, and Koning and Delaroche) for the \(nA\) and \(pA\) pairs.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron elastic scattering from \(\C\), \(\Ox\), \(\A{28}{Si}\), \(\A{40}{Ca}\), and \(\A{58}{Ni}\) was calculated at \(E_d = 56\) MeV; results were compared to experimental data from Matsuoka (1986) and Hatanaka (1980).
- Deuteron elastic scattering from \(\C\) and \(\A{28}{Si}\) was calculated at \(E_d = 29.5\) MeV; results were compared to data from Perrin (1977).
- Proton-\(\Cn\) elastic scattering was calculated at proton lab energies \(E_p = 17.5\) and 35 MeV.
- The transfer reaction \(\vec{p} + \Cn \to d + \C\) was calculated at \(E_p = 65\) MeV.
- For the \(\vec{d}+\C\) and \(\vec{d}+\Ox\) systems at 56 MeV, the Menet et al. (M) potential described data better than the Watson et al. (W) potential, but both failed to reproduce the vector analyzing power \(A_y\) data at small angles \(\Theta_{c.m.} < 40\) deg.
- For heavier nuclei (\(\A{28}{Si}\), \(\A{40}{Ca}\), \(\A{58}{Ni}\)) at 56 MeV, the Menet et al. (M) potential overpredicted the differential cross section at \(\Theta_{c.m.} > 40\) deg.
- For \(\vec{d}+\C\) and \(\vec{d}+\A{28}{Si}\) at 29.5 MeV, a larger discrepancy in \(A_y\) (or \(iT_{11}\)) occurred at scattering angles of 30 to 55 deg and 45 to 70 deg, respectively.
- The present Faddeev/AGS results for \(A_y\) at \(\Theta_{c.m.} \geq 60\) deg were higher by about 0.05 to 0.10 compared to previous CDCC results from Iseri (1988).

## Context

This work extends the exact three-body Faddeev/AGS treatment of direct nuclear reactions, previously applied to cross sections (Deltuva, 2007), to include spin observables. It directly compares its results to those of the approximate continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) method used by Iseri (1988), finding good agreement and thus validating CDCC for spin observables in deuteron-nucleus elastic scattering. The systematic discrepancies identified, particularly in the vector analyzing power at small angles, point to shortcomings in the nucleon-nucleus optical potentials (Watson et al., Menet et al., Becchetti and Greenlees, Koning and Delaroche) or the three-body model itself, and motivate the development of improved optical potentials or the inclusion of additional degrees of freedom.
