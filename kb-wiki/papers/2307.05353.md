# New Measurements of $^{71}$Ge Decay: Impact on the Gallium Anomaly

> A dedicated high-statistics measurement of the $^{71}$Ge half-life is found to be in accurate agreement with an accepted value of 11.43$\pm$0.03 d, eliminating a recently proposed route to bypass the "gallium anomaly" affecting several neutrino experiments. Our data also severely constrain the possibility of $^{71}$Ge decay to low-energy excited levels of the $^{71}$Ga daughter nucleus as a solution to this puzzle. Additional unpublished measurements of this decay are discussed. Following the incorporation of this new information, the gallium anomaly survives with high statistical significance.

# New Measurements of $^{71}$Ge Decay: Impact on the Gallium Anomaly

## Key claim
A dedicated high-statistics measurement of the $^{71}$Ge half-life yields 11.46±0.04 d, in accurate agreement with the accepted value of 11.43±0.03 d, eliminating a recently proposed route to bypass the gallium anomaly that would have required a half-life of approximately 12.5 d. The data also severely constrain the possibility of a ~10% branching ratio of $^{71}$Ge decay into low-energy excited levels of $^{71}$Ga as a solution to this puzzle, with the caveat that any new excited level(s) might be sufficiently long-lived (T$_{1/2}$ ≳ 12.6 yr) to escape the 0.4% branching ratio sensitivity.

## Method
A small 1 cm³ n-type germanium diode was shielded with 10 cm of lead under a 6 m.w.e. overburden, background was measured for 2.7 days, then the detector was activated in $^{71}$Ge via a four-day exposure to a moderated $^{252}$Cf neutron source inside a 20 cm polyethylene sphere. Post-activation data were collected over 42 days (with a single 5.7 day interruption), and the decay rates under the 1.29 keV (L-shell) and 10.37 keV (K-shell) peaks characteristic of $^{71}$Ge electron capture were fitted to extract the half-life and the L/K capture ratio.

## Key numbers
- $^{71}$Ge half-life measured as 11.46±0.04 d, in agreement with the accepted value of 11.43±0.03 d.
- The ratio of L-shell to K-shell electron capture probabilities P$_{L}$/P$_{K}$ = 0.116±0.004 from the data of a prior detector (Collar and Yoon cite this as "ppc"), and 0.125±0.008 from the present detector; both agree with a theoretical value of 0.12258(17).
- The most significant peak-like structure not observed pre-activation corresponds to a mere 0.4% branching ratio, which has negligible impact on the gallium anomaly.
- Unpublished half-life measurements from other detectors: 10.43±0.30 d, 10.91±0.91 d, 11.57±2.66 d, and 11.80±0.05 d (the last considered less reliable).
- The gallium anomaly survives with statistical significance as large as 6σ in some analyses.

## Context
This work directly addresses proposals by Giunti (2022) and Kopp (2022) that a slightly larger $^{71}$Ge half-life (~12.5 d) or a ~10% branching ratio to excited $^{71}$Ga levels could resolve the gallium anomaly observed in the GALLEX, SAGE, and BEST experiments. The measurement also updates the P$_{L}$/P$_{K}$ ratio used in the cross-section calculation of Haxton (1998), finding consistency with the 1971 proportional-counter value and with recent theoretical calculations by Mougeot (2020). The results enable continued interpretation of the gallium anomaly as evidence for sterile neutrino oscillations, while ruling out nuclear-physics explanations based on $^{71}$Ge decay properties.
