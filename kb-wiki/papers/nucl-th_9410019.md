# The Spectral Function for Finite Nuclei in the Local Density Approximation

> The spectral function for finite nuclei is computed within the framework of the Local Density Approximation, starting from nuclear matter spectral functions obtained with a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction. The spectral function is decomposed into a single-particle part and a ''correlated'' part; the latter is treated in the local density approximation. As an application momentum distributions, quasi-particle strengths and overlap functions for valence hole states, and the light-cone momentum distribution in finite nuclei are computed.

## Key claim

The spectral function for finite nuclei can be constructed from nuclear matter spectral functions using the Local Density Approximation, but only after decomposing it into a single-particle part (treated with a generalized mean field) and a correlation part (treated in LDA); the naive global application of LDA to the full spectral function leads to a divergent momentum distribution at low momentum.

## Method

The hole spectral function for finite nuclei is decomposed into a single-particle part, which retains the mean-field wavefunctions and discrete energies of the finite system but replaces the delta-function energy distribution with the nuclear matter spectral function at the local density, and a correlation part, which is taken directly from the nuclear matter spectral function for momenta above the local Fermi momentum. The nuclear matter input comes from correlated basis function (CBF) calculations using the Urbana v14 NN interaction plus a three-nucleon force, at densities 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, and 1.0 times nuclear matter saturation density. For comparison, a parametrization of nuclear matter momentum distributions by Baldo et al. based on Brueckner reaction matrix calculations with the Paris interaction is also used.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Momentum density at zero momentum in nuclear matter: ν(ρ, k≈0) = 0.86, 0.87, 0.86 for ρ = ρ₀, ¾ρ₀, and ½ρ₀ respectively
- Mean-field kinetic energy contribution: approximately 17 MeV, increasing with A
- Correlation part of kinetic energy: approximately 17 MeV, increasing with A
- Mean removal energy <E> increases from 33 MeV in mean-field approximation to about 55 MeV when correlations are included
- Binding energy from Koltun sum rule gives about 2.4 MeV too much binding compared to experiment
- Total quasi-particle strength Z = 0.50 for valence hole shells in ²⁰⁸Pb when surface vibrations are included
- Volume term for kinetic energy: T_vol = 39.38 MeV; surface term: T_surf = 18.87 MeV
- EMC ratio at x=0.60: R_A(x) = 0.83 + 0.18 A⁻¹/³

## Context

This work extends the Local Density Approximation approach previously applied to inclusive electron scattering by Sick, Benhar, and Fabrocini (1990s), using the same nuclear matter input but with a different decomposition of the spectral function. The results agree with Variational Monte-Carlo calculations by Pieper et al. for ¹⁶O up to k=2 fm⁻¹. The method enables computation of momentum distributions, spectroscopic factors, overlap functions, and light-cone momentum distributions for finite nuclei, with applications to understanding the EMC effect in deep-inelastic scattering. The finding that valence overlap functions are hardly affected by short-range correlations agrees with Green function perturbation theory results by Müther and Dickhoff (1990s).
