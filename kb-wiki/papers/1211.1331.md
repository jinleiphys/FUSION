# Transport properties of bottomed mesons in a hot mesonic gas

> In this work we evaluate the B-meson drag and diffusion coefficients in a hot medium constituted of light mesons (pions, kaons and eta mesons). We treat the B-meson and B*-meson interaction with pseudo-Goldstone bosons in chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order within the constraints from heavy quark symmetry, and employ standard unitarization techniques of NLO amplitudes in order to account for dynamically generated resonances (leading to a more efficient heavy-flavor diffusion) and thus reach higher temperatures. We estimate individual meson contributions from the gas to the transport coefficients and perform a comparison with other findings in literature. We report a bottom relaxation length of about 80 fm at a temperature of 150 MeV and for typical momenta of 1 GeV, at which our approach is reliable. Compared to a charm relaxation length of 40 fm in the same conditions, we conclude that the B mesons provide a cleaner probe of the early stages of a heavy-ion collision.

## Key claim

The authors report a bottom relaxation length of about 80 fm at a temperature of 150 MeV and for typical momenta of 1 GeV, but this value is a specific estimate from their unitarized chiral perturbation theory approach; the actual relaxation length extracted from the drag coefficient in the text is approximately 100 fm (1/F ≈ 100 fm at T=150 MeV, p=1 GeV), and the abstract's 80 fm is a rounded figure that also reflects a comparison with a charm relaxation length of 40 fm under the same conditions.

## Method

The authors compute B-meson and B*-meson drag and diffusion coefficients in a hot gas of pions, kaons, and eta mesons using chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order (NLO) with heavy quark symmetry constraints. They unitarize the NLO scattering amplitudes via the Bethe-Salpeter equation to account for dynamically generated resonances (e.g., in the Bπ I=1/2 and BK I=0 channels) and evaluate the transport coefficients from the Fokker-Planck equation using the unitarized isospin-averaged amplitudes. The low-energy constants h1, h3, and h5 are fixed using the B_s-B mass difference and by fitting the B*π scattering amplitude peak to the B1(5721) resonance mass.

## Key numbers

- Bottom relaxation length λ_B ≈ 100 fm at T = 150 MeV and p = 1 GeV (from F ≈ 0.01 fm⁻¹); the abstract reports about 80 fm.
- Charm relaxation length λ_D ≈ 40 fm at T = 150 MeV and p = 1 GeV (from prior work LADCFLEJT:2011).
- Drag coefficient for B mesons is about 3 times smaller than for D mesons at small momenta, consistent with the mass ratio m_B/m_D ≈ 2.8.
- Bπ I=1/2 S-wave scattering length a^{1/2}_{Bπ} = 0.38 fm (m_π a = 0.26); Bπ I=3/2 scattering length a^{3/2}_{Bπ} = -0.11 fm.
- Bπ I=1/2 cross section at threshold is 18.1 mbarn; Bπ I=3/2 cross section at threshold is 1.5 mbarn.
- Resonance mass from Bπ I=1/2 unitarized amplitude: m_{B0} = 5534 MeV.
- Resonance mass from B*π I=1/2 unitarized amplitude at LO: √s ≈ 5580 MeV; at NLO the peak is about 100 MeV below the B1(5721) mass (5723.5 ± 2.0 MeV).
- Low-energy constants: h1 = -1.042 (average), h3 = 2.5, h5 = -0.04 GeV⁻².
- Pion decay constant F = 92 MeV; subtraction constant a(μ) = -3.47 at renormalization scale μ = 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of charmed meson transport in a hot pion gas (LADCFLEJT:2011) to bottomed mesons, using the same unitarized chiral perturbation theory framework. It contrasts with approaches that use perturbative NLO amplitudes (Ghosh 2011) which overestimate diffusion at high temperatures, or scattering-length approximations (Das 2011) which underestimate resonant contributions. The finding that B mesons have a longer relaxation length than D mesons suggests they are a cleaner probe of the early stages of heavy-ion collisions, and the results enable comparisons with heavy-quark diffusion in the quark-gluon plasma phase (Rapp 2008, vanHees 2007).
