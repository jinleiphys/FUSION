# Influence of a Z+(1540) resonance on K+N scattering

> The impact of a (I=0, J^P=1/2^+) Z^+(1540) resonance with a width of 5 MeV or more on the K+N (I=0) elastic cross section and on the P01 phase shift is examined within the KN meson-exchange model of the Juelich group. It is shown that the rather strong enhancement of the cross section caused by the presence of a Z^+ with the above properties is not compatible with the existing empirical information on KN scattering. Only a much narrower Z^+ state could be reconciled with the existing data -- or, alternatively, the Z^+ state must lie at an energy much closer to the KN threshold.

# Influence of a Z+(1540) resonance on K+N scattering

## Key claim
A Z+(1540) resonance with a width of 5 MeV or more produces an enhancement of the K+N (I=0) elastic cross section that is incompatible with existing empirical data; only a much narrower Z+ state (width of order 1 MeV or less) or a state lying much closer to the KN threshold could be reconciled with the data.

## Method
The authors used the Juelich meson-exchange model for the KN interaction, which includes single-meson and baryon exchanges plus higher-order box diagrams involving NK*, Delta K, and Delta K* intermediate states. They added a pole diagram for the Z+(1540) resonance with a bare mass and bare coupling constant, then solved a Lippmann-Schwinger equation in time-ordered perturbation theory to obtain the reaction amplitude, phase shifts, and cross sections. The bare parameters were adjusted to produce physical masses and widths of 5 MeV and 20 MeV for the Z+.

## Key numbers
- The Z+(1540) resonance corresponds to a laboratory momentum of p_lab = 0.44 GeV/c.
- The Juelich model describes KN data up to beam momenta of p_lab approximately 1 GeV/c.
- A Z+ with a width of 5 MeV or more produces a cross section enhancement incompatible with data; the analysis of Arndt et al. suggests a width of Gamma = 1 MeV or less would be compatible.
- The lowest available KN data point is at p_lab = 0.336 GeV/c, leaving room for a Z+ at energies much closer to the KN threshold.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Nussinov (2003), who used K+d scattering data to restrict the Z+ width to less than 6 MeV, and the partial-wave analysis of Arndt et al. (2003), who found that a Z+ around 1540 MeV should have a width of 1 MeV or less to be compatible with KN and K+d data. The Juelich model provides a consistent framework where the non-pole background is constrained by fits to all KN partial waves simultaneously, unlike simpler analyses. The results contradict the experimental claims from the LEPS, ITEP, JLab, and ELSA collaborations that reported a Z+ with a width around 20 MeV, and also challenge the chiral quark-soliton model prediction of Diakonov et al. (1997) for a 15 MeV width. The work enables future experimental re-measurements of KN scattering around the Z+(1540) energy region with modern accelerators and detectors.
