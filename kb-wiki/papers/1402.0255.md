# Study of freeze-out dynamics in STAR at RHIC Beam Energy Scan Program

> The STAR detector at RHIC, due to its large uniform acceptance and excellent particle identification capabilities, has measured a variety of hadron species ($π^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$, $p$, $\bar{p}$, $K^{0}_{S}$, $Λ$, $\barΛ$, $Ξ^{-}$, $\barΞ^{+}$) produced in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt {s_{NN}}= 7.7$, 11.5, 27 and 39 GeV. These data are part of the Beam Energy Scan (BES) program at RHIC and provide an opportunity to measure the yields and transverse momentum spectra ($p_{T}$) of the particles produced in the collisions. The corresponding measurements allow to study the freeze-out properties and dynamics of heavy ion collisions. A statistical thermal model analysis of particle production in BES energies in both grand canonical and strangeness canonical ensembles, is used to extract the chemical freeze-out parameters. The $p_{T}$ spectra, particle ratios, and the energy and centrality dependence of freeze-out parameters determined from the thermal fit of particle ratios are discussed.

## Key claim

The chemical freeze-out parameters extracted from a statistical thermal model fit to particle ratios in Au+Au collisions at BES energies show a centrality dependence at lower collision energies, a feature not observed at higher energies such as Au+Au 200 GeV.

## Method

The STAR detector measured yields and transverse momentum spectra of nine hadron species (π±, K±, p, p̄, K⁰S, Λ, Λ̄, Ξ⁻, Ξ̄⁺) in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 7.7, 11.5, 27, and 39 GeV. Mid-rapidity particle ratios were fitted using the THERMUS statistical thermal model in both grand canonical ensemble (GCE) and strangeness canonical ensemble (SCE) to extract chemical freeze-out temperature (Tch), baryon chemical potential (μB), strangeness chemical potential (μS), and strangeness saturation factor (γS).

## Key numbers

- For 0–5% centrality Au+Au at √sNN = 27 GeV, the thermal model fit to particle ratios yields χ²/d.o.f = 23.8/6.
- The baryon chemical potential (μB) range extracted from BES measurements extends from 20 to 400 MeV across the QCD phase diagram.
- The π⁻/π⁺ ratio at lower BES energies has values larger than unity, attributed to resonance decays such as from Δ baryons.
- The K⁻/K⁺ ratio approaches unity as collision energy increases, indicating dominance of kaon-pair production at higher energies.

## Context

This work extends the Beam Energy Scan (BES) program at RHIC, which aims to explore the QCD phase diagram and search for the QCD critical point. The centrality dependence of chemical freeze-out parameters observed at lower BES energies contrasts with results from Au+Au 200 GeV (reported in Ref. [10]), where no such centrality dependence was seen. The measurements enable further quantitative analysis of freeze-out dynamics using different statistical ensembles, with ongoing studies using particle yields for all BES energies.
