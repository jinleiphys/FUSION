# Universal Correlations in Pion-less EFT with the Resonating Group Model: Three, Four, and Six Nucleons

> A systematic connection between QCD and nuclear few- and many-body properties in the form of the Effective Field Theory "without pions" is applied to $A\le 6$ nuclei to determine its range of applicability. We present results at next-to-leading order for the Tjon correlation and for a correlation between the singlet S-wave $^3$He-neutron scattering length and the triton binding energy. In the A=6 sector we performed leading order calculations for the binding energy and the charge and matter radii of the halo nucleus $^6$He. Also at leading order, the doublet S-wave 4-He-neutron phase shifts are compared with R-matrix data. These analysis provide evidence for a sufficiently fast convergence of the effective field theory, in particular, our results in $A\le 4$ predict an expansion parameter of about 1/3, and they converge to data within the predicted uncertainty band at this order. A properly adjusted three-body contact force which we include together with the Coulomb interaction in all calculations is found to correctly renormalize the pion-less theory at leading- and next-to-leading order, i.e. the power counting does not require four-body forces at the respective order.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that pion-less Effective Field Theory (EFT$_\slashed{\pi}$) converges sufficiently fast for nuclei up to A=6, with an expansion parameter of about 1/3 in the A≤4 sector, and that no four-body contact force is required at next-to-leading order (NLO) to renormalize the four-nucleon system.

## Method

The authors solved the many-body Schrödinger equation using the Refined Resonating Group Model (RRGM) in coordinate space, employing a NLO EFT$_\slashed{\pi}$ potential consisting of nine four-nucleon contact terms and one six-nucleon vertex, plus a three-body contact force and Coulomb interaction. Nine different NLO potentials were fitted to the deuteron binding energy and neutron-proton phase shifts below 0.3 MeV from the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis, with the three-body parameter fixed to the triton binding energy. Renormalization scheme dependence was tested by varying the Gaussian regulator cutoff Λ between 400 and 700 MeV and by varying the P-wave phase shift input data.

## Key numbers

- Expansion parameter of EFT$_\slashed{\pi}$ in A≤4: about 1/3
- NLO prediction for $^4$He binding energy: $B(^4\text{He})^{\text{NLO}} = (28 \pm 2.5)\text{ MeV}$, consistent with the expected 10% NLO uncertainty
- NLO prediction for the real part of the singlet S-wave $^3$He-neutron scattering length: $\text{Re}\{a_0(^3\text{He-n})\}^{\text{NLO}} = (7.5 \pm 0.6)\text{ fm}$
- LO results for $^6$He: charge radius $\langle \vec{r}^2\rangle_{\text{ch}}^{1/2} = 3.261\text{ fm}$, matter radius $\langle \vec{r}^2\rangle_{\text{m}}^{1/2} = 5.678\text{ fm}$
- $^4$He binding energy datum: $B(^4\text{He}) = 28.5\text{ MeV}$
- Cutoff variation range for renormalization scheme tests: $\Lambda \in [400; 700]\text{ MeV}$

## Context

This work extends the application of pion-less EFT beyond the well-established two- and three-nucleon sector (see reviews by the authors' group) to A=4, 5, and 6 nuclei, addressing the question of whether the theory converges for systems where the typical momentum scale is comparable to the pion mass. The results confirm and refine the earlier LO Tjon-line analysis by Platter et al. (2005) and show that the four-nucleon bound state is a universal consequence of the three-body system, with no four-body force needed at NLO. The preliminary $^6$He results suggest that the halo structure is not solely a consequence of unnaturally large two-body scattering lengths but also depends on three-body properties, enabling future systematic studies of halo nuclei and applications to bosonic atomic gases.
