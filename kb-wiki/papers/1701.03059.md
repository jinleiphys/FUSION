# On the microscopic structure of $πNN$, $πNΔ$ and $πΔΔ$ vertices

> We use a hybrid constituent-quark model for the microscopic description of $πN N$, $πN Δ$ and $πΔΔ$ vertices. In this model quarks are confined by an instantaneous potential and are allowed to emit and absorb a pion, which is also treated as dynamical degree of freedom. The point form of relativistic quantum mechanics is employed to achieve a relativistically invariant description of this system. Starting with an $SU(6)$ spin-flavor symmetric wave function for $N_0$ and $Δ_0$, i.e. the eigenstates of the pure confinement problem, we calculate the strength of the $πN_0 N_0$, $πN_0 Δ_0$ and $πΔ_0 Δ_0$ couplings and the corresponding vertex form factors. Interestingly the ratios of the resulting couplings resemble strongly those needed in purely hadronic coupled-channel models, but deviate significantly from the ratios following from SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry in the non-relativistic constituent-quark model.

# On the microscopic structure of πNN, πNΔ and πΔΔ vertices

## Key claim

The ratios of the bare πN₀N₀, πN₀Δ₀, and πΔ₀Δ₀ coupling strengths calculated within a relativistic point-form constituent-quark model resemble those needed in purely hadronic coupled-channel models, but deviate significantly from the SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry predictions of the non-relativistic constituent-quark model, particularly for the πΔ₀Δ₀ vertex.

## Method

The authors use a hybrid constituent-quark model where three quarks are confined by an instantaneous potential and can emit and absorb a pion as a dynamical degree of freedom, with relativistic invariance achieved via point-form relativistic quantum mechanics and the Bakamjian-Thomas construction. The mass eigenvalue problem is formulated as a two-channel system coupling the 3q and 3qπ Fock components, and after eliminating the 3qπ channel, the resulting optical potential for pion emission and reabsorption is expressed in terms of matrix elements between bare baryon states N₀ and Δ₀. The momentum part of the 3q wave function for N₀ and Δ₀ is parameterized by a Gaussian with oscillator parameter α, and the model parameters (M₀, α, fπqq) are tuned to reproduce the physical nucleon and Δ masses (mN = 0.9385 GeV, mΔ = 1.233 GeV) via mass renormalization from pion loops.

## Key numbers

- Physical nucleon mass: mN = 0.9385 GeV; physical Δ mass: mΔ = 1.233 GeV (used as fit targets)
- Bare baryon mass (degenerate N₀ and Δ₀): M₀ = 1.350 GeV
- Oscillator parameter: α = 2.915 GeV⁻¹
- Constituent quark mass: mq = 263 MeV (fixed from Pasquini (2007))
- πqq coupling strength: fπqq = 0.6602
- Bare coupling constants: fπN₀N₀ = 1.0027, fπN₀Δ₀ = 1.2123, fπΔ₀Δ₀ = 0.6097
- Ratio of bare couplings: fπN₀Δ₀ : fπN₀N₀ : fπΔ₀Δ₀ = 1.209 : 1 : 0.608
- Normalization point for πN₀Δ₀ form factors: Q₀² = -0.079 GeV² (close to the pion pole Q² = -mπ²)

## Context

This work extends the constituent-quark model approach to meson-baryon vertices by treating pions as dynamical degrees of freedom within a relativistic point-form framework, building on earlier work by Biernat (2010) and Kupelwieser (2016). The calculated bare coupling ratios differ markedly from the non-relativistic SU(6) prediction of Brown (1975) (fπNΔ : fπNN : fπΔΔ = 1.13 : 1 : 1.8) but agree well with the bare couplings needed in the dynamical coupled-channel model of Kamano (2013) (1.26 : 1 : 0.42). The results enable self-consistent calculations of electromagnetic form factors for physical nucleons and Deltas where strong and electromagnetic vertices are treated on the same footing, with pion-loop effects expected to be significant for Q² ≲ 0.5 GeV², particularly for Δ and N→Δ transition form factors.
