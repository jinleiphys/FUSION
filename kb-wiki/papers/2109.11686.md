# Revealing pion and kaon structure via generalised parton distributions

> Clear windows onto emergent hadron mass (EHM) and modulations thereof by Higgs boson interactions are provided by observable measures of pion and kaon structure, many of which are accessible via generalised parton distributions (GPDs). Beginning with algebraic GPD Ansaetze, constrained entirely by hadron-scale $π$ and $K$ valence-parton distribution functions (DFs), in whose forms both EHM and Higgs boson influences are manifest, numerous illustrations are provided. They include the properties of electromagnetic form factors, impact parameter space GPDs, gravitational form factors and associated pressure profiles, and the character and consequences of all-orders evolution. The analyses predict that mass-squared gravitational form factors are stiffer than electromagnetic form factors; reveal that $K$ pressure profiles are tighter than $π$ profiles, with both mesons sustaining near-core pressures at magnitudes similar to that expected at the core of neutron stars; deliver parameter-free predictions for $π$ and $K$ valence, glue, and sea GPDs at the resolving scale $ζ=2$GeV; and predict that at this scale the fraction of meson mass-squared carried by glue and sea combined matches that lodged with the valence degrees-of-freedom, with a similar statement holding for mass-squared radii.

## Key claim

Using algebraic GPD Ansätze constrained entirely by hadron-scale valence-parton distribution functions, the authors predict that pion and kaon structure measures reveal emergent hadron mass (EHM) and its modulation by Higgs boson interactions, with the caveat that the predictions rely on factorised light-front wave function approximations whose accuracy typically exceeds the precision of foreseeable experiments.

## Method

The authors constructed algebraic models for pion and kaon light-front wave functions (LFWFs) using perturbation theory integral representations (PTIRs) and factorised representations, constrained by hadron-scale valence-parton distribution functions (DFs) from continuum analyses. They employed the overlap representation to define generalised parton distributions (GPDs) on the DGLAP domain, extended these to the ERBL domain using a Radon transform scheme, and evolved the hadron-scale distributions to ζ=2 GeV using QCD's process-independent effective charge to integrate DGLAP/ERBL evolution equations.

- Dressed u-quark mass scale M_u = 0.31 GeV and dressed s-quark mass scale M_s = 1.2 × M_u used in the kaon spectral representation
- At resolving scale ζ=2 GeV, the fraction of meson mass-squared carried by glue and sea combined matches that lodged with valence degrees-of-freedom

## Context

This work extends and modernises the studies of Zhang (2020) and Zhang (2021) on meson GPDs, building upon continuum and lattice analyses that generated coherent predictions for pion and kaon DAs and DFs as summarised by Roberts (2021). The predictions for meson gravitational form factors, pressure profiles, and parton mass-squared fractions at ζ=2 GeV enable future comparisons with experimental data from JLab 12 GeV and the Electron-Ion Collider, and provide parameter-free predictions for valence, glue, and sea GPDs that can be tested against empirical extractions.
