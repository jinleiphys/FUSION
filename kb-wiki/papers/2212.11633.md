# Non-Hermitian topological Fermi superfluid near the $p$-wave unitary limit

> We theoretically discuss the non-Hermitian superfluid phase transition in one-dimensional two-component Fermi gases near the $p$-wave Feshbach resonance accompanied by the two-body loss associated with the dipolar relaxation. For the first time we point out that this system gives us an opportunity to explore the interplay among various non-trivial properties such as universal thermodynamics at divergent $p$-wave scattering length, topological phase transition at vanishing chemical potential, and non-Hermitian Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer(BCS) to Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) transition, in a unified manner. In the BCS phase, the loss-induced superfluid-normal transition occurs when the exceptional point appears in the effective non-Hermitian Hamiltonian. In the BEC phase, the diffusive gapless mode can be regarded as a precursor of the instability of the superfluid state. Moreover, we show that the superfluid state is fragile against the two-body loss near the topological phase transition point.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a one-dimensional two-component Fermi gas near a p-wave Feshbach resonance, the superfluid state becomes unstable due to two-body loss when a gapless mode exists, and is particularly fragile near the topological phase transition point at inverse scattering length (k_F a_R)^{-1} = 4/pi, contrary to the smooth BCS-BEC crossover expected in the Hermitian case.

## Method

The authors develop a non-Hermitian BCS-Leggett mean-field theory for a one-dimensional two-component Fermi gas with complex p-wave interaction, where the imaginary part of the coupling constant represents two-body loss from dipolar relaxation. They solve the complexified gap equation and number equation self-consistently to obtain the chemical potential and superfluid order parameter as functions of the real and imaginary parts of the inverse p-wave scattering length, and identify phase boundaries by the appearance of exceptional points in the BCS phase and diffusive gapless modes in the BEC phase.

## Key numbers

- The topological phase transition in the Hermitian limit occurs at (k_F a_R)^{-1} = 4/pi approximately equal to 1.27, where the chemical potential changes sign.
- At the p-wave unitary limit with zero two-body loss, the Bertsch parameter (real chemical potential divided by Fermi energy) is xi_B approximately equal to 0.75, which increases with loss as xi_B approximately equal to 0.75 + 0.22 (k_F a_I)^{-2}.
- In the weak-coupling BCS regime at (k_F a_R)^{-1} = -1, the superfluid solution disappears at (k_F a_I)^{-1} approximately equal to 0.9 due to the exceptional point.
- The excitation gap E_gap becomes zero at the phase boundaries, marking the appearance of the exceptional point in the BCS phase and the diffusive gapless mode in the BEC phase.

## Context

This work extends the non-Hermitian BCS theory previously developed for s-wave pairing by PhysRevLett.123.123601 and PhysRevA.103.013724 to the p-wave case in one dimension, and builds on the Hermitian treatment of one-dimensional p-wave Fermi gases by PhysRevB.105.064508 which identified the topological phase transition. The results contradict the expectation of a smooth BCS-BEC crossover when two-body loss is present, and suggest that experimental realizations of p-wave superfluid states should avoid the topological phase transition point. The work also connects to measurements of complex p-wave scattering volumes in three dimensions by PhysRevA.96.062704 and to proposals for loss suppression in one-dimensional confinement by PhysRevA.95.032710.
