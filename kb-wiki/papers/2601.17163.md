# Degenerate coupled-cluster theory

> A size-extensive, converging, black-box, ab initio coupled-cluster ($Δ$CC) ansatz is introduced that computes the energies and wave functions of stationary states from any degenerate or nondegenerate Slater-determinant references with any numbers of $α$- and $β$-spin electrons, any patterns of orbital occupancy, any spin multiplicities, and any spatial symmetries. For a nondegenerate reference, it reduces to the single-reference coupled-cluster ansatz. For a degenerate multireference, it is a natural coupled-cluster extension of degenerate Rayleigh-Schrödinger perturbation ($Δ$MP) theory. For ionized and electron-attached references, it can be viewed as a coupled-cluster Green's function, although the present theory is convergent toward the full-configuration-interaction (FCI) limits, while Feynman-Dyson many-body Green's function (MBGF) theory generally is not. Additionally, a new state-universal multireference coupled-cluster theory for general model spaces is developed by slightly modifying the $Δ$CC ansatz. This quasidegenerate coupled-cluster (QCC) theory is size-extensive, converging, but not black-box, which is expected to be well suited for strong correlation. Determinant-based, general-order algorithms of $Δ$CC and QCC theories are implemented, which are compared with configuration-interaction (CI) and equation-of-motion coupled-cluster (EOM-CC) theories through octuple excitations and with $Δ$MP and MBGF theories up to the nineteenth order. For transition energies, the order of performance is: QCC $\approx$ $Δ$CC $>$ EOM-CC $>$ CI at the same excitation order or QCC $\approx$ $Δ$CC $>$ $Δ$MP $>$ MBGF at the same cost scaling.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a size-extensive, converging, black-box coupled-cluster ansatz (ΔCC) that can treat any degenerate or nondegenerate Slater-determinant reference, but the abstract's claim of "black-box" operation is qualified by the fact that the quasidegenerate coupled-cluster (QCC) variant, which is better suited for strong correlation, is explicitly not black-box, and the ΔCC method's black-box nature depends on the user specifying only the nuclear-repulsion energy, degenerate reference determinants, and truncation rank of the excitation operators.

## Method

The ΔCC ansatz generalizes single-reference coupled-cluster theory by assigning each degenerate reference determinant its own exponential excitation operator \(e^{\hat{T}_I}\), then solving a set of projection equations that couple all degenerate references through a matrix form of the energy. The T-amplitudes in the external space are determined by solving the Schrödinger equation projected onto the space of determinants reachable by \((1+\hat{T}_I)|I\rangle\), while the internal-space amplitudes are fixed by enforcing orthonormality of the exponential wave functions (the "C condition"), after which the energy matrix is diagonalized to obtain state energies. Determinant-based, general-order algorithms were implemented for both ΔCC and QCC theories, and algebraic optimal-scaling algorithms were computer-synthesized at the ΔCCS and ΔCCSD levels using the Tensor Contraction Engine.

## Key numbers

- ΔCCSD has optimal \(O(n^6)\) operation cost scaling with the number of spin-orbitals \(n\); ΔCCS has \(O(n^4)\) scaling.
- Comparisons were carried out through octuple excitations for CI and EOM-CC, and up to the nineteenth order for ΔMP and MBGF.
- The order of performance for transition energies is: QCC ≈ ΔCC > EOM-CC > CI at the same excitation order, or QCC ≈ ΔCC > ΔMP > MBGF at the same cost scaling.

## Context

This work extends the single-reference coupled-cluster hierarchy (CCSD, CCSDT, CCSDTQ, etc.) to treat degenerate and nondegenerate references on an equal footing, directly addressing the long-standing question of how to apply CC theory to excited states without relying on the equation-of-motion formalism (EOM-CC) of Monkhorst (1977) and Harris (1977). It is the natural coupled-cluster extension of degenerate Møller–Plesset perturbation theory (ΔMP) of Hirata (2017, 2021), and is related to the two-determinant coupled-cluster method of Balková and coworkers (1991–1994) and the state-universal multireference coupled-cluster theory of Li and Paldus (2003). The new QCC variant is intended for strong-correlation problems where a non-black-box method is acceptable, while ΔCC itself enables black-box treatment of ionized, electron-attached, and excited states with any spin multiplicity or spatial symmetry.
