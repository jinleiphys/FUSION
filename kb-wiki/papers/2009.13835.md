# Novel feature of doubly bubble nuclei in 50$\leq$Z(N)$\leq$82 region along with magicity and weakly bound structure

> In this work, we identify a unique and novel feature of central density depletion in both proton and neutron named as doubly bubble nuclei in 50$\leq$Z(N)$\leq$82 region. The major role of 2d-3s single-particle (s.p.) states in the existence of halo and bubble nuclei is probed. The occupancy in s.p. state 3s$_{1/2}$ leads to the extended neutron density distribution or halo while the unoccupancy results in the central density depletion. By employing the Relativistic Mean-Field (RMF) approach along with NL3* parameter, the separation energies, single-particle energies, pairing energies, proton, and neutron density profiles along with deformations of even-even nuclei are investigated. Our results are in concise with few other theories and available experimental data. Emergence on new shell closure and the magicity of conventional shell closures are explored systematically in this yet unknown region.

## Key claim
The paper identifies a novel class of "doubly bubble nuclei" in the 50 ≤ Z(N) ≤ 82 region, where both proton and neutron central densities are depleted. The key condition is that the 3s₁/₂ single-particle state must be unoccupied for both protons and neutrons, and the claim is hedged by noting that the depletion fraction analysis provides only preliminary hints of magicity that require further investigation.

## Method
The authors employed the Relativistic Mean-Field (RMF) approach with the NL3* parameter set, combined with a state-dependent BCS pairing calculation using a delta force with strength V₀ = 350 MeV fm³. They also used the triaxially deformed Nilsson-Strutinsky (NS) model for comparison. Ground-state properties including binding energies, separation energies, pairing energies, single-particle energies, deformations, and density distributions were calculated for even-even nuclei in the 50 ≤ Z(N) ≤ 82 region.

## Key numbers
- Two new magic numbers identified: Z = 58 (proton sub-shell closure) and N = 70 (neutron magic number)
- Single-particle energy gap for N = 70 shell closure reaches a maximum of 5.6 MeV for Z = 28 (⁹⁸Ni)
- N = 50 shell gap decreases from 7 MeV (at ¹⁰⁰Sn) to 2 MeV (at ⁷⁰Ca) as one moves toward the proton-deficient side
- N = 82 shell gap remains significantly large around 4-7 MeV across the isotonic chain
- Depletion fraction (DF) = (ρ_max - ρ_c)/ρ_max is used to quantify central density depletion
- Doubly bubble nuclei identified: ¹⁰⁰₅₀Sn, ¹³⁴₆₄Gd, and ¹⁹⁶₇₀Yb
- Proton DF is always larger than neutron DF in the 50 ≤ Z(N) ≤ 82 region, attributed to Coulomb repulsion

## Context
This work extends previous studies of bubble and halo phenomena from the sd-shell region (e.g., ³⁴Si studied by Nature (2019) and others) into the heavier 2d-3s shell region between magic numbers 50 and 82. It builds on prior RMF calculations by Saxena et al. (various years) for bubble structure and magicity, and on studies of giant halos in Ca isotopes by Terasaki et al. and Kaushik et al. The identification of new magic numbers at Z = 58 and N = 70 follows earlier suggestions by Nica (2007), Nakada (2014), and Madhu (2005). The work enables future experimental searches for doubly bubble nuclei and further systematic studies of the interplay between occupancy of low-angular-momentum states and central density depletion.
