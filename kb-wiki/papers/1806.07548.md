# Analytical description of the excited state phase transition to octupole deformed shape in alternating parity bands

> Angular momentum dependences of the parity splitting and electric dipole transitions in the alternating parity bands of heavy nuclei have been analyzed. It is shown that these dependences can be treated in a universal way with a single parameter of critical angular momentum, which characterizes phase transition from octupole vibrations to the stable octupole deformation. Using the simple but useful model of axially-symmetric reflection-asymmetric mode, the analytical expression for the parity splitting and electric dipole transitional moment have been obtained. The results obtained are in a good agreement with the experimental data for various isotopes of Ra, Th, U, and Pu.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the angular momentum dependence of parity splitting and electric dipole transitions in alternating parity bands of heavy nuclei can be described by a universal function depending on a single parameter, the critical angular momentum \(I_{\text{crit}}\), which characterizes the phase transition from octupole vibrations to stable octupole deformation; the authors note that the model is most reliable for well-deformed nuclei and that deviations at high angular momentum may arise from centrifugal stretching or Coriolis coupling to non-axially symmetric modes not included in the model.

## Method

The authors use an axially-symmetric reflection-asymmetric collective model with a Hamiltonian for the \(\beta_{30}\) degree of freedom, where the positive-parity ground state wave function is approximated as a superposition of two Gaussians centered at \(\pm\beta_m(I)\). The parity splitting \(\Delta E(I)\) is expressed as \(\hbar\omega(I) f(\xi_I)\), where \(\xi_I = \sqrt{B\omega(I)/\hbar}\,\beta_m(I)\) is a dimensionless parameter, and the universal function \(f(\xi)\) is obtained analytically with an approximate closed form fitted to numerical diagonalization. The angular momentum dependence of \(\xi\) is taken as \(\xi(I) = cI\), with the constant \(c\) related to the critical angular momentum by \(c I_{\text{crit}} = 1/\sqrt{2}\), and \(\hbar\omega\) is set equal to the experimental parity splitting at zero angular momentum \(\Delta E_{\text{exp}}(0)\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Jolos (2012), who first defined a critical angular momentum for the octupole phase transition in actinides, by providing a fully analytical description of both parity splitting and electric dipole transition probabilities. The model uses a simple Gaussian ansatz for the collective wave function, similar in spirit to the cluster approach of Shneidman (2003) and the octupole model of Nazmitdinov (1987), but achieves a universal parametrization with only two parameters (\(\Delta E(0)\) and \(I_{\text{crit}}\)) that can be fitted to experimental data for Ra, Th, U, and Pu isotopes. The results enable systematic predictions of octupole correlations in heavy nuclei and can be used to extract critical angular momenta from experimental spectra, with the next step being microscopic calculation of these parameters from mean-field models such as those of Robledo (2011) or Zhou (2016).
