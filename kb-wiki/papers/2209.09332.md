# Large-Momentum Effective Theory vs. Short-Distance Operator Expansion: Contrast and Complementarity

> Although equivalent in the infinite-momentum limit, large-momentum effective theory (LaMET) and short-distance operator product expansion (SD-OPE) are two different approaches to extract parton distribution functions (PDFs) from coordinate-space correlation functions in large-momentum hadrons. LaMET implements a momentum-space expansion in $Λ_{\rm QCD}/[x(1-x)P^z]$ to directly calculate PDFs $f(x)$ in a middle region of Bjorken $x\in [x_{\rm min}\sim Λ_{\rm QCD}/P^z, x_{\rm max}\sim 1-x_{\min}]$. SD-OPE applies perturbative QCD at small Euclidean distances $z$ to extract a range $[0,λ_{\rm max}]$ of leading-twist correlations, $h(λ=zP^z)$, corresponding to the Fourier transformation of PDFs. Similar to the quantum mechanical uncertainty principle, an incomplete leading-twist correlation cannot be readily converted to a momentum-space local distribution, and the methods to solve the ``inverse problem'' involve essentially modelling of the missing information beyond $λ_{\rm max}$. On the other hand, short-distance correlations, along with the expected end-point asymptotics, can be used to phenomenologically fit the PDFs in the LaMET-complementary regions: $x\in [0,x_{\rm min}]$ and $[x_{\rm max}, 1]$. We use the recent results of the pion valence quark distribution from the ANL/BNL collaboration to demonstrate this point.

## Key claim

Although LaMET and SD-OPE are equivalent in the infinite-momentum limit, they provide two different approximation schemes at finite hadron momenta: LaMET directly calculates PDFs in a middle region of Bjorken x via a momentum-space expansion, while SD-OPE extracts only a finite range of leading-twist correlations that cannot be readily converted to a local x-space distribution without additional modeling.

## Method

The paper contrasts the two approaches by analyzing their respective systematic expansions: LaMET implements a power counting in Λ_QCD/[x(1-x)P^z] to compute PDFs f(x) directly in the region x ∈ [x_min ∼ Λ_QCD/P^z, x_max ∼ 1-x_min], while SD-OPE applies perturbative QCD at small Euclidean distances z to extract leading-twist correlations h(λ=zP^z) over a range [0, λ_max]. The author examines the breakdown scales of each expansion using perturbative QCD matching, renormalon analysis, and comparisons with lattice data for the heavy-quark potential, hadron correlators, quasi-PDF correlators, and the QCD soft function.

## Key numbers

- LaMET expansion valid for x ∈ [x_min ∼ Λ_QCD/P^z, x_max ∼ 1-x_min]; with P^z around 2.4 GeV, fixed-order perturbative matching is accurate down to x ∼ 0.15-0.2
- SD-OPE can extract twist-2 correlation h(λ) up to λ_max = zP^z ∼ 3 for P^z = 3 GeV
- Perturbative QCD and twist-expansion break down at z ∼ 0.3-0.4 fm; to keep higher-twist contributions at 10% or less, distance must be smaller than 0.2 fm
- Gluon condensate scale from first-principles determination: (0.53 GeV)^4, indicating breakdown of twist expansion at distance scale ∼ 0.4 fm
- Average instanton size in QCD vacuum: about 0.3 fm
- Abelian gluons acquire effective mass of order 0.36 GeV, with non-perturbative effects reaching 50% at distance scale about 0.4 fm
- Quasi-PDF correlators match NLO perturbation theory up to 0.25 fm, beyond which deviations occur
- QCD soft function perturbative expansion breaks down for transverse distance b larger than 0.3 fm

## Context

This paper extends the theoretical comparison between LaMET, developed by Ji (2013), and SD-OPE approaches to extracting PDFs from lattice QCD. It builds on the observation by Izubuchi et al. (2018) that the two methods are equivalent in the infinite-momentum limit but differ at finite momenta. The work uses the ANL/BNL collaboration's pion valence quark distribution calculations (Gao et al., 2020, 2021) as a demonstration case. The paper argues that LaMET results should provide the key constraints on PDFs in the middle-x region, while short-distance correlations and known end-point asymptotics can be used to phenomenologically bridge the gaps in the end-point regions where LaMET's expansion breaks down. This suggests a complementary strategy where SD-OPE data is re-purposed to constrain the x regions inaccessible to LaMET, rather than being used as a standalone method for determining x-dependent PDFs.
