# Reconstructing the nucleon-nucleon potential by a new coupled-channel inversion method

> A second-order supersymmetric transformation is presented, for the two-channel Schrödinger equation with equal thresholds. It adds a Breit-Wigner term to the mixing parameter, without modifying the eigenphase shifts, and modifies the potential matrix analytically. The iteration of a few such transformations allows a precise fit of realistic mixing parameters in terms of a Pade expansion of both the scattering matrix and the effective-range function. The method is applied to build an exactly-solvable potential for the neutron-proton $^3S_1$-$^3D_1$ case.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new coupled-channel inversion method that can reconstruct a nucleon-nucleon potential from scattering data by separating the problem into independent fits of eigenphase shifts (via standard single-channel supersymmetric transformations) and of the mixing parameter (via a new eigenphase-preserving transformation), overcoming a previous restriction that the mixing parameter must be an odd function of energy.

## Method

The method first fits experimental eigenphase shifts for each channel separately using single-channel supersymmetric quantum mechanics, which expresses the S-matrix as a product of poles and yields an analytic potential via a Wronskian determinant. Then, a new second-order supersymmetric transformation (eigenphase-preserving transformation) is applied iteratively to modify only the mixing parameter by adding Breit-Wigner terms, without changing the eigenphase shifts; a final constant rotation ensures the mixing parameter vanishes at zero energy and removes spurious \(r^{-2}\) off-diagonal terms. The transformation is applied to the neutron-proton \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) coupled channels using the Reid93 potential as input data.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy reproduced: \(B_d = 2.2245\) MeV (fixed by the pole \(s_1 = 0.23154\) fm\(^{-1}\)).
- Five S-matrix poles for the \(^3S_1\) channel: \(s_m = 0.23154\), \(-0.45146\), \(0.43654\), \(1.6818\), \(2.3106\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- Five S-matrix poles for the \(^3D_1\) channel: \(d_m = -0.36719\), \(-0.54420\), \(0.34828\), \(0.71766\), \(3.3758\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- Asymptotic normalization constant for the \(s\)-wave inversion potential: \(A_{s;5} = 0.8854\) fm\(^{-1/2}\).
- Ratio of ANCs for the Reid93 potential: \(\eta = A_d/A_s = 0.0251\).
- Slope of the mixing parameter at zero energy: \(\epsilon'(0) = 0.297\) MeV\(^{-1}\).
- For \(P=1\) transformation: \(E_{R1} = -0.1463\) fm\(^{-2}\), \(E_{I1} = 6.372 \times 10^{-3}\) fm\(^{-2}\).
- Deuteron \(d\)-wave probability for Reid93: \(P_d = 5.699\%\); for \(P=3\) inversion potential: \(P_d = 5.73\%\).

## Context

This work extends the single-channel supersymmetric inversion method of Sparenberg (1997) and Samsonov (2003) to the two-channel case with equal thresholds, and subsumes earlier coupled-channel integral-equation approaches by Newton and Fulton (1957) and Kohlhoff and von Geramb (1993). The method enables the construction of simple, exactly-solvable matrix potentials directly from scattering-matrix poles, which could be used in phase-shift analysis of experimental data to directly provide the corresponding potential.
