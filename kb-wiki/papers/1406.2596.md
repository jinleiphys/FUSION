# Impact of neutrino flavor oscillations on the neutrino-driven wind nucleosynthesis of an electron-capture supernova

> Neutrino oscillations, especially to light sterile states, can affect the nucleosynthesis yields because of their possible feedback effect on the electron fraction (Ye). For the first time, we perform nucleosynthesis calculations for neutrino-driven wind trajectories from the neutrino-cooling phase of an 8.8 Msun electron-capture supernova, whose hydrodynamic evolution was computed in spherical symmetry with sophisticated neutrino transport and whose Ye evolution was post-processed by including neutrino oscillations both between active and active-sterile flavors. We also take into account the alpha-effect as well as weak magnetism and recoil corrections in the neutrino absorption and emission processes. We observe effects on the Ye evolution which depend in a subtle way on the relative radial positions of the sterile MSW resonances, of collective flavor transformations, and on the formation of alpha particles. For the adopted supernova progenitor, we find that neutrino oscillations, also to a sterile state with eV-mass, do not significantly affect the element formation and in particular cannot make the post-explosion wind outflow neutron rich enough to activate a strong r-process. Our conclusions become even more robust when, in order to mimic equation-of-state dependent corrections due to nucleon potential effects in the dense-medium neutrino opacities, six cases with reduced Ye in the wind are considered. In these cases, despite the conversion of active neutrinos to sterile neutrinos, Ye increases or is not significantly lowered compared to the values obtained without oscillations and active flavor transformations. This is a consequence of a complicated interplay between sterile-neutrino production, neutrino-neutrino interactions, and alpha-effect.

## Key claim

For the first time, nucleosynthesis calculations are performed for neutrino-driven wind trajectories from the neutrino-cooling phase of an 8.8 solar mass electron-capture supernova, including neutrino oscillations between active and active-sterile flavors. The authors find that neutrino oscillations, including to an eV-mass sterile state, do not significantly affect element formation and cannot make the post-explosion wind outflow neutron rich enough to activate a strong r-process, with this conclusion becoming more robust when six cases with reduced electron fraction are considered.

## Method

The authors used one-dimensional long-time simulations of an 8.8 solar mass electron-capture supernova progenitor (Model Sf 21 from Huedepohl et al. 2010), following the proto-neutron star cooling for approximately 10 seconds. They post-processed the electron fraction evolution by including neutrino oscillations both between active flavors and active-sterile flavors, incorporating the alpha-effect as well as weak magnetism and recoil corrections in neutrino absorption and emission processes. Nucleosynthesis calculations were performed using a network of 6300 species between the proton-drip line and neutron-drip line up to Z=110 isotopes, using 98 ejecta trajectories with 7 representative trajectories selected for detailed oscillation analysis.

## Key numbers

- Progenitor mass: 8.8 solar masses (electron-capture supernova)
- Total ejecta mass of the 98 mass-shell trajectories: 1.1 x 10^{-2} solar masses
- Postbounce times for representative trajectories: 0.5, 1, 2, 2.9, 4.5, 6.5, and 7.5 seconds
- Neutrinosphere radii range from 25.0 x 10^5 cm at 0.5 seconds to 14.5 x 10^5 cm at 7.5 seconds
- Asymptotic electron fractions (at r = 3 x 10^7 cm) range from 0.546 at 1.0 second to 0.574 at 4.5 seconds
- Neutrino luminosities for electron neutrinos range from 9.5 x 10^51 erg/s at 0.5 seconds to 0.6 x 10^51 erg/s at 7.5 seconds
- Mean energies for electron neutrinos range from 16.8 MeV at 0.5 seconds to 9.9 MeV at 7.5 seconds
- Electron fraction at the neutrinosphere ranges from 0.0547 at 0.5 seconds to 0.0223 at 7.5 seconds

## Context

This work extends the nucleosynthesis studies of Wanajo (2011) for electron-capture supernovae, which did not support production of elements heavier than mass number approximately 110 in early ejecta, by examining the later neutrino-driven wind phase. The study contradicts earlier proposals by Beun (2006), Keranen (2007), and McLaughlin (1999) that active-sterile neutrino oscillations could favor neutron-rich outflow environments, and extends the work of Wu (2013) on active-sterile mixing in early ejecta by including the full proto-neutron star cooling phase and neutrino-neutrino interactions. The results enable future studies to focus on nucleon potential effects in dense-medium neutrino opacities as a more promising avenue for achieving neutron-rich conditions, as suggested by Roberts (2012) and Martinez-Pinedo (2012).
