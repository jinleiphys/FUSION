# Chiral symmetry breaking and the spin content of the rho and rho' mesons

> Using interpolators with different SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R transformation properties we study the chiral symmetry and spin contents of the rho- and rho'-mesons in lattice simulations with dynamical quarks. A ratio of couplings of the $\qbarγ^iτq$ and $\qbarσ^{0i}τq$ interpolators to a given meson state at different resolution scales tells one about the degree of chiral symmetry breaking in the meson wave function at these scales. Using a Gaussian gauge invariant smearing of the quark fields in the interpolators, we are able to extract the chiral content of mesons up to the infrared resolution of ~1 fm. In the ground state rho meson the chiral symmetry is strongly broken with comparable contributions of both the (0,1) + (1,0) and (1/2,1/2)_b chiral representations with the former being the leading contribution. In contrast, in the rho' meson the degree of chiral symmetry breaking is manifestly smaller and the leading representation is (1/2,1/2)_b. Using a unitary transformation from the chiral basis to the {2S +1}L_J basis, we are able to define and measure the angular momentum content of mesons in the rest frame. This definition is different from the traditional one which uses parton distributions in the infinite momentum frame. The rho meson is practically a 3S_1 state with no obvious trace of a "spin crisis". The rho' meson has a sizeable contribution of the 3D_1 wave, which implies that the rho' meson cannot be considered as a pure radial excitation of the rho meson.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the chiral symmetry breaking and angular momentum content of the rho and rho' mesons can be measured at infrared resolution scales up to ~1 fm using lattice simulations, with the caveat that the definition of angular momentum content used (in the rest frame) differs from the traditional one based on parton distributions in the infinite momentum frame, and that the results depend on the resolution scale of the probe.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with two mass-degenerate dynamical quarks using Chirally Improved fermions and the Luscher-Weisz gauge action on a 16^3 x 32 lattice with lattice spacing ~0.15 fm. They constructed 4x4 and 6x6 cross-correlation matrices using vector (qbar gamma^i tau q) and pseudotensor (qbar sigma^{0i} tau q) interpolators with different Gaussian smearing radii (0.33-0.85 fm) to probe the hadron structure at different resolution scales. The ratio of vector to pseudotensor couplings extracted from the eigenvectors of the generalized eigenvalue problem provides a measure of chiral symmetry breaking, and a unitary transformation connects the chiral basis to the angular momentum basis in the rest frame.

## Key numbers

- The ratio a_V/a_T for the ground state rho meson at infrared scales is within 1.14-1.19
- The ratio a_V/a_T for the rho' meson at infrared scales is within 0.48-0.84
- The rho meson angular momentum content is approximately 0.99 |^3S_1> - 0.1 |^3D_1>
- The rho' meson angular momentum content lower bound is 0.88 |^3S_1> - 0.48 |^3D_1> and upper bound is 0.97 |^3S_1> - 0.25 |^3D_1>
- Lattice spacing values: Set A = 0.1507(17) fm, Set B1 = 0.1500(12) fm, Set B2 = 0.1406(11) fm, Set C = 0.1440(12) fm
- Pion masses: Set A = 526(7) MeV, Set B1 = 469(5) MeV, Set B2 = 296(6) MeV, Set C = 323(5) MeV
- Smearing radii: narrow = 0.33-0.36 fm, wide = 0.66-0.69 fm, ultrawide = 0.81-0.85 fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Glozman, Lang, Limmer 2009) where only two resolution scales were used with a 4x4 correlation matrix, by adding a third smearing radius to create a 6x6 matrix, enabling extrapolation to the ~1 fm scale. The results contradict the traditional "spin crisis" picture from deep inelastic scattering (European Muon Collaboration 1988) by showing that in the rest frame definition, the rho meson spin is carried by its valence quarks. The finding that the rho' meson has significant D-wave admixture and weaker chiral symmetry breaking supports the concept of effective chiral restoration in highly excited hadrons (Glozman 2007) and shows that the rho' cannot be treated as a pure radial excitation of the rho.
