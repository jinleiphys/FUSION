# Mechanisms of the $f_0(980)$ production in the reaction $ŽĆ^-p\toŽĆ^0 ŽĆ^0n$

> The model of the pure one-pion exchange mechanism, which gives a good description of the GAMS results on the alteration of the $S$-wave $ŽĆ^0ŽĆ^0$ mass spectrum in the $f_0(980)$ region in the reaction $ŽĆ^-p\toŽĆ^0ŽĆ^0n$ with increasing $-t$, is compared with the recent detailed data on the $m$ and $t$ distributions of the $ŽĆ^-p\toŽĆ^0ŽĆ^0n$ events obtained by the BNL-E852 Collaboration. It is shown that the predictions of this model are not confirmed by the BNL data. Therefore the observed phenomenon should be explained by the different exchange mechanism. It is most likely to be the $a_1$ exchange mechanism.

# Mechanisms of the $f_0(980)$ production in the reaction $\pi^-p\to\pi^0 \pi^0n$

## Key claim

The pure one-pion exchange (POPE) model, which successfully described the GAMS Collaboration data on the $f_0(980)$ dip-to-peak transition in the $S$-wave $\pi^0\pi^0$ mass spectrum with increasing $-t$, fails to reproduce the detailed $m$ and $t$ distributions from the BNL-E852 Collaboration; the observed phenomenon is most likely explained by an $a_1$ exchange mechanism rather than by one-pion exchange alone.

## Method

The authors compared the predictions of the POPE model of Ref. [5] (Achasov and Shestakov, 1999) against the high-statistics BNL-E852 data on the reaction $\pi^-p\to(\pi^0\pi^0)_S\,n$ for nine fine $t$ bins covering $0<-t<0.4$ GeV$^2$ and one wide interval $0.4<-t<1.5$ GeV$^2$, with 0.04 GeV-wide steps in the invariant mass $m$. They used the POPE model parameters that gave the best fit to the GAMS data and computed the double differential distribution $d^2N/(dm\,dt)$ from the model, normalizing to the total number of events in the $0.6<m<1.2$ GeV region for $0.01<-t<0.03$ GeV$^2$. The theoretical $m$ and $t$ distributions were then compared directly to the BNL data without refitting.

## Key numbers

- The POPE model predicts that the residue $g_\pi(t)$ of the lowest-mass resonance vanishes at $t \approx -0.0728$ GeV$^2$ (from the best fit of Ref. [5]).
- The zero of the $S$-wave $\pi^*(t)\pi\to\pi\pi$ amplitude shifts from $m \approx 0.986$ GeV at $t=m_\pi^2$ to lower masses as $-t$ increases; for example, as $-t$ increases from 0.09 GeV$^2$ by only 0.026 GeV$^2$, the zero crosses the mass region from 0.91 to 0.60 GeV.
- In the POPE model, the form factor $F(t)=[(\Lambda-m_\pi^2)/(\Lambda-t)]^4$ with $\Lambda=0.1607$ GeV$^2$ causes the one-pion exchange contribution to the $pn\to np$ differential cross section to drop like $\exp(56t)$ in the region $0<-t<0.2$ GeV$^2$.
- The mass of the lightest resonance in various $K$-matrix parametrizations varies from 0.65 to 0.86 GeV; the residue zero occurs at $-t=0.117$ GeV$^2$ in Ref. [6] (solution I), at $-t=0.0683$ GeV$^2$ in Ref. [7] (solution I), and at $-t=0.038$ GeV$^2$ in Ref. [9].

## Context

This work extends the earlier debate between the POPE model of Achasov and Shestakov (1999) and the alternative $a_1$ exchange mechanism proposed by the same authors (Achasov and Shestakov, 1999). The GAMS Collaboration (1990s) had published only a single global $dN/dm$ distribution for $0<-t<0.2$ GeV$^2$, which the POPE model could fit; the BNL-E852 Collaboration (2000) provided the fine-binned $m$ and $t$ distributions needed for a decisive test. The paper concludes that the POPE model is ruled out by the BNL data and that the $a_1$ exchange mechanism is the most likely explanation, enabling future measurements on polarized targets to separate $\pi$ and $a_1$ exchange contributions explicitly.
