# Dominance of tensor correlations in high-momentum nucleon pairs studied by (p,pd) reaction

> The isospin character of p-n pairs at large relative momentum has been observed for the first time in the 16O ground state. A strong population of the J,T=1,0 state and a very weak population of the J,T=0,1 state were observed in neutron pick up domain of 16O(p,pd) at 392 MeV. This strong isospin dependence at large momentum transfer is not reproduced by the distorted-wave impulse approximation calculations with known spectroscopic amplitudes. The results indicate the presence of high-momentum protons and neutrons induced by the tensor interactions in ground state of 16O.

## Key claim

The isospin character of proton-neutron pairs at large relative momentum has been observed for the first time in the 16O ground state, showing a strong population of the J,T=1,0 state and a very weak population of the J,T=0,1 state. However, the observed ratio of cross sections between these two states is as much as 5 times smaller than the distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) prediction, indicating that current calculations with known spectroscopic amplitudes cannot reproduce the strong isospin dependence at large momentum transfer.

## Method

The experiment used 392 MeV protons incident on a windowless self-supporting ice-sheet target of 16O at the RCNP cyclotron facility. Scattered deuterons were momentum analyzed by the Grand Raiden spectrometer at forward angles, while coincident protons were detected by an array of plastic scintillators at backward angles covering the zero recoil momenta of 14N. The triple differential cross sections for the 16O(p,pd)14N reaction were measured and compared with factorized amplitude DWIA calculations using the code THREEDEE, with the off-shell p+d cross section approximated by three conventional prescriptions and the d*(p,p)d cross section for the S,T=0,1 channel deduced from Faddeev calculations using the CD-Bonn interaction.

## Key numbers

- Incident proton energy: 392 MeV
- Target thickness: 56.2(4) mg/cm2
- Excitation energy resolution in (p,d) reaction: 260 keV full width at half maximum (FWHM)
- Excitation energy resolution in (p,pd) reaction: 1.6 MeV FWHM
- Deuteron bound-state wave function parameters: radius r0 = 1.25 fm, diffuseness a0 = 0.65 fm
- Perey factor for nonlocality correction: 0.54 fm
- The ratio of cross sections between the 2.31 MeV (J,T=0,1) and 3.95 MeV (J,T=1,0) states was overestimated by DWIA calculations by as much as a factor of 5
- The ratio of cross sections for the two reactions from around 37.5 degrees at 75 MeV to that at 392 MeV was estimated to be roughly a factor of 3

## Context

This work extends previous studies of high-momentum components in nuclei via (p,d) reactions at intermediate energies by Ong (2013), which showed sensitivity to tensor interactions from the energy dependence of cross sections populating specific excited states in 15O. The results contradict the expectations from DWIA calculations using known spectroscopic amplitudes from Cohen and Kurath (1970), and the observed dominance of the S,T=1,0 channel over the S,T=0,1 channel provides direct experimental evidence for tensor correlations in the 16O ground state. The findings enable future studies of tensor correlations as a function of transferred momentum and nuclear mass, and call for more realistic structure information and reaction treatments in theoretical calculations.
