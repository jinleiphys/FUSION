# Energy dependence of $\bar{K}N$ interaction in nuclear medium

> When the $\bar{K}N$ system is submerged in nuclear medium the $\bar{K}N$ scattering amplitude and the final state branching ratios exhibit a strong energy dependence when going to energies below the $\bar{K}N$ threshold. A sharp increase of $\bar{K}N$ attraction below the $\bar{K}N$ threshold provides a link between shallow $\bar{K}$-nuclear potentials based on the chiral $\bar{K}N$ amplitude evaluated at threshold and the deep phenomenological optical potentials obtained in fits to kaonic atoms data. We show the energy dependence of the in-medium $K^{-}p$ amplitude and demonstrate the impact of energy dependent branching ratios on the $Λ$-hypernuclear production rates. \keywords{kaon-nucleon amplitude \and nuclear medium \and hypernuclei

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the in-medium $\bar{K}N$ scattering amplitude and branching ratios exhibit a strong energy dependence below the $\bar{K}N$ threshold, with a sharp increase in attraction at subthreshold energies that reconciles shallow chiral potentials evaluated at threshold with deep phenomenological optical potentials from kaonic atom fits.

## Method

The author employs chirally motivated coupled-channel potentials in separable form, including meson-baryon channels from $\pi\Lambda$, $\pi\Sigma$, $\bar{K}N$, $\eta\Lambda$, $\eta\Sigma$, and $K\Xi$ states, with parameters fitted to kaonic hydrogen and low energy $K^{-}p$ reaction data. In-medium modifications are implemented through Pauli blocking and hadron selfenergies (including kaon, baryon, and pion selfenergies) at nuclear density $\rho = \rho_0 = 0.17~{\rm fm}^{-3}$, using a leading order chiral model labeled TW1.

## Key numbers

- Chirally based coupled channels calculations lead to $K^-$ nuclear optical potentials about 80 MeV deep at nuclear densities $\rho = \rho_0 = 0.17~{\rm fm}^{-3}$; incorporation of kaon selfenergy reduces the depth to approximately 40-50 MeV
- Deep phenomenological potentials obtained from fits to kaonic atoms data give ${\rm Re}\:V_{K^-} (\rho_0)\sim -$(150--200) MeV
- Pauli blocking shifts the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance structure about 30-40 MeV above the $\bar{K}N$ threshold; addition of kaon selfenergies brings it back below threshold
- The $K^{-}p$ interaction becomes much stronger at energies about 30 MeV below the $K^{-}p$ threshold compared to its strength at threshold

## Context

This work extends the author's previous self-consistent treatment of chirally motivated $K^-N$ amplitudes reported in Cieply et al. (2011), which produced deep $K^-$ nuclear potentials considerably deeper than the shallow potentials from earlier chiral models by Waas, Kaiser, and Weise (1996) and Ramos and Oset (2000). The energy-dependent branching ratios are applied to $\Lambda$-hypernuclear production rates in ($K^-_{\rm stop}, \pi^{-}$) reactions, using experimental data from the FINUDA collaboration (2011) for five $p$-shell nuclear targets from $^{7}$Li to $^{16}$O, enabling improved understanding of kaonic atoms and kaon-nuclear states.
