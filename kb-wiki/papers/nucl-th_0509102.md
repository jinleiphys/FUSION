# The last bursts of Quark Gluon Plasma

> We study the behavior of the scalar glueball inside a Quark Gluon Plasma. We follow the fireball from the plasma phase to the hadronic phase and observe that an interesting phenomenon might occur which could have experimental consequences as a signature of Quark Gluon Plasma.

# The last bursts of Quark Gluon Plasma

## Key claim

The scalar glueball remains stable across the QGP phase transition and only decays into pions well below the transition temperature (T < T_C/2), producing delayed bursts of pions that could serve as a signature of Quark Gluon Plasma formation. The author hedges that this scenario depends on the OZI Dynamics (OZID) approximation where glueballs and mesons are almost decoupled, and on the assumption that the sigma meson is the O(4) partner of the pion in chiral symmetric QCD.

## Method

The author uses the scale anomaly relation from Gluodynamics to connect the scalar glueball mass m_g and decay constant f_g to the gluon condensate, then combines this with lattice results and model calculations showing the condensate is constant up to T_C (150-300 MeV) and dilutes at (2-3)T_C. The scenario assumes a strong color Coulomb phase above T_C where binary gluon bound states form copiously, and follows the cooling fireball from the plasma phase through the phase transition to the hadronic phase, tracking the scalar glueball's stability and decay via mixing with the sigma meson.

## Key numbers

- Phase transition temperature T_C: 150 MeV < T_C < 300 MeV
- Dilution temperature where gluon condensate evaporates: (2-3) T_C
- Scalar glueball decay width Γ_g is much less than the sigma meson width Γ_σ ~ 100 MeV
- Glueball decay occurs only when T < T_C/2, where the sigma mass approaches the glueball mass
- Ratio of glueball to meson channels changes from 1 to 8 below the phase transition to 1 to 2 above the phase transition

## Context

This work extends the traditional scenario of Leutwyler and Miller (1980s) and lattice studies by Langfeld and others (early 2000s) regarding the gluon condensate behavior across the phase transition. It builds on Shuryak's (2004) proposal of a strong color Coulomb phase with binary bound states above T_C, and on the author's own prior work (Vento, 2005) interpreting the low-lying scalar glueball spectrum. The scenario enables experimental searches for QGP formation at RHIC through delayed pion bursts that follow the elliptic flow geometry, potentially distinguishable from prompt hadronic emissions.
