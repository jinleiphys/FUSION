# The Four-Boson First-Excited State Near Two-Body Unitarity

> Near two-body unitarity, the three-boson system is characterized by an approximate discrete scale invariance manifest in a geometric tower of bound states (the Efimov effect). In the absence of a strong four-body force, the four-boson system has two states associated with each Efimov state, one very nearly unstable, the other several times deeper. We study correlations between the excited- and ground-state properties, such as binding energies and radii, up to next-to-leading order in an effective field theory for short-range forces. We obtain the parameters in these correlations from similar correlations arising from existing precise calculations based on short-range potentials. We also derive correlations among excited-state properties that emerge from the proximity of the state to the break-up threshold into a boson and a three-boson bound state, using an effective field theory for "halo" states.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the correlation between the excited and ground four-body binding energies near two-body unitarity can be described at next-to-leading order in Short-Range Effective Field Theory, provided a four-body force is included perturbatively; the correlation parameters extracted from existing precise calculations are consistent with this framework, though the sign and magnitude of the effective-range contribution remain undetermined.

## Method

The authors employ Short-Range Effective Field Theory up to next-to-leading order, treating the two-body system at unitarity and incorporating perturbative corrections from a finite two-body scattering length, effective range, and a four-body contact interaction. They extract the universal correlation parameters lambda_1/lambda_0 and eta_1/eta_0 by fitting existing numerical results from Deltuva (2012) and Hadizadeh (2011) for four-boson binding energies across the Efimov tower, using linear regressions in the plane of excited versus ground-state energies scaled by the three-body binding energy. They also apply Halo/Cluster Effective Field Theory to derive correlations involving the excited-state radius.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order universal ratio of four-body ground to three-body binding energy is kappa_0 = 4.6108 (Deltuva 2010).
* The leading-order universal ratio of four-body first-excited to three-body binding energy is kappa_1 = 1.00228 (Deltuva 2010).
* The ratio kappa_1/kappa_0 = 0.21738 at leading order.
* The slope lambda_1/lambda_0 extracted from Deltuva (2012) data is 0.0159741, giving the correlation B_{4,1}/B_3 = 0.928624 + 0.0159741 * B_{4,0}/B_3.
* The slope eta_1/eta_0 extracted from Hammer (2006) data is 0.0207917, giving the correlation B_{4,1}/B_3 = 0.921944 + 0.0207917 * B_{4,0}/B_3 for variation of the scattering length.
* The leading-order universal width parameters for four-body states are gamma_0 = 1.484 * 10^{-2} and gamma_1 = 2.38 * 10^{-4} (Deltuva 2010).

## Context

This work extends the Efimov effect for three bosons (Efimov 1970) to the four-body sector, building on earlier Short-Range EFT calculations by Platter (2004) and Hammer (2006) that identified two four-body states per Efimov trimer. It refines the correlation first discovered by Hadizadeh (2011) in zero-range Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations, showing that the linear portion of that correlation can be reproduced at NLO in EFT. The results enable future calculations of four-body observables such as radii and widths using Halo/Cluster EFT, and clarify the role of a four-body scale in systems near unitarity.
