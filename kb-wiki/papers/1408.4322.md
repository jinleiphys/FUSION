# Nickel isotopes in stellar matter

> Isotopes of nickel play a key role during the silicon burning phase up to the presupernova phase of massive stars. Electron capture rates on these nickel isotopes are also important during the phase of core contraction. I present here the microscopic calculation of ground and excited states Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions for key nickel isotopes. The calculation is performed within the frame-work of pn-QRPA model. A judicious choice of model parameters, specially of the Gamow-Teller strength parameters and the deformation parameter, resulted in a much improved calculation of GT strength functions. The excited state GT distributions are much different from the corresponding ground-state distributions resulting in a failure of the Brink's hypothesis. The electron capture and positron decay rates on nickel isotopes are also calculated within the framework of pn-QRPA model relevant to the presupernova evolution of massive stars. The electron capture rates on odd-A isotopes of nickel are shown to have dominant contributions from parent excited states during as early as silicon burning phases. Comparison is being made with the large scale shell model calculation. During the silicon burning phases of massive stars the electron capture rates on $^{57,59}$Ni are around an order of magnitude bigger than shell model rates and can bear consequences for core-collapse simulators.

# Nickel isotopes in stellar matter

## Key claim
The pn-QRPA model, with carefully tuned Gamow-Teller strength parameters and deformation parameters, produces electron capture rates on odd-A nickel isotopes that are around an order of magnitude larger than large-scale shell model rates during silicon burning phases, potentially affecting core-collapse simulations; however, the model does not exactly reproduce measured data, and the calculated rates are presented as a fair estimate where no experimental data exist.

## Method
The proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model was used to calculate ground and excited state Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions for nickel isotopes (mass numbers 57–65). Single-particle energies and wave functions were computed in the Nilsson model with BCS pairing, and the particle-hole and particle-particle interaction strengths were fine-tuned to reproduce experimental GT centroids and total strengths for even-even isotopes ⁵⁸,⁶⁰,⁶²,⁶⁴Ni, yielding optimal values χ = 0.001 MeV and κ = 0.052 MeV. Deformation parameters were taken from experimental quadrupole moments for even-even isotopes and calculated from electric quadrupole moments for odd-A isotopes. Around 300 parent excited states and as many daughter states were included, covering up to 15–20 MeV excitation energy, with an average quenching factor of 0.6 applied.

## Key numbers
- Total B(GT₊) strength up to 8 MeV in daughter for ⁵⁸Ni: measured 3.8 ± 0.4, pn-QRPA calculated 6.95
- Total B(GT₊) strength up to 8 MeV in daughter for ⁶⁰Ni: measured 3.11 ± 0.08, pn-QRPA calculated 5.83
- Total B(GT₊) strength up to 8 MeV in daughter for ⁶²Ni: measured 2.53 ± 0.07, pn-QRPA calculated 3.58
- Total B(GT₊) strength up to 8 MeV in daughter for ⁶⁴Ni: measured 1.72 ± 0.09, pn-QRPA calculated 1.77
- B(GT₊) strength up to 2.4 MeV in ⁶⁰Co: measured 1.34 ± 0.22, pn-QRPA calculated 2.89
- B(GT₊) strength up to 2.3 MeV in ⁶²Co: measured 1.28 ± 0.29, pn-QRPA calculated 1.63
- For ⁵⁷Ni at T₉ = 3 GK and ρ = 10⁸·⁵ g cm⁻³: ground state electron capture rate 0.35 s⁻¹, first excited state rate 5.75 s⁻¹, total from all excited states 0.71 s⁻¹, ratio of ground to total = 0.47
- For ⁵⁸Ni at T₉ = 3 GK and ρ = 10⁸·⁵ g cm⁻³: ground state electron capture rate 0.291 s⁻¹, total rate 0.293 s⁻¹, ratio of ground to total = 0.995
- pn-QRPA electron capture rates on ⁵⁷Ni are bigger than LSSM rates by more than a factor of 6 during silicon burning phases (ρ = 10⁷ g cm⁻³, T₉ = 5 K)
- pn-QRPA electron capture rates on ⁵⁹Ni are bigger than LSSM rates by up to a factor of 7 at low densities (ρ = 10⁷–10⁸ g cm⁻³)
- Half-life of ⁶⁴Ni decreases by roughly 50 orders of magnitude as stellar temperature increases

## Context
This work extends the pn-QRPA calculations of weak interaction rates previously applied to iron isotopes (Nabi 2009) and to ⁵⁶Ni (Nabi 2005), and provides a systematic calculation for nickel isotopes ⁵⁷–⁶⁵. The results are compared with the large-scale shell model (LSSM) calculations of Langanke and collaborators (2000) and with earlier work by Pruet and Fuller (2003). The paper enables core-collapse simulators to incorporate updated electron capture rates that differ significantly from shell model predictions, particularly for ⁵⁷,⁵⁹Ni during silicon burning, and for ⁶³,⁶⁵Ni during the presupernova phase. The failure of Brink's hypothesis for these nuclei is explicitly demonstrated, motivating microscopic treatment of excited state GT distributions rather than the common approximation of shifted ground-state distributions.
