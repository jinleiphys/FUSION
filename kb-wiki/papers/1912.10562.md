# Dissipative type theories for Bjorken and Gubser flows

> We use the dissipative type theory (DTT) framework to solve for the evolution of conformal fluids in Bjorken and Gubser flows from isotropic initial conditions. The results compare well with both exact and other hydrodynamic solutions in the literature. At the same time, DTTs enforce the Second Law of thermodynamics as an exact property of the formalism, at any order in deviations from equilibrium, and are easily generalizable to more complex situations.

## Key claim

Dissipative type theories (DTTs) provide a hydrodynamic description of conformal fluids in Bjorken and Gubser flows that compares well with exact kinetic theory solutions and third-order Chapman-Enskog approximations, while enforcing the Second Law of thermodynamics as an exact property of the formalism at any order in deviations from equilibrium, not just perturbatively.

## Method

The authors constructed a DTT from a variational principle that extremizes entropy production for a given energy-momentum tensor, using an Anderson-Witting collision term with relaxation time tau_R = c/T_0 to preserve conformal invariance. They derived closed dynamical systems for the temperature T and the single independent component zeta of the traceless tensor zeta^mu_nu in both Bjorken flow (Milne coordinates) and Gubser flow (conformal coordinates), then solved these systems numerically from isotropic initial conditions. The DTT results were compared to exact solutions of the Boltzmann equation obtained via iterative integral equations and to third-order Chapman-Enskog approximations from ChHPV18 and J13.

## Key numbers

- Specific shear viscosity used: eta/s = 1/(4 pi), saturating the Kovtun-Son-Starinets bound (KSS05)
- For Bjorken flow: initial proper time tau_i = 0.25 fm/c, initial temperature T_i = 1 (arbitrary units), integration from tau = 0.25 to tau = 10
- For Gubser flow: initial conformal time rho_i = -10, initial temperature T_i = 0.002, integration from rho = -10 to rho = 10
- Stefan-Boltzmann constant for the conformal gas: sigma_SB = 3/pi^2
- The DTT equations are well-defined in the domain -1/2 < zeta < 1 for Bjorken flow

## Context

This work extends the testing of dissipative type theories (introduced by L72, LMR86, GL90, GL91, and others) to the standard benchmark flows of relativistic heavy-ion hydrodynamics, where exact kinetic theory solutions exist. It complements prior tests of other frameworks in these flows, including anisotropic hydrodynamics (ST14, ST14b, FMR15, FRST16), BRSSS (BRSSS08), DNMR (DMNR10 and subsequent works), and third-order Chapman-Enskog theory (ChHPV18, J13). The results enable further application of DTTs to more complex flows and to studies of hydrodynamization and thermalization processes, while demonstrating that thermodynamic consistency need not come at the cost of accuracy.
