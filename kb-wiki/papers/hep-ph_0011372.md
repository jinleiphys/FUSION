# Light-Front-Quantized QCD in Light-Cone Gauge: The Doubly Transverse Gauge Propagator

> The light-front (LF) quantization of QCD in light-cone gauge has a number of remarkable advantages, including explicit unitarity, a physical Fock expansion, the absence of ghost degrees of freedom, and the decoupling properties needed to prove factorization theorems in high momentum transfer inclusive and exclusive reactions. We present a systematic study of LF-quantized gauge theory following the Dirac method and construct the Dyson-Wick S-matrix expansion based on LF-time-ordered products. The free theory gauge field is shown to satisfy the Lorentz condition as an operator equation as well as the light-cone gauge condition. Its propagator is found to be transverse with respect to both its four-momentum and the gauge direction. The interaction Hamiltonian of QCD can be expressed in a form resembling that of covariant theory, except for additional instantaneous interactions which can be treated systematically. The renormalization constants in YM theory are shown to satisfy the identity $Z_1=Z_3$ at one loop order. The QCD $β$ function computed in the noncovariant light-cone gauge agrees with that known in the conventional framework. Some comments on the relationship of our LF framework, with the doubly transverse gauge propagator, to the analytic effective charge and renormalization scheme defined by the pinch technique, the unitarity relations and the spectral representation are also made. LF quantization thus provides a consistent formulation of gauge theory, despite the fact that the hyperplanes $x^{\pm}=0$ used to impose boundary conditions constitute characteristic surfaces of a hyperbolic partial differential equation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that light-front (LF) quantized QCD in light-cone gauge yields a gauge field propagator that is transverse with respect to both its four-momentum and the gauge direction (doubly transverse), and that this framework provides a consistent formulation of gauge theory despite the characteristic surfaces of the hyperbolic partial differential equations involved.

## Method

The authors follow the Dirac method for constrained Hamiltonian systems to quantize QCD on the light-front in light-cone gauge (A⁺=0). They construct the Dyson-Wick S-matrix expansion based on LF-time-ordered products, eliminate nondynamical fields (A₊, ψ₋, and B) using constraint equations, and derive the free gauge field propagator. The renormalization procedure is illustrated at one-loop order for pure Yang-Mills theory using dimensional regularization with the Mandelstam-Leibbrandt prescription for handling the 1/(n·k) singularity.

## Key numbers

- The free gauge field propagator in momentum space is given by D_μν(k) = -g_μν + (n_μ k_ν + n_ν k_μ)/(n·k) - [k²/(n·k)²] n_μ n_ν, where n_μ is a null four-vector with components n_μ = δ_μ⁺
- The renormalization constants in Yang-Mills theory satisfy the identity Z₁ = Z₃ at one-loop order
- The QCD β-function computed in the noncovariant light-cone gauge agrees with that known in the conventional framework
- The equal-LF-time commutator of transverse gauge field components is [A_⟂(τ,x⁻,x^⟂), A_⟂'(τ,y⁻,y^⟂)] = iδ_⟂⟂' K(x,y) where K(x,y) = -(1/4)ε(x⁻-y⁻)δ²(x^⟂-y^⟂)

## Context

This work extends the Dirac method for constrained systems (Dirac, 1950) to LF-quantized gauge theory and provides a systematic foundation for earlier LF perturbation theory rules (Lepage and Brodsky, 1980). The doubly transverse propagator differs from those derived in equal-time quantized light-cone gauge QCD (Bassetto, 1997; Leibbrandt, 1987). The framework enables consistent nonperturbative approaches such as DLCQ (Pauli and Brodsky, 1985) and provides tools for proving factorization theorems in high momentum transfer reactions.
