# Deuteron Distribution in Nuclei and in Correlated Nuclear Matter

> We compute the distribution of quasideuterons in doubly closed shell nuclei and infinite correlated nuclear matter. The ground states of $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca are described in $ls$ coupling using a realistic hamiltonian including the Argonne $v_{8}^\prime$ and the Urbana IX models of two-- and three--nucleon potentials, respectively. The nuclear wave function contains central and tensor correlations, and correlated basis functions theory is used to evaluate the distribution of neutron-proton pairs, having the deuteron quantum numbers, as a function of their total momentum. By computing the number of deuteron--like pairs we are able to extract the Levinger's factor and compare to both the available experimental data and the predictions of the local density approximation, based on nuclear matter estimates. The agreement with the experiments is excellent, whereas the local density approximation is shown to sizably overestimate the Levinger's factor in the region of the medium nuclei.

# Deuteron Distribution in Nuclei and in Correlated Nuclear Matter

## Key claim
The Correlated Basis Function (CBF) approach, using the Argonne v8' plus Urbana IX (AU8') interaction, yields Levinger factors for 16O and 40Ca that agree excellently with experimental photoreaction data, while the local density approximation (LDA) sizably overestimates the Levinger factor in medium nuclei; however, the nuclear matter Levinger factor remains about 15% larger than the empirical infinite-A extrapolation, and the Jastrow model systematically underestimates the Levinger factor.

## Method
The ground states of 16O and 40Ca were described in ls coupling using a realistic Hamiltonian with the Argonne v8' two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon potentials. The nuclear wave function included central and tensor correlations, and correlated basis functions theory was used to evaluate the momentum distribution of neutron-proton pairs having deuteron quantum numbers as a function of their total momentum. The total number of deuteron-like pairs per particle was computed by integrating the momentum distribution, and the Levinger factor was extracted via L(A) = 4 * P_D/A for symmetric N=Z nuclei.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear matter (AU8' interaction): P_D(NM)/A = 2.707, corresponding to L_NM = 10.83 (compared to 2.895 and L=11.63 with the Urbana v14 + TNI model from prior work).
- 16O: P_D(16O)/A = 1.090, giving L_f6(16O) = 4.36; the variational Monte Carlo estimate from Forest (1996) using Argonne v18 + Urbana IX gave L_VMC(16O) = 4.70.
- 40Ca: P_D(40Ca)/A = 1.370.
- Binding energies per nucleon from full minimization: B/A = 5.48 MeV in 16O and B/A = 6.97 MeV in 40Ca (experimental: 7.97 MeV and 8.55 MeV, respectively).
- Root mean square radii from partial minimization: R = 2.67 fm (16O) and R = 3.39 fm (40Ca), compared to experimental R_expt = 2.73 fm and 3.48 fm.
- Occupation depletion of fully occupied shell model states: 9.6% in 16O and 10.5% in 40Ca, with maximum depletion of about 22% for the 2s state in 40Ca.
- LDA parameterization for AU8' interaction: L_LDA(A) = 10.83 - 9.76 A^{-1/3}.
- The tail of P_D(k_D) at |k_D| = 4k_F differs by a factor of about 10 between finite nuclei and nuclear matter for both 16O and 40Ca.

## Context
This work extends the CBF many-body approach previously applied to infinite nuclear matter by Benhar et al. (2002) to finite doubly closed shell nuclei 16O and 40Ca, using the same theoretical framework. It contrasts with the independent particle model (IPM) and Jastrow correlation models, showing that the Jastrow model underestimates the Levinger factor while IPM gives results relatively close to the full calculation. The results enable microscopic interpretation of the phenomenological Levinger factor used in the quasideuteron model of nuclear photoabsorption, and demonstrate that LDA fails for light-to-medium nuclei, motivating improved treatments of surface effects in heavier systems.
