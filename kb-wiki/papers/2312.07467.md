# Probing nuclear structure at the Electron-Ion Collider and in ultra-peripheral nuclear collisions

> Within the Color Glass Condensate framework, we demonstrate that exclusive vector meson production at high energy is sensitive to the geometric deformation of the target nucleus and subnucleon scale fluctuations. Deformation of the nucleus enhances the incoherent cross section in the small $|t|$ region. Subnucleon scale fluctuations increase the incoherent cross section in the large $|t|$ region. In ultra-peripheral collisions (UPCs), larger deformation leads to a wider distribution of the minimal impact parameter $B_{min}$ required to produce a UPC. This, together with larger incoherent cross sections for larger deformation, results in smaller extracted radii. Our results demonstrate great potential for future studies of nuclear structure in UPCs and electron-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Within the Color Glass Condensate framework, exclusive vector meson production at high energy is sensitive to both the geometric deformation of the target nucleus and subnucleon scale fluctuations, but the abstract's claim that this "demonstrates great potential" is hedged by the fact that the results are model-dependent calculations that have not yet been experimentally verified at the Electron-Ion Collider or in ultra-peripheral collisions.

## Method

The authors performed calculations of exclusive vector meson (J/ψ and ρ) photoproduction in electron-uranium collisions and ultra-peripheral U+U collisions using the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework with the dipole picture. The scattering amplitude was computed by averaging over target color charge configurations, with the coherent cross section obtained from the squared average amplitude and the incoherent cross section from the variance of the amplitude. For ultra-peripheral collisions, quantum mechanical interference between the two possible photon sources (either nucleus) was included, along with non-zero transverse momentum of the incoming photons.

## Key numbers

- Incoherent J/ψ photoproduction cross section in e+U collisions at x_p = 1.7 × 10⁻³ shows enhancement at small |t| < 0.02 GeV² due to quadrupole (β₂) deformation
- Subnucleonic fluctuations alter the slope and magnitude of the incoherent cross section at |t| > 0.25 GeV²
- ρ meson photoproduction in γ+U → ρ+U(*) at Q² = 0.0 GeV² shows flatter total dN/d|t| distribution with increasing β₂ deformation
- Minimal impact parameter B_min distributions for UPCs between two uranium nuclei at 193 GeV become wider for larger β₂ values

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate framework calculations of Mantysaari et al. (2022, 2023) to systematically study nuclear deformation effects in exclusive vector meson production. It builds on earlier observations by the STAR collaboration (2022) of cos(2ΔΦ) modulations in exclusive ρ meson photoproduction in UPCs and their extraction of nuclear radii for ¹⁹⁷Au and ²³⁸U. The results enable future experiments at the Electron-Ion Collider, LHeC/FCC-he, and EicC to disentangle nuclear structure effects at multiple length scales by combining measurements from electron-ion collisions and ultra-peripheral collisions.
