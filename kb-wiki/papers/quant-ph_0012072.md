# Generalized uncertainty relations and coherent and squeezed states

> Characteristic uncertainty relations and their related squeezed states are briefly reviewed and compared in accordance with the generalizations of three equivalent definitions of the canonical coherent states. The standard SU(1,1) coherent states are shown to be the unique states that minimize the Schroedinger uncertainty relation for every pair of the three generators and the Robertson relation for the three generators. The characteristic uncertainty inequalities are naturally extended to the case of several states. It is shown that these inequalities can be written in the equivalent complementary form.

# Generalized uncertainty relations and coherent and squeezed states

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the standard SU(1,1) coherent states are the unique states that simultaneously minimize the Schroedinger uncertainty relation for every pair of the three generators and the Robertson relation for all three generators, but this uniqueness holds only under the specific condition that the minimization is performed with respect to the more precise Schroedinger-Robertson inequality rather than the simpler Heisenberg inequality.

## Method
The author reviews and compares three equivalent definitions of canonical coherent states (eigenstate, displacement operator, and uncertainty minimization) and extends them to generalized squeezed states. The analysis uses the Schroedinger uncertainty relation (which includes the covariance term) and the Robertson uncertainty relation for n observables to define optimal uncertainty states, deriving the conditions under which these states coincide with group-related coherent states for SU(1,1) and SU(2) algebras.

## Key numbers
- The canonical Schroedinger uncertainty relation for p and q: (Delta p)^2 (Delta q)^2 - (Delta pq)^2 >= 1/4, where the right-hand side is 1/4 (dimensionless units).
- For canonical squeezed states |alpha,u,v>, the variances are (Delta q)^2 = (1/2)|u-v|^2, (Delta p)^2 = (1/2)|u+v|^2, and the covariance Delta pq = -Im(uv*).
- The less-precise inequality (Delta X)^2 + (Delta Y)^2 >= |<[X,Y]>| is minimized by states with equal variances: (Delta X)^2 = (Delta Y)^2 = |<[X,Y]>|/2.
- For the SU(1,1) coherent states |xi;k>, the parameter |xi| = tanh|z| lies in the unit disk D_1.

## Context
This work extends the foundational studies of coherent states by Glauber, Klauder, and Sudarshan (1963) and the squeezed state formalism developed by Caves (1981), Hollenhorst, and Yuen. It builds on the group-theoretic approach to coherent states by Perelomov (1972) and the Barut-Girardello coherent states (1971), while providing a unified uncertainty-based framework that connects the three definitions of coherent states. The results enable finer classification of quantum states and construction of observable-induced metrics between quantum states, with applications to quantum interferometry as shown by Brif and Mann.
