# Low-Energy Antinucleon-Nucleus Interaction Revisited

> Annihilation cross sections of antiprotons and antineutrons on the proton between 50 and 400 MeV/c show Coulomb focusing below 200 MeV/c and almost no charge-dependence above 200 MeV/c. Similar comparisons for heavier targets are not possible for lack of overlap between nuclear targets studied with $\bar p$ and $\bar n$ beams. Interpolating between $\bar p$-nucleus annihilation cross sections with the help of an optical potential to compare with $\bar n$-nucleus annihilation cross sections reveal unexpected features of Coulomb interactions in the latter. Direct comparisons between $\bar n$-nucleus and $\bar p$-nucleus annihilations at very low energies could be possible if $\bar p$ cross sections are measured on the same targets and at the same energies as the available cross sections for $\bar n$. Such measurements may be feasible in the foreseeable future.

## Key claim

Annihilation cross sections for antineutrons on nuclear targets follow the calculated cross sections for antiprotons rather than the calculated cross sections for antineutrons, which is in sharp contrast to the behavior observed on a proton target; the author states that "no explanation for this result is known at present."

## Method

The author compared experimental annihilation cross sections for antiprotons and antineutrons on the proton between 50 and 400 MeV/c to establish the Coulomb focusing effect. For nuclear targets, an optical potential was derived from a global analysis of 90 data points from antiprotonic atoms across the periodic table, using a t-rho form with a Yukawa-type antinucleon-nucleon interaction and setting the isovector coefficient to zero. This potential was then used to calculate annihilation cross sections for both antiprotons and antineutrons on the six nuclear targets (C, Al, Cu, Ag, Sn, Pb) at the seven energies studied by Astrua et al. (2002), and these calculations were compared with the experimental antineutron data.

## Key numbers

- For a proton target, the Coulomb enhancement factor reaches 2 at a lab momentum of 43 MeV/c (lab energy 1.0 MeV), consistent with the observed differences between antiproton and antineutron annihilation cross sections.
- For a Sn target, the Coulomb enhancement factor reaches 2 at a lab momentum of 136 MeV/c (lab energy 9.8 MeV), suggesting antineutron annihilation cross sections near 100 MeV/c should be a factor of 3 smaller than the corresponding antiproton cross sections.
- The global optical potential analysis used 90 data points from antiprotonic atoms across the periodic table, with the minimum chi-squared obtained for neutron density parameters consistent with results from a variety of other methods.
- A single preliminary experimental result for antiproton annihilation on Sn at 100 MeV/c is available, and the measured antineutron cross sections around that momentum are larger than this antiproton point.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of antinucleon-nucleus interactions by Friedman (2014) and the experimental measurements of antineutron annihilation cross sections on six nuclear targets by Astrua et al. (2002). It contradicts the expectation from the proton-target results that Coulomb focusing would make antiproton cross sections larger than antineutron cross sections at low momenta, as the experimental antineutron data instead follow the calculated antiproton curves. The paper enables future experimental work by proposing that measuring antiproton annihilation cross sections on the same six targets at the same energies as the existing antineutron data could resolve this puzzle, and notes such measurements may be feasible in the foreseeable future.
