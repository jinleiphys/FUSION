# Mesoscopic dynamics of fermionic cold atoms ---Quantitative analysis of transport coefficients and relaxation times---

> We give a quantitative analysis of the dynamical properties of fermionic cold atomic gases in normal phase, such as the shear viscosity, heat conductivity, and viscous relaxation times, using the novel microscopic expressions derived by the renormalization group (RG) method, where the Boltzmann equation is faithfully solved to extract the hydrodynamics without recourse to any ansatz. In particular, we examine the quantum statistical effects, temperature dependence, and scattering-length dependence of the transport coefficients and the viscous relaxation times. The numerical calculation shows that the relation $τ_π=η/P$, which is derived in the relaxation-time approximation (RTA) and is used in most of the literature, turns out to be satisfied quite well, while the similar relation for the viscous relaxation time $τ_J$ of the heat conductivity is satisfied only approximately with a considerable error.

## Key claim

The relation τ_π = η/P, derived from the relaxation-time approximation (RTA) and widely used in the literature, is satisfied quite well quantitatively for both classical and Fermi gases, while the analogous relation for the viscous relaxation time τ_J of the heat conductivity is satisfied only approximately with a considerable error.

## Method

The authors solved the linearized Boltzmann equation for a fermionic cold atomic gas with s-wave scattering, using the renormalization group (RG) method to derive microscopic expressions for the shear viscosity, heat conductivity, and viscous relaxation times. They numerically evaluated these expressions by discretizing the linear integral equations in momentum space and solving them without any ansatz or approximation, then compared the exact results with those obtained from the relaxation-time approximation (RTA).

## Key numbers

- At unitarity, the shear viscosity of the classical Boltzmann gas follows η/n = 2.77 (T/T_F)^(3/2) (from the upper panel of Fig. 3).
- The superfluid-transition temperature is T_c ≃ 0.167 T_F and the pairing-formation temperature is T* ≃ 0.22 T_F (from Fig. 3 caption).
- The ratio η^(exact)/P well reproduces the viscous relaxation time τ_π^(exact) for both classical and Fermi gases regardless of temperature and scattering length (from Section 5).
- The RTA always underestimates the viscous relaxation time of the heat conductivity τ_J compared with the exact evaluation (from Section 5).

## Context

This work extends the RG method previously applied to derive second-order hydrodynamics from the Boltzmann equation for both relativistic and non-relativistic systems (Tsumura 2012, 2013, 2015) and provides the first quantitative test of the RTA relations for viscous relaxation times. The results confirm the analytical examination by Schafer (2014) that τ_π = η/P is reliable, but show that the analogous relation for τ_J is not, enabling future studies to use the exact expression for τ_J rather than the RTA approximation when modeling mesoscopic dynamics of cold atomic gases.
