# Consistent Treatment of Relativistic Effects in Electrodisintegration of the Deuteron

> The influence of relativistic contributions to deuteron electrodisintegration is systematically studied in various kinematic regions of energy and momentum transfer. As theoretical framework the equation-of-motion and the unitarily equivalent S-matrix approaches are used. In a (p/M)-expansion, all leading order relativistic $π$-exchange contributions consistent with the Bonn OBEPQ model are included. In addition, static heavy meson exchange currents including boost terms, $γπρ/ω$-currents, and $Δ$-isobar contributions are considered. Sizeable effects from the various relativistic two-body contributions, mainly from $π$-exchange, have been found in inclusive form factors and exclusive structure functions for a variety of kinematic regions.

## Key claim

Relativistic two-body contributions, particularly from pion exchange, produce sizeable effects in inclusive form factors and exclusive structure functions for deuteron electrodisintegration across various kinematic regions, but these effects are strongly dependent on the specific kinematic regime and can be partially cancelled by heavy meson exchange contributions.

## Method

The authors used the equation-of-motion method and the unitarily equivalent S-matrix approach as theoretical frameworks, performing a (p/M)-expansion to include all leading order relativistic π-exchange contributions consistent with the Bonn OBEPQ model. They calculated the T-matrix elements explicitly for all electric and magnetic multipoles up to order L=4, including final state interaction in partial waves up to j=5, and used Born approximation for higher multipoles. The calculations were performed in the center-of-mass system of the final n-p state, using the Bonn OBEPQ potential versions A, B, and C with appropriate box renormalization for NΔ configurations.

## Key numbers

- In the inclusive form factor F_L at E_np=120 MeV, relativistic contributions increase at low q² up to 7 percent, dominantly from π-contributions
- In F_T at low q², π and heavy meson exchange lead to an increase up to almost 20 percent
- At high q² in F_T, there is an almost complete cancellation between π and heavy meson contributions leaving a tiny increase of about 1 percent
- At -q_μ²=20 fm⁻² near break-up threshold, relativistic one-body currents yield an enhancement of the cross section of about 60%, while relativistic π-contributions give a reduction by more than a factor five
- The overall reduction from all relativistic contributions near threshold at -q_μ²=20 fm⁻² amounts to a little less than one half relative to the nonrelativistic result
- The M1 transition coupling for NΔ was taken as G^{M1}_{ΔN}=4.7 below pion production threshold, with a dipole q_μ²-dependence of (1 - q_μ²/0.71 GeV²)⁻²
- The γπρ/ω coupling constants used were g_{ρ1}=3.2 (maximum setting) with λ_ρ=0.11, and g_{ω1}=14 (maximum setting) with λ_ω=0.36

## Context

This work systematically extends the study of relativistic effects in deuteron electrodisintegration beyond previous work by Tamura et al. (1992), which focused mainly on elastic electron-deuteron scattering and only studied the inelastic process in the threshold region. It also builds on the framework used by Goeller and Arenhoevel (1992) for deuteron photodisintegration, which showed that although the spin-orbit current gives the most important relativistic contribution, other terms of the same order cannot be neglected. The results enable a more complete assessment of relativistic contributions needed to interpret experimental data at higher momentum transfers, particularly for distinguishing subnuclear degrees of freedom from possible quark-gluon effects in nuclear structure.
