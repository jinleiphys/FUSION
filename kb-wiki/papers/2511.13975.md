# Formulation of fully covariant Quantum-Molecular Dynamics for an N-body system with scalar and vector potentials

> We present a fully covariant transport framework for Molecular Dynamics that enables a consistent description of the evolution of relativistic N-body systems. For the first time, we derive relativistic equations of motion incorporating both scalar and vector interactions within a manifestly covariant formulation. This approach addresses several fundamental issues in relativistic many-body dynamics: the implications of different choices of time-constraints, the emergence of the non-relativistic limit, the frame independence of the system's evolution, and the distinct dynamical roles of scalar and vector potentials. These aspects are investigated in detail for the scattering of two- and four-body systems, offering new insights into the consistency and physical interpretation of relativistic interactions in a covariant setting.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first fully covariant formulation of Quantum Molecular Dynamics that incorporates both scalar and vector potentials within a manifestly covariant framework, but the actual derivation depends on specific choices of time constraints that must satisfy the world line condition to guarantee frame independence of the trajectories.

## Method

The authors employ Dirac's constrained Hamiltonian dynamics to reduce the 8N-dimensional relativistic phase space to 6N+1 dimensions by introducing first-class mass-shell constraints and second-class time constraints. They derive relativistic equations of motion for two-body and N-body systems by expressing the mechanical momentum as \(p^*_{i,\mu} \equiv p_{i,\mu} - A_{i,\mu}\) and incorporating scalar potentials \(\Phi_i\) that depend on the transverse distance \(q_T^2\). The parameters \(\lambda_i\) in the equations of motion are fixed by requiring conservation of the time constraints, and the formalism is tested by solving the evolution equations in two distinct Lorentz frames to demonstrate frame independence.

## Key numbers

* The phase space is reduced from 8N to 6N+1 dimensions using N first-class constraints \(H_i = p^*_{i,\mu}p^{*\mu}_i - m_i^2 + \Phi_i = 0\) and N second-class time constraints such as \(\chi_1 = \frac{1}{2}(q_1^\mu - q_2^\mu)U_\mu = 0\) and \(\chi_2 = \frac{1}{2}(q_1^\mu + q_2^\mu)U_\mu - \tau = 0\).
* In the center-of-mass frame with zero total momentum, the time constraint \(\chi_2\) gives \(t_1 = t_2 = \tau\), meaning the time components of the position four-vectors coincide with the system time \(\tau\).
* The transverse distance \(q_T^\mu\) is defined as \(q_T^\mu = q^\mu - (q_\nu P^\nu / P^2) P^\mu\), and in the center-of-mass frame \(q_T^0 = 0\) while \(q_T^i\) represents the ordinary spatial distance between particles.

## Context

This work extends the constrained Hamiltonian dynamics framework initiated by Dirac (1949) and later developed by Komar (1978), Samuel (1982), and Todorov, which was previously applied to relativistic QMD by Sorge, Stöcker, and Greiner (1989) using only scalar potentials, and by Nara (2020, 2023) using vector potentials in a non-covariant approximate way. The fully covariant formulation with both scalar and vector potentials enables consistent treatment of relativistic heavy-ion collisions at facilities such as RHIC-BESII, CBM, HIAF, and NICA, where the equation of state depends on baryon density and requires proper handling of the zero component of the baryon current.
