# Transverse momentum broadening and collinear radiation at NLO in the $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM plasma

> We compute O(g) NLO corrections to the transverse scattering kernel and transverse momentum broadening coefficient $\hat{q}$ of weakly-coupled $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM. Based on this, we also compute NLO correction to the collinear splitting rates. For $\hat{q}$ we find that the NLO/LO ratio is similar to the QCD one, with large NLO corrections. This is contrasted by our findings for the collinear splitting rate, which show a much better convergence in SYM than in QCD, providing further support to earlier expectations that NLO corrections have signs and relative magnitudes controlled by the specifics of the theory. We also compare the ratio of $\hat{q}$ in QCD and in $\mathcal{N}=4$ theory to strong coupling expectations.

## Key claim

The NLO corrections to the collinear photon production rate in weakly-coupled N=4 SYM are small (at most a 30% decrease in the IR and a 20% increase at k ~ 5T for lambda=10), in sharp contrast to QCD where the NLO correction is approximately a 100% increase for alpha_s=0.3, demonstrating that the sign and magnitude of NLO corrections are controlled by the specific particle content of the theory.

## Method

The authors compute O(g) NLO corrections to the transverse scattering kernel C(q_perp) and the jet quenching parameter q-hat in weakly-coupled N=4 SYM by evaluating soft one-loop bosonic diagrams (including scalar contributions) within the dimensionally-reduced Euclidean theory ESYM. They then apply these results to compute NLO corrections to the collinear photon production rate by solving the LPM-resummed integral equation for the function f(p_perp, p+, k) with NLO corrections to both the collision kernel C(q_perp) and the thermal asymptotic mass m_infinity.

## Key numbers

- The NLO correction to the thermal asymptotic mass in N=4 SYM is delta m_infinity^2 = -g^3 C_A^(3/2) T^2 (3+sqrt(2))/(2pi)
- In N=4 SYM at lambda=10, the NLO correction to the collinear photon rate represents at most a 30% decrease in the IR and a 20% increase at k ~ 5T
- In QCD with alpha_s=0.3 and N_c=N_f=3, the NLO correction to the collinear rate is approximately a 100% increase
- The ratio of the coefficients of delta C'(x_perp) and C'(x_perp) is 0.71 sqrt(lambda) in N=4 SYM, compared to 2.45 g in QCD with N_c=N_f=3
- The delta m_infinity^2/m_infinity^2 ratio is approximately -0.70 sqrt(lambda) in SYM and -0.78 g in QCD with N_c=N_f=3

## Context

This work extends the NLO calculations of the transverse scattering kernel and jet quenching parameter from QCD (CaronHuot, 2008) to N=4 SYM, and extends the LO collinear photon rate in N=4 SYM (CaronHuot, 2006) to NLO. The results enable comparison between weak-coupling N=4 SYM and QCD results, and between weak and strong coupling within N=4 SYM, providing a bridge to holographic calculations via the AdS/CFT correspondence. The analysis of scalar contributions is also relevant for extending NLO calculations to the electroweak theory, where the Higgs scalar doublet contributes similarly.
