# On the Methods for Constructing Meson-Baryon Reaction Models within Relativistic Quantum Field Theory

> Within the relativistic quantum field theory, we analyze the differences between the $πN$ reaction models constructed from using (1) three-dimensional reductions of Bethe-Salpeter Equation, (2) method of unitary transformation, and (3) time-ordered perturbation theory. Their relations with the approach based on the dispersion relations of S-matrix theory are dicusssed.

## Key claim

The paper analyzes the differences between three methods for constructing πN reaction models within relativistic quantum field theory, showing that each method leads to different analytic structures in the scattering amplitudes, particularly in the unphysical region where the nucleon pole is identified, and that there is no compelling theoretical reason to favor either the dynamical models or the dispersion-relation approach in developing phenomenological models to analyze data.

## Method

The authors compare three approaches for constructing πN scattering models: (1) three-dimensional reductions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation (3dBS models), (2) the method of unitary transformation, and (3) time-ordered perturbation theory, using a simple πNN interaction Lagrangian with a single-channel πN scattering framework. For each method, they derive the scattering equations and examine the analytic structure of the resulting amplitudes, particularly the nucleon pole condition and the role of form factors. They also discuss the relation of each approach to dispersion relations from S-matrix theory.

## Key numbers

- The renormalized πNN coupling constant is identified with the empirical value g²_πNN/4π = (2m_N/m_π)²(f²_πNN/4π) = 14.3
- The nucleon pole condition requires m_N = m⁰_N + Σ_N(m_N), where m_N is the physical nucleon mass and m⁰_N is the bare nucleon mass
- The renormalization constant Z_0 is given by Z_0⁻¹ = 1 + ∫ dk k² |Γ_0(k)|²/(m_N - E_π(k) - E_N(k))²
- The on-shell amplitude in the 3dBS approach with a dipole form factor behaves as t(k₀,k₀,E) ~ [Λ²/(k₀²+Λ²)]² × 1/(E-m₀-Σ⁰_N(E)) × [Λ²/(k₀²+Λ²)]², where k₀ is defined by E = E_N(k₀) + E_π(k₀)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of πN reaction models by comparing the three-dimensional reduction methods reviewed in Klein (1970s) and studied numerically by Hung (1990s) with the unitary transformation approach of Sato, Kobayashi, and Ohtsubo (1990s) and the time-ordered perturbation theory used in the Juelich models (1990-2000). The paper clarifies that the analytic structure of dynamical models derived from relativistic quantum field theory cannot be completely consistent with that defined by dispersion relations of S-matrix theory, as reviewed in the textbook of Goldberger and Watson (1964), and that the Aaron-Amado-Young approach (1960s) using the Blankenbecler-Sugar reduction requires consistent treatment of the propagator and interaction to satisfy ππN unitarity. This analysis enables practitioners to understand the model dependence in extracting N-N* transition form factors from experimental data.
