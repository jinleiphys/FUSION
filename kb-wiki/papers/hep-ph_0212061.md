# Jet quenching and high-pt azimuthal asymmetry

> The azimuthal anisotropy of high-pt particle production in non-central heavy ion collisions is among the most promising observables of partonic energy loss in an azimuthally non-symmetric volume of quark-gluon plasma. We discuss the implications of nuclear geometry for the models of partonic energy loss in the context of recent RHIC data and consequences for observation of jet quenching at the LHC.

## Key claim

The azimuthal anisotropy of high-\(p_T\) particle production in non-central heavy ion collisions is a promising observable for partonic energy loss in an azimuthally non-symmetric quark-gluon plasma volume, but the actual magnitude of the elliptic flow \(v_2\) at high \(p_T\) depends sensitively on the nuclear geometry (diffuse Wood-Saxon vs. sharp cylinder) and on the treatment of transverse expansion, which can strongly reduce the predicted asymmetry.

## Method

The authors generalized the GLV reaction operator formalism for gluon bremsstrahlung energy loss to include 1+1D Bjorken longitudinal expansion and, separately, a transversely expanding elliptic density profile. They computed the opacity \(\chi(\phi)\) and the mean energy loss \(\Delta E^{(1)}(\phi)\) as a function of azimuthal angle \(\phi\) relative to the reaction plane, averaging over jet production points using the nuclear overlap geometry (Wood-Saxon and sharp cylinder). For LHC predictions, they simulated jet rates with PYTHIA 5.7 and a Monte Carlo model of rescattering and energy loss (collisional plus radiative via BDMS formalism) in a boost-invariant longitudinally expanding fluid.

## Key numbers

- Initial gluon density in central \(Au+Au\) at \(\sqrt{s}=130,200\) AGeV: \(\rho_g(\tau_0) \sim 15-50 / \mathrm{fm}^3\)
- Initial gluon density in central \(Pb+Pb\) at \(\sqrt{s}=5500\) AGeV: \(\rho_g(\tau_0) \sim 100-400 / \mathrm{fm}^3\)
- Effective radius of sharp cylinder equivalent to Wood-Saxon geometry for \(Au+Au\): \(R_{\mathrm{eff}} \approx 6\) fm
- Hydrodynamic elliptic flow parameterization at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) AGeV: \(v_{2s}(p_T) \approx \tanh(p_T/(10 \pm 2\ \mathrm{GeV}))\)
- For LHC, initial gluon rapidity density estimates vary from \(dN^g/dy = 2500\) to \(dN^g/dy = 8000\)
- LHC jet simulation: \(E_T^{\mathrm{jet}} > 100\) GeV, \(|\eta^{\mathrm{jet}}| < 2.5\), with luminosity \(L = 5 \times 10^{26}\ \mathrm{cm}^{-2}\mathrm{s}^{-1}\) over \(R = 1.2 \times 10^6\) s
- Resolution of impact parameter measurement in CMS forward calorimeters: \(\sigma_b \sim 0.5\) fm for central and semi-central \(Pb+Pb\) collisions
- Jet azimuthal anisotropy coefficient \(v_2^{\mathrm{jet}}\) increases almost linearly with impact parameter \(b\) and becomes maximum at \(b \sim 1.2 R_A\)

## Context

This work extends the jet quenching and elliptic flow predictions of Wang (2000) and Gyulassy (2000) by incorporating longitudinal and transverse expansion of the quark-gluon plasma, and by providing detailed LHC simulations. It contrasts with hydrodynamic approaches (Ollitrault, 1992; Kolb, 2001) that describe low-\(p_T\) elliptic flow, and with saturation-based models (Teaney, 2002) that predict a rapid \(v_2 \propto k_T^{-2}\) falloff. The results enable experimental tests at RHIC (where preliminary STAR data up to \(p_T \simeq 10\) GeV support a slow decrease of \(v_2\)) and at LHC, where jet azimuthal anisotropy and impact-parameter-dependent dijet rates can distinguish between energy-loss mechanisms and constrain initial conditions.
