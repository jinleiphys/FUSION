# Faddeev-type calculation of eta-d threshold scattering

> The scattering length for the eta-meson collision with deuteron is calculated on the basis of rigorous few-body equations (AGS) for various eta-N input. The results obtained strongly support the existence of a resonance or quasi-bound state close to the eta-d threshold.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the eta-deuteron scattering length using exact AGS few-body equations and find that the results "strongly suggest" a resonance or quasi-bound state could exist near the eta-d threshold, but the existence depends on the poorly known eta-nucleon input parameters, particularly the real part of the eta-nucleon scattering length exceeding approximately 0.5 fm.

## Method

The authors solved the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) three-body equations for the eta-deuteron system, using separable S-wave two-body T-matrices for both the nucleon-nucleon and eta-nucleon interactions. The eta-nucleon interaction was treated as a one-channel complex potential with parameters fitted to the S11 resonance N*(1535), and the scattering length was evaluated at zero collision energy by solving the resulting one-dimensional integral equations numerically.

## Key numbers

- The triplet NN scattering length used was a_NN = 5.424 fm, with effective range r_NN = 1.759 fm, giving a deuteron binding energy of 2.205 MeV and RMS radius 1.887 fm.
- The eta-nucleon scattering length a_etaN is known only within the range 0.27 fm <= Re a_etaN <= 0.98 fm and 0.19 fm <= Im a_etaN <= 0.37 fm.
- For a_etaN = (0.55 + i0.30) fm with alpha = 3.316 fm^-1, the AGS calculation gives A_etaD = (1.64 + i2.99) fm.
- For a_etaN = (0.98 + i0.37) fm with alpha = 3.316 fm^-1, the AGS calculation gives A_etaD = (-4.69 + i1.59) fm.
- The S11 resonance N*(1535) has width Gamma = 150 MeV and lies at E_res = 49 MeV above the eta-N threshold.

## Context

This work extends earlier finite-rank approximation (FRA) calculations by the same group (Rakityansky et al., 1995-1996) by using exact AGS equations that properly account for nuclear excitations, which were neglected in the FRA approach. The results are compared with approximate methods including the Multiple Scattering Theory (MST) of Green, Niskanen, and Wycech (1996) and the earlier AGS calculation by Ueda (1991). The findings support the possibility of eta-mesic nuclei and are consistent with the observed enhancement in the p+n -> d+eta reaction cross section near threshold reported by Calen et al. (1998).
