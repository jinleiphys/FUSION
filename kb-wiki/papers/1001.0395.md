# Response functions of cold neutron matter: density fluctuations

> We compute the finite temperature density response function of nonrelativistic cold fermions with an isotropic condensate. The pair-breaking contribution to the response function is evaluated in the limit of small three-momentum transfers q within an effective theory which exploits series expansion in powers of small q/p_F, where p_F is the Fermi momentum. The leading order O(q^2) contribution is universal and depends only on two fundamental scales, the Fermi energy and the pairing gap. The particle-hole Landau Fermi-liquid interaction contributes first at the next-to-leading-order O(q^4). The scattering contribution to the polarization tensor is nonperturbative (in the above sense) and is evaluated numerically. The spectral functions of density fluctuations are constructed and the relevance of the q^2 scaling for the pair-breaking neutrino emission from neutron stars is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the leading-order term in the low-momentum expansion of the pair-breaking polarization tensor for cold neutron matter is of order \(q^2\) and is nonzero, contrary to some earlier work that found the leading contribution at order \(q^4\); this result is universal, depending only on the Fermi energy and the pairing gap, and is independent of the particle-hole interaction strength.

## Method

The authors compute the finite-temperature density response function of nonrelativistic cold fermions with an isotropic \(^1S_0\) pair condensate using a systematic expansion in powers of \(y = q/p_F\) (the ratio of momentum transfer to Fermi momentum) for the pair-breaking contribution. The particle-hole Landau Fermi-liquid interaction is included at next-to-leading order \(O(q^4)\), and the scattering contribution to the polarization tensor is evaluated numerically via one-dimensional energy integrals after angular integration. The vertex functions are derived from integral equations that distinguish particle-particle and particle-hole interactions, and the weak-coupling BCS limit is applied.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order pair-breaking polarization tensor scales as \((q/p_F)^2\), with the imaginary part given by \(\text{Im}\,\Pi^R(q) = \text{Im}\,\mathcal{P}_2(\omega) (q/p_F)^2 + O(q^4)\), where \(\text{Im}\,\mathcal{P}_2\) is expressed analytically in terms of \(\Delta\), \(\mu\), \(T\), and \(\omega\) (see Eq. (calP_final)).
* The zero-temperature gap at Fermi momentum \(p_F = 0.1\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) is \(\Delta(0) = 1\ \text{MeV}\), with critical temperature \(T_c = \Delta(0)/1.76\).
* The pair-breaking threshold is at \(\omega = 2\Delta(T)\); below this threshold the imaginary part of the pair-breaking polarization tensor is identically zero.
* The scattering contribution is evaluated numerically for fixed momentum transfer \(q = 0.1 p_F\) at temperatures \(T = 0.2 T_c\) and \(T = 0.9 T_c\).

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature response theory of pair-correlated nuclear matter, building on the earlier polarization tensor formalism of Sedrakian (2006) and the zero-temperature results of Larkin and Migdal. It contradicts the findings of Leinson (2006) and Kolomeitsev (2008), who reported that the leading-order pair-breaking density response vanishes and the first nonzero term appears at order \(q^4\). The results enable improved calculations of vector-current neutrino emission via pair-breaking processes in neutron star cooling and superbursts, and provide a systematic low-momentum expansion that can be tested against exact numerical evaluations.
