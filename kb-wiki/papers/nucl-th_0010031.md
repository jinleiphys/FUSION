# Effective Field Theory in Nuclear Many-Body Physics

> Recent progress in Lorentz-covariant quantum field theories of the nuclear many-body problem (quantum hadrodynamics, or QHD) is discussed. The importance of modern perspectives in effective field theory and density functional theory for understanding the successes of QHD is emphasized. To appear in: 150 Years of Quantum Many-Body Theory: A conference in honour of the 65th birthdays of John W. Clark, Alpo J. Kallio, Manfred L. Ristig, and Sergio Rosati.

# Effective Field Theory in Nuclear Many-Body Physics

## Key claim

Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) can be understood as a low-energy effective field theory derived from QCD, where the mean-field parametrization of the energy functional provides an excellent approximation to the exact density functional in the relevant density regime, provided the parameters are fit to empirical bulk and single-particle nuclear data. The authors argue that the hierarchy of interaction terms in the effective Lagrangian is organized by a counting scheme where successive terms are reduced by roughly a factor of five, allowing truncation at low order for any reasonable desired accuracy.

## Method

The authors construct a Lorentz-covariant effective Lagrangian for nuclear many-body physics using hadronic degrees of freedom (baryons and mesons), incorporating spontaneously broken chiral symmetry through a nonlinear realization of SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R. They then relate this Lagrangian to density functional theory via the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem and Kohn-Sham potentials, where the energy functional becomes a functional of both scalar and vector densities. The resulting Dirac-Hartree equations are solved self-consistently for closed-shell nuclei (¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁸Ca, ⁸⁸Sr, and ²⁰⁸Pb) and in nuclear matter, with parameters best-fit to 29 empirical values using a χ² figure of merit.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless combinations g_s φ₀/M ≈ φ₀/f_π and g_v V₀/M ≈ V₀/f_π are roughly 1/3, providing convenient expansion parameters
- Each successive term in the hierarchy of interaction terms is reduced by roughly a factor of five in nuclear matter energy/particle
- The total binding energy of nuclear matter is ε₀ = 16.1 MeV
- The χ² figure of merit improves by more than a factor of 35 in going from ν = 2 to ν = 4 truncation order
- No further improvement in χ² is observed when going from ν = 4 to ν = 5 truncation order
- Only six or seven parameters at order ν = 4 are determined by the data set of 29 empirical values

## Context

This work extends the program of Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) developed by Serot and Walecka over the preceding decades, as reviewed in Serot (1986) and the text by Walecka. The paper connects relativistic mean-field theory to density functional theory via the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem and Kohn-Sham potentials, placing earlier phenomenological success on a firmer theoretical foundation. The results enable systematic calculation of corrections to mean-field parametrizations using the effective hadronic Lagrangian, with an open goal of finding a nonperturbative way to match the low-energy effective field theory to the underlying QCD Lagrangian.
