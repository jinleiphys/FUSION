# Repercussions of the Peccei-Quinn axion on QCD

> The axion, originally postulated by Peccei and Quinn to solve the strong CP problem, has become of great interest in particle and astroparticle phenomenology. Yet it has a problem. It is widely assumed that the axion leaves the nonperturbative features of QCD unscathed. This is, however, not the case. On the contrary, the axion extension is found to be in conflict with seminal results on the low-energy properties of QCD. The key is that the anomalous coupling of the axion to the gauge bosons can be integrated out, leaving behind a path integral over topologically trivial gauge potentials.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Peccei-Quinn axion extension of QCD is incompatible with established low-energy properties of QCD because integrating out the axion's anomalous coupling to gauge bosons forces the path integral to project onto gauge field configurations with zero topological charge, contradicting the nontrivial topological structure essential to QCD phenomenology.

## Method

The author integrates out the axion field's zero-mode component (the spacetime average \(\bar{\phi}\)) from the generating functional, which yields a Kronecker delta \(\delta_{Q,0}\) that restricts the gauge fields to the sector of zero topological charge \(Q=0\). The remaining axion fluctuations are absorbed into the derivative coupling to quark axial currents via partial integration, leaving a path integral over topologically trivial gauge potentials. The consequences for QCD observables are then derived by analyzing expectation values in the \(Q=0\) sector, using the index theorem linking topological charge to Dirac zero modes and lattice QCD results.

## Key numbers

* Topological susceptibility from lattice QCD: \(\chi_t = (180.5(5)(43)\,\text{MeV})^4\) (from Ce et al., 2015), which the paper argues is invalidated by the axion extension.
* Axion mass estimate from lattice QCD: \(m_a = 50(4)\,\mu\text{eV}\) (from Borsanyi et al., 2016), which the paper argues is invalidated.
* String tension from pure SU(3) Yang-Mills theory: \(\sqrt{\sigma} = 445(19)\,\text{MeV}\) (from Nakamura et al., 2021), which the paper argues does not hold in the \(Q=0\) sector.
* Upper bound on neutron electric dipole moment: \(|d_n| < 1.8 \times 10^{-13}\,e\,\text{fm}\) (from Abel et al., 2020), motivating the strong CP problem.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical critique of the Peccei-Quinn mechanism by showing that the axion's shift symmetry conflicts with the nontrivial \(\theta\) dependence of QCD predicted by 't Hooft (1979) and observed in lattice simulations by Ce et al. (2015) and Nakamura et al. (2021). It contradicts the widespread assumption that the axion leaves QCD's nonperturbative features unchanged, and it invalidates the Witten-Veneziano relation (Witten, 1979; Veneziano, 1979) and axion dark matter mass estimates from finite-temperature QCD (Borsanyi et al., 2016). The paper enables future work to reexamine axion phenomenology under the constraint that the axion extension forces QCD into the \(Q=0\) sector, potentially affecting confinement, chiral symmetry breaking, and the \(\eta'\) mass.
