# Relativistic Equation of State for Core-Collapse Supernova Simulations

> We construct the equation of state (EOS) of dense matter covering a wide range of temperature, proton fraction, and density for the use of core-collapse supernova simulations. The study is based on the relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory, which can provide an excellent description of nuclear matter and finite nuclei. The Thomas--Fermi approximation in combination with assumed nucleon distribution functions and a free energy minimization is adopted to describe the non-uniform matter, which is composed of a lattice of heavy nuclei. We treat the uniform matter and non-uniform matter consistently using the same RMF theory. We present two sets of EOS tables, namely EOS2 and EOS3. EOS2 is an update of our earlier work published in 1998 (EOS1), where only the nucleon degree of freedom is taken into account. EOS3 includes additional contributions from $Λ$ hyperons. The effect of $Λ$ hyperons on the EOS is negligible in the low-temperature and low-density region, whereas it tends to soften the EOS at high density. In comparison with EOS1, EOS2 and EOS3 have an improved design of ranges and grids, which covers the temperature range $T=0.1$--$10^{2.6}$ MeV with the logarithmic grid spacing $Δ\log_{10}(T/\rm{[MeV]})=0.04$ (92 points including T=0), the proton fraction range $Y_p=0$--0.65 with the linear grid spacing $ΔY_p = 0.01$ (66 points), and the density range $ρ_B=10^{5.1}$--$10^{16}\,\rm{g\,cm^{-3}}$ with the logarithmic grid spacing $Δ\log_{10}(ρ_B/\rm{[g\,cm^{-3}]}) = 0.1$ (110 points).

## Key claim

The authors construct two new equation-of-state tables (EOS2 and EOS3) for core-collapse supernova simulations, where EOS3 includes contributions from Lambda hyperons under the assumption that weak equilibrium (mu_n = mu_Lambda) holds at high density, while noting that the nuclear EOS at extremely high temperature and/or density is not reliable due to the QCD phase transition to quark-gluon plasma around T_c = 175 MeV.

## Method

The equation of state is constructed using relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory with the TM1 parameter set, which includes nonlinear sigma and omega terms. For non-uniform matter at low temperature and density where heavy nuclei form, the Thomas-Fermi approximation is used with assumed nucleon distribution functions and free energy minimization within a Wigner-Seitz cell, treating the matter as a mixture of a single species of heavy nuclei, alpha particles, and free nucleons. For EOS3, Lambda hyperons are included under the equilibrium condition mu_n = mu_Lambda, with coupling constants g_omega^Lambda/g_omega = 2/3 based on the naive quark model and g_sigma^Lambda/g_sigma = 0.621 fitted to experimental binding energies of single-Lambda hypernuclei.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range: T = 0.1 to 10^2.6 MeV with logarithmic grid spacing Delta log10(T/[MeV]) = 0.04 (92 points including T=0)
- Proton fraction range: Y_p = 0 to 0.65 with linear grid spacing Delta Y_p = 0.01 (66 points)
- Baryon mass density range: rho_B = 10^5.1 to 10^16 g cm^-3 with logarithmic grid spacing Delta log10(rho_B/[g cm^-3]) = 0.1 (110 points)
- Nuclear matter saturation density with TM1 parameter set: 0.145 fm^-3, binding energy per nucleon 16.3 MeV, symmetry energy 36.9 MeV, incompressibility 281 MeV
- Lambda potential depth in nuclear matter at saturation density: approximately -30 MeV
- Critical temperature for QCD phase transition at zero baryon density: T_c = 175 MeV
- Alpha-particle binding energy: B_alpha = 28.3 MeV, taken from Lattimer (1991)
- Effective volume of alpha-particle: v_alpha = 24 fm^-3, taken from Lattimer (1991)
- Surface energy parameter: F_0 = 70 MeV fm^5
- BCC lattice Coulomb correction constant: C_BCC = 0.006562, taken from Oyamatsu (1993)

## Context

This work updates the earlier Shen EOS (EOS1, Shen 1998) by improving the grid design with finer temperature spacing, a linear Y_p grid, and extended ranges, while also adding Lambda hyperon contributions in EOS3. The new tables are intended for use in core-collapse supernova simulations, black hole formation studies, neutron star mergers, and nucleosynthesis, extending the existing realistic EOSs commonly used in simulations by Lattimer (1991) and Shen (1998). The inclusion of Lambda hyperons follows the approach of Ishizuka (2008) who examined hyperon effects in supernova matter, and the results are constrained by the neutron star mass measurement of PSR J1614-2230 at 1.97 +/- 0.04 solar masses from Demorest (2010).
