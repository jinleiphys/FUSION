# van der Waals and Casimir-Polder interactions between neutrons

> We investigate the van der Waals interaction between neutrons using the theory of Casimir and Polder, wherein the potential for asymptotically large separations falls off as the inverse seventh power, and compare it to the similar interaction between a neutron and a proton, for which the asymptotic interaction falls off as the inverse fourth power. Modifications of the formalism to extend the validity to smaller separations using dynamic electric and magnetic dipole polarizability data are discussed

## Key claim

The long-range van der Waals interaction between two neutrons falls off as the inverse seventh power of separation at asymptotically large distances, while the neutron-proton interaction falls off as the inverse fourth power; these results are asymptotic limits of more general expressions involving dynamic electric and magnetic dipole polarizabilities, and the authors are using dynamic polarizabilities from chiral effective field theory to extend the validity to smaller separations.

## Method

The authors apply the Casimir-Polder formalism to compute the asymptotic long-range electromagnetic interactions between two neutrons and between a neutron and a proton, using static electric and magnetic dipole polarizabilities of the neutron from the Particle Data Group (Olive et al. 2014). They also discuss incorporating dynamic polarizabilities from chiral effective field theory (χEFT) to extend the calculations to smaller separations, building on earlier work by Arnold (1973) and the general formalism described by Babb (2010).

## Key numbers

- Neutron electric dipole polarizability: α_n = (11.8 ± 1.1) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (from Olive et al. 2014)
- Neutron magnetic dipole polarizability: β_n = (3.7 ± 1.2) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (from Olive et al. 2014)
- Asymptotic neutron-neutron potential: V^∞_nn(r) = -(c/4π)[23(α_n α_n + β_n β_n) - 14(α_n β_n)] R⁻⁷ + O(R⁻⁹), where c = 137 in atomic units
- Asymptotic neutron-proton potential: V^∞_pn(R) = -(1/2) α_n R⁻⁴ + (1/4πcM_p)(11α_n + 5β_n) R⁻⁵ + O(R⁻⁷), where M_p is the proton mass

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis by Arnold (1973), who considered parts of the neutron-neutron Casimir-Polder potential and its effect on the neutron scattering length but at a time when β_n was unknown and the accepted α_n was twice today's value. The authors also build on the general Casimir-Polder formalism described by Babb (2010) and the neutron polarizability calculations from chiral effective field theory by Griesshammer et al. (2012). The results enable more accurate calculations of neutron-neutron and neutron-proton scattering, as well as neutron-wall interactions, by incorporating dynamic polarizabilities that include contributions from excited states of the neutron.
