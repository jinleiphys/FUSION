# Statistical Hadronization probed by resonances

> We study to what extent a measurement of the $m_\bot$ spectra for hadrons and their resonances can resolve ambiguities in statistical model description of particle production. We describe in quantitative analysis how physical assumptions about the freeze-out geometry and dynamics influence the particle spectra. Considering ratios of $m_\bot$ distribution of resonance-particle ratios (such as $K^*/K$, $Σ^*/Λ, η'/η$) we observe significant sensitivity to fireball freeze-out geometry and flow dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the ratio of resonance to daughter particle yields as a function of transverse mass ($m_\bot$) is sensitive to freeze-out geometry and flow dynamics, and that measuring this $m_\bot$ dependence can resolve ambiguities between different statistical hadronization models that currently yield conflicting freeze-out temperature values ranging from 110 MeV to 180 MeV.

## Method

The authors compute particle spectra using the Cooper-Frye formula with different freeze-out hypersurfaces (constant time, Hubble flow, and blast-wave/burning-log models) and flow profiles. They then calculate the $m_\bot$ dependence of resonance-to-particle ratios (such as $K^*/K$, $\Sigma^*/\Lambda$, $\eta'/\eta$) for each model, including feed-down corrections from resonance decays, to identify which observables can distinguish between hadronization scenarios.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature values from different groups range from as low as 110 MeV to as high as 180 MeV at both SPS and RHIC energies
- Introduction of phase space occupancy parameters $\gamma_i$ lowers the freeze-out temperature by approximately 30 MeV compared to chemical equilibrium fits
- Two representative freeze-out conditions are compared: $T=140$ MeV with $v_{max}/c=0.55$ and $T=170$ MeV with $v_{max}/c=0.3$
- Approximately 50% of hadronic spectra comprise contributions from resonance decays
- 95% of $\Sigma^*$ decays occur through the $p$-wave $\Sigma^* \rightarrow \Lambda \pi$ channel

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model approach used by Becattini (2001), the NA57 collaboration, and others at SPS and RHIC energies, where conflicting freeze-out temperatures have been reported. The paper builds on earlier proposals by the same authors and others (2001) that resonances can differentiate between freeze-out models, and on the Cooper-Frye formalism (1974) for particle emission. The results enable experimental discrimination between competing hadronization scenarios (constant-time freeze-out, Hubble expansion, and sudden hadronization) using resonance measurements at RHIC and future facilities.
