# Hadronic matter under an external magnetic field: in medium modification of the pion mass

> The covariant propagator of a fermion with intrinsic magnetic moment interacting with a uniform external magnetic field is presented for finite temperature and baryonic density. The case of a scalar boson is also considered. The final expressions are given in terms of a four-dimensional momentum representation. These results, which take account of the full effect of the magnetic field, are used to evaluate the modification of the pion mass at zero temperature as a function of the density and the magnetic intensity. For this purpose a self-consistent calculation, including one- and two-pion vertices, is employed.

# Hadronic matter under an external magnetic field: In-medium modification of the pion mass

## Key claim

The covariant propagator of a Dirac fermion with intrinsic magnetic moment in a uniform external magnetic field is presented in a pure four-momentum representation (improving on the mixed-coordinate representation of Aguirre and De Paoli (2016)), and this formalism is used to evaluate the modification of the pion mass at zero temperature as a function of baryonic density and magnetic intensity; the dependence of the neutral pion mass on the magnetic field in pure neutron matter is an effect exclusively due to the neutron magnetic moment.

## Method

The fermion propagator is constructed via canonical expansion using exact eigenfunctions of the Dirac equation with anomalous magnetic moment in a uniform magnetic field, then transformed to a four-momentum representation via a gauge transformation following Schwinger's approach. The in-medium pion polarization is evaluated in a Quantum Hadro-Dynamics (QHD) model at the one-loop level, including the Weinberg-Tomozawa contact term (first order) and the pseudovector one-pion vertex (second order), with nucleon propagators incorporating the mean-field approximation for the scalar and vector meson fields. The effective pion mass is defined as the solution of the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the pion propagator pole at zero three-momentum.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field intensities considered: $10^{16}$ G to $10^{19}$ G
- Baryonic densities considered: $0 < n_B < 3 n_0$, where $n_0$ is the nuclear saturation density
- For charged pions in pure neutron matter, the effective mass can be approximated as $m_{\pm}^*/m_\pi = \sqrt{1 + (a r)^2} \pm a r$, where $a = n_0 / (4 f_\pi^2 m_\pi)$ and $r = n_B / n_0$
- For symmetric nuclear matter at $B = 10^{19}$ G, a drop of roughly 4% in the negatively charged pion effective mass occurs at $n_B/n_0 \simeq 1.6$
- The imaginary part of the pion polarization satisfies $\tau = |\text{Im} \, \Pi(p_0 = m^*)| / m^* < 0.12$ for all densities and magnetic intensities considered
- For $B = 10^{17}$ G, the proton Landau level occupation reaches $n = 120$; for $B = 10^{18}$ G, it reaches $n = 12$

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Aguirre and De Paoli (2016) by providing a pure four-momentum representation for the fermion propagator with intrinsic magnetic moment, improving on the mixed-coordinate representation previously available. It complements studies of pion effective mass in magnetized matter by Colucci et al. and Adhya et al., which did not include intrinsic nucleon magnetic moments. The results enable more complete diagrammatic calculations in magnetized hadronic matter and are relevant for understanding pion propagation in neutron stars and heavy-ion collision environments, where magnetic fields of order $10^{15}$ G at stellar surfaces or $eB \sim 10^2$ MeV$^2$ in collisions have been estimated.
