# Pion and kaon condensation in a 3-flavor NJL model

> We analyze the phase diagram of a three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model at finite temperature $T$ and chemical potentials $μ_u, μ_d, μ_s$. We study the competition of pion and kaon condensation and we propose a physical situation in which kaon condensation could be led only by light quark finite densities.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that kaon condensation could occur driven only by light quark finite densities (with low or zero strange quark chemical potential), but within the approximations used this does not actually happen because the kaon mass is higher than the critical value of the light quark chemical potential for melting the dynamical part of the light quark condensate.

## Method

The authors use a three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a form factor that mimics asymptotic freedom, at finite temperature and chemical potentials for u, d, and s quarks. They compute the one-loop effective potential via the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation, introducing bosonic collective fields for scalar and pseudoscalar condensates, and determine the phase diagram by finding the absolute minimum of the effective potential in the space of temperature and chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_pi = 142 MeV; kaon mass: m_K = 500 MeV (from model parameter fit)
- Light quark condensate in vacuum: <bar Psi_f Psi_f> = -(252 MeV)^3
- Constituent light quark mass: M_q = 385 MeV
- Critical isospin chemical potential for pion condensation at T=0: mu_I^C = m_pi/2 (agreement within 1%)
- Critical hypercharge chemical potential for kaon condensation at T=0 and mu_u = mu_d: mu_Y^C = m_K/2 (agreement within 4%)
- Kaon condensed phase at mu_I=0 disappears for T > 85 MeV
- Pion condensation disappears above T = 192 MeV
- Model parameters: Lambda = 1000 MeV, g = G*Lambda^2 = 6, m = 1.7 MeV, m_s = 42 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous two-flavor NJL analyses by Barducci et al. (2004) and Toublan and Kogut (2003) to include strange quarks and kaon condensation, and compares with a chiral Lagrangian study by Kogut and Toublan (2001). The results accurately reproduce the chiral model predictions only at low densities; at high strangeness density, approximate chiral symmetry restoration in the strange sector disfavors kaon relative to pion condensation. The analysis enables future inclusion of diquark condensation (superconductive phases) and beta-equilibrium with charge and color neutrality for applications to compact star physics.
