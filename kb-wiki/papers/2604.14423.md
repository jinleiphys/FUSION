# Constraining the $N=16$ Shell Gap in $^{17}$C via Transfer to the Continuum in the $^{16}$C$(d,p)^{17}$C Reaction

> Recently, a semi-microscopic structure model has been presented to study the structure of a weakly-bound, two-body nucleus with a deformed core, including Pauli-blocking effects. The model has been successfully applied within the adiabatic distorted wave approximation (ADWA) reaction framework to study the reactions $^{16}$C(d, p)$^{17}$C, restricting the analysis to bound states of the residual $^{17}$C nucleus. In these calculations, the structure of $^{17}$C is described using the recently presented semimicroscopic Nilsson+AMD model (NAMD), considering different Pauli-blocking methods. In the present work, the analysis is extended to unbound states of this nucleus with the aim of constraining the location of the $1d_{3/2}$ single-particle strength and infer the $N=16$ shell-gap. Comparing the measured energy differential cross section for this reaction with calculations in which the position of the $1d_{3/2}$ orbital is arbitrarily varied, we conclude that a large shell-gap (>5 MeV) is required, in agreement with recently reported value from [J. Lois-Fuentes et al., Phys. Lett. B 867, 139600 (2025)].

## Key claim

By extending transfer-to-the-continuum calculations to unbound states of ¹⁷C using the semi-microscopic Nilsson+AMD (NAMD) model, the authors conclude that the N=16 shell gap must be larger than 5 MeV to reproduce the measured energy differential cross section, corroborating the lower limit of Δ = 5.08⁺⁰·⁴³₋₀·₃₃ MeV reported by Lois-Fuentes et al. (2025).

## Method

The authors combined the adiabatic distorted wave approximation (ADWA) reaction framework with the NAMD structure model for ¹⁷C, treating the continuum via a pseudo-states discretisation method using a transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) basis. They calculated energy-differential cross sections for the ¹⁶C(d,p)¹⁷C reaction at 17.2 MeV/nucleon, comparing two Pauli-blocking treatments (no-blocking, NoB; total blocking, TB) and three variants of the NAMD model with different spin-orbit splittings corresponding to N=16 shell gaps of 5.8, 5.0, and 4.0 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The N=16 shell gap lower limit from the experimental data analysis is Δ = 5.08⁺⁰·⁴³₋₀·₃₃ MeV (Lois-Fuentes et al., 2025).
- The NAMD model with total blocking (TB) yields a shell gap of Δ = 5.8 MeV.
- The ¹⁶C beam energy was 17.2 MeV/nucleon; protons were measured at laboratory angles 147° ≤ θ_lab ≤ 167°, corresponding to ¹⁷C center-of-mass angles θ_CM < 11°.
- Pseudo-states were included up to 13 MeV above the neutron separation threshold, with total angular momenta J ≤ 9/2 (positive parity) and J ≤ 7/2 (negative parity).
- Transfer to the continuum becomes significant from approximately 2 MeV above the neutron separation threshold.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous NAMD model calculations (Punta et al., 2023, 2025) from bound states of ¹⁷C to unbound states, enabling direct comparison with the experimental transfer data from GANIL reported by Lois-Fuentes et al. (2025). The results support the existence of a large N=16 shell gap for neutron-rich carbon isotopes, consistent with the shell evolution observed near the neutron dripline for Oxygen (Hoffman et al., 2008; Tshoo et al., 2012). The formalism is intended for application to other exotic nuclei such as ¹⁹C and ³¹Ne to study shell evolution in islands of inversion.
