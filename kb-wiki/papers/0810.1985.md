# Light quark energy loss in strongly-coupled N = 4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma

> We compute the penetration depth of a light quark moving through a large $N_c$, strongly coupled $\mathcal N = 4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma using gauge/gravity duality and a combination of analytic and numerical techniques. We find that the maximum distance a quark with energy $E$ can travel through a plasma is given by $Δx(E) = (\mathcal C/T) (E/T \sqrtλ)^{{1}/{3}}$ with $\mathcal C \approx 0.5$.

## Key claim

The maximum distance a light quark with energy \(E\) can travel through a strongly coupled \(\mathcal N = 4\) supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma scales as \(\Delta x(E) = (\mathcal C/T) (E/T \sqrt\lambda)^{1/3}\) with \(\mathcal C \approx 0.5\), but this bound is only valid for "reasonable" initial conditions; the instantaneous energy loss rate is non-universal and depends strongly on the precise choice of initial string profile and its time derivative.

## Method

The authors used gauge/gravity duality to model light quarks as open strings in an AdS-Schwarzschild geometry, with the string endpoints attached to a D7 brane representing a massless \(\mathcal N = 2\) hypermultiplet. They solved the classical Nambu-Goto equations of motion numerically for many different initial conditions where the string is created at a single spacetime point, and also performed analytic analysis by treating the strings as small perturbations away from null string configurations. The penetration depth was defined using the centroid of the baryon density, which approximately coincides with the string endpoint position when the endpoint is close to the boundary.

## Key numbers

- The maximum penetration depth is \(\Delta x = (0.526/T) \, (E / T \sqrt{\lambda})^{1/3}\), with the constant \(\mathcal C \approx 0.5\) determined numerically from the upper envelope of data points in a log-log plot.
- The temperature of the equilibrium SYM plasma is \(T \equiv (\pi u_h)^{-1}\), where \(u_h\) is the horizon location in the AdS-Schwarzschild metric.
- The energy contained near a string endpoint scales like \(1/u_*^3\), where \(u_*\) is the radial coordinate of the endpoint when it is close to the boundary.
- The total spatial distance a null geodesic can travel before falling into the horizon scales like \(u_h^2 / u_0\), where \(u_0\) is the initial radial coordinate.

## Context

This work extends the qualitative scaling estimate \(\Delta x \sim E^{1/3}\) suggested by Gubser (2008) for gluons in strongly coupled SYM plasma, but provides the first quantitative, controlled study with full solutions to the string equations of motion rather than assuming light-like geodesics. The authors find that the relationship between geodesic parameters and string energy is more complicated than the surmises in Gubser (2008). The results enable future studies of jet thermalization in strongly coupled plasmas and provide a benchmark for comparing energy loss mechanisms between light and heavy quarks, where heavy quarks (studied by Herzog (2006) and others) have a velocity-dependent energy loss rate that monotonically decreases, unlike the light quark case where the loss rate can increase with time and peak in an explosive burst.
