# Final-state interactions in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering off the Deuteron

> Semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering off the Deuteron with production of a slow nucleon in recoil kinematics is studied in the virtual nucleon approximation, in which the final state interaction (FSI) is calculated within general eikonal approximation. The cross section is derived in a factorized approach, with a factor describing the virtual photon interaction with the off-shell nucleon and a distorted spectral function accounting for the final-state interactions. One of the main goals of the study is to understand how much the general features of the diffractive high energy soft rescattering accounts for the observed features of FSI in deep inelastic scattering(DIS). Comparison with the Jefferson Lab data shows good agreement in the covered range of kinematics. Most importantly, our calculation correctly reproduces the rise of the FSI in the forward direction of the slow nucleon production angle. By fitting our calculation to the data we extracted the $W$ and $Q^2$ dependences of the total cross section and slope factor of the interaction of DIS products, $X$, off the spectator nucleon. This analysis shows the $XN$ scattering cross section rising with $W$ and decreasing with an increase of $Q^2$. Finally, our analysis points at a largely suppressed off-shell part of the rescattering amplitude.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the observed final-state interactions (FSI) in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering off the deuteron can be largely accounted for by the general properties of diffractive high-energy soft rescattering, without requiring detailed knowledge of the specific hadronic intermediate state produced after the initial DIS. The analysis points at a largely suppressed off-shell part of the rescattering amplitude.

## Method

The authors used the virtual nucleon approximation (VNA) to treat the electromagnetic interaction with the bound nucleon, and the generalized eikonal approximation (GEA) to calculate the final-state interaction of the DIS products with the spectator nucleon. The cross section was derived in a factorized approach, splitting the calculation into a part describing the virtual photon interaction with the off-shell nucleon and a distorted spectral function that accounts for FSI. The distorted spectral function was computed by integrating over the intermediate spectator nucleon momentum, using the pole structure of the deuteron wave function parameterizations and the propagator pole, with the on-shell forward scattering amplitude parameterized in diffractive form.

## Key numbers

- The analysis used data from the Jefferson Lab *Deeps* experiment (Klimenko 2005), with events binned in \(Q^2\), \(p_s\), \(\cos \theta_s\), and \(\hat{x}\) or the invariant mass \(W\).
- The validity of the VNA is limited to spectator proton momenta \(p_s \leq 700\) MeV/c, as stated in the text.
- The factorization approximation (DWIA) was checked for quasielastic scattering and found to work reasonably well for \(p_s=400\) MeV/c at \(Q^2=2-4\) GeV\(^2\), but systematically underestimates FSI at larger momenta.
- The diffractive slope factor \(B\) is estimated as \(B = 4-6\) GeV\(^{-2}\), giving \(k_{\perp,RMS} \approx 500-600\) MeV/c.
- The ratio of longitudinal to transverse cross sections for scattering off the nucleon was taken as \(R = \sigma_L/\sigma_T \approx 0.18\).

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical approaches to FSI in semi-inclusive DIS off the deuteron, including those by Simula (1996), Melnitchouk (1996), Sargsian (2005), and Ciofi degli Atti (1999, 2002, 2003). The study enables extraction of the \(W\) and \(Q^2\) dependences of the total cross section and slope factor for the interaction of DIS products with the spectator nucleon, which can inform models of hadronization and the space-time structure of the hadronic state produced in DIS. The results also constrain the off-shell part of the rescattering amplitude, which was found to be largely suppressed.
