# The I^G J^{PC}=1^- 1^{-+} Tetraquark States

> We study the tetraquark states with I^G J^{PC}=1^- 1^{-+} in the QCD sum rule. After exhausting all possible flavor structures, we analyses both the SVZ and finite energy sum rules. Both approaches lead to a mass around 1.6 GeV for the state with the quark contents q q q_bar q_bar, and around 2.0 GeV for the state with the quark contents q s q_bar s_bar. The flavor structure 3_bar * 6_bar + 6 * 3 is preferred. Our analysis strongly indicates that both pi1(1600) and pi1(2015) are also compatible with the exotic tetraquark interpretation, which are sometimes labeled as candidates of the 1^{-+} hybrid mesons. Moreover one of their dominant decay modes is a pair of axial-vector and pseudoscalar mesons such as b1(1235) pi, which is sometimes considered as the characteristic decay mode of the hybrid mesons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the experimentally observed exotic mesons π₁(1600) and π₁(2015) are compatible with a tetraquark interpretation rather than exclusively being hybrid mesons, with the caveat that π₁(1400) cannot be explained by the tetraquark structure considered here.

## Method

The authors classified all possible flavor structures for tetraquark states with quantum numbers I^G J^PC = 1⁻ 1⁻⁺, constructing interpolating currents using both diquark-antidiquark and quark-antiquark pair bases. They performed QCD sum rule analyses using both the SVZ (Shifman-Vainshtein-Zakharov) method and finite energy sum rules, calculating operator product expansions up to dimension twelve for the two-point correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- Mass around 1.6 GeV for the tetraquark state with quark contents q q \bar q \bar q (light quarks only)
- Mass around 2.0 GeV for the tetraquark state with quark contents q s \bar q \bar s (containing one strange quark pair)
- Experimental masses: π₁(1400) at 1376 ± 17 MeV, π₁(1600) at 1653^{+18}_{-15} MeV, π₁(2015) at 2014 ± 20 ± 16 MeV
- Experimental widths: π₁(1400) 300 ± 40 MeV, π₁(1600) 225^{+45}_{-28} MeV, π₁(2015) 230 ± 21 ± 73 MeV
- The production rate of π₁(1600) in photo-production was less than 2% of the a₂(1320) meson

## Context

This work extends the systematic method for tetraquark studies previously developed by Chen, Hosaka, and Zhu (2006-2008) to the specific exotic quantum number channel 1⁻⁺. It directly addresses the ongoing debate about whether π₁(1600) and π₁(2015) are hybrid mesons (as suggested by lattice QCD simulations by McNeile (1998), Hedditch (2005), and Bernard (2003) giving masses around 1.7-2.0 GeV) or tetraquark states. The negative CLAS photo-production result for π₁(1600) (Nozar, 2008) motivated the alternative tetraquark interpretation. The analysis enables future experimental discrimination between hybrid and tetraquark interpretations through decay pattern studies, particularly the b₁(1235)π decay mode.
