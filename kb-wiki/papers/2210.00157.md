# Deconstructing experimental decay energy spectra: the $^{26}$O case

> In nuclear reaction experiments, the measured decay energy spectra can give insights into the shell structure of decaying systems. However, extracting the underlying physics from the measurements is challenging due to detector resolution and acceptance effects. The Richardson-Lucy (RL) algorithm, a deblurring method that is commonly used in optics and has proven to be a successful technique for restoring images, was applied to our experimental nuclear physics data. The only inputs to the method are the observed energy spectrum and the detector's response matrix also known as the transfer matrix. We demonstrate that the technique can help access information about the shell structure of particle-unbound systems from the measured decay energy spectrum that isn't immediately accessible via traditional approaches such as chi-square fitting. For a similar purpose, we developed a machine learning model that uses a deep neural network (DNN) classifier to identify resonance states from the measured decay energy spectrum. We tested the performance of both methods on simulated data and experimental measurements. Then, we applied both algorithms to the decay energy spectrum of $^{26}\mathrm{O} \rightarrow ^{24}\mathrm{O}$ + n + n measured via invariant mass spectroscopy. The resonance states restored using the RL algorithm to deblur the measured decay energy spectrum agree with those found by the DNN classifier. Both deblurring and DNN approaches suggest that the raw decay energy spectrum of $^{26}\mathrm{O}$ exhibits three peaks at approximately 0.15~MeV, 1.50~MeV, and 5.00~MeV, with half-widths of 0.29~MeV, 0.80~MeV, and 1.85~MeV, respectively.

## Key claim

The Richardson-Lucy deblurring algorithm and a deep neural network classifier can both recover resonance structures from the measured decay energy spectrum of \(^{26}\mathrm{O}\) that are not immediately accessible via traditional chi-square fitting, but the deblurring method requires a regularization term that increases with energy to suppress noise amplification at higher decay energies.

## Method

The authors applied the Richardson-Lucy (RL) iterative deblurring algorithm to the measured decay energy spectrum of \(^{26}\mathrm{O} \rightarrow ^{24}\mathrm{O} + n + n\), using only the observed energy spectrum and the detector response matrix (transfer matrix) as inputs, with a total variation regularization whose strength increases with energy. Separately, they trained a deep neural network (DNN) classifier on simulated Breit-Wigner resonance distributions folded with the experimental response matrix and sampled according to Poisson statistics, then applied it to the same measured spectrum to identify the number of resonance states. Both methods were tested on simulated data and on experimental measurements from the MoNA-LISA detector setup.

## Key numbers

- Three peaks were identified in the raw decay energy spectrum of \(^{26}\mathrm{O}\) at approximately 0.15 MeV, 1.50 MeV, and 5.00 MeV.
- The half-widths of the three peaks were 0.29 MeV, 0.80 MeV, and 1.85 MeV, respectively.
- The transfer matrix was constructed using a binning of 0.2 MeV in decay energy.
- The regularization parameter \(\lambda\) was defined as \(\lambda = \lambda_0 (1 + (E/E_0)^2)\) with \(\lambda_0 = 0.035\) and \(E_0 = 6\) MeV.
- The deblurring algorithm used a few hundred iterations to reach a stable solution.

## Context

This work extends the application of the Richardson-Lucy deblurring algorithm, previously used in optics and demonstrated for heavy-ion collision momentum distributions by Danielewicz and Kurata-Nishimura (2022), to the analysis of decay energy spectra from invariant mass spectroscopy of neutron-unbound nuclei. The study also introduces a deep neural network classifier as a complementary method to traditional chi-square fitting approaches commonly used in analyses of neutron-unbound resonances, such as those by Caesar (2013) and Chrisman (2021). The results enable future extraction of shell-structure information from particle-unbound systems near the neutron drip line, particularly for nuclei measured by the MoNA Collaboration.
