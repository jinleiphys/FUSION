# Influence of heavy resonances in SMASH

> Recent lattice QCD results, comparing to a hadron resonance gas model, have shown the need for hundreds of particles in hadronic models. These extra particles influence both the equation of state and hadronic interactions within hadron transport models. Here, we introduce the PDG21+ particle list, which contains the most up-to-date database of particles and their properties. We then convert all particles decays into 2 body decays so that they are compatible with SMASH in order to produce a more consistent description of a heavy-ion collision.

## Key claim

The paper introduces the PDG21+ particle list, an updated database of hadronic resonances and their properties, and adapts it for use in the SMASH hadron transport code by converting all multi-body decays into sequences of two-body decays; the authors note that further adjustments to the SMASH internal framework are still needed to reproduce experimental elementary cross-sections.

## Method

The authors compiled the PDG21+ list by updating masses, widths, decay channels, and branching ratios from the Particle Data Group's 2020 edition, adding 24 new particles and removing 10 relative to the earlier PDG16+ list. They then converted all three- and four-body decays into sequences of two-body decays using intermediate resonances, selecting the least massive intermediate state that could decay into the final state, and enforced angular momentum conservation. A code was written to output formats compatible with both SMASH and ThermalFIST.

## Key numbers

- PDG21+ contains 418 particles (151 mesons, 267 baryons), compared to 408 in PDG16+ (153 mesons, 255 baryons).
- 24 particles were added and 10 were removed relative to PDG16+; 4 particles were renamed.
- The Hagedorn temperature extracted from the PDG21+ spectrum is T_H^PDG21+ ≈ 170 MeV, lower than the SMASH default value of T_H^SMASH ≈ 179 MeV, approaching the QCD phase transition temperature of ~155 MeV.
- 25 decay channels were deleted when no viable intermediate state could be found or mass conservation was violated.
- The default SMASH release contains 222 states.

## Context

This work extends the PDG16+ list (Alba 2017) used in hadron resonance gas models and equations of state, motivated by lattice QCD results showing the need for more hadronic states to describe susceptibilities and other observables. The new PDG21+ list enables consistent matching between the hadronic equation of state and the SMASH afterburner in hybrid heavy-ion collision simulations, addressing a known mismatch that can cause problems in modeling. The authors plan to use the updated list in future studies of low-energy SMASH comparisons to experimental data and in hydrodynamics coupled to SMASH via a hybrid approach.
