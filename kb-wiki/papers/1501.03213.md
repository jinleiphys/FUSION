# The Relativistic Green's function model and charged-current inclusive neutrino-nucleus scattering at T2K kinematics

> We compare the results of the relativistic Green's function model with the experimental data of the charged-current inclusive differential neutrino-nucleus cross sections published by the T2K Collaboration. The model, which is able to describe both MINER$ν$A and MiniBooNE charged-current quasielastic scattering data, underpredicts the inclusive T2K cross sections.

## Key claim

The Relativistic Green's function (RGF) model, which successfully describes both MINERνA and MiniBooNE charged-current quasielastic scattering data, underpredicts the fully inclusive T2K cross sections that include pion production, indicating that the imaginary part of the optical potential does not recover enough strength from inelastic channels to match these data.

## Method

The authors compared RGF model calculations with T2K flux-averaged charged-current inclusive double-differential νμ-12C cross sections (as functions of muon momentum and scattering angle) and νe-12C differential cross sections (as functions of electron momentum and angle). The RGF model uses a complex energy-dependent relativistic optical potential to describe final-state interactions, with two parametrizations tested: the Energy-Dependent and A-Independent (EDAI) potential and the Democratic (DEM) global parametrization. Bound nucleon states were taken as self-consistent Dirac-Hartree solutions from a relativistic mean-field approach, and the standard axial mass MA = 1.03 GeV/c² was used throughout.

## Key numbers

- The RGF-EDAI total CCQE νμ-12C cross section per target neutron is larger than the RGF-DEM result and agrees with both MiniBooNE and T2K data within error bars over the entire energy range of the data
- The RPWIA (relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation) results for the CC-inclusive double differential cross section are approximately 50% lower than the T2K data
- For the νe analysis, only electrons with momentum p > 0.550 GeV/c and cosθ > 0.72 were considered, matching the T2K reduced phase-space selection
- The T2K νμ beam peaks at approximately 600 MeV, while the νe beam peaks at approximately 500 MeV and extends to energies larger than 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RGF model comparisons with MiniBooNE CCQE data (Meucci 2011) and MINERνA data, responding to a suggestion by Nieves (2014) that the RGF results should be confronted with fully CC-inclusive data. The paper shows that while the RGF model can describe CCQE data without increasing the axial mass, it cannot reproduce the T2K inclusive data, unlike the model of Martini (2014) which includes np-nh excitations and single-pion production and achieves satisfactory agreement. This indicates that explicit inclusion of multinucleon channels is required for CC-inclusive data, and that the phenomenological optical potential approach cannot disentangle the specific contributions of pion production channels.
