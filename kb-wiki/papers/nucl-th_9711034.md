# Improved $d$+$^4$He potentials by inversion, the tensor force and validity of the double folding model

> Improved potential solutions are presented for the inverse scattering problem for $d$+$^4$He data. The input for the inversions includes both the data of recent phase shift analyses and phase shifts from RGM coupled-channel calculations based on the NN Minnesota force. The combined calculations provide a more reliable estimate of the odd-even splitting of the potentials than previously found, suggesting a rather moderate role for this splitting in deuteron-nucleus scattering generally. The approximate parity-independence of the deuteron optical potentials is shown to arise from the nontrivial interference between antisymmetrization and channel coupling to the deuteron breakup channels. A further comparison of the empirical potentials established here and the double folding potential derived from the M3Y effective NN force (with the appropriate normalisation factor) reveals strong similarities. This result supports the application of the double folding model, combined with a small Majorana component, to the description even of such a loosely bound projectile as the deuteron. In turn, support is given for the application of iterative-perturbative inversion in combination with the double folding model to study fine details of the nucleus-nucleus potential. A $d$-$^4$He tensor potential is also derived to reproduce correctly the negative $^6$Li quadrupole moment and the D-state asymptotic constant.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the parity-dependence (odd-even splitting) of the deuteron-⁴He potential is actually rather moderate, contrary to earlier claims, and that the approximate parity-independence arises from a nontrivial interference between antisymmetrization effects and channel coupling to deuteron breakup channels, a conclusion that the abstract presents as a finding but which depends on the specific choice of simultaneous inversion over separate inversion of odd and even partial waves.

## Method

The authors performed iterative-perturbative (IP) inversion of both empirical phase-shift analysis data (from the Zürich group and Kuznetsova et al.) and theoretical RGM phase shifts (using the Minnesota NN force with u=0.97) for d+⁴He scattering in the energy range 0–15 MeV. They compared two inversion strategies: "separate" inversion of odd and even partial amplitudes independently, and "simultaneous" inversion that determines the Wigner and Majorana components together by expanding each in separate basis sets. The RGM calculations included coupling to deuteron breakup channels via the pseudo-state method with Δl=0 transfer, and the resulting S-matrices were inverted to extract local potentials. A tensor potential was also derived separately to reproduce the ⁶Li quadrupole moment and the ε₁ mixing parameter.

## Key numbers

- The volume integral of the Majorana central term relative to the Wigner term: J_M ≈ 8.5% of J_W, from the relation J^{(c)}_{even} - J^{(c)}_{odd} = 2J^{(c)}_M ≈ 0.17 J^{(c)}_W
- The normalization factor for the double folding potential (Minnesota NN force) that best reproduces the even-parity phase shifts: N_DF = 1.572
- The volume integral of the even parity potential from simultaneous inversion differs by only ~1% from that of the DDM3Y double folding potential with adjusted N_DF
- The energy range for the inversion: 0 < E_d ≤ 15 MeV
- The RGM exchange mixture parameter: u = 0.97

## Context

This work extends earlier inverse-scattering studies of d+⁴He by Kukulin et al. (1990s) and the Zürich group's phase-shift analyses, and directly compares with the double folding model using the M3Y effective NN force as applied by Satchler and collaborators. The simultaneous inversion method was first introduced for p+¹⁶O scattering by Mackintosh and Cooper, and the RGM calculations follow the approach of Kanada et al. using the pseudo-state method for breakup channels. The results support the use of the double folding model with a small Majorana component for loosely bound projectiles like the deuteron, and enable future application of iterative-perturbative inversion combined with double folding to study fine details of nucleus-nucleus potentials.
