# Dynamics of quark-gluon plasma from Field correlators

> It is argued that strong dynamics in the quark-gluon plasma and bound states of quarks and gluons is mostly due to nonperturbative effects described by field correlators. The emphasis in the paper is made on two explicit calculations of these effects from the first principles: one analytic using gluelump Green's functions and another using independent lattice data on correlators. The resulting hadron spectra are investigated in the range T_c < T < 2T_c. The spectra of charmonia, bottomonia, light s-sbar mesons, glueballs and quark-gluon states calculated numerically are in general agreement with lattice MEM data. The possible role of these bound states in the thermodynamics of quark-gluon plasma is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that nonperturbative dynamics in the quark-gluon plasma above the critical temperature \(T_c\) is dominated by the electric field correlator \(D_1^E(x)\), which remains nonzero for \(T \geq T_c\) and produces bound states of quarks and gluons; the claim is hedged by noting that the analytic continuation of the gluelump-based expression from the confined phase to the deconfined phase is an approximation, and that the lattice-based fit assumes temperature-independent mass and perturbative coefficients over the range \(T_c < T < 2T_c\).

## Method

The authors computed the static \(Q\bar{Q}\) potential \(V_1(r,T)\) from two independent sources: an analytic expression derived from gluelump Green's functions using the Fock-Feynman-Schwinger formalism and the Background Field Formalism, and a fit to quenched lattice data for the electric correlators \(D_\parallel^E\) and \(D_\perp^E\) from Di Giacomo et al. (2003) using a parametrization \(D_1^E(x) = B/x \exp(-M x) + b/x^4\) with temperature-dependent coefficient \(B(T)\). They then solved the relativistic Hamiltonian equation (with einbein variables for constituent masses) for charmonia, bottomonia, light \(s\bar{s}\) mesons, glueballs, and quark-gluon states, using the static potentials to find bound-state masses and comparing them to lattice maximum entropy method (MEM) data.

## Key numbers

- The mass \(M\) of the nonperturbative term in \(D_1^E\) from the lattice fit is about 1 GeV (Table I, central value).
- The nonperturbative coefficient \(B\) drops rapidly from \(T/T_c = 1.007\) to \(T/T_c = 1.261\), becoming consistent with zero within errors at the highest temperature.
- The perturbative coefficient \(a+b\) from the lattice fit is \(0.90(3)/N_c = 0.30\) (with \(N_c=3\)), consistent with the value found in Di Giacomo et al. (2003).
- The gluelump mass \(M_0\) from the analytic calculation is \(1.49 \div 0.98\) GeV for \(\sigma_f = 0.18\) GeV\(^2\) and \(\bar\alpha_s = 0 \div 0.195\), compared to the lattice asymptotic value \(M_0 \approx 1\) GeV.
- The static potential \(V_1^{(np)}(\infty)\) from the lattice fit has a relative error of at least 50% due to uncertainties in \(B\) (30%) and \(M\) (10%).
- The asymptotic self-energy of a single quark is \(E_Q = \frac12 V_1^{(Q\bar{Q})}(\infty, T)\) and of a gluon is \(E_g = \frac98 V_1^{(Q\bar{Q})}(\infty, T)\).

## Context

This work extends the Vacuum Correlator Method (also called the Stochastic Vacuum Model) of Dosch, Simonov, and collaborators to the deconfined phase, building on earlier predictions by Di Giacomo et al. (1992-1997) that \(D^E\) vanishes above \(T_c\) while \(D_1^E\) persists. It directly uses lattice correlator data from Di Giacomo, Meggiolaro, and collaborators (2003) and the analytic gluelump formalism of Simonov (2001) to compute bound-state spectra, which are compared to lattice MEM results for charmonia, bottomonia, and light mesons. The results enable a quantitative study of the role of nonperturbative bound states in the thermodynamics of the quark-gluon plasma, particularly the equation of state and the difference \(\varepsilon - 3p\) above \(T_c\).
