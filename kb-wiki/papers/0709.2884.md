# Nonperturbative Heavy-Quark Diffusion in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We evaluate heavy-quark (HQ) transport properties in a Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) employing interaction potentials extracted from thermal lattice QCD. Within a Brueckner many-body scheme we calculate in-medium T-matrices for charm- and bottom-quark scattering off light quarks in the QGP. The interactions are dominated by attractive meson and diquark channels which support bound and resonance states up to temperatures of ~1.5 T_c. We apply pertinent drag and diffusion coefficients (supplemented by perturbative scattering off gluons) in Langevin simulations in an expanding fireball to compute HQ spectra and elliptic flow in \sqrt{s_{NN}}=200 GeV Au-Au collisions. We find good agreement with semileptonic electron-decay spectra which supports our nonperturbative computation of the HQ diffusion coefficient, suggestive for a strongly coupled QGP.

# Nonperturbative Heavy-Quark Diffusion in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim

The authors claim that a nonperturbative T-matrix calculation of heavy-quark diffusion, using interaction potentials extracted from thermal lattice QCD, yields transport coefficients that agree with RHIC heavy-flavor electron data, suggesting a strongly coupled QGP; however, the claim is hedged by significant uncertainties in the definition and extraction of the potential from lattice QCD, with variations between different potential parametrizations producing approximately 40% variation in the resulting transport coefficients.

## Method

The authors employ a Brueckner many-body scheme to calculate in-medium T-matrices for charm- and bottom-quark scattering off light quarks in the QGP, using static interaction potentials extracted from thermal lattice QCD via the internal energy (rather than free energy). They include color-singlet (meson), color-octet, antitriplet, and sextet (diquark) channels with S-wave and P-wave partial waves, then compute drag and diffusion coefficients from the T-matrices and implement them in relativistic Langevin simulations of an expanding fireball to compute heavy-quark spectra and elliptic flow in sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV Au-Au collisions.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass set to m_c = 1.5 GeV and bottom quark mass set to m_b = 4.5 GeV
- Light quark effective mass m_q = 0.25 GeV and width Gamma_q = 0.2 GeV
- Resonance structures in the color-singlet channel persist up to temperatures of approximately 1.7 T_c and in the antitriplet channel up to approximately 1.4 T_c
- Charm quark in-medium width Gamma_c approximately 200 MeV from the nonperturbative selfenergy
- Nonperturbative thermal relaxation rate reaches gamma approximately 1/(7 fm/c) at low momenta close to T_c, a factor of approximately 4 larger than leading-order perturbative QCD elastic scattering
- Spatial heavy-quark diffusion constant D_HQ = T/(m_c gamma) approximately 5/(2 pi T) at T = 200 MeV, a factor of approximately 4 smaller than in perturbative QCD
- Initial temperature of the fireball T_0 = 340 MeV in semicentral collisions
- Theoretical uncertainty from different lattice QCD potential extractions is up to approximately 30% in the final observables

## Context

This work extends earlier nonperturbative approaches to heavy-quark diffusion, including the resonance model of van Hees (2004) and the T-matrix approach of Mannarelli (2005), by using interaction potentials extracted from finite-temperature lattice QCD computations by Kaczmarek (2003) and others. The results contradict perturbative QCD calculations by Armesto (2005) and Wicks (2005), which cannot explain the observed suppression and elliptic flow of heavy-flavor electrons at RHIC, and instead support the picture of a strongly coupled QGP. The approach enables future work to scrutinize uncertainties in the potential extraction from lattice QCD and to supplement elastic scattering with radiative energy loss at high transverse momentum.
