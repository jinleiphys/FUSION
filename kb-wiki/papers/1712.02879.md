# Microscopic optical potentials derived from ab initio translationally invariant nonlocal one-body densities

> We derive a microscopic optical potential for intermediate energies using ab initio translationally invariant nonlocal one-body nuclear densities computed within the no-core shell model (NCSM) approach utilizing two- and three-nucleon chiral interactions as the only input. The optical potential is derived at first-order within the spectator expansion of the non-relativistic multiple scattering theory by adopting the impulse approximation and using the same chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction as that used to compute densities. The ground state local and nonlocal densities of 4,6,8He, 12C, and 16O are calculated and applied to optical potential construction. The differential cross sections and the analyzing powers for the elastic proton scattering off of these nuclei are then calculated for different values of the incident proton energy. The impact of nonlocality and the COM removal is discussed. The use of nonlocal densities has a substantial impact on the differential cross sections and improves agreement with experiment in comparison to results generated with the local densities especially for light nuclei.

## Key claim
Using ab initio translationally invariant nonlocal one-body densities from the no-core shell model (NCSM) with chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions substantially improves agreement with experimental elastic proton scattering data compared to using local densities, especially for light nuclei; however, for halo nuclei the model still requires further sophistication to describe analyzing powers.

## Method
The authors derived a first-order optical potential within the spectator expansion of non-relativistic multiple scattering theory under the impulse approximation, folding the free nucleon-nucleon t matrix with nonlocal nuclear densities. The nonlocal one-body densities were computed from NCSM eigenstates using chiral NN-N4LO(500) and 3Nlnl interactions, with spurious center-of-mass (COM) components removed directly in coordinate space via a generalization of the method of Navratil (2004). The resulting optical potential was used to solve the one-body Lippmann-Schwinger equation for elastic proton scattering on 4,6,8He, 12C, and 16O at various incident energies.

## Key numbers
- Differential cross sections and analyzing powers were calculated for 4He, 12C, and 16O at multiple incident proton energies in the laboratory frame (specific energy values not provided in the truncated text).
- For 4He, NCSM calculations used a basis space up to Nmax = 14 with oscillator frequency hbar-Omega = 20 MeV and SRG flow parameter lambda_SRG = 2.0 fm^-1.
- For 6He, NCSM calculations used Nmax = 12 with hbar-Omega = 20 MeV and lambda_SRG = 2.0 fm^-1.
- For 8He, NCSM calculations used Nmax = 10 with hbar-Omega = 20 MeV and lambda_SRG = 2.0 fm^-1.
- For 12C and 16O, NCSM calculations used importance-truncated Nmax = 8 basis with hbar-Omega = 20 MeV and lambda_SRG = 1.8 fm^-1 and 2.0 fm^-1, respectively.
- The normalization of the local density integrates to A (the mass number) for both COM-contaminated and translationally invariant densities.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies using chiral NN potentials at N3LO (Gennari et al., 2016) and N4LO (Gennari et al., 2017) in a factorized optical potential model, where nonlocality came only from the t matrix. By now computing nonlocal densities from the same chiral interaction used for the t matrix, the authors achieve a more consistent microscopic optical potential. The method generalizes the translationally invariant density construction of Navratil (2004) to nonlocal densities, differing from the momentum-space COM removal approach of Burrows (2017). This enables improved predictions for elastic scattering on light and halo nuclei, with future work needed to incorporate medium effects beyond the impulse approximation for analyzing powers.
