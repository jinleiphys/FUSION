# The beta Decay of 32Cl: Precision gamma-Ray Spectroscopy and a Measurement of Isospin-Symmetry Breaking

> Background: Models to calculate small isospin-symmetry-breaking effects in superallowed Fermi decays have been placed under scrutiny in recent years. A stringent test of these models is to measure transitions for which the correction is predicted to be large. The decay of 32Cl decay provides such a test case. Purpose: To improve the gamma yields following the beta decay of 32Cl and to determine the ft values of the the beta branches, particularly the one to the isobaric-analogue state in 32S. Method: Reaction-produced and recoil-spectrometer-separated 32Cl is collected in tape and transported to a counting location where beta-gamma coincidences are measured with a precisely-calibrated HPGe detector. Results: The precision on the gamma yields for most of the known beta branches has been improved by about an order of magnitude, and many new transitions have been observed. We have determined 32Cl-decay transition strengths extending up to E_x~11 MeV. The ft value for the decay to the isobaric-analogue state in 32S has been measured. A comparison to a shell-model calculation shows good agreement. CONCLUSIONS: We have experimentally determined the isospin-symmetry-breaking correction to the superallowed transition of this decay to be (δ_C-δ_NS)_exp=5.4(9)%, significantly larger than for any other known superallowed Fermi transition. This correction agrees with a shell-model calculation, which yields δ_C-δ_NS=4.8(5)%. Our results also provide a way to improve the measured ft values for the beta decay of 32Ar.

## Key claim

The experimentally determined isospin-symmetry-breaking correction for the superallowed Fermi transition in 32Cl decay is (δ_C - δ_NS)_exp = 5.4(9)%, which is significantly larger than for any other known superallowed Fermi transition; this value agrees with a shell-model calculation yielding 4.8(5)%, but the abstract does not highlight that the experimental uncertainty is nearly twice the theoretical uncertainty.

## Method

Reaction-produced 32Cl was separated using the Momentum Achromatic Recoil Separator (MARS) at Texas A&M University, collected on a fast tape-transport system, and transported to a shielded counting station where beta-gamma coincidences were measured with a precisely-calibrated HPGe detector. The absolute photopeak efficiency of the HPGe was calibrated up to 3.5 MeV with ±0.2% precision and extended to 7.2 MeV using Cyltran and Penelope Monte Carlo simulations with conservative uncertainties of ±1% from 3.5-5 MeV and ±5% above 5 MeV. Gamma peak areas were extracted using a fitting function that included a Gaussian, a low-energy tail from incomplete charge collection, a constant background, and a background histogram from accidental coincidences.

## Key numbers

- Isospin-symmetry-breaking correction: (δ_C - δ_NS)_exp = 5.4(9)% experimentally, compared to shell-model calculation of 4.8(5)%
- Q_EC value for 32Cl decay: 12680.9(6) keV, derived from mass excess ME(32Cl) = -13334.60(57) keV and ME(32S) = -26015.535(2) keV
- Half-life of 32Cl: 0.3012(13) s from 2230-keV gamma events, consistent with accepted value of 0.298(1) s
- Beam purity: 91% 32Cl after implantation, with 7.5% 30S as the largest contaminant
- Beta efficiency of plastic scintillator: η(Q_β) = [32.32(41) - 0.022(45)Q_β]%, consistent with being flat over the 4-12 MeV range of end-points
- Gamma efficiency uncertainties: ±1% from 3.5-5 MeV, ±5% above 5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections in superallowed Fermi decays, which have been measured to ≤0.3% precision for thirteen cases in A=4n+2 nuclei (Hardy, 2009), where δ_C values are typically ~0.5% for A<56 and ~1.5% for A>56. The 32Cl decay (A=4n) provides a more demanding test because the daughter analog state at 7001-keV excitation in 32S sits near a known 1+, T=0 state at 7190 keV, enabling larger isospin mixing. The results also enable improved determination of δ_C for the T=2 decay of 32Ar (Armini, 2011; Wrede, 2011), where 32Cl provides an in situ efficiency calibration since 32Ar beta decay is followed by 32Cl decay 64.4(2)% of the time.
