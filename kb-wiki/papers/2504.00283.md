# $^3$H and $^3$He nuclei production in a combined thermal and coalescence framework for heavy-ion collisions in the few-GeV energy regime

> A thermal model describing hadron production in heavy-ion collisions in the few-GeV energy regime is combined with the idea of nucleon coalescence to make predictions for the $^3$H and $^3$He nuclei production. A realistic parametrization of the freeze-out conditions is used, which reproduces well the spectra of protons and pions. It also correctly predicts the deuteron yield that agrees with the experimental value. The predicted yields of $^3$H and $^3$He appear to be smaller by about a factor of two compared to the experimental results. The model predictions for the spectra can be compared with future experimental data.

## Key claim

The combined thermal and coalescence framework predicts yields of ^3H and ^3He that are smaller than experimental results by factors of approximately 2.7 and 2.0 respectively, despite correctly reproducing the order of magnitude of the multiplicities and the deuteron yield.

## Method

The authors combine a thermal model with spheroidal hydrodynamic expansion (Harabasz 2020, Harabasz 2022) that reproduces proton and pion spectra, with a coalescence framework using nucleon distributions from the thermal freeze-out surface. The formation rate coefficients A_FR are calculated using a uniform spherical source of radius R=6.06 fm rather than the Gaussian profile commonly used in coalescence models, together with Gaussian approximations for the ^3H and ^3He wave functions with radii 1.76 fm and 1.96 fm respectively.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature T = 70.3 MeV, baryon chemical potential μ_B = 876 MeV, isospin chemical potential μ_I3 = -21.5 MeV, system radius R = 6.06 fm, radial flow v_R = 0.60, Lorentz gamma factor at boundary γ_R = 1.25
- Formation rate coefficient A_FR for ^3H = 5.51 × 10^11 MeV^6, for ^3He = 5.03 × 10^11 MeV^6
- Model predicts total yield N = 3.16 for ^3H compared to experimental value 8.65 ± 0.01_stat ± 1.05_sys (underestimate by factor 2.7)
- Model predicts total yield N = 2.26 for ^3He compared to experimental value 4.55 ± 0.01_stat ± 0.29_sys (underestimate by factor 2.0)
- Rapidity density at y=0: (dN/dy)_y=0 = 4.06 for ^3H, 2.89 for ^3He
- Beam energy √s_NN = 2.4 GeV for Au-Au collisions at 10% centrality (HADES experiment)

## Context

This work extends the thermal-plus-coalescence framework previously applied successfully to deuteron production (Florkowski 2023) to the next lightest nuclei ^3H and ^3He. The approach contrasts with the usual treatment of thermal and coalescence models as exclusive alternatives (Mrowczynski 2020), instead showing they can coexist at lower energies. The results enable comparison with preliminary HADES data (Szala 2019) and suggest that improved wave function calculations for the formation rate coefficients could reduce the discrepancy, while other frameworks such as IQMD + MST also underestimate these yields.
