# Decay properties of $^{22}\mathrm{Ne} + α$ resonances and their impact on $s$-process nucleosynthesis

> The astrophysical $s$-process is one of the two main processes forming elements heavier than iron. A key outstanding uncertainty surrounding $s$-process nucleosynthesis is the neutron flux generated by the ${}^{22}\mathrm{Ne}(α, n){}^{25}\mathrm{Mg}$ reaction during the He-core and C-shell burning phases of massive stars. This reaction, as well as the competing ${}^{22}\mathrm{Ne}(α, γ){}^{26}\mathrm{Mg}$ reaction, is not well constrained in the important temperature regime from ${\sim} 0.2$--$0.4$~GK, owing to uncertainties in the nuclear properties of resonances lying within the Gamow window. To address these uncertainties, we have performed a new measurement of the ${}^{22}\mathrm{Ne}({}^{6}\mathrm{Li}, d){}^{26}\mathrm{Mg}$ reaction in inverse kinematics, detecting the outgoing deuterons and ${}^{25,26}\mathrm{Mg}$ recoils in coincidence. We have established a new $n / γ$ decay branching ratio of $1.14(26)$ for the key $E_x = 11.32$ MeV resonance in $^{26}\mathrm{Mg}$, which results in a new $(α, n)$ strength for this resonance of $42(11)~μ$eV when combined with the well-established $(α, γ)$ strength of this resonance. We have also determined new upper limits on the $α$ partial widths of neutron-unbound resonances at $E_x = 11.112,$ $11.163$, $11.169$, and $11.171$ MeV. Monte-Carlo calculations of the stellar ${}^{22}\mathrm{Ne}(α, n){}^{25}\mathrm{Mg}$ and ${}^{22}\mathrm{Ne}(α, γ){}^{26}\mathrm{Mg}$ rates, which incorporate these results, indicate that both rates are substantially lower than previously thought in the temperature range from ${\sim} 0.2$--$0.4$~GK.

## Key claim
The paper reports a new neutron-to-gamma decay branching ratio of 1.14(26) for the key 11.32 MeV resonance in 26Mg, which is a factor of about 3 lower than values extracted from direct measurements, leading to a substantially lower 22Ne(alpha,n)25Mg resonance strength of 42(11) micro-eV. Monte-Carlo calculations incorporating these results indicate that both the 22Ne(alpha,n)25Mg and 22Ne(alpha,gamma)26Mg stellar rates are substantially lower than previously thought in the temperature range from about 0.2 to 0.4 GK.

## Method
The experiment used the 22Ne(6Li,d)26Mg reaction in inverse kinematics with a 154 MeV 22Ne beam on a 6LiF target at the Texas A&M University Cyclotron Institute. Outgoing deuterons were detected in the backward hemisphere by a double-sided annular silicon detector, while beam-like 25Mg and 26Mg recoils were identified in coincidence at the focal plane of the MDM spectrometer using energy loss, total energy, and position signals. The branching ratio Gamma_n/Gamma_gamma was extracted from the efficiency-corrected ratio of 25Mg to 26Mg recoils in coincidence with the state of interest, and angular distributions were compared to Distorted Wave Born Approximation calculations using the FRESCO code.

## Key numbers
- Gamma_n/Gamma_gamma = 1.14(26) for the Ex = 11.32 MeV resonance in 26Mg
- New 22Ne(alpha,n)25Mg resonance strength for the 11.32 MeV state: omega-gamma_(alpha,n) = 42(11) micro-eV
- Weighted average of direct-measurement (alpha,gamma) strength used for normalization: 37(4) micro-eV from Wolke (1989) and Hunt (2019)
- 25Mg/26Mg recoil detection efficiency ratio: 0.858(33), with individual acceptances of 77.8(11)% for 25Mg and 90.8(5)% for 26Mg
- New upper limits (90% confidence level) on alpha partial widths for neutron-unbound resonances at Ex = 11.112, 11.163, 11.169, and 11.171 MeV

## Context
This work resolves a long-standing discrepancy between direct 22Ne(alpha,n)25Mg measurements (Harms 1991, Drotleff 1993, Giesen 1993, Jaeger 2001) and indirect transfer studies (Talwar 2016), showing that the direct (alpha,n) strengths were systematically overestimated. The results, combined with the sub-Coulomb transfer study of Jayatissa (2019), strongly suggest a J-pi = 0+ assignment for the 11.32 MeV resonance and indicate that the same state is observed in both (alpha,n) and (alpha,gamma) measurements, contrary to suggestions of a doublet (Longland 2012, Adsley 2017). The new, lower reaction rates will significantly impact predicted weak s-process yields in massive stars during He-core and C-shell burning phases.
