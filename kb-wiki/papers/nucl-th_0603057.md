# Parity shift and beat staggering structure of octupole bands in a collective model for quadrupole-octupole deformed nuclei

> We propose a collective model formalism which describes the strong parity shift observed in low-lying spectra of nuclei with octupole deformations together with the fine rotational band structure developed at higher angular momenta. The parity effect is obtained by the Schroedinger equation for oscillations of the reflection asymmetric (octupole) shape between two opposite orientations in an angular momentum dependent double-well potential. The rotational structure is obtained by a collective quadrupole-octupole rotation Hamiltonian. The model scheme reproduces the complicated beat staggering patterns observed in the octupole bands of light actinide nuclei. It explains the angular momentum evolution of octupole spectra as the interplay between the octupole shape oscillation (parity shift) mode and the stable quadrupole-octupole rotation mode.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the complete structure of alternating-parity (octupole) bands in light actinide nuclei can be described by combining two distinct collective modes: a soft octupole oscillation in an angular-momentum-dependent double-well potential (which produces the strong parity shift at low spins) and a stable quadrupole-octupole rotation described by the Quadrupole-Octupole Rotation Model (QORM, which produces the beat staggering patterns at higher spins). The model reproduces the observed spectra only when both modes are combined, with the transition between them occurring near I = 8-10.

## Method

The authors solve the Schroedinger equation for a one-dimensional octupole deformation variable beta in a double-well potential whose barrier height increases with angular momentum I due to a centrifugal term with a deformation-dependent moment of inertia. The resulting parity-dependent oscillation energy is added to the energy from the QORM Hamiltonian, which contains diagonal and off-diagonal terms built from cubic angular-momentum operators classified by the octahedron point-symmetry group. The QORM energy is obtained by minimizing the diagonal part with respect to the intrinsic projection K and then diagonalizing the full Hamiltonian in the basis of |IK> states. The combined model is fitted to experimental level energies of 224,226Ra and 224,226Th.

## Key numbers

- RMS deviation between theoretical and experimental levels: 19.0 keV for 224Ra, 20.4 keV for 226Ra, 8.1 keV for 224Th, and 9.8 keV for 226Th.
- The octupole mass parameter B3 is fixed at 200 hbar^2/MeV for all nuclei studied.
- For 226Th, the off-diagonal octupole parameter f12 = 0.023 keV (non-zero, indicating non-axial octupole deformation); for the other three nuclei f12 = 0.
- The model reproduces levels up to I = 28 for 224Ra and 226Ra, and up to I = 20 for 224Th and 226Th (table 1).
- The parity shift is observed experimentally to decrease rapidly near I = 8-10, and the model reproduces this transition region.

## Context

This work extends earlier collective model treatments of octupole bands that used double-well potentials without angular-momentum dependence (Leander, 1969) or that described only low-spin parity splitting (Jolos, 1993; Jolos, 1994). It also builds on the QORM formalism (Minkov, 2001) which explained beat staggering patterns at high spins but could not describe the low-spin parity shift. The combined model successfully reproduces the complete alternating-parity band structure in light actinides (Rn, Ra, Th) where earlier works (Cocks, 1997; Zamfir, 2001; Raduta, 2003) covered only limited spin ranges. The results enable predictions of further beat staggering regions at higher angular momenta (e.g., near I=30 for 224Ra and 226Ra) and suggest a possible shape phase transition between octupole deformation and octupole vibration, as recently discussed by AQOA.
