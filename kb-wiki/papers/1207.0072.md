# Quark deconfinement phase transition in nuclear matter for improved quark mass density-dependent model

> The improved quark mass density-dependent (IQMDD) model, which has been successfully used to describe the properties of both infinite nuclear matter and finite nuclei, is applied to investigate the properties of quark deconfinement phase transition. By using the finite-temperature quantum field theory, we calculate the finite temperature effective potential and extend the IQMDD model to finite temperature and finite nuclear matter density. The critical temperature and the critical density of nuclear matter are given and the QCD phase diagram is addressed. It is shown that this model can not only describe the saturation properties of nuclear matter, but also explain the quark deconfinement phase transition successfully.

## Key claim

The improved quark mass density-dependent (IQMDD) model can simultaneously describe both the saturation properties of nuclear matter and the quark deconfinement phase transition, but the model predicts a first-order phase transition across the entire phase diagram and cannot predict a critical point along the critical line due to its lack of chirality.

## Method

The authors extended the IQMDD model to finite temperature and finite nuclear matter density using finite-temperature quantum field theory, calculating the one-loop effective potential under the mean field approximation. The effective potential was used to compute the temperature- and density-dependent bag constant, defined as the difference between the effective potential in the perturbative false vacuum and the physical real vacuum. The critical temperature and critical density were determined as the points where the bag constant vanishes.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter: $\rho_0 = 0.15$ fm$^{-3}$ with binding energy per particle $E/A = -15$ MeV at zero temperature
- Compression constant at saturation: $K(\rho_0) = 210$ MeV
- Deconfining baryon density at zero temperature: $1.63$ fm$^{-3}$, approximately ten times the saturation density
- Deconfining temperature at zero baryon density: $139$ MeV
- Bag constant at zero temperature: $B = 174$ MeV fm$^{-3}$
- Coupling constants: $g_\omega^q = 2.44$, $g_\sigma^q = 4.67$; parameter $b = -1460$ MeV
- Meson masses: $m_\omega = 783$ MeV, $m_\rho = 770$ MeV, $m_\sigma = 509$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous IQMDD model (Wu et al., 2005, 2008) from zero-temperature nuclear matter to finite temperature and density, addressing the quark deconfinement phase transition. The model differs from the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model of Guichon (1988) by replacing the MIT bag with a Friedberg-Lee soliton bag, removing the MIT boundary condition and allowing quark deconfinement. The results are compared with lattice QCD predictions (Rischke, 2004; Gupta et al., 2011) which give a deconfining temperature of about 170-175 MeV at zero baryon density and suggest a more complex phase diagram possibly including a critical point, which the IQMDD model cannot reproduce.
