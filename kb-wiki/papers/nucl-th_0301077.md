# On energy densities reached in heavy-ion collisions at the CERN SPS

> We present a few estimates of energy densities reached in heavy-ion collisions at the CERN SPS. The estimates are based on data and models of proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus interactions. In all of these estimates the maximum energy density in central Pb+Pb interactions is larger than the critical energy density of about 0.7 GeV/fm^3 following from lattice gauge theory computations. In estimates which we consider as realistic the maximum energy density is about twice the critical value. In this way our analysis gives some support to claims that deconfined matter has been produced at the CERN SPS. Any definite statement requires a deeper understanding of formation times of partons and hadrons in nuclear collisions. We also compare our results with implicit energy estimates contained in earlier models of anomalous J/psi suppression in nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The maximum energy density in central Pb+Pb collisions at the CERN SPS is about twice the critical energy density of 0.7 GeV/fm³ from lattice QCD, but any definite statement about deconfined matter production requires a deeper understanding of formation times of partons and hadrons in nuclear collisions.

## Method

The authors use a Glauber-type model of tube-on-tube interactions, where nuclei are treated as hard spheres with homogeneous density and nucleon-nucleon non-diffractive cross-section of 30 mb. They compute the rapidity distribution of energy from secondary particle production (using the Wong-Lu parametrization) and from stopped nucleons, then divide by the estimated volume occupied by the central rapidity unit after the tube-on-tube collision is finished. The volume includes contributions from the Lorentz-contracted tube lengths and a formation time parameter t₀.

## Key numbers

- Critical energy density from lattice QCD: ε_c ≈ 0.7 GeV/fm³ (with statistical error leading to range 0.35–1.05 GeV/fm³)
- Maximum energy density in realistic estimates: about 2 × ε_c ≈ 1.4 GeV/fm³
- Nucleon rapidity loss per collision: Δy = 0.5 (supported by NA49 data) and Δy = 0.7 (for comparison)
- Lorentz contraction factor for Pb+Pb at SPS: γ ≈ 9
- Nucleon-nucleon non-diffractive cross-section: σ = 30 mb = 3 fm²
- Critical temperature from lattice: T_c ≈ 173 MeV
- Coherence time from Drell-Yan data analysis by Gale, Jeon and Kapusta: t₀ = 0.4 ± 0.1 fm/c
- Maximum tube length for Pb: 2L_A^max = 14.22 fm
- Condition for QGP formation in the Blaizot-Ollitrault model: 2L_A + 2L_B ≥ 23.5 fm
- Condition for QGP formation in the Kharzeev-Lourenço-Nardi-Satz model: 2L_A × 2L_B ≥ 5.87 fm × (2L_A + 2L_B)

## Context

This work extends the Glauber-type tube-on-tube interaction models previously developed by Wong and Lu (1994), Date (1980s), and others, and compares its energy density estimates with lattice QCD results by Karsch (2002). The results are compared with the phenomenological models of anomalous J/ψ suppression by Blaizot and Ollitrault (1996) and by Kharzeev, Lourenço, Nardi and Satz (1996), showing that the energy density required for plasma formation in those models is about 1.8 times ε_c, suggesting delayed thermalization and rapid expansion. The analysis enables future work to refine formation time measurements and incorporate transverse expansion effects.
