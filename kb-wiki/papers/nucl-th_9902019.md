# Superfluid nuclear matter calculations

> We present a method to calculate nuclear matter properties in the superfluid phase. The method is based on the use of self-consistent off-shell nucleon propagators in the T-matrix equation. Such a complete treatment of the spectral function, is required below and around $T_c$ due to a pseudogap formation in the spectral function. In the superfluid phase we introduce the anomalous self-energy in the fermion propagators and in the T-matrix equation, consistently with the strong coupling BCS equations. The equations for the nucleon spectral function include both a contribution of condensed and scattering pairs. The method is illustrated by numerical calculations. Above $T_c$ pseudogap formation is visible in the spectral function and below $T_c$ a superfluid gap also appears.

# Superfluid nuclear matter calculations

## Key claim

The paper presents a method for calculating nuclear matter properties in the superfluid phase that combines a self-consistent T-matrix treatment of off-shell nucleon propagators with a mean-field, frequency-independent superfluid energy gap; the method is required because the formation of a pseudogap in the spectral function above the critical temperature reduces the value of the energy gap and critical temperature compared to the BCS result, an effect that can only be captured using self-consistent off-shell propagators rather than a quasiparticle approximation.

## Method

The method solves the T-matrix equation for symmetric nuclear matter using self-consistent off-shell nucleon propagators, where the imaginary part of the self-energy is obtained from the T-matrix and the real part includes both a Hartree-Fock term and a dispersive contribution from a Kramers-Kronig relation. In the superfluid phase, the anomalous self-energy is introduced into the fermion propagators and the T-matrix equation, with the gap equation derived from the condition that the singularity of the T-matrix remains at zero energy and momentum for all temperatures below the critical temperature, consistent with the Kadanoff-Martin formulation. Numerical calculations are performed using a Yamaguchi separable potential of rank one in the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\) channels, with nucleon momenta restricted to below 700 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature in the self-consistent calculation: \(T_c = 1.63\) MeV at density \(\rho = 0.45 \rho_0\)
- Critical temperature in the quasiparticle approximation: \(T_c = 5.03\) MeV at density \(\rho = 0.45 \rho_0\)
- Yamaguchi potential parameters: \(\lambda_{^1S_0} = -0.841\) GeV\(^2\), \(\lambda_{^3S_1} = -0.8591\) GeV\(^2\), \(\beta = 285.9\) MeV
- Nuclear density for all numerical results: \(\rho = 0.45 \rho_0\)
- Momentum cutoff for nucleons: \(|p| < 700\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent T-matrix approach of earlier studies (J. Phys. G, 1998) to include the superfluid phase, addressing the instability of the normal Fermi liquid state when pairing is present. The method contrasts with the Brueckner approximation, which does not consider pairing in the ground state, and with quasiparticle-based T-matrix calculations (Vonderfecht et al., 1991; Roepke et al., 1998) that fail to capture the feedback of the pseudogap on the T-matrix. The approach enables future calculations of nuclear matter spectral properties below the critical temperature using more realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, and is relevant to understanding the crossover between BCS pairing and boson condensation in nuclear systems.
