# Inhomogeneous phases in the Nambu-Jona-Lasino and quark-meson model

> We discuss inhomogeneous ground states of the Nambu-Jona-Lasino (NJL) and quark-meson (QM) model within mean-field approximation and their possible existence in the respective phase diagrams. For this purpose we focus on lower dimensional modulations and point out that known solutions in the 2+1 and 1+1 dimensional (chiral) Gross-Neveu (GN) model can be lifted to the to the 3+1 dimensional NJL model. This is worked out in detail for one-dimensional modulations and numerical results for the phase diagrams are presented. Focus is put on the critical point and on vanishing temperatures. As an interesting result the first order transition line in the phase diagram of homogeneous phases gets replaced by an inhomogeneous phase which is bordered by two second order transition lines.

# Inhomogeneous phases in the Nambu-Jona-Lasino and quark-meson model

## Key claim
The first order phase transition line in the homogeneous phase diagram of the Nambu-Jona-Lasino (NJL) model is replaced by an inhomogeneous phase bordered by two second order transition lines, at least in the vicinity of the chiral critical point; this result is established within mean-field approximation and for one-dimensional modulations, relying on the mapping of the 3+1 dimensional NJL model to the 1+1 dimensional chiral Gross-Neveu model.

## Method
The author uses the mean-field approximation for the NJL and quark-meson (QM) models, expressing the thermodynamic potential through the eigenvalues of a Dirac-type Hamiltonian. By exploiting Lorentz symmetry, the problem of finding self-consistent inhomogeneous solutions with a one-dimensional modulation in the 3+1 dimensional NJL model is reduced to solving the 1+1 dimensional chiral Gross-Neveu (GN) model. The known self-consistent solutions of the GN model, including finite current quark masses, are then lifted to the NJL model, and the thermodynamic potential is evaluated numerically using a Pauli-Villars regularization of the vacuum contribution.

## Key numbers
- The effective density of states for the homogeneous phase limit (ν=1) is given by ρ̃(E) = (1/π²) θ(E-Δ) √(E²-Δ²) E, where Δ is the quasiparticle gap.
- The effective density of states for the ultra-relativistic gas limit (ν=0) is ρ̃(E) = (1/π²) E².
- The asymptotic expansion of the effective density of states is ρ̃(E) = E²/π² - ⟨M(z)²⟩/(2π²) - (⟨M(z)⁴⟩ + ⟨M'(z)²⟩)/(8π²E²) + O(1/E⁴).
- The constituent quark mass in the vacuum from the NJL model with proper-time regularization is approximately 200 MeV, while with a sharp three-momentum cutoff it is significantly larger than 300 MeV.
- The phenomenologically acceptable chemical potential for nuclear matter is μ < (M_N - B)/3 ≈ 308 MeV, where M_N is the nucleon mass and B the binding energy.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of inhomogeneous phases in the NJL model beyond the chiral density wave approximation of Sadzikowski (2000) and Nakano (2004), and confirms the picture suggested by Nickel (2009) that the first order transition is replaced by an inhomogeneous phase near the critical point. The method builds on the classification of self-consistent solutions in the chiral Gross-Neveu model by Schnetz (2004, 2005), Thies (2006), and Correa (2009), and enables the study of inhomogeneous phases at finite quark masses for the first time in the NJL model. The results enable future investigations of the QM model and comparisons with holographic models and the quarkyonic matter picture of McLerran (2007).
