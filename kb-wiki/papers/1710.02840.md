# Nuclear Axial Currents from Scale-Chiral Effective Field Theory

> By incorporating hidden scale symmetry and hidden local symmetry in nuclear effective field theory, combined with double soft-pion theorem, we predict that the Gamow-Teller operator coming from the space component of the axial current should remain unaffected by the QCD vacuum change caused by baryonic density whereas the first forbidden beta transition operator coming from the time component should be strongly enhanced. While the latter has been confirmed since some time, the former is given a support by a powerful recent {\it ab initio} quantum Monte Carlo calculation in light nuclei, also confirming the old "chiral filter hypothesis." Formulated in terms of Fermi-liquid fixed point structure of strong-coupled nuclear interactions, we offer an extremely simple resolution to the long-standing puzzle of "quenched $g_A$", $g_A^{\rm eff}\approx 1$~\cite{quenched}, in nuclear Gamow-Teller beta transitions, giant Gamow-Teller resonances and double beta decays.

# Nuclear Axial Currents from Scale-Chiral Effective Field Theory

## Key claim

The authors predict that the Gamow-Teller operator (space component of the axial current) should remain unaffected by the QCD vacuum change caused by baryonic density, while the first-forbidden beta transition operator (time component) should be strongly enhanced. This prediction is conditional on the assumption that one can approach the vicinity of an infrared fixed point in medium and study fluctuations around it, even though such an IR fixed point may not exist in the QCD vacuum.

## Method

The authors construct a scale-invariant chiral effective field theory incorporating hidden scale symmetry and hidden local symmetry, combined with the double soft-pion theorem. They apply the Crewther-Tunstall counting scheme, expanding around an assumed infrared fixed point where the QCD beta function vanishes, and derive the leading-order scale symmetry (LOSS) Lagrangian for nucleons and axial currents. The medium dependence enters through the dilaton condensate VeV, while the axial coupling constant itself remains unscaled in the Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of two-body over one-body matrix elements for the axial-charge operator is R = 0.5 ± 0.1, ranging from A=12 to A=208.
- At nuclear matter density, the scaling factor Φ(n₀) ≈ 0.8, inferred from deeply bound pionic nuclei.
- The enhancement factor for the axial-charge operator at nuclear matter density is ε(n₀) ≈ 2.0 ± 0.2.
- The experimental enhancement factor from Pb nuclei is ε^exp(n₀) = 2.01 ± 0.05.
- The Landau effective axial coupling at nuclear matter density is g_A^L(n₀) ≈ 0.79 g_A ≈ 1.0, using g_A = 1.27.
- The pionic Landau parameter contribution is (1/3) F̃₁^π(n₀) = -0.153.
- The quantum Monte Carlo calculations in A=6-10 nuclei verify the chiral filter at the level of ≲ 3%.

## Context

This work extends the "chiral filter hypothesis" first proposed by Kubodera, Delorme, and Rho (1978) using soft-pion theorems, and later by Rho (1991) using chiral perturbation theory. It provides a resolution to the long-standing puzzle of "quenched g_A" (g_A^eff ≈ 1) observed in nuclear Gamow-Teller beta transitions, giant Gamow-Teller resonances, and double beta decays. The prediction that g_A should not be affected by the chiral condensate decreasing with density is supported by the recent ab initio quantum Monte Carlo calculation by Pastore et al. (2017). The formalism also connects to the Brown-Rho scaling relations (1991) and the Fermi-liquid fixed point approach of Friman and Rho (1996). This work enables a unified understanding of axial current behavior in nuclei from light to heavy systems, with implications for compact star properties and nucleosynthesis.
