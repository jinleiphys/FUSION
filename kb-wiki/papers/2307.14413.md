# Axial-vector transition form factors and $e^+ e^- \to f_1 π^+ π^-$

> We study the transition form factors (TFFs) of axial-vector mesons in the context of currently available experimental data, including new constraints from $e^+ e^- \to f_1(1285) π^+ π^-$ that imply stringent limits on the high-energy behavior and, for the first time, allow us to provide an unambiguous determination of the couplings corresponding to the two antisymmetric TFFs. We discuss how these constraints can be implemented in a vector-meson-dominance picture, and, in combination with contributions from the light-cone expansion, construct TFFs as input for the evaluation of axial-vector contributions to hadronic light-by-light scattering in the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that existing data for the process $e^+ e^- \to f_1(1285) \pi^+ \pi^-$, when interpreted as a limit on the non-resonant contribution, provide stringent constraints on the high-energy behavior of axial-vector transition form factors and, for the first time, allow an unambiguous determination of the couplings corresponding to the two antisymmetric TFFs, resolving ambiguities that persisted in previous analyses due to insufficient data.

## Method

The authors construct vector-meson-dominance (VMD) parameterizations for the three independent transition form factors (TFFs) of the $f_1(1285)$ meson, extending previous work by including a third vector-meson multiplet to achieve the correct singly-virtual asymptotic behavior required by the $e^+ e^- \to f_1 \pi^+ \pi^-$ data. They combine these VMD parameterizations with asymptotic contributions from the light-cone expansion (LCE) and perform a global fit to experimental constraints including the equivalent two-photon decay width from L3, branching ratios for $f_1 \to \rho \gamma$ and $f_1 \to \phi \gamma$, the helicity ratio in $f_1 \to \rho \gamma \to \pi^+ \pi^- \gamma$, and the $e^+ e^- \to f_1 \pi^+ \pi^-$ cross section from BaBar.

## Key numbers

- Equivalent two-photon decay width: $\widetilde{\Gamma}_{\gamma \gamma}^{f_1} = 3.5(6)(5) \text{ keV}$ from L3 collaboration (Achard 2001)
- Symmetric TFF normalization: $C_{\sT} = 0.93(11)$ derived from the two-photon decay width
- Isoscalar-to-isovector coupling ratio for $f_1$: $R_{\text{S/V}} = -4.7(3.4)\%$ based on mixing angle $\theta_\Ax = 62(5)^\circ$
- Isoscalar coupling ratios: $R^\omega = 1/9$ and $R^\phi = -0.158(34)$ from U(3) symmetry
- Asymptotic coefficients $D_\asym \times 10^3$ for matching points $\sqrt{\sm} \in \{1.0, 1.3, 1.5, 1.7\} \text{ GeV}$: $0.125(12)$, $0.032(3)$, $0.017(2)$, $0.009(1)$

## Context

This work extends the VMD parameterization approach of Zanke (2021) by adding a third vector-meson multiplet to satisfy the asymptotic constraints from $e^+ e^- \to f_1 \pi^+ \pi^-$ data, which previous analyses by Rudenko (2017) and Milstein (2019) could not resolve due to insufficient constraints on the antisymmetric TFFs. The resulting TFF parameterizations, combined with LCE contributions from Hoferichter (2020), provide input for evaluating axial-vector contributions to hadronic light-by-light scattering in the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon, where the axial-vector contribution currently accounts for a large fraction of the final uncertainty ($a_\mu^\text{HLbL} = 92(19) \times 10^{-11}$).
