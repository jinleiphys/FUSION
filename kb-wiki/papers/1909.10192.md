# A quartet BCS-like theory

> We introduce a BCS-like theory for the quartet correlations induced by the isovector pairing interaction. It is based on a coherent state of BCS type and, unlike usual mean field approaches, it displays a vanishing pair anomalous density $\langle c^\dagger c^\dagger\rangle =0$. We find good agreement between our theory and the exact results. We discuss how the pairing and quarteting correlations share some similar qualitative features within the BCS approach. However, there is no sharp quarteting phase transition. We also present various ways in which our theory may be further developed.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a BCS-like theory for quartet correlations that, unlike standard mean-field approaches for proton-neutron pairing, has a vanishing pair anomalous density and shows no sharp quarteting phase transition, only a smooth crossover.

## Method

The authors construct a coherent state ansatz of BCS type using an isoscalar quartet operator, then linearize the exponent via Gaussian integration to obtain a factorizable form. They compute ground-state correlations by minimizing the expectation value of the isovector pairing Hamiltonian subject to a particle number constraint, and compare the results to the exact Quartet Condensation Model (QCM) for nuclei above \(^{16}\)O and \(^{100}\)Sn cores.

## Key numbers

- Relative error \(\Delta E_c/E_c\) between QBCS and QCM correlation energies reaches \(\mathcal{O}(1\%)\) around \(n_q=10\) quartets.
- For the \(^{16}\)O core and \(^{100}\)Sn core, the relative particle number fluctuation \(\Delta N/N\) decreases as \(\sim 1/N\), faster than the \(1/\sqrt{N}\) rate of standard BCS.
- The QBCS norm for \(N_\text{lev}=2\) is \(\langle QBCS |QBCS\rangle=225 x_1^4 x_2^4+12 x_1^2 x_2^2+9 x_1^4+ 9x_2^4+1\), while the BCS norm is \(\langle BCS |BCS\rangle=x_1^2x_2^2+x_1^2+x_2^2+1\).

## Context

This work extends the Quartet Condensation Model (QCM) of Sandulescu et al. (2012) and the earlier Brémond-Valatin (1963) approach to proton-neutron pairing, providing a simpler BCS-like framework that avoids the complexity of particle-number projection. The theory enables future studies of combined pairing and quarteting correlations, excited states, and non-separable mixing coefficients, and may serve as a simplified approximation or as a stabilizer for the nonlinear minimization in the QCM.
