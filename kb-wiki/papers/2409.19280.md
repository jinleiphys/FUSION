# Quarkonium production in pp and heavy-ion collisions

> We describe bottomonium production not only in pp collisions but also in heavy-ion collisions by using the Remler's formalism where quarkonium density operator is applied to all possible combination of heavy quark and heavy antiquark pairs. In pp collisions heavy (anti)quark momentum is provided by the PYTHIA event generator after rescaling $p_T$ and rapidity to imitate the FONLL calculations. Then spatial separation between heavy quark and heavy antiquark is introduced based on the uncertainty principle. In heavy-ion collisions quarkonium wavefunction changes with temperature assuming heavy quark potential equals the free energy of heavy quark and heavy antiquark system in heat bath. The density operator is updated whenever heavy quark or heavy antiquark scatters in QGP produced in heavy-ion collisions. Our results are consistent with the experimental data from ALICE and CMS Collaborations assuming that the interaction rate of heavy (anti)quark in quarkonium is suppressed to 10 \% that of unbound heavy (anti)quark. We also find that off-diagonal recombination of bottomonium barely happens even in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the Remler formalism can describe bottomonium production in both pp and heavy-ion collisions in a unified manner, but only under the condition that the interaction rate of heavy (anti)quarks bound in quarkonium is suppressed to 10% of that of unbound heavy (anti)quarks.

## Method

The authors generate heavy (anti)quark momenta using the PYTHIA event generator, rescaling transverse momentum and rapidity to match FONLL calculations, and introduce spatial separation via the uncertainty principle. In heavy-ion collisions, the quarkonium wavefunction evolves with temperature by assuming the heavy quark potential equals the free energy of the heavy quark-antiquark system in a heat bath, and the density operator is updated whenever a heavy quark or antiquark scatters in the quark-gluon plasma. The Wigner projection of the density operator is performed when the local temperature falls below the dissociation temperature of the quarkonium state.

## Key numbers

- In pp collisions at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV, the results for Upsilon(1S), Upsilon(2S), and Upsilon(3S) pT spectra and rapidity distributions are consistent with experimental data from the CMS and ALICE Collaborations.
- The dissociation temperature of Upsilon(1S) is roughly 3 Tc, while those of excited states are near Tc.
- The interaction rate of Upsilon(1S) is roughly estimated to be 40-100 MeV at T=0.2-0.4 GeV.
- Off-diagonal recombination of bottomonium barely happens even in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV.
- The RAA of Upsilon(1S) and Upsilon(2S) as a function of transverse momentum and number of participants in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV is consistent with experimental data from the CMS Collaboration.

## Context

This work extends the Remler formalism, previously applied by Song (2023), to describe quarkonium production in both pp and heavy-ion collisions, contrasting with approaches that treat these systems separately. The study builds on the original suggestion by Matsui (1986) that quarkonium suppression signals QGP formation, and addresses the regeneration effects observed by Manceau (2012). The results enable future studies of bottomonium production at LHC energies, particularly regarding the negligible role of off-diagonal recombination even in central Pb+Pb collisions.
