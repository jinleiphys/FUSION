# The production of charm mesons from quark matter at CERN SPS and RHIC

> We study the production of charm mesons and other charm baryons from quark matter at CERN SPS and RHIC energies. Using quark coalescence models as hadronization mechanism, we predict particle ratios, absolute yields and transverse momentum spectra.

# The production of charm mesons from quark matter at CERN SPS and RHIC

## Key claim

Using quark coalescence models (ALCOR and MICOR) extended to include charm quarks, the authors predict that charm hadron yields at CERN SPS energies are enhanced by a factor of 12 relative to perturbative QCD predictions but reach only 40% of the predictions from the statistical model of Gazdzicki (1999), contingent on the assumption that deconfinement occurs and that in-medium string breaking (ropes) dramatically boosts charm pair production.

## Method

The authors extended the ALCOR and MICOR hadronization models to include charm quarks, using quark coalescence as the hadronization mechanism for deconfined matter. The number of directly produced hadrons is given by the product of constituent quark numbers multiplied by coalescence coefficients and non-linear normalization coefficients determined from eight coupled equations enforcing quark number conservation. Input charm quark numbers were fixed by reproducing the measured J/psi / h- ratio of 1.0 x 10^-6 at SPS, and then scaled to RHIC energies using HIJING model predictions for charged hadron multiplicities.

## Key numbers

- At CERN SPS energy (sqrt(s) = 17.4 A GeV), the model requires N_c cbar = 3.4 charm quark-antiquark pairs to reproduce the J/psi / h- ratio of 1.0 x 10^-6
- The predicted charm hadron yields at SPS show a factor of 12 enhancement relative to perturbative QCD predictions (which give N_c cbar approximately 0.1) but reach only 40% of the statistical model prediction (N_c cbar approximately 8.5 from Gazdzicki 1999)
- At SPS, predicted yields include: D0 + anti-D0 = 2.4, Lambda_c = 2.2, anti-Lambda_c = 0.3, and J/psi / (D0 + anti-D0) = 3.0 x 10^-4
- At RHIC sqrt(s) = 130 A GeV, predicted yields scale to: N_c cbar = 12, D0 + anti-D0 = 6.4, Lambda_c = 6.1, anti-Lambda_c = 4.0, and J/psi / h- = 1.6 x 10^-6
- The MICOR model yields effective slope parameters for transverse momentum spectra at SPS: T_eff^D = 259 +/- 19 MeV and T_eff^J/psi = 315 +/- 35 MeV, with hadronization temperature T_HAD = 175 MeV and transverse flow v_T = 0.46 +/- 0.05

## Context

This work extends the ALCOR model (previously applied successfully to reproduce experimental data including the anti-Omega/Omega ratio at SPS) and the MICOR model by including charm quark degrees of freedom. The results contradict the small charm production predicted by perturbative QCD and the much larger production predicted by the statistical model of Gazdzicki (1999), instead finding an intermediate value. The paper enables future experimental measurements of charm meson transverse momentum spectra and yields at both SPS and RHIC to distinguish between hadronization scenarios and confirm QGP formation.
