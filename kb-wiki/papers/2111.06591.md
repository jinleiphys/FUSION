# Quark-mass and $1/N_c$ corrections to the vector-meson pseudoscalar-meson photon ($VPγ$) interaction

> We analyze quark-mass and $1/N_c$ corrections to all of the radiative transitions between the vector-meson nonet and the pseudoscalar-meson nonet within a chiral effective Lagrangian approach. We perform fits of the available coupling constants to experimental data and discuss the corresponding approximations. In terms of five (six) coupling constants, we obtain a reasonably good description of the 12 experimental decay rates.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a chiral effective Lagrangian including both leading-order terms and next-to-leading-order corrections from quark masses and $1/N_c$ expansion can describe the 12 experimental radiative decay rates of vector mesons into pseudoscalar mesons and photons using only five or six coupling constants, though the leading-order fit with a single coupling constant fails to provide a good description.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral effective Lagrangian in the vector-field formalism, including leading-order $VP\gamma$ interactions and next-to-leading-order corrections from $1/N_c$ double-trace terms and quark-mass single-trace terms. They perform fits of the low-energy coupling constants to experimental partial decay widths from the Particle Data Group, using the Mathematica package NonLinearModelFit, and compare different levels of approximation including leading order, next-to-leading order with $1/N_c$ corrections, and next-to-leading order with quark-mass corrections.

## Key numbers

- Leading-order fit yields $|c_1| = (3.82 \pm 0.25) \times 10^{-2}$ with reduced chi-squared $\chi^2_\text{red} = 94$ for 10 degrees of freedom, indicating poor description
- Pseudoscalar mixing angles obtained: $\theta^{[0]}_P = -19.7^\circ$ at leading order and $\theta^{[1]}_P = -12.4^\circ$ at next-to-leading order
- Vector mixing angle obtained: $\theta_V = 39.8^\circ$, close to ideal mixing $\theta_V = 35.3^\circ$
- Experimental decay widths used include $\rho^0 \rightarrow \pi^0\gamma$ at $70. \pm 12$ keV, $\rho^\pm \rightarrow \pi^\pm\gamma$ at $67.1 \pm 7.5$ keV, and $\omega \rightarrow \eta\gamma$ as the only decay with $|\delta_2| < 1$ at leading order
- Lowest-order masses: $\mathring{M}_\pi = 137.7$ MeV, $\mathring{M}_K = 518.7$ MeV; decay constant $F = 90.9$ MeV; LECs $L_5 = 1.62 \times 10^{-3}$ and $L_8 = 0.642 \times 10^{-3}$
- Quark mass ratio $r = m_s/\hat{m} = 27.37$

## Context

This work extends previous studies of radiative vector-meson decays using chiral effective Lagrangians, building on the large-$N_c$ ChPT framework developed by Moussallam (1994), Leutwyler (1996), and Kaiser (2000), as well as earlier SU(3) symmetry predictions by Glashow (1963) and quark model calculations by Anisovich (1965). The inclusion of both $1/N_c$ and quark-mass corrections systematically improves upon leading-order treatments and enables comparison with lattice QCD results such as those by Shultz (2015) and Alexandrou (2018), while the formalism can be extended to other odd-intrinsic-parity processes in low-energy QCD.
