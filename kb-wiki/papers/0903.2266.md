# 1^{-+} light exotic mesons in QCD

> We systematically re-examine the extraction of the masses and couplings of the 1^{-+} hybrid, four-quark and molecule mesons from QCD spectral sum rules (QSSR). To NLO for the perturbative and power corrections, the hybrid mass is M_H=1.81(6) GeV and M_H < 2.2(2) GeV from the positivity of the spectral function. In the same way, but to LO, the four-quark state mass is M_{4q}= 1.70(4) GeV and M_{4q} < 2.4(1) GeV, while the molecule mass is about 1.3(1) GeV. The observed π_1(1400) and π_1(1600) might be explained by a two-component mixing with the set of input masses (1.2-1.3 ; 1.70-1.74) GeV and with a mixing angle θ= -(11.7+- 2.2)^0, which slightly favours a molecule/four-quark mixing, and which eventually suggests that the π_1(2015) is mostly an hybrid meson. Isospin and non-exotic partners of the previous states and some of their radial excitations are also expected to be found in the energy region around 2 GeV. Further tests of this phenomenological scenario are required.

## Key claim

The paper systematically re-examines the extraction of masses and couplings for 1⁻⁺ exotic mesons (hybrid, four-quark, and molecule states) using QCD spectral sum rules, finding that the observed π₁(1400) and π₁(1600) can be explained by a two-component mixing of molecule and four-quark states, while π₁(2015) is mostly a hybrid meson, though further tests of this phenomenological scenario are required.

## Method

The author uses both Laplace sum rules (LSR) and finite energy sum rules (FESR) applied to two-point correlators built from hadronic local currents that select the 1⁻⁺ quantum numbers. The QCD side includes next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative corrections, power corrections from condensates up to dimension six, the effect of a tachyonic gluon mass, and operator anomalous dimensions. The spectral function is parametrized using a single resonance plus QCD continuum ansatz, with stability criteria in the sum rule variables τ and t_c used to extract optimal mass predictions.

## Key numbers

- Hybrid meson mass M_H = 1.81(6) GeV from the average of FESR and LSR results, with an upper bound M_H ≤ 2.2(2) GeV from positivity of the spectral function
- Four-quark state mass M_{4q} = 1.70(4) GeV from FESR, with upper bound M_{4q} ≤ 2.4(1) GeV from LSR
- Molecule state masses: M_{mol1} = 1.41(3) GeV and M_{mol2} ≈ (1.2–1.4) GeV
- Mixing angle θ = -(11.7 ± 2.2)° for the two-component mixing of input masses (1.2–1.3; 1.70–1.74) GeV
- Tachyonic gluon mass parameter (α_s/π)λ² ≈ -(0.07 ± 0.03) GeV²
- Renormalization group invariant quark condensate μ̂_u = -(263 ± 7) MeV

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule studies of hybrid mesons by Balitsky, D'Yakonov, and Yung (1982), Govaerts et al. (1983, 1984), and Latorre, Narison, and Pascual (1987), incorporating NLO radiative corrections partly obtained by Chetyrkin and Narison (2000) and completed by Jin, Korner, and Steele (2003). The results are compared with lattice predictions from Michael and Macneile (2006) giving M_H ≈ 2.2(2) GeV with two dynamical quarks, and with the analysis by Yang (2007) who claimed a much lower hybrid mass of 1.2 GeV. The four-quark and molecule analyses revisit work by Chen, Hosaka, and Zhu (2008) and Zhang and Jin (2005), using updated QCD parameters including the violation of vacuum saturation for the four-quark condensate. The phenomenological mixing scenario enables a unified description of the observed π₁(1400), π₁(1600), and π₁(2015) states, with predictions for isospin and non-exotic partners around 2 GeV.
