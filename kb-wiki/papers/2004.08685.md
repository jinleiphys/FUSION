# The fourth- and fifth-order virial coefficients from weak-coupling to unitarity

> In the current era of precision quantum many-body physics, one of the most scrutinized systems is the unitary limit of the nonrelativistic spin-$1/2$ Fermi gas, due to its simplicity and relevance for atomic, condensed matter, and nuclear physics. The thermodynamics of this strongly correlated system is determined by universal functions which, at high temperature, are governed by universal virial coefficients $b_n$ that capture the effects of the $n$-body system on the many-body dynamics. Currently, $b_2$ and $b_3$ are well understood, but the situation is less clear for $b_4$, and no predictions have been made for $b_5$. To answer these open questions, we implement a nonperturbative analytic approach based on the Trotter-Suzuki factorization of the imaginary-time evolution operator, using progressively finer temporal lattice spacings. Implementing these factorizations and automated algebra codes, we obtain the interaction-induced change $Δb_n$ from weak coupling to unitarity. At unitarity, we find: $Δb_3 = -0.356(4)$, in agreement with previous results; $Δb_4 = 0.062(2)$, in agreement with all previous theoretical estimates but at odds with experimental determinations; and $Δb_5 = 0.078(6)$, which is a prediction. We show the impact of those answers on the density equation of state and Tan contact, and track their origin back to their polarized and unpolarized components.

## Key claim

The authors present a nonperturbative calculation of the fourth- and fifth-order virial coefficients for attractively interacting spin-1/2 fermions from weak coupling to unitarity, finding that at unitarity their result for Δb₄ = 0.062(2) agrees with all previous theoretical estimates but disagrees with experimental determinations, while Δb₅ = 0.078(6) is a first prediction.

## Method

The authors implement a Trotter-Suzuki factorization of the imaginary-time evolution operator e^(-βĤ) using progressively finer temporal lattice spacings (order k), then calculate the diagonal matrix elements in a complete set of Slater determinant multi-particle states built from plane-wave single-particle states. The resulting momentum sums become Gaussian integrals that are carried out analytically using automated algebra codes, and the results are extrapolated to the continuous-time (large-k) limit. Two renormalization procedures are employed: tuning the coupling to reproduce the Beth-Uhlenbeck formula for Δb₂, and tuning to reproduce the exact two-body ground-state energy.

## Key numbers

- At unitarity, Δb₃ = -0.356(4), in agreement with the exact-diagonalization result of approximately -0.3551 from Liu, Hu, and Drummond
- At unitarity, Δb₄ = 0.062(2), compared with Yan and Blume's Monte Carlo result 0.078(18), Endo and Castin's conjecture 0.0620(8), and Ngampruetikorn et al.'s approximate result 0.06
- At unitarity, Δb₅ = 0.078(6), which is the first estimate of this quantity
- The subspace contributions at unitarity: Δb₃₁ = 0.0931(8), Δb₂₂ = -0.1244(7), Δb₄₁ = -0.0598(7), Δb₃₂ = 0.0988(29)
- For trapped gases at unitarity in the high-temperature limit: Δbᵀ₃ = -0.0685(8), Δbᵀ₄ = 0.00775(25), Δbᵀ₅ = 0.0070(5)

## Context

This work extends the Trotter-Suzuki factorization approach previously used by Hou et al. (2019) for lower-order coefficients, dramatically improving the implementation to reach factorization orders k=21 for n=3, k=12 for n=4, and k=9 for n=5. The results for Δb₄ disagree with experimental equation-of-state analyses from ENS (Nascimbene, 2010) and MIT (2011), which gave values around 0.096, while the authors suggest that the large Δb₅ they predict could interfere with experimental determinations of Δb₄. The results enable improved virial expansion predictions for the density equation of state and Tan contact at unitarity, showing better agreement with experimental data when fifth-order contributions are included.
