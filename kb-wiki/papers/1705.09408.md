# SU(4) flavor symmetry breaking in D-meson couplings to light hadrons

> The validity of SU(4)-flavor symmetry relations of couplings of charmed $D$ mesons to light mesons and baryons is examined with the use of $^3{\rm P}_0$ quark-pair creation model and nonrelativistic quark model wave functions. We focus on the three-meson couplings $ππρ$, $KKρ$ and $DDρ$ and baryon-baryon-meson couplings $NNπ$, $NΛK$ and $NΛ_c D$. It is found that SU(4)-flavor symmetry is broken at the level of 30% in the $DDρ$ tree-meson couplings and 20% in the baryon-baryon-meson couplings. Consequences of these findings for DN cross sections and existence of bound states D-mesons in nuclei are discussed.

## Key claim

SU(4)-flavor symmetry relations for D-meson couplings to light hadrons are broken at the level of 30% in the DDρ three-meson coupling and 20% in the NΛcD baryon-baryon-meson coupling, when evaluated using the ³P₀ quark-pair creation model with nonrelativistic quark model wave functions; the breaking originates solely from quark-mass differences in the Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors used the ³P₀ quark-pair creation model to compute three-hadron vertex amplitudes for ππρ, KKρ, DDρ, NNπ, NΛK, and NΛcD couplings. Hadron wave functions were obtained by solving the nonrelativistic quark model Schrödinger equation with a confining plus Coulomb-plus-hyperfine interaction, using a Gaussian basis expansion and the Rayleigh-Ritz variational principle. The SU(4) breaking was quantified by computing ratios of the transition amplitudes at zero momentum transfer and at the ρ-meson pole, and by extracting coupling constants through matching the ³P₀ amplitudes to meson-exchange Lagrangians.

## Key numbers

- SU(4) breaking in the DDρ coupling relative to ππρ is 30% at q² = 0 (ratio A_DDρ/A_ππρ = 1.26).
- SU(4) breaking in the NΛcD coupling relative to NNπ is 20% at q² = 0 (ratio A_NΛcD/A_NNπ = 0.83).
- At the ρ-meson pole (q² = -m²ρ), the DDρ breaking relative to ππρ is 10% (ratio = 1.07).
- The calculated coupling constants (for γ = 0.4-0.5) are: g_ππρ = 5.85-7.32, g_DDρ = 2.34-2.90, g_NNπ = 10.83-13.54, g_NΛcD = 13.56-16.95.
- The ratio g_NΛK/g_NΛcD from the ³P₀ model is 0.92 at q² = 0, compared to 0.68 from QCD sum rules (lc-sr).
- Quark-model masses: m_D = 1866 MeV (experiment 1866 MeV), m_Λc = 2195 MeV (experiment 2286 MeV, 4% discrepancy).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of SU(4) symmetry breaking in three-hadron couplings that used vector meson dominance (Mat98, Lin00a), Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter equations (ElBennich 2011), QCD sum rules (Bracco 2011, lc-sr), lattice QCD (Can 2012), and holographic QCD (Ballon-Bayona 2017). The results are directly relevant to calculations of DN and anti-DN cross sections and the possible existence of D-mesic nuclei, which have been studied using hadronic Lagrangians with SU(4) symmetry assumptions (Haidenbauer 2007, 2008, 2010; Fontoura 2012). The pattern of SU(4) breaking found here (A_DDρ < A_KKρ < A_ππρ) is opposite to that of ElBennich (2011) but agrees with the holographic QCD calculation of Ballon-Bayona (2017). Further studies are needed to determine the impact on DN cross sections and D-meson binding in nuclei.
