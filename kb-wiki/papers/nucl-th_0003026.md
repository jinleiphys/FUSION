# Borromean Binding of Three or Four Bosons

> We estimate the ratio $R=g_{3}/g_{2}$ of the critical coupling constants $g_{2}$ and $g_{3}$ which are required to achieve binding of 2 or 3 bosons, respectively, with a short-range interaction, and examine how this ratio depends on the shape of the potential. Simple monotonous potentials give $R\simeq 0.8$. A wide repulsive core pushes this ratio close to R=1. On the other hand, for an attractive well protected by an external repulsive barrier, the ratio approaches the rigorous lower bound $R=2/3$. We also present results for N=4 bosons, sketch the extension to $N>4$, and discuss various consequences.

# Borromean Binding of Three or Four Bosons

## Key claim

The ratio R = g3/g2 of critical coupling constants required to bind three versus two bosons with a short-range interaction is not universal but depends strongly on the potential shape: simple monotonous potentials give R ≈ 0.8, a wide repulsive core pushes R close to 1, and an attractive well protected by an external repulsive barrier drives R toward the rigorous lower bound of 2/3.

## Method

The authors computed critical coupling constants g2, g3, and g4 for identical bosons by solving the Schrödinger equation variationally, using both exponential and Gaussian basis functions with stochastic variational methods. For each potential shape (Yukawa, exponential, Gaussian, Morse, Pöschl–Teller, and a combined Gaussian barrier potential), they determined g2 from exact or numerical solutions of the two-body problem, then extrapolated g3 and g4 from the behavior of the N-body binding energy as a function of coupling strength near the threshold where binding vanishes.

## Key numbers

- For monotonous potentials (Yukawa, exponential, Gaussian): R = g3/g2 ≈ 0.80, and g4/g3 ≈ 0.80, with g4/g2 ≈ 0.64
- For the pure Gaussian potential: g2 = 2.680, g3/g2 = 0.79, g4/g3 = 0.80
- For the external barrier potential (a,b) = (1,-2): g2 = 21.20, g3/g2 = 0.672, g4/g3 = 0.754, approaching the lower bound g3/g2 = 2/3
- For the Morse potential with large repulsive core: R ≈ 1 − 0.43/r0, where r0 is the core size parameter
- The rigorous lower bound for the ratio is R ≥ 2/3, and the upper bound is R ≤ 1 for purely attractive potentials

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of Borromean binding in nuclear physics, where systems like 6He (treated as α + n + n) are bound while all two-body subsystems are unbound, a phenomenon named after the Borromean rings. The authors build on the rigorous bound R ≥ 2/3 established by Richard (1994) and the observation that simple potentials all give R ≈ 0.8, and they systematically explore how potential shape modifies this ratio. The results enable predictions for when Efimov states and the Thomas collapse occur, and they connect to the infinite boson matter limit where the critical coupling g∞ corresponds to the vanishing of the scattering length, as noted by Bruch (1976).
