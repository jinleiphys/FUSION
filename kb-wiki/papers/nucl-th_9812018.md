# Surface behaviour of the pairing gap in semi-infinite nuclear matter

> The $^1S_0$-pairing gap in semi-infinite nuclear matter is evaluated microscopically using the effective pairing interaction recently found explicitly in the coordinate representation starting from the separable form of the Paris NN-potential. Instead of direct iterative solution of the gap equation, a new method proposed by V.A.Khodel, V.V.Khodel and J.W.Clark was used which simplifies the procedure significantly. The gap $Δ$ obtained in our calculations exibits a strong variation in the surface region with a pronounced maximum near the surface.

# Surface behaviour of the pairing gap in semi-infinite nuclear matter

## Key claim

The $^1S_0$ pairing gap in semi-infinite nuclear matter exhibits a strong surface maximum, with the ratio of the surface peak to the asymptotic interior value reaching approximately 1.8 for a chemical potential of $\mu = -8$ MeV and approximately 1.5 for $\mu = -16$ MeV, but this surface enhancement is less pronounced than the surface enhancement of the effective pairing interaction itself due to a large pairing correlation length that couples surface and volume regions.

## Method

The authors solved the gap equation for semi-infinite nuclear matter using the KKC method (Khodel, Khodel, and Clark, 1998), which separates the gap into a temperature-independent shape function $\chi$ and a temperature-dependent amplitude $\Delta_{\rm F}(T)$. At the critical temperature $T_c$, the gap equation becomes linear, allowing the shape function to be obtained by solving a set of homogeneous integral equations in coordinate space, using a Fourier expansion over an interval of $(-40\ \text{fm}, 10\ \text{fm})$ with $N=101$ basis functions. The effective pairing interaction was taken from prior work (Baldo et al., 1998) using the separable $3\times 3$ form of the Paris $NN$-potential, and the gap amplitude $\Delta_{\rm F}$ was determined from infinite nuclear matter calculations.

## Key numbers

- For chemical potential $\mu = -8$ MeV: critical temperature $T_c = 0.124$ MeV and gap amplitude $\Delta_{\rm F} = 0.167$ MeV in infinite nuclear matter.
- For chemical potential $\mu = -16$ MeV: critical temperature $T_c = 0.57$ MeV and gap amplitude $\Delta_{\rm F} = 0.94$ MeV in infinite nuclear matter.
- Ratio of surface maximum to asymptotic interior value of the gap: approximately 1.8 for $\mu = -8$ MeV, approximately 1.5 for $\mu = -16$ MeV.
- The ratios $C_2/C_1$ and $C_3/C_1$ describing the momentum dependence of the gap in infinite nuclear matter at $k_{\rm F} = 0.8\ \text{fm}^{-1}$ remain constant within 2-3% accuracy from $T=0$ up to $T_c \approx 1.55$ MeV.
- The correlation length $\xi \sim v_{\rm F}/\Delta_{\rm F}$ exceeds 10 fm for $\Delta_{\rm F} \lesssim 1$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic treatment of pairing in semi-infinite nuclear matter developed by Baldo et al. (1998) for the effective pairing interaction, using the KKC method (Khodel, Khodel, and Clark, 1998) to avoid the slow convergence of direct iterative solution of the gap equation. The surface enhancement of the gap arises from the large surface value of the effective pairing interaction found in that prior work, but is partially suppressed by the large pairing correlation length. The results are relevant for understanding pairing in finite nuclei and near the nuclear drip-line where the chemical potential approaches zero, and the authors note that analysis of the small-$\mu$ regime is in progress.
