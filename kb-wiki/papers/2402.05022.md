# Once-in-a-lifetime encounter models for neutrino media: From coherent oscillations to flavor equilibration

> Collective neutrino oscillations are typically studied using the lowest-order quantum kinetic equation, also known as the mean-field approximation. However, some recent quantum many-body simulations suggest that quantum entanglement among neutrinos may be important and may result in flavor equilibration of the neutrino gas. In this work, we develop new quantum models for neutrino gases in which any pair of neutrinos can interact at most once in their lifetimes. A key parameter of our models is $γ=μΔz$, where $μ$ is the neutrino coupling strength, which is proportional to the neutrino density, and $Δz$ is the duration over which a pair of neutrinos can interact each time. Our models reduce to the mean-field approach in the limit $γ\to0$ and achieve flavor equilibration in time $t \gg (γμ)^{-1}$. These models demonstrate the emergence of coherent flavor oscillations from the particle perspective and may help elucidate the role of quantum entanglement in collective neutrino oscillations.

# Once-in-a-lifetime encounter models for neutrino media: From coherent oscillations to flavor equilibration

## Key claim

The authors develop quantum models for neutrino gases where any pair of neutrinos can interact at most once in their lifetimes, and show that these models reduce to the mean-field approximation in the limit γ → 0 and achieve flavor equilibration at times t ≫ (γμ)⁻¹, where γ = μΔz is the product of neutrino coupling strength and interaction duration. The abstract's claim of "demonstrating the emergence of coherent flavor oscillations" is qualified by the paper's stated limited goal: not to compute neutrino oscillations in realistic astrophysical environments, but to show how mean-field and many-body results can appear as different limits of the same quantum model.

## Method

The authors construct two "once-in-a-lifetime encounter" (OILE) models treating each neutrino as an open quantum system with the rest of the neutrino gas as its environment. In the first model, a single foreground neutrino passes through a uniform background medium, encountering one distinct background neutrino in each spatial interval Δz, with the two-neutrino density operator evolving under a constant forward-scattering Hamiltonian. In the second model, an ensemble of N particles evolves in discrete time steps of size Δz, where in each step all particles undergo one interaction in random, mutually exclusive pairs. The authors derive approximate master equations for the Bloch vectors describing the flavor evolution and compare their solutions to numerical simulations for various values of γ.

## Key numbers

- γ = μΔz ∼ 10⁻¹⁰ for typical neutrino wave packet sizes (Δz ∼ 10⁻¹¹ cm, μ⁻¹ ∼ 0.1 cm)
- For γ ≪ 1, the foreground neutrino exhibits coherent oscillation with period 2π/μ
- The flavor of the foreground neutrino approaches that of the medium on a timescale ≳ (γμ)⁻¹
- Simulations use N = 100 particles with 60 νₑ (ω = 0.1μ) and 40 νₓ (ω = 0.2μ) initially, with effective mixing angle θ = 10⁻³
- Three values of γ are simulated: 10⁻¹, 10⁻², and 10⁻³
- For γ = 10⁻³, flavor-pendulum-like evolution persists for μt ≲ 30 before kinematic decoherence sets in
- For γ = 10⁻¹, quantum decoherence shrinks individual Bloch vector magnitudes before the flavor pendulum falls
- The geometric factor ∑_b (1 − v_a·v_b)²/N ≈ 4/3 appears in the decoherence term of the ensemble master equation
- Quantum decoherence becomes significant at t ≳ (γμ)⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the ongoing debate about the adequacy of the mean-field approximation for collective neutrino oscillations, which has been the standard approach in the literature (reviewed by Duan (2010), Chakraborty (2016), Tamborra (2020), and Volpe (2023)). Recent quantum many-body simulations by Cervia (2019), Patwardhan (2021), Illa (2022), and Martin (2023) suggest that quantum entanglement may be important and could lead to flavor equilibration, though counterarguments have been raised by Shalgar (2023) and Johns (2023). Unlike existing many-body models that involve closed systems of a limited number of neutrinos interacting indefinitely, the OILE models treat each neutrino as an open quantum system, which is more realistic for astrophysical environments like core-collapse supernovae or binary-neutron star mergers where neutrinos interact only briefly while their wave packets overlap. The models may serve as a starting point for future studies incorporating more realistic physics such as non-forward scattering and probabilistic interaction times.
