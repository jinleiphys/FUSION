# Gauge Independence and Chiral Symmetry Breaking in a Strong Magnetic Field

> The gauge independence of the dynamical fermion mass generated through chiral symmetry breaking in QED in a strong, constant external magnetic field is critically examined. We present a (first, to the best of our knowledge) consistent truncation of the Schwinger-Dyson equations in the lowest Landau level approximation. We demonstrate that the dynamical fermion mass, obtained as the solution of the truncated Schwinger-Dyson equations evaluated on the fermion mass shell, is manifestly gauge independent.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the bare vertex approximation (BVA) is a consistent truncation of the Schwinger-Dyson equations in the lowest Landau level approximation (LLLA) for QED in a strong magnetic field, but only if the truncated fermion self-energy is evaluated on the fermion mass shell; this condition, which the authors state has gone unnoticed in prior literature, makes the dynamical fermion mass manifestly gauge independent.

## Method

The authors truncate the Schwinger-Dyson equations for QED in a strong, constant external magnetic field by replacing the full vertex with the bare vertex (the BVA, also known as the improved rainbow approximation) and restricting fermion propagation to the lowest Landau level (the LLLA). They then prove that the truncated vacuum polarization is transverse and that the gauge-dependent contribution to the fermion self-energy vanishes identically when evaluated on the fermion mass shell, using the Ward-Takahashi identity in the BVA. Finally, they solve the resulting on-shell gap equation numerically to obtain the dynamical fermion mass as a function of the fine-structure constant for several values of fermion flavors.

## Key numbers

* The polarization function is given by \(\Pi(\qpara^2,\qperp^2)=\frac{2\alpha}{\pi}\,|eH|\,\exp\left(-\frac{q_\perp^2}{2|eH|}\right) F\left(\frac{\qpara^2}{4m^2}\right)\), where \(\alpha=e^2/4\pi\) is the fine-structure constant and \(F(u)\) has asymptotic behavior \(F(u)\simeq 0\) for \(|u|\ll 1\) and \(F(u)\simeq 1\) for \(|u|\gg 1\).
* Photons with momenta \(m^2\ll|\qpara^2|\ll|eH|\) and \(\qperp^2 \ll |eH|\) are screened with a characteristic length \(L=(2 \alpha |eH|/\pi)^{-1/2}\).
* The dynamical fermion mass solution is fit by \(m=a\,\sqrt{2|eH|}\;\beta(\alpha)\,\exp\left[-\frac{\pi}{\alpha\log(b/N_f\,\alpha)}\right]\), where \(a\) is a constant of order one, \(b\simeq 2.3\), and \(\beta(\alpha) \simeq N_f\,\alpha\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of chiral symmetry breaking in a strong magnetic field using the rainbow and improved rainbow approximations, specifically those by Gusynin et al. (1995, 1998) and Kuznetsov et al. (2002), which evaluated the fermion self-energy off the mass shell and often worked in a particular gauge (e.g., Feynman gauge) or invoked a special noncovariant gauge. The authors argue that those earlier results contain gauge-dependent artifacts, such as the claimed existence of a critical number of fermion flavors \(N_{cr}\) and a double splitting of the dynamical mass, which they show are incorrect. The gauge-independent on-shell approach presented here enables a consistent and reliable calculation of the dynamical fermion mass in the LLLA and is general enough to be applied to other nonperturbative problems in gauge theories.
