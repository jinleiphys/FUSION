# Dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions with in-medium spectral functions of vector mesons

> The in-medium spectral functions of $ρ$ and $ω$ mesons and the broadening of the nucleon resonances at finite baryon density are calculated self-consistently by combining a resonance dominance model for the vector meson production with an extended vector meson dominance model. The influence of the in-medium modifications of the vector meson properties on the dilepton spectrum in heavy-ion collisions is investigated. The dilepton spectrum is generated for the C+C reaction at 2.0$A$ GeV and compared with recent HADES Collaboration data. The collision dynamics is then described by the Tübingen relativistic quantum molecular dynamics transport model. We find that an iterative calculation of the vector meson spectral functions that takes into account the broadening of the nucleon resonances due to their increased in-medium decay branchings is convergent and provides a reasonable description of the experimental data in the mass region $0.45\leq M \leq 0.75$ GeV. On the other side, the theoretical calculations slightly underestimate the region $m_π\leq M \leq 0.4$ GeV. Popular in-medium scenarios such as a schematic collisional broadening and dropping vector mesons masses are discussed as well.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a self-consistent iterative calculation of the in-medium spectral functions of rho and omega mesons, which accounts for the broadening of nucleon resonances due to their increased in-medium decay branchings, converges and provides a reasonable description of the HADES dilepton data in the mass region 0.45 to 0.75 GeV, while slightly underestimating the region from the pion mass to 0.4 GeV.

## Method

The authors combined a resonance dominance model for vector meson production with an extended vector meson dominance model to calculate the in-medium spectral functions of rho and omega mesons and the broadening of nucleon resonances at finite baryon density. The vector meson self-energies were computed from the forward scattering amplitude of vector mesons on nucleons using a Breit-Wigner form for resonance scattering, with helicity amplitudes obtained from the same relativistic approach and set of baryonic resonances previously applied to dilepton and vector meson production in proton-proton collisions. The collision dynamics for the C+C reaction at 2.0A GeV was described by the Tubingen relativistic quantum molecular dynamics transport model, and the resulting dilepton spectrum was compared with HADES Collaboration data.

## Key numbers

* The dilepton spectrum was generated for the C+C reaction at 2.0A GeV and compared with HADES data in the mass region 0.45 to 0.75 GeV, where the model provided a reasonable description.
* The theoretical calculations slightly underestimated the region from the pion mass (m_pi) to 0.4 GeV.
* The on-shell decay width of the rho meson is 150 MeV at its physical mass m_rho.
* The on-shell decay width of the omega meson is 8.4 MeV at its physical mass m_omega.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous NRD+eVMD model, which had been applied to vector meson production in elementary proton-proton reactions and dilepton production in heavy-ion reactions using phenomenological collisional broadening and dropping mass scenarios (Shekter 2003, Cozma 2006). The present paper goes beyond those phenomenological analyses by calculating the in-medium spectral functions of rho and omega mesons and nucleon resonances self-consistently within the same unified model. This enables a first self-consistent theoretical description of dilepton spectra based on a unified model for nucleon resonances, vector mesons, dilepton production, and their in-medium modifications, and it provides a framework for interpreting upcoming HADES measurements at GSI.
