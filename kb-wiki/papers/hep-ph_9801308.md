# Electromagnetic form factors in the J/ψmass region: The case in favor of additional resonances

> Using the results of our recent analysis of e^+e^- annihilation, we plot the curves for the diagonal and transition form factors of light hadrons in the time-like region up to the production threshold of an open charm quantum number. The comparison with existing data on the decays of J/ψinto such hadrons shows that some new resonance structures may be present in the mass range between 2 GeVand the J/ψmass. Searching them may help in a better understanding of the mass spectrum in both the simple and a more sophisticated quark models, and in revealing the details of the three-gluon mechanism of the OZI rule breaking in K\bar K channel.

# Electromagnetic form factors in the J/ψ mass region: The case in favor of additional resonances

## Key claim
The comparison of existing data on J/ψ decays into light hadrons with predictions from vector dominance models (VDM) and more sophisticated amplitudes including known ρ′ and ω′ resonances shows systematic discrepancies, suggesting that new resonance structures likely exist in the mass range between 2 GeV and the J/ψ mass. The authors argue that the one-photon mechanism alone cannot explain the observed decay rates, and that three-gluon contributions or additional isovector and isoscalar resonances above 2 GeV are required.

## Method
The authors use previously published analyses of e⁺e⁻ annihilation data (Achasov and Kozhevnikov, 1997, 1998) to compute diagonal and transition electromagnetic form factors of light hadrons in the time-like region up to the open charm threshold. They compare these calculated form factors with experimental branching ratios of J/ψ decays into π⁺π⁻, K⁺K⁻, ωπ⁰, ρη, and ρ⁰π⁺π⁻ final states, using both simple VDM expressions and amplitudes that incorporate mixing with heavier ρ′, ω′, and φ′ resonances.

## Key numbers
- The squared pion form factor at the J/ψ mass, |F_π(m²_J/ψ)|² = (11.9 ± 1.5 ± 0.9) × 10⁻³ (Baltrusaitis et al., 1985), or (9.8 ± 1.5) × 10⁻³ from the averaged π⁺π⁻ branching ratio (Particle Data Group, 1996); the VDM estimate is 4.3 × 10⁻³.
- The squared pion form factor at the ψ(2S) mass, |F_π(m²_ψ(2S))|² = (36 ± 23) × 10⁻³ (Brandelik et al., 1979).
- The VDM estimate for the isovector kaon form factor at the J/ψ mass, |F_K^(1)(VDM)(m²_J/ψ)| = 0.033; the ratio of the extracted value to this estimate is 2, 1, or 2/3 depending on the relative phase θ = 62°, 22°, or 0° between I=0 and I=1 contributions.
- The isoscalar kaon form factor at the J/ψ mass from the fit including heavier resonances: F_K^(0)(m²_J/ψ) = (6.5 − 6.3i) × 10⁻³; the isovector part: F_K^(1)(m²_J/ψ) = (3.1 − 1.3i) × 10⁻².
- The ratio |F_ωπ⁰(m²_J/ψ)| / |F_ωπ⁰(0)| from VDM is 0.0659, while the experimental value is 0.0335 ± 0.0059 (Coffman et al., 1988).
- The three-gluon coupling constant satisfies |a_K^(ggg)| ≥ 0.9|g₀|, where g₀ is the J/ψ to K̄K coupling in the I=0 state.
- Estimated cross sections at √s = 2.5 GeV: π⁺π⁻ = 0.03 nb, K⁺K⁻ = 0.02 nb, ωπ⁰ = 0.04 nb, ρ⁰η (π⁺π⁻η) = 0.04 nb, π⁺π⁻π⁺π⁻ = 0.6 nb.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analyses of e⁺e⁻ annihilation into light hadrons (Achasov and Kozhevnikov, 1997, 1998) to the J/ψ mass region, comparing their form factor calculations with decay data from Baltrusaitis et al. (1985), Coffman et al. (1988), and others. The systematic discrepancies between VDM predictions and experimental data, particularly for π⁺π⁻ and ωπ⁰ channels, suggest the need for additional resonance structures above 2 GeV, such as the ρ(2150) listed in the Particle Data Group (1996). The results motivate future experimental searches in the energy range from 2 GeV to the J/ψ mass at facilities like VEPP-4M and BEPC, and could help clarify the three-gluon mechanism of OZI rule breaking in the K̄K channel.
