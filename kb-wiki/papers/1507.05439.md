# Landau quantization and mass-radius relation of magnetized White Dwarfs in general relativity

> Recently, several white dwarfs have been proposed with masses significantly above the Chandrasekhar limit, known as Super-Chandrasekhar White Dwarfs, to account for the overluminous Type Ia supernovae. In the present work, Equation of State of a completely degenerate relativistic electron gas in magnetic field based on Landau quantization of charged particles in a magnetic field is developed. The mass-radius relations for magnetized White Dwarfs are obtained by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations. The effects of the magnetic energy density and pressure contributed by a density-dependent magnetic field are treated properly to find the stability configurations of realistic magnetic White Dwarf stars.

## Key claim

Magnetized white dwarfs can have masses significantly above the Chandrasekhar limit (up to ~3 \(M_\odot\)) provided they have an appropriate density-dependent magnetic field profile with high central field strength (up to \(4.414 \times 10^{14}\) gauss) and high central density, when the full general relativistic Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved with the exact equation of state including magnetic energy density and pressure contributions.

## Method

The authors developed the equation of state for a completely degenerate relativistic electron gas at zero temperature in a strong magnetic field using Landau quantization of electron states, including both the matter contributions and the explicit magnetic energy density (\(\varepsilon_B = B^2/8\pi\)) and pressure (\(P_B = B^2/24\pi\)). They solved the general relativistic Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations numerically using the Runge-Kutta method for both non-magnetic and magnetized white dwarfs, employing a density-dependent magnetic field profile that is maximum at the center and falls to \(10^9\) gauss at the surface, without fitting the equation of state to a polytropic form.

## Key numbers

- Maximum stable mass of magnetized white dwarfs: ~3 \(M_\odot\) (from Table IV, central density \(4.661657 \times 10^{-6}\) fm\(^{-3}\), central magnetic field \(B_{dc} = 10 B_c\) where \(B_c = 4.414 \times 10^{13}\) gauss)
- For a uniformly magnetized white dwarf with single Landau level throughout: mass up to 2.4521 \(M_\odot\) at central density \(5.0 \times 10^{-6}\) fm\(^{-3}\) with minimum magnetic field \(B_{dmin} = 253 B_c\) (Table II)
- For a uniformly magnetized white dwarf with multiple Landau levels: mass 2.4491 \(M_\odot\) at central density \(3.5147 \times 10^{-6}\) fm\(^{-3}\) with magnetic field \(B_d = 200 B_c\) (Table III)
- Non-magnetic white dwarf Chandrasekhar limit asymptotically reached at central density \(3.343 \times 10^{10}\) gms/cc, with maximum mass ~1.3905 \(M_\odot\) (Table I)
- Surface magnetic field for realistic models: \(10^9\) gauss; maximum central magnetic field used: \(10 B_c = 4.414 \times 10^{14}\) gauss
- Virial theorem estimate for maximum white dwarf magnetic field: \(B_{max} \sim 10^{12}\) gauss for \(M = 1.4 M_\odot\), \(R = 0.0086 R_\odot\)

## Context

This work extends the study of super-Chandrasekhar white dwarfs beyond the non-relativistic Lane-Emden approach used by Das and collaborators (2012, 2013) and Doval (2014), who obtained masses up to 2.3-2.6 \(M_\odot\) by fitting the equation of state to a polytropic form and assuming constant magnetic field throughout the star. The present calculation uses the exact equation of state in full general relativity, including magnetic field contributions that are absent in the Lane-Emden treatment. The results enable explanation of overluminous Type Ia supernovae such as SN2006gz, SN2007if, SN2009dc, and SN2003fg, which require progenitor white dwarf masses above the Chandrasekhar limit. The central magnetic field strengths used (up to \(10^{14}\)-\(10^{16}\) gauss) are consistent with upper limits from electron capture and pycnonuclear reaction instabilities discussed by Chamel and collaborators (2013).
