# Treatment of Two Nucleons in Three Dimensions

> We extend a new treatment proposed for two-nucleon (2N) and three-nucleon (3N) bound states to 2N scattering. This technique takes momentum vectors as variables, thus, avoiding partial wave decomposition, and handles spin operators analytically. We apply the general operator structure of a nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential to the NN T-matrix, which becomes a sum of six terms, each term being scalar products of spin operators and momentum vectors multiplied with scalar functions of vector momenta. Inserting this expansions of the NN force and T-matrix into the Lippmann-Schwinger equation allows to remove the spin dependence by taking traces and yields a set of six coupled equations for the scalar functions found in the expansion of the T-matrix.

## Key claim

The authors extend a previously developed three-dimensional (vector-momentum) formulation for two-nucleon and three-nucleon bound states to two-nucleon scattering, avoiding partial-wave decomposition by using a set of six time-reversal-invariant spin-momentum operators. The key hedge is that the method is presented as a formal derivation of coupled integral equations for the scalar functions of the T-matrix, with no numerical results or convergence tests shown in this paper.

## Method

The authors expand both the nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential and the NN T-matrix in a basis of six spin-momentum operators (scalar products of spin operators and momentum vectors) multiplied by scalar functions of the vector momenta. They insert these expansions into the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, then remove the spin dependence by multiplying from the left with each spin-momentum operator and taking the trace, yielding a set of six coupled integral equations for the scalar functions of the T-matrix. The scalar functions of the T-matrix are then connected to the Wolfenstein parameters of the anti-symmetrized scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

* The deuteron wave function is expanded into two scalar functions, phi_1(p) and phi_2(p), connected to the standard partial-wave s-wave psi_0(p) and d-wave psi_2(p) by psi_0(p) = phi_1(p) and psi_2(p) = (4 p^2 / (3 sqrt(2))) phi_2(p).
* Example trace results for the deuteron equations include A^d_11(p) = 3 and A^d_22(p) = (8/3) p^4.
* Example trace results for the NN scattering equations include A_24(p', p) = A_42(p', p) = 4 (p x p')^2 and A_56(p', p) = A_65(p', p) = 4 (p'^2 - p^2)^2.
* The relation between the M-matrix and T-matrix is given as M = -mu (2 pi)^2 T, where mu is the reduced mass of the NN system.

## Context

This work extends the three-dimensional bound-state formulation of Fachruddin, Elster, and Gloeckle (2004) to NN scattering, providing an alternative to the momentum-helicity basis approach of the same group. The authors note that one spin-momentum operator used in the earlier bound-state work violated time reversal, so they adopt a new set of six time-reversal-invariant operators. The technique is intended to enable realistic three-body scattering calculations at higher energies (hundreds of MeV to GeV), where partial-wave decompositions become cumbersome, building on the success of three-boson scattering calculations using vector-momentum Faddeev equations by Liu, Elster, and Gloeckle (2004).
