# Baryon anomaly and strong color fields in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76A TeV at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> With the HIJING/BBbar v2.0 heavy ion event generator, we explore the phenomenological consequences of several high parton density dynamical effects predicted in central Pb+Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energies. These include (1) jet quenching due to parton energy loss (dE/dx), (2) strangeness and hyperon enhancement due to strong longitudinal color field (SCF), and (3) enhancement of baryon-to-meson ratios due to baryon-anti-baryon junctions (JJbar) loops and SCF effects. The saturation/minijet cutoff scale p0(s)and effective string tension kappa(s,A) are constrained by our previous analysis of LHC p+p data and recent data on the charged multiplicity for Pb+Pb collisions reported by the ALICE collaboration. We predict the hadron flavor dependence (mesons and baryons) of the nuclear modification factor RAA(pT)$ and emphasize the possibility that the baryon anomaly could persist at the LHC up to pT=10 GeV, well beyond the range observed in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The baryon anomaly (enhanced baryon-to-meson ratios) observed at RHIC up to pT ≈ 4-5 GeV could persist at the LHC up to pT ≈ 10 GeV in central Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76A TeV, but this prediction depends on the combined effects of strong longitudinal color fields (SCF) and baryon-anti-baryon junction (JJbar) loops, and is not a guaranteed signature independent of model parameters.

## Method

The authors used the HIJING/BBbar v2.0 Monte Carlo event generator, which combines a two-component model of soft string fragmentation (LUND/DPM) with hard pQCD minijet production, and added three dynamical effects: parton energy loss (dE/dx) for jet quenching, strong longitudinal color fields (SCF) with enhanced string tension κ(s,A) for strangeness enhancement, and baryon-anti-baryon junction (JJbar) loops for baryon-to-meson ratio enhancement. The saturation/minijet cutoff scale p0(s,A) and effective string tension κ(s,A) were constrained by fitting to ALICE data on charged particle multiplicity in central Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV and to previous p+p data.

## Key numbers

- Charged particle multiplicity density per participant pair (2dNch/dη)/Npart in central (0-5%) Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV is a factor of 2.2 higher than in central Au+Au collisions at top RHIC energy
- Best fit parameters for central Pb+Pb at LHC: p0(s,A) = 3.7 GeV/c, κ(s,A) = 6.0 GeV/fm, with (dE/dx)2 set to dEq/dx = 2 GeV/fm and λq = 1.0 fm
- For quark jets at LHC: (dEq/dx)LHC ≈ 2 GeV/fm and mean free path (λq)LHC ≈ 1 fm, compared to (dEq/dx)RHIC ≈ 1 GeV/fm and (λq)RHIC ≈ 2 fm
- The saturation/minijet cutoff scale follows p0(s,A) = 0.416 A^0.128 √s^0.191 GeV/c, increasing from p0 = 1.5 GeV/c at √s = 0.02 TeV to p0 = 4.2 GeV/c at √s = 5.5 TeV
- Effective string tension κ(s) = κ0 (s/s0)^0.06 GeV/fm, with κ0 = 1 GeV/fm, giving κ = 1.88 GeV/fm at RHIC (√s = 0.2 TeV) and κ = 3.14 GeV/fm at √s = 14 TeV
- Nuclear modification factor RAA reaches approximately 0.4 at pT ≈ 20 GeV/c in central Pb+Pb collisions

## Context

This work extends the HIJING/BBbar v2.0 model previously applied to RHIC energies (Topor Pop et al., 2007, 2005) to LHC energies, and builds on the HIJING2.0 model (Deng et al., 2010) which did not include jet quenching or SCF effects. The predictions for the baryon anomaly persisting to higher pT at LHC than at RHIC are motivated by recent Tevatron data (CDF, 2011) showing Ξ-/Λ and Ω-/Λ ratios rising at low pT and plateauing up to 10 GeV/c in p+pbar collisions. The results enable future experimental tests by ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS of whether the baryon anomaly mechanism (SCF + JJbar loops) or alternative explanations (e.g., recombination, hydrodynamics) better describe the data at LHC energies.
