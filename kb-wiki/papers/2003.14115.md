# Light Neutral Clusters in Supernova Matter

> The role of weakly bound neutral clusters, such as dineutrons and tetraneutrons, in matter of high density and high temperature is discussed. Under such conditions, which are characteristic of core-collapse supernovae, the lifetime of multineutrons may prove to be sufficiently long for them to have a pronounced effect on the formation of the chemical composition. The influence of the multineutron binding energy and other nuclear properties on the magnitude of the effect being considered is examined.

# Light Neutral Clusters in Supernova Matter

## Key claim
Weakly bound neutral clusters such as dineutrons and tetraneutrons may have a pronounced effect on the chemical composition of supernova matter at high density and high temperature, but the effect on summed equation-of-state features is relatively small (about 5–10% changes), and the actual contribution depends on the as-yet-unknown lifetimes and production cross sections of these clusters.

## Method
The authors used two nuclear statistical equilibrium equations of state (EoS NY from Nadyozhin and Yudin (2015) and EoS BPRS from Blinnikov, Panov, Rudzsky and Sumiyoshi (2010)) to calculate the equilibrium chemical composition of supernova matter at a time about 1 ms before core bounce. They included dineutrons and tetraneutrons as additional neutral species with binding energies set to −66 keV and −0.8 MeV respectively, and compared the resulting weight fractions and integrated properties (average mass number, average charge number, and coherent neutrino scattering cross section) against calculations with a standard set of nuclei only.

## Key numbers
- Dineutron binding energy set to −66 keV (unbound state) and tetraneutron binding energy set to −0.8 MeV, based on experimental results from Marques et al. (2012).
- At densities above about 10¹¹ g cm⁻³, dineutrons become at least as abundant as other light nuclei; in the central core they are second only to free neutrons.
- Inclusion of multineutrons leads to a decrease of about 7% (EoS NY) and about 5% (EoS BPRS) in the total weight fraction of heavy nuclei (Z > 2).
- Inclusion of multineutrons leads to a decrease of about 5% in the average charge and mass of heavy nuclei.
- The parameter σ (sum over all species of Y_i A_i², related to coherent neutrino scattering) decreases by up to 10% when multineutrons are included.
- The dineutron binding energy was varied over the range −1 to +1 MeV; the tetraneutron binding energy was varied over −2 to +2 MeV. The calculated multineutron concentrations are weakly sensitive to these variations at T = 4 MeV, ρ = 10¹³ g cm⁻³, and Y_e = 0.32.
- An order-of-magnitude estimate gives the equilibrium mass fraction of multineutrons as X_mn ≈ 10²² × τ(s) × ρ₁₃ × √(T₅) × Σ Y_i Y_j σ_b(i,j), where τ is the multineutron lifetime, ρ₁₃ is density in units of 10¹³ g cm⁻³, T₅ is temperature in units of 5 MeV, and σ_b are production cross sections in barns.
- Multineutron lifetimes in the range 10⁻¹² to 10⁻²¹ s are considered sufficient for them to participate in determining the properties of hot dense nuclear plasma.

## Context
This work extends the line of research on light clusters in supernova matter, following the finding by Arcones et al. (2008) and later confirmation by another group that highly neutron-rich hydrogen and helium isotopes (such as ⁴H and ⁸He) can have unexpectedly high concentrations in collapsing stellar cores. The authors note that previous equations of state (Nadyozhin and Yudin 2015; Blinnikov, Panov, Rudzsky and Sumiyoshi 2010) did not include multineutrons, and that the role of such "exotic" neutral clusters had not been previously considered in this context. The results suggest that multineutrons could affect neutrino transport and the chemical composition available for subsequent alpha-process or r-process nucleosynthesis, but the authors emphasize that definitive conclusions require new experimental and theoretical data on multineutron lifetimes and production cross sections.
