# Hadron-Hadron Interactions

> The present status of the chiral approach to the $NN$ interaction as proposed by Weinberg is discussed. The important role of correlation effects and explicit vector meson ($ρ$, $ω$) exchange in the dynamics of baryon-baryon interactions is demonstrated. As an example, the inclusion of the exchange of a correlated pair of $π$ and $ρ$ meson between two nucleons appears to be mandatory in order to resolve a long-standing puzzle concerning the formfactor at the pion-nucleon-nucleon vertex.

## Key claim

The chiral approach to the NN interaction, while formally systematic, shows noticeable deficiencies in describing empirical data already at very low energies because it treats important contributions like vector-meson exchange and correlation effects via zero-range contact terms rather than explicitly; a meson-exchange model that violates chiral symmetry to some extent can describe the data quantitatively.

## Method

The paper reviews and compares the chiral NN potential of Ref. [6] (based on the most general effective chiral Lagrangian with pions, nonrelativistic nucleons, and Delta-isobars up to third order in the chiral expansion) against the full Bonn potential of Ref. [7]. It then evaluates the contribution of correlated pi-rho exchange to the NN interaction using a dynamical pi-rho T-matrix model, with driving terms adjusted to a1 resonance parameters from tau decay and charge exchange production data, and inserts this T-matrix into a dispersion-theoretic treatment to obtain spectral functions for the pionic channel.

## Key numbers

- The chiral model of Ref. [6] predicts a deuteron binding energy of 2.18 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 2.2245754 MeV.
- The chiral model predicts a deuteron quadrupole moment of 0.231 fm^2, compared to the experimental value of 0.2859 +/- 0.0003 fm^2.
- The chiral model predicts an asymptotic D/S ratio of 0.0239, compared to the experimental value of 0.0256 +/- 0.0004.
- The Bonn potential (Ref. [7]) predicts a deuteron binding energy of 2.2247 MeV, quadrupole moment of 0.2807 fm^2, and asymptotic D/S ratio of 0.0267.
- The Bonn potential with a soft piNN formfactor (Lambda = 1 GeV) plus correlated pi-rho exchange predicts a deuteron binding energy of 2.2246 MeV, quadrupole moment of 0.2791 fm^2, and asymptotic D/S ratio of 0.0266.
- The piNN cutoff mass required in the Bonn potential is 1.3 GeV, while lattice calculations find a monopole mass of 0.75 +/- 0.14 GeV.
- The a1 resonance parameters used are m_a1 = 1.26 GeV and Gamma_a1 = 0.4 GeV.
- The Gaussian cutoff used in the chiral approach has L = 3.9 fm^-1.
- The correlated pi-rho exchange spectral function peaks around 1.1 GeV, compared to the phenomenological pi' mass of 1.2 GeV used in some NN models.

## Context

This paper extends the critique of the chiral NN approach of Weinberg by comparing it to the Bonn potential of Machleidt, Holinde, and Elster (1987). It addresses a long-standing puzzle: the piNN formfactor cutoff required in potential models (1.3 GeV) is much larger than values from other sources including lattice calculations (0.75 +/- 0.14 GeV). The work enables a resolution by showing that including correlated pi-rho exchange, as evaluated using a dynamical model by the author and collaborators, can compensate for using a softer piNN formfactor, allowing consistency with both deuteron data and independent formfactor determinations.
