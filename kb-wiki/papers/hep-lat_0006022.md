# Casimir scaling of SU(3) static potentials

> Potentials between static colour sources in eight different representations are computed in four dimensional SU(3) gauge theory. The simulations have been performed with the Wilson action on anisotropic lattices where the renormalised anisotropies have been determined non-perturbatively. After an extrapolation to the continuum limit we are able to exclude any violations of the Casimir scaling hypothesis that exceed 5% for source separations of up to 1 fm.

## Key claim
After extrapolation to the continuum limit, violations of the Casimir scaling hypothesis for SU(3) static potentials are excluded at the 5% level for source separations up to 1 fm, but only for distances smaller than the string breaking scale of about 1.2 fm.

## Method
The author performed lattice simulations of four-dimensional SU(3) gauge theory using the Wilson action on anisotropic lattices with renormalised anisotropy approximately 4, determined non-perturbatively. Static potentials were extracted from smeared Wilson loops in eight different representations (3, 6, 8, 10, 15a, 15s, 24, 27) at three lattice spacings corresponding to beta values 5.8, 6.0, and 6.2, with volumes ranging from 8^3 x 48 to 16^3 x 96 lattice sites. The potentials were obtained from correlated exponential fits to Wilson loop expectation values, and ratios of potentials in higher representations to the fundamental potential were extrapolated to the continuum limit.

## Key numbers
- Casimir scaling violations excluded at the 5% level for source separations up to 1 fm after continuum extrapolation
- Renormalised anisotropies: xi = 4.076(25) at beta=5.8, xi = 4.084(74) at beta=6.0, xi = 3.957(51) at beta=6.2
- Sommer scale r0/a_sigma: 3.074(29) at beta=5.8 (L=8), 3.052(34) at beta=5.8 (L=12), 4.437(71) at beta=6.0, 6.106(66) at beta=6.2
- String breaking scale r_c approximately 1.2 fm, beyond which Casimir scaling is not expected to hold
- Ground state overlaps c_D at r approximately r0 range from 0.62(3) (worst case, representation 10 at beta=5.8) to 0.97(1) (best case, fundamental at beta=6.2)
- Self energy shift Delta V_self between sigma-sigma and sigma-tau potentials: 0.091(9) at beta=5.8, 0.096(18) at beta=6.0, 0.124(11) at beta=6.2

## Context
This work extends previous lattice studies of static potentials in higher representations of SU(N) gauge groups, which had been performed mainly for SU(2) in three and four dimensions (Ambjorn 1984, Bernard 1982, Michael 1985) and for SU(3) at zero temperature (Campbell 1986, Michael 1992, Deldar 1998). The results strongly support the Casimir scaling hypothesis, which states that potentials between sources in different representations are proportional to the ratios of their quadratic Casimir eigenvalues, and rule out models predicting different behaviour such as bag models, flux tube models, and the stochastic vacuum model. The findings enable more precise discrimination between confinement models and provide a benchmark for non-perturbative QCD calculations, though the study is restricted to distances below the string breaking scale.
