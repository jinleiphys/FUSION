# Lattice simulations of the QCD chiral transition at real baryon density

> State-of-the-art lattice QCD studies of hot and dense strongly interacting matter currently rely on extrapolation from zero or imaginary chemical potentials. The ill-posedness of numerical analytic continuation puts severe limitations on the reliability of such methods. Here we use the more direct sign reweighting method to perform lattice QCD simulation of the QCD chiral transition at finite real baryon density on phenomenologically relevant lattices. This method does not require analytic continuation and avoids the overlap problem associated with generic reweighting schemes, so has only statistical but no uncontrolled systematic uncertainties for a fixed lattice setup. This opens up a new window to study hot and dense strongly interacting matter from first principles. We perform simulations up to a baryochemical potential-temperature ratio of $μ_B/T=2.5$ covering most of the RHIC Beam Energy Scan range in the chemical potential. We also clarify the connection of the approach to the more traditional phase reweighting method.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that sign reweighting, a method that avoids both the overlap problem of generic reweighting and the ill-posedness of numerical analytic continuation, is viable for phenomenologically relevant lattice QCD simulations at real baryon density up to a baryochemical potential-temperature ratio of μ_B/T = 2.5, covering most of the RHIC Beam Energy Scan range. The method has only statistical uncertainties for a fixed lattice setup, unlike other commonly used approaches.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations of the sign quenched ensemble using 2+1 flavors of rooted staggered fermions with a tree-level Symanzik improved gauge action and two steps of stout smearing on 16^3 × 6 lattices. They used the reduced matrix formalism to calculate the fermion determinant exactly, modifying the RHMC algorithm at μ_B=0 by including an extra accept/reject step accounting for the factor |Re det M(μ)|/det M(0). They performed a chemical potential scan at fixed T=140 MeV and a temperature scan at fixed μ_B/T=1.5, measuring the renormalized chiral condensate and light quark number density.

## Key numbers

- Simulations performed up to μ_B/T = 2.5, covering most of the RHIC Beam Energy Scan range in chemical potential
- Lattice size 16^3 × 6 with aspect ratio LT ≈ 2.7
- Temperature scan performed between 130 MeV and 165 MeV at μ_B/T = 1.5
- Chemical potential scan performed at T = 140 MeV at μ_B/T = 1, 1.5, 2, 2.2, 2.5
- The chiral condensate at T=140 MeV crosses its μ_B=0 crossover value at μ_B/T ≈ 2.2
- The Gaussian model for sign problem severity deviates less than 1σ from actual measurements up to μ_B/T = 2, with at most 25% deviation at the upper end of the range
- The asymptotic ratio of sign quenched to phase quenched sign problem severity is π/2 ≈ 1.57, corresponding to a factor of (π/2)^2 ≈ 2.5 less statistics needed for sign quenched simulations
- At μ_B/T = 1.5, the collapse plot uses κ ≈ 0.012 for the chiral condensate and κ ≈ 0.016 for the quark number

## Context

This work extends previous sign reweighting studies on toy lattices (Giordano, 2020) to phenomenologically relevant lattices, and provides the first lattice QCD results at real baryon density without unknown systematic uncertainties from analytic continuation or the overlap problem. The results are compatible with analytic continuation from imaginary chemical potential methods (Borsanyi, 2020; Borsanyi, 2021) but achieve an order of magnitude smaller errors at the upper half of the μ_B range, enabling direct study of the chiral transition region where extrapolation methods stop being predictive. This opens the possibility of a full scan of the chiral transition region in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan range with currently available computing resources, potentially determining whether the crossover transition becomes stronger as expected for a nearby critical endpoint.
