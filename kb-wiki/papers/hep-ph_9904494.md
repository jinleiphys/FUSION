# Flux Tube Zero-Point Motion, Hadronic Charge Radii, and Hybrid Meson Production Cross Sections

> Flux tube zero-point motion produces quark displacements transverse to the flux tube which make significant contributions to hadronic charge radii. In heavy quark systems, these contributions can be related by Bjorken's sum rule to the rates for semileptonic decay to hybrid mesons. This connection can be generalized to other leptoproduction processes, where transverse contributions to elastic form factor slopes are related to the cross sections for the production of the associated hybrid states. I identify the flux tube overlap integral responsible for these effects as the strong QCD analogue of the Sudakov form factor of perturbative QCD.

# Flux Tube Zero-Point Motion, Hadronic Charge Radii, and Hybrid Meson Production Cross Sections

## Key claim

Flux tube zero-point oscillations produce transverse quark displacements that contribute significantly to hadronic charge radii, and by Bjorken's sum rule these contributions are directly related to inclusive hybrid meson production cross sections in heavy quark semileptonic decay. The author notes that the precise numerical values quoted for these corrections should not be taken too seriously, since the gluonic fields in ground state mesons are certainly not pure flux tubes, but argues that the qualitative character of the corrections will not be lost.

## Method

The author adopts the flux tube model of Isgur and Paton (1985), discretizing the flux tube into N cells with transverse degrees of freedom, and constructs full flux tube model state vectors for both ordinary mesons (built on the gluonic ground state potential V0(r)) and hybrid mesons (built on the one-phonon potential V1(r)). The elastic form factor for heavy quark scattering is computed via the Isgur-Wise function, and the inelastic form factors for hybrid production are calculated by expanding the transition amplitude to first order in the velocity transfer, using the overlap between ground state and excited flux tube wavefunctions.

## Key numbers

- The transverse correction to the squared charge radius of a heavy quark is given by the term (2b/π³ m²_Q) ζ(3) ⟨r²⟩, where ζ(3) ≈ 1.20
- Using canonical parameters, the transverse correction is 51% in light quark systems where m_Q = m_d, and 13% in heavy quark systems
- The sum ζ(3) is approximately 80% saturated by its p=1 term, indicating hybrid production is dominantly in the lowest phonon mode
- The fractional rate to the lowest-lying p=1 hybrid mesons is about 40% of the total hybrid production
- The perturbative QCD contribution to ρ² from the velocity-dependent anomalous dimension is Δρ²_pert ≈ 0.13 ± 0.05

## Context

This work extends the flux tube model of Isgur and Paton (1985) and the heavy quark sum rule of Bjorken (1990), as formalized by Isgur and Wise (1991). The author identifies the flux tube overlap integral as the strong QCD analogue of the Sudakov form factor from perturbative QCD. The results support the earlier qualitative argument of Isgur, Kokoski, and Paton (1985) that hybrid meson production cross sections should be comparable to ordinary meson production cross sections, and generalize this connection to electroproduction via the operator product expansion following Cabibbo and Radicati (1966). The work enables quantitative predictions for hybrid meson searches at facilities such as Jefferson Lab and BNL.
