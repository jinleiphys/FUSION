# Pressure isotropization in high energy heavy ion collisions

> The early stages of high energy heavy ion collisions are studied in the Color Glass Condensate framework, with a real-time classical lattice simulation. When increasing the coupling constant, we observe a rapid increase of the ratio of longitudinal to transverse pressure. The transient regime that precedes this behavior is of the order of 1 fm/c.

## Key claim

When the coupling constant is increased from very weak (g=0.1) to still modest (g=0.5), the ratio of longitudinal to transverse pressure in the Color Glass Condensate framework rises dramatically, approaching 0.60 at times of order 1 fm/c; however, the abstract's claim of a "rapid increase" is conditioned on the coupling being large enough to trigger Weibel instabilities, and the simulations are limited to g=0.5 (α_s=2×10^{-2}) because larger couplings cause severe numerical subtraction errors.

## Method

The authors performed real-time classical lattice simulations of SU(2) Yang-Mills theory in comoving (τ,η,x_⊥) coordinates, using the Color Glass Condensate framework with a McLerran-Venugopalan model for the background fields. They added Gaussian-distributed quantum fluctuations (derived from linearized mode functions over the CGC background) to the classical initial conditions at τ_0=0.01 Q_s^{-1}, then solved the classical Yang-Mills equations on a 64×64×128 lattice with N_conf=200 (g=0.1) or N_conf=2000 (g=0.5) configurations. The energy-momentum tensor was computed after subtracting a zero-point contribution (vacuum with no background) and fitting a counterterm A/τ^2 to remove subleading divergences.

## Key numbers

- For g=0.1 (α_s=8×10^{-4}), the ratio P_L/P_T ≈ 0.01 at all times, indicating near free-streaming behavior with energy density decreasing approximately as τ^{-1}.
- For g=0.5 (α_s=2×10^{-2}), the ratio P_L/P_T approaches 0.60 at times of order 1 fm/c (with Q_s=2 GeV used to calibrate the time axis).
- The transient regime before isotropization is of order 1 fm/c for realistic Q_s=2 GeV.
- The shear viscosity extracted from a fit ε = a/τ^{4/3} - 2η_0/τ^2 gives η/ε^{3/4} ≈ 0.3, compared to the LO perturbative value of order 300 for g=0.5.
- The lattice used Q_s a_⊥=1, a_η=1/64, covering two units of rapidity, with initial time Q_s τ_0=0.01.

## Context

This work extends the resummation approach previously applied to scalar field theory (Dusling, Epelbaum, Gelis, Venugopalan, 2010-2012) to the Yang-Mills case directly relevant for heavy ion collisions. It contradicts the expectation that weak coupling necessarily prevents isotropization, showing that even at g=0.5 the system enters a viscous hydrodynamical regime. The results enable future studies of the transition from free-streaming to hydrodynamical behavior as a function of coupling, and provide a framework for computing the shear viscosity from first-principles CGC dynamics. The approach is closely related to that of Berges, Scheffler, and Venugopalan (2009) but differs in using CGC-specific mode functions rather than vacuum mode functions with rescaled occupation numbers.
