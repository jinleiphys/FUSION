# Aspects of a0-f0 mixing in the reaction pn->da0

> Some aspects of a0-f0 mixing effects in the reaction ${\vec p} n\to da^0_0$ with perpendicular polarized proton beam are discussed. An angular--asymmetry parameter $A$ is defined to study those effects. It is shown that, for energies close to the production threshold, the angular--asymmetry parameter $A(θ, ϕ)$ is proportional to the a0-f0 mixing amplitude for arbitrary polar and azimuthal angles $θ$ and $ϕ$ of the outgoing $a_0$ meson. This statement is also valid for arbitrary energies, but then only at polar angles $θ=0^0$ and $θ=90^0$. The mass dependence of the differential cross section $dσ/dm_{π^0η}$ in the reaction $pn\to dπ^0η$ in the presence of \mix mixing is also discussed.

## Key claim

The angular-asymmetry parameter \(A(\theta, \phi)\) in the reaction \(\vec p n \to d a^0_0\) with a perpendicularly polarized proton beam is proportional to the \(a_0\)-\(f_0\) mixing amplitude for all angles near threshold, but at arbitrary energies this proportionality holds only at polar angles \(\theta = 0^\circ\) and \(\theta = 90^\circ\).

## Method

The authors construct the production amplitude for \(pn \to d\) plus a scalar meson using effective transition operators constrained by parity, angular momentum, and isospin conservation, with the \(a_0\) produced in a \(p\) wave and the \(f_0\) in an \(s\) wave near threshold. They incorporate \(a_0\)-\(f_0\) mixing via a non-diagonal self-energy matrix in the propagator, using a Flatté-like form for the scalar meson widths, and derive expressions for the differential cross section and analyzing power in the \(\pi^0\eta\) final state. The mixing parameter \(\xi(m)\) is defined in terms of the off-diagonal self-energy \(\Sigma_{01}(m)\) and the \(f_0\) propagator, and the mass dependence of the \(\pi^0\eta\) invariant mass spectrum is computed for two mixing scenarios: constant \(\Sigma_{01}\) versus \(\Sigma_{01}\) dominated by the \(K\bar K\) loop.

## Key numbers

- The mixing parameter \(\xi(m)\) is defined as \(\xi(m) = \Sigma_{01}(m) \, \tilde G_{f_0}(m^2)\), where \(\Sigma_{01}\) is the off-diagonal self-energy.
- The ratio \(g_{12}^2/(g_{11}g_{22}) < 10^{-2}\) is cited as justification for the approximation that the \(a_0\) and \(f_0\) propagate as pure isospin states.
- The third term in the amplitude for \(pn \to d\pi\eta\) is suppressed by a factor \(\approx 0.12\) relative to the second term, estimated using \(\Sigma_{01} \approx 5000\) MeV\(^2\) at \(m \approx 2m_K\) and \(\pi\eta\) mixing amplitude \(\lambda_{\pi^0\eta} \approx -5000\) MeV\(^2\).
- The masses and widths used are \(m_{a_0} = m_{f_0} = 980\) MeV/\(c^2\) and \(\Gamma_{a_0} = \Gamma_{f_0} = 50\) MeV/\(c^2\).
- The calculations for the \(\pi^0\eta\) mass spectrum are performed at a proton kinetic energy \(T_p = 2645\) MeV/\(c\).
- The near-threshold energy dependence of the cross section scales as \(Q^{3/2}\) for \(a_0\) production and \(Q^{1/2}\) for \(f_0\) production, where \(Q(m) = \sqrt{s} - M_d - m\) is the excess energy.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of \(a_0\)-\(f_0\) mixing, particularly the analysis by Close (2000) which found that \(80 \pm 25\%\) of \(a^0_0\) mesons in \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=29.1\) GeV originate from \(f_0\) decay, and the theoretical framework of Achasov (1979) and Kudryavtsev (2000) on mixing mechanisms. The paper enables experimental extraction of the mixing amplitude from angular asymmetry measurements in \(pn \to d\pi^0\eta\) using polarized beams, and distinguishes between mixing driven by direct \(a_0\)-\(f_0\) transitions versus mixing mediated by the \(K\bar K\) loop. The proposed asymmetry at \(\theta = 90^\circ\) provides a new, clean signature of isospin violation that is independent of higher partial waves.
