# Short time dynamics with initial correlations

> The short-time dynamics of correlated systems is strongly influenced by initial correlations giving rise to an additional collision integral in the non-Markovian kinetic equation. Exact cancellation of the two integrals is found if the initial state is thermal equilibrium which is an important consistency criterion. Analytical results are given for the time evolution of the correlation energy which are confirmed by comparisons with molecular dynamics simulations (MD).

# Short time dynamics with initial correlations

## Key claim
Initial correlations in a correlated system produce an additional collision integral in the non-Markovian kinetic equation that exactly cancels the standard Levinson collision integral when the initial state is thermal equilibrium, thereby resolving a consistency problem in the Levinson equation. The cancellation holds within the Born approximation when the initial interaction potential equals the dynamical interaction potential and the initial distribution is equilibrium.

## Method
The authors start from the Martin-Schwinger hierarchy for causal Green's functions and use the polarization approximation for the three-particle Green's function to obtain a closed integral equation for the density-density correlation function. They derive a Dyson equation containing two new terms from initial correlations: a self-energy correction Σ₀ and a source term S_init, then apply the generalized Kadanoff-Baym ansatz to obtain a kinetic equation with three collision integrals: the standard Levinson precursor I, an initial-correlation term I₀ from S_init, and a higher-order term I₁ from Σ₀. Analytical expressions for the time-dependent correlation energy are obtained in the classical high-temperature plasma limit using a Debye potential and compared with molecular dynamics simulations.

## Key numbers
- Plasma parameter Γ = e²/(a_e T), where a_e = (3/(4π n))^(1/3) is the Wigner-Seitz radius
- Dimensionless time τ = t ω_p/√2, where ω_p is the plasma frequency
- The time-dependent correlation energy for a Debye potential is given by E_coll(t)/(nT) = -(√3 Γ^(3/2))/(4x) ∂_y (y F(y))|_{y=xτ}, where F(y) = 1 - e^(y²) erfc(y) and x = κ_D/κ with κ² = 4π e² n/T = ω_p² T/m
- The initial correlation energy contribution is E_init(t)/(nT) = -(√3 Γ^(3/2))/(2(x₀² - x²)) [x F(xτ) - x₀ F(x₀τ)], where x₀ = κ₀/κ characterizes the initial Debye correlations
- The total energy including initial correlations is E_total/(nT) = √3 Γ^(3/2)/(2(x + x₀))

## Context
This work extends the non-Markovian Levinson equation (Levinson 1965, 1969) by including initial correlations that were previously omitted, addressing a deficiency noted by Lee et al. (1970) that the Levinson equation gives spurious time evolution when starting from equilibrium. The results are consistent with the treatment of initial correlations by Kremp et al. (1999) and provide analytical benchmarks for molecular dynamics simulations such as those by Zwicknagel (1999). The derived kinetic equation enables quantitative study of short-time correlation dynamics in plasmas and other correlated systems, including the decay of over- or under-correlated initial states toward equilibrium on time scales of the inverse plasma frequency.
