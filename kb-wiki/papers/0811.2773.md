# On the $n\bar{n}$ transitions in the medium

> The corrections to the model of $n\bar{n}$ transitions in the medium and additional baryon-number-violating processes are considered. We focus on the time-dependence since it is different for the $n\bar{n}$ transition followed by annihilation (basic process) and processes mentioned above.

# On the $n\bar{n}$ transitions in the medium

## Key claim
The standard one-particle potential model for $n\bar{n}$ transitions in matter is incomplete; the field-theoretical approach reveals that the basic $n\bar{n}$ transition followed by annihilation (Fig. 1a) has a fundamentally different time-dependence (quadratic in time) compared to correction processes (exponential decay), and the corrections are negligible for sufficiently large observation times.

## Method
The author calculates the amplitudes and widths for the basic $n\bar{n}$ transition followed by annihilation in the medium (Fig. 1a) and for several correction diagrams (Figs. 2a-2d) using a field-theoretical approach with a finite time interval (FTA) to handle the infrared divergence arising from zero momentum transfer in the $n\bar{n}$ transition vertex. The correction processes include meson radiation before the $n\bar{n}$ transition (Fig. 2a), the baryon-number-violating decay $n\rightarrow \bar{n}\Phi$ (Fig. 2b), the conversion $n\rightarrow \bar{\Lambda}$ (Fig. 2c), and a residual $\bar{n}$-medium interaction (Fig. 2d). The time-dependence of each process is derived from the structure of its amplitude, and the relative contributions are compared.

## Key numbers
- Free-space $n\bar{n}$ oscillation time limit: $\tau_{n\bar{n}} > 10^{16}$ yr
- Annihilation width of $\bar{n}$ in the medium: $\Gamma \sim 100$ MeV for $n\bar{n}$ transitions in nuclei
- Observation time in proton-decay type experiments: $t = T_0 = 1.3$ yr
- Ratio of diagram 2a probability to diagram 1a probability: $W_{2a}(t)/W_1(t) = \Gamma / (m_\pi^2 t)$, where $m_\Phi = m_\pi$
- Condition for diagram 2a contribution to be negligible: $m_\pi^2 t / \Gamma \gg 1$ and $\Gamma t \gg 1$
- Annihilation cross section exceeds scattering cross section at low energies: $\sigma_a > 2.5 \sigma_s$
- Medium correction to the vertex: $\tau_{n\bar{n}} > (1 + \Delta\epsilon) \times 10^{16}$ yr, with $\Delta\epsilon$ less than 0.25 based on nuclear $\beta$ decay renormalization effects

## Context
This work extends the field-theoretical approach to $n\bar{n}$ oscillations in absorbing matter proposed by Nazaruk (2008) [Ref. 10], correcting the earlier one-particle potential model used in Refs. [4-7] which does not describe the total transition probability or the absorption channel. The paper shows that the basic $n\bar{n}$ transition followed by annihilation (Fig. 1a) has a quadratic time-dependence $W_1 \sim t^2$, unlike the exponential decay of correction processes, making the corrections negligible for long observation times. This result enables more reliable interpretation of $n\bar{n}$ oscillation searches in underground experiments, and the author notes that similar considerations apply to $K^0\bar{K}^0$ oscillations in matter.
