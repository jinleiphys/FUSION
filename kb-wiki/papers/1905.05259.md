# The Photon and Light $1^{--}$ Mesons

> In this paper we look into the world data related to the photon and the connection to light mesons with the same spin parity quantum number as the photon $J^p = 1^-$.

## Key claim

The paper performs a global fit to world data on the photon and light \(1^{--}\) mesons, finding that the Soding Model fit to STAR ultra-peripheral photoproduction data has a \(\chi^2\) of 1926 for 1669 degrees of freedom (a 4.8\(\sigma\) deviation), and that adding re-scattering terms beyond the Soding Model improves the fit by \(\Delta\chi^2 = 257\) (also a 4.8\(\sigma\) improvement), though the higher-mass region improvement of \(\Delta\chi^2 = 20\) is not statistically significant.

## Method

The author performs a global K-matrix fit to STAR Au+Au ultra-peripheral coherent photoproduction data at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV producing \(\pi^+\pi^-\) pairs, combined with \(e^+e^- \to \pi^+\pi^-\) and \(e^+e^- \to \pi^+\pi^-\pi^0\) scattering data, plus p-wave partial wave analysis of \(\pi^+\pi^- \to \pi^+\pi^-\). A 4-channel K-matrix (channels: \(\pi^+\pi^-\), \(\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0\), \(e^+e^-\), \(\mu^+\mu^-\)) is used for the low-mass region (threshold to 1.1 GeV) and a 5-channel K-matrix (adding \(\eta\pi^+\pi^-\)) for the high-mass region (1.1 GeV to 1.9 GeV), within a generalized Soding Model framework that is later extended with re-scattering bubble terms.

## Key numbers

* STAR Au+Au ultra-peripheral coherent photoproduction data taken at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV (the highest RHIC energy)
* Global Soding Model fit: \(\chi^2 = 1926\) for 1669 degrees of freedom, corresponding to a 4.8\(\sigma\) deviation
* Beyond-Soding fit with re-scattering in low-mass region: \(\chi^2\) improvement of 257 (4.8\(\sigma\))
* Beyond-Soding fit in high-mass region: \(\chi^2\) improvement of 20 (not statistically significant)
* Final beyond-Soding global fit: \(\chi^2 = 1649\) for 1649 degrees of freedom
* Five S-matrix poles found: \(\rho(760)\) with mass 0.764 GeV and width 0.148 GeV; \(\omega(783)\) with mass 0.783 GeV and width 0.008 GeV; \(\rho(1330)\) with mass 1.333 GeV and width 0.334 GeV; \(\rho(1630)\) with mass 1.629 GeV and width 0.221 GeV; \(\rho(1730)\) with mass 1.733 GeV and width 0.246 GeV
* Branching fractions for \(\rho(760)\): \(\pi\pi\) 100%, \(e^+e^-\) 0.0047%; for \(\omega(783)\): \(\pi\pi\) 2%, \(\pi\pi\pi\) 89%, \(e^+e^-\) 0.0074%, other (radiative \(\gamma\pi\)) 9%
* The parameter \(\alpha\) for the real part of the pion loop re-scattering is determined to be 2.0

## Context

This work extends the Soding Model (Soding, 1966) approach to photoproduction by incorporating a multi-channel K-matrix formalism and re-scattering bubble terms, using world data from STAR, \(e^+e^-\) experiments (eepipi, eepipipi), and partial wave analysis (pipipipi). The identification of five poles in the \(1^{--}\) S-matrix, including the \(\rho(760)\), \(\omega(783)\), and three higher-mass states (\(\rho(1330)\), \(\rho(1630)\), \(\rho(1730)\)), enables a consistent description of the photon coupling to light mesons and provides a framework for future studies of meson spectroscopy and photoproduction mechanisms.
