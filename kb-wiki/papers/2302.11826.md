# Regeneration of bottomonia in an open quantum systems approach

> We demonstrate the importance of quantum jumps in the nonequilibrium evolution of bottomonium states in the quark-gluon plasma. Based on nonrelativistic effective field theory and the open quantum system framework, we evolve the density matrix of color singlet and octet pairs. We show that quantum regeneration of singlet states from octet configurations is necessary to understand experimental results for the suppression of both bottomonium ground and excited states. The values of the heavy-quarkonium transport coefficients used are consistent with recent lattice QCD determinations.

## Key claim
Quantum regeneration (quantum jumps) of color-singlet bottomonium states from color-octet configurations is necessary to simultaneously describe the experimentally measured suppression of both ground and excited bottomonium states in the quark-gluon plasma; without including quantum jumps, the model under-predicts the nuclear modification factor for excited states.

## Method
The authors used potential nonrelativistic QCD (pNRQCD) combined with the open quantum system formalism to derive a Lindblad equation for the evolution of the heavy quarkonium density matrix, including color singlet and octet states. They solved this equation numerically using the quantum trajectories algorithm (QTraj code) with (3+1)-D anisotropic hydrodynamics backgrounds, evolving the system from an initial time of 0.6 fm until the temperature dropped below 190 MeV. The heavy-quarkonium momentum diffusion coefficient $\hat{\kappa}$ and its dispersive counterpart $\hat{\gamma}$ were taken from recent unquenched lattice QCD determinations, and the authors performed a parameter scan over $\hat\kappa \in \{2,3,4,5\}$ and $\hat\gamma \in \{-3.5,-2.6,0,1\}$.

## Key numbers
- Best description of experimental data obtained with $\hat\kappa = 3.9$ and $\hat\gamma = 0$ (interpolated minimum of combined distance measure $\Delta^2_\text{min} = 0.065$ when including quantum jumps).
- Fully integrated $R_{AA}$ values with quantum jumps at $\hat\kappa=4$, $\hat\gamma=0$: $\Upsilon(1S)$ = $0.3598 \pm 0.0028$, $\Upsilon(2S)$ = $0.1118 \pm 0.0019$, $\Upsilon(3S)$ = $0.0775 \pm 0.0007$.
- Without quantum jumps, the minimal combined distance measure was $\Delta^2_\text{min} = 0.22$ at $\hat\kappa=3.3$ and $\hat\gamma=1$, with no overlap between best description regions for 1S and 2S states.
- The evolution was terminated at a final temperature $T_F = 190$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' prior studies (Brambilla et al., 2020, 2021, 2022) that used the same pNRQCD and open quantum system framework but neglected quantum jumps, finding reasonable agreement only for the ground state. The inclusion of quantum regeneration confirms in a fully quantum QCD setting earlier semiclassical studies of singlet-octet transitions by Blaizot et al. (2018) and Yao et al. (2018, 2020). The results favor larger values of $\hat\kappa$ and smaller values of $|\hat\gamma|$ than earlier determinations, consistent with recent lattice QCD measurements by Larsen et al. (2019) and Bala et al. (2021). This enables future studies of temperature dependence in transport coefficients, fluctuating hydrodynamic backgrounds, and further improvements to the pNRQCD treatment.
