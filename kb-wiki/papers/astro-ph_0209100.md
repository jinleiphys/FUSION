# Bose-Einstein condensation in dense nuclear matter and strong magnetic fields

> Bose-Einstein condensation of antikaons in cold and dense beta-equilibrated matter under the influence of strong magnetic fields is studied within a relativistic mean field model. For magnetic fields $> 5 \times 10^{18}$G, the phase spaces of charged particles are modified resulting in compositional changes in the system. The threshold density of $K^-$ condensation is shifted to higher density compared with the field free case. In the presence of strong fields, the equation of state becomes stiffer than that of the zero field case.

# Bose-Einstein condensation in dense nuclear matter and strong magnetic fields

## Key claim

The threshold density for antikaon Bose-Einstein condensation in cold, beta-equilibrated neutron star matter is shifted to higher density when the magnetic field exceeds approximately 5 × 10¹⁸ G, and the equation of state becomes stiffer than in the field-free case, but only if the magnetic field is strong enough that the effects of baryon anomalous magnetic moments overwhelm the softening effects of Landau quantization.

## Method

The authors employed a relativistic mean field (RMF) model with the TM1 parameter set, including the interaction of baryon anomalous magnetic moments with the magnetic field and the electromagnetic field contribution to energy density and pressure. They solved the coupled field equations for neutrons, protons, electrons, muons, and K⁻ mesons under chemical equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions, with the K⁻ condensation condition given by the equality of the K⁻ chemical potential with the electron chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The critical field for electrons is B_c^e = 4.414 × 10¹³ G.
- Threshold density of K⁻ condensation for B_m = 0: 2.67 n₀ (where n₀ = 0.145 fm⁻³ is normal nuclear matter density).
- Threshold density of K⁻ condensation for B_m = 1.5 × 10⁵ B_c^e (≈ 6.6 × 10¹⁸ G): 3.85 n₀.
- Maximum masses of neutron stars without and with antikaon condensation (U_K⁻ = -160 MeV) in the TM1 model: 2.179 M_⊙ and 1.857 M_⊙, respectively; corresponding central densities: 5.97 n₀ and 6.37 n₀.
- Magnetic energy density and pressure: ε_f = P_f = B_m²/(8π) = 4.814 × 10⁻⁸ (B_m/B_c^e)² MeV fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of antikaon condensation in neutron stars by Kaplan and Nelson (1986) and subsequent authors (Pal, Gle99, Pra97) by incorporating strong magnetic fields, building on the treatment of magnetic field effects in dense matter by Broderick, Prakash, and Lattimer (2000). It also relates to the magnetar hypothesis of Duncan and Thompson (1992) and the possibility of interior neutron star fields up to ~10¹⁸ G from the scalar virial theorem (Chandrasekhar, 1953) and general relativistic calculations (Bocquet, 1995; Prasanna, 2000). The results enable future studies of neutrino emissivity and mass-radius relationships for compact stars with antikaon condensates in strong magnetic fields.
