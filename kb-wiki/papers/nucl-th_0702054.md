# Signature of Shallow Potentials in Deep Sub-barrier Fusion Reactions

> We extend a recent study that explained the steep falloff in the fusion cross section at energies far below the Coulomb barrier for the symmetric dinuclear system 64Ni+64Ni to another symmetric system, 58Ni+58Ni, and the asymmetric system 64Ni+100Mo. In this scheme the very sensitive dependence of the internal part of the nuclear potential on the nuclear equation of state determines a reduction of the classically allowed region for overlapping configurations and consequently a decrease in the fusion cross sections at bombarding energies far below the barrier. Within the coupled-channels method, including couplings to the low-lying 2+ and 3- states in both target and projectile as well as mutual and two-phonon excitations of these states, we calculate and compare with the experimental fusion cross sections, S-factors, and logarithmic derivatives for the above mentioned systems and find good agreement with the data even at the lowest energies. We predict, in particular, a distinct double peaking in the S-factor for the far subbarrier fusion of 58Ni+58Ni which should be tested experimentally.

## Key claim
The steep falloff of fusion cross sections at deep sub-barrier energies can be explained by standard coupled-channels calculations if the nuclear potential includes a shallow pocket produced by a repulsive core calibrated to the nuclear equation of state, rather than by invoking new physics beyond the coupled-channels method.

## Method
The authors constructed double-folding potentials using the M3Y effective nucleon-nucleon interaction supplemented with a short-range repulsive core, whose strength was calibrated to the nuclear incompressibility from the Thomas-Fermi model. They performed coupled-channels calculations in the rotating-frame approximation, including couplings to low-lying 2+ and 3- states in both target and projectile as well as mutual and two-phonon excitations, for the systems 58Ni+58Ni, 64Ni+64Ni, and 64Ni+100Mo.

## Key numbers
- For 58Ni+58Ni: the repulsive core parameters are a_rep = 0.341 fm and V_rep = 510.1 MeV·fm^3, with nuclear incompressibility K = 234 MeV and pocket depth V_pocket = 89.31 MeV.
- For 64Ni+64Ni: the repulsive core parameters are a_rep = 0.405 fm and V_rep = 511.0 MeV·fm^3, with K = 228 MeV and pocket depth V_pocket = 84.98 MeV.
- For 64Ni+100Mo: the repulsive core parameters are a_rep = 0.375 fm and V_rep = 488.7 MeV·fm^3, with K = 226 MeV and pocket depth V_pocket = 117.8 MeV.
- The experimental fusion cross sections for 64Ni+64Ni were measured down to 10 nb, and for 64Ni+100Mo down to 10 nb.
- The empirical scaling law for the energy E_s where the S-factor develops a maximum is E_s = 0.355 [Z1 Z2 sqrt(A1 A2/(A1+A2))]^(2/3).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Misicu and Esbensen, 2006) that explained the hindrance in 64Ni+64Ni to the symmetric system 58Ni+58Ni and the asymmetric system 64Ni+100Mo. It contradicts the suggestion by some authors that the standard coupled-channels method is unable to explain the steep falloff, and it addresses the failure of Woods-Saxon potentials with standard diffuseness (a = 0.65 fm) noted by Hagino et al. (2003) and the need for larger diffuseness values up to a = 1.3 fm. The approach enables predictions such as a distinct double peaking in the S-factor for 58Ni+58Ni at far sub-barrier energies that should be tested experimentally.
