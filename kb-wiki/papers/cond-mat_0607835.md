# Fermi gas near unitarity around four and two spatial dimensions

> We construct systematic expansions around four and two spatial dimensions for a Fermi gas near the unitarity limit. Near four spatial dimensions such a Fermi gas can be understood as a weakly interacting system of fermionic and bosonic degrees of freedom. To the leading and next-to-leading orders in the expansion over epsilon=4-d, with d being the dimensionality of space, we calculate the thermodynamic quantities and the fermion quasiparticle spectrum, both at unitarity and around the unitarity point. Then the phase structure of the polarized Fermi gas in the unitary regime is studied in the epsilon expansion. At unitarity the unpolarized superfluid state and a fully polarized normal state are separated by a first order phase transition. However, on the BEC side of the unitarity point, in a certain range of the two-body binding energy, these two phases are separated in the phase diagram by more exotic phases, including gapless superfluid phases with one or two Fermi surfaces and a superfluid phase with a spatially varying condensate. We also show that the unitary Fermi gas near two spatial dimensions is weakly interacting, and calculate the thermodynamic quantities and the fermion quasiparticle spectrum in the expansion over \barε=d-2.

## Key claim

The unitary Fermi gas can be systematically studied via expansions in the spatial dimensionality \(d\) around four and two dimensions, where the system becomes weakly interacting; the expansion parameter \(\epsilon = 4-d\) (or \(\bar\epsilon = d-2\)) is used to compute thermodynamic quantities and phase structure, with results extrapolated to the physical case \(d=3\) where the expansion parameter is unity.

## Method

The authors start from a Lagrangian of spin-1/2 fermions with a contact interaction and perform a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce a bosonic field representing fermion pairs. They develop Feynman rules and a power-counting scheme in \(\epsilon = 4-d\) (and separately in \(\bar\epsilon = d-2\)), identifying which diagrams contribute at each order. The effective potential is computed to leading and next-to-leading order in \(\epsilon\), and the gap equation is solved to determine the condensate \(\phi_0\) and the chemical potential \(\mu\). For the polarized case, the phase diagram is mapped by comparing free energies of normal, superfluid, and exotic gapless superfluid phases.

## Key numbers

* The effective coupling near four dimensions is \(g^2 = 8\pi^2\epsilon / m^2\), where \(\epsilon = 4-d\).
* The effective coupling near two dimensions is \(\bar g^2 = 2\pi\bar\epsilon / m\), where \(\bar\epsilon = d-2\).
* The condensate \(\phi_0\) is related to the chemical potential and binding energy as \(\phi_0 = (2\mu + \epsilon_b)/\epsilon\) to leading order in \(\epsilon\).
* The ratio of the energy density of the unitary Fermi gas to that of the free Fermi gas, \(\xi = E/E_{\text{free}}\), approaches 1 as \(d \to 2\) and approaches 0 as \(d \to 4\).
* At unitarity, the unpolarized superfluid and fully polarized normal state are separated by a first-order phase transition.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier epsilon-expansion approach (Nishida and Son, 2006) for the unitary Fermi gas near four dimensions, which was inspired by the observation of Nussinov and Nussinov (2004) that the unitary limit becomes noninteracting at \(d=4\) and \(d=2\). The method provides a controlled perturbative framework complementary to Monte Carlo simulations (Carlson 2003, Astrakharchik 2004) and enables systematic study of polarized Fermi gases, predicting exotic gapless superfluid phases on the BEC side of unitarity. The results can be extrapolated to three dimensions to guide experiments on ultracold atomic gases and to inform theories of dilute nuclear matter and neutron stars.
