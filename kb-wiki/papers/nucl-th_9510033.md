# About the decay of the $Λ$ in a nucleus

> A few aspects of the $Λ$ mesonic and non-mesonic decay in nuclei are shortly addressed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the mesonic decay of the Lambda hyperon in a nucleus is strongly Pauli blocked for all but the lightest nuclei, while the non-mesonic decay (one-nucleon and two-nucleon induced) becomes dominant; however, the theoretical calculations for the non-mesonic width and the neutron-to-proton ratio still show significant discrepancies with experimental values, possibly indicating a violation of the Delta I=1/2 rule.

## Method

The authors review calculations of the Lambda self-energy in nuclear matter using the polarization propagator formalism, where the mesonic decay width is obtained from the free pion propagator modified by the medium, and the non-mesonic decay width is computed from the coupling of the virtual pion to particle-hole (1p-1h) and two-particle-two-hole (2p-2h) excitations. For the one-nucleon induced decay, they compare results from a meson-exchange model (Ramos and Bennhold, 1995) with an RPA approach that includes both nucleon and Delta excitations and a Landau-Migdal short-range correlation parameter g'_Lambda. For the two-nucleon induced decay, they use a phenomenological parametrization of the 2p-2h polarization propagator calibrated to electron scattering and pionic atom data.

## Key numbers

- Free Lambda lifetime: tau_Lambda = 2.63 x 10^{-10} s
- Mass difference m_Lambda - (m_N + m_pi) is approximately 40 MeV
- For Lambda decay at rest, the emitted nucleon has kinetic energy approximately 5 MeV and momentum approximately 100 MeV/c
- Fermi momentum range for nuclei: 220 to 270 MeV/c; Pauli blocking sets in for A approximately 15 or less
- For one-nucleon induced decay, each outgoing nucleon has energy approximately 89 MeV and momentum approximately 400 MeV/c, well above the Fermi momentum
- In the calculation by Ramos and Bennhold (1995) for 12_Lambda C: Gamma_nm/Gamma_free = 0.87 (theory) versus Gamma_exp/Gamma_free = 1.14 +/- 0.20 (experiment); the ratio Gamma_n/Gamma_p = 0.19 (pion only) or 0.3 (full meson set) versus experimental 1.33^{+1.12}_{-0.81}
- Free decay coupling constants: s_pi = 2.34 x 10^{-7}, p_pi = 1.17 x 10^{-7}
- For the two-nucleon induced decay in nuclear matter: Im C^0 approximately 0.18 m_pi^{-6}, Re C^0 approximately 0.40 m_pi^{-6}
- In the refined calculation by Ramos, Oset and Salcedo (1995): C*_0 rho^2 = (0.105 + i 0.096) m_pi^{-6}
- For 12_Lambda C from Ramos, Oset and Salcedo (1995): Gamma_pi = 0.31, Gamma_{1p1h} = 1.45, Gamma_{2p2h} = 0.27 (units not specified but presumably in the same arbitrary units as the free width)
- For 208_Lambda Pb: Gamma_pi not given, Gamma_{1p1h} = 1.93, Gamma_{2p2h} = 0.30

## Context

This paper reviews and compares two theoretical approaches to Lambda decay in nuclei: the meson-exchange model of Ramos and Bennhold (1995) and the RPA-based nuclear matter approach of Alberico et al. (1991). It extends earlier work by Oset and Nieves (1995) on mesonic decay and by Block and Dalitz (1963) who first conjectured the non-mesonic channel. The results enable future studies of the Delta I=1/2 rule violation in hypernuclear decay, particularly through the ratio Gamma_n/Gamma_p, and motivate more refined calculations that include both the Delta resonance and finite-nucleus effects.
