# Exclusive muon-pair productions in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions: Realistic nucleus charge form factor and differential distributions

> The cross sections for exclusive muon pair production in nucleus - nucleus collisions are calculated and several differential distributions are shown. Realistic (Fourier transform of charge density) charge form factors of nuclei are used and the corresponding results are compared with the cross sections calculated with monopole form factor often used in the literature and discussed recently in the context of higher-order QED corrections. Absorption effects are discussed and quantified. The cross sections obtained with realistic form factors are significantly smaller than those obtained with the monopole form factor. The effect is bigger for large muon rapidities and/or large muon transverse momenta. The predictions for the STAR and PHENIX collaboration measurements at RHIC as well as the ALICE and CMS collaborations at LHC are presented.

## Key claim

The cross sections for exclusive muon-pair production obtained with realistic (Fourier transform of charge density) nuclear charge form factors are significantly smaller than those obtained with the monopole form factor often used in the literature, with the effect being larger for large muon rapidities and/or large muon transverse momenta.

## Method

The authors calculated exclusive muon-pair production in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions using both the equivalent photon approximation (EPA) in impact parameter space and a full momentum-space QED calculation. They compared results using a realistic charge form factor (Fourier transform of the two-parameter Fermi model charge density) against the commonly used monopole form factor, and included absorption effects via a step-function cutoff at impact parameter b = 2R_A.

## Key numbers

- For gold nuclei (197Au): root mean square charge radius <r²>^(1/2) = 5.3 fm, corresponding to monopole parameter Λ = 0.091 GeV
- For lead nuclei (208Pb): root mean square charge radius <r²>^(1/2) = 5.5016 fm, corresponding to monopole parameter Λ = 0.088 GeV
- At W_γγ = 10 GeV, the cross section ratio (realistic/monopole) is approximately 0.2 (factor of about 5 difference)
- Central charge density for Au: ρ_Au(0) = 0.1694/A fm^(-3); for Pb: ρ_Pb(0) = 0.1604/A fm^(-3)
- The nuclear radius cutoff for peripheral collisions: b > R_1 + R_2 ≈ 14 fm

## Context

This work extends the study of realistic charge form factors previously applied to exclusive ρ⁰ meson pair production (Klusek-Gawenda and Szczurek, 2009) to the case of muon-pair production. It contradicts the common practice of using the simple monopole form factor (Heisenberg, 1994) for differential distributions, showing that while the monopole approximation may suffice for total cross sections, it fails for distributions at large rapidities or transverse momenta. The results enable more accurate predictions for the STAR and PHENIX detectors at RHIC and the ALICE and CMS detectors at LHC, where experimental cuts on lepton transverse momenta and rapidities are required.
