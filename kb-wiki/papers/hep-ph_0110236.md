# The Spin of the Nucleon in Effective Models

> The three flavor soliton approach for baryons is utilized to discuss effects of flavor symmetry breaking in the baryon wave--functions on axial current matrix elements. The flavor content of the singlet axial current matrix elements, that parameterizes the quark spin contribution to the total angular momentum, is disentangled and studied as a function of the effective flavor symmetry breaking. Here the nucleon and the Lambda--hyperon are considered.

## Key claim

The three-flavor soliton approach, with flavor symmetry breaking treated as an effective parameter, reproduces hyperon beta-decay data equally well as the established flavor-symmetric F&D fit, despite strong deviations from flavor-covariant wave-functions (e.g., a 23% contamination of the antidecuplet representation in the nucleon at the chosen symmetry-breaking strength).

## Method

The Skyrme model is extended to three flavors by embedding the hedgehog soliton in the isospin subgroup of SU(3) and adding the Wess-Zumino-Witten term plus a flavor-symmetry-breaking Lagrangian that accounts for different masses and decay constants of pseudoscalar mesons. Collective coordinates are quantized, yielding a Hamiltonian whose eigenstates mix different SU(3) representations; the effective symmetry-breaking strength omega^2 is determined from baryon mass differences. Axial current matrix elements are computed using operators with coefficients fixed by the empirical nucleon axial charge and the Lambda-to-proton decay ratio, and the strange quark contribution to the nucleon spin is studied as a function of omega^2.

## Key numbers

- The moment of inertia parameter e is fixed at approximately 4.0 by matching the Delta-nucleon mass difference of about 300 MeV.
- At omega^2 = 6.0, the nucleon amplitude contains a 23% contamination from the antidecuplet representation.
- For the nucleon with DeltaSigma = 0.2, the strange quark polarization DeltaS_N is -0.131 at zero symmetry breaking and decreases in magnitude to -0.085 at omega^2 = 6.0.
- In the realistic vector meson model, the singlet matrix element DeltaSigma for the Lambda hyperon ranges from 0.233 to 0.256 across three parameter sets, while for the nucleon it ranges from 0.256 to 0.291.
- The empirical g_A/g_V ratio for n to p decay is 1.267 +/- 0.004; for Lambda to p decay it is 0.718 +/- 0.015.

## Context

This work extends the chiral soliton approach of Skyrme (1961) and Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (1983) to three flavors with explicit flavor symmetry breaking, following the formalism of Yabu and Ando (1988) and earlier work by Weigel (1996). It addresses the proton spin puzzle, i.e., the smallness of the observed axial singlet current matrix element, and shows that flavor symmetry breaking in the nucleon wave-function significantly reduces the polarization of strange quarks inside the nucleon. The results enable further studies of baryon structure using more realistic vector meson models, as developed by Jain et al. (1988) and Park and Weigel (1992).
