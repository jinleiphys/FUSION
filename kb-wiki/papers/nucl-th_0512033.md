# Pairing Correlations in Odd-Mass Carbon Isotopes and Effect of Pauli Principle in Particle-Core Coupling in 13C and 11Be

> We present an exploratory study of structure of 13C, 15C, 17C and 19C, showing that the simple one-quasiparticle projected BCS (PBCS) model is capable to account for several important properties of these nuclei. Next we discuss the importance of the Pauli Principle in the particle-core models of normal-parity states in 13C and 11Be. This is done by considering the pairing interaction between nucleons moving in an over-all deformed potential. To assess the importance of pairing correlations in these light nuclei we use both the simple BCS and the PBCS approximations. We show that the Pauli Principle plays a crucial role in the parity inversion in 11Be. It is also found that the effect of the particle number conservation in relatively light and/or exotic nuclei is quite significant. Comparison of our results with several recent papers on the same subject, as well as with some experimental data, is presented.

## Key claim

The simple one-quasiparticle projected BCS (PBCS) model can account for several important properties of odd-mass carbon isotopes 13C, 15C, 17C, and 19C, but the model is limited to seniority-one states and cannot describe states with seniority-three configurations, such as the 3/2+ ground state in 17C.

## Method

The authors performed BCS and projected BCS (PBCS) calculations for odd-mass carbon isotopes using a delta-force residual pairing interaction with a configuration space including neutron orbitals from 1s1/2 through 1f5/2. Single-particle energies and the singlet pairing strength were fixed by fitting to experimental quasiparticle and quasihole energies in 13C and 11C, then the same parameters were used to compute spectra and spectroscopic factors for 15C, 17C, and 19C by changing only the neutron number. For 13C and 11Be, the authors extended the particle-rotor model (PRM) and particle-vibrator model (PVM) to quasiparticle versions (QPRM and QPVM) and their projected versions (PQPRM and PQPVM), incorporating pairing correlations and Pauli principle effects through overlap factors Fjj'.

## Key numbers

- The pairing strength used for the carbon isotope chain was v_s^{pair} = 23.46 (BCS) and 24.22 (PBCS), dimensionless
- For 19C, the calculated ground state energy was -0.33 MeV (BCS) and 0.12 MeV (PBCS), compared to experimental separation energies ranging from 160 ± 95 keV to 580 ± 90 keV
- The quadrupole deformation of the 12C core was β = -0.6, corresponding to a measured quadrupole moment Q0 = -(22 ± 10) e·fm²
- For 13C and 11Be, the pairing strength used in the particle-core models was v_s^{pair} = 30
- The 2+ state energy in 12C is 4.438 MeV; in 10Be it is 3.368 MeV
- The spin-orbit splitting between p-subshells is Δ_ls(p) = 5.73 MeV
- The energy difference between 1f5/2 and 2p3/2 states for the harmonic oscillator potential is Δ_HO = 3.82 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier BCS and PBCS studies of 13C by Krmpotic et al. (2002, 2005) to heavier carbon isotopes 15C, 17C, and 19C, and addresses the long-standing problem of Pauli principle violation in particle-core coupling models for normal-parity states in 13C and 11Be. The paper builds on prior particle-rotor model calculations by Esbensen et al. (1995), Nunes et al. (1995, 1996), and Ridikas et al. (1998), and particle-vibrator model work by Vinh Mau (1995), showing that incorporating pairing correlations through quasiparticle methods resolves the parity inversion in 11Be and correctly reproduces the level ordering in 13C. The results enable future systematic large-scale shell-model calculations of all four odd-mass carbon isotopes using the same Hamiltonian and single-particle space.
