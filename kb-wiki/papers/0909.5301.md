# Subbarrier fusion reactions with dissipative couplings

> Using the random matrix model, we discuss the effect of couplings to non-collective states on the penetrability of a one dimensional potential barrier. We show that these non-collective excitations hinder the penetrability and thus smear the barrier distribution at energies above the barrier, while they do not affect significantly the penetrability at deep subbarrier energies. The energy dependence of the Q-value distribution obtained with this model is also discussed.

# Subbarrier fusion reactions with dissipative couplings

## Key claim
Non-collective (single-particle) excitations hinder the penetrability and smear the barrier distribution at energies above the barrier, but they do not significantly affect the penetrability at deep subbarrier energies. The steep fall-off of deep subbarrier fusion cross sections observed in experiments does not appear to be accounted for by this mechanism.

## Method
The authors applied a random matrix model based on the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) to a one-dimensional Gaussian potential barrier of height 100 MeV, coupling a collective level at 1 MeV to 200 discretized single-particle channels spanning 3 to 13 MeV with 0.05 MeV spacing. They solved the coupled-channels equations quantum mechanically for 20 random matrix realizations per energy and averaged the results to obtain penetrabilities, barrier distributions, logarithmic slopes, and Q-value distributions.

## Key numbers
- The Gaussian potential barrier height is 100 MeV.
- The single-particle spectrum was discretized from 3 MeV to 13 MeV with an energy spacing of 0.05 MeV, yielding 200 single-particle channels.
- The collective level is placed at 1 MeV.
- 20 random matrices were generated and coupled-channels calculations performed 20 times for each energy for ensemble averaging.
- The Q-value distribution was smeared with a Lorenzian function of width 0.2 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the conventional coupled-channels approach (DHRS98, BT98) by including couplings to non-collective states, which are usually neglected because they are not strongly coupled to the ground state. The model addresses recent experimental puzzles including the surface diffuseness anomaly (N04), steep fall-off at deep subbarrier energies (J02, D07, S08), smearing of quasi-elastic barrier distributions (T95, P05), and energy dependence of Q-value spectra (E08, L09). The random matrix model was originally developed by Weidenmüller and collaborators (AKW77) for deep inelastic collisions; this work applies it quantum mechanically to subbarrier fusion. The results suggest that quasi-elastic scattering is more sensitive to single-particle excitations than fusion, and that future work should apply the model to realistic systems and to quantum mechanical descriptions of deep inelastic collisions.
