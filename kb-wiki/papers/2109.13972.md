# Gaussian process error modeling for chiral effective-field-theory calculations of $np\leftrightarrow dγ$ at low energies

> We calculate the energy-dependent cross section of the $np\leftrightarrow dγ$ process in chiral effective field theory and apply state-of-the-art tools for quantification of theory uncertainty. We focus on the low-energy regime, where the magnetic dipole and the electric dipole transitions cross over, including the range relevant for big-bang nucleosynthesis. Working with the leading one- and two-body electromagnetic currents, we study the order-by-order convergence of this observable in the chiral expansion of the nuclear potential. We find that the Gaussian process error model describes the observed convergence very well, allowing us to present Bayesian credible intervals for the truncation error with correlations between the cross sections at different energies taken into account. We obtain a 1$σ$ estimate of about 0.2\% for the uncertainty from the truncation of the nuclear potential. This is an important step towards calculations with statistically interpretable uncertainties for astrophysical reactions involving light nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first chiral effective-field-theory calculations of the energy-dependent \(np\leftrightarrow d\gamma\) cross section with Bayesian uncertainty quantification, but the quoted 0.2% uncertainty only accounts for truncation error in the nuclear potential, not from the electromagnetic currents or from fitting low-energy constants.

## Method

The authors solved Lippmann-Schwinger equations using momentum-space regularized semilocal chiral potentials at orders N\(^0\)LO through N\(^4\)LO from Reinert et al. (2017), with fixed leading one-body and one-pion-exchange two-body electromagnetic currents. They applied the Gaussian process error model developed by Melendez et al. (2019) to the order-by-order expansion coefficients of the cross section, using training data at 5 MeV intervals and a squared-exponential correlation function, to produce Bayesian credible intervals for the truncation error that account for correlations across energies.

## Key numbers

- The 1\(\sigma\) truncation error from the nuclear potential is about 0.2% for the \(np\rightarrow d\gamma\) cross section.
- At thermal neutron energy \(E = 1.2625 \times 10^{-8}\) MeV, the N\(^4\)LO prediction for \(\sigma_{np}\) is \(321.009 \pm 0.71496\) mb.
- At \(E = 9.607513 \times 10^{-3}\) MeV, the N\(^4\)LO prediction for \(\sigma_{np}\) is \(0.32739 \pm 0.00073\) mb.
- The maximum a posteriori correlation length \(\ell_\mathrm{MAP}\) is 10.4 MeV.
- The expansion parameter \(Q/\Lambda\) is approximately 0.23, with \(\Lambda = 600\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian truncation-error framework previously applied to nucleon-nucleon scattering (Melendez et al., 2019; Reinert et al., 2020) and properties of light nuclei (Wesolowski et al., 2021) to an electromagnetic reaction cross section for the first time. It provides statistically interpretable uncertainties for the \(np\rightarrow d\gamma\) reaction used in big-bang nucleosynthesis simulations, which currently rely on pionless EFT results from Rupak (2000) and Ando et al. (2006). The results enable future work that includes higher-order currents and simultaneous fitting of low-energy constants with truncation error as a guard against overfitting, following the strategy of Wesolowski et al. (2021).
