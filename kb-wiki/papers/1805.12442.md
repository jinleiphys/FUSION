# Analytical and numerical analysis of the complete Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick Hamiltonian

> The Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick is a simple, but not trivial, model of a quantum many-body system which allows us to solve the many-body Schrödinger equation without making any approximation. The model, which in its unperturbed case is composed only by two energy levels, includes two interacting terms. A first one, the $V$ interaction, which promotes or degrade pairs of particles, and a second one, the $W$ interaction, which scatters one particle in the upper and another in the lower energy level. In comparing this model with other approximation methods, the $W$ term interaction is often set to zero. In this paper, we show how the presence of this interaction changes the global structure of the system, generates degeneracies between the various eigenstates and modifies the energy eigenvalues structure. We present analytical solutions for systems of two and three particles and, for some specific cases, also for four, six and eight particles. The solutions for systems with more than eight particles are only numerical but their behaviour can be well understood by considering the extrapolations of the analytical results. Of particular interest it is the study of how the $W$ interaction affects the energy gap between the ground state and the first-excited state.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including the \(W\) interaction term (often set to zero in traditional Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick studies) fundamentally alters the eigenvalue structure, introducing degeneracies and level crossings that are absent when only the \(V\) term is present; the energy gap between the ground state and first-excited state can vanish for specific relations between the interaction strengths \(\nu\) and \(\omega\).

## Method

The authors diagonalize the complete LMG Hamiltonian \(\mathcal{H} = J_0 - \frac{\nu}{2}(J_+^2 + J_-^2) - \frac{\omega}{2}(J_+ J_- + J_- J_+)\) in the basis of \(J_0\) eigenstates labeled by \(j=N/2\) and \(m\). For \(N=2\) and \(N=3\) particles, fully analytical expressions for eigenvalues and eigenvectors are derived. For \(N=4, 6, 8\) particles, analytical solutions are obtained only for \(\omega=0\); for larger \(N\) and general \(\omega\), numerical diagonalization is performed. The study focuses on the energy differences \(\Delta_{r,s}^{[N]}(\nu,\omega)\) between eigenstates, particularly the gap between the ground state and first-excited state.

## Key numbers

- For \(N=3\) with \(\nu=0\), the first and second energy eigenvalues cross at \(\omega = 1/2\).
- For \(N=3\), the crossing condition between the two lowest states is \(\omega_{2,1}^{[3]} = \sqrt{\nu^2 + 1/4}\).
- For \(N=4\) with \(\nu=1\) and \(\omega=0\), the energy gap \(\Delta_{2,1}^{[4]}(1,0) = 4 - \sqrt{10} \approx 0.838\).
- For \(N=6\) with \(\nu=1\) and \(\omega=0\), \(\Delta_{2,1}^{[6]}(1,0) = \sqrt{38 + 4\sqrt{61}} - 8 \approx 0.321\).
- For \(N=8\) with \(\nu=1\) and \(\omega=0\), \(\Delta_{2,1}^{[8]}(1,0) = 2\sqrt{43 + 6\sqrt{14}} - \sqrt{118 + 4\sqrt{589}} \approx 0.093\).
- For \(N=8\) with \(\nu=0\), triple degeneracy occurs at \(\omega = 1/3\) and \(\omega = 1/2\), and fourfold degeneracy at \(\omega = 1\).
- The general relation for the degeneracy of the two lowest states in an \(N\)-particle system is \(\omega_{2,1}^{[N]} = \sqrt{\nu^2 + 1/(N-1)^2}\).

## Context

This work extends the original Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model (Lipkin, Meshkov, and Glick, 1965), which typically sets \(W=0\) to test approximations like random phase approximation and Hartree-Fock theory. By restoring the full \(W\) interaction, the authors correct missing factors in the original analytical expressions for \(N=6\) and \(N=8\) systems (a factor of 4 and 6, respectively). The results enable systematic study of how the \(W\) term induces degeneracies and ground-state changes, providing a benchmark for testing many-body approximation methods on a solvable model with both interaction channels.
