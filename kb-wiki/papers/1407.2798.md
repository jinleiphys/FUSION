# SU(4) symmetry of the dynamical QCD string and genesis of hadron spectra

> A large degeneracy of mesons of a given spin has recently been discovered upon reduction of the quasi-zero modes of the Dirac operator in a dynamical lattice simulation. Here a symmetry group that is responsible for this degeneracy is established, which is SU(4) \supset SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R \times U(1)_A \times C_i. It is argued that this symmetry group is a symmetry of the dynamical QCD string. Implications of this picture for a genesis of light hadron spectra are discussed.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the large degeneracy of all eight $J=1$ mesons observed in lattice simulations after removing quasi-zero Dirac eigenmodes is explained by an emergent $SU(4)$ symmetry of the dynamical QCD string, not by a symmetry of the QCD Lagrangian itself. This symmetry is conditional on the truncation of the lowest-lying eigenmodes and applies only to mesons with $J \geq 1$.

## Method
The author constructs the relativistic spherical helicity basis vectors for $\bar q q$ states with definite chirality and classifies them into irreducible representations of the parity-chiral group $SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R \times {\cal C}_i$. By identifying transformations that connect all basis vectors from the four distinct chiral representations, the minimal symmetry group is shown to be $SU(4) \supset SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R \times U(1)_A \times {\cal C}_i$, acting on the fundamental four-component vector $N^T = (u_L, u_R, d_L, d_R)$. The degeneracy of the $^3S_1$, $^3D_1$, $^1P_1$, and $^3P_1$ states in the $LS$ basis is used to prove the absence of spin-spin, spin-orbit, and tensor interactions.

## Key numbers
- The observed degeneracy involves all eight $J=1$ mesons from the four chiral representations: $(0,0)$, $(1/2,1/2)_a$, $(1/2,1/2)_b$, and $(0,1)\oplus(1,0)$.
- The $SU(4)$ representation is reducible with dimension 16: $\bar{4} \times 4 = 15 + 1$.
- The truncation removes "of order 10" quasi-zero modes from "more than one million" total modes in the lattice simulation.
- The symmetry group generalizes to $SU(2N_f)$ for $N_f$ light flavors.

## Context
This work extends the lattice simulation results of Glozman et al. (2013, 2014) that discovered meson degeneracy upon removing quasi-zero Dirac eigenmodes, and the earlier lattice studies with the Wilson Dirac operator by Lang and Schrock (2011) and Glozman et al. (2012). The proposed $SU(4)$ symmetry is distinct from the nonrelativistic Wigner spin-isospin $SU(4)_{SI}$ symmetry of the constituent quark model (Wigner 1937, Gursey and Radicati 1964) and from the Pauli-Gursey $SU(4)$ symmetry in $N_c=2$ QCD (Pauli 1957, Gursey 1958). The picture enables a new interpretation of light meson spectra as primary levels of the dynamical QCD string split by instanton-induced dynamics (Hooft 1976, Shuryak 1982, Diakonov and Petrov 1986).
