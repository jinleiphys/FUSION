# Extended SAID Partial-Wave Analysis of Pion Photoproduction

> A unified Chew-Mandelstam description of single-pion photoproduction data, together with pion- and eta-hadroproduction data, has been extended to include measurements carried out over the last decade. We consider photo-decay amplitudes evaluated at the pole with particular emphasis on ng couplings and the influence of weighting on our fits. Both energy-dependent and single-energy analysis (energy-binned data) are considered.

## Key claim

The SAID partial-wave analysis of single-pion photoproduction has been extended to include measurements from the last decade, but the quality of the fit depends on the weighting of neutron-target data, particularly the poorly constrained $\gamma n\to\pi^0 n$ channel, which can be qualitatively predicted from other charge channels but requires arbitrary weighting factors to achieve acceptable $\chi^2$ values.

## Method

The analysis uses a Chew-Mandelstam K-matrix formalism extended to include an electromagnetic channel, with hadronic K-matrix elements fixed from the previous SAID solution SP06 and only electromagnetic elements varied. Energy-dependent fits were performed from threshold to $E_\gamma = 2.7$ GeV, and single-energy fits to $E_\gamma = 2.2$ GeV, using a modified $\chi^2$ function that allows re-normalization of angular distributions with associated normalization uncertainties. Several supplemental fits were produced: SM44 (with $\pi^0 n$ data weighted by a factor of 4), NM22 (excluding $\pi^0 n$ data entirely), and WM22 (weighting all poorly-fitted data by a factor of 4).

## Key numbers

- The SAID database contains 35,898 $\gamma p\to\pi^{0}p$, 12,494 $\gamma p\to\pi^{+}n$, 13,473 $\gamma n\to\pi^{-}p$, and 2,515 $\gamma n\to\pi^{0}n$ data points below $E_\gamma = 2700$ MeV
- Since 2012, 21,190 $\gamma p\to\pi^{0}p$, 1,502 $\gamma p\to\pi^{+}n$, 10,923 $\gamma n\to\pi^{-}p$, and 1,763 $\gamma n\to\pi^{0}n$ data points have been added
- The SM22 fit yields total $\chi^2$/data values of 1.92 for $\pi^0 p$, 2.25 for $\pi^+ n$, 1.84 for $\pi^- p$, and 3.48 for $\pi^0 n$ channels
- The previous CM12 fit (2012) gives total $\chi^2$/data of 4.92 for $\pi^0 p$, 4.51 for $\pi^+ n$, 33.39 for $\pi^- p$, and 5.89 for $\pi^0 n$ when evaluated against the current database
- The NM22 fit (excluding $\pi^0 n$ data) gives $\chi^2$/data of 7.08 for $\pi^0 n$ data, compared to 3.48 for SM22

## Context

This work updates the SAID partial-wave analysis program, which has been producing pion photoproduction analyses since 1990 (Arndt 1989, 1990), with the previous full analysis reported by Workman (2012) and Svarc (2014). The extended database incorporates new measurements from Jefferson Lab (CLAS), MAMI (A2/Crystal Ball), CBELSA/TAPS, SPring-8/LEPS2, and MAX-lab, with 60% of new data in the $\gamma p\to\pi^{0}p$ channel. The results enable improved determination of photodecay amplitudes and pole positions for comparison with quark models and lattice QCD predictions, and can be used in evaluating the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule as reported by Strakovsky (2022).
