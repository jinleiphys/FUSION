# Continuous Time Monte Carlo for Lattice QCD in the Strong Coupling Limit

> We present results for lattice QCD in the limit of infinite gauge coupling, obtained from a worm-type Monte Carlo algorithm on a discrete spatial lattice but with continuous Euclidean time. This is obtained by sending both the anisotropy parameter gamma^2 \sim a/a_t and the number of time-slices N_τto infinity, keeping the ratio γ^2/N_τ\sim aT fixed. The obvious gain is that no continuum extrapolation N_τ-> \infty has to be carried out. Moreover, the algorithm is faster and the sign problem disappears. We compare our computations with those on discrete lattices. We determine the phase diagram as a function of temperature and baryon chemical potential.

## Key claim
The paper presents a continuous-time Monte Carlo algorithm for lattice QCD in the strong coupling limit that eliminates the need for a continuum extrapolation in the temporal direction, removes the sign problem entirely, and yields a faster algorithm compared to discrete-time approaches.

## Method
The authors send both the anisotropy parameter γ² (ratio of spatial to temporal lattice spacing) and the number of temporal slices Nτ to infinity while keeping the ratio γ²/Nτ fixed, which corresponds to the temperature aT. This continuous-time limit is implemented via a worm-type Monte Carlo algorithm on a discrete spatial lattice, where spatial dimers become resolved into single dimers and baryons become static, allowing the partition function to be written in terms of vertices at which spatial meson hoppings occur.

## Key numbers
- The continuous-time limit is defined by Nτ → ∞ and γ → ∞ with γ²/Nτ fixed, where γ²/Nτ represents the temperature aT.
- L-vertices have weight v_L = γ⁻¹ and T-vertices have weight v_T = 2γ⁻¹/√3 in the continuous-time partition function.
- The probability distribution for the lengths of dashed or solid intervals follows an exponential distribution P(Δβ) = exp(-λ Δβ) with Δβ ∈ [0, β = 1/aT].
- The decay constant λ for spatial dimer emissions is given by λ = d_M(x,t)/4, where d_M(x,t) is the number of mesonic neighbors at a given coordinate.

## Context
This work extends the strong coupling limit of lattice QCD (SC-QCD) studied by Karsch (1989) and the directed path algorithm introduced by Adams (1989), while correcting systematic errors from mean field approximations by Nishida (2004) and fixed-Nτ Monte Carlo by de Forcrand (2010). The continuous-time approach enables more precise determination of the phase diagram as a function of temperature and baryon chemical potential, including the location of the tricritical point and a re-entrance predicted by mean field analysis.
