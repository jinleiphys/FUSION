# A theoretical analysis of the semileptonic decays $η^{(\prime)}\toπ^0l^+l^-$ and $η^\prime\toηl^+l^-$

> A complete theoretical analysis of the $C$-conserving semileptonic decays $η^{(\prime)}\toπ^0l^+l^-$ and $η^\prime\toηl^+l^-$ ($l=e$ or $μ$) is carried out within the framework of the Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) model. An existing phenomenological model is used to parametrise the VMD coupling constants and the associated numerical values are obtained from an optimisation fit to $V\to Pγ$ and $P\to Vγ$ radiative decays ($V=ρ^0$, $ω$, $ϕ$ and $P=π^0$, $η$, $η^{\prime}$). The decay widths and dilepton energy spectra for the two $η\toπ^0l^+l^-$ processes obtained using this approach are compared and found to be in good agreement with other results available in the published literature. Theoretical predictions for the four $η^{\prime}\toπ^0l^+l^-$ and $η^\prime\toηl^+l^-$ decay widths and dilepton energy spectra are calculated and presented for the first time in this work.

## Key claim

The paper provides a complete theoretical analysis of the C-conserving semileptonic decays η(′)→π0l+l− and η′→ηl+l− within the Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) model, but the authors acknowledge that using a Breit-Wigner approximation for the ρ0 propagator (rather than an energy-dependent width) is a potential source of error, and that neglecting the q2 dependence of the VPγ transition form factor is a tolerable but not fully rigorous approximation.

## Method

The authors calculated the six decay processes using the VMD model, where the decays proceed through a C-conserving two-photon intermediate state mediated by vector meson exchanges (ρ0, ω, φ). They parametrised the VMD coupling constants using a phenomenological quark-based model and obtained numerical values from an optimisation fit to V→Pγ and P→Vγ radiative decays. The loop integrals were performed using Feynman parametrisation and evaluated numerically with adaptive Monte Carlo methods, with the unpolarised squared amplitude integrated over three-body phase space.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of η→π0l+l− decays by Cheng (1967), Smith (1968), and Ng et al. (1992, 1993), providing updated calculations with fewer approximations and including both electron and muon modes. The predictions for the four η′→π0l+l− and η′→ηl+l− decay widths and dilepton energy spectra are presented for the first time. The results enable comparison with experimental upper limits from the WASA-at-COSY collaboration (Adlarson et al., 2018) and provide benchmarks for future experiments such as REDTOP, which will search for violations of Standard Model symmetries in η and η′ decays.
