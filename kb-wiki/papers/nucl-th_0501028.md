# Possible Resolutions of the D-Paradox

> We propose possible ways of explaining the net charge event-by-event fluctuations in Au+Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider within a quark recombination model. We discuss various methods of estimating the number of quarks at recombination and their implications for the predicted net charge fluctuations. We also discuss the possibility of diquark and quark-antiquark clustering above the deconfinement temperature.

## Key claim

The measured net charge fluctuations in Au+Au collisions at RHIC (D ≈ 2.8) can be reconciled with a deconfined quark-gluon plasma if hadronization proceeds via valence quark recombination, but only within the present systematic uncertainties; the recombination picture explains about 80% of the observed fluctuations when using the full resonance gas at chemical freezeout, and the agreement depends on whether one includes only ground-state hadrons or all known resonances.

## Method

The authors use a quark recombination model to calculate the expected net charge fluctuations from the valence quark content of hadrons at chemical freezeout, starting from the measured charged particle multiplicity in central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 130 GeV. They estimate the number and flavor distribution of recombining quarks using three different methods: (1) from the statistical hadronization model including all known resonances, (2) including only ground-state meson nonets and octet/decuplet baryons, and (3) from the entropy per particle derived from experimental data by Pal and Pratt. They also explore scenarios where diquark and quark-antiquark pairs participate as elementary constituents in the recombination process.

## Key numbers

- STAR measured D = 2.8 ± 0.05 in central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 130 GeV, before corrections for global charge conservation
- For a free quark-gluon plasma D ≈ 1, while for a free pion gas D ≈ 4
- Using the full resonance gas at Tc, 507 ± 35 hadrons contain 1125 quarks and antiquarks, yielding predicted d⟨δQ²⟩q/dy = 286 ± 23, compared to the measured d⟨δQ²⟩had/dy = 368 ± 33
- Using only ground-state hadrons, 635 ± 43 hadrons contain about 1377 quarks, yielding d⟨δQ²⟩q/dy = 345 ± 29
- Using the entropy method (dS/dy = 4450 ± 400 from Pal and Pratt), dNhad/dy = 864 ± 78, yielding d⟨δQ²⟩q/dy = 487 ± 39
- The average entropy per hadron in the full resonance gas at T = Tc = 170 MeV is ⟨S/N⟩ = 5.15; including only ground-state hadrons gives ⟨S/N⟩ = 4.72
- Lattice QCD results from CP-PACS show s/sSB = 0.612 ± 0.043 at T/Tc = 1.04 for mPS/mV = 0.65, and s/sSB = 0.636 ± 0.044 at T/Tc = 1.12 for mPS/mV = 0.80

## Context

This paper addresses the "D-paradox", the discrepancy between the measured net charge fluctuations (D ≈ 2.8 by STAR, D ≈ 3 by PHENIX) and the value D ≈ 1 expected for a free quark-gluon plasma, despite other observables indicating deconfinement at RHIC. The work extends the argument by Bialas (2002) that valence quark counting could reconcile the measurements, and builds on quark recombination models (ALCOR by Biro and collaborators, 1994) that successfully describe hadron abundances at SPS and RHIC. The analysis uses lattice QCD entropy density calculations from CP-PACS and the Bielefeld group, and entropy per particle estimates from Pal and Pratt. The results enable future work to better constrain the entropy production in the hadronic phase and to determine whether diquark clustering above Tc is compatible with the observed constituent quark number scaling in elliptic flow data.
