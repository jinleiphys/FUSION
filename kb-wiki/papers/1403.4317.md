# Spin Asymmetry of J/psi in Peripheral Pb+Pb Collisions at LHC

> By generalizing the statistical model for particle production to the spin degree of freedom of initially produced J/psi, we study the spin projection J_y of J/psi perpendicular to the reaction plane in peripheral heavy ion collisions at the LHC energy that leads to a strong, albeit of short duration, magnetic field. We find that for J/psis produced directly from charm and anticharm quarks in the color singlet state, like that in the Color-Singlet Model, their yield in the presence of the magnetic field is larger for J_y=0 than for J_y=1 or -1. This leads to a spin asymmetry of finally produced J/ψeven after including their final-state scattering in the produced quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The authors claim that in peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies, the strong but short-lived magnetic field can produce a spin asymmetry of prompt J/ψ mesons, specifically a larger yield for spin projection J_y=0 than for |J_y|=1, but only if the J/ψ is produced directly from charm and anticharm quarks in a color-singlet state (Color-Singlet Model); no asymmetry is expected in the Color-Octet Model scenario.

## Method

The authors generalized the statistical hadronization model to include the spin degree of freedom of initially produced J/ψ. They computed the magnetic field from the Liénard-Wiechert potentials of the two colliding Pb nuclei, including a medium effect that slows the field decay. The time evolution of J/ψ distribution functions for different spin states was described by a transport equation including dissociation, regeneration, and spin-flipping rates, with the fireball dynamics given by 2+1 dimensional ideal hydrodynamics with boost invariance.

## Key numbers

- Maximum magnetic field at impact parameter b=10 fm: −eB_y = 1.1 GeV², leading to an energy difference between spin states E_max = 0.5 GeV.
- Initial fraction of J/ψ with J_y=0 in the Color-Singlet Model: f_max = 0.83 at the maximum field, compared to f=1/3 in the absence of a magnetic field.
- For peripheral collisions at b=10.5 fm and √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, the total nuclear modification factor R_AA integrated over transverse momentum is 0.65.
- The fraction F of J/ψ with J_y=0 increases monotonically with p_T from 0.34 at p_T≈0 to 0.42 at p_T=10 GeV (with no spin flipping, ε=0).
- With spin-flipping rate equal to dissociation rate (ε=1), the fraction F at p_T=10 GeV is still above 0.39.

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model of Andronic et al. (2008) and Braun-Munzinger et al. (2000) to the spin degree of freedom, and uses the Color-Singlet Model of Chang (1979) as the production mechanism, following the higher-order CSM calculations of Lansberg (2011) that reproduce p+p data. The results contrast with the Color-Octet Model of Bodwin (1994), which predicts no spin asymmetry. The study enables experimental tests of J/ψ production mechanisms in heavy ion collisions by measuring spin asymmetry, and can be generalized to dilepton asymmetries from the initial stage of relativistic heavy ion collisions.
