# The Energy-Weighted and Non Energy-Weighted Gamow-Teller Sum Rules in Relativistic Random Phase Approximation

> The non energy-weighted Gamow-Teller(GT) sum rule is satisfied in relativistic models, when all nuclear density-dependent terms, including Pauli blocking terms from nucleon-antinucleon excitations, are taken into account in the RPA correlation function. The no-sea approximation is equivalent to this approximation for the giant GT resonance state and satisfies the sum rule, but each of the total $β_-$ and $β_+$ strengths is different in the two approximations. It is also shown that the energy-weighted sum of the GT strengths for the $β_-$ and $β_+$ transitions in RPA is equal to the expectation value of the double commutator of the nuclear Hamiltonian with the GT operator, when the expectation value is calculated with the ground state in the mean field approximation. Since the present RPA neglects renormalization of the divergence, however, the energy-weighted strengths outside of the giant GT resonance region become negative. These facts are shown by calculating in an analytic way the GT strengths of nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The non energy-weighted Ikeda-Fujii-Fujita (IFF) Gamow-Teller sum rule is satisfied in relativistic random phase approximation (RPA) models only when all nuclear density-dependent terms, including Pauli blocking terms from nucleon-antinucleon excitations, are included; the no-sea approximation also satisfies the sum rule but yields different individual \(\beta_-\) and \(\beta_+\) strengths compared to the no-free-term approximation, while the energy-weighted sum rule holds formally but produces negative strengths outside the giant resonance region due to neglected renormalization of divergences.

## Method

The authors derived analytic expressions for the Gamow-Teller response functions of nuclear matter using relativistic RPA with a contact Landau-Migdal interaction of pseudovector form. They compared two regularization schemes: the no-free-term approximation (NFA), which retains all density-dependent terms including Pauli blocking from nucleon-antinucleon excitations while discarding divergent density-independent terms, and the no-sea approximation (NSA), which modifies the antinucleon Green function's imaginary part to eliminate divergences. The non energy-weighted and energy-weighted sum rules were evaluated by contour integration of the RPA correlation functions.

## Key numbers

* The non energy-weighted IFF sum rule value is exactly \(2(N-Z)\) in both NFA and NSA, independent of the Landau-Migdal parameter \(g'\).
* The nucleon-sector Gamow-Teller strength is quenched by a factor \((1 - \frac{2}{3}v_{\text{F}}^2)\) relative to the non-relativistic value, where \(v_{\text{F}} = k_{\text{F}}/E_{\text{F}}\) is the Fermi velocity.
* The quenching of the nucleon-sector strength is approximately 6% in finite nuclei and 12% in nuclear matter, as reported in prior work by the same authors.
* The imaginary part of the Pauli blocking correlation function \(I_{\text{Pauli}}(k_f, q_0)\) is nonzero only for \(2M^* < q_0 < 2E_{\text{F}}\), with explicit form \(-\frac{4\pi^2}{3}\frac{(q_0^2 - 4M^{*2})^{3/2}}{q_0}\).
* The excitation energy of the giant Gamow-Teller resonance is approximately \(\omega_0 \approx \chi_5 \alpha_{\text{ph}}\), and its strength is approximately \(S_{\text{GT}} \approx \frac{V}{(2\pi)^3}\alpha_{\text{ph}}\), both nearly independent of the Landau-Migdal parameter.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigations of the Ikeda-Fujii-Fujita sum rule in relativistic models (Kurasawa, Suzuki, 2001-2002), which showed that nucleon-sector GT strength is quenched by relativistic effects. It contrasts with non-relativistic analyses of \((p,n)\) reactions (Wakasa, 2003) that attributed all 10% quenching to \(\Delta\)-hole coupling (Sakai, Bentz). The results enable refined interpretation of experimental quenching: if 6% arises from relativistic effects, only 4% may stem from \(\Delta\) degrees of freedom, implying a very small Landau-Migdal parameter \(g'_{\text{N}\Delta}\) that affects predictions for pion condensation critical density and spin-dependent response functions. The paper also establishes that the Thouless theorem for energy-weighted sum rules holds in relativistic RPA for charge-exchange excitations, though renormalization of divergences remains an open problem for future work.
