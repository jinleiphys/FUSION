# Vortex solutions in the Abelian Higgs Model with a neutral scalar

> We construct an extension of the Abelian Higgs model, which consists of a complex scalar field by including an additional real, electromagnetically neutral scalar field. We couple this real scalar field to the complex scalar field via a quartic coupling and investigate $U(1)$ vortex solutions in this "extended Abelian Higgs Model". Since this model has two additional homogeneous ground states, the $U(1)$ vortices that can form in this model have a richer structure than in the Abelian Higgs Model. We also find the "phase diagram" of the model showing the parameter space in which the real scalar particle condenses in the vortex state while having a zero vacuum expectation value in the homogeneous ground state.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an extension of the Abelian Higgs model by adding a real, electromagnetically neutral scalar field coupled to the charged scalar via a quartic interaction, and demonstrates that this model supports U(1) vortex solutions with a richer structure than the standard Abelian Higgs model. The key hedge is that the neutral scalar can condense inside the vortex core even when its vacuum expectation value is zero in the homogeneous ground state, but this occurs only in a specific region of parameter space.

## Method

The authors construct a Lagrangian with a complex scalar field Φ and a real scalar field φ, coupled via a quartic term f|Φ|²|φ|², and analyze the homogeneous ground states by minimizing the potential to find four possible vacuum phases. They then impose a cylindrically symmetric vortex ansatz with winding number n=1, derive the equations of motion, and solve them numerically for various values of the coupling parameters b, d, and f, with e=1 and sign(a)=-1, using |a| to set the energy scale.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic flux quantization condition gives flux = 2nπ/e, with n an integer; for unit flux, n=1.
- The scalar masses are m_Φ = √a and m_φ = √(2c); the photon mass is m_A = ev_Φ.
- For the case v_Φ = v_φ ≠ 0, numerical solutions are shown with b=40 and f varied between -75 and 75.
- For the case v_Φ ≠ 0, v_φ = 0, numerical solutions use b=10 and c/a = 8/10 for f > 16.
- The asymptotic decay rate of the vector potential is α/2 = √2 ev_Φ, and the scalar field fluctuations decay with rates √λ_± and α = 2√2 ev_Φ.
- The phase diagram is shown for two values of c/a: 8/10 and 1/2, with the neutral scalar condensation region smaller for c/a = 1/2.

## Context

This work extends the standard Abelian Higgs model (Nielsen and Olesen, 1970s) by adding a neutral scalar, motivated by systems such as QCD in large magnetic fields where ρ-mesons may condense (Maxim and collaborators, 2010s) and chiral perturbation theory where only charged pions condense in vortices (Julia, 1990s). The model enables future study of the thermodynamics of Abrikosov vortex lattices in the presence of an external magnetic field, and the resulting phase diagram as a function of field strength and model parameters.
