# The $γπ\toππ$ anomaly from lattice QCD and dispersion relations

> We propose a formalism to extract the $γπ\toππ$ chiral anomaly $F_{3π}$ from calculations in lattice QCD performed at larger-than-physical pion masses. To this end, we start from a dispersive representation of the $γ^{(*)}π\toππ$ amplitude, whose main quark-mass dependence arises from the $ππ$ scattering phase shift and can be derived from chiral perturbation theory via the inverse-amplitude method. With parameters constrained by lattice calculations of the $P$-wave phase shift, we use this combination of dispersion relations and effective field theory to extrapolate two recent $γ^{(*)}π\toππ$ calculations in lattice QCD to the physical point. Our formalism allows us to extract the radiative coupling of the $ρ(770)$ meson and, for the first time, the chiral anomaly $F_{3π}=38(16)(11)\,\text{GeV}^{-3}$. The result is consistent with the chiral prediction albeit within large uncertainties, which will improve in accordance with progress in future lattice-QCD computations.

## Key claim

The authors propose a formalism to extract the chiral anomaly \(F_{3\pi}\) from lattice QCD calculations performed at larger-than-physical pion masses, using a combination of dispersion relations (Khuri-Treiman equations) and chiral perturbation theory via the inverse-amplitude method, with the caveat that the extracted value \(F_{3\pi}=38(16)(11)\,\text{GeV}^{-3}\) is consistent with the chiral prediction but carries large uncertainties that will only improve with future lattice computations.

## Method

The authors start from a dispersive (Khuri-Treiman) representation of the \(\gamma^{(*)}\pi\to\pi\pi\) amplitude, whose main quark-mass dependence enters through the \(\pi\pi\) \(P\)-wave phase shift. They describe this phase shift using the next-to-leading-order inverse-amplitude method in SU(2) chiral perturbation theory, with parameters constrained by fitting to lattice QCD calculations of the \(\pi\pi\) energy levels via Luscher's quantization condition. The resulting phase shift is used to compute Lellouch-Luscher factors, which convert finite-volume lattice matrix elements for \(\gamma^{(*)}\pi\to\pi\pi\) into infinite-volume partial-wave amplitudes; the subtraction functions in the Khuri-Treiman equations are parameterized with a linear dependence on \(M_\pi^2\) and fitted to two sets of lattice QCD data at unphysical pion masses, then extrapolated to the physical point.

## Key numbers

* Chiral anomaly extracted at the physical point: \(F_{3\pi} = 38(16)(11)\,\text{GeV}^{-3}\), where the first uncertainty is statistical and the second systematic.
* Chiral perturbation theory prediction for the anomaly: \(F_{3\pi} = 1/(4\pi^2 F_\pi^3) = 32.23(10)\,\text{GeV}^{-3}\), using \(F_\pi = 92.28(10)\,\text{MeV}\).
* Pion decay constant in the chiral limit used in the inverse-amplitude method: \(F = 86.89(58)\,\text{MeV}\), from the \(N_f=2+1\) FLAG average combined with the PDG value of \(F_\pi\).
* Omega meson mass as a function of pion mass: \(M_\omega(M_\pi^2) = 0.7686(2)\,\text{GeV} + 0.719(9)\,\text{GeV}^{-1} M_\pi^2\), from Dax (2018).

## Context

This work extends the dispersive Khuri-Treiman framework for \(\gamma\pi\to\pi\pi\) developed by Hoferichter (2012, 2017) to unphysical pion masses by combining it with the inverse-amplitude method for \(\pi\pi\) scattering, following the strategy outlined by Niehus (2019). It enables the first extraction of the chiral anomaly \(F_{3\pi}\) from lattice QCD data, and provides a formalism to extract the radiative coupling of the \(\rho(770)\) meson; the approach can be improved as future lattice QCD computations at additional pion masses become available, and it provides a testing ground for frameworks analyzing three-particle scattering directly, such as those by Hansen (2019) and Mai (2021).
