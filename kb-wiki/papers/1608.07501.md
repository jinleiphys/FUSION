# Systematic study of actinide and pre-actinide fission modes

> In this work, we present new experimental data on mass distribution of fission fragments from $^{241}$Am proton-induced fission at $660$ MeV measured at the LNR Phasotron (JINR). The systematic analysis of several measured fragment mass distributions from different fission reactions available in the literature is also presented. The proton-induced fission of $^{241}$Am, $^{237}$Np and $^{238}$U at 26.5, 62.9 and 660 MeV was studied. The proton-induced fission of $^{232}$Th was studied at 26.5, 62.9 and 190 MeV. The fission of $^{208}$Pb also by a proton was investigated at 190, 500 and 1000 MeV. The fission of $^{197}$Au was studied for 190 and 800 MeV protons. Bremsstrahlung reactions with maximum photon energies of 50 and 3500 MeV were studied for $^{232}$Th and $^{238}$U. The framework of the Random Neck Rupture Model was applied in the analysis. The roles of the neutron excess and of the so called fissility parameter were also investigated.

## Key claim

The paper presents new experimental data on mass distributions from proton-induced fission of $^{241}$Am at 660 MeV and performs a systematic analysis of fission modes across actinide and pre-actinide targets, finding that the symmetric (Superlong) fission mode dominates at high excitation energies but asymmetric modes persist even at 660 MeV, with the super-asymmetric Standard 3 mode increasing with target mass and competing with Standard 1 in the case of americium.

## Method

The CRISP Monte Carlo model was used to simulate the full reaction chain from intranuclear cascade through evaporation and fission, with fission fragment masses determined by the Random Neck Rupture Model (multi-modal fission with Superlong, Standard 1, Standard 2, and Standard 3 modes). Experimental mass distributions were obtained via the induced-activity method using HPGe and Ge(Li) detectors at the LNR Phasotron (JINR), with targets of $^{241}$Am, $^{237}$Np, $^{238}$U, $^{232}$Th, $^{208}$Pb, and $^{197}$Au irradiated by protons at energies of 26.5, 62.9, 190, 500, 660, 800, and 1000 MeV, plus bremsstrahlung reactions on $^{232}$Th and $^{238}$U at maximum photon energies of 50 and 3500 MeV.

## Key numbers

* Total fission cross section for $^{241}$Am at 660 MeV: 1811 mb, with relative contributions of 82.2% Superlong, 12.7% Standard 2, 2.6% Standard 3, and 2.5% Standard 1
* Total fission cross section for $^{237}$Np at 660 MeV: 1360 mb, with 80% Superlong, 15.5% Standard 2, 1.2% Standard 3, and 3.3% Standard 1
* Total fission cross section for $^{238}$U at 660 MeV: 1140 mb, with 49% Superlong, 43% Standard 2, 0.6% Standard 3, and 7.4% Standard 1
* New measured cross sections for $^{241}$Am at 660 MeV include $^{28}$Mg at 0.27 $\pm$ 0.03 mb (cumulative) and $^{150m}$Eu at 3.93 $\pm$ 0.4 mb (independent)
* Proton beam intensity was about $3 \times 10^{14}$ protons per minute with irradiation time of 27 minutes
* Target thicknesses: natural uranium 0.0487 mm (0.164 g), neptunium 0.193 mm (0.742 g), americium 0.043 mm (0.177 g)

## Context

This work extends the systematic fission studies of Karapetyan (2009), Balabekyan (2010), and Deppman (2013b, 2013c) by adding $^{241}$Am data and analyzing a broader set of targets and energies within the CRISP model framework. The results enable better understanding of multi-modal fission at intermediate and high excitation energies, relevant for accelerator-driven systems (ADS) design, and challenge the assumption that asymmetric modes vanish at high energy by showing their persistence even at 660 MeV. The analysis also investigates the Chung (1981, 1982) criterion based on the fissility parameter $Z^2/A$ and proposes cold cluster emission to explain features in pre-actinide fragment mass distributions.
