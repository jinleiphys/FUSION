# Transient topological objects in high energy collisions

> The possible topology of quantum fluctuations which take place at the earliest stage of high-energy processes is studied. A new exact solution of Yang-Mills equations with fractional topological charge and carrying a single color is found.

## Key claim

A new exact solution of Yang-Mills equations exists that describes a transient topological gluon configuration (dubbed an "ephemeron") which interpolates between Euclidean and Minkowski space, carrying a fractional topological charge that can be a continuous parameter rather than an integer.

## Method

The author constructs the solution using the tetrad formalism, mapping the SU(2)×SU(2) color group onto the O(4) space-time symmetry group in the Euclidean domain before the interaction. The gauge field potential is set equal to the spin connection of the metric, yielding a self-dual field configuration in curvilinear coordinates (τ, r, φ, η) that cover the interior of the past light cone. The Euclidean solution is then continued to Minkowski space as propagating waves after collapse at τ=0.

## Key numbers

- The topological charge of the ephemeron in the Euclidean domain covering the interior of the past light cone is Q = 1/8.
- The total topological charge summed over all domains of the full chart is Q_tot = 1.
- With the scale factor h retained explicitly, the topological charge becomes Q = h²/8, a continuous parameter.
- The Euclidean action satisfies the standard relation S_E = -(8π²/g²)|Q|.
- The field strengths are given by expressions involving Bessel functions: F³_τη/τ = -F³_rφ/r = λ² K₀(λτ)J₀(λr) and F³_τφ/r = -F³_rη/τ = λ² J₁(λr)K₁(λτ), where ρ = λ⁻¹ is the ephemeron "size".

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of causal QCD evolution in high-energy collisions (Makhlin 1995, Makhlin and Surdutovich 1998) by providing explicit field configurations that bridge the Euclidean instanton picture (Schäfer and Shuryak 1998) with Minkowski-space dynamics. The ephemeron solution contrasts with the spherically symmetric BPST instantons (Belavin, Polyakov, Schwartz, Tyupkin 1975) which can only have integer topological charges, and it offers a framework to connect perturbative and non-perturbative QCD in the description of ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions.
