# Elastic and inelastic scattering of $^{16}$O on $^{27}$Al and $^{28}$Si at 240 MeV

> The nuclear scattering at energies well above the Coulomb barrier allows for a fairly sensitive examination of the parameters of the optical potential when the relevant couplings are included into the calculations. In this work we present experimental angular distribution data for the elastic and inelastic scatterings of $^{16}$O impinging on $^{27}$Al and $^{28}$Si target nuclei at \textit{E}$_{lab}=240$ MeV. The experimental data were measured at $7\degree < θ_{c.m.} < 16\degree$ with good angular resolution. Experimental data are compared with coupled channel calculations with the inclusion of couplings to excited states in the target and projectile. We show that the shape of angular distributions are sensitive to the mass diffuseness parameter and the best agreement is achieved for $a = 0.62$ fm.

## Key claim
The shape of the elastic and inelastic angular distributions for 240 MeV 16O on 27Al and 28Si is sensitive to the mass diffuseness parameter of the optical potential, and the best agreement with coupled-channel calculations is achieved for a diffuseness of a = 0.62 fm, which is slightly higher than the standard Sao Paulo potential value of 0.56 fm; however, even with this adjustment, the inelastic angular distributions for 27Al are not as well reproduced as those for 28Si, possibly due to experimental contamination.

## Method
Angular distributions for elastic and inelastic scattering of a 240 MeV 16O beam on 27Al (89 ug/cm2) and 28Si (148 ug/cm2) targets were measured using the MAGNEX spectrometer at INFN-LNS Catania, covering laboratory angles from 4 to 12 degrees with an angular resolution better than 0.6 degrees. The experimental data were compared with coupled-channel (CC) calculations using the FRESCO code, employing a Sao Paulo potential (SPP) based optical potential with normalization factors Nr = 1.0 and Ni = 0.78, and including couplings to low-lying excited states in both the target and projectile (including the 3- state of 16O at 6.1 MeV). The calculations also examined the effects of varying the imaginary normalization factor, deforming the imaginary potential, and adjusting the nuclear mass diffuseness parameter.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: Elab = 240 MeV for 16O on 27Al and 28Si.
- Angular range measured: 7 degrees < theta_c.m. < 16 degrees (from abstract; full text states 4 degrees < theta_lab < 12 degrees).
- Angular resolution: better than 0.6 degrees (full width at half maximum of elastic peak about 0.6 MeV in excitation energy).
- Best-fit mass diffuseness parameter: a = 0.62 fm, compared to the standard SPP value of a = 0.56 fm.
- Target thicknesses: 27Al = 89 ug/cm2; 28Si = 148 ug/cm2.
- Reduced transition probabilities used: B(E2) for 27Al states range from 0.004 to 0.019 e2b2; for 28Si 2+ state B(E2) up = 0.033 e2b2; for 16O 3- state B(E3) up = 0.0015 e2b3.
- Electric quadrupole moments: Q = +0.14(1) b for 27Al and Q = +0.16(1) b for 28Si.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of 16O + 27Al scattering at 100 MeV and 280 MeV (Refs. PLO12, CNL16) and 16O + 60Ni at 260 MeV (ZCL18, 2018), where the inclusion of the 3- excited state of 16O and deformation of the imaginary potential were found to be important. The present measurements at 240 MeV provide new high-resolution data for both 27Al and its isotone 28Si, enabling a detailed test of the mass diffuseness parameter in the Sao Paulo potential. The results enable future work to incorporate transfer reaction channels (e.g., p-transfer and alpha-transfer) into full coupled reaction channel calculations, which were not included here.
