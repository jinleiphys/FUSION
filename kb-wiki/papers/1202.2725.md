# Odd-even staggering of reaction cross sections for 22,23,24O isotopes

> The interaction cross sections of $^{22,23}$O nuclei at 900 MeV/nucleon have been measured recently by Kanungo {\it et al.}. We show that the odd-even staggering parameter of interaction cross sections deduced from these new data agrees well with the theoretical systematics expected for the neutron separation energy of $S_n$=2.74$\pm$0.120 MeV for $^{23}$O. We also discuss briefly the energy dependence of the staggering parameter.

# Odd-even staggering of reaction cross sections for 22,23,24O isotopes

## Key claim

The odd-even staggering (OES) parameter of interaction cross sections for 22,23,24O, extracted from the new Kanungo data for 22,23O combined with the previous Ozawa data for 24O, agrees well with the theoretical systematics expected for a neutron separation energy of Sn = 2.74 ± 0.120 MeV for 23O, in marked contrast to the previous measurement which had shown an anomalously large cross section.

## Method

The authors calculated reaction cross sections for 22,23,24O + 12C using the optical limit approximation of Glauber theory with higher-order corrections. Density distributions for 24O were constructed with a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method using a Woods-Saxon mean-field potential and a density-dependent zero-range pairing interaction, while densities for 22,23O were constructed without pairing, with the valence neutron in 23O occupying the 2s1/2 state. The OES parameter γ3 was computed as a function of the one-neutron separation energy of 23O by varying the depth of the Woods-Saxon potential for the s1/2 states.

## Key numbers

- The OES parameter from the previous Ozawa data was γ3 = 63 ± 32 mb, plotted at Sn = 2.74 ± 0.120 MeV for 23O.
- The OES parameter from the new Kanungo data (for 22,23O) combined with the previous Ozawa data (for 24O) was γ3 = 4.5 ± 50.0 mb.
- The theoretical OES parameter at Sn ≈ 2.74 MeV is around 2.4 mb.
- The neutron separation energy for 23O is Sn = 2.74 ± 0.120 MeV.
- The experimental interaction cross sections for 22,23O were measured at 900 MeV/nucleon.
- The theoretical calculations were performed for incident energies of E = 1000 MeV/nucleon and E = 240 MeV/nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Hagino and Sagawa, 2011) which argued that large OES in reaction cross sections can be attributed to pairing correlations, and which had shown consistency for the 30,31,32Ne system with Sn = 0.29 ± 1.64 MeV for 31Ne. The new Kanungo data for 22,23O resolve a discrepancy with the earlier Ozawa measurement (Ozawa, 2001) that had suggested an anomalously large cross section for 23O. The results are now consistent with ab initio coupled-cluster theory predictions and a simple 22O+n description for 23O, together pointing to the absence of a halo structure in 23O. The weak energy dependence of the OES parameter demonstrated here enables its use as a robust tool for studying pairing correlations in weakly bound nuclei.
