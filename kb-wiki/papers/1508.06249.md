# Dilepton rate and quark number susceptibility with the Gribov action

> We use a recently obtained resummed quark propagator at finite temperature which takes into account both the chromoelectric scale gT and the chromomagnetic scale g^2T through the Gribov action. The electric scale generates two massive modes whereas the magnetic scale produces a new massless spacelike mode in the medium. Moreover, the non-perturbative quark propagator is found to contain no discontinuity in contrast to the standard perturbative hard thermal loop approach. Using this non-perturbative quark propagator and self-consistent vertices, we compute the non-perturbative dilepton rate at vanishing three-momentum at one-loop order. The resulting rate has a rich structure at low energies due to the inclusion of the non-perturbative magnetic scale. We also calculate the quark number susceptibility, which is related to the conserved quark number density fluctuation in the deconfined state. Both the dilepton rate and quark number susceptibility are compared with results from lattice quantum chromodynamics and the standard hard thermal loop approach. Finally, we discuss how the absence of a discontinuity in the imaginary part of the non-perturbative quark propagator makes the results for both dilepton production and quark number susceptibility dramatically different from those in perturbative approaches and seemingly in conflict with known lattice data.

## Key claim

Using a non-perturbative quark propagator derived from the Gribov-Zwanziger action that incorporates both the chromoelectric scale \(gT\) and the chromomagnetic scale \(g^2T\), the authors compute the dilepton production rate and quark number susceptibility at one-loop order, finding that the absence of a discontinuity in the quark propagator's imaginary part (and thus the absence of a Landau cut) produces results that are "dramatically different" from perturbative hard-thermal-loop approaches and "seemingly in conflict with known lattice data."

## Method

The authors use a resummed quark propagator obtained from the Gribov-Zwanziger action, which modifies the gluon propagator by introducing a Gribov parameter \(\gamma_G\) that regulates infrared behavior and effectively confines gluons. They compute the quark self-energy in the high-temperature limit using this modified gluon propagator, then construct self-consistent quark-gluon vertices satisfying Slavnov-Taylor identities. Using these non-perturbative propagators and vertices, they evaluate the one-loop photon self-energy at zero three-momentum and apply the Braaten-Pisarski-Yuan prescription to obtain the dilepton production rate; they also compute the quark number susceptibility from the same quark propagator.

## Key numbers

* The Gribov parameter at asymptotically high temperatures is given by \(\gamma_G = \frac{D-1}{D}\frac{N_c}{4\sqrt{2}\pi}g^2T\), where \(D\) is the spacetime dimension and \(N_c=3\) is the number of colors.
* The one-loop running coupling is \(g^2(T) = \frac{48\pi^2}{(33-2N_f)\ln(Q_0^2/\Lambda_0^2)}\), with \(\Lambda_0 = 176\) MeV fixed by requiring \(\alpha_s(1.5\ \text{GeV}) = 0.326\).
* The modified thermal quark mass in the presence of the Gribov term is \(m_q^2(\gamma_G) = \frac{g^2C_F}{4\pi^2}\sum_\pm\int_0^\infty dk\, \frac{k^2}{E_\pm^0}\, \tilde{n}_\pm(k,\gamma_G)\), where \(C_F = 4/3\).
* The dilepton production rate at zero three-momentum is given by Eq. (24) in the paper, with contributions from five distinct processes: \(q_+\bar{q}_+\rightarrow\gamma^*\), \(q_-\bar{q}_-\rightarrow\gamma^*\), \(q_G\bar{q}_G\rightarrow\gamma^*\), \(q_+\bar{q}_-\rightarrow\gamma^*\), and \(q_+\rightarrow q_-\gamma^*\).
* The quark number susceptibility is computed and compared with lattice QCD results and standard hard-thermal-loop approach results (specific numerical values for the susceptibility are not provided in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends the Gribov-Zwanziger approach to finite-temperature QCD, building on prior studies of quark propagation in a deconfined medium that included both electric and magnetic mass effects (Nansu et al., 2015). The results contrast with standard hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (Braaten, Pisarski, and Yuan, 1990) which predicts a Landau cut contribution and van Hove singularities with an energy gap, and also differ from lattice QCD calculations of the dilepton rate (Ding et al., 2011; Laermann et al., 2010) and quark number susceptibility (Borsanyi et al., 2012; Bazavov et al., 2012). The new massless spacelike mode arising from the magnetic scale and the complete absence of the Landau cut make the predictions "seemingly in conflict with known lattice data," suggesting that the Gribov action may oversuppress the non-perturbative physics responsible for the Landau cut in a high-temperature quark-gluon plasma.
