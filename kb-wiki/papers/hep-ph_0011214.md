# Can the Lambda pi scattering phase shifts be large?

> Motivated by the presence of nearby thresholds in other baryon-meson channels with I=1 and S=-1, we investigate whether the Lambda pi scattering phase shifts at a center-of-mass energy equal to the Xi mass could be larger than suggested by lowest-order chiral perturbation theory. Within a coupled-channel K-matrix approach, we find that the S-wave phase shift could be as large as -7 degrees.

# Wiki Page: Can the Lambda pi scattering phase shifts be large?

## Key claim
The S-wave Lambda-pi scattering phase shift at a center-of-mass energy equal to the Xi mass could be as large as -7 degrees, contrary to the vanishing or near-zero result from leading-order chiral perturbation theory, but only if coupled-channel effects from nearby thresholds (particularly the Sigma-pi channel) are included via a K-matrix approach.

## Method
The authors used a coupled-channel K-matrix formalism incorporating five channels with strangeness S=-1 and isospin I=1 (Lambda-pi, Sigma-pi, N-anti-K, Sigma-eta, and Xi-K). For the K-matrix elements, they employed the leading-order chiral perturbation theory amplitudes derived from the heavy-baryon Lagrangian including decuplet-baryon contributions. They also performed a separate estimate using next-to-leading-order (O(p^2)) heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory with coupling constants taken from the model of Kaiser and Weise (1998).

## Key numbers
- S-wave Lambda-pi phase shift at sqrt(s) = m_Xi: delta_S = -7.3 degrees (from the K-matrix approach with all four inelastic channels included)
- S-wave phase shift reduced to delta_S = -2.8 degrees when the heavier Sigma-eta and Xi-K channels are dropped
- S-wave phase shift delta_S = -3.6 degrees when only the Xi-K channel is dropped
- From the O(p^2) heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory estimate with central parameter values: delta_S = -2.5 degrees, delta_P = -3.3 degrees
- From the O(p^2) heavy-baryon estimate with parameter ranges: -3.0 degrees < delta_S < +0.4 degrees, -3.5 degrees < delta_P < -1.2 degrees
- Leading-order heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (including decuplet): delta_S = 0 degrees, delta_P = -1.5 degrees (with D=0.8, C=1.7)
- Relativistic leading-order calculation (redone by authors): delta_S = -1.2 degrees, delta_P = -1.7 degrees
- Pion momentum at the Xi mass: |k| = 0.137 GeV
- Pion decay constant: f_pi = 92.4 MeV

## Context
This work addresses a discrepancy between an early calculation by Nath and Kumar (1971) which found a large S-wave phase shift of -18.7 degrees, and later leading-order chiral perturbation theory calculations by Lu, Savage, and Wise (1994) which gave delta_S = 0 degrees. The authors extend the analysis of Donoghue and Pakvasa (1996) who considered only the Sigma(1750) resonance and found contributions to delta_S of at most 0.5 degrees. By including coupled-channel effects from the nearby Sigma-pi threshold (only 10 MeV above the Xi mass), the authors find significantly larger phase shifts than those from lowest-order chiral perturbation theory, which is important for interpreting CP-violation measurements in the HyperCP (E871) experiment at Fermilab.
