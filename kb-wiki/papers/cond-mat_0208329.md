# SO(5) as a Critical Dynamical Symmetry in the SU(4) Model of High-Temperature Superconductivity

> An SU(4) model of high-temperature superconductivity and antiferromagnetism has recently been proposed. The SO(5) group employed by Zhang is embedded in this SU(4) as a subgroup, suggesting a connection between our SU(4) model and the Zhang SO(5) model. In order to understand the relationship between the the two models, we have used generalized coherent states to analyze the nature of the SO(5) subgroup. By constructing coherent-state energy surfaces, we demonstrate explicitly that the SU(4) supset SO(5) symmetry can be interpreted as a critical dynamical symmetry interpolating between superconducting and antiferromagnetic phases, and that this critical dynamical symmetry has many similarities to critical dynamical symmetries identified previously in other fields of physics. More generally, we demonstrate with this example that the mathematical techniques associated with generalized coherent states may have powerful applications in condensed matter physics because they provide a clear connection between microscopic many-body theories and their broken-symmetry approximate solutions. In addition, these methods may be interpreted as defining the most general Bogoliubov transformation subject to a Lie group symmetry constraint, thus providing a mathematical connection between algebraic formulations and the language of quasiparticle theory. Finally, we suggest that the identification of the SO(5) symmetry as a critical dynamical symmetry implies deep algebraic connections between high-temperature superconductors and seemingly unrelated phenomena in other field of physics.

## Key claim

The SO(5) subgroup embedded in the SU(4) model can be interpreted as a critical dynamical symmetry that interpolates between superconducting and antiferromagnetic phases as the hole doping parameter is varied, but the paper demonstrates that an exact SO(5) symmetry cannot account for the detailed phenomenology of cuprate superconductors and must be broken explicitly (not spontaneously) in a particular way to recover Mott insulator normal states at half filling.

## Method

The authors used generalized coherent state methods, constructing coherent-state energy surfaces for the SU(4) model by mapping the algebra onto a 4-dimensional matrix representation and applying a symmetry-constrained Bogoliubov transformation. They parameterized the most general Hamiltonian for the SU(4) model in terms of effective interaction strengths and analyzed the energy surfaces in various dynamical symmetry limits corresponding to the subgroup chains SU(4) ⊃ SO(5), SU(4) ⊃ SO(4), and SU(4) ⊃ SU(2).

## Key numbers

- The collective subspace is associated with SO(6) irreps of the form (σ₁, σ₂, σ₃) = (Ω/2, 0, 0), where Ω/2 is the electron-pair degeneracy.
- The expectation value of the SU(4) Casimir operator in this collective subspace is Ω/2 (Ω/2 + 4).
- The particle number n is related to variational parameters by n = Ω(v₊² + v₋²) = 2Ω(α² + β²), with ranges 0 ≤ β ≤ √(n/4Ω) and √(n/4Ω) ≤ α ≤ √(n/2Ω).
- The staggered magnetization Q = ⟨Q_z⟩ = ½Ω(v₊² − v₋²) = 2Ωβ(n/(2Ω) − β²)^(1/2).

## Context

This work extends the SU(4) model of high-temperature superconductivity and antiferromagnetism proposed by Guidry (1999), which embeds the SO(5) group employed by Zhang (1997) as a subgroup. The paper introduces generalized coherent state techniques and the concept of critical dynamical symmetries from nuclear structure physics (Wu 1994, IBM) and molecular physics (Ia 1995, Ia 1999) into condensed matter physics, suggesting deep algebraic connections between high-temperature superconductors and phenomena in other fields such as the SO(7) subgroup in the SO(8) fermion dynamical symmetry model of nuclear structure.
