# Quadrupole Contribution to Two Neutron Removal in Heavy Ion Collisions

> In this report, electric quadrupole corrections to the two neutron removal cross section measured in heavy ion collisions are estimated for $^{197}$Au and $^{59}$Co targets. The quadrupole process is assumed to proceed primarily through excitation of the giant isovector quadrupole resonance, which then decays by neutron emission. For $^{59}$Co, the contribution from E2 radiation is found to be small, while for $^{197}$Au we find the quadrupole contribution resolves the discrepancy between experiment and the simple predictions of the Weissacker-Williams virtual photon method.

## Key claim

Electric quadrupole (E2) corrections to two-neutron removal cross sections in heavy-ion collisions, proceeding primarily through excitation of the isovector giant quadrupole resonance (IVGQR), resolve the discrepancy between experiment and the simple Weissacker-Williams virtual photon method for \({}^{197}\)Au targets, but the contribution is small for \({}^{59}\)Co targets.

## Method

The author modeled the quadrupole photo-cross section as a Lorentzian resonance (the IVGQR) multiplied by a threshold factor for two-neutron decay, using parameters from photonuclear data and statistical model calculations. The electromagnetic dissociation cross section was computed by folding equivalent photon fluxes for the E1 and E2 multipoles over the photo-cross sections, using experimental photoneutron cross sections for the E1 part and the modeled IVGQR for the E2 part. The nuclear contribution was subtracted from measured total cross sections using factorization to extract the electromagnetic component.

## Key numbers

- For \({}^{197}\)Au, the IVGQR resonance energy is \(E_{IVGQR} = 23\) MeV, width \(\Gamma = 7\) MeV, and the product of sum-rule fraction and two-neutron branching ratio is \(f \approx 0.8\).
- For \({}^{59}\)Co, using parameters from \({}^{58}\)Ni, \(f \approx 0.1\), \(E_{IVGQR} = 29\) MeV, \(\Gamma = 9\) MeV.
- The two-neutron threshold for \({}^{197}\)Au is 15 MeV, while the giant dipole resonance energy is 13.8 MeV.
- The threshold parameter \(\Lambda\) is roughly 2 MeV for both targets.
- For the Au target, the E2 correction increases the electromagnetic dissociation cross section by roughly one-third for all projectiles.

## Context

This work extends the Weissacker-Williams virtual photon method for electromagnetic dissociation by including quadrupole excitations, following earlier studies that resolved discrepancies in single-neutron removal through careful choice of impact parameter (Author, 1992), inclusion of quadrupole excitations (Author, 1992), and Rutherford bending (Author, 1992). It directly addresses the two-neutron removal systematics measured by the Iowa State group (Author, 1992) and contrasts with the approach of Norbury (1992), who attributed discrepancies to underestimated experimental uncertainties. The results enable more reliable extraction of low-energy nuclear cross sections of astrophysical interest from heavy-ion dissociation experiments and improve estimates of electromagnetic dissociation backgrounds at RHIC.
