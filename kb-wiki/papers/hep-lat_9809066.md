# The Pion-Nucleon sigma-Term with Dynamical Wilson Fermions

> We calculate connected and disconnected contributions to the flavour singlet scalar density amplitude of the nucleon in a full QCD lattice simulation with $n_f=2$ dynamical Wilson fermions at $β=5.6$ on a $16^3 \times 32$ lattice. We find that both contributions are of similar size at the light quark mass. We arrive at the estimate $σ_{πN} = 18(5)$MeV. Its smallness is directly related to the apparent decrease of $u$, $d$ quark masses when unquenching QCD lattice simulations. The $y$ parameter can be estimated from a semi-quenched analysis, in which there are no strange quarks in the sea, the result being $y=0.59(13)$.}

# The Pion-Nucleon sigma-Term with Dynamical Wilson Fermions

## Key claim
The pion-nucleon sigma-term is estimated to be 18(5) MeV, which is substantially smaller than the experimental value of approximately 45 MeV. This smallness is directly attributed to the apparent decrease of the light quark masses when unquenching QCD lattice simulations, though the authors note that a scaling analysis at multiple lattice cutoffs is needed to determine whether this is a finite cutoff effect.

## Method
The SESAM collaboration performed a full QCD lattice simulation with n_f=2 dynamical Wilson fermions at beta=5.6 on a 16^3 x 32 lattice, corresponding to a lattice cutoff of approximately 2.3 GeV and spatial length of about 1.4 fm. They generated 200 statistically independent gauge configurations at each of four sea quark masses, with pion-to-rho mass ratios ranging from 0.833(3) to 0.686(11). Connected contributions were extracted using both the global summation method and the plateau density method, while disconnected contributions were computed using the plateau accumulation method (PAM) with stochastic estimator techniques employing complex Z_2 noise with 100 estimates per configuration.

## Key numbers
- sigma_{piN} = 18(5) MeV, obtained from the product of the lattice cutoff a_rho^{-1} = 2.30 GeV, the light quark mass m_ud = 0.000901(54) in lattice units, and the total scalar density amplitude C_u + C_d + 2D_q = 8.82(2.52)
- Ratio of disconnected to connected contributions at the light quark mass: R_{d/c} = 1.26(57)
- y parameter from semi-quenched analysis (no strange quarks in the sea): y = 0.59(13)
- Light quark mass in physical units: m_ud = 2.7(2) MeV, obtained using tadpole improved renormalization with alpha_MSbar(1/a) = 0.215
- Connected amplitudes at the light quark mass (kappa_light = 0.158462): C_u = 2.40(19), C_d = 1.53(15), 2D_q = 4.95(2.36) (all in lattice units, unrenormalised)

## Context
This work extends previous quenched lattice studies of the pion-nucleon sigma-term by Fukugita et al. (1995) and Dong et al. (1996), which found values consistent with the experimental estimate of approximately 45 MeV and a disconnected-to-connected ratio of about 2:1. The present full QCD result of 18(5) MeV is significantly lower than both the experimental value from Gasser, Leutwyler and Sainio (1991) and the quenched estimates, suggesting a dramatic unquenching effect primarily driven by the reduction of the light quark mass by roughly a factor of two compared to quenched estimates at equal cutoff. The authors note that preliminary results from the CP-PACS collaboration at multiple cutoffs hint at a small light quark mass in the continuum limit, supporting their finding, but emphasize that a full scaling analysis and a quenched reference simulation at equal lattice spacing are needed to consolidate the conclusion.
