# Hadronic Transport Models

> Hadronic transport models may be utilized to extract bulk nuclear properties. Deduction of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections and of nuclear viscosity is discussed, as well as the extraction of momentum dependence of nucleonic mean field. Moreover, determination of the nuclear incompressibility and of constraints on the nuclear pressure at supranormal densities is described.

# Hadronic Transport Models

## Key claim

Hadronic transport models can be used to extract bulk nuclear properties from heavy-ion collision data, but the extraction requires identifying observables that are primarily sensitive to a single uncertain nuclear property, as competing physical effects must be disentangled.

## Method

The Boltzmann equation for particle phase-space distributions is solved numerically for heavy-ion collisions, with the single-particle energies and collision integrals treated as adjustable inputs. By comparing simulated stopping observables (linear momentum transfer, ERAT), elliptic flow coefficients, and giant monopole resonance energies to experimental data from the Stony Brook group, FOPI Collaboration, KaoS Collaboration, and Youngblood et al., the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections, momentum dependence of the mean field, nuclear incompressibility, and pressure at supranormal densities are inferred.

## Key numbers

- In-medium cross sections in lower-energy reactions are reduced by approximately 25% compared to free-space cross sections, as inferred from stopping observables in Ar + Ag and Au + Au collisions.
- Nuclear viscosity in the reactions studied is higher by about 25% compared to that expected on the basis of free cross sections.
- The momentum dependence of the nucleonic mean field at normal density is characterized by an effective mass ratio m*/m ~ 0.70, favored by Bi + Bi collision data at 400 MeV/nucleon from the KaoS Collaboration.
- The nuclear incompressibility is determined to be K = 225 ± 15 MeV from giant monopole resonance measurements in spherical nuclei by Youngblood et al.
- Constraints on nuclear pressure are placed within the density range 2 < ρ/ρ₀ < 5, eliminating extreme models such as the relativistic NL3 model and models assuming a phase transition at relatively low densities.

## Context

This work extends the program of extracting nuclear matter properties from heavy-ion collisions by systematically addressing multiple uncertainties simultaneously. The stopping analysis builds on earlier work by the Stony Brook group (1980s) and the Rostock group on in-medium cross sections, while the mean-field momentum dependence is compared to Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations with the Bonn-A interaction. The giant monopole resonance analysis extends the measurements of Youngblood et al. (1999) and the flow constraints follow the approach of Danielewicz et al. (2002) using spectator timing. The results enable more reliable extraction of the nuclear equation of state and elimination of extreme theoretical models.
