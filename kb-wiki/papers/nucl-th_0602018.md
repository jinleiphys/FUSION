# Phase diagram of neutral quark matter at moderate densities

> We discuss the phase diagram of moderately dense, locally neutral three-flavor quark matter using the framework of an effective model of quantum chromodynamics with a local interaction. The phase diagrams in the plane of temperature and quark chemical potential as well as in the plane of temperature and lepton-number chemical potential are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper maps the phase diagram of locally neutral three-flavor quark matter using an NJL model with dynamically generated quark masses, finding that the ground state at moderate densities and low temperatures is the gapless CFL (gCFL) phase rather than the 2SC phase previously suggested in the literature, but only within a specific range of the diquark coupling strength and model parameters.

## Method

The authors use a three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a local four-fermion interaction, a diquark interaction in the color-antitriplet channel, and a 't Hooft determinant term. They solve the mean-field gap equations for the diquark condensates (Delta_1, Delta_2, Delta_3) and the quark-antiquark condensates (sigma_u, sigma_d, sigma_s) together with the local neutrality conditions for electric and color charges, then compare the pressure of eight candidate phases (normal, 2SC, 2SC_us, 2SC_ds, uSC, dSC, sSC, and CFL) to identify the ground state at each temperature and quark chemical potential.

## Key numbers

* The chiral phase transition first-order line lies in the range 336 MeV less than or similar to mu less than or similar to 368 MeV, with a critical endpoint at (T_cr, mu_cr) approximately (56, 336) MeV.
* At T = 0, the gCFL phase becomes the ground state at mu_gCFL approximately 443 MeV, and the regular CFL phase appears at mu_CFL approximately 457 MeV.
* The vacuum constituent quark masses are M_u = M_d = 367.7 MeV and M_s = 549.5 MeV, using current masses m_u = m_d = 5.5 MeV, m_s = 140.7 MeV, a cutoff Lambda = 602.3 MeV, and couplings G_S Lambda^2 = 1.835, K Lambda^5 = 12.36.
* The diquark coupling is set to G_D = (3/4) G_S, following from the Fierz identity.
* The uSC phase appears at nonzero temperature in the interval 5 less than or similar to T less than or similar to 10 MeV for mu approximately 444 MeV, and the g2SC phase appears for T greater than about 11 MeV at mu approximately 365 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the phase diagram studies of Ruester et al. (2005) and Shovkovy et al. (2005) by treating quark masses dynamically rather than as free parameters, and by including the effect of a nonzero lepton-number chemical potential relevant for protoneutron stars. It corrects the earlier claim by Ruester et al. (2005) that a 2SC phase exists near mu approximately 450 MeV at T = 0, showing instead that the gCFL phase is the ground state there. The results enable future studies of mixed phases and crystalline color-superconducting phases in compact star interiors, and provide a baseline for comparing with lattice QCD at nonzero chemical potential.
