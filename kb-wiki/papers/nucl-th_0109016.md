# What determines the $K^-$ multiplicity at energies around 1-2 AGeV?

> In heavy ion reactions at energies around 1-2 AGeV the measured $K^-$ yields appear rather high as compared to pp collisions as shown by the KaoS collaboration. Employing IQMD simulations, we show that this is caused by the fact that the dominant production channel is not $BB\to BBK^+K^-$ but the mesonic $Λ(Σ) π\to K^-B$ reaction. Because the $Λ$ ($Σ$) stem from the reaction $BB \to Λ(Σ) K^+ B$, the $K^+$ and the $K^-$ yield are strongly correlated, i.e. the $K^-/K^+$ ratio occurs to be nearly independent of the impact parameter as found experimentally. $K^-$ are continuously produced but also very quickly reabsorbed leading to an almost identical rate for production and reabsorption. The final $K^-$ yield is strongly influenced by the $K^+N$ (due to their production via the $Λ(Σ)$) but very little by the $K^-$N potential.

## Key claim

The observed high $K^-$ yields in heavy-ion reactions at 1–2 AGeV (compared to pp collisions) are explained by the dominance of the mesonic $\Lambda(\Sigma)\pi \to K^-B$ channel over the $BB\to BBK^+K^-$ pair-production channel, with the $K^-/K^+$ ratio remaining nearly independent of impact parameter due to the strong correlation between $K^-$ production and the $\Lambda$ (produced alongside $K^+$), not necessarily due to thermal equilibrium.

## Method

The authors performed Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) simulations for Au+Au and C+C collisions at 1.5 AGeV incident energy, incorporating a density-dependent $KN$ potential from relativistic mean field calculations and parametrizations of elementary kaon production and absorption cross sections. Kaons were treated perturbatively, and the $\Lambda$ and $\Sigma$ were combined into a single particle type. The time evolution of production and absorption rates for different channels was tracked, and the impact parameter dependence of the $K^-/K^+$ ratio was studied by varying the effective cross sections in the $\pi\Lambda$ channel by a multiplication factor $n$.

## Key numbers

- For central Au+Au collisions at 1.5 AGeV, more than 90% of $K^-$ produced via the $BB$ and $\pi B$ channels are reabsorbed, leaving essentially only $K^-$ from the $\pi\Lambda$ channel.
- In C+C collisions at 1.5 AGeV, about 50% of $K^-$ survive; in Au+Au collisions at the same energy, less than 20% survive.
- Changing the $\pi\Lambda$ cross section by a factor of two changes the $K^-$ number by 56% in Au+Au and 42% in C+C (not by a factor of two).
- The $K^-/K^+$ ratio remains nearly constant for impact parameters smaller than 8 fm in Au+Au collisions at 1.5 AGeV.
- The $K^-$ yield depends strongly on the $K^+N$ potential (which affects $\Lambda$ production at around twice nuclear matter density) but very little on the $K^-N$ potential (tested at low densities).

## Context

This work extends the experimental findings of the KaoS collaboration (Barth et al., 1997; Menzel et al., 2000) showing high $K^-$ yields and impact-parameter-independent $K^-/K^+$ ratios in Ni+Ni reactions at 1.0 and 1.8 AGeV, and similar observations at AGS energies (Ahle et al., 1998). It builds on prior suggestions by Cassing et al. and Koli et al. that $K^-$ can be produced via the strangeness-exchange channel $\pi\Lambda \to K^-B$, and on thermal model predictions by Cleymans et al. (2000) that a constant $K^-/K^+$ ratio arises from chemical equilibrium. The simulations show instead that the system is not in thermal equilibrium but in a steady state where production and absorption rates are nearly equal, and that the final $K^-$ yield is determined by dynamical quantities (cross sections and the coupling to $K^+$ production) rather than equilibrium thermodynamics. This enables future studies of $KN$ potentials at different densities: the $K^+N$ potential is probed at high density (around twice nuclear matter density) while the $K^-N$ potential is tested only at low densities.
