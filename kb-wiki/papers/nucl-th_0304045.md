# Quark Coalescence and Elliptic Flow of Charm Hadrons

> Elliptic flow of charm hadrons is investigated based on the quark coalescence model. Due to the large difference between the charm quark and light quark masses, hadrons containing both light and charm quarks show a qualitatively different $v_2(p_\perp)$ from hadrons containing only light quarks. Simple relations are proposed to infer quark elliptic flow from those of hadrons. The effects of the finite momentum spread of hadron wavefunctions are also studied, and are found to be small for charm hadrons.

# Quark Coalescence and Elliptic Flow of Charm Hadrons

## Key claim

Charm hadrons containing both light and charm quarks show a qualitatively different elliptic flow $v_2(p_\perp)$ from hadrons containing only light quarks, with a much slower increase and saturation at a much higher momentum scale; however, these predictions assume the quark coalescence formalism is valid and that the finite momentum spread of hadron wavefunctions is small, which the authors find to be a good approximation for charm hadrons but not for light mesons like pions.

## Method

The authors extend the quark coalescence formalism to hadrons with constituent quarks of unequal masses, deriving relations between hadron and parton elliptic flow that account for the asymmetric momentum sharing between heavy and light quarks. They model the hadron wavefunctions using a valon-model form with exponents determined from the constituent quark masses and QCD scale, then compute elliptic flow for various charm hadrons ($D$ mesons, $\Lambda_c$ baryons, $J/\psi$) using parameterized light quark and charm quark transverse momentum distributions and elliptic flow inputs from parton transport simulations (MPC). The effect of finite momentum spread in hadron wavefunctions is included via weight functions that integrate over the quark momentum fraction distributions.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses used: $m_u = m_d = 0.3$ GeV, $m_s = 0.5$ GeV, $m_c = 1.5$ GeV; QCD scale $\Lambda_{\rm QCD} = 0.2$ GeV
- Light quark elliptic flow parameterization: $v_{2,q}(p_\perp) = v_2^{\rm max} \tanh(p_\perp/p_0)$ with $v_2^{\rm max} = 0.05$ and $p_0 = 0.75$ GeV, based on MPC parton transport simulations
- At $p_\perp = 6$ GeV, the elliptic flow reduction from the narrow-wavefunction limit due to momentum spread is: 36% for pions, 32% for kaons, 11% (24%) for $D$ mesons with (without) charm flow, 9% for protons, 10% for $\Lambda$, 5% for $\Xi$, 10% (17%) for $\Lambda_c$ with (without) charm flow
- Momentum spread effect is negligible for massive hadrons with similar-mass quarks: 0.8% reduction for $\phi$, 0.01% for $J/\psi$, 1% for $\Omega$
- Wavefunction exponent values for $D$ mesons: $(a,b) = (4.9, 28)$; for $\Lambda_c$ baryons: $(a,b,d) = (7.2, 7.2, 40)$
- Light quark transverse momentum distribution parameterization: $h_i(p_\perp) \propto e^{-p_\perp/T} + c_H/(1+p_\perp/\Lambda_H)^w$ with $T = 0.18$ GeV, $c_H = 0.36$, $\Lambda_H = 1.3$ GeV, $w = 8.3$
- Charm quark spectrum from PYTHIA at $\sqrt{s} = 200$ GeV: $h_c(p_\perp) \propto (p_\perp + 0.5\ {\rm GeV})^2 / (1 + p_\perp/6.8\ {\rm GeV})^{21}$

## Context

This work extends the quark coalescence formalism previously applied to light-flavor hadrons at RHIC (Lin 2002, Voloshin 2002, Molnar 2003) to heavy-flavor charm hadrons, addressing the controversy raised by Batsouli (2002) that both perturbative QCD charm production without final-state interactions and complete thermal equilibrium for charm hadrons were consistent with PHENIX single-electron data. The proposed simple relations to unfold quark elliptic flow from hadron measurements, combined with the predicted slow increase of charm hadron $v_2(p_\perp)$, provide a way to distinguish between these scenarios using upcoming experimental data from RHIC and LHC. The study does not include independent fragmentation effects that dominate at very high $p_\perp$ (Fries 2003, Greco 2003, Molnar 2003) or medium effects such as charm quark energy loss.
