# First observation of a nuclear $s$-state of $Ξ$ hypernucleus, $^{15}_Ξ{\rm C}$

> Bound-systems of $Ξ^-$--$^{14}_{}{\rm N}$ are studied via $Ξ^-$ capture at rest followed by emission of a twin single-$Λ$ hypernucleus in the emulsion detectors. Two events forming extremely deep $Ξ^-$ bound states were obtained by analysis of a hybrid method in the E07 experiment at J-PARC and reanalysis of the E373 experiment at KEK-PS. The decay mode of one event was assigned as $Ξ^-+^{14}_{}{\rm N}\to^{5}_Λ{\rm He}$+$^{5}_Λ{\rm He}$+$^{4}_{}{\rm He}$+n. Since there are no excited states for daughter particles, the binding energy of the $Ξ^-$ hyperon, $B_{Ξ^-}$, in $^{14}_{}{\rm N}$ nucleus was uniquely determined to be 6.27 $\pm$ 0.27 MeV. Another $Ξ^-$--$^{14}_{}{\rm N}$ system via the decay $^{9}_Λ{\rm Be}$ + $^{5}_Λ{\rm He}$ + n brings a $B_{Ξ^-}$ value, 8.00 $\pm$ 0.77 MeV or 4.96 $\pm$ 0.77 MeV, where the two possible values of $B_{Ξ^-}$ correspond to the ground and the excited states of the daughter $^{9}_Λ{\rm Be}$ nucleus, respectively. Because the $B_{Ξ^-}$ values are larger than those of the previously reported events (KISO and IBUKI), which are both interpreted as the nuclear $1p$ state of the $Ξ^-$--$^{14}_{}{\rm N}$ system, these new events give the first indication of the nuclear $1s$ state of the $Ξ$ hypernucleus, $^{15}_Ξ{\rm C}$.

## Key claim
Two events of the $\Xi^-$--$^{14}{\rm N}$ system exhibit binding energies ($B_{\Xi^-}$) of 6.27 $\pm$ 0.27 MeV and either 8.00 $\pm$ 0.77 MeV or 4.96 $\pm$ 0.77 MeV, which are larger than those of the previously reported KISO and IBUKI events (interpreted as nuclear $1p$ states), providing the first indication of a nuclear $1s$ state of the $\Xi$ hypernucleus $^{15}_{\Xi}{\rm C}$.

## Method
$\Xi^-$ hyperons produced via the quasi-free $p(K^-,K^+)\Xi^-$ reaction were stopped in nuclear emulsion detectors in the J-PARC E07 and KEK-PS E373 experiments. Twin single-$\Lambda$ hypernuclei emitted from $\Xi^-$ capture at rest were identified by track-following and optical microscopy; the ranges and angles of all charged particles were measured, and the binding energy $B_{\Xi^-}$ was reconstructed from the invariant mass of the final state using conservation of momentum and mass-energy, accounting for possible neutron emission and excited states of daughter hypernuclei.

## Key numbers
- $B_{\Xi^-}$ = 6.27 $\pm$ 0.27 MeV for the event assigned as $\Xi^- + ^{14}{\rm N} \to ^{5}_{\Lambda}{\rm He} + ^{5}_{\Lambda}{\rm He} + ^{4}{\rm He} + n$, with no excited states in the daughters.
- $B_{\Xi^-}$ = 8.00 $\pm$ 0.77 MeV (ground state of $^{9}_{\Lambda}{\rm Be}$) or 4.96 $\pm$ 0.77 MeV (excited state of $^{9}_{\Lambda}{\rm Be}$ at 3.04 $\pm$ 0.02 MeV) for the event $\Xi^- + ^{14}{\rm N} \to ^{9}_{\Lambda}{\rm Be} + ^{5}_{\Lambda}{\rm He} + n$.
- For comparison, the KISO event gave $B_{\Xi^-}$ = 3.87 $\pm$ 0.21 or 1.03 $\pm$ 0.18 MeV, and the IBUKI event gave $B_{\Xi^-}$ = 1.27 $\pm$ 0.21 MeV, both interpreted as $1p$ states.

## Context
This work extends the study of $\Xi$ hypernuclei from the earlier KISO event (Nakazawa 2015) and IBUKI event (Hayakawa 2020), which were both interpreted as nuclear $1p$ states of the $\Xi^-$--$^{14}{\rm N}$ system with $B_{\Xi^-}$ values around 1--4 MeV. The deeper binding observed here (6--8 MeV) provides the first experimental evidence for a $1s$ orbital state of the $\Xi$ hyperon in a nucleus, enabling direct tests of $\Xi N$ interaction models such as the Ehime model, relativistic mean field theory, Skyrme-Hartree-Fock, quark-meson coupling, and lattice QCD calculations near the physical point (Sasaki 2019).
