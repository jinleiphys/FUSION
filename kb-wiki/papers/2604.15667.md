# sPHENIX measurement of Open-Charm Baryon-to-Meson Ratios in $p$+$p$ collisions at RHIC

> sPHENIX is a state-of-the-art experiment at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC), dedicated to the study of heavy-flavor and jet physics. Its precision tracking system, combined with streaming readout, enables heavy-flavor measurements with high-statistics and essentially unbiased data samples. During the 2024 run, sPHENIX was fully commissioned and recorded a sample of 100 billion unbiased $p$+$p$ collisions, together with a minimum-bias Au+Au dataset. The 2025 run further expanded the sPHENIX dataset with high-statistics $p$+$p$, O+O and Au+Au collisions. This extensive $p$+$p$ sample opens the door to heavy-flavor measurements with orders of magnitude more statistics than previously available at RHIC. Notably, there has been no prior measurement of the $Λ_c^+ / D^0$ baseline in $p$+$p$ collisions at RHIC energies. The large sPHENIX dataset now enables the first exploration of key open questions, such as the hadronization mechanism of baryons and the strange-to-light flavor meson ratio.

## Key claim
The sPHENIX experiment has recorded the first observations of $\Lambda_c^+$ and $D^+$ hadrons in $p$+$p$ collisions at RHIC, enabling the first measurement of the $\Lambda_c^+/D^0$ baseline at RHIC energies, though the results presented are based on only a small subset of the tracking data (roughly one hour of data taking) and are still limited by current understanding of silicon alignment and TPC distortion corrections.

## Method
The sPHENIX detector used its precision tracking system (MVTX, INTT, TPC, TPOT) with streaming readout to record unbiased $p$+$p$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV. Open-charm hadrons were reconstructed through their hadronic decay channels ($\Lambda_c^+ \to pK^-\pi^+$, $D^0 \to K^-\pi^+$, $D^+ \to K^-\pi^+\pi^+$) using the KFParticle package, with combinatorial background suppression from displaced secondary vertex requirements and TPC dE/dx information.

## Key numbers
- 100 billion unbiased $p$+$p$ events recorded during Run 24, corresponding to an integrated luminosity of 2.9 pb$^{-1}$
- Tracking detectors recorded unbiased $p$+$p$ collisions at a rate of approximately 200 kHz, about 20-50 times higher than achievable with a purely minimum-bias trigger
- The MVTX vertex detector provides primary vertex resolutions less than 10 microns
- The INTT has timing resolution sufficient to resolve individual 106.5 ns RHIC bunch crossings
- In 2025, sPHENIX collected Au+Au collisions with 6.6 nb$^{-1}$, streaming $p$+$p$ collisions with 9.7 pb$^{-1}$, and streaming O+O collisions with 8.9 nb$^{-1}$

## Context
This work extends the heavy-flavor program at RHIC, where the STAR experiment (STAR, 2019) measured the $\Lambda_c^+/D^0$ ratio in Au+Au collisions but no corresponding $p$+$p$ baseline existed at RHIC energies. The measurements complement LHC results from CMS (CMS, 2019) and ALICE (ALICE, 2020; ALICE, 2023) that showed the $\Lambda_c^+/D^0$ ratio in $p$+$p$ collisions is significantly larger than expectations from fragmentation functions extracted from $e^+e^-$ and $e^-p$ data. The results will enable tests of proposed hadronization mechanisms including color reconnection (Christiansen, 2015), statistical hadronization (He, 2019), and coalescence (Plumari, 2017), and will provide a crucial baseline for interpreting heavy-ion results at RHIC.
