# Two-channel approach to the average retarding force of metals for slow singly ionized projectiles

> Based on the fundamental momentum-transfer theorem [Phys. Rev. Lett. 15, 11 (1965)] a novel contribution to the retarding force of metallic systems for slow intruders is derived. This contribution is associated with sudden charge-changing cycles during the path of projectiles. The sum of the novel and the well-known conventional contributions, both expressed in terms of scattering phase shifts, are used to discuss experimental data obtained for different targets. It is found that our two-channel modeling, with two nonlinear channels, improves the agreement between several data and theory and thus, as predictive modeling, can contribute to the desired convergence between experimental and theoretical attempts on the retarding force.

# Two-channel approach to the average retarding force of metals for slow singly ionized projectiles

## Key claim

The paper derives a novel contribution to the retarding force (stopping power) of metals for slow singly ionized projectiles, associated with sudden charge-changing cycles during the projectile's path, and claims that the sum of this new term and the conventional contribution improves agreement with experimental data. The hedge is that this improvement is demonstrated for selected targets and projectile species, and the authors acknowledge that the weighting of the two channels assumes random-collision conditions, which may not hold under channeling-like conditions.

## Method

The authors start from the momentum-transfer theorem (Lippmann and Mal, 1965) and derive a new scattering cross section for charge-changing processes by considering the difference between force matrix elements computed with self-consistent scattering states and unperturbed plane-wave states, regularized to avoid divergences. They compute partial phase shifts from orbital-based density functional theory (DFT) at the Fermi momentum, using values from earlier works (Puska, 1983; Echenique, 1985; Nagy, 1989), and evaluate the low-velocity stopping power coefficients Q(1) (conventional) and Q(2) (novel) for various atomic numbers Z1 and electron densities characterized by the Wigner-Seitz radius rs. The total stopping power is taken as the sum of these two channel contributions with unit-weight assumption.

## Key numbers

- For Z1=2 at rs=1.5: Q(1)=0.755 and Q(2)=0.069 (atomic units)
- For Z1=2 at rs=2: Q(1)=0.427 and Q(2)=0.134 (atomic units)
- For Z1=2 at rs=3: Q(1)=0.135 and Q(2)=0.250 (atomic units)
- For Z1=12 at rs=2: Q(1)=0.199 and Q(2)=0.572 (atomic units)
- For Z1=12 at rs=1.5: Q(1)=0.690 and Q(2)=0.520 (atomic units)
- For Z1=28 (Ni ion) at rs=2: Q(1)=0.28 and Q(2)=0.72 (atomic units), with the ratio [Q(1)+Q(2)]/Q(1) approximately 11 at q=2 and v=1, corresponding to dE/dx approximately 160 eV/A
- The experimental data for Al target are compared at projectile velocities v=0.411 and v=0.826 (atomic units)

## Context

This work extends the conventional theory of electronic stopping power for slow ions in metals, which is based on scattering phase shifts from DFT (Puska, 1983; Echenique, 1985; Nagy, 1989), by adding a charge-changing channel motivated by the Firsov (1959) quasiclassical model. The two-channel approach addresses discrepancies between conventional nonlinear theory and experimental data for helium projectiles in aluminum (Primetzhofer, 2011) and for heavier projectiles (Z1>2), where the conventional Q(1) alone underestimates the stopping power, especially around minima in the Z1-oscillation pattern. The results are compared with time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) simulations (Zeb, 2013; Correa, 2015; Maliyov, 2020) and with surface-scattering experiments (Winter, 2003). The approach enables predictions for self-irradiated materials and for dissociative adsorption of diatomic molecules on metallic surfaces.
