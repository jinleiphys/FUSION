# Two-photon exchange correction in elastic unpolarized electron-proton scattering at small momentum transfer

> We evaluate the two-photon exchange (TPE) correction to the unpolarized elastic electron-proton scattering at small momentum transfer $ Q^2 $. We account for the inelastic intermediate states approximating the double virtual Compton scattering by the unpolarized forward virtual Compton scattering. The unpolarized proton structure functions are used as input for the numerical evaluation of the inelastic contribution. Our calculation reproduces the leading terms in the $ Q^2 $ expansion of the TPE correction and goes beyond this approximation by keeping the full $ Q^2 $ dependence of the proton structure functions. In the range of small momentum transfer, our result is in good agreement with the empirical TPE fit to existing data.

## Key claim

The authors evaluate the two-photon exchange (TPE) correction to unpolarized elastic electron-proton scattering at small momentum transfer by approximating the double virtual Compton scattering (VVCS) process with the unpolarized forward virtual Compton scattering, which is valid only for the first two terms of the inelastic TPE expansion (proportional to Q² ln Q² and Q²) and does not claim validity beyond that expansion due to neglected VVCS amplitudes.

## Method

The inelastic TPE correction is expressed as a double integral over the virtual photon energy and virtuality of the unpolarized proton structure functions F₁ and F₂, using a "near-forward" approximation for the VVCS tensor that replaces the off-forward amplitudes with forward Compton amplitudes evaluated at an effective virtuality. Dispersion relations for the forward virtual Compton amplitudes T₁ and T₂ are employed, with the imaginary parts obtained from the optical theorem relating them to the unpolarized structure functions, and the subtraction function in T₁ is shown to be negligible for elastic electron-proton scattering.

## Key numbers

- The inelastic threshold for the dispersion integrals is W²_thr = (M + m_π)² ≈ 1.15 GeV², where m_π is the pion mass.
- The leading elastic TPE correction at small Q² contains a Feshbach term δ_F → (α π Q)/(2E) and an infrared-divergent term δ^IR_(2γ) → (α/π) (Q²/(M E)) ln(μ²/Q²).
- The elastic TPE correction at small Q² includes a term (α/π) (Q²/(M E)) ln(Q/(2E)) [ln(Q/(2E)) + 1] plus higher-order terms O(Q²/M², Q²/(M E), Q²/E²).
- The T₁ subtraction function contribution to the TPE amplitude vanishes in the limit of massless electrons.

## Context

This work extends the low-Q² limit of the inelastic TPE contribution beyond the leading Q² ln Q² term derived by Brown (1970), which expressed that term as an energy integral over the total photoabsorption cross section on a proton target. The calculation reproduces the known Q² ln Q² term of Brown (1970) and removes the hadronic scale Λ dependence that arises at order Q² ln Q², partially accounting for the Q² term and higher terms. This enables more accurate extraction of proton form factors at low momentum transfer, where recent high-precision measurements require better quantification of TPE effects, and is relevant to the proton charge radius discrepancy between muonic hydrogen spectroscopy (Pohl 2010, Antognini 2013) and electron experiments.
