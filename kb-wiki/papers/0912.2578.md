# Large elliptic flow in low multiplicity pp collisions at LHC energy $\sqrt{s}$=14 TeV

> We explore the possibility of observing elliptic flow in low multiplicity events in central pp collisions at LHC energy, $\sqrt{s}$=14 TeV. It is assumed that the initial interactions produces a number of hot spots. Hydrodynamical evolution of two or more hot spots can generate sufficiently large elliptic flow to be accessible experimentally in 4-th order cumulant analysis.

# Large elliptic flow in low multiplicity pp collisions at LHC energy $\sqrt{s}$=14 TeV

## Key claim

If two or more "hot spots" are formed in central pp collisions at LHC energy, hydrodynamic evolution can generate elliptic flow $v_2 \approx 0.15 \pm 0.05$ in low multiplicity events ($n_{mult} \sim 8-10$), which would be experimentally accessible via 4th order cumulant analysis; however, the applicability of hydrodynamics in such a small system is uncertain and depends on rapid isotropisation via Weibel instabilities on a timescale of about 0.2 fm.

## Method

The author assumes that in central pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}$=14 TeV, a number $N_s$ of hot spots with Gaussian density distribution ($\sigma$=0.3 fm) are created, with their centers randomly distributed within a sphere of radius R=1.2 fm. Boost-invariant ideal hydrodynamics (using the AZHYDRO-KOLKATA code) evolves the system from an initial time $\tau_i$=0.2 fm with zero initial fluid velocity, using a lattice-based equation of state with a confinement-deconfinement crossover at $T_{co}$=196 MeV. Freeze-out is performed at $T_F$=130 MeV, and charged particle multiplicity, mean transverse momentum, and elliptic flow are computed for ensembles of 200 random hot-spot configurations.

## Key numbers

- For 2 hot spots: average charged particle multiplicity $\langle n_{mult} \rangle = 7.75 \pm 1.17$, average elliptic flow $\langle v_2 \rangle = 0.147 \pm 0.071$, participant eccentricity $\epsilon = 0.532 \pm 0.052$.
- For 3 hot spots: $\langle n_{mult} \rangle = 9.68 \pm 2.24$, $\langle v_2 \rangle = 0.160 \pm 0.053$, $\epsilon = 0.536 \pm 0.051$.
- For 4 hot spots: $\langle n_{mult} \rangle = 11.05 \pm 2.58$, $\langle v_2 \rangle = 0.161 \pm 0.050$, $\epsilon = 0.457 \pm 0.048$.
- For mixed event EII (10% one, 50% two, 30% three, 10% four hot spots): $\langle v_2 \rangle = 0.138 \pm 0.022$, $\langle n_{mult} \rangle = 8.45 \pm 2.36$.
- In minimally viscous simulations ($\eta/s=0.08$) with 3 hot spots and adjusted peak energy density $\varepsilon_0=50$ GeV/fm$^3$, average multiplicity $n_{mult}=11.39 \pm 0.83$ and approximately 15% of events satisfy the 4th order cumulant threshold $v_2 n_{mult}^{3/4} > 1$.
- For 2-3 hot spots with $\varepsilon_0=100$ GeV/fm$^3$, more than 20% of events cross the threshold $v_2 n_{mult}^{3/4} > 1$ required for 4th order cumulant analysis.

## Context

This work extends the conjecture by Casalderrey-Solana (2009) and Bozek (2009) that high multiplicity ($n_{mult} > 50$) pp collisions at LHC could show large elliptic flow (10-20%) if hot spot structures are produced, and contrasts with Prasad (2009) who predicted only 2-3% elliptic flow in peripheral pp collisions using smooth initial energy density. The paper specifically addresses the bulk of pp events (low multiplicity $n_{mult} \sim 10$) and shows that even these events could yield measurable elliptic flow via 4th order cumulant analysis, provided hot spot formation occurs. The work also notes that double parton scattering, estimated by CDF (1993) as approximately 52% in certain reactions at $\sqrt{s}$=1.8 TeV, may provide a mechanism for forming multiple hot spots.
