# A New Open-Source Nuclear Equation of State Framework based on the Liquid-Drop Model with Skyrme Interaction

> The equation of state (EOS) of dense matter is an essential ingredient for numerical simulations of core-collapse supernovae and neutron star mergers. The properties of matter near and above nuclear saturation density are uncertain, which translates into uncertainties in astrophysical simulations and their multi-messenger signatures. Therefore, a wide range of EOSs spanning the allowed range of nuclear interactions are necessary for determining the sensitivity of these astrophysical phenomena and their signatures to variations in input microphysics. We present a new set of finite temperature EOSs based on experimentally allowed Skyrme forces. We employ a liquid drop model of nuclei to capture the non-uniform phase of nuclear matter at sub-saturation density, which is blended into a nuclear statistical equilibrium EOS at lower densities. We also provide a new, open-source code for calculating EOSs for arbitrary Skyrme parametrizations. We then study the effects of different Skyrme parametrizations on thermodynamical properties of dense astrophysical matter, the neutron star mass-radius relationship, and the core collapse of 15 and 40 solar mass stars.

## Key claim

The authors present a new open-source framework for generating finite-temperature nuclear equations of state using experimentally allowed Skyrme forces, but the results depend on the specific Skyrme parametrization chosen and the single nucleus approximation (SNA) used for the non-uniform phase, which introduces uncertainties that are not fully quantified.

## Method

The authors employ a liquid-drop model within the single nucleus approximation to describe non-uniform nuclear matter at sub-saturation densities, using Skyrme interactions in the mean-field approximation for bulk nucleonic matter. They extend the Lattimer and Swesty (1991) model by including non-local isospin asymmetric terms, treating the size of heavy nuclei consistently, and improving the treatment of nuclear surfaces, then blend this into a nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) EOS at lower densities. The code solves for the equilibrium conditions that minimize the free energy of nuclear matter across a grid of density, temperature, and proton fraction, and the resulting EOS tables are tested for thermodynamic consistency.

## Key numbers

- The alpha particle binding energy is set to the experimentally measured value of 30.887 MeV.
- The alpha particle hard-sphere volume is taken as 24 fm^3, following Lattimer (1985).
- Core-collapse simulations are performed for 15 solar-mass and 40 solar-mass presupernova stars, with the 40 solar-mass simulations followed to black hole formation.
- The critical temperature for symmetric nuclear matter T_c0 is determined from solving the coexistence conditions for the bulk pressure.

## Context

This work extends the open-source model of Lattimer and Swesty (1991) for the single nucleus approximation, incorporating a wider range of Skyrme parametrizations and improving convergence across temperature, density, and composition space. It enables systematic studies of how different Skyrme interactions affect neutron star mass-radius relationships and core-collapse supernova simulations, and provides a publicly available code for generating EOS tables. The authors note that their simulations show small differences between SNA and NSE low-density treatments lead to mild variations in collapse time and postbounce accretion rate, but little impact on overall postbounce evolution and black hole formation.
