# Constraining equation of state of nuclear matter by charge-changing cross section measurements of mirror nuclei

> The nuclear symmetry energy plays a key role in determining the equation of state (EoS) of dense, neutron-rich matter, which connects the atomic nuclei with the hot and dense matter in universe, thus has been the subject of intense investigations in laboratory experiments, astronomy observations and theories. Various probes have been proposed to constrain the symmetry energy and its density dependence. Currently, the extensive data yield already a good and consistent constraint to the symmetry energy ($E_\text{sym}(ρ)$) at saturation density, but do not yet give a consistent result of one critical EoS parameter, $L$, the density dependence of the symmetry energy. In this work, we report a new probe of $L$ at saturation density. A good linear correlation is found between $L$ and the charge changing cross section difference ($Δσ_\text{cc}$) of mirror nuclei $^{30}$Si-$^{30}$S for both the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock theory (SHF) and covariant (relativistic) density functionals (CDF). We found that the pairing effect for this mirror pair is essential to get a consistent correlation between $L$ and $Δσ_\text{cc}$ in both the SHF and CDF. Here, the cross sections are calculated on the same target and at the same energy using the zero-range optical-limit Glauber model. The linearity is found to be in the same precision as those found between $L$ and neutron skin thickness or proton radius difference.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the difference in charge-changing cross sections (Δσ_cc) of mirror nuclei, specifically the ³⁰Si-³⁰S pair, is a new probe for the slope parameter L of the nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density, but the linear correlation between Δσ_cc and L is only consistent across both Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) and covariant density functional (CDF) models when pairing correlations are included.

## Method

The authors calculated charge-changing cross sections (σ_cc) for the mirror pair ³⁰Si-³⁰S on a proton target at 900 MeV/nucleon using the zero-range optical-limit Glauber model, with proton density distributions obtained from 39 different Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) forces and several covariant density functionals (CDF). They then examined the linear correlation between the cross section difference (Δσ_cc) and the symmetry energy slope parameter L, and tested the effect of including pairing correlations by fitting pairing gaps to experimental values from five-point mass-difference formulas.

## Key numbers

- The linear correlation coefficient between L and Δσ_cc for the ³⁰Si-³⁰S pair using SHF functionals is 0.95, comparable to 0.92 for L versus neutron skin thickness and 0.94 for L versus proton radius difference R_mirr.
- At 900 MeV/nucleon, the linear fit between Δσ_cc and R_mirr gives Δσ_cc = 102.0(77) × R_mirr + 26.5(9) mb, with a correlation coefficient of 0.91.
- Taking Δσ_cc = 42.0 ± 0.5 mb as an example, SHF and CDF results give L = 135 ± 11 MeV and L = 79 ± 8 MeV respectively before pairing correction; after including pairing, the two models become consistent.
- The relative difference Δσ_cc/σ_cc reaches a maximum of about 13% at 250 MeV/nucleon, and Δσ_cc is more than 40% larger at 900 MeV/nucleon than at 250 MeV/nucleon.
- The relative variance of Δσ_cc/σ_cc varies from about 29% at A=20 to 10% at A=52 for the T_z=2 chain, and from about 22% at A=14 to about 5% at A=58 for the T_z=1 chain.

## Context

This work extends the previously identified linear correlation between charge radius differences of mirror nuclei and the slope parameter L, first found by Wang (2013) for the ³⁰S-³⁰Si pair and later examined by Brown (2017) using Skyrme functionals. The new probe avoids the model uncertainty inherent in extracting charge radii from Glauber-type model analysis of cross sections, and the authors note that dedicated experiments have been proposed at HIRFL-CSR and HIMAC facilities. The results support the recent study by Reinhard (2022) that neglecting pairing artificially increases the correlation between R_mirr and L.
