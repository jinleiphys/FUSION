# The Quark Beam Function at NNLL

> In hard collisions at a hadron collider the most appropriate description of the initial state depends on what is measured in the final state. Parton distribution functions (PDFs) evolved to the hard collision scale Q are appropriate for inclusive observables, but not for measurements with a specific number of hard jets, leptons, and photons. Here the incoming protons are probed and lose their identity to an incoming jet at a scale μ_B << Q, and the initial state is described by universal beam functions. We discuss the field-theoretic treatment of beam functions, and show that the beam function has the same RG evolution as the jet function to all orders in perturbation theory. In contrast to PDF evolution, the beam function evolution does not mix quarks and gluons and changes the virtuality of the colliding parton at fixed momentum fraction. At μ_B, the incoming jet can be described perturbatively, and we give a detailed derivation of the one-loop matching of the quark beam function onto quark and gluon PDFs. We compute the associated NLO Wilson coefficients and explicitly verify the cancellation of IR singularities. As an application, we give an expression for the next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic order (NNLL) resummed Drell-Yan beam thrust cross section.

## Key claim

The beam function has the same renormalization group (RG) evolution as the jet function to all orders in perturbation theory, and unlike parton distribution function (PDF) evolution, the beam function evolution does not mix quarks and gluons and changes the virtuality of the colliding parton at fixed momentum fraction. This result is proven for the quark beam function at next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) order, with the caveat that the proof relies on the soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) framework and the factorization theorem for beam thrust.

## Method

The authors define the quark beam function as a proton matrix element of SCET operators that include collinear fields with zero-bin subtractions, and perform a one-loop matching calculation onto quark and gluon PDFs using an offshellness regulator. They compute the NLO Wilson coefficients for the matching, explicitly verify the cancellation of infrared (IR) singularities between the beam function and PDFs, and derive the RG evolution equation for the beam function. As an application, they combine the beam function with the hard and soft functions to obtain the NNLL resummed Drell-Yan beam thrust cross section.

## Key numbers

- The beam scale is set by \(\mu_B \simeq \sqrt{\tau_B} Q\), where \(\tau_B\) is the beam thrust variable and \(Q\) is the hard collision scale.
- The soft scale is \(\mu_S \simeq \tau_B Q\).
- The beam thrust cross section is valid for small \(\tau_B \ll 1\), with corrections of order \(O(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}/Q, \tau_B)\).
- The one-loop matching coefficients \(\mathcal{I}_{qq}\) and \(\mathcal{I}_{qg}\) are computed at NLO, with the IR singularities cancelling against those in the PDFs.
- The NNLL resummation uses the three-loop QCD cusp anomalous dimension and two-loop standard anomalous dimensions.

## Context

This work extends the factorization theorem for beam thrust in Drell-Yan production from Stewart, Tackmann, and Waalewijn (2009), providing the detailed derivation of the quark beam function at one loop and its RG evolution. The beam function is the initial-state analog of the final-state jet function, and the result that they share the same anomalous dimension simplifies resummation. The paper enables NNLL resummation for the Drell-Yan beam thrust cross section, and the beam function formalism is expected to have wider applicability to processes such as \(pp \to W/Z + n\) jets.
