# Proton correlations and apparent intermittency in the UrQMD model with hadronic potentials

> It is shown that the inclusion of hadronic interactions, and in particular nuclear potentials, in simulations of heavy ion collisions at the SPS energy range can lead to obvious correlations of protons. These correlations contribute significantly to an intermittency analysis as performed at the NA61 experiment. The beam energy and system size dependence is studied by comparing the resulting intermittency index for heavy ion collisions of different nuclei at beam energies of $40A$, $80A$ and $150A$ GeV. The resulting intermittency index from our simulations is similar to the reported values of the NA61 collaboration, if nuclear interactions are included. The observed apparent intermittency signal is the result of the correlated proton pairs with small relative transverse momentum $Δp_{t}$, which would be enhanced by hadronic potentials, and this correlation between the protons is slightly influenced by the coalescence parameters and the relative invariant four-momentum $q_{inv}$ cut.

## Key claim

The inclusion of hadronic mean-field potentials in UrQMD simulations of heavy-ion collisions at SPS energies produces a non-zero intermittency index that is consistent with NA61 experimental data, but this signal is an "apparent" intermittency caused by correlated proton pairs with small relative transverse momentum rather than a genuine critical phenomenon.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD transport model in two modes: cascade mode (UrQMD/C, without hadronic potentials) and mean-field mode (UrQMD/M, with density-dependent and momentum-dependent hadronic potentials). They simulated central collisions of Ar+Sc, Xe+La, and Au+Au at beam energies of 40A, 80A, and 150A GeV, generating over 600K events per scenario. The second-order scaled factorial moments (SSFMs) of protons in transverse momentum space were calculated for real events and mixed events, and the correlator ΔF₂(M) was fitted to extract the intermittency index φ₂ for M² > 100.

## Key numbers

- Intermittency index φ₂ = 0.32 ± 0.03 for 5-10% central ⁴⁰Ar+⁴⁵Sc collisions at 40A GeV in UrQMD/M (mean-field mode)
- Approximately 0.029 additional proton pairs per event in real events compared to mixed events for Δpₜ < 64 MeV/c in 5-10% central Ar+Sc at 40A GeV
- Proton pair correlations appear for Δpₜ ≲ 60 MeV/c and ΔRₜ ≲ 1.5 fm in simulations with hadronic potentials
- Beam energies studied: 40A, 80A, and 150A GeV
- Centralities studied: 0-5%, 5-10%, and 10-15% for Ar+Sc and Xe+La; 0-10% for Au+Au
- Transverse momentum acceptance: |p_{x,y}| ≤ 1.5 GeV/c; rapidity range: |y| ≤ 0.75

## Context

This work extends the intermittency analysis performed by the NA49 and NA61 collaborations (NA49, 2009; NA61, 2018) by providing a transport-model explanation for the observed non-zero intermittency index. The results contradict the interpretation that the signal necessarily indicates critical fluctuations near a QCD critical endpoint, instead showing that hadronic potentials alone can produce similar φ₂ values. The study enables future work to disentangle genuine critical signals from background correlations by comparing transport simulations with and without mean-field potentials, and motivates further investigation of the stiffness of the equation of state and final-state interactions at lower collision energies.
