# Convergence of Particle-Hole Expansions for the Description of Nuclear Correlations

> The convergence properties of a multiparticle-multihole (mp-mh) configuration mixing approach whose purpose is to describe ground state correlations in nuclei without particle number and Pauli violations is investigated in the case of an exactly solvable pairing hamiltonian. Two different truncation schemes are tested by looking at quantities as correlation energies and single-particle occupation probabilities. Results show that pairing correlations present in usual superfluid nuclei can be accurately described using up to 6 particle-6 hole excitations, a convergence fast enought for envisaging extensions to fully microscopic calculations.

## Key claim

The multiparticle-multihole (mp-mh) configuration mixing approach, which conserves particle number and respects the Pauli principle, can accurately describe pairing correlations in superfluid nuclei using up to 6 particle-6 hole excitations, with convergence fast enough for extensions to fully microscopic calculations; this conclusion is based on tests against an exactly solvable pairing hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors tested the convergence of the mp-mh expansion by applying it to the exactly solvable Richardson pairing hamiltonian for systems of 2N=8 and 2N=16 identical fermions distributed over equally spaced, twofold degenerate levels. They compared ground state correlation energies, odd-even mass differences, and single-particle occupation probabilities from the mp-mh approach truncated at various orders (2p2h, 4p4h, 6p6h, 8p8h) against the exact Richardson solution, and also against plain BCS (unprojected) results. Two truncation schemes were examined: one based on the maximum particle-hole order and another based on an energy cut-off for the mp-mh configurations.

## Key numbers

* For the 2N=8 system at pairing strength g=0.6, the 6p6h truncation gave a correlation energy error of 0.01% and an odd-even mass difference error of 0.00%, compared to exact values of -4.136 (correlation energy) and 1.739 (odd-even mass difference, in units of level spacing d).
* For the 2N=16 system at g=0.54, the 6p6h truncation gave a correlation energy error of 0.60% and an odd-even mass difference error of 1.64%, compared to exact values of -8.704 and 3.844 respectively.
* For the 2N=16 system at g=0.54, achieving approximately 0.5% accuracy in correlation energy required 3250 configurations with an energy cut-off of 50 (in units of level spacing d), compared to 3984 configurations when truncating by order alone (up to 6p6h).
* For the 2N=8 system at g=0.2, the 2p2h truncation already gave a correlation energy error of only 0.36% and an odd-even mass difference error of 1.65%.
* BCS correlation energies were underestimated by more than 20% for all pairing strengths listed, and BCS gave zero correlation energy below the critical coupling (g_c approximately 0.31 for 2N=8 and 0.24 for 2N=16).

## Context

This work extends the mp-mh configuration mixing method proposed by Pillet (2002) by testing its convergence for pairing correlations against the exactly solvable Richardson model (Richardson, 1964, 1966). The approach differs from particle-number-conserving treatments of the pairing hamiltonian proposed by Wu (1989) and Molique, and from large-scale shell model calculations, by using single-particle states from a self-consistent mean-field and considering the whole single-particle spectrum. The results show that the method can describe pairing correlations without the particle number violations of BCS/HFB or the Pauli principle violations of RPA, and the demonstrated fast convergence suggests that fully microscopic self-consistent calculations in nuclei are feasible with current numerical capabilities.
