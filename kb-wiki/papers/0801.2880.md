# The Transverse Electron Scattering Response Function of 3He

> The transverse response function R_T(q,omega) for 3He is calculated using the configuration space BonnA nucleon-nucleon potential, the Tucson-Melbourne three-body force, and the Coulomb potential. Final states are completely taken into account via the Lorentz integral transform technique. Non-relativistic one-body currents plus two-body pi- and rho-meson exchange currents as well as the Siegert operator are included. The response R_T is calculated for q=174, 250, 400, and 500 MeV/c and in the threshold region at q=174, 324, and 487 MeV/c. Strong MEC effects are found in low- and high-energy tails, but due to MEC there are also moderate enhancements of the quasi-elastic peak (6%-10%). The calculation is performed both directly and via transformation of electric multipoles to a form that involves the charge operator. The contribution of the latter operator is suppressed in and below the quasielastic peak while at higher energies the charge operator represents almost the whole MEC contribution at the lowest q value. The effect of the Coulomb force in the final state interaction is investigated for the threshold region at q=174 MeV/c. Its neglect enhances R_T by more than 10% in the range up to 2 MeV above threshold. In comparison to experimental data one finds relatively good agreement at q=250 and 400 MeV/c, while at q=500 MeV/c, presumably due to relativistic effects, the theoretical quasi-elastic peak position is shifted to somewhat higher energies. The strong MEC contributions in the threshold region are nicely confirmed by data at q=324 and 487 MeV/c.

## Key claim

The transverse response function \(R_T(q,\omega)\) for \(^3\)He is calculated with the BonnA nucleon-nucleon potential and the Tucson-Melbourne three-body force, finding that meson-exchange currents (MEC) produce strong effects in the low- and high-energy tails and moderate enhancements of the quasi-elastic peak (6%-10%), but the agreement with experimental data is qualified by the fact that at \(q=500\) MeV/c the theoretical quasi-elastic peak position is shifted to somewhat higher energies, presumably due to relativistic effects not included in the non-relativistic calculation.

## Method

The transverse response \(R_T(q,\omega)\) was computed using the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) technique to fully include final states, with the non-relativistic Hamiltonian comprising the configuration-space BonnA NN potential, the Tucson-Melbourne TM' three-body force, and the Coulomb potential. The current operator included non-relativistic one-body currents plus two-body \(\pi\)- and \(\rho\)-meson exchange currents, and the calculation was performed both directly and via the Siegert form of the electric multipole operators (which involves the charge operator) to test consistency and to capture additional MEC contributions beyond \(\pi\)- and \(\rho\)-exchange.

## Key numbers

* Quasi-elastic peak enhancement due to MEC: 10% at \(q=174\) and 250 MeV/c, 7% at \(q=400\) MeV/c, and 6% at \(q=500\) MeV/c.
* MEC enhancement near threshold (15-30 MeV above threshold): more than 30% for the two higher \(q\)-values; very close to threshold, up to 200% at \(q=487\) MeV/c.
* MEC enhancement at the pion threshold: 180% at \(q=174\) MeV/c, 95% at \(q=250\) MeV/c, 22% at \(q=400\) MeV/c, and 5% at \(q=500\) MeV/c.
* Neglect of the Coulomb force in the final state interaction at \(q=174\) MeV/c enhances \(R_T\) by more than 10% in the range up to 2 MeV above threshold.
* At \(q=500\) MeV/c, the theoretical quasi-elastic peak position is shifted by about 5-10 MeV relative to experimental data.

## Context

This work extends prior LIT-method calculations of the longitudinal response \(R_L\) for \(^3\)He (Leidemann et al., 2004) to the transverse channel, and it provides the first fully realistic LIT computation of \(R_T\) for \(^3\)He with consistent MEC. It contrasts with the Faddeev-based calculations of Golak et al. (2005) (using AV18+UrbanaIX) and Deltuva et al. (2006) (using CD-Bonn+\(\Delta\)), which did not include the Coulomb interaction in final states; the present results show that Coulomb effects are significant near threshold. The findings enable future investigations of relativistic corrections for \(q\) up to 1 GeV/c and of isobar current contributions including \(\Delta(1232)\) degrees of freedom.
