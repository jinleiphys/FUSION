# The Role of Lambda(1405) in Kaon-Proton Interactions

> S-wave $K^-p$ scattering into various channels near threshold are analyzed in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory with $Λ(1405)$ introduced as an independent field. This is the approach that predicted the critical density $2<=ρ_c/ρ_0 <=3$ for negatively charged kaon condensation. We show that chiral perturbation expansion treating the $Λ(1405)$ as elementary is consistent with {\it all} threshold data including a double-charge-exchange process suppressed at leading order of chiral expansion in the absence of the $Λ(1405)$. We also discuss S-wave $K^+ p$ scattering phase shifts at low energy.

## Key claim

The authors show that treating the Lambda(1405) as an elementary field in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, rather than as a dynamically generated resonance, can consistently describe all threshold K^-p scattering data including the double-charge-exchange process K^-p -> pi+ Sigma- that is suppressed at leading chiral order without the Lambda(1405). This approach is justified for kaon condensation predictions because the condensation occurs far from the Lambda(1405) pole position.

## Method

The authors construct a heavy-baryon chiral Lagrangian to order Q^2, introducing the Lambda(1405) as an independent matter field with a coupling constant g_{Lambda*}^2 = 0.15 fixed to the empirical decay width of 50 MeV. They compute S-wave K^-p scattering amplitudes at leading order (O(Q^1)) using only the Weinberg-Tomozawa term and the Lambda(1405) pole contribution, then fit the three remaining O(Q^2) counterterm combinations to reproduce the experimental threshold branching ratios.

## Key numbers

- Lambda(1405) mass: 1405 MeV; decay width: 50 MeV; coupling constant g_{Lambda*}^2 = 0.15
- Leading-order predictions for threshold branching ratios: gamma = 1.93, R_c = 0.64, R_n = 0.11 (experimental values: gamma = 2.36 +/- 0.04, R_c = 0.664 +/- 0.011, R_n = 0.19 +/- 0.02)
- Best-fit O(Q^2) parameters (in fm): d1+d7 = 0.039, d3+d7 = -3.35, d7+d9 = -5.90, yielding gamma = 2.36, R_c = 0.63, R_n = 0.19
- KN sigma term: Sigma_KN = 438 MeV, consistent with lattice calculations giving 450 +/- 30 MeV
- K^+p S-wave phase shift at P_lab = 145 MeV: predicted -9.05 degrees (experimental: -8.2 +/- 0.9 degrees); at P_lab = 265 MeV: predicted -18.3 degrees (experimental: -15.0 +/- 0.2 degrees)
- Critical density for kaon condensation: 2 <= rho_c/rho_0 <= 3

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous chiral perturbation theory calculations (Lee, Min, Rho, 1994; Lee, Brown, Min, Rho, 1994) that predicted kaon condensation thresholds in neutron star matter, addressing a challenge from Weise (1995) who argued that a dynamical coupled-channel treatment of the Lambda(1405) might give different results. The paper demonstrates that the elementary-field approach is consistent with all low-energy KN data, including the OZI-suppressed double-charge-exchange process, and confirms that the Lambda(1405) plays a negligible role in kaon condensation predictions. This enables future O(Q^3) calculations of the equation of state for kaon-condensed matter.
