# Axial, Scalar and Tensor Charges of the Nucleon from 2+1+1-flavor Lattice QCD

> We present results for the isovector axial, scalar and tensor charges $g^{u-d}_A$, $g^{u-d}_S$ and $g^{u-d}_T$ of the nucleon needed to probe the Standard Model and novel physics. The axial charge is a fundamental parameter describing the weak interactions of nucleons. The scalar and tensor charges probe novel interactions at the TeV scale in neutron and nuclear $β$-decays, and the flavor-diagonal tensor charges $g^{u}_T$, $g^{d}_T$ and $g^{s}_T$ are needed to quantify the contribution of the quark electric dipole moment (EDM) to the neutron EDM. The 9 ensembles, generated by the MILC Collaboration using the HISQ action with 2+1+1 dynamical flavors, span three lattice spacings $a \approx 0.06, 0.09$ and 0.12 fm and light-quark masses corresponding to the pion masses $M_π\approx 135, 225$ and 315 MeV. High-statistics estimates on five ensembles using the all-mode-averaging method allow us to quantify all systematic uncertainties and perform a simultaneous extrapolation in the lattice spacing, lattice volume and light-quark masses for the connected contributions. Our final estimates, in the $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scheme at 2 GeV, of the isovector charges are $g_A^{u-d} = 1.195(33)(20)$, $g_S^{u-d} = 0.97(12)(6) $ and $g_T^{u-d} = 0.987(51)(20)$. The first error includes statistical and all systematic uncertainties except that due to the extrapolation Ansatz, which is given by the second error estimate. Combining our estimate for $g_S^{u-d}$ with the difference of light quarks masses $(m_d-m_u)^{\rm QCD}=2.67(35)$ MeV given by FLAG, we obtain $(M_N-M_P)^{\rm QCD} = 2.59(49)$ MeV. Estimates of the connected part of the flavor-diagonal tensor charges of the proton are $g^{u}_T=0.792(42)$ and $g^{d}_T=-0.194(14)$. Combining our new estimates with precision low-energy experiments, we update constraints on novel scalar and tensor interactions, $ε_{S,T}$, at the TeV scale.

## Key claim

The paper presents lattice QCD calculations of the nucleon's isovector axial, scalar, and tensor charges with quantified systematic uncertainties, claiming that the scalar charge can now be determined with approximately 15% uncertainty using the all-mode-averaging (AMA) method, though the final estimates carry a second error from the extrapolation ansatz that is not included in the first combined error.

## Method

The authors analyzed nine ensembles of 2+1+1-flavor HISQ gauge configurations generated by the MILC Collaboration, using Wilson-clover valence fermions on these ensembles (a mixed-action approach). They calculated two-point and three-point nucleon correlation functions at zero momentum, applied the all-mode-averaging (AMA) error-reduction technique on five ensembles to increase statistics, and performed a simultaneous extrapolation in lattice spacing, lattice volume, and light-quark masses to obtain the charges in the MS-bar scheme at 2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Isovector axial charge: g_A^{u-d} = 1.195(33)(20) in the MS-bar scheme at 2 GeV, where the first error includes statistical and all systematic uncertainties except the extrapolation ansatz error given by the second
- Isovector scalar charge: g_S^{u-d} = 0.97(12)(6) in the MS-bar scheme at 2 GeV
- Isovector tensor charge: g_T^{u-d} = 0.987(51)(20) in the MS-bar scheme at 2 GeV
- Connected part of the proton flavor-diagonal tensor charges: g_T^u = 0.792(42) and g_T^d = -0.194(14)
- Combined with the light quark mass difference (m_d - m_u)^QCD = 2.67(35) MeV from FLAG, the QCD contribution to the neutron-proton mass difference is (M_N - M_P)^QCD = 2.59(49) MeV
- The nine ensembles span three lattice spacings a approximately 0.06, 0.09, and 0.12 fm, and pion masses M_pi approximately 135, 225, and 315 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous lattice QCD calculation of the tensor charge (Bhattacharya et al., 2015) by applying the AMA method to achieve higher statistics, particularly improving the scalar charge determination which was about five times noisier than the tensor charge in the earlier study. The results enable updated constraints on novel scalar and tensor interactions (epsilon_S, epsilon_T) at the TeV scale when combined with precision low-energy beta decay experiments, and the flavor-diagonal tensor charges are needed to quantify quark electric dipole moment contributions to the neutron electric dipole moment for planned experiments aiming to reduce the bound from 2.9 x 10^{-26} e cm to around 10^{-28} e cm.
