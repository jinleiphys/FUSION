# Open-Shell Nuclei from No-Core Shell Model with Perturbative Improvement

> We introduce a hybrid many-body approach that combines the flexibility of the No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) with the efficiency of Multi-Configurational Perturbation Theory (MCPT) to compute ground- and excited-state energies in arbitrary open-shell nuclei in large model spaces. The NCSM in small model spaces is used to define a multi-determinantal reference state that contains the most important multi-particle multi-hole correlations and a subsequent second-order MCPT correction is used to capture additional correlation effects from a large model space. We apply this new ab initio approach for the calculation of ground-state and excitation energies of even and odd-mass carbon, oxygen, and fluorine isotopes and compare to large-scale NCSM calculations that are computationally much more expensive.

## Key claim

The authors introduce a hybrid many-body approach that combines the No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) in small model spaces with a second-order Multi-Configurational Perturbation Theory (MCPT) correction to compute ground- and excited-state energies in arbitrary open-shell nuclei, claiming that this method provides accuracy on par with more demanding approaches like the multi-reference IM-SRG while requiring two to three orders of magnitude less computing time than importance-truncated NCSM calculations.

## Method

The method uses an NCSM calculation in a small model space (typically Nmax=0, 2, or 4) to define a multi-determinantal reference state containing the most important multi-particle multi-hole correlations. A second-order MCPT correction is then evaluated in a large model space (single-particle basis truncated at e_max=12) to capture additional correlation effects, using a normal-ordered two-body (NO2B) approximation for the inclusion of three-nucleon interactions. The Hamiltonian is a chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction at N3LO by Entem and Machleidt with a chiral three-nucleon interaction at N2LO, softened via SRG transformation with flow parameter alpha=0.08 fm^4.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energies for carbon isotopes 11-20, oxygen isotopes 16-26, and fluorine isotopes 17-31 computed with NCSM-PT using Nmax_ref=2 reference states and e_max=12 model space truncation
- SRG flow parameter alpha=0.08 fm^4 used for Hamiltonian softening
- Three-body cutoff Lambda_3N=400 MeV for the chiral 3N interaction
- Harmonic-oscillator basis at hbar Omega=20 MeV used for Hartree-Fock optimized single-particle basis
- For oxygen-19 and oxygen-20 with Nmax_ref=4, an additional truncation c_mu c_mu' >= 10^{-6} (mu != mu') was introduced for second-order energy corrections
- NCSM-PT calculations with Nmax_ref=2 require two to three orders of magnitude less computing time than corresponding importance-truncated NCSM calculations
- High-order partial sums for lithium-6 and lithium-7 agree within a few ten keV with direct NCSM calculations in the same model space

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior demonstration that many-body perturbation theory with Hartree-Fock single-particle orbitals yields rapidly convergent perturbation series (Tichai, 2016), and builds on multi-reference IM-SRG developments (Hergert, 2015) and the combination of multi-reference IM-SRG with NCSM (Gebrerufael, 2017). The approach enables the first no-core ab initio calculations for neutron-rich fluorine isotopes out to fluorine-31, relevant for understanding the oxygen anomaly (the drastic shift of the neutron dripline from oxygen to fluorine isotopic chains), and is ideally suited for exploratory calculations over a large range of nuclei to test and constrain new chiral interactions.
