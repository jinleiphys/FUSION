# On non-conformal kinetic theory and hydrodynamics for Bjorken flow

> Using and comparing kinetic theory and second-order Chapman-Enskog hydrodynamics, we study the non-conformal dynamics of a system undergoing Bjorken expansion. We use the concept of `free-streaming fixed lines' for scaled shear and bulk stresses in non-conformal kinetic theory and hydrodynamics, and show that these `fixed lines' behave as early-time attractors and repellors of the evolution. In the conformal limit, the free-streaming fixed lines reduce to the well-known fixed points of conformal Bjorken dynamics. A new fixed point in the free streaming regime is identified which lies at the intersection of these fixed lines. Contrary to the conformal scenario, both kinetic theory and hydrodynamics predict the absence of attractor behavior in the normalised shear stress channel. In kinetic theory a far-off-equilibrium attractor is found for the normalised effective longitudinal pressure, driven by rapid longitudinal expansion. Second-order viscous hydrodynamics fails to accurately describe this attractor. From a thorough analysis of the free-streaming dynamics in Chapman-Enskog hydrodynamics we conclude that this failure results from an inaccurate approximation of the fixed lines and a related incorrect description of the nature of the fixed point. A modified anisotropic hydrodynamic description is presented that provides excellent agreement with kinetic theory results and reproduces the far-from-equilibrium attractor for the scaled longitudinal pressure.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for non-conformal Bjorken flow, second-order Chapman-Enskog hydrodynamics fails to reproduce the far-from-equilibrium attractor found in kinetic theory for the normalized effective longitudinal pressure, contrary to the conformal case where hydrodynamics works well even far from equilibrium; this failure is traced to an inaccurate approximation of the free-streaming fixed lines and the nature of the fixed point in the hydrodynamic truncation.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation (RTA) for a gas of massive particles (mass m = 200 MeV) undergoing boost-invariant Bjorken expansion, using an analytic solution for the distribution function and iteratively solving for the temperature via Landau matching. They then compare these kinetic-theory results to second-order Chapman-Enskog hydrodynamics and to a modified anisotropic hydrodynamic description, analyzing the early-time free-streaming limit (tau_R to infinity) to identify fixed lines and fixed points in the space of normalized shear stress (pi/P) and bulk stress (Pi/P).

## Key numbers

- Initial conditions are set at proper time tau_0 = 0.1 fm/c with initial temperature T_0 = 500 MeV and particle mass m = 200 MeV (giving m/T_0 = 0.4).
- The relaxation time is tau_R(tau) = 5 C / T(tau) with C = 10/(4 pi), corresponding to an initial specific shear viscosity eta/s approximately 0.8.
- In the free-streaming limit, the repulsive fixed point for forward evolution is at (Pi/P = 0, pi/P = -2), matching the conformal repulsive fixed point.
- A new fixed point is identified at the intersection of the fixed lines P_L = 0 and P_T = 0, located at (Pi/P = -1, pi/P = 0).

## Context

This work extends the study of far-from-equilibrium attractors in conformal Bjorken flow (e.g., Heller (2016), Romatschke (2017), Blaizot (2017)) to non-conformal systems with massive particles, directly following the recent comparison by Chattopadhyay (2021) that showed second-order non-conformal hydrodynamics fails to reproduce kinetic theory attractors. The paper enables the development of improved anisotropic hydrodynamic descriptions for non-conformal systems, which are relevant for early-time dynamics in heavy-ion collisions where the quark-gluon plasma has a finite mass scale and undergoes rapid longitudinal expansion.
