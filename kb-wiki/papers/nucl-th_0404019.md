# Correlation energy contribution to nuclear masses

> The ground state correlation energies associated with collective surface and pairing vibrations are calculated for Pb- and Ca-isotopes. It is shown that this contribution, when added to those predicted by one of the most accurate modern nuclear mass formula (HFBCS MSk7 mass formula), reduces the associated rms error by an important factor, making mean field theory, once its time dependence is taken into account, a quantitative predictive tool for nuclear masses.

## Key claim

The ground state correlation energies from collective surface and pairing vibrations, when added to the HFBCS MSk7 mass formula, reduce the rms error by roughly a factor of two for Ca and Pb isotopes, but only after a slight renormalization of the Skyrme parameters (by factors 0.9964 for Ca and 0.99945 for Pb) to shift the calculated masses upward by approximately 4.3 MeV and 3.5 MeV respectively.

## Method

The authors performed Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations using the MSk7 Skyrme interaction self-consistently to compute ground state correlation energies from particle-hole vibrations (quadrupole and octupole modes below 7 MeV exhausting at least 2% of the non-energy weighted sum rule) and from pairing vibrations (monopole, quadrupole, and hexadecapole pair addition and pair removal modes) for Pb-isotopes (204,206,208,210,212Pb) and Ca-isotopes (40-48Ca). The correlation energies were then added to the HFBCS MSk7 mass formula, and the Skyrme parameters were slightly renormalized to minimize the rms deviation for each isotopic chain separately.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb, the ground state correlation energy from neutron pairing vibrations is -0.399 MeV and from proton pairing vibrations is -0.449 MeV (monopole modes only).
- The total summed contribution from monopole, quadrupole, and hexadecapole pairing vibrations in 208Pb amounts to approximately -1.981 MeV (-1.196 MeV from neutrons, -0.785 MeV from protons).
- For Pb-isotopes, the rms error of the HFBCS MSk7 mass formula is 0.646 MeV; adding RPA correlations reduces it to 0.543 MeV.
- For Ca-isotopes, the rms error of the HFBCS MSk7 mass formula is 1.200 MeV; adding RPA correlations reduces it to 0.466 MeV.
- The averaged rms deviation (over Ca and Pb) is 0.505 MeV with correlations, compared to 0.964 MeV without.
- The Skyrme parameters were renormalized by a factor 0.9964 for Ca-isotopes and 0.99945 for Pb-isotopes.

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Bertsch and Hagino to include ground state correlation energies from collective modes in nuclear mass calculations, and builds on earlier realistic calculations for quadrupole degrees of freedom in light nuclei using the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM). The paper specifically addresses the largest deviations found in the HFBCS MSk7 mass formula of Goriely et al. (2001), which occur at closed shell systems, by including pairing vibrations that are most collective around closed shells. The results enable a path toward making mean-field theory, augmented by time-dependent effects, a quantitative predictive tool for nuclear masses, particularly for extrapolations to the neutron drip line relevant for r-process nucleosynthesis.
