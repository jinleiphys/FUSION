# Nuclear fission in covariant density functional theory

> The current status of the application of covariant density functional theory to microscopic description of nuclear fission with main emphasis on superheavy nuclei (SHN) is reviewed. The softness of SHN in the triaxial plane leads to an emergence of several competing fission pathes in the region of the inner fission barrier in some of these nuclei. The outer fission barriers of SHN are considerably affected both by triaxiality and octupole deformation.

## Key claim

Covariant density functional theory (CDFT) provides a good description of experimental fission barrier heights in actinides without using fission-barrier or isomer data in the fit of its parameters, but this success does not translate into consistent predictions for superheavy nuclei, where different models (including different macroscopic-microscopic models) disagree by up to 6 MeV.

## Method

The authors review systematic calculations of inner and outer fission barriers in actinides and superheavy nuclei using the triaxial RMF+BCS approach with the NL3* parametrization, comparing results with other CDFT parametrizations (DD-ME2, PC-PK1, DD-PC1) and with non-relativistic approaches (Skyrme DFT with SkM*, Gogny DFT with D1S, and macroscopic-microscopic models). Potential energy surfaces are computed as functions of quadrupole (β₂, γ) and octupole deformations to identify saddle points along competing fission paths.

## Key numbers

- The average deviation per inner fission barrier in actinides (δ, in MeV) is comparable across models: CDFT, macroscopic-microscopic (Dobrowolski, Möller), and Gogny DFT all show similar δ-values (exact numbers not given in the text).
- In superheavy nuclei, the difference in inner fission barrier heights between different models reaches 6 MeV in some nuclei.
- Inclusion of triaxiality or octupole deformation lowers the outer fission barrier in superheavy nuclei by around 2 MeV in the majority of nuclei.
- The lowest saddle point for outer fission barriers in superheavy nuclei is obtained in triaxial calculations for proton-rich nuclei with N < 174, and in octupole-deformed calculations for neutron-rich nuclei with N > 174.
- Pairing scheme differences can introduce errors in fission barrier calculations of up to 1 MeV (citing Kalra et al., 2010).

## Context

This review extends the systematic CDFT studies of fission barriers in actinides (Afanasjev et al., 2010) to superheavy nuclei, and compares with macroscopic-microscopic calculations (Möller et al., 2009; Kowal et al., 2010) and Skyrme DFT (SBN, 2013). The work highlights that while all modern models describe actinide fission barriers with similar accuracy, their predictions diverge sharply for superheavy nuclei, posing a major theoretical challenge for future studies of superheavy element synthesis and stability.
