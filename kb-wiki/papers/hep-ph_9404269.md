# The Threshold Pion-Photoproduction of Nucleons In The Chiral Quark Model

> In this paper, we show that the low energy theorem (LET) of the threshold pion-photoproduction can be fully recovered in the quark model. An essential result of this investigation is that the quark-pion operators are obtained from the effective chiral Lagrangian, and the low energy theorem does not require the constraints on the internal structures of the nucleon. The pseudoscalar quark-pion coupling generates an additional term at order $μ=m_π/M$ only in the isospin amplitude $A^{(-)}$. The role of the transitions between the nucleon and the resonance $P_{33}(1232)$ and P-wave baryons are also discussed, we find that the leading contributions to the isospin amplitudes at $O(μ^2)$ are from the transition between the P-wave baryons and the nucleon and the charge radius of the nucleon. The leading contribution from the P-wave baryons only affects the neutral pion production, and improve the agreement with data significantly. The transition between the resonance $P_{33}(1232)$ and the nucleon only gives an order $μ^3$ corrections to $A^{(-)}$.

## Key claim

The low energy theorem (LET) for threshold pion-photoproduction can be fully recovered in the quark model without requiring constraints on the internal structure of the nucleon, provided the quark-pion operators are derived from the effective chiral Lagrangian; the pseudoscalar quark-pion coupling generates an additional term at order μ = mπ/M only in the isospin amplitude A(−).

## Method

The author derived the threshold pion-photoproduction amplitudes for a three-quark system by starting from the effective chiral Lagrangian of Manohar and Georgi, performing a nonrelativistic expansion of the quark-photon-pion interaction (Seagull diagram) and the direct and crossed resonance pole diagrams. The center-of-mass motion was separated from internal motion following the approach used for Compton scattering by Berg and P. (1992) and Li (1993), and the transition operators were evaluated in the SU(6) symmetry limit for intermediate P-wave baryons and the Δ resonance P33(1232).

## Key numbers

- The Goldberger-Treiman relation is recovered: gπN = gA M / fπ, with fπ ≈ 93 MeV and gA = 1.26.
- For the pseudoscalar coupling, the isospin amplitude A(−) becomes 1 + μ/2 + O(μ²), differing from the pseudovector result by a term μ/2.
- The leading contribution from P-wave baryon transitions to the isospin amplitude A(+) at order μ² is given by M(+) = (4 e μ² M²) / (9 ωh fπ mq), where ωh = 0.6 GeV is the average mass difference between P-wave baryons and the nucleon, and M/mq = 2.79.
- The nucleon-Δ transition contributes to A(−) at order μ³: M(−)Δ = (e gA μ³ M κn) / (2 fπ EΔ), where EΔ is the nucleon-Δ mass difference and κn is the anomalous magnetic moment of the neutron.
- Including P-wave baryon contributions, the E0+ amplitudes for neutral pion production (in units of 10⁻³/mπ) are: E0+(γp → π0p) = −1.7 and E0+(γn → π0n) = 1.0, compared to LET-only predictions of −2.4 and 0.4 respectively.
- The charge radius of the nucleon contributes a correction −M²/(6α²) μ² to A(−), with α² = 0.175 GeV², reducing charged pion production by 0.5 × 10⁻³/mπ.

## Context

This work extends the proof of the low energy theorem from the constituent quark model studies by Drechsel and Tiator (1992, 1984) and Scherer and Schoch (1987), showing that no constraints on nucleon size or internal transitions are needed when starting from the chiral quark model of Manohar and Georgi (1984). The result that P-wave baryon transitions dominate the neutral pion production at order μ², while the Δ resonance contributes only at order μ³, contradicts earlier emphasis on nucleon-Δ transitions by N. and S. (1993). This framework enables systematic calculation of η and K photoproduction near threshold and provides a theoretical justification for the ³P₀ model of strong decays, showing it is consistent with the LET while the ³S₁ model is not.
