# The NJL-jet model for quark fragmentation functions

> A description of fragmentation functions which satisfy the momentum and isospin sum rules is presented in an effective quark theory. Concentrating on the pion fragmentation function, we first explain why the elementary (lowest order) fragmentation process q --> q πis completely inadequate to describe the empirical data, although the "crossed" process π--> q \bar{q} describes the quark distribution functions in the pion reasonably well. Taking into account cascade-like processes in a generalized jet-model approach, we then show that the momentum and isospin sum rules can be satisfied naturally, without the introduction of ad hoc parameters. We present results for the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the invariant mass regularization scheme and compare them with the empirical parametrizations. We argue that the NJL-jet model, developed herein, provides a useful framework with which to calculate the fragmentation functions in an effective chiral quark theory.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the elementary (lowest order) fragmentation process q → q π is completely inadequate to describe empirical data, but by incorporating cascade-like processes in a generalized jet-model approach within the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, the momentum and isospin sum rules can be satisfied naturally without introducing ad hoc parameters.

## Method

The authors start from the NJL model to compute elementary quark-to-pion and quark-to-quark splitting functions, then introduce a generalized product ansatz that allows a quark to fragment into a finite number of pions according to a distribution function, taking the limit of infinitely many pions. This leads to an integral equation for the total fragmentation function, which is solved numerically using the invariant mass regularization scheme. The resulting fragmentation functions are compared with empirical parametrizations from the literature.

## Key numbers

- The momentum sum rule for fragmentation functions requires ∑_h ∫_0^1 dz z D_q^h(z) = 1 (Eq. 13), meaning 100% of the initial quark light-cone momentum must be transferred to hadrons.
- For the elementary fragmentation function, ∫_0^1 dz ∑_{τ_π} d_q^π(z) = 1 - Z_Q (Eq. 27), where Z_Q (the bare quark probability) is typically between 0.8 and 0.9 in constituent quark models.
- The NLO analysis of Hirai (2007) found a momentum sum of approximately 0.74 for empirical fragmentation functions, which is much larger than what the elementary process alone can provide.
- The elementary fragmentation functions are one or two orders of magnitude smaller than the empirical functions.

## Context

This work extends the successful description of quark distribution functions in the NJL model (Cloet 2005, Bentz 1999) to fragmentation functions, addressing a long-standing failure of effective quark theories to describe fragmentation without introducing ad hoc normalization constants. The approach contrasts with the Drell-Levy-Yan relation (Drell 1969, Blumlein 2000), which would suggest computing fragmentation functions by analytic continuation of distribution functions but fails due to regularization issues and the inadequacy of single-step processes. The NJL-jet model enables first-principles calculations of fragmentation functions in an effective chiral quark theory, with potential extensions to other meson channels and other effective theories.
