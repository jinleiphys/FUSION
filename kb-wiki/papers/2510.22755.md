# Emergent spin polarization from $ρ$ meson condensation in rotating hadronic matter

> The behavior of vector mesons in extreme environments provides a unique probe of non-perturbative Quantum Chromodynamics. We investigate the conditions for Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) of spin-1 $ρ$ mesons in dense rotating hadronic matter, a regime relevant to the peripheral heavy-ion collisions and the interiors of rapidly rotating neutron stars. When the $ρ$ meson chemical potential ($μ_ρ$) approaches its effective mass ($m_ρ^*$), a phase transition to BEC occurs. We demonstrate that this transition is non-trivially influenced by global rotation, which couples to the spin of the $ρ$ mesons, leading to a macroscopic spin alignment of the condensate along the axis of rotation. This interplay between condensation and rotation results in distinct polarization patterns, which can serve as a possible signature of a BEC in experiments. The results suggest that rapidly rotating neutron stars may harbor an anisotropic, spin-polarized $ρ$-condensed phase, which could impact their equation of state.

## Key claim

Global rotation enhances Bose-Einstein condensation of spin-1 ρ mesons by lowering the critical chemical potential required for the transition, and the condensate itself amplifies spin alignment beyond what rotation alone would produce; the effect is strongest at moderate rotation speeds, with the critical temperature showing a non-monotonic dependence on angular velocity.

## Method

The authors model a non-interacting gas of ρ mesons in a rotating cylindrical container of radius R = 5 fm, using relativistic Bose-Einstein statistics with the single-particle energy shifted by the rotation as ε_l = sqrt(k_r² + k_z² + m²) - (l + s)ω, where l is the orbital angular momentum quantum number and s is the spin projection along the rotation axis. The radial momentum is quantized by the boundary condition J_l(ξ_{l,i}) = 0 at r = R, and thermodynamic quantities are evaluated at the edge r = R to isolate rotational effects. The BEC transition condition is determined by setting the chemical potential equal to the minimum single-particle energy ε_min, which is found by minimizing over l and s, and the spin polarization is computed separately for the condensate and thermal components from the densities of the three spin substates.

## Key numbers

- Total ρ meson density fixed at n_tot = 0.15 fm⁻³ throughout the study.
- ρ meson mass m = 0.775 GeV (used as the chemical potential at BEC for ω = 0).
- System radius R = 5 fm, giving a causality-imposed maximum rotation ω_max = 0.2 fm⁻¹ ≈ 0.0394 GeV.
- Rotation values studied: ω = 0.003, 0.005, and 0.02 GeV, with ω ≈ 0.015 GeV cited as the vorticity in heavy-ion collisions from STAR (2017).
- At ω = 0.015 GeV, the critical chemical potential for BEC is lowered to roughly two-fifths of the ρ meson mass.
- Heavy-ion collisions in the range sqrt(s_NN) ≈ 3 to 10 GeV are identified as providing suitable conditions for observing the predicted effects.

## Context

This work extends the study of Bose-Einstein condensation from pseudoscalar pions (studied extensively by Begun (2008), Savchuk (2020), and Pradhan (2022)) to vector ρ mesons, which carry spin-1 and thus exhibit a richer polarization structure. Prior work on ρ meson condensation in neutron stars was done by Voskresensky (1997), Mallick (2014), and Shivam (2019), and in effective field theories by Brauner (2016), but none considered the interplay with rotation. The authors also build on the formalism for rotating Bose gases developed by Fujimoto (2021) and Mukherjee (2023). The results enable experimental searches for ρ meson BEC via dilepton decay angular distributions in low-energy heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, FAIR, and NICA, and suggest that rapidly rotating neutron stars may harbor a spin-polarized ρ condensate that could soften the equation of state and produce anisotropic pressure.
