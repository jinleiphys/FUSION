# Simulating collectivity in dense baryon matter with multiple fluids

> We report on construction of a modern multi-fluid approach to heavy-ion collisions at FAIR/BES energies (MUFFIN) and show the reproduction of basic experimental observables in Au-Au collisions in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program. We also show the $p_T$-differential and $p_T$-integrated polarization of (anti-)$Λ$ hyperons. In MUFFIN simulations, we observe a strong splitting between polarizations of $Λ$ and anti-$Λ$. The splitting is driven purely by a finite baryon chemical potential.

# Simulating collectivity in dense baryon matter with multiple fluids

## Key claim

The MUFFIN multi-fluid model reproduces basic experimental observables in Au-Au collisions across the RHIC Beam Energy Scan range and predicts a strong splitting between Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperon polarization driven purely by finite baryon chemical potential, though the authors note this splitting appears to contradict original STAR results while updated STAR measurements show a splitting more consistent with zero within error bars.

## Method

The authors implement a three-fluid dynamical model (MUFFIN) where projectile and target nuclei are treated as two cold, baryon-rich fluids that interpenetrate and decelerate via frictional forces, with energy and momentum loss generating a third "fireball" fluid representing produced particles. The friction terms are derived from kinetic theory of NN and N-pi collisions and scaled to account for QGP phase emergence and fit experimental data. Hyperon polarization is evaluated at the particlization hypersurface using the formula from Becattini (2013) including shear-spin coupling from Becattini (2021), with no re-adjustment of model parameters for polarization observables.

## Key numbers

- Pseudorapidity distributions of charged hadrons and net proton rapidity distributions shown for Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV and 19.6 GeV, compared to PHOBOS data and NA49 data at 17.3 GeV respectively
- Transverse momentum spectra of positive pions, kaons, protons and anti-protons shown for Au-Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 GeV compared to STAR data
- Transverse momentum dependent polarization of Lambda hyperons computed at mid-rapidity for 20-50% central Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV
- Momentum-averaged (global) polarization of Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons computed for 20-50% central Au-Au collisions across multiple collision energies, compared to STAR experimental data and prior UrQMD+vHLLE hybrid model results from Karpenko (2016)

## Context

This work extends the multi-fluid dynamics approach to heavy-ion collisions at FAIR/BES energies (a few to 100 GeV center-of-mass), where weaker Lorentz contraction requires modeling of nuclei passing through each other over several fm/c. The MUFFIN model builds on prior multi-fluid frameworks and is compared to the conventional "sandwich-type" hybrid model UrQMD+vHLLE from Karpenko (2015). The results enable further investigation of baryon chemical potential effects on hyperon polarization and provide predictions for upcoming experimental measurements at FAIR and BES energies.
