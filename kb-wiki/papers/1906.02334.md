# Equilibrium thermodynamic susceptibilities for a dense degenerate Dirac field

> Parity preserving relativistic fluids in four spacetime dimensions admit seven independent thermodynamic susceptibilities at the second order in the hydrodynamic derivative expansion. We compute all parity-even second order thermodynamic susceptibilities for a free massive Dirac field at zero temperature and a nonzero chemical potential, based on the Kubo formulas reported in P. Kovtun and A. Shukla, Kubo formulas for thermodynamic transport coefficients, J. High Energy Phys. 10 (2018) 007. We also compute the second order constitutive relations for the energy-momentum tensor and the conserved current in the absence of external gauge fields.

## Key claim

The paper computes all seven parity-even second-order thermodynamic susceptibilities for a free massive Dirac field at zero temperature and nonzero chemical potential, using Kubo formulas from Kovtun and Shukla (2018). The results are valid only for chemical potential magnitude greater than the fermion mass, with all susceptibilities vanishing for \(|\mu| < m\).

## Method

The author evaluates zero-frequency flat-space two-point correlation functions of the energy-momentum tensor and the conserved current at one-loop order in Euclidean signature, using the Kubo formulas derived by Kovtun and Shukla (2018). The chemical potential is incorporated by shifting the zeroth component of the Euclidean momentum as \(k^0 \rightarrow k^0 + i\mu\) in the Dirac propagator. The resulting momentum integrals are performed using contour integration in the complex \(q^0\) plane, with UV divergences removed by standard renormalization, keeping only the \(\mu\)-dependent terms.

## Key numbers

* \(f_1 = -\frac{1}{48\pi^2} \left[ |\mu|\sqrt{\mu^2 - m^2} - m^2 \log\left(\frac{|\mu|+\sqrt{\mu^2 - m^2}}{m}\right) \right]\) for \(|\mu| > m\), zero otherwise
* \(f_2 = \frac{|\mu|}{24\pi^2} \frac{2m^2 - 3\mu^2}{\sqrt{\mu^2 - m^2}}\) for \(|\mu| > m\)
* \(f_3 = -\frac{|\mu|}{96\pi^2} \sqrt{\mu^2 - m^2}\) for \(|\mu| > m\)
* \(f_4 = \frac{1}{12\pi^2} \log\left(\frac{|\mu| + \sqrt{\mu^2 - m^2}}{m}\right)\) for \(|\mu| > m\)
* \(f_5 = \frac{\text{Sign}(\mu)}{24\pi^2} \sqrt{\mu^2 - m^2}\) for \(|\mu| > m\)
* \(f_6 = -\frac{1}{24\pi^2} \left[ \frac{|\mu|}{\sqrt{\mu^2 - m^2}} + 2 \log\left(\frac{|\mu|+\sqrt{\mu^2 - m^2}}{m}\right)\right]\) for \(|\mu| > m\)
* \(f_7 = \frac{\text{Sign}(\mu)}{12\pi^2} \frac{3\mu^2 - 2m^2}{\sqrt{\mu^2 - m^2}}\) for \(|\mu| > m\)

## Context

This work extends the equilibrium generating functional formalism of Banerjee (2012) and Jensen (2012) by providing explicit microscopic computations for a free Dirac field, using the Kubo formulas derived by Kovtun and Shukla (2018). The results are relevant for astrophysical applications such as white dwarfs and neutron stars, and for QCD at high density. The susceptibilities satisfy the expected Weyl invariance conditions in the conformal limit \(m \rightarrow 0\) and reproduce the correct trace anomaly contribution from the one-loop beta function coefficient \(b_0 = 1/6\pi^2\).
