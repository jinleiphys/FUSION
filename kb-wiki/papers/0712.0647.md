# Bridging the primordial A=8 divide with Catalyzed Big Bang Nucleosynthesis

> Catalysis of nuclear reactions by metastable charged particles X^- opens the possibility for primordial production of elements with A>7. We calculate the abundance of ^9Be, where synthesis is mediated by the formation of (^8Be X^-) bound states, finding a dramatic enhancement over the standard BBN prediction: ^9Be/^1H = 10^{-13}\times(Y_X/10^{-5}). Thus observations of ^9Be abundances at low metallicity offers a uniquely sensitive probe of many particle physics models that predict X^-, including variants of supersymmetric models. Comparing the catalytically-enhanced abundances of primordial ^6Li and ^9Be, we find the relation ^9Be/^6Li = (2-5)\times 10^{-3} that holds over a wide range of X^- abundances and lifetimes.

## Key claim

Catalysis of nuclear reactions by metastable negatively charged particles X^- can bridge the A=8 nuclear divide, producing primordial ^9Be at levels up to 10^-13 relative to hydrogen, but this enhancement is conditional on the X^- abundance Y_X and lifetime tau_X, with the relation ^9Be/^6Li = (2-5)x10^-3 holding over a wide range of parameters.

## Method

The author calculates the catalyzed neutron capture rate for (^8Be X^-) + n -> ^9Be + X^- using a Breit-Wigner resonance formalism, extracting the nuclear E1 transition strength from the known gamma-decay width of the 1/2+ excited state of ^9Be and connecting it to the p-wave decay width of the (^9Be X^-) system. The formation rate of (^8Be X^-) is computed via two channels: radiative alpha capture on (^4He X^-) through resonant atomic states with n=3, and molecular formation via (^4He X^- X^-) intermediates. These rates are incorporated into a reaction network to calculate freezeout abundances of ^6Li and ^9Be at temperatures below T_9 = 0.12.

## Key numbers

- ^9Be/^1H abundance from CBBN: 10^-13 x (Y_X/10^-5), where Y_X is the X^- abundance per baryon at t = 2x10^4 seconds
- Binding energies of (^8Be X^-) and (^9Be X^-) bound states: 1408 keV and 1477 keV respectively, with correlated uncertainty up to 50 keV depending on charge distribution model
- Catalyzed neutron capture rate: N_A <sigma_n v_n> = 2x10^9 cm^3 mol^-1 s^-1 at T_9 = 0.1
- Decay width for (^9Be 1/2+ X^-) -> ^9Be 3/2- + X^-: Gamma_out = 5 keV, four orders of magnitude larger than the gamma width Gamma_gamma = 0.57 eV
- ^9Be/^6Li ratio: (2-5)x10^-3, valid for tau_X > 2000 seconds
- For tau_X = 2000 s and Y_X = 0.1: ^6Li = 1.3x10^-11, ^9Be = 7x10^-14

## Context

This work extends the catalyzed Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (CBBN) framework introduced by Pospelov (2007) and Bird (2007), which showed that X^- particles can bridge the A=5 divide by catalyzing ^6Li production. The paper demonstrates that the A=8 divide is also bridged through (^8Be X^-) bound states, enabling primordial production of ^9Be at levels far above the standard BBN prediction of ^9Be/^1H < 10^-18 from the work of Be_SBBN. The results reinforce the lithium bound on X^- lifetimes from Pospelov (2007) and provide a distinctive observational signature that can be tested against low-metallicity ^9Be observations from Be9_obs and ^6Li measurements from Li6.
