# The role of vector-baryon channels and resonances in the $γp \to K^0 Σ^+$ and $γn \to K^0 Σ^0$ reactions near the $K^* Λ$ threshold

> We have studied the $γp \to K^0 Σ^+$ reaction in the energy region around the $K^* Λ$ and $K^* Σ$ thresholds, where the CBELSA/TAPS cross section shows a sudden drop and the differential cross section experiences a transition from a forward-peaked distribution to a flat one. Our coupled channel model incorporates the dynamics of the vector meson-baryon interaction which is obtained from the hidden gauge formalism. We find that the cross section in this energy region results from a delicate interference between amplitudes having $K^* Λ$ and $K^*Σ$ intermediate states. The sharp downfall is dictated by the presence of a nearby $N^*$ resonance produced by our model, a feature that we have employed to predict its properties. We also show results for the complementary $γn \to K^0 Σ^0$ reaction, the measurement of which would test the mechanism proposed in this work.

# Wiki Page: Role of Vector-Baryon Channels in γp → K⁰Σ⁺ and γn → K⁰Σ⁰ Near the K*Λ Threshold

## Key claim

The sharp drop in the γp → K⁰Σ⁺ cross section around √s = 2000 MeV arises from a delicate destructive interference between amplitudes with intermediate K*Λ and K*Σ states, magnified by a dynamically generated N* resonance near 1970–2035 MeV; the model does not reproduce the forward-peaked differential cross section at lower energies, and the authors note that explicit background contributions and genuine resonant terms would be needed for a complete description.

## Method

The authors construct a coupled-channel model using the hidden gauge formalism to describe vector meson-baryon (VB) interactions, where the photon converts into ρ⁰, ω, or φ mesons that then scatter via VB coupled channels to produce intermediate K*Λ or K*Σ states. These intermediate states are converted to the final K⁰Σ⁺ or K⁰Σ⁰ via pion exchange, with the VB amplitudes taken from the earlier work of Angelsvec et al. (2012). A Kroll-Ruderman contact term is added to preserve gauge invariance, and the loop integrals are regularized with subtraction constants at a scale μ = 630 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The K*Λ threshold lies at √s = 2010 MeV and the K*Σ threshold at √s = 2087 MeV.
- The original model (all subtraction constants a_l = -2) gives a resonance pole at z = 1977 + i53 MeV; the real-axis method yields M_R = 1972 MeV and Γ_R = 64 MeV.
- Adjusting subtraction constants to -1.65 (except a_ρN = -2) shifts the resonance to M_R = 2035 MeV and Γ_R = 125 MeV.
- The K* meson width used in the loop functions is Γ = 50.5 MeV.
- The Yukawa form factor cut-off is Λ = 850 MeV, adjusted to reproduce the experimental cross section size.
- The ratio (σ(0°) − σ(90°))/(σ(0°) + σ(90°)) for γp → K⁰Σ⁺ varies from 0.14 at W = 1823 MeV to 0.31 at W = 2018 MeV, then decreases to 0.16 at W = 2197 MeV.

## Context

This work addresses the failure of existing K-matrix coupled-channel models such as K-MAID and SAID (both cited as 2000s-era models) and those of Anisovich (2005), Sarantsev (2005), and Usov (2005) to reproduce the CBELSA/TAPS data for γp → K⁰Σ⁺. The approach extends the vector-baryon interaction model of Angelsvec et al. (2012), which dynamically generates 1/2⁻ and 3/2⁻ resonances, and builds on the interference mechanism identified by Mishanaka et al. (2010) for γp → ηp. The results predict a peak in γn → K⁰Σ⁰ where the charged reaction shows a drop, offering a testable signature; a measurement of the neutral reaction would test the proposed mechanism. The predicted resonance around 2035 MeV supports the existence of odd-parity N* states near 2080 MeV previously suggested by Xie et al. (2010) from LEPS data on γp → K⁺Λ(1520).
