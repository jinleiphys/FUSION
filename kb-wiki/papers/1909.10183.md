# Towards a combined analysis of inclusive/exclusive electroproduction

> In view of the major advances achieved by the CLAS experiments in studying the N* electroexcitation amplitudes, as well as further extension of these studies in the experiments with CLAS12, we present an approach for the evaluation of the resonant contributions to inclusive electron scattering off protons. For the first time, the resonant contributions are determined from the experimental results on N* electrocouplings available from the data analyses of exclusive meson electroproduction off protons. This is a useful benchmark for future endeavours on understanding the transition region between low and high-energy regions, strongly related to tests on quark-hadron duality.

# Towards a combined analysis of inclusive/exclusive electroproduction

## Key claim
For the first time, resonant contributions to inclusive electron scattering off protons are determined from experimental results on N* electrocouplings obtained from exclusive meson electroproduction data, rather than from inclusive data themselves. This approach provides a benchmark for understanding the transition region between low and high energies and tests of quark-hadron duality, but the results are currently limited to the kinematic range where exclusive electroproduction data exist (N* masses below 1.8 GeV and Q² below 5.0 GeV²).

## Method
The authors evaluate resonant contributions to inclusive virtual photon-proton cross sections using a relativistic Breit-Wigner ansatz, with resonance masses, spins, and total hadronic decay widths taken from the PDG, and electromagnetic decay widths computed from CLAS data on transverse (A₁/₂, A₃/₂) and longitudinal (S₁/₂) electrocouplings. The resonant contributions to the unpolarized inclusive cross section and structure functions F₁ and F₂ are then computed, and the difference between CLAS inclusive data and these independently-determined resonant contributions yields an estimate of the non-resonant background. A bootstrap-based approach propagates experimental uncertainties from the electrocoupling data to the inclusive observables.

## Key numbers
- CLAS data on resonance electrocouplings are available for most excited nucleon states in the mass range M_N* < 1.8 GeV and at Q² < 5.0 GeV²
- The interpolation/extrapolation tools cover electrocouplings from Q² = 0.5 GeV² to Q² = 5.0 GeV²
- The inclusive structure function data used for comparison cover the region 1.07 GeV < W < 4.0 GeV and Q² < 7.0 GeV²
- The longitudinal piece of the resonant cross section does not exceed 30% of the total cross section in the kinematic region shown
- The N(1440)1/2⁺ resonance has a total decay width of 300 MeV, causing its contribution to appear in all three resonance regions at W < 1.7 GeV
- CLAS12 will achieve coverage up to Q² < 12 GeV² in the resonance region
- Future CLAS data will provide electrocouplings in the mass range from 1.6 GeV to 2.0 GeV and 0.4 GeV² < Q² < 5.0 GeV²

## Context
This work extends the program of CLAS experiments (Aznauryan 2011, Mokeev 2018) that determined N* electroexcitation amplitudes from exclusive meson electroproduction, and builds on the formalism developed in Hiller Blin (2019). It enables a new approach to studying the transition between resonance-dominated and deep inelastic scattering regimes, relevant to tests of quark-hadron duality (Bloom 1970). The results provide a phenomenological tool for analyzing upcoming CLAS12 inclusive data (Burkert 2018) and may impact understanding of parton distributions at Bjorken x values close to unity within the resonance region, complementing earlier inclusive analyses by Christy (2007) and Osipenko (2003).
