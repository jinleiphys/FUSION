# Three-body optical potentials in $(d,p)$ reactions and their influence on indirect study of stellar nucleosynthesis

> Model uncertainties, arising due to suppression of target excitations in the description of deuteron scattering and resulting in a modification of the two-body interactions in a three-body system, are investigated for several $(d,p)$ reactions serving as indirect tools for studying the astrophysical $(p,γ)$ reactions relevant to $rp$-process. The three-body nature of deuteron-target potential is treated within adiabatic distorted wave approximation (ADWA) which relies on dominant contribution from the components of the three-body deuteron-target wave function with small $n$-$p$ separations. This results in a simple prescription for treating the explicit energy-dependence of two-body optical potentials in a three-body system requiring nucleon optical potentials to be evaluated at a shifted energy with respect to the standard value of half the deuteron incident energy. In addition, the ADWA allows for leading-order multiple scattering effects to be estimated, which leads to a simple renormalization of the adiabatic potential's imaginary part by the factor of two. These effects are assessed using both nonlocal and local optical potential systematics for $^{26}$Al, $^{30}$P, $^{34}$Cl and $^{56}$Ni targets at deuteron incident energy of 12 MeV typical for experiments with radioactive beams in inverse kinematics. The model uncertainties induced by the three-body nature of deuteron-target scattering are found to be within 40$\%$ both in the main peak of angular distributions and in total $(d,p)$ cross sections. At higher deuteron energies, around 60 MeV, model uncertainties can reach 100\% in the total cross sections. A few examples of application to astrophysically interesting proton resonances in $^{27}$Si and $^{57}$Cu obtained using $(d,p)$ reactions and mirror symmetry are given.

## Key claim

The paper quantifies model uncertainties in $(d,p)$ reactions used as indirect tools for astrophysical $(p,\gamma)$ studies, finding that induced three-body effects (I3B) from target excitations cause uncertainties within 40% at 12 MeV deuteron energy but can reach 100% at 60 MeV, with the magnitude depending on the choice of nucleon-nucleon interaction model and whether the I3B force is included.

## Method

The authors used the adiabatic distorted wave approximation (ADWA) to treat the three-body deuteron-target system, incorporating an energy shift for nucleon optical potentials (half the deuteron energy plus the average n-p kinetic energy) and a doubling of the imaginary part of the adiabatic potential to account for leading-order multiple scattering effects. They performed calculations for four reactions, $^{26}$Al$(d,p)^{27}$Al, $^{30}$P$(d,p)^{31}$P, $^{34}$Cl$(d,p)^{35}$Cl, and $^{56}$Ni$(d,p)^{57}$Ni, at a deuteron incident energy of 12 MeV using both nonlocal (GRZ) and local (KD03) optical potential systematics, with four different nucleon-nucleon interaction models (Hulthen, AV18, CD-Bonn, and chiral EFT at N4LO) to assess model dependence.

## Key numbers

- Model uncertainties from three-body effects are within 40% both in the main peak of angular distributions and in total $(d,p)$ cross sections at 12 MeV deuteron energy.
- At higher deuteron energies around 60 MeV, model uncertainties can reach 100% in total cross sections.
- The energy shift $\Delta E$ varies from 57 MeV (Hulthen model) to 123.6 MeV (chiral EFT at N4LO with 0.8 fm regulator), with AV18 giving 63.1 MeV and CD-Bonn giving 56.3 MeV.
- Without I3B effects, the spread in angular distributions due to different NN model choice is up to 25% for $l=2$ and $l=3$ transfers, and 5-15% for $l=0$ and $l=1$ transfers.
- With I3B effects included, the spread between NN models does not exceed 15% for $l=2,3$ transfers and is no larger than 10% for $l=0,1$ transfers.
- The ratio $\sigma_{\rm GRZ}^{\rm I3B}/\sigma_{\rm GRZ}$ (peak cross section with vs. without I3B) ranges from 0.602 to 0.818 across all studied reactions and states.
- For $^{56}$Ni$(d,p)^{57}$Ni, the ratio $\sigma_{\rm GRZ}/\sigma_{\rm KD03}$ (nonlocal GRZ vs. local KD03 without I3B) ranges from 1.212 to 1.659 for different states.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Johnson and Tandy (1974) for ADWA and the three-body optical potential developments of Johnson (2014) and Dinmore et al. (2019), applying it to astrophysically relevant $(d,p)$ reactions for the first time. The reactions studied serve as indirect probes for $(p,\gamma)$ capture in the rp-process, specifically for $^{26}$Al$(p,\gamma)^{27}$Si (important for Wolf-Rayet and AGB stars), $^{30}$P$(p,\gamma)^{31}$S (dominating sulfur isotope abundance uncertainties), $^{34}$Cl$(p,\gamma)^{35}$Ar (relevant to oxygen-neon novae and pre-solar grains), and $^{56}$Ni$(p,\gamma)^{57}$Cu (critical for late rp-process and type Ia supernovae). The results enable more reliable extraction of asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) from $(d,p)$ reactions and their use via mirror symmetry to determine proton widths for astrophysical resonances, as demonstrated for $^{27}$Si and $^{57}$Cu.
