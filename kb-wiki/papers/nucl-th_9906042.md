# Weak Decay of $Λ$ in Nuclei: Direct Quark Mechanism vs Meson Exchange

> Nonmesonic decays of $Λ$ in nuclear medium and light hypernuclei are studied by using the $ΛN \to NN$ weak transition potential derived from the meson exchange mechanism and the direct quark mechanism. The long range part of the transition potential is described by exchanges of the pseudoscalar mesons ($π$, $K$, $η$), while the vector mesons ($ρ$, $ω$, $K^\ast$) may be considered as the medium- and short-range part in the meson exchange picture. We propose the direct quark transition potential as the short range part, which is derived from the matrix elements of the $ΔS=1$ effective weak Hamiltonian in the two baryon states. The results indicate that the direct quark contribution is significantly large and its behavior is qualitatively different from the vector meson exchanges. We also find that the decay rate is sensitive to the choice of form factor and that a soft cutoff must be used for the pion-baryon verteces so that the strong tensor transition is suppressed. We find that the $π+ K + DQ$ results are compatible with experiment although the $n/p$ ratio is still too large. The $π^+$ decays of light hypernuclei are related to the $\DI=3/2$ amplitudes of the nonmesonic decay. The role of chiral symmetry for the pionic decays are discussed.

## Key claim

The direct quark (DQ) mechanism for the short-range part of the ΛN → NN weak transition potential gives a significantly large contribution that is qualitatively different from vector meson exchanges, and when combined with π and K meson exchange using a soft cutoff form factor, the results are compatible with experiment for total decay rates, although the predicted n/p ratio remains too small (≈0.2) compared to the observed value (≈1 for heavy hypernuclei).

## Method

The authors derived the ΛN → NN weak transition potential from two sources: (1) meson exchange (ME) for the long-range part, using pseudoscalar mesons (π, K, η) and vector mesons (ρ, ω, K*), with coupling constants determined from Λ → Nπ decay amplitudes and SU(6)w symmetry; and (2) a direct quark (DQ) transition potential for the short-range part, calculated from matrix elements of the ΔS=1 effective weak Hamiltonian in nonrelativistic valence quark cluster model wave functions. They applied these potentials to compute nonmesonic decay rates of Λ in nuclear matter (using plane waves with short-range correlations) and in light s-shell hypernuclei (⁵ΛHe, ⁴ΛHe, ⁴ΛH) using harmonic oscillator shell model wave functions, testing three form factor choices (single pole, double pole, Gaussian) for the meson-baryon vertices.

## Key numbers

- The momentum transfer in ΛN → NN is about 400 MeV/c, or (0.5 fm)⁻¹.
- The energy transfer q₀ ≈ (mΛ − mN)/2 ≈ 88 MeV, which reduces the effective pion mass by about 25%.
- For OPE alone in nuclear matter, the total decay rate ranges from 1.103 ΓΛ (SP form factor, Λπ = 630 MeV) to 2.819 ΓΛ (no form factor), with Γn/Γp ratios from 0.097 to 0.342.
- For the "π + K + DQ" model with soft DP form factor (Λπ = 800 MeV) in nuclear matter: total rate = 1.204 ΓΛ, Γp = 0.998 ΓΛ, Γn = 0.206 ΓΛ, Γn/Γp = 0.207.
- For ⁵ΛHe with "π + K + DQ": total rate = 0.521 ΓΛ, Γp = 0.435 ΓΛ, Γn = 0.085 ΓΛ, Γn/Γp = 0.195; experimental values from one measurement are total = 0.41 ± 0.14 ΓΛ, Γn/Γp = 0.93 ± 0.55.
- For ⁴ΛHe with "π + K + DQ": total rate = 0.309 ΓΛ, Γp = 0.302 ΓΛ, Γn = 0.007 ΓΛ, Γn/Γp = 0.024; experimental total = 0.19 ± 0.04 ΓΛ.
- For ⁴ΛH with "π + K + DQ": total rate = 0.120 ΓΛ, Γp = 0.060 ΓΛ, Γn = 0.059 ΓΛ, Γn/Γp = 0.983; experimental total = 0.15 ± 0.13 ΓΛ.
- The DQ mechanism alone gives Γn/Γp = 1.071 in nuclear matter, much larger than meson-exchange predictions.
- The π⁺/π⁻ ratio from ⁴ΛHe is about 5% experimentally, but conventional charge-exchange mechanisms explain only 1.2%.

## Context

This work extends prior meson-exchange studies of nonmesonic Λ decay (McGovern and Miller (1991), Dubach et al. (1990), Parreño et al. (1997)) by adding a direct quark mechanism derived from the ΔS=1 effective weak Hamiltonian, following the authors' own earlier work (Oka et al. (1995), Inoue et al. (1998)). The paper addresses the long-standing "n/p ratio problem" where meson-exchange models predict Γn/Γp ≈ 0.1 while experiments suggest ≈1. The DQ mechanism improves this ratio but does not fully resolve it. The work also shows that soft π⁺ emission from hypernuclei probes the ΔI=3/2 component of the weak Hamiltonian, providing a new experimental handle on the isospin structure of the weak interaction. The results enable future studies combining quark and meson degrees of freedom in weak baryon-baryon interactions, and highlight the need for better experimental data on the n/p ratio and parity-violating observables.
