# Relativistic mean-field approximation with density-dependent screening meson masses in nuclear matter

> The Debye screening masses of the $σ$, $ω$ and neutral $ρ$ mesons and the photon are calculated in the relativistic mean-field approximation. As the density of the nucleon increases, all the screening masses of mesons increase. It shows a different result with Brown-Rho scaling, which implies a reduction in the mass of all the mesons in the nuclear matter except the pion. Replacing the masses of the mesons with their corresponding screening masses in Walecka-1 model, five saturation properties of the nuclear matter are fixed reasonably, and then a density-dependent relativistic mean-field model is proposed without introducing the non-linear self-coupling terms of mesons.

## Key claim
The authors propose a density-dependent relativistic mean-field model where the masses of the sigma, omega, and neutral rho mesons are replaced by their Debye screening masses, which increase with nucleon density. This contradicts Brown-Rho scaling (which predicts meson masses decrease in nuclear matter) and allows the model to reproduce nuclear matter saturation properties without introducing nonlinear self-coupling terms.

## Method
The self-energies of virtual sigma, omega, neutral rho mesons and the photon were calculated in the relativistic mean-field approximation at zero time-space momentum (static infrared limit k^0=0, k→0). These self-energies were used to compute Debye screening masses via m*_α = sqrt(m²_α + Σ_α). The screening masses were then substituted for the bare meson masses in the Walecka-1 model Lagrangian, and the three coupling constants (g²_σ/m²_σ, g²_ω/m²_ω, g²_ρ/m²_ρ) were fitted to reproduce nuclear matter saturation properties.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density: 0.149 fm^{-3}
- Binding energy: 16.669 MeV
- Compression modulus: 280.1 MeV
- Symmetry energy coefficient: 32.8 MeV
- Effective nucleon mass: 0.808 M_N (where M_N is the bare nucleon mass)
- Fitted parameters: g²_σ/m²_σ = 8.297 fm², g²_ω/m²_ω = 3.683 fm², g²_ρ/m²_ρ = 5.187 fm²
- Photon screening mass in symmetric nuclear matter at nucleon density 0.16 fm^{-3}: about 5.42 MeV
- Ratio of fractional change of scalar meson mass to fractional change of nucleon mass at low density: approximately -1/2

## Context
This work extends the relativistic mean-field approach of Walecka (1974) and Serot and Walecka (1986) by incorporating density-dependent screening masses, providing an alternative to models that introduce nonlinear self-coupling terms such as Boguta and Bodmer (1977) or the TM1 parameter set of Sugahara and Toki (1994). The results contradict Brown-Rho scaling (Brown, 1991), which predicts meson masses decrease in nuclear matter. The model produces a softer equation of state at high density compared to NL3 (Piekarewicz, 1997), NLSH (Piekarewicz, 1993), and TM1, making it potentially applicable to neutron star cores and relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The approach is similar to the density-dependent quark-meson coupling model QMC-2 (1997).
