# Towards a coupled-channel optical potential for rare-earth nuclei

> We present an outline of an extensive study of the effects of collective couplings and nuclear deformations on integrated cross sections as well as on angular distributions in a consistent manner for neutron-induced reactions on nuclei in the rare-earth region. This specific subset of the nuclide chart was chosen precisely because of a clear static deformation pattern. We analyze the convergence of the coupled-channel calculations regarding the number of states being explicitly coupled. A model for deforming the spherical Koning-Delaroche optical potential as function of quadrupole and hexadecupole deformations is also proposed, inspired by previous works. We demonstrate that the obtained results of calculations for total, elastic, inelastic, and capture cross sections, as well as elastic and inelastic angular distributions are in remarkably good agreement with experimental data for scattering energies around a few MeV.

## Key claim
Deforming the spherical Koning-Delaroche optical potential and using it in coupled-channel calculations without further modification provides good agreement with experimental data for neutron-induced reactions on rare-earth nuclei, consistent with the insight that scattering in this region is very close to the adiabatic (frozen nucleus) limit.

## Method
The authors deformed the spherical Koning-Delaroche optical potential by applying quadrupole (\(\beta_2\)) and hexadecupole (\(\beta_4\)) deformation parameters to the nuclear radii, then performed coupled-channel calculations using the reaction model code Empire (with direct reactions computed by the code Ecis). They applied a volume-conservation correction to the reduced radius \(R_0\) and tested the sensitivity of results to variations in deformation parameters of up to 30%. Calculations were performed for 34 deformed rare-earth nuclei and compared to experimental data for total, elastic, inelastic, and capture cross sections, as well as elastic and inelastic angular distributions.

## Key numbers
- Total cross sections were calculated for 34 deformed nuclei including \(^{152,154}\)Sm, \(^{153}\)Eu, \(^{155,156,157,158,160}\)Gd, \(^{159}\)Tb, \(^{162,163,164}\)Dy, \(^{165}\)Ho, \(^{166,167,168,170}\)Er, \(^{169}\)Tm, \(^{171,172,173,174,176}\)Yb, \(^{175,176}\)Lu, \(^{177,178,179,180}\)Hf, \(^{181}\)Ta, and \(^{182,183,184,186}\)W.
- The highest deformation among studied nuclei was \(\beta_2 = 0.353\) for \(^{160}\)Gd, corresponding to a radius correction of less than 1%.
- For \(^{184}\)W, with \(\beta_2 = 0.236\), the volume-conservation radius correction was lower than one half percent.
- Inelastic angular distributions were shown for the first \(2^+\) state of \(^{184}\)W at excitation energy 111.2 keV.
- For \(^{162}\)Dy, total cross sections were insensitive to deformation variations at incident energies around 40 keV, 2 MeV, and 5 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Dietrich (2012), which showed that scattering from rare-earth and actinide nuclei is very near the adiabatic (frozen nucleus) limit, suggesting that loss of flux to rotational excitations might be unimportant. The authors test this idea by deforming the global spherical Koning-Delaroche optical potential, which was not designed for deformed nuclei, and performing coupled-channel calculations without further adjustments. This contrasts with the alternative approach of Kunieda et al. (2007), which attempted to unify scattering from spherical and deformed nuclei by treating all nuclei as statically deformed. The results provide a first step toward filling the current lack of optical model potentials for the rare-earth region.
