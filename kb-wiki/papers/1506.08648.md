# Drag induced radiative loss from semi-hard heavy quarks

> The case of gluon bremsstrahlung off a heavy quark in extended nuclear matter is revisited within the higher twist formalism. In particular, the in-medium modification of "semi-hard" heavy quarks is studied, where the momentum of the heavy quark is larger but comparable to the mass of the heavy quark ($p \gtrsim M$). In contrast to all prior calculations, where the gluon emission spectrum is entirely controlled by the transverse momentum diffusion parameter ($\hat q$), both for light and heavy quarks, in this work, we demonstrate that the gluon emission spectrum for a heavy quark (unlike that for flavors) is also sensitive to $\hat e$, which so far has been used to quantify the amount of light-cone drag experienced by a parton. This mass dependent effect, due to the non-light-like momentum of a semi-hard heavy-quark, leads to an additional energy loss term for heavy-quarks, while resulting in a negligible modification of light flavor (and high energy heavy flavor) loss. This result can be used to estimate the value of this sub-leading non-perturbative jet transport parameter ($\hat e$) from heavy flavor suppression in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that for semi-hard heavy quarks (where momentum is larger but comparable to mass, \(p \gtrsim M\)), the gluon bremsstrahlung spectrum is sensitive not only to the transverse momentum diffusion parameter \(\hat q\) but also to the drag coefficient \(\hat e\) (the loss of light-cone momentum per unit light-cone length), which prior calculations had only used to quantify non-radiative drag loss. This mass-dependent effect arises from the non-light-like momentum of a semi-hard heavy quark and leads to an additional radiative energy loss term for heavy quarks, while producing negligible modification for light flavors or high-energy heavy flavors.

## Method

The calculation is performed within the higher twist formalism for deep inelastic scattering (DIS) of a virtual photon off a heavy quark inside a large nucleus, using power-counting techniques borrowed from Soft-Collinear-Effective-Theory (SCET) to identify the regime where mass-dependent terms cause detectable effects. The authors consider the case of single scattering and single gluon emission, evaluating all real diagrams (with an emitted gluon in the final state) in axial gauge (\(A^- = 0\)), then performing a gradient expansion in the exchanged transverse momentum (\(k_\perp \rightarrow 0\)) to isolate the next-to-leading twist contribution. The formation length of the radiated gluon for semi-hard heavy quarks is short (\(\tau_Q \sim 1/(\lambda Q)\)), which justifies treating single scattering per emission as the leading contribution.

## Key numbers

* The momentum of the final outgoing heavy quark scales as \(p \sim (\sqrt{\lambda}, \sqrt{\lambda}, 0) Q\), with the heavy quark mass scaling as \(M \sim \sqrt{\lambda} Q\), where \(Q \sim 100\) GeV is the hard scale and \(\sqrt{\lambda} Q \sim 10\) GeV.
* The radiated gluon momentum components scale as \(l \sim (\lambda, \lambda, \lambda) Q\) with \(\lambda Q \sim 1\) GeV, and the fraction of light-cone momentum carried by the gluon is \(y = l^-/p^- \sim \sqrt{\lambda}\).
* The formation length of the gluon from a semi-hard heavy quark is \(\tau_Q = 2 l^- / l_\perp^2 \sim 1/(\lambda Q)\), which is shorter than the formation length \(\tau_q \sim 1/(\lambda^2 Q)\) for gluon radiation from near on-shell light flavors.
* The virtual Glauber gluon momentum scales as \(k \sim (\lambda^{3/2}, \lambda^{3/2}, \lambda) Q\) with \(k^2 \simeq -k_\perp^2\), and \(\lambda^{3/2} Q \sim \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\).

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of heavy flavor energy loss that had only considered radiative loss stimulated by transverse momentum diffusion (quantified by \(\hat q\)) and separate drag loss (quantified by \(\hat e\) or \(dE/dx\)), as in the approaches of Zhang (2004), Qin (2009), Mustafa (2004), Abir (2011, 2012), and Wicks (2005). The result enables estimation of the sub-leading non-perturbative jet transport parameter \(\hat e\) from heavy flavor suppression data in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where measurements by STAR (Abelev, 2006), PHENIX (Adare, 2006), and ALICE (2012) have shown larger-than-expected suppression of heavy mesons and non-photonic electrons in the semi-hard regime.
