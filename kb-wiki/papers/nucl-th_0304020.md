# Meson-Nucleon Vertex Form Factors at Finite Temperature Using a Soft Pion Form Factor

> The temperature and density dependence of the meson-nucleon vertex form factors is studied in the framework of thermofield dynamics. Results are obtained for two rather different nucleon-nucleon potentials: the usual Bonn potential and the variation with a softer $πNN$ form factor, due to Holinde and Thomas. In general, the results show only a modest degree of sensitivity to the choice of interaction.

## Key claim

The temperature and density dependence of meson-nucleon vertex form factors shows only a modest degree of sensitivity to the choice of a hard versus soft πNN form factor, except for the ρ meson, where the critical temperature behavior changes qualitatively between the two potentials.

## Method

The authors used thermofield dynamics (TFD) to calculate finite-temperature modifications of meson-nucleon vertex functions from one-meson-exchange Feynman diagrams. They compared results for two different nucleon-nucleon potentials: the standard Bonn potential (OBEP) and a modified Bonn potential with a softer πNN form factor (OBEPTI) due to Holinde and Thomas (1993). The zero-temperature coupling constants and vertex form factors for each potential were taken from published parameter sets, and the temperature and density dependence of the coupling ratios and cutoff parameters were extracted.

## Key numbers

- For the OBEP parametrization, the πNN cutoff mass Λ = 1.05 GeV with g²/4π = 14.9; for the OBEPTI parametrization, the πNN cutoff mass Λ = 0.8 GeV with g²/4π = 14.6.
- The critical temperature at zero density for the π meson is 406 MeV (OBEP) and 429 MeV (OBEPTI); for the ρ meson it is 285 MeV (OBEP) and 281 MeV (OBEPTI); for the ω meson it is 170 MeV (OBEP) and 171 MeV (OBEPTI); for the σ meson it is 93 MeV for both potentials.
- For the π meson, the difference between the two potentials is smallest at a density of order ~5 ρ₀, where ρ₀ is normal nuclear matter density.
- The critical temperature for the π meson decreases more rapidly with increasing density for the softer pion vertex, while for the ρ meson T_c(ρ) is practically constant for OBEPTI but decreases rapidly for OBEP.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by the same authors (2002) that studied temperature dependence of meson-nucleon form factors using only the Bonn potential. It addresses the suggestion by Holinde and Thomas (1993) that a softer πNN form factor with Λ ≈ 700–800 MeV is more consistent with the measured axial-vector form factor, and shows that the choice of form factor mainly affects the ρ meson behavior. The parametrization provided can be used in studies of the equation of state for heavy-ion collisions and the hadron-to-quark-gluon phase transition.
