# From neutron skins and neutron matter to the neutron star crust

> We present the first Bayesian inference of neutron star crust properties to incorporate neutron skin data, including the recent PREX measurement of the neutron skin of $^{208}$Pb, combined with recent chiral effective field theory predictions of pure neutron matter with statistical errors. Using a compressible liquid drop model with an extended Skyrme energy-density functional, we obtain the most stringent constraints to date on the transition pressure $P_{\rm cc}=0.33^{+0.07}_{-0.07}$ MeV fm$^{-3}$ and chemical potential $μ_{\rm cc}=12.6^{+1.8}_{-1.9}$ MeV (which control the mass, moment of inertia and thickness of a neutron star crust), the proton fractions that bracket the pasta phases $y_{\rm p}=0.115^{+0.016}_{-0.017}$ and $y_{\rm cc}=0.041^{+0.007}_{-0.006}$, as well as the relative mass and moment of inertia $ΔM_{\rm p} / ΔM_{\rm c}\approx ΔI_{\rm p} / ΔI_{\rm c} = 0.54^{+0.05}_{-0.09}$ and thickness $ΔR_{\rm p} / ΔR_{\rm c}=0.129^{+0.019}_{-0.030}$ of the layers of non-spherical nuclei (nuclear pasta) in the crust.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first Bayesian inference of neutron star crust properties that incorporates neutron skin data (including the PREX measurement of the 208Pb neutron skin) together with chiral effective field theory predictions of pure neutron matter, but the results depend significantly on the choice of priors for the surface energy parameters, particularly the parameter p controlling the isospin dependence of the interface energy.

## Method

The authors used a compressible liquid drop model with an extended Skyrme energy-density functional to model the neutron star crust, varying seven model parameters: three controlling the bulk equation of state (J, L, Ksym) and four controlling the surface energy (sigma0, sigma0,c, p, and c). They performed Bayesian inference using five different data combinations: neutron skin measurements alone (including PREX), pure neutron matter predictions from chiral effective field theory, and combinations thereof, with two different surface priors (uniform and fit to three-dimensional Hartree-Fock calculations).

## Key numbers

- Crust-core transition pressure Pcc = 0.33+0.07-0.07 MeV fm-3 (using PNM+Skins data with uniform surface priors)
- Crust-core transition chemical potential mucc = 12.6+1.8-1.9 MeV (using PNM+Skins data with uniform surface priors)
- Proton fraction at top of pasta phases yp = 0.115+0.016-0.017 (using PNM+Skins data with uniform surface priors)
- Proton fraction at crust-core transition ycc = 0.041+0.007-0.006 (using PNM+Skins data with uniform surface priors)
- Relative mass and moment of inertia of pasta layers DeltaMp/DeltaMc = DeltaIp/DeltaIc = 0.54+0.05-0.09 (using PNM+Skins data with uniform surface priors)
- Relative thickness of pasta layers DeltaRp/DeltaRc = 0.129+0.019-0.030 (using PNM+Skins data with uniform surface priors)
- From PREX data alone: J = 35.6+5.3-6.6 MeV, L = 83.2+31.5-33.4 MeV, Ksym = -149+199-201 MeV (68% credible intervals)
- The PREX data alone, PNM data alone, and both combined give approximately 10% of models predicting zero pasta in the crust

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian inferences of neutron star crust properties by incorporating neutron skin data for the first time, building on the compressible liquid drop model approach of Lattimer (1991) and the extended Skyrme functional of Zhang (2016) and Lim (2017). The results provide the most stringent constraints to date on the crust-core transition pressure and chemical potential, which control the mass, moment of inertia, and thickness of a neutron star crust. The paper enables more precise predictions for observable phenomena such as crustal oscillation modes, crust cooling, crust shattering, persistent gravitational waves from mountains, and magnetic field evolution, while identifying the surface parameter p as the dominant source of remaining uncertainty that needs to be addressed in future work.
