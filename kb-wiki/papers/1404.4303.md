# Nucleation and cluster formation in low-density nucleonic matter: A mechanism for ternary fission

> Ternary fission yields in the reaction 241Pu(nth,f) are calculated using a new model which assumes a nucleation-time moderated chemical equilibrium in the low density matter which constitutes the neck region of the scissioning system. The temperature, density, proton fraction and fission time required to fit the experimental data are derived and discussed. A reasonably good fit to the experimental data is obtained. This model provides a natural explanation for the observed yields of heavier isotopes relative to those of the lighter isotopes, the observation of low proton yields relative to 2H and 3H yields and the non-observation of 3He, all features which are shared by similar thermal neutron induced and spontaneous fissioning systems.

## Key claim

Ternary fission yields in the reaction 241Pu(nth,f) can be explained by a nucleation-time moderated approach to nuclear statistical equilibrium in the low-density neck region, where the time constraint prevents heavier isotopes from reaching their equilibrium yields, rather than by barrier penetration or evaporative mechanisms.

## Method

The authors employed a nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) code built on libnuceq to calculate relative yields of ternary isotopes, then introduced a nucleation model that modulates the equilibrium yields with a time-dependent complementary error function term. The input parameters (temperature, density, proton fraction, fission time, and critical cluster size) were varied in a multi-parameter minimization to fit the experimental yields of 42 isotopes from Koester et al. (2000) for the 241Pu(nth,f) reaction, using a fit metric M² defined by Lestone (2012).

## Key numbers

- Temperature of 1.4 MeV, density of 4×10⁻⁴ fm⁻³, and proton fraction of 0.34 were derived as best-fit parameters.
- Nucleation time of 6400 fm/c and critical cluster size Ac = 5.4 provided the best fit to the experimental yields.
- Fit metric M² = 1.18 over the entire range of isotopes, and M² = 1.19 for Z ≤ 6 (the range used by Lestone (2012)).
- The ³H yield is 1.56×10⁻⁴ per fission, while ³He is not detected; the model predicts a ³He yield of 1.81×10⁻⁸ per fission.
- The calculated free neutron-to-proton ratio is 4.97×10³.
- The predicted ⁷Li/⁷Be ratio is 1.61×10⁴, giving a predicted ⁷Be yield of 8.22×10⁻¹¹ per fission, compared to an experimental upper limit of ≤ 3.96×10⁻⁸.
- The fit to the mass distribution alone gave M² = 0.561.

## Context

This work extends the statistical equilibrium approach of Valskii (1970s), who also used a four-parameter fit but overpredicted heavier isotope yields, by adding a nucleation time constraint that suppresses heavy cluster formation. It contrasts with dynamic models such as those by Lestone (2012), which use time-dependent emission barriers, and by Rubchenya (1990s), which emphasize neck instabilities. The model enables interpretation of ternary fission as a mesoscopic analog of nuclear pasta phases predicted in neutron star crusts, and suggests that clusterization in low-density nuclear matter may affect analyses of neutron skin thicknesses and the nuclear phase diagram at low temperature and density.
