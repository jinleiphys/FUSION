# Ab Initio Description of Open-Shell Nuclei: Merging No-Core Shell Model and In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group

> We merge two successful ab initio nuclear-structure methods, the no-core shell model (NCSM) and the multi-reference in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) to define a new many-body approach for the comprehensive description of ground and excited states of closed and open-shell nuclei. Building on the key advantages of the two methods---the decoupling of excitations at the many-body level in the IM-SRG and the access to arbitrary nuclei, eigenstates, and observables in the NCSM---their combination enables fully converged no-core calculations for an unprecedented range of nuclei and observables at moderate computational cost. We present applications in the carbon and oxygen isotopic chains, where conventional NCSM calculations are still feasible and provide an important benchmark. The efficiency and rapid convergence of the new approach make it ideally suited for ab initio studies of the complete spectroscopy of nuclei up into the medium-mass regime.

## Key claim

The paper merges the no-core shell model (NCSM) and the multi-reference in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) into a combined approach (IM-NCSM) that can describe ground and excited states of closed and open-shell nuclei, but the accuracy is limited by the truncation of the IM-SRG flow equations at the normal-ordered two-body level, which introduces deviations of approximately 1-4% for ground-state energies in the carbon and oxygen isotopic chains.

## Method

The authors first perform an NCSM calculation in a small model space (Nmax=0) to obtain a multi-determinantal reference state for the nucleus of interest. They then normal-order the Hamiltonian with respect to this reference state and solve the multi-reference IM-SRG flow equations, discarding residual normal-ordered three-body contributions. Finally, the IM-SRG-evolved Hamiltonians are used in NCSM calculations for a range of truncation parameters Nmax to obtain ground- and excited-state energies and eigenvectors.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state energy of O-20 from IM-NCSM deviates by less than 2% from importance-truncated NCSM results with explicit 3N interactions
* Ground-state energy of C-12 from IM-NCSM deviates by slightly above 4% from importance-truncated NCSM results with explicit 3N interactions
* IM-NCSM and MR-IM-SRG with HF-Bogoliubov reference states differ by almost 6% for C-12 ground-state energy
* Maximum deviation between IM-NCSM and NCSM for oxygen isotopes is around 1.8% for the heaviest isotopes
* The first excited 0+ state in C-12 drops from about 14 MeV to about 8 MeV during the IM-SRG evolution
* All calculations use a chiral N3LO NN interaction with cutoff 500 MeV/c and local N2LO 3N interaction with cutoff 400 MeV/c, softened by free-space SRG evolution to 0.08 fm^4
* IM-SRG calculations use a single-particle basis including 13 major shells (emax=12)
* Initial three-body matrix elements are truncated at total HO energy quantum number E3max=14

## Context

This work extends the NCSM approach of Barrett (2013) and the multi-reference IM-SRG approach of Hergert (2016) by combining their complementary strengths: the decoupling of excitations at the many-body level in IM-SRG and the access to arbitrary nuclei, eigenstates, and observables in NCSM. It differs from valence-space shell-model approaches using effective interactions derived from IM-SRG (Stroberg, 2016) or coupled-cluster (Jansen, 2014) because IM-NCSM is a no-core approach where convergence with respect to all model-space truncations can be demonstrated explicitly. The method enables fully converged no-core calculations for an unprecedented range of nuclei and observables at moderate computational cost, making it suitable for ab initio studies of medium-mass nuclei up to the full range of the sd shell and beyond.
