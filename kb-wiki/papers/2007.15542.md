# Two-neutrino double-beta decay in pionless effective field theory from a Euclidean finite-volume correlation function

> Two-neutrino double-beta decay of certain nuclear isotopes is one of the rarest Standard Model processes observed in nature. Its neutrinoless counterpart is an exotic lepton-number nonconserving process that is widely searched for to determine if the neutrinos are Majorana fermions. In order to connect the rate of these processes to the Standard Model and beyond the Standard Model interactions, it is essential that the corresponding nuclear matrix elements are constrained reliably from theory. Lattice quantum chromodynamics (LQCD) and low-energy effective field theories (EFTs) are expected to play an essential role in constraining the matrix element of the two-nucleon subprocess, which could in turn provide the input into ab initio nuclear-structure calculations in larger isotopes. Focusing on the two-neutrino process $nn \to pp \, (ee \barν_e\barν_e)$, the amplitude is constructed in this work in pionless EFT at next-to-leading order, demonstrating the emergence of a renormalization-scale independent amplitude and the absence of any new low-energy constant at this order beyond those present in the single-weak process. Most importantly, it is shown how a LQCD four-point correlation function in Euclidean and finite-volume spacetime can be used to constrain the Minkowski infinite-volume amplitude in the EFT. The same formalism is provided for the related single-weak process, which is an input to the double-$β$ decay formalism. The LQCD-EFT matching procedure outlined for the double-weak amplitude paves the road toward constraining the two-nucleon matrix element entering the neutrinoless double-beta decay amplitude with a light Majorana neutrino.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the two-neutrino double-beta decay amplitude for the $nn \to pp \, (ee\bar{\nu}_e\bar{\nu}_e)$ process can be constructed in pionless effective field theory at next-to-leading order without introducing any new low-energy constant beyond those already present in the single-weak process, provided the amplitude is renormalization-scale independent; the authors further show how a lattice QCD four-point correlation function in Euclidean finite-volume spacetime can be used to constrain the Minkowski infinite-volume amplitude within this EFT framework.

## Method

The authors construct the hadronic amplitude for the two-neutrino double-beta decay process $nn \to pp \, (ee\bar{\nu}_e\bar{\nu}_e)$ using pionless effective field theory with nucleonic degrees of freedom, working up to next-to-leading order in the Kaplan-Savage-Wise power counting. They derive the infinite-volume amplitude by solving the Schrödinger equation with strong interaction potentials and weak current insertions, using the power-divergence subtraction scheme for renormalization. The finite-volume formalism is then developed to relate Euclidean lattice QCD four-point correlation functions to the physical Minkowski amplitude, accounting for the necessary subtraction of on-shell intermediate-state contributions that differ between Euclidean and Minkowski spacetimes.

## Key numbers

- The experimental half-lives of two-neutrino double-beta decay isotopes range from approximately $10^{19}$ to $10^{24}$ years, as observed in a dozen isotopes from $^{48}$Ca to $^{238}$U.
- The pionless EFT calculation is performed at a pion mass of approximately 800 MeV, matching the first LQCD computation of the relevant matrix element.
- The loop function $I_0(E^+)$ in the PDS scheme evaluates to $-\frac{M}{4\pi}(\mu + ip)$, where $p = \sqrt{ME}$ and $\mu$ is the renormalization scale.
- The renormalization-group equations for the strong couplings are $\mu\frac{dC_0}{d\mu} = \frac{M\mu}{4\pi}C_0^2$ and $\mu\frac{dC_2}{d\mu} = \frac{M\mu}{2\pi}C_0 C_2$, with analogous relations for the spin-triplet channel.

## Context

This work extends the pionless EFT formalism previously applied to neutrinoless double-beta decay by Cirigliano et al. (2017, 2018, 2019), which required a new short-distance coupling at leading order, to the two-neutrino mode where no such coupling is needed at next-to-leading order. The finite-volume matching procedure builds upon the LQCD-EFT framework developed by Shanahan et al. (2017) and Tiburzi et al. (2017) using a dibaryon formulation, but here employs a nucleonic formulation more suitable for connecting to ab initio nuclear-structure calculations in larger isotopes. The formalism enables future LQCD computations of the two-nucleon matrix element to constrain the EFT input needed for systematic calculations of double-beta decay rates in nuclear isotopes.
