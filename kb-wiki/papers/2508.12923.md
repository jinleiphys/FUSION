# Polarized electromagnetic radiation by chiral media with time-dependent chiral chemical potential

> We investigate photon production from media characterized by a time-dependent chiral chemical potential $μ_5(t)$. We first consider the chiral anomaly as a small perturbation and show that $μ_5\to 2γ$ process generates non-polarized radiation, as the probabilities of producing right and left-handed photons are equal. In chiral semimetal with a vanishing chiral chemical potential but a finite separation of Weyl nodes, ${\bf Δ}(t)$, the photon production vanishes at the leading order of perturbation theory. We then employ the method of Bogolyubov transformation to obtain the photon spectrum that sums up all powers of $μ_5$. Employing the exactly solvable model $μ_5(t) \propto A+B\tanh(t/τ)$, we demonstrate that the spectrum exhibits strong circular polarization, whose direction is determined by the sign of $μ_5$. The spectrum contains resonances associated with the instability of the electromagnetic field in chiral media. We discuss potential phenomenological applications of these findings.

# Polarized electromagnetic radiation by chiral media with time-dependent chiral chemical potential

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that photon production from a chiral medium with a time-dependent chiral chemical potential μ₅(t) exhibits strong circular polarization when the full non-perturbative calculation is performed, contrary to the leading-order perturbative result which predicts equal production of right- and left-handed photons. The polarization direction is determined by the sign of μ₅, and the effect becomes significant only when the product of the chiral chemical potential and the photon momentum or transition time is of order unity or larger.

## Method

The author employs the method of Bogolyubov transformation to canonically quantize the electromagnetic field at different equal-time hypersurfaces, summing all powers of the chiral chemical potential μ₅. The wave equation for the vector potential in the radiation gauge is solved exactly for the analytically solvable model b₀(t) = A + B tanh(t/τ), where b₀ = c_A μ₅. The Bogolyubov coefficients relating the in- and out-mode expansions are computed using hypergeometric functions, yielding the exact photon spectrum. The perturbative approach treating the chiral anomaly as a small perturbation is also presented for comparison.

## Context

This work extends previous perturbative calculations of photon production in chiral media, which were limited to leading order in μ₅ and predicted no net polarization (Fukushima 2012, Yee 2013, Jia 2022-2024, Wang 2024). The polarization of the photon spectrum was previously emphasized in Tuchin (2019), Hansen (2024), and Hansen (2025). The exact solution using the tanh model was previously employed to investigate chiral Cherenkov radiation in time-dependent μ₅ (Tuchin 2025). The resonances in the spectrum are associated with the instability of the electromagnetic field in chiral media, which has been studied in Joyce (1997), Boyarsky (2011), Hirono (2015), Akamatsu (2013), Tuchin (2014), Buividovich (2015), Manuel (2015), and Kirilin (2017). The results enable quantitative predictions for polarized photon emission from quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions and from chiral materials in the THz frequency range, with potential applications in characterizing chiral media.
