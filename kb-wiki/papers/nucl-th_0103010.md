# NN potentials from inverse scattering in the J-matrix approach

> An approximate inverse scattering method [7,8] has been used to construct separable potentials with the Laguerre form factors. As an application, we invert the phase shifts of proton-proton in the $^1S_0$ and $^3P_2-^3F_2$ channels and neutron-proton in the $^3S_1-^3D_1$ channel elastic scattering. In the latter case the deuteron wave function of a realistic $np$ potential was used as input.

## Key claim

The authors construct separable nucleon-nucleon potentials using an approximate inverse scattering method within the J-matrix approach, where the potentials are constrained to reproduce phase shifts up to 350 MeV and, in the neutron-proton case, also approximately reproduce the deuteron wave function of the Nijm II potential via a phase-equivalent transformation.

## Method

The method expands the potential operator in a finite basis of Laguerre (Coulomb-Sturmian) functions and reconstructs the potential parameters from the K-matrix (or S-matrix) by inverting the J-matrix formalism to obtain the P-matrix, whose poles and residues determine the Hamiltonian matrix eigenvalues and eigenvector components. For the coupled-channel cases (³P₂-³F₂ and ³S₁-³D₁), the authors first determine optimal basis parameters (scaling lengths a_α and basis sizes N_α) from uncoupled single-channel fits, then fix "internal" poles from the P-matrix and treat remaining "external" parameters via orthonormalization conditions. The deuteron bound state is incorporated by adjusting the phase-equivalent transformation matrix Q to match the Nijm II deuteron wave function components in the interior region.

## Key numbers

- Phase shift input data covers the energy range 0 – 350 MeV (Nijmegen partial wave analysis)
- Deuteron binding energy: ε = -κ² = -0.0536401 fm⁻²
- For the ¹S₀ channel: basis size N = 4 with one free external parameter λ₄
- For the ³P₂-³F₂ channel: N₁ = 2 (³P₂), N₂ = 6 (³F₂), with three external parameter triplets (j = 6, 7, 8)
- For the ³S₁-³D₁ channel: N₁ = 4 (³S₁), N₂ = 4 (³D₁), with three external parameter triplets (j = 6, 7, 8) and four variable parameters after orthonormalization

## Context

This work extends the J-matrix inverse scattering method of Zaitsev and Kramar (2000) and Zaitsev and Kramar (1999) to charged-particle scattering by using Laguerre (Coulomb-Sturmian) form factors, making it applicable to proton-proton channels. The approach is analogous to R-matrix methods used by Zakhariev (1991) for potential reconstruction, and the phase-equivalent transformation follows techniques from Papp (1991) and Zaitsev and Kramar (1999). The resulting separable potentials are intended for use in three-body calculations within the J-matrix framework of Bang and Vaagen (1980), simplifying many-body computations compared to using realistic potentials like Nijmegen (Nijmegen partial wave analysis, 1993) directly.
