# Emergence of rotational bands in ab initio no-core configuration interaction calculations of the Be isotopes

> The emergence of rotational bands is observed in no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations for the Be isotopes (7<=A<=12), as evidenced by rotational patterns for excitation energies, electromagnetic moments, and electromagnetic transitions. Yrast and low-lying excited bands are found. The results indicate well-developed rotational structure in NCCI calculations, using the JISP16 realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction within finite, computationally-accessible configuration spaces.

## Key claim

Rotational band structures emerge in ab initio no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations for Be isotopes (A=7–12) using the JISP16 realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction, but only within finite, computationally accessible configuration spaces (Nmax=10 or 11) and without the Coulomb interaction, so the results indicate well-developed rotational structure under these specific truncation and interaction conditions rather than in the fully converged infinite-space limit.

## Method

The authors performed no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations for ⁷–¹²Be isotopes using the JISP16 charge-independent two-body interaction (without renormalization to the truncated space) in a harmonic oscillator basis with truncation by Nmax (10 or 11 depending on parity). They diagonalized the Hamiltonian using the Lanczos algorithm in the proton-neutron M-scheme with the code MFDn, omitting the Coulomb interaction to ensure exact isospin conservation, and analyzed excitation energies, electric quadrupole moments, E2 transition matrix elements, magnetic dipole moments, and M1 transition matrix elements for rotational signatures.

## Key numbers

- Basis oscillator energies: ℏω = 20 MeV for ⁷,⁸Be and ℏω = 22.5 MeV for ⁹–¹²Be
- Basis truncation: Nmax = 10 or 11, depending on parity
- Ground state separation from remaining states: ~20 MeV for ⁸Be
- Energy range of low-lying unnatural parity states: within a few MeV of the lowest natural parity states in ⁹,¹¹Be

## Context

This work extends ab initio NCCI calculations (previously applied to light nuclei by Navratil (2000), Barrett (2013), and Maris (2009)) to demonstrate rotational collectivity directly from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, complementing phenomenological descriptions such as the Elliott SU(3) rotation model (Elliott, 1958) and the Bohr-Mottelson collective model (Bohr, 1998). The results enable future studies of how rotational structure converges with increasing Nmax and how three-body forces or Coulomb effects might modify the observed rotational patterns.
