# Hyperonic Neutron Star Matter in Light of GW170817

> Since 2013 the mass of pulsar PSR J0348+0432 ($M = 2.01\,M_{\odot}$) has provided a tight constraint on neutron star equation of state. However, a number of different analyses of the recently detected BNS merger (GW170817) point to a maximum neutron star mass around $2.16\,M_{\odot}$. In addition, a recent study determined the mass of the millisecond pulsar PSR J2215+5135 to be $2.27^{+0.17}_{-0.15}\,M_{\odot}$. In this work we investigate the presence of hyperons in neutron star matter in light of these new mass measurements using equations of state calculated in the relativistic mean-field approximation. Particular attention is paid to the use of the available empirical data from the study of hypernuclei and that of the SU(3) symmetry relations in fixing the meson-hyperon coupling constants. We find that hyperonic equations of state with reasonable choices for the meson-hyperon coupling constants can satisfy these new mass constraints, with hyperons potentially accounting for more than 10% of the baryons in the core of a neutron star.

## Key claim

Hyperonic equations of state with reasonable choices for the meson-hyperon coupling constants can satisfy the new mass constraints from PSR J0348+0432 and GW170817, with hyperons potentially accounting for more than 10% of the baryons in the core of a neutron star, but only if the SU(3) coupling parameters are chosen outside the conventional SU(6) symmetry values.

## Method

The authors compute equations of state for cold, nonrotating neutron stars using the relativistic mean-field approximation, including the full baryon octet (nucleons plus hyperons). Scalar meson-hyperon coupling constants are fit to empirical hyperon single-particle potentials from hypernuclei data, while vector meson-hyperon couplings are fixed by SU(3) symmetry relations. The authors vary the SU(3) coupling parameters alpha_V and z to traverse the entire vector meson-hyperon coupling space and calculate neutron star properties including maximum mass, strangeness fraction, and hyperon fraction.

## Context

This work addresses the "hyperon puzzle" where including hyperons softens the neutron star equation of state, often producing maximum masses inconsistent with observations of massive pulsars like PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis 2013) and PSR J2215+5135 (Linares 2018), as well as constraints from the binary neutron star merger GW170817 (Abbott 2017). The authors use a relativistic mean-field parameterization consistent with constraints on the symmetry energy and its slope from multiple experimental and theoretical studies (Hagen 2015, Hebeler 2013, Lattimer 2014, Danielewicz 2014, Roca-Maza 2015, Birkhan 2017, Tews 2017). The work extends prior studies by systematically exploring the full SU(3) vector meson-hyperon coupling parameter space, showing that hyperonic equations of state can satisfy the new mass constraints if coupling constants are chosen consistent with the Nijmegen extended-soft-core model (Rijken 2010) rather than SU(6) symmetry. This enables future work to focus on constraining the Xi hyperon self-potential through additional hypernuclei experiments, which would further refine the allowed parameter space.
