# Three-Body Scattering without Partial Waves

> The Faddeev equation for three-body scattering at arbitrary energies is formulated in momentum space and directly solved in terms of momentum vectors without employing a partial wave decomposition. In its simplest form the Faddeev equation for identical bosons is a three-dimensional integral equation in five variables, magnitudes of relative momenta and angles. The elastic differential cross section, semi-exclusive d(N,N') cross sections and total cross sections of both elastic and breakup processes in the intermediate energy range up to about 1 GeV are calculated based on a Malfliet-Tjon type potential, and the convergence of the multiple scattering series is investigated in every case. In general a truncation in the first or second order in the two-body t-matrix is quite insufficient.

## Key claim

The Faddeev equation for three-body scattering can be solved directly in momentum space using vector variables (magnitudes of momenta and angles) without a partial wave decomposition, and this approach works at intermediate energies up to about 1 GeV. The authors note that a truncation of the multiple scattering series at first or second order in the two-body t-matrix is "quite insufficient" for accurate results.

## Method

The authors formulated the Faddeev equation for identical bosons (neglecting spin and isospin) as a three-dimensional integral equation in five variables: magnitudes of relative momenta and angles. They solved this equation using a Malfliet-Tjon type potential (short-range repulsive plus intermediate-range attractive Yukawa forces) with parameters adjusted to produce a two-body bound state at -2.23 MeV. They calculated elastic differential cross sections, semi-exclusive d(N,N') cross sections, and total cross sections for both elastic and breakup processes, and investigated the convergence of the multiple scattering series by comparing the full Faddeev solution with successive truncations at first, second, third, and fourth order in the two-body t-matrix.

## Key numbers

- Two-body bound state energy: E_d = -2.23 MeV (used to set Malfliet-Tjon potential parameters)
- Elastic differential cross sections calculated at projectile energies of 0.2 GeV and 1.0 GeV
- Semi-exclusive d(N,N') cross sections calculated at 0.5 GeV laboratory incident energy for emission angles of 15 degrees and 33 degrees
- Semi-exclusive d(N,N') cross sections calculated at 1.0 GeV laboratory incident energy for emission angles of 15 degrees and 33 degrees
- Energy range of the study: intermediate energies up to about 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the traditional approach to three-nucleon scattering, which relies on partial wave decomposition of the Faddeev equations. At low projectile energies, partial wave truncation is physically justified by centrifugal barrier arguments, but at energies of a few hundred MeV the number of partial waves needed for convergence proliferates, making the method computationally prohibitive and less physically transparent. The authors present what they state are "the first calculations of this kind" using vector variables directly, and their approach includes all partial waves exactly. This method enables future calculations that can scan the complete three-body phase space for total breakup, which may be important for understanding previous theoretical analyses of p(d,ppn) reactions that relied on low-order reaction mechanisms.
