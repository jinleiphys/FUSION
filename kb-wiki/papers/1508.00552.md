# Bridging Soft-Hard Transport Properties of Quark-Gluon Plasmas with CUJET3.0

> A new model of jet quenching in nuclear collisions, CUJET3.0, is constructed by generalizing the perturbative QCD based CUJET2.0 model to include two complementary non-perturbative features of the QCD confinement cross-over phase transition near $T_c\approx 160$ MeV: (1) the suppression of quark and gluon degrees of freedom and (2) the emergence of chromo-magnetic monopole degrees of freedom. Such a semi-Quark-Gluon-Monopole Plasma (sQGMP) microscopic scenario is tested by comparing predictions of the leading hadron nuclear modification factors, $R^h_{AA}(p_T>10{\rm GeV/c},\sqrt{s})$, and their azimuthal elliptic asymmetry $v^h_2(p_T>10{\rm GeV/c},\sqrt{s})$ with available data on $h=π,D,B$ jet fragments from nuclear collisions at RHIC($\sqrt{s}=0.2$ ATeV) and LHC($\sqrt{s}$=2.76 ATeV). The sQGMP model is shown to solve the long standing $R_{AA}$ vs $v_2$ puzzle by predicting a maximum of the jet quenching parameter field $\hat{q}(E,T)/T^3$ near $T_c$. The consistency of jet quenching with observed bulk perfect fluidity is demonstrated by extrapolating the sQGMP $\hat{q}$ down to thermal energy $E\sim 3 T$ scales and showing that the sQGMP shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $η/s \approx T^3/\hat{q}$ falls close to the unitarity bound, $1/4π$, in the range $(1-2)T_c$. Detailed comparisons of CUJET2.0 and CUJET3.0 reveal that the remarkably different $\hat{q}(T)$ could be consistent with the same $R_{AA}$ data and could only be distinguished by anisotropy observables. These findings demonstrate clearly the inadequacy of focusing on the jet path averaged quantity $<\hat{q}>$ as the only relevant medium property to characterize jet quenching, and point to the crucial roles of other essential factors, such as the chromo electric and magnetic composites of the plasma, the screening masses and the running couplings at multiple scales that all strongly influence jet energy loss.

## Key claim

The CUJET3.0 model demonstrates that a semi-Quark-Gluon-Monopole Plasma (sQGMP) incorporating both suppressed chromo-electric and emergent chromo-magnetic degrees of freedom near the QCD crossover temperature can simultaneously describe high-\(p_T\) jet quenching observables (\(R_{AA}\) and \(v_2\)) and bulk perfect fluidity, provided the jet quenching parameter \(\hat{q}(E,T)/T^3\) peaks near \(T_c \approx 160\) MeV. The hedge is that this consistency relies on the specific non-perturbative composition and screening properties of the sQGMP, and that remarkably different \(\hat{q}(T)\) dependences could fit the same \(R_{AA}\) data, being distinguishable only via anisotropy observables like \(v_2\).

## Method

The authors generalized the perturbative QCD based CUJET2.0 framework to CUJET3.0 by incorporating two non-perturbative features of the QCD confinement crossover: (1) suppression of quark and gluon chromo-electric-charged degrees of freedom via the semi-QGP model controlled by the Polyakov loop, and (2) emergence of chromo-magnetic monopole degrees of freedom. They computed leading hadron nuclear modification factors \(R^h_{AA}(p_T>10\ \text{GeV}/c)\) and elliptic flow \(v^h_2(p_T>10\ \text{GeV}/c)\) for \(h = \pi, D, B\) jet fragments by integrating the DGLV opacity series energy loss kernel over (2+1)D viscous hydrodynamic backgrounds (VISH2+1D) with lattice-QCD-constrained thermodynamic properties, screening masses, and running couplings.

## Key numbers

* The QCD crossover temperature is \(T_c \approx 160\) MeV.
* The sQGMP shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/s \approx T^3/\hat{q}\) falls close to the unitarity bound \(1/4\pi\) in the temperature range \((1-2)T_c\).
* The model was tested against data from RHIC at \(\sqrt{s}=0.2\) ATeV and LHC at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\) ATeV for hadrons with \(p_T > 10\ \text{GeV}/c\).
* The pp baseline for light hadrons at LHC used a K-factor of 1.76 (fitting ATLAS datum at \(p_T = 6.26\) GeV), and for heavy flavor at LHC a K-factor of 0.43 (fitting ALICE datum at \(p_T = 7\) GeV).
* The Debye screening mass \(\mu(\vz)\) is determined from \(\mu^2(\vz) = \sqrt{4\pi\alpha_s(\mu^2(\vz))}T(\vz)\sqrt{1+N_f/6}\).
* The running coupling \(\alpha_s(Q^2)\) is cutoff in the infrared with \(\alpha_{max}\) for \(Q \le Q_{min}\), where \(Q_{min}=\Lambda_{QCD}\exp\left\lbrace {2\pi}/{9\alpha_{max}}\right\rbrace\) and \(\Lambda_{QCD}=200\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative CUJET2.0 model (Xu, Liao, Gyulassy 2014) by adding non-perturbative features motivated by the dual superconductor picture of confinement (Mandelstam 1974, Nambu 1974, Polyakov 1976, 't Hooft 1981) and the semi-QGP model (Pisarski 2006, Hidaka 2008-2009). It addresses the long-standing \(R_{AA}\) vs \(v_2\) puzzle where pQCD-based models (e.g., Molnar 2001, Noronha 2010, Betz 2013-2014) failed to simultaneously describe both observables. The sQGMP model enables constraining the \(\hat{q}(E,T)\) and \(\eta/s(T)\) fields over a wider range than previously possible, bridging soft bulk perfect fluidity with hard jet quenching phenomenology.
