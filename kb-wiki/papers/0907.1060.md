# A relativistic quark model for the Omega- electromagnetic form factors

> We compute the Omega- electromagnetic form factors and the decuplet baryon magnetic moments using a quark model application of the Covariant Spectator Theory. Our predictions for the Omega- electromagnetic form factors can be tested in the future by lattice QCD simulations at the physical strange quark mass.

## Key claim
The authors compute the Omega- electromagnetic form factors and decuplet baryon magnetic moments using a covariant spectator quark model, but the Omega- magnetic moment is not a prediction, it is used as input to fix the strange quark anomalous magnetic moment parameter kappa_s. The predictions for the Q^2 dependence of the Omega- form factors can be tested by future lattice QCD simulations at the physical strange quark mass.

## Method
The authors extend a covariant spectator quark model, previously applied to nucleon and Delta baryons, to the full decuplet of baryons containing strange quarks. The model describes baryons as a quark-diquark system in a pure S-wave state, with the electromagnetic current computed in a relativistic impulse approximation where the photon couples to each of the three quarks in turn. The quark form factors are parametrized using vector meson dominance (VMD) with rho, omega, and phi mesons, and the strange quark anomalous magnetic moment kappa_s is fixed by fitting the experimental Omega- magnetic moment.

## Key numbers
- Omega- magnetic moment: mu_Omega- = (-2.02 ± 0.05) mu_N (experimental world average)
- Omega- magnetic moment from SU'(3) estimate: mu_Omega- = -2.41 mu_N (deviates by about 20% from experiment)
- Fitted strange quark anomalous magnetic moment: kappa_s = 1.462
- Quark anomalous magnetic moments from nucleon fit: kappa_u = 1.778, kappa_d = 1.915
- Predicted magnetic moments for strange decuplet baryons (CST model): Sigma*− = -2.44 mu_N, Sigma*0 = 0.06 mu_N, Sigma*+ = 2.56 mu_N, Xi*− = -2.23 mu_N, Xi*0 = 0.21 mu_N
- Lattice QCD data used for parameter constraints: Q^2 = 0.230 GeV^2 for Sigma*+, Sigma*−, and Xi*− form factors (from Boinepalli09)
- Vector meson mass parametrization: m_rho = 0.766 GeV + 0.427 GeV^(−1) * m_pi^2
- Physical phi meson mass: m_phi = 1019 MeV

## Context
This work extends the covariant spectator quark model from the SU(2) light quark sector (previously applied to nucleon and Delta form factors by the same authors) to the full decuplet including strange quarks. It provides predictions for the Omega- electromagnetic form factors that can be directly compared with lattice QCD data at the physical strange quark mass, unlike the Delta case where lattice results require extrapolation from unphysical pion masses. The model uses quenched lattice QCD data from Boinepalli (2009) for Sigma* and Xi* form factors to constrain the remaining free parameters, and the results enable future tests of the model when lattice QCD simulations at the physical strange quark mass become available.
