# Femtoscopy of the system shape fluctuations in heavy ion collisions

> Dipole, triangular, and higher harmonic flow that have an origin in the initial density fluctuations has gained a lot of attention as they can provide additional important information about the dynamical properties (e.g. viscosity) of the system. The fluctuations in the initial geometry should be also reflected in the detail shape and velocity field of the system at freeze-out. In this talk I discuss the possibility to measure such fluctuations by means of identical and non-identical particle interferometry.

# Femtoscopy of the system shape fluctuations in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

Femtoscopic measurements of identical and non-identical particle correlations can directly observe triangular, quadrangular, and higher harmonic shapes of the freeze-out system, provided that the azimuthal dependence of HBT radii is analyzed relative to the corresponding symmetry planes for each harmonic.

## Method

The author employs a blast wave model with longitudinally boost-invariant source and freeze-out at constant temperature T=100 MeV, parametrizing the expansion velocity profile with spatial eccentricity and boost modulation parameters for harmonics n=2, 3, and 4. Additionally, the AMPT model is used to simulate Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, extracting HBT radii as functions of azimuth relative to each harmonic event plane.

## Key numbers

- Blast wave model freeze-out temperature: T=100 MeV
- Average expansion velocity: mean(v_r) approximately 0.7c, with mean(p_t) for pions = 0.40 GeV
- Spatial eccentricity parameter a_space approximately 0.05, based on assumption that spatial eccentricity is reduced about two times during system evolution
- Boost modulation parameter a_boost in the range 0.03-0.07, estimated from v_n(p_t) dependence
- Maximum transverse expansion parameter rho_t,max = 0.9
- AMPT model simulations performed for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV

## Context

This work extends the understanding of initial density fluctuations in heavy ion collisions, which were recognized as important for flow development by Aguiar (2001) and later linked to ridge correlations by Voloshin (2003) and Takahashi (2009). The approach follows earlier azimuthally sensitive femtoscopic analyses proposed by Voloshin (1995, 1997), and enables direct experimental measurement of triangular and higher harmonic system shapes at freeze-out, complementing flow fluctuation studies by Mishra (2008), Sorensen (2010), Teaney (2010), Staig (2011), and Shuryak (2009).
