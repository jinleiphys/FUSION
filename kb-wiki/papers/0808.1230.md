# Range Corrections to Three-Body Observables near a Feshbach Resonance

> A non-relativistic system of three identical particles will display a rich set of universal features known as Efimov physics if the scattering length a is much larger than the range l of the underlying two-body interaction. An appropriate effective theory facilitates the derivation of both results in the |a| goes to infinity limit and finite-l/a corrections to observables of interest. Here we use such an effective-theory treatment to consider the impact of corrections linear in the two-body effective range, r_s on the three-boson bound-state spectrum and recombination rate for |a| much greater than |r_s|. We do this by first deriving results appropriate to the strict limit |a| goes to infinity in coordinate space. We then extend these results to finite a using once-subtracted momentum-space integral equations. We also discuss the implications of our results for experiments that probe three-body recombination in Bose-Einstein condensates near a Feshbach resonance.

## Key claim

The authors derive the linear-in-effective-range correction to the Efimov three-body bound-state spectrum and recombination rate, showing that in the unitary limit (|a| → ∞) the first-order correction proportional to r_s κ_* vanishes due to discrete scale invariance, but away from unitarity the correction is nonzero and can be computed perturbatively.

## Method

The authors use a hyperradial coordinate formalism to derive the three-body Schrödinger equation in the adiabatic approximation, obtaining a hyperradial potential that includes a 1/R^3 term proportional to the effective range r_s. They then compute first-order perturbative corrections to the Efimov spectrum by evaluating matrix elements of this potential between leading-order hyperradial wave functions, renormalizing the short-distance physics with a three-body counterterm. For finite scattering length, they extend the calculation using once-subtracted momentum-space integral equations to obtain numerical results for the bound-state spectrum and recombination rate.

## Key numbers

- s_0 = 1.00624, the numerical constant characterizing the discrete scale invariance of the Efimov spectrum
- The lowest eigenvalue λ_0(R) = -s_0^2 (1 + 1.897 R/a + 0.480 r_s/R + ...) in the region |r_s| ≪ R ≪ |a|
- The second-lowest eigenvalue λ_1(R) = 19.94 (1 - 0.1872 R/a + 0.9333 r_s/R + ...)
- The Efimov spectrum follows B_n = (e^{-2π/s_0})^{n-n_*} B_{n*}, with e^{-2π/s_0} ≈ 1/515

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory treatment of Efimov physics by Braaten and Hammer (2004) and the perturbative range-correction calculations of Hammer and Mehen (2001), which considered r_s/|a| effects that vanish in the unitary limit. The authors show that a linear-in-r_s correction survives away from unitarity and provide the first calculation of the function G_n that quantifies this correction. The results enable more precise comparisons with three-body recombination experiments in Bose-Einstein condensates near Feshbach resonances, such as those performed by the Innsbruck group (2006) with ¹³³Cs atoms.
