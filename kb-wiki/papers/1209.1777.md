# Density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy constrained by mean-field calculations

> We establish a correlation for the symmetry energy at saturation density $S_{0}$, slope parameter $L$ and curvature parameter $K_{\text{sym}}$ based on widely different mean field interactions. With the help of this correlation and available empirical and theoretical information, the density dependent behavior around the saturation density is determined. We compare the results obtained with the present approach with those by other analyses. With this obtained density dependent behavior of the symmetry energy, the neutron skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb and some properties of neutron stars are investigated. In addition, it is found that the expression $S(ρ)=S_{0}(ρ/ρ_{0})^γ$ or $S(ρ)=12.5(ρ/ρ_{0}) ^{2/3}+C_{p}(ρ/ρ_{0}) ^γ$ does not reproduce the density dependence of the symmetry energy as predicted by the mean-field approach around nuclear saturation density.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a universal correlation among the symmetry energy at saturation density \(S_{0}\), its slope \(L\), and its curvature \(K_{\text{sym}}\) using widely different mean-field interactions, and then uses this correlation together with empirical \(S_{0}\) values to constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy around saturation. The authors find that simple power-law forms \(S(\rho)=S_{0}(\rho/\rho_{0})^{\gamma}\) or \(S(\rho)=12.5(\rho/\rho_{0})^{2/3}+C_{p}(\rho/\rho_{0})^{\gamma}\) fail to reproduce the mean-field predictions, especially for soft symmetry energies, whereas a modified DDM3Y shape works well.

## Method

The authors collected 37 relativistic and non-relativistic mean-field interactions (e.g., SLy4, NL3, DDME2, SkM*) and computed \(S_{0}\), \(L\), and \(K_{\text{sym}}\) for each. They tested three analytic forms for the density dependence of the symmetry energy, a simple power law, a power law plus a kinetic term, and a DDM3Y-inspired shape, by comparing the predicted relations among \(S_{0}\), \(L\), and \(K_{\text{sym}}\) to the mean-field results. A least-squares fit to the mean-field data yielded an improved linear relation \(L = -11.76 + 3S_{0} + K_{\text{sym}}/4.55\) with a root-mean-square deviation of 6.35 MeV for \(L\). Using this relation together with the empirical constraint \(S_{0}=31.6\pm2.2\) MeV from the literature and a linear fit between \(K_{\text{sym}}\) and \(L\) (correlation coefficient \(r=0.972\)), they extracted \(L\) and \(K_{\text{sym}}\) and then computed the neutron skin thickness of \(^{208}\)Pb and neutron-star properties.

## Key numbers

*   \(L = 56 \pm 24\) MeV and \(K_{\text{sym}} = -125 \pm 79\) MeV, obtained with \(S_{0}=31.6\pm2.2\) MeV from the literature.
*   Neutron skin thickness of \(^{208}\)Pb: \(\Delta R_{np} = 0.185 \pm 0.035\) fm, derived from the \(K_{\text{sym}}\) value above.
*   Symmetry energy at density \(\rho = 0.1\) fm\(^{-3}\): \(S(\rho) = 23.6 \pm 0.02\) MeV.
*   Linear relation between \(K_{\text{sym}}\) and \(L\): \(K_{\text{sym}} = -307.862 + 3.292 L\) with a root-mean-square deviation of 29.8 MeV for \(K_{\text{sym}}\) and correlation coefficient \(r=0.972\).
*   Linear relation between \(\Delta R_{np}\) (in fm) and \(K_{\text{sym}}\) (in MeV) for \(^{208}\)Pb: \(\Delta R_{np} = 0.236210 + 0.000415 K_{\text{sym}}\).
*   Critical density for the transition from nonuniform to uniform neutron-rich matter: \(\rho_{c} \approx 0.09\) fm\(^{-3}\), derived from the \(\Delta R_{np}\) value.

## Context

This work extends the established linear correlation between the neutron skin thickness of heavy nuclei and the slope parameter \(L\) (e.g., Centelles et al. (2009), Warda et al. (2009)) to include the curvature parameter \(K_{\text{sym}}\). It contradicts the simple power-law parameterizations of the symmetry energy used in transport-model analyses by Chen et al. (2005) and Tsang et al. (2009), showing that those forms cannot reproduce the mean-field predictions for soft symmetry energies. The results enable simultaneous extraction of \(L\) and \(K_{\text{sym}}\) from future precise measurements of the neutron skin thickness of \(^{208}\)Pb, and they provide constraints on neutron-star crust properties and the direct URCA cooling process.
