# Novel Pion Electroproduction Low-Energy Theorems

> We present novel low--energy theorems for the P--wave multipoles $2M_{1+} + M_{1-}$, $M_{1+} -M_{1-}$, $E_{1+}$ and $L_{1\pm}$ for neutral pion electroproduction off protons. These should be very useful for the analysis of existing or future threshold data.

## Key claim

The paper derives novel low-energy theorems (LETs) for certain P-wave multipoles in neutral pion electroproduction off protons, valid to one-loop order in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT). The theorems are presented as model-independent constraints for multipole analyses, but the authors explicitly note that the S-wave multipoles \(E_{0+}\) and \(L_{0+}\) require higher-order calculations because their chiral expansions converge slowly.

## Method

The authors use heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT) to compute the threshold expansion of the P-wave multipoles \(2M_{1+}+M_{1-}\), \(M_{1+}-M_{1-}\), \(E_{1+}\), and \(L_{1\pm}\) for the reaction \(\gamma^\star p \to \pi^0 p\). They work to one-loop order \({\cal O}(p^3)\) in the chiral expansion, where \(p\) denotes a small momentum, and express the results in terms of the dimensionless parameters \(\mu = M_\pi/m\) and \(\rho = -k^2/M_\pi^2\). The calculation uses the effective Lagrangian \({\cal L}_{\pi N} = {\cal L}_{\pi N}^{(1)} + {\cal L}_{\pi N}^{(2)} + {\cal L}_{\pi N}^{(3)}\) and incorporates the low-energy constants \(\kappa_p = 1.793\) (proton anomalous magnetic moment) and \(b_P = 15.8\) GeV\(^{-3}\) (determined by resonance exchange, 80% from \(\Delta(1232)\) and 20% from vector mesons).

## Key numbers

- The slope of the magnetic multipole combination \((2M_{1+}+M_{1-})\) at threshold: \({1\over |\vec q|}(2M_{1+}+M_{1-})^{\rm thr} = e M_\pi\sqrt{1+\rho}\biggl(b_P + {g_{\pi N}\over 16\pi m^3}\biggr) + {\cal O}(\mu^2)\), with \(M_\pi = 134.97\) MeV, \(m = 928.27\) MeV, \(g_{\pi N}=13.4\), and \(e^2/4\pi = 1/137.036\).
- The slope of the magnetic multipole combination \((M_{1+}-M_{1-})\) at threshold: \({1\over |\vec q|}(M_{1+}-M_{1-})^{\rm thr} = {e g_{\pi N}\over 8\pi m^2}\sqrt{1+\rho}\biggl\{1+\kappa_p + {\mu\over4}\biggl[-5-2\kappa_p + {g_{\pi N}^2\over 8\pi}\biggl({4+3\rho\over 1+\rho} - {(2+\rho)^2\over 2(1+\rho)^{3/2}}\arccos{-\rho\over 2+\rho}\biggr)\biggr]\biggr\} + {\cal O}(\mu^2)\).
- The slope of the electric multipole \(E_{1+}\) at threshold: \({1\over |\vec q|}E_{1+}^{\rm thr} = {e g_{\pi N}\mu\over 96\pi m^2}\sqrt{1+\rho}\biggl[1+{g_{\pi N}^2\over 8\pi}\biggl({8+5\rho\over 3(1+\rho)^2} - {(2+\rho)^2\over 2(1+\rho)^{5/2}}\arccos{-\rho\over 2+\rho}\biggr)\biggr] + {\cal O}(\mu^2)\).
- The slope of the longitudinal multipole \(L_{1+}\) at threshold: \({1\over |\vec q|}L_{1+}^{\rm thr} = {e g_{\pi N}\mu\over 96\pi m^2}\sqrt{1+\rho}\biggl[1+{g_{\pi N}^2\over 16\pi}\biggl(-{4+\rho\over (1+\rho)^2} + {4-\rho^2\over 2(1+\rho)^{5/2}}\arccos{-\rho\over 2+\rho}\biggr)\biggr] + {\cal O}(\mu^2)\).
- The slope of the longitudinal multipole \(L_{1-}\) at threshold: \({1\over |\vec q|}L_{1-}^{\rm thr} = {e g_{\pi N}\mu\over 12\pi m^2}\sqrt{1+\rho}\biggl[1+{g_{\pi N}^2\over 32\pi}\biggl({2\over 1+\rho} - {2+\rho\over (1+\rho)^{3/2}}\arccos{-\rho\over 2+\rho}\biggr)\biggr] + {\cal O}(\mu^2)\).
- The cusp in \(L_{0+}\) is suppressed relative to that in \(E_{0+}\) by a factor of \((2+\rho)^{-1}\); at \(\rho = 3\) (\(k^2 = -0.055\) GeV\(^2\)) it is diminished to 20% of the magnitude in \(E_{0+}\).
- The slope of \(E_{0+}^{\rm thr}\) with respect to \(k^2\) at \(k^2=0\): \({\partial E_{0+}^{\rm thr}\over \partial k^2}\big|_{k^2=0} = -{e g_{\pi N}\over 16\pi m^3}\biggl(1+\kappa_p + {4-\pi\over 16\pi}{m^2\over F_\pi^2}\biggr) + {\cal O}(\mu)\), where the chiral loop term (last term in brackets) amounts to a 62% correction to the magnetic moment piece.

## Context

This work extends the low-energy theorem approach previously applied to neutral pion photoproduction (Bernard et al., 1994; Bernstein et al., 1994) to the electroproduction case, providing model-independent constraints for multipole analyses of threshold data from MAMI at Mainz and NIKHEF at Amsterdam. The results are derived within heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (Jenkins and Manohar, 1991; Bernard et al., 1992) and make explicit the P-wave LETs that were implicitly contained in earlier relativistic baryon CHPT calculations (Bernard et al., 1992). The theorems are intended to enable reliable extraction of the S-wave multipoles \(E_{0+}\) and \(L_{0+}\) from upcoming precise data, where higher-order calculations are needed for the S-waves due to slow convergence of their chiral expansions.
