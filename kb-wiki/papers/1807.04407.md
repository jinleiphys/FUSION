# Perturbative $NN$ scattering in chiral effective field theory

> Within the framework of chiral effective field theory, perturbative calculation for $NN$ scattering is carried out in partial waves with orbital angular momentum $L \geqslant 1$. The primary goal is to identify the lowest angular momenta at which perturbative treatment of chiral forces can apply. Results up to the order where the subleading two-pion exchange appears are shown. It is concluded that perturbation theory applies to all partial waves but ${}^1S_0$, ${}^3S_1 - {}^3D_1$, and ${}^3P_0$. Where it is applicable, perturbation theory with the delta-less chiral forces produces good agreement with the empirical phase shifts up to $k_\text{c.m.} \simeq 300$ MeV.

## Key claim

Perturbative treatment of chiral nuclear forces (including one-pion exchange, two-pion exchanges, and contact interactions) applies to all nucleon-nucleon partial waves except the \(^1S_0\), \(^3S_1-^3D_1\), and \(^3P_0\) channels, with good agreement to empirical phase shifts up to center-of-mass momentum \(k_{\text{c.m.}} \simeq 300\) MeV when using delta-less chiral forces.

## Method

The authors performed perturbative calculations of nucleon-nucleon scattering in partial waves with orbital angular momentum \(L \geq 1\) within chiral effective field theory, treating one-pion exchange (OPE) as a next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbation rather than iterating it to all orders. They included the leading and subleading two-pion exchanges (TPEs) and systematically incorporated contact interactions up to N\(^4\)LO, using a Gaussian momentum cutoff regulator with cutoff values varied from 0.8 to 4.8 GeV. Phase shifts were computed order-by-order and compared to empirical values from the SAID program, with fits favoring points near \(k_{\text{c.m.}} = m_\pi\) (between 130 and 200 MeV).

## Key numbers

- OPE strength scale: \(\MNN \equiv 16\pi f_\pi^2/(g_A^2 m_N) \simeq 290\) MeV, characterizing the momentum scale below which OPE might be treated perturbatively.
- Critical momenta for nonperturbative OPE in triplet channels from Birse (2005): \(^3S_1-^3D_1\) at 66 MeV, \(^3P_0\) at 182 MeV, \(^3P_1\) at 365 MeV, \(^3P_2-^3F_2\) at 470 MeV, \(^3D_2\) at 403 MeV, \(^3D_3-^3G_3\) at 382 MeV.
- P-wave scattering volumes at NLO (Born approximation of OPE): \(a_{^1P_1} = 2 \MNN^{-1} m_\pi^{-2}\), \(a_{^3P_0} = -2 \MNN^{-1} m_\pi^{-2}\), \(a_{^3P_1} = -\frac{4}{3} \MNN^{-1} m_\pi^{-2}\), \(a_{^3P_2} = 0\).
- N\(^4\)LO EFT phase shifts differ from empirical GWU values by about 1 degree in all channels at \(k_{\text{c.m.}} \simeq 300\) MeV.
- The delta-less chiral forces break down beyond \(k_{\text{c.m.}} \simeq 300\) MeV, consistent with the nucleon-delta mass splitting \(\delta \simeq 300\) MeV.
- Cutoff variation range: \(\Lambda = 0.8\) to 4.8 GeV for most channels; \(\Lambda = 0.8\) to 2.4 GeV for \(^3P_0\) at N\(^4\)LO.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies on peripheral nucleon-nucleon scattering by Kaiser (1997), Entem (2002), Birse (2003), Epelbaum (2003), and others, by including multiple iterations of OPE (up to three) and systematic contact interactions in \(P\) and \(D\) waves. It builds on the analysis of Birse (2005) which examined the correlation between angular momentum and perturbativeness of the tensor part of OPE, and on the work of Pavon Valderrama (2016) which studied centrifugal suppression factors for OPE in spin-singlet channels. The results enable future application of this perturbative scheme to chiral forces with explicit delta-isobar degrees of freedom, and suggest that for many-body calculations, many partial waves can be treated perturbatively rather than requiring nonperturbative resummation.
