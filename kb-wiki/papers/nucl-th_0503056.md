# Thermodynamical consistent CJT calculation in studying nuclear matter

> We have attempted to apply the CJT formalism to study the nuclear matter. The thermodynamic potential is calculated in Hartree-Fock approximation in the CJT formalism. After neglecting the medium effects to the mesons, the numerical results are found very consistent with those obtained from the mean field calculation. In our calculation the thermodynamical consistency is also preserved.

# Thermodynamical consistent CJT calculation in studying nuclear matter

## Key claim

The authors apply the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) formalism to nuclear matter in the Walecka model and show that, after neglecting medium effects on the meson propagators, the Hartree-Fock approximation in the CJT framework reproduces results very close to those of standard mean field theory (MFT), while automatically preserving thermodynamical consistency.

## Method

The thermodynamic potential is computed at two-loop level in the CJT formalism (Hartree-Fock approximation) using the imaginary-time finite-temperature formalism, with all loop lines treated as full propagators. The meson propagators are replaced by free propagators (neglecting medium effects), and the nucleon gap equation is solved by taking the effective nucleon mass at the pole in the limit of zero three-momentum. The coupling constants are refitted to reproduce saturation properties of nuclear matter at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: 0.16 nucleons per fm³ with binding energy 16 MeV per nucleon.
- Fitted coupling constants: g_σ² = 155.6 and g_ω² = 521.5 (compared to MFT values of g_σ² = 91.6 and g_ω² = 136.2).
- Compressibility at saturation in CJT formalism: K = 485 MeV (compared to MFT value of K = 540 MeV).
- Critical temperature for liquid-gas phase transition in CJT calculation: approximately 19 MeV (almost the same as in MFT).
- Meson masses used: m_N = 939 MeV, m_σ = 550 MeV, m_ω = 783 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the CJT composite-operator formalism, originally developed by Cornwall, Jackiw, and Tomboulis (1974) for zero temperature and extended to finite temperature by Amelino-Camelia and Pi, to the study of nuclear matter in the Walecka model (QHD-I). The results are compared directly to standard mean field theory calculations, which are known to be thermodynamically consistent. The authors note that prior beyond-mean-field calculations (e.g., by the authors cited as reference s in the text) required additional compensatory terms to maintain thermodynamical consistency, whereas the CJT formalism automatically enforces it via stationary conditions. This work enables future calculations that include medium effects on mesons to go beyond mean field in a thermodynamically consistent manner.
