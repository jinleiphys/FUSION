# Magnetic moment operator of non-Dirac particles and some elements of polarization $ep$-experiments

> An explicit form of the magnetic moment tensor operator for non-Dirac particles with rest spin 1/2 and its essential difference from the spin operator are established. Possible consequences of the last fact for the description of the spin rotation in the magnetic field and for the values of magnetic moments of some nuclei, as well as the impact of this and other facts on the validity of modelling the azimuthal asymmetry of the secondary-scattered protons in $ep$-experiments are noted.

# Magnetic moment operator of non-Dirac particles and some elements of polarization $ep$-experiments

## Key claim

For non-Dirac particles with rest spin 1/2, the magnetic moment tensor operator is generally not proportional to the spin tensor operator, unlike the Dirac case; however, for states with definite 4-momentum, the proportionality holds in any inertial frame due to Lorentz covariance, but it is expected to break down in external fields, potentially affecting the validity of the Bargmann-Michel-Telegdi formula and the interpretation of polarization experiments.

## Method

The author derives the explicit form of the normal part of the magnetic moment tensor operator for a general relativistic field theory describing particles with rest spin 1/2, where the field transforms under a representation of the proper Lorentz group that may be finite- or infinite-dimensional. Starting from the minimal electromagnetic interaction and using the linear relativistic-invariant equation (Γ^ν p_ν - R)ψ(p) = 0, the operator is expressed in terms of the 4-vector operator Γ^ν, the infinitesimal Lorentz group operators I^{νρ}, and the 4-momentum operators P^σ. The analysis then examines the consequences for spin rotation in magnetic fields, the validity of the Bargmann-Michel-Telegdi formula, and the modeling of azimuthal asymmetry in secondary proton scattering in polarization ep-experiments.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic moment of deuterium (²H) is 0.857 μ_N, where μ_N is the nuclear magneton (0.880 μ_N for the sum of free proton and neutron magnetic moments).
- The magnetic moment of ⁶Li is 0.822 μ_N.
- The magnetic moment of ¹⁴N is 0.404 μ_N.
- The diamagnetic susceptibility of graphite perpendicular to graphene planes (along the c-axis) is about two orders of magnitude higher than that parallel to these planes.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous analysis (Slad, 2012) of the theoretical foundations of elastic electron-proton scattering experiments, specifically addressing the assumption that the proton is a Dirac particle. It builds on earlier work by Bargmann, Michel, and Telegdi (1959) on spin rotation in electromagnetic fields, and by Frenkel (1926) and Uhlenbeck and Goudsmit (1925) on the magnetic moment-spin relation. The paper challenges the interpretation of polarization experiments at Jefferson Laboratory (Jones et al., 2000; Gayou et al., 2002; Punjabi et al., 2005) that extracted the ratio G_E/G_M of proton form factors, suggesting that if the proton is a non-Dirac particle, the modeling of azimuthal asymmetry in secondary proton scattering may be unreliable. It also offers an alternative explanation for the magnetic moments of odd-odd nuclei (²H, ⁶Li, ¹⁴N) that have traditionally been explained by orbital momentum admixture (Blin-Stoyle, 1953).
