# No-Core shell model for A = 47 and A = 49

> We apply an {\it ab-initio} approach to the nuclear structure of odd-mass nuclei straddling $^{48}Ca$. Starting with the NN interaction, that fits two-body scattering and bound state data we evaluate the nuclear properties of $A = 47$ and $A = 49$ nuclei in a no-core approach. Due to model space limitations and the absence of 3-body interactions, we incorporate phenomenological terms determined by fits to $A = 48$ nuclei in a previous effort. Our modified Hamiltonian produces reasonable spectra for these odd mass nuclei. In addition to the differences in single-particle basis states, the absence of a single-particle Hamiltonian in our no-core approach obscures direct comparisons with valence effective NN interactions. Nevertheless, we compare the fp-shell matrix elements of our initial and modified Hamiltonians in the harmonic oscillator basis with a recent model fp-shell interaction, the GXPF1 interaction of Honma, Otsuka, Brown and Mizusaki. Notable differences emerge from these comparisons. In particular, our diagonal two-body $T = 0$ matrix elements are, on average, about 800-900keV more attractive. Furthermore, while our initial and modified NN Hamiltonian fp-shell matrix elements are strongly correlated, there is much less correlation with the GXPF1 matrix elements.

## Key claim

The no-core shell model (NCSM) with a modified Hamiltonian that adds phenomenological two-body terms (central modified gaussians with isospin-dependent strengths plus a tensor term) to the CD-Bonn NN interaction produces reasonable spectra for A=47 and A=49 odd-mass nuclei, despite the absence of three-body interactions and limited model space (Nmax=0, hbar-Omega=10 MeV). The authors note that the ab-initio CD-Bonn Hamiltonian alone produces spectra that are "far too compressed relative to experiment," and the modified Hamiltonian rectifies this primarily by improving the inferred neutron spin-orbit splitting.

## Method

The authors applied the no-core shell model (NCSM) with a two-body cluster approximation (a=2) using a harmonic oscillator basis at Nmax=0 and hbar-Omega=10 MeV, starting from the CD-Bonn NN interaction. They added three phenomenological terms (central modified gaussians with isospin-dependent strengths and a tensor term) that were previously fitted to the total binding energies of 48Ca, 48Sc, and 48Ti along with the lowest positive and negative parity excitations of 48Ca. The effective Hamiltonian was diagonalized using the m-scheme Lanczos method, and the resulting fp-shell two-body matrix elements in the harmonic oscillator basis were compared with the GXPF1 interaction of Honma, Otsuka, Brown and Mizusaki.

## Key numbers

- Ground states calculated with the ab-initio CD-Bonn Hamiltonian lie above experimental values by approximately 20 MeV for A=47 and A=49 nuclei
- For the modified Hamiltonian (CD-Bonn + 3 terms), deviations from experimental binding energies are "much less than 1%"
- The average shift applied to GXPF1 monopole matrix elements to match the CD-Bonn average is 0.899768 MeV for T=0 and T=1 combined
- The average shift applied to GXPF1 monopole matrix elements to match the CD-Bonn+3terms average is 0.83485 MeV
- Diagonal two-body T=0 matrix elements of the NCSM Hamiltonians are, on average, about 800-900 keV more attractive than those of GXPF1
- The 1p3/2 single-particle state centroid in 49Sc with the modified Hamiltonian is at -423.79 MeV, constructed from three states with occupancies of 51%, 28%, and 21%
- The 0d5/2 single-hole state in 47K is found at -386.17 MeV (13.36 MeV excitation energy) with 30% vacancy
- The 1p1/2 single-particle state in 49Ca with the modified Hamiltonian appears at 5.235 MeV excitation energy

## Context

This work extends the NCSM approach to heavier odd-mass nuclei (A=47 and A=49) following the authors' previous study of A=48 nuclei (Vary, Negoita, Stoica 2006). It contrasts with the full fp-shell calculations of Martinez-Pinedo, Zuker, Poves and Caurier (1996) who used the KB3 interaction with phenomenological adjustments, and with the GXPF1 interaction developed by Honma, Otsuka, Brown and Mizusaki (2004). The results demonstrate that the same modified Hamiltonian fitted to A=48 nuclei can describe odd-A neighbors without additional fitting, opening the path for multi-shell NCSM investigations of these nuclei and for testing the differences between ab-initio and valence effective interactions.
