# ABSSM - a new field-theoretic method of describing scattering/production processes of composite particles by asymptotic in-/outgoing Bethe-Salpeter states

> A new relativistic field-theoretic formalism called "Asymptotic Bethe-Salpeter State Method" (ABSSM) is presented, which allows to calculate both electromagnetic formfactors and hadronic scattering and production amplitudes of elementary and composite systems (being described by Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes) within one theoretical framework. The ABSSM is then applied to the deuteron as a composite system of two nucleons.

## Key claim

The Asymptotic Bethe-Salpeter State Method (ABSSM) provides a relativistic field-theoretic formalism that can calculate both electromagnetic formfactors and hadronic scattering/production amplitudes of composite systems within one framework, but its practical application relies on two key assumptions: that inverse LSZ-reduction is possible (yielding Heisenberg operators with renormalization constants) and that causality allows composite states to project onto time-ordered products of field operators.

## Method

The ABSSM constructs composite particle state vectors by decomposing them into free one-particle Fock states, then uses inverse LSZ-reduction to replace free-field operators with Heisenberg operators and time-ordered products. Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes (BSAs) are introduced via Fourier transformation of the time-ordered matrix elements in Jacobi coordinates, and the formalism is applied to the deuteron as a two-nucleon system using an ansatz for the BSA that includes S-wave and D-wave components.

## Key numbers

- The normalization condition for the deuteron S-wave and D-wave components yields the standard form: $1 = \int^\infty_0 d|\vec{q}| \; 8 |\vec{q}|^2 (|u|^2 + |w|^2)$
- The deuteron state is treated with nucleon mass $m_N$ and the ansatz uses the factor $\sqrt{2 \, \omega_{d^+} (\oabs{P}) / (2 \, \omega_N (|\vec{p}_1|) \; 2 \, \omega_N (|\vec{p}_2|))}$ for the wavefunction normalization
- The tensor operator ${\cal S}_{12} (\hat{\vec{q}})$ includes the factor $\sqrt{1/8}$ multiplying the D-wave component $w(|\vec{q}|,P)$ relative to the S-wave component $u(|\vec{q}|,P)$

## Context

This work extends the long tradition of relativistic field-theoretic treatment of asymptotic composite states dating back to Haag (1958), aiming to interpolate between theoretical requirements and computational capabilities. The ABSSM is applied to the deuteron as a test case, with results comparable to 4-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter results and previous quasi-potential (QP) calculations. The formalism enables calculation of deuteron electromagnetic formfactors in Impulse Approximation and total cross sections for reactions such as $NN \rightarrow d\pi$, $d\eta$, $d\eta^\prime$, with results to be discussed in a forthcoming publication.
