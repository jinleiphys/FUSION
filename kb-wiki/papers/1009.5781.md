# Transversity relations, chiral and holographic models, and pion wave functions from lattice QCD

> We analyze the equal-time Bethe-Salpeter quark wave functions of the pion in various models. We discuss how the quenched lattice QCD results with delocalized pion interpolators can be identified with the coarse grained wave functions, typical of low-energy effective models. Actually, we find that one-loop chiral quark models predict that pseudoscalar and tensor wave functions have the same shape, while the axial component is more extended. These facts are accurately confirmed by the lattice. We also show how the transversity information, relevant for the light-cone physics, can be straightforwardly obtained from the equal-time rest-frame lattice calculations. This remarkable relation provides a way to extract, for instance, the equal-time holographic wave functions and compare them, quite favorably, to the lattice calculations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that quenched lattice QCD results with delocalized pion interpolators can be identified with coarse-grained wave functions typical of low-energy effective models, and that one-loop chiral quark models predict pseudoscalar and tensor wave functions have the same shape while the axial component is more extended, a fact accurately confirmed by the lattice.

## Method

The authors analyze equal-time Bethe-Salpeter quark wave functions of the pion by comparing quenched lattice QCD calculations (using smeared or delocalized interpolators) with predictions from the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, the Spectral Quark Model (SQM), and holographic models. They establish transversity relations connecting equal-time rest-frame wave functions to light-cone wave functions integrated over Bjorken-x, allowing extraction of holographic wave functions from lattice data.

## Key numbers

- NJL model with constituent quark mass M=300 MeV reproduces lattice data; the asymptotic behavior gives Ψ_P(r)=2Ψ_T(r) ~ e^{-Mr}/r^{3/2} and Ψ_A(r) ~ e^{-Mr}/r^{1/2}
- SQM fits to lattice data yield M_V/2 = 505(30), 520(20), 530(14) MeV for m_π = 345, 475, 575 MeV respectively; quadratic extrapolation to chiral limit gives M_V/2 = 493(20) MeV
- Holographic model parameters: κ = 950 MeV and A² = 2/π² = 0.2 from fixing f_π = 92.4 MeV
- Lattice data at r=0.6 fm show the ratio r Ψ_A(r)/Ψ_P(r) approaches a constant
- Holographic model fits to lattice data yield m=140 MeV for m_π=575 MeV and m=51 MeV for m_π=345 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of pion wave functions (Broniowski 2009) and compares them to chiral quark models reviewed by Ruiz Arriola (2002). The transversity relations connect to earlier work on Generalized Parton Distributions for the nucleon (Miller 2007) and pion (Broniowski 2007), and enable direct comparison of light-cone holographic models (de Teramond 2008, Vega 2009) with Euclidean lattice calculations. The approach provides a framework for extracting infinite-momentum frame pion wave functions from equal-time rest-frame lattice calculations.
