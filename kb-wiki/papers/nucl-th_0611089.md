# Global study of the spectroscopic properties of the first 2+ state in even-even nuclei

> We discuss the systematics of the 2+ excitation energy and the transition probability from this 2+ to the ground state for most of the even-even nuclei, from O16 up to the actinides, for which data are available. To that aim we calculate their correlated J=0 ground state and J=2 first excited state by means of the angular-momentum and particle-number projected generator coordinate method, using the axial mass quadrupole moment as the generator coordinate and self-consistent mean-field states only restricted by axial, parity, and time-reversal symmetries. The calculation, which is an extension of a previous systematic calculation of correlations in the ground state, is performed within the framework of a non-relativistic self-consistent mean-field model using the same Skyrme interaction SLy4 and a density-dependent pairing force to generate the mean-field configurations and mix them.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that beyond-mean-field correlations, specifically angular-momentum and particle-number projection combined with configuration mixing via the Generator Coordinate Method, systematically improve the description of first 2+ excitation energies and B(E2) transition probabilities across the nuclear chart, but the theoretical excitation energies still overpredict experiment by an average factor of about 1.5.

## Method

The authors performed angular-momentum and particle-number projected Generator Coordinate Method calculations using the axial mass quadrupole moment as the generator coordinate, starting from self-consistent mean-field states generated with the Skyrme interaction SLy4 and a density-dependent pairing force. They applied two approaches: minimization after projection (MAP), which selects a single projected configuration for each angular momentum, and a truncated configuration mixing scheme (HW-6) that mixes typically six projected states. The calculations covered 359 even-even nuclei from O-16 to the actinides, using a topological Gaussian overlap approximation to reduce computational cost for the angular-momentum projection integrals.

## Key numbers

* The theoretical 2+ excitation energies span more than 2 orders of magnitude, ranging below 100 keV in deformed actinide nuclei to a few MeV in doubly-magic nuclei.
* The average ratio of theoretical to experimental excitation energy is about 1.5 for both MAP and HW-6 approaches.
* For the configuration mixing approach (HW-6), the mean error on experimental B(E2) values is only 50%.
* For the test case of Pu-240, the MAP excitation energy is 0.076 MeV compared to the experimental value of 0.043 MeV, and the reduced transition matrix element is 377 e fm² versus 361 e fm² experimentally.
* For Ar-38, the MAP excitation energy is 3.9 MeV versus the experimental 2.17 MeV, and the HW-6 value is 3.8 MeV.
* The HW-6 truncation reproduces the full calculation 2+ excitation energies to an r.m.s. accuracy of better than 0.1 MeV for the tested nuclei.
* The theoretical energies range from 39 keV to 6.9 MeV across the 522 nuclei with known 2+ excitation energies.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous systematic study of ground-state correlations in even-even nuclei (Sabbey et al., 2005; Bender et al., 2006) to the first 2+ excitation energy and B(E2) transition probabilities. It builds on earlier projected GCM studies of individual nuclei using Skyrme interactions (Bender et al., 2003, 2004) and Gogny interactions (Rodriguez et al., 2002; Egido et al., 2004), as well as relativistic Lagrangians (Nikšić et al., 2006). The results enable systematic comparison of beyond-mean-field methods with experimental data across the nuclear chart, identifying where deficiencies in the energy density functional or the restricted configuration space (axial symmetry, no broken-pair states) most strongly affect predictions, particularly for light nuclei and shape-coexisting systems like the light Pb isotopes.
