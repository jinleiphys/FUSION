# Production of a pion in association with a high-Q2 dilepton pair in antiproton-proton annihilation at GSI-FAIR

> We evaluate the cross section for anti-p p -> l+ l- pi0 in the forward direction and for large lepton pair invariant mass. In this kinematical region, the leading-twist amplitude factorises into a short-distance matrix element, long-distance dominated antiproton Distribution Amplitudes and proton to pion Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDA). Using a modelling inspired from the chiral limit for these TDAs, we obtain a first estimate of this cross section, thus demonstrating that this process can be measured at GSI-FAIR.

## Key claim

The authors evaluate the cross section for $\bar p p \to \ell^+ \ell^- \pi^0$ in the forward direction and for large lepton pair invariant mass, demonstrating that the process can be measured at GSI-FAIR, but only under the condition that the leading-twist amplitude factorises and using a modelling of the proton-to-pion Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs) inspired from the chiral limit, which is valid only at large values of the skewedness parameter $\xi$ (i.e., small pion momentum in the laboratory frame).

## Method

The authors compute the leading-twist amplitude for the exclusive annihilation process $\bar p p \to \gamma^\star \pi^0$ at small momentum transfer $t$ (forward direction) and large $Q^2$, factorising it into a perturbatively-calculable hard-scattering amplitude, antiproton Distribution Amplitudes (DAs), and proton-to-pion Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs). The TDAs are modelled from the chiral-limit soft-pion theorem, which relates them to the proton DAs of Chernyak and Zhitnitsky (CZ) and King and Sachrajda (KS) at large $\xi$ (where $\xi \approx Q^2/(2W^2-Q^2)$). The differential cross section for the dilepton final state is then obtained by integrating over the lepton azimuthal angle and the lepton polar angle $\theta_\ell$.

## Key numbers

* The differential cross section $d\sigma/dt$ for $\bar p p \to \gamma^\star \pi^0$ is evaluated at $\Delta_T=0$ for $W^2$ up to $\simeq 30$ GeV$^2$ (the maximal reachable value at GSI-FAIR for $E_{\bar p}=15$ GeV), with the highest possible photon invariant mass $Q^2_{max} \simeq 30$ GeV$^2$.
* The integral ${\cal I}$ over the DAs and TDAs yields ${\cal I} \simeq 1.28 \times 10^5$ for the CZ model and ${\cal I} \simeq 2.15 \times 10^5$ for the KS model, inducing an uncertainty of order 3 in the cross-section estimates.
* For a specific kinematic region with $W^2=10$ GeV$^2$, $Q^2$ window $7$ GeV$^2 < Q^2 < 8$ GeV$^2$ (corresponding to $\xi \approx 1/2$ and pion momentum $\approx 310$ MeV), and a $t$-bin with $\Delta_T < 500$ MeV, the integrated cross section is around 100 femtobarns.
* The pion decay constant used is $f_\pi = 131$ MeV, and the proton wave function at the origin is $f_N \sim 5.2 \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^2$.

## Context

This work extends the concept of Transition Distribution Amplitudes (TDAs), previously introduced for backward electroproduction of a pion (Lansberg, 2007) and for $\gamma^\star \gamma \to \rho \pi$ and $\gamma^\star \gamma \to \pi \pi$ reactions, to the antiproton-proton annihilation channel. It provides the first numerical estimate for the process $\bar p p \to \ell^+ \ell^- \pi^0$ at GSI-FAIR, enabling future experimental studies with the PANDA or PAX detectors. The same TDAs also appear in backward electroproduction of a pion, and the authors note that other channels such as $\bar p p \to l^+ l^- \eta$ or $\bar p p \to l^+ l^- \rho^0$ are of interest but lack model estimates for the corresponding $p\to \eta$ and $p\to \rho$ TDAs.
