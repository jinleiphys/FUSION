# Microscopic and non-adiabatic Schrödinger equation derived from the Generator Coordinate Method based on 0 and 2 quasiparticle HFB states

> A new approach called the Schrödinger Collective Intrinsic Model (SCIM) has been developed to achieve a microscopic description of the coupling between collective and intrinsic excitations. The derivation of the SCIM proceeds in two steps. The first step is based on a generalization of the symmetric moment expansion of the equations derived in the framework of the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM), when both Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) states and two-quasi-particle excitations are taken into account as basis states. The second step consists in reducing the generalized Hill and Wheeler equation to a simpler form to extract a Schrödinger-like equation. The validity of the approach is discussed by means of results obtained for the overlap kernel between HFB states and two-quasi-particle excitations at different deformations.

## Key claim

The paper derives a microscopic, non-adiabatic Schrödinger equation (the Schrödinger Collective Intrinsic Model, SCIM) from the Generator Coordinate Method by including both Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov ground states and two-quasiparticle excitations, but the derivation assumes that the series expansions of the overlap and Hamiltonian kernels converge rapidly, limiting the approach to collective motions with momenta that are not too large (moderate energies).

## Method

The authors first generalize the symmetric moment expansion of the Hill-Wheeler equation to include both 0 and 2 quasiparticle HFB states as basis states, producing a set of coupled integro-differential equations. They then reduce this expansion to a local Schrödinger-like equation by inverting the overlap kernel expansion, truncating both the overlap and Hamiltonian kernel expansions at second order in the collective momentum. The overlap kernel between HFB states and 2-quasiparticle excitations is calculated numerically for the symmetric fission of 236U using a constrained Hartree-Fock + BCS method with an axial harmonic oscillator basis of 14 major shells.

## Key numbers

* The symmetric fission barrier of 236U has its ground state near 30 barns, first barrier at 50 barns, second well at 80 barns, and second saddle point at 160 barns.
* Overlap kernel calculations were performed at deformations in the range [20 barns, 350 barns] with a step of 10 barns up to 100 barns and a step of 25 barns beyond.
* The lowest 2-quasiparticle excitation energy is around 3 MeV near q=70 barns.
* For the 236U fission barrier, an average of 6.5 proton two-quasiparticle excitations and 11.5 neutron excitations per deformation are found with K^pi=0^+, energy below 5 MeV, and mean particle numbers in the range 92-1 to 92+1 for protons and 144-1 to 144+1 for neutrons.
* A level repulsion between two K^pi=1/2^+ single-quasiparticle levels occurs at q_r=29.5 barns, with crossings observed at 34 barns and 40 barns.

## Context

This work extends the Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) beyond the adiabatic approximation used in prior studies such as Bertsch (2007) and Delaroche (2010) for five-dimensional collective Hamiltonians, and Goutte (2005) for fission dynamics. It builds on earlier GCM+2-quasiparticle approaches by Didong (1973) and Muther (1977), but provides a fully microscopic derivation of a Schrödinger equation rather than relying on semi-classical formalisms like Focker-Planck equations (Nix 1984) or Langevin equations (Nadtochy 2007). The formalism enables microscopic study of non-adiabatic effects in fission, shape coexistence, backbending, and decay of superdeformed states.
