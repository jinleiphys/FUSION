# Undersaturation of quarks at early stages of relativistic nuclear collisions: the hot glue initial scenario and its observable signatures

> The early stage of high multiplicity nuclear collisions is represented by a nearly quarkless, hot, deconfined pure gluon plasma. This new scenario should be characterized by a suppression of high $p_T$ photons and dileptons as well as by reduced baryon to meson ratios. We present the numerical results for central Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC energies by using the ideal Bjorken hydrodynamics with time-dependent quark fugacity. It is shown that about 25\% of final total entropy is generated during the hydrodynamic evolution of chemically undersaturated quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim
The early stage of high-multiplicity nuclear collisions may be a nearly quarkless, hot, deconfined pure gluon plasma, and this "hot glue initial scenario" predicts suppression of high-$p_T$ photons and dileptons as well as reduced baryon-to-meson ratios. The claim is hedged by the assumption that a thermally (but not necessarily chemically) equilibrated QGP is created initially, and that the quark fugacity evolves from zero toward unity over a characteristic time $\tau_*$ that is not precisely known.

## Method
The authors use one-dimensional scaling (Bjorken) hydrodynamics with an ideal-gas equation of state for massless gluons, quarks, and antiquarks at zero net baryon density. Chemical undersaturation of quarks is parameterized by a time-dependent quark fugacity $\lambda(\tau)$, which evolves from zero to unity via an analytic parametrization $\lambda(\tau)=1-\exp[(\tau_0-\tau)/\tau_*]$, where $\tau_*$ is the quark chemical equilibration time. The hydrodynamic evolution is solved for central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV, with the freeze-out temperature fixed to $T_f=156$ MeV from thermal fits of hadron ratios, and the initial conditions are constrained by the observed final pion yield.

## Key numbers
- About 25% of the final total entropy is generated during the hydrodynamic evolution of the chemically undersaturated quark-gluon plasma.
- The freeze-out temperature is taken as $T_f = 156$ MeV, extracted from thermal fits of hadron ratios in central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV (Stachel et al., 2014).
- The observed pion yield at midrapidity is $dN_\pi/dy \simeq 2700$ (Abbas et al., 2013), and the entropy per pion at freeze-out is $\nu \simeq 6.3$, giving a total entropy per unit space-time rapidity of $dS(\tau_f)/d\eta \simeq 1.7 \times 10^4$.
- The quark chemical equilibration time $\tau_*$ is estimated to range from $\tau_* \sim 1$ fm/$c$ (Ruggieri et al., 2015) to $\tau_* \sim 5$ fm/$c$ (Xu & Greiner, 2005).
- For the pure glue scenario (no quarks), the first-order phase transition temperature from lattice QCD is $T_c \simeq 270$ MeV (Celik et al., 1983a, 1983b; Karsch, 2002; Borsanyi et al., 2012; Francis et al., 2015).

## Context
This work extends the two-step equilibration scenario proposed by Raha (1990), Shuryak (1992), and others, in which gluons thermalize faster than quarks. It builds on transport calculations (Biro et al., 1993; Roy et al., 1997; Elliott & Rischke, 2000; Blaizot et al., 2013; Uphoff et al., 2015) that found very few soft quarks at early times. The paper provides quantitative predictions for dilepton and photon spectra that can be tested against LHC data (e.g., the ALICE photon data from Lohner et al., 2013), and it suggests that future extractions of viscosity from data should account for the initial suppression of quarks. The pure glue limit also connects to lattice QCD studies of the SU(3) Yang-Mills first-order phase transition and to the Frautschi-Hagedorn approach for glueball decays (Beitel et al., 2014).
