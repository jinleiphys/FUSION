# Approaching Pomeranchuk Instabilities from Ordered Phase: A Crossing-symmetric Equation Method

> We explore features of a 3D Fermi liquid near generalized Pomeranchuk instabilities using a tractable crossing symmetric equation method. We approach the instabilities from the ordered ferromagnetic phase. We find quantum multi-criticality as approach to the ferromagnetic instability drives instability in other channel(s). It is found that a charge nematic instability precedes and is driven by Pomeranchuk instabilities in both the l = 0 spin and density channels.

## Key claim
The authors find that when approaching a ferromagnetic Pomeranchuk instability from the ordered ferromagnetic phase, a charge nematic instability (ℓ=2) precedes and is driven by the approach to both the ℓ=0 spin and density channel instabilities, even when only ℓ=0 quantum fluctuations are included and the underlying interaction is zero-range.

## Method
The authors employ the tractable crossing symmetric equation (TCSE) method, a diagrammatic many-particle approach that treats underlying interactions and quantum fluctuations on the same footing. They solve coupled non-linear integral equations for the Landau interaction functions Fℓ^{s,a} in a 3D isotropic Fermi liquid, starting from the ferromagnetic state described by Abrikosov-Dzyaloshinskii-Kondratenko theory. Solutions are found using both graphical and numerical techniques, including contour integration to handle finite-momentum divergences (generalized Pomeranchuk instabilities) in the parameter space.

## Key numbers
- The Pomeranchuk instability condition for a given angular momentum channel ℓ is Fℓ^{s,a} ≤ -(2ℓ+1); for ℓ=0 this gives F0^{s,a} ≤ -1, and for ℓ=2 charge channel F2^{s} ≤ -5.
- In the paramagnetic branch for repulsive interactions, F0^{a} approaches -0.63 from the positive side as the interaction strength U increases.
- In the strongly ferromagnetic branch, F0^{a} approaches -U/3 and F0^{s} approaches -0.63 from the positive side.
- In the weakly ferromagnetic branch near the FM instability, F0^{a} approaches -2 from the negative side (the q=2kF generalized Pomeranchuk instability) and F0^{s} approaches -1 from the positive side.
- The Lindhard function χ0(q') ranges from 0.5 to 1 for momentum transfers q' between 0 and 2kF, giving finite-q divergences when -2 ≤ F0^{s,a} ≤ -1.

## Context
This work extends previous TCSE method calculations by Quader, Bedell, and Brown (1987) that approached Pomeranchuk instabilities from the paramagnetic state, and contrasts with Hertz-Millis-type effective theories and the 2D Fermi liquid analysis of Chubukov and collaborators (2010) near charge nematic and spin instabilities. The results enable further study of quantum multicriticality and the possibility of switching between singlet and triplet pairing via symmetry-breaking mechanisms in ferromagnetic Fermi liquids.
