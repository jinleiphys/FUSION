# Composite nature of $Z_b$ states from data analysis

> We use a near-threshold parameterization with explicit inclusion of the Castillejo-Dalitz-Dyson poles, which is more general than the effective range expansion, to study the bottomonium-like states $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$. In terms of the partial-wave amplitude, we fit the event number distribution of $B^{(*)}\bar B^*$ system to the experimental data for these resonances from Belle Collaboration. The data could be described very well in our method, which supports the molecular interpretation. Then the relevant physical quantities are obtained, including the $B^{(*)}\bar{B}^*$ scattering length ($a$), effective range ($r$), and residue squared ($γ_s^2$) of the pole in the complex plane. In particular, we find the compositeness can range from about 0.4 up to 1 for the $B\bar B^*$ ($B^*\bar B^*$) component in the resonance $Z_b(10610)$ ($Z_b(10650)$).

# Composite nature of $Z_b$ states from data analysis

## Key claim
The data can be described very well by a near-threshold parameterization that includes a Castillejo-Dalitz-Dyson (CDD) pole, supporting the molecular interpretation of the $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ resonances; however, the current data quality is not sufficient to pin down the CDD pole position, and the compositeness coefficient $X$ of the two-meson component can range from about 0.4 up to 1 for both resonances.

## Method
The authors use a partial-wave amplitude for $S$-wave $B^{(*)}\bar B^*$ scattering that includes one CDD pole explicitly, which is more general than the effective range expansion (ERE). They fit the event number distribution of the $B^{(*)}\bar B^*$ invariant mass spectra from Belle Collaboration data for the decays $\Upsilon(10860)\rightarrow [B^{(*)}\bar{B}^*]^{\pm}\pi^{\mp}$, using a signal function $d(E)$ derived from the scattering amplitude and a background parameterization, with the resonance pole mass and width fixed to experimental values.

## Key numbers
- $Z_b(10610)$ mass: $10607.2 \pm 2.0$ MeV, width: $18.4 \pm 2.4$ MeV
- $Z_b(10650)$ mass: $10652.2 \pm 1.5$ MeV, width: $11.5 \pm 2.2$ MeV
- $Z_b(10610)$ sits 3 MeV above the $B\bar B^*$ threshold; $Z_b(10650)$ sits 2.8 MeV above the $B^*\bar B^*$ threshold
- Compositeness $X$ for $Z_b(10610)$ ranges from 0.39 to 1.00; for $Z_b(10650)$ ranges from 0.36 to 1.00
- For the $X=1$ case: scattering length $a = -4.44$ fm and effective range $r = -1.65$ fm for $Z_b(10610)$; $a = -3.31$ fm and $r = -1.72$ fm for $Z_b(10650)$
- $\chi^2/\text{dof}$ values range from 0.78 to 1.13 across fits

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Kang (2016), which used only the pole position to estimate compositeness, by fitting the full mass spectrum data from Belle (2015). The method addresses the breakdown of effective range expansion when CDD poles lie near threshold, as discussed by Baru (2010). The results enable future studies where additional observables such as polarization and forward-backward asymmetry, as explored in Zhang (2020) and Haidenbauer (2014), could further constrain the inner structure of these exotic states.
