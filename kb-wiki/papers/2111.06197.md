# The Second Love Number of Dark Compact Planets and Neutron Stars with Dark Matter

> We study the mass-radius relation and the second Love number of compact objects made of ordinary matter and non-selfannihilating fermionic dark matter for a wide range of dark matter particle masses, and for the cases of weakly and strongly interacting dark matter. We obtain stable configurations of compact objects with radii smaller than 10 km and masses similar to Earth- or Jupiter-like stellar objects. In certain parameter ranges we find second Love numbers which are markedly different compared to those expected for neutron stars without dark matter. Thus, by obtaining the compactness of these compact objects and measuring their tidal deformability from gravitational wave detections from binary neutron star mergers, the extracted value of second Love number would allow to determine the existence of dark matter inside neutron stars irrespective of the equation of state of ordinary matter.

## Key claim

The second Love number of compact objects containing both ordinary matter and non-selfannihilating fermionic dark matter can differ markedly from that of ordinary neutron stars in certain parameter ranges, potentially allowing the existence of dark matter inside neutron stars to be determined from gravitational wave tidal deformability measurements irrespective of the ordinary matter equation of state.

## Method

The authors solve coupled two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for ordinary nuclear matter (using the EoSI equation of state from chiral effective theory and perturbative QCD) and fermionic dark matter that interact only gravitationally. They compute the second Love number by solving the differential equation for the tidal deformability alongside the TOV equations, considering dark matter particle masses from 1 to 100 GeV and two interaction strengths: weakly interacting (strength parameter y=0.1) and strongly interacting (y=1000), with the ratio of central pressures between dark and ordinary matter varied to explore different configurations.

## Key numbers

- Stable compact object configurations with radii smaller than 10 km and masses similar to Earth- or Jupiter-like stellar objects are obtained
- For weakly interacting dark matter with m_F=1 GeV and intermediate pressure ratio, the second stable branch contains 21% dark matter mass at maximum mass configuration
- For strongly interacting dark matter with m_F=1 GeV, the dark matter fraction at maximum mass is 98.5%
- The maximum compactness for pure fermionic dark matter without interactions is C=0.11, while for strongly interacting matter it reaches C=0.21
- For strongly interacting dark matter with m_F=1 GeV, the second Love number reaches approximately 0.22 at a compactness of about 0.02
- For weakly interacting dark matter with m_F=1 GeV, the maximum compactness in the mixed configuration is approximately 0.2

## Context

This work corrects and extends previous calculations by Tolos et al. (2015) and Deliyergiyev et al. (2019) on dark compact planets, providing a systematic parametric study of second Love numbers for fermionic dark matter admixed with ordinary matter that was missing in the literature. Prior work by Nelson et al. (2018) found that interacting dark matter fermions in the MeV-GeV range form halos increasing tidal deformability, while Ellis et al. (2018) found reduced tidal deformability for bosonic dark matter cores; this paper bridges these results by examining both weakly and strongly interacting fermionic dark matter across a wide mass range. The results enable future gravitational wave detections from binary neutron star mergers to potentially constrain dark matter properties through tidal deformability measurements.
