# On the widths and binding energies of $K^-$ nuclear states and the role of $K^-$ multi-nucleon interactions

> We report on our recent self-consistent calculations of $K^-$ nuclear quasi-bound states using $K^-$ optical potentials derived from chirally motivated meson-baryon coupled channels models [1,2]. The $K^-$ single-nucleon potentials were supplemented by a phenomenological $K^-$ multi-nucleon interaction term introduced to achieve good fits to $K^-$ atom data. We demonstrate a substantial impact of the $K^-$ multi-nucleon absorption on the widths of $K^-$ nuclear states. If such states ever exist in nuclear many-body systems, their widths are excessively large to allow observation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including phenomenological $K^-$ multi-nucleon interaction terms, fitted to kaonic atom data, causes a radical increase in the widths of $K^-$ nuclear quasi-bound states, making them excessively large for experimental observation, if such states exist at all in nuclear many-body systems.

## Method

The authors solved the Klein-Gordon equation self-consistently for $K^-$ nuclear quasi-bound states using $K^-$ optical potentials constructed from chirally motivated meson-baryon coupled channels models (P and KM). The $K^-$ single-nucleon potentials were supplemented by a phenomenological multi-nucleon interaction term $V_{K^-}^{(2)} = -4\pi B (\rho/\rho_0)^{\alpha} \rho$, with parameters fitted to kaonic atom data by Friedman and Gal (2016), applied either throughout the entire nucleus (full density, FD) or fixed at half-saturation density (half density, HD).

## Key numbers

- In $^{208}$Pb, the 1s $K^-$ binding energy calculated with the KM single-nucleon potential alone is 78 MeV, with width 38 MeV; adding the multi-nucleon potential with $\alpha=1$ in the HD option gives $B_{K^-}=64$ MeV and $\Gamma_{K^-}=108$ MeV, while the FD option gives $B_{K^-}=33$ MeV and $\Gamma_{K^-}=273$ MeV.
- In $^{208}$Pb with the P model, the single-nucleon potential yields $B_{K^-}=99$ MeV and $\Gamma_{K^-}=14$ MeV; the HD option with $\alpha=1$ gives $B_{K^-}=82$ MeV and $\Gamma_{K^-}=92$ MeV, while the FD option gives $B_{K^-}=37$ MeV and $\Gamma_{K^-}=302$ MeV.
- For $^{12}$C, the FD option of the multi-nucleon potential predicts no bound $K^-$ state in either the P or KM model, while the HD option yields widths of 96–114 MeV.
- The energy shift $\delta\sqrt{s}$ in $^{208}$Pb reaches values near the $K^-N \rightarrow \pi\Sigma$ threshold at nuclear densities, with the KM1 and P1 models (FD, $\alpha=1$) producing shallower shifts than the single-nucleon potentials.
- The imaginary part of the $K^-$ optical potential in $^{208}$Pb for the FD option of the KM model exceeds the real part in magnitude inside the nucleus ($|$Im$V_{K^-}| \gg |$Re$V_{K^-}|$).

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent calculations of $K^-$ nuclear quasi-bound states by Friedman and Gal (2016), who fitted multi-nucleon interaction parameters to kaonic atom data using the P and KM chiral coupled channels models. The results contradict earlier optimistic predictions of narrow $K^-$ nuclear states and show that the widths (typically 100–500 MeV) far exceed binding energies (17–96 MeV) in all nuclei studied, making experimental identification highly unlikely. The paper enables future studies to focus on whether any observable signature of $K^-$ absorption in nuclei might survive despite these large widths.
