# Cooling compact stars and phase transitions in dense QCD

> We report new simulations of cooling of compact stars containing quark cores and updated fits to the Cas A fast cooling data. Our model is built on the assumption that the transient behaviour of the star in Cas A is due to a phase transition within the dense QCD matter in the core of the star. Specifically, the fast cooling is attributed to an enhancement in the neutrino emission triggered by a transition from a fully gapped, two-flavor, red-green color-superconducting quark condensate to a superconducting crystalline or an alternative gapless, color-superconducting phase. The blue colored condensate is modeled as a Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS)-type color superconductor with spin-one pairing order parameter. We study the sensitivity of the fits to the phase transition temperature, the pairing gap of blue quarks and the time-scale characterizing the phase transition (the latter modelled in terms of a width parameter). Relative variations in these parameter around their best fit values larger than $10^{-3}$ spoil the fit to the data. We confirm the previous finding that the cooling curves show significant variations as a function of compact star mass, which allows one to account for dispersion in the data on the surface temperatures of thermally emitting neutron stars.

## Key claim

The fast cooling of the compact star in Cassiopeia A is attributed to a phase transition within dense QCD matter in the star's core, specifically from a fully gapped two-flavor red-green color-superconducting condensate to a crystalline or gapless color-superconducting phase, but the model requires that relative variations in the phase transition temperature, blue quark pairing gap, and transition width larger than $10^{-3}$ around their best fit values spoil the fit to the data.

## Method

The author constructed equilibrium models of hybrid compact stars with a sharp phase transition interface between a nucleonic envelope and a quark core, using equations of state from an effective Nambu-Jona-Lasinio four-fermion interaction model with pairing interactions. The thermal evolution was computed using an isothermal core approximation, with neutrino emissivities modeled for unpaired quark matter, red-green gapless or crystalline color-superconducting phases, blue BCS-type paired quarks, and standard hadronic phases including modified Urca processes and Cooper pair-breaking processes. The phase transition from the two-flavor color-superconductor (2SC) to the Fulde-Ferrell crystalline phase was parameterized by a temperature-dependent suppression factor controlled by the transition temperature $T^*$, the blue quark pairing gap $\Delta_b$, and the transition width $w$.

## Key numbers

- The best estimate from Elshamouty et al. (2013) indicates a decline in the surface temperature of the Cas A neutron star of $2.9\pm 0.9\%$ over 10 years of observation (2003-2013).
- Relative variations in the phase transition temperature, blue quark pairing gap, and transition width larger than $10^{-3}$ around their best fit values spoil the fit to the Cas A data.
- The models with low central densities ($\rho_c \le 3\rho_0$) have masses $M \le 1.85 M_\odot$ and are purely hadronic; models with larger central densities contain quark cores, with the most massive model listed having total mass $M = 2.050 M_\odot$, quark core radius $R_Q = 6.77$ km, and central density $\rho_{c,14} = 21.0$ (in units of $10^{14}$ g cm$^{-3}$).
- The critical temperature for the nucleon superfluid phase transition is $T_{c,n} \simeq 10^9$ K.
- The blue quark pairing gaps are estimated to be in the range 10-100 keV from theoretical calculations.
- The envelope model uses parameters $\beta = 0.55$ and $\alpha = 18.1$ for the relation between surface and core temperatures.

## Context

This work extends previous cooling simulations of hybrid stars containing superconducting quark matter by Sedrakian (2013) and Hess and Sedrakian (2011), specifically interpreting the rapid cooling of the Cas A compact star as a phase transition within the QCD phase diagram. The model assumes a sharp interface between nucleonic and quark phases, as opposed to the mixed phase approach used by Negreiros et al. (2013). The results confirm earlier findings that cooling curves vary significantly with compact star mass, enabling interpretation of the dispersion in observed surface temperatures of thermally emitting neutron stars, and the model enables future constraints on the poorly known pairing interaction among quarks in the non-perturbative regime of QCD.
