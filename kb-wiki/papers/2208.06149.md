# Constraints on neutron skin thickness and nuclear deformations using relativistic heavy-ion collisions from STAR

> In these proceedings, we present the measurements of neutron skin thickness and nuclear deformation using isobar $^{96}_{44}$Ru+$^{96}_{44}$Ru and $^{96}_{40}$Zr+$^{96}_{40}$Zr collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=200$ GeV by the STAR detector. The significant deviations from unity of the isobar ratios of elliptic flow $v_{2}$, triangular flow $v_{3}$, mean $p_{\rm T}$ fluctuations $\langleδp_{\rm T}^{2}\rangle/\langle p_{\rm T}\rangle^{2}$, and asymmetric cumulant ${\rm ac}_{2}\{3\}$ indicate large differences in their quadrupole and octuple deformations. The significant deviations of the isobar ratios of produced hadron multiplicity $N_{\rm ch}$, mean transverse momentum $\langle p_{\rm T}\rangle$, and net charge number $ΔQ$ indicate a halo-type neutron skin for the Zr nucleus, much thicker than for the Ru nucleus, consistent with nuclear structure calculations. We discuss how we extract the neutron skin thickness, the symmetry energy slope parameter, and deformation parameters from data.

# Constraints on neutron skin thickness and nuclear deformations using relativistic heavy-ion collisions from STAR

## Key claim
The isobar ratio measurements of multiplicity, mean transverse momentum, and net charge indicate a halo-type neutron skin for the Zr nucleus that is much thicker than for the Ru nucleus, consistent with nuclear structure calculations; however, the extracted values depend on model choices (TRENTo vs. Glauber) and are dominated by systematic uncertainties from nuclear deformation effects.

## Method
The STAR collaboration analyzed approximately 2 billion minimum-bias events each for isobar collisions of Ru-96 and Zr-96 at a center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair of 200 GeV. They measured isobar ratios of elliptic flow v2, triangular flow v3, mean transverse momentum fluctuations, asymmetric cumulant ac2{3}, charged hadron multiplicity Nch, mean transverse momentum, and net charge number ΔQ. The experimental ratios were compared to simulations using the AMPT model, Monte Carlo Glauber model, and iEBE-VISHNU hydrodynamic model, with nuclear density distributions calculated from energy density functional theory (DFT) with varying symmetry energy slope parameters.

## Key numbers
- Quadrupole deformation parameter for Ru: β2,Ru = 0.16 ± 0.02 (statistical and systematic uncertainties combined)
- Octuple deformation parameter for Zr: β3,Zr = 0.20 ± 0.02 (statistical and systematic uncertainties combined)
- Symmetry energy slope parameter from multiplicity distributions: L(ρc) = 53.8 ± 1.7 (stat.) ± 7.8 (sys.) MeV, where ρc = 0.11 fm^-3 is nuclear subsaturation cross density
- Symmetry energy slope parameter from mean transverse momentum: L(ρc) = 56.8 ± 0.4 (stat.) ± 10.4 (sys.) MeV
- Neutron skin thickness for Ru from multiplicity: (Δrnp)Ru = 0.051 ± 0.009 fm
- Neutron skin thickness for Zr from multiplicity: (Δrnp)Zr = 0.195 ± 0.019 fm
- Neutron skin thickness for Ru from mean pT: (Δrnp)Ru = 0.052 ± 0.012 fm
- Neutron skin thickness for Zr from mean pT: (Δrnp)Zr = 0.202 ± 0.024 fm
- Ratio of mean multiplicity in top 2% central collisions: 1.0019 with statistical uncertainties smaller than 0.0001

## Context
This work extends predictions from DFT calculations by Xu (2017) and Li (2018) that nuclear structure differences between isobar nuclei would affect experimental observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The measurements provide an alternative to traditional low-energy hadronic and nuclear scattering experiments for determining neutron skin thickness, and address tensions with the PREX-II experiment (2021) which reported a large neutron skin thickness for Pb. The results enable improved constraints on the symmetry energy slope parameter and nuclear equation of state relevant for neutron star physics, with future improvements possible as nuclear deformation parameters become better constrained.
