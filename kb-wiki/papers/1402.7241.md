# Symmetric Tamm-Dancoff q-oscillator: representation, quasi-Fibonacci nature, accidental degeneracy and coherent states

> In this paper we propose a symmetric q-deformed Tamm-Dancoff (S-TD) oscillator algebra and study its representation, coordinate realization, and main properties. In particular, the non-Fibonacci (more exactly, quasi-Fibonacci) nature of S-TD oscillator is established, the possibility of relating it to certain p,q-deformed oscillator family shown, the occurrence of the pairwise accidental degeneracy proven. We also find the coherent state for the S-TD oscillator and show that it satisfies completeness relation. Main advantage of the S-TD model over usual Tamm-Dancoff oscillator is that due to (q<-->q^{-1})- symmetry it admits not only real, but also complex (phase-like) values of the deformation parameter q.

## Key claim

The symmetric Tamm-Dancoff (S-TD) q-oscillator, unlike the usual Tamm-Dancoff oscillator, admits not only real but also complex (phase-like) values of the deformation parameter q due to its (q ↔ q⁻¹) symmetry, and this enables pairwise accidental energy level degeneracy for complex q that does not occur for real positive q.

## Method

The authors define the S-TD oscillator algebra via a modified commutation relation and structure function φ_STD(n) = (n/2)(qⁿ⁻¹ + q⁻ⁿ⁺¹). They construct its Fock-type representation, develop a corresponding non-standard q-calculus (deformed derivative D_x = ½(T_q + T_q⁻¹)∂_x and deformed integral), and derive the S-TD q-exponential function. They test the Fibonacci recurrence relation on the energy eigenvalues, find it incompatible for q ≠ 1, and instead derive explicit n-dependent quasi-Fibonacci coefficients λ_n and ρ_n. They relate the S-TD oscillator to a special case of a five-parameter p,q-deformed oscillator family with t = ½ and p = q. They solve for the complex q values (q = e^(iθ)) that produce pairwise energy degeneracy, and construct coherent states as eigenstates of the annihilation operator, proving completeness via a weight function.

## Key numbers

- Structure function: φ_STD(n) = (n/2)(qⁿ⁻¹ + q⁻ⁿ⁺¹)
- Energy eigenvalues: E_n = ¼[(n+1)(qⁿ + q⁻ⁿ) + n(qⁿ⁻¹ + q¹⁻ⁿ)], with E_0 = ½
- For real q > 0, ΔE_n = ¼[2(qⁿ⁺¹ + q⁻ⁿ⁻¹) + n(q - q⁻¹)(qⁿ - q⁻ⁿ)] > 0, so no degeneracy
- For complex q = e^(iθ): E₁ = E₀ holds at θ = ±π/2 (q = ±i)
- E₂ = E₁ holds at θ = ±arcsin(√3/3)
- E₃ = E₂ holds at θ = ±arcsin(√2/4) and also at θ = ±π/2
- E₄ = E₃ holds at θ = ±arcsin(√((17 ± √209)/40))
- E₂ = E₀ holds at θ = -π or θ = arccos(2/3)
- E₃ = E₁ holds at θ = -π or θ = arccos((5 ± √89)/16)
- In the limit q → 1, quasi-Fibonacci coefficients become λ_n → 2 and ρ_n → -1

## Context

This work extends the Tamm-Dancoff (TD) q-deformed oscillator model previously studied by Odaka, Chaturvedi, and Gavrilik & Rebesh (2007), which possessed accidental pairwise degeneracy but only for real q. The S-TD model's (q ↔ q⁻¹) symmetry enables complex q values, analogous to how the Biedenharn-Macfarlane (BM) oscillator (Biedenharn, 1989; Macfarlane, 1989) admits complex deformation parameters while the Arik-Coon model (1976) does not. The quasi-Fibonacci classification follows the framework of Gavrilik, Kachurik, & Rebesh (2010). The work enables further study of S-TD q-deformed Bose gas thermostatistics and potential physical applications where complex q appears, such as linking q = e^(iθ) to the Cabibbo angle in quark mixing (Gavrilik & NP), or to the cosmological constant in 2+1 quantum gravity (Nelson & Regge, 1997).
