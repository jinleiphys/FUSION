# Searches for New Physics with the ALICE Experiment in pp Collisions

> The performance of the ALICE detector in searches for new heavy stable charged particles in pp collisions is discussed in this paper. Gluino R-hadron was chosen as an example of a candidate, and cross sections and kinematic properties were obtained from PYTHIA simulations for various gluino masses ($100-500 GeV/c^2$). Detector response simulations for R-hadrons in the TPC acceptance ($|η|<1$) were performed for $100 GeV/c^2$ R-hadron mass.

# Searches for New Physics with the ALICE Experiment in pp Collisions

## Key claim
The ALICE detector can identify heavy stable charged particles such as gluino R-hadrons using its energy loss and time-of-flight capabilities, but the separation from Standard Model particles is only possible on an event-by-event basis for a subset of R-hadrons (31% are slow enough for TPC separation), while the remaining R-hadrons can only be separated statistically, which is unlikely to be feasible due to low yield.

## Method
The study used PYTHIA 6.4.10 to simulate gluino production at masses of 100, 200, 300, and 500 GeV/c², with gluinos hadronizing into R-hadron pairs. Detector response simulations were performed for charged R-hadrons with mass fixed to 100 GeV/c² within the TPC acceptance (|η|<1), and the results were compared with simulations of pions, protons, and muons having the same momentum distribution, using a magnetic field of 0.5 T.

## Key numbers
- Gluino masses simulated: 100, 200, 300, and 500 GeV/c²
- Probability for an R-hadron to be charged: approximately 50%
- Mean β of R-hadrons in TPC acceptance: approximately 0.7
- Reconstruction efficiency for ITS and TPC: 83%
- Reconstruction efficiency when TOF signal required: 65%
- Fraction of slow R-hadrons separable by TPC: 31% (with prec > 10 GeV/c and dE/dx > 100)
- For prec > 10 GeV/c and 1/β > 1.04, 99% of R-hadrons have 5σ separation from protons in TOF
- R-hadrons separable by ITS up to momenta p < 100 GeV/c
- R-hadrons reach 1/β approaching 1 at very high momenta p > 250 GeV/c

## Context
This work extends previous phenomenological studies of stable gluinos by Kraan and Mackeprang, focusing specifically on the ALICE detector's capabilities for Split Supersymmetry models as proposed by Giudice. The study enables future searches for new physics at the LHC using ALICE's particle identification systems, though the authors note that discovery may require at least one "golden event" with two back-to-back high pT tracks showing consistent high-mass signatures across ITS, TPC, and TOF detectors. Two trigger scenarios to enhance the R-hadron sample are under study.
