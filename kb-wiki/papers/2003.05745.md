# The Roper Resonance in a finite volume

> We calculate the energy levels corresponding to the Roper resonance based on a two-flavor chiral effective Lagrangian for pions, nucleons, deltas and the Roper resonance at leading one-loop order. We show that the Roper mass can be extracted from these levels for not too large lattice volumes.

## Key claim

The Roper resonance mass can be extracted from finite-volume energy levels computed using a two-flavor chiral effective Lagrangian at leading one-loop order, but only for lattice volumes that are not too large.

## Method

The authors constructed a chiral effective Lagrangian with pions, nucleons, delta baryons, and the Roper resonance as dynamical degrees of freedom, working in the SU(2) flavor and isospin limits. They computed the Roper self-energy at third chiral order (O(p³)) from one-loop diagrams with internal Roper, nucleon, and delta states plus a contact interaction, then replaced the infinite-volume loop integrals with finite-volume sums over discretized spatial momenta in a cubic box of side length L. The finite-volume energy levels were obtained by solving the pole condition for the dressed Roper propagator, using the difference between the finite-volume and infinite-volume self-energies.

## Key numbers

* The physical Roper Breit-Wigner mass is approximately m_R = 1.44 GeV (from prior work, cited as Tanabashi (2018)).
* The calculation is performed at leading one-loop order, O(p³), where the pion mass and the mass differences m_R - m_N, m_Δ - m_N, and m_R - m_Δ are all counted as order p.
* The finite-volume corrections to the scalar loop integrals involve sums over integer vectors n⃗ ∈ ℤ³ with discretized momenta k⃗ = (2π/L) n⃗, and the corrections decay exponentially for large L, typically becoming negligible for M_π L > 4.

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume treatment previously applied to the delta resonance by Bernard (2007) to the Roper resonance, using the chiral Lagrangian framework developed by Gegelia (2016). The study is motivated by lattice QCD calculations such as Lang (2016), which found no low-lying Roper eigenstate from Nπ elastic scattering alone, suggesting that coupling to Nππ channels is important. The present approach enables extraction of the Roper mass from lattice energy levels via avoided level crossing, providing a complementary method to direct lattice simulations that avoids the challenge of fully simulating three-particle decays.
