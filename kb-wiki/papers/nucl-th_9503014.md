# Measuring hadron properties at finite temperature

> We estimate the numbers and mass spectra of observed lepton and kaon pairs produced from $ϕ$ meson decays in the central rapidity region of an Au+Au collision at lab energy 11.6 GeV/nucleon. The following effects are considered: possible mass shifts, thermal broadening due to collisions with hadronic resonances, and superheating of the resonance gas. Changes in the dilepton mass spectrum may be seen, but changes in the dikaon spectrum are too small to be detectable.

# Measuring hadron properties at finite temperature

## Key claim

Changes in the dilepton mass spectrum from phi meson decays in Au+Au collisions at 11.6 GeV/nucleon may be detectable, but changes in the dikaon spectrum are too small to be observable, despite the larger total dikaon signal, because most dikaons come from phi mesons that decay after freezeout of the hot matter.

## Method

The authors model the hot matter produced in central Au+Au collisions using a resonance gas equation of state with an excluded volume, assuming the matter remains in the hadronic phase with strong superheating and never transitions to a quark-gluon plasma. They compute the evolution of the hot matter using boost-invariant hydrodynamics, then convolute the four-volume per unit rapidity with the phi meson density, a Breit-Wigner mass distribution with temperature-dependent mass and width, and the decay rates to dileptons or kaons, including a kaon escape probability for the dikaon channel.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 11.6 GeV/nucleon (lab frame), with gamma = 2.7 for fixed target collisions
- Nuclear radius for Au: r_N = 7 fm; nuclear length in cm frame: l_N = 2.6 fm
- Observed baryon rapidity density at y=0: dN_B/dy = 120; observed pion rapidity density: dN_(pi+ + pi-)/dy = 120
- Estimated entropy rapidity density: dS/dy ~ 1700 for freezeout temperature T_f = 100-120 MeV
- Maximum temperature reached in the hot matter: 169 MeV, with superheating of 20-30 MeV above T_c
- Bag constant B^(1/4) = 217 MeV (for R_exc = 0.5 fm) and 220 MeV (for R_exc = 1 fm) for strangeness equilibrium case
- Haglin-Gale mass parametrization: m_phi(T) = 1020 + 4*(T/200 MeV)^4 MeV
- Asakawa-Ko mass parametrization: m_phi(T) = 1020 - 200*(T/200 MeV)^4 MeV
- Width parametrization: Gamma(T) = 4.43 + 25*(T/200 MeV)^2 MeV
- Vacuum kaon mass: m_K = 495 MeV
- Kaon interaction time parametrization: tau_K = 3*(150 MeV/T)^4 fm/c
- Difference in dilepton signal between mass parametrizations: over 10% near the phi peak
- Dikaon spectra differ significantly only when signal drops below 1% of peak value

## Context

This work extends calculations of finite-temperature phi meson properties by Haglin and Gale (1994) and Asakawa and Ko (1994), who predicted very different mass shifts (small positive versus large negative), and by Ko and Seibert (1994) and Haglin (1995) on the thermal width. The paper responds to preliminary experimental results from the E859 Collaboration at the AGS showing no observable change in the phi peak from kaon pairs in Si+Au collisions. The authors argue that dilepton measurements remain a promising probe of in-medium meson properties, in contrast to dikaon measurements, and that such studies could provide insight into the equation of state of hot strongly-interacting matter.
