# The isoscalar monopole strength of 13C

> To identify the 3alpha BEC state with the excess neutron, we have investigated the monopole strength of the excited states of 13C by using the theoretical framework of the real-time evolution method. The calculations have revealed several candidates of the Hoyle-analog states in a highly excited region.

## Key claim

The real-time evolution method (REM) calculation identifies three excited \(1/2^-\) states of \(^{13}\mathrm{C}\) as candidates for the Hoyle-analog state (a \(3\alpha\) Bose-Einstein condensate with an excess neutron), but the calculated excitation energies overestimate the observed values, indicating a need for improvement in the Hamiltonian and model wave functions.

## Method

The authors used the real-time evolution method (REM) with a Brink-Bloch wave function consisting of three \(\alpha\) clusters in \((0s)^4\) configuration coupled to an excess neutron, using the Volkov No. 2 force and G3RS spin-orbit force. They solved the equation of motion for the cluster centroids, then applied the generator coordinate method (GCM) with parity and angular momentum projection to obtain the wave functions of \(^{13}\mathrm{C}\) states. From these wave functions, they calculated the electric (E0) and isoscalar (IS0) monopole transition matrix elements between the ground state and excited \(1/2^-\) states.

## Key numbers

- The three excited \(1/2^-\) states have calculated excitation energies of 14.1, 14.6, and 15.1 MeV, compared to experimental values of 8.86, 11.08, and 12.50 MeV.
- The calculated isoscalar monopole transition matrix elements \(M_{IS0}\) for the three excited states are 6.0, 3.7, and 12.5 \(\mathrm{fm}^2\), respectively; experimental values are 6.1, 4.2, and 4.9 \(\mathrm{fm}^2\).
- The calculated electric monopole transition matrix elements \(M_{E0}\) are 2.2, 1.1, and 5.6 \(e\mathrm{fm}^2\), compared to the Weisskopf estimate of 5.9 \(e\mathrm{fm}^2\).
- The matter radii of the three excited states are 3.14, 3.03, and 3.00 fm, while the ground state matter radius is 2.44 fm; the Hoyle state matter radius calculated with the same Hamiltonian is 3.7 fm.
- The proton radii of the excited states (2.57, 2.61, 2.63 fm) are smaller than the Hoyle state proton radius of 3.7 fm.

## Context

This work extends the study of \(\alpha\)-particle condensates in \(^{12}\mathrm{C}\) (the Hoyle state and its rotational band studied by Freer (2009, 2011) and Itoh (2011)) to the neutron-rich system \(^{13}\mathrm{C}\). It builds on previous theoretical studies by Furutachi (2011), Yamada (2015), Ebran (2017), and Chiba (2020) that have investigated how an excess neutron disturbs the \(3\alpha\) BEC, but where the conclusion remained controversial. The results enable future investigation of spectroscopic factors and occupation probabilities to precisely identify the BEC state, with calculations ongoing.
