# The ideal relativistic spinning gas: polarization and spectra

> We study the physics of the ideal relativistic rotating gas at thermodynamical equilibrium and provide analytical expressions of the momentum spectra and polarization vector for the case of massive particles with spin 1/2 and 1. We show that the finite angular momentum J entails an anisotropy in momentum spectra, with particles emitted orthogonally to J having, on average, a larger momentum than along its direction. Unlike in the non-relativistic case, the proper polarization vector turns out not to be aligned with the total angular momentum with a non-trivial momentum dependence.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for a relativistic ideal gas in thermodynamic equilibrium with fixed, large angular momentum **J**, the proper polarization vector of massive spin-1/2 and spin-1 particles is *not* aligned with the total angular momentum and acquires a non-trivial momentum dependence, unlike in the non-relativistic case; this result holds under the conditions of Boltzmann statistics and for angular velocities satisfying ω/T ≪ 1.

## Method

The authors start from the microcanonical and grand-canonical partition functions for an ideal relativistic quantum gas with fixed angular momentum, previously derived by one of them (Becattini, 2005). They perform a saddle-point expansion of the integral representation of the partition function, showing equivalence to a rigidly rotating system described by a rotational grand-canonical ensemble with angular velocity ω. From this ensemble, they derive analytical expressions for inclusive momentum spectra and polarization vectors for massive particles with spin 1/2 and spin 1, working in the Boltzmann limit and assuming ω/T ≪ 1 so that the exponential factor exp[ω·(x×p)/T] can be expanded to first order.

## Key numbers

- The condition for the validity of the linear approximation in ω is ωR ≪ 1, where R is the maximal distance from the rotation axis; this leads to different requirements for non-relativistic (p = O(√(mT)) with m ≫ T) and ultrarelativistic (p = O(T)) gases.
- The relation between total angular momentum J and angular velocity ω for small ω is given by: J_i = Σ_j ⟨n_j⟩ [ (⟨p_j²⟩/(3T)) ∫_V d³x (r²δ_{ik} − x_i x_k) + V S_j(S_j+1)/(3T) δ_{ik} ] ω_k, where ⟨n_j⟩ is the mean density and ⟨p_j²⟩ the mean squared momentum of species j.
- The Fourier coefficients v_{2k} of the azimuthal angle distribution of the momentum spectrum are given by an expression involving modified Bessel functions I_{2k}(p_T z ω/T) and I_0(p_T z ω/T); at low p_T, v_{2k} ∝ p_T^{2k}, while at large p_T, all v_{2k} → 1.

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamics of rotating systems from astrophysics (compact stars) and nuclear multifragmentation (Moretto, 1993; Gross, 1997) to the fully relativistic case including spin degrees of freedom, which had been missing. It builds on the calculation of the microcanonical partition function with angular momentum conservation by Becattini (2005) and on Hagedorn's (1971) earlier observation that large intrinsic angular momentum induces anisotropy in particle spectra. The results provide analytical tools for interpreting polarization and momentum anisotropies in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where formation of a system with large intrinsic angular momentum has been envisaged (Satz, 2000).
