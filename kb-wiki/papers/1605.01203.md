# Kaon-Nucleon systems and their interactions in the Skyrme model

> We study kaon-nucleon systems in the Skyrme model in a method based on the bound state approach of Callan-Klebanov but with the kaon around the physical nucleon of the rotating hedgehog. This corresponds to the variation after projection, reversing the order of semiclassical quantization of $1/N_c$ expansion. The method, however, is considered to be suited to the study of weakly interacting kaon-nucleon systems including loosely $\bar{K}N$ bound states such as $Λ(1405)$. We have found a bound state with binding energy of order ten MeV, consistent with the observed state. We also discuss the $\bar{K}N$ interaction and find that it consists of an attraction in the middle range and a repulsion in the short range.

## Key claim

The authors find a bound state in the \(\bar{K}N\) system with a binding energy of order ten MeV, consistent with the observed \(\Lambda(1405)\), but only when using the experimental value of the pion decay constant; the result is parameter-dependent and the method does not strictly obey the \(1/N_c\) expansion, being justified only for weakly bound systems where the kaon motion is slower than the hedgehog rotation.

## Method

The authors construct a new ansatz in the Skyrme model where the hedgehog soliton is first quantized by SU(2) rotation to generate the physical nucleon, and then kaon fluctuations are introduced around this rotated nucleon, reversing the order of the Callan-Klebanov bound state approach. They derive the equation of motion for the kaon radial wave function from the Skyrme Lagrangian expanded to second order in the kaon field, obtaining a Klein-Gordon-like equation with a potential containing central and spin-orbit terms with and without isospin dependence. The bound state energies and wave functions are computed numerically for three parameter sets of the pion decay constant \(F_\pi\) and the Skyrme parameter \(e\).

## Key numbers

* For parameter set 1 (\(F_\pi = 129\) MeV, \(e = 5.45\)): binding energy 82.9 MeV for \(I=0\) \(\bar{K}N\) bound state, with kaon root mean square radius \(\langle r_K^2 \rangle^{1/2} = 0.99\) fm and nucleon radius \(\langle r_N^2 \rangle^{1/2} = 0.59\) fm.
* For parameter set 2 (\(F_\pi = 186\) MeV, \(e = 5.45\)): binding energy 27.2 MeV for \(I=0\) \(\bar{K}N\) bound state, with \(\langle r_K^2 \rangle^{1/2} = 1.19\) fm and \(\langle r_N^2 \rangle^{1/2} = 0.41\) fm.
* For parameter set 3 (\(F_\pi = 186\) MeV, \(e = 4.82\)): binding energy 32.9 MeV for \(I=0\) \(\bar{K}N\) bound state, with \(\langle r_K^2 \rangle^{1/2} = 1.18\) fm and \(\langle r_N^2 \rangle^{1/2} = 0.46\) fm.
* The ratio of volume integrals of the potential between set 1 and set 2 is approximately 2.5, compatible with the squared ratio of pion decay constants \((186/129)^2 \sim 2.1\).
* In the Callan-Klebanov approach for parameter set 3, the s-wave (\(l=0\)) bound state has binding energy 61.7 MeV with \(\langle r_K^2 \rangle^{1/2} = 0.93\) fm, and the p-wave (\(l=1\)) bound state has binding energy 326.6 MeV with \(\langle r_K^2 \rangle^{1/2} = 0.54\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the bound state approach of Callan and Klebanov (1985) for hyperons in the Skyrme model by reversing the order of semiclassical quantization, making it suited to weakly bound \(\bar{K}N\) systems such as the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance. The method contrasts with phenomenological potentials by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002) and with chiral unitary approaches based on the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction, which require regularization parameters; the Skyrme model approach is parameter-free aside from inputs fixed by nucleon properties. The results enable future studies of kaon-nucleon scattering states and coupled-channel effects with \(\pi\Sigma\), as well as applications to kaonic nuclear matter at high density.
