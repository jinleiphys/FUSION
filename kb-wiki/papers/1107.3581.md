# Global systematics of octupole excitations in even-even nuclei

> We present a computational methodology for a theory of the lowest octupole excitations applicable to all even-even nuclei beyond the lightest. The theory is the well-known generator-coordinate extension (GCM) of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov self-consistent mean field theory (HFB). We use the discrete-basis Hill-Wheel method (HW) to compute the wave functions with an interaction from the Gogny family of Hamiltonians. Comparing to the compiled experimental data on octupole excitations, we find that the performance of the theory depends on the deformation characteristics of the nucleus. For nondeformed nuclei, the theory reproduces the energies to about 20 % apart from an overall scale factor of about 1.6. The performance is somewhat poorer for (quadrupole) deformed nuclei, and for both together the dispersion of the scaled energies about the experimental values is about 25 %. This compares favorably with the performance of similar theories of the quadrupole excitations. Nuclei having static octupole deformations in HFB form a special category. These nuclei have the smallest measured octupole excitation energies as well as the smallest predicted energies. However, in these cases the energies are seriously underpredicted by the theory. We find that a simple two-configuration approximation, the Minimization After Projection method, (MAP) is almost as accurate as the full HW treatment, provided that the octupole-deformed nuclei are omitted from the comparison. This article is accompanied by a tabulation of the predicted octupole excitations for 818 nuclei extending from dripline to dripline, computed with several variants of the Gogny interaction.

## Key claim

The HFB/GCM/HW methodology reproduces experimental octupole excitation energies to about 20% for nondeformed nuclei after applying an overall scale factor of approximately 1.6, but the performance is poorer for quadrupole-deformed nuclei and seriously underpredicts energies for nuclei with static octupole deformations in HFB.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny D1S interaction, then applied the discrete-basis Hill-Wheeler (HW) method as a generator-coordinate extension (GCM) to compute correlated wave functions for the lowest octupole excitations. The generator coordinate was the mass octupole moment, and the basis consisted of configurations with octupole deformation β₃ ranging from -0.5 to +0.5 (extended to ±1.2 for light nuclei). They also tested a simpler two-configuration approximation called Minimization After Projection (MAP).

## Key numbers

- For nondeformed nuclei (spherical and soft), the theory reproduces experimental energies to about ±20% after applying an overall scale factor of approximately 1.6.
- For all nuclei together (excluding those with static octupole deformation), the dispersion of scaled energies about experimental values is about ±25%.
- The average logarithmic ratio R̄_E = ⟨log(E(th)/E(exp))⟩ is 0.45 for all 284 nuclei, corresponding to a systematic overprediction by a factor of e^0.45 ≈ 1.6.
- For the subset of 196 spherical nuclei (β₃=0, nondeformed), the dispersion σ_E is 0.19, corresponding to errors in the ratio of theory to experiment of approximately -30% to +50%.
- For 59 deformed nuclei (β₃=0, R₄₂>2.9), the dispersion σ_E is 0.32, with average R̄_E = 0.62.
- For 7 nuclei with static octupole deformation in HFB, the theory seriously underpredicts excitation energies; for ²²⁶Ra, the HW excitation energy is 0.16 MeV compared to the experimental 0.32 MeV.
- The study includes 818 nuclei from Z=8 to Z=110, with about 6% predicted to have static octupole deformation in HFB.
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, the HW excitation energy is 4.0 MeV (experimental: 2.62 MeV); for ¹⁵⁸Gd, the HW value is 1.93 MeV (experimental K=0 bandhead: 1.263 MeV); for ²⁰Ne, the HW value is 6.7 MeV (experimental: 5.6 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the global systematic approach previously applied to quadrupole excitations by Sarriguren et al. (2007) and Delaroche et al. (2010) to the octupole degree of freedom, using the same Gogny D1S interaction and HFB/GCM methodology. It is the first global study to encompass magic, semimagic, quadrupole-deformed, and octupole-deformed nuclei within a single theoretical framework, following earlier case-by-case studies of octupole excitations using GCM/HW by M¨uller et al. (1983) and using QRPA by Terasaki et al. (2002), Col`o et al. (2003), and Ansari et al. (2009). The results enable systematic assessment of the Gogny interaction's performance for octupole modes and provide a benchmark for future theories incorporating angular momentum projection and two-dimensional treatments of octupole and quadrupole degrees of freedom.
