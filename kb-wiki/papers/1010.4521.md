# Extraction of the Compton Form Factor H from DVCS Measurements in the Quark Sector

> Working at twist 2 accuracy and assuming the dominance of the Generalized Parton Distribution H we study the helicity-dependent and independent cross sections measured in Hall A, the beam spin asymmetries measured in Hall B at Jefferson Laboratory and beam charge, beam spin and target spin asymmetries measured by Hermes. We extract the real and imaginary parts of the Compton Form Factor H, the latter being obtained with a 20--50% uncertainty. We pay extra attention to the estimation of systematic errors on the extraction of H. We discuss our results and compare to other extractions as well as to the popular VGG model.

## Key claim

Working at twist 2 accuracy and assuming the dominance of the Generalized Parton Distribution \(H\), the real and imaginary parts of the Compton Form Factor \(\mathcal{H}\) are extracted from DVCS data, with the imaginary part obtained at 20–50% uncertainty; the paper explicitly notes that the interpretation of JLab Hall A measurements is difficult and likely requires going beyond the \(H\)-dominance approximation.

## Method

The analysis uses the harmonic formalism of Belitsky, Müller and Kirchner (2002) for the \(ep \rightarrow ep\gamma\) cross section, updated with the expressions of Guichon and Vanderhaeghen (2008) for a proton target. Two fitting strategies are applied: local fits, where \(Re \mathcal{H}\) and \(Im \mathcal{H}\) are free parameters per kinematic bin, and a global fit using a dual-model parametrization expanded in Gegenbauer and Legendre polynomials. Systematic uncertainties from the \(H\)-dominance hypothesis are estimated by comparing fits with subdominant GPDs set to zero versus set to their VGG model values, and by varying the number of polynomials in the global fit.

## Key numbers

- Imaginary part of \(\mathcal{H}\) extracted with a relative accuracy of 20–50% (stated in abstract and text).
- Real part of \(\mathcal{H}\) never extracted with a precision better than 50% (stated in text).
- Data restricted to kinematic configurations with \(|t|/Q^2 < 1/2\) (stated in text).
- Hall B kinematics cover \(1 < Q^2 < 4~\text{GeV}^2\) and four \(t\) bins: \(0.09 < -t < 0.2~\text{GeV}^2\), \(0.2 < -t < 0.4~\text{GeV}^2\), \(0.4 < -t < 0.6~\text{GeV}^2\), and \(0.6 < -t < 1.0~\text{GeV}^2\) (from figure caption).
- Hall A kinematics at \(x_B = 0.36\) and \(Q^2 = 2.3~\text{GeV}^2\) with \(0.1 < -t < 0.4~\text{GeV}^2\) (from figure caption).

## Context

This work extends local fitting methods of Guidal (2008, 2010) and global fits of Kumericki and Muller (2009) by applying both approaches to the same JLab Hall A and Hall B data, and to Hermes asymmetries. The results show good agreement among three different groups for Hall B kinematics, but reveal discrepancies for Hall A data that suggest contributions from GPDs other than \(H\) (such as \(\tilde{H}\) and \(\tilde{E}\)) are needed. The extraction enables future inclusion of additional beam spin asymmetry measurements on unpolarized and longitudinally polarized proton targets to reduce systematic uncertainties and clarify the interpretation of Hall A helicity-independent cross sections.
