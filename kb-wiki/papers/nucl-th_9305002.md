# Quark-based Description of Nuclear Matter with Simulated Annealing

> We calculate ground-state properties of a many-quark system in the string-flip model using variational Monte Carlo methods. The many-body potential energy of the system is determined by finding the optimal grouping of quarks into hadrons. This (optimal) assignment problem is solved by using the stochastic optimization technique of simulated annealing. Results are presented for the energy and length-scale for confinement as a function of density. These results show how quarks clustering decreases with density and characterize the nuclear- to quark-matter transition. We compare our results to a previously published work with a similar model which uses, instead, a pairing approach to the optimization problem.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a three-quark-cluster (TQC) string-flip model using simulated annealing to solve the optimal grouping problem yields a smooth, continuous transition between hadronic and quark matter phases, in contrast to the step-like discontinuous transition found in the multi-quark-cluster (MQC) model using a pairing algorithm, though both models produce roughly the same transition density.

## Method

The authors model nuclear matter using N=3A quarks confined by harmonic strings into colorless triplets, with the many-body potential energy minimized by finding the optimal grouping of quarks into hadrons via simulated annealing (TQC model). A variational wavefunction with a single parameter lambda (inverse square of confinement length-scale) is used, and expectation values are computed using Metropolis Monte Carlo. Results are compared to a multi-quark-cluster (MQC) model that uses an efficient pairing algorithm (cost proportional to N^3) to independently pair quarks of two colors at a time, allowing clusters of any multiple of three quarks.

## Key numbers

- Transition between nuclear-matter and quark-matter phases occurs near density rho = 0.12 (in units of oscillator length (Mk)^(-1/4))
- For 10 hadrons (30 quarks), there are 10^13 possible groupings, making exhaustive search prohibitive
- The simulated annealing algorithm achieves a 95 percent confidence level for finding the global minimum, determined from statistics on 18 randomly distributed quarks
- The TQC model with N=24 quarks shows roughly the same finite-size effect as the MQC model with N=96 quarks
- At low density, the variational parameter lambda approaches the isolated hadron value of 1/sqrt(3); at high density, lambda tends to zero

## Context

This work extends the string-flip model previously developed by Horowitz and Piekarewicz (1992) which used a pairing algorithm (MQC model) rather than simulated annealing. The TQC model enforces strict three-quark clustering, while the MQC model allows multi-quark clusters. The immediate followup is to use the determined variational parameter lambda to calculate additional ground-state observables like the quark-quark correlation function, with longer-range goals including adding spin-dependent interactions and light-quark flavor degeneracy to study nuclear matter saturation from quark degrees of freedom.
