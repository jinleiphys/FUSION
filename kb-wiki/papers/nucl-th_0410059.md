# Weak Decay of Hypernuclei

> The focus of these Lectures is on the weak decay modes of hypernuclei, with special attention to Lambda-hypernuclei. The subject involves many fields of modern theoretical and experimental physics, from nuclear structure to the fundamental constituents of matter and their interactions. The various weak decay modes of Lambda-hypernuclei are described: the mesonic mode and the non-mesonic ones. The latter are the dominant decay channels of medium--heavy hypernuclei, where, on the contrary, the mesonic decay is disfavoured by Pauli blocking effect on the outgoing nucleon. In particular, one can distinguish between one-body and two-body induced decays. Theoretical models employed to evaluate the (partial and total) decay widths of hypernuclei are illustrated, and their results compared with existing experimental data. Open problems and recent achievements are extensively discussed, in particular the determination of the ratio Gamma_n/Gamma_p, possible tests of the Delta I=1/2 rule in non-mesonic decays and the puzzling asymmetric non-mesonic decay of polarized hypernuclei.

## Key claim

These lectures review the weak decay modes of Lambda-hypernuclei, emphasizing that while the total mesonic and non-mesonic decay rates are well explained by several calculations, the longstanding disagreement between theoretical estimates and experimental determinations of the ratio Gamma_n/Gamma_p remains a major open problem, along with the puzzling asymmetric non-mesonic decay of polarized hypernuclei.

## Method

The authors present two main theoretical approaches for calculating hypernuclear decay widths: the Wave Function Method (WFM), which uses shell model nuclear and hypernuclear wave functions with pion wave functions generated by pion-nucleus optical potentials; and the Polarization Propagator Method (PPM), which evaluates the Lambda self-energy in nuclear matter within the Random Phase Approximation and extends to finite nuclei via the Local Density Approximation. The non-mesonic decay is modeled through one-meson-exchange (OME) mechanisms involving pseudoscalar (pi, eta, K) and vector (rho, omega, K*) mesons, with the weak coupling constants for heavier mesons evaluated theoretically and subject to model dependence.

## Key numbers

* Free Lambda lifetime: tau_free = 2.632 x 10^-10 sec
* Free Lambda mesonic decay branching ratios: pi- p (63.9 x 10^-2), pi0 n (35.8 x 10^-2)
* Ratio of free Lambda decay widths: Gamma_free(pi- p)/Gamma_free(pi0 n) = 1.78 (experimental), close to 2 predicted by Delta I=1/2 rule
* Ratio of Delta I=1/2 to Delta I=3/2 transition amplitudes: |A_1/2/A_3/2| = 30
* Q-value for free Lambda mesonic decay at rest: Q_Lambda = 40 MeV; final nucleon momentum p = 100 MeV
* Q-value for non-mesonic decay: Q = m_Lambda - m_N = 176 MeV; final nucleon momentum p_N = 420 MeV for one-nucleon induced channels, p_N = 340 MeV for two-nucleon induced channels
* For A <= 11, Gamma_NM/Gamma_free = 0.1A
* In OPE approximation with Delta I=1/2 rule, theoretical Gamma_n/Gamma_p ratios range from 0.05 to 0.20
* Experimental Gamma_n/Gamma_p values range from 0.5 to 2
* Hypernuclear lifetime: tau_Lambda = (0.5 to 1) x tau_free
* Weak coupling constants from free Lambda decay: G = 2.211 x 10^-7/m_pi^2, A = 1.06, B = -7.10

## Context

This review extends the work on hypernuclear weak decay summarized in Alberico (2002) and complements the lectures by Gal, Nagae, and Outa presented at the same Varenna school. The paper discusses the Delta I=1/2 rule, which is also valid for Sigma hyperon decay and pionic kaon decays, noting that Standard Model calculations with perturbative QCD corrections give too small |A_1/2/A_3/2| ratios (about 3 to 4) compared to the observed value of 30, suggesting non-perturbative QCD effects are responsible. The resolution of the Gamma_n/Gamma_p puzzle and the asymmetric decay of polarized hypernuclei require improved experiments to guide deeper theoretical understanding of hypernuclear dynamics and decay mechanisms.
