# Effects of Inelastic Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering on Supernova Dynamics and Radiated Neutrino Spectra

> Based on the shell model for Gamow-Teller and the Random Phase Approximation for forbidden transitions, we have calculated reaction rates for inelastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (INNS) under supernova (SN) conditions, assuming a matter composition given by Nuclear Statistical Equilibrium. The rates have been incorporated into state-of-the-art stellar core-collapse simulations with detailed energy-dependent neutrino transport. While no significant effect on the SN dynamics is observed, INNS increases the neutrino opacities noticeably and strongly reduces the high-energy tail of the neutrino spectrum emitted in the neutrino burst at shock breakout. Relatedly the expected event rates for the observation of such neutrinos by earthbound detectors are reduced by up to about 60%.

## Key claim

Inelastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (INNS) has no significant effect on supernova dynamics or shock propagation, but it strongly reduces the high-energy tail of the neutrino spectrum emitted during the shock breakout burst, which in turn reduces expected event rates in earthbound detectors by up to about 60%.

## Method

The authors calculated INNS reaction rates using the shell model for Gamow-Teller transitions and the Random Phase Approximation for forbidden transitions, assuming a matter composition given by Nuclear Statistical Equilibrium. These rates were incorporated into the neutrino-hydrodynamics code Vertex, which performs spherically symmetric core-collapse simulations with detailed energy-dependent neutrino transport. The simulations used three different nuclear equations of state (Lattimer and Swesty, Shen et al., and Hillebrandt and Wolff) for a 15 solar mass progenitor star.

## Key numbers

- Total composition-averaged INNS cross sections at T=1 MeV, rho=6e11 g/cm^3, Ye=0.397: 0.48e-42 cm^2 for E=10 MeV, 38e-42 cm^2 for E=30 MeV, 243e-42 cm^2 for E=50 MeV
- Total composition-averaged INNS cross sections at T=2 MeV, rho=2.5e12 g/cm^3, Ye=0.275: 0.97e-42 cm^2 for E=10 MeV, 41e-42 cm^2 for E=30 MeV, 267e-42 cm^2 for E=50 MeV
- INNS increases the total energy transfer rate in non-conservative scatterings ahead of the shock by typically a factor of 2-3 during the first less than 50 ms after core bounce
- Mean spectral energy of the electron neutrino burst is reduced by only 0.4-0.5 MeV
- Reduction in detection cross sections for the electron neutrino burst spectrum: 3% for electrons, 8% for deuterons, 37% for carbon-12, 58% for oxygen-16, 11% for argon-40, 17% for iron-56 and lead-208

## Context

This work extends the exploratory study of Bruenn and Haxton (1991), which approximated supernova matter composition by a single representative nucleus (iron-56) and used temperature-zero nuclear models. The present study uses a full Nuclear Statistical Equilibrium composition with temperature-dependent cross sections, following the approach of Sampaio et al. (2002) who demonstrated that finite-temperature effects drastically increase INNS cross sections at low neutrino energies. The results enable more accurate predictions for neutrino detection rates in experiments such as Super-Kamiokande, SNO, Borexino, MiniBooNE, ICARUS, Minos, and OMNIS.
