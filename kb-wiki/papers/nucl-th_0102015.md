# Core excitation in Coulomb breakup reactions

> Within the pure Coulomb breakup mechanism, we investigate the one-neutron removal reaction of the type A(a,b$γ$)X with $^{11}$Be and $^{19}$C projectiles on a heavy target nucleus $^{208}$Pb at the beam energy of 60 MeV/nucleon. Our intention is to examine the prospective of using these reactions to study the structure of neutron rich nuclei. Integrated partial cross sections and momentum distributions for the ground as well as excited bound states of core nuclei are calculated within the finite range distorted wave Born approximation as well as within the adiabatic model of the Coulomb breakup. Our results are compared with those obtained in the studies of the reactions on a light target where the breakup proceeds via the pure nuclear mechanism. We find that the transitions to excited states of the core are quite weak in the Coulomb dominated process as compared to the pure nuclear breakup.

# Core excitation in Coulomb breakup reactions

## Key claim

The authors find that transitions to excited states of the core are quite weak in Coulomb-dominated breakup reactions compared to pure nuclear breakup, with the sum of partial cross sections to all excited states being less than 1% of that to the ground state for the $^{11}$Be case, but they caution that their results should be viewed as complementing nuclear breakup contributions, not replacing them, and that in any complete theory both contributions must be considered on an equal footing.

## Method

The authors calculate integrated partial cross sections and longitudinal momentum distributions for ground and excited bound states of $^{10}$Be and $^{18}$C cores using both the finite-range distorted wave Born approximation (FRDWBA) and the adiabatic model of pure Coulomb breakup, for one-neutron removal reactions of $^{11}$Be and $^{19}$C projectiles on a $^{208}$Pb target at 60 MeV/nucleon. The neutron single-particle wave functions are calculated in a central Woods-Saxon well with radius 1.15 fm and diffuseness 0.50 fm, with the depth adjusted to reproduce the corresponding separation energy. Results are compared with previous studies on light $^9$Be targets where breakup proceeds via pure nuclear mechanisms.

## Key numbers

- For $^{11}$Be on $^{208}$Pb at 60 MeV/nucleon: ground state (0$^+$) partial cross section $\sigma_C^{FRDWBA} = 1536.48$ mb (with $C^2S = 0.74$); excited 2$^+$ state at 3.368 MeV: 2.09 mb; excited 1$^-$ state at 5.956 MeV: 2.45 mb; excited 2$^-$ state at 6.256 MeV: 2.07 mb; sum of excited states: 6.69 mb (less than 1% of ground state)
- For $^{19}$C on $^{208}$Pb at 60 MeV/nucleon: ground state (0$^+$) partial cross section $\sigma_C = 993.2$ mb (with $C^2S = 0.58$); excited 2$^+$ state at 1.6 MeV: 8.80 mb; excited 0$^+$ state at 4.0 MeV: 13.38 mb; excited 2$^+$,3$^+$ state at 4.9 MeV: 1.08 mb; sum of excited states: 23.26 mb (about 2% of ground state)
- Full width at half maximum of the calculated longitudinal momentum distribution for the ground state of $^{10}$Be: 44 MeV/c, consistent with the experimental value of (47.5 $\pm$ 6) MeV/c from measurements on a $^9$Be target (Aumann 2000)
- For the $^{197}$Au($^{14}$B,$^{13}$B(g.s))X reaction at 60 MeV/nucleon: calculated pure Coulomb partial cross section = 401 mb; experimental value = 638 $\pm$ 45 mb (Guimaraes 2000), suggesting nuclear and Coulomb-nuclear interference terms could contribute up to 40-50%
- One-neutron separation energy for $^{11}$Be ground state: $S_n = 0.504$ MeV; for $^{19}$C ground state: $S_n = 0.530$ MeV
- Root mean square radius of $^{11}$Be ground state: 2.91 fm, for assumed $^{10}$Be core rms radius of 2.28 fm

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of Coulomb breakup reactions to include core excitation, building on the FRDWBA formalism developed by Chatterjee (2000) and the adiabatic model of Tostevin (1998). The results contrast with measurements on light $^9$Be targets by Aumann (2000) and Navin (1998, 2000), where excited states of $^{10}$Be represented about 22% of the total partial cross section in nuclear breakup. The findings are supported by the experiment of Guimaraes (2000) on $^{197}$Au($^{14}$B,$^{13}$B$\gamma$)X, where no core-excited transitions were seen. The work enables future investigations of non-adiabatic corrections to the adiabatic model for excited states, and highlights the need to extend the FRDWBA theory to include nuclear breakup effects for a complete description of these reactions.
