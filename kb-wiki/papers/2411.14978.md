# Hyperons in neutron star mergers

> We discuss the effects induced by the potential presence of hyperons in hot and ultra-dense matter within the context of neutron star mergers. Specifically, we address their effect on the dominant post-merger frequency of the gravitational waves. By performing a simulation campaign with a large sample of hyperonic and nucleonic equations of state, we explicitly show that the unique thermal behavior of hyperonic equations of state results in a systematic shift of the dominant frequency with respect to the nucleonic reference level. The predicted shift has values of up to 150 Hz, and it could be detected with the newest generations of gravitational wave detectors. Thus this approach opens a new path for signaling the presence of hyperons in neutron star remnant matter.

# Hyperons in neutron star mergers

## Key claim
The presence of hyperons in hot post-merger matter produces a systematic upward shift of the dominant gravitational-wave frequency relative to purely nucleonic matter, reaching up to 150 Hz, but this shift is not unique to hyperons, other exotic matter such as quark matter could produce a similar signature, and detection requires both accurate gravitational-wave measurements and a well-constrained zero-temperature equation of state.

## Method
The authors performed numerical simulations of 1.4-1.4 solar mass binary neutron star mergers using a large set of temperature-dependent hyperonic and nucleonic equations of state. For each equation of state, they computed the dominant post-merger gravitational-wave frequency from the full temperature-dependent simulation and compared it to a reference simulation where the zero-temperature slice was supplemented with an ideal-gas thermal treatment using a constant thermal index of 1.75, which mimics purely nucleonic thermal behavior. The difference between these two frequencies defines the frequency shift.

## Key numbers
- Frequency shift of up to 150 Hz for hyperonic models relative to the nucleonic reference level
- Reference thermal index value of 1.75 used to mimic purely nucleonic thermal behavior
- Binary merger simulations used a 1.4-1.4 solar mass system
- Time window of 5 ms starting 2.5 ms after merging used for averaging the thermal index in the remnant

## Context
This work extends studies of hyperonic effects on cold neutron star equations of state (Tolos 2020, Burgio 2021, Sedrakian 2022) and finite-temperature hyperonic matter (Oertel 2016, Schaffner-Bielich 2020, Motta 2022) by connecting them to gravitational-wave observables. The approach addresses the "masquerade problem" identified by Alford (2005) for hybrid stars, which also applies to hyperonic matter: cold observables like mass-radius relations may not distinguish hyperonic from nucleonic composition. The method enables a new observational channel for detecting hyperons using next-generation gravitational-wave detectors, though the authors note that quark matter (Blacker 2023) could produce similar frequency shifts, requiring complementary constraints.
