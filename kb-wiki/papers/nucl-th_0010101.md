# Chaos and isospin symmetry breaking in rotational nuclei

> For nuclei with N = Z, the isospin degree of freedom is important and, for deformed systems, rotational bands of different isospin may be expected at low excitation energies. We have investigated, in a simple model space, the influence of the isospin-breaking Coulomb interaction on the degree of chaoticity of these rotational bands. The statistical measures used rely on an analysis of level-spacing distributions, which are extremely difficult to measure experimentally. We show, however, that the overlap intergrals between states of similar frequency reflect well the degree of chaoticity. This quantity is closely related to the experimentally more accessible gamma-decay ``spreading width''.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the degree of chaoticity in rotational bands of N≈Z deformed nuclei, as measured by level-spacing statistics, is sensitive to isospin symmetry breaking from the Coulomb interaction; however, the authors note that the isospin mixing in their simple model is quite small (e.g., 0.02% for the lowest state) and that realistic mixing is likely stronger, which may explain apparent discrepancies with earlier work on spherical nuclei.

## Method

The authors diagonalize a cranked deformed shell-model Hamiltonian exactly for four neutrons and four protons in the f7/2 shell, including a scalar-isoscalar two-body delta-function residual interaction and empirically obtained Coulomb matrix elements. They analyze the eigenvalue spectra using nearest-neighbour distribution (NND) with Brody, Berry-Robnik, and spectral-rigidity parametrizations, both with and without the Coulomb interaction, at two deformation values (κ=1.75 and 3.50) and rotational frequencies (ħω=0.5 and 2.5 MeV). They also compute overlap integrals between cranking wavefunctions at neighbouring frequencies as a proxy for the experimentally accessible γ-decay spreading width.

## Key numbers

- The Coulomb matrix elements used in the f7/2 shell are Vc(J)=0.578, 0.486, 0.374, and 0.330 MeV for J=0, 2, 4, and 6, respectively.
- The dimensionality of the matrix diagonalized for α=0 (favoured configuration) is 2468.
- For κ=1.75, the deformation parameter β is approximately 0.16.
- Isospin mixing for the lowest state at ħω=0.5 MeV is about 0.02%.
- At ħω=0.5 MeV, the energies of the 14th and 15th states are -33.43 and -33.41 MeV, respectively, with large isospin mixing due to near degeneracy.
- For ħω=2.5 MeV, isospin mixing is lower than at ħω=0.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of isospin symmetry breaking and chaos in spherical nuclei (e.g., Muñoz et al. (1988) and Guhr and Weidenmüller (1990)) to the case of deformed rotational nuclei, motivated by the recent observation of rotational bands with good isospin for N≈Z (Rudolph et al., 1996). The authors find that the degree of chaoticity depends on deformation and rotational frequency, with more regular dynamics at larger deformation (κ=3.5) and higher frequencies, and that the overlap integral between cranking wavefunctions at neighbouring frequencies reflects the degree of chaoticity, offering a connection to experimentally measurable γ-decay spreading widths. The results enable future work using the projected shell model with realistic interactions and model spaces to calculate E2 transition probabilities between states of neighbouring spin.
