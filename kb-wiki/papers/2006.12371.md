# Study of proton capture resonant state of ^{15}O at 7556 keV

> The slowest reaction in the CNO cycle 14N(p, gamma)15O has been studied by populating the E^lab_p =278 keV (E^r_c.m.=259 keV) proton capture resonant state of ^{15}O at 7556 keV. The strength of the resonance has been determined from the experimental data. The level lifetime of the sub-threshold resonant state at Ex=6792 keV, as well as the lifetimes of the 5181 keV and 6172 keV states, have been measured using the Doppler shift attenuation method (DSAM). The structural properties of the nucleus ^{15}O, such as, the level energies, transition strengths, level lifetimes, and spectroscopic factors, have been calculated theoretically by using the large basis shell model, which agrees reasonably well with the present as well as the previous experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper reports a measurement of the resonance strength of the 259 keV (center-of-mass) proton capture resonance in the 14N(p,γ)15O reaction, and lifetime measurements of three excited states in 15O using the Doppler shift attenuation method, but for the 6172 keV and 6792 keV states only upper limits on the lifetime could be established because the experimental F(τ) values were very close to 1.

## Method

The 14N(p,γ)15O resonance reaction was studied by bombarding an implanted 14N target in Ta backing with a proton beam at energies around 278 keV (lab). The resonance strength was determined from the thick-target yield curve using the 1384 keV primary γ-ray. Lifetimes of the 5181 keV, 6172 keV, and 6792 keV states were measured using the Doppler shift attenuation method (DSAM) by detecting γ-rays at multiple angles with HPGe and BEGe detectors, and comparing centroid shifts to theoretical F(τ) curves calculated using SRIM stopping powers. Large basis shell model calculations using the NuShellX code with the ZBM model space and REWIL interaction were performed to compute level energies, transition strengths, lifetimes, and spectroscopic factors.

## Key numbers

- Resonance strength ωγ = 12.78 ± 0.29 (stat.) ± 0.92 (sys.) meV for the 259 keV (c.m.) resonance; weighted average with previous values gives 12.7 (2) meV
- Lifetime of the 5181 keV state: τ = 10.45 +2.07/−2.21 fs (F(τ) = 0.82 ± 0.03)
- Upper limit on lifetime of the 6172 keV state: τ < 1.22 fs (F(τ) = 1.00 ± 0.03)
- Upper limit on lifetime of the 6792 keV state: τ < 1.18 fs (F(τ) = 0.99 ± 0.02)
- Theoretical width of the 7556 keV resonant state from partial wave analysis: 1.2 keV, compared to experimental width of 0.99(10) keV
- Spectroscopic factor for the 7556 keV state from ratio of experimental to theoretical width: S = 0.82(9)

## Context

This work addresses the long-standing discrepancy in the lifetime of the sub-threshold 6792 keV state in 15O, which is critical for determining the 14N(p,γ)15O reaction rate, the slowest reaction in the CNO cycle. Previous measurements by Bertone (2002) gave τ = 1.60 +0.75/−0.72 fs, while Schürmann (2006) could only set an upper limit τ < 0.77 fs, and Galinski (2014) gave τ < 1.84 fs. The present results support the shorter lifetime scenario with τ < 1.18 fs, which implies a larger radiative width and impacts the extrapolated astrophysical S-factor at zero energy. The large basis shell model calculations provide theoretical predictions for level energies, lifetimes, and spectroscopic factors that can be compared with experimental data to improve inputs for stellar model calculations of globular cluster ages.
