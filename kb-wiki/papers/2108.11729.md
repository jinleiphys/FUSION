# Extraction of the second and fourth radial moments of nuclear charge density from the elastic electron-nucleus scattering

> The expression for the radial moments $\left\langle r^{n}\right\rangle_{c}$ of the nuclear charge density has been discussed under the plane wave Born approximation (PWBA) method recently, which is significant to investigate the nuclear surface thickness and neutron distribution radius. In this paper, we extend the studies of extracting second-order moment $\left\langle r^{2}\right\rangle_{c}$ and fourth-order moment $\left\langle r^{4}\right\rangle_{c}$ from the Coulomb form factors $|F_{C}(q)|^2$ by the distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) at the small momentum transfer $ q $ region. Based on the relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations, the DWBA form factors $F_{C}^{DW}(q)$ are expanded into $ q^4 $, where the corresponding charge distributions are corrected by the contributions of neutron and spin-orbit densities. In the small $ q $ region, it is found that the experimental $|F_{C}(q)|^2$ can be well reproduced by considering the contributions of the $\left\langle r^{4}\right\rangle_{c}$ at the small $ q $ region. Through further analyzing the second-order and fourth-order expansion coefficients of the $F_{C}^{DW}(q)$, the relationship between the expansion coefficients and proton number $ Z $ is obtained. By the relationship, we extract the $\left\langle r^{2}\right\rangle_{c}$ and $\left\langle r^{4}\right\rangle_{c}$ from the limited experimental data of form factors at the small $ q $ region. Within the permissible range of error, the extracted $\left\langle r^{n}\right\rangle_{c}$ are consistent with the experimental data in this paper.

# Extraction of the second and fourth radial moments of nuclear charge density from the elastic electron-nucleus scattering

## Key claim

The authors extend the extraction of the second and fourth radial moments of nuclear charge density from Coulomb form factors from the plane wave Born approximation (PWBA) to the distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) at small momentum transfer, finding that the expansion of DWBA form factors to fourth order in momentum transfer is sufficient to reproduce experimental data, but only when the charge distributions include corrections from neutron and spin-orbit densities.

## Method

The authors calculated nuclear charge density distributions using the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with NL3* and FSUGold parameter sets, including contributions from both proton and neutron densities as well as spin-orbit densities. They computed Coulomb form factors using both PWBA (with effective momentum transfer) and DWBA (solving the Dirac equation for electron wave functions) methods, then expanded the DWBA form factors as a polynomial series in q^2 up to fourth order. The expansion coefficients were obtained by polynomial fitting in the momentum transfer range 0.3 fm^-1 < q < 0.7 fm^-1, and these coefficients were related to the radial moments through correction factors D_{2n}.

## Key numbers

- For 40Ca, the RMF model with NL3* gives ⟨r²⟩_c = 11.937 fm² (experimental: 11.902 fm²) and ⟨r⁴⟩_c = 205.225 fm⁴ (experimental: 199.991 fm⁴)
- For 48Ca, the RMF model with NL3* gives ⟨r²⟩_c = 11.763 fm² (experimental: 11.910 fm²) and ⟨r⁴⟩_c = 191.563 fm⁴ (experimental: 194.714 fm⁴)
- For 116Sn, the RMF model with NL3* gives ⟨r²⟩_c = 21.095 fm² (experimental: 21.405 fm²) and ⟨r⁴⟩_c = 579.258 fm⁴ (experimental: 603.180 fm⁴)
- For 124Sn, the RMF model with NL3* gives ⟨r²⟩_c = 21.467 fm² (experimental: 21.873 fm²) and ⟨r⁴⟩_c = 594.497 fm⁴ (experimental: 624.196 fm⁴)
- The polynomial fitting for DWBA form factors was performed in the range 0.3 fm^-1 < q < 0.7 fm^-1
- The neutron quadratic moment ⟨r²⟩_{ρ_cn} contributes negative values: for 40Ca it is -0.1200 fm², for 48Ca it is -0.1680 fm²

## Context

This work extends the methodology of Liu et al. (2021) from PWBA to DWBA for extracting radial moments, addressing the limitation that PWBA neglects Coulomb distortion effects which become significant for medium and heavy nuclei. The study is motivated by recent elastic electron scattering experiments on 132Xe at the SCRIT facility at RIKEN (Tsukada et al., 2017), where form factors were only measured at q < 1.5 fm^-1, making the small-q expansion approach particularly relevant. The results enable extraction of ⟨r²⟩_c and ⟨r⁴⟩_c from limited experimental form factor data at small momentum transfer, which can be applied to future electron scattering experiments on unstable nuclei.
