# Impact parameter dependence of collective flow and its disappearance for different mass asymmetries

> We study the role of impact parameter on the collective flow and its disappearance for different mass asymmetric reactions. The mass asymmetry is varied from 0 to 0.7 keeping the total mass of the system fixed. Our results clearly indicate a significant role of impact parameter on the collective flow and its disappearance for the mass asymmetric reactions. The impact parameter dependence is also found to vary with mass asymmetry of the reaction.

# Impact parameter dependence of collective flow and its disappearance for different mass asymmetries

## Key claim
The impact parameter significantly influences collective transverse flow and its disappearance (balance energy) in mass-asymmetric heavy-ion reactions, and this dependence itself varies with the mass asymmetry of the reaction, a finding that extends prior work which had focused mainly on symmetric or nearly symmetric systems.

## Method
The Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model was used to simulate collisions of nuclei with total masses A_TOT = 40, 80, 160, and 240, with mass asymmetry η varied from 0 to 0.7 in steps (e.g., η = 0, 0.1, 0.3, 0.5, 0.7). The directed transverse momentum ⟨P_x^dir⟩ was computed as a function of incident energy (40–800 MeV/nucleon) and reduced impact parameter b/b_max (0.25, 0.5, 0.75), using a soft equation of state with isotropic energy-dependent Cugnon cross-section. Balance energy (E_bal) was extracted as the energy where ⟨P_x^dir⟩ crosses zero.

## Key numbers
- The percentage variation in Geometry of Vanishing Flow (GVF) from η = 0 to η = 0.7 was −40.9% for A_TOT = 40, −24.59% for A_TOT = 80, −16.44% for A_TOT = 160, and −20.93% for A_TOT = 240.
- Incident energies studied ranged from 40 MeV/nucleon to 800 MeV/nucleon for the A_TOT = 240 system.
- Reduced impact parameters studied were b/b_max = 0.25, 0.5, and 0.75.
- Mass asymmetry η was varied from 0 to 0.7.

## Context
This work extends prior studies on collective flow and balance energy that had focused on symmetric and nearly symmetric reactions (e.g., studies cited as references 13 and 14 in the paper, which found E_bal increases approximately linearly with impact parameter). The paper uses the QMD model as developed by Puri and coworkers (referenced as 8, 15, 16), which has also been applied to low-energy phenomena such as fusion, fission, and cluster radioactivity. The results enable systematic understanding of how both impact parameter and mass asymmetry jointly affect the disappearance of collective flow, providing guidance for future experimental studies of the nuclear equation of state and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross-section using asymmetric systems.
