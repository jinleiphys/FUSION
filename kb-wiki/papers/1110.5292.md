# The Wake of a Heavy Quark in Non-Abelian Plasmas : Comparing Kinetic Theory and the AdS/CFT Correspondence

> We compute the non-equilibrium stress tensor induced by a heavy quark moving through weakly coupled QCD plasma at the speed of light and compare the result to N = 4 Super Yang Mills theory at strong coupling. The QCD Boltzmann equation is reformulated as a Fokker-Planck equation in a leading log approximation which is used to compute the induced stress. The transition from nonequilibrium at short distances to equilibrium at large distances is analyzed with first and second order hydrodynamics. Even after accounting for the obvious differences in shear lengths, the strongly coupled theory is significantly better described by hydrodynamics at sub-asymptotic distances. We argue that this difference between the kinetic and AdS/CFT theories is related to the second order hydrodynamic coefficient $τ_π$. $τ_π$ is numerically large in units of the shear length for theories based on the Boltzmann equation.

## Key claim

Even after rescaling distances by the shear length to account for the different shear viscosities, the strongly coupled N=4 Super Yang Mills plasma is significantly better described by hydrodynamics at sub-asymptotic distances than the weakly coupled QCD plasma, and this difference is related to the second order hydrodynamic coefficient tau_pi being numerically large in units of the shear length for theories based on the Boltzmann equation.

## Method

The authors computed the non-equilibrium stress tensor induced by a heavy quark moving at the speed of light through weakly coupled QCD plasma by reformulating the linearized Boltzmann equation as a Fokker-Planck equation in a leading log approximation and solving it via matrix inversion in Fourier space. For the strongly coupled N=4 Super Yang Mills theory, they solved the linearized Einstein equations sourced by a trailing string in the AdS-Schwarzschild geometry using pseudospectral methods, extracting the boundary stress tensor from diffeomorphism-invariant fields. The transition from non-equilibrium at short distances to equilibrium at large distances was analyzed using first and second order hydrodynamics in both theories.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy ratio for weakly coupled QCD: eta/s = 0.4613 * T/mu_A (where mu_A is the momentum diffusion coefficient)
- Second order hydrodynamic coefficient for weakly coupled QCD: tau_pi / (eta/sT) = 6.32
- Shear viscosity to entropy ratio for strongly coupled N=4 SYM: eta/s = 1/(4pi)
- Second order hydrodynamic coefficient for strongly coupled N=4 SYM: tau_pi / (eta/(e+P)) = 4 - 2 log(2) approximately 2.61
- Drag coefficient for heavy quark at v=1 in weakly coupled QCD: mu_F = (g^2 C_F m_D^2)/(8pi) log(T/m_D)
- Drag coefficient for strongly coupled N=4 SYM: mu_F(v) = (pi/2) * sqrt(lambda) * T^2 / sqrt(1-v^2)

## Context

This work extends the comparison of plasma response to a heavy quark probe between weakly coupled kinetic theory and strongly coupled AdS/CFT, following the hydrodynamic analysis of Chesler (2007) for the strongly coupled case and the kinetic theory source determination of Neufeld (2008). The authors had previously determined the hydrodynamic transport coefficients through second order in the gradient expansion for the leading-log kinetic theory (Hong 2010). The results enable precise determination of the hydrodynamic source through second order for both theories and provide a framework for understanding how the medium response to energetic partons approaches hydrodynamics, which may be useful for phenomenological studies of jet-medium interactions in heavy ion collisions.
