# Single-Particle Properties from Kohn-Sham Green's Functions

> An effective action approach to Kohn-Sham density functional theory is used to illustrate how the exact Green's function can be calculated in terms of the Kohn-Sham Green's function. An example based on Skyrme energy functionals shows that single-particle Kohn-Sham spectra can be improved by adding sources used to construct the energy functional.

# Single-Particle Properties from Kohn-Sham Green's Functions

## Key claim

The exact single-particle Green's function can be expressed in terms of the Kohn-Sham Green's function through a Dyson-like equation, but the Kohn-Sham single-particle eigenvalues are not physical except at the Fermi surface; adding additional local sources (such as a kinetic energy density source) to the energy functional can systematically improve the Kohn-Sham single-particle spectrum, at least at the Hartree-Fock level.

## Method

The authors use an effective action formalism with path integrals, introducing a generating functional that includes both a local source coupled to the density operator and a non-local source coupled to the fermion bilinear. A functional Legendre transformation from the source to the density yields the effective action, which is split into a zeroth-order (Kohn-Sham) piece and an interacting piece. Functional derivatives with respect to the non-local source produce the exact Green's function, and the relation between the exact and Kohn-Sham Green's functions is derived as a Dyson equation with a self-energy defined by functional derivatives of the interacting effective action. The formalism is applied to a dilute Fermi gas with a Skyrme-like effective Lagrangian, comparing functionals of the density only versus functionals that also depend on the kinetic energy density.

## Key numbers

- For the density-only functional in a uniform system, the Kohn-Sham spectrum is given by ε_k^ρ = k²/(2M) - J_0^ρ, where J_0^ρ = -(ν-1)/ν C_0 ρ - [ (ν-1)C₂/(15π²) + (ν+1)C'_2/(15π²) ] (6π²ρ/ν)^(5/3)
- For the ρτ functional in a uniform system, the Kohn-Sham spectrum is ε_k^{ρτ} = k²/(2M*) - J_0^{ρτ}, with 1/(2M*) = 1/(2M) + [(ν-1)/ν · C₂/4 + (ν+1)/ν · C'_2/4] ρ
- The difference between the two spectra is ε_k^ρ - ε_k^{ρτ} = [(ν-1)/ν · C₂/4 + (ν+1)/ν · C'_2/4] (k_F² - k²) ρ, showing they coincide only at the Fermi surface

## Context

This work extends the effective action approach to Kohn-Sham density functional theory developed by Valiev (1997, 1997b) for Coulomb systems and by Puglia (2002) and Furnstahl (2004b) for nuclear systems. The formalism clarifies the relationship between Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations (which have been widely applied by Vautherin and Brink (1972), Ring and Schuck, and others) and exact single-particle properties. The results enable systematic improvement of single-particle spectra from Kohn-Sham calculations by including additional source terms, and provide a framework for calculating spectral functions and incorporating the effects of low-lying vibrational states through self-energy diagrams that sum particle-hole bubbles.
