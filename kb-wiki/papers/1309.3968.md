# Three-loop HTLpt Pressure and Susceptibilities at Finite Temperature and Density

> We present results of a three-loop hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory calculation of the thermodynamical potential of a finite temperature and baryon chemical potential system of quarks and gluons. We compare the resulting pressure and diagonal quark susceptibilities with available lattice data. We find reasonable agreement between our analytic results and lattice data at both zero and finite chemical potential.

## Key claim

The three-loop hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) calculation of the QCD thermodynamic potential yields reasonable agreement with lattice data for the pressure and diagonal quark susceptibilities down to temperatures around 2 times the QCD pseudo-critical temperature, but only when using the Braaten-Nieto mass prescription for the gluon Debye mass and setting the fermion thermal mass to zero, which is the self-consistent solution to the quark gap equation at NNLO.

## Method

The authors performed a three-loop (NNLO) HTLpt calculation of the thermodynamic potential for SU(N_c) Yang-Mills theory with N_f massless quarks at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential, using dimensional regularization with the MSbar scheme. They made a Taylor expansion in the scaled mass parameters m_D/T and m_q/T to obtain a fully analytic result through O(g_s^5), then compared the resulting pressure and diagonal quark number susceptibilities to available lattice data from multiple collaborations.

## Key numbers

- For N_f=3, the g_s^3 contribution to the QCD free energy is smaller than the g_s^2 contribution only for g_s less than approximately 0.9, corresponding to T ~ 10^5 GeV ~ 5 x 10^5 T_c, with T_c ~ 175 MeV
- The one-loop running coupling uses Lambda_MSbar = 176 MeV, giving alpha_s(1.5 GeV) = 0.326 for N_f=3
- Central renormalization scales are Lambda_g = 2 pi T and Lambda_q = 2 pi sqrt(T^2 + mu^2/pi^2)
- Pressure comparisons are shown at mu_B = 0 and mu_B = 400 MeV
- Pressure difference Delta P is shown at mu_B = 300 MeV and mu_B = 400 MeV
- Second-order and fourth-order diagonal quark number susceptibilities are compared with lattice data from WB, BNL-BI, TIFR, and MILC collaborations

## Context

This work extends previous NNLO HTLpt calculations at zero chemical potential (Strickland et al., 2012) and previous LO and NLO HTLpt work at finite chemical potential (Andersen et al., 2011) to NNLO at finite density. The poor convergence of standard weak-coupling perturbation theory for QCD thermodynamics, which requires T ~ 10^5 GeV for the g_s^3 term to be smaller than g_s^2, motivated the use of HTLpt as a reorganization around massive quasiparticles rather than massless particles. The analytic NNLO result enables comparisons with lattice data for both pressure and susceptibilities down to T ~ 2 T_c, and the authors suggest the method could be applied to compute other processes relevant to heavy-ion phenomenology.
