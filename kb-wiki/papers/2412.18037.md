# Alpha-Particle Monopole Form Factors with Ab Initio No-Core Shell Model

> The state-of-the-art ab initio nuclear many-body approaches with modern nuclear forces are challenged by the recent experimental measurement of the monopole form factor of the $0^+_1\rightarrow 0^+_2$ transition in the $α$ particle [Kegel et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 130, 152502 (2023)]. We investigate the elastic and inelastic $0^+_1\rightarrow 0^+_2$ transition form factors using the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM). We observe a good convergence of both form factors with respect to the basis size employing the Daejeon16 nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) interaction. Our NCSM results are very close to the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonic calculations using $NN$ plus three-nucleon interactions based on the chiral effective field theory which take into account the continuum effects via the Lorentz integral transform. The significant difference between the ab initio results with various modern nuclear interactions and of some of them with the recent experimental data provides motivations for deeper investigation of this observable.

## Key claim
The authors find that their ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) calculation of the monopole form factor for the \(0^+_1\rightarrow 0^+_2\) transition in the alpha particle, using the Daejeon16 nucleon-nucleon interaction, exceeds the experimental data from Kegel et al. (2023) by a sizable amount, and is comparable to the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonic (EIHH) results of Bacca et al. (2012) that also disagree with experiment. This suggests that the coupling to the continuum may not be essential for this observable, contrary to some claims, and that the discrepancy with experiment remains unresolved.

## Method
The authors used the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) with the Daejeon16 nucleon-nucleon interaction, which is a softened version of the N\(^3\)LO chiral interaction that mimics three-nucleon forces via off-shell modifications. They diagonalized the nuclear Hamiltonian in a harmonic oscillator basis truncated by the maximum number of oscillator quanta \(N_{\rm max}\), treating the \(0^+_2\) resonance as a bound state. The translational-invariant point-proton transition density was computed, and the monopole form factor was obtained via a Fourier transform, including a microscopic proton form factor.

## Key numbers
- The Daejeon16 interaction gives a \(^4\)He ground state binding energy of 28.372(0) MeV.
- The extrapolated energy of the \(0^+_2\) state is \(-8.377(56)\) MeV, corresponding to an excitation energy of 19.995(56) MeV, in good agreement with the experimental value of 20.210 MeV.
- The Daejeon16 interaction yields a \(^4\)He point-proton radius of 1.514 fm, close to the experimental value of 1.484(5) fm.
- The experimental point-proton radius is derived from the charge radius \(r_c = 1.681 \pm 0.004\) fm (Sick, 2008) using a proton charge radius of 0.84075(64) fm (Mohr, 2024) and a neutron finite size correction \(R_n^2 = -0.1161(22)\) fm\(^2\).
- The NCSM results for the transition form factor at \(\hbar\Omega = 7\) MeV show good convergence, with results at \(N_{\rm max} = 16, 18, 20\) being nearly indistinguishable.

## Context
This work extends the line of ab initio calculations of the \(^4\)He monopole transition form factor, following the experimental measurement by Kegel et al. (2023) at the Mainz Microtron. It directly compares with the EIHH calculations of Bacca et al. (2012) using AV18+UIX and chiral interactions, and with the hyperspherical harmonic (HH) calculations of Viviani et al. (2024). The results contradict the claim by Michel et al. (2023) that continuum coupling resolves the puzzle, and raise concerns about the lattice EFT results of Meissner et al. (2023) due to a reported inconsistency between their transition density and form factor. The paper enables future benchmark studies using the same inter-nucleon interactions across different theoretical methods to understand the persistent discrepancy with experiment.
