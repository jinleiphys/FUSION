# Tensor Forces and the Ground-State Structure of Nuclei

> Two-nucleon momentum distributions are calculated for the ground states of nuclei with mass number $A\leq 8$, using variational Monte Carlo wave functions derived from a realistic Hamiltonian with two- and three-nucleon potentials. The momentum distribution of $np$ pairs is found to be much larger than that of $pp$ pairs for values of the relative momentum in the range (300--600) MeV/c and vanishing total momentum. This order of magnitude difference is seen in all nuclei considered and has a universal character originating from the tensor components present in any realistic nucleon-nucleon potential. The correlations induced by the tensor force strongly influence the structure of $np$ pairs, which are predominantly in deuteron-like states, while they are ineffective for $pp$ pairs, which are mostly in $^1$S$_0$ states. These features should be easily observable in two-nucleon knock-out processes, such as $A(e,e^\prime np)$ and $A(e,e^\prime pp)$.

## Key claim

The tensor components of the nucleon-nucleon potential produce a universal, order-of-magnitude enhancement of the momentum distribution of neutron-proton pairs relative to proton-proton pairs for relative momenta between 300 and 600 MeV/c and vanishing total pair momentum, an effect that should be observable in two-nucleon knockout reactions.

## Method

The authors used variational Monte Carlo wave functions derived from the Argonne v18 two-nucleon potential plus the Urbana-IX three-nucleon interaction (AV18/UIX) to compute two-nucleon momentum distributions for the ground states of ³He, ⁴He, ⁶Li, and ⁸Be. The double Fourier transform of the pair density was evaluated by Monte Carlo integration using a symmetric displacement method with Gauss-Legendre grids of up to 96 points for the relative coordinate and 80 points for the center-of-mass coordinate, summing over all nucleon pairs.

## Key numbers

- The np momentum distribution exceeds the pp momentum distribution by roughly an order of magnitude for relative momenta in the range 300–600 MeV/c (1.5–3.0 fm⁻¹) at total pair momentum Q=0, observed in all nuclei studied (³He, ⁴He, ⁶Li, ⁸Be).
- The ratio of np to pp pair numbers in the ground states is 2 for ³He, 4 for ⁴He, 3 for ⁶Li, and 8/3 for ⁸Be.
- The deuteron D-state component dominates the np momentum distribution over the range 1.4–4.0 fm⁻¹, while the S-state component has a node at 2.1 fm⁻¹.
- In ⁴He, the np momentum distribution at Q=0 scales with the deuteron momentum distribution for q > 1.5 fm⁻¹.
- A BNL experiment on ¹²C found the ratio of (p,ppn) to (p,pp) events over relative momenta 275–550 MeV/c to be roughly 20.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of two-nucleon density distributions (Forest 1996) and nucleon momentum distributions (Schiavilla 1986, Pieper 1992) by directly computing the momentum distributions of np and pp pairs. The results demonstrate that tensor correlations, already known to be crucial for reproducing low-energy spectra in light nuclei up to A=10 (Wiringa 2002), produce a universal signature in pair momentum distributions that should be measurable in A(e,e'np) and A(e,e'pp) reactions. The calculations enable direct comparison with knockout experiments at Jefferson Lab and BNL, and the authors suggest extending such measurements to ³He and ⁴He to further verify the role of the tensor force in nuclear structure.
