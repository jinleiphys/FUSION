# Shell Model Monte Carlo studies of neutron-rich nuclei in the 1s-0d-1p-0f shells

> We demonstrate the feasibility of realistic Shell-Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) calculations spanning multiple major shells, using a realistic interaction whose bad saturation and shell properties have been corrected by a newly developed general prescription. Particular attention is paid to the approximate restoration of translational invariance. The model space consists of the full sd-pf shells. We include in the study some well-known T=0 nuclei and several unstable neutron-rich ones around N=20,28. The results indicate that SMMC can reproduce binding energies, B(E2) transitions, and other observables with an interaction that is practically parameter free. Some interesting insight is gained on the nature of deep correlations. The validity of previous studies is confirmed.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that Shell-Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) calculations spanning the full sd-pf shells are feasible for neutron-rich nuclei, using a realistic G-matrix interaction whose monopole centroids have been corrected by a newly developed general prescription, but they note that the center-of-mass problem cannot be rigorously solved in incomplete model spaces and must instead be controlled through a variational procedure with a finite center-of-mass penalty term.

## Method

The authors constructed a renormalized G-matrix effective interaction starting from the charge-dependent Bonn potential, solved the Bethe-Goldstone equation with a 16O core, and included core-polarization diagrams through second order in the G-matrix. They replaced the monopole centroids of this interaction with those from a newly developed monopole field H_m fitted to single-particle and single-hole spectra around doubly magic nuclei using six parameters. SMMC calculations were performed in the zero-temperature formalism with a cooling parameter of 1/beta = 0.5 MeV and Delta beta = 1/32 MeV^-1, using 4096 samples at each extrapolation parameter value, with the sign problem handled by breaking the interaction into good and bad parts and extrapolating from negative g-values to the physical g=1 case.

## Key numbers

- The oscillator energy used for the G-matrix calculation was hbar Omega = 45 A^{-1/3} - 25 A^{-2/3} = 13.9 MeV for A=16
- The center-of-mass penalty term beta_CM = 1 was found to push spurious components up by approximately 14 MeV (hbar omega = 45 A^{-1/3} - 25 A^{-2/3} MeV) while leaving desired components relatively unscathed
- The monopole field fit yielded an rms deviation of 220 keV for 90 data points
- In 56Ni, the two-body (no Coulomb) contribution to binding energy in the pf shell is approximately 73 MeV, of which only 5 MeV comes from configuration mixing (H_M)
- The effective interaction was evaluated at a model space starting energy omega = -20 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of neutron-rich nuclei near N=20 and N=28 (Brown et al., 1990; Warburton et al., 1990; Poves and Retamosa, 1994; Fukunishi et al., 1992; Caurier et al., 1995) by performing calculations in the full sd-pf model space rather than truncated spaces, using SMMC methods (Koonin et al., 1997; Langanke, 1993) instead of direct diagonalization. The monopole correction approach follows the earlier work of Pasquini (1977) and Zuker (1994) showing that G-matrix failures are confined to their centroids, and adopts the general parametrization of the monopole field from Duflo and Zuker (1998) that can be applied to any model space. The results enable future studies of binding energies, B(E2) transitions, and other observables for unstable neutron-rich nuclei without the need for interaction fits specific to each truncated model space.
