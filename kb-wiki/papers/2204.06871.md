# Calculation of $β$-decay half-lives with Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov+$pn$-QRPA and isoscalar pairing strengths optimized by a Bayesian method

> For radioactive nuclear data, $β$ decay is one of the most important information and is applied to various fields. However, some of the $β$-decay data are not available due to experimental difficulties. From this respect, theoretically calculated results have been embedded in the $β$-decay data to compensate the missing information. To calculate the $β$-decay half-lives, a proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation on top of a Skryme energy density functional is applied with an assumption of spherical symmetry. The isoscalar pairing strength is estimated by a Bayesian neutral network (BNN). We verify the predicted isoscalar pairing strengths by preparing the training data and test data. It was confirmed that the finite-range isovector pairing ensures the $β$-decay half-lives insensitive to the model space, while the zero-range one was largely dependent on it. The half-lives calculated with the BNN isoscalar pairing strengths reproduced most of experimental data, although those of highly deformed nuclei were underestimated. We also studied that the predictive performance on new experimental data that were not used for the BNN training and found that they were reproduced well. Our study demonstrates that the isoscalar pairing strengths determined by the BNN can reproduce experimental data in the same accuracy as other theoretical works.

## Key claim

The isoscalar pairing strengths determined by a Bayesian neural network (BNN) can reproduce experimental beta-decay half-lives with accuracy comparable to other theoretical works, but the half-lives of highly deformed nuclei are systematically underestimated, indicating that nuclear deformation is important for more precise predictions.

## Method

The authors performed beta-decay half-life calculations using a proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pnQRPA) built on top of a Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) ground state, assuming spherical symmetry. The SkO' functional was used for the particle-hole channel, the D1S finite-range Gogny force was used for the isovector pairing channel, and a two-Gaussian finite-range force was used for the isoscalar spin-triplet pairing channel. The isoscalar pairing strengths were optimized by training a Bayesian neural network (BNN) with one hidden layer of 30 neurons on experimental half-life data, and the BNN was then used to predict isoscalar pairing strengths for neutron-rich nuclei without experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The physical constant D = 6144.4 ± 2.0 s was taken from Hardy (2009) for the beta-decay rate formula.
- The ratio of weak axial-vector to vector coupling constants was set to g_A = -1 (quenching factor of about 0.784) instead of the free value g_A = -1.2762(5) from PDG (2020).
- For nuclei with experimental half-lives less than 1 s or 0.1 s, the standard deviation s was approximately 0.5 for isoscalar pairing strengths in the range 0 ≤ V ≤ 140 MeV, corresponding to half-lives within about a factor of 10^0.5 ≈ 3.1 on average.
- For nuclei with experimental half-lives less than 10^3 s, the standard deviation s was approximately 1.1.
- The cutoff energy for the HFB calculation was set to ε_cut = 40 MeV, and the two-quasiparticle energy cutoff was set to 80 MeV, which satisfied the Ikeda sum-rule for the Gamow-Teller transition by more than 99.8%.
- The end-point energy for 128Cd with the finite-range force was 3.35 MeV at ε_cut = 20 MeV and 3.33 MeV at ε_cut = 40 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the systematic beta-decay half-life calculations within the Skyrme energy-density functional framework by introducing a finite-range pairing force to reduce model-space dependence, addressing issues raised by Engel (1999) regarding zero-range isoscalar pairing. It serves as a non-relativistic counterpart to the covariant density functional plus pnRQRPA calculations by Tomislav (2016), and applies the BNN method previously used for nuclear mass predictions by Utama (2016) and Z. M. Niu (2018) and for beta-decay half-lives by Z. M. Niu (2019) to the estimation of isoscalar pairing strengths. The results enable systematic predictions of beta-decay half-lives for neutron-rich nuclei relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis, though the spherical approximation limits accuracy for deformed nuclei.
