# Coherent Vector Meson Production from Deuterons

> In this paper we discuss coherent photo- and leptoproduction of vector mesons from deuterons at intermediate (virtual) photon energies, 3 < nu < 30 GeV. We consider the scattering from unpolarized and polarized targets as well as processes where the polarization of the recoil deuteron is measured. Our main motivation results from the need for a quantitative analysis of the space-time structure of photon-induced processes. In this respect we suggest several possibilities to explore the characteristic longitudinal interaction length in coherent vector meson production. Furthermore, we outline methods for an investigation of color coherence effects. In addition to the presentation of benchmark values for the maximal possible color coherence effect in various kinematic regions, we illustrate the anticipated phenomena within the color diffusion model. Finally we recall that the determination of vector meson-nucleon cross sections is not a closed issue. Besides being not known to a satisfying accuracy, they can be used to determine the strength of the D-state in low mass vector mesons.

# Coherent Vector Meson Production from Deuterons

## Key claim

Coherent photo- and leptoproduction of vector mesons from deuterons at intermediate photon energies (3 ≤ ν ≤ 30 GeV) can provide a quantitative probe of the space-time structure of photon-induced processes, particularly the longitudinal interaction length and color coherence effects, but only if the non-trivial t-dependence of the longitudinal interaction length is properly accounted for, an effect that has never been quantitatively studied before.

## Method

The authors decompose the vector meson production amplitude into single scattering (Born) and double scattering contributions using the eikonal approximation, with the deuteron form factor modified to include recoil effects through the longitudinal momentum transfer l_−. For the kinematic regime of vector meson dominance (Q² ≤ 1 GeV²), they parametrize the production amplitudes using standard VMD formulas with empirical slopes and Regge-based real parts; for large Q² (≫ 1 GeV²), they employ the quantum diffusion model to describe the evolution and re-scattering of the initially produced quark-gluon wave packet. The differential cross section is evaluated for unpolarized and polarized deuteron targets, as well as for processes where the recoil deuteron polarization is measured.

## Key numbers

- Photon energy range: 3 ≤ ν ≤ 30 GeV
- Momentum transfer range: 0 ≤ Q² ≤ 10 GeV²
- For ρ meson photoproduction at ν = 17 GeV: A ≈ 68 μb (amplitude normalization)
- Slope parameters for ρ production: B(Q² < 1 GeV²) = 7 GeV⁻², B(1 < Q² < 2 GeV²) = 6 GeV⁻², B(2 < Q² < 10 GeV²) = 5 GeV⁻²
- ρ-nucleon cross section: σ_ρN ≈ 27 mb
- Formation time at ν ≈ 10 GeV: τ_f ≈ 4 fm (using δm_ρ² ≈ 0.7 GeV²)
- Longitudinal interaction length for single scattering at ν = 4 GeV and t = −0.2 GeV²: δ₁ ≈ 2 fm
- At t ≈ −0.1 GeV² and ν = 6 GeV, the cross section ratio R_δ rises by 15% when photon energy increases from 3 to 6 GeV
- For polarized deuterons at t ≈ −0.35 GeV², the full production cross section decreases by more than two orders of magnitude when ν rises from 3 to 20 GeV
- At t ≈ −0.1 GeV² and ν = 6 GeV, R_δ decreases by approximately 50% when Q² rises from 0.5 to 2 GeV²
- For polarized φ meson production at t ≈ −0.45 GeV² and ν = 6 GeV, cross sections for σ_φN = 10 mb versus 15 mb differ by approximately one order of magnitude

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous derivation of vector meson production amplitudes (Frankfurt et al., 1997) by providing detailed phenomenological predictions and experimental signatures. It builds on the vector meson dominance framework of Bauer et al. (1978) and the quantum diffusion model of Farrar et al. (1988), while addressing the need for systematic investigation of longitudinal interaction lengths, a feature emphasized by Gribov (1970), Gribov et al. (1965), and Ioffe (1969) but never quantitatively tested. The results enable experimental searches for color coherence effects at facilities such as TJNAF and HERMES, and provide methods to improve the poorly known vector meson-nucleon cross sections, which are relevant for hadron production studies and nucleus-nucleus collision analyses.
