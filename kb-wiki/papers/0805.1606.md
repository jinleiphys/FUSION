# Absorption of phi mesons in near-threshold proton-nucleus reactions

> In the framework of the nuclear spectral function approach for incoherent primary proton--nucleon and secondary pion--nucleon production processes we study the inclusive $ϕ$ meson production in the interaction of 2.83 GeV protons with nuclei. In particular, the A-dependences of the absolute and relative $ϕ$ meson yields are investigated within the different scenarios for its in-medium width as well as for the cross section ratio $σ_{pn \to pnϕ}/{σ_{pp \to ppϕ}}$. Our model calculations take into account the acceptance window of the ANKE facility used in a recent experiment performed at COSY. They show that the pion--nucleon production channel contributes distinctly to the $ϕ$ creation in heavy nuclei in the chosen kinematics and, hence, has to be taken into consideration on close examination of the dependences of the phi meson yields on the target mass number with the aim to get information on its width in the medium. They also demonstrate that the experimentally unknown ratio $σ_{pn \to pnϕ}/{σ_{pp \to ppϕ}}$ has a weak effect on the A-dependence of the relative $ϕ$ meson production cross section at incident energy of present interest, whereas it is found to be appreciably sensitive to the phi in-medium width, which means that this relative observable can indeed be useful to help determine the above width from the direct comparison the results of our calculations with the future data from the respective ANKE-at-COSY experiment.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the A-dependence of the relative phi meson production cross section in proton-nucleus reactions at 2.83 GeV is appreciably sensitive to the phi in-medium width but only weakly sensitive to the experimentally unknown cross section ratio sigma_pn→pnφ / sigma_pp→ppφ, provided that the secondary pion-nucleon production channel is properly accounted for and the ANKE spectrometer acceptance cuts are applied.

## Method

The authors employed the nuclear spectral function approach to calculate inclusive phi meson production from both primary proton-nucleon processes (pp→ppφ, pn→pnφ, pn→dφ) and secondary pion-nucleon processes (πN→φN) in proton-nucleus collisions at 2.83 GeV. The calculations incorporated the ANKE spectrometer acceptance window (phi laboratory angles 0°–8° and momenta 0.6–1.8 GeV/c) and used four different scenarios for the total phi in-medium width, ranging from the free width of 4.45 MeV to a broadened width of about 60 MeV at normal nuclear density. The cross section ratio sigma_pn→pnφ / sigma_pp→ppφ was varied between a constant value of 2.3 and an energy-dependent parametrization based on effective meson-nucleon theory.

## Key numbers

- Incident proton kinetic energy: 2.83 GeV
- Free phi meson vacuum width: Γ_φ = 4.45 MeV
- ANKE spectrometer acceptance: laboratory polar angles 0° ≤ θ_φ ≤ 8°, momentum range 0.6 GeV/c ≤ p_φ ≤ 1.8 GeV/c
- In the second in-medium scenario, the total phi width reaches approximately 30 MeV at normal nuclear density ρ₀
- In the third scenario, the total phi width at ρ₀ is about 45 MeV, with a collisional width of 25.4 MeV at ρ₀
- In the fourth scenario, the total phi width at ρ₀ is about 60 MeV, with a collisional width of 40.4 MeV at ρ₀
- The free threshold energy for pp→ppφ is approximately 2.59 GeV
- The free threshold energy for πN→φN is 1.43 GeV
- The parametrization for sigma_pp→ppφ uses the form 10(1 - s_th/s)^1.26 (s_th/s)^1.66 in microbarns
- The cross section sigma_pn→dφ near threshold is given by 48√(ε_d/MeV) in nanobarns, where ε_d is the excess energy

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Paryev (2004) and others who studied phi meson production in proton-nucleus and photon-nucleus reactions using the spectral function approach, but those earlier calculations did not incorporate the actual ANKE spectrometer acceptance window. The study also builds on the method of using A-dependence of phi production cross sections to probe in-medium broadening, previously applied to omega mesons by Mertens (2005) and to Λ(1520) hyperons by Paryev (2007). The results enable direct comparison with upcoming data from the ANKE-at-COSY experiment to extract the phi meson in-medium width, which is predicted by hadronic models (Klingl 1997, Lutz 2002) to increase by a factor of 5–10 at normal nuclear density.
