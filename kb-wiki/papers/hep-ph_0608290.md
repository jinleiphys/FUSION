# Non-Perturbative Study of the Light Pseudoscalar Masses in Chiral Dynamics

> We perform a non-perturbative chiral study of the masses of the lightest pseudoscalar mesons. In the calculation of the self-energies we employ the S-wave meson-meson amplitudes taken from Unitary Chiral Perturbation Theory (UCHPT) that include the lightest nonet of scalar resonances. Values for the bare masses of pions and kaons are obtained, as well as an estimate of the mass of the η_8. The former are found to dominate the physical pseudoscalar masses. We then match to the self-energies from Chiral Perturbation Theory (CHPT) to O(p^4), and a robust relation between several O(p^4) CHPT counterterms is obtained. We also resum higher orders from our calculated self-energies. By taking into account values determined from previous chiral phenomenological studies of m_s/\hat{m} and 3L_7+L^r_8, we determine a tighter region of favoured values for the O(p^4) CHPT counterterms 2L^r_6-L^r_4 and 2L^r_8-L^r_5. This determination perfectly overlaps with the recent determinations to O(p^6) in CHPT. We warn about a likely reduction in the value of m_s/\hat{m} by higher loop diagrams and that this is not systematically accounted for by present lattice extrapolations. We also provide a favoured interval of values for m_s/\hat{m} and 3L_7+L^r_8.

## Key claim

The paper performs a non-perturbative chiral study of light pseudoscalar meson masses using Unitary Chiral Perturbation Theory (UCHPT) to include the effects of the lightest scalar resonances, finding that the bare masses of pions and kaons dominate the physical pseudoscalar masses, and warns that higher loop diagrams likely reduce the value of \(m_s/\hat{m}\) in a way not systematically accounted for by present lattice extrapolations.

## Method

The authors calculate pseudoscalar self-energies using S-wave meson-meson scattering amplitudes from UCHPT, which resums the unitarity cut to all orders and includes the lightest nonet of scalar resonances (sigma, kappa, f0(980), a0(980)) both as dynamically generated states and as explicit tree-level exchanges for the higher-mass octet around 1.4 GeV. They isolate the scalar contributions to the self-energies from the unitarized amplitudes and the tadpole diagrams, regularize the ultraviolet divergences using a dispersion relation representation with one subtraction, and match the results to the O(p^4) CHPT self-energies to extract relations between chiral counterterms.

## Key numbers

* The bare masses of pions and kaons are found to dominate the physical pseudoscalar masses (no explicit numerical bare mass values given in the truncated text).
* The renormalization scale \(\mu\) is varied between 0.5 and 1.2 GeV to estimate uncertainties from the regularization scheme.
* The S-wave \(K\pi\to K\pi\) amplitude has a pole at around \(s = -0.25\) GeV\(^2\) due to the kinematical pole at \(s=0\) in the lowest-order CHPT amplitudes for channels with different masses.
* The three-momentum cutoff for regularizing loops is estimated to be around 0.3-0.4 GeV based on comparison of results.

## Context

This work extends the O(p^6) CHPT calculations of pseudoscalar masses by Bijnens et al. (2006), which found that two-loop contributions to self-energies are much larger than the full O(p^4) contributions and that O(p^6) counterterms from resonance saturation seemed "severely overestimated." The paper addresses the question of how to improve calculations of contributions from the lightest scalar resonances, which are known to cause large higher-order corrections in processes like the I=0 pi-pi scattering length (40% correction relative to lowest order) and Gamma(eta to 3pi) (more than a factor of 2 correction). The results enable a determination of the O(p^4) CHPT counterterms \(2L^r_6-L^r_4\) and \(2L^r_8-L^r_5\) that overlaps with recent O(p^6) determinations, and provide a favoured interval for \(m_s/\hat{m}\) and \(3L_7+L^r_8\) that can inform lattice QCD extrapolations.
