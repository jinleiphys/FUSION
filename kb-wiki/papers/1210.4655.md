# Forward particle correlations in the color glass condensate

> Multiparticle correlations, such as forward dihadron correlations in pA collisions, are an important probe of the strong color fields that dominate the initial stages of a heavy ion collision. We describe recent progress in understanding two-particle correlations in the dilute-dense system, e.g. at forward rapidity in deuteron-gold collisions. This requires evaluating higher point Wilson line correlators from the JIMWLK equation, which we find well described by a Gaussian approximation. We then calculate the dihadron correlation, including both the "elastic" and "inelastic" contributions, and show that our result includes the double parton scattering contribution.

# Forward particle correlations in the color glass condensate

## Key claim
The Gaussian approximation for higher-point Wilson line correlators, which assumes the same relation between multipoint functions and the dipole as in the MV model, remains surprisingly well conserved by JIMWLK evolution, while the "naive large Nc" approximation fails already at the initial condition. The full dihadron correlation calculation including both "elastic" and "inelastic" contributions naturally contains a double parton scattering contribution that must be subtracted and treated separately.

## Method
The authors solved the JIMWLK equation numerically to evolve Wilson line correlators in rapidity, then compared the resulting quadrupole operator expectation values to two approximations: the Gaussian approximation and the "naive large Nc" (elastic) approximation where the quadrupole is assumed to be a product of two dipoles. They then computed the dihadron production cross section at parton level by convolving a linear combination of Wilson line operators (quadrupole-dipole, dipole-dipole, and dipole expectation values) with the q→qg splitting wavefunction, and compared the results to PHENIX and STAR dihadron correlation data.

## Key numbers
- The JIMWLK evolution was performed for 5.18 units of rapidity from the initial condition (MV model at y=0)
- For PHENIX data with trigger transverse momentum range 1.1–1.6 GeV, the calculated pedestal was 0.11 GeV⁻¹ versus the experimental value of 0.176 GeV⁻¹
- For PHENIX data with trigger transverse momentum 1.6–2 GeV, the calculated pedestal was 0.08 GeV⁻¹ versus the experimental value of 0.163 GeV⁻¹
- For STAR data, the calculated pedestal was 0.02 versus the experimental value of 0.0145
- The initial saturation scales used for comparison to experimental data were Qs0² = 1.51 GeV² (solid line) and Qs0² = 0.72 GeV² (dashed line)

## Context
This work extends the CGC description of forward dihadron correlations in dilute-dense systems (pA or forward AA collisions), building on earlier phenomenological work by Albacete (2010) that used the "naive large Nc" approximation. The numerical JIMWLK study of the quadrupole operator follows Dumitru (2011), and the double parton scattering contribution connects to the discussion by Strikman (2010). The results enable more accurate phenomenological comparisons to RHIC data from PHENIX (Adare 2011) and STAR (Braidot 2011), though a factor of approximately 2 uncertainty remains in the normalization of the single inclusive spectrum.
