# Hexadecapole Interaction and the Delta I=4 Staggering Effect in Rotational Bands

> A role of the multipole interaction in the description of the $\triangle I$=4 staggering phenomenon is investigated in a model consisting of a single-$j$ shell filled by identical nucleons. Exact diagonalization of the quadrupole-plus-hexadecapole Hamiltonian shows that the hexadecapole-hexadecapole interaction can produce a $\triangle I$=4 periodicity in the yrast sequence.

## Key claim
The hexadecapole-hexadecapole interaction can produce a Delta I=4 staggering in the yrast sequence of a single-j shell model, but only when the hexadecapole coupling strength is within a specific range (approximately 0.3 to 0.5 MeV relative to a fixed quadrupole strength of 1 MeV); for larger values the band structure is lost, and for zero hexadecapole strength no staggering appears.

## Method
The authors diagonalized exactly a Hamiltonian consisting of quadrupole-quadrupole and hexadecapole-hexadecapole interactions for N=8 identical nucleons in a j=15/2 shell. They fixed the quadrupole coupling constant chi_2 at 1 MeV to set the energy scale and varied the hexadecapole coupling constant chi_4 from 0 to 10 MeV. The staggering parameter was extracted by comparing the gamma-ray energy differences Delta E_gamma(I) to a smoothed reference curve Delta E_gamma^ref(I) defined as a four-point average.

## Key numbers
- The quadrupole coupling constant was fixed at chi_2 = 1 MeV to set the energy scale.
- The hexadecapole coupling constant chi_4 was varied from 0 to 10 MeV.
- Staggering appeared for chi_4 values of 0.3, 0.4, and 0.5 MeV; for chi_4 larger than 0.5 MeV the yrast band structure was lost.
- In the harmonic oscillator approximation, the coupling constants are estimated as chi_2 = 176/A MeV and chi_4 = 165/A MeV.
- The energy splitting associated with the staggering effect in superdeformed bands is about 100 eV.

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological approach of Hamamoto (1994), who used higher-order angular momentum terms to describe the Delta I=4 staggering observed in superdeformed bands by Haas (1990), Flibotte (1993), and Cederwall (1994). The model provides a more microscopic explanation by showing that hexadecapole-hexadecapole interactions, rather than quadrupole interactions alone, can generate the staggering. This enables future studies of hexadecapole deformations and multipole interactions in nuclear rotational bands, particularly for understanding the C_4 symmetry remnants suggested by the data.
