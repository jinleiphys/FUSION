# Two-Body Dirac Equations for Relativistic Bound States of Quantum Field Theory

> We review a little-known treatment of the relativistic two-body bound-state problem - that provided by Two-Body Dirac Equations obtained from constraint dynamics. We describe some of its more important results, its relation to older formulations and to quantum field theory. We list a number of features crucial for the success of such a formulation, many of which are missing from other methods; we show how the treatment provided by Two-Body Dirac Equations encompasses each of them.

## Key claim

The Two-Body Dirac Equations from constraint dynamics provide a manifestly covariant, quantum-mechanically well-defined framework for relativistic two-body bound states that corrects defects in both the Breit equation and the ladder approximation to the Bethe-Salpeter equation, exorcising quantum ghosts by covariantly controlling the relative time variable; the authors claim these equations have passed numerous tests showing they reproduce correct QED perturbative results when solved nonperturbatively, but note that for QCD the invariant functions must be chosen on semiphenomenological grounds.

## Method

The authors derive a compatible pair of Dirac-like equations for two spin-one-half particles interacting through world vector and scalar interactions, using constraint dynamics to eliminate the relative time degree of freedom in a covariant manner. The potentials in these equations take hyperbolic forms involving a function \({\cal G} = -\frac12 \ln(1-2{\cal A}/w)\) for vector interactions, where \({\cal A}\) is an invariant function of the covariant spacelike separation \(x_\perp\). For QCD applications, the authors apportion the Adler-Piran static quark-antiquark potential between the scalar and vector invariants \(S\) and \({\cal A}\), imposing that at short distance the potential is strictly vector while at long distance the confining portion is scalar with Coulomb vector portions, then solve the resulting Schrodinger-like forms nonperturbatively.

## Key numbers

- Singlet positronium exact spectral solution: \(w = m\sqrt{2+2/\sqrt{1+\frac{\alpha^2}{(n+\sqrt{(l+\frac12)^2-\alpha^2}-l-\frac12)^2}}}\)
- Upsilon \(b\bar b\) \(1^3S_1\): theory 9.453 GeV vs. experiment 9.460 GeV (0.2 MeV uncertainty)
- Pion \(u\bar d\) \(1^1S_0\): theory 0.144 GeV vs. experiment 0.140 GeV (0.0 MeV uncertainty)
- Rho \(u\bar d\) \(1^3S_1\): theory 0.792 GeV vs. experiment 0.767 GeV (1.2 MeV uncertainty)
- Overall chi-squared for meson spectrum fit: 101.0
- The pion mass tends toward zero numerically in the limit where the quark mass numerically goes toward zero; the rho meson mass approaches a finite value in the chiral limit

## Context

This work extends the constraint dynamics approach developed by the authors in the 1980s (Crater and Van Alstine, 1982, 1987) and provides an alternative to the Bethe-Salpeter equation, which suffers from negative norm ghost states and difficulties with relative time variables. The authors compare their results to those of Godfrey and Isgur, who use six parametric functions for spin dependence and require smoothing parameters, whereas the Two-Body Dirac Equations achieve comparable fits with only two parametric functions and no ad hoc smoothing. The framework enables nonperturbative calculations for both QED bound states (positronium, muonium) and QCD meson spectroscopy from heavy upsilonium to the light pion, with the potential to connect to lattice gauge theory results for meson spectroscopy.
