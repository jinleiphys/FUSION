# Chiral magnetic waves in strongly coupled Weyl semimetals

> Propagating chiral magnetic waves (CMW) are expected to exist in chiral plasmas due to the interplay between the chiral magnetic and chiral separation effects induced by the presence of a chiral anomaly. Unfortunately, it was pointed out that, because of the effects of electric conductivity and dissipation, CMW are overdamped and therefore their signatures are unlikely to be seen in heavy-ion collision experiments and in the quark gluon plasma. Nonetheless, the chiral anomaly plays a fundamental role in Weyl semimetals and their anomalous transport properties as well. Hence, CMW could be potentially observed in topological semimetals using table-top experiments. By using a holographic model for strongly coupled Weyl semimetals, we investigate in detail the nature of CMW in presence of Coulomb interactions and axial charge relaxation and estimate whether, and in which regimes, CMW could be observed as underdamped collective excitations in topological materials.

## Key claim

Using a holographic model for strongly coupled Weyl semimetals, the authors show that underdamped chiral magnetic waves (CMW) can exist in certain parameter regimes when both Coulomb interactions and axial charge relaxation are taken into account, contrary to the earlier conclusion that CMW are always overdamped and unobservable in heavy-ion collision experiments.

## Method

The authors employ a holographic Weyl semimetal model based on a five-dimensional bulk action with Chern-Simons terms, working in the probe limit with an AdS-Schwarzschild background at finite temperature and magnetic field. They compute the quasi-normal modes of the system to extract the dispersion relations of collective excitations, and incorporate Coulomb interactions via mixed boundary conditions (double-trace deformation) for the bulk gauge fields. The axial charge relaxation time is extracted from the purely imaginary pole in the axial charge density-density correlation function.

## Key numbers

* The axial relaxation rate follows approximately a quadratic power law: $\Gamma \simeq a \tilde{M}^2$, where $\tilde{M} \equiv M/(\pi T)$ is the dimensionless fermion mass parameter
* The dimensionless axial relaxation time $(\tau_5 T)^{-1}$ decreases monotonically with increasing $\tilde{b} \equiv b/(\pi T)$ (dimensionless Weyl node separation), and in the small $\tilde{b}$ regime the decay follows a quadratic power law scaling
* A universal relation holds: $(\tau_5 T)^{-1} \simeq (2/\chi_A) \phi_h^2$, where $\phi_h$ is the horizon value of the bulk scalar field and the coefficient $2/\chi_A$ depends only on the magnetic field $B$ and not on $\tilde{b}$
* The anomalous Hall conductivity is given by $\sigma_{\text{AHE}} = 8\alpha A_z(r_h)$, depending only on the horizon value of the axial gauge field

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic analysis of chiral magnetic waves by Jimenez-Alba (2014) and Shovkovy (2018), who showed that CMW become overdamped in quark-gluon plasma due to axial charge relaxation and Coulomb screening. The authors apply these considerations to strongly coupled Weyl semimetals, where the axial charge can be nearly conserved as suggested by the presence of large negative magnetoresistance (Burkov 2015). The holographic approach provides a microscopic determination of all hydrodynamic coefficients, going beyond the purely hydrodynamic framework of Sukhachov (2018). The results enable theoretical guidance for potential table-top experiments in topological semimetals to observe underdamped chiral magnetic waves.
