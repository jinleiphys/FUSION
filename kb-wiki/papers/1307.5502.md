# Correlated two-neutron emission in the decay of unbound nucleus 26O

> The particle unbound $^{26}$O nucleus is located outside the neutron drip line, and spontaneously decays by emitting two neutrons with a relatively long life time due to the centrifugal barrier. We study the decay of this nucleus with a three-body model assuming an inert $^{24}$O core and two valence neutrons. We first point out the importance of the neutron-neutron final state interaction in the observed decay energy spectrum. We also show that the energy and and angular distributions for the two emitted neutrons manifest a clear evidence for the strong neutron-neutron correlation in the three-body resonance state. In particular, we find an enhancement of two-neutron emission in back-to-back directions. This is interpreted as a consequence of {\it dineutron correlation}, with which the two neutrons are spatially localized before the emission.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed decay energy spectrum of the unbound nucleus 26O can only be reproduced when the neutron-neutron final state interaction is fully included, and that the resulting angular distribution of emitted neutrons, showing an enhancement in back-to-back directions, is a clear signature of dineutron correlation in the three-body resonance state, though the authors note that the energy and angular distributions have not yet been measured experimentally.

## Method

The authors construct a three-body model with an inert 24O core and two valence neutrons, using a density-dependent contact interaction between the valence neutrons. They first build the ground state of 27F with a 25F+n+n structure, then assume a sudden proton removal to obtain the initial state for 26O decay. The decay is evolved using a Green's function technique that includes couplings to continuum states, and the decay energy spectrum, energy distributions, and angular distributions of the emitted neutrons are computed from the decay amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The two-neutron separation energy of 27F used to calibrate the interaction is S2n = 2.80(18) MeV.
- The single-particle energy of the 1d3/2 state in 24O is 770+20-10 keV, with a width of Γ1d3/2 = 92.9 keV (compared to the empirical value of 172(30) keV).
- The peak in the correlated decay energy spectrum appears at 0.14 MeV, in good agreement with experimental data, whereas without the final state nn interaction the peak appears at twice the single-particle resonance energy (approximately 1.54 MeV).
- The width of the three-body resonance state is extremely small, experimentally of the order of 10-10 MeV.
- The angular distribution shows a clear enhancement at θ12 = π (back-to-back emission) when the final state nn interaction is included.

## Context

This work extends the three-body model previously applied to ground state properties and Coulomb breakup of neutron-rich nuclei (Hagino and Sagawa 2005; Esbensen, Bertsch, and Hagino 1997) to the spontaneous two-neutron decay of 26O, which was recently discovered experimentally (Liddick et al. 2012; Caesar et al. 2012). The study contrasts with earlier Coulomb breakup measurements of Borromean nuclei like 11Li and 6He, where the angular distributions were largely determined by neutron-core properties and difficult to interpret for nn correlations (Hagino, Sagawa, Nakamura, and Shimoura 2009; Kikuchi et al. 2010). The results enable future experimental measurements at facilities like the SAMURAI facility at RIBF at RIKEN to directly probe dineutron correlations via two-neutron decay angular distributions.
