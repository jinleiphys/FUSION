# Scale-free download network for publications

> The scale-free power-law behavior of the statistics of the download frequency of publications has been, for the first time, reported. The data of the download frequency of publications are taken from a well-constructed web page in the field of economic physics (http://www.unifr.ch/econophysics/). The Zipf-law analysis and the Tsallis entropy method were used to fit the download frequency. It was found that the power-law exponent of rank-ordered frequency distribution is $γ\sim 0.38 \pm 0.04$ which is consistent with the power-law exponent $α\sim 3.37 \pm 0.45$ for the cumulated frequency distributions. Preferential attachment model of Barabasi and Albert network has been used to explain the download network.

# Scale-free download network for publications

## Key claim
The download frequency of papers from an econophysics web page follows a scale-free power-law distribution, with the power-law exponent of the rank-ordered frequency distribution (γ ≈ 0.38 ± 0.04) being consistent with the exponent from the cumulative frequency distribution (α ≈ 3.37 ± 0.45). This is the first reported observation of scale-free behavior in download statistics, though the analysis is limited to a single web page in the field of econophysics and the last few data points below x ~ 1000 downloads show large fluctuations beyond the power-law fit.

## Method
The authors collected download frequency data from the econophysics web page (http://www.unifr.ch/econophysics/) maintained by Y. C. Zhang since 1998, using the 100 top-downloaded papers as of March 28, 2004. They applied Zipf-law analysis to the rank-ordered download frequencies and fitted the cumulative frequency distribution with a power law, then additionally fitted the rank-ordered data using the Tsallis non-extensive entropy formalism to extract the characteristic parameter q. The preferential attachment model of Barabasi and Albert (BA model) was used to interpret the observed scale-free behavior.

## Key numbers
- Power-law exponent for the cumulative download frequency distribution: α = 3.37 ± 0.45, extracted from the distribution N(x) ~ x^(-α) for the 100 top-downloaded papers.
- Zipf-law exponent for the rank-ordered download frequency: γ = 0.38 ± 0.04, obtained from averaging 12 Zipf plots spanning from September 28, 2003 to March 28, 2004.
- From γ, the derived power-law exponent α = 1 + 1/γ = 3.63 ± 0.28, consistent with the directly measured α = 3.37 ± 0.45.
- Tsallis entropy parameter: q = 1.351 ± 0.006, yielding q/(q-1) = 3.846 ± 0.068, in agreement with the α values.
- The power-law fit shows large fluctuations for data points below x ~ 1000 downloads.

## Context
This work extends the study of scale-free networks in scientific publications, following Redner (1998) who found power-law citation distributions with α ~ 3 for ISI and Physical Review D data, and Laherrere and Sornette who studied citation distributions of authors. The authors compare their download frequency exponent (γ ≈ 0.38) to Redner's Zipf exponent for citations (γ close to 1/2), noting the similarity suggests a common preferential attachment mechanism. The BA model of Barabasi and Albert, originally developed for Internet link networks, is invoked to explain the download network dynamics. This work enables future studies comparing download and citation networks across different scientific fields, though the analysis is restricted to a single econophysics web page and would need validation on larger, more diverse datasets.
