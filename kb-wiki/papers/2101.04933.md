# The $\mathbf{sQ\bar{q}\bar{q}}$ $\mathbf{(q=u,\,d;\, Q=c,\,b)}$ tetraquarks in the chiral quark model

> The low-lying $sQ\bar{q}\bar{q}$ $(q=u,\,d,\, Q=c,\,b)$ tetraquark states with $J^P=0^+$, $1^+$ and $2^+$, and in the isoscalar and isovector sectors, are systematically investigated in the framework of real- and complex-scaling range of a chiral quark model, whose parameters have been fixed in advance describing hadron, hadron-hadron and multiquark phenomenology, and thus all results presented here are pure predictions. Each tetraquark configuration, compatible with the quantum numbers studied, is taken into account; this includes meson-meson, diquark-antidiquark and K-type arrangements of quarks with all possible color wave functions in four-body sector. Among the different numerical techniques to solve the Schrödinger-like 4-body bound state equation, we use a variational method in which the trial wave function is expanded in complex-range Gaussian basis functions, because its simplicity and flexibility. Several compact bound states and narrow resonances are found in both charm-strange $cs\bar{q}\bar{q}$ and bottom-strange $bs\bar{q}\bar{q}$ tetraquark sectors, most of them as a product of the strong coupling between the different channels. The so-called $X_{0,1}(2900)$ signals, recently found by the LHCb collaboration, are unstable in our formalism with several candidates in the single-channel computations.

## Key claim

The chiral quark model predicts several compact bound states and narrow resonances in both charm-strange and bottom-strange tetraquark sectors, but the experimentally observed \(X_{0,1}(2900)\) signals are not reproduced as stable states; instead, they appear as unstable with several candidates in single-channel computations.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrodinger-like 4-body bound state equation using a variational method with complex-range Gaussian basis functions, within the framework of a chiral quark model whose parameters were fixed in advance from hadron, hadron-hadron, and multiquark phenomenology. They considered all tetraquark configurations compatible with the quantum numbers studied, including meson-meson, diquark-antidiquark, and K-type arrangements with all possible color wave functions, and applied the complex-scaling method to distinguish bound, resonance, and scattering states.

## Key numbers

*   \(X_0(2900)\): mass \(M = (2866 \pm 7) \,\text{MeV}\), width \(\Gamma = (57 \pm 13) \,\text{MeV}\) (LHCb experimental parameters from \(B^+\rightarrow D^+D^-K^+\) decays)
*   \(X_1(2900)\): mass \(M = (2904 \pm 5) \,\text{MeV}\), width \(\Gamma = (110 \pm 12) \,\text{MeV}\) (LHCb experimental parameters)
*   Theoretical masses of \(1S\) mesons used as thresholds: \(K\) at 481 MeV, \(K^*\) at 907 MeV, \(D\) at 1897 MeV, \(D^*\) at 2017 MeV, \(B\) at 5278 MeV, \(B^*\) at 5319 MeV
*   Model parameters include quark masses: \(m_q = 313\) MeV, \(m_s = 555\) MeV, \(m_c = 1752\) MeV, \(m_b = 5100\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous chiral quark model studies of hidden-charm pentaquarks \(P_c^+(4312)\), \(P_c^+(4380)\), \(P_c^+(4440)\) and \(P_c(4457)^+\) (Yang 2015), doubly-charm pentaquarks (Yang 2020), and doubly-heavy tetraquarks \(QQ\bar{q}\bar{q}\) (Yang 2020). It directly addresses the LHCb collaboration's 2020 observation of \(X_0(2900)\) and \(X_1(2900)\) signals, which may be the first experimental detection of tetraquark candidates with four different quark flavors. The results enable future experimental investigations to distinguish between compact tetraquark interpretations and alternative models such as hadronic molecule or re-scattering effects.
