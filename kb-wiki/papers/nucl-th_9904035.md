# Inclusive and Direct Photons in S + Au Central Collisions at 200A GeV/c

> A hadron and string cascade model, JPCIAE, which is based on LUND string model, PYTHIA event generator especially, is used to study both inclusive photon production and direct photon production in 200A GeV S + Au central collisions. The model takes into account the photon production from the partonic QCD scattering process, the hadronic final-state interaction, and the hadronic decay and deals with them consistently. The results of JPCIAE model reproduce successfully both the WA93 data of low p_T inclusive photon distribution and the WA80 data of transverse momentum dependent upper limit of direct photon. The photon production from different decay channels is investigated for both direct and inclusive photons. We have discussed the effects of the partonic QCD scattering and the hadronic final-state interaction on direct photon production as well.

## Key claim

The JPCIAE hadron and string cascade model, which includes partonic QCD scattering, hadronic final-state interactions, and hadronic decays consistently, reproduces both the WA93 low-\(p_T\) inclusive photon data and the WA80 transverse-momentum-dependent upper limits on direct photons in 200A GeV S + Au central collisions, with the caveat that the model assumes no quark-gluon plasma formation.

## Method

The JPCIAE model, based on the LUND string model and the PYTHIA event generator, simulates nucleus-nucleus collisions in the laboratory frame with a Woods-Saxon spatial distribution for nucleons, Lorentz contraction for the projectile, and a formation time for each particle. Collision pairs are ordered by time, and for hadron-hadron collisions with center-of-mass energy \(\sqrt{s} \geq 4\) GeV, string states are formed and PYTHIA generates final-state hadrons (including low-\(p_T\) and high-\(p_T\) processes); otherwise, conventional two-body scattering is used. Photon production is included from partonic QCD processes (e.g., \(q\bar{q} \rightarrow g\gamma\)), hadronic interactions (\(\pi\pi \rightarrow \rho\gamma\) and \(\pi\rho \rightarrow \pi\gamma\)), and decays of unstable particles (\(\pi^0\), \(\eta\), \(\rho\), \(\omega\), \(\eta'\), \(\Delta\), etc.).

## Key numbers

- The \(\pi^0\) decay accounts for nearly 98.8% of total decay photons in inclusive photon production in 200A GeV S + Au central collisions.
- The total neutral pion yield with rescattering is nearly twice the yield without rescattering; at \(p_T = 0.05\) GeV/c, the \(\pi^0\) yield with rescattering is about 2.4 times that without rescattering.
- In the high transverse momentum region, rescattering increases the \(\pi^0\) yield by nearly a factor of 1.5.
- The JPCIAE results for direct photons (inclusive photons after subtracting \(\pi^0\) and \(\eta\) decay photons) are compared to WA80 upper limits at the 90% confidence level in the rapidity range \(2.1 \leq y \leq 2.9\).
- The JPCIAE inclusive photon results are normalized to WA93 data at \(p_T = 0.5\) GeV/c, while VENUS 4.12 results are normalized at \(p_T = 0.3\) GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical efforts to explain WA80 direct photon upper limits and WA93 inclusive photon data in 200A GeV S + Au collisions, which had been addressed by models assuming QGP phase transition (Sri94, Dum94), a relativistic hadronic transport model with in-medium meson masses (Li97), and rate theory with hydrodynamics (Hun96). The JPCIAE model improves upon these by consistently including partonic QCD processes and hadronic final-state interactions without invoking QGP formation, and it reproduces the low-\(p_T\) inclusive photon enhancement that VENUS 4.12 could not match. This enables further study of photon production mechanisms at SPS energies and provides a baseline for distinguishing QGP signals at higher energies (RHIC, LHC).
