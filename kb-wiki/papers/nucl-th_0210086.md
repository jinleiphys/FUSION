# Role of effective interaction in nuclear disintegration processes

> A simple superasymmetric fission model using microscopically calculated nuclear potentials has shown itself to be outstandingly successful in describing highly asymmetric spontaneous disintegration of nuclei into two composite nuclear fragments. The nuclear interaction potentials required to describe these nuclear decay processes have been calculated by double folding the density distribution functions of the two fragments with a realistic effective interaction. The microscopic nucleus-nucleus potential thus obtained, along with the Coulomb interaction potential and the minimum centrifugal barrier required for the spin-parity conservation, has been used successfully for the lifetime calculations of these nuclear disintegration processes.

# Role of effective interaction in nuclear disintegration processes

## Key claim

The superasymmetric fission model (SAFM) using microscopically calculated nuclear potentials obtained by double folding with the M3Y effective interaction provides excellent estimates for half-lives of alpha and heavier cluster decays without adjusting the depth of the nuclear potential, though the chi-square per degree of freedom can be further reduced from 7.84 to 4.42 for alpha decays and from 7.05 to 2.21 for heavier clusters by applying normalization constants of 0.979 and 0.870 respectively.

## Method

The nuclear interaction potentials were calculated by double folding the density distribution functions of the emitted cluster and residual daughter nucleus with the finite-range M3Y effective interaction. The total interaction energy was obtained by summing the nuclear potential, Coulomb potential (assuming spherical charge distribution), and the minimum centrifugal barrier required by spin-parity conservation. Half-lives were then calculated using the WKB barrier penetration probability with zero-point vibration energies proportional to the Q-value.

## Key numbers

- For alpha decay half-lives across 106 nuclei, the present SAFM with M3Y interaction yields chi-square per degree of freedom of 7.84, compared to 96.31 for ASAFM(1986), 74.86 for ASAFM(1991), 78.33 for VSS, and 224.13 for LDM
- Using DDM3Y interaction, the chi-square per degree of freedom for alpha decays improves to 4.77
- For heavier cluster decays across 40 cases, the present model yields chi-square per degree of freedom of 7.05, compared to 6.61 for ASAFM(1986), 3.60 for ASAFM(1991), and 6.30 for LDM
- The M3Y effective interaction is given by v(s) = 7999 exp(-4s)/(4s) - 2134 exp(-2.5s)/(2.5s) in MeV
- The zero-range pseudo-potential for single-nucleon exchange is J00(E) = -276 (1 - 0.005E/A_alpha) MeV.fm^3
- The density-dependent factor uses beta = 1.6, independent of energy for cluster decay processes
- The density distribution for alpha particles has the Gaussian form rho(r) = 0.4229 exp(-0.7024 r^2)
- For heavier fragments, the Fermi distribution uses diffuseness a = 0.54 fm and radius parameter r_rho = 1.13 A_d^(1/3)

## Context

This work extends the analytical superasymmetric fission model (ASAFM) of Poenaru, Greiner, and collaborators (1986, 1991) and the preformed cluster model of Buck, Merchant, and Perez (1993) by replacing phenomenological nuclear potentials with microscopically calculated double-folding potentials using the M3Y effective interaction. The approach is contrasted with the Viola-Seaborg parametrization with Sobiczewski constants (VSS) and the liquid drop model (LDM) of Royer and Moustabchir (2001). The calculations enable reliable lifetime predictions for cluster radioactivity across the entire domain of exotic nuclei, including both alpha decay and heavier cluster emissions such as C-14, O-20, and Ne-24.
