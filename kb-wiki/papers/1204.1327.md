# Contributions of hyperon-hyperon scattering to subthreshold cascade production in heavy ion collisions

> Using a gauged flavor SU(3)-invariant hadronic Lagrangian, we calculate the cross sections for the strangeness-exchange reactions YY to NΞ(Y=Λ, Σ) in the Born approximation. These cross sections are then used in the Relativistic Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RVUU) transport model to study Ξproduction in Ar+KCl collisions at incident energy of 1.76A GeV and impact parameter b=3.5 fm. We find that including the contributions of hyperon-hyperon scattering channels strongly enhances the yield of Ξ, leading to the abundance ratio Ξ^{-}/(Λ+Σ^{0})=3.38E-3, which is essentially consistent with the recently measured value of $(5.6 \pm 1.2_{-1.7}^{+1.8})\times 10^{-3}$ by the HADES collaboration at GSI.

## Key claim

Including hyperon-hyperon scattering channels (YY → NΞ) in the RVUU transport model strongly enhances the predicted yield of Ξ particles in Ar+KCl collisions at 1.76A GeV, producing an abundance ratio Ξ⁻/(Λ+Σ⁰) = 3.38×10⁻³ that is "essentially consistent" with the HADES measurement of (5.6 ± 1.2⁻¹.⁸⁺¹.⁷)×10⁻³, though the central value is about 40% lower than the experimental central value.

## Method

The authors calculated cross sections for the strangeness-exchange reactions YY → NΞ (Y = Λ, Σ) in the Born approximation using a gauged flavor SU(3)-invariant hadronic Lagrangian, with coupling constants determined from empirical values and SU(3) relations, and a monopole form factor with cutoff parameter Λ = 0.7 GeV fitted to reproduce coupled-channel results for the related reactions K̄Y → πΞ. These cross sections were then implemented in the Relativistic Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RVUU) transport model, which includes mean-field potentials for baryons, kaons, and antikaons, to simulate Ξ production in Ar+KCl collisions at 1.76A GeV and impact parameter b = 3.5 fm.

## Key numbers

- Ξ⁻/(Λ+Σ⁰) abundance ratio = 3.38×10⁻³ from the full calculation including YY → NΞ, compared to the HADES measured value of (5.6 ± 1.2⁻¹.⁷⁺¹.⁸)×10⁻³
- The reaction YY → NΞ contributes 97.5% of the total Ξ yield; K̄Y → πΞ contributes 2.40%; K̄N → KΞ contributes 0.1%
- Central baryon density reaches a maximum of 1.87 ρ₀ at about 7 fm/c
- Final abundances saturate at: π = 10.3, K = 5.32×10⁻², K̄ = 1.15×10⁻³, Λ = 2.60×10⁻², Σ = 2.60×10⁻², Ξ = 2.34×10⁻⁴
- Ξ⁻ number = 1.17×10⁻⁴ (assuming isospin symmetry), compared to the HADES measured value of (2.3 ± 0.9)×10⁻⁴
- With a stiff EOS, abundances reduce to Λ = 1.74×10⁻², Σ = 1.77×10⁻², Ξ = 1.46×10⁻⁴, but the ratio Ξ⁻/(Λ+Σ⁰) = 3.13×10⁻³ remains essentially unchanged
- Including isospin asymmetry (N/Z ≈ 1.14) increases the ratio to 3.60×10⁻³, a 6.5% increase

## Context

This work extends prior studies of subthreshold particle production in heavy ion collisions, particularly the earlier RVUU study by Li et al. (2012) that included only the K̄Y → πΞ reaction and found Ξ yields about 10-20 times smaller than the HADES measurement. The statistical model also failed to reproduce the measured ratio. The present calculation resolves this puzzle by demonstrating that hyperon-hyperon scattering (YY → NΞ) dominates Ξ production at subthreshold energies, producing a factor of about 16 enhancement over the K̄Y → πΞ channel alone. This enables future studies of the nuclear equation of state using doubly strange baryon production, and the authors note that a more accurate treatment using the stochastic method of Cassing (2002) based on transition probability would be desirable.
