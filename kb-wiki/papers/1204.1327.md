# Contributions of hyperon-hyperon scattering to subthreshold cascade production in heavy ion collisions

> Using a gauged flavor SU(3)-invariant hadronic Lagrangian, we calculate the cross sections for the strangeness-exchange reactions YY to NОһ(Y=Оӣ, ОЈ) in the Born approximation. These cross sections are then used in the Relativistic Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RVUU) transport model to study Оһproduction in Ar+KCl collisions at incident energy of 1.76A GeV and impact parameter b=3.5 fm. We find that including the contributions of hyperon-hyperon scattering channels strongly enhances the yield of Оһ, leading to the abundance ratio Оһ^{-}/(Оӣ+ОЈ^{0})=3.38E-3, which is essentially consistent with the recently measured value of $(5.6 \pm 1.2_{-1.7}^{+1.8})\times 10^{-3}$ by the HADES collaboration at GSI.

## Key claim

Including hyperon-hyperon scattering channels (YY вҶ’ NОһ) in the RVUU transport model strongly enhances the predicted yield of Оһ particles in Ar+KCl collisions at 1.76A GeV, producing an abundance ratio ОһвҒ»/(Оӣ+ОЈвҒ°) = 3.38Г—10вҒ»Ві that is "essentially consistent" with the HADES measurement of (5.6 Вұ 1.2вҒ»В№.вҒёвҒәВ№.вҒ·)Г—10вҒ»Ві, though the central value is about 40% lower than the experimental central value.

## Method

The authors calculated cross sections for the strangeness-exchange reactions YY вҶ’ NОһ (Y = Оӣ, ОЈ) in the Born approximation using a gauged flavor SU(3)-invariant hadronic Lagrangian, with coupling constants determined from empirical values and SU(3) relations, and a monopole form factor with cutoff parameter Оӣ = 0.7 GeV fitted to reproduce coupled-channel results for the related reactions KМ„Y вҶ’ ПҖОһ. These cross sections were then implemented in the Relativistic Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RVUU) transport model, which includes mean-field potentials for baryons, kaons, and antikaons, to simulate Оһ production in Ar+KCl collisions at 1.76A GeV and impact parameter b = 3.5 fm.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of subthreshold particle production in heavy ion collisions, particularly the earlier RVUU study by Li et al. (2012) that included only the KМ„Y вҶ’ ПҖОһ reaction and found Оһ yields about 10-20 times smaller than the HADES measurement. The statistical model also failed to reproduce the measured ratio. The present calculation resolves this puzzle by demonstrating that hyperon-hyperon scattering (YY вҶ’ NОһ) dominates Оһ production at subthreshold energies, producing a factor of about 16 enhancement over the KМ„Y вҶ’ ПҖОһ channel alone. This enables future studies of the nuclear equation of state using doubly strange baryon production, and the authors note that a more accurate treatment using the stochastic method of Cassing (2002) based on transition probability would be desirable.
