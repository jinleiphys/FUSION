# Time-dependent properties of proton decay from crossing single-particle metastable states in deformed nuclei

> A dynamical study of the decay of a metastable state by quantum tunneling through an anisotropic, non separable, two-dimensional potential barrier is performed by the numerical solution of the time-dependent Schrodinger equation. Initial quasi- stationary proton states are chosen in the framework of a deformed Woods-Saxon single-particle model. The decay of two sets of states corresponding to true and quasi levels-crossing is studied and the evolution of their decay properties as a function of nuclear deformation is calculated around the crossing point. The results show that the investigation of the proton decay from metastable states in deformed nuclei can unambiguously distinguish between the two types of crossing and determine the structure of the nuclear states involved.

# Time-dependent properties of proton decay from crossing single-particle metastable states in deformed nuclei

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the time-dependent decay properties of proton emission from metastable states in deformed nuclei can unambiguously distinguish between true level crossing (states with different quantum numbers that do not mix) and quasi-crossing (states with same quantum numbers that mix and repel), thereby determining the structure of the nuclear states involved. The authors hedge that their results are obtained for a hypothetical nucleus with Z=82 and A=208, and note that qualitatively the results are independent of the choice of decaying nucleus.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the two-dimensional time-dependent Schrödinger equation for initial quasi-stationary proton states prepared in a deformed Woods-Saxon single-particle model with an axially symmetric potential described by Cassinian ovals. They follow the time evolution of the wavefunction on a finite spatial grid using a multistep predictor method (MSD2), calculating the total tunneling probability, decay rate, tunneling angular distribution, and mean value of angular momentum for two sets of states at and around their crossing deformations. The nuclear shape is described by a single deformation parameter ε, ranging from spheres (ε=0) to scission point configurations (ε=1).

## Key numbers

- True crossing occurs at deformation ε_tc = 0.25, where the two states ψ_2h (Λπ=3-) and ψ_1k (Λπ=3+) have equal energy E_2h = E_1k
- For the true crossing case, the two asymptotic decay rates λ(∞) differ by about one order of magnitude at the same deformation, with mean angular momentum values <l> ≈ 5.26 for ψ_2h and <l> ≈ 7.56 for ψ_1k
- Quasi-crossing occurs at deformation ε_qc ≈ 0.458 for two states with (Λπ)=3+, with minimum energy difference ΔE_qc ≈ 290 keV and angular momentum difference Δ<l>_qc = 0.01
- At the quasi-crossing point, the ratio of asymptotic decay rates λ_E>(∞)/λ_E<(∞) is about 3
- The nuclear potential depth is set to V_0 = -62.66 MeV for the true-crossing case and V_0 = -58.7 MeV for the quasi-crossing case

## Context

This work extends earlier time-dependent studies of quantum tunneling applied to α-decay (Ser94) and fission (Car94) in one dimension, and generalizes the authors' own two-dimensional method (Tal98) to specifically address the level-crossing phenomenon in deformed nuclei. The paper builds on the well-known Nilsson model (Nil55) for single-particle levels in deformed nuclei and the classification of crossing types from Bohr and Mottelson (BM75) and Sheline (She67). The results enable future experimental studies of proton decay in strongly deformed nuclei, as recently observed (Dav98), to determine the structure of nuclear states involved and to study the Landau-Zener effect in proton emission.
