# Pion form factor from a contact interaction

> In a Poincare'-covariant vector-boson-exchange theory, the pion possesses components of pseudovector origin, which materially influence its observable properties. For a range of such quantities, we explore the consequences of a momentum-independent interaction, regularised in a symmetry-preserving manner. The contact interaction, whilst capable of describing pion static properties, produces a form factor whose: evolution for Q^2>0.17GeV^2 disagrees markedly with experiment; and asymptotic power-law behaviour conflicts strongly with perturbative-QCD.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a momentum-independent (contact) vector-exchange interaction, treated in a symmetry-preserving truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations, can reproduce pion static properties but produces a form factor that disagrees with experiment for momentum transfers above approximately 0.17 GeV² and has an asymptotic constant behavior that conflicts with perturbative QCD predictions.

## Method

The authors embed a contact interaction (momentum-independent gluon propagator with a mass scale m_G) in a rainbow-ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations, regularized in a symmetry-preserving manner using infrared and ultraviolet cutoffs. They solve the gap equation to obtain a momentum-independent dressed-quark mass M, then compute the pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude (including both pseudoscalar and pseudovector components) and evaluate the electromagnetic form factor in the generalized impulse approximation.

## Key numbers

- The contact interaction yields a dressed-quark mass M = 0.40 GeV in the chiral limit (with m_G = 0.11 GeV, Λ_ir = 0.24 GeV, Λ_uv = 0.823 GeV)
- Chiral-limit pion decay constant f_π⁰ = 0.094 GeV; when the pseudovector component F_π is artificially set to zero, f_π⁰ becomes 0.11 GeV (a 15% increase)
- The pion charge radius in the chiral limit is r_π⁰ = 0.29 fm (compared to the experimental value 0.672 ± 0.008 fm)
- For Q² > 0.17 GeV², the contact interaction form factor differs from the QCD-based DSE prediction of Maris and Tandy (2000) by more than 20%
- The mixed pseudoscalar-pseudovector contribution F_π,EF^em passes through zero at Q² ≈ 1.5 GeV² and approaches a nonzero constant at large Q²

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to pion structure by testing a contact interaction as an extreme case, contrasting with the momentum-dependent renormalization-group-improved one-gluon exchange interaction used by Maris and Tandy (2000), which successfully predicted the measured pion form factor. The study demonstrates that form factor measurements at upgraded facilities like Jefferson Laboratory can distinguish between different quark-quark interaction mechanisms and map the running of the dressed-quark mass function, which is momentum-independent in the contact model but falls as 1/Q² in QCD-based DSE studies and lattice QCD.
