# Transport coefficients of the Gribov-Zwanziger plasma

> We study dynamic features of a plasma consisting of gluons whose infrared dynamics is improved by the Gribov-Zwanziger quantization. This approach embodies essential features of color confinement which set the plasma apart from conventional quasiparticle systems in several aspects. Our study focusses on a boost-invariant expansion for in- and out-of-equilibrium settings, which at late times can be characterized by the sound velocity, $c_s$, and the shear, $η$, and bulk, $ζ$, viscosities. We obtain explicit expressions for the transport coefficients $η$ and $ζ$ and check that they are consistent with the numerical solutions of the kinetic equation. At high temperature, keeping both the Gribov parameter and the relaxation time constant, we find a scaling $ζ/η\propto 1/3 - c_s^2$ which manifests strong breaking of conformal symmetry in contrast to the case of weakly coupled plasmas.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Gribov-Zwanziger (GZ) plasma, which incorporates infrared confinement effects via the Gribov parameter, exhibits a scaling relation \(\zeta/\eta \propto 1/3 - c_s^2\) at high temperature when both the Gribov parameter and relaxation time are held constant, indicating strong breaking of conformal symmetry; this scaling differs from weakly coupled plasmas and holds with universal prefactors \(\kappa_{\mathrm{GZ}} = 5\) at low temperature and \(\kappa_{\mathrm{GZ}} = 5/2\) at high temperature, independent of the Gribov parameter value.

## Method

The authors study a boost-invariant, transversally homogeneous (0+1)-dimensional plasma of gluons described by the Gribov-Zwanziger dispersion relation \(E(\mathbf{k}) = \sqrt{\mathbf{k}^2 + \gamma_{\mathrm{G}}^4 / \mathbf{k}^2}\), where \(\gamma_{\mathrm{G}}\) is the Gribov parameter. They employ the relaxation-time approximation for the kinetic equation and derive expressions for shear viscosity \(\eta\) and bulk viscosity \(\zeta\) from linear response around equilibrium, verifying consistency with numerical solutions of the kinetic equation for out-of-equilibrium expansion. The transport coefficients are computed as functions of temperature, and the ratio \(\zeta/\eta\) is analyzed in low- and high-temperature limits.

## Key numbers

- The Gribov parameter is fixed at \(\gamma_{\mathrm{G}} = 0.7\) GeV to describe lattice data for SU(3) Yang-Mills theory near the phase transition temperature \(T_c \approx 260\) MeV.
- At high temperature, the speed of sound squared for the GZ plasma is \(c_s^2 = \frac{1}{3}\left[1 - \frac{2}{3\sqrt{2} \pi c_{\mathrm{SB}}} \left(\frac{\gamma_{\mathrm{G}}}{T}\right)^3\right] + \mathcal{O}(1/T^4)\), where \(c_{\mathrm{SB}} = g_0 \pi^2/90\) with degeneracy \(g_0 = 16\) for SU(3).
- The scaling relation \(\zeta/\eta \simeq \kappa_{\mathrm{GZ}} (1/3 - c_s^2)\) holds with \(\kappa_{\mathrm{GZ}} = 5\) for \(T \to 0\) and \(\kappa_{\mathrm{GZ}} = 5/2\) for \(T \to \infty\), independent of \(\gamma_{\mathrm{G}}\).
- At low temperature, the sound velocity squared approaches \(c_s^2 = T / (\sqrt{2} \gamma_{\mathrm{G}})\).
- The effective mass for comparison with a massive Bose-Einstein plasma is \(m_{\mathrm{eff}} = \sqrt{2} \gamma_{\mathrm{G}}\), corresponding to the minimum of the Gribov dispersion relation at \(|\mathbf{k}| = \gamma_{\mathrm{G}}\) where \(E = \sqrt{2} \gamma_{\mathrm{G}}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the Gribov-Zwanziger plasma (Florkowski 2015) by providing detailed derivations of shear and bulk viscosities and their ratio, complementing perturbative QCD calculations of transport coefficients (Arnold 2000, Arnold 2003, Arnold 2006, Moore 2008) and lattice QCD results (Meyer 2007). The scaling relation \(\zeta/\eta \propto 1/3 - c_s^2\) resembles findings from gauge-gravity duality (Benincasa 2005, Buchel 2005) but differs from perturbative QCD expectations, and the framework enables further study of non-equilibrium dynamics in heavy-ion collisions where bulk viscous effects are important (Fries 2008, Bozek 2009, Ryu 2015).
