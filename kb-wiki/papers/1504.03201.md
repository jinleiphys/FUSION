# Chiral vortical wave and induced flavor charge transport in a rotating quark-gluon plasma

> We show the existence of a new gapless collective excitation in a rotating fluid system with chiral fermions, named as the Chiral Vortical Wave (CVW). The CVW has its microscopic origin at the quantum anomaly and macroscopically arises from interplay between vector and axial charge fluctuations induced by vortical effects. The wave equation is obtained both from hydrodynamic current equations and from chiral kinetic theory and its solutions show nontrivial CVW-induced charge transport from different initial conditions. Using the rotating quark-gluon plasma in heavy ion collisions as a concrete example, we show the formation of induced flavor quadrupole in QGP and estimate the elliptic flow splitting effect for Lambda baryons that may be experimentally measured.

## Key claim

The paper claims the existence of a new gapless collective excitation called the Chiral Vortical Wave (CVW) in a rotating fluid system with chiral fermions, but this wave only propagates linearly when the background fluid has a nonzero charge density (μ₀ ≠ 0); in a charge-neutral background the density fluctuations follow a nonlinear inviscid Burgers' equation instead.

## Method

The authors derive the CVW equation from two independent frameworks: first from anomalous hydrodynamic current equations combining the Chiral Vortical Effect (CVE) expressions for vector and axial currents with continuity equations, and second from chiral kinetic theory by analyzing the low-frequency, long-wavelength limit of the kinetic equations for right- and left-handed Weyl fermions in a rotating frame. They then apply the wave equation to a rotating quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in heavy ion collisions, using a Glauber model initial condition and the AMPT model for vorticity estimates, and compute the resulting flavor quadrupole moment and its translation into Λ baryon elliptic flow splitting via a blast-wave model.

## Key numbers

- CVW propagation speed: V_Ω = μ₀ ω / (2π² χ_μ₀), where μ₀ is the background vector chemical potential, ω is the vorticity magnitude, and χ_μ₀ is the thermodynamic susceptibility.
- For heavy ion collisions at √s = 200 GeV, impact parameter b = 7 fm, initial time τ₀ = 0.6 fm, and propagation time τ = 8 fm: the induced flavor quadrupole moment q^f_Ω ≈ -0.03 (V_Ω Δτ)², with Δτ in fm/c.
- AMPT model simulations give an initial vorticity ω ≈ 0.5 fm⁻¹; lattice susceptibility at initial temperature T₀ ≈ 350 MeV is χ_f ≈ 3 fm⁻².
- Using a background density μ₀ of 0.1 to 1 fm⁻¹ gives an estimated average CVW speed ⟨V_Ω⟩ ≈ 10⁻² to 10⁻³, leading to an induced quadrupole |q^f_Ω| at the ~10⁻⁴ level.

## Context

This work extends the concept of gapless collective modes from the Chiral Magnetic Wave (CMW) found by Kharzeev and Yee (2011) and Burnier et al. (2011) in magnetized systems to the vorticity-driven case, and it complements the previously established Chiral Vortical Effect (CVE) quantified by Banerjee et al. (2008), Erdmenger et al. (2008), and Son and Surowka (2009). The CVW provides an independent experimental probe of the chiral anomaly in heavy ion collisions that may be more robust than magnetic-field-driven signals, since vorticity lasts significantly longer than the strong magnetic field; it also enables future studies in spin-orbit coupled cold Fermi gases where only vorticity effects can be easily induced.
