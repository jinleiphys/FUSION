# Nucleon-quark mixed matter and neutron star EOS

> The nucleon-quark mixed matter is defined in the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock framework, in which quark densities are determined by equilibrium conditions between nucleon and quark chemical potentials, and nucleon-quark interactions play critical roles for resulting EoSs (equation of state). The two models of EoSs are derived from the nucleon-quark mixed matter (NQMM): The NQMM-A EoSs are based on the simple assumption that nucleons and free quarks occupy their respective Fermi levels and their Fermi spheres overlap from each other. In NQMM-B EoSs, the quark Fermi repulsion effect is incorporated on the basis of quakyonic matter, meaning that the nucleon Fermi levels are pushed up from the quark Fermi sphere by the Pauli exclusion principle. For the NQMM-A EoSs, the neutron-star mass-radius ($MR$) curves are pushed up above the region of $M \sim 2.1M_\odot$ and $R_{2.1M_\odot}\sim$ 12.5 km indicated by the recent observations, as the $qN$ repulsions increase. For the NQMM-B EoSs, the similar results are obtained by the combined contributions from the $qN$ repulsion and the quark Fermi repulsion. In both models of EoSs, the important roles of the $qN$ di-quark exchange repulsions are demonstrated to reproduce reasonable values of $M_{max}$ and $R_{2.1M_\odot}$.

# Nucleon-quark mixed matter and neutron star EOS

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nucleon-quark mixed matter (NQMM) models, formulated in the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock framework with realistic quark-nucleon interactions, can reproduce neutron-star masses near 2.1 solar masses and radii near 12.5 km, but only when di-quark exchange repulsions between quarks and nucleons are sufficiently strong; without these repulsions, the maximum masses and radii fall short of the observational constraints.

## Method

The authors construct two models of nucleon-quark mixed matter: NQMM-A, where nucleons and free quarks occupy overlapping Fermi spheres, and NQMM-B, which incorporates quark Fermi repulsion by pushing nucleon Fermi levels above the quark Fermi sphere (quarkyonic matter). Quark densities are determined by chemical equilibrium conditions between neutron and quark chemical potentials, without ad hoc parameters like Fermi-layer thickness. The EoSs are derived using Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations with realistic nucleon-nucleon, quark-quark, and quark-nucleon interactions, including meson-exchange and di-quark exchange potentials, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved to obtain mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- The adopted observational criterion for comparison is M = 2.1 solar masses and R = 12.5 km at that mass, based on median values from NICER data for PSR J0740+6620 (Salmi 2024).
- For hadronic matter with MPb three-body repulsions and Lambda mixing, the maximum mass is 2.06 solar masses and R at 2 solar masses is 11.3 km; without Lambda mixing, these become 2.19 solar masses and 11.8 km, both substantially below the 12.5 km target.
- Quark onset densities are tuned to 1.6, 2.0, and 2.5 times nuclear saturation density (rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}), labeled A16, A20, A25 (or B16, B20, B25) for NQMM-A (or NQMM-B).
- Constituent quark masses are taken as 362 MeV, with corrections Delta m_q ranging from -28 MeV to +200 MeV depending on the coupling strength and onset density.
- The nucleon-triquark coupling constant lambda_3/sqrt(4pi) is varied from 0 (lambda 0, meson-exchange only) to 1.5 (lambda 15), with intermediate values 0.1, 0.2, 0.4, and 0.8.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous quark-hadron transition models (Yamamoto, Yasutake, Rijken 2022, 2023) and addresses the "hyperon puzzle" where hyperon mixing softens the EoS and reduces maximum masses below 2 solar masses. It contrasts with purely hadronic approaches using multi-pomeron exchange potentials (Yamamoto et al. 2014, 2016, 2017) which could not simultaneously reproduce both M ~ 2.1 solar masses and R ~ 12.5 km. The NQMM framework enables systematic study of quark-nucleon interactions without the ad hoc Fermi-layer thickness parameter used in earlier quarkyonic matter models (McLerran and Reddy 2019). The results enable future extensions to include strangeness degrees of freedom and color-superconducting diquark pairing.
