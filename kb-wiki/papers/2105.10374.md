# Quark Nova with the Producing of Color-Flavor Locked Quark Matter

> In this paper, we suggest that the process in quark nova explosion may exist widely in various kinds of supernova, although it only happens in a small part in the core in most cases. And the contribution to the energy releasing of whole supernova explosion can also be provided by QCD interacting term. In this way we derive a general equation of energy quantity to be released in quark nova process related to several parameters. After quark nova explosion process, the remnant can be a quark star, or a neutron star with quark matter core if this process only happens in a small part inside the compact star instead of a "full" quark nova. We will also use a more generalized approach to analyse the strangelets released from quark nova and will draw a possible interpretation of why effects caused by strangelets have not been observed yet. Our result suggests that the ordinary matter can only spontaneously transform into strange quark matter by crushing them into high pressure under the extreme condition in compact star, although generally the reaction would really be exergonic.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that quark nova explosions may occur widely in various types of supernovae, but only in a small part of the core in most cases, and that the QCD interaction term can contribute to the total energy release of the supernova explosion.

## Method

The author derives a general equation for the energy released in a quark nova process using the Cornell Potential to describe QCD interactions, considering the transformation of nuclear matter into Color-Flavor Locked (CFL) quark matter. The analysis incorporates thermodynamic potentials, symmetry energy contributions, gravitational effects via an equivalent gravitational constant, and the conservation of baryon number and electric charge across three stages: collapse, phase transformation to CFL, and energy release. The study also generalizes the analysis to strangelets escaping compact stars to explain why their effects have not been observed.

## Key numbers

* The ratio of densities before and after phase transformation to CFL is given as ρ₂/ρ₁ = n₂/n₁ = 7/3, with n₁ = n_B and n₂ = (1/3)n_q.
* The difference in quark interaction potential energy per baryon number between nuclear matter and CFL quark matter is roughly 0.3 GeV.
* The neutron mass used in the CFL thermodynamic potential calculation is m_n = 939 MeV.
* The paper presents a table of supernova types with approximate energies: Type Ia at 1 foe, faint Ib/c at 0.1 foe, bright Ic and In up to above 5 foe, with absolute magnitudes ranging from -14 to -22.

## Context

This work extends the quark nova hypothesis proposed by Ouyed, Dey, and Dey (2002), which suggested that phase transformation to quark matter releases significant energy. The paper addresses the missing energy problem in ordinary supernova explosion models by proposing that quark nova processes can contribute additional energy. It also attempts to resolve the observational puzzle of why strangelets have not been detected, suggesting they can only form under extreme pressure inside compact stars and may not escape or trigger chain reactions in ordinary conditions. The analysis builds on prior work on CFL quark matter by Alford (1999) and on the Cornell Potential by Eichten (1975).
