# Dual Superconductors and SU(2) Yang-Mills

> We propose that the SU(2) Yang-Mills theory can be interpreted as a two-band dual superconductor with an interband Josephson coupling. We discuss various consequences of this interpretation including electric flux quantization, confinement of vortices with fractional flux, and the possibility that a closed vortex loop exhibits exotic exchange statistics.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that SU(2) Yang-Mills theory can be interpreted as a two-band dual superconductor with an interband Josephson coupling, but this interpretation relies on the London limit (assuming constant non-vanishing densities for the two scalar fields) which must be justified separately, for example by numerical investigations.

## Method

The author decomposes the SU(2) gauge field into its U(1) Cartan component and off-diagonal components, then rewrites the Yang-Mills Lagrangian in terms of two complex scalar fields and a complex vector field. By imposing the Landau version of maximal abelian gauge and expressing the Lagrangian in U(1)-invariant variables, the theory is mapped onto the Landau-Ginzburg free energy of a two-gap superconductor with interband Josephson couplings. The properties of vortex-like configurations (called "egorons") are then analyzed in the London limit.

## Key numbers

- Electric flux quantization for an egoron: Φ = (π/g)(N₂ - N₁), where N₁ and N₂ are integers representing phase increments of the two scalar fields
- For egorons with (N₁,N₂) = (±1,0) or (0,±1), flux is quantized in half units of an Abrikosov vortex
- For egorons with N₁ = -N₂ = -N (vanishing interband Josephson flow), flux is quantized in integer units: Φ = 2π/g N
- The inverse London penetration length is proportional to ρ (the combined density of the two scalar fields)

## Context

This work extends the dual superconductor approach to confinement (citing multiple "conf" references) and builds on Faddeev's proposal (Faddeev, 2004) that string variables in Yang-Mills theory relate to a decomposition of the gauge field. The two-band superconductor formalism is borrowed from condensed matter models used for high-Tc materials (Egorov, 2003; Egorov, 2004) and MgB₂ (HTC reference). The paper enables further investigation of confining strings as egorons with possible exotic exchange statistics, and proposes that the resulting abelian two-condensate model reproduces the correct SU(2) β-function at one loop.
