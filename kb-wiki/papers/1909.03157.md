# Searching for $^4\overline{L}i$ by momentum correlation function of $\overline{p}-^3\overline{H}e$

> The observed heaviest anti-nucleus so far is $^4\overline{H}e$ which is found in relativistic heavy ion collider in 2011. The yield of $^4\overline{L}i$ is four times bigger than that of $^4\overline{H}e$ according to the thermal model. From previous scattering experiment, we know that the $^4Li$ has a very short life time about $1.197\times10^{-22}s$. It decays into $^3{H}e$ and ${p}$. In experiment, the correlation function of $\overline{p}-^3\overline{H}e$ will offer us a method to observe $^4\overline{L}i$ by CPT symmetry. In this paper, we use the blast-wave model and Lednicky-Lyuboshitz analytical model to obtain a prediction of the correlation function of $\overline{p}-{}^3\overline{H}e$ with/without $^4\overline{L}i$ decay in Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{S_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV. The correlation function with $^4\overline{L}i$ decay is found to exhibit a peak at $k^* \approx$ 0.073GeV/$c$. The results offer a guide for the experimental search for $^4\overline{L}i$ in relativistic heavy ion collision.

## Key claim

The momentum correlation function of antiproton-antihelium-3 pairs, computed using the blast-wave model and the Lednicky-Lyuboshitz analytical model, exhibits a peak at a relative momentum of approximately 0.073 GeV/c when the decay of anti-lithium-4 is included, providing a method to search for this unstable antinucleus in relativistic heavy ion collisions; the detectability depends on the yield ratio of anti-lithium-4 to anti-helium-4, which is assumed to be 4.36 from the thermal model.

## Method

The authors used a blast-wave model to simulate the phase-space distribution of particles emitted from the fireball in Au+Au collisions at a center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair of 200 GeV, including the production and subsequent decay of anti-lithium-4 into an antiproton and anti-helium-3. The Lednicky-Lyuboshitz analytical model was then applied to calculate the two-particle correlation function for antiproton-anti-helium-3 pairs, incorporating Coulomb and strong final-state interactions with scattering lengths and effective ranges taken from previous proton-helium-3 scattering experiments. The correlation functions were computed for both central and peripheral collisions, with and without the inclusion of anti-lithium-4 decay, and the required event statistics for experimental detection were estimated from the error of the correlation function.

## Key numbers

- The lifetime of anti-lithium-4 is taken as 1.197 x 10^{-22} s, based on the known lifetime of lithium-4.
- The correlation function with anti-lithium-4 decay exhibits a peak at a relative momentum k* of approximately 0.073 GeV/c.
- The yield ratio of anti-lithium-4 to anti-helium-4 predicted by the thermal model is 4.36.
- The yield ratio of anti-lithium-4 to anti-helium-3 is 0.0148, deduced from the STAR measurement of anti-helium-4 to anti-helium-3.
- For central collisions, the chemical freeze-out temperature is 0.156 GeV and the kinetic freeze-out temperature is 0.091 GeV; for peripheral collisions, these are 0.16 GeV and 0.11 GeV, respectively.
- The baryon chemical potential is 0.022 GeV for central collisions and 0.019 GeV for peripheral collisions.
- The source radius is assumed to be 5 fm for central collisions and 3 fm for peripheral collisions.
- The momentum resolution assumed for antiprotons is 1.5% and for anti-helium-3 is 2%.
- The decay width of anti-lithium-4 is 6 MeV.
- The mass of anti-lithium-4 is 3.751296 GeV, the mass of the antiproton is 0.93827 GeV, and the mass of anti-helium-3 is 2.80923 GeV.
- The ratio of antiproton to proton multiplicity is set to 0.77 for central collisions and 0.8 for peripheral collisions.
- Approximately 1 billion Au+Au events at 200 GeV are estimated to be required to observe the anti-lithium-4 signal when the yield ratio of anti-lithium-4 to anti-helium-4 is 4.36; about 5 billion events are required if the yield ratio is reduced to 1:1.

## Context

This work extends the experimental search for antimatter nuclei beyond the heaviest observed antinucleus, anti-helium-4, which was detected by the STAR Collaboration (2011). It builds on the thermal model prediction that anti-lithium-4 has a yield about four times larger than anti-helium-4, and on previous scattering experiments that determined the lifetime of lithium-4. The method of using momentum correlation functions to infer the presence of short-lived resonances was previously applied to study lithium-4 via the proton-helium-3 correlation function, as considered in earlier work (hbt87, li4h4). The results provide a quantitative guide for experimental searches for anti-lithium-4 at RHIC, and the authors suggest that measuring the anti-lithium-4 to anti-helium-4 ratio could help discriminate between thermal and coalescence models of light nuclei production.
