# Comment on "Sensitivity of (d,p) Reactions to High n-p Momenta and the Consequences for Nuclear Spectroscopy Studies"

## Key claim
The strong sensitivity of low-energy (d,p) transfer cross sections to the choice of n-p potential model reported by Bailey, Timofeyuk, and Tostevin (2016) using the adiabatic distorted-wave approximation (ADWA) with nonlocal optical potentials (NLOP) disappears when the same reactions are treated with rigorous three-body Faddeev-type calculations. The sensitivity found in the earlier work is an artifact of shortcomings in the ADWA treatment of NLOP.

## Method
The author performed exact three-body Faddeev calculations for the 26Al(d,p)27Al(g.s.) reaction at a deuteron beam energy of 12 MeV, using the same p-26Al and n-26Al NLOP and 27Al binding potential as in the earlier work. The calculations employed multiple n-p potentials including CD Bonn, Argonne V18 (AV18), chiral N4LO (with regulators of 0.8 and 1.2 fm), Reid93, and similarity renormalization group (SRG) potentials, and results were converged with respect to partial-wave expansion, momentum integration, and Coulomb treatment.

## Key numbers
- ADWA in the earlier work predicted a spread of forward-angle cross section by a factor of 2.5 when using different realistic n-p potentials, while Faddeev-type results show only 10% sensitivity.
- The Faddeev-type cross section magnitude is lower by a factor of 2 to 5 compared to ADWA.
- For transfer to the 27Al first excited state (9/2+), the sensitivity is 1% and agreement with ADWA is within 30%.
- The deuteron D-state probability varies from 2.53% (SRG) to 5.76% (AV18) across the n-p potentials used.
- The equivalent local optical potential (ELOP) energy shift Delta E is approximately 40 to 70 MeV depending on the n-p potential, while the Faddeev formalism allows only nucleon-nucleus energies E_NA below 9 MeV for this system.

## Context
This comment directly contradicts the findings of Bailey, Timofeyuk, and Tostevin (2016), who reported strong sensitivity of (d,p) cross sections to high-momentum components of the n-p interaction using ADWA with NLOP. The author's Faddeev results instead agree with the generally expected insensitivity of low-energy observables to short-range physics, and with earlier work (Nunes, 2011) showing ADWA accuracy of 20% or better with local potentials and no sensitivity to the n-p model. The analysis reveals that ADWA with NLOP misses important features of the three-body scattering state, particularly the energy dependence of the nucleon-nucleus subsystem, and that the ELOP approximation used in the earlier work employs an unnatural energy shift from the Faddeev perspective.
