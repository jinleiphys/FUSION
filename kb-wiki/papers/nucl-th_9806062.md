# Density dependence of meson - nucleon vertices in nuclear matter

> Changes in the meson-nucleon coupling constant and the vertex form factor in nuclear matter are studied in a modified Skyrme Lagrangian including the sigma-meson field that satisfies the scale invariance. Renormalization of the axial-vector coupling constant, and the nucleon mass are studied in a consistent model. The results are consistent with the empirical evidence. A calculation of pi N commutator, sigma-term, indicates that the medium changes its magnitude considerably.

## Key claim
The meson-nucleon coupling constants and vertex form factors are modified in nuclear matter, with the pion-nucleon coupling reduced by approximately 25% at normal nuclear density, but the exact magnitude depends on the inclusion of the scalar dilaton field which introduces additional attraction that partially counteracts the quenching.

## Method
The authors construct a medium-modified Skyrme Lagrangian that includes a sigma-meson field as a dilaton to satisfy scale invariance, with medium effects on pions introduced via self-energy operators (P-wave delta-hole model and S-wave term) and on the dilaton field via renormalization of the gluon condensate. The semiclassical quantization procedure is used to calculate meson-nucleon form factors, axial coupling constants, and hadron masses from the soliton solutions of the Lagrangian at various nuclear densities.

## Key numbers
- Nucleon effective mass ratio at normal nuclear density: M_N*/M_N = 0.82, compared to QCD sum rule estimate of 0.72 ± 0.09
- N-Delta mass splitting at free space: δM_NΔ = 284 MeV (experimental: 293 MeV)
- N-Delta mass splitting ratio at normal density: δM_NΔ*/δM_NΔ = 0.75 (without mixed derivative term)
- Gluon condensate change at normal density: approximately 5%
- Sigma meson mass parametrization: m_σ*/m_σ = 1 - 0.12(ρ/ρ_0)
- Pion-nucleon sigma term at free space: Σ_πN ≈ 20.1 MeV
- Pion-nucleon sigma term at normal density: Σ_πN* ≈ 40.1 MeV, giving ratio Σ_πN*/Σ_πN ≈ 2
- Axial coupling constant ratio at normal density: g_A*/g_A = 0.9 (with scalar meson included)
- Pion-nucleon coupling reduction at normal density: approximately 25%

## Context
This work extends the medium-modified Skyrme model previously developed by the same authors (Rakhimov et al., 1997) for static nucleon properties, now including dynamical meson-nucleon vertices and a dilaton sigma field. It builds on the Skyrme form factor approach of Holzwarth and Machleidt and the delta-hole model for pion self-energy. The results enable improved modeling of in-medium nucleon-nucleon interactions for heavy ion collisions and provide predictions for sigma meson observables in nuclei, with the sigma meson decay width decreasing significantly in the medium.
