# Electromagnetic sum rules for 22O from coupled-cluster theory

> In this contribution, we discuss recent results on the determination of the electric dipole polarizability $α_D$ for the neutron-rich isotope $^{22}$O within the ab initio framework. Our calculations utilize the Lorentz integral transform coupled-cluster (LIT-CC) approach with two different chiral potentials that incorporate two and three-nucleon interactions. We compare our predictions with the available experimental data and find good agreement in the low-energy region.

# Electromagnetic sum rules for 22O from coupled-cluster theory

## Key claim

The coupled-cluster calculations with two different chiral potentials predict electric dipole polarizability values for 22O that agree with experimental data in the low-energy region, but only when comparing over the restricted energy range up to 24.9 MeV; the full theoretical polarizability at infinite energy exceeds the experimental value because the measurement misses charged-particle emission components that contribute to the giant dipole resonance.

## Method

The authors applied the Lorentz integral transform coupled-cluster (LIT-CC) method to compute the electric dipole response of 22O, using two chiral Hamiltonians (NNLOsat and Delta NNLOGO(394)) that include two- and three-nucleon forces. Ground-state calculations were performed at the CCSD and CCSDT-1 truncation levels, while the equations-of-motion for the auxiliary states included up to 2p-2h excitations. The spherical Hartree-Fock reference was built from model spaces of up to 15 major harmonic oscillator shells, with optimal frequencies determined as 12 MeV for Delta NNLOGO(394) and 14 MeV for NNLOsat.

## Key numbers

* Electric dipole polarizability for 22O at epsilon = 24.9 MeV: 0.61(4) fm^3 for Delta NNLOGO(394) and 0.56(4) fm^3 for NNLOsat(450), compared to the experimental value of 0.41(13) fm^3
* Extrapolated total electric dipole polarizability (epsilon to infinity): 0.83(2) fm^3 for Delta NNLOGO(394) and 0.86(3) fm^3 for NNLOsat(450)
* For the EM(500) interaction without three-nucleon forces: alpha_D(epsilon=24.9 MeV) = 0.69 fm^3 and alpha_D(epsilon to infinity) = 0.91 fm^3
* Optimal harmonic oscillator frequencies: 12 MeV for Delta NNLOGO(394) and 14 MeV for NNLOsat(450)
* Experimental neutron separation energy for 22O: 6.9 MeV
* Maximum experimental excitation energy available: 24.9 MeV
* Proton separation energy for 22O: 23.2 MeV

## Context

This work extends the LIT-CC method, previously applied to closed-shell nuclei by Bacca (2013, 2014) and Miorelli (2016, 2018), to the neutron-rich isotope 22O, for which photoneutron cross sections were measured by Aumann (2019) and the O22response collaboration. The results are consistent with earlier calculations by Miorelli (2018) and Bonaiti (2024), and show that including three-nucleon forces and triples correlations improves the description of low-energy strength distribution compared to the EM(500) interaction results of Bacca (2014). Ongoing work aims to reconstruct the full response function via inversion of the LIT, enabling direct comparison with experimental cross sections for both closed- and open-shell nuclei.
