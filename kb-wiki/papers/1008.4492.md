# The effective interaction hyperspherical harmonics method for non-local potentials

> A different formulation of the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonics (EIHH) method, suitable for non-local potentials, is presented. The EIHH method for local interactions is first shortly reviewed to point out the problems of an extension to non-local potentials. A viable solution is proposed and, as an application, results on the ground-state properties of 4- and 6-nucleon systems are presented. One finds a substantial acceleration in the convergence rate of the hyperspherical harmonics series. Perspectives for an application to scattering cross sections, via the Lorentz transform method are discussed.

# The effective interaction hyperspherical harmonics method for non-local potentials

## Key claim

The paper presents a reformulation of the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonics (EIHH) method that extends its applicability to non-local potentials, achieving substantial acceleration in convergence of the hyperspherical harmonics series for few-nucleon systems, though the method is explicitly limited to bound-state calculations and the non-local effects regarding the hyperradial coordinate are excluded from the effective interaction construction.

## Method

The authors modify the standard EIHH approach by subtracting and adding a term that is local only in the hyperradius, isolating a quasi two-body Hamiltonian that incorporates non-local effects in the hyperspherical angles while excluding hyperradial non-locality. They then apply the Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation to this quasi two-body Hamiltonian to obtain a partial effective interaction, which is used in a model space defined by a maximum hyperspherical momentum K_max, and the model space is increased until convergence is reached.

## Key numbers

- For 4He with the non-local Idaho N3LO potential, the bare HH expansion at K_max=20 gives a ground-state energy of -25.370 MeV and root-mean-square radius of 1.515 fm; the effective interaction gives -25.381 MeV and 1.518 fm at the same K_max.
- The converged extrapolated values for 4He are -25.37(2) MeV (bare) and -25.38(1) MeV (effective) for energy, and 1.515(4) fm (bare) and 1.518(1) fm (effective) for radius.
- With the effective interaction, K_max=8 for ground-state energy and K_max=12 for radius are sufficient to achieve convergence accuracy of less than 1%.
- For 4He with the bare potential, the energy at K_max=2 is -3.507 MeV, while with the effective interaction it is -17.773 MeV at the same K_max.

## Context

This work extends the EIHH method, originally developed for local potentials (Barnea, Leidemann, Orlandini, 2000-2001), to handle non-local interactions such as the Idaho N3LO potential (Entem, Machleidt, 2003) and the JISP16 force. The approach is analogous to the No Core Shell Model philosophy (Navratil, Barrett, 1996) but uses hyperspherical harmonics instead of harmonic oscillator basis, offering faster convergence in relevant quantum numbers. The method enables future application to reaction cross sections via the Lorentz Integral Transform method (Efros, Leidemann, Orlandini, 1994; 2007), allowing structure and reaction calculations for few-nucleon systems within the same framework using modern non-local potentials from effective field theory.
