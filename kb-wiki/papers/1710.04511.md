# Features of $蠅$ photoproduction off nucleon target at forward angles : Dominance of $蟺$ exchange with Regge cuts and scaling of differential cross sections

> We investigate photoproduction of $蠅$ off a nucleon target $纬N\to蠅N$ by using a Reggeized model where $蟺(135)+蟽(500)+f_1(1285)+f_2(1270)$+Pomeron exchanges are included in the $t$-channel for the reaction at forward angles. The reaction mechanism at low energy is featured by the dominance of the $蟺$ exchange with the absorptive cuts introduced to modulate the pion contribution to both $纬p\to 蠅p$ and $纬n\to 蠅n$ reactions. Necessity of $蟽$ exchange is illustrated in the analysis of the cross section from natural parity exchanges. Cross sections for differential, total, and spin density matrix with beam polarization are reproduced and compared with existing data on $纬p\to 蠅p$. Scaled differential cross sections of Jefferson Lab data on $纬p\to 蠅p$ are investigated at the production angle $胃=90^\circ$ with the nonlinear trajectories for a saturation. Differential and total cross sections for the $纬n\to蠅n$ reaction are analyzed to compare with recent experimental data at the CBELSA/TAPS Collaboration. An application to the $纬p\to蠅螖^+$ reaction is presented to demonstrate the significance of the $蟺$ exchange in the total and differential cross sections.

## Key claim

The paper claims that $\pi$ exchange dominates $\omega$ photoproduction at forward angles, but only after introducing absorptive Regge cuts to modulate the otherwise excessive pion contribution; without these cuts, the pure $\pi$ Reggeon overestimates the unnatural parity cross section by a factor of two.

## Method

The authors construct a Reggeized model for $\gamma N \to \omega N$ including $\pi(135)$, $\sigma(500)$, $f_1(1285)$, $f_2(1270)$, and Pomeron exchanges in the $t$-channel. They separate the cross section into natural and unnatural parity contributions using SLAC/LBL data, and introduce absorptive cuts from $\pi$-$f_2$ and $\pi$-Pomeron exchanges to reduce the pion contribution. The model is applied to both proton and neutron targets, and extended to the $\gamma p \to \omega \Delta^+$ reaction.

## Context

This work extends the Regge model of Kong (2015) for $\phi$ photoproduction to $\omega$ photoproduction, addressing the distinctive role of $\pi$ exchange which is more prominent than in $\rho^0$ and $\phi$ processes near threshold as noted by Friman (1995). The analysis uses SLAC/LBL data from Ballam (1973) separating natural and unnatural parity contributions, and compares with recent CBELSA/TAPS data from Dietz (2015) for the neutron target. The scaling analysis at $\theta=90^\circ$ tests the dimensional scaling predictions of Brodsky (1973) and enables future discrimination between direct photon coupling ($s^7$ scaling) and vector meson dominance ($s^8$ scaling) if data beyond $\sqrt{s} \approx 3$ GeV become available.
