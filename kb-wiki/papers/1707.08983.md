# Chiral symmetry constraints on resonant amplitudes

> We discuss the impact of chiral symmetry constraints on the quark-mass dependence of meson resonance pole positions, which are encoded in non-perturbative parametrizations of meson scattering amplitudes. Model-independent conditions on such parametrizations are derived, which are shown to guarantee the correct functional form of the leading quark-mass corrections to the resonance pole positions. Some model amplitudes for $ππ$ scattering, widely used for the determination of $ρ$ and $σ$ resonance properties from results of lattice simulations, are tested explicitly with respect to these conditions.

# Chiral symmetry constraints on resonant amplitudes

## Key claim

The quark-mass expansion of resonance pole positions in ππ scattering must be free of terms proportional to M² log M (so-called "forbidden logarithms") as a consequence of chiral symmetry, and several widely used model amplitudes for the ρ and σ resonances violate this condition, making them unreliable for chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD results.

## Method

The authors derive a model-independent condition on the inverse K-matrix (or generalized potential) in partial-wave amplitudes of the form t_l(s)^{-1} = 16π(K_l^{-1}(s) + I(s)), requiring that the coefficient of the M² log M term in the expansion of K_l^{-1}(s) equals -(8π²s)^{-1} to cancel analogous terms from the loop function I(s). They test this condition explicitly for three types of parametrizations: a unitarized Weinberg term, a Bethe-Salpeter approach with local chiral potential, and the Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM). An equivalent test is performed by expanding the resonance pole condition in powers of M and checking for non-vanishing coefficients of M² log M in the mass and width.

## Key numbers

- The loop function I(s) in dimensional regularization with MS-bar subtraction contains a term (2M²/s)(log(-M²/s)) in its expansion, which must be canceled by the K-matrix to avoid forbidden logarithms.
- The condition for cancellation of forbidden logarithms is ω_l^(2)(s) = -(8π²s)^{-1}, where ω_l^(2) is the coefficient of M² log(M²/μ²) in the expansion of K_l^{-1}(s).
- The chiral expansion of a heavy resonance mass m_H takes the form m_H = °m_H + c₁ᴴ M² + c₂ᴴ M³ + c₃ᴴ M⁴ log M + O(M⁴), with no M² log M term in the generic case.
- The unitarized Weinberg term for the isoscalar s-wave (I=0) with K₀ = (2s - M_π²)/(2F_π²) satisfies the condition, while the isovector p-wave (I=1) with K₁ = (s - 4M_π²)/(6F_π²) violates it.
- The Bethe-Salpeter approach with K₁(s) = 32π q(s)² / [48π(F² - 8M_π² l̂₁ + 4s l̂₂) + 2q(s)²(a(μ)+1)] fails the condition regardless of whether the running of F_π is included.

## Context

This work extends the chiral symmetry constraints previously established for heavy meson masses and widths (Gasser 1983, Jenkins 1995, Bijnens 1997, Bruns 2004, Djukanovic 2009) to the specific case of resonant ππ scattering amplitudes used in lattice QCD analyses. The paper directly tests models employed by Hu (2016, 2017), Guo (2016), and Dudek (2012) for extracting ρ and σ properties from lattice data. The IAM (Truong 1988, Pelaez 2010) is shown to satisfy the constraints, making it preferable for chiral extrapolations. The work enables a systematic reduction of model dependence in extracting resonance parameters from lattice simulations by providing a simple consistency test that parametrizations must pass.
