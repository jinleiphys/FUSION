# Baryons and Low-Density Baryonic Matter in 1+1 Dimensional Large N_c QCD with Heavy Quarks

> This paper studies baryons and baryonic matter in the combined large N_c and heavy quark mass limits of QCD in 1+1 dimension. In this non-relativistic limit, baryons are composed of N_c quarks that interact, at leading order in N_c, through a color Coulomb potential. Using variational techniques, very accurate calculations of single baryon masses and interaction energies of low-density baryon crystal are performed. These results are used to cross-check a general numerical approach applicable for arbitrary quark masses and baryon densities recently proposed by Bringoltz, which is based on a lattice in a finite box with periodic boundary conditions. The Bringoltz method differs from a previous approach of Salcedo, et al. in its treatment of a finite box effect - namely gauge configurations that wind around the box. One might expect these effects to be small for large enough boxes, in which the baryon density approaches zero to high accuracy at the edges. However, the effects of these windings appear to be quite large even in such boxes. The large mass infinite volume calculations performed here are consistent with the results of numerical calculations using the Bringoltz method. The calculation of the baryon crystal interaction energy requires the assumption that at low-densities the ground state is composed of individual baryons, each in a color-singlet state and orthogonal to each other. This assumption is plausible but ad hoc in that one can construct configurations in which the entire state is color-singlet but cannot be broken into individual color-singlet baryons. The interaction energy of low-density baryon crystals calculated with the assumption is consistent with numerical results based on Bringoltz's approach suggesting that the assumption is justified. This further supports a similar assumption that was made in 3+1 dimensions, where no alternative means of calculation exist.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in the combined large \(N_c\) and heavy quark mass limits of QCD in 1+1 dimensions, a simple variational mean-field calculation of single baryon masses and low-density baryon crystal interaction energies agrees with the more general numerical approach of Bringoltz (2011), which includes nontrivial finite-box winding effects that are surprisingly large even for boxes large enough to comfortably contain a baryon.

## Method

The authors performed a variational calculation of the single baryon ground state using a trial wave function parameterized as a polynomial times a Gaussian with up to 10 variational parameters, minimizing the nonrelativistic mean-field energy functional derived from Witten's (1979) mean-field argument. For the baryon crystal at low density, they assumed the ground state consists of individual color-singlet baryons with orthogonal wave functions and computed the interaction energy per unit cell including only nearest-neighbor interactions. They compared these results to numerical solutions of the lattice-regularized Hamiltonian of Bringoltz (2011), which includes gauge configurations that wind around the periodic box, extrapolating to the continuum and infinite-winding limits.

## Key numbers

- The variational ground state energy per color is \(\frac{\langle\psi|H|\psi\rangle}{N_c\sqrt{\lambda}} \approx \frac{m_q}{\sqrt{\lambda}} + 0.25574 \left( \frac{m_q}{\sqrt{\lambda}} \right)^{-1/3}\), with the interaction energy accurate to five significant figures.
- For \(\frac{m_q}{\sqrt{\lambda}} = 100\), the baryon mass from the numerical Bringoltz method is \(\frac{E}{N_c\sqrt{\lambda}} = 100.0550 \pm 0.0001\), while the variational result is \(\frac{E_{\rm var}}{N_c\sqrt{\lambda}} = 100.055098\).
- For \(\frac{m_q}{\sqrt{\lambda}} = 200\), the numerical baryon mass is \(\frac{E}{N_c\sqrt{\lambda}} = 200.0440 \pm 0.0003\), while the variational result is \(\frac{E_{\rm var}}{N_c\sqrt{\lambda}} = 200.043731\).
- The relative difference in interaction energy between the \(M=1\) truncation (no windings) and the \(M \to \infty\) extrapolation (full windings) is approximately 7 percent for both quark masses, despite the baryon density dropping to near zero well before the box edges.
- The scaling parameter \(R \approx 1.079746 \left( \frac{\lambda}{4} m_q \right)^{-1/3}\) and the dimensionless Lagrange multiplier \(\bar{\epsilon} \approx 1.0741901\).
- The asymptotic wave function constant \(k \approx 1.21\) with \(\bar{\epsilon} \approx 1.07\).

## Context

This work extends Witten's (1979) mean-field analysis of baryons in large \(N_c\) QCD to 1+1 dimensions and provides an independent cross-check of the numerical Hamiltonian approach of Bringoltz (2011), which itself differs from the earlier approach of Salcedo et al. (2001) by including gauge configurations that wind around the periodic box. The agreement between the variational and numerical results validates the inclusion of these winding effects, which are surprisingly large even for boxes where the baryon density has dropped to near zero at the edges. The study also supports a similar assumption about the absence of hidden-color states in low-density baryonic matter that was previously made in 3+1 dimensions by the same group (Adhikari et al., 2011), where no alternative means of verification exist.
