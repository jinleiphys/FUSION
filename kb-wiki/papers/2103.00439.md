# Quadrupole radiation and $e^+e^-$ pair production in the collision of nonrelativistic nuclei

> We analyze the one-photon mechanism of $e^+e^-$ pair production in the collision of nonrelativistic nuclei. The contribution of electric quadrupole radiation of virtual photon to the corresponding cross section is found. The effect of the finite nuclear size is considered in detail. A comparison is made with the contribution of electric dipole radiation of virtual photon and with the contribution of the two-photon pair production mechanism. It is shown that the contribution of electric quadrupole radiation is dominant in a wide range of relative velocities. The cross section for the production of $e^+e^-$ pair with the capture of an electron by one of the nuclei is also analyzed.

# Quadrupole radiation and e⁺e⁻ pair production in the collision of nonrelativistic nuclei

## Key claim

The electric quadrupole (E2) contribution to the one-photon mechanism of e⁺e⁻ pair production dominates over both the electric dipole (E1) contribution and the two-photon mechanism contribution in a wide range of relative velocities β < 1 for nonrelativistic nuclear collisions, provided the factor 𝒟₂ ≈ 1 (which holds for most nuclei) while the dipole factor 𝒟₁ is very small (typically 10⁻³–10⁻⁵) for all but the lightest nuclei.

## Method

The authors compute the one-photon (bremsstrahlung) mechanism of e⁺e⁻ pair production in nonrelativistic nuclear collisions by evaluating the spectral intensity of electric quadrupole radiation using classical electrodynamics with Coulomb trajectories, then converting the bremsstrahlung cross section to the pair production cross section via a general formula involving the function Φ(2mₑ/ω). The finite nuclear size is accounted for by introducing a minimum impact parameter cutoff ϵₘᵢₙ = √(1 + R²/a²) where R = R₁ + R₂ is the sum of nuclear radii. The bound-free (electron capture) case is treated using the nonrelativistic wave function at the origin for the bound electron, summed over principal quantum numbers n.

## Key numbers

- The two-photon free-free cross section at β ≪ 1 is σ_ff^(γγ) = (η₁η₂)²β⁶/(1050π mₑ²) {π²(η₁+η₂)² + (592/105)β²}, showing β⁶ suppression
- The two-photon bound-free cross section at β ≪ 1 is σ_bf^(γγ) = 16(η₁η₂)²(η₁³+η₂³)β⁶/(15015 mₑ²) ζ₃ {π²(η₁+η₂)² + (976/153)β²}, where ζ₃ ≈ 1.2
- The electric dipole free-free cross section is σ_ff^(E1) = [16(η₁η₂)² 𝒟₁/(27π β² Mₚ²)] ln³(ω₀/(mₑϵₘᵢₙ))
- The electric quadrupole free-free cross section is σ_ff^(E2) = [4(η₁η₂)² 𝒟₂/(45π Mₚ²)] ln³(ω₀/(mₑϵₘᵢₙ))
- The electric dipole bound-free cross section is σ_bf^(E1) = [416 ζ₃ (η₁η₂)² 𝒟₁/(105 β² Mₚ²)] (η₁³+η₂³) ln(ω₀/(mₑϵₘᵢₙ))
- The electric quadrupole bound-free cross section is σ_bf^(E2) = [104 ζ₃ (η₁η₂)² 𝒟₂/(175 Mₚ²)] (η₁³+η₂³) ln(ω₀/(mₑϵₘᵢₙ))
- For iron on copper, 𝒟₁ = 0.8 × 10⁻⁴; for gold on silver, 𝒟₁ = 1.2 × 10⁻³; for lead on copper, 𝒟₁ = 4 × 10⁻³; for gold on lead, 𝒟₁ = 3 × 10⁻⁵; while 𝒟₂ ≈ 1
- The threshold velocity below which both mechanisms are exponentially suppressed is β₀ = [mₑ(η₁+η₂)/(2Mₚ)]^(1/3)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of e⁺e⁻ pair production in nonrelativistic nuclear collisions beyond the electric dipole contribution calculated by Lifshitz (1935) and Okun (1955), and beyond the two-photon mechanism with Coulomb corrections computed by Lee and Milstein (2016). The earlier two-photon results (Lee and Milstein, 2016) showed β⁶ suppression at low velocities, while the electric dipole contribution contains the small factor 𝒟₁ which is typically 10⁻³–10⁻⁵ for medium and heavy nuclei. The present work demonstrates that electric quadrupole radiation, which is parametrically smaller than dipole radiation by β² but has 𝒟₂ ≈ 1, becomes the dominant production mechanism for most nuclear pairs. The results enable quantitative predictions for e⁺e⁻ pair production in low-energy heavy-ion collisions, including both free-free and bound-free channels, with applicability limited to Z < 40 for the bound-free case due to relativistic corrections to the bound electron wave function.
