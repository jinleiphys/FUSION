# Quadrupole radiation and $e^+e^-$ pair production in the collision of nonrelativistic nuclei

> We analyze the one-photon mechanism of $e^+e^-$ pair production in the collision of nonrelativistic nuclei. The contribution of electric quadrupole radiation of virtual photon to the corresponding cross section is found. The effect of the finite nuclear size is considered in detail. A comparison is made with the contribution of electric dipole radiation of virtual photon and with the contribution of the two-photon pair production mechanism. It is shown that the contribution of electric quadrupole radiation is dominant in a wide range of relative velocities. The cross section for the production of $e^+e^-$ pair with the capture of an electron by one of the nuclei is also analyzed.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of eвҒәeвҒ» pair production in nonrelativistic nuclear collisions beyond the electric dipole contribution calculated by Lifshitz (1935) and Okun (1955), and beyond the two-photon mechanism with Coulomb corrections computed by Lee and Milstein (2016). The present work demonstrates that electric quadrupole radiation, which is parametrically smaller than dipole radiation by ОІВІ but has рқ’ҹвӮӮ вүҲ 1, becomes the dominant production mechanism for most nuclear pairs. The results enable quantitative predictions for eвҒәeвҒ» pair production in low-energy heavy-ion collisions, including both free-free and bound-free channels, with applicability limited to Z < 40 for the bound-free case due to relativistic corrections to the bound electron wave function.
