# πN scattering in relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory revisited

> We have analyzed pion-nucleon scattering using the manifestly relativistic covariant framework of Infrared Regularization up to {\cal O}(q^3) in the chiral expansion, where q is a generic small momentum. We describe the low-energy phase shifts with a similar quality as previously achieved with Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory, \sqrt{s}\lesssim1.14 GeV. New values are provided for the {\cal O}(q^2) and {\cal O}(q^3) low-energy constants, which are compared with previous determinations. This is also the case for the scattering lengths and volumes. Finally, we have unitarized the previous amplitudes and as a result the energy range where data are reproduced increases significantly.

## Key claim
The authors show that Infrared Regularization (IR) baryon chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) at O(q^3) can describe low-energy pion-nucleon phase shifts up to sqrt(s) less than or about 1.14 GeV with quality similar to Heavy Baryon CHPT, contradicting earlier pessimistic conclusions by Becher and Leutwyler (1999) that IR CHPT at this order was restricted to very low center-of-mass pion kinetic energy (less than 40 MeV). However, the caveat about a large violation (20-30 percent) of the Goldberger-Treiman relation in a full IR calculation at O(q^3) remains.

## Method
The authors performed a manifestly relativistic covariant calculation of pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes using Infrared Regularization up to O(q^3) in the chiral expansion, re-evaluating all one-loop diagrams independently. They fitted the resulting low-energy constants (c_i and d_i) to the Karlsruhe (KA85) and GWU (WI08) partial wave analyses of pi N phase shifts for S- and P-waves, using a chi-squared minimization with systematic and relative errors. They also unitarized the perturbative amplitudes using Unitary CHPT (UCHPT) to extend the energy range where data are reproduced.

## Key numbers
- The fit reproduces KA85 phase shifts with chi-squared per degree of freedom below 1 for sqrt(s)_max less than or about 1.13 GeV, rising to 2.1 at sqrt(s)_max = 1.14 GeV and 3.6 at sqrt(s)_max = 1.15 GeV.
- The low-energy constants from the KA85-1 fit: c_1 = -0.71 +/- 0.49 GeV^{-1}, c_2 = 4.32 +/- 0.27 GeV^{-1}, c_3 = -6.53 +/- 0.33 GeV^{-1}, c_4 = 3.87 +/- 0.15 GeV^{-1}, with d_1+d_2 = 2.48 +/- 0.59 GeV^{-2}, d_3 = -2.68 +/- 1.02 GeV^{-2}, d_5 = 2.69 +/- 2.20 GeV^{-2}, d_14-d_15 = -1.71 +/- 0.73 GeV^{-2}, d_18 = -0.26 +/- 0.40 GeV^{-2} at renormalization scale lambda = 1 GeV.
- The numerical inputs used were F_pi = 92.4 MeV, M_pi = 139 MeV, m_N = 939 MeV, g_A = 1.267, and mu = m_N.
- The inelastic threshold from one-pion production occurs at |p| about 210 MeV in the center-of-mass frame.

## Context
This work extends the O(q^3) Heavy Baryon CHPT calculations of Fettes and Meissner (2000) to the manifestly covariant IR framework, and contradicts the pessimistic conclusions of Becher and Leutwyler (1999) who found IR CHPT at O(q^3) described data only below 40 MeV pion kinetic energy. The authors also compare with the O(q^4) IR calculation of Becher and Leutwyler (2001) and with dispersive analyses by Buttiker et al. (2004). The unitarized amplitudes enable reproduction of data up to significantly higher energies, including the Delta(1232) resonance region via Castillejo-Dalitz-Dyson poles, providing a framework for future higher-order calculations and improved determinations of pion-nucleon scattering lengths.
