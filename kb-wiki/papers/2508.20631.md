# Electromagnetic properties of heavy-light mesons

> Within the Bethe-Salpeter framework, we present a computation of space-like electromagnetic form factors for pseudoscalar mesons, including light and heavy-light systems. Our approach employs a flavour-dependent variation of the standard Taylor effective charge, which contains key contributions from the quark-gluon vertices. This effective interaction is a common ingredient of all relevant dynamical equations, and accommodates the crucial mass differences between the various quark flavours. Particular attention is paid to the nonperturbative determination of the quark-photon vertex. The computed electromagnetic form factors for the pion and the kaon mesons show excellent agreement with experimental determinations. In addition, the predictions for the charge radii of heavy-light systems are in overall good agreement with lattice QCD.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a flavour-dependent effective interaction, which incorporates quark-gluon vertex contributions into the Bethe-Salpeter framework, produces electromagnetic form factors for light mesons in excellent agreement with experiment and charge radii for heavy-light mesons in good agreement with lattice QCD, but only when the axial Ward-Takahashi identity violation from flavour-asymmetric kernels is quantified and found to be small (maximum ~9% error in constituent quark masses for the u-b case).

## Method
The authors solve the quark gap equation, homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states, and the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the quark-photon vertex, all using a common flavour-dependent effective interaction kernel. The quark-photon vertex is decomposed into a longitudinal Ball-Chiu part that satisfies the Ward-Takahashi identity and a transverse part expanded in an eight-dimensional basis, with both components computed nonperturbatively. Electromagnetic form factors are then obtained via the impulse approximation, where the photon couples to each quark in the meson through the fully-dressed quark-photon vertex and Bethe-Salpeter amplitude.

## Key numbers
- Current quark masses fixed at: m_u/d = 0.005 GeV, m_s = 0.094 GeV, m_c = 1.1 GeV, m_b = 3.5 GeV, at renormalization point μ = 4.3 GeV
- The D meson mass computed using off-diagonal kernels yields 1.99 GeV, compared to 1.93 GeV from diagonal kernels in prior work (Gao 2024)
- Maximum error in quark wave-function A(p²) for u-b flavour combination is slightly over 3%
- Maximum discrepancy in constituent quark masses for u-b flavour combination is about 9%
- The momentum partitioning parameter η varies by meson: η_us = 0.47, η_cb = 0.41, η_sc = 0.38, η_sb = 0.25, η_cd = 0.24, η_ub = 0.16

## Context
This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter framework for meson form factors, previously applied extensively to light mesons by Maris (1999), Eichmann (2019), and Miramontes (2021), to include heavy-light systems such as D, B, D_s, and B_s mesons. It implements the flavour-dependent interaction kernel developed by Gao (2024) that introduces mass-dependence via quark wave-functions, enabling treatment of asymmetric quark masses. The results enable future comparison with upcoming experimental data from Jefferson Lab (Accardi 2023), the Electron-Ion Collider, and the Electron-Ion Collider in China (Anderle 2021), and provide benchmarks for lattice QCD calculations of heavy-light meson structure.
