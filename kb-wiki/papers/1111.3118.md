# Four-nucleon contact interactions from holographic QCD

> We calculate the low energy constants of four-nucleon interactions in an effective chiral Lagrangian in holographic QCD. We start with a D4-D8 model to obtain meson-nucleon interactions and then integrate out massive mesons to obtain the four-nucleon interactions in 4D. We end up with two low energy constants at the leading order and seven of them at the next leading order, which is consistent with the effective chiral Lagrangian. The values of the low energy constants are evaluated with the first five Kaluza-Klein resonances.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the low-energy constants (LECs) of four-nucleon contact interactions in an effective chiral Lagrangian using the D4/D8 holographic QCD model, finding two LECs at leading order and seven at next-to-leading order, with the values evaluated using the first five Kaluza-Klein (KK) resonances; the results are consistent with the effective chiral Lagrangian but depend on the holographic model's input parameters (the 't Hooft coupling \(\lambda\) and the KK scale \(M_{KK}\)).

## Method

The authors start from a D4-D8 holographic QCD model (Sakai-Sugimoto model) with explicit bulk baryon fields to derive a 4D meson-nucleon Lagrangian. They then integrate out massive mesons (isosinglet \(\omega\) and \(f_1\) mesons, and isotriplet \(\rho\) and \(a_1\) mesons) to obtain relativistic four-nucleon contact interactions, which are subsequently reduced to non-relativistic form using a derivative expansion up to \(Q^2\) order. The LECs are computed from the overlap integrals of the meson and baryon profile functions, summing contributions from the first five Kaluza-Klein modes.

## Key numbers

- The model yields two LECs at leading order (\(Q^0\)) and seven LECs at next-to-leading order (\(Q^2\)), consistent with the effective chiral Lagrangian of Weinberg (1990) and the operator counting of Epelbaum et al. (2001).
- The nucleon mass \(m_{\cal N}\) is approximately 0.94 GeV, and the pion decay constant \(f_\pi \sim 93\) MeV, requiring the 't Hooft coupling \(\lambda = g_{YM}^2 N_c \simeq 17\) for \(N_c=3\).
- The \(\eta'\) mass is given by \(m_{\eta'}^2 = \frac{1}{27\pi^2} \frac{N_f}{N_c} \lambda^2 M_{KK}^2\).
- The baryon size parameter is \(\rho_{\text{baryon}}^2 \simeq \frac{(2 \cdot 3^7 \cdot \pi^2 / 5)^{1/2}}{M_{KK}^2 \lambda}\).
- The cubic couplings to mesons are expressed via overlap integrals \(A_n\), \(B_n\), and \(C_n\) (defined in Eq. (3.3) of the paper), with specific relations for vector and axial-vector couplings (e.g., \(g_V^{(k)\text{triplet}} = A_{2k-1} + 2C_{2k-1}\), \(g_A^{(k)\text{triplet}} = 2C_{2k} + A_{2k}\)).

## Context

This work extends the resonance saturation approach of Epelbaum et al. (2001) by computing the LECs from a top-down holographic QCD model (the D4/D8 Sakai-Sugimoto model) rather than fitting to experimental data or using phenomenological models. The results provide a first-principles estimate of the short-range four-nucleon contact interactions, which are essential for describing nuclear forces in chiral effective field theory (Weinberg, 1990) and for understanding bulk nuclear matter properties (e.g., the Walecka model connection discussed by the authors). This enables future comparisons with lattice QCD calculations and phenomenological determinations of the LECs.
