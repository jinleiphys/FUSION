# Matching the quark model to the 1/Nc expansion

> We compute the coefficients of the effective mass operator of the 1/Nc expansion for negative parity L=1 excited baryons using the Isgur-Karl model in order to compare the general approach, where the coefficients are obtained by fitting to data, with a specific constituent quark model calculation. We discuss the physics behind the fitted coefficients for the scalar part of the most general two-body quark-quark interaction. We find that both pion exchange and gluon exchange lead to the dominance of the same operator at the level of the effective mass operator, which is also observed from data.

# Wiki Page: Matching the Quark Model to the 1/N_c Expansion

## Key claim
The coefficients of the effective mass operator in the 1/N_c expansion for negative parity L=1 excited baryons, when computed using the Isgur-Karl model, show that both pion exchange and gluon exchange lead to dominance of the same operator (S_c^2) at the level of the effective mass operator, but this equivalence holds strictly only for contact (zero-range) scalar interactions; for pion exchange the vanishing of one coefficient (tilde c_1) holds regardless of the spatial range of the interaction.

## Method
The authors computed the coefficients of the effective mass operator in the 1/N_c expansion by matching the Isgur-Karl (IK) model Hamiltonian, which contains a hyperfine interaction approximating gluon exchange, onto the operator basis used in the 1/N_c expansion for excited baryons. They derived the mass operator for the IK model with a general confining potential (IK-V(r)) in terms of three unknown orbital overlap integrals, then evaluated these integrals explicitly for the harmonic oscillator case. They also considered the most general two-body quark Hamiltonian with scalar contact interactions of three types (color-color, spin-spin, and spin-flavor) and matched each onto the 1/N_c operator basis, then compared the resulting operator coefficients to values extracted from experimental baryon masses and mixing angles.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of fitted coefficients from experimental data: tilde c_1 / tilde c_2 = 0.03 ± 0.09, indicating suppression of tilde c_1 relative to tilde c_2.
- Individual fitted coefficients from masses and mixing angles: tilde c_1 = 3.9 ± 11.0 MeV, tilde c_2 = 129 ± 18 MeV.
- Mixing angles used for the fit: (theta_N1, theta_N3) = (0.39 ± 0.11 rad, 2.82 ± 0.11 rad) = (22° ± 6°, 162° ± 6°).
- In the harmonic oscillator IK model, the entire L=1 spectroscopy is fixed by a single constant delta = M_Delta - M_N ~ 300 MeV, with coefficients a = 1/2 delta, b = 1/20 delta, c = -1/5 delta.

## Context
This work extends the matching procedure between quark models and the 1/N_c expansion developed by Pirjol and Schat (2007) and applied to the Isgur-Karl model by Galeta et al. (2009). It builds on prior 1/N_c expansion studies of excited baryons by Goity (1996), Carlson (1998), and Schat (2001), and on the Isgur-Karl model (Isgur 1977). The results enable a direct comparison between the operator coefficients obtained from fitting experimental data and those predicted by specific quark model interactions, showing that a mixture of pion exchange and gluon exchange is compatible with data and supporting the physical picture of Manohar (1983) and Weinberg (2010).
