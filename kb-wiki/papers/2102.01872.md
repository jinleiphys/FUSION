# Parton collisional effect on the conversion of geometry eccentricities into momentum anisotropies in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We explore parton collisional effects on the conversion of geometry eccentricities into azimuthal anisotropies in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV using a multi-phase transport model. The initial eccentricity $\varepsilon_{n}$ (n = 2,3) and flow harmonics $v_{n}$ (n = 2,3) are investigated as a function of the number of parton collisions ($N_{coll}$) during the source evolution of partonic phase. It is found that partonic collisions leads to generate elliptic flow $v_{2}$ and triangular flow $v_{3}$ in Pb+Pb collisions. On the other hand, partonic collisions also result in an evolution of the eccentricity of geometry. The collisional effect on the flow conversion efficiency is therefore studied. We find that the partons with larger $N_{coll}$ show a lower flow conversion efficiency, which reflect differential behaviors with respect to $N_{coll}$. It provides an additional insight into the dynamics of the space-momentum transformation during the QGP evolution from a transport model point of view.

## Key claim

Partons with a larger number of parton-parton collisions (\(N_{coll}\)) show a lower flow conversion efficiency (the ratio of the change in flow harmonics to the change in eccentricity), which reflects differential behaviors with respect to \(N_{coll}\); however, the authors note that small-\(N_{coll}\) and large-\(N_{coll}\) partons are complemented with each other during evolution, making it hard to rank their roles in generating flow.

## Method

The authors used the string-melting version of the AMPT (a multi-phase transport) model to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 5.02 TeV, with a parton-parton elastic scattering cross section set to 3 mb. They traced the collisional history of each parton to define its number of parton-parton collisions (\(N_{coll}\)) before freeze-out, and then calculated the initial and final partonic eccentricities (\(\varepsilon_n\)) and flow harmonics (\(v_n\)) as functions of \(N_{coll}\) using both the participant-plane method and multi-particle cumulant methods.

## Key numbers

- The average number of parton collisions \(\langle N_{coll} \rangle\) in 0–10% centrality is roughly three times as large as that in 40–60% centrality.
- For 0–10% most central collisions, a peak in the \(N_{coll}\) probability distribution is observed around \(N_{coll} \sim 6\).
- For 0–10% centrality, a maximum value of \(v_2^f\) (elliptic flow of freezeout partons) is observed around \(N_{coll} \sim 5\).
- The ordering of elliptic flow values from different methods is \(v_2\{2\} > v_2\{\text{part}\} > v_2\{4\}\).
- The conversion efficiency \(\Delta v_n / \Delta \varepsilon_n\) (n=2,3) is strongest in the 0–10% centrality class.

## Context

This work extends studies of the relationship between initial geometric eccentricity and final flow anisotropy in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, which have been explored using hydrodynamic models (e.g., Heinz 2013, Gale 2013) and the AMPT model (e.g., Chen 2004, Lin 2005). It builds on the idea that partonic interactions are essential for generating flow (HeL 2016, Lin 2015) and that the participant eccentricity implies testable predictions for flow (Ma 2011, Ma 2016). The differential analysis by number of parton collisions provides a new transport-model perspective on the space-momentum transformation during QGP evolution, and the authors suggest that further study tracing hadronization and hadronic evolution would be needed for a complete understanding.
