# Proton drip-line nuclei in Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory

> Ground-state properties of spherical even-even nuclei $14\leq Z \leq 28$ and $N=18,20,22$ are described in the framework of Relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov (RHB) theory. The model uses the NL3 effective interaction in the mean-field Lagrangian, and describes pairing correlations by the pairing part of the finite range Gogny interaction D1S. Binding energies, two-proton separation energies, and proton $rms$ radii that result from fully self-consistent RHB solutions are compared with experimental data. The model predicts the location of the proton drip-line. The isospin dependence of the effective spin-orbit potential is discussed, as well as pairing properties that result from the finite range interaction in the $pp$ channel.

# Proton drip-line nuclei in Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory

## Key claim

The Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theory with the NL3 effective interaction in the mean-field channel and the finite-range Gogny D1S interaction in the pairing channel provides a good description of ground-state properties of spherical even-even proton-rich nuclei with 14 ≤ Z ≤ 28 and N = 18, 20, 22, though the model shows larger deviations for N = Z nuclei such as ³⁶Ar and ⁴⁴Ti, suggesting that additional correlations (particularly proton-neutron pairing) not included in the model may be important for these cases.

## Method

The authors solved the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equations self-consistently by expanding nucleon spinors and meson fields in a basis of spherical harmonic oscillators for N = 20 oscillator shells, using the NL3 parameter set for the mean-field Lagrangian and the pairing part of the finite-range Gogny interaction D1S in the particle-particle channel. For nuclei at the proton drip-line, coordinate-space RHB calculations were also performed to verify the predictions. The model was applied to spherical even-even nuclei with 14 ≤ Z ≤ 28 and N = 18, 20, 22, and results for binding energies, two-proton separation energies, and proton rms radii were compared with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies for ²⁶Si: calculated 269.02 MeV vs. empirical 271.41 MeV; for ⁴⁰Ca: calculated 341.99 MeV vs. empirical 342.05 MeV; for ⁵⁰Ni: calculated 385.52 MeV vs. empirical 385.50 MeV (Table I)
- The reduction of the spin-orbit term V_s.o. from ³⁴Si to ⁴⁸Ni is approximately 20% in the surface region for the N = 20 isotones
- The Coulomb barrier increases from 3 MeV for ³⁴Si to 6 MeV for ⁴⁸Ni in the N = 20 isotones
- Average proton pairing gaps at the Fermi surface for ⁴⁴Cr are between 1.5 and 2 MeV
- The average proton pairing gap for the 1f₇/₂ orbital is approximately 2.5 MeV for N = 22 isotones
- The model predicts positive Fermi levels (λ > 0) for ⁴²Cr, ⁴⁶Fe, and ⁵⁰Ni, even though the calculated two-proton separation energies for ⁴⁶Fe and ⁵⁰Ni are positive

## Context

This work extends the application of Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory, previously used for neutron-rich nuclei and stable isotopes, to the proton-rich side of the nuclear chart for the first time. The results are compared with non-relativistic Hartree-Fock and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations by Nazarewicz (1996) and with shell-model calculations by Ormand (1996), finding agreement on the location of the proton drip-line between ⁴²Cr and ⁴⁴Cr, ⁴⁴Fe and ⁴⁶Fe, and ⁴⁸Ni and ⁵⁰Ni. The study enables more reliable predictions for two-proton radioactivity candidates in the 1f₇/₂ region, particularly around ⁴⁸Ni, and demonstrates that the NL3 effective interaction, previously validated for stable and neutron-rich nuclei, also performs well on the proton-rich side.
