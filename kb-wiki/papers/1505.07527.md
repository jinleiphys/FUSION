# Electric Characteristics of Rotational States positive parity in isotopes 170,172,174Yb

> Accounting for Coriolis mixing of experimentally known rotational bands with $K^π< 3^+$, non-adiabatic effects in energy and electric characteristics of excited states are investigated, within phenomenological model. The energy and wave function structure of excited states are calculated. The finding reveals that the bands mixing has been found to have considerable impact on the wave function of low-lying states $0^+$ and $2^+$ bands. In addition, the probabilities of $E2$-- transitions have been calculated. The values from calculations of $B(E2)$-- transitions from $0_2^+$, $0_3^+$, $2_1^+$, and $2_2^+$ bands are compared with the experimental data.

# Electric Characteristics of Rotational States positive parity in isotopes 170,172,174Yb

## Key claim

Coriolis mixing of all experimentally known rotational bands with K^π < 3^+ produces non-adiabatic effects in the energies and electric characteristics of low-lying states in 170,172,174Yb, but the model shows noticeable deviations from experimental energies for spin values I ≥ 12 ħ, and the authors restrict their electromagnetic analysis to states with I < 10 ħ.

## Method

The authors employ a phenomenological Hamiltonian that includes Coriolis mixing between rotational bands with K^π = 0^+, 1^+, and 2^+ in the isotopes 170Yb, 172Yb, and 174Yb. The rotational part of the Hamiltonian uses Harris parameterization with two inertia parameters ℑ₀ and ℑ₁, determined from experimental ground-band energies up to spin I ≤ 8 ħ. Bandhead energies and Coriolis matrix elements (j_x) between bands are fitted to experimental energy spectra separately for positive and negative signature states. Using the resulting wave functions, reduced E2 transition probabilities B(E2) are calculated and compared with experimental data and with predictions from the rotational-vibrational model (RVM2) and the IBA-1 model.

## Key numbers

- For 170Yb, the basis includes n = 25 rotational bands: m = 5 bands with K^π = 0_m^+, ℓ = 1 band with K^π = 2_ℓ^+, and ν = 19 bands with K^π = 1_ν^+.
- For 172Yb, the basis includes n = 15 rotational bands: m = 5, ℓ = 2, and ν = 8.
- For 174Yb, the basis includes n = 22 rotational bands: m = 5, ℓ = 2, and ν = 15.
- Intrinsic quadrupole moments used: Q₀ = 780(4) e·fm² for 170Yb, 791(4) e·fm² for 172Yb, and 782(4) e·fm² for 174Yb.
- For 172Yb, the reduced matrix element for the transition 20₁ → 00₁ is 2.45(12) e·b (experimental), used to normalize model calculations.
- For 170Yb, the B(E2) value for the transition 22₁ → 00₁ is 151(35) e²·fm⁴ (experimental) versus 90 e²·fm⁴ (theory).
- For 172Yb, the B(E2) value for the transition 22₁ → 00₁ is 75.6(63) e²·fm⁴ (experimental) versus 82 e²·fm⁴ (theory).
- For 174Yb, the B(E2) value for the transition 00₂ → 20₁ is 81(+64/-29) e²·fm⁴ (experimental) versus 64 e²·fm⁴ (theory).

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological model of Usmanov (2010) that accounts for Coriolis mixing of rotational bands in deformed nuclei, applying it specifically to the ytterbium isotopes 170,172,174Yb which have been experimentally studied via radioactive decay of 170,172,174Lu and various nuclear reactions (Baglin; Singh; Browne). The calculations are motivated by the discovery of a collective isovector magnetic dipole mode (Bohle; Zilges) and the need to understand how K^π = 1^+ states influence low-lying level properties. The results are compared with the rotational-vibrational model (RVM2) of Faessler and the IBA-1 model of Arima, and the authors find that their model provides better correspondence with experimental data for reduced E2 matrix elements in 172Yb. The work enables future study of electromagnetic properties of low-lying states with I < 10 ħ, while noting that the model does not account for the influence of rotation on internal nuclear structure at higher spins.
