# Improved determination of the oscillator parameters in nuclei

> The oscillator parameter in nuclei is refitted to reproduce the available charge radius data. As an important improvement, we include the Coulomb term evaluated within the assumption of a uniformly charged sphere, and take into account the symmetry effect induced by the difference between N and Z numbers in a straightforward manner using the conventional parameterization. The Coulomb interaction has repulsive effect, causing the wave functions to extend further toward the nucleus exterior, resulting in an effectively larger oscillator length parameter. The symmetry effect is attractive for protons in neutron-rich nuclei and for neutrons in proton-rich nuclei, and repulsive for the other cases. Therefore, three distinct oscillator parameters are determined: one for protons, one for neutrons, and one isospin-invariant version, which is obtained by subtracting the Coulomb and symmetry contributions. Additionally, we explore the direct fit of the harmonic oscillator wave functions to the eigenfunctions of the Hartree-Fock mean field using the Skyrme interaction. Generally, this method agrees well with the others for light nuclei, typically up to $^{40}$Ca. Beyond this nucleus, however, the results begin to diverge over the orbits chosen for the fit. Only the parameters values obtained for the last occupied states agree remarkably well with the conventional ones throughout the mass range under consideration.

## Key claim
The authors refit the harmonic oscillator length parameter to reproduce charge radius data for 797 nuclei, explicitly including Coulomb repulsion (modeled via a uniformly charged sphere) and a symmetry term proportional to \((N-Z)/A\), yielding three distinct parameter sets (proton, neutron, and isospin-invariant). The abstract's claim of "improved determination" is hedged by the finding that the direct fit of oscillator wave functions to Skyrme-Hartree-Fock eigenfunctions (Method III) agrees with the conventional charge-radius fit only for light nuclei up to \(^{40}\)Ca, and beyond that the results diverge depending on which single-particle orbit is chosen for the fit.

## Method
The authors first fit the proton oscillator length parameter \(b_\pi^2\) to experimental charge radii (797 nuclei, \(A=2\) to 248, from Angeli and Marinova (2013)) using Eq. (1) with five corrective terms (center-of-mass, Darwin-Foldy, spin-orbit, single-proton and single-neutron mean-square radii). The neutron parameter \(b_\nu^2\) is then derived via the neutron skin thickness \(\Delta r_{np}\), calculated from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov with Skyrme interactions (SLY4, SLY5, SKM*, SGII, SII–SIV, UNEDF0–UNEDF2). In Method II, Coulomb and symmetry terms are added to the oscillator Hamiltonian, yielding effective frequencies \(\omega_{t_z}\) and an isospin-invariant \(b^2\). Method III directly fits harmonic oscillator radial wave functions to spherical Skyrme-Hartree-Fock eigenfunctions by maximizing the overlap integral \(\Omega_{nlj}^{t_z}\), focusing on \(s_{1/2}\) states of doubly magic \(N=Z\) nuclei.

## Key numbers
- Proton squared length parameter: \(b_\pi^2 = 0.214(20) + 1.034(5) A^{1/3} - 1.554(144) I + 5.634(544) I^2\) fm\(^2\), with \(\chi^2/\nu = 48.869\) (1068 data points, 4 parameters).
- Neutron squared length parameter: \(b_\nu^2 = 0.626(19) + 0.903(4) A^{1/3} - 1.274(119) I + 2.679(442) I^2\) fm\(^2\), with \(\chi^2/\nu = 0.861\).
- Isospin-invariant squared length parameter: \(b^2 = 0.846(12) + 0.760(5) A^{1/3} + 1.688(236) I + 12.759(1060) I^2\) fm\(^2\), with \(\chi^2/\nu = 6.853\) and \(\nu = 1064\).
- Neutron skin thickness fit: \(\Delta r_{np} = -0.032(2) + 0.808(41) I + 0.598(207) I^2 \pm 0.05_L\) fm, with \(\chi^2/\nu = 1.616\) (317 nuclei, \(A \ge 10\)).
- Symmetry parameter adopted: \(\kappa = 0.75(12)\), from global fits of Woods-Saxon parameter sets.
- For \(^{40}\)Ca, Method III yields \(b_\nu^2 = 4.02(20)\) fm\(^2\) for the \(0s_{1/2}\) state (overlap 0.99936) versus Method I value \(b_\nu^2 = 3.714(23)\) fm\(^2\); for \(^{208}\)Pb, \(b_\nu^2 = 8.34(12)\) fm\(^2\) (\(0s_{1/2}\)) versus Method I value \(5.827(44)\) fm\(^2\).

## Context
This work extends the earlier parameterizations of Blomqvist and Molinari (1968) and Kirson (2007) by explicitly including Coulomb and symmetry terms in the oscillator Hamiltonian, and by using an updated charge radius compilation (Angeli and Marinova, 2013) plus newer data from Li (2021) and Ohayon (2022). The neutron skin thickness is calculated from Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov rather than from empirical antiproton or hadron-scattering formulas used by Kirson (2007). The results enable shell-model calculations to use distinct proton and neutron oscillator parameters when isospin-symmetry breaking is considered, or a single isospin-invariant parameter for conventional calculations; the Method III comparison highlights that for heavy nuclei the oscillator parameter fitted to charge radii may not match the one that best reproduces Skyrme-Hartree-Fock single-particle wave functions.
