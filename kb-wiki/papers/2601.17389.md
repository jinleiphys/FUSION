# Dynamical study of $πN\to ππN$ reactions revisited

> Using the Argonne National Laboratory-The University of Osaka (ANL-Osaka) DCC model of meson-nucleon reactions, we extend the study of Phys. Rev. C 79, 025206 (2009) and Phys. Rev. C 88, 045203 (2013) to predict the cross sections of the $πN \to ππN$ reactions. The model was constructed by fitting only the two-body reactions: $πN,γN \to πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ$. Thus, the results for $πN \to ππN$ presented here are predictions of the ANL-Osaka DCC model, which serve to examine the extent to which the forthcoming data from J-PARC can be described. This study provides information for improving the extraction of nucleon resonances that have large decay widths to $ππN$ states. We present results for the total cross sections, invariant mass distributions, and angular distributions. We also identify the observables and energy regions where the higher mass nucleon resonances in the $S_{31}$, $P_{33}$, $D_{33}$, $F_{37}$, $D_{13}$, $D_{15}$, and $F_{15}$ partial waves can be most effectively investigated.

## Key claim
The ANL-Osaka DCC model, fitted only to two-body reactions (πN, γN → πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ), provides predictions for πN → ππN cross sections that show reasonable agreement with existing data at low energies but significantly overestimate data above W ∼ 1.65 GeV, indicating that the πΔ, ρN, and σN couplings to nucleon resonances are poorly constrained by single-meson production data alone.

## Method
The authors employed the 2016 ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channel (DCC) model, which solves coupled-channel equations for partial-wave amplitudes including seven reaction channels (πN, ηN, KΛ, KΣ, πΔ, ρN, σN). The T-matrix for πN → ππN was decomposed into direct (T^dir) and resonant contributions from intermediate πΔ, ρN, and σN states, with the two-body amplitudes T_{MB,πN} computed from the partial-wave amplitudes. The sensitivity of observables to nucleon resonance couplings was investigated by uniformly scaling the dressed vertices for specific partial waves by factors of 0.5 and 1.5.

## Key numbers
- Total cross sections for π⁺p → π⁺π⁺n show good agreement with data up to W = 2 GeV
- For π⁺p → π⁺π⁰p, π⁻p → π⁺π⁻n, π⁻p → π⁰π⁰n, and π⁻p → π⁻π⁰p, predictions significantly overestimate data above W ∼ 1.65 GeV
- The upcoming J-PARC experiment plans beam momenta of 0.98–1.15 GeV/c, corresponding to W = 1.66–1.76 GeV
- A cusp structure appears at W ∼ 1.68 GeV in the π⁻p → π⁻π⁰p total cross section, attributed to KΣ channel opening
- The P₃₃ partial wave dominates π⁺p → π⁺π⁺n from threshold to W = 1.4 GeV; S₃₁ and D₃₃ dominate both π⁺p reactions in the W = 1.5–1.75 GeV region; F₃₇ takes over above W = 1.8 GeV
- For π⁻p reactions between W = 1.5 and 1.75 GeV, D₁₃ and D₁₅ are dominant in all three charged states, with F₁₅ also dominant for π⁺π⁻n and π⁰π⁰n final states
- The P₁₁ partial wave dominates π⁻p → π⁺π⁻n and π⁻p → π⁰π⁰n up to W ∼ 1.4 GeV (Roper resonance region)
- HADES data at W = 1487 MeV (p_in = 685 MeV/c) show significant deviations from predictions for π⁻p → π⁺π⁻n invariant mass distributions

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Kamano and Lee (2009, 2013) using the updated 2016 ANL-Osaka DCC model, which includes γn → πN data in its fit. The predictions are intended to guide the upcoming J-PARC experiment, which will measure π⁺p → π⁺π⁺n, π⁺p → π⁺π⁰p, π⁻p → π⁺π⁻n, and π⁻p → π⁻π⁰p reactions. The results demonstrate that incorporating πN → ππN data into coupled-channel analyses is essential for resolving ambiguities in nucleon resonance parameters, particularly for high-mass states where two-pion production dominates πN reaction cross sections. The study contrasts with photoproduction, where the Roper resonance makes minor contributions, showing that πN → ππN reactions offer crucial insights into high-mass N* states and the elusive Roper resonance.
