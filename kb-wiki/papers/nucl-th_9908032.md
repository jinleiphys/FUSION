# Deuteron formation in nuclear matter

> We investigate deuteron formation in nuclear matter at finite temperatures within a systematic quantum statistical approach. We consider formation through three-body collisions relevant already at rather moderate densities because of the strong correlations. The three-body in-medium reaction rates driven by the break-up cross section are calculated using exact three-body equations (Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas type) that have been suitably modified to consistently include the energy shift and the Pauli blocking. Important quantities are the lifetime of deuteron fluctuations and the chemical relaxation time. We find that the respective times differ substantially while using in-medium or isolated cross sections. We expect implications for the description of heavy ion collisions in particular for the formation of light charged particles at low to intermediate energies.

## Key claim

Deuteron formation in nuclear matter proceeds via three-body collisions whose in-medium break-up cross sections, calculated from exact three-body equations modified to include energy shifts and Pauli blocking, differ substantially from isolated cross sections; this leads to shorter reaction time scales for deuteron fluctuations and chemical equilibration, particularly at moderate densities and low energies.

## Method

The authors used a cluster mean-field approach within quantum statistics to derive effective one-, two-, and three-body Green functions at finite temperature, then solved the three-body problem using Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) type equations modified to include medium effects via Pauli blocking factors and self-energy shifts. The in-medium break-up transition operator was computed with a rank-one Yamaguchi potential for the nucleon-nucleon interaction, and the resulting cross sections were used to evaluate the deuteron break-up lifetime and chemical relaxation time from linearized Boltzmann equations.

## Key numbers

- At nucleon density n = 0.007 fm⁻³ and temperature T = 10 MeV, the deuteron break-up time for small deuteron momenta is shorter by a factor of three when using the in-medium cross section compared to the isolated cross section.
- The in-medium break-up cross section is significantly enhanced relative to the isolated cross section at small center-of-mass momenta and small relative energies of the dN-system.
- The threshold for break-up shifts to smaller energies with increasing density due to the weakening of deuteron binding energy (Mott effect).

## Context

This work extends the quantum statistical approach of Beyer (1996, 1997) by providing explicit numerical solutions of the in-medium three-body equations for finite temperatures, and it contrasts with earlier work by Danielewicz and Bertsch (1991) that used experimental cross sections with only final-state Pauli blocking. The results enable improved microscopic simulations of heavy ion collisions, such as Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) or quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) approaches, by providing medium-dependent cross sections for deuteron formation and break-up at low to intermediate energies.
