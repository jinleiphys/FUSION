# Three-body force effect on nucleon momentum distributions in asymmetric nuclear matter within the framework of the extended BHF approach

> We have investigated the three-body force (TBF) effect on the neutron and proton momentum distributions in asymmetric nuclear matter within the framework of the extended Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach by adopting the $AV18$ two-body interaction plus a microscopic TBF. In asymmetric nuclear matter, it is shown that the neutron and proton momentum distributions become different from their common distribution in symmetric nuclear matter. The predicted depletion of the proton hole states increases while the neutron one decreases as a function of isospin-asymmetry. The TBF effect on the neutron and proton momentum distributions turns out to be negligibly weak at low densities around and below the normal nuclear density. The TBF effect is found to become sizable only at high densities well above the saturation density, and inclusion of the TBF leads to an overall enhancement of the depletion of the neutron and proton Fermi seas.

## Key claim

The three-body force (TBF) effect on neutron and proton momentum distributions in asymmetric nuclear matter is negligibly weak at densities around and below normal nuclear density, and becomes sizable only at high densities well above saturation density, where inclusion of the TBF leads to an overall enhancement of the depletion of both neutron and proton Fermi seas.

## Method

The authors used the extended Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (EBHF) approach with the Argonne AV18 two-body interaction plus a microscopic three-body force constructed via the meson-exchange current approach. The TBF was reduced to an effective two-body interaction, and the nucleon momentum distributions were calculated from the off-energy-shell mass operator including second-order (Pauli rearrangement) and third-order (renormalization) contributions in the hole-line expansion. Calculations were performed for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter at densities of 0.17 fm⁻³ and 0.34 fm⁻³ for various isospin asymmetries β = 0, 0.2, 0.4, 0.6, and 0.8.

## Key numbers

- Depletion of hole states at zero momentum (k=0) in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³ is roughly 15%, consistent with previous predictions and experimental results.
- At high density ρ = 0.34 fm⁻³ (twice saturation density), the TBF effect becomes noticeable and enhances the depletion of both neutron and proton Fermi seas.
- The TBF effect on the iso-depletion (difference between neutron and proton occupation at k=0) is quite small up to ρ = 0.34 fm⁻³.
- Neutron occupation at k=0 increases and proton occupation at k=0 decreases almost linearly with increasing isospin asymmetry β.

## Context

This work extends the previous EBHF calculation of Baldo (1991) for symmetric nuclear matter to asymmetric nuclear matter, and investigates the TBF effect on momentum distributions. The results agree with the Green function method predictions of Rios (2009) regarding the isospin-asymmetry dependence of neutron and proton depletions, and with the correlated basis function results of Fantoni (1984) regarding the negligible TBF effect at low densities. The findings have implications for neutron star cooling via direct URCA processes, nucleon pairing, and transport properties in dense neutron star matter.
