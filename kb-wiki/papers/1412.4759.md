# Limitation of multi-particle correlations for studying the event-by-event distribution of harmonic flow in heavy-ion collisions

> The sensitivity of flow harmonics from cumulants on the event-by-event flow distribution $p(v_n)$ is investigated using a simple central moment expansion approach. For narrow distribution whose width is much smaller than the mean $σ_n\ll < v_n >$, the difference between the first three higher-order cumulant estimates $v_n\{4\}$, $v_n\{6\}$ and $v_n\{8\}$ are not very sensitive to the shape of $p(v_n)$. For broad distribution $σ_n\gtrsim < v_n >$, the higher-order cumulant estimates differ from each other but may change sign and become ill-defined. This sign change arises from the choice of $p(v_n)$, without the need to invoke non-flow effects. Direct extraction of $p(v_n)$ via a data-driven unfolding method is a more preferred approach for flow distribution measurement.

## Key claim

The paper argues that higher-order cumulant estimates \(v_n\{4\}\), \(v_n\{6\}\), and \(v_n\{8\}\) are not very sensitive to the shape of the event-by-event flow distribution \(p(v_n)\) for narrow distributions (\(\sigma_n \ll \langle v_n \rangle\)), and for broad distributions (\(\sigma_n \gtrsim \langle v_n \rangle\)) these estimates may change sign and become ill-defined due to the choice of \(p(v_n)\) itself, without requiring non-flow effects.

## Method

The authors use a central moment expansion approach to express the cumulants \(c_n\{2k\}\) and the corresponding flow harmonics \(v_n\{2k\}\) in terms of the mean \(\langle v_n \rangle\) and reduced central moments \(\theta_j\) of \(p(v_n)\). They then analyze two simple analytic probability distributions (a uniform distribution and a triangular distribution) parameterized by a width parameter \(a\), and compute the behavior of \(v_n\{2k\}\) as the distribution broadens from \(\sigma_n \ll \langle v_n \rangle\) to \(\sigma_n > \langle v_n \rangle\). A toy Monte Carlo simulation with \(M=500\) particles and \(v_2=0.05\) is also used to study event-by-event cumulants.

## Key numbers

* For narrow distributions (\(\sigma_n \ll \langle v_n \rangle\)), the flow harmonics for all higher-order cumulants are approximately \(v_n\{2k\} \approx \langle v_n \rangle (1 - \frac{1}{2}\theta_2)\) for \(k>1\), where \(\theta_2 = \sigma_n^2 / \langle v_n \rangle^2\).
* The difference between \(v_n\{6\}\) and \(v_n\{8\}\) is about one order of magnitude smaller than the difference between \(v_n\{4\}\) and \(v_n\{6\}\) for narrow distributions.
* For the triangular distribution (Eq. 2), \(v_n\{4\}\) becomes negative for \(a \gtrsim 0.84\).
* In the toy simulation with \(M=500\) and \(v_2=0.05\), event-by-event cumulants \(v_2'\{2k\}^2\) are nearly identical to each other and to \(q_2^2\), showing no additional non-flow suppression within a single event.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of flow cumulants beyond the Gaussian or Bessel-Gaussian assumption for \(p(v_n)\), which was previously used to interpret the equality \(v_n\{4\} \approx v_n\{6\} \approx v_n\{8\}\) in A+A collisions (Aad 2014, Bilandzic 2011) and high-multiplicity p+A collisions (Quan) as evidence of a Gaussian distribution. The paper contradicts the interpretation that a sign change of \(c_2\{4\}\) in low-multiplicity events necessarily indicates a transition from non-flow to flow dominance (Aad 2013, Chatrchyan 2013, Abelev 2014). It enables a more critical assessment of cumulant-based flow measurements and advocates for the direct unfolding method used by the ATLAS Collaboration (Aad 2013) as a more robust approach for extracting \(p(v_n)\).
