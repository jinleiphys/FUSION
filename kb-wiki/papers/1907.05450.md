# Correlations between azimuthal anisotropy Fourier harmonics in PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\mathrm{NN}}}}=2.76$ TeV in the HYDJET++ and AMPT models

> Correlations between azimuthal anisotropy Fourier harmonics $v_{n}$ ($n = 2, 3, 4$) are studied using the events from PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\mathrm{NN}}}}=2.76$ TeV generated by the HYDJET++ and AMPT models, and compared to the corresponding experimental results obtained by the ATLAS Collaboration. The Fourier harmonics $v_{n}$ are measured over a wide centrality range using the two-particle azimuthal correlation method. The slopes of the $v_{2}$--$v_{3}$ correlation from both models are in a good agreement with the ATLAS data. The HYDJET++ model predicts a stronger slope for the $v_{2}$--$v_{4}$ and $v_{3}$--$v_{4}$ correlations than the ones experimentally measured, while the results from the AMPT model are in a rather good agreement with the experimental results. In contrast to the HYDJET++ predictions, the AMPT model predicts a boomerang-like shape in the structure of the correlations as found in the experimental data.

## Key claim

The AMPT model reproduces the experimentally observed boomerang-like shape in the correlations between Fourier harmonics of different orders, while the HYDJET++ model does not; however, the AMPT model's boomerang turn in the v2-v3 correlation is much sharper than the one seen in the ATLAS data.

## Method

The authors simulated approximately 1 million PbPb collision events at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV using the HYDJET++ (version 2.3) and AMPT (version 1.25-2.25) models. They measured the Fourier harmonics v2, v3, and v4 over a wide centrality range (0-70%) using the two-particle azimuthal correlation method, selecting particles with 0.5 < pT < 2 GeV/c and |eta| < 2.5, and requiring |Delta-eta| >= 2 to remove short-range correlations.

## Key numbers

*   The v2 in the HYDJET++ model continuously increases up to 70% centrality, while in the AMPT model it reaches its maximum at 50-60% centrality and then starts to decrease, similar to the experimental data.
*   The AMPT model used a parton cross section of 3 mb in ZPC, with running coupling alpha_s = 0.4714 and screen mass mu = 3.2264 fm^-1, giving an effective specific shear viscosity eta/s of 0.137.
*   The v3 extracted from both models is, up to 50% centrality, in good agreement with the experimentally measured v3; for centralities above 50%, both models give v3 greater than the experimentally measured one.
*   The AMPT model prediction for v4 is in very good agreement with the experimentally measured v4 for practically the whole centrality range, while the HYDJET++ model disagrees with the experimental data.
*   The slopes of the v2-v3 correlation from both models are in good agreement with the ATLAS data, but the HYDJET++ model predicts a stronger slope for the v2-v4 and v3-v4 correlations than the ones experimentally measured.

## Context

This work extends comparisons of the HYDJET++ and AMPT models to correlations between Fourier harmonics, following experimental measurements by the ATLAS Collaboration (2015). The study builds on prior work showing that ideal hydrodynamics (as in HYDJET++) predicts nearly linear centrality dependence of vn harmonics, while viscous hydrodynamics with positive eta/s values produces maxima and then a decrease in vn going to peripheral collisions, as seen in Qian (2016). The results enable further tuning of transport and hydrodynamic models to reproduce the detailed correlation structure observed in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC.
