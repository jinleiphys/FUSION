# Electromagnetic reactions from coupled-cluster theory

> We review recent results for electromagnetic reactions and related sum rules in light and medium-mass nuclei obtained from coupled-cluster theory. In particular, we highlight our recent computations of the photodisintegration cross section of 40Ca and of the electric dipole polarizability for oxygen and calcium isotopes. We also provide new results for the Coulomb sum rule for 4He and 16O. For 4He we perform a thorough comparison of coupled-cluster theory with exact hyperspherical harmonics.

# Electromagnetic reactions from coupled-cluster theory

## Key claim
Coupled-cluster theory with single and double excitations (CCSD), combined with the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method, can reproduce experimental photodisintegration cross sections and electric dipole polarizabilities in medium-mass nuclei such as 40Ca, but the accuracy depends on the inclusion of three-nucleon forces and the choice of chiral Hamiltonian; the CCSD approximation differs from exact hyperspherical harmonics calculations for 4He by only 1-2%.

## Method
The authors merge the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method with coupled-cluster theory to compute nuclear response functions without explicitly calculating all excited states in the continuum. They solve a bound-state-like equation involving the similarity-transformed Hamiltonian and excitation operators within the CCSD approximation (T = T1 + T2 and R = R1 + R2). For the Coulomb sum rule, they compute the proton-proton correlation density and elastic form factor as expectation values on the ground state rather than through multipole expansion.

## Key numbers
- The CCSD approximation for 4He differs from exact hyperspherical harmonics results by only 1-2%
- Photodisintegration cross section of 40Ca computed with a two-nucleon chiral force (Entem03) shows good agreement with experimental data from Ahrens et al. (1975)
- Coulomb sum rule calculations for 4He and 16O are compared to experimental data at low momentum transfers q = 200 and 250 MeV/c from Buki et al.
- The NNLO_sat potential (Ekstroem15), fit to reproduce the 16O charge radius, enables correct prediction also for 40Ca

## Context
This work extends the LIT method, previously applied to few-body systems using hyperspherical harmonics (barnea2001), to medium-mass nuclei by exploiting the mild computational scaling of coupled-cluster theory (Coester and Kummel, 1960; hagen2013c). The authors benchmark their CCSD approach against exact hyperspherical harmonics for 4He, following earlier Green's function Monte Carlo work on the Coulomb sum rule by Lovato et al. The method enables future ab initio studies of neutrino-nucleus cross sections relevant for long-baseline neutrino experiments, and has already provided constraints on neutron skin thickness and electric dipole polarizability in 48Ca when combined with electron scattering data (Hagen16, Birkhan17).
