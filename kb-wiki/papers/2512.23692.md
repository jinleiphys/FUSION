# The Effect of Hadronic Matter on Parton Energy Loss

> Modified thermal distributions (dispersion relations) are introduced within both the MATTER and LBT event generators used to describe jet modification in a heavy-ion collision, within the JETSCAPE framework. Hard partons, propagating through dense matter, scatter off the partonic substructure of the medium, leading to stimulated emission, accompanied by recoiling medium partons. We introduce a simple modification, a multiplicative $(1 + a/T)$ correction to the dispersion relation of quarks and gluons (equivalent to an effective fugacity). This leads to calculated transport coefficients (e.g. $\hat{q}/T^3$) showing the expected behavior of depreciating at lower temperatures, including within the hot hadronic gas. This simple modification recovers the light-like dispersion relations at high temperatures, and introduces an excess depreciation factor for parton populations at lower temperatures, allowing partonic energy loss and recoil calculations to be extended into the hadronic phase. This modified distribution, in combination with initial state cold nuclear matter effects (shadowing), is used to simultaneously describe the nuclear modification factor and elliptic anisotropy of jets and leading hadrons, over multiple centralities and collision energies.

## Key claim

The authors introduce a simple multiplicative $(1 + a/T)$ correction to the dispersion relations of quarks and gluons (equivalent to an effective fugacity) within the MATTER and LBT event generators in the JETSCAPE framework, which allows partonic energy loss and recoil calculations to be extended into the hadronic phase. This modification, combined with initial-state nuclear shadowing, simultaneously describes the nuclear modification factor and elliptic anisotropy of jets and leading hadrons over multiple centralities and collision energies, resolving tensions in prior JETSCAPE simulations that had not included either effect.

## Method

The authors modified the thermal distributions of quarks and gluons used as scattering targets in the MATTER (high-virtuality) and LBT (low-virtuality) Monte Carlo event generators within the JETSCAPE framework, replacing standard Fermi-Dirac and Bose-Einstein distributions with reduced-temperature distributions where the effective temperature is shifted by a constant $a_0$ (yielding a multiplicative $(1 + a_0/T)$ correction to the dispersion relation). They also incorporated nuclear shadowing via the EPPS16 nuclear parton distribution functions, which had been omitted in all prior JETSCAPE simulations. The modified framework was then used to simulate jet and hadron production in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, computing the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ and elliptic anisotropy $v_2$ for comparison with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The minimum hadron $p_T$ considered is $> 10$ GeV and the minimum jet $p_T$ is $> 50$ GeV, to avoid recombination effects and hadronic energy loss contributions not included in the calculation.
- The transition scale between MATTER and LBT modules is set to $Q_{\rm SW} = 2$ GeV; partons with virtuality $\mu \geq Q_{\rm SW}$ are retained in MATTER, those below are transferred to LBT.
- The minimum virtuality for parton evolution is $Q_{min} = 1$ GeV.
- The simulation evolves in time steps of $0.1$ fm/c.
- The formation time estimate for hadronization gives $E_{min} \gtrsim 6$ GeV, using $\Lambda_{QCD} \simeq 0.5$ GeV and maximal medium size $L_{fo} \simeq 10$ fm.
- The Bjorken-$x$ range probed at LHC (5.02 TeV) is $x \gtrsim 4 \times 10^{-3}$ for hadrons with $p_T \gtrsim 10$ GeV, up to $x \lesssim 0.4$ for jets with $p_T^{max} \sim 1000$ GeV.
- At top RHIC energies (0.2 TeV), the $x$ range probed is $0.1 \lesssim x \lesssim 0.4$.

## Context

This work extends the multi-stage recoil-based jet modification framework previously developed by the JETSCAPE collaboration (JETSCAPE, 2022; JETSCAPE, 2023; JETSCAPE, 2024), which had successfully described jet and hadron suppression at RHIC and LHC energies but showed tensions for leading hadrons with $p_T < 30$ GeV, at centralities above 50%, and for elliptic anisotropy $v_2$. The authors note that none of the prior JETSCAPE efforts included nuclear shadowing effects (Eskola, 2009; Eskola, 2016). The modified distributions enable the recoil formalism to be extended into the hadronic phase, addressing the unphysical behavior of $\hat{q}/T^3$ rising with decreasing temperature until a sharp cutoff at $T_C$, which lattice QCD calculations (Kumar, 2020) indicate should instead show a gradual reduction. This work enables simultaneous description of $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ for both jets and hadrons across multiple centralities and collision energies, which had not been achieved previously.
