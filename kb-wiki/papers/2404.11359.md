# Momentum dependent nucleon-nucleon contact interactions and their effect on p-d scattering observables

> Starting from a complete set of relativistic nucleon-nucleon contact operators up to order $O(p^4)$ of the expansion in the soft (relative or nucleon) momentum $p$, we show that non-relativistic expansions of relativistic operators involve twenty-six independent combinations, two starting at $O(p^0)$, seven at order $O(p^2)$ and seventeen at order $O(p^4)$. This demonstrates the existence of two low-energy free constants that parameterize interactions dependent on the total momentum of the pair of nucleons $P$. The latter, through the use of a unitary transformation, can be removed in the two-nucleon fourth-order contact interaction of the Chiral Effective Field Theory, generating a three-nucleon interaction at the same order. Within a hybrid approach in which this interaction is considered together with the phenomenological potential AV18, we show that the LECs involved can be used to fit very accurate data on the polarization observables of the low-energy $p-d$ scattering, in particular the $A_y$ asymmetry.

## Key claim

Starting from a complete set of relativistic nucleon-nucleon contact operators up to order O(p^4), the non-relativistic expansion yields twenty-six independent combinations, including two low-energy constants (LECs) that parameterize interactions dependent on the total momentum of the nucleon pair; these LECs, which vanish in the two-nucleon center-of-mass frame, can be removed via a unitary transformation that generates a three-nucleon interaction at the same order, and within a hybrid approach using the AV18 potential, these LECs can fit low-energy p-d scattering polarization observables, particularly the A_y asymmetry.

## Method

The authors performed a non-relativistic reduction of a manifestly Lorentz-invariant two-nucleon contact Lagrangian density up to order 1/m^4, identifying two additional LECs (D_16 and D_17) that appear in the general reference frame. They then applied a unitary transformation to shift these LECs into a three-nucleon contact interaction, and solved the three-body Schrodinger equation using the Hyperspherical Harmonic method with the Kohn variational principle, treating the AV18 potential as the two-nucleon interaction and the induced three-nucleon force as the fitting parameters. The fit targeted experimental n-d scattering lengths, the ^3H binding energy, and six p-d scattering observables at center-of-mass energy 2 MeV, using the POUNDerS algorithm for minimization.

## Key numbers

- The non-relativistic expansion yields twenty-six independent combinations: two at O(p^0), seven at O(p^2), and seventeen at O(p^4)
- The cutoff value used for the three-nucleon interaction is Lambda = 500 MeV
- The LO contact interaction constants fitted to AV18 singlet and triplet n-p scattering lengths are C_S = -66.53 GeV^{-2} and C_T = -3.47 GeV^{-2}
- The experimental n-d doublet scattering length is ^2a_nd = (0.645 +/- 0.003 +/- 0.007) fm; the quartet scattering length is ^4a_nd = (6.35 +/- 0.02) fm
- The ^3H binding energy is fixed to 8.469 MeV, accounting for ~7 keV from neutron-proton mass difference and ~6 keV from HH expansion truncation
- The chi-squared per degree of freedom is ~2.1 for the two-parameter fit and ~1.9 for the three-parameter fit
- The fitted dimensionless parameters for the two-parameter fit are: tilde_alpha_4 C_S = 1.751, tilde_alpha_5 C_S = -0.495; for the three-parameter fit: e_0 = 0.459, tilde_alpha_4 C_S = 1.894, tilde_alpha_5 C_S = -1.175
- The resulting n-d doublet scattering length from the two-parameter fit is 0.573 fm; from the three-parameter fit it is 0.599 fm

## Context

This work extends the analysis of relativistic constraints on nucleon-nucleon contact interactions previously studied by Xiao (2018) and Filandri (2023), and builds on the unitary transformation framework developed by Girlanda (2020, 2023). The finding that two additional LECs at N3LO can be transformed into a three-nucleon interaction addresses the long-standing A_y puzzle in low-energy p-d scattering, which has resisted accurate description by most nuclear interaction models including the AV18 potential and the Urbana IX three-nucleon interaction. The results enable future fully consistent calculations within Chiral Effective Field Theory and suggest that these LECs should be quantified in systems with A greater than 2.
