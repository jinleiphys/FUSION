# Non-perturbative formulation of the static color octet potential

> By dressing Polyakov lines with appropriate functionals of the gauge fields, we construct observables describing a fundamental representation static quark-antiquark pair in the singlet, adjoint and average channels of SU(N) pure gauge theory. Each of the potentials represents a gauge invariant eigenvalue of the Hamiltonian. Numerical simulations are performed for SU(2) in 2+1 dimensions. The adjoint channel is found to be repulsive at small and confining at large separations, suggesting the existence of a metastable (N^2-1)-plet bound state. For small distances and temperatures above the deconfinement transition, the leading order perturbative prediction for the ratio of singlet and adjoint potentials is reproduced by the lattice data.

## Key claim

By dressing Polyakov loops with eigenfunctions of the spatial Laplacian, the singlet, adjoint, and average static potentials can each be extracted as gauge invariant eigenvalues of the Hamiltonian, with the adjoint channel showing repulsion at short distances and confinement at large separations, suggesting a metastable bound state.

## Method

The authors construct dressed Wilson lines and Polyakov loops by multiplying the undressed line with matrices formed from the lowest eigenfunctions of the spatial lattice Laplace operator on each timeslice. They then compute correlation functions of these dressed loops in SU(2) pure gauge theory in 2+1 dimensions using the Wilson action, extracting the singlet, adjoint, and average potentials from the exponential decay of the correlators. The transfer matrix formalism is used to demonstrate that the extracted potentials are eigenvalues of the Hamiltonian and thus gauge invariant.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Philipsen (2002) for constructing gauge invariant two-point functions of charge carriers, previously used to define a gluon parton mass, to the case of static quark-antiquark potentials. It provides a non-perturbative, gauge invariant definition of the adjoint (octet) channel potential, which previously could only be studied in fixed gauges such as Landau gauge (Attig, 1990s) or via the color-averaged potential from Polyakov loop correlators (Nadkarni, 1986; Laine, 1990s). The results enable future studies of metastable octet bound states relevant for heavy quark phenomenology, such as J/Ψ production, and can be extended to construct gauge invariant symmetric and antisymmetric quark-quark potentials.
