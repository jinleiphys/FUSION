# T-odd Parton Distribution Functions and Azimuthal Anisotropy at High Transverse Momentum in $p$-$p$ and $p$-$A$ Collisions

> Various azimuthal anisotropies ($v_1, v_2, v_3, v_4$), at high transverse momentum (high-$p_T$), are shown to arise from the asymmetric scattering of transverse polarized quarks and gluons, arising from unpolarized nucleons (the Boer-Mulders' effect) and resulting in unpolarized hadrons (the Collins effect). Combined with the asymmetric scattering of partons from polarization independent but transverse momentum dependent (TMD) distributions, we obtain a possible mechanism to understand the azimuthal anisotropy of hadrons at large transverse momentum observed in $p$-$p$ collisions. Constraining the ratio of polarization dependent TMD distributions to polarization independent distributions by comparing with the data from $p$-$p$ collisions, we find that scaling the acquired transverse momentum of the initial state partons from the proton, due to prescattering before the hard interaction, with the length of the nucleus ($k_\perp^2 \propto A^{1/3}$), straightforwardly yields the azimuthal anisotropy at high $p_T$ in $p$-$A$ collisions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that azimuthal anisotropies (v1, v2, v3, v4) at high transverse momentum in p-p and p-A collisions can arise from transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions (TMDPDFs) and fragmentation functions (TMDFFs), specifically the Boer-Mulders effect (polarized partons from unpolarized nucleons) combined with the Collins effect (polarized parton fragmentation into unpolarized hadrons), without requiring a quark-gluon plasma. The mechanism works only if the dijet pair momentum direction is correlated with the event plane angle of the bulk system, which the authors demonstrate using PYTHIA simulations.

## Method

The authors compute semi-analytic cross sections for inclusive pion production using factorized TMDPDFs and TMDFFs with a Gaussian ansatz for the transverse momentum dependence, including both polarization-averaged and polarization-dependent (Boer-Mulders and Collins) contributions for gluon-gluon and quark-quark scattering channels. They parameterize the mean transverse momentum with an x-dependent ansatz and scale the proton-side transverse momentum by A^(1/3) for p-A collisions to account for pre-scattering in the nucleus. The polarized distributions are taken proportional to the unpolarized distributions, constrained by the Soffer bound, with the overall product B·b varied between 0.2 and 0.4. PYTHIA simulations are used to demonstrate the correlation between the dijet pair momentum vector and the event plane angle.

## Key numbers

- The mean transverse momentum parameterization uses ⟨k⊥²⟩₀ = 0.9 GeV², x₀ = 10⁻³, √s₀ = 8.16 TeV, and b₀ = 0.1
- The transverse momentum scaling for p-A collisions is k⊥² ∝ A^(1/3), where A is the nuclear mass number
- The product of polarized distribution normalization factors B·b is varied in the range 0.2 to 0.4
- A kinematic constraint k⊥ ≤ √s × min(x, √(x(1-x))) is imposed on parton transverse momenta
- The effective mean transverse momentum from the x-dependent ansatz yields values below 1 GeV for all scales Q
- Experimental data for p-p collisions is measured up to 10 GeV and for p-Pb up to 50 GeV

## Context

This work extends the application of TMD factorization (Collins 1981) and the Boer-Mulders effect (Boer 1997) combined with the Collins effect (Collins 1992) to explain high-pT azimuthal anisotropies observed by ATLAS (2016, 2019) in p-p and p-Pb collisions, where no suppression of the angle-integrated spectrum is seen in p-Pb (CMS 2016, ALICE 2015, ATLAS 2022). The approach contrasts with traditional heavy-ion explanations that attribute high-pT anisotropy to path-length-dependent energy loss in a quark-gluon plasma (Wang 2003, Majumder 2006, Bass 2008). The paper enables future extraction of polarized TMD distributions from small-system data and provides a baseline for disentangling initial-state effects from QGP signatures in p-A collisions.
