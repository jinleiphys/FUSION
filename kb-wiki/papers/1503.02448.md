# Neutron skin and centrality classification in high-energy heavy-ion collisions at the LHC

> The concept of centrality in high-energy nuclear collisions has recently become a subject of an active debate. In particular, the experimental methods to determine the centrality that have given reasonable results for many observables in high-energy lead-lead collisions at the LHC have led to surprising behaviour in the case of proton-lead collisions. In this letter, we discuss the possibility to calibrate the experimental determination of centrality by asymmetries caused by mutually different spatial distributions of protons and neutrons inside the nuclei --- a well-known phenomenon in nuclear physics known as the neutron-skin effect.

# Neutron skin and centrality classification in high-energy heavy-ion collisions at the LHC

## Key claim

The neutron-skin effect, the gradual increase of neutron concentration relative to protons toward the nuclear surface in neutron-rich nuclei like 208Pb, produces measurable centrality-dependent asymmetries in W± boson production at the LHC that could be used to calibrate experimental centrality classification methods, provided those methods truly reflect collision geometry. The author stresses that these results are first rough estimates that neglect event-by-event fluctuations and should be taken as indicative rather than definitive.

## Method

The author uses the Optical Glauber Model with two-parameter Fermi distributions for proton and neutron densities in 208Pb, taking parameter values from the recent measurement by Tarbert et al. (2013). Effective numbers of protons and neutrons are computed for each centrality class in both p+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions, and these are used to construct centrality-dependent parton distribution functions for the lead nucleus. The ratio of charged-lepton yields from W+ and W− decays is then calculated at next-to-leading order using the MCFM Monte-Carlo code with CT10NLO proton PDFs, integrating over lepton transverse momentum above 25 GeV.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb: Z = 82 protons, N = 126 neutrons, giving an average Z/N ratio of approximately 0.65.
- Proton density parameters: a_p = 0.447 fm, d_p = 6.680 fm; neutron density parameters: a_n = 0.55 ± 0.03 fm, d_n = 6.70 ± 0.03 fm, taken from Tarbert et al. (2013).
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross sections used: 65 mb at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV for Pb+Pb, and 70 mb at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV for p+Pb, from Antchev et al. (2013).
- The calculation for Pb+Pb collisions compared to ATLAS data yields chi-squared per data point of approximately 0.6.
- The ATLAS measurement reports an average W+/W− ratio of 1.03 for the 0-80% centrality class.

## Context

This work extends the ongoing debate about centrality classification in high-energy nuclear collisions, where experimental methods that work for Pb+Pb collisions have shown surprising behavior in p+Pb collisions (Chatrchyan 2014, ATLAS 2014). Prior proposals to explain these discrepancies include energy-momentum conservation effects (Armesto 2015) and event-by-event fluctuations in nucleon profiles. The paper suggests that W± production, which is theoretically well understood up to next-to-next-to-leading order (Anastasiou 2003, Catani 2009), could serve as a calibration tool for experimental centrality definitions. The neutron-skin effect could also be studied in high-pT photon and charged hadron production at RHIC, and in charged-current reactions at future facilities like the Electron-Ion Collider (Aschenauer 2014) or LHeC (Abelleira Fernandez 2012).
