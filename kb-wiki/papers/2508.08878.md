# On the High Excitation 7$α$ De-exciting States in $^{28}$Si$^*$

> A direct comparison and analysis of published spectra for the 7$α$ disassembly of $^{28}$Si projectiles excited in collisions with $^{12}$C at 35 MeV/u reveals significant agreement in the derived excitation energies of high excitation energy resonances observed in two different experiments, in contrast to some earlier conclusions reported in the literature. Many of the observed resonances have excitation energies consistent with those arrived at in recent theoretical investigations explicitly predicting the excitation energies and spins of toroidal nuclei. An AI-assisted application of well-established statistical filtering techniques reveals identical structures in all spectra investigated. Some additional peaks are observed. The possibility that they correspond to other favored geometries is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that high-excitation 7α de-exciting states in ²⁸Si, previously reported as conflicting between two experiments, actually show significant agreement in derived excitation energies when analyzed with background-free second derivative methods and AI-assisted statistical filtering, contrary to earlier conclusions by Hannaman et al. (2023) that "confidently excludes the reported state properties."

## Method

The authors directly compared published 7α excitation energy spectra from two experiments: the NIMROD-ISiS 4π array experiment by Cao et al. (2019) and the higher-statistics FAUST forward array experiment by Hannaman et al. (2023), both using the ¹²C(²⁸Si,7α) reaction at 35 MeV/u. They applied second derivative analyses without background assumptions, then employed a multi-stage statistical filtering pipeline including Savitzky-Golay smoothing, Hampel filtering, Gaussian Mixture Models with Bayesian Information Criterion, and Bayesian matched-filter with false discovery rate control at 5%. For the lower-statistics Cao data, peak widths were constrained to ≥3.3 MeV based on the higher-statistics results.

## Key numbers

- The three originally reported peaks from Cao et al. (2019) were recalibrated upward by ~2% to 116 MeV, 128 MeV, and 141 MeV
- The FAUST experiment had 180,000 events versus 8,943 events for the NIMROD experiment
- At 95% confidence level, nine resonances were identified across all five spectra (four time-ordered segments and total sum) of the Hannaman data: 67±1, 75±1, 83±1, 91±1, 101±1, 109±1, 117±1, 127±1, and 169±1 MeV
- For the Cao data with constrained widths (≥3.3 MeV), robust peaks were found at 83, 91, 101, 117, 127, and 165 MeV
- Individual resonance cross sections range from ~35 µb at the peak of the 7α excitation function to ~10 µb at higher and lower excitation energies
- Approximately 25% of the 7α cross section is found in the observed resonances
- In 100,000 simulated random spectra, the probability of finding the same 9 energies across four segments was 6×10⁻⁴%
- The 8α spectrum, shifted downward by 22 MeV, shows matching second derivative minima at 75, 81, 86, 91, 97, 107, 115, 127, 135, and 143 MeV

## Context

This work resolves an apparent contradiction between Cao et al. (2019), who reported toroidal state candidates in ²⁸Si, and Hannaman et al. (2023), who claimed their higher-statistics experiment excluded those states. The analysis supports earlier re-evaluations by Wada et al. (2023) using ⁸Be precursor filtering and by Depastas et al. (2024) using AI-assisted machine learning, both of which found underlying structure. The observed excitation energies align with lattice DFT predictions for toroidal ²⁸Si states by Ren et al. (2023). The results enable searches for toroidal structures in other α-conjugate exit channels and motivate experiments to determine angular momenta of these states.
