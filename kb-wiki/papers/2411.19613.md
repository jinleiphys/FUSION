# Non-perturbative three-nucleon simulation using chiral lattice EFT

> We study the three-nucleon system at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order ($\mathrm{N^3LO}$) in the framework of chiral effective field theory (EFT) on the lattice. Our calculations do not rely on a perturbative treatment of subleading contributions to the nuclear forces. For the two-nucleon potential, we apply the previously developed $\mathrm{N^3LO}$ lattice interaction. For the leading contribution to the three-nucleon force, we determine the two low-energy constants (LECs) in the contact interactions by adjusting the ground state energy and half-life of triton, where the latter employs the nuclear axial current at $\mathrm{N^2LO}$ in chiral EFT. Additionally, the ground state energy of helion and the charge radii of the two considered nuclei are computed. No effect of the smearing regularization in the three-nucleon contact interaction is observed here. We compare our results with recent lattice-EFT calculations that are based on a potential tuned to light and medium-mass nuclei using the wave-function-matching technique to circumvent the Monte-Carlo sign problem.

# Non-perturbative three-nucleon simulation using chiral lattice EFT

## Key claim
The authors perform a non-perturbative three-nucleon simulation at N3LO in chiral lattice EFT without relying on perturbation theory for subleading nuclear forces, but the truncation uncertainty estimates should be taken with care because only NLO and N3LO results are available and the N3LO contribution to the nuclear axial current is neglected.

## Method
The three-nucleon system is solved exactly on a periodic cubic lattice with spacing 1.9733 fm by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian using a Lanczos algorithm. The two-nucleon potential uses a previously developed N3LO lattice interaction with 24 contact interactions fitted to nucleon-nucleon phase shifts, while the leading three-nucleon force includes contact, single-pion-exchange, and double-pion-exchange terms. The low-energy constants c_D and c_E (or C_0,3N) are determined by fitting the triton ground state energy and half-life, with the half-life computed using the nuclear axial current at N2LO.

## Key numbers
- Triton ground state energy: -8.48(7) MeV (c_D+c_E fit) and -8.51(8) MeV (c_D+C_0,3N fit), compared to experimental -8.481795(2) MeV
- Helion ground state energy: -7.73(7) MeV (c_D+c_E fit) and -7.77(7) MeV (c_D+C_0,3N fit), compared to experimental -7.718040(2) MeV
- Triton charge radius: 1.695(10) fm (c_D+c_E fit) and 1.702(10) fm (c_D+C_0,3N fit), compared to experimental 1.7591(363) fm
- Helion charge radius: 1.914(14) fm (c_D+c_E fit) and 1.920(13) fm (c_D+C_0,3N fit), compared to experimental 1.9661(30) fm
- Triton half-life: 12.31(10) yr (c_D+c_E fit) and 12.30(10) yr (c_D+C_0,3N fit), compared to experimental 12.32(3) yr
- Fitted LEC values: c_D = -0.0625 ± 0.0625, c_E = -0.39844 ± 0.00781, C_0,3N = (-0.7246 ± 0.0234) fm^5
- Lattice spacing: a = 1.9733 fm

## Context
This work extends the non-perturbative lattice EFT approach to three-nucleon systems at N3LO, building on the two-nucleon lattice interaction developed by Li (2018). It contrasts with perturbative lattice-EFT calculations by Elhatisari (2022, 2024) that use wave-function-matching to circumvent the sign problem and tune potentials to light and medium-mass nuclei. The results show that fitting only to three-nucleon data produces comparable charge radii to fits including heavier nuclei, and the smearing regularization in the three-nucleon contact interaction shows no significant effect for three nucleons, unlike the behavior observed in many-nucleon systems by Elhatisari (2016). The interaction constructed here enables future applications such as nucleon-deuteron scattering via the adiabatic projection method.
