# Baryon-Baryon Interactions

> After a short survey of some topics of interest in the study of baryon-baryon scattering, the recent Nijmegen energy dependent partial wave analysis (PWA) of the nucleon-nucleon data is reviewed. In this PWA the energy range for both pp and np is now 0 < Tlab < 350 MeV and a chi^2_{d.o.f.}=1.08 was reached. The implications for the pion-nucleon coupling constants are discussed. Comments are made with respect to recent discussions around this coupling constant in the literature. In the second part, we briefly sketch the picture of the baryon in several, more or less QCD-based, quark-models that have been rather prominent in the literature. Inspired by these pictures we constructed a new soft-core model for the nucleon-nucleon interaction and present the first results of this model in a chi^2 -fit to the new multi-energy Nijmegen PWA. With this new model we succeeded in narrowing the gap between theory and experiment at low energies. For the energies Tlab = 25-320 MeV we reached a record low chi^2_{p.d.p.} = 1.16. We finish the paper with some conclusions and an outlook describing the extension of the new model to baryon-baryon scattering.

# Baryon-Baryon Interactions

## Key claim

The new extended-soft-core (ESC) model for the nucleon-nucleon interaction, inspired by chiral-quark-model pictures of baryon structure, achieves a record low chi-squared per data point of 1.16 for laboratory energies between 25 and 320 MeV when fitted to the multi-energy Nijmegen partial wave analysis, but the results are described as preliminary because the inclusion of additional pi-rho and pi-omega potentials is expected to modify the meson-pair couplings.

## Method

The author constructed a new soft-core potential model for nucleon-nucleon scattering that combines one-boson-exchange potentials (including pseudoscalar, vector, and scalar mesons plus the Pomeron) with two-pion-exchange potentials and phenomenological nucleon-nucleon-meson-meson pair vertices. The model was fitted to the 1993 Nijmegen multi-energy partial wave analysis of combined pp and np data, which itself divides the internucleon distance into three regions: a long-range region (r >= 2.0 fm) with electromagnetic and one-pion-exchange potentials, an intermediate region (1.4 to 2.0 fm) with heavy-boson exchanges, and a short-range region (r < 1.4 fm) with energy-dependent square well potentials. The radial Schrodinger equation was solved in coordinate space to obtain phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- The multi-energy Nijmegen PWA reached chi-squared per degree of freedom = 1.08 for the combined pp+np data (chi-squared = 4263.8, N_dof = 4301) over the energy range 0 < T_lab < 350 MeV
- The new ESC model achieved chi-squared per data point = 1.16 for 3709 data points in the energy range 25 <= T_lab <= 320 MeV
- The predicted deuteron binding energy from the PWA extrapolation was B = 2.2247(35) MeV, compared to the experimental value B = 2.224575(9) MeV
- The mixing parameter epsilon_1 reaches 4.57 +/- 0.25 degrees at T_lab = 350 MeV
- The neutral pion-nucleon coupling constant from the combined NN PWA was f^2_{pp pi^0} = 74.5(0.6) x 10^{-3}
- The charged pion-nucleon coupling constant from the same analysis was f^2_c = 74.8(0.3) x 10^{-3}
- The ESC model pion coupling was f^2_{NN pi} = 0.072
- The rho meson coupling was g_rho^2 = 0.53 with (f/g)_rho = 4.52
- The sigma meson mass used was m_sigma = 500.4 MeV
- The form factor masses were: Lambda_PV = 844.8 MeV, Lambda_V,1 = 777.6 MeV, Lambda_V,0 = 744.9 MeV, Lambda_S,1 = 767.1 MeV, Lambda_S,0 = 835.6 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Nijmegen soft-core model (NRS78) and responds to the new multi-energy partial wave analysis (SKRS93), which provides a more stringent test of theoretical NN models than earlier phase shift analyses. The author notes that previous theoretical models could not explain NN data better than chi-squared per data point of about 1.8, and the ESC model narrows this gap. The paper situates itself within the broader program of understanding baryon-baryon interactions through meson-exchange models, with the chiral-quark-model picture (Manohar 1984) providing the physical motivation. The results enable future extension to hyperon-nucleon and other baryon-baryon channels using SU(3) symmetry, and the Reidlike phenomenological potentials derived from the PWA have already been used in triton binding energy calculations (FPSS93), yielding predictions of 7.62-7.72 MeV.
