# Mind the gap

> In this summary of the application of Dyson-Schwinger equations to the theory and phenomenology of hadrons, some deductions following from a nonperturbative, symmetry-preserving truncation are highlighted, notable amongst which are results for pseudoscalar mesons. We also describe inferences from the gap equation relating to the radius of convergence of a chiral expansion, applications to heavy-light and heavy-heavy mesons, and quantitative estimates of the contribution of quark orbital angular momentum in pseudoscalar mesons; and recapitulate upon studies of nucleon electromagnetic form factors.

## Key claim

The paper summarizes applications of Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) to hadron physics, highlighting that a nonperturbative, symmetry-preserving truncation of the DSEs can yield exact results for pseudoscalar mesons, heavy-light and heavy-heavy mesons, and nucleon electromagnetic form factors, but these results depend on the specific truncation scheme and the model for the quark-gluon interaction.

## Method

The authors employ QCD's gap equation (the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator) and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states, using a symmetry-preserving truncation (rainbow-ladder truncation) that respects the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity. For baryons, they use a Poincaré covariant Faddeev equation truncated to include dressed-quark and diquark correlations, with the nucleon appearing as a pole in a six-point quark Green function. The calculations are performed with a renormalisation-group-improved rainbow-ladder DSE truncation using a specific interaction model with a gluon mass-scale parameter.

## Key numbers

- Critical current-quark mass for chiral expansion: \(\hat m_{\rm cr} \sim 60\)–\(70\) MeV, corresponding to a flavour-nonsinglet \(0^{-}\) meson mass \(m_{0^{-}} \sim 0.45\) GeV
- Constituent-quark spectrum masses: \(M^S_c = 2.00\) GeV, \(M^S_b = 5.34\) GeV
- Diquark masses from models: \(m_{[ud]_{0^+}} = 0.74\)–\(0.82\) GeV; \(m_{(uu)_{1^+}} = m_{(ud)_{1^+}} = m_{(dd)_{1^+}} = 0.95\)–\(1.02\) GeV
- Electromagnetic size of scalar diquark: \(r_{[ud]_{0^+}} \approx 0.7\) fm; estimated axial-vector diquark size: \(r_{(ud)_{1^+}} \sim 0.8\) fm
- Gluon mass-scale parameter: \(m_g = 720\) MeV
- For ground-state vector mesons up to \(J/\psi\), standard deviation between calculated width and experiment is \(\lesssim 15\%\); adding \(\Upsilon(1S)\) raises this to \(\lesssim 25\%\)
- Proton ratio \(\mu_p G_E^p(Q^2)/G_M^p(Q^2)\) passes through zero at \(Q^2 \approx 6.5\) GeV\(^2\)
- Neutron electric radius relation: \(\mu_p G_E^n(Q^2)/G_M^n(Q^2) = - (r_n^2/6) Q^2\) for \(r_n^2 Q^2 \lesssim 1\)
- Model prediction for \(\eta_b\) mass: \(m_{\eta_b} = 9.42\) GeV, with \(b\)-quark mass fitted to give \(m_{\Upsilon(1S)} = 9.46\) GeV
- Calculated mass of first excited pseudoscalar state in chiral limit: \(1.04\) GeV

## Context

This work extends the DSE-based approach to hadron phenomenology summarized in Maris and Roberts (2002) and builds on prior studies of the gap equation by Chang et al. (2006) regarding the radius of convergence of chiral expansions. The results for heavy-light mesons follow from Ivanov et al. (1997, 1998), while the heavy-heavy meson analysis connects to non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) and potential model studies by Lakhina and collaborators (2006). The nucleon form factor calculations extend the Faddeev equation framework of Oettel et al. (1999) and Hecht et al. (2002), and the predictions for the neutron electric form factor ratio can be tested against experimental data from facilities such as Jefferson Lab.
