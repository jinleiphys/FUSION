# $D_s$ decays into $ϕ$ and $f_0(980)$ mesons

> We consider the nonleptonic and semileptonic decays of $D_s$-mesons into $ϕ$ and $f_0(980)$ mesons. QCD sum rules are used to calculate the form factors associated with these decays, and the correspondig decay rates. On the basis of data on $D_s^+\toπ^+π^+π^-$, which goes dominantly via the transition $D_s^+\to π^+f_0(980)$, we conclude that there is space for a sizeable light quark component on $f_0(980)$.

# Wiki Page: $D_s$ Decays into $\phi$ and $f_0(980)$ Mesons

## Key claim

Using QCD sum rules to compute form factors and decay rates, the authors find that the ratio $\Gamma(D_s^+ \to f_0(980)\pi^+)/\Gamma(D_s^+ \to \phi\pi^+)$ is larger than the value inferred from experimental data on $D_s^+ \to \pi^+\pi^+\pi^-$, provided one assumes $f_0(980)$ is a pure $\bar{s}s$ state. This discrepancy indicates a sizeable nonstrange (light quark) component in the $f_0(980)$ meson, or a more complex structure than the naive quark model suggests.

## Method

The authors use the QCD sum rule technique to compute the form factors $f_+(t)$, $A_1(t)$, $A_2(t)$, and $V(t)$ for the transitions $D_s \to f_0(980)$ and $D_s \to \phi$ from three-point correlation functions. The form factors are evaluated in the range $-0.5 < t < 0.4$ GeV$^2$, fitted to a monopole parametrization, and extrapolated to the full kinematical region. The resulting form factors are then used in the factorization approximation to compute nonleptonic decay widths for $D_s^+ \to \phi\pi^+$ and $D_s^+ \to f_0(980)\pi^+$, as well as semileptonic decay widths for $D_s^+ \to \phi\ell^+\nu_\ell$ and $D_s^+ \to f_0(980)\ell^+\nu_\ell$.

## Key numbers

- Form factors at $t=0$: $f_+(0) = 0.40$ to $0.48$; $V(0) = 0.71$ to $0.89$; $A_1(0) = 0.32$ to $0.42$; $A_2(0) = -0.43$ to $-0.37$.
- Monopole pole masses from $t$-dependence fits: $M_P = (1.6 \pm 0.2)$ GeV for $f_+(t)$; $M_P = (4.2 \pm 0.5)$ GeV for $A_1(t)$; $M_P = (8.0 \pm 2.0)$ GeV for $A_2(t)$; $M_P = (1.95 \pm 0.10)$ GeV for $V(t)$.
- Predicted branching ratio: $\mathcal{B}(D_s^+ \to \phi\pi^+) = (2.8 \pm 0.7)\%$ (experimental: $(3.6 \pm 0.9)\%$).
- Predicted branching ratio: $\mathcal{B}(D_s^+ \to \phi\ell^+\nu) = (1.4 \pm 0.4)\%$ (experimental: $(2.0 \pm 0.5)\%$).
- Ratio $\Gamma(D_s^+ \to f_0(980)\pi^+) / \Gamma(D_s^+ \to \phi\pi^+) = 0.44 \pm 0.18$ (assuming $f_0(980)$ as $\bar{s}s$).
- Experimental ratio inferred from E791 data: $0.210 \pm 0.069$ (assuming $\mathcal{B}(f_0(980) \to \pi^+\pi^-) \sim 2/3$).
- Predicted branching ratio for semileptonic decay: $\mathcal{B}(D_s^+ \to f_0(980)\ell^+\nu) = (0.55 \pm 0.10)\%$ (assuming $\bar{s}s$ structure).

## Context

This work extends QCD sum rule calculations of $D_s$ decays to final states containing scalar and vector mesons, following the approach used for $D \to K^*$ semileptonic decays by Ball, Braun, and Dosch (1991). The ratio $R = \Gamma(D_s^+ \to f_0(980)\pi^+) / \Gamma(D_s^+ \to \phi\pi^+)$ had been previously evaluated using the spectral integration technique by Anisovich et al. and the constituent quark meson model by Deandrea et al., both concluding that the $s\bar{s}$ component dominates $f_0(980)$. The present QCD sum rule result, combined with experimental data from the E791 collaboration (Aitala et al., 2001), suggests instead a significant nonstrange component. The authors propose that measurement of the semileptonic decay $D_s^+ \to f_0(980)\ell^+\nu$ could definitively test the $f_0(980)$ structure, as factorization approximation issues do not arise in semileptonic decays.
