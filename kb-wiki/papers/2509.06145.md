# Equation of State Extrapolation Systematics: Parametric vs. Nonparametric Inference of Neutron Star Structure

> The equation of state (EOS) of cold dense matter is a central open problem in nuclear astrophysics. Its inference is hindered by the lack of \textit{ab initio} control above about twice nuclear saturation density, requiring extrapolation. Parametric schemes such as piecewise polytropes (PP) are efficient but restrictive, while nonparametric approaches like Gaussian processes (GP) allow more flexibility at the cost of larger prior volumes. We extend our hybrid EOS framework by replacing the high-density polytropic extension with a GP representation of the squared sound speed, anchored at low densities by the SLy crust EOS and a nuclear meta-model constrained by $χ$EFT and laboratory data. Using hierarchical Bayesian analysis, we jointly constrain the EOS and neutron star mass distribution with multi-messenger observations, including NICER radii, GW170817 and GW190425 tidal deformabilities, pulsar masses, and neutron skin experiments. We examine four scenarios defined by high-density extrapolation (PP vs.\ GP) and hotspot geometry in the NICER modeling of PSR~J0030$+$0451 (ST+PDT vs.\ PDT-U). GP extrapolations generally yield softer EOS posteriors with broader uncertainties. Hotspot assumptions also play an important role, shifting inferred mass--radius relations. Bayesian evidence strongly favors the ST+PDT geometry over PDT-U under both extrapolations, while GP is mildly preferred over PP. These results underscore the impact of observational modeling and EOS extrapolation on neutron star inferences, and show that a GP-based extension offers a robust way to quantify systematic uncertainties in high-density matter.

# Equation of State Extrapolation Systematics: Parametric vs. Nonparametric Inference of Neutron Star Structure

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Gaussian Process (GP) extrapolations of the high-density equation of state (EOS) are consistently preferred over piecewise polytrope (PP) extrapolations by Bayesian evidence, but the inferred neutron star properties depend sensitively on both the extrapolation method and the assumed hotspot geometry in NICER pulse-profile modeling of PSR J0030+0451, with GP-based extensions yielding softer EOS posteriors with broader uncertainty bands.

## Method

The authors construct a hybrid EOS framework combining a fixed SLy crust at low densities, a nuclear meta-model anchored near saturation density with free symmetry energy slope L and curvature K_sym, and a high-density extension using either a piecewise polytrope or a Gaussian Process representation of the squared sound speed. They perform hierarchical Bayesian analysis jointly constraining the EOS and neutron star mass distribution using multi-messenger data including NICER radius measurements for four pulsars, gravitational-wave tidal deformabilities from GW170817 and GW190425, pulsar mass measurements, neutron skin experiments (PREX-II and CREX), and theory priors from chiral effective field theory and perturbative QCD. The crust-core junction employs a thermodynamically consistent finite-interval blending construction with a C^infty weight function, and the GP hyperparameters (correlation length, variance, reference sound speed) are drawn hierarchically from broad normal priors.

## Key numbers

- The GP extrapolation with ST+PDT geometry yields median shifts of about 350 m smaller radius and about 80 smaller tidal deformability for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star compared to PP with the same geometry
- Changing from ST+PDT to PDT-U hotspot geometry increases R_1.4 by roughly 1.1 km and Lambda_1.4 by about 270 in the GP case
- Bayesian evidence difference between ST+PDT and PDT-U for the PP model is Delta log Z = 3.68 (log10 BF = 1.59), constituting strong evidence for ST+PDT
- Bayesian evidence difference between ST+PDT and PDT-U for the GP model is Delta log Z = 4.52 (log10 BF = 1.96), constituting very strong evidence for ST+PDT
- GP extension is preferred over PP with Delta log Z = 2.08 for ST+PDT (Bayes factor of about 8) and Delta log Z = 1.24 for PDT-U (Bayes factor of about 3.5)
- The GP hyperparameter correlation length l shifts to smaller values than its prior mean of 1.0 n_s in both hotspot geometries
- The transition density n_t is sampled with a uniform prior from 1 to 2 n_s
- The symmetry energy slope L has a uniform prior from 0 to 150 MeV, and curvature K_sym has a uniform prior from -600 to 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends the hybrid EOS framework of Biswas (2024) by replacing the piecewise polytropic high-density extrapolation with a Gaussian Process representation of the squared sound speed, following the nonparametric approach of Landry (2018) and Essick (2019). It directly quantifies systematic uncertainties from parametric versus nonparametric extrapolation strategies, building on earlier findings by Legred (2022) that restrictive functional forms can bias inferred pressure-density relations. The results enable robust, bias-minimized constraints on the dense matter EOS and demonstrate that GP-based extensions provide a framework for quantifying systematic uncertainties in high-density matter, while also showing that NICER hotspot geometry assumptions (ST+PDT versus PDT-U for PSR J0030+0451) produce systematic shifts in inferred mass-radius relations that are comparable in magnitude to the choice of extrapolation method.
