# Probing axial quark generalized parton distributions through exclusive photoproduction of a $γ\,π^\pm$ pair with a large invariant mass

> Exclusive photoproduction of a $γ\,π^\pm$ pair in the kinematics where the pair has a large invariant mass and the final nucleon has a small transverse momentum is described in the collinear factorization framework. The scattering amplitude is calculated at leading order in $α_s$ and the differential cross sections for the process are estimated in the kinematics of the JLab~12-GeV experiments. The order of magnitude of the predicted cross-sections seems sufficient for a dedicated experiment to be performed. The process turns out to be very sensitive to the axial generalized parton distribution combination $\tilde{H}_u - \tilde{H}_d$.

## Key claim

Exclusive photoproduction of a $\gamma\,\pi^\pm$ pair with large invariant mass and small nucleon transverse momentum can be described in collinear QCD factorization at leading order in $\alpha_s$, and the predicted cross sections appear sufficient for a dedicated experiment at JLab 12-GeV, provided the process is sensitive to the axial generalized parton distribution combination $\tilde{H}_u - \tilde{H}_d$.

## Method

The authors computed the scattering amplitude for $\gamma + N \rightarrow \gamma + \pi^\pm + N'$ at leading order in $\alpha_s$ using collinear factorization, where the hard scale is the invariant mass $M_{\gamma\pi}^2$ of the final photon-pion pair. The hard part was calculated from 20 Feynman diagrams grouped into QED gauge-invariant blocks, and the non-perturbative inputs were the asymptotic pion distribution amplitude $\phi_\pi^{\rm as}(z)=6z(1-z)$ and models for generalized parton distributions (GPDs) constructed from double distributions using GRV-98 for unpolarized parton distributions and GRSV-2000 for polarized parton distributions, with a fixed factorization scale $\mu_F^2=10$ GeV$^2$ and no QCD evolution.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: $f_\pi = 131$ MeV
- The total squared center-of-mass energy of the $\gamma$-$N$ system is $S_{\gamma N} \sim 6$–$22$ GeV$^2$ for JLab kinematics
- The hard scale $M_{\gamma\pi}^2$ is related to the skewness parameter by $\tau \approx M_{\gamma\pi}^2 / (S_{\gamma N} - M^2)$ and $\xi = \tau/(2-\tau)$
- The asymptotic pion distribution amplitude used is $\phi_\pi^{\rm as}(z) = 6z(1-z)$
- The common normalization coefficient for the amplitude is $C_\pi = i \frac{4}{9} f_\pi \alpha_{em} \alpha_s \pi^2$

## Context

This work extends the study of exclusive photoproduction of a $\gamma\,\rho^0$ pair with large invariant mass (Boussarie et al., 2016) to the case of charged $\gamma\,\pi^\pm$ pairs, where two-gluon intermediate states do not contribute. The process is analogous to timelike Compton scattering (Mueller et al., 1998; Berger et al., 2001; Pire et al., 2011) and complements deeply virtual Compton scattering and deeply virtual meson production (Goeke et al., 2001; Diehl, 2003; Belitsky et al., 2005; Boffi et al., 2007; Burkert et al., 2007; Guidal et al., 2008) as a probe of GPD universality. The results enable experimental extraction of the axial GPD combination $\tilde{H}_u - \tilde{H}_d$ at JLab 12-GeV, with the neutral pion case left for future studies.
