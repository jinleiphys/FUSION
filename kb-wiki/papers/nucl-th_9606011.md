# Multiple electromagnetic electron positron pair production in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We calculate the cross sections for the production of one and more electron-positron pairs due to the strong electromagnetic fields in relativistic heavy ion collisions. Using the generating functional of fermions in an external field we derive the N-pair amplitude. Neglecting the antisymmetrisation in the final state we find that the total probability to produce N pairs is a Poisson distribution. We calculate total cross sections for the production of one pair in lowest order and also include higher-order corrections from the Poisson distribution up to third order. Furthermore we calculate cross sections for the production of up to five pairs including corrections from the Poisson distribution.

# Multiple electromagnetic electron positron pair production in relativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The total probability to produce N electron-positron pairs in relativistic heavy ion collisions follows a Poisson distribution when antisymmetrization in the final state is neglected, and this approximation is justified because exchange terms contribute only about 1% corrections to two-pair production probabilities, with their importance decreasing at higher Lorentz factors.

## Method

The authors used the path integral formalism to derive the N-pair production amplitude from the generating functional of fermions in an external electromagnetic field, obtaining the amplitude as the product of the vacuum amplitude and an antisymmetrized product of reduced one-pair amplitudes. They calculated total cross sections for one-pair production in lowest order and included higher-order corrections from the Poisson distribution up to third order, using a monopole form factor with cutoff Lambda = 83 MeV for gold ions and performing the multidimensional integrals with the Monte Carlo integration routine VEGAS. For multiple-pair production up to five pairs, they integrated the full Poisson distribution over impact parameters starting from twice the nuclear radius to restrict to peripheral collisions.

## Key numbers

- For Au-Au collisions at gamma = 100 (RHIC), the total cross section for one-pair production with monopole form factor is 31.8 kb
- For Pb-Pb collisions at gamma = 8.916 (SPS), the one-pair cross section is 2.29 kb with monopole form factor
- For Pb-Pb collisions at gamma = 3400 (LHC), the one-pair cross section is 223 kb with monopole form factor
- For Au-Au at gamma = 100, the two-pair cross section is 0.899 kb and the three-pair cross section is 0.1122 kb with monopole form factor
- For Pb-Pb at gamma = 3400, the five-pair cross section is 6.94 x 10^(-2) kb with monopole form factor
- The cross section for free electron-positron pair production in antiproton-Xenon collisions at gamma_tar = 1.8 is sigma_0 = 14 nb per Z^2
- The monopole form factor cutoff is Lambda = 83 MeV for gold ions

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Hencken, Trautmann, and Baur (1995) who showed that the N-pair amplitude reduces to a product of the vacuum amplitude and antisymmetrized reduced one-pair amplitudes, and by Bertulani and Baur (1987) who noted that lowest-order perturbation theory violates unitarity. The results enable improved background estimates for quark-gluon plasma studies at RHIC and LHC, and provide cross sections for free pair production that are more than three orders of magnitude larger than the bound-free transitions used for antihydrogen production at LEAR. The method can also be applied to muon and tau pair production at higher ion energies.
