# Flow harmonic correlations via multi-particle symmetric and asymmetric cumulants in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 200 GeV

> We study multi-particle azimuthal correlations in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV. We use initial conditions obtained from a Monte-Carlo Glauber model and evolve them within a viscous relativistic hydrodynamics framework that eventually gives way to a transport model in the late hadronic stage of the evolution. We compute the multi-particle symmetric and asymmetric cumulants and present the results for their sensitivity to the shear and bulk viscosities during the hydrodynamic evolution. We also check their sensitivity to resonance decay and hadronic interactions. We demonstrate that while some of these observables are more sensitive to transport properties than traditional flow observables, others are less sensitive, making them suitable for studying different stages of the evolution.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that multi-particle symmetric cumulants (SC) and asymmetric cumulants (AC) of flow harmonics in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV show differential sensitivity to shear and bulk viscosities, as well as to hadronic afterburner effects, with some normalized observables being less sensitive than traditional flow coefficients and others more sensitive, making them suitable for probing different stages of the evolution.

## Method
The authors used a Monte Carlo Glauber model to generate initial conditions, then evolved the system using the viscous relativistic hydrodynamics code MUSIC with a temperature-dependent bulk viscosity parametrization and a constant shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of 0.08. The hydrodynamic evolution was matched to a hadron resonance gas via the iSS particlization code at a switching energy density of 0.26 GeV/fm³, followed by the UrQMD transport model for the hadronic stage. Multi-particle symmetric cumulants SC(m,n) and asymmetric cumulants AC_{2,1}(m,n) were computed from the final-state particles and compared across three parameter sets (ideal, shear only, shear plus bulk) and three evolution stages (hydrodynamic, after resonance decay, after full hadronic transport).

## Key numbers
- Shear viscosity variation: SC(2,3) shows approximately 40-50% change across the full centrality range, compared to about 10% change for v₂{4} in mid-central collisions.
- Asymmetric cumulant AC_{2,1}(2,3) shows approximately 40-60% variation due to shear viscosity across different centrality ranges.
- Resonance decay effect: v₂{4} shows approximately 15% variation due to resonance decay, while SC(2,3) shows roughly 40% change across the full centrality range.
- Hadronic interaction effect: SC(2,3) shows approximately 50% change from the combined effects of hadronic interactions and resonance decay in mid-central collisions.
- The hydrodynamic evolution started at τ₀ = 0.6 fm, with a switching energy density e_sw = 0.26 GeV/fm³ (equivalent to approximately 151 MeV).
- Transverse momentum ranges used: [0.2, 2.0] GeV for SC(m,n) and [0.01, 3.0] GeV for AC_{2,1}(m,n).
- Ensemble sizes: 4 million events for 0-10% centrality, 6 million for 10-20%, 8 million for 20-30%, 3 million for 30-40%, and 4 million for 40-50% centrality.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of symmetric cumulants in hydrodynamic and transport models (Zhu 2016, Schenke 2019, Mordasini 2019, Taghavi 2020, Hirvonen 2022, Magdy 2022, Magdy 2024, Gardim 2016) and asymmetric cumulants in transport models (Magdy 2022, Magdy 2024) by computing both observables within a full hybrid hydrodynamic+transport framework for Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies. The results enable systematic comparison with experimental measurements from the STAR Collaboration (2018) and ALICE Collaboration (2017, 2023), and demonstrate that normalized cumulants NSC(2,3) and NAC_{2,1}(2,3) are particularly valuable for constraining initial conditions due to their insensitivity to transport coefficients and hadronic afterburner effects, while NSC(2,4) and NAC_{2,1}(2,4) provide enhanced sensitivity to medium properties.
