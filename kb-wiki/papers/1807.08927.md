# Experimental results on fluctuations of conserved charges confronted with predictions from canonical thermodynamics

> The study of multiplicity distributions of identified particles in terms of their higher moments is at the focus of contemporary experimental and theoretical studies. In a thermalized system, combinations of these moments are directly related to the Equation of State (EoS). The ultimate goal of the experimental measurements in relativistic nuclear collisions is, by systematic comparison to QCD and QCD inspired calculations, to probe the dynamics of genuine phase transitions between a hadron gas and the quark-gluon plasma. However, the comparison between experiment and theory is far from trivial, because several non-dynamical effects on fluctuations need to be controlled prior to a meaningful comparison to theoretical predictions. In this report we present quantitative estimates for these non-dynamical contributions using the Canonical Ensemble (CE) formulation of statistical mechanics. Together with analytical formulas we provide also results from Monte Carlo (MC) simulations within the CE and compare our predictions with the corresponding measurements from the STAR experiment.

# Experimental results on fluctuations of conserved charges confronted with predictions from canonical thermodynamics

## Key claim
The deviations from Grand Canonical Ensemble (GCE) baselines observed in net-proton cumulant measurements by STAR at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies above $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 11.5$ GeV can be quantitatively described by the combined effects of global baryon number conservation (treated in the Canonical Ensemble) and event-by-event fluctuations of participating nucleons, without invoking critical phenomena. However, the STAR results for $\kappa_4/\kappa_2$ below $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 11.5$ GeV constitute a dramatic exception that cannot be explained by these non-dynamical effects.

## Method
The authors derived analytical formulas for the cumulants of net-baryon distributions within the Canonical Ensemble (CE) partition function, where baryon number is exactly conserved in each event, and then folded these distributions with experimental acceptance using a binomial selection procedure. They also performed Monte Carlo simulations generating $5 \times 10^8$ events in the CE, using mean baryon and anti-baryon numbers of $\langle N_B \rangle = 370$ and $\langle N_{\bar{B}} \rangle = 20$ respectively, to validate the analytical expressions. The STAR experimental data on $\kappa_3/\kappa_2$ for net-protons were first corrected for participant fluctuations using the procedure from their earlier work (Braun-Munzinger et al., 2017), then used to extract the acceptance parameter $\alpha$, which was subsequently inserted into the CE formulas to predict $\kappa_4/\kappa_2$.

## Key numbers
- The CE analytical result for the normalized second cumulant: $[\kappa_2(n_B - n_{\bar{B}})/\kappa_2(Skellam)]_{CE} = 1 - \alpha$, where $\alpha$ is the fraction of baryons falling into the experimental acceptance.
- The CE result for the normalized fourth cumulant: $[\kappa_4(n_B - n_{\bar{B}})/\kappa_2(n_B - n_{\bar{B}})]_{CE} = 1 - 6\alpha(1-\alpha) \times F$, where $F$ is a function of full-phase-space and acceptance baryon/anti-baryon numbers.
- The Monte Carlo simulation used $\langle N_B \rangle = 370$ and $\langle N_{\bar{B}} \rangle = 20$ for baryons and anti-baryons respectively in the full phase space.
- The quantitative description of STAR $\kappa_4/\kappa_2$ data holds above $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 11.5$ GeV; the points at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7$ and $11.5$ GeV are exceptions.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Braun-Munziger et al., 2017) on participant fluctuations by adding the treatment of exact baryon number conservation via the Canonical Ensemble. It builds on earlier treatments of conservation law effects by Koch (2008) and by Schuster et al. (2009) and Keranen et al. (2004). The results are directly relevant for interpreting STAR Beam Energy Scan data and will inform the research programs at FAIR at GSI and NICA at JINR. The paper also notes that ALICE measurements at LHC energies can be explained by baryon number conservation alone (Rustamov, 2017).
