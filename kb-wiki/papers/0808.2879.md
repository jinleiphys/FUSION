# Critical temperature of antikaon condensation in nuclear matter

> We investigate the critical temperature of Bose-Einstein condensation of $K^-$ mesons in neutron star matter. This is studied within the framework of relativistic field theoretical models at finite temperature where nucleon-nucleon and (anti)kaon-nucleon interactions are mediated by the exchange of mesons. The melting of the antikaon condensate is studied for different values of antikaon optical potential depths. We find that the critical temperature of antikaon condensation increases with baryon number density. Further it is noted that the critical temperature is lowered as antikaon optical potential becomes less attractive. We also construct the phase diagram of neutron star matter with $K^-$ condensate.

## Key claim

The critical temperature of antikaon condensation in neutron star matter increases with baryon number density and is lowered when the antikaon optical potential becomes less attractive; the condensate melts at a temperature defined as the critical temperature, and for a given baryon density the critical temperature is larger for an optical potential depth of -160 MeV than for -100 MeV.

## Method

The authors used relativistic field theoretical models at finite temperature, where nucleon-nucleon and (anti)kaon-nucleon interactions are mediated by the exchange of sigma, omega, and rho mesons. They studied the melting of the antikaon condensate for antikaon optical potential depths of -100 MeV and -160 MeV, determining the critical temperature as the point where the condensate density drops to zero with increasing temperature. The calculation was performed for beta-equilibrated neutron star matter with fixed entropy per baryon, using the GM1 parameter set for nucleon-meson couplings.

## Key numbers

- Threshold density for K- condensation at zero temperature: 3.4 n0 for U_K = -100 MeV and 2.4 n0 for U_K = -160 MeV, where n0 = 0.153 fm^-3 is the normal nuclear matter density.
- For entropy per baryon S=2, threshold densities shift to 4.0 n0 for U_K = -100 MeV and 2.7 n0 for U_K = -160 MeV.
- Nuclear matter saturation properties used: binding energy -16 MeV, saturation density 0.153 fm^-3, asymmetry energy coefficient 32.5 MeV, effective nucleon mass m_N*/m_n = 0.70, and incompressibility K = 300 MeV.
- For U_K = -160 MeV, entropy per baryon was varied from S = 0 to 5.5 to obtain critical temperatures; for U_K = -100 MeV, S was varied from 0 to 2.75.

## Context

This work extends the study of antikaon condensation in neutron star matter, previously investigated at zero temperature by Gle99, Bani2, and others, to finite temperature where the melting of the condensate had not been examined. The authors build on the finite-temperature treatment of antikaon condensation by Pons et al. (2001) and use the GM1 parameter set from Gle91. The results enable construction of a phase diagram for beta-equilibrated nuclear matter with K- condensate, which may be relevant for future heavy ion experiments at FAIR, GSI, where matter with density a few times normal nuclear matter density and temperature a few tens of MeV could be produced.
