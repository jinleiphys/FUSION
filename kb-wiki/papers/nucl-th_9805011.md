# Chiral Dynamics of Low-Energy Kaon-Baryon Interactions with Explicit Resonance

> The processes involving low energy $\bar{K}N$ and $Yπ$ interactions (where $Y= Σ$ or $Λ$) are studied in the framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory with the $Λ$(1405) resonance appearing as an independent field. The leading and next-to-leading terms in the chiral expansion are taken into account. We show that an approach which explicitly includes the $Λ$(1405) resonance as an elementary quantum field gives reasonable descriptions of both the threshold branching ratios and the energy dependence of total cross sections.

## Key claim

The authors show that including the Λ(1405) resonance as an elementary quantum field in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, rather than generating it dynamically through iteration of a potential, gives reasonable descriptions of both threshold branching ratios and energy dependence of total cross sections for low-energy K⁻p and Yπ interactions, provided that next-to-leading order terms are included.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective chiral Lagrangian for low-energy K⁻p and Yπ interactions using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, including both leading-order (O(Q)) and next-to-leading order (O(Q²)) terms, with the Λ(1405) resonance introduced as an independent quantum field with a coupling constant g²_Λ* = 0.15 extracted from the Λ(1405) → πΣ decay width. They fixed the low-energy constants {b} from baryon mass splittings and the πN sigma term, then constrained the {d} constants using the isospin-even πN S-wave scattering length a⁺_πN and isospin zero kaon-nucleon S-wave scattering length a⁰_KN, with the remaining three undetermined constants fitted to experimental total cross sections for K⁻p reactions.

## Key numbers

- Λ(1405) coupling constant: g²_Λ* = 0.15, extracted from the decay width Γ = 50 MeV for Λ(1405) → πΣ
- Low-energy constants: b_D = 0.06 GeV⁻¹, b_F = -0.22 GeV⁻¹, b_0 = -0.34 GeV⁻¹
- Isospin-even πN S-wave scattering length: a⁺_πN = (-0.012 ± 0.06) fm
- Isospin zero kaon-nucleon S-wave scattering length: a⁰_KN = (-0.1 ± 0.1) fm
- Threshold branching ratio γ = Γ(K⁻p → π⁺Σ⁻)/Γ(K⁻p → π⁻Σ⁺) = 2.36 ± 0.04 (experimental)
- Threshold branching ratio R_c = Γ(K⁻p → charged particles)/Γ(K⁻p → all) = 0.664 ± 0.011 (experimental)
- Threshold branching ratio R_n = Γ(K⁻p → π⁰Λ)/Γ(K⁻p → all neutral states) = 0.189 ± 0.015 (experimental)
- Second-order contributions to γ and R_c constitute 18% and 48% corrections, respectively, to the first-order results

## Context

This work extends the approach of Lee, Min, and Rho (1996), who first proposed treating the Λ(1405) as an elementary field in the chiral Lagrangian but only used leading-order terms and did not compare with experimental cross sections. The results are compared with those of Kaiser, Siegel, and Weise (1995), who generated the Λ(1405) dynamically by iterating a chiral potential in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation; both approaches give similar cross sections despite very different expansion constants. The authors note that loop corrections at O(Q³) are needed to improve the description of the K⁻p → π⁰Λ channel and the R_n branching ratio, but such calculations are not currently feasible due to the large number of undetermined constants.
