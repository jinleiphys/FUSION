# Nuclei with enhanced Schiff moments in practical elements for atomic and molecular EDM measurements

> This is a resource paper concerning enhancement of observable time-reversal breaking effects by nuclear structure, aimed at AMO experimentalists. It's intended to support a white paper by providing some orientation on what can be said about some particular isotopes. Any conclusions are qualitative, and the reader should consult and cite the primary references and reviews rather than this arXiv alone.

## Key claim
This resource paper qualitatively surveys which isotopes in practical elements may exhibit enhanced Schiff moments from nuclear octupole deformation (static or vibrational), but explicitly states that any conclusions are qualitative and that readers should consult primary references rather than this arXiv paper alone.

## Method
The author informally examines suggested low-lying parity doublets in nuclei of interest to AMO experimentalists, evaluating the extent to which existing experimental nuclear structure information and microscopic calculations can identify and quantify possible Schiff moment enhancement. The analysis distinguishes between static octupole deformation and collective octupole vibrations, noting that both can enhance Schiff moments but require different experimental signatures. The paper reviews available data on charge radii changes with neutrons (IDNCR), B(E3) and B(E1) transition strengths, and g-factor measurements for candidate isotopes across rare earths and Z≥86 nuclei.

## Key numbers
- $^{229}$Pa parity doublet energy splitting: 60±50 eV (Ahmad 2015)
- $^{223}$Fr parity-doublet splitting: 161 keV (Sheline 1995)
- $^{229}$Th vibrational octupole partner band energy splitting: 146 keV (Ruchowska 2006, Gulda 2002)
- $^{153}$Eu Schiff moment enhancement: 30x over spherical nuclei (Sushkov 2024)
- $^{152,154}$Eu half-lives: 13 and 8 years (Sheline 1989)
- $^{225}$Ac half-life: 10 days (Ahmad 1993)
- $^{153}$Eu g-factors of opposite-parity band: about 3 times larger than ground state band (Smith 1998, Pearson 1994)
- $^{227}$Ac B(E1) values: an order of magnitude smaller than other static octupole cases (Ahmad 1993)

## Context
This paper extends the long-standing effort to identify definitive experimental nuclear signatures for static and vibrational octupole deformation (Ahmad 1993, Butler 1996, Butler 2016, Butler 2020a) and applies it to practical isotopes for AMO EDM experiments. It builds on the theoretical framework of Engel, Friar, and Hayes (2000) showing that Schiff moment enhancement from collective octupole vibrations scales with the inverse of parity doublet energy splitting, and on Flambaum and Dzuba (2020) and Flambaum and Feldmeier (2020) suggesting stable rare earth candidates. The work enables experimentalists to prioritize isotopes for EDM searches, particularly those beyond $^{225}$Ra that are long-lived enough for use away from isotope production facilities, and identifies the need for microscopic calculations with quantified uncertainty to move from discovery-phase experiments to multiple complementary measurements extracting different TRV sources.
