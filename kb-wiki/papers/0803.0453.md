# Suppression of the Shear Viscosity in a "semi" Quark Gluon Plasma

> We consider QCD at temperatures T near Tc, where the theory deconfines. We distinguish between a "complete" Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP), where the vacuum expectation value of the renormalized Polyakov loop is near unity, essentially constant with T, and the "semi"-QGP, where the loop changes strongly with T. Lattice simulations indicate that in QCD, there is a semi-QGP from below Tc to a few times that. Using a semi-classical model, we compute the shear viscosity, eta, to leading order in perturbation theory. We find that near Tc, where the expectation value of the Polyakov loop is small, that eta/T^3 is suppressed by two powers of the loop. For heavy ions, this suggests that during the initial stages of the collision, hydrodynamic behavior at the LHC will be characterized by a shear viscosity which is significantly larger than that at RHIC.

# Suppression of the Shear Viscosity in a "semi" Quark Gluon Plasma

## Key claim
The shear viscosity eta divided by T^3 is suppressed by two powers of the Polyakov loop near the critical temperature Tc, where the loop expectation value is small. This suppression arises from a background field that universally reduces the propagation of colored fields, rather than from strong coupling, and implies that hydrodynamic behavior at the LHC may be characterized by a significantly larger shear viscosity than at RHIC during the initial stages of collision.

## Method
The authors compute the shear viscosity to leading order in the gauge coupling g^2 and in log(1/g) using a semi-classical model that expands about a constant, diagonal background field A_0^cl = Q/g for the timelike component of the vector potential in an SU(Nc) gauge theory. They treat the Polyakov loop expectation value as an input from lattice simulations, assume infinite Nc and Nf/Nc fixed, and use a Boltzmann equation with hard thermal loop (HTL) approximations for soft gluon exchange in 2-to-2 scattering processes, expanding the statistical distribution functions as power series in moments of the Wilson line.

## Key numbers
- For a SU(3) gauge theory without quarks, the renormalized Polyakov loop is zero below Tc; at Tc^+ it is approximately 0.5; it crosses one at about 2.8 Tc; and reaches about 1.1 by about 4 Tc, remaining constant up to about 12 Tc.
- In QCD with 2+1 flavors of dynamical quarks, the Polyakov loop is very small below 0.8 Tc; it rises to about 0.3 at Tc; and is near one at about 2 Tc.
- The shear viscosity result is given by eta/T^3 = c_eta / (g^4 log(1/g)) * f(L), where f(L)=1 when the Wilson line L=1.
- At small loop values, f(L) approximately equals a_2(lambda) * ((ell + 4 lambda)/(ell + lambda)) * ell^2, where ell is the Polyakov loop, lambda = Nf/Nc, a_2(0) is about 3.31, and a_2(1) is about 1.01.
- The effective coupling at Tc from a two-loop computation in an effective three-dimensional theory is alpha_s^eff approximately 0.3.

## Context
This work extends the semi-QGP concept developed by the authors and collaborators (Hidaka and Pisarski, 2008) to transport properties, contrasting with the "strong" QGP picture where eta/s is small and temperature-independent as in N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory (Kovtun, Son, and Starinets, 2005). The calculation uses the background field ansatz previously applied to the QCD pressure (Pisarski, 2006) and to interfaces (Dumitru, Guo, and others). The result suggests a minimum in eta/T^3 near Tc, analogous to non-relativistic systems (Csernai, Kapusta, and McLerran, 2006), and predicts qualitatively different hydrodynamic behavior between RHIC and LHC energies, testable via lattice simulations (Meyer, 2007).
