# Chiral phase transition in a covariant nonlocal NJL model

> The properties of the chiral phase transition at finite temperature and chemical potential are investigated within a nonlocal covariant extension of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model based on a separable quark-quark interaction. We consider both the situation in which the Minkowski quark propagator has poles at real energies and the case where only complex poles appear. In the literature, the latter has been proposed as a realization of confinement. In both cases, the behaviour of the physical quantities as functions of T and μis found to be quite similar. In particular, for low values of T the chiral transition is always of first order and, for finite quark masses, at certain "end point" the transition turns into a smooth crossover. In the chiral limit, this "end point" becomes a "tricritical" point. Our predictions for the position of these points are similar, although somewhat smaller, than previous estimates. Finally, the relation between the deconfining transition and chiral restoration is also discussed.

## Key claim

The chiral phase transition in a covariant nonlocal NJL model is found to be qualitatively similar whether the quark propagator has real poles (non-confining) or only complex poles (confining), with the predicted location of the tricritical/end point being somewhat smaller than previous estimates from alternative models.

## Method

The authors extend a nonlocal SU(2) NJL model with a separable quark-quark interaction to finite temperature and chemical potential using the imaginary time formalism, expressing the gap equation, quark condensate, and quark density in terms of sums over the poles of the Euclidean quark propagator. They numerically solve these equations for a Gaussian regulator using two parameter sets: Set I (confining, no purely imaginary poles) and Set II (non-confining, with two pairs of purely imaginary poles), including only the first few poles in the sums.

## Key numbers

- Set I parameters: G = 50 GeV⁻², m_c = 10.5 MeV, Λ = 627 MeV; Set II: G = 30 GeV⁻², m_c = 7.7 MeV, Λ = 760 MeV
- Zero-momentum self-energy at T=μ=0: Σ(0) = 350 MeV (Set I), Σ(0) = 300 MeV (Set II)
- Chiral quark condensate at T=μ=0: −(200 MeV)³ (Set I), −(220 MeV)³ (Set II)
- Tricritical point (chiral limit) at (T_P, μ_P) = (70 MeV, 130 MeV) for Set I and (70 MeV, 140 MeV) for Set II
- End point (finite quark mass) at (T_E, μ_E) = (70 MeV, 180 MeV) for Set I and (55 MeV, 210 MeV) for Set II
- Critical self-energy for confinement loss in Set I: Σ(0)_crit = 267 MeV
- Critical temperature at μ=0: about 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nonlocal NJL models at finite temperature (BBKMT00) to include finite chemical potential, and provides a covariant alternative to the non-covariant nonlocal model used by Berges and Rajagopal (1998). The predicted tricritical point (T_P ≈ 70 MeV, μ_P ≈ 130-140 MeV) is smaller than the estimates of Berges and Rajagopal (1998) and Halasz et al. (1998) (T_P ≈ 100 MeV, μ_P ≈ 200-230 MeV). The results enable future extensions to include vector/axial-vector meson channels, color superconductivity, and SU(3) flavor to study the role of strangeness.
