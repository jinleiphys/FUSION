# Quark mass effects on the stability of hybrid stars

> We perform a study of the possible existence of hybrid stars with color superconducting quark cores using a specific hadronic model in a combination with an NJL-type quark model. It is shown that the constituent mass of the non-strange quarks in vacuum is a very important parameter that controls the beginning of the hadron-quark phase transition. At relatively small values of the mass, the first quark phase that appears is the two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phase which, at larger densities, is replaced by the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase. At large values of the mass, on the other hand, the phase transition goes from the hadronic phase directly into the CFL phase avoiding the 2SC phase. It appears, however, that the only stable hybrid stars obtained are those with the 2SC quark cores.

## Key claim

The existence of stable hybrid stars with color-superconducting quark cores depends critically on the value of the constituent mass of non-strange quarks in vacuum; only when this mass is relatively small (around 335 MeV or less) can a two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phase appear and yield stable hybrid stars, whereas larger masses (368 MeV) force a direct transition to the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase, which always produces unstable configurations.

## Method

The authors combined a specific hadronic equation of state (from Hanauske et al.) with an NJL-type quark model for three-flavor quark matter, using two different parameter sets (RKH and HK) that give different vacuum constituent masses for up/down quarks. They computed the pressure as a function of baryon chemical potential for electrically and color neutral matter in beta equilibrium, identified first-order phase transitions between hadronic, normal quark, 2SC, and CFL phases, and then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations for static compact stars.

## Key numbers

* Vacuum constituent mass of non-strange quarks: 368 MeV for the RKH parameter set (used in Buballa et al. 2002) versus 335 MeV for the HK parameter set (21 MeV larger than the 314 MeV used in Shovkovy and Huang 2003).
* Critical baryon chemical potential for hadron-to-2SC transition with HK parameters: 1216 MeV, with baryon densities of 3.0 rho_0 below and 3.4 rho_0 above the transition, and energy densities of 536 and 627 MeV/fm^3 respectively.
* Critical baryon chemical potential for hadron-to-CFL transition with RKH parameters: 1284 MeV, with baryon densities of 3.6 rho_0 below and 6.0 rho_0 above, and energy densities of 657 and 1188 MeV/fm^3.
* Stable hybrid stars with 2SC cores exist only in a mass window between 1.62 and 1.66 solar masses, with a maximum 2SC core radius of 3.5 km (about one quarter of the total star radius).

## Context

This work resolves apparent contradictions between earlier NJL-model studies: Buballa et al. (2002) found no stable hybrid stars with pure quark cores, while Shovkovy and Huang (2003) found stable hybrid stars with 2SC cores but without including strange quarks. The present paper shows that the difference stems primarily from the vacuum constituent mass of non-strange quarks, and demonstrates that even when strange quarks are included, stable hybrid stars can exist if the 2SC phase appears first. The results contrast with bag-model predictions by Alford and Reddy (2003) and with the argument by Alford and Rajagopal (2002) that the 2SC phase is absent in compact stars. The study enables future systematic investigations of how the diquark coupling strength, strange quark mass, and hadronic equation of state affect the stability window for hybrid stars.
