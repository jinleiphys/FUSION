# Spectroscopy of reflection-asymmetric nuclei with relativistic energy density functionals

> Quadrupole and octupole deformation energy surfaces, low-energy excitation spectra and transition rates in fourteen isotopic chains: Xe, Ba, Ce, Nd, Sm, Gd, Rn, Ra, Th, U, Pu, Cm, Cf, and Fm, are systematically analyzed using a theoretical framework based on a quadrupole-octupole collective Hamiltonian (QOCH), with parameters determined by constrained reflection-asymmetric and axially-symmetric relativistic mean-field calculations. The microscopic QOCH model based on the PC-PK1 energy density functional and $δ$-interaction pairing is shown to accurately describe the empirical trend of low-energy quadrupole and octupole collective states, and predicted spectroscopic properties are consistent with recent microscopic calculations based on both relativistic and non-relativistic energy density functionals. Low-energy negative-parity bands, average octupole deformations, and transition rates show evidence for octupole collectivity in both mass regions, for which a microscopic mechanism is discussed in terms of evolution of single-nucleon orbitals with deformation.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the quadrupole-octupole collective Hamiltonian (QOCH) based on the PC-PK1 relativistic energy density functional and delta-interaction pairing accurately describes the empirical trend of low-energy quadrupole and octupole collective states across fourteen isotopic chains, but the authors note that the model does not include triaxial deformations or two-quasiparticle configurations, which may explain discrepancies in nearly spherical nuclei.

## Method
The authors performed constrained reflection-asymmetric and axially-symmetric relativistic mean-field (RMF+BCS) calculations to determine the collective potential, mass parameters, and moments of inertia for each nucleus. These microscopic inputs were used to construct a quadrupole-octupole collective Hamiltonian (QOCH), which was then diagonalized to obtain excitation spectra and transition rates for even-even nuclei in two mass regions: medium-heavy (Z=54-64, N=84-100) and heavy (Z=86-100, N=130-152).

## Key numbers
- 150 nuclei were calculated across fourteen isotopic chains: Xe, Ba, Ce, Nd, Sm, Gd, Rn, Ra, Th, U, Pu, Cm, Cf, and Fm.
- For the illustrative case of 224Ra, the global minimum of the deformation energy surface occurs at (beta2, beta3) = (0.18, 0.14).
- The average calculated Q2 value for 224Ra is approximately 650 e fm^2, consistent with the measured value of 632 +/- 10 e fm^2 (Gaff13).
- The calculated Q3 moments for 224Ra are almost constant at approximately 2950 e fm^3.
- For medium-heavy nuclei, the pairing strength parameters used were Vn = 353.0 MeV fm^3 and Vp = 367.0 MeV fm^3, adjusted to the spherical configuration of 144Ba.
- For heavy nuclei, the pairing strengths were Vn = 319.0 MeV fm^3 and Vp = 358.3 MeV fm^3, adjusted to the spherical configuration of 224Ra.
- The octupole deformation beta3 at equilibrium minima for octupole-deformed nuclei ranges between 0.13 and 0.16, with a gain in binding energy of approximately 0.5 MeV.
- Ground-state octupole deformation (beta3) peaks at N approximately 90 for medium-heavy nuclei and at N approximately 136 for heavy nuclei, with values greater than or equal to 0.12 for octupole-deformed nuclei and approximately 0.09 for octupole-soft nuclei.

## Context
This work extends previous systematic studies of octupole correlations using relativistic energy density functionals (Agbe 2016, Nomura 2014) and non-relativistic functionals (Robledo 2013, Nomura 2015), as well as macroscopic-microscopic models (Moller 2008). The QOCH approach provides a computationally efficient alternative to full generator coordinate method calculations (Bernard 2016, Yao 2015, Zhou 2016), enabling systematic surveys across multiple isotopic chains. The results enable future comparisons with experimental data from Coulomb excitation experiments with radioactive beams, particularly for nuclei such as 224Ra (Gaff 2013), 144Ba (Bucher 2016), and 146Ba (Bucher 2017).
