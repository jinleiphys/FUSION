# Toward emulating nuclear reactions using eigenvector continuation

> We construct an efficient emulator for two-body scattering observables using the general (complex) Kohn variational principle and trial wave functions derived from eigenvector continuation. The emulator simultaneously evaluates an array of Kohn variational principles associated with different boundary conditions, which allows for the detection and removal of spurious singularities known as Kohn anomalies. When applied to the $K$-matrix only, our emulator resembles the one constructed by Furnstahl et al. [Phys. Lett. B 809, 135719] although with reduced numerical noise. After a few applications to real potentials, we emulate differential cross sections for $^{40}$Ca$(n,n)$ scattering based on a realistic optical potential and quantify the model uncertainties using Bayesian methods. These calculations serve as a proof of principle for future studies aimed at improving optical models.

## Key claim
Eigenvector continuation (EC) can be extended to construct efficient emulators for two-body scattering observables by using the general (complex) Kohn variational principle (KVP), which allows for the detection and removal of spurious Kohn anomalies, but the method’s accuracy is contingent on the training set and the specific KVPs used, as anomalies can persist if all considered KVPs are anomalous at overlapping regions in phase space.

## Method
The authors construct trial wave functions as linear combinations of exact scattering solutions for a set of training potentials using eigenvector continuation. They then apply an array of Kohn variational principles with different asymptotic boundary conditions (e.g., for the K-, S-, and T-matrices) to obtain stationary approximations for scattering observables. To mitigate Kohn anomalies, they perform a relative consistency check between the S-matrix estimates from different KVPs, using a weighted sum of averages for consistent pairs, and iteratively remove batches of training wave functions if all checks fail.

## Key numbers
- For $^{40}$Ca$(n,n)$ scattering at $E=5$ MeV, the emulator (mixed approach with $N_b=8$) yielded mean parameter values and standard deviations in good agreement with the exact solution; e.g., the emulated volume potential depth $V_v$ was $54.82 \pm 8.95$ MeV compared to the exact $52.39 \pm 7.94$ MeV.
- The relative consistency check for detecting Kohn anomalies uses a threshold of $\varepsilon_\text{rel} = 10^{-1}$.
- The radial Schrodinger equation was solved numerically with relative and absolute tolerances set to $10^{-9}$ or less.

## Context
This work extends the eigenvector continuation emulator for the K-matrix developed by Furnstahl et al. (2020) to a general KVP framework, enabling the simultaneous evaluation of multiple scattering matrices (K, S, T) and the automated detection of Kohn anomalies. It serves as a proof of principle for applying Bayesian parameter estimation to optical models for reactions like $^{40}$Ca$(n,n)$ scattering, enabling future studies aimed at improving optical models by leveraging computationally inexpensive emulators for Monte Carlo sampling.
