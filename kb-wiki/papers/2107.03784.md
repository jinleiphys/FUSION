# $Ξ$ hyper-nuclear states predicted by NLO chiral baryon-baryon interactions

> The $Ξ$ single-particle potential obtained in nuclear matter with the next-to-leading order baryon-baryon interactions in chiral effective field theory is applied to finite nuclei by an improved local-density approximation method. As a premise, phase shifts of $ΞN$ elastic scattering and the results of Faddeev calculations for the $ΞNN$ bound state problem are presented to show the properties of the $ΞN$ interactions in the present parametrization. First, the $Ξ$ states in $^{14}$N are revisited because of the recent experimental progress, including the discussion on the $ΞN$ spin-orbit interaction that is relevant to the location of the $p$-state. Then the $Ξ$ levels in $^{56}$Fe are calculated. In particular, the level shift which is expected to be measured experimentally in the near future is predicted. The smallness of the imaginary part of the $Ξ$ single-particle potential is explicitly demonstrated.

## Key claim
The chiral NLO baryon-baryon interactions predict that the $\Xi$ single-particle potential in nuclei is shallow but attractive enough to support bound states, though no $\Xi NN$ three-body bound state is expected. The predicted atomic level shift for $\Xi^-$ in $^{56}$Fe is small (about 1.78 keV for the $\ell=4$ state), and the imaginary part of the $\Xi$ potential is very small, but these predictions carry uncertainties from the $G$-matrix calculations and the improved local-density approximation method.

## Method
The authors used next-to-leading order (NLO) chiral effective field theory baryon-baryon interactions from the Jülich-Bonn-München group to calculate $\Xi N$ $G$-matrices in symmetric nuclear matter via a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach. These $G$-matrices were then applied to finite nuclei ($^{14}$N and $^{56}$Fe) using an improved local-density approximation (ILDA) method with Gaussian smearing. For $^{14}$N, the single-particle energies were computed with an energy-dependent potential, and the effect of a $\Xi$ spin-orbit potential was estimated using the Scheerbaum factor formalism. For $^{56}$Fe, the potential was fitted to a Woods-Saxon form and used to solve the Schrödinger equation for bound and atomic states, including the Coulomb potential from a uniform charge distribution.

## Key numbers
- $\Xi^-$ binding energy in $^{14}$N: experimental $0p$ state at $1.27 \pm 0.21$ MeV (J-PARC) and $0.90 \pm 0.62$ MeV (KEK/J-PARC compilation); experimental $0s$ candidates at $8.00 \pm 0.77$, $4.96 \pm 0.77$, and $6.27 \pm 0.27$ MeV.
- Calculated $\Xi^-$ $0s$ energy in $^{14}$N: $-5.40$ MeV with the ILDA potential; a factor of $1.10$ to $1.19$ is needed to reproduce the $6.27$ MeV candidate.
- Scheerbaum factor for $\Xi$ in symmetric nuclear matter: $S_\Xi \simeq 21.5$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^5$ at $\bar{q} \approx 0.7$ fm$^{-1}$, about one-fifth of the nucleon value $S_N = 102$ MeV$\cdot$fm$^5$.
- $\Xi$-$^{56}$Fe single-particle potential fitted to Woods-Saxon form: real part depth $V_R = -8.39$ MeV, radius $R_{0,R} = 5.01$ fm, diffuseness $a_R = 0.499$ fm; imaginary part depth $V_I = -0.247$ MeV, radius $R_{0,I} = 5.32$ fm, diffuseness $a_I = 0.303$ fm.
- Predicted atomic level shift for $\Xi^-$ in $^{56}$Fe: $\ell=4$ state shift $\Delta e = 1.78$ keV (from $e_C = -927.87$ keV to $e_{real} = -929.66$ keV), with width $\Gamma = 0.20$ keV.
- Widths of $\Xi$ states in $^{56}$Fe are at most 0.5 MeV; the $\ell=4$ state width is $0.20$ keV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Kohno, 2019) on $\Xi$ potentials in light nuclei ($^9$Be, $^{12}$C, $^{14}$N) using the same chiral NLO interactions, now revisiting $^{14}$N in light of new experimental data from J-PARC (Hayakawa et al., 2021) and a compilation by Yoshimoto et al. (2021). It also extends the calculation to $^{56}$Fe, for which an atomic level shift measurement is planned at J-PARC. The results are compared with predictions from HAL-QCD lattice QCD parametrizations (Inoue et al., 2019; Sasaki et al., 2020) and with earlier Faddeev calculations using the Nijmegen ESC08c model (Filikhin et al., 2017) that found $\Xi NN$ bound states, which the present chiral interactions do not support. The work enables future experimental tests of the $\Xi$-nucleus potential via X-ray spectroscopy and inclusive $(K^-,K^+)$ reactions.
