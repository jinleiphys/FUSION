# Matter-induced omega -> pi pi decay

> We calculate the width for the omega -> pi pi decay in nuclear matter in a hadronic model including mesons, nucleons and Delta isobars. We find a substantial width of the longitudinally polarized omega modes, reaching ~100MeV for mesons moving suitably fast with respect to the nuclear medium.

## Key claim
Nuclear matter induces a substantial width for the decay ω → ππ, reaching ~100 MeV for longitudinally polarized ω mesons moving with momentum above ~200 MeV relative to the medium, but only when the ω mass is reduced to 70% of its vacuum value; the effect is proportional to the square of the baryon density.

## Method
The authors calculate the in-medium ω → ππ decay width using a hadronic effective theory with mesons, nucleons, and Δ(1232) isobars, working to leading order in nuclear density. They include four classes of Feynman diagrams: ω-σ mixing (bubble diagram), triangle nucleon diagrams, and two diagrams involving Δ isobars. The amplitude is decomposed into longitudinal and transverse polarization components, and the width is evaluated at nuclear saturation density (ρ_B = 0.17 fm⁻³) as a function of the ω three-momentum |q|.

## Key numbers
- Longitudinal width reaches ~250 MeV for m_ω* = m_ω = 780 MeV, and ~100 MeV for m_ω* = 0.7 m_ω, at peak |q| of a few hundred MeV.
- Transverse width is strictly zero with only diagram (a), less than 1 MeV with diagrams (a-b), and reaches a few MeV when Δ isobar diagrams are included.
- At |q| = 400 MeV and q² = m_ω², the transverse-mode contribution to the ππ annihilation cross section is 0.4 μb for m_ω* = m_ω (Γ^T = 1.8 MeV) and 1.4 μb for m_ω* = 0.7 m_ω (Γ^T = 5 MeV).
- The σ meson parameters used are m_σ = 789 MeV and Γ_σ = 237 MeV, fitted to ππ scattering at q² = (780 MeV)².
- The coupling constants used: g_ω = 9, g_πNN = 12.7, g_σππ = 12.8 m_π (m_π = 139.6 MeV), f_πNΔ = 2.1, and Δ width Γ_Δ = 120 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the earlier calculation by Wolf, Friman, and Soyeur (1998), who only considered the ω-σ mixing mechanism (diagram a), by adding triangle nucleon diagrams and Δ isobar contributions. The results show that the full calculation substantially increases the width compared to the ω-σ mixing alone. The process is relevant for understanding dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for the CERES and HELIOS experiments, and the authors note that the ππ annihilation cross section via the ω channel (which they calculate) can be compared to the 3.5 μb from decay via the ρ resonance. The work enables improved Monte-Carlo simulations of heavy-ion collisions and suggests that momentum-dependent measurements of dilepton pairs could probe in-medium meson dynamics.
