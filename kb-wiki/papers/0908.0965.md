# Nonextensive Quantum H-Theorem

> A proof of the quantum $H$-theorem taking into account nonextensive effects on the quantum entropy $S^Q_q$ is shown. The positiveness of the time variation of $S^Q_q$ combined with a duality transformation implies that the nonextensive parameter $q$ lies in the interval [0,2]. It is also shown that the equilibrium states are described by quantum $q$-power law extensions of the Fermi-Dirac and Bose-Einstein distributions. Such results reduce to the standard ones in the extensive limit, thereby showing that the nonextensive entropic framework can be harmonized with the quantum distributions contained in the quantum statistics theory.

## Key claim

The paper proves that the quantum $H$-theorem holds for the Tsallis $q$-entropy in the quantum regime, but only when the nonextensive parameter $q$ lies in the interval [0,2]; outside this range the entropy becomes a decreasing function of time, violating the second law.

## Method

The authors start from the standard quantum kinetic equation for the time derivative of the occupation number $n_\kappa$ under binary collisions, using the collision term $Z_{\kappa\lambda,\mu\nu} = A_{\kappa\lambda,\mu\nu} n_\kappa n_\lambda (g_\mu \pm n_\mu)(g_\nu \pm n_\nu)$ with the upper sign for bosons and lower for fermions. They introduce a generalized quantum $q$-entropy $S_q^Q$ built from $q$-logarithms and $q$-exponentials, compute its time derivative, and symmetrize the resulting expression. By applying the duality transformation $q^* = 2-q$ and requiring positivity of the entropy production, they derive the allowed range for $q$ and obtain the equilibrium condition that yields the $q$-generalized Fermi-Dirac and Bose-Einstein distributions.

## Key numbers

- The nonextensive parameter $q$ is constrained to the interval $[0,2]$ by the positivity of $dS_q^Q/dt$ combined with the duality transformation $q^* = 2-q$.
- When $q=0$, the quantum $q$-entropy does not change with time ($dS_q^Q/dt = 0$).
- For $q < 0$ or $q > 2$, the quantum $q$-entropy is a decreasing function of time.
- In the limit $q \rightarrow 1$, the generalized quantum distributions reduce to the standard Fermi-Dirac and Bose-Einstein distributions.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic and relativistic nonextensive $H$-theorems previously proved by the same group (Silva et al., 2001; Silva et al., 2005) into the quantum domain, complementing Pauli's (1928) original quantum $H$-theorem. The derived $q$-quantum distributions reproduce the result originally obtained by Tsallis et al. (1995) for the Fermi-Dirac case with $g_\kappa=1$, and the $q \in [0,2]$ bound is consistent with constraints from Clausius' inequality (Abe, 2003) and other independent studies. The formalism enables future work on relativistic quantum nonextensive statistics and applications to systems with long-range interactions or strong correlations.
