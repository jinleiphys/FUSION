# Revisiting shear stress tensor evolution: Non-resistive magnetohydrodynamics with momentum-dependent relaxation time

> This study aims to develop second-order relativistic viscous magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) derived from kinetic theory within an extended relaxation time approximation (momentum/energy dependent) for the collision kernel. The investigation involves a detailed examination of shear stress tensor evolution equations and associated transport coefficients. The Boltzmann equation is solved using a Chapman-Enskog-like gradient expansion for a charge-conserved conformal system, incorporating a momentum-dependent relaxation time. The derived relativistic non-resistive, viscous second-order MHD equations for the shear stress tensor reveal significant modifications in the coupling with dissipative charge current and magnetic field due to the momentum dependence of the relaxation time. By utilizing a power law parametrization to quantify the momentum dependence of the relaxation time, the anisotropic magnetic field-dependent shear coefficients in the Navier-Stokes limit have been investigated. The resulting viscous coefficients are seen to be sensitive to the momentum dependence of the relaxation time.

## Key claim

The paper derives second-order relativistic viscous magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) from kinetic theory using a momentum-dependent relaxation time (Extended Relaxation Time Approximation, ERTA), finding that the coupling between shear stress tensor evolution and dissipative charge current vanishes in the conventional momentum-independent RTA but becomes nonzero when the relaxation time depends on particle energy.

## Method

The authors solved the Boltzmann equation using a Chapman-Enskog-like gradient expansion for a conformal, charge-conserved system of massless quarks and gluons, incorporating a momentum-dependent relaxation time parameterized as a power law in energy. They derived first-order and second-order evolution equations for the shear stress tensor in the absence of a magnetic field, then extended the formalism to include a magnetic field to obtain non-resistive, relativistic second-order MHD equations. The transport coefficients were expressed in terms of thermodynamic integrals that depend on the momentum-dependence parameter ℓ.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity in the Navier-Stokes limit: η₀ = (K⁺₃₂)/T, where K⁺₃₂ is a thermodynamic integral
- Relaxation time parameterization: τ_R(x,p) = τ₀(x) (u·p/T)^ℓ, with τ₀(x) = κ̄/T, where κ̄ is a dimensionless constant and ℓ is an arbitrary parameter
- Shear relaxation time: τ_π = (κ̄ Γ(5+2ℓ))/(T Γ(5+ℓ)) for ℓ > -5/2
- Shear viscosity coefficient: η₀ = (e^α d_g κ̄ Γ(5+ℓ))/(30 π² β³) for ℓ > -5
- Second-order coefficient τ_{ππ} = (2 Γ(6+2ℓ))/(7 Γ(5+ℓ)) for ℓ > -5/2

## Context

This work extends the ERTA framework developed by Dash et al. (2021, 2023) for relativistic hydrodynamics to the magnetized QCD medium, where previous studies by Jaiswal et al. (2015) within conventional RTA found that shear stress tensor and charge current evolution decouple for massless quarks and gluons. The authors show that momentum-dependent relaxation time introduces coupling terms (l_{πn}, τ_{πn}, λ_{πn}) that vanish when ℓ=0 (constant relaxation time), and these coefficients change sign with the sign of ℓ. The range 0 ≤ ℓ ≤ 1 is argued to be most relevant for the QCD medium based on work by Dusling et al. (2009) and Denicol et al. (2012). This formalism enables more realistic magnetohydrodynamic simulations of the quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions where the collision timescale depends on particle energy.
