# Charged particle to photon ratio in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC: a different point of view

> We report an alternative explanation of the variation of charged particle to photon ratio in Cu+Cu collisions and Au+Au collisions from 1.4 $\pm$ 0.1 to 1.2 $\pm$ 0.1 at $\sqrt{\it{s}_{\rm NN}}$ = 62.4 GeV and 200 GeV respectively in STAR experiment at RHIC. Based on the theoretical predictions and experimental results we argue that the additional contribution of direct photons at $\sqrt{\it{s}_{\rm NN}}$ = 200 GeV in both Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions are improving the ratio $\frac{N_{ch}}{N_γ}$ to 1.2 $\pm$ 0.1 from 1.4 $\pm$ 0.1.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed decrease in the charged-particle-to-photon ratio from 1.4 ± 0.1 at 62.4 GeV to 1.2 ± 0.1 at 200 GeV in both Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions is not due to a suppression of baryon contributions at higher energy, but rather to an additional contribution from direct photons at the higher energy that reach the forward-rapidity detector.

## Method

The authors compare experimental data from the STAR experiment for the ratio of charged particles to photons in the pseudorapidity range -3.7 < η < -2.3 for Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 62.4 GeV and 200 GeV. They examine three lines of evidence: the increase of direct photon cross-section with center-of-mass energy (comparing WA80 data at 19 GeV with PHENIX data at 200 GeV), theoretical predictions from the Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff (GBW) color dipole model showing direct photon production at forward rapidities, and experimental results from the WA80 experiment indicating that direct photons constitute approximately 15% of neutral meson production.

## Key numbers

- The charged-particle-to-photon ratio is 1.4 ± 0.1 for Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 62.4 GeV in the pseudorapidity range -3.7 < η < -2.3.
- The charged-particle-to-photon ratio is 1.2 ± 0.1 for Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV in the pseudorapidity range -3.7 < η < -2.3.
- Direct photons constituted no more than 15% of the inclusive photon yield for O+Au collisions at each pT in the range 0.4 < pT < 2.4 GeV/c, as observed in the WA80 experiment.

## Context

This work offers an alternative explanation to the conventional interpretation (Kharzeev 1996, Bearden 2003) that the energy dependence of the charged-particle-to-photon ratio arises from suppressed baryon contributions at higher energies. The authors instead argue that direct photons, which increase in yield and energy with collision energy, become detectable at forward rapidities only at √sNN = 200 GeV, thereby lowering the ratio. The paper extends the discussion of direct photon production in heavy-ion collisions by invoking the GBW color dipole model (Jalilian-Marian 2007, Jeon 2002) and experimental results from WA80 (Albrecht 1990) and ATLAS (Aad 2015) to support the existence of direct photons in forward regions.
