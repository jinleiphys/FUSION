# The damping width of giant dipole resonances of cold and hot nuclei: a macroscopic model

> A phenomenological macroscopic model of the Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) damping width of cold- and hot-nuclei with ground-state spherical and near-spherical shapes is developed. The model is based on a generalized Fermi Liquid model which takes into account the nuclear surface dynamics. The temperature dependence of the GDR damping width is accounted for in terms of surface- and volume-components. Parameter-free expressions for the damping width and the effective deformation are obtained. The model is validated with GDR measurements of the following nuclides, $^{39,40}$K, $^{42}$Ca, $^{45}$Sc, $^{59,63}$Cu, $^{109-120}$Sn,$^{147}$Eu, $^{194}$Hg, and $^{208}$Pb, and is compared with the predictions of other models.

# The damping width of giant dipole resonances of cold and hot nuclei: a macroscopic model

## Key claim

The authors develop a parameter-free macroscopic model based on a generalized Fermi liquid model that successfully describes the GDR damping width for cold and hot nuclei with spherical and near-spherical ground-state shapes, but the model is limited by the adiabatic coupling assumption at temperatures above about 3 MeV and by the phenomenological determination of the global parameter C₁ from ground-state data rather than from first principles.

## Method

The model combines a volume component from collisional damping in the Fermi liquid framework (quadratic in temperature) with a surface component from coupling of the GDR to quadrupole surface vibrations. The effective quadrupole deformation β(A,T,J) is derived from liquid drop model results combined with thermal shape fluctuation and angular momentum contributions added in quadrature. The single free global parameter C₁ is determined by fitting measured ground-state GDR widths of spherical and near-spherical nuclei with masses from 40 to 209 using nonlinear least-squares fitting.

## Key numbers

- Global parameter C₁ = 0.0131 ± 0.004 MeV⁻¹, determined from ground-state GDR width data with 95% confidence interval
- C₂ is set to the theoretical value 1.05 (derived from 2.35√(5/8π))
- In-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section at the Fermi energy: 49 ± 2 mb
- The model describes GDR widths for temperatures up to 3 MeV and angular momenta up to 60ħ
- For A ≈ 45, angular momentum effects on GDR width become significant at J = 10ħ; for A ≈ 110 at J = 30ħ; for A ≈ 180 at J = 40ħ
- For ¹⁹⁴Hg at <J> = 24ħ, 27ħ, 36ħ and average T = 1.3 MeV, measured GDR width is 6.2 ± 0.5 MeV; model predicts 6.6 MeV
- For ⁴⁵Sc, measurements cover <J> = 13ħ to 23.5ħ at temperatures 1.7 MeV and 2.3 MeV
- For ¹⁰⁹Sn, measurements cover <J> = 10ħ to 54ħ at average temperatures 1.4 MeV to 1.8 MeV
- For ¹⁴⁷Eu, measurements at average temperature 1.3 MeV and <J> = 37ħ to 55ħ

## Context

This work extends the generalized Fermi liquid model previously applied by the authors to E1 photon strength functions (Mughabghab and Sonzogni, prior work) and addresses the long-standing debate between the thermal shape fluctuation model (which predicts T¹/² dependence) and the Fermi liquid model (which predicts T² dependence). The model reconciles both mechanisms by including surface and volume components, and it enables determination of the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section at the Fermi energy, a quantity with large disagreement between theoretical predictions (Pandharipande and collaborators, 1990s; Li and Machleidt, 1990s). The approach can be extended to giant quadrupole and octupole resonances.
