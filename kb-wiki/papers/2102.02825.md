# The Fermi constant from muon decay versus electroweak fits and CKM unitarity

> The Fermi constant ($G_F$) is extremely well measured through the muon lifetime, defining one of the key fundamental parameters in the Standard Model (SM). Therefore, to search for physics beyond the SM (BSM) via $G_F$, the constraining power is determined by the precision of the second-best independent determination of $G_F$. The best alternative extractions of $G_F$ proceed either via the global electroweak (EW) fit or from superallowed $β$ decays in combination with the Cabibbo angle measured in kaon, $τ$, or $D$ decays. Both variants display some tension with $G_F$ from muon decay, albeit in opposite directions, reflecting the known tensions within the EW fit and hints for the apparent violation of CKM unitarity, respectively. We investigate how BSM physics could bring the three determinations of $G_F$ into agreement using SM effective field theory and comment on future perspectives.

# The Fermi constant from muon decay versus electroweak fits and CKM unitarity

## Key claim

The Fermi constant extracted from muon decay ($G_F^\mu$) disagrees with the value from the global electroweak fit at the $2\sigma$ level and with the value from superallowed beta decays assuming CKM unitarity at the $3\sigma$ level, but bringing all three determinations into agreement within $1\sigma$ requires beyond-Standard-Model effects in at least two of the underlying processes.

## Method

The authors performed a global electroweak fit in a Bayesian framework using the HEPfit package, including $W$ mass, $\sin^2\theta_W$, and $Z$-pole observables, to extract an independent value of $G_F$. They also extracted $G_F$ from superallowed beta decays by combining $|V_{ud}|$ with $|V_{us}|$ from kaon, tau, or $D$ decays under the assumption of first-row CKM unitarity. They then analyzed possible beyond-Standard-Model explanations of the tensions using dimension-6 operators in the Standard Model Effective Field Theory (SMEFT), classifying operators into four-fermion contributions to muon decay, four-fermion contributions to beta decay, modified $W$ couplings to quarks, modified $W$ couplings to leptons, and other operators affecting the electroweak fit.

## Key numbers

- $G_F^\mu = 1.1663787(6) \times 10^{-5} \text{ GeV}^{-2}$ from muon lifetime, at $0.5$ ppm precision
- $G_F^\text{EW} = 1.16716(39) \times 10^{-5} \text{ GeV}^{-2}$ from the global electroweak fit, lying $2\sigma$ above $G_F^\mu$
- $G_F^\text{CKM} = 1.16550(29) \times 10^{-5} \text{ GeV}^{-2}$ from superallowed beta decays and CKM unitarity, lying $3\sigma$ below $G_F^\mu$
- Tension between $G_F^\text{EW}$ and $G_F^\text{CKM}$ amounts to $3.4\sigma$
- First-row CKM unitarity sum: $|V_{ud}|^2 + |V_{us}|^2 + |V_{ub}|^2 = 0.9985(5)$
- To bring $G_F^\text{CKM}$ and $G_F^\mu$ into $1\sigma$ agreement via the four-fermion operator $Q_{\ell\ell}^{2112}$ requires $C_{\ell\ell}^{2112} \approx -1.4 \times 10^{-3} G_F$
- The Cabibbo angle anomaly prefers $C_{\ell q}^{(3)1111} \approx 0.7 \times 10^{-3} G_F$ for the four-fermion operator in $d \to u e \nu$ transitions
- ATLAS nonresonant dilepton searches constrain $C_{\ell q}^{(3)1111} \lesssim 1.6 \times 10^{-3} G_F$

## Context

This work extends the original proposal by Marciano (1999) to compare $G_F$ from muon decay with independent determinations, updating both the electroweak fit precision (improved by a factor of 5 since 1999) and the CKM unitarity determination. The tensions reflect the known tensions within the electroweak fit (Baak 2014, de Blas 2016) and the Cabibbo angle anomaly (Belfatto 2019, Bryman 2019, Crivellin 2020). The SMEFT analysis identifies which beyond-Standard-Model scenarios could simultaneously explain all three $G_F$ determinations, providing a benchmark for future experimental searches at LHC, Belle-II, and future $e^+e^-$ colliders such as FCC-ee, ILC, CEPC, or CLIC.
