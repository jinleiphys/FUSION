# Linear response in infinite nuclear matter as a tool to reveal finite size instabilities

> Nuclear effective interactions are often modelled by simple analytical expressions such as the Skyrme zero-range force. This effective interaction depends on a limited number of parameters that are usually fitted using experimental data obtained from doubly magic nuclei. It was recently shown that many Skyrme functionals lead to the appearance of instabilities, in particular when symmetries are broken, for example unphysical polarization of odd-even or rotating nuclei. In this article, we show how the formalism of the linear response in infinite nuclear matter can be used to predict and avoid the regions of parameters that are responsible for these unphysical instabilities.

## Key claim

The linear response formalism in infinite symmetric nuclear matter can predict the regions of parameter space that lead to finite-size spin and isospin instabilities in finite nuclei, but the exact correspondence between instabilities in infinite matter and nuclei requires further study due to nontrivial finite-size and shell effects.

## Method

The authors computed the RPA response function for symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the particle-hole propagator, using the residual interaction derived from the second functional derivative of the Skyrme energy density functional. They identified unstable modes as poles in the response function at zero excitation energy and mapped the critical densities (where such poles occur) as a function of momentum transfer q for different spin-isospin channels. They compared these critical densities to the saturation density of nuclear matter to determine stability conditions, and tested the method on two families of Skyrme functionals: modified SLy5 functionals with varying isovector gradient coefficient C1^Δρ, and the TIJ interaction series with tensor terms.

## Key numbers

- The coefficient C1^Δρ for the SLy5 interaction is 16.375 MeV fm^5; varying it from 16 MeV fm^5 to 37.1 MeV fm^5 in ^48Ca causes the total HF energy to decrease from -415.9 MeV to -417.2 MeV, with an isospin instability appearing at C1^Δρ ≈ 37.1 MeV fm^5
- The saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter used as reference is ρ_sat = 0.16 fm^-3
- For the modified SLy5 functionals in the S=0, I=1 channel, critical density curves are shown for C1^Δρ values of 16, 37, and 47 MeV fm^5, with poles approaching ρ_sat as C1^Δρ increases
- The TIJ interactions are defined by setting C0^T = 30(J+I-4) MeV fm^5 and C1^T = 30(J-I) MeV fm^5 for integers I and J from 1 to 6; none of these interactions satisfy the stability condition ρ_min > ρ_sat in all S=1 channels

## Context

This work extends earlier findings by Lesinski et al. (2006) that linked the coefficient C1^Δρ to isospin instabilities, and by Colo et al. (2010) who showed that Landau parameter constraints only avoid global polarizations of infinite matter, not finite-size instabilities. The method addresses recent observations of spin instabilities in cranked HFB calculations by Hellemans et al. and in odd-even nuclei calculations by Schunck et al., where time-reversal symmetry is broken. The linear response approach provides a numerically fast tool that could be incorporated into future fitting procedures for Skyrme effective forces and functionals, though the exact correspondence between infinite matter and finite nuclei instabilities remains to be established.
