# Anisotropic tomography of heavy quark dissociation by using general propagator structure at finite magnetic field

> In this work we have explored the imaginary part of the Heavy Quark (HQ) potential and subsequently the dissociation of heavy quarkonia at finite temperature and magnetic field. With respect to earlier investigations on this topic, present work contain three new ingredients. First one is considering all Landau level summation, for which present work can be applicable in entire magnetic field domain - from weak to strong. Second one is the general structure of the gauge boson propagator in a hot magnetized medium, which is used here in heavy quark potential problem first time. Third one is a rich anisotropic structure of the complex heavy quark potential, which explicitly depends on the longitudinal and transverse distance. By comparing with earlier references, we have attempted to display our new contributions by plotting heavy quark potential tomography and dissociation probability at finite temperature and magnetic field.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the imaginary part of the heavy quark potential and the subsequent dissociation of heavy quarkonia in a hot magnetized medium are significantly different from earlier calculations when using a general gauge boson propagator structure and summing over all Landau levels, rather than relying on strong-field (lowest Landau level) or weak-field approximations. The authors hedge that their results in both the weak and strong field limits do not merge with earlier estimations due to these new ingredients.

## Method
The authors compute the imaginary part of the heavy quark potential by relating it to the temporal component of the effective gluon propagator in a hot magnetized medium. They use a general structure for the gluon propagator, incorporating the full sum over all Landau levels for the fermionic loop contribution to the gluon self-energy, rather than using the lowest Landau level (LLL) or weak-field approximations. The resulting anisotropic potential, which depends separately on the longitudinal ($z$) and transverse ($r_\perp$) distances, is then used to calculate the decay width of heavy quarkonia via first-order time-independent perturbation theory.

## Key numbers
- The magnetic field strength at LHC is estimated to be $B = 15 m_{\pi}^2 = 1.5 \times 10^{19}$ Gauss.
- The string tension used is $\sigma = 0.174$ GeV$^2$.
- The strong coupling constant $g$ is defined with $\Lambda_{\overline{\rm MS}} = 0.176$ GeV.
- The imaginary part of the potential is plotted for temperatures $T = 0.2$ GeV and $T = 0.4$ GeV at a fixed magnetic field of $eB = 15 m_{\pi}^2$.
- The full result for the imaginary part of the potential is shown to saturate after summing over $n=10$ Landau levels, with $n=50$ used as the full result.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of heavy quark potential in a magnetized medium, specifically the strong-field (LLL) approximation by Singh et al. (2017) and the weak-field approximation by Hasan et al. (2020). By using a general propagator structure and summing all Landau levels, the authors claim their result is applicable across the entire magnetic field domain, unlike these earlier works. The anisotropic nature of the potential, which depends on the direction relative to the magnetic field, is a new feature that enables a more detailed "tomography" of quarkonium dissociation.
