# Windowed multipole representation of R-matrix cross sections

> Nuclear cross sections are basic inputs to any nuclear computation. Campaigns of experiments are fitted with the parametric R-matrix model of quantum nuclear interactions, and the resulting cross sections are documented - both point-wise and as resonance parameters (with uncertainties) - in standard evaluated nuclear data libraries (ENDF, JEFF, BROND, JENDL, CENDL, TENDL): these constitute our common knowledge of fundamental nuclear physics. In the past decade, a collaborative effort has been deployed to establish a new nuclear cross section library format - the Windowed Multipole Library - with the goal of considerably reducing the cost of cross section calculations in nuclear transport simulations. This article lays the theoretical foundations underpinning these efforts. From general R-matrix scattering theory, we derive the windowed multipole representation of nuclear cross sections. Though physically and mathematically equivalent, the windowed multipole representation is particularly well suited for subsequent temperature treatment of angle-integrated cross sections: we show that accurate Doppler broadening can be performed analytically up to the first reaction threshold; and we derive cross sections temperature derivatives to any order. Furthermore, we here establish a way of converting the R-matrix resonance parameters uncertainty (covariance matrices) into windowed multipole parameters uncertainty. We show that generating stochastic nuclear cross sections by sampling from the resulting windowed multipole covariance matrix can reproduce the cross section uncertainty in the original nuclear data file. Through this foundational article, we hope to make the Windowed Multipole Representation accessible, reproducible, and usable for the nuclear physics community, as well as provide the theoretical basis for future research on expanding its capabilities.

## Key claim

The windowed multipole representation is derived as a physically and mathematically equivalent parametrization of R-matrix cross sections that is particularly well suited for subsequent temperature treatment, enabling accurate analytic Doppler broadening up to the first reaction threshold and cross section temperature derivatives to any order, while also allowing conversion of R-matrix resonance parameter uncertainties into windowed multipole parameter uncertainties.

## Method

The authors derive the windowed multipole representation from general R-matrix scattering theory by showing that R-matrix cross sections are the sum of threshold phenomena (with Laurent expansions in wavenumber \(k_c\) space) and resonance phenomena (expressible as sums of Single-Level Breit-Wigner resonances in energy space). They establish analytic sensitivities of windowed multipole parameters to Wigner-Eisenbud R-matrix resonance parameters, enabling first-order conversion of standard resonance parameter covariance matrices into windowed multipole covariance matrices. The representation is then extended to account for temperature effects through analytic Doppler broadening and arbitrary-order temperature derivatives.

## Key numbers

* The spin statistical factor is defined as \(g_{J^\pi_c} = (2J+1)/[(2I_1+1)(2I_2+1)]\) for cross section calculations.
* The dimensionless wavenumber is defined as \(\rho_c = k_c a_c\), where \(a_c\) is the channel radius.
* For massive particles, the wavenumber-energy relation is \(k_c(E) = \sqrt{[2m_{c,1}m_{c,2}/(m_{c,1}+m_{c,2})\hbar^2](E - E_{T_c})}\).
* For photon channels, the wavenumber-energy relation is \(k_c(E) = (E - E_{T_c})/(2\hbar c)[1 + m_{c,1}c^2/((E - E_{T_c}) + m_{c,1}c^2)]\).
* The partial cross section is \(\sigma_{cc'}(E) = 4\pi g_{J^\pi_c} |T_{cc'}(E)/k_c(E)|^2\).
* The total cross section for a channel is \(\sigma_c(E) = 4\pi g_{J^\pi_c} \Re[T_{cc}(E)]/|k_c(E)|^2\).

## Context

This work extends the pole representation approach originally developed by Hwang (1987, 1992, 1998, 2003) from Argonne National Laboratory, who showed that Humblet-Rosenfeld pole expansions in wavenumber space enable accurate analytic Doppler broadening. The formalism was further developed into the windowed multipole representation by Forget (2013), Josey (2016), and Ducru (2016, 2018) for efficient on-the-fly computations of neutron cross sections. This article generalizes the representation to all cross sections (Coulomb, photon, neutron, with or without thresholds) and provides uncertainty conversion methods, laying the theoretical foundations for a full Windowed Multipole Library encompassing all present nuclear data.
