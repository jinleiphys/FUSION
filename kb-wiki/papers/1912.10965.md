# Radiative processes and jet modification at the EIC

> A U.S.-based Electron-Ion Collider will provide the ultimate capability to determine both the structure and properties of nucleons and nuclei, as well as how matter and energy can be transported through a strongly interacting quantum mechanical environment. The production and propagation of long-lived heavy subatomic particles is a unique and critical part of this planned decade-long research program. In these proceedings we report the derivation of all branching processes in nuclei that lead to a modification of semi-inclusive hadron production, jet cross sections, and jet substructure when compared to the vacuum. This work allows for their evaluation to any desired order in opacity. As an example, we show an application to the modification of light hadron and open heavy flavor fragmentation functions at the EIC. We discuss how this observable can shed light on the physics of hadronization and parton energy loss in large nuclei.

# Radiative processes and jet modification at the EIC

## Key claim
The derivation of all medium-induced branching processes in nuclei for semi-inclusive hadron production, jet cross sections, and jet substructure can be evaluated to any desired order in opacity, going beyond the soft gluon approximation that limited previous work to first order in opacity. The formalism shows that heavy flavor mesons (D and B) provide uniquely discriminating power between models of parton energy loss and in-medium hadronization, whereas light hadrons cannot differentiate between these models.

## Method
The author derived the full set of in-medium splitting kernels (q→qg, g→gg, q→gq, g→qq̅) using a lightcone wavefunction formalism that captures parton flavor and mass dependence. The calculation expresses medium-induced radiative processes as a sum of Initial/Initial, Initial/Final, Final/Initial, and Final/Final contributions, with recursion relations cast as a triangular matrix equation relating N and N−1 orders in opacity. A Mathematica code solved the recursion relation to obtain explicit results to second order in opacity, with the fully differential spectrum evaluated numerically.

## Key numbers
- The calculation is performed for ν = 20 GeV and Q² integrated over the relevant kinematic range for semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) on nuclear targets.
- The measurement simulation assumes one year of low luminosity running at center-of-mass energy √s = 69.3 GeV at the EIC.
- The yellow band in the results shows the effect of a factor of two uncertainty in nuclear opacity on the distribution of D-mesons in e+A collisions.
- Data taken with a forward silicon tracker (FST) can constrain nuclear opacity quantities at the ∼20% level.

## Context
This work extends the formalism of Sievert (2018) that computed the gluon spectrum of a quark jet to arbitrary order in opacity, going beyond previous work that computed the full gluon spectrum only to first order in opacity. The results address the inability of existing HERMES measurements (Airapetian, 2007) to differentiate between competing models of light hadron attenuation (Kopeliovich, 2003; Arleo, 2003) in SIDIS on nuclear targets. The formalism enables broad phenomenological applications for jet and heavy flavor production at the EIC, and the heavy flavor measurements can constrain transport properties of cold nuclear matter essential for understanding particle transport in dense environments such as neutron stars.
