# Triplet structure of nuclear scissors mode

> The fine structure of the scissors mode is investigated within the Time Dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) approach. The solution of TDHFB equations by the Wigner Function Moments (WFM) method predicts a splitting of the scissors mode into three intermingled branches. Together with the conventional scissors mode two new modes arise due to spin degrees of freedom. They generate significant $M1$ strength below the conventional energy range. The results of calculations of scissors resonances in Rare Earths and Actinides by WFM and QPNM methods are compared with experimental data. A remarkable coherence of both methods together with experimental data is observed.

## Key claim

The scissors mode in deformed nuclei splits into three intermingled branches rather than one, with two new modes arising from spin degrees of freedom that generate significant M1 strength below the conventional energy range, though the authors note this prediction depends on solving the coupled dynamical equations without artificial isovector-isoscalar decoupling.

## Method

The authors solved the Time Dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) equations using the Wigner Function Moments (WFM) method, integrating over phase space with weights including tensor products of position and momentum coordinates. They linearized the equations in small amplitude approximation, separated the dynamics into isoscalar and isovector variables, and solved for the collective variables describing the scissors mode with quantum number K^pi = 1^+.

## Key numbers

- For nuclei with A=150-186, pairing parameters used were V_0^p = 27 MeV, V_0^n = 23 MeV, r_p^p = 1.50 fm, r_p^n = 1.85 fm
- For Actinides, pairing parameters were V_0^p = 25.5 MeV, V_0^n = 21.5 MeV, r_p^p = 1.5 fm, r_p^n = 1.80 fm
- The isovector and isoscalar strength constants are related by kappa_1 = alpha kappa_0 with alpha = -2

## Context

This work extends the WFM method previously applied to TDHF equations including spin dynamics by Balbutsev and Molodtsova (2014), which predicted the spin scissors mode. It also generalizes the approach outlined in Balbutsev, Molodtsova, and Schuck (2017) that incorporated both spin degrees of freedom and pair correlations simultaneously. The results enable comparison with experimental data on scissors resonances in Rare Earths and Actinides, and the authors note that the Quasiparticle-Phonon Nuclear Model (QPNM) method shows remarkable coherence with both the WFM results and experimental data.
