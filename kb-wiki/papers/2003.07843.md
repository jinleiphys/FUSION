# Derivation of K-matrix reaction theory in a discrete basis formalism

> The usual derivations of the S and K matrices for two-particle reactions proceed through the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with formal definitions of the incoming and outgoing scattering states. Here we present an alternative derivation that is carried out completely in the Hamiltonian representation, using a discrete basis of configurations for the scattering channels as well as the quasi-bound configurations of the combined fragments. We use matrix algebra to derive an explicit expression for the K matrix in terms of the Hamiltonian of the internal states of the compound system and the coupling between the channels and the internal states. The formula for the K matrix includes explicitly a real dispersive shift matrix to the internal Hamiltonian that is easily computed in the formalism. That expression is applied to derive the usual form of the S matrix as a sum over poles in the complex energy plane. Some extensions and limitations of the discrete-basis Hamiltonian formalism are discussed in the concluding remarks and in the Appendix.

# Derivation of K-matrix reaction theory in a discrete basis formalism

## Key claim

The authors derive the K-matrix for two-particle reactions entirely within a discrete basis Hamiltonian representation using matrix algebra, avoiding the formal Lippmann-Schwinger equation and continuum Green's functions. The derivation produces an explicit real dispersive shift matrix Δ to the internal Hamiltonian that is often ignored in other formulations, though the formalism is limited to cases where the channel starting point R₀ lies beyond the range of interactions between reactants.

## Method

The authors discretize the two-particle Hilbert space into internal compound-nucleus configurations (labeled by λ) and scattering channel configurations on a radial mesh with spacing Δr. They write the Hamiltonian matrix elements for channel relative motion using a second-order difference formula with hopping parameter t_c = ħ²/(2M_c(Δr)²), and couple the channel at the first mesh site (n=1) to internal states via matrix elements v_{λ,c}. By eliminating the internal-state amplitudes from the coupled Hamiltonian equations and substituting the plane-wave form of the channel wave functions, they derive linear relations between incoming and outgoing flux-normalized amplitudes, from which the D matrix, K matrix, and S matrix are obtained through matrix algebra. The appendix extends the formalism to include elastic scattering potentials V_c(r) in the channels.

## Key numbers

- The hopping parameter t_c = ħ²/(2M_c(Δr)²) defines the channel Hamiltonian matrix elements between adjacent mesh points
- The energy-momentum dispersion relation is E - E_c = 2t_c(1 - cos κ_c), where κ_c = k_c Δr, reducing to the quadratic dispersion E - E_c = (ħ²/2M_c)k_c² in the continuum limit
- The probability current in channel c is J_c = ±2 t_c sin κ_c |a_c^(±)|², leading to flux-normalization factor d_c = (2 t_c sin κ_c)^(-1/2)
- The K-matrix expression is K = π W^T (H + Δ - E)^(-1) W, where the real shift matrix Δ_{λλ'} = -π Σ_c W_{λc} W_{λ'c} cot κ_c
- The S-matrix expression is S = 1 - 2πi W^T [E - (H + Δ - iπWW^T)]^(-1) W

## Context

This work extends the discrete-basis Hamiltonian approach previously applied in nuclear reaction theory by Fanto et al. (2018) and Bertsch et al. (2018), which computed only diagonal S-matrix elements for single channels, to the general case of any number of two-particle channels. The derivation provides a computationally simpler alternative to the R-matrix theory of Wigner (1947) and Lane (1958) and to standard K-matrix derivations using the Lippmann-Schwinger equation as presented in textbooks by Taylor (2006) and by Mitchell et al. (2010). The formalism is well-suited to many-body Hamiltonians of equal-mass particles (nuclei, atomic condensates) where identifying a relative coordinate is difficult, and may enable simplified treatments of nuclear fission and fusion reactions between atomic condensate droplets.
