# Feynman diagrams versus Fermi-gas Feynman emulator

> Precise understanding of strongly interacting fermions, from electrons in modern materials to nuclear matter, presents a major goal in modern physics. However, the theoretical description of interacting Fermi systems is usually plagued by the intricate quantum statistics at play. Here we present a cross-validation between a new theoretical approach, Bold Diagrammatic Monte Carlo (BDMC), and precision experiments on ultra-cold atoms. Specifically, we compute and measure with unprecedented accuracy the normal-state equation of state of the unitary gas, a prototypical example of a strongly correlated fermionic system. Excellent agreement demonstrates that a series of Feynman diagrams can be controllably resummed in a non-perturbative regime using BDMC. This opens the door to the solution of some of the most challenging problems across many areas of physics.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that Bold Diagrammatic Monte Carlo (BDMC) can produce controllable, non-perturbative results for the normal-state equation of state of the unitary Fermi gas, but only when combined with Abelian resummation methods whose mathematical validity remains unproven and relies on experimental validation as a substitute for proof.

## Method
BDMC stochastically sums all skeleton diagrams for the irreducible single-particle self-energy and pair self-energy up to order 9, expressed in terms of fully dressed single-particle and pair propagators determined self-consistently through Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter equations. The diagrammatic series does not converge in the quantum degenerate regime, so three different Abelian resummation methods (Lindelof, shifted Lindelof, and Gaussian) are applied with extrapolation to zero resummation parameter. On the experimental side, the equation of state is extracted from in situ absorption imaging of trapped ultracold lithium-6 atoms at a broad Feshbach resonance, using an iterative fitting procedure that starts from the virial expansion in the non-degenerate cloud wings.

## Key numbers
- The experimental systematic error is determined to be about 1%, controlled by the independent determination of the equation of state of the non-interacting Fermi gas
- The experimental statistical error is less than 0.5%, achieved because scale invariance of the balanced unitary gas allows all experimental profiles to contribute to the same scaled equation-of-state function
- The Feshbach resonance position for lithium-6 is known to be at 834.15 +/- 1.5 G from spectroscopic measurements
- The atom cloud contains N = 8 x 10^4 atoms for each spin state, with a local Fermi energy of E_F = 370 nK at the center
- The temperature determined from fitting the density profile in one example is T = 113 nK with beta*mu = 1.63

## Context
This work provides the first cross-validation between BDMC and precision ultracold atom experiments for a strongly correlated fermionic system, extending beyond previous first-order diagrammatic approaches such as those by Haussmann (1994) and Haussmann et al. (2007). The results discriminate against earlier lattice Monte Carlo data from Bulgac et al. (2006) and Goulko et al. (2010), as well as previous experimental pressure equations of state from the ENS group (Nascimbene et al., 2010) and the Tokyo group (Horikoshi et al., 2010). The validated BDMC approach opens the door to solving challenging problems across many areas of physics where Feynman diagram series can be formally written but not previously resummed.
