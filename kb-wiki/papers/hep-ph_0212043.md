# The F/D Ratio and Meson-Baryon Couplings from QCD Sum Rules

> Coupling constants of the pseudoscalar mesons to the octet baryons are computed in the QCD sum rule approach. The pi-NN, eta-NN, pi-XiXi, eta-XiXi, pi-SigmaSigma, eta-SigmaSigma as well as pi-Lambda-Sigma couplings are studied. Determining the pertinent Dirac structure in the correlation function, we analyze the couplings in the SU(3) limit. We find the F/D ratio to be \sim 0.6-0.8 that is consistent with the SU(6) value. We also estimate the SU(3) breaking effect using a projected correlation function method.

# The F/D Ratio and Meson-Baryon Couplings from QCD Sum Rules

## Key claim

The F/D ratio for pseudoscalar meson-octet baryon couplings is determined to be 0.6–0.8 from QCD sum rules using the tensor Dirac structure, which is consistent with the SU(6) quark model value of 2/3 but slightly higher than the value extracted from semi-leptonic hyperon decay rates (F/D ~ 0.57); the result is obtained under the SU(3) flavor symmetry limit and is shown to be insensitive to the choice of baryon interpolating current and continuum threshold.

## Method

The authors compute two-point correlation functions with an external pseudoscalar meson field for baryon-diagonal couplings (πNN, ηNN, πΞΞ, ηΞΞ, πΣΣ, ηΣΣ) in the QCD sum rule approach, going beyond the soft-meson limit by keeping finite meson momentum. They evaluate three distinct Dirac structures (iγ₅p̸, iγ₅, and γ₅σ_{μν}q^μp^ν) in the correlation function, compute the operator product expansion up to dimension 8 at O(p²) and dimension 7 at O(p), and select the tensor structure as the most reliable based on its insensitivity to the continuum threshold and to the mixing parameter t of the baryon interpolating current. For the off-diagonal πΛΣ coupling, a projected correlation function method is employed that properly accounts for baryon mass differences and defines the coupling without reference to an effective Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- F/D ratio = 0.6–0.8, determined in the SU(3) limit from the tensor Dirac structure, consistent with the SU(6) quark model value of 2/3
- The F/D ratio from the iγ₅ structure shows strong dependence on the baryon current mixing parameter θ, preventing a reliable estimate
- g_{πΛΣ} ≃ 6–7, estimated from preliminary Borel analysis without continuum effects, with small SU(3) breaking effects when including m_s ≠ m_q, strange condensate differences, and baryon mass differences

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule method for meson-baryon couplings beyond the soft-meson limit, which previously reduced to the Goldberger-Treiman relation with g_A=1 as shown by Shiomi and Hatsuda. The authors improve upon earlier sum rule studies by Kim, Doi, and collaborators (KD1, KD2, DK1) by systematically evaluating the reliability of different Dirac structures and the dependence on the baryon interpolating current. The results enable more fundamental determination of hyperon-nucleon interactions used in hypernuclear physics, where meson-baryon coupling constants have often been treated as free parameters or assumed from SU(3) symmetry without foundation. The projected correlation function method developed by Kondo provides a framework for future studies of SU(3) breaking effects in off-diagonal couplings.
