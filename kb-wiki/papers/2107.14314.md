# Effective shell-model interaction for nuclei southeast of $^{100}$Sn

> We construct an effective shell-model interaction for the valence space spanned by single-particle neutron and single-hole proton states in $^{100}$Sn. Starting from chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon forces and single-reference coupled-cluster theory for $^{100}$Sn we apply a second similarity transformation that decouples the valence space. The particle-particle components of the resulting effective interaction can be used in shell model calculations for neutron deficient tin isotopes. The hole-hole interaction can be used to calculate the $N = 50$ isotones south of $^{100}$Sn, and the full particle-hole interaction describes nuclei in the region southeast of $^{100}$Sn. We compute low-lying excited states in selected nuclei southeast of $^{100}$Sn, and find reasonable agreement with data. The presented techniques can also be applied to construct effective shell-model interactions for other regions of the nuclear chart.

# Effective shell-model interaction for nuclei southeast of $^{100}$Sn

## Key claim
The authors construct an effective shell-model interaction for the valence space of neutron-particle and proton-hole states around $^{100}$Sn using a particle-hole shell-model coupled-cluster approach, finding reasonable agreement with experimental data for low-lying excited states in selected nuclei southeast of $^{100}$Sn, though the quality depends on the chosen chiral potential and the truncation of many-body terms.

## Method
Starting from chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon forces, the authors perform single-reference coupled-cluster calculations for the $^{100}$Sn core, then apply a secondary similarity transformation (the particle-hole shell-model coupled-cluster approach, ph-SMCC) that decouples the valence space from the excluded space at the two-body level, with perturbative inclusion of leading three-body contributions. The resulting effective Hamiltonian contains particle-particle, hole-hole, and particle-hole channels, which are used in standard shell-model calculations for nuclei southeast of $^{100}$Sn. Two chiral interactions are employed: the 1.8/2.0(EM) potential and the $\Delta \text{NNLO}_\text{GO}$ potential.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy of $^{100}$Sn: $-837$ MeV for 1.8/2.0(EM) and $-816$ MeV for $\Delta \text{NNLO}_\text{GO}$, compared to the experimental datum of $-825.3$ MeV
- Single-hole energies for $p_{3/2}$: $-6.502$ MeV (1.8/2.0(EM)) and $-4.798$ MeV ($\Delta \text{NNLO}_\text{GO}$)
- Single-hole energies for $g_{9/2}$: $-3.106$ MeV (1.8/2.0(EM)) and $-2.267$ MeV ($\Delta \text{NNLO}_\text{GO}$)
- Single-particle energy for $g_{7/2}$: $-10.832$ MeV (1.8/2.0(EM)) and $-10.072$ MeV ($\Delta \text{NNLO}_\text{GO}$)
- Single-particle energy for $d_{5/2}$: $-10.548$ MeV (1.8/2.0(EM)) and $-9.375$ MeV ($\Delta \text{NNLO}_\text{GO}$)
- Single-particle energy for $h_{11/2}$: $-5.596$ MeV (1.8/2.0(EM)) and $-5.012$ MeV ($\Delta \text{NNLO}_\text{GO}$)
- For $^{101}$Sn, the experimental splitting between $5/2^+$ and $7/2^+$ states is 171 keV; the 1.8/2.0(EM) yields $E(g_{7/2})$ 284 keV lower than $E(d_{5/2})$, while $\Delta \text{NNLO}_\text{GO}$ gives a splitting of 700 keV
- Hartree-Fock calculations use harmonic oscillator basis with frequency $\hbar\omega=16$ MeV, 13 shells ($N_{\text{max}}=12$), and three-body matrix elements truncated at $E_{\text{3max}}=16\hbar\omega$

## Context
This work extends the shell-model coupled-cluster (SMCC) approach of Sun (2018) to a particle-hole formulation (ph-SMCC), enabling calculations for nuclei southeast of $^{100}$Sn where protons are removed and neutrons are attached. It contrasts with traditional shell-model calculations starting from a $^{88}$Sr or $^{90}$Zr core (Faestermann 2013, Morris 2018), which become computationally expensive for elements close to tin. The method is similar in spirit to the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) approach of Hergert (2016, 2017) and Stroberg (2017), but uses coupled-cluster theory to treat three-body correlations more accurately. The results enable future shell-model studies of neutron-deficient tin isotopes, $N=50$ isotones, and other regions of the nuclear chart where a doubly-magic core can be identified.
