# Light pseudoscalar meson and doubly charmed baryon scattering lengths with heavy diquark-antiquark symmetry

> We adopt the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) to calculate the scattering lengths of $蠒B_{cc}^{(*)}$ up to $\mathcal{O}(p^3)$, where $蠒$ is the pseudoscalar mesons. The recoil effect and the mass splitting between the spin-$1\over 2$ and spin-$3\over 2$ doubly charmed baryons are included. In order to give the numerical results, we construct the chiral Lagrangians with heavy diquark-antiquark (HDA) symmetry in a formally covariant approach. Then, we relate the low energy constants (LECs) of the doubly charmed baryons to those of $D^{(*)}$ mesons. The LECs for the $蠒D^{(*)}$ scattering are estimated in two scenarios, fitting lattice QCD results and using the resonance saturation model. The chiral convergence of the first scenario is not good enough due to the the large strange quark mass and the presence of the possible bound states, virtual states and resonance. The final results for two scenarios are consistent with each other. The interaction for the $[蟺螢^{(*)}_{cc}]^{(1/2)}$, $[K螢^{(*)}_{cc}]^{(0)}$, $[K惟^{(*)}_{cc}]^{(1/2)}$, $[畏螢^{(*)}_{cc}]^{(1/2)}$, $[畏惟^{(*)}_{cc}]^{(0)}$ and $[\bar{K}螢^{(*)}_{cc}]^{(0)}$ channels are attractive. # Light pseudoscalar meson and doubly charmed baryon scattering lengths with heavy diquark-antiquark symmetry

## Key claim
The scattering lengths of pseudoscalar mesons with doubly charmed baryons are calculated up to next-to-next-to-leading order in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, with the heavy diquark-antiquark symmetry used to relate the unknown low-energy constants to those of charmed mesons; the chiral convergence is not good enough for channels involving strange particles due to the large strange quark mass and the possible presence of bound states, virtual states, or resonances.

## Method
The authors employed heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) to compute the threshold T-matrices for phi-B_cc and phi-B*_cc scattering up to O(p^3), including recoil effects and the mass splitting between spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 doubly charmed baryons. They constructed chiral Lagrangians with heavy diquark-antiquark (HDA) symmetry in a covariant formalism, relating the low-energy constants for doubly charmed baryons to those for D(*) mesons. The LECs for phi-D(*) scattering were estimated in two scenarios: fitting lattice QCD results and using a resonance saturation model.

## Key numbers
- Mass of Xi_cc: 3621 MeV; mass of Omega_cc: 3727 MeV; mass splitting delta = 150 MeV between spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 doubly charmed baryons
- Pion decay constant F_pi = 92 MeV; kaon decay constant F_K = 113 MeV; F_eta = 1.2 F_K
- Axial coupling constant g = 0.59, determined from the decay width of D*+
- In Scenario I, fitted LECs: C_0 = 0.81(30) GeV^{-1}, C_1 = 3.98(20) GeV^{-1}, kappa = 0.55(7) GeV^{-2}
- Input lattice QCD scattering lengths used for fitting: a_piD^{(3/2)} = -0.10 fm, a_piD_s^{(1)} = -0.002 fm, a_KD_s^{(1/2)} = -0.18 fm
- Recoil correction to T-matrices at O(p^2) ranges from 0.0 to 1.2 x 10^{-2} fm, with relative size R less than 0.28%
- The most attractive channel [bar-K Xi_cc]^{(0)} has T-matrix 4.48 fm (real part) at O(p^3) with delta = 150 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous leading-order calculations of B_cc-phi scattering lengths by Guo (2017) to next-to-next-to-leading order, and builds on chiral perturbation theory studies of D-phi scattering by Liu (2009, 2011) and lattice QCD simulations by Liu (2012). The HDA symmetry approach follows the formalism of Savage (1990) and Hu (2005) for relating doubly charmed baryons to charmed mesons. The results enable searches for bound states in the doubly heavy sector analogous to the D_s0*(2317) and D_s1(2460) mesons, particularly in the [bar-K Xi_cc]^{(0)} channel which shows the strongest attraction.
