# Restoration of QCD classical symmetries in excited hadrons

> Restoration of chiral and $U(1)_A$ symmetries in excited hadrons is reviewed. A connection of these restorations with the semiclassical regime in highly excited hadron is discussed. A solvable confining field-theoretical toy model that exhibits chiral restoration is presented. Implications of the string description of the highly excited hadrons that suggests an additional dynamical symmetry of the spectra on the top of $U(2)_L \times U(2)_R$ are presented.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in highly excited hadrons, the effects of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking (the Nambu-Goldstone mode) become progressively irrelevant, so that the spectra asymptotically approach those of the Wigner-Weyl mode, even though the vacuum itself remains in the broken phase. The restoration is defined by two conditions: (i) states fall into approximate multiplets of SU(2)_L x SU(2)_R and U(1)_A with splittings Delta M vanishing at large radial quantum number n or large angular momentum J, and (ii) the splitting within a multiplet is much smaller than the spacing between subsequent multiplets.

## Method

The paper reviews phenomenological evidence from experimental meson spectra (particularly from antiproton-proton annihilation at LEAR) and classifies observed states into chiral multiplets of SU(2)_L x SU(2)_R and U(1)_A. It presents a solvable confining field-theoretical toy model that exhibits chiral restoration, and discusses the connection to the semiclassical regime in highly excited hadrons, where the action S >> hbar and the path integral is dominated by classical paths. The paper also examines implications from a string description of highly excited hadrons, which suggests an additional dynamical symmetry beyond U(2)_L x U(2)_R.

## Context

This review extends the work of Glozman and collaborators (early 2000s) on chiral symmetry restoration in excited hadrons, building on earlier phenomenological observations of baryon and meson spectra. It addresses a controversy with Jaffe, Pirjol, and Scardicchio (2005-2006) who had misinterpreted the restoration as implying the Wigner-Weyl mode for the vacuum. The paper connects the phenomenon to the semiclassical regime and string descriptions of hadrons, and identifies missing states in J=0,1,3,4 multiplets that could be searched for in future antiproton experiments at the NESR ring at GSI.
