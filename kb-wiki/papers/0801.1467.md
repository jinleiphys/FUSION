# Chiral SU(3) Dynamics and Antikaon-Nuclear quasibound States

> Recent developments are summarised concerning low-energy KbarN interactions as they relate to the possible existence of antikaon-nuclear quasibound states. An exploratory study of antikaons bound to finite nuclei is performed, with emphasis on the evolution of such states from light to heavy nuclei (A = 16 - 208). The energy dependent, driving attractive KbarN interactions are constructed using the s-wave coupled-channel amplitudes involving the Lambda(1405) and resulting from chiral SU(3) dynamics, plus p-wave amplitudes dominated by the Sigma(1385). Effects of Pauli and short-range correlations are discussed. The decay width induced by K^-NN two-body absorption is estimated and found to be substantial. It is concluded that Kbar-nuclear quasibound states can possibly exist with binding energies ranging from 60 to 100 MeV, but with short life times corresponding to decay widths of the similar magnitudes.

## Key claim

Antikaon-nuclear quasibound states can possibly exist with binding energies ranging from 60 to 100 MeV, but with short lifetimes corresponding to decay widths of similar magnitudes; the authors emphasize that these states are only possible and that the decay width induced by K⁻NN two-body absorption is substantial, making detection difficult.

## Method

The authors constructed energy-dependent K⁻N interactions using s-wave coupled-channel amplitudes involving the Λ(1405) from chiral SU(3) dynamics, plus p-wave amplitudes dominated by the Σ(1385). They solved the Klein-Gordon equation with a complex, energy-dependent K⁻ self-energy for finite nuclei from A = 16 to 208, incorporating Pauli and short-range correlations, and estimated the decay width from K⁻NN two-body absorption using a quadratic density dependence.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies for K⁻-nuclear quasibound states range from 60 to 100 MeV (abstract, no specific nucleus given for this range)
- For K⁻ in ²⁰⁸Pb at standard nuclear central density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³, the attractive Coulomb potential contributes about 25 MeV to binding energy
- The absorptive width from K⁻NN → YN absorption is estimated at roughly 30 MeV at ρ = ρ₀
- For the K⁻pp system, Faddeev calculations give binding energies B(K⁻pp) ∼ 55–80 MeV and widths Γ(K⁻pp → πΣN) ∼ 75–110 MeV
- For K⁻pp, variational calculations using chiral SU(3) effective interactions suggest weaker binding between 15 and 25 MeV with widths 40–60 MeV (excluding absorption)
- The FINUDA measurement reported B(K⁻pp) = (115 ± 9) MeV and Γ = (67 ± 16) MeV, but this interpretation was criticized
- The FINUDA measurement for K⁻ppn reported B(K⁻ppn) = (58 ± 6) MeV and Γ = (37 ± 14) MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of antikaon-nuclear bound states by Mares et al. (2006) and Friedman and Gal (2007), but uses a chiral SU(3) dynamics approach rather than phenomenological potentials or relativistic mean field models. The authors note that their results differ from the extremely dense and compact systems suggested by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002), and that the far subthreshold extrapolation of the effective interaction based on chiral SU(3) dynamics is substantially less attractive than the phenomenological one. The paper enables future experimental searches for antikaonic nuclei by providing theoretical predictions for binding energies and widths across a range of nuclear masses, while highlighting the large uncertainties that remain.
