# Bulk Viscosity of Interacting Hadrons

> We show that first approximations to the bulk viscosity $η_v$ are expressible in terms of factors that depend on the sound speed $v_s$, the enthalpy, and the interaction (elastic and inelastic) cross section. The explicit dependence of $η_v$ on the factor $(\frac 13 - v_s^2)$ is demonstrated in the Chapman-Enskog approximation as well as the variational and relaxation time approaches. The interesting feature of bulk viscosity is that the dominant contributions at a given temperature arise from particles which are neither extremely nonrelativistic nor extremely relativistic. Numerical results for a model binary mixture are reported.

## Key claim
The bulk viscosity of interacting hadrons can be expressed in terms of the sound speed, enthalpy, and interaction cross sections, with the dominant contributions at a given temperature arising from particles that are neither extremely nonrelativistic nor extremely relativistic. The explicit dependence on the factor \((\frac{1}{3} - v_s^2)\) is demonstrated across multiple theoretical approaches, but the numerical results are limited to a model binary mixture with constant cross sections.

## Method
The authors derived expressions for bulk viscosity using the Chapman-Enskog approximation, the variational approach, and the relaxation time approximation, all applied to a Boltzmann gas. For a binary mixture, they extended the Chapman-Enskog formalism to include both same-species and cross-species collision integrals, using constant cross sections of 1 fm² for all collisions. Numerical results were computed for a binary mixture where one particle mass was fixed at 140 MeV and the other mass was varied.

## Key numbers
- Bulk viscosity for a single-component gas in the ultrarelativistic limit: \(\eta_v \rightarrow \frac{225}{8} \frac{kT}{2w_0^{(2)}} \left(\frac{1}{3}-v_s^2 \right)^2\), with \(v_s^2 \rightarrow \frac{1}{3}\) for weakly interacting massless particles.
- Bulk viscosity for a single-component gas in the nonrelativistic limit: \(\eta_v = \frac{kT}{2w_0^{(2)}}\frac{z^2}{4} \left[ 9\left(\frac{1}{3}-v_s^2 \right)^2 + 6\left(\frac{1}{3}-v_s^2 \right)+1 \right]\), with \(v_s^2 \rightarrow \frac{2}{3}\) for weakly interacting nonrelativistic particles.
- For the binary mixture, particle 1 mass fixed at 140 MeV; particle 2 mass varied as shown in Figure 1 (numerical values not given in text).
- Constant cross section of 1 fm² used for all collisions in the binary mixture calculations.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework for bulk viscosity in hadronic matter, building on prior studies by Kapusta (2009) on the phase transition from hadrons to quarks and gluons, and by Gavin (1985) on the relaxation time approach. The results enable future calculations of bulk viscosity for realistic hadronic mixtures containing many resonances with masses up to 2 GeV, as noted by the authors as work in progress. The paper contradicts the notion that bulk viscosity is dominated by either extremely relativistic or nonrelativistic particles, instead showing intermediate masses contribute most.
