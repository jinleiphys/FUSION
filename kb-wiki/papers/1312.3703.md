# Truncation scheme of time-dependent density-matrix approach

> A truncation scheme of the Bogoliubov-Born-Green-Kirkwood-Yvon hierarchy for reduced density matrices, where a three-body density matrix is approximated by the antisymmetrized products of two-body density matrices, is proposed. This truncation scheme is tested for three model hamiltonians. It is shown that the obtained results are in good agreement with the exact solutions.

## Key claim
The authors propose that approximating the three-body density matrix by antisymmetrized products of two-body density matrices (rather than neglecting it entirely) can simulate the time-evolution of the three-body density matrix without explicitly solving its equation of motion, and that this truncation scheme improves upon standard time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM) which neglects genuine three-body correlations.

## Method
The authors truncate the BBGKY hierarchy by replacing the three-body correlation matrix with expressions involving products of two-body correlation matrices, derived from perturbative considerations of coupled-cluster theory. They test this scheme by computing stationary ground-state solutions of the TDDM equations for three model Hamiltonians (Lipkin model, one-dimensional Hubbard model, and trapped dipolar fermion gas) using an adiabatic method where the interaction is gradually increased, and compare results with exact solutions and with standard TDDM where the three-body correlation matrix is neglected.

## Key numbers
- Lipkin model (N=4): Ground-state energies, occupation probabilities, and two-body correlation matrix $C_{pp'-p-p'}$ are shown as functions of $\chi=(N-1)|V|/\epsilon$ up to $\chi=2$; RPA becomes unstable at $\chi=1$
- Hubbard model (six sites, half-filling): Ground-state energy and correlation energy shown as functions of $U/t$ up to $U/t=4$; RPA becomes unstable at $U/t=2.4$
- Dipolar fermion gas (N=8): Ground-state energy, occupation probabilities, and correlation energy shown as functions of $C=d^2/\hbar\omega\xi^3$ up to $C=0.6$; RPA becomes unstable at $C=0.34$
- Hubbard model stability test: At $U/t=3$, the sum $S$ of absolute violations of the one-body identity is tracked over 6000 time steps, showing that the proposed scheme stabilizes the time evolution

## Context
This work extends the time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM) developed by Wang and Cassing (1985) and by Gong and Tohyama (1990), which approximates the three-body density matrix using one-body and two-body density matrices. The authors previously showed (Tohyama and Schuck, 2010) that including the full equation of motion for the three-body density matrix drastically improves results for a model Hamiltonian, but that approach is impractical for realistic systems. The proposed truncation scheme enables practical calculations that correct the overestimation of ground-state correlations and instabilities observed in standard TDDM for strongly interacting cases (Tohyama et al., 2007; Tohyama, 2012), and makes TDDM viable for realistic nuclear-physics applications.
