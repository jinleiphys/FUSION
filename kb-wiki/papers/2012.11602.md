# Towards complete leading-order predictions for neutrinoless double $β$ decay

> The amplitude for the neutrinoless double $β$ ($0νββ$) decay of the two-neutron system, $nn\to ppe^-e^-$, constitutes a key building block for nuclear-structure calculations of heavy nuclei employed in large-scale $0νββ$ searches. Assuming that the $0νββ$ process is mediated by a light-Majorana-neutrino exchange, a systematic analysis in chiral effective field theory shows that already at leading order a contact operator is required to ensure renormalizability. In this work, we develop a method to estimate the numerical value of its coefficient in analogy to the Cottingham formula and validate the result by reproducing the charge-independence-breaking contribution to the nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths. Our central result, while derived in the $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scheme, is given in terms of the renormalized amplitude $\mathcal{A}_ν(|\mathbf{p}|,|\mathbf{p}^\prime|)$, matching to which will allow one to determine the contact-term contribution in regularization schemes employed in nuclear-structure calculations. Our results thus greatly reduce a crucial uncertainty in the interpretation of searches for $0νββ$ decay.

## Key claim

The paper provides a first estimate of the complete leading-order amplitude for neutrinoless double beta decay of the two-neutron system, including a previously unknown contact-term contribution, by generalizing the Cottingham formula approach to the two-nucleon system. The central result is derived in the MS scheme and carries an uncertainty of about 20-30%, with the caveat that inelastic contributions are not fully calculated but estimated.

## Method

The authors split the neutrino-momentum integral in the amplitude into low-energy, intermediate, and high-energy regions, then interpolate between chiral effective field theory constraints at low momentum and operator product expansion constraints at high momentum. They evaluate the elastic contributions using nucleon form factors and nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes, and determine the contact-term coefficient by matching the full amplitude to the chiral EFT expression in dimensional regularization with minimal subtraction. The approach is validated by applying the same method to charge-independence breaking in nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths and comparing with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Contact-term coefficient at MS scale mu_chi = M_pi: tilde C_1 = 1.32(50)_inel(20)_r(5)_par = 1.3(6)
- Charge-independence-breaking contact-term combination at MS scale mu_chi = M_pi: (tilde C_1 + tilde C_2) = 2.9(1.1)_inel(0.3)_r(0.3)_par = 2.9(1.2)
- Renormalized amplitude at kinematic point |p|=25 MeV, |p'|=30 MeV: A_nu exp[-i(delta_S0(|p|)+delta_S0(|p'|))] = -0.0195(5) MeV^{-2}
- Predicted charge-independence-breaking scattering length from contact-term result: a_CIB = 15.5^{+4.5}_{-4.0} fm, compared to empirical value 10.4(2) fm
- Pion mass difference low-energy constant from elastic Cottingham approach: Z = 0.63 at cutoff Lambda=2 GeV, approaching 0.67 for Lambda to infinity, compared to phenomenological value Z=0.81

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT analysis of neutrinoless double beta decay by Cirigliano et al. (2018, 2019), which showed that a contact operator is required at leading order for renormalizability, but left its coefficient unknown. The method generalizes the Cottingham formula approach used for electromagnetic contributions to hadron masses (Cottingham 1963) to the two-nucleon system. The result enables complete leading-order calculations of neutrinoless double beta decay rates in ab-initio nuclear-structure calculations for nuclei such as 6He, 48Ca, and 76Ge, and reduces a crucial uncertainty in interpreting experimental searches from KamLAND-Zen, EXO, GERDA, and other collaborations.
