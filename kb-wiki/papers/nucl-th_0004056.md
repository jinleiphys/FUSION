# Nuclear Muon Capture on the Proton and 3He within the Standard Model and Beyond

> Nuclear muon capture on the proton and $^3$He is considered both within and beyond the Standard Model in terms as general as is possible. Explicit and precise analytic expressions for all possible observables are given, assuming only a Dirac neutrino in the limit of vanishing mass. These results allow both for precision tests of the Standard Model and new physics, as well as for the assessment of the potential physics reach of experiments designed to measure specific observables. Using these expressions, stringent constraints can already be inferred from a recent precision measurement of the statistical capture rate on $^3$He. Likewise, similar constraints should follow the completion of a precision measurement in progress of the singlet capture rate on the proton.

## Key claim

The paper provides a general theoretical framework for nuclear muon capture on spin-1/2 isospin doublets (proton and 3He) that includes not only all Standard Model effects but also any possible beyond-Standard-Model contributions through an effective four-fermi interaction, assuming only a massless Dirac neutrino with conserved muon leptonic flavor. The authors claim that their results enable precision tests of the Standard Model and new physics, but note that most observables beyond simple rate measurements require challenging polarization measurements that may be extremely difficult to perform with high precision.

## Method

The authors use the "elementary particle model" approach, parametrizing nuclear matrix elements in terms of form factors rather than relying on nuclear structure models, and derive analytic expressions for all possible muon capture observables on spin-1/2 isospin doublets. They employ a Dirac representation method to compute the capture amplitude squared without a nonrelativistic expansion, incorporating vector, axial, scalar, pseudoscalar, and tensor interactions through complex coupling coefficients and form factors, including CP- or T-odd effects. The calculation assumes the initial muonic atom is at rest and ignores nuclear recoil effects associated with scalar, pseudoscalar, and tensor interactions beyond the Standard Model, as these couplings are necessarily small.

## Key numbers

- The experimental statistical capture rate on 3He is measured as 1496 ± 4 s⁻¹ (0.4% precision), in perfect agreement with the theoretical prediction of 1496 ± 21 s⁻¹ (Ackerbauer et al.)
- The induced pseudoscalar nucleon form factor g_P has a combined uncertainty of 22% from the last twenty years (Bardin et al.)
- A new 8% precise measurement of g_P from radiative muon capture on hydrogen gives a value differing by 4.2 sigma from the expected value (Jonkmans, Wright)
- The value for g_P extracted from the 3He capture experiment, though at the expected level, is still precise to only 19% (Congleton et al.)
- The singlet capture rate on the proton is targeted for measurement to 0.5% to 1% precision (muH, Vorobyov experiments)

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of nuclear muon capture that only included vector and axial interactions through the usual (V-A) charged electroweak coupling, such as those by Kim (Kim2), Holstein (Holstein1, Holstein3), and others (Bernstein, Santisteban, Hwang). The paper builds on the precise 3He capture measurement by Ackerbauer et al. (1998) and the ongoing effort to measure the singlet capture rate on the proton (muH, Vorobyov). The results enable future experiments to test Standard Model predictions and constrain new physics, particularly through the induced pseudoscalar form factor which provides a crucial low-energy test of QCD's chiral symmetry breaking, given the unresolved 4.2 sigma discrepancy between the radiative muon capture measurement and theoretical expectations.
