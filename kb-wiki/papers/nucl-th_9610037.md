# Fission barriers and asymmetric ground states in the relativistic mean field theory

> The symmetric and asymmetric fission path for 240Pu, 232Th, and 226Ra is investigated within the relativistic mean field model. Standard parametrizations which are well fitted to nuclear ground state properties are found to deliver reasonable qualitative and quantitative features of fission, comparable to similar nonrelativstic calculations. Furthermore, stable octupole deformations in the ground states of Radium isotopes are investigated. They are found in a series of isotopes, qualitatively in agreement with nonrelativistic models. But the quantitative details differ amongst the models and between the various relativsitic parametrizations.

## Key claim

The relativistic mean field model, using standard parametrizations NL1 and PL-40 that are well fitted to nuclear ground state properties, delivers reasonable qualitative and quantitative features of fission comparable to nonrelativistic calculations, but the quantitative details of octupole deformations in Radium isotopes differ amongst the models and between the various relativistic parametrizations.

## Method

The authors performed fully selfconsistent relativistic mean field calculations of potential energy surfaces for symmetric and asymmetric fission of 240Pu, 232Th, and 226Ra, using three parametrizations (NL1, PL-40, and NL-SH) with axial symmetry and no reflection symmetry restriction. They employed a constant gap pairing approach with Δ = 11.2 MeV/√A and constrained the quadrupole moment while allowing octupole deformations to adjust freely, using a grid in cylindrical coordinates with 0.7 fm spacing. For the Radium isotopes (216Ra through 230Ra), they computed potential energy surfaces as a function of octupole deformation to identify stable octupole-deformed ground states.

## Key numbers

- For 240Pu with PL-40: first barrier height approximately 4-6 MeV higher than experimental value; second barrier comes very close to experimental point
- For 240Pu: asymmetric shapes develop only for quadrupole deformations Q₂ > 120 barn, affecting only the second barrier
- For 226Ra: ground state is asymmetric, 2 MeV lower than the nearby symmetric minimum
- For 226Ra: symmetric and asymmetric PES separate in the range 120 barn < Q < 160 barn with two branches distinguished by hexadecupole moment
- Pairing gap used: Δ = 11.2 MeV/√A
- Grid spacing: 0.7 fm in each direction, with typical grid sizes of 25 × 75
- Centre-of-mass correction estimate: E_cm = (3/4) × 41 × A^(-1/3) MeV
- Collective zero-point energy estimates: first barrier lowered by 0.5 MeV, second barrier by 2 MeV
- For Radium isotopes: relative deviation from experimental binding energies generally below 0.5% for all parametrizations, and below 0.3% for PL-40

## Context

This work extends earlier relativistic mean field calculations of fission barriers by Blu94 to include asymmetric fission and a larger variety of actinides, and investigates stable octupole deformations in Radium isotopes previously studied in the macroscopic-microscopic method by Mol81 and Lea82 and in nonrelativistic Hartree-Fock calculations with the Skyrme III force by Bon86 or the Gogny force by rob87. The results enable future searches for improved relativistic parametrizations, particularly among stabilized nonlinear parametrizations of the power-law type, and motivate fully fledged collective dynamics calculations in multiple degrees of freedom including triaxial shapes, collective mass tensors, and temperature effects.
