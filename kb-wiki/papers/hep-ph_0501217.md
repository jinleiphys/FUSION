# Quark-hadron duality in electron scattering

> The duality between partonic and hadronic descriptions of physical phenomena is one of the most remarkable features of strong interaction physics. A classic example of this is in electron-nucleon scattering, in which low-energy cross sections, when averaged over appropriate energy intervals, are found to exhibit the scaling behavior expected from perturbative QCD. We present a comprehensive review of data on structure functions in the resonance region, from which the global and local aspects of duality are quantified, including its flavor, spin and nuclear medium dependence. To interpret the experimental findings, we discuss various theoretical approaches which have been developed to understand the microscopic origins of quark-hadron duality in QCD. Examples from other reactions are used to place duality in a broader context, and future experimental and theoretical challenges are identified.

## Key claim

Quark-hadron duality in electron-nucleon scattering holds that low-energy cross sections in the resonance region, when averaged over appropriate energy intervals, reproduce the scaling behavior expected from perturbative QCD; the paper reviews evidence that this duality works down to Q² values as low as 1 GeV² or even below, considerably lower than previously believed, and appears to hold for each prominent resonance region individually (local duality).

## Method

The authors present a comprehensive review of inclusive electron-nucleon scattering data on unpolarized and polarized structure functions in the nucleon resonance region (W < 2 GeV), comparing averaged resonance-region cross sections with deep inelastic scaling curves. They quantify global and local aspects of duality using structure function moments, including Nachtmann moments that account for target mass corrections, and examine the flavor, spin, and nuclear medium dependence of duality. Theoretical interpretations are surveyed using the operator product expansion (twist expansion), dynamical models of confinement, and finite energy sum rules.

## Key numbers

- Duality holds down to Q² values as low as 1 GeV² or even below, considerably lower than previously believed (Section I).
- The resonance region is traditionally defined as W < 2 GeV (Section I).
- The Nachtmann scaling variable is defined as ξ = 2x / (1 + √(1 + 4M²x²/Q²)) (Section II.D).
- The n-th Cornwall-Norton moments of structure functions are defined as M₁^(n)(Q²) = ∫₀¹ dx x^(n-1) F₁(x,Q²) and M_(2,L)^(n)(Q²) = ∫₀¹ dx x^(n-2) F_(2,L)(x,Q²) (Section II.D).
- For spin-dependent scattering, the n-th moments are Γ_(1,2)^(n)(Q²) = ∫₀¹ dx x^(n-1) g_(1,2)(x,Q²) (Section II.D).

## Context

This review extends the original observations of Bloom and Gilman (1970) from SLAC data, who first noted that inclusive structure functions at low hadronic mass W follow a global scaling curve describing high-W data. The work builds on the reformulation of Bloom-Gilman duality by De Rújula, Georgi, and Politzer (1970s) in terms of the operator product expansion and higher-twist operators. The new Jefferson Lab data with unprecedented accuracy have revived interest in duality, enabling precision studies of the high-x (x > 0.7) region and allowing comparisons with lattice QCD calculations of structure function moments over Q² ≈ 1-10 GeV².
