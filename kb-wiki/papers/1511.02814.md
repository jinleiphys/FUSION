# Approximate restoration of translational and rotational symmetries within the Lipkin method

> Background: Nuclear self-consistent mean-field approaches are rooted in the density functional theory and, through the spontaneous symmetry breaking mechanism, allow for including important correlations, while keeping the simplicity of the approach. Because real ground states should have all symmetries of the nuclear Hamiltonian, these methods require subsequent symmetry restoration. Purpose: We implement and study Lipkin method of approximate variation after projection applied to the restoration of the translational or rotational symmetries. Methods: We use Lipkin operators up to quadratic terms in momenta or angular momenta with self-consistently determined values of the Peierls-Yoccoz translational masses or moments of inertia, respectively. Calculations based on Skyrme energy-density functional are performed for heavy, deformed, and paired nuclei. Results: In deformed nuclei, the Peierls-Yoccoz translational masses along three different principal-axes directions of the intrinsic system can be different, which illustrates different widths of the total-momentum distributions. Numerically, the differences are only of the order of a few per cent. For the rotational-symmetry restoration, the Lipkin method compares favorably with the exact angular-momentum projection, which requires much larger computational effort. Conclusions: The Lipkin method of translational and rotational symmetry restoration is a practical low-cost method of determining the corresponding correlation energies. It allows for a simultaneous restoration of several symmetries and can be relatively easily implemented in the standard self-consistent mean-field calculations, including those required for the parameter adjustments.

## Key claim

The Lipkin method, using quadratic Lipkin operators with self-consistently determined Peierls-Yoccoz masses or moments of inertia, provides a practical low-cost approximation to exact variation-after-projection for restoring translational and rotational symmetries in nuclear mean-field calculations, with the caveat that the quadratic approximation is only appropriate for well-deformed nuclei exhibiting collective rotational behavior.

## Method

The authors implemented the Lipkin method by constructing corrective Lipkin operators up to quadratic terms in linear momenta (for translational symmetry) or angular momenta (for rotational symmetry), with self-consistently determined Peierls-Yoccoz translational masses or moments of inertia. Calculations were performed using the code hfodd (v2.73m) solving Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations on a 3D Cartesian harmonic oscillator basis with 16 major spherical shells and the Skyrme SLy4 parametrization, using a volume zero-range pairing interaction with a cutoff window of 60 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Peierls-Yoccoz translational masses along three principal-axes directions in deformed nuclei differ by up to a few per cent, illustrating different widths of total-momentum distributions.
- For translational symmetry restoration in 168Er, Lipkin mass parameters show weak dependence on shifts from 0.05 to 1.5 fm, with shifts fixed at 0.5 fm for subsequent calculations.
- For rotational symmetry restoration, the Lipkin moment of inertia very weakly depends on the Euler rotation angle beta, with results obtained using beta = 0.1.
- Pairing strengths for translational case: Vn = -159 MeV fm^3 for neutrons and Vp = -152 MeV fm^3 for protons, adjusted to reproduce experimental odd-even mass staggering in the entire rare-earth region.
- Pairing strengths for rotational case: Vn = -202.17 MeV fm^3 and Vp = -221.70 MeV fm^3, adjusted to reproduce experimental odd-even mass staggering in nuclei around 168Er.
- Cranking calculations for moment of inertia comparison were performed at frequency omega = 0.05 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the translational-symmetry Lipkin variation-after-projection method of Dobaczewski (2009) from the Hartree-Fock case to deformed paired nuclei, and provides the first full implementation for rotational-symmetry restoration. The method enables simultaneous restoration of several symmetries at low computational cost compared to exact angular-momentum projection, and can be implemented in standard self-consistent mean-field calculations including those required for parameter adjustments.
