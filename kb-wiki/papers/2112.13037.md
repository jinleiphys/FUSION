# Microscopic description of cluster decays based on the generator coordinate method

> \noindent\textbf{Background:} While many phenomenological models for nuclear fission have been developed, a microscopic understanding of fission has remained one of the most challenging problems in nuclear physics. \noindent\textbf{Purpose:} We investigate an applicability of the generator coordinate method (GCM) as a microscopic theory for cluster radioactivities of heavy nuclei, which can be regarded as a fission with large mass asymmetry, that is, a phenomenon in between fission and $α$-decays. \noindent\textbf{Methods:} Based on the Gamow theory, we evaluate the preformation probability of a cluster with GCM while the penetrability of the Coulomb barrier is estimated with a potential model. To this end, we employ Skyrme interactions and solve the one-dimensional Hill-Wheeler equation with the mass octupole field. We also take into account the dynamical effects of the pairing correlation using BCS wavefunctions constructed with an increased strength of the pairing interaction. \noindent\textbf{Results:} We apply this scheme to the cluster decay of $^{222}$Ra, i.e., $^{222}$Ra$\to^{14}$C+$^{208}$Pb, to show that the experimental decay rate can be reproduced within about two order of magnitude. We also briefly discuss the cluster radioactivities of the $^{228}$Th and $^{232}$U nuclei. For these actinide nuclei, we find that the present calculations reproduce the decay rates with the same order of magnitude and within two or three order of magnitude, respectively. \noindent\textbf{Conclusions:} The method presented in this paper provides a promising way to describe microscopically cluster decays of heavy nuclei.

# Microscopic description of cluster decays based on the generator coordinate method

## Key claim
The generator coordinate method (GCM) with Skyrme interactions can reproduce experimental cluster decay rates for 222Ra, 228Th, and 232U within two to three orders of magnitude, but the agreement depends on the choice of Skyrme parametrization (SkM* versus SLy4) and on the inclusion of dynamical pairing effects via the maximum coupling approximation, which enhances the decay rate by a factor of about two.

## Method
The authors combine the Gamow theory with the generator coordinate method: they solve the Hartree-Fock+BCS equation with Skyrme interactions (SkM* and SLy4) under a constraint on the mass octupole moment Q3, then solve the one-dimensional Hill-Wheeler equation to obtain the collective wave function and the preformation probability S at the crossing point Q_t between the one-body and two-body configurations. The barrier assault frequency f and penetrability P are computed separately using a Woods-Saxon nuclear potential plus Coulomb potential in the WKB approximation, with the potential depth adjusted to reproduce the experimental Q-value. Dynamical pairing is incorporated through the maximum coupling approximation, where the pairing strength is increased by a factor alpha and the modified Hamiltonian's ground state is used in the GCM superposition.

## Key numbers
- For 222Ra → 14C + 208Pb (Q = 33.05 MeV): the calculated decay rate with SkM* is 5.43 × 10^{-14} s^{-1}, compared to experimental values of 6.7 (±1.8) × 10^{-12} s^{-1} (Price et al., 1985), 5.6 (±2.2) × 10^{-12} s^{-1} (Hourani et al., 1985), and 4.20 (±1.18) × 10^{-12} s^{-1} (Hussonnois et al., 1991)
- For 228Th → 208Pb + 20O (Q = 44.72 MeV): the GCM decay rate with SkM* is 3.20 × 10^{-21} s^{-1}, compared to the experimental value of 1.29 (±0.22) × 10^{-21} s^{-1} (Bonetti et al., 1993)
- For 232U → 208Pb + 24Ne (Q = 62.33 MeV): the GCM decay rate with SkM* is 1.32 × 10^{-18} s^{-1}, compared to experimental values of 6.3 (±1.5) × 10^{-22} s^{-1} (Barwick et al., 1985) and 2.72 (±0.23) × 10^{-21} s^{-1} (Bonetti et al., 1990)
- The preformation probability S for 222Ra at Q_t = 15000 fm^3 is of order 10^{-10} for both SkM* and SLy4 interactions
- The dynamical pairing enhancement factor at the optimal alpha is 1.82 for SkM* and 2.04 for SLy4 in 222Ra

## Context
This work extends the microscopic approach of Bertsch (2020) and Hagino (2020a, 2020b) from schematic Hamiltonians to realistic Skyrme energy functionals for cluster radioactivity, which sits between alpha decay and spontaneous fission. The results are compared to the least-action WKB calculations of Warda (2011) using the Gogny D1S interaction, which gave a decay rate of 8.73 × 10^{-10} s^{-1} for 222Ra. The method enables a unified microscopic description of quantum tunneling decays in nuclear many-body systems, with future applications to spontaneous fission requiring inclusion of additional degrees of freedom such as the neck and quadrupole moment.
