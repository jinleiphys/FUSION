# Quark-quark Double Scattering and Modified (Anti-)Quark Fragmentation Functions in Nuclei

> Quark-quark double scattering in eA DIS and its contribution to quark and anti-quark fragmentation functions are investigated with the generalized factorization of the relevant twist-four processes in pQCD. It is shown that the resulting modifications to quark and anti-quark fragmentation functions are different. While the numerical size of these effects cannot be determined from pQCD, the structure of our result leads to a number of qualitative predictions for the relative size of the effect for different hadrons. These qualitative predictions agree with the multiplicity ratios for positive and negative hadrons as observed by HERMES.

# Quark-quark Double Scattering and Modified (Anti-)Quark Fragmentation Functions in Nuclei

## Key claim
Quark-quark double scattering in electron-nucleus deep inelastic scattering (eA DIS) produces different modifications to quark and anti-quark fragmentation functions, with the ratio of these modifications approximately equal to the ratio of anti-quark to quark distributions in a nucleon (which is less than 1). While the absolute numerical size of these effects cannot be determined from perturbative QCD alone, the structure of the result yields qualitative predictions for the relative suppression of different hadron species that agree with HERMES multiplicity ratio data.

## Method
The authors used the generalized factorization formalism of twist-four processes in perturbative QCD (the LQS framework) to compute all leading-order and next-to-leading order cut diagrams for quark-quark double scattering in eA DIS. They summed these contributions along with virtual corrections to derive a modified fragmentation function that includes the effects of quark-quark rescattering in the nuclear medium, then applied constituent quark model relations to translate these modifications into predictions for hadron multiplicity ratios.

## Key numbers
- The modification ratio for quark versus anti-quark fragmentation functions is given by $\Delta \widetilde{D}_{q\to h} / \Delta \widetilde{D}_{\bar q\to h} \approx f_{\bar q}^N(x_T) / f_{q}^N(x_T) < 1$, where $x_T = \langle k_T^2 \rangle / (2p^+ q^- z)$ and $k_T$ is the typical transverse momentum of the anti-quark.
- The modified fragmentation function includes terms proportional to $\alpha_s x_B / (x_A Q^2)$ and $\alpha_s^2 x_B / (x_A Q^2)$, where $x_A = 1/(m_N R_A)$ with $R_A$ the nuclear radius.
- The multiplicity ratio $R_M^h(z,\nu)$ is defined as the ratio of hadron production per DIS event on a nuclear target of mass A to that on deuterium, with $N_h(z,\nu)$ the number of semi-inclusive hadrons in a given $(z,\nu)$-bin and $N_e(\nu)$ the number of inclusive DIS positrons in the same $\nu$-bin.

## Context
This work extends the study of nuclear effects in hard processes beyond the dominant quark-gluon double scattering mechanism previously investigated by Guo and Wang (2000) and others, which primarily describes leading quark energy loss. The quark-quark double scattering mechanism introduces a new effect: mixing of quark and gluon fragmentation functions through flavor conversion, as first noted by Wang and Guo (2000). The qualitative predictions for the ordering of suppression among hadron species (pions of all charges suppressed equally, anti-protons more suppressed than protons, negative kaons more suppressed than positive kaons, and negative hadrons more suppressed than positive hadrons) agree with HERMES collaboration observations and provide a framework for interpreting future measurements of nuclear-modified fragmentation functions in eA, hA, and AA collisions at RHIC and LHC.
