# Chiral symmetry restoration and properties of Goldstone bosons at finite temperature

> We study chiral symmetry restoration by analyzing thermal properties of QCD's (pseudo-)Goldstone bosons, especially the pion. The meson properties are obtained from the spectral densities of mesonic imaginary-time correlation functions. To obtain the correlation functions, we solve the Dyson-Schwinger equations and the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equations in the leading symmetry-preserving rainbow-ladder approximation. In the chiral limit, the pion and its partner sigma degenerate at the critical temperature $T_c$. At $T \gtrsim T_c$, it is found that the pion rapidly dissociates, which signals deconfinement phase transition. Beyond the chiral limit, the pion dissociation temperature can be used to define the pseudo-critical temperature of chiral phase crossover, which is consistent with that obtained by the maximum point of the chiral susceptibility. The parallel analysis for kaon and pseudoscalar $s\bar{s}$ suggests that heavy mesons may survive above $T_c$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the pion dissociation temperature (defined by the steepest ascent of the width-to-mass ratio) can serve as an alternative definition of the pseudo-critical temperature for chiral phase crossover, yielding a value consistent with that obtained from the maximum of chiral susceptibility, but the authors note that numerical uncertainties in spectral function reconstruction make the small deviation between these two definitions negligible.

## Method

The authors solved the quark gap equation (Dyson-Schwinger equation) and the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation in the rainbow-ladder approximation at finite temperature using the imaginary-time formalism. Meson spectral functions were reconstructed from Euclidean mesonic correlation functions using the maximum entropy method, with the asymptotic non-interacting spectral function as the default model. The interaction kernel was the simplified Qin-Chang model with parameters fixed by fitting vacuum pseudoscalar meson masses.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature in the chiral limit: \(T_c \sim 131\) MeV, consistent with lattice result \(T_c = 132^{+3}_{-6}\) MeV from HotQCD (2019)
- Pseudo-critical temperature from chiral susceptibility maximum (beyond chiral limit, \(m_{u/d}=4.8\) MeV): \(T_{pc} \sim 137\) MeV
- Pion dissociation temperature (beyond chiral limit): \(T_d^\pi = 0.142\) GeV
- Kaon dissociation temperature: \(T_d^K \approx 0.142\) GeV
- Pseudoscalar \(s\bar{s}\) dissociation temperature: \(T_d^{s\bar{s}} \approx 0.160\) GeV (approximately \(1.14 T_d^\pi\))
- Vacuum chiral condensate: \(-\langle\bar{\psi} \psi\rangle = (0.26\ \text{GeV})^3\)
- Vacuum pion mass: \(m_\pi = 0.135\) GeV; vacuum kaon mass: \(m_K = 0.495\) GeV
- Vacuum decay constants: \(f_\pi = 0.095\) GeV; \(f_K = 0.110\) GeV
- Current quark masses used: \(m_{u/d} = 4.8\) MeV; \(m_s = 116\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to finite-temperature meson properties following the methodology of Qin (2013) and Chen (2020). It complements lattice QCD studies by Brandt (2015) and Cheng (2010), chiral perturbation theory by Toublan (1997), and NJL model calculations by Bernard (1987). The finding that heavy mesons may survive above the chiral restoration temperature is consistent with results from Chen (2020). The analysis enables future extension to the QCD phase diagram on the temperature-chemical potential plane and can be improved by using truncation schemes beyond the rainbow-ladder approximation.
