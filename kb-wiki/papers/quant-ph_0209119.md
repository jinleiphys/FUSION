# A Renormalisation Approach to Effective Interactions in Hilbert Space

> The low-lying bound states of a microscopic quantum many-body system of $n$ particles and the related physical observables can be worked out in a truncated $n$--particle Hilbert space. We present here a non-perturbative analysis of this problem which relies on a renormalisation concept and work out the link with perturbative approaches.

## Key claim

The paper presents a non-perturbative renormalisation framework for constructing effective Hamiltonians in truncated Hilbert spaces, but the method requires prior knowledge of the exact lowest eigenvalue and corresponding eigenvector components at each reduction step, which are not directly accessible in practice.

## Method

The author divides the N-dimensional Hilbert space into a P-subspace of dimension N-1 and a Q-subspace of dimension 1 using the Feshbach projection formalism, then imposes the constraint that the lowest eigenvalue remains unchanged after reduction. This generates a quadratic equation for the renormalised coupling constant g^(N-1), which is solved iteratively by choosing the root closest to the previous value g^(N). The process is extended to the continuum limit to derive a non-linear flow equation for the running coupling constant, and a separate finite-temperature formulation is developed using the canonical partition function and the Trotter formula.

## Key numbers

- The effective Hamiltonian in the projected subspace is given by H_eff(E) = P H P + P H Q (E - Q H Q)^{-1} Q H P, where dim P H^(N) = N-1 and dim Q H^(N) = 1
- The renormalised coupling constant g^(N-1) satisfies a quadratic equation a^(N-1) g^2 + b^(N-1) g + c^(N-1) = 0, with coefficients defined in terms of matrix elements and the lowest eigenvalue lambda_1^(N)
- At finite temperature, the partition function Z^(k) = Tr_k exp(-beta H^(k)) is constrained to satisfy Z^(k) = Z^(k-1) during dimensional reduction
- The flow equation in the continuum limit is dg/dx = (1/(2a(x)g(x)+b(x))) (dc/dx + (db/dx)g(x) - (da/dx)g(x)^2)

## Context

This work extends the projection methods of Brandow (1967), Kuo (1974), and Schucan and Weidenmuller (1972) by providing a non-perturbative alternative to perturbation expansions that avoids convergence problems when states from different subspaces approach each other. The approach connects to the renormalisation concepts of Glazek and Wilson (1993) and shows that exceptional points where perturbation series diverge correspond to fixed points of the running coupling constant, linking the formalism to quantum phase transitions as studied by Sachdev (1999). The method enables systematic construction of effective interactions for nuclear shell-model calculations and other many-body systems where strong interactions make perturbation theory unreliable.
