# Existence of Density Functionals for Excited States and Resonances

> We show how every bound state of a finite system of identical fermions, whether a ground state or an excited one, defines a density functional. Degeneracies created by a symmetry group can be trivially lifted by a pseudo-Zeeman effect. When complex scaling can be used to regularize a resonance into a square integrable state, a DF also exists.

## Key claim

Every bound state of a finite system of identical fermions, whether ground or excited, defines a density functional, and this existence proof extends to narrow resonances when complex scaling regularizes them into square integrable states; however, the construction requires either an approximate energy estimate closer to the target state than to any other eigenvalue, or the exact eigenvalue for resonances, and degeneracies must be lifted by "pusher" terms that commute with the Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors modify the Hamiltonian by adding a semipositive definite operator, either \(({\bf H}-\widetilde E_n)^2\) for bound excited states or \(({\bf H}'^{\dagger} - E_n^*)({\bf H}' - E_n)\) for complex-scaled resonances, so that the target eigenstate becomes the ground state of the modified operator. Degeneracies from symmetry groups are lifted by adding "pusher" terms such as \(C\,({\bf G}-\gamma)^2\) that commute with the original Hamiltonian, creating a unique ground state. A Legendre transform of the ground-state energy of the modified Hamiltonian with respect to an external one-body potential then yields a density functional, and a derivative with respect to the energy estimate extracts the expectation value of the original Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

* For an even number of fermions with good angular momentum \(L\), the density \(\rho_{LM}\) is parametrized by \((L+1)\) scalar form factors \(\tau_0, \tau_2, \dots, \tau_{2L}\) (even \(\lambda\) only), with the "\(2L\) cut-off" arising from the quadratic nature of the density observable.
* The Clebsch-Gordan expansion \(\rho_{LM}(\vec r) = \sum_{\lambda=0}^{2L} (-)^{L-M} \langle L -M L M | \lambda 0 \rangle Y_{\lambda 0}(\hat r) \tau_{\lambda}(r)\) provides the angular Fourier decomposition of the density for a state with angular momentum \(L\) and projection \(M\).

## Context

This work extends the Hohenberg-Kohn (1964) existence theorem for ground-state density functionals to every bound excited state and to narrow resonances, generalizing prior treatments of degeneracies and symmetries by Göring (1996) and others. It also builds on the complex scaling method of Ho (1983) and Moiseyev (1998) to handle resonances. The paper enables a formal foundation for constructing density functionals for excited states and resonances in nuclear, atomic, and molecular systems, though it notes that constructive algorithms and a Kohn-Sham scheme for the squared-Hamiltonian versions remain open challenges.
