# On the behaviour of the nuclear spectral function at high momentum and removal energy

> The extrapolation of the nuclear spectral function at large nucleon three-momentum and removal energy is suggested.

## Key claim

The authors propose a phenomenological procedure to extrapolate the nuclear spectral function to values of nucleon three-momentum and removal energy beyond the region accessible to nonrelativistic many-body calculations (up to ~0.7 GeV/c and ~0.6 GeV), based on the assumption that at very high momentum and energy the energy dependence remains dominated by the removal of a nucleon from a strongly correlated pair.

## Method

The extrapolation uses the relationship between the nuclear spectral function and the distribution function f_A(z), where z is a relativistic invariant variable related to the light-cone component of the nucleon four-momentum. The correlation contribution P_B(|k|,E) is factorized into a momentum distribution n_B(|k|) and an energy-dependent function F(|k|,E) describing the removal of a nucleon from a correlated pair. The momentum distribution at large |k| is obtained by fitting the computed f_A^B(z) (derived from Regge asymptotics and quark distributions in correlated nucleon clusters) using a parametrized n_B(|k|), with the fit constrained by the relationship between f_A^B(z) and the spectral function.

## Key numbers

- Only about 70% of nucleons are in low-momentum, low-removal-energy mean-field states; the remaining 30% are in correlated states.
- Many-body calculations describe the correlation tail of the spectral function up to k_max ~ 0.7 GeV/c and E_max ~ 0.6 GeV.
- The Fermi momentum k_F ~ 0.25 GeV/c.
- The fit to n_B(|k|) at k_F < |k| < k_max uses parameters G_1 = 3.30 (GeV/c)^{-3}, B_1 = 6.2 (GeV/c)^{-1}, and alpha = 1.14.
- The extrapolated parametrization uses G_1 = 2.90 (GeV/c)^{-3}, B_1 = 6.08 (GeV/c)^{-1}, G_2 = -21.2 (GeV/c)^{-3}, B_2 = 26.7 (GeV/c)^{-1}, and beta = 0.001.
- The fit to f_A^B(z) at z > 1.7 yields chi^2 ~ 0.009.
- The average removal energy associated with P_B(|k|,E) is <E_B> ~ 40 MeV.
- The extrapolated momentum distribution is needed to describe proton spectra at kinetic energy T >= 0.3 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic many-body calculations of the spectral function for nuclear matter by Benhar, Fabrocini, and Fantoni (1989) and the local density approximation for medium-heavy nuclei by Benhar et al. (1994). It builds on the approach of Benhar, Fantoni, Lykasov, and Slavin (1997, 1998) for evaluating the asymptotic behavior of f_A(z) using Regge theory and quark distributions in correlated nucleon clusters, originally proposed by Efremov, Kaidalov, Kim, Lykasov, and Slavin (1988, 1994). The extrapolated spectral function enables quantitative analysis of semi-inclusive electron-nucleus reactions producing backward protons, particularly in the kinematic region forbidden for scattering off free nucleons, and can be tested against SLAC data from Degtyarenko et al. (1994).
