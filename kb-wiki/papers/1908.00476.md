# Anomalous quartic term in the expansion of the symmetry energy

> The quartic term in the framework of relativistic mean field theory with inclusion of scalar meson interactions is investigated. It is shown that the quartic term in the asymmetric expansion of nuclear matter energy may reach very large values. This makes the even power expansion of asymmetry questionable and suggests possible non-analytic contributions to the energy of matter.

## Key claim

The quartic term \(S_4(n)\) in the even-power expansion of nuclear matter energy in isospin asymmetry can reach unexpectedly large values (over 1000 MeV) at densities a few times saturation density when scalar meson interactions (\(\sigma\)-\(\delta\) coupling) are included in relativistic mean field theory, making the standard parabolic approximation and even the full power-series expansion questionable for asymmetric matter at high density.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic mean field (RMF) model with nucleons and four meson fields (\(\sigma, \omega, \rho, \delta\)), extended by an explicit \(\sigma\)-\(\delta\) interaction term of the form \(\tilde{g}_\alpha \sigma^\alpha \vec{\delta}^2\) for two cases: linear (\(\alpha=1\)) and quadratic (\(\alpha=2\)). They derived analytic expressions for the quartic coefficient \(S_4(n)\) from the fourth derivative of the energy density with respect to proton fraction at \(x=1/2\), solving the coupled field equations for the meson fields and their derivatives up to third order. The coupling constants were fitted to reproduce the saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter (binding energy \(-16\) MeV, compressibility \(K=230\) MeV) and the symmetry energy \(S_2(n_0)=30\) MeV with its slope \(L\) in the range 46–112 MeV.

## Key numbers

- At saturation density \(n_0 = 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), \(S_4(n_0)\) values range between 0.44 and 0.65 MeV for the linear model and between 0.49 and 0.61 MeV for the quadratic model, for \(C_\delta^2\) from 0 to 3.5 \(\mathrm{fm}^2\).
- For the quadratic model at densities a few times \(n_0\), \(S_4\) reaches over 1000 MeV; for the linear model at these densities, \(S_4\) oscillates between \(-100\) and 100 MeV.
- The kinetic contribution to \(S_4\) at saturation is 0.5 MeV (nonrelativistic) and at very high densities does not exceed 3 MeV; the experimental value of \(S_2(n_0)\) is 31.2 MeV.
- The symmetry energy slope \(L\) for the most appropriate case (\(C_\delta^2 = 3.5\ \mathrm{fm}^2\)) is around 50 MeV (46.8 MeV for linear model, 55.2 MeV for quadratic model).

## Context

This work extends earlier RMF studies of the quartic term by Lee (1998) and Cai (2011), which found \(S_4\) typically one order of magnitude smaller than \(S_2\), and Hartree-Fock calculations by Chen (2009), Xu (2010), Pu (2017), and Liu (2018) that reached similar conclusions. It also connects to recent chiral effective field theory results by Kaiser (2015) that suggested a logarithmic asymmetry dependence \((1-2x)^4 \ln|1-2x|\), questioning the analyticity of the even-power expansion. The anomalously large quartic term found here, especially for the quadratic \(\sigma\)-\(\delta\) coupling, suggests that the standard power-series expansion in asymmetry may break down at high density, potentially requiring non-analytic contributions or alternative expansions for neutron star matter modeling.
