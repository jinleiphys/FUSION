# Transverse momentum broadening of vector boson production in high energy nuclear collisions

> We calculate in perturbative QCD the transverse momentum broadening of vector boson production in high energy nuclear collisions. We evaluate the effect of initial-state parton multiple scattering for the production of the Drell-Yan virtual photon and $W/Z$ bosons. We calculate both the initial- and final-state multiple scattering effect for the production of heavy quarkonia and their transverse momentum broadening in both NRQCD and Color Evaporation model of quarkonium formation. We find that J/$ψ$ and $Υ$ broadening in hadron-nucleus collision is close to $2C_A/C_F$ times the corresponding Drell-Yan broadening, which gives a good description of existing Fermilab data. Our calculations are also consistent with RHIC data on J/$ψ$ broadening in relativistic heavy ion collisions. We predict the transverse momentum broadening of vector boson (J/$ψ$, $Υ$, and $W/Z$) production in relativistic heavy ion collisions at the LHC, and discuss the role of the vector boson broadening in diagnosing medium properties.

## Key claim

The paper calculates that the transverse momentum broadening of J/ψ and Υ in hadron-nucleus collisions is close to 2CA/CF times the corresponding Drell-Yan broadening, which is twice the naive expectation of CA/CF = 9/4, and this result depends on the inclusion of both initial-state and final-state multiple scattering effects as well as the specific hadronization model (NRQCD or Color Evaporation model).

## Method

The authors performed perturbative QCD calculations of the averaged transverse momentum square ⟨qT²⟩ for vector boson production, evaluating double-scattering diagrams in a covariant gauge using collinear factorization with twist-4 parton correlation functions. For Drell-Yan and W/Z bosons, only initial-state parton multiple scattering was considered, while for heavy quarkonia (J/ψ, Υ), both initial-state and final-state rescattering effects were calculated within the NRQCD and Color Evaporation models of quarkonium formation.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of heavy quarkonium broadening to Drell-Yan broadening is close to 2CA/CF, where CA = 3 and CF = 4/3, giving a numerical factor of approximately 4.5
- The Drell-Yan broadening Δ⟨qT²⟩DY is proportional to CF × (8π²αs/(Nc²-1)) × λ² × A^(1/3), where λ² is a non-perturbative parameter and A is the atomic weight
- The initial-state rescattering alone would give a ratio CA/CF = 9/4 = 2.25, but the data show a ratio as large as 5, twice the naive expectation
- The new analysis by Johnson et al. (2006) found Drell-Yan broadening about twice as large as previously published values, representing a difference of more than three standard deviations

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD treatment of Drell-Yan transverse momentum broadening by Xiaofeng (1999) and Fries (2002) to heavy quarkonium production, addressing the discrepancy noted by Johnson et al. (2006) and JCPeng (2004) between the naive color-factor expectation (CA/CF) and the observed ratio of quarkonium to Drell-Yan broadening. The calculations enable the use of vector boson broadening as a diagnostic tool for medium properties in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC, particularly by comparing W/Z boson broadening (which has no final-state interaction) with heavy quarkonium broadening to separate initial-state from final-state effects.
