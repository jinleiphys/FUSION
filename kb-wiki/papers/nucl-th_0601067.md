# Systematic study of isoscaling behavior in projectile fragmentation by the statistical abrasion-ablation model

> The isospin effect and isoscaling behavior in projectile fragmentation have been systematically investigated by a modified statistical abrasion-ablation (SAA) model. The normalized peak differences and reduced isoscaling parameters are found to decrease with ($Z_{proj}-Z$)/$Z_{proj}$ or the excitation energy per nucleon and have no significant dependence on the size of reaction systems. Assuming a Fermi-gas behavior, the excitation energy dependence of the symmetry energy coefficients are tentatively extracted from $α$ and $β$ which looks consistent with the experimental data. It is pointed out that the reduced isoscaling parameters can be used as an observable to study excitation extent of system and asymmetric nuclear equation of state in heavy ion collisions.

# Systematic study of isoscaling behavior in projectile fragmentation by the statistical abrasion-ablation model

## Key claim
The reduced isoscaling parameters α/Δ[(Z/A)²] and β/Δ[(N/A)²] extracted from projectile-like fragments in the statistical abrasion-ablation model show a universal dependence on excitation energy per nucleon, independent of reaction system size, and can serve as an observable for studying the asymmetric nuclear equation of state, but the symmetry energy coefficients extracted from these parameters (around 15 MeV at E*/A ≈ 1 MeV) are lower than the standard liquid-drop value of 25 MeV, and the extraction relies on a tentative Fermi-gas assumption with an inverse level density parameter a = 10 MeV⁻¹.

## Method
The authors used a modified statistical abrasion-ablation (SAA) model to simulate projectile fragmentation reactions for six pairs of neutron-rich and neutron-deficient projectiles (⁴⁰/³⁶Ar, ⁴⁸/⁴⁰Ca, ⁶⁴/⁵⁸Ni, ⁸⁶/⁷⁸Kr, ¹²⁴/¹¹²Sn, and ¹²⁹/¹³⁶Xe) on a ¹¹²Sn target at 60A MeV. The first stage (abrasion) describes nucleon removal via independent nucleon-nucleon collisions in the overlap zone using interacting tubes and binomial distributions, while the second stage (ablation) treats de-excitation by cascade evaporation of light particles using a statistical model with excitation energy E* = 13.3 ⟨ΔA(b)⟩ MeV. Isoscaling parameters α and β were extracted from exponential fits to yield ratios R₂₁(N,Z) = C exp(αN + βZ) for the more neutron-rich versus less neutron-rich projectile pairs.

## Key numbers
- Symmetry energy coefficient extracted from α and β at E*/A ≈ 1 MeV: around 15 MeV, compared to the standard liquid-drop value of 25 MeV.
- Excitation energy per nucleon for the prefragment is calculated using E* = 13.3 ⟨ΔA(b)⟩ MeV, where 13.3 MeV is the mean excitation energy per abraded nucleon.
- Inverse level density parameter a = 10 MeV⁻¹ used in the Fermi-gas relation E*/A = (1/a)T² to convert excitation energy to temperature.
- Normalized peak difference ΔA_peak/ΔA_proj decreases exponentially with (Z_proj − Z)/Z_proj and shows "very slight difference" among different-size projectiles.
- Isoscaling parameters α and |β| decrease with increasing E*/A, with values differing across reaction systems before reduction.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous SAA model studies (Fang 2000, 2001) on isospin effects in projectile fragmentation, where they demonstrated that isotopic distribution shifts toward neutron-rich side for neutron-rich projectiles but disappear when (Z_proj−Z)/Z_proj > 0.5. It applies the isoscaling formalism developed by Tsang (2001) for multifragmentation of hot sources to projectile-like fragments, showing that the same scaling laws hold. The extracted symmetry energy coefficients are compared with experimental results from Le Fevre (2003) at 300 and 600A MeV and Souliotis (2005) at around 25A MeV, finding consistency despite different incident energies. The work enables using reduced isoscaling parameters as a system-size-independent probe of excitation extent and the asymmetric nuclear equation of state in heavy-ion collisions, though the authors caution that the SAA model does not include the symmetry energy term explicitly.
