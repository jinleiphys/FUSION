# Open heavy flavor and J/psi at RHIC and LHC within a transport model

> The production and space-time evolution of heavy quarks and J/psi in the quark gluon plasma is studied within the partonic transport model Boltzmann Approach to MultiParton Scatterings (BAMPS). This framework allows interactions among all partons: gluons, light quarks and heavy quarks. Heavy quarks, in particular, interact with the rest of the medium via binary scatterings with a running coupling and a more precise Debye screening which is derived from hard thermal loop calculations. We compare our results of the elliptic flow and nuclear modification factor not only to experimental data of heavy flavor electrons at RHIC, but also to LHC data of heavy flavor electrons, muons, D mesons, and non-prompt J/psi. Where no data is available yet, we make predictions for those observables. Furthermore, results on prompt J/psi elliptic flow are reported for RHIC energy within the same framework, taking the dissociation as well as regeneration of J/psi in the quark-gluon plasma into account.

# Open heavy flavor and J/psi at RHIC and LHC within a transport model

## Key claim
Elastic scatterings alone, even with a running coupling and improved Debye screening, cannot reproduce both the nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow of heavy flavor particles; an effective scaling factor K=3.5 applied to the elastic cross section is used to mimic missing radiative contributions, but the authors note this is an effective description whose validity requires future study.

## Method
The partonic transport model BAMPS (Boltzmann Approach to MultiParton Scatterings) solves the Boltzmann equation for on-shell partons with pQCD interactions, including all binary collisions among gluons, light quarks, and heavy quarks (charm and bottom). Elastic processes are regularized with a screening mass derived from hard thermal loop calculations, and a running coupling is employed for heavy quark interactions. To account for missing radiative 2-to-3 processes, the elastic cross section is scaled by a factor K=3.5, determined by fitting PHENIX heavy flavor electron elliptic flow data at RHIC.

## Key numbers
- Heavy flavor electron elliptic flow v2 at RHIC: BAMPS with K=3.5 reproduces PHENIX data for 20-40% centrality and STAR data for 0-60% centrality, including a bump around pT = 1.7 GeV
- D meson nuclear modification factor RAA at LHC for very central events (b = 3.6 fm): BAMPS prediction is slightly smaller than experimental data points
- Prompt J/psi elliptic flow v2 at RHIC: BAMPS calculations with regeneration are compatible with experimental data showing very small v2 values

## Context
This work extends the BAMPS framework previously applied to open heavy flavor (Uphoff 2010, 2011, 2012) and J/psi (Uphoff 2011, 2012) by providing simultaneous predictions for both open and hidden heavy flavor observables at RHIC and LHC energies. The results enable comparison with data from PHENIX, STAR, and ALICE collaborations, and motivate the ongoing implementation of explicit radiative 2-to-3 processes in BAMPS to test whether they can account for the constant scaling factor K=3.5 used as an effective description.
