# Net-baryon number fluctuations in the Hybrid Quark-Meson-Nucleon model at finite density

> We study the mean-field thermodynamics and the characteristics of the net-baryon number fluctuations at the phase boundaries for the chiral and deconfinement transitions in the Hybrid Quark-Meson-Nucleon model. The chiral dynamics is described in the linear sigma model, whereas the quark confinement is manipulated by a medium-dependent modification of the particle distribution functions, where an additional scalar field is introduced. At low temperature and finite baryon density, the model predicts a first-, second-order chiral phase transition, or a crossover, depending on the expectation value of the scalar field, and a first-order deconfinement phase transition. We focus on the influence of the confinement over higher-order cumulants of the net-baryon number density. We find that whereas the cumulants show a substantial enhancement around the chiral phase transition, they are not as sensitive to the deconfinement transition.

## Key claim

The paper shows that higher-order cumulants of the net-baryon number density are substantially enhanced around the chiral phase transition but are not as sensitive to the deconfinement transition, with the order of the chiral transition (first-order vs. crossover) depending on the value of the parameter \(\alpha b_0\) that controls the momentum cutoff in the nucleon distribution functions.

## Method

The authors employ the Hybrid Quark-Meson-Nucleon (Hybrid QMN) model in a mean-field approximation, combining a parity doublet model for nucleons with quark degrees of freedom from the linear sigma model. Statistical confinement is implemented via an auxiliary scalar field \(b\) that acts as a momentum cutoff: nucleon momenta are restricted below \(\alpha b\) and quark momenta above \(b\), with the bag field \(b\) generated from a vacuum potential. The thermodynamic potential is minimized to obtain mean-field values of the sigma, omega, dilaton, and bag fields, and the net-baryon number density and its cumulants up to fourth order are computed from the resulting equations of state.

## Key numbers

- The positive-parity nucleon mass is fixed to \(m_+ = 938\) MeV and the negative-parity partner to \(m_- = 1500\) MeV, with the chirally invariant mass \(m_0 = 790\) MeV.
- Nuclear matter saturation properties are set at \(E/A(\mu_B = 923\ \text{MeV}) - m_+ = -16\) MeV and \(\rho_B(\mu_B = 923\ \text{MeV}) = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
- For \(\alpha b_0 = 300\) MeV at \(T = 10\) MeV, the first-order chiral transition occurs at \(\mu_B = 1137\) MeV and the deconfinement transition at \(\mu_B = 1573\) MeV.
- The lower bound for \(\alpha b_0\) is \(\gtrsim 300\) MeV to preserve nuclear ground-state properties; the upper bound is set by requiring quark density \(Y_q \leq 0.01\) before deconfinement.

## Context

This work extends the Hybrid QMN model introduced by Benic (2015) from zero temperature to finite temperature, studying the interplay between chiral and deconfinement transitions. It contrasts with Polyakov loop-extended models (e.g., PNJL and PQM models by Fukushima (2003), Ratti (2005), and Schaefer (2007)) by using a statistical confinement mechanism based on a scalar bag field rather than the Polyakov loop, which is questionable at high density. The results enable future studies of net-baryon number fluctuations as probes of the QCD phase diagram, particularly in the low-temperature, high-density region relevant for heavy-ion collisions and neutron star physics.
