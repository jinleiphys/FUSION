# Understanding the radiative decays of vector charmonia to light pseudoscalar mesons

> We show that the newly measured branching ratios of vector charmonia ($J/ψ$, $ψ^\prime$ and $ψ(3770))$ into $γP$, where $P$ stands for light pseudoscalar mesons $π^0$, $η$, and $η^\prime$, can be well understood in the framework of vector meson dominance (VMD) in association with the $η_c$-$η(η^\prime)$ mixings due to the axial gluonic anomaly. These two mechanisms behave differently in $J/ψ$ and $ψ^\prime\to γP$. A coherent understanding of the branching ratio patterns observed in $J/ψ(ψ^\prime)\to γP$ can be achieved by self-consistently including those transition mechanisms at hadronic level. The branching ratios for $ψ(3770)\toγP$ are predicted to be rather small.

## Key claim

The branching ratio patterns observed in $J/\psi$ and $\psi^\prime$ radiative decays to light pseudoscalars can be coherently understood only by including both vector meson dominance (VMD) mechanisms and $\eta_c$-$\eta(\eta^\prime)$ mixings due to the axial gluonic anomaly, with the two mechanisms playing different roles in the two charmonium states.

## Method

The author extracted effective couplings $g_{\psi VP}$ from experimental branching ratios for $J/\psi(\psi^\prime, \psi(3770))\to VP$ (where $V$ is a vector meson and $P$ a pseudoscalar) using the VMD framework, then computed the radiative decay amplitudes for $J/\psi(\psi^\prime, \psi(3770))\to \gamma P$ by summing over intermediate vector meson poles. These VMD amplitudes were added coherently with amplitudes from $\eta_c$-$\eta(\eta^\prime)$ mixing, with a relative phase $\delta$ determined by fitting to the measured branching ratios.

## Key numbers

- $\lambda_{\eta\eta_c} = -4.6 \times 10^{-3}$ and $\lambda_{\eta^\prime\eta_c} = -1.2 \times 10^{-2}$: mixing angles between $\eta_c$ and light pseudoscalars extracted from axial anomaly (Chao 1990)
- $BR(J/\psi\to \gamma\pi^0) = (3.49^{+0.33}_{-0.30})\times 10^{-5}$ (experiment) vs. $(1.64\sim 2.04)\times 10^{-5}$ (theory, VMD only)
- $BR(J/\psi\to \gamma\eta) = (1.104\pm 0.034)\times 10^{-3}$ (experiment) vs. $(1.05\sim 1.06)\times 10^{-3}$ (theory, coherent sum)
- $BR(J/\psi\to \gamma\eta^\prime) = (5.28\pm 0.15)\times 10^{-3}$ (experiment) vs. $(5.20\sim 5.22)\times 10^{-3}$ (theory, coherent sum)
- $BR(\psi^\prime\to \gamma\pi^0) = (1.58\pm 0.40\pm 0.13)\times 10^{-6}$ (experiment) vs. $(0.66\sim 1.15)\times 10^{-7}$ (theory, VMD only)
- $BR(\psi^\prime\to \gamma\eta) = (1.38\pm 0.48\pm 0.09)\times 10^{-6}$ (experiment) vs. $(1.39\sim 1.53)\times 10^{-6}$ (theory, coherent sum)
- $BR(\psi^\prime\to \gamma\eta^\prime) = (1.26\pm 0.03\pm 0.08)\times 10^{-4}$ (experiment) vs. $(1.14\sim 1.19)\times 10^{-4}$ (theory, coherent sum)
- Predicted $BR(\psi(3770)\to \gamma\eta^\prime) = 1.64\times 10^{-6}$ (VMD only, $J/\psi$ pole)

## Context

This work extends the earlier QCD sum rule analysis of Novikov (1979) and the $\eta_c$-$\eta(\eta^\prime)$ mixing extraction by Chao (1990) and Feldmann (1998), which assumed saturation of $J/\psi\to\gamma\eta^{(\prime)}$ by the mixing mechanism alone. The new BESIII data for $\psi^\prime\to\gamma P$ showed that the mixing mechanism alone fails for $\psi^\prime$, and the author demonstrates that the VMD contribution via $\psi^\prime\to J/\psi\eta\to\gamma\eta$ is essential. This enables a unified hadronic-level description connecting the radiative decays to the $J/\psi(\psi^\prime)\to VP$ hadronic decays, with implications for the "$\rho\pi$ puzzle" in charmonium physics.
