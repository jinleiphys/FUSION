# White Dwarf Structure and Binary Inspiral Gravitational Waves from Quantum Hadrodynamics

> White dwarfs, one of the compact objects in the universe, play a crucial role in astrophysical research and provide a platform for exploring nuclear physics. In this work, we extend the relativistic mean field approach by using a Walecka-type quantum hadrodynamics model to capture the intricate structure of white dwarfs. We calculate nuclear properties, Coulomb energy, and photon energy within white dwarfs in a unified framework. By carefully calibrating the model parameters to align with nuclear matter properties, we successfully reproduce the structures of several elements in white dwarfs, such as the isotopes of $\rm C$ and $^{16}\rm O$, except for the unnaturally deeply bound state $^4$He. Furthermore, we predict the characteristics of white dwarfs composed of atom-like units and the gravitational waves stemming from binary white dwarf inspirals incorporating tidal deformability contributions up to the 2.5 post-Newtonian order. These results shed light on the structure of white dwarfs and provide valuable information for future gravitational wave detection. This methodological advancement allows for a cohesive analysis of white dwarfs, neutron stars, and the nuclear pasta within a unified theoretical framework.

## Key claim

The authors extend the relativistic mean field (RMF) approach using a Walecka-type quantum hadrodynamics model to describe white dwarfs as composed of atom-like units (Wigner-Seitz cells), but note that the model fails to reproduce the binding energy of \(^{4}\)He and shows increasing deviation from experimental data for nuclei with large neutron-proton differences, attributed to oversimplified isospin interactions.

## Method

The authors employed a Walecka-type quantum hadrodynamics Lagrangian including nucleons, electrons, photons, and sigma, omega, and rho mesons, solved under the relativistic mean field approximation with spherical symmetry. They calculated nuclear properties by iteratively solving the equations of motion for meson fields within a sphere of radius 5 fm, using boundary conditions of vanishing nuclear density and its derivative at the surface. The white dwarf equation of state was obtained by varying the volume of atom-like units (each containing a nucleus surrounded by homogeneous electron gas) and applying the thermodynamic relation for pressure, then solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for mass-radius relations and the Love number equation for tidal deformability.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean field approach previously applied to homogeneous nuclear matter and nuclear matter clusters (Xia 2022) to white dwarf structure, building on the Wigner-Seitz cell concept introduced by Salpeter (1961) and the relativistic Feynman-Metropolis-Teller model of Rotondo (2011). The approach enables a unified theoretical framework for describing neutron stars, neutron star crusts, and white dwarfs, and provides gravitational wave predictions for binary white dwarf inspirals that could guide future space-based detectors such as LISA, Taiji, and Tianqin.
