# QCD Equation of State and Phase Diagram from Holographic Black Holes

> By using the AdS/CFT correspondence, we construct an Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton model to map the thermodynamics of strongly interacting matter. The holographic model, constrained to reproduce the lattice QCD equation of state at zero baryon chemical potential, predicts a critical end point and a first order phase transition line. We also obtain the equation of state of the model for a large region of the phase diagram. We characterize the crossover transition region by two lines, one associated to the inflection of the second order baryon susceptibility, and another one associated to the minimum of the square of the speed of sound along trajectories of constant entropy per baryon number. We observe that both lines merge at the critical point.

## Key claim

The holographic Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton model, constrained to reproduce lattice QCD thermodynamics at zero baryon chemical potential, predicts a critical end point and a first-order phase transition line at finite chemical potential; the crossover region is characterized by two distinct lines (the inflection of the second-order baryon susceptibility and the minimum of the square of the speed of sound along constant entropy-per-baryon trajectories) which merge at the critical point.

## Method

The authors constructed a five-dimensional Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton gravitational action with two free functions: the dilaton potential V(phi) and the Maxwell-dilaton coupling f(phi). V(phi) was fixed by matching the holographic equation of state to lattice QCD results from Borsanyi et al. (2013) at zero baryon chemical potential, while f(phi) was fixed by matching the second-order baryon susceptibility to lattice results from Bellwied et al. (2015) at zero chemical potential. Black hole initial conditions (phi_0, Phi_1) were varied to map a rectangular region of the QCD phase diagram up to mu_B = 1100 MeV, and the first-order transition line was located by integrating the entropy density over temperature in the multivalued region and finding where the resulting pressure curve crosses itself, analogous to a Maxwell equal-area construction.

## Key numbers

- Critical end point located at T_c = 89 MeV and mu_B^c = 724 MeV, identified by the divergence of the second-order baryon susceptibility.
- The holographic model maps the phase diagram up to mu_B = 1100 MeV, though solutions can be extended to the maximum value where black hole fields remain asymptotically AdS.
- The crossover region is characterized by two lines (inflection of chi_2^B and minimum of c_s^2) that merge at the critical point.

## Context

This work extends the holographic approach of Critelli et al. (2017) and Grefa et al. (2021) by providing a full equation of state over a large region of the QCD phase diagram, constrained by lattice QCD results at zero chemical potential. The model addresses the Fermi sign problem that prevents lattice QCD calculations at finite baryon chemical potential, and it provides a tool to guide experimental searches for the QCD critical point in beam energy scan programs at RHIC and the LHC. The results enable future hydrodynamic simulations of heavy ion collisions that require an equation of state with a critical point and first-order transition.
