# Minimizing Effective Many-Body Interactions

> A simple two-level model is developed and used to test the properties of effective interactions for performing nuclear structure calculations in truncated model spaces. It is shown that the effective many-body interactions sensitively depend on the choice of the single-particle basis and they appear to be minimized when a self- consistent Hartree-Fock basis is used.

## Key claim

The effective many-body interactions (three-body, four-body, etc.) that are neglected in truncated shell-model calculations depend sensitively on the choice of the single-particle basis, and these neglected contributions appear to be minimized when a self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) basis is used, making the two-body effective interaction alone a good approximation.

## Method

The authors constructed a simple two-level model with two single-particle states (each holding up to four nucleons) and defined a Hamiltonian with specified single-particle energies and 14 antisymmetrized two-body matrix elements. They first diagonalized the Hamiltonian in the full two-level space for A=2, 3, and 4 to obtain exact ground-state energies. Then they truncated the model space to only the lower level, calculated the energy-independent two-body effective interaction using the Lee-Suzuki iterative procedure on the starting-energy-dependent Brueckner G matrix, and compared the resulting ground-state energies (neglecting three- and more-body effective interactions) to the exact results for both an arbitrary (non-self-consistent) basis and a self-consistent HF basis.

## Key numbers

- For A=4 in the arbitrary basis, the two-body effective interaction alone gave a ground-state energy of -23.916 MeV, compared to the exact result of -27.703 MeV (a discrepancy of about 3.8 MeV).
- For A=4 in the self-consistent HF basis, the two-body effective interaction alone gave a ground-state energy of -27.645 MeV, extremely close to the exact result of -27.703 MeV (discrepancy of 0.058 MeV).
- The HF single-particle energies were epsilon_1' = -15.0323 MeV and epsilon_2' = 5.3808 MeV, with the new basis states given by |1'> = 0.92785|1> + 0.37295|2> and |2'> = -0.37295|1> + 0.92785|2>.
- The two-body effective interaction matrix elements in the HF basis were (V_eff^(2))^01 = 6.091 and (V_eff^(2))^10 = 4.738.

## Context

This work extends the effective interaction theory developed by Lee and Suzuki (1980) and the folded-diagram approach, using a simple two-level model to test how the choice of single-particle basis affects the convergence of the many-body effective interaction expansion. The results suggest that for realistic nuclear structure calculations using codes such as OXBASH, one should choose a single-particle basis close to self-consistent for each mass number A in order to minimize neglected effective many-body forces, enabling more accurate shell-model calculations in truncated model spaces.
