# DUNE as the Next-Generation Solar Neutrino Experiment

> We show that the Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment (DUNE), with significant but feasible new efforts, has the potential to deliver world-leading results in solar neutrinos. With a 100 kton-year exposure, DUNE could detect $\gtrsim 10^5$ signal events above 5 MeV electron energy. ## Key claim

DUNE, with significant but feasible new investments in its MeV detection capabilities, could deliver world-leading solar neutrino results, including separate precision measurements of neutrino-mixing parameters and the \({}^{8}\)B flux, a \(>10\sigma\) day-night effect, and the first detection of the _hep_ flux, provided that systematic uncertainties on the \(\nu_e + {}^{40}\)Ar cross section and detector calibration are reduced to the 1% level.

The authors simulate DUNE's solar neutrino sensitivity using a 100 kton-year exposure, modeling two detection channels: the charged-current \(\nu_e + {}^{40}\)Ar reaction and the elastic-scattering \(\nu_{e,\mu,\tau} + e^{-}\) channel. ## Key numbers

* With 100 kton-year exposure, DUNE could detect \(\gtrsim 10^5\) signal events above 5 MeV electron energy.
* For \({}^{8}\)B neutrinos, the \(\nu_e + {}^{40}\)Ar channel yields \(9.9 \times 10^4\) events outside the forward cone; the \(\nu_{e,\mu,\tau} + e^{-}\) channel yields \(2.6 \times 10^4\) events inside the forward cone.
* The day-night asymmetry is expected to be \(A_{D/N} \simeq -(7.9 \pm 0.8)\%\), a \(10.4\sigma\) effect.
* DUNE could measure \(\sin^2\theta_{12}\) to 3.0% and \(\Delta m^2_{21}\) to 5.9%, a factor of \(\simeq 1.5\) and \(\simeq 3\) better than all previous solar experiments combined.
* The \({}^{8}\)B flux could be measured to 2.5% precision, a factor \(\simeq 1.6\) better than SNO's final precision of \(\simeq 4\%\).
* The _hep_ flux could be detected for the first time with 11% precision, a factor \(\simeq 3\) better than the current theoretical uncertainty of 30%.
* For _hep_ neutrinos, the \(\nu_e + {}^{40}\)Ar channel yields 150 events above 11 MeV electron energy.

## Context

This work extends the solar neutrino program beyond the achievements of Super-Kamiokande and SNO (Aharmim 2011, Abe 2016), which are limited by statistics and the inability to separately measure mixing parameters and fluxes with a single channel. The proposed DUNE program would enable a precision comparison of neutrino-mixing parameters with the reactor-based JUNO experiment (An 2015), potentially revealing new physics such as CPT violation or non-standard interactions. It also enables the most precise measurement of the \({}^{8}\)B flux, which is sensitive to the solar core temperature, and the first detection of the _hep_ flux, probing physical conditions far from the solar center.
